Ferrari's Pivotal Canada: Leclerc Needs This Weekend to Go Right

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 19, 2026

The number tells the story. Ferrari's constructors' championship lead over McLaren was 44 points after the Japanese Grand Prix. It is now 16 points. Twenty-eight points lost in two races, as McLaren's improving form and Ferrari's own difficulties have converged to create an urgent situation for the Scuderia heading into Montreal.

Charles Leclerc, Ferrari's lead driver and the man most capable of reversing this trend, arrives in Canada third in the drivers' championship with 59 points — 41 behind Kimi Antonelli. The deficit to the championship leader is real. The more pressing concern, race by race, is stopping the points drain in the constructors' battle that could see McLaren overtake Ferrari before the summer break.

What Went Wrong

Miami was the most damaging single event for Ferrari's championship cause in 2026. Leclerc received a 20-second post-race penalty for cutting the circuit on the final lap — a decision he accepted responsibility for, saying "all on me" in his post-race comments. The penalty dropped him from a points-scoring position to eighth, erasing nearly all of the good work the team had done during the race.

Hamilton, who joined Ferrari this season, was collected in a lap-1 collision with Franco Colapinto and never recovered the track position necessary to score the points Ferrari needed. Two cars. Two disasters. McLaren collected 33 points from the same race with Norris second and Piastri third. The swing was enormous.

Why Montreal Could Suit Ferrari

Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve is a street circuit with permanent barriers and a stop-start character that tests mechanical grip, aerodynamic downforce, and brake management. These characteristics have historically aligned well with Ferrari's car design philosophy — the Scuderia's machinery tends to shine in environments where low-speed corner traction and stability under braking are at a premium.

Leclerc has been notably strong at high-downforce circuits across his career. The demanding chicane sequences at Gilles-Villeneuve require patience and precision — attributes he consistently demonstrates. In qualifying trim, he is capable of pushing close to the limit of any circuit, and Montreal's layout rewards drivers willing to commit to late braking at the hairpin.

The Strategy Demand

Beyond pace, Ferrari's Canada challenge is also about strategy. The sprint weekend format adds complexity: setting the car up without a free practice session for the sprint format, while also ensuring the main race specification is competitive. Ferrari's engineering team will need to be clinical about the balance between sprint performance and race pace.

The Wall of Champions at the final chicane adds a constraint that affects strategy — drivers under pressure who push too hard at the exit of the second chicane end up in the barrier. Leclerc's discipline in managing risk while racing at maximum effort will be tested throughout the weekend.

What's Next

Ferrari needs more than a points finish in Canada — they need a result that changes the momentum of their season. A Leclerc podium, combined with McLaren scoring minimal points, would stabilize the constructors' standings and remind the paddock that Ferrari's early-season lead was not an accident. The Canadian Grand Prix runs May 22-24 at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve in Montreal. Sprint qualifying begins Friday evening. The race is Sunday. Ferrari's 2026 season may hinge significantly on what happens this week.
