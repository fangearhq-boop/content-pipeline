Miami Grand Prix Preview: New Rules, Sprint Format, and a Championship That's Wide Open

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | April 24, 2026

Formula 1 returns on May 1 after its longest enforced break of the 2026 season, and it returns with a vengeance. The Miami Grand Prix — Round 4 of the championship — is a Sprint weekend with new technical regulations, multiple teams bringing significant car upgrades, and a title race that remains genuinely unsettled.

If the first three rounds felt like an opening act, Miami is where the 2026 season truly declares itself.

The Championship Picture

Going into Miami, Mercedes' Andrea Kimi Antonelli leads the Drivers' Championship with 72 points — nine ahead of teammate George Russell on 63. At just 19 years and 216 days old when he first took the lead, Antonelli is the youngest driver in Formula 1 history to sit atop the standings. He has won back-to-back races in China and Japan after Russell's Australian victory, and the nine-point gap is meaningful — but not yet comfortable.

Ferrari's Charles Leclerc and Lewis Hamilton sit third and fourth. Hamilton took his first Ferrari podium in China and has spoken about being "back to his best" — which makes Miami a compelling circuit to watch him operate on, given the long straights and high-downforce sections that favor a confident, committed driving style.

In the constructors', Mercedes leads with 135 points, with Ferrari second on 90. McLaren are third on 56 after a difficult start to the season that included both Norris and Piastri failing to start in China.

The Regulations and the Upgrades

The FIA's revised 2026 technical regulations come into full effect in Miami. Energy recharge has been reduced in qualifying (8MJ to 7MJ), while the super-clip peak power has been increased for more consistent straightline speed. All teams must adapt their software for these changes — which is precisely why FP1 has been extended to 90 minutes.

Several teams are bringing meaningful hardware updates. Ferrari tested a new floor and their Macarena aerodynamic wing during a filming day at Monza in late April, with the package designed to close the 45-point constructors' deficit to Mercedes. McLaren's first significant aero upgrades arrive in Miami. Red Bull ran an extensive set of changes at Silverstone, hoping to address the fundamental balance issues in the RB22 that have left Verstappen sixth in the standings through three rounds.

What's Next

The Miami Grand Prix weekend begins Friday May 1 with the extended FP1 session (12:00-13:30 local). Sprint qualifying and the Sprint race follow on Saturday, with full qualifying also on Saturday. The race itself takes place Sunday May 3 at 4 PM ET. After five weeks without racing, the paddock, the teams, and the fans are ready. Miami, as always, will not disappoint.
