Verstappen Racing GT3 at Nurburgring April 18-19 During F1's Five-Week Break

By Ryan Calloway April 7, 2026

No Time Off for the Four-Time Champion

Formula 1's five-week break between the Japanese Grand Prix and the Miami GP was supposed to give drivers time to rest, recharge, and work at their factories. Max Verstappen has other plans. The four-time world champion has confirmed he will race in the 24h Nurburgring qualifiers on April 18-19 — a pair of four-hour endurance events that serve as preparation for the 24-hour Nurburgring race in May.

Verstappen will pilot a Mercedes-AMG GT3 in Red Bull livery — an intriguing combination given the current dynamic between Red Bull and Mercedes in F1. His entry runs under the "Verstappen Racing" banner, with car number 3. His driver lineup includes Dani Juncadella, Jules Gounon, and Lucas Auer alongside him.

Redemption at the Nordschleife

The Nurburgring carries specific significance for Verstappen. He was disqualified from an earlier NLS race at the Nordschleife and carries motivation to return for a clean result. The qualifying events on April 18-19 are each four-hour races — substantial endurance challenges that test driver consistency and car management in a way that differs entirely from F1's sprint format.

The 24-hour Nurburgring race itself — the ADAC Ravenol 24h Nürburgring — is scheduled for May 14-17, running during the F1 season calendar. Whether Verstappen participates in that event alongside his F1 commitments remains to be seen, but the April qualifiers are his confirmed starting point.

What It Says About Verstappen

In the context of his current frustration with F1's 2026 regulations, Verstappen's decision to spend his only significant break of the season racing a GT3 at the Nurburgring speaks volumes. He is not stepping away from motorsport — he is chasing it in every available form. That is the profile of a driver who loves racing itself, not just the prestige that comes with winning a world championship.

The questions about his F1 future remain open. The 2026 formula continues to frustrate him. The Red Bull RB22 has not delivered. But whatever Verstappen ultimately decides about his future in Formula 1, nobody will ever question his commitment to competition. He's using his vacation to race at the Nurburgring. That says it all.

What's Next

Verstappen will race the Nurburgring qualifiers on April 18-19 before returning to F1 preparations for the Miami Grand Prix (May 1-3). The April 9 FIA summit two days from now will have significant implications for how the rest of his 2026 F1 season looks. If rule changes provide relief, his Miami weekend could look very different from Japan. If not, the Nurburgring may represent one of the more enjoyable weekends of his entire 2026 campaign.
