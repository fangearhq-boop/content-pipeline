Ferrari ADUO Power Unit Cleared for Canadian GP — Closing the Gap to Mercedes

By Marcus Cole April 7, 2026

What ADUO Means and Why Ferrari Qualified

The Additional Development and Upgrade Opportunities mechanism — ADUO — is Formula 1's mid-season catch-up provision for power unit manufacturers who fall significantly behind their rivals. It allows them to introduce performance upgrades beyond the normal in-season development limitations, specifically to close performance deficits that have become too large to address through conventional means.

Ferrari qualified for ADUO because their SF-26 power unit is estimated to be 20-30 horsepower behind the Mercedes unit — a gap large enough to meet the eligibility threshold. The FIA has cleared the Scuderia to introduce the updated power unit from the Canadian Grand Prix onward.

What Changes in the ADUO Update

The upgrade targets the thermal side of the internal combustion engine, with revisions that also include slightly larger battery cells complementing the revised ICE architecture. Ferrari engineers have been developing the package during the April break, and the Monza filming day on April 22 — 15 days from today — will allow Lewis Hamilton and Charles Leclerc to evaluate the unit on one of the most power-sensitive circuits in the calendar.

Monza's long straights and minimal aerodynamic load make it an ideal testing environment for raw power unit performance. If the ADUO upgrade delivers its target improvement, engineers will have meaningful data before the package races for the first time at the Canadian Grand Prix.

The Stakes for Hamilton and Leclerc

Hamilton and Leclerc have been fighting not just Mercedes — the championship leader — but their own equipment in the opening phase of 2026. Leclerc sits third in the championship with 49 points, 23 behind Antonelli. Hamilton is fourth with 41. The gap between Ferrari and Mercedes as constructors stands at 45 points, with 90 and 135 respectively after three races.

The ADUO update is not a guaranteed championship fix. Twenty or thirty horsepower of improvement is meaningful, but Mercedes has been dominant in ways that extend beyond raw power unit output — their chassis and aerodynamic package have also been strong. Ferrari will need everything to work together at Miami and beyond. The ADUO is the first piece of a broader recovery bid.

What's Next

Hamilton and Leclerc will share driving duties at the Monza filming day on April 22 before the season resumes at Miami on May 1-3. Ferrari is also bringing its revised floor — internally called the 'Macarena' rear wing configuration — and PU software updates to Miami as part of their three-point upgrade plan. If all three elements work together, the SF-26 entering Miami should be a meaningfully faster car than the one that raced in Japan. The Canadian Grand Prix, where the ADUO PU will debut, provides a final development opportunity before the European races that begin in earnest in May and June.
