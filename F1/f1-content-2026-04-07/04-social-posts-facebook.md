### STORY 2: FIA April 9 Summit — Two Days Away, Unanimous Agreement Required

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM ET

Two days from now, every major player in Formula 1 will sit down to try to fix the 2026 season.

The FIA's April 9 summit brings together team technical chiefs, power unit manufacturers, and F1 leadership — all in one room with six proposed rule changes and a requirement for unanimous agreement. No team veto. All ten teams must say yes.

The proposals on the table address the core problems that have emerged in the season's first three races: the dangerous speed differentials created when cars are in "boost mode" versus "energy saving mode," the excessive qualifying lift-and-coast tactics that make qualifying spectacles less clean, and the fixed aero zones that restrict racing. The proposed fixes include raising the super clipping limit from 250kW to 350kW, reducing the maximum energy recovery per lap from 9MJ to as low as 6MJ, and removing the aero zone concept entirely.

The goal is to have changes in place before the Miami Grand Prix on May 3. That's a very tight window. And all 10 teams need to agree. This is Formula 1, where disagreement is practically a sport itself.

Whatever happens Thursday will define the character of the 2026 season.

### STORY 5: Ferrari ADUO Power Unit Cleared — Closing the 20-30bhp Gap to Mercedes

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM ET

Ferrari has received the green light from the FIA to introduce its ADUO (Additional Development and Upgrade Opportunities) power unit upgrade from the Canadian Grand Prix onward.

The context matters here. Ferrari's current SF-26 power unit is estimated to be somewhere between 20 and 30 horsepower behind the Mercedes unit — a gap significant enough to qualify for the ADUO eligibility threshold, which is essentially F1's mid-season catch-up mechanism for manufacturers that fall behind. Ferrari applied and was approved.

The upgrade targets the internal combustion engine side, with slightly larger batteries complementing the revised ICE. The Monza test on April 22 — 15 days away — will allow Hamilton and Leclerc to evaluate the package on one of the calendar's most power-sensitive circuits before Miami.

Ferrari won't close the full gap overnight. But any additional horsepower narrows the chasm, and if the 'Macarena' rear wing and revised floor from the previous upgrade cycle combine with this PU improvement, the SF-26 entering Miami could be meaningfully quicker than the one that raced in Japan.
