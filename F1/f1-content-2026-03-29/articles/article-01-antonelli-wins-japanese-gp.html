Antonelli Wins Japanese GP: Second Victory in Three Races at Suzuka

March 29, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Kimi Antonelli is 19 years old and already has two Formula 1 victories in three races. The teenager from Bologna converted a commanding pole position into a dominant victory at the 2026 Japanese Grand Prix at Suzuka — his second win of the season, continuing a run of performances that have already rewritten the F1 record books.

Antonelli's pole time of 1:28.778 — nearly 0.3 seconds clear of teammate George Russell in qualifying — underlined Mercedes' advantage. He led throughout, managing a mid-race safety car period triggered by a high-speed crash involving Haas driver Ollie Bearman, before pulling away in the final laps to take the checkered flag at the home of Japanese motorsport.

Piastri Returns — and Immediately Podiums

The most remarkable subplot of the race came from McLaren. Oscar Piastri had not started a single F1 race in 2026 before Sunday — a double disaster at McLaren had cost him both Australia (formation lap crash) and China (electrical failure). Arriving at Suzuka as a newcomer to his own season, Piastri qualified P3 and delivered a composed, relentless drive to finish second.

For McLaren, the result was transformational. The team had scored zero championship points from the opening two rounds. Piastri's P2 at Suzuka, combined with Lando Norris's points in Australia and China, moves McLaren to third in the constructors' championship with 56 points.

Leclerc P3, Russell P4 — Ferrari and Mercedes Take the Rest

Charles Leclerc completed the podium in P3 for Ferrari, continuing a consistent season for the Monegasque driver who enters the 5-week break third in the drivers' championship. It was another demonstration of Ferrari's SF-26 performing at a level that makes them the clear second-best team on the grid — even if the gap to Mercedes remains substantial.

George Russell finished P4, his worst result of the season in a weekend where he was simply outpaced by his teammate from qualifying through to the flag. Russell trails Antonelli by nine points in the championship heading into the break — a manageable deficit, but one that may have felt larger given how commanding Antonelli's day was.

Bearman's Crash Adds Drama

The safety car was triggered by a significant high-speed crash involving Ollie Bearman in the Haas, ending what had been a promising race for the young Briton. Bearman entered Japan as the fifth-place driver in the championship — a remarkable position for a driver in his debut full season. His DNF will be a costly blow to his points tally heading into the break.

Antonelli handled the safety car restart with the composure of a driver far older than his 19 years — pulling the gap back out methodically and denying any opportunity for those behind to challenge.

What's Next

The Japanese GP marks the last race on the Formula 1 calendar until the Miami Grand Prix on May 3. The Bahrain and Saudi Arabia Grands Prix were cancelled due to the ongoing conflict in the region, leaving a five-week gap in the schedule.

Mercedes enter that break leading the constructors' championship (135 points) by 45 points from Ferrari (90). Antonelli leads Russell by nine in the drivers' standings. Red Bull, who had no drivers in the points at Suzuka, will spend those five weeks attempting to address the RB22's fundamental problems before the season resumes in Florida.
