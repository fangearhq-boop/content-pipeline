Verstappen Calls RB22 "Undriveable" After Suzuka — Rookie Hadjar Outqualifies Him

March 29, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

After three races of the 2026 Formula 1 season, Max Verstappen has 8 championship points. He is eighth in the drivers' standings. His rookie teammate, Isack Hadjar, outqualified him at Suzuka. And after Saturday's qualifying session, the four-time world champion used one word to describe his car: "undriveable."

The Red Bull crisis is real, it is deep, and it is not going away quickly.

What Happened at Suzuka

Verstappen was eliminated in Q2 at the Japanese Grand Prix, ending the session P11. Hadjar, in his debut Formula 1 season, reached Q3 and qualified P8. The image of a four-time world champion being out-qualified by his debut-season teammate — at a circuit where Verstappen has extensive experience — encapsulated everything that is wrong with Red Bull's 2026 campaign.

In the race, Verstappen finished outside the points. Red Bull collected just 1 point from Suzuka, a deeply grim return for a team that won four consecutive constructors' championships from 2022 through 2025.

After qualifying, Verstappen was direct in his assessment. The RB22, he said, is "undriveable" — his strongest public criticism of the car to date. He added that it would take "months" to close the performance gap to Mercedes and Ferrari.

The Technical Problem: Weight

Reports from within the paddock have identified the core issue: the RB22 is approximately 10 kilograms overweight under Formula 1's new 2026 regulations, which introduced lighter minimum weight requirements as part of the sweeping technical reset. Ten kilograms represents a significant aerodynamic and mechanical handicap — in a sport where fractions of a second per lap determine championship outcomes, carrying excess weight through 23 race weekends is potentially season-defining.

Verstappen has been vocal about the 2026 regulations more broadly, suggesting the new framework has made cars more difficult to drive. Mercedes team principal Toto Wolff offered a pointed response, noting that Verstappen's criticism of the regulations appears colored by his own car's performance. The implication was clear: if the RB22 were competitive, the regulations would not be the story.

The Championship Picture

The scale of Red Bull's problem becomes clearer in the standings. Verstappen has 8 points after three races — fewer than Bearman (17), Norris (15), and Gasly (9). He trails Antonelli, the championship leader, by 64 points. Red Bull sit fourth in the constructors' championship with 16 points, 119 behind Mercedes.

This is the same team, with the same driver, that won every race for a stretch in 2023. The 2026 regulation reset has been a complete reversal of fortunes — from dominant force to midfield struggler in a single winter.

The Hadjar Factor

Isack Hadjar's performance adds a dimension to the Red Bull story that the team would rather not address publicly. The 20-year-old Frenchman, in his first Formula 1 season, has outqualified Verstappen at Suzuka. Whether this reflects Hadjar's talent, Verstappen's frustration impacting his driving, or simply the RB22 being more consistent at certain settings is unclear — but the headline is damaging regardless.

Red Bull's history includes a period, around 2019-2020, when Verstappen was held back by an uncompetitive car relative to Mercedes. He eventually won the championship in 2021. Whether the 2026 season represents a similar trough — from which a recovery is possible — or a more fundamental unraveling remains the defining question for the second half of the season.

What Comes Next

The five-week break before Miami is Red Bull's most valuable asset right now. Verstappen says the team is working on upgrades, and the break provides runway to address the weight issue and recalibrate the car's setup direction. Whether five weeks is enough to close a gap of this magnitude is deeply uncertain.

The Miami Grand Prix on May 3 will be the first real test of whether Red Bull's response to the crisis has been adequate — or whether the 2026 season is already beyond rescue for the reigning constructors' champions.
