Hamilton Races Suzuka in Ferrari Red: The Five-Time Winner at His Favorite Circuit

March 29, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Lewis Hamilton has won the Japanese Grand Prix five times. He has won at Suzuka in McLaren silver, in Mercedes silver, in the wet and the dry, in title fights and in dominant seasons. On Sunday, he arrived at his favorite circuit for the twentieth time — wearing red.

It was the latest in a series of landmark moments that have defined Hamilton's first season at Ferrari. Every race weekend in 2026 carries the weight of context: the same driver, different machinery, different colors, a different chapter of a career that has already placed him among the greatest in the sport's history.

"This Car Has My DNA In It"

Hamilton's transition to Ferrari has been unlike any other car change in his career. Rather than arriving in Maranello and adapting to a car that had already been built, Hamilton spent the winter working directly with Ferrari's simulation team on the SF-26's development. He logged extensive simulator hours, providing feedback on setup direction, aerodynamic balance, and mechanical feel that fed directly into the car's final design.

"Last year I inherited a car," he said ahead of the Suzuka weekend. "This one has my DNA in it." The SF-26, he added, feels "like a go-kart — lighter and nimble, quite fun." The language of a driver who has found his footing in a new home.

That input appears to have made a material difference. The SF-26 is the clear second-fastest car on the grid — ahead of McLaren and far ahead of Red Bull — and Hamilton's familiarity with its character has allowed him to extract performance quickly. A 16-month podium drought that defined his final Mercedes season ended at the Chinese Grand Prix two weeks ago. At Suzuka, he finished P3 in qualifying and delivered another points-scoring performance in the race.

The Suzuka Record

Hamilton's five Japanese GP victories — in 2007, 2014, 2015, 2017, and 2019 — span four different eras of the sport and two different teams. Suzuka is a circuit that demands precision and commitment: its high-speed Esses, the blind crests through Degner, and the 130R corner have separated great drivers from the field for generations. Hamilton's record there is among the best of any driver at any single venue in F1 history.

Whether he adds a sixth Suzuka win in 2026 remains to be seen. At this stage of the season, Mercedes' pace advantage is significant enough that a Hamilton victory at any circuit requires either a safety car lottery or a substantial swing in conditions. But the possibility is real in a way that it was not in 2025, and that is the point.

The 2026 Resurgence

The contrast with 2025 is striking. Hamilton's final year at Mercedes was, by his own account, the worst of his Formula 1 career — a season in which the car's performance and his own form converged downward, leaving him unable to fight for victories or the championship. He arrived at Ferrari carrying questions about whether his best years were behind him.

Three rounds into 2026, those questions look premature. Hamilton has 33 championship points, is one point behind teammate Charles Leclerc, and has shown the kind of consistency that defined his peak Mercedes years. He is fourth in the drivers' standings — not yet a championship threat to Antonelli and Russell, but well inside the conversation.

The five-week break before Miami gives Hamilton time to further develop his relationship with the SF-26 and to continue influencing the car's development direction. He enters that break having achieved something that seemed uncertain twelve months ago: relevance, in red, at 41 years old.
