### STORY 1: Miami GP Sunday Faces Thunderstorm Threat (TIER 1)
Sunday's Formula 1 Miami Grand Prix (4PM local) faces a real weather disruption risk. Forecasts show 100% cloud cover at race start, 37% chance of rain, and thunderstorms possible. Under Miami International Autodrome's lightning safety plan, the event may need to be postponed or stopped if thunderstorms materialize. Friday and Saturday expected dry and hot (30-32°C). The threat specifically targets race day and could scramble strategies or force a red flag stoppage.

Sky Sports' Martin Brundle says Lewis Hamilton's start to 2026 at Ferrari has been "a fascinating study." Brundle: "If there's a sniff of a victory, I think Lewis is still very much capable of taking that." Hamilton 4th in standings with 41 points. Miami's full SF-26 upgrade package gives Ferrari its best chance yet.

### STORY 3: Verstappen "Fundamentally Wrong" — FIA Changes Not Enough (TIER 1)

He has called for a return to V10 or V8 engines. Verstappen sits 9th in the standings with 12 pts. Exit clause concerns remain. F1 president Domenicali continues private talks with the four-time champion.

### STORY 4: FP1 Extended to 90 Minutes — What Teams Need to Learn (TIER 2)
The FIA has extended Friday's Miami FP1 session from 60 to 90 minutes — the first on-track action after a 5-week break, the new regulatory tweaks, and the Sprint format (no free practice Saturday). Teams effectively get one shot to understand the cars under the new rules before the Sprint Qualifying session. FP1 starts noon local time (1PM ET), Friday May 1.

### STORY 5: Constructors' Championship Snapshot — Miami is the Reset (TIER 2)
Mercedes lead with 135 points (Antonelli 72, Russell 63 — won all 3 races). Ferrari 2nd with 90 pts (Leclerc 49, Hamilton 41). McLaren 3rd at 56 pts (Norris 25, Piastri 21). Red Bull last of the big four at 16 pts (Verstappen 12). Miami brings major upgrades from Ferrari and McLaren. The gap to Mercedes could shrink dramatically — or the Silver Arrows could extend their advantage. The real pecking order is about to be revealed.
