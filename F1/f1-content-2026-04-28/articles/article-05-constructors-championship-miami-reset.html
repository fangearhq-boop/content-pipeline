Constructors' Championship Snapshot: Can Anyone Close the Gap to Mercedes at Miami?

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

April 28, 2026

Through three rounds of the 2026 Formula 1 World Championship, the pattern has been consistent and commanding: Mercedes win, everyone else watches. Kimi Antonelli and George Russell have shared race victories across Australia, China, and Japan, and the Silver Arrows lead the constructors' standings with 135 points — 45 ahead of Ferrari, 79 clear of McLaren, and a remarkable 119 ahead of a Red Bull team that has yet to find its footing in the new formula.

But those numbers represent the past. Miami, with its unprecedented package of upgrades and the first race in five weeks, is what teams across the paddock have been calling a "season reset." The question heading into Friday's extended FP1 session is simple: have any of the chasing teams found enough time to meaningfully close the gap to Mercedes — and if so, how much?

Ferrari: 45 Points Back, Betting on Transformation

Ferrari's constructors' position — second with 90 points, combining Charles Leclerc's 49 and Lewis Hamilton's 41 — is respectable on paper but tells a story of underperformance relative to expectations. The Scuderia expected to be Mercedes' primary challenger from race one. A comprehensive SF-26 overhaul, previewed positively in a filming day at Monza in April, is intended to bridge that gap. Team principal Fred Vasseur's declaration that "a new championship will start" in Miami was not rhetorical optimism — it was the result of Ferrari's engineers believing their upgrade package fundamentally changes the car's aerodynamic efficiency. If the simulations match reality on Friday, Ferrari could be a genuine race contender by Sunday.

McLaren: The Bold Claim and the History

McLaren's assessment of their own Miami upgrade is the most dramatic in the paddock. Team principal Andrea Stella described the package as "a completely new car from an aerodynamic standpoint," a claim that positions Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri as genuine contenders rather than development projects. McLaren sit third with 56 points, and the gap to Mercedes — 79 points — is the kind that can only close if race wins start accumulating. Norris won his maiden Grand Prix at Miami in 2024, a fact that adds narrative weight to the weekend. If McLaren's upgrade delivers as advertised, the papaya cars could be first or second on the grid by Saturday Sprint Qualifying.

Red Bull: The Uncomfortable Question

Red Bull's 16 points represent the sport's most uncomfortable subplot. The defending champions — who dominated as recently as last season — have produced Max Verstappen's ninth-place standing and a mounting existential crisis about the team's ability to develop under the 2026 regulations. Their Miami upgrade includes the Macarena-style wing design that has become the aerodynamic shorthand for 2026 development, but early expectations are cautious. Even optimistic projections put Red Bull closing part of the gap to the front — not erasing it overnight.

What's Next

Mercedes' Antonelli leads the drivers' standings with 72 points, nine ahead of teammate Russell. The Silver Arrows will be watching the upgrade results carefully — if Ferrari and McLaren genuinely close to within a second per lap, the constructors' title becomes a real fight over the remaining 19 races. If Mercedes' advantage holds, the championship conversation may effectively be over before the European summer. FP1 on Friday will tell the opening chapter of that story.
