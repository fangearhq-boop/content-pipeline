The 2027 F1 Driver Market Takes Shape: Piastri Confirmed, Verstappen's Options Narrow

By Marcus Cole

July 21, 2026

The summer break is when Formula 1's driver market traditionally moves from background noise to centre stage. With the Hungarian Grand Prix closing out the season's first half this weekend, the landscape of confirmed and unconfirmed seats for 2027 is beginning to take clearer shape — and Monday's statements from Piastri and McLaren CEO Zak Brown have accelerated the picture considerably.

The headline: the market's most prominent rumour has been denied. The question: what happens next?

Who Is Confirmed

The top teams have significant clarity heading into the break. At Mercedes, Kimi Antonelli is on a multi-year deal that will keep him at the team through at least 2027, and George Russell's contract extends through the same period. The championship-leading team will not be reshaping its driver lineup.

Ferrari confirmed Lewis Hamilton through 2027 when team principal Frédéric Vasseur answered "Yes" to a direct question about Hamilton's 2027 presence. Charles Leclerc has a long-term deal that covers 2027 and beyond. Ferrari's lineup is also settled.

McLaren sealed the last significant question Monday: Oscar Piastri confirmed his 2027 commitment at the team, backed by CEO Zak Brown's denial of any conversations with Max Verstappen's camp. Lando Norris is under contract. McLaren's driver lineup for next season is fixed.

The One Question Mark That Matters

Verstappen's contract with Red Bull runs through the end of the 2028 season. However, a performance-related exit clause embedded in the agreement allows him to leave the team if he falls outside the top two in the Drivers' Championship — and with the Belgian Grand Prix result, that condition has been unambiguously met. He can trigger the clause between August and October.

Whether he activates it is a different question from whether he is eligible to activate it. Verstappen's public statements have been carefully minimal — "No" and "Nothing" when asked for updates at Spa. His manager, Raymond Vermeulen, said earlier in the season that Verstappen "wants to finish with Red Bull" — though that statement was made before the McLaren denials removed the most obvious alternative landing spot.

Red Bull's internal situation adds complication. Sources have described Chalerm Yoovidhya and Christian Horner as split on how aggressively to resist the clause and what concessions might be offered to keep Verstappen. The franchise that has structured itself around a single dominant driver is now navigating a future where that driver's commitment is genuinely uncertain.

The Remaining Options

With McLaren off the board, the realistic landing spots for Verstappen — should he exercise the clause — narrow considerably. Audi's F1 programme is accelerating toward its 2026-27 works entry, and a partnership between Verstappen and a major manufacturer building toward competitiveness carries its own appeal. Verstappen himself has connections through the Volkswagen Group network.

A scenario where Verstappen stays at Red Bull on renegotiated terms — a new deal that reflects his leverage and perhaps includes updated exit provisions — also remains possible. The clause gives him power; using that power to extract better conditions internally is a tactical option, not just a transfer threat.

What is clear is that the 2027 grid's biggest remaining variable belongs to Verstappen alone. The summer break begins Sunday night. The conversations that determine the grid's shape may well begin Monday morning.

What's Next

The Hungarian Grand Prix closes the season's first half. When Formula 1 returns from its summer shutdown in late August, the driver market storyline is expected to be at its hottest. Verstappen will have had three weeks to consider his options, consult his management, and decide whether the exit clause — now eligible — is a card worth playing.

By the time autumn arrives, the 2027 grid may look very different from what it does today — or Verstappen may have committed to Red Bull, and the market will close around him. Either outcome will define one of the most compelling off-track stories of the 2026 season.
