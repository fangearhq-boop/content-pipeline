Piastri Confirms McLaren, Brown Denies Verstappen Talks: The Swap That Won't Happen

By Ryan Calloway

July 21, 2026

Weeks of paddock rumour, insider reports, and breathless speculation came to a formal end Monday when both Oscar Piastri and McLaren CEO Zak Brown made their positions unmistakable.

Piastri is staying at McLaren. There are no conversations with Max Verstappen's team about a potential swap deal. The rumour is dead.

"Yes" — that was Piastri's complete answer when asked if he would remain a McLaren driver in 2027. Brown, pressed about reported talks between McLaren and Verstappen's camp, was equally direct: "They're rumours? No conversations." Brown confirmed he had received assurances from both himself and team principal Andrea Stella that Piastri's seat at McLaren is secure.

The Rumour That Ran for Weeks

The speculation had been substantial. Reports from PlanetF1, GPBlog, and several paddock sources described McLaren and Verstappen's team as being in "closing stages" of discussions about a 2027 move — one that would see Verstappen take Piastri's seat and Piastri move to Red Bull in his place. The narrative fit: Verstappen is frustrated at Red Bull, his exit clause is now formally eligible, and McLaren has one of the fastest cars on the grid.

ESPN pushed back almost immediately on the "closing stages" framing, with sources describing the reports as "wide of the mark." At the Belgian GP, Verstappen himself declined every opportunity to offer clarity. Asked for an update, he answered: "No." Asked about the speculation, he replied: "Nothing." His silence, deliberately maintained, had fuelled the rumours rather than quenched them.

Now Piastri and Brown have spoken — and the answer is unambiguous.

What Verstappen's Silence Actually Means

Piastri offered a telling interpretation of Verstappen's lack of denial: the Dutchman may be "exploring options" — speaking to teams informally, listening to offers — without those conversations rising to the level of genuine negotiation. At the top level of motorsport, that distinction matters enormously. A driver "exploring options" is not committing to anything. A driver "in talks" suggests real progress.

According to Brown and McLaren, nothing rose above the first level. Which means the 2027 driver market, as it pertains to McLaren, is settled: Piastri and Norris are the team's drivers, and that is not changing.

Verstappen's exit clause, however, is very much still in play. The performance-related provision in his Red Bull contract allows him to leave the team between August and October if he remains outside the top two in the Drivers' Championship — a condition clearly met after the Belgian GP. Whether he exercises that clause, and where he would go, remains the most compelling open question in Formula 1's driver market.

The Market After the Denial

With McLaren closed off, the landscape of viable 2027 seats for Verstappen narrows significantly. Ferrari's two seats are occupied by Lewis Hamilton (confirmed through 2027) and Charles Leclerc (under a long-term deal). Mercedes has Kimi Antonelli and George Russell, both signed. Norris is at McLaren alongside Piastri.

The credible options become more exotic: an Audi partnership as that team's cars mature, a midfield team that attracts significant works support, or — perhaps the most significant possibility — a scenario where Verstappen's pressure on Red Bull produces an internal resolution that keeps him in Milton Keynes on better terms.

None of those paths are confirmed. But as of Monday, the Piastri-to-Red Bull, Verstappen-to-McLaren swap scenario has been firmly eliminated.

What's Next

The Hungarian Grand Prix (July 24-26) represents the final race before Formula 1's three-week summer shutdown. In the 72 hours between the Hungarian GP and the official summer break, industry observers expect the Verstappen situation to either clarify or intensify. If he exercises his exit clause — or signals he intends to — it will generate the biggest F1 transfer story since Hamilton's move to Ferrari.

For now, Piastri is at McLaren. Brown has no conversations with Verstappen. And Verstappen remains, conspicuously, silent.
