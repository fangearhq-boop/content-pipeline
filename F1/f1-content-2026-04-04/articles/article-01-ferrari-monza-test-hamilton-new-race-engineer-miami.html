Ferrari Plans Monza Test, New Hamilton Race Engineer — Treating Miami Like a New Season

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | April 4, 2026

Ferrari is heading to Monza before Miami, and the scope of their preparation signals just how seriously the Scuderia is taking the fifth round of the 2026 Formula 1 World Championship.

The team has scheduled a private test session at the Autodromo Nazionale Monza in the weeks before the Miami Grand Prix, with both Lewis Hamilton and Charles Leclerc expected to take part. The session — run under FIA filming day regulations limiting mileage to 200km — will debut a new rear wing package that has been in development since the pre-season. Ferrari team principal Fred Vasseur has publicly framed Miami as "a new championship" for the team, and the preparation schedule backs that language up with concrete action.

A New Voice in Hamilton's Ear

Alongside the aerodynamic work, Ferrari is making a significant personnel change: Lewis Hamilton will have a new permanent race engineer at Miami. Cedric Michel-Grosjean takes over the role in what represents a fresh start in one of the most important communication partnerships in motorsport.

The relationship between a Formula 1 driver and their race engineer is foundational to performance. The engineer manages everything from strategy calls to technical feedback during the race, and the chemistry — or lack of it — can be the difference between a podium and an off-pace result. Ferrari's decision to make this change ahead of their most prepared race weekend of the season suggests confidence that Michel-Grosjean can elevate Hamilton's performance in the car's most important developmental phase.

Hamilton currently sits fourth in the drivers' championship with 41 points — 31 behind leader Andrea Antonelli. Getting the best out of him at Miami, on a circuit where Ferrari historically has a stronger performance window, is a priority for the team.

Closing the Power Gap

The new rear wing package is only part of Ferrari's Miami preparation. The Scuderia is also bringing a revised power unit software update — the ADUO package — which has already cleared FIA homologation approval. The combined target is clear: close the estimated half-second-per-lap deficit to Mercedes that has kept Ferrari from genuinely threatening the championship lead through the opening three rounds.

Leclerc is third in the standings at 49 points, while Hamilton is fourth at 41. The gap to Mercedes is real but not insurmountable. A meaningful performance step at Miami — combined with the pressure of an internal championship fight between Antonelli (72 points) and George Russell (63 points) — could create the kind of strategic unpredictability Ferrari needs to capitalize.

The Miami Stakes

Miami has historically suited Ferrari better than some circuits on the calendar. The Hard Rock Stadium layout — 5.41km, 19 corners, three DRS zones — rewards mechanical grip and aerodynamic efficiency in ways that could play to the updated Ferrari package's strengths. Vasseur's "new championship" framing isn't just motivational language. It's a recognition that Ferrari needs a reset moment, and they're engineering one deliberately.

The Monza test is the clearest signal that reset is underway. When Ferrari commits a filming day to a purpose-built pre-race program, it means the upgrade matters too much to debut cold in Florida.

What's Next

The Monza test takes place in the weeks ahead of Miami. Watch for any official Ferrari statements post-session — the team is unlikely to share specific lap time data, but any indication of the package's feel from Hamilton or Leclerc will be telling. Miami qualifying begins May 2. If the rear wing and power unit update delivers on its promise, Ferrari's race pace in Round 4 could be a different conversation entirely from the first three rounds.
