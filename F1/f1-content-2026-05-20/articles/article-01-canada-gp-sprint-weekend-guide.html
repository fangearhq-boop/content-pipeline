Canada GP 2026: Your Complete Sprint Weekend Guide for Circuit Gilles Villeneuve

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 20, 2026

The 2026 Canadian Grand Prix weekend begins Friday in Montreal, and it may be the most consequential race of the season so far. Circuit Gilles Villeneuve hosts a Sprint format event — only the second of 2026 — which means double the action, double the points, and maximum pressure on every driver from the moment practice ends.

Kimi Antonelli arrives with three consecutive wins. George Russell arrives knowing he won from pole at this circuit in 2025. The championship is separated by 20 points. Both drivers work for the same team. Canada cannot come fast enough.

Full Weekend Schedule

The Sprint format adds an additional competitive session — the Sprint Qualifying and Sprint Race — meaning teams get significantly less practice time before the Grand Prix qualifying session. Here is the complete schedule:

  • Friday May 22, 5:30 PM ET: Practice 1 (the only free practice of the weekend)
  • Friday May 22, 9:30 PM ET: Sprint Qualifying
  • Saturday May 23, 5:00 PM ET: Sprint Race (100km, 8 points for the winner)
  • Saturday May 23, 9:00 PM ET: GP Qualifying (sets the grid for Sunday's race)
  • Sunday May 24: Race (full Grand Prix)

The compressed schedule means strategy, tire management, and setup compromises will define the weekend even before a wheel is turned in anger.

Key Storylines to Watch

The championship context alone would make this weekend unmissable. Antonelli leads Russell by 20 points — and if Antonelli wins both the Sprint and the Grand Prix while Russell struggles, the title race could effectively be decided before the summer break. Conversely, a Russell double and an Antonelli retirement could put the pair level at the halfway point.

Charles Leclerc and Ferrari will be desperate to arrest a slide that saw Leclerc drop from a potential podium to eighth at Miami thanks to a final-lap crash and a 20-second time penalty. Ferrari is expected to bring further upgrades to Canada, and Leclerc — sitting third in the championship — needs the points that only a podium can deliver.

Max Verstappen and Red Bull face a circuit that will test every weakness of the RB22. The Wall of Champions — the concrete barrier at Turn 13 where champions from Hill to Schumacher to Damon Hill have crashed — is unforgiving, and Verstappen will need to manage a car he has described as "incredibly tough to drive" around one of the most punishing circuits on the calendar.

What's Next

Sprint Qualifying on Friday night sets the grid for Saturday's 100km Sprint — a result that carries its own points haul and shapes momentum heading into GP Qualifying. After Canada, the championship picture will be substantially clearer. Six rounds into the year, with Antonelli threatening to run away with the title, the teams that have been chasing need to find their answer this weekend. Montreal may be where this season turns.
