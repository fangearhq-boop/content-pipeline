F1 Meets the Beautiful Game: Racing Bulls Unveil Football Livery for Spanish GP

By Elena Voss | June 11, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Formula 1 cars are many things. Football kits are another. This weekend at Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya, they are both.

Racing Bulls, the Red Bull junior team formerly known as AlphaTauri and now competing as the Visa Cash App RB Formula One Team, will run a special football-inspired livery at the 2026 Spanish Grand Prix. The centerpiece of the design is the VCARB FC crest: an emblem featuring three stars, each representing one of the three host nations of the 2026 FIFA World Cup — the United States, Canada, and Mexico.

The Creative Vision

The team unveiled not just a livery but an entire football-themed identity for the weekend, including race suits styled after football kits for their drivers. The goal was to bring the visual language of the world's most popular sport — jerseys, crests, club iconography — into the cockpit of one of the sport's most technically advanced cars.

The timing is pointed. The FIFA World Cup begins later this summer across North America, and the convergence of two global sporting events creates a natural creative window. Red Bull's junior operation has consistently shown a willingness to experiment with its visual identity in ways that differentiate it from the more conservative branding of the senior Red Bull Racing team.

Why It Works in Barcelona

There are few places on the Formula 1 calendar where a football-themed livery lands with more cultural resonance than Spain. Football is not just a sport in this country — it is identity, community, and conversation. FC Barcelona plays minutes from the Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya. The fans who fill the grandstands on Sunday morning will understand the reference immediately.

For Racing Bulls, the Spanish GP offers maximum exposure for a piece of design that is genuinely unlike anything the sport has seen before. Whether or not the car performs well in the race, the VCARB FC livery will generate conversations that extend well beyond the paddock.

Part of a Broader Trend

Special liveries in Formula 1 have become more ambitious in recent years. Teams have run tribute designs, anniversary editions, and one-race rebrandings for promotional partners. What Racing Bulls has done this weekend is take the concept further by building an entirely new fictional brand identity — VCARB FC — rather than simply adding graphics to an existing car design.

It is a small thing in the context of a race weekend where championship stakes and lap times define the narrative. But it is the kind of small thing that sticks in the memory and earns the team attention from audiences who might not otherwise follow the standings.

What's Next

The VCARB FC livery makes its track debut in FP1 on Friday morning. Qualifying follows on Saturday and the race runs Sunday at 9:00 AM ET on ESPN. The car's visual identity will be on display for the full race weekend before returning to the team's regular design for the next round.

If F1 and football are going to share space in the sports conversation over the coming summer, this weekend in Barcelona is the place it starts.
