Miami GP Race Day: Start Moved to 1 PM ET to Beat Thunderstorms — Antonelli on Pole, McLaren Lurking

By Elena Voss | May 3, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

The 2026 Miami Grand Prix starts at 1:00 PM ET today — three hours earlier than originally scheduled — after Formula 1 officials moved the race start time in response to a significant thunderstorm threat forecast to arrive over South Florida this afternoon.

It is the kind of decision that reflects how seriously the sport treats safety when the American sunshine turns volatile, and it means fans watching from home will need to adjust their Sunday plans accordingly. The race goes — but it goes early, before the worst of what the Florida weather can throw at a racing circuit has a chance to arrive.

Why the Race Was Moved

The Miami weekend has operated under a rolling weather threat all week. The original 4 PM ET start time sat directly within the window where South Florida's afternoon storm pattern was most likely to produce lightning activity, and US law is unambiguous: any outdoor sporting event must be suspended if lightning is detected within eight miles of the venue.

With 88% precipitation probability and 53% thunderstorm chance in the late-afternoon window, the risk of a disrupted or abandoned race was real enough that F1 and the race promoter agreed to act early. The 1 PM ET start gives drivers and teams 57 laps of racing in the morning window before conditions deteriorate, though monitoring will continue throughout.

The Grid That Awaits

Kimi Antonelli lines up on pole position in his Mercedes — a composed and authoritative qualifying lap on Saturday evening that followed his sprint race penalty and confirmed that the championship leader's confidence is intact. Max Verstappen starts second for Red Bull in the most competitive grid position the four-time world champion has earned in this difficult 2026 campaign.

Charles Leclerc is third for Ferrari, Lando Norris fourth for McLaren, and George Russell fifth in the second Mercedes. The top five drivers represent virtually every major championship storyline of the season, compressed into the first couple of corners of Turn 1. It will be decisive.

What the Race Means

The championship stakes are considerable. Antonelli leads Russell by seven points heading into the race — a gap that shrank from nine after Saturday's sprint penalty cost him finishing position. If Antonelli wins today, he extends his advantage and underlines his title credentials. If Norris or Piastri convert their sprint form into a race win, and Antonelli finishes poorly, the championship could look entirely different by the time the podium ceremony concludes.

McLaren's upgraded MCL40 showed genuinely competitive pace all sprint weekend. Norris rolling off from fourth on the main race grid, with the team's best car of 2026 underneath him, represents a threat that Antonelli and Mercedes cannot afford to underestimate.

Fifty-seven laps. Championship implications. A thunderstorm possibly building somewhere over the Florida horizon. The 2026 Miami Grand Prix is ready to go.

What's Next

The race concludes late morning/early afternoon ET. Results will determine the championship standings heading into the next round of the 2026 season. Follow live timing on Formula1.com and streaming coverage on Apple TV+ and ESPN.
