Norris Wins Miami Sprint in McLaren 1-2 as Antonelli Hit With Penalty and Drops to P6

By Ryan Calloway | May 3, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

McLaren's ambitious Miami upgrade arrived with a considerable amount of promise and a great deal of pre-race buzz. On Saturday afternoon at the Miami International Autodrome, it delivered everything the team had hoped for and more.

Lando Norris drove a dominant sprint race to give McLaren their first win of the 2026 Formula 1 season, with Oscar Piastri 3.766 seconds behind in second to complete a papaya 1-2. Charles Leclerc was third for Ferrari. Championship leader Kimi Antonelli, who had been running fourth on the road, was issued a five-second time penalty for repeat track limits violations at the end — dropping him to sixth in the final classification.

McLaren's Upgrade Vindicated

The MCL40 that showed up in Miami was, by Andrea Stella's own description, a "completely new car from an aerodynamic standpoint." McLaren fitted a new floor, new bodywork, a new rear wing, and new front and rear brake ducts — the most comprehensive single-event upgrade the team has produced in the 2026 season. The sprint result confirmed it was worth every hour of wind tunnel time.

Norris controlled the race from the front after taking sprint pole position on Friday — the first non-Mercedes sprint pole of the 2026 season. Piastri kept within striking distance throughout, and by the time the chequered flag fell, McLaren had two cars in the top two positions and a statement result to carry into Sunday's grand prix.

McLaren won the Miami sprint. The team also won Miami in 2024 and 2025. If there's a single circuit on the calendar where McLaren has found something that agrees with their car, this is it.

Antonelli's Sprint Frustration

For Kimi Antonelli, the sprint was a lesson in the margins that define championship campaigns. The 19-year-old Mercedes driver was circulating in an eventual fourth place on the road — a respectable result given the McLaren pace — when the stewards issued a five-second penalty for repeat track limits violations at a corner that had been monitored all weekend.

The penalty dropped him to sixth behind Leclerc, George Russell (fourth), and Max Verstappen (fifth). The points cost was modest but the symbolic impact was significant: the man leading the championship left Miami's sprint with fewer points than expected, and his lead over Russell trimmed from nine points to seven heading into Sunday's main event.

Gabriel Bortoleto's disqualification from 11th — Audi's rookie penalised for an engine technical infringement — shuffled the lower end of the final results but didn't change the headline story: McLaren had the fastest car in the sprint.

What's Next

Today's Miami Grand Prix starts at 1 PM ET — moved up three hours from its original slot to avoid the afternoon thunderstorm window forecasters are monitoring. Antonelli starts from pole position after a composed qualifying session on Saturday evening, with Verstappen alongside him and Leclerc third. Norris rolls off from fourth.

McLaren's sprint win has energized the team heading into a race where they have won for the past two consecutive years. If Norris can convert sprint pace into grand prix pace, and if Antonelli encounters any trouble at the front, today could represent a genuine turning point in the 2026 title fight.
