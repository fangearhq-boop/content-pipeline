F1 Championship Standings: Antonelli Leads Russell by 7 Points as McLaren Hunts Heading Into Miami Race

By Ryan Calloway | May 3, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Seven points. That is the gap separating Kimi Antonelli from George Russell at the top of the 2026 Formula 1 Drivers' Championship as the Miami Grand Prix field lines up on the grid this morning. It is close enough to feel urgent, large enough to feel comfortable — and today's 57-lap race at the Miami International Autodrome could widen it, narrow it, or flip it entirely.

The championship picture has shifted meaningfully over the past 24 hours. Antonelli arrived in Miami with a nine-point advantage over Russell. His sprint race penalty — issued for repeat track limits violations — cost him positions and points, trimming that buffer to seven as the main event approaches.

The Title Contenders

Antonelli's season has been one of the most remarkable rookie championship campaigns in Formula 1 history. The 19-year-old Italian joined Mercedes from the academy ranks and has already accumulated wins at the circuit level that put him at the head of the standings after three completed rounds. His championship lead reflects consistent excellence — but Saturday showed that even the leader isn't immune to the kind of small mistakes that compound into costly outcomes.

George Russell sits seven points back and has been meticulous about how he approaches the team-mate dynamic. When asked about the standings gap, he has repeatedly deflected attention from any sense of internal conflict, but the math is simple: if Antonelli slips today and Russell doesn't, this championship has a new leader by lunchtime.

Charles Leclerc occupies third place with 55 points, representing Ferrari's best championship hope. Lewis Hamilton is fourth in the second Ferrari. Max Verstappen — ninth overall — has been the story of early-season frustration, with Red Bull's car failing to deliver the kind of pace the four-time champion needs to mount a title challenge.

The McLaren Factor

The most significant development in the championship picture is not about any single driver — it's about McLaren. Lando Norris's sprint win on Saturday, combined with Oscar Piastri's second place, confirmed that the team's massive Miami upgrade has given them a car capable of winning races. Norris starts fourth today, Piastri presumably further back, but both carry the threat of a team that has won Miami for the past two consecutive years and arrived with upgraded machinery.

If McLaren can replicate sprint performance in the main race, and if that performance delivers a victory, the constructors' championship will tighten considerably and the drivers' fight will expand from a two-Mercedes battle to something considerably more complex.

What's Next

Today's race starts at 1 PM ET. Whatever happens in the next few hours will redefine what this 2026 championship means, who it belongs to, and how the battles ahead of us will unfold. The standings might look very different by this evening — and that is exactly why we watch.
