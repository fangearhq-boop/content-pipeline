### STORY 2: FIA Stewards Meeting

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM ET

Something unusual is happening this weekend at the Hungarian Grand Prix, and it has nothing to do with lap times.

The entire Formula 1 driver grid has formally requested — and been granted — a meeting with the FIA stewards. The topic: stewarding consistency and driving standards. The meeting is scheduled for this weekend in Budapest.

The flashpoint was the Belgian Grand Prix at Spa, two weeks ago. In the same race:

Lewis Hamilton received a 5-second time penalty for an unsafe pit release. A mechanic was in the path of Hamilton's car during his stop, and the stewards ruled it a dangerous release. Ferrari was also separately fined. In a race where seconds matter, the penalty affected Hamilton's final position.

In the same race, Charles Leclerc was investigated for contact with Oscar Piastri. The stewards cleared Leclerc — no further action.

Sky Sports commentator David Croft called out the inconsistency publicly. Ferrari challenged the stewards' approach. And behind the scenes, the drivers were talking.

The result: every driver on the grid — not just the ones involved in the specific incidents — signed off on a unified request for dialogue with the FIA. That's a remarkable level of collective action in a sport where drivers are typically competing against each other.

What they're asking for, broadly, is clearer and more consistent application of the rules. Whether this meeting produces meaningful change or not, it represents one of the most significant moments of driver unity in recent F1 history.

We'll report what comes out of Budapest.
