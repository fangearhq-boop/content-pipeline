Verstappen's Exit Clause Is 25 Days Away: What Happens Next at Red Bull Changes Everything

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 1, 2026

The most consequential date in Formula 1 this season is not a race date. It is July 26 — the Hungarian Grand Prix — the trigger point for Max Verstappen's performance-based exit clause at Red Bull Racing.

Twenty-five days from now, that clause becomes activatable. The conditions for activation are already met, and barring something extraordinary in the standings, they will remain met when Verstappen takes the chequered flag in Budapest. The biggest driver move in a decade is approaching.

The Clause, Explained

Verstappen's Red Bull contract, extended following his four consecutive world championships, contains a performance escape route. If he is not in the top two of the Drivers' Championship after the Hungarian Grand Prix, the clause becomes available — giving him a window through October to decide whether to formally activate it and leave the team.

The clause was inserted during contract negotiations amid Verstappen's concerns about the 2026 technical regulations. His worry was that the new hybrid formula — drastically different from the regulation era that made Red Bull dominant — could leave the team behind. That concern has proven well-founded.

The Numbers

Verstappen currently sits seventh in the championship with 73 points. Kimi Antonelli leads with 171. The gap is approximately 98 points with nine races remaining — an essentially insurmountable deficit. Short of a complete Mercedes collapse and a Verstappen run of six consecutive wins, the top-two threshold will not be reached before the trigger date.

His manager Raymond Vermeulen has not been subtle about the team's frustration. "He wasn't born to compete in midfield," Vermeulen told reporters — a statement that functions simultaneously as a warning to Red Bull, a message to rival teams, and a signal of intent.

Red Bull's Response

Red Bull are not passive observers. The team has offered Verstappen a significant buyout package — a figure in the eight-figure range — as an incentive to remain committed to the Red Bull project regardless of 2026's standing. The argument is straightforward: the 2026 regulations are a transition year, Red Bull will develop the car, and Verstappen's greatness deserves a longer commitment than one difficult season.

But money has never been Verstappen's primary motivation. Championships have been. The Red Bull shareholder group is reported to be split over how aggressively to pursue retaining him versus accepting the transition cost.

Where Does He Go?

The market reality is constraining. Mercedes has confirmed its 2026 pairing of George Russell and Kimi Antonelli. Ferrari has Charles Leclerc through 2029 and Hamilton through the foreseeable future. McLaren has Norris and Piastri. Alpine confirmed Gasly through 2028.

Secret talks between Verstappen's camp and McLaren — involving a possible arrangement around Oscar Piastri — have been reported, though McLaren has not confirmed any such discussions publicly.

What's Next

The British Grand Prix this weekend produces more data on where teams stand. By July 26 in Hungary, the clause triggers. By October, Verstappen decides whether to pull the trigger. The domino effect — wherever he lands — will reshape every contract negotiation on the grid. Twenty-five days until the clock runs out on Red Bull's strongest argument for keeping their four-time champion.
