F1's 2027 Driver Market Crisis: 17 Seats Unconfirmed, Verstappen's Exit Triggers Everything

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 1, 2026

The British Grand Prix weekend begins in two days. But for Formula 1's driver managers, sporting directors, and team principals, the more pressing negotiation isn't happening at Silverstone — it's happening everywhere else.

Seventeen of the twenty Formula 1 driver seats for 2027 are currently unconfirmed. The market is not just unsettled — it is structurally dependent on decisions that haven't been made yet, most critically Max Verstappen's choice after his exit clause triggers on July 26.

Who Is Confirmed for 2027

The confirmed names for next year can be listed quickly. Verstappen remains at Red Bull through 2028 with performance-based exit clauses that may override that contract. Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri are confirmed at McLaren. Charles Leclerc has a long-term deal at Ferrari running through 2029. Pierre Gasly is committed to Alpine through 2028.

Five drivers. Fifteen seats remaining — and those five confirmed names are themselves subject to external pressures. If Verstappen activates his exit clause, Red Bull begins a search. If McLaren pursues Verstappen, Piastri or Norris moves. Every ripple creates new waves.

The Mercedes Question

Perhaps the most surprising uncertainty involves the team currently dominating the championship. Both George Russell and Kimi Antonelli are at Mercedes for 2026, but their 2027 contracts are not settled. Mercedes team principal Toto Wolff confirmed in June that the 2026 lineup was locked — but 2027 remains open discussion.

Russell, at 28, is in his prime and producing championship-level results. Antonelli, at 20, is the most exciting young talent in the sport. The question for Mercedes is not lack of options — it's whether both drivers remain the right pairing once Verstappen's market entry potentially reshuffles available talent.

Audi, Racing Bulls, and the Mid-Field Cascade

Audi's arrival as a works team for 2026 — purchasing the Sauber/Alfa Romeo entry — created new market dynamics. The German manufacturer is pursuing Carlos Sainz, who left Ferrari for Audi amid the 2026 regulation changes. That negotiation affects what happens at Williams and mid-field teams further down the order.

Racing Bulls, the junior Red Bull outfit, has neither Liam Lawson nor Arvid Lindblad confirmed for 2027. Both are young, both are performing acceptably, but neither has a locked deal. If Verstappen leaves Red Bull, does the team promote from within or pursue outside talent? Every scenario has a cascade.

What's Next

The next 90 days define the 2027 grid. Verstappen's clause activates July 26. His October decision window is the real deadline. Signings and confirmations typically cascade through October and November as each team reacts to its neighbors' decisions. The 2026 season is not yet half complete, but Formula 1's 2027 chess match has already begun in earnest. Pay attention to who shows up where at season events — the real deals are made in hospitality suites and private meetings on race weekends exactly like this one.
