Hamilton's Ferrari Homecoming: Targeting an Exclusive 10th British Grand Prix Win at Silverstone

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 1, 2026

This weekend, Lewis Hamilton races at Silverstone for the first time in a Ferrari. The emotional weight of the occasion is significant — but so is the sporting one. Hamilton arrives at his home circuit with nine British Grand Prix victories, an exclusive record, and a genuine shot at a 10th.

No driver in Formula 1 history has more than four British GP wins. Hamilton's nine stand alone. A victory on Sunday would push that number into territory that may never be approached again.

Ferrari's Silverstone Upgrade Push

Ferrari have not arrived at Silverstone with their standard package. The team is introducing a new Macarena wing design and a low-drag diffuser — developments specifically targeted at Silverstone's high-speed straight-line sections. Those sections have been Ferrari's weakness under the 2026 regulations, where the Scuderia has struggled to match Mercedes' straight-line speed. The upgrades represent a direct attempt to close that gap on a circuit that will expose it most clearly.

Hamilton has also hinted at a special yellow Ferrari race suit for the weekend — a nod to the Maranello tradition of special liveries at home circuits, and a personal touch from a driver who has always understood the theater of the sport as well as the mechanics of it.

Hamilton's 2026 Form

The Spanish Grand Prix at Barcelona in mid-June was the turning point. Hamilton won by nearly 20 seconds — a dominant display that ended a 686-day win drought since his move to Ferrari. It was the only non-Mercedes victory of the 2026 season through eight rounds. The win confirmed that Hamilton, in the right conditions with the right car, is still a race winner at the highest level.

His Austrian GP result — fifth place — was less impressive, but Silverstone promises better conditions. The upgrades target exactly the track characteristics that hurt Ferrari most in Austria and similar high-speed venues. If the package works as intended, Hamilton enters Sunday's race as a genuine front-row contender.

The Championship Context

Hamilton sits third in the championship with 125 points — 46 behind leader Kimi Antonelli and 6 behind second-placed George Russell. The British GP is a sprint weekend, offering a maximum of 33 points. A perfect weekend for Hamilton (sprint win + race win) paired with poor results for Antonelli and Russell could cut the championship gap significantly.

The scenario is improbable, but this is Silverstone. Hamilton's track record here speaks for itself. No circuit in the world brings out the best in Lewis Hamilton quite like this one.

What's Next

Free Practice 1 begins Friday at 12:30 BST — Hamilton's only practice session before parc ferme locks the car in. Sprint Qualifying follows Friday at 16:30 BST. The British Grand Prix race goes off Sunday at 15:00 BST. The crowd at Silverstone will be enormous, passionate, and hoping to witness history. Hamilton has done this before. He might just do it again.
