Lewis Hamilton has finally done it in red.

### STORY 2: Antonelli DNF — Battery Failure 3 Laps From the Flag

**Long-Form Post** | 10:00 AM ET

The 19-year-old had just made a bold move to re-pass George Russell at Turn 1, putting himself in P2 with the race nearly done. Then his Mercedes simply stopped — an electrical shutdown, later confirmed by the team to be battery-related.

Antonelli coasted to a halt beyond Turn 6. His race was over. His championship lead, which had stood at 59 points entering the weekend, suddenly shrunk to 41.

"I feel a bit empty," Antonelli told Formula1.com after the race. It is hard to imagine a more painful way to lose a chunk of a championship cushion.

The context makes it worse: Mercedes has now suffered two significant reliability failures in 2026 that directly cost race positions. George Russell was LEADING in Montreal before a similar battery-related failure ended his race. Now Antonelli has suffered the same fate in Barcelona, from second place, with three laps remaining.

These are not isolated incidents. They are a pattern — and one that the Mercedes engineers will need to address urgently before the Austrian Grand Prix on June 26-28.

The championship still firmly belongs to Antonelli. A 41-point lead with Lewis Hamilton (115 pts) and George Russell (106 pts) as the closest challengers is still a significant buffer. But Barcelona turned what felt like a comfortable position into something that requires urgent action.

The question now: can Mercedes fix the battery problem before Red Bull Ring? Because if it happens a third time, the title conversation changes completely.

The bigger picture is stark. Verstappen has zero race wins in 2026. He sits seventh in the drivers' standings with 55 points — 101 behind championship leader Kimi Antonelli. In previous seasons, Verstappen was untouchable; in 2026, he has not yet found the pace to challenge at the front.

Norris and Piastri are close in the standings (73 and 68 points). Barcelona's third-place result shows McLaren can score on a chaotic Sunday. Austria could be another.
