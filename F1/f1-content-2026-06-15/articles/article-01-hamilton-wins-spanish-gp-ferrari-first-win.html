Hamilton Wins Spanish GP for Ferrari: First Victory in Red, All-British Podium Since 1968

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap

June 15, 2026

Lewis Hamilton finally has his Ferrari win.

The seven-time world champion executed a perfect three-stop strategy at the 2026 Barcelona-Catalunya Grand Prix on Sunday, capitalizing on a virtual safety car gamble on lap 41 to claim his first victory in Ferrari red — and end a nearly two-year win drought stretching back to the 2024 Belgian Grand Prix.

Hamilton crossed the line 19.561 seconds ahead of George Russell (Mercedes), with Lando Norris (McLaren) completing the podium — the first all-British top-three in Formula 1 since the 1968 US Grand Prix, which featured Jackie Stewart, Graham Hill, and John Surtees.

The Strategy That Won the Race

On a baking Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya — air temperature 31°C, track temperature peaking at 54°C — tyre management was always going to separate the contenders. Ferrari chose the most aggressive path: three stops. It proved decisive.

Hamilton pitted on lap 12, switching from softs to hards, then again on lap 28 for mediums. The critical moment came on lap 41 when Fernando Alonso's Aston Martin stopped at Turn 9, triggering a virtual safety car. Ferrari reacted immediately, calling Hamilton in for a third stop — giving him a free pitstop for fresh rubber while his rivals could not respond. He drove away from the field without incident in the final stint.

The strategy requires perfect timing and perfect nerve. Ferrari delivered both.

History at the Top of the Podium

Hamilton launched from P2 after Russell had claimed pole position, and he ran consistently through the chaos to take a first Ferrari win in the 2026 season — ending the Silver Arrows' unbeaten run of eight Grand Prix victories dating back to the start of the campaign.

The podium — Hamilton, Russell, Norris — was a moment that stopped the paddock. Three British drivers. Three different teams. No all-British podium in the sport's top category for 58 years. The occasion was not lost on any of them.

For Hamilton, 41, the win carried a particular weight. He joined Ferrari to compete for championships, not podiums — and Barcelona proved he has the pace and the team to do it. Championship leader Kimi Antonelli retired from second with three laps remaining, cutting his lead from 59 to 41 points. The title race has a new shape now.

What's Next

The 2026 Formula 1 season heads to Austria next. The Austrian Grand Prix at the Red Bull Ring in Spielberg runs June 26-28. Hamilton arrives with momentum. Antonelli arrives with questions about Mercedes reliability. And Verstappen arrives with a home crowd and zero wins in 2026 — desperate for a breakthrough.

Whatever happens in Spielberg, the 2026 championship just became a genuine three-way fight. Barcelona lit the fuse.
