F1 Championship Standings After the Spanish GP: Three-Way Title Fight Takes Shape

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

June 15, 2026

The Spanish Grand Prix reshuffled the 2026 Formula 1 championship in ways that will define the second half of the season. Lewis Hamilton's victory for Ferrari, combined with Kimi Antonelli's battery failure and George Russell's second-place finish, produced a standings picture that suddenly looks like a genuine three-way title contest.

The Updated Standings

After Barcelona, the top of the drivers' championship reads as follows:

Kimi Antonelli (Mercedes) leads on 156 points. Lewis Hamilton (Ferrari) has moved to 115 points, 41 behind Antonelli. George Russell (Mercedes) is third on 106 points, 50 behind his teammate.

Further back: Charles Leclerc (Ferrari) on 75 points, Lando Norris (McLaren) on 73, Oscar Piastri (McLaren) on 68, Max Verstappen (Red Bull) on 55, and Pierre Gasly (Alpine) on 41.

How the Race Changed the Maths

Hamilton entering Barcelona was 66 points behind Antonelli. A single race — a VSC gamble, a Ferrari strategy call, and an Antonelli battery failure — turned a 66-point gap into a 41-point gap. That is a swing of 25 points to Hamilton in one afternoon.

For context: a race win is worth 25 points. Two more Hamilton wins while Antonelli finishes second each time would reduce the gap to zero. The mathematics of the championship can move very quickly in the right circumstances, and Barcelona showed that the right circumstances can arrive without warning.

Russell's position is also worth watching. He is only 50 points behind Antonelli — his Mercedes teammate. Russell has pace, experience, and a team that knows how to win championships. If Antonelli suffers further reliability problems, Russell could become the de facto title contender the team rallies behind.

What's Next

The next race is the Austrian Grand Prix at Red Bull Ring, Spielberg — June 26-28. It is Red Bull's home circuit, and Verstappen will be desperate for a win there. But for the championship contenders, it is a critical opportunity. Every point from here to the finale matters, and Barcelona has just made them matter a great deal more.

Antonelli is still the favourite. But the gap that once felt comfortable has been cut in a single Sunday afternoon. The 2026 title fight is, officially, on.
