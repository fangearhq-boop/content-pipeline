Miami Sprint Weekend Preview — Sprint at Noon, Qualifying Tonight, Race Sunday Under Storm Clouds

By Marcus Cole | May 2, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Formula 1's first visit to the United States in 2026 is a sprint weekend, which means today — Saturday at the Miami International Autodrome — is one of the most packed single days on the F1 calendar.

The Miami Sprint race gets underway at noon ET, followed by main qualifying at 5 PM ET. Those results will determine both the sprint podium and the full Sunday race grid. Then, on Sunday at 4 PM ET, the Miami Grand Prix itself runs 57 laps around the Autodrome — with a weather forecast that may be the most significant variable in any race this season.

Saturday: Sprint and Qualifying

Lando Norris starts from pole for the sprint after his dominant sprint qualifying performance on Friday, with championship leader Kimi Antonelli alongside him on the front row. Oscar Piastri is third in the second McLaren, with Charles Leclerc fourth, Max Verstappen fifth, and George Russell sixth.

Sprint races are 19 laps and award points to the top eight finishers on a reduced scale. For Norris and McLaren, a sprint victory would be a powerful momentum builder heading into the evening qualifying session. For Antonelli, maintaining his sprint grid position and banking points is the priority — with the championship margin always front of mind.

Main qualifying at 5 PM ET is the critical session. The Q1-Q2-Q3 knockout format will set Sunday's grid, and teams will have new data from the Sprint to adjust their setups with.

Sunday: Race Under a Question Mark

The Miami Grand Prix starts at 4 PM ET on Sunday — but whether it finishes as planned is genuinely uncertain. Current meteorological forecasts put the chance of precipitation at 88%, with a 53% probability of thunderstorms. Florida weather is notoriously difficult to predict, but those numbers represent serious risk.

The FIA has confirmed contingency plans are in place for the event. If conditions deteriorate to the point of a red flag, the race can be restarted or shortened. More dramatically, under Florida state law, outdoor public events must be halted if lightning is detected within eight miles of the venue. A sustained thunderstorm during the race could force a suspension under legal mandate, not just sporting regulation.

The McLaren Factor

McLaren won here in both 2024 and 2025. They've brought their most comprehensive upgrade package of the 2026 season specifically for this circuit. Norris on sprint pole is their most visible statement yet that the championship fight is not settled.

What's Next

Sprint at noon ET. Qualifying at 5 PM ET. Then get some rest and check the weather apps obsessively before Sunday's 4 PM ET start. The 2026 Miami Grand Prix may prove to be the most dramatic race of the year — rain or shine.
