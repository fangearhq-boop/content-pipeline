Norris Ends Mercedes' 2026 Qualifying Streak — McLaren's Sprint Pole Signals Real Title Threat

By Ryan Calloway | May 2, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

For the first time in the 2026 Formula 1 season, a driver other than a Mercedes sat at the top of the timing sheets after qualifying — and the manner in which it happened suggested this is not a one-weekend anomaly.

Lando Norris claimed sprint pole position at the Miami Grand Prix with a lap of 1:27.869, breaking a qualifying streak that Mercedes had maintained for every single session of the season to date. Championship leader Kimi Antonelli was a solid 0.222 seconds back in second, with McLaren's Oscar Piastri completing a papaya-flanked top three at 0.239 seconds off the pace.

The Upgrade That Changed Everything

McLaren arrived in Miami with what team principal Andrea Stella called "completely new from an aerodynamic standpoint." That was not marketing language. The list of changed components was comprehensive: new floor, new bodywork, new rear wing, new front brake ducts, new rear brake ducts. When a team changes that many aero-related components simultaneously, it represents a fundamental rethinking of the car's performance envelope.

The preparation was just as serious. Both Norris and Piastri ran extensive simulator programs and completed a tyre test at the Nürburgring before heading to Florida. They didn't arrive with a new car and hope for the best — they arrived fully prepared to use it.

The rest of the sprint qualifying grid reflected the clear pecking order that followed: Charles Leclerc fourth for Ferrari, Max Verstappen fifth, George Russell sixth for Mercedes — struggling at 0.624 seconds off Norris — and Lewis Hamilton seventh in the second Ferrari.

What It Means for the Championship

The significance of this pole extends beyond the sprint race itself. It's a proof of concept. McLaren has been competitive throughout the 2026 season but hasn't been able to threaten Mercedes on pure qualifying pace until now. If this upgrade translates to Sunday's race — a full 57-lap distance with a very different tire and strategy demand — McLaren could take points away from Mercedes at exactly the right moment.

Antonelli leads the championship at 72 points with Russell 9 behind. McLaren's Norris and Piastri have been in the mix but haven't been consistent podium threats across all conditions. A Miami victory for either McLaren driver would dramatically tighten the title battle heading into the second half of the 2026 season.

What's Next

The Sprint race runs at noon ET on Saturday, with main qualifying following at 5 PM ET. Sunday's Grand Prix starts at 4 PM ET — though weather forecasts showing 88% chance of rain and 53% thunderstorm probability add an element of chaos to the weekend that no upgrade package can account for.

For McLaren, the mission is clear: convert this weekend's promise into race pace and championship points. For Mercedes, the answer to this new challenge begins tonight in qualifying.
