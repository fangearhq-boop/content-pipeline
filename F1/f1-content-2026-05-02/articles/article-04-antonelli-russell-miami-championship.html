Antonelli 72, Russell 63 — The Miami Sprint Revealed Cracks in Mercedes' Perfect Season

By Ryan Calloway | May 2, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

It has been almost flawlessly Mercedes through the first races of 2026 — two drivers from the same team running one-two in the championship, controlling qualifying at every circuit, managing their rivalry with the kind of professional discipline that team boss Toto Wolff demands.

Miami may be where cracks start to show.

Kimi Antonelli arrived as the youngest championship leader in Formula 1 history with 72 points. George Russell, his teammate and closest rival, sat nine points back at 63. But in Friday's sprint qualifying session, Antonelli managed only second place while Russell struggled to sixth — 0.624 seconds behind McLaren's Lando Norris and 0.402 seconds behind his own teammate.

Russell's Struggle and What It Means

The intra-Mercedes tension has been simmering for weeks. Russell has publicly downplayed his teammate's championship lead as "not even a consideration," while Wolff has issued clear warnings to both drivers about the limits of internal competition. The gap in sprint qualifying — with Russell in sixth while Antonelli was a comfortable second — is the kind of performance differential that fuels rather than dampens that tension.

Russell will need to regroup for tonight's main qualifying session. If he can't match Antonelli's pace, he risks falling further in the championship standings while simultaneously watching McLaren eat into the Silver Arrows' overall points advantage.

Antonelli's Own Challenge

Antonelli has not had a perfect 2026 season either. The 19-year-old has acknowledged a "fundamental" issue with his race starts that his engineers are working to resolve — an issue that has cost him positions and points in multiple races. Strong qualifying pace doesn't always translate if a driver loses three positions off the line.

His sprint pole for the start of the Miami sprint race — second on the grid — gives him a strong position for noon's sprint. But the championship is won over the full race distance on Sunday, and how Antonelli handles the pressure of a McLaren challenge combined with his persistent starts concern will define his weekend.

The Bigger Picture

Through the opening races of 2026, Mercedes' greatest competition has been itself. That calculation changed on Friday evening when Norris set a lap time 0.222 seconds clear of the championship leader. McLaren has arrived as a genuine external threat for the first time this season.

For Mercedes, the priority now is maintaining the intra-team performance advantage they've built while managing a new rival. For Antonelli, the priority is simple: keep winning, keep leading, and deal with the starts problem before it costs him something irreplaceable.

What's Next

Main qualifying tonight at 5 PM ET will be the next defining moment for both drivers. A Russell pole and Antonelli P3 would completely reframe the weekend's narrative. An Antonelli pole with Russell in the midfield would raise serious questions about the Mercedes car's consistency across its two setups. Whatever happens, Sunday's race — if the 88% rain forecast doesn't intervene — will be a championship inflection point either way.
