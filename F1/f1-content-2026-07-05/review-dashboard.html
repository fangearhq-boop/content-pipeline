### Long-Form Post ⚠️ VERSTAPPEN ON RED BULL'S PROBLEM: "IT'S EXTRA PAINFUL" Max Verstappen does not usually admit to having a car problem in public. But what happened at Silverstone this weekend left him with little choice. After qualifying seventh for Sunday's British Grand Prix — a position made even more uncomfortable by the fact that Red Bull's junior partner driver Isack Hadjar outqualified him by two positions (P5) — Verstappen laid out what he described as a "double whammy" plaguing the RB22 at this circuit. The problem, per Verstappen, runs deeper than a simple lack of downforce. On Silverstone's long straights — the Hangar Straight in particular — the Red Bull lacks the top speed to compete with Mercedes and Ferrari. That alone would be painful. But the second layer of the problem makes it worse: because the car cannot maintain speed on the straights, it burns through more battery energy trying to keep up. That energy deficit then compounds through Silverstone's high-speed, high-load corners, where the car needs that stored energy for stability and performance. "It's extra painful," Verstappen said. He also reported that the engine was "not responding normally" during Q2 of main qualifying — an additional mechanical concern on top of the car's inherent weaknesses at this type of circuit. The result: P6 in the sprint (3 points), P7 in qualifying, and the uncomfortable sight of a junior Hadjar lining up ahead of a four-time world champion for Sunday's start. Red Bull addressed Verstappen's concerns heading into Sunday's race. Whether those fixes translate to a better result remains to be seen. Is this a one-circuit issue for Red Bull, or a deeper problem? Tell us below. ### Image Post Caption Verstappen on Red Bull's Silverstone problem: "It's extra painful." ### Long-Form Post 🚨 TURN 3, LAP 2: THE INCIDENT THAT WRECKED PEREZ'S DAY The British GP sprint race had barely started when it produced its first controversy. On Lap 2 of the 17-lap sprint, Sergio Perez and the Cadillac team found themselves embroiled in the day's most significant incident. At Turn 3, Perez made an ambitious — and ultimately uncommitted — move up the inside on Fernando Alonso's Aston Martin. The move was vague and imprecise. Perez's car made contact with the left-rear of Alonso's AMR26. Alonso was sent spinning off the racing line. Cadillac had no choice but to pit Perez at the end of Lap 2 for a nose change, costing him significant track position. The stewards then reviewed the incident. Their verdict: Perez received a +10-second post-race time penalty for causing a collision. The penalty dropped him to last place — P22 — behind Lance Stroll by over 11 seconds once applied. Perez was not the only driver penalized on the day. Nico Hulkenberg received a +5-second penalty for leaving the track and gaining an advantage, dropping him from P13 to P15 in the final classification. Liam Lawson was investigated for aggressive driving in his defence against teammate Isack Hadjar — the stewards issued a warning but allowed him to retain P8 and the championship point that came with it. For Cadillac, the incident was a frustrating one — the American team had been running competitive this weekend and the Lap 2 mistake cost them any chance at a strong sprint result.
