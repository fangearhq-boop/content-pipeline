2026 F1 Championship Standings After British GP Sprint: Antonelli's Command, a Russell-Hamilton Battle, Red Bull's Slide

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

The British Grand Prix sprint race at Silverstone has been run, and with Sunday's main event still to come, the 2026 Formula 1 Drivers' and Constructors' Championships tell a clear story: Kimi Antonelli is in command, the battle for second is tighter than the gap suggests, and Red Bull's fall down the standings is accelerating.

Drivers' Championship

Kimi Antonelli leads the 2026 Drivers' Championship with 179 points. The Mercedes rookie has won the sprint race at Silverstone and taken pole position for Sunday, adding 8 points to his total today. His advantage over second-placed George Russell stands at 43 points — a gap that would require several consecutive misfortunes to close in the remaining races.

George Russell is second with 136 points. Russell won the previous round at the Austrian Grand Prix, his first victory of the season away from Australia. He starts Sunday from P4 on the Silverstone grid — behind Antonelli, Leclerc, and Hamilton. A strong result here could begin to apply real pressure on Antonelli.

Lewis Hamilton is third with 132 points, just 4 behind Russell. Hamilton's weekend at Silverstone has been one of the most emotionally charged of his remarkable career — taking sprint pole at his home circuit, leading the sprint race, then being overtaken by Antonelli on Lap 8. He starts Sunday's race from P3 and is very much in the title conversation despite the 47-point gap to Antonelli.

Behind the top three, Lando Norris (McLaren, 85 points), Charles Leclerc (Ferrari, 83 points), and Oscar Piastri (McLaren, 82 points) form a tight midfield championship cluster. Max Verstappen sits seventh with 76 points, 103 behind the leader.

Constructors' Championship

Mercedes lead the Constructors' Championship with 315 points — 100 clear of Ferrari in second with 215 points. McLaren are third with 167 points. Red Bull Racing hold fourth with 118 points.

The Mercedes total reflects a dominant team effort: Antonelli's first-place points combined with Russell's consistent podium presence have built a lead that Ferrari, despite Hamilton's strong individual performances, are struggling to close. McLaren's position as a consistent points-scorer keeps them in the constructors' fight, while Red Bull's relative decline at high-speed circuits has seen them slip further from the top.

The Bigger Picture

The 2026 season opened with a regulation change that levelled the playing field between teams. Mercedes, with Antonelli's breakout season paired with Russell's experience, have capitalised most effectively. Ferrari's engineering has produced a competitive car — Hamilton and Leclerc are both regularly near the front — but it has not been quite enough to match Mercedes' raw pace.

What's Next

Sunday's British Grand Prix — 52 laps, 15:00 local start — represents the next championship scoring opportunity. Antonelli on pole, Leclerc P2, Hamilton P3, and Russell P4 set the stage for a race that could either extend Antonelli's lead further or see Hamilton and Russell begin to close the gap. The British GP has a long history of producing unexpected results. After a Saturday of dominant performances, Kimi Antonelli begins Sunday as the firm favourite — but Silverstone has surprised us before.
