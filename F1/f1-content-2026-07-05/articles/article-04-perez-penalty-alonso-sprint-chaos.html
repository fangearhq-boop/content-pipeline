Sprint Day Chaos: Perez Penalised, Alonso Spun, and Hulkenberg Also Punished

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

The British Grand Prix sprint race was barely two laps old when it produced the most consequential incident of the day. A collision between Sergio Perez and Fernando Alonso at Turn 3 on Lap 2 triggered a chain of events that cost Cadillac their best chance at a solid sprint result — and left Alonso spinning in the gravel run-off with a race to recover from.

The Perez-Alonso Collision

Heading into Turn 3 on Lap 2, Perez saw a gap to Alonso's inside and committed to the move — though witnesses and the stewards' analysis characterised it as uncommitted and vague. The Cadillac's front-right clipped the left-rear of Alonso's Aston Martin AMR26 as Perez turned in. The contact was enough to spin Alonso off the racing line, temporarily eliminating him from the active battle at the front of the field.

Cadillac brought Perez into the pit lane at the end of Lap 2 for a nose change. The repair cost significant track position and ultimately placed Perez well outside the points. The stewards reviewed the incident and issued their verdict after the race: Perez received a +10-second time penalty for causing a collision. When applied to his classified finishing position, the penalty dropped him to last — P22 — 11.289 seconds behind Lance Stroll.

It was a frustrating end to what had been a competitive weekend for Cadillac, whose American entry had run strongly through practice and sprint qualifying.

More Penalties: Hulkenberg and Lawson

Perez was not the only driver to face stewards on Saturday. Nico Hulkenberg received a +5-second post-race penalty for leaving the track and gaining an advantage during the sprint. The penalty dropped him from his classified P13 to P15 in the final standings.

Liam Lawson's aggressive defensive driving against Racing Bulls teammate Isack Hadjar was also investigated. The stewards reviewed the move but determined it did not merit a time penalty, issuing only a warning. Lawson retained P8 and the single championship point it provided — a relief for a Racing Bulls team that had otherwise enjoyed a strong day, with Hadjar qualifying fifth for Sunday's race.

What It Means for Sunday

The sprint penalties have no direct effect on the Sunday race grid — qualifying results determine starting positions for the main event. Perez starts from his qualifying position, as does Alonso. But the collision between the two drivers will be fresh in the minds of both Cadillac and Aston Martin as they plan their race-day strategies.

What's Next

Sunday's British Grand Prix — 52 laps of Silverstone — is the main event of the weekend. Cadillac will be looking to recover from a difficult sprint, while Alonso, in his return to Silverstone in a competitive Aston Martin package, will want a strong result in front of the British crowd that has long appreciated the Spaniard's tenacity. The stewards' office was busy on Saturday; Sunday's race at the world's most storied circuit will inevitably produce its own moments. Formula 1 at Silverstone rarely disappoints.
