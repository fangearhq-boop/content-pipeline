For Kimi Antonelli, Belgium represents damage control. His championship lead has been reduced from a dominant 66 points to just 25 in three races. Two mechanical failures. For Verstappen and Red Bull, the two-week break is a race to find and fix the rear wing fault that has caused two dangerous crashes. He cannot race at Spa with the same mechanical risk that haunted him in Austria and at Silverstone. Something has to change.

And for Ferrari — for Leclerc and Hamilton — Belgium arrives as a chance to maintain momentum and push the constructors' fight into a genuine sprint finish across the second half of the season.
