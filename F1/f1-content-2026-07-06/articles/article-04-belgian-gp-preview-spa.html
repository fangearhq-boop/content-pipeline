Belgian Grand Prix Preview: What Every Contender Needs from Spa After Silverstone's Chaos

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | July 6, 2026

Formula 1 takes two weeks off after one of its most dramatic Sundays of the 2026 season. When the paddock reconvenes, it will be at Spa-Francorchamps — the 7.00-kilometre Belgian circuit that flows through the Ardennes forest, up and over Eau Rouge, through the sweeping Pouhon corner, and along the longest and fastest straights on the calendar. The Belgian Grand Prix runs July 17-19, and after the Silverstone chaos, every team arrives with different priorities and different problems to solve.

Antonelli: Rebound Required

Kimi Antonelli arrives at Spa needing a result, and probably a win. His 66-point lead after Monaco has been trimmed to 25 — two retirements in three races have done what no rival driver managed all spring. The 19-year-old's composure under pressure has been a feature of his championship campaign; how he responds to back-to-back mechanical disasters will reveal something important about his character at this level.

Spa suits the Mercedes. The Silver Arrows have traditionally performed well at circuits that demand both high-speed stability and mid-corner precision. If the car is reliable — the biggest if of the current campaign — Antonelli is the natural favourite.

Russell: Maintain the Pressure

George Russell has been the most consistent driver in the field since Monaco. Three consecutive podiums. Points in every round while his teammate's race has twice dissolved into mechanical failure. He does not need to manufacture drama or take risks at Belgium — he simply needs to finish ahead of where Antonelli finishes, collect maximum points, and let the mathematics of the season work in his favour.

Russell at Spa is a known quantity: fast, precise, rarely makes errors under pressure. The circuit rewards exactly his kind of smooth, committed commitment through the sustained high-speed corners. He will be dangerous here.

Verstappen: Fix the Car

Red Bull's priority over the two-week break is unambiguous: identify and repair the rear wing fault that has caused Max Verstappen to crash out of two race weekends in a row. Spa-Francorchamps features the Kemmel Straight — one of the longest flat-out sections on the calendar, where DRS is used at full effect. Arriving at Spa with a rear wing that cannot be trusted on straights is not an option. Red Bull's technical problem has become an urgent safety matter.

Ferrari: Protect the Momentum

For Leclerc and Hamilton, Belgium offers the chance to build on Sunday's 1-3 result and apply sustained pressure in both the drivers' and constructors' championships. Ferrari's package has shown genuine improvement through the European season. Whether that pace translates to Spa — with its unique demands on both aero efficiency and mechanical grip — will tell us whether Silverstone was a one-off opportunity created by Antonelli's misfortune or evidence of a genuine competitive step.

What's Next

The Belgian Grand Prix at Spa-Francorchamps gets underway on Friday July 17 with practice, qualifying on Saturday July 18, and the race on Sunday July 19. After the twists of the British Grand Prix, it is the most anticipated round of the 2026 season. The championship could look completely different by the time the chequered flag falls at Spa on a Sunday afternoon in Belgium.
