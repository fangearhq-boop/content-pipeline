Leclerc Wins Chaotic British Grand Prix as Antonelli Fails and Verstappen Crashes

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | July 6, 2026

Charles Leclerc has won the British Grand Prix at Silverstone, claiming his ninth career victory and his first ever at the legendary circuit in a race that will be discussed for weeks. Ferrari finished one-three on the day — Leclerc took the win, Lewis Hamilton completed the podium in third — as championship leader Kimi Antonelli suffered a mechanical failure that unraveled what had been a strong drive, and Max Verstappen's afternoon ended in another dangerous crash triggered by the same technical fault that almost took him out in Austrian GP qualifying.

When Antonelli took pole on Saturday, the expectation was clear: Mercedes would control another race weekend and Antonelli would extend his championship advantage. What actually happened was something else entirely.

Leclerc and Hamilton Pass Antonelli at the Start

Both Ferrari cars launched brilliantly off the line. Leclerc and Hamilton swept past Antonelli at Turn 1, immediately putting the Mercedes on the back foot. The championship leader spent the opening stages in third, managing his pace as the Ferraris set the early tempo.

As the race developed and tyre strategies diverged, Antonelli pitted to take fresh rubber and began what looked like a decisive charge. He was faster than Leclerc on the newer compound, eating into the gap lap by lap. With the pace differential, a pass looked inevitable.

Then his front-left wheel shield failed.

Antonelli's Race Unravels

Antonelli was twice forced into the pits as the wheel shield continued to malfunction. What had been a race-winning drive became a damage-limitation exercise. He crossed the line in ninth on the road — but was then hit with a five-second time penalty for accumulating too many track-limits violations, dropping him to sixteenth in the final classification.

For the 19-year-old who had dominated the championship narrative since Monaco, it was the second major mechanical retirement in three races. The 66-point cushion he'd built has now been reduced to 25.

Verstappen Crashes at Stowe

The drama was not over. On Lap 48, Max Verstappen lost control of his Red Bull at Stowe — the rear wing opening for additional top speed on the straight and then failing to close again, sending him spinning off the circuit. It was the same fault that caused his crash in Austrian Grand Prix qualifying. The Safety Car came out. The race would finish under it.

For Leclerc, already clear in the lead, the Safety Car meant the result was secure. He crossed the line to take a win that owed as much to reliability as to pace — but given the pressures of a championship fight at Silverstone, no Ferrari fan would be complaining.

The Full Top Ten

Russell finished second ahead of Hamilton, with Lando Norris fourth, Isack Hadjar fifth, and Liam Lawson sixth. Arvid Lindblad, Gabriel Bortoleto, Franco Colapinto, and Pierre Gasly rounded out the points positions. Hamilton received a reprimand rather than a penalty for a yellow flag infringement, preserving his podium.

What's Next

Formula 1 takes two weeks off before reconvening at Spa-Francorchamps for the Belgian Grand Prix on July 17-19. For Leclerc and Ferrari, Belgium arrives on a wave of momentum. For Antonelli and Mercedes, the break comes as a relief — time to regroup, to fix the wheel shield issue, and to reset before the title fight enters its critical second half. And for Verstappen, two weeks to solve a rear wing problem that has now twice turned a race into a near-miss.
