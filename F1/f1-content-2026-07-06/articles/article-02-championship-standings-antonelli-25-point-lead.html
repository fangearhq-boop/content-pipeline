From 66 to 25: How Antonelli's Championship Lead Has Collapsed Since Monaco

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 6, 2026

On June 7, Kimi Antonelli crossed the line in Monaco for his fifth consecutive Formula 1 victory. He held a 66-point championship lead. The season looked like a formality.

Three races later, that lead is 25 points.

The 2026 F1 World Drivers' Championship standings after the British Grand Prix: Antonelli leads on 179 points. George Russell sits in second on 154. Lewis Hamilton is third on 147. Charles Leclerc, who won on Sunday, climbs to fourth on 108. In three rounds since Monaco, 41 points have been erased from Antonelli's advantage — nearly a full race win's worth — and the season that once looked like a procession has become a genuine three-way fight.

Two Retirements in Three Races

The numbers alone do not capture the frustration in the Mercedes garage. Antonelli's two retirements in three races since Monaco have not come from driver error. The wheel shield failure at Silverstone — forcing him twice into the pits on a day when he was closing on Leclerc — is mechanical misfortune of the cruellest kind. His pace has not disappeared. His confidence, heading into each race, looks intact. But the car has let him down at exactly the wrong moments in exactly the wrong races.

Russell, meanwhile, has been immaculate. A third place, a second place, and a second place again in the three rounds since Monaco. He has maximised every opportunity while his teammate's car has fractured at critical moments. If this continues, Russell will be champion not because he outdrove Antonelli, but because he outran his own team's reliability problems by not having them.

Hamilton Still in the Picture

Hamilton's third place at Silverstone — his home race, his first podium as a Ferrari driver — keeps him 32 points behind Antonelli with a full half-season remaining. It is a significant deficit, but not an impossible one in a year where no one, at this point, fully trusts any of the front-running cars to stay on track for a full race distance. If Antonelli suffers even one more retirement, if Ferrari continues to find pace, the championship could look entirely different by the time the summer break ends.

What's Next

The Belgian Grand Prix at Spa-Francorchamps on July 17-19 is the most important race on the upcoming calendar. Spa is a circuit that rewards raw pace and punishes mechanical fragility in equal measure. Antonelli needs a clean result and ideally a win. Russell needs another podium. For Hamilton and Leclerc, every point matters. What was supposed to be Antonelli's coronation has become the most compelling title battle F1 has produced in several seasons.
