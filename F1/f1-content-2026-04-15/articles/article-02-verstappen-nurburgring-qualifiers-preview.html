Verstappen Returns to the Nordschleife This Weekend — Redemption and Preparation at the 24H Qualifiers

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | April 15, 2026

The 24 Hours of Nürburgring Qualifiers begin in three days — and Max Verstappen is going back to the circuit that took his win away earlier this season.

The Story So Far

Max Verstappen won the NLS2 race at the Nürburgring-Nordschleife earlier this season. Then his team was disqualified.

The infringement was a relatively straightforward tyre regulation violation — Verstappen's car had used seven tyre sets during the race, one more than the permitted six-set limit. The victory was struck from the results. The circuit that had briefly appeared in Verstappen's win column was erased.

Most drivers, with a Formula 1 championship battle consuming their professional lives, would move on. Verstappen, who has always treated his endurance racing participation as genuine rather than ceremonial, is returning to the Nordschleife this weekend — three days from now.

The Schedule

The 24 Hours of Nürburgring Qualifiers take place across Saturday, April 18 and Sunday, April 19, using the full 25.378-kilometer Nürburgring-Nordschleife loop — the circuit that has defined danger, endurance, and motorsport mythology for nearly a century.

Saturday begins with qualifying from 08:30 to 10:00, which sets the grid for the first of two four-hour races, starting at 17:30. Sunday opens with morning qualifying from 08:15 to 09:45, followed by Top Qualifying that determines the final starting order for Race 2 at 13:00. Two four-hour races, two days, one of the most unforgiving pieces of racing circuit on earth.

Verstappen's co-driver for both races is Lucas Auer — a partnership that has been developing alongside the Nürburgring programme through the season.

Why the Nordschleife, Why Now

The timing of Verstappen's Nürburgring participation this weekend is partly a product of circumstance. The cancellation of the Bahrain and Saudi Arabian Grands Prix — both called off due to the ongoing regional conflict — created an extended April break in the Formula 1 calendar. What was originally a standard inter-race gap became five clear weeks, giving drivers rare freedom in their schedules.

Verstappen has committed to the 24 Hours of Nürburgring main event later in the year. The Qualifiers are not simply warm-up events for entertainment — they are genuine preparation races, and experience on the Nordschleife counts toward entry requirements for the 24-hour race. Each lap of the circuit, each race hour, builds the familiarity that the Green Hell demands before teams commit to overnight endurance competition on its 25-kilometer stretch.

What's Next

After this weekend's two four-hour races, Verstappen returns his focus to Formula 1 as the April break winds down and preparation for Miami intensifies. The Miami Grand Prix on May 3 represents Red Bull's most significant upgrade opportunity of the season — team principal Laurent Mekies has described it as a "second season launch." The contrast between Verstappen's free-spirited Nordschleife racing and the enormous pressure riding on Miami is a study in what it means to be the sport's most dominant driver of the previous era, fighting to return to the front of the grid.
