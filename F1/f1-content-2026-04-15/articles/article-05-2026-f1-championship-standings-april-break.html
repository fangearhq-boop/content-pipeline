Where the 2026 F1 Championship Stands at the April Break Midpoint — Antonelli's Historic Lead

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | April 15, 2026

Three races in, five weeks without racing — the April break is the right moment to take stock of where the 2026 championship stands before Miami changes everything.

The Youngest Championship Leader in F1 History

Kimi Antonelli was a teenager when he won his first Formula 1 Grand Prix in China, and he is still a teenager now — 19 years old and sitting at the top of the 2026 drivers' championship with 72 points. That's nine points ahead of his Mercedes team-mate George Russell, and enough to make Antonelli the youngest championship leader in the history of Formula 1.

He is the only driver to have finished on the podium in all three races contested so far. He has been consistent not just in results but in performance — capable of winning, capable of managing the car, and demonstrating at each circuit a maturity that his age suggests shouldn't be possible yet. It is, in many ways, the story of the 2026 season so far.

The Full Standings Picture

Behind the Mercedes 1-2, Charles Leclerc holds third place with 49 points and Lewis Hamilton is fourth with 41. Ferrari's drivers are present in the top four — a reflection of the SF-26's genuine competitiveness — but the constructors' championship tells a harsher story about where the two teams actually stand. Mercedes leads with 135 points to Ferrari's 90, a margin of 45 points after just three races.

Oliver Bearman's presence in fifth (17 points) remains one of the genuine upsets of the 2026 season — the Haas driver, racing on the new regulations that have partially equalized the field, is outscoring Lando Norris at McLaren (15 points in sixth). The regulation reset has partially delivered on its promise of disrupting the established order at the midfield level.

Max Verstappen, who won four consecutive world championships, sits ninth with 12 points — the most startling statistic in the entire standings. A P6, a retirement, and a P8 have brought the four-time champion to a points total that can only be described as crisis-level by his historical standards.

The Championship Math

With 21 rounds remaining after Miami, no championship lead at this stage is remotely decisive. Antonelli's nine-point advantage over Russell amounts to less than half a race weekend's worth of points in a good scenario. The drivers' title will be decided in October.

What matters more right now is trajectory — and Mercedes' trajectory, with both their drivers in the top two of a championship that has run only three races, is exceptional. Russell is close enough to challenge and consistent enough to be dangerous. If Antonelli has an unexpected issue in Miami — a retirement, a penalty, a strategy miscalculation — the lead changes hands instantly.

Ferrari's trajectory depends on what the Monza filming day delivers before Miami. If the revised floor and aero package work as modeled, the Scuderia arrives in Florida meaningfully faster. If not, the 45-point constructors' gap starts to look structural rather than closeable.

What's Next

Miami, May 3. The first sprint format weekend of 2026 offers extra points availability — the Saturday sprint race adds to the championship arithmetic in a way that standard race weekends don't, potentially amplifying both gains and losses. Red Bull arrives with its "second season launch" upgrade. Ferrari arrives with the Monza-tested aero package. Mercedes arrives as the team everyone else has been chasing since the lights went out in Australia. The five-week April break ends in 18 days. The real 2026 championship begins in Miami.
