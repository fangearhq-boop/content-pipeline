Ferrari Heads to Monza Next Week — Hamilton and Leclerc to Test Miami Aero Package

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | April 15, 2026

Ferrari has confirmed a filming day at Monza on April 22, where both race drivers will evaluate the aerodynamic upgrades intended for the Miami Grand Prix.

The Upgrade That Never Got to Race

Ferrari's revised floor was designed and built for the Bahrain Grand Prix. It never got there. When the Bahrain race was cancelled — along with Saudi Arabia, as the regional conflict that had already reshaped the 2026 calendar continued to escalate — the upgrade sat in Ferrari's Maranello facility, ready for a circuit that no longer existed on the schedule.

Next Tuesday at Monza, the floor finally gets its chance. Ferrari has confirmed a filming day on April 22 at the legendary Italian circuit, where Lewis Hamilton and Charles Leclerc will put the SF-26 through its paces with the Miami aerodynamic package fitted. Both drivers will take turns behind the wheel — giving the Scuderia a comprehensive dual-driver evaluation of the upgrades before they commit them to race specification for May 3.

What Ferrari Is Testing — and Why Monza

Beyond the revised floor, the Monza session will also evaluate components connected to the halo wings that made a brief appearance during China practice sessions before being withdrawn. The halo wing elements were removed not because they were aerodynamically ineffective, but because they failed to meet a specific material compliance requirement in their current form. Ferrari expects to resolve that compliance issue before Miami.

The choice of Monza as the filming day venue is deliberate. The circuit — known primarily as the home of the Italian Grand Prix and the fastest track on the F1 calendar — presents one of the most demanding energy management scenarios under 2026 regulations. Its extended straights and limited low-speed corners create a power-unit environment where energy recovery is particularly challenging, making it an ideal development environment for Ferrari to stress-test how the SF-26 behaves with the upgraded aerodynamic package across different energy states.

It is also, not coincidentally, Ferrari's home turf. The team's presence at Monza carries logistical advantages, and the circuit allows for rapid iteration if data from early runs suggests changes are needed before the package is locked for Miami.

The Championship Context

Ferrari's urgency is not manufactured. Charles Leclerc sits third in the drivers' standings with 49 points, nine ahead of Lewis Hamilton in fourth. But in the constructors' championship, the gap to Mercedes stands at 45 points — a deficit that has accumulated over just three races and reflects the extent of Mercedes' advantage with the W17 under the 2026 regulations.

If Ferrari can land a strong result in Miami — ideally with both drivers finishing ahead of at least one Mercedes — the constructors' picture changes meaningfully. The Miami sprint format weekend adds extra points availability from the Saturday sprint race, making the potential swing even greater.

What's Next

The Monza filming day on April 22 gives Ferrari less than two weeks before Miami qualifying begins. If the data from the session confirms the upgrade delivers as modeled, the SF-26 will arrive in Florida as a meaningfully faster car than the one that contested Japan. Whether that's enough to challenge the Mercedes machinery consistently — rather than winning individual sessions — is the question Ferrari is trying to answer. The April 22 data will be the first real piece of evidence either way.
