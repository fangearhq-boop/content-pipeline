Red Bull's Historic Milestone: Verstappen's P3 Delivers First Podium With Their Own Engine

By Marcus Cole | May 27, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Max Verstappen's third-place finish at the Canadian Grand Prix was significant for several reasons. It was his first podium of the 2026 Formula 1 season. It was confirmation that Red Bull's development trajectory is moving in the right direction. And it was something no Formula 1 team had ever achieved before: a podium finish powered entirely by Red Bull Ford Powertrains — the engine division Red Bull built from scratch after their partnership with Honda ended.

Red Bull principal Laurent Mekies was direct about the significance. "To claim our very first podium with our own power unit, in only its fifth race, is truly a historic moment," he said. "Especially when you consider the pedigree and experience of our competitors."

What RBPT Represents

When Red Bull announced they would build their own Formula 1 power unit, the reaction from much of the paddock ranged from skepticism to disbelief. Engine programs in Formula 1 take decades to develop. Mercedes, Ferrari, and Honda have accumulated engineering knowledge across hundreds of race weekends, countless development cycles, and billions of dollars in investment. Red Bull were starting from a different position — informed by their partnership experience with Honda, but building their own architecture.

The partnership with Ford, announced in 2023, brought manufacturing expertise and financial resources. Red Bull built a dedicated facility in Milton Keynes to house the engine program. And at the 2026 season opener, the RBPT power unit took to the grid in a Verstappen-driven Red Bull for the first time as a fully homologated competitor.

Four races passed without a podium. Then in Montreal, on Lap 1, Verstappen was in the points. By the midpoint of the race, he was running third. He held that position, withstanding pressure in the closing stages, until Lewis Hamilton found a way past in the final phase of the race. Verstappen finished P3. The RBPT engine had its first Formula 1 podium in just its fifth attempt.

Verstappen's Perspective: Pleased But Not Satisfied

For Verstappen, the podium was a starting point rather than a destination. "Very happy with that," he said after the race. "For us to have our first podium is just very positive. And I also know we can do better."

The four-time World Champion was explicit about what "better" means: he was passed by Hamilton on Lap 70, surrendering second place in the closing stages. That hurt. Verstappen does not race for third place — he has the competitive context of four consecutive world championships in his recent memory. Third place, however historically significant for the team, is not where he intends to remain.

The positive trajectory he referenced is real. Red Bull's pace relative to the front runners has improved across the first five rounds of 2026. In the season opener, they were several tenths off the pace. By Canada, Verstappen was in genuine contention for a podium from the start of the race, not through strategic fortune but through outright performance.

What Comes Next

Verstappen sits seventh in the drivers' championship with 44 points after Canada — 87 points behind Antonelli's leading 131. A championship challenge in 2026 is almost certainly beyond reach. The four rounds Verstappen lost without points while the Mercedes drivers accumulated massive tallies represent a gap that realistically cannot be closed.

But that framing misses the larger story. Red Bull's 2026 season is not about winning the championship — it is about proving that their engine program can reach the front. Each race weekend generates data, development insight, and component performance improvements that feed the next generation of the RBPT power unit.

A podium in race five. Verstappen saying they can do better. Mekies calling it a historic moment. These are not the words of a team in crisis — they are the words of a program on an upward curve. Monaco is next, and the Circuit de Monaco is not traditionally a Red Bull stronghold. But after Canada, the team heads there with a confidence they didn't have at the start of the season.
