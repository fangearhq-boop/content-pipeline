# F1 Fanrecap — Seasonal Calendar

Use this to calibrate story selection and tiering throughout the season.

## 2026 Key Context

**2026 is a major regulation year.** New power unit regulations (increased electrical power, sustainable fuels), significant aerodynamic rule changes, and active aerodynamics. This is the biggest technical shakeup since 2022 and will dominate pre-season and early-season narratives. The competitive order may be reshuffled entirely.

## Race Weekend Structure

| Day | Sessions | Notes |
|-----|----------|-------|
| Friday | FP1 (60 min), FP2 (60 min) | Or FP1 + Sprint Qualifying on sprint weekends |
| Saturday | FP3 (60 min), Qualifying | Or Sprint Race + Qualifying on sprint weekends |
| Sunday | Race | Grand Prix (varies 50-70 laps depending on circuit) |

Sprint weekends feature a shorter Saturday race (~100 km) with separate points (8-7-6-5-4-3-2-1 for top 8).

## Key Races & Events

### Marquee Grands Prix (always Tier 1)
- **Monaco Grand Prix** — the crown jewel, street circuit, glamour, history
- **British Grand Prix (Silverstone)** — home of F1, massive attendance, passionate crowd
- **Italian Grand Prix (Monza)** — the Temple of Speed, Ferrari's home race, tifosi
- **Abu Dhabi Grand Prix** — season finale, championship decider, night race under lights

### Other High-Profile Races
- **Australian Grand Prix** — traditional season opener (or early race), Albert Park
- **Canadian Grand Prix (Montreal)** — Wall of Champions, unpredictable weather
- **Belgian Grand Prix (Spa)** — Eau Rouge, rain drama, one of the great circuits
- **Japanese Grand Prix (Suzuka)** — driver favorite, technical circuit, passionate fans
- **Las Vegas Grand Prix** — night race on the Strip, spectacle event
- **Singapore Grand Prix** — original night race, street circuit, high attrition

## Monthly Guide

### January – February
- Car launches and livery reveals
- Driver lineup confirmations
- Pre-season testing (2-3 days, single venue)
- Testing lap times and reliability are Tier 2-3 (DO NOT overreact to testing pace)
- Regulation analysis and technical previews

### March
- **Season opens.** Currently March 2026 = first few races of the season.
- Opening race always Tier 1
- Early form guide — who adapted best to 2026 regulations
- First championship points awarded

### April – May
- European season begins
- Sprint weekends start appearing on calendar
- Early championship picture takes shape
- Monaco Grand Prix (late May) — always Tier 1

### June – July
- Heart of the European season
- Canadian GP, British GP (Silverstone), Austrian GP, Hungarian GP
- Silverstone is always Tier 1
- Driver market rumors intensify before summer break
- Mid-season driver/team assessments

### August
- Summer break (mandatory ~2-week factory shutdown)
- Driver contract announcements and silly season
- No racing — fill with historical features, driver profiles, regulation previews
- Belgian GP (Spa) often right before or after break

### September – October
- Italian GP (Monza) — always Tier 1
- Championship battles intensify
- Japanese GP (Suzuka) — driver favorite
- Singapore GP — night race, high drama potential
- Americas swing (Austin, Mexico, Sao Paulo)

### November – December
- Las Vegas GP — spectacle event
- Middle East closing swing (Qatar, Abu Dhabi)
- Abu Dhabi finale — always Tier 1 (championship could be decided here)
- Post-season testing
- Silly season for next year's driver market
- FIA Prize Giving Gala

## Story Selection Reminders

- **Championship-deciding races always Tier 1** — any race where a title can be mathematically won is automatic lead story
- **Last-lap overtakes and photo finishes always Tier 1** — regardless of position or teams involved
- **Major crashes and red flags always Tier 1** — safety is paramount; report facts, not speculation
- **Driver transfers between teams always Tier 1** — reshapes the competitive order
- **Regulation changes always Tier 2+** — 2026 regs are Tier 1 because of magnitude
- **Team bias awareness** — cover all 10 teams, not just Red Bull/Ferrari/Mercedes/McLaren
- **Midfield matters** — the fight for P6-P10 is often the best racing on track
- **Race times always in ET** — F1 is a global sport; many races are early morning or overnight ET. Always note local time too.
- **Sprint weekends get extra coverage** — additional qualifying and race means more content opportunities
