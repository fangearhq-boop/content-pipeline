McLaren's Quiet Threat at Canada — Why Norris and Piastri Are the Wild Card at Gilles-Villeneuve

By Elena Voss | May 18, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Everyone is talking about what Mercedes is bringing to Canada. Not enough people are talking about McLaren.

The papaya team arrives in Montreal with Lando Norris fourth in the drivers' championship with 51 points and Oscar Piastri sixth with 43. Both trail leader Kimi Antonelli by 49 and 57 points respectively. On paper, that looks comfortable for Mercedes. In practice, the Miami Grand Prix suggested the performance gap is narrowing faster than the standings show.

McLaren finished P2 and P3 in Miami with the second phase of their 2026 upgrade sequence arriving and making an immediate difference. By the end of the Miami race, McLaren arguably had the fastest car on the circuit. If that rate of development continues to Canada, they arrive in Montreal as genuine contenders for race victory — not just the podium.

The Math That Makes McLaren Dangerous

Here is the scenario Mercedes needs to avoid: Norris or Piastri wins both the sprint and the race in Canada while the Mercedes drivers split points between them.

If Antonelli finishes second and Russell third in both sessions, Mercedes collects 18 + 8 = 26 points from the sprint and 18 + 15 = 33 points from the race. If Norris wins both, he collects 8 + 25 = 33 sprint and race points. The net result: Norris would gain 7 points on Antonelli across the weekend. Do that twice more and Norris is suddenly within a race win of the championship lead.

The math only works if McLaren beats Mercedes. But after Miami, that is no longer an unreasonable expectation — it is a genuine possibility.

The Internal Dynamic

McLaren's internal championship battle adds its own complexity. Norris (2025 world champion) and Piastri are both title-eligible in 2026, and both have demonstrated the pace and racecraft to challenge for wins. That creates a scenario that teams dread: two genuine championship contenders on the same team, with team orders becoming an increasingly uncomfortable topic as the season progresses.

In Miami, both drivers were on the podium — an ideal outcome that avoided the question entirely. In Canada, with a sprint race adding a second championship session to the weekend, the chances of a genuine internal conflict increase. If Norris is ahead of Piastri in the sprint but Piastri has the faster race pace, what does McLaren do? If Piastri overtakes Norris for the lead in the race, does the team intervene?

Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve's unique layout — long straights, heavy braking zones, and the critical Wall of Champions finale — rewards different driving styles at different points of the lap. The internal McLaren battle here could be as compelling as the battle against Mercedes.

What's Next

McLaren enters Canada as the most interesting team at the circuit, even if they are not the most discussed. Their upgrade trajectory, their internal dynamic, and the specific circuit characteristics all point to a weekend where they will push Mercedes harder than the standings suggest they should be able to. The Canadian Grand Prix weekend begins Friday, May 22 with sprint qualifying at 9:30 PM ET. The sprint race follows Saturday afternoon, and the grand prix runs Sunday from approximately 2:00 PM ET.
