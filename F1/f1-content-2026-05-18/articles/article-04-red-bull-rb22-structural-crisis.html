Red Bull's RB22 Crisis Explained — Overweight, Overdue, and Running Out of Time

By Ryan Calloway | May 18, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Max Verstappen is a four-time Formula 1 world champion. He enters the 2026 Canadian Grand Prix weekend ranked seventh in the drivers' championship with 26 points — behind Oliver Bearman, Pierre Gasly, and Liam Lawson.

This is not a story about Verstappen's ability. It is a story about the car underneath him.

Red Bull's RB22 has significant structural problems that the team's own engineers have acknowledged may not be fully resolved until an entirely new chassis is available for the 2027 season. In the meantime, the four-time champion is fighting the car as much as his rivals.

The Weight Problem

The central issue is straightforward: the RB22 is overweight. In Formula 1, where margins of performance are measured in hundredths of a second, carrying excess mass has cascading effects on everything else the car does.

A heavier car puts more load on the tyres under braking and cornering. More load means more heat generated in the rubber. More heat means faster degradation. Faster degradation means more pit stops, less strategic flexibility, and lower sustained pace. It is a self-reinforcing problem that Red Bull has been unable to solve through setup adjustments alone.

Verstappen described the car as "incredibly tough to drive" after the Chinese Grand Prix, saying that "every lap is a fight." Red Bull admitted to "significant shortcomings" following that race. The language was unusually direct for a team accustomed to presenting confidence to the outside world. The problems are real, and the team knows it.

The Exit Clause Timeline

The weight problem becomes particularly acute in the context of Verstappen's contract. His agreement with Red Bull contains a performance-related exit clause: if he is not within the top two of the drivers' championship by the time of the Hungarian Grand Prix (July 24-26), he has the right to terminate his contract early.

He currently sits 54 points behind second-placed George Russell. With several races between now and Hungary, and with an underperforming car, the mathematical path to second place is extremely narrow. Canada alone cannot close that gap — but a strong result could at least demonstrate that the trajectory is moving in the right direction.

The alternative — that Verstappen triggers the clause and exits Red Bull — opens a period of extraordinary speculation about his next destination. McLaren, Mercedes, and Ferrari have all been mentioned in the context of potential future Verstappen moves. His engineer Gianpiero Lambiase is already confirmed for McLaren at the end of 2027. The pieces of a larger picture are already being arranged.

What's Next

The Canadian Grand Prix does not fix Red Bull's structural problems. No single weekend can. What it can do is provide data about whether the incremental updates the team has been applying are making any meaningful difference, and whether Verstappen's championship situation is salvageable with the current package. Sprint qualifying begins Friday evening at 9:30 PM ET. The sprint race follows Saturday. The main race is Sunday. Red Bull needs every point it can get — and at a circuit that typically demands chassis efficiency over raw power, the RB22's weight problem is unlikely to become less visible in Montreal.
