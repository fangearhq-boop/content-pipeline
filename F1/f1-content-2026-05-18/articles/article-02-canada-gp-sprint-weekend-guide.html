Canada GP 2026: Your Complete Sprint Weekend Guide — Schedule, Stakes, and What to Watch

By Marcus Cole | May 18, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Four days from now, Formula 1 descends on Montreal for one of the most anticipated weekends of the 2026 season.

The 2026 Canadian Grand Prix at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve runs May 22-24, and it brings a historic first to one of F1's most beloved venues: for the first time ever, the legendary Montreal circuit will host a sprint race weekend. The island circuit on the St. Lawrence River, which has hosted grands prix since 1978, has never before seen the compressed, high-stakes format of a sprint weekend. It does now.

Here is everything you need to know about what's happening, when it's happening, and why it matters.

Full Weekend Schedule

Friday, May 22:
Free Practice — 12:30 PM ET (the only practice session of the entire weekend)
Sprint Qualifying — 9:30 PM ET (sets the grid for Saturday's sprint race)

Saturday, May 23:
Sprint Race — 5:00 PM ET (approximately 25 laps, maximum 30 minutes, 8 points for the winner)
Grand Prix Qualifying — 9:00 PM ET (sets the grid for Sunday's main race)

Sunday, May 24:
Canadian Grand Prix — approximately 2:00 PM ET (70 laps around the 4.361km circuit)

The critical point for fans: with only one free practice session in the weekend, teams have almost no time to understand new components before points are at stake. Mercedes is bringing a major upgrade. McLaren has been developing rapidly. Everyone will be reading the car on the fly from almost the moment they arrive.

The Circuit

Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve is a semi-permanent track built on the Île Notre-Dame in the middle of the St. Lawrence River. It is one of F1's most distinctive venues: flat, fast, and punishing to drivers who push even slightly beyond the limit.

The circuit's signature feature is the Wall of Champions — the concrete barrier at the exit of the final chicane that has collected world champions since 1999. Flat-out through the back section, a high-speed hairpin, and then the final chicane where champions and backmarkers alike have hit the wall. It rewards commitment and punishes hesitation equally.

The circuit is traditionally considered low-downforce and power-sensitive, which should theoretically favor the most powerful power units. But the 2026 technical regulations' emphasis on active aerodynamics changes the calculation. Teams can now deploy aerodynamic drag reduction through the straight sections in ways that were previously impossible, making the circuit's traditional characteristics less deterministic than they used to be.

Championship Stakes at Each Session

Each session this weekend carries specific championship weight. The sprint offers 8 points to the winner, 7 for second, and down through the points. Combined with the main race (25 points for the winner), there are approximately 34 points available across the full weekend — the highest single-weekend points haul of the 2026 season.

Antonelli leads Russell by 20 points. If Russell wins both the sprint and the race while Antonelli finishes third in both, the gap drops from 20 to effectively zero entering the following race. That is the scale of what is possible in a sprint weekend. Every session matters from the moment the cars leave the pit lane on Friday evening.

What's Next

Sprint qualifying begins Friday evening at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve. The sprint race follows Saturday afternoon, with grand prix qualifying that evening. The Canadian Grand Prix itself runs Sunday. All sessions are available on ESPN and ESPN+ in the United States, Sky Sports in the United Kingdom, and TSN in Canada. The championship picture entering Canada is tight, the upgrade packages are significant, and the format guarantees something will happen at every session. This is one to watch from start to finish.
