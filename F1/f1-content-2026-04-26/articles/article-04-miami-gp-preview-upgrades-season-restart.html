Miami GP Preview: Upgrades, New Rules, and F1's Season Restart

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | April 26, 2026

Formula 1 has not been on a racing circuit since March 29 — and after five weeks, it returns with something to prove. The Miami Grand Prix Sprint weekend, running May 1-3 at the Miami International Autodrome, is the most consequential race weekend of the early season: a competitive reset where the gains made during the enforced April break will be put on display for the first time in anger.

The break was created by necessity. The Bahrain and Saudi Arabian Grands Prix were cancelled due to the ongoing conflict in the Middle East, leaving a five-round gap in the calendar between Japan and Miami. Teams used every available hour of the pause for development work, simulator runs, and — in the cases of Red Bull and Ferrari — filming days at Silverstone and Monza respectively to evaluate the packages they will debut in South Florida.

New Rules in Effect

The FIA announced a series of regulatory changes that take effect from Miami onward. The most significant: super-clipping is increased from 250 kilowatts to the maximum of 350 kilowatts, meaning drivers can harvest more energy while remaining at full throttle on long straights. The deployment recharge rate simultaneously decreases from eight to seven megajoules. The changes affect all power units equally in theory, but teams with more efficient energy management systems may extract more benefit in practice.

Free Practice 1 has been extended to 90 minutes — up from the standard 60 — to compensate for the extended absence and give engineers more time to set up their cars for the Sprint format, which removes FP2 entirely from the schedule. The first competitive session after FP1 will be Sprint Qualifying on Friday afternoon, meaning teams must be ready to extract maximum performance very quickly.

The Upgrade Battle

Red Bull's overhaul of the RB22 is the most dramatic upgrade of the restart: new floor, reworked sidepods, revised front wing, and the Macarena rear wing concept borrowed from Ferrari. Team principal Laurent Mekies called it a "second season launch" — a phrase that captures both the scope of the changes and the urgency behind them.

Ferrari arrives with the Macarena wing in full competitive trim for the first time, alongside a reworked floor package. McLaren has new components planned. Mercedes, which leads the constructors' championship by 45 points and has won all three races of 2026, will be watching carefully to see which rivals have made the most progress before deciding whether to accelerate their own development timeline.

Championship standings entering Miami: Kimi Antonelli (Mercedes) 72 pts, George Russell (Mercedes) 63 pts, Charles Leclerc (Ferrari) 49 pts, Lewis Hamilton (Ferrari) 41 pts, Lando Norris (McLaren) 25 pts, Oscar Piastri (McLaren) 21 pts. Constructors: Mercedes 135, Ferrari 90, McLaren 56, Red Bull 16.

What's Next

FP1 runs Friday, May 1, with the extended 90-minute session giving teams their first competitive track time in five weeks. Sprint Qualifying follows Friday afternoon, setting the grid for Saturday's Sprint race. Full qualifying takes place Saturday, and the Miami Grand Prix runs Sunday at 4 PM ET. If the upgrades across the field deliver their intended performance gains, Miami could be the moment the 2026 season becomes genuinely unpredictable.
