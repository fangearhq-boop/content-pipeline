Hamilton Outruns Leclerc at Monza: Ferrari's Miami Upgrade and an Internal Battle Worth Watching

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | April 26, 2026

Lewis Hamilton's transition to Ferrari has been described in many ways over the past season: as a late-career gamble, as a reunion with a team he nearly joined years ago, as a final chapter written in red. But during Ferrari's Monza filming day to test their Miami upgrade package, something simpler happened — the seven-time world champion went faster than Charles Leclerc.

Hamilton completed 15 laps and set a best time of 1:26.585. Leclerc ran more laps — 19 in total — but Hamilton's pace was sharper. Both drivers reported positive feedback on the package, which Ferrari will bring to Miami in full competitive trim for the first time. The test was officially a filming day, meaning its purpose was to gather data on the SF-26's response to a significantly revised aerodynamic configuration, including the Macarena rear wing and a reworked floor.

For Ferrari, the session accomplished its technical goal. But the lap time split between their two drivers set off a conversation that will not be resolved until the Miami Grand Prix is run.

Hamilton's Resurgence

Hamilton has spoken publicly this season about feeling energized in a way he hasn't since the early 2020s — before the ground-effect regulation transition stranded Mercedes at the back of the top teams and drained what he described as his competitive fire. At Ferrari, that fire has apparently returned. In three races, he has taken three podium finishes. He sits fourth in the championship with 41 points, eight behind Leclerc.

The Monza lap time is one data point, not a verdict. Filming days use control compounds, the cars are not always at full performance mode, and both drivers were prioritizing setup learning over outright speed. But in the world of Formula 1, where every number is scrutinized for narrative meaning, a Hamilton lap time faster than Leclerc's carries weight regardless of context.

The seven-time champion was the most decorated driver Ferrari has ever signed. Whether he is also the fastest in the car by Miami will be one of the most-watched questions of the season restart.

Leclerc's Position

Leclerc enters Miami as the clear No. 1 driver in Ferrari's championship calculation — third in the standings, consistent, and driving with the maturity of a driver who has been through multiple title campaigns. His 19 laps in the Monza test show focused, methodical work. The additional running was likely data-driven rather than a response to Hamilton's pace.

Ferrari's hierarchy heading into Miami is not in crisis. But the presence of Hamilton as a genuine threat to Leclerc's status is a subplot that will only intensify if the SF-26 upgrade makes Ferrari competitive in the fight for race wins.

What's Next

Miami represents Ferrari's first real chance to challenge Mercedes with the full upgrade package. If the Macarena wing and revised floor deliver the performance gain the team has modeled, Leclerc and Hamilton could both be fighting for the podium — or for the race win. How Ferrari manages that situation will tell us a great deal about where the team's internal dynamics are heading for the rest of 2026.
