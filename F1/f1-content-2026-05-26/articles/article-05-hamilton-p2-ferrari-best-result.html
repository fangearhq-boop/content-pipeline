Hamilton's P2 in Canada Is Ferrari's Best 2026 Result — And He Did It Without the Simulator

By Ryan Calloway | May 26, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Lewis Hamilton's second place finish at the 2026 Canadian Grand Prix was more than a podium. It was a statement: the seven-time world champion is finding his feet with Ferrari, and his instincts — not his simulator — got him there.

Hamilton crossed the line 10.7 seconds behind race winner Kimi Antonelli and held off Max Verstappen's Red Bull in the final laps to secure P2, his best result since joining the Scuderia for the 2026 season. His previous best had been a P3 in China back in Round 2.

The No-Simulator Decision

Before the Canadian Grand Prix, Hamilton made a decision that raised eyebrows in the paddock: he would not use Ferrari's simulator to prepare for the race weekend. His reasoning was direct.

At the Miami Grand Prix, Hamilton had followed the simulator's set-up recommendations — and found the car didn't behave as predicted at the actual circuit. "It took me in the wrong direction," he said. "The correlations were off." His Miami result was poor.

Notably, his strongest 2026 result before Canada — the P3 in China — was the one race he'd approached without simulator preparation. Hamilton decided to apply that logic to Montreal, trusting instead the six previous race wins he had accumulated at the Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve during his McLaren and Mercedes years.

The gamble paid off with a podium finish.

Ferrari's Internal Championship Fight

Hamilton's P2 and Charles Leclerc's P4 in Canada have created an intriguing situation inside the Ferrari garage. Hamilton now sits just 3 points behind his teammate in the drivers' championship standings — the closest they have been all season.

Leclerc has been the nominal No. 1 through the early rounds, but Hamilton's late-season surge appears to be arriving just as the European leg of the calendar begins — a stretch that includes Monaco, a circuit Hamilton knows intimately and where his instincts-based approach could pay dividends once again.

Ferrari is also just 2 points behind Mercedes in the constructors' standings, meaning every result matters to the team. Both drivers being competitive at once is exactly what the Scuderia needs at this point of the season.

What's Next

Hamilton heads to Monaco (June 5-7) with momentum and a new approach to race preparation. The Circuit de Monaco is a circuit where simulator correlation is notoriously difficult — another potential advantage for a driver who is now deliberately working outside that framework. Ferrari expects to be the team to beat at Monaco. Hamilton, finding form at exactly the right moment, will be one of the drivers to watch.
