Antonelli Wins Canadian Grand Prix After Russell Retires from Lead — Championship Gap Grows to 43

By Elena Voss | May 26, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Kimi Antonelli claimed his fourth consecutive Formula 1 victory at the 2026 Canadian Grand Prix on Sunday, extending his drivers' championship lead to 43 points after teammate George Russell was forced to retire from the race lead on Lap 30 due to a power unit failure.

The result delivers the 2026 championship's most dramatic swing yet. Russell had been leading the race at the Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve in Montreal when his Mercedes W16 lost all power — leaving him stranded on track while Antonelli inherited the lead and drove home unchallenged.

Podium: Antonelli, Hamilton, Verstappen

With Russell out of the equation, Lewis Hamilton produced Ferrari's best result of the 2026 season, crossing the line in second place 10.7 seconds behind Antonelli. The seven-time world champion held off Max Verstappen's Red Bull in the closing stages to secure P2, his highest finish in Ferrari colors this year.

Verstappen took the third step of the podium (+11.2 seconds), recording Red Bull's first rostrum result of 2026. Charles Leclerc finished a distant fourth for Ferrari (+44.1 seconds), failing to capitalize on a day when his teammate did the damage in the standings.

The full top-10: Antonelli, Hamilton, Verstappen, Leclerc, Hadjar, Colapinto, Lawson, Gasly, Sainz, Bearman.

Four in a Row

Antonelli, 19, has now won all four of the 2026 grands prix he has completed — having won in China, Japan, Miami, and now Canada. His early-season dominance has been built on pole positions, clean starts, and the kind of strategic awareness that suggests a far more experienced driver.

The Canadian win came with an element of fortune, but Antonelli had already been racing intelligently before Russell's retirement gifted him the lead. He was the team's strongest asset on the day even before the incident unfolded.

With 131 points, Antonelli now has a 43-point cushion over Russell (88 points) with the Monaco Grand Prix approaching on June 5-7.

What's Next

The 2026 F1 season continues at the Monaco Grand Prix, June 5-7 at the Circuit de Monaco in Monte Carlo. The 3.337km street circuit is the shortest on the calendar and renders the sort of power unit advantage that has propelled Mercedes thus far largely irrelevant. Ferrari, whose SF-26 is built for high-downforce, low-speed circuits, is expected to be the team to beat. Even Antonelli acknowledged it: "Ferrari will be the team to beat in Monaco."
