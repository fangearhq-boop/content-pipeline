F1 2026 Championship Standings: Antonelli Leads by 43 After Canada — Monaco Next

By Marcus Cole | May 26, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

The 2026 Formula 1 drivers' championship has never looked more one-sided since the season began. After five races — four of them won by Kimi Antonelli — the standings show a 43-point gap at the top, the largest lead anyone has held at any point this season.

Antonelli leads with 131 points. George Russell, his Mercedes teammate, sits second with 88. The gap grew by 25 points in Canada alone, where Antonelli won and Russell retired from the lead with a power unit failure.

Standings After Five Rounds

At the top, the headline is Antonelli's dominant run: wins in China, Japan, Miami, and Canada. His sole non-win came in Bahrain, where he still collected strong points. The consistency is extraordinary for a driver in his first full F1 season.

Russell's season has been the counterpoint — brilliant pace, multiple poles, a Sprint win in Canada, but a string of results-robbing misfortunes. A 43-point deficit with the Monaco, Spanish, Canadian, and British Grands Prix still ahead is difficult but not impossible to overcome.

Further down the standings, the Ferrari internal battle has intensified. Charles Leclerc holds P3, but Lewis Hamilton is just 3 points behind in P4 after his P2 in Montreal — his best result since joining the Scuderia for 2026. Ferrari sits just 2 points behind Mercedes in the constructors' standings, making every race critical.

Max Verstappen, who took P3 in Canada for Red Bull's first podium of 2026, sits seventh in the championship. The three-time champion has struggled to find the form that made him dominant in prior seasons, but his Montreal result signals the Red Bull may be improving.

The Monaco Wildcard

The championship standing entering Monaco (June 5-7) is deceptive. Monte Carlo is the one circuit where the power unit advantage that has made Mercedes dominant through the first five races becomes irrelevant. The Circuit de Monaco has no long straights, tight corners, and essentially no overtaking — meaning qualifying determines everything.

Antonelli himself has identified Ferrari as the Monaco favorites. If Leclerc or Hamilton wins the race and Antonelli struggles, the standings will tighten in a hurry. For Russell, any result that closes the gap matters enormously — his season is at a crossroads.

What's Next

The Monaco Grand Prix takes place June 5-7 at the Circuit de Monaco. Practice begins Friday. For Antonelli, it is a chance to extend a lead that has become genuinely commanding. For Ferrari, it may be the best opportunity of the season to challenge Mercedes at the front of the grid.
