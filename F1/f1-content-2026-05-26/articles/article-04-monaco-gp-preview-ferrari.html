Monaco Grand Prix Preview: Antonelli Says Ferrari Will Shine — Everything to Know

By Elena Voss | May 26, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

The Monaco Grand Prix is the race that changes championships. And for the 2026 Formula 1 season — so far defined by Kimi Antonelli's four-win domination — Monte Carlo on June 5-7 represents the biggest challenge to Mercedes' authority yet.

Even Antonelli knows it. "Ferrari will be the team to beat in Monaco," the championship leader told Sky Sports F1 after winning the Canadian Grand Prix. "Their car provides a lot of downforce at low speeds. Our engine advantage won't matter there."

Why Monaco Favors Ferrari

The Circuit de Monaco runs just 3.337 kilometers through the streets of Monte Carlo, featuring some of the tightest corners in motorsport and no meaningful straight. Every other circuit on the 2026 calendar has at least one long straight where Mercedes' power unit advantage — decisive in Bahrain, Shanghai, Suzuka, Miami, and Montreal — can be deployed.

Monaco has none. What matters here is mechanical grip, low-speed downforce, and the ability to thread a racing car through barriers that never forgive. The Ferrari SF-26 was designed with these characteristics in mind, and Ferrari's engineers have been pointing to the European street circuits as their strongest opportunity of the season.

Charles Leclerc, who was born in Monaco and has come agonizingly close to winning his home grand prix on multiple occasions, will be among the pre-race favorites. Lewis Hamilton arrives in Monte Carlo with momentum after his P2 in Canada — his best Ferrari result of 2026.

Qualifying Is Everything

At Monaco, the starting grid largely determines the finishing order. Overtaking is nearly impossible — the barriers are close, the straights are short, and the pit lane entry is tight. Any driver who secures pole position in Saturday qualifying has a significant advantage heading into Sunday's race.

For Antonelli and Russell, who have dominated qualifying at most 2026 circuits, Monaco represents an unfamiliar dynamic. Ferrari and possibly Red Bull — whose low-speed package showed promise at Baku last season — may out-qualify Mercedes for the first time all year.

What's Next

The Monaco Grand Prix weekend begins with practice sessions on Thursday and Friday, June 5-6. Qualifying takes place Saturday, June 6. The race is Sunday, June 7. All sessions will be broadcast on Sky Sports F1, and the race is available on ABC/ESPN in the United States.

For the first time since the season opened in Bahrain, Mercedes does not arrive as the clear favorite. Monaco could reshape the 2026 championship — and the fight is very much alive.
