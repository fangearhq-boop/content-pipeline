### STORY 2: Russell's Montreal Meltdown

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM ET

George Russell's 2026 Canadian Grand Prix ended in heartbreak — and briefly, in controversy.

After retiring from the race lead on Lap 30 due to a power unit failure, Russell threw his headrest from the stricken car onto the circuit. It was a moment of raw frustration from a driver who had just watched 25 championship points evaporate in an instant.

FIA stewards took note. Russell was summoned to an investigation hearing, where he acknowledged the danger of his actions and issued a public apology. The outcome: a suspended €5,000 fine, contingent on the apology.

Russell described his behavior as "embarrassing." He also provided the quote of the race weekend: "It feels like somebody doesn't want me to fight for this championship."

Mercedes team principal Toto Wolff confirmed the cause of the failure: "The car was literally going dark — there was no electricity in the car anymore." There were no warning signs. Russell could not have prevented it.

The gap to Antonelli is now 43 points with six races remaining before Monaco.

There are no long straights where Mercedes' power unit advantage can express itself. The Ferrari SF-26, designed with exceptional low-speed downforce, is perfectly suited to Monte Carlo.

Charles Leclerc has made Monaco his personal circuit — it is, famously, his home race. Lewis Hamilton's P2 in Canada showed he is finding his Ferrari feet just in time for the European swing.

For Antonelli and Russell, Monaco represents both a threat and an opportunity. For Ferrari, it could be the weekend where they remind everyone that this championship is not over.
