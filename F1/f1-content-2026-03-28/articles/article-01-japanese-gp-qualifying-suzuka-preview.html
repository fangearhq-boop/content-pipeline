2026 Japanese GP Qualifying: What to Expect at Suzuka

By F1 Fanrecap Staff | March 28, 2026

There is no circuit in Formula 1 quite like Suzuka. The Japanese Grand Prix venue sits in a category of its own — a track that has defined careers, settled championships, and produced some of the sport's most unforgettable moments across four decades of World Championship racing. On Saturday March 28, the 2026 season's grid for Round 3 will be decided here.

Mercedes came into the weekend as the clear form team. George Russell and Kimi Antonelli led practice across all three sessions, continuing the form that delivered 1-2 finishes in both Australia and China. But teams rarely show their full qualifying potential in practice, and the competitive order in Q3 could be considerably more compressed than the Friday and Saturday morning sessions suggested.

Why Qualifying at Suzuka Matters More Than Almost Anywhere

Suzuka is notoriously difficult to overtake at during the race. The circuit's layout — with its flowing high-speed sections, technical infield, and the iconic 130R corner — rewards cars and drivers who can put together a perfect lap, but offers few opportunities for position changes once the race begins. Starting from pole position at Suzuka carries a premium that doesn't exist at the same level elsewhere on the calendar.

For the title contenders, the implications are especially pointed. Russell leads Antonelli by just 4 points in the championship. If one starts ahead of the other and can dictate pace from the front, the gap could swing meaningfully in either direction. For Lando Norris and McLaren — desperate to end their catastrophic run of reliability failures — a strong qualifying slot could be the difference between a race-winning opportunity and another forgettable result.

The Key Battles to Watch in Qualifying

The Mercedes internal battle between Russell and Antonelli will be the headline number to watch — specifically whether the rookie can repeat his China momentum by splitting the front row or taking pole. Antonelli has shown extraordinary composure for a driver in his debut season, and a Suzuka pole would be a statement of intent.

Ferrari will be hoping that Leclerc and Hamilton can get within striking distance of the top two. Leclerc is just 17 points behind Russell in the championship, and Ferrari have shown genuine race-day pace even when qualifying has put them slightly behind Mercedes. Hamilton at Suzuka — racing the circuit he knows as well as any in his career — will be one of the session's most captivating storylines.

For Red Bull, qualifying at Suzuka represents a potential opportunity. The circuit's high-downforce requirements should theoretically suit Max Verstappen, who remains one of the best qualifying drivers in the sport's history despite his car's struggles this season. A front-row start for Verstappen would dramatically reshape the race narrative.

What's Next

Qualifying results will set the stage for Sunday's race at 6:00 AM GMT (2:00 AM ET). The Japanese GP is the last round on the 2026 calendar until the Miami Grand Prix on May 3, giving Sunday's result an unusual weight — whatever the championship standings look like after Suzuka will stand for five full weeks before the next race.
