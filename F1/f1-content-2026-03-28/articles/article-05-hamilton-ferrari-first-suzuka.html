Hamilton at Suzuka in Ferrari Red: A Historic First for a Legend

By F1 Fanrecap Staff | March 28, 2026

Lewis Hamilton has been to Suzuka 19 times. He knows the layout by heart — every bump, every camber change, the exact line through the Esses, the late apex that unlocks the straight before Degner. The Japanese Grand Prix circuit is part of the fabric of his career in ways that few other venues match.

But when Hamilton walks to the grid on Sunday March 29, 2026, he will be doing so as a Ferrari driver for the first time. After 12 years at Mercedes, 7 world championships, and a final season in silver that produced not a single podium, Hamilton chose the move that has been a subject of Formula 1 speculation for the better part of a decade. And through two races, the second act of his career is going exactly as he hoped.

The Ferrari Honeymoon

Hamilton finished P4 at the Australian Grand Prix season opener — strong, measured, and exactly the kind of race that suggested he had adapted to his new car quickly. But it was China that delivered the emotional moment the sport had been waiting for: his first Ferrari podium, in third place, earning him his first rostrum in 16 months.

"I'm back to my best," he said after the Chinese GP. The declaration was not boastful — it was a statement from a man who had gone through a difficult final year at Mercedes, had questions to answer about whether he still belonged at the front of the grid, and had answered them with characteristic directness.

His partner at Ferrari, Charles Leclerc, sits third in the championship at 34 points. The Ferrari is consistently quick across different types of circuits. The strategic decisions in Australia cost Ferrari a potential victory — they were leading before a suboptimal pit call during Virtual Safety Car periods. Hamilton will want to avoid similar issues as the season progresses.

Suzuka and Hamilton's History

Hamilton's relationship with Suzuka spans his entire F1 career. He raced there for the first time in 2007, his debut season. He has won the Japanese Grand Prix multiple times, and the circuit has been the venue for both championship-clinching moments and near-misses. This depth of experience — the institutional knowledge of how to attack the circuit, where to find lap time, how to manage tires across a race distance — is invaluable in a new car.

The challenge for Hamilton is translating decades of accumulated Suzuka knowledge from the reference points of previous cars to the very different feel and behaviour of the 2026 Ferrari. Different braking points, different rotation characteristics, different weight balance. The circuit's demands remain the same; the tools with which he must meet them have changed.

What's Next

Hamilton faces qualifying at Suzuka on March 28 looking to build on his China momentum and put his Ferrari in striking distance of the championship lead. A strong weekend at Japan — particularly given the 5-week gap before Miami — would validate his move to Ferrari in a way that even a China podium, impressive as it was, couldn't fully provide. At 40 years old, at a circuit he has raced for nearly two decades, Hamilton's first Suzuka performance in red is one of this weekend's most eagerly anticipated stories.
