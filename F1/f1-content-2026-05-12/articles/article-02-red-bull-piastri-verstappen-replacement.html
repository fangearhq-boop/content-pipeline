Red Bull Eyes Piastri as Verstappen Replacement — F1's Biggest Driver Market Domino

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

May 12, 2026

The 2026 Formula 1 silly season is heating up early, and the biggest story yet has just broken. Red Bull Racing has shortlisted Oscar Piastri as their preferred replacement for Max Verstappen, should the four-time world champion activate his contract exit clause and leave the team at or before the summer break — according to multiple reports from F1 insiders.

It is, on the surface, an extraordinary scenario. Verstappen is a living legend of the sport, the driver who dominated Formula 1 for years in the most comprehensive fashion in recent memory. But in 2026, with the team struggling under new technical regulations and Verstappen languishing seventh in the championship with 26 points — 74 behind leader Kimi Antonelli — the exit clause in his contract has become a central subplot of the entire season.

Why Piastri Makes Sense for Red Bull

Piastri, 25, has established himself as one of the finest drivers in Formula 1 over recent seasons at McLaren. He is technical, composed, rapid over a single lap, and — critically — proven at the front of the grid in race conditions. He is exactly the profile Red Bull would want to anchor a multi-year rebuild project.

There is also an intriguing personal connection that has not been lost on F1 observers: Piastri's manager is Mark Webber, the Australian who became one of the most beloved figures in Red Bull history during his years racing alongside Vettel. The relationship between Webber and Red Bull's current management may not be a key factor — but in F1, these things matter.

The Obstacles

The significant complication is Piastri's contract. He is locked to McLaren through 2027, and the Woking team has shown no indication of willingness to release him. McLaren's management has made clear that both Piastri and Lando Norris are central to their long-term vision.

Red Bull's team principal Laurent Mekies is understood to be more open to external talent than the team's previous leadership under Christian Horner, who famously prioritised the Red Bull junior programme. But openly pursuing Piastri before Verstappen makes any announcement would risk destabilising relationships across the paddock.

The Clock Is Ticking

Verstappen's exit clause is the key variable. The reported trigger point is a requirement to be inside the top two of the World Championship at the time of the summer break — measured after the Hungarian Grand Prix on July 24-26. With Verstappen currently seventh and 74 points behind Antonelli, meeting that threshold looks increasingly unrealistic given the Red Bull car's competitiveness deficit.

Verstappen has been openly frustrated with the 2026 regulations, repeatedly describing the cars as difficult and unpleasant to drive. Whether those public comments translate into a formal exit request remains to be seen — but the fact that Red Bull has already begun planning for a Piastri-shaped contingency says everything about the seriousness with which they view the possibility.

What's Next

The Canadian Grand Prix in ten days will offer another data point on Red Bull's competitiveness. If the RB22 continues to struggle at a circuit where power and traction are paramount, the gap to the front of the grid will widen further. The Hungarian Grand Prix at the end of July is the critical deadline.

For Piastri, for McLaren, and for the entire F1 paddock — the summer of 2026 promises to be one of the most consequential in the sport's recent history.
