The Verstappen-Mercedes Deal Is Dead — Antonelli Ended It in Four Races

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap

May 12, 2026

For years, one question dominated the Formula 1 transfer market: could Max Verstappen ever end up at Mercedes? The two-time champion and the most successful constructor of the hybrid era — a marriage that would have shaken the sport to its foundations.

It is not going to happen. According to multiple F1 insiders, the heavily rumored Verstappen-to-Mercedes deal is effectively dead in the water — and the reason has nothing to do with Verstappen and everything to do with a 19-year-old Italian who has won four consecutive grands prix from four consecutive pole positions.

Antonelli Changed the Equation

When Lewis Hamilton departed Mercedes for Ferrari ahead of the 2026 season, the question immediately arose about who would fill the void left by arguably the greatest driver in F1 history. Kimi Antonelli was the answer — young, untested in Formula 1, but highly regarded within the Mercedes junior programme.

What nobody fully anticipated was the speed with which Antonelli would make the seat irrelevant to any external conversation. He didn't just fill Hamilton's shoes — he won every race of the season, broke historical records, and leads the championship by 20 points over teammate George Russell. Mercedes doesn't have a driver vacancy. They have two drivers in the top two of the standings, both wearing silver.

The Deal That Was Never Quite Made

Reports had put the rumored Verstappen-Mercedes contract in the range of $80-90 million per year, which would have made it one of the most valuable driver deals in the history of motorsport. The narrative had been building for years — Verstappen's dominance at Red Bull, the possibility of a fresh challenge at Mercedes, the symbolic weight of the move.

But timing is everything in Formula 1. Verstappen's struggles in 2026 — he sits seventh in the standings — have simultaneously made him less of a must-have commodity, and Antonelli's brilliance has made Mercedes less of an open landing spot. The deal that was speculated about so breathlessly never found its moment, and now the moment has passed.

Where Does Verstappen Go?

The more pressing question now is not whether Verstappen goes to Mercedes — it's whether he leaves Formula 1 entirely, remains at Red Bull hoping the car improves, or finds a third option that hasn't yet surfaced publicly. Reports about Red Bull eyeing Piastri as his replacement suggest the team is beginning to plan seriously for a post-Verstappen future.

Verstappen himself has spoken openly about his frustrations with the 2026 regulations, describing the cars in unflattering terms on more than one occasion. Those comments, combined with his exit clause and his current standing in the championship, paint a picture of a driver at a genuine crossroads.

What's Next

The Hungarian Grand Prix on July 24-26 is Verstappen's exit clause deadline. If he cannot position himself inside the top two of the championship by that point — which appears mathematically improbable given the current standings — his options at Red Bull change significantly. The summer break that follows is when the market traditionally moves.

In the meantime, Kimi Antonelli heads to Canada in ten days still undefeated, still leading the championship, and still unwittingly rewriting the narrative of Formula 1's most expensive driver story. The teenager didn't just fill a seat. He closed a door.
