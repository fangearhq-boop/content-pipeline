Antonelli on Course to Break Vettel's Record — No Teenager Has Ever Won the F1 Title

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap

May 12, 2026

No teenager has ever won the Formula 1 World Championship. The record for youngest champion belongs to Sebastian Vettel, who was 23 years and 134 days old when he claimed his first title with Red Bull in 2010. That record has stood for more than 15 years — and right now, a 19-year-old Italian named Kimi Antonelli is on a trajectory to shatter it.

Antonelli leads the 2026 Drivers' Championship with 100 points through four rounds. George Russell, his Mercedes teammate, is 20 points behind in second. Charles Leclerc is third at 59. Every race this year, Antonelli has started from pole position and won. Nobody in F1 history has ever done that through their first four starts in the sport.

Record After Record

The records are stacking up. In China — his debut — Antonelli became the youngest polesitter in the race's history. In Japan, he became the youngest World Championship leader in F1 history when he swept to victory and took control of the standings. In Miami, he converted his fourth consecutive pole into a fourth consecutive victory, making him the first driver ever to win his first three starts from pole position in the sport's history.

Formula 1's own data showed that Antonelli's points-scoring start is the strongest in 10 years — the last time a driver opened a season so dominantly from a Mercedes, it was the period when the sport's previous generation was setting records of their own.

The Youngest Champion Question

Vettel's record has an asterisk worth noting: he was 23, not a teenager, when he claimed that title. Antonelli turned 19 later in the year he entered F1, and his birthday falls in late summer. Whether he eclipses Vettel will depend on when the title is mathematically clinched — but on the current trajectory, it could happen well before the final race of the season.

The Canadian Grand Prix on May 22-24 is next, and it presents the first sprint format weekend of 2026. McLaren is bringing a significant upgrade package. Mercedes will counter with their own. If anyone is going to mount a challenge to Antonelli's dominance, the next month is the most credible opportunity they'll have.

Pressure? What Pressure?

What is perhaps most remarkable about Antonelli's start is the absence of visible pressure. Stepping into the car vacated by Lewis Hamilton — arguably the greatest driver in F1 history — could have crushed a young driver's confidence from the first lap. Instead, Antonelli has driven with a composure that recalls the greats.

He has consistently deflected the championship talk, saying it is too early to think about the title. But the mathematics do not care about humility. At 100 points through four rounds, he is on pace for a record-breaking total if the form holds.

What's Next

Antonelli and Mercedes head to Montreal in ten days for a race that could tell us a great deal about whether this perfection can continue. Canada's Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve is demanding and unpredictable, with heavy braking zones and walls that punish small mistakes. It's a circuit that has ended many dominant seasons prematurely.

But through four rounds in 2026, nothing has stopped Kimi Antonelli yet. The history books are watching.
