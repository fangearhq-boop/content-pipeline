### Long-Form Post Lewis Hamilton has been one of the most data-obsessed drivers in Formula 1 history. His preparation before grands prix is legendary — hours and hours of simulator work, track visualization, engineering briefings, strategy analysis. For the Canadian Grand Prix, he's doing none of that. Hamilton confirmed this week that he won't be using the simulator at all between the Miami GP and his arrival in Montreal. It's a deliberate departure — a gamble on feel, instinct, and a fresh perspective after a Miami weekend that went wrong early. That gap could vanish entirely in Canada if McLaren's upgrade package delivers. Hamilton's decision to scrap the simulator and go back to raw driving instinct isn't just a preparation strategy — it's a statement. Sometimes, you need to trust yourself more than the data.
