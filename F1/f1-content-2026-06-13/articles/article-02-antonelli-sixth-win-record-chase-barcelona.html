Kimi Antonelli Hunts Sixth Straight Win at Barcelona — and a Place in F1 History

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | June 13, 2026

Kimi Antonelli turned 19 less than eight months ago. On Sunday at Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya, he will attempt to win his sixth consecutive Formula 1 race — a feat that would put him in company with only the greatest drivers the sport has ever produced.

The Mercedes driver arrives in Spain with 156 points in the Drivers' Championship, a 66-point lead over Lewis Hamilton, and a momentum that shows absolutely no sign of stopping. Five wins in his first full F1 season — all consecutive, starting with Monaco and working backward through the schedule — have transformed what was expected to be a competitive title fight into something approaching a coronation for a teenager who seems unbothered by the weight of history bearing down on him.

The History Waiting to Be Written

Six consecutive race wins is not something Formula 1 sees every season. The names attached to the sport's longest winning streaks — Michael Schumacher, Sebastian Vettel, Lewis Hamilton — are also the names attached to the sport's greatest careers. Antonelli is building a case to join that list, and he is doing it in his very first season.

What makes the achievement more remarkable is the competition he is operating against. This is not a field of backmarkers. Hamilton — his teammate two seats over at Ferrari — has nine points at stake every race and is fighting for every one of them. The McLarens of Norris and Piastri have genuine pace. And yet Antonelli has found a way to be consistently better than all of them on the day that counts most.

Barcelona as the Ultimate Test

If Antonelli wins at Barcelona, the conversation about his talent needs to change in scope. Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya is not a track that flatters one aerodynamic approach or rewards a single circuit characteristic. It is comprehensive. Brutal to tire compounds. Demanding of driver precision in the high-speed third sector. Unforgiving of setup compromises.

Practice on Friday offered a small piece of doubt. Antonelli was fifth in FP2 — off Norris, Russell, and Piastri. The team indicated it was a planned tire strategy deviation. But Barcelona is where doubts get tested under race conditions, and if Antonelli handles that test the way he has handled every other test this season, the record books will need updating.

He is 66 points ahead of Hamilton, 68 ahead of Russell, and backed by a Mercedes car that has dominated in qualifying every single week. The record, for now, keeps extending itself.

What's Next

Sunday's race at Barcelona-Catalunya starts in the early afternoon local time. A win for Antonelli extends his lead, cements his record, and puts the Drivers' Championship essentially out of reach for all pursuers with less than half the season remaining. A loss — to Russell, Norris, or anyone else — resets the conversation entirely. The suspense starts on Saturday's qualifying grid and resolves on Sunday afternoon.
