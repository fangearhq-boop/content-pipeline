Cadillac Unveils Stars-and-Stripes Livery for British GP: Racing on America's 250th Birthday

By Ryan Calloway | July 2, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Cadillac Formula 1 has zero championship points after eight races in 2026. What they unveiled Thursday is something no points total can give you: the most striking livery on the Formula 1 grid this season.

The American constructor revealed a special stars-and-stripes design for the British Grand Prix. White base. Blue nose studded with white stars. Bold red-and-white angled stripes running across the bodywork. Both Valtteri Bottas and Sergio Perez will carry the design for this round only, and the timing of the reveal is anything but accidental.

America's 250th Birthday at F1's Birthplace

Saturday at Silverstone — the day of the Sprint race and qualifying for the main British Grand Prix — falls on July 4, 2026. America's 250th birthday. The coincidence was not lost on Cadillac's communications team, who released an official statement titled: "Cadillac Formula 1 Team Marks America's 250 at F1's Original Venue."

That last phrase carries its own significance. Silverstone is not just any Formula 1 circuit. It is the venue where the Formula 1 World Championship held its very first race in 1950. The sport was born here. An American team flying the flag on Independence Day at the birthplace of Formula 1 is a story that required no embellishment — it wrote itself.

The Season Behind the Spectacle

The context around the livery is sobering by results alone. Cadillac entered 2026 as the sport's newest constructor, carrying enormous expectations from the United States motorsport community. Eight races in, they have scored zero points. The Austrian Grand Prix was their low point: both cars retired before lap 5, Bottas on lap 2 and Perez on laps 4 to 5, both victims of brake fires that ended what had been described internally as the team's biggest upgrade package to date.

The team has been candid about their expectations, publicly stating they are "not aiming for points in 2026." This is framed as a development year — building the infrastructure, the tooling, the operational systems, and the institutional knowledge that a new constructor needs to eventually compete. Cadillac's target is not the 2026 championship. It is the 2028 or 2029 championship.

Both drivers bring relevant experience to Silverstone specifically. Bottas has two career pole positions and five podiums at this circuit. Perez set the fastest lap at Silverstone in 2021 and finished second in 2022. The knowledge is there. The car is not yet at the level to use it.

Why This Still Matters

Cadillac's patriotic livery is easy to dismiss as a marketing exercise. It is also something more: a statement of presence. A reminder that an American team is here, competing on the world stage, wearing their colors with pride even while the scoreboard stays blank.

Formula 1's popularity in the United States has grown enormously in recent years. A successful American team — eventually — would accelerate that growth further. The path is long and the 2026 season has been painful. But the stars-and-stripes car at Silverstone on America's 250th birthday is a visual that will be remembered beyond whatever the stopwatch says this weekend.

What's Next

On-track action begins Friday, July 3, with FP1 at 12:30 BST and Sprint Qualifying at 16:30 BST. The Sprint runs Saturday at noon, qualifying at 16:00. The British Grand Prix takes the green flag Sunday at 15:00 BST. Cadillac will be racing in red, white, and blue every lap of the way.
