Hamilton's Ferrari Homecoming: Racing in Red at Silverstone for the First Time

By Marcus Cole | July 2, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

There are weekends in Formula 1 that carry weight beyond the points on offer. Lewis Hamilton arriving at Silverstone in a Ferrari for the first time is one of them.

British Grand Prix race week has begun, and the backdrop is unlike anything Silverstone has seen before. Hamilton — nine-time winner at this circuit, seven-time pole sitter, the man who holds every significant record the track can offer — climbs into his Ferrari for the first time on home soil. Ferrari published a piece on their official website this week titled simply "Home turf for Lewis." It did not need elaboration.

The Records He Carries

No driver in history has won more British Grand Prix races than Lewis Hamilton. His nine victories at Silverstone span more than a decade and represent some of the most dominant performances in the history of the sport at this venue. His seven pole positions are also a circuit record.

He arrives with 125 points in the championship — third overall, 46 behind leader Kimi Antonelli. A win here, in the Sprint format where 33 points are available across the weekend, would dramatically alter the shape of the title fight. More immediately, it would answer the question that every Ferrari fan has been asking since February: can this car win at a circuit that punishes straight-line speed deficits?

Ferrari's Silverstone Upgrade Push

Ferrari has not come to Silverstone unprepared. The team has brought an updated low-drag "Macarena" rear wing and a revised low-drag diffuser — upgrades specifically targeting the circuit's long Hangar Straight, where Ferrari's known straight-line speed weakness under the 2026 regulations has been most exposed.

The reaction from rivals has been notable. Mercedes team principal Toto Wolff acknowledged Ferrari's development pace with candor: "We're a little surprised that Ferrari can throw such massive upgrades at the car the way they do." That is as close to a compliment from a competitor as this sport allows.

Hamilton has his own challenge with the track's unique demands in the 2026 era. He warned: "There's lots of straights in Silverstone, lots of straights and lots of deployment, and not many places to recover the power." The removal of the MGU-H under the new regulations means energy recovery is purely kinetic — and Silverstone's fast sweeping corners between Luffield and Stowe offer almost no braking zones to recharge. The Ferrari upgrades address drag. Managing the battery will test everyone.

The Emotional Dimension

Hamilton has won at Silverstone in a Mercedes. He has won in a McLaren. He has taken pole in silver, been cheered in silver, built his legend in silver.

This weekend he shows up in red. The British crowd's response to that shift — to Hamilton in Ferrari colors at their national race — will be fascinating. He is still their driver. He is still the most successful British Formula 1 driver in history. But the iconography is different, and those who follow F1 even casually will feel the strangeness of seeing him under Ferrari's banner at Silverstone.

Whether the car can match the emotion of the moment is the defining question of the British Grand Prix weekend.

What's Next

On-track action begins Friday, July 3, with FP1 at 12:30 BST followed by Sprint Qualifying at 16:30 BST. The Sprint race on Saturday at noon will set the stage for qualifying at 16:00 before Sunday's 52-lap British Grand Prix at 15:00 BST.
