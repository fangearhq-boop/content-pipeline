Ferrari's Mini Pre-Season: Monza Test, ADUO Power Unit Update, and a Declaration of Intent for Miami

By Marcus Cole April 2, 2026

No Rest for the Red

While Formula 1 sits in its 5-week break between the Japanese Grand Prix and Miami, Ferrari is treating April as a compressed pre-season. Team principal Frédéric Vasseur has been unambiguous about the team's intentions: "Miami is a new championship."

The words carry more weight when backed by action. Ferrari has scheduled a filming day at Monza on April 22 — using the full 200 kilometers permitted under the FIA's demonstration tyre regulations — and has received FIA approval for an ADUO power unit update that will make its race debut at the Miami Grand Prix on May 4.

Monza and the Method

The choice of Monza for the filming day was deliberate. Ferrari's engineers identified the circuit's energy management demands as the most directly applicable preparation for Miami — both venues punish power unit inefficiency and reward precision in energy deployment. Running 200 kilometers of developmental laps at Monza, under controlled conditions, gives Ferrari data that wind tunnels and simulators cannot fully replicate.

Lewis Hamilton, who played a "heavy role" in developing the SF-26 through extensive 2025 simulator work, has said he expects more to come from the car as the season progresses. The SF-26's novel "Macarena" rear wing — which rotates its upper flap completely upside down under active aerodynamics — drew considerable attention at the Australian GP and hints at the unconventional engineering thinking that Ferrari has applied to the 2026 technical challenge.

The Power Gap and the Fix

Ferrari's acknowledged weakness in 2026 is straightforward: an estimated 0.5-second-per-lap deficit versus the Mercedes power unit in race conditions. When you are racing against the fastest PU on the grid across 57 laps, that gap accumulates. It is the primary reason Hamilton and Charles Leclerc — both capable of winning at any circuit on driver ability alone — are sitting third and fourth in the championship rather than first and second.

The ADUO power unit update is Ferrari's answer. Having already secured FIA approval, the updated unit will be installed for Miami and is specifically designed to address the energy harvesting and deployment characteristics where Mercedes currently holds the advantage. Whether it closes the half-second gap entirely or merely narrows it is what the paddock will be watching when qualifying begins in Miami on May 3.

What's Next

The Monza filming day on April 22 will be Ferrari's final major preparation step before Miami. If the ADUO update delivers even a portion of its projected performance gain, Leclerc and Hamilton become legitimate Miami race winners — not just podium contenders. Vasseur's declaration that Miami represents "a new championship" is either bold confidence or calculated pressure on a team that has learned from past seasons that development windows don't last forever. April 22 is the first answer. May 4 is the second.
