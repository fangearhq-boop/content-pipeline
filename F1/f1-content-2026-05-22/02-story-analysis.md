## Story Angles

### STORY 1: Canada Sprint Weekend Begins
**Primary angle:** Montreal debuts the sprint format for the first time — a historic first for one of F1's most iconic circuits.
**Secondary angle:** Tonight's Sprint Qualifying is the first competitive data point from Canada — it will tell us whether the upgrade packages from Mercedes, McLaren, and Red Bull have changed the order.
**Article hook:** "60 years of Canadian GP history. The sprint format is new. So is everything else."

### STORY 2: Hamilton's Canadian GP Mission
**Primary angle:** The greatest Canadian GP driver in history — tied for 7 wins with Schumacher — walks into his best circuit driving a Ferrari still looking for its first win with him.
**Secondary angle:** The stakes are personal as much as competitive. Canada is where Hamilton's F1 journey began (first win, 2007). It's where he has been most himself.
**Article hook:** "Seven wins. One more to write alone in the record books. One team that hasn't won with him yet."

### STORY 3: Russell vs Antonelli
**Primary angle:** The internal Mercedes tension is the dominant championship narrative. If Russell wins Canada and Antonelli doesn't podium, the 20-point gap swings entirely.
**Secondary angle:** Russell's measured "no panic" public stance versus the reality that a third consecutive Antonelli win would make the championship a one-horse race.

### STORY 4: Mercedes Upgrade Package
**Primary angle:** Every top team brought upgrades, but Mercedes' is the biggest — 0.3 sec/lap from a revised aero floor. If it works, they were already winning. If it backfires, rivals see an opening.
**Secondary angle:** The deliberate upgrade timing (held back from Miami to concentrate the package) shows engineering confidence — and adds pressure.

### STORY 5: Verstappen's Reset
**Primary angle:** The four-time champion is seventh with 26 points and no wins. He went to the Nurburgring expecting a win and suffered a mechanical failure. Canada is a reset — but it's also a circuit that has historically challenged Red Bull.
**Secondary angle:** Red Bull's Miami improvement offers some hope. The new Red Bull upgrade package in Canada could define whether the team becomes a title factor again.
