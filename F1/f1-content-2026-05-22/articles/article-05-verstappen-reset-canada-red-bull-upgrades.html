Verstappen's Canadian Reset: Can Red Bull's Upgrades Reignite the Four-Time Champion's 2026 Season?

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | May 22, 2026

Max Verstappen is seventh in the Formula 1 World Championship with 26 points after four races. He has not won. He has not been on the podium. The car that carried him to four consecutive world titles has not found the performance that made it dominant under the previous regulations.

Then he flew to the Nurburgring during the Miami break to drive endurance racing, led by more than 30 seconds with hours remaining — and watched a driveshaft failure end his race before the finish.

Adversity has followed Max Verstappen in 2026 across two different categories of motorsport.

The State of Red Bull

The Milton Keynes team began 2026 with a car that struggled to adapt to the new aerodynamic regulations. The RB22's floor concept — so dominant in the previous era — has required significant revision as engineers work to understand the different downforce characteristics of the current ruleset.

Miami offered encouragement. Red Bull team principal Laurent Mekies described the Miami update as "an important step forward" — the first genuine confidence-building result from a development program that has taken longer than the team would have liked to find direction. A new package arrives at Circuit Gilles Villeneuve with the expectation that the Montreal weekend will confirm the improvement is real and not circuit-specific.

Verstappen's relationship with his new race engineer is developing across a difficult baseline. He has spoken publicly about the process of building the communication rhythm that allowed him to extract maximum performance in his championship years. That process takes time — and 2026 has been that time, compressed into a frustrating championship position.

The Montreal Challenge

Circuit Gilles Villeneuve has not historically been a Red Bull stronghold. The long straights and stop-go nature of the circuit have traditionally suited cars with strong straight-line speed, and Red Bull's aero-heavy designs have sometimes paid a drag penalty at Montreal that costs them more than elsewhere.

The 2026 car's technical direction — and the latest upgrade — will face a specific test at this circuit. Verstappen's qualifying performance tonight in Sprint Qualifying will be an early indicator of whether Red Bull has closed the gap to the front-runners or whether Montreal presents the same challenges the RB22 has faced elsewhere.

For Verstappen himself, the focus is simple: stop the narrative of things going wrong and start the narrative of things going right. One clean weekend — a competitive qualifying session, a strong sprint result, a podium on Sunday — would change the conversation entirely.

What's Next

Sprint Qualifying tonight at 9:30 PM ET will be Verstappen's first competitive read at this circuit. Verstappen has 61 Grand Prix wins and four world titles; he is not defined by the first four rounds of a new regulation era. But Montreal needs to be the start of something different.
