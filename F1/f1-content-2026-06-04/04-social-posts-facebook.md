#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM ET

For five rounds, the 2026 Formula 1 cars have caused debate. Drivers have complained about power delivery. Teams have struggled to understand their new machinery. The championship has been more volatile than anyone expected.

Monaco might change all of that.

Multiple drivers — including championship leader Kimi Antonelli and local hero Charles Leclerc — said the same thing this week: the 2026 cars will "flourish" at Monaco.

The reasons are technical. The new regulations produced slightly narrower cars than in previous years, giving more room between the barriers and the cars on the tightest circuit in F1. The Circuit de Monaco's combination of extremely slow corners and very short straights means the battery regeneration issue that has plagued drivers at other circuits simply does not apply. Active aero can be deployed throughout the lap without concern.

The result, drivers believe, is a package that should produce genuine racing at a circuit that has sometimes felt more like a parade in recent years. Leclerc predicted drivers will be able to "push without concern" — a phrase that, at Monaco, is almost revolutionary.
