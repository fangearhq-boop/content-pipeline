Hamilton Hasn't Won Monaco Since 2019. Ferrari's Car Is Perfect for It. This Weekend Could Be Everything.

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | June 4, 2026

Lewis Hamilton joined Ferrari in 2026 to win a championship. That goal remains in progress. But Monaco — the race that has defined his legacy at least three times already — arrives this weekend with a unique alignment of factors that could make Sunday the most significant day of his first season in red.

Hamilton has not won the Monaco Grand Prix since 2019. He took victory on these streets with Mercedes in 2008, 2016, and 2019. Three wins, three different decades, all for the same team. This weekend, for the first time, he arrives at Monte Carlo as a Ferrari driver — and the circumstances have rarely been more favorable.

Why Ferrari Is Built for Monaco

The SF-26 is the fastest car on the grid through slow corners. Ferrari's mechanical grip, suspension setup philosophy, and the power delivery of their 2026 power unit combine to produce a package that excels precisely where Monaco demands it most — in the narrow streets, the hairpins, the chicane, and the swimming pool complex where speeds rarely exceed 160 km/h.

At circuits like Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, and Miami, where long straights reward high-speed power, Mercedes has held an advantage. Their active aero system — which can be deployed on straights meeting a minimum three-second threshold — has produced straightaway speed that Ferrari's SF-26 struggles to match.

Monaco changes that calculation entirely. No section of the Circuit de Monaco meets the three-second threshold required for active aero deployment. The tool Mercedes has been using to separate themselves at high-speed circuits is neutralized. Ferrari's low-speed excellence becomes the primary differentiator.

Hamilton said it himself this week: "Power is not king there." He knows this track as well as anyone on the grid.

The Canada Momentum

Hamilton finished second in Canada — 10.768 seconds behind race winner Kimi Antonelli after Antonelli's team mate George Russell retired from the lead with a power unit failure. It was Hamilton's best result of the 2026 season and, by his own account, the day he has felt most comfortable and competitive in the Ferrari.

He described Canada as "the happiest day at Ferrari." That confidence now carries into Monaco, a circuit where psychological comfort and familiarity with the barriers matter as much as technical setup.

Hamilton knows these walls. He has kissed them, sometimes avoided them by millimetres, and occasionally used them. His three previous Monaco victories required exactly the combination of pace, precision, and composure that defines the best drivers on this track. He is 41 years old this season and has not shown any sign of diminished capability. The physical awareness required to navigate Monaco — the spatial instinct, the touch, the patience — does not depreciate with age.

The Championship Context

Hamilton is fourth in the Drivers' Championship with 72 points, trailing Antonelli by 59 points. He is not in a championship lead battle at this moment in the season. But Monaco has a way of changing the conversation entirely.

A Monaco win by Hamilton would be his first for Ferrari, ending a 40-year-plus drought for the team at the Principality. It would signal that Hamilton has fully settled into the SF-26 and that Ferrari's challenge for the Constructors' Championship — currently third with 147 combined points — is real.

It would also be personally historic. A 4th Monaco win, a first with Ferrari, at age 41. If the conditions for a legendary result ever existed, this weekend provides them.

What's Next

Qualifying on Saturday at 9:00 AM ET on Apple TV+ will tell the most important story. Hamilton's qualifying record at Monaco is exceptional — his three wins all came from pole or very near the front of the grid. Overtaking at Monaco is nearly impossible, which means Saturday's grid position defines Sunday's result.

If Hamilton qualifies on the front row, the conversation begins in earnest. If he qualifies in the top three alongside Leclerc, Ferrari has the ability to strategize collaboratively — managing tires, using team orders, and controlling the race from the front.

Race Sunday at 9:00 AM ET. Hamilton. Ferrari. Monaco. After 2019, the wait has been long enough.
