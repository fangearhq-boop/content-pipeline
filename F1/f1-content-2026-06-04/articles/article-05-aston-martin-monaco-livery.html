Aston Martin Unveils Special Monaco Livery — The Principality Brings Out the Best in F1

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | June 4, 2026

There are twenty-two races on the Formula 1 calendar. For most of them, teams arrive with the same livery they ran at the previous race. Monaco is the exception. It has always been the exception.

Aston Martin unveiled their special livery for the 2026 Monaco Grand Prix this week, and it is exactly what the race demands: distinctive, elegant, and worthy of the most glamorous setting in motorsport. The British Racing Green that has defined Aston Martin's identity since the brand's earliest racing days is the foundation, complemented by bespoke design elements that acknowledge where the team is racing this weekend.

Why Monaco Gets Special Treatment

The Monaco Grand Prix is not a race. It is an event. The Circuit de Monaco runs through streets that most people in the world will never drive, past a harbour filled with superyachts, through a tunnel that leads from the sunshine into darkness and back out again, around Casino Square where the road is barely wider than two cars side by side.

The setting demands a response. Teams that embrace Monaco's identity — its combination of luxury, history, and danger — become part of the spectacle in a way that carries meaning beyond the race itself. Special liveries at Monaco are not a gimmick. They are an acknowledgment that this race is different from every other race on the calendar, and that the visual language of a Formula 1 car should reflect that difference.

Aston Martin, as a brand, understands this instinctively. British Racing Green is one of the most recognizable colors in motorsport. The Aston Martin AMR26, in its standard livery, is one of the most visually striking cars on the grid. The Monaco edition takes that foundation and adds to it.

The Heritage Behind the Green

British Racing Green has a history in Monaco that predates the current Aston Martin Formula 1 team by decades. British constructors carrying the color have raced these streets since the early days of Formula 1 as a championship. There is something fitting about seeing the shade run through Monaco — a nod to a tradition that connects the current Aston Martin operation to a lineage much longer than its recent history.

The team has been building its identity methodically since entering the championship. Under Fernando Alonso's tenure through 2025, Aston Martin's public profile grew considerably. The transition to post-Alonso racing has continued that development. The Monaco livery represents a brand that is increasingly comfortable asserting its personality on the world's most watched stage.

What the Team Needs on Track

The beauty of the car is one thing. How it performs is another. Lance Stroll drives for Aston Martin in 2026, and the team has had mixed results through the opening five rounds as they adapt to the new regulations.

Monaco's demands — mechanical grip over raw power, aerodynamic precision through slow corners, the ability to find lap time in sections where the barriers are close enough to touch — suit a car that has been developed with downforce sensitivity in mind. The AMR26's setup philosophy, which has shown competitiveness at circuits with slow and medium-speed corners, could give Aston Martin a better showing at Monaco than their standing in the constructors suggests.

The team arrived at Monaco this week with the special livery unwrapped and the car prepared. Now the work happens on track, starting Friday morning when the barriers close and the circuit comes alive.

What's Next

Free Practice 1 opens on Friday at 7:30 AM ET on Apple TV+, giving Aston Martin and every other team the first live data from the Monaco streets in 2026. The special livery will be visible from the first lap of FP1 — and if the AMR26 shows competitive pace through Monaco's tight sections, it will be one of the more complete packages the Principality will see this weekend.

Qualifying on Saturday at 9:00 AM ET determines where Stroll starts Sunday's race. At Monaco, front-row starting positions matter more than anywhere. The race follows Sunday at 9:00 AM ET.

The car looks stunning. Whether the racing matches the visuals, the weekend will answer. For now, as Monaco race week begins, Aston Martin's green is exactly where it belongs.
