Race Week Is Here: Monaco GP FP1 Tomorrow — 5 Things to Watch This Weekend

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | June 4, 2026

Monaco race week is here. Free Practice 1 begins Friday at 7:30 AM ET on Apple TV+, and by Sunday at 9:00 AM ET, one driver will have won the most iconic race in motorsport.

The 2026 edition of the Monaco Grand Prix arrives with more storylines than usual — a dominant championship leader facing his toughest test, a seven-time world champion hunting his first Ferrari win, and a four-time champion whose entire 2026 future could hinge on the next three days in Monte Carlo.

Here are the five things that matter most heading into this weekend at the Circuit de Monaco.

1. Can Antonelli Extend His Historic Streak?

Kimi Antonelli enters Monaco as championship leader with 131 points and four consecutive Grand Prix victories — a record no driver has ever achieved from their first four career wins. China, Japan, Miami, and Canada have all gone to the 19-year-old Mercedes driver who replaced Lewis Hamilton at the Silver Arrows.

Monaco is the hardest test of the season so far. The circuit does not favor Mercedes, the active aero ban removes the team's advantage, and Ferrari is heavily favored here. Antonelli acknowledged as much this week, saying Ferrari is "the team to beat" on the streets of the Principality.

If he wins here, the championship conversation changes entirely. If Ferrari ends the streak, the season becomes genuinely competitive again.

2. Ferrari's Big Opportunity

Charles Leclerc and Lewis Hamilton arrive in Monaco as the favorites to win. The SF-26 is the most competitive car at low-speed circuits, and Monaco — 78 laps of a 3.337-kilometer street circuit with barely a meaningful straight — plays directly to Ferrari's strengths.

Leclerc won this race in 2024. He grew up in Fontvieille, just outside Monte Carlo, and considers Monaco his home race in every meaningful sense. Hamilton has won here three times and has not won since 2019. The odds reflect it: Leclerc at 2/1, Hamilton at 4/1, Antonelli at 4/1.

The active aero ban for Monaco — no section of the circuit meets the three-second threshold for deployment — removes one of the key tools that has separated Mercedes from the field this year. Ferrari's mechanical grip and low-speed excellence becomes the deciding factor.

3. Verstappen's Lifeline

Max Verstappen is 7th in the championship, 88 points behind Antonelli, with his Red Bull exit clause deadline approaching on July 27. His Canada result — third place, the first podium for Red Bull Racing Powertrains in 2026 — offered the first real sign that the package might be turning a corner.

He won Monaco in 2021 and 2023. The circuit has historically suited his precise, aggressive driving style. A Monaco win would not close the gap to Antonelli on its own, but it would change the narrative around Red Bull's season and restart Verstappen's championship hopes when they are needed most.

4. How the 2026 Cars Behave Here

The 2026 regulations have caused more discussion than anyone expected through five rounds. Drivers across the grid have noted that the new cars are slightly narrower than their 2025 predecessors, and Monaco's tight confines may actually benefit from that reduction in width. Multiple drivers — including Antonelli and Leclerc — said the cars should "flourish" in Monaco because battery regeneration is not a concern on the short, slow circuit.

This could produce better racing than Monaco has seen in years. FP1 and FP2 Friday will be the first evidence.

5. Russell's Comeback

George Russell was leading the Canadian Grand Prix on Lap 30 when his Mercedes suffered a catastrophic power unit failure. He never won Monaco. He now trails Antonelli by 43 points in a championship that looked competitive as recently as three weeks ago.

Monaco is a circuit where momentum matters and where drivers who need to prove something often do. Russell has never won on these streets. This weekend, he needs to at least challenge at the front to demonstrate that his championship campaign is not finished.

What's Next

FP1 kicks off Friday at 7:30 AM ET, with FP2 following at 11:00 AM ET. Saturday brings FP3 at 6:30 AM ET and qualifying at 9:00 AM ET. The 2026 Monaco Grand Prix races Sunday at 9:00 AM ET, all on Apple TV+.

Weather will not be a factor: dry and hot conditions are forecast throughout the weekend, with temperatures around 28°C. No rain is expected for qualifying or the race. Five big storylines, three days, one of the greatest circuits in sports. Monaco race week starts now.
