Antonelli's Warning: The Championship Leader Expects Ferrari to Close in Miami

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | April 14, 2026

Kimi Antonelli leads the 2026 World Championship with 72 points — and he's already telling anyone who'll listen that the gap to Ferrari is about to shrink.

The Leader's Admission

There is a specific kind of confidence that separates exceptional drivers from merely great ones: the willingness to acknowledge a threat before it arrives. Kimi Antonelli demonstrated that quality this week when he told Autosport that he expects Ferrari to close on Mercedes at the Miami Grand Prix — specifically because of the ADUO regulation tweaks being discussed in the FIA's April 20 vote.

ADUO — the Active DRS Underbody Optimization framework built into the 2026 regulations — governs how cars actively manage aerodynamic downforce based on energy availability. Mercedes has maximized the system across the opening rounds, contributing to their 45-point Constructors' Championship lead over Ferrari. If the FIA refines the ADUO parameters, it could narrow the efficiency gap that Mercedes currently enjoys.

Antonelli's position is not alarmist. He's leading by nine points over teammate George Russell and 23 over Ferrari's Charles Leclerc. But the champion's mindset — treating Miami as a genuine competitive reset rather than a comfortable title defense — is exactly what's required to keep the gap from closing on the track.

The Numbers So Far

After three races, the 2026 World Championship standings tell a story of Mercedes dominance that is real but not unassailable. Antonelli at 72 points with two race wins. Russell at 63 with one. Leclerc at 49, Hamilton at 41. In the Constructors' standings, Mercedes leads Ferrari 135 to 90.

Antonelli won the season opener and followed that with a second victory at the Australian Grand Prix, becoming the youngest driver to win back-to-back Formula 1 races. His consistency has been remarkable for a driver in his second full season. But the margin to Russell — only nine points over three races — tells a different story internally: the silver cars are capable of producing two genuine title contenders, and the team dynamic will only intensify as the season develops.

The Start Problem

Antonelli also identified his race starts as an area of underperformance. "Miami is going to be, for me, also a restart," he said, making clear that his preparation during the April break includes work on an element of his driving that hasn't matched his in-race excellence.

The timing matters. Miami is a sprint weekend — the first sprint format event of the 2026 season. That means sprint qualifying, a sprint race, standard qualifying, and the full race across three days. Poor starts in a sprint format can cost points twice rather than once. If Antonelli loses positions at the start of the sprint race and then the main race, the championship gap could tighten significantly in a single weekend even without any performance changes from Ferrari or Mercedes.

What's Next

The ADUO regulatory decision comes April 20, and Antonelli will be watching the outcome closely. If the refinements pass and Ferrari does close the performance gap at Miami, the 2026 championship enters an entirely new phase. The opener and Australia were Mercedes' races to control. Miami — under new rules, on a circuit that rewards energy management, in a sprint format — could be the first real indicator of what the rest of this season actually looks like.
