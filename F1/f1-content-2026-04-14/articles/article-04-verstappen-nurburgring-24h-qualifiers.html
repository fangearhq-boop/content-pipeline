Verstappen Returns to the Ring: Redemption Runs Begin Saturday at the 24H Qualifiers

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | April 14, 2026

Four days from Tuesday, Max Verstappen begins the 24 Hours of Nürburgring qualifying weekend — two four-hour races on the Nordschleife that double as redemption for a win that was stripped from him in the NLS2.

What Happened in NLS2

The last time Verstappen was at the Nürburgring Nordschleife in a racing context, he crossed the line first in the NLS2 round and celebrated a win. The celebration didn't last. Post-race scrutineering revealed that his Verstappen Racing Mercedes GT3 had used seven sets of tyres during the race — one more than the regulation limit of six. The victory was stripped and handed to the second-placed BMW of Harper and Pepper.

It was a technical breach, not a driving error, but that distinction doesn't soften the sting of having a result taken away. Verstappen acknowledged the situation publicly and confirmed his intention to return. He skipped NLS3 due to a scheduling conflict at Paul Ricard, which only extended the wait.

The 24 Hours of Nürburgring Qualifiers on April 18-19 mark his return to the Ring — and the context of his F1 season makes the timing more pointed than usual. While Formula 1 factories are running simulations and wind tunnel tests during the April break, Verstappen is getting actual racing kilometers under actual competitive pressure.

The Format and His Setup

The qualifying weekend consists of two four-hour races. On Saturday April 18, a qualifying session from 8:30 to 10:00 determines the starting grid for the first race, which begins at 17:30 local time. Sunday's second four-hour race follows. The results across both races contribute to the overall qualifier standings for the 24 Hours of Nürburgring main event.

Verstappen will again share the car with Austrian driver Lucas Auer. The pair competed together in NLS2 and have developed an understanding of how to manage the Verstappen Racing Mercedes GT3 across the Nordschleife's demanding 25-kilometer circuit — including the 73 corners, significant elevation changes, and the ever-present threat of changeable weather that makes every Nordschleife race a genuine test of preparation and judgment.

The Bigger Picture

Verstappen's participation in these races was made possible, coincidentally, by the cancellation of the Bahrain and Saudi Arabian Grands Prix due to the ongoing Iran conflict situation. The absence of those F1 rounds gave him an open racing window that most Formula 1 drivers simply don't have during an active season.

He described racing at the Nürburgring as bringing "a big smile" — a phrase worth noting given the frustrations of the 2026 F1 season. At the Ring, he's not defending a championship or managing team politics or waiting for an upgrade package. He's racing. The NLS is his own program, his own car, his own rules. That mental reset may have more value than the lap times suggest.

What's Next

The April 18-19 qualifier weekend results will clarify whether Verstappen and Auer have found the consistency needed to be competitive in the 24 Hours main event. A clean result — no tyre regulation breaches, competitive lap times — would restore confidence heading into Miami the following week. After Miami, the 24 Hours of Nürburgring itself is scheduled for May. Verstappen's plan, carefully constructed around the F1 calendar gap, involves using these qualifier races as genuine preparation. Saturday's first race is the start of that journey.
