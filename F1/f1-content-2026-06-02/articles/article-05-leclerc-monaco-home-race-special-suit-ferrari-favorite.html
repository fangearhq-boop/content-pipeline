Leclerc's Monaco Obsession: Special Suit, Home Streets, and a Ferrari That Was Built for This

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | June 2, 2026

Charles Leclerc unveiled a special race suit this week for the Monaco Grand Prix. It is almost entirely white with red accents — the colors of the Monegasque flag. For any other race on the calendar, a special uniform would be a commercial gesture. Here, it means something different.

Monaco is Leclerc's home. He grew up in the principality. He has ridden buses on these streets. Every corner of the 3.337-kilometer circuit holds a memory — childhood races, Formula 3 sessions, the first time he drove through the Tunnel in a Formula 1 car in competition. He said it plainly this week: "Those are the streets that I've been taking my bus since I was a kid. On the track, whichever corner I am in, I've got a story."

The Long Wait, and the Win That Ended It

Leclerc waited longer than anyone should have to win his own Grand Prix. He took pole at Monaco three times and failed to convert any of them into a victory. In 2022, a strategy failure. In 2023, mechanical failure. The heartbreaks accumulated in the principality's streets, in front of his family and friends.

He finally won Monaco in 2024, a moment that carried more emotional weight than most championships. It was a maiden home race victory after years of near-misses on the most personal circuit he knows. He returns as the defending champion of this event.

Canada's Shadow, Monaco's Promise

The Canadian Grand Prix was painful for Leclerc. He described it as "the worst weekend of my career" — a weekend in which his teammate Lewis Hamilton took a podium and the spotlight while Leclerc struggled to find pace. The results in Montreal are irrelevant to what happens this week, but the hunger to respond is real.

He arrives at Monaco with the paddock's consensus behind him. Ferrari is the pre-race favorite. The FIA's active aerodynamics ban removes the one area where Ferrari's SF-26 is at a disadvantage against Mercedes. The SF-26 is built for slow-speed mechanical grip — which is exactly what Monaco demands from the first meter to the last.

What's Next

Charles Leclerc will put on his white and red suit on Sunday and drive streets he has known his entire life. He is the defending Monaco champion, three-time Monaco pole sitter, and in the fastest chassis for this circuit. The only question is whether Ferrari can keep the two-car battle clean, and whether Mercedes finds something unexpected in qualifying. If this weekend goes to form, Leclerc and Hamilton will both be at the front — and the result will depend on who blinks first.
