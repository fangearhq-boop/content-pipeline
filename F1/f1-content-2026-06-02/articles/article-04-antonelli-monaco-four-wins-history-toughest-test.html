Antonelli's Four-Win History and Monaco's Test: Can the Streak Survive Its Hardest Circuit?

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | June 2, 2026

Kimi Antonelli has done something that no Formula 1 driver before him has managed. He has won the first four races of a season consecutively — a record that required both exceptional speed and the kind of mental durability most drivers spend a full career searching for.

The question Monaco asks of Antonelli is different from every circuit that preceded it.

The History Made So Far

Antonelli's four consecutive wins to open 2026 — Australia, Japan, Miami, Canada — represent the strongest start to an F1 season in a decade by points accumulated. He entered Monaco with 149 championship points and a 43-point lead over his Mercedes teammate George Russell, who retired from the Canadian GP lead with a power unit failure.

Antonelli also became the youngest-ever championship leader in F1 history earlier in the season, a record he set after his second win of the year. He is 19 years old.

An Honest Admission About Monaco

What distinguishes Antonelli from many champions-in-waiting is his willingness to acknowledge competitive reality. Before practice had begun, he told the media that Ferrari was the "team to beat" at Monaco — an unusual concession from a championship leader, and one that reflects genuine tactical awareness.

He is right. The FIA's active aerodynamics ban removes Straight Mode from the equation, stripping away the W17's biggest advantage on a circuit that has no qualifying straights. Ferrari's chassis is built for Monaco's slow-speed demands. Antonelli knows his car may not be the fastest this weekend.

He also has history here: Antonelli struggled at Monaco during his 2025 rookie season. The circuit's unforgiving barriers, its premium on precision over multiple qualifying laps, and the near-impossibility of overtaking mean that pace alone does not guarantee a result. One brush with the wall in the Swimming Pool section ends the weekend.

What's Next

If Antonelli wins Monaco while Ferrari is the acknowledged favorite, it would be one of the most impressive results of his young career — delivering on a circuit where the machinery is against him. If Ferrari wins — whether that is Leclerc or Hamilton — the streak ends but the championship lead may not change dramatically. What matters to Antonelli is points, and five points in Monaco is still five points more than Russell.
