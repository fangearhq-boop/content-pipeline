Monaco GP 2026 Race Week Preview: Active Aero Ban Tilts Competitive Order Toward Ferrari

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | June 2, 2026

Monaco race week is here — and for the first time in 2026, the competitive order looks genuinely different from what we have seen at every other circuit.

The Formula 1 field arrives in the Principality this week for a Grand Prix unlike any other on the 2026 calendar. The FIA has confirmed that active aerodynamics — the "Straight Mode" system that has been the backbone of Mercedes' dominant season — will not be used at any point during the Monaco Grand Prix. The consequence is a reshuffled field before a wheel has turned in practice.

Why Active Aero Is Banned at Monaco

The FIA's regulations require each active aerodynamics activation zone to remain active for a minimum of three seconds. The safety-driven rationale: at the speeds generated when wings flatten and drag is reduced, drivers need sustained time in the zone before braking and cornering. At Monaco, no stretch of circuit meets that threshold. The Tunnel exit — where run-off is minimal and walls are close — was specifically cited as a safety concern with high-speed active aero operation.

The result: every team arrives at Monte Carlo with their wings fixed in place. The power unit advantages that have defined the 2026 season so far become largely irrelevant. What matters here is chassis dynamics, mechanical grip, and low-speed aerodynamic downforce.

Who Benefits and Who Suffers

Mercedes has won all five races of the 2026 season. Their dominance is built on a combination of the new regulations' power unit performance and the active aero system extracting maximum efficiency on straights. Remove the straights and the active aero, and much of that advantage disappears.

Ferrari enters as the favorite. The SF-26 is widely regarded as the strongest chassis in slow-speed and medium-speed sectors — precisely the conditions Monaco demands through the hairpin, the Casino, and the Swimming Pool complex. Even championship leader Kimi Antonelli acknowledged the obvious this week, declaring Ferrari the "team to beat" at Monaco before practice had begun.

McLaren also sees an opportunity. They endured a disastrous Canadian Grand Prix, scoring zero points through a combination of strategic errors, driver mistakes, and mechanical unreliability. Monaco's characteristics — tight, low-speed, short-wheelbase-friendly — should help them bounce back.

Schedule and How to Watch

Practice 1 begins Friday, June 5 at 13:30 local time (7:30 AM ET), with Practice 2 at 17:00 local (11:00 AM ET). Qualifying is Saturday, June 6 at 15:00 local (9:00 AM ET). The race takes place Sunday, June 7 at the local start time, with a 2:00 PM ET broadcast on Apple TV+ in the United States.

What's Next

Practice on Friday will tell us quickly whether the anticipated competitive realignment is real. If Ferrari's drivers are fastest through Sector 2 and the Ferrari-favored low-speed sections, this weekend could produce the Scuderia's second win of 2026. Watch for Leclerc and Hamilton to both show pace — and then fight each other, which may create an entirely different set of storylines by Sunday.
