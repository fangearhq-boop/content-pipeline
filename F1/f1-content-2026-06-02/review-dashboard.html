NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Monaco race week is here — and for the first time in 2026, the competitive order looks genuinely different. The Monaco Grand Prix weekend begins Friday, June 5, with FP1 at 13:30 local time and FP2 at 17:00. Qualifying is Saturday June 6 at 15:00 local, and the race is Sunday, June 7 (2 PM ET, Apple TV+). This is the most unpredictable Grand Prix of the season so far, and here is why. The FIA has confirmed that active aerodynamics — the "Straight Mode" that has been the backbone of Mercedes' 2026 dominance — will not be used at any point during Monaco. The reason: FIA regulations require each straight mode activation zone to last more than three seconds. No stretch of tarmac in Monte Carlo meets that threshold. Safety was also a factor: the limited run-off at the Tunnel exit made active aero activation genuinely dangerous. The result: Mercedes loses its most significant competitive weapon. Ferrari and McLaren — both of whom carry shorter-wheelbase chassis with outstanding low-speed downforce — gain an enormous advantage. Ferrari is the pre-race favorite by a wide margin. Even championship leader Kimi Antonelli has declared Ferrari the "team to beat" this weekend. McLaren arrives motivated after a disastrous Canada weekend that yielded zero points through a combination of strategic errors and unreliability. They know this circuit can suit them. ### Image Post Caption Race week in Monaco. 🏎️ Active aero banned. Mercedes' advantage gone. Ferrari and McLaren step forward. ### Long-Form Post Lewis Hamilton has been waiting for a moment like this since the day he signed for Ferrari. The Canadian Grand Prix gave him the closest thing to it yet. Starting from fifth, Hamilton worked his way through the field, passed Max Verstappen in a battle that had the whole paddock watching, and crossed the line in second place. After the race, he called it "the happiest day at Ferrari so far." His mother was in the stands. The emotion was real. Now comes Monaco — and Hamilton believes this is where his Ferrari story could truly turn. "Power is not king there," he said before the weekend. "I think our car could be really strong." He is right. The FIA's active aerodynamics ban means Mercedes — who have dominated all five races of 2026 — will arrive in Monte Carlo without their most powerful competitive tool. Ferrari's SF-26, which suffers from a power deficit at high speeds, is built for exactly the kind of slow-corner, mechanical-grip circuit that Monaco is. Hamilton identified Monaco as Ferrari's golden opportunity before the weekend even began. He has never won Monaco in red. He has seven World Championships and 104 wins. ### Long-Form Post Kimi Antonelli has done something no Formula 1 driver has ever done. He has won his first four starts of a season — the Canadian, Miami, Japanese, and Australian Grand Prix victories in 2026. Not only has Antonelli won four consecutive races to open a season, he is only the second driver in the last 10 years to score 149 championship points through the first five rounds of a season. All of that achievement arrives at Monaco with a catch: Antonelli has admitted that Ferrari is the team to beat this weekend. That is an unusual concession from a championship leader — but an honest one. He knows that the active aero ban removes Mercedes' primary advantage and that his SF-26 rival handles Monaco's demands better than the W17. Antonelli struggled here in 2025. The circuit's unforgiving walls, its demand for perfect precision over multiple laps, and its extreme difficulty in overtaking mean that momentum does not guarantee safety. One mistake at the barriers ends a perfect season in an instant. The most compelling storyline of Monaco 2026: can the F1 history-maker keep the sequence going on a circuit that exposed him last year? ### Image Post Caption 4 wins. 4 races. History made. ### Long-Form Post Charles Leclerc unveiled a special race suit for Monaco this week. It is almost entirely white with red accents — a nod to the colors of the Monegasque flag. For this race, the uniform reflects something deeper than marketing. Monaco is Leclerc's home circuit. He grew up in the principality. He has been riding buses on these streets since childhood. Every corner holds a memory — a go-kart race, a Formula 3 qualifying lap, the first time he drove through the tunnel in a Formula 1 car. He told the media: "Those are the streets that I've been taking my bus since I was a kid. On the track, whichever corner I am in, I've got a story." He has waited a long time to stand at the top of this race. He finally won it in 2024, ending years of heartbreak in his own streets. Three Monaco pole positions came before that maiden victory. The win meant more to Leclerc than any podium elsewhere could. He arrives this week after the worst Grand Prix weekend of his career in Canada, where his teammate Lewis Hamilton took a podium while Leclerc struggled. He will not carry that into Monaco. These streets are different. Ferrari is the favorite. The active aero ban plays directly into the SF-26's strengths. The paddock believes Leclerc has both the car and the circuit knowledge to win.
