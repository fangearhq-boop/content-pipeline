Ferrari's Miami SF-26 Overhaul: The Biggest Upgrade of 2026 Is Almost Here

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

Ferrari's Monza filming day last week was not a filming day in any meaningful sense. It was a development session — a carefully constructed final validation of an upgrade package that the Scuderia have been working on since the first days of the 2026 season revealed the SF-26's competitive limitations. By all accounts, Charles Leclerc and Lewis Hamilton both came away from their laps at Monza with positive assessments of the changes. Ferrari's engineers have concluded the package is ready for race deployment. Miami will be its debut.

The scope of what Ferrari are bringing to Florida is unusual. Some technical analysts have described the package as touching roughly 50 per cent of the car's aerodynamic components — a headline figure that, even as an approximation, speaks to how seriously the Scuderia have taken the task of closing the gap to Mercedes. The key components include a revised and lighter floor, a new aerodynamic package designed to work in concert across the car's surfaces, and the full competitive debut of the "Macarena" rear wing that Ferrari have been developing for several months.

The Macarena Wing: From Promise to Race Duty

The Macarena rear wing has been one of the most discussed technical developments of the early 2026 season. The rotating device is designed to reduce aerodynamic drag on straights — a persistent weakness for the SF-26 compared to Mercedes — while maintaining the downforce and stability that Ferrari's drivers need through high-speed corners. Leclerc and Hamilton have both given encouraging feedback about the wing's behaviour during testing, and Ferrari's engineering team believes the concept has reached the maturity level required for competitive use.

Team principal Frédéric Vasseur has described Miami as a "reset" moment for the Scuderia. That language is loaded with intent. A reset does not mean a minor improvement — it means a step change in competitive position. Whether the SF-26 upgrade package delivers that step change will be one of the defining questions of the 2026 season's opening phase.

The Gap to Close

Ferrari enter Miami sitting 45 points behind Mercedes in the constructors' championship — a significant but not insurmountable deficit through five rounds of a 24-race season. In the drivers' standings, Leclerc has been solid and points-efficient through the first three races, while Hamilton has adapted to his first season in red with more difficulty than expected given his exceptional record at Mercedes. Together, they represent a driver pairing capable of winning races and championships if the car gives them the tools.

The challenge is that Mercedes have also been developing their car during the break. And McLaren are bringing their own set of upgrades to Miami. Ferrari are not the only team looking to the American street circuit as a turning point — but they may be the team with the most at stake given the scale of what they are introducing.

What's Next

The Miami Grand Prix Sprint weekend begins May 1, with the main race on May 3. Ferrari will have 90 minutes of extended free practice — up from the usual 60 — to evaluate the new package before the Sprint shootout session. Vasseur has been clear that expectations need to be managed: this is a significant upgrade, but it will require time to extract the full potential from the data gathered in Miami. For Leclerc and Hamilton, however, patience is a luxury that becomes harder to justify as the championship gap to Mercedes grows. Miami is Ferrari's best chance in 2026 to announce that the title fight is genuinely open.
