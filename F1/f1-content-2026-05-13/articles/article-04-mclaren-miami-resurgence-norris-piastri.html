McLaren Is Back: Norris-Piastri P2-P3 in Miami Signals Real Title Threat

By Ryan Calloway

May 13, 2026

After a frustrating opening to the 2026 Formula One season, McLaren got their campaign back on track in Miami — and did so with authority.

Lando Norris finished second behind race winner Kimi Antonelli, and Oscar Piastri came home third in a 2-3 that represented easily McLaren's best result of the new season. For a team that had struggled to unlock the pace of the MCL40 in the opening rounds, Miami was confirmation that the car, when properly set up, belongs near the front.

Norris Makes His Move

The reigning Formula One World Champion has had a difficult opening to his title defense in 2026. The combination of the new regulations and early-season car setup issues put Norris on the back foot, but Miami showed the talent that made him champion a year ago is fully intact. He pushed Antonelli hard for the win and only the Mercedes's raw pace advantage at the front kept the McLaren from claiming the top step.

Norris is now fourth in the championship with 51 points, 49 behind Antonelli. With McLaren recovering form, that gap could shrink quickly. Piastri sits sixth with 43 points, and the prospect of both McLaren cars applying sustained pressure on Mercedes through the European season is real.

The Package Is Working

Multiple reports emerging from Miami pointed to McLaren having finally resolved the package issues that had hampered them through the first three races. The MCL40 appeared more consistent on tire wear and more effective through the medium-speed corners that define competitive F1 performance. Getting both drivers onto the podium in the same race was the clearest possible proof of concept.

For teams that had written off McLaren as a 2026 contender after the opening rounds, Miami served as a reminder. In a year where the grid has been reshuffled by new regulations, form can change dramatically from race to race. The papaya cars look like they have found their groove.

What's Next

The Canadian Grand Prix at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve on May 22-24 is next. Montreal has historically been a circuit where McLaren can be competitive, and if the Miami form carries over, Norris and Piastri will again be challenging for the podium — and possibly threatening for a win if Mercedes has any kind of off-weekend. Norris has won before and he knows what it takes. The championship is not over in Round 5.
