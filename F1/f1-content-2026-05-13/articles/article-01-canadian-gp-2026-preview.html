Canada Grand Prix Preview: Can Anyone Stop Kimi Antonelli's Historic Winning Streak?

By Ryan Calloway

May 13, 2026

The Formula 1 circus heads to Montreal in nine days for Round 5 of the 2026 season, and the question on everyone's mind is the same: can anyone actually beat Kimi Antonelli?

The 19-year-old Mercedes driver arrives at the Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve having won three consecutive grands prix from three consecutive pole positions — a feat never before accomplished in Formula One history. He leads the championship with 100 points, 20 ahead of teammate George Russell. It is, to put it mildly, an extraordinary start to a Formula 1 career.

The Circuit and Its Demands

Montreal's Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve is unlike any other on the calendar. Built on Île Notre-Dame in the St. Lawrence River, it combines long straights with tight chicanes, a hairpin, and the fearsome Wall of Champions — a barrier that has claimed cars from Schumacher, Damon Hill, and Jacques Villeneuve himself, among many others. Safety cars appear with regularity here, and strategy calls can be the difference between a podium and the gravel trap.

The circuit rewards quick reaction to the unexpected. Weather can shift rapidly, tires degrade in particular ways on the abrasive surface, and the walls close in at exactly the moments when drivers are at full attack. It is one of the most spectacular and dramatic races of any season.

The Challengers

Mercedes has been dominant, but the most interesting development heading into Canada is McLaren's resurgence. Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri went second and third in Miami, signaling that the MCL40 has found meaningful pace after a troubled start to the season. Norris, the reigning world champion, now sits fourth in the championship and is clearly motivated to apply pressure on his old team.

Ferrari brings a complicated picture. Charles Leclerc is third in the standings but received a post-race penalty in Miami that cost him two positions. Lewis Hamilton sits fifth and has openly questioned his current preparation approach. Ferrari needs both cars fighting at the front if they want any realistic shot at the constructors' championship.

Then there is Red Bull. Max Verstappen is seventh in the standings with 26 points — 74 behind Antonelli — and the four-time world champion is having the most difficult regulatory transition of his career. Montreal is traditionally a high-downforce circuit, which may or may not play to Red Bull's current strengths depending on the progress they have made with their car.

What's Next

Practice sessions begin Friday, May 22, with qualifying Saturday, May 23 and the race Sunday, May 24 at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve. Antonelli has been untouchable so far in 2026, but Montreal has a long history of throwing curveballs. If there is going to be a first interruption to the Antonelli era, it might just happen here.
