Austrian GP Recap: Russell Wins as Mercedes Complete Stunning 1-3 Finish

By Elena Voss July 3, 2026 F1 Fanrecap

Pole to Flag in Spielberg

George Russell did not give anyone the opportunity to challenge him. From the moment lights went out at the Red Bull Ring on June 28, the Mercedes driver controlled the Austrian Grand Prix with authority, leading every lap from pole position to the chequered flag. When it ended, he crossed the line 1.611 seconds clear of Max Verstappen in second place — a margin that flatters Russell's dominance. This was a race that felt decided early and stayed that way.

Kimi Antonelli completed the podium in third for Mercedes, turning the Austrian Grand Prix into a 1-3 result for the Silver Arrows and adding another commanding performance to what is already a historic season for the Brackley-based team. The constructors' championship continues to move in one direction: Mercedes now hold 302 points, a full 98 clear of Ferrari in second place.

The Qualifying Drama That Almost Wasn't

The weekend's tension arrived in qualifying on Saturday, when Russell's pole position lap was placed under investigation. The concern: a potential yellow flag infringement during the lap that set the fastest time. Stewards reviewed the incident, examined the data, and ultimately cleared Russell entirely. He kept his pole and his place at the front of the grid. But the brief uncertainty added a layer of drama to what might otherwise have been a straightforward Saturday for Mercedes.

Max Verstappen's qualifying session ended far more definitively — and far worse. The four-time world champion crashed at Turn 9, damaging his Red Bull and eliminating himself from the running for a strong grid position. That crash cost Verstappen any realistic shot at winning the race. He recovered to second in the race itself, which was a creditable result from wherever he started, but the gap to Russell at the front underlined how difficult his weekend had been from Saturday onwards.

Antonelli's Quiet Brilliance

While Russell took the glory of victory, Kimi Antonelli's third-place finish deserves its own examination. The 21-year-old championship leader started from a position further back than his teammate and worked his way through the field to claim a podium. It was not the most dramatic drive of his season — he has wins that were far more assertive — but it was the kind of composed, points-maximising performance that separates genuine title contenders from occasional race winners.

Antonelli arrived at the Red Bull Ring leading the championship. He left still leading it. His points total moved to 171, and with Russell claiming second on the road rather than first, the gap between the two Mercedes drivers actually shifted in Antonelli's favour in relative terms. Damage limitation when you do not win is a skill, and at 21 years old, Antonelli is demonstrating it with impressive regularity.

The Rest of the Field

Behind the top three, Oscar Piastri brought his McLaren home in fourth place. The Australian has been a consistent points scorer this season, and fourth at a circuit where McLaren have historically been competitive was a reasonable return. Lewis Hamilton crossed the line fifth for Ferrari. The five-time world champion — now in his second season with the Scuderia — scored ten points from the Austrian Grand Prix, a result that keeps him third in the drivers' championship but does little to close the gap to the Mercedes drivers ahead of him.

Ferrari's constructors' position tells a difficult story. With 204 points after eight rounds, they sit 98 behind Mercedes. The pace gap that was visible at some circuits earlier in the season has not fully closed, and the Austrian Grand Prix did not provide the breakout result the Maranello team would have been hoping for. Hamilton and Ferrari need a different kind of weekend — and Silverstone may test them further.

Championship Standings After Austria

The drivers' championship picture after Round 8 is as follows: Antonelli leads on 171 points, Russell is second on 131, and Hamilton third on 125. The gap between Antonelli and Russell is 40 points — meaningful but not insurmountable over the remaining rounds before summer break. The gap between Antonelli and Hamilton is 46 points, with Ferrari's constructors deficit suggesting Hamilton may need near-perfection and Mercedes near-disaster to mount a realistic challenge.

What's Next

Russell departs the Austrian Grand Prix heading directly into one of the most emotionally significant races of his career — the British Grand Prix at Silverstone, his home circuit, starting today with the sprint weekend format. He arrives as the reigning race winner, in form, and with his home crowd at his back. For Antonelli, Silverstone is an away race — but one where points are just as valuable. The championship battle that defined Austria now heads to England, where the stakes and the spectacle will only grow.
