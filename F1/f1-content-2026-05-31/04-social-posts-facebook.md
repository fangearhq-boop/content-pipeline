So why did the FIA pull the plug? It comes down to two key reasons.

First, the safety concern: at Monaco, Formula 1 cars are pushing the absolute limit of tyre grip at almost every corner. The FIA's guidance is that Straight Mode zones should only be deployed where cars aren't near the grip limit — and at Monaco, that's almost nowhere.

What does this mean for the race? Overtake Mode (the system that gives drivers extra electrical power within one second of the car ahead) remains available. But the wholesale drag reduction of Straight Mode is gone.

And the impact on the championship? This is huge for Ferrari and Lewis Hamilton. The 2026 regulations have heavily favored teams with powerful power units. Take that straight-line speed advantage away, and Monaco becomes a contest of aerodynamic balance, mechanical grip, and driver precision — exactly where the SF-26 excels.
