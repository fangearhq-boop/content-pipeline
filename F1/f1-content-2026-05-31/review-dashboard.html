FIA Bans Active Aero (Straight Mode) at Monaco — Safety Decision
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Formula 1's most dramatic technical decision of 2026 just landed — and it's going to reshape the Monaco Grand Prix. The FIA has confirmed that active aerodynamics — specifically "Straight Mode," the system that replaced DRS in the 2026 regulations — will NOT be available to drivers at Monaco. Cars will race with fixed wings throughout the weekend. So why did the FIA pull the plug? It comes down to two key reasons. First, the safety concern: at Monaco, Formula 1 cars are pushing the absolute limit of tyre grip at almost every corner. The FIA's guidance is that Straight Mode zones should only be deployed where cars aren't near the grip limit — and at Monaco, that's almost nowhere. Second, the "three-second rule": every Straight Mode zone must last at least three seconds to give drivers sufficient reaction time and stability. Monaco's famous "straights" — barely long enough to be called that — don't satisfy this requirement. What does this mean for the race? Overtake Mode (the system that gives drivers extra electrical power within one second of the car ahead) remains available. But the wholesale drag reduction of Straight Mode is gone. And the impact on the championship? This is huge for Ferrari and Lewis Hamilton. The 2026 regulations have heavily favored teams with powerful power units. Take that straight-line speed advantage away, and Monaco becomes a contest of aerodynamic balance, mechanical grip, and driver precision — exactly where the SF-26 excels. This Monaco Grand Prix, June 5-7 on Apple TV+, just became even more unpredictable. ### Image Post Caption No Straight Mode. No active aero. Just pure driver skill and car balance at Monaco. The FIA just changed everything. 🇲🇨 #F1 #MonacoGP
