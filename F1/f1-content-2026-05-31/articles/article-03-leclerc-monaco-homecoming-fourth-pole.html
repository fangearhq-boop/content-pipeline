Leclerc's Monaco Homecoming: A Fourth Pole, a Repeat Win, and Ferrari's Best Circuit

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | May 31, 2026

For most drivers, Monaco is the race they spend the year building toward. For Charles Leclerc, it is something more personal than that. He was born here. He grew up on these streets. He learned to drive on the roads that host the most famous race in the world. When the barriers come up and the circuit comes alive in early June, Leclerc is, quite literally, home.

He arrives in 2026 as the early favorite at 21/10 — and with every reason for that confidence.

The Record That Speaks for Itself

No active driver has more Monaco qualifying poles than Charles Leclerc. He has taken pole position at his home Grand Prix three times in his career. He finally converted that pole-to-win cycle in 2024, winning the Monaco Grand Prix for the first time and cementing a story that had been written in anticipation for years.

Now he comes back to write the next chapter. The statistics are firmly in his corner. In 27 of the last 30 Monaco Grands Prix, the driver who started on pole went on to win. At a circuit where overtaking is functionally impossible for most of the race, qualifying defines everything. Leclerc's lap in Monaco's Saturday session is one of the most reliably exceptional performances in the sport.

Ferrari's Machine Meets Monaco's Demands

Beyond Leclerc's individual talent, the SF-26 looks ideally suited to what Monaco asks of a Formula 1 car in 2026. Ferrari's car has demonstrated consistent strength in low-speed corners throughout the season. The FIA's decision this week to ban active aerodynamics at Monaco removes the one area where rival teams have leveraged their advantages — high-speed, drag-reduction zones that rarely exist at Monte Carlo anyway.

The competitive endorsement is striking. Lando Norris, one of the season's fastest qualifiers, has tipped Ferrari for Monaco pole. Andrea Stella, McLaren's team principal, agreed. When your primary rivals publicly acknowledge that Ferrari will be leading the way in qualifying, it removes any doubt about where the SF-26 fits in the Monaco pecking order.

What's Next

Charles Leclerc will take to the streets of Monte Carlo for Free Practice on Friday, June 5. Qualifying begins Saturday, June 6 at 3:00 PM local time. The 2026 Monaco Grand Prix starts Sunday, June 7, live on Apple TV+ at 2:00 PM ET. A fourth Monaco pole and a second consecutive Monaco win are both within realistic reach for the man who calls this circuit home. For Ferrari and for Leclerc, Monaco represents the kind of opportunity that confirms whether a season's early momentum translates into meaningful championship implications. The circuit, the car, and the driver are pointing in the same direction.
