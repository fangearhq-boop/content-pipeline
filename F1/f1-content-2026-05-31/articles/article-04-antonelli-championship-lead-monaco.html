Antonelli Has a 43-Point Championship Lead and Says He's "Not Looking at It"

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | May 31, 2026

Kimi Antonelli arrives at Monaco leading the 2026 Formula 1 World Championship by 43 points — 131 to 88 over his Mercedes teammate George Russell. He has won four of the first five races. He is currently on a run that would, if sustained, produce one of the most dominant title-winning campaigns in the modern era.

When asked about all of this, he said: "I'm not looking at the championship."

Whether that reflects extraordinary mental discipline or the slightly detached confidence of someone who simply can't yet fully comprehend what's happening, the effect is the same: Antonelli is performing at a level that is making the 2026 drivers' title look like a formality, while projecting the calm of someone who has done nothing remarkable at all.

Russell's Stark Assessment

Antonelli's teammate has been less coy about the situation. After his retirement from the Canadian GP lead — his Mercedes losing power on Lap 30 while he was pulling away from the field — Russell told Sky Sports: "It feels like it is his to lose now." That admission, from the 2026 Australian Grand Prix winner and Antonelli's closest rival, carries weight. Russell hasn't given up, but he has acknowledged the reality: the gap is large, the deficit his own reliability problems created are significant, and the window to change the championship picture is narrowing.

Mercedes team principal Toto Wolff has drawn a clear line: both drivers are free to race each other, but not in a way that costs the team wins or championship points. At Monaco, where the barriers are inches away and the margin for contact-free racing is almost nonexistent, that instruction will be tested.

What Monaco Means for the Championship

Monaco is not a natural Mercedes circuit in 2026. The SF-26 from Ferrari and the active aero ban announced this week give rivals genuine ground to make up. If Leclerc wins and Antonelli finishes lower in the points, the gap could shrink. A Russell win combined with Antonelli outside the top three could trim the deficit significantly.

But history suggests these scenarios are hard to engineer at Monaco — especially against a driver who is currently converting almost everything into points. Antonelli has shown no sign of over-confidence or complacency, and his ability to extract the maximum from whatever situation the race presents has been the defining quality of his 2026 season.

What's Next

The Monaco Grand Prix weekend begins Friday, June 5 and culminates with Sunday's race on June 7, live on Apple TV+ at 2:00 PM ET. A win for Antonelli in Monte Carlo would push the championship gap beyond 58 points with over half the season remaining. For Russell, a podium finish — and ideally a Antonelli retirement or poor result — is the minimum required to keep the title fight mathematically alive in any meaningful sense. Monaco's unpredictability is the only ally Russell has right now. Whether it delivers will define the rest of the 2026 season.
