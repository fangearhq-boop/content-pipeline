Verstappen's Monaco Shot: The Exit Clause Is Ticking and This Is His Best Circuit

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 31, 2026

Max Verstappen has won the Monaco Grand Prix twice. In 2021, in the early years of his Red Bull dominance, he took a masterful victory through the streets of Monte Carlo. In 2023, he did it again. Monaco has been as close to a home race for Verstappen as any circuit on the calendar — a track that rewards his particular brand of commitment and car control.

He needs it now more than ever.

The Exit Clause Reality

Verstappen's contract with Red Bull contains a performance exit clause: if he is outside the top two in the drivers' championship when the summer break begins on July 27, he gains the right to activate the clause and explore the open market. Currently sitting ninth in the championship, with the title leaders separated by dozens of points and his Red Bull RBPT package still finding its feet in the 2026 regulations, the clause is firmly in play.

The clause was reportedly inserted at Verstappen's insistence, reflecting his "longstanding apprehension" about how Red Bull's homegrown power unit would perform against the established manufacturers when the new era of regulations launched. That apprehension was well-founded. The RB26 has been a car with genuine potential hampered by an engine that has struggled to match the straight-line performance of the Mercedes, Ferrari, and McLaren packages. Red Bull are developing rapidly, but the gap remains.

A Turning Point in Canada

The Canadian Grand Prix delivered a result that changed the tone, if not the championship math. Verstappen finished third — Red Bull and RBPT's first podium finish of the entire 2026 season. In a race dominated by the Mercedes intra-team battle, with Antonelli taking the win and Russell retiring from the lead, Verstappen navigated a clean race and brought home the points. It was the first concrete evidence that the car can operate at the front of the field under the right conditions.

Monaco amplifies those conditions. With the FIA banning active aerodynamics this week, the straight-line speed differential that has hurt Verstappen at power-circuit tracks becomes largely irrelevant. Monaco is about mechanical grip, aerodynamic balance, and the ability to find hundredths of a second through hairpins and chicanes — exactly where Verstappen's talent has always shown most vividly.

What's Next

The Monaco Grand Prix weekend begins Friday, June 5, with the race on Sunday, June 7 at 2:00 PM ET on Apple TV+. A Monaco win for Verstappen would not solve his championship position overnight, but it would fundamentally change the narrative of his 2026 season. It would silence the exit clause conversation, at least temporarily. It would prove that the RBPT package can win — not just podium — at a street circuit. And it would remind everyone watching why Max Verstappen is a four-time world champion. The clock is ticking. June 7 is the next page of what happens next.
