### STORY 2: Ferrari's Major Miami Upgrade Package — "Reset" Moment for SF-26
**Tier:** 1 | **Priority:** Breaking/Trending
**Status:** NEW STORY

Ferrari is bringing its most significant upgrade package of the 2026 season to Miami (May 1-3), following a private test at Monza that included Hamilton and Leclerc on the revised aerodynamic package. The headline piece is a new floor originally intended for Sakhir, designed to increase overall downforce and improve airflow under the car. The Macarena rear wing — which rotates its upper flap upside down — also forms part of the Miami package. Ferrari trails Mercedes by 45 points in the Constructors' Championship (90 vs. 135) and team principal Frédéric Vasseur has internally communicated that Miami represents a "reset" moment in the championship fight.

**Key Facts:**
- New floor: intended for Sakhir, debuts Miami — increases downforce, improves airflow
- Macarena rear wing: rotates upper flap upside down, tested pre-season and briefly in China
- Private Monza filming day test: Hamilton + Leclerc evaluated revised aero package
- Ferrari 45 pts behind Mercedes in Constructors (90 vs 135)
- Leclerc 23 pts behind Antonelli in Drivers (49 vs 72)
- Vasseur: Miami is the "reset" moment

### STORY 3: FIA Confirms Rule Changes for Miami — 2026 Regulations Refined
**Tier:** 1 | **Priority:** Breaking/Trending
**Status:** FOLLOW-UP (April 18 pipeline — FIA April 20 vote)

Following the April 21 online FIA stakeholder meeting (FIA, team principals, PU manufacturers, FOM), all parties agreed a package of 2026 regulation refinements to be implemented from Miami onward. Key changes: super clipping increased from 250kW to maximum 350kW; maximum permitted recharge reduced from 8MJ to 7MJ (aimed at reducing excessive harvesting, more consistent flat-out driving); MGU-K deployment limited to 250kW outside key acceleration zones; a new "low power start detection system" will be trialed in Miami to address unpredictable car behavior at race starts (a criticism raised by Verstappen and others).

**Key Facts:**
- Super clipping: 250kW → 350kW (from Miami)
- Recharge limit: 8MJ → 7MJ (reduces excessive harvesting)
- MGU-K: 350kW in key zones, 250kW elsewhere on lap
- New "low power start detection system" trialed at Miami
- Agreed by all stakeholders: FIA, team principals, PU manufacturers, FOM (April 21)
- Changes go live Miami, May 1-3
