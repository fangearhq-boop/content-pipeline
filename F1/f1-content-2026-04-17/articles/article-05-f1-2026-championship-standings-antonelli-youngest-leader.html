F1 2026 Championship Snapshot: Antonelli's Historic Lead, Mercedes Civil War, and the Road to Miami

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | April 17, 2026

Three races into the 2026 Formula 1 World Championship, the season has already produced historical milestones, unexpected rivalries, and a championship picture that rewards close attention. As the paddock takes a five-week break before the Miami Grand Prix Sprint weekend, here is where everything stands.

The Drivers' Championship

Kimi Antonelli leads the Drivers' Championship with 72 points, nine clear of Mercedes teammate George Russell in second place on 63. Ferrari's Charles Leclerc occupies third with 49 points, and Lewis Hamilton sits fourth on 41. The gap between Antonelli and the bottom of the top four is 31 points — substantial but far from insurmountable in a 22-race season.

Antonelli's position at the top carries historic significance. At 19 years and 216 days old, he is the youngest championship leader in the history of Formula 1. He is also the first Italian driver to hold the championship lead since Alberto Ascari — who not only led but ultimately won the 1953 World Championship. In a sport where Italian drivers and Italian passion are woven into the sport's DNA, Antonelli's emergence as a genuine title contender has generated enormous enthusiasm around the paddock.

The Mercedes Intra-Team Battle

With Antonelli and Russell comprising the top two, Mercedes is in a position no team has successfully managed many times before: trying to both protect its constructors' lead and adjudicate an increasingly competitive intra-team rivalry. Russell, a seasoned campaigner and former world champion in his own right, was widely expected to lead the team's championship bid into 2026. Antonelli's emergence as a genuine points threat has created a dynamic that will only intensify as the season progresses.

The relationship between the two drivers — professional but clearly competitive — will be one of the defining narratives of the next 19 races. Whether Mercedes maintains team orders or allows the fight to escalate naturally could determine which driver stands in Yas Marina at the end of the year.

Constructors' Championship

Mercedes holds 135 constructors' points, leading Ferrari by 45 (90 points). McLaren sits third on 56, while Red Bull has accumulated just 16 points across three events. Aston Martin remains scoreless — a situation that has been extensively documented as a multi-system crisis involving Honda power unit vibrations and a significant performance deficit.

Ferrari's 90-point tally is respectable, reflecting the contributions of both Leclerc and Hamilton. The 45-point gap to Mercedes reflects the Silver Arrows' dominance in the opening three races, but it is by no means a final verdict. Ferrari made substantial progress through the first half of several recent seasons; 2026 may follow a similar trajectory.

What's Next

The Miami Grand Prix Sprint weekend runs from May 1 to 3 at the Miami International Autodrome. The Sprint format — featuring both a Sprint Qualifying, Sprint Race, full Qualifying, and main Grand Prix — creates additional points opportunities that can compress or extend championship leads in a single weekend. Antonelli, Russell, Leclerc, and Hamilton are all within mathematical striking distance of each other, and Miami's circuit characteristics — the high-speed sweeps, the stop-start stadium section, and the tight infield — suit different car philosophies in different ways. The 2026 season is genuinely open. What happens next is anyone's guess.
