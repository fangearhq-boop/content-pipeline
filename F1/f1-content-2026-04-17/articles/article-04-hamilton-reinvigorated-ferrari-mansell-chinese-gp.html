Hamilton "Reinvigorated": Mansell's Message as Seven-Time Champion Ends 477-Day Podium Drought

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | April 17, 2026

Nigel Mansell, the 1992 Formula 1 world champion, has long been an admirer of Lewis Hamilton. But his comments during the 2026 April break carry a weight that transcends ordinary praise. "Lewis is fired up again," Mansell said. "He's now reinvigorated." And then, with characteristic directness: "I'm going to be romantic — I'd like Lewis to do it. Come on Lewis, let's just finish it off."

The catalyst for this renewed optimism is clear. At the Chinese Grand Prix, Hamilton climbed onto the Formula 1 podium for the first time in 477 days — the end of a drought that had stretched across the closing chapters of his Mercedes career, his controversial mid-season move to Ferrari in 2025, and a difficult year of adaptation in red. The third-place finish was not just a result. It was, as Hamilton described it, "one of the most enjoyable races I've had."

The Chinese GP Battle

Hamilton's podium was earned through one of the more entertaining intra-team duels of the young 2026 season. Running close with Ferrari teammate Charles Leclerc through the middle stages of the race, the two drivers exchanged positions and pushed each other harder than team orders typically allow. The wheel-to-wheel battle drew "feisty" descriptions from paddock observers and reminded a broader audience — those who had perhaps grown accustomed to Hamilton in dominant, controlled form — that his racing instincts remain as sharp as they have ever been.

The result ended a 477-day wait for a rostrum finish, which included his final races at Mercedes and his entire first season at Ferrari. It was his first podium in Ferrari red. Hamilton credited the team's support structure — the mechanics, engineers, and the culture at Scuderia Ferrari — for helping him build the confidence that translated into on-track performance.

What Has Changed in 2026

The 2025 season at Ferrari was marked by difficulty. Hamilton was adapting to a new team, a different car philosophy, and the scrutiny that accompanies every move he makes. Results were mixed. The championship points did not reflect his capabilities. But his own accounts of the 2026 campaign suggest a different environment: one where trust has been built, the car has been developed to better suit his preferences, and the team around him has aligned its approach to maximizing his performance.

He is fourth in the 2026 Drivers' Championship on 41 points — 31 behind leader Kimi Antonelli. The gap is significant, but three races into a 22-event season, no deficit is insurmountable. Ferrari's constructors' position (90 points, second behind Mercedes' 135) suggests the car is broadly competitive, even if Mercedes currently holds the advantage.

What's Next

Hamilton and Ferrari head to Miami as the fourth-fastest driver in the standings, with a Sprint weekend format that creates additional points opportunities. The question is whether the Chinese GP podium represents a temporary peak or the start of a sustained run of form that could genuinely threaten the front-running Mercedes. Mansell, for one, believes the answer is the latter. Hamilton's own actions on the circuit at China made the strongest possible case that the seven-time champion is still capable of adding to that number — and that this chapter of his career is far from its final pages.
