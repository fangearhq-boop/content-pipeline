Verstappen's Exit Clause Will Trigger: Austria May Be His Last Race as a Red Bull Driver

By Marcus Cole June 25, 2026

The Clause and the Math

Max Verstappen's contract with Red Bull Racing contains an exit clause that activates if he is not within the top two of the drivers' championship standings at the summer break. At this moment, he sits at approximately P7, more than 100 points behind championship leader Kimi Antonelli and more than 55 points behind Hamilton in second. It is not just unlikely that he reaches the top two before the break — it is mathematically impossible, even with a perfect run of results across every remaining race.

Multiple insiders have described the clause as "impossible to block." The mechanism exists, the threshold has not been met, and the summer break that activates it is approaching.

The Red Bull Internal Situation

What makes the Verstappen situation more complicated than a simple contractual exit is the state of Red Bull Racing's ownership. The Thai ownership side is reportedly open to releasing Verstappen and allowing the clause to be exercised. The Mateschitz and Mintzlaff side opposes it. A financial buyout was offered and declined. Manager Raymond Vermeulen has said publicly that a decision will come before the summer break.

The disagreement at ownership level means the situation has not resolved cleanly into a straightforward departure announcement, even as the underlying contractual reality makes the outcome appear inevitable. Red Bull's preferred outcome — retaining Verstappen while he competes for results — has been closed off by a 2026 season that has not gone their way on track.

Where He Goes Next

Mercedes are widely considered the frontrunner for Verstappen's signature. The specifics of timing, term, and financial structure have not been publicly confirmed. Verstappen has also, privately, raised the possibility of retirement — a complete exit from Formula 1 rather than a move to a rival manufacturer. The retirement option has not been ruled out by his camp, and at 28 years old, the prospect of walking away from a sport in which Red Bull no longer has a competitive car has reportedly been considered seriously.

The driver market implications extend beyond Verstappen himself. His departure from Red Bull would reshape the grid at multiple teams and potentially trigger a cascade of signings and announcements in the weeks following the summer break.

Six Wins, a Final Lap

Verstappen has won six times at the Red Bull Ring. Four consecutive victories between 2021 and 2023 made this circuit one of the defining venues of his dominant Red Bull era, and the crowd at Spielberg — even accounting for the international Dutch fanbase that follows him across Europe — has consistently produced some of the loudest reactions he receives anywhere on the calendar.

Whatever comes after the summer break, the Austrian GP carries the weight of a possible farewell. A driver who defined a circuit for years, racing on it potentially for the last time under his current team's name.

What's Next

The exit clause itself will not be formally exercised until the summer break arrives. But the Austrian GP weekend — Red Bull's home race, at Red Bull's home circuit — is where the narrative crystallizes. How Verstappen performs across qualifying and Sunday's race, how the Red Bull crowd responds, and whether any official word emerges from the team or his management will all be watched more closely than the lap times themselves. The decision is coming. Austria is the last race before it becomes unavoidable.
