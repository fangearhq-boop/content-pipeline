### STORY 1: FIA April 9 Summit — Three Days Away, Six Fixes on the Table

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM ET

The FIA's April 9 summit is three days away, and it may be the most consequential meeting in the 2026 Formula 1 season.

On the agenda are six potential fixes to the energy deployment regulations that have defined — and destabilized — the first three rounds of the season. The most significant proposals: raising the super clipping threshold from 250kW to 350kW (reducing the differential between manufacturer outputs) and cutting the maximum energy per lap from 9MJ to 7MJ. Three additional measures are also under discussion.

Here's the catch: unanimous agreement is required from all 10 teams before any change can be made. Teams with more powerful energy deployment systems (Mercedes primarily) have a commercial incentive to maintain the status quo. Teams without it want the field leveled. Getting ten teams to agree on anything in F1 is difficult under normal circumstances. Getting them to agree on changes that disadvantage some and benefit others is close to miraculous.

The safety imperative makes this summit non-optional. Oliver Bearman's 50G crash at Suzuka on March 29 — caused directly by the energy deployment disparity between manufacturers — demonstrated that this isn't just a competitive issue. It's a safety issue. Hamilton, typically measured, expressed public skepticism. Sainz has been the summit's most vocal advocate.

The goal is to implement changes before Miami on May 3. That gives approximately three weeks for teams to develop and test fixes. Can they do it? Find out April 9.

### STORY 6: McLaren's 10-Change Miami Upgrade

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM ET

McLaren is arriving at Miami with something to prove — and a 10-change upgrade package to prove it with.

Andrea Stella confirmed the significance of the MCL40 update, which targets the operating window of the car. In Formula 1, "operating window" refers to the temperature and handling range in which the car performs optimally — tires, brakes, and aerodynamic balance all function best within a specific envelope. McLaren's belief is that expanding this window will make their car more competitive across a wider range of conditions, particularly at a circuit like Miami where temperatures vary and track evolution is significant.

It's worth noting that McLaren won the Miami Grand Prix in both 2024 (Norris) and 2025 (Piastri). They understand this circuit. They know what it takes to win there. Whether the upgrade is the bridge between the third-best car on the grid and a genuine championship threat remains to be seen.

Lando Norris sits 47 points behind Antonelli. Oscar Piastri is 51 back. Both championships are not yet mathematically close to decided — there are more than 19 races remaining. If this upgrade delivers even two to three tenths of pace, McLaren could be a very different proposition by mid-season.

The McLaren upgrade story is one to watch closely when Miami practice begins on May 1.

### STORY 7: Miami GP Round 4 Preview — Sprint Format, May 1-3

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM ET

In 25 days, Formula 1 returns from its five-week break for the Miami Grand Prix — Round 4 of the 2026 season, and the first Sprint format weekend of the year.

Sprint weekends run a compressed schedule: one free practice session on Friday, qualifying and the Sprint on Saturday, and the main race on Sunday. The Sprint (approximately 100km) awards championship points for the top eight finishers. Less practice time means more uncertainty, more variables for the strategists, and a higher probability of the unexpected.

Miami International Autodrome presents a demanding mix: tight, technical sections and faster sweeps, with a surface grip that improves significantly across the weekend. Tire management, DRS (Drag Reduction System) effectiveness, and pit stop strategy are all significant factors.

The previous four Miami GP winners: Max Verstappen (2022, 2023), Lando Norris (2024), Oscar Piastri (2025). Can Antonelli make it three different McLaren-or-above drivers in three years? Can Red Bull use Stage 1 of their fix plan to score their first real points?

Everything in the 2026 F1 season converges at Miami International Autodrome on May 3.
