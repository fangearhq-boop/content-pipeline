Russell Wins Canadian Grand Prix as Red Bull Protest Rejected After Five-Hour Wait

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 24, 2026

George Russell delivered the kind of weekend that changes championships.

The Mercedes driver took pole position on Saturday, won the Sprint, and then led all 70 laps of the Canadian Grand Prix on Sunday at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve to claim the race victory. It is Mercedes' first Grand Prix win of the 2026 Formula 1 season and Russell's first victory of the year — delivered in a manner that even the post-race controversy could not diminish.

The controversy in question came from Red Bull Racing, who lodged two protests against Russell following a Safety Car incident late in the race. The team alleged that Russell had driven erratically in front of Max Verstappen under the Safety Car, and separately, that Russell had engaged in unsportsmanlike conduct by reporting Verstappen for an alleged overtake. After more than five hours of deliberation, the FIA stewards rejected both accusations. Russell's victory stands.

A Dominant Performance From Start to Finish

Russell qualified on pole Saturday by 0.068 seconds over teammate Kimi Antonelli — a gap of almost nothing across 4.361 kilometers of Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve — and converted it immediately into a commanding lead at the race start. Verstappen finished second, inheriting the position after Russell's opening pace left the field behind.

The Safety Car intervention late in the race created the flashpoint that Red Bull would use for their post-race protests. Russell maintained his position ahead of Verstappen throughout, slowing in compliance with the rules. Verstappen's radio suggested he believed Russell braked aggressively in front of him. Russell, asked about the incident afterward, said he drove within the regulations.

The stewards agreed. Both protests were rejected after deliberations that stretched long into the Montreal evening. Mercedes team principal Toto Wolff, who had described the protest process as "embarrassing," saw the outcome he expected confirmed by the FIA's panel.

Mercedes Ends a Win Drought

The Canadian Grand Prix victory is Mercedes' first race win of 2026 — a significant milestone for a team that had dominated Sprint races but had not yet stood atop the Grand Prix podium this season. The car, the W17, has the pace. The drivers — two of the strongest in the field — have the talent. Canada was the race where the formula came together perfectly for Russell, even as the relationship between him and Antonelli generated drama of its own.

For Wolff, managing two championship contenders in the same garage, Canada represents both a success and a warning. The success is obvious: his team won. The warning is in the Sprint, where Russell and Antonelli made contact and Antonelli's frustration spilled publicly onto the radio. Wolff moved quickly to contain it, and the message from the team was clear — intra-team disputes are resolved internally, not broadcast to the world. How long that containment holds under championship pressure remains an open question.

What's Next

Formula 1 travels next to Monte Carlo for the Monaco Grand Prix on June 7, 2026. Russell arrives with championship momentum, back-to-back victories, and the confidence of a driver who has won in one of the sport's most pressure-loaded environments. Antonelli must respond at a circuit that rewards the same precision that has made him exceptional this season. Next race: Monaco Grand Prix — Sunday June 7, 2026, Circuit de Monaco, Monte Carlo.
