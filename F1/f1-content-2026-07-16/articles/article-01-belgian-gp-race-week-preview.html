Belgian GP Race Week: A Championship Battle Three Drivers Can Still Win Descends on Spa

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | July 16, 2026

Antonelli leads by 25 points over Russell with Hamilton just 32 back as Formula 1 arrives at Spa-Francorchamps for Round 10 of the 2026 season.

Formula 1's Belgian Grand Prix weekend is here, and it carries the weight of a championship that is very much unresolved. Kimi Antonelli sits atop the Drivers' standings with 179 points. George Russell is 25 behind. Lewis Hamilton is 32 back. Three drivers. Three genuinely distinct paths to the title. And they're all converging on Spa-Francorchamps — the longest, most demanding circuit on the Formula 1 calendar.

Free Practice 1 gets underway Friday July 17 at 7:30 AM ET. Qualifying runs Saturday July 18 at 10:00 AM ET. The race is Sunday July 19 at 9:00 AM ET. The Ardennes weather forecast adds a layer of unpredictability: a 50% rain chance for Friday practice, 65% for Saturday qualifying, and a more manageable 20% for Sunday's race.

Why This Race Matters

Three races ago, this title fight looked effectively decided. Antonelli had a 66-point advantage — the kind of gap that typically signals a runway to the championship. Five straight wins, flawless execution, and a young Italian driver that seemed destined to become the youngest champion in the sport's modern history.

Then came the reliability failures. A wheel shield issue at Silverstone ended Antonelli's race early. Ferrari capitalized by winning two of the last three rounds. And now the Italian from Bologna arrives at his worst circuit from the 2025 season having finished 16th here a year ago — with Russell and Hamilton right behind him and momentum swinging toward his rivals.

Belgium and Hungary are back-to-back. The next two weekends could fundamentally reshape this championship fight.

The Circuit and the Challenge

Spa-Francorchamps measures 7.004 kilometers — the longest circuit on the Formula 1 calendar. Under 2026's new regulations, five Straight Mode activation zones have been added, placing extraordinary demands on battery energy management throughout the race distance. Teams will run just 44 laps in total, but every one of those laps tests both engineering and driver skill at the limit.

The classic Eau Rouge-Raidillon complex remains one of the most breathtaking challenges in motorsport. The Kemmel Straight that follows it rewards raw engine power. For 2026, with hybrid energy deployment more performance-critical than ever, the ability to manage power across a lap at Spa could be the differentiating factor in both qualifying and the race itself.

What's Next

Friday's practice sessions will set the tone. Teams will gather data on wet-weather performance, energy deployment strategies, and tire behavior ahead of what promises to be a dramatic qualifying session Saturday. If the rain falls as forecast, anything is possible. And in a championship fight this close, anything can happen. Set your alarm for 7:30 AM ET Friday — this race week starts now.
