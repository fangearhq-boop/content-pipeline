Antonelli's Championship Lead Has Fallen From 66 Points to 25 in Three Races — Can He Stop the Slide at Spa?

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | July 16, 2026

The young Italian's title advantage has evaporated as Ferrari's surge and Mercedes reliability failures transform a commanding lead into a genuinely contested three-way battle.

There was a moment, not long ago, when the 2026 Formula 1 World Drivers' Championship looked like it belonged to Kimi Antonelli. Five consecutive race victories — his first five wins in Formula 1 — had given the young Italian a 66-point lead over the field. He was composure personified, a 19-year-old talent controlling a championship like a veteran.

Now he leads by 25 points, with two back-to-back races left before the summer break, heading to a circuit where he finished 16th last year.

Three Races That Changed Everything

The shift in the championship picture has happened quickly and through a combination of Mercedes reliability failures and Ferrari's resurgence. Charles Leclerc and the SF-26 have won two of the last three races. And in that same stretch, Antonelli has suffered technical failures in two of those three rounds — including a wheel shield issue at the British Grand Prix that ended a race in which he was positioned for a strong points finish.

The mathematics of the championship have tightened as a result. George Russell is 25 points behind. Lewis Hamilton — who has found consistency where Antonelli has found mechanical misfortune — is 32 back. With 10 or 11 rounds remaining after Belgium, a 25-point gap is the kind of lead that keeps championship leaders awake at night, not the kind that lets them sleep soundly.

Belgium and Hungary: The Defining Stretch

The next two weekends are unforgiving in their importance. Spa-Francorchamps is followed immediately by the Hungarian Grand Prix — two contrasting circuits, both with enormous championship implications. Belgium is a high-speed power circuit where Antonelli's 2025 pace was genuinely poor. Hungary is a tight, technical arena where Mercedes has historically been strong.

If Russell or Hamilton wins at Spa — or takes maximum points — while Antonelli finishes outside the top three, the championship arithmetic becomes uncomfortable for the Italian. His advantage could shrink to single digits with multiple rounds remaining. That is the scenario Antonelli's engineers need to prevent.

What's Next

Antonelli needs a clean weekend. Pole position in Saturday's rain-soaked qualifying, a dominant race drive, and maximum championship points would stop the momentum swing and re-establish his authority over the title fight. Anything less and the narrative shifts further toward Russell and Hamilton. The 2026 championship is not decided. But Spa is where Antonelli must demonstrate that his early-season dominance was not a mirage.
