Verstappen-McLaren Transfer Saga Reaches Critical Stage as Exit Clause Looms After Hungary

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 16, 2026

The four-time world champion's performance-related Red Bull exit clause is set to activate after the Hungarian Grand Prix, intensifying speculation over the biggest driver market story since Hamilton left Mercedes for Ferrari.

Max Verstappen arrived at Spa-Francorchamps this week carrying more baggage than usual. On track, Red Bull faces a structurally difficult circuit under 2026 regulations. Off track, the paddock is consumed by what could be the most significant driver transfer in Formula 1 since Lewis Hamilton's departure from Mercedes for Ferrari.

The four-time world champion is contracted to Red Bull Racing until 2028. But his contract contains performance-related exit clauses, and those clauses are understood to activate after the Hungarian Grand Prix — the race immediately following this weekend. The decision on whether Verstappen stays or goes is expected before the F1 summer break begins.

McLaren the Frontrunner

McLaren are widely regarded as the frontrunner to secure Verstappen's services from 2027. Reports emerged earlier in the season that Verstappen had held discussions with the Woking team, though both Red Bull and McLaren moved quickly to publicly deny the scope of those talks. Despite those denials, the speculation has not subsided — and Verstappen's increasingly vocal frustration with Red Bull's technical performance has only added fuel to the fire.

At Silverstone, after his rear wing failed and sent him into the gravel for the second consecutive race weekend, Verstappen made no effort to hide his feelings. The four-time champion has always demanded a competitive car above all else — and by his own standards, the RB22 has fallen short in 2026.

A potential driver swap has been discussed as the mechanism for any deal: Verstappen leaving Red Bull for McLaren, with Oscar Piastri moving in the other direction to Milton Keynes. Lando Norris, who would be Verstappen's teammate at McLaren if the deal happens, has publicly responded to the speculation — adding to the sense that this story is developing rapidly toward a conclusion.

Red Bull's Position

Red Bull Racing has not publicly indicated any willingness to release Verstappen early from his 2028 contract. Reports suggesting they refused a financial buyout offer of €8 million from Verstappen's camp indicate the team is not simply standing aside. But exit clauses exist precisely for situations like this — and if performance metrics have been met that trigger Verstappen's right to explore his options, Red Bull's ability to hold him becomes legally complicated.

The timing matters enormously. Both Belgium and Hungary are on the calendar before the summer break. Belgium offers Verstappen the chance to demonstrate he can still win despite the car's issues. Hungary, where the exit clause reportedly activates, is where everything becomes official.

What's Next

The next ten days are pivotal. A win at Spa or Hungary could shift the narrative — a Verstappen victory would remind the paddock what he is capable of and potentially strengthen his negotiating position. Continued mechanical failures would deepen his frustration and accelerate any departure timeline. The F1 world will not have to wait long for clarity. Before the summer break closes, the answer to the biggest question in driver market history will be public.
