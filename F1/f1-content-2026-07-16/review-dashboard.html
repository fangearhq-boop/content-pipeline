Red Bull's Spa Problem — Verstappen vs Energy-Hungry Circuit
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Max Verstappen has always loved Spa-Francorchamps. He lives nearby, the Orange Army crosses the border in huge numbers, and the circuit historically has suited Red Bull's downforce-heavy setup philosophy. But 2026 is different. Under the new regulations, Spa is a uniquely energy-intensive circuit. The long flat-out sections through Eau Rouge, Raidillon, and the Kemmel Straight drain battery systems faster than almost anywhere else on the calendar — and energy management has been one of Red Bull's persistent weaknesses this season. On top of that, Verstappen has suffered consecutive race-ending incidents at both the Austrian Grand Prix and the British Grand Prix, both attributed to a rear wing fault that failed to close properly through corners. The so-called 'Macarena' wing issue sent Verstappen into the gravel at high speed in Austria during qualifying, and beached him with six laps to go at Silverstone while running third. Red Bull's engineers have been working on fixes, but the underlying challenge at Spa — where the circuit demands both raw power and flawless energy deployment — remains. Verstappen can still win here. But the team needs to solve its problems fast if it wants to compete with Mercedes and Ferrari over the next two critical weekends. ### Image Post Caption Two consecutive crashes.
