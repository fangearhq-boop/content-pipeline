McLaren Reveals 10-Change Miami Upgrade Package — Norris and Piastri Eyeing Three in a Row

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | April 5, 2026

McLaren won the Miami Grand Prix in 2024. They won it again in 2025. As the 2026 edition approaches on May 3, the Woking team is leaving nothing to chance — arriving in Florida with the most significant upgrade package of their 2026 season so far.

Team principal Andrea Stella confirmed that McLaren is bringing a major upgrade list to Miami, with 10 key changes to the MCL40. The overarching target is the car's operating window — the specific envelope of downforce levels, temperature ranges, and balance characteristics in which the MCL40 performs at its theoretical best. Getting the car into that window more consistently has been an ongoing challenge in 2026, and the Miami package is designed to address that problem directly.

Why the Operating Window Matters

A Formula 1 car is not equally fast in all conditions. Each car has an operating window — a set of parameters in which its tyres generate peak grip, its aerodynamics produce maximum downforce efficiency, and its balance allows the driver to push to the limit. Outside that window, performance drops sharply. Drivers often describe this as the car feeling "different" or "difficult to read."

The MCL40 has shown glimpses of the pace needed to challenge Mercedes when it is in its window, but maintaining that window across qualifying runs and race stints has been inconsistent. The Miami upgrade targets that inconsistency, attempting to broaden the conditions under which the car performs at or near its best.

The Miami Stakes

McLaren is not the only team bringing significant upgrades to Florida. Ferrari has its three-point plan ready following the April 22 Monza test. Red Bull continues its development push. Mercedes, as championship leaders, has its own refinements in preparation.

But McLaren has a specific advantage at Miami: track knowledge. Two consecutive victories at the Miami International Autodrome gives Norris, Piastri, and the engineering team a deep understanding of what the circuit requires from the car and the tyres. That knowledge base, combined with a 10-change upgrade package, makes McLaren a genuine threat for victory in May.

In the championship, Norris currently sits fifth with 25 points — adrift of the leaders but not out of contention. A victory in Miami, combined with a strong points haul, could rapidly reshape the title picture in McLaren's favour.

Ferrari and Mercedes on Alert

The scope of McLaren's Miami upgrade has caught the attention of their main rivals. A team arriving with 10 specific changes to their car — all targeting a circuit they know well — is a credible threat to the established order. Ferrari is focused on its own upgrade preparation. Mercedes is defending a commanding championship lead. But both are aware that Miami could be a McLaren race if the upgrade delivers.

What's Next

McLaren's upgrade preparation continues through the April break. The Miami Grand Prix begins May 3. For Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri, a third consecutive Miami victory would be more than a symbolic achievement — it would be a statement that McLaren's 2026 season is not going to be decided before June. The 10-change package is their argument. The race will be the verdict.
