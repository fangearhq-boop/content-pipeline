Kimi Antonelli Is the Youngest F1 Championship Leader in History — and He's Just Getting Started

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | April 5, 2026

Kimi Antonelli leads the 2026 Formula 1 World Championship. This is remarkable enough on its own. What makes it historically significant is the age at which he is doing it.

After winning the Japanese Grand Prix at Suzuka — his third victory of the 2026 season — Antonelli moved to 72 points and took the championship lead from his Mercedes team-mate George Russell. In doing so, he became the youngest driver in Formula 1 history to lead the world championship, breaking the record previously held by Lewis Hamilton from the 2007 Spanish Grand Prix. Antonelli is also the first teenager in F1 history to hold the top of the standings.

The Record He Broke

Lewis Hamilton was 22 years, five months, and six days old when he led the championship after the 2007 Spanish Grand Prix in his debut F1 season. That record stood for nearly two decades. Antonelli is younger.

The comparison carries extra weight given the circumstances. Hamilton is now Antonelli's team-mate at Mercedes — the seven-time world champion who came to Ferrari seeking more titles, and who currently sits fourth in the 2026 standings with 41 points. His protege, effectively, is rewriting the record books while racing alongside him.

Where the Championship Stands

Antonelli leads with 72 points. Russell is second with 63, keeping the Mercedes 1-2 formation intact. Leclerc sits third with 49 points, and Hamilton is fourth with 41. Lando Norris has 25 points in fifth for McLaren.

In the constructors' championship, Mercedes holds 135 points — 45 clear of Ferrari in second with 90. McLaren has 56. Red Bull has 16. The Silver Arrows have dominated both championships so far, a reflection of how comprehensively their 2026 power unit and car package has outperformed the field.

What Antonelli Is Doing

Antonelli's 2026 season has been defined by composure under pressure. Three race victories in three rounds are not the product of dominance alone — the 2026 Mercedes is the best car on the grid, but Antonelli has made fewer mistakes than Russell, extracted maximum performance when it matters, and demonstrated a level of race management that is unusual for a driver in his first full Formula 1 season.

He is also racing alongside two of the most experienced drivers on the grid in Russell and his former team-mate context at Prema. The internal benchmark is demanding. He is meeting it and, more often than not, exceeding it.

What's Next

The Miami Grand Prix on May 3 is the next opportunity for Antonelli to extend his lead — or for Russell, Leclerc, or Hamilton to begin chipping away at it. Ferrari arrives in Florida with a major upgrade package. McLaren has 10 key changes ready. The competition is tightening. For a teenager who is already rewriting record books, the challenge of holding the top of the championship for another race weekend is the next test. Based on what he has shown so far in 2026, there is every reason to believe he is up to it.
