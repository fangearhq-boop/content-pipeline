### STORY 2: Bearman's 50G Crash — 10 Penalty Points, 2 From Race Ban

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM ET

Oliver Bearman's crash at the Japanese Grand Prix was not just a frightening accident. It was a warning.

At 308 kilometres per hour, Bearman encountered a closing speed gap of 45km/h between himself and Franco Colapinto's Alpine ahead of him at Suzuka's Spoon Curve. The gap was not caused by a mistake from either driver — it was caused by the way the 2026 energy regulations work, with different manufacturers deploying and harvesting battery power at different points on the track. One car was fully charged and at full speed. The other was harvesting — significantly slower.

Bearman swerved onto the grass to avoid a collision, skidded back across the track, and hit the barrier at 50G. He walked away. He was lucky.

The aftermath has not been quiet. A poll on PlanetF1.com found that 93 percent of F1 fans believe the rules need to change following the incident. Bearman himself is now at 10 penalty points — two away from the 12 that trigger an automatic one-race ban. The FIA April 9 summit will look closely at the underlying cause of this crash and what can be done before Miami.

Haas team boss Ayao Komatsu put it well: Formula 1 "cannot make a knee-jerk reaction." But something must change.

### STORY 3: Ferrari's Three-Point Miami Upgrade Plan

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM ET

Ferrari is not going to Miami without ammunition.

The Scuderia has laid out a three-part upgrade plan for the Miami Grand Prix — their most significant technical push of the 2026 season so far. The headline upgrade is a revised floor targeting aerodynamic downforce. But the more intriguing elements are the software overhaul for the power unit — aimed at limiting the effects of super clipping that have hurt Ferrari on the straights — and the potential race debut of the 'Macarena' rear wing, Ferrari's radical design that flips completely inside out, which has been run only in practice sessions so far in 2026.

Ferrari is also awaiting FIA approval for an ADUO (Additional Development and Upgrade Opportunity) on their power unit. The estimated performance deficit to Mercedes is around 20 horsepower — a significant gap that cannot be closed by aerodynamics alone. ADUO approval would allow Ferrari to begin closing that gap through the season.

Can Ferrari make up the deficit to Mercedes by Miami? The Monza test will be the first real indicator.
