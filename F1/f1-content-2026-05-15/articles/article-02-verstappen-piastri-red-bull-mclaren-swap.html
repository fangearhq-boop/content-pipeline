The Verstappen-Piastri Swap: Is Formula 1's Biggest Driver Deal Actually Happening?

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | May 15, 2026

A few weeks ago, the idea of Max Verstappen leaving Red Bull for McLaren while Oscar Piastri moved in the opposite direction would have seemed like the kind of transfer fantasy that fills F1 fan forums. Today it reads as a credible possibility that insiders are taking seriously. And with Gianpiero Lambiase now confirmed for McLaren, the conditions for exactly that scenario are aligning in ways that would have seemed impossible eighteen months ago.

Here is where each piece of the puzzle stands right now.

Verstappen's Position at Red Bull

Max Verstappen is seventh in the 2026 Drivers' Championship with 26 points — 74 behind leader Kimi Antonelli. The Red Bull RB22 has not matched the pace of the Mercedes W17 or the McLaren MCL40 since the new regulations came into force. Verstappen's contract runs through 2028, but it contains an exit clause: he can terminate the deal early if he is not within the top two of the championship by the time the Hungarian Grand Prix concludes in late July.

He is currently sixth — the clause trigger is becoming increasingly plausible unless Red Bull produces a significant performance breakthrough in the next two months.

Red Bull's Piastri Pursuit

Red Bull have reportedly shortlisted Oscar Piastri as their preferred Verstappen replacement. The logic is not complicated: Piastri is 25 years old, under contract with McLaren through 2027, and has already demonstrated the speed and racecraft to compete for a world title. His manager, Mark Webber, is a former Red Bull driver himself — a connection that makes the conversation less awkward than it might otherwise be.

McLaren, for their part, have built an environment around Norris and Piastri that would be difficult to disrupt. Both are contracted. Zak Brown has said McLaren are in the market to "attract talent" — a quote many interpreted as a reference to Verstappen himself — but a unilateral release of Piastri is not something McLaren would accept unless offered something genuinely transformational in return.

The McLaren-Verstappen Angle

Veteran F1 journalist Joe Saward has reported that Verstappen has held preliminary talks with McLaren about a future move. McLaren already employs Newey's design heir in Marshall. Lambiase is joining in 2028. Verstappen has previously worked alongside Norris in the paddock and the two have maintained a professional relationship despite their competitive history.

A Verstappen-McLaren partnership — with Lambiase as team principal — would be a reunion of the key personnel from Red Bull's dominant era, now operating under a different letterhead. For McLaren, signing the four-time world champion would represent a dramatic statement of intent. For Verstappen, joining the team that just stole his closest collaborator might be the most fitting possible response.

The Timeline

Verstappen's exit clause at the Hungarian GP (July 24-26) is the first decision point. If he is not in the top two of the championship by that date — and the math suggests he would need a dramatic Red Bull performance surge to get there — the mechanism exists to trigger a departure. Any move would be for 2027 at the earliest given contract structures on all sides.

The next three months may determine the shape of Formula 1 for the next decade.

What's Next

The Hungarian Grand Prix at the end of July is Verstappen's exit clause deadline. Between now and then, Red Bull must demonstrate competitive progress or risk triggering the clause. McLaren will need to decide whether Verstappen arriving means Piastri leaving, and on what terms. Piastri's response to Red Bull's interest has not been publicly confirmed. Formula 1's off-track story is moving faster than many of its on-track ones.
