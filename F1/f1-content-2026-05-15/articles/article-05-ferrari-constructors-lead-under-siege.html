Ferrari's Constructors Lead Has Collapsed — Canada Must Be a Reset

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | May 15, 2026

After the Japanese Grand Prix, Ferrari held a 44-point lead in the Formula 1 Constructors' Championship. It was a cushion that felt secure — a testament to consistent points-scoring from Charles Leclerc and Lewis Hamilton across the opening rounds of the new regulations era. Then came Miami. And in a single chaotic final lap, most of that cushion evaporated.

Ferrari's constructors' lead now stands at 16 points. It is not a crisis yet. But it is a warning.

What Happened in Miami

Charles Leclerc was running fourth in the Miami Grand Prix's closing stages when he spun at Turn Three on the final lap, sustaining car damage. The spin itself was costly enough — he recovered but fell back toward the other competitors. Then the stewards reviewed his final laps and determined he had repeatedly cut the circuit during the closing period, issuing a 20-second post-race penalty under Article B1.8.6 of the Sporting Regulations.

The penalty dropped Leclerc from fourth to eighth in the final classification. Ferrari went from collecting the points of a fourth-place finish to the points of an eighth-place finish — a loss of 8 constructor points. Hamilton, meanwhile, crossed the line sixth after carrying floor and sidepod damage from a first-lap collision with Alpine's Franco Colapinto.

"The last-lap mistake is all on me," Leclerc said after the race. He demanded a detailed internal investigation to prevent a recurrence. Leclerc's accountability is admirable. But the damage to the constructors' standing was real.

McLaren's Rise Is Not Slowing

The reason Ferrari's lead shrunk so dramatically is not only about Ferrari's own points loss — it is also about McLaren's performance. Lando Norris finished second at Miami, Oscar Piastri was third. McLaren collected 33 points from the race while Ferrari collected 8. That is a swing of 25 points in a single grand prix.

McLaren is the fastest-improving team in the 2026 field on current data. They arrived in Miami with a significant upgrade package and are bringing a second package to Canada. If the MCL40 reaches the performance level of the Mercedes W17 by mid-season, Ferrari's constructors' lead — even at 16 points — may become very difficult to defend.

The Canada Opportunity

Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve has historically been favorable to Ferrari over the longer arc of the sport's history, and Leclerc has managed strong results there across his career. A clean, mistake-free weekend from both Ferrari drivers in Montreal would restore some of the constructors' cushion and send a message that Miami was an aberration rather than a trend.

For Hamilton, Canada is a circuit where his technical precision and smoothness can shine. His Miami race was compromised from the opening lap. A clean run from the front — or at minimum a podium — would remind rivals that Ferrari has two genuine weapons in the constructors' battle.

Ferrari has the talent and the machinery to compete. But they can no longer afford the kind of self-inflicted damage that Miami delivered. The margin for error is shrinking, and McLaren is accelerating.

What's Next

Ferrari heads to the 2026 Canadian Grand Prix (May 22-24) with a 16-point constructors' lead over McLaren. The sprint race on May 23 and the main race on May 24 offer 34 points total — a single weekend that could either rebuild Ferrari's cushion or eliminate it entirely. The constructors' championship remains one of the closest battles of the 2026 season, and Canada is the most important test Ferrari has faced since the new regulations began.
