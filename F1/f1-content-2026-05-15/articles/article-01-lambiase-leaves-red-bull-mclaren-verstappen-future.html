Lambiase to McLaren: The Engineer Bombshell That Could End Verstappen's Red Bull Era

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 15, 2026

Gianpiero Lambiase is leaving Red Bull. The man who has been Max Verstappen's race engineer since 2016 — the voice on the other end of the radio through four consecutive World Championship campaigns — will depart for McLaren at the end of 2027, where he will become team principal. The announcement sent shockwaves through the paddock and immediately raised the question that Formula 1 has been dancing around for months: will Verstappen follow?

Verstappen has said, clearly and on multiple occasions, that he would retire from Formula 1 when Lambiase leaves. That departure is now confirmed and scheduled. The only question is whether Verstappen's words become action.

Ten Years of Partnership

The Lambiase-Verstappen relationship is one of the most successful driver-engineer partnerships in the history of the sport. When Red Bull promoted Verstappen from Toro Rosso in 2016, Lambiase was assigned as his engineer — a pairing that initially required significant diplomatic management given how blunt both men could be with each other on the radio. Over time, that directness became the foundation of an extraordinarily effective working relationship.

Together they won the 2021, 2022, 2023, and 2024 World Championships. Each campaign involved Lambiase guiding strategy from the pit wall, managing Verstappen's tire management, keeping him composed under pressure, and delivering the kind of calm technical clarity that allowed Verstappen to extract maximum performance from any car he drove.

Red Bull confirmed that Lambiase will remain in his dual role as Head of Racing and race engineer until his contract expires at the end of 2027. But everyone understands that confirmation is a formality. The relationship is effectively in its final chapter.

McLaren's Talent Accumulation

McLaren's recruitment of Lambiase is the latest in a series of high-profile appointments that have transformed the team from Lando Norris's 2025 championship-winning outfit into arguably the best-resourced team on the grid heading into the next cycle. Before Lambiase, they hired Rob Marshall as Technical Director in 2024 — a man who was Red Bull's chief designer during the ground effect era of dominance. They added Will Courtenay as Sporting Director at the start of 2026.

The pattern is obvious and deliberate. McLaren are building an infrastructure around their improving car that can sustain championship-level performance for years. Adding a team principal of Lambiase's technical depth and driver relationship expertise is an extraordinary coup.

The Verstappen Variable

Verstappen currently sits seventh in the 2026 Drivers' Championship with 26 points. His Red Bull car has not found the performance level of the Mercedes or McLaren machinery since the regulation reset at the start of this season. He has a contract through 2028, but an exit clause allows him to leave if he is not inside the top two of the championship at the Hungarian Grand Prix in late July.

With Lambiase now publicly committed to McLaren, with Red Bull circling Oscar Piastri as a potential Verstappen replacement, and with reports of preliminary talks between Verstappen's camp and McLaren already circulating, the pieces of one of the most dramatic driver market stories in Formula 1 history are all in motion.

Whether or not Verstappen formally acts on his stated retirement intention, the Lambiase announcement has fundamentally shifted the conversation about the sport's next chapter.

What's Next

Lambiase will remain at Red Bull through 2027, and Verstappen is under contract through 2028. The exit clause at the Hungarian Grand Prix (July 24-26) is the first potential decision point. If Verstappen activates it, a move to McLaren in 2027 alongside Lambiase becomes the most logical destination. Red Bull's response — and whether Piastri would accept a move away from McLaren — will be the next major development in this story. Formula 1 enters its most complex off-track period in decades.
