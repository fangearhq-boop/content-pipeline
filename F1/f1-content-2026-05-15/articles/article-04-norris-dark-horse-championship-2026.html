Lando Norris Is 49 Points Back and Closing — Don't Count Out the Champion

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 15, 2026

The narrative of the 2026 Formula 1 season has been dominated by Kimi Antonelli's stunning three-race winning streak from pole, and the internal Mercedes battle between Antonelli and George Russell for the team's favor and the championship lead. In all of that, it is easy to lose sight of a player sitting quietly in the background — Lando Norris, the defending world champion, who is 49 points behind Antonelli with more than half the season still to run.

Norris finished second at the Miami Grand Prix. His McLaren is getting faster. And he has been here before.

The 2025 Champion Knows How This Works

Lando Norris won the 2025 World Championship in one of the closest finishes in fifteen years — edging Max Verstappen by two points in Abu Dhabi. He knows what it feels like to need a specific result in the final race of the season, and he also knows what it takes to build a championship campaign over a full year rather than chasing individual grand prix wins.

The 2026 season has not started as Norris hoped. Antonelli's pace and consistency have been remarkable, and McLaren needed time to bed in the new regulations relative to Mercedes. But the Miami Grand Prix showed signs of a turning point. Norris qualified on the front row, finished second, and demonstrated in the closing stages that the MCL40 has the pace to run with the Mercedes W17.

The Math That Makes Norris Dangerous

The Mercedes internal battle is Norris's best friend. Every time Antonelli and Russell fight each other through the field — taking points from each other, potentially colliding, burning tire life in wheel-to-wheel combat — McLaren benefits from a clean run. Norris collects 25 points unimpeded while Mercedes collects 37 between two drivers. The net swing is smaller than the headline gap suggests.

If Norris wins in Canada while Antonelli is second and Russell is third, the gap closes to approximately 32 points. If McLaren bring their second upgrade package and it performs as hoped, a repeat of that scenario is not implausible. String three or four of those together in the summer months, and Norris is back in serious contention heading into the second half of the season.

Canada as a Proving Ground

Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve has been a strong circuit for McLaren in recent seasons. The papaya cars tend to perform well on circuits with a mixture of slow-speed corners and long acceleration zones, and Montreal provides both. If the second part of the Miami upgrade package — arriving this weekend — delivers the performance step McLaren is projecting, Canada could be the weekend that officially reinserts Norris into the title conversation.

He has won before under pressure at the end of a long season. He is 25 points behind Russell in the championship, which means he is also fighting for second place overall. And he has a team principal and car that is trending in the right direction.

What's Next

Norris arrives at the 2026 Canadian Grand Prix as one of the favorites to win the race outright. With 34 points available across the sprint and the main race and his car improving, a strong Montreal weekend could bring him within striking distance of both Russell and Antonelli heading into the European summer stretch. The championship is not over. Not by a long way.
