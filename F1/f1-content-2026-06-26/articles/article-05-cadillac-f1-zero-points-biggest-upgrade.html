Cadillac F1 Brings Their Biggest Upgrade to Austria in a Season of Zero Points

By Marcus Cole June 26, 2026

A Season That Was Supposed to Be Different

Seven races into the 2026 Formula 1 season, Cadillac F1 remains the only team in the field without a single championship point. When General Motors brought the Cadillac brand into Formula 1 as the first American constructor since 1986, the expectation was a competitive debut — not a winless, pointless run through the opening third of the season.

Now at the Red Bull Ring for the Austrian Grand Prix, Cadillac is bringing the biggest upgrade package of their young existence as a Formula 1 team. Whether it is enough to finally move them off zero is the question that will define how the second half of their debut season begins.

The Lap-15 Problem

Cadillac's fundamental issue is specific: their car loses rear stability around lap 15, far earlier than any other team. Where competitors see gradual tire degradation across 20 to 30 laps, Cadillac hits a wall. Performance collapses rather than fades, costing them significant race pace in the crucial second stint of every grand prix.

The upgrade package targets two areas. First, revised floor geometry to improve rear downforce stability in the load range where the car currently goes unstable. Second, rear suspension changes intended to reduce rear tire thermal stress in high-speed corners. Engineering staff have modeled an improvement in the degradation window of between four and six laps, which would push the cliff from lap 15 to lap 19 or 20 — close enough to a normal degradation curve to allow competitive strategy options.

Where They Stand in Context

Williams has scored points twice this season. Haas has scored. Audi, in only their second full year as a rebranded works outfit, is on the board. The teams historically associated with the back of the grid have found their way to points. Cadillac has not.

The Red Bull Ring's 4.3-kilometer layout was chosen as the debut circuit for the upgrade because its stint lengths and track temperature profile align with the window where the engineering team expects the floor change to show its benefit before the degradation cliff normally arrives. It is not a guaranteed points finish. It is an opportunity to show that the upgrade direction is correct and that the season has a second half worth anticipating.

What's Next

FP1 and FP2 on Friday, June 26, give Cadillac their first live data on the upgrade package under race-weekend conditions. Saturday qualifying reveals where they genuinely sit relative to the field. If the upgrade delivers even a portion of its modeled performance, Cadillac expects to be fighting at the edge of the points zone for the first time in their Formula 1 history. The Austrian GP race on Sunday, June 28, is the moment of truth — either a turning point for the program, or seven consecutive pointless rounds that extend to eight going into the summer break.
