### STORY 5: Canada Sprint Debut

**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM ET)**
Buckle up. The Canadian Grand Prix just got even bigger.

The 2026 Formula 1 Canadian Grand Prix (May 22-24 at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve in Montreal) will host the track's first-ever sprint race. In a tradition stretching back to 1978, this legendary circuit has never had an F1 sprint event — until now.

Here's what the weekend looks like:
— Friday: Practice + Sprint Qualifying
— Saturday: Sprint Race + Main Qualifying
— Sunday: Grand Prix (70 laps)

With approximately 34 points available across the sprint and the main race, Montreal has the potential to dramatically reshape the championship standings in one weekend. Antonelli (100 pts) vs. Russell (80) vs. Leclerc (59) vs. Hamilton/Norris (51) — every point at a sprint weekend counts double.

For fans, it means more action, more strategy decisions, more drama. Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve is already one of the most exciting venues on the calendar. Add a sprint race to the mix and you have something truly special.
