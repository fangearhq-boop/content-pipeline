Canada GP Goes Sprint: Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve Hosts Its First-Ever F1 Sprint Race on May 23

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | May 14, 2026

Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve has been a fixture on the Formula 1 calendar since 1978. In 48 years of hosting grand prix racing, it has never had an F1 sprint race. That changes on May 23, 2026 — eight days from now — when Montreal becomes the site of the third sprint race of the 2026 Formula 1 season.

The Sprint Format at a Legendary Venue

The 2026 Canadian Grand Prix sprint weekend runs across three days. On Friday, drivers complete free practice and then compete in sprint qualifying — a short, knockout format session that sets the grid for Saturday's sprint race. On Saturday, the sprint itself takes place before teams and drivers reset for main qualifying, which determines Sunday's grand prix grid. Sunday brings the grand prix over the circuit's full distance.

The sprint race itself runs for approximately 100 kilometres — considerably shorter than the main event, but fast, strategic, and fully points-scoring. The combination of sprint and grand prix points makes a sprint weekend significantly more valuable than a standard race weekend in championship terms.

Points That Could Change Everything

With roughly 34 points available across the sprint and main race, the Canadian Grand Prix can shift the 2026 championship picture dramatically in a single weekend. Consider the current standings: Kimi Antonelli leads with 100 points, George Russell is second at 80. A maximum Montreal weekend for Russell combined with a poor one for Antonelli could narrow or eliminate the gap entirely — before the summer break.

The same dynamic applies in the constructors' championship, where Ferrari leads Mercedes by 16 points. A sprint weekend with upgrades and optimal performance from both Mercedes drivers could erase that lead over 72 hours of racing.

Why Gilles-Villeneuve Suits the Sprint Format

Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve is a semi-street circuit built on the Île Notre-Dame in the St. Lawrence River. It is characterised by tight chicanes, long straights, and a wall — the famous Wall of Champions — that penalises even the slightest error. These traits translate well to the sprint format: overtaking is possible on the straights, strategy is compressed, and mistakes are immediately visible.

Unlike some circuits where sprint races produce processional racing, Montreal's layout encourages close-quarters racing. The debut sprint race at Gilles-Villeneuve is not simply a championship-points exercise. It has a genuine chance of being spectacular.

What's Next

The 2026 Canadian Grand Prix weekend: Friday, May 22 — Free Practice and Sprint Qualifying; Saturday, May 23 — Sprint Race and Grand Prix Qualifying; Sunday, May 24 — Grand Prix. Key subplots: Can Russell reverse Antonelli's form at his best circuit? Can Ferrari stem the points bleed? Can McLaren continue closing on the constructors' podium? All of it begins in eight days at one of the greatest circuits in the sport's history.
