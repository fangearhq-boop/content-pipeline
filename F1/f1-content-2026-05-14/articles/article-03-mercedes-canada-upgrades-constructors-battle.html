Mercedes Arrives at Canadian Grand Prix With Major Upgrade Package — Ferrari's Constructors Lead Under Threat

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | May 14, 2026

Mercedes already leads the constructors' championship. They already have the fastest car. They already have the championship leader in Kimi Antonelli. And now, with the Canadian Grand Prix eight days away, they are coming to Montreal with a significant upgrade package that could deepen every advantage they already hold.

According to reports from the paddock, the Silver Arrows plan to bring considerably more development to Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve than the two upgrades that appeared in Miami. The Canadian GP is also a sprint weekend — meaning the upgrades will face immediate, high-intensity testing across every session.

What This Means for the Championship Fight

Ferrari currently holds a 16-point lead in the constructors' championship over Mercedes. That number sounds more secure than it is — after the Japanese Grand Prix, the gap was 44 points. In the space of one race weekend in Miami, following Leclerc's 20-second post-race penalty and Hamilton's collision-hampered sixth-place finish, that cushion was cut nearly in half.

A sprint weekend in Montreal, where Mercedes is bringing genuine technical upgrades, adds another layer of risk for Maranello. With approximately 34 points available across the sprint and the main race, a dominant Mercedes weekend could erase Ferrari's remaining constructors' lead in a single trip.

McLaren Is Still Watching

The constructors' battle is not a two-team fight. McLaren — with Lando Norris in fourth in the drivers' standings and Oscar Piastri in sixth — sits behind Ferrari in the constructors' table, but their 7-part Miami upgrade was considered a major step. If Mercedes is bringing another significant package and McLaren continues developing, Ferrari faces pressure from two directions simultaneously.

The Scuderia's already-shaken confidence after Miami's double disappointment — Leclerc's penalty, Hamilton's damage — means they arrive in Canada needing a strong showing just to hold position, let alone fight back.

The Intra-Team Dynamic at Mercedes

The upgrades will benefit both Antonelli and Russell, which adds an interesting wrinkle to the intra-team dynamics. Russell is widely considered one of the strongest drivers at a high-speed, low-grip circuit like Gilles-Villeneuve — he won here in 2025. If the upgrade helps his particular driving style disproportionately, he has a chance to cut into Antonelli's championship lead. If Antonelli adapts to the new package faster, he could extend it.

In either case, Montreal may prove decisive for the shape of the 2026 title race.

What's Next

The 2026 Canadian Grand Prix runs from May 22-24 at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve in Montreal. It is the sixth round of the season and the third sprint weekend. The constructors' standings heading in: Ferrari leads Mercedes by 16 points, with McLaren chasing both. The upgrade packages coming to Montreal could change all three of those numbers before the race is done.
