### STORY 1: Antonelli Makes F1 History — Youngest Championship Leader Ever
- **Tier:** 1
- **Status:** FOLLOW-UP (3/29 Antonelli win)
- **Angle:** Historic milestone deepens; championship lead after Japan; Mercedes vs. Mercedes title fight
- **Posting window:** 8:00 AM ET

**Summary:**
Kimi Antonelli's Japanese GP victory now carries an even bigger headline: the 19-year-old is the youngest driver to ever lead the Formula 1 World Championship. At 19 years and 216 days, he surpassed Lewis Hamilton's record of 22 years and 5 months set at the 2007 Spanish GP — the same Hamilton he replaced at Mercedes. Antonelli leads with 72 points to Russell's 63, a 9-point gap after 3 of 22 races. He's also the first Italian to lead the standings since Giancarlo Fisichella in 2005. His post-race quote: "It's still early days to think about the championship, but we're on a good way."

### STORY 2: Bearman's 50G Crash Exposes Safety Crisis
- **Tier:** 1
- **Status:** FOLLOW-UP (3/29 Bearman DNF)
- **Angle:** Safety controversy; FIA response; 2026 regulations under scrutiny
- **Posting window:** 10:00 AM ET

**Summary:**
Oliver Bearman's 50G crash at Spoon Curve on lap 22 has triggered the most significant safety debate of the 2026 season. The crash was caused by a 45 km/h speed differential between Bearman's Haas and Colapinto's Alpine, created by differing electrical energy harvest/deploy states — the Alpine's rear warning lights were not illuminated, giving Bearman no indication of the speed difference. Bearman escaped with bruising. GPDA director Carlos Sainz demanded action. The FIA confirmed it will hold meetings in April to assess the energy deployment regulations. Piastri said he had a similar near-miss in practice with Hülkenberg.
