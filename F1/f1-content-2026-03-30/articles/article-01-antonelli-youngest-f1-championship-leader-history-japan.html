Antonelli Becomes Youngest F1 Championship Leader in History After Japanese GP Victory

By F1 Fanrecap Staff | March 30, 2026

He replaced Lewis Hamilton. Now he has broken his record.

Kimi Antonelli is the youngest driver in Formula 1 history to lead the World Championship, a status he earned with Sunday's Japanese Grand Prix victory at Suzuka. At 19 years and 216 days, the Mercedes teenager obliterated the record set by Hamilton himself — 22 years and 5 months — during his 2007 rookie season. That Antonelli broke this record in the same car Hamilton drove for over a decade gives the achievement a quality that fiction writers would struggle to invent.

Antonelli leads the 2026 drivers' standings with 72 points, nine clear of his Mercedes teammate George Russell. It is only the third race of the season. There are 19 more to go.

How He Won at Suzuka

The Japanese GP win was not without fortune, but Antonelli had the pace to deserve it even if circumstances had not intervened. Starting from pole position, he suffered a terrible launch and slipped to sixth on the opening lap — the third consecutive race start in which the Mercedes cars have been beaten off the line due to the new 2026 turbo spool requirements. He recovered through the field, but it was the safety car triggered by Oliver Bearman's heavy crash on lap 22 that proved decisive.

Oscar Piastri and George Russell had already pitted from the front of the race when the safety car appeared. Antonelli, who was leading the queue of cars that hadn't yet stopped, took a free pit stop and rejoined at the head of the field. From there, he was faultless — pulling 14 seconds clear of Piastri by the chequered flag and recording the fastest lap of the race (1:32.432) in the process.

"It feels pretty good," Antonelli said on the podium. "Of course it's still early days to think about the championship, but we're on a good way. In the race, I had a terrible start — I just need to check what happened — but then I was lucky with the Safety Car. The pace was incredible in the second stint. I felt very good with the car."

A Record That Puts the Career in Perspective

The championship lead milestone is the latest in a string of records Antonelli has accumulated since arriving in Formula 1 last season. He is also the first Italian to lead the F1 standings since Giancarlo Fisichella after the 2005 Australian Grand Prix — a detail that resonates deeply in a country where Ferrari's struggles have long dominated the motorsport narrative.

At the Suzuka podium, Antonelli was denied the customary champagne celebrations because Japan's legal drinking age is 20. He was handed an unlabeled bottle — assumed to be sparkling water — instead. The image of the youngest championship leader in history raising a bottle of water rather than champagne captured the extraordinary moment perfectly. He is a teenager making history in a sport that has never seen anything like him.

What's Next

Formula 1 takes a five-week break following the Japanese GP — the longest gap in the 2026 calendar, created by the cancellation of the Bahrain and Saudi Arabian rounds due to the conflict in the Middle East. The next race is the Miami Grand Prix on May 1-3, a Sprint weekend that will see additional championship points on offer.

Antonelli's focus will be on the one area that still needs work: his race starts. Three races, three suboptimal launches. The Mercedes W17 is fast enough to absorb those losses, but in a closer championship fight, start issues could prove costly. He acknowledged as much post-race, saying he needs to "check what happened" at each getaway.

With 19 races remaining and a nine-point lead, Kimi Antonelli enters the Miami break as the most exciting young driver Formula 1 has ever seen. He is 19 years old. He leads the world championship. And he just made history in the country where motor racing legends are made.
