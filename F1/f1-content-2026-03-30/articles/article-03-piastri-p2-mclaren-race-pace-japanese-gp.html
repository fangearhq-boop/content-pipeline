Piastri Leads 22 Laps and Finishes P2 in First 2026 Race Start — McLaren Is Mercedes' Closest Rival

By F1 Fanrecap Staff | March 30, 2026

Oscar Piastri arrived at Suzuka having failed to start either of the first two races of the 2026 season. A formation lap crash in Australia and an electrical failure in China cost him both attempts. On Sunday, he started his first race of the year — and nearly won it.

Piastri launched from fourth on the grid, outgunned both Mercedes cars in the initial sprint to Turn 1, and took the race lead before the first corner sequence was complete. He then led cleanly for 22 laps, managing the gap, managing his tyres, and managing his electrical deployment with what he called strategic precision. Kimi Antonelli, who started from pole and dropped to sixth at the start, was not even in the picture for the first half of the race.

What denied Piastri the win was not speed but timing. McLaren brought him in on lap 18 for fresh tyres. Two laps later, George Russell stopped. Then Oliver Bearman crashed at Spoon Curve on lap 22, triggering the safety car. Antonelli, still running on old tyres at the head of the queue, took a free stop and rejoined as race leader. On the restart, he found pace Piastri couldn't match. Final result: Antonelli by 14 seconds, Piastri in second.

The Best He's Ever Been

"I think this weekend was probably one of my best weekends in F1," Piastri said after the race. "I felt like I hit the ground running in practice and qualifying. In the race, there wasn't anything more we could have done. We got a good start, the pace was good. I felt like I was strategic with how I used the boost. Our strategy was good, we didn't blink too early."

The nuance in that last phrase is important. McLaren resisted the temptation to pit early and surrender their strategic initiative. They played the hand they were dealt perfectly. What they couldn't account for was a safety car that arrived at precisely the worst possible moment for them and the best possible moment for Antonelli.

Lando Norris added P5 to give McLaren both cars in the top five, and the Woking outfit now sit third in the constructors' standings with 56 points.

The Bigger Picture

What Suzuka confirmed is that McLaren has genuine race pace to challenge Mercedes. Not just in one-lap qualifying — Piastri was matching Russell and Antonelli in race trim while leading for over a third of the grand prix. The 2026 McLaren, once McLaren sorted the reliability issues that plagued their opening two rounds, is a proper racing car.

That is significant for the remainder of the season. Mercedes is dominant, but Sunday showed they are not untouchable. Piastri very nearly won from P4 on the grid in his first race of the year. If McLaren can fix the issues that prevented both cars from competing in Australia and China, the constructors' championship will be more than a two-team race.

What's Next

The next race is the Miami Grand Prix on May 3 — five weeks away following the cancellation of the Bahrain and Saudi Arabian rounds. McLaren will use every day of that gap for development work. For a team that enters Miami with momentum, genuine pace, and a driver in P2 who is already calling the weekend one of his best in Formula 1, the Florida Sprint weekend looms as a crucial opportunity.

Piastri is still looking for his first race win of 2026. Given what he showed at Suzuka, Miami could be the place where he finds it.
