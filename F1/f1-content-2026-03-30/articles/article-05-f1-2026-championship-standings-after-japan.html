F1 2026 Championship Standings After Japan — Antonelli Leads, Red Bull in Crisis, McLaren Emerging

By F1 Fanrecap Staff | March 30, 2026

Three of 22 races are complete in the 2026 Formula 1 World Championship. Here is a full picture of where every team and driver stands heading into the five-week break before the Miami Grand Prix.

Drivers' Championship

Kimi Antonelli leads the championship with 72 points after three races, nine clear of his Mercedes teammate George Russell on 63. The gap reflects their race results: Russell won Australia and came second in China, while Antonelli took victories in China and Japan. The intra-team battle between the 19-year-old Italian and the 28-year-old Briton is the championship's defining storyline.

Charles Leclerc sits third with 34 points and Lewis Hamilton fourth with 33. The Ferrari drivers have been consistently present at the front of the field and both appear capable of winning races if Mercedes has an off day. Leclerc finished on the podium in Japan; Hamilton was sixth after being an early podium threat before losing ground at the restart.

McLaren's Oscar Piastri enters Miami in a points position that barely reflects his pace — his first race start of 2026 resulted in P2, but two DNFs in the opening rounds have cost him significantly. Lando Norris has been more consistent but has not yet challenged for a win. Both drivers should be in the mix more regularly once the team's early-season reliability issues are behind them.

Max Verstappen is ninth in the championship with 12 points — a situation that would have seemed unthinkable before the 2026 season began. The four-time world champion is being held back entirely by the RB22's fundamental instability, not any lack of ability. Red Bull's best result from three races is Verstappen's P6 in Melbourne.

Constructors' Championship

Mercedes leads the constructors' standings with 135 points — 45 ahead of Ferrari's 90. The Silver Arrows have won every race so far and have both drivers in the top two. It is the most dominant opening to a season since their previous period of dominance in the hybrid era.

Ferrari's 90-point total keeps them in realistic contention for the constructors' title if Mercedes' advantage narrows. The Scuderia has two fast, experienced drivers and a car that appears to be within a development step of genuinely challenging the W17.

McLaren's 56 points place them third, 34 behind Ferrari. Given Piastri's pace on Sunday and the team's trajectory, closing that gap is entirely achievable. Red Bull's 16 points — Verstappen P8 in Japan, various points finishes elsewhere — represent the most surprising storyline in the sport.

What's Next

Formula 1 returns for the Miami Grand Prix on May 1-3, a Sprint weekend that adds additional championship points to the regular race weekend format. Miami will be the first genuine test of whether teams have been able to close gaps in the factory during the break.

With 475 championship points still available and 19 races remaining, no outcome is certain. But the early evidence is clear: Mercedes and its two drivers hold the dominant position in 2026 Formula 1. Whether the rest of the field can bridge the gap over the coming months will determine whether this season becomes a runaway title or a genuine championship fight.
