Verstappen's Exit Clause Is Live: Red Bull's Four-Time Champion Free to Negotiate as McLaren Talks Advance

By Ryan Calloway July 10, 2026

The Clause Has Triggered

Max Verstappen's performance exit clause in his Red Bull contract is no longer a theoretical concern — it is a mathematical reality. After the British Grand Prix, the numbers finalized what many had been anticipating since mid-June: Verstappen cannot reach the top two in the championship standings before the summer break, which means his exit clause has activated.

Verstappen sits seventh in the standings with 76 points. George Russell is second with 154. The gap between them is 78 points. Between now and the Hungarian Grand Prix on July 26 — the last race before the summer shutdown — 50 points are available. The arithmetic is conclusive. Red Bull cannot block his exit on performance grounds.

What the Clause Actually Means

The performance clause in Verstappen's Red Bull contract was designed as a safeguard. If he was not within a realistic range of the top two in the championship by the summer break, he would be permitted to seek a better situation. The clause does not allow him to walk away immediately — it allows him to negotiate with other teams and sign a contract for 2027 without Red Bull being able to legally obstruct him.

The formal window for those negotiations opens after the Hungarian Grand Prix. At that point, Verstappen can begin official discussions with any team — and those discussions, per SportBible reporting earlier this week, appear to have already been happening informally with McLaren for some time. The outlet described the state of those talks as being in the "closing stages."

The Piastri Complication

The simplest version of the Verstappen-to-McLaren story assumed a direct swap with Oscar Piastri, who would go to Red Bull in exchange. That version now appears to be off the table. Multiple sources reported Thursday that Piastri is staying at McLaren. His camp and the team both indicated "nothing has changed" about his relationship with McLaren, and CEO Zak Brown reiterated at the British Grand Prix that he has no desire to change his driver lineup.

That leaves McLaren with two contracted drivers — Lando Norris and Piastri — and no obvious vacancy for the most in-demand driver on the market. How Verstappen, if talks are genuinely in closing stages, would actually fit into a McLaren seat is the central question no one has answered publicly. One possibility is a 2028 start date rather than 2027, but Verstappen's clause creates urgency around a 2027 move. Another is that there is a mechanism not yet in the public domain that changes the Norris or Piastri picture.

Red Bull's Situation

Red Bull are "annoyed" as the rumors intensify, per Total Motorsport. The team has invested enormous resources in developing Verstappen into a four-time world champion, and watching him publicly depart through a contractual performance clause is not the narrative they want. Laurent Mekies, who joined from Ferrari, called the situation "very unpleasant" earlier in the season.

At Silverstone, Verstappen's frustration with the car boiled over on team radio after he crashed out. The emotional dimension of his exit is no longer being hidden. The question is not whether he wants to leave Red Bull — it is where he goes when he does.

What's Next

The Hungarian Grand Prix runs on July 26. After that race, the clause is formally activatable and any team can make Verstappen an official offer. McLaren's position — publicly denying while reported sources say "closing stages" — will either be revealed as strategic misdirection or genuine separation between perception and reality. The summer break is three weeks long. When Formula 1 returns in late August for the Dutch Grand Prix, the landscape will likely have changed significantly. Verstappen's destination is the most consequential decision in the sport right now.
