Six Wins, One New Color: Hamilton Heads to Spa in Ferrari Red for the First Time

By Elena Voss July 10, 2026

The Record That Belongs to Him

No active Formula 1 driver has won more Belgian Grand Prix races than Lewis Hamilton. He has won six times at the Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps, the legendary 7.004-kilometer circuit in the Ardennes region of Belgium. The record is his, and when the 2026 Belgian Grand Prix begins on July 19, he will pursue a seventh victory in a way the circuit has never seen from him before: wearing red.

Hamilton's move from Mercedes to Ferrari at the start of 2026 was one of the most significant transfers in the sport's history. His first Ferrari season has not been without complications — adapting to a new team, a new culture, and a new car always takes time — but the trajectory has been positive. He finished third at Silverstone two weeks ago, behind only Leclerc and Russell, and he arrives at Spa with Ferrari carrying genuine momentum after their first race victory of the season.

Why Spa Matters for the Championship

Hamilton sits third in the 2026 Drivers' Championship with 147 points, 32 behind championship leader Kimi Antonelli and 7 behind George Russell. The gap is real but not fatal — there are 11 races remaining after Belgium, and Antonelli's Silverstone reliability failure has reminded everyone that the Mercedes championship lead is not guaranteed.

A Belgian Grand Prix win for Hamilton, combined with another difficult day for Antonelli, would close the championship gap to around 25 points and make the second half of the season an entirely different conversation. Spa is worth 25 points to the winner. Winning it matters for more than nostalgia.

What Makes Spa Different From Any Other Circuit

The Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps is one of the most demanding and exhilarating tracks in motor racing. Its 44-lap race distance covers almost 308 kilometers through the Ardennes hills, combining low-speed technical sections with high-speed corners that no other venue on the calendar replicates. Raidillon — the right-hand kink at the top of Eau Rouge — remains one of the most breathtaking sections of the championship. The Kemmel Straight offers genuine overtaking opportunities. The infield section through Bus Stop provides strategy and setup complications that separate the good from the great.

Hamilton has earned his record here through a combination of raw pace and racecraft. He has won at Spa in wet conditions, in mixed strategies, and in straightforward dominant fashion. The circuit rewards experience, and no active driver has more experience winning here.

Ferrari and the Spa Challenge

Historically, Spa has favored power unit strength and straight-line speed. Mercedes built their reputation at this circuit during their dominant period in part because their engine advantage expressed itself most clearly at power-sensitive tracks. Ferrari's 2026 power unit has been competitive throughout the season, and the team's technical development has closed the gap considerably. After Leclerc's British GP win, Ferrari's engineering team will arrive at Spa with data showing their car can compete at the front.

Weather is a factor worth watching. Forecasters have projected thundery showers and light winds through the Belgian GP weekend, with temperatures around 23°C. Wet weather at Spa has historically suited Hamilton, who has shown particular brilliance in mixed-conditions races throughout his career.

What's Next

The Belgian Grand Prix runs July 17-19 at Spa-Francorchamps, with free practice beginning Friday. It is the final race before the summer shutdown and one of the most important opportunities of 2026 for Hamilton to make a championship statement. Six wins already. New car, new colors, new chapter. If Hamilton can add to his Spa record this season, it will be one of the most resonant victories in his extraordinary career — not in spite of the Ferrari switch, but because of it.
