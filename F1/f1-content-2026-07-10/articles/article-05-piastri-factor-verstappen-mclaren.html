The Piastri Factor: McLaren's Australian Star Is the Key to the Verstappen Puzzle

By Marcus Cole July 10, 2026

The Confirmation That Complicates Everything

The most straightforward version of the Max Verstappen-to-McLaren story required one thing: Oscar Piastri going to Red Bull in a straight swap. On Thursday, that version of the story became significantly harder to maintain. Multiple sources from within McLaren and within Piastri's inner circle confirmed to RacingNews365 that "nothing has changed" about the Australian's relationship with the team. He is staying.

McLaren CEO Zak Brown made the same point at the British Grand Prix, denying any desire to alter his driver lineup. McLaren currently fields Lando Norris and Piastri — two of the most talented drivers in Formula 1, who have been developing alongside each other for several seasons. The team has results, internal chemistry, and no obvious reason to disrupt what has been working.

The Math That Doesn't Add Up

This creates a problem for the "closing stages" Verstappen/McLaren narrative. If Piastri is staying and Norris has not been mentioned as a departure, McLaren has no open seat. Verstappen's exit clause is active, his desire to leave Red Bull is clear, and SportBible's sources describe his negotiations with McLaren as being in "closing stages." Yet the team he is supposedly about to join has both of its current drivers publicly committed to staying.

There are a limited number of scenarios that resolve this contradiction. One is that one of the current drivers has a contract provision — a performance clause of their own, or an opt-out — that the public is not aware of. Another is that the "closing stages" reporting is premature or inaccurate, and the deal is less advanced than the language suggests. A third possibility is that the timeline extends to 2028, with Verstappen signing now for a future start date while one of the current drivers exits after next season.

Why Piastri Is the Central Figure

In a straightforward Verstappen-to-McLaren deal, Piastri going to Red Bull was the mechanism that made the finances and logistics work. Red Bull would get a top-tier replacement for Verstappen in Piastri. McLaren would get the most decorated active driver. Everyone would benefit, with the possible exception of Norris, who might find himself racing alongside an even higher-profile teammate than Piastri.

Now that Piastri has reportedly confirmed his McLaren future, Red Bull lacks the replacement candidate that made the swap logical. The teams would have to find a different arrangement — perhaps Red Bull signing someone else from the mid-field to replace Verstappen, while McLaren makes room through different means. None of those alternate arrangements are as clean or as mutually beneficial as the Piastri swap.

What Verstappen Said at Silverstone

At the British Grand Prix, Verstappen crashed out of the race and delivered an expletive-filled team radio message about his car's performance. He has repeatedly expressed frustration with Red Bull's inability to provide him with a race-winning car in 2026. The emotional case for his departure is not in doubt. The contractual mechanism for that departure is now active.

What remains unclear is whether the McLaren negotiations will produce a deal that requires Piastri to move — which Piastri and McLaren deny — or whether this story finds a different resolution entirely. Formula 1's driver market has historically produced outcomes that looked logistically impossible in the weeks before they were announced. The Hungarian Grand Prix is in sixteen days.

What's Next

The summer break following July 26 is when official negotiations between Verstappen and any team can formally begin. McLaren, Red Bull, and Verstappen's management team will be central figures in whatever resolution emerges. Piastri's confirmation of his McLaren stay makes the simple swap less likely — but in Formula 1's driver market, simple stories rarely survive contact with reality. Watch the Hungarian GP weekend for any signals that the picture has shifted. The paddock will be talking about nothing else.
