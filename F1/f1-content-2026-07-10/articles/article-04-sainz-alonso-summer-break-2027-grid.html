Sainz and Alonso Are Holding the 2027 F1 Grid Hostage Until the Summer Break

By Ryan Calloway July 10, 2026

Two Drivers, One Answer

Ask Carlos Sainz about his 2027 plans and he will tell you he is "not really" thinking about it yet. Ask Fernando Alonso the same question and he will tell you the same thing in different words. Both are out of contract with their current teams at the end of 2026. Both have indicated the summer break — which follows the Hungarian Grand Prix on July 26 — is when they expect to make their decisions. And both of those decisions, whenever they come, will rewrite a significant portion of the 2027 Formula 1 grid.

According to Formula 1.com's analysis of the driver market, Sainz and Alonso are described alongside Verstappen as the three drivers whose choices "will define what the 2027 grid looks like." With 15 of 22 seats still officially unconfirmed for next year, that is not an overstatement.

The Sainz Situation

Sainz joined Williams at the start of 2026 after Ferrari chose not to renew his contract when Hamilton arrived. He has been competitive for Williams throughout the season, and team principal James Vowles has publicly stated that the contract, if certain conditions are met, could run to four years through 2028. Sainz, however, will not commit to anything until the summer.

His market value remains high. If a seat opens at a top-three team — which depends heavily on Verstappen's situation at Red Bull and the ripple effects through McLaren — Sainz would be one of the first names to emerge as a candidate. He has victories in his career, championship-level experience, and the motivation of proving he belongs at the front of the grid. Williams is competitive but not yet a title challenger. If a better option presents itself in August, Sainz will evaluate it seriously.

The Alonso Question

Fernando Alonso is 44 years old and entering his 23rd Formula 1 season. He drives for Aston Martin, a team that has been developing rapidly under Lawrence Stroll's ownership. His contract expires at the end of 2026, and he has been deliberately vague about whether he plans to continue into 2027 or retire.

Alonso's influence on the driver market is described as uniquely powerful because his decision creates a vacancy that touches multiple teams simultaneously. If Alonso retires, Aston Martin needs a replacement. That replacement likely comes from a team below them, which creates another vacancy. The cascade continues across the mid-field grid in ways that affect teams from Haas to Alpine. By contrast, if Alonso re-signs with Aston Martin, the downstream movement does not happen and the grid stays closer to its current shape.

Why July 26 Changes Everything

The Hungarian Grand Prix on July 26 is the last race before the summer break. After that, Verstappen's exit clause formally activates and he can begin official negotiations. The summer break is when those negotiations become real offers and, potentially, real contracts.

Sainz and Alonso have both attached their decisions to the same timeline. When Verstappen's situation resolves — whatever form that takes — the seats that open or stay filled will determine what Sainz and Alonso are choosing between. They are not making their decisions in a vacuum. They are waiting to see what the market offers them after the biggest domino falls.

What's Next

The summer break begins after July 26 and Formula 1 teams scatter for roughly three weeks before the Dutch Grand Prix in late August. That window is when the 2027 driver market is expected to crystallize. Sainz, Alonso, and Verstappen — the three drivers whose decisions matter most — will all be making calls, taking meetings, and finalizing plans during those weeks away from the track. When the paddock reconvenes, the grid picture should look dramatically different from what it does today.
