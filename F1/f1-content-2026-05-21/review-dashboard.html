Hamilton's No-Simulator Gamble for Canada
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Lewis Hamilton has made thousands of simulator laps in his career. He's one of the most meticulous, data-driven competitors in motorsport history. And for the Canadian Grand Prix, he's doing zero laps in Ferrari's simulator. The decision came directly from what happened in Miami. Hamilton had spent extensive time in the simulator before the race, working on set-up correlations. When he arrived at the track, the setup that had worked virtually didn't translate to reality. He described it as the simulator taking him "in the wrong direction." What changed his mind is even more revealing: at the Chinese Grand Prix — the one weekend he didn't use the simulator — Hamilton finished third. His best result of 2026 by a significant margin. The correlation was obvious to him. So for Canada, Hamilton is trusting his instincts. He'll still attend factory meetings and remain involved in the engineering process, but the simulator work is off the table until further notice. In an era of Formula 1 where data and technology drive almost every preparation decision, it's a fascinatingly human choice from a driver who has seen every approach and is now betting on himself. If it works in Montreal, it will reshape how Ferrari approaches Hamilton's preparation for the rest of the season. ### Image Post Caption Hamilton dumped Ferrari's simulator before Canada. ### Long-Form Post Max Verstappen spent the past week doing what most F1 drivers only dream about: racing a GT3 car at the Nurburgring 24 Hours. And for most of that race, he was going to win it. His team had built more than a 30-second lead over their nearest rival. The crew was managing the car conservatively. With three hours remaining on the clock, it looked like Verstappen was going to add a Nurburgring 24H victory to a career that already includes four F1 world championships.
