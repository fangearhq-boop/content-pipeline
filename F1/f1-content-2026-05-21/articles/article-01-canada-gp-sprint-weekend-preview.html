F1 Canada GP Sprint Weekend Preview: Antonelli's Reign Faces Its Biggest Test Yet in Montreal

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | May 21, 2026

The Circuit Gilles Villeneuve has hosted Formula 1 since 1978. In that nearly five-decade history, Montreal has never staged a sprint weekend. That changes this Friday, when Sprint Qualifying opens a compressed, high-intensity version of the Canadian Grand Prix — and the timing could not be more charged.

Kimi Antonelli arrives in Montreal leading the world championship with 100 points, 20 clear of Mercedes teammate George Russell. The Italian teenager has won every race he has started from pole position this season. He has done things no driver in F1 history has done. And now he comes to a circuit where Russell won from pole last year — a weekend that could either cement Antonelli's dominance or start a genuine title battle.

The Sprint Format Changes Everything

For the first time, Circuit Gilles Villeneuve's legendary combination of tight chicanes, the Wall of Champions, and long straights will be contested under sprint format rules. Sprint Qualifying opens Friday May 22 at 9:30 PM ET — the championship battle begins essentially tomorrow evening. Sprint Race follows Saturday May 23 at 5:00 PM ET, with Qualifying at 9:00 PM ET that same night. The main race closes the weekend Sunday afternoon.

The sprint format has historically been a wildcard. Points are available, mistakes are costly, and the compressed timeframe means there is no room for a team to take a cautious weekend and rebuild from a poor Friday. Everything matters from the first lap of Sprint Qualifying.

For Russell, who enters 20 points down, sprint weekends are not necessarily bad news. They create additional opportunities to score points and can generate the kind of tactical complexity where experience and race-reading ability — areas where Russell is strong — become decisive factors.

McLaren, Ferrari, and a Wet Sunday

Lando Norris arrives in Montreal having won the Miami Sprint — McLaren's first victory of 2026 — and with more upgrades on the car. The trajectory of McLaren's development pace this season has been consistently upward, and Montreal, though not historically their strongest venue, is a place where tire management and strategy can override raw pace.

Lewis Hamilton comes to Canada with an unconventional preparation: no simulator work. He ditched Ferrari's simulator after discovering it produced set-up correlations that didn't translate to the real track. His best result of 2026 — third in China — came when he skipped the simulator entirely. The question is whether instinct-driven preparation will unlock the Ferrari performance that has been close but not quite there all season.

Rain is forecast for Sunday's race. Wet conditions are the great equalizer in Formula 1 — they reward sensitivity, precision, and risk tolerance over raw aerodynamic advantage. In a field where Mercedes has dominated dry conditions, a wet Sunday in Montreal could shuffle the order dramatically.

Verstappen's Return

Max Verstappen is back in his Red Bull RB22 after spending the previous week co-driving in the Nurburgring 24 Hours. His team led by more than 30 seconds when a driveshaft failure ended their run three hours from the finish. He returns to Formula 1 carrying whatever emotions a competitor like Verstappen carries after a heartbreak of that magnitude: motivation, focus, and a desire to put the wheel in his hands again at full speed.

What's Next

The Canadian Grand Prix sprint weekend is the most compressed, high-stakes version of this championship battle yet. Five key stories converge in Montreal this week — and by Sunday night, the championship picture will look significantly clearer than it does this morning.

The sprint format rewards the bold. Antonelli has been nothing but bold in 2026. Whether that continues — or whether Russell, Norris, or Hamilton finds the perfect weekend to interrupt it — begins Friday evening in Montreal. Sprint Qualifying: Friday May 22, 9:30 PM ET. Sprint Race: Saturday May 23, 5:00 PM ET. Qualifying: Saturday 9:00 PM ET. Race: Sunday May 24.
