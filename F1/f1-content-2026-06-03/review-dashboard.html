NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Kimi Antonelli walks into Monaco as the most dominant points leader in Formula 1 in a decade. Four wins from five races. The youngest championship leader in the sport's history. A 43-point cushion over the field. He is 19 years old. And for the first time this season, he is not the favorite. Antonelli himself acknowledged it: Ferrari, he said, "will be the team to beat" at Monaco. The circuit's emphasis on low-speed traction, mechanical grip, and precision over raw power gives Ferrari and the SF-26 an inherent edge that even the W17 cannot match on these streets. That is not a crisis for Antonelli. It is a test. He has become the youngest championship points leader in F1 history. He won in Australia, won in Japan, won in Miami, and won again in Canada — making himself the first driver since the sport's early era to convert his first four pole positions into four consecutive victories. His consistency has been extraordinary. Monaco will require something different: resilience under adversity, the ability to finish second when he cannot win, and the mental strength to protect a championship lead rather than chase a fifth victory. At 19, in his first real title battle, that may be the hardest thing he has ever done. ### Image Post Caption 4 wins. 43-point lead. Youngest champion-in-waiting ever. ### Long-Form Post Max Verstappen inserted a very specific clause into his Red Bull contract before the 2026 season began. If he is outside the top two in the Drivers' Championship when the summer break starts on July 27, he has the right to walk away from the team. He currently sits seventh. The clause was added out of "apprehension" about Red Bull's new power unit — the RBPT3 — which was developed under the 2026 regulations. That apprehension has proven justified. The Red Bull RB21 has been competitive but not dominant. Verstappen's best result so far is a third-place finish at the Canadian Grand Prix — Red Bull's first podium of the 2026 season. Monaco is a special case for Verstappen. He won here in 2021 and again in 2023. The Circuit de Monaco rewards driver craft, experience, and the ability to extract lap time from a car's mechanical limits rather than its raw performance. Verstappen has demonstrated exactly those skills on these streets. But the mathematics are severe. He needs to make up 45 points on George Russell — who sits second in the standings — in the next three races before the summer break. Even if Verstappen wins Monaco and Russell scores zero, the gap barely closes. What Monaco can do is give Red Bull evidence that the RB21 can fight. If Verstappen wins, suddenly the July deadline feels less like a farewell and more like a countdown. ### Image Post Caption Verstappen won Monaco in 2021. And in 2023. ### Long-Form Post George Russell was in total control of the Canadian Grand Prix. He had taken pole, survived a dramatic battle with Antonelli in the opening stint, and had the race in his hands when his power unit failed on Lap 30. He went from leading to not-classified in a single moment. The championship gap jumped from 18 points to 43. Russell flew home from Montreal and has spent the two weeks since doing the only productive thing available: preparing for Monaco. He has never won the Monaco Grand Prix. In a career that already includes race wins, a World Championship race, and consistent podiums, it is the one obvious gap on his résumé. This weekend, the personal and the strategic align perfectly — Monaco is exactly the race where Russell can score maximum points, demonstrate his speed on a circuit that rewards raw qualifying pace, and narrow Antonelli's lead in a single afternoon. The complication: Mercedes is not favored this weekend. The active aero ban removes the power unit advantage that has been their margin all season. Russell will have to extract more from his car here than anywhere in 2026 to challenge Ferrari on equal terms. He has done it before. Montreal, where he took pole by 0.068 seconds, showed he can find the last thousandth of a second under pressure. The question is whether the W17 can give him enough to fight with this weekend. ### Image Post Caption Russell led Canada. His power unit failed on Lap 30. He's now 43 points behind Antonelli.
