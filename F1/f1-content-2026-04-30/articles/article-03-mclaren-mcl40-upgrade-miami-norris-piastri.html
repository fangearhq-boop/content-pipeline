McLaren's "Entirely New MCL40" Is Here — And Miami Is Their Best Chance Yet

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap

April 30, 2026

McLaren has spent the five-week break between the Japanese Grand Prix and Miami doing one thing above all others: rebuilding their car. Not updating it. Rebuilding it.

Team principal Andrea Stella confirmed ahead of the Miami Grand Prix that McLaren is bringing what he describes as "a completely new car from aerodynamic standpoint" to Florida — and to the Canadian Grand Prix that follows. The upgraded MCL40 features new front brake ducts, rear brake ducts, bodywork, floor, and rear wing. Every major aerodynamic surface has been revised. The changes target two goals: improve raw pace, and make the car "easier to understand" for the drivers.

That second objective is revealing. The MCL40 has been the third-fastest car on the 2026 grid behind Mercedes and Ferrari — not because McLaren lacks engineering talent, but because the car's behavior has been inconsistent in ways that make setup decisions difficult. McLaren are the shortest car on the 2026 grid, with a smaller floor as a consequence, and have been generating less downforce than their rivals as a result. The Miami package is designed to address both issues simultaneously.

The Historical Case for McLaren at Miami

McLaren's track record at the Miami International Autodrome is unmatched. Lando Norris won here in 2024 in what remains one of the most emotionally charged victories in recent Formula 1 history — his maiden Grand Prix win after 110 races. Oscar Piastri followed him to victory lane in 2025. Two consecutive Miami wins, two different drivers: it is the circuit that has defined McLaren's recent transformation from also-rans to genuine contenders.

To arrive here in 2026 with their biggest upgrade package of the year is not a coincidence. It is a deliberate choice to take their best shot at a circuit where they know how to win. Norris and Piastri spent significant time in the simulator during the break, and both participated in a Pirelli tyre test at the Nürburgring alongside Mercedes — gathering data on the new rubber compounds that will be used in Florida.

The Challenge That Awaits

Mercedes has won every race this season. They arrive in Miami leading the constructors' championship by 79 points ahead of McLaren. They have championship leaders in both cars. And they are also bringing updates.

Ferrari is bringing the most complete version of its SF-26 yet. Red Bull has its own upgrade package. Miami, far from being a breather, is a full-scale upgrade war — and McLaren enters it having bet its development chips on a broad, sweeping overhaul rather than incremental refinements.

Whether that bet pays off is the technical story of the weekend. If the new MCL40 delivers the downforce and predictability that Stella described, Norris and Piastri will be genuine contenders for the win on Sunday. If it requires further calibration, the gap to Mercedes may actually grow before it closes.

What's Next

Free Practice 1 on Friday is the first real data point. With only 90 minutes of practice available in a Sprint weekend format, McLaren's engineers will have limited time to assess the upgrade before Sprint Qualifying requires a committed setup. That time pressure is real — and it means Miami will tell us something definitive about McLaren's chances that no amount of simulation time can reveal.

They have the history here. They have the upgrade. They have two drivers who are hungry after a frustrating start to the 2026 season. Now comes the moment of truth on the track.
