Hamilton's Ferrari Honeymoon Is Over — Miami Is Where It Gets Real

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

April 30, 2026

Lewis Hamilton called Shanghai "the most enjoyable race I've had in a long time." He finished third — his first podium in a Ferrari — came from a car that was genuinely competitive for the first time, and briefly felt like the move to Maranello was beginning to make sense. That was Round 3. Now comes Round 5, and the context has changed.

Hamilton arrives at the Miami Grand Prix in fourth place in the drivers' championship with 41 points. His Ferrari teammate Charles Leclerc is ahead of him in third with 49. Championship leader Kimi Antonelli has 72. With 19 races remaining, the gap is not fatal — but it is growing, and the pattern of being outscored by the teammate he was supposed to lead is beginning to accumulate weight.

Miami is where Hamilton needs to change that pattern. And for the first time this season, the car might finally let him.

Ferrari's Miami Package

Ferrari has brought the fullest specification of its SF-26 to Miami: the Macarena rear wing that Vasseur named after the rotational motion of its upper flap, combined with a revised floor, updated software package, and aerodynamic refinements developed during the five-week break. The Monza private test in late April — where Hamilton and Leclerc both reported positive feedback — provided confidence that the package is a genuine step forward.

Martin Brundle, analyzing the situation before the weekend, offered a characteristically direct assessment. "If there's a sniff of a victory," Brundle said on Sky Sports, "I think Lewis is still very much capable of taking that." The caveat — "a sniff of a victory" — is significant. For Hamilton to win, Ferrari needs to be fastest or close to it. At Miami, for the first time in 2026, that is at least a realistic possibility.

The Stakes Are Clear

Hamilton turns 42 this year. Every race in his Ferrari career now carries additional weight — not because he lacks the skill, but because the window for making his Maranello move a success story rather than a footnote is finite, and the seasons do not stop passing.

Leclerc has outscored him in two of three races. The constructors' championship requires Ferrari to extract the maximum from both drivers — and Vasseur has made clear that Miami's upgrades represent what he has called a "new championship" moment for the team. Hamilton needs to be at the front of that new chapter, not chasing his teammate through it.

What's Next

Free Practice 1 on Friday will be Hamilton's first real-time evaluation of the upgraded SF-26 at a racing circuit under competitive conditions. With only one practice session in the Sprint weekend format, he will need to adapt quickly. His ability to dial into a new aerodynamic package on a single run has always been one of his strengths — and Miami will test that strength under maximum pressure.

He has been patient in building toward this moment. He has processed a difficult start to his Ferrari career without deflection or drama. Now the car is ready, the track is fast, and the upgrade is on. Hamilton's 2026 story — the one that actually matters, not the prologue — starts this weekend.
