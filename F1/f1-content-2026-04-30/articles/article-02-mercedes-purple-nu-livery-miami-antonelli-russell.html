Mercedes Goes Purple: Special Nu Livery Unveiled for Miami Grand Prix

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap

April 30, 2026

The Silver Arrows are silver no more — at least not this weekend.

Mercedes has unveiled a special purple livery for the 2026 Miami Grand Prix, replacing their iconic look with the signature color of Nu, the Brazilian fintech company that has partnered with the team since January. Championship leader Kimi Antonelli and teammate George Russell will each race in three different custom purple race suits over the course of the three-day Sprint weekend — each suit, according to the partnership's activation materials, encoding a distinct chapter of Nu's brand story.

The activation extends beyond the paddock and the pit lane. Nu's "House of Nu" experience is running all weekend in Miami's Wynwood neighborhood, giving fans an off-circuit engagement point tied to the livery reveal. The campaign is one of the most ambitious brand activations in Formula 1 this season, matching Miami's reputation for spectacle with a visual statement that is hard to miss.

A Championship Platform for the Campaign

The timing of the partnership's showcase could hardly be more advantageous for both parties. Antonelli arrives in Miami leading the 2026 drivers' championship with 72 points — nine ahead of Russell in second — after winning the Chinese and Japanese Grands Prix at the start of the season. Mercedes has won all three races held under the 2026 regulations, making the Silver Arrows — now temporarily the Purple Arrows — the sport's undisputed No. 1 team entering the weekend.

Russell, who won the season-opening Australian Grand Prix, is a joint lead performer who has made no secret of his ambitions to reclaim the championship lead from his younger teammate. The two drivers in purple, each chasing the same prize, give the campaign an additional competitive edge that extends well beyond its marketing purpose.

Miami and Special Liveries

Miami has become one of the most aesthetically distinctive events on the Formula 1 calendar, with teams regularly using the American race as an opportunity to debut special liveries or activation campaigns tied to major sponsors. The Mercedes-Nu reveal follows a pattern of the sport leaning into its growing US fanbase with visual content that performs well beyond traditional automotive or motorsport audiences.

It is the first major special livery of the 2026 season — appropriate, given that Miami is also the round that has been dubbed by Ferrari team principal Frederic Vasseur as the start of a "new championship," with nearly every team bringing significant upgrades to the circuit.

What's Next

Antonelli and Russell take to the track in purple for the first time on Friday in a 90-minute Free Practice 1 session — the only practice opportunity of the Sprint weekend. The session is critical for every team, but especially for Mercedes, who will need to quickly diagnose how their rivals' upgrades have changed the competitive picture.

Whether the purple livery brings a fourth consecutive race victory for Mercedes is the question the sport wants answered by Sunday at 4 PM ET. McLaren won here in 2024 and 2025. Ferrari has its most complete car of the season. The championship leader in a purple car, at a circuit no one has dominated the way Mercedes has this year, enters the weekend as the team to beat — and the team everyone else is hoping to dethrone.
