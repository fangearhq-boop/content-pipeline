Miami GP Sprint Weekend Guide: Full Schedule, TV Times, and What to Watch

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap

April 30, 2026

Formula 1 returns to racing this weekend after a five-week break, and it does so at one of the most distinctive venues on the calendar. The 2026 Miami Grand Prix at Hard Rock Stadium is a Sprint weekend — a format that compresses the schedule, raises the stakes on every session, and gives fans substantially more meaningful on-track action than a standard Grand Prix weekend.

Here is everything you need to know before the action begins on Friday morning.

Full Session Schedule (All Times ET)

Free Practice 1 opens the weekend on Friday, May 1 at 1:00 PM ET, running for 90 minutes until 2:30 PM ET. The session has been extended from the standard 60 minutes by the FIA specifically for this weekend — recognizing the five-week gap between races, the debut of new regulations, and the Sprint format's constraints on preparation time. This is the only free practice session of the entire weekend. Every team will arrive with major upgrades and every setup decision counts.

Sprint Qualifying follows on Friday, May 1 in the late afternoon. This determines the grid for the Sprint Race, not the main Grand Prix.

Saturday, May 2 features the Sprint Race — a shorter, championship-point-scoring event that has genuine implications for the weekend narrative. The Sprint is followed by main Qualifying on Saturday evening, which sets the grid for Sunday's Grand Prix.

The Miami Grand Prix itself runs on Sunday, May 3 at 4:00 PM ET. US coverage is available on ESPN, ESPN2, and Peacock. The race runs 57 laps around the 5.41-kilometer Miami International Autodrome circuit.

New Rules That Take Effect This Weekend

This is not just any Miami Grand Prix — it is the first race weekend where the FIA's updated power unit deployment regulations take effect. MGU-K output will be limited to 250 kilowatts outside designated key acceleration zones, and 350 kilowatts within those zones. The change is designed to address the complex energy management strategies that have defined the 2026 season so far and to create more consistent lap time performance.

Additionally, the FIA is debuting a new low-power start detection system this weekend. The system is capable of identifying cars with abnormally low acceleration at clutch release — a measure targeted at improving the fairness of race starts, which have been a frequent topic of debate under the new 2026 regulations. Race starts have been one of the few areas where Mercedes has shown relative weakness despite their overall dominance.

What's Next

The storylines heading into this weekend are among the richest of the season: championship leader Kimi Antonelli facing his first real test at a circuit with a two-year McLaren winning streak; Max Verstappen absent from media day but expected to race; Mercedes in a special purple livery; McLaren with their largest upgrade package of the year; and Ferrari bringing the fullest version of the SF-26 with Lewis Hamilton finally equipped for a genuine title push.

Sprint weekends create drama that standard Grand Prix formats cannot replicate — a result on Saturday can change the psychological dynamic heading into Sunday, and a mechanical failure in the Sprint costs points that matter. Tune in Friday and do not look away. The 2026 championship genuinely restarts this weekend.
