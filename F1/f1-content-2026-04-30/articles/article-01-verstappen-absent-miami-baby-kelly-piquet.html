Verstappen Skips Miami Media Day as First Child with Kelly Piquet Arrives

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

April 30, 2026

Max Verstappen will not attend the FIA press conference at the Miami Grand Prix on Thursday. The reason has nothing to do with his championship position, his car, or the controversy that has swirled around him all season — it has everything to do with what is about to happen at home.

According to multiple reports, Verstappen is absent from Miami's official media day because partner Kelly Piquet is expected to give birth imminently to their first child together. The four-time world champion, currently No. 9 in the 2026 drivers' standings with 12 points, has made his priorities clear: some things come before Formula 1.

The FIA's official press conference roster for Thursday confirms the absence. The first slot features Nico Hulkenberg, Sergio Perez, and Oscar Piastri, while the second brings Isack Hadjar, Carlos Sainz, and championship leader Kimi Antonelli. Verstappen is not listed among them.

Context in the Championship

The sporting backdrop to Verstappen's absence is impossible to ignore, even if the personal circumstances make the absence entirely understandable. He sits in ninth place in the 2026 championship — a stunning fall from grace for the driver who has dominated Formula 1 for the past four seasons. Red Bull's RB22 has been uncompetitive since the new regulations were introduced at the start of the year, and Verstappen has publicly voiced his frustration with both the car and the regulations themselves on multiple occasions.

The question of his performance-related exit clause has been a persistent undercurrent all season. Whether that clause — reportedly tied to championship position benchmarks during a summer review window — is in genuine danger of being triggered depends on Red Bull's ability to close the gap to Mercedes in the coming rounds. Miami, where every team is bringing major upgrades including Red Bull with their Macarena rear wing package, was supposed to be a turning point.

Whatever it represents on track, Verstappen will begin the weekend from a different location entirely.

What's Next

Verstappen is expected to travel to Miami once family circumstances allow and is expected to participate in Free Practice 1 on Friday morning. The FIA's press conference, held on Thursday, is a traditional part of race week but is not a mandatory race weekend session — Verstappen's absence from Thursday does not affect his ability to race on Sunday.

The bigger storyline in Miami — whether Red Bull's upgrades can deliver on Mekies' promises, and whether Verstappen's name stays away from the exit clause conversation — will play out over the next three days. For now, the man himself has something more important on his mind, and few in the paddock will fault him for it.
