### STORY 5: Miami GP Viewer's Guide — Sprint Weekend Schedule and What to Watch

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Formula 1 returns this weekend after a five-week break, and it comes back with a bang: the Miami Grand Prix is a Sprint weekend, which means more on-track action, higher stakes, and a compressed schedule that rewards preparation.

Here's everything you need to know about the 2026 Miami Grand Prix weekend:

Free Practice 1 runs 90 minutes on Friday, May 1, from 1:00 PM to 2:30 PM ET. This is the only practice session of the weekend, which makes it critical — every team will be diagnosing their new upgrades and setting up the car in real-time without a second chance to correct mistakes. Sprint Qualifying follows later Friday afternoon.

The Sprint Race runs Saturday, May 2, followed by main Qualifying on the same day. The Sprint is a shorter, points-paying race that can shift the weekend narrative dramatically before the main event even begins.

The Grand Prix itself is Sunday, May 3 at 4:00 PM ET. Coverage is available on ESPN, ESPN2, and Peacock in the United States.

One other note: this is the first race weekend where the FIA's new power unit deployment rules take effect. MGU-K output will be capped at 250 kilowatts outside key acceleration zones, and 350 kilowatts within them. A new low-power start detection system also debuts — aimed at improving the fairness of race starts that have been a consistent talking point this season.

Championship leader Kimi Antonelli. McLaren's biggest upgrade package of the year. A Mercedes in purple. Hamilton at Ferrari with a genuine weapon for the first time. And Verstappen watching from home, at least for now.

This is not a weekend to miss.
