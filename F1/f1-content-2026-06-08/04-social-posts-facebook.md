### STORY 5: Alonso Blasts 2026 Cars

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM ET

Fernando Alonso has won the Formula 1 World Championship twice. He has driven at Monaco more times than most current drivers have been alive as professional racing drivers. He has seen the best regulations the sport has produced.

And he has a verdict on 2026: they are the worst.

"This is probably the worst generation of cars I ever drove in Monaco," Alonso said following the Monaco Grand Prix weekend. "Hybrid cars should not be racing. It's as simple as that."

The two-time champion went on to explain the specific technical issue that infuriates him. The 2026 regulations feature a heavily hybrid power unit that charges its battery during braking. The problem, according to Alonso, is that this creates fundamental inconsistency in engine braking behavior.

"Sometimes you have less, sometimes you have push and sometimes not," he explained. "If the battery is completely full, then you don't recharge because the battery is full. So you don't have engine braking."

The result at Monaco — a circuit where brake consistency is everything — was what he called "random downshifts" that make the car unpredictable at the worst possible moments.

On top of the hybrid issues, Alonso's Aston Martin was also dealing with "chronic understeer" throughout the Monaco weekend. The team was among the slowest on circuit.

When a driver of Alonso's experience and bluntness says a generation of cars is the worst he has ever driven, it carries weight. The 2026 regulations are still new — but the questions being raised about them are not going to disappear.
