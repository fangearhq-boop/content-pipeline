Antonelli Opens 66-Point Championship Gap — Hamilton Is Now His Closest Rival

By Elena Voss | June 8, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

The 2026 Formula 1 Drivers' Championship standings after Monaco tell a story with two headlines. The first is the dominance of Kimi Antonelli, who now leads the title race by 66 points with five consecutive victories to his name. The second is the quiet ascendancy of Lewis Hamilton, who has slipped into second place in the standings — ahead of teammate George Russell, whose penalty-ruined Monaco afternoon cost him crucial points.

The Championship Picture

Antonelli leaves Monaco with 156 championship points. The Italian teenager won his fifth consecutive race on Sunday, converting his pole position into a controlled lights-to-flag victory in one of the most chaotic Grands Prix of the season. His lead over the driver in second place is now 66 points — a gap that reflects not just individual brilliance but structural domination. Mercedes, as a team, has won every race Antonelli has started.

In second place sits Lewis Hamilton, who climbed the standings after his P2 finish for Ferrari at Monaco. Hamilton now sits on approximately 90 points, having leapfrogged his former teammate George Russell, who fell to third after his penalty-laden afternoon sent him to 14th place at Monaco.

Charles Leclerc remains fourth after a DNF in his home race. His Monaco crash — at the final corner while preparing for the restart — cost him a podium finish and left Hamilton as Ferrari's sole representative in the championship fight.

Hamilton's Renaissance

There is something remarkable about where Lewis Hamilton finds himself in June 2026. The seven-time world champion moved to Ferrari in the off-season with questions hanging over whether his career had one final chapter worth reading. Through Monaco, the answer appears to be yes.

Hamilton's P2 at Monaco was described as Ferrari's best result at the principality in 2026. While Leclerc crashed and Russell was buried by penalties, Hamilton simply drove a clean race and collected 18 points. He is not yet close enough to challenge Antonelli's title lead — 66 points is a vast distance when five races have produced five identical results — but he is firmly established as the story of the midfield championship fight.

Russell's Damage Control Required

George Russell needs to respond. He entered Monaco as Antonelli's closest championship rival, with a 43-point gap that, while significant, was not insurmountable if Antonelli encountered problems. After Monaco, that gap has widened and Russell has also lost ground to Hamilton — sliding from second to third in the standings.

Russell's Monaco unraveled through a combination of pit lane speeding and procedural errors. Neither is uncommon in Formula 1, but the timing — with the championship this important — makes it far more costly. He will need the Austrian Grand Prix to go cleanly and favorably if he is to recover his position as the natural Antonelli challenger.

What's Next

The Austrian Grand Prix at the Red Bull Ring takes place June 26-28, giving the paddock just under three weeks to regroup after Monaco. The Red Bull Ring is a high-speed circuit that suits different car characteristics than Monaco — and for teams like Red Bull, who have struggled on the streets of Monte Carlo, it offers a potential opportunity to find performance.

For Antonelli, the task is straightforward: protect the lead, collect more points, and take the title one step closer to inevitable. For Hamilton, the challenge is remaining in second while Antonelli dominates. And for Russell, Monaco was a reminder that in Formula 1, a single afternoon of penalties can reshape an entire championship narrative.
