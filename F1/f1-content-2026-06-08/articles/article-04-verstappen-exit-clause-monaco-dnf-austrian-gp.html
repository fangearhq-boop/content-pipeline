Lap 1, DNF, Exit Clause Ticking: Verstappen's Monaco Disaster Makes His Future Murkier

By Marcus Cole | June 8, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

There is a clause in Max Verstappen's contract with Red Bull Racing that allows him to leave the team before his deal expires. The condition is specific: he must be in the top two of the Formula 1 Drivers' Championship at the end of July 2026. If he is not, the clause cannot be activated.

Seven weeks before that deadline, Verstappen's Monaco Grand Prix lasted approximately one lap.

A power unit failure caused Verstappen to stall at the race start on Sunday. He was the first retirement of the day, sitting stationary on the Monaco streets while 19 other drivers accelerated away. He scored zero points. His championship position, already dire, worsened further.

The Math Against Him

Verstappen's best result of the 2026 season is a third-place finish in Canada. That race was described at the time as a turning point — a sign that perhaps the RB22 was finding form. Then came Monaco, and the power unit failure, and the conversation has reset to its baseline: Red Bull is not competitive in 2026, and Verstappen cannot outscore a 66-point championship gap on results alone.

The exit clause requires top-2. After Monaco, Verstappen is not in the top 2. The drivers ahead of him — Antonelli at 156 points, Hamilton at approximately 90 — are collecting finishes in the top three of most races while Verstappen cycles through retirement and midfield results.

To move into the top two by the end of July, Verstappen would need a dramatic reversal: consistent podiums for himself and simultaneous reliability problems for both Antonelli and Hamilton. Neither scenario is likely given the current form of the respective cars.

The Red Bull Ring Is Next

The Austrian Grand Prix at the Red Bull Ring on June 26-28 is Verstappen's most significant remaining opportunity before the July exit clause deadline. The venue — Red Bull's home circuit in Spielberg — is one where the team has traditionally performed well. The high-speed layout and the altitude suit certain engine characteristics, and Red Bull have historically converted home advantages into strong results.

But the 2026 Red Bull RB22 is not the car that dominated from 2021 to 2024. The new regulations have not suited Red Bull's design philosophy, and the team has found itself in an unfamiliar position: midfield, chasing upgrades, watching the championship from a distance rather than controlling it.

If there is any circuit left on the 2026 calendar that gives Verstappen genuine hope before the clause deadline, it is the Red Bull Ring. He knows it better than almost any other venue in the world. He has won there repeatedly. And his home crowd will be waiting.

What Activation Would Mean

If Verstappen activates the clause and departs Red Bull, it would represent one of the most significant driver market events in recent Formula 1 history. Verstappen is still, despite a difficult season by his standards, one of the sport's elite talents. Any team with a competitive car would want him.

The question of where he would go, and what it would mean for Red Bull, is a storyline that has shadowed the entire 2026 season. For now, the Austrian Grand Prix takes center stage.

What's Next

Austrian Grand Prix, Red Bull Ring, June 26-28. For Max Verstappen, it is not just another race. It may be the most important weekend of his 2026 campaign — and one of the most consequential of his career. Whether the RB22 can deliver the performance he needs is a question only the next three weeks will answer.
