'Hybrid Cars Should Not Be Racing': Fernando Alonso Calls 2026 F1 Cars the Worst of His Career

By Elena Voss | June 8, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Fernando Alonso does not say things he does not mean. The two-time Formula 1 world champion has never been known for diplomatic hedging or vague platitudes. When he has a problem with something in Formula 1, he says so clearly and publicly, with the kind of conviction that comes from 25 years of competing at the sport's highest level.

At Monaco, he had a problem with the cars.

"This is probably the worst generation of cars I ever drove in Monaco," Alonso said following the race weekend. "Hybrid cars should not be racing. It's as simple as that."

What He Means — and Why He's Right to Raise It

Alonso's criticism is not born from a difficult weekend alone. It is grounded in a specific technical complaint about the 2026 F1 power unit regulations — and his explanation reveals exactly how the new rules create problems that are particularly acute at Monaco.

The 2026 regulations include a heavily hybrid power unit that charges its battery during braking. The charging happens through the rear axle — meaning every time a driver brakes hard for a corner, the battery is potentially absorbing energy. The problem, as Alonso explains it, is that this creates inconsistency in the braking behavior itself.

"Sometimes you have less, sometimes you have push and sometimes not," he said. "If the battery is completely full, then you don't recharge because the battery is full. So you don't have engine braking."

At Monaco — a circuit where every braking zone matters, where consistency through slow corners is the difference between being quick and being average — variable engine braking is not a minor inconvenience. It is a fundamental problem. Alonso described the result as "random downshifts" that made the car unpredictable at the moments when predictability matters most.

Aston Martin's Additional Problems

Alonso was not only dealing with the hybrid regulation issues shared by all teams. His Aston Martin was also suffering from what he called "chronic understeer" throughout the Monaco weekend. He and teammate Lance Stroll were among the slowest cars through the streets of Monte Carlo in both practice sessions, leaving Aston Martin well outside the competitive picture before the race had even begun.

Stroll's retirement during the race — a crash at the Anthony Noghes hairpin that triggered the safety car — brought the team's Monaco weekend to its unhappy conclusion. The results were difficult; the underlying concerns Alonso raised are bigger than one weekend.

The Broader Question

Formula 1 introduced its 2026 regulations with significant fanfare. The new power unit philosophy — a 50/50 split between internal combustion and electrical energy recovery — was designed to make the sport more relevant to road car manufacturers and to reduce the complexity that had made earlier hybrid systems so expensive for smaller teams.

The reality, through Monaco 2026, has included seven retirements in a single race, multiple complaints from drivers about car behavior, and one of the sport's most respected voices declaring the generation fundamentally unsuited to racing. Whether the FIA and Formula 1's commercial rights holders will take this feedback seriously — or whether the regulations will evolve to address the specific mechanical complaints being raised — remains to be seen.

Alonso, for his part, is not waiting for an answer he does not expect. He drove his race, stated his view, and made clear what he believes. When a two-time champion speaks with that kind of directness, the sport has always been better served by listening.

What's Next

The Austrian Grand Prix at the Red Bull Ring runs June 26-28. The high-speed Spielberg circuit is a very different challenge from Monaco — a venue where the specific hybrid braking complaints Alonso raised at Monte Carlo may be less acute. Whether Aston Martin can find the performance step needed to move away from the bottom of the field is the more pressing question for the team's 2026 season.
