Antonelli Wins Monaco Chaos: Five Straight, Leclerc DNF, Verstappen Stalled, Russell Buried by Penalties

By Marcus Cole | June 8, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

The 2026 Monaco Grand Prix delivered everything the principality has become famous for: drama at every corner, chaos at the critical moments, and a finish that left the paddock shaking its head. Through all of it, one driver was untouchable. Kimi Antonelli crossed the line first for the fifth consecutive Grand Prix, extending a streak that is beginning to enter historic territory.

Antonelli started from pole and controlled his race from the front. Lewis Hamilton finished second at +6.271 seconds, with Red Bull's Isack Hadjar third at +23.394 seconds. Oscar Piastri was fourth. But the results tell only part of the story.

Seven DNFs and a Race That Came Apart

Seven drivers retired from the 2026 Monaco Grand Prix — one of the highest attrition rates in the modern era at this circuit.

Max Verstappen was the first to go. A power unit failure caused him to stall at the race start, and he was retired before most of the field had completed a lap. It was the worst possible result for a driver with a looming exit clause deadline.

The race grew stranger from there. At lap 68 of 78, a crash from Lance Stroll's Aston Martin at the Anthony Noghes hairpin — the final corner of the circuit — brought out the safety car and eventually a red flag. Stewards discovered significant track break-up at the corner that required inspection before any restart could proceed.

And then came the moment that defined Monaco 2026. Charles Leclerc, who had been running third throughout the race and was positioned to take a vital championship podium, crashed at the exact same Anthony Noghes corner while preparing for the restart. His front wing clipped the barriers, his race was over, and the home fans watched in stunned silence.

Lando Norris, Carlos Sainz, Valtteri Bottas, and Oliver Bearman rounded out the retirements, all for mechanical failures.

Russell's Penalty Nightmare

George Russell's Monaco was a study in how quickly a Formula 1 afternoon can unravel. Running competitively and positioned for a potential podium, Russell received a five-second penalty for exceeding the pit lane speed limit. When he failed to correctly serve the penalty at his next stop, the punishment was upgraded to a mandatory drive-through.

The penalties buried Russell to 14th place — a result that not only damaged his race day but may have had significant consequences in the championship fight. His teammate Antonelli was winning races while Russell was fighting from the back of the pack on the same afternoon.

Antonelli's Dominance in Context

Kimi Antonelli is 19 years old. He has now won five consecutive Formula 1 Grand Prix races — a streak that requires no qualifier, no asterisk, and no hedging. Five in a row at Monaco, one of the most technically demanding circuits on the calendar, against the full field of Formula 1's best drivers.

The Italian started his season as the heir apparent to Mercedes' dominant years. He has delivered on that expectation in a way that has surprised even those who believed in him most. His championship lead after Monaco stands at 66 points — a gap that would be very difficult to recover regardless of who is chasing.

What's Next

Formula 1 returns for the Austrian Grand Prix at the Red Bull Ring, June 26-28. The race will be Red Bull's home event — a venue where they have historically performed well and a circuit that Verstappen will desperately need given the pressure building around his exit clause situation.

For Antonelli, every race now carries a different kind of pressure: the pressure of protecting a massive lead rather than building one. He has handled it brilliantly so far. Monaco, as chaotic as it was, proved he is capable of winning in any conditions the sport throws at him.
