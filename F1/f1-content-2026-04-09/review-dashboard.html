FIA's Crunch Technical Meeting Underway — April 9 Discussions Begin
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET The meetings that could determine the fate of the 2026 Formula 1 season have officially begun. On Thursday, April 9, the FIA held the first of its crunch technical discussions, bringing together top-level technical representatives from all teams, the FIA, and FOM. Team principals were not involved — this first meeting was a structured technical discussion, not a decision-making session. No binding changes were agreed. But the process is now in motion. What's on the table? The key proposals center around the controversial energy management rules that have made the 2026 cars so difficult for drivers. The FIA is considering raising the "super clipping" threshold from 250 kW to 350 kW — which would reduce the severe speed differentials that occur when one car is in boost mode and another is conserving energy. There's also a proposal to lower the maximum energy recovery per lap from 9 MJ to 6 MJ. The safety concern that pushed these talks into emergency territory is Oliver Bearman's 50G crash at Spoon Curve during the Japanese Grand Prix — caused by a roughly 50km/h speed offset between his car in boost mode and Franco Colapinto's Alpine in energy-saving mode. That crash, and Bearman's escape from serious injury, has put safety at the top of the agenda. A second technical meeting follows Thursday's, before team principals gather on April 20 for a session where binding decisions could be made. Any changes would require World Motor Sport Council approval. The goal remains getting new rules in place for the Miami Grand Prix on May 1-3. --- ### Image Post Caption Window: 10:00 AM ET The FIA's crunch meetings to fix F1's controversial 2026 regulations have officially started. Discussion today, decisions April 20, Miami deadline May 1. Super clipping, energy recovery, safety — everything is on the table.

NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 12:00 PM ET It's no longer a retirement scare. According to ESPN sources, Max Verstappen is now leaning toward a sabbatical — a calculated step away from Formula 1 racing while maintaining ties to Red Bull as an ambassador or in another off-track role. The distinction matters. Retirement sounds final. A sabbatical is strategic: step back, let the 2026 regulations either be fixed or fully clarified, assess the team landscape, and return in 2027 to a car and situation of his own choosing. The motivation is clear. Verstappen is ninth in the championship with 12 points after three races. He's been outspoken about the new formula — calling it "terrible," "a joke," and comparing it to "Mario Kart." His exit clause (activated if he's outside the top two at the summer break) is triggered by the very situation he's currently living through. And his trusted mechanic of 20+ years is leaving alongside a series of other key figures who built the team around him. Verstappen was candid on Thursday: he expects whatever rule changes come from the current FIA meetings to be "marginal" — not the fundamental fix he believes the regulations need. Those changes, he's made clear, matter for his "life decisions." The language of a man preparing to walk is increasingly difficult to ignore. The April 20 team-principal meeting is now crucial — not just for the 2026 season, but potentially for whether Verstappen remains in Formula 1 at all. --- ### Image Post Caption Window: 12:30 PM ET ESPN sources say Verstappen is leaning toward a sabbatical, not full retirement. Ferrari's April Mini Pre-Season — Monza Test April 22
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 5:00 PM ET Ferrari is doing something unusual this April: winning the off-season within the season. While the enforced five-week break — created by the cancellation of the Bahrain and Saudi Grands Prix — left most teams with extended downtime, Ferrari moved quickly to turn it into preparation time. The Scuderia has arranged testing sessions at Mugello and Fiorano, and has confirmed a filming day at Monza on April 22 with Lewis Hamilton and Charles Leclerc sharing driving duties. What's being tested matters. Ferrari will evaluate a revised floor originally intended for Bahrain, updated ADUO (Additional Development and Upgrade Opportunities) power unit software designed to better manage energy deployment and reduce the effects of super clipping, and the "Macarena" rear wing — a redesigned piece that was tested in China in Bahrain specification and found to lack stability under combined braking and cornering inputs. The Monza circuit's long straights make it an ideal testing ground for evaluating the ADUO's impact on energy management. Ferrari is in a race against time to finalize all three packages before Miami, where the team is hoping to arrive with a significantly upgraded SF-26 capable of challenging Mercedes more consistently. After three races, Ferrari sits third in the constructors' championship. The April break is their best chance to close the gap. --- ### Image Post Caption Window: 5:30 PM ET Ferrari has booked a Monza filming day on April 22 with Hamilton and Leclerc. New floor, ADUO PU software, "Macarena" rear wing — the Scuderia is treating the April break as a mini pre-season. Miami is the target.
