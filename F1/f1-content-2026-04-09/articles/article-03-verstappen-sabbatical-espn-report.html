Verstappen Sabbatical Now Most Likely Outcome, ESPN Sources Say

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

April 9, 2026

For weeks, the conversation around Max Verstappen's Formula 1 future toggled between "will he stay?" and "will he retire?" According to ESPN sources, neither framing quite captures where Verstappen actually is right now. The more accurate answer, sources say, is a sabbatical — a calculated step away from driving that preserves his options without permanently closing the door.

The distinction carries real weight. Retirement from F1 at 28 carries a finality that doesn't fit Verstappen's competitive profile. A sabbatical is different: step away from a formula you find "terrible," let the regulations stabilize or be reformed, and return in 2027 to a car and team worth driving for. Red Bull's contract structure reportedly allows him to remain with the organization in an ambassador or advisory capacity if he chooses not to race — meaning he wouldn't need to walk away from the brand entirely.

The Context That Makes It Rational

Three races into the 2026 season, Verstappen is ninth in the drivers' championship with 12 points. He's suffered a retirement and rarely cracked the top five. The RB22 has been widely criticized as fundamentally uncompetitive, and Verstappen himself has called the formula "terrible," a "joke," and comparable to "Mario Kart." His frustration appears genuine rather than strategic.

His exit clause window opens between August and October 2026, triggered if he's outside the top two in the championship at the summer break. He is currently 60 points behind championship leader Kimi Antonelli. Getting into the top two from ninth, 60 points back, in a car that hasn't finished in the top five consistently, is a task that requires either dramatic Red Bull improvement or remarkable misfortune for the championship leaders — or both.

The FIA Meeting Connection

Verstappen made his position clear on Thursday: he expects whatever rule changes emerge from the current FIA process to be "marginal." Not the fundamental restructuring he believes the 2026 regulations need, but minor adjustments at the edges. Those changes, he said, are important for his "life decisions" — language that has consistently pointed toward departure being a live possibility.

The April 20 team-principal meeting — where binding decisions on the super clipping and energy recovery proposals could be made — has acquired enormous importance in that context. If the FIA delivers meaningful changes that improve the racing and reduce the speed differentials that make the current cars dangerous, Verstappen might find renewed motivation. If the changes are incremental, as he expects, the sabbatical scenario advances.

What's Next

The FIA process continues through April 20. Miami is May 1-3. Verstappen will be racing a GT3 car at the Nürburgring between now and then — the NLS qualifier races on April 18-19, preparation for the 24 Hours of Nürburgring in May — which offers an interesting window into what he finds fulfilling in racing right now.

Whether he's still a Formula 1 driver through the end of 2026 may ultimately depend less on points and more on whether anyone can convince him the formula is worth staying for. That answer, currently, appears to be no.
