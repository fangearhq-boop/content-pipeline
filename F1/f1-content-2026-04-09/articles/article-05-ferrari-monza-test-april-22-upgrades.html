Ferrari Books Monza Filming Day for April 22 in Push for Miami Upgrades

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap

April 9, 2026

Ferrari has booked a filming day at Monza on April 22 as part of an intensive preparation period the team is calling a "mini pre-season" ahead of the Miami Grand Prix. Lewis Hamilton and Charles Leclerc will share driving duties. The goal is to evaluate and finalize three upgrade packages before May 1.

The enforced April break — created by the cancellation of the Bahrain and Saudi Grands Prix due to regional instability — gave Ferrari an unusual window of preparation time within a racing season. Rather than treat it as downtime, the Scuderia has filled the calendar with testing sessions at Mugello, Fiorano, and now Monza, using each circuit's different characteristics to validate different aspects of the upgraded SF-26.

Three Packages Being Tested

The Monza filming day centers on a revised floor originally intended for Bahrain before those races were cancelled. It's the primary upgrade for increasing aerodynamic downforce and is expected to give the car better balance through medium-speed corners. Ferrari has been running deficit to Mercedes in overall downforce efficiency since the start of the season, and the new floor addresses that gap directly.

Also being evaluated is updated ADUO (Additional Development and Upgrade Opportunities) power unit software, designed to improve how Ferrari manages its energy deployment. The super clipping phenomenon — where cars cut power delivery when the battery system is recovering energy — has been one of Ferrari's largest competitive disadvantages on high-speed straights. The software update won't change the physics, but better management of when and how the system operates can meaningfully improve lap time.

The most technically complex piece is the "Macarena" rear wing — a redesigned aero element that was tested in China in its Bahrain-specification form and found to have an instability problem: under combined braking and cornering inputs, it couldn't provide sufficient rear stability. Ferrari's engineers are working on a revised version. Monza's long straights make it useful for assessing the wing's behavior under sustained high-speed loads.

Ferrari's Championship Math

Ferrari sits third in the constructors' championship entering Miami. Leclerc is third individually at 49 points, 23 behind championship leader Antonelli. Hamilton, in his first full Ferrari season, is finding his footing with the SF-26 after a challenging opening swing. The April break represents Ferrari's most significant opportunity since preseason to close the gap to Mercedes before the championship takes shape.

If the Monza test confirms that all three packages can race in Miami, the SF-26 that lines up on May 1 will be meaningfully different — and faster — than the one that raced in Japan. Ferrari's technical team has consistently shown the ability to bring effective upgrades in-season. The question is whether the gap to Mercedes is closeable this year, or whether it requires waiting for the full 2027 regulation cycle.

What's Next

The April 22 Monza filming day is the centerpiece of Ferrari's preparation, but the team's testing program runs through the final days before Miami. By the time Hamilton and Leclerc arrive in Florida, Ferrari expects to have a clear picture of what the updated car can do.

For the championship, Miami is where answers start arriving. For Ferrari, those answers will reflect whether the April preparation paid off.
