Antonelli Leads Championship by 9 as Mercedes Staff Signal Teenager Over Russell

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap

April 9, 2026

The numbers say Kimi Antonelli leads the 2026 Formula 1 championship by nine points. Antonelli: 72. Russell: 63. Leclerc: 49. But behind those standings is a story that goes further than points — because according to multiple reports, the people inside Mercedes don't think George Russell is the favorite to win the title, even though he's only nine points back.

That's an extraordinary thing to say about a driver of Russell's caliber — multiple wins, a consistent podium threat, a driver who opened this season with victory in Australia. And it says something significant about what Kimi Antonelli has done in his first full Formula 1 campaign.

What Antonelli Has Built

Three races into the 2026 season, the 19-year-old Italian has won two of them. He won China, lights-to-flag. He won Japan, getting ahead on a safety car restart and controlling the race from there. He became the youngest driver in Formula 1 history to lead the world championship — breaking Lewis Hamilton's record set during the 2007 Spanish Grand Prix, when Hamilton was 22 years, five months, and six days old.

Antonelli was 19 years and 216 days old when he took the Japan victory and the championship lead. That number will be cited for years regardless of how this season ends.

He also won in a way that looked sustainable. Not lucky, not reliant on circumstances. His qualifying pace has been there. His race management has been there. He said publicly heading into the April break that he "must be perfect" to hold off Russell — which is both a sincere assessment and a statement of someone who knows what they're doing.

Russell's Position

George Russell is not out of this championship. He won Australia. He won the China Sprint from pole. He is a driver with real speed and experience, and nine points is a gap that can be erased in a single afternoon. But the internal Mercedes sentiment reflects what the data shows: when Antonelli and Russell have been in equal machinery, the younger man has been faster more often than not this season.

Pirelli is expected to bring softer compounds to Miami, which may suit Russell's driving style. The first Sprint race of the season also means additional championship points available over the weekend. Miami represents Russell's clearest opportunity to reassert himself as the title favorite before Antonelli's lead becomes psychological as well as numerical.

What's Next

Ferrari's Leclerc is 23 points behind Antonelli at 49, and McLaren's Oscar Piastri showed pace with a Japan podium in his first race start of the season. The championship may not be a two-car fight forever. But entering the April break, the central question in Formula 1 is whether a 19-year-old is genuinely better than one of the sport's most accomplished active drivers — and increasingly, the answer from inside his own team looks like yes.
