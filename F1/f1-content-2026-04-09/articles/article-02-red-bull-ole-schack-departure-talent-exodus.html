Ole Schack Set to Leave Red Bull: Another Pillar Falls as Verstappen Watches

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap

April 9, 2026

There's a version of this story that is about a mechanic retiring. But Ole Schack leaving Red Bull Racing is not that story.

Schack, Max Verstappen's personal front-end mechanic for over 20 years, is set to depart the team. He has attended every single Formula 1 Grand Prix since the 2005 Australian Grand Prix — the only member of Red Bull's trackside personnel to hold that unbroken record, dating back to before Red Bull even purchased the former Jaguar operation. His departure represents the loss of a thread connecting this Red Bull to every era of their F1 existence.

A Cascade That Keeps Going

Schack's expected exit does not happen in isolation. It follows, in relatively quick succession: the departure of team principal Christian Horner, the exit of long-time advisor Helmut Marko, the loss of lead strategist Will Courtenay, and the departure of chief designer Craig Skinner. These are not peripheral figures. They are the people who built, managed, and strategically directed the most dominant Formula 1 team of the modern era.

What's left is an organization in transition — a word that feels generous given the pace and seniority of the exits. Red Bull's 2026 challenger, the RB22, has been widely criticized as uncompetitive. Verstappen is ninth in the championship with 12 points after three races. Reports suggest the team has internally discussed abandoning the current car's concept in favor of a redesign — an extraordinary measure that, if true, would consume resources and timeline at precisely the moment Red Bull can least afford the distraction.

What This Means for Verstappen

Verstappen's Red Bull contract contains an exit clause activated if he is not in the top two of the drivers' championship by the summer break. He currently sits 60 points behind championship leader Kimi Antonelli. His personal mechanic is leaving. The strategist is gone. The chief designer is gone. The team principal who presided over four of his championship titles is gone.

ESPN sources have reported that Verstappen is now leaning toward a sabbatical rather than retirement — stepping away from F1 racing in 2027 while remaining with Red Bull in a non-driving capacity, then returning to a team of his choosing when the regulatory picture clarifies. That option becomes more attractive each time another trusted figure walks out the door.

What's Next

Red Bull's FIA meetings involvement continues Thursday and through April 20. The team has to determine whether the RB22's problems are solvable within the current philosophy or require fundamental changes. Helmut Marko, despite initially being reported as retired, was confirmed to still be involved with the team in a driver development capacity — a small signal of continuity amid significant disruption.

For Ole Schack, who has been at Grands Prix across the world for two decades, his story continues separately from Red Bull's. For the team that once felt invincible, another link to that era has now been severed.
