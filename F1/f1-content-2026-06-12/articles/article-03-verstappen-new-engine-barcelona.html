Verstappen Gets New Engine at Barcelona After Monaco DNF — "A Step in the Right Direction"

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

Max Verstappen's 2026 Formula 1 season has been one frustration after another, but the four-time world champion arrives in Barcelona with a piece of good news: Red Bull has identified the cause of his Monaco Grand Prix retirement and fitted a new power unit for this weekend's Spanish GP.

The Monaco DNF — which came on the formation lap when Verstappen lost power releasing the clutch at the start — was one of the most deflating moments of a difficult season. Engineers traced the failure, resolved it, and the new engine is now in the back of RB26. Verstappen described the overall package of changes as "a step in the right direction."

Barcelona as the Real Test

Verstappen was measured in his optimism, and for good reason. He acknowledged that high-speed corners remain a weakness for the 2026 Red Bull — an honest assessment that makes Barcelona a genuine test. This circuit blends slow and medium sections with genuinely fast corners that stress aerodynamic performance, and those high-speed zones have been where Red Bull has struggled most against the new regulations' demands.

He has been clear throughout this season that the 2026 technical regulations — which introduced new engine specifications alongside the aerodynamic rule changes — have not suited Red Bull's development direction. His frustration with the new power unit rules was voiced earlier in the year, when he suggested they could even push him toward retirement. Whether the tone has softened as results have improved slightly remains to be seen.

Signs of Recovery

The Canadian Grand Prix provided a ray of light — Verstappen secured a podium finish in Montreal, a circuit that historically rewards Red Bull's strengths. That result showed a team capable of competing at the front under the right conditions. Barcelona's conditions are different: it is a comprehensive test, not a speciality circuit. If the new engine helps Red Bull in the high-speed sections they've been missing, this could be the weekend the gap to Mercedes and Ferrari starts to close meaningfully.

Verstappen sits seventh in the championship with 43 points and no race victories through seven rounds. In the four previous seasons, he was champion by this stage of the year.

What's Next

Qualifying on Saturday will give the first real indication of where Red Bull stands. If Verstappen can challenge in the top five, the new engine has done its job. If the gap to the Silver Arrows remains, Red Bull faces a harder conversation about the deeper origins of their 2026 difficulties. The answer comes Sunday at 9:00 AM ET on ESPN.
