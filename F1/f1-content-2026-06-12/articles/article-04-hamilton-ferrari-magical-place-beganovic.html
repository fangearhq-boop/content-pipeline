Hamilton Sees Ferrari Reaching a "Magical Place" — Beganovic Takes His FP1 Seat in Barcelona

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

Lewis Hamilton arrived at Barcelona with quiet momentum and a direct message for those tracking Ferrari's progress. Speaking to Sky Sports ahead of the Spanish Grand Prix weekend, the seven-time world champion said that Ferrari is heading toward a "magical place" at the top of the 2026 Formula 1 order.

"I think we can get to a magical place," Hamilton said, building on early 2026 improvement. The comment was not hollow optimism — it came from a driver who has posted back-to-back second-place finishes, leads the internal Ferrari battle over Charles Leclerc, and arrives at one of his most historically successful circuits on the calendar.

Hamilton's Track Record at Barcelona

Hamilton has won at Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya six times in his career — all with Mercedes, and all at a circuit whose characteristics are deeply familiar to him. That institutional knowledge matters: setup direction, tire behavior across a long race, when to push and when to manage. In his first year at Ferrari, bringing that experience to a new car is part of what is making the team's progress feel accelerating.

At 90 championship points, Hamilton is the closest driver outside the two Mercedes cars to Kimi Antonelli's leading 156. He is not yet a title contender on the numbers, but the trajectory is positive, and his confidence is evident in the way he is speaking publicly.

Beganovic in FP1

Friday's first practice session came with a notable wrinkle. Under Formula 1's mandatory rookie running regulation — which requires every full-time driver to yield their seat twice during the season to give a young driver experience — Hamilton's Ferrari was driven by Dino Beganovic in FP1.

Beganovic, 22, is a Swedish driver who has been part of Ferrari's academy since 2020. He currently sits sixth in the F2 championship, where his best result this year was second place in the Miami feature race. This was his third FP1 appearance at a Formula 1 Grand Prix, having run previously in Bahrain and Austria during the 2025 season.

Hamilton returned to the cockpit for FP2 and the remainder of the weekend as normal. The arrangement is entirely routine — seven drivers across the grid made way for rookies on Friday afternoon — but it served as a reminder of the pipeline Ferrari is building for the years ahead.

What's Next

Hamilton takes his seat back on Saturday for qualifying, where Ferrari will look to push into the front row and challenge the Mercedes dominance that Antonelli has built through seven rounds. If the "magical place" is real, Barcelona is where it starts to show. The Spanish GP race is Sunday at 9:00 AM ET on ESPN.
