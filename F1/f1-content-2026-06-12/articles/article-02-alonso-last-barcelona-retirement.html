Fernando Alonso Says Barcelona Is "Probably My Last Home F1 Race" — The End of an Era Draws Near

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

The words came quietly, but they landed heavily. Fernando Alonso, speaking to media at the Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya on Thursday, offered what may have been the most poignant sentence of this Formula 1 season.

"2026 is probably my last Barcelona race in F1."

There was no dramatic announcement, no press conference staging. Just the 44-year-old Aston Martin driver, standing in the paddock of his home circuit, in his home country, acknowledging what the calendar may already have made inevitable.

A Career That Refused to Surrender

Fernando Alonso has 32 Formula 1 victories and two World Championships — earned in 2005 and 2006 — to his name. He won at Barcelona in his pomp, when the Spanish crowd cheered him as a national hero and the entire sport orbited around his rivalry with Michael Schumacher and later Lewis Hamilton.

What made Alonso extraordinary was not just his talent but his stubbornness. When McLaren's partnership with Honda produced cars barely worthy of the name, he drove them with a ferocity that humiliated the equipment's limitations. When Red Bull dominated, he extracted results that looked impossible. He came back to F1 after a mid-career hiatus and won races for Renault and Alpine when many had written him off.

Now at 44, in a 2026 season where Aston Martin has not been competitive, Alonso sits deep in the championship standings. But that has never been the measure of his legacy.

What This Weekend Means

Barcelona is singular for Alonso. This is the circuit where Spanish fans first fell in love with him. The grandstands here have carried his name on banners in three different decades. Racing at home, before a crowd that has always believed in him, carries a weight that no team briefing or strategy note can capture.

The word "probably" leaves technical wiggle room, and Alonso is not one to commit to a narrative he can't control. But the signal is clear. Those who love this sport — and those who have followed Alonso's 24-year journey through it — should watch every lap at Barcelona this weekend with full attention.

What's Next

Alonso will race at Barcelona on Sunday, and then continue the 2026 season through its remaining rounds. There has been no official retirement announcement, and he is contracted through the season with Aston Martin. But when a two-time world champion says the words "probably my last" at his home circuit, Formula 1 has entered a farewell chapter. Savor it.
