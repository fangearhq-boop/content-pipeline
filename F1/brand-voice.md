# F1 Fanrecap — Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality

If F1 Fanrecap were a person, they'd be **the friend who wakes up at 5 AM for every race, knows the tire strategy before the commentators call it, and won't shut up about DRS zones** — the one who's already broken down the undercut window before the pit wall has even radioed. They're technical but never condescending, genuinely thrilled by wheel-to-wheel racing, aware that F1 is equal parts sport and soap opera, and cover the entire grid without defaulting to one team or driver. They have opinions on strategy calls, penalties, and team decisions — and they'll tell you why they're right.

## Voice Attributes

### Technical but Accessible
- **We are**: Aerodynamics-aware, strategy-literate, regulation-informed. We know the difference between an undercut and an overcut, why tire degradation matters, and how DRS activation works — and we explain it so anyone can follow.
- **We are not**: Gatekeeping, jargon-heavy without context, or assuming everyone has an engineering degree.
- **This sounds like**: "Norris pitted on Lap 22 to undercut Leclerc — the hard tires gave him 0.8s/lap more pace and he jumped him by Lap 25."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Well, if you actually understood the Cp distribution over the rear diffuser and its effect on rear-axle downforce..."

### Passionate
- **We are**: Genuinely excited about great racing — a last-lap overtake, a rain-soaked qualifying, a strategy gamble that pays off.
- **We are not**: Over-the-top, fake-hype, or resorting to empty superlatives every post.
- **This sounds like**: "Hamilton went from P9 to P2 on a one-stop while everyone else pitted twice. THAT is racecraft."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "OMG BEST RACE EVER!!! F1 IS THE GREATEST SPORT IN THE WORLD!!!!"

### Global Perspective
- **We are**: Full-grid coverage across all 10 teams and 20 drivers. Midfield battles get the same energy as the fight for P1.
- **We are not**: A one-team fan account pretending to be neutral.
- **This sounds like**: "Haas scoring double points at their home race is a genuinely great story — Magnussen drove the race of his life."
- **This does NOT sound like**: (silence on every team outside the top 3)

### Drama-Aware
- **We are**: Tuned in to team politics, driver rivalries, team orders, and the paddock intrigue that makes F1 compelling beyond the racing.
- **We are not**: Gossip columnists — we report the drama, we don't manufacture it.
- **This sounds like**: "Red Bull telling Perez to let Verstappen through on Lap 45 is going to reignite the team orders debate. Was it the right call for the constructors' championship?"
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Perez should quit, he's basically just a paid moving chicane at this point."

### Opinionated
- **We are**: Willing to take a stance on strategy calls, steward decisions, penalties, and team moves — always backed by evidence.
- **We are not**: Reckless, trolling, or controversial for controversy's sake.
- **This sounds like**: "Hot take: the 5-second penalty for Leclerc was soft. He gained a lasting advantage, left the track, and kept the position. That's a drive-through, not a slap on the wrist."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "FIA is rigged, everyone knows it."

### Respectful
- **We are**: Supportive of drivers and teams. We critique performance and decisions, not character.
- **We are not**: Tearing down drivers personally, attacking their nationality, or piling on after a bad weekend.
- **This sounds like**: "Stroll qualified P18 at a track where Aston Martin expected points. That's a weekend to forget — the team needs answers."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Stroll is only here because of daddy's money. Embarrassing."

## Platform-Specific Voice

### X/Twitter
- Enthusiastic, fast-paced, like live race commentary
- ALL CAPS for emphasis on key stats or moments (use sparingly, 1-2 per post)
- Dashes and periods for dramatic pacing: "P1. Fastest lap. Grand Slam. On a one-stop."
- Strong opinions backed by data
- Max 3 hashtags per tweet
- #F1Fanrecap always included + 2 contextual hashtags (e.g., #F1, #MonacoGP, team/driver tags)
- 1-3 emoji max per post (checkered flag, fire, eyes most common)

### Facebook
- More explanatory — assume the reader needs context on strategy and regulations
- Narrative storytelling across 3-4 paragraphs
- Opinionated but less aggressive than X
- One emoji per caption OK, no emojis in long-form posts
- No hashtags in Facebook posts
- End with a forward-looking statement or question to drive engagement
- Photo caption voice is punchier than long-form

### Articles
- Professional motorsport journalism tone
- Accessible to casual fans — explain strategy, tire compounds, DRS without assuming knowledge
- Stats woven into narrative, not listed in isolation
- Always end with "What's Next" forward-looking section
- No first-person ("I think") — use "the data suggests" or just state the take
- Credit sources: "(per FIA official classification)" or "(via formula1.com)"

## Style Rules

### Grammar & Mechanics

| Rule | F1 Fanrecap Standard | Example |
|------|---------------------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes | "braking, cornering, and tire management" |
| Headings | Title Case | "Top 5 Strategy Calls of the Season" |
| Contractions | Yes, always | "he's" not "he is" |
| Numbers | Spell out 1-9, numerals 10+ | "three pit stops" but "15 laps" |
| Stats | Always numerals | "1:28.432 lap time", "0.8s gap", "25 points" |
| Score format | Finishing order | "Verstappen P1, Norris P2, Hamilton P3" |
| Rankings | P + number | "P1", "P3", "No. 1 in the championship" — never "#1" (creates hashtag) |
| Time format | 12-hour with ET + local noted | "Race start: 9:00 AM ET (2:00 PM local)" |
| ALL CAPS | Sparingly for emphasis | "TWENTY-THREE seconds ahead by the flag" — max 1-2 per post |
| Exclamation marks | OK in social, never in articles | Social: "What an overtake!" Article: never |
| Emoji (X) | 1-3 max per post | Common: checkered flag, fire, eyes, trophy |
| Emoji (Facebook) | 1 max in captions | Never in long-form posts |
| Emoji (Articles) | Never | Articles are emoji-free |

### Terminology

| Use This | Not This | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| constructor name | engine supplier name | "Red Bull" not "Red Bull Honda" unless discussing power units |
| surname after first mention | always full name | "Max Verstappen" first, then "Verstappen" or "VER" in casual context |
| pit stop | pitstop | Two words |
| DRS (explain on first use) | just "DRS" | First mention: "DRS (Drag Reduction System)" |
| tyre (FIA spelling) or tire | Pick one per piece | Either is acceptable; be consistent within a single post/article |
| grid penalty | grid drop | "Grid penalty" is the official FIA term |
| stewards' decision | FIA decision | Stewards make race-day decisions, not "the FIA" |
| constructors' championship | team championship | Official FIA name |
| drivers' championship | world championship | Be specific — "drivers'" or "constructors'" |
| Grand Prix | GP | "Grand Prix" on first mention, "GP" acceptable after |
| sprint | sprint qualifying | "Sprint" is the current official name for the Saturday short race |
