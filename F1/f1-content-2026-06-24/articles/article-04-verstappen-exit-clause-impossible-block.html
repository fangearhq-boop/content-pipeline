Verstappen Exit Clause 'Impossible to Block': What Happens Next for Red Bull's Star

By Elena Voss June 24, 2026

The Clause That Changes Everything

Max Verstappen's contract with Red Bull Racing contains an exit clause tied to his championship position. If he is not positioned in the top two of the drivers' standings by a specified point in the season — reportedly around the summer break — he has the right to leave. He currently sits seventh with 55 points, 101 behind Antonelli. The clause is not theoretical. According to an F1 insider who spoke to GPFans this week, it is now "impossible for Red Bull to block."

That language is significant because it suggests Red Bull explored whether they could contest the clause's activation and found they could not. Teams sometimes build contractual language designed to complicate early departures. In this case, the mechanism is apparently clear-cut: Verstappen is well outside the top two, and the clause is valid.

The Internal Fight at Red Bull

Inside Red Bull's ownership structure, the situation is not straightforward. Shareholders aligned with Thai investor Chalerm Yoovidhya reportedly support exercising a financial buyout to keep Verstappen under contract regardless of the clause. The Mateschitz family and CEO Oliver Mintzlaff are opposed. The debate reflects different visions for what Red Bull Racing is — a marketing vehicle for Verstappen's success, or an engineering project that should survive on its own merits.

What settled the immediate question was Verstappen himself. He declined the buyout offer Red Bull made to him. Manager Raymond Vermeulen confirmed that a decision on Verstappen's 2027 plans will come before the summer break. Whatever is decided will be binding — there is no scenario in which this ambiguity persists through the end of the season.

Where He Goes — or Whether He Does

Mercedes remains the most plausible destination if Verstappen leaves Red Bull. Toto Wolff has publicly described himself as "content" with Antonelli and George Russell, which in F1 terms means nothing until it means everything. Verstappen alongside Antonelli at Mercedes would be the most expensive and most scrutinized driver pairing in the sport's history.

Verstappen has also introduced a different possibility. In comments this year, he has hinted at genuine dissatisfaction with the 2026 technical regulations, suggesting that retirement from Formula 1 is not off the table. His first podium of the 2026 season came in Canada, Round 6. For a driver who won four consecutive world championships and 58 races between 2021 and 2024, the current situation is a significant shift.

What's Next

The Austrian GP this weekend puts Verstappen at the circuit he has dominated for the better part of a decade. He has six wins at Red Bull Ring. He will qualify and race on Friday and Sunday with the exit clause activated and no decision made. Vermeulen's timeline — before the summer break — means that by August, the situation will be resolved. Watch for signals from Verstappen's Austrian GP press conference for any indication of which direction he is leaning.
