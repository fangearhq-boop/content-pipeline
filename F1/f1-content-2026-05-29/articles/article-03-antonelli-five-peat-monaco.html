Antonelli's Five-Peat: Can the Championship Leader Win Monaco and Make History?

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | May 29, 2026

The numbers Kimi Antonelli has produced in the first five weeks of the 2026 Formula 1 season defy straightforward comparison. Four races. Four pole positions. Four victories. The 19-year-old Italian leads the drivers' championship by 43 points over his Mercedes teammate George Russell. Nothing in the modern era quite matches what he has done.

Monaco is next. And Monaco, historically, is where dominant drivers meet the greatest circuit in the world and are reminded that Formula 1 is never entirely predictable.

What Makes Monaco Different

The Circuit de Monaco is not won by speed alone. It is won by qualifying excellence, tire strategy, patience, and an ability to read the narrow streets with millimeter precision. Drivers who are fastest on the raw charts at power-sensitive circuits often find Monaco levels the playing field in ways that pure championship mathematics do not.

Antonelli has been dominant on circuits where pace is the defining factor. Canada — a power-sensitive layout where Mercedes's straight-line speed is an asset — was another demonstration of his superiority in 2026. But Monaco's 19 corners demand something slightly different: absolute car control in extremely tight confines where a brush of the barrier ends not just a lap but a race.

Neither Antonelli nor Russell has won in Monaco. Mercedes, while building a remarkable 2026 car, has not historically been among the dominant forces at Monte Carlo the way Ferrari has. This creates genuine uncertainty about whether Antonelli's run can continue or whether the principality breaks the streak.

The Magnitude of a Fifth Win

If Antonelli wins Monaco, he will have achieved something that does not have a direct precedent in the modern Formula 1 era. Five consecutive victories from five consecutive poles, at the age of 19, starting with his first four races would represent a level of dominance that analysts would be writing about for decades.

The pressure is entirely external — Antonelli has shown remarkable composure under scrutiny all season. His Canadian GP performance, managing a tense battle with Russell while controlling championship momentum, demonstrated that the championship weight does not appear to slow him down.

What's Next

Practice begins at the Circuit de Monaco on Friday, June 5 (FP1: 12:30 PM ET, FP2: 4:00 PM ET on Apple TV). Qualifying is Saturday, June 6 at 3:00 PM ET, followed by the Monaco Grand Prix on Sunday, June 7 at 2:00 PM ET.

Ferrari will believe they have an advantage. Hamilton and Leclerc will try to make that belief a reality. Verstappen, with his Monaco-specific wins in 2021 and 2023, remains a wildcard. Russell will be desperate to end his winning drought after Canada's DNF.

Antonelli arrives as the favorite by virtue of his season-long form. But Monaco has a way of creating its own narrative. Whether the 19-year-old's remarkable run continues or whether the principality finally shows him its other face — that is the story of the coming week.
