Hamilton's "Happiest Day" at Ferrari Sets Up Monaco's Final Frontier

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | May 29, 2026

Lewis Hamilton has won 103 Formula 1 races. He has won seven World Championships. He has driven through rain, snow, and controversy to set records that may stand for a generation. And after everything he has accomplished, after everything he has seen, one thing has consistently evaded him: winning in Monaco.

He goes to the principality next week carrying momentum that hasn't felt like this in Ferrari red before. After finishing second at the Canadian Grand Prix — overtaking Max Verstappen in the closing laps to claim his best result since joining the Scuderia — Hamilton called it "my happiest day at Ferrari so far." He stood on the podium feeling, in his words, "very light." A 41-year-old champion rediscovering what joy feels like in the car.

What Changed in Canada

Canada was the weekend where Hamilton's 2026 form crystallized into something tangible. He out-qualified teammate Charles Leclerc for both the Sprint race and the Grand Prix — a significant statement given that Leclerc is one of the grid's fastest qualifiers. He raced intelligently through traffic, stayed out of trouble, and in the final laps found a way past the Red Bull of Verstappen when it mattered.

It was his second podium of 2026, following an earlier result at the Chinese Grand Prix in March. But Canada felt different. The margin was clear. The execution was precise. The emotion was real.

"I feel very light right now," Hamilton said after the race, speaking directly to the relief and happiness that came with finally delivering at the level he knows he's capable of in Ferrari red. Ferrari's report from the weekend confirmed his composure throughout — he was "back on the podium" and his recovery from last season's difficult debut has been one of the most compelling stories of the 2026 championship.

The Monaco Window

Hamilton's history in Monaco is fascinating precisely because it reveals something about the nature of this sport. His record at every other circuit in the world is extraordinary. Monaco — where he has finished on the podium multiple times, where he came desperately close in 2008 and other years — has simply refused to give him a win.

The Ferrari SF-26 may be the best car he has ever had at Monaco. The slow-corner characteristics that made Ferrari competitive all season are ideally suited to Monte Carlo's technical demands. If the car qualifies near the front of the grid on Saturday afternoon, Hamilton will be in a position to fight for a win that would mean as much to him as any championship he has ever claimed.

He is fourth in the drivers' championship with 72 points — three behind Leclerc, 59 behind Antonelli. A Monaco victory would dramatically change the complexion of his season. More than the points, it would close a career chapter that has remained unfinished for two decades.

What's Next

The Monaco Grand Prix weekend begins Friday, June 5 with practice sessions at 12:30 PM ET and 4:00 PM ET. Qualifying is Saturday, June 6 at 3:00 PM ET. The race is Sunday, June 7 at 2:00 PM ET. All sessions air on Apple TV.

There are no guarantees in Monaco. Antonelli has been unstoppable in 2026. Leclerc has the home-track advantage and may out-qualify everyone. Circumstances on the narrow streets can end a perfect race weekend in an instant.

But Hamilton goes to Monaco in better form, better spirit, and with a better car than at any point in his Ferrari tenure. His happiest day at the team was last Sunday. The next happiest one could be a week from this Sunday.
