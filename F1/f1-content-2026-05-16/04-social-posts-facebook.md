### STORY 1: Verstappen at Nürburgring 24 Hours

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

While the rest of the F1 paddock prepares for Canada next weekend, Max Verstappen has chosen a rather different way to spend his off-weekend.

The four-time Formula 1 world champion made his debut at the legendary Nürburgring 24 Hours endurance race on Saturday, driving a Mercedes-AMG GT3. His team qualified fourth on the grid for the race, which started at 2 PM BST and will run for exactly 24 hours around the Nordschleife — one of the most dangerous and demanding racetracks in the world.

Verstappen described qualifying as "super difficult," which from a driver of his calibre tells you everything about the challenge this circuit presents. The Nürburgring's Nordschleife layout is nearly 26 km long and features over 100 corners, dramatic elevation changes, and weather that can shift from sunshine to fog to rain within a single hour.

This is very different from Formula 1. Endurance racing demands patience, consistency, and teamwork over 24 hours rather than the sprint excellence that defines F1. But then again, Verstappen has never shied away from a challenge.

We'll have the result Sunday. The question is: can the reigning (well, former) champion conquer the Green Hell?

### STORY 2: Lambiase-McLaren Saga — Verstappen's Future

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM ET

The most consequential off-track story in Formula 1 this season just got more complicated.

Red Bull has confirmed that Gianpiero Lambiase — Verstappen's race engineer since 2016 and the man who has been on the radio for all four of his world championships — is leaving for McLaren. The exact role is disputed: Red Bull's Laurent Mekies says Lambiase will become McLaren's team principal, while McLaren CEO Zak Brown insists the team already has a team principal (Andrea Stella, who he calls "the best in the pit lane").

What we know for certain: Lambiase is going. Verstappen gave him his blessing — "I couldn't tell him this directly, but I absolutely think he should do it" — and Verstappen himself admitted that McLaren convinced Lambiase with "a lot of money."

The chemistry between Verstappen and Lambiase has been one of F1's most celebrated driver-engineer partnerships. "That's very rare in racing," Verstappen said. "You don't see that often that a driver and engineer, they gel that well together."

Now the question everyone is asking: does Verstappen follow his engineer? He has an exit clause in his Red Bull contract that kicks in at the Hungarian Grand Prix (July 24-26). McLaren's Zak Brown is publicly dismissing the speculation — "zero intention of changing our driver lineup" — but this story is far from over.

### STORY 4: Canadian Grand Prix Preview

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM ET

The Formula 1 circus heads to Montreal for the Canadian Grand Prix — and this year it comes with a twist.

For the first time in the history of the Canadian Grand Prix (which has run since 1967), the race will feature a sprint format weekend. Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve has hosted some of F1's most dramatic moments, but it has never seen an F1 sprint race. That changes May 22-24.

The schedule: Sprint qualifying on Friday, Sprint race on Saturday morning, full qualifying on Saturday afternoon, and the Grand Prix on Sunday. The Wall of Champions — the notorious chicane that has claimed the cars of world champions including Michael Schumacher, Damon Hill, and Jacques Villeneuve — looms as always.

Montreal's circuit is one of the lowest-downforce tracks on the calendar. Long straights reward engine power and DRS efficiency, while the stop-start chicanes and the hairpin reward braking precision. It's a circuit where strategy, safety car timing, and avoiding the barriers can matter as much as pure pace.

Oh, and the race is in May for the first time. Usually held in mid-June, this year's calendar shift brings Canada into spring. Expect cooler temperatures and a real chance of showers.

McLaren is in the fight but needs more consistency. And Max Verstappen? The four-time champion is seventh with just 26 points — over 70 points behind the leader — as Red Bull continues to struggle to find the pace of their rivals in the new 2026 regulations.

With Canada next weekend and the European summer approaching, the battle is far from over. But Antonelli's momentum is hard to ignore.
