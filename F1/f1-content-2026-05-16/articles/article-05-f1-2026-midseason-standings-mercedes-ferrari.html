F1 2026 Midseason Check: Mercedes Pulling Away as Ferrari Scrambles and Verstappen Struggles

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

Six events into the 2026 Formula 1 season, the championship picture is becoming clearer — and it's one dominated by silver.

Kimi Antonelli leads the Drivers' Championship with 100 points. His Mercedes teammate George Russell is second with 80. Ferrari's Charles Leclerc sits third on 59 points, followed by McLaren's Lando Norris and Ferrari's Lewis Hamilton (both 51 points). Oscar Piastri (McLaren) is sixth with 43, and Max Verstappen (Red Bull) is seventh with just 26 points — a full 74 behind the championship leader.

Mercedes: Dominant in Both Titles

The numbers tell the story clearly. Antonelli and Russell have combined for 180 points in the Drivers' Championship — far ahead of any other team's combined total. In the Constructors' Championship, Mercedes leads with that total, ahead of Ferrari (Leclerc 59 + Hamilton 51 = 110 points).

Antonelli's three consecutive victories from pole position are the headline, but Russell's consistent points-scoring has been equally crucial. Mercedes have built a team where both drivers are genuine points threats every weekend, and their car's pace has been the class of the field in the new 2026 regulations.

Ferrari's Fading Lead

The most dramatic shift in 2026 has been Ferrari's constructors' position. After the Japanese Grand Prix, Ferrari led Mercedes by 44 points in the Constructors' Championship — a formidable advantage that suggested a Ferrari title challenge was very much alive.

Then came Miami, and a post-race disaster. Leclerc was handed a 20-second time penalty for cutting the circuit on the final lap of the race — a penalty that cost the team crucial points and sent the constructors' lead tumbling from 44 points to just 16. Hamilton's Miami weekend was also disrupted by a first-lap collision with Franco Colapinto that compromised his race.

Ferrari remain in the fight, but the momentum has shifted unmistakably toward Brackley.

Verstappen and Red Bull: A Championship at a Crossroads

Perhaps the most striking figure in the standings is Max Verstappen's tally of 26 points. The four-time champion, who dominated the sport through 2023 and 2024, is seventh in the championship — over 70 points behind Antonelli. Red Bull has clearly not unlocked the new 2026 regulations the way Mercedes has.

Combined with the Lambiase-to-McLaren saga and the looming exit clause at the Hungarian GP in July, Verstappen's place in the 2026 championship narrative feels increasingly like a subplot rather than the main story. That's a remarkable shift for the driver who has been F1's central figure for the past four years.

What's Next

The Canadian Grand Prix (May 22-24, Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve) is the next event, marking the first sprint format weekend ever held in Montreal. With Antonelli leading and Mercedes in fine form, the question is whether anyone can disrupt the championship leader before the European summer season truly begins. Ferrari needs a strong Montreal. McLaren needs more consistency. And Verstappen needs Red Bull to find answers — fast. Canada will be a telling early indicator of whether the standings gap is set to widen further or whether the championship field is about to tighten up.
