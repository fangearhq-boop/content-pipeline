Lambiase to McLaren: What Verstappen's Engineer's Departure Means for the Four-Time Champion's Future

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

In Formula 1, the driver-engineer partnership is one of the most intimate and consequential relationships in sport. For Max Verstappen, that partnership has been with Gianpiero Lambiase since 2016 — and now, it is coming to an end.

Red Bull has confirmed that Lambiase, who has been Verstappen's race engineer throughout his four championship-winning seasons, is leaving the team for McLaren. The exact timing remains tied to his contract, which Red Bull says runs until the end of 2027. McLaren have not confirmed his precise role, though Red Bull boss Laurent Mekies says Lambiase will become McLaren's team principal — a claim that McLaren CEO Zak Brown has complicated by insisting that role is already filled by Andrea Stella.

The specifics of Lambiase's McLaren role matter less than the broader implication: after nearly a decade together, one of F1's most celebrated driver-engineer partnerships is being dissolved.

What Verstappen Has Said

Verstappen has been unusually candid about the relationship and about Lambiase's departure. He gave Lambiase his blessing to make the switch — and he was honest about why McLaren won: "a lot of money."

But his deeper feelings about the partnership were clear. "The general understanding that we have with each other, and the chemistry works really well," Verstappen said. "That's very rare in racing. You don't see that often that a driver and engineer, they gel that well together."

That admission — that what he had with Lambiase is rare — makes the departure more significant. It's not just a job change. It's the end of a working relationship that produced four world championships and redefined the standard of F1 dominance.

The Exit Clause Question

Verstappen's Red Bull contract contains an exit clause that becomes available at the Hungarian Grand Prix, scheduled for July 24-26. Should Verstappen choose to activate it, he would be free to explore the market.

McLaren's Zak Brown has been emphatic in dismissing any possibility of signing Verstappen: "I couldn't be happier with our driver line-up. Lando and Oscar are not only two awesome guys, on and off the track, but as teammates... Zero intention of changing." That's a clear public statement. But in Formula 1, public statements and private conversations often diverge significantly.

The question for Verstappen is not simply whether McLaren would take him — it's whether he wants to stay at a Red Bull team that has struggled in the new 2026 regulations, where he sits seventh in the championship. The dominant machine of his title years has not materialized in 2026.

What's Next

The Hungarian Grand Prix exit clause window opens in late July, and all eyes in F1 will be on what Verstappen decides. In the meantime, the priority is Canada (May 22-24) and finding the pace Red Bull has been missing. Lambiase remains at Red Bull through his contract period, so the partnership isn't over yet — but the countdown has begun. For a driver defined by his partnership with one engineer, the transition to a new working relationship could be one of the most telling tests of Verstappen's career.
