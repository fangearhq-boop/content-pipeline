Antonelli Leads F1 2026 Championship at 100 Points — Canada Is Next

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

Three races into the 2026 Formula 1 season, Kimi Antonelli has done something that has never been done in the sport's history: won from pole position in three consecutive events.

The 19-year-old Mercedes driver leads the drivers' championship with 100 points — a 20-point advantage over his teammate George Russell (80 points), who has himself been an outstanding No. 2 this season. Ferrari's Charles Leclerc (59 points) and the McLaren duo of Lando Norris and Lewis Hamilton (51 points each) trail further behind.

The Canadian Grand Prix (May 22-24, Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve, Montreal) is next. Can Antonelli make it four wins from four races?

Historic Achievement

Antonelli's hat-trick of pole-to-win results is unprecedented in Formula 1. Converting one pole into a victory is impressive. Converting three consecutive poles into three consecutive victories, without any rivals finding gaps to exploit, represents genuine dominance.

His season has been a remarkable story from the start. Stepping into the seat vacated by Lewis Hamilton at Mercedes — widely considered the most scrutinized driver change in the sport's recent history — Antonelli arrived with enormous expectations and enormous skepticism. He has answered both with results.

At just 19 years old, he is the youngest driver ever to lead the Formula 1 World Championship at this point in a season. He is being compared to Sebastian Vettel's record of youngest champion (23 years and 134 days, set in 2010) as a real, if distant, possibility.

The Russell Factor

Antonelli's success hasn't come at the expense of his teammate. Russell, with 80 points and a steady stream of strong finishes, is the second-best driver in the championship. The two Mercedes drivers have combined for 180 points — a formidable total for the constructors' standings.

Russell is a genuine threat to Antonelli within the team. Canada will test the intra-team dynamic as both push for their best results at a circuit that rewards racecraft and strategy as much as raw pace.

What's Next

The Canadian Grand Prix (May 22-24) at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve will be the first sprint format weekend ever held at the historic Montreal circuit. Sprint qualifying takes place Friday, the sprint race Saturday morning, full qualifying Saturday afternoon, and the Grand Prix on Sunday. Montreal's low-downforce layout, long straights, and famous Wall of Champions chicane will challenge every driver and team. Whether Antonelli can extend his unprecedented run to four consecutive pole-to-win results is the question everyone in F1 will be watching. Based on his 2026 form so far, betting against him feels reckless.
