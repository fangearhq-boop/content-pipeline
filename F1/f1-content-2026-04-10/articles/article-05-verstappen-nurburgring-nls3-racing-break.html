Verstappen Takes to the Nurburgring as Red Bull Regroups During F1's Five-Week Break

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap

April 10, 2026

When the Formula 1 car is not cooperating, Max Verstappen finds another track to race on.

While the Red Bull engineers work to address the RB22's fundamental shortcomings during the extended break between the Japanese Grand Prix and the Miami Grand Prix, Verstappen is targeting Saturday's Nurburgring Langstrecken-Serie (NLS) Round 3 race — April 11 — at the legendary Nurburgring Nordschleife. He drives the Mercedes-AMG GT3 for his own team, Verstappen Racing, in car No. 3.

The Nurburgring activities are not a one-off diversion. Verstappen has confirmed participation in the NLS qualifier races on April 18-19, with the 24 Hours of Nurburgring on May 16 as the season highlight for this sportscar program. The calendar works neatly around the F1 schedule: the Miami GP is May 1-3, giving him time to return to the Nordschleife for the 24H race before the next F1 event.

The Competitive Outlet

Verstappen has never been a driver who is content to wait. The competitive DNA that drove him to four consecutive World Championships — and to victories from his first F1 start at age 18 — does not allow for passive waiting while engineers solve problems. The Nurburgring Nordschleife, the 20.8km circuit known as the "Green Hell" and one of the most demanding tracks in the world, provides the kind of challenge that keeps a racing driver sharp.

The GT3 Mercedes is a different discipline from the RB22. Where the F1 car in 2026 has been frustrating and unresponsive to Verstappen's inputs, the AMG GT3 is a machine he knows well — a car he can exploit to its limits and enjoy the feedback from. The sportscar racing community has welcomed his presence, and the Verstappen Racing entry is treated as a serious competitor in the NLS field.

Context: The F1 Situation

Verstappen's Nurburgring program is happening against the backdrop of the most difficult stretch of his Formula 1 career. He is ninth in the championship with 16 points, 60 behind Kimi Antonelli. He has called the 2026 cars "terrible" and "Mario Kart." His longtime front-end mechanic Ole Schack is set to leave Red Bull. His exit clause — triggered if results don't improve between August and October — is counting down.

ESPN sources have reported that Verstappen is leaning toward a sabbatical from F1 in 2027 rather than retirement, with the intention of returning when either the regulations change or a more competitive drive becomes available. The Nurburgring activities are consistent with that framing: he is not walking away from racing, he is simply finding ways to compete while his F1 situation resolves itself.

What's Next

Max Verstappen's Nurburgring Langstrecken-Serie activities begin Saturday, April 11 (NLS Round 3, subject to confirmation), with qualifier races on April 18-19 confirmed. The 24 Hours of Nurburgring follows on May 16, after the Miami Grand Prix (May 1-3). Back in Milton Keynes, Red Bull use the same five-week window to develop the RB22 before the season restarts. Whether the team can make meaningful progress in time for Miami will determine whether Verstappen returns to the F1 paddock with optimism — or with his exit clause conversations growing louder.
