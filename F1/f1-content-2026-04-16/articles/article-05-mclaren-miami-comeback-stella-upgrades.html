Stella's Promise: McLaren Will "Fight for More Important Positions" at Miami

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap

April 16, 2026

Andrea Stella is not a man who makes promises he can't keep. The McLaren team principal, who guided the team through a rebuild and back toward championship contention in recent seasons, has stated clearly what he expects from his upgraded car at the Miami Grand Prix: McLaren will be in position to fight for results that matter.

"We will be in condition to fight for more important positions," Stella said when describing McLaren's plans for Miami. "I'm confident that McLaren will be in condition to compete for podiums and victories on merit within this season."

Given what the 2026 season has looked like so far, those words represent a significant step-up in expectation setting.

A Difficult Start to the New Era

The 2026 regulations were supposed to represent a reset — a new formula that would give every team a chance to start fresh. McLaren's opening round in Australia was competitive but not brilliant. The Chinese Grand Prix was a disaster: both Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri suffered separate electrical power unit failures before the race even began, producing a double DNS that left the team with zero points from a circuit where they had legitimate podium expectations.

Norris currently sits fifth in the championship with 25 points; Piastri sixth with 21. McLaren is fourth in the Constructors', with 56 points — 79 behind leaders Mercedes. For a team of their resources and engineering talent, the gap between their potential and their results has been painful.

The April Development Push

The five-week break created by the Bahrain and Saudi race cancellations has given McLaren exactly the time their development program needed. The team participated in a Pirelli tyre test at the Nürburgring earlier this week, gathering real-world data to complement their simulator and wind tunnel work.

Stella has confirmed that aerodynamic efficiency upgrades are on the way, with the Miami Grand Prix serving as the launch point. "These are happening in the next couple of events," he explained, suggesting the development push is a staged process rather than a single transformative upgrade. The direction is clear: McLaren needs to reduce drag and improve aerodynamic efficiency under the 2026 regulations, where the energy-harvesting demands of the new power unit architecture interact directly with chassis downforce and drag characteristics.

What's Next

McLaren's upgraded MCL40 arrives in Miami for the sprint weekend beginning May 1. The sprint format gives Norris and Piastri two standing starts, two chances to demonstrate that the aerodynamic efficiency upgrades translate into on-track pace. If the car performs to Stella's expectations, McLaren could re-enter the conversation around race wins much sooner than the standings currently suggest. That scenario — a fourth team genuinely capable of challenging Mercedes — would transform the 2026 championship from an internal Mercedes battle into a season-long spectacle. Stella is betting on it.
