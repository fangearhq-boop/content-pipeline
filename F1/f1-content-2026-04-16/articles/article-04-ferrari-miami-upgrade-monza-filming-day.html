Ferrari's April "Mini Pre-Season": What the Monza Filming Day Means for Miami

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap

April 16, 2026

The five-week gap between the Japanese Grand Prix and the Miami Grand Prix was created by circumstances — the cancellation of the Bahrain and Saudi Arabian races following the escalation of the conflict in the Middle East. Ferrari intends to use every day of it.

The Scuderia has scheduled a filming day at Monza on April 22, which functions in practice as a development test for the upgrade package destined for the Miami sprint weekend. Monza was selected deliberately: the high-speed, low-downforce nature of the Italian circuit imposes demanding conditions on energy management systems, making it an ideal venue to validate components that will face similar challenges at Miami's fast sweepers and long straights.

What's Being Tested

The April 22 session is expected to evaluate additional aerodynamic changes, weight reduction modifications, and cooling management components designed for Miami's combination of heat, humidity, and high energy demand. Ferrari's engineers have identified cooling as a particular challenge for a circuit like Miami, and the revised components should provide data on whether their solutions perform as projected before they commit to running them in a race weekend environment.

Lewis Hamilton and Charles Leclerc are both expected to be involved in the Monza filming day — giving both drivers time in the updated car before they arrive in Florida.

The Championship Context

Ferrari sits 45 points behind Mercedes in the Constructors' Championship after three rounds — Mercedes has 135 points to Ferrari's 90. That gap is significant but not yet decisive at this early stage of the season. Leclerc has 49 drivers' points and Hamilton has 41, placing both of them within striking distance of the Mercedes duo (Antonelli 72, Russell 63) if Ferrari can deliver meaningful performance gains at Miami.

Ferrari has been competitive at key moments in the opening three rounds — Hamilton's podium in China was evidence that the SF-26 is capable of challenging the Silver Arrows when everything works — and the team's race start advantage has been one of the few areas where they've genuinely outperformed Mercedes.

The Miami upgrade package is aimed at converting that competitive promise into raw performance. A 45-point deficit over 19 remaining races is entirely recoverable if Ferrari arrives in Florida with a meaningfully faster car.

What's Next

Ferrari's filming day at Monza on April 22 is the final step in their April development program before the car ships to Miami. The Miami Grand Prix weekend runs May 1 through May 3, with sprint qualifying on Friday and the sprint race on Saturday. For Ferrari, the sprint format provides an extra standing start and extra championship points — two opportunities to capitalize on their launch advantage and demonstrate whether the Miami upgrade package has delivered what they've been building toward since the Japanese Grand Prix.
