Ferrari's Best Shot at a 2026 Win: Why Monaco Is Different for Leclerc and Hamilton

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | June 5, 2026

Five races into the 2026 Formula 1 season, Ferrari has not won. Mercedes has taken all five victories. Kimi Antonelli has four of them.

Monaco is where Ferrari believes that changes.

The reasoning is not wishful thinking — it is technical. The Principality's narrow streets, low-speed hairpins, and absence of the sustained high-speed straights that have allowed Mercedes to deploy their power unit advantage throughout the season create a fundamentally different competitive environment. At Monaco, the factors that have determined the 2026 season so far matter less. The factors that have always defined Leclerc's driving — precision, car feel, control through walls — matter more.

Friday's opening practice confirmed the early signs. Leclerc topped FP1 with a time of 1 minute 11.964 seconds, 0.163 seconds ahead of Verstappen in second. Lewis Hamilton, Leclerc's Ferrari teammate, was not far off. Ferrari had the pace early.

Leclerc's Case

The numbers around Leclerc at Monaco are remarkable. He won here in 2024. He has three pole positions at this circuit across his career. He grew up in Monaco — he knows these streets in a literal sense that goes beyond any data sheet or simulator session.

Ferrari's bookmakers' odds heading into the weekend reflect that belief: Leclerc is the race favorite at +185. That is not a small edge. It represents a genuine collective expectation that Ferrari and Leclerc have the combination to convert this weekend into the win the Italian team has been waiting all season to achieve.

One additional technical factor works in Leclerc's favor this weekend. The FIA's active aero ban — the regulation that outlaws "Straight Mode," where drivers deploy maximum active aerodynamic assistance on long straights — provides no benefit at Monaco. No section of the Monte Carlo circuit runs for long enough to trigger the three-second activation threshold. That means the power unit advantage Mercedes has exploited on faster circuits essentially disappears. The playing field is the most level it has been all year.

Hamilton's Role

Lewis Hamilton finished second at the Canadian Grand Prix — his best result since joining Ferrari — and described it as "the happiest day" of his time with the team. The context matters: Hamilton has been adapting to a new car, a new engineering environment, and the specific demands of the Scuderia since the beginning of the year. Canada showed that the adaptation is working.

Hamilton is a seven-time world champion who has won in Monaco multiple times. He brings experience and circuit knowledge to a weekend where those qualities are directly translatable to lap time. If Ferrari can get both drivers into Q3 in competitive positions on Saturday, the Scuderia will have two genuine race-win candidates in the best possible starting slots.

That has not happened yet in 2026. Monaco is the circuit where it could.

What's Next

Free Practice 2 runs later Friday afternoon, with FP3 Saturday morning before qualifying. The Monaco Grand Prix takes place Sunday June 7 at 9:00 AM ET on Apple TV+. For Ferrari, the entire weekend comes down to Saturday — securing track position on a circuit where it is nearly impossible to overtake once the race begins. If Leclerc and Hamilton can front-row lock out qualifying, Sunday becomes Ferrari's opportunity to deliver on what has been months of expectation and a season of near-misses.

The defending Monaco champion, driving for his home circuit team, with the fastest practice session of the weekend already behind him. This is Ferrari's moment. All that is left is to take it.
