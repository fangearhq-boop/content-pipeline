Leclerc Fastest in Monaco FP1 Despite Stroll Collision and Red Flag Drama

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | June 5, 2026

Charles Leclerc topped the opening practice session for the 2026 Monaco Grand Prix with the fastest time of the day — a 1 minute 11.964 seconds — even though nine minutes of it were spent under a red flag after Lance Stroll drove across him at the Loewes Hairpin and broke his front wing.

The collision set the tone for what already promises to be a chaotic Monaco weekend. Stroll moved back onto the racing line just as Leclerc was arriving, leaving the Ferrari driver nowhere to go. Debris scattered across the track. Stroll's Aston Martin required a gearbox change. FIA stewards later found Stroll "wholly to blame" and handed him a one-place grid drop.

Leclerc walked back to the garage, replaced the front wing, and then went out and posted the fastest time in the session — 0.163 seconds quicker than Max Verstappen in second, with Lando Norris third.

This is Monaco. Drama is not a bug. It is the feature.

The Collision and Its Consequences

FP1 was barely underway when the incident unfolded at the hairpin. Stroll had been on a slow lap when he began moving back toward the inside of the corner — standard procedure on an out-lap. But Leclerc was already there on a flying lap, and the Aston Martin cut directly across his path. The contact was unavoidable from Leclerc's perspective.

The red flag was out for several minutes while marshals cleared the debris. Stroll's car was badly damaged — the rear suspension was compromised, and the gearbox had to be swapped, which triggered an automatic five-place grid penalty in addition to the stewards' one-place drop for causing the collision. Stroll will have a significant grid deficit to overcome on Saturday.

Leclerc's front wing took the brunt of the impact, but the chassis was undamaged. Ferrari had a replacement fitted quickly, and Leclerc returned to work without apparent further incident.

Leclerc's Day

The 1:11.964 is a strong early benchmark for Monaco, though FP2 later Friday and FP3 on Saturday will provide better data as teams optimize their setups for qualifying. On this circuit, pole position is decisive — overtaking is nearly impossible once the race begins — so every tenth in qualifying will matter enormously.

Leclerc is the 2024 Monaco winner. He has three Monaco pole positions in his career. He grew up in the Principality. This is not a circuit where Ferrari's 2026 struggles should necessarily translate — Monaco rewards the driver and car combination that finds a clean, precise rhythm through the barriers, and Leclerc has consistently been among the very best at doing exactly that.

Verstappen was 0.163 seconds back in second. Norris was third. The gap to the Mercedes of Kimi Antonelli and George Russell — who have been the championship pace-setters all season — was not revealed in the early session results, but the opening day belonged to the Scuderia.

What's Next

Free Practice 2 takes place Friday afternoon at Frost Bank Center, with teams expected to run longer stints to gather tire data. Qualifying is Saturday, and the race is Sunday June 7 at 9:00 AM ET on Apple TV+. Ferrari's objective is clear: translate FP1's fastest time into a front-row lockout on Saturday and give Leclerc — and perhaps Lewis Hamilton, who finished second at the Canadian Grand Prix — the track position they need to finally secure Ferrari's first 2026 victory.

Stroll will start from further back than intended. Leclerc will start from wherever he qualifies, with all the momentum of a Monaco FP1 session that he refused to let go of. That feels like the appropriate way to begin a Monaco week.
