Kimi Antonelli Is Leading F1 at 19 — The Numbers Behind a Historic Season

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

April 12, 2026

When Lewis Hamilton led the 2007 Formula 1 World Championship for the first time at the Spanish Grand Prix, he was 22 years and 4 months old. The record stood for 19 years. It fell on March 29, 2026, when Kimi Antonelli crossed the finish line in Suzuka at 19 years and 7 months old — making the youngest championship leader in the history of the sport.

Antonelli now holds 72 points. His nearest rival, Mercedes teammate George Russell, has 63. Third-placed Charles Leclerc (Ferrari) sits at 49. The championship lead entering what is effectively a five-week intermission — before the Miami Grand Prix on May 1-3 — is nine points over his own teammate, which reflects how completely Mercedes has dominated the early 2026 season and how decisively Antonelli has led that domination.

Three Races, Two Wins, One Name on Top

Antonelli opened 2026 in Australia, China, and Japan. The results were remarkable for a driver in just his second year of F1 competition. He won in China and Japan — lights to flag at Suzuka in a dominant display — and accumulated enough points across all three weekends to open a gap not just over the field but over Russell, who is himself a race winner and one of the most technically profished drivers in the paddock.

What separates Antonelli from other young talents who have made early-season splashes is the quality of competition he has beaten. Russell is not a foil — he is genuinely capable of winning this championship. Leclerc is a multiple race winner. Hamilton, in his first season with Ferrari, is still among the most experienced drivers in the world. Antonelli has beaten all of them on points.

The Championship Picture

The full Drivers' standings after Japan: Antonelli 72, Russell 63, Leclerc 49, Hamilton 41, Norris 25, Piastri 21, Bearman 17, Gasly 15, Verstappen 12. The Constructors' standings: Mercedes 135, Ferrari 90, McLaren 46, Red Bull 16.

The gap from Antonelli to the first non-Mercedes driver (Leclerc) is already 23 points — more than a race win in the sprint-weekend era. The gap from Mercedes to Ferrari in Constructors is 45 points. These numbers suggest structural dominance, not lucky form. But Formula 1 championships have turned on fewer things than a five-week development window and a binding regulatory vote.

What's Next

Antonelli's championship lead faces its first major test in Miami. The sprint weekend format — with sprint qualifying, a sprint race, full qualifying, and the Grand Prix itself — provides four distinct moments where the standings can shift. Red Bull will arrive with a comprehensive upgrade designed to close their one-second-per-lap deficit. Ferrari tests their revised aero package at Monza on April 22. The FIA may change the energy rules before a wheel is even turned in anger. Antonelli enters Miami as the youngest championship leader in Formula 1 history, and as the man every other driver is trying to catch.
