Verstappen Returns to the Nordschleife: 24H Qualifiers Are Next

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

April 12, 2026

Max Verstappen won the NLS2 at the Nurburgring in March. Then it was taken away. He was supposed to return for the NLS3 on April 11. He couldn't. Now he turns his attention to the 24H Nurburgring Qualifiers on April 18-19 — and by all accounts, the Green Hell feels more like home to him right now than anything Formula 1 is offering.

"It brings a big smile," Verstappen said recently about racing at the Nordschleife. In a season where the RB22 has been difficult, unreliable, and — in his teammate's assessment — "terrible," the Nurburgring provides something Formula 1 is temporarily not: a circuit where he can compete without the machinery undermining him.

The NLS2 Disqualification

The context matters. In March, Verstappen and the #3 Winward Racing Mercedes-AMG GT3 team won NLS2 by nearly a minute — a dominant display that had the Nurburgring community excited about his engagement in endurance racing. Hours after the finish, the win was stripped. The team had used seven tyre sets during the race; the regulations permit only six. The breach occurred during the qualifying session, and the stewards — as team boss Hohenadel acknowledged — had no discretion. The victory was awarded to the #99 Rowe Racing BMW of Dan Harper and Jordan Pepper.

It was a painful outcome, and an avoidable one. Verstappen did not cause it — the responsibility rested with the team's logistical management — but he bore the sporting consequence.

NLS3 Skip and What Comes Next

The NLS3 on April 11 was not available to Verstappen. His team was committed to the GT World Challenge Europe round at Paul Ricard that same weekend — a scheduling conflict that made the Nordschleife impossible. He did not race.

The 24H Nurburgring Qualifiers on April 18-19 are different. These are not a warm-up event — they are the primary qualification race for the full 24-hour main event, typically held in May, and they carry full sporting significance in the NLS calendar. Verstappen is confirmed for the event, and it represents the first chance to put the NLS2 disqualification behind him and approach the 24H race with credibility earned at the circuit.

What's Next

The 24H Nurburgring Qualifiers run April 18-19. Following that, the next major event is the Miami Grand Prix on May 1-3, where Red Bull's comprehensive upgrade package will be Verstappen's first real opportunity to fight at the front in Formula 1 this season. For now, the Green Hell offers a version of racing that the RB22 has not — and Verstappen has made clear he intends to take full advantage of it.
