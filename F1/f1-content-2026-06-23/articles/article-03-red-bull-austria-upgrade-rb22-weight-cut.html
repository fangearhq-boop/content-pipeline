Red Bull's Final Diet: The RB22's Last 6kg Weight Cut Arrives at Austria as Verstappen Eyes Home Glory

By Elena Voss | June 23, 2026

Red Bull Racing's second major upgrade of the 2026 Formula 1 season arrives this weekend at the Austrian Grand Prix — and it is the most significant technical story at the Red Bull Ring. After spending the first half of the season carrying excess weight that has hobbled the RB22's competitiveness, the team is set to complete the car's weight-reduction program in front of its home crowd.

From 12kg to Zero

The RB22 began the 2026 season with a structural problem: the car was 12 kilograms over the FIA's minimum weight limit of 768 kilograms. In Formula 1, where aerodynamic forces and tire loads are measured in fractions, carrying 12 kilograms of unnecessary mass is crippling. It costs lap time, but more critically, it increases vertical load on the tires — accelerating degradation and making long stints far more costly than they should be.

Red Bull's first response came at the Miami Grand Prix, where a major upgrade package cut the excess from 12 kilograms down to six. That package delivered measurable improvement, allowing Verstappen and Isack Hadjar to close the gap to the leading Mercedes and Ferrari cars. The Austria package is designed to eliminate the remaining six kilograms entirely, bringing the RB22 to legal minimum weight for the first time in the season.

Tire Benefits at the Red Bull Ring

The Red Bull Ring is an ideal venue to deploy this upgrade. The circuit's high-speed sweeping corners place consistent lateral loads on Pirelli tires throughout each lap. A heavier car generates more friction and heat in those corners, degrading rubber faster and forcing more conservative race strategies. A car at minimum weight rotates more freely through those sweeps and places gentler demands on the tires — exactly the improvement Red Bull needs.

Team principal Laurent Mekies has tempered expectations. "The Austrian package alone will not be enough for victory," he said ahead of the weekend. "But we stay on this continuous, closing-the-gap trajectory that we have been on since post-Japan." It is a careful framing — honest about limitations while signaling that progress is directional, not yet transformational.

Verstappen's Home Race

For Max Verstappen, the Austrian Grand Prix carries an extra layer of significance beyond its technical narrative. He won here four times in the V6 hybrid era — in 2018, 2019, 2021, and 2023 — and the Red Bull Ring crowd treats him as something close to a local hero. A competitive result this weekend, whatever form it takes, would provide a meaningful counterpoint to the exit clause speculation that has surrounded him through the first half of the season.

What's Next

The true test of the Austria package comes in qualifying on Saturday. If the RB22 demonstrates improved one-lap pace and — critically — better tire management in Practice Two and Three data, the upgrade will be confirmed as working. If Verstappen and Hadjar can run longer stints without the tire cliff that has characterized the RB22 in race conditions, the team can claim the upgrade achieved what it promised. The season's second half, with the summer break following the Hungarian Grand Prix, will then determine whether Red Bull's recovery trajectory is enough to solve the championship — or only enough to make the exit clause conversation more complicated.
