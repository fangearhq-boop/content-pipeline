Cadillac's Big Austria Gamble: Can the American Team Finally Score Its First F1 Championship Points?

By Marcus Cole | June 23, 2026

Seven races into the 2026 Formula 1 season, Cadillac remains the only constructor on the grid without a single championship point. That unwanted distinction is something the American manufacturer is determined to change at the Austrian Grand Prix, where Sergio Perez and Valtteri Bottas will race the team's most comprehensive upgrade package yet.

What's Going Wrong — and What the Upgrade Targets

Perez has been specific in his diagnosis of the MAC-26's core weakness: tire degradation. The Cadillac hits what the Spaniard describes as a severe "cliff" in tire performance around lap 15 of any stint. When the tires fall off that edge, pace drops sharply — making it nearly impossible to hold off faster rivals or compete for the points positions where pit-stop strategies compress the field.

The Austria upgrade is designed to address this issue directly. By improving the car's aerodynamic setup and handling balance, the team hopes to reduce the thermal load on the Pirelli tires and smooth out the performance drop that has been costing them on race day. Bottas, who suffered a reliability-related retirement at Barcelona-Catalunya, has flagged that reliability improvements are also part of the package.

Race Pace vs. Results

What makes Cadillac's situation particularly frustrating is that the MAC-26 has not been entirely uncompetitive. The team's data shows the car has been marginally faster than Aston Martin — a fellow midfield manufacturer — on pure pace metrics. But converting lap time into championship points requires more than raw pace: it requires staying in the race, managing tires across longer stints, and making clean pit stops and strategic calls.

Cadillac has, as yet, done none of those things well enough. Austria changes the tire picture through the upgrade. The team's operational experience is accumulating race by race. At some point, those factors combine to produce a result.

Perez Sets the Silverstone Benchmark

Perez has been measured in his timeline expectations. While hopeful for progress in Austria, he has flagged the British Grand Prix at Silverstone — a high-speed, aerodynamically demanding circuit — as the weekend where he expects the team to "really stretch its legs." Silverstone's long, sweeping corners would punish the tire degradation problem if it persists, making the Austria fix a prerequisite for any meaningful Silverstone result.

If Austria delivers even a single points finish for Perez or Bottas, the narrative around Cadillac's debut season shifts significantly. A first point would validate the upgrade trajectory and give the team something to build on through the summer triple-header.

What's Next

The Red Bull Ring's 71-lap race on Sunday June 28 gives Cadillac one of the better overtaking circuits on the calendar to make their first mark. Three long straights mean DRS opportunities and legitimate passing chances — an advantage for a car that has shown burst speed even when tire consistency has been lacking. Qualifying position matters, as it always does, but the MAC-26's most important number at the end of Sunday is not its qualifying time — it is what appears next to the team's name in the points table. Still zero? Or something better? Austria answers that question.
