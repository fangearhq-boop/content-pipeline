### STORY 5: The Two-Stop Rule Is Dead — Monaco Strategy Is Free Again
- **Tier:** 2
- **Category:** Regulation News / Race Preview
- **Why it matters:** The FIA has scrapped the controversial mandatory two-stop rule that debuted at Monaco in 2025. Under the old rule, teams were required to use three tyre sets, forcing a two-stop strategy. Teams exploited it by backing up traffic, drivers lapped up to 4 seconds slower, and fans were unimpressed. For 2026, Monaco returns to standard tyre regulations — teams can choose any strategy from a single stop to multiple stops. Free strategy means genuine racing intelligence, unpredictable pit windows, and real jeopardy.
- **Angle:** The worst rule Monaco has ever seen is gone. The chess match is back.
