Antonelli's Race Start Problem: Why Monaco Could Be His First Real Test of 2026

By Elena Voss | May 28, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Kimi Antonelli has been nearly perfect in 2026. Four consecutive wins. A 43-point championship lead. A driving style that has earned admiration from teammates, rivals, and analysts alike. But former Formula 1 driver Jolyon Palmer has identified one recurring issue that could become a genuine problem at Monaco — and the timing of the warning, with the principality race just over a week away, carries real weight.

The issue is Antonelli's race starts.

The Miami Evidence

The clearest example came at the Hard Rock Stadium Circuit in Miami. Antonelli had qualified on pole — the fastest driver in a single lap, no question. But when the lights went out for the race, Antonelli's getaway was slow. Charles Leclerc, starting from second on the grid, used the slight hesitation to drive up the inside and take the lead before Turn 1.

The race outcome reflected that start. Leclerc converted the Turn 1 lead into a race win. Antonelli, despite his pace advantages over the rest of the field, never reclaimed the position he should have held from pole. A chassis-level advantage can only compensate for so much once the race order is determined in the opening seconds.

In Canada, the start situation was different — a direct battle with Russell — but the pattern of Antonelli not converting his qualifying advantage cleanly at the race start continued. Toto Wolff acknowledged the intensity of the Mercedes duel at the start of that race was "a bit close."

Why Monaco Amplifies the Problem

Palmer's warning is well-founded on a circuit-specific basis. Monaco has one of the shortest distances from the starting grid to the first significant braking point on the entire Formula 1 calendar. The run to Turn 1 — the Sainte Dévote hairpin — is extremely tight, with barely enough room to set up a defensive or attacking line in the brief gap between lights-out and the first corner.

At most circuits, a driver who makes a marginally slow start can recover their position with pace advantage and a later move. Monaco removes that possibility almost entirely. The barriers on both sides of the circuit make overtaking nearly impossible once the field has found its race order. If Antonelli loses his lead at Turn 1, there may be no realistic opportunity to win it back.

Palmer was direct in his assessment: if Antonelli cannot improve his launch by Monaco, starting from pole will not guarantee victory. On any other circuit, a driver with Antonelli's pace can find a way through. On the streets of Monte Carlo, the start is often the finish.

Context and Championship Safety Net

It is worth noting that Antonelli's start issues have not cost him championships or races in the majority of his wins. He has won four times in 2026 from a variety of grid positions. His pace advantage over the field, outside of the intra-Mercedes duel with Russell, has been substantial enough to overcome most positional compromises.

With 43 points in hand over Russell, Antonelli enters Monaco without a "must win" obligation. If Russell beats him in Monaco, the gap closes to roughly 18 points — serious but not decisive. The championship is still Antonelli's to control with sensible racing.

But bad habits compound. The evidence from Miami is the most striking example of how a starting vulnerability can turn a probable win into a defeat. In Monaco, where the race often ends on the first lap in terms of running order, it only has to happen once to define the weekend.

What's Next

The Monaco GP weekend begins June 5. Qualifying is June 6. By Saturday afternoon, we will know whether Antonelli has taken pole and whether the start-line tension is real or theoretical. Palmer's warning is one to watch — not as a prediction that Antonelli will fail, but as a reminder that the most dominant driver in F1 2026 has a weakness the circuit is designed to expose.

Antonelli will be aware of it. His engineers will be working on it. Whether the solution arrives in time for Monte Carlo is one of the best storylines heading into the most glamorous weekend of the Formula 1 calendar.
