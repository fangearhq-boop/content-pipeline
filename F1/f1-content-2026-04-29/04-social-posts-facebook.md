### STORY 1: Lambiase Leaving Red Bull for McLaren

**Post 1A — Long-Form** (8:00 AM ET)
This is a bombshell.

Gianpiero Lambiase — Max Verstappen's race engineer through all four of his Formula 1 World Championships — will leave Red Bull at the end of the 2027 season to join McLaren as Chief Racing Officer.

Lambiase has been on Verstappen's radio since the Dutchman made his debut at Toro Rosso in 2016. That's a decade of partnership through the most dominant run in modern F1 history. He wasn't just an engineer — he was Max's most trusted voice in the car, the person who could push him when needed and calm him when things went sideways.

And now he's going to the team Red Bull is trying to beat.

According to multiple reports, Lambiase sought Verstappen's blessing before making the decision. Max's response was immediate: "You would be stupid not to do that."

It's a classy response — and a telling one. Verstappen isn't angry. He's not publicly questioning the decision. He understands that his most trusted ally has found an opportunity that's right for him. But the subtext is impossible to ignore: if Lambiase is already planning his post-Red Bull chapter, what is Max thinking about his own?

Red Bull team boss Laurent Mekies has broken his silence, insisting the factory is "on fire" trying to get back to the front. But Red Bull sits fourth in the Constructors' Championship with just 16 points after three races.

The question is no longer whether Red Bull can win. The question is how long Verstappen will give them to figure it out. 🏎️

### STORY 2: Miami Race Week Preview

**Post 2A — Long-Form** (10:00 AM ET)
Miami Race Week is here — and it feels like the real start of the 2026 Formula 1 season.

After a five-week gap caused by the cancellation of the Bahrain and Saudi Arabian Grands Prix, F1 returns to Florida with a Sprint format weekend and every team armed with significant upgrades. The gap gave engineers, designers, and mechanics more time than anyone expected to develop their cars — and the result is that the Miami International Autodrome could look completely different to what we've seen through the first three rounds.

Kimi Antonelli heads into this weekend leading the Drivers' Championship with 72 points. His Mercedes team-mate George Russell trails by 9 points. Mercedes leads the Constructors' with 135 points. Ferrari sit second at 90, McLaren third at 56.

The Sprint weekend format means the action starts quickly:
📅 FP1 + Sprint Qualifying: Friday, May 1
🏎️ Sprint Race: Saturday, May 2
🏁 Race: Sunday, May 3 at 4:00 PM ET

For Antonelli, this is a test unlike anything he has faced in his young career. Leading a championship feels different from chasing one. The pressure is real, the upgrades from rivals are real, and the Miami heat adds a physical challenge that the early European rounds don't provide.

This is where the 2026 title race truly begins. 🇺🇸

### STORY 3: Ferrari vs McLaren Upgrade War

**Post 3A — Long-Form** (12:00 PM ET)
McLaren team principal Andrea Stella used the phrase "completely new car from aerodynamic standpoint." Those are not words you choose lightly.

Ferrari, for their part, are bringing the full SF-26 specification to Miami — approximately 50 percent of the car changed since the Japanese Grand Prix. The Macarena wing is expected to debut in competition. The software managing their electrical system has been significantly revised. Ferrari tested at Monza specifically to prepare for this weekend.

Now both teams arrive in Miami at the same moment. Former F1 driver and pundit Jolyon Palmer has called it an "upgrade-off" — and that framing is exactly right.

Mercedes heads into this weekend with a 135-point constructors' lead and the intention to resist. Their car has been the benchmark through three rounds. But teams have had the longest development window of the season, and both Ferrari and McLaren have been very deliberate about targeting Miami as their relaunch point.

The question is whether these upgrades close the gap, eliminate it, or — in the most dramatic scenario — actually flip the order. If either Ferrari or McLaren emerges from Miami as the fastest package, the 2026 Constructors' Championship becomes a very different competition.

Qualifying on Saturday will be the moment of truth. 🔧

### STORY 4: Cadillac Stars and Stripes Livery

**Post 4A — Long-Form** (2:00 PM ET)
Of all the things Formula 1 has brought to the United States, perhaps nothing captures the moment as perfectly as this: an American F1 team, racing an American car, in their first home race, wearing red, white, and blue.

Cadillac have revealed a special one-off livery for the Miami Grand Prix — Stars and Stripes from front to back. The front wing features 50 stars, one for each US state. The rear wing carries the word "USA" alongside shades of red, white, and blue. The design was presented in partnership with primary partner TWG AI, and it integrates the patriotic motif seamlessly into Cadillac's signature black and white colour scheme.

Valtteri Bottas and Sergio Perez will carry the livery into battle starting Friday.

For Cadillac, this is a genuine milestone. F1's newest entry was built around the idea of American motorsport heritage competing at the highest level of global racing. They've been building toward this moment. And now, at the Miami International Autodrome, in front of what will likely be the loudest and most enthusiastic crowd either driver will have experienced in a while, the car will look the part.

It's the kind of moment F1's American expansion was always meant to produce. 🇺🇸

He knows exactly how Verstappen thinks, how he processes frustration, when he needs to be pushed and when he needs to be settled. That relationship isn't replaceable. It was built over a decade of shared high-pressure moments.

Now Lambiase is gone — and he's going to McLaren, one of the teams Verstappen would theoretically join if he ever left Red Bull.

Red Bull boss Laurent Mekies insists the team is working flat out: "The campus is on fire," he told reporters. He's been diplomatic about discussing Verstappen's exit clause. The team is clearly doing everything it can to keep Max engaged and committed.

But when the person Verstappen trusted most in the car is already planning their post-Red Bull chapter, it's hard not to wonder whether Max is quietly doing the same.

Nothing is confirmed. Nothing is imminent. But the signals are accumulating. 👀
