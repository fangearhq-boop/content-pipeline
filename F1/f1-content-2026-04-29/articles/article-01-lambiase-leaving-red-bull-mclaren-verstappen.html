Gianpiero Lambiase Is Leaving Red Bull for McLaren — and the Verstappen Question Grows Louder

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap

April 29, 2026

In ten years alongside Max Verstappen, Gianpiero Lambiase was there for everything. The early days at Toro Rosso in 2016. The first championship in 2021. The second, third, and fourth titles that followed. Every heated radio exchange, every strategic call under pressure, every moment of doubt and every moment of triumph. Lambiase was the most trusted voice in Max Verstappen's ear — and now he is leaving Red Bull for McLaren.

Red Bull confirmed Tuesday that Lambiase will depart at the end of his current contract following the 2027 season, joining McLaren in the role of Chief Racing Officer. Formula1.com first reported the move. It is arguably the most significant non-driver personnel news of the 2026 Formula 1 season.

Verstappen's response, when asked for comment, was characteristically direct. "You would be stupid not to do that," he said — and explained that Lambiase had sought his blessing before making the decision. He gave it without hesitation. There is a graciousness in that which speaks to the maturity of their relationship. But there is also a subtext that Formula 1 observers will not miss: if Lambiase is already planning his post-Red Bull chapter while Verstappen still has a year to run on his contract, what does the four-time champion's own future look like?

What Lambiase Meant to Verstappen

Race engineers come in many forms. Some are purely technical, translating telemetry into strategy calls. Others are motivators, pushing drivers through difficult stints. Lambiase was something rarer — a genuine partner. He understood Verstappen's psychology with the precision of a decade's worth of high-stakes collaboration. He knew when to push Max harder and when to let him find his own rhythm. Their radio communications, sometimes tense and sometimes warm, became one of the defining soundtracks of F1 in the early 2020s.

Losing that relationship is not something Red Bull can simply replace with a capable substitute. The dynamic between a world champion and their race engineer is built over thousands of laps and countless hours of debrief sessions. Starting that process over — with a driver who is already publicly frustrated by his car's performance — is a significant challenge.

Red Bull's Position

Team boss Laurent Mekies was diplomatic but pointed in his response to Lambiase's announced departure. "The campus is on fire," he told reporters, insisting that Red Bull is working with extraordinary intensity to resolve the fundamental performance issues that have left Verstappen ninth in the championship with just 16 points — fourth in the Constructors' standings — after three races without a podium.

Mekies did not address Verstappen's reported exit clause, which is understood to allow the Dutchman to leave the team if certain performance benchmarks are not met. The clause has not been triggered, and Verstappen has not made any public statement about activating it. But with Lambiase — the person who more than anyone understood how to get the best from Max inside the car — now committed to a future elsewhere, the environment at Red Bull looks less stable with every passing week.

What's Next

McLaren's acquisition of Lambiase is a long-term strategic move. He will join the Woking-based team as Chief Racing Officer after the 2027 season, taking on responsibilities that free up team principal Andrea Stella to focus elsewhere. McLaren are building infrastructure around their own championship ambitions, and bringing in the man who engineered four world titles is exactly the kind of move that signals serious intent.

For Verstappen, Miami is the immediate focus — getting his Red Bull into competitive shape for a Sprint weekend where the team needs any kind of positive result. But the larger question — how long can he stay committed to a project that is struggling and now losing its most important non-driving voice — will follow him all the way to the end of the season. For now, at least, Max gave Lambiase his blessing. Whether he eventually follows his engineer out the door remains one of the biggest stories in Formula 1.
