Stars, Stripes, and 50 Stars: Cadillac's Miami Livery Is Everything Their First Home Race Deserves

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap

April 29, 2026

There are moments in motorsport history that feel like they were always destined to happen. An American Formula 1 team, racing for the first time in their home country, in red, white, and blue — with 50 stars on the front wing, one for every US state — is exactly that kind of moment.

Cadillac revealed their special one-off Miami Grand Prix livery on Tuesday, and it is everything the occasion demands. The Stars and Stripes motif is integrated seamlessly into Cadillac's signature black and white colour scheme, presented in partnership with primary team partner TWG AI. The front wing carries 50 stars in honour of the United States. The rear wing displays "USA" alongside blocks of red, white, and blue. The design is bold, unmistakably patriotic, and entirely appropriate for a team that was built on the promise of American motorsport competing at the highest level of the world's most prestigious racing series.

Valtteri Bottas and Sergio Perez will carry the livery into competition across the Miami Grand Prix weekend, beginning with Free Practice 1 on Friday, May 1.

Why This Matters

Cadillac's journey to Formula 1 was not without obstacles. The team's entry was contested, debated, and delayed before it was ultimately confirmed. General Motors — through the Cadillac brand — had to navigate a regulatory process that tested the patience of everyone involved. But the end result is here: a fully American team, with American backing, competing against the sport's most established giants.

Miami is not just any race in that context. It is the first American Grand Prix that Cadillac will race as a home entry. The Miami International Autodrome sits in the shadow of the Hard Rock Stadium in Florida, drawing a crowd that represents the new generation of American F1 fans — the ones who came to the sport through Netflix, through the sport's US TV deal, through the cultural moment that Formula 1 has become in the United States over the past several years. Cadillac racing in their home colours, on American soil, in front of that crowd, is exactly what the sport's expansion to America was always supposed to deliver.

The Detail That Stands Out

The 50 stars on the front wing is the kind of design detail that elevates a livery from clever to meaningful. It's not decorative — it's intentional. Every American state gets represented on an F1 car racing in Miami. From Alaska to Florida, from Hawaii to Maine, all 50 are there. It is the kind of storytelling through design that the best special liveries always manage, and it gives casual fans and dedicated followers alike something specific to connect with.

What's Next

Cadillac's performance on track has been mixed in their debut season, as expected for a new team navigating the 2026 regulations. Bottas brings veteran experience from his Mercedes years, while Perez — who knows what it takes to compete at the front of the field — provides leadership. The Miami crowd will be enthusiastically behind them regardless of where they finish. For this weekend at least, the occasion is as important as the result. Cadillac has arrived at home. The Stars and Stripes are on the grid. That, in itself, is a landmark worth celebrating.
