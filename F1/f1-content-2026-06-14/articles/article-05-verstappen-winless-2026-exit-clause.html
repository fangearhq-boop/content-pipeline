Verstappen's Winless 2026: Zero Victories Through Seven Rounds as Exit Clause Looms

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

June 14, 2026

Max Verstappen arrived in Barcelona this weekend with a new power unit, cautious optimism, and zero race victories in the 2026 Formula 1 season.

The triple world champion qualified fifth for Sunday's Spanish Grand Prix after Red Bull installed an upgraded power unit ahead of the Barcelona weekend. His assessment was measured: "a step in the right direction." It is the language of a driver who has learned not to promise what his car cannot yet deliver.

The Scale of the Problem

Through seven rounds of the 2026 season, Verstappen has not won a race. He sits seventh in the drivers' championship with 43 points. Championship leader Kimi Antonelli has accumulated 156. That is not a gap — it is an ocean.

Red Bull's difficulties in 2026 stem from their car's adaptation to the new technical regulations. The aerodynamic and power unit framework changes that arrived this season have, to date, suited Mercedes more than any other constructor. Verstappen is driving at the absolute limit of his package's capability, but that limit is currently somewhere around fifth on the grid.

His Monaco retirement, the cause of which has since been confirmed by Red Bull's engineers, cost him points he could not afford to lose. The Barcelona new power unit is the team's latest attempt to close the gap to the front.

The Contract Clock

Verstappen's Red Bull contract contains an exit clause that activates on July 27. The specific triggering conditions are not public, but the date is known — and it is approaching. The Austrian Grand Prix on June 26-28 at Red Bull Ring, the team's home circuit, represents one more critical test before that date arrives.

If Red Bull's new power unit brings genuine performance gains this weekend, and if those gains carry to Austria, the narrative of Verstappen's departure may shift. If the results remain what they have been, the conversation about his future will only intensify.

What's Next

Verstappen races from fifth today in Barcelona. The Austrian Grand Prix at Red Bull Ring follows on June 26-28 — the team's most symbolically important race of the year. Between now and July 27, Verstappen and Red Bull have a limited number of opportunities to demonstrate that 2026 is a recoverable situation.

Verstappen has been here before — not precisely here, but in seasons that required patience and engineering recovery. He has three world championship titles that prove he is capable of coming back. The 2026 season is asking a question he has not yet been asked at this level. How he answers it will define the next chapter of his career.
