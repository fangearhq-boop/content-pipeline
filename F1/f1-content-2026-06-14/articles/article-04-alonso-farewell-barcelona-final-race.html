Alonso's Barcelona Farewell: "Probably My Last Race in F1 in This City"

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap

June 14, 2026

Fernando Alonso did not announce his retirement this weekend. But the words he spoke to Formula1.com before the 2026 Spanish Grand Prix carry the weight of a goodbye.

"This is probably my last race in F1 in this city," Alonso said. He is 44 years old. He has 32 race victories. He is a two-time Formula 1 world champion, having claimed the title back-to-back in 2005 and 2006. And he is racing at Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya knowing that the track will rotate off the Formula 1 calendar from 2027, when a new Madrid street circuit, the Madring, takes its place.

What This Circuit Means

For Alonso, Barcelona is not just a circuit on the calendar. It is the place where he grew up watching Formula 1 — where he sat in the grandstands as a child and watched Ayrton Senna race before he was old enough to fully understand what he was witnessing. The memories here are personal before they are professional.

He has raced at Barcelona as a Formula 1 driver many times over. His career has spanned generations, rivals, rule changes, and technological revolutions. The Alonso who races this weekend in 2026 is a different driver than the one who broke Michael Schumacher's championship stranglehold two decades ago — but he is still here, still competitive, still racing at the highest level past an age when most drivers have long since retired.

No Official Announcement

It should be noted that Alonso has made no formal announcement about retirement. Formula 1 calendars change, and it is possible that he races in Spain again — just not at this specific circuit. But the tone of his comment suggests he is at a reflective moment in his career, aware that certain chapters are closing.

Two world championships. Thirty-two wins. Hundreds of podiums. A career that has made him one of the most complete and celebrated racing drivers in the sport's history. If Barcelona 2026 is indeed his last race here, the circuit will not be able to forget him.

What's Next

Alonso races today in the Spanish Grand Prix. His 2026 campaign continues beyond Barcelona regardless of today's result. But for fans who have followed his career since the beginning, this weekend carries a significance that goes beyond the championship standings.

Some moments in sport are recognized as milestones only in retrospect. Alonso, to his credit, seems to understand what this one might be — and is saying so, in his own way.
