Red Bull's Mekies 'Confident' Verstappen Will Have a Faster Car From Miami — But So He Was Before Japan

By F1 Fanrecap Staff March 31, 2026

The Five-Week Promise

Red Bull Team Principal Laurent Mekies has said he is "confident" that Max Verstappen will have a faster car when the Formula 1 season resumes at the Miami Grand Prix on May 1-3. The declaration, reported by Sky Sports F1, comes after a turbulent three-race opening to the 2026 season in which Red Bull's new RB22 has failed to match the pace or reliability of the front-running Mercedes, Ferrari, and McLaren outfits.

Mekies' confidence follows an uncomfortable pattern. A significant bodywork upgrade introduced at the Japanese Grand Prix — the first major development package of the season — failed to deliver the expected performance improvement. Verstappen finished P8 at Suzuka, left ninth in the drivers' championship with 12 points, and described the car as going "from one extreme to another." The gap between Red Bull's stated optimism and on-track results has become one of the defining sub-plots of the season's opening act.

What the Five-Week Break Offers Red Bull

The break between Japan and Miami is the longest gap in the 2026 calendar — an unusual stretch created by the cancellation of the Bahrain and Saudi Arabian Grand Prix races. For most teams, an extended break is a welcome opportunity to recover and reset. For Red Bull, it is something more urgent: potentially their last genuine window to understand and fix what is fundamentally wrong with the RB22 before the season slips irretrievably away from them.

Red Bull's engineering operation in Brackley has significant technical resources. The question is whether the RB22's performance issues are related to aerodynamic configuration — adjustable via updates — or something more foundational in the car's concept. Mekies has acknowledged that the team has not yet identified the root cause of the car's inconsistency. That admission, combined with the Japan upgrade failure, suggests the problems may run deeper than a simple aerodynamic fix.

The Pressure of Verstappen's Warning

Mekies' confidence statement landed on the same day that reports emerged of Verstappen issuing his clearest warning yet about potentially leaving Formula 1 at the end of 2026. The combination of public pressure from their star driver and a genuine performance deficit creates an unusual dynamic at one of the sport's most successful constructors. Red Bull won four consecutive constructors' championships (2022-2025) and have arguably the deepest motorsport engineering DNA outside of Mercedes.

Whether that pedigree can be translated into rapid recovery under the 2026 regulations — which represent a fundamentally different car architecture — is the question every Red Bull engineer is trying to answer between now and Miami. The team's credibility, and potentially Verstappen's future with the sport, depends on the answer.

What's Next

The Miami Grand Prix on May 1-3, 2026 will be the definitive test of Red Bull's break-time work. A Sprint format weekend means Verstappen will have multiple competitive sessions across the weekend, giving the team rapid feedback on any upgrades. If the RB22 shows genuine improvement in Miami, the crisis narrative can begin to unravel. If Verstappen ends the weekend inside the top five for the first time in four races, the story changes. If not, the warnings — from the driver and from the paddock — will grow louder still.
