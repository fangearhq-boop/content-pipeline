Verstappen's Clock Is Ticking: 26 Days to Hungary, 98 Points Behind, No Easy Way Out

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | June 30, 2026

Twenty-six days. That's all that stands between Max Verstappen and the most consequential contract decision in Formula 1 in years.

The Hungarian Grand Prix on July 26 is the deadline for Verstappen's exit clause to activate. If he is not within striking distance of the top two in the drivers' championship — and he is not, sitting 98 points behind Kimi Antonelli — the clause allows him to walk away from Red Bull Racing at the end of the season. The mathematics make the top-two requirement impossible to satisfy. The only question is what happens next.

Red Bull's Offer

Red Bull Racing is not giving up without a fight. The team has offered Verstappen an eight-figure buyout — a substantial financial package designed to keep the four-time world champion at the team he's called home since 2016.

But money alone may not be the deciding factor for a driver who has won everything Red Bull has to offer. Verstappen's manager, Raymond Vermeulen, has been clear about the priority: Max needs to be in a car that can win the championship. The 2026 Red Bull has not been that car. Through eight races, Verstappen has managed only a P2 in Austria — charged from near the back after a Q3 crash — and the RB22's underlying performance deficit remains significant.

The shareholders are divided. Red Bull's Thai ownership group reportedly supports a buyout deal to retain Verstappen. The Mateschitz estate and co-owner Mintzlaff are less enthusiastic about the financial commitment required. That internal division makes a clean resolution harder to reach before July 26.

The McLaren Dimension

The most intriguing development in the Verstappen saga is the emergence of McLaren as a potential destination. Reports indicate ongoing talks between Verstappen's camp and McLaren — with the complicating factor that McLaren's lineup currently features Oscar Piastri and Lando Norris, both under multi-year contracts.

A potential arrangement involving Piastri in some kind of role change or swap appears to be part of the conversation. The details are murky and likely to remain so until closer to July 26. What is clear is that McLaren represents a genuine option — they are championship-caliber, their car is fast, and Verstappen's presence would make them the clear favorite for the constructors' title in 2027.

Mercedes has been ruled out as a destination, which is a surprise given the Silver Arrows' 2026 dominance. The reason appears to be that Mercedes views their current driver lineup — with Antonelli developing into a genuine title contender — as settled.

What the Deadline Means for the Rest of the Grid

Verstappen's decision is the domino that unlocks the entire driver market. Fifteen of 22 seats for the 2027 season remain without confirmed drivers. Teams across the grid are holding their breath, waiting to understand where Max lands before making their own moves.

Audi is pursuing Carlos Sainz. Several midfield teams are waiting for clarity on what talent becomes available once Verstappen's choice creates a cascade of decisions. The July 26 deadline is, in practical terms, the starting gun for the entire 2027 silly season.

What's Next

The British GP is three days away. Then comes the summer break, and then the Hungarian GP on July 26. Between now and then, Verstappen's future will dominate the paddock conversation at Silverstone. Watch for any signals from his camp — or from Red Bull — in the days ahead. The clock is ticking, and 26 days passes faster than it seems.
