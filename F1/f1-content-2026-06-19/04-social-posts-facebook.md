The Austrian GP — at Red Bull Ring, his home race — is the highest-visibility moment to demonstrate progress.

If the upgrade delivers visible performance gains, the conversation shifts to whether Verstappen can get back into the title picture. If it doesn't, the conversation will be something very different by Silverstone.

### STORY 4: Mercedes Battery Fix

#### Long-Form Post — 2:00 PM ET

Three races. Three battery failures. Three different teams. Same symptom.

Russell DNF Canada. Norris DNF Monaco. Antonelli DNF Barcelona — from the lead, with three laps remaining.

Toto Wolff called it plainly: "We can't compete for the championship every second race losing points." He made a direct pledge that the battery issue would be resolved before the Austrian Grand Prix.

Here's what makes this complicated: George Russell's battery from the Canadian GP is on sea freight, because the failure mode means it literally cannot be flown. It may not arrive for months. That means the solution for Austria has to be a new battery specification — not a repaired unit from Montreal, but something different entirely.

The pattern across all three failures is the same: the car switches off mid-race. Different teams (Mercedes-works, McLaren, Mercedes-customer). Different circuits. Different timing in the race. But the same result. That points toward a shared component in the Mercedes power unit supply chain.

Mercedes leads the Constructors' Championship with 262 points, 72 ahead of Ferrari. Every DNF in a Mercedes-powered car is a gift to Maranello. Fixing this is not optional.

Seven days until Austria. The clock is running. ⏱️
