Ferrari's Austrian GP Engine Upgrade: 30 Horsepower and the Shot at Halving the Mercedes Gap

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | June 19, 2026

Lewis Hamilton arrives at the Austrian Grand Prix in the form of his career. His 106th victory in Barcelona last Sunday — the first for Ferrari in the 2026 season — cut his deficit to championship leader Kimi Antonelli from 66 points to 41. Seven days from now, he'll have something else: a new engine.

Ferrari will introduce their first ADUO-specification power unit upgrade at the Austrian GP (June 26-28, Red Bull Ring). Reports from multiple technical outlets indicate the upgrade is expected to yield approximately 30 horsepower. More significantly, the first upgrade step is described as capable of halving the current power gap between Ferrari and Mercedes — a gap that has been one of the key performance differentials across the first seven rounds of 2026.

What ADUO Changes

The ADUO (Approved Development of Unique Origin) framework, introduced for the 2026 regulations, allows teams to submit controlled mid-season power unit upgrades for FIA approval. Ferrari received clearance for their first development step under these rules earlier this year and have been working toward this deployment ever since.

The modification targets the thermal efficiency of the internal combustion engine, with slightly larger batteries complementing the ICE changes. It is not a complete power unit replacement — it is an evolution of the existing SF-26 specification — but the performance implications are significant.

For Hamilton and Charles Leclerc, who is fourth in the championship with 75 points, the upgrade arrives at the optimal moment: coming off a race win, with confidence high, and entering the first of a three-race stretch (Austria / Britain / Belgium) where momentum can compound quickly.

What It Means for the Championship

Before Barcelona, the consensus among technical analysts was that Ferrari's aerodynamic package had closed the chassis gap to Mercedes significantly, but the power unit remained a meaningful deficit. A 30-horsepower gain — if the upgrade performs as described — changes that calculus. A Ferrari that is both aero-competitive and engine-competitive represents a genuine threat to Mercedes' constructors lead and Antonelli's drivers' advantage.

Antonelli leads with 156 points to Hamilton's 115. With 15 races remaining and 25 points available per race, the gap is recoverable. Hamilton needs roughly 2.7 additional points per race compared to Antonelli over the rest of the season. That is a slim but achievable target, particularly if the ADUO upgrade delivers sustained performance across the triple-header.

What's Next

Austrian GP free practice begins Friday, June 26. Ferrari's plan is to run the upgraded power unit across the full race weekend at the Red Bull Ring. The first data on real-world performance — speed trap numbers, long-run pace, engine deployment — will be visible by qualifying on Saturday June 27.

If the upgrade works as advertised, the 2026 F1 championship will have a very different complexion by the time the grid heads to Silverstone on July 5.
