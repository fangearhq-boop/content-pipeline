F1 Silly Season 2026: 15 Drivers Without 2027 Seats

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

June 28, 2026

The Austrian Grand Prix is taking place today in Spielberg. The 2027 Formula 1 driver market is already the most consequential subplot in the paddock. Fifteen of the 22 drivers on the current grid have no confirmed seat for next season. More than two-thirds of the field is in a holding pattern, and virtually all of it is waiting on one man and one deadline.

The deadline is July 26 — the Hungarian Grand Prix. The man is Max Verstappen. Until his situation is resolved, the market will not move in any meaningful way.

The Verstappen Domino

Verstappen's contract at Red Bull contains an exit clause that his manager Raymond Vermeulen has confirmed publicly. The clause activates at the Hungarian Grand Prix if Verstappen is not in the top two of the Drivers' Championship at that point. He is currently seventh, 113 points behind championship leader Kimi Antonelli. He mathematically cannot close that gap to reach the top two by the summer break.

The exit clause is not a hypothetical any more. It is a countdown.

Mercedes has emerged as the consistent frontrunner in reporting around Verstappen's likely next destination. The logic is coherent: Mercedes has the fastest car in 2026, a championship-leading driver in Antonelli, and the resources and prestige to attract a four-time world champion even in a below-par year. Should that move be confirmed, it reshapes two or three additional seats immediately — every team adjacent to a Mercedes move has to adjust their own planning.

That is why nothing moves until July 26. The Verstappen domino, once it falls, is the mechanism that unlocks the rest of the market.

What Is Already Confirmed

The list of confirmed 2027 seats is short. Lando Norris is under contract at McLaren through the 2027 season. Oscar Piastri has a McLaren deal running through 2028. Lewis Hamilton's contract with Ferrari extends through 2027 and beyond — a deal that was already set before Hamilton even turned a wheel for the Scuderia this season.

Three drivers. Three confirmed seats. Nineteen drivers, nineteen seats, and a market that has not yet begun to clear.

The Key Uncertainties

Fernando Alonso's contract at Aston Martin expires at the end of 2026. Lance Stroll, his teammate and the son of the team's owner, is in the same position. Neither has announced plans for 2027. Whether Alonso continues into what would be his 26th season in Formula 1, and under what terms, is an open question that the Aston Martin team has not publicly addressed.

At Williams, Carlos Sainz is described as looking beyond his current team situation. Performance frustrations at Williams are reported to have Sainz eyeing a potential move to Audi — a team with factory backing, a development roadmap, and the resources to make a credible multi-year offer. Sainz is experienced, fast, and available. That combination makes him exactly the kind of driver Audi is pursuing.

Audi's Aggressive Pursuit

The most active force in the unsettled 2027 market is Audi, which is in the process of taking over the Sauber operation and building toward a full factory Formula 1 programme. The German manufacturer has been pursuing top-tier drivers with three-year contracts and significant financial backing — a package designed to attract names who might otherwise wait for a seat at an already-established frontrunner.

Three-year deals are unusual in Formula 1. They signal commitment and financial certainty in a sport where annual contracts and rolling options have become the norm. For a driver weighing a move to a team in transition, the security of a long contract from a factory operation with Audi's resources is a genuine competitive offer. The team needs to land the right drivers to give their 2027 project credibility. They are making it worth their while to say yes.

The Bigger Picture

The 2026 season is seven races old. Fifteen drivers are racing without knowing where they will be next year. That degree of uncertainty at this stage of the calendar is unusual, and it is almost entirely a product of one unresolved situation at the top of the market. In previous seasons, a dominance like Verstappen's would have meant his contract renewal was already signed and announced before the year began. In 2026, the championship has not delivered what was expected, the exit clause has become the story, and the entire market below him has stalled accordingly.

When the Hungarian GP arrives on July 26 and the clause activates, the pace of announcements across the paddock will accelerate sharply. Confirmed signings, reported negotiations, and formal denials will fill the weeks that follow. The silly season is not slow — it is paused. One announcement will unpause it.

What's Next

July 26 is the key date — the Hungarian Grand Prix and the activation point for Verstappen's exit clause. Between now and then, the Austrian GP today and the British GP at Silverstone will provide additional championship context that shapes how urgently various teams feel the need to move. F1 Fanrecap will track every confirmed signing, reported negotiation, and market development as the summer break approaches and the 2027 grid begins to take shape.
