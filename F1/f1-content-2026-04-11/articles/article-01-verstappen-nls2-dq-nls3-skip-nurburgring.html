Verstappen's Nurburgring Win Stripped, Skips NLS3 — Next Stop April 18-19 Qualifiers

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap

April 11, 2026

Max Verstappen's relationship with the Nurburgring has taken another complicated turn. First his NLS2 victory was stripped due to a technical infraction. Now, contrary to earlier reports, he will not be competing in NLS3 at the Nordschleife on Saturday, April 11 either.

Verstappen confirmed ahead of the weekend that the #3 Winward Racing Mercedes-AMG GT3 — the car he shares with Jules Gounon and Daniel Juncadella — is competing at Paul Ricard this weekend in the GT World Challenge Europe, not at the Nurburgring. His next appearance at the famous German circuit will be the ADAC 24H Nurburgring Qualifiers on April 18-19, serving as preparation for the full 24 Hours of the Nurburgring on May 16.

The NLS2 Disqualification

The NLS2 story is what generated most of the Nurburgring headlines in recent days. Verstappen, Gounon, and Juncadella appeared to have won NLS2 by a commanding margin — dominating the race from pole position. Hours after crossing the line, the technical commission delivered a damaging verdict: the car had used seven sets of tyres during race day when the regulations permit only six.

The infraction did not occur during the race itself but in qualifying, where the team cycled through multiple driver and tyre changes during the morning session. The error was administrative rather than strategic, but the penalty was clear-cut: disqualification.

The win was awarded to the #99 Rowe Racing BMW of Dan Harper and Jordan Pepper. Winward team boss Christian Hohenadel did not challenge the decision. "An internal error that left the stewards with no choice," he said. It is a difficult outcome for a program built partly to give Verstappen somewhere to feel competitive while his F1 team rebuilds.

The Nurburgring 24H Build-Up

The 24H Nurburgring Qualifiers on April 18-19 represent the next opportunity for the Verstappen-Gounon-Juncadella crew to recalibrate. The full 24 Hours of the Nurburgring takes place May 16 — the same weekend as a free window before the Monaco Grand Prix — and has been flagged by Verstappen as a major personal goal for the year. The Qualifiers are a crucial shakedown ahead of that event, setting grid positions and serving as the final serious preparation run.

For fans tracking the Verstappen story across both his F1 and GT commitments, the message from this week is clear: the NLS3 story was premature, the NLS2 win is gone, and the real target on the Nordschleife remains May 16.

What's Next

Max Verstappen returns to Nurburgring action on April 18-19 for the ADAC 24H Nurburgring Qualifiers. The full 24 Hours of the Nurburgring follows on May 16. In Formula 1, his next race is the Miami Grand Prix on May 1-3 — a sprint weekend that Verstappen enters in ninth place in the championship with 12 points. The five-week F1 break ends at Miami, where Red Bull will need to show meaningful improvement to keep Verstappen's exit clause from becoming a reality.
