### STORY 4: FIA April 20 Vote — Nine Days to Change F1 2026

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Formula 1's biggest off-track story is approaching its climax.

Nine days from now — April 20 — the FIA will convene a full F1 Commission meeting with all stakeholders to vote on proposed changes to the 2026 technical regulations. The meeting follows two rounds of preparatory sessions: a Sporting Regulations meeting on April 15 and a technical session on April 16. April 20 is the binding decision day.

The proposed changes center on energy management — specifically the rules governing how much electrical power cars can deploy and how quickly that energy is replenished. The current system has drawn complaints from drivers who find qualifying sessions compromised by energy management tactics, while safety concerns were raised after Oliver Bearman's approximately 50G crash at the Spoon Curve in Japan, caused in part by a large speed differential as his Haas ran out of battery power.

The key proposals on the table include increasing the super clip limit from 250kW to 350kW — allowing more consistent deployment — and reducing the energy recovery quota from 9MJ to 6MJ, simplifying the in-race energy calculus.

The goal is to have changes in place for the Miami Grand Prix on May 1-3. If the vote passes on April 20, ratification by the FIA World Motor Sport Council would follow within days.

Whether Formula 1 can move that quickly is the question. But with driver safety on the line and a new era of regulations at stake, there is urgency in the room.
