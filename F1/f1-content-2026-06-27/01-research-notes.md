- Worth approximately 0.1s per lap at Red Bull Ring

## Story 3: Verstappen Exit Clause

### Contract Details (Confirmed)
- Exit clause inserted due to Verstappen's "apprehension" about 2026 technical regulations
- Trigger condition: NOT in top-2 at summer break
- Reference race: Hungarian GP July 26
- Activation window: August to October 2026
- Financial terms: Verstappen can leave unilaterally — Red Bull receive NO compensation
- Manager Raymond Vermeulen confirmed publicly: "He should have the chance to win"

### Current Situation
- Verstappen P7 in championship, ~113 points behind Antonelli
- Mathematically near-impossible to reach top-2 by Hungary
- Red Bull shareholders reportedly split on buyout attempt
- F1 insiders: clause "impossible for Red Bull to block"
- Mercedes described as leading destination; Aston Martin (Newey connection) also mentioned

### Red Bull Upgrade Context
- 7-part upgrade package: sidepod inlet, forward floor, outer floor boards, engine cover, rear suspension fairings, rear wing pylons, tailpipe
- Goal: "halve the gap to Mercedes" at their home race (Mekies quote)
- Verstappen stopped in FP1 with power unit issue despite the upgrade
