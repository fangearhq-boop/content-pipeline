### STORY 5: Ferrari ADUO — 25 Horsepower Closing the Gap

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM ET

Ferrari is coming for Mercedes, and the weapon is called ADUO.

The Additional Development and Upgrade Opportunity power unit has received FIA approval for use from the Canadian Grand Prix onwards. The upgrade targets the thermal side of the internal combustion engine and closes an estimated gap of around 25 horsepower — roughly two percent of total output — to the dominant Mercedes unit.

On April 22, Ferrari will conduct a filming day at Monza with Lewis Hamilton and Charles Leclerc sharing driving duties. A revised floor will also be evaluated. These aren't cosmetic changes — they're the foundation of Ferrari's mid-season comeback bid.

The ADUO timeline is important: Canadian GP is where it debuts. That gives Ferrari and their engine partners approximately six weeks to prepare. By the time the summer arrives, Ferrari could be in a fundamentally different performance position than they're in right now.

Hamilton will be central to this. His experience extracting performance from a new power unit is unmatched, and his role in Monza's April 22 development session underscores how seriously Ferrari is treating this upgrade.
