Verstappen's Red Bull Future: Exit Clause Gone, $100M Buyout, and the Pressure of Zero 2026 Wins

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | June 17, 2026

Through the first half of the 2026 Formula 1 season, the question of whether Max Verstappen would remain at Red Bull Racing has consumed nearly as much paddock conversation as the championship fight itself. The answer, as of now, is yes — but the resolution came at a cost to Red Bull, and the Austrian Grand Prix will say more about where this partnership goes from here than any contract document.

What the Exit Clause Was

Verstappen's Red Bull contract included a performance-based exit clause that would allow him to terminate his agreement if he was outside the top two in the drivers' championship at a specific juncture in the 2026 season. Given the new technical regulations and Red Bull's difficult start to the year, the clause was widely regarded as a genuine risk of activation.

Through seven rounds, Verstappen sits seventh in the championship with 55 points — 101 behind leader Kimi Antonelli. He has not won a race. The exit clause as originally structured would have become a serious conversation point in the summer months.

The Resolution

Reports indicate that Red Bull effectively removed or bought out the exit clause at significant cost. According to published reports, any rival team seeking to acquire Verstappen's services would now need to pay approximately $100 million in a contract buyout fee — a figure designed to deter even the wealthiest competitors.

Helmut Marko subsequently confirmed that Verstappen is committed to Red Bull through at least the end of the 2027 season. The four-time world champion publicly endorsed the team's direction and declined to escalate tensions publicly — even after a difficult run of results that included a penalty point for a racing incident with George Russell at the Spanish Grand Prix.

The Performance Picture

Verstappen's 2026 season in numbers: zero race wins. Seventh in the championship. 101 points from the leader. Fourth in Barcelona, which — given where Red Bull started the season — was actually encouraging. His first podium came in Canada (third place), and the team appears to be finding small amounts of pace with each passing race.

Red Bull is bringing what is described as a major upgrade package to the Austrian Grand Prix — their home race, at a circuit where Verstappen has delivered some of his most memorable performances. The expectation in the paddock is that Austria is the circuit where Red Bull either confirms a genuine step forward or faces a reality check about the scale of the gap they need to close.

What's Next

The Austrian Grand Prix runs June 26-28 at the Red Bull Ring. Verstappen will be the sentimental favorite of the home crowd regardless of his championship position, and a strong result could reframe the narrative around Red Bull's 2026 season entirely. Expect him to be a genuine top-five contender in qualifying — and potentially on the podium if the upgrade delivers what the team is hoping for. Whether that translates to his first 2026 victory remains the defining question heading into Spielberg.
