Christian Horner Is Back in the F1 Paddock — One Year After Being Fired at the Same Venue

By Ryan Calloway | July 8, 2026

F1 Paddock News

Christian Horner returned to the Formula 1 paddock at Silverstone for the 2026 British Grand Prix — his first appearance at an F1 race weekend since Red Bull Racing dismissed him following the 2025 British Grand Prix at the same circuit. The timing and the venue were not, by any reading, coincidental.

Horner spent nearly 20 years as Red Bull's team principal, winning six Constructors' Championships and six Drivers' Championships with the Austrian outfit. He was sacked in the summer of 2025 amid internal tensions at Red Bull, and his presence at Silverstone this weekend was his most visible public re-engagement with the sport since his departure.

The Verstappen Connection

Horner's visit to Silverstone became more notable when Max Verstappen made a remark that drew immediate attention. Speaking to reporters during the British GP weekend, Verstappen confirmed that he speaks to Horner "almost every week" since the former team principal's departure from Red Bull.

That detail is significant for several reasons. Verstappen and Horner built four consecutive world championships together (2021-2024). Their working relationship was the defining partnership of the most successful era in Red Bull's history. The fact that the relationship continues — and does so frequently — suggests that Horner's influence on Verstappen, even now, remains real.

This matters most in the context of Verstappen's contract situation. The exit clause in his Red Bull deal is now mathematically certain to activate after the Hungarian Grand Prix on July 26. Verstappen is on the verge of leaving the team. The next chapter of his career is the biggest story in Formula 1. And his closest confidant in that process, apparently, is the man Red Bull fired a year ago.

Horner's Own Next Chapter

When asked whether he might return to Formula 1 in a leadership capacity, Horner was direct but non-specific: he would do so, he said, but "only on his terms." He did not name a team or indicate a timeline.

The F1 landscape heading into 2027 is unusually unsettled. The Verstappen domino — wherever it lands — will cascade through the driver market in ways that may also affect team leadership structures. Red Bull, in particular, faces a transition period that will test the team principal hired to replace Horner. If the team's results deteriorate further without Verstappen, and if Horner remains on good terms with the drivers and backers within the Red Bull ecosystem, the possibility of a return cannot be dismissed entirely.

A Symbolic Return to the Scene

There is an inherent drama in Horner choosing Silverstone — of all circuits, at this moment — for his return. Last year's British Grand Prix was his last race in the role he had held since Red Bull's debut in Formula 1. The circuit, the paddock, the grandstands: all of it unchanged since his departure. The cars and drivers and storylines are very different.

He stood in that paddock, presumably, and watched Max Verstappen drive a Red Bull Racing car that he no longer runs, against teams chasing a championship built on a foundation he spent 20 years constructing. Whatever his emotions on the day, the image is one that captures a particular kind of transition in sport — the former principal, back at the scene, watching from the outside.

What's Next

Horner's reappearance at Silverstone may be a one-off or the beginning of a more regular presence at race weekends. His own words — "only on my terms" regarding an F1 return — suggest an active interest in coming back, on a timeline and in a role that he controls. As the Verstappen drama resolves over the coming weeks and the 2027 driver market begins to take definitive shape, Horner's name may appear in contexts that go beyond a paddock visit.
