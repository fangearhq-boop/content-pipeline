Lewis Hamilton's Ferrari Future Confirmed: Seven-Time Champion Stays at Maranello Into 2027

By Ryan Calloway | July 8, 2026

F1 Driver News

Ferrari confirmed during the 2026 British Grand Prix weekend at Silverstone that Lewis Hamilton's contract has been extended into the 2027 Formula 1 season. The seven-time world champion, who joined the Scuderia for the first time this year, will continue his partnership with Charles Leclerc at least through the end of next season.

The announcement was made against the backdrop of Hamilton's strongest performance of his debut Ferrari season — a sprint qualifying pole, a sprint race that was won by Antonelli when he passed Hamilton on Lap 8 at the Hangar Straight, and a Grand Prix that ended with Hamilton on the podium in third place, retained after a stewards' review issued him only a reprimand (not a time penalty) for a yellow flag infringement.

The Numbers Behind the Extension

Hamilton's statistics are well established: 103 race victories, 104 pole positions, seven world championship titles, and records across virtually every major category in the sport's modern era. At 41 years old, he has shown no meaningful signs of decline. His 147 championship points this season — third place overall, 32 behind leader Kimi Antonelli — are the product of consistent pace and tactical intelligence that have defined his career.

Ferrari are second in the Constructors' Championship, 98 points behind Mercedes, with nine races remaining. Hamilton has been part of a Ferrari resurgence that has included Leclerc's win at the British Grand Prix — a 1-3 finish for the team that would have seemed implausible at the start of the season when Mercedes' technical advantage appeared overwhelming.

The Homecoming That Set the Stage

The British Grand Prix has always been emotionally charged for Hamilton. He won his first F1 race at Silverstone in 2008. He has won nine times in total at the circuit — a record that may never be matched. He holds the outright pole position record there with seven.

Racing at Silverstone for the first time in Ferrari red was, by his own description, a different experience. The Tifosi who traveled from Italy to Northamptonshire, the British fans whose complicated affection for Hamilton has evolved over his career, and the symbolic weight of a champion choosing a new path — all of it converged on the Silverstone weekend in a way that, perhaps, made the contract announcement feel like a natural complement to the occasion.

Ferrari chose this moment, and this venue, to confirm what was already widely understood: Hamilton is not going anywhere for at least another year.

What It Means for the 2027 Driver Market

Hamilton's confirmation into 2027 locks in one of the most intriguing driver pairings in the sport. Leclerc and Hamilton — a two-time world champion alongside a seven-time world champion — will continue to push each other and Ferrari toward the top of both championships. Whether that produces an eighth title for Hamilton is a question that belongs to next season. For now, the focus is on the remaining nine races of 2026 and whether Ferrari can close the 98-point gap to Mercedes in the Constructors' standings.

What's Next

Hamilton's next race is the Belgian Grand Prix at Spa-Francorchamps (July 17-19) — a circuit where he has won six times. A strong result at Spa would further narrow the gap to Antonelli in the drivers' championship and continue Ferrari's momentum heading into the summer break.
