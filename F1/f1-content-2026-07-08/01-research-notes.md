- Formula1.com — "Rare Sainz penalty for safety car infringement confirmed after British Grand Prix" (July 5)
- RaceFans — "FIA blames software error after criticism over failure to restart British Grand Prix" (July 5-6)
- Sky Sports — FIA safety car explanation
- The-Race — Sainz penalty and FIA explanation

**Key Facts (HIGH confidence — 2+ sources):**
- Sainz (Williams) originally classified P12 after the British GP
- FIA issued a unique "one-lap penalty" — added a lap to his final total — dropping him to P17, one lap behind the leader
- Reason: Sainz unlapped himself under the safety car when NOT included in the "lapped cars may overtake" message
- Williams failed to recognize this because of Silverstone's unusual pit lane layout — Sainz's car had temporarily unlapped itself by crossing the pit lane entry
- Described across multiple outlets as "unprecedented" — the first such penalty in F1 history
- This is the first F1 race to finish under a full safety car since the 2022 Italian Grand Prix
- On Lap 54, the FIA briefly displayed "Safety Car in this lap" — implying a restart — then cancelled it
- FIA post-race explanation: "software error" caused the erroneous message
