NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET Fred Vasseur didn't mince words. The Ferrari team principal said it plainly: "A new championship will start" at Miami. After a five-week break — the longest mid-season gap in modern F1 history, forced by the cancellations of the Bahrain and Saudi Arabian Grands Prix — every team has had unprecedented factory time to transform their 2026 car. The result: Miami will look nothing like Japan. Ferrari are bringing a near-complete overhaul of the SF-26, with estimates suggesting approximately half the car has been changed since the Japanese Grand Prix. McLaren boss Andrea Stella confirmed his team is delivering "a completely new car from an aerodynamic upgrades point of view" for the North American races. Red Bull will debut their full Macarena wing package — the Ferrari-inspired rear wing concept that could unlock significantly more top speed. And then there's Mercedes, who have won all three races and one Sprint so far, sitting 79 points clear in the constructors' championship. They've had five weeks too. Vasseur added that every team will bring new software improvements alongside hardware upgrades, suggesting the competitive picture could shift dramatically from Friday's practice session. If Vasseur is right — if Miami truly is the start of a "new championship" — this weekend could be one of the most consequential in years. #F1Fanrecap | #F1 | #MiamiGP

Norris & Piastri Respond to Verstappen Retirement Talk (Tier 1)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 10:00 AM ET Max Verstappen is ninth in the championship with 12 points, Red Bull's RB22 has been a mess all season, and the four-time world champion has been openly critical of F1's 2026 regulations. Retirement talk has followed. Now his rivals are responding. Lando Norris was asked about a potential Verstappen exit from Formula 1 ahead of Miami. His answer was both generous and candid: "It would be a shame and a miss for the sport," Norris said. "He probably is one of the best drivers you'll ever see in Formula 1." But Norris also pushed back on the retirement narrative: "I just saw that he said he wants to win a fifth world championship at the minute, so I'm sure he'll stay longer than people say." Oscar Piastri was less philosophical and more direct. A Verstappen departure, he said, would be "not a great look" for Formula 1. At the other end of the paddock, Verstappen has been raising concerns about the new regulations at FIA meetings, and his frustration with Red Bull's uncompetitive car is no secret. He reportedly has an exit clause that activates if he's not in the top two of the championship by summer — and at ninth, he is nowhere near that threshold. Verstappen's engineer and closest ally, Gianpiero Lambiase, is already confirmed to be leaving for McLaren in 2028. The clock is ticking. #F1Fanrecap | #F1 | #Verstappen

### Long-Form Post Window: 5:00 PM ET Lando Norris won the 2024 Miami Grand Prix. It was his first Formula 1 victory — ending a 110-race drought — and it came on the back of a race-changing upgrade package McLaren debuted at that same circuit. Two years later, history might repeat itself. McLaren team principal Andrea Stella confirmed that the team plans to arrive at Miami with what he described as "a completely new car from an aerodynamic upgrades point of view" for the North American swing covering Miami and Montreal. It's the most significant upgrade package McLaren has produced since the 2026 regulations launched, and the team believes it could close the gap to a Mercedes squad that has dominated the opening three races. McLaren sit third in the constructors' championship with 56 points — 79 behind Mercedes — and Norris is fifth in the drivers' standings on 25 points, with Oscar Piastri sixth on 21. The reigning constructors' champions are not where anyone expected them to be through three rounds. But the factory has been working hard over the five-week break. Stella's "completely new car" framing is not empty language — it reflects genuine belief that Miami could mark a turning point. If McLaren can split Mercedes at the front, the 2026 season suddenly looks like the title fight everyone hoped for. Miami is where it starts — or doesn't. #F1Fanrecap | #F1 | #MiamiGP | #McLaren

Five weeks after the Japanese Grand Prix, Formula 1 returns this week in Miami — and the world championship looks nothing like it did when the season began. These changes will affect how teams manage power through the Hard Rock Stadium circuit's unique mixed layout. **The Question** Can anyone challenge Mercedes?
