Ferrari to Finally Unleash Complete SF-26 at Miami After Positive Monza Test

By Elena Voss | April 27, 2026

Ferrari used the Monza filming day to test what was coming. Lewis Hamilton completed 15 laps and recorded a time of 1:26.585. Charles Leclerc ran 19 laps. Both drivers reported positive feedback from the updated package. Now, after weeks of preparation, the complete version of that package makes its race debut at Miami.

Analysts estimate that approximately half of the SF-26 has been changed since the Japanese Grand Prix — a level of overhaul that reflects how significantly Ferrari have recalibrated their understanding of the 2026 regulations over a single extended development cycle.

The Complete Package

The centerpiece of Ferrari's Miami upgrade is the full debut of the Macarena rear wing — the innovative design that rotates its upper elements to dramatically reduce drag on long straights while maintaining downforce through corners. Ferrari pioneered the concept and have been gradually refining it, but Miami will be the first time the complete design races at full specification.

Alongside the Macarena wing, Ferrari bring a redesigned floor and comprehensive aerodynamic overhaul. The changes address the SF-26's performance deficit relative to the dominant Mercedes W16 and aim to make the car more competitive across a wider range of circuit characteristics.

The Miami International Autodrome, with its mix of slow-speed sections near the Hard Rock Stadium and faster flowing sectors, will provide an immediate test of whether Ferrari's changes are working across the full spectrum of demands.

The Championship Context

Ferrari trail Mercedes by 45 points in the constructors' championship — a gap that reflects the Silver Arrow's dominance through the opening three rounds. Charles Leclerc is third in the drivers' standings on 49 points, Hamilton fourth on 41. Strong upgrades from Ferrari in Miami could see the Scuderia meaningfully close the gap — or at least begin to apply pressure that changes how Mercedes approaches subsequent races.

Ferrari team principal Fred Vasseur has spoken of a "new championship" beginning at Miami, a phrase that reflects both the optimism the team feels about their upgrade package and the genuine uncertainty about whether the pecking order will hold. If half the car has changed, the competitive picture could shift significantly.

What's Next

Friday's extended FP1 session — 90 minutes — begins the real answer to how effective the complete SF-26 specification truly is. Hamilton and Leclerc will have time to explore the full upgrade package, and the lap times from Friday morning will be studied closely by the entire paddock. If Ferrari shows up in Miami genuinely faster than they were in Japan, the 2026 title fight suddenly looks very different. That's the prospect that has everyone watching the Scuderia this week.
