McLaren Brings "Completely New Car" to Miami — Stella's Bold Promise Ahead of American Swing

By Marcus Cole | April 27, 2026

Lando Norris crossed the line in Miami two years ago for his first Formula 1 victory, ending a 110-race drought in the most emotional fashion. The circuit holds significance for McLaren, and now the team is returning with what their boss describes as the most significant upgrade of the 2026 season.

McLaren team principal Andrea Stella confirmed ahead of the Miami Grand Prix that the team plans to deliver "a completely new car from an aerodynamic upgrades point of view" for the North American swing covering both Miami and Montreal. It is a statement of intent from the reigning constructors' champions, who find themselves third in the championship — 79 points adrift of Mercedes — after three rounds of disappointing relative performance.

What Stella's "Completely New Car" Means

Stella's language is deliberately specific. McLaren are not talking about incremental improvements or single-component swaps. The team has targeted the North American races as their first major upgrade window since the 2026 regulations launched, and the aerodynamic overhaul is comprehensive.

McLaren used the five-week break between the Japanese Grand Prix and Miami to complete what their engineers had been building toward since pre-season. For a team that has historically excelled at in-season development — their 2024 Miami upgrade transformed their entire competitive season — this moment carries significant weight.

The reigning constructors' champions enter Miami in a position they didn't anticipate. Mercedes' dominance under the new regulations has been total through the opening three rounds, with Kimi Antonelli and George Russell taking turns at the front. Norris sits fifth in the drivers' standings on 25 points; Oscar Piastri is sixth on 21.

The 2024 Precedent

History provides a template for what McLaren can achieve when they arrive at a circuit with a transformative upgrade. In 2024, they debuted a game-changing package at Miami that Norris turned into a victory. That win began a run of McLaren form that ultimately delivered them the constructors' championship.

The team is now chasing lightning twice. If Stella's "completely new car" functions as intended on the Hard Rock Stadium circuit, McLaren could emerge from Miami as genuine title contenders — not just in 2026 terms, but as the team most capable of disrupting Mercedes' early dominance before it becomes insurmountable.

What's Next

Friday's extended FP1 session — 90 minutes rather than the usual hour — gives McLaren additional time to calibrate their new aerodynamic package. Sprint Qualifying and the Sprint Race on Saturday will provide competitive data, followed by Qualifying and the race itself on Sunday, May 3. Mercedes, Ferrari and Red Bull all arrive with their own significant upgrades, meaning the Miami weekend could genuinely reshape the 2026 championship picture. McLaren believe they're ready for it.
