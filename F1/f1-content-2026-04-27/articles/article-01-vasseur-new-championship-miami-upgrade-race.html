Vasseur Declares "New Championship" as Every F1 Team Arrives in Miami With Radical Upgrades

By Elena Voss | April 27, 2026

Fred Vasseur wasn't mincing words. The Ferrari team principal, speaking ahead of the Miami Grand Prix, said plainly: "A new championship will start" this weekend at the Hard Rock Stadium circuit. Five weeks after the Japanese Grand Prix — and following the cancellations of the Bahrain and Saudi Arabian rounds — every team in the paddock arrives in Florida with a transformed machine.

It may be the most significant upgrade weekend in recent Formula 1 memory. The question isn't whether the competitive order will shift. It's by how much.

The Factory Work Behind Vasseur's Words

An unprecedented gap between rounds gave every team extra time not just for hardware changes but for software development — the complex hybrid power unit calibration that defines so much of 2026 performance. Vasseur noted that software improvements, alongside the physical car updates, would make Miami a genuine reset point.

Ferrari arrive with what analysts estimate is approximately half of the SF-26 changed since Japan. The full Macarena rear wing — a concept pioneered by Ferrari in the wind tunnel — makes its race debut alongside a revised floor and comprehensive aerodynamic overhaul. It is, in the truest sense, a "complete" car that Ferrari believe can challenge Mercedes in a way they haven't been able to through the opening three rounds.

McLaren team principal Andrea Stella confirmed that his team is bringing "a completely new car from an aerodynamic upgrades point of view" to the North American swing covering Miami and Canada. For the reigning constructors' champions, who sit 79 points behind Mercedes despite their 2025 title pedigree, the upgrade represents the moment they've been building toward since the season began.

Red Bull, meanwhile, debut their own Macarena wing package — adopting the Ferrari-inspired concept that Laurant Mekies has called the key to unlocking the RB22's potential. Mekies tempered expectations, saying no miracles should be expected, but confirmed the package should represent a meaningful step.

What Mercedes Must Defend

The Silver Arrow arrives in Miami having won every race of 2026 so far — three Grands Prix and one Sprint. Kimi Antonelli leads the championship with 72 points, 9 ahead of teammate George Russell. The Mercedes W16 has been the class of the field under the new regulations, and the team has had five weeks of its own to prepare for the anticipated challenge.

That's the unanswerable question ahead of Friday's extended FP1 session: has the break helped Mercedes as much as their rivals? The team that wins the initial development race with the new regulations rarely stands still.

What's Next

Practice begins Friday, May 1, in an extended 90-minute session designed to give teams additional setup time with their new packages. Sprint Qualifying and the Sprint Race follow on Saturday, May 2, before Qualifying and the race itself on Sunday, May 3 at 4 PM ET. Miami could reshape the 2026 championship completely — or cement Mercedes' authority even further. Either way, Vasseur is right about one thing: a new chapter begins this week.
