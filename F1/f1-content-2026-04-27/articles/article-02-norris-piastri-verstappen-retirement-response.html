Norris and Piastri Weigh In on Verstappen Retirement: "A Miss for the Sport"

By Ryan Calloway | April 27, 2026

Max Verstappen is ninth in the 2026 Formula 1 championship with 12 points. Red Bull's RB22 has been one of the most uncompetitive cars of the new regulations era. And the four-time world champion has made no secret of his frustration — with the 2026 technical regulations and with his team's struggles to find performance. Retirement talk has been swirling for weeks.

Now the rest of the grid is responding — and the picture they paint is more nuanced than simple rival satisfaction at Verstappen's struggles.

Norris: "A Shame for the Sport"

Lando Norris was asked directly about a potential Verstappen exit from Formula 1 ahead of the Miami Grand Prix. The McLaren driver — and one of Verstappen's fiercest on-track rivals — was generous in his assessment.

"It would be a shame and a miss for the sport," Norris said. "He probably is one of the best drivers you'll ever see in Formula One."

Norris didn't stop there, pushing back against the retirement narrative itself: "I just saw that he said he wants to win a fifth world championship at the minute, so I'm sure he'll stay longer than people say."

The McLaren driver acknowledged the complexity of Verstappen's situation. He made clear that while Verstappen "makes our lives incredibly tough at times," that competitive friction is part of what makes Formula 1 compelling. Losing it would diminish the sport.

Piastri: "Not a Great Look"

Oscar Piastri took a more pointed line. If Verstappen were to walk away from Formula 1 amid his frustrations with the new regulations, Piastri said it would be "not a great look" for the sport.

The subtext is clear: one of the sport's biggest stars departing because his car isn't competitive enough — rather than exhausting every avenue to fix it — would send an uncomfortable message about F1's current direction.

The Backstory

Verstappen's situation at Red Bull has deteriorated significantly since the 2026 regulations launched. The RB22 has been consistently off the pace, with Verstappen failing to reach Q3 in Japan for the first time since his 2015 rookie season. He has zero wins and 12 points through three rounds.

He reportedly has a contract exit clause that could activate if he falls outside the top two of the championship by summer — and at ninth, he is a long way from qualifying. Gianpiero Lambiase, his engineer and closest ally since 2016 and through all four world championships, has already been confirmed to be joining McLaren in 2028.

Verstappen has been vocal in FIA meetings about his concerns with the 2026 technical regulations, and his criticism has extended publicly to the competitive picture Red Bull finds itself in. Red Bull's Miami upgrade package — including a Ferrari-inspired Macarena wing — is designed to begin closing that gap. But it may not be enough, fast enough.

What's Next

Miami is a critical weekend for Verstappen and Red Bull's upgraded RB22. A strong result would quiet the retirement conversation and demonstrate that the team's technical direction is working. A poor weekend — particularly if rivals like Ferrari and McLaren make big gains with their own upgrade packages — could reignite the speculation. The paddock is watching, and so is the world.
