McLaren's Miami Upgrade Could Reset the 2026 Season — And Norris' Title Defense

By F1 Fanrecap Staff April 1, 2026

A Title Defense Under Pressure

Lando Norris won the 2025 Formula 1 World Championship by two points from Max Verstappen at the final race in Abu Dhabi. Three rounds into his title defense, he sits fifth in the standings with 25 points — 47 behind championship leader Kimi Antonelli. The gap to Mercedes is the story of McLaren's opening stretch, and team principal Andrea Stella has been unusually candid about its origins: "We know it's not just power issues. We know we can do better aerodynamically."

That admission is significant. After McLaren dominated 2025 with the fastest car over a sustained period, the MCL40's aerodynamic deficit to Mercedes in 2026 represents a development miss under the new regulations — not a power unit disadvantage inherited from a supplier. The solution, Stella suggested, is already in the pipeline. A major aerodynamic update is expected to arrive at Miami.

From Disaster to Podium in Three Rounds

The arc of McLaren's first three rounds is almost too dramatic to be believed. Piastri crashed on the formation lap in Australia, costing him a race start in front of his home crowd. In China, both McLaren drivers failed to start due to separate electrical failures — a double technical DNF that handed 50 potential points to their rivals. The team's sole results from that stretch: Norris's fifth place in Australia.

Japan provided the first sign of recovery. Oscar Piastri — who had not completed a single Grand Prix lap to that point in 2026 — finished second, scoring McLaren's first podium of the season. Norris finished fifth, compromised by limited practice running after multiple car issues forced the team to keep him "on the back foot," per Stella. The constructors' tally climbed from zero to 56, still 79 points behind Mercedes.

The Miami Package

What arrives in Miami will be decisive. Team insiders describe the aerodynamic package as significant enough to address the MCL40's core competitiveness gap — not a tweak but a substantive rethink of the car's downforce generation. If the package performs as expected in testing and Sprint Saturday, McLaren could realistically be fighting for race victories in the second quarter of the season.

For Norris personally, Miami is a threshold moment. He came from behind in 2025 to win his championship, but that deficit was smaller and arrived in a car that was already fast. A 47-point deficit at Round 4, with Mercedes and Ferrari clearly ahead on pace, requires more than courage. It requires the aerodynamic package to deliver.

What's Next

McLaren's trajectory from Miami forward depends entirely on the update's performance. If the aerodynamic package delivers the gains Stella has implied, the 2026 championship picture — currently a Mercedes walkover — becomes genuinely multi-team. Piastri, who has now demonstrated podium pace at Japan, is as hungry for the title as Norris. A competitive McLaren with two race-ready drivers changes every team's calculus. Miami is their entry ticket back into the conversation.
