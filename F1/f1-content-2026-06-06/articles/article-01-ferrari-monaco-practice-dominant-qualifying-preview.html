Ferrari Fastest at Monaco: Hamilton, Leclerc Dominate Practice as Qualifying Looms

By Ryan Calloway | June 6, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

After five races of Mercedes dominance, Monaco has produced the first real challenge to Kimi Antonelli's grip on the 2026 Formula 1 season — and the color is red.

Ferrari finished first and second in both Friday practice sessions at the Circuit de Monaco. Charles Leclerc led a Ferrari 1-2 in FP1. Lewis Hamilton turned the fastest lap of the day in FP2 with a 1:13.026, edging Leclerc by the narrowest of margins. Behind them, Max Verstappen was the best of the rest — but still 0.168 seconds off Hamilton's benchmark. The Mercedes drivers of Kimi Antonelli and George Russell were adrift by roughly half a second.

Qualifying for the Monaco Grand Prix takes place today, Saturday June 6, at 4:00 PM local time (10:00 AM ET on Apple TV+). What happens in the next few hours will almost certainly determine who wins tomorrow's race.

Why Monaco Practice Matters More Than Anywhere Else

At most Formula 1 venues, practice sessions offer data and setup information, but the race itself allows for multiple variables — strategy, safety cars, tire degradation — to scramble the running order. Monaco is different.

The streets of Monte Carlo are too narrow, too unforgiving, and too difficult to overtake on for a driver who qualifies poorly to rescue a strong result on Sunday. The last three Monaco Grand Prix races have each been won wire-to-wire by the pole sitter. There is almost no passing under green-flag conditions, and the modern width of the 2026 cars makes the circuit even tighter than it has historically been.

That is why Ferrari's practice dominance has the paddock paying close attention. The Silver Arrows have been the team to beat all season, but Mercedes acknowledge they find Monaco challenging. Their deficit in Friday sessions was not marginal — it was enough to raise genuine questions about whether they can compete for pole position.

Hamilton and Leclerc: Ferrari's Dual Threat

The intra-Ferrari competition adds another layer of interest to the weekend. Hamilton and Leclerc have been close throughout practice, with the top position swapping between them across the two sessions. Leclerc, who has won three times at his home circuit and knows every millimeter of these barriers, described qualifying as "tight" and played down any sense of inevitability about Ferrari's advantage.

Hamilton's form coming into Monaco has been building. His second-place finish in Canada — the best result of his Ferrari career — was earned through a patient and precise race performance. He arrives at Monaco with confidence, and his fastest FP2 time demonstrates that confidence is translating to results on track.

Verstappen and the Challenge for the Rest

For Max Verstappen, Monaco practice offered a more encouraging reading than the last several races. Red Bull have appeared to suit slower, more technical circuits, and Verstappen was the best non-Ferrari driver across the weekend's opening day. But 0.168 seconds is a meaningful gap on a circuit where hundredths of seconds separate the grid positions, and where clean laps are determined as much by luck as by setup.

Antonelli and Russell, carrying the weight of their Canada sprint clash and a bruising championship weekend, will need to find performance that Friday simply did not reveal. The championship leader has an exit available — a circuit that doesn't suit his car — and his second-place rival has everything to gain from a strong weekend.

What's Next

Monaco GP qualifying takes place Saturday, June 6 at 4:00 PM local time (10:00 AM ET on Apple TV+). The race is Sunday, June 7 at 3:00 PM local time (9:00 AM ET on Apple TV+). Based on three consecutive Monaco races won by the pole sitter, whoever puts it on pole today will be the heavy favorite to lift the trophy in Monte Carlo tomorrow.
