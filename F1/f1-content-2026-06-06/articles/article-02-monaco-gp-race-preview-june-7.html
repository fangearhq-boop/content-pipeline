Monaco GP Race Preview: Ferrari Favored at Circuit Where Pole Position Wins the Race

By Marcus Cole | June 6, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

The most glamorous race in Formula 1 returns to the calendar on Sunday — and for once, it arrives with the genuine prospect of a result that matters to the championship, not just the history books.

The 2026 Monaco Grand Prix takes the green flag at 3:00 PM local time (9:00 AM ET on Apple TV+), with Charles Leclerc entering as the +185 betting favorite. Lewis Hamilton follows at 17% implied odds, championship leader Kimi Antonelli at 16%, and George Russell at 14% — all against a backdrop where Ferrari have dominated both Friday practice sessions and Mercedes look out of sorts on these narrow streets.

The Iron Law of Monaco

There is one rule at Monaco that overrides everything else: position on the grid at the start of the race is the position you will most likely finish. The circuit is so narrow, the barriers so close, and the overtaking opportunities so minimal that track position is everything. For three consecutive Monaco Grand Prix races, the driver who started on pole has won wire-to-wire without serious challenge.

That reality means qualifying today is, in a very real sense, the actual race. The fastest lap this afternoon does not just earn a trophy — it almost certainly earns the victory on Sunday.

Ferrari's practice dominance accordingly makes them the team to beat this weekend. Charles Leclerc was fastest in FP1. Lewis Hamilton was fastest in FP2 with a 1:13.026. Mercedes, who have won four of the first five races of the 2026 season, were half a second adrift — a chasm in Monaco terms.

Leclerc's Home Circuit Advantage

Charles Leclerc's record at Monaco is remarkable. Three wins on these streets, the most recent in 2024. He knows every apex, every barrier, every place where the margin between glory and the Armco is measured in centimeters. His ability to extract the absolute maximum from a lap at Monaco — on the limit, never over it — is one of the most impressive feats in modern Formula 1.

For Ferrari, a Monaco win would mean more than just points. They have not won a race in 2026 despite consistently being close. A victory here would announce their championship credentials in unmistakable terms and put pressure on Antonelli heading into the Spanish Grand Prix the following weekend.

The Mercedes Problem

Kimi Antonelli arrives at Monaco as the championship leader with 131 points and a 43-point advantage over Russell. But the Silver Arrows look exposed this weekend. The characteristics of Monaco — slow corners, technical braking zones, low aerodynamic load — favor the mechanical balance of the Ferrari over the raw speed that has powered Mercedes through the opening races.

Antonelli and Russell will need something from qualifying that their Friday pace didn't suggest was coming. If Ferrari converts practice form into a front-row lockout, and pole goes on to win as it has the last three years, Mercedes could lose significant championship points to their rivals this Sunday.

What's Next

The 2026 Monaco Grand Prix race starts Sunday, June 7 at 3:00 PM local time (9:00 AM ET on Apple TV+). Following Monaco, the Formula 1 calendar moves to the Spanish Grand Prix at Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya, June 12-14, for Round 8 of the 22-race season.
