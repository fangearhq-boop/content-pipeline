Three Monaco Wins, One Dream Left: Lewis Hamilton Chases History With Ferrari

By Ryan Calloway | June 6, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Lewis Hamilton has achieved almost everything there is to achieve in Formula 1. Eight world championships. The most victories in the sport's history. A record for pole positions that may never be broken. The only box that remains unchecked in his extraordinary career — a Monaco win in the red of Ferrari — is suddenly, realistically, within reach.

Hamilton arrived at Monaco's Circuit de Monaco on Friday and proceeded to set the fastest time of the day in FP2 with a 1:13.026. He and teammate Charles Leclerc finished the two practice sessions with Ferrari in first and second each time. The form is real.

The Monaco Legacy

Hamilton won the Monaco Grand Prix three times during his years with Mercedes. Each was a product of the same quality — pristine racecraft, impeccable car control in the tightest of spaces, and an ability to push to the absolute limit without making contact with the barriers that have ended so many other drivers' weekends at this circuit.

His move to Ferrari at the start of the 2026 season brought with it enormous expectation and immediate intrigue. But the first months in red were difficult — he finished outside the points in two of the first four races before his breakthrough P2 in Canada changed the narrative entirely.

The Canada Turning Point

The 2026 Canadian Grand Prix at Circuit Gilles Villeneuve was, by Hamilton's own assessment, one of the best races of his Ferrari career. Starting fifth, he methodically worked his way through the field during the middle phase of the race before hunting down Max Verstappen's Red Bull. On Lap 62, he dove down the inside at Turn 1 and completed the pass for second place.

"I love that hunt," Hamilton said afterward. "That's my whole life has been about that since a kid, since I had a really old go-kart, I was just always hunting." He thanked Ferrari for "moving mountains" — a phrase that reflected how much the team had developed the SF-26 in the weeks between Miami and Montreal.

The result was Ferrari's best of the 2026 season. It was also Driver of the Day — the fans' vote for the driver whose performance most deserved recognition.

Monaco Is Different

What makes Monaco special in Hamilton's potential narrative is the combination of factors that have come together this weekend. Ferrari are the fastest car on this circuit. Hamilton is in the form of his Ferrari career. And Monaco has historically rewarded the qualities — precision, patience, commitment — that have defined his driving throughout his career.

Leclerc, his teammate, is the circuit's three-time winner and its de facto hometown hero. There will be genuine competition between them in qualifying. But if Hamilton takes pole today, the pole-to-win pattern of the last three Monaco races would put him in the most commanding position of his 2026 season.

What's Next

Monaco GP qualifying takes place Saturday, June 6 at 4:00 PM local time (10:00 AM ET on Apple TV+). Race day is Sunday, June 7, 9:00 AM ET. A Hamilton win at Monaco would be his first of the 2026 season with Ferrari, and his fourth career victory at the Circuit de Monaco — matching Ayrton Senna's total at the circuit from a different era.
