Verstappen's Manager Confirms Exit Clause Active as McLaren Talks Reach Closing Stages

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | July 17, 2026

The landmark admission from Verstappen's management makes official what paddock insiders have been reporting for weeks — and a resolution could come before the summer break.

The Verstappen-McLaren saga took a decisive turn on Thursday when his manager publicly confirmed for the first time that Max Verstappen's Red Bull exit clause is officially active. The acknowledgment, reported by multiple outlets, marks the end of any pretense that the four-time world champion is simply fulfilling his contract through 2028. The clause is real, it is live, and negotiations with McLaren are in what multiple sources describe as "the closing stages."

The exit clause was triggered when Verstappen's championship position made it mathematically certain he would not finish in the top two of the Drivers' Championship. That threshold was built into his Red Bull contract as an escape hatch — a concession Red Bull made to sign him, now coming back to haunt them.

Red Bull Tries to Block It

Red Bull reportedly attempted to neutralize the clause by offering to buy it out, but Verstappen declined. That decision tells you everything about his intentions. If he were planning to stay, accepting the buyout would have been a straightforward way to cash in on leverage. By refusing, Verstappen signaled that no amount of additional compensation from Red Bull would keep him from exploring the McLaren opportunity.

Red Bull's position is now significantly weakened. They are potentially losing their marquee driver to their strongest rival — a scenario that would reshape the competitive balance of Formula 1 for years.

The McLaren Situation

For McLaren, the calculus involves a question they would rather not have to answer: what happens to Oscar Piastri? The Australian is under contract through 2028 with McLaren, and the team has invested heavily in developing him as a future world champion. A scenario involving a Piastri-Verstappen swap — Verstappen to McLaren, Piastri to Red Bull — has been discussed in paddock circles, though journalist Mat Coch has characterized the specific details as "hot air" at this stage.

More likely, McLaren is exploring whether they can accommodate both Verstappen and Piastri, at least for a transitional period, or whether a clean swap is the preferred outcome for all parties. Norris, under contract through 2027, is the third major variable in any McLaren lineup change.

What's Next

The summer break follows the Belgian Grand Prix, making the next two days a natural deadline for any announcement — or at least significant progress. Verstappen is free to conclude a deal before then. If he does sign with McLaren, the domino effects across the entire driver market will be immediate. Red Bull would need a replacement, teams with expiring contracts would recalibrate their strategies, and the 2027 grid would look dramatically different. The most significant driver move in Formula 1 since Hamilton's shocking switch to Ferrari appears to be close.
