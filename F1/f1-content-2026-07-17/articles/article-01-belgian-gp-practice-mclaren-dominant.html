McLaren Dominates Belgian GP Practice — Piastri Leads FP1, Norris FP2 at Spa

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 17, 2026

McLaren locked up the top of both practice sessions at Spa-Francorchamps, with Norris going fastest in FP2 at 1:42.260 while Mercedes struggled and the championship contenders watched from a distance.

McLaren arrived at Spa-Francorchamps as the favorites for the Belgian Grand Prix — and Friday's practice sessions did nothing to change that assessment. The papaya cars topped both Free Practice 1 and Free Practice 2, establishing dominant pace on a dry track before Saturday's potential rain scrambles everything.

In FP1, Oscar Piastri set the benchmark with Max Verstappen second and Lando Norris third. Mercedes' George Russell was fourth, Ferrari's Charles Leclerc fifth, and championship leader Kimi Antonelli sixth. Lewis Hamilton, needing a strong weekend in the title fight, finished seventh.

FP2 saw the McLaren hierarchy shift slightly. Norris went fastest with a 1:42.260 lap, Piastri second, and Verstappen third. Leclerc improved to fourth with Ferrari's Carlos Sainz fifth. But the headline was where Mercedes finished: Russell sixth, Hamilton a distant 10th.

Mercedes Under Pressure

For a team that won seven of the first nine races this season, Friday at Spa felt like a warning sign. Both Mercedes drivers finished well outside the top five in FP2, a session that typically reflects the true long-run and qualifying-simulation pace. Hamilton, who is 32 points behind Antonelli in the championship, needs a big weekend. Friday did not suggest it will come easily.

Hamilton has been vocal this week about his belief that the Mercedes driver pair "should be a lot further ahead" in the championship standings. But with McLaren outpacing them in practice, and Ferrari strong on raw pace, the path to closing the gap looks narrower than it did at the start of the season.

Antonelli's Spa Concern

Kimi Antonelli has a complicated history at Spa. In 2025, he was eliminated in Q1 of qualifying and finished 16th in the race — his worst result of that season. He arrives in Belgium as championship leader, but his P6 in FP1 does not project the kind of authority he has shown at other circuits this year.

If Saturday is dry and McLaren maintains this form, Antonelli faces a difficult qualifying battle on a track where he historically has not maximized his potential. The title implications of another difficult Spa weekend could be significant.

Aston Martin Encouraging

One Friday subplot worth monitoring: Aston Martin brought an upgrade package to Spa and showed early signs of progress. In FP1, both Lance Stroll and Fernando Alonso broke into the top 10, finishing ahead of Racing Bulls' Isack Hadjar. The improvement is modest but meaningful for a team looking to gain ground in the constructors' championship.

What's Next

Qualifying takes place Saturday at Spa-Francorchamps, with a 65 percent rain forecast hanging over the session. Wet conditions would completely change the grid picture — McLaren's dry pace advantage could evaporate in a downpour, and drivers like Hamilton and Leclerc have historically excelled in variable conditions. The Belgian Grand Prix race follows on Sunday, July 19, the final event before the Formula 1 summer break.
