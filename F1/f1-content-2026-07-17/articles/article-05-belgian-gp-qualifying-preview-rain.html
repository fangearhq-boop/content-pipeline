Belgian GP Qualifying Preview: Rain Could Erase McLaren's Friday Advantage at Spa

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | July 17, 2026

A 65 percent rain forecast for Saturday qualifying could neutralize McLaren's dominant dry-track pace and open the door for Hamilton, Leclerc, and others who excel in changeable conditions at Spa-Francorchamps.

If you watched McLaren's dominance in Friday practice at Spa-Francorchamps and assumed the Belgian Grand Prix grid is settled, the weekend's weather forecast would like a word.

Saturday qualifying carries approximately a 65 percent probability of rain at the Spa-Francorchamps circuit. At a venue famous for weather systems that can differ dramatically from one corner to the next, that forecast changes everything about what the grid might look like ahead of Sunday's race.

Hamilton's Wet-Weather Edge

Lewis Hamilton has won six times at Spa-Francorchamps across his career, at least some of those victories delivered in variable and challenging conditions. In wet or mixed-condition qualifying sessions, the seven-time champion's ability to extract performance through feel and adaptability has historically made him one of the hardest drivers to beat.

Hamilton is 32 points behind championship leader Kimi Antonelli. A pole position in wet qualifying followed by a strong race result would significantly alter the title fight picture heading into the summer break. The circuit suits his instincts, and Saturday's weather might give him exactly the opportunity the dry practice sessions have not.

Leclerc's Spa Track Record

Charles Leclerc is the other driver the rain forecast favors. The Ferrari driver has taken pole position at Spa in 2019, 2023, and 2024 — an extraordinary consistency at a circuit that punishes those who do not commit fully through Eau Rouge and Raidillon. Ferrari showed legitimate race pace in FP2, with Leclerc fourth. On a wet Saturday, he could threaten the front row or better.

McLaren's Position

McLaren led both practice sessions decisively. Norris posted a 1:42.260 in FP2, Piastri was second, and neither Mercedes nor Ferrari could match their pace in the dry. If conditions remain dry for qualifying, McLaren is the clear favorite to claim the front row and control the race.

The question is whether the papaya cars can manage their pace as effectively in the wet. McLaren has been stronger on dry circuits this season. A rain-affected qualifying would test their adaptability and potentially hand the advantage to rivals who perform better in unpredictable conditions.

What's Next

Qualifying at Spa-Francorchamps takes place Saturday afternoon. Whatever the conditions produce, the Belgian Grand Prix grid will set the stage for the last race of the season before the summer break. With the championship battle now tightened to a 32-point spread across three drivers, Sunday's result at Spa carries more weight than perhaps any race in the 2026 season so far.
