Aston Martin's Spa Upgrade Shows Promising Signs — Stroll and Alonso Break Into FP1 Top 10

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 17, 2026

Both Aston Martin cars cracked the top 10 in the opening practice session at Spa-Francorchamps, with an upgrade package giving the team its most encouraging Friday data in several rounds.

Aston Martin came to Belgium looking for a turning point. They may have found early evidence of one on Friday.

The British team brought a new upgrade package to Spa-Francorchamps, and in Free Practice 1, both Lance Stroll and Fernando Alonso broke into the top 10 — ahead of Racing Bulls' Isack Hadjar. For a team that has struggled to find consistent pace in the mid-season, the result provides a genuine positive datapoint ahead of qualifying and the race.

Why Spa Could Suit the Upgrade

Spa-Francorchamps rewards a particular combination of aerodynamic efficiency and mechanical grip. The Kemmel Straight, one of the longest full-throttle sections on the Formula 1 calendar, favors cars with strong straight-line speed. The upgraded package is reported to have improved Aston Martin's aerodynamic efficiency, which translates directly into pace on circuits like this one.

Fernando Alonso knows Spa as well as any driver in the field. The two-time world champion has won here, taken poles here, and extracted the maximum from whatever machinery he has been handed at this circuit across a career spanning two decades. If the upgrade delivers what Friday's data suggested, Alonso is well-positioned to maximize it.

Context for the Mid-Field

The constructors' championship battle in the mid-field has been tight throughout 2026. Racing Bulls, Alpine, and Aston Martin have traded blows regularly, with points swings determined as much by mechanical reliability as outright pace. A strong Belgian GP for Aston Martin could meaningfully alter their standing in that battle.

Stroll, often overshadowed by Alonso's experience and media profile, has shown flashes of strong race pace this season. A circuit like Spa, where aerodynamic drag plays a premium role, could be where the upgrade benefits him most.

What's Next

The real test of the upgrade package comes Saturday in qualifying. The rain forecast — approximately 65 percent probability — could muddy the data picture, but any dry-track lap in qualifying will confirm whether Friday's FP1 top-10 result translates to genuine competitive improvement. The Belgian Grand Prix race on Sunday July 19 will be the clearest measure of whether Spa represents a sustainable step forward for Aston Martin, or a one-off result at a favorable circuit.
