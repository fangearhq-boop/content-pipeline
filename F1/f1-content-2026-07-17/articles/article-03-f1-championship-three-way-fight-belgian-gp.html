The F1 Title Race Is Now a Three-Way Fight — Belgian GP Is a Must-Win

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 17, 2026

With Antonelli's 66-point lead reduced to 25 over three races, Russell and Hamilton have transformed what looked like a procession into one of the most compelling championship finales in recent memory.

Three races ago, Formula 1's 2026 championship looked settled. Kimi Antonelli held a 66-point lead and had reeled off five wins and five poles in the opening half of the season. The teenager from Bologna was rewriting the rookie record books with a performance so dominant it left rivals searching for explanations.

Then everything changed. A wheel shield failure at Silverstone, Ferrari's surge, and consistent scoring from George Russell and Lewis Hamilton have compressed the standings into something genuinely unpredictable. Heading into the Belgian Grand Prix, Antonelli leads with 179 points. Russell is 25 behind at 154. Hamilton trails by just 32 at 147.

How the Gap Closed

The arithmetic of the last three races has been stark. Antonelli, who won seven of the first nine rounds between himself and team-mate Russell, lost 41 net points to the chasing pack over that span. Ferrari won two of those three races. The Scuderia's resurgence — powered by a combination of upgrades and race-day strategies — has turned the constructors' battle into a genuine fight while simultaneously elevating Hamilton's individual title chances.

Hamilton was candid in Belgium this week. The seven-time champion said his Mercedes duo "should be a lot further ahead" — an acknowledgment that the season, despite its promising start, has not gone according to plan. Hamilton is right that Mercedes had the pace to dominate. But the sport rarely respects the plan.

Why Belgium Matters So Much

The Belgian Grand Prix is Round 10 and the final race before the Formula 1 summer break. That context transforms it from a regular race into a statement race. Whatever the standings show on Sunday evening in Belgium will define the narrative going into the second half of the season.

If Antonelli wins or finishes on the podium while Russell and Hamilton struggle, his lead stabilizes or grows and the championship conversation shifts back to a manageable advantage. If McLaren dominates — as Friday's practice suggested is possible — and Antonelli struggles again at a circuit where he has historically underperformed, the gap could close to nearly nothing heading into the break.

Friday's practice was not encouraging for Antonelli or Mercedes. McLaren went fastest in both sessions. Russell was sixth in FP2. Hamilton was 10th. Ferrari's Leclerc was fourth. The pre-break picture is complex.

What's Next

Qualifying Saturday, race Sunday. If rain falls on Saturday as forecast, the grid could look completely different from what Friday suggested. The championship will not be decided in Belgium — there are still many rounds remaining after the break — but the psychological and mathematical stakes of this weekend are as high as any race of the 2026 season. After the summer, the pressure only increases. Whoever leads the standings out of Spa carries the momentum into autumn.
