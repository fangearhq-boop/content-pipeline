Antonelli Makes F1 History: First Driver to Win First Three Races from Pole

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | May 17, 2026

In more than seven decades of Formula 1, no driver had ever won their first three Grand Prix starts from pole position. Then came a teenager from Bologna.

Kimi Antonelli, 19, has achieved something that has never been done in the sport's history. Three starts. Three poles. Three victories. The Mercedes driver has not just made a strong start to his second F1 season — he has made the kind of start that no one who came before him ever managed.

After the Miami Grand Prix in early May, Antonelli leads the 2026 Formula 1 World Championship with 100 points — a 20-point advantage over Mercedes teammate George Russell. He arrives at the Canadian Grand Prix next weekend as both the championship leader and the holder of a record that may stand for a very long time.

Three Races, Three Historic Wins

Antonelli's first victory came at the Chinese Grand Prix in a commanding drive ahead of Russell. He followed it with a win at the Japanese Grand Prix, where he moved to the top of the championship standings. Then came Miami — arguably the hardest of the three.

At the Miami Grand Prix, Antonelli didn't just win from pole. He had to earn it. Wheel-to-wheel exchanges with Charles Leclerc and Lando Norris made for the kind of race that tests composure as much as speed. The teenager held his nerve and crossed the line first, extending his lead and cementing the record.

The formula1.com described his start as "the strongest points-scoring start to an F1 season in ten years." For context, his 100 points through four race weekends (which include the sprint race format) reflects a near-maximum points haul at a pace that dwarfs what rivals have managed.

The Youngest Championship Leader in F1 History

When Antonelli first took the championship lead at the Japanese Grand Prix, he did so at 19 years, 7 months, and 4 days — the youngest championship leader in the sport's history. It was another record that had to wait for the right driver to arrive.

Antonelli joined Mercedes as Lewis Hamilton's replacement for the 2025 season. In his debut year, he impressed without dominating. In 2026, with the regulation changes that introduced entirely new power unit and aerodynamic rules, he has taken command in a way that has left the paddock searching for explanations.

The Mercedes package is excellent in the new regulations — that much is clear from both Antonelli and Russell's performances. But the consistency and accuracy Antonelli has shown in wheel-to-wheel battles — at age 19, in his second full F1 season — is something beyond the car.

The Championship Picture

Behind Antonelli: Russell at 80 points, Leclerc at 59, Hamilton and Norris both at 51, Piastri at 43, and Verstappen down in seventh with just 26 points as Red Bull continues to struggle. The gap between Antonelli and the next non-Mercedes driver (Leclerc) is already 41 points. In a 24-race season, nothing is decided — but the lead is substantial.

For the record to be challenged, Antonelli would need an opponent to win a race before this season ends and then establish themselves at the front of the grid for future seasons. The record of three consecutive opening wins from pole stands on its own, regardless of how the rest of 2026 unfolds.

What's Next

Antonelli heads to the Canadian Grand Prix (May 22-24) at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve seeking a fourth consecutive win. The circuit is a low-downforce layout with heavy braking zones — different in character from the races he has already won. It will be his first sprint weekend in Canada. If the historical pattern holds, worrying about Antonelli's preparation seems like a low-priority concern.
