Verstappen on Lambiase's McLaren Move: "You'd Be Crazy Not to Take It"

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 17, 2026

Max Verstappen said the quiet part out loud — and it tells you everything about the atmosphere at Red Bull in 2026.

Race engineer Gianpiero Lambiase, who has guided Verstappen through four Formula 1 world championships, will leave Red Bull at the end of 2027 to take a senior role at McLaren. This week, Verstappen spoke publicly about the moment Lambiase told him the news.

"He told me about the offer he received," Verstappen explained. "I told him, 'You'd be crazy not to take it.' We've achieved so much together and now he has a chance to move forward."

The response was gracious — and revealing. A driver who has won four consecutive championships with the same engineer, blessing that engineer's move to a rival team, speaks to a relationship built on genuine mutual respect rather than contractual loyalty alone.

What Lambiase's Move Means

Lambiase's exact role at McLaren has been a topic of speculation. Red Bull Team Principal Laurent Mekies has described Lambiase as going to McLaren as "a team principal." McLaren's Andrea Stella, for his part, has played down reports of "astronomical salaries and mythical pre-contracts," suggesting some details remain fluid.

What is confirmed: Lambiase will fulfill his contract at Red Bull through 2027 before making the switch. McLaren has secured one of the most respected engineering minds in the sport for a long-term leadership position.

Red Bull and their engine partner Ford have been firm in messaging. Ford stated their "F1 commitment is not based on one person," a reference to concerns that Lambiase's departure might destabilize Verstappen's relationship with the team. Red Bull's position is that the team and driver remain aligned.

The Exit Clause Hanging Over Everything

Verstappen still has a contract exit clause that becomes active at the Hungarian Grand Prix (July 24-26). Publicly, he has committed to Red Bull. Privately, the speculation around his future has not quieted despite those assurances.

The context is hard to ignore. Verstappen sits seventh in the 2026 championship with just 26 points after four races. Red Bull, powered by the new Red Bull-Ford power unit, has struggled significantly with the 2026 regulations while Mercedes dominates and Ferrari and McLaren compete for second. The team that produced dominant machinery from 2021-2024 has yet to find its footing in the new era.

Lambiase joining McLaren from 2028 is one part of a puzzle. Verstappen's 2026 season performance, Red Bull's car development trajectory, and the Hungarian exit clause are the other pieces. The full picture won't be clear until midsummer.

What's Next

The Canadian Grand Prix (May 22-24) gives Verstappen another opportunity to extract something from a Red Bull car that has not matched the front-runners in 2026. Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve's low-downforce characteristics could suit or hinder — the development direction of any team's 2026 car is still being understood. The Hungarian GP exit clause date is 10 weeks away. The Lambiase era at Red Bull continues through 2027, regardless of what comes before or after it.
