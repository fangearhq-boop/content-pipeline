Verstappen and McLaren in Closing Stages of Deal — Red Bull's Buyout Refused, Exit Clock Ticking

By Marcus Cole | July 15, 2026

The biggest driver move in a generation is approaching its conclusion, with multiple sources confirming negotiations between the four-time champion and McLaren are in their final stages.

Max Verstappen is leaving Red Bull Racing.

The only question now is when the announcement comes, not whether it will. Multiple paddock sources confirm that Verstappen and McLaren are in the closing stages of contract negotiations for the 2027 Formula 1 season. Red Bull attempted to prevent the departure by offering €8 million to buy out the exit clause embedded in Verstappen's contract. He refused. The exit clause is active.

The trigger was mathematical. Verstappen's contract contained a performance clause allowing him to exit if he found himself outside the top two in the drivers' championship at the start of the summer break. After his crash at Stowe corner during the British Grand Prix — an incident he described on team radio as "super dangerous" — he became mathematically unable to finish in the top two entering the break. The clause activated. Red Bull's money didn't change his mind.

Why McLaren?

McLaren has transformed itself over the past two seasons into one of the most competitive operations in Formula 1. Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri have developed into a genuinely dangerous pair, capable of winning races and challenging for championships. The infrastructure behind them — the engineering team, the factory, the simulator resources — has been rebuilt to the standard required to fight at the very top of the sport.

For Verstappen, McLaren represents what Red Bull no longer can offer: a car competitive enough to fight for the title. The RB22 has been outpaced by Mercedes and Ferrari through the first half of 2026. Four championships or not, Verstappen will not accept a car that cannot win. McLaren gives him a realistic path back to championship contention in 2027.

The complicating factor is McLaren's existing lineup. Norris is contracted through 2027. Piastri through 2028. Exactly how McLaren restructures to accommodate Verstappen — whether one driver is bought out, moved aside, or whether a three-driver situation is somehow navigated — has not been confirmed publicly. What has been confirmed is that negotiations are in closing stages. The rest follows from that.

The End of an Era at Red Bull

Four consecutive world championships from 2021 through 2024. A record-breaking 2023 season that was as dominant as anything seen in the sport's modern era. Verstappen's partnership with Red Bull produced results that will be debated for decades. The decision to leave, at this stage and in these circumstances, says something specific about where that partnership stands in 2026.

The car is not what it was. The relationship, apparently, is not either. Verstappen refused €8 million to stay. That tells you everything about how committed he is to this move.

What's Next

Verstappen must formally notify Red Bull of his intention to exit by October 2026 at the latest. The announcement could come much sooner. If negotiations are truly in closing stages, the paddock at Spa-Francorchamps this weekend could be the setting where everything becomes official. The F1 driver market is about to experience its biggest shock since Hamilton left Mercedes.
