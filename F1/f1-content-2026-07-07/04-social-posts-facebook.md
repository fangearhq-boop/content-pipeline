### STORY 5: Cadillac's Rocky F1 Debut

The long-awaited Cadillac F1 entry debuted in 2026, bringing star power (Valtteri Bottas and Sergio Perez as its driver pairing) and significant investment from General Motors. After nine races, the picture is what everyone who understands F1 entry realities predicted: it's incredibly hard to compete as a new team.

Bottas and Perez finished P16 and P14 respectively at Silverstone, continuing a season where the team has largely run in the second half of the grid. That's not a criticism — it's the reality of entering Formula 1 in a year where the technical regulations completely changed. Every existing team spent years developing knowledge under the old rules. Cadillac is building from scratch, competing against teams with decades of accumulated infrastructure, data, and expertise.

The more interesting question is where Cadillac goes from here. Their 2026 campaign is a learning exercise — gathering data, developing the car, building the culture and processes that eventually turn a new team into a competitive one. Red Bull wasn't Red Bull in their first year. Mercedes didn't become the dominant force they were for a decade overnight.

For Perez, there's a personal dimension to process as well. The Mexican star spent years at Red Bull and was a race winner. A year at Cadillac in the mid-pack is a humbling but potentially meaningful reset. For Bottas, who spent years as a very fast second driver at Mercedes, the chance to lead a team's development is its own kind of career evolution.

The American F1 dream is real — it just needs time to grow. 🏎️
