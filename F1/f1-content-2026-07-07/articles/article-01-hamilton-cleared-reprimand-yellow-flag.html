Hamilton Cleared: Stewards Issue Reprimand Only After British GP Yellow Flag Investigation

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 7, 2026

Lewis Hamilton will keep his third-place finish at the British Grand Prix. The FIA stewards, following a post-race investigation into the Ferrari driver's behavior under yellow flags during Max Verstappen's crash at Stowe, issued a reprimand and nothing more — no time penalty, no points deduction, no grid drop for Belgium.

The investigation centered on Hamilton's passage through the yellow flag zone activated by Verstappen's crash. In theory, drivers must reduce speed under yellows and be prepared to stop. In practice, the question was whether Hamilton had done enough.

The Stewards' Reasoning

The stewards' decision acknowledged something that changes the context of the infringement entirely: Hamilton was checking his mirrors in the moments before and during the yellow flag zone. The reasoning is straightforward — with Verstappen crashing ahead, Hamilton's instinct was to monitor what was behind him, watching for any counter-attack from competitors or debris risks from the incident zone. The stewards accepted that Hamilton's speed through the yellow zone was consistent with a driver managing a developing, dangerous situation rather than deliberately exploiting the caution period to gain advantage.

It is the kind of nuanced decision that F1's stewards occasionally make when the letter of the rule and the spirit of the moment aren't perfectly aligned. Hamilton didn't benefit from ignoring the yellows — he was in third place and holding position in a race that had already largely settled. The stewards gave him the benefit of the doubt, and the reprimand serves as a formal acknowledgment that his actions were at the edge of acceptability without crossing into penalizable territory.

Championship Implications

The stakes of this decision were significant. Hamilton entered the race in third in the championship, behind Kimi Antonelli (179 points) and George Russell (154 points) with 147 of his own. A five-second penalty would have reshuffled the podium, potentially costing him 3-5 points at a moment when every point between the title contenders matters.

Instead, Hamilton walks away from Silverstone with his 147 points intact, still 32 behind Antonelli with 13 rounds remaining. The math still requires a significant swing in his direction, but it's possible — especially given Antonelli's own nightmare race where a wheel shield failure cost the championship leader 13-17 points he would have otherwise scored.

A reprimand has no direct penalty attached unless Hamilton accumulates a third reprimand in the same season. He'll need to be mindful of that as the second half of 2026 unfolds — a third incident, even a minor one, triggers an automatic 10-place grid drop.

What's Next

Hamilton heads to the Belgian Grand Prix at Spa-Francorchamps on July 17. Spa is a circuit that historically favors drivers who can manage tires over a demanding lap — a strength Hamilton has demonstrated throughout his career. He's won at Spa multiple times across his career and will be motivated to convert Silverstone's near-miss into a clean result. The three-way battle among Antonelli, Russell, and Hamilton for the 2026 championship is very much alive.
