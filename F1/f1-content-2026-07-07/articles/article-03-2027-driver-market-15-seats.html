The 2027 F1 Driver Market Is Already Here — 15 of 22 Seats Still Unconfirmed

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 7, 2026

By the time the 2026 Belgian Grand Prix is contested on July 17, the F1 paddock will be running two simultaneous competitions: the championship battle on track, and the increasingly urgent contract negotiations off it. With 15 of the 22 drivers currently competing in Formula 1 still without confirmed seats for the 2027 season, the transfer market that begins in earnest during the second half of this year will be among the most consequential in recent memory.

The number is worth sitting with for a moment. Nearly seven in ten active F1 drivers are, to varying degrees, without a guaranteed future in the sport. Some of those 15 will be straightforward renewals at their current teams. Others will move. A few will not have seats at all by the time 2027 arrives.

Who Is Confirmed

The short list of confirmed 2027 seats includes some of the sport's biggest names. Max Verstappen is at Red Bull through 2028, though his performance-based exit clauses mean that contract should be read with an asterisk. Oscar Piastri extended with McLaren through 2028, making him one of the most clearly committed drivers on the grid. Lewis Hamilton signed a multi-year deal with Ferrari that runs through 2027 with an option for 2028 — at 41, Hamilton's career arc makes any extension beyond that less certain.

Beyond those three, the confirmed list thins rapidly. Only four teams have at least one driver confirmed for 2027, according to reporting from Sky Sports and PlanetF1. That means the vast majority of the grid is in play.

The Most Interesting Cases

George Russell is perhaps the most intriguing contract situation on the grid. He is second in the championship, having had the best season of his career at Mercedes alongside the phenomenon that is Kimi Antonelli. Russell is a proven race winner, a consistent top-three qualifier, and exactly the kind of driver that would attract interest from multiple teams if he became available. The question is whether Mercedes views his future as equally central to theirs, or whether Antonelli's emergence changes how the team views its long-term driver lineup.

Charles Leclerc won the British Grand Prix and is fourth in the standings — a driver whose deal at Ferrari must be among the most stable on the grid, even if no official confirmation has been issued. Carlos Sainz, who moved to Williams in 2026 and has delivered solid performances despite Williams' mid-pack standing, faces a different kind of uncertainty: does he commit to another year in a developing team, or does the market open a door to a more competitive seat?

Then there's Antonelli himself. You would expect Mercedes to secure the 18-year-old championship leader on a long-term contract immediately. No official announcement has been made public. That's the kind of detail that generates speculation even when the underlying reality is almost certainly straightforward.

What's Next

Contracts in F1 are typically finalized in the second half of the season, with some teams announcing their 2027 lineups as early as August and others waiting until the final races. The Verstappen situation — specifically, whether his performance-exit clauses become relevant — is the story that could catalyze the entire market. If Verstappen moves, it triggers a chain reaction across every team that might receive him and every team that might lose a driver to make room. The Belgian GP is two weeks away. The silly season has already started.
