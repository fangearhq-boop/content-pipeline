F1 2027 Driver Market: Norris and Piastri Locked In, Verstappen the Wildcard That Changes Everything

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 11, 2026

Formula 1 is only four rounds into its 2026 season, but the 2027 driver market is already beginning to take shape — and it has one enormous variable at its center. Most of the grid's most competitive seats are occupied by drivers under multi-year contracts. And then there is Max Verstappen, whose future at Red Bull depends on events unfolding in real time over the next two months.

Understanding the market requires separating the stable from the uncertain. Let's start with what is known.

The Locked-In Lineup

At McLaren, the picture is as settled as it gets. Lando Norris, the 2025 Formula 1 World Champion, signed an extended multi-year contract with the team that runs through at least the end of the 2027 season. Oscar Piastri, Norris' teammate and himself a genuine title contender, is contracted through 2028. McLaren's driver lineup for the foreseeable future is one of the most formidable in the paddock — two drivers with championship pedigree, both in their peak years.

At Mercedes, Kimi Antonelli is tied to a long-term development deal with the team that has nurtured him from junior formulas. George Russell, now second in the 2026 championship, remains under contract. The current Mercedes driver pairing is running first and second in the world championship. It is not a lineup that needs external reinforcement — unless an external reinforcement of historic magnitude presents itself.

The Verstappen Variable

Max Verstappen's Red Bull contract contains a performance-linked exit clause. If he is not in the top two of the Drivers' Championship when the season pauses for the Hungarian Grand Prix on July 24-26, he can trigger a departure process — formally notifying Red Bull within three months of his wish to leave the team.

With Verstappen currently seventh in the championship at 26 points, 54 behind second-placed Russell, the likelihood of triggering that clause approaches certainty with each passing race. Red Bull has shown improvement — they halved their aerodynamic weight deficit at Miami — but the gap between seventh and second is not a gap that circuit-by-circuit upgrades can close in nine races.

The obvious destination, if the clause triggers, is Mercedes. Toto Wolff has made no secret of his admiration for Verstappen and has publicly pursued him in each of the past two seasons. Industry sources cited by multiple major outlets estimate that a Mercedes-Verstappen deal would start at $80-90 million annually — a figure that would be the largest contract in Formula 1 history. Verstappen would be trading a struggling car for the dominant car on the grid, combining with the team currently running 1-2 in the championship.

The Questions That Follow

The questions that a potential Verstappen departure from Red Bull would trigger cascade through the entire paddock. If Verstappen joins Mercedes, where does that leave Russell? Or Antonelli, in his second season and currently leading the championship? Mercedes cannot run three drivers. Every seat adjustment creates ripple effects that move other drivers, affecting teams across the grid.

For Red Bull, the loss of Verstappen — the driver who delivered four consecutive world championships — would require a generational talent acquisition that does not obviously exist at the ready in the current driver pool. Their new Ford-powered engine is a genuine achievement; the RB22 chassis needs development work. Without Verstappen, the incentive structure for that development changes significantly.

What's Next

The summer break is the first major inflection point. The Hungarian Grand Prix on July 24-26 triggers the evaluation window in Verstappen's contract. His decision about whether to activate the exit clause will follow. The consequences of that decision — wherever they lead — will be the dominant off-track story of the second half of the 2026 season.

For the rest of the grid, the stable picture is less dramatic but equally important. Norris at McLaren, Piastri at McLaren, Antonelli at Mercedes — the championship contenders are locked in their seats. The driver market will move around Verstappen. It usually does.
