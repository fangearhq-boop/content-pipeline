Three Wins, Three Poles, No Precedent: How Kimi Antonelli Became F1's Most Historic Story

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | May 11, 2026

In 76 years of Formula 1, no driver has ever won their first three race starts from pole position. Until now. Kimi Antonelli, 19 years old and in his second season with Mercedes, has done exactly that — and he is doing it at the top of the Drivers' Championship with 100 points.

The records that have fallen in front of Antonelli's Mercedes this season are not minor footnotes. He is the youngest driver ever to lead the F1 World Drivers' Championship. He is the first driver in the sport's history to win their first three starts from pole. And he has done all of it while driving against a grid that includes four former world champions.

"He's gaining credibility," multiple figures in the F1 paddock have noted in recent weeks, using language that understates the phenomenon unfolding in front of them. The correct word might be "unprecedented."

The Record-Breaking Sequence

Antonelli's 2026 season opened in China, where he took pole and converted it into a race win. Then Japan — same story: pole, win. Then Miami, the most recent round, where he claimed his third consecutive pole-to-victory. According to F1 Records, no driver in the modern era or before has achieved three consecutive wins from pole to open a Formula 1 season.

The championship picture reflects the dominance. With 100 points from four rounds — including Sprint points where applicable — Antonelli has extended his lead over Mercedes teammate George Russell to 20 points. Russell, at 80 points, is no slouch. Their proximity to each other speaks to Mercedes' mechanical superiority this season. The gap between Antonelli and the nearest non-Mercedes driver, Charles Leclerc of Ferrari at 59 points, is already 41.

Defending champion Lando Norris and Lewis Hamilton are tied at 51 points in fourth place. Oscar Piastri sits on 43. It is a tight midfield — and then there is the top two, which are both driving silver cars.

The Context Behind the Kid

Antonelli came into Formula 1 as one of the most hyped prospects in the sport's history — a Mercedes junior who graduated directly from junior formulas to the grid's most sought-after seat. His 2025 rookie season produced 150 points, three podiums, and a maiden Grand Prix win, quieting early doubts about whether the jump was too fast.

In 2026, with the new technical regulations bringing every team to reset their development programs, Antonelli has found himself in the right car at the right moment. Mercedes has executed the new rules better than any other manufacturer. But the teenager's ability to extract maximum performance from that package — to qualify on pole, manage race pace, and handle the pressure of a championship fight — has gone beyond what any team's superiority alone can explain.

The Canadian Grand Prix, a Sprint weekend at Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve from May 22-24, will be the next chapter. Antonelli took his first career podium at that very circuit in his 2025 rookie year. He arrives now not as a promising newcomer but as the championship leader going for a fourth consecutive win.

What's Next

The mathematical ceiling of Antonelli's lead is not yet visible. With Russell as the nearest challenger — a teammate who by definition uses the same machinery — Antonelli's ability to finish ahead of him in races is the key variable. So far he has managed it in each of the first three Grands Prix.

The rest of the field — Leclerc, Norris, Hamilton, Piastri — have the talent. But they need a car capable of bridging a 41-point gap, or more, across the remaining 18 race weekends of the season. At the current rate of dominance, no one has provided evidence they can. The 19-year-old who has never done this before continues to be the most composed figure in the paddock.
