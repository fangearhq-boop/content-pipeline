### STORY 5: Antonelli's Spa Redemption Arc

**Long-Form Post:**
Kimi Antonelli leads the Formula 1 World Championship. He has five wins, five poles, and seven podiums in eleven races this season. He is, by any measurable standard, the best driver in the field so far in 2026.

And then there is Spa.

Last year at the Belgian Grand Prix, Antonelli had the worst weekend of his F1 career. He was eliminated in the first segment of Sprint Qualifying, finished the Sprint Race in P17. He was knocked out in Q1 for the main session and came home P16 in the Grand Prix. For a driver who has spent 2026 dominating the championship narrative, Spa remains the one circuit that exposed him.

Now he comes back as the leader. The circuit has not changed. The Eau Rouge-Raidillon complex still demands total commitment at full speed. The Kemmel Straight still comes fast. But Antonelli is not the same driver he was in 2025. He is the points leader, the benchmark, the man everyone is chasing.

Whether he can exorcise his Spa demons — with rain potentially in the mix and the championship within reach — is the defining storyline of Belgian GP week.
