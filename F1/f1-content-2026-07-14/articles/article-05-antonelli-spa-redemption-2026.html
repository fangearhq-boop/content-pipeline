From P16 to Championship Leader: Kimi Antonelli Returns to Spa for His Biggest Test Yet

By Marcus Cole | July 14, 2026

Spa-Francorchamps was the worst circuit of Kimi Antonelli's debut season. Now he arrives as F1 championship leader — and with rain on the forecast to add pressure to every corner.

Last year at the Belgian Grand Prix, Kimi Antonelli had the worst weekend of his Formula 1 career. He was eliminated in the first segment of Sprint Qualifying. He finished P17 in the Sprint Race. In the main session, he was knocked out in Q1. In the Grand Prix itself, he finished P16.

This year, he arrives at Spa as the Formula 1 World Championship leader.

The track has not changed. The high-speed flow of Eau Rouge-Raidillon still demands unwavering commitment. The Kemmel Straight still punishes a car that is not working correctly. The Bus-Stop chicane still catches drivers who arrive a fraction too fast. Spa is Spa — demanding, unforgiving, and absolutely indifferent to who is leading the championship.

Who Antonelli Is Now

The 2026 version of Kimi Antonelli is a different proposition from the driver who struggled through Belgium in 2025. He has five wins, five pole positions, and seven podiums in eleven races this season. He leads the championship by 25 points over George Russell and 32 over Lewis Hamilton. He is the benchmark that every other driver is chasing.

The gap between the 2025 and 2026 versions of himself is not primarily about talent — the talent was always there. It is about experience, about understanding the limits of the car, about the thousand small adjustments that only come from having done it before. Antonelli has done it before at Spa now. He knows what went wrong. Whether the corrections have held is what this weekend will tell us.

The Rain Adds Another Layer

Saturday qualifying at Spa currently carries a 65% rain probability. That makes the session not just technically demanding but genuinely unpredictable — the kind of qualifying where any driver's accumulated knowledge of the circuit can be rendered temporarily irrelevant by standing water in the wrong place at the wrong time.

For Antonelli, whose Spa problems in 2025 were partly rooted in setup issues that manifested under Saturday qualifying conditions, a wet Q1 is the scenario that demands the most from his mental preparation. For Lewis Hamilton — whose six Spa victories include races won in exactly this kind of weather — the rain is less threat than invitation.

What's Next

The Belgian Grand Prix is Round 12 of 22, and one of only two races remaining before the summer break. A strong result for Antonelli would reassert his championship credentials at the circuit that previously exposed him and extend a lead that Russell and Hamilton are working hard to erase.

A difficult weekend, conversely, would compress the standings further and bring the three-way title fight into the break in genuine equilibrium. Spa is the test Antonelli has been preparing for. Starting Friday morning in the Ardennes, we find out if the preparation was enough.
