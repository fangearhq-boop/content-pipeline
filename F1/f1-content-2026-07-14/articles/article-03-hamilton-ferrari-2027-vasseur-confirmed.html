Lewis Hamilton Staying at Ferrari in 2027 — Fred Vasseur Gives the One-Word Answer That Changes Everything

By Elena Voss | July 14, 2026

Ferrari team principal Fred Vasseur told an Italian newspaper "Yes" when asked if Hamilton will remain at the team in 2027 — the clearest confirmation yet that the seven-time champion is not going anywhere.

Fred Vasseur was asked a direct question by Italian newspaper Corriere della Sera this week: will Lewis Hamilton stay at Ferrari in 2027?

His answer: "Yes."

One word. No qualification. No reference to ongoing negotiations or timing. Just yes. From the Ferrari team principal. About one of the most consequential driver market questions in Formula 1 this season.

For a team known for measured communication and deliberate opacity on contract matters, it was a remarkably direct statement. And it answered the question that has been lingering over Hamilton's Ferrari tenure since the day he signed.

The Shift in Tone

Until recently, Vasseur's responses on Hamilton's 2027 future had been carefully non-committal. As recently as Silverstone, the team principal had said "it is not time to discuss an extension." The change in position — from deliberate deflection to a one-word confirmation — signals that the internal decision has been made and communicated.

Hamilton's current contract includes a 2027 option that sits on his side of the agreement. He can exercise it; Ferrari does not control whether it is activated. Vasseur's confirmation suggests that Hamilton has communicated his intention to exercise the option, and that Ferrari is fully aligned with keeping him.

What Hamilton Has Done at Ferrari

Hamilton joined Ferrari in 2026 in the most anticipated driver move of the decade, and his first full season in red has delivered on significant parts of the promise. He won at the Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya, his first Ferrari victory, and has added podiums at multiple other venues. He sits P3 in the 2026 championship with 147 points — just 32 behind championship leader Kimi Antonelli.

Heading into the Belgian Grand Prix, Hamilton has another storyline to pursue. He has six career wins at Spa-Francorchamps — more than any other active driver. A seventh this weekend would establish him as the outright record holder for victories at the Belgian circuit.

Ferrari's 2027 Lineup

With Hamilton confirmed for 2027 and Charles Leclerc signed to a multi-year extension through at least 2028 (confirmed ahead of the Monaco Grand Prix), Ferrari's driver lineup for the near future is set. Hamilton and Leclerc continue together on the SF-26 and its successor.

The contrast with Mercedes is stark. Mercedes enters 2027 with Kimi Antonelli and George Russell — both confirmed — opposing Ferrari's Hamilton and Leclerc. The next era of Formula 1 already has its marquee driver rivalry.

What's Next

An official Ferrari announcement formally confirming Hamilton's 2027 contract will likely follow in the weeks or months ahead. For now, Vasseur's word is the signal the market has been waiting for. Hamilton is staying in red.
