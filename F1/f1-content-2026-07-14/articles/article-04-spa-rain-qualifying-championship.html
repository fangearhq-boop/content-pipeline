Spa Weather Watch: 65% Rain for Belgian GP Qualifying Could Scramble the Championship Picture

By Ryan Calloway | July 14, 2026

Saturday's qualifying at Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps carries a 65% rain probability — a figure that benefits some championship contenders and threatens others.

The Ardennes microclimate is famously indifferent to Formula 1 storylines. It rains when it wants to rain. And the current forecast for Saturday qualifying at the 2026 Belgian Grand Prix suggests it very much wants to rain.

Weather models are currently giving Spa's Saturday qualifying session a 65% probability of rain. That is not a marginal figure — it is a legitimate expectation of wet conditions at one of the most challenging circuits on the calendar. And when Spa gets wet, the normal order of a Formula 1 session becomes considerably less predictable.

Full Spa Forecast

The current weather forecast for Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps race week:

  • Friday, July 17 (Practice): Light showers, approximately 25°C, 31% rain probability throughout the day
  • Saturday, July 18 (Qualifying): 65% rain probability, 23-25°C — the weekend's highest-risk session for significant wet conditions
  • Sunday, July 19 (Race): Rain probability drops to approximately 20%; sunshine expected to break through during the race window, 23°C

Winds expected from the north-west, creating a tailwind down the Kemmel Straight and headwind into the Bus-Stop chicane.

Who Benefits, Who Is at Risk

Lewis Hamilton has won the Belgian Grand Prix six times — more than any other active driver. Several of those victories came in mixed or changing weather conditions, where Hamilton's ability to read the track and manage tire temperature has historically been a significant edge. A wet Saturday qualifying at a circuit he knows better than most gives Hamilton a genuine opportunity to put himself in a stronger race position than his championship standing might suggest.

Championship leader Kimi Antonelli presents the opposite case. In 2025, his first Belgian Grand Prix in Formula 1, Antonelli was eliminated in the first segment of Sprint Qualifying, finished P17 in the Sprint Race, was knocked out in Q1 of the main qualifying session, and came home P16 in the Grand Prix. Spa exposed weaknesses in his setup and tire management that the team has worked on since. Whether those corrections hold under Saturday's forecast conditions is one of the defining questions of the weekend.

What's Next

Friday practice will give teams their first read on the circuit in conditions that may range from damp to fully wet. The strategic choices made in free practice — particularly around understanding the limits of each compound in wet conditions — will directly inform Saturday's qualifying approach.

For all three championship contenders, a scrambled Saturday grid creates an element of Sunday unpredictability that no amount of simulation can fully prepare for. In the Ardennes, the weather always has the final word.
