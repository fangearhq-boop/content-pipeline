Belgian GP Race Week Preview: Schedule, Championship Stakes, and Why Rain Changes Everything at Spa

By Ryan Calloway | July 14, 2026

Round 12 at Spa-Francorchamps arrives with a three-way title fight, 65% rain probability for qualifying, and everything still to play for before the summer break.

The Belgian Grand Prix is here. The Ardennes hills, the long fast straights, the sweeping left-right of Eau Rouge-Raidillon — Spa-Francorchamps is ready for Round 12 of the 2026 Formula 1 World Championship, and the championship picture has never been more compelling heading into a race week.

Here is everything you need to know going into the weekend.

Full Race Weekend Schedule

Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps hosts a standard race format for 2026 — two practice sessions on Friday, qualifying on Saturday, and the Grand Prix on Sunday.

  • FP1/FP2 — Friday, July 17 (FP1 approximately 7:30 AM ET)
  • Qualifying — Saturday, July 18 (approximately 10:00 AM ET / 16:00 CEST)
  • Race — Sunday, July 19 (approximately 9:00 AM ET / 15:00 CEST)

The circuit is 7.004 kilometers long, and the race runs to 44 laps.

Championship Standings Entering Belgium

Kimi Antonelli leads the World Drivers' Championship with 179 points after his teammate George Russell cut the gap at Silverstone. The standings going into Belgium:

  • No. 1 — Kimi Antonelli (Mercedes): 179 points
  • No. 2 — George Russell (Mercedes): 154 points
  • No. 3 — Lewis Hamilton (Ferrari): 147 points
  • No. 4 — Charles Leclerc (Ferrari): approximately 108 points

Hamilton is just 32 points behind Antonelli — within reach of a single dramatic weekend. In the constructors' championship, Mercedes leads Ferrari 333 to 255.

Belgium and Hungary are the last two rounds before the summer break, which adds urgency to every session. Every point earned or lost between now and the Hungarian GP will define the psychological state of the championship battle going into the back half of the season.

The Weather Factor

Spa's Ardennes microclimate is famously unpredictable, and the current forecast suggests this weekend will be no different. Saturday qualifying has a 65% probability of rain — a meaningful figure that could scramble the starting grid significantly. Friday practice also has showers in the forecast, at around 31% probability. Sunday's race looks slightly drier, with rain probability dropping to around 20% and some sunshine expected during the race window.

For Lewis Hamilton, the wet forecast is potentially welcome news. He has six career Belgian GP victories, several in mixed or changeable conditions, and arrives in better form than at any point in his Ferrari tenure. For Kimi Antonelli, who finished P16 at Spa in 2025 after qualifying problems in both the Sprint and the main session, a wet Saturday qualifying represents an additional variable to manage.

What's Next

Free practice begins Friday with teams trying to understand the balance of their cars in potentially wet conditions. Saturday's qualifying will be the key session — a scrambled grid from wet conditions could set up a dramatic race on Sunday. Beyond the individual race result, Belgium is the penultimate chance before the summer break for any of the three championship contenders to reshape the standings on their terms.
