Verstappen's Exit Clause Is Active — 13 Days Remain Before the Formal Deadline

By Marcus Cole | July 14, 2026

The math that Formula 1 has been dreading is now complete. Max Verstappen cannot reach the top two before the summer break, his exit clause is triggered, and the deadline falls the day after the Hungarian Grand Prix.

The numbers have been run. The calculations are complete. The conversation that Formula 1 has been building toward for months has arrived.

Max Verstappen cannot mathematically finish inside the top two of the 2026 Drivers' Championship before the summer break. With George Russell sitting P2 on 154 points and Verstappen P7 on 76, the maximum available across the Belgian and Hungarian Grands Prix is 50 points — not enough to close a 78-point gap. The exit clause embedded in Verstappen's Red Bull contract through 2028 has been mathematically triggered.

The formal activation deadline is understood to be the day after the Hungarian Grand Prix — approximately July 27 or 28. The clock is ticking. There are roughly 13 days left.

What the Clause Means

Verstappen's contract includes a performance-based exit clause that allows him to leave Red Bull if he falls outside a defined window relative to the championship's top two positions. The clause was negotiated during a period when Red Bull was the dominant force in the sport; it was designed as a theoretical safeguard, not a practical concern.

The 2026 season changed that. Two consecutive DNFs at tracks where Verstappen had previously excelled deepened the points deficit. His manager Raymond Vermeulen has confirmed the clause exists. Multiple Formula 1 insiders describe it as "impossible for Red Bull to block" once the mathematical conditions are met.

What the clause does not do is make the decision for Verstappen. He still must choose whether to activate it, and if he does, he must have a destination.

The McLaren Question

Multiple sources, including PlanetF1 and Motorsport Week, have described Verstappen-McLaren negotiations as being in "the closing stages." Zak Brown, McLaren's CEO, denied publicly that any talks are occurring. Both things cannot be simultaneously true.

The complication: Oscar Piastri is contracted with McLaren through 2028, and any Verstappen arrival would presumably require Piastri's departure or a delayed entry. The financial and contractual gymnastics of a Verstappen-McLaren deal — even before considering the sporting implications — are significant.

Retirement has also been floated as a genuine possibility. Verstappen's comments about his current driving environment being "not mentally doable" in its current state have been widely cited as a signal that his commitment to remaining in Formula 1 is not unconditional.

What's Next

The Belgian Grand Prix and Hungarian Grand Prix are Verstappen's last two race weekends before the summer break closes the activation window. Whether he uses the Belgian paddock to signal his intentions — or stays deliberately opaque until the deadline — will tell us something about how close a decision actually is.

The formal deadline arrives on July 27 or 28. After that, the clause either has been activated or the opportunity passes.
