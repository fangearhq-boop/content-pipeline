Verstappen's Trusted Engineer Lambiase to Leave Red Bull for McLaren in 2028 — Is Max Next?

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | April 23, 2026

In the most significant paddock personnel story of the 2026 season, Gianpiero Lambiase — Max Verstappen's race engineer for all four of the Dutchman's world championships — will leave Red Bull Racing at the end of 2027 to join McLaren as Chief Racing Officer in 2028.

The announcement sent shockwaves through the F1 community. Lambiase and Verstappen have worked together since 2016, building one of the most successful driver-engineer partnerships in modern Formula 1 history. Their communication — honest, direct, sometimes blunt — became the soundtrack of Red Bull's dominant championship runs.

McLaren's Systematic Raid Continues

Lambiase's arrival at McLaren is not a random event. It is the latest chapter in a systematic, multi-year effort by the Woking team to acquire Red Bull's best people. Rob Marshall, the aerodynamicist credited with much of Red Bull's championship-winning car design, joined McLaren as Technical Director in 2024. Will Courtenay began as Sporting Director in 2026. Now Lambiase for 2028.

McLaren is building a technical operation that looks remarkably like the one Red Bull used to dominate the sport. The question the paddock keeps asking is whether this is the team being built around someone — and if so, who.

Verstappen's Reaction

When Lambiase informed Verstappen of the McLaren offer, the four-time champion's response was characteristically direct. "You'd be stupid not to take it," Verstappen told his engineer.

The pragmatism is vintage Verstappen. But multiple analysts have noted that Lambiase's departure accelerates questions about Verstappen's own future. His Red Bull contract runs through 2028 — but so did Lambiase's. The inner circle of people Verstappen trusts most in motorsport is shrinking at Red Bull, while growing at McLaren.

Some observers have gone further, suggesting that Lambiase would not make this move without some knowledge of Verstappen's longer-term intentions. Red Bull and McLaren have declined to comment on that theory.

What's Next

Lambiase remains at Red Bull through 2027, and will work with Verstappen for at least one more full season. Laurent Mekies and the Red Bull leadership now face two parallel challenges: fixing the RB22's pace deficit ahead of Miami, and planning for a 2028 that will likely look very different from anything Red Bull has built before. Meanwhile, McLaren's ascent — on the technical side and now on the personnel side — is accelerating. The next two seasons in Formula 1 could represent the most significant power shift the paddock has seen in a decade.
