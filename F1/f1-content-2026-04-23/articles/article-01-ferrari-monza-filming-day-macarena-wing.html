Ferrari Tests Macarena Wing at Monza — Hamilton and Leclerc Gather Crucial Pre-Miami Data

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | April 23, 2026

Ferrari didn't wait for Miami to put their upgrade package through its paces. On Wednesday at Autodromo Nazionale Monza, the Scuderia used their April filming day to give the "Macarena" rear wing and a revised floor their first meaningful mileage since pre-season testing — with both Charles Leclerc and Lewis Hamilton taking turns behind the wheel of the SF-26.

Leclerc drove in the morning session, completing approximately 19 laps for just over 100 kilometres and posting a best lap of 1:27.138. Hamilton followed in the afternoon. The standard regulations allow 200 kilometres per car for filming days, split evenly between the two drivers.

The Macarena Wing Explained

The "Macarena" wing — a name given to it by team principal Fred Vasseur — features an upper flap that rotates completely upside down under specific aerodynamic conditions to generate downforce in a novel way. Ferrari first revealed the concept at Bahrain pre-season testing and briefly ran it at the Chinese Grand Prix before the team found rear instability issues during braking while turning. A revised version addresses those handling concerns.

Alongside the wing, Ferrari also tested a revised floor developed for the Sakhir circuits but held back pending further data. Both parts are earmarked for official debut at the Miami Grand Prix on May 3.

The Stakes at Miami

Ferrari arrive at Miami 45 points behind Mercedes in the constructors' standings (90 vs 135). While the Scuderia has been on the podium in all three races of the 2026 season — including Hamilton's P3 in China — they have yet to win. Vasseur has described the Miami upgrade package as "a package and a half," signaling genuine ambition for what these parts could unlock.

If the Macarena wing delivers its expected downforce and balance improvements, Ferrari could challenge Mercedes for race pace for the first time in 2026. The Monza filming day was less about laptimes and more about data collection — engineers observing real-world aerodynamic behavior under track conditions before the clock starts counting at Miami.

What's Next

The Ferrari SF-26 heads to Miami with both Hamilton and Leclerc having driven the updated package. Official practice begins May 1 at the Miami International Autodrome. The Miami Grand Prix is a Sprint weekend — extra competitive laps that give Ferrari more chances to evaluate the new components in race conditions. If the Macarena wing performs as hoped, expect Ferrari's championship deficit to narrow considerably before June.
