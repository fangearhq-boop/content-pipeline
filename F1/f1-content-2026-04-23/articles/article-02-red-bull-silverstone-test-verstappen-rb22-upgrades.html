Red Bull Calls Miami a "Second Season Launch" After Verstappen Tests Heavily Upgraded RB22 at Silverstone

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | April 23, 2026

Red Bull Racing's message heading into Miami is unmistakable. Team principal Laurent Mekies has described the upcoming Grand Prix as "a bit like a second season launch" for the Milton Keynes outfit — and on Wednesday at Silverstone, they backed those words with action.

Max Verstappen drove a significantly upgraded RB22 during Red Bull's second filming day of the 2026 season, putting new parts through their paces at the British circuit. He was the sole driver. The session marked the first time the revamped package turned laps in anger.

What Changed on the RB22

The car that appeared at Silverstone featured a new floor, completely redesigned sidepods with a distinctive acute kink partway through their profile, and updated front and rear aerodynamic surfaces. The rear wing in particular showed a different pivot point for its moving elements — a potential response to the handling characteristics that have plagued Red Bull in the first three races of the season.

Red Bull are acutely aware that filming day restrictions cap the session at 200 kilometres. The mileage was used purposefully: to gather performance data on whether the aerodynamic changes address the fundamental issues that have left Verstappen off the pace of Mercedes.

A Struggling Season by Red Bull's Standards

The numbers tell the story bluntly. Red Bull sit sixth in the constructors' championship with just 16 points after three races. In comparison, Mercedes leads with 135. Verstappen's best result in 2026 was fifth place at the Japanese Grand Prix — the only race where he scored points. The defending champions have looked adrift in the new aerodynamic and power unit regulations.

Calling Miami a "second season launch" is a frank acknowledgment from Mekies that the first three rounds were effectively written off. What happens at the Miami International Autodrome from May 1-3 will be the first real test of whether Red Bull's upgrade programme can claw back the gap to Mercedes.

What's Next

The revamped RB22 travels to Miami carrying the weight of Red Bull's hopes and Verstappen's championship aspirations. If the upgrades work, the four-time champion could be in the fight at the front again for the first time this year. If they don't, the questions about Verstappen's future at the team — already swirling with the Gianpiero Lambiase departure — will only grow louder. The Miami GP race day is May 3. The reset starts there.
