Miami GP 10 Days Out: Upgrades, New Rules, and a Sprint Weekend That Could Reshape the 2026 F1 Season

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | April 23, 2026

The Formula 1 Crypto.com Miami Grand Prix is ten days away, and the build-up has already been more eventful than any normal pre-race week. Every major team has used the five-week break productively. The FIA's regulatory tweaks are confirmed and ready. And the championship, which began 2026 with Mercedes dominating unopposed, is about to be tested in a way it has not been yet.

Upgrades Across the Field

Ferrari lead the development offensive. Their "Macarena" rear wing and revised floor — tested at Monza's filming day on Wednesday with both Charles Leclerc and Lewis Hamilton — will debut officially in Miami. Team principal Fred Vasseur has called the package "a package and a half." Ferrari sit 45 points behind Mercedes (90 vs 135) and need these upgrades to perform.

Red Bull are also arriving with a substantially revised RB22. Max Verstappen drove a new floor, redesigned sidepods and updated wings at Silverstone on Wednesday in the team's second filming day of the season. Laurent Mekies has called Miami "a bit like a second season launch" — an admission that the first three rounds were not what Red Bull expected from the defending constructors' champions.

McLaren, currently third in the constructors' standings with 56 points, are also due to introduce their first aero upgrades of the year. Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri have shown consistent pace; the upgrades are intended to give them a genuine title fight platform.

New FIA Rules Take Effect

Miami marks the first race under the FIA's revised 2026 regulations. The key changes: super-clipping power limits raised from 250 to 350 kilowatts (meaning drivers can deploy more electrical energy more freely), recharge limits reduced from 8 to 7 megajoules per lap in qualifying, MGU-K deployment of 250 kW allowed outside previously restricted zones, and a new start detection system being trialled for the first time. The changes are designed to reduce the amount of lifting and coasting drivers must do and make for more "flat-out driving" — a direct response to driver feedback, particularly from Verstappen, who has been vocal about the 2026 regulations.

What's Next

The Miami International Autodrome hosts a Sprint format weekend. Practice 1 runs May 1 at 4:30 PM ET, followed by Sprint Qualifying at 8:30 PM ET. The Sprint Race is Saturday May 2 at 4 PM ET. Qualifying follows at 8 PM ET. The Grand Prix itself runs Sunday May 3 at 4 PM ET — 57 laps of the 5.41-kilometre circuit. With every team bringing upgrades, new rules in force and the championship on the line, Miami may well be the most significant race of the 2026 season so far.
