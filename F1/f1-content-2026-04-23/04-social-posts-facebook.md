### STORY 1: Ferrari Monza Filming Day — Macarena Wing on Track

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 8:00 AM ET

Ferrari didn't wait until Miami to show their cards. On Wednesday at Monza's iconic Autodromo Nazionale — the Temple of Speed — the Scuderia put their upcoming upgrade package on track for the first time.

Charles Leclerc drove in the morning session, completing approximately 19 laps and covering just over 100 kilometres, setting a best lap of 1:27.138. Lewis Hamilton took the wheel in the afternoon. Together they gave Ferrari's engineers their first real on-track data on the Macarena rear wing and a revised floor — both set to officially debut at the Miami Grand Prix on May 3.

The "Macarena" wing, named by team principal Fred Vasseur, features an upper flap that rotates completely upside down to generate downforce in a novel way. Ferrari has been developing the concept since pre-season testing in Bahrain. The revised floor was built for the Sakhir circuits but held back until the team could gather more data.

Vasseur has called the Miami upgrade package "a package and a half" — language that signals genuine ambition. Ferrari sit 45 points behind Mercedes in the constructors' standings. They need Miami to work.

### STORY 2: Red Bull Silverstone Test — The Miami Reset Begins

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 10:00 AM ET

Red Bull Racing is not giving up on 2026. Not even close.

On Wednesday, the Milton Keynes team took a significantly upgraded RB22 to Silverstone for a filming day — only their second of the season — with Max Verstappen as the sole driver. The car that showed up at the circuit was virtually unrecognizable in key areas. Engineers had fitted a new floor, completely redesigned sidepods featuring a distinctive acute kink partway through, and updated front and rear wings. The rear wing in particular showed a different pivot point for its moving elements.

Team principal Laurent Mekies chose striking language to describe what's coming at Miami: "It's a bit like a second season launch."

That's an admission. Red Bull is sixth in the constructors' championship with 16 points after three races. The team that won the last three championships has been outpaced by Mercedes at every event this year. Verstappen finished fifth in Japan — the only race where he scored points.

Mekies clearly believes the fundamentals of the RB22 can be fixed, and that Miami represents a genuine reset. If the upgrades work, the championship battle becomes considerably more interesting.

### STORY 3: Lambiase Bombshell — McLaren Signs Verstappen's Most Trusted Ally

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 12:00 PM ET

The paddock is still processing this one. Gianpiero Lambiase — the man who has guided Max Verstappen to four consecutive Formula 1 world championships since 2016 — is leaving Red Bull Racing. He'll join McLaren as Chief Racing Officer in 2028.

Lambiase began his motorsport career as a data engineer at Jordan in 2005, moved to Force India and became a race engineer, then joined Red Bull in 2015. When Verstappen arrived from Toro Rosso in 2016, Lambiase was his engineer. The relationship that followed produced 2021, 2022, 2023 and 2024 titles — four in a row.

The partnership was famous for its honest, sometimes blunt communication. Verstappen trusted Lambiase completely.

So when Lambiase brought the McLaren offer to Verstappen, what did the four-time champion say? "You'd be stupid not to take it."

McLaren's systematic recruitment of Red Bull's best people continues: Rob Marshall joined as Technical Director in 2024; Will Courtenay began as Sporting Director in 2026; and now Lambiase for 2028. Multiple analysts and insiders are now openly wondering whether Verstappen himself could follow his inner circle to a rival team before his Red Bull contract expires.

Red Bull team boss Laurent Mekies has much more than an engineering gap to fill.

### STORY 4: Antonelli 9 Points Clear — The Championship Battle Is Real

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 2:00 PM ET

The numbers are simple: Kimi Antonelli leads the 2026 F1 Drivers' Championship with 72 points. His teammate George Russell trails by 9 with 63. The same team has won all three races so far — Russell in Australia, Antonelli in China and Japan.

And yet the doubters persist.

Former champion David Coulthard has said he is "not confident" about Antonelli's title chances. The logic is understandable — Russell is more experienced, more calculated, and has previously shown he can perform when it matters most. The concern is that Antonelli's hot start might cool as the season becomes a war of attrition.

Antonelli's response to all of it? "Unfazed." His word. His posture. His entire approach to a season where he's supposed to be the underdog.

Toto Wolff is managing carefully — he has said publicly that he wants to "best handle" the intra-team battle, which suggests both drivers are being given equal treatment for now. Russell, for his part, says he is "not thinking about" Antonelli or the championship. Whether that's genuine focus or careful message control is for everyone to decide.

At 19 years old, Antonelli is the youngest driver ever to lead the F1 standings. Miami will tell us whether he's the real thing or a fast starter.

### STORY 5: Miami GP — 10 Days Out and Every Team Brings Upgrades

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 5:00 PM ET

Ten days from now, the 2026 Formula 1 season resumes in Miami — and for the first time all year, the championship picture could look very different after the chequered flag drops.

Ferrari arrive with the most significant upgrade Ferrari has brought in years: the Macarena rear wing (first tested at Monza this Wednesday) and a revised floor. Team principal Fred Vasseur has described the package as "a package and a half."

Red Bull arrive with a revamped RB22, featuring new floor, sidepods and wings that Max Verstappen drove at Silverstone on Wednesday. Team boss Laurent Mekies has called Miami "a second season launch" for the team that sits sixth in the constructors' championship.

McLaren arrive with new aero upgrades that were first expected to arrive earlier in the season. Lando Norris and Oscar Piastri have shown flashes of pace — now they need the hardware to match.

And for the first time, the FIA's new regulatory tweaks will be in force: super-clipping raised from 250 to 350 kW, recharge limits adjusted from 8 to 7 MJ, and a new start detection system being trialled. The changes are designed to reduce the amount drivers need to "lift and coast" and allow more flat-out racing.

Miami is a Sprint weekend. That means double the action across May 1-3. The race itself runs Sunday May 3 at 4 PM ET.

If you've been waiting for 2026 to get interesting — this is the weekend.
