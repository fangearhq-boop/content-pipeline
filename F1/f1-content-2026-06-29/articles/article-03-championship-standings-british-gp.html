2026 F1 Championship Standings: Three Drivers, Forty-Six Points, Nine Races Left

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap

June 29, 2026

The 2026 Formula 1 drivers' championship is one of the tightest three-way title fights the sport has produced in years — and it is about to enter its most concentrated stretch. Three drivers represent the realistic title contenders: Kimi Antonelli leads, George Russell is 40 points back, and Lewis Hamilton trails by 46. All three drive for Mercedes. And this week, all three head to Silverstone for the British Grand Prix.

After eight rounds and a dominant Austrian GP win for Russell, the standings reflect a season-long battle between teammates operating within the same supremely competitive car — with the internal competition at Mercedes proving as compelling as the fight against Ferrari and Red Bull.

The Drivers' Championship in Full

Kimi Antonelli holds the lead with 171 points — a performance that, for a driver in only his second full Formula 1 season, has been extraordinary. Antonelli's consistency and pace across circuits of all types have marked him as a genuine title contender rather than a future prospect. He is racing in the present tense.

George Russell at 131 points is the most dangerous threat to Antonelli's lead. His Austrian GP victory — lights-to-flag from pole — was his most convincing win of the season. It established a pattern: when Russell is at full speed and the car is at its best, nobody catches him. The question over the final nine rounds is whether he can replicate that performance consistently enough to overcome a 40-point deficit.

Hamilton's 125 points represents a different kind of title campaign. Six points behind Russell and 46 behind Antonelli, Hamilton requires results to go his way — but Formula 1 history is full of championships decided in the final races, and Hamilton at Silverstone in front of the home crowd is among the sport's most potent performance combinations. He has won the British Grand Prix eight times.

The Constructors' Championship

The constructors' title looks far less competitive. Mercedes leads with 302 points, Ferrari sits at 204 — a gap of 98 points across nine remaining rounds. With their current pace advantage, Mercedes is the strong favorite to claim the constructors' championship for the third time in four years.

McLaren is around 159 points, Red Bull approximately 115. The Verstappen situation (his performance clause deadline at Hungary on July 26) is the main subplot at Red Bull; McLaren remains in the midfield hunt but is unlikely to challenge for the title without a dramatic swing in pace.

British GP This Week: Sprint Format

The British Grand Prix at Silverstone — Round 9, July 3-5 — is a sprint weekend, only the second of the 2026 season. The format means championship points are distributed across two race events rather than one, effectively amplifying the consequences of every session.

The schedule runs as follows: Friday, July 3 — Free Practice 1 at 12:30 BST, Sprint Qualifying at 16:30 BST. Saturday, July 4 — Sprint Race at 12:00 BST, Grand Prix Qualifying at 16:00 BST. Sunday, July 5 — Grand Prix at 15:00 BST.

Both Russell and Antonelli arrive at Silverstone carrying momentum — Russell from his Austrian victory, Antonelli from maintaining a disciplined points lead through a challenging Austrian weekend that saw him finish third behind two competitors at peak performance. Hamilton arrives at his home circuit with the Silverstone crowd's full support and a Ferrari that struggled in Austria but will hope the specific demands of the high-speed British circuit suit it better.

What's Next

The British GP sprint format compresses the narrative into fewer days than a standard race weekend. By Sunday evening, July 5, the championship picture could look considerably different — either Antonelli extending to a more comfortable lead or Russell closing to within striking distance with a run of results that redefines the title conversation entirely.

The final three rounds of 2026 — Abu Dhabi, Japan, and Brazil — have a history of dramatic championship conclusions. But Silverstone this week is where the second half of this fight truly begins. Three drivers. Forty-six points. Nine races. It is very much on.
