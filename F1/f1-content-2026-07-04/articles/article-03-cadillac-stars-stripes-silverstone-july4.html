Stars, Stripes, and Silverstone: Cadillac Honors America's 250th at F1's Birthplace

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 4, 2026

July 4, 2026. America turns 250. And the American Formula 1 team is racing at the most historically significant circuit in the world.

Cadillac F1 is running a special stars-and-stripes patriotic livery at the 2026 British Grand Prix at Silverstone, honoring the United States' 250th Independence Day anniversary. The red, white, and blue color scheme gives the team's cars a dramatic visual presence on a circuit steeped in its own extraordinary history.

The Birthplace of Formula 1

Silverstone is not just another racing venue. On May 13, 1950, this circuit in Northamptonshire, England, hosted the very first Formula 1 World Championship race in history. The sport that now reaches hundreds of millions of fans on every continent was born on this specific patch of tarmac, 76 years ago.

For Cadillac to celebrate American independence at the literal birthplace of Formula 1 is the kind of coincidence that makes for great sports storytelling. The sport's oldest landmark playing host to America's newest team on America's most celebrated holiday.

A Challenging First Season

The patriotic livery is one of the highlights of what has been a difficult debut campaign for Cadillac. After eight races in 2026, the team has yet to score a World Championship point. The competition at the front of the field — particularly the dominant Kimi Antonelli in the Mercedes — sets a high bar for newcomers, and Cadillac is still closing the performance gap.

But July 4 is not a day for standings tables. It is a day for flags, fireworks, and the kind of symbolic moments that fans remember long after the race results fade.

What's Next

Cadillac will be on track Saturday for both the sprint race at noon BST and the main GP qualifying session at 4 PM BST. The British Grand Prix runs Sunday at 3 PM BST (10 AM ET). The team will be hoping that a patriotic July 4 performance gives them something to celebrate — on the circuit where Formula 1 was born.
