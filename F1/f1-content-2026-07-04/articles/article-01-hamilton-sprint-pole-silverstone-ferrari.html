Hamilton Takes Sprint Pole at Silverstone in Ferrari — "An Amazing Surprise" at His Home Race

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 4, 2026

Lewis Hamilton has spent his entire Formula 1 career making Silverstone feel like his living room. On Friday, he did it again — only this time wearing red.

The seven-time world champion secured sprint pole position for the 2026 British Grand Prix, posting a lap of 1:28.376 to beat championship leader Kimi Antonelli by just 0.011 seconds. Max Verstappen was third, 0.343 seconds off the pace.

Hamilton called the result "an amazing surprise" — a quote that raised eyebrows, given that he led free practice and then topped all three segments of sprint qualifying without interruption.

Ferrari at the Front at Silverstone

This is Hamilton's first British Grand Prix as a Ferrari driver, after his headline-making move from Mercedes ahead of the 2026 season. The circuit has been a personal stronghold throughout his career — he has won at Silverstone nine times across his championship years.

Teammate Charles Leclerc qualified fourth, giving Ferrari an impressive front-of-grid representation. The Scuderia's form at the circuit the British crowd calls home will fuel enormous expectations ahead of both Saturday's sprint and Sunday's main race.

Sprint Grid Top 10

The sprint race will start from this order:

  1. Lewis Hamilton (Ferrari) — 1:28.376
  2. Kimi Antonelli (Mercedes) — +0.011s
  3. Max Verstappen (Red Bull) — +0.343s
  4. Charles Leclerc (Ferrari)
  5. George Russell (Mercedes)
  6. Lando Norris (McLaren)
  7. Oscar Piastri (McLaren)
  8. Isack Hadjar (Red Bull)
  9. Liam Lawson (Racing Bulls)
  10. Arvid Lindblad (Racing Bulls)

Russell had a difficult session and qualified fifth — a significant gap to championship-leading teammate Antonelli, who has been the benchmark all season.

What's Next

The sprint race runs Saturday at noon BST (7 AM ET) over 17 laps. Main GP qualifying follows at 4 PM BST (11 AM ET), setting the grid for Sunday's 52-lap British Grand Prix at 3 PM BST (10 AM ET). In front of more than 100,000 fans at Silverstone, Hamilton will attempt to convert his sprint pole into a win at home.
