Antonelli Wins Third Straight Miami GP as Leclerc's Final-Lap Disaster Drops Ferrari to P8

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 10, 2026

Kimi Antonelli extended his perfect 2026 Formula 1 record on May 3, winning the Miami Grand Prix to claim his third consecutive victory from his third consecutive pole position. The 19-year-old Mercedes driver crossed the finish line 3.264 seconds ahead of Lando Norris, with Oscar Piastri completing the podium for a McLaren 2-3 finish behind the dominant Mercedes car.

The result cements Antonelli's position as the runaway early-season favourite for the 2026 world championship. He now leads the standings with 100 points — 20 ahead of team-mate George Russell, and 41 clear of Charles Leclerc, who endured one of the most painful race endings of his career in Miami.

Leclerc's Nightmare Final Lap

Charles Leclerc appeared headed for a solid points haul when disaster struck on the final lap. Running inside the top six, the Ferrari driver spun at Turn 3 after losing third position to Piastri on the penultimate lap, damaging his SF-26 and forcing him to cut chicanes to reach the finish line.

The stewards found him guilty of gaining a lasting advantage by leaving the track and issued a 20-second time penalty, dropping Leclerc from P6 on the road to P8 in the final classification — behind team-mate Lewis Hamilton (P6) and Alpine's Franco Colapinto (P7). A race that could have yielded 10 or 12 points ended with just 4. "It's all on me," Leclerc admitted after the race. Ferrari has called for an investigation into the performance drop that contributed to the incident.

History in the Making for Antonelli

Formula 1's official records confirm that no driver had previously converted their first three pole positions of a season into race victories — a feat Antonelli has now achieved at just 19 years old. A chaotic opening corner saw him briefly lose ground to Verstappen and Leclerc, but an undercut pit strategy put him back in front, and he managed pressure from Norris expertly in the closing laps.

George Russell was fourth, Verstappen fifth for Red Bull, and Hamilton sixth for Ferrari. McLaren's double podium — Norris second, Piastri third — confirmed that their extensive Miami upgrade package is working and that the gap to Mercedes is closing.

What's Next

The Formula 1 season resumes at the Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve in Montreal, Canada on May 22-24 in a sprint format weekend. Antonelli will arrive as the red-hot championship leader — but McLaren's improved form and a motivated Norris mean Montreal could produce F1's most competitive weekend of 2026 yet.
