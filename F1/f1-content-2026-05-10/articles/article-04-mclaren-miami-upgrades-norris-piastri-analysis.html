McLaren's Seven-Part Miami Upgrade Has Reawakened a Championship Contender

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 10, 2026

When Andrea Stella described McLaren's Miami upgrade package as the most significant technical step his team had taken in 2026, he wasn't exaggerating. Seven changes to the MCL40 — covering the front wing, nose cone, sidepods, engine cover, floor, and rear wing — transformed the car's performance level at a single race weekend, producing a Sprint win for Lando Norris and a race-day podium double of Norris P2 and Oscar Piastri P3.

The results didn't just reflect better pace — they reflected a fundamentally more driveable car. Stella's stated goal for the upgrade was twofold: improve outright lap time, and widen the MCL40's operating window so that Norris and Piastri can extract consistent performance across different track conditions and tyre compounds. Both objectives appear to have been met.

Norris and Piastri React

Lando Norris, characteristically careful with his optimism, said the team "needs a larger sample size" before drawing firm conclusions — but in the same breath acknowledged feeling "silly" if he didn't feel confident about the future given how much improved the car felt. His Sprint win — McLaren's first race victory in the format this season — was achieved from pole position, showing the upgrade's one-lap pace is genuine.

Oscar Piastri was more direct: the upgrades "exceeded expectations," and he expressed specific optimism about the Canada Grand Prix. McLaren is expected to bring additional development to Montreal, suggesting this Miami package is a platform rather than a ceiling.

The Race That Got Away

Stella's one frustration was that "execution and optimisation" — not pace — kept McLaren from a potential race win. The team's strategy and pit stop execution in the Miami Grand Prix left room for improvement, with Antonelli successfully undercutting them through his pit stop strategy to maintain his lead. That's a fixable problem, and one that gives McLaren legitimate reason to believe Canada could produce a different result.

What's Next

McLaren heads to the Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve in Montreal (May 22-24 sprint weekend) with momentum and further upgrades in the pipeline. A track that rewards car balance — rather than raw Mercedes-level aerodynamic efficiency — could play directly into the improved MCL40's strengths. If McLaren can combine their new pace with sharper race-day execution, the 2026 championship story could get significantly more interesting by the end of May.
