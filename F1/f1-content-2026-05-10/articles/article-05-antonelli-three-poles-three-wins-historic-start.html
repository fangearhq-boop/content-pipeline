Kimi Antonelli: Three Poles, Three Wins, 100 Points — The Most Dominant F1 Start Since the Records Began

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | May 10, 2026

Consider the numbers: three pole positions taken. Three race victories achieved. 100 championship points earned. A 20-point lead over the nearest challenger — his own team-mate. All of this accomplished before Kimi Antonelli's 20th birthday.

Formula 1's own records confirm what every fan watching in 2026 already suspects: Antonelli has done something nobody in the sport's history had done before. No driver had ever converted their first three pole positions of a season into race victories in Formula 1. Antonelli has done it in back-to-back-to-back fashion across Bahrain, China, and Miami.

What Makes His Performances Special

The pole positions are impressive enough — the fastest single lap in qualifying at three different circuit types (high-speed Bahrain, technical Shanghai, the hybrid Miami layout). But the race victories are what truly set Antonelli apart. He has won from the front, won after losing positions at the start, and won while managing late-race pressure from improving rivals like Lando Norris.

In Miami specifically, Antonelli dropped to third on the opening lap in a three-way battle, rebuilt his lead through strategy, then resisted Norris's improved McLaren over the final laps to claim victory. That's not a driver benefiting from a dominant car alone — that's a racer with composure under pressure that typically takes years to develop.

Mercedes' Historic Season

Behind Antonelli's individual brilliance is a Mercedes team at the peak of its technical powers. The Silver Arrows lead the constructors' championship with 180 points — 68 ahead of Ferrari in second. George Russell, himself a race winner at this level, has been outqualified and out-raced every weekend but still sits second in the drivers' standings at 80 points.

The 2027 regulations — which will shift the power unit balance from the current 50/50 ICE/ERS split to a 60/40 ICE/ERS distribution, reducing the "super clipping" phenomenon that has plagued the new cars — have been agreed by all stakeholders, though they still require a World Motor Sport Council rubber stamp. Until then, the 2026 formula that produced this dominance continues unchanged.

What's Next

The Canadian Grand Prix in Montreal (May 22-24) is next. Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve is a true driver's circuit, one where precision and bravery matter as much as machinery. Antonelli has an opportunity to extend his advantage further and begin writing an opening chapter to his 2026 career that the sport has never seen before. For now, the 2026 championship belongs to him — and he shows no sign of giving it back.
