Verstappen's Exit Clause is About to Be Triggered — What Happens Next at Red Bull?

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | May 10, 2026

When Max Verstappen negotiated a performance-linked exit clause into his Red Bull contract ahead of the 2026 season, it seemed like a reasonable precaution against the uncertainty of new regulations. Six months later, with the four-time world champion sitting 7th in the championship and 74 points behind leader Kimi Antonelli, that clause looks set to be triggered — and the implications for Formula 1 are enormous.

The clause gives Verstappen the right to terminate his contract if he is not within the top three in the drivers' standings at the summer break, following the Hungarian Grand Prix on July 24-26. He then has until October to formally notify Red Bull of his intention to leave. At 26 points — with Mercedes, McLaren, Ferrari, and even Alpine fielding more competitive machinery — achieving top-three by Hungary is a mathematical long shot that would require both a Red Bull revival and misfortune for multiple rivals.

A Season of Frustrations

Verstappen's dissatisfaction has been no secret. Following the Japanese Grand Prix earlier in 2026, he reportedly threatened to retire from the sport entirely, citing the difficult drivability of cars under the new power unit regulations. The 2026 rules shifted the ICE/ERS power split and introduced new aerodynamic philosophies that have left Red Bull struggling relative to the teams that developed their cars more effectively for the new formula.

Red Bull teammate Isack Hadjar has similarly struggled to make inroads, confirming that the issue is the car rather than the driver. The team reportedly halved its weight deficit at Miami, showing signs of progress — but that improvement came too late to affect the championship picture materially.

What Comes Next?

If Verstappen invokes the exit clause, the paddock's transfer market would ignite. A four-time champion available mid-contract would attract interest from every top team — though most seats at Mercedes, McLaren, and Ferrari are currently occupied. The more realistic scenario may be a longer-term deal elsewhere starting in 2027, or a sabbatical.

Red Bull, meanwhile, would face the challenge of rebuilding around Hadjar and potentially a new signing. For a team that has defined F1 for the better part of the last decade, the 2026 season represents an unprecedented period of turbulence.

What's Next

The Canada Grand Prix in Montreal (May 22-24) offers Verstappen another opportunity to mount pressure on his rivals — but Circuit Gilles-Villeneuve has historically favoured car balance over raw power, which may not play to Red Bull's current strengths. The summer break exit window is approaching fast, and every race between now and Hungary carries enormous weight for Verstappen's future in Formula 1.
