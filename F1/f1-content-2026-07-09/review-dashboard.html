FIA Officially Confirms Mid-Season Engine Rule Change — Loophole Closed
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The FIA has officially confirmed the mid-season power-unit rule change, and the impact could reshape the second half of the championship. The change is technical at its core: compression ratio enforcement will now be measured at hot operating conditions (130°C) rather than cold ambient temperature. That shift closes what Ferrari, Honda, and Audi had publicly identified as a thermal expansion exploit — a method of running higher effective compression ratios during a race than the regulations were intended to allow. The teams alleged to have benefited most are Mercedes and Red Bull. The performance advantage that closes has been estimated at approximately 0.3 seconds per lap. That is not a marginal number. In a championship where the gap between P2 and P5 in qualifying can be measured in tenths, 0.3 seconds represents a meaningful correction. The enforcement date was originally set for August 1. The FIA moved it forward, applying the rule 6 races earlier than originally planned. That acceleration in the timeline signals how seriously the governing body took the complaints from multiple manufacturers. The same announcement confirmed a tweak to qualifying format. Details on that change are expected before the Belgian GP weekend. The rule is now confirmed. The question is how much of the competitive picture changes when Spa qualifying runs on Saturday. ### Image Post Caption The loophole is closed. FIA confirms the compression ratio rule change — effective immediately, 6 races early. The ~0.3s/lap advantage is gone.

### Long-Form Post Fernando Alonso has given himself a deadline. The two-time world champion confirmed publicly Thursday that he will use the summer break to make his decision about 2027 — whether to continue into what would be his 24th Formula 1 season or bring one of the sport's longest careers to an end. The context makes the timeline meaningful. Aston Martin arrives at the Hungarian Grand Prix (July 24-26) with what Alonso described as a "substantial" aerodynamic revamp — a package developed under Adrian Newey that brings significant weight savings alongside the aerodynamic changes.
