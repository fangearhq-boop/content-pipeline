### STORY 2: FIA Engine Rule Change Confirmed

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM ET)**

The FIA has officially confirmed the mid-season power-unit rule change, and the impact could reshape the second half of the championship.

The change is technical at its core: compression ratio enforcement will now be measured at hot operating conditions (130°C) rather than cold ambient temperature. That shift closes what Ferrari, Honda, and Audi had publicly identified as a thermal expansion exploit — a method of running higher effective compression ratios during a race than the regulations were intended to allow.

The teams alleged to have benefited most are Mercedes and Red Bull. The performance advantage that closes has been estimated at approximately 0.3 seconds per lap. That is not a marginal number. In a championship where the gap between P2 and P5 in qualifying can be measured in tenths, 0.3 seconds represents a meaningful correction.

The enforcement date was originally set for August 1. The FIA moved it forward, applying the rule 6 races earlier than originally planned. That acceleration in the timeline signals how seriously the governing body took the complaints from multiple manufacturers.

The same announcement confirmed a tweak to qualifying format. Details on that change are expected before the Belgian GP weekend.

The rule is now confirmed. The question is how much of the competitive picture changes when Spa qualifying runs on Saturday.
