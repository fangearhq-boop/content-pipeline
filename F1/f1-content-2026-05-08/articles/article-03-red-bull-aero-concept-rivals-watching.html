Red Bull's New Aero Concept Has Rivals Watching — McLaren Boss Says Direction 'Drawing Attention'

By Elena Voss | May 8, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Max Verstappen finished fifth in Miami. He sits seventh in the drivers' championship with 26 points — 74 behind leader Kimi Antonelli. Red Bull Racing has spent most of 2026 dealing with a technical package that is significantly over the minimum weight limit and has been struggling to compete at the front of the grid.

And yet McLaren's team principal is watching them nervously.

"Red Bull's new aerodynamic direction is drawing attention across the grid," said Andrea Stella, after the Miami Grand Prix. That kind of admission — from the boss of a team that finished second and third in the same race — suggests something important: even when Red Bull isn't winning, they're building toward something that the rest of the field takes seriously.

Miami Was a Meaningful Step

Red Bull arrived in Miami with a comprehensive upgrade package that addressed their most significant problem: the RB22 has been running substantially over the 768-kilogram minimum weight limit since the start of the season, a handicap that cost them roughly 0.9 seconds per lap in performance compared to where they needed to be. The Miami package halved that deficit — reducing the overage from approximately 30 kilograms to around 15.

In real terms, that means a car that was a second per lap off the pace is now something closer to half a second off. That is still a gap that keeps them from winning races right now. But the trajectory — and the direction of the aerodynamic concept — is what Stella was acknowledging. Red Bull is not iterating incrementally. They are pursuing a conceptual direction that is distinct from what Mercedes, McLaren, and Ferrari are doing, and it has drawn the attention of teams who understand that today's concept is tomorrow's winner under a new regulation cycle.

The Verstappen Question

Team principal Laurent Mekies has been characteristically direct about the stakes. Red Bull "must keep improving to keep Verstappen," Mekies said after Miami — a phrase that carries enormous weight given the exit clause embedded in the four-time world champion's contract. Verstappen's clause is tied to his championship position at the summer break: if he is outside the top three of the championship at that reference point, he would be entitled to leave unilaterally, without Red Bull receiving any compensation.

Right now, Verstappen is seventh. The summer break is several months away, but the gap to third place in the championship — currently occupied by Charles Leclerc with 59 points — is 33 points. With Canada's sprint weekend offering additional points opportunities and the RB22 improving each race, there is a path for Verstappen to climb the standings if the development curve continues. But it requires results that haven't yet come.

Why the Concept Matters More Than the Results

Formula 1 team principals do not comment publicly on rival concepts unless they mean it. Stella's admission that Red Bull's aerodynamic direction is generating discussion in the paddock is not a throwaway comment — it reflects genuine analysis of what they observed on Red Bull's car throughout the Miami weekend and what that data suggests about where the RB22 could be in six to eight months.

New regulations favor the team that identifies the right conceptual direction early. Red Bull's history under Newey — who built dominant cars at Red Bull before joining Aston Martin — was defined by pursuing bold aerodynamic solutions before competitors recognized their value. The current Red Bull technical leadership appears to be walking a similar path, and the grid is watching.

What's Next

Canada (May 22-24) is Red Bull's next opportunity to demonstrate whether the Miami upgrade was a one-race step or the beginning of a genuine resurgence. Circuit Gilles Villeneuve's low-drag, high-speed sections tend to reward aerodynamic efficiency, and Verstappen has historically been strong at Montreal.

The championship math is daunting. But in Formula 1, the team that figures out the right technical direction in a new regulation cycle eventually wins. Red Bull's rivals are paying attention — and that is the most significant compliment the team has received in 2026.
