### STORY 4: Hamilton Goes Sim-Free for Canada

**Long-Form Post:**
Lewis Hamilton is running an experiment on himself — and so far, the data supports his hypothesis.

Hamilton's best result in 2026 is a third-place finish at the Chinese Grand Prix, a race weekend where he arrived without any Ferrari simulator preparation, relying instead on instinct and his back-to-back race experience. In Miami, he went in the opposite direction — heavy simulator preparation — and finished sixth despite Ferrari's largest upgrade of the season.

The conclusion Hamilton reached is the kind of thing that would make engineers uncomfortable: the simulator might be sending him in the wrong direction. "The car feels different when you get to the track," he explained after Miami. So for the Canadian Grand Prix — May 22-24 in Montreal — Hamilton confirmed he will skip the Ferrari simulator entirely and approach the weekend fresh.

The sample size is still small. P3 without sim prep, P6 with heavy preparation. But for a driver who spent his entire career at Mercedes trusting the simulation process, this is a genuinely striking departure. Canada's sprint format offers him an immediate opportunity to see whether the approach holds up in a competitive weekend, and with Ferrari needing every point they can find to stay in the constructors' fight, Hamilton's experiment is one the entire team is watching closely.
