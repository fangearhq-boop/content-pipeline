Red Bull's Final 6kg Austria Upgrade: Mekies Manages Expectations — Again

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | June 22, 2026

The pattern is becoming familiar: Red Bull arrives at a race weekend with a significant technical upgrade, and team principal Laurent Mekies tells the world why it won't be enough to win. Austria is no different — but the stakes attached to this upgrade are unlike anything that came before it.

The RB22 started the 2026 season with a serious problem: the car was approximately 12 kilograms over the minimum weight limit of 768 kilograms mandated under the new regulations. Excess weight is a direct performance penalty in Formula 1 — it slows lap times, worsens tire management, and limits the aerodynamic balance teams can achieve.

The Diet Plan

Red Bull's 2026 technical campaign has been framed, with some dark humor, as putting the RB22 "on a diet." The Miami upgrade was the first major step — cutting the excess from 12kg to approximately 6kg, a 50% reduction that represented significant engineering progress.

The Austria package is designed to finish the job. By eliminating the final six kilograms, the RB22 would reach the minimum weight threshold, unlocking the full performance potential of a car that has shown genuine speed in certain conditions but has been hampered by the ballast penalty all season.

Mekies confirmed the ambition of the package with characteristic honesty: "On that continuous closing-the-gap trajectory that we have been onto since post-Japan, is that we continue to get closer." That's an improvement statement, not a winning statement.

Why "Not Enough" Keeps Being the Answer

Mekies has now said some version of "the upgrade alone will not be enough" twice in 2026. After Miami, it was delivered with optimism — the team was making visible progress. Ahead of Austria, the same phrase carries a different weight.

The clock is running on the Verstappen exit clause situation. If Red Bull is seventh in the constructors' standings and Verstappen is seventh in the drivers' championship heading into the summer break, the clause becomes live. An honest assessment from the team principal about the upgrade's limitations is the right communication strategy — but it also makes the internal tension within the team unavoidable.

Red Bull are currently fourth in the constructors' championship. Mercedes lead with 262 points, followed by Ferrari at 190. The gap is not one upgrade package wide — it is multiple race victories wide.

The Bigger Picture

The Austria upgrade matters for reasons beyond this single race. Getting the RB22 to minimum weight by midseason means every subsequent development step — new aerodynamic pieces, suspension upgrades, floor revisions — can be evaluated on a proper baseline. The diet plan was never just about Austria. It was about giving Red Bull a real car to develop for the second half of the season.

If Mekies is right and the package closes the gap without enabling victory, that's still progress. But in the context of Verstappen's exit clause, Verstappen's seven-race winless streak, and Red Bull's home grand prix, the pressure to finally deliver a result has never been higher.

What's Next

Practice at the Red Bull Ring begins Thursday June 26. The upgrade will be evaluated across the first two sessions, and Red Bull will have real data on whether the weight reduction has translated into the lap time improvement they've been targeting. Race day is Sunday June 28 — Red Bull's home crowd will be watching very closely.
