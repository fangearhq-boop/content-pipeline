Cadillac's "Big" Austria Upgrade: Racing for Formula 1's First American Points

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | June 22, 2026

Seven races into the 2026 Formula 1 season, one team has yet to score a single championship point. That team is Cadillac — F1's newest entrant, American-backed, and racing with a car that has genuinely impressed on single-lap pace while frustrating the team's ambitions when it matters most.

Austria is the next opportunity. And Cadillac is arriving with what they're describing as a "big upgrade package" targeting the specific weakness that has held them back: tire degradation.

The Pace Is There — Just Not the Race Craft

The Cadillac storyline in 2026 has been one of the more compelling in the midfield. Drivers Sergio Perez — making his return to the grid after a year away — and Valtteri Bottas have been quick in qualifying. At Barcelona, the most recent race, both Cadillacs were ahead of the Aston Martin pairing by over a second in the sessions.

On pure speed, Cadillac looks like a car that should be scoring points regularly. In races, it's been a different story.

Perez identified the root cause bluntly: "Whenever we have a stint longer than 15 laps, we seem to struggle a lot with a massive cliff." That cliff — the sharp drop in tire performance that arrives without warning — has been Cadillac's race-day nemesis. Teams around them have been able to manage their tires further into stints. Cadillac cannot. And in modern F1, tire management over race distance is as important as outright pace.

What the Austria Package Targets

The upgrade Cadillac is bringing to the Red Bull Ring specifically aims at the degradation problem. Whether through aerodynamic changes that reduce rear tire load, suspension geometry adjustments, or other modifications to the car's mechanical behavior, the team believes this package addresses the core race-pace limitation.

If successful, Cadillac could become a genuine points-scoring threat not just in Austria but across the remaining 15 races of the season. Perez has already flagged the British Grand Prix in July as a broader inflection point — where he expects the team to "really start stretching its legs" against rivals.

The Austrian GP as a Points Opportunity

The Red Bull Ring is actually a favorable circuit for teams chasing upgrades. The layout — short, with clear overtaking zones on the three long straights — creates more race action than many European circuits, which means positions are decided by racing rather than qualifying solely. A car that manages tires well into the second half of the race can climb significantly from its starting grid slot.

If Cadillac's upgrade resolves even part of the degradation problem, and if race conditions are favorable, an Austrian GP points finish is achievable. The team knows it. The driver lineup knows it. The question is whether the engineering translation from wind tunnel to race track delivers as promised.

What's Next

Cadillac's upgrade will face its first real-world test in Free Practice 1 on Thursday June 26. The team will evaluate the tire behavior in detail across FP1 and FP2 before finalizing the race strategy setup. If the degradation cliff has moved even five laps further out, Sunday's race becomes genuinely exciting for the American team. First F1 points are close — Austria could finally deliver them.
