### STORY 3: Red Bull Austria Upgrade

**Long-Form Post:**
RED BULL'S AUSTRIA UPGRADE IS HERE — BUT Laurent MEKIES IS ALREADY MANAGING EXPECTATIONS.

The Milton Keynes squad arrives at their home race — the Red Bull Ring in Spielberg — with what they're calling a significant update package for the RB22. Here's the technical context:

📋 THE STORY SO FAR:
• The RB22 started the 2026 season 12 kilograms over the minimum weight limit
• The Miami upgrade successfully cut that deficit in half — down to 6kg
• The Austria package is designed to eliminate those final six kilograms

🔧 WHAT THE UPDATE DELIVERS:
Getting to minimum weight has been a priority because excess weight directly hurts lap times. Every kilogram saved is meaningful in modern F1. Reaching the 768kg minimum would unlock performance Red Bull has been chasing all season.

BUT HERE'S THE CATCH:
Team principal Laurent Mekies said: "The Austrian package alone will not be enough. We know we'll have some further steps needed. But what is important is that on that continuous closing-the-gap trajectory that we have been onto since post-Japan, we continue to get closer."

That's a measured statement. It's not a victory lap. Mekies is being honest: Red Bull are improving, but they're not ready to win yet.

And with Verstappen's exit clause potentially activating after the summer break, "improving but not winning" may not be enough to keep their four-time world champion.

CADILLAC IS RACING FOR ITS FIRST F1 POINTS — AND AUSTRIA COULD BE THE MOMENT.

Formula 1's newest team enters the Austrian Grand Prix with what they're describing as a "big upgrade package" — and the goal is clear: finally get off zero in the championship standings.

📊 THE SITUATION:
After 7 races in 2026, Cadillac is the only constructor that has yet to score a point. Drivers Sergio Perez and Valtteri Bottas have shown genuine pace in qualifying — they arrived at Barcelona ahead of both Aston Martin cars by over a second — but race results have been a different story.

🔥 THE PROBLEM: TIRE DEGRADATION
Perez has been blunt about the limiting factor: "Whenever we have a stint longer than 15 laps, we seem to struggle a lot with a massive cliff." That cliff is what has kept Cadillac from converting pace into points.

The upgrade specifically targets that degradation issue. If Cadillac can extend their tire windows even marginally, they become a genuine points threat. Perez has also flagged the British GP as the point where he expects the team to "really start stretching its legs."

The midfield battle in Austria could be fascinating. An American team scoring their first F1 points at a European heritage circuit would be a massive storyline for the sport.
