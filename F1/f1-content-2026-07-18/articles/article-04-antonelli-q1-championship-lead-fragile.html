Championship Leader Antonelli Eliminated in Q1 — Again. His 25-Point Lead Just Got Complicated.

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | July 18, 2026

Kimi Antonelli arrived at the Belgian Grand Prix as the most dominant driver in Formula 1 this season. He leads the championship with 179 points. He has delivered five victories in the first half of the year. He topped Free Practice 2 at Spa-Francorchamps on Friday with the fastest time in the field.

Then he was knocked out of Q1 in the sprint shootout on Friday. Then he was knocked out of Q1 in Saturday's main qualifying session.

The championship leader starts near the back of the grid for Sunday's Belgian Grand Prix.

What Went Wrong

The details of exactly where Mercedes's preparation broke down over two consecutive qualifying sessions have not been fully confirmed. What is clear is a startling disconnect between Antonelli's race pace — which put him clear of every other driver in free practice — and his qualifying performance in both the sprint and grand prix sessions at Spa.

Qualifying at Spa-Francorchamps demands a different setup approach than race pace runs. The long, high-speed corners of the Ardennes circuit reward aerodynamic efficiency; the lower-downforce balance that aids lap time in qualifying can hurt tire behavior in race trim. Mercedes, it appears, has not found the optimal qualifying calibration this weekend.

His teammate George Russell qualified P6 — far from ideal, but dramatically better than Antonelli's position. An analysis published by The Race during the Belgian GP weekend highlighted how Mercedes's qualifying approach seems to expose a difference in how the two drivers extract performance from the W17, with the setup potentially less suited to Antonelli's style in pure qualifying conditions.

The Championship Implications

Antonelli holds a 25-point lead over Russell heading into Sunday. A difficult race for the Italian — combined with a strong result from Russell — could cut that gap meaningfully. At a circuit where a fully committed drive from the back can still produce points, but where strategy is far more constrained starting deep in the field, the challenge is significant.

The Hungarian Grand Prix follows next weekend, the last race before the summer break. For Antonelli, maximizing points in Belgium despite the grid position will be essential to maintaining the cushion that has defined his championship campaign.

What's Next

Belgian Grand Prix race day is Sunday at 9:00 AM ET. Watch for Mercedes's strategy with Antonelli — an aggressive tire choice and early gamble may be the team's best chance to recover toward the points. The Hungarian GP follows July 26.
