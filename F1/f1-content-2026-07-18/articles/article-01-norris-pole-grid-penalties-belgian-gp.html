Norris Claims Belgian GP Pole — Then Loses It to a 10-Place Penalty

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | July 18, 2026

Lando Norris gave McLaren their fourth pole position of the 2026 Formula 1 season at Spa-Francorchamps on Saturday, lapping the legendary Belgian circuit in 1:40.562 — 0.085 seconds ahead of his teammate Oscar Piastri. It was a dominant qualifying display from the Woking-based outfit.

It also became irrelevant almost immediately.

McLaren revealed after qualifying that Norris had taken on a fourth control electronics unit at the Belgian Grand Prix — one beyond the permitted season allocation. Under the sport's regulations, that triggered an automatic 10-place grid penalty, meaning Norris can start no higher than 11th on Sunday regardless of his qualifying time.

Piastri Inherits the Front

With Norris's penalty applied, Oscar Piastri effectively moves to the head of the grid for Sunday's Belgian Grand Prix. Piastri had an excellent session in his own right — qualifying just 0.085 seconds off his teammate's pole lap — and with a clean starting position, he enters the race as the man to beat.

Charles Leclerc gave Ferrari reason for optimism with P3 in qualifying, while Max Verstappen put the Red Bull fourth. Perhaps the day's most impressive performance came from Williams's Alex Albon, who qualified fifth — ahead of Russell, ahead of both Red Bulls and the entire Mercedes lineup — in what represented a significant afternoon for a team that has punched well above its weight in 2026.

George Russell, meanwhile, qualified sixth for Mercedes. His teammate, championship leader Kimi Antonelli, was not in the session — eliminated in Q1 in circumstances detailed separately.

A Complicated Sunday Grid

The grid for Sunday's Belgian Grand Prix will look markedly different from the qualifying classification. Beyond Norris's 10-place drop, Isack Hadjar (Racing Bulls) receives a 20-place penalty for taking on new power unit components, sending him to the back of the grid.

Gabriel Bortoleto made Q3 in the Sauber to qualify P10 — a result that speaks to the Swiss-affiliated team's considerable progress over the winter. The session as a whole reinforced McLaren's pace advantage in dry conditions, but with Norris starting deep in the field, Sunday's race takes on a different tactical shape.

Qualifying ran in predominantly dry conditions at Spa. The 65% rain forecast for Saturday afternoon failed to materialize, and the session was completed under cloud cover but without the wet-weather disruption many teams had prepared for.

What's Next

The Belgian Grand Prix starts Sunday at 9:00 AM ET (3:00 PM CEST) from Spa-Francorchamps. With Norris starting from the midfield and Antonelli deep in the pack, the race has championship implications that go well beyond a normal mid-season round.
