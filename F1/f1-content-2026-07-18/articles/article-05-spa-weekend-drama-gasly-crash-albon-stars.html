Gasly Crash, Track Limits Chaos, Albon Stars: Spa's Belgian GP Weekend Was Never Going to Be Quiet

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 18, 2026

Belgian Grand Prix weekends at Spa-Francorchamps tend to produce drama in volume. The 2026 edition has delivered on that reputation across every session of the weekend, with a litany of incidents, surprises, and talking points before Sunday's race has even begun.

Gasly's Heavy FP2 Crash

Free Practice 2 on Friday was interrupted in the most dramatic fashion when Pierre Gasly suffered a significant crash at the Les Fagnes section of the circuit. The Alpine driver lost the rear end of his car with a sudden snap of oversteer. He went through the gravel trap and made heavy contact with the barrier — the impact ripping the rear wing from his car entirely.

Alpine confirmed after the session that a rear wing failure had caused the incident, rather than driver error. The crash triggered a red flag that brought FP2 — already interrupted earlier in the session — to an extended halt. Gasly's car was badly damaged; the Alpine mechanics faced an intensive repair job ahead of Saturday's running.

Track Limits Dominate the Narrative

The corner combination of Eau Rouge and Raidillon has been the weekend's most contentious technical talking point. Stewards tightened enforcement of track limits at the top of the famous hill, and the consequences were felt across sessions.

Charles Leclerc had a lap deleted at Raidillon during FP2. Lewis Hamilton's best Q1 lap — competitive enough to advance — was wiped out by the same infraction in Saturday's qualifying session, dropping him to 16th and out of Q1. The strictness of enforcement at this particular point of the circuit became the dominant pre-qualifying conversation.

Esteban Ocon received a fine from the FIA for late arrival at the mandatory post-qualifying press conference. Haas's Nico Hulkenberg and Sauber's... both drivers were summoned before stewards during the weekend, though final rulings on their cases were pending.

The Unexpected Stars

Amid the chaos, two drivers delivered performances that deserved considerably more attention than the surrounding drama allowed.

Alex Albon put the Williams FW49 into Q3 and qualified fifth overall — a result that placed him ahead of both the Red Bull of Yuki Tsunoda, both of the Racing Bulls, and the entire Mercedes lineup. Albon has been consistently impressive in 2026, and Saturday at Spa offered one of his best qualifying results of the year.

Gabriel Bortoleto also made Q3 for Sauber, qualifying tenth. For a team that entered 2026 as one of the clear backmarkers, breaking into the top ten in qualifying at one of the sport's most demanding circuits is evidence of meaningful progress.

Isack Hadjar, meanwhile, despite a solid qualifying performance, will start the race at the back of the grid — a 20-place grid penalty for new power unit components erasing his session result entirely.

What's Next

The Belgian Grand Prix starts Sunday at 9:00 AM ET from Spa-Francorchamps. Given the multiple grid penalties and Q1 eliminations of championship contenders, the race will almost certainly resemble nothing its qualifying order suggested. At Spa, that is rarely a disappointment.
