Hamilton Calls Belgian GP Qualifying "Unacceptable" After Track Limits Delete His Q1 Lap

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 18, 2026

Lewis Hamilton had the pace to advance from Q1 at Spa-Francorchamps on Saturday. He had the lap time. What he didn't have was compliance at the top of Eau Rouge and Raidillon — and that one infraction unraveled his entire Belgian Grand Prix qualifying session.

His fastest Q1 lap was deleted by the stewards for exceeding track limits at the most iconic corner combination on the Formula 1 calendar. The deletion dropped Hamilton from contention to 16th position and out of qualifying. He will start near the back of the grid for Sunday's Belgian Grand Prix.

"Unacceptable," Hamilton said afterward. He offered nothing more. The word said enough.

A Weekend That Promised More

The frustration runs deeper than a single qualifying session. Hamilton had been one of the faster drivers at Spa throughout practice. He finished seventh in Free Practice 1 on Friday and tenth in FP2 — solid positions that gave the Ferrari camp genuine hope for qualifying. The pace was there. The preparation had been thorough.

But Spa-Francorchamps has always demanded absolute precision, particularly at Eau Rouge and the long, flat-out run through Raidillon to the top of the hill. The combination is one of the fastest sections in motorsport, and the white lines at the exit have been strictly enforced all weekend — track limits violations had been flagged across multiple sessions.

Hamilton's session ended where so many others have been complicated this weekend: at the top of that hill.

Championship Context

Hamilton enters the Belgian Grand Prix weekend sitting third in the Formula 1 championship standings with 147 points — 32 points behind championship leader Kimi Antonelli, 7 behind George Russell in second. The gap to Antonelli had already been reduced from a much larger deficit during a run of strong results, meaning momentum was firmly with the championship contenders.

Starting deep in the grid at a circuit where overtaking can happen but track position still matters makes Sunday's points target significantly harder to achieve. A top-five finish was realistic from the front of the midfield. From the back of the grid, it will require either a safety car or a circuit that plays to Ferrari's strengths.

There is also a bitter footnote: Antonelli, the championship leader, was eliminated in Q1 as well. Both of Hamilton's closest rivals — Russell (P6 in qualifying) and Antonelli — are in dramatically different positions heading into Sunday. Russell will run near the front; Antonelli starts deep. Hamilton joins him in the back half of the field.

What's Next

The Belgian Grand Prix starts Sunday at 9:00 AM ET from Spa-Francorchamps. Hamilton will need a near-perfect race to recover championship points. Monitor Ferrari's tire strategy — a longer first stint could help leapfrog cars on pit stops.
