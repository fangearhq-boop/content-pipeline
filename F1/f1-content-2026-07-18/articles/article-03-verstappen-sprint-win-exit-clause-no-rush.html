Verstappen Wins the Sprint — And His Red Bull Exit Clause Is Now Active. But His Manager Says "No Rush."

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 18, 2026

Max Verstappen began Saturday at Spa-Francorchamps as he has begun so many sessions in his career — by winning. He passed Oscar Piastri on Lap 1 of the Belgian Grand Prix sprint race, held off the McLaren driver through to the finish, and took the sprint victory ahead of Piastri and Lando Norris.

Hours later, the larger story of the Belgian GP weekend returned to the foreground. With Verstappen's championship standing now placing him outside the top two in the drivers' standings — championship leader Kimi Antonelli holds 179 points, George Russell 154, Verstappen further back — the exit clause in his Red Bull contract is mathematically active.

And his manager promptly told everyone not to overreact.

What the Clause Means

The clause in Verstappen's contract — running through the 2028 season — can be triggered if he falls outside the top two in the championship standings at certain defined thresholds. With those conditions now met, the mechanism exists for Verstappen to request an exit from his current deal and pursue other options.

The McLaren link has been the dominant storyline. Multiple reports during the Belgian GP weekend described Verstappen's switch to McLaren for 2027 as being in the "closing stages" of negotiation. McLaren has Norris and Piastri under multi-year contracts, which would create a complicated picture if Verstappen joins the team.

Manager's Message: Don't Read Into It

Manager Raymond Vermeulen moved quickly to dampen speculation. "Max wants to finish his time with Red Bull," Vermeulen said Saturday. "He has a contract until 2028 and would like to fulfil it. The fact that this clause exists doesn't mean we'll activate it. We could have activated it in recent years as well, and we haven't."

Verstappen himself was characteristically opaque at Thursday's drivers' press conference. When asked for an update on his future: "No." When pressed further: "I don't want to go say yes and no, and this and that about my future. I said already many times that if there was something new, I would say it myself." He added: "Everything falls into place naturally in time."

One small footnote that caught attention in the paddock: Verstappen personally announced during the Belgian GP weekend that his racing junior programme had signed Belgian McLaren junior driver Dries van Langendonck — while stressing the young driver would remain part of McLaren's development setup. A curious cross-team connection at an interesting moment.

What's Next

Verstappen starts P4 on Sunday's Belgian Grand Prix grid (taking into account Norris's 10-place penalty). His race from a strong grid position could give him meaningful championship points; Antonelli starts deep in the field, providing Verstappen an opportunity to make inroads. His F1 future remains the paddock's biggest question heading into the summer break.
