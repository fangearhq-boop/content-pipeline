### Long-Form Post If you missed the 2026 Miami Grand Prix, here is the short version: a car was flipped upside down, the race leader spun on the first lap, multiple penalties were handed out after the finish, and the whole thing started three hours earlier than planned because of thunderstorms. Pierre Gasly's Alpine was the most shocking moment of the weekend. Heading into Turn 17, Gasly moved to the outside to attempt an overtake on Liam Lawson when Lawson's Racing Bulls suffered a sudden gearbox failure. Lawson was unable to brake in time and made contact with the rear of Gasly's car, sending the Frenchman's Alpine flipping upside down into the barrier. Both drivers walked away unharmed. The stewards investigated but did not penalize Lawson — his gearbox failure was deemed a mechanical failure beyond his control. Meanwhile, at the very start of the race, Verstappen and Leclerc made contact going into Turn 1, causing the Red Bull driver to spin and duck into the pit lane for an early stop under the Safety Car. Verstappen was then handed a five-second time penalty for crossing the pit exit white line during that stop. Leclerc received a 20-second penalty after the race for repeatedly leaving the track without justifiable reason — a penalty that dropped him from sixth to eighth. The race had been moved from its original 3 p.m. local start to 6 p.m. because of a thunderstorm threat. Even with the earlier start, nothing could contain the chaos that followed. ### Image Post Caption Gasly's car upside down. Verstappen spinning Turn 1. Leclerc penalized 20 seconds.
