F1 2026 Championship Standings After Miami: Antonelli Has 100 Points and a 20-Point Lead — But He Says the Road Is Still Long

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 4, 2026

Four races into the 2026 Formula 1 World Championship, the standings tell a story of dominance — but also of a championship that is far from decided.

Kimi Antonelli leads the Drivers' Championship with 100 points after his Miami Grand Prix victory. His Mercedes teammate George Russell sits second with 80 points — a gap of 20 heading into the three-week break before the Canadian Grand Prix. In a 24-race season, 20 points represents a manageable deficit for any title challenger, and that is the critical context for everything that follows.

Charles Leclerc holds third in the standings for Ferrari, though his Miami weekend was deflating — a 20-second post-race penalty for repeated track limit violations cost him what would have been a stronger points haul. Lando Norris, after his Miami Sprint win and second-place main race finish, has climbed above Lewis Hamilton into fourth. Hamilton, in his first Ferrari season, sits fifth.

Mercedes and the Constructors' Championship

In the Constructors' Championship, Mercedes leads with 180 points — a total built on Antonelli's three race victories and Russell's consistent contributions. The gap to the chasing pack is substantial, but both Ferrari and McLaren have shown that they possess the machinery to challenge when everything goes right.

McLaren's Miami weekend — a sprint win for Norris, sprint second for Piastri, race second for Norris, race third for Piastri — was their best of the season and demonstrated the kind of all-weekend performance that could threaten Mercedes at the right circuit. Ferrari's pace has been there in flashes; Leclerc qualified on the front row at Miami and was a podium contender until the penalties removed him from the conversation.

The Antonelli Factor

The number that defines the 2026 season to this point is simple: three wins from three races. Antonelli's perfect conversion of pole positions into victories has no precedent in Formula 1 history. His qualifying pace has been devastating. His race management — particularly in the chaotic Miami race — has been exceptional for a 19-year-old in his first season.

He called the Canadian Grand Prix "a long road" when discussing the championship after Miami. It was a reminder that he is not taking anything for granted — and that 20 points is not an insurmountable lead in a season that has 20 more races to go.

What's Next

The Canadian Grand Prix at Circuit Gilles Villeneuve in Montreal runs May 22-24. It is traditionally one of the most unpredictable races on the calendar — a circuit where wall contact, rain, and safety cars can overturn any apparent advantage. For Antonelli and Mercedes, the challenge is maintaining perfection at a track that punishes mistakes. For Norris, Leclerc, and Hamilton, Canada represents the first real opportunity to change the championship narrative. Three weeks to prepare. Twenty points to close. The season is just getting started.
