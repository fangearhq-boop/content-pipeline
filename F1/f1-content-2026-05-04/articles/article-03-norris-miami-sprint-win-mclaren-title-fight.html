Norris Finally Wins in 2026 — Miami Sprint Shows McLaren Is Ready to Challenge for the Title

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | May 4, 2026

MIAMI — Lando Norris has been fast, consistent, and agonizingly close throughout the first four rounds of the 2026 season. On Saturday in Miami, he finally got the result his performances have deserved.

Norris claimed victory in the Miami Sprint — McLaren's first win of the 2026 Formula 1 season — leading from start to finish in a lights-to-flag performance that reminded the paddock why the Englishman is considered one of the fastest drivers on the grid. Oscar Piastri followed him home in second for a McLaren 1-2 finish, with Ferrari's Charles Leclerc completing the top three.

Kimi Antonelli, who had qualified second for the sprint, was running fourth when the stewards determined he had repeatedly exceeded track limits. A five-second penalty dropped him to sixth, putting the championship leader outside the points' top tier for the first time all season — though only in the sprint format.

The result was McLaren's emphatic answer to a narrative that had been building through the season opener: that Mercedes had a gap. After Miami, that gap looks considerably smaller.

A Weekend That Belonged to Papaya

Norris backed up his sprint win with a second-place finish in Sunday's main race, pushing Antonelli hard over 57 laps before crossing the line 3.264 seconds behind the winner. Piastri was third. McLaren's haul from the full Miami weekend — a sprint win, a sprint second, a race second, and a race third — was the best of any constructor over the four-day event.

"The pace was there all weekend," Norris said after the sprint. "We needed to convert and we did." His championship gap to Antonelli now stands at 47 points after Round 4. It is still significant — but the direction of travel has changed. Norris closed on the leader where he was further back before Miami.

For McLaren as a team, the results validate months of development work. The MCL2026 has shown consistent improvement through the opening rounds. The sprint format in Miami was a concentrated stress test that proved the car could perform at the front for an extended period — not just in isolated qualifying laps.

What's Next

The next race is the Canadian Grand Prix at Circuit Gilles Villeneuve in Montreal, May 22-24. The layout of the circuit — with its long straights, tight chicanes, and notorious wall at the exit of the final hairpin — could favor different technical packages than Miami. McLaren will arrive in Canada as a team with genuine momentum, a team that has tasted victory and knows the gap to Antonelli is closeable. Norris, for one, will not be content with close calls for much longer.
