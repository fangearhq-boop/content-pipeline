No Driver Has Ever Done This: Antonelli Claims Third Straight Win From Third Pole, Makes F1 History in Miami

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | May 4, 2026

MIAMI — Kimi Antonelli has done something no Formula 1 driver has ever done before.

The Mercedes driver claimed victory at the 2026 Miami Grand Prix on Sunday, winning his third consecutive race of the season from his third consecutive pole position. In doing so, he became the first driver in Formula 1 history to convert their maiden three pole positions into victories — a feat that places him in the company of the sport's greatest names, while simultaneously surpassing all of them.

Ayrton Senna and Michael Schumacher both claimed three consecutive pole positions to open their respective careers. Neither converted all three into wins. Antonelli, 19 years old, did.

Lando Norris pushed hard throughout the 57 laps at Miami International Autodrome, crossing the line just 3.264 seconds behind in second place. Oscar Piastri completed a McLaren double-podium in third, more than 27 seconds adrift. George Russell was fourth for Mercedes, while Max Verstappen salvaged fifth after a chaotic race that included a first-lap spin and a post-race penalty for a pit exit breach.

A Race That Required Everything

The Miami Grand Prix was not a comfortable procession for Antonelli. The race was moved to a 6 p.m. local start time due to thunderstorm concerns, and the opening laps were immediately dramatic — Verstappen and Charles Leclerc made contact at Turn 1, sending the Red Bull spinning and prompting an early Safety Car period. Through that chaos, Antonelli kept his cool, managed the Safety Car restart cleanly, and built the lead he needed.

Leclerc, who had qualified third and looked like a genuine podium threat, received a 20-second post-race penalty for repeatedly leaving the track without justifiable reason. The penalty dropped him from sixth to eighth in the final standings. Verstappen's penalty for crossing the pit exit white line was five seconds, but it barely affected his position given Leclerc's larger sanction.

Norris made it close in the closing stages, but Antonelli's tyre management was exceptional. He had the pace when he needed it and the discipline to preserve his advantage when he did not.

The Record Books Rewritten

Before Sunday's result, Antonelli had already made history by becoming the youngest-ever Formula 1 championship leader. He had won the Chinese and Japanese Grands Prix from pole in the opening weeks of the season. Miami was the third act — and the one that sealed a place no driver had ever reached.

"The road is still long," Antonelli said in the post-race press conference. It was a measured, almost understated assessment for a driver who has just rewritten the sport's record books. The championship standings now read: Antonelli 100 points, Russell 80. Twenty points in four races is not an insurmountable gap — but it is a statement of intent from a driver who has been perfect so far.

What's Next

The 2026 Formula 1 season now pauses for three weeks before the Canadian Grand Prix at Circuit Gilles Villeneuve in Montreal, scheduled for May 22-24. It will be Antonelli's first race at a circuit notorious for its unpredictability — the Wall of Champions, the hairpin, the potential for rain and safety cars. After four races, the youngest champion-in-waiting has earned the right to be considered the favorite in Canada, too. But Montreal has a way of upending expectations. The next chapter begins in three weeks.
