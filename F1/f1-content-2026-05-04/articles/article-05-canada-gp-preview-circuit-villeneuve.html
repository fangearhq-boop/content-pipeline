Three Weeks, One Question: Can Anyone Stop Antonelli's Perfect Season at the Canadian Grand Prix?

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | May 4, 2026

Formula 1 arrives at the Circuit Gilles Villeneuve in Montreal on May 22, and the question hovering over the weekend is the same one that has defined the first four races of 2026: will anyone finally stop Kimi Antonelli?

Antonelli's perfect start — three wins from three poles — has set a benchmark that no driver in Formula 1 history has matched at the start of a career. He heads into the Canadian Grand Prix with a 20-point championship lead over teammate George Russell and the kind of momentum that is difficult to disrupt. But if any circuit on the calendar could do it, Circuit Gilles Villeneuve is a good candidate.

The Montreal track is a unique challenge. Named after the legendary Canadian driver who lost his life racing in 1982, it sits on the man-made Île Notre-Dame in the middle of the St. Lawrence River. Its characteristics are unlike any other circuit on the calendar: long, flat-out straights punctuated by tight chicanes and hairpin braking zones, with walls that are frustratingly close to the racing line. The infamous Wall of Champions at the exit of the final chicane has claimed Damon Hill, Michael Schumacher, and Jacques Villeneuve in a single 1999 weekend. No driver is immune to its reach.

What the Key Storylines Will Be

For Antonelli, Canada is the first test of whether his perfect season survives contact with a circuit that demands humility. His qualifying pace has been devastating this year — but Montreal's mix of braking zones and wall proximity punishes even the best drivers. The setup requirements are different from the Miami or Japanese circuits that preceded it.

For Lando Norris, the Miami sprint win and race podium have given McLaren genuine confidence that the MCL2026 can fight at the front. Norris's lap-to-lap pace in Miami was arguably the fastest of any driver in the field over the final third of the race. Canada would suit a driver of his style — aggressive on the brakes, precise in the chicanes.

Max Verstappen and Red Bull will be watching the circuit's characteristics carefully. The high-speed, high-downforce nature of Miami was not ideal for the RB22's current setup. Montreal's different demands could give the team a better platform to show what they have been developing over the break. A strong result in Canada would represent a significant statement of recovery from a team that has struggled through the opening rounds.

What's Next

The 2026 Canadian Grand Prix runs May 22-24 at Circuit Gilles Villeneuve, Montreal. Practice sessions begin on Friday, qualifying on Saturday, and the race on Sunday. Three weeks from Miami, the championship story continues — and the Wall of Champions does not care who is leading the points.
