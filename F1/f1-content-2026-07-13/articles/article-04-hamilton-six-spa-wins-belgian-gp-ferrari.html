Six Wins, One New Chapter: Hamilton Arrives at Spa in Ferrari Red

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 13, 2026

F1 | Lewis Hamilton | Ferrari | Belgian Grand Prix | Spa-Francorchamps

Lewis Hamilton and Spa-Francorchamps have a history that goes beyond statistics, though the statistics are extraordinary on their own.

Hamilton has won the Belgian Grand Prix six times — more than any other active driver currently on the Formula 1 grid. He has done it in different conditions, in different cars, and across different phases of his career. Now he is about to attempt something new: a seventh win at this circuit in Ferrari red, four months into the most significant team change of his career.

A Record That Defines an Era

Six Belgian GP victories over a career tells a story about versatility and adaptability. Spa is a circuit that punishes mistakes and rewards precision — the combination of high-speed sectors, elevation changes, and unpredictable weather creates one of motorsport's most demanding tests. Hamilton's record here is not the product of a dominant car alone; it is the accumulated result of execution under pressure at a track where the margin for error is genuinely narrow.

Among active drivers, no one else is close to Hamilton's total at Spa. The circuit has been his stage repeatedly, and his ability to read and manage it through varying weather conditions is a well-documented strength.

Hamilton's 2026 Season With Ferrari

Hamilton joined Ferrari for the 2026 season after a storied run at Mercedes where he won seven world championships. The transition has been managed with characteristic professionalism. He currently sits third in the drivers championship with 147 points — 32 behind leader Kimi Antonelli and seven behind teammate Charles Leclerc.

At the British GP, Hamilton finished third after receiving a five-second penalty for a false start, but his underlying pace was strong. Ferrari's resurgence is real — Leclerc won Silverstone, and the team has demonstrated the ability to lead races and manage strategy at the front of the field. The constructors battle with Mercedes is ongoing, and Ferrari needs every point.

Why the Rain Matters

The forecast for Spa this weekend calls for thundery showers throughout all three days. This is not incidental to Hamilton's chances — it may be the most important factor. His wet-weather record across his career is exceptional, and a genuinely mixed-conditions race at Spa creates the kind of shifting strategic puzzle that favors experience and adaptability over raw pace.

Ferrari as a team has shown competent race strategy this season. If Hamilton can get himself into position through the opening laps and the rain disrupts the expected order, the conditions exist for a memorable performance at a circuit he has made his own over the years.

What's Next

Friday's practice sessions will give us the first real read on Ferrari's Spa pace in 2026. By Saturday qualifying, we will know whether Hamilton is in genuine contention for the front row. And if the weather delivers on its forecast Sunday, one of F1's most reliable rain specialists will be in the mix at a circuit he knows better than perhaps any driver alive.
