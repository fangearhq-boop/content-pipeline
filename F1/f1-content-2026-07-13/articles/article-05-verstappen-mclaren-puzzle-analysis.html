The Verstappen-McLaren Puzzle: Why the Deal Nobody Will Confirm Won't Go Away

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 13, 2026

F1 Driver Market | Max Verstappen | McLaren | Red Bull | F1 Silly Season

On paper, there is no Verstappen-McLaren deal. Zak Brown said so himself. "They didn't go anywhere. I've got my two drivers, so there's nothing to report," the McLaren CEO told reporters when asked about negotiations with the four-time champion. Asked directly whether a 2027 deal would happen, Brown answered with a simple: "Correct." McLaren has Lando Norris under a multi-year arrangement and Oscar Piastri contracted through 2028.

And yet the story will not die. The question is why — and whether the persistence of the rumor tells us something the official statements do not.

The Details That Keep the Story Alive

Start with GianPiero Lambiase. He is Max Verstappen's race engineer — arguably the most important individual relationship in a racing driver's professional life. Lambiase has worked with Verstappen through all four of his world championships at Red Bull. In April 2026, McLaren announced that Lambiase would be joining their operation, effective no later than 2028. No driver drafted their most trusted collaborator to a competing team without some expectation of following. The timing and the nature of the move have been noted widely in the paddock.

Then there is Sportbible's July 7 report. The outlet stated that Verstappen is in the "closing stages" of a "huge F1 move" — without specifying the team. The language is deliberate. It suggests a deal is being structured without confirming its destination, which is a common pattern in F1 driver negotiations where teams want the commercial agreement locked before anything public is confirmed.

And Oscar Piastri. Multiple reports have referenced an exit clause in Piastri's McLaren contract, the existence of which would theoretically create space for a headline signing even within a "locked" driver lineup.

The Retirement Alternative

Not everyone in the paddock is convinced the story ends at McLaren. Yahoo Sports reported that full F1 retirement in 2027 is increasingly being taken seriously as a genuine option for Verstappen — not a negotiating tactic, but a real possibility. Verstappen has been candid about his frustrations with the current technical regulations, which mandate a demanding split between power from the combustion engine and energy recovery systems. He described the current driving style as "not mentally doable" in the long run.

The FIA agreed to adjust the balance to a more driver-friendly 60/40 ratio from 2027, but teams including Ferrari, Audi, and Cadillac have objected. If those objections hold and the current style persists into 2027, Verstappen's calculus on returning at all could change.

At 29, Verstappen has won four consecutive championships. Leaving on those terms is not without historical precedent in motorsport — some champions decide there is nothing left to prove.

What We Actually Know

What is confirmed: the exit clause is mathematically triggered, the formal activation deadline is October 2026, and Red Bull failed in its attempt to remove the clause. What is reported but officially denied: McLaren negotiations. What is credibly speculated: retirement as an alternative. The full picture will emerge before October — and whatever Verstappen decides will reshape the 2027 grid more dramatically than any other single move available.

What's Next

The Belgian GP this weekend provides a backdrop for continued speculation and possibly new signals. Any statement from Verstappen, his management, or Red Bull in the next few weeks carries elevated significance. F1 Fanrecap will track every development as the October deadline approaches.
