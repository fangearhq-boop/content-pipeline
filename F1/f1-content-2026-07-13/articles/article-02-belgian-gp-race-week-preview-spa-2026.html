Belgian GP Race Week Is Here: Championship Battle, Rain, and High Stakes at Spa

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 13, 2026

F1 Race Preview | Belgian Grand Prix | Spa-Francorchamps | F1 Championship

Race week for the 2026 Belgian Grand Prix officially begins this week, and the stakes could hardly be higher.

Round 12 of 22 takes place at Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps, the longest circuit on the Formula 1 calendar at 7.004 kilometers. Free Practice 1 and 2 begin Friday, qualifying on Saturday, and the race on Sunday July 19 at 15:00 CEST (9:00 AM ET). The weather forecast calls for thundery showers throughout all three days — which, at Spa, transforms an already challenging circuit into something else entirely.

The Championship Heading Into Belgium

Four drivers hold realistic championship aspirations with 11 rounds remaining after this weekend. Kimi Antonelli leads with 179 points, but he arrives in Belgium as the research notes describe — wounded. His British GP ended in a DNF when a front-left wheel shield detached on Lap 41, triggering two additional pit stops and ultimately forcing him out. He carries 25 points over second-placed George Russell.

Russell sits at 154 points and drives for the same Mercedes team that provides Antonelli's car. The internal battle between a sophomore finding his footing and an experienced former grand prix winner is one of the most compelling subplots of the season. Russell's results since the midpoint have been strong, and a wet Spa circuit is not unfavorable territory for him.

Lewis Hamilton at 147 points is seven behind Russell and 32 behind the leader. He drives for Ferrari now, in his first season with the Scuderia, and Charles Leclerc — who sits fourth at 108 points — demonstrated just last week at the British GP that Ferrari is capable of winning races. Leclerc beat Antonelli from T1 at Silverstone in one of the overtakes of the season.

Why Belgium Matters More Than Usual

Belgium and Hungary are the final two rounds before the summer break. That means every point scored or dropped this weekend carries compounded significance — it will sit on the standings for six weeks while teams and drivers reset for the second half of the season. A driver who leaves Belgium and Hungary with momentum often carries it into September.

Antonelli's lead looks comfortable on paper. It is less comfortable when you remember he has already been bitten by reliability once this season, and Spa's technical demands create additional risk. Russell or Hamilton could close to within single-figure points with two strong performances before the break.

Spa's Unique Character

Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps is beloved by drivers and fans for reasons that no other circuit quite replicates. The run through Eau Rouge and Raidillon is one of the most thrilling sequences in motorsport — a compression into a high-speed left-right that demands commitment and precision. The long sweeps of Pouhon and Blanchimont reward downforce efficiency. The Kemmel Straight offers overtaking opportunities into Les Combes.

Rain at Spa is not unusual — it is almost expected. And wet conditions at this circuit tend to produce memorable races, often shuffling strategies and creating the kind of uncertainty that no team can fully plan for.

What's Next

Free Practice 1 begins Friday at 7:30 AM ET, giving teams their first data from the Spa circuit this season. Qualifying on Saturday afternoon will set the grid. And Sunday's race — with rain likely and the championship on the line — has all the ingredients for a defining moment in the 2026 F1 season. F1 Fanrecap will have full coverage throughout the weekend.
