George Russell Confirms His 2027 Mercedes Seat: "I Can Confirm 100 Percent"

By Ryan Calloway | F1 Fanrecap | July 13, 2026

F1 Driver Contracts | George Russell | Mercedes | 2027 F1 Grid

George Russell is staying at Mercedes — and he said so himself, in no uncertain terms.

The 28-year-old British driver, who sits second in the 2026 championship standings with 154 points, has publicly confirmed that he has secured his 2027 seat with the Silver Arrows. Speaking at different points during the season, Russell delivered a pair of clear statements that close the chapter on any speculation about his future. "My seat for 2027 is in my hands. So I'm not being strung along here," Russell told The Telegraph earlier this year. And more recently, per reporting from GPFans, Russell confirmed with absolute certainty: "I can confirm 100 percent."

How the Contract Works

Russell's arrangement with Mercedes is structured around a performance clause. His 2026 contract does not automatically guarantee him the 2027 seat — instead, it contains a renewal mechanism that activates if he hits a defined performance target. The exact metric is not publicly confirmed, but it is understood to be tied to his position in the championship relative to his teammate Kimi Antonelli, the team's long-term cornerstone.

Russell confirmed in April 2026 that he expected to fulfil the performance clauses given Mercedes' strong season. His current P2 standing — 25 points behind Antonelli — makes clear that he has met whatever standard was required. The clause has been activated, and 2027 at Mercedes is secured.

What This Means for the Silly Season

Russell's confirmation closes one of the most significant doors in the 2026 driver market. Max Verstappen — whose Red Bull exit clause is now mathematically triggered — had been linked to multiple teams, with Mercedes mentioned alongside McLaren as a potential destination. That possibility is now off the table.

Mercedes will field Antonelli alongside Russell in 2027. The team has also confirmed Lewis Hamilton is contracted through 2027 at Ferrari, Leclerc is locked in beyond 2028, and Norris and Piastri are both under multi-year McLaren arrangements. The top of the 2027 grid is increasingly settled — which makes Verstappen's situation all the more fascinating. If he leaves Red Bull, his options are genuinely limited to specific vacancies that don't currently exist on paper.

Russell's 2026 Season

Russell's performance this year has justified his continued tenure. He sits second in the championship, has been consistently competitive, and has been the more experienced guiding force alongside the 18-year-old Antonelli. The partnership has been productive without being easy — Russell has had to push hard every race, knowing his 2027 future was partly determined by how he performed against his young teammate.

With that uncertainty now resolved, Russell can approach the Belgian GP and the remainder of the season with full clarity. He is fighting for a championship that, while difficult, is not out of reach — the gap to Antonelli is 25 points, which is one race's worth of fortune or misfortune in Formula 1.
