**Long-Form Post:**

And now, for the first time in his career, he arrives at this iconic circuit wearing the red of Ferrari. A seventh Belgian GP win would be historic. And with rain in the forecast, the conditions could suit a driver who has won here more than anyone else.

### STORY 5: The Verstappen-McLaren Question Nobody Will Answer

**Long-Form Post:**
Here is everything we know about Max Verstappen's future — and everything we don't.

What is confirmed: Verstappen's exit clause from Red Bull is now mathematically triggered. He sits seventh in the championship with no path to second place before the summer break. He has until around October to formally activate the clause. That is the established fact.

What is rumored but denied: Sportbible reported on July 7 that Verstappen is in the "closing stages" of a major F1 move, without naming the team. McLaren has been the most discussed destination for months. But McLaren CEO Zak Brown has publicly stated: "They didn't go anywhere. I've got my two drivers, so there's nothing to report." When asked if a 2027 deal was happening, Brown answered with a flat: "Correct." Norris and Piastri are both under multi-year contracts.

The most fascinating wrinkle: Verstappen's longtime race engineer GianPiero Lambiase has signed with McLaren, effective no later than 2028. Nobody drafts their most trusted collaborator to a team they're not planning to join — or so the theory goes.

And then there is the retirement question. Yahoo Sports has reported that F1 retirement in 2027 is "increasingly" being taken seriously as a real option. Verstappen has previously said that the sport's current active-regen driving style is "not mentally doable" long-term, and despite a 60/40 regulation change promised for 2027, several teams are objecting.

The answer is coming before October. Until then, every race is a subplot.
