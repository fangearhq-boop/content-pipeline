Hamilton's Monaco Window: Why 2026 Is Ferrari's Best Chance at the Principality

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | June 1, 2026

Lewis Hamilton has won the Monaco Grand Prix before. He knows the rhythm of the streets, the way the barriers rush past at the Swimming Pool complex, the patience required at Casino Square. But he has never won Monaco with Ferrari — and as he prepares for the 2026 edition, the conditions could not be more favorable for him to change that.

After finishing P2 in the Canadian Grand Prix — his best result of the season so far in Ferrari red — Hamilton was direct about his Monaco ambitions. "Power is not king there. I think our car could be really strong," he told reporters. Those words carry weight, because he is right on both counts.

The Ferrari Chassis Advantage Unlocked

Throughout the 2026 season, Ferrari has struggled at high-speed, power-sensitive circuits where Mercedes' active aerodynamics have been most effective. The SF-26 is widely regarded as having excellent chassis characteristics — exceptional mechanical grip, precise aerodynamic downforce, and balance through slow-speed corners — but those strengths have been masked at circuits where Straight Mode activation changes the competitive order dramatically.

Monaco changes that calculation entirely. The FIA has banned active aerodynamics for the complete Grand Prix weekend. No activation zone on the Circuit de Monaco meets the required three-second safety threshold. For the first time all season, Ferrari will race on its own terms — with its chassis doing all the talking.

Building Momentum

Canada was a turning point in Hamilton's season. After a slow start adapting to the Ferrari, he has been gaining confidence with each race. The P2 finish — achieved after a late overtake on Max Verstappen — showed a driver who has found his rhythm in the car. He outqualified Leclerc for both the Sprint and the race in Montreal, demonstrating that when the car suits him, he is as fast as anyone in the field.

At Monaco, the circuit's characteristics play to his strengths: precision, timing, and the ability to extract maximum grip from a car that is balanced rather than brutal. The Hamilton who dominated Monaco in the Mercedes years is still very much present. He simply needs the right car, at the right circuit, on the right weekend.

What's Next

Hamilton qualified P7 in Monaco for the 2025 season, a result that will serve as extra motivation for the 2026 edition. Practice begins Friday, June 5. Qualifying on Saturday will tell the true story of where Ferrari sits competitively with the active aero ban in effect.

If Hamilton can convert pole or a front-row start into a win on Sunday, June 7, he will have answered every question being asked about his Ferrari chapter in the most emphatic way possible. The conditions are aligned. The opportunity is real.
