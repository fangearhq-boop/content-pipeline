Hamilton Stands on Monaco Podium in Ferrari Red for the First Time

By Marcus Cole | June 7, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Lewis Hamilton has been to the Monaco Grand Prix podium before. Three times, in fact — in 2008, 2016, and 2019 — all wearing the silver livery of Mercedes. Sunday added a new dimension to that history: his first Monaco podium wearing red.

Hamilton finished third at the 2026 Monaco Grand Prix, completing a consistent weekend with Ferrari that began with the fastest time in Free Practice 2 on Saturday and ended with 15 championship points and a view of the Mediterranean from the Monte Carlo podium.

What Saturday Set Up

Hamilton's P3 finish was very nearly a P3 qualifying result by design. In Saturday's qualifying, he set the third-fastest time at 0.228 seconds behind his Mercedes successor Kimi Antonelli and 0.185 seconds behind Max Verstappen. For a driver in his first season with a new team at one of the sport's most demanding circuits, that was a composed and effective performance.

In Free Practice 2, Hamilton had been the fastest man on the circuit — 1m 13.026 seconds, leading a Ferrari 1-2 ahead of Charles Leclerc. Ferrari had every reason to believe Monaco could be a strong weekend for them. And in Hamilton's case, it was.

The Race

From third on the grid at Monaco, the job was straightforward: stay out of trouble, manage the tires, and bring the car home in position. At a circuit that almost never produces position changes, Hamilton executed exactly what was required. He held third from lights to flag.

It is not the kind of race that generates dramatic highlight moments. It is the kind of race that earns 15 championship points and a podium trophy. For a driver who is still finding his rhythm in a new car with a new team, those are meaningful things.

A New Chapter at a Familiar Setting

Monaco holds a special place in Hamilton's career history. His first Monaco win came in 2008 during his debut championship season. He returned to the top step in 2016 and 2019, becoming one of the most successful drivers in the race's modern era. Each of those victories came in Mercedes silver.

The decision to join Ferrari for 2026 was one of the most scrutinized career moves in Formula 1 history. Seven months into that chapter, Sunday's Monte Carlo podium suggests the partnership has genuine potential. His P2 finish in Canada was described by Hamilton as one of his happiest days with the team. Monaco adds another positive data point.

He did not win. He may not win Monaco in 2026. But he has been on the podium more times this season than many critics expected, and the 2026 campaign is still less than half over.

What's Next

Hamilton and Ferrari head to the Spanish Grand Prix following Monaco. Barcelona is a circuit where aerodynamic efficiency and race strategy play larger roles than at Monaco — an environment in which Ferrari has historically been competitive. If Hamilton can build on his Monaco and Canada results at Barcelona, the case for a successful first Ferrari season continues to strengthen.
