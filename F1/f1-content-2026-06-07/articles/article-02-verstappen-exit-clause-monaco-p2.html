Verstappen Claims Monaco P2 — But His Exit Clause Deadline Is July 27

By Ryan Calloway | June 7, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Max Verstappen finished second at Monaco on Sunday. Back-to-back podiums: third in Canada, second in Monaco. By any conventional measuring stick, that represents a genuine turnaround for a driver and team that had been struggling to find their rhythm under the 2026 technical regulations.

But Verstappen's contract with Red Bull Racing does not operate by a conventional measuring stick. It contains an exit clause that will define the rest of his 2026 season — and potentially his entire future in Formula 1.

The Clause Explained

Verstappen's Red Bull contract includes a performance-based exit clause that allows him to terminate the deal without financial penalty if he is not inside the top two of the Drivers' Championship at the summer break. The deadline is July 27, 2026.

The clause was reportedly added at Verstappen's insistence due to his longstanding concerns about the 2026 aerodynamic regulations — a sweeping technical overhaul that was always going to produce new hierarchies among the teams. Red Bull, which had dominated with the previous car concept, has found itself running as the fourth-fastest team on the grid through much of 2026.

If Verstappen is not in the top two by July 27, he can formally trigger the exit clause between August and October. That would open the door to a move to any team with a seat available.

The Math After Monaco

Verstappen started Monaco from second on the grid and finished second. That is 18 additional championship points — a strong haul from a circuit where he had limited options to overtake the car in front. Combined with his P3 finish in Canada, he has accumulated 33 points across the last two races.

However, the championship standings still leave him outside the top two. George Russell, who finished sixth at Monaco, remains ahead of Verstappen in the standings — and while that gap narrowed slightly with Monaco's result, Russell is still significantly ahead of Verstappen in total points.

Meanwhile, Kimi Antonelli's Monaco victory and Russell's P6 has not dramatically reshuffled the standings in Verstappen's favor. To reach P2 in the championship by July 27, Verstappen needs multiple strong results combined with poor performances from Russell and others.

What Verstappen Needs

Between now and July 27, the Formula 1 calendar includes the Spanish Grand Prix and potentially one or two additional races depending on scheduling. Verstappen needs to maximize every point available — ideally wins — while hoping for difficulties from the drivers above him.

Red Bull's performance at Monaco is encouraging. Starting P2 and finishing P2 is a solid result for a team that has been chasing Mercedes all season. But the Barcelona circuit, which comes next, has very different car requirements that may expose the same weaknesses that plagued Red Bull earlier in the year.

The clock is ticking. And it does not care about back-to-back podiums.

What's Next

Verstappen will head to the Spanish Grand Prix needing another strong performance. The exit clause deadline is July 27 — roughly seven weeks away. What he does between now and then will determine whether he stays at Red Bull or begins one of the most consequential free agent negotiations in Formula 1 history.
