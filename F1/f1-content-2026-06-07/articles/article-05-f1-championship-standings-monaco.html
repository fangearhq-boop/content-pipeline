F1 Championship Standings After Monaco: Antonelli Extends Lead — Can Anyone Stop Him?

By Ryan Calloway | June 7, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Five wins from eight races. Kimi Antonelli walked away from Monte Carlo on Sunday with his 2026 Monaco Grand Prix trophy, a fresh batch of championship points, and a lead that is beginning to look genuinely difficult to reverse.

After Monaco, the 2026 Formula 1 Drivers' Championship picture is clearer than ever — and the picture shows a 19-year-old Mercedes driver running away from the field at a pace that no one in the paddock can comfortably explain.

Antonelli's Advantage

Antonelli entered Monaco with 131 championship points and a 43-point lead over his Mercedes teammate George Russell. His P1 finish in Monte Carlo adds 25 points to his total. Russell finished sixth, collecting eight points. The gap between them has grown further — now approaching 60 points — with significant stretches of the season still to be run.

To put that in perspective: at most circuits, a single race win is worth 25 points. For Russell to close a 60-point gap, he would need Antonelli to retire from multiple races while he wins consecutively. That is not impossible in Formula 1, but it requires a sustained combination of good fortune and perfect execution that has been entirely absent for the Mercedes No. 2 so far in 2026.

Russell's Difficult Run

George Russell came into 2026 as a genuine title contender. His Canada weekend encapsulated the problems that have defined his season: he led the Grand Prix until a catastrophic ERS failure on Lap 30 ended his race. Monaco followed with a P6 finish from a starting position further back on the grid.

The pace is there. The results are not consistently following. And every race that slips by with Antonelli taking points increases the improbability of a championship reversal.

Verstappen's Climbing Path

Max Verstappen's back-to-back podiums — P3 in Canada, P2 in Monaco — have been the most encouraging development from a non-Mercedes perspective in recent weeks. Red Bull has been the fourth-fastest team through much of 2026, and consecutive podiums suggest the package is improving.

The complication is Verstappen's contract exit clause. He must be in the top two of the Drivers' Championship by July 27 or he can formally trigger his right to leave Red Bull. Even after Monaco P2, he remains outside the top-two championship positions. The math is tight, and the window is narrowing.

The Ferrari Picture

Lewis Hamilton P3 and Charles Leclerc P4 represents a solid double-points haul for Scuderia Ferrari in Monaco, but not the breakthrough result that would change the championship conversation. Leclerc, who was the pre-race favorite, is frustrated to have been limited by his qualifying position. Hamilton continues to build his relationship with the car and the team.

Ferrari will have higher expectations of the Spanish Grand Prix, where their traditional aero philosophy tends to perform well.

What's Next

The Spanish Grand Prix at Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya is next on the Formula 1 calendar. For everyone chasing Antonelli, Spain represents one of the best opportunities to take ground: unlike Monaco, Barcelona allows overtaking, rewards strategy, and gives teams a chance to recover from poor qualifying positions.

But the question of whether anyone can actually stop Kimi Antonelli in 2026 grows more pressing with each passing race weekend. Five victories in eight rounds. The lead keeps growing. And the season is just past its first third.
