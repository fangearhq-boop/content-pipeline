Antonelli Wins Monaco Grand Prix for Fifth F1 Victory of 2026

By Elena Voss | June 7, 2026 | F1 Fanrecap

Kimi Antonelli crossed the finish line at Circuit de Monaco on Sunday to claim the 2026 Monaco Grand Prix, his fifth victory of the Formula 1 season and another emphatic statement of dominance in a year that keeps looking more and more like a coronation.

The Mercedes driver had converted his maiden Monte Carlo pole position — secured by just 0.043 seconds over Max Verstappen on Saturday — into a controlled race victory in the manner that Monaco invariably rewards. He never led a lap he wasn't supposed to, and the result felt settled almost from the first corner.

How Monaco Was Won on Saturday

Formula 1 purists will tell you that the Monaco Grand Prix is often decided in qualifying. Overtaking through the circuit's twisting barriers and unforgiving walls is nearly impossible. Whoever lines up in the prime positions tends to finish in them.

Antonelli secured pole with a lap of 1m 12.051 seconds during Saturday's qualifying session. Behind him, Verstappen slotted into second at 0.043s — an agonizingly close gap that underlined how competitive the top of the grid was at Monaco. Lewis Hamilton completed a strong weekend by qualifying third for Ferrari, just 0.228 seconds off Antonelli's pole.

Charles Leclerc, who won the Monaco Grand Prix in 2024 and was considered the pre-race favorite at +185 before qualifying, could only manage fourth on the grid. At Monaco, that was nearly as damaging as a technical retirement.

The Race

The start was clean, the order never fundamentally changed, and Antonelli managed his race with the same composure that has characterized his entire 2026 season. Verstappen held second. Hamilton held third. Leclerc remained fourth despite his Ferrari's evident pace advantages in the practice sessions.

The final classification read: Antonelli, Verstappen, Hamilton, Leclerc, Isack Hadjar, George Russell, Oscar Piastri, Lando Norris, Pierre Gasly, Liam Lawson.

For Russell — who sits second in the Drivers' Championship — a P6 finish was a particularly painful result. He entered the weekend already 43 points behind Antonelli following his Lap 30 retirement in Canada. Monaco only pushed that gap higher.

Five Wins, One Season

Antonelli's fifth victory of 2026 in eight races gives him a win rate that only the sport's greatest drivers have matched through the first portion of a season. He has now taken pole and victory at Monte Carlo — adding to his haul from earlier in the year — and his championship lead continues to extend at a rate that will make other teams increasingly anxious.

He is 19 years old. He is driving for the team that produced his results with Hamilton. And he is making it look easy.

What's Next

The Formula 1 calendar moves on from Monaco to the Spanish Grand Prix in Barcelona. Unlike Monte Carlo, Barcelona is a conventional circuit where aerodynamic balance, tire management, and pit strategy all play meaningful roles. Teams that have struggled to keep pace with Mercedes in the narrow confines of Monaco may find more opportunities to close the gap in Spain.

Antonelli's lead is substantial. The rest of the field is not giving up. But after Monaco, the task of catching him just got harder.
