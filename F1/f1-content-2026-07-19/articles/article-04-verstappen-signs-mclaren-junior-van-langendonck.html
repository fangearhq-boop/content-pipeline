Verstappen Signs McLaren Junior Driver to His Management Company — While Reportedly Negotiating to Join McLaren

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 19, 2026

Max Verstappen made a surprise announcement at the Belgian Grand Prix this weekend: he has signed Dries Van Langendonck — a Belgian Formula 4 driver and McLaren junior — to his personal management and sponsorship company, Verstappen Racing.

The announcement drew immediate attention, not for what it says about Van Langendonck specifically, but for what it might say about Verstappen himself.

The Details

Van Langendonck, who competes in British Formula 4, will remain under McLaren's official junior driver development program. Verstappen Racing's role is one of mentorship and financial support — not a team switch or formal transfer of contractual rights. Verstappen framed it as a way to support a talented young Belgian driver as he works through the junior ranks.

"He stays a McLaren junior," Verstappen clarified publicly. "I'm just helping him along the way."

On the surface, the arrangement is relatively straightforward. High-profile drivers occasionally support young prospects in junior formulas — it is a form of giving back to the sport that has become more common in recent years.

The Timing, However

The timing of this announcement is where the eyebrows go up. Multiple sources within the Formula 1 paddock have told outlets that Verstappen's discussions with McLaren about a 2027 seat are in the closing stages. His exit clause with Red Bull Racing is now mathematically active. He has until October to formally pull the trigger.

Verstappen choosing this particular moment — at his home race weekend, in the paddock at Spa, while exit clause conversations dominate coverage — to announce a formal connection to McLaren's junior ecosystem is, at minimum, notable. Formula 1 is a sport where nothing happens without multiple layers of calculation, and the optics of Verstappen Racing and McLaren's junior program now sharing a driver's name are not subtle.

A Calculated Signal?

Verstappen Racing's announcement could be read as a goodwill gesture toward McLaren at a moment when Verstappen needs the team to want him as much as he wants them. Or it could be entirely coincidental — a mentorship deal struck on its own merit for a promising young driver who happens to be part of McLaren's program.

What it is not, by any account, is a formal confirmation of Verstappen's future. His manager Raymond Vermeulen has maintained publicly that Verstappen intends to honor his Red Bull contract. Red Bull, for their part, has said nothing publicly to suggest otherwise.

But the narrative around Verstappen's 2027 destination has been building since Silverstone. This announcement is one more brick in that wall.

What's Next

Any formal announcement about Verstappen's 2027 plans is expected in the autumn, if the exit clause is activated. In the meantime, Van Langendonck continues his British Formula 4 campaign under the dual support of McLaren's development program and Verstappen Racing's mentorship. Watch for further Verstappen/McLaren news through the summer break and into September.
