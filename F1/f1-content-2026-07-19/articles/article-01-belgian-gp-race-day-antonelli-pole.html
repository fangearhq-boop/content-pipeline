Belgian GP Race Day: Antonelli Leads From Pole as Championship Battle Reaches Spa

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | July 19, 2026

The 2026 Belgian Grand Prix is here — and if you needed a reason to care about a mid-July Formula 1 race, Kimi Antonelli just handed you one.

The Mercedes driver lines up on pole position at Spa-Francorchamps after qualifying for Saturday's session in 1:44.361 — the fastest time of the weekend by 0.317 seconds over Max Verstappen, his largest pole advantage of the season. It was, by multiple accounts, an exhilarating lap through the Ardennes forest.

The Championship Picture

Antonelli carries 179 championship points into today's race. His nearest rival, Mercedes teammate George Russell, starts P3 on the grid and holds 154 points — a deficit of 25. Lewis Hamilton, now at Ferrari, starts P5 and sits 32 points behind the leader.

This means two things simultaneously: Antonelli is comfortably ahead, and every point in Belgium is contested by teammates or rivals who are just close enough to make a charge meaningful. A mistake at the front today could reset the championship conversation entirely. The same is true of the race order among Russell, Hamilton, and Charles Leclerc (P4).

It is the kind of race where the finishing order matters more than the margin — because the next major shake-up could come from the middle of the pack as much as the front row.

Norris and the Recovery Challenge

Defending world champion Lando Norris starts 13th. The McLaren driver — who qualified P3 on raw pace — drops into the midfield after a 10-place penalty for exceeding the permitted electronics unit allocation. It is the sub-plot that runs throughout today's race: how far can the fastest team's fastest driver work back through the field at one of the sport's great overtaking circuits?

Spa-Francorchamps has historically been kind to drivers with genuine speed. The Kemmel Straight, the run from Raidillon to Les Combes — these are places where a fast car can create opportunities that narrower circuits simply do not allow. McLaren made a calculated decision to take this penalty here rather than at Hungary or the Netherlands. The hope is that the gamble pays off through the afternoon.

The Grid as It Stands

Beyond the headline names, today's grid has been thoroughly rearranged by penalties. Fernando Alonso starts last — 22nd — after a 20-place drop for exceeding multiple component allocations in his Aston Martin. Isack Hadjar (Red Bull) and Lance Stroll (Aston Martin) each lose 10 places. Gabriel Bortoleto in the Audi starts eighth, a reflection of continued steady progress from the Hinwil-based team.

For a race where so many headline drivers start away from their natural grid positions, the opening lap at La Source and Raidillon will be particularly chaotic. Position changes will happen early and often.

What's Next

The Belgian Grand Prix is the final race before the summer break. A victory for Antonelli today would send the championship leader into the break with significant breathing room. For Norris, any points scored from P13 are a bonus; for Russell and Hamilton, the race represents a final chance to close the gap before August. Watch the closing stages for the championship standings impact — that is today's real story.
