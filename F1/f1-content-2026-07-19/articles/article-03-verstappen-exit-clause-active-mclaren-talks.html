Verstappen's Exit Clause Is Now Active — and McLaren Talks Are "In the Closing Stages"

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | July 19, 2026

The Formula 1 paddock has been talking about Max Verstappen's future for months. Now the conversation has entered its most serious phase yet.

Verstappen's contract with Red Bull Racing contains a performance-based exit clause: if he is outside the top two in the Drivers' Championship at the summer break, he is entitled to leave the team. After his retirement at the British Grand Prix — where a rear-wing failure on Lap 48 at Silverstone ended another race he had been running competitively — the mathematics made one thing unavoidable. He cannot reach second place in the championship before the break. The clause is active.

How It Became Mathematically Certain

Verstappen is currently approximately seventh in the Drivers' Championship, roughly 78 points behind second place. With fewer than 50 points available in the remaining race before the summer break, there is no path to second place. The Silverstone retirement — his second rear-wing failure in eight days, following an incident in Austrian qualifying — removed the final mathematical possibility.

His public frustration with Red Bull's car has been building. After Silverstone, Verstappen spoke openly about being "fed up" with the aerodynamic instability of the 2026 Red Bull — comments that, in the context of the exit clause, landed differently than they might have before the clause was active.

McLaren Talks: "Closing Stages"

Multiple paddock sources have told PlanetF1 and other outlets that Verstappen's conversations with McLaren about a 2027 drive are "in the closing stages." These reports align with earlier signals: Verstappen has been among the most complimentary voices about McLaren's 2026 car in casual paddock conversation, and the team's trajectory — building from midfield in 2023 to championship contender in 2025 and beyond — is exactly the kind of project that would attract a driver of his calibre.

McLaren currently carries Norris and Piastri on its 2026 lineup — both signed on long-term contracts. How a Verstappen move would be accommodated remains unclear. One possible explanation: Verstappen's announcement this weekend that he has signed McLaren junior driver Dries Van Langendonck to his personal management company may be a signal that his connection to McLaren's organizational ecosystem is being formalized ahead of a larger announcement.

What Vermeulen Says

Raymond Vermeulen, Verstappen's manager, has publicly downplayed reports of an imminent departure. He has stated that Verstappen "wants to see out" his Red Bull contract, and that the activation of the exit clause does not automatically mean it will be triggered. That much is accurate — Verstappen has until October 2026 to formally notify Red Bull of his intention to leave.

But in Formula 1, the gap between a manager's public statement and a driver's private deliberations is often considerable. The clause is active. The talks are reportedly ongoing. And Verstappen, who has built his career on demanding to compete with the best equipment available, has been vocal this season about the Red Bull not meeting that standard.

What's Next

Watch for any announcement regarding the 2027 driver market through the summer break and into August. If Verstappen formally activates his exit clause, the announcement would likely come in late summer or early autumn. McLaren would then need to address its 2027 lineup publicly. This is the biggest driver story in Formula 1 this season — and it is approaching its resolution.
