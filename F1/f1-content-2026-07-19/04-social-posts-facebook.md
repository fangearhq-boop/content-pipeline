### STORY 2: Norris P13 Recovery Drive

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Lando Norris goes into the Belgian Grand Prix as the defending Formula 1 world champion. Today, he starts P13.

That painful disconnect stems from a 10-place grid penalty applied after qualifying, triggered when McLaren gave Norris a fourth control electronics unit — one beyond the permitted season allocation. Despite qualifying P3 on pace, he drops to 13th on Sunday's grid.

McLaren made a calculated decision: if they had to take the penalty this season, Belgium was the best option. Spa-Francorchamps is famous for its overtaking opportunities — from La Source down to Raidillon, through Les Combes, and down the Kemmel Straight. Any other circuit in the coming weeks would have made a recovery even harder.

Norris told the media his approach going into the race: "Wait and see." That understated response reflects both the challenge ahead and the pace McLaren has shown all weekend. The car is fast enough — the question is whether 13 places can be recovered in a single afternoon at Spa.

It will be worth watching where Norris finishes. That number will tell you as much about McLaren's race-day performance as anything else on the grid.

### STORY 3: Verstappen Exit Clause Active

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM ET

The Max Verstappen-Red Bull situation has entered a new phase — and it's more serious than his manager would like to admit.

Verstappen's contract contains a performance clause that allows him to leave the team if he is outside the top two in the Formula 1 championship at the summer break. After retiring from the British Grand Prix at Silverstone — where his Red Bull suffered a rear-wing failure on Lap 48 while running competitively — the mathematics made the clause unavoidable. It was Verstappen's second rear-wing failure in eight days (the first occurred in Austrian qualifying). He is currently approximately 78 points behind second place in the championship with fewer than 50 points available before the break. The clause is active.

He has until October 2026 to formally trigger it.

His manager, Raymond Vermeulen, has publicly said Verstappen wants to "see out" his Red Bull contract. But multiple paddock sources say talks with McLaren for a 2027 seat are "in the closing stages." The timing — and Verstappen's public frustration with Red Bull's car performance — suggests the exit clause conversation is not merely hypothetical.

Where Verstappen drives in 2027 may be the biggest story of the F1 season. And that story is close to being written.
