### STORY 4: FIA Energy Management Vote April 20

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM ET

```
⚙️ THE VOTE THAT COULD RESHAPE F1'S 2026 SEASON HAPPENS ON MONDAY ⚙️

On April 20, the FIA holds a high-level meeting with team principals, F1 management, and power unit manufacturers to vote on changes to 2026's most controversial regulation: energy management.

The key proposals on the table:

1. SUPER CLIPPING: Increase the limit from 250kW to 350kW. Currently, drivers who use "super clipping" — a technique of lifting partially while keeping the ERS harvesting — are penalized with a lower power limit than drivers who use conventional "lift and coast." This has led to dangerous, unpredictable throttle behavior in corners. Equalizing the limits reduces those risks.

2. QUALIFYING ENERGY TARGET: Cut the qualifying energy recovery limit from around 8-9 MJ to 6 MJ, as was trialed at the Japanese Grand Prix in Suzuka. This reduces the gap between cars that are brilliant at energy management and those that struggle, making qualifying more about raw pace.

3. ADUO REFINEMENTS: Adjustments to the Automatic DRS / Undercut Override system that several teams have been exploiting for competitive advantage.

The Bahrain Bearman crash — where a high-speed incident was linked to energy management tactics — has strengthened the FIA's resolve to act quickly. If Monday's meeting results in consensus, an e-vote will follow within days, with ratification by the FIA World Motor Sport Council to follow.

If all goes to plan, these changes go live at the Miami Grand Prix on May 1. The competitive landscape could look very different on May 1 compared to what we saw in the season's first three races.
```
