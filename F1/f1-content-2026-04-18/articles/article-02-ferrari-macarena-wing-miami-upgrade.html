Ferrari's "Macarena" Wing Returns: How the Revised Rear Wing Could Unlock Ferrari's Miami Comeback

By Marcus Cole | F1 Fanrecap | April 18, 2026

The most innovative piece of active aerodynamics in F1 2026 is getting a critical redesign. Ferrari's private Monza test on April 22 is the last hurdle before Miami — and the title race may hinge on what Lewis Hamilton and Charles Leclerc find there.

There are standard F1 upgrades, and then there is the Ferrari SF-26's Macarena rear wing. The concept, named by team principal Fred Vasseur because it mimics the dance move, does something no Formula 1 rear wing has done before: when activated, the upper flap rotates completely upside down, radically altering the aerodynamic profile of the car to maximize straight-line speed.

Ferrari first tested the wing during pre-season testing in Bahrain, where it generated significant interest from rivals and media alike. Both Lewis Hamilton and Charles Leclerc deployed it briefly in China — and immediately encountered a problem. When braking while turning, the wing's active position created rear instability that neither driver could manage comfortably. The concept was bold; the execution needed refinement.

That refinement is now complete, according to multiple technical sources. Ferrari have developed a revised version of the Macarena wing that addresses the China stability issues while preserving — and potentially expanding — the aerodynamic advantages. The revised wing is estimated to deliver approximately 6 kW of additional power compared to a conventional setup, with corresponding improvements in top speed and energy distribution efficiency.

The Monza Test: April 22

Before the wing makes its race debut at the Miami Grand Prix on May 1, Ferrari have arranged a private filming day at the Autodromo Nazionale Monza on April 22. Both Hamilton and Leclerc are expected to drive, testing the revised aerodynamic package and updated hybrid management systems ahead of the Miami race.

The choice of Monza is deliberate. While the Autodromo might initially seem counterintuitive for a Miami preparation — the two circuits look nothing alike — the Italian temple of speed offers uniquely demanding energy recovery characteristics. The circuit's long straights require maximum ERS deployment while its braking zones provide intense harvesting opportunities, closely mirroring the energy management demands of the Miami International Autodrome.

Ferrari's SF-26 Miami upgrade package extends beyond the wing itself. The package includes a revised floor design that traces its origins to development work done in Bahrain, modifications targeting overall weight savings, and cooling configurations optimized for Miami's heat and humidity.

The Championship Context

Ferrari arrives at the Miami Grand Prix 45 points behind Mercedes in the constructors' championship — a significant deficit after just three rounds. Mercedes have dominated the 2026 season to date, with Kimi Antonelli (72 points) and George Russell (63 points) occupying the top two places in the drivers' championship. Hamilton sits fourth with 41 points, and Leclerc third with 49.

The Macarena wing's potential performance impact is directly relevant to closing that gap. If the revised wing delivers the expected power gains and allows Hamilton and Leclerc to run closer to their natural pace in both qualifying and the race, Ferrari's upgrade package for Miami could shift the competitive order meaningfully. Ferrari's engineering department believes the SF-26 is fundamentally capable of challenging the Mercedes W16 — the wing has been the primary variable between theoretical pace and actual results.

What's Next

April 22 at Monza is the critical day. If the revised Macarena wing performs as Ferrari's simulations suggest, Hamilton and Leclerc will arrive at Miami with genuine confidence that the gap to Mercedes can be closed. If problems emerge, the team faces a difficult choice between deploying an unproven upgrade or reverting to the conventional setup that kept them behind through the season's first three rounds.

Ferrari's title hunt in 2026 doesn't begin or end at Monza on April 22. But the outcome of that filming day will go a long way toward determining whether Miami is a turning point or another frustrating near-miss.
