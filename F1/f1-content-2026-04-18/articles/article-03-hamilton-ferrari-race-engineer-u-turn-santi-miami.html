Ferrari's Engineer U-Turn: Hamilton Warns of "Detrimental" Disruption as Carlo Santi Stays for Miami

By Elena Voss | F1 Fanrecap | April 18, 2026

Ferrari reversed course on Lewis Hamilton's race engineer transition, with Carlo Santi confirmed to remain on the pit wall at Miami instead of the expected Cédric Michel-Grosjean. Hamilton is not hiding his frustration.

Lewis Hamilton came to Ferrari with championship expectations — a new team, a new chapter, and eventually a new set of support staff built around maximizing his final competitive years at the highest level of the sport. The engineer transition announced earlier this season was part of that plan: Cédric Michel-Grosjean, an experienced technical operator who spent years at McLaren, was set to take over from Carlo Santi as Hamilton's race engineer at the Miami Grand Prix.

That plan has now been reversed. Ferrari confirmed in the build-up to Miami that Santi will remain in the race engineer role for at least the near future, with no official date set for when Michel-Grosjean will actually step into the position.

The reason is practical but inconvenient: Michel-Grosjean is still on gardening leave following his departure from McLaren. Until that period expires, he cannot formally take up his new responsibilities at Ferrari. Ferrari chose to keep Santi in place rather than introduce a temporary interim arrangement or rush an unplanned transition.

Hamilton's Candid Reaction

Hamilton's public response was measured but pointed. "It's actually quite a difficult period," he told journalists, "because it [Santi's appointment] is not long term. The solution that I currently have, it's only going to be a few races. So early on into the season, it's going to be switching up again and I'll have to learn to work with someone new — so that's detrimental to me."

The frustration behind those words is understandable. Building a productive race engineer relationship requires time, trust, and a shared working language built across sessions, race weekends, and debriefs. Hamilton and Carlo Santi have that — they have been working together since Hamilton's arrival at Ferrari in 2025. But if Hamilton knows that relationship is temporary, every investment in it carries an expiration date.

Mid-season transitions in working relationships are not uncommon in F1 — engineers move between roles regularly, and teams adapt. But they are rarely welcomed by the driver, particularly one who is already navigating a new team environment and fighting to close a 31-point gap to the championship leader.

Who Is Carlo Santi?

Carlo Santi's career at Ferrari is longer than most fans realize. He served as Kimi Räikkönen's race engineer during the Finn's second tenure at the Maranello team, a period that included some of Ferrari's most competitive — and ultimately unsuccessful — championship challenges against Lewis Hamilton himself at Mercedes. After Räikkönen's departure, Santi moved into a factory-based engineering role before being called back to the pit wall for the 2026 season.

Santi is respected within the paddock as a calm, methodical operator — qualities that complement Hamilton's own technical demands from an engineer. Whatever Hamilton's frustrations about the arrangement's temporary nature, the relationship itself appears functional.

What's Next

The immediate focus is Miami, where Hamilton needs a strong result to arrest his drift in the championship standings. He arrives fourth in the drivers' standings with 41 points, 31 behind leader Kimi Antonelli. Ferrari's Macarena wing upgrade offers hope that the SF-26's performance ceiling is higher than its first-three-race results have suggested.

The Cédric Michel-Grosjean handover will happen eventually — Ferrari have not abandoned that plan, merely delayed it. But at a team where the gap to Mercedes already defines the competitive challenge, the last thing Hamilton needs is additional uncertainty in the people and processes around him. "Detrimental" is a strong word for a driver who chose his words carefully throughout his seven-championship career at Mercedes. Ferrari would do well to resolve this situation quickly.
