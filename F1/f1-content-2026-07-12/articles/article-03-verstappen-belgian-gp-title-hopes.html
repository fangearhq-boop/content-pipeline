Verstappen's Last Real Window — Belgian and Hungarian GPs Must Deliver Before Summer Break

By Marcus Cole | July 12, 2026

F1 / Max Verstappen

Max Verstappen is 103 points behind Kimi Antonelli in the 2026 Formula 1 Drivers' Championship. He has two races left before the summer break. And he needs both to go well.

The Belgian Grand Prix on July 19 and the Hungarian Grand Prix on July 26 represent Verstappen's clearest opportunity to reshape the championship narrative before August's pause gives teams time to regroup and restrategize. If neither race delivers meaningful points gains, the conversation heading into the second half of the season becomes uncomfortable for Red Bull and their four-time world champion.

The Math That Matters

At 103 points behind Antonelli with 11 rounds remaining after Belgium, Verstappen needs to average nearly 10 more points per race than the Mercedes driver across the rest of the season just to close the gap. That requires winning races while Antonelli finishes fifth or lower — repeatedly.

The exit clauses in Verstappen's Red Bull contract add another dimension. Those clauses were reported to trigger if Verstappen cannot realistically finish in the top two of the Drivers' Championship. The precise threshold has never been officially confirmed, but the mathematics of his current position make the top-two conversation more abstract with each passing race.

Belgium and Hungary are the benchmark. How Verstappen performs in the double-header before summer will determine whether the exit clause discussion remains theoretical or becomes concrete when teams reconvene for the second half of the season.

Red Bull's Recent Signs of Life

The Austrian Grand Prix offered something Red Bull had been missing for much of 2026: genuine pace. Verstappen finished second behind Russell at the Red Bull Ring — a home race with additional atmospheric significance — and the margin to the leaders was narrower than in previous rounds.

Whether that performance translated specifically to Red Bull's car development or to Verstappen's familiarity with a circuit he has dominated historically, the Austrian result suggests the car is not permanently disadvantaged. The question is whether Spa's very different character — the long, fast corners, the elevation changes, the likely wet conditions — suits the Red Bull package in the same way the Ring's medium-speed layout does.

Verstappen's record at Spa is historically excellent. He has won there multiple times. The track complexity suits his driving style, and his comfort in wet conditions is well established. If Red Bull has solved some of their earlier pace deficit, Spa is a reasonable place to expect that improvement to show.

The Horner Connection

The dynamic between Verstappen and former Red Bull team principal Christian Horner — who was dismissed after the 2025 British Grand Prix — continues to play in the background of the 2026 season. Reports have confirmed that Verstappen and Horner speak regularly, on a near-weekly basis. Horner attended the 2026 Silverstone race, his first appearance at a grand prix since his firing.

Horner's presence at Silverstone was noted by the paddock. His comments since leaving Red Bull have included statements that he would return to Formula 1 "only on my terms," suggesting ambition to re-enter the sport in a leadership capacity somewhere. How that future might involve Verstappen is speculation — but the relationship is clearly active, and its context matters when considering how Verstappen thinks about his options.

What's Next

Belgium is the immediate test. A podium result — ideally a victory — alongside a difficult weekend for Antonelli would reshape the mathematics and the narrative simultaneously. Even a second-place finish, if accompanied by Antonelli dropping points, could restore some sense of contest to what has otherwise been a one-car championship over most of the season.

The summer break comes after Hungary. If Verstappen enters that pause having gained meaningful ground, Red Bull and their driver have something to build on. If he doesn't, the second half of the 2026 season becomes a different kind of story entirely.
