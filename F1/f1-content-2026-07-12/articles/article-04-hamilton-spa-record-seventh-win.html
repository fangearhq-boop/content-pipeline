Hamilton at Spa: Ferrari Star Targets Record-Extending Seventh Belgian GP Win With Rain in Forecast

By Elena Voss | July 12, 2026

F1 / Lewis Hamilton / Ferrari

No active Formula 1 driver has won the Belgian Grand Prix more often than Lewis Hamilton. His six victories at Spa-Francorchamps represent one of the most remarkable driver-circuit relationships in the sport's modern era.

When Formula 1 returns to Belgium on July 17-19, Hamilton arrives in a very different situation from most of those previous wins — he drives for Ferrari, not Mercedes — but the circuit, the conditions, and the championship stakes make this one of the most compelling Belgian GP entries of his career.

Six Wins, One History

Hamilton's relationship with Spa began with his McLaren years and deepened throughout his Mercedes era. Wins in 2010, 2015, 2017, 2020, 2021 (before the race was later controversially abbreviated), and across other seasons built a record that no current competitor approaches.

What makes the Spa victories significant isn't just the number — it's the variety of circumstances. Hamilton has won there in the wet, in the dry, from pole, from mid-grid after misfortune. The circuit demands something specific: commitment at high speed, precision through the changes in elevation and grip, and the ability to read conditions as they shift lap by lap. Hamilton has demonstrated all of those qualities at Spa more consistently than anyone else still competing.

A seventh win would extend a record that already stands alone. At 39 years old, still competing at the front of the championship, Hamilton's ability to mark Spa as uniquely his would be a statement about longevity and circuit-specific mastery that transcends any single season.

Ferrari's Position in the Fight

Hamilton sits third in the Drivers' Championship at 147 points, 32 behind leader Antonelli. Charles Leclerc's Silverstone win on July 5 was Ferrari's best result of the season — and it came at a circuit where pace gaps between manufacturers had previously favored Mercedes.

Leclerc's victory demonstrated that Ferrari can not only compete at the front but win. For Hamilton, who joined the team ahead of the 2026 season, the Silverstone result validates the competitive environment he chose when he made his unexpected move from Mercedes to Maranello.

In the constructors' standings, Ferrari is approximately 78 points behind Mercedes with 11 rounds remaining after Belgium. Closing that gap requires both cars scoring at the top level consistently — which means Hamilton's individual ambitions and the team's collective goals are tightly aligned heading into Spa.

Rain as a Variable

The forecast for the Belgian GP weekend projects thundery showers across all three days, with highs around 23 degrees Celsius. Changeable and wet conditions at Spa create a race environment where driver skill in marginal conditions becomes a primary differentiator.

Hamilton's reputation in wet-weather driving is among the most respected in the sport's history. His wet performances have included some of the most celebrated drives of the past two decades, starting with his debut victory at the 2007 European Grand Prix in Monaco — a race in the rain that announced his talent on the global stage — and continuing through multiple occasions where chaotic conditions produced extraordinary results.

For a Ferrari team that is still finding its footing at the front of the grid in the new regulations, rain could be the great equalizer that elevates Hamilton's performance relative to the Mercedes pair even in a car that isn't the quickest in dry conditions.

What's Next

The race starts Sunday, July 19 at 15:00 CEST. Hamilton will need to qualify well — Spa's long first sector, beginning with the Eau Rouge-Raidillon complex, rewards cars with strong downforce and power delivery, and grid position matters more than at circuits with easier overtaking opportunities.

Six Belgian GP wins. Rain in the forecast. A championship fight with 32 points to close. Hamilton at Spa is always worth watching. This version of that equation may be the most interesting one yet.
