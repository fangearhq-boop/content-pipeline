How the 2026 Belgian GP Was Won: Antonelli's Dominant Spa and the Moments That Defined It

By Elena Voss

July 20, 2026

The Belgian Grand Prix at Spa-Francorchamps is always one of the most unpredictable races on the Formula 1 calendar. In 2026, it delivered. From a chaotic opening lap to a pivotal VSC moment mid-race, the Belgian GP produced the kind of strategy and drama that makes Spa a permanent fixture on the sport's most celebrated circuits.

Here is how it unfolded.

Qualifying Set the Stage

Kimi Antonelli set the tone on Saturday with a pole lap of 1:44.361 — the largest pole margin of the 2026 season. Mercedes had genuine pace at Spa, and qualifying confirmed it. Antonelli had the measure of the field before a single racing lap had been completed.

The grid was complicated by penalties. Lando Norris started 13th after receiving a 10-place penalty for using his fourth electronics unit of the season — a technical infringement that cost McLaren a front-row contender. Lance Stroll, Isack Hadjar, and Fernando Alonso all served penalties of their own, starting further back than their pace might have suggested.

The Opening Lap

Spa's long Kemmel Straight and the following Les Combes braking zone is one of the most demanding sequences in Formula 1. It rewards commitment and punishes hesitation. On lap one of the 2026 Belgian GP, it caught out George Russell.

Russell, experiencing a power deployment issue that compromised his braking confidence, moved to the outside of Hamilton on approach to the chicane. Contact was made. Russell went into the gravel and out of the race on the spot. Hamilton received a 5-second penalty from the stewards but continued racing.

The incident was consequential beyond just Belgium. Russell's retirement handed Antonelli a 50-point championship lead over the man who had been fighting him hardest for the title.

The VSC Window

The decisive strategic moment of the race came mid-event under a Virtual Safety Car deployment. Ferrari brought Charles Leclerc in for his pit stop during the VSC window — a move designed to minimize time lost on pit road while the field is moving at controlled speeds. It briefly elevated Leclerc to the nominal race lead.

Mercedes responded quickly, pitting Antonelli in a subsequent VSC period and executing the stop faster than Ferrari's. When racing resumed and cycles resolved, Antonelli was back ahead. Leclerc held second but had ceded the position he had briefly appeared to have.

The VSC sequence illustrated one of Spa's defining tactical realities: timing is everything, and the team with faster pit stop execution can negate even an aggressive strategic call from a rival.

What's Next

Formula 1 now enters the summer break with the championship standings locked at Antonelli's 45-point advantage. When the season resumes, teams will carry forward the lessons from Spa: Mercedes' dominant pace, Ferrari's strategic aggression, and the dangers that Kemmel's braking zone presents when things go wrong.

Spa delivered everything it typically does — speed, drama, consequence. The 2026 Belgian GP will be studied as the race that may have defined this season, even with races still to come.
