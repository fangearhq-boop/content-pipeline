Antonelli Wins Belgian Grand Prix, Extends Championship Lead to 45 Points

By Marcus Cole

July 20, 2026

Kimi Antonelli drove a flawless race from pole position to take victory at the 2026 Belgian Grand Prix at Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps. The 19-year-old Mercedes driver led from start to finish, capitalizing on a chaotic opening lap that eliminated his most immediate championship rival and a mid-race VSC window that shuffled the order in his favor.

Charles Leclerc finished second for Ferrari after briefly leading on strategy before the Virtual Safety Car pit cycle swung the advantage back to Antonelli. Max Verstappen completed the podium in third. Lewis Hamilton, who received a 5-second time penalty for first-lap contact with his Mercedes teammate George Russell, was classified fourth. Oscar Piastri of McLaren rounded out the top five.

The Belgian GP result extended Antonelli's Formula 1 championship lead to 45 points over Hamilton, with Russell now 50 points in arrears after his retirement on lap one.

A Perfect Weekend for Mercedes' Young Star

Antonelli's dominant pole position on Saturday — a lap of 1:44.361 that represented the largest pole margin of the 2026 season — had already underscored Mercedes' pace advantage at Spa. Sunday confirmed it.

From the front of the grid, Antonelli had the luxury of avoiding the first-lap chaos that eliminated Russell and compromised Hamilton. While his championship rivals were dealing with incidents and penalties, Antonelli simply drove. He managed the gap, executed his pit stop, and emerged cleanly when the VSC disrupted Ferrari's strategy.

The race was, in that sense, emblematic of Antonelli's 2026 season. He is rarely flashy or dramatic. He executes. He converts qualifying form into race points. He avoids the incidents that punish those around him. For a 19-year-old in his first championship season, the composure has been the most striking element of his rise.

Leclerc Loses the Lead He Never Quite Had

Charles Leclerc spent a portion of the Belgian GP leading the race on paper — the result of Ferrari executing a pit stop during the Virtual Safety Car window while Antonelli was still out on track. It was an aggressive call, and it gave Leclerc a brief nominal lead.

It did not last. Mercedes responded immediately to the VSC, and when pit cycles resolved, Antonelli was back ahead. Leclerc finished second and took valuable championship points, but he also took a hard lesson: at Spa in 2026, Antonelli had too much pace to be beaten by strategy alone.

Leclerc now sits fourth in the standings, 78 points behind Antonelli. The mathematical challenge facing Ferrari is steep.

What's Next

The summer break follows the Belgian Grand Prix, and Antonelli enters it with a commanding 45-point lead. The gap to Russell and Hamilton is real — and with Russell taking zero points in Belgium, the trajectory of the 2026 championship is pointing in one direction.

Antonelli will resume racing after the break as the clear favorite for his first Formula 1 world title. The question is no longer whether the young Mercedes driver can win the championship — it is whether anyone has the pace and the consistency to stop him.
