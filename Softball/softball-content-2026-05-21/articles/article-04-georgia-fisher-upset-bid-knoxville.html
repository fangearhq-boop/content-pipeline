Addisen Fisher Has Georgia Believing — Bulldogs Enter Knoxville With a Real Upset Chance

By Jordan Reeves

May 21, 2026

Georgia's pitcher threw a complete-game shutout in the Regional Final. Now she takes that form into one of college softball's most electric environments tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN2.

The setting tonight favors Tennessee. The seedings favor Tennessee. The home crowd, the pitching matchup record, the momentum of a program that has hosted four consecutive Super Regionals — all of it points in one direction.

And Addisen Fisher doesn't care.

Georgia's pitcher delivered one of the cleaner performances of the entire regional round in the game that mattered most: a complete-game shutout, 5-0, against Clemson in the Regional Final. On the biggest stage of her season, when Georgia's tournament life was on the line, Fisher was composed and dominant. That is not the profile of a pitcher who is going to shrink in Knoxville's Sherri Parker Lee Stadium.

What the Regional Final Showed

Complete-game shutouts in elimination games are not routine. A pitcher who can go all seven innings without allowing a run while her team's season depends on every pitch has proven something about her competitive makeup that statistics do not fully capture.

Fisher held Clemson — a program that reached the Regional Final for a reason — to zero runs across all seven innings. That was not a weak opponent on a bad day. It was Clemson, competing in the final game of their season, trying to extend their program's run.

Georgia (40-18) has earned their spot in Knoxville. Fisher's performance is why.

The Knoxville Problem for Georgia

Tennessee at home in a Super Regional is one of the more challenging environments in college softball. Sherri Parker Lee Stadium is sold out tonight. The Lady Vols are 45-10, operating from the confidence of a program that has been in this position before — four consecutive times, in fact. Karlyn Pickens, Tennessee's ace, is as capable as any pitcher in the country at this level.

Road wins in Super Regionals against superior-seeded home teams happen. They happen because softball, like all sports, plays games rather than awarding victories based on paper credentials. Georgia has beaten programs with better records in 2026. Their 40 wins came against real competition.

Fisher gives Georgia something concrete to build a game plan around: if she pitches the way she pitched against Clemson, the Bulldogs can keep any game close enough to win.

Why Georgia Is a Legitimate Threat

Upsets in the Super Regional round do not require miracles. They require one pitcher having a great game, one offense finding timely hits, and one team believing that the favorite's record doesn't determine the outcome of a specific game on a specific night.

Georgia has the pitcher. They have the belief — the Regional Final performance proved that. What happens tonight in Knoxville is a test of whether the Bulldogs can carry that energy into the most intimidating environment they've faced this season.

Tennessee is favored. Tennessee should probably be favored. But Fisher's arm makes Georgia's path to an upset real, not theoretical.

What's Next

Game 1 is Thursday, May 21 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 from Sherri Parker Lee Stadium in Knoxville. Game 2 is Saturday at 3 PM ET on ESPN. A potential Game 3 follows. The winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. If Fisher pitches tonight the way she pitched in the Regional Final, Georgia will make this series go at least to Game 2.
