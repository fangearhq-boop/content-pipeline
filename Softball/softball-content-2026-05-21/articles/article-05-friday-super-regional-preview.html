Six Games, One Day: Your Complete Preview of Friday's Super Regional Openers

By Sarah Mitchell

May 21, 2026

Thursday starts the Super Regionals with two games. Friday detonates with six simultaneously — the biggest single day of the NCAA softball tournament outside the WCWS itself.

Thursday night brings Tennessee-Georgia at 7 PM ET and Nebraska-Oklahoma State at 9 PM ET. Two games. Two great storylines.

Friday delivers six Super Regional openers across the country, all of them carrying the kind of drama that only the tournament's second round can generate. Here is everything you need to know about the biggest day in college softball.

11 AM ET — Florida vs. Texas Tech (ESPN2)

Florida (51-10) opens the day at Katie Seashole Pressly Stadium in Gainesville against Texas Tech (55-6) — the nation's winningest program. The Gators have gone 36-1 in home NCAA games since 2013, a 22-game home winning streak that represents the most dominant home-field advantage in college softball. They have beaten Texas Tech four times already this season, twice in February and twice in the Gainesville Regional last weekend.

Texas Tech's 55 wins are not a mirage. The Red Raiders are excellent. But they walk into a stadium where Florida has beaten almost everyone who has tried, and where familiarity with the opposing team is already in the Gators' favor.

11 AM ET — Arkansas vs. Duke (ESPNU)

The most loaded narrative of the morning slate: Arkansas (No. 5 seed, 45-11) has been to five Super Regionals under coach Deifel and has never reached the WCWS. Duke (No. 12 seed, 43-15) is making its fourth Super Regional appearance in five years, arriving after surviving an elimination-game gauntlet in the regional. Arkansas hosts at Bogle Park in Fayetteville.

The pressure here sits almost entirely on Arkansas. Duke has nothing to lose and a track record of performing in tournament environments. If Arkansas doesn't advance this year, the conversation about a program that can win regionals but can't win Super Regionals grows louder.

1 PM ET — Oklahoma vs. Mississippi State (ESPN2)

This is the game the sport is watching. Oklahoma (48-9) hosts Mississippi State with Kendall Wells at 37 home runs — tied with Laura Espinoza's 1995 all-time NCAA single-season record. One home run separates Wells from sole ownership of the most storied individual record in college softball.

Miss State's pitchers face the most challenging assignment in Friday's slate. Challenge Wells and risk becoming an answer to a trivia question. Pitch around her and give Oklahoma's lineup a steady diet of walks. There is no good option. Wells is batting close to .380 and plays for one of the nation's top offenses.

7 PM ET — Alabama vs. LSU (ESPN2)

No. 1 Alabama (52-7) hosts No. 16 LSU (40-17) in Tuscaloosa in the evening's marquee matchup. Alabama's pitching depth — led by Vic Moten, who was near-perfect in the regional, and Briski — is the deepest in the tournament field. LSU outscored regional opponents 23-2, meaning they arrive with genuine offensive momentum.

These programs did not meet in the regular season, which makes this a true first look at each other in 2026. Alabama is the heavier favorite, but LSU's regional performance says they are not arriving as a statistic.

9 PM ET — UCF vs. UCLA (ESPNU) and Texas vs. Arizona State (ESPN2)

UCF (No. 11 seed) travels to Los Angeles to face No. 8 UCLA after one of the regional round's best stories: the Knights upset No. 9 FSU 4-2 in the Tallahassee Regional Final — the program's first-ever NCAA win over Florida State — and went unbeaten in the regional. It is UCF's second-ever Super Regional appearance. The road trip to Los Angeles doesn't appear to have bothered their confidence.

Texas (No. 2 seed, 45-10) hosts Arizona State (No. 15 seed) at McCombs Field in Austin, chasing an eighth consecutive Super Regional appearance. Arizona State makes their first Super Regional since 2022, having swept their way through a regional that included a 9-1 run-rule over Texas A&M.

What's Next

All six Friday series continue Saturday with Game 2. A potential Game 3 for all series would also be Saturday. The eight Super Regional winners advance to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, with opening day on May 28. By Sunday night, the WCWS field will be set.
