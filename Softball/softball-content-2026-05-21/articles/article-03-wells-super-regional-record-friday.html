Kendall Wells Enters the Super Regional Stage One Home Run From NCAA History

By Emily Rawlings

May 21, 2026

With 37 home runs tied to the 1995 all-time NCAA record, Wells and Oklahoma open Super Regional play Friday against Mississippi State. The question is no longer if she breaks it — it's when.

Thirty-one years ago, Laura Espinoza of Arizona hit 37 home runs in a single season and set a record that has stood untouched through every era of the sport's growth. No one has matched it. Some came close. Most didn't get within ten.

Kendall Wells has 37 home runs.

The Oklahoma freshman has spent the 2026 season dismantling every significant home run record in college softball, and now she steps into the biggest stage of the year with the most historic mark in front of her. Oklahoma opens Super Regional play against Mississippi State on Friday, May 22, at 1 PM ET on ESPN2. Every Wells at-bat in Norman this weekend is an event unto itself.

The Record That Has Waited Since 1995

The numbers that define Wells' season are not just impressive — they are extraordinary in the context of the sport's history. She broke the NCAA freshman home run record at 31. She broke the SEC single-season home run record at 27. She broke the Oklahoma program record at 35, surpassing Jocelyn Alo — one of the most decorated power hitters in college softball history.

And then she tied Espinoza's mark at 37.

The all-time record is not just the next milestone in a long list. It is the final one — the one that, if Wells breaks it, stands alone as the signature achievement of one of the most remarkable freshman seasons any sport has produced. College softball has never had a player accumulate this kind of home run total while also batting close to .380 and playing for a team with legitimate national title aspirations.

The Mississippi State Problem

Mississippi State's pitching staff faces something that has no clean solution. Kendall Wells is too dangerous to challenge carelessly. Walk her too freely and Oklahoma's lineup continues to get quality at-bats — the Sooners have one of the nation's top offenses well beyond Wells alone. Challenge her and accept the possibility of becoming the program that gave up home run No. 38.

There is no great answer. There never is when the best hitter in the sport is in the box.

What Miss State can control is the quality of their execution. Keep the ball down, work the edges, and hope Wells has one of those rare games where the barrel doesn't find the ball. It happens. Wells has had games without home runs. This is still college softball, not a highlight reel on a loop.

But the Oklahoma lineup punishes any mistakes. And the Super Regional stage, with a sold-out crowd in Norman and the record in plain view, is not the environment where Mississippi State will get Wells' worst day.

What's Next

Oklahoma hosts Mississippi State in Game 1 on Friday, May 22 at 1 PM ET on ESPN2. Game 2 is Saturday, May 23. A potential Game 3 would also be Saturday. The winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. Wells needs one home run to own the all-time NCAA single-season record outright. The entire sport is watching Norman this weekend.
