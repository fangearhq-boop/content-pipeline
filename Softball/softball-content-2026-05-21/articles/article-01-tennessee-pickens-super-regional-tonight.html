Tennessee Makes History Tonight: Four Consecutive Super Regional Hosts, Karlyn Pickens in the Circle

By Jordan Reeves

May 21, 2026

No program in NCAA softball history has ever hosted four straight Super Regionals. Tennessee does it tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 — and Sherri Parker Lee Stadium is sold out.

There is a first time for everything in college softball. And Thursday night in Knoxville, Tennessee claims one that nobody has claimed before.

The Lady Vols open the Super Regional round against Georgia at Sherri Parker Lee Stadium at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 — their fourth consecutive home Super Regional. No program in the history of the NCAA tournament has hosted four straight. Tennessee has never done it before. Nobody has.

They're doing it tonight, in front of a sold-out crowd, with ace pitcher Karlyn Pickens ready for the biggest start of the college softball season.

A Program in Full Flight

Tennessee enters the Super Regional at 45-10, one of the better records in the field. The Lady Vols have been consistent throughout the 2026 season — a program that has built itself into an elite postseason presence under sustained investment and recruiting depth.

Four consecutive Super Regional hosts is not an accident. It is the product of a program that wins enough regular season games to earn hosting rights, then wins enough postseason games to keep coming back. Tennessee has done both, and now they have the distinction that goes with it: unprecedented consecutive Super Regional hosting.

The 2026 Super Regional against Georgia is not just another home game. It carries the weight of a streak that no program has ever maintained.

What Georgia Brings to Knoxville

The Bulldogs arrive at 40-18 and carry momentum from a dominant Regional Final performance. Georgia pitcher Addisen Fisher threw a complete-game shutout — 5-0 against Clemson — in the final game of the regional. A shutout in a winner-take-all game, on the road to a Super Regional, does not happen by accident. Fisher is capable of silencing offenses, and Tennessee's offense knows it.

Georgia is not a team that arrived in Knoxville hoping to compete. They arrived expecting to. Fisher's performance in the Regional Final gives the Bulldogs a legitimate claim to belief, and that belief is the most dangerous thing a visiting team can carry into a sold-out venue.

Tennessee is the favorite. That is clear from the seedings, the records, and the home-field advantage that the Lady Vols have built over years of postseason play. But Georgia's Fisher is the kind of pitcher who can make favorites uncomfortable, and one great game from her makes this series as competitive as any in the Super Regional round.

The Crowd Factor

College softball home-crowd advantages are real, and Sherri Parker Lee Stadium amplifies them. A sold-out building behind the home team is worth something in a best-of-three series — not because it changes the physical game, but because the energy it provides to the home team and the pressure it places on the visiting team are genuine factors in tight moments.

Tennessee has played in this environment. Georgia will be playing in it for the first time this season. The Lady Vols know exactly what a raucous Knoxville crowd feels like in the late innings of a close game. The Bulldogs will find out tonight.

What's Next

Game 1 is Thursday, May 21 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 from Knoxville. Game 2 is Saturday at 3 PM ET on ESPN. A potential Game 3 would also be Saturday. The winner of the Tennessee-Georgia Super Regional advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, which begins May 28. Tennessee's historic Super Regional hosting streak either extends into WCWS history tonight, or Georgia ends it in Knoxville with Fisher on the mound.
