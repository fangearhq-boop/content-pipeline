Nebraska's Night Has Finally Come: Cornhuskers Open First-Ever Super Regional Tonight

By Sarah Mitchell

May 21, 2026

After 13 years away from the WCWS and a 49-6 season that broke program records, Nebraska opens its first-ever home Super Regional against Oklahoma State at 9 PM ET on ESPN2.

Nebraska softball has not been to the Women's College World Series since 2013. That is 13 years of trying, building, and coming just short of the moment the program has been working toward.

Thursday night in Lincoln, that drought either ends — or it gets one step closer to ending.

The Cornhuskers open their first-ever home Super Regional against Oklahoma State at Bob Devaney Sports Center at 9 PM ET on ESPN2. The building is sold out. The 2026 Nebraska softball team is 49-6, a program record in wins. And Jordy Frahm — a Lincoln native who grew up dreaming of nights like this — is ready to pitch in front of the crowd that has always been hers.

A Program Record Season

Nebraska's 49-6 record entering the Super Regional is not a fluke of scheduling or a soft conference. The Cornhuskers dominated the Big Ten with a 20-1 conference record, built a 13-game win streak late in the season, and allowed just one run across their entire regional — one run in three games.

That last number is striking. Oklahoma State arrived in their regional and scored in double digits in every game. Nebraska held their opponents so thoroughly that the gap between the two teams' regional performances could not be wider. The Cornhuskers are not just a feel-good story. They are one of the best teams in college softball.

They just haven't gotten to show it at this level in 13 years.

Frahm vs. Neylan: A Nebraska Story Within the Story

College softball occasionally produces matchups that carry their own separate narrative entirely. Thursday night in Lincoln delivers one of those.

Jordy Frahm, Nebraska's ace, is a Lincoln native. She grew up in this city, became the centerpiece of this program, and now takes the mound for the biggest game in Nebraska softball history — at home, in Lincoln, for a crowd that has watched her grow up.

On the other side of the game: Ruby Neylan, Oklahoma State's pitcher. Neylan is from Omaha. She and Frahm were travel ball teammates once — two young players from Nebraska who went different directions and built careers at different programs. They haven't faced each other at this level before. Tonight they do, on the biggest stage either has ever stood on.

There is no better plotline in the Super Regional round.

Oklahoma State Is Not Just a Prop

It would be easy to frame this entire story as Nebraska's night, Nebraska's stage, Nebraska's moment — and that framing would miss something important. Oklahoma State came to Lincoln having scored in double digits in every game of their regional. That is an offense capable of blowing up any pitcher's plans.

Neylan is a capable pitcher who helped Oklahoma State get here. The Cowboys are not in Lincoln to serve as a backdrop for Nebraska's homecoming. They are here to win a Super Regional and reach OKC themselves.

What makes tonight great is that both of those things are true at once: this is Nebraska's night AND Oklahoma State is a legitimate threat. The tension between those two facts is what sports is for.

What's Next

Game 1 is Thursday, May 21 at 9 PM ET on ESPN2 from Lincoln. Game 2 is Friday. A potential Game 3 would be Saturday. The winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. For Nebraska, winning tonight would put them one step closer to ending 13 years of waiting. Jordy Frahm gets the ball. Lincoln gets its night.
