**Long-Form Post**
🌽 THIRTEEN YEARS. TONIGHT IT ENDS. 🌽

Nebraska softball hasn't been to the Women's College World Series since 2013.

That drought ends this season — or it doesn't. And the answer starts tonight.

The Cornhuskers (49-6) host their first-ever Super Regional at Bob Devaney Sports Center in Lincoln. The building is sold out. The city has waited over a decade for this. Nebraska allowed just one run in the entire regional — that's dominant, methodical, program-defining softball.

And then there's the pitching matchup.

Jordy Frahm, a Lincoln native who grew up dreaming of this, is on the mound for Nebraska. She faces Ruby Neylan of Oklahoma State. Neylan is from Omaha. They were travel ball teammates once — two Nebraska kids who went separate directions and now find themselves on opposite sides of the most significant night in Cornhusker softball history.

Oklahoma State scored in double digits in all three of their regional games. They are not a pushover. But this Nebraska team hasn't lost more than six games all season, and tonight they play in front of a crowd that has poured its entire heart into this program.

13 years. One night. 9 PM ET on ESPN2.

### STORY 3: Wells Enters Super Regional Stage — HR No. 38 Could Come Friday

**Long-Form Post**
🥎 ONE SWING FROM FOREVER — ROUND TWO 🥎

The record has been tied for a week. Oklahoma's Kendall Wells is sitting at 37 home runs — tied with Laura Espinoza of Arizona, who set the all-time NCAA single-season record in 1995.

Thirty-one years. That mark has stood untouched.

One home run separates Wells from owning it outright.

And now the stage just got bigger.

Oklahoma hosts Mississippi State in the Super Regional starting Friday, May 22 at 1 PM ET on ESPN2. Every at-bat in Norman this weekend carries history. Every pitch that Wells sees, the Mississippi State pitching staff knows exactly what's on the line — for Wells, for the record, and for themselves if they're the ones who served up No. 38.

How do you pitch to someone like that? You can try to walk her, but Oklahoma's offense is too dangerous to let that go on for a series. You can attack the zone and hope for the best. There's no great answer.

Wells isn't chasing the record. She's chasing the WCWS. The record is what happens when you're the most dangerous hitter in college softball playing at the highest level of the sport.

No. 38 could come Friday. Or Saturday. Or maybe not this weekend at all.

Every at-bat is must-watch. That's just Kendall Wells.

Tennessee is the favorite. That's real. But tonight is a baseball game — played by human beings, on one specific night, with momentum and preparation and matchup factors that don't show up in a season record.

Georgia believes they can win. Addisen Fisher gives them a real reason to.
