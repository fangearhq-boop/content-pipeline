25 and Counting: Kendall Wells Ties the SEC Freshman Home Run Record

By I Love Softball Staff March 29, 2026

The Milestone That Keeps Moving

In the first week of March, the conversation around Kendall Wells was about when she'd hit her 20th home run. Three weeks later, she's at 25 — tied for the most home runs ever hit by a freshman in SEC history. The Oklahoma catcher has turned record-chasing into a weekly ritual, and with more than 20 games still left on the schedule, the question isn't whether she'll break the all-time NCAA freshman record. It's by how much.

Wells leads the nation in home runs by a significant margin. Through 33 games, her 25 long balls represent a pace that no freshman in the history of the sport has ever sustained for a full season. The all-time NCAA freshman home run record stands at 30, shared by three legends: Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma), Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma), and Kaitlyn Majam. Wells needs five more to join that company — and she's still two months from the end of the regular season.

What Makes Wells Different

Oklahoma head coach Patty Gasso has seen elite power hitters throughout her legendary career — Jocelyn Alo alone hit 122 home runs at Oklahoma, including the all-time NCAA record of 52 in a single season. But even Gasso has offered a candid assessment of what Wells brings: "This is something new, this is different. It is some of the most elite power I've ever seen from a young player."

What makes Wells's production even more striking is the level of competition she's doing it against. These are not mid-majors she's teeing off on. Her home run totals have climbed through SEC play, which features the best pitching in the country. Her six home runs and 13 RBI at the Mary Nutter Collegiate Classic in February earned her both D1Softball National Player of the Week and Softball America Freshman of the Week honors — and that was only the beginning.

Oklahoma's Record-Setting Power

Wells hasn't been operating in isolation. Oklahoma as a team broke the SEC single-season home run record this year, reaching 122 or more long balls in just 32 games — shattering the previous conference record. The Sooners have used their power to run up dominant margins, and their team approach to offense allows Wells to see better pitches in the lineup. Still, no one can take credit for 25 individual home runs except the person swinging the bat.

LSU held Wells without a home run in Game 2 on Saturday, one of the few times in the past month when she's been shut down by an opposing staff. That only heightens the drama for Sunday's rubber match — Wells coming off a homerless outing against the same team, in the same ballpark, with a series on the line.

What's Next

Watch Wells closely in the Game 3 rubber match against LSU today. After going without a home run in the series's second game, she will be locked in. If Wells continues her current pace, the all-time freshman record of 30 should fall sometime in April — making the conversation around the 2026 season's most compelling individual story only more electric as the postseason approaches.
