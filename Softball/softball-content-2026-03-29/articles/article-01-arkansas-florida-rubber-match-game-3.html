Winner-Take-All: Arkansas and Florida Meet in Game 3 After Wild Series Split

By I Love Softball Staff March 29, 2026

A Series Split That Demanded a Game 3

College softball's best rivalry game of the weekend wasn't the one anyone predicted — it was the one that unfolded across three days in Fayetteville. Arkansas and Florida split the first two games of their SEC series in dramatic fashion, setting up a winner-take-all Game 3 at Bogle Park on Sunday afternoon.

In Game 1 on Friday, Robyn Herron dealt against No. 3 Florida, holding the Gators to season-low totals while Reagan Johnson sealed the performance with a record-breaking RBI single. Arkansas won 6-2. Florida's ace Keagan Rothrock (17-3 on the year) took the loss — only her third of the season — and the Gators were held to just three hits. It was a dominant performance that immediately reshuffled the national conversation.

Florida's Bounce-Back and the Rubber Match Setup

Game 2 was a different story. Florida erupted for 12 runs on Saturday, hitting three home runs and scoring in every inning but the first. Arkansas starter Payton Burnham couldn't find her footing early, and the Gators ran up the score to 7-1 before the Razorbacks mounted a rally. Dakota Kennedy delivered a key two-run double to cut the deficit to 7-6 in the sixth inning, but Florida added five more in the seventh to close out a 12-6 final. Series tied, one-all, heading to Sunday.

The rubber match goes at 1:00 PM CT at Bogle Park before what is expected to be a packed, electric crowd. The stakes are simple: win and own a massive series victory; lose and absorb a significant hit to the WCWS resume heading into April.

The Power Shift No One Can Ignore

What makes this series feel like more than just three games is the historical context. Arkansas has won eight of the last 10 meetings against Florida dating back to 2022. The all-time series still heavily favors Florida at 52-19, but the recent trend tells a different story — one of a program that has genuinely closed the gap with the SEC's elite programs.

Arkansas entered the weekend at 29-5 overall with a 4-4 SEC record, while Florida came in as the No. 3 team in the country at 32-2 overall and 5-3 in conference. That conference record for Florida is worth noting — the Gators have already dropped three SEC games, meaning today's rubber match carries more weight for their postseason seeding than it might appear. For Arkansas, a series win over Florida would be among the program's biggest regular-season results in years.

What's Next

Game 3 at Bogle Park today will go a long way toward determining the narrative surrounding both programs entering April. A Florida win keeps their WCWS path on solid ground and demonstrates that their Game 2 offensive outburst was no fluke. An Arkansas win cements their standing as a legitimate SEC contender and hands them a signature result against one of the nation's top programs. The SEC standings and the national top 25 could look noticeably different by Sunday evening.
