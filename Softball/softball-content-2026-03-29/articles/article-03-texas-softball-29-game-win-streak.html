Texas Clinches the Lone Star Showdown — Win Streak at 29 and Counting

By I Love Softball Staff March 29, 2026

Atwood's Bat Powers Another Close Win

Reese Atwood hit a solo home run and went 2-for-3 with two RBI on Saturday as No. 1 Texas edged Texas A&M 3-2 at McCombs Field, clinching the Lone Star Showdown series 2-0. The win pushed the Longhorns' winning streak to 29 consecutive games and their overall record to 31-1 — the best mark in college softball.

Viviana Martinez also shined in the win, per the Daily Texan's game coverage. Texas A&M's Sydney Lessentine threw a complete game for the Aggies (now 9-3 on the season), striking out six and allowing only five hits and two earned runs — and still lost. That's a testament to how efficient Texas has been in manufacturing runs when they need them.

The Anatomy of a Historic Streak

The numbers surrounding Texas' streak are staggering. The Longhorns are 31-1 overall with an 8-0 record in SEC play — the only undefeated team in conference action. The previous program record for consecutive wins was 24 games, set back in 2006. Texas surpassed that mark in mid-March and has continued adding to it with a combination of dominant performances and grind-it-out wins like Saturday's 3-2 victory.

The Lone Star Showdown series illustrated both sides of Texas' championship profile. Game 1 on Friday was a chaotic 9-8 win over No. 15 Texas A&M that required a walk-off. Game 2 was tighter and more methodical — pitching and timely hitting carrying the day. The ability to win different types of games is what separates great teams from truly elite ones, and Texas has demonstrated that quality repeatedly this season.

The Defense of a Championship

Texas enters Sunday as the defending national champions, having won their first Women's College World Series title in 2025. This team is built to repeat. They're playing for another national title, and the first 31 games of the 2026 season suggest they are squarely on track.

What's Next

Texas plays Game 3 against Texas A&M today, then returns to a demanding conference schedule as the SEC season intensifies in April. The national picture is clear: until someone beats Texas, the Longhorns are the team to beat heading toward the WCWS in June. Win No. 30 today would make that statement louder than ever.
