Emilee Boyer's Complete Game Stuns No. 4 Tennessee, 2-1

By I Love Softball Staff March 29, 2026

Boyer Delivers the Biggest Game of the Season

No. 4 Tennessee came to Sherri Parker Lee Stadium on Friday carrying one of the most talented rosters in college softball. They left with a loss. Emilee Boyer, Ole Miss's pitcher, went the full seven innings on 132 pitches, allowing just one run in a 2-1 Rebels victory that counts among the most surprising results of the 2026 season.

Kennedy Bunker hit the deciding blow — a solo home run that gave Ole Miss a lead they would never relinquish. Boyer's job from there was to hold on, and she did it convincingly. Tennessee's offense, built to punish even elite pitching, couldn't manufacture another run against a pitcher from a program that entered the game 0-9 in SEC play.

Tennessee's Alarm Bells Are Ringing

Tennessee entered 2026 as a genuine WCWS favorite — loaded with returning talent from a program that has made two Women's College World Series appearances in the last four years. The pitching staff remains one of the best in the country, featuring two-time first-team All-SEC ace Karlyn Pickens, Sage Mardjetko (who posted a 2.50 ERA with 118 strikeouts in 95.1 innings in 2025), and emerging sophomore Erin Nuwer. That's a rotation most programs could only dream of building.

But Tennessee's offense has been inconsistent at key moments during SEC play, and the loss to Ole Miss — a team that had not won a single conference game entering Friday — exposed that vulnerability in the most direct way possible. Dropping to 6-3 in SEC play with the conference schedule heating up means the Lady Vols are no longer in control of their own seeding narrative. Every remaining SEC series carries extra weight.

What This Means for the National Picture

Tennessee's loss will register in next week's NFCA poll and could push them down from their No. 4 ranking. Beyond the rankings, the real concern is whether the offense can find consistency before the postseason. Boyer's complete-game effort on 132 pitches was remarkable, but Tennessee's inability to score more than one run against any pitcher — regardless of the opponent — is a pattern worth monitoring.

Ole Miss, despite their 0-9 SEC record, now has a signature result to show what their pitching staff can do on a given day. Boyer's performance will be remembered regardless of how the season ends for the Rebels. For the right team on the right day, she is clearly capable of shutting down a top-5 lineup.

What's Next

Tennessee will need to respond forcefully in the remainder of their SEC schedule to protect their postseason seeding. The Lady Vols still have elite pitching on their side — but the offense must step up to match it, or the program's WCWS aspirations will continue to feel uncertain. Heading into April, each remaining series takes on the urgency of a must-win situation for Tennessee's RPI and national seed.
