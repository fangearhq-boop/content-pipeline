2026 SEC Softball Tournament Preview — Bracket, Schedule, and What to Watch

By Emily Rawlings | May 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

The most competitive conference tournament in college softball begins Tuesday at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky. The 2026 SEC Softball Tournament features 14 teams, four automatic qualifier contenders, and at least one freshman slugger who could break a 31-year-old record in front of a national audience. Here is everything you need to know before first pitch.

The Double-Bye Teams to Know

The top four seeds all earned double byes and will not take the field until Thursday's quarterfinals. That quartet represents the deepest set of double-bye seeds the SEC has produced in recent memory.

No. 1 Oklahoma (20-4 SEC) is the clear favorite. The Sooners won their 14th consecutive SEC regular season title, enter with the nation's most dangerous lineup, and have Kendall Wells — who is one home run from tying the all-time NCAA single-season record — in the middle of the order. The Sooners play the winner of LSU vs. Georgia on Thursday.

No. 2 Alabama (19-5 SEC) finished as one of the most consistent teams in the conference after a stretch-run push following a midseason stumble against Tennessee. The Crimson Tide have the pitching and the offense to win the tournament outright.

No. 3 Florida (17-7 SEC) enters having closed the regular season strong. The Gators are dangerous and battle-tested from a full slate of challenging SEC competition. A healthy Florida team is a threat to anyone in this bracket.

No. 4 Texas (16-8 SEC) rounds out the double-bye group. The Longhorns have shown flashes of the kind of pitching that could limit even Oklahoma's offense. Getting to the quarterfinals on Thursday without burning pitching early is a significant advantage.

First-Round Storylines (Tuesday and Wednesday)

Seeds 5 through 14 open action Tuesday and Wednesday, with the winners advancing to set up Thursday's quarterfinal matchups. LSU (No. 8 seed) plays one of the more anticipated early games — the winner earns the right to face Oklahoma Thursday. A&M, fresh off pushing the No. 1 Sooners to three games in the regular season finale, also enters as a threat to make a run from the lower half of the bracket.

Seeds 9 through 14 have the longest road — they must win two games in two days just to reach the quarterfinals. But in single-elimination tournament play, any hot pitcher can change the equation quickly.

The Wells Factor

No preview of this tournament would be complete without acknowledging what Kendall Wells means to the storyline. The Oklahoma freshman enters Thursday with 36 home runs and needs one more to tie Lauren Espinoza's all-time NCAA record (37, Arizona, 1995). Every OU game this week will have national audience eyes tracking her at-bats in real time. That kind of individual drama has not been part of the SEC Tournament in many years.

What's Next

First-round games begin Tuesday, May 5 at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, with games airing on the SEC Network. Quarterfinals run Thursday, semifinals Friday, and the championship game is Saturday, May 9. The SEC champion earns an automatic bid to the NCAA Tournament. The NCAA Selection Show airs May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2, setting the full 64-team bracket with 16 national seeds hosting Regionals beginning May 15. The Women's College World Series runs May 28–June 3 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City.
