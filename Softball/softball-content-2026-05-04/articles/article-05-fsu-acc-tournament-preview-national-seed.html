Florida State Heads to Charlottesville as No. 1 ACC Seed — National Seed Push Begins

By Emily Rawlings | May 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

Florida State has spent the 2026 season making its case as one of college softball's elite programs. This week in Charlottesville, the Seminoles get the chance to make it undeniable.

No. 1 Florida State (45-8, 20-3 ACC) enters the 2026 ACC Softball Tournament (May 6-9, Palmer Park, Charlottesville, VA) as the top seed after clinching a share of the 19th ACC regular season title in program history. A tournament championship would not only add a trophy to the case — it would give the Selection Show committee one more reason to place the Seminoles among the top eight national seeds on May 10.

What Makes FSU a National Seed Contender

Florida State's combination of offensive production, pitching depth, and coaching experience makes the Seminoles dangerous on any stage. Isa Torres has been one of the most productive offensive players in the ACC all season — a versatile threat who creates problems for opposing pitchers with both power and contact ability. The FSU pitching staff has logged quality starts throughout conference play, maintaining a sub-par ERA against a demanding ACC schedule.

At 45-8, FSU owns one of the most impressive overall records in the country. The concern for the Selection Show committee has always been strength of schedule and how the Seminoles perform in high-leverage games late in the season. A deep ACC Tournament run — especially a championship — answers those questions definitively.

ACC Tournament Structure

The 2026 ACC Softball Championship opens Wednesday, May 6 at Palmer Park in Charlottesville, Virginia. The first game of the tournament features Notre Dame vs. Georgia Tech at 11 AM ET. Florida State, as the No. 1 seed, has a bye through the first round and will play Thursday in the quarterfinals — the second consecutive day of games at Palmer Park.

The championship game is Saturday, May 9 at 2:30 PM ET. All tournament games air on the ACC Network and ESPN.

FSU has won 19 ACC regular season titles in its history, but the tournament crown is a separate measure of postseason readiness. Head coach Lonni Alameda has navigated the Seminoles to the national stage before, and this year's team has the talent to make another run.

The Bigger Picture

The ACC is not the SEC or the Big Ten in terms of depth, but FSU has proven it belongs in conversations with the nation's best programs. A 20-3 conference record in one of the sport's historically competitive leagues is a genuine achievement. The Seminoles' path to Devon Park runs through Charlottesville this week, then through Regionals and Super Regionals after the May 10 Selection Show.

If FSU wins the ACC Tournament and earns a national seed, the Seminoles could host the first two rounds at JoAnne Graf Field in Tallahassee — a home advantage that would mean a great deal heading into the postseason.

What's Next

Florida State's first game of the ACC Tournament is Thursday at Palmer Park in Charlottesville, Virginia, with first pitch time to be announced on the ACC Network. The championship game is Saturday, May 9 at 2:30 PM ET. The ACC champion earns an automatic NCAA Tournament bid. The NCAA Selection Show airs May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. Regionals begin May 15. The WCWS runs May 28–June 3 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City.
