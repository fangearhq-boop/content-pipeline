Kendall Wells Enters SEC Tournament at 36 HRs — One From Tying the All-Time NCAA Record

By Sarah Mitchell | May 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

The 2026 regular season is done. Kendall Wells is not.

The Oklahoma freshman slugger closed out one of the most remarkable seasons in college softball history with 36 home runs — one short of tying Lauren Espinoza's all-time NCAA single-season record of 37, set at the University of Arizona in 1995. Now Wells gets the SEC Tournament to chase the final milestone that has eluded her thus far, and the most watched at-bat in college softball begins Thursday in Lexington, Kentucky.

The Record That Has Stood for 31 Years

Espinoza's 37 home runs in 1995 have outlasted decades of challengers. No player in Division I softball has come this close in the modern era. Wells, who entered the 2026 season as a highly recruited prospect, has dismantled every benchmark in her path: she broke the NCAA freshman home run record with her 31st homer in April, passed Jocelyn Alo's Oklahoma program record with No. 35, and set the SEC single-season record with her 27th. Each one fell in order. The all-time mark is next.

The only question was always whether she would reach it before the postseason. The answer, as of Sunday: not quite, but close enough that every SEC Tournament game will carry extra electricity.

No. 1 Oklahoma — The Perfect Stage

Oklahoma enters the SEC Tournament as the No. 1 overall seed, having won the SEC regular season title for the 14th consecutive year. Head coach Patty Gasso's Sooners posted a 20-4 conference record and hold the top line of the bracket — along with a double bye. Oklahoma does not play until Thursday, when the Sooners face the winner of the LSU vs. Georgia quarterfinal at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington.

That means Wells gets at least one game — potentially several — to reach the record. The SEC Tournament runs through Saturday, May 9, with the championship game that afternoon. The Selection Show follows on May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2.

For Oklahoma, winning the SEC Tournament would add to an already exceptional season and strengthen a national seed case that is already among the strongest in the country. For Wells, every at-bat from Thursday forward will be watched by an audience hungry for history.

What the Numbers Mean

Thirty-six home runs in a single season means Wells has hit a home run every 2.6 at-bats on average. She finishes the regular season leading the nation in home runs, RBIs, and slugging percentage. Among the records she has already broken, the SEC single-season mark required only 27 home runs — Wells surpassed that by nine. The NCAA freshman record required 30 — she went six beyond. Each time, she cleared the bar by a margin that surprised even the most optimistic observers.

Now she needs one. One home run, on any pitch, in any at-bat, in a tournament where Oklahoma is the favorite to make the final. The math has never been simpler. The moment has never been bigger.

What's Next

Oklahoma opens SEC Tournament play Thursday at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky. The Sooners' quarterfinal opponent will be determined by Tuesday and Wednesday's first-round games between seeds 5 through 14. Oklahoma is favored to reach the championship game on Saturday, May 9 — giving Wells multiple opportunities to add to her home run total and potentially rewrite the record books. Fans can follow SEC Tournament coverage on the SEC Network and ESPN. The NCAA Selection Show airs May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2, setting the full 64-team bracket for Regionals beginning May 15.
