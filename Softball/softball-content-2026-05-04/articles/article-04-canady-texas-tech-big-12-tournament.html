NiJaree Canady's Perfect Game Sends Texas Tech Into Big 12 Tournament on Top of the World

By Sarah Mitchell | May 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

NiJaree Canady saved the best for last.

The Texas Tech senior and one of the most decorated pitchers of her generation threw the first perfect game of her college career on Senior Day, shutting out Baylor 8-0 in five innings with 9 strikeouts on just 62 pitches. It was the sixth perfect game in Texas Tech program history, and it sent the Red Raiders into the 2026 Big 12 Tournament (May 7-9, Devon Park, OKC) as the No. 1 seed and the first program in the country to reach 50 wins this season.

A Perfect Farewell to the Regular Season

Canady entered Senior Day having already secured her place among the sport's all-time greats. She crossed the 1,000 career strikeout threshold earlier in the season — the first pitcher in Texas Tech history to reach that milestone — and finishes the regular season with a 21-4 record. But the perfect game was something new. Nine batters retired on nine at-bats across five innings. No baserunners. No close calls. Just Canady at the top of her craft, doing what she does better than almost anyone in the country.

For a senior who has given everything to the Texas Tech program over four seasons, the timing was fitting. The farewell gift she gave herself and her teammates was the performance of her career.

Texas Tech at 50-4 — The Red Raiders' Historic Season

The win pushed Texas Tech to 50-4, making the Red Raiders the first team in college softball to reach 50 victories in 2026. Back-to-back Big 12 regular season champions, Texas Tech has been one of the sport's two or three best programs all season. Only Oklahoma's No. 1 ranking and the Wells home run circus has kept the Red Raiders from dominating national headlines week after week.

The Big 12 Tournament at Devon Park — the same venue that hosts the Women's College World Series — gives Texas Tech its ideal stage. The Red Raiders are the No. 1 seed and open Thursday at 1:30 PM CT against No. 8 Baylor in a rematch of the Senior Day perfect game. The quarterfinals, semifinals (Friday), and championship (Saturday) all take place at the same facility where the WCWS will be held later this month.

Canady's Legacy

It is worth pausing to appreciate what Canady has built over four years in Lubbock. She arrived as one of the most heralded recruits in the sport's history, and she delivered. The career strikeout record. The dominant ERA figures year after year. The leadership role on a program that has won back-to-back Big 12 titles. Now, a perfect game to close the regular season chapter of her career.

Whatever happens in the Big 12 Tournament and beyond, Canady leaves Texas Tech as one of the greatest pitchers in program history. The postseason is just the exclamation point on a legacy that was already set.

What's Next

Texas Tech opens the Big 12 Tournament Thursday, May 7 at 1:30 PM CT at Devon Park in Oklahoma City against No. 8 Baylor. The quarterfinals are Thursday, the semifinals Friday, and the championship Saturday, May 9. The Big 12 champion earns an automatic NCAA Tournament bid. Texas Tech is a national seed contender — a tournament title would cement that case before the May 10 Selection Show at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. The WCWS runs May 28–June 3 at Devon Park, the same venue where the Red Raiders are playing this week.
