### STORY 3:

#### Long-Form Post -- 11:00 AM
Nebraska has been one of the best teams in college softball all season.

Now it's official.

The Huskers (42-6, 22-1 Big Ten) swept Penn State in a doubleheader Saturday to clinch the outright Big Ten regular season title — the first outright title in program history. It capped a season in which Nebraska won 13 consecutive games, dominated the Big Ten field, and positioned itself as a legitimate national seed contender.

Nebraska is the No. 1 seed in the Big Ten Tournament (May 6-9, College Park, MD). The Huskers have a first-round bye and will play Thursday at 1:30 PM ET against the winner of Michigan vs. Ohio State.

Jordy Frahm has been the anchor of the pitching staff, and the lineup has the power and the depth to make a run in May. A Big Ten Tournament title would only add to the case for hosting a Regional at Bowlin Stadium in Lincoln.

Nebraska. Historic. 🌽

### STORY 4:

#### Long-Form Post -- 5:30 PM
NiJaree Canady didn't leave anything on the table.

The Texas Tech senior — one of the most decorated pitchers in the history of the sport — threw the first perfect game of her college career on Senior Day, shutting out Baylor 8-0 in five innings. Nine strikeouts. Sixty-two pitches. Not a single Baylor baserunner reached base. It was the sixth perfect game in Texas Tech program history.

It was the perfect send-off to a regular season in which Canady crossed the 1,000 career strikeout threshold and led the Red Raiders to 50-4 — the first team in the country to reach 50 wins in 2026.

Texas Tech is the No. 1 seed in the Big 12 Softball Tournament (May 7-9, Devon Park, OKC) and enters as one of the top programs in the country. The Red Raiders open Thursday at 1:30 PM CT against No. 8 Baylor — a rematch of the Senior Day masterclass.

Canady has been dominant. The Red Raiders are dangerous. And Devon Park is the home of the WCWS.

Let's go. #WreckEm 🥎
