Oklahoma Earns No. 3 Seed, Hosts Norman Regional — Sooners Enter NCAA Tournament With Nation's Most Dangerous Offense

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | May 11, 2026

Oklahoma's .389 team batting average leads the nation entering the NCAA Tournament, where the No. 3 Sooners host Binghamton, Michigan, and Kansas in Norman beginning Friday.

No team in college softball hits the ball like Oklahoma.

The Sooners enter the 2026 NCAA Tournament as the No. 3 national seed with a .389 team batting average — the best in the country — and 174 home runs as a program. Oklahoma finished the season 48-8, won their second consecutive SEC regular season title with a 20-4 conference record, and earned the right to host the Norman Regional beginning Friday, May 15, at Love's Field.

The Norman Regional field: Oklahoma opens against Binghamton at 2:30 PM, with Michigan taking on Kansas at 5 PM in the other first-round game.

An Offense Built to Do Damage

The .389 team batting average isn't a typo. Oklahoma's lineup is built from top to bottom to punish pitching — and in a tournament where every game is elimination pressure, the Sooners' offensive depth is their greatest weapon. When a lineup hits .389 collectively, there is no easy out in the order. There is no spot where the opposing pitcher can exhale.

With 174 home runs on the season, Oklahoma has the power to end games in a single swing. Their offensive production reflects not just individual talent, but a systematic approach to hitting that carries through every plate appearance.

Opponents in Norman will face a lineup that can score from anywhere, at any moment.

Back-to-Back SEC Regular Season Titles

The No. 3 seed reflects more than just offensive numbers. Oklahoma's 20-4 conference record — good for their second consecutive SEC regular season championship — shows they can handle elite competition week in and week out. The SEC's gauntlet schedule includes multiple top-10 programs every week; surviving it at 20-4 is a significant accomplishment.

Oklahoma's 48-8 overall record places them among the sport's most consistent programs this season. They came into the Selection Show with a body of work that justified the top-three placement.

The Norman Regional at a Glance

Oklahoma's regional field — Binghamton, Michigan, and Kansas — includes programs that all earned their spots in the 64-team field, but the Sooners are the heavy favorite to advance. As the host and top seed, Oklahoma controls the schedule, the crowd, and the home-field advantage that matters deeply in early-round NCAA play.

If the Sooners play to their ability — which, given their offensive firepower, they're capable of doing almost every night — they should advance to the super regionals with relative ease. The real tests come after Norman.

What's Next

Oklahoma opens regional play Friday at 2:30 PM against Binghamton. The Norman Regional runs through Sunday, May 17. The regional champion advances to super regionals (May 22-24), hosted again on campus for the top seeds. Should Oklahoma advance through the super regional round, their destination is the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City (May 28-June 5) — a short drive from Norman that the Sooner fanbase has made many times before.

Oklahoma has been to Devon Park before. They know what it takes. This roster has the offense to get there again.
