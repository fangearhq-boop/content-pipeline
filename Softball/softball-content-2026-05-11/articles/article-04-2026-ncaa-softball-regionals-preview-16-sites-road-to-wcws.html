2026 NCAA Softball Regionals Preview — 16 Sites, All Play Starts Friday, Road to Devon Park Begins Now

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | May 11, 2026

The 2026 NCAA Softball Tournament opens Friday at 16 regional sites across the country, with all 64 teams chasing one destination: the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City.

Sixty-four teams. Sixteen sites. One destination.

The 2026 NCAA Division I Softball Tournament begins Friday, May 15, when all 16 regional sites open simultaneously. Over three days of double-elimination play, the field narrows from 64 to 16. Those 16 advance to super regionals. Then eight head to Devon Park in Oklahoma City for the Women's College World Series (May 28-June 5).

Here's what to know about the first weekend of tournament play.

A New Seeding Structure

This is the first NCAA Softball Tournament to seed the top 32 teams nationally in pods of four — a departure from previous formats. The top 16 national seeds host regionals, with the seeds grouped into four quadrants. National seeds 1 through 4 anchor each quadrant as No. 1 regional seeds. The structure is designed to reward résumé over geography while still maintaining some regional balance.

The result: the top programs in the country host their regionals at home, with significant advantages in crowd support, familiarity, and travel logistics.

The Top Four Regional Sites

The four top-seeded regional hosts set the anchor points for the bracket:

Tuscaloosa Regional (No. 1 Alabama): Alabama (49-7) opens against USC Upstate on Friday at 1 PM on the SEC Network. Belmont faces Southeastern Louisiana in the other first-round game (3:30 PM, ESPN+). The Crimson Tide are the No. 1 overall seed and heavy favorites to advance.

Austin Regional (No. 2 Texas): The SEC Tournament champion Longhorns (42-10) host Wagner (Friday, 3 PM, ESPN+). Wisconsin faces Baylor in the other first-round game (12:30 PM, ESPN+). Texas earned its first SEC title two days before the Selection Show; now they play for a regional title at home.

Norman Regional (No. 3 Oklahoma): Oklahoma (48-8) opens against Binghamton (2:30 PM). Michigan and Kansas play the other first-round game at 5 PM. The Sooners lead the nation with a .389 team batting average and are the most explosive offensive team in the bracket.

Lincoln Regional (No. 4 Nebraska): Nebraska (45-6) hosts at Bowlin Stadium. The Big Ten champions and the team that witnessed Megan Grant's all-time NCAA home run record enter the tournament with one of the most complete rosters in the country.

Other Regionals to Watch

Baton Rouge Regional (No. 16 LSU): LSU hosts Virginia Tech, South Alabama, and Akron. Virginia Tech — the team that pushed Florida State to a 2-1 final in the ACC title game — is a dangerous No. 2 seed in this regional. LSU will need to be sharp.

Athens Regional (No. 10 Georgia): Georgia hosts Clemson, UNC Greensboro, and the College of Charleston. This is one of the more open regionals in the bracket — Clemson enters as a program capable of an upset.

Stillwater Regional (Oklahoma State): Oklahoma and Oklahoma State both hosting NCAA regionals in the same state at the same time is a rare and exciting situation for the state's softball fans. OSU hosting in Stillwater gives the Cowgirls every opportunity.

Eugene Regional (Oregon): Oregon hosting the Eugene Regional gives the Ducks a home-field advantage that's difficult for any visiting team. The Pacific Northwest crowd brings energy to every game.

The Schedule at a Glance

All 16 regional sites open Friday, May 15, with games running through Sunday, May 17. Super regionals follow May 22-24, hosted on the campus of the higher-seeded team. The Women's College World Series runs May 28 through June 5 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City.

Sixteen champions will emerge from this weekend. Eight will make it to super regionals. Only eight will reach Devon Park — and only one will raise the national championship trophy.

What's Next

First pitch Friday, May 15. Watch across ESPN, ESPN2, ESPN+, and the SEC Network throughout the weekend. Regional winners advance to super regionals (May 22-24). The eight super regional winners head to the 2026 Women's College World Series at Devon Park, Oklahoma City, starting May 28. This is the tournament everyone in college softball has been building toward all season. It starts Friday.
