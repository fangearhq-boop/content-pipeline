Texas Earns No. 2 Seed, Hosts Austin Regional — SEC Champions Get Home-Field Reward for Historic Season

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | May 11, 2026

The Longhorns won the program's first SEC Tournament title Saturday, then learned Sunday they would play the opening NCAA regional at home in Austin as the No. 2 national seed.

Two days after making history, Texas gets to play in its own backyard.

The Longhorns earned the No. 2 national seed in the 2026 NCAA Softball Tournament and will host the Austin Regional beginning Friday, May 15 — a reward for a season that culminated in Texas's first-ever SEC Tournament championship. The regional field: Texas opens against Wagner at 3 PM on ESPN+, while Wisconsin faces Baylor at 12:30 PM on ESPN+.

Texas enters at 42-10, one of the strongest records in the country. The home crowd in Austin will be loud, and the Longhorns know their facility better than any of their regional opponents.

The No. 2 Seed After Winning the SEC

There's a mild irony in the seedings: Texas won the SEC Tournament, defeating Alabama 7-1 in Saturday's championship game — and still received the No. 2 slot while Alabama received No. 1. The committee made clear that Alabama's overall season body of work — including wins over two Top 10 opponents in the tournament before the title game — was sufficient to maintain the top seed.

For the Longhorns, the response is straightforward: the No. 2 seed still means home games. Texas will play in front of its own fans in Austin for all three days of regional play, carrying full home-field advantage through the first weekend. Whether the seed is No. 1 or No. 2, the Longhorns start in their own stadium — and that's a significant advantage.

The Austin Regional Field

Texas's path through the Austin Regional runs through a manageable regional field. Wagner is the first-round opponent — a program that earned their bid but enters the regional at a significant talent disadvantage against the No. 2 national seed. Wisconsin and Baylor play the other first-round game, with the survivor facing whichever team Texas plays on Saturday.

The Longhorns are heavy favorites in every scenario. The Austin Regional is Texas's to lose — and everything about this program's recent performance suggests they won't.

A Program Writing Its Own Chapter

Texas's 2026 season has already produced one historic moment: the program's first-ever SEC Tournament championship. Winning the conference title as a relatively recent SEC member — and doing it convincingly, with Teagan Kavan's 12-strikeout complete game and Katie Stewart's 3-for-5 performance — announced Texas as a legitimate national contender in its new league home.

Now the Longhorns enter the NCAA Tournament as the No. 2 seed, playing in front of a home crowd that knows they're watching something special. The Austin Regional is the next chapter. If Texas plays the way they've been playing, it won't be the last one written this season.

What's Next

Texas opens against Wagner on Friday, May 15 at 3 PM on ESPN+. The Austin Regional runs through Sunday, May 17. The regional champion advances to super regionals (May 22-24), hosted at the campus of the higher-seeded team. Should Texas advance past super regionals, their destination is the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City (May 28-June 5). The Longhorns have built a résumé worthy of that stage. Now they need to earn the trip.
