**Long-Form Post**

For Florida fans, the possibility of landing the No. 1 transfer would be a massive recruiting win heading into 2027.

With one year of eligibility remaining, Beachum brings immediate impact wherever she lands. She hits for power, draws walks at an elite rate, and plays premium defense at third base. Any program that lands her becomes a legitimate 2027 threat overnight.

Whatever happens in the next 24 hours — this has been a remarkable portal saga. And it ends tomorrow.

### STORY 3: FSU Adds Edmiaston — Second Portal Piece

**Long-Form Post**
Florida State lost two of the best players in program history to the transfer portal. Now they're putting the pieces back together.

The Seminoles have added former Stetson first baseman Nicole Edmiaston as their second portal piece of the 2026 window. She joins Tennessee transfer Ella Dodge — the No. 10 overall portal player and No. 4 middle infielder — who committed to FSU last week.

Let's put FSU's situation in context. Isa Torres, the D1 Softball Player of the Year with a .530 batting average, left for two-time defending champion Texas. Jaysoni Beachum, the No. 1 transfer and a .417 hitter, is still weighing her options with one day left in the portal. Those are massive losses for any program to absorb.

But head coach Lonni Alameda isn't starting from scratch. The Seminoles return strong core pieces and are adding six incoming freshmen alongside their two portal additions. Dodge gives FSU a two-year player with positional versatility and power (11 HR in 2026). Edmiaston adds depth and power at first base.

Is FSU an immediate contender in 2027? The losses are significant. But with Alameda at the helm and a recruitment class taking shape, the Seminoles are building toward something. This portal window is the foundation, not the finished product.

### STORY 4: Portal Winner Final Verdict

**Long-Form Post**
With one day left in the 2026 transfer portal, the verdict is nearly in. And it comes down to two programs — with a few others making strong cases.

**Texas** made the boldest moves of anyone. Adding D1 Softball Player of the Year Isa Torres (a Georgetown, TX native coming home) alongside Swan (All-Big 12 catcher from the same hometown), Lincoln, and Altmeyer gives two-time defending champion Texas arguably the most talented transfer class in college softball history. If Torres transitions seamlessly from FSU to Austin, the Longhorns are the prohibitive favorite for a three-peat.

**Texas Tech** made history of its own. The 61-8 runner-up assembled three All-Americans: Kenna Polar (.555 BA / 101 hits), Heavener (the nation's top-ranked pitcher), and Mackenzie Pickering. Softball America rated their class No. 1. The Red Raiders went from WCWS runner-up to potential title favorite in the span of two weeks.

**Arkansas** had a strong, efficient window. Landing Mountain West Freshman of the Year Madison Wetteland and UCF utility player Kendall Yarnell gives Dave Engel quality depth and versatility.

**South Carolina** closed strong. Saviya Morgan (Tennessee OF) and Bella Foran (Gold Glove OF) make the Gamecocks an improved team heading into 2027.

**Tennessee** played defense smartly — adding South Carolina second baseman Shelton while retaining senior pitcher Mardjetko.

**FSU** starts their rebuild with Ella Dodge and Nicole Edmiaston.

84 of 120 portal players are committed. One name — Beachum — remains. Whoever gets her claims the final headline of the 2026 portal.
