Beachum Watch — Day 14: The No. 1 Transfer Has One Day Left to Decide

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 21, 2026

Jaysoni Beachum has one day left.

The Softball America No. 1 rated transfer is entering the final 24 hours of her portal window, and the college softball world is holding its breath. The transfer portal closes Monday, June 22. Whatever Beachum decides, she must decide tomorrow — and every program still in pursuit knows it.

The Florida State junior finished the 2026 season with a .417 batting average, 16 home runs, 66 RBI, and a .549 on-base percentage — the third-highest in Seminoles program history. She drew 54 walks. She earned NFCA Second-Team All-American honors. And now she has visited the Florida Gators, the in-state rival that FSU fans have long considered the enemy.

The Rivalry Angle

Beachum is a Tallahassee native. She grew up in the shadow of FSU's program and played three years for the Seminoles. The idea of her crossing the state to join Florida — a program Seminoles fans have feuded with for decades — would be one of the most striking in-state flips in recent college softball memory.

She visited the Gators and the visit confirmed what the college softball community had suspected: Florida is in this to the end. Head coach Tim Walton's program has been building for 2027 and adding the No. 1 transfer at third base — where Kendall Grover's graduation left an immediate hole — would be a massive recruiting win.

For Beachum, the calculus is different. This isn't about rivalry. It's about opportunity, fit, and what a final season of college softball looks like. She has one year of eligibility remaining. She will compete one more time on the biggest stages. The question is which program gives her the best shot at a championship and the best environment to do it in.

What She Brings

Beachum's 2026 numbers speak for themselves. A .417 batting average from a junior who also draws walks at an elite rate (.549 OBP) is the kind of offensive profile that every program in the country wants. She hits for power — 16 home runs in 2026, 37 for her career — and she plays premium defense at third base.

Her career line: 37 home runs, 180 RBI, 202 hits. At 20 years old, she has produced like an elite power hitter for three seasons. Whoever lands her gets an immediate impact player who does not need time to adjust or develop.

At a program like Florida, she would step in on day one and anchor the lineup. At nearly any program in the country, she would do the same.

The Final Hours

The portal opened June 8. Today is Day 14 of 15. Out of roughly 120 players who entered the portal, 84 or more have already committed to new programs. The biggest names — Isa Torres (Texas), Kenna Polar (Texas Tech), Courtney Shelton (Tennessee) — have long since landed. Beachum is the last major domino.

Her decision doesn't just affect her destination program. It's the final headline of the 2026 portal, the last piece of a transfer cycle that reshaped the 2027 landscape for every program in college softball.

Whatever happens tomorrow, the wait is nearly over.
