Who Won the 2026 Transfer Portal? The Final Verdict With One Day Left

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 21, 2026

The 2026 transfer portal closes tomorrow. With 84 of roughly 120 players committed, the picture is clear enough to render a verdict — even with Jaysoni Beachum still outstanding.

Two programs stand above everyone else. Several others made the portal work for them. And a few name programs were left looking up at the teams that executed better during the 15-day window.

No. 1: Texas — The Generational Class

Two-time defending national champion Texas entered the portal with a mission: reload after losing key seniors and sustain a dynasty. They delivered on a scale that no reasonable observer anticipated.

Adding D1 Softball Player of the Year Isa Torres — a .530 hitter with a .978 slugging percentage and an NCAA-record 16 consecutive hits — alongside All-Big 12 catcher Taylor Swan (from the same Georgetown, Texas hometown as Torres), pitcher Lincoln, and Altmeyer gives the Longhorns arguably the most talent-dense portal class in college softball history. Head coach Mike White didn't just reload. He potentially upgraded an already loaded roster.

The path to a three-peat just got clearer for Texas. Rivals should be alarmed.

No. 2: Texas Tech — Three All-Americans

The 61-8 WCWS runner-up entered the portal with a chip on its shoulder and the resources to back it up. Texas Tech assembled a class that Softball America rated No. 1 overall — three All-Americans, each elite at their position.

Kenna Polar (.555 batting average / 101 hits / nation's top hitter in 2026) headlines the class. NiJaree Canady's college career is over, but Heavener — the nation's top-ranked pitcher — gives Texas Tech an ace replacement. Mackenzie Pickering, the On3 No. 2 overall transfer at the outset of the portal window, rounds out a class that made the Red Raiders look like a program built not just to compete with Texas, but to beat them.

The national title Texas Tech couldn't finish in 2026? This portal class is the blueprint to get back.

The Rest of the Field

Arkansas won the efficiency category. Landing Mountain West Freshman of the Year Madison Wetteland and UCF utility player Kendall Yarnell gives the Razorbacks quality depth and versatility without overspending their recruiting relationships. Dave Engel's program is better.

South Carolina had the best closing stretch. Saviya Morgan and Bella Foran give the Gamecocks a rebuilt outfield and one of the more complete two-player portal hauls of the cycle.

Tennessee played defense smartly — adding Courtney Shelton from South Carolina (.345 BA / 40 RBI) at second base and retaining senior pitcher Emma Mardjetko, who could have entered the portal for a fifth year elsewhere. Keeping Mardjetko was as important as any addition.

FSU starts their rebuild with Ella Dodge and Nicole Edmiaston. The losses they absorbed were enormous. The pieces they added are not equivalent replacements — they're a foundation to build from.

The Last Name

Jaysoni Beachum, the Softball America No. 1 transfer, is still uncommitted. Her destination — whether it's Florida, another program, or a surprise — will write the final sentence of the 2026 portal story. She could make one program's portal window from good to great. But even without her, the winner analysis is clear.

Texas won the portal. Texas Tech won the portal. Both, in different ways, are more dangerous than they were two weeks ago.

What's Next

The portal window closes Monday, June 22. Beachum's decision will be the final story of the 2026 cycle. Then attention shifts to fall practice reports, where the new rosters take shape in real time and the 2027 preseason rankings begin to crystallize.
