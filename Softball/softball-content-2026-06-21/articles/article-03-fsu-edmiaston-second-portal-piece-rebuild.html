FSU Adds Edmiaston as Second Portal Piece — Seminoles Rebuild Takes Shape

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | June 21, 2026

Florida State softball lost two of the best players in program history to the transfer portal. Now the Seminoles are responding — one piece at a time.

FSU has added former Stetson first baseman Nicole Edmiaston as their second portal acquisition of the 2026 window. She joins Tennessee transfer Ella Dodge — the No. 10 overall portal player and No. 4 middle infielder — to form the foundation of head coach Lonni Alameda's rebuild heading into 2027.

What FSU Lost

The losses are significant and worth acknowledging. Isa Torres, the D1 Softball Player of the Year with a .530 batting average, left for two-time defending champion Texas. Torres was a three-time All-American, the ACC Player of the Year, and the defining offensive force of FSU's lineup. Her departure for a program that won back-to-back national championships makes complete sense for her — and it still hurts for the Seminoles.

Jaysoni Beachum — the .417 hitter with 16 home runs and 66 RBI, the Softball America No. 1 transfer — remains uncommitted with one day left in the portal. If Beachum leaves, FSU will have lost the two players most responsible for their offensive identity in 2026.

What FSU Has Added

Ella Dodge fills a genuine need. The Tennessee transfer slashed .285 with 11 home runs, 46 RBI, and 49 hits in 64 starts during the 2026 season. As a redshirt junior with two years of eligibility remaining and a No. 10 portal ranking, Dodge isn't a stopgap — she's a building block. A Florida native from Bradenton returning home, she brings familiarity with the state's recruiting landscape and a skill set that fits what FSU needs at the infield.

Edmiaston adds power at first base. Coming from Stetson, she brings a different kind of profile than the high-rated portal names — a player who earned her scholarship through production at a smaller program and now gets to prove herself on a bigger stage. FSU is betting that the right opportunity will unlock her ceiling.

The Bigger Picture

The Seminoles also bring in six incoming freshmen, giving Alameda a full recruiting class to work with alongside her portal additions. Not every rebuild starts with splashy moves. Some of the most successful program turnarounds in college softball history started with smart, targeted portal decisions and a strong freshman class.

FSU is not the same team they were in 2026. They will need time to develop their new core. But Alameda is a proven coach with a track record of development, and the pieces she's assembled give the Seminoles a path forward that isn't starting from zero.

What's Next

The portal closes Monday, June 22. FSU's final roster picture will depend on whether Beachum makes a last-minute decision to stay in-state — and whether Alameda pursues any additional portal additions in the final hours. If the rebuild is two portal additions and six freshmen, the Seminoles have the foundation. The question is whether they can add one more piece before the window closes.
