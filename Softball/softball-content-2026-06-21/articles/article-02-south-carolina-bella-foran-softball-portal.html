South Carolina Lands Bella Foran — Gamecocks Quietly Win the Transfer Portal

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 21, 2026

While the college softball world fixated on Texas Tech's historic haul and Texas's generational class, South Carolina was quietly putting together a portal window that deserves its own headline.

The Gamecocks landed Gold Glove outfielder Bella Foran on June 20, adding her to a portal class that already included Tennessee transfer Saviya Morgan. With the transfer window closing Monday, June 22, South Carolina has emerged as one of the under-the-radar winners of the 2026 cycle — a program that identified its needs and filled them efficiently.

Foran Adds Elite Defense

Bella Foran brings Gold Glove-caliber defense to the Gamecocks' outfield. In a sport where outfield defense can determine playoff outcomes, adding a player of Foran's defensive caliber is not a minor detail — it's a program upgrade.

The Gamecocks needed outfield help heading into 2027, and they addressed it by the close of the portal window. Foran gives South Carolina a defensive anchor who can hold her own offensively as well. Head coach Beverly Smith's program didn't reach into the portal for a project — they landed a proven commodity.

The Morgan-Foran Pairing

Tennessee transfer Saviya Morgan was already a significant addition. The top-ranked outfielder brings offensive production that South Carolina needed after roster turnover from the 2026 season. Adding Foran alongside her creates a potent 1-2 combination in the outfield that should make the Gamecocks significantly more dangerous in 2027.

Morgan provides the offensive dimension; Foran provides the defensive anchor. Together, they represent the kind of complementary portal strategy that wins championships. Most programs either go all-in on one player or grab whatever is available at the deadline. South Carolina built a package.

The Quiet Portal Winner

The Gamecocks won't make anyone's flashiest portal list. Texas's class featuring D1 Softball Player of the Year Isa Torres generated national headlines. Texas Tech's three-All-American haul was the talk of the portal. But South Carolina accomplished exactly what a program needs to do in the portal era — find their gaps, prioritize quality over quantity, and close by the deadline.

Two outfielders. Two positions of need. Two proven players. That's a winning portal strategy regardless of the name on the building.

What's Next

South Carolina enters 2027 with a meaningfully improved roster and the foundation of a legitimate contender in the SEC. Watch for the Gamecocks' complete 2027 roster picture to come into focus as fall practice approaches. For now, they've done their portal work — and they've done it well.
