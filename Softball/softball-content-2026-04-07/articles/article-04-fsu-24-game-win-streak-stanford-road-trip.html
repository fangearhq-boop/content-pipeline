FSU's 24-Game Win Streak Heads West: The Seminoles Face Stanford This Weekend

By Sarah Mitchell | April 7, 2026

Florida State has not lost a game since February. Now they travel to California to face a ranked Stanford team — and the nation's longest active win streak gets its biggest road test yet.

Florida State's 24-game win streak began in the middle of February and has not slowed since. The Seminoles have swept through the ACC without a conference loss, rallied from multi-run deficits, and beaten every program put in front of them. Their record stands at 35-4 overall, and they have not lost a conference game all season.

This week, they board a plane for California.

Florida State is heading to the west coast to face Santa Clara and No. 24 Stanford in a series beginning Friday, April 10. It is the first time all season the Seminoles have traveled this far from home — and it represents the most significant road test the streak has faced.

What the Streak Looks Like From the Inside

The numbers that define FSU's run are staggering. During the 24-game win streak, the Seminoles are batting .396 as a team. They average more than nine runs per game. They have walked 116 batters while striking out only 44 times at the plate — a ratio that reflects both disciplined hitting and a willingness to attack the strike zone.

These are not the numbers of a hot team riding luck. They are the numbers of a program that has been executing at an elite level for seven consecutive weeks. Head coach Lonni Alameda has built a roster capable of winning games in multiple ways — with power, with pitching, and with the kind of situational hitting that does not show up on a highlight reel but does show up in the win column.

The streak is the fifth-longest in Florida State softball history. When FSU trailed Notre Dame 4-0 in the series finale on April 4 and rallied to win 8-4, extending the streak to 24, it was the kind of win that tests a team's character. They passed that test.

Why Stanford Matters

Stanford is ranked No. 24 in Softball America's Week 10 poll. The Cardinal play in a competitive Pac-12, in front of their own crowd, on a field they know better than any visiting team. For a Florida State team that has dominated its conference, a road series against a nationally ranked opponent in an unfamiliar environment is a different challenge.

Head coach Lonni Alameda has said throughout the season that she wants this team to be tested. She wants road wins. She wants the Seminoles to prove they can win anywhere, not just in ACC ballparks where the rosters and conditions are familiar. The Stanford series is exactly what she asked for.

If FSU can sweep or take the series, they make a compelling national title case with a road win over a ranked program. If Stanford takes the series, it does not end FSU's WCWS chances — but it does put the spotlight on whether the Seminoles' dominance is conference-specific. The stakes, even without a conference title on the line, are real.

What's Next

Florida State begins their west coast trip Friday, April 10, with games against Santa Clara before facing No. 24 Stanford. If the win streak survives the road trip intact, the Seminoles will return to ACC play as one of the most compelling national title stories in the sport. Their next conference series after the west coast trip will carry added significance — FSU has not yet been challenged in the ACC, and the remaining schedule will determine how they are seeded in the NCAA Tournament. The 24-game streak has made them must-watch softball. The Stanford series will tell us whether they deserve to be talked about in the same breath as the top programs in the country.
