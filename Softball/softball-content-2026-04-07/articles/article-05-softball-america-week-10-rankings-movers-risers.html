Softball America Week 10 Rankings: Alabama Is No. 1, Nebraska Rises, and North Alabama Makes History

By Emily Rawlings | April 7, 2026

A new No. 1. A surprising No. 5. A mid-major program's landmark debut. Softball America's Week 10 top 25 captures a sport in the middle of its most consequential stretch of the regular season.

Softball America's Week 10 poll looks different at the top, different in the middle, and different at the bottom. After a week of results that reshuffled power dynamics across the sport, the rankings have responded accordingly — with Alabama at No. 1 for the first time in 2026, Nebraska climbing into the top five, and North Alabama debuting in a major national poll for the first time in program history.

Here is a breakdown of what the Week 10 rankings tell us about where the sport stands heading into the final stretch of conference play.

The Top: Alabama Takes the Throne

Alabama is No. 1. The Crimson Tide knocked off Texas in a series on April 2-4 — their first series win over a top-ranked team since 2016 — and the polls have responded. Texas Tech holds at No. 2 with its 34-2 record and outstanding pitching. Oklahoma is No. 3 at 38-3. Texas falls to No. 4 after the Alabama series loss.

At No. 5, Nebraska is the story of the top ten. The Huskers have been one of the most consistent teams in the country all season, quietly building a resume without the national spotlight of the SEC powers above them. A jump into the top five reflects a season-long body of work finally getting its due.

Florida State is at No. 6 with a 24-game win streak — a ranking that many observers will argue undersells what the Seminoles have done. Being No. 6 with the longest active win streak in the country and a 12-0 conference record is a product of how deep the sport is at the top. In any other year, that FSU run would define the conversation. This year, there are simply a lot of extraordinary teams.

The Middle: Arkansas, Florida, UCLA, Tennessee

Arkansas is No. 7, Florida No. 8, UCLA No. 9, and Tennessee No. 10 — four programs with legitimate tournament aspirations and the records to back them up. The separation between the teams ranked No. 4 through No. 10 in this poll is thin. Any of these programs could realistically finish in the top five by the end of the regular season depending on how their conference schedules play out.

Tennessee's fall to No. 10 reflects a rough stretch of results in SEC play. Arkansas at No. 7 is the surprise riser of the group — the Razorbacks have been playing their best softball of the season at the right time. Florida's spot at No. 8 reflects both their overall record and the challenge of playing in the SEC, where every series carries elimination-level intensity.

The Story: North Alabama Debuts at No. 18

The feel-good moment of the Week 10 rankings is buried in the bottom half of the top 25, but it deserves to be at the top of every college softball conversation this week. North Alabama — a program in the Atlantic Sun Conference, not the SEC or Big 12 — has debuted in the Softball America Top 25 at No. 18.

The Lions are 27-8 overall and 10-2 in the Atlantic Sun Gold Division, leading their conference. They are not on the schedule of the Alabamas and Oklahomas of the world. They do not have the recruiting budgets or stadium infrastructure of power-conference programs. They earn wins against teams with similar constraints, in smaller venues, without the national television exposure that keeps big-program names in front of voters.

And yet they are in the poll. That is what good coaching and sustained program-building looks like. North Alabama's debut at No. 18 is a milestone worth celebrating — for the program, for the Atlantic Sun, and for the idea that college softball's depth extends well beyond the power conferences.

What's Next

The NFCA Coaches Poll — the official poll voted on by 31 Division I head coaches — dropped Tuesday morning at 10 AM ET. Its results will carry the most weight in the official record, but the Softball America poll's Week 10 picture reflects the consensus view of where the sport stands: Alabama at the top, Oklahoma close behind, and a deep field of contenders positioned to make a run when the NCAA Tournament begins in May. The next major ranking update will come after this weekend's games — including the Red River Rivalry between Texas and Oklahoma — and that one could be even more dramatic.
