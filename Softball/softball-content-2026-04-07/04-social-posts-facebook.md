### STORY 1: Alabama Is the New No. 1

#### Long-Form Post -- 9:00 AM

For the first time in 2026, Alabama is the No. 1 softball team in the country.

Softball America's Week 10 poll came out this week with the Crimson Tide at the top — and the NFCA Coaches Poll dropped this morning at 10 AM ET, expected to reflect the same seismic shift. Texas, which held the top spot for the majority of the season, has been knocked off the throne by the team that just beat them in a series.

The math is simple: Alabama traveled to Tuscaloosa and beat No. 1 Texas two games to one at Rhoads Stadium. It was their first series win over a top-ranked team since April 2016. Katie Stewart drove in the clinching run with two home runs in Game 3. That kind of result demands a response from the polls — and the polls delivered.

Texas Tech is steady at No. 2 with a 34-2 record and one of the most dominant pitching staffs in the country. Oklahoma climbs to No. 3 at 38-3. Nebraska surges to No. 5. Florida State sits at No. 6 with its 24-game win streak still intact.

The WCWS is four months away. But the standings at the top of the sport have never been clearer — or more competitive.

### STORY 2: Red River Rivalry — Three Days Away

#### Long-Form Post -- 1:00 PM

Three days. That is all that stands between college softball and its most anticipated series of the year.

Texas (32-4, 9-3 SEC) hosts Oklahoma (38-3, 11-1 SEC) at Red and Charline McCombs Field in Austin beginning this Friday, April 10. Game 1 tips off at 6 PM CT on ESPN2. Game 2 is Saturday at 7 PM CT on ESPN. Game 3 is Sunday on ESPN. These are prime-time slots for a prime-time rivalry, and the sport has earned every second of them.

The storylines are stacked. Texas arrives wounded — they just dropped a series to Alabama, their first conference series loss of 2026. A team that won 29 straight games to open the season is now searching for answers. The Red River Rivalry is the perfect crucible to find them, or to fall further.

Oklahoma arrives as perhaps the hottest team in college softball. At 38-3 with an 11-1 SEC record, the Sooners have run-ruled opponents, beaten marquee programs, and gotten this far on the back of one of the most historic freshman seasons in the sport's history. Kendall Wells has 30 home runs — tied for the all-time NCAA freshman record. Home run No. 31 could happen in Austin, in front of a national television audience, against Texas.

The loser of this series will face a steep climb toward a national seed. The winner could be looking at a top-eight host site come regionals. Do not miss this.

### STORY 3: Kendall Wells — Waiting for the Moment

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM

Kendall Wells has been sitting at 30 home runs for three days.

No games. No at-bats. Just anticipation — and the knowledge that when Oklahoma takes the field again on Friday at McCombs Field, the stage could not be bigger.

Wells tied the NCAA freshman single-season home run record on April 4 against Kentucky, launching her 30th of the year — a 261-foot blast to right-center field in the fourth inning of a 12-2 run-rule win. Three players have reached 30 in a freshman season: Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma, 2012), Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma, 2018), and Kelly Majam (Hawaii, 2010). Wells joined all of them.

Now she needs one more.

What makes this wait meaningful is the context. When Wells hits No. 31, it will happen in front of a national television audience, in Austin, against Texas, in the Red River Rivalry. If there is a more fitting stage for a record-breaking moment in college softball, it has not been built yet.

She is batting .383. She has 60-plus RBIs. She plays for a 38-3 team that is hungry for a national title. The only thing left to do is swing.

### STORY 4: FSU Heads West — 24-Game Streak on the Road

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 PM

Florida State has not lost a game since February. Tonight, they board a plane for California.

The Seminoles (35-4 overall, 12-0 ACC) take their nation-leading 24-game win streak on the road this week, traveling west to face Santa Clara and No. 24 Stanford beginning Friday, April 10. It is their first road trip to the west coast of the season — and it represents a legitimate test for a team that has been nearly unbeatable since mid-February.

During the streak, FSU has batted .396 as a team. They average more than nine runs per game. They have walked 116 batters while striking out just 44. These are not the numbers of a team running hot on luck — they are the numbers of a program in the middle of a historic run.

Head coach Lonni Alameda has said all season that she wants this team to be tested on the road. This is that test. Stanford (No. 24 in Softball America) plays in the Pac-12 and will draw a crowd. The Seminoles have answered every challenge thrown at them for seven weeks. Can they do it against a ranked opponent 2,700 miles from home?

### STORY 5: Softball America Week 10 — Rankings Breakdown

#### Long-Form Post -- 11:00 AM

Softball America dropped its Week 10 Top 25 poll and the sport looks different at every level of the rankings.

At the top, Alabama is No. 1 for the first time in 2026, riding the wave of their series win over Texas. Texas Tech is steady at No. 2 with their 34-2 record. Oklahoma is No. 3. Texas falls to No. 4. Nebraska makes a statement at No. 5 — one of the bigger risers in the poll.

The new entrants are the real intrigue. Kansas, Louisville, and Southeastern Louisiana all crack the Top 25 for the first time this week. The full top 10: Alabama, Texas Tech, Oklahoma, Texas, Nebraska, FSU, Arkansas, Florida, UCLA, Tennessee.

### STORY 6: North Alabama Makes History

#### Long-Form Post -- 5:30 PM

North Alabama is in the Softball America Top 25.

The Lions — playing in the Atlantic Sun Conference, not the SEC or Big 12 — have earned a No. 18 ranking in Softball America's Week 10 poll and a No. 22 spot in the D1Softball poll. Their record: 27-8 overall, 10-2 in the Atlantic Sun Gold Division, and leading their conference.

For a program that does not have the recruiting budget of an Alabama or Oklahoma, this is meaningful. The Atlantic Sun is not a power conference. UNA earns their wins against teams with fewer resources, smaller stadiums, and less national attention. When they crack a major poll, it means something different.

College softball's depth is one of the sport's great strengths. Programs like North Alabama are proof that you do not have to play in a power conference to build something worth watching.
