Teagan Kavan's 600th Career Strikeout: Texas Shuts Out Mississippi State 4-0 to Stay Alive at the WCWS

By Emily Rawlings

May 30, 2026

Kavan pitched a 4-hit complete game in 78 pitches, registering the 600th strikeout of her career on the final out. Texas advances. Mississippi State is eliminated.

There are moments in a career that arrive exactly the way they should.

Teagan Kavan got her 600th career strikeout on the final out of a complete game shutout in an elimination game at the Women's College World Series. She had thrown 77 pitches to get there. The game was decided. All that was left was the last out. And she made it a strikeout — the 600th of a career that has made her one of the most accomplished pitchers in Texas softball history.

Texas 4, Mississippi State 0. Defending champions survive.

A Masterclass in Efficiency

Kavan's line on Friday is the kind of stat sheet that coaches show young pitchers as an example of what command looks like. Four hits. Zero walks. Two strikeouts. Thirteen flyouts. Six groundouts. Seventy-eight pitches total. She never needed to be dominant in the conventional sense — she simply made Mississippi State put the ball in play and let the defense do its job.

That efficiency matters more than most casual fans realize. Kavan is a senior. She is the engine of a Texas pitching staff that went from Game 2 of the opening round — a 6-3 loss to Tennessee — to a Friday night elimination game against a Mississippi State team that had nothing to lose. She could not afford to work deep into counts or give the Bulldogs extra at-bats. She came out and disposed of them in a way that made it look almost routine.

It was not routine. Mississippi State won 47 games this season (per UT Athletics). They made the Women's College World Series for the first time. And Kavan blanked them without breaking a sweat — or so it appeared.

The 600th Strikeout

Kavan is just the fifth Texas pitcher in program history to reach 600 career strikeouts (per University of Texas Athletics). She recorded it on the final out — a strikeout, naturally — of a shutout in the Women's College World Series. That's a sentence that captures what makes her career remarkable.

The program records keep accumulating. Kavan now has seven career WCWS wins, the most in Texas program history. She has four career WCWS shutouts, tying former Longhorn Cat Osterman for the program record. She walked into Friday's game as one of the best postseason performers in Texas softball history and walked out having cemented that status.

Offensive Support When It Mattered

Kaiah Altmeyer hit a two-out, two-run home run in the second inning — her first home run of the season and the 11th of her career — to give Texas the lead it would never relinquish. Kayden Henry added her ninth home run of the season for additional cushion.

For Altmeyer, a senior left fielder, the timing of her first home run could not have been better. A two-out, two-run shot in an elimination game at the Women's College World Series is the kind of contribution that defines how a career gets remembered.

What's Next

Texas now waits for Sunday, when they will face the loser of tonight's Game 8 between Nebraska and Alabama. If Nebraska loses, the defending champions get to face the 2026 USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year, Jordy Frahm, in an elimination game. If Alabama loses, Texas faces a program with the deepest lineup in the tournament.

Either way, Texas has Kavan. And Kavan, fresh off a 78-pitch masterpiece, will be fully available Sunday. Mississippi State's season ends at 47 wins and a program-first WCWS appearance. That is a history-making year for a program that had never been to Oklahoma City. But on Friday night, the defending champions showed exactly why they are dangerous regardless of bracket position. Kavan is the reason.
