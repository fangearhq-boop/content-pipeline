WCWS 2026 Bracket: Six Teams Left, What Sunday Looks Like After Day 2

By Emily Rawlings

May 30, 2026

Two teams are out. Six teams are alive. Here is everything you need to know about where the 2026 Women's College World Series stands heading into the final push.

After two full days of softball at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, the 2026 Women's College World Series has its field of six.

Mississippi State is out. Arkansas is out. Both were first-time WCWS programs that made program history just by making it to Oklahoma City. Both gave legitimate effort before falling in the double-elimination format. The bracket now belongs to the programs with the deepest résumés — and it will get harder from here.

The Unbeaten Teams

Four teams enter Saturday having won their only game at the WCWS so far. Nebraska (51-6) and Alabama are on a collision course tonight — Game 8 at 7 PM ET on ESPN. The winner of that matchup stays on the winners' bracket side. The loser drops into the elimination round Sunday.

Tennessee and Texas Tech will settle their own winners' bracket question this afternoon in Game 7 at 3 PM ET on ABC. Both teams came to Devon Park and won in dominant fashion on Thursday. Tennessee upset the defending national champion Texas behind freshman Elsa Morrison's three-run home run. Texas Tech's NiJaree Canady run-ruled Mississippi State in four innings.

These are the four teams that have not lost a game at Devon Park yet. After today, two of them will have.

The Survivors

Texas and UCLA are both 1-1, having survived Friday's elimination round to stay alive. They will play Sunday, but not against each other — they will face the losers from Saturday's winners'-bracket games.

Texas, the defending national champion, blanked Mississippi State 4-0 on Friday behind Teagan Kavan's complete-game shutout and 600th career strikeout. They are dangerous regardless of the bracket position. Kavan at full strength after a 78-pitch outing is a legitimate championship contender on any given day.

UCLA just set one of the most historic offensive marks in WCWS history — Megan Grant's record 42nd home run, a nine-run second inning, four Bruins homering in a single game to tie the all-time WCWS record. Their offense can explode on any pitcher at any time. The loser of Tennessee–Texas Tech will find that out on Sunday.

What Sunday Looks Like

Sunday's elimination games will feature the Saturday losers playing against UCLA and Texas. The exact matchups depend on today's results:

The loser of Game 7 (Texas Tech vs. Tennessee) will face UCLA in an elimination game Sunday. The loser of Game 8 (Nebraska vs. Alabama) will face Texas in an elimination game Sunday.

Lose Sunday, and the season ends. Win Sunday, and a team lives to fight another day — setting up what would be an extraordinary final stretch toward the championship series, which begins June 3.

What's Next

Today is the most important day of the 2026 Women's College World Series so far. Game 7 (Tennessee vs. Texas Tech, 3 PM ET, ABC) and Game 8 (Nebraska vs. Alabama, 7 PM ET, ESPN) will clarify the bracket and determine who is fighting for their season on Sunday versus who has the luxury of approaching Sunday's games with a loss to spare.

Six teams remain. Two will be eliminated Sunday. The championship series — a best-of-3 format — begins June 3. The road there requires winning every game from this point forward. All six remaining teams have the talent to make it. Very few will.
