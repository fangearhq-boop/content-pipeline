## Key Facts Summary

- **Alabama:** No. 1 national seed, 49-7 record. Second-best ERA in the country at 1.60 with 22 shutouts. Rhoads Stadium, Tuscaloosa. 27th consecutive NCAA Tournament appearance, 21st straight regional hosting stint. Tuscaloosa Regional: Alabama vs. USC Upstate (Friday 12 PM CT / 1 PM ET, SEC Network), Belmont vs. Southeastern Louisiana (Friday 2:30 PM CT, ESPN+). USC Upstate finished 36-21 (12-6 Big South).
- **Oklahoma:** No. 3 national seed, 48-8 record, 20-4 in SEC (back-to-back SEC regular season championships). .389 BA leads the nation. 174 home runs — one of two teams to set or tie the NCAA single-season team HR record. Norman Regional at Love's Field: Oklahoma vs. Binghamton (2:30 PM Friday), Michigan vs. Kansas. 15th consecutive season hosting a regional. Has won four straight national titles (2021–2024) and has appeared in 9 consecutive CWSs. Eyes set on a 9th national title overall.
- **Nebraska:** No. 4 national seed, won their 11th Big Ten championship in program history. First time hosting a regional since 2013. Lincoln Regional: Nebraska vs. South Dakota (5:30 PM ET / 4:30 PM CT Friday, ESPN+), Louisville vs. Grand Canyon (3:00 PM CT Friday). Nebraska won the Big Ten championship game 7-2 over UCLA (game in which Megan Grant hit her record-breaking 38th HR off Jordy Frahm in the 3rd inning, but Nebraska still won).
- **Megan Grant/UCLA:** UCLA is the No. 8 national seed. Grant hit 38 HRs to set the all-time NCAA single-season record (previous: 37, Laura Espinoza, Arizona, 1995). Grant broke it in 55 games. Teammate Jordan Woolery has 33 HRs — first teammates in NCAA softball history both with 30+ HRs in same season. L.A. Regional at Easton Stadium (12th straight year UCLA hosts). Field: UCLA vs. Cal State Fullerton, South Carolina vs. Cal Baptist, May 15–17.
- **Virginia Tech:** 46-10 record, 18-6 ACC. Ineligible to host a regional — placed as No. 2 seed in Baton Rouge Regional behind No. 16 seed LSU. VT's first game: vs. South Alabama (Friday 4 PM ET, ESPN+). LSU vs. Akron (Friday 6:30 PM ET). VT is the consensus pick to win the Baton Rouge Regional and meet Alabama in super regionals.
- **TV Guide:** All 16 regionals run May 15–17. Networks: ESPN, ESPN2, ESPNU, ESPN+, SEC Network, Big Ten Network. WCWS Super Regionals: May 22–24. Women's College World Series: May 28–June 4/5 at Devon Park, Oklahoma City.
