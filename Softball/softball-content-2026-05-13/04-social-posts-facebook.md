### STORY 4: Megan Grant, UCLA L.A. Regional

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM ET

Megan Grant rewrote the record books this season. Now she wants to finish the job.

The UCLA senior hit her 38th home run of 2026 in the Big Ten championship game to break the all-time NCAA Division I single-season record previously held by Laura Espinoza (Arizona, 37 HRs in 1995). Grant broke the record in 55 games — 17 fewer than Espinoza needed. She did it with power, consistency, and a presence at the plate that made every at-bat must-watch television.

Teammate Jordan Woolery has 33 home runs, making Grant and Woolery the first teammates in NCAA softball history to both hit 30 or more home runs in the same season.

Now comes the L.A. Regional. UCLA is the No. 8 seed and hosts at Easton Stadium for the 12th straight year. The field: Cal State Fullerton, South Carolina, and Cal Baptist. Games begin Friday, May 15.

Grant is a senior. These could be the final games of a college career that already stands alone statistically. She hasn't just had a great season — she's had one of the greatest individual offensive seasons in the history of the sport.

A national championship would be the perfect ending.
