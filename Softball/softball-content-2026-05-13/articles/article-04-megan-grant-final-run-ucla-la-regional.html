Megan Grant's Final Run: 38 HRs and a Shot at a Championship Finale at the L.A. Regional

By Jordan Reeves | May 13, 2026 | I Love Softball

Megan Grant has already made history. The UCLA senior slugger broke the all-time NCAA Division I single-season home run record with her 38th of the year — surpassing Laura Espinoza's 31-year-old mark of 37 set at Arizona in 1995. Grant did it in 55 games, 17 fewer than Espinoza needed. She did it in style, hitting home runs in all three of UCLA's Big Ten tournament games.

Now comes the part Grant cares about most: winning.

UCLA is the No. 8 national seed in the 2026 NCAA Tournament and hosts the L.A. Regional at Easton Stadium for the 12th straight year. The field includes Cal State Fullerton, South Carolina, and Cal Baptist. Games begin Friday, May 15. These may be the final college games of one of the most remarkable individual offensive careers in softball history.

A Season That Stands Alone

The record book numbers are hard to fully absorb. Thirty-eight home runs in a single season. Not for a career — for a season. Grant hit home runs at a rate that put her on pace to redefine what's possible for a college softball hitter. She hit home runs against elite pitching. She hit home runs in conference tournament games. She hit her 37th to tie the record and her 38th the very next day to break it.

Teammate Jordan Woolery has contributed 33 home runs of her own in 2026. Grant and Woolery became the first teammates in NCAA softball history to both hit 30 or more home runs in the same season. As a pair, they represent an offensive combination that doesn't have a historical comparable in the sport.

The L.A. Regional and What Winning Means

Easton Stadium has been UCLA's home for regional play a dozen consecutive years. It is a site that the program understands and one where the crowd — deep in softball territory — shows up. Cal State Fullerton and Cal Baptist are familiar regional opponents for a program that regularly hosts in Southern California. South Carolina, the ACC's automatic bid representative, provides the stiffest first-round competition in the pod.

UCLA's path forward is clear: advance through the regional, host a super regional the following week, and earn a trip to Oklahoma City. The record is set. The history is written. But for Megan Grant, the ultimate individual achievement would be a national championship in her final season — the thing that turns a great career into a complete one.

What's Next

If UCLA advances from the L.A. Regional, they host a super regional May 22–24. The Women's College World Series follows May 28 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. For Grant, every win from this point forward is a final: the last time she plays at Easton, the last time she bats in a regional game, potentially the last time she competes in front of a hometown crowd that has followed this season like few others in recent memory.

She broke the record. The final chapter is about what comes next.
