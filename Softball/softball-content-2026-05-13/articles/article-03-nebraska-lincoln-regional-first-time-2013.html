Nebraska's Long-Awaited Return: Big Ten Champs Host Lincoln Regional for First Time Since 2013

By Emily Rawlings | May 13, 2026 | I Love Softball

The last time Nebraska hosted an NCAA softball regional, it was 2013. Twelve years later, the Huskers are back — and they arrive as one of the most legitimate programs in the country.

Nebraska earned the No. 4 national seed in the 2026 NCAA Tournament after winning their 11th Big Ten championship in program history. The Lincoln Regional opens Friday with Louisville facing Grand Canyon at 3 PM CT, followed by Nebraska against South Dakota at 4:30 PM CT (5:30 PM ET) on ESPN+. The Huskers, as a top-8 national seed, are also guaranteed to host a super regional the following weekend if they advance.

The Big Ten Championship and the Grant Connection

Nebraska's path to the No. 4 seed included one of the most memorable games of the college softball season. In the Big Ten championship game on May 9, UCLA's Megan Grant hit her record-breaking 38th home run off Nebraska pitcher Jordy Frahm in the third inning. It was the all-time NCAA single-season home run record, set in real time during a title game.

Nebraska still won, 7-2.

That result says something about this program. Giving up a historic moment and not letting it derail the game, the tournament, or the season — that kind of resilience is what separates programs that talk about competing at the top from programs that actually do it. Nebraska took that Big Ten title banner and used it to earn a No. 4 national seed and their first home regional in 12 years.

The Lincoln Regional Field

The Huskers face a Lincoln Regional field that includes South Dakota, Louisville, and Grand Canyon. South Dakota earned an automatic bid from their conference and represents Nebraska's opening-round opponent. Louisville and Grand Canyon complete the pod.

The format is double elimination across three days, May 15–17. Nebraska has home-field advantage, a motivated roster, and the No. 4 seed's guaranteed super regional hosting position waiting for them if they advance. The program hasn't been this well-positioned entering May in more than a decade.

What's Next

Nebraska advancing through the Lincoln Regional would set up a super regional matchup the weekend of May 22–24. As the No. 4 seed, the Huskers would host — continuing what could become the most significant tournament run the program has produced in years.

The Big Ten championship proved Nebraska belongs among the elite. The Lincoln Regional is the chance to prove they can perform under tournament pressure. Twelve years is a long time to wait for a hosting opportunity. The Huskers don't plan to waste it.

First pitch is Friday evening. Lincoln is ready.
