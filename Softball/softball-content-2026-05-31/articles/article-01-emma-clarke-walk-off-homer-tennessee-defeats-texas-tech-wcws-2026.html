Emma Clarke's Walk-Off Blast Sends Tennessee to WCWS Semifinals

By Sarah Mitchell | May 31, 2026

Emma Clarke stepped into the batter's box in the bottom of the ninth inning 0-for-3 on the night, with Tennessee tied 1-1 against Texas Tech and everything on the line. What happened next became a moment the Vols program will talk about for decades.

Clarke lifted a solo home run to end it — the first walk-off in Women's College World Series history for Tennessee softball. The Lady Vols won 2-1 and advanced to Monday's semifinal, remaining unbeaten at the 2026 WCWS.

The Walk-Off Moment

Clarke led off the ninth inning as the game sat knotted at one. She had gone hitless through her first three at-bats, generating little offensive threat. But the at-bat count did not matter in the ninth, and Clarke turned on a pitch and sent it deep to left field for the winner.

The blast made her the author of the first walk-off win in Tennessee WCWS history — a milestone that will live alongside the program's signature postseason moments. Her teammates stormed the field, and the celebration at Devon Park was immediate and jubilant.

"She was 0-for-3 and she came up there and did it," Tennessee head coach Karen Weekly said postgame. "That's what great players do. They find a way."

How Tennessee Got There

The Vols had drawn first blood in the fifth inning when Taelyn Holley hit a leadoff solo home run to give Tennessee a 1-0 lead. The Lady Vols carried that advantage into the late innings before Texas Tech tied the game.

Sage Mardjetko entered in relief and was sharp, holding the Red Raiders scoreless through 2.1 innings to set up Clarke's heroics. Mardjetko, who entered with a 16-2 record on the season, recorded the win. Alannah Leach contributed defensively with a stunning diving catch in left field during the late innings to keep the score level.

Texas Tech's NiJaree Canady pitched 3.2 innings in relief, allowing two hits and striking out three, but the offense could not generate enough against Tennessee's pitching staff.

Clarke's Tournament Surge

Clarke's walk-off homer was her fourth home run of the 2026 NCAA Tournament, tying her for second in the entire field behind UCLA's Aleena Garcia. Her timing has been excellent — multiple big hits in high-leverage spots throughout the postseason.

The sophomore has quietly emerged as one of the most dangerous bats in the tournament, and her ninth-inning heroics Saturday elevated her into the program's postseason lore with a single swing.

Texas Tech's Difficult Night

Texas Tech fought hard and made Tennessee earn every run. The Red Raiders had entered the WCWS with a 1.62 team ERA — third-best in the nation — but pitching has been a challenge at the tournament. Canady's 7.16 WCWS ERA reflects how the expanded offensive environment at Devon Park has challenged even the nation's elite arms.

Texas Tech now drops into Sunday's elimination game, where they face UCLA at 7 PM ET on ESPN. A win there would send the Red Raiders to Monday's semifinal against Alabama. The Red Raiders showed enough Saturday to suggest they can compete; the question is whether Canady can find her dominant form against UCLA's powerful lineup.

What's Next

Tennessee advances to Monday's semifinal as one of the final four teams remaining. Their opponent will be determined Sunday afternoon — Texas and Nebraska meet in a 3 PM ET elimination game on ABC, with the winner facing the Lady Vols on Monday.

Tennessee is now 2-0 at the 2026 WCWS, having beaten Texas in Day 1 and now dispatching Texas Tech in nine dramatic innings. Monday's semifinal tips off at noon ET on ESPN. The WCWS championship series begins Wednesday, June 3.
