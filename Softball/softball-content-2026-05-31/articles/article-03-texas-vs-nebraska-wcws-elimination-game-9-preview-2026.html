Texas vs. Nebraska: Defending Champs Face Elimination at 3 PM on ABC

By Jordan Reeves | May 31, 2026

The Women's College World Series continues Sunday with a matchup that has everything: a defending national champion fighting to survive, a first-time WCWS participant trying to keep a historic season alive, and one of the most compelling pitching questions in tournament history.

Texas and Nebraska meet in Game 9 at 3 PM ET on ABC. One program's 2026 season ends this afternoon. The other advances to face Tennessee in Monday's semifinal.

Texas: Kavan Rested and Ready

The Longhorns arrived in Oklahoma City as defending national champions, and Thursday's Game 3 reminder of why: Teagan Kavan threw a 78-pitch complete-game shutout against Mississippi State, recording her 600th career strikeout on the final out to advance Texas past the elimination round.

Kavan is fully rested for Sunday's game. With three days between starts, the All-American ace should be at her sharpest. Texas has been here before — the Longhorns have appeared in seven consecutive Super Regionals and have won the last national championship. That experience matters in elimination settings.

Texas went 7-3 against Alabama over the course of the 2026 season, including a 7-1 victory in the SEC championship game where Kavan threw a complete-game three-hitter. Against Nebraska, the Longhorns split two regular-season games at the UTSA Invitational in January: Texas won 15-10 and lost 8-5.

Nebraska: The Frahm Question Looms Again

Nebraska's season has been defined by two things: a 27-game winning streak (now over) and Jordy Frahm's two-way dominance. The junior ace and 2026 USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year is the engine that powered Nebraska to its first WCWS appearance since 2013.

But the pitch count question is back. After throwing 133 pitches on Thursday against Arkansas, Frahm started against Alabama on Saturday night and lasted only two innings before being pulled. Head coach Rhonda Revelle acknowledged that Frahm simply did not have her best stuff.

The key question for Sunday: Is Frahm recovered enough to pitch a full game, or will Nebraska need to lean on its secondary arms? If Frahm is limited, the offensive burden shifts to a Husker lineup that managed just one run Saturday night.

The Matchup Breakdown

If Frahm goes and is at her best, this is an evenly contested game. She has the stuff to handle Texas's lineup — 20 home runs and a 20-4 pitching record this season speak to her elite two-way ability.

If Frahm is limited, Kavan's advantage becomes significant. The Texas ace threw 78 pitches Thursday and has had two full days of rest. She is capable of another complete-game performance, and Nebraska's secondary pitching would struggle to match her output.

Texas's offense is built around patience and power. Nebraska's defense has been reliable all year. The team that controls the early innings will likely dictate the game's outcome.

What's Next

The winner of Sunday's Game 9 advances to face Tennessee in Monday's WCWS semifinal at noon ET on ESPN. Tennessee is rested and riding Emma Clarke's walk-off momentum from Saturday night.

The loser ends a season that, regardless of Sunday's result, featured remarkable achievements. Texas would exit as back-to-back finalist and defending champion with a program record for consecutive Super Regional appearances. Nebraska would close a historic comeback year defined by the return to Oklahoma City after a 13-year drought.

Game time is 3 PM ET on ABC. Devon Park will be loud. The stakes don't get higher than this.
