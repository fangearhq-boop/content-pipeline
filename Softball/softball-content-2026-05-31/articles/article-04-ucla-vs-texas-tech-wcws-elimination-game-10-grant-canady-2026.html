Grant vs. Canady: UCLA and Texas Tech Meet in WCWS Must-Win Game Tonight

By Sarah Mitchell | May 31, 2026

If you're looking for the most star-powered game left in the 2026 Women's College World Series, look no further than tonight at Devon Park.

UCLA's Megan Grant — the NCAA's all-time single-season home run record holder with 42 — faces Texas Tech's NiJaree Canady — one of the most decorated pitchers in college softball history — in an elimination game at 7 PM ET on ESPN. One of these legendary seniors will not make it to Monday. That alone makes this must-see television.

Grant's Record Season on the Line

Megan Grant has done things this season that nobody in college softball has ever done. Her 42 home runs broke her own NCAA single-season record. Her 91st career home run — hit Friday night in UCLA's 11-0 run-rule win over Arkansas — broke the UCLA career record set by Stacey Nuveman in 2002.

Her 68 home runs over the last two seasons tie the all-time Division I record. She is, by any measure, the most feared hitter in college softball this year and possibly in the sport's history.

UCLA's lineup is not just Grant, though. Aleena Garcia leads all tournament players in home runs. Soo-Jin Berry, Jolyna Lamar, and the rest of the Bruins have contributed to what is the most offensively prolific lineup remaining in the WCWS. Texas Tech's pitching staff will need to handle more than just the slugger wearing No. 17.

Canady's Redemption Story

NiJaree Canady was the best pitcher in college softball for most of the 2026 season. She entered the WCWS with a 1.62 ERA — third-best in the nation — and a reputation as the most dominant arm in the tournament field.

The WCWS has not unfolded that way. Canady has a 7.16 ERA in 14-plus innings pitched at the tournament, allowing runs at a rate she almost never approached during the regular season. Texas Tech's team ERA, third-best in the country entering Oklahoma City, ballooned to 6.46 as a unit.

Saturday night, Canady pitched 3.2 innings in relief against Tennessee before Emma Clarke's walk-off homer ended the game. Canady allowed two hits and struck out three, but the game slipped away.

Tonight is a chance for redemption. Canady has the talent. The question is whether she can rediscover her regular-season command against the toughest offensive lineup she has faced.

The UCLA Advantage and Texas Tech's Path

UCLA enters this game with clear momentum. Grant's record-breaking performance Friday was electric, and the Bruins have proven they can win in multiple ways — through the long ball, through pitching, and through defensive situational play.

Texas Tech's path to winning runs directly through Canady. If the ace can limit UCLA's extra-base hit production and keep Grant in the park, the Red Raiders have enough offensive capability to stay in the game. Kaitlyn Terry has provided capable support for Canady all season, giving Texas Tech a two-arm tandem that kept many opponents off balance.

Tech's offense will need contributions up and down the lineup. They showed they can score runs — their regular-season output was excellent — but UCLA's pitching has been solid at the WCWS.

What's Next

The winner of tonight's Game 10 advances to face Alabama in Monday's WCWS semifinal at 7 PM ET on ESPN. Alabama is 2-0 at the WCWS and has shown no weaknesses — Jocelyn Briski's 1-hitter Saturday and Marlie Giles' four-RBI night have set the standard for championship-level performance.

Whether it's UCLA or Texas Tech that survives tonight, they will face a formidable opponent in Alabama on Monday. But first, one of college softball's greatest seniors must fight to keep playing. Tonight at 7 PM on ESPN, someone's season ends. Whose is the question that makes this game unmissable.
