Briski's 1-Hitter Leads Alabama Past Nebraska 5-1, Ending 27-Game Win Streak

By Emily Rawlings | May 31, 2026

The question that hung over Nebraska all week was answered in the first inning Saturday night. With Jordy Frahm still recovering from a 133-pitch outing on Thursday, Alabama's Marlie Giles sent a 3-run home run deep into the Devon Park night — and the Crimson Tide never looked back.

Alabama won 5-1, ending Nebraska's 27-game winning streak and advancing to Monday's WCWS semifinal. Jocelyn Briski's complete-game 1-hitter was as dominant a pitching performance as the WCWS has seen this year.

Briski Was Untouchable

Briski was perfect through 3.2 innings. She retired the first 10 Cornhuskers she faced before Hannah Camenzind broke it up with a solo home run to center field in the fourth inning — the only damage Nebraska managed all night.

The final line for the SEC Pitcher of the Year was exceptional: 7 innings, 1 hit, 6 strikeouts, 0 walks, 83 pitches with 62 for strikes. She threw with command, confidence, and pace, and Nebraska's lineup — as good as it is — simply could not solve her.

"She was on a different level tonight," Alabama head coach Patrick Murphy said. "She's been like that all year, and she showed it on the biggest stage."

Giles Sets the Tone Early

If Briski was the story defensively, Marlie Giles provided the offensive statement. With two outs in the first inning, Giles turned on a Frahm pitch and sent it over the fence for a 3-run homer — putting Alabama ahead 3-0 before Nebraska had fully settled in.

Giles was not finished. She added a sacrifice fly in the third inning to push the lead to 4-0, finishing the night with four RBI. The combination of Briski and Giles dismantled the game's biggest narrative before it could develop.

The Frahm Question Was Answered Hard

Jordy Frahm lasted two innings. She walked the leadoff batter, hit another, and left a pitch on the plate that Giles destroyed. Nebraska head coach Rhonda Revelle acknowledged after the game that Frahm simply did not have her best stuff — a predictable result after the 133-pitch marathon she had thrown Thursday night.

Frahm had been the engine of Nebraska's historic season. Her 26-win streak run was one of the most compelling narratives in all of college softball in 2026. But asking her to turn around in two days after a 133-pitch outing was always a risk, and Alabama executed perfectly to exploit the vulnerability.

Nebraska's Historic Season Honored

The 27-game winning streak that defined Nebraska's WCWS run was the longest active streak in the country entering the tournament. The Huskers reached the WCWS for the first time since 2013, finishing 51-7 — a record that would have been extraordinary in any season.

Hannah Camenzind's homer in the fourth inning — the team's only run — preserved a sliver of dignity in a difficult night. Nebraska played with pride throughout the 2026 season and gave their fans a WCWS run to remember.

What's Next

Alabama advances to Monday's WCWS semifinal with a perfect 2-0 record at Devon Park. The Crimson Tide, now 56-7 overall, will face the winner of Sunday's UCLA vs. Texas Tech elimination game (7 PM ET, ESPN). The winner of that game earns the right to face Alabama on Monday.

Nebraska's season is over. The championship series begins June 3, and Alabama currently looks like the team everyone will need to beat. Briski and Giles have set a high standard for what the final rounds of this tournament will look like.
