WCWS 2026: Tennessee and Alabama Are In. Four Teams Fight for Two More Spots Today.

By Jordan Reeves | May 31, 2026

Saturday night at Devon Park was a masterclass in how the Women's College World Series can deliver drama and dominance in equal measure.

Tennessee won on Emma Clarke's walk-off solo home run in nine innings — the first WCWS walk-off in Vols program history — sending the Lady Vols into Monday's semifinal. Then Alabama followed with a clinical dismantling of Nebraska, with Jocelyn Briski throwing a complete-game 1-hitter and Marlie Giles driving in four runs in a 5-1 victory that ended the Huskers' 27-game winning streak.

Two semifinal spots are filled. Two more are up for grabs today.

Today's Elimination Games

Sunday's schedule features two elimination games that will complete the WCWS Final Four:

Game 9 — 3 PM ET on ABC: Defending champion Texas faces Nebraska. Teagan Kavan is rested and ready after throwing 78 pitches on Thursday. Nebraska's Jordy Frahm is the question — she lasted only 2 innings Saturday and must bounce back in a must-win game. The winner faces Tennessee on Monday.

Game 10 — 7 PM ET on ESPN: UCLA takes on Texas Tech. Megan Grant's record-setting offense against NiJaree Canady's need for a bounce-back performance. Grant has 42 home runs — an NCAA record — and is playing the best softball of her career. Canady has a 7.16 WCWS ERA but the talent to change the narrative in one outing. The winner faces Alabama on Monday.

Tennessee's Semifinal Position

Tennessee comes in riding the emotional high of Clarke's walk-off blast. The Lady Vols are 2-0 at the 2026 WCWS — they beat defending champion Texas in Day 1 and then outlasted Texas Tech in nine innings on Saturday. That unbeaten run means Tennessee enters the semifinal with rest and momentum.

Their semifinal opponent will be determined Sunday afternoon at 3 PM. If Texas wins, the defending champion returns to face the team that eliminated them in the winners' bracket. If Nebraska wins, the Vols face a resilient team that has overcome adversity all season.

Tennessee's Monday game tips off at noon ET on ESPN.

Alabama's Dominant Path

Alabama has been the most impressive team at Devon Park so far. The Crimson Tide are 2-0 with two dominant performances — a Day 1 win and Saturday's 5-1 dismantling of Nebraska. Briski has been near-unhittable. Giles has provided the timely power hitting. The rest of the lineup has been consistent.

Alabama's semifinal opponent comes from tonight's Game 10. If UCLA wins, Grant's all-time record-setting offense gets a shot at the Tide. If Texas Tech wins, Canady gets a chance to face the team she could not solve in the regular season. Either way, Alabama has proven they are prepared for whoever shows up Monday night.

Alabama's semifinal is scheduled for 7 PM ET Monday on ESPN.

What's Next

Sunday's two elimination games determine the complete WCWS Final Four. Monday's semifinals could feature two of the most compelling matchups of the tournament — and both will be nationally televised on ESPN.

The WCWS championship series begins Wednesday, June 3, at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. The road to the title runs through the Final Four, and two teams will be eliminated before Devon Park sees its last game.

The drama of Day 4 starts at 3 PM ET with Texas vs. Nebraska. Set your reminders. This is the WCWS at its best.
