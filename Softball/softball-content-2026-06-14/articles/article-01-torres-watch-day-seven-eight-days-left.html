Torres Watch: Day 7, Eight Days Left and Still No Answer

By Jordan Reeves

June 14, 2026

Seven days. No commitment. The silence surrounding Isa Torres has become a story in itself.

The D1 Softball Player of the Year — and the most coveted transfer portal target in the history of women's college softball — enters Day 7 of the 15-day window without a decision. The portal closes June 22. Eight days remain. Texas and Texas A&M are still waiting.

What Makes Her Historic

The numbers behind the wait are extraordinary. In 2026, Isa Torres slashed .530/.591/.978 with 78 runs scored — every single figure a Florida State program record. She set an NCAA mark when she recorded a hit in 16 consecutive at-bats. The ACC named her conference Player of the Year. D1Softball named her the national Player of the Year. She is, by any measure, the most dominant offensive player of the 2026 college softball season.

She has one year of eligibility remaining. Wherever she lands, that program becomes an instant national title contender.

Two Programs, Two Pitches

The field has narrowed to two. Texas and Texas A&M have been linked to Torres throughout the window, and the positioning of each program makes for a genuinely compelling choice.

Texas is the two-time defending national champion. Head coach Mike White returns Teagan Kavan — the only two-time Most Outstanding Player in Women's College World Series history — along with Katie Stewart and Kayden Henry as seniors. The Longhorns' 2027 roster already has a three-peat foundation. They also just signed Samantha Swan, the Georgetown, Texas catcher who grew up in the same hometown as Torres — 30 minutes north of the UT Austin campus. The pitch to Torres is simple: come home, be part of history, chase a third title with one of the best rosters ever assembled.

Texas A&M offers a different proposition. The Aggies have the resources and the ambition, and landing Torres would redefine the program overnight. Head coach Trisha Ford has already shown she can recruit at an elite level; adding the nation's best offensive player would be a statement that College Station is a legitimate national title destination.

The Clock and the Pressure

Players are not required to commit by any particular point within the 15-day window. Torres has done nothing wrong by taking her time. But as the window narrows, programs lose flexibility. Every day a top target goes uncommitted is a day a recruiting board stays unsettled.

The portal world is watching. Every softball insider, every fan base, every coaching staff with a roster spot to fill is refreshing their phone waiting for Torres's announcement. The longer the wait, the bigger the moment when it finally comes.

What's Next

Eight days remain before the June 22 deadline. Torres has not given any public indication of a timeline. What is certain is that the decision — when it comes — will be one of the most scrutinized announcements in college softball history. Texas and Texas A&M are both credible landing spots with legitimate title paths. The wait continues. The softball world is watching.
