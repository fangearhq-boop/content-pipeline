Beachum — Day 7 Uncommitted, No. 1 Transfer Still Building Leverage

By Sarah Mitchell

June 14, 2026

Jaysoni Beachum is still available. And that is not a sign of weakness — it is a sign of leverage.

The Softball America No. 1 transfer enters Day 7 of the transfer portal window without a commitment. The window closes June 22, which means eight days remain. In that time, every program still pursuing Beachum is watching and waiting, unwilling to pivot because she is simply too good to walk away from.

The Resume of a No. 1 Transfer

Beachum's standing in this portal class is not a marketing position — it is earned. In 2026, the Florida State third baseman hit .417 with 16 home runs, 66 RBIs, and a .549 on-base percentage. Across three seasons in Tallahassee, she accumulated 37 home runs, 180 RBIs, and 202 hits while maintaining a career .375 batting average and a .654 slugging percentage.

She won NFCA Freshman of the Year as a true freshman — the first Seminole ever to claim that award. In 2026, she earned Second-Team All-American recognition despite playing for a program that was simultaneously losing its two best players to the portal at the same time she was leaving.

No team in the country has a better player available in this class. That is not an opinion — it is the consensus of every ranking service covering the portal.

The Patience Play

Beachum's strategy — whether intentional or natural — is textbook leverage management. The more programs compete for her signature, the better the deal they are willing to make. By staying uncommitted deep into the window, she ensures that every interested program remains actively engaged, not able to shift their recruiting energy elsewhere.

The risk of waiting — that a program pulls its offer or pivots to another target — is largely theoretical for a player of Beachum's caliber. At her level, programs do not pivot. They wait.

What She Brings

Beachum is not just a power hitter — she is a complete offensive player whose production at third base would elevate any lineup she joins. Her 16 home runs in 2026 came alongside a .549 on-base percentage, meaning she reached base in roughly half of all plate appearances. Programs chasing national titles need players who do exactly that: get on base and hit for power.

She also provides defensive value at third base, a position that is difficult to fill with elite talent at the transfer level. The combination of offense, defense, and experience makes her the most complete player in the portal.

What's Next

Eight days remain. Beachum's timeline is her own, and she has given no public indication of urgency. What is known is that when she announces, it will reshape a program's 2027 outlook overnight. The wait is almost certainly the correct play. The decision, when it comes, will be the biggest transfer commitment of this portal cycle. The softball world is watching.
