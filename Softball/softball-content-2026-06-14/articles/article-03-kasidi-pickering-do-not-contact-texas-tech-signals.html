Pickering's "Do Not Contact" Tag Has Texas Tech Fans Buzzing

By Emily Rawlings

June 14, 2026

While Isa Torres and Jaysoni Beachum have dominated transfer portal headlines, there is another name worth watching closely: Kasidi Pickering. And unlike Torres and Beachum, Pickering may have already made her decision.

The Oklahoma outfielder entered the transfer portal on June 8 with a "Do Not Contact" tag attached to her profile — a designation that carries a very specific meaning in the portal world.

What a "Do Not Contact" Tag Means

In college athletics' transfer portal system, a player may enter the portal with a "Do Not Contact" status, which instructs programs not to initiate contact with the player or her representatives. The designation is widely understood among coaches and recruiters as a strong signal that the player has already privately committed to a destination and is entering the portal only as a procedural step to complete her transfer.

It does not guarantee a commitment to any specific program. But it does suggest that Pickering is not accepting recruiting pitches or visits — because she does not need them.

The Texas Tech Evidence

Circumstantial signals point toward Texas Tech. When the Red Raiders finished the 2026 Women's College World Series as national runner-up, Pickering shared a video of the celebration on Instagram with the comment "This is everything." The post was noted widely in Oklahoma recruiting circles.

Additionally, several of Pickering's former travel ball teammates from coach Nathan Nelson's program have ended up in Lubbock playing for Jerry Glasco's Red Raiders. The pipeline between that travel ball program and Texas Tech has been active and consistent.

Texas Tech, fresh off a WCWS runner-up finish to two-time champion Texas, is also operating from a position of strength as a recruiting destination. A player of Pickering's caliber would arrive in Lubbock as a cornerstone of a program that is already a national championship contender.

The Profile of the No. 2 Transfer

Pickering is not just a high-ranking portal entry — she is a legitimate star. In 2026, she hit .370 with 20 home runs and 61 RBIs for the Sooners. Over her career at Oklahoma, she accumulated a .384 batting average, 50 home runs, and 169 RBIs. She earned First-Team All-American honors in 2025 and First-Team All-Midwest Region recognition in 2026.

On3 ranks her second overall in the transfer portal, just behind Torres. She is a power bat in the outfield who can protect any middle-of-the-order lineup.

What's Next

Pickering has not made an official announcement, and the "Do Not Contact" tag does not confirm any specific destination. But the combination of circumstantial evidence — the Instagram post, the travel ball network, the program fit — makes Texas Tech the logical conclusion. If the Red Raiders have quietly added the nation's No. 2 transfer to pair with their existing talent, the 2027 national championship conversation just got more interesting. An announcement could come at any time between now and June 22.
