Nebraska Closes the Regular Season at Penn State — A Top-4 National Seed Is Within Reach

By Sarah Mitchell | April 30, 2026

Nebraska softball is not done proving itself.

The Cornhuskers (40-6, 20-1 Big Ten) travel to Penn State this weekend for the final regular-season series of the year — three games at Happy Valley on Big Ten Network, beginning Friday at 4 PM CT — with a 13-game win streak, a No. 3 national ranking, and a top-4 national seed firmly in their sights.

For a program that has rebuilt itself into one of the most dominant teams in the country, this final series is both a formality and a statement opportunity. Nebraska has not lost a Big Ten conference series all season. Sixteen series, sixteen wins. The Huskers have been the standard-bearer of the Big Ten in 2026, and this weekend is a chance to close the regular season without a blemish on that record.

What's at Stake

The primary prize is a top-4 national seed in the NCAA Tournament, which brings home regional hosting to Lincoln for the first time in recent program memory. Nebraska is No. 4 in the NCAA RPI — a figure that carries enormous weight with the Selection Committee when it sits down on May 10 to assign seeds and regions.

A sweep of Penn State would reinforce Nebraska's case and keep the Cornhuskers in the conversation for the No. 3 overall seed. Losing the series — or even splitting — would not knock Nebraska out of the top-4 conversation entirely, but it would invite scrutiny and give other programs ammunition to argue for a better seed.

The Huskers reached 40 wins with last week's sweep of Iowa, the first time Nebraska had reached 40 wins this early in a season in program history. The win total matters: 40 victories is the kind of number that jumps off a Selection Committee resume sheet.

Penn State Is Not a Pushover

Penn State (32-17, 11-10 Big Ten) has won four straight games heading into this series and wants to close the home schedule with a bang. The Nittany Lions play every game at home with a passion that has given opponents trouble all year, and their 32-17 record is a solid enough base to show they can compete.

Nebraska won't overlook them. Coach Rhonda Revelle has built a program culture that takes every opponent seriously, and the stakes of this weekend — national seeds, momentum heading into the Big Ten Tournament, the win streak — mean the Huskers will be locked in from the first pitch Friday afternoon.

What's Next

After Penn State, Nebraska enters the Big Ten Tournament before the NCAA Regional (Regionals begin May 15). If the Cornhuskers close the regular season strong, home regionals in Lincoln could become reality — a scenario that would be one of the most exciting moments in the program's modern era. Game 1 is Friday, May 1, at 4 PM CT on Big Ten Network. Saturday's game starts at 1:30 PM CT, and Sunday at 11 AM CT.
