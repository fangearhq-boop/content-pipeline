Kendall Wells Is One Home Run From Tying a 31-Year NCAA Record — And She Gets Three Chances Tonight

By Sarah Mitchell | April 30, 2026

Thirty-one years ago, Lauren Espinoza hit 37 home runs for the Arizona Wildcats and set a record that has outlasted every era of college softball since. No freshman, no sophomore, no senior with a year of extra eligibility has ever touched it. The record has stood through conference realignments, coaching dynasties, and the emergence of the sport into a mainstream national audience.

Kendall Wells needs one home run to tie it.

The Oklahoma freshman enters Thursday night's game at No. 12 Texas A&M with 36 home runs — already the most ever hit by an NCAA freshman, already the most by any Sooner in a single season, already the SEC all-time single-season mark. But Espinoza's record of 37 from 1995 has been the target since mid-April, and Wells now has three games — tonight's opener at 8 PM CT on SEC Network plus two more this weekend — to reach it.

"She's not thinking about records," Oklahoma head coach Patty Gasso has said throughout the chase. "She's playing softball." But the scoreboard doesn't lie: 36 home runs in a freshman season, against the best pitching in the country, is something that defies easy explanation.

What the Record Means

Espinoza's 37-home-run season came in 1995 — before most of today's college players were born. The record has been tested by elite power hitters across three decades and has survived every attempt. Wells is the first player in the sport's modern era to get this close, and she's doing it as a freshman, against an SEC schedule that features some of the most dominant pitching staffs in the country.

By any measure, Wells' 2026 season has already been historic. Her 36 home runs broke the NCAA freshman record of 30 (which she surpassed on April 12 against Texas), shattered the OU program record, and set the SEC all-time single-season mark. Oklahoma's team has also shattered the NCAA single-season home run record, a mark that is a direct reflection of how Wells has elevated the offense around her.

To tie Espinoza at 37 would be one thing. To hit No. 38 and break the record outright would be something the sport has never seen.

The Stakes at Texas A&M

Wells doesn't get to chase the record in a vacuum. The series at Texas A&M carries enormous team stakes of its own. Oklahoma (45-6) leads the SEC standings at 17-3, and the Sooners need just one win this weekend to clinch at least a share of the SEC Regular Season title. Two wins locks it up outright — back-to-back SEC crowns in only Oklahoma's second year in the conference.

Thursday's Game 1 (8 PM CT, SEC Network) is the first-ever SEC matchup between Oklahoma and Texas A&M, two programs that spent decades as Big 12 rivals. The Aggies (34-16, 10-11 SEC) are No. 12 nationally and have been competitive in conference play despite a losing SEC record. Playing at home in College Station, they will not be easy.

But if the Sooners are going to close the regular season the way they've played it — dominant, historic, and Kendall Wells at the center of everything — College Station in the final week feels like exactly the right stage.

What's Next

Wells has three games remaining in the regular season: tonight (8 PM CT, SEC Network), Friday (5 PM CT, SEC Network), and Saturday (4 PM CT, ESPN2). Whether she reaches the record this weekend or carries the chase into the SEC Tournament (May 5-9, Lexington, KY), one thing is clear: this freshman season is already one for the history books — and the biggest chapter may not be written yet.
