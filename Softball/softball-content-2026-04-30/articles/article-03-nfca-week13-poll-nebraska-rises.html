NFCA Week 13 Poll: Nebraska Climbs to No. 3 for the First Time — Alabama Drops After Tennessee Series

By Jordan Reeves | April 30, 2026

For the first time all season, Nebraska is ranked ahead of Alabama in the national polls.

The NFCA/GoRout Division I Top 25 Coaches Poll for Week 13 — released Monday, April 28 — shows a significant shift at the top of the rankings entering the final week of the regular season. Oklahoma remains No. 1, Texas Tech stays at No. 2, but Nebraska has climbed past Alabama to No. 3. The Crimson Tide, coming off their first conference series loss of the year, drop to No. 4.

This is the first time all of 2026 that Nebraska sits third nationally. It is a reflection of a season that has been quietly exceptional: the Cornhuskers are 40-6 overall with a 20-1 Big Ten record and a 13-game win streak that has included wins over ranked opponents, dominant conference series sweeps, and increasingly national attention as a legitimate top-4 national seed contender.

The Full Top Five

Oklahoma (45-6) holds the top spot with 12 first-place votes. The Sooners are the consensus No. 1 team in the country and lead the SEC standings at 17-3. They open their final regular-season series tonight at Texas A&M.

Texas Tech (47-4) stays at No. 2 after clinching back-to-back Big 12 regular season championships on April 26. The Red Raiders close the regular season at home against Baylor before entering the Big 12 Tournament (May 6-9, Devon Park, OKC) as the No. 1 seed.

Nebraska (40-6) jumps to No. 3 — the highest ranking in the program's recent history. The Cornhuskers travel to Penn State for the regular season finale (May 1-3, Big Ten Network) with a win streak that has defined their month of April.

Alabama (44-6) drops to No. 4 following the 2-1 series loss at Tennessee — the first SEC series loss of the entire season for the Crimson Tide. Alabama's SEC Tournament performance (May 5-9, Lexington, KY) will be crucial for their national seed positioning.

Florida (46-7) rounds out the top five at No. 5. The Gators open at No. 14 Georgia tonight in a top-25 regular season finale.

What the Rankings Mean for Seeding

The NFCA poll does not directly determine NCAA Tournament seeding — that is the Selection Committee's domain, working from RPI, strength of schedule, and head-to-head results. But the poll reflects national perception, and perception matters. Nebraska's rise to No. 3 signals that voters believe the Cornhuskers deserve to be in the top-4 national seed conversation. Alabama's drop signals that the Tennessee series loss changed some minds.

The Selection Show is May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. Conference tournament performances between now and then will carry significant weight.

Receiving votes this week: Washington (24), Clemson (21), South Carolina (21), Southeastern Louisiana (18), Belmont (12), Omaha (10), Kansas (1).

What's Next

The Week 13 poll will be the final regular-season poll before conference tournaments begin. After that, results in the SEC, Big 12, ACC, and other conference tournaments will shape the national landscape. The next major inflection point is May 10 — Selection Sunday — when the 64-team NCAA Tournament field is announced and the road to Devon Park becomes official.
