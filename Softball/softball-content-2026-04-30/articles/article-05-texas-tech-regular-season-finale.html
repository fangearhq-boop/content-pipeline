Texas Tech Has Already Won the Big 12 — Now It's Setting Its Sights on Something Bigger

By Emily Rawlings | April 30, 2026

Texas Tech clinched back-to-back Big 12 regular season championships last Sunday. But the Red Raiders (47-4) are not playing for a conference title right now. They are playing for a national seed — and everything that comes with it.

TTech closes the regular season this week hosting Baylor before turning its full attention to the Big 12 Tournament (May 6-9, Devon Park, Oklahoma City), where the Red Raiders enter as the No. 1 seed. They are No. 2 nationally in the NFCA/GoRout Coaches Poll, behind only Oklahoma. A second straight Big 12 Tournament title — combined with a 47-4 record — would be an almost impossible argument to ignore for a top-2 national seed.

NiJaree Canady and the Case for No. 2

At the center of every conversation about Texas Tech this season is NiJaree Canady. The ace of the Red Raiders' pitching staff has been one of the most dominant pitchers in college softball this year — her career has already included surpassing 1,000 strikeouts and multiple no-hitters in 2026, and her ERA and strikeout numbers place her firmly in the Pitcher of the Year conversation nationally.

What makes Canady so hard to beat is not just pure stuff — it is the consistency. She gives Texas Tech a true frontline ace who can take the ball in any situation, against any lineup, and deliver. When she is on, the Red Raiders are one of the best teams in the country regardless of matchup.

The offense has matched her pace. Texas Tech has been one of the most productive lineups in the Big 12 all season, and the 47-4 record reflects a team that wins in multiple ways — with the ace, without the ace, by run-ruling opponents, and by grinding out close games.

Devon Park is the Destination

The Big 12 Tournament at Devon Park in Oklahoma City (May 6-9) is the next stop. TTech is the No. 1 seed and will open against the No. 8 seed. But the Red Raiders are thinking beyond the Big 12. Devon Park is also the site of the Women's College World Series (May 28–June 3) — and for Texas Tech, the ultimate goal is being back there at the end of the month with a national championship on the line.

A top-2 national seed would give the Red Raiders a home regional in Lubbock, a second-round super regional at home, and a clear path to the WCWS with maximum crowd advantage at every step. The case is strong. The regular season close against Baylor is an opportunity to add one more chapter to a record that is already among the best in the country.

What's Next

Texas Tech closes the regular season this week before the Big 12 Tournament (May 6-9, Devon Park, OKC). The Selection Show is May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. If the Red Raiders win the Big 12 Tournament and finish with a record around 50-4, the national seed argument becomes nearly bulletproof. Watch for Canady to take the ball in the biggest games — that is what Texas Tech has built this season around.
