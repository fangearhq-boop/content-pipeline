#### Long-Form Post -- 9:00 AM

Kendall Wells is one home run away from tying the greatest single-season home run record in college softball history.

The Oklahoma freshman phenom enters Thursday night's game at Texas A&M with 36 home runs — one shy of Lauren Espinoza's 31-year-old NCAA record of 37, set at Arizona in 1995. Wells already has broken just about every other record in her path: the NCAA freshman single-season record, the OU program record, and the SEC all-time mark. Now she's three games away from the biggest one of all.

What makes tonight even more electric is the context. The No. 1 Sooners (45-6) are opening their final regular-season series at No. 12 Texas A&M in College Station — the first-ever SEC matchup between two programs that spent decades as Big 12 rivals. One Oklahoma win clinches at least a share of the SEC Regular Season title. Two wins locks it up outright, giving OU back-to-back SEC crowns in just its second year in the conference.

Game 1 is tonight at 8 PM CT on SEC Network. The weight of 31 years of history is in every at-bat Kendall Wells steps into. Don't look away.

#### Long-Form Post -- 11:00 AM

For the first time all season, Alabama softball has lost a conference series.

The Crimson Tide (44-6) entered Knoxville as one of the most dominant teams in the country — winners of their first 21 SEC series of 2026. But Tennessee had other plans, taking the rubber match 4-1 on Monday to complete a 2-1 series victory that sent shockwaves through the national seeding picture.

What hurt Alabama most wasn't just the rubber match — it was Game 2. The Tide were held scoreless in a 2-0 loss, the first shutout they had suffered all season long. That was the kind of performance that reminded everyone how dangerous Tennessee's pitching staff can be when everything is clicking.

The loss dropped Alabama from No. 3 to No. 4 in the NFCA Week 13 poll, with Nebraska leapfrogging the Tide for the first time this year. Alabama is still very much a national seed — 44-6 is an elite record — but the margin for error is shrinking with the SEC Tournament set to begin May 5 in Lexington, Kentucky. The Tide will need to close the regular season strong and make a deep tournament run to cement their seeding.

Tennessee, meanwhile, just made a major statement. At 40-8 with 14-7 in the SEC, the Lady Vols are playing their best softball of the season at exactly the right time.
