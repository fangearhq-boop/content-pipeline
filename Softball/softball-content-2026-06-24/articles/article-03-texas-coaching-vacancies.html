Texas Softball Has Two Coaching Vacancies — And the Hires Matter as Much as the Roster

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | June 24, 2026

Everyone is talking about what Texas's 2027 roster looks like. Isa Torres. Katie Stewart. Samantha Swan. Teagan Kavan. A top national recruiting class. The consensus is forming fast: this may be the most dangerous team assembled in college softball history.

What fewer people are discussing: the Longhorns also need to hire two coaches before fall practice.

Associate head coach Ehren Earleywine departed Texas after the 2026 championship, taking an associate head coaching role at Tennessee. Earleywine was the architect of one of the most productive offenses in program history — the 2026 Longhorns hit 99 home runs as a team, with Stewart setting the program record at 31. His fingerprints were everywhere in that lineup.

Associate head coach Kristen Zaleski, who was promoted before the 2026 season, will not return for 2027. Two departures from a staff that just won back-to-back national titles is a significant personnel challenge — even if the program itself is in the best position in its history.

Why the Hires Matter

Head coach Mike White has been recognized as one of the elite coaches in college softball — his staff was named ATEC/NFCA Division I National Coaching Staff of the Year for the 2026 season. Filling two vacancies on that staff is not a small task.

The good news: there is no more attractive coaching destination in the sport right now. Texas is the two-time defending champion. The 2027 roster may be the most talented in the program's history. Any coach hired in Austin will have a genuine shot at a third consecutive national title.

The challenge: finding candidates who fit White's system, who can develop relationships quickly with a roster full of established veterans and high-profile transfers, and who can step into a role with immediate responsibilities. The recruiting class Texas is managing is among the top four nationally. Whoever replaces Earleywine on the offensive side will be coaching players like Torres, Stewart, and Swan — stars who know exactly what they want from their development.

What Remains

Assistant coach Pattie Ruth Taylor continues on staff, providing continuity within the program. White himself is the stable axis around which everything else rotates — and his track record as head coach speaks for itself.

The NFCA Convention comes to Atlantic City in December, which typically serves as the informal hiring season in college softball. It is reasonable to expect the Texas vacancies will be filled well before then, likely by late summer or early fall, before the new coaches begin their work with the team in August practices.

The Larger Stakes

Back-to-back championships are remarkable. Three straight titles would be unprecedented. The staff that guides this 2027 Texas roster — whoever fills those two open spots — will be part of that story. Alternatively, a poor hire could introduce friction into a roster full of strong personalities and high expectations.

White has earned trust. His hiring decisions since taking over the program have been consistently strong. But this offseason, with more attention on the Longhorns than any softball program has ever faced, the coaching search is the hidden variable that could shape how 2027 actually unfolds.

What's Next

Announcements on coaching hires are expected before fall camp begins in August. Watch for names from within the Texas staff pipeline and from programs where White has existing connections. The NFCA coaching transaction board will be worth monitoring through July. The roster is set. The staff is the last piece of the puzzle.
