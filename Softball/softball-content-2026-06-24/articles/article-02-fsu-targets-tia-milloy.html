FSU Softball Is Targeting Tia Milloy — The Seminoles' Last Big Offseason Move

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 24, 2026

The 2026 transfer portal window is closed. The 15-day scramble that saw 122 players enter and 89 commit is over. But Florida State's offseason work may not be finished.

The Seminoles are targeting Tia Milloy, the Oklahoma utility player who entered the portal after the Sooners' Super Regional exit in May. Milloy hit .353 with four home runs, 22 RBIs, and a .706 slugging percentage across 41 games for OU this past season. She has two years of eligibility remaining — making her one of the more valuable uncommitted players from the 2026 portal cycle.

For Florida State, landing Milloy would represent the completion of a summer rebuild that began under extraordinarily difficult circumstances.

A Brutal Window — Then a Response

FSU lost two of the most decorated players in program history during the 2026 portal cycle. Isa Torres — the national Player of the Year — committed to Texas. Jaysoni Beachum — the No. 1 transfer in the portal — committed to Florida. It was, by any measure, a devastating 15 days for the Tallahassee program.

But the Seminoles did not sit still. Within days of those departures, FSU secured two significant additions of their own.

Ella Dodge arrived from Tennessee, where she hit 23 home runs. Nicole Edmiaston transferred from Stetson, adding 11 more. Those two additions alone account for 34 combined home runs — more than Torres and Beachum produced together at FSU in 2026.

And crucially, the program's foundation was never at risk. ACC Pitcher of the Year Jazzy Francik returns. Two-way player Ashtyn Danley returns. The backbone that has kept Florida State competitive through multiple coaching cycles is intact.

Why Milloy Makes Sense

Milloy offers something FSU genuinely needs: versatility and left-handed power. The Redmond, Washington native was a three-time all-state selection in high school and showed flashes of her potential at Oklahoma — particularly her .706 slugging percentage, which points to power that her home run total doesn't fully capture in a limited sample.

With two years of eligibility remaining, Milloy would give FSU a player who can grow within the program. For a coaching staff rebuilding after losing two stars, adding a young, versatile piece with upside is exactly the profile that makes sense.

Whether the commitment comes in the next few days or takes longer to develop, Milloy represents FSU's opportunity to add one more piece to what is quietly becoming a more formidable rebuild than early headlines suggested.

The Bigger Picture for 2027

Florida State was the preseason No. 2 team in the country heading into 2026, behind only Texas. The program has the infrastructure, the coach, and now — piece by piece — a roster that could compete for a return to the WCWS.

Francik alone makes FSU dangerous. A team built around an ace pitcher, a reliable two-way threat in Danley, two power additions from the portal, and potentially Milloy is not a team to dismiss heading into the fall.

The 2026 portal window hurt. It also forced a rebuild that, when complete, may surprise people.

What's Next

Watch for any announcement on Milloy in the coming days. If FSU secures her commitment, head coach Lonni Alameda will have navigated one of the toughest offseasons in recent program history and emerged with a competitive roster. Fall practice in August will be the first true test of this new group's chemistry and depth. The Seminoles aren't rebuilding — they're reloading.
