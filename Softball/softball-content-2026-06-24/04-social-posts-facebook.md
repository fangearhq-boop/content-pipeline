### STORY 2: FSU Targets Tia Milloy — Seminoles' Last Big Offseason Move

**Long-Form Post**
Florida State's summer rebuild isn't finished — and that's actually good news for Seminole fans.

The FSU softball program lost the two biggest names in the 2026 transfer portal. Isa Torres went to Texas. Jaysoni Beachum went to Florida. It was a painful 15-day window in Tallahassee.

But the Seminoles have been building back, and there's one more move in play.

FSU is pursuing Tia Milloy, who entered the transfer portal after Oklahoma's Super Regional exit. Milloy hit .353 with four home runs and a .706 slugging percentage in 41 games for the Sooners. She has two years of eligibility remaining. The Seminoles see her as the final piece of their power-hitting overhaul.

Here's what FSU already has for 2027:

Jazzy Francik won ACC Pitcher of the Year. She is the anchor.

Ashtyn Danley is a proven two-way player who returns.

Ella Dodge (Tennessee) committed to Tallahassee, bringing 23 home runs from her time with the Vols.

Nicole Edmiaston (Stetson) adds 11 more.

That's 34 combined home runs from two portal additions alone — more than Torres and Beachum produced together at FSU.

If Milloy joins this group, Florida State will enter 2027 with a roster that's tougher than it looks on paper. Programs built on elite pitching survive. And Francik is elite.

Don't write off the Seminoles just yet.

### STORY 4: Nebraska 2027 — Can the Cornhuskers Return to OKC Without Jordy Frahm?

**Long-Form Post**
Nebraska's 2026 season was one for the record books.

The Cornhuskers went 51-6. They made the Women's College World Series for the first time since 2013. They won a walk-off thriller over Tennessee in pool play. They reached the semifinals before falling to the Vols 2-1 in nine innings.

And Jordy Frahm — their two-way superstar — was named USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year. She hit 19 home runs. She went 20-4 on the mound. She threw 133 pitches in a single WCWS game. She was everything to a Nebraska team that hadn't been to Oklahoma City in more than a decade.

Now Frahm's college career is over.

The question for head coach Rhonda Revelle-Deifel and the Cornhuskers is simple, and also enormous: can this program sustain that level without the player who made it happen?

The evidence for yes: Nebraska built a 27-game win streak. They swept strong opponents. They went 51-6 in total — that kind of dominant record requires depth, not just one great player.

The evidence for concern: replacing a two-way Player of the Year is extraordinarily hard. There's no precedent for finding someone who can do what Frahm did.

The offseason will tell us a lot. Watch for Nebraska's portal activity, their fall camp reports, and whether Revelle-Deifel can restock the pitching staff.

One of 2026's great stories. One of 2027's most compelling questions.
