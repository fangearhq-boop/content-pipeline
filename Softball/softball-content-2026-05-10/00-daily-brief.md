## Key Facts Summary

- **Texas wins first SEC title:** Texas defeated Alabama 7-1 in the SEC Softball Tournament championship. Teagan Kavan threw a complete game with 12 strikeouts — second-most ever in an SEC Tournament title game. Katie Stewart (2026 SEC Player of the Year) went 3-for-5 with 2 RBI and a solo HR. Texas improved to 42-10. Alabama scored first on an Alexis Pupillo solo HR but Texas answered and never looked back. Texas's first-ever SEC Tournament championship.
- **Megan Grant breaks NCAA HR record:** UCLA's Megan Grant hit her 38th home run of the season in the Big Ten Championship game vs. Nebraska, breaking the all-time NCAA single-season record previously held by Laura Espinoza (Arizona, 1995 — 37 HR in 72 games). Grant set the record in 55 games. Nebraska still won 7-2 behind Jordy Frahm (CG, 8 K). Grant tied the record (37th) vs Wisconsin in the semis, then broke it in the final.
- **Arizona State wins Big 12 title:** No. 6 seed Arizona State defeated No. 3/4 Texas Tech 4-0 in the Big 12 Championship at Devon Park, OKC. Kenzie Brown pitched a complete game two-hit shutout (9 K). Three Sun Devils homered in the third inning: Tanya Windle, Emily Schepp, and Katie Chester. ASU's first Big 12 Tournament title in the conference's era; fourth conference championship in program history. Brown named Big 12 Tournament MVP.
- **FSU wins 20th ACC title:** Florida State defeated Virginia Tech 2-1 in the ACC Championship at Palmer Park, Charlottesville VA. Jazzy Francik struck out 10, allowed one earned run in 11.2 innings in the tournament, named MVP. Kennedy Harp doubled; Bella Ruggiero scored on a passed ball for FSU's go-ahead run. FSU's 20th ACC Tournament title, 10th under coach Lonni Alameda. VT was the No. 3 seed.
- **Selection Show tonight:** Sunday, May 10, 7 PM ET on ESPN2. Beth Mowins, Jessica Mendoza, Michele Smith, Amanda Scarborough, Holly Rowe in studio. 64-team field, 31 automatic bids + 33 at-large. Texas projected as No. 1 national seed (42-10, SEC champion, leads D-I with 20 Quad 1 wins). Nebraska projected No. 4 (45-6, Big Ten champion). FSU guaranteed top-16 seed (regional host). Regionals May 15-18, Super Regionals May 22-24, WCWS May 28-June 3 Devon Park OKC.
