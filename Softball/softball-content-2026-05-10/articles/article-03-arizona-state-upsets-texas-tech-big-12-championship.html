Arizona State Stuns Texas Tech 4-0 to Win Big 12 Softball Championship — Kenzie Brown's Masterclass

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | May 10, 2026

The No. 6 seed Sun Devils shut out the nation's most dominant team behind Kenzie Brown's two-hit complete game and three home runs in one inning to claim ASU's first Big 12 Tournament title.

Nobody outside of Tempe believed Arizona State could do this.

The No. 6 seed Sun Devils walked into Devon Park in Oklahoma City on Saturday and delivered one of the most complete upsets of the 2026 softball season, defeating No. 3/4 Texas Tech 4-0 to claim the Big 12 Softball Tournament championship. It was clean, dominant, and exactly what Arizona State needed to announce itself to the national seed committee ahead of Sunday night's Selection Show.

Kenzie Brown Was the Difference

Everything starts with Kenzie Brown. The All-American pitcher went the distance against one of the sport's most feared lineups, throwing a complete game two-hit shutout with nine strikeouts. Brown allowed just one run total across 14 tournament innings. She was named the Big 12 Tournament Most Valuable Player, and it wasn't close.

Texas Tech had been rolling all season behind NiJaree Canady's brilliance. But Saturday belonged to Brown. She attacked hitters early in counts, worked efficiently through a stacked Red Raiders lineup, and gave the Sun Devils exactly what they needed: clean innings with no margin for error. She delivered that, and then some.

Three Home Runs, One Inning

Arizona State scored all four of its runs in the third inning, and they came in the most emphatic way possible. Tanya Windle hit a solo home run to get things started. Emily Schepp followed with another blast. Then Katie Chester went deep as well — three home runs in the same inning, turning a tense 0-0 game into a 4-0 Sun Devils lead before Texas Tech could blink.

Once Brown had that cushion, the game was over. The Red Raiders never mounted a real threat against her precise, aggressive pitching, and the Sun Devils rolled to the final out without breaking a sweat.

A Revenge Win With Program Significance

The context makes this win even richer. Texas Tech swept Arizona State in the regular season, using those meetings to help clinch the Big 12 regular season title. The Sun Devils lost both times. Saturday was their answer — and it was delivered on the biggest stage available.

It is Arizona State's first Big 12 Tournament title in the conference's era, and the program's fourth conference championship overall. The previous three came as Pac-12 titles in 2008, 2011, and 2022. This one is the newest, and arguably the most meaningful, given what they had to overcome to get it.

What's Next

Arizona State earned an automatic bid to the 2026 NCAA Tournament with the Big 12 championship. The bracket drops tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. As conference champions with a high-profile tournament win, the Sun Devils could receive a top-16 seed depending on their RPI and the committee's evaluation — which would mean hosting a regional on their home field.

For Texas Tech, despite the loss, the Red Raiders are still a top national seed candidate based on their dominant regular season. They expect to hear their name called early during tonight's Selection Show.

But right now, the spotlight belongs to Arizona State — the team nobody expected to win the Big 12 Tournament, that just went out and did exactly that.
