NCAA Softball Selection Show Preview: Texas Favored for No. 1 Seed as 64-Team Bracket Drops Tonight

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | May 10, 2026

Championship Weekend delivered four conference champions, a broken all-time record, and a completely reshaped national seed race — all heading into tonight's Selection Show on ESPN2.

The bracket drops tonight.

At 7 PM ET on ESPN2, the 2026 NCAA Softball Tournament Selection Show will reveal the 64-team field, announce the 16 national seeds, and set the path to the Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City. After one of the most dramatic Championship Weekends in recent memory, the race for the No. 1 overall seed is as compelling as it's been in years.

Beth Mowins, Jessica Mendoza, Michele Smith, Amanda Scarborough, and Holly Rowe will be in studio for the reveal. If you have any interest in college softball, tonight's show is appointment viewing.

The Conference Champions

All four major conference tournaments wrapped up Saturday, producing results that will shape the bracket in significant ways.

Texas claimed its first-ever SEC Tournament championship with a 7-1 win over Alabama behind Teagan Kavan's 12-strikeout complete game. Nebraska won the Big Ten title 7-2 over UCLA, extending the Huskers' win streak to 21 games. Florida State earned its 20th ACC Tournament crown with a 2-1 victory over Virginia Tech. And Arizona State delivered the week's biggest upset, shutting out Texas Tech 4-0 behind Kenzie Brown's two-hit masterpiece to win the Big 12 title as the No. 6 seed.

All four programs earn automatic bids to the NCAA Tournament. The national seeds, however, are determined by the full body of work — not just the last game.

The National Seed Race

Texas is the projected No. 1 national seed entering tonight's show. The Longhorns lead Division I softball with 20 Quad 1 wins — the metric that carries the most weight with the selection committee — and their 42-10 record includes the SEC Tournament title. Teagan Kavan's dominance gives them elite pitching to match the résumé.

Nebraska is projected as the No. 4 national seed. The Huskers are 45-6 with a No. 3 RPI and a 21-game winning streak that makes them one of the hottest teams in the country. Their Big Ten championship is the exclamation point on a remarkable regular season.

Florida State is guaranteed a top-16 seed — a regional hosting spot — based on their 49-8 record and top-7 RPI. Texas Tech, despite losing the Big 12 final, enters as a strong at-large candidate for a high seed given their dominant regular season performance.

The Tournament Path

The 64-team field breaks down into 16 regionals (May 15-18), with national seeds hosting four-team pods. Winners of each regional advance to super regionals (May 22-24), also hosted by the higher national seed. The eight super regional winners travel to Devon Park in Oklahoma City for the Women's College World Series, which runs May 28-June 3.

The committee evaluates teams on RPI, strength of schedule, record against top-50 opponents, and conference performance. With Oklahoma's early exit from the SEC Tournament having removed the preseason favorite from national seed contention, the top of the bracket is legitimately open.

What's Next

Tonight's Selection Show at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 is where the season's narrative shifts from conference play to the national stage. Once the bracket is set, the conversation turns to regional matchups, upset potential, and which programs can navigate the road to Oklahoma City.

Texas, Nebraska, Florida State, and Arizona State all bring different strengths. The teams that handle the pressure of regional weekends — and then survive super regionals — will be the ones celebrating on the WCWS diamond in late May and early June.
