Texas Wins First-Ever SEC Softball Championship, Kavan Strikes Out 12 in 7-1 Rout of Alabama

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | May 10, 2026

The Longhorns dominated Alabama with a 12-strikeout complete game from Teagan Kavan and a 3-for-5 performance from SEC Player of the Year Katie Stewart to claim the program's first SEC Tournament title.

Texas softball has its first SEC Tournament championship.

The No. 4 seed Longhorns defeated Alabama 7-1 in Saturday's SEC Softball Tournament championship game at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky, with Teagan Kavan delivering a complete-game masterpiece that announced Texas as a legitimate contender for the No. 1 national seed heading into Sunday night's Selection Show.

In the history of the SEC Softball Tournament, Texas had never won the title. Saturday changed that — emphatically.

Kavan Was Untouchable

Teagan Kavan struck out 12 batters in a complete game, the second-most strikeouts ever recorded in an SEC Tournament title game. She allowed one run — a solo home run by Alabama's Alexis Pupillo in the bottom of the first inning — and nothing else. After that early deficit, Kavan locked in and held one of the sport's best offenses to near silence for the rest of the afternoon.

This is what elite pitching looks like on the biggest stage. Kavan has been the engine of Texas's dominant season, and in the moment the Longhorns needed her most, she was at her absolute best.

Stewart Backs It Up Offensively

If Kavan carried the Longhorns on the mound, Katie Stewart drove them home at the plate. The 2026 SEC Player of the Year went 3-for-5 with a solo home run and two RBI. Texas answered Alabama's first-inning run with two runs of their own in the next half-inning to take the lead, and the Longhorns never looked back. The final hit count: Texas 13, Alabama several less.

Stewart has been the best position player in the SEC this season, and she played like it when the conference championship was on the line. Her performance underscores why she holds the conference's most prestigious individual award.

A Historic Night for the Program

Texas entered the tournament as the No. 4 seed, which meant the road to the championship ran through opponents who were expected to be better. They handled each one, and Saturday's 7-1 victory over the No. 2 seed Alabama was the statement game the program needed going into NCAA play.

The Longhorns improved to 42-10 on the season with the win — a record that includes the most Quad 1 victories (20) of any program in Division I softball. That metric, which measures wins against the sport's elite opponents, is central to how the NCAA selection committee evaluates national seed candidates.

Texas is now, by most projections, the favorite for the No. 1 national seed when the bracket is revealed Sunday night on ESPN2 at 7 PM ET.

What's Next

The 2026 NCAA Softball Tournament Selection Show airs tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. As the projected No. 1 national seed, Texas would host a regional (May 15-18) and a super regional (May 22-24) at home in Austin before any potential trip to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City (May 28-June 3).

Nebraska (Big Ten champion), Florida State (ACC champion), and Arizona State (Big 12 champion) are also expected to receive top seeds. But Texas, with its first SEC title and a résumé built on elite competition, has a strong case to host them all.

For a program still finding its footing in its first years as an SEC member, winning the conference tournament championship in this fashion — on Kavan's arm and Stewart's bat — is a defining moment. The Longhorns are ready for what comes next.
