Florida State Wins 20th ACC Softball Championship — Jazzy Francik Named Tournament MVP in 2-1 Win Over Virginia Tech

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | May 10, 2026

The Seminoles earned program milestone No. 20 in the ACC Tournament with a hard-fought victory over the Hokies, powered by Jazzy Francik's dominant pitching across the week.

Twenty.

Florida State softball has won more ACC Tournament championships than any program in the conference's history, and on Saturday at Palmer Park in Charlottesville, Virginia, the Seminoles added a milestone entry to that legacy with a 2-1 victory over Virginia Tech.

It is the program's 20th ACC Tournament title and the 10th under head coach Lonni Alameda — numbers that belong in a different category than anything else the conference has produced.

Francik Commands the Moment

Jazzy Francik was the heartbeat of Florida State's tournament run, and Saturday was her signature performance. She struck out 10 batters in the championship game, allowing just one earned run across 11.2 total tournament innings. For those efforts, she was named the ACC Softball Championship Tournament MVP.

Francik pitched with the composure of a veteran who has been in big games before, and it showed against a Virginia Tech lineup that had just upset No. 2 seed Duke in the semifinals. The Hokies tested her — but not enough. When FSU needed her to close the door, she did.

A Two-Run Rally That Held

Virginia Tech scored first, putting the Seminoles in an early hole. The Hokies had every reason to believe they could pull off another upset — they'd already stunned Duke 5-0 in the semis as the No. 3 seed.

But Florida State answered in the second inning with a two-run rally that proved decisive. Bella Ruggiero reached on a walk. Kennedy Harp doubled to the wall in center field. Ruggiero came around to score, and later, Harp scored on a passed ball. Just like that, FSU led 2-1. With Francik on the mound, that was enough.

A Program Built for Championship Moments

FSU improved to 49-8 on the season with the win. The Seminoles hold a top-7 national RPI, which guarantees them a spot among the top 16 national seeds in tonight's Selection Show — meaning they will host a regional at Tallahassee.

This championship comes in addition to FSU's 19th ACC regular season title this year. The combination of regular season and tournament crowns in the same season is the kind of conference dominance that makes a program the standard-bearer of its conference.

Lonni Alameda has built something at Florida State that others in the ACC simply cannot match. Twenty titles don't happen by accident. They happen through sustained excellence, elite recruiting, and championship-level preparation — and that's exactly what FSU has demonstrated year after year.

What's Next

The 2026 NCAA Softball Tournament bracket is revealed tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. Florida State is guaranteed a spot as a top-16 national seed, which means hosting a regional (May 15-18) and potentially a super regional (May 22-24) at home. The WCWS runs May 28-June 3 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City.

FSU has the pitching, the lineup depth, and the championship experience to make a run. If Francik is this sharp in the postseason, the Seminoles will be one of the most dangerous teams in the tournament — and one of the hardest to beat at home.
