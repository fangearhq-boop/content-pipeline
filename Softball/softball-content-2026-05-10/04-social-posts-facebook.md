### STORY 1: Texas Wins First-Ever SEC Softball Championship

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET

For the first time in program history, Texas softball is the SEC Tournament champion.

Pitcher Teagan Kavan went the distance, striking out 12 batters — the second-most strikeouts ever recorded in an SEC Tournament title game. She was absolutely locked in from the first pitch.

The Longhorns improved to 42-10 on the season. As the No. 4 seed in the SEC Tournament, they were considered the underdog — but they played their best softball of the year when it mattered most.

This win doesn't just mean a banner at Austin. It means Texas enters tonight's Selection Show with arguably the strongest résumé in the country: SEC Tournament champion, first in D-I with 20 Quad 1 wins, and a record that speaks for itself.

### STORY 2: Megan Grant Breaks All-Time NCAA Single-Season HR Record

#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET

Thirty-one years. That's how long Laura Espinoza's record had stood.

In 1995, Espinoza — then at Arizona — hit 37 home runs in a single season, a mark that seemed untouchable. It survived decades of elite sluggers, technological advances in training and nutrition, and an entire generation of the sport's evolution. Nobody could get to 37.

Until Saturday.

In the 2026 Big Ten Softball Tournament championship game, UCLA's Megan Grant stepped to the plate and launched her 38th home run of the season off Nebraska's Jordy Frahm — a solo shot to left-center that gave the Bruins a 2-0 lead in the third inning. The record was hers.

Grant had tied the record — 37 HRs — in the semifinal victory over Wisconsin just a day before. So in two games on the sport's biggest stage, she did what no one had done in three decades.

The numbers that make this even more remarkable: Espinoza needed 72 games to hit 37. Grant shattered that mark in just 55 appearances. She is the greatest single-season power hitter in the history of college softball.

Nebraska rallied to win 7-2 and claimed the Big Ten title, but the legacy of that championship game now belongs to Grant's moment. Records define eras, and Megan Grant just defined this one.

### STORY 3: Arizona State Stuns Texas Tech 4-0 — Big 12 Champions

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

Nobody saw this coming — except maybe Arizona State.

The No. 6 seed Sun Devils walked into Devon Park in Oklahoma City on Saturday and did something nobody had done all season: they completely shut down Texas Tech, the nation's most dominant team, in a 4-0 championship game victory to claim the Big 12 Softball Tournament title.

Kenzie Brown was the story. The All-American pitcher went the distance with a two-hit shutout, striking out nine batters and allowing just one run total across 14 tournament innings. She was named the Big 12 Tournament MVP. Against a Texas Tech lineup that had demolished opponents all year — the Red Raiders entered ranked No. 3 or 4 nationally — Brown was simply dominant.

The Sun Devils scored all their runs in the third inning when Tanya Windle, Emily Schepp, and Katie Chester all homered, turning a tight game into a 4-0 advantage that Brown made stand up with ease.

The storyline writes itself: Texas Tech swept Arizona State in the regular season. The Sun Devils got their revenge on the biggest stage. This is their first Big 12 Tournament title and the program's fourth conference championship ever (joining their 2008, 2011, and 2022 Pac-12 titles).

For NiJaree Canady and the Red Raiders, the loss doesn't erase a remarkable season — Texas Tech still expects a top national seed tonight. But for Arizona State, this is a banner day.

### STORY 4: Florida State Wins 20th ACC Softball Championship

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Twenty. The number stands alone in ACC softball history.

Florida State defeated Virginia Tech 2-1 on Saturday in the ACC Softball Championship at Palmer Park in Charlottesville, earning the program's 20th conference tournament title — the most in ACC history and the 10th under head coach Lonni Alameda. Nobody else is close.

It was a hard-fought game. Virginia Tech, the No. 3 seed that stunned No. 2 Duke in the semifinals, played FSU tough. The Hokies scored first, putting the Seminoles in an early hole. But FSU answered in the second inning — Bella Ruggiero reached on a walk, Kennedy Harp doubled to the wall in center field, Ruggiero came around to score, and Harp later scored on a passed ball. Just like that, FSU led 2-1.

Then Jazzy Francik locked things down. The ACC Championship Tournament MVP struck out 10 batters and allowed just one earned run across 11.2 tournament innings. She pitched the kind of game that championships are built on — calm, efficient, and devastating when needed.

FSU improved to 49-8 and holds a top-7 RPI. They enter tonight's Selection Show with a guaranteed spot among the top 16 national seeds, which means they will host a regional. After that, anything is possible.

Twenty ACC titles. The dynasty is real, and it just added another chapter.
