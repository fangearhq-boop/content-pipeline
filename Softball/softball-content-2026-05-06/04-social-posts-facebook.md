### STORY 2:

#### Long-Form Post -- 11:00 AM ET

Thirty-one years ago, Lauren Espinoza hit her 37th home run of the season at the University of Arizona and set a record that has never been equaled.

Today, Kendall Wells sits at 36.

One home run. That's the entire distance between where the Oklahoma freshman is and where history lives.

Wells' 2026 season has already been one of the most remarkable individual campaigns in college softball history. She broke the NCAA freshman home run record. She shattered the SEC single-season record. She surpassed the Oklahoma program record set by Jocelyn Alo. One by one, every record below the all-time mark has fallen. The last one standing is Espinoza's 37.

Oklahoma won the double bye as the No. 1 seed. The Sooners don't play again until Thursday in Lexington. That means every day this week builds anticipation for the moment when Wells steps back into the batter's box.

In the A&M series, opposing pitchers walked her six times. That strategy is understandable — nobody wants to be the pitcher who gives up HR No. 37 or 38. But the SEC Tournament changes the calculus. You can't walk Wells in crucial moments with a tournament berth on the line.

At some point this week in Lexington, a pitcher will have to challenge her. And when they do, the entire softball world will be watching.

The record has waited 31 years. It may not survive May 2026.
