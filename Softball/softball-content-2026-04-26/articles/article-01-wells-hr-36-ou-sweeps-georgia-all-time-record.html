Kendall Wells Hits HR No. 36, Oklahoma Sweeps Georgia — All-Time NCAA Record Is One Swing Away

By Sarah Mitchell | April 26, 2026 | I Love Softball

One home run. That is all that separates Kendall Wells from the most storied individual record in the history of college softball.

The Oklahoma freshman hit her 36th home run of the season Sunday in the Sooners' 3-1 series-clinching win over No. 12 Georgia at Love's Field, sweeping the three-game series and pushing her record-breaking season into genuinely uncharted territory. Lauren Espinoza hit 37 home runs for Arizona in 1995 — a record that has stood for 31 years. Wells is one swing away from tying it.

Miali Guachino improved to 13-1, pitching efficiently through a Bulldog lineup that had no answer for OU's offensive firepower. Oklahoma improved to 45-6 on the season, 17-3 in the SEC.

The Math Is Simple. The Moment Is Not.

Wells came into Sunday's game at 35 home runs. She left it at 36. The path to history narrows to a single home run.

The all-time record is not just a number. Espinoza's 37 home runs came in 72 games in 1995, a year when the sport was a different proposition physically and competitively. The fact that Wells is approaching it in her freshman season, against SEC-caliber competition week after week, reframes the record entirely. She is not chasing it through a soft schedule or a fortuitous draw. She has hit 36 home runs against some of the best pitching in the country.

Georgia entered Sunday having lost just 15 games all season. Their pitching staff, led by Randi Roelling (14-7 going in), was not a pushover. Wells hit No. 36 anyway.

Oklahoma opened the scoring in the first inning with a two-run shot. Georgia pulled within one through an RBI single from senior Tyler Ellison. The Sooners answered in the third inning with a solo blast to extend the lead. That was enough for Guachino, who closed it out cleanly for her 13th win of the season.

What a Sweep Means for the SEC Race

Clinching the Georgia series matters beyond the individual narrative. Oklahoma is now 17-3 in SEC play, holding a one-game lead over Alabama (16-3) in the race for the No. 1 seed at the SEC Tournament. Alabama has been dominant as well — Saturday night they run-ruled Tennessee 12-0 in five innings — but OU's series sweep Sunday keeps that gap at one game heading into the final week of the regular season.

The SEC Tournament begins May 5 in Lexington, Kentucky. A No. 1 seed means a double bye, home field advantage in positioning, and the top bracket placement heading into the tournament. For a program with WCWS aspirations, it matters. OU is fighting to keep every edge they can.

Sweeping Georgia — a program ranked No. 12 and sitting at 10-10 in SEC play — was the kind of dominant series win that reinforces OU's place at the top. Guachino's 13th win of the season confirms that the pitching staff, even without a single dominant ace-level performance, is deep enough to complement what Wells and the lineup can do.

What's Next

Oklahoma's final week of the regular season begins this week, with Wells sitting at 36 home runs and the all-time NCAA record within reach at 37. Every at-bat is now appointment viewing — and every Sooner home game is a potential history moment. The SEC Tournament follows on May 5 in Lexington, with the WCWS selection show on May 10 and the Women's College World Series beginning May 28 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. For Wells, the all-time record is one swing away. For Oklahoma, the mission is clear: keep winning until the very end of the season.
