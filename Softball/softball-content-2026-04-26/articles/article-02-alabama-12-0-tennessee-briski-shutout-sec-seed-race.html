Alabama Crushes Tennessee 12-0, Briski Dominant in First Run-Rule of Lady Vols' Season

By Emily Rawlings | April 26, 2026 | I Love Softball

It was supposed to be a top-10 showdown on Tennessee's Senior Night. It became Alabama's most emphatic statement of the season.

No. 2 Alabama run-ruled No. 8 Tennessee 12-0 in five innings Saturday night at Sherri Parker Lee Stadium in Knoxville, handing the Lady Vols their first run-rule loss of the entire 2026 season. Jocelyn Briski threw a complete-game shutout, chasing Tennessee's Karlyn Pickens from the circle after just 2.2 innings as the Crimson Tide scored in four of the five innings to turn the game into a rout.

Alabama improved to 44-4 on the season, 16-3 in the SEC. With Oklahoma sweeping Georgia on Sunday to go 17-3, the Crimson Tide trail the Sooners by one game in the SEC race heading into Game 2 today at 3 PM CT on ESPN2.

Briski Sets the Tone, Pickens Silenced

Karlyn Pickens is one of the best pitchers in college softball. The Tennessee ace came into the weekend with a resume that made her a WCWS contender and an All-American candidate. Saturday night, Alabama's lineup made her work from the very first inning — and the work wore her down quickly.

Pickens was pulled after 2.2 innings, unable to stop the tide. Alabama chased her before the game reached the midpoint, a sequence that defined the evening's tone. Peyton Hardenburger and Erin Nuwer absorbed the bulk of the remaining innings as Alabama continued to pile on.

Briski, meanwhile, was immaculate. The complete-game shutout was not just clean — it was commanding. Tennessee never got into scoring position in a meaningful way, and Briski's command through five innings left the Lady Vols without answers from start to finish. For a team that prided itself on avoiding run-rule losses all season, Saturday's 12-0 defeat was a sobering experience.

The SEC Race Is One Game

Alabama's emphatic win on Saturday, combined with Oklahoma's series sweep of Georgia on Sunday, sets up a simple equation for the final week of SEC play. The Sooners lead the Tide by one game in the conference standings. Alabama controls their own destiny — win every game left on the schedule and wait to see if OU stumbles.

The prize is the SEC Tournament No. 1 seed, which means a double bye in Lexington starting May 5. That's not a minor distinction. In a conference as deep as the SEC, getting into the tournament bracket without playing the first day or two could be the difference between a title and an early exit.

Alabama's response to the SEC race pressure on Saturday was to produce their best performance of the week. That kind of answer, against a Senior Night crowd at one of the conference's best venues, says something about the Crimson Tide's composure at this stage of the season.

What's Next

Game 2 of the Alabama-Tennessee series is today, Sunday April 26, at 3 PM CT on ESPN2. Alabama leads the series 1-0 and enters riding the momentum of Saturday's dominant performance. Tennessee, playing the final home series of the regular season for their senior class, will be motivated by the painful loss to bounce back in front of their home crowd. Alabama (44-4, 16-3 SEC) needs every conference win to stay within striking distance of Oklahoma (45-6, 17-3 SEC). The SEC Tournament begins May 5 in Lexington, Kentucky. The race is down to one game with one week to play.
