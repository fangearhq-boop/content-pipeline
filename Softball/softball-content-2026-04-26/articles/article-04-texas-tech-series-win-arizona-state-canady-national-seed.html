Texas Tech Wins Series at Arizona State, Canady Continues Big 12 Dominance

By Sarah Mitchell | April 26, 2026 | I Love Softball

Texas Tech came to Tempe for one final road trip of the regular season — and left with the series win that keeps their Big 12 title march on track.

No. 2/5 Texas Tech claimed the series against No. 24/RV Arizona State, with NiJaree Canady handling the majority of work in the circle and the Red Raiders' offense delivering when it needed to. Gerry Glasco's program is 9-1 on the road in Big 12 conference play, holding a .895 conference winning percentage — numbers that reflect sustained dominance, not occasional flashes.

Canady's Final Road Stretch

This was the final road series of the regular season for Texas Tech, and Canady treated it like any other Big 12 weekend — methodically efficient. She struck out nine batters and allowed three earned runs across her outings, maintaining her position as the most decorated pitcher in college softball's 2026 class. Her 18-3 record and 1.29 ERA, combined with the AUSL Golden Ticket she received on-field in a ceremony earlier this month, underscore how dominant a season she is having.

Arizona State came in at No. 24/RV and has been one of the surprises of the Big 12 this season, building a Sun Belt-level resume into genuine conference contender territory. Canady and the Red Raiders treated their series as professional business, taking the series win and returning the Arizona State crowd to silence.

Game 1 Friday was a shutout. Game 2 Saturday was another TTech win. The Red Raiders' consistency on the road — outscoring opponents, outpitching them, and closing out series — is the reason they are positioned where they are in the national conversation.

The Big 12 Title Is Within Reach

Texas Tech entered this final road series in control of the Big 12 race. The series win at Arizona State further tightens the Red Raiders' grip on the title. One more series win locks it up outright.

From a national seed perspective, TTech is firmly in the top-2 conversation alongside Oklahoma. Both programs have cases. OU has the SEC dominance and Wells's home run record. TTech has Canady's statistical brilliance and a near-perfect conference record. The selection committee will have a compelling debate on May 10.

Finishing the road schedule 9-1 against Big 12 opposition is not something that happens by accident. It is the result of Canady's ability to compete in hostile environments, a lineup that creates runs even when opposing pitchers are at their best, and a coaching staff that has the Red Raiders in a groove at exactly the right point in the calendar.

What's Next

Texas Tech returns home to close out the regular season before the Big 12 Tournament. The WCWS selection show is May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. TTech's national seed case is built on Canady's resume, a .895 conference winning percentage, and a road record that proves the Red Raiders can win anywhere. The final rankings of the regular season will be released Monday in the NFCA Week 13 poll — and TTech figures to remain firmly in the top two alongside Oklahoma.
