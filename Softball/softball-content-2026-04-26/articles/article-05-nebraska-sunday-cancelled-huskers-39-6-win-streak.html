Nebraska's Sunday Game Cancelled by Weather, But the Huskers' Win Streak and National Seed Are Safe

By Emily Rawlings | April 26, 2026 | I Love Softball

Mother Nature stepped in Sunday to cancel Nebraska's series finale against Iowa, but the Huskers had already done the work that mattered.

Nebraska swept a Friday doubleheader at Bowlin Stadium, beating Iowa 9-1 and then 5-2 to extend their winning streak to 14 games and push their record to 39-6, 19-1 in the Big Ten. The Sunday game was called off due to expected severe weather — a footnote on a weekend that the Huskers controlled from the very first pitch on Friday.

Win No. 40 will have to wait. Nebraska's place in the national seed conversation will not.

A Week That Defined Nebraska's Season

The Friday doubleheader was not just about the two wins. It was about the crowd. The 3,396 fans who packed Bowlin Stadium for Game 2 set a new program attendance record — a signal that Nebraska softball is not just performing well on the field, but capturing the attention and imagination of a fan base that historically has given more of its energy to football and volleyball.

The Huskers swept the doubleheader cleanly. Iowa, sitting at 5-13 in Big Ten play, was not positioned to challenge Nebraska's momentum. The 9-1 opener and the 5-2 nightcap extended the win streak and kept Nebraska on pace for one of the most successful regular seasons in program history. The Huskers entered the weekend ranked No. 4 in the NFCA poll — the highest ranking in program history — and spent the weekend confirming that ranking was deserved.

The 14-game win streak spans multiple top-25 opponents, road series, and high-pressure Big Ten matchups. This is not a soft schedule inflating a record. Nebraska has earned every win.

39-6 and Still Counting

Win No. 40 is one game away. The Big Ten title — Nebraska is 19-1, needing just a few more wins to clinch — is essentially secured. The national seed conversation, once a topic of speculation for a program that has never hosted an NCAA Regional, is now a genuine expectation.

Softball America's bracketology has listed Nebraska as a national seed candidate for several consecutive weeks. The NFCA committee will make the final determination on May 10, but Nebraska has presented a compelling case: non-conference wins over Texas, LSU, Texas A&M, Stanford, and South Carolina; a Big Ten record that is historically dominant; a winning streak that has now reached 14 games with no sign of stopping.

The Sunday cancellation is the kind of thing that can be rescheduled or left behind without consequence for a team in Nebraska's position. The Huskers have more than enough quality wins to make their case without a rematch against Iowa.

What's Next

Nebraska concludes the regular season this week, with the Big Ten Tournament to follow. The WCWS selection show is May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2, where Nebraska is expected to hear their name called as a national seed for the first time in program history. Win No. 40 — and the chance to make even more history — is just one more game away. The 14-game win streak and a 39-6 record make the Huskers one of the stories of the 2026 college softball season.
