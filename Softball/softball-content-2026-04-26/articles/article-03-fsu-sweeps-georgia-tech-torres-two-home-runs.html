FSU Sweeps Georgia Tech, Torres Continues Historic Week With Two More Home Runs

By Jordan Reeves | April 26, 2026 | I Love Softball

Isa Torres is doing something remarkable — and the timing could not be better for Florida State.

The Seminoles completed their series sweep at Georgia Tech on Sunday, with Torres hitting two more home runs to pace the offense. It was the third time this week Torres had produced a multi-home run performance, turning what was already an impressive offensive campaign into something the entire college softball world is paying attention to. FSU sweeps the series and exits Atlanta with a cleaner national seed argument than they arrived with.

Torres' Power Surge

Thursday, Torres hit two home runs against No. 5 Florida. Friday, Jazzy Francik threw a no-hitter and Torres hit two more against Georgia Tech in Game 1. Sunday, two more in the series-clinching Game 3. Three multi-home run games in the span of four days against ACC competition.

Torres opened Sunday's game with a leadoff home run, setting the tone immediately. Her second blast later in the game turned the lead into a comfortable margin FSU did not relinquish. The pattern this week — lead off with a homer, add another — has been the recurring signature of the Seminoles' offensive identity during this stretch.

For context: hitting multiple home runs in a single game is an accomplishment. Doing it three times in four days, against teams with quality pitching, borders on the extraordinary. Torres has carried the FSU offense on her back during one of the most important weeks on the schedule, and the Seminoles responded with a series sweep of an ACC opponent that came into the weekend with a winning conference record.

The National Seed Case Gets Stronger

Florida State entered this weekend at 41-7, 16-3 in the ACC. The three-game sweep of Georgia Tech adds three more wins to a resume that already featured the series-clinching win over Florida earlier in the week. FSU is finishing the regular season on one of the hottest stretches in the country.

In the national seed conversation, what matters is how you look in the final weeks — and FSU looks excellent. The Seminoles have quality wins, a strong conference record, and a pitcher in Francik who can throw a no-hitter in any game she takes the ball. Torres provides the kind of elite offensive upside that can change a regional or super regional in a single at-bat.

The WCWS selection show is on May 10. FSU has done everything asked of them in the final stretch. Gerry Glasco and the Seminoles' national-caliber program is entering the postseason in the best possible form.

What's Next

Florida State's regular season concludes this coming week, with the ACC Tournament to follow. The WCWS selection show is May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. FSU's case for a national seed — built on Torres's historic offensive stretch, Francik's no-hitter, and a series sweep of a road opponent on Senior Day — is one of the strongest they have submitted in recent years. Watch for the Seminoles' seeding when the bracket is revealed.
