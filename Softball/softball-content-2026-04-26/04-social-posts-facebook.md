## STORY 1: Wells HR No. 36 / OU Sweeps Georgia

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET
Kendall Wells is one home run away from history.

The Oklahoma freshman slugger hit her 36th home run of the season Sunday in OU's 3-1 series-clinching win over No. 12 Georgia at Love's Field. Miali Guachino earned the win to improve to 13-1 on the season as the Sooners swept the three-game series and moved to 45-6, 17-3 in the SEC.

Lauren Espinoza hit 37 home runs for Arizona in 1995 — a record that has stood for over 30 years. Kendall Wells, a freshman, is one swing away from tying it.

The context makes it even more remarkable. Wells isn't doing this against cupcake competition in non-conference play. She's doing it in the SEC, in series against top-25 teams, in moments that carry weight. The 36th came in a 3-1 win that clinched a series against one of the better teams in the country.

There is no precedent for what she is doing. Watch every at-bat.

## STORY 2: Alabama 12-0 Tennessee / SEC No. 1 Seed Race

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM ET
Alabama made a statement Saturday night in Knoxville — and it was a loud one.

The No. 2/3 Crimson Tide run-ruled No. 8/9 Tennessee 12-0 in five innings on Tennessee's Senior Night, handing the Lady Vols their first run-rule loss of the entire season. Jocelyn Briski threw a complete-game shutout, and Alabama chased Karlyn Pickens from the circle after just 2.2 innings. The Crimson Tide scored in four of the five innings, turning what was a top-10 showdown into a dominant display.

Alabama is now 44-4, 16-3 in the SEC. Oklahoma, after sweeping Georgia today, is 45-6, 17-3. The SEC No. 1 seed race is still alive — Alabama is one game back.

Game 2 is this afternoon at 3 PM CT on ESPN2. For Alabama to stay in the hunt, they need to keep winning. For Tennessee, a win today in front of the home crowd is a matter of pride on what was already an emotional weekend.
