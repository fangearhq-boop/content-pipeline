Texas Tech Run-Rules Florida 16-7 in Five Innings, Claims Second Straight WCWS Berth

By Jordan Reeves

May 25, 2026

Texas Tech's offensive explosion at Katie Seashole Pressly Stadium produced the first game in NCAA tournament history where both teams scored seven or more runs through the first two innings — and ended with the Gators absorbing their first home mercy-rule loss in program history.

The scoreboard at Katie Seashole Pressly Stadium read 16-7 after five innings on Sunday afternoon, and the Texas Tech Red Raiders were already celebrating on the Gainesville turf. Florida's fans sat stunned. The Gators had never been mercy-ruled at home in program history. They were now.

The 11th-seeded Red Raiders defeated Florida 16-7 in a run-rule Game 3, securing their second consecutive trip to the Women's College World Series and producing one of the most offensively dominant performances in Super Regional history. Five Texas Tech home runs. Sixteen runs in five innings. A tournament record for combined early-inning scoring. And a trip to Devon Park for the Red Raiders, who finished as national runners-up last season.

Historic Offense

This game entered the record books before it was halfway complete. The first two innings produced more than 14 combined runs — the first time in NCAA tournament history that both teams scored seven or more runs through the opening pair of innings. Texas Tech and Florida traded blows like two teams determined to outscore each other into submission, and by the time the dust cleared, the Red Raiders had pulled away decisively.

Texas Tech hit five home runs in the five innings of play. The power display was exactly what an 11-seed wasn't supposed to produce against a Florida program that hosted the Super Regional with a distinct home advantage. But the Red Raiders didn't flinch. They hit the ball over the fence, ran the bases aggressively, and made the Gators pay at every opportunity.

Florida scored seven runs — no small effort — and still ended the game with a 16-7 deficit after just five innings. The Gators' home Super Regional Game 3 record, which had been pristine, was now broken.

The Mia Williams Story

Texas Tech outfielder Mia Williams was at the center of the series' most-discussed subplot. Williams, who transferred from Florida after the 2024 season, was hit by pitches five times across the three-game series — including three times in Game 3 alone. The repeated plunkings generated significant attention, particularly after her father — former NBA star Jason Williams — was ejected from the stands following a confrontation with Florida fans.

Williams responded the way only the best players do. She stayed in the lineup, kept competing, and finished the season as one of the most productive hitters in the country: .435 batting average, 23 home runs, and a .908 slugging percentage. Whatever the circumstances of the Super Regional, Williams performed through them.

Back-to-Back for the Red Raiders

Texas Tech's 2026 WCWS appearance is their second consecutive. Last season, the Red Raiders made their first-ever WCWS trip and advanced all the way to the national championship game, where they fell to Texas. They return to Devon Park not as a Cinderella story, but as a program that has established itself as a legitimate national contender.

Advancing as an 11-seed adds another layer to the accomplishment. Texas Tech didn't back into this tournament. They beat a Florida program at Florida's own venue, in a deciding game, by nine runs.

What's Next

Texas Tech opens WCWS play Thursday, May 28, at noon ET on ESPN against Mississippi State — a fellow upstart program making its first-ever WCWS appearance. The matchup is the most intriguing first-round game on the bracket for neutral fans: two programs outside the traditional powers, both having made stunning runs to reach Devon Park. For Texas Tech, the goal is simple — go further than last year's runner-up finish, and bring home the title the Red Raiders came within one game of winning in 2025.
