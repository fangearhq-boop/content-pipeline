Teagan Kavan's Shutout Sends Texas to Third Straight WCWS as Defending Champions

By Sarah Mitchell

May 25, 2026

Texas's ace absorbed a loss in Game 1, took the circle again in Game 3, and delivered a complete-game shutout to punch the defending champions' ticket to Devon Park for a third consecutive year.

On Saturday, Teagan Kavan walked off McCombs Field in Austin having absorbed a loss. Arizona State had beaten her in Game 1, pushed the defending national champions to the brink, and entered Sunday needing just one more win to end Texas's season.

On Sunday, Kavan walked off the same field after 106 pitches of complete-game shutout softball, and the Longhorns were headed to Devon Park for the third consecutive year.

Texas defeated Arizona State 5-0 in Game 3 of the Austin Super Regional, completing the series comeback and sending the defending champions back to the Women's College World Series. Kavan was the story from the first pitch to the last.

Kavan Under Pressure

The measure of a great pitcher is not the games she dominates — it's the games she absolutely has to win. Sunday was that game. Texas's season was on the line. Arizona State came in with confidence after winning Game 1 of the series. Kavan had already started twice in this Super Regional. And yet she was the answer her coaching staff turned to.

She threw 106 pitches and didn't allow a run. Arizona State managed hits, but they didn't score. Texas played defense behind her. The 5-0 final was not as close as a one-run game's tension would suggest — Kavan was in command from the opening inning, and Arizona State's lineup never found a way through.

Over three starts in this Super Regional, Kavan faced a team that beat her once. The season finale was hers.

Arizona State's Remarkable Run

For Arizona State, the Super Regional loss ends a 45-17 season that featured one of the most impressive tournament runs a non-seeded team has made in recent years. The Sun Devils beat Texas in Game 1 — the defending national champions, at home — and forced a Game 3. That alone is an achievement that will be remembered in Tempe.

Arizona State showed that mid-major programs can compete on the biggest stage when they're healthy, pitching well, and playing with confidence. They weren't supposed to push Texas this hard. They did. The program will carry that experience into the 2027 season.

Three Straight for the Longhorns

Texas has now reached the WCWS in three consecutive seasons. They won the national championship in 2025, and they return to Devon Park in 2026 with the target on their back that defending champions always carry.

No team in the current era has won back-to-back WCWS titles. Texas will have the chance to change that. Kavan's arm, the Longhorn offense, and the program's elite recruiting base give them every tool they need to compete for another championship. Whether they can sustain that level through a full week of double-elimination softball at Devon Park is the question that will define their 2026 season.

What's Next

Texas opens the 2026 WCWS on Thursday, May 28, at 2:30 PM ET on ESPN against No. 2 Tennessee — another program that finished with a top-10 national seed and has been dominant all season. It is the kind of first-round matchup that could serve as a preview of the championship series. Kavan vs. Tennessee's lineup, on the sport's biggest stage, with a spot in the winners bracket on the line — that's must-watch softball.
