Mississippi State Shuts Out Oklahoma 6-0, Ends 9-Year WCWS Streak in Historic Upset

By Sarah Mitchell

May 25, 2026

In one of the most stunning upsets in Super Regional history, the Bulldogs blanked the Sooners on their home field — ending a nine-year dynasty run and sending Mississippi State to their first Women's College World Series.

Nobody expected this. Nobody drew it up. And yet, on Sunday afternoon at Love's Field in Norman, Mississippi State outfielder Kinley Keller jogged across home plate, the scoreboard read 6-0, and one of the most dominant programs in the history of college softball watched their season end.

The Bulldogs defeated No. 1 Oklahoma 6-0 in Game 3 of the Norman Super Regional, ending Oklahoma's nine-consecutive-year streak of reaching the Women's College World Series — the longest active WCWS streak in the sport — and clinching Mississippi State's first-ever trip to Devon Park.

The Pitcher Nobody Knew

In the biggest game in Mississippi State softball history, head coach Samantha Ricketts handed the ball to Delainey Everett. The junior had thrown just 13.1 innings all season before this Super Regional. She was not the ace. She was not the known commodity. She was the wildcard.

She was also, on Sunday, untouchable.

Everett went the distance against Oklahoma's lineup, allowing three hits and striking out three. She walked nobody. More significantly, she became the first pitcher to hold Oklahoma scoreless in 399 games — the last time the Sooners were shut out was at the 2019 WCWS. Patty Gasso's program had not been blanked in nearly seven years of competition. Everett did it in the most important game of the season, against the team everyone expected to win it all.

It was a performance that defied statistical expectation. But moments that change program trajectories rarely arrive on schedule.

The Offense Did Its Job

Mississippi State wasn't just a one-pitcher story. The Bulldogs scored six runs against Oklahoma — no small feat against a program known for elite pitching. Kinley Keller went 2-for-4 with three RBIs, providing the offensive foundation that gave Everett enough room to work. Morgan Bernardini and Paige Ernstes each hit home runs.

Oklahoma's offense, which had averaged well over six runs per game on the season, generated three hits and nothing more. The game wasn't close. The 6-0 final read like a statement.

Wells Hit by Pitch, Returns — But the Season Ends

Amid the historic result, Oklahoma sophomore catcher Kendall Wells absorbed a painful moment of her own. In the bottom of the third inning, Everett's pitch struck Wells in the jaw. She left the field, returned to the dugout holding ice to her face, and was then back behind the plate in the fourth inning — the kind of toughness that defined her entire 2026 season.

Wells finishes the year with 39 home runs — second all-time in NCAA history behind UCLA's Megan Grant (40). She came to Sunday needing just two home runs to tie and pass Grant's record. She didn't get the swings she needed, and now she'll wait until 2027. But the fact that she came back after a pitch to the face to try to keep Oklahoma's season alive says everything about the competitor she is.

Oklahoma ends the 2026 season at 52-10. It is their finest record in years. It is also their first season without a WCWS appearance since 2015.

The Streak That Defined an Era

Nine years is a number that demands perspective. Since 2017, Oklahoma under Patty Gasso won multiple national championships, developed generations of elite players, and set the standard for what a college softball dynasty looks like. The WCWS was not just a destination for the Sooners — it was a given. Something you scheduled around.

Now it's over. Not forever, almost certainly. But for the 2026 season, the Sooners will watch from home as the sport's biggest stage opens May 28 in Oklahoma City. No. 1 seed. 52-10 record. A freshman slugger with 39 home runs. And still, none of that was enough against a pitcher who barely pitched all year.

Mississippi State didn't just beat Oklahoma. They ended an era's unbroken run — and started one of their own.

What's Next

Mississippi State heads to Devon Park in Oklahoma City for the first Women's College World Series appearance in program history. The Bulldogs open Thursday, May 28, against Texas Tech at noon ET on ESPN — two programs that few expected to see at this stage. For Mississippi State, every inning at Devon Park will be uncharted territory. For Everett, whose name nobody outside the program knew a week ago, the stage just got larger. And for a fanbase that has never seen their team play in the WCWS, Thursday can't come soon enough.
