Kendall Wells' Season Ends at 39 Home Runs — Megan Grant's Record Survives the 2026 Season

By Emily Rawlings

May 25, 2026

The most captivating home run chase in recent college softball history concluded Sunday without the record-breaking swing. Wells leaves the 2026 season with 39 — and a reason to come back swinging in 2027.

In the third inning of Sunday's Game 3 in Norman, a pitch struck Kendall Wells in the jaw. She left the field. Her teammates gathered. Her season — and perhaps her best chance at Megan Grant's all-time home run record — appeared to wobble in real time.

Wells came back out in the fourth inning. She wore her catcher's gear. She got back behind the plate. She is, after all, that kind of player.

But Oklahoma's season ended anyway, 6-0, against a Mississippi State team that nobody expected to be there. And Wells finished the 2026 season with 39 home runs — one fewer than Grant's all-time NCAA record of 40.

A Record That Didn't Fall

Grant set the NCAA single-season home run record at 40 this season for UCLA. Wells arrived at the Super Regional with 39 — needing one swing to tie, two to pass. For three games, that swing never came on the right pitch.

The Wells-Grant chase was the most compelling individual narrative in college softball this season. Two players, same season, racing toward a record that had stood for years. At various points, Wells was tracking ahead of Grant's pace, behind it, and right alongside it. The script seemed to be building toward a dramatic WCWS showdown — both players, both records, both teams at Devon Park.

Sunday ended that possibility. Grant (40 HRs) will play at Devon Park with UCLA. Wells (39 HRs) will watch from home.

The Kind of Player Wells Is

Taking a pitch to the face in a win-or-go-home game and returning to play says as much about Wells as any of her 39 home runs. Oklahoma needed leadership in that moment. She provided it by walking back out to the pitcher's circle and doing her job. The Sooners still lost. But Wells didn't make her season smaller on the way out.

Wells finishes 2026 as the second-greatest single-season home run hitter in NCAA history. No catcher in college softball has ever hit 39 home runs in a season. No freshman has ever come this close to the record she chased. The 39 sits in the books as one of the great individual seasons in the sport's history, regardless of outcome.

Oklahoma's Season Ends at 52-10

The Sooners finish with a 52-10 record — a number that would represent a tremendous year for almost any program in the country. For Oklahoma, it is simultaneously an achievement and a disappointment. Nine straight years of WCWS appearances. A No. 1 seed. Kendall Wells. None of it was enough.

Mississippi State ended the streak with a shutout, and Oklahoma's season concluded without the WCWS trip their record suggested they deserved. The sport is unforgiving in that way — a single series, a single game, a single pitch to the jaw can change everything.

What's Next

Kendall Wells is expected to return to Oklahoma for the 2027 season. At that point, Grant's record of 40 will be the target — a mark set by a player who played at the same time, in the same season, chased by the same competitor who came within one home run of matching it. The sport will be watching. And Wells, who came back after a pitch to the face on the biggest day of her college career so far, has shown every indication that she will too.
