WCWS 2026 Field Is Set: Eight Teams, Four Matchups, Everything You Need to Know

By Emily Rawlings

May 25, 2026

Three decisive Game 3s on Sunday completed the 2026 Women's College World Series bracket. Devon Park opens Thursday, May 28 — here's your complete guide to the eight teams, the matchups, and the storylines that will define the tournament.

Three games. Three results. Eight teams bound for Oklahoma City.

Sunday's Super Regional finales — Mississippi State over Oklahoma, Texas Tech over Florida, and Texas over Arizona State — completed the 2026 Women's College World Series bracket. Devon Park opens Thursday, May 28, with a full day of softball beginning at noon ET and running through the night. The field features defending champion Texas, two first-time participants (Arkansas and Mississippi State), last year's runners-up Texas Tech, and NCAA home run record-holder Megan Grant of UCLA.

Here's what you need to know.

The Opening-Day Schedule (May 28)

Game 1 at noon ET on ESPN: No. 3-seeded Texas Tech vs. No. 5 Mississippi State. The 11th-seeded Red Raiders and the first-time Bulldogs open the WCWS in what may be the year's most compelling Cinderella bracket.

Game 2 at 2:30 PM ET on ESPN: No. 1 Texas vs. No. 2 Tennessee. The defending national champions against one of the sport's most consistent programs. This may be the marquee first-round matchup.

Game 3 at 7:00 PM ET on ESPN2: No. 1 Alabama vs. No. 2 UCLA. Alabama enters on a dominant run after sweeping LSU. UCLA carries Megan Grant (40 HRs) and the all-time single-season home run record into Devon Park.

Game 4 at 9:30 PM ET: No. 4 Nebraska vs. No. 6 Arkansas. Nebraska snapped a 13-year WCWS drought. Arkansas makes their first-ever appearance. Two programs experiencing something entirely new in Oklahoma City.

The Stories Worth Following

Texas opens as the defending national champion, making their third straight WCWS appearance. No team in recent memory has won back-to-back titles. Whether the Longhorns and ace Teagan Kavan — who threw a complete-game shutout Sunday in a win-or-go-home Game 3 — can sustain that level over another week of pressure softball is the tournament's central question.

Megan Grant needs only to take the field to make history. The UCLA outfielder broke the NCAA single-season home run record at 40 this year — a mark Kendall Wells was chasing with 39. Grant at Devon Park, with the all-time record and a potential national title in the same trip, is a story that extends beyond softball.

Texas Tech returns as last year's national runners-up. The Red Raiders, an 11-seed, knocked out Florida in a Game 3 run-rule on Sunday and are now in their second consecutive WCWS. They open against Mississippi State — a fellow first-timer — in a matchup that figures to be one of the tournament's most wide-open contests.

The SEC brings four teams to Oklahoma City: Alabama, Arkansas, Mississippi State, and Tennessee. No conference has more participants, and no conference better positioned itself through Super Regional play.

The First-Timers

Arkansas and Mississippi State are the WCWS's first-time participants this season, and both programs earned their spots in stunning fashion. Arkansas swept Duke to secure their first-ever berth. Mississippi State blanked No. 1 Oklahoma 6-0 in Game 3 — ending Oklahoma's nine-year WCWS streak in the process.

First-time WCWS teams play with a different kind of freedom. They have nothing to defend, no expectation to manage, and a fanbase experiencing something unprecedented. In recent years, first-timers have made deep runs precisely because they play without the weight of history. Both Arkansas and Mississippi State will bring that energy to Devon Park.

What's Next

Devon Park opens Thursday, May 28, with the full first round. The double-elimination tournament will run through the championship series, which concludes June 4 or 5. All games will be broadcast on ESPN or ESPN2. I Love Softball will have coverage of every round, every result, and every major development as the 2026 champion is crowned in Oklahoma City.
