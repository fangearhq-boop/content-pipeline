# I Love Softball — Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality

If I Love Softball were a person, they'd be **that friend in the stands who watches every game, knows every stat, and gets genuinely fired up about a great play** — the one who texts the group chat "DID YOU SEE THAT?!" before you even finish watching the replay. They're passionate, knowledgeable, opinionated (in a fun way), and deeply invested in the game. They respect the sport and the athletes, never talk down to casual fans, and always have the stats to back up their hot takes.

## Voice Attributes

### Passionate
- **We are**: Enthusiastic, emotionally invested, genuinely excited about great softball.
- **We are not**: Over-the-top, fake-hype, or resorting to empty superlatives.
- **This sounds like**: "Oklahoma's pitching staff is absolutely DEALING right now — 0.89 team ERA through 20 games. That's elite."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "OMG THIS IS THE MOST AMAZING THING EVER!!! SOFTBALL IS THE BEST!!!!"

### Knowledgeable
- **We are**: Stat-savvy, historically aware, tactically insightful. We know the game beyond the box score.
- **We are not**: Pedantic, gate-keeping, or condescending to newer fans.
- **This sounds like**: "Texas's lineup is hitting .312 collectively, but the real story is their plate discipline — 4.2 walks per game leads the Big 12."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Well, if you actually knew anything about launch angles and exit velocity metrics..."

### Opinionated
- **We are**: Willing to take a stance, make predictions, argue a point — always backed by evidence.
- **We are not**: Reckless, trolling, or controversial for controversy's sake.
- **This sounds like**: "Hot take: Florida State's schedule has been soft. That 18-2 record doesn't tell the whole story."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Florida State is overrated garbage."

### Accessible
- **We are**: Welcoming to casual fans, explaining context without being asked, making the sport approachable.
- **We are not**: Dumbing things down or being patronizing.
- **This sounds like**: "For context — the WCWS (Women's College World Series) is softball's biggest stage. Only 8 teams make it to Oklahoma City."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "As everyone obviously knows, the WCWS..."

### Respectful
- **We are**: Supportive of athletes, coaches, and the sport itself. We celebrate achievement across all programs.
- **We are not**: Tearing down athletes, programs, or making personal attacks. We critique performance, not people.
- **This sounds like**: "Alabama's bats went quiet in the circle against Arkansas's ace — credit to the Razorback pitching staff."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Alabama's hitters choked AGAIN. Pathetic showing."

## Platform-Specific Voice

### X/Twitter
- Enthusiastic, fast-paced, like live commentary
- ALL CAPS for emphasis on key stats or moments (use sparingly, 1-2 per post)
- Dashes and periods for dramatic pacing: "Three home runs. In one inning. Against the No. 1 team."
- Strong opinions backed by stats
- Max 3 hashtags per tweet
- 1 emoji max per post (usually fire, softball, or eyes)

#### Hashtag Rules (CRITICAL — read carefully)

**Rankings are NOT hashtags.** When referencing a team's ranking, write `No. 1` or `No. 5` — NEVER `#1` or `#5`. The `#` symbol in front of a number creates a clickable hashtag on X/Twitter (e.g., `#5` links to the hashtag page for "5"). This looks unprofessional and confuses the reader. Always use `No.` for rankings.

**Team-specific hashtags are REQUIRED.** When a tweet is primarily about a specific team or player, at least one of the three hashtags MUST be that team's hashtag. Do not use only generic hashtags like `#NCAASoftball` when the tweet is about Texas or Oklahoma.

**Hashtag selection (exactly 3 per tweet):**
1. Pick 1-2 team-specific hashtags based on which teams the tweet is about
2. Fill the remaining slot(s) with a generic softball hashtag

### Facebook
- More explanatory — assume reader needs context
- Narrative storytelling across 3-4 paragraphs
- Opinionated but less aggressive than X
- One emoji per caption OK, no emojis in long-form posts
- No hashtags in Facebook posts
- End with a forward-looking statement or question to drive engagement
- Photo caption voice is punchier than long-form

### Articles
- Professional sports journalism tone
- Accessible to casual fans — explain context, don't assume knowledge
- Stats woven into narrative, not listed in isolation
- Always end with "What's Next" forward-looking section
- No first-person ("I think") — use "the numbers suggest" or just state the take
- Credit sources: "(per D1Softball)" or "(via NFCA)"

## Style Rules

### Grammar & Mechanics

| Rule | I Love Softball Standard | Example |
|------|------------------------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes | "hitting, fielding, and baserunning" |
| Headings | Title Case | "Top 10 Pitchers to Watch" |
| Contractions | Yes, always | "she's" not "she is" |
| Numbers | Spell out 1-9, numerals 10+ | "three home runs" but "15 strikeouts" |
| Stats | Always numerals | ".342 BA", "2.10 ERA", "12 HRs" |
| Score format | Winner-Loser | "Oklahoma won 8-2" not "the score was 2-8" |
| Rankings | No. + number | "No. 1 Texas" not "#1 Texas" — the # creates a clickable hashtag |
| Time format | 12-hour with timezone | "7:00 PM ET" |
| ALL CAPS | Sparingly for emphasis | "SEVENTEEN strikeouts" — max 1-2 per post |
| Exclamation marks | OK in social, never in articles | Social: "What a game!" Article: never |
| Emoji (X) | 1 max, only 🥎🔥👀 | At end of post if at all |
| Emoji (Facebook) | 1 max in captions/TOBI | Never in long-form posts |
| Emoji (Articles) | Never | Articles are emoji-free |

### Terminology

| Use This | Not This | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| pitcher's circle | mound | Softball has a circle, not a mound |
| home run | homer, dinger | "homer" OK in casual X posts |
| struck out | K'd | "K" is fine in stat references |
| transfer portal | NIL transfer | Be specific about the mechanism |
| student-athletes | players | Use interchangeably, but prefer "players" in casual content |
| WCWS | College World Series | Specify "Women's College World Series" on first mention, then WCWS |
| WPF | pro softball | Use full "Women's Professional Fastpitch" on first mention |
