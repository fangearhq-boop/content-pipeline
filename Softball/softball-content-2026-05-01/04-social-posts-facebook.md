### STORY 1:

#### Long-Form Post -- 9:00 AM ET

She needs one more.

Kendall Wells is sitting at 36 home runs — one swing away from tying Lauren Espinoza's 31-year-old all-time NCAA single-season record of 37, set at Arizona back in 1995. Tonight, Wells and No. 1 Oklahoma take the field at Texas A&M for Game 2 of their final regular-season series (5 PM CT, SEC Network, Davis Diamond in College Station).

The backdrop is staggering. Wells has already broken the NCAA freshman record, the SEC all-time record, and the OU program record. She's been the most talked-about player in college softball all year — and she's just getting started as a freshman.

Oklahoma won Game 1 of this series. One more win tonight clinches the SEC regular season title outright. So the Sooners are playing for a record AND a championship in the same at-bat. That's the kind of thing that makes college softball must-watch television.

This is the first-ever SEC series between Oklahoma and Texas A&M. Two former Big 12 rivals now battling on a bigger stage, with history hanging on every pitch.

If Wells hits No. 37 tonight, the internet breaks. If she doesn't — she gets two more chances Sunday.

Either way, we're watching.

The SEC Tournament runs May 5-9 at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky. The top 4 seeds don't play until the quarterfinals — a massive advantage. Seeds 5-9 get an opening-round bye. Seeds 10-15 play on the opening day (Tuesday, May 5).

Every game this weekend matters. The bracket is being written right now.

### STORY 4:

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 PM ET

Texas Tech has been college softball's best-kept open secret all season.

The Red Raiders are 47-4. Back-to-back Big 12 regular season champions. No. 2 in the NFCA poll. And they have NiJaree Canady — one of the most dominant pitchers in the country — at the top of their rotation.

Canady crossed 1,000 career strikeouts earlier this season, becoming one of the rare pitchers to reach that milestone. She's been a workhorse and an ace all in one, giving Texas Tech a weapon that most programs can only dream about.

Now the Red Raiders head to Devon Park in Oklahoma City for the Big 12 Tournament (May 7-9). Texas Tech is the No. 1 seed and faces the No. 8 seed on May 7 at 1:30 PM CT. A tournament title would cement their case for a top-2 national seed — and potentially a regional host site.

From Lubbock to Oklahoma City to the Women's College World Series — that's the path the Red Raiders are walking. And with Canady on the mound, it's a path that runs straight through everyone else.

### STORY 5:

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM ET

Four Sooners. One list. No other team is even close.

Oklahoma leads the nation with FOUR players named to the USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year Top 25 list, announced April 29:

🔹 Kendall Wells — .377 BA, 36 HR, 79 RBI, 1.113 slugging percentage. The freshman phenomenon chasing the all-time NCAA home run record is also one of the top award candidates in the country.

🔹 Gabbie Garcia — 22 home runs, 66 RBI. The sophomore shortstop is one of just two sophomores on the entire list.

🔹 Kai Minor — One of only two freshmen on the 25-player list, joining Wells.

🔹 Ella Parker — A first-time selection to the list.

The list narrows to 10 finalists on May 13. Then to a Top 3 on May 18. The winner is announced ahead of the WCWS (May 28).

Oklahoma having four players in this conversation isn't just impressive — it's historically dominant. No other program comes close with multiple honorees, and OU has four.

Coach Patty Gasso has built something special in Norman. And this list is just one more reminder.
