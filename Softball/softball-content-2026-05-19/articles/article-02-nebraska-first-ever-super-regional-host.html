Nebraska Hosts Its First-Ever Super Regional — And Two Omaha Natives Make It Unforgettable

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | May 19, 2026

In the history of Nebraska softball, the Cornhuskers have never hosted an NCAA Super Regional. That changes Thursday night.

Nebraska (49-6) will welcome No. 13 Oklahoma State to Lincoln for Game 1 of a best-of-three series at 9 PM ET on ESPN2, the Cornhuskers' first home Super Regional in program history. It's the culmination of a season that has been historically dominant, and it comes with a pitching storyline that is, frankly, almost too good to be true.

The Cornhuskers' Season

Nebraska's 49-6 record needs no embellishment. The Cornhuskers have been one of the most efficient defensive teams in the country all season, and their regional performance underscored that dominance: they allowed just one run across three regional games. One run. That is the kind of pitching and defensive performance that championship runs are built on.

The program has not appeared in the Women's College World Series since 2013 — a 13-year drought that has felt longer with each postseason exit. Head coach Rhonda Revelle's program has reached the Super Regional before, but never as host. Thursday represents a new chapter.

The Pitching Duel Nobody Saw Coming

Nebraska's Jordy Frahm and Oklahoma State's Ruby Neylan are two of the best pitchers in college softball. Both have Omaha, Nebraska addresses on their high school transcripts. And both once threw in the same bullpen as youth softball travel ball teammates.

Neylan, now the ace for the Cowgirls, has 29 victories this season and is on track for another All-American nod. Frahm anchored the Nebraska staff that allowed just one regional run. The symmetry of their paths — from the same travel ball dugout in Omaha to opposite circles in a Super Regional — is the kind of story that makes college sports singular.

Oklahoma State (41-15) arrives with momentum of their own. The Cowgirls scored 34 runs in their regional, putting up double-digit totals in every game. If Nebraska's defense is the story of this series for the home team, Oklahoma State's offense is the counter-narrative.

What's Next

Nebraska and Oklahoma State split their two-game regular season series earlier in 2026, with a third game washed out by rain. There is no clear edge from head-to-head history — this series will be decided on merit, in front of what figures to be a sold-out crowd in Lincoln hungry for its first WCWS berth in 13 years.

Game 1 is Thursday, May 21 at 9 PM ET on ESPN2. Game 2 follows Friday, May 22 at 5 PM ET on ESPN2. A deciding Game 3, if necessary, is Saturday at 5 PM ET on ESPN. The winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28.
