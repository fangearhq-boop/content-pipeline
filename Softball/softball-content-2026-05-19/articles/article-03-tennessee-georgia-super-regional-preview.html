Tennessee vs. Georgia: An All-SEC Super Regional in Knoxville for a WCWS Trip

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | May 19, 2026

Knoxville gets another Super Regional. Tennessee is once again the host. The opponent this time is Georgia, a fellow SEC program that has punched its fourth consecutive ticket to this stage. Two orange-blooded rivals — well, orange and red — will settle it at Sherri Parker Lee Stadium with a Women's College World Series trip at stake.

Game 1 tips off Thursday, May 21 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. Game 2 follows Friday, May 22 at 3 PM ET on ESPN2. If a decisive Game 3 is necessary, the teams return Saturday, May 23 at 11 AM ET on ESPN.

Tennessee: A New Program Milestone

The Lady Vols enter at 45-10 overall, 16-8 in SEC play. Tennessee has been one of the sport's most consistent programs, and this week they accomplish something no Tennessee team has ever done before: host a Super Regional for the fourth consecutive year.

Tennessee's Super Regional hosting streak is historic. The program has now appeared in 15 all-time Super Regionals, but four consecutive home series is a milestone that belongs to this generation of Lady Vols. Head coach Karen Weekly's program is seeking its 10th overall WCWS appearance and third in the last four seasons.

The Lady Vols swept the Knoxville Regional without a loss, closing with a 5-1 win over Virginia in the Regional Final.

Georgia: Fisher Makes Her Case

Georgia (41-18, 12-12 SEC) arrives as the No. 10 national seed and a team that has genuinely found its footing when it matters most. The Bulldogs also advanced without a loss from their Athens Regional, closing out Clemson with a dominant 5-0 performance.

The hero of that Regional Final was sophomore pitcher Addisen Fisher, who threw a complete-game shutout — allowing just two hits, issuing two walks, and striking out three. It was the kind of lights-out start that gives Georgia genuine confidence in the circle. Fisher's performance has become the storyline heading into Knoxville: can she replicate that level against a Tennessee lineup that has been one of the SEC's most dangerous all season?

What's Next

Tennessee has the home field advantage and the program pedigree. Georgia has momentum and a pitcher who just looked unbeatable. Something has to give in Knoxville — and the winner adds another chapter to one of the SEC's great postseason rivalries. The road to Oklahoma City runs through East Tennessee this week.

Winners advance to the WCWS at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, where play begins May 28.
