Texas Makes It Seven Straight — Arizona State Arrives in Austin Ready to Interrupt the Streak

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | May 19, 2026

No. 2 Texas does not flinch in the postseason. The Longhorns have been here seven consecutive times, and they have not changed their approach: dominate at McCombs Field, control the circle, and make opponents prove they can keep up.

Arizona State arrives Friday night with a nine-run margin of victory in their most recent game. Consistency versus momentum. Experience versus hunger. That is the Super Regional at McCombs Field.

Texas: Ruthless in the Regional

Texas (45-10) did not waste energy in the Austin Regional. The Longhorns defeated Wagner, Wisconsin, and Baylor — each game by at least seven runs. The closest game ended 7-0. There were no miracles needed, no comeback required. Texas was simply better than every team they faced and handled business efficiently.

This is the program's seventh consecutive Super Regional appearance. That kind of run requires sustained excellence in recruiting, pitching development, and tournament execution. Under head coach Mike White, Texas has become the model of postseason consistency in women's softball.

A seventh straight Super Regional berth is impressive. A WCWS trip would validate the entire 2026 season.

Arizona State: Back and Dangerous

Arizona State has not appeared in a Super Regional since 2022. Their return in 2026 was announced loudly: the Sun Devils traveled to College Station and dismantled regional host Texas A&M 9-1 by run rule. Run-ruling a Power Four host in a regional final is one of the harder things to do in this sport. Arizona State made it look manageable.

The Sun Devils carry legitimate momentum into Austin. They are not the same program that Texas might have faced in a more favorable matchup. They run-ruled an SEC school on the road to get here, and that is the kind of performance that follows a team into the next round.

What's Next

Game 1 is Friday, May 22 at 9 PM ET on ESPN2 at McCombs Field in Austin. Game 2 follows Saturday, May 23 at 7 PM ET on ESPN. A deciding Game 3, if necessary, is Sunday at a time to be announced.

Texas is the prohibitive favorite in this series — home field, program pedigree, and seven consecutive appearances do not happen by accident. But Arizona State just showed what they are capable of against a hostile crowd and a quality opponent. This series may be closer than the seeds suggest.

The winner advances to the WCWS at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, opening May 28.
