Kendall Wells Ties All-Time NCAA HR Record at 37 — One Homer From History

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | May 19, 2026

Oklahoma freshman Kendall Wells needs one home run. Just one. And when she gets it — possibly as early as this Thursday in Norman — she will own the most famous individual record in college softball history.

Wells entered the 2026 Super Regionals with 37 home runs on the season, tying the all-time NCAA single-season mark set by Arizona's Laura Espinoza in 1995. That record has stood for 31 years. It survived the sport's golden age of power hitters. It survived the transfer portal era. And now a 19-year-old freshman from Oklahoma is one swing away from making it hers.

A Record-Breaking Spring

The numbers are almost impossible to believe. Wells didn't just approach the record — she detonated every milestone standing between her and Espinoza's mark.

She broke the NCAA freshman home run record at 31. She set the SEC single-season home run record at 27. She surpassed Jocelyn Alo — one of the sport's greatest power hitters — to claim the Oklahoma program record at 35. With each milestone, the national spotlight on Wells grew brighter.

Her 37th came in Oklahoma's Norman Regional last weekend, a blow that matched Espinoza's 31-year-old standard. Wells is hitting close to .380 on the season and leads the entire country in home runs. She is not a one-tool player who stumbled into a hot streak. She is the most dominant offensive force the sport has seen in a generation.

What the Record Means

Espinoza's record from 1995 predates much of modern college softball. The sport has evolved dramatically since then — better pitching, more advanced scouting, larger fields, and the physical grind of conference play in the SEC. The fact that the record has endured this long speaks to how truly exceptional Espinoza's season was.

What makes Wells' achievement even more staggering is the level of competition she faced. Oklahoma plays in the Southeastern Conference, where every weekend series features elite pitching. Wells hit 27 of her home runs in SEC play alone, setting the conference record in the process.

No. 3 Oklahoma (seeded second among Big 12 programs in the tournament) opens its Super Regional against Mississippi State on Friday, May 22 at 1 PM ET on ESPN2. Alyssa Faircloth, who threw a no-hitter and a program record 14-strikeout shutout to help Mississippi State advance, will be the primary obstacle standing between Wells and history.

What's Next

The Oklahoma-Mississippi State Super Regional is one of the most compelling matchups of the round, with Wells' record chase adding a layer of national intrigue that extends far beyond the softball community. A packed Love's Field in Norman will be watching every at-bat.

If Wells reaches 38, she becomes the undisputed all-time single-season home run leader in NCAA softball history. If Oklahoma advances to the WCWS, her record chase could continue all the way to Oklahoma City. And if the Sooners win it all, Wells' freshman season will go down as the greatest individual offensive performance in the sport's history — not a debate, just a fact.
