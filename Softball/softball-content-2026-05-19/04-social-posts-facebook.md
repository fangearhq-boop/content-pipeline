### STORY 1: Kendall Wells — Tied at 37, One HR from History

**Long-Form Post**
🥎 ONE HOME RUN FROM HISTORY 🥎

Oklahoma freshman Kendall Wells needs just one more home run to own the most remarkable record in college softball history — the NCAA single-season home run record.

Wells hit her 37th homer of the season during the Sooners' Norman Regional last weekend, tying the mark set by Arizona's Laura Espinoza back in 1995. Let that sink in for a second: a record that has stood for 31 years is now within one swing of being erased.

And Wells didn't just sneak up on 37. She obliterated records along the way:

• Broke the NCAA freshman home run record at 31
• Set the SEC single-season home run record at 27
• Surpassed Jocelyn Alo to set the Oklahoma program record at 35
• Tied the all-time NCAA record at 37

She's a FRESHMAN. Batting close to .380. Leading the entire nation in home runs. And the stage could not be bigger — Oklahoma faces Mississippi State in the Super Regionals beginning Friday, May 22.

One homer and history is rewritten. We might be watching the greatest individual offensive season in college softball history, and it's not over yet.

### STORY 2: Nebraska Hosts First-Ever Super Regional

**Long-Form Post**
🌽 HISTORY IN LINCOLN 🌽

This Thursday, Nebraska softball will do something it has never done before in program history: host an NCAA Super Regional.

The Cornhuskers (49-6) have been almost perfect in 2026. They allowed just ONE run across all three of their regional games, shutting out every opponent until the final out. They've done it with elite pitching from Jordy Frahm and an offense that never seems to run dry.

And now? They get to do it at home.

But here's the storyline that makes this Super Regional truly special: Nebraska's Jordy Frahm will face Oklahoma State's Ruby Neylan in the pitching circle. Both are from the Omaha area. Both are considered among the best college pitchers in the country. And both once threw side-by-side as travel ball teammates in high school.

Now they're on opposite sides of a game that could end one program's 13-year WCWS drought.

Nebraska hasn't been to the Women's College World Series since 2013. Oklahoma State missed it just last season — ending a run they'd like to resume.

Game 1 is Thursday, May 21 at 9 PM ET on ESPN2. Something special is brewing in Lincoln.

### STORY 3: Tennessee vs Georgia

🍊 ALL-SEC IN KNOXVILLE 🍊

When the NCAA brackets were revealed last week, the Tennessee-Georgia Super Regional jumped off the page: a battle of two SEC programs at Sherri Parker Lee Stadium for a trip to Oklahoma City.

Tennessee comes in at 45-10. The Lady Vols have been one of the sport's most consistent programs — and now they're hosting a Super Regional for the fourth consecutive year. That's never happened in program history.

For Tennessee, this is more than just a Super Regional. It's a program-defining week. They've made two WCWS trips in the last four seasons. Another appearance would cement this era as one of the best in Lady Vols softball history.

### STORY 4: Arkansas vs Duke

**Long-Form Post**
🐗 ARKANSAS' UNFINISHED BUSINESS 🐗

Five Super Regionals. Five losses. That's the Arkansas softball story under head coach Courtney Deifel — a program that has done everything right except reach the Women's College World Series.

The Razorbacks are back for a sixth try.

Arkansas (45-11) dominated the Fayetteville Regional, winning every game by run rule. The combined score across three games: 27-3. They're playing some of the best softball of the Deifel era, and Bogle Park is one of the most hostile environments in the sport.

Standing in their way: No. 12 seed Duke. The Blue Devils (43-15) are one of college softball's great consistent programs — this is their FOURTH Super Regional appearance in the last five years. They came through an elimination gauntlet at the Durham Regional, losing to Arizona before beating Marshall, then Arizona twice in a row to advance.

Duke doesn't just show up. They find a way to win.

Something has to give Friday morning in Fayetteville. Arkansas chasing their first WCWS bid. Duke refusing to go home quietly. This is exactly the kind of matchup that defines the Super Regional round.

Game 1: Friday, May 22 at 11 AM ET on ESPNU. Game 2: Saturday, May 23 at 4 PM ET on ESPN2.

When the NCAA Tournament started, Texas didn't blink. The Longhorns defeated Wagner, Wisconsin, and Baylor in Austin — winning each game by at least seven runs. No drama, no close calls, no sweat.

Now comes the real test.

No. 2 Texas (45-10) hosts Arizona State in a Super Regional beginning Friday night at McCombs Field. For the Longhorns, it's the seventh consecutive Super Regional appearance — a run of postseason consistency that few programs in any sport can match.

The Sun Devils will face the defending program that never lets up. This one could surprise people.

Game 1: Friday, May 22 at 9 PM ET on ESPN2. Game 2: Saturday, May 23 at 7 PM ET on ESPN.
