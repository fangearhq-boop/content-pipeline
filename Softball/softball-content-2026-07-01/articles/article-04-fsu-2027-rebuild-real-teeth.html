FSU 2027: The Seminoles Lost Two Stars — Then Added 22 Home Runs and Kept the ACC's Best Pitcher

By Sarah Mitchell July 1, 2026

The Losses Everyone Is Talking About

Florida State's 2026 portal cycle generated its share of damaging headlines. Isa Torres — who hit .530 with 78 runs scored, making her one of the most dynamic offensive players in the ACC — transferred to Texas. Jaysoni Beachum — who hit .417 with 16 home runs — transferred to Florida. Two elite bats, one portal cycle, two rival conference programs. The narrative that emerged was predictable: FSU is in trouble.

The narrative is incomplete.

What the Seminoles Actually Did

Florida State did not absorb those departures and wait. Head coach Lonni Alameda went to work in the portal, and the additions deserve the same analytical attention as the losses.

Ella Dodge arrived from Tennessee. She hit .285 with 11 home runs in 2026 for the Volunteers. Nicole Edmiaston arrived from Stetson, bringing another 11 home runs. Together, FSU's portal additions account for 22 home runs entering the 2027 roster — not a number that replaces Torres and Beachum's offensive production on a one-for-one basis, but a number that represents legitimate, proven run-producing talent joining the program.

Then there is the question of who is returning. Jazzy Francik — who won ACC Player of the Year as a pitcher in 2026 — is back. So is Ashtyn Danley. The Seminoles are not starting from scratch in the circle or the lineup. They are adding around a pitching anchor who is already the best in the conference.

The Francik Equation

Francik's continued presence at FSU changes the calculus for every projection of the 2027 season. The ACC Player of the Year in the circle is not a piece you replace through the portal if you lose her — she is the piece every other program's portal additions are chasing. FSU does not have to find a Francik. FSU has a Francik. That matters more than any pair of outfield departures when building the full accounting of what the Seminoles will look like next spring.

Programs in genuine freefall do not retain ACC Players of the Year. They watch them transfer to programs that are better positioned. The fact that Francik is returning says something about what FSU's internal environment looks like heading into 2027 — and about the faith Alameda's staff has in what they are building.

The Honest Assessment

FSU in 2027 will be different from FSU in 2026. Torres's .530 average and 78 runs are not easily replaced by any standard of portal math. Beachum's 16 home runs created a specific brand of pressure that Dodge and Edmiaston will now need to provide, in a conference where opposing pitching staffs are built to suppress exactly this type of production.

But "different" is not "diminished." The Seminoles have 22 new home runs from proven college players, an established pitching ace, and a coaching staff experienced enough to have survived transition cycles before. The question FSU faces in 2027 is not whether they can compete — it is whether Dodge and Edmiaston perform closer to their ceiling or their floor against ACC-level competition. That is a genuine unknown. It is also a team with a real chance.

What's Next

The most revealing information about FSU's 2027 ceiling will come out of PGF Nationals and fall practice. Alameda's staff is now looking forward to the next recruiting cycle — the Class of 2028 contact window opens September 1. What the Seminoles accomplish in the immediate term on the recruiting trail will determine how deep this rebuild can go and how quickly. The headline about losing Torres and Beachum was written the day they left. The headline about what FSU built in their wake is still being written.
