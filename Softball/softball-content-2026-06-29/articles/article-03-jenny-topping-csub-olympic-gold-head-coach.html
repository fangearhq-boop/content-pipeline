Jenny Topping Won Olympic Gold in Athens. Now She's Running a College Softball Program.

By Jordan Reeves June 29, 2026

The Hire That Most People Missed

On June 24, Cal State Bakersfield announced that Jenny Topping would be their new head softball coach. The announcement did not generate the kind of national coverage that a Power Four hire would receive. CSUB is a Big West program in a mid-major conference. Most of the sport's attention in late June is on transfer portal tracking and power rankings.

But the Topping hire deserves a closer look — because her résumé is unlike anything that typically ends up at a mid-major program, and what she could build in Bakersfield is a genuinely compelling story.

What She Accomplished as a Player

Topping was a catcher. In 2004, she stood on the Olympic podium in Athens, Greece, and received a gold medal as part of the United States national softball team. In 2006, she was part of another Team USA squad that won the World Championship. Those are not participation credentials. They represent the highest level the sport has ever reached, achieved by a player who operated at its most demanding position.

Playing at that level provides a kind of knowledge that no amount of coaching development can fully replicate. Topping stood in the box against the best pitching in the world, called games behind the plate at international tournaments, and competed in pressure situations where nothing about the environment is controlled. That experience does not automatically translate into great coaching — history is full of elite players who could not teach — but it is a foundation that, in the right person, can become something extraordinary.

What She Built as a Coach

Before the CSUB hire, Topping spent the 2025 and 2026 seasons as the hitting and catching coach at Oregon State. The Beavers' team batting average climbed from .271 in 2025 to .288 in 2026 while she was responsible for that part of the program. One year of offensive improvement can come from any number of factors. Two years of consistent growth in the same direction is harder to explain without crediting the coach responsible for it.

She now steps up from coordinator to head coach, which is a meaningful transition. Head coaches manage everything — staff, recruiting, budget, culture, opponent preparation — in ways that coordinators do not. It is a different job. But the question was never whether Topping could hit and teach hitting. The question is whether she can run a program. At Cal State Bakersfield, she gets the chance to answer that.

What CSUB Gets

Cal State Bakersfield plays in the Big West Conference and has been working to rebuild its program after a difficult 2025 season. The hire of an Olympic gold medalist with recent Power Five coaching experience signals ambition — a willingness to look outside the traditional pipeline of coaches and bet on someone whose credentials begin at the very top of the sport.

CSUB also gets recruiting credibility that the previous staff may not have had. When Topping sits across from a prospect's family, she has a story to tell that no coaching clinic or assistant coaching career can match. She wore USA across her chest at the Olympic Games. That narrative opens doors — particularly in California, where elite softball talent is plentiful and the competition for top recruits is fierce even at the mid-major level.

What's Next

Topping will begin building her staff and recruiting class for 2027 immediately. Fall camp will be her first opportunity to install the offensive philosophy that she developed at Oregon State on a full program scale. The timeline for what success looks like at CSUB is measured in years, not months. But the trajectory is interesting, and the foundation is unusual. Not many programs in college softball can say their head coach is a two-time world champion and Olympic gold medalist. Cal State Bakersfield can — and that alone makes the 2027 season worth watching.
