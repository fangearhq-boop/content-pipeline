Tennessee Made the WCWS Semifinals and Still Overhauled Its Coaching Staff

By Sarah Mitchell June 29, 2026

The Departures

When Tennessee exited the Women's College World Series at the semifinal stage in 2026, the expectation was that the program would regroup, address a few roster gaps through the transfer portal, and return largely intact for 2027. What happened instead signals that head coach Karen Weekly is pushing for something more than a semifinal finish.

Coordinators Craig Snider and Stephanie Sanders were not retained after the season. Both had been part of the Tennessee staff that guided the Vols to the WCWS. Their departures are not an indictment of what they did — reaching the final four of the national tournament is an achievement — but they are a statement of intent. Weekly is looking for something different in her coaching room heading into 2027, and those changes are starting at the coordinator level.

Leach-Gartner Joins the Staff

Aubrey Leach-Gartner was added to Karen Weekly's staff on June 15, filling one of the vacancies left by the departures. Leach-Gartner is a former Oklahoma player — an infielder and catcher with Sooner experience in one of the most competitive conferences in the sport — who spent time working in professional softball before making the move to Knoxville.

The hire carries an interesting layer given the Oklahoma-Tennessee dynamic. The Sooners and Vols have competed in the same regional bracket multiple times in recent years, and players who succeed at Oklahoma understand firsthand what it takes to compete at the WCWS level. Leach-Gartner brings that background into a Tennessee program that is actively trying to take the next step.

Roster Turnover

Two Tennessee players also entered the transfer portal after the season: outfielder Saviya Morgan, who hit .293 in 2026, and freshman pitcher Kailey Plumlee, a left-handed arm with four years of eligibility remaining. Morgan is an experienced outfielder moving on. Plumlee is a more significant departure — four years of eligibility is a long window, and losing her before she established herself in the rotation leaves a gap that Weekly's staff will need to address.

Tennessee's 2027 roster will be built around returning core players, new staff voices, and whatever additions the Vols make through traditional recruiting and any remaining portal activity. The program has the resources and recruiting relationships to reload. The question is whether the staff reshuffling produces the different outcome Weekly is clearly pushing toward.

What This Signals About Weekly's Direction

Karen Weekly has been one of the most respected coaches in college softball for decades. She does not make major staff changes without careful deliberation. The decision to part with two coordinators after a WCWS semifinal finish is the kind of move that a coach makes when she believes the current ceiling is not the ceiling she is aiming for.

Tennessee has been close. WCWS appearances have been consistent. But a national championship has been elusive, and the programs currently sitting between Tennessee and that goal — Texas, Texas Tech, Florida, Alabama — are all making significant moves of their own. For Weekly, standing still may not be an option. The staff overhaul is her answer to that pressure.

What's Next

Tennessee's full 2027 staff will come into focus over the next few weeks as any remaining coordinator positions are filled. The Vols' recruiting class for 2027 will reflect whether this staff transition attracts the kind of talent that can push Tennessee from WCWS semifinalist to WCWS champion. The summer showcase season beginning with PGF Nationals in July will be the first major recruiting test for Leach-Gartner and whatever staff takes shape around her. Watch for Tennessee to be active in the 2027 high school class as they rebuild their recruiting pipeline around the new coaching voices.
