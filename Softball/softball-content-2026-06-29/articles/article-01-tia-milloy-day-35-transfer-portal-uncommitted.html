Day 35. Still Uncommitted. Tia Milloy Is Not Just a Pinch-Runner.

By Sarah Mitchell June 29, 2026

What the Numbers Actually Say

Tia Milloy entered Oklahoma's transfer portal on May 25. Today is June 29. That is 35 days without a commitment — and in a sport that moves fast in the summer, the slow-play is worth examining. Not just for the drama of it, but because the narrative around who Milloy actually is has been imprecise in ways that matter.

She is not just a pinch-runner. In 41 games for Oklahoma in 2026, Milloy hit .353 with 4 home runs in 34 at-bats and a .706 slugging percentage. Those are the numbers of a player with genuine bat-to-ball skills and power upside — not a speed-only specialist who squeezed out a few spot starts. In two seasons at Oklahoma she appeared in 91 games with 16 starts, building a utility profile that includes experience at multiple positions across two years in one of the most competitive conferences in the sport.

Two Years Changes Everything

Milloy has two years of eligibility remaining. That is the most important number in this recruitment. A player with one year left calculates the decision differently — she takes the best available offer quickly because the window is narrow. A player with two years remaining can afford to be deliberate. She is not picking a landing spot for one season. She is choosing where she will spend two full spring campaigns, and that difference shapes how she weighs each option.

Two years also gives the receiving program more flexibility. The team that lands Milloy does not need to extract everything from her immediately. She can develop, establish herself in a new system, and produce across two seasons without the pressure of an immediately-or-never situation. That makes her more valuable and gives her more leverage, which is precisely why she has not rushed.

The Programs and Their Cases

FSU's case begins with the roster gap. The Seminoles lost Isa Torres and Jaysoni Beachum in the same offseason — two of the most productive players in the country. They added Ella Dodge from Tennessee and Nicole Edmiaston to fill some of those holes. But an experienced utility player who can start at multiple positions and bring Power Four credibility to a 2027 roster that is still rebuilding its identity? That is exactly what Milloy represents for FSU, and the Seminoles have not hidden their interest.

Tennessee's argument is stability. Head coach Karen Weekly has built one of the most consistent programs in the SEC, and the Vols made the WCWS semifinals in 2026. They offer a winning environment without the transition chaos of programs that just lost multiple All-Americans in a single offseason. The recent staff changes — adding Aubrey Leach-Gartner while parting with coordinators Craig Snider and Stephanie Sanders — signal that Weekly is retooling rather than rebuilding, which might appeal to a player who wants established structure.

Texas Tech assembled what Softball America called the No. 1 portal class in the country this cycle — Polar, Heavener, and Pickering are three All-American-caliber players arriving in one window. Adding Milloy would give the Red Raiders experienced utility depth that no amount of star power in their class addresses. But the scholarship math may not work as cleanly as it did six weeks ago, and there is a legitimate question about whether a player of Milloy's caliber wants to be roster piece eight on a team already loaded with higher-profile arrivals.

Why July Matters

The summer calendar is moving. PGF Nationals in Huntington Beach begins in late July, and the evaluation period for the 2027 high school class will occupy a growing share of every coaching staff's attention. Milloy has commanded meaningful focus from three Power Five programs for over a month. That focus does not disappear, but it does get divided as July progresses.

The realistic announcement window is narrowing — not because Milloy has lost leverage, but because the summer showcase season shifts the sport's attention regardless of who is still uncommitted. The program that lands her will have made a compelling final case before that shift fully takes hold.

What's Next

Watch for movement in the first two weeks of July. If Milloy has not committed by the time PGF Nationals begins, the story becomes about whether any of the pursuing programs will hold out through the entire summer or redirect recruiting resources toward the 2027 class. Either scenario is newsworthy. Her commitment, whenever it comes, ends one of the longer-running individual recruitment stories of this offseason and signals a real addition for a program that knows exactly what it is getting.
