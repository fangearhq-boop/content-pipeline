Washington's Ramuno and Rehbein Combine for No-Hitter, Extend Win Streak to 19

By I Love Softball Staff March 30, 2026

A Combined No-Hitter for the Huskies

While much of the college softball spotlight focused on SEC rubber matches on Sunday, No. 16 Washington had already made its own statement on Saturday. Sophia Ramuno and Rylee Rehbein combined to hold Iowa hitless through seven innings in a 10-0 Washington victory at Iowa City — the Huskies' first combined no-hitter of the 2026 season and their 12th shutout of the year. The win extended Washington's win streak to 19 consecutive games.

Ramuno carried the bulk of the work, taking the no-hitter into the sixth inning with a line of 5.2 innings pitched, zero hits, two walks, and six strikeouts. Rehbein finished the final 1.1 innings to secure the complete-game gem. The performance was dominant from first pitch to last — and it continued Washington's run as one of the most quietly impressive programs in the country this spring.

The Numbers Behind Washington's Surge

Twelve shutouts in a season is a remarkable figure. It means that in more than one-third of their games, Washington has held opponents scoreless for seven innings — and most of those games have been against quality competition. The Huskies play in the Big Ten, a conference that has produced multiple top-20 programs this season, making that 12-shutout total even more impressive.

The 19-game win streak places Washington among the hottest programs in the country. The Huskies entered the season ranked but have built their résumé steadily through results. Sitting at No. 16 in the NFCA's Week 7 poll, Washington has a genuine case to crack the top 10 in the Week 8 rankings that drop Tuesday morning — particularly after the top of the rankings picture was disrupted by Texas's loss to A&M.

Washington as a WCWS Threat

The Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City features eight teams battling through a double-elimination bracket. To win it all, a program needs to be capable of beating anyone in the field. Washington's pitching staff — with Ramuno and Rehbein both capable of throwing no-hitters — gives the Huskies exactly that kind of ceiling. A team that can throw 12 shutouts in a season is built for elimination-game environments where run prevention is paramount.

Washington is one of several programs that doesn't get the same national media attention as SEC or Big 12 schools, but the results speak for themselves. Nineteen consecutive wins. Twelve shutouts. A no-hitter to add to the résumé. The Huskies are making their case one game at a time.

What's Next

Washington continues Big Ten conference play with upcoming games that will further shape their profile as a potential national seed. If the Huskies can maintain this pitching consistency and the offense continues to put up double-digit scores — as it did in the 10-0 blanking of Iowa — Washington will be one of the most difficult programs to eliminate in the NCAA Tournament field. Ramuno and Rehbein have announced themselves as one of the best pitching tandems in college softball. The rest of the country is starting to notice.
