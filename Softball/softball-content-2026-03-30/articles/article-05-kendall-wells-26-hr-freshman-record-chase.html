Kendall Wells Hits No. 26: Four Home Runs From the NCAA Freshman Record

By I Love Softball Staff March 30, 2026

Where Wells Stands Right Now

Kendall Wells hit her 26th home run of the season on Sunday in Oklahoma's 8-4 Game 3 win over LSU, and the context around that number is staggering. Wells leads all of college softball — not just freshmen — in home runs. She has homered in 20 of 37 games this season. She is averaging nearly one home run per game. And she is a true freshman doing all of this in March, with at least 20 games remaining on Oklahoma's schedule.

The number she's chasing is 30 — the NCAA freshman home run record, held jointly by former Oklahoma Sooners Jocelyn Alo and Lauren Chamberlain, along with Soumya Majam. Wells was tied with that SEC freshman record mark last week at 25 and has now moved past it. At 26, she needs just four more to share the all-time NCAA freshman record and five to own it outright.

The Legends She's Chasing

For anyone who follows Oklahoma softball, the names on that record are sacred. Jocelyn Alo finished her career as the all-time home run leader in NCAA softball history with 122 career home runs. Lauren Chamberlain, who played for Oklahoma from 2011 to 2015, held the all-time record for years and was one of the most feared hitters of her generation. Both players were All-Americans, both were program cornerstones, and both set the freshman record of 30 that Wells is now approaching.

To be chasing the record set by two of the sport's most celebrated power hitters — as a true freshman — is a remarkable statement about Wells's talent. Oklahoma head coach Patty Gasso has already drawn comparisons between Wells and those legends. Through 37 games, those comparisons appear well-earned.

Could Wells Chase the All-Time Single-Season Record?

Once Wells matches or surpasses the freshman record, the next milestone in view is the all-time single-season home run record: 37, set by Arizona's Laura Espinoza in 1995. That number is more difficult to reach — Wells would need 11 more home runs while still averaging close to her current pace — but it's no longer an outlandish possibility. With conference play and the postseason still ahead, Wells has plenty of opportunities remaining to make history at multiple levels.

Her impact goes beyond the numbers. Oklahoma's offense is already one of the most feared in college softball, with Isabela Emerling, Gabbie Garcia, and others all capable of going deep. But Wells is the anchor. She bats in a run-producing spot in the lineup, draws pitchers' attention, and forces defensive decisions that benefit everyone around her. She is, by any measure, the most compelling individual storyline in college softball right now.

What's Next

Wells and Oklahoma continue SEC play this week, with the Sooners sitting at 34-3 overall and 8-1 in the conference. The freshman record watch will intensify with every at-bat, and fans across the country have reason to follow Wells's progress regardless of which team they cheer for. The record stands at 30. She needs four more. With the season still in full swing, the history Wells is chasing could arrive at any moment.
