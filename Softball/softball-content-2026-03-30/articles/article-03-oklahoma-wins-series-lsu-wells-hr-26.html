Guachino's Complete Game and Wells's 26th HR Power Oklahoma Past LSU in Series Finale

By I Love Softball Staff March 30, 2026

Oklahoma Bounces Back With Dominant Game 3 Win

After suffering a stunning 3-1 upset at Tiger Park on Saturday — LSU's first win over Oklahoma in 11 years — the Sooners came back on Sunday with a complete performance. Sophomore Miali Guachino threw all seven innings, striking out 11 batters on 149 pitches, as Oklahoma beat LSU 8-4 to claim the series two games to one. The Sooners improve to 34-3 overall and 8-1 in the SEC, securing their third straight conference series win.

Guachino doesn't typically receive the spotlight that comes with Oklahoma's top arm, but Sunday she shouldered the complete-game workload and gave the Sooners precisely what they needed after a difficult previous afternoon. Her command was sharp throughout, and she recovered smoothly from LSU's pair of back-to-back home runs in the second inning to keep Oklahoma's offense in control of the game.

The Offensive Firepower That Decided the Game

Oklahoma wasted no time in Game 3. Kendall Wells hit her 26th home run of the season in the opening inning, and Gabbie Garcia added another shot to build an early advantage. When LSU responded with back-to-back home runs from Kylee Edwards and a teammate in the second to pull within 4-2, the Sooners answered definitively in the fifth. Isabela Emerling launched a three-run home run over the center field wall to push the lead to 7-2 and put the game out of reach. Oklahoma finished the afternoon with four total home runs — a typically explosive display from a lineup that has been one of the most dangerous in college softball all season.

LSU's Kylee Edwards had an impressive individual game, going yard twice and accounting for two of her team's four runs. But it wasn't enough against Guachino's consistency. LSU falls to 23-12 overall and 4-8 in SEC play, a difficult start to conference season that will make the postseason path challenging for the Tigers.

Wells's Record Chase Intensifies

Sunday's home run moved Kendall Wells to 26 on the season — four away from the NCAA freshman record of 30, held jointly by Oklahoma legends Jocelyn Alo and Lauren Chamberlain, along with Soumya Majam. Wells leads all of college softball in home runs, not just the freshman category, and is averaging nearly one per game. With Oklahoma's regular-season schedule still carrying at least 20 more games, the freshman record is firmly within reach.

The larger conversation has already begun: Wells is also within striking distance of the all-time single-season home run record of 37, set by Arizona's Laura Espinoza back in 1995. That's a bigger mountain to climb, but Wells is proving that comparisons to the sport's greatest power hitters are not hyperbole. Coach Patty Gasso has compared her to Alo and Chamberlain, and through 37 games, those comparisons are holding up.

What's Next

Oklahoma continues SEC play with midweek action this week before another weekend series. Wells's record chase will remain the central narrative surrounding the program heading into April, and Guachino's ability to be a reliable second arm behind the Sooners' primary starters adds significant depth to Oklahoma's postseason outlook. For LSU, the focus shifts to winning the remaining conference games and earning a spot in the NCAA Tournament field — the Tigers will need their bats to find more consistency than they showed this weekend.
