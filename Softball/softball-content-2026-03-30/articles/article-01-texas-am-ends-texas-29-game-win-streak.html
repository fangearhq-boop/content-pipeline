Texas A&M Ends Texas's 29-Game Win Streak With 9-7 Upset in Austin

By I Love Softball Staff March 30, 2026

The Streak Ends at 29

For weeks, every game Texas played felt like a victory lap. The Longhorns had strung together 29 consecutive wins — a new program record, surpassing the previous mark of 24 set back in 2006 — and were rolling through the SEC schedule as the nation's No. 1 ranked team and defending national champions. On Sunday at McCombs Field in Austin, that run came to a stunning end.

No. 15 Texas A&M defeated No. 1 Texas 9-7, snapping both the Longhorns' win streak and their 19-game home win streak in one afternoon. It was Texas A&M's first regular-season win over Texas since 2009. Despite the loss, Texas still won the Lone Star Showdown series 2-1, improving to 31-2 overall and 8-1 in SEC play.

How Texas A&M Did It

The Aggies wasted no time establishing their intent. In the first inning, Ariel Kowalewski drove in a run with an RBI double, and a two-run home run extended the lead to 3-0. Then, leading off the second inning, KK Dement hit a solo home run — her sixth of the season — to push the Aggies' advantage to 4-1 before Texas had really settled in.

Texas starter Teagan Kavan — the reigning Women's College World Series Most Outstanding Player — worked through early pressure but was ultimately pulled in a chaotic sixth inning that defined the game. With A&M up 4-3, the Aggies erupted for six runs in the top of the sixth, aided by defensive miscues and timely hitting. A throwing error allowed two runs to score, and Dement added an RBI double to extend the lead to 9-3. Texas coach Mike White was ejected during the inning after arguing calls with home plate umpire Rick Cowan, kicking dirt at the plate as he exited.

Texas mounted a spirited comeback in the seventh, with Reese Atwood and Katie Stewart hitting back-to-back solo home runs to make it 9-7 — but A&M's Kate Munnerlyn held on to secure the historic win. Munnerlyn (3-3) earned the victory, while Kavan finished with 5.2 innings pitched, allowing eight runs on 11 hits.

Perspective on Texas's Bigger Picture

One loss changes nothing about who Texas is as a program. The Longhorns are 31-2, lead the SEC with an 8-1 conference record, and still project as a top national seed in the NCAA Tournament. The 29-game streak was remarkable — it represented a historic run of dominance for a team that won its first national championship just last year. Winning 29 in a row is extraordinary. Losing one game to an inspired opponent doesn't erase that.

Still, this result matters. Texas A&M's win raises genuine questions about vulnerabilities in the Longhorns' lineup and whether Kavan — who allowed eight runs on 11 hits Sunday — will be fully dialed in come postseason. The team will also need to find answers with or without Coach White for any potential ejection-related suspension. Texas has proven it can play through adversity, but Sunday was a test they didn't pass.

What's Next

Texas returns to conference play with a week of midweek action before the next weekend series. The Longhorns will look to reset and refocus after Sunday's setback, knowing the SEC title race and NCAA seeding are still very much in play. The NFCA Week 8 rankings, which drop Tuesday morning, will show where Texas lands after losing the No. 1 spot — and which team claims the top ranking in their absence. Texas A&M, meanwhile, celebrates a signature win in one of the most dramatic moments of the 2026 regular season.
