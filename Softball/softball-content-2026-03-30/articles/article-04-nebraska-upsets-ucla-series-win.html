Nebraska's Four-Homer Explosion Sends Huskers Past No. 7 UCLA in Series Finale

By I Love Softball Staff March 30, 2026

Three Home Runs in One Inning

If Nebraska needed a way to announce themselves to the college softball world, Sunday's second inning was the statement. The Huskers stepped to the plate against No. 7 UCLA with the game still scoreless and proceeded to score six runs — anchored by three consecutive home runs. Alexis Jensen hit a two-run shot, Hannah Camenzind followed with a three-run blast, and Ava Kuszak added a solo home run. Just like that, Nebraska led 7-1 before the Bruins could catch their breath.

The Huskers added one more run later in the game and held on for an 8-4 victory, winning the rubber match to claim the Big Ten series two games to one. No. 9 Nebraska defeating No. 7 UCLA is the definition of a quality win in the national standings, and the Huskers earned it emphatically.

Nebraska's Case for National Recognition

Nebraska entered the weekend already ranked No. 9 in the country, but winning a rubber match over a higher-ranked UCLA program adds significant credibility to that position. The Huskers have been building one of the more dangerous lineups in college softball — and Sunday's four-HR game against a legitimate ACC rival demonstrated their ability to put up crooked numbers when it matters.

The WCWS is the goal for every program, and Nebraska has the ingredients. Four home runs in a rubber match on the road shows mental toughness and offensive depth that goes beyond one or two featured hitters. If Jensen, Camenzind, and Kuszak can all contribute at this level regularly, the Huskers become one of the most difficult offensive teams to plan for in the country.

UCLA Falls to 0-2 in Sunday Rubber Matches

UCLA entered the week as a top-10 program expected to contend for a national seed, but the Bruins have now dropped two series rubber matches in recent weeks. Rylee Slimp hit a two-run home run in the seventh inning to keep UCLA within striking distance at 8-4, but the deficit proved too large to overcome. The Bruins will need to look at how they respond in deciding games as the season progresses — particularly with the NCAA Tournament and seeding implications growing with every week.

UCLA is still a talented team with postseason aspirations, but Sunday exposed a vulnerability that opponents will now look to exploit. Coming back from six runs down in a single-elimination environment is extremely difficult, and UCLA will be well-served by addressing that pattern before it matters most.

What's Next

Nebraska heads into the final weeks of the Big Ten regular season with significant momentum and the kind of series win that could move them into the top seven or eight in the next NFCA poll. The Huskers are one of the most intriguing teams in college softball right now, and their ability to put up four home runs against a quality opponent suggests their ceiling in this tournament is higher than most realize. For UCLA, the goal is simple: respond. The Bruins are too talented to spiral, and with plenty of conference games remaining, the opportunity to rebuild their case for a top seed is still there.
