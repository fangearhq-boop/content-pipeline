Dakota Kennedy's Walk-Off Home Run Completes Arkansas's Stunning Comeback Over Florida

By I Love Softball Staff March 30, 2026

Down 4-0, Then the Walk-Off

The definition of a baseball or softball expression is never more real than when it happens. Down 4-0 entering the bottom of the seventh inning at Bogle Park, Arkansas had three outs to save its Game 3 against No. 5 Florida — and its chance at a fourth straight series win over the Gators. What followed was one of the most dramatic finishes of the 2026 college softball season.

Reagan Johnson singled to lead off the inning. Brinli Bain walked to put two on base. Ella McDowell struck out for the second out, leaving Arkansas one strike from ending the inning down three runs. Then left fielder Dakota Kennedy stepped to the plate and hit the very first pitch she saw over the left field wall — a three-run home run to win the game 6-4 and clinch the series for the Razorbacks.

The Game That Set the Stage

Florida had been dominant for most of the afternoon, scoring three runs in the first inning — highlighted by an RBI single from Jocelyn Erickson and a two-run home run by Kenleigh Cahalan. Keagan Rothrock (17-4) was strong on the mound, and a Kendall Grover RBI single in the third extended the lead to 4-0. Through six innings, Florida appeared comfortable and in control.

Arkansas ace Saylor Timmerman (7-0) kept the Razorbacks in the game despite the deficit. Timmerman held Florida scoreless after the third inning, giving her offense the opportunity to mount a comeback. It ultimately took six innings of patience and Timmerman's grit for the Razorbacks to reach the seventh with a chance — and Kennedy made it count.

Arkansas Owns This Rivalry Right Now

Sunday's win marked Arkansas's fourth straight series victory over Florida — an extraordinary run for a program that has turned the Gators into a nemesis matchup. Coach Courtney Deifel has built something genuine in Fayetteville. The Razorbacks are 30-5 overall and 5-4 in the SEC, with a quality win résumé that should place them firmly in the top-10 conversation when the Week 8 rankings arrive Tuesday.

Florida falls to 33-4 overall and 9-3 in SEC play. The Gators remain a national title contender and a likely top regional host, but their pattern of falling to Arkansas in series play is a real storyline. Rothrock absorbs the loss (17-4), which is a mark that speaks to her excellence — but Sunday added another chapter to what has become a challenging rivalry story for Florida.

What's Next

Both programs return to midweek action before the next weekend series. For Arkansas, this win is a momentum-builder heading into the final weeks of SEC play — and positions the Razorbacks for potential improvement in the national rankings. For Florida, the challenge is resetting and not letting a fourth series loss to Arkansas become a distraction as the season enters its final stretch. Kennedy's quote after the game captured the moment perfectly: "The pressure is on the other team. My teammates had done their job, and I was just trying to get a pitch I could hit." She got one, and Arkansas walked off winners.
