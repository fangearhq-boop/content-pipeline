Brooke Wells' 24th HR Fuels Alabama's Comeback Over UCLA 6-3 at the 2026 WCWS

By Sarah Mitchell

May 29, 2026

Megan Grant hit her 41st HR to extend her own NCAA record. Then Brooke Wells answered with No. 24 — and Alabama turned a 3-1 deficit into a 6-3 win.

For three innings Thursday night, it appeared Megan Grant might write the most compelling story of WCWS Day 1 entirely by herself.

Then Brooke Wells reminded Devon Park that Alabama came here to compete with anyone.

Top-seeded Alabama fell behind UCLA 3-1 after Grant launched a solo home run in the third inning — her 41st of the season, an extension of her own all-time NCAA record. It was a moment that seemed to signal a narrative: Grant, in her senior year, leading her program past the tournament's No. 1 seed on opening day. The story was taking shape.

Alabama rewrote it in the sixth inning. With Kristen White and Jena Young on base with one out, Brooke Wells stepped to the plate and hit a three-run home run off Taylor Tinsley. Alabama 4, UCLA 3. The Crimson Tide added two more runs and closed the game at 6-3.

Wells' 24th HR and a School Record

Wells' 24th home run was the decisive swing, but the context that surrounds it is equally significant. Wells and her teammate Lexi Pupillo have now both hit 20-plus home runs this season — the first time in Alabama program history that two Tide players have accomplished that feat in the same year. It is a dual school record set in the middle of a WCWS comeback, which is exactly the kind of context that makes a tournament run feel different from a regular season.

Alabama's pitching staff has been the story of this team's season — 22 shutouts entering the tournament, a 1.60 team ERA, and a defensive identity that has suffocated most opponents. But the Tide's ability to produce power offensively alongside that pitching depth is what makes them the No. 1 overall seed. Wells' HR was a reminder of that dimension when it mattered most.

Grant's 41st HR in Defeat

Megan Grant hit 41 home runs this season — the most by any player in college softball history. She extended her own record with that third-inning shot at Devon Park. Even in a loss, the number stands.

Grant also tied Stacey Nuveman's UCLA school record with the 90th home run of her career in Thursday's game. She is the most accomplished power hitter in the history of this sport, and her pursuit of a national championship — the one credential her career still doesn't have — continues tonight. UCLA faces Arkansas in an elimination game at 9:30 PM ET on ESPN2.

UCLA lost Thursday night for the first time in 42 games when leading after four innings. That is not a number that reflects anything but the quality of Alabama's response and the pressure of the WCWS environment. The Bruins took a lead into the sixth inning of a Women's College World Series game against the No. 1 seed. They did not lose this game because they played poorly.

What's Next

Alabama advances to the winners' bracket and faces No. 4 Nebraska tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN. Nebraska survived Arkansas 5-3 in 10 innings on Thursday night, with Jordy Frahm throwing 133 pitches before Ava Kuszak ended it with a walk-off two-run HR. Nebraska's pitching staff absorbs significant workload entering tonight's matchup.

Alabama, meanwhile, goes in relatively fresh — Jocelyn Briski's availability and the Tide's pitching rotation are in a more favorable position heading into the winners' bracket. The No. 1 seed has the advantage it came in with, and they will carry it into what shapes up as one of the tournament's marquee matchups.

For UCLA, the 2026 WCWS continues tonight. Megan Grant is still playing. The championship she has been chasing is still out there.
