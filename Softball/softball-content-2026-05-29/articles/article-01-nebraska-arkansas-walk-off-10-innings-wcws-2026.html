Ava Kuszak Walk-Off HR: Nebraska Survives Arkansas 5-3 in 10 Innings at the WCWS

By Sarah Mitchell

May 29, 2026

Nebraska needed 133 Jordy Frahm pitches and a walk-off home run from Ava Kuszak to survive the Razorbacks. The drama was real — and so was the result.

This was not the game Nebraska had in mind when they arrived at Devon Park.

The Huskers came to Oklahoma City as one of the most dominant teams in the country — 51-6, 26-game win streak, the 2026 USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year in the circle. Game 4 of WCWS Day 1 against Arkansas was, on paper, a winnable opener. What it became was a 10-inning test that burned 133 pitches from Jordy Frahm and required Ava Kuszak to end it with a walk-off two-run home run in extra innings.

Nebraska 5, Arkansas 3. Final. Ten innings. One of the most dramatic WCWS openers in recent tournament history.

Frahm's 133-Pitch Night

Jordy Frahm threw 133 pitches Thursday night. That number is not a typo, and it is not a minor footnote — it is the central fact that shapes Nebraska's immediate future in this tournament.

Frahm is the best pitcher in college softball. Her 20-4 record, 1.14 ERA, and 234 strikeouts through the regular season and NCAA Tournament made her the unanimous choice as the 2026 USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year. None of that is in question. What is in question — now more than ever — is what 133 pitches in Game 1 means for the bracket ahead.

The Huskers face No. 1 Alabama tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN in a winners'-bracket matchup. Nebraska's pitching depth will be tested in ways it wasn't during their historic 26-game win streak. The Frahm workload is the story that will follow this team through the rest of the WCWS.

Kuszak's Walk-Off Moment

In the 10th inning of a tied game, with the stakes as clear as they get in a double-elimination tournament, Ava Kuszak stepped to the plate and hit a two-run home run to end it. Nebraska 5, Arkansas 3.

Kuszak's walk-off was the kind of moment that tournament teams point to later — the moment they found a hero who wasn't the obvious one. The WCWS has a history of unexpected contributors defining runs. Kuszak wrote herself into that tradition on Thursday night.

Arkansas's Extraordinary Effort

Arkansas came to the 2026 WCWS for the first time in program history. Coach Courtney Deifel's Razorbacks had gone 0-for-5 in Super Regional appearances before finally breaking through this year, and they showed in Game 4 exactly why they belong on this stage.

Pushing Nebraska to 10 innings against Jordy Frahm — one of the most dominant pitchers in the country — is a genuine achievement. Arkansas didn't come to Devon Park to lose quietly. They came to compete, and they did.

After the game, Deifel and Nebraska's Rhonda Revelle followed through on their pre-game commitment: they met at home plate and hugged, just as they had promised they would before the game. It was a moment that belonged to the sport as much as it belonged to either coach. In competition this fierce, that kind of humanity stands out.

What's Next

Nebraska moves to the winners' bracket and faces top-seeded Alabama tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN. Alabama beat UCLA 6-3 in Game 3 behind Brooke Wells' go-ahead three-run HR in the sixth inning. This is the matchup the tournament had been anticipating — the No. 4 seed against the No. 1 overall seed, with the winner staying in the winners' bracket and the loser falling to the elimination side.

Arkansas's 2026 WCWS run is over. But their first appearance in this tournament set a tone for what this program is capable of. Courtney Deifel didn't build a WCWS team to lose in the first round — she built one that pushed the best program in the country to extra innings. That matters.
