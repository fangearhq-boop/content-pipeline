WCWS Day 2: Defending Champion Texas and Gutsy Arkansas Face Elimination Tonight

By Emily Rawlings

May 29, 2026

Two teams that defined Day 1 for different reasons now face the same reality: win tonight or go home. Texas and Arkansas have one game to save their 2026 WCWS.

Twenty-four hours ago, eight teams arrived at Devon Park with equal tournament access and a full double-elimination bracket ahead. Now four of them are one loss from going home.

Tonight's two losers'-bracket games carry some of the highest stakes of the early tournament. At 7 PM ET on ESPN2, the defending national champion Texas faces Mississippi State in a first-round elimination game. At 9:30 PM ET on ESPN2, UCLA — with Megan Grant still chasing the one thing her career doesn't have — meets Arkansas in a game that carries its own weight of narrative and history.

Win and live. Lose and go home. The WCWS losers' bracket does not offer comfortable margins.

Texas: The Defending Champion Fights for Its Season

Texas entered the 2026 Women's College World Series as a No. 2 national seed and the reigning national champions. They lost 6-3 to Tennessee on Day 1 — a game in which Tennessee freshman Elsa Morrison hit a three-run home run in the second inning and the Volunteers never surrendered the lead. Texas did not make a disqualifying error or play a terrible game. They faced a motivated, well-prepared opponent and lost.

Now they face Mississippi State at 7 PM ET on ESPN2, and the stakes are absolute. Texas Tech run-ruled Mississippi State 8-0 in five innings on Thursday, but a run-rule loss in a five-inning opener is not a measure of what a team can do in a full, urgency-driven elimination game. Mississippi State made its first-ever WCWS appearance by upsetting No. 1 Oklahoma in the super regional round. This is a program that has already proven it can beat a team it wasn't supposed to beat.

If Texas wins tonight, they stay in the bracket and continue building toward a potential run. If Texas loses, the defending national champions exit the 2026 WCWS having played three games. That outcome is possible. The losers'-bracket schedule makes sure of it.

UCLA and Grant: One More Night to Chase History

Megan Grant hit her 41st home run Thursday night, extending her own all-time NCAA home run record. She also tied Stacey Nuveman's UCLA school record with the 90th home run of her career. UCLA led Alabama 3-1 going into the sixth inning before Brooke Wells hit a go-ahead three-run HR and the Tide pulled away for a 6-3 win.

The loss does not erase what Grant has built. She is the most accomplished power hitter in the history of college softball. A national championship is the one credential her résumé still doesn't include, and UCLA's elimination game tonight against Arkansas is another opportunity to extend her shot at it.

Arkansas comes in having played perhaps the most emotionally draining game of Day 1 — a 10-inning loss to Nebraska that required Ava Kuszak's walk-off home run to resolve. Coach Courtney Deifel's program pushed Jordy Frahm to 133 pitches before finally yielding. The Razorbacks have 24 hours to reset and respond in a game against a UCLA program that is well-rested and motivated by a close loss to the No. 1 seed.

Deifel has built Arkansas into a WCWS-caliber program, and tonight her team has the chance to show it can compete across consecutive games against high-level opponents.

What's Next

While the losers' bracket resolves tonight, the winners' bracket continues this afternoon. Texas Tech faces Tennessee at 3 PM ET on ABC — a marquee matchup between two teams that each dominated their Day 1 opponents. Alabama faces Nebraska at 7 PM ET on ESPN in the evening winners'-bracket game.

By the end of today's schedule, the tournament will have a clearer structure. Two of the four teams in tonight's elimination games will be gone. The survivors join winners who are already through to the next bracket round.

The WCWS compresses time and removes hesitation. Every game matters now. Every game has consequences. That is what makes Devon Park in late May the most compelling place in college softball, and why tonight's elimination games belong in prime time.
