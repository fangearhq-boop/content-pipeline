Freshman Elsa Morrison Three-Run HR: Tennessee Stuns Defending Champion Texas 6-3 at WCWS

By Emily Rawlings

May 29, 2026

A freshman catcher who had never played a WCWS game walked up in the second inning and hit a three-run homer against the defending national champion. Tennessee's revenge was swift.

Tennessee has been carrying something since the 2025 Women's College World Series semifinals. A 2-0 loss to Texas in that game ended the Lady Vols' season and left a specific, named piece of unfinished business.

On Thursday night at Devon Park, a freshman ended it.

Elsa Morrison, a first-year catcher who had never played a single Women's College World Series game before Game 2 of the 2026 WCWS, went 2-for-3 with a three-run home run in the second inning and three RBIs total. Tennessee beat Texas — the defending national champion — 6-3. The Longhorns, who entered as a No. 2 national seed and the reigning title holders, are now in the losers' bracket facing elimination tonight against Mississippi State.

Morrison's Moment

There is no more pressure-saturated environment in college softball than a Women's College World Series opener against the defending champion. It is the game that tests whether a team's preparation has become genuine belief or whether the moment is too large to absorb cleanly.

Morrison absorbed it and hit a ball over the fence.

The three-run homer in the second inning gave Tennessee a lead they never surrendered. It was not a cheaply earned opportunity — Morrison stepped up with the bases loaded and delivered against one of the most accomplished programs in the sport. The kind of swing that defines a tournament for the player who hits it and for the team that watches it leave the yard.

The Revenge Narrative Is Complete

Tennessee entered this WCWS having been eliminated by Texas in 2025. The Lady Vols have since become a stronger, deeper program — Karlyn Pickens earned her fourth consecutive First-Team All-American honor, Sage Mardjetko was named a First-Team All-American this May, and the team returned to Devon Park with a specific opponent in mind.

Pickens came on in relief and finished the game, holding Texas to three runs over the final innings. The pitching system — which Tennessee head coach Karen Weekly built around giving the ace support rather than asking her to carry everything — held up under pressure exactly as designed.

Texas got on the board, but Tennessee never felt threatened after Morrison's second-inning swing. The 6-3 final was, in that sense, not as close as the score suggests in terms of the game's narrative arc.

What Comes Next

Tennessee moves to the winners' bracket and faces Texas Tech at 3 PM ET today on ABC. Texas Tech run-ruled Mississippi State 8-0 in five innings on Day 1, with NiJaree Canady throwing four scoreless innings and Jackie Lis hitting a two-run HR in the first inning. It is the kind of matchup — two teams that dominated Day 1 — that feels like a tournament preview game, not a second-round bracket game.

Texas faces Mississippi State tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. The defending national champion must win that game or their title defense ends in three games total at Devon Park. A program that won the national championship less than a year ago will be fighting for its tournament life against a team making its first-ever WCWS appearance.

For Tennessee, the work is not done — it is just beginning. But the first step involved a freshman catcher, a packed second inning, and a walk into the WCWS record books that Elsa Morrison will not forget.
