NiJaree Canady Delivers: Texas Tech Run-Rules Mississippi State 8-0 in WCWS Game 1

By Jordan Reeves

May 29, 2026

Four scoreless innings. Two hits. Three strikeouts. Jackie Lis with a two-run HR in the first. Texas Tech opened the 2026 WCWS with a complete run-rule statement.

The Women's College World Series is a tournament where reputations are tested and where the best players in college softball either rise to the occasion or get exposed by it. NiJaree Canady has been building a reputation for three seasons. On Thursday at Devon Park, she confirmed it.

Texas Tech's ace threw four scoreless innings in Game 1 of the 2026 WCWS, allowing just two hits and striking out three batters. The offense gave her everything she needed from the first inning — Jackie Lis hit a two-run home run and Texas Tech never trailed. Kaitlyn Terry handled the fifth inning in relief, facing the minimum as Texas Tech run-ruled Mississippi State 8-0 in five innings to open the tournament.

Canady's Four Scoreless Innings

Canady entered this WCWS with a record of 30-5, a postseason ERA of 0.89, more than 1,000 career strikeouts, and 96 career wins. Those numbers carried the weight of years of work at the highest level of college softball. The question was never whether she was good enough for this stage — it was whether she would perform under the specific pressure of a WCWS opener against a program making its first-ever tournament appearance with nothing to lose.

She answered that question cleanly. Four innings. Eight outs. Two hits. No runs. Three strikeouts. It was not a performance that required Canady to be exceptional — it required her to be exactly herself, and she was. Efficient, composed, and in command from the first batter.

Mississippi State's Difficult Opening

Mississippi State arrived at the 2026 WCWS as a first-timer — the program had never before reached Devon Park, and they got here by upsetting No. 1 Oklahoma in the super regional round. That is a legitimate achievement, and nothing about Game 1 erases it.

But being run-ruled in five innings in a WCWS opener is a difficult way to begin a first tournament appearance. Mississippi State faces Texas tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 in a first-round elimination game. Texas — the defending national champion — also lost on Day 1, dropping a 6-3 decision to Tennessee. The Mississippi State-Texas elimination game is a matchup between two teams that arrived with expectations and are now fighting to stay alive after a day one loss.

For Mississippi State, the opportunity to compete against a program of that caliber in an elimination setting is exactly the kind of experience a first-time WCWS team needs — if they can respond to a run-rule loss with enough energy to take the field tonight and compete.

Texas Tech's Championship Ceiling

The run-rule win in Game 1 did more than put Texas Tech in the winners' bracket — it sent a message to every other team at Devon Park. This is not a program here to survive the tournament; it is here to win it. Canady is pitching at her best at the moment when her best matters most, and the offense is producing early and often.

Texas Tech faces Tennessee today at 3 PM ET on ABC in a winners'-bracket matchup. Tennessee beat defending champion Texas 6-3 on Day 1, with freshman Elsa Morrison hitting a three-run HR in the second inning. That is the game the tournament has been waiting for — two teams that dominated Day 1 meeting to determine who stays in the winners' bracket.

What's Next

Canady's pitch count in Game 1 was light enough that she will likely be available to pitch again quickly — an advantage for Texas Tech that cannot be overstated in a tournament that rewards pitching depth. How head coach Gerry Glasco deploys her against Tennessee today will tell us a great deal about Texas Tech's confidence in this pitching staff and this team's championship intentions.

The final act that Canady has been building toward for her entire college career is off to the start it deserved.
