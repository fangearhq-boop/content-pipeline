Kendall Wells Ties NCAA Freshman Home Run Record at 30

By Sarah Mitchell | April 5, 2026

The Oklahoma star matched Lauren Chamberlain, Jocelyn Alo, and Kelly Majam with her 30th home run of the season, while the Sooners completed a sweep of Kentucky.

Kendall Wells settled into the batter's box in the fourth inning on Saturday with history on her mind — and she delivered. The Oklahoma star crushed a three-run home run to right-center field against Kentucky, her 30th of the season, tying the all-time NCAA single-season freshman home run record.

Wells is now in elite company. The record is shared by Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma, 2013), Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma, 2018), and Kelly Majam (Hawaii, 2010). Wells becomes just the fourth player in NCAA history to reach 30 home runs as a freshman.

The Moment

The historic swing came in the fourth inning of Oklahoma's 12-2 run-rule win over Kentucky in Game 3 of their series at Love's Field. With runners on base, Wells pulled a pitch to right-center and sent the crowd into a frenzy. The Sooners had already won the series in Games 1 and 2, but this was the moment everyone was waiting for.

Kasadi Pickering put the finishing touch on the game with a walk-off grand slam in the bottom of the fifth inning, ending the game on the run rule and completing the series sweep.

A Season for the Ages

Wells' numbers are staggering for a freshman: a .383 batting average, 30 home runs, and 60 RBIs. She leads the nation in home runs and has been the most feared hitter in the Big 12 and SEC since the conference schedule began.

She set the SEC single-season freshman home run record on March 31, reaching 27 home runs in a win over Wichita State. From there, she added three more in quick succession to reach the national mark. The outright NCAA record — 31 — is within reach.

Oklahoma Rolls On

The Sooners improved to 38-3 overall and 11-1 in SEC play with the sweep of Kentucky. Oklahoma has positioned itself as one of the top contenders for a No. 1 national seed and a deep run in the Women's College World Series.

The next challenge arrives quickly: the Red River Rivalry. Texas visits Austin for a three-game series beginning April 10 at McCombs Field. Texas arrives having just dropped a series to No. 4 Alabama, and Oklahoma — riding Wells' historic momentum — will be ready.

What's Next

Kendall Wells needs just one more home run to own the NCAA freshman record outright. The Red River Rivalry, scheduled for April 10-12 at McCombs Field in Austin, will be one of the most watched series in college softball history. The NFCA Week 9 poll drops Tuesday, April 7, and Oklahoma should maintain or improve its standing after the impressive sweep.
