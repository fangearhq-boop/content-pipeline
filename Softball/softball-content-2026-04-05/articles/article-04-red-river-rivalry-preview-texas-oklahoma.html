Red River Rivalry Preview: Texas vs. Oklahoma — The Series of the Year Starts April 10

By Sarah Mitchell | April 5, 2026

With Kendall Wells chasing history and Texas needing a statement win, the April 10-12 series at McCombs Field in Austin is the most anticipated matchup of the regular season.

A week from Friday, the two most storied programs in college softball take the field together for the most important regular-season series of 2026. Texas and Oklahoma — rivals, national championship contenders, and programs defined by this matchup — meet for a three-game series at Red and Charline McCombs Field in Austin, April 10-12.

The timing could not be more dramatic. Texas has stumbled. Oklahoma is surging. And Kendall Wells is one home run away from the outright NCAA freshman record.

Texas: Licking Its Wounds

The Longhorns enter as the reigning national champions — but they have lost two series in their last three. The 29-game win streak that had defined their season was snapped on March 29 by Texas A&M. Then last weekend, No. 4 Alabama took a series from them at Rhoads Stadium, winning 7-4 in the clincher.

Texas still boasts one of the most dangerous lineups in the country. Katie Stewart is at 20 home runs on the season, and pitcher Teagan Kavan has been one of the premier arms in the Big 12-turned-SEC era. But the Longhorns need a statement. A home series win over Oklahoma — in Austin, in front of their fans — would be that statement.

Oklahoma: Riding Historic Momentum

The Sooners arrive at 38-3 overall and 11-1 in SEC play after sweeping Kentucky last weekend. But the biggest story is the player at the heart of their lineup: Kendall Wells, who tied the all-time NCAA single-season freshman home run record at 30 on Saturday with a three-run shot against Kentucky.

Wells is batting .383 with 60 RBIs on the season. She leads the nation in home runs. She is one swing away from owning the record outright. The Red River Rivalry will be her stage.

How to Watch

Game 1 is Friday, April 10 at 6 PM CT on ESPN2. Games 2 and 3 are on ESPN. All three games are at McCombs Field in Austin.

This is a must-watch series for any college softball fan. The SEC title implications are real, the individual storylines are compelling, and these two programs have a history of delivering in moments that matter.

What's Next

The Red River Rivalry begins April 10 at McCombs Field in Austin. Texas needs a series win to reassert itself at the top; Oklahoma needs a road series win to firmly establish itself as the No. 1 team in the country. Kendall Wells's pursuit of the outright NCAA freshman home run record adds a historic subplot that will make this series unmissable.
