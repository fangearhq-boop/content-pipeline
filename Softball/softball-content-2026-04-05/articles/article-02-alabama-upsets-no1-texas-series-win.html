Alabama Stuns No. 1 Texas, Takes Series 2-1 at Rhoads Stadium

By Emily Rawlings | April 5, 2026

Katie Stewart hit two home runs in the series clincher as No. 4 Alabama handed Texas its second series loss of the season — and its biggest.

The score read 7-4 when the final out was recorded at Rhoads Stadium on Saturday afternoon, but the statement had been made long before that. No. 4 Alabama had taken the series from No. 1 Texas, winning it 2-1 in one of the most significant upsets of the 2026 college softball season.

It was Alabama's first series win over a No. 1-ranked opponent since 2016 — and the Crimson Tide did it at home, in front of a crowd exceeding 11,000 over the weekend, with authority.

Stewart Delivers in the Clutch

Katie Stewart was the story of Game 3. The Alabama slugger went 3-for-3 with a two-run home run and a solo home run — her 20th homer of the season — while driving in three runs in the series clincher. Reese Atwood added a solo shot of her own, her 15th of the year, as the Crimson Tide's power gave them the cushion they needed.

Combined, Stewart and Atwood hit three home runs in Game 3 alone. That kind of production — especially against the nation's top team — is the hallmark of a WCWS-caliber lineup.

The Series Arc

Texas took Game 1 comfortably, winning 9-1. The Longhorns looked like the dominant program they have been for most of 2026. But Alabama answered in Game 2 by rallying from a 4-0 deficit with a six-run second inning, winning 11-4. It was the kind of momentum shift that can flip a series — and it did.

In Game 3, the Longhorns never recovered. Alabama controlled the contest from the first inning, building a lead and then extending it with Stewart's power display. The 7-4 final felt comfortable by the end.

What This Means for Texas

Texas enters the week with two series losses in its last three — the first was to No. 15 Texas A&M on March 29, and now this. The Longhorns remain one of the nation's elite programs, with a deep pitching staff and a formidable lineup. But the NFCA Week 9 poll, dropping April 7, will almost certainly push them down in the rankings.

The stakes only intensify: Texas hosts Oklahoma in the Red River Rivalry beginning April 10 at McCombs Field. If the Longhorns are going to reassert themselves at the top, they'll need to do it against the Sooners — who arrive 38-3, with Kendall Wells tied for the NCAA freshman home run record.

What's Next

The NFCA Week 9 poll drops Tuesday, April 7. Alabama is poised to make a major move in the rankings — potentially into the top three. Texas will focus on regrouping before the Red River Rivalry begins April 10. For the Crimson Tide, this weekend confirmed what their fans have believed all season: this team is capable of beating anyone in the country.
