Kylee Edwards Hits for the Cycle — First in LSU Softball History

By Emily Rawlings | April 5, 2026

The LSU junior shortstop went 4-for-6 with a home run, single, double, and triple in a 16-4 win over Missouri, becoming the first player in program history to accomplish the feat.

LSU softball has a rich history — championship seasons, legendary players, moments that have defined a program. But in Friday's 16-4 win over Missouri, Kylee Edwards did something no LSU player had ever done before: she hit for the cycle.

The junior shortstop from Baton Rouge went 4-for-6 with four RBIs, reaching base in a different way each time. A two-run home run in the second inning. A single in the third. A double in the sixth. And a triple in the seventh. Four hits, four types, one historic performance.

How It Unfolded

Edwards' home run in the second inning gave LSU an early edge, and the Tigers never looked back in what became a comfortable victory. Her single in the third was quiet — just a baserunner — but her double in the sixth added another RBI as LSU extended its lead. The triple in the seventh completed the cycle and sent the Baton Rouge crowd into a celebration.

She finished the game going 4-for-6 with 4 RBIs as LSU rolled to the 16-4 final. Edwards had been in remarkable form entering the game, having homered in each of her previous six games. To cap that stretch with the first cycle in LSU softball history is the kind of achievement that gets remembered long after a season ends.

A Program Milestone

LSU softball has produced All-Americans, SEC champions, and players who have gone on to pro careers. But no player in the program's history had ever recorded all four types of extra-base and base hits in a single game before Friday. Edwards' feat stands alone in the record books.

The cycle is one of the rarest achievements in softball — requiring not just talent but circumstance, timing, and a bit of fortune across each at-bat. Edwards made it look natural.

LSU's SEC Position

LSU's 16-4 win was part of a series sweep of Missouri. The Tigers continue to build their SEC resume as the conference race tightens. The NFCA Week 9 poll drops Tuesday, April 7, and LSU will be watching to see where they land after a dominant weekend.

What's Next

LSU continues SEC play this week as the Tigers push for postseason positioning. Kylee Edwards will enter the week as one of the hottest hitters in college softball — and now, one of the most decorated players in LSU history. The NFCA Week 9 poll on April 7 will offer a clearer picture of where the Tigers stand in the national picture.
