FSU Rallies From 4-0 Down to Sweep Notre Dame, Win Streak Reaches 24

By Jordan Reeves | April 5, 2026

Jaysoni Beachum's clutch triple ignited a four-inning scoring run as the Seminoles extended the nation's longest active win streak to 24 games.

Notre Dame scored four runs in the second inning and seemed to have put Florida State in an unfamiliar position: behind, in danger of losing a game for the first time in nearly two months.

The Seminoles had other plans.

Florida State scored in four consecutive innings, erased the deficit with a Jaysoni Beachum two-out triple in the fourth, and pulled away with a big sixth inning to win Game 3, 8-4. The sweep extended the nation's longest active win streak to 24 games.

The Comeback

Trailing 4-0 entering the fourth inning, FSU's comeback began with two outs. Beachum turned on a pitch and lined a triple down the line, scoring two runners to close the gap to 4-2. It was the kind of swing that changed the energy of the entire ballpark.

In the fifth, Kennedy Harp delivered an RBI double to give FSU its first lead. The Seminoles broke the game open in the sixth: Harp added an RBI single, and Anna Hinde connected for a two-RBI single that pushed the lead to 8-4. Notre Dame could not respond.

The Streak in Context

At 24 games, FSU's win streak is the longest active streak in Division I softball. The Seminoles' program record is 32 games, set in 1985, meaning this group still has a long way to go before reaching historic territory — but 24 is already a remarkable achievement in a sport defined by close games and postseason upsets.

FSU is now 35-4 overall and a perfect 12-0 in ACC play. They have not lost a conference game all season.

Positioned for Postseason

The Seminoles' combination of offensive production, pitching depth, and resilience has made them a legitimate contender for a national seed and a deep Women's College World Series run. They have shown the ability to win in multiple ways — run-ruling opponents one game and grinding out tough comeback wins the next.

Head coach Lonni Alameda's program continues to build its case among the nation's elite, even as the attention nationally has largely focused on the Texas-Oklahoma-Alabama triangle at the top of the SEC.

What's Next

The NFCA Week 9 poll drops Tuesday, April 7. FSU, currently ranked No. 10, has a strong case for movement after this sweep and a perfect ACC record. The Seminoles continue conference play next weekend as the postseason picture continues to take shape.
