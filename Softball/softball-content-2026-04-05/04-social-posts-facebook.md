### STORY 1: Kendall Wells — NCAA Freshman HR Record Tied

#### Long-Form Post -- 9:00 AM
She did it.

Kendall Wells stepped into the box in the fourth inning on Saturday, dug in, and launched a three-run shot to right-center field. Home run No. 30.

With that swing, Wells tied the all-time NCAA single-season freshman home run record — a mark previously held by Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma, 2013), Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma, 2018), and Kelly Majam (Hawaii, 2010). Four players. Thirty home runs. Elite company.

The junior from Broken Arrow, Oklahoma, is batting .383 with 60 RBIs on the season — and she still has the rest of the SEC schedule ahead of her. Oklahoma (38-3, 11-1 SEC) completed the sweep of Kentucky with a 12-2 run-rule win in five innings, with Kasadi Pickering adding a walk-off grand slam to end the game.

The record is there for the taking. The Red River Rivalry starts April 10.

### STORY 2: Alabama Upsets No. 1 Texas

#### Long-Form Post -- 11:00 AM
Alabama just took down No. 1 Texas — and it wasn't close by the end.

Down 9-1 after Game 1, the Crimson Tide responded with back-to-back wins (11-4, then 7-4) to claim the series 2-1 at Rhoads Stadium. The crowd of 3,962 for Game 3 was part of a weekend total exceeding 11,000 — the kind of atmosphere you'd expect when the nation's top program comes to town.

The hero of the weekend? Katie Stewart. In the series clincher, she went 3-for-3 with a two-run home run and a solo shot — her 20th homer of the season. Reese Atwood added a solo home run as well, her 15th. Together, they drove the offense and handed Texas just its second series loss of the year.

It's Alabama's first series win over a No. 1 ranked team since 2016. The NFCA Week 9 poll drops Tuesday, and the Crimson Tide could be looking at a historic climb.

### STORY 3: FSU Win Streak to 24

#### Long-Form Post -- 1:00 PM
Florida State trailed Notre Dame 4-0 after two innings.

If you know this FSU team, you already know what happened next.

Jaysoni Beachum came up with two outs in the fourth and ripped a triple to tie the game. In the fifth, Kennedy Harp doubled home the go-ahead run. Then in the sixth, the floodgates opened — Harp singled home another run, Anna Hinde lined a two-RBI single, and the Seminoles had an 8-4 lead they would not relinquish.

The sweep over Notre Dame extended FSU's win streak to 24 games — the nation's longest active streak. The Seminoles are now 35-4 overall and a perfect 12-0 in ACC play. They have not lost a conference game all season.

A Final Four berth isn't just possible — it's looking increasingly inevitable.

### STORY 4: Red River Rivalry Preview

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM
The most anticipated series of the regular season is one week away.

Texas hosts Oklahoma at McCombs Field in Austin, April 10-12, starting Friday at 6 PM CT on ESPN2. Games 2 and 3 are on ESPN.

The stakes could not be higher. Texas (previously ranked No. 1) just dropped its second series of the year to Alabama, while Oklahoma enters on a three-game sweep of Kentucky — with Kendall Wells tied for the all-time NCAA freshman home run record at 30. The Sooners are 38-3.

Between these two programs, they have combined for each of the last several national championships. Texas won the 2025 WCWS matchup 4-2 on its way to the national title. Oklahoma wants that back.

Wells vs. the Texas rotation. Stewart and Atwood vs. Kavan and Gutierrez. The Red River Rivalry in softball doesn't disappoint.

Set your reminders now.

### STORY 5: Kylee Edwards Hits for the Cycle

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 PM
In LSU softball's long, storied history, no player had ever hit for the cycle.

On Friday, Kylee Edwards changed that.

The junior shortstop went 4-for-6 against Missouri, reaching base in a different way each time: a two-run home run in the second inning, a single in the third, a double in the sixth, and a triple in the seventh. Four RBIs. One historic line. LSU won the game 16-4.

Edwards had been on one of the hottest stretches of her career entering Friday — she had homered in each of her previous six games. To cap that run with the first cycle in program history? Remarkable.

These are the moments that remind you why college softball is so special.

### STORY 6: Texas A&M Claims Georgia Series

#### Long-Form Post -- 5:30 PM
No. 15 Texas A&M walked into Davis Diamond this weekend and left with a 2-1 series win over No. 11 Georgia.

The Aggies won Game 1 in dramatic fashion when Madi Sauni delivered a pinch-hit RBI single in the sixth inning to win 3-2. After Georgia bounced back to take Game 2 (10-6), the rubber match was tight — a one-run game won on Kennedy Powell's go-ahead RBI double in the fourth. Starter Sydney Lessentine went 5.2 innings, and Sidne Peters came in to shut the door with three strikeouts.

It's A&M's second consecutive series win over Georgia, which is the first time in program history the Aggies have accomplished that feat. With the NFCA Week 9 poll dropping Tuesday, this weekend could help propel Texas A&M up the rankings.
