Nebraska Ends 13-Year WCWS Drought with Dominant Super Regional Sweep

By Sarah Mitchell

May 24, 2026

A seven-run fifth inning against Oklahoma State sent Nebraska to Devon Park for the first time since 2013. The Huskers finish 51-6 with a 26-game winning streak — the longest active run in Division I softball.

Nebraska waited 13 years. On Saturday afternoon in Lincoln, in front of a sold-out Bob Devaney Stadium, the wait ended.

The Huskers defeated Oklahoma State 9-1 in Super Regional Game 2, completing a sweep and punching their ticket to the Women's College World Series for the first time since 2013. A seven-run fifth inning turned a competitive game into a statement, and Nebraska walked off the field with the moment their fans have been waiting over a decade to see.

The Numbers Behind the Season

Nebraska finishes the regular season and tournament run at 51-6 — among the most impressive records in school history. The 26-game winning streak they carry into Devon Park is the longest active streak in Division I softball and the longest in Nebraska program history.

Their last loss came in late March. From that point forward, every team that stepped onto the field with Nebraska lost. Oklahoma State was the latest — and the last — opponent to find out how dominant this Husker team has been. The 8-1 Game 1 win on Friday and the 9-1 Game 2 run-rule on Saturday produced a sweep so convincing it barely felt like a series.

Nebraska won the Big Ten Tournament and finished with four 50-win seasons in program history to their name. The 2026 season goes in the books as one of the great years in Husker softball.

The Seven-Run Fifth

Saturday's Game 2 was the kind of performance where Nebraska's depth showed itself. The Huskers were in control from the early innings, but the five-inning run rule came through that massive fifth frame where seven runs crossed the plate in a single inning against Oklahoma State's pitching staff.

It was a fitting end to a sweep. Nebraska had the kind of top-to-bottom lineup that could explode at any moment, and when the fifth inning opened up, the Huskers took full advantage. Oklahoma State was outscored 17-2 in the series — outplayed across every dimension of the game.

The 13-Year Wait

Nebraska's last WCWS appearance was in 2013. Since then, the program has maintained consistent Big Ten competitiveness, but the Super Regional hurdle proved difficult year after year. This is Nebraska's eighth overall WCWS appearance in program history, and their first in thirteen years.

The home fans who filled Devaney Stadium on Friday and Saturday were part of something significant. Nebraska softball had never hosted a Super Regional before this year — and they used it, winning both games and sending their program back to Oklahoma City with authority. The 13-year drought is over.

What's Next

Nebraska advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. The Huskers will receive their WCWS bracket assignment and first-round matchup when the full eight-team field is confirmed after Sunday's Game 3s are completed. Nebraska enters Devon Park as one of the tournament's most complete teams — 51-6, riding a 26-game streak, with pitching and offense that held up through one of the most grueling stretches of games any team has had this season.
