Three Game 3s Sunday: The WCWS Field and Wells' HR Record All Decided Today

By Jordan Reeves

May 24, 2026

Florida vs. Texas Tech, Oklahoma vs. Mississippi State, and Texas vs. Arizona State all go to deciding games today. The final three WCWS berths — and a potential home run record — hang in the balance.

College softball's postseason arrives at its most consequential day. Three Super Regional series tied at one game apiece send their deciding Game 3s to Sunday, and by the time the last out is recorded tonight, the 2026 Women's College World Series field will be complete.

Three programs will celebrate. Three programs will go home. And in Norman, Oklahoma, the afternoon carries an extra layer that has been building since March: Kendall Wells enters her Game 3 with 39 home runs, one behind Megan Grant's all-time NCAA record of 40.

The Three Games

Florida hosts Texas Tech in Gainesville in the first of Sunday's elimination games, with first pitch around noon ET. Texas Tech shocked Florida 10-8 in Game 1, breaking the Gators' 36-1 home tournament record — one of the most remarkable numbers in college softball. Florida responded with a dominant 10-2 win in Game 2 to force Sunday's deciding game. Florida's track record at home gives them the edge, but Texas Tech arrived in Gainesville and already won one game. A second is entirely possible.

Oklahoma hosts Mississippi State at 1:30 PM ET on ESPN at Love's Field in Norman. The series has been extraordinary. Miss State pulled off one of the biggest Super Regional upsets in recent memory with an 11-9 Game 1 win, but Oklahoma bounced back with a controlled 7-1 victory in Game 2. The deciding game tips the balance back toward the home team in a series that has gone back and forth all weekend.

Texas hosts Arizona State in Austin with a first pitch TBD. The defending national champions have twice now been pushed to the edge by No. 19 Arizona State, who took Game 1 4-1 before Texas survived Game 2 4-3. Texas has not lost consecutive games at McCombs Field in the postseason in recent memory, but Arizona State has already proven they can win there. One more game.

Wells Enters at 39

Kendall Wells went 4-for-5 with home runs No. 38 and 39 in Game 1's loss to Mississippi State — two of the most remarkable at-bats in Super Regional history, both ultimately on the losing side. Oklahoma recovered in Game 2, but Wells' total remained at 39 going into Sunday.

Megan Grant set the all-time NCAA single-season home run record at 40 with UCLA. Grant's Bruins have already advanced to the WCWS after sweeping UCF. Wells has one game left in her Super Regional — and the possibility of tying or breaking that record with the season on the line.

One home run ties the record. Two or more would break it outright. Wells has proven she can hit in the biggest moments of the biggest games. Sunday in Norman gives her another chance.

What Today Completes

Five teams are already confirmed for Devon Park: Tennessee, Nebraska, Arkansas, Alabama, and UCLA. Today's three Game 3s determine the remaining spots. By tonight, the full bracket for the 2026 Women's College World Series will be set, with first games scheduled for May 28 in Oklahoma City.

The range of possible outcomes is wide. Florida advancing sends a veteran program to the WCWS. Texas Tech advancing continues one of college softball's most dramatic weeks. Oklahoma advancing means Wells gets more at-bats and the HR race lives. Arizona State advancing means the defending champion's title defense ends on their home field.

What's Next

The three Game 3s begin around noon ET and run through the evening. The final result sets the complete WCWS bracket, which Devon Park will announce with seedings and first-round matchups. The Women's College World Series opens May 28 in Oklahoma City with group-stage play in a double-elimination format. Today's survivors join Tennessee, Nebraska, Arkansas, Alabama, and UCLA as the eight teams competing for a national championship.
