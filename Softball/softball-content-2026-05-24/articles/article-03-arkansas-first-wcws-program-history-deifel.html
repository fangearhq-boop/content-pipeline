Arkansas Reaches WCWS for First Time in Program History

By Emily Rawlings

May 24, 2026

A 10-2 win over Duke on Saturday in Fayetteville completed Arkansas's Super Regional sweep and sent the Razorbacks to Devon Park for the first time in program history. Coach Courtney Deifel was 0-for-5 in Super Regionals before this week.

It took nine straight NCAA Tournament appearances, five Super Regional losses, and years of building something that might finally break through. On Saturday in Fayetteville, Arkansas softball did exactly that.

The Razorbacks defeated Duke 10-2 in Super Regional Game 2 at Bogle Park, completing a sweep of the Blue Devils and clinching the program's first-ever trip to the Women's College World Series. There has never been an Arkansas softball team at Devon Park. Until now.

The Breakthrough Deifel Built

Coach Courtney Deifel arrived at Arkansas and systematically transformed the program. Nine consecutive NCAA Tournament appearances. A pitching staff that competed annually in one of college softball's toughest conferences. Players who developed into All-Americans and professionals. And through all of it, five Super Regional exits — the most painful possible way to fall short of the WCWS.

That number ended this weekend. Arkansas won Game 1 over Duke 14-5 on Friday, then followed it with a 10-2 Game 2 victory on Saturday to close the door. Deifel's 0-for-5 Super Regional record became 1-for-6 and, more importantly, the program's first-ever WCWS berth was secured.

The combination of pitching stability and offensive production that Arkansas brought to this Super Regional was the product of years of program development. They handled Duke — a team in their fourth Super Regional in five years — in two games without letting the pressure of history create paralysis. They played their game and they won.

The Bogle Park Moment

Bogle Park in Fayetteville has hosted nine consecutive NCAA Tournament games under Deifel. For the first eight years, the tournament ended at Bogle Park — sometimes in the Regional, sometimes in the Super Regional. This time it ended differently. This time Arkansas won.

The fans who have watched this program knock on the door repeatedly understood the weight of Saturday's final out. Nine years of work, five Super Regional heartbreaks, and now a WCWS ticket in hand. The program is going to Oklahoma City. The wait is over.

Duke's Season in Context

Duke reached their fourth Super Regional in five years and went 0-for-4 in the format. That does not diminish what the Blue Devils built. A program that hosts Super Regionals is a successful program. They arrived in Fayetteville as legitimate competitors — and they pushed Arkansas in spots throughout the series. Duke's 2026 season is one to be proud of. Their breakthrough will come.

What's Next

Arkansas advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. The Razorbacks will receive their bracket assignment and first-round opponent when the full eight-team WCWS field is finalized after Sunday's three remaining Game 3s. Arkansas enters Devon Park as a first-timer with something to prove — and a coaching staff that has been building toward exactly this kind of opportunity for nine years.
