UCLA Sweeps UCF — Megan Grant's Bruins Head to Devon Park

By Sarah Mitchell

May 24, 2026

UCLA won both games of the Los Angeles Super Regional, with Rylee Slimp's walk-off three-run homer ending Game 1 in a run-rule. Megan Grant, who broke the all-time NCAA single-season home run record with her 40th home run, is heading to the WCWS.

Megan Grant is going to Devon Park.

UCLA swept UCF in the Los Angeles Super Regional — 9-1 in a run-rule in Game 1, then a decisive Game 2 win on Saturday — advancing the Bruins to the Women's College World Series. Grant, who broke the all-time NCAA single-season home run record with her 40th home run earlier this month, gets the WCWS as her final stage.

Rylee Slimp Ends Game 1 Early

Game 1 did not go to seven innings. Rylee Slimp hit a walk-off three-run home run at Easton Stadium in Los Angeles, triggering the run rule and ending the game early in UCLA's favor. The 9-1 final gave the Bruins a comfortable lead in the series and set up Saturday's Game 2 with UCLA needing one more win to advance.

UCLA delivered that win Saturday, completing the sweep and sending their roster to Oklahoma City with momentum from a dominant two-game showing against a UCF team that had no business going quietly. UCF's Cinderella story — their second Super Regional appearance in program history — ended in Los Angeles.

Grant Gets the WCWS Stage

Megan Grant has been the most remarkable individual story in college softball this year. She hit her 40th home run this season, breaking the NCAA all-time single-season record previously held by Laura Espinoza (37, Arizona, 1995). Grant set the record, then kept playing — and UCLA kept winning.

Now Grant gets Devon Park. The WCWS is the most visible stage in college softball, and she arrives there with the most impressive single-season home run total in the sport's history. Whether she extends the record further at the WCWS remains to be seen, but she will do it in front of the largest audience the sport offers.

The Wells-Grant Scenario

There is another dimension to Grant's WCWS arrival that depends on Sunday's results. Kendall Wells enters Oklahoma's Game 3 against Mississippi State today with 39 home runs — one behind Grant's record of 40. If Wells reaches the WCWS, the greatest home run race in NCAA softball history will have a final chapter in Oklahoma City.

Grant at 40. Wells at 39. Both in the WCWS. That scenario is possible — it just requires Oklahoma to win their Game 3 at 1:30 PM ET today. The stage is set for one of the sport's best stories to continue.

What's Next

UCLA advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. The Bruins will receive their bracket assignment and first-round matchup when the full eight-team WCWS field is confirmed after Sunday's three Game 3s are completed. UCLA enters Devon Park with momentum from a Super Regional sweep, a home run record holder in the lineup, and the possibility of a defining final series if the rest of the bracket falls the right way.
