Alabama Sweeps LSU with Dominant Pitching to Advance to 16th WCWS

By Jordan Reeves

May 24, 2026

Jocelyn Briski threw a one-hit shutout with 11 strikeouts in Game 1. Alabama then closed out the series 4-1 in Game 2. The Crimson Tide advance to their 16th Women's College World Series.

Alabama entered the Tuscaloosa Super Regional as one of the tournament's most respected pitching programs, and in two games against LSU, they confirmed exactly why. The Crimson Tide swept the Tigers 7-0 and 4-1, outscoring LSU 11-1 across the series, and punched their ticket to Devon Park for the 16th time in program history.

Briski's Game 1 Masterpiece

Jocelyn Briski did not give LSU much on Friday night. One hit. Eleven strikeouts. A 7-0 shutout at Rhoads Stadium that was so complete it felt surgical rather than competitive.

LSU arrived in Tuscaloosa having outscored their Regional opponents 23-2 — a dominant regional performance that suggested they could generate offense against anyone. Briski did not allow that to translate. She worked through the Tigers' lineup with command and missed bats consistently, giving the Crimson Tide exactly what they needed to build a comfortable lead and never look back.

A one-hit shutout with 11 strikeouts in a Super Regional game is one of the best individual pitching performances of this tournament. Briski delivered it when it mattered most.

Game 2 Closes the Series

Alabama did not need another dominant outing in Game 2 — they just needed to win. A three-run fourth inning provided the margin, and the Crimson Tide held on for a 4-1 victory to complete the sweep. The series result — 11-1 in Alabama's favor — was as lopsided as the numbers suggest. LSU had no real answer for Alabama's pitching across two full games.

Alabama's 16th WCWS

This is Alabama's 16th Women's College World Series appearance. That number speaks to a level of sustained program excellence that few programs in any sport can match. The Crimson Tide go to Devon Park regularly because they are built to get there — recruiting, pitching development, and in-game execution all functioning at the highest level season after season.

The 2026 version of Alabama softball was anchored by one of the country's best pitching staffs. Briski's Game 1 shut out LSU completely. The combination of Briski and Vic Moten gave Alabama a rotation that proved dominant throughout the tournament, and that pitching depth carries directly into the WCWS bracket.

What's Next

Alabama advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. The Crimson Tide will receive their bracket assignment when the full WCWS field is confirmed after Sunday's three remaining Game 3s. Alabama enters Devon Park as one of the tournament favorites, armed with elite pitching and a history of deep WCWS runs. Their 16th appearance is not just a number — it reflects a program that knows how to perform in Oklahoma City.
