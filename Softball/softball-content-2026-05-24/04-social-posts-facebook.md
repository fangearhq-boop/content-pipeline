### STORY 2: Nebraska 9-1 Over Oklahoma State — WCWS

**Long-Form Post**
🌽 NEBRASKA IS GOING TO THE WCWS 🌽

It has been 13 years. The Huskers waited 13 years.

Saturday afternoon in Lincoln, Nebraska run-ruled Oklahoma State 9-1 — using a seven-run fifth inning — to complete the Super Regional sweep and punch their ticket to Devon Park for the first time since 2013.

This is what that means:

51-6 final record. Not a typo. Fifty-one wins and six losses.

A 26-game winning streak — the longest active streak in Division I softball and the longest in Nebraska program history.

The eighth Women's College World Series appearance in program history, and the first since a 13-year drought that frustrated one of the most passionate fanbases in the Big Ten.

Nebraska won 8-1 on Friday, then came back Saturday and used that five-inning burst to take care of business. The Huskers didn't let the moment get big. They swept their Super Regional and they looked every bit like a team that could win a national championship.

Bob Devaney Stadium sold out. The fans who waited 13 years watched it happen. Nebraska softball is going to Oklahoma City.

### STORY 5: UCLA Sweeps UCF — Grant WCWS-Bound

**Long-Form Post**
🔵 MEGAN GRANT IS GOING TO THE WCWS 🔵

Let's talk about what that means.

Megan Grant broke the all-time NCAA single-season home run record this year. She's sitting at 40 — more than any player in the history of the sport. She earned that record with her UCLA team still in the tournament, which means she gets one more stage to perform on.

The biggest stage in college softball.

UCLA swept UCF in the Los Angeles Super Regional — 9-1 in a run-rule Friday (Rylee Slimp hit the walk-off three-run homer to end it early), then won again convincingly on Saturday. The Bruins earned their WCWS spot with two dominant home performances, and they send Grant to Devon Park with a chance to extend the all-time record.

And here is the part that makes Devon Park even more compelling: Kendall Wells (Oklahoma) enters today's Game 3 with 39 home runs — one behind Grant. If Wells wins today and advances to the WCWS, the all-time home run race will have a final chapter in Oklahoma City.

Two players. One all-time record. The WCWS.

UCF made their second Super Regional appearance in program history and won hearts along the way. But UCLA's talent was too much. Grant goes to Devon Park.
