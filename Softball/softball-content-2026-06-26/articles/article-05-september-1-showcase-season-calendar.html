Why September 1 Matters: The Summer Softball Calendar Every Fan Should Know

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 26, 2026

The transfer portal closed June 22. The Women's College World Series ended June 5. For most college softball fans, these events signal the end of the content calendar until preseason polls arrive in January.

That reading misses what is actually happening right now. The people who build college softball rosters five years into the future are not taking the summer off. This is when the work happens.

July 19: PGF Nationals Opens

The Premier Girls Fastpitch National Championship is the most important recruiting event in college softball. The 2026 edition runs July 19 through August 4, with Week One (July 19–26) featuring the 18U and 16U age divisions.

The players competing in those divisions will form the backbone of recruiting classes entering college in 2028 and 2029. College coaches at every level attend in force. The stands at PGF Nationals are filled with assistants holding clipboards and head coaches who have made the trip specifically to see one player in one at-bat.

Programs that came up short in the 2026 transfer portal — unable to land the players they targeted in the 15-day window — are now watching the summer showcases. The next transfer portal star is a 17-year-old right now competing for her club team. PGF Nationals is where she announces herself to the world.

The PGF All-American Games, held during the event, feature the top 40 high school seniors in the country. These are not future prospects — they are the Class of 2027, the players who will be signing their National Letters of Intent before the fall semester ends. The All-American game is the final public evaluation before those signatures happen.

August: Fall Practice Begins

College programs open fall practice in August, giving coaching staffs the first opportunity to see their 2027 rosters take shape in person. Transfer additions who committed during the portal window now meet their new teammates. Incoming freshmen from the Class of 2027 arrive on campus for the first time in a team environment.

Fall practice does not count in the win-loss record. It is, however, where the season's competitive picture actually forms. Coaches identify which players are ahead of schedule, which depth pieces need more development, and whether the offseason construction holds up against the daily grind of practice.

September 1: The Most Important Date in Summer Softball

September 1 is the date that the entire recruiting machinery has been building toward since the previous season ended. On September 1, Division I coaches can contact Class of 2028 players — current high school juniors — directly for the first time.

Before September 1, coaches can attend showcases and evaluate players in person, but the direct communication that moves a recruitment from "interest" to "relationship" cannot legally happen. On September 1, every coach in the country calls, texts, and sends mail to the same list of names.

For families of Class of 2028 players, September 1 means their phones will not stop. For coaches, it means the summer of evaluation pays off in the fall of contact. For fans, it means the programs that finished summer with the best scouting lists will have the best shots at the Class of 2028 signings that arrive in November and December.

What's Next

The NFCA Convention in the fall brings the coaching community together for clinics, networking, and the informal recruiting conversations that happen between sessions. After that, early signing period opens and the Class of 2027 commitments become official. By the time January arrives and preseason polls are released, the programs that used summer well will already have built two years of their future roster. The offseason is not quiet. It just sounds that way if you are only listening for game scores.
