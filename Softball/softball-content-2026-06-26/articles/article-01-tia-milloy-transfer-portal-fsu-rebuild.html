Tia Milloy Enters Transfer Portal: FSU's Rebuild Just Got Harder

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 26, 2026

Florida State spent the past two weeks in contact with a player who, as of Thursday, is no longer available through quiet conversation — she is now officially on the open market.

Oklahoma utility player Tia Milloy has announced she is entering the transfer portal with two years of eligibility remaining. Milloy appeared in 41 games for the Sooners in 2026, making five starts. She is now available to any program with a need and a scholarship to offer.

For FSU, it is not a disaster. But it is a complication that arrives at a moment when the Seminoles need straightforward answers, not more open questions.

Why FSU Was Watching

FSU's interest in Milloy stemmed directly from the losses of the spring. The Seminoles watched Isa Torres commit to Texas and Jaysoni Beachum commit to Florida — two of their most productive offensive players — during the 15-day transfer portal window that closed June 22.

Milloy, as a player who had not used the standard portal window, was available through a separate pathway outside the window. FSU was in contact. The plan, as understood, was to bring in a versatile utility piece to supplement the existing roster rather than chase a headline addition the program could not realistically land after the big moves had already settled.

That option is now gone. Milloy chose to enter the portal fully, making herself available to the entire market rather than committing quietly to one program.

What Milloy Brings to the Market

Utility players with major-conference experience and two years of eligibility remaining are not common portal additions. Milloy fills multiple positions, which is precisely the attribute that made her attractive to a program trying to rebuild a roster that lost its most versatile contributors.

She appeared in 41 games in 2026 — significant volume for a role player — and brings the kind of positional flexibility that programs value most when assembling a complete lineup. With two years remaining, any program that lands her gets a substantial return on the scholarship investment.

The list of programs interested in a two-year utility player with SEC experience will not be short.

What It Means for FSU

The honest assessment: FSU's 2027 roster remains competitive without Milloy. The Seminoles have Jazzy Francik, the ACC Pitcher of the Year, returning as one of the nation's elite arms. They added Ella Dodge (23 home runs from Tennessee) and Nicole Edmiaston (11 home runs from Stetson) through the portal. Ashtyn Danley returns as a reliable presence in the lineup.

The program is not in crisis. The portal losses were painful, but FSU has responded with legitimate replacements in the areas it controlled.

What changes with Milloy now in the open market is the plan for positional depth. If FSU was counting on her to fill a specific roster gap, that gap remains open. The program will need to identify another solution — either from the existing roster or through a mid-summer move outside the standard portal window.

What's Next

Milloy's commitment will be among the most closely watched developments of the summer. Programs that came up short in the portal window and still have roster flexibility at utility positions now have a two-year player to pursue. FSU, meanwhile, enters fall camp with a strong — if not complete — roster picture. The Seminoles have shown they can compete without adding every piece they target. The 2027 season will be the test of whether that remains true.
