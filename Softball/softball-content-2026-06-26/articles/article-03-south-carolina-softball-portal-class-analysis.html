South Carolina Adds Quietly: What the Gamecocks' Portal Class Actually Means

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | June 26, 2026

The post-portal power rankings placed South Carolina 5th. That is a meaningful position — it means the softball analytics community believes the Gamecocks improved their roster during the 15-day window. But South Carolina's offseason moves have received a fraction of the attention directed at Texas, Florida, Texas Tech, and Nebraska.

That is partly by design. South Carolina does not land the headline name. They land the right names for the right roles. The 2026 portal class is a case study in targeted roster construction.

What Came In

Brooke Bennett arrives as a catcher from Longwood University. The catching position is one of the most difficult to fill through the portal — elite catchers are rare and programs hold onto them. Bennett gives South Carolina a proven option behind the plate to anchor the lineup and the defense.

Lexie Shaver joins as a utility player from Kennesaw State. Utility players are the connective tissue of competitive rosters — they fill lineup gaps, allow coaches to deploy matchup-specific lineups, and provide insurance against injury at multiple positions. Shaver brings Atlantic Sun experience and the kind of positional flexibility that the Gamecocks identified as a roster need.

Bella Foran arrives as a Gold Glove transfer. Defensive reliability at her position fills a gap that existed after South Carolina's departure list formed. A Gold Glove-caliber defender adds immediate defensive value that teams can build around.

Three additions. Three specific roster needs addressed directly.

What Went Out

South Carolina lost six players in the portal: the starting shortstop, pitchers Nealy Lamb and KG Favors, outfielder-infielder Dakota Potter, Nia McKnight, and senior infielder Sage Scarmardo.

Scarmardo's departure is a graduation-equivalent roster exit. The others represent real losses. Lamb and Favors together constituted depth in the pitching rotation — not top-line starters, but reliable options. The starting shortstop is the most significant gap: that position requires daily reliability at a premium defensive spot, and replacing a starter there with portal depth is a meaningful ask.

Six out, three in. The net number looks unfavorable. The positional math, however, is more balanced than the raw count suggests.

The Strategic Read

South Carolina's portal approach reflects a philosophy that not all roster additions require national recognition. Programs like Texas and Florida can attract transfers whose names generate headlines. Programs like South Carolina — excellent but not operating at that tier of brand recognition — succeed by identifying role-specific needs and filling them with players who fit precisely, rather than chasing stars who may or may not integrate into an existing system.

Bennett at catcher, Shaver in utility, Foran in the field: these are calculated additions designed to address specific weaknesses. The pitching depth loss is the area of genuine concern. South Carolina's coaching staff will need to develop internal options or identify additional pitching solutions before fall camp ends.

What's Next

South Carolina heads into fall practice with a reshaped but purposefully constructed roster. The Gamecocks ranked 5th in the post-portal power rankings — a position that reflects realistic expectations: better than most programs, not yet at the tier of Texas or Florida, but with a legitimate path to the postseason and capable of exceeding that projection if the pitching development works out. The 2027 SEC schedule will be the first real test of whether this offseason construction holds up.
