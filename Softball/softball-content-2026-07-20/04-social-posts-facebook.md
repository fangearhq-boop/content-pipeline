### STORY 1: AUSL Final Day — OKC Spark Clinches, Cascade Out

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET

Nobody told the Oklahoma City Spark it was over.

The 2026 AUSL regular season ended Monday with one of the most dramatic final-day swings the league has produced: the Spark defeated the Carolina Blaze to clinch the league's third and final playoff berth — eliminating the Portland Cascade, who had appeared to hold that spot entering the day.

Here is what happened. The Utah Talons locked in the No. 1 seed by winning their season finale against the Chicago Bandits. The Bandits secured the No. 2 seed. And the third spot — which the Cascade thought was theirs — came down to one last game between the Spark and the Blaze on Monday evening. The Spark won. The Cascade's season is over.

The postseason bracket is now set:

No. 1 Utah Talons — direct bye to the AUSL Championship Series, July 25-27 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas.
No. 2 Chicago Bandits vs. No. 3 Oklahoma City Spark — single-elimination Play-in Game on Thursday, July 23, at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond.

The winner of the play-in faces the Talons in the best-of-three Championship. Game 1 on July 25 airs on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television.

The Cascade lost two straight to the Texas Volts in their final regular-season home series last week. They never recovered. The Spark needed it to go exactly that way, and it did.

This is the AUSL delivering the kind of final-day drama that reminds everyone why regular-season games matter.

### STORY 2: PGF Nationals — Bracket Play Begins Today

#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET

Pool play is finished. Now it gets real.

PGF Nationals 2026 is moving into bracket/elimination play today across the five Southern California venues — Great Park Irvine, Bill Barber Park, Fountain Valley Sports Park, Harvard Park, and Huntington Beach Sports Complex. After two days of pool play over the weekend establishing seeds and records, the 18U and 16U Premier divisions are now in win-or-go-home territory.

More than 300 teams from 34 states are competing this week. The 18U Premier alone brought 72 teams from 22 states into 18 pools. Those pools have sorted themselves. The bracket begins now.

Here is why the next few days matter so much beyond the championship games themselves: September 1 is 42 days away. That is when Division I college coaches can, for the very first time, directly contact Class of 2028 recruits. They have been watching all week and will keep watching through Friday. Bracket play is when the players they've been evaluating in pool play must prove they can deliver under elimination pressure — the same thing a coach needs to know before they pick up the phone on September 1.

Top contenders heading into bracket play in the 18U Premier include GA Impact Taylor, Mojo-Lewis, Arizona Storm-Mendez, and Alliance Fastpitch. The 18U Premier Championship game will air on ESPNU. The 16U Premier Championship airs on ESPN+.

The pool play weekend is over. The part that actually matters most is just beginning.

### STORY 3: Play-in Preview — Bandits vs. OKC Spark

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

Here is your AUSL Play-in Game.

No. 2 Chicago Bandits vs. No. 3 Oklahoma City Spark. Thursday, July 23. 9 PM ET on ESPN. Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas.

Win and you go to the Championship. Lose and your season is done.

The Bandits arrive at this game on the back of the most impressive run in AUSL history: a nine-game winning streak — the longest in the league's brief existence. They've been playing their best softball of the season heading into the postseason and earned the No. 2 seed by staying relentless through the second half. Maya Brady, with 8 home runs and a .415 batting average, has been their biggest offensive weapon.

The Spark arrive at this game by a narrower margin. They needed to win their final regular-season game on Monday to eliminate the Portland Cascade. They did exactly that. OKC shortstop Sydney Romero made a diving glove-flip play on July 19 to record the final out of a crucial 6-5 win that kept their season alive heading into the Monday clincher. Jailyn Ford pitched a complete game against the Texas Volts that proved the Spark could handle big moments.

This is the matchup professional softball needed: the team on a historic run against the team that refused to accept it was over.

The winner goes to Davis Diamond to face the Utah Talons in the Championship Series beginning July 25.

### STORY 4: Megan Grant Season Capsule

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Megan Grant's 2026 season is over.

The Portland Cascade was eliminated Monday when the Oklahoma City Spark clinched the third playoff seed. Grant, who signed with the Cascade as the fourth overall pick in the AUSL Draft, will not play in the postseason. Her team didn't make it.

Her year, however, was unlike anything the sport has seen from a single player in a single year.

In the spring, playing her senior season at UCLA, Grant broke the NCAA Division I single-season home run record — hitting 42 home runs to surpass Laura Espinoza's mark of 37, which had stood since 1995. A 31-year record. Her UCLA career ended with 91 home runs, also a program record.

Then came the professional debut. On June 10, in her first AUSL at-bat, she hit a 313-foot home run — the longest in AUSL history. In roughly 20 professional games, she added 8 more home runs for a combined total estimated at 50 home runs and 106 RBIs across her entire 2026 season. Both of those numbers are extraordinary for a single calendar year. Most players never reach 50 home runs in their careers.

Her former UCLA teammate Maya Brady is still playing. Brady — with 8 home runs, a .415 batting average, and 17 RBIs for the Chicago Bandits — is still chasing the first Triple Crown in AUSL history. The two Bruins began 2026 as teammates in college and ended the regular season tied for the AUSL home run lead. One of them is still alive. The other goes home.

Grant's year needs no defense. It was extraordinary on its own terms.
