Nobody Told the Spark It Was Over

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 20, 2026

The Portland Cascade thought they were going to the postseason. The Oklahoma City Spark made sure they weren't.

On the final day of the 2026 AUSL regular season, the Spark defeated the Carolina Blaze to clinch the league's third and final playoff berth — eliminating the Cascade, who had appeared to hold that spot entering Monday. The upset reshuffled the postseason picture entirely on the last possible day it could.

What Happened

The AUSL regular season ran 25 games per team from June 9 through July 20. Three playoff berths were available. The Utah Talons locked in the No. 1 seed by defeating the Chicago Bandits in Monday's season finale. The Bandits secured No. 2. And the third spot came down to a single game: the Spark against the Blaze at Smith Family Stadium in Durham, North Carolina.

The Cascade entered the day with a 14-11 record. The Spark were 13-11. OKC needed a win over last-place Carolina to force the Cascade out. They got it. The Cascade's season ends without a postseason game. The Spark's season continues to Thursday.

The result flipped what had seemed like a settled picture entering the week. The Cascade had lost two consecutive games to the Texas Volts in their final home series — a 7-2 defeat on July 17 and another loss on July 18. Those losses left the door open for OKC, and the Spark walked through it.

How OKC Got Here

The Spark didn't just get lucky. They won the game they needed, when they needed it, against a team that had nothing to play for.

The sequence actually began Sunday, July 19, when the Spark defeated the Blaze 6-5 to keep their playoff hopes alive. In that game, shortstop Sydney Romero made a diving stop and flipped the ball to second base with her glove to record the final out — a play that kept OKC's season alive with one game remaining. Monday, the Spark delivered again.

Jailyn Ford had already demonstrated she could handle big-game pressure after spinning a complete game win for OKC against the Texas Volts earlier in the month. The Spark went into the final week as a team that had earned its shot at the final day by winning when it counted.

The Final Bracket

The 2026 AUSL postseason bracket is now official:

No. 1 Utah Talons earn a direct bye to the best-of-three AUSL Championship Series at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas, beginning July 25. No. 2 Chicago Bandits face No. 3 Oklahoma City Spark in a single-elimination Play-in Game on Thursday, July 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond. The play-in winner advances to face the Talons in the Championship.

Game 1 of the Championship Series on July 25 airs on ABC — making it the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television.

What's Next

The Cascade's season is over. The Spark's just got more interesting. The AUSL Play-in Game between the Chicago Bandits and the Oklahoma City Spark is Thursday, July 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond. One game. Win and you go to the Championship. Lose and your summer ends. The Bandits bring an AUSL-record nine-game winning streak into Thursday. The Spark bring the momentum of having won the game nobody expected them to win.
