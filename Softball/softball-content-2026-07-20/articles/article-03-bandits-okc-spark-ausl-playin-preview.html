One Game. Everything on the Line. Here Is Your AUSL Play-in Matchup.

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 20, 2026

The 2026 AUSL postseason begins Thursday at 9 PM ET on ESPN, and it begins with a matchup that professional softball could not have written better if it tried.

No. 2 Chicago Bandits vs. No. 3 Oklahoma City Spark. Single-elimination. Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. One team advances to the Championship. One team goes home.

The Bandits: Built for This

The Chicago Bandits did not stumble into the No. 2 seed. They earned it by putting together the longest winning streak in AUSL history — nine consecutive wins that carried them through the second half of the regular season and kept the No. 1 seed race alive until the final week.

The Bandits enter Thursday on the strength of that run, with an offense that has been the league's most consistent. Maya Brady leads that attack. The former UCLA Bruin finished the regular season with 8 home runs and a .415 batting average, compiling 17 RBIs and generating power that has made her one of the postseason's most dangerous hitters to face. Brady entered the year tied with former UCLA teammate Megan Grant for the AUSL home run lead — now Grant's season is over and Brady is still here.

Pitching coach Taryne Mowatt-McKinney has shaped a staff capable of shutting down opposing lineups in high-pressure environments. The Bandits have the experience and the talent to advance.

The Spark: Earned Their Way In

The Oklahoma City Spark clinched the No. 3 seed on Monday — the final day of the regular season — by defeating the Carolina Blaze to eliminate the Portland Cascade. They got here the hardest way a team can get to a playoff game: by winning the last game they absolutely had to win.

Before Monday's clincher, the Spark kept their season alive with a 6-5 win over the Blaze on July 19. Shortstop Sydney Romero made a diving stop and flipped the ball to second base with her glove to record the final out — the kind of play that defines a team's identity. The Spark are a team that wins on defense when the margin is thin.

Jailyn Ford proved earlier this month she could pitch a complete game in a meaningful situation, spinning a complete-game win against the Texas Volts when OKC needed a statement performance. The Spark go into Thursday without a nine-game win streak but with something else: the belief of a team that just did what everyone assumed they couldn't.

The Stakes

The winner faces the Utah Talons in the best-of-three AUSL Championship Series at Davis Diamond beginning July 25. The Talons earned the No. 1 seed and a direct bye to the Championship — they have been resting and waiting since Monday. Montana Fouts leads their pitching staff. The Talons have been the league's best team all season and will have had more than a week of rest by the time Thursday's play-in winner arrives.

Game 1 of the Championship Series on July 25 airs on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television. The team that gets through Thursday's play-in game will play in front of the largest audience professional softball has ever reached.

What's Next

The AUSL Play-in Game is Thursday, July 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. The AUSL Championship Series begins July 25 at Davis Diamond, with Game 1 on ABC. One team from Thursday's game will be on that broadcast. The other will be watching at home.
