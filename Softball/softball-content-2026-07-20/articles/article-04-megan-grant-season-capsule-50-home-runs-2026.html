Fifty Home Runs. One Year. Megan Grant's 2026 Season Is Over.

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 20, 2026

Megan Grant's 2026 softball season ended Monday without a postseason game.

The Portland Cascade was eliminated when the Oklahoma City Spark defeated the Carolina Blaze on the final day of the AUSL regular season. Grant, who signed with the Cascade as the fourth overall pick in the AUSL Draft, will not play in the league's first postseason. Her team did not make it.

What she did in 2026 still needs to be said out loud.

The Spring: Rewriting the Record Book

In her senior season at UCLA, Megan Grant broke the NCAA Division I single-season home run record. She hit 42 home runs — surpassing the mark of 37 set by Laura Espinoza in 1995. Espinoza's record had stood for 31 years. Grant ended it in the spring of 2026.

Her final college season numbers were historic across every category: a .455 batting average, a .639 on-base percentage, a 1.308 slugging percentage, 91 RBIs, and 88 runs scored. She walked 76 times in a season where pitchers had every reason to avoid giving her something to hit — and she still found the ball when they did. Grant finished her UCLA career with 91 home runs, a program record.

The Summer: Making the AUSL Take Notice Immediately

The professional debut came on June 10 against the Carolina Blaze. Her first home run traveled 313 feet — the longest in AUSL history at the time. The league's distance record fell in her first game.

She added 8 home runs in approximately 20 professional games, bringing her combined 2026 total to an estimated 50 home runs and 106 RBIs across the entire calendar year. Both figures are extraordinary. Most players never reach 50 career home runs. Grant hit them in a single season spanning two levels of competition.

The number 313 matters here. The distance of Grant's debut home run off Karlyn Pickens — the No. 1 overall pick in the AUSL Draft and the league's marquee pitcher — was not a warning shot. It was a statement that the AUSL had a hitter capable of making professional pitching look like college pitching in the very first moment she stepped in against it.

The Teammate Who Is Still Playing

Maya Brady — Grant's former UCLA teammate, now with the Chicago Bandits — finished the AUSL regular season tied with Grant at 8 home runs. Brady's batting average is .415 with 17 RBIs. She is still alive in the postseason, chasing what would be the first Triple Crown in AUSL history.

The two Bruins began 2026 wearing the same uniform in Westwood. They ended the regular season tied for the AUSL home run lead on different teams. On Monday, one of them made the playoffs. The other is done.

Brady's postseason continues Thursday. Grant's year ended when the final score from Durham came in.

What's Next

Grant's 2026 is finished. The Cascade did not reach the postseason. What comes next for Grant is an offseason that begins earlier than she wanted, the knowledge that she spent her first professional season building a foundation rather than playing in October. Brady and the Bandits play Thursday in the AUSL Play-in Game against the Oklahoma City Spark at Davis Diamond. The AUSL's best story from the regular season — 50 combined home runs in one year — ended Monday. The league's next chapter starts Thursday without Grant in it.
