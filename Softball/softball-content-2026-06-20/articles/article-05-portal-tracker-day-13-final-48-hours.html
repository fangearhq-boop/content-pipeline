Portal Tracker: Day 13 of 15 — The Final 48 Hours Begin

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 20, 2026

Day 13 of 15. The NCAA Division I softball transfer portal — which opened June 8 and closes June 22 — is down to its final 48 hours. At least 120 players entered the window. More than 79 have already committed, a rate exceeding 66 percent. Here is where the 2026 portal stands as the deadline approaches.

Texas Tech: Moriah Polar (Purdue, .555 BA, 101 hits, nation's top hitter by average), Jayden Heavener (No. 1 portal pitcher, 2.81 ERA, 128 K), Kasidi Pickering (Oklahoma, .370 BA, 20 HR in 2026, 50 career HR)

Still Uncommitted

Jaysoni Beachum (FSU, 3B) — Softball America No. 1: Three seasons at Florida State produced numbers that define her tier — a .417 batting average, 16 home runs, 66 RBI, and a .549 on-base percentage in 2026 alone. Career totals: 37 home runs, 180 RBI, 202 hits. She won the 2024 NFCA Freshman of the Year award and earned Second-Team All-American recognition in 2026. She has visited Florida. She has not committed. With 48 hours remaining, she is the final major story of this portal window.

How This Window Unfolded

The 2026 transfer portal produced two elite classes and several strong supporting moves, all compressed into 13 days.

Texas built the most narratively powerful class by landing Isa Torres — the national Player of the Year, a Texas native, and the single most impactful addition in any portal window this offseason. Paired with Swan, Lincoln, and Altmeyer, the Longhorns fortified a two-time defending championship roster rather than rebuilt one.

Texas Tech answered with what Softball America immediately ranked the No. 1 class nationally. Polar arrived as the nation's top hitter by average. Heavener arrived as the top available pitcher in the portal. Pickering arrived as a 50-career-home-run power bat from Oklahoma. All three to a program that went 61-8 and played for a national championship in 2026. The Red Raiders don't just look dangerous heading into 2027 — they look like the team most capable of knocking Texas off the top.

Arkansas, Tennessee, Oklahoma State, Alabama, and Texas A&M filled meaningful roster needs without producing the kind of class volume that makes national headlines. Each move addresses a specific need. That's competent roster management in the portal era.

Florida State started from the worst position of any major program in the window. Torres left for Texas. Beachum's status remains unresolved. Ella Dodge — a top-10 portal prospect with two years of eligibility — gave the Seminoles a legitimate building block. Whether that's enough depends heavily on what happens before June 22.

The Numbers Behind the Window

120 players entered the portal. More than 79 have committed — a commitment rate north of 66 percent with two days remaining. The final 48 hours historically produce a secondary wave of depth signings as programs fill roster spots and players who held out for preferred destinations make decisions they can't delay any longer.

The window that opened June 8 was always going to close with Beachum as the last major question. That is exactly what is happening. Everything else has landed. The final chapter waits on one player's decision.

What's Next

The portal closes June 22 at midnight. After that, the 2027 rosters are set — at least as far as portal-era movement allows — and the conversation shifts from transactions to preparation.

Beachum's decision is the last significant news event of this cycle. Where she goes will determine how complete Florida State's rebuild looks at portal close, and which program — if any — upgrades into the upper tier of 2027 contenders by landing the No. 1 available player. Every major program without a third baseman of her caliber is watching.

After the window shuts, expect the preseason rankings conversation to heat up quickly. Texas and Texas Tech will both have legitimate claims to the No. 1 spot when those polls open in the fall. The portal just ensured that the most compelling rivalry in college softball in 2027 will be two programs separated by roughly 350 miles on Interstate 35.

Forty-eight hours. One big name still undecided. Watch the June 22 deadline closely — the final hours of this window have earned the attention.
