Beachum Watch: Day 13, Two Days Left and Still No Answer

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 20, 2026

Forty-eight hours.

That is what remains in the 2026 Division I softball transfer portal window, and Jaysoni Beachum — Softball America's No. 1 rated transfer, the last elite name uncommitted on the board — has not made her decision. Day 13 of 15 is here. The window closes June 22. And college softball is holding its breath.

This is not hyperbole. When the portal opened June 8, the field of elite available players was deep. Torres, Beachum, Polar, Pickering, Heavener, Wetteland, Hartley — name after name came off the board. One by one, programs made their moves and players found homes. Beachum has outlasted every single one of them. She remains the last piece of a portal window that reshaped the sport, and she has not said a word.

The Numbers Behind the Wait

To understand why every program with a scholarship available has been waiting on Beachum, you have to understand what she produced at Florida State.

In 2026, her final season in Tallahassee, Beachum posted a .417 batting average with 16 home runs, 66 RBIs, and a .549 on-base percentage. Those are not good numbers — they are elite numbers, the kind that belong on an All-American ballot, which is exactly where they landed.

Over her career at FSU, she accumulated 37 home runs, 180 RBIs, and 202 hits. She won Second-Team All-American honors in 2026. She was a two-time All-ACC selection. In 2024, as a freshman, the NFCA named her Division I Freshman of the Year — a distinction that told anyone paying attention that she was something special long before she posted a .417 average as an upperclassman.

She has one year of eligibility remaining. Softball America ranks her No. 1 among all available transfers. There is no qualifier on that rating — she is the best player in the portal, full stop.

The Florida Visit and What It Means

The most talked-about moment of Beachum's portal process came when she visited the Florida Gators — Florida State's fiercest in-state rival. For a player who spent three seasons at FSU, a trip to Gainesville was about as pointed a statement as you can make without actually committing anywhere.

Florida has a genuine case. The Gators have a proven program under Tim Walton, infrastructure to compete nationally, and a real need at third base following Kendall Grover's graduation. Beachum fits the positional profile and the production profile. The visit was not a courtesy call.

But Florida has not gotten a commitment. The visit happened days ago, and Beachum has remained silent. That silence is either the quiet before an imminent announcement — or evidence that she is still genuinely weighing options and the Florida visit was one stop among several.

The rivalry dimension is impossible to ignore. Beachum leaving Tallahassee for Gainesville would be one of the most charged portal decisions the sport has seen. Whether that matters to her, only she knows.

The Context of This Wait

The portal opened June 8. It closes June 22. That is a 15-day window, and Beachum entered it having already been one of the most-discussed players in college softball before a single visit was made.

She is not the only player still without a landing spot — but she is unquestionably the most consequential one. Every other elite transfer found a home. Isa Torres went to Texas. Moriah Polar, Kasidi Pickering, and Jayden Heavener went to Texas Tech. Katie Wetteland went to Arkansas. Madison Hartley went to Oklahoma State. The portal reshuffled the sport's top tier, and Beachum watched it all happen while keeping her own decision private.

The programs still recruiting her are not passive. Active pursuit in the final days of a portal window is a different energy — there is no more waiting for the market to develop. There is only making the case and hoping it lands.

What's Next

June 22 is the hard deadline. Players who do not commit before the portal closes lose access to the scholarship flexibility and program options the portal provides. There is no extension, no grace period. The window shuts, and whatever Beachum decides at that point is final.

The programs most likely still in the conversation include Florida — the visit already happened, and the fit is real — along with other national contenders that have the roster space and the recruiting infrastructure to have stayed in the running this deep into the window. Oklahoma, Oklahoma State, and Alabama are programs that have routinely pursued elite portal talent at this level, and all have reasons to want a bat like Beachum's in their 2027 lineup.

When the announcement comes — and it has to come in the next 48 hours — it will likely arrive the way most of these portal decisions do: a graphic post on social media, a caption with her new program's colors, and an immediate wave of reactions across the college softball world.

For the program that lands her, it ends a long wait and adds the No. 1 transfer in the country to a roster that will be built around her final season. For everyone else, the window closes and the work turns to 2027.

Forty-eight hours. The answer is coming.
