Ella Dodge Commits to FSU: Tennessee Transfer Brings Two Years, Top-10 Talent to Tallahassee

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 20, 2026

Florida State is not done.

While the softball world has been focused on what the Seminoles lost in this transfer portal window — Isa Torres to Texas, and Jaysoni Beachum still waiting somewhere — FSU quietly landed a significant piece on Thursday evening. Ella Dodge, a Tennessee Lady Vols transfer who Softball America ranks as the No. 10 overall portal prospect and No. 4 middle infielder available, committed to Florida State on June 19.

Dodge is a Florida native from Bradenton — Lakewood Ranch specifically — who leaves Knoxville with two full seasons of eligibility remaining. She is a redshirt junior. She is talented, productive, and young enough to make a real long-term impact in Tallahassee. For a program rebuilding its infield in real time, this is exactly the kind of addition that matters.

What Dodge Brings

The production at Tennessee tells a clear story. In 2026, Dodge hit .285 with 49 hits, 44 runs scored, 46 RBIs, and 11 home runs across 64 starts. Those are not empty numbers — 46 RBIs on 49 hits reflects consistent production with runners on base, and 11 home runs from an infielder is legitimate power, not accidental power.

Over her career with the Lady Vols — 120 games, 113 starts — she hit .262 with 18 home runs and 20 doubles. She developed into one of the more reliable bats in Tennessee's lineup, a player who showed up in big spots and produced across multiple seasons at a program that competes nationally.

Softball America lists her as a utility player, but her primary home is the infield, specifically third base. She brings experience, athleticism, and a versatility that gives Florida State's coaching staff options depending on how the 2027 roster takes shape around her.

She is also a Florida girl coming home. Lakewood Ranch is about three hours from Tallahassee. Recruiting local talent back to the state is never a bad story.

The Holes She Helps Fill

Florida State's 2026 transfer portal window has been defined more by departures than arrivals — at least publicly. Torres, the D1 Softball Player of the Year and the most decorated hitter in the sport last season, is now at Texas. Beachum, the No. 1 rated transfer, remains uncommitted but has visited Florida and has not returned to FSU.

Losing Torres and potentially Beachum from the same program in the same window would represent a significant production gap. Torres hit .530 last season. Beachum hit .417. Those are generational numbers leaving or having left the same roster. Replacing that output is not a one-player job — it is a roster-building project that takes multiple moves, multiple pieces, and time.

Dodge is one piece of that project. She's not Torres — nobody in this portal is Torres — but she is a ranked, proven, multi-year contributor who brings infield depth, legitimate power, and two years of runway. That runway matters as much as anything else. FSU is not just patching 2027; the program is building toward 2027 and 2028 simultaneously, and Dodge gives them a player who is part of both conversations.

What's Next

The transfer portal window closes in 48 hours, on June 22. Florida State still has time to add, and given what has moved off the roster this summer, it would be surprising if Dodge is the only addition before the deadline.

The most obvious remaining question for the Seminoles is Beachum. FSU has not publicly been named as a finalist in Beachum's process, and her Florida visit shifted the conversation elsewhere — but until she commits somewhere, nothing is final. If Beachum's destination turns out to be Tallahassee, the entire tenor of FSU's portal window changes immediately. Dodge plus Beachum plus whatever else the staff adds would constitute a genuine rebuild at the infield.

For the 2027 roster specifically, Dodge figures to compete for a starting role at third base and could see time at multiple infield spots depending on how the full picture develops. Her two seasons of eligibility give head coach Lonni Alameda flexibility that a one-year rental doesn't — Dodge can be part of a multi-year plan, not just a stopgap.

Florida State has been one of college softball's elite programs for years. Rosters turn over. The question is always whether the next generation of players can maintain the standard. Ella Dodge is the first confirmed answer to that question this summer — a ranked, proven, Florida-native infielder who chose Tallahassee when she had options elsewhere.

The portal window closes June 22. There are 48 hours left to see what else comes through the door.
