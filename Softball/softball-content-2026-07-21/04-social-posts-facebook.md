### STORY 1: AUSL Play-in — 2 Days Out

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET

The game everyone has been waiting for is two days away.

Thursday, July 23. 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. No. 2 Chicago Bandits vs. No. 3 Oklahoma City Spark. Win-or-go-home. One team advances to the AUSL Championship. One team goes home.

The matchup has everything.

The Bandits finished 15-10, built a nine-game winning streak that stands as the longest in AUSL history, and bring the most dominant pitcher in the league in Lexi Kilfoyl — who has a 0.60 ERA and has held opponents to a .163 average across 23-plus innings since returning in late June. They went on their historic run without a great deal of national attention, and they want it that way.

The Spark finished 14-11 after winning the final game of the regular season to eliminate the Portland Cascade on Tuesday morning. They clinched by one game. They needed everything to go exactly right. It did. And they're bringing Maya Brady into Thursday — the No. 1 overall pick in the AUSL Expansion Draft, hitting .415 with 8 home runs and 17 RBIs, the best active Triple Crown case in the league.

The Bandits are the higher seed. The Spark are the team that clawed into the postseason on the last possible day. The winner faces the Utah Talons — who are waiting at Davis Diamond on a bye — in the Championship Series beginning July 25.

Game 1 of the Championship airs on ABC. Professional softball has never been on network television before. This is the game that gets you there.

### STORY 3: Skylar Wallace — AUSL History

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

Four days ago, Skylar Wallace made AUSL history. This is the story that didn't get the attention it deserved.

On July 17, the Chicago Bandits outfielder became the first player in the history of the Athletes Unlimited Softball League to hit three home runs in a single game. The occasion: a 15-7 Bandits win over the Carolina Blaze at Smith Family Stadium in Durham. Wallace went 5-for-5 with 7 RBIs. She hit a 2-run home run in the second inning. A 3-run shot to left-center in the third. A solo home run to right in the sixth.

The Bandits were already in the midst of their AUSL-record nine-game win streak. Wallace made sure that particular game was not a close decision.

Over two games in that Blaze series, she went 9-for-10 with four home runs, nine RBIs, and nine runs scored. She had been hitting .500 earlier in the season. She is a Florida Gators alum. She is 28 years old and playing in the second season of the best professional softball league this country has ever built.

She hosted a Reddit AMA the following Monday. The play-in against OKC Spark is Thursday.

Nobody has been writing about the fact that the Bandits don't just have the best pitcher in the league heading into Thursday. They also have the hottest hitter in the league. Skylar Wallace just made history, and two days from now she gets to try to do it again.

### STORY 4: Maya Brady Triple Crown

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Maya Brady doesn't just need to win Thursday. She needs to survive Thursday.

The Oklahoma City Spark's No. 1 overall draft pick has had the best individual season in AUSL history. She enters the play-in game against the Chicago Bandits hitting .415 with 8 home runs and 17 RBIs — leading the league in all three Triple Crown categories now that Megan Grant's Portland Cascade was eliminated. MLB.com has already asked the question publicly: can Maya Brady take home the AUSL's first Triple Crown?

The answer depends entirely on what happens July 23.

Brady was the No. 1 pick in the AUSL Expansion Draft when the Spark were created. She played her college career at UCLA, where she was a teammate of Megan Grant — who broke the NCAA single-season home run record with 42 this spring. Both Bruins began 2026 as teammates in college and ended the regular season tied at 8 AUSL home runs each. Grant's season is now over. Brady keeps going.

An ESPN feature published on July 17 was headlined: "A natural-born leader: Spark star Maya Brady is all about winning." That's who is walking into Davis Diamond on Thursday.

If the Spark lose, Brady's stats are locked where they are. The Triple Crown bid ends. The milestone goes unclaimed in AUSL history. If the Spark win, she has at least two more games — and potentially three — to complete the most statistically dominant individual season the league has seen.

She has something to win Thursday. She has something to lose, too.

### STORY 5: AUSL Viewership and ABC Broadcast

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM ET

When the AUSL announced that Game 1 of the Championship Series would air on ABC on July 25, some people asked: why ABC? How does professional softball in its second season land a network television broadcast?

Here is the answer.

AUSL viewership is up 181 percent over the same point in the 2025 season. The OKC Spark vs. Portland Cascade game on June 21 drew 404,000 average viewers — a number that stands as the league record for a regular-season game. The 2026 season has been distributed across ESPN, ESPN2, ESPNU, ESPN Deportes, CBS Sports Network, and MLB Network. Each platform has contributed to an audience that, collectively, represents the single fastest year-over-year growth in professional softball history.

The ABC broadcast is not a promotional gesture. It is not charity from the network. It is the result of numbers that justify the placement.

Game 1 of the AUSL Championship Series at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas, on July 25 will be the first professional softball game ever broadcast on a U.S. broadcast television network. Not cable. Not streaming. Network.

Four years ago, the idea of a viable professional softball league in the United States was still largely a question. Two years ago, the AUSL played its first game. On Saturday, July 25, the league's championship begins on ABC.

The numbers made it happen. Now comes the moment.
