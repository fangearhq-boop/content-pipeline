The Best Individual Season in AUSL History Goes on the Line Thursday

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 21, 2026

The AUSL Play-in Game on Thursday is a team elimination game. One franchise survives. One goes home. That is the obvious story. The less obvious one is personal — and it belongs to Maya Brady.

The Oklahoma City Spark's No. 1 overall pick from the 2025 AUSL Expansion Draft enters the play-in game against the Chicago Bandits hitting .415 with 8 home runs and 17 RBIs. She leads the league in all three Triple Crown categories. MLB.com published a piece asking the direct question: can Maya Brady take home the AUSL's first Triple Crown? The answer depends entirely on what happens Thursday at Davis Diamond.

If the Spark lose, Brady's season ends. The stats are locked where they are. The Triple Crown bid goes unclaimed. The milestone — which would be the first in AUSL history — stays unfulfilled. All of that can happen with a single loss. In a single game.

The Numbers

Brady's statistical season deserves a full accounting before anyone writes a conclusion on it.

She is hitting .415. In a full college season, that would be outstanding. In professional softball, against the best pitchers in the country, it is exceptional. She has 8 home runs — tied for the league lead with Megan Grant, whose Portland Cascade was eliminated Tuesday, making Brady the sole active leader. She has 17 RBIs, also the league lead.

The Triple Crown — leading a league in batting average, home runs, and RBIs simultaneously — is one of the rarest individual achievements in softball. The AUSL is only two seasons old. No player has won one yet. Brady is the first player in the league's history to enter the postseason with a legitimate claim on all three categories at once.

She is the No. 1 pick from the 2025 AUSL Expansion Draft, chosen when the Spark were created to anchor the franchise. She played her college career at UCLA, where she was a teammate of Megan Grant — who broke the NCAA single-season home run record with 42 this spring. Both Bruins ended the regular season tied at 8 AUSL home runs each. Grant's season is over. Brady keeps going. For now.

The Opponent

Standing between Brady and more plate appearances Thursday is Lexi Kilfoyl, the most dominant pitcher in the AUSL right now. Since returning from the inactive list on June 24, Kilfoyl has a 0.60 ERA and has held opponents to a .163 batting average across 23-plus innings. She went 3-0 in her recent starts. She has allowed only two earned runs since coming back.

Those two players — Brady at the plate, Kilfoyl in the circle — represent the central tension of the play-in game. The Bandits need Kilfoyl to do what she has done for weeks. The Spark need Brady to do what she has done all season. Both cannot be true at once, at least not in the ways that matter most.

ESPN published a feature profile on Brady on July 17 under the headline "A natural-born leader: Spark star Maya Brady is all about winning." That is the player standing in the batter's box Thursday. She has something to win. She also has something to lose.

What It Means

If the Spark advance, Brady gets at least two more games — potentially three — to cement her stats and finish what she started. She would be the first player in AUSL history to win a Triple Crown, in only the league's second season. That is a legacy-building moment.

If the Spark lose, the numbers freeze. The milestone stays in the future — waiting for some other player in some other season to come along and claim it. Brady's 2026 campaign would already rank as the most statistically dominant individual season the AUSL has produced. It would just end without the title it was chasing.

Thursday is a team game. It is also a personal one. Brady doesn't just need to help the Spark win. She needs to survive Thursday to find out how her own story ends.

What's Next

Maya Brady and the Oklahoma City Spark face the Chicago Bandits in the AUSL Play-in Game on Thursday, July 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. The winner advances to face the Utah Talons in the best-of-three AUSL Championship Series beginning July 25. Game 1 of the Championship airs on ABC.
