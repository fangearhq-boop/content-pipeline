The Historic Performance Nobody Talked About Enough

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 21, 2026

Four days ago, Skylar Wallace rewrote the record book. The moment deserves its full accounting.

On July 17, the Chicago Bandits outfielder became the first player in the history of the Athletes Unlimited Softball League to hit three home runs in a single game. In a 15-7 Bandits win over the Carolina Blaze at Smith Family Stadium in Durham, North Carolina, Wallace went 5-for-5 with 7 RBIs and put herself alone in the AUSL record books. The play-in game against the Oklahoma City Spark is Thursday. But this is the story from four days before that.

The Game

It started with a 2-run home run to left field in the second inning. Then a 3-run shot to left-center in the third. Then a solo home run to right field in the sixth.

Three home runs. Three different locations. Three different innings. Five at-bats, five hits. Seven runs batted in. The Bandits won 15-7, and it wasn't particularly close after the third inning.

No player had ever done what Wallace did on July 17 in an AUSL game. The league has now existed for two full seasons and has developed some of the best players in professional softball. Wallace is the first to hit three home runs in a single contest. That is a meaningful milestone — not just a good night, but a historical mark that will stand until someone else does it.

The Streak Within the Streak

The Bandits were already in the midst of their AUSL-record nine-game winning streak when Wallace made history. The July 17 win over the Blaze wasn't a close decision — it was a statement. And the series made the statement twice.

Over the two games of that Blaze series, Wallace went 9-for-10 with 4 home runs, 9 RBIs, and 9 runs scored. Two games. Nine at-bats, nine hits — with one out in ten plate appearances that included 4 home runs. She scored 9 times and drove in 9. Those are not statistical flukes. Those are the numbers of a player operating at a level that is genuinely hard to sustain over even a short stretch.

She had been hitting .500 earlier in the season. She is a Florida Gators alum. She is 28 years old. She is playing in the second season of the best professional softball league this country has ever built.

The Context Thursday Adds

When the national attention arrives for Thursday's play-in game between the Bandits and OKC Spark, the focus will fall on Lexi Kilfoyl's arm and Maya Brady's bat. Both deserve the attention. Kilfoyl has a 0.60 ERA and has held opponents to a .163 average in 23-plus innings since late June. Brady leads the league in all three Triple Crown categories and is chasing the first Triple Crown in AUSL history.

What will be easy to miss: the Bandits also bring the player who just made history with the best individual offensive performance in AUSL history. Wallace hosted a Reddit AMA on Monday. She is the hottest hitter in the league. And on Thursday, she gets another chance to do something the AUSL has never seen.

The July 17 game didn't get enough attention when it happened. The play-in spotlight is arriving now. Wallace already earned it four days ago.

What's Next

Skylar Wallace and the Chicago Bandits face the Oklahoma City Spark in the AUSL Play-in Game on Thursday, July 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. The Bandits enter as the No. 2 seed on a nine-game win streak. The winner advances to the AUSL Championship Series beginning July 25.
