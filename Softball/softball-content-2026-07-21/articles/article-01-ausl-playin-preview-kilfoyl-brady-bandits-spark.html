Two Days. One Game. This Is What the AUSL Has Been Building To.

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 21, 2026

On Thursday night at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas, two of the three best stories in professional softball will collide in a single elimination game. One team advances to the AUSL Championship. One team goes home. And the one that advances will have earned it — because what the Chicago Bandits and Oklahoma City Spark bring into July 23 represents everything that makes this league worth watching.

The game is July 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN. The Bandits are the No. 2 seed. The Spark are the No. 3. Win-or-go-home. No margin. No second chances.

What the Bandits Bring

Chicago finished the 2026 regular season 15-10, but the record undersells them. The Bandits closed out the season on a nine-game winning streak — the longest in AUSL history — and did it against real competition, not the bottom of the bracket.

The engine of that streak is Lexi Kilfoyl. The former Oklahoma State star has been the most dominant pitcher in the league since her return from the temporary inactive list on June 24. In 23-plus innings since coming back, she has a 0.60 ERA. Opponents are hitting .163 against her — the lowest mark among qualified AUSL pitchers. She has allowed only two earned runs. She went 3-0 in her recent starts. When she is dealing, the Bandits are almost impossible to beat.

But that is not the complete picture. The Bandits don't just have the best arm in the league. They also have the hottest bat. Four days ago, outfielder Skylar Wallace became the first player in AUSL history to hit three home runs in a single game. She went 5-for-5 with 7 RBIs in a 15-7 Bandits win over the Carolina Blaze on July 17. Over two games in that Blaze series, she went 9-for-10 with 4 home runs, 9 RBIs, and 9 runs scored. Wallace walks into Thursday as arguably the hottest hitter in professional softball right now.

Chicago is not a one-player team. They are a team that has figured out how to win, and they have been winning for more than three weeks.

What the Spark Bring

Oklahoma City finished 14-11 after winning the final game of the regular season on Tuesday morning to eliminate the Portland Cascade on the last possible day. They clinched by one game. They needed everything to go right, and it did.

They bring Maya Brady.

The No. 1 overall pick in the 2025 AUSL Expansion Draft — a former UCLA Bruin — enters Thursday's play-in game hitting .415 with 8 home runs and 17 RBIs. She leads the league in all three Triple Crown categories now that Megan Grant's Cascade has been eliminated. MLB.com has already published the question publicly: can Brady take home the AUSL's first Triple Crown? The answer hinges entirely on what happens at Davis Diamond.

Brady doesn't just produce numbers. ESPN profiled her on July 17 under the headline "A natural-born leader: Spark star Maya Brady is all about winning." That is the player walking into the most important game of the AUSL's short history.

The Stakes

The winner of Thursday's game faces the Utah Talons, who are waiting on a bye at Davis Diamond. The Talons finished No. 1 and won the right to skip the play-in. They will be rested. The play-in winner will have just 48 hours to prepare.

The Championship Series begins July 25. Game 1 airs on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television. That historic broadcast is on the other side of Thursday. The team that wins this play-in is the team that gets to be part of that moment.

The Bandits bring a streak. The Spark bring Brady. Neither ingredient should be underestimated.

What's Next

The AUSL Play-in Game — No. 2 Chicago Bandits vs. No. 3 Oklahoma City Spark — is Thursday, July 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. The winner advances to face the Utah Talons in the best-of-three AUSL Championship Series beginning July 25. Game 1 of the Championship airs on ABC.
