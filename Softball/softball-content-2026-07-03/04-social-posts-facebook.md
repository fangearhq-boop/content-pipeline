### STORY 2: NiJaree Canady's AUSL Reality Check

#### Facebook Long-Form Post

The numbers are jarring when you line them up.

Stanford Year 1: 0.57 ERA. Stanford Year 2: 0.73 ERA. Texas Tech Year 1: 1.11 ERA. Texas Tech Year 2: 1.87 ERA. AUSL debut: 6.87 ERA.

NiJaree Canady — the most decorated pitcher in recent college softball history, the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL Draft — has allowed 17 earned runs and 7 home runs in her first professional outings with the Texas Volts. She is 0-3.

The Texas Volts aren't panicking. But they're responding.

They called Stanford. Specifically, they brought in Tori Nyberg — Canady's pitching coach during her two years with the Cardinal, the coach who recruited her out of high school, and the person who guided her through the 0.57 and 0.73 ERA seasons that made her a legend. Nyberg identified the issue: injuries at Texas Tech changed Canady's body positioning, and her mechanics haven't returned to where they were.

Volts GM Cat Osterman is publicly encouraging. Nyberg's message is that the original Canady — what he calls the "OG NiJa" — is still in there and is recoverable. The plan is mechanical, not mental.

The college-to-pro transition is the least-discussed challenge in softball's growing professional landscape. Even the best collegiate players carry mechanical patterns formed over years of heavy workloads, and the AUSL's pro hitters are different from anything a college pitcher has faced. Canady's struggle doesn't diminish what she accomplished. It names something real about the transition.

Canady will also be featured in the MLBx All-Star 3-on-3 at Citizens Bank Park on July 12, part of All-Star Weekend in Philadelphia. The sport's biggest name is still the sport's biggest draw. The ERA numbers will sort themselves out.

### STORY 4: PGF Nationals — 16 Days, Roster Freeze in 10

#### Facebook Long-Form Post

There are two countdowns happening right now in youth fastpitch.

The first one everyone talks about: 16 days to PGF Nationals. July 19 at Great Park Irvine. Week 1 features 18U and 16U. Week 2 features 14U. Every major coaching staff in the country will be there, watching every field simultaneously, evaluating the next two recruiting classes across 15-plus competition venues.

The second countdown is the one that has actual teeth: 10 days to the PGF roster freeze.

PGF rules require that national rosters match the team's insured roster as of July 13 at 11:59 PM — exactly 10 days from today. No player may be added after that date for any reason. Teams that have been finalizing their rosters through summer league tryouts, late-season additions, and injury replacements are out of time next Thursday. The roster you have on July 13 is the roster you bring.

For college coaches, that roster freeze matters because it tells them who to target in their evaluation runs. A player who was expected to play for a Top Gun 18 Gold team but missed the insurance deadline is playing somewhere else — or not playing. Coaches who've been tracking specific athletes need confirmed tournament entries to plan their field time.

September 1 opens the 2028 class contact window in 59 days. PGF Nationals is where the evaluations happen that make those September calls worth making. The countdown to July 19 is real. But the countdown to July 13 at 11:59 PM is the one that changes the logistics.

### STORY 5: September 1 — 59 Days Away

#### Facebook Long-Form Post

The contact window opens in 59 days.

September 1, 2026: the moment when college coaches can pick up the phone and call the families of 2028 prospects directly — the first direct contact allowed under NCAA recruiting rules for that class. Between now and then, coaches can watch these athletes compete. They can accept calls from families. They cannot reach out first.

That restriction creates a different kind of evaluation pressure. Everything happening at PGF Nationals on July 19 feeds into the September 1 target list. A coach who attends three days at Great Park Irvine and watches 40 athletes from the 2028 class isn't there recreationally. They're building the list of families they'll call on September 2.

The House Settlement changed the math. With rosters capped at 25 scholarship players, every freshman slot is a deliberate decision made against a finite budget. Programs can't carry 30 players hoping a few develop. Every spot has a cost, and every cost has to be justified against other options. That makes the evaluation period sharper and the September list more curated.

59 days sounds like a lot. For programs that know exactly what positions they need in the 2028 class and which players at PGF Nationals can fill those roles, it's not that long at all.
