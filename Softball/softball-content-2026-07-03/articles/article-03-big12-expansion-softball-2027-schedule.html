The Big 12 Is Bigger: How UCF, Houston and BYU Reshape the 2027 Softball Calendar

By Jordan Reeves July 3, 2026

Official as of July 1

The conference realignment that had been discussed, debated, and anticipated for the better part of a year became official on July 1. UCF, Houston, and BYU are now Big 12 members. Charlotte, FAU, North Texas, UAB, and UTSA joined the AAC. The paperwork is signed. The scheduling work is just beginning.

For softball programs, this matters in two specific ways: the 2027 conference schedule has to be rebuilt around three new opponents, and the recruiting geography of the Big 12 just expanded significantly into territory the conference did not previously own.

What UCF Brings

UCF plays in Orlando, which puts it in the heart of Florida's recruiting corridor. The state of Florida produces a disproportionate share of Division I infield talent every year, and the Big 12 has historically recruited there through existing relationships rather than through a geographic foothold. UCF's membership gives Big 12 programs a natural home-and-away matchup in Florida — a built-in reason to schedule a trip to Orlando, and a built-in reason for Florida high school athletes to attend Big 12 showcase games close to home.

UCF softball under coach Renee Luers-Gillispie has been a consistent mid-tier American Athletic Conference program. Their first Big 12 season in spring 2027 will be a significant step up in competition. For established Big 12 powers like Texas, Texas Tech, and Oklahoma, UCF on the schedule fills a nonconference slot without requiring travel outside the South.

What Houston and BYU Add

Houston's inclusion is the most immediately practical addition for the conference's power programs. The Cougars play in a city where Big 12 programs have already been recruiting heavily for years. Texas and Texas Tech both draw from Houston-area high schools annually. A Houston program in the Big 12 creates conference competition for that talent rather than a nonconference gap year where those athletes choose between different league affiliations.

BYU adds something different: geographic reach into Utah and the Mountain West corridor. The Cougars have a strong athletic brand and a recruiting network that extends through the intermountain states. For Big 12 programs looking to expand beyond the traditional Texas-Oklahoma-Florida-California quadrant, BYU's conference membership opens conversations that were previously harder to make in the Mountain West.

The Conference That Keeps Winning

Context matters here. The Big 12 already produced the last three national champions — Texas won back-to-back titles in 2025 and 2026, and Texas Tech was the runner-up in 2026. Oklahoma made the Super Regionals before their exit. The conference is not adding marginal programs to water down its profile. It is adding programs in markets that strengthen its overall footprint while the competitive ceiling remains as high as it has ever been.

The AAC's additions — Charlotte, FAU, North Texas, UAB, UTSA — give that league its own foothold in southern recruiting markets that previously sent a significant percentage of players to Power Four programs without a conference tie to that region. That reshapes the mid-major pipeline in ways that will take several recruiting cycles to fully measure.

What's Next

The first complete Big 12 season with all new members is spring 2027. Nonconference schedules for the 2026-27 academic year are being finalized now. Programs that want a Big 12 nonconference opponent in Florida, Texas, or the Mountain West have new options available that did not exist on July 1. The full scheduling picture will emerge when programs release their 2027 slates later this fall. What is clear now: the conference that already produced the last three champions just got bigger in the places that matter most.
