The July 4 Window Is Open: Tia Milloy Has Arrived at the Moment That Matters

By Sarah Mitchell July 3, 2026

Today Is the Day

There is a difference between the moment approaching and the moment being here. Twenty-four hours ago, the July 4 window was two days away. Today, it is open. Tia Milloy is 39 days into the transfer portal — she entered on May 25 after Oklahoma's Super Regional exit — and she has not committed. What changes today is not the number of programs pursuing her. It is the character of the pursuit.

July 4 weekend is the last stretch in the recruiting calendar when a head coach can give a single recruit their full and undivided attention. Florida State, Tennessee, and Texas Tech have been the consistent names in Milloy's recruitment since the portal opened. All three are still in contact. All three have the same Saturday morning available that they will not have in 16 days when PGF Nationals begins consuming their schedules and travel logistics at Great Park Irvine.

What She Brings

The package Milloy offers has not changed since May 25. She is a 5-foot-8 utility player from Woodinville, Washington, with two full years of remaining eligibility. Her batting line at Oklahoma was .353 average and .706 slugging percentage. She played 91 games across two seasons with the Sooners, demonstrating the kind of versatility that allows a program to deploy her at multiple positions depending on roster need.

Two years of eligibility is the key variable. Milloy is not a rental. A program that lands her gets a foundational piece for two full seasons — a 2027 contributor and a 2028 leader. That kind of flexibility is exactly what the House Settlement era rewards: players who can grow into roles rather than fill them for a single year.

For FSU, the case is the most direct. The Seminoles lost two key pieces to other Power Four programs in the same portal cycle and are building around Jazzy Francik's return and a pair of portal additions who bring power but require time to integrate. A utility player with Milloy's experience in big games fills a specific need without requiring anything to be rebuilt around her.

The Post-Holiday Calendar

The recruiting conversation does not end if Milloy doesn't commit this weekend. But it changes. Once PGF Nationals begins on July 19, the recruiting attention that has been focused primarily on Milloy expands to include every 2027 and 2028 prospect on the evaluation field. Phone calls get shorter. Return calls get delayed. The head coaches who have been engaged in sustained conversations with her camp will be physically present at a tournament that requires their full attention for eight consecutive days.

That does not make a post-Nationals commitment impossible. Players have committed in August, September, and into the fall. But the nature of the pursuit changes when evaluation season takes over. A coach who had a two-hour phone conversation with a recruit in late June is not replicating that in late July from a hotel lobby in Irvine.

Milloy has been deliberate throughout this process. She entered the portal with leverage — two years of eligibility, proven production — and she has held that leverage. The programs pursuing her have remained consistent rather than escalating. The dynamic favors a player who knows what she wants and is waiting for the right offer to arrive.

What's Next

The July 4 weekend is the most likely remaining window for a quiet announcement. The absence of a commitment today or Saturday shifts the timeline. If no announcement comes before July 13 — when PGF roster rosters freeze and coaching staffs begin final pre-tournament preparations — the story enters its third chapter: the post-Nationals stretch, where the 2028 recruiting class takes center stage and Milloy's decision becomes one of several major narratives competing for attention. She controls the pace. The calendar is narrowing either way.
