FSU Win Streak Reaches 23 Games After 10-0 Run-Rule of Notre Dame

By Jordan Reeves | April 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

Florida State softball kept the wins — and the dominance — coming on Friday night. The Seminoles run-ruled Notre Dame 10-0 in five innings, extending their active win streak to 23 consecutive games. It is the longest active streak in Division I softball and the program's longest since 2018.

FSU is now 34-4 overall and a perfect 11-0 in ACC play. Notre Dame dropped to 16-21 on the season and 5-9 in the conference.

Danley Dominant, Offense in High Gear

Ashtyn Danley was sharp in the circle, tossing four innings and allowing just one hit while striking out four batters. The effort continued what has been an outstanding stretch for FSU's pitching staff as a unit.

The offense supplied more than enough support. Isa Torres hammered her 8th home run of the season, continuing one of the more impressive offensive seasons for the senior utility player. Kennedy Harp added her 4th homer of the year, and Hayley Griggs drove a triple in the run-rule victory. It marked FSU's 10th shutout of the 2026 season — a number that speaks to the consistent quality of their pitching corps.

The Streak in Context

The 23-game win streak is the sixth-longest in FSU program history and represents the program's peak run since their last deep postseason push. Head coach Lonni Alameda reached her 1,000th career win earlier this season, and this streak has underscored just how well-constructed this team is from top to bottom of the roster.

Isa Torres carries one of the nation's most impressive batting averages, and the pitching depth gives FSU multiple options to deploy in any matchup. With a 23-game streak and an 11-0 conference record, the Seminoles are firmly in the national conversation.

What's Next

The series finale against Notre Dame is scheduled for Sunday, April 5 at 2:00 p.m. A win would extend FSU's streak to 24 games and complete the sweep. The Seminoles will then continue ACC play, with the national spotlight growing brighter with each game added to the streak.
