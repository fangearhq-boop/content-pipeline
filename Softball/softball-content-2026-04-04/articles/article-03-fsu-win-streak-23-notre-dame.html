FSU's Win Streak Reaches 23 — Seminoles Dominate Notre Dame, Eye Sweep Sunday

By Jordan Reeves | April 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

Florida State has not lost a game since February, and the Seminoles don't appear to be slowing down. FSU's win streak reached 23 games this week after a dominant 10-0 run-rule victory over Notre Dame in five innings on Friday, giving the Seminoles a commanding series lead heading into Sunday's Game 3 at 2 p.m. on ACC Network.

The streak is the longest active win streak in Division I softball and the program's longest in eight years. With wins of 7-2 (Game 1) and 10-0 (Game 2), FSU outscored Notre Dame 17-2 across the first two games of the series — a statement of dominance that goes well beyond the final scores.

Danley Delivers, Torres Continues Historic Season

Ashtyn Danley was sharp in the circle during Game 2, working four innings and allowing just one hit while striking out four. The defense behind her was equally clean, and FSU's offense needed no coaxing — the Seminoles put up 10 runs and ran the Irish off the field in five innings.

Isa Torres added to her already-remarkable season with her eighth home run, as she continues to post one of the most impressive offensive seasons in the ACC. Torres' batting average has hovered around .615 for much of the spring, giving FSU a middle-of-the-lineup force that opposing pitchers can't neutralize.

FSU's collective resume is equally impressive: 34-4 overall, 11-0 in ACC play, and 32-7 all-time against Notre Dame. The Irish entered the series at 16-21 in the program — a tough matchup for a team still trying to find its footing.

24 Straight on Sunday

The narrative entering Sunday is simple: can FSU complete the sweep and push the streak to 24? A win would match the 24-game win streak the program posted in 2018 — the last time FSU sustained a run of this length. With the streak at 23 and a series already clinched, Sunday's game carries the weight of history alongside the routine of conference play.

Game 3 is scheduled for 2 p.m. on ACC Network. FSU is 32-7 all-time against Notre Dame and has won the last 18 meetings in the series. The Seminoles have not faced a meaningful challenge to the streak in weeks, and that trend doesn't figure to end Sunday in Tallahassee.

What's Next

If FSU wins Sunday, the streak reaches 24 and the program enters the final weeks of the regular season as one of the most dangerous teams in the country — regardless of ranking. The NFCA Week 9 poll drops April 7, and the Seminoles' continued dominance should keep them firmly in the top 10. The next major test will come later in April as the conference schedule intensifies heading toward the postseason.
