Kendall Wells Hits HR No. 29 — One Swing Away from NCAA Freshman Record

By Emily Rawlings | April 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

Kendall Wells is one home run away from the record books.

The Oklahoma freshman launched her 29th homer of the season on Friday night — a solo shot to right field in the third inning — as the No. 3 Sooners run-ruled Kentucky 9-1 in five innings at Love's Field. Wells, who already holds the SEC single-season freshman home run record, now stands just one swing away from tying the all-time NCAA freshman record of 30.

With Game 3 of the series scheduled for Saturday morning at 11 a.m. CT, the entire softball world will be watching every at-bat she takes.

A Record-Setting Season

The marks Wells is chasing carry real historical weight. The NCAA all-time freshman home run record of 30 is shared by three players: Lauren Chamberlain of Oklahoma (2012), Jocelyn Alo of Oklahoma (2019), and Jordan Majam of Utah (2023). For Wells to tie it — and potentially break it — before the end of her first college season would be extraordinary.

Wells already made history last week when she crushed her 27th home run of the season against Wichita State, breaking the SEC single-season freshman home run record. She leads the nation in home runs at 29, and her pace this season is one of the most remarkable individual offensive stretches in college softball in recent memory.

Oklahoma's Big Picture

While Wells draws the individual headlines, Oklahoma is rolling as a team. The Sooners won Game 1 over Kentucky 10-2 on Thursday and added a convincing 9-1 run-rule victory in Game 2 on Friday. Lexi McDaniel drove in a game-high five RBIs in Game 2, underscoring that this lineup has plenty of weapons beyond its freshman slugger.

Oklahoma is 37-3 overall and 10-1 in the SEC, sitting just a half-game behind Texas at the top of the conference standings. A sweep of Kentucky today keeps the pressure on No. 1 Texas, which dropped Game 2 to Alabama on Friday night.

What's Next

Game 3 of OU vs. Kentucky is set for Saturday, April 4 at 11:00 a.m. CT at Love's Field in Norman, streaming on ESPN+. If Wells doesn't tie the record today, she'll have plenty of opportunities over the remainder of the regular season — including the Red River Rivalry against No. 1 Texas on April 10-12. Every game from here out is a potential record-breaking moment.
