Red River Rivalry Preview: Texas Hosts Oklahoma in Austin, April 10-12

By Emily Rawlings | April 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

The most anticipated series of the college softball regular season is now one week away. No. 1 Texas hosts No. 3 Oklahoma in Austin on April 10-12 for the Red River Rivalry, and the stakes could not be higher. SEC title implications, top-five national rankings, and one of the most compelling individual storylines of the season — Kendall Wells' pursuit of the NCAA freshman home run record — all converge in what figures to be a three-game series watched by the entire softball world.

Texas enters the series at 10-2 in SEC play following the Tuscaloosa series. Oklahoma comes in at 10-1. The margin is thin, and the series outcome could tip the balance in the conference standings at exactly the point in the season when every result carries maximum weight.

Wells vs. Texas Pitching

The individual storyline that elevates this series above any other in the conference schedule is Kendall Wells' home run chase. Wells entered this week with 29 home runs, one away from tying the NCAA Division I freshman record of 30 shared by Kelly Majam (Hawaii, 2010), Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma, 2012), and Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma, 2018).

If Wells doesn't tie the record before April 10 — and she certainly has that opportunity this weekend — Austin becomes the stage. Every at-bat in a packed UFCU Disch-Falk Field crowd will carry the electricity of potential history. Texas pitching will try to prevent it. The entire sport will be watching to see who wins that chess match.

Oklahoma as a team has also been devastating at the plate, led by Wells' 29 home runs and 60 RBI on the season. The Sooners enter the week at 37-3 overall — one of the best records in the country.

Texas After the Alabama Test

The Longhorns just went through one of the toughest series of their season in Tuscaloosa, facing No. 4 Alabama in a three-game set. Texas entered the series as the No. 1 team in the country and remains there in the NFCA standings heading into Week 9. The depth of Texas pitching — anchored by Teagan Kavan, who threw a complete game in Game 1 of the Alabama series with nine strikeouts — will face a rigorous test against one of the most powerful offensive lineups in the country.

Head coach Mike White's Longhorns have shown they can bounce back from adversity. Whatever the outcome of the Tuscaloosa series, playing host to Oklahoma in Austin is exactly the kind of series that defines Texas' path to an SEC title and a national championship.

What's Next

April 10 in Austin. The Red River Rivalry is here. With SEC title implications, the Wells record watch, and two programs at the absolute top of the college softball landscape, this three-game series may be the best regular-season storyline of the 2026 season. NFCA Week 9 poll releases April 7 and will set the table for where both programs stand entering the series. Don't miss it.
