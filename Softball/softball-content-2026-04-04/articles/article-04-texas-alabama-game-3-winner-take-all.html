Texas vs. Alabama Game 3: Winner-Take-All at 12:30 PM CT on SEC Network

By Sarah Mitchell | April 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

The stage is set for a winner-take-all series finale, and fans should make sure they know about a significant schedule change before the first pitch is thrown.

Due to inclement weather in the Tuscaloosa area, the Game 3 matchup between No. 1 Texas and No. 4 Alabama at Rhoads Stadium has been moved up from its originally scheduled 5:00 p.m. CT start time on ESPN. The game will now begin at 12:30 p.m. CT and air on the SEC Network. Beth Mowins handles play-by-play duties with Hall of Famer Michele Smith providing analysis.

How We Got Here

Texas took Game 1 on Thursday by a convincing 9-1 margin, looking like the dominant No. 1 team the preseason polls expected. But Alabama came out on Friday night with a completely different level of energy and effort, defeating Texas 11-4 to even the series at one game apiece.

Game 3 now carries stakes that extend well beyond the weekend. Texas is 10-2 in the SEC and sits at the top of the conference standings alongside Oklahoma. A series loss would represent a significant road setback for a team with national title aspirations. Alabama, at 8-3 in the SEC, needs a series win to maintain pace in the conference race and build momentum heading into the final month of the regular season.

What to Watch

Texas will lean on its pitching staff, which has been one of the most consistent units in the nation this season. The Longhorns' ability to limit Alabama's powerful lineup — which erupted for 11 runs on Friday — will be the decisive factor.

For Alabama, the emotion of winning Game 2 at home and the energy of the Rhoads Stadium crowd will be a genuine advantage. Coach Patrick Murphy, in his 30th season leading the program, has never been afraid of a big moment, and his team proved that on Friday night.

What's Next

Game 3: Saturday, April 4 at 12:30 p.m. CT on SEC Network from Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa, Alabama. After this series concludes, Texas turns its attention to the Red River Rivalry against No. 3 Oklahoma on April 10-12 in Austin — a series that could define the SEC regular season race.
