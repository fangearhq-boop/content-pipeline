One Swing Away: Kendall Wells at 29 HRs and Chasing the NCAA Freshman Record

By Emily Rawlings | April 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

There are numbers that stop you mid-scroll and demand your attention. Kendall Wells' current home run total — 29 — is one of them. The Oklahoma freshman entered this past week one home run away from tying the NCAA Division I all-time freshman home run record of 30, a mark that has stood in the record books since 2018 and was set by three of the most celebrated names in Oklahoma softball history.

The record of 30 freshman home runs in a single season is shared by Kelly Majam (Hawaii, 2010), Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma, 2012), and Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma, 2018). Chamberlain and Alo are Sooners legends. The idea that Wells — herself a Sooner freshman — could join them as the sole holder of the record has the entire softball world watching every at-bat.

A Historic Trail of Milestones

Wells' season has been a steady progression of record-breaking moments. On March 31, she launched her 27th home run against Wichita State to break the SEC single-season freshman home run record, a mark previously held by Bri Ellis of Arkansas, Bailey Hemphill of Alabama, and Mia Davidson of Mississippi State. Then came home run No. 28 in Game 1 of the Oklahoma-Kentucky series, and No. 29 in Game 2 — a solo blast to right field that had Love's Field buzzing about what comes next.

The raw numbers underscore how dominant her season has been. Wells leads the nation in home runs, carries 60 RBI, and is batting .383. For a freshman, in the Southeastern Conference, on one of the country's most scrutinized rosters, those are staggering totals.

The Red River Stage

The next opportunity for No. 30 comes in the most dramatic setting possible: the Red River Rivalry. Texas hosts Oklahoma in Austin on April 10-12, with SEC title implications layered on top of the personal record chase. If Wells ties the NCAA freshman record on Texas soil, in a series with national championship consequences, it would be one of the most memorable moments in recent softball history.

Oklahoma enters the week at 37-3 overall and 10-1 in SEC play, locked in a fierce race with No. 1 Texas (10-2 SEC). Every game matters, and the Sooners have arguably the most exciting player in the country entering the biggest series of the regular season.

What's Next

Oklahoma's next opponent is No. 1 Texas in Austin, starting April 10. All eyes will be on Wells every time she steps to the plate. A tie of the NCAA freshman record would be a historic milestone. If she surpasses it and hits a 31st — well, that would be territory no freshman has ever reached. The Red River Rivalry just became the most compelling series of the regular season.
