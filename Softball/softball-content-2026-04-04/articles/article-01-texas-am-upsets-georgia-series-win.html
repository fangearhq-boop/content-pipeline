No. 15 Texas A&M Stuns No. 11 Georgia to Claim SEC Series

By Sarah Mitchell | April 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

College softball's weekend delivered a genuine shock on Friday at Davis Diamond, and it came courtesy of the Texas A&M Aggies. No. 15 Texas A&M claimed the series over No. 11 Georgia, winning two of three games including a decisive 2-1 rubber match in the back half of a Friday doubleheader. It was the kind of result that shakes standings, rattles poll voters, and reminds everyone why you play every game.

The series opened Thursday with a 3-2 A&M victory. Sydney Lessentine was sharp in the circle, punching out six batters. Offensively, Tallen Edwards scored an early run, Mya Perez drove another in, and Micaela Wark delivered a key RBI to push the lead. Georgia answered in the sixth inning on a two-run home run from Marisa Miller, but it wasn't enough. Maddie Sauni's RBI single sealed the opener for the Aggies.

Georgia Fights Back, A&M Fights Harder

No. 11 Georgia wasn't going quietly. In the doubleheader's first game on Friday, the Bulldogs exploded for a 10-6 win to even the series at one apiece. It looked like Georgia had regained control of the narrative — a top-10 team defending home territory and forcing a winner-take-all finale.

But Texas A&M had one more answer. The rubber match — the second game of the doubleheader — went the Aggies' way, 2-1. A tight, tense affair ended with A&M collecting a series win that few outside College Station predicted when the weekend began.

The series win is significant beyond the box score. Georgia entered the weekend ranked No. 11 nationally. Dropping a home series to a No. 15 opponent creates pressure in the NFCA poll, which releases its Week 9 rankings on April 7. For A&M, it's an injection of momentum at exactly the right time in conference play.

What's Next

The NFCA's Week 9 poll drops Tuesday, April 7, and Georgia's loss could trigger a notable ranking drop. For Texas A&M, the series win is a statement in a crowded SEC field. Both programs will be jockeying for position as the conference schedule enters its final weeks. The Red River Rivalry between Texas and Oklahoma on April 10-12 will dominate the spotlight next, but the Aggies gave fans plenty to talk about heading into another loaded week of college softball.
