OU vs. Kentucky Game 3: Record Watch Begins for Wells at Love's Field

By Emily Rawlings | April 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

Oklahoma leads the Kentucky series 2-0 and has been dominant in both games. But Saturday's Game 3 at Love's Field carries a subplot that has every softball fan in the country paying attention: Kendall Wells is one home run away from making NCAA history.

Game 3 is scheduled for 11:00 a.m. CT on Saturday at Love's Field in Norman, streaming on ESPN+. It is the final game of a series that OU has dominated, but Wells' pursuit of the all-time NCAA freshman home run record makes this far more than a routine series clincher.

The Record Chase

Wells hit her 29th home run of the season on Friday — a solo shot to right field in the third inning of OU's 9-1 run-rule win in Game 2. The NCAA all-time freshman home run record is 30, shared by Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma, 2012), Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma, 2019), and Jordan Majam (Utah, 2023). One more homer puts Wells in that exclusive company.

The fact that Wells could tie this record in the same ballpark where two of the three record-holders played — Love's Field in Norman — adds a layer of significance that is hard to overstate. Chamberlain and Alo are two of the most celebrated hitters in Oklahoma and college softball history.

The Team Behind the Star

Oklahoma's success this season goes beyond any single player. Lexi McDaniel drove in five runs in Game 2, showing that the Sooners have legitimate depth at the plate. The team is 37-3 overall and 10-1 in the SEC, tied with Texas for the conference lead after the Longhorns dropped Game 2 to Alabama on Friday night.

A sweep of Kentucky today keeps Oklahoma right alongside Texas at the top of the standings ahead of the Red River Rivalry on April 10-12 in Austin.

What's Next

Game 3 of OU vs. Kentucky is Saturday, April 4 at 11:00 a.m. CT at Love's Field, Norman, on ESPN+. If Wells doesn't tie the record today, she has plenty of regular season games remaining to get there. The Red River Rivalry — No. 3 Oklahoma at No. 1 Texas — starts April 10 and could be the moment the nation sees HR No. 30.
