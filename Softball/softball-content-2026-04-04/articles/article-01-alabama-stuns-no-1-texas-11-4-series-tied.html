Alabama Stuns No. 1 Texas 11-4, Series Tied Heading Into Game 3

By Sarah Mitchell | April 4, 2026 | I Love Softball

What looked like a one-sided series after Game 1 turned into something far more compelling on Friday night in Tuscaloosa. Alabama softball dismantled No. 1 Texas 11-4 at Rhoads Stadium, erasing a nine-run deficit from the previous night and forcing a winner-take-all Game 3 on Saturday afternoon.

The Crimson Tide had dropped Game 1 on Thursday by a score of 9-1, looking outclassed by the nation's top-ranked team. But Friday told an entirely different story. Alabama's offense came alive and its pitching kept Texas from finding any rhythm, producing one of the most dramatic single-game turnarounds in this season's SEC schedule.

A Statement Win for Alabama

Alabama entered Friday's game at 33-3 on the year and 7-3 in the SEC. Texas was 32-2 heading into the series, riding the momentum of one of the best starts in program history. The 11-4 final sent a message across the conference: this Alabama team will not be intimidated by anyone's ranking or reputation.

The Crimson Tide improved to 34-4 overall and 8-3 in SEC play with the win. Texas dropped to 32-3 overall and 10-2 in conference games. The series now heads to a decisive Game 3 — and the implications go well beyond this weekend's standings column.

Game 3 Details: Time Change, New Channel

Fans planning to watch the series finale on ESPN at 5 p.m. CT will need to adjust their plans. Due to inclement weather in the Tuscaloosa area, the game has been moved up to 12:30 p.m. CT and will air on the SEC Network rather than ESPN. Beth Mowins handles play-by-play with Michele Smith providing analysis.

Texas will look to its deep pitching staff to close out the series on the road. Alabama will draw energy from a Rhoads Stadium crowd that watched their team turn around the series in 24 hours. The loser faces a reckoning in the SEC standings heading into a brutal stretch of the schedule.

SEC Standings and What's at Stake

Texas entered this series at 10-1 in the SEC after Friday and sits tied atop the conference standings with Oklahoma at 10-1. Alabama is at 8-3 and needs wins to stay relevant in the SEC race. A series win Saturday would boost Alabama to 9-3 and make a real statement about their credentials as a postseason threat.

For Texas, a loss in Game 3 means dropping the series on the road — a genuine blemish on the nation's No. 1 résumé. The Longhorns have not had many setbacks this season, and how they respond in a winner-take-all environment will say a great deal about their championship potential.

What's Next

Game 3 of the Texas-Alabama series is set for Saturday, April 4 at 12:30 p.m. CT on the SEC Network at Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa. Following this series, Texas prepares for one of the most anticipated matchups of the regular season — the Red River Rivalry against No. 3 Oklahoma on April 10-12 in Austin. Saturday's result will shape the narrative heading into that blockbuster showdown.
