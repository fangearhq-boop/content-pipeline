AUSL Final 9 — Three Games Today, No. 1 Seed Race Enters Critical Week

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 11, 2026

The AUSL regular season has nine games left. Three of them are today.

Saturday's schedule is the most action-packed single day of the AUSL's final stretch — three games spread across three time zones, three venues, and six hours. Every result shifts the standings. Every team in contention for the No. 1 seed knows exactly what today means.

Today's Schedule

  • 2:00 PM ET — Carolina Blaze vs. Texas Volts (Dell Diamond, Round Rock TX)
  • 5:00 PM ET — Chicago Bandits vs. Portland Cascade (Hillsboro Ballpark, Hillsboro OR)
  • 8:00 PM ET — Utah Talons vs. Oklahoma City Spark (Tom Heath Field, Oklahoma City OK)

All three games are available on MLB.TV.

What's at Stake

The Utah Talons currently hold the No. 1 position in the AUSL standings. The prize for finishing first: a direct bye to the Championship Series, set for July 25-27 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. Every other team must win the July 23 play-in game before they can advance to the championship round.

That bye is worth at least one extra game of rest and preparation for a team that has been playing through a compressed regular season schedule. For the Talons, protecting the No. 1 seed is now the primary objective heading into the final nine games. A win tonight against the Spark — particularly with Lonni Alameda in the broadcast booth on MLB.TV — would be a statement in the sport's most-watched week of the year.

The Earlier Games Matter Too

Before the featured Talons-Spark matchup, the afternoon games give the standings their first nudge of the day. The Blaze-Volts game at Dell Diamond in Round Rock puts two Texas-region rivals on the same field in a late-season game with real implications. The Bandits-Cascade matchup in Hillsboro pits the second-place Chicago Bandits against the Portland Cascade — making it the most directly competitive game on the schedule for teams chasing the Talons.

The Bandits need wins to stay in range. The Cascade — playing at home at Hillsboro Ballpark — would gain crucial ground with a win. These are not throwaway games.

Nine Games Left

The AUSL regular season ends July 20. From today's three games through the final day, every matchup carries championship implications. Teams that go 3-0 over a given weekend change the conversation. Teams that drop two games in a row can fall out of contention.

Today's three-game slate gives the standings three separate opportunities to shift. By tonight's final out, the picture heading into the final week will be clearer than it was this morning.

What's Next

Today's games: 2:00 PM, 5:00 PM, and 8:00 PM ET on MLB.TV. The regular season finale is July 20. The No. 1 seed goes directly to the Championship Series (July 25-27, Davis Diamond, College Station TX). The remaining seeds compete in a play-in game on July 23.
