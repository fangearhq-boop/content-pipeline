MLBx All-Star 3-On-3 Is Tomorrow — Full Team Rosters Confirmed

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 11, 2026

The full rosters are locked. The teams are set. And the inaugural MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 Presented by Ito En is less than 24 hours away.

Tomorrow, Sunday July 12, Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia hosts a co-ed home run competition that brings together four AUSL softball stars, four Philadelphia Phillies legends, and four NFL players — including two current Philadelphia Eagles. First pitch is 3:00 PM ET on Peacock.

With the complete team assignments now confirmed via MLB.com, here is what tomorrow's competition looks like on paper.

The Four Teams

Team Howard is built around Hall of Famer Ryan Howard, the 2006 NL MVP and cornerstone of the Phillies' dynasty years. His AUSL partner is Alyssa Brito of the Carolina Blaze — a three-time WCWS champion at Oklahoma and a member of the U.S. Women's National Team. They'll be joined by DeVonta Smith, the Eagles wide receiver and 2021 Biletnikoff Award winner.

Team Rollins pairs Jimmy Rollins — the 2007 NL MVP and 2008 World Series champion — with Tiare Jennings of the Texas Volts, a four-time WCWS champion at Oklahoma. Their NFL star is Terrell Owens, the Hall of Fame wide receiver and one of the most electrifying players in NFL history.

Team Victorino features Shane Victorino, the "Flyin' Hawaiian" and two-time World Series champion, alongside Rachel Garcia of the Texas Volts. Garcia brings one of the most decorated resumes in amateur softball history: an Olympic silver medal (Tokyo 2020) and a 2019 WCWS title at UCLA. Their NFL partner is Jordan Mailata, the Eagles offensive tackle who has become one of the most popular players in Philadelphia over the past four seasons.

Team Jones is headlined by Andruw Jones, who was inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame just this week in Cooperstown. Jones teams up with NiJaree Canady of the Texas Volts — the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL Draft and a two-time NFCA Pitcher of the Year. Team Jones is the only team competing without a current NFL star, making Canady's bat the centerpiece of their lineup.

How the Format Works

MLBx runs two 30-minute semifinal games followed by a championship game. Each player gets a two-minute batting window per game. Home run equals one point. Catching a ball in the outfield also equals one point. There's a center-field target bonus and a Hot Streak power-up — a button each team can press that grants five additional swings at double points.

The format rewards athleticism across disciplines. A 450-foot blast from Ryan Howard and a line-drive homer from Alyssa Brito are worth exactly the same. An outfield catch by DeVonta Smith counts the same as one from Tiare Jennings. The competition is genuinely co-ed by design, not by token inclusion.

The Broader Picture

The Phillies legends at the center of this competition — Howard, Rollins, Victorino, and Jones — represent an unusual cross-section of Philadelphia baseball history. Howard, Rollins, and Victorino were all part of the 2008 World Series championship team. Jones, the newest Hall of Famer, was their opponent that year as a member of the Tampa Bay Rays — but he is a Philadelphia icon now in a very different way.

For the AUSL, this event puts Brito, Jennings, Garcia, and Canady on a national stage at Citizens Bank Park during the sport's most-watched week of the year. All four players are members of the Texas Volts or Carolina Blaze, the two teams that finished at the top of the AUSL's early-season standings.

What's Next

MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 tips off tomorrow at 3:00 PM ET at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. Coverage is on Peacock. After the event, the AUSL resumes regular-season play on Monday with the final nine games of the 2026 regular season. The No. 1 seed earns a direct bye to the Championship Series, set for July 25-27 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas.
