Megan Grant ESPYS — 4 Days Left to Vote for Best Record-Breaking Performance

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 11, 2026

Four days. Four votes left. Four chances to send Megan Grant to the ESPYS stage on Wednesday night.

Grant — the Portland Cascade outfielder and former UCLA slugger — is nominated for Best Record-Breaking Performance at the 34th ESPYs. Voting is open once per day at ESPN.com through July 15. The ESPYs air Wednesday, July 15, at 8:00 PM ET on ABC and the ESPN app, hosted by Marcello Hernández.

What She Did

In 2026, Megan Grant hit 42 home runs at UCLA — breaking Laura Espinoza's 1995 Arizona record of 37 home runs, which had stood for 31 years. She did not just break the record. She broke it by five. She also set the UCLA career home run record with 91, passing Stacey Nuveman in the process.

Grant was selected No. 4 overall in the 2026 AUSL College Draft and is currently playing for the Portland Cascade.

The Competition

Grant is up against three male athletes from other sports:

  • Myles Garrett, defensive end for the Los Angeles Rams, who set an NFL milestone this past season
  • Johannes Høsflot Klæbo, Norwegian cross-country skier and multiple World Championship and Olympic gold medalist
  • Sabastian Sawe, Kenyan long-distance runner and world record holder

All three are legitimate nominees. None of them broke a 31-year-old record with a stat line that's objectively more than five units above the previous mark. Grant's achievement is singular — a generational offensive season at the collegiate level, recognized at the sport's highest national awards platform.

Why Every Vote Counts Now

Voting closes July 15. That means today through Wednesday, you have one vote per day. If you haven't voted since July 4 when the daily countdown started, you have missed seven votes. The remaining four — July 12, 13, 14, and 15 — are not replaceable. Each one is final.

Softball does not have a long history of winning at the ESPYs. Grant's nomination in a category that includes a football player, a skier, and a world-class distance runner is exactly the kind of mainstream crossover moment the sport needs. A win would put softball on the broadcast Thursday night on ABC with 20-plus million viewers watching.

What's Next

Vote at ESPN.com — once per day through July 15. The 34th ESPYs air Wednesday, July 15, at 8:00 PM ET on ABC and the ESPN app. Grant continues her AUSL season with the Portland Cascade, who return to regular-season play after the All-Star break.
