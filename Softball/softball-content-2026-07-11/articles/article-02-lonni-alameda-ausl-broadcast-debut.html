Lonni Alameda Makes Her AUSL Broadcast Debut Tonight — Gasso Returns Sunday

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 11, 2026

Florida State head coach Lonni Alameda makes her AUSL broadcast debut tonight, calling the Utah Talons vs. Oklahoma City Spark at Tom Heath Field in Oklahoma City. First pitch is 8:00 PM ET on MLB.TV.

The appearance is the second consecutive historic night at the AUSL microphone. Patty Gasso — the Oklahoma head coach with eight national championships — debuted in the AUSL booth on July 9 on ESPNU. Alameda follows tonight. Gasso returns Sunday, July 12, on MLB Network for Game 2 of the Talons-Spark series.

Three nights. Two of the most accomplished coaches in college softball history. The most-watched stretch of the AUSL regular season.

Alameda's Credentials

Alameda has built Florida State into one of the premier programs in college softball. Since taking over as head coach, she has guided the Seminoles to multiple College World Series appearances and consistently ranked among the sport's elite programs. Her reputation as a tactician and her ability to develop elite pitchers have made her one of the most respected voices in the game.

As a broadcast analyst, she brings the kind of in-game tactical knowledge that television audiences rarely get — the kind that comes from coaching at the highest level for more than a decade. Gasso's debut on July 9 drew strong reviews for exactly that reason. Expect the same from Alameda tonight.

The Broadcast Arc

The AUSL's decision to bring in Gasso and Alameda during All-Star Week is a deliberate identity statement. The league is in its first full season, and the broadcast team it puts on the air during its highest-viewership stretch tells fans something about who it is and where it's going.

Gasso and Alameda are not novelties. They are not cameo appearances. They are two coaches who have won at the highest level in college softball, calling games during the most critical stretch of the AUSL's inaugural season. The message is that the AUSL takes its product seriously enough to put the sport's most credible voices in the booth when the national spotlight is brightest.

Tonight's Full AUSL Slate

Tonight is the capstone of a full three-game AUSL Saturday. Two earlier games set the table:

  • 2:00 PM ET — Carolina Blaze vs. Texas Volts (Dell Diamond, Round Rock TX)
  • 5:00 PM ET — Chicago Bandits vs. Portland Cascade (Hillsboro Ballpark, Hillsboro OR)
  • 8:00 PM ET — Utah Talons vs. Oklahoma City Spark (Tom Heath Field, OKC) — Alameda in booth on MLB.TV

The Talons currently hold the No. 1 seed in the AUSL standings with nine games remaining in the regular season. A win tonight helps them protect their position heading into the final week. The Spark, sitting in the lower half of the standings, needs a strong finish to secure a postseason spot before the July 20 regular-season finale.

What's Next

Alameda calls tonight's Talons-Spark game at 8:00 PM ET on MLB.TV. Tomorrow, Sunday July 12, Gasso returns for Game 2 of the same series on MLB Network. The AUSL regular season ends July 20, with the No. 1 seed earning a direct bye to the Championship Series (July 25-27, Davis Diamond, College Station TX).
