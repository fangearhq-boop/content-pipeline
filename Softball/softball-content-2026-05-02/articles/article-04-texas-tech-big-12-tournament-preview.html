Texas Tech 47-4 Enters Big 12 Tournament as No. 1 Seed — NiJaree Canady Leads the Way

By Sarah Mitchell | May 2, 2026 | I Love Softball

Texas Tech softball has already done the hard part. The Red Raiders clinched their second straight Big 12 regular season title — finishing 47-4 overall — and now arrive as the No. 1 seed in the Big 12 Tournament at Devon Park in Oklahoma City (May 7-9) with NiJaree Canady ready to close the deal.

Canady, who crossed the 1,000 career strikeout threshold earlier this season, has been the cornerstone of one of the most dominant pitching performances in Big 12 softball history. With multiple no-hitters in 2026, a sub-1.50 ERA, and the kind of raw stuff that has baffled lineups from coast to coast, she has been the driving force behind TTech's back-to-back championship run.

Back-to-Back Big 12 Titles

Winning back-to-back Big 12 regular season titles is no small feat in a conference that features competitive programs from across the country. Texas Tech did it with balance — powerful offense behind their top hitters plus Canady's dominance in the circle — and consistency that never wavered even through a challenging Big 12 schedule.

The Red Raiders finished 47-4, a record that speaks for itself. They have been ranked No. 2 in the NFCA coaches poll for much of the second half of the season, sitting just behind Oklahoma. The national seed conversation absolutely includes Texas Tech.

Devon Park Awaits

The Big 12 Tournament at Devon Park in Oklahoma City runs May 7-9. As the No. 1 seed, Texas Tech will face the No. 8 seed in Thursday's opening round at 1:30 PM CT. Devon Park is also the home of the WCWS — meaning Texas Tech will be competing in a venue they know well and hope to return to in late May.

Winning the Big 12 Tournament would strengthen TTech's case for a top national seed and would give Canady one more chance to put on a showcase performance heading into Selection Weekend.

What's Next

Texas Tech's final regular season weekend wraps up before the Big 12 Tournament begins Thursday, May 7. The Selection Show is May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. If the Red Raiders earn a top-4 national seed, they could host Regionals in Lubbock — another massive home-field advantage for a program that has made the most of its rise to the top of college softball.

NiJaree Canady and the Red Raiders have unfinished business. Devon Park awaits.
