Nebraska Faces Penn State Doubleheader — Big Ten Title and National Seed on the Line

By Emily Rawlings | May 2, 2026 | I Love Softball

Nebraska's historic 2026 season has one final obstacle before the postseason: a Saturday doubleheader at Penn State, forced by Friday's weather postponement. The stakes are enormous — a Big Ten regular season title and a potential national seed to host an NCAA Regional at Bowlin Stadium hang in the balance.

The Huskers (40-6, 20-1 Big Ten) enter the day riding a 13-game win streak and sitting third in the NFCA coaches poll. Game 1 starts at 10 AM CT, Game 2 at 1:30 PM CT — both on Big Ten Network. Win two of the three scheduled games in the series, and Nebraska clinches the Big Ten title outright regardless of what any other team does.

A Season for the Record Books

Nebraska's 2026 campaign has been one of the most dominant in program history. The Huskers went 20-1 in Big Ten play — a single conference loss all season — and their 13-game win streak coming in is a testament to the consistency of this roster. The program set an attendance record at Bowlin Stadium earlier this season, and the fanbase has rallied around this squad all year.

Pitching and defense have been the hallmarks of the Husker season. The staff has posted shutouts and quality starts repeatedly, and the lineup has produced runs at will against Big Ten competition. Head coach Rhonda Revelle has guided Nebraska to one of its best seasons in recent memory.

Penn State's Senior Day Fight

Don't overlook the Nittany Lions. Penn State (32-17, 11-10 Big Ten) enters Saturday having won four straight, and this is their Senior Day — emotional moments that can elevate a team. State College in early May can be a challenging environment, and Penn State will be playing for pride and for their seniors on the final home weekend of the regular season.

Nebraska has seen this before. The Huskers know that road doubleheaders on Senior Days can produce surprises. Their championship experience should serve them well.

What's Next

Win or lose today's series, Nebraska heads directly into the Big Ten Tournament (May 6-9, College Park, MD) — likely as the No. 1 seed. From there, the national picture comes into focus at the Selection Show on May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2.

A top-4 national seed — and the right to host a Regional at Bowlin Stadium — is the goal. Today's two games are the Huskers' last chance to make their case. Nebraska is 40-6. The window to the WCWS in Devon Park starts with today's doubleheader.
