A&M Stuns No. 1 Oklahoma 8-5 — Games 2 and 3 Decide Series and SEC Title Today

By Sarah Mitchell | May 2, 2026 | I Love Softball

No. 12 Texas A&M pulled off one of the most dramatic comebacks of the 2026 college softball season Friday, rallying from a 5-3 deficit to defeat No. 1 Oklahoma 8-5 in the weather-delayed series opener — and the drama is far from over.

Game 1 had been suspended Thursday night at Davis Diamond in College Station with Oklahoma leading 5-3. When play resumed Friday afternoon, the Aggies went to work. With the bases loaded and two outs in the bottom of the sixth, Micaela Wark delivered a single up the middle that cleared the bags and gave A&M an 8-5 advantage they would not relinquish. The crowd at Davis Diamond erupted — it was A&M's first home win over a top-ranked opponent since 2016.

High Stakes on Saturday

Friday's weather had cascading consequences. Game 2 of the series was moved to Saturday at 12:15 PM CT, and Game 3 remains at its original slot of 4 PM CT on ESPN2. That means Oklahoma and Texas A&M will play two games in College Station on Saturday — with the SEC regular season title implications riding on every pitch.

Oklahoma entered the series at 18-3 in SEC play. After Friday's loss, the Sooners are 18-4. Florida sits at approximately 16-5 and Alabama at 15-5 entering their final weekend games. OU controls its own destiny: sweep today and take the series. But a single A&M win hands the Aggies the series in this first-ever SEC meeting between the programs.

Wells Still One Away

Amid the series drama, every eye remains on Kendall Wells. The Sooners freshman enters Saturday at 36 home runs — one from tying Lauren Espinoza's 31-year-old all-time NCAA single-season record of 37, set at the University of Arizona in 1995.

Wells has already broken the NCAA freshman record, the SEC single-season record, and the Oklahoma program record this season. Every at-bat in College Station carries the weight of history. She has hit home runs against some of the toughest pitching staffs in the country, and A&M's staff will be no exception.

What's Next

Today's doubleheader in College Station is as high-stakes as the regular season gets. If Oklahoma sweeps, they take the series and head into the SEC Tournament — beginning May 5 in Lexington, KY — as the clear conference leader. A loss would complicate the seeding picture heading into the postseason.

Watch Game 2 on SEC Network at 12:15 PM CT and Game 3 on ESPN2 at 4 PM CT. The race to the WCWS — Devon Park, OKC, May 28 — runs through every pitch thrown in College Station today.
