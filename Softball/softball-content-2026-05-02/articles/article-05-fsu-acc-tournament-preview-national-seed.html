No. 10 FSU Closes Regular Season — ACC Tournament and National Seed Push Dead Ahead

By Emily Rawlings | May 2, 2026 | I Love Softball

Florida State's 2026 season has been a study in excellence. The No. 10 Seminoles (43-8, 18-3 ACC) close the regular season this weekend at Boston College with the ACC Tournament — and a potential national seed — squarely in their sights.

Boston College (13-35, 3-18 ACC) is a manageable final opponent, but FSU's focus has already shifted to what comes next: the ACC Tournament at Palmer Park in Charlottesville, Virginia, running May 6-9, and then the NCAA Tournament Selection Show on May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2.

A Season of Consistency

At 43-8, Florida State has been one of the most reliable teams in the country from wire to wire. The Seminoles posted an 18-3 conference record in a competitive ACC that includes Duke (38-13, 20-4 ACC) and Louisville. FSU's pitching depth has been a strength all season, and the offense has been consistently productive.

Isa Torres has been the offensive anchor, contributing a historically dominant hitting performance that placed her among the national leaders in batting average throughout the season. The combination of Torres in the lineup and FSU's veteran pitchers makes the Seminoles a tough out at any time of year.

National Seed Conversation

At 43 wins and ranked No. 10, Florida State is squarely in the national seed debate. The top 4 teams in the national rankings earn the right to host Regionals and Super Regionals — a massive advantage in the NCAA Tournament. For FSU, the ACC Tournament will be a critical showcase.

Head coach Lonni Alameda — who crossed the 1,000 career wins milestone earlier this season — has built FSU into a program that is taken seriously at the national level every single year. A deep run in Charlottesville next week could be the final argument needed for a hosting spot.

What's Next

Florida State finishes the regular season at Boston College this weekend, then heads to Charlottesville for the ACC Tournament (May 6-9 at Palmer Park). Win the ACC Tournament, and FSU's case for a national seed becomes nearly bulletproof.

The road to Devon Park — and the WCWS on May 28 — runs through this postseason. The Seminoles have the talent, the experience, and the coaching to make noise. Game time at Palmer Park starts next Wednesday.
