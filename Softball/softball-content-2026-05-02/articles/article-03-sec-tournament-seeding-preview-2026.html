SEC Tournament Seedings Take Shape — Double Byes at Stake as Regular Season Ends

By Jordan Reeves | May 2, 2026 | I Love Softball

In three days, the 2026 SEC Softball Tournament opens at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky — and the final standings are still being decided in real time. With Oklahoma, Florida, and Alabama all playing critical series this weekend, Saturday's games will shape the bracket and the national seed picture heading into the postseason.

The tournament runs May 5-9 and features all 15 SEC teams. The top four seeds earn coveted double byes — entering directly into the quarterfinals and skipping the opening rounds. Those four spots also tend to correlate with the most favorable national seedings in the NCAA Tournament Selection Show on May 10.

Where the Standings Stand

Oklahoma leads the conference at 18-4 in SEC play after Friday's loss to Texas A&M. Even so, the Sooners remain well ahead of the field. Florida is at approximately 16-5 and Alabama at 15-5 heading into their final weekend. Texas A&M and Texas are both around 15-6.

OU remains the clear favorite for the No. 1 seed. Florida and Alabama — both of which have jockeyed for the second and third spots all season — will battle for double-bye positioning through Saturday. Alabama's late-season series loss to Tennessee shifted some standings calculus, giving Florida an opportunity to finish second.

National Seeds Are Part of the Picture

Conference records and tournament seedings matter not just for the bracket in Lexington — they matter for the NCAA Tournament as well. The top 4 teams in the national seed rankings will host Regionals and Super Regionals at their home parks, giving them a massive advantage in the path to the WCWS.

Oklahoma and Texas Tech are widely projected as top-4 national seeds. Nebraska (No. 3 NFCA) and Florida are likely the next two contenders. Alabama, Florida State, and a few others are on the bubble. How teams perform in their conference tournaments over the next week will be the defining factor for Selection Show weekend.

What's Next

The SEC Tournament bracket will be finalized after Sunday's games. Head to Lexington — or tune into SEC Network — starting Tuesday, May 5. The final is Saturday, May 9. From there, everything points to the Selection Show on May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 and ultimately to the WCWS in Devon Park, Oklahoma City, beginning May 28.

The regular season ends this weekend. The postseason — and the road to the WCWS — begins next Tuesday.
