Stetson Stuns No. 9 Florida State 8-3: The Biggest Upset of the 2026 NCAA Softball Regionals

By Sarah Mitchell | May 16, 2026

It was supposed to be a formality. Florida State — the No. 9 national seed, the 2026 ACC Tournament champion, the program that had hosted 12 consecutive NCAA Regionals at Joanne Graf Field — was opening at home against Stetson, an ASUN Conference team with a 36-20 record. The script was written. The result was supposed to be predictable.

Stetson never read the script.

The Hatters defeated Florida State 8-3 on Friday evening, delivering the biggest first-round upset of the 2026 NCAA Softball Championship and sending a stunned Tallahassee Regional crowd home with a reminder that nothing in double-elimination tournament play is guaranteed — not seeding, not history, not twelve years of home dominance.

Arnold Was Dominant From the Start

Stetson pitcher Hayley Arnold (11-3) was the story of the night. Arnold controlled the game from beginning to end, working efficiently through an FSU lineup that had been one of the most dangerous in the ACC all season. The Hatters collected 12 hits, including two home runs, to back Arnold's effort. Florida State managed three runs on seven hits but could never string together the sustained offensive pressure they needed to flip the script.

Eight runs. Twelve hits. Two home runs. That's not a lucky upset win. That's a team that came prepared, executed its game plan, and outperformed a superior seed in every meaningful statistical category.

What This Means for Florida State

Florida State entered the 2026 NCAA Tournament with a 49-8 record and a freshly minted ACC Tournament championship in their trophy case. They had gone 21-3 in conference play. The Seminoles were viewed as a potential Super Regional participant — a program with the experience, depth, and home-field advantage to navigate a regional comfortably.

Now they face an elimination game Saturday.

After UCF defeated Jacksonville State 2-1 in eight innings in the regional's other opening game, Florida State is scheduled to face Jacksonville State in Game 4 at 2:30 PM on Saturday. A loss sends the Seminoles home. A win keeps their season alive — but means they'd need to win twice on Sunday to reach the Super Regionals, including a rematch with Stetson.

This has happened before in tournament softball. Programs with far greater resumes than Stetson have crumbled against unexpected pressure. But the FSU coaching staff and players know better than anyone that the loser's bracket is survivable. Programs that have hosted 12 straight regionals have seen chaos before.

The Bigger Picture

The Stetson upset is part of a broader narrative that makes the 2026 NCAA Softball Tournament one of the most compelling in recent memory. Across all 16 regional sites, the first day of play sent dozens of favorites scrambling. South Florida knocked off No. 7 Washington in Fayetteville. Wisconsin upset Baylor in Austin. The seeding bracket doesn't determine who goes home — the diamond does.

Stetson came to Joanne Graf Field as a decided underdog. They leave the opener as the regional's biggest story and the winners' bracket representative, waiting to face UCF on Saturday while Florida State fights to stay alive.

What's Next

Saturday's schedule in Tallahassee sets up two dramatic games. UCF meets Stetson in the winners' bracket at noon, with a spot in the regional final on the line. Florida State faces Jacksonville State at 2:30 PM in a must-win elimination game. If FSU survives, they'll face the Game 3 loser — potentially setting up a rematch with Stetson before Sunday's regional final. One loss ended Florida State's comfort zone. Now the Seminoles must show what they're made of in the loser's bracket — where programs earn respect the hard way.
