Alabama Saves Its Ace: Freshman Pallozzi Shines in 8-0 Run-Rule Win Over USC Upstate

By Sarah Mitchell | May 16, 2026

The No. 1 national seed didn't need their best pitcher to get the job done. That's how good Alabama is.

Freshman Kaitlyn Pallozzi started the Tuscaloosa Regional opener against USC Upstate on Friday and went the distance, throwing a complete-game shutout in Alabama's 8-0 run-rule win in five innings. She allowed four hits. She was efficient, clean, and composed in her first NCAA Tournament start.

Meanwhile, SEC Pitcher of the Year Jocelyn Briski sat and waited. Saturday is coming, and Alabama's coaching staff wanted their ace fresh for the tougher tests ahead.

What Pallozzi's Start Reveals About Alabama

The decision to start a freshman over a proven SEC Pitcher of the Year isn't just about saving Briski's arm. It's a signal. It tells the rest of the regional field that Alabama has the depth to deploy a freshman in a high-stakes game and not blink. It means their No. 2 pitcher can handle tournament pressure. And it means the Crimson Tide enter Saturday's games with their best option fully rested.

That's the advantage of coaching. Alabama (49-7) didn't survive 21 consecutive NCAA Regionals as a national seed by accident. They manage their pitching staff with purpose, and the Pallozzi decision is a masterclass in tournament resource management.

Belmont Complicates the Picture

The other Friday opener in the Tuscaloosa Regional produced a development that changes Saturday's narrative. Belmont defeated No. 8 Southeastern Louisiana 2-0, earning a spot in the winners' bracket opposite Alabama.

Belmont entered the tournament at 40-11 and is no one's idea of a soft opponent. They executed against a seeded team on Friday. Now they have to face Alabama — and Briski — in a winners' bracket game at 2 PM on Saturday.

For Alabama, the Belmont result is actually better than a more straightforward path. Playing a battle-tested opponent that has already proven it can beat a seeded team will prepare the Crimson Tide for whatever awaits in the Super Regionals. Alabama's 74-12 all-time regional record doesn't get built by coasting through weak opposition.

The Long View: Alabama's Regional Track Record

Alabama has an all-time NCAA Regional record of 74-12. They've hosted 21 consecutive regionals as a national seed. Their 1.60 ERA and 22 shutouts this season reflect the same program that has dominated tournament softball for over two decades. The Pallozzi performance on Friday is the latest entry in a long ledger of Alabama depth.

Whether Pallozzi, Briski, or Vic Moten is on the mound, Alabama finds ways to win. That's the challenge every opponent in the Tuscaloosa Regional faces this weekend.

What's Next

Alabama faces Belmont (40-11) in the Tuscaloosa Regional winners' bracket game Saturday at 2 PM, with Jocelyn Briski expected to start. A win puts the Crimson Tide in command of the regional with a path to two Sunday advancement opportunities. Simultaneously, USC Upstate faces Southeastern Louisiana in the loser's bracket at 4:30 PM Saturday. Alabama's schedule is set. Their best pitcher is rested. The No. 1 national seed is doing exactly what No. 1 seeds are supposed to do — advancing without drama while preserving resources for the battles that matter.
