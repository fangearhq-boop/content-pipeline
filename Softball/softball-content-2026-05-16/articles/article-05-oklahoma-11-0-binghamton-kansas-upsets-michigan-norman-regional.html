Oklahoma Blasts Binghamton 11-0; Kansas Stuns Michigan 1-0 in Eight — Norman Regional Sets Up Must-Watch Saturday

By Emily Rawlings | May 16, 2026

The Norman Regional delivered two games and two completely different stories on Friday — and both of them are worth your attention heading into Saturday.

Oklahoma did what No. 3 overall seeds are supposed to do. They crushed Binghamton 11-0 in five innings, hitting four home runs along the way. Patty Gasso's Sooners looked exactly as dangerous as their 48-8 record, .389 team batting average, and 174 home runs this season suggested they would.

Then came Kansas.

The Jayhawks played eight innings of some of the most compelling pitcher's-duel softball of the regional weekend, defeating Michigan 1-0 on a walk-off hit by Ava Wallace. Pitcher Lila Partridge threw all eight innings without giving up a run. This was not how anyone expected the Norman Regional to set up.

Oklahoma Looks Like a Contender

Four home runs in five innings against Binghamton tells you everything you need to know about Oklahoma's offensive identity. The Sooners don't manufacture runs — they impose their will. With the nation's best team batting average and more home runs than any program in the country, Oklahoma's approach at the plate is straightforward: attack, and don't stop attacking.

For Patty Gasso, the Binghamton game was exactly what a tournament opener should look like — efficient, decisive, and a demonstration of strength that puts every other regional team on notice. Oklahoma has won four straight national championships (2021-2024) and is in pursuit of a ninth overall. They arrived in Norman looking every bit like a program with championship experience on every roster spot.

Kansas Was the Story of the Night

Ava Wallace was everywhere on Friday. She made a leaping catch at the wall in the field that could have shifted momentum for Michigan. Then she hit the walk-off RBI to win the game in the eighth inning. A defensive gem and the game-winning hit — Wallace announced herself on the biggest stage she's played on.

Behind her, Lila Partridge threw eight scoreless innings in a 1-0 game. Eight innings. Zero runs. That's not a fluke performance — that's a pitcher locked in and executing at the highest level of her season. Michigan's Gabby Ellis was nearly as brilliant, going 7.1 scoreless innings before the Jayhawks finally broke through.

The final: Kansas 1, Michigan 0 in eight innings. It was the best-pitched game of the regional first round.

Saturday: Oklahoma vs Kansas at 3 PM

Nobody had this on their radar as a marquee Norman Regional matchup. Oklahoma vs Kansas at 3 PM Saturday is now must-see softball. The powerhouse offense that runs-ruled Binghamton vs. the pitcher who threw eight scoreless innings and the walk-off hero. The No. 3 seed with 174 home runs against the team that just proved it can shut anyone down.

Kansas earned the right to face Oklahoma by winning the hardest kind of game — the one that requires one run to score over eight tense innings. They're not afraid of the moment. Oklahoma has seen pressure before. Saturday afternoon in Norman is appointment viewing.

What's Next

Oklahoma and Kansas meet in the Norman Regional winners' bracket Saturday at 3 PM. The winner moves a step closer to a Super Regional bid; the loser drops to the elimination bracket for Sunday. In the other Saturday game, Michigan faces Binghamton in an elimination contest at 5:30 PM. Both games carry real stakes. The Norman Regional had two compelling openers — Saturday promises more of the same. Watch Partridge vs. Oklahoma's lineup. Watch Gasso's Sooners face their first real test of the weekend. This is tournament softball at its best.
