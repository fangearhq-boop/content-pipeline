Nebraska Rallies Past South Dakota 4-1, Ending Cinderella's First NCAA Tournament Run

By Emily Rawlings | May 16, 2026

South Dakota had never played an NCAA Tournament game before Friday night. Founded in 1978, the Coyotes program had spent nearly five decades in search of a moment like this one — standing on the Bowlin Stadium diamond in Lincoln, Nebraska, playing against a No. 4 national seed in front of thousands of fans, with the history of their program on every pitch.

They made the most of it, at least for a while.

South Dakota led Nebraska 1-0 going into the fifth inning. The Cinderella story had real legs. Then the Huskers rallied, scored four unanswered runs, and won 4-1 to advance in the Lincoln Regional winners' bracket. But the result doesn't capture what South Dakota accomplished this weekend — or how hard Nebraska had to fight for this win.

The Comeback That Defined the Game

Nebraska trailed 1-0 entering the fifth inning, a deficit that made the home crowd uncomfortable. The Huskers were No. 4 nationally, hosting a Lincoln Regional for the first time since 2013, and they were being tested by a team that had barely reached .500 in regular season play.

Then Jordy Frahm doubled. Hannah Coor tripled to score her and tie the game. Hannah Camenzind followed with an RBI double to give Nebraska the lead. In the sixth inning, Kacie Hoffmann added two more with a double, and the game was effectively over.

Freshman pitcher Alexis Jensen started and earned the win, improving her record to 23-2 on the season. She allowed just two hits in 5.0 innings, keeping the Coyotes from doing more damage with their bats. Jensen handled the moment like a veteran — composed, efficient, and decisive.

What South Dakota Accomplished

The Coyotes arrived in Lincoln with a 20-34-1 regular season record and a Summit League Tournament championship that nobody outside of Vermillion expected. They won four games in four days to earn an automatic bid, with pitcher Madison Evans earning All-Tournament honors as they peaked at the perfect moment.

Seven Nebraska natives were on South Dakota's roster, returning to their home state for the biggest game in program history. The story wrote itself. And when South Dakota led a No. 4 national seed in the fifth inning of their first-ever NCAA Tournament game, the fairy tale felt almost plausible.

It wasn't meant to last. But the Coyotes gave a crowd that came expecting a comfortable Nebraska win something to think about. The 20-34-1 team played the defending Big Ten champions close for four and a half innings. In their first-ever NCAA Tournament appearance. On the road. That is not nothing.

What's Next

Nebraska advances in the Lincoln Regional winners' bracket and will await the survivor of the South Dakota vs. Louisville or Grand Canyon elimination game. The Huskers showed they can come from behind when they need to — a useful trait for a team building momentum toward Super Regional aspirations. South Dakota's remarkable first-ever NCAA Tournament journey ends at 0-2, but the Coyotes program goes home with something it never had before: the experience of competing at the highest level of college softball. That foundation matters when you build toward a return.
