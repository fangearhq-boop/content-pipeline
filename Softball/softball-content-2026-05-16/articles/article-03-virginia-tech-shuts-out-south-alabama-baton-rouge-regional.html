Aldridge's Grand Slam Sends Virginia Tech Past South Alabama 6-0; Hokies Face LSU Saturday

By Jordan Reeves | May 16, 2026

Virginia Tech didn't come to Baton Rouge to survive. They came to make a statement.

Kylie Aldridge's two-out grand slam in the third inning broke a scoreless game and set the tone for everything that followed. The Hokies shut out South Alabama 6-0 in the Baton Rouge Regional opener, delivering the kind of dominant performance that the softball world expected from a team ranked among the consensus top upset picks of the entire regional field.

Virginia Tech is now 47-10. They're in the winners' bracket. And Saturday afternoon, they get exactly the matchup the sport spent a week anticipating: No. 2 seed Virginia Tech vs. No. 1 host LSU at 1 PM.

Aldridge's Grand Slam Was the Turning Point

The Baton Rouge Regional opener was scoreless through two and a half innings before Aldridge stepped in with two outs. The grand slam changed everything. Four runs scored on a single swing. South Alabama never recovered from the deficit, and Virginia Tech's pitching staff did the rest.

The final margin — 6-0 — is the kind of result that sends a message across the bracket. Virginia Tech wasn't tentative in an unfamiliar environment. They imposed their game immediately.

LSU Equally Sharp: 8-0 Over Akron

Host LSU was not about to be upstaged on its own field. The Tigers dismissed Akron 8-0 in six innings in the regional's second opener, with pitcher Jayden Heavener striking out 11 batters in a complete-game shutout. Sierra Daniel went 3-for-4. Kylee Edwards drove in three runs. LSU looked every bit as sharp as a regional host is expected to look at home.

The stage is set for Saturday's winners' bracket game. Two teams that just shut out their opponents. One home crowd. A matchup with national implications.

The Context: Why VT Is Here

Virginia Tech (47-10, 18-6 ACC) could not host a regional in 2026. Despite their elite record, a graduation-rate eligibility requirement blocked the Hokies from being placed as a national seed at home. They were assigned as the No. 2 seed in a pod hosted by LSU — placed in an opponent's backyard for a tournament they arguably could have hosted themselves.

The softball community noticed. Analysts named Virginia Tech the top upset pick of the regional weekend — not because they doubted the Hokies, but because the anger and competitive fuel that comes from a perceived slight can elevate already-good teams to great ones.

Virginia Tech has now been to seven consecutive NCAA Regionals. They've built a program that belongs in this conversation. And now they get their shot at proving it against the host team in front of the host team's fans.

What's Next

Saturday's 1 PM matchup between Virginia Tech and LSU is the most-anticipated game of the Baton Rouge Regional — and arguably one of the marquee games across all 16 sites. A VT win would put the Hokies in command of the regional and on a direct path to the Super Regionals. An LSU win sends VT to a loser's bracket where a single loss ends their season. Both teams are playing some of the best softball of their seasons. One of the best regional matchups of the 2026 tournament tips off Saturday at 1 PM in Baton Rouge.
