2026 D1 Player of the Year Isa Torres Enters Transfer Portal — Texas Ties Fuel Recruiting Race

By Jordan Reeves June 9, 2026

The Portal Just Changed

The 2026 softball transfer portal was already stacked. Then Isa Torres walked in.

Florida State's junior shortstop — the 2026 D1 Softball Player of the Year and ACC Player of the Year — announced she is entering the NCAA transfer portal just one day after the 15-day window opened. The announcement sent immediate shockwaves through a sport that had already been tracking the portal's first wave closely. Torres is not just the best player in this portal cycle. She may be the best player in the sport.

A Season for the Record Books

What Torres accomplished in 2026 is almost difficult to put in context. She hit .530 for the season — a new Florida State program record. She posted a .978 slugging percentage, also a new FSU record. Her .591 on-base percentage broke the program mark. Her 78 runs scored set another program standard.

All four of those numbers represent the best single-season marks in FSU softball history. In a single year, Torres didn't just have a great season — she redefined what the program's ceiling looks like for a position player. She earned two consecutive First Team All-American selections, becoming one of the most decorated players in the Seminoles' program history.

She is also a two-time All-American entering the final year of her collegiate eligibility. Whoever lands her gets one shot — one season — to build around the best offensive player in the sport.

The Texas Connection

Early reporting has linked Torres to Texas and Texas A&M as potential transfer destinations. The connection is not arbitrary. Torres is a Georgetown, Texas native — a hometown pull that few programs outside the Lone Star State can compete with.

For a Texas program that just won back-to-back national championships and is returning Teagan Kavan and Katie Stewart as seniors for 2027, the calculus is straightforward: adding the D1 Player of the Year to a roster that already has the two-time WCWS MOP and one of the sport's most dangerous hitters would make the Longhorns an overwhelming favorite for a third consecutive title.

Texas A&M represents an alternative path — a program hunting for an identity-defining addition and with the resources and facilities to make a compelling pitch. The Aggies have the financial infrastructure and the SEC recruiting pull to compete for a player like Torres. A landing at College Station would immediately reframe the conversation about where A&M fits in the 2027 power structure.

What Programs Are Buying

Landing Isa Torres is a one-year investment. That is not a liability — it is a specific kind of opportunity. Programs that need a veteran, proven offensive force for a single title push are the natural landing spots. Torres comes fully formed. There is no development phase, no adjustment period. She steps in, hits .530, and competes for an NCAA championship.

The bidding — informal, as recruiting conversations always are in the portal window — begins immediately. In a 15-day window, the programs that move with urgency and clarity are the ones that win. The window closes June 22. Torres may be committed well before then.

What's Next

The softball world will watch Torres's destination closely over the coming days. Programs with roster flexibility, championship infrastructure, and the ability to offer a compelling vision for her senior season are in the conversation. Follow the On3 and D1Softball transfer trackers for the latest on Torres's recruitment — her commitment will be one of the most consequential announcements of this portal cycle.
