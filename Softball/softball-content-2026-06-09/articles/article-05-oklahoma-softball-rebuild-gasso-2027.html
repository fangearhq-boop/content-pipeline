Oklahoma's Reset: Patty Gasso Has Rebuilt Before — The Question Is How Fast

By Sarah Mitchell June 9, 2026

The Year Oklahoma Wasn't There

Devon Park hosted the 2026 Women's College World Series without Oklahoma in the field for the first time since 2015. The Sooners — eight national championships, four consecutive Big 12 titles from 2021 to 2024, a program that has defined elite college softball for more than two decades — were eliminated in the Super Regionals by Mississippi State and did not make the trip to Oklahoma City.

For a program accustomed to competing for national titles, missing the WCWS is not a small thing. For Patty Gasso, entering this offseason is the most significant rebuild she has managed in years. And she is managing it in public, against a backdrop of programs that are actively building to take Oklahoma's place in the sport's hierarchy.

What Was Lost

The most prominent departure is Kendall Wells. The Oklahoma slugger hit 39 home runs in 2026 — one home run short of matching Megan Grant's all-time NCAA single-season record. Wells was the offensive centerpiece of the Sooners' lineup and one of the most feared power hitters in the sport. Her college career is over.

Beyond Wells, Oklahoma graduated multiple key contributors from a program that was still competing at a high level despite the early tournament exit. Rebuilding around losses of this magnitude requires a clear-eyed assessment of what the roster looks like going forward and what needs to be addressed through recruiting and the portal.

Why Oklahoma Always Comes Back

The short answer is Patty Gasso.

Gasso has built eight national championships at Oklahoma across a career that has tracked every evolution of the sport. Before the transfer portal existed, she recruited players who became legends. Since the portal emerged, she has used it as effectively as any coach in the country. The program's infrastructure — facilities, fanbase, recruiting network, NIL capabilities — remains elite regardless of any single season's result.

Oklahoma's most recent WCWS absence before 2026 was in 2015. They returned the very next season. From 2016 through 2025, the Sooners were a fixture in Oklahoma City. That is not a coincidence — it is program culture built over decades, and it does not dissolve in a single offseason.

The Portal Window as a Reset Tool

This 15-day transfer portal window is Oklahoma's first major move in its reload. Gasso's track record in the portal era is strong — she has identified and signed transfers who made immediate contributions and helped keep OU competitive at the highest level. With Wells and others departed, the Sooners need both pitching and offensive reinforcement.

The portal class of 2026-27 is deep enough to provide options at every position. Whether Oklahoma can attract marquee names in a competitive market — against programs like Texas and Alabama who are also active — will say something about where the program's recruiting power stands in the post-Wells era.
