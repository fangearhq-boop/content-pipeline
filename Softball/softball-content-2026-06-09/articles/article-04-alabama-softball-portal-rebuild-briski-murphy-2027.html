Alabama's Portal Mission: Patrick Murphy Has 11 Days to Build an Offense Around Briski

By Jordan Reeves June 9, 2026

The Losses Are Real

Alabama went 49-7 in 2026. They were the No. 1 overall seed in the NCAA Tournament. They posted a 1.60 team ERA and threw 22 shutouts — one of the great pitching-staff seasons in the modern history of the sport. And they were eliminated in the WCWS semifinals by an 11-seed Texas Tech team built almost entirely of transfers.

Seven seniors from that historic season are gone: Abby Duchscherer, Marlie Giles, Alea Johnson, Kinley Pate, Larissa Preuitt, Alexis Pupillo, and Kristen White. These were not roster depth pieces. These were contributors to a team that was, by the numbers, one of the best Alabama has ever fielded. The offense that scored enough runs to support a 22-shutout staff is largely departed.

The Anchor Holds

The reason Alabama is not a rebuilding program right now — despite significant losses — is one name: Jocelyn Briski.

The SEC Pitcher of the Year is returning for 2027. Briski was the engine of that historic pitching staff, and her return transforms the nature of Alabama's offseason challenge. It is not a question of whether the Crimson Tide can compete — it is a question of who they add around the best returning pitcher in the SEC.

For Patrick Murphy, that framing changes everything about his portal strategy. He is not shopping to plug a hole; he is shopping to build a lineup around an elite arm. The pitch to offensive transfers is compelling: come to Tuscaloosa, play in front of one of the best crowds in college softball, and swing behind a pitcher who gives you a chance to win every single game she starts.

What Alabama Needs

With seven offensive contributors departed, Murphy needs to address multiple positions simultaneously. The most urgent needs are in the middle of the lineup — the run-producing bats that generated enough offense to support Briski's dominance. Alabama does not need a single splash addition; they need a class of portal pieces that collectively restores the lineup depth a championship-level program requires.

The portal class of 2026-27 is deep enough to provide it. Several of the available players — including some of the headliners who have not yet committed — fit the profile Murphy is looking for. A program that can make a convincing championship pitch, backed by Briski's presence on the mound, has a genuine argument to make to every offensive-minded transfer in this cycle.

Murphy's Moment

This portal window is, in many ways, the defining stretch of Patrick Murphy's tenure as Alabama's head coach. He inherited a program with elite infrastructure and a historic coaching legacy. He carried forward a staff that posted a 1.60 ERA. Now he has to prove he can recruit — portal-recruit — at the level the program's standard demands.

Eleven days remain. The Crimson Tide's 2027 season will be shaped largely by what happens in them.
