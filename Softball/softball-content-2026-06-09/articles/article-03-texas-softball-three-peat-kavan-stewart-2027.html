Teagan Kavan and Katie Stewart Are Coming Back: Texas Just Made Its Three-Peat Case

By Emily Rawlings June 9, 2026

They're Coming Back

The question after Texas won its second consecutive national championship was always going to be: who stays? Two championships in a row attract attention from every level of the sport — professional leagues, other programs, career opportunities. The Longhorns needed their best players to make a choice.

Teagan Kavan made hers. Katie Stewart made hers. And so did Kayden Henry. All three are returning for their senior seasons in 2027, giving Texas the foundation of a three-peat bid that goes beyond ambition and into genuine structural advantage.

Kavan: The Best Pitcher in the Country, for One More Year

Teagan Kavan is the first pitcher in WCWS history to win the Most Outstanding Player award twice. In 2026, she went 4-1 with a 1.47 ERA across 33.1 innings in the tournament, struck out 30 batters, and closed out the championship series with a 5-strikeout performance in the final game. Before the tournament, she had already posted one of the most efficient seasons in recent program history.

Kavan's return as a senior means Texas goes into 2027 with the tournament's most accomplished active pitcher at the top of its staff. No rotation adjustment. No experience gap. The same arm that delivered back-to-back titles takes the mound again with a senior's poise and a champion's track record.

Stewart: Power and Presence in the Middle of the Lineup

Katie Stewart hit four home runs in the 2026 WCWS. She set a new Texas single-season program record with 30 home runs during the regular season. She is one of the most dangerous hitters in the sport at any level, and she is not done.

Stewart's return means Texas's offense does not need to be rebuilt — it needs to be supplemented. The Longhorns can approach the transfer portal this cycle from a position of strength: they are adding to a lineup anchored by a 30-HR hitter, not patching holes. That changes the nature of what they're looking for and who they can attract.

Chasing History

Three consecutive national championships in college softball is rare. Oklahoma won three in a row from 2021 to 2023. UCLA won three straight from 1988 to 1990. Arizona won back-to-back titles in 2006 and 2007 but not a third. The programs that have sustained at that level share a common thread: exceptional pitching, elite offensive production, and coaching staff continuity.

Texas checks every box. And with the portal window open for 11 more days, the Longhorns have the opportunity to add the depth and experience that separates national contenders from national champions in April and May. They are not shopping for a foundation. They already have one.

What's Next

Watch for Texas to be aggressive in the portal window over the next 11 days. Their recruiting pitch is straightforward: come play for the back-to-back champions, pitch alongside the best pitcher in the country, swing behind a program-record home run hitter, and compete for a title that no program has ever won three times in a row in the modern WCWS era.
