Softball Portal Day 2: Polar and Rijo Enter as the Class of 2026-27 Keeps Growing

By Sarah Mitchell June 9, 2026

The Wave Keeps Coming

One day in and the 2026-27 softball transfer portal is already one of the deepest in recent memory. The window opened Monday, June 8, and Day 1 brought an immediate wave of headline entries: Jaysoni Beachum from Florida State, Tori Edwards from LSU, Samantha Swan from Arizona State, and then Isa Torres — the D1 Softball Player of the Year — on Tuesday morning. And through it all, not one of those marquee names has committed.

By Tuesday afternoon, two more All-American-caliber players had added their names to the available list. The class is growing. The commitments are not.

Moriah Polar (Purdue)

Purdue outfielder Moriah Polar entered the portal Tuesday, announcing her move via social media. The news surprised some observers — Polar had been a cornerstone of the Boilermakers' program over three seasons and accumulated the kind of career numbers that speak for themselves.

Across three seasons at Purdue, Polar produced 218 hits, 103 RBI, and 137 runs scored. She earned All-American recognition for her production and was a central figure in Purdue's offensive identity. She has one year of eligibility remaining, making her the same profile as Beachum — a proven, veteran producer available for one championship push.

For a contender looking to add an experienced outfielder who can hit for average and drive in runs against elite competition, Polar represents exactly that. She has done it in the Big Ten. She can do it anywhere.

Atalyia Rijo (Arkansas)

Arkansas shortstop Atalyia Rijo entered the portal after a career-best 2026 season. The Razorbacks made the Women's College World Series this year, and Rijo contributed directly to that run. Her 2026 numbers represented personal highs in virtually every offensive category: .342 batting average, 13 doubles, 7 home runs, and 42 RBI.

At shortstop, premium defensive ability combines with her offensive production to create one of the more attractive two-way packages in this portal class. Programs that need positional stability at a premium defensive position, combined with an above-average bat, should be paying attention.

The Uncommitted Headliners

As of Tuesday afternoon, the five biggest names from the first 36 hours of the portal window remain without a commitment. Beachum (.417 BA, 16 HR, 66 RBI at FSU) is the No. 1 ranked transfer and a third baseman with one year remaining. Edwards (.802 SLG, 18 HR at LSU) is the redshirt sophomore with two full years of eligibility — the most valuable long-term piece in the class. Swan (.383 BA, 12 HR, 55 RBI at ASU) is an All-Big 12 outfielder who chose the portal despite her program's success. And Torres, as noted, is the D1 Player of the Year with one year remaining and connections to Texas.

Six players of this caliber simultaneously on the open market is genuinely unusual. The compressed timeline of the 15-day window means decisions that would typically take weeks in traditional recruiting happen in days here. Commitments could come in clusters, or one announcement could trigger a cascade of others.

What's Next

The portal window runs through June 22 — eleven days remain. The pace of new entries typically slows after the first week as programs lock in on specific targets and conversations move from broad exploration to focused negotiation. Watch the D1Softball and On3 transfer trackers daily. The commitment announcements from Beachum, Edwards, Torres, and Polar will reshape the 2027 national championship landscape when they come.
