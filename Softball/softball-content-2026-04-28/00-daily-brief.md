## Top Stories

### STORY 1:
**Tennessee Wins Series Over Alabama 4-1 — Tide Drop First Conference Series of 2026**
Tennessee completed the series upset Monday night, topping No. 3 Alabama 4-1 in Game 3 at Sherri Parker Lee Stadium. The Lady Vols scored three runs in the fourth inning and one in the fifth for a 4-0 lead. Alabama's Mari Hubbard hit a pinch-hit homer in the seventh but three straight strikeouts ended the rally. Tennessee wins the series 2-1, dealing Alabama its first conference series loss of 2026. Alabama falls to 44-6, 16-5 SEC, effectively ending its SEC title hopes. Oklahoma remains the clear No. 1 seed favorite. Tennessee improves to approximately 34-15, 11-7 SEC.

### STORY 2:
**Kendall Wells Still at 36 — The Nation Waits for HR No. 37**
OU freshman Kendall Wells remains at 36 home runs after Monday's action, one shy of tying Lauren Espinoza's 31-year NCAA single-season record of 37 (Arizona, 1995). Wells already broke the NCAA freshman HR record (No. 31, April 11), the Oklahoma program record, and the SEC single-season HR record. Oklahoma is 45-6, 17-3 SEC with five regular-season games remaining. Every at-bat is a national event.
**Tier:** 1 | **Follow-up:** Wells HR No. 37 tying record; potential No. 38 breaking it; OU remaining schedule

### STORY 3:
**Texas Tech Clinches Second Straight Big 12 Regular Season Title**
The No. 2/5 Texas Tech Red Raiders clinched the Big 12 regular season softball championship for the second consecutive year, finishing 47-4 overall, 19-2 in Big 12 play. Texas Tech sweeps the Big 12 title and earns the No. 1 seed in the Big 12 Tournament, which begins April 30. The Red Raiders are a consensus top-2 national seed candidate heading into conference tournament play.
**Tier:** 1 | **Follow-up:** Big 12 Tournament; national seed announcement May 10; WCWS path

### STORY 4:
**UCLA's Woolery and Grant Rewrite Bruin Record Books**
No. 6 UCLA completed a series sweep of No. 25 Washington (run-rule 9-1, Sunday) as the Bruins continue their 12-of-13 hot streak. Jordan Woolery broke a 26-year UCLA record surpassing Stacey Nuveman's single-season RBI mark of 91 (1999) — now the most RBIs in a single season in UCLA history. Megan Grant also tied Nuveman's single-season home run record of 31. Two historic marks in one season from one team.
