Nebraska Sits at 39-6 as the Huskers Close In on Win No. 40

By Emily Rawlings

April 28, 2026

Nebraska softball is one win away from a milestone. The Huskers are 39-6 on the season and 19-1 in Big Ten play after Sunday's series finale against Iowa was cancelled due to severe weather, preserving their 14-game win streak while leaving Win No. 40 tantalizingly close. A program-record crowd of 3,396 fans watched Nebraska sweep a Friday doubleheader against Iowa 9-1 and 5-2 — the enthusiasm in Lincoln reflects a fan base that knows it is watching something special.

Nebraska has won 25 of their last 26 games. Their Big Ten record of 19-1 is the best in the conference by a wide margin, and the Big Ten regular season title is effectively theirs. The question now is not whether the Huskers reach 40 wins — it's a matter of when.

The 40-Win Milestone

In college softball, reaching 40 wins in a regular season is a meaningful benchmark. It signals a program that has combined consistent excellence with tournament-caliber results — winning against quality opposition rather than padding a record against lesser competition. Nebraska's path to 39 wins has run through the Big Ten's best, and their overall schedule reflects the strength of a team that has earned every victory.

The Huskers' 14-game win streak includes sweeps of multiple conference opponents and has been punctuated by dominant pitching and powerful offense. The program-record crowd on Friday is a reminder that Nebraska's rise as a national softball contender is generating genuine excitement throughout the region. When Win No. 40 comes, it will be celebrated in a city that has fully embraced this program.

Big Ten Dominance

Nebraska's 19-1 conference record stands alone at the top of the Big Ten standings. No other team in the league is within striking distance. The Huskers have won their last 25 of 26 overall and have carried the Big Ten's best record through the final stretch of the regular season. Their RPI and strength-of-schedule metrics have them firmly in the conversation for a top-8 national seed heading into the Selection Show on May 10.

After spending years developing the program under coach Rhonda Revelle and building toward national relevance, Nebraska has arrived. This team is not just the Big Ten's best — it is one of the best programs in the country heading into the postseason.

What's Next

Nebraska will have the opportunity to reach Win No. 40 in their next scheduled game before conference tournament play begins. The Big Ten Tournament follows, offering another stage for the Huskers to demonstrate their national seed credentials. The Selection Show on May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 will reveal Nebraska's Regional seeding and path in the NCAA Tournament, with Regional play beginning May 15 and the Women's College World Series opening May 28 in Oklahoma City.

Win No. 40 is coming. And based on what Nebraska has shown this season, it won't be the last milestone they celebrate in 2026.
