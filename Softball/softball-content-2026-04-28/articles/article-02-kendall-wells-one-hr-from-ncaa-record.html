Kendall Wells Is One Home Run From the NCAA All-Time Record — And the World Is Watching

By Emily Rawlings

April 28, 2026

Every time Kendall Wells steps into the batter's box at Love's Field in Norman, the entire college softball world holds its breath. The Oklahoma freshman phenom has 36 home runs this season — just one short of tying Lauren Espinoza's NCAA all-time single-season record of 37, set at Arizona in 1995. That mark has stood for 31 years. It could fall any day now.

Oklahoma is 45-6 on the season, 17-3 in SEC play, with five regular-season games remaining before conference tournament season. Wells has five games — at minimum — to hit that record-tying home run. Given the pace she has maintained all season, the question isn't if she reaches 37. It's when.

A Season of Records

The records Wells has broken in 2026 read like a list that would define an entire career, let alone a freshman season. On April 11, she surpassed the NCAA freshman single-season home run record with her 31st homer of the year in a top-five series against Texas. Weeks later, she broke Oklahoma legend Jocelyn Alo's program record for home runs in a single season. In between, she shattered the SEC single-season home run record, previously set at 26.

When Wells hit her 35th home run against Georgia — a solo shot that opened the scoring in OU's eventual win — it also marked Oklahoma's 162nd team home run of the season, setting a new NCAA single-season team record. Even the records she breaks while chasing other records are historically significant.

The Target: Espinoza's 1995 Record

Lauren Espinoza's 37 home runs in the 1995 season at Arizona have outlasted every offensive threat the sport has produced for three decades. The fact that it has endured through eras of talent that include Olympic gold medalists and perennial All-Americans makes it one of college softball's most enduring benchmarks. Wells at 36 — with games remaining — represents the most credible threat to that record in the modern era.

Wells entered the 2026 season as a highly recruited freshman from North Oconee High School in Georgia, but nothing could have prepared the sport for what she would become. She has hit home runs in clusters, put up multi-homer games, and shown the kind of consistency and power that defies freshmen norms. Oklahoma head coach Patty Gasso has guided legends through Norman, and Wells is already writing her name among them.

What's Next

With five regular-season games remaining and the SEC Tournament to follow, Wells has ample opportunity to not just tie but break Espinoza's record. Should she reach 38, she would be the sole owner of the NCAA all-time single-season home run record — a milestone that would reverberate across the sport for decades.

The college softball world is watching every at-bat. Don't look away.
