**Post S2F1 — Long-Form**
Window: 11:00 AM ET

Freshman sensation Kendall Wells is sitting at 36 home runs on the season — just ONE swing away from tying Lauren Espinoza's NCAA all-time single-season record of 37 home runs, set at Arizona in 1995. That record has stood for 31 years.

Think about what Wells has already accomplished this season:
✅ Broke the NCAA freshman single-season home run record (surpassed 30, set April 11 vs. Texas)

Oklahoma is 45-6, 17-3 in SEC play with five regular-season games remaining before the conference tournament. Wells has plenty of chances to reach 37 — and potentially surpass it.

The moment she hits No. 37, it will be one of the most celebrated moments in college softball history. History has never been this close.

### STORY 4: UCLA Records

**Post S4F1 — Long-Form**
Window: 3:00 PM ET

UCLA softball is rewriting its own history books — two decades of records are being shattered in a single season.

No. 6 UCLA completed a series sweep at No. 25 Washington on Sunday, winning 9-1 in a dominant run-rule performance as two incredible individual milestones came into focus:

📊 Jordan Woolery broke Stacey Nuveman's 26-year-old UCLA record for RBIs in a season, surpassing the legendary catcher's mark of 91 set back in 1999
💥 Megan Grant tied Nuveman's single-season home run record of 31, another mark that had stood for 26 years

Nuveman is a UCLA legend — a four-time All-American and Olympic gold medalist. To have not just one but two players challenge her single-season records in 2026 is remarkable.

The Bruins are 12-of-13 to close out the regular season and are building a strong case for a top-8 national seed in the NCAA Tournament. In their second year in the Big Ten, UCLA is proving they belong among the sport's elite programs.

Records are falling in Westwood. This team is special. 💛💙
