### STORY 3: PGF Nationals Tomorrow

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

Tomorrow, the biggest girls' fastpitch softball tournament in the United States begins.

PGF Nationals 2026 opens at Great Park in Irvine, California — along with four other Southern California venues — with Week 1 bringing the 18U and 16U Premier divisions (July 19-25). Week 2 (July 26–August 2) adds 14U, 12U, and 10U.

The 18U Premier bracket alone has 72 teams from 22 states. The championship games air on ESPNU (18U at 3 PM) and ESPN+ (16U at 11:30 AM).

Top contenders going into the 18U Premier:
• GA Impact Taylor (SEC-heavy roster with Oklahoma and Florida signees)
• Mojo-Lewis (Power Four commits from multiple programs)
• Defending champions returning with elite 2026 and 2027 talent

But here's the bigger picture: September 1 is 44 days away. That's the date when Division I college coaches can begin directly contacting Class of 2028 recruits for the first time. For 16U and 18U players in this tournament, every at-bat this weekend is a live recruiting audition. Every error, every clutch hit, every inning pitched happens in front of college coaches who are watching closely and have 44 days until they can make their move.

The PGF Nationals don't just crown champions. They launch careers.

### STORY 4: Megan Grant and the Play-In Path

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Megan Grant broke a 31-year-old NCAA home run record in April.

She hit her 42nd home run at UCLA this past spring to shatter Laura Espinoza's mark — a record that had stood since 1995. She was nominated for an ESPY for Best Record-Breaking Performance. She hit a 313-foot home run in her pro debut that set the AUSL's distance record. She leads the league in home runs with 8 in approximately 20 games.

And now she needs to win one play-in game to keep her season alive.

The Portland Cascade, after Friday's 7-2 loss to the Texas Volts, is locked into the No. 3 seed. That means a single-elimination play-in on July 23 at Davis Diamond against whoever finishes No. 2. Win and they're in the AUSL Championship Series. Lose and the 2026 season ends — for Megan Grant and for every Cascade player who has been fighting all season to get to this moment.

The play-in isn't a consolation prize. It's a genuine championship path. But it's also the hardest possible road.

Megan Grant came to the pros to win. The play-in is where she finds out if the professional game is ready for what she can do.

### STORY 5: Davis Diamond and the ABC Championship

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM ET

One week from today, professional softball makes history.

The 2026 AUSL Championship Series begins July 25 at Davis Diamond — a premier softball facility on the campus of Texas A&M in College Station, Texas. Game 1 airs on ABC. That's the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television.

Here's how we get there:

**The No. 1 seed** (whoever finishes first in the regular season, which ends Monday July 20) walks directly into the best-of-three Championship Series. No play-in. No extra game. Straight to the Championship.

**The No. 2 and No. 3 seeds** — right now, that race sits with Chicago Bandits at 13-7 and Portland Cascade at 11-10 — play a single-elimination game on July 23 at Davis Diamond. One game. The winner faces the No. 1 seed beginning July 25.

Davis Diamond is one of the best softball venues in the country. 5,000-capacity. Texas A&M campus. State-of-the-art in every respect.

And on July 25, ABC brings it into living rooms across the United States for the first time.

Women's professional softball has been building toward this moment. It arrives in seven days.
