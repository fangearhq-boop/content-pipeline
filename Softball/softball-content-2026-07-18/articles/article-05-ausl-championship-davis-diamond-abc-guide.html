One Week to the AUSL Championship. Here's Your Guide to Davis Diamond and the ABC Debut.

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 18, 2026

Seven days from today, professional softball arrives on ABC for the first time.

The 2026 AUSL Championship Series begins July 25 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. Game 1 airs nationally on the ABC network — a milestone for women's professional sports that the sport has been building toward for years. If you are just now paying attention to the AUSL, here is what you need to know before history happens.

Where Is Davis Diamond?

Davis Diamond is located on the campus of Texas A&M University in College Station, Texas. It is one of the premier dedicated softball venues in the United States — a 5,000-capacity facility with a modern design built specifically for the sport. Texas A&M is one of the most passionate college softball environments in the country, and Davis Diamond sits in the heart of it. The AUSL selected it as the Championship venue for what it represents: a statement that professional softball belongs at the same level as the sport's best college programs.

How Does the Postseason Work?

The AUSL regular season ends Monday, July 20. The top three teams in the standings earn postseason berths. Here is how each path unfolds:

No. 1 Seed

The No. 1 seed receives a direct bye into the best-of-three Championship Series. They do not play the play-in game. They wait. The Utah Talons — who enter Saturday at 14-6 and lead the league — are currently positioned to claim this seed. A win today against the Bandits would all but lock it up.

No. 2 and No. 3 Seeds

The No. 2 and No. 3 seeds play each other in a single-elimination play-in game on July 23 at Davis Diamond. One game. The winner advances to the Championship Series. The loser goes home. Right now, the Chicago Bandits (13-7) are the likely No. 2 seed and the Portland Cascade (11-10) are locked into No. 3.

The Championship Series

The AUSL Championship Series is best-of-three, running July 25-27 at Davis Diamond. The No. 1 seed hosts every game in the series. Game 1 on July 25 airs nationally on ABC. It is the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television — an audience reach the sport has not previously had access to at the professional level.

Why Does the No. 1 Seed Matter So Much?

Softball championships are often decided by pitching depth. The team that avoids an extra elimination game going into a best-of-three series has a meaningful advantage — their best pitchers are rested and available for the full three games. The No. 2 seed arrives at the Championship having already pitched through a play-in two days before. The No. 1 seed arrives fresh.

The AUSL's three-team postseason structure makes that advantage larger than it would be in a deeper playoff bracket. There is no room to absorb a loss in the play-in. Every game matters immediately. And Game 1 of the Championship — on ABC, in front of a national audience — is where the No. 1 seed's advantage turns into a genuine edge.
