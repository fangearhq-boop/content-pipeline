Megan Grant Came to the Pros to Win. Now She Has to Do It Through a Play-In.

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 18, 2026

She broke a record that stood for 31 years. She hit the longest home run in AUSL history in her professional debut. She leads the league in home runs. She was nominated for an ESPY.

Now Megan Grant's 2026 season comes down to one game on July 23.

The Record and the Record That Came After It

Grant hit her 42nd home run of the 2026 college season at UCLA to surpass Laura Espinoza's single-season NCAA record — a mark that had stood since 1995. The record-breaking home run made Grant a finalist for the ESPY Award for Best Record-Breaking Performance. She didn't win — Myles Garrett took the award for his 23 NFL sacks with the Los Angeles Rams — but being on that ballot alongside a landmark NFL achievement put softball on a stage it rarely reaches in the middle of July.

Then she went pro.

In her AUSL debut with the Portland Cascade, Grant hit a 313-foot home run off Karlyn Pickens — the No. 1 overall pick and Carolina Blaze ace — that set the professional league's distance record. The exit velocity: 86 miles per hour. The record, like the NCAA mark before it, landed immediately.

The 2026 Cascade Season

Portland has been one of the three playoff teams from early in the AUSL season. Grant has driven the offense, hitting 8 home runs in approximately 20 games to lead the league. Her combined 2026 total across her college season and pro debut — 50 home runs — is historically unprecedented for a player in the first year of her career.

The Cascade entered the final week of the regular season fighting for the No. 2 seed. On July 16, they knocked first-place Utah out of their two-game cushion with a 10-2 run-rule victory in Hillsboro. It was the single biggest win of their season.

On July 17, the Texas Volts beat them 7-2 and locked them into the No. 3 seed.

The Play-In

The No. 3 seed plays the No. 2 seed in a single-elimination game on July 23 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. One game. A win advances to the best-of-three Championship Series beginning July 25 — with Game 1 airing nationally on ABC in the first professional softball broadcast on network television. A loss ends the Cascade's 2026 season.

The Cascade is not a team to count out. They have the pitching to stay competitive, and in Grant they have the kind of hitter who can change a game by herself in a single inning. The play-in is hard, but it is not hopeless.

What they know going in: if Grant is healthy, locked in, and getting pitches to hit, the Cascade can beat anyone in a single game. She has done it all year against the best competition the AUSL has offered.

What's Next

The AUSL regular season ends Monday, July 20. The No. 3 seed play-in game is July 23 at Davis Diamond. If the Cascade wins, they advance to the Championship beginning July 25. Watch for Grant in every single at-bat on July 23 — this is exactly the moment those 42 college home runs were building toward.
