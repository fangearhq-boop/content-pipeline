72 Teams. 22 States. The PGF Nationals Open Tomorrow.

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 18, 2026

The biggest girls' fastpitch tournament of the summer starts tomorrow in Southern California. And for the players, coaches, and families who have spent the last year building toward this weekend, tomorrow cannot come fast enough.

The 2026 PGF National Championships open Sunday at Great Park in Irvine, California, along with four other venues across the region — Bill Barber Park, Fountain Valley Sports Park, Harvard Park, and the Huntington Beach Sports Complex. Week 1 runs July 19-25 with the 18U and 16U Premier divisions. Week 2 follows July 26–August 2, bringing 14U, 12U, and 10U to the same venues.

The Scale

The 18U Premier bracket has 72 teams from 22 states. The 16U Premier is comparable in depth. These are not regional tournaments with local invitees — they are national events where the top clubs in the country come together to compete for titles that will define the summer's recruiting narrative.

The championship games air on ESPN: 16U on ESPN+ at 11:30 AM and 18U on ESPNU at 3:00 PM on July 25. That broadcast reach is unusual for a youth tournament, and it reflects the national significance PGF has built over decades as the premier showcase event in girls' softball.

What Is at Stake

For 18U and 16U players, the stakes this weekend extend beyond trophies. September 1 — the date when Division I college coaches are allowed to begin directly contacting Class of 2028 recruits for the first time — is 44 days away. The PGF Nationals is the last major evaluation event before that window opens.

College coaches from every program in the country will be in Southern California this week, watching carefully. Every at-bat, every inning pitched, every play in the field at Great Park this weekend is a live audition for programs that will begin making calls on September 1. Players who perform at PGF plant a flag in coaches' minds. Players who falter carry that into the recruiting conversation.

Teams to Watch

In the 18U Premier, GA Impact Taylor enters with one of the most college-committed rosters in the field — including a Florida signee and multiple Oklahoma-committed players. Mojo-Lewis brings a deep group of Power Four commits. The defending champion returns with elite talent at the top of the 2026 and 2027 recruit classes.

In the 16U Premier, Arizona Storm Appel — which finished third nationally last year — returns loaded with Top 100 recruits committed to Alabama, Auburn, Oklahoma, and Northwestern.

These teams have spent the entire year building toward this weekend. The rosters locked July 13. The only thing left is playing.

What's Next

Pool play begins tomorrow, Sunday July 19, with bracket play starting July 21. Championship games are July 24-25. The September 1 recruiting contact window opens in 44 days. Watch the 18U Premier championship on ESPNU and the 16U championship on ESPN+. This weekend starts something that will play out in the recruiting landscape for months to come.
