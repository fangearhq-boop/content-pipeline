Utah Leads Chicago by One Game — and They Play Today

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 18, 2026

The AUSL regular season has three games left. The No. 1 seed race has one game that matters. And it starts at 4 PM ET today.

Utah Talons at Chicago Bandits. ESPN2. No. 1 seed on the line.

Where Things Stand

The Utah Talons are 14-6. The Chicago Bandits are 13-7. The gap is one game — the same one it was a week ago, minus the drama that filled the space between.

On Wednesday night, the Portland Cascade came to Hillsboro Ballpark and ran Utah off the field. The Cascade won 10-2 in five innings — a run-rule victory that erased Utah's two-game cushion and turned this final weekend into exactly the tense, one-game-at-a-time race the Bandits needed. The same night, Chicago beat the Carolina Blaze 15-7 in its own statement win.

The math heading into today: if Utah wins, they lead by two with two left and effectively clinch the No. 1 seed. If Chicago wins, the race is tied at 14-7 with two games remaining and everything gets harder.

What the No. 1 Seed Means

In the AUSL, the seedings are not cosmetic. The No. 1 seed walks directly into the AUSL Championship Series, which begins July 25 at Davis Diamond on the Texas A&M campus in College Station, Texas. Game 1 of the Championship will air nationally on ABC — the first professional softball game ever on network television. The No. 1 seed arrives at Davis Diamond fresh, rested, and with the full benefit of knowing their opponent.

The No. 2 and No. 3 seeds get no such luxury. They play each other in a single-elimination play-in game on July 23 at Davis Diamond — and whoever loses goes home. The winner then turns around and faces the No. 1 seed two days later, having already burned one more pitcher and absorbed one more high-pressure game.

The difference between first and second is enormous. Today is where that difference gets decided.

The Teams Arriving Today

The Utah Talons have been first place all season. They clinched the league's first postseason berth on July 16, when Sharlize Palacios hit a go-ahead two-run home run in the seventh inning and Megan Faraimo threw a complete game with 10 strikeouts in a 5-4 win over Oklahoma City. They are the steadiest team in the AUSL and have held first place since the season opened.

The Chicago Bandits are the hottest team in the second half of the season. They ran off nine consecutive wins — an AUSL record — before Portland snapped the streak, and they responded Wednesday with a 15-7 demolition of Carolina. Pitchers Lexi Kilfoyl and Kat Sandercock have been among the best in the league, and Sami Williams has anchored the offense in the run-producing role all season.

One team is built for consistency. One team is built for moments. Today finds out which kind of game defines the AUSL regular season.

What's Next

Talons at Bandits, 4 PM ET today on ESPN2. The AUSL regular season ends Monday, July 20. After that: the play-in game on July 23 at Davis Diamond, and the Championship Series on July 25-27 with Game 1 on ABC. There are three games left in the 2026 AUSL regular season. The most important one is this afternoon.
