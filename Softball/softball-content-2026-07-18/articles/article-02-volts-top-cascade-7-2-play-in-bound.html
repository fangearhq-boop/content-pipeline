Volts Beat Cascade 7-2. Portland's Path to Davis Diamond Now Runs Through a Play-In.

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 18, 2026

The Texas Volts have been outside the playoff picture for weeks. On Friday night, they beat the team that has not.

The Volts defeated the Portland Cascade 7-2 at Hillsboro Ballpark, limiting Portland to four hits in a decisive result that sealed the Cascade's postseason fate. They are headed to the July 23 play-in game. Win that game and they go to the Championship. Lose and the 2026 season is done.

What Happened in Hillsboro

The Volts were the better team on Friday in nearly every phase. Their offense generated 10 hits against a Cascade pitching staff that had been solid all season. The Cascade, meanwhile, managed just four hits — a number that tells the story of a lineup that never found its footing.

The result left Portland at 11-10 with two games remaining in the regular season, which ends Monday, July 20. The Cascade holds the No. 3 seed. With the Talons (14-6) and Bandits (13-7) both ahead, Portland cannot catch either team. The No. 3 seed means the play-in game. There is no longer any other math.

The Canady Question

NiJaree Canady has been the most-watched pitcher in the AUSL all season — for the wrong reasons, and then, slowly, for the right ones.

The No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL College Draft opened the season 0-3 with a 6.87 ERA. Injuries at Texas Tech had altered her mechanics — her stance had shifted from the dominant square position she used at Stanford and in her record-breaking college career to an open position that stripped power and precision from her delivery. The Volts called in Tori Nyberg, the former Stanford pitching coach who recruited Canady out of high school and coached her during seasons in which she posted ERAs of 0.57 and 0.73. Nyberg, Canady, and Volts assistant coach Josh Trevino worked together to rebuild the delivery from the ground up.

The results have been emerging. Canady won her first AUSL game on July 10 — a complete-game effort against the Carolina Blaze with six strikeouts. The Texas Volts followed that up Friday with the 7-2 win over Portland. The Canady arc is not finished; it is ongoing. But the direction has changed.

Cascade's Play-In Path

The Portland Cascade will play the No. 2 seed in a single-elimination game on July 23 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. As of Saturday, the Bandits are the most likely No. 2 seed — though that depends on today's Talons-Bandits result and the final two days of the regular season.

The Cascade has the offensive firepower to win a single-elimination game. Megan Grant — who set the all-time NCAA home run record at 42 in her final UCLA season and leads the AUSL with 8 home runs — is the kind of bat that can change any game in a single at-bat. The pitching staff has been reliable over the second half of the season.

Friday night was a setback. The play-in is not a death sentence. But it is the hardest path.

What's Next

The Cascade and Volts play again tonight at Hillsboro Ballpark. The AUSL regular season ends Monday, July 20. The play-in game is July 23 at Davis Diamond. The Championship Series begins July 25, with Game 1 on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television.
