### STORY 1: PGF Nationals Opens Today

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET

The wait is over.

PGF Nationals 2026 is open.

As of this morning, 72 teams from 22 states are competing in the 18U and 16U Premier divisions at Great Park in Irvine, California — along with Bill Barber Park, Fountain Valley Sports Park, Harvard Park, and Huntington Beach Sports Complex. This is Week One of the biggest girls' fastpitch tournament in the country, and it runs through July 25.

Here's the number that matters for every player on those fields: **43.**

That's how many days remain until September 1 — the date when Division I college coaches can, for the very first time, directly contact Class of 2028 recruits. Every game at PGF Nationals this week is a live audition conducted in front of coaches who have been prohibited from calling, texting, or visiting those players until the clock hits September 1.

The 18U Premier championship airs on ESPNU (3:00 PM). The 16U Premier championship airs on ESPN+ (11:30 AM). Top contenders in the 18U bracket include GA Impact Taylor, Mojo-Lewis, Arizona Storm-Mendez, and Alliance Fastpitch among others.

The tournament doesn't just crown champions. It changes futures.

### STORY 2: AUSL Final Day Tomorrow

#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET

The AUSL regular season ends tomorrow.

One more day of games — Monday, July 20 — and then it's over. The first professional softball regular season in the league's history comes down to its final day, and the Utah Talons are in the driver's seat.

Utah clinched the league's first-ever postseason berth earlier this month in dramatic fashion: a walk-off two-run homer by Sharlize Palacios in the seventh inning of a comeback win over the OKC Spark. The Talons have held the No. 1 seed since the early weeks of the season. They are one day away from officially locking in the best path to the first AUSL Championship.

Here's what the No. 1 seed means: no play-in game. The top team goes directly to the best-of-three Championship Series at Davis Diamond, July 25-27, with Game 1 airing on ABC — the first professional softball game ever on network television.

The No. 2 and No. 3 seeds must play each other first — a single-elimination play-in game on July 23 at Davis Diamond. The winner advances; the loser goes home. One more game changes who has to play that game and who gets to skip it.

The Chicago Bandits — who put together a nine-game winning streak (the longest in AUSL history) and never stopped competing — kept the race alive all second half. The final games Monday will tell us exactly who goes where.

Everything is in play. The clock runs out tomorrow.

### STORY 3: Megan Grant and Maya Brady — Two Former Bruins Tied for the AUSL HR Lead

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

Megan Grant has 8 home runs in the AUSL.

Maya Brady has 8 home runs in the AUSL.

They're tied for the league lead. And they're former teammates from UCLA.

Grant and Brady spent their college careers wearing the same uniform at Westwood. Grant became one of the most celebrated players in college softball history — breaking the all-time NCAA single-season home run record with 42 this past spring, erasing a mark set by Laura Espinoza in 1995 that had stood for 31 years. She also set the AUSL distance record with a 313-foot home run off Karlyn Pickens (the No. 1 overall pick) in her professional debut.

Brady came to the AUSL with her own power credentials and has matched Grant step for step — both sitting at 8 HRs through the same stretch of the regular season.

Two former Bruins. Two power bats. Now on opposite sides of a professional league race, with the regular season ending tomorrow and two games left to separate them.

Who finishes on top? It's one of the best matchups of the AUSL's first season — and it's playing out through the final days.

### STORY 4: NiJaree Canady Watch — She Didn't Pitch Friday

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Friday night at Hillsboro Ballpark, the Texas Volts beat the Portland Cascade 7-2.

NiJaree Canady didn't throw a pitch.

Ally Carda started for Texas, and Aliyah Binford came on in relief. Together they held the Cascade to 4 hits. Canady — the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL Draft, the pitcher who came to the professional game as one of the most anticipated arms in the sport — spent Friday watching from the dugout.

She has been working with former Stanford pitching coach Tori Nyberg all season. Mechanics altered by injuries at Texas Tech, and Nyberg has been helping her reset to the form that made her the most dominant pitcher in college softball. She started the AUSL season 0-3 with a 6.87 ERA in her early appearances, and the work with Nyberg has been described as a "squared-up position" reset designed to restore what her coaches call the "OG NiJa."

The regular season ends Monday. The Volts have one game left.

If Canady starts Monday, it's her last regular-season chance to take a step toward the pitcher she was. If she doesn't, this story carries into next year without a resolution.

The comeback arc of the AUSL's first season isn't finished yet.

### STORY 5: 6 Days to Davis Diamond

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM ET

Six days from now, professional softball makes history.

The 2026 AUSL Championship Series begins July 25 at Davis Diamond — the premier softball facility on the campus of Texas A&M in College Station, Texas. Game 1 airs on ABC. For the first time ever, a professional softball game will broadcast on network television.

Here's how the final stretch works:

**Monday, July 20:** Last regular-season games.

**Thursday, July 23:** Play-in game. The No. 2 and No. 3 seeds meet at Davis Diamond in a single-elimination game. Win and you're in the Championship. Lose and your season is over.

**Saturday, July 25:** AUSL Championship Series begins. The No. 1 seed — whoever emerges from Monday's final games — enters fresh, skipping the play-in entirely. Game 1 airs nationally on ABC.

**July 25-27:** Best-of-three Championship Series, all at Davis Diamond.

Davis Diamond is 5,000-capacity, purpose-built for elite softball, and one of the finest venues in the sport. Texas A&M's campus hosts the first professional softball championship in the AUSL's history — and the ABC broadcast makes it the first pro softball game most of the country will ever see on network television.

Women's professional softball has been building toward this moment. Six days from now, it arrives.
