72 Teams. 22 States. The PGF Nationals Are Finally Here.

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 19, 2026

The preview is over. The rosters are locked. The fields at Great Park are live.

PGF Nationals 2026 begins today.

The Tournament That Defines the Summer

The Premier Girls Fastpitch National Championships are the single most important event in girls' travel softball — and Week One is underway. Starting today and running through July 25, the 18U and 16U Premier divisions will compete across five Southern California venues: Great Park in Irvine, Bill Barber Park, Fountain Valley Sports Park, Harvard Park, and Huntington Beach Sports Complex.

The 18U Premier bracket alone brings 72 teams from 22 states. Championship games for the 18U division air on ESPNU at 3:00 PM. The 16U Premier championship broadcasts on ESPN+ at 11:30 AM. This is the biggest media footprint girls' travel softball gets all summer, and it starts today.

The Number That Matters: 43

Here's why this week carries such weight for the players on these fields.

September 1 is 43 days away. That is the date when Division I college coaches can, for the very first time, directly contact Class of 2028 recruits. Phone calls, text messages, home visits — all of it is prohibited until September 1. But watching a player compete at PGF Nationals? That is entirely legal.

Every at-bat in the 18U Premier bracket this week is a live recruiting audition for coaches who have been building their evaluations in silence. A player who goes 0-for-3 in pool play won't be forgotten by July 21. A player who drives in the winning run in bracket play could receive a phone call six weeks from now that changes the course of her life.

The PGF Nationals don't just crown champions. They feed the pipeline that makes the sport run.

The Field This Year

The 18U Premier bracket is as deep as it has ever been. Top contenders heading into Week One include GA Impact Taylor — carrying a roster heavy with Power Four commits from Oklahoma and Florida — and Mojo-Lewis, which brings multiple Power Four signees from across the country. Arizona Storm-Mendez and Alliance Fastpitch are also among the programs expected to compete deep into bracket play.

The defending champion enters with the target on their back that comes with that distinction. Across all five venues, the quality of play in the 18U Premier is the highest it has been in years.

The 16U Premier features its own elite field, with programs competing to position their rising sophomores and juniors for the evaluation windows still to come. For the Class of 2029 and 2030 players in that bracket, this week is an early statement on where their recruitment might go.

What's Next

Pool play continues through the early part of the week, with bracket play beginning as pools resolve. Championship Sunday closes Week One, with the 18U title game on ESPNU and the 16U title on ESPN+. Week Two of PGF Nationals follows separately and features 14U, 12U, and 10U divisions. The September 1 recruiting window opens 43 days from today — and the players who make their case this week will remember exactly what they did under the lights in Irvine when that date arrives.
