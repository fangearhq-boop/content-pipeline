One Day Left. The AUSL's No. 1 Seed Race Is Almost Over.

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 19, 2026

The AUSL's debut regular season ends tomorrow.

Monday, July 20, is the last day of games before the postseason begins. Three teams will emerge from the regular season to play for the AUSL Championship — and the most important number separating them is one: the difference between first place and second, between a direct path to the ABC Championship and a win-or-go-home play-in game.

Where Things Stand

The Utah Talons have held the No. 1 seed for virtually the entire season, and they enter the final day with the advantage. Utah clinched the first postseason berth in AUSL history in dramatic fashion: a comeback win over the OKC Spark featuring a walk-off two-run home run by Sharlize Palacios in the seventh inning, backed by a complete game from Megan Faraimo with 10 strikeouts. They have been the steadiest team in the league from opening day through this weekend.

The Chicago Bandits never stopped competing. They put together a nine-game winning streak — the longest in AUSL history — and kept the No. 1 seed race alive through the second half. Pitchers Lexi Kilfoyl and Kat Sandercock have been among the best in the league, and Sami Williams has been one of the AUSL's most productive offensive players. The Bandits pushed Utah to the wire because they never stopped competing for the top spot.

What the No. 1 Seed Actually Means

In the AUSL, the seedings are not symbolic. The No. 1 seed walks directly into the best-of-three Championship Series at Davis Diamond, which begins July 25. Game 1 airs nationally on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television. The top team arrives fresh, rested, and with full knowledge of their opponent.

The No. 2 and No. 3 seeds do not get that luxury. They play each other first in a single-elimination play-in game on July 23 at Davis Diamond. One game. The loser goes home. The winner advances to face the No. 1 seed two days later, having already expended pitching and absorbed the emotional weight of a win-or-go-home contest.

The difference between finishing first and finishing second is enormous. Monday is where that difference becomes permanent.

The Final Day

The AUSL plays its final regular-season games on Monday, July 20. After that, the calendar moves quickly: play-in game July 23 at Davis Diamond, then the Championship Series July 25-27, with Game 1 on ABC. The Portland Cascade is locked into the No. 3 seed and the play-in path after their Friday loss to the Texas Volts. Whatever happens Monday, Portland's next game is July 23.

For the Talons and Bandits, Monday is the last chance to write the final line of the 2026 regular season standings. Utah enters with the edge. Chicago has shown all season that edges can be overcome.

What's Next

Final regular-season games: Monday, July 20. Play-in game: Thursday, July 23 at Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. AUSL Championship Series: July 25-27 at Davis Diamond. Game 1 airs on ABC. The team that finishes first on Monday starts the Championship on the best possible footing.
