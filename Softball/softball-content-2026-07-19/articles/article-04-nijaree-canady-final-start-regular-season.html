NiJaree Canady Didn't Pitch Friday. One Start Remains Before the Offseason.

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 19, 2026

The Texas Volts beat the Portland Cascade 7-2 on Friday night at Hillsboro Ballpark.

NiJaree Canady didn't throw a pitch.

Who Pitched Instead

Ally Carda took the mound for Texas and started against the Cascade. Aliyah Binford came on in relief. Together they held Portland to 4 hits over 7 innings — a solid performance by both pitchers. The Volts won convincingly. Canady, the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL Draft, was not part of the outing.

This is not an isolated development. Canady opened the AUSL season 0-3 with a 6.87 ERA in her early appearances and has been working through a mechanical reset with former Stanford pitching coach Tori Nyberg. Injuries sustained at Texas Tech altered the mechanics of her delivery, and the result in the early part of the AUSL season was a pitcher who looked nothing like the player who dominated college softball. The Volts responded by bringing Nyberg in to work on what she described as a "squared-up position" reset.

The Arc of the Season

Canady came to professional softball with more anticipation than almost any player in the draft. At Texas Tech, she was one of the most dominant pitchers in Division I — dominant enough that the Volts selected her with the second pick in the entire draft. The expectation was that her professional debut would look something like her college peak.

It hasn't worked out that way yet. And the reasons — mechanical damage from injuries, adjustment to a different level of competition, the mechanics work now underway — are real and documented. But they don't change the fact that Canady's 2026 season has been defined by struggle, not the dominance that made her the No. 2 pick.

The goal, as described by those close to the situation, is to restore the "OG NiJa" — the version of Canady that was one of college softball's most feared pitchers. That version hasn't shown up yet at the professional level.

One Game Left

The AUSL regular season ends Monday, July 20. The Volts have one game remaining. That is the last opportunity Canady has to take a professional regular-season start before the offseason.

If she pitches Monday, it becomes the final chapter of what has been the AUSL's biggest individual story — a gifted pitcher fighting to rediscover herself in the spotlight of a new league. If she doesn't, the question of whether the Nyberg reset worked carries into 2027 without a definitive answer.

The Volts are out of the playoff picture. The stakes for Monday are limited in the standings. But for the story of NiJaree Canady's professional debut, Monday is the last page.

What's Next

Texas Volts regular-season finale: Monday, July 20. If Canady starts, that is her last opportunity to build toward a resolution before the offseason. The AUSL play-in game is July 23 at Davis Diamond; the Championship Series follows July 25-27. The Volts, outside the playoff picture, will watch those games from the outside. For Canady, the focus from that point shifts to preparation for 2027 and the continued mechanical work with Tori Nyberg.
