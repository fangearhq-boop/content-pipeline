Six Days to Davis Diamond. Here's Your Complete AUSL Championship Guide.

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 19, 2026

Six days from now, professional softball makes history.

The 2026 AUSL Championship Series begins July 25 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. Game 1 airs on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television. If you've been hearing about this moment and want to understand what's actually happening, here's the complete guide.

The Venue: Davis Diamond

Davis Diamond is a premier softball facility on the campus of Texas A&M University in College Station, Texas. With a capacity of 5,000, it is one of the finest venues in the sport — purpose-built for elite softball and capable of generating the kind of atmosphere the AUSL Championship deserves. The Texas A&M Aggies play their home games at Davis Diamond, which means the facility has hosted major college softball games and knows how to handle championship-level competition.

The Format

The AUSL postseason has two stages, and which stage a team enters depends entirely on where they finish in the regular season — which ends tomorrow, Monday July 20.

The No. 1 seed earns a direct bye into the best-of-three Championship Series. No extra game, no extra pressure, no burned pitchers. They wait at Davis Diamond and meet whoever wins the play-in.

The No. 2 and No. 3 seeds play a single-elimination play-in game on Thursday, July 23, also at Davis Diamond. One team wins and advances to the Championship. The other team's season ends on the spot. It is the most unforgiving format in professional softball.

The Championship Series runs July 25-27 at Davis Diamond in a best-of-three format. Three possible games; the first team to win two advances their name onto the trophy of the AUSL's inaugural season.

The ABC Broadcast

Game 1 of the Championship Series on July 25 airs on ABC. That sentence represents something that has never happened before in the history of women's professional softball: a game on network television, reaching every household in the country that has access to a broadcast signal.

The AUSL's first season has drawn attention for its talent level, its stars, and its accessibility. The ABC broadcast takes it from a sport fans seek out to one that finds fans who haven't been looking. For the players who have spent their careers building toward a professional league, July 25 is the moment the rest of the country gets to see what they've been doing all year.

What's Next

Regular season ends Monday, July 20. Play-in game at Davis Diamond: Thursday, July 23. AUSL Championship Series at Davis Diamond: July 25-27. Game 1 on ABC. Six days from today, professional softball has its first champion — and the country gets to watch it happen.
