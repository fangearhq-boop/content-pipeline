They Were Teammates at UCLA. Now They're Tied for the AUSL Home Run Lead.

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 19, 2026

Megan Grant has 8 home runs in the AUSL. Maya Brady has 8 home runs in the AUSL. And until two days ago, they shared a place in the lineup at the same school in Westwood.

The AUSL's home run race is a Bruin vs. Bruin story.

Grant's Record-Breaking Season

Megan Grant entered the professional game as one of the most decorated players in college softball history. In her final season at UCLA, she hit 42 home runs — breaking Laura Espinoza's all-time Division I single-season record of 37, a mark that had stood since 1995. She was nominated for an ESPY Award for Best Record-Breaking Performance. She set the AUSL distance record with a 313-foot home run off Karlyn Pickens — the No. 1 overall pick — in her very first professional at-bat.

In the AUSL's first regular season, Grant has led or been tied for the home run lead consistently. She leads the Portland Cascade as their most dangerous offensive presence, and her production has made the professional game's power conversation inseparable from her name.

Brady's Parallel Path

Maya Brady's professional career has moved in parallel. A former UCLA standout like Grant, Brady joined the Chicago Bandits and has matched her former teammate step for step in the home run column. Both sit at 8 HRs heading into the AUSL's final two games of the regular season.

The timing makes this storyline sharper: Grant's Cascade and Brady's Bandits are on opposite ends of the playoff race. The Bandits are competing for the No. 1 seed while the Cascade is locked into a play-in path. Grant and Brady's home run race is one of the few threads connecting the two sides of the bracket.

The Play-In Adds Pressure to Grant

For Grant, every at-bat in the final two regular-season games and the July 23 play-in carries additional weight. The Cascade's path to the AUSL Championship runs through a single-elimination game at Davis Diamond — win or go home. Grant's 42 HRs changed college softball history. Her AUSL HRs have been building toward a postseason moment.

If the Cascade survive the play-in, Grant's home run total would become one of the defining numbers of the AUSL Championship. If they don't, the record book will show what she accomplished — and the home run race between her and Brady will be resolved by whoever logs one more before the final out.

What's Next

The AUSL regular season concludes Monday, July 20, giving Grant and Brady two final regular-season games to break the tie. The Portland Cascade plays their play-in game July 23 at Davis Diamond against whoever finishes No. 2. The Chicago Bandits — if they hold the No. 2 or No. 1 seed — either join the play-in or advance directly to the Championship Series. Both former Bruins are two days from a resolution. There's no certainty who finishes on top.
