### STORY 2: Kendall Wells All-Time HR Chase

#### Long-Form Post — 11:30 AM ET

Lauren Espinoza hit 37 home runs for Arizona in 1995. That record has stood for 31 years. 🥎

Kendall Wells has 31.

The Oklahoma freshman needs six more home runs to break one of the most durable records in college softball history. With Oklahoma carrying 12-plus regular season games still to play, the math is very much in her favor. Wells is batting .383 with 60 RBIs and leads the entire country in home runs — and she's doing it as a first-year player.

Tonight, in front of a national TV audience on ESPN2 during the Bedlam game at Devon Park, Wells gets another shot. Every time she steps into the batter's box, the record gets a little closer.

Thirty-one years is a long time for any record to stand. A freshman from Georgia might be the one to finally knock it down.

Are you watching Wells' every at-bat, or is it too stressful? 😅

### STORY 5: WCWS 32-Team Field

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Here's news that matters for every college softball fan with a team still in the hunt. 🥎

The 2026 Women's College World Series expands to a 32-team field with a new seeding "bucket" format designed to limit early conference matchups. The tournament is set for Devon Park in Oklahoma City, May 28 through June 4 or 5, and the expanded bracket means more bids, more paths to OKC, and more chaos in the early rounds.

The SEC is projected to lead all conferences in regional hosting spots. The ACC, Big 12, and Big Ten all have multiple projected hosts as well. For bubble programs like Stanford, Virginia Tech, and Duke — teams on the edge of national seed consideration — the wider field is very good news.

Six weeks out, the WCWS bracket picture is starting to take shape. The games between now and the end of May will decide who goes home early and who ends up in Oklahoma City.
