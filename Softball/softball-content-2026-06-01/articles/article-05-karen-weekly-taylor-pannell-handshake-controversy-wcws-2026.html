The WCWS Handshake Drama: Karen Weekly, Taylor Pannell, and What We Know

By Emily Rawlings | June 1, 2026

After Tennessee's nine-inning walk-off win over Texas Tech on Saturday, the story was supposed to be Emma Clarke's historic home run. Instead, the conversation at the Women's College World Series shifted to a postgame handshake line and a dispute that has now spilled into national headlines.

Here is what we know — and what remains in dispute.

The Claim

Texas Tech third baseman Taylor Pannell said after the loss that Tennessee head coach Karen Weekly told her in the handshake line that she had "made a mistake" — a reference, Pannell suggested, to her decision to transfer from Tennessee to Texas Tech last summer. Pannell described the moment as "kind of crazy" and said she found it funny that Weekly was still thinking about it.

The remark, if it occurred as described, would represent a pointed message from a coach to a former player who left her program — delivered in the handshake line of a nationally televised WCWS game.

Weekly's Response

Weekly was direct in her denial. She called Pannell's account "an outright lie," said she went past Pannell in the handshake line just as quickly as any other Red Raiders player, and said she did not even register where Pannell was standing in the line.

ABC then aired video of the handshake line during Sunday's Texas-Nebraska broadcast. Reporter Holly Rowe, who was positioned near the line at the time, said she heard Weekly say "good game" to each Texas Tech player. Based on the video, it was consistent with Weekly's account — though the footage was not conclusive enough to determine definitively whether any additional exchange occurred.

The Background

Understanding the tension requires knowing the history between these programs and individuals. Pannell played three seasons at Tennessee under Weekly before entering the transfer portal in June and announcing her transfer to Texas Tech. At the time, Weekly publicly alleged that Texas Tech had tampered in recruiting Pannell — a serious accusation that Pannell's new program denied.

That allegation has been part of the backdrop between the two programs since last summer. Pannell performed strongly at Texas Tech this season, and her path back to Devon Park on the opposite side of the handshake line from her former coach set up exactly the kind of emotionally charged moment the handshake controversy reflects.

Pannell's father, Brandon, added fuel to the social media conversation when he posted publicly after the incident, writing that "Karen Weekly was right that my kid did make a mistake and that was to play for her."

What's Next

The controversy continues to exist in the background as both programs move through the tournament. Tennessee plays Texas in today's noon ET semifinal. Texas Tech faces Alabama tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN.

The on-field games will eventually settle everything that matters. But the handshake controversy — a viral moment rooted in the transfer portal dynamics that increasingly define modern college softball — will remain part of the 2026 WCWS story regardless of how today's semifinals unfold. 🥎
