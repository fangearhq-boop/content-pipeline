Rematch: Tennessee and Texas Meet Again in the WCWS Semifinals

By Jordan Reeves | June 1, 2026

They played once at this Women's College World Series already. Tennessee won 2-1, sending Texas to the loser's bracket and raising questions about whether the defending champions could fight their way back. Today the Longhorns have their answer. They fought all the way back — and now they face the Lady Vols again, this time with the championship series on the line.

Tennessee vs. Texas. WCWS semifinal. Noon ET on ESPN. This is the rematch nobody was certain would happen, and the one that every softball fan now wants to see.

Two Very Different Roads to This Point

Tennessee's path has been the cleaner one. The Lady Vols opened with the 2-1 win over Texas, then dispatched Texas Tech 2-1 on Emma Clarke's walk-off solo home run in the ninth inning — the first WCWS walk-off in Tennessee program history. They have not trailed in either game at Devon Park, and they enter Monday's semifinal with 2-0 record and considerable momentum.

Texas has earned this rematch through sheer survival. After falling to Tennessee in the opener, the Longhorns beat Mississippi State in an elimination game where Teagan Kavan threw a 78-pitch complete-game shutout and recorded her 600th career strikeout on the final out. Then on Sunday, they were on the wrong side of Jordy Frahm's near no-hitter before Katie Stewart's three-run homer in the sixth inning turned the game around in a 3-1 victory that eliminated Nebraska and ended Frahm's career.

Texas has now won three of its last three games to reach this moment. Both wins since the Tennessee loss have been in elimination situations. The defending champions are battle-tested and alive.

The Ace Matchup

Karlyn Pickens is one of the most decorated pitchers in the country — a first-team All-American who has been Tennessee's backbone all season. In the Texas Tech game, she pitched 6.2 innings, allowing one earned run while striking out six, before Sage Mardjetko entered in relief and earned the win. Pickens has been asked to do a lot in this postseason, and she has delivered consistently.

Teagan Kavan arrives at this game fresh. Her 78-pitch outing against Mississippi State was efficient and dominant — 600th career K on the final pitch. She has since had time to rest while the Longhorns survived without her in the rotation. Kavan is the backbone of a Texas program that has won seven consecutive Super Regionals. She has been on this stage before.

Both pitchers are at their best in high-leverage situations. Whichever ace gets the better of the other will likely determine the outcome.

Clarke's Power Factor

Emma Clarke has become Tennessee's postseason X factor. The sophomore has four home runs in the 2026 NCAA tournament — tied for second most in the entire field — and her walk-off blast against Texas Tech last week showed exactly what she is capable of in the biggest moments. She entered that at-bat 0-for-3 on the night and still found a way to win the game.

If Pickens delivers a strong outing and Clarke continues her power surge, Tennessee's path to the championship series becomes a straightforward one. The Lady Vols have the arms and the bats to be here, and they have the psychological edge of having already beaten Texas this week.

What's Next

Today's noon ET game on ESPN decides which program advances to the WCWS championship series beginning Wednesday, June 3. The other semifinal — No. 1 Alabama vs. No. 11 Texas Tech — follows tonight at 7 PM on ESPN.

Tennessee won the first meeting. Texas won back its right to play for the title. Today, one program's season ends and one moves on to the final stage. The rematch is set. 🥎
