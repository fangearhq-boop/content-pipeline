Five Outs From History: Stewart's Homer Eliminates Nebraska, Ends Frahm's Career

By Sarah Mitchell | June 1, 2026

Jordy Frahm was five outs from the 20th no-hitter in Women's College World Series history. She had retired the first sixteen Texas batters she faced, and the defending champions were running out of time. Then the sixth inning arrived, and everything changed in the span of three at-bats.

Freshman Jaycie Nichols broke up the no-hit bid with an infield single. Kayden Henry followed with a single to right. And then Katie Stewart — the most dangerous bat in the Texas lineup — turned on a Frahm pitch and sent it over the left-field fence for a three-run home run that gave the Longhorns a 3-1 lead they would not relinquish.

Texas won 3-1. Nebraska is eliminated. And Jordy Frahm — one of the most accomplished pitchers in college softball history — played the final game of her career at Devon Park on Sunday.

The Near No-Hitter

What Frahm built through the first five-plus innings was something that appeared destined for the WCWS record books. The USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year and four-time NFCA first-team All-American was commanding every pitch, and the Texas lineup — armed with the home-run power of Stewart and the depth of a championship-caliber roster — was generating nothing.

Through 5.2 innings, not a single Texas batter had reached base. Frahm was perfect. The crowd at Devon Park could sense something historic was brewing, and Frahm seemed as sharp as she had been all season despite the accumulated fatigue of the postseason.

Then Nichols, a freshman, laced a single through the infield to end it. Just like that, the no-hit bid was gone. But the moment still belonged to Frahm — she had carried it as far as one could carry it in elimination circumstances.

Stewart Delivers the Blow

After Nichols' single, Kayden Henry kept the inning alive with a base hit of her own. With two on and nobody out, Stewart stepped in — and she sent Frahm's pitch deep to left field for a three-run homer, her 28th of the season.

The blast set a new Texas single-season program record and was the defining moment of the elimination game. Texas, which had been scoreless against Frahm to that point, had a 3-1 lead. The Longhorns did not look back.

Frahm had opened the game with her own home run — a solo shot in the first inning that was her 20th of the season, a remarkable number for a pitcher — but it wasn't enough in the end.

Frahm's Career Comes to a Close

Few careers in recent memory have combined pitching dominance and offensive production quite like Frahm's. The four-time NFCA first-team All-American spent two seasons at Oklahoma, where she won back-to-back national championships, before transferring to Nebraska for her final year.

At Nebraska, she was everything the program needed — a dual threat who hit 20 home runs while anchoring the staff. She led the Huskers on a 27-game winning streak before the WCWS, and her ability to compete in high-pressure moments was on full display even in Sunday's loss. Coming within five outs of a WCWS no-hitter, after throwing 133 pitches just three days earlier and two innings the night before, spoke to her competitive drive until the very end.

Nebraska's season ends at 52 wins. It was the program's first WCWS appearance since 2013 — a remarkable achievement by any standard, and one made possible largely because of what Frahm brought to Lincoln in her final year.

What's Next

Texas advances to today's noon ET semifinal on ESPN against Tennessee — a rematch of their opening-round WCWS meeting, which the Lady Vols won 2-1. Teagan Kavan, fresh off her 78-pitch complete-game shutout against Mississippi State, will start for the defending champion Longhorns.

Katie Stewart's program-record 28th home run is the moment that sent them there. Against the backdrop of Frahm's near no-hitter and the end of a legendary career, it will be remembered as one of the defining moments of the 2026 WCWS elimination round.
