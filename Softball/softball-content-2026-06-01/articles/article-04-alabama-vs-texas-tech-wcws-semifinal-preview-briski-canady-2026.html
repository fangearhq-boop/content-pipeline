No. 1 Alabama vs. No. 11 Texas Tech: The WCWS Semifinal Nobody Predicted

By Sarah Mitchell | June 1, 2026

When the 2026 WCWS bracket was set, this was not a semifinal anyone circled. No. 1 Alabama against No. 11 Texas Tech — the most dominant program in the tournament against the Cinderella that nobody saw coming. Tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN, that matchup becomes reality.

One team has been perfect since arriving in Oklahoma City. The other has survived multiple elimination games to reach the Final Four. Both believe they can win. Only one will.

Alabama's Dominant Case

The Crimson Tide have looked precisely like the No. 1 seed they are. In their two WCWS games, Alabama has not trailed once. They beat UCLA 6-3 in the opener, then dismantled Nebraska 5-1 as Jocelyn Briski threw a complete-game one-hitter — perfect through 3.2 innings, six strikeouts, zero walks. Marlie Giles drove in four runs against Nebraska (three-run home run in the first inning off Frahm, plus a sacrifice fly).

Briski is the SEC Pitcher of the Year with a 25-3 record — and she has been even better in the postseason than her season numbers suggest. The one-hitter against Nebraska was her fourth in her last six starts. She has thrown 16 complete games in 2026. There is no weak link in Alabama's staff, and the offense has punished every mistake.

Alabama entered the WCWS with more wins than any team in the field, and their performance in Oklahoma City has done nothing to dim that reputation. They are the clear favorite tonight.

Texas Tech's Cinderella Arc

No. 11 seeds are not supposed to reach national semifinals. Texas Tech has ignored that script entirely.

After beating Mississippi State in their WCWS opener, the Red Raiders fell to Tennessee 2-1 in nine innings, sending them to the loser's bracket. Since then, they have won two consecutive elimination games — including Sunday's 8-7 nine-inning thriller against UCLA, where NiJaree Canady's game-ending strikeout in the ninth sent the program to the Final Four for the first time.

Canady has not been at her statistical best at this WCWS — her ERA in Oklahoma City does not reflect the pitcher who dominated all season — but she has delivered when the moment is biggest. That combination of potential and proven will makes her dangerous in tonight's semifinal, even against the most complete team remaining in the bracket.

The Briski-Canady Showdown

Both pitchers have claims to being the finest arm in the country. Briski's consistency and complete-game efficiency have been the story of this tournament. Canady's reputation and raw ability, even while struggling, command respect from every opponent.

If Canady can find the form that gave her a 1.62 season ERA, Alabama's offense — formidable as it is — faces a genuine challenge. If Briski is the pitcher she has been in Oklahoma City, Texas Tech will need their offense to carry more weight than it has so far in elimination play.

What's Next

The winner tonight advances to the WCWS championship series beginning Wednesday, June 3. The loser's season ends at Devon Park.

Alabama has been the best team in college softball all year. Texas Tech has been the best story at this WCWS. Tonight they share a field, and only one survives to play for the national title. 🥎
