The Handshake Heard Around the WCWS — Weekly vs. Pannell (Tier 2)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The WCWS had its biggest off-field moment of the tournament Saturday night — and it happened in the postgame handshake line. Texas Tech third baseman Taylor Pannell — who played three seasons at Tennessee before transferring to Texas Tech last summer — said after the Red Raiders' 2-1 elimination loss to Tennessee that Lady Vols coach Karen Weekly told her in the handshake line that she "made a mistake" by leaving Tennessee. Weekly responded directly: she called it "an outright lie," said she went just as fast past Pannell as any other player, and didn't even know where Pannell was in the line. ABC then aired video during Sunday's Texas-Nebraska broadcast. Reporter Holly Rowe, standing near the handshake line, said she heard Weekly say "good game" to each Red Raiders player. The video itself was inconclusive. Pannell's father, Brandon, entered the conversation on social media, posting that "Karen Weekly was right that my kid did make a mistake and that was to play for her." The backstory adds context: Weekly publicly alleged tampering when Pannell transferred from Tennessee to Texas Tech. That accusation has been part of the narrative between these programs ever since. The story continues tonight — the two programs' rivalry doesn't disappear just because it's Alabama in the other dugout. Tennessee plays Texas in the noon semifinal. Texas Tech faces Alabama at 7 PM. And the drama of the past 48 hours hangs over the day at Devon Park. 🥎 ### Image Post Caption She said "you made a mistake." The coach says that's a lie.
