25 Seasons, One School: Frank Reed's Legacy at Chattanooga

By Emily Rawlings June 28, 2026

A Quarter Century at One School

Frank Reed retired from the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga on June 22, 2026. He had been the head softball coach there for 25 seasons. That number is worth sitting with for a moment before moving on to anything else.

Twenty-five years at one school in Division I athletics is exceptional by any standard. The average tenure for a head coach has compressed across every sport as the profession has become more volatile -- earlier departures, more lateral moves, more programs willing to make a change after a single bad season. Staying at one place for a quarter century through all of that requires a specific kind of commitment to a program and its players that goes beyond any individual won-loss record.

What a 25-Year Tenure Actually Means

Reed's career at Chattanooga spanned multiple roster generations. He recruited and developed players who went through the program in the early 2000s, coached through the NIL era's arrival, navigated the transfer portal's expansion, and finished his tenure in an offseason defined by the loudest transfer activity the sport has ever seen. He was there through all of it, at the same school, with the same commitment to the same program.

The mid-major level of college softball does not generate the recruiting coverage of the Power Four. Programs like Chattanooga do not land five-star prospects or make the portal splashes that drive national news cycles. What they do is develop players over four-year careers, build program identity through consistency, and provide the institutional stability that makes the sport function at every level below the national headlines. Reed spent 25 seasons doing exactly that.

The June Coaching Carousel Closes

Reed's retirement was part of a broader closing of the June coaching carousel across college softball. Abilene Christian hired Larry Hays on June 23. Augustana named Colleen Powers on June 22. Southern Virginia elevated assistant Kolby Kemp to head coach. Arizona State added Mitch Roberts from St. Cloud State as an assistant to round out their staff.

None of those hires generated the attention of a Power Four opening. But for the programs involved, and for the players who committed before the transition, they are significant. A new coaching staff changes the trajectory of a program in ways that take full recruiting cycles to fully understand. The players who chose those schools because of the previous staff now begin their relationships with new coaches.

What's Next

Chattanooga will conduct a search to replace Reed and begin building whatever comes after a 25-year era. The program he handed over is more defined and more established than it would have been without a sustained commitment at the top. For the rest of the coaching carousel, the new staffs at Abilene Christian, Augustana, Southern Virginia, and Arizona State will spend the summer beginning their recruiting outreach and learning their rosters. The June calendar is officially closed. The summer preview season starts now.
