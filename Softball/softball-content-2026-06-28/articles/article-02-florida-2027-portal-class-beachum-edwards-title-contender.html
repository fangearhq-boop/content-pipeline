Florida Just Built a Monster: Beachum, Edwards, and the Gators' 2027 Title Case

By Emily Rawlings June 28, 2026

Two Additions That Change Everything

Florida lost three starters to graduation after the 2026 season. That kind of roster attrition would put most programs in a rebuilding posture heading into the following year. What Tim Walton did instead was go get two of the five best transfer portal players in the country and bring them both to Gainesville.

Jaysoni Beachum hit .417 with 16 home runs and 66 RBIs for Florida State in 2026. Tori Edwards hit .383 with 18 home runs and 73 RBIs for LSU in 2026 while earning 2025 SEC Freshman of the Year honors and a spot on the NFCA All-American watch list. They are both power-hitting corner players with elite on-base skills. Add Duke catcher Kairi Rodriguez and Florida did not rebuild after losing three starters. They upgraded.

The Beachum Angle

Beachum's commitment to Florida carries an extra layer because of where she came from. She grew up in the Tallahassee area and played her first three seasons at Florida State -- the program closest to home, the program she built her early college career with. Choosing the Gators over any other option, and specifically over the program that recruited her out of high school, is the kind of in-state flip that matters beyond the stat line.

FSU now has to replace a .417 hitter who ranked among the SEC and ACC's most productive bats. They will. But the fact that Beachum went to Gainesville -- and not somewhere neutral -- adds to Florida's recruiting narrative in a way that sticks.

What Edwards Brings

Tori Edwards is two years into what already looks like an All-American career. She won the SEC Freshman of the Year award in 2025 and followed it with a 2026 season that put her in the conversation for the sport's best corner hitter. At LSU, she posted a .383 average with 73 RBIs across a full SEC schedule. That number puts her among the most productive run producers in the country.

She is a contact hitter with real power, which is the combination that moves the needle in postseason brackets. Florida's lineup was productive before her arrival. With Edwards slotted into the middle of the order alongside Beachum, the Gators can put together multi-run innings in a way that few programs outside of Texas can match.

The One Question Left

Florida's offensive case for 2027 is easy to make. The open question is pitching. Walton addressed the offense directly and completely. The rotation depth that carries a team through a WCWS bracket -- especially the kind of bracket pressure that Texas, Florida, and Texas Tech will all face -- depends on arms, not bats.

Florida's pitching picture heading into 2027 is less settled than its lineup. That is the one variable that could separate the Gators from a legitimate national title run. The offensive ceiling is there. Whether the staff can match it is the question the sport will spend all summer trying to answer.

What's Next

Florida closed its portal class before June 22 and enters fall practice as a legitimate top-three program nationally. The pre-portal recruiting class ranked in the top four per On3. The Beachum and Edwards additions push that class into the top-two conversation. Whether the Gators can close the gap on Texas in Oklahoma City is what the 2027 season will ultimately answer. For now, Walton's program has done everything it can do in the offseason to make that question worth asking.
