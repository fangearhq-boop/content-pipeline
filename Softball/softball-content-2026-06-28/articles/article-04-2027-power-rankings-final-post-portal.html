2027 Power Rankings: Texas Still No. 1 -- But Florida Just Made It Interesting

By Sarah Mitchell June 28, 2026

The Portal Closed. Now the Rankings Are Real.

The transfer portal window closed June 22. Coaching staffs have finalized their assistants. The rosters that will compete in the 2027 Women's College World Series are largely set. These are the definitive post-portal power rankings for college softball -- the best look we will have until fall camp opens in August.

No. 1: Texas Longhorns

Texas is No. 1 and it is not particularly close. Isa Torres won the D1 Player of the Year award in 2026 and is returning for another season. Katie Stewart hit 31 home runs in 2026 and moves to first base. Teagan Kavan is a two-time WCWS Most Outstanding Player. Samantha Swan comes in from Arizona State with a .383 average, 12 home runs, and 55 RBIs.

The Longhorns also finalized their coaching staff with Amber Freeman from Arizona joining to lead the offense and Rick Fremin coming in from SLU where he went 46-16. Pitching coach Pattie Ruth Taylor stays. This is not a roster that needs to prove it belongs. It is a roster built to win three in a row in Oklahoma City.

No. 2: Texas Tech Red Raiders

Texas Tech finished as the WCWS runner-up in 2026 and went into the portal with a mission. Softball America called their haul the No. 1 portal class in the country this cycle -- Polar, Heavener, and Pickering are three All-Americans arriving in one window. Pitcher Canady remains through graduation, which gives the Red Raiders a legitimate ace to build around in a seven-game bracket.

The question for Tech is whether a portal class assembled this quickly can build the kind of chemistry that sustains deep tournament runs. The talent is elite. The integration is the variable.

No. 3: Florida Gators

Florida entered this offseason facing graduation losses at three starter spots. They leave it with Jaysoni Beachum (.417/16 HR/66 RBI from FSU) and Tori Edwards (.383/18 HR/73 RBI from LSU) in the same lineup. Add Duke catcher Kairi Rodriguez and Tim Walton's program has assembled one of the most dangerous offensive cores in the sport.

The open question is pitching depth. The Gators did not make the kind of splashy rotation additions that their lineup additions generated. Whether their pitching staff can carry them through a WCWS bracket is the one question that separates them from Texas on paper.

No. 4: Nebraska Cornhuskers

Nebraska went 51-6 in 2026, reached their first Women's College World Series in 13 years, and have not lost the core of what made them dangerous. They added Boutté from Ohio State to a young roster that already proved it could compete at the WCWS level. The Cornhuskers are the team most likely to be underrated by analysts who saw their performance and assumed it was a one-year peak.

No. 5: South Carolina Gamecocks

South Carolina did not make the loudest portal moves of the offseason. They made targeted ones. The Gamecocks return experienced pieces and assembled a class designed to address specific roster needs rather than chase headlines. That kind of surgical approach to roster construction is how programs build toward sustained postseason relevance rather than one-year surges.

What's Next

These rankings reflect what each program looks like now, with the information available after the portal closed. Fall camp opens in August and October exhibitions will give the first look at how the new additions actually fit. The 2027 preseason polls open in January. Between now and then, watch for late portal movement if eligibility situations change, coaching staff additions, and the first recruiting rankings for the 2027 high school class. The race begins when fall practice opens.
