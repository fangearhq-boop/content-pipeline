Tia Milloy Is Still Uncommitted. Here's Why That Makes Sense.

By Sarah Mitchell June 28, 2026

More Than 30 Days Without a Decision

The transfer portal window opened June 8 and closed June 22. Tia Milloy entered the portal on May 25 — more than two weeks before the window even opened. As of June 28, she has been in the portal for over 30 days and has not announced a commitment anywhere. In a college softball offseason that moves at a relentless pace, that kind of deliberate approach stands out.

Milloy is a utility player who appeared in 41 games for Oklahoma in 2026 with two years of eligibility remaining. She is left-handed. She can play multiple positions. And she comes from a program that has produced multiple professional players and first-team All-Americans over the past decade. Those credentials don't diminish while she waits. If anything, the longer she takes, the more it signals that she is making a serious decision rather than a reactive one.

The Programs in the Hunt

Florida State has been the most publicly connected program from the start. The Seminoles lost Isa Torres and Jaysoni Beachum in the same offseason. They added Ella Dodge from Tennessee and Nicole Edmiaston through the portal. But a utility player with experience at a power conference program — someone who can play multiple positions and start at any of them — is exactly what FSU's 2027 roster needs to remain relevant after two significant departures.

Tennessee has its own case. The Vols return Jayda Mardjetko for 2027 and have roster flexibility that programs like Texas and Texas Tech currently don't have. Head coach Karen Weekly has built one of the more stable programs in the SEC and has a compelling offer for any player who wants consistent postseason appearances without the pressure of chasing a dynasty label.

Texas Tech rounds out the main field. The Red Raiders already assembled what Softball America called the No. 1 portal class in the country this cycle — Polar, Heavener, and Pickering are three All-Americans arriving in one window. Adding Milloy would give them utility depth that their current class doesn't address. But the question is whether Tech still has scholarship room and whether the fit makes sense given how much star power is already in Lubbock.

What Her Patience Tells Us

The most important detail in the Milloy recruitment is the one that's easy to overlook: she didn't commit during the portal window. That means she's not being driven by the kind of urgency that closed several high-profile recruitments in the first week of June. She is evaluating each school on her own terms, not responding to any external clock.

Two years of eligibility changes the calculation significantly. A player with one year remaining takes the best fit available because the window is narrow. Two years means this decision shapes the back half of her college career. Programs get her for a full spring season of evaluation before she would even need to consider another move. That makes the destination more important, not less.

What's Next

Milloy's commitment, whenever it comes, will land in one of the highest-profile roster spots available in the sport this summer. July brings fall camp previews and the PGF Nationals recruiting evaluation period. Programs that haven't filled roster gaps by then will feel the urgency. Watch for an announcement before the end of July, when the summer recruiting calendar shifts from portal to showcase season and attention moves to the 2027 high school class.
