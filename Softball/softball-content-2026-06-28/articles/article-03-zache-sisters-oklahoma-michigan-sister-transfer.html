Better Together: The Zache Sisters Choose Michigan Over Going Their Separate Ways

By Jordan Reeves June 28, 2026

The Decision

Berkeley Zache and Riley Zache both played for Oklahoma in 2026. Both entered the transfer portal when the window opened in June. Both committed to Michigan -- at the same time, to the same program, for the same fall camp.

That almost never happens. Siblings playing together at the same college program is already uncommon. Siblings transferring to the same new destination simultaneously requires two roster spots to open at the right moment, two coaches who want both players as a package, and both players choosing that same school over whatever other options were on the table. The Zache sisters navigated all of that and landed in Ann Arbor together.

What Michigan Gets

Michigan adds two players from a program that spent weeks at No. 1 in the national rankings during 2026. Oklahoma's season ended at the super regional stage -- short of expectations given the talent on the roster -- but the standard the Sooners hold their players to is among the highest in college softball. Anyone who played consistent minutes for that program brings Big 12 experience and preparation that transfers directly to the Big Ten level.

For the Wolverines, two additions from Oklahoma in a single portal cycle is a signal of recruiting trajectory. Michigan has been building its program toward consistent postseason appearances. Adding players with Big 12 experience and a familiarity with competing against the sport's best programs gives them exactly the kind of roster depth they need to take a step forward in 2027.

What Oklahoma Loses

The Zache sisters represent another piece of portal movement for Oklahoma after a season that ended earlier than expected in the super regionals. The Sooners have experienced roster turnover before and have proven the ability to reload from within and through recruiting. The staff there has built one of the sport's most reliable pipelines for player development.

But each departure adds to the question of how Oklahoma rebuilds the depth that made them a national title contender through most of the 2026 spring. The sister transfer is not a crisis for the program. It is a real roster adjustment that the Sooner staff will need to address heading into fall camp.

The Human Side of the Portal

The transfer portal is transactional by design. Players evaluate programs on scholarship offers, playing time, roster fit, and coaching relationships. The decision calculus is professional even at the college level. What the Zache sisters did is something different.

They stayed together. Through the decision to leave, through the recruiting process, and through the commitment itself. That kind of continuity -- maintaining the relationship that matters most while also starting over in a new program -- is not something that happens by accident. It requires both players to genuinely want the same thing at the same time. This one was personal, and it shows.

What's Next

Berkeley and Riley Zache will report to Michigan for fall camp and begin integrating into a Wolverines program that just added two Big 12 veterans in one move. Their first full season in Ann Arbor will come in spring 2027 when the Big Ten schedule opens and Michigan tests itself against the conference's top programs. For Oklahoma, the roster rebuild continues through the summer recruiting calendar and whatever portal activity still develops before fall. For the Zache sisters, the next chapter starts together.
