### STORY 1: Tia Milloy Still Uncommitted — The Transfer Race Entering Week Five

**Long-Form Post**
The transfer portal closed on June 22. Tia Milloy has been in it since May 25.

That is more than 30 days in the portal with no announcement — and in a sport that moves fast in the summer, that kind of patience means something. Milloy is a left-handed utility player from Oklahoma with two years of eligibility remaining. She appeared in 41 games for the Sooners in 2026. She is not a program piece who is still figuring out where she fits. She knows exactly what she offers.

FSU is the program most openly connected to her. The Seminoles lost Isa Torres and Jaysoni Beachum this offseason and added Ella Dodge and Nicole Edmiaston. But the roster gap that Milloy fills specifically — an experienced utility player who can slide into multiple positions — is real. Tennessee and Texas Tech have also been linked to her at various points.

The more interesting question is what the slow play tells us. When top players take longer than expected to commit, it usually means one of two things: they are genuinely torn between programs, or they are waiting for a specific situation to change. Milloy has leverage. Two years of eligibility does that.

Whoever lands her makes a significant roster addition. Watch for movement on this one as July camps begin.

### STORY 2: Florida's 2027 Portal Class Is Complete — Beachum and Edwards Make Walton a Title Contender

**Long-Form Post**
Tim Walton needed a response after losing three starters to graduation. What he put together is one of the most impressive portal hauls the sport has seen in a single offseason.

First came Jaysoni Beachum from FSU — .417 batting average, 16 home runs, 66 RBIs, and a .549 on-base percentage in 2026. Then came Tori Edwards from LSU — .383, 18 home runs, 73 RBIs, the 2025 SEC Freshman of the Year and an NFCA All-American. Both are power-hitting corner players with elite on-base skills. Put them in the same lineup and you do not need to wonder about Florida's offensive ceiling for 2027.

Beachum's commitment had its own drama — she grew up in the Tallahassee area and was being pursued by FSU before choosing Florida. That kind of in-state flip matters for recruiting momentum.

The open question for Florida is pitching. Walton's offensive additions are clear. The rotation depth that would carry Florida through a WCWS bracket is less certain.

Florida was a top-four recruiting class per On3 before the portal closed. With Beachum and Edwards added, they are a legitimate top-three program heading into 2027. Whether they can close the gap on Texas in Oklahoma City is the question the whole sport will be watching.

### STORY 3: The Sister Transfer — Berkeley and Riley Zache Leave Oklahoma for Michigan

**Long-Form Post**
The transfer portal is not always about rankings and recruiting rankings. Sometimes it is about family.

This week, Berkeley Zache and Riley Zache committed to the University of Michigan — together. The sisters both played for Oklahoma in 2026, both entered the transfer portal after the Sooners' season ended, and both chose the same destination. That almost never happens. Transferring with a sibling to the same program requires two roster spots to open at the right time, two coaches to want both players, and both players to want the same school over all other options.

For Michigan, this is a meaningful addition. Two players from a program that was ranked No. 1 at various points in 2026 give the Wolverines Big 12 experience and immediate roster depth.

For Oklahoma, it is more portal movement after a season that ended short of expectations in the super regionals. The Sooners have navigated portal turnover before — the rebuilding process under their staff continues.

The Zache sisters' story is a reminder that the people in these programs have real relationships. The portal is transactional by nature. This one was personal.

### STORY 5: June Coaching Carousel Closes — Frank Reed Retires After 25 Seasons

**Long-Form Post**
Frank Reed coached softball at the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga for 25 seasons. He retired June 22.

That is not a detail that should scroll past without pause. Twenty-five years at one school in college athletics is exceptional. The average tenure for a Division I head coach across all sports has shrunk significantly in recent years. Staying at one program through roster cycles, conference alignment changes, NIL's arrival, and the transfer portal era requires commitment to a place that goes beyond any single win total.

The closing of the June coaching carousel also brought several mid-major changes. Abilene Christian hired Larry Hays on June 23. Augustana named Colleen Powers on June 22. Southern Virginia elevated assistant Kolby Kemp to head coach. Arizona State added Mitch Roberts from St. Cloud State as an assistant.

None of those moves will get the coverage of the Power Four hires. But they matter for the programs involved — and for the players who committed to those schools before the new coach arrived.

Reed's 25-year run deserves its own moment. What he built at Chattanooga over a quarter century is the kind of sustained commitment the sport relies on at every level to grow.
