**Alabama vs. Tennessee Game 3 Tonight — SEC Title Implications on the Line**

### STORY 2:
**Kendall Wells One Home Run From NCAA All-Time Record**
Oklahoma freshman Kendall Wells hit her 36th home run of the season Sunday in OU's 3-1 win over No. 11 Georgia (Miali Guachino 13-1). Wells needs just one more to tie Lauren Espinoza's all-time NCAA single-season record of 37 home runs, set by Arizona in 1995 — a mark that has stood for 31 years. As a freshman, Wells already broke the NCAA freshman HR record (HR No. 31, April 11 vs. Texas). Oklahoma is 45-6, 17-3 SEC. The Sooners have five regular-season games remaining.
**Tier:** 1 | **Follow-up:** Wells HR No. 37 — all-time record watch; OU remaining schedule

### STORY 3:
**Katie Stewart Breaks Texas Program HR Record in Kentucky Sweep**
Texas No. 4 swept Kentucky over the weekend, with junior first baseman Katie Stewart hitting her 24th home run Sunday to break Reese Atwood's single-season Texas program record of 23 (set in 2025). Stewart also hit her 23rd on Saturday via a go-ahead grand slam in the seventh. Texas won 6-1 Saturday and 11-2 Sunday for the sweep (3-0 in series). Head coach Mike White was ejected during the series. Texas is now 40-9 overall, 14-7 SEC. Stewart leads the SEC in home runs.
