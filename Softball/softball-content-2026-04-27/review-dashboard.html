### Long-Form Post Window: 11:00 AM ET 🏠 ONE HOME RUN FROM HISTORY Lauren Espinoza set the all-time NCAA single-season home run record with 37 home runs for Arizona in 1995 — 31 years ago. No one has touched it since. Until now, maybe. Oklahoma freshman Kendall Wells hit her 36th home run of the season Sunday in the Sooners' 3-1 win over No. 11 Georgia. Just one home run separates her from the most celebrated record in college softball history. What makes this even more remarkable: Wells already owns the NCAA freshman home run record (HR No. 31, April 11 vs. Texas). She's doing things no first-year player has ever done, in a season that has No. 1 Oklahoma at 45-6 and firmly in control of their national championship destiny. If Wells hits No. 37, she'll join Espinoza as the only player in Division I softball history to hit that many in a single season. And she'll do it as a freshman — someone who hadn't played a single college game before February. Oklahoma still has five regular-season games remaining, plus conference and postseason play. Wells has plenty of chances. The record is right there. Will she get it? #Sooners #OUSoftball #CollegeSoftball #MakeHistory --- ### Image Post Caption Window: 11:00 AM ET 36 home runs.
