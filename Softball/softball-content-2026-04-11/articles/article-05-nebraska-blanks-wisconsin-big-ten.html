Nebraska Blanks Wisconsin 5-0 for 10th Shutout — The Big Ten's Quiet Powerhouse Is Real

By Emily Rawlings | April 11, 2026

While college softball's biggest headlines came from the SEC and ACC on Friday night, Nebraska went quietly to work in Madison, Wisconsin — and the results deserve attention.

The Huskers blanked Wisconsin 5-0 in Game 1 of their Big Ten road series, recording their 10th shutout of the season and improving to 31-6 overall and 12-1 in conference play. Freshman Alexis Jensen started in the pitcher's circle and kept the Badgers off the board in a dominant showing. Nebraska leads the Big Ten and is making a compelling case as a legitimate national title contender heading toward May.

What 10 Shutouts in 37 Games Means

Let's put that number in context. Nebraska has played 37 games this season. In 10 of them, their opponents have been completely shut out — held to zero runs for an entire game. That's a shutout rate approaching 27 percent of their games.

Achieving that in college softball, where hitters are skilled and lineups deep, requires more than one great pitcher. It requires a staff with real depth and consistent execution. Nebraska has both. Jensen's performance Friday against a Wisconsin team that had won eight of their previous 10 games is evidence that the Huskers aren't relying on a single arm to carry them.

The 12-1 conference record also tells the story. Nebraska has been playing Big Ten opponents week after week, and they have dominated. Only one conference loss in 13 games is the kind of sustained excellence that earns national seed consideration.

Jensen and the Big Ten Race

Alexis Jensen's emergence as a reliable starter is one of the storylines of Nebraska's season. Starting a freshman in a Big Ten road game — against a Wisconsin team that had been competitive all year — and watching her deliver a shutout speaks to both Jensen's talent and the Huskers' trust in her development.

Nebraska coaches have built their pitching staff the right way: developing young arms, giving them big moments early, and letting them grow into their roles. That approach is producing results. Jensen on Friday night was the latest example.

Wisconsin entered the series having won eight of their last 10 games, so this was no cupcake opponent. The Badgers had momentum and home-field advantage. Nebraska came in, pitched a shutout, and left with an early series lead.

The National Picture for Nebraska

Nebraska is currently ranked in the top 10 nationally across multiple polls. Their 31-6 record and 12-1 Big Ten mark put them firmly in the conversation for a national seed — potentially a top-eight seed — when the NCAA selection committee meets in May.

The Huskers don't get the same national media attention as Texas Tech, Oklahoma, or Texas because the Big Ten doesn't carry the same softball profile as the SEC or Big 12. But the numbers don't care about conference prestige. Nebraska is winning at a rate that demands respect.

Their schedule through May will need to hold up under scrutiny, but the body of work is already impressive. Ten shutouts. A 12-1 conference record. Thirty-one wins. A freshman starter throwing zero runs in Madison against a hot opponent.

Nebraska is real. And they're just getting started.

What's Next

The Huskers play Game 2 of the Wisconsin series on Saturday at noon CT in Madison, followed by the series finale on Sunday. A series win would extend their Big Ten dominance and keep their national seed profile strong heading into the final month of the regular season.

Nebraska is the team that doesn't always make the highlight reel — until the NCAA tournament. This spring, they're building quietly toward a deep run. Friday's shutout in Madison is just the latest piece of that puzzle.
