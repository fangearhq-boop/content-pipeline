Stanford Snaps FSU's 25-Game Winning Streak in Late-Game Thriller

By Sarah Mitchell | April 11, 2026

The longest active winning streak in college softball is over.

No. 23 Stanford ended Florida State's 25-game run on Friday night in Palo Alto, holding off a late Seminole rally to hand No. 9 FSU its first loss since February. The result stunned a college softball world that had watched the Seminoles roll through opponents all spring — and immediately reshuffled the national conversation about who the best teams in the country really are.

Florida State was not going down without a fight. The Seminoles staged a comeback in the final innings, with Ashtyn Danley delivering a bases-clearing double that gave FSU a brief lead. For a moment, it looked like the streak would survive on the road. It didn't. Stanford answered, protected their lead, and secured the biggest home win in their program this season.

How the Streak Was Built

To understand the magnitude of Friday's loss, consider what FSU had done to get to 25 wins in a row. The Seminoles opened the season on a tear, beating ranked opponents and lesser competition with equal authority. Isa Torres, batting .610 at the start of the road trip — a figure that leads all of Division I softball — has been one of the sport's most dominant individual performers. The pitching staff has been equally impressive, shutting down opposing lineups week after week.

The 25-game streak climbed into the top five in FSU program history. It had become the signature storyline of their season, a number that grew with every game and put the Seminoles in the national title conversation despite playing in the ACC while other top programs battle through the SEC and Big 12.

Stanford, to their credit, came prepared. The Cardinal entered the series ranked No. 23 nationally and playing with home-field advantage — a combination that proved decisive.

What This Means for the Rankings

FSU entered the weekend at No. 5 in the NFCA Top 25 Coaches Poll and No. 9 in the ESPN/USA Softball rankings. A road loss to a ranked team will cost them votes in Tuesday's Week 10 poll, likely knocking them out of the top five and opening the door for Alabama, Nebraska, or another contender to move up.

The bigger question is whether this loss changes how the Seminoles are viewed as a tournament team. The honest answer: it shouldn't. A single loss — especially on the road against a top-25 program — does not define a season. FSU's overall body of work remains excellent, and their roster still features one of the most talented collections of players in college softball.

What it does do is give the Seminoles a data point they lacked coming in. For all the wins during the streak, there were questions about whether FSU had been tested enough. Stanford provided an answer Friday night. Now the Seminoles have film on how a good team can beat them, and they can use it.

Torres and the Bigger Picture

Even in a loss, Isa Torres' presence makes FSU dangerous. Her .610 batting average is not a typo — it leads all of Division I and makes her arguably the best pure hitter in college softball this season. When a team's leading hitter is getting on base in six of every ten plate appearances, opponents have to plan around her every single time.

The Seminoles will return to ACC play with a chip on their shoulder. They are not a team that loses often, and they have the talent to respond. The question now is how quickly they answer — and whether Saturday's Game 2 in Palo Alto provides the immediate bounce-back the fanbase needs.

What's Next

FSU and Stanford play Game 2 on Saturday and conclude the series on Sunday before the Seminoles head home. These road games represent FSU's chance to salvage the series and prove that Friday was an aberration rather than a warning sign.

In the larger picture, FSU still has their NCAA tournament résumé largely intact. One loss to a ranked road opponent will not define their seeding conversation. But the Seminoles now face the rest of the season with something they haven't had since February: the weight of defeat. How they carry it will say a great deal about who they are as a team.
