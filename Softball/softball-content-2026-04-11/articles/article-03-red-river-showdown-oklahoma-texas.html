Red River Showdown Heats Up: Oklahoma vs. Texas Is College Softball's Marquee Weekend

By Jordan Reeves | April 11, 2026

Every year, the Oklahoma-Texas softball series produces at least one moment the sport talks about all season. This year, the stakes are higher than ever.

No. 2 Oklahoma arrived in Austin on Friday to open a three-game series against No. 4 Texas — the reigning national champions — with the SEC title, national seeding implications, and Kendall Wells' home run record chase all packed into one weekend. Game 1 began with the Sooners immediately pressuring the Longhorns, building a 3-0 lead in the late innings before a rain delay halted play. Game 2 is set for Saturday night at 7:00 PM ET on ESPN.

Oklahoma enters the series at 38-3 overall and 11-1 in SEC play, leading the conference. Texas is 32-4 overall and 9-3 in the SEC. A Sooner sweep would push their SEC lead to three full games. A Texas series win narrows the gap to one. The SEC title is not officially on the line — there's still a lot of conference play remaining — but the psychological and seeding weight of this series is immense.

The Rivalry Context

Oklahoma leads the all-time series with Texas 48-14, a number that reflects decades of Sooner softball dominance. But this version of Texas is different. The Longhorns won their first Women's College World Series (WCWS) title in 2025, ending their own long wait for a national championship, and they did it with a team that has returned most of its core pieces in 2026.

Texas is 17-1 at McCombs Field this season. Home-field advantage is real, and the Longhorns know how to use it. Their pitching staff — the group that held elite lineups at bay through a full WCWS run in 2025 — now has to find a way to slow down the most explosive freshman in college softball history.

Kendall Wells enters the series with 30 home runs and needs seven more to break the all-time NCAA single-season record. She's already broken the SEC single-season record and tied the NCAA freshman record. Every at-bat she gets against Texas pitching is a storyline unto itself.

What the SEC Lead Actually Means

Oklahoma's 11-1 SEC record reflects one of the most dominant conference stretches in recent memory. Their two losses across the entire season came against ranked programs, and their league schedule has included plenty of quality opponents. They've played like the best team in the SEC all spring.

Texas has also been outstanding, but they've dropped three conference games — all against top-10 competition. The Longhorns' strength lies in their pitching and their ability to grind out wins in tight games. They're 17-1 at home for a reason. This is a team that knows how to win in Austin.

The series could go either way. Oklahoma has the offensive firepower to score in bunches. Texas has the pitching and the home-field experience to keep games close. Both teams are legitimate national title contenders, and both know it.

The ESPN Audience

All three games of the Oklahoma-Texas series are on ESPN family of networks — ESPN2 on Friday, ESPN on Saturday and Sunday. That means the national softball audience gets to see this in prime time, exactly the kind of visibility the sport has earned after years of growing viewership.

A prime-time ESPN audience for college softball in April is not something that would have been guaranteed a decade ago. It reflects both the sport's growth and the specific magnetism of this rivalry. Oklahoma and Texas do not need a marketing push. The story sells itself.

What's Next

Game 2 of the Red River series tips off Saturday night at 7:00 PM ET on ESPN. Game 3 follows Sunday at 1:00 PM ET on ESPN. Whichever team wins two of three leaves Austin with a crucial series result — and the narrative momentum heading into the final stretch of the regular season.

Watch for Wells' at-bats in every game. Watch for the pitching matchups that decide who scores and who doesn't. And watch for the moments this rivalry always produces — because in Oklahoma-Texas softball, they always come.
