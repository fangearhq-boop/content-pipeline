Texas Tech Crushes Utah 10-2 on the Road, Canady Leads the Way

By Emily Rawlings | April 11, 2026

Some teams look different on the road. No. 1 Texas Tech is not one of them.

The Red Raiders traveled to Salt Lake City on Friday night and dismantled Utah 10-2, opening a six-game away stretch with the kind of dominant performance that has defined their entire season. Four home runs, 12 hits, and complete control from start to finish — Texas Tech never gave Utah a moment to breathe. The Red Raiders improved to 39-2 overall and 15-1 in Big 12 play with the win.

NiJaree Canady took the ball in the pitcher's circle and contributed on both sides of the game. The nation's most feared arm kept Utah off balance throughout, while Canady also came up with an RBI double in the third inning to help Texas Tech build their lead early. It was the kind of two-way performance that has made her the defining player of the 2026 college softball season.

The Offense Showed Up in Salt Lake City

Pitching has always been Texas Tech's calling card, but their lineup has quietly become one of the most dangerous in the country. Four home runs in a single road game against a competitive Mountain West opponent is not a given — the thin air might help, but the Red Raiders earned every hit.

The 12-hit attack was spread across the lineup, a sign of the depth that separates Texas Tech from most college softball programs. When every spot in the order can hurt you, pitching to contact becomes a losing strategy. Utah found that out the hard way Friday night.

The lopsided final score reflects just how complete this Texas Tech team is. They did not need to come from behind. They did not survive a late scare. They led from early, extended the margin steadily, and closed it out without drama. That's the hallmark of an elite program.

Canady and the No. 1 Seed Case

Before the road trip, NiJaree Canady entered at 15-2 with a 1.29 ERA — numbers that would have every other program's ace in the conversation for national pitcher of the year. At Texas Tech, she's almost expected. The sophomore right-hander has been dominant all season, named Big 12 Pitcher of the Week three times and reaching 1,000 career strikeouts in April. She became just the 102nd pitcher in NCAA Division I softball history to hit that mark, and she did it faster than nearly all of them.

Canady also threw three career no-hitters before the 2026 season — the first pitcher in Texas Tech program history to accomplish that. She's not just good. She's historic. And at 39-2, the team around her is giving her the support to be fully appreciated.

The case for Texas Tech earning the No. 1 national seed for the NCAA tournament keeps getting stronger. A 39-2 record with dominant road wins, led by the best pitcher in college softball — the selection committee has to notice.

The Six-Game Road Trip Continues

Texas Tech will face Utah again on Saturday and Sunday to complete the series before moving to their next road opponents. Going 6-0 on this stretch would be a significant statement — a top-ranked team away from home, handling business in hostile environments without missing a beat.

After seeing what they did in Game 1, it's hard to bet against the Red Raiders.

What's Next

Texas Tech plays Game 2 of the Utah series on Saturday afternoon, with Game 3 to follow Sunday. After the Utah series closes, the Red Raiders remain on the road for three more games. A perfect road trip would all but cement their status as the nation's premier team heading into May.

For the rest of college softball, the calculus is simple: Texas Tech at 39-2 and rolling is the standard everyone else is chasing. Right now, no one has found an answer.
