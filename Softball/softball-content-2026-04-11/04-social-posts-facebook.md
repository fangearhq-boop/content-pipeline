### STORY 1: FSU's 25-Game Win Streak Ends at Stanford

**Long-Form Post**
*Posting Window: 9:30 AM ET*

After 25 consecutive wins — the longest active streak in college softball — Florida State finally lost Friday night.

Stanford, ranked No. 23, held off a determined Seminole comeback on their home field in Palo Alto. FSU rallied and took a brief lead on a bases-clearing double by Ashtyn Danley, but the Cardinal answered to end the Noles' remarkable run.

For context: FSU had not lost a game since February. This was a streak that climbed into the top five in program history and was threatening even longer records.

The Seminoles are still one of the best teams in the country. Isa Torres is batting .610, their pitching staff is still elite, and they'll head into the rest of conference play with a real test now on their résumé. This might actually be good for them before the NCAA tournament.

But the streak is over — and that's genuinely stunning.

What do you think: Was this a wake-up call, or is FSU still a title contender?

### STORY 2: No. 1 Texas Tech Dominates Utah 10-2

**Long-Form Post**
*Posting Window: 12:00 PM ET*

No. 1 Texas Tech didn't waste any time making a statement on the road.

The Red Raiders traveled to Salt Lake City to open their six-game road trip and came away with a 10-2 demolition of Utah on Friday night. Four home runs. 12 hits. Total control. NiJaree Canady worked in the circle and added an RBI double herself.

Texas Tech is now 39-2 on the season with a 15-1 mark in Big 12 play. For perspective — their two losses are all that's keeping them from an undefeated season against one of the toughest schedules in the country.

Canady (15-2, 1.29 ERA heading into this road trip) is the defining player of the 2026 college softball season. She strikes out everyone, she handles the bat, and she makes her teammates better. Texas Tech winning 10-2 on the road like it's a home game is a testament to what she brings.

The Red Raiders are looking very much like a No. 1 national seed.

### STORY 4: Kendall Wells at 30 HRs

**Long-Form Post**
*Posting Window: 3:00 PM ET*

Kendall Wells is seven home runs away from rewriting the record books forever.

The Oklahoma freshman already has 30 home runs — an achievement that set the SEC single-season record and tied the NCAA freshman single-season record. She did it faster than anyone before her: No. 30 came in just her 40th game of the season.

Now she's chasing the all-time NCAA single-season mark of 37, set by Janie Reed Espinoza of Arizona back in 1995 — a record that has stood for 31 years.

Seven home runs might sound like a lot. But Wells is averaging better than a homer per every 1.5 games this season. She has the ability to get there.

The most exciting part? She gets to chase the record against Texas — the defending national champions — in the biggest series of the year. If Wells goes yard in Austin, the softball world stops.

Watch this weekend. You might be witnessing history.

### STORY 5: Nebraska Blanks Wisconsin 5-0

**Long-Form Post**
*Posting Window: 5:30 PM ET*

Nebraska might be the most underrated team in the country right now — and they keep proving it.

The Huskers shut out Wisconsin 5-0 on Friday night in Madison, recording their 10th shutout of the season and improving to 31-6 overall. In Big Ten play, they are an extraordinary 12-1. Freshman Alexis Jensen was excellent in the pitcher's circle.

Here's the thing about Nebraska that doesn't get enough national attention: 10 shutouts in 37 games is not a fluke. That's a pitching staff with real depth. And a 12-1 conference record against the Big Ten, which has become a legitimate softball power, is worth noting.

Nebraska isn't just running up numbers against weak opponents. They're dominating, and they're doing it consistently.

With regionals approaching in May, the Huskers are building the kind of profile that earns a top-16 national seed — and possibly a top-8.

Game 2 is today at noon CT in Madison. Keep watching.
