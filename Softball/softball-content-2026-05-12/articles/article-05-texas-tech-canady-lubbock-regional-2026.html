NiJaree Canady and Texas Tech Have Unfinished Business — The Lubbock Regional Starts Friday

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | May 12, 2026

Texas Tech went to the WCWS finals in 2025 and lost to Texas. Now they're hosting the Lubbock Regional for the second straight year with 50-plus wins and the same pitcher who made them one of the hardest teams to score on in the country.

Last June, Texas Tech walked into Devon Park for the Women's College World Series finals and faced Texas. They came up short. That loss, to the team that's now the No. 2 national seed and defending champion, is exactly the kind of result that defines what a program is chasing the following season.

Texas Tech spent the 2026 season chasing it. They finished with 50-plus wins, lost the Big 12 championship to No. 6 seed Arizona State in what qualified as one of the more surprising results of conference tournament weekend, and earned the No. 11 overall national seed. That earns them a home regional at Tracy Sellers Field in Lubbock for the second consecutive year.

NiJaree Canady pitches Friday afternoon at 3:30 PM against Marist. The road back to Devon Park starts at home.

Canady's Place in the Sport

Canady won the 2024 USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year. She's one of the few pitchers in college softball who opponents build entire game plans around, not just scouting reports but actual strategic decisions about lineup construction, base running aggressiveness, and when to take risks in the count. When Canady is right, scoring against Texas Tech isn't a plan, it's a hope.

This season she continued to log the kind of numbers that make her one of the elite pitchers in the country. The Red Raiders' pitching-first identity starts with her, and the teams that have succeeded against Texas Tech in recent years have done it by stringing together baserunners rather than waiting for one mistake pitch to drive over the fence.

The Lubbock Regional Field

Texas Tech hosts the Lubbock Regional at Tracy Sellers Field, with four teams in the bracket: Texas Tech, Ole Miss, Boston University, and Marist. Friday's schedule: Ole Miss vs. Boston University at 1 PM on ESPNU, then Texas Tech vs. Marist at 3:30 PM on ESPN+.

Ole Miss is the most dangerous team in the regional outside of Texas Tech. The Rebels have momentum heading in and are a program that knows how to beat top-seeded hosts on the road. Boston University and Marist earned automatic bids from their respective conferences and will need their best pitching to extend their tournaments past the first game.

An Ole Miss vs. Texas Tech matchup later in the regional bracket is the game the national audience will want to see from this field. If Ole Miss beats Boston University Friday and Texas Tech handles Marist, that showdown could come Sunday.

What a Title Run Would Mean

Texas Tech hasn't won a national championship yet. They've been close. They've built the roster, the pitching staff, and the program identity to make it happen. A run through Lubbock, into super regionals, and finally through the WCWS field would complete something the program has been building toward for years.

It would also complete something personal for Canady. The 2025 title went to Texas, the team that beat Texas Tech in the finals. If the Red Raiders win the 2026 championship, the narrative writes itself: the pitcher who should have been the champion came back the next year and finished what she started.

What's Next

Texas Tech opens the Lubbock Regional on Friday against Marist at 3:30 PM on ESPN+. If they advance out of the regional (May 15-17), they host a super regional May 22-24. A super regional win sends the Red Raiders to Devon Park in Oklahoma City for the Women's College World Series, beginning May 28.
