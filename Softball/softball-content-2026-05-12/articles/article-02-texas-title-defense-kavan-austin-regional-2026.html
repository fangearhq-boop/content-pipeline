Teagan Kavan and Texas Are Going for Back-to-Back — The Title Defense Begins Friday in Austin

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | May 12, 2026

Texas won the 2025 national championship, hired the SEC by winning the conference tournament this spring, and enters the 2026 bracket as the No. 2 seed with the same pitcher who dominated last year's WCWS.

The last time Texas lost in the NCAA Tournament was in the 2024 super regionals. Since then, the Longhorns won a national championship, joined the SEC, won the SEC Tournament, and returned as the No. 2 overall seed with a 42-10 record and the same ace who carried them to the title last June.

Teagan Kavan was named the Most Outstanding Player of the 2025 Women's College World Series. She earned it. Kavan pitched with the kind of composure under pressure that defines tournament pitchers, the ones who seem to get better when the stage gets bigger. Her SEC championship performance on May 9 confirmed she hasn't lost a step. Twelve strikeouts, a complete game, seven runs of support against Alabama. Texas won 7-1 and claimed the program's first SEC Tournament title.

Now she gets the ball again Friday. Austin Regional. Wagner in the first round. The title defense is on.

What Repeating Would Mean

Back-to-back national titles in college softball are uncommon enough to stand out in program history. Florida won consecutive titles in 2013 and 2014. Oklahoma won four straight from 2021 through 2024, which is the standard for modern sustained excellence. Before Oklahoma, multiple programs fell short of repeating after winning once.

Texas would be joining a very short list. They came to the SEC as a program that had never won a conference title, let alone a national one. Then they won the national title in Year 1. Now they're 42-10, they won the conference tournament, and they have the No. 2 seed. The Longhorns have built something in Austin.

Austin Regional Preview

Texas hosts the Austin Regional at McCombs Field beginning Friday, May 15. The bracket: Texas vs. Wagner, Wisconsin vs. Baylor. Wisconsin and Baylor open the day, with Texas playing in the second game. All four teams play a double-elimination format over three days, May 15-17.

Wagner earned an at-large bid and will be a massive underdog against Texas in the first round. Wisconsin was a solid Big Ten program that made the field on the strength of their conference tournament performance. Baylor is a conference rival that Texas knows well from their years together in the Big 12. None of these programs are expected to seriously challenge the Longhorns, but regional upsets happen every year for exactly the reason teams expect them not to.

Kavan as the Key

Everything for Texas runs through Kavan. She's not just a pitcher — she's the identity of the program right now. When she's on, Texas wins. Her career numbers going back to the 2025 WCWS show a pitcher who elevates in big moments rather than wilts. That's the hardest trait to find and the hardest to replace.

Texas also has enough offensive depth to support her. Katie Stewart batted .340 this season and has pop from the middle of the lineup. The Longhorns don't need to put 10 runs on the board every game to win. They need to give Kavan three or four and let her work.

What's Next

Texas opens the Austin Regional on Friday, May 15 against Wagner. If the Longhorns advance out of regionals, they host a super regional (May 22-24) in Austin. A super regional win sends them to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. The defending champion's path to a repeat goes through Austin first.
