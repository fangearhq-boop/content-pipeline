Megan Grant Has 38 Home Runs. Now She Wants a Title. UCLA's L.A. Regional Starts Friday.

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | May 12, 2026

Grant shattered a 31-year-old NCAA single-season home run record. Friday, she takes that power into the 2026 NCAA Tournament at Easton Stadium as the No. 8 Bruins host the L.A. Regional.

For 31 years, Laura Espinoza's record stood. The Arizona outfielder hit 37 home runs in 1995, and the number survived through three decades of the sport's evolution. Better athletes, longer seasons, stronger competition. Still 37.

Megan Grant hit number 38 in the Big Ten championship game. It was a solo shot to left-center off Nebraska pitcher Jordy Frahm in the third inning, and it broke a record that had been standing since the Clinton administration. Grant had tied the record with her 37th the day before in the semifinal against Wisconsin. She made sure 38 happened fast.

Now she's carrying that season into the NCAA Tournament. Friday, Easton Stadium. South Carolina and Cal State Fullerton in the field. Time to see if the best power season in NCAA history can end with a national title.

The Record in Context

Espinoza's 37 home runs in 1995 came over 72 games. Grant broke the record in 55 games. That's the pace element of the story that gets overlooked in the home run number. Grant didn't just tie and pass 37. She reached 38 in 17 fewer games than the previous record holder took to reach 37. The rate is historic, not just the total.

Grant's power comes from a short, quick swing with elite bat speed. She hits to all fields but does the most damage to left-center and center, where she can get full extension on pitches in the outer half. Pitchers who miss inside against her pay immediately. Those who miss outside and lose velocity pay just as quickly. There isn't a safe zone in the strike zone when she has a pitch to hit.

UCLA's L.A. Regional and the Bracket

UCLA earned the No. 8 national seed and will host Easton Stadium for the 12th consecutive regional. The L.A. Regional field: UCLA vs. Cal State Fullerton, South Carolina vs. California Baptist. Games begin Friday, May 15-17.

South Carolina is a capable team that earned an at-large bid out of the SEC. The Gamecocks have the pitching to keep games close. Cal State Fullerton and Cal Baptist earned automatic bids. UCLA is the clear favorite to advance, but a South Carolina-UCLA matchup deep in the regional bracket would be a genuine test.

As the No. 8 seed, UCLA would host a super regional if they advance. Their potential super regional opponent would come from the No. 9 seed's regional. That kind of path, playing at home all the way through super regionals, is exactly what a record-breaking offensive player needs to close out her college career.

Final College Run

Grant is in her final year at UCLA. Whatever happens from Friday forward in Westwood is the last stretch of her college career. Programs would love to send their record-breaker out with a title. Easton Stadium's atmosphere is one of the best in the sport for postseason play.

She hit 38 home runs this season. She broke a record that stood for 31 years. The last thing left to collect is a Women's College World Series ring.

What's Next

UCLA opens the L.A. Regional on Friday, May 15 against Cal State Fullerton. South Carolina and Cal Baptist play the other first-round game. The regional runs May 15-17, with the winner advancing to super regionals (May 22-24) at Easton Stadium. A super regional win sends UCLA to the Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28.
