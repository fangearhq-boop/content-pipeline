Their bracket: Virginia Tech vs. South Alabama (May 15, 4 PM ET), LSU vs. Akron (May 15, 6:30 PM ET). VT is widely considered the top upset pick of the opening regional round.
- **Texas title defense:** Texas is the reigning national champion (2025) and the No. 2 overall seed in 2026. Pitcher Teagan Kavan was the 2025 WCWS Most Outstanding Player. Kavan threw a complete-game 12-strikeout gem in the SEC championship win over Alabama on May 9. Texas enters at 42-10, having won the program's first SEC Tournament title. Austin Regional: Texas vs. Wagner, Wisconsin vs. Baylor, May 15.
- **Georgia momentum:** Georgia upset No. 1 Oklahoma 10-5 in the SEC Tournament quarterfinals on May 8 — the largest comeback win in SEC Tournament history. Georgia is the No. 10 overall seed and hosts the Athens Regional: Georgia vs. Charleston, Clemson vs. UNC Greensboro, May 15. Athens Regional winner plays Knoxville Regional winner in super regionals.
- **Megan Grant/UCLA:** Megan Grant hit her 38th home run in the Big Ten championship game, breaking the NCAA single-season record (previous record: 37, Laura Espinoza, Arizona, 1995). Grant broke the record in 55 games. UCLA is the No. 8 overall seed and hosts the L.A. Regional at Easton Stadium for a 12th straight year: UCLA vs. Cal State Fullerton, South Carolina vs. California Baptist, May 15-17.
- **Texas Tech/Canady:** Texas Tech hosts the Lubbock Regional at Tracy Sellers Field for the second straight year. The Red Raiders finished the 2026 season with 50-plus wins and were the 2025 national runners-up (lost to Texas in the WCWS). NiJaree Canady leads their pitching staff. Lubbock Regional: Texas Tech vs. Marist (3:30 PM, ESPN+), Ole Miss vs. Boston University (1 PM, ESPNU), May 15.
- **WCWS predictions:** Top contenders based on seeding and body of work: No. 1 Alabama (49-7, 1.60 ERA, 22 shutouts), No. 2 Texas (defending champ, Kavan returning), No. 3 Oklahoma (nation's best offense, .389 BA, 174 HRs), No. 4 Nebraska (45-6, Big Ten champ). Analysts also name Georgia and Texas Tech as dark horse picks.
