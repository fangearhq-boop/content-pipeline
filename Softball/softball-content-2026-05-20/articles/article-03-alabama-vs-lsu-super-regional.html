Alabama vs. LSU Super Regional: The SEC's No. 1 vs. No. 16 Showdown in Tuscaloosa

By Jordan Reeves

May 20, 2026

Alabama's pitching is the tournament's best. LSU just outscored three regional opponents 23-2. The matchup arrives Friday in Tuscaloosa.

On paper, No. 1 Alabama against No. 16 LSU reads like an obvious result. The top seed hosting the bottom seed in the Super Regionals should favor the Crimson Tide in nearly every analysis.

But seedings are just numbers. LSU just outscored three opponents 23-2 in the Baton Rouge Regional. The Tigers went 3-0 and controlled every game they played. This is not a No. 16 seed that stumbled into the Super Regionals — they arrived with momentum and a pitching staff capable of keeping any game competitive.

Alabama (52-7) hosts LSU (40-17) at Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa with Game 1 set for Friday, May 22 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 and Game 2 on Saturday at 3 PM on ESPN.

Alabama's Pitching Depth Sets Them Apart

The Crimson Tide's argument for being the best team in the country begins and ends with their pitching staff. Vic Moten was near-perfect in the regional — she carried a no-hitter into the fifth inning in Alabama's 9-0 win over USC Upstate in the regional final. She walked off the mound having thrown one of the most dominant regional performances of the entire tournament.

And Moten is not even Alabama's only elite arm. Jocelyn Briski, the SEC Pitcher of the Year, gives head coach Patrick Murphy a two-headed rotation that most programs can only dream about. Alabama can match any offensive lineup they face — and then hand the ball to pitchers who make that offense look ordinary.

That depth is why Alabama earned the No. 1 overall seed. It is why they are the favorite not just in this Super Regional, but in the entire WCWS field.

LSU's Path to an Upset

For LSU to advance, they will need to do something no team has been able to do consistently against Alabama's pitching this season: string hits together and manufacture runs early. The Tigers' 40-17 overall record and 13-11 SEC mark reflect a team that has had its share of difficult moments in conference play.

The intrigue of this Super Regional lies partly in its novelty: Alabama and LSU did not face each other during the 2026 regular season. Neither program has fresh film on the other from this year. In a sport where advance scouting matters enormously, both coaches will be working with some degree of uncertainty about what specific tendencies they're dealing with in 2026.

LSU has momentum, an opponent they have not faced this year, and nothing to lose. That combination has produced upsets before in the Super Regionals.

What's Next

Game 1 of the Alabama vs. LSU Super Regional is Friday, May 22 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. Game 2 is Saturday, May 23 at 3 PM on ESPN. A potential Game 3 would be Sunday, May 24. The series winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28.
