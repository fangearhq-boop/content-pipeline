Florida vs. Texas Tech Super Regional: The Gators' Fortress Meets the Nation's Winningest Program

By Emily Rawlings

May 20, 2026

Florida has gone 36-1 in home NCAA regionals since 2013. Texas Tech is 55-6. Something has to give in Gainesville.

Texas Tech softball enters the 2026 Super Regionals with 55 wins and only six losses — the most wins of any program in the country this season. The Red Raiders have been dominant, consistent, and capable of beating anyone on any given day. They have earned the right to be taken seriously.

They are also about to walk into the most hostile home environment in college softball.

Florida (51-10) hosts Texas Tech at Katie Seashole Pressly Stadium in Gainesville for the Super Regional, with Game 1 set for Friday, May 22 at 11 AM ET on ESPN2 and Game 2 on Saturday at 12:30 PM on ABC. The Gators have gone 36-1 in home NCAA games dating back to 2013 — a stretch that includes 22 consecutive wins and a run-differential that borders on absurd: Florida has outscored its opponents 268-17 in those games, with 18 mercy-rule victories.

A Familiar Opponent

What makes this Super Regional particularly interesting is that these programs know each other. Florida and Texas Tech met twice in February at the Florida Classic hosted by the Gators, with Florida winning both games. They then squared off twice more in last weekend's Gainesville Regional, with Florida winning both of those as well.

The Gators have beaten Texas Tech four times in 2026. The Red Raiders arrive in Gainesville having faced this exact opponent four times already — and lost four times. For Texas Tech to advance to the Women's College World Series, they will need to solve the Gators in a building where Florida is almost impossible to beat.

That is a significant ask, even for a 55-6 team.

Florida's Third Consecutive Home Super Regional

This is Florida's 15th home Super Regional in program history and their third consecutive. The Gators have built a culture of not just qualifying for the postseason, but hosting it — and the advantage they gain from playing in front of their home crowd at Pressly Stadium is real and documented.

Florida's 36-1 record in home NCAA play since 2013 is not a coincidence. It reflects how the program prepares, how they recruit players who perform in big moments, and how much the home environment affects their opponents. Teams that arrive in Gainesville face a crowd, a stadium, and an atmosphere specifically designed to rattle visitors.

Texas Tech, to their credit, is not a program easily rattled. They have been winning games all season in hostile environments. But this is a different level of hostile.

What's Next

Game 1 of the Florida vs. Texas Tech Super Regional is Friday, May 22 at 11 AM ET on ESPN2. Game 2 is Saturday, May 23 at 12:30 PM on ABC. A potential deciding Game 3 would be Sunday, May 24. The series winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28.
