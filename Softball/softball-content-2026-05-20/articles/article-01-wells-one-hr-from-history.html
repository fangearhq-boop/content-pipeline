Kendall Wells Is One Home Run From Owning the NCAA Record

By Sarah Mitchell

May 20, 2026

With 37 home runs, the Oklahoma freshman has tied the 31-year-old all-time NCAA single-season record. Now comes the Super Regional.

There is a number in college softball history that has stood untouched for 31 years. In 1995, Arizona's Laura Espinoza hit 37 home runs in a single season and wrote her name into the record books. No one has matched it since — not through three decades of recruiting evolution, equipment changes, and a sport that has only grown in talent and depth.

Kendall Wells has 37 home runs.

The Oklahoma freshman tied Espinoza's mark during the Norman Regional, and the softball world briefly held its breath. One home run stands between Wells and a record she would own outright. One swing separates her from a moment that college softball has never seen.

And the stage for that moment? The NCAA Super Regionals, where Oklahoma hosts Mississippi State beginning Friday, May 22 at 1 PM ET on ESPN2.

A Season Unlike Any Other

The 37-home-run total does not capture the full scope of what Wells has accomplished in her freshman season. She did not just arrive at this number — she bulldozed every record standing in the way.

Wells broke the NCAA freshman home run record at 31, erasing a mark that had long been considered a ceiling for first-year players. She set the SEC single-season home run record at 27, surpassing every player in the conference's storied history. She broke the Oklahoma program record at 35, passing Jocelyn Alo — one of the most decorated power hitters in college softball history.

Each milestone arrived not with celebration and pause, but with Wells moving immediately to the next at-bat. She has been the nation's home run leader for most of the season while also batting close to .380 — a number that would make her dangerous even if she had never picked up extra-base power.

She is a freshman doing things no freshman, no player, in the modern era has done.

What Happens Next

Oklahoma's Super Regional against Mississippi State is a best-of-three series. Game 1 is Friday, Game 2 is Saturday, and a potential Game 3 would be Sunday. That gives Wells multiple opportunities to step to the plate with the record at stake.

Mississippi State's pitching staff will be well aware of what No. 38 means. Every pitch to Wells in this series arrives with history attached. Walk her and the crowd groans. Challenge her and risk becoming the answer to a trivia question that lives forever.

For Wells, none of that noise seems to register. She has played the entire season as though records are a byproduct of her work rather than its purpose. She wants to win. She wants to get to Devon Park. The home run record is what happens when you're the best hitter on the best offensive team in college softball.

What's Next

Oklahoma hosts Mississippi State in the Super Regional beginning Friday, May 22 at 1 PM ET on ESPN2. If the Sooners advance, they will compete in the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. Wells needs one home run to own the NCAA single-season record outright. Every at-bat in Norman this weekend is a potential piece of history.
