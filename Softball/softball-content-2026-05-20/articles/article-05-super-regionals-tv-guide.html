2026 Super Regionals Viewer's Guide: Every Game, Time, and Channel

By Emily Rawlings

May 20, 2026

Eight series. Four days. Sixteen teams all chasing the same destination: Devon Park in Oklahoma City. Here is everything you need to watch the 2026 Super Regionals.

The NCAA regional round is over. Sixteen teams remain, and every one of them is two wins away from the Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City. The 2026 Super Regionals run from Thursday, May 21 through Sunday, May 24, with all eight series broadcast on the ESPN family of networks.

Below is the complete schedule for every Super Regional, organized by start date, with the stories to watch in each matchup.

Thursday, May 21

No. 3 Tennessee vs. No. 10 Georgia — 7 PM ET, ESPN2 (Knoxville)

Tennessee (45-10) hosts Georgia (41-18) at Sherri Parker Lee Stadium. The Lady Vols are hosting a Super Regional for the fourth consecutive season — the first time that has happened in program history. Georgia's sophomore Addisen Fisher threw a complete-game shutout in the Athens Regional Final. This all-SEC matchup pits a veteran Tennessee squad against a Georgia team that has shown a talent for delivering on big stages.

No. 4 Nebraska vs. No. 13 Oklahoma State — 9 PM ET, ESPN2 (Lincoln)

Nebraska (49-6) hosts its first-ever NCAA Super Regional. The Cornhuskers allowed just one run in three regional games and have Jordy Frahm — one of the best pitchers in college softball — ready for the spotlight. Oklahoma State pitcher Ruby Neylan was equally dominant in the Stillwater Regional. The added storyline: Frahm and Neylan are both from the Omaha area and were once travel ball teammates before becoming each other's opponents at the collegiate level.

Friday, May 22

No. 7 Florida vs. No. 2 Texas Tech — 11 AM ET, ESPN2 (Gainesville)

Florida (51-10) has already beaten Texas Tech (55-6) four times in 2026. The Gators host their 15th home Super Regional in program history. Their 36-1 record in home NCAA play dating to 2013 is one of the most remarkable numbers in the sport.

No. 3 Oklahoma vs. No. 6 Mississippi State — 1 PM ET, ESPN2 (Norman)

This is the series where Kendall Wells — sitting at 37 home runs, tied with Laura Espinoza (Arizona, 1995) for the all-time NCAA single-season record — could make history. Wells needs one home run to stand alone in the record books.

No. 1 Alabama vs. No. 16 LSU — 7 PM ET, ESPN2 (Tuscaloosa)

Alabama's Vic Moten and Jocelyn Briski give the Crimson Tide the deepest pitching staff in the tournament. LSU arrives having outscored three regional opponents 23-2. These programs did not meet during the regular season.

No. 5 Arkansas vs. No. 12 Duke — 11 AM ET, ESPNU (Fayetteville)

Arkansas has been to five Super Regionals under head coach Courtney Deifel without reaching the WCWS. Duke is making its fourth Super Regional appearance in five seasons. Upset potential is real.

No. 2 Texas vs. No. 15 Arizona State — 9 PM ET, ESPN2 (Austin)

Texas (45-10) has made seven consecutive Super Regionals. They won their three regional games by a combined 21-plus runs. Arizona State made their first Super Regional since 2022 after a 9-1 run-rule victory over Texas A&M in the regional.

No. 8 UCLA vs. UCF — 9 PM ET, ESPNU (Los Angeles)

UCF is in just their second-ever Super Regional after upsetting No. 9 FSU — their first-ever NCAA win over Florida State. The Knights travel cross-country to face UCLA on their home field.

What's Next

All Super Regionals run Thursday, May 21 through Sunday, May 24, with best-of-three formats where the first team to win two games advances. The eight winners will compete at the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, with opening day on May 28. All games in the Super Regionals are broadcast on the ESPN family of networks — ESPN, ESPN2, and ESPNU.
