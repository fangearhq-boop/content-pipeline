UCF vs. UCLA Super Regional: Cinderella Takes Her Story Cross-Country to Los Angeles

By Sarah Mitchell

May 20, 2026

UCF upset No. 9 FSU in the regional final — the first time they had ever beaten Florida State in an NCAA game. Now the Knights fly to L.A. for only their second-ever Super Regional.

UCF softball had never beaten Florida State in an NCAA game. Not once, in all the years these programs had crossed paths in the postseason, had the Knights found a way to knock out the Seminoles in a tournament setting.

Then came Sunday in Tallahassee.

UCF defeated No. 9 FSU 4-2 in the Tallahassee Regional Final, completing an unbeaten run through the regional and making history in the process. The win was the program's first-ever NCAA victory over Florida State. The result ended FSU's season at 49-9 and sent UCF to just the second Super Regional in program history.

Now the Knights fly approximately 2,500 miles to face No. 8 UCLA in Los Angeles, with Game 1 set for Friday, May 22 at 9 PM ET on ESPNU and Game 2 on Saturday at 9 PM on ESPN.

A Program That Keeps Defying Expectations

The narrative around UCF's 2026 postseason run is one of consistent refusal to behave like anything other than a legitimate contender. In the Tallahassee Regional, the Knights did not survive on luck or a single inspired game — they went unbeaten, handled each opponent methodically, and then delivered the biggest win in program history when it mattered most.

That kind of performance does not happen by accident. UCF arrived in Tallahassee prepared, confident, and capable of winning on the road in hostile territory. They beat Florida State in Tallahassee. The idea that they cannot go to Los Angeles and compete with UCLA needs to be measured against that reality.

This is UCF's second-ever Super Regional. Their first ended without a trip to the Women's College World Series. The goal now is to go further than any UCF softball program has gone before.

UCLA's Home Advantage

No. 8 UCLA hosts this Super Regional on their home campus in Los Angeles, and the Bruins are an experienced tournament program. UCLA has been to the WCWS multiple times and knows what it takes to survive a Super Regional pressure cooker.

For UCF, every advantage belongs to the other team: home crowd, home field, a program with national championship experience. In the Super Regionals, that kind of advantage usually holds up. Usually.

But the same was said about playing at No. 9 FSU in Tallahassee. UCF went in anyway, competed anyway, and came home with the regional championship.

What's Next

Game 1 of the UCF vs. UCLA Super Regional is Friday, May 22 at 9 PM ET on ESPNU. Game 2 is Saturday, May 23 at 9 PM on ESPN. A potential Game 3 would be Sunday, May 24. The series winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28.
