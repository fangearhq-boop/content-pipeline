### STORY 1: Kendall Wells — One HR From Breaking the All-Time NCAA Record

**Long-Form Post**
🥎 ONE SWING FROM FOREVER 🥎

The record has waited 31 years. In 1995, Arizona's Laura Espinoza hit 37 home runs in a single season and set a mark that no one — not anyone, at any level of college softball — had matched since.

Until now.

Oklahoma's Kendall Wells walked into the Norman Regional and hit her 37th homer of the year. She didn't just tie the record — she tied it as a freshman. As someone who still has three more seasons of college softball ahead of her.

She did it after breaking pretty much every other significant record in her path:

• 31 HRs — broke the NCAA freshman home run record
• 27 HRs — set the SEC single-season home run record
• 35 HRs — broke the Oklahoma program record (surpassing Jocelyn Alo)
• 37 HRs — tied the all-time NCAA single-season record (Espinoza, 1995)

And now? Oklahoma hosts Mississippi State in the Super Regionals starting this Friday, May 22 at 1 PM ET on ESPN2.

Every. Single. At-bat. Is appointment television.

She's not just chasing a record. She's chasing a Women's College World Series title. The record is just what happens when you're Kendall Wells.

### STORY 2: Florida vs. Texas Tech — The Gators' Fortress Opens for a Monster Matchup

**Long-Form Post**
🐊 36-1. HOME. THIS WEEK. 🐊

If there is a more dominant home-field advantage in all of college softball, we haven't found it.

Florida softball has been hosting NCAA regionals and super regionals since 2013. In that time, they've gone 36-1 at Katie Seashole Pressly Stadium in NCAA play — 22 consecutive wins at the time of this writing — and have outscored opponents a staggering 268-17 across those games. Eighteen of those wins came by mercy rule.

This Friday, Texas Tech (55-6) — the nation's winningest program in 2026 — walks into that.

The Red Raiders are incredible. There's no getting around it. Fifty-five wins and only six losses by the time Super Regionals roll around? That's a historically dominant season. They arrive in Gainesville with everything to prove.

But here's the kicker: Florida has already beaten Texas Tech four times this season. Twice in February at the Florida Classic. Twice in the Gainesville Regional this past weekend.

The Gators (51-10) know exactly who they're playing. And they're playing at home.

Game 1 is Friday, May 22 at 11 AM ET on ESPN2. Game 2 is Saturday at 12:30 PM on ABC.

This is the marquee Super Regional of the weekend — and it's going to be electric.

### STORY 3: Alabama vs. LSU — No. 1 Meets No. 16 in Tuscaloosa

**Long-Form Post**
🏆 THE SUPERPOWER MEETS THE GIANT KILLER 🏆

On paper, it looks like a mismatch.

No. 1 Alabama — 52-7, the nation's top seed, with pitching that has been virtually untouchable all season — hosts No. 16 LSU at Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa this Friday.

But LSU isn't here to be an afterthought.

The Tigers went 3-0 in the Baton Rouge Regional and outscored their opponents 23-2 across all three games. That's not surviving on luck — that's a team that showed up to play. And while Alabama's Vic Moten dazzled in the regional with a near-perfect outing (no-hitter through five innings, final: 9-0 over USC Upstate), LSU has momentum of their own.

The wrinkle that makes this Super Regional fascinating? Alabama and LSU never played each other during the 2026 regular season. Neither team has scouted the other from the dugout this year. Fresh film, a one-series everything's-on-the-line environment — and an SEC rivalry that doesn't need any introduction.

Game 1 is Friday, May 22 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. Game 2 is Saturday at 3 PM on ESPN.

Crimson Tide fans are circling this one. And they should be.
