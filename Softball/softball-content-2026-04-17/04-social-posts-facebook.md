### STORY 2: Wells Record Chase

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM ET

Thirty-one down. Six to go. 🥎

Kendall Wells enters this weekend's Arkansas series needing just six more home runs to break the most storied record in college softball history. Lauren Espinoza hit 37 for Arizona in 1995 — that mark has stood for 31 years, survived two generations of elite hitters, and become the gold standard for single-season power in the sport. Wells is now on the doorstep.

The Oklahoma freshman broke the NCAA freshman single-season home run record on April 12 against Texas, hitting her 31st of the year to surpass the shared mark of 30 held by Jocelyn Alo, Lauren Chamberlain, and Kelly Majam. She bats .383 with 60 RBIs and leads the nation in home runs by a significant margin.

This weekend against Arkansas is the next chapter. Oklahoma has three games at Love's Field starting tonight at 5:30 p.m. on ESPN2, then multiple SEC series remaining before the postseason. The math is squarely in Wells' favor — she has time, she has the at-bats, and she is clearly in the hottest stretch of her freshman season.

Every Wells at-bat this weekend on national television carries the weight of 31 years of history. If she hits one tonight, she will be five away from the record.

Are you tracking her HR count this weekend?
