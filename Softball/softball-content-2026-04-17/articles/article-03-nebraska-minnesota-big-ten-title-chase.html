Nebraska Heads to Minnesota — Huskers One Step Closer to Big Ten Title

By Jordan Reeves | April 17, 2026 | I Love Softball

No. 4 Nebraska is quietly putting together one of the best seasons in program history, and this afternoon's road opener at Minnesota is the next test. The Huskers (33-6, 14-1 Big Ten) travel to Jane Sage Cowles Stadium for a three-game series with first pitch moved up to 3 p.m. CT due to incoming weather. All three games stream on B1G+.

Nebraska enters having won 21 of its last 22 games. The Huskers swept Wisconsin and Rutgers in consecutive conference series — back-to-back sweeps that have become the standard for how this team operates. Head coach Rhonda Revelle has built a program that wins consistently, wins deeply, and wins against quality competition. At 14-1 in Big Ten play, Nebraska leads the conference by three games with the regular season winding down.

The prize in front of them is the Big Ten regular-season title. Nebraska joined the conference in 2011 and has never claimed the crown. This is the season.

Why This Series Matters

Minnesota (6-9 in Big Ten play entering the weekend) is fighting to stay in the upper half of the conference standings heading into the postseason. The Gophers are at home, playing in front of their own fans, and fully aware that a series win over the No. 4 team in the country would be a program-defining result. In college softball, the home team always has an edge that statistics cannot fully quantify.

Nebraska, however, has shown no vulnerability in conference play this season. The Huskers have won every single Big Ten series. Their 14-1 conference record is built on a combination of pitching depth that has kept opponents from stringing together big innings, an offense that can score early and often, and a bullpen that does not give back leads. Rhonda Revelle's program is not just talented — it is disciplined.

The travel factor is also worth noting. Nebraska made the trip to Minneapolis knowing weather would affect the start time. The Huskers adjusted and moved on. That kind of professionalism characterizes a program in championship form.

The National Picture

Nebraska's run has not gotten the same national attention as the SEC and Big 12 race, but the Huskers' national seed positioning is firmly in the top eight. A sweep at Minnesota this weekend would all but lock up the Big Ten title and strengthen their case for a No. 4 national seed — which would mean hosting a regional and super regional at home in Lincoln.

For Big Ten fans and Nebraska faithful, this series is a chance to watch a program that has been building toward exactly this moment since the start of the season. No. 4 in the country. Three games from cementing their conference title lead. Coach Revelle has this team ready.

What's Next

Game 1 streams on B1G+ at 3 p.m. CT today. Game 2 follows Saturday at 1 p.m. CT, with the series finale Sunday at noon. If Nebraska wins all three, they are one extended run from clinching the Big Ten regular-season title with games to spare. Watch how the Huskers handle the emotional challenge of a road series where Minnesota has everything to gain and nothing to lose.
