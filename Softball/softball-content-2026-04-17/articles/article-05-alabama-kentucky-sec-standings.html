No. 3 Alabama Opens Home Series vs. Kentucky — Crimson Tide Surging in April

By Emily Rawlings | April 17, 2026 | I Love Softball

No. 3 Alabama opens a three-game home series against Kentucky tonight at 5 p.m. CT on ESPN2 — the second of two SEC games airing on the network simultaneously, alongside No. 1 Oklahoma hosting Arkansas. It is that kind of Friday in college softball: the entire sport is happening at once, and the standings race could look completely different by the time the sun goes down.

Alabama enters the weekend ranked No. 3 in the NFCA Coaches Poll with 734 points — just 13 behind No. 1 Oklahoma's 747. The gap at the top is closer than it has been all season. With Oklahoma having dropped two consecutive games (the Red River Rivalry Game 3 to Texas and Wednesday's Bedlam upset by Oklahoma State), the Week 12 NFCA poll released April 21 could see significant reshuffling. Alabama's results this weekend will be part of that equation.

Alabama's April Surge

The Crimson Tide have been one of the best teams in the SEC over the final month of the regular season. Earlier this month, Alabama swept Auburn in a home series, then won a series against Texas — one of the most significant results in the country during that stretch. These are not just routine wins; they are the kind of results that stack up favorably in the national seed matrix and strengthen the case for hosting a regional.

Coach Patrick Murphy's program has the pitching depth to control this series from the start. Alabama's rotation has been reliable all season, and the lineup carries the kind of depth that makes it difficult for opposing pitchers to work around — you cannot simply avoid the best hitters and expect to survive in a three-game series.

Kentucky's Role

Kentucky is not the type of opponent Alabama can overlook. The Wildcats are competitive in the SEC and arrive in Tuscaloosa with conference standings implications of their own. A road win over No. 3 Alabama would be a remarkable result for Kentucky's postseason push and would further complicate Alabama's weekend calculations.

For the Crimson Tide, focus is everything. The national seed race rewards teams that take care of their series one game at a time, and Alabama has the talent and depth to do exactly that. Tonight's Game 1 on ESPN2 is an opportunity to stake an early claim on the series and send a message to the rest of the sport about where the Tide stands entering the final weeks.

The Bigger Picture

If Oklahoma struggles against Arkansas and Alabama wins its series over Kentucky, the Week 12 NFCA poll could bring Alabama to within single digits of the No. 1 spot — or even push them to the top. That outcome seemed improbable a month ago. Tonight, it feels entirely plausible. Alabama has earned its place in this conversation with a sustained April run that deserves more attention than it has received.

What's Next

Game 2 follows Saturday, with Game 3 Sunday — all at home for Alabama. A series win keeps the Crimson Tide firmly in the national seed top 3 and potentially inside the top 2 when the Week 12 poll releases April 21. Watch tonight's ESPN2 broadcast for the first indication of where this series is headed.
