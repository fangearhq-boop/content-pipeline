Oklahoma Hosts No. 6 Arkansas in Game 1 Tonight — Sooners Seek Bounce-Back on ESPN2

By Sarah Mitchell | April 17, 2026 | I Love Softball

Oklahoma opens a three-game home series against No. 6 Arkansas tonight at Love's Field in Norman, with first pitch set for 5:30 p.m. CT on ESPN2. The start time was moved up from the originally scheduled 7 p.m. due to incoming weather, which should give the Sooners and Razorbacks a window to play before conditions deteriorate.

The timing of this series could not be more consequential for Oklahoma. The Sooners (40-5, 13-2 SEC) enter the weekend having lost two consecutive marquee games — a walk-off defeat in Game 3 of the Red River Rivalry against Texas, followed by a 6-4 Bedlam upset at the hands of Oklahoma State on Wednesday. Two losses in four days for a program that had been one of the most dominant in Division I softball since February represents a genuine inflection point.

Arkansas (35-6, 10-5 SEC) is not the kind of opponent Oklahoma can afford to overlook. The Razorbacks are ranked No. 6 in the ESPN/USA Softball poll and No. 8 in the NFCA poll. They enter having won SEC series over both Mississippi State and Florida in April — results that demonstrate consistent ability to beat ranked opponents. Oklahoma leads the all-time series 29-2 and has gone 15-1 when hosting Arkansas at Love's Field, but history only goes so far when a team has just dropped two straight in the spotlight.

Broadcast Details

ESPN2 carries Game 1, with Matt Schumacker handling play-by-play and Amanda Scarborough serving as the analyst. Game 2 on Saturday and Game 3 on Sunday are both scheduled for SEC Network+. This Friday broadcast gives the series immediate national visibility — and that matters, because the biggest individual storyline of the game sits in the middle of Oklahoma's batting order.

Kendall Wells arrives at Love's Field with 31 home runs on the season. She needs six more to break Lauren Espinoza's all-time NCAA single-season record of 37, set at Arizona in 1995. The Arkansas pitching staff will know exactly what it is facing, and every Wells at-bat on ESPN2 will carry historical weight. If she connects tonight, the record chase enters its final stretch with games still to spare.

What Oklahoma Needs

Beyond the individual storyline, the team narrative is equally compelling. Oklahoma has not faced a situation like this since the early part of the season. The Sooners still lead the SEC at 13-2, but Texas Tech sits at 40-3 and was separated from No. 1 by just two points in the most recent NFCA poll. If Oklahoma drops this series, the No. 1 ranking will almost certainly transfer to the Red Raiders when the Week 12 poll is released on April 21.

A series win over Arkansas, on the other hand, would reassert Oklahoma's position at the top of the sport. It would also offer compelling evidence that the back-to-back losses were a stumble — not a slide. Head coach Patty Gasso's teams have a long history of responding to adversity, and the Sooners have more than enough talent to take control of this series from the first inning.

Arkansas, for its part, has every reason to play loose. A road series win over the No. 1 team in the country would transform its NCAA Tournament resume and potentially elevate the Razorbacks into top-8 national seed consideration heading into the final weeks of the regular season.

What's Next

Game 2 follows Saturday at 7 p.m. CT on SEC Network+, with Game 3 scheduled for Sunday at 1 p.m. CT. Tonight's result will set the tone for the entire weekend. Oklahoma is built to win big games at home — but the Sooners need to play like it from the first inning. Keep an eye on the bullpen usage: whoever pitches Game 1 sets the rotation for the back half of the series.
