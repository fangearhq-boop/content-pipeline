Pickens Strikes Out 10, Vols Take Super Regional Game 1 Over Georgia 3-1

By Sarah Mitchell

May 22, 2026

Tennessee needed 10 Karlyn Pickens strikeouts and three solo home runs to beat Georgia in Super Regional Game 1. The Lady Vols are one win from Devon Park.

Karlyn Pickens walked into the biggest game of the Tennessee softball season and delivered exactly what the Lady Vols needed. A complete game. Ten strikeouts. One run allowed.

Tennessee defeated Georgia 3-1 in Super Regional Game 1 on Thursday night at Sherri Parker Lee Stadium in Knoxville. The victory puts the Lady Vols one win away from their next trip to the Women's College World Series. Game 2 is today at 3 PM ET on ESPN2 — and Tennessee now controls the series.

The formula was simple. Pickens dominated on the mound. Three different Tennessee hitters made single swings count. And Georgia, despite coming in with momentum and a legitimate ace of their own, left Knoxville without a win.

Pickens' Night in Full

Pickens spread seven Georgia hits across seven innings, walking three batters and allowing just one run. That run came in the top of the second inning, when Emily Digby launched a solo home run to give Georgia an early 1-0 lead. It was the only time all night that Tennessee trailed.

The strikeout total tells the story of the rest of the game. Ten strikeouts in seven innings in an NCAA Tournament game is dominant pitching by any measure. Georgia's lineup — which included ace pitcher Addisen Fisher and a team that went 40-18 this season — did not solve Pickens at any point after that first-inning lead.

For Pickens, this is her element. The postseason stage, the sold-out crowd, the high-stakes situation — she has been here before and responded before. Thursday night added another chapter to a career that has made her one of the most respected pitchers in college softball.

Three Solo Shots Do the Work

Tennessee's offense was not prolific. It did not need to be. Sophia Knight, Emma Clarke, and Alannah Leach each hit solo home runs — one each, spread across the game — and those three swings provided all three Tennessee runs.

Three solo shots winning a Super Regional Game 1 is a statement about how well Pickens performed. Tennessee did not need big innings, did not need runners in scoring position, and did not need to rely on the bullpen. Three home runs. One complete game. One win.

The back-and-forth between Georgia's opening run and Tennessee's three solo responses gave the game exactly the feel of a tight, tournament-level contest. But Pickens never let Georgia answer — after the Digby shot in the second, the Bulldogs went scoreless for the remaining five innings.

What Georgia Showed

Georgia was not dominated easily. Seven hits across seven innings means Addisen Fisher's team put the ball in play repeatedly and made Pickens work. The Bulldogs reached base. They created situations. They simply could not convert with Pickens on the mound.

Fisher herself had a serviceable night — she kept Tennessee's powerful lineup to three solo shots rather than allowing any multi-run innings. A different night, a different opponent, three runs might be enough to win. On Thursday, it was not.

Georgia goes into Game 2 needing to win two consecutive games to advance. That is a difficult path. But this is a program that threw a complete-game shutout in the Regional Final and traveled to a sold-out Knoxville crowd without flinching. The Bulldogs are not done.

What's Next

Game 2 is Friday, May 22 at 3 PM ET on ESPN2 from Sherri Parker Lee Stadium in Knoxville. Tennessee wins the series with a victory today. Georgia must win today to force a deciding Game 3 on Saturday. The winner of the Knoxville Super Regional advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. For Tennessee, a fourth consecutive Super Regional hosting title and a WCWS berth would complete one of the most sustained runs of postseason dominance in program history.
