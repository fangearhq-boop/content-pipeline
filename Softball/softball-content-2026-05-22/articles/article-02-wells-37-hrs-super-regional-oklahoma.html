Wells at 37 HRs: Oklahoma's Super Regional Opens Today vs. Mississippi State

By Emily Rawlings

May 22, 2026

Kendall Wells has 37 home runs and a Super Regional to play. Oklahoma faces Mississippi State today at 1 PM ET on ESPN2, and every at-bat carries the possibility of history.

Before the first pitch today in Norman, the question Mississippi State's pitching staff had to answer was the one that has followed Kendall Wells for months. What do you do with her?

Wells enters Oklahoma's Super Regional against Mississippi State with 37 home runs — tied with Arizona's Laura Espinoza, who set the single-season mark in 1995. That number has stood for over three decades. One swing changes it. And today, Wells gets her first chance in the Super Regional round to produce that swing.

Game 1 is at 1 PM ET on ESPN2 at Love's Field in Norman, Oklahoma. The atmosphere will be what you expect when a record hangs over every at-bat of a tournament game.

The Pitch-to-Her Problem

There is no clean way for Mississippi State to handle Wells. The options reduce to two: attack the strike zone and risk history, or work around her and face the rest of Oklahoma's lineup.

Oklahoma's offense has not been built on Wells alone. The Sooners have power throughout their lineup. Walking Wells repeatedly to avoid the record does not eliminate the threat — it puts runners on base for the hitters behind her, who have proven they can drive them in.

The coverage from the week before the Super Regional has described Mississippi State's approach as trying to "crack the code." That phrase implies there is a code to crack. Pitchers against Wells have been searching for it all season and none have found it with consistency.

Today is game one of what could be a three-game series. Mississippi State's pitching staff will get multiple looks at Wells. How they handle the first at-bat of the series will set the tone for everything that follows.

The Weight of the Season Behind Her

Wells has already broken records before reaching 37 home runs. She set the NCAA freshman home run record at 31. She broke the SEC's single-season record. She surpassed Oklahoma's own program record, which had been held by Jocelyn Alo at 35.

The trajectory of her 2026 season has been one long, sustained march through the record books. And each milestone has come in the context of wins — Wells does not accumulate home runs in losses. She does it while Oklahoma is winning, which is why the Sooners are here hosting a Super Regional instead of watching on television.

The pressure of the number — 37, tied with Espinoza — has been something Wells has carried for weeks. She tied the mark in the regional and then had days between rounds with the number sitting unchanged. Today, she gets to play again. She gets to swing.

Mississippi State's Case

This is not a Mississippi State team that arrived in Norman having given up. They earned this Super Regional. They have a pitching staff capable of getting elite hitters out. The challenge they face is simply that Wells operates on a different level than most hitters they have seen this season.

If Mississippi State can limit Wells to a quiet game and find ways to score against Oklahoma's pitching, they give themselves a chance to win this series. The Bulldogs have the talent to do that. The question is whether they can do it against this specific opponent in this specific environment.

What's Next

Game 1 of the Norman Super Regional is today, May 22, at 1 PM ET on ESPN2. Game 2 is Saturday, May 23 at 1 PM ET on ESPN. A potential Game 3 would also be Saturday. The winner of the Oklahoma-Mississippi State series advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. Whether Wells breaks the record today, tomorrow, or in Oklahoma City — if Oklahoma gets there — the chase continues.
