UCF Takes Cinderella Run to Hollywood — Knights Face UCLA in Super Regional Tonight

By Emily Rawlings

May 22, 2026

UCF already won the game everyone said they couldn't. Now they fly across the country to face No. 8 UCLA in a 9 PM ET Super Regional. The Cinderella story has a new chapter tonight.

When UCF beat Florida State 4-2 in Tallahassee to advance out of the regional, it was the kind of win that changes the way a program sees itself. UCF had never won an NCAA game against Florida State. They did it in the regional final, on FSU's home field, in one of the highest-pressure environments in college softball.

That win earned UCF their second Super Regional appearance in program history. And it earned them a flight to Los Angeles to face No. 8 UCLA at Easton Stadium.

Game 1 is tonight at 9 PM ET on ESPNU. The Knights are the road team, the lower seed, and the team nobody expected to be here. They also have not lost since the tournament began.

What the FSU Win Means in This Context

Context matters when evaluating upsets. UCF did not beat a struggling Florida State team. They beat a No. 9 national seed that had won the ACC multiple times and built one of the most competitive programs in college softball. Winning in Tallahassee, in a regional final, is not an accident.

The road experience from that win is directly applicable to tonight. UCF has already proven they can silence a hostile crowd, manage the pressure of a road tournament game, and execute when it matters most. Those same skills apply in Los Angeles against a UCLA crowd that will be loud and expectant.

The specific circumstances differ. UCLA's Easton Stadium is a different environment than Florida State's facility. But the skill set UCF has demonstrated — performing on the road under pressure — does not expire between rounds.

UCLA's Perspective

UCLA enters this Super Regional as the host and the significant favorite. The Bruins are a top-10 program with deep experience at this stage of the tournament. Home-field advantage in college softball is meaningful — the crowd, the familiarity of the field, the absence of travel fatigue all contribute to outcomes in tight games.

UCLA's preparation for UCF will be thorough. The Bruins have seen the film from UCF's regional performance. They know how the Knights beat FSU. They will be ready for the road Cinderella energy that made UCF dangerous in Tallahassee.

The question for UCLA is not whether they are the better team on paper. They likely are. The question is whether they can execute against a team that does not care what the paper says.

Program Trajectory

UCF's second-ever Super Regional is a program-building moment regardless of what happens this weekend. A program with limited postseason history reaching the Super Regional round — twice — is establishing itself as a legitimate national player. The recruitment implications, the program confidence, the culture shift that comes from winning on the national stage — all of that is already in motion for UCF.

The most valuable thing for UCF tonight is this: they have been here before. Not many times. But once. And that once — the Super Regional appearance that preceded this one — means the moment is not entirely foreign to the program's identity.

What's Next

Game 1 is tonight, Friday May 22, at 9 PM ET on ESPNU from Easton Stadium in Los Angeles. Game 2 is Saturday, May 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN. A potential Game 3 would also be Saturday. The winner of the UCLA-UCF Super Regional advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28.
