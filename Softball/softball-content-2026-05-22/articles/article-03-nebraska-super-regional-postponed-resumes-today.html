Nebraska's Super Regional Resumes Today — Rain Delayed It, Didn't Cancel It

By Jordan Reeves

May 22, 2026

Game 1 was suspended 23 minutes in by weather. Everything that made Thursday night remarkable is still in place — the sold-out crowd, the Frahm-Neylan matchup, and 13 years of waiting. It all continues today at 4 PM CT.

Thursday night in Lincoln was supposed to be the night. Nebraska's first-ever home Super Regional. A sold-out Bob Devaney Sports Center. Jordy Frahm on the mound for the home team in front of the city that produced her.

Then the rain came. Lightning followed. Twenty-three minutes into Game 1, play stopped in the bottom of the first inning with Nebraska at the plate, a runner on first, and one out.

The announcement came: postponed. The Cornhuskers went from the doorstep of something unprecedented to waiting another day. The crowd went home without seeing a result.

But today the game resumes. Game 1 picks up where it stopped at 4 PM CT, and every element of Thursday's buildup is still intact.

What Was Suspended, Not Lost

Nebraska entered the Super Regional at 49-6, one of the best records in the country. They allowed just one run across their entire regional — the kind of defensive dominance that makes a team dangerous in a tournament where one run can decide a series.

Game 1 began normally enough. Oklahoma State completed the top of the first inning. Nebraska had a runner on base in the bottom of the first with one out when play stopped. The score was tied at zero — a game still entirely undecided.

When play resumes today, Nebraska steps back in with the same baserunner, the same count, the same momentum they had built going into the game. The delay did not reset anything structurally. It just added a night of waiting.

The Frahm-Neylan Matchup

The storyline that made this Super Regional one of the most talked-about of the weekend did not require the rain to become compelling. Jordy Frahm is a Lincoln native who grew up blocks from campus and dreamed of pitching in this exact building. Ruby Neylan, Oklahoma State's pitcher, is from Omaha. They were travel ball teammates — two Nebraska kids who came up playing the same sport, on the same teams, until their careers took them in opposite directions.

Now Frahm wears red and Neylan wears orange and black, and today they face each other in the most important game either has played in college softball. There is nothing manufactured about that narrative. It simply exists, waiting for the game to play out.

Frahm's ability to perform in Lincoln, with the crowd behind her and the history of this program visible in every angle of the building, will be a factor. Neylan's ability to silence that crowd — as a player who grew up knowing Nebraska softball — will be an equal and opposite force.

What Oklahoma State Brings

Nebraska's dominant record can obscure the fact that Oklahoma State is a legitimate Super Regional team. The Cowgirls scored in double digits in all three of their regional games — an offense with genuine firepower. They did not arrive in Lincoln to lose quietly.

The extra night between the postponement and today's resumption was an adjustment for both teams. Oklahoma State had additional time to prepare for Frahm. Nebraska had additional time to maintain focus. In a tight series, margins are small, and both programs will use every preparation hour they have.

What's Next

Game 1 of the Lincoln Super Regional resumes today, Friday May 22, at 4 PM CT at Bob Devaney Sports Center. Game 2 is scheduled for Saturday at 4 PM CT. A potential Game 3 would also be Saturday. The winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. Nebraska's 13-year WCWS drought — last appearing in 2013 — will either end or continue after this weekend in Lincoln.
