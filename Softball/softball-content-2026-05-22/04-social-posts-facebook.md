### STORY 2: Wells/Oklahoma Super Regional Begins — HR Record Within Reach

**Long-Form Post**
🥎 THE MOST ANTICIPATED AT-BATS IN COLLEGE SOFTBALL — TODAY 🥎

Kendall Wells enters the Super Regional with 37 home runs.

That ties the single-season mark set by Laura Espinoza of Arizona in 1995. Thirty-one years, and that number has stood. One swing separates Wells from making it her own.

Today, Oklahoma hosts Mississippi State in Super Regional Game 1 at 1 PM ET on ESPN2 at Love's Field in Norman.

Mississippi State's pitching staff has had a week to think about this. How do you pitch to the most dangerous hitter in college softball? There's no clean answer. Walk her and you face the rest of an Oklahoma lineup that has been destroying pitching all season. Attack the zone and you might be the one who ends up serving up history.

Wells isn't chasing the record. She's chasing the WCWS. The record is what happens when you put up a season like she has.

Every at-bat today will carry weight. Every pitch will matter. This is why we watch college softball.

### STORY 3: Nebraska Postponement — Resumes Today at 4 PM CT

**Long-Form Post**
🌽 RAIN TRIED TO STOP NEBRASKA. IT FAILED. 🌽

Thursday night in Lincoln, the build-up was everything you'd expect.

Bob Devaney Sports Center was sold out. Nebraska (49-6) was about to play their first-ever home Super Regional. Jordy Frahm — a Lincoln native, who grew up dreaming of this exact moment — was ready to pitch on the biggest night in Nebraska softball history.

And then it rained. Lightning struck. Twenty-three minutes into Game 1, play stopped. Nebraska had a runner on first with one out in the bottom of the first inning. The crowd waited. The announcement came: postponed.

But this morning, the announcement that matters: Game 1 resumes TODAY at 4 PM CT.

Everything that made last night incredible is still there. The sold-out crowd. Frahm on the mound. Ruby Neylan of Oklahoma State — an Omaha native who once played travel ball with Frahm, now wearing Cowgirl colors — waiting to face her.

Nebraska's 13-year WCWS drought. Their first-ever home Super Regional. All of it still on the line.

The rain didn't stop Lincoln. It just made them wait a little longer.
