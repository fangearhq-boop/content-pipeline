Nebraska Hosts a Regional for the First Time Since 2013 — And the Wait Has Only Made It Sweeter

By Emily Rawlings | May 14, 2026 | I Love Softball

The last time Nebraska softball hosted an NCAA regional, social media looked different, the coaches were different, and most of the players set to take the field Friday were in middle school. That was 2013.

Thirteen years later, the Lincoln Regional is coming home.

Nebraska earned the No. 4 national seed in the 2026 NCAA tournament — which, under the new hosting format that seeds the top 32 teams nationally in pods of four, means the Huskers get to host in their own backyard. After more than a decade of qualifying for the tournament and watching the bracket send them elsewhere, this year is different. This year, the games come to Nebraska.

The Season That Earned This Moment

Nebraska's 2026 campaign has been the kind of season that demands a home tournament stage. The Huskers claimed their 11th Big Ten championship in program history, capturing the conference tournament title in a championship game that featured one of the season's most talked-about individual moments — UCLA's Megan Grant breaking the NCAA single-season home run record in the third inning, only to see Nebraska rally and win the game 7-2 anyway.

That resilience — the ability to absorb a historic opponent moment and still close out a championship — says something about a Nebraska team that has won consistently all year. The Huskers did not reach the No. 4 national seed by accident. They earned it by beating quality programs in the Big Ten over a full schedule.

The Lincoln Regional Field

Nebraska's opener on Friday is against South Dakota at 5:30 PM ET on ESPN+. The other first-round game in the regional sends Louisville against Grand Canyon at 3:00 PM CT, with the winners meeting on Saturday.

South Dakota, Louisville, and Grand Canyon are programs that earned their tournament spots — but all three face a Nebraska team playing at home with 13 years of built-up energy behind every at-bat. The Huskers will have the crowd. They will have the familiarity of their own environment. And they will have the knowledge that this moment — the first home regional since 2013 — is not one they intend to let pass without making something of it.

What Home Means in This Format

The double-elimination regional format is three days of softball where fatigue, travel, and pressure compound. Home teams have meaningful advantages that compound over the weekend: familiar warm-up routines, no hotel stays between games, and the energy of a home crowd that wants it as badly as the players do.

Nebraska knows all of that. Their coaches have been preparing for this specific situation — the chance to turn home-field advantage into a regional title and a super-regional berth. After 13 years away from this stage as a host, the preparation has had a long time to build up.

What's Next

Nebraska opens the Lincoln Regional on Friday at 5:30 PM ET on ESPN+. Winning the regional would send the Huskers to a super regional the following weekend, and from there — if the bracket cooperates — to Oklahoma City for the Women's College World Series starting May 28.

The 2013 edition of Nebraska softball would not recognize this team. The 2026 team would not trade places with anyone. Friday is finally their moment to show what a home regional looks like in Lincoln.
