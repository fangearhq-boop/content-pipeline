One Record Broken. One Title Won. Now Megan Grant Wants the Ring That Matters Most.

By Jordan Reeves | May 14, 2026 | I Love Softball

Somewhere this spring, Megan Grant ran out of individual milestones to chase.

She hit 38 home runs — shattering a 31-year-old NCAA single-season record that belonged to Arizona's Laura Espinoza since 1995. She did it in 55 games, 17 fewer than it took the previous record holder. She walked off the Big Ten championship game field having just driven a ball to deep left-center that rewrote a record most people assumed would never fall in their lifetime.

She also won a ring in a different sport. UCLA's 2026 NCAA basketball championship was Grant's to share, a testament to a dual-sport career that most athletes could not pull off in a single discipline.

And she is a senior. The L.A. Regional — starting Friday at Easton Stadium — could be the beginning of her last weeks in a UCLA uniform. When that final out is recorded, this career is over. The question the softball world is now asking is simple: can she add a softball championship to the collection before it ends?

The Numbers That Make This Team Dangerous

Grant does not carry UCLA alone. Teammate Jordan Woolery has 33 home runs this season, making the two of them the first pair of teammates in NCAA softball history to each clear the 30-home run mark in the same year. When a lineup has two players capable of changing a game with a single swing, no deficit feels safe for the opposing team.

UCLA is the No. 8 national seed in the tournament — a seeding that places them as a regional host but also means they could face a No. 1 seed in a super regional if the bracket holds. That is a steep climb, and the Bruins will need more than two bats to make it to Oklahoma City.

The L.A. Regional field includes Cal State Fullerton, South Carolina, and Cal Baptist. Easton Stadium is one of the most celebrated venues in college softball, and this will be UCLA's 12th straight year hosting a regional — a streak that speaks to the program's sustained excellence.

The Unfinished Business

Grant's résumé already defies easy summary. But in the conversation about great college softball careers, the women who reached Oklahoma City — who played in Devon Park under the lights with a championship on the line — occupy a different category.

That is the one thing she does not have. The record is hers. The basketball title is hers. What remains is softball's ultimate prize, and the window to claim it is measured in days, not months.

Her teammates know what this moment means. Her coaches have built the schedule around reaching this stage. The only thing left is to play the games and see how far the bat can carry them.

What's Next

UCLA opens L.A. Regional play on Friday at Easton Stadium. The Bruins are the No. 8 seed and will host through the regional and, if they advance, the super regional as well. Super regionals run May 22–24, with the Women's College World Series beginning May 28 in Oklahoma City.

For Megan Grant, every remaining game is the final few pages of a chapter that does not come with extensions. Friday is how those pages start being written.
