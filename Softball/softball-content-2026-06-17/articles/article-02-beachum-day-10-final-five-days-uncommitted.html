Beachum Watch — Day 10, Five Days Left, Last Elite Transfer Still Uncommitted

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 17, 2026

Isa Torres announced her commitment to Texas on Tuesday evening. The biggest name in the transfer portal is off the board. And now every program that was chasing Torres just shifted its full attention to Jaysoni Beachum.

Beachum — the Florida State third baseman, the 2024 NFCA Freshman of the Year, the 2026 Second-Team All-American, and Softball America's No. 1 overall transfer — enters Day 10 of the 15-day portal window still uncommitted. The window closes June 22. Five days remain.

What Beachum Brings

The case for Beachum as the No. 1 transfer in this cycle starts with raw production. In 2026, she hit .417 with 16 home runs, 66 RBIs, and a .549 on-base percentage. Her season was not a statistical outlier — it was the continuation of a three-year career of elite output at Florida State.

Over three seasons with the Seminoles, Beachum accumulated 37 home runs, 180 RBIs, and 202 hits. She earned NFCA Freshman of the Year honors in 2024, which announced her as a player worth watching. By 2026, she was a Second-Team All-American at the third base position — a power bat in a lineup that consistently competed at the national level.

She is not just a bat. She is a presence. The kind of player who changes the dynamic of a lineup from the moment she steps into it.

The Final Week Landscape

The portal this cycle has been dominated by two storylines: who goes to Texas and who goes to Texas Tech. Torres chose Texas. Moriah Polar and Jayden Heavener went to Texas Tech. Kasidi Pickering is expected to follow to Lubbock.

Beachum is the last truly elite player without a destination. The programs competing for her signature are aware of the clock. Every day she waits is leverage — leverage she has earned with three seasons of production that made her the No. 1 name in this portal from the moment she entered on June 2.

The field of serious suitors is not public, but programs that fell short of landing Torres are almost certainly among them. This is the final prize of the 2026 portal cycle, and the bidding is real.

What's Next

Beachum has five days to announce — or the portal closes without her making a decision, which seems unlikely given the competitive interest surrounding her.

Her destination will define the final narrative of the 2026 transfer cycle. If she chooses a program already carrying portal momentum, she adds a title contender. If she chooses a program that has not yet had its portal moment this cycle, she becomes the defining move of an entire offseason.

The countdown is real. Day 10. Five days left. Jaysoni Beachum is the last player who can shape the story of what 2027 softball looks like — and she has not said a word yet.
