Isa Torres Commits to Texas — D1 Player of the Year Says "I'm Coming Home"

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 17, 2026

The wait is over.

Isa Torres — the 2026 D1 Softball Player of the Year, the most coveted transfer in the history of this portal cycle — committed to Texas on Tuesday evening, posting a photo in burnt orange on Instagram with a two-word caption: "I'm coming home."

The Georgetown, Texas native is headed back to her home state to play for the two-time national champions. After three seasons at Florida State, Torres is joining a Longhorns program that already had two titles on the shelf and just became considerably more dangerous.

The Player Who Did Everything at FSU

The numbers Torres produced in 2026 at Florida State were not just good — they were historically exceptional.

A .530 batting average. A .978 slugging percentage. A .591 on-base percentage. Seventy-eight runs scored. She set an NCAA record when she recorded a hit in 16 consecutive at-bats. She was named the ACC Player of the Year, the ACC Defensive Player of the Year, and a 2026 First Team All-American. D1Softball, Softball America, and NFCA all agreed: Torres was the best player in college softball this season.

She posted those numbers as a shortstop — a position where elite offensive production is already rare. Combine that with her defensive recognition and you have the complete picture of why every elite program in the country was recruiting her.

Coming Home to Georgetown Country

The story is more than just a transfer commitment. Torres grew up in Georgetown, Texas — the same hometown as Texas catcher commit Samantha Swan. The two players went their separate paths from Georgetown to Power programs across the country. Now they are being reunited in burnt orange.

Swan transferred from Arizona State to Texas earlier in this portal cycle, bringing a .383 batting average, a 1.151 OPS, 12 home runs, and 55 RBIs with her. She was ranked the No. 1 catcher available in the 2026 portal and No. 9 overall by Softball America.

When Torres announced her commitment on Tuesday, she was not just picking a team. She was going home — back to the state, back to the team she could have grown up rooting for, and back alongside a familiar face from Georgetown.

Texas's Portal Class Is Complete

Torres joins Swan and former Texas Tech pitcher Samantha Lincoln in Mike White's 2026 portal class. White has turned the transfer portal into an extension of Texas's recruiting dominance. The Longhorns won back-to-back national championships in 2025 and 2026, and this offseason, they attacked the portal without hesitation.

The class reads like a roster that was designed to leave nothing on the table. The D1 Player of the Year at shortstop. The No. 1 catcher in the portal. A pitcher who adds depth to a staff that already knew how to win.

Torres's arrival fills a legitimate positional need for Texas while simultaneously raising the ceiling of a program that was already at the top. Her offensive profile — elite contact, elite on-base skills, elite run production — slots into a lineup built for deep postseason runs.

What's Next

Torres's commitment effectively closes Texas's portal window for this cycle. The Longhorns have their class, and the attention now turns to what they do with it when the 2027 season begins.

The bigger question for the portal itself: who is next? With Torres off the board, Jaysoni Beachum — Softball America's No. 1 overall transfer, a three-year FSU standout with 37 career home runs and a .417 average in 2026 — is now the last elite name still uncommitted. The portal window closes June 22. Five days remain, and the race for Beachum is the final chapter of a remarkable transfer cycle.

For Texas, this is not a chapter — it is an opening statement. The three-peat chase starts now.
