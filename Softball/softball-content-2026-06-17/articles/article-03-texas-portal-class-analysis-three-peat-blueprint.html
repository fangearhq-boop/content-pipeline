Texas's Portal Masterpiece — Torres, Swan, and Lincoln Build the Three-Peat Blueprint

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | June 17, 2026

Texas won back-to-back national championships in 2025 and 2026. In the offseason between those years, the Longhorns used the transfer portal to address roster needs and stay ahead of the competition.

They just did it again. Only this time, the class they assembled might be the best in the program's history.

Head coach Mike White added three transfers in the 2026 portal cycle: Isa Torres, the D1 Softball Player of the Year from Florida State; Samantha Swan, the No. 1 catcher in the portal from Arizona State; and Samantha Lincoln, a pitcher from Texas Tech. Individually, each addition is significant. Together, they form a portal class that addressed every weakness and amplified every strength.

Torres: The Capstone

Isa Torres is the headliner. A shortstop who hit .530 with a .978 slugging percentage, a .591 on-base percentage, and 78 runs scored at Florida State in 2026, Torres was the ACC Player of the Year, the ACC Defensive Player of the Year, and a First Team All-American. She set an NCAA record for hits in 16 consecutive at-bats and was unanimously regarded as the best player available in the portal.

Torres is from Georgetown, Texas. She chose Texas with the announcement "I'm coming home." She is not arriving as a visitor — she is arriving as a Texan who belongs there.

Her addition fills the shortstop role for Texas with a generational talent. She is not a plug-in replacement. She is a transformation.

Swan: The Returning Teammate

Samantha Swan transferred from Arizona State earlier in this portal cycle, bringing a .383 batting average, a 1.151 OPS, 12 home runs, and 55 RBIs from a season in which she started all but one of 63 games. Softball America ranked her No. 9 overall in the portal and No. 1 among catchers.

Swan, like Torres, is from Georgetown, Texas. The two were raised in the same small city, and now they play in the same lineup. That kind of familiarity — built from years of shared experience before college — is the type of chemistry programs spend years trying to manufacture. Texas gets it naturally.

Lincoln: Pitching Depth

Samantha Lincoln came to Texas from Texas Tech, where she spent her early career before entering the portal. She adds pitching depth to a staff that already knows how to win at the national level. The addition also carries a degree of symbolism: Texas just recruited a pitcher away from the program that is assembling the most formidable portal class on the other side of the sport.

The Longhorns beat Texas Tech in the 2026 WCWS championship game. They spent the offseason adding the D1 POY and the No. 1 catcher, and then recruited a pitcher directly from their title-game opponent. The message is not subtle.

What's Next

Texas's 2026 portal class is complete. Three additions across three positions — shortstop, catcher, and the pitching staff — give White's program the depth to weather the long postseason grind that defines championship runs.

The three-peat is not guaranteed. Texas Tech is building something formidable. Other programs will compete. Softball is not a foregone conclusion.

But when you look at what Texas has assembled — two national titles, three elite portal additions, a lineup that now features the D1 Player of the Year at shortstop and the No. 1 portal catcher behind the plate — the burden of proof shifts to everyone else. Texas has shown, consistently, that this is what a dynasty looks like. The 2027 season will tell us whether anyone can interrupt it.
