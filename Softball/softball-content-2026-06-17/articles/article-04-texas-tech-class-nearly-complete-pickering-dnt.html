Texas Tech's Transfer Class Is Nearly Complete — Pickering's DNT Tag Is the Last Piece

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 17, 2026

Texas made its move. Now look at what is being built 320 miles west in Lubbock.

Texas Tech's 2026 transfer class is one announcement away from being complete — and what Jerry Glasco has assembled in this portal cycle is not just the No. 1 class in college softball. It may be the most concentrated collection of elite talent ever added by a single program in a single offseason.

What's Already Confirmed

Moriah Polar committed to Texas Tech on June 15. The Purdue transfer led all of college softball in 2026 with a .555 batting average, compiling 101 hits, 67 runs scored, and 47 RBIs. She is the NFCA Third-Team All-American who, in her junior season, simply hit the ball more consistently than any player in the country.

Jayden Heavener committed to Texas Tech on June 13. The lefty from LSU was ranked the No. 1 pitcher available in the portal. She went 13-9 with a 2.81 ERA and 128 strikeouts across 139.1 innings in 2026 and was on USA Softball's Player of the Year watch list. When programs were evaluating the top pitching arm available this cycle, the answer was Heavener — and she chose Lubbock.

Texas Tech's transfer class is already ranked No. 1 in college softball by Softball America, even before the final piece falls into place.

The Pickering Situation

Kasidi Pickering entered the transfer portal June 8 after three seasons at Oklahoma. She carried a "Do Not Contact" tag when she entered — the designation used when a player has privately decided where she is going and does not want to be contacted by outside programs.

The reporting, the social signals from the transfer network, and the DNT tag itself have pointed to Texas Tech since the moment Pickering entered the portal. No official commitment has been announced as of Tuesday.

What Pickering would add: a .370 batting average, 20 home runs, and 61 RBIs in 2026. A career line of .384 with 50 home runs and 169 RBIs across three seasons at OU. She is a proven power hitter who plays outfield and has the kind of track record — built against Big 12 competition — that translates immediately at the Power-program level.

The Rematch Is Being Built

Texas Tech fell to Texas in the 2026 WCWS championship game. The Red Raiders were runner-up. That is close, and it clearly served as motivation.

Glasco went into this portal cycle with an intent that is hard to miss. Polar and Heavener committed on back-to-back days. Pickering's expected confirmation would make it three elite additions in ten days. The Red Raiders did not approach this offseason as a program consolidating — they approached it as a program that finished second and intends to fix that.

The Texas vs. Texas Tech dynamic is now the defining rivalry frame for 2027 college softball. Texas has the D1 POY and the No. 1 catcher. Texas Tech has the No. 1 pitcher and the No. 1 hitter — and almost certainly, the No. 2 overall transfer in the portal.

What's Next

Kasidi Pickering's official announcement is the remaining question for Texas Tech. The DNT tag makes official confirmation largely a formality, but until Pickering posts, no one should state it as settled. That announcement could come at any time before the portal closes June 22.

For softball fans, the bigger picture is this: the two programs that met in the 2026 WCWS final both spent the offseason going all-in through the portal. Texas got better. Texas Tech got better. When they meet again — and they will — it will be worth every moment of the wait.
