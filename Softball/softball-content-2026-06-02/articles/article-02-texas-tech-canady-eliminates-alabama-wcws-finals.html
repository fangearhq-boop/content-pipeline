Texas Tech Stuns No. 1 Alabama Twice — Walk-Off, Then Canady Shutout Send Red Raiders to WCWS Finals

By Emily Rawlings | June 2, 2026

When Monday began, No. 1 Alabama was one of the most formidable teams in college softball. When Monday ended, they were going home.

No. 11 seed Texas Tech — the lowest-seeded program remaining in the tournament — eliminated Alabama twice in the same day at Devon Park, pulling off one of the most stunning semifinal performances in WCWS history. Mia Williams delivered a walk-off home run in Game 1 to force a deciding game. Then NiJaree Canady delivered a complete game shutout in Game 2 to close it out.

The Red Raiders are going to the national championship series for the second straight year.

Williams Walks It Off

Game 1 came down to the seventh inning. Alabama's Jana Young hit a two-run home run in the top half to tie the game at 4–4, and it looked like the No. 1 seed might escape. Then Williams stepped in to lead off the bottom of the seventh.

On the fourth pitch she saw, Williams launched her 26th home run of the season to left field. The walk-off gave Texas Tech a 5–4 win and forced the decisive Game 2. Williams celebrated at home plate with a team that had just punched back against the tournament's top seed in the most dramatic way possible.

Canady earned the win in relief, throwing 2⅔ innings and recording four strikeouts while allowing one run to help keep the game close enough for Williams to decide it.

Canady's Masterpiece

Game 2 removed all doubt. NiJaree Canady took the mound and proceeded to dismantle a lineup that had been one of the best in college softball all season.

Seven innings. Two hits. Six strikeouts. Zero runs allowed. It was a complete game shutout — the kind of performance that stamps a postseason run as something more than a bracket story and makes it a legacy moment for the pitcher.

Jasmyn Burns provided all the offense Texas Tech needed, hitting a solo home run in the fourth inning to put the Red Raiders in front. An error-aided run in the seventh added insurance. The defense did the rest, standing behind Canady while she navigated her way through an Alabama lineup that had no answer.

Canady's record improved to 28–6 on the season. The senior right-hander, who entered Devon Park with 1,000-plus career strikeouts and 96 career wins, is now one series win from a national title — the one thing missing from an otherwise transcendent career.

No. 11 Seed in the Finals

Texas Tech entered the WCWS as the No. 11 seed. To reach the championship series as a double-elimination team — needing to win every game with no margin for error — is the kind of run that college sports are built on.

They beat UCLA in nine innings to advance. They swept the semifinals against the No. 1 overall seed in a single day. Every game has had a different hero, from Burns to Williams to Canady, with each stepping up when her name was called.

Coach Gerry Glasco's program has not lost the belief or the execution when the stakes are highest. This is what the WCWS can look like when a team finds its peak at the right moment.

Alabama's Stunning Exit

For Alabama, the outcome was a jarring conclusion to a season that carried enormous expectations. The Crimson Tide came in as the No. 1 overall seed with a dominant pitching staff, led by Jocelyn Briski's 1.30 ERA, and an offense built around Brooke Wells and Alexis Pupillo. They had done nearly everything right during the regular season.

But at Devon Park, under the lights, it all came apart in the space of one extraordinary Texas Tech performance. Alabama's last national title came in 2012, and this felt like a season that could change that. Texas Tech had other ideas.

What's Next

Texas Tech faces No. 2 Texas in the WCWS Championship Series beginning Wednesday, June 3, at 8 p.m. ET on ESPN. It is a rematch of last year's finals, which Texas won. The Red Raiders went 1–2 in that series, ending a 15-game losing streak to the Longhorns before ultimately falling short.

Now they return with Canady at her best, an offense that has delivered in every high-stakes moment, and the confidence that comes from knocking out the No. 1 seed. If Texas Tech wins, it would be one of the most improbable championship runs in WCWS history. Game 1 is Wednesday. Do not miss it.
