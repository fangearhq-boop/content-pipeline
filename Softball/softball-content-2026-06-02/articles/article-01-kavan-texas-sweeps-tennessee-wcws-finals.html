Teagan Kavan Throws Fifth Career WCWS Shutout, Texas Advances to Third Straight Championship Series

By Sarah Mitchell | June 2, 2026

Teagan Kavan walked to the mound for Game 2 of the WCWS semifinals on Monday knowing exactly what was required. Two games. Two wins. A third straight trip to the national championship series.

She delivered all of it, and then some.

Kavan threw a two-hit, 10-strikeout complete game shutout to silence Tennessee 4–0 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, completing a sweep that sends No. 2 Texas to the WCWS Championship Series for the third consecutive year. It was her fifth career WCWS shutout and ninth career WCWS win — both program records for the Longhorns.

Kavan Was Untouchable

In a game that demanded everything, Kavan gave nothing away. Tennessee did not advance a runner past first base in all seven innings. The Lady Vols managed only two hits off the Texas ace, who walked nobody and retired batters with the kind of command that has defined her entire postseason run.

It was her fifth career shutout on this stage. Kavan now owns the Texas program record in both that category and career wins at the WCWS, with nine. For a pitcher who has been here before, this performance felt like the product of someone who has turned Devon Park into a personal domain.

Kavan entered the day with a save in Game 1, striking out Elsa Morrison looking with the bases loaded to preserve the Longhorns' 5–2 win. Then she came back out to close the series with arguably the more impressive of the two outings.

Stewart Sets the Program Record

The offense provided by Katie Stewart did its part. The junior first baseman launched her 31st home run of the season in Game 2 — a new Texas program record for home runs in a single season. It was a performance that underscored just how dangerous this lineup is even in the shadow of Kavan's dominance.

Stewart's power has been a defining thread throughout the postseason. She came to Devon Park and kept swinging, adding to a record that now belongs to her alone.

Three Straight for the Longhorns

With the sweep complete, Texas advances to the championship series for the third straight year and the fourth time in the last five seasons. The Longhorns are the defending national champions, having defeated Texas Tech in last year's championship series.

That opponent awaits them again. Texas Tech eliminated top-seeded Alabama twice on the same day — first via a walk-off home run in Game 1, then via a Canady complete game shutout in Game 2 — to set up an all-Texas championship series rematch.

It is the first WCWS Finals rematch since the best-of-three format began in 2005.

Tennessee's Season Ends

For Tennessee, the outcome marked the second consecutive WCWS semifinal exit to Texas. The Lady Vols saw the final collegiate game of Karlyn Pickens, the three-time First-Team All-American who threw six innings in the first game before Kavan's shutout closed the day in the second. Pickens leaves without a national championship, but with a legacy that belongs to the best the sport has produced.

Coach Karen Weekly's program will return next season with most of the core intact. But the chance to end the program's long wait for a national title slipped away at the same stage, against the same team, for the second year running.

What's Next

Texas faces Texas Tech in the WCWS Championship Series beginning Wednesday, June 3, at 8 p.m. ET on ESPN. Game 2 is Thursday at 8 p.m. ET on ESPN, with Game 3 (if necessary) on Friday at the same time.

The Longhorns will look to become the first Texas program to win back-to-back WCWS titles. Kavan is the obvious X-factor — if she pitches like she did Monday, it is difficult to see any team stopping the Longhorns. But Texas Tech has NiJaree Canady, who has matched Kavan at nearly every turn this postseason. The stage is set for one of the great pitching duels in WCWS championship history.
