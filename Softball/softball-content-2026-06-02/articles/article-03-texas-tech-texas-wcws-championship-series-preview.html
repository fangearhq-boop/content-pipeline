Texas vs. Texas Tech: The WCWS Championship Series Returns — Kavan and Canady Set for a Rematch

By Jordan Reeves | June 2, 2026

Last June, Texas and Texas Tech played for the national championship at Devon Park. Texas won in three games. In the process, something started that would not be forgotten: this rivalry, at this moment, belongs to everyone who watches college softball.

Now they're back. Same teams. Same stage. Different stakes — because this time, Texas Tech is carrying the weight of what it means to be a runner-up who found its way back.

The 2026 WCWS Championship Series is set. Game 1 is Wednesday, June 3, at 8 p.m. ET on ESPN.

How They Got Here

Texas came through its side of the bracket by sweeping Tennessee in the semifinals on Monday — one game where Kavan saved, one where she shut out the Lady Vols completely. The Longhorns are the defending national champions. They have been to three consecutive championship series and have won two titles in that stretch.

Texas Tech arrived by doing something even harder. The No. 11 seed — the lowest-seeded team in the bracket — needed to win every game they played after their first loss. They beat UCLA in nine innings. Then they beat No. 1 Alabama twice in the same day: a walk-off home run in Game 1 from Mia Williams, a complete game shutout from NiJaree Canady in Game 2. The Red Raiders have not had a margin for error and have not needed one.

The Pitching Matchup

Teagan Kavan vs. NiJaree Canady. In a season full of elite pitching, these two have operated on a separate level at Devon Park.

Kavan now has five career WCWS shutouts — a Texas program record — and nine career wins at this tournament. Her two-hit, 10-strikeout performance against Tennessee on Monday was one of the great individual WCWS pitching outings of this era.

Canady, a senior who entered the tournament with more than 1,000 career strikeouts and 96 wins, threw a complete game shutout against the No. 1 team in the country in a must-win game. Her career is defined by this kind of performance, and the championship series is where her legacy will be settled.

History and Context

Texas leads the all-time series against Texas Tech, 65–13. The Longhorns dominated that rivalry for years before Texas Tech's 4–3 win in Game 2 of last year's WCWS Finals snapped a 15-game losing streak.

This is the first WCWS championship series rematch since the best-of-three format began in 2005. History has not been kind to runners-up: only once has the team that finished second come back the following year to win the title.

Texas Tech is aware of those numbers. They eliminated two teams that were supposed to stop them, and nothing about this run suggests they are bothered by what the record books say.

What to Watch

The first question is who starts Game 1 for each team. Both Kavan and Canady are capable of throwing deep into games with short rest, having done exactly that throughout the tournament. Whether coaches hold them for Game 2 or send them out immediately in Game 1 will shape the entire series.

Offensively, watch Texas's Katie Stewart, who hit her 31st home run of the season Monday to break the Texas program record. And watch Texas Tech's Mia Williams, whose walk-off shot against Alabama showed she has the clutch gene in the biggest moments.

What's Next

Game 1 of the WCWS Championship Series: Wednesday, June 3, 8 p.m. ET, ESPN. Game 2: Thursday, June 4, 8 p.m. ET, ESPN. Game 3 (if necessary): Friday, June 5, 8 p.m. ET, ESPN.

Set your reminders. This is the one the sport has been building toward all year — and after Monday's semifinals, it has never felt more earned by both teams on the field.
