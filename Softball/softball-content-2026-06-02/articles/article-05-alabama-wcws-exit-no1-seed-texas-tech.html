Alabama's Season Ends at the WCWS — No. 1 Seed Falls to No. 11 Texas Tech in Stunning Semifinals Exit

By Emily Rawlings | June 2, 2026

Alabama came to Oklahoma City as the No. 1 overall seed, the team with the best record, the team with the best pitching, and the team that many believed was built to finally end the program's long wait for a second national title. They leave the WCWS having lost twice in the same day to the No. 11 seed.

Texas Tech eliminated the Crimson Tide Monday — a walk-off home run in Game 1, a complete game shutout in Game 2 — in what may stand as one of the biggest upsets in WCWS semifinal history. The seeding said Alabama should win. The scoreboard said otherwise.

A Season That Delivered Everything but a Ring

Alabama's 2026 season was dominant by nearly every measure. They entered the WCWS having won more than 50 games and carrying a staff anchored by Jocelyn Briski, whose ERA of 1.30 was among the best in the country. Briski had won more than 20 games on the season and been recognized as the SEC Pitcher of the Year.

On offense, Brooke Wells provided power from the middle of the lineup, and Alexis Pupillo hit over .400 for much of the season. This was a team built to play in June — and they did, right up until Texas Tech made sure the season ended here.

How the Upset Unfolded

Game 1 was tight all the way through. Alabama appeared to have turned the momentum in the seventh inning when Jana Young's two-run home run tied the game at 4–4. What followed was Mia Williams' walk-off — a no-doubt shot to left field in the bottom of the seventh that gave Texas Tech the win before a Game 2 was even necessary.

Then came Game 2. NiJaree Canady took the mound and proceeded to answer every question about whether she could be great in a must-win situation against the best team in the country. Seven innings, two hits, six strikeouts, no runs allowed. Jasmyn Burns' solo home run in the fourth inning was all the offense Texas Tech needed.

Alabama's bats — productive all season — went quiet when they could least afford it.

The History Behind the Loss

Alabama's last national championship came in 2012 under Patrick Murphy, who has guided the program to WCWS appearances year after year. The gap between regular-season success and a national title has been a persistent storyline for the Crimson Tide, one that this season seemed positioned to finally resolve.

Instead, they exit as the latest entry in a long list of top seeds who did not survive the WCWS semifinals. The format is unforgiving; one bad day eliminates even the best programs. Alabama had one bad day, and Texas Tech was the reason.

What's Next for Alabama

Briski's eligibility status will be one of the most closely watched storylines of the offseason. If she returns, Alabama's pitching foundation remains in place for another run. Wells' status and the broader roster picture will take shape over the coming weeks.

Murphy has never had trouble recruiting to Tuscaloosa, and a program that wins 50-plus games in a season is not one that rebuilds — it reloads. This loss hurts. It will fade, and Alabama will be back.

What's Next

The WCWS Championship Series is set: No. 2 Texas vs. No. 11 Texas Tech. Game 1 is Wednesday, June 3, at 8 p.m. ET on ESPN. Alabama's season is over, but the stage they helped set — by being the No. 1 seed, by providing the biggest semifinal matchup — belongs to the two Texas programs who fought their way through it.

Alabama will watch from home. The talent that carried them this far will carry them forward. But for now, the WCWS belongs to someone else.
