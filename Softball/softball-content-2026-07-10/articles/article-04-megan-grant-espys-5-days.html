5 Days Left to Vote for Megan Grant's ESPY — Here's What the Award Means

By Jordan Reeves | July 10, 2026

Five days. That is all that remains in the voting window for Megan Grant's shot at the Best Record-Breaking Performance ESPY.

Voting closes July 15 — the same night the 34th annual ESPYs air at 8 PM ET on ABC. One vote per day at ESPN.com. If you have not voted today, you have one fewer opportunity remaining after tonight.

What She Did

Megan Grant broke the NCAA Division I single-season home run record in 2026, hitting 42 home runs in 63 games at UCLA. The previous record — 37 home runs by Arizona's Laura Espinoza in 1995 — had stood for 31 years. Grant did not just inch past it. She cleared it by five, rewriting the benchmark for what the sport's best hitter in a single season could look like.

She also became UCLA's career home run leader with 91, passing Stacey Nuveman. And she did it while leading the Bruins deep into their 2026 season under one of the most demanding scoring and pitching environments the college game has to offer.

Now she is playing for the Portland Cascade in the AUSL, selected No. 4 overall in the 2026 College Draft — and nominated for one of the most visible individual honors ESPN awards in any sport.

The Competition

The nomination category includes serious athletes with genuine credentials:

Myles Garrett (LA Rams) won the NFL's Defensive Player of the Year in 2025. Johannes Høsflot Klæbo (Norway) has won multiple Olympic gold medals in cross-country skiing and is one of the most dominant winter sports athletes of his generation. Sabastian Sawe (Kenya) holds the world marathon record.

This is not a category to sleep on. Grant is the only softball player and the only record-breaker in a sport that does not have the NFL's platform or the Olympics' global reach. To win, the sport needs its community to show up.

What's Next

Vote for Megan Grant at ESPN.com — once per day through July 15. The 34th annual ESPYs, presented by Capital One, air Wednesday, July 15 at 8 PM ET on ABC and on the ESPN app. Host: Marcello Hernández.

Five days. Five votes still available. Use them.
