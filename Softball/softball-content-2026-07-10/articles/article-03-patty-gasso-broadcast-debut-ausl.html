Patty Gasso Made Her Broadcast Debut Last Night. The AUSL Is Better For It.

By Emily Rawlings | July 10, 2026

Patty Gasso stepped into a broadcast booth for the first time as a professional analyst on Thursday night, calling the Utah Talons vs. Oklahoma City Spark game live on ESPNU.

Eight national championships, two decades of building programs, and a résumé that makes her arguably the greatest softball coach in the history of the sport — and now she has a microphone and a national broadcast platform to bring all of that expertise to AUSL game coverage during the league's most critical stretch of the regular season.

The debut matters. Here's why.

What Gasso Brings to the Booth

Color commentators in any sport can describe what is happening on the field. Great analysts understand why it is happening — what the pitcher is seeing from the rubber, what the hitter is sitting on, what the coach is thinking in the circle during a 3-2 count with runners in scoring position.

Gasso has more context for the AUSL than virtually any other broadcast voice could offer. She recruited many of these players. She coached some of them at Oklahoma. She watched others compete against her for years. When Fouts throws a 12-K complete game, Gasso understands the mechanics that made it possible. When Canady struggles through a mechanical adjustment, Gasso has the vocabulary to explain it to a national audience.

That's what Thursday's debut represented: expertise that goes beyond credentials. Gasso doesn't just know this sport historically. She knows these players, this league, and this moment.

What Comes Next

Gasso returns to the AUSL broadcast booth on Saturday for the next Talons game, airing on MLB Network at 8:00 PM ET. Lonni Alameda — the Louisiana-Lafayette head coach with multiple Sun Belt Conference titles and a career built on developing players who others overlooked — is set to make her own broadcast debut on Saturday, July 11, on MLB.TV.

Two consecutive nights. Two legendary coaches. Both lending their voices to the most important stretch of the AUSL's second season.

The Talons lead the AUSL at 14-5 with 10 games remaining. The playoffs begin July 23. The Championship Series is July 25-27. The games in between are the ones Gasso and Alameda will be calling — and the combination of their expertise and the league's rising stakes makes for exactly the kind of broadcast moment that professional softball has been building toward.
