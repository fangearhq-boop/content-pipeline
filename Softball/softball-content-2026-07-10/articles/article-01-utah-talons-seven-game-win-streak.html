Utah Talons Roll to Seven-Game Win Streak With 10 Games Left in AUSL Season

By Jordan Reeves | July 10, 2026

The Utah Talons have won seven games in a row, and the AUSL regular season is down to its final ten.

Utah beat the Oklahoma City Spark on Thursday night at Tom Heath Field to extend the longest active winning streak in the league and push their record to 14-5 — giving them a comfortable grip on first place in the standings with the regular season set to end on July 20. In a league where the No. 1 seed earns a direct bye to the Championship Series, skipping the July 23 play-in game entirely, those final ten games are everything.

The Talons are not just winning. They are building separation at exactly the right moment.

What Seven Straight Wins Means

The AUSL is a short regular season — 24 games for each team, with the playoff bracket seeded directly from the final standings. In that compressed format, a 7-game win streak is not just impressive. It is potentially season-defining.

The Chicago Bandits entered Thursday at 10-7 and had their own 6-game winning streak coming in. They did not play Thursday night, meaning the gap between Utah and Chicago grew to four games in the loss column before the Bandits can respond. The Carolina Blaze and Portland Cascade, tied at 8-7, are both in mathematical contention for the top-two playoff spots but need the Talons to stumble.

With pitcher Montana Fouts setting the AUSL single-game strikeout record earlier this week with 12 Ks against the Texas Volts, Utah has both the momentum and the ace. Fouts is 3-1 with a 2.04 ERA on the season — a line that places her firmly in the conversation for the league's best pitcher.

Tonight's Doubleheader Has Standings Implications

While the Talons have the night off, Friday features two important AUSL games that will reshape the standings by morning.

At 7:00 PM ET, the Carolina Blaze host the Texas Volts at Dell Diamond in Round Rock, Texas. A Blaze win closes their gap with the Bandits and keeps Carolina's play-in positioning live. At 9:30 PM ET, the Chicago Bandits travel to Hillsboro Ballpark in Portland to face the Portland Cascade — a matchup that pits second place against fourth in a game that matters to everyone in the standings.

The Bandits need wins to stay within reach of Utah. The Blaze and Cascade need wins to stay alive in the top-two race. The Talons, sitting at 14-5 and watching, have set the target number for everyone else.

Gasso in the Booth

Thursday's Talons-Spark game also marked a historic moment for AUSL broadcasts: Oklahoma legend Patty Gasso made her professional broadcast debut on ESPNU, calling the game live from Tom Heath Field. Gasso, who won eight national titles at Oklahoma — the most in college softball history — is back in the booth on Saturday for the next Talons game on MLB Network. Lonni Alameda (Louisiana-Lafayette) joins the broadcast on Saturday as well.

The presence of two of the most knowledgeable analysts in the sport's history coincides with the regular season's most meaningful stretch. The AUSL's biggest games are coming, and they will have the best voices to call them.
