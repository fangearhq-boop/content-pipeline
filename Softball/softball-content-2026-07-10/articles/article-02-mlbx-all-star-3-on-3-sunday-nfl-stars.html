MLBx All-Star 3-on-3 Is Sunday — NFL Stars Join AUSL Players for Philadelphia Showcase

By Sarah Mitchell | July 10, 2026

The biggest softball moment of MLB All-Star Week is two days away — and it's turned into something bigger than anyone expected.

The inaugural MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 takes place Sunday, July 12, at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, following the All-Star Futures Game, streaming live on Peacock. Four AUSL players are participating. Four Philadelphia legends are leading their teams. And confirmed NFL stars — including DeVonta Smith and Terrell Owens — are part of the mix, making this a co-ed, multi-sport competition unlike anything softball fans have seen on the All-Star stage.

The Four Teams

Each team is led by a Phillies-era icon and anchored by an AUSL standout:

Team Howard is led by Ryan Howard, the Phillies' all-time home run leader. AUSL player: Alyssa Brito of the Carolina Blaze — a three-time WCWS champion at Oklahoma and first-time U.S. Women's National Team member.

Team Rollins is led by Jimmy Rollins, the 2008 World Series MVP shortstop. AUSL player: Tiare Jennings of the Texas Volts — a four-time WCWS champion at Oklahoma and one of the most decorated players to enter professional softball.

Team Victorino is led by Shane Victorino, "The Flyin' Hawaiian." AUSL player: Rachel Garcia of the Texas Volts — an Olympic silver medalist (Tokyo 2020) and 2019 WCWS champion from UCLA. Garcia, widely considered one of the most dominant two-way players in the history of the sport, brings the most experienced international profile of the four.

Team Jones is led by Andruw Jones, the Braves center field legend widely regarded as one of the greatest defensive outfielders in MLB history. AUSL player: NiJaree Canady of the Texas Volts — the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL College Draft, two-time NFCA Pitcher of the Year, and one of TIME Magazine's 100 Most Influential People in Sports in 2026.

NFL Makes It a Multi-Sport Spectacle

DeVonta Smith and Terrell Owens are among the NFL participants confirmed for the event — extending the co-ed competition beyond its original baseball-and-softball framing into a full multi-sport crossover. The result is a format that puts professional softball players on a platform alongside the biggest names in American sports, at a venue and during a week that commands a national audience.

The event runs 30 minutes per game — two semi-finals followed by a final. The scoring combines offense and defense, with home runs and catches each earning points, and the "hot streak" bonus doubling all points during a designated 5-swing window. It's a fast, high-scoring format designed for exactly the kind of prime-time showcase that softball's top athletes have rarely had at this scale.

Why This Matters

Professional softball has long had the talent. What it hasn't always had is the stage. The MLBx event at Citizens Bank Park, with 40,000 seats and the full weight of MLB All-Star Week behind it, is the largest co-ed platform the sport has ever reached at the national level.

When Brito, Jennings, Garcia, and Canady take the field Sunday, they won't just be playing a game. They'll be showing the full All-Star Week audience — and everyone watching on Peacock — that professional softball belongs in that building.

What's Next

MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3, Sunday July 12, Citizens Bank Park, Philadelphia. The event airs live on Peacock following the All-Star Futures Game. Check the AUSL's official channels and MLB.com/AUSL for the start time and streaming details.
