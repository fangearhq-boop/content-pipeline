Samantha Swan Signs with Texas: Two-Time Champs Fill Biggest Portal Need

By Sarah Mitchell

June 12, 2026

The two-time defending national champions moved fast, and it paid off. Texas softball has officially signed Samantha Swan, the No. 1 catcher in the transfer portal and a unanimous First-Team All-Big 12 selection out of Arizona State. Two days after entering the portal, Swan is coming home to Texas.

The Georgetown, Texas, native fills the most urgent roster hole the Longhorns faced entering the offseason. With Reese Atwood's graduation leaving catching depth as the program's primary concern, head coach Mike White turned to the portal — and secured one of its top prizes almost immediately.

Swan's Numbers Speak for Themselves

In 62 games during the 2026 season, Swan slashed .383/.450/.700 with 69 hits, 12 home runs, 55 RBIs, and 21 doubles. Softball America ranked her No. 9 overall in the portal and the No. 1 available catcher. Her .917 career OPS reflects a player who hits for both average and power at a premium defensive position.

Swan's defensive metrics were equally impressive. She posted a .984 fielding percentage this past season and brings an arm and game-calling ability that Austin head coaches have recruited to replace in recent years.

Coming Home

Swan's decision to choose Texas wasn't purely about championships — though those help. Georgetown, Texas, sits just 30 minutes north of the University of Texas campus. For Swan, this signing is a homecoming.

"She's a hometown kid coming back to play for one of the best programs in the country," one source familiar with her recruitment noted. The pull of proximity, combined with the chance to compete for a third consecutive national title, made the decision straightforward.

Her connection to Austin runs even deeper. In the 2026 Super Regional, Swan went 4-for-10 against the Longhorns — the team she now joins — with a home run and five RBIs. She's already proven she can compete at the highest level against this program. Now she gets to compete with them.

The 3-Peat Roster Takes Shape

Swan's signing lands at an interesting moment. The Longhorns are navigating a significant coaching staff transition — associate head coach Karen Zaleski and offensive coordinator Ehren Earleywine both departed in the same week — but the portal work shows the program's recruiting infrastructure remains elite.

With catching now covered, attention turns to the remaining blue-chip portal targets. D1 Softball Player of the Year Isa Torres — also from Georgetown, Texas — remains uncommitted on Day 5 of the portal window. Torres and Swan grew up in the same community. Whether Swan's signing influences Torres's decision is the question everyone in softball is asking right now.

What's Next

Swan arrives with one year of eligibility and immediate eligibility, meaning she'll be on the field from Day 1 of the 2027 season. With Swan locked in and the portal window running through June 22, Texas now turns its full recruiting attention to the remaining elite targets — including Torres, who could make her decision any day. The 3-peat chase is officially on, and the roster pieces are falling into place.
