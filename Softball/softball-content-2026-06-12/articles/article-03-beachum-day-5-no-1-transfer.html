The Bidding War Continues: Jaysoni Beachum, Day 5 Uncommitted, Nation's No. 1 Transfer

By Jordan Reeves

June 12, 2026

In a portal window full of elite names, Softball America has a clear opinion about who sits at the top. Jaysoni Beachum — third baseman, Florida State, three-year starter, 2024 NFCA Division I Freshman of the Year, 2026 Second-Team All-American — is ranked the No. 1 transfer available. And as of Day 5, she still hasn't committed.

The programs that have been in contact read like a who's who of college softball's elite tier. But Beachum isn't rushing.

Three Years That Defined a Career

The numbers Beachum produced over three seasons at FSU are staggering. Career: 37 home runs, 35 doubles, 180 RBIs, 158 runs scored, 202 hits. Three years of consistent, dominant, power-hitting production from a player who arrived at Florida State as the most hyped freshman recruit in the program's recent history.

In 2024, as a freshman, she didn't just perform — she made history. Her freshman season was so dominant that she became the first Florida State player and the first ACC athlete to ever win the NFCA Division I Freshman of the Year award. The honor had previously gone exclusively to players from the SEC and Pac-12.

Her 2026 season added a different dimension: .417 batting average, 16 home runs, 66 RBIs. But the number that defines her evolution as a hitter is the walk rate. She drew 54 walks, producing a .549 on-base percentage. That's the mark of a disciplined power hitter — not a free-swinger chasing home runs, but a batter who forces pitchers to work and punishes them when they miss their spot.

What Programs Are Getting

Any program landing Beachum isn't just getting one year of elite production. They're getting a player who has already proven she gets better each season. From freshman year to junior year, her plate discipline improved, her power numbers held, and her defensive profile at third base remained one of the better ones in the ACC.

She arrives with one year of eligibility remaining, meaning she'll immediately be the most experienced player in her new lineup. For a program chasing a championship, that kind of veteran presence in the middle of the batting order is invaluable.

No Rush, No Leaks

Recruiting sources have been unusually quiet around Beachum's process. Unlike some portal situations where destinations are reported within hours of entry, Beachum's recruitment has remained largely contained. No confirmed visits have been publicly reported. No program has been named with any reliability.

That quiet is itself a signal. Players with the most options tend to run the most deliberate processes. Beachum has no reason to rush. Her leverage is maximized as long as she remains uncommitted.

What's Next

With ten days remaining in the portal window, Beachum's decision could come any time between now and June 22. Watch for any official visit reports as the clearest signal of timing. Wherever she commits, expect an immediate announcement from that program — landing the No. 1 transfer in the nation will be treated as a major recruiting victory. The sport's most coveted player will find her new home before the month is out.
