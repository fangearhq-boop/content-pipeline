The Torres Watch: Day 5, Ten Days Left, and the Softball World Still Waiting

By Emily Rawlings

June 12, 2026

Day five of the transfer portal window has arrived, and Isa Torres still hasn't made her decision. The best shortstop in college softball — the D1 Softball Player of the Year, the ACC's top player, the woman who hit .530 with an .978 slugging percentage and set an NCAA record with a hit in 16 consecutive at-bats — is out there, available, and taking her time.

The portal window opened June 8. It closes June 22. Ten days remain. And the two-time defending national champions have just made their roster significantly more attractive.

The Numbers That Made Her Untouchable

What Torres accomplished at Florida State in 2026 demands context. A .530 batting average in Division I softball is historically extraordinary — the kind of number that appears in record books, not box scores. Her .591 on-base percentage meant she was reaching base more than half the time she stepped to the plate. Her .978 slugging percentage put her in a tier of offensive production that virtually no shortstop in the sport's history has occupied.

She scored 78 runs. She set an NCAA record for consecutive at-bats with a hit, reaching 16. She was named both D1 Softball Player of the Year and ACC Player of the Year. Now she has one year of eligibility remaining and can play anywhere she chooses.

The Frontrunners

Texas and Texas A&M remain the most-reported programs in contact with Torres. The geography is compelling on both fronts. Torres is from Georgetown, Texas — the same Georgetown that just sent catcher Samantha Swan to the Longhorns. The two grew up in the same community, and with Swan now committed to Texas, the narrative around Torres's decision has intensified further.

Austin sits 30 minutes from Georgetown. The University of Texas already has two national championships on the wall. The program offers Torres the chance to win a third straight title while competing closer to home than she's been in years.

Texas A&M, meanwhile, offers a different kind of appeal — the chance to be the signature piece of a program building toward its first title, in a city that would treat her as the face of a movement.

The Bigger Picture

Torres's deliberate approach is not unusual for a player of her caliber. The transfer portal can be chaotic — quick decisions made under recruiting pressure — but a player with Torres's profile has the leverage to take her time. She doesn't need to rush. Programs will wait.

What's notable is the layered context building around her choice. In the same week she entered the portal, Texas lost two assistant coaches and gained the top catcher in the transfer market. The program is in motion. A yes from Torres would stabilize it at every level.

What's Next

With ten days remaining in the portal window, the Torres decision could come at any moment. Official visits, if scheduled, typically precede a commitment by days rather than weeks at this stage of the process. Watch for visit reports as the clearest signal of which direction she's leaning. If Torres announces before June 22, it will be the single biggest portal story in college softball this offseason.
