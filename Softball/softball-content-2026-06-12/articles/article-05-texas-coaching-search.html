Two Open Seats, One Championship Window: Texas Races to Rebuild Its Coaching Staff

By Emily Rawlings

June 12, 2026

Mike White is the head coach of the most successful program in college softball right now. He also has two open coaching positions, a 15-day portal window that's already five days old, and a three-peat to build toward. The next hire at Texas isn't just a staffing decision. It's a statement about whether this dynasty can sustain itself through transition.

What Texas Lost

In the span of one week following the national championship, Texas lost two key members of its coaching staff.

Associate head coach Karen Zaleski — recently promoted to that role before the 2026 season — departed for opportunities elsewhere. Her role touched multiple areas of the program's day-to-day operation and player development infrastructure.

The larger operational impact comes from the departure of Ehren Earleywine, the offensive coordinator who is now heading to Tennessee as associate head coach under Kim Weekly. Earleywine was the architect of Texas's historically explosive offense in 2026: 99 team home runs, a school record that stands alongside the unprecedented production from Katie Stewart's program-record 31-HR season. His offense turned a championship-caliber team into something that resembled a video game.

He didn't just leave. He went to the team most motivated to compete with Texas in 2027.

What White Did First

Before the coaching hire conversation even started, White made a portal move. Arizona State graduate transfer catcher Samantha Swan — No. 1 catcher in the portal, First-Team All-Big 12, .383 BA, .917 career OPS — signed with Texas within 48 hours of entering the market.

That recruitment speaks to the program's operational efficiency even in transition. No staff changes slowed down the portal response. The machinery of Texas softball's recruiting network is still working.

The Coaching Carousel Is Active

The NFCA coaching transaction board has been busy across the sport. Colorado State hired Michelle Gascoigne, the longtime Northwestern pitching coach. Tulsa added DJ Gasso, the son of Oklahoma's legendary Patty Gasso, from Arkansas. Nebraska added Jordy Frahm, a four-time NFCA All-American. The market for qualified coaches is competitive, and programs that move quickly tend to win these searches.

White's two openings will attract interest from ambitious assistants across the sport. The question isn't whether Texas can hire quality replacements — it's whether they can hire them quickly enough to keep recruiting momentum from stalling.

What's Next

The portal window closes June 22. Mike White needs to be in those conversations — actively recruiting players like Isa Torres and Moriah Polar — while simultaneously building his coaching staff. Every day without named replacements is a day of uncertainty for the program's development infrastructure. Expect the searches to move quickly. The Texas brand attracts applicants; White's track record retains them. The program is in transition, but its foundation is intact. The next chapter of this dynasty starts with two phone calls and a hire date.
