Portal Roundup: Alabama Lands Plumlee, Polar Still Available as Window Hits Midpoint

By Sarah Mitchell

June 12, 2026

The transfer portal window has been open for five days. The headlines have centered on Isa Torres and Jaysoni Beachum, but the moves happening around those stories are shaping the sport's competitive landscape just as meaningfully. Alabama secured a foundational piece for its future pitching staff. And the nation's leading hitter is still out there, waiting for the right call.

Alabama Adds Left-Handed Arm in Plumlee

Kailey Plumlee, a redshirt freshman out of Tennessee, committed to Alabama on June 10 — becoming the first left-handed pitcher on the Crimson Tide's roster since Lauren Esman in 2024. She brings four full years of eligibility, giving Alabama a long-range planning asset at a premium pitching position.

Plumlee's path has been unconventional. The 2023-2024 Gatorade Player of the Year at Tennessee — a remarkable individual honor for a high school athlete in the sport — sat out the entire 2026 season without appearing in a game. Now she's heading to Tuscaloosa with a fresh start and an opportunity to develop alongside one of college softball's premier pitching staffs.

The timing is sharp for Alabama. The Crimson Tide ranked second nationally with a 1.57 ERA last season, anchored by SEC Pitcher of the Year Jocelyn Briski and freshmen Vic Moten and Kaitlyn Pallozzi. Adding a four-year left-handed arm deepens a rotation that went to the national semifinals — and gives head coach Patrick Murphy flexibility he currently doesn't have.

Moriah Polar: The Nation's Leading Hitter, Still Waiting

While Plumlee found her home, Moriah Polar is still looking. The Purdue outfielder — who led all of Division I with a .555 batting average and 101 hits in 2026 — is on Day 5 of the portal window with no announced commitment.

Polar's career numbers at Purdue put her among the best pure hitters the sport has produced: 218 hits, 103 RBIs, 137 runs scored over three seasons. She was a second-team All-American and one year of eligibility remains. For a program needing a capable, proven bat in the middle of its lineup, Polar is a program-altering addition.

No confirmed destinations have been reported. Her recruitment, like Beachum's, has remained unusually quiet for a player of her profile.

The Broader Portal Picture

Beyond the marquee names, several programs have made smaller-scale moves that add up. Georgia added FAU graduate transfer outfielder Kylie Hammonds. Arizona State's transfer portal activity has included a pitcher departing for UCLA. The Mountain West and Big 12 programs are actively navigating a portal window that affects roster-building at every level of the sport.

With ten days remaining and the portal closing June 22, the pace of commitments is expected to accelerate. Programs that have been in evaluation mode will begin finalizing offers. Players who have completed official visits will start announcing. The next 72 hours could bring clarity on several of the window's biggest names.

What's Next

Alabama's Plumlee addition is complete — four years of development time for a left-handed pitcher in a program that knows how to develop pitchers. For Moriah Polar, the decision timeline is narrowing: with the portal window closing June 22, she has ten days to choose her next chapter. The program that lands her gains the nation's best pure hitter with proven production. Her announcement, when it comes, will be one of the portal window's major stories.
