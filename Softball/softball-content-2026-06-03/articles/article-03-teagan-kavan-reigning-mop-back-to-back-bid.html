Teagan Kavan Won It All Last Year. Now She's Coming for More.

By Jordan Reeves

June 3, 2026

The reigning WCWS Most Outstanding Player threw a dominant semifinal shutout and carries nine WCWS wins into tonight's championship opener. Two more wins would give Teagan Kavan a back-to-back title and a legacy few college softball pitchers can match.

She Already Won It. She Wants It Again.

Winning the Women's College World Series once is a career achievement. Winning it twice — back-to-back, as the ace of the team carrying the target — would put Teagan Kavan in a category that barely exists in college softball history.

Right now, standing on the eve of Game 1 of the 2026 championship series, Kavan looks completely capable of doing exactly that.

On Monday, in the semifinal against Tennessee, the Texas senior threw a complete-game shutout: 10 strikeouts, 2 hits allowed, 0 walks, 4-0 final. It was a performance so thorough that it barely felt like a contest. The Lady Vols — led by all-time great Karlyn Pickens in her final career start — never had an answer. Kavan dictated every at-bat, changed speeds when she needed to, and made the sport look simple.

It was not simple. It was elite.

Building a Texas Legacy

The shutout was Kavan's fifth career shutout at the WCWS — tying Texas legend Cat Osterman's program record. It was also her ninth career WCWS win, the most in Texas program history.

Those numbers matter in context. Osterman is one of the most celebrated figures in the history of the sport. She was a two-time national champion, a three-time WCWS Most Outstanding Player, and the pitcher synonymous with a generation of Texas softball dominance. Kavan has now matched her on the shutouts list. And she still has games to pitch this week.

Kavan was named the 2025 WCWS Most Outstanding Player after leading Texas to the title. She is the reigning champion's ace, the most decorated pitcher in Oklahoma City this week, and the reason Texas enters tonight's Game 1 as the favorite.

The Challenge Tonight

Texas Tech is not a team you can approach passively. NiJaree Canady is the best opposing pitcher in the tournament — a historic talent on her own right, having just become only the fourth pitcher in WCWS history to record a shutout in four different tournaments.

The Red Raiders also have Mia Williams (26 HRs) and a lineup that has shown it can put up runs in bunches. They beat No. 1 Alabama twice in one day. They are not here to lose a rematch gracefully.

For Kavan, that is the challenge she has earned by being so good for so long: the sport's best pitching matchup, on its biggest stage, when every pitch matters most. She has been here before. She knows what it takes to close it out.

What's on the Line

If Texas wins the 2026 WCWS championship, Kavan will finish her college career with two rings, two or more MOP awards, the program record for WCWS wins, and a shutout record that equals the program's all-time great. That resume would make her one of the most decorated pitchers in the history of college softball.

It starts tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN.

What's Next

Teagan Kavan and No. 2 Texas open the 2026 WCWS Championship Series tonight against No. 11 Texas Tech at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, 8 PM ET on ESPN. A Texas sweep in the best-of-three series would deliver back-to-back national championships and cement Kavan's legacy as one of the most decorated pitchers in program history.
