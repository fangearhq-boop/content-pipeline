History on the Line: Texas vs. Texas Tech in WCWS Championship Game 1

By Sarah Mitchell

June 3, 2026

For the first time since the Women's College World Series adopted its best-of-three championship format in 2005, the same two programs are meeting in back-to-back national title series. Tonight at Devon Park, No. 2 Texas and No. 11 Texas Tech renew their rivalry with everything on the line.

A Rematch 21 Years in the Making

The Women's College World Series has crowned many great champions since switching to its best-of-three championship format in 2005. But in all those years, no two programs had ever met in consecutive national championship series — until now.

Texas (51-12) and Texas Tech (61-8) are back in the same spot they stood a year ago, facing each other in the championship series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. Last June, the Longhorns won that series 2-1 to claim the national title. Now the Red Raiders are back, and they believe this time the result will be different.

Game 1 tips off at 8 PM ET on ESPN.

How They Got Here

Texas's path to the championship series was clinical and convincing. The Longhorns swept Tennessee in Monday's semifinal, with ace Teagan Kavan throwing a complete-game shutout in the clincher — limiting the Lady Vols to two hits while striking out 10 in a 4-0 victory. Kavan, the reigning WCWS Most Outstanding Player, now has nine WCWS wins in her career — a Texas program record.

Texas Tech's road was more dramatic, but equally impressive. The Red Raiders were the No. 11 national seed entering the tournament, meaning they had to win six games to reach the title. They beat UCLA 8-7 in extra innings to advance to the semifinals, then faced No. 1 Alabama twice in the same day. In Game 1, Mia Williams launched a walk-off home run — the 26th of her season and the first walk-off of her career — to force a deciding game. Then ace NiJaree Canady took over, throwing a complete-game 2-hit shutout to eliminate the Crimson Tide and punch the Red Raiders' ticket to the finals.

The Pitching Matchup

When Canady and Kavan share a tournament, college softball reaches its ceiling. Both pitchers have been elite this spring and at their best in Oklahoma City.

Kavan has been virtually unhittable. After her 10-strikeout shutout of Tennessee — in which she allowed just two hits and zero walks — the senior right-hander enters Game 1 with five career WCWS shutouts, tying Texas legend Cat Osterman on the program's all-time list.

Canady has been equally dominant from the other side. The Texas Tech ace became only the fourth pitcher in WCWS history to record a shutout in four different tournament appearances, joining Nancy Evans, Lisa Longaker, and Debbie Doom in that exclusive group. Her complete-game performance against Alabama — two hits, six strikeouts — showed she can be at her best on the sport's biggest stage.

Whoever wins this pitching duel likely wins the game. And possibly the series.

Power Enters the Equation

While the pitching gets the headlines, offense is how these teams will ultimately try to win. Texas enters with the most dangerous lineup in college softball. Slugger Katie Stewart hit her 29th and 30th home runs of the season against Tennessee, setting a new Texas single-season program record and helping the Longhorns reach 97 team home runs on the year — also a program record, surpassing the 94 hit in 2025.

Texas Tech counters with its own power. Williams (26 HRs) showed she can deliver in the most pressure-packed moments. If Canady keeps Texas off the scoreboard, the Red Raiders' offense only needs one big moment to change the game.

What History Says About Rematches

The runner-up returning the next year to face the same champion is rare — and historically, it doesn't go well for the team that lost. In the three documented rematch sequences in WCWS history, the runner-up broke through and won only once. Texas Tech knows what history says. They also know they're not interested in following it.

The Red Raiders have won 61 games this season. They beat the No. 1 team in the country twice in one day. They believe — genuinely — that this is their year.

Tonight at Devon Park, we'll find out if they're right.

What's Next

Game 1 of the WCWS Championship Series tips off tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN from Devon Park in Oklahoma City. If Texas wins Game 1, the Longhorns will need one more win to repeat as national champions. If Texas Tech takes Game 1, the series shifts back on the Red Raiders' terms. Game 2 is scheduled for Thursday, June 4. A potential Game 3 would follow on Friday, June 5.
