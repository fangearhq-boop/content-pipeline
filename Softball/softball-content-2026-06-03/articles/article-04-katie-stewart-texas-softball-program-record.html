Katie Stewart Just Broke the Texas Home Run Record. Now She Wants the Title.

By Sarah Mitchell

June 3, 2026

With her 29th and 30th home runs of the season against Tennessee, Texas slugger Katie Stewart set a new single-season program record. The Longhorns enter the championship series with 97 team home runs — also a program record — and Stewart is the engine driving it all.

Record-Breaking in the Middle of the Biggest Game of the Year

Most players dream of setting a program record. Katie Stewart did it in the middle of a Women's College World Series semifinal sweep.

Against Tennessee on Monday, Stewart launched her 29th home run of the season in one game and followed it up with No. 30 before the series was over. The 30 home runs exceeded the previous Texas single-season program record of 28, meaning Stewart now owns the most powerful offensive season in the history of the Texas Longhorns softball program.

The record came quietly, in the context of a championship push, which says everything about how this team operates: history happens here, but winning is the only thing that matters.

The Most Powerful Team in College Softball

Stewart's record is remarkable in isolation. It becomes even more striking in team context. Texas as a unit has now hit 97 home runs in 2026 — surpassing the program's 2025 record of 94 and making the Longhorns the most prolific power-hitting team in the history of Texas softball.

That production doesn't happen by accident. Texas has built a lineup with depth and power at almost every spot in the order. But Stewart is the centerpiece. She is the hitter opposing pitchers most want to avoid, the player whose at-bats shift the energy in a game, and the most dangerous home run threat on the field tonight at Devon Park.

Against the Best Pitching She'll Face All Season

The challenge for Stewart in the championship series is clear: NiJaree Canady. Texas Tech's ace has been historically dominant at the WCWS, and holding batters like Stewart in check is exactly what she is capable of doing.

But this is also where the 2026 season has proven something about this Texas lineup. The Longhorns have faced elite pitching before and produced. They have 97 home runs as a team across a schedule that included some of the best pitchers in the country.

Stewart, in particular, has shown an ability to hit for power in every context. Her 30 home runs didn't come only against weak competition — they came against SEC opponents and high-caliber postseason pitching. Canady will test her. That's not in question. But Stewart has passed every test she's been given this season.

Why She Matters in the Championship Series

In a game where both pitching aces figure to dominate, runs will be at a premium. The team that can scratch out one or two critical hits — or connect on a momentum-swinging home run — will have a significant advantage. That is the exact kind of at-bat Katie Stewart was built to deliver.

If Texas is going to win back-to-back championships, Stewart's bat will play a central role in how it happens. Game 1 is tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN, and her matchup against Canady is among the most anticipated individual battles of the entire WCWS.

What's Next

Katie Stewart and Texas open the 2026 WCWS Championship Series tonight against Texas Tech at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, 8 PM ET on ESPN. A Texas victory would put the Longhorns one win away from back-to-back national titles. Stewart's matchup against NiJaree Canady is one of the most compelling storylines of the championship series.
