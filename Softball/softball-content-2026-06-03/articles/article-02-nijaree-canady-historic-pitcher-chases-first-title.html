NiJaree Canady Has Made WCWS History. Now She Wants a Ring.

By Emily Rawlings

June 3, 2026

Texas Tech's ace became the fourth pitcher in Women's College World Series history to record a shutout in four different tournaments. She has done everything but win a national championship. Tonight, she gets her best chance yet.

Four Tournaments. Four Shutouts. No Title — Yet.

Numbers don't always capture greatness, but in NiJaree Canady's case, they come remarkably close.

In Monday's decisive semifinal against No. 1 Alabama, Canady threw a complete-game, 2-hit shutout with six strikeouts — a 2-0 win that sent Texas Tech to the national championship series. With that performance, she became just the fourth pitcher in Women's College World Series history to record a shutout in four different tournament appearances. The other three — Nancy Evans, Lisa Longaker, and Debbie Doom — are names that belong in any serious conversation about the greatest college softball pitchers ever.

Canady belongs there too. And tonight she has a chance to make her legacy undeniable.

A Dominant Tournament Performance

Canady came to Oklahoma City this year carrying a 2026 season record of 29-6 and the reputation of someone who rises when the stakes are highest.

She backed it up immediately. Against Alabama in the semifinal opener, Canady came on in relief and threw 2.2 innings, giving up just one run and keeping the game close enough for Mia Williams to win it with a walk-off home run in the seventh. Then, in the deciding game, Canady stepped back to the circle and delivered one of the tournament's most dominant performances — nine batters up, none reaching base safely on anything beyond two scattered singles, six punched out.

When it was over, Texas Tech was in the championship series, and Canady had etched her name into WCWS history.

The One Thing Missing

In most conversations about Canady, there is an asterisk that those outside of Lubbock are too quick to apply: she has never won a national championship.

That framing, while technically accurate, misses almost everything about what Canady has accomplished. She has taken Texas Tech — a program that was not considered a national power five years ago — to the WCWS multiple times and now to consecutive championship series. She has put up numbers that would be legendary in any era of college softball.

But championships are what define legacies, and Canady knows it. After Monday's win, she didn't linger on the history she'd just made. She turned her attention, quickly and completely, to what comes next.

The Matchup She's Been Built For

Canady will face Texas — the defending champions — in Game 1 tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN. The Longhorns are led by ace Teagan Kavan and slugger Katie Stewart, who just set a new Texas single-season home run record with 30 on the season. This is the best team in college softball, and stopping them requires pitching at the highest level.

Canady has never been afraid of that challenge. She has beaten the best lineups in the country throughout her career. She has done it at the WCWS in four different years. She has shown, repeatedly, that she can shut down any offense in the country when it matters most.

Tonight is the moment she has been building toward. And if anyone can pitch Texas Tech to its first national championship, it is NiJaree Canady.

What's Next

NiJaree Canady and Texas Tech take on defending champion Texas in Game 1 of the 2026 WCWS Championship Series tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN from Devon Park in Oklahoma City. Game 2 is scheduled for Thursday, June 4. A potential Game 3 would be played Friday, June 5. A Texas Tech win in the best-of-three series would give Canady and the Red Raiders their first national championship in program history.
