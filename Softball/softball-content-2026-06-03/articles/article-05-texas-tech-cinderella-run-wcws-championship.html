How Texas Tech Became the Most Unlikely Team at the WCWS Championship

By Emily Rawlings

June 3, 2026

The Red Raiders entered the Women's College World Series as the No. 11 national seed. They beat the No. 1 team twice in one day. Tonight they play for the national championship. This is how it happened.

They Were Never Supposed to Be Here

No. 11. That was Texas Tech's national seeding entering the 2026 Women's College World Series.

In practical terms, that means the Red Raiders earned no hosting rights during the regional rounds of the NCAA tournament, faced a long travel schedule, and arrived in Oklahoma City as the lowest-seeded team among the final eight programs standing. It meant they had to win six games at the WCWS to reach the championship — more than any other contender.

They did it anyway.

Game by Game: The Most Improbable Run

Texas Tech opened the WCWS with a gritty challenge from UCLA — a program loaded with power, including Megan Grant, who set the NCAA single-season home run record this year. The Red Raiders and Bruins went to extra innings in a game that came down to the final out. Texas Tech won 8-7, with NiJaree Canady punching out the final batter to preserve the victory. One game in. One elimination avoided.

Then came Monday — the day that should be studied in college softball programs for years. Texas Tech needed two wins over No. 1 Alabama to advance to the championship series. The Crimson Tide were the top seed in the tournament, the program with the most dominant regular season record in the country, and one of the best rosters assembled in recent memory.

Game 1 went back and forth. Alabama tied it late. Then, in the bottom of the seventh inning, Mia Williams stepped to the plate and sent a solo shot out of Devon Park — her 26th home run of the season and the first walk-off home run of her career. Final: 5-4 Texas Tech. The game was forced into a winner-take-all rematch scheduled for later that same evening.

In that decisive game, Canady was brilliant. She allowed just two hits. She struck out six. She did not give Alabama a moment to breathe, pitching her way to a complete-game shutout and a 2-0 final. Texas Tech was going to the championship.

The People Who Made It Happen

Two players define this run more than anyone else. Canady — who made WCWS history in the process, becoming only the fourth pitcher to record a shutout in four different tournaments — provided the pitching excellence that made everything possible. Without her, none of this happens.

But Williams deserves her own moment. Her 26 home runs represent one of the best power seasons in Texas Tech program history, and her walk-off against Alabama was the turning point of the entire tournament. It was the hit that changed what this week could be.

Behind them is a 61-8 Texas Tech team that has been built methodically and is playing its best softball at the perfect time.

One More Win

Tonight at Devon Park, Texas Tech faces Texas — the defending champions, the program that beat them in last year's championship series, the team they are hunting. The Longhorns enter as the favorite. They should. They are the defending champions with the best pitcher in the field and a lineup that just broke its own program home run record.

But Texas Tech was the No. 11 seed too. And they beat No. 1 Alabama. Twice.

They are not afraid of being the underdog. This entire season has been about proving something. Tonight is just the latest — and most important — chapter of that story.

What's Next

No. 11 Texas Tech opens the 2026 WCWS Championship Series tonight against No. 2 Texas at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, 8 PM ET on ESPN. A Texas Tech win in the best-of-three series would deliver the program's first national championship and cap one of the most dramatic underdog runs in WCWS history. Game 2 is scheduled for Thursday, June 4, with a potential Game 3 on Friday, June 5.
