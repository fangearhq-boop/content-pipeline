Six Days Left: The Softball Community Must Vote for Megan Grant at the ESPYs

By Sarah Mitchell | July 9, 2026

Six days. One vote per day. This is not the time to stop.

Megan Grant is nominated for Best Record-Breaking Performance at the 2026 ESPYs, which air Wednesday, July 15, at 8 PM ET on ABC. Voting closes the same night — and the softball community has six more votes available to each person who shows up every day between now and then.

What She Did

In case anyone needs a reminder: Grant set the NCAA Division I single-season home run record in 2026 with 42 home runs in 63 games at UCLA. The previous record of 37 had been set by Arizona's Laura Espinoza in 1995 — a mark that stood for 31 years before Grant finally broke it.

She also became UCLA's all-time career home run leader with 91, passing Stacey Nuveman. And she did all of this while leading the Bruins deep into the postseason before taking her game to the professional level as the No. 4 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL College Draft, landing with the Portland Cascade.

A 31-year record. 42 home runs. The all-time UCLA mark. By any objective standard, what Grant accomplished in 2026 belongs in the conversation for the best single-season performance in the history of college softball.

The Competition

The Best Record-Breaking Performance category is legitimate. Grant's competitors include Myles Garrett of the Los Angeles Rams, Norwegian cross-country skier Johannes Høsflot Klæbo — who has won multiple Olympic gold medals — and Kenyan marathon runner Sabastian Sawe.

These are genuine, elite athletic performances. This is not a category where the softball community can assume Grant wins by default. The NFL has a larger fan base. Cross-country skiing has passionate followers. Marathon running has a global audience. The ESPY in this category will go to the athlete whose community votes hardest — and softball needs to show up.

How to Vote

Go to ESPN.com. Navigate to the ESPY voting page. Find Best Record-Breaking Performance and vote for Megan Grant. You can vote once per day — which means there are still six votes left for every person who starts today. You do not need a cable subscription or an ESPN+ account to vote.

The voting window opened June 25 and closes July 15. The ceremony airs at 8 PM ET on ABC and on the ESPN app. If the softball community wants to see one of its own on that stage in New York, this week is when it happens.
