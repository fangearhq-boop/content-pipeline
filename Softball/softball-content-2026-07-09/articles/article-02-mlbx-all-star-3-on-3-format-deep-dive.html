Three Days Until MLBx: What the All-Star 3-On-3 Is and Why Softball Fans Should Watch

By Emily Rawlings | July 9, 2026

Sunday is three days away. If you haven't started paying attention to the MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3, now is the time.

Four AUSL softball stars are heading to Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia for one of the most unique events in All-Star Week history — a co-ed, power-hitting showcase where the rules are different from anything most softball fans have seen before. Here's exactly how it works, and why it's worth clearing your Sunday schedule.

How the Scoring Works

MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 is built around a three-inning structure. Each player on each team gets a two-minute at-bat per inning — so the rounds move fast, and every swing matters. The scoring is simple on the surface: home runs count as one point for the offense, and defensive catches count as one point for the fielding side. That's not a typo — great defense can actually outscore a team's offense, which turns the dynamic inside-out compared to traditional softball.

But the wildcard that will make this event genuinely unpredictable is the hot streak bonus. Any team can call a hot streak, and on the next five consecutive swings, every point scored — offense or defense — is doubled. It's a high-risk, high-reward play that creates momentum swings in real time. A team trailing by three points can call a hot streak and, with the right five swings, erase the deficit entirely.

Bonus points are also available by hitting through a center-field target — a pinpoint accuracy challenge layered on top of the power format.

The Format

The event runs as two semi-finals followed by a final, with each game lasting 30 minutes. Winners advance, and by the end of Sunday afternoon at Citizens Bank Park, one of the four teams will be crowned champion of the inaugural MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3.

The event follows the All-Star Futures Game as part of a doubleheader — meaning softball gets a built-in audience of baseball fans who are already in the park and already paying attention to the sport's next generation of talent.

The Matchups

Each of the four teams is anchored by a Philadelphia Phillies legend or MLB veteran alongside one AUSL softball captain:

Alyssa Brito of the Carolina Blaze captains Team Howard — paired with Ryan Howard, the Phillies' all-time home run leader and one of the most feared left-handed power hitters in baseball history. Brito is one of the AUSL's premier offensive players, and the power pairing with Howard is natural.

Tiare Jennings of the Texas Volts leads Team Rollins — alongside Jimmy Rollins, the 2007 NL MVP and Phillies shortstop legend. Jennings has been a consistent offensive presence for the Volts all season.

Rachel Garcia — Olympic silver medalist and one of the most decorated pitchers in softball history — captains Team Victorino, paired with Shane Victorino, "The Flyin' Hawaiian." Garcia brings championship experience and composure to a format that rewards athleticism and adaptability.

And NiJaree Canady of the Texas Volts leads Team Jones — alongside Andruw Jones, one of the greatest defensive center fielders in baseball history and a 10-time Gold Glove winner. Canady's appearance in a hitting-focused format turns the spotlight away from her current pitching struggles and onto her all-around athleticism. That's a good thing.

Why Watch

Citizens Bank Park seats more than 43,000 people. Four AUSL softball players will be on that field as team captains, competing in a format designed for the biggest casual audience in baseball. This is the AUSL's highest-profile national broadcast moment of the 2026 season — and one of the biggest stages professional softball has ever had.

Sunday, July 12, at Citizens Bank Park. The semi-finals and final follow the Futures Game. Check the MLB All-Star Week schedule for broadcast details. You won't want to miss it.
