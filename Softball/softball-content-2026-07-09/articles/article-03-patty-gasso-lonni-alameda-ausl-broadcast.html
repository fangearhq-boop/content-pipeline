Patty Gasso and Lonni Alameda Join the AUSL Broadcast Booth — Why It Matters

By Jordan Reeves | July 9, 2026

The AUSL's broadcast just added two of the most decorated minds in the history of softball.

Patty Gasso and Lonni Alameda are joining the AUSL broadcast booth this week as analysts, lending their voices to professional game coverage at a critical moment in the league's second season. If you know softball, you know exactly what these two names mean — and why their presence in a broadcast booth elevates the entire enterprise.

Patty Gasso: Eight Titles, One Standard

Gasso spent decades at Oklahoma building the most dominant program in the history of college softball. Eight national championships. Multiple trips to the Women's College World Series. Dozens of Olympians and professional players developed under her watch. She is, by any measure, the most successful softball coach the sport has ever produced.

But what makes Gasso's presence in the AUSL booth especially meaningful is not just the résumé — it's the knowledge base. She recruited and developed many of the players now competing in the AUSL. She understands their mechanics, their mental approach, the way they respond to pressure. When Gasso comments on a pitching sequence or a baserunning decision, she's not offering color commentary — she's offering a coaching analysis that most broadcast booths can only approximate.

When Oklahoma won national titles, it wasn't just because Gasso had better players. It was because she built a culture and a system that made average players better and great players elite. That context sharpens every observation she makes.

Lonni Alameda: Building Champions Without a Recruiting Budget to Match

Alameda's tenure at Louisiana-Lafayette tells a different but equally instructive story. She built sustained excellence at a program that doesn't have the recruiting budgets of the SEC powers — earning multiple Sun Belt Conference titles and developing players who went on to compete at the highest levels of the sport.

She knows what it looks like when a player who wasn't a five-star recruit finds her footing and becomes something special. That perspective adds a layer to broadcast analysis that is often missing: not just evaluating talent, but evaluating development and trajectory.

Between Gasso's championship pedigree and Alameda's under-the-radar building expertise, the AUSL booth gets something close to a complete picture of what coaching at the highest levels of softball actually looks like.

The Right Moment

Their arrival comes with eleven games left in the AUSL regular season and the playoff picture tightening. The Utah Talons lead at 13-5. The Chicago Bandits are on a winning streak in second place. The Carolina Blaze and Portland Cascade are fighting for playoff positioning at 8-7 each. The race for the No. 1 seed — and the direct bye to the Championship Series that comes with it — is very much alive.

This is when the AUSL broadcasts need to be at their best. And they just added two of the best minds in the game to help make that happen.
