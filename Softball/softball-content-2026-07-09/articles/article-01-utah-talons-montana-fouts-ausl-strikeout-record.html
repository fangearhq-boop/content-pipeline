Montana Fouts Sets AUSL Strikeout Record as Utah Talons Lead the League

By Sarah Mitchell | July 9, 2026

The Utah Talons have been the AUSL's most consistent team all season. Montana Fouts just made sure everybody knows it.

Fouts set the AUSL single-game strikeout record this week with 12 strikeouts in a complete-game 4-3 win over the Texas Volts — going all seven innings, allowing five hits, and putting the league's best pitching performance of the season on the board with eleven regular-season games remaining.

The Talons are 13-5, in first place, and playing the Oklahoma City Spark tonight at 8:00 PM ET at Tom Heath Field in Oklahoma City. A win would extend one of the most important cushions in the league standings right now.

Fouts Has Been Here Before

The 12-K game is the latest chapter in a season Fouts has defined from the very first pitch.

On Opening Day, she threw a complete game shutout against the Chicago Bandits — and recorded the very first strikeout in AUSL history, getting Bubba Nickles-Camarena swinging in the bottom of the first inning. Now, two months into the season, she's set the standard for what a dominant pitching performance looks like in a professional softball game.

Her season line reads 3-1 with a 2.04 ERA — the third-best in the league among qualified pitchers. For a team that entered the AUSL's second season without the national spotlight of a NiJaree Canady or a Rachel Garcia, Fouts has been the engine that quietly built a first-place standing.

The Standings Picture

The Talons have a three-game lead over the Chicago Bandits, who enter Thursday at 10-7 and riding a 6-game winning streak of their own. The Carolina Blaze and Portland Cascade are tied for third and fourth at 8-7 — close enough to matter but far enough back that the top-two seeds remain a Talons-Bandits race.

The standings matter for a specific reason: the No. 1 seed in the AUSL earns a direct bye to the Championship Series, skipping the play-in game on July 23. With the regular season ending July 20 and the Championship Series set for July 25-27 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas, every game from here through the finale carries playoff implications.

The Bandits' 6-game winning streak is the subplot that makes tonight's Talons-Spark game more meaningful than a mid-July midweek matchup normally would be. The Talons need to hold ground while Utah's edge is still three games.

A New Voice in the Booth

The Talons-Spark game will also benefit from a broadcast upgrade. Oklahoma legend Patty Gasso — eight national titles, the most in college softball history — and Louisiana-Lafayette head coach Lonni Alameda are joining the AUSL broadcast booth this week. Their presence brings two of the most knowledgeable voices in the sport into professional game coverage at exactly the moment the regular season is becoming must-watch.

Both coaches know these AUSL players well from recruiting and competing against them. Their insights into the pitching-to-hitting match-ups and the personnel decisions down the stretch should add a layer of analysis that the AUSL's broadcast hasn't had before.
