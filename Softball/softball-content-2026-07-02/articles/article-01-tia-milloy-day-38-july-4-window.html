Day 38 and Counting: Tia Milloy's July 4 Window Is Now

By Sarah Mitchell July 2, 2026

Where Things Stand

Tia Milloy entered Oklahoma's transfer portal on May 25. Today is July 2 — Day 38 — and she still has not made a public commitment announcement. The formal 15-day contact window for the 2026 portal cycle opened June 8 and closed June 22. She chose not to announce during it. She is now navigating July on her own terms, and the calendar is narrowing in ways that are starting to matter.

Her market remains strong. A .353 batting average, a .706 slugging percentage, and two full years of remaining eligibility make her one of the most valuable available players in the country. Florida State has been described in multiple reports as actively engaged in conversations with her camp. Tennessee and Texas Tech have maintained contact throughout. None of that has changed. What changes on July 2 is the proximity of a hard deadline.

The July 4 Factor

July 4 weekend falls two days from today. In the recruiting calendar, it functions as the last moment of undivided coaching attention before travel season consumes the summer. Head coaches are home. Recruiting conversations happen without the competing backdrop of tournament evaluation, hotel logistics, or packed evaluation game schedules. A phone call on July 4 weekend carries a different weight than the same conversation made while navigating the sidelines of PGF Nationals.

This is not a manufactured milestone — it is a practical one. Every program pursuing Milloy knows that the recruiting intensity they can bring right now is different from what they can bring starting July 13, when pre-travel preparation begins. The week of July 4 is the last stretch when a head coach can give a single recruit their full and undivided attention for sustained periods.

From Milloy's perspective, that matters too. The programs that want her most are in the same position they were in during the formal portal window — but the calendar is now doing something the formal window never did. It is creating urgency that is not manufactured by a closing date but by the reality that everyone is about to get very busy with very different priorities.

What PGF Nationals Changes

PGF Nationals opens July 19 at Great Park Irvine. The annual showcase draws the full coaching staffs of every major program in the country for over a week of evaluating the 2027 and 2028 high school classes. It is the most important youth fastpitch evaluation event of the calendar year, and attending it is not optional for any program trying to recruit at a national level.

That does not mean Milloy disappears from anyone's board after July 19. What it means is that the recruiting attention being directed at her will be distributed differently. A coach who is watching six evaluation games per day at Great Park Irvine, sitting in on showcase presentations, and managing 10-minute conversations with families across 15 different fields is not able to maintain the same focused pursuit they can right now. The physics of recruiting attention are real.

FSU's case is the most urgent among the three programs. The Seminoles lost both Isa Torres and Jaysoni Beachum in the same portal cycle — both to conference rivals — and while they added Ella Dodge and Nicole Edmiaston and retain Jazzy Francik, an experienced utility player with Power Five production fills an identifiable role. The Seminoles cannot go to PGF Nationals without knowing whether Milloy is coming. If she is, they recruit around that. If she is not, they need to know early enough to pivot.

What's Next

July 4 weekend is the most natural remaining window for an announcement. The absence of a commitment heading into the holiday weekend shifts the story's center of gravity toward the post-Nationals period, when the recruiting calendar opens fully for the 2028 class and the conversation about Milloy becomes part of a much larger summer narrative. For now, the window is open. Two days remain before it changes character again.
