NiJaree Canady Ends AUSL Holdout, Signs With Texas Volts — Pro Career Is On

By Jordan Reeves July 2, 2026

The Holdout and the Resolution

NiJaree Canady did not pitch in the Texas Volts' first two American Softball League regular-season games. She was absent — the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL Draft — as the league she was supposed to anchor opened its inaugural regular season without its most prominent name. The reason was a contract holdout. Three days after the season began, Canady reached a deal with the Volts and is now eligible to pitch.

The fact that it resolved quickly is the better story. The AUSL is in its first season of regular-season play, and having its marquee player sitting out due to a contract dispute would have been a complicated narrative for a league still building its identity with fans. The holdout ended. Canady is in uniform. The sport's most decorated collegiate pitcher of the last two years is now a professional athlete, in competition, making history of the new kind rather than the kind we have spent two years documenting on the college stage.

Who She Is

Canady finished her college career having accumulated over 100 wins and over 1,000 strikeouts — a combination of dominance and durability that placed her among the most accomplished pitchers in modern NCAA softball history. She began her career at Stanford, transferred to Texas Tech, and in 2026 led the Red Raiders to the Women's College World Series championship game for the first time in program history. Texas defeated Texas Tech 4-1 in Game 2 of the championship series, ending Canady's college career without a national title — but not without a place in the record books.

At the 2026 WCWS, she delivered a complete-game shutout of Alabama in the semifinals — two hits, six strikeouts, one of the most composed performances in recent championship history. She then pitched Games 1 and 2 of the championship series in a short-series format against Texas, logging the innings that put the Red Raiders within one win of history. No national title, but an AUSL Draft slot and a professional future that arrived with considerable fanfare.

What the AUSL Is and What This Season Means

The American Softball League is the new professional fast-pitch circuit backed by MLB, launched in 2026. The regular season runs through July 20; the postseason begins July 23. The Volts also have Reese Atwood — the Texas catcher from the back-to-back WCWS champions — on their roster, giving the Austin-area franchise two of the sport's most recognizable names from the 2026 college season.

The league's long-term viability depends heavily on star power translating to attendance and viewership, and Canady's name is the most recognized in the sport outside of the college coaching carousel. A three-day holdout matters less than the pitch she throws in her first regular-season appearance. That moment is now approaching on schedule.

What's Next

The Texas Volts have games remaining through July 20. Canady is expected to start in rotation. The AUSL postseason begins July 23, and Texas — with Atwood and Canady anchoring the roster — enters the second half of the regular season as a viable contender. For a league in its inaugural year, a championship series featuring its two most prominent names would be exactly the outcome it is hoping for. The holdout is over. The pitching begins.
