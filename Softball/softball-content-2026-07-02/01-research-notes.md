## STORY 4: Four Paid Coaches NCAA Rule

### Source: D1Softball / NFCA
- **July 1, 2026**: First day D1 softball programs can officially employ **four paid coaches** (up from three)
- Part of broader NCAA reform tied to NIL, House Settlement, competitive parity
- Rule passed by NCAA membership (timeline: approved fall 2025, effective July 1, 2026)
- Impact on major programs: fourth spot likely used to retain high-value coordinators (hitting coaches, pitching coaches) who previously had to leave for head coaching roles to get salary increases
- Impact on smaller programs: budget constraint — fourth salary adds roughly $60,000-$120,000 in annual costs; some programs may struggle to fill all four spots competitively
- Several July 1 coaching announcements (Georgia Tech, Utah State, others) tied to this date
- Chattanooga (Frank Reed retired June 22), Illinois (Perry fired), Minnesota (Ritter fired) are also in head coaching searches
- Towson: Lisa Costello announces retirement effective July 8 after 33 seasons

**Confidence: HIGH** — multiple sources confirm July 1 as the rule effective date
