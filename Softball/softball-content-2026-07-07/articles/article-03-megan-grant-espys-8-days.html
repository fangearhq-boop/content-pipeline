Megan Grant Is 8 Days From the ESPYs — And She Needs Every Vote

By Jordan Reeves

July 7, 2026

On July 15, the 2026 ESPY Awards air live on ABC at 8 PM ET. Megan Grant is nominated for Best Record-Breaking Performance. And with eight days left to vote, she needs the softball community to show up in numbers every single day.

The stakes are higher than a trophy. This is about visibility. It is about whether a softball player — a college softball player — can compete on the biggest stage in sports award television against names that come with global fan bases and billions of views. If Grant wins, softball wins. If softball shows up and votes, that is a statement regardless of the outcome.

What She Did This Season

In 2026, Megan Grant hit 42 home runs for UCLA — breaking an NCAA Division I single-season record that had stood since 1995. The previous record of 37 home runs was set by Arizona's Laura Espinoza. That record held for 31 years. Grant did not just break it. She surpassed it by five home runs.

Grant also finished her UCLA career with 91 home runs — a school record. She passed UCLA Athletic Hall of Famer Stacey Nuveman on the career list and left the program as its all-time home run leader.

Those are not soft credentials. A 31-year-old record broken by five home runs, a career marks at a program with UCLA's history, and a season that generated national attention from fans and media across sports — Grant's nomination is entirely deserved.

The Competition

Winning would be an upset. That is an honest assessment of the field, and it does not diminish what Grant accomplished.

Her fellow nominees for Best Record-Breaking Performance include Myles Garrett, the NFL defensive lineman who is one of the most recognizable players in the country. Also nominated is Johannes Høsflot Klæbo, a Norwegian cross-country skier who has built one of the most devoted followings in Olympic sports. And marathon runner Sabastian Sawe, who competes in an event with massive global viewership, particularly in East Africa and across the running community worldwide.

These are enormous platforms. The NFL has 200 million American fans. Cross-country skiing has dedicated international followings numbering in the tens of millions. Marathon has global reach that extends far beyond any single national sport.

Grant's path to winning runs through a simple mechanism: softball fans vote every day, and they do it consistently through July 15.

How to Vote

Voting is open at ESPN.com through July 15. Fans can vote once per day. That means eight more opportunities to cast a vote between now and the ceremony. It takes under a minute. The accumulated effect of thousands of softball fans voting daily can absolutely shift the outcome of a fan-voted award.

Grant has already received support from her AUSL team, the Portland Cascade, and from multiple college softball programs. The momentum exists. The question is whether it translates into the daily voting action that competitive fan voting always demands.

What's Next

The 2026 ESPYS air Wednesday, July 15, at 8 PM ET on ABC and streaming on the ESPN app. Vote once per day at ESPN.com from now through the broadcast. Eight votes remaining. Whether Grant wins or not, the softball community's participation in the campaign is itself a statement about the sport's growing presence in the mainstream sports landscape.
