Garcia Carries the Volts While Canady Works on Her Comeback

By Sarah Mitchell

July 7, 2026

The Texas Volts have two stories happening simultaneously right now, and both of them matter for different reasons.

On Monday night, Rachel Garcia threw her third complete game of the season. The Volts beat the Bandits 5-3. The team snapped a four-game losing streak and moved to 5-8 with 13 days left in the regular season. Garcia did what she does: compete, execute, win.

Meanwhile, NiJaree Canady continued working on her mechanics — away from the mound, working through the reset that former Stanford coach Tori Nyberg came to Round Rock to help her accomplish.

Both things need to keep happening. The Volts cannot make the Championship Series on Garcia alone, and they cannot afford to rush Canady back before the work is done. The challenge is holding both truths at the same time.

The Garcia Foundation

Rachel Garcia rarely gets discussed in the same breath as NiJaree Canady, Karlyn Pickens, or the other marquee names in the 2026 AUSL season. That is partly the nature of working in proximity to someone whose reputation precedes her everywhere.

But Garcia's results are real. Three complete games in a 25-game regular season is a meaningful contribution. Her ability to work deep into games preserves the bullpen, gives the team clean innings, and generates the kind of reliable performance that a team needs to stay in a playoff race.

Garcia is a Stanford alum who knows Canady's background well — they overlapped in the program's orbit. She understands what Canady is working toward and, by all indications, has no interest in making the current situation about anything other than winning games. That team-first approach is part of what makes the Volts work even when their marquee arm is sidelined.

What Nyberg Is Doing

The Texas Volts brought Tori Nyberg in to address a specific mechanical problem. During Canady's time at Texas Tech, injuries altered the pitching mechanics that had made her nearly untouchable at Stanford. Nyberg — who recruited Canady out of high school and spent those two foundational seasons refining her delivery — identified that her body positioning had drifted from the "squared up" stance toward home plate back to the "open" position she used before Stanford.

That kind of mechanical drift is subtle but devastating for a pitcher at the elite level. When your release point changes even slightly, your command changes. When your positioning shifts, your pitch movement shifts. At college ball, you can sometimes compensate through stuff alone. Against AUSL hitters, you cannot.

Nyberg, Canady, and Volts pitching coach Josh Trevino developed a plan for addressing the issue. The goal, in Nyberg's words, is to get back to "the OG NiJa" — the version of Canady whose 0.57 ERA in her final Stanford season made her look like a finished product at 21 years old.

The Window Is Closing

The AUSL regular season ends July 20. The play-in game is July 23. The Championship Series runs July 25 through 27. That gives Canady a limited number of remaining starts to show what the reset has produced before the season reaches its decisive phase.

The Volts at 5-8 need wins from anyone who can deliver them. Garcia is delivering right now. The hope is that Canady can return to the circle before July 20 and give the team something additional — not Garcia's replacement, but Garcia's complement. A team with both pitchers operating effectively is a team capable of making noise in the postseason.

Monday's win was one step in that direction. Garcia won. Canady worked. For now, that is the plan, and it is working.

What's Next

The Texas Volts continue their AUSL schedule with 13 days remaining before the July 20 regular season cutoff. Rachel Garcia will likely remain in the circle for the near term as Canady's mechanics reset continues with Tori Nyberg. The team's playoff chances depend on stringing together wins in this final stretch. If both pitchers are contributing by the time the play-in game arrives July 23, the Volts may be more dangerous than their record suggests.
