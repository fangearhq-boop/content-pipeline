Rachel Garcia Delivers Complete Game as Texas Volts Snap Four-Game Skid

By Sarah Mitchell

July 7, 2026

The Texas Volts needed a win. They got one — and they got it from a pitcher who doesn't make headlines but consistently makes outs.

Rachel Garcia threw her third complete game of the 2026 AUSL season on Monday night, leading the Texas Volts to a 5-3 victory over the Chicago Bandits at the Stadium at Parkway Bank Sports Complex in Rosemont, Illinois. The win snapped a four-game losing streak for the Volts, who improved to 5-8 with 13 days remaining in the regular season.

Garcia was steady from start to finish. The Bandits managed three runs but never found sustained pressure against her, and the Volts offense provided the cushion when it mattered. It was a complete team performance — the kind the Volts have been searching for through a difficult first half of the season.

What the Win Means in the Standings

The AUSL regular season concludes on July 20. The play-in game is July 23. The Championship Series runs July 25 through 27. For a Volts team sitting at 5-8, the math is unforgiving — but it is still math that works.

Monday's result gave Texas five wins in eight of their games have been losses, but the Volts are capable of playing better softball than their record shows. The wins, when they come, tend to be decisive. Garcia's three complete games underscore a rotation that can perform at a high level when the pieces align.

The question now is whether this win jumpstarts a run in the final stretch. The Volts play a full remaining slate before the July 20 cutoff, and every game is elimination-adjacent at this point in the standings.

Garcia's Quiet Consistency

Rachel Garcia has spent the 2026 AUSL season being the pitcher everyone knows can get the job done but never quite the one generating the marquee storyline. That distinction has belonged to two other Volts pitchers — including one still working her way back.

Three complete games in a single AUSL season is significant. It reflects not just talent but stamina and pitch efficiency. Garcia is not trying to overpower hitters in every at-bat. She works hitters into weak contact and lets her defense operate behind her. When it works, it looks exactly like Monday night: clean, controlled, decisive.

The Bandits are no easy out. Chicago entered Monday's game as one of the league's steadier offensive units, and Garcia neutralized them over a full game. That is the kind of performance that changes how a rotation gets organized down the stretch.

Tonight's Game: Blaze vs. Cascade

Tuesday night brings a different headliner. The Carolina Blaze travel to Portland to face the Cascade at Hillsboro Ballpark, with a 8 PM ET first pitch. Carolina (led by Karlyn Pickens, who is 2-0 with a 1.47 ERA and has been selected to the AUSL All-Star Game) is one of the two teams at the top of the standings alongside the Bandits.

Monday's Bandits loss to the Volts tightens the race at the top. Every team in the six-team league is watching tonight's Blaze-Cascade result — including the Volts, who suddenly have something to play for again.

What's Next

The Texas Volts return to action in the coming days with a tight window to climb the standings. Rachel Garcia will be central to any late-season push. Meanwhile, NiJaree Canady continues her mechanics work with former Stanford coach Tori Nyberg, with the goal of returning to the circle before the regular season ends July 20. If both Garcia and Canady contribute down the stretch, the Volts become a team that could cause serious problems in the play-in game — and potentially beyond.
