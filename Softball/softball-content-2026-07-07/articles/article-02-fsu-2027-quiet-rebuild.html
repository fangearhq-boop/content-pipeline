Don't Sleep on FSU: The Seminoles' 2027 Rebuild Is More Dangerous Than You Think

By Emily Rawlings

July 7, 2026

The narrative around Florida State softball heading into 2027 goes something like this: Isa Torres left. Jaysoni Beachum left. The Seminoles are in trouble.

That narrative is incomplete.

Yes, Florida State lost two of the most impactful players to enter the transfer portal this summer. Torres, the ACC and D1 Player of the Year with a .530 batting average, committed to Texas. Beachum, the No. 1-ranked transfer and one of the top outfielders in the country, landed at Texas Tech. Losing both players in one offseason is painful. Nobody is pretending otherwise.

But the full picture of FSU's 2027 roster tells a very different story than the one being written by the departure headlines.

The Foundation Holds

Pitcher Jazzy Francik — the 2026 ACC Pitcher of the Year — is returning to Tallahassee. Francik was one of the best pitchers in college softball this season, and her decision to come back gives FSU a legitimate top-tier arm around which to build the rotation.

Ashtyn Danley, an All-American outfielder, is also returning. Danley provides a reliable, experienced bat in a lineup that will need proven contributors alongside the incoming pieces.

Together, Francik and Danley represent the kind of continuity that turns a rebuild into something more modest — a reload.

The Portal Additions Add Fire

Florida State did not stand still in the transfer portal. The Seminoles added two impactful pieces before the June 22 closing date.

Ella Dodge, an infielder who spent the last two seasons at Tennessee, comes to Tallahassee after a breakout 2026 campaign — 11 home runs, 46 RBI, and a starter in all 61 games for the Lady Vols. She hit .265 in 2026 and brings ACC-level experience to FSU's infield.

Nicole Edmiaston arrives from Stetson, where she posted one of the most dominant single-season stat lines in recent small-school history: a .453 batting average, a .561 on-base percentage, a .900 slugging percentage, 23 home runs, and 70 RBI in 57 games. Edmiaston is a pure offensive weapon, and her transition from the Atlantic Sun to the ACC will be the storyline to watch — but the talent is undeniable.

The Incoming Class Adds Depth

FSU's recruiting class, ranked No. 13 nationally by Softball America, is led by five-star infielder McLanie Hudson — Softball America's No. 5 player in the country. Hudson heads a six-player class that includes infielder Addyson Ware, outfielders Kaylee Goodpaster and Callee Leffler, and pitchers Ava Lorenzatti and Missy Odom.

That is not a class assembled by a program going into survival mode. That is a class built by a program that knows it is competing for national championships and needs to add elite talent at multiple positions to stay there.

What's Next

FSU opens fall practice in the coming months with one of the more intriguing roster construction projects in the country. The Seminoles have returning stars in Francik and Danley, experienced portal additions in Dodge and Edmiaston, and elite incoming talent in Hudson and her classmates. The 2027 ACC race will be shaped in large part by how quickly this new-look FSU squad develops chemistry. Programs being underestimated often use that underestimation as fuel. The Seminoles look like exactly that kind of team.
