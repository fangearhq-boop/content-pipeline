Texas Tech Wins Utah Series 2-1 — Canady Bounces Back With 11 Strikeouts in Game 3

By Sarah Mitchell | April 14, 2026 | I Love Softball

It was a brief wobble — but Texas Tech answered it the right way. After dropping Game 2 of their road series at Utah by a 4-0 score last Saturday, the No. 1 Red Raiders returned to form Sunday in a 7-1 Game 3 victory that completed a series win and sent a message to the rest of the country: the brief stumble was not a sign of vulnerability.

NiJaree Canady was the message. The ace right-hander took the circle in Game 3 and delivered one of her most dominant performances of a dominant season — 11 strikeouts in a complete game that sent Texas Tech home from Salt Lake City with a 2-1 series victory. Canady improved to 17-2 on the season. The Red Raiders moved to 40-3 overall, 16-2 in the Big 12.

The Game 2 Stumble

Context matters. Game 2 was played under difficult conditions — two weather delays stretching the game to nearly five hours — at Dumke Family Softball Stadium in Salt Lake City. Utah's pitching held Texas Tech scoreless. It was the kind of game where circumstances aligned against the visiting team: weather disruptions, a fired-up opponent playing at home, and a short turnaround after a long travel schedule.

The loss briefly raised questions about whether Texas Tech was truly the measuring stick of the sport. Utah, a solid Mountain West program, proved capable of shutting down the Red Raiders on the right day. It was a reminder that in college softball, any team can win any game.

But Game 3 erased those questions. Canady took the ball and made quick work of Utah's lineup. The 11 strikeouts were a statement. Seven innings, one run allowed — that's Canady at her best, which is a level no other pitcher in Division I softball can consistently reach.

Canady's Season in Context

At 17-2, NiJaree Canady continues to build one of the more remarkable individual pitching seasons in recent college softball memory. The right-hander enters the week among the national leaders in strikeouts and ERA, and her presence in the Texas Tech rotation gives the Red Raiders a genuine ace-of-aces for every critical game moment.

The two losses on her record are not indictments — they come against a challenging schedule, and Texas Tech's record (40-3) is as strong as any program in the country. Canady's ability to recover from adversity — as she demonstrated in Game 3 against Utah — is arguably as important as the wins themselves.

With the Big 12 race effectively in hand at 16-2, Texas Tech's attention now turns to building the case for the No. 1 national seed in the NCAA Tournament. Oklahoma's climb to No. 1 in this week's poll means the Red Raiders have work to do in the polls, but the on-field credentials remain elite.

What's Next

Texas Tech returns home for mid-week action before heading into another Big 12 series this weekend. The Red Raiders' remaining schedule includes opportunities to pad their record and strengthen their national seed argument. For Canady, every start is an opportunity to add to a season already worthy of major individual awards — and to position Texas Tech for a deep NCAA Tournament run beginning in late May.
