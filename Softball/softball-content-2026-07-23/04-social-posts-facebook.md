### STORY 1: AUSL Play-in TONIGHT — Bandits vs. Cascade

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET

This is the game.

Tonight at 9 PM ET on ESPN, the No. 2 Chicago Bandits (16-9) host the No. 3 Portland Cascade (14-11) at Davis Diamond on the Texas A&M campus in College Station, Texas. Single elimination. Win or go home. The winner advances to the AUSL Championship Series against the Utah Talons, beginning Saturday on ABC.

Here is what makes this game different from most play-in situations: Portland Cascade has already beaten both teams remaining in this postseason.

On July 12, the Cascade beat the Bandits 5-4 and ended Chicago's nine-game winning streak — the longest in AUSL history. On July 15, they beat the No. 1 seed Utah Talons 10-2 by run rule. This is the most dangerous No. 3 seed this league has seen.

Tonight's game has a pitching matchup worth watching. Lexi Kilfoyl is 6-0 this season, the most dominant starting pitcher in the AUSL this year outside of Montana Fouts. But Kilfoyl was on the mound when the Bandits lost to Portland in July. Now she gets the rematch on the biggest stage of her professional career.

On the other side, Megan Grant enters tonight on a 17-game on-base streak — the longest active streak in the AUSL. She has 10 home runs this season. She hit a 313-foot blast in her professional debut. And she is a former UCLA All-American who set the NCAA single-season home run record with 42 at UCLA this spring.

There is also a home connection worth noting. Portland infielder Tori Vidales played her college ball at Texas A&M. Davis Diamond is her home stadium. Tonight she plays the biggest game of her career on her home field.

Game time is 9 PM ET on ESPN. This is the game the whole AUSL postseason has been building toward.

### STORY 2: PGF Nationals Championship Weekend Preview

#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET

The bracket play is almost over. The championships are Saturday.

PGF Nationals Week 1 closes out today, and the teams still alive head into Championship Weekend at Great Park in Irvine, California. The 18U Premier Championship airs on ESPNU on Saturday July 25, with first pitch at 3 PM ET. The 16U Premier Championship is on ESPN+ at 11:30 AM ET. The PGF High School All-American Games also run Saturday evening.

This is the moment that everyone who came into the week with a roster full of recruitable talent has been building toward. More than 300 programs from 34 states started this tournament. What's left Saturday are the programs that earned it.

And the number every coach and parent is watching: September 1 is 39 days away.

That's the date when Division I coaches can, for the first time, directly contact the current high school sophomore class — the Class of 2028 — via phone, text, and email. Before that date, coaches can watch, they can evaluate, but they cannot reach out. The games being played this week at PGF Nationals are being watched by coaches who are building their lists right now for those September 1 messages.

Every win this week is an audition in front of the people who matter most.

### STORY 3: Megan Grant HR Watch — The Bruin Record Race Has One More Inning

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

On Tuesday morning, Maya Brady hit her 11th home run of the 2026 AUSL season in the Oklahoma City Spark's elimination loss.

That hit set a new AUSL single-season home run record. Brady's season was over five outs later. The record stayed.

Megan Grant has 10.

Grant — Brady's former UCLA teammate and the player who pushed her in the home run race all season — enters tonight's AUSL play-in game with the second-most home runs in league history. She needs two home runs tonight to tie the record. She needs three to break it outright.

She has also been on base in each of her last 17 games. That is the longest active on-base streak in the AUSL this season. She is not in a slump. She is not struggling. She is the most locked-in hitter in this postseason.

If the Cascade wins tonight, Grant will carry the home run race into the Championship Series too. That means three more games — Saturday, Sunday, and potentially Monday — to add to the total.

Brady set the record in a loss. Grant can break it in a win. The UCLA softball rivalry that defined the AUSL's regular season has one more chapter to write.

### STORY 4: Lexi Kilfoyl vs. Portland Cascade — The Biggest Start of Her Professional Career

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Lexi Kilfoyl is undefeated.

Through the entire 2026 AUSL regular season, Kilfoyl went 6-0. She is the most successful starting pitcher the Chicago Bandits have had, and her record this season is the kind that makes a play-in appearance feel like a formality — except this is not a formality.

The last time Kilfoyl pitched against the Portland Cascade, the Bandits lost 5-4.

That was July 12. Chicago was riding a nine-game win streak — the longest in AUSL history. Portland came in and ended it. Kilfoyl was on the mound.

Tonight, Kilfoyl faces the same team in the biggest game of her professional career. She is 6-0. She knows what Portland is capable of. And the player standing between her and a championship berth is Megan Grant, who has 10 home runs and has been on base in 17 consecutive games.

This is the matchup that defines tonight's play-in. Kilfoyl's ability to neutralize Grant and limit what Portland's lineup does early will determine whether Chicago goes to the Championship Series. If Kilfoyl keeps the Cascade quiet, the Bandits have the edge. If Grant gets to her, tonight is a different game.

An undefeated pitcher with one loss on her recent résumé. A hitter chasing a record. Davis Diamond. 9 PM ET on ESPN.

That is tonight.

### STORY 5: Utah Talons Championship Prep — Montana Fouts, ABC, and the Long Wait

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM ET

The Utah Talons have been waiting since July 20.

That's when the regular season ended and they locked in the No. 1 seed with a bye directly to the AUSL Championship Series. Since then, they have watched the Bandits and Cascade prepare for tonight's play-in. They've rested their pitchers. They've had three days to watch film and set their rotation.

That rotation starts with Montana Fouts.

Fouts finished the 2026 regular season at 7-4 with a 2.36 ERA. She led the AUSL in innings pitched (62.1), strikeouts (63), and complete games (7). She set the league's single-game strikeout record with 12 Ks in a win over the Texas Volts. She is the most dominant pitcher in professional softball right now, and she has had more than three days to rest.

The defending champions are going to ABC. Game 1 of the AUSL Championship Series is this Saturday, July 25, at Noon ET — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television. Whatever team comes out of tonight's Bandits-Cascade play-in will walk into the Championship Series knowing they face a rested Fouts in Game 1.

One more detail worth noting: on July 15, Portland Cascade beat the Utah Talons 10-2 by run rule. If the Cascade advances tonight, they will have beaten every team in this playoff bracket during the regular season. The Talons will know that walking in.

Game 1 of the Championship Series. Saturday. Noon ET. ABC.
