Lexi Kilfoyl Is 6-0. The Last Time She Faced Portland, Her Team Lost.

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 23, 2026

The record is 6-0. The number looks simple from the outside: Lexi Kilfoyl has started six games this AUSL season and has not lost one. She is the Bandits' ace, the most reliable starting arm on a Chicago staff that finished the regular season with the same record as the No. 1 seed Utah Talons.

And yet, the one game that hangs over her heading into tonight has a different result in the box score.

On July 12, the Portland Cascade beat the Chicago Bandits 5-4. Kilfoyl was on the mound. The 6-0 pitcher was pitching in a game the Bandits lost. She did not take the loss — that statistic is what it is — but the team that beat her team is the same team she faces tonight in the AUSL play-in game at Davis Diamond.

What Makes Portland a Difficult Matchup

The Cascade does not look like a conventional threat on paper. They're the No. 3 seed with a 14-11 record — a team that barely made the postseason after the Oklahoma City Spark were eliminated to open their spot. But their lineup does not play like a 14-11 team.

Megan Grant is the obvious name. The former UCLA All-American leads Portland's offense with 10 home runs, the second-highest total in the league. She has reached base in each of her last 17 games. Grant can take any pitch in any count to any part of the yard. For a pitcher who relies on commanding the strike zone, that kind of hitter is a constant threat.

But Portland's lineup does not lean entirely on Grant. Sierra Sacco-Ferrie posted a .450 batting average in AUSL regular season games — a contact hitter who makes pitchers work deeper counts. When Grant is turning pitches into home runs and Sacco-Ferrie is spraying line drives the other way, the Cascade lineup is difficult to manage from the first out to the last.

What Kilfoyl Controls

Kilfoyl's 6-0 record is built on command. She has been able to get ahead in counts, work hitters away from their preferred locations, and limit hard contact in situations where other pitchers give up runs. Her ground ball rate and contact management are among the best in the AUSL for a reason.

The key for Kilfoyl tonight is attacking early. If she falls behind in counts against Grant, she is pitching into a swing that has produced 10 AUSL home runs and is on a 17-game on-base streak. If she gets ahead, the equation changes. Pitchers win play-in games by forcing contact; hitters win them by waiting for mistakes. Kilfoyl needs the former to stay the case.

The Biggest Start of Her Professional Career

No matter how the framing goes, tonight is different from anything Kilfoyl has pitched before. Six wins in the regular season is a career-defining stretch. A play-in start on ESPN, in front of the largest AUSL audience of the season, with a Championship Series berth on the line, is another thing entirely.

The best pitchers find that difference clarifying rather than paralyzing. The spotlight narrows the focus. The stakes remove the noise. Every pitch means something, which means every pitch gets the full effort of preparation and execution that regular-season games sometimes don't demand.

Kilfoyl is 6-0. She has done this before. She has just never done it against Portland with this much riding on the result.

What's Next

Lexi Kilfoyl starts for the Chicago Bandits against the Portland Cascade tonight at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. A Bandits win sends Chicago to the AUSL Championship Series against the Utah Talons beginning Saturday, July 25. A Cascade win sends Portland — and Megan Grant — to the Championship for the first time in the franchise's history.
