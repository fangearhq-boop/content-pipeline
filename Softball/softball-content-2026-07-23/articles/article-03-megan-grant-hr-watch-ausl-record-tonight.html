Maya Brady Set the AUSL Home Run Record Tuesday. Megan Grant Could Break It Tonight.

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 23, 2026

Two former UCLA Bruins entered this season as the most closely watched home run hitters in professional softball. One is done. One plays tonight.

Maya Brady hit her 11th home run of the 2026 AUSL season on July 22, in the bottom of the fifth inning of the Oklahoma City Spark's elimination loss to the Carolina Blaze. It set a new AUSL single-season home run record. The previous mark was nine, held by Morgan Zerkle from the 2025 inaugural season. Brady's 11th broke it. Her season was over five outs later.

Megan Grant has 10 home runs. She plays tonight in the AUSL play-in game.

The Math

Grant needs two home runs in tonight's play-in game against the Chicago Bandits to tie Brady's new record at 11. She needs three to break it outright and set a new mark of 12.

That's a specific and demanding threshold. Two home runs in a single postseason game is not something that happens on demand. But Grant enters the game as the most dangerous long-ball threat remaining in the AUSL postseason, and her recent form makes the possibility real rather than hypothetical.

Grant has been on base in each of her last 17 games. That is the longest active on-base streak in the AUSL this season. She is not cold. She is not pressing. She is the most consistent offensive presence in this playoff bracket, and tonight she faces a Bandits pitching staff that has seen her but has not been able to keep her off the bases when it mattered.

What Makes This Different from an Ordinary Milestone Chase

Brady and Grant were teammates at UCLA. They came up through the same program, pushed each other in practice, and split national headlines in Los Angeles before both entered the 2026 AUSL Draft. Grant went to the Portland Cascade as the No. 4 overall pick. Brady went to Oklahoma City.

All season, the two Bruins traded places at the top of the AUSL home run leaderboard. It was the league's best internal subplot — two former teammates, now on different professional teams, racing toward a record that neither of them had ever chased before. Grant held the lead, then Brady caught up, then Grant hit two in a game to go back ahead, then Brady hit another.

Brady hit No. 11 in the elimination loss. The record belongs to her — for now. Grant plays tonight.

The If-She-Wins Scenario

If Portland advances past the Bandits tonight, Grant's home run chase extends into the Championship Series — three potential games against Utah on Saturday, Sunday, and possibly Monday. That gives her more at-bats, more opportunities, and more time to catch and pass Brady's record.

Brady's record was set in a moment of defeat. Grant's record, if she sets it, will be built in the postseason, on the biggest stage in professional softball. The UCLA storyline that defined the AUSL regular season would have one more chapter to write.

What's Next

Megan Grant and the Portland Cascade face the Chicago Bandits tonight at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. The winner advances to the AUSL Championship Series beginning Saturday, July 25. Grant carries 10 AUSL home runs into the play-in. Maya Brady's AUSL record stands at 11.
