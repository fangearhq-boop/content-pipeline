Bandits vs. Cascade: Tonight at Davis Diamond. Here's What You Need to Know.

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 23, 2026

The game is tonight.

No. 2 Chicago Bandits (16-9) vs. No. 3 Portland Cascade (14-11). Thursday, July 23. 9 PM ET on ESPN. Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. Single elimination. The loser goes home. The winner faces the Utah Talons in the AUSL Championship Series beginning Saturday on ABC.

Everything this postseason has been building toward starts in a few hours. Here is a complete breakdown of what to watch.

How Portland Got Here

Portland Cascade is an expansion team. They did not exist before the 2026 AUSL season. In their first year, they went 14-11 and clinched the No. 3 playoff seed after the Oklahoma City Spark were eliminated by the Carolina Blaze on July 22.

The record is not what makes Portland dangerous. The schedule is.

On July 12, the Cascade beat the Chicago Bandits 5-4. That win ended the Bandits' nine-game winning streak — the longest streak in AUSL history at the time. On July 15, the Cascade beat the No. 1 seed Utah Talons 10-2 by run rule. The Cascade is the only team in this postseason bracket to have beaten both the Bandits and the Talons during the regular season.

The Players to Watch

Megan Grant is Portland's most dangerous hitter. The former UCLA All-American set the Division I single-season home run record with 42 at UCLA this spring, finishing second in AUSL home runs with 10 this season. She enters tonight on a 17-game on-base streak — the longest active streak in the league. Grant hit a 313-foot blast in her professional debut and has not slowed down since.

On the other side, Lexi Kilfoyl carries the Bandits' pitching hopes. Kilfoyl is 6-0 this season, the best record of any AUSL starting pitcher. She has not lost a game since turning professional. But Kilfoyl was on the mound on July 12 when Portland beat Chicago 5-4. That 6-0 record came against every team except the one that beat her team.

Portland also features Tori Vidales, an infielder who played her college ball at Texas A&M. Davis Diamond is on the Texas A&M campus in College Station. Vidales plays the biggest game of her professional career at her home stadium tonight.

What Chicago Needs to Do

The Bandits need to keep Megan Grant in the yard. Grant's 17-game on-base streak means she has been in scoring position constantly, and her 10 home runs mean every pitch she sees in a hitter's count is a high-leverage moment. If Kilfoyl can limit the Cascade's power from the left side and force Portland into contact situations, Chicago's defense and lineup give them the edge.

Chicago also has the experience advantage. The Bandits played meaningful late-season games at Davis Diamond multiple times this season and are comfortable in that environment. Their 16-9 record, identical to the Talons' regular season mark before tiebreakers, shows a team that wins.

What Portland Needs to Do

Portland has done it before. Twice. They beat the Bandits and the Talons in the regular season using the same formula: aggressive base running, timely hitting, and Sierra Sacco-Ferrie's quality at-bats in the lineup alongside Grant. If Portland can make early contact and get their runners moving, they can unsettle Kilfoyl in ways that force Chicago's bullpen into the game.

Grant does not need to hit a home run for Portland to win. But if she does, the Cascade will take their crowd with them to the Championship Series.

What's Next

Tonight's AUSL Play-in Game — No. 2 Chicago Bandits vs. No. 3 Portland Cascade — begins at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond, Texas A&M, College Station, Texas. The winner advances to the AUSL Championship Series beginning Saturday, July 25. Game 1 of the Championship airs on ABC at Noon ET — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television. The Utah Talons, the defending champions, hold the No. 1 seed and await tonight's winner.
