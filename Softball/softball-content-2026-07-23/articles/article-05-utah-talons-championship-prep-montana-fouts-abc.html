The Talons Have Been Waiting Since July 20. Montana Fouts Is Ready. ABC Is Saturday.

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 23, 2026

The Utah Talons have not played a game since the regular season ended on July 20. While the Bandits and Cascade played out the final seed race and Portland narrowly clinched the postseason, while Maya Brady set and then held the AUSL home run record, while Megan Grant built a 17-game on-base streak — the Talons have been watching from the No. 1 seed position, resting their pitchers and preparing for Saturday.

That Saturday is two days away. Game 1 of the AUSL Championship Series is July 25 at Noon ET on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television. Whatever comes out of tonight's Bandits-Cascade play-in will walk into Davis Diamond on Saturday facing a team that has had four days to prepare for them specifically.

Montana Fouts and the Case for the Talons' Dominance

Montana Fouts is the reason the Utah Talons are where they are.

Fouts finished the 2026 AUSL regular season at 7-4 with a 2.36 ERA. She led the league in innings pitched (62.1), strikeouts (63), and complete games (7). She set the AUSL single-game strikeout record with 12 strikeouts in a win over the Texas Volts — a performance that stands as the most dominant individual pitching display in the league's two-year history. She is a former Alabama All-American who was the Crimson Tide's ace before her professional career, and she is pitching at the same level now that her record shows.

The difference between Fouts and every other pitcher in the AUSL postseason right now is rest. She has not pitched in a competitive game in days. Her arm is fresh. Her preparation has been uninterrupted. The team coming out of tonight's play-in will arrive Saturday having just played an elimination game 24 hours earlier. Fouts will be ready when her name gets called for Game 1.

The Defending Champions

Utah won the 2025 AUSL Championship by sweeping the Chicago Bandits in two games. A repeat would make them the first back-to-back champions in AUSL history. That is the goal the Talons have carried all season — and finishing with a 16-9 record, the best in the league before tiebreakers, suggests they are built for it.

The Talons ranked first in the AUSL in team ERA, strikeouts, and complete games. They allowed the fewest home runs and the fewest earned runs in the league. This is a team constructed around pitching and defense — the most reliable combination in professional softball — and Fouts is the centerpiece of that structure.

One Wrinkle the Talons Have to Manage

On July 15, the Portland Cascade beat the Utah Talons 10-2 by run rule. That is not ancient history. That is eight days ago. If Portland wins tonight's play-in game, the Talons will face a team that has beaten them in the regular season.

That context matters for both teams. The Cascade will not walk into Davis Diamond on Saturday awed by the defending champions. They've already beaten them. The Talons will know that the Cascade is capable of run-ruling them — and they will have prepared accordingly.

A Bandits win tonight would set up a different dynamic. Chicago is 16-9, the same record as Utah. The Bandits won back-to-back games against the Talons in early July before Utah pulled away in the seed race. The Bandits would bring a matchup the Talons know well — and one that has gone both ways this season.

What's Next

The AUSL Championship Series begins Saturday, July 25, at Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. Game 1 airs at Noon ET on ABC — the first professional softball game ever on U.S. network television. Game 2 is Sunday, July 26 on ESPN. A deciding Game 3, if necessary, would be Monday, July 27. The Utah Talons, the defending champions, hold the No. 1 seed. Their opponent is determined tonight when the Chicago Bandits face the Portland Cascade at 9 PM ET on ESPN.
