Megan Grant Is Still Making History — and the Portland Cascade Are Chasing a No. 2 Seed

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 17, 2026

In the spring, Megan Grant hit 42 home runs for UCLA and broke a record that had stood since 1995. In July, she was nominated for an ESPY Award against athletes from football, skiing, and marathon running. This week, she is fighting for postseason position in the AUSL's final three days of regular-season play.

The story has not stopped moving.

The Record and What It Meant

Laura Espinoza hit 37 home runs for Arizona in 1995. That number survived three decades of challengers. Grant ended its run this spring at Jackie Robinson Stadium, finishing the season with 42. The margin was five. The record had stood for 31 years.

The ESPY nomination for Best Record-Breaking Performance placed Grant's name on a ballot alongside Myles Garrett of the NFL's Los Angeles Rams, Norwegian cross-country skier Johannes Klæbo, and marathon runner Sabastian Sawe. Garrett won the award — his sack record was formidable — but Grant being on that ballot was a statement about how her 42-homer season was perceived outside softball's own audience.

Millions of viewers who would never attend a college softball game saw Megan Grant's name on the screen in that company on July 15. That matters to the sport.

What She's Done in the AUSL

Grant was Portland's No. 4 pick in the 2026 AUSL College Draft. In her first week of professional play, she set an AUSL home run record with a 313-foot shot. She has continued hitting in the league and currently leads the AUSL in home runs.

The power has not been an artifact of the NCAA's shorter fences and college pitching. Grant is doing it against professional arms. That matters when evaluating whether the college production was sustainable.

The Portland Situation

The Cascade enter the final week of the regular season at approximately 11-9 and in position for the No. 2 or No. 3 seed. Both seeds advance to the postseason — but there is a difference.

The No. 2 seed plays in the July 23 play-in game as the higher seed. The No. 3 seed plays in the same game as the lower seed. In a single-elimination format, that distinction can matter. Home-field advantage, opponent familiarity, and pressure dynamics all shift based on seeding.

More to the point: the Championship Series at Davis Diamond on July 25-27 will be the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television, with Game 1 airing on ABC. The team that arrives there will be seen by an audience that most softball players and coaches have spent years trying to reach.

Megan Grant is playing her way toward that stage. Every game this week is a step in that direction.

What's Next

The AUSL regular season ends July 20. Portland will play at least one more regular-season game before the final standings are set. The play-in game is July 23 at Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. The AUSL Championship Series begins July 25. Game 1 airs on ABC. What happens between now and July 20 determines where Grant and the Cascade stand when they get there.
