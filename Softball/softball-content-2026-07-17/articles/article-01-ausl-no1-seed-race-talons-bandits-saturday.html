AUSL Final Week: Utah and Chicago Are Playing for the No. 1 Seed — and Saturday Is the Game

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 17, 2026

Three days left in the AUSL regular season. One game that defines the postseason structure. One seed with a direct path to the Championship. One seed that has to win a play-in first.

Saturday is the game that decides which is which.

Where Things Stand

The Utah Talons enter the final stretch of the 2026 AUSL regular season in first place, having clinched the league's first postseason berth last week. The Chicago Bandits are one game behind, riding a stretch of dominant play that has included a 15-7 rout of the Carolina Blaze on Thursday night.

Both teams have already secured their postseason spots. What they're fighting for now is the seed — and the seed matters more in the AUSL than it does in most leagues.

The No. 1 seed gets a direct bye into the AUSL Championship Series. The No. 2 and No. 3 seeds both advance to the postseason, but they must meet each other in a single-elimination play-in game on July 23 before they can reach the finals. Win the play-in, you move on. Lose, and the season ends one game short of the Championship.

The Utah Talons do not want to play a play-in game. Neither do the Chicago Bandits.

Saturday at Chicago

The AUSL schedule has handed the two best teams in the league exactly what the moment requires: Talons at Bandits, Saturday, July 18 at 4 PM ET on ESPN2.

If Utah wins, the Talons extend their lead with two games remaining and are in strong position to hold the No. 1 seed through July 20. If Chicago wins, the teams are tied with two games left — and the math becomes genuinely complicated.

The Bandits have been playing their best softball of the season when it matters most. Lexi Kilfoyl and Kat Sandercock have anchored the pitching rotation at an elite level for weeks, and the Chicago offense — led by Sami Williams — has been one of the most productive in the league. Their nine-game winning streak was snapped by Portland, but the Blaze's loss on Thursday shows the Bandits responded.

Utah's postseason clinch came on the back of Autumn Faraimo's complete game with 10 strikeouts in a 5-4 win over Oklahoma City on Wednesday. The Talons have been steady all season — they were the first team to clinch, they hold the best record — and they are one win from making the stretch run irrelevant.

What the Championship Looks Like

The 2026 AUSL Championship Series is July 25-27 at Davis Diamond on the campus of Texas A&M in College Station, Texas. The play-in game is July 23 at the same venue. Davis Diamond is one of the premier softball facilities in the country, and the venue will host the biggest moment in the young league's history.

Game 1 of the Championship will air nationally on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television.

The No. 1 seed walks into that series fresh. The No. 2 seed walks in having already played a high-stakes elimination game two days earlier.

Saturday's game is not just about pride or seeding math. It is about whether the Talons or the Bandits arrive at Davis Diamond with an advantage.

What's Next

Saturday: Talons at Bandits, 4 PM ET, ESPN2. The regular season ends Monday, July 20. After that: the play-in game July 23, and the Championship Series July 25-27, with Game 1 live on ABC. Three days of regular-season softball remain, and the most important one is this weekend.
