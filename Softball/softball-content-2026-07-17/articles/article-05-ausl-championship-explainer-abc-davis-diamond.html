AUSL Championship Is 8 Days Away — Here's How the Postseason Works

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 17, 2026

The 2026 AUSL regular season ends July 20. Three days after that, the postseason begins. And eight days from today, professional softball will be on ABC for the first time ever.

If you're following the AUSL for the first time — or if you've been watching all season and want to understand exactly what the postseason structure looks like — here is the complete picture.

Who Makes the Postseason

The AUSL regular season is played by six teams: the Utah Talons, Chicago Bandits, Portland Cascade, Texas Volts, Carolina Blaze, and Oklahoma City Spark. The three teams with the best records at the end of the regular season (July 20) advance to the postseason.

As of today, the Utah Talons have already clinched a postseason spot — they were the first team to do so. The Chicago Bandits are one game behind and also positioned to advance. Portland Cascade is fighting for the third postseason seed.

The Play-In Game — July 23

The No. 2 and No. 3 seeds from the regular season meet in a single-elimination play-in game on Thursday, July 23 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. One game. Winner advances. Loser goes home.

The No. 1 seed does not play in the play-in game. They wait.

The Championship Series — July 25-27

The 2026 AUSL Championship Series begins Saturday, July 25 at Davis Diamond on the campus of Texas A&M University in College Station, Texas. The series is best-of-three: the first team to win two games wins the championship.

Game 1 of the Championship will air nationally on ABC. This is the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television.

Davis Diamond is one of the premier softball facilities in the United States. The Texas A&M campus gives the Championship a built-in college softball energy — a setting that feels appropriate for a league whose rosters came directly from the top programs in the country.

Why the No. 1 Seed Is So Valuable

The No. 1 seed skips the play-in game entirely. They advance directly to the Championship Series, with two full days of rest while the No. 2 and No. 3 seeds compete in a high-pressure elimination game.

The No. 2 and No. 3 seeds must play that play-in game Thursday. Whoever wins faces the No. 1 seed in the Championship starting Saturday — with less rest and more miles on the roster.

Saturday's game between Utah and Chicago (4 PM ET, ESPN2) decides which team earns that bye. It is the most consequential game left in the AUSL regular season.

What's Next

Regular season ends July 20. Play-in game July 23 at Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. AUSL Championship Series July 25-27 at Davis Diamond, Game 1 on ABC. The best professional softball in the world, on the biggest stage the league has ever seen. Eight days from now.
