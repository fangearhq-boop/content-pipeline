NiJaree Canady Takes the Mound Tonight — and the Volts' Playoff Hopes Go With Her

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 17, 2026

Tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN2, the Texas Volts play the Portland Cascade at Hillsboro Ballpark in a game that will matter for the standings — and one that will matter for the longer story of professional softball's most anticipated debut.

The AUSL regular season ends July 20. The Volts are currently outside the playoff picture. This is not the moment for process or patience. This is the moment for results.

The Numbers That Haven't Gone Away

NiJaree Canady was picked second overall in the 2026 AUSL College Draft on May 4. She signed with the Texas Volts on June 12. The expectations that arrived with her were the kind of expectations that had been building for years — a pitcher who had been one of the most dominant forces in college softball, with mechanics and stuff that set her apart from every contemporary.

Her 2026 AUSL numbers: 0-3 record, 6.87 ERA.

Those numbers have not gone away. They do not disappear by changing the subject.

What has emerged through the season is an explanation — not an excuse, but a diagnosis. At Texas Tech, injuries forced Canady to adjust her delivery. Her stance shifted from what coaches called "squared up" to an "open" position. That shift altered the release point that made her Stanford-era performance what it was. She still had the arm. She still had the intelligence. The mechanical foundation had changed.

The Fix in Progress

The Texas Volts responded by calling Tori Nyberg — Canady's former pitching coach at Stanford. Nyberg's goal, as described by sources close to the program, is to restore what coaches call the "OG NiJa" delivery: the mechanics that made Canady one of the most unhittable pitchers in college softball history.

The work has been ongoing. Canady did earn her first AUSL win on July 10, throwing a complete game with six strikeouts. One win did not close the conversation, but it opened a different one — the possibility that the adjustment is happening in real time, game by game, mechanical correction by mechanical correction.

Tonight is the next data point in that sequence.

The Volts' Situation

The Texas Volts have more on their roster than NiJaree Canady. Rachel Garcia — the MLBx MVP winner from Citizens Bank Park in June — pitches for this team. Ally Carda and Alyssa Denham are part of the staff. The Volts have elite arms.

But they are outside the playoff picture with three games remaining. They need wins, and they need help. Tonight's game at Portland is as real as it gets in terms of what the season can still offer.

The Portland Cascade are 11-9, in position to advance to the postseason. A win over the Volts helps Portland; a loss does not end their season but tightens the race. For the Volts, a win makes the math possible. A loss ends the conversation.

What's Next

Tonight: Volts at Cascade, 8 PM ET, ESPN2. The AUSL regular season ends July 20. If the Volts can string together wins in the final three games and get help from the rest of the schedule, the playoff door is not yet shut. Canady is the pitcher at the center of that possibility. What she does tonight tells the rest of the story.
