### STORY 2: Volts at Cascade Tonight — NiJaree Canady's Playoff Last Stand

**FB Long-Form (5:30 PM ET)**

Tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN2, the Texas Volts travel to Portland to face the Cascade. It's a game that means different things to each team — but for the Volts and for NiJaree Canady, it may be the most important outing left.

Canady was the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL College Draft. She was one of the most dominant pitchers in college softball history at Stanford — a Golden Ticket winner, a pitcher who made hitters look broken. Then came injuries at Texas Tech, and with them, a mechanical shift that coaches have been trying to undo all summer.

Her 2026 AUSL line: 0-3 with a 6.87 ERA. Not what anyone expected.

The Texas Volts brought in Tori Nyberg — Canady's former Stanford pitching coach — specifically to address the mechanics. The issue, according to sources: Canady shifted from a "squared up" to an "open" stance after her Texas Tech injuries, and it's cost her the release point that made her elite.

One win won't close this story. But tonight could tell us whether the Nyberg reset is working — because the regular season ends July 20, and the Volts are outside the playoff picture.

Watch tonight. 8 PM ET, ESPN2.

### STORY 4: Megan Grant and the Cascade Fight for No. 2 Seed

**FB Long-Form (3:00 PM ET)**

Megan Grant arrived at the AUSL as the most talked-about player in the league — and she's backed it up.

In the spring, playing for UCLA, she hit 42 home runs in a single season. That broke a record that had stood since 1995, when Laura Espinoza hit 37 for Arizona. Thirty-one years of challengers, and none of them got there. Grant got there by five.

The nomination for the ESPY Award for Best Record-Breaking Performance brought her name to a national audience beyond softball. Myles Garrett won the award — his NFL sack record was formidable — but Grant being on that ballot meant something. It meant the sport's biggest individual performance of 2026 was compared directly to the best in every other major sport.

Now she's in the AUSL, leading the league in home runs, and the Portland Cascade are in a three-team race for position in the final week of the regular season.

Both the No. 2 and No. 3 seeds advance to the postseason. But the No. 2 seed enters the July 23 play-in game as the higher seed — and with the Championship on national television on ABC, how a team arrives at Davis Diamond matters.

Grant is doing everything she can to make sure Portland arrives with the right number next to their name.

### STORY 5: AUSL Championship in 8 Days — What You Need to Know

**FB Long-Form (1:00 PM ET)**

If you're new to the AUSL — or if you're a softball fan who's been following along but hasn't sorted out exactly how the postseason works — here's everything you need to know with eight days left.

**The format:**
Three teams advance from the AUSL regular season. The team that finishes first gets a direct bye into the Championship Series. The second and third seeds play each other in a single-elimination play-in game on Thursday, July 23 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas.

The winner of that game meets the No. 1 seed in the best-of-three AUSL Championship Series, beginning Saturday, July 25 — also at Davis Diamond on the Texas A&M campus.

**The broadcast:**
Game 1 of the 2026 AUSL Championship will air nationally on ABC. This is the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television.

**The venue:**
Davis Diamond is on the campus of Texas A&M University in College Station, Texas. It's one of the premier softball venues in the country.

**The timeline:**
Regular season ends July 20. Play-in game July 23. Championship Series July 25-27.

The game Saturday between the Utah Talons and Chicago Bandits (4 PM ET, ESPN2) could determine who gets that No. 1 seed — and who walks into Davis Diamond with a bye instead of a must-win.
