100+ Wins, 1,000+ Ks, One Title Missing: NiJaree Canady's Final Chase Comes Down to Tonight

By Emily Rawlings June 4, 2026

A Career That Defies Easy Summary

NiJaree Canady has accomplished more in college softball than almost any pitcher of her generation. Over 100 career wins. Over 1,000 career strikeouts. Two-time NFCA Pitcher of the Year. The first pitcher in WCWS history with multiple shutouts at two different schools. No. 2 pick in the AUSL Draft, heading to the Texas Volts as a professional. The resume is extraordinary.

And yet there's one thing she doesn't have. A national championship ring. Tonight, in what could be her final college game, she gets one more chance to add it.

From Stanford to Lubbock to Devon Park

Canady's path to this moment wasn't a straight line. She built her reputation at Stanford, going deep in the WCWS twice and making a national name for herself. Then she transferred to Texas Tech, took the highest-profile NIL deal in softball history, and arrived in Lubbock with one goal: win the title that had eluded her.

Her 2026 season was extraordinary: a 21-4 record, 1.30 ERA, 194 strikeouts. She worked through struggles earlier in this tournament, posting a 7.16 ERA through the first several games before flipping the switch in the semifinal against No. 1 Alabama. That performance, a complete-game two-hitter, was the Canady everyone came to Texas Tech to see.

Rested, Ready, and Riding the Semifinal Wave

Canady pitched only 1.2 innings in relief during Game 1 on Wednesday — she wasn't needed as Texas Tech fell 7-3. That means she heads into tonight's elimination game as fresh as she's going to be, with the confidence of that Alabama shutout still fresh. Game 2 is her game to win or lose.

She's been in high-leverage moments before. The difference tonight is that this is the last one of her college career — win or lose. If Texas Tech wins, there's a Game 3. But if Texas closes it out, Canady pitches the final innings of her college career and walks off toward the professional game having come closer than ever to the one honor she chased most.

What's Next

Game 2 of the WCWS Championship Series is tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN. Canady is expected to start for Texas Tech. Whatever happens, her professional career with the Texas Volts in the AUSL begins this summer. But first, one last college game at Devon Park.
