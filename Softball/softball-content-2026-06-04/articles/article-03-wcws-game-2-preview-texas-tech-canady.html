WCWS Game 2 Tonight: Canady Fresh, Texas Tech in Survival Mode at 8 PM ET on ESPN

By Jordan Reeves June 4, 2026

Everything on the Line at Devon Park

Game 2 of the 2026 Women's College World Series Championship Series is tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN. The setup could not be simpler or more dramatic: Texas wins and celebrates back-to-back national championships. Texas Tech wins and forces a winner-take-all Game 3 on Friday.

After Texas's 7-3 win in Game 1 on Wednesday, the Longhorns are one win from completing one of the most dominant runs in recent college softball history. The Red Raiders are not done, though. They've been told they couldn't do this all tournament long, and they've been proving doubters wrong at every stop.

Canady Comes In Fresh

The biggest X-factor for Texas Tech heading into tonight is NiJaree Canady's availability. The ace pitched only 1.2 innings in relief in Game 1 — she was not needed for the complete game Kavan delivered — which means she enters Game 2 rested and with full preparation time. This is the Canady that shut out No. 1 Alabama in a two-hitter during the semis. That version is what Texas Tech needs.

Texas Tech's coaching staff will almost certainly go with Canady from the start. She's the reason this team is here. Her career has been built for moments exactly like this one, and a title-or-go-home scenario against a team she's faced before is as close to ideal conditions as she's going to get.

Can Texas Close It?

Texas has been in this position before. The Longhorns won the championship last year and know what it takes to close out a series. Their offense generated 10 hits in Game 1 and will look to attack early again. The question is whether Texas goes back to Teagan Kavan on short rest or looks at the bullpen for Game 2.

Texas Tech's lineup showed it can hit in Game 1 — Mia Williams had a two-run home run — but the Red Raiders will need more production from the top of their order if they want to extend the series. Texas Tech has the Cinderella story, the crowd narrative, and Canady. That's not nothing.

What's Next

Game 2 tips off tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN from Devon Park in Oklahoma City. If Texas wins, the celebration begins. If Texas Tech wins, Game 3 is scheduled for Friday night. Either way, tonight is one of the two biggest games on the college softball calendar this year.
