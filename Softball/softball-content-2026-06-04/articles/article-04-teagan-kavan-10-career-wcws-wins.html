Seven Pitchers Ever: Teagan Kavan Reaches 10 Career WCWS Wins With Game 1 Performance

By Sarah Mitchell June 4, 2026

A Milestone Few Have Reached

Teagan Kavan walked off the Devon Park mound Wednesday night after pitching a complete game against Texas Tech and became just the seventh pitcher in Women's College World Series history to earn 10 career wins at the tournament. She gave up three hits and three runs over seven innings in a 7-3 Texas victory — efficient, composed, and exactly what the Longhorns have come to expect from her in the postseason.

The WCWS has been around since 1982. Reaching 10 career wins at the tournament requires not just talent but consistency and longevity at the highest level of college softball. Kavan has delivered both.

How the Record Was Built

Kavan came into the 2026 championship series with nine career WCWS wins — already a Texas program record. She tied Cat Osterman for WCWS shutouts in program history earlier this tournament with her complete-game blanking of Tennessee in the semifinal. Each postseason has added another chapter to her resume.

Wednesday's complete game was her second in a row at the WCWS this year. She threw all seven innings against Tennessee in the semifinal with 10 strikeouts and two hits allowed. Then she came back in Game 1 of the championship and did it again. Two complete games. No doubt about who carries this staff when the pressure is highest.

Where She Stands Among the Greats

Kavan's place in the all-time WCWS conversation is no longer a hypothetical. She's on the short list of the most accomplished pitchers the tournament has ever seen. Cat Osterman, Lisa Fernandez, and others before her set the standard. Kavan isn't just approaching their territory, she's already carved out her own section of it.

She finished the regular season with a 28-6 record. Her ERA, strikeout numbers, and walk rate have all pointed to a pitcher who has been getting better each year. There's a legitimate argument she's pitching the best softball of her career right now, at exactly the right moment.

What's Next

Kavan may or may not take the circle again tonight in Game 2 depending on Texas's decision around using her on short rest. If Texas needs her and she goes, a win would give her 11 career WCWS victories. Game 2 is tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN, with Texas one win from back-to-back national championships.
