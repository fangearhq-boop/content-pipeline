Texas Rolls to 7-3 Win in WCWS Championship Game 1, One Win From Back-to-Back Titles

By Sarah Mitchell June 4, 2026

Longhorns Take Control From the First Inning

Texas wasted no time in Game 1 of the Women's College World Series Championship Series, putting up a run in the first inning and never looking back. Katie Stewart's two-run home run in the bottom of the first set the tone, and Teagan Kavan handled the rest. Final score: Texas 7, Texas Tech 3.

The Longhorns collected 10 hits against one of the most accomplished pitching staffs in college softball. That kind of offensive output against Texas Tech is not easy to come by. It speaks to how locked-in Texas was from the opening pitch.

Kavan Delivers the Complete Game

Teagan Kavan pitched all seven innings in Game 1, allowing three hits, three runs, and two walks. She was in control throughout, mixing speeds and keeping Texas Tech's bats off balance. Her complete game was her 10th career WCWS win — a milestone that puts her among the seven pitchers in tournament history to reach that mark.

Kavan has been the backbone of Texas's postseason run. She came into Game 1 with momentum from a complete-game shutout against Tennessee in the semifinal. She's now produced two consecutive complete games at the WCWS, and Texas needs just one more performance like these to celebrate back-to-back championships.

Texas Tech Still Fighting

Texas Tech didn't fold. Mia Williams hit a two-run home run to center field — a reminder that the Red Raiders have legitimate power throughout the lineup. But the deficit was too large by then, and NiJaree Canady pitched only 1.2 innings in relief in Game 1, which means she's available and fresh for tonight's must-win Game 2.

Texas Tech came into the championship round as a No. 11 seed with a 61-8 record and knocked out No. 1 Alabama twice in the same day to get here. They've earned their spot, and they're not treating this like a moral victory.

What's Next

Game 2 of the WCWS Championship Series is tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN. Texas wins the title with a victory. Texas Tech needs a win to force a Game 3 on Friday. Expect Canady to take the circle fresh for the Red Raiders, while Texas will decide whether to bring Kavan back on two days' rest or go with the bullpen.
