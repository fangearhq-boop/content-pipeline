WCWS History: Katie Stewart Is the First Player Ever to Homer in Four Straight Games

By Emily Rawlings June 4, 2026

The Record Nobody Had Until Now

The Women's College World Series has been played since 1982. Across all of those seasons, all of those talented hitters and postseason legends, one record stood unclaimed: no player had ever homered in four consecutive WCWS games. On Wednesday night, Katie Stewart took it.

Stewart's two-run shot in the first inning of Game 1 against Texas Tech was her seventh career WCWS home run. But the number that matters more is four — four straight WCWS games with a home run, a streak that has never been accomplished before in the tournament's history. She owns that record alone.

How the Streak Built

Stewart's run started in the earlier rounds of the tournament and carried into the championship series. Each game brought its own pressure, and in each one she delivered. The Game 1 blast against Texas Tech came in the first inning — early, loud, and decisive. Texas never trailed after that swing.

Stewart finished the 2026 season as one of Texas's primary power threats. She hit 30 home runs in the regular season, breaking the Texas single-season program record. Now she's added a WCWS record to the list of things nobody had done before she did them.

The Quiet Star Beside Kavan

Teagan Kavan gets most of the attention on this Texas team, and fairly so. But Stewart has been just as valuable in the postseason. Seven career WCWS home runs places her in company with the best power hitters in tournament history. And her ability to come through in the biggest moments, against the best pitching, separates her from most.

Texas coach Mike White has pointed to Stewart's consistency all season as one of the program's underappreciated assets. In an era where softball is getting more attention than ever, Stewart's contributions deserve recognition beyond the final box score.

What's Next

If Texas closes out the series tonight in Game 2, Stewart finishes her season with 30 regular-season home runs, the program record, and a WCWS four-game HR streak that may not be broken for a generation. Game 2 is tonight at 8 PM ET on ESPN, with Texas one win from back-to-back national championships.
