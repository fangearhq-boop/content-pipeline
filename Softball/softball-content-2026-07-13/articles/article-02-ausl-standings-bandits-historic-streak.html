From 0-4 to History: The Chicago Bandits Have the Longest Win Streak in AUSL History

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 13, 2026

The Chicago Bandits started the inaugural AUSL season 0-4. Through the first four games, they could not buy a win. The team that had spent months preparing to launch professional softball's most visible league was sitting at the bottom of the standings before the end of April.

Seven wins later — seven consecutive wins, the longest in AUSL history — they are in second place and hunting the Utah Talons for the No. 1 seed with seven games left in the regular season.

The comeback arc of the 2026 AUSL season belongs to Chicago.

What the Streak Means

Seven consecutive wins sets a new record for sustained excellence in the league's inaugural campaign. The Bandits surpassed whatever previous high-water mark existed in the young league with their most recent victory, and they are showing no signs of slowing down with the regular season finale approaching July 20.

The Bandits' run has been built on offensive depth. Morgan Zerkle's two-run triple in the first inning of the July 11 win over Portland Cascade set the tone, followed by Sami Williams' three-run home run that turned a lead into a statement. Chicago won that game 11-3 via run rule. In a league where every game is competitive, winning by run rule is a demonstration of dominance.

The Standings Race

Utah sits at the top of the AUSL standings, approximately 14-6, built on what had been a seven-game winning streak of their own — before falling to the Oklahoma City Spark in a walk-off on July 11. Chicago, at approximately 13-7, is a game and a half back with a week to play.

The No. 1 seed matters enormously in the AUSL's postseason structure. The top team earns a direct bye to the Championship Series, scheduled for July 25-27 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas, with the broadcast on ESPN. Teams finishing second through fourth compete in a play-in round before getting to that stage.

Utah's recent stumble gives Chicago a real path. If the Bandits win out and the Talons drop two games this week, the standings could flip entirely. With seven games left, the race between the league's two best records is genuinely alive.

The Context for Portland and OKC

While Utah and Chicago have pulled ahead at the top, the Portland Cascade (approximately 11-9) remains a threat for a playoff position. The Oklahoma City Spark — the expansion team that beat the Talons in walk-off fashion on July 11 — has shown enough to complicate things at the bottom of the playoff picture. Every game this week carries postseason implications for multiple teams.

What's Next

The AUSL regular season concludes July 20. From there, the playoff picture shakes out: the No. 1 seed goes directly to the Championship Series (July 25-27, Davis Diamond, College Station, TX, on ESPN) while teams 2-4 navigate a play-in. Follow the standings closely this week — the Bandits are building something that could carry them all the way to College Station.
