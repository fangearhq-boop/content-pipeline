NiJaree Canady's Comeback Window Is Closing — Can She Find "OG NiJa" Before the Season Ends?

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 13, 2026

NiJaree Canady and the Texas Volts face the Carolina Blaze tonight at Dell Diamond in Round Rock, Texas. First pitch is at 7:00 PM ET. And for anyone who has been following the story of her AUSL debut, tonight is a game that carries more weight than the box score will show.

The regular season ends July 20. There are approximately seven games left. Canady is 0-3 with a 6.87 ERA. The window to turn this around is not closing — it is nearly closed.

What Happened and Why

Canady arrived in professional softball as one of the most celebrated players of her generation. At Stanford, she went 36-6 over her career, posting some of the most dominant statistical seasons in Pac-12 history. She was the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL Draft by the Texas Volts. The expectation entering the year was not that she would struggle — it was what level of dominance she would bring to the professional game.

Instead, she struggled. The early returns in her AUSL debut — a 15.00 ERA in her first appearance, 17 earned runs allowed in her first three appearances — pointed to something mechanical, not talent-based. The Volts, to their credit, responded quickly.

They called Tori Nyberg.

The Nyberg Reset

Tori Nyberg was Canady's pitching coach at Stanford. She recruited Canady out of high school and spent years building the mechanics that made the right-handed pitcher one of the best in the country. The specific technique Nyberg focused on was a "squared up" stance — Canady facing home plate directly, which generates power and deception from a stable base.

At Texas Tech, where Canady transferred before the AUSL Draft, her mechanics shifted. The cause, per reporting by Sports Illustrated, was injury adjustments during her time at Tech — her body positioning gradually moved from squared up to "open," a change that altered her pitch movement and command. When she entered professional ball, the habits from Tech came with her.

Nyberg came to Round Rock to reverse that. Working with Canady and Volts assistant coach Josh Trevino, the project has been straightforward in concept and hard in execution: restore the stance, restore the command, restore "OG NiJa."

The Stakes Tonight

Tonight's game against the Blaze is not just a regular-season contest. It is a measuring point for whether the mechanical work has translated into results. Canady's AUSL debut has been defined by the gap between what she is capable of and what the numbers have shown. The reset with Nyberg was designed to close that gap.

Sunday's MLBx 3-on-3 event offered a glimpse at the contrast: while Canady competed on Team Jones at Citizens Bank Park, it was Rachel Garcia — the Texas Volts' other AUSL star — who walked away with MVP honors in the event. Garcia delivered the defining performance of softball's biggest national showcase moment. The competitive context is real.

Seven games remain. The AUSL Championship Series is July 25-27 at Davis Diamond in College Station. The time for the comeback is now.

What's Next

The Volts host the Blaze tonight at Dell Diamond in Round Rock, Texas — 7:00 PM ET. The remaining regular-season schedule runs through July 20. Whatever happens tonight, the series of events from July 3 to tonight — the Nyberg intervention, the mechanical work, the return to "squared up" — will be visible in how Canady pitches.
