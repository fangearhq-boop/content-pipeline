Team Victorino Wins the Inaugural MLBx 3-On-3 — Rachel Garcia Is the Story

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 13, 2026

The Celebrity Softball Game is gone. In its place, something that actually mattered — and someone who made it unforgettable.

Team Victorino won the inaugural MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 Presented by Ito En on Sunday, defeating Team Howard 48-39 in the championship game at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. The event aired live on Peacock as part of MLB All-Star Week, reaching an audience that would never have sought out a professional softball broadcast on its own.

The MVP was Rachel Garcia.

What Garcia Did

Garcia, a Texas Volts pitcher and two-time National Team member, scored a game-high 18 points in the championship round. That total put her ahead of every other participant in the event — including Ryan Howard, the 2006 NL MVP who hit 58 home runs in his prime, and Alyssa Brito of the Carolina Blaze, who brought her own decorated resume from three WCWS title runs at Oklahoma.

In the semifinal, Garcia did something even the event format could not have scripted better: she hit a walk-off home run to eliminate Team Jones. That team featured Andruw Jones — newly inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame this week in Cooperstown — and NiJaree Canady, the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL Draft and arguably professional softball's most recognized name heading into the season.

Garcia knocked out Canady's team to advance to the final. Then she won the final.

Who Garcia Is

Rachel Garcia is not a newcomer to big moments. She was the ace of UCLA's 2019 WCWS championship team, one of the most dominant pitcher-athlete combinations in recent collegiate softball history. She earned an Olympic silver medal with the U.S. Women's National Team at Tokyo 2020. At UCLA, she was a two-time USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year.

She joined the Texas Volts as one of the founding AUSL stars in 2026. Before Sunday, her most visible work had been on the pitcher's mound at Dell Diamond in Round Rock. On Sunday, she stepped into a co-ed power competition at Citizens Bank Park and walked away as the MVP of the whole thing.

Why the Format Worked

MLBx 3-on-3 earned its moment because the format is genuinely competitive. Each player gets a two-minute batting window per game. A home run earns one point. Catching a ball in the outfield also earns one point. There is a Hot Streak power-up that doubles points for five swings — pressed at the right moment, it can flip a game entirely.

The critical design element: there is no separation by gender. Garcia's home run in the semifinal counted exactly the same as Ryan Howard's in the championship round. When she scored 18 points to lead all competitors, those points were earned against the same format that Howard, Brito, Terrell Owens, and Tiare Jennings all competed under.

The Celebrity Softball Game put entertainers at the center of the broadcast. MLBx 3-on-3 put competitors there. The first MVP of the inaugural event is a professional softball player.

What's Next

For Rachel Garcia and the Texas Volts, the regular season resumes with approximately seven games remaining before the July 20 finale. The AUSL Championship Series is scheduled for July 25-27 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas, broadcast on ESPN. Garcia's MLBx performance will follow her into that stretch — a calling card from professional softball's biggest stage moment of 2026.
