Alabama Must Respond in Game 2 Against Texas — Series Survival on the Line at Rhoads

By Sarah Mitchell | April 3, 2026 | I Love Softball

Alabama has been waiting for this series all season. Thursday's Game 1 was not what the Crimson Tide expected.

No. 1 Texas handled Alabama 9-1 in the series opener at Rhoads Stadium — scoring nine runs, racking up 13 hits with four home runs, and getting a complete game from Teagan Kavan. Alabama's lone run came in the fourth inning. The result left Alabama at 7-3 in SEC play and staring at a must-win Friday night situation.

Game 2 is tonight at 7:30 PM CT on SEC Network. If Alabama loses, Texas clinches the series and deals a significant blow to Alabama's standing in the SEC race. If Alabama wins, Game 3 on ESPN Saturday remains a true series decider.

Murphy's Challenge

Patrick Murphy is in his 30th season coaching Alabama softball, and he has coached his share of must-win games. His teams have beaten ranked opponents at Rhoads Stadium before — most recently defeating defending champion Oklahoma at this same venue in 2025. The crowd in Tuscaloosa on a Friday night in early April will be loud, and Alabama has used home-field advantage to great effect throughout the season.

The question is whether Alabama can find an answer to a Texas offense that has been nearly unstoppable in SEC play. Texas is 9-1 in the conference heading into Friday. They have outscored opponents by an average of more than six runs per game in those nine wins. Thursday's four-home-run explosion was not an outlier — it was consistent with what this Texas team has done all year.

What Alabama Needs

For Alabama to win Game 2, the pitching staff will need to limit the extra-base damage Texas inflicted in the opener. The Longhorns hit four of their five extra-base hits in the first three innings on Thursday — setting the tone early and never allowing Alabama back into the game.

On offense, Alabama needs to work counts and create traffic. Marlie Giles's RBI single in the fourth was the only run the Crimson Tide scored in 21 at-bats. Against Kavan, that's not entirely unexpected — but if a different Texas pitcher starts Game 2, Alabama will need to take advantage early in the count.

What's Next

Game 2 is tonight, Friday April 3, at 7:30 PM CT on SEC Network. Game 3 — regardless of Friday's result — is Saturday April 4 at 5 PM CT on ESPN. A Texas win tonight clinches the series for the Longhorns and would represent one of the signature road wins in college softball this season.

Alabama has the crowd, the history, and a 30-year head coach who knows how to respond after difficult losses. But Texas has the momentum, the No. 1 ranking, and a team that looks every bit as good as advertised. Tonight at Rhoads Stadium is a must-watch.
