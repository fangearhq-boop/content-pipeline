Kendall Wells Hits HR No. 28 — Oklahoma Freshman Now Two Away from NCAA Record

By Emily Rawlings | April 3, 2026 | I Love Softball

Kendall Wells has two home runs left to make history, and she has two games this weekend to do it.

The Oklahoma freshman slugger hit her 28th home run of the season on Thursday night — a 275-foot shot to straightaway center — in a 10-2 run-rule win over Kentucky at Love's Field. The blast was the latest in a season that has already rewritten the SEC record book, and now Wells is closing in on the all-time NCAA freshman single-season record.

The record is 30 home runs, shared by three players: Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma, 2018), Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma, 2012), and Kelly Majam (Hawaii). Wells set the SEC freshman home run record last Tuesday when she hit No. 27 against Wichita State. She reached 28 on Thursday. She needs two more.

A Run-Rule Win and a Record Countdown

Thursday's win came in five innings, with Oklahoma run-ruling Kentucky by eight runs. Wells's home run — her 275-foot blast to center field — was one of the game's defining moments, but it was not alone. Kasidi Pickering added her 15th home run of the season as well, a reminder that Oklahoma's offense extends well beyond its freshman sensation.

Oklahoma improved to 36-3 overall and 9-1 in SEC play with the win, tying Texas atop the conference standings after the Longhorns beat Alabama 9-1 in Tuscaloosa on Thursday night. The Sooners are on a remarkable April run, and Thursday's outcome kept them in lockstep with the defending national champions at the top of the SEC standings.

The Numbers Behind the Record Chase

Wells's season statistics are staggering for a first-year player. She is hitting .384 on the season with 57 RBI and an OPS over 1.500. She plays catcher — one of the most demanding defensive positions in softball — and has handled her duties in the field without issue while simultaneously becoming the most talked-about power hitter in the sport.

Head coach Patty Gasso — who has coached both Jocelyn Alo and Lauren Chamberlain — has described Wells's season as something genuinely unique. She hit 12 home runs in March alone. She entered April needing three for the record. She now needs two.

What's Next

Oklahoma hosts Kentucky in Games 2 and 3 of their series this weekend. Game 2 is tonight at Love's Field. Game 3 follows Saturday. Wells will have multiple opportunities to reach the record this weekend in front of the Norman crowd.

The nation is watching. History is within reach. And with two games still to play against Kentucky at home, Kendall Wells has every opportunity to tie — or break — the NCAA freshman home run record before the weekend is over.
