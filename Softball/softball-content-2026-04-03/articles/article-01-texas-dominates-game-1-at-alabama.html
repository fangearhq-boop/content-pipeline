Texas Dominates Game 1 at Alabama, 9-1 — Kavan Goes the Distance in Tuscaloosa

By Sarah Mitchell | April 3, 2026 | I Love Softball

No. 1 Texas arrived at Rhoads Stadium on Thursday night and delivered an immediate statement: the Longhorns had not come to Tuscaloosa to lose.

Texas battered Alabama 9-1 in Game 1 of the teams' three-game SEC series, pounding out 13 hits — including four home runs — against one of the conference's most respected defenses. Teagan Kavan took the ball and never gave it back, throwing a complete game and striking out nine batters in seven innings to improve Texas's record to 32-2 on the season.

The outcome moved Texas to 9-1 in SEC play. Alabama dropped to 7-3.

Kavan in Command

Kavan was efficient and dominant throughout the night. She allowed five hits and one earned run — an RBI single by Marlie Giles in the fourth inning — while keeping Alabama's offense largely off-balance from first pitch to last. The nine strikeouts represented another signature performance for a pitcher who has been one of the most consistent arms in the country all season.

Texas has now won four consecutive complete game performances from its starting pitchers in SEC play, a streak that reflects both the depth of the pitching staff and the confidence of head coach Mike White in letting his starters go deep into games.

Goode's Career Night

The most memorable individual moment of the game belonged to senior second baseman Leighann Goode, who hit two home runs on Thursday — the first multi-home run game of her career. Goode entered the night without a two-homer performance in her career and delivered it on the biggest stage the regular season has offered in 2026.

Texas finished with six extra-base hits in the 13-hit output, demonstrating the lineup's ability to hit for both average and power. Alabama, by contrast, was held to five hits against a Kavan who worked efficiently through the lineup and rarely gave anything away.

What's Next

The series continues Friday night with Game 2 at Rhoads Stadium at 7:30 PM CT on SEC Network. Alabama must win to keep the series alive — a loss tonight ends any hope of taking the series.

If Texas wins Game 2, it will clinch the series and move to 10-1 in SEC play with its most significant road win of the season. Game 3 is scheduled for Saturday at 5 PM CT on ESPN, regardless of Friday's result.

Patrick Murphy's team has shown it can respond in tough moments throughout this season. Thursday, though, was not close — and Texas doesn't appear to be in any kind of a mental slump following the A&M series loss. The Longhorns came to Tuscaloosa ready to play, and it showed.
