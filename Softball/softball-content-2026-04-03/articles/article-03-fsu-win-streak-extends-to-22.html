FSU Beats Notre Dame 7-2 to Extend Win Streak to 22 — Longest Active in Division I

By Jordan Reeves | April 3, 2026 | I Love Softball

Florida State has the longest active winning streak in Division I softball, and the Seminoles added another game to it Thursday night with a 7-2 win over Notre Dame at JoAnne Graf Field.

The victory extended FSU's streak to 22 consecutive games — a run that began in February and has carried the program to a 33-4 record and a 10-0 mark in the ACC. Pitcher Francik was strong in the opener, working five innings with seven strikeouts and allowing just one earned run.

Notre Dame, now 16-20 on the year and 5-8 in the ACC, has won just seven times in its all-time history against Florida State. Thursday did nothing to change that dynamic.

Sustained Excellence

What separates this FSU run from a simple hot streak is how long it has lasted and how complete the team's performance has been throughout it. During the 22-game streak, FSU has scored more than eight runs per game and held opponents well below their averages. The Seminoles have won with pitching, with offense, and with defense — a reflection of a genuinely well-rounded team operating near its ceiling.

Head coach Lonni Alameda, who earned her 1,000th career coaching win last week, has built a program that peaks in conference play. FSU's 10-0 ACC record is a testament to preparation and consistency.

Torres Leading the Offense

Isa Torres remains the offensive centerpiece. The FSU standout is batting .615 on the season — the highest average in all of Division I softball — with a .664 on-base percentage. She set the NCAA record for consecutive hits (16) earlier this season and has continued to produce at an extraordinary pace since returning from injury.

In her last 15 games, Torres has gone 37-for-52 (.712) with five home runs and 22 RBI. She is the leading contender for the NFCA Player of the Year award and will be one of the most prominent names in postseason discussions when the bracket is announced in May.

What's Next

Game 2 of the Notre Dame series is today, Friday April 3 at 6 PM ET on ACC Network Extra. Game 3 is Saturday at 2 PM ET on ACC Network. FSU has a chance to sweep the series and push the streak to 24 by Sunday.

The Seminoles are playing their best softball of the season at the right time of year. With the postseason approaching, their combination of the nation's longest active win streak and its statistical offensive leader makes them one of the most compelling teams in the sport.
