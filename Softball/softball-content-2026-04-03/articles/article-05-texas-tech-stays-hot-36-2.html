Texas Tech Run-Rules BYU 11-1 — Raiders Stay at No. 2 With 36th Win

By Emily Rawlings | April 3, 2026 | I Love Softball

Texas Tech did what Texas Tech does on Thursday night: the No. 2-ranked Red Raiders run-ruled BYU 11-1 and improved to 36-2 on the season, maintaining their position directly behind No. 1 Texas in the national polls.

The win was Tech's 23rd run-rule victory of the year — a staggering number that underscores just how dominant this program has been through the first two months of the season. Tech is 12-1 in Big 12 play and has barely broken a sweat against most of their opposition. They have been playing at the level of a top national seed since opening day.

Canady and the Rotation

NiJaree Canady remains the most recognizable name on the roster and one of the best pitchers in the country. The junior right-hander — who has been named college softball's highest-paid NIL player — has been a consistent force in the circle all season, recording strikeout totals that rank among the national leaders.

Tech's rotation extends beyond Canady, but she sets the tone. When she takes the mound, opponents know what they are facing. Opposing lineups have not found reliable answers, and the run-rule wins are the most direct evidence of that.

Nine Points Behind Texas

In the NFCA Coaches Poll Week 8 standings, Texas Tech trails No. 1 Texas by just nine points — a gap that has persisted for several weeks as both programs have continued winning. Every time Texas wins, Tech must also win to avoid the gap widening. Thursday, both did exactly that: Texas beat Alabama 9-1, and Tech beat BYU 11-1.

The scenario that closes the gap is a Texas loss. If the Longhorns drop a game in Tuscaloosa this weekend, Tech would have an argument to be elevated by the NFCA's 31 voting coaches when the Week 9 poll releases on Tuesday. That poll will reflect this weekend's results — making every game this Saturday critical for the national picture.

What's Next

Texas Tech continues Big 12 play this weekend. The Raiders have been remarkably consistent — their two losses this season are the only blemishes on what is otherwise a dominant record — and they enter the postseason picture as one of the strongest teams in the country regardless of their final ranking.

For now, Tech watches, wins, and waits. If Texas stumbles, the door opens. If Texas keeps winning, the Week 9 poll may look nearly identical to Week 8. Either way, the Raiders will be exactly where they need to be: unbeaten in their own schedule and ready for the postseason.
