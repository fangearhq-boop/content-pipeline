### STORY 1: Kendall Wells Breaks NCAA Freshman HR Record With No. 31

**Long-Form Post**
*Posting Window: 9:30 AM ET*

History was made Saturday night in Austin.

Kendall Wells hit home run No. 31 in the fifth inning of Oklahoma's 4-3 win over Texas — and with that swing, she officially became the greatest freshman power hitter in NCAA softball history. The three-run shot broke the all-time freshman single-season home run record of 30, previously shared by Lauren Chamberlain, Janie Reed Espinoza, and Dejah Mulipola.

Think about the stage: a back-and-forth Game 2 of the Red River Showdown, on the road against the defending national champions, with the SEC title and a postseason seed all hanging in the balance. Wells didn't just break the record — she did it in the most dramatic situation imaginable.

And she's not done. Six more home runs would break the all-time NCAA single-season record of 37, set by Espinoza at Arizona back in 1995 — a record that has stood for 31 years. Wells is on a pace no freshman has ever approached. She has 32 regular-season games remaining.

This is one of those seasons you'll tell people you watched live.

**Long-Form Post**
*Posting Window: 12:00 PM ET*

### STORY 4: Coach Patrick Murphy — First SEC Coach to 500 Conference Wins

**Long-Form Post**
*Posting Window: 3:30 PM ET*

It happened Saturday in Auburn, and it belongs in the history books.

Alabama head coach Patrick Murphy won his 500th conference game against Auburn — becoming the first softball coach in SEC history to reach that milestone. Not the first recently. The first ever. No other SEC coach has even reached 400 conference wins.

His record in the SEC now stands at 500-205, a winning percentage of .709. That's not just longevity — that's sustained excellence over decades of competition against the most talented conference in college softball.

The game itself was a fitting tribute. Pitcher Vic Moten threw a complete-game shutout against Auburn, allowing just one hit in a 4-0 Alabama win. The Crimson Tide clinched the series at Auburn and did so the way great Alabama teams do — with clean, dominant softball.

And the context makes it even richer: Alabama swept the series, improved to 38-3 on the season, and is now the No. 1 team in the Softball America rankings for the first time this season.

Murphy has been building something special in Tuscaloosa for a very long time. Saturday, it received the recognition it deserves.
