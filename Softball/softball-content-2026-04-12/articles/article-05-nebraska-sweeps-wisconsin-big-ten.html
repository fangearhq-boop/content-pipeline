Seven Straight: Nebraska Sweeps Wisconsin With Back-to-Back Shutouts

By Sarah Mitchell | April 12, 2026

Nebraska doesn't make much noise nationally. They just keep winning.

The Huskers swept Wisconsin in Madison this weekend with two consecutive shutouts — a 5-0 win on Friday and a 6-0 win on Saturday — extending their winning streak to seven games and improving to 13-1 in Big Ten play. Nebraska is now 32-6 on the season, and a program that often flies under the national radar is building an undeniable case for a top national seed heading toward regionals.

Two Shutouts in Two Days

Friday's opener showed Nebraska's pitching at its most precise. Wisconsin managed just two hits all night in a game that was never in doubt. Nebraska won 5-0 and the game had the feel of a team completely in control on the road.

Saturday's game was more about offense — but only in one inning. Nebraska scored all six runs in the fourth, then went back to work shutting Wisconsin out the rest of the way. The 6-0 final made it a clean sweep, and the back-to-back shutouts underlined the consistency of Nebraska's pitching staff over a full weekend.

Two games. Two shutouts. Zero runs allowed. That's the kind of defensive performance that changes how tournament committees think about a team.

The National Seed Conversation

Nebraska has quietly assembled one of the most compelling résumés in the country. Their 13-1 Big Ten record is the product of a conference that, while not the SEC in terms of overall depth, still features ranked programs and legitimate tournament teams. Going 13-1 requires winning at every level of competition in the conference.

Their overall record of 32-6 includes wins against quality opponents throughout the season. Their pitching staff — which has delivered multiple shutouts and kept opposing run totals consistently low — provides the kind of reliability that tournament teams depend on when every loss means going home.

The case for Nebraska as a top-8 national seed is real. A top-8 seed means hosting a regional and super regional, which provides a significant competitive advantage in the early rounds of the tournament. Nebraska's numbers say they've earned that conversation.

What's Next

Nebraska completes the Wisconsin series Sunday before returning to their Big Ten schedule. The winning streak stands at seven, the conference record is 13-1, and the season is approaching the stretch where every result carries added postseason weight.

For a team that rarely dominates national headlines, Nebraska has done something quietly impressive: built one of the best records in college softball through steady, consistent performance week after week. The Huskers may not have the star power of Oklahoma or the narrative momentum of Alabama, but they have the wins — and in the tournament, wins are the only thing that matters.
