Patrick Murphy Makes History: First SEC Coach to Reach 500 Conference Wins

By Jordan Reeves | April 12, 2026

In a conference where softball coaches have been winning games at an elite level for decades, no one had ever reached 500 of them. Patrick Murphy just became the first.

The Alabama head coach reached the milestone on Saturday in Auburn, earning his 500th SEC win as Vic Moten threw a complete-game shutout in a 4-0 Alabama victory. The Crimson Tide clinched the series at Auburn, improved to 38-3 on the season, and gave their coach a win that stands alone in conference history.

Murphy's all-time SEC record now reads 500-205, a winning percentage of .709. No other coach in the Southeastern Conference has accumulated even 400 conference wins. The gap between Murphy and the rest of the field isn't just a gap — it's a generation.

The Win Itself

Game 2 against Auburn provided a fitting backdrop for the milestone. Moten worked efficiently through the Auburn lineup, allowing just one hit over the complete game while walking three. It was her fourth shutout of the season — a performance that complemented the milestone perfectly.

Alabama had already taken Game 1 by a 1-0 score on Friday, with pitcher Jocelyn Briski earning the decision in a tight win. The series sweep over Auburn — the first time since 2014 that Alabama won each of its first five conference series — reflects the depth and consistency of a program that has been playing at an elite level for years under Murphy's direction.

What 500 SEC Wins Actually Means

The Southeastern Conference is the deepest, most competitive conference in college softball. Every season, multiple SEC programs are ranked nationally. Conference series are played against opponents who are themselves ranked, experienced, and well-coached. Winning 500 games in that environment requires sustained excellence over an extended period — not one good run, not a few great seasons, but decades of competitive softball.

Murphy has done it across multiple eras of the sport, adapting his program to changes in recruiting, rule modifications, and the evolving landscape of college softball. The fact that no other SEC coach is within 100 wins of his total tells the complete story.

Alabama Rises to No. 1

The milestone came alongside a broader achievement for the program: Alabama entered the week as the No. 1 team in the Softball America composite rankings for the first time this season. The rise reflects a combination of the Crimson Tide's strong recent results and the stumbles of other programs — Texas Tech dropped Game 2 to Utah, and FSU lost twice at Stanford.

Alabama at 38-3 with a 11-3 SEC record is playing the best softball of anyone in the country right now. Murphy's 500th win landed at the perfect moment.

What's Next

Alabama plays Game 3 at Auburn on Sunday before returning to their home schedule. The NFCA Week 10 coaches' poll releases Monday and will likely reflect Alabama's rise to the top of the national conversation. The official poll may differ from Softball America's composite, but the trajectory is clear.

For Murphy, the milestone is a number — one he'll move past on Monday and probably not think about much. That's the nature of coaches who build programs for the long term. The 500th win is a moment to acknowledge. The 501st is the one that matters next.
