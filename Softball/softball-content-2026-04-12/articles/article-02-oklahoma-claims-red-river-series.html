Oklahoma Claims the Red River Showdown: Sooners Take Series 2-0 in Austin

By Sarah Mitchell | April 12, 2026

Oklahoma traveled to Austin for the biggest series of the college softball regular season and left with exactly what they came for.

The No. 2 Sooners took the Red River Showdown series from No. 4 Texas with back-to-back wins at McCombs Field — 3-0 in Game 1 and 4-3 in Game 2 — delivering a statement that reverberates all the way to the WCWS seeding conversation. Game 3 on Sunday remains, but the series belongs to Oklahoma.

The sweep of one of college softball's most storied programs, on the road, in the second-ranked opponent's home stadium, speaks to the kind of team Oklahoma has built in 2026.

Game 1: Lowry's Complete-Game Masterpiece

Friday's opener belonged to pitcher Audrey Lowry from the first pitch. She threw a complete-game shutout against the defending national champions — seven innings, no runs allowed, total control. Texas, which had been nearly unbeatable at McCombs this season, never found a rhythm against her.

The offense did its part efficiently. Ella Parker drove in two runs with a first-inning home run. Isabela Emerling added a solo shot in the sixth to make it 3-0. Lowry made sure the lead was never threatened.

The loss came with a notable footnote for Texas: pitcher Teagan Kavan recorded her 500th career strikeout during the game — a genuine program milestone that was overshadowed by the 3-0 final. Kavan's achievement deserves recognition even in defeat.

Game 2: Wells Makes History, Oklahoma Takes the Series

Saturday's clincher had more drama. Texas pushed ahead 2-1 and threatened to extend the series into a decisive Game 3. Then the fifth inning arrived, and Kendall Wells stepped to the plate.

What followed was the biggest swing in college softball this season: a three-run home run — HR No. 31 — that gave Oklahoma a 4-2 lead and simultaneously broke the NCAA all-time freshman single-season home run record. The previous mark of 30 had been shared by three players, including Lauren Chamberlain and Janie Reed Espinoza. Wells owns it now.

Oklahoma held the 4-3 lead the rest of the way. The series was theirs.

What the Series Win Means for the SEC

The Red River Showdown result has direct standings implications. Oklahoma entered the weekend leading the SEC and extended that advantage with the series win. Texas, which had been within two games of the top, now finds itself in a more difficult position heading into the final stretch of conference play.

An Oklahoma sweep would have been significant. A Texas series win would have created a genuine race. The actual result — OU claiming the series — tilts the balance firmly in the Sooners' favor with conference play winding down.

What's Next

Oklahoma and Texas complete the series in Game 3 on Sunday at 1 p.m. CT on ESPN. The pressure is gone for the Sooners — they have the series. Texas needs to win to salvage something from a disappointing home weekend.

For Oklahoma, the focus now shifts to finishing the regular season strong and building toward the NCAA tournament. They have a legitimate case as the best team in the country. This Red River series — particularly the road shutout in Game 1 and the record-setting drama of Game 2 — reinforced that case as convincingly as any two days of softball this season.
