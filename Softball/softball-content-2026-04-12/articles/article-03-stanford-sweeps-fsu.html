Stanford Completes the Sweep: FSU Falls in Two Games at Palo Alto

By Emily Rawlings | April 12, 2026

One game would have been a story. Two games made it a message.

Stanford swept Florida State this weekend in Palo Alto, delivering back-to-back losses to the Seminoles for the first time this season and completing one of the most significant results in college softball so far in 2026. Game 1 ended 9-8 on Addyson Sheppard's walk-off grand slam. Game 2 wasn't close — Stanford won 7-2 in a more clinical performance.

FSU arrived in Palo Alto with a 25-game winning streak, the longest active run in the country. They leave with consecutive losses, a bruised national ranking, and some real questions about what the rest of this season holds.

Game 1: The Grand Slam That Started It All

Friday's opener had everything. FSU built a lead, let it slip, built it back. The Seminoles scored six runs in the top of the seventh inning to take what appeared to be a game-winning advantage — a stunning display of resilience from a team that had won 25 in a row.

Then Stanford came to bat in the bottom of the seventh. Addyson Sheppard ended it with a grand slam. Stanford 9, FSU 8. The streak was over.

The walk-off nature of the loss made it harder to process. FSU had done everything right for most of the game. They fought back when it looked hopeless. And still, it wasn't enough.

Game 2: No Drama, All Stanford

If Game 1 required a miracle to beat the Seminoles, Game 2 showed that Stanford simply outplayed them. The Cardinal won 7-2 without the late-inning drama — a cleaner, more decisive performance that made the sweep feel even more complete.

Two road losses in two days against a ranked opponent is a different kind of challenge than the Seminoles had faced all season. The 25-game streak had built through strong performances against a range of competition. Stanford offered a different test, on their home field, and passed it convincingly.

What This Means for FSU's Season

The rankings damage will be real. FSU entered the weekend at No. 5 in the NFCA poll. Two losses to a ranked team — particularly a sweep — will cost votes and likely push the Seminoles out of the top five when the Week 10 poll releases Monday. How far they fall depends on how other voters weigh the rest of the weekend's results.

What it does not mean is that FSU is no longer a legitimate tournament team. One difficult road trip does not undo a season that includes 25 consecutive wins and a roster that features Isa Torres — whose elite batting average continues to set her apart as one of the sport's best players regardless of team results. The Seminoles have the talent to recover, and the ACC schedule still gives them opportunities to rebuild their résumé.

But the path to a top national seed now requires more work. The sweep by Stanford is the kind of result that regional selection committees notice, and FSU will need to answer it with a strong finish to the regular season and a tournament run through the ACC.

What's Next

Stanford and FSU may play a Game 3 on Sunday before the Seminoles return to ACC play. For FSU, the challenge is simple: respond. The program has the history, the roster, and the coaching to bounce back from a difficult road trip. The question is whether they do it quickly enough to preserve their postseason positioning.

Stanford, meanwhile, has announced themselves as a genuine postseason threat with this series. Sweeping a team that had won 25 straight games is a résumé-building result of the highest order. The Cardinal will enter the rest of their season with significantly more national attention than they had a week ago.
