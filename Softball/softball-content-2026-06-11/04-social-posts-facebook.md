### STORY 1: Isa Torres — Day 4 Uncommitted

**FB Long-Form Post**
Window: 9:00 AM ET

Day 4 of the transfer portal window. The most anticipated commitment in college softball is still pending.

Isa Torres entered the portal on June 8 — the first day it opened — after a season that defied description. The Florida State shortstop hit .530 with a .591 on-base percentage and .978 slugging percentage, scoring 78 runs while winning both the ACC Player of the Year and D1 Softball Player of the Year awards. Those numbers weren't just good — they were program records at one of the most decorated programs in the country. She reportedly set an NCAA record by recording a hit in 16 consecutive at-bats.

One year of eligibility remains. Two programs — Texas and Texas A&M — have emerged as the clear frontrunners. Both have the facilities, the resources, and the ambition for a 2027 national championship run. Both would be transformed by adding a player of Torres's caliber.

For Texas, signing Torres would be the anchor of a portal class meant to maintain the dynasty through a coaching transition. Teagan Kavan and Katie Stewart are returning. Add Torres to that lineup and the Longhorns aren't just defending champions — they're favorites again.

For Texas A&M, Torres would be a program-defining commitment. The Aggies have been building steadily. Landing the Player of the Year would announce their arrival as a genuine title threat.

Eleven days remain in the portal window. The clock is running. But these decisions are never rushed — visits need to happen, conversations need to deepen, and Torres deserves the time to get it right.

What we do know: wherever she lands reshapes the 2027 national title race.

That power-and-average combination puts her in elite company for any portal class. At 16 home runs in 2026, Beachum is exactly the kind of player coaches at power programs covet — someone who can stand in against elite pitching in the postseason and make the pitcher pay for a mistake. Good hitters hit for average. Elite power hitters hit for both. Beachum does both.

The portal window runs through June 22. Eleven days remain. We're watching closely.

She is a proven, decorated hitter who was the best at her craft across all of Division I softball in 2026. Programs don't often get a chance to add a player like this in a portal window. When they do, they move fast.

### STORY 4: Samantha Swan — Commitment Watch

**FB Long-Form Post**
Window: 8:00 PM ET

Sometimes the most important portal development isn't the headline name — it's the visit that tells you where things are heading.

Arizona State graduate transfer Samantha Swan visited Texas on Monday, and what that visit means is clear: both sides are interested. Swan, a first-team All-Big 12 catcher out of Georgetown, TX, hit .383 with 12 home runs, 55 RBIs, and 21 doubles in 2026. Her .984 fielding percentage behind the plate confirms what scouts and coaches already know — she's one of the most complete catchers to come through the portal in recent years.

She's from Georgetown. That's 30 miles from Austin.

Texas has a specific need. Reese Atwood, the Longhorns' starting catcher, graduated after the 2026 championship season. That's a gap that needs to be filled before the fall. Swan doesn't just fill it — she potentially elevates it.

But a visit is one step. A commitment is another. Swan may also be visiting other programs. The portal window is only four days old. There's still time for multiple conversations to happen.

The question now is whether Texas can close. Head coach Mike White is simultaneously managing two coaching vacancies and building a portal class that could extend the dynasty into a three-peat. Landing Swan would be the first concrete win of this portal window for the Longhorns — and a clear signal that the machine is still running at full speed.

### STORY 5: Texas Staff Search

**FB Long-Form Post**
Window: 3:00 PM ET

The dynasty is still standing. But the coaching staff that built it looks very different today than it did two weeks ago.

In less than a week, Texas lost both Ehren Earleywine (offensive coordinator, now at Tennessee) and Kristen Zaleski (associate head coach, also departed). That's two of the program's most important non-head-coaching positions sitting vacant — and they need to be filled before fall practices begin.

Head coach Mike White remains. And critically, so do the two players who make Texas the team everyone is chasing: pitcher Teagan Kavan, the two-time Women's College World Series Most Outstanding Player, and outfielder Katie Stewart, who broke the program's single-season home run record in 2026. The talent is there. The infrastructure is there. The recruiting pipeline is there.

But coaching continuity matters. The staff that helped Texas execute at the highest level — including the offensive genius that engineered 99 home runs in a single season — has gaps to fill. Finding coaches who can maintain and advance what Earleywine and Zaleski built isn't a simple checklist item. It takes time, effort, and the right people.

What makes this moment particularly challenging: White is simultaneously managing the biggest portal window in recent memory. He's recruiting players, facilitating visits, and having scholarship conversations at the same time he's searching for coaches.

That's the reality of running a dynasty in today's college softball landscape. Everything moves at once. The programs that manage both the roster side and the staff side of this offseason will define the 2027 national title conversation.

Texas has the pieces. Now they need the people.
