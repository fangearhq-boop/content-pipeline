Jaysoni Beachum in the Portal — FSU's Power Bat Is One of the Best Available

By Emily Rawlings | June 11, 2026 | I Love Softball

All the headlines have gone to Isa Torres. That is understandable — Torres is the D1 Player of the Year, and her recruitment is the most closely followed story in the sport right now. But there is another Florida State player in this portal class who deserves serious attention, and she has been waiting patiently for her moment in the spotlight.

Jaysoni Beachum entered the transfer portal on June 8 — Day 1 of the window — after a 2026 season at Florida State in which she hit .417 with 16 home runs and 66 RBIs. That production is not supplementary. That is lineup-changing power from a player who proved herself against the best pitching in the ACC. Beachum is now available, and four days into the portal window, she has not yet announced where she'll finish her career.

What Beachum Brings

The combination of a .417 batting average and 16 home runs is not common in college softball. You can find hitters who average — they punch singles through the infield, get on base, and move runners. You can find power hitters who take three-pitch strikeouts against elite arms and still mash 15 home runs because they hit everything hard. Beachum can do both. Her .417 average demonstrates the hand-eye coordination and pitch recognition to make consistent contact. Her 16 home runs demonstrate what happens when she connects on something in the zone. Programs in the market for a middle-of-the-order presence need to be calling.

She spent her career at Florida State — one of the historically powerful programs in the sport. That means she was tested against elite competition in the ACC, the toughest conference in college softball. Her numbers came against those teams, against those pitchers, in high-pressure situations. This is not a player who put up gaudy numbers against weaker competition. This is a player who produced at a high level while wearing a target.

The FSU Portal Class Is a Package Deal

The fact that both Torres and Beachum are available from the same program is unusual. FSU had two of the most productive offensive players in the sport under one roof, and now both are in the market at the same time. For programs tracking this portal class, the theoretical dream scenario is landing both. One program adds Torres and Beachum to its roster and instantly has the most dangerous pair of offensive players acquired in any portal window in recent memory.

That scenario may be unlikely — roster space, scholarship limits, and position fit all create constraints — but the possibility makes this class fascinating. More likely, the programs finish divided: one lands Torres, another lands Beachum. But the program that secures even one of these two will have made a significant step toward contending in 2027.

What's Next

Eleven days remain in the portal window. Beachum has not yet committed, and with Torres drawing the primary spotlight, it is possible that Beachum will have more options than expected as programs scramble to evaluate their fallback plans. Her commitment announcement, when it comes, will matter. Coaches who have been focused on Torres will need to make a quick pivot if they miss her — and Beachum will be the name they call first.
