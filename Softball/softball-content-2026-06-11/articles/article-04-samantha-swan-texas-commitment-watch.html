Samantha Swan Visited Texas — The Catcher Commitment Watch Is On

By Sarah Mitchell | June 11, 2026 | I Love Softball

In the middle of a portal window dominated by headlines about Isa Torres and Moriah Polar, a visit happened on Monday that could quietly be the most important development in Texas's offseason. Arizona State graduate transfer Samantha Swan — first-team All-Big 12 catcher, .383 hitter, power arm behind the plate — visited the Longhorns.

She hasn't committed yet. But the visit tells you something: both sides want to have this conversation. For Texas, adding a catcher of Swan's quality is not optional — it is necessary.

What Texas Needs and What Swan Offers

Starting catcher Reese Atwood graduated after the 2026 national championship season. Atwood was a critical piece of the back-to-back championship puzzle — her defense and leadership behind the plate helped Texas manage its pitching staff through a postseason that required everything the Longhorns had. She is gone now. That gap has to be filled.

Swan fills it, and then some. The Arizona State graduate transfer hit .383 in 2026 with 12 home runs, 55 RBIs, and 21 doubles — a complete offensive profile for a player who catches every game. Her .984 fielding percentage confirms that her defense matches her offense. She was named first-team All-Big 12 and earned NFCA All-West Region Third Team honors. Over four seasons with ASU, she posted a career .322 batting average and .917 OPS. She is not a prospect. She is a proven, decorated catcher with one final year of eligibility.

And she is from Georgetown, Texas — 30 miles from Austin. The homecoming angle is not nothing. When programs are otherwise equal, familiarity and proximity can matter.

Texas's Broader Portal Picture

Swan is one piece of a larger puzzle for the Longhorns. Head coach Mike White is managing an unusually complex offseason — two coaching departures in the same week, a loaded portal class to pursue, and the weight of back-to-back championships creating expectations that only winning a third straight title will satisfy.

Adding Swan would give Texas a capable, experienced catcher around whom the coaching staff can build a defensive game plan. With Teagan Kavan expected to return, having a battery partner she can develop chemistry with through the fall and spring is valuable. Swan has four years of catching pitchers in big games. She understands how to call a game and work behind the plate under pressure.

What's Next

Swan has not committed, and it would be wrong to assume she will go to Texas without conducting full due diligence on her other options. But the visit is the first and most important signal. Programs that visit on the first Monday of the portal window are serious. Expect Swan's decision — wherever it falls — in the coming days. For Texas, this one matters.
