Isa Torres Portal Day 4 — Texas and A&M Still in Pursuit as Window Narrows

By Sarah Mitchell | June 11, 2026 | I Love Softball

Four days. No commitment. Eleven days left.

The transfer portal window opened June 8, and the most-watched recruitment in college softball is still unresolved. Isa Torres — the D1 Softball Player of the Year, the ACC Player of the Year, and the most decorated player available in any portal class in recent memory — has not announced her next destination. Texas and Texas A&M are the programs most frequently linked to her. The rest of college softball is watching and waiting.

Torres arrived at this moment after a 2026 season that can only be described as historic. The Florida State shortstop hit .530 — a program record — while posting a .591 on-base percentage and .978 slugging percentage. She scored 78 runs, all program records. She reportedly set an NCAA record with a hit in 16 consecutive at-bats. There was no more productive offensive player at any position in Division I softball in 2026. She then earned both the ACC Player of the Year and the national D1 Player of the Year award to close it out. One year of eligibility remains.

Texas or Texas A&M — What's at Stake

Both programs can make a compelling case, and both would be meaningfully transformed by adding Torres.

For Texas, the argument starts with continuity and ends with history. The Longhorns are the back-to-back national champions. Teagan Kavan — the two-time Women's College World Series Most Outstanding Player — is expected to return for 2027. So is outfielder Katie Stewart, who broke the program's single-season home run record with 31 in 2026. Add a player of Torres's caliber to that lineup and the Longhorns are not just defending their title — they are the overwhelming favorites to win it. The only concern: Texas is navigating coaching staff turnover, having lost both Ehren Earleywine and Kristen Zaleski in the same week. The program is in transition, but the talent core is still elite.

For Texas A&M, Torres would represent something different: arrival. The Aggies have been building a program capable of competing at the highest level, but a national championship has remained just out of reach. Landing the Player of the Year signals to recruits, fans, and opponents alike that the Aggies are ready to win now — not someday, not maybe, but now.

The Clock Is Running

The 15-day transfer portal window closes June 22. As of today, 11 days remain. That sounds like a lot of time, but portal windows compress quickly. Visits need to be scheduled and completed. Financial aid packages need to be worked out. Final conversations happen in the last few days before decisions get announced. Torres has every right to take her time — this is a consequential decision about the final year of her college career — but programs can feel urgency building even when the clock is still ticking.

What is clear is that this decision will be one of the most significant in the 2026-27 offseason cycle. The programs that miss on Torres will regroup. The program that lands her will spend the next several months explaining why they are the most dangerous team in college softball.

What's Next

The next major milestone in this story is a commitment announcement — which could come any day now. Both Texas and Texas A&M are presumed to be in active communication with Torres. Additional visits or campus trips, if they haven't already occurred, may be in the pipeline. When she announces, the reverberations will be felt across the sport. Until then, the wait continues — and the entire college softball world is paying attention.
