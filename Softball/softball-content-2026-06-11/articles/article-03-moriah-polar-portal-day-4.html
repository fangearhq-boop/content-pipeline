Moriah Polar — The Nation's Best Hitter Is Still in the Portal on Day 4

By Jordan Reeves | June 11, 2026 | I Love Softball

She led Division I softball in batting average. She led Division I softball in hits. She is a second-team All-American with one year of eligibility remaining, and she is currently available to any program that can make the right offer.

Moriah Polar entered the transfer portal at the start of the window, and four days later, the Purdue outfielder has not committed anywhere. That is not a red flag — these decisions take time, and the portal window runs for 15 days for a reason. But the longer this story develops, the more the college softball world is left wondering: who is going to land the best available hitter in the portal class?

The Numbers That Define Her

Polar hit .555 in 2026 — a number that would seem unbelievable if she hadn't spent three seasons at Purdue demonstrating that she simply hits. Her 101 hits in 2026 led all of Division I softball. Over three seasons with the Boilermakers, she accumulated 218 hits, 103 RBIs, and 137 runs scored. She was named a second-team All-American and was among 25 finalists for USA Softball Player of the Year.

Batting average, unlike many statistics, is not context-dependent. It does not inflate against weaker opponents. It requires consistent, repeatable contact against every pitcher you face in every conference. Polar posted a .555 mark in 2026 — that means she got a hit in more than half of her plate appearances that resulted in outs or hits. Against Big Ten pitching, in tournament games, in every context she faced. The number is real.

What Any Program Gets

Adding Polar to a roster is not complicated to evaluate: every lineup she enters immediately becomes more dangerous. A .555 hitter in your lineup means pitchers cannot afford to be careless. She makes the hitters around her better because pitchers cannot work around the whole lineup without exposing someone. She changes the dynamic of every at-bat before and after hers on the card.

She has one year of eligibility. That means a program gets one season with the nation's leading hitter from 2026 — and then she moves on. The urgency is real. The window is 15 days. Programs have already been in conversation with Polar, and the competition for her services is significant.

What's Next

The portal window closes June 22. As of today, 11 days remain. Polar's commitment announcement could come at any time, and when it does, it will trigger a wave of analysis about which program just got better — and which programs missed their chance. The best pure hitter available in the 2026 portal class won't stay available forever. The clock is running.
