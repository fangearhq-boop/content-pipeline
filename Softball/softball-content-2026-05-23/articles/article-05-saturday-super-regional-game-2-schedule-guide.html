Saturday Super Regionals: Seven Game 2s, Seven WCWS Spots on the Line

By Emily Rawlings

May 23, 2026

Seven Super Regional Game 2s are on the schedule Saturday. Four home teams must win to survive. Three home teams can clinch a WCWS berth. The field of eight that goes to Devon Park gets decided today.

Saturday is the biggest single day of games in the 2026 NCAA softball tournament. Seven Super Regional Game 2s will be played across the country, with WCWS berths on the line in every series. Tennessee is already in Oklahoma City. The other seven spots get decided starting at 12:30 PM ET.

Friday produced multiple stunning results. Mississippi State upset No. 1 Oklahoma 11-9. Arizona State beat defending champion Texas 4-1. Texas Tech won at Florida 10-8, snapping the Gators' 36-1 home tournament record. Four of the seven home teams lost Game 1 — meaning today those four programs are in must-win territory while three others are one win from the WCWS.

Texas Tech at Florida — 12:30 PM ET, ABC

Texas Tech enters as the series leader after their 10-8 Game 1 win at Katie Seashole Pressly Stadium. Mia Williams hit the go-ahead two-run home run in the seventh. Florida was 36-1 in home NCAA Tournament games since 2013 before Friday. That record is now 36-2. The Gators must win today to survive. Texas Tech wins to advance to the WCWS for the first time.

Mississippi State at Oklahoma — 1:00 PM ET

Mississippi State stunned No. 1 Oklahoma 11-9 in Game 1, with a five-run seventh inning completing a dramatic comeback at Love's Field in Norman. Kendall Wells hit two home runs (her 38th and 39th) for Oklahoma in a losing effort. Oklahoma must win Game 2 to stay alive. Mississippi State wins the series with a victory today. The Wells home run chase — she is one behind Megan Grant's all-time record of 40 — adds an individual stakes layer to every at-bat.

LSU at Alabama — 3:00 PM ET, ESPN

Alabama blanked LSU 7-0 in Game 1 Friday evening at Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa. The No. 1 national seed's pitching — led by Vic Moten — was dominant in the opener. Alabama wins the series and advances to the WCWS with a Game 2 victory today. LSU must win to force a deciding Game 3.

Duke at Arkansas — 4:00 PM ET, ESPN2

Arkansas won Game 1 in run-rule fashion, 14-5 in five innings, at Bogle Park in Fayetteville. Head coach Courtney Deifel's team — in its sixth Super Regional appearance and still searching for a first WCWS berth — can end the drought today with a Game 2 win. Duke must win to extend the series.

Oklahoma State at Nebraska — 5:00 PM ET, ESPN2

Nebraska won Game 1 (which was originally postponed Thursday due to weather) by a score of 8-1 at Bob Devaney Sports Center in Lincoln. The Cornhuskers — in their first-ever home Super Regional — can reach the WCWS for the first time since 2013 with a Game 2 win today. Oklahoma State must win to extend the series.

UCF at UCLA — Evening

UCLA won Game 1 at home, 9-1, at Easton Stadium in Los Angeles. The Bruins lead the series 1-0. UCLA can clinch a WCWS berth with a Game 2 win. UCF — the tournament's Cinderella story after beating No. 9 FSU in Tallahassee during the Regional — must win to stay alive and force a Game 3.

Arizona State at Texas — 7:00 PM ET, ESPN

Arizona State leads the Austin Super Regional 1-0 after their 4-1 Game 1 upset of defending national champion Texas. The Sun Devils can eliminate the defending champions tonight with a Game 2 win. Texas must win to force a Game 3 and keep their title defense alive. McCombs Field will be the most emotionally charged stadium in the country for this game.

What's Next

The Women's College World Series begins May 28 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. Tennessee has already clinched a berth after sweeping Georgia in the Knoxville Super Regional. The remaining seven WCWS spots will be filled by the winners of today's Game 2s and any Game 3s that result from split series. Game 3s, if needed, would be played Sunday May 24. The complete WCWS bracket will be set once all eight berths are filled.
