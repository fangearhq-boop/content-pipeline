Mississippi State Stuns No. 1 Oklahoma 11-9 — Wells Hits HRs 38 and 39 in Losing Effort

By Emily Rawlings

May 23, 2026

Mississippi State scored five runs in the seventh inning to complete a stunning comeback at Love's Field. Kendall Wells was extraordinary — and Oklahoma still lost.

Kendall Wells hit her 38th and 39th home runs of the season Friday afternoon at Love's Field in Norman. She went 4-for-5 with six RBIs. She helped Oklahoma build leads of four runs — twice. And when the final out was recorded, Mississippi State had won 11-9 in one of the most stunning Super Regional upsets in recent memory.

Mississippi State scored five runs in the top of the seventh inning to complete the comeback and take a 1-0 series lead. Game 2 is today at 1 PM ET in Norman. Oklahoma must win to survive. Mississippi State must win to complete one of the tournament's great upsets.

The No. 1 national seed has not lost a Super Regional game since 2015. Friday afternoon, they did.

Wells' Historic Day in a Losing Game

There is no clean way to describe what Kendall Wells did Friday except to say it was extraordinary in a game that somehow ended in a loss.

In the third inning, Wells jumped on the first pitch she saw from Mississippi State pitcher Faircloth and sent a 279-foot blast over the fence — her 38th home run of the season. The Love's Field crowd erupted. Oklahoma followed with additional runs in the inning to build what looked like a comfortable advantage.

In the sixth inning, Wells hit her 39th — a moon shot to deep center field that put the Sooners up by four. At that point, Oklahoma had every reason to believe the game was under control.

It was not. Mississippi State had other plans. Wells is now at 39 home runs for the season — second all-time in NCAA softball history, one behind UCLA's Megan Grant (40).

The Five-Run Seventh That Changed Everything

Oklahoma led 9-6 entering the top of the seventh inning. The Sooners were three outs from escaping with a Game 1 victory that would put pressure squarely on Mississippi State to win two straight.

Mississippi State scored five runs in the seventh. The comeback was complete when the inning ended, and the stunned silence in Norman was followed by the realization that the No. 1 seed had just dropped Game 1 at home.

Oklahoma's pitching staff, which had been reliable throughout the season and the regional, could not hold the lead when it mattered most. Mississippi State's hitters kept fighting even as Wells put on a historical performance at the plate. The Bulldogs did not fold when they fell behind multiple times.

What This Means for the Series

Mississippi State taking Game 1 means Oklahoma now faces the same position the Bulldogs were in before the game started — a must-win situation in a hostile environment. The Sooners have won before under pressure. The question is whether the momentum shift from this loss can be absorbed by a team that has never lost a Super Regional since 2015.

For Mississippi State, the challenge is simple: play one more game the way they played Game 1 — aggressive, unafraid, and willing to keep swinging even when a historic opponent is hitting home runs on the other side.

What's Next

Game 2 is today, Saturday May 23, at 1 PM ET at Love's Field in Norman. Oklahoma must win to force a deciding Game 3 on Sunday. Mississippi State wins the series and advances to the Women's College World Series with a victory. A Game 3, if needed, would be Sunday at Love's Field. For Kendall Wells, every at-bat today comes with the weight of both a team's season and the most compelling individual HR race in college softball history — she trails UCLA's Megan Grant by one home run at 39 to 40.
