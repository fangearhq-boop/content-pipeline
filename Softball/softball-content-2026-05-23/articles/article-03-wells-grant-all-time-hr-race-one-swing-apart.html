Wells (39) vs. Grant (40): The All-Time Home Run Race Is One Swing Away From a Tie

By Jordan Reeves

May 23, 2026

For the first time in NCAA softball history, two players have 39 or more home runs in the same season. One swing separates Kendall Wells from tying Megan Grant's all-time record — and both play this weekend.

College softball has never seen this before.

Megan Grant entered the 2026 NCAA Tournament as the all-time home run record holder, having hit her 40th on May 17 in the Regional Final — a grand slam that extended the record she had already broken earlier in the season. Grant's 40 home runs were unprecedented. No player in NCAA softball history had ever reached that number.

Then Friday happened.

Kendall Wells hit home run No. 38 and home run No. 39 in Oklahoma's Super Regional Game 1 against Mississippi State. In the span of one afternoon, Wells went from tied for the old all-time record to second all-time — one home run behind the woman who now holds the record. Both players are still competing. Both are in the Super Regional round right now.

Grant's Historic Season

Megan Grant entered this season as one of the nation's best power hitters. What she did in 2026 was something else entirely. Grant broke the old NCAA single-season home run record — 37, held by Laura Espinoza of Arizona from 1995 — earlier this season, and has been adding to her mark ever since.

The record-breaking 38th home run came with the weight of history. The 40th — a grand slam in the Regional Final — was the kind of swing that announces a season for the ages. Grant has done what no player in the history of Division I softball has done: hit 40 home runs in a single season.

UCLA plays Game 2 of their Super Regional against UCF in Los Angeles today. Grant's number — 40 — is the target. Wells is one behind it.

Wells' Pursuit

Kendall Wells entered the Super Regional weekend at 37 home runs — tied with Espinoza's old mark. She hit her 38th and 39th in Game 1 against Mississippi State Friday. The fact that Oklahoma still lost 11-9 says everything about how remarkable Wells' season has been — she can hit two home runs and drive in six runs in a Super Regional game and the story becomes about something bigger than her numbers.

Wells' home run totals this season have been a steady march through history. Freshman record. SEC single-season record. Oklahoma program record. The all-time tie at 37. And now 39 — one behind Grant with at least one more game to play in Game 2 today.

Every at-bat Wells takes in Game 2 comes with the possibility of a tie for the all-time record. Oklahoma also needs to win the game to survive the series, which adds a second layer of stakes to everything Wells does today.

An Unprecedented Competition

There is no historical precedent for two players having at least 39 home runs in the same NCAA season. The old record — 37 — stood for 31 years before Grant broke it. Now both Grant and Wells have surpassed that number, with Wells at 39 and Grant at 40.

Whether Wells ties Grant today, extends the chase into a potential Game 3, or sees her season end before she catches up, the 2026 season has already produced something the sport has never seen. Two players. Both in the Super Regional round. One home run apart. A record that stood for three decades now pushed to heights that seemed impossible a year ago.

What's Next

Kendall Wells and Oklahoma play Game 2 today at 1 PM ET at Love's Field in Norman. A Wells home run today would tie Grant's record at 40. Megan Grant and UCLA face UCF in Game 2 in Los Angeles today. A Grant home run would extend her lead to two. The WCWS, which begins May 28 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, could bring both players to the same stage for the final act of this race — if both advance through the Super Regionals. The outcome of both series today will determine whether this individual story has more chapters to write.
