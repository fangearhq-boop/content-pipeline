Arizona State Upsets Defending Champion Texas 4-1 in Super Regional Game 1

By Sarah Mitchell

May 23, 2026

No. 19 Arizona State went into Austin and beat the defending national champions 4-1 in Game 1. Texas now faces a must-win situation tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN.

The defending national champion is on the ropes.

No. 19 Arizona State traveled to McCombs Field in Austin on Friday night and defeated No. 3 Texas 4-1 in Super Regional Game 1, handing the defending national champions a loss in their own building and putting them in a must-win situation in Game 2. The Sun Devils lead the series 1-0. Texas must win tonight at 7 PM ET on ESPN to force a deciding Game 3.

Arizona State outpitched and outplayed the Longhorns across seven innings, limiting a Texas lineup that won the national title last May to a single run. The result was not a fluke. It was a statement.

Arizona State's Performance

The Sun Devils entered the Super Regional as the No. 19 national seed — the lowest-seeded team in this round of the tournament. They did not play like it in Game 1. Arizona State's pitching controlled Texas's lineup throughout the game, limiting damage and keeping the Longhorns off rhythm at critical moments.

Arizona State scored four runs — enough to handle everything Texas could produce in response. The four-run margin tells the story of a game that was never particularly close despite the eventual 4-1 final. The Sun Devils played with confidence throughout, which is exactly what you'd expect from a team that has earned a spot in the Super Regional round.

Texas's Situation

Texas won the national championship a year ago. They returned in 2026 with a roster built to defend it, a home Super Regional as the No. 3 national seed, and the weight of expectation that comes with being the defending champion. McCombs Field was supposed to be their fortress.

Game 1 did not go according to plan. The Longhorns managed one run against Arizona State's pitching and could not find the multi-run innings that have defined their offense all season. The defending champions now face exactly the position they put opponents in all year — a must-win game with a season on the line.

Texas has won in elimination scenarios before. The 2025 national championship run required winning from behind multiple times. But losing Game 1 at home to a double-digit seed is a different kind of pressure, and Game 2 will test how this team responds when the title defense is genuinely in jeopardy.

The Upset Picture in Context

Arizona State's win is not an isolated result. Friday's Super Regional round produced multiple major upsets: Mississippi State stunned No. 1 Oklahoma 11-9, Texas Tech beat Florida 10-8 at Gainesville — where Florida was 36-1 since 2013. The 2026 Super Regional round is producing bracket chaos at a rate not seen in recent tournament history.

Arizona State fits into that picture as a team that arrived, prepared, and executed against a program with significantly more resources and a banner already hanging in the building. The Sun Devils are here because they played well enough to be here — and Friday proved they belong.

What's Next

Game 2 is tonight, Saturday May 23, at 7 PM ET on ESPN at McCombs Field in Austin. Texas wins to force a deciding Game 3, which would be played Sunday. Arizona State wins to advance to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. For the Longhorns, tonight is the most important game of their 2026 season — a loss ends the title defense and the year. For Arizona State, it is an opportunity to complete one of the more stunning upsets in Super Regional history and send the Sun Devils to the WCWS for the first time.
