Tennessee Sweeps Georgia, Returns to WCWS for Second Straight Year

By Sarah Mitchell

May 23, 2026

Sage Mardjetko completed the job in Game 2, holding Georgia to four hits in a 2-1 victory. Tennessee is heading to Devon Park for the 10th time in program history.

Tennessee did it again. The Lady Vols swept Georgia in the Knoxville Super Regional on Friday night, claiming a 2-1 Game 2 victory that sends them to the Women's College World Series for the second straight year and the 10th time in program history.

Sage Mardjetko delivered the complete-game win in Game 2, allowing four hits and one run while striking out several Georgia batters across seven innings. One night after Karlyn Pickens struck out 10 in a complete-game Game 1 victory, Mardjetko matched her intensity and finished the job.

Tennessee finishes the season 47-10. The Lady Vols are Devon Park-bound. The only question now is how far they go in Oklahoma City.

Mardjetko Quiets Georgia's Offense

Game 2 had the feel of a game that could go either way — Georgia came in having nothing to lose after losing Game 1, while Tennessee needed one more win to close out the series. The Bulldogs scored the only run Mardjetko would allow, but she kept the margin tight throughout and never gave Georgia the multi-run inning they needed.

The four-hit, one-run complete game in an elimination setting is a statement from Mardjetko about who she is as a pitcher. The postseason spotlight didn't change how she threw. Georgia hit the ball well enough in spots but could not string enough together to change the outcome.

The Pickens-Mardjetko one-two in the Super Regional gave Tennessee back-to-back complete-game efforts from two different pitchers. That kind of pitching depth is what separates the programs that make deep WCWS runs from the ones that don't.

Georgia's Season in Context

Georgia finished at 41-20. That record — more than 40 wins, 12-12 in the SEC — reflects a program that earned their Super Regional appearance. The Bulldogs were not here by accident. Addisen Fisher threw a complete-game shutout in the Regional Final. They arrived in Knoxville with legitimate momentum.

But Tennessee was simply the better team across these two games. Back-to-back complete-game performances from the Lady Vols' pitching staff meant Georgia's offense got one sustained opportunity in four innings of competitive at-bats and couldn't convert. The Bulldogs will build on this season. Their program is in good shape.

For Tennessee, sweeping a team that capable in a sold-out home building is exactly the kind of momentum that carries into Oklahoma City.

Tennessee's Road to the WCWS

The Lady Vols have now hosted four consecutive Super Regionals — the first program in NCAA softball history to accomplish that. And in each of those four, they have advanced. The combination of home-field advantage, consistent pitching depth, and a lineup that doesn't beat itself has made Tennessee the standard for sustained Super Regional success.

The 10th WCWS appearance in program history adds Tennessee to a short list of programs that define what sustained excellence in college softball looks like. Karlyn Pickens, Sage Mardjetko, and the rest of this roster have earned their spot in Oklahoma City.

What's Next

Tennessee advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. As one of the eight remaining teams in the country, the Lady Vols will receive their bracket placement and first-round matchup when the full WCWS field is set. Tennessee enters Devon Park as one of the tournament favorites, armed with two complete-game pitching performances and a 47-10 record that speaks to the quality of this group. For Tennessee fans, the next chapter begins May 28 in Oklahoma City.
