Megan Grant Hit 42 Home Runs, Broke a 31-Year Record, and Is Now Up for an ESPYS

By Sarah Mitchell

July 4, 2026

On a Fourth of July built for celebrating American achievement, here is one worth knowing about if you missed it during the season: Megan Grant of UCLA broke the NCAA Division I single-season home run record in 2026.

Forty-two home runs. Sixty-three games. A record that had stood since 1995.

The previous mark was 37, set by Arizona's Laura Espinoza in 1995. It survived the Title IX boom, the dramatic growth in scholarship money and player development, and 31 years of elite college hitters who couldn't reach it. Grant reached it — and went five past it.

What 42 Home Runs in One Season Looks Like

To understand the scale of what Grant did, consider the frame: she hit 42 home runs in 63 games. That's a home run every 1.5 games, for an entire college season, against Division I pitching. Softball seasons are compressed — typically February through June — so the concentration of power over such a short window makes the number even more remarkable.

She finished her career at UCLA as the program's all-time home run leader with 91. That number — 91 career home runs — is its own separate conversation. No Bruin before her got there. She left Los Angeles with a record that will likely stand for years, if not decades.

Laura Espinoza's 37 held for 31 years. Grant's 42 is a harder number to imagine anyone reaching in a single season.

The ESPYS Nomination

The recognition extends beyond the softball community. Grant has been nominated for a 2026 ESPYS Best Record-Breaking Performance award, alongside NFL defensive end Myles Garrett, Norwegian cross-country skier Johannes Høsflot Klæbo, and marathon runner Sabastian Sawe.

The ESPYS air on July 15 at 8 PM ET on ABC — just 11 days from now. A win in this category would be a significant moment for college softball's visibility on the national sports stage.

She is not alone from the softball world in this year's nominations: Texas softball has been nominated for ESPYS Best Team, recognizing the Longhorns' back-to-back national championship run. The sport is getting its moment.

Why This Record Matters Beyond the Number

Records in college softball don't always travel. The general sports audience tends to overlook milestones in women's team sports unless they come with a dramatic national broadcast moment. Grant's record was set during the regular season, not on a championship stage.

But the ESPYS nomination puts it in front of a massive audience on a mainstream platform. That's the kind of distribution softball rarely gets — and it comes at a moment when the sport is more visible than it has ever been, with AUSL's professional league in its inaugural season and two consecutive national championships for Texas driving national coverage.

What's Next

Mark July 15 on the calendar: the 2026 ESPYS, 8 PM ET on ABC, where Grant is up for Best Record-Breaking Performance and Texas softball is up for Best Team. Whether either wins, the nominations represent a meaningful moment for the sport's national profile. Grant's professional future — should she pursue it — is also worth tracking. A hitter with 91 career home runs and a record-breaking 2026 season is exactly the kind of player the AUSL was built to showcase.
