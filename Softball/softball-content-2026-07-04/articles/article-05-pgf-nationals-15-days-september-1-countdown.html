PGF Nationals Is 15 Days Away: What the Summer Countdown Means for College Softball

By Jordan Reeves

July 4, 2026

The Fourth of July is a good day to take stock of where the college softball calendar stands, because the next six weeks are going to move quickly.

PGF Nationals opens in 15 days — July 19 at Great Park in Irvine, California. The roster freeze deadline is July 13, which is just nine days away. And September 1, when the Class of 2028 contact window officially opens, is 59 days out. The summer that felt like it had space is almost out of it.

PGF Nationals: The Most Important Summer Event

Premier Girls Fastpitch Nationals at Great Park Irvine (8000 Great Park Blvd, Irvine, CA 92618) is the single most important summer evaluation event in college softball. Every major program sends its coaching staff. Every top recruit who wants to be seen in front of the widest audience shows up.

Week 1 (July 19-26) features the 18U and 16U age groups — the brackets where the Class of 2027 and the opening wave of Class of 2028 prospects compete. Week 2 covers the 14U, 12U, and 10U fields.

For the Class of 2027 — the primary portal and transfer cycle still working its way through resolution — PGF Nationals is the last major evaluation window before staffs shift fully into the 2028 cycle. Programs with open scholarship spots are using these two weeks to evaluate recruits who might fill them in the freshman class.

The Roster Freeze Deadline: Nine Days

July 13 is the roster freeze deadline for the transfer portal. Any uncommitted portal transfer who hasn't found a home by then goes into a waiting period. This is the mechanism that creates urgency in the next week — and it's why stories like Tia Milloy's portal saga are entering their most consequential hours right now.

Programs that haven't closed their 2027 portal classes are working against this clock. The freeze concentrates recruiting activity into a tight window and then releases it on the other side: once the freeze lifts, portal movement continues but at a lower volume as fall camps approach.

September 1: The Class of 2028 Window Opens in 59 Days

September 1 is when coaches can officially contact prospects in the Class of 2028 — current rising high school sophomores who would be the freshman class entering college in the fall of 2028. Under the House Settlement 25-scholarship cap, each freshman spot carries more weight than it did under the previous 12-scholarship limit. Programs can't carry depth by overloading on walk-ons. Every roster spot matters.

PGF Nationals is the last major evaluation event before September 1 opens. Programs use these July weeks to identify their top 2028 targets in person, so when the contact window opens, they know exactly who they're calling first.

The PGF Elite Camp on July 26 — NCAA compliant and open to graduation years 2027 through 2031 — adds another layer of access for prospects who want direct face time with coaching staffs in an instructional environment.

What's Next

The next nine days are the most active in the 2026-27 transfer cycle. Watch for uncommitted portal players — led by Tia Milloy — to find homes before July 13. PGF Nationals begins July 19 and runs through July 26. The September 1 class of 2028 contact window opens 59 days from today. For anyone who follows college softball recruiting, the next six weeks will generate more news than the prior six combined.
