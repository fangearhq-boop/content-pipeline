Tia Milloy Day 40: A "Forks Up" Post and the Last Holiday Window

By Emily Rawlings

July 4, 2026

Forty days ago, Tia Milloy entered the transfer portal. And on the Fourth of July — a day when the college softball world is paying close attention — she posted two words on X/Twitter that sent it spinning.

"Forks up 😈"

The phrase, paired with a pitchfork emoji, carries immediate association with Arizona State, whose Sun Devils use the pitchfork as their signature hand gesture. No formal commitment followed. No announcement was attached. But in a portal saga that has now stretched 40 days without resolution, any signal reads as a signal.

Milloy has not commented publicly beyond the post.

The Race to Land Her

Florida State has been the most consistently reported suitor, with the Seminoles in active conversations with Milloy's camp. Their urgency is real: FSU lost Isa Torres (.530 batting average) to Texas and Jaysoni Beachum (.417 BA, 16 HR) to Florida in this portal cycle. The program needs a bat, and Milloy's left-handed power profile fits what FSU runs offensively.

Tennessee and Texas Tech have also remained engaged. Tennessee's interest has been noted since the portal opened. Texas Tech, fresh off a runner-up finish at the Women's College World Series, has the recruiting capital and program momentum to be a legitimate player.

If the "forks up" post is what it looks like, Arizona State enters — or has been quietly in — the picture as well.

Who Is Tia Milloy?

Milloy is a 5-foot-8 utility player from Woodinville, Washington, who appeared in 91 games across two seasons at Oklahoma, with 16 starts. She hit a combined .295 with nine home runs. Her sisters Amirah and Kiki both played college softball — Amirah at Washington, Kiki at Tennessee — so she understands program culture and comes from a family with a serious relationship with the sport.

She entered the portal with two years of eligibility remaining after the Sooners were eliminated by Mississippi State in the Norman Super Regional in late May. That's her value: not a one-year contributor, but a multi-year foundational piece at whatever program lands her.

The July 4 Window and What Comes Next

July 4 weekend is the last significant stretch of coaching attention before staffs begin traveling to PGF Nationals on July 19. That tournament — at Great Park Irvine — is when recruiting energy splits between finishing uncommitted portal players and evaluating the Class of 2028 for the upcoming September 1 contact window.

The roster freeze deadline hits July 13. That means any portal transfer without a destination has nine days to find one before the window closes.

For 40 days, Milloy has been the most-watched uncommitted player in college softball. The "forks up" post may be a tease. It may be a commitment signal. It may be nothing at all. But in a portal cycle that has moved fast everywhere else, this saga has been notably quiet — and that quiet may be ending soon.

By the time the fireworks stop tonight, the softball world may know more.

What's Next

Watch Milloy's social media channels closely. A formal commitment post typically follows within 24-72 hours of any signal post. If the destination is Arizona State, it would represent a significant recruiting win for a Sun Devils program working to re-establish itself among the sport's elite. If it's FSU, Tennessee, or Texas Tech, the story continues with her joining a contender immediately. And if the portal freeze on July 13 arrives without a commitment, Milloy would be among the most notable uncommitted players remaining in the cycle.
