No. 1 Oklahoma Opens SEC Quarterfinals Tonight vs. Georgia — What to Watch

By Jordan Reeves May 7, 2026

Oklahoma's Tournament Begins

After watching the chaos of the first two rounds unfold — Tennessee eliminated, Texas A&M sent home — it's time for the top teams to take the floor. Tonight at 7 PM CT at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, No. 1 Oklahoma opens the 2026 SEC Tournament quarterfinals against No. 9 Georgia. The Sooners enter as the top overall seed, and for good reason: they are 48-7 on the season and won the 2026 SEC Regular Season Championship with a 20-4 conference record.

Oklahoma earned a bye through the first two rounds — one of the privileges of finishing as the league's best team — and spent the early part of the week watching the bracket develop around them. Georgia advanced by defeating No. 8 LSU 7-3 in the opening round, then navigated to tonight's quarterfinal slot. The Bulldogs are battle-tested. They will not be paralyzed by the moment.

Why This Matters More Than the Seed Line

The obvious read on this matchup is a heavy Oklahoma favorite vs. a team just happy to be in the quarterfinals. But that framing ignores what is at stake for the Sooners specifically. Oklahoma is in its second year in the SEC. They have already made the conference's expectations their own by winning the regular-season title, but postseason confirmation — specifically a deep run here in Lexington — would cement their standing as the program to beat heading into the NCAA Tournament.

The SEC Tournament winner earns an automatic bid and typically receives a strong national seed. For Oklahoma, winning this tournament would be a signal to every other top program that the Sooners are operating at a different level entering Oklahoma City. That is the context that makes every game starting tonight feel significant.

Georgia's Path to an Upset

Georgia beat LSU, which is not nothing. The Bulldogs showed real offensive capability and came out of that game with momentum. To upset Oklahoma tonight, they would need to neutralize the Sooners' lineup depth — which is one of the most balanced in the country — while generating sustained pressure of their own. That is a tall order. Oklahoma does not have obvious vulnerabilities. Their 48-7 record was built against real competition.

Still, the first two rounds of this tournament should serve as a reminder: No. 5 Tennessee is gone. No. 6 Texas A&M is gone. Upsets happen, and Georgia has at least earned the right to be taken seriously.

How to Watch

Oklahoma vs. Georgia is scheduled for 7 PM CT tonight at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky. The winner advances to the SEC Tournament semifinals. For Oklahoma, this is the opening act of what the program hopes will be a tournament-winning run. For Georgia, it's a chance to write their own version of what has already been a wild week in Lexington.
