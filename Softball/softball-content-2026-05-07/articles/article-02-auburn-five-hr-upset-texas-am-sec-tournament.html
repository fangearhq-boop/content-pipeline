Auburn's 5-HR Explosion Stuns A&M 11-8 — Ratliff Walk-Off Sends Tigers to SEC Quarterfinals

By Emily Rawlings May 7, 2026

Tournament Softball at Its Finest

If you need a reminder of why conference tournament softball is unlike anything else in the sport, Wednesday in Lexington provided one. Auburn — a team that finished the 2026 SEC regular season with a 4-20 conference record — walked into the second round of the SEC Softball Tournament and beat Texas A&M 11-8 with five home runs. The final run came courtesy of freshman Ava Ratliff, who stepped to the plate in the top of the seventh inning with two outs, the game tied at 8-8, and delivered a three-run home run that ended the game.

This is not a minor upset. Texas A&M is a program with real NCAA Tournament hopes, and their ability to host a Regional was on the line in Wednesday's game. Auburn, by contrast, was fighting just to get back to .500 and make a case for an at-large bid. The result was a five-home run clinic that the Aggies simply could not answer.

Five HRs and a Walk-Off

Auburn's power display on Wednesday was total. McKaela Walker got things started. AnnaLea Adams followed. Ma'Nia Womack and Haven Roebuck added to the total, and Auburn built what appeared to be a safe cushion. Then Texas A&M answered, rattling off runs in the fifth and sixth innings to pull even at 8-8 and make the game feel entirely alive again.

That is when Ratliff stepped in. The freshman — not a household name coming into the tournament — put all three pitches she saw in play, and the one that mattered most left the park. A two-out, three-run home run in the seventh to put Auburn ahead 11-8 and close the book on the Aggies' tournament run. It is the kind of moment that defines a young player's career before it has really started.

A&M's Hosting Hopes Take a Hit

For Texas A&M, this loss arrives at the worst possible time. The NCAA Tournament selection committee will release the bracket in about two weeks, and where teams finish in conference play — combined with results at their conference tournament — influences whether they host a Regional or travel. A second-round loss to a team that went 4-20 in conference play does not help A&M's hosting case.

The Aggies still have a strong regular-season résumé, but there is no way to frame this result as a neutral outcome. They needed a run. They did not get it. The committee will weigh all of this when the bracket comes out.

Auburn's Unlikely Run Continues

Auburn advances to face No. 3 Florida in the SEC quarterfinals. Florida is one of the top four seeds in the tournament and is a legitimate WCWS contender. By any reasonable measure, the Tigers should not be in this conversation. But the numbers on the scoreboard Wednesday said otherwise — 11 runs, five home runs, and a walk-off from a freshman who made it look easy. In single-elimination tournament softball, that is all that counts.
