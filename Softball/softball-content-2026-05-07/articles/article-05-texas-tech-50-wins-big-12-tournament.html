Texas Tech Reaches 50 Wins — First D-I Team to the Milestone in 2026

By Emily Rawlings May 7, 2026

Fifty Wins and Counting

Texas Tech finished the 2026 regular season with a 50-5 record — the first program in Division I softball to reach the 50-win threshold this year. That number alone is a statement. Fifty wins means you beat good teams repeatedly, handled the games you were supposed to win, and performed when the stakes were real. The Red Raiders have done all three, and they did it against the Big 12's best competition while ranked in the top four nationally all season.

The Red Raiders also claimed the Big 12 Regular Season Championship for the second consecutive year, finishing 21-3 in conference play. They are the No. 3 team in the NFCA Coaches Poll, entering the Big 12 Tournament as the No. 1 seed and the defending tournament champions. There is very little about this program's current arc that does not point toward a serious NCAA Tournament run.

Canady's Perfect Game

The exclamation point on Texas Tech's regular season came on May 2 — Senior Day. Ace pitcher Canady threw a perfect game, retiring all 21 hitters she faced and giving the program's senior class a send-off they will not forget. A perfect game is one of the rarest individual achievements in softball. Doing it in front of your home crowd on Senior Day, while your team is ranked in the top four nationally, is about as good as it gets.

Canady's performance is also a signal about where Texas Tech's pitching stands heading into the postseason. An ace who can throw a perfect game in early May is an ace who has the stuff and the command to win in Oklahoma City. The Red Raiders' pitching is not a weakness — it is their competitive edge.

The Big 12 Tournament Picture

Texas Tech opens the 2026 Big 12 Softball Tournament today at Devon Park in Oklahoma City — the home of the NCAA Women's College World Series — facing No. 8 Baylor at 1:30 PM CT. There is some poetry in a team with WCWS aspirations opening their tournament run at the same venue where those dreams would be realized. Whether the Red Raiders take advantage of that home-venue comfort is one of the storylines to watch this week.

As the defending tournament champions, Texas Tech enters with the experience of having won this bracket before. The pressure on a defending champion can be real, but so can the confidence that comes from knowing what it takes. The Red Raiders have both in abundance.

The Case for Texas Tech in Oklahoma City

When the NCAA Tournament bracket comes out, Texas Tech will almost certainly be among the top national seeds. Their combination of a 50-5 record, back-to-back conference titles, No. 3 national ranking, and elite pitching puts them in a short list of programs that can realistically win the Women's College World Series. The next two weeks will tell us a great deal about whether the Red Raiders are ready to make that final step.
