Nebraska's 18-Game Win Streak Rolls Into the Big Ten Tournament

By Sarah Mitchell May 7, 2026

The Huskers Are Quietly Having a Historic Season

While the SEC has dominated the softball conversation this spring, a program in Lincoln has been quietly building one of the most impressive seasons in college softball. Nebraska enters the 2026 Big Ten Tournament with a 43-6 record, an 18-game win streak, the program's first-ever Big Ten Regular Season Championship, and the No. 2 national ranking in the NFCA Top 25. That is not a program that sneaks up on anyone. That is a genuine contender.

The 18-game win streak is the longest active in Division I — and the second-longest in Huskers program history. Nebraska closed the regular season by sweeping Penn State in a doubleheader to clinch the Big Ten title outright, finishing 23-1 in conference play and tying the Big Ten record for most conference wins in a single season. By any measure, this is a historically good Nebraska team.

Built for the Postseason

What makes Nebraska's case compelling heading into the postseason is that the win streak is not a product of a soft schedule. The Huskers have played and beaten real opponents consistently. Their 43-6 record through the regular season places them among the handful of programs nationally with legitimate WCWS aspirations. Nebraska is not hoping to make noise at Oklahoma City — they are building a résumé that puts them in the conversation as a potential finalist.

The Huskers enter the Big Ten Tournament as the No. 1 overall seed and have earned a first-round bye. They open Thursday's quarterfinals against the winner of the Ohio State-Michigan first-round game. On paper, this is a bracket they should be able to navigate. But they know that no game is a given once you get to single elimination.

The National Picture

Nebraska at No. 2 nationally is not a statistical accident. The NFCA coaches who voted them there have watched this team all season and recognize what the numbers reflect: a program that is consistently dominant, well-coached, and playing its best softball heading into May. That is exactly what you want in a tournament team.

The question the softball world is starting to ask is whether Nebraska can match up with the top SEC programs if they meet in Oklahoma City. That answer will not come this week — but a Big Ten Tournament championship would be a compelling data point in the argument that the Huskers belong in that conversation.

What's Next

Nebraska opens Big Ten Tournament quarterfinal play today, with games getting underway in Bloomington. A Huskers tournament title this week would set them up as one of the top national seeds when the NCAA Tournament bracket is announced. For a program claiming its first-ever Big Ten title this spring, there would be no better way to confirm what this season has been about.
