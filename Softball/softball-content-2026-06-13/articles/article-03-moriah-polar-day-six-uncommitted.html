Moriah Polar: The Portal's Overlooked Gem Is Still Available on Day 6

By Jordan Reeves

June 13, 2026

Isa Torres dominates the headlines. Jaysoni Beachum gets the second-loudest buzz. And somewhere in the background, Moriah Polar — the nation's leading hitter in 2026 — quietly enters Day 6 of the transfer portal window still uncommitted.

That may be the most underreported story in this entire portal cycle.

The Numbers Demand Attention

Polar hit .555 with 101 hits in the 2026 season for Purdue — the best single-season batting average in the Big Ten and among the top marks nationally. Over three seasons with the Boilermakers, she accumulated 218 hits, 103 RBIs, 137 runs scored, and went 81-of-94 in stolen base attempts for an 86 percent success rate. Her career batting average stands at .436.

On3 ranks her No. 5 overall in the transfer portal at a 97.00 rating. She earned Second-Team All-American recognition for her 2026 campaign. She has one year of eligibility remaining.

Contact rate, speed, on-base ability, baserunning IQ — Polar brings all of it. Any program landing her instantly upgrades the top of its lineup.

Why She Gets Less Attention

Part of Polar's lower profile in this portal cycle is circumstantial. She came out of Purdue, a Big Ten program that does not command the same recruiting spotlight as FSU, Texas, or the SEC. Torres and Beachum both came from Florida State — a program in the national conversation every year — and both were named All-Americans at a school where WCWS runs are expected.

Polar was doing equally dominant work in a conference that doesn't always get its due in national softball coverage. Her .555 average should have generated more noise, and it still might once her commitment lands.

The Right Fit Matters

At 81-of-94 stolen bases, Polar is a speed weapon who needs to play in a system that deploys her correctly. A lineup that bats her first or second, with license to run and put pressure on defenses, will extract her full value. A passive offensive system that stacks power hitters and limits running games would waste her best skill.

That fit question may explain some of the patience. Polar is not waiting for a bigger offer — she is waiting for the right situation.

What's Next

Nine days remain in the portal window. Polar is a top-5 player available right now, and the programs that recognize the gap between her profile and her attention level have a real opportunity. When she finally commits, it will almost certainly be to a program that made her feel like the priority — not an afterthought to the Torres and Beachum sweepstakes. The wait, for the right school, will be worth it.
