Torres Watch: Day 6, Nine Days Left and No Decision

By Sarah Mitchell

June 13, 2026

Six days. No commitment. The most anticipated transfer portal decision in college softball history continues to unfold in slow motion.

Isa Torres — D1 Softball Player of the Year, ACC Player of the Year, and the most statistically dominant offensive player in Florida State history — enters Day 6 of the 15-day transfer portal window without a commitment. The window closes June 22. Nine days remain.

The Numbers Behind the Wait

It is worth revisiting what makes Torres the most coveted player in this portal class. In 2026, she slashed .530/.591/.978 with 78 runs scored — breaking every offensive record FSU had ever recorded. She set an NCAA record by recording a hit in 16 consecutive at-bats. The ACC honored her as conference Player of the Year. D1Softball named her the national Player of the Year.

She has one year of eligibility remaining. Wherever she lands, that program immediately becomes a national title contender.

Texas vs. Texas A&M

The race has narrowed to two programs. Texas and Texas A&M have both been identified as the frontrunners since Torres entered the portal. The angles each school can make are compelling and distinct.

Texas is the two-time defending national champion. Head coach Mike White has built a dynasty around elite transfers and developed talent — Teagan Kavan, Katie Stewart, and Kayden Henry all return as seniors for the 2027 three-peat chase. Two days ago, Torres's hometown neighbor Samantha Swan officially signed with the Longhorns, giving Texas a catcher who grew up 30 minutes from the UT Austin campus in Georgetown, Texas — the same town Torres calls home.

Texas A&M offers a different pitch: be the centerpiece, not the complement. The Aggies have championship ambitions of their own and the resources to compete. Bringing in Torres would redefine what A&M softball is capable of.

The Georgetown Connection

The geographic detail is not trivial. Torres grew up in Georgetown, Texas. So did Swan. The two players share a hometown, and Swan's decision to return home and play for Texas happened just as Torres is making her own decision.

Whether that connection influences Torres is unknown. But the idea of two Georgetown products anchoring the middle of Texas's lineup — Swan behind the plate, Torres at shortstop — would be one of the most compelling storylines in college softball heading into 2027.

What's Next

Every day Torres remains uncommitted becomes a story unto itself. The portal window gives players until June 22 to decide, and Torres has shown no urgency to end the waiting game. With Texas and Texas A&M both at the table, the decision when it comes will be one of the most scrutinized announcements in softball recruiting history. Nine days remain. The softball world is watching.
