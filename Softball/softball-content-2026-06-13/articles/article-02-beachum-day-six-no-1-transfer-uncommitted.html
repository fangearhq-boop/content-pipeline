Beachum Watch: No. 1 Transfer Still Uncommitted on Day 6

By Emily Rawlings

June 13, 2026

Jaysoni Beachum is in no rush. Six days into the 15-day transfer portal window, the nation's No. 1-ranked transfer by Softball America has not made a commitment. The window closes June 22. She has nine days to decide — or wait until the very end if she chooses.

That patience tells a story. When you are the most coveted transfer in college softball, you do not have to act quickly. Every program comes to you.

What Beachum Brings

The case for Beachum as the most impactful transfer in this class is straightforward. In 2026, the Florida State third baseman slashed .417/.549/.654 with 16 home runs, 66 RBIs, and 54 walks. Over three seasons in Tallahassee, she accumulated 37 home runs, 180 RBIs, 202 hits, and a .375 career batting average.

Those career totals arrived by age 21. Beachum was NFCA Division I Freshman of the Year in 2024 — the first Florida State player and first ACC athlete to win the award. She earned Second-Team All-American honors in 2026. At third base, she also brings defensive versatility that gives any coaching staff options.

Softball America's ranking is not hyperbole. Beachum is the most complete transfer available in this cycle.

The Leverage Game

There is a strategy to waiting. The longer Beachum stays uncommitted, the more programs remain actively engaged. Coaches who might have redirected recruiting resources to other targets continue to make calls, arrange visits, and build offers. Beachum sees every hand before she has to show hers.

It is worth noting she entered the portal on June 2 — before the official 15-day undergraduate window opened on June 8. She has been in the system longer than most players in this class. Whatever she has seen over the last 11 days has not yet produced the right fit or the right offer at the right time.

Who Wins This Race?

No program has been publicly identified as a clear frontrunner for Beachum, unlike the Texas-versus-A&M narrative that defines the Torres pursuit. That ambiguity may be by design. Beachum or her inner circle may be deliberately keeping the field wide open to maximize options.

What is certain: every power program in the country is paying attention. The SEC, Big 12, ACC, and Pac-12 all have programs that need her bat, her glove, and her experience. One of them will win this race.

What's Next

With nine days left in the portal window, Beachum remains the most impactful uncommitted player in college softball. Her decision — when it comes — will immediately reshape the national title conversation for 2027. Programs should continue recruiting hard until the moment she announces, because Beachum has shown she will take every day the window gives her to find the right home.
