Portal Midpoint: Arkansas and Texas A&M Fill Roster Holes While the Headlines Wait

By Sarah Mitchell

June 13, 2026

The 2026 transfer portal window is at its midpoint, and the programs not waiting for blue-chip commitments are getting to work. Arkansas and Texas A&M are among the programs making meaningful additions as the window reaches its halfway point, adding pieces to rosters while simultaneously staying in the hunt for the portal's top names.

Alabama Already Set the Standard

The most notable mid-window commitment happened on June 10, when Tennessee LHP Kailey Plumlee committed to Alabama. Plumlee — a redshirt freshman with four years of eligibility remaining — gives the Crimson Tide their first left-handed pitcher since Lauren Esman in 2024. That addition lands on a staff that ranked second nationally in ERA in 2026, anchored by Jocelyn Briski.

Alabama did not wait for the window to generate a marquee announcement. They identified a need, found the player who fit, and closed the deal at the midpoint. It is a template other programs are following.

The Depth Market Is Real

Behind the headlines of Torres, Beachum, and Polar, programs are quietly addressing depth. Arkansas has been among the most active, adding to a roster that needs to replace key contributors after a postseason run in 2026. Texas A&M, simultaneously pursuing Torres with the full weight of its recruiting staff, is also adding supporting pieces.

The Aggies understand that landing Torres — if they do — is not the end of the roster build. She needs teammates who can execute around her. Programs that plan for both scenarios — landing the star and not landing the star — will be the ones with complete, competitive rosters in 2027.

The Middle of the Portal Class

Not every portal success story involves a top-5 player. The depth of this window has been notable — multiple second-tier transfers with legitimate SEC and Big 12 starting experience are available, and programs picking them up are making less-publicized but equally important additions.

Pitching depth, backup catching, outfield versatility — the programs addressing those needs now will not be scrambling in August when fall practice starts.

What's Next

Nine days remain in the window. The second half of any portal window tends to produce the biggest commitments — players who take the full period to evaluate options often end up making the most consequential decisions. Arkansas and Texas A&M will keep working the depth market while holding spots for the top targets. The programs willing to do both simultaneously will be the ones lifting trophies in Oklahoma City in June 2027.
