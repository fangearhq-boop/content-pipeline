Portal Countdown: Nine Days Left, Three Elite Players Still Up for Grabs

By Emily Rawlings

June 13, 2026

Day 6 of the 15-day transfer portal window is here. The window opened June 8 and closes June 22. Here is exactly where things stand at the halfway point, sorted by what has happened and what has not.

The Signed Column

Three notable commitments are already in the books. Samantha Swan — First-Team All-Big 12 catcher from Arizona State, .383 BA / 12 HR / 55 RBI — signed with Texas on June 10. She fills the Longhorns' most urgent positional need after Reese Atwood's graduation and adds a hometown player to a program already targeting Georgetown native Isa Torres.

Kailey Plumlee — Tennessee LHP with four years of eligibility — committed to Alabama on June 10 as well, joining Jocelyn Briski on one of the country's most dominant pitching staffs. Former Arizona utility player Kaiah Altmeyer also joined the Longhorns, giving Texas another portal addition to supplement Swan.

The Available Column

Three players with elite profiles remain uncommitted as of Day 6, and their names have defined this portal cycle from the beginning.

Isa Torres — D1 Softball Player of the Year, .530 BA / .978 SLG / .591 OBP — is the most coveted player in this class. Texas and Texas A&M are the reported frontrunners. Nine days remain.

Jaysoni Beachum — No. 1 overall transfer by Softball America, .417 BA / 16 HR / 66 RBI in 2026 — has been in the portal since June 2 and has not committed to anyone. Her patience is calculated. Whoever gets her wins this portal cycle outright.

Moriah Polar — On3 No. 5 overall, .555 BA / 101 hits in 2026, career .436 BA at Purdue — is the overlooked gem. Her stolen base efficiency (81-of-94) and contact ability make her a complete player who elevates any lineup.

The Stakes of the Final Nine Days

This portal window was always going to be defined by those three names. Whoever lands Torres gets the D1 Player of the Year and a Georgetown, Texas native who could help Texas build a three-peat or A&M build a dynasty. Whoever lands Beachum gets the No. 1 power bat in the class. Whoever lands Polar gets the best pure hitter available.

Any program landing two of the three becomes an immediate national title threat. Landing all three would be the most dominant single-cycle recruiting haul in recent memory.

What's Next

Portal windows rarely produce their biggest announcements in the first half. The final days — and especially the final 48 to 72 hours — tend to produce the commitments that define the cycle. With nine days remaining and three elite players still available, the most important decisions in this portal class are still ahead. Check back daily.
