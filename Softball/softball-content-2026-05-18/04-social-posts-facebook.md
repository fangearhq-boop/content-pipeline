### STORY 2: Alabama's NCAA-Record 20th Super Regional — Vic Moten Near-Perfect

#### Long-Form Post
Twenty Super Regionals. No other program in NCAA softball history has made 20.

Alabama got there Sunday with a 9-0 win over USC Upstate in Tuscaloosa, and the performance itself was worthy of the milestone. Pitcher Vic Moten was perfect through four-plus innings and didn't allow a hit until the sixth. The offense backed her with four home runs. It was the kind of complete, dominant performance that No. 1 seeds are supposed to deliver.

What makes this even more notable is what Alabama did not have to use: ace Jocelyn Briski. The SEC Pitcher of the Year never threw a pitch in the regional. Alabama won three games by a combined 20-0 and saved their best arm for the run ahead.

Up next is No. 16 seed LSU, who swept their own regional at 23-2. The game is Friday, May 22 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. On paper this is a mismatch. But LSU has been the most dominant team in regionals aside from the Tide themselves — so Friday should be very interesting.

History has been made. Now let's see if Alabama can win a championship to go along with it.

### STORY 5: No. 10 Georgia Earns 4th Straight Super Regional — Fisher Blanks Clemson 5-0

#### Long-Form Post
Being seeded 10th nationally means you're not supposed to be here. Georgia didn't get the memo.

For the fourth consecutive year, the Bulldogs are heading to the NCAA Super Regionals. Sunday's 5-0 win over Clemson in the Athens Regional Final was the latest chapter in a stretch of consistent postseason excellence that has become the expectation for this program.

Sophomore Addisen Fisher was the story. She threw a complete-game shutout, allowing just two hits, two walks, and striking out three Clemson hitters. On the biggest game of the regional weekend in Athens, she looked entirely in control.

Senior Marisa Miller provided the offense, launching her third home run of the season in the fourth inning to put Georgia ahead. Two more runs in the fifth inning put the game away.

Now Georgia faces No. 3 seed Tennessee in the Super Regionals, beginning Thursday, May 21 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. It's the kind of matchup that looked impossible when the regional bracket was announced. But that's what four straight Super Regionals will do for a program's confidence.

The Bulldogs are not here by accident.
