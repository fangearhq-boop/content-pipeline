LSU Sweeps Baton Rouge Regional 23-2, Faces No. 1 Alabama in Super Regionals

By Jordan Reeves | May 18, 2026

Nobody assigned LSU the No. 16 national seed expecting them to cruise through a regional. But that is exactly what happened. The Tigers swept the Baton Rouge Regional with a 7-2 win over Virginia Tech in Sunday's final, and when you add up the three-game scoring margin, it's a staggering number: LSU 23, opponents 2.

Jalia Lassiter's solo home run in the third inning lit the fuse Sunday. Maci Bergeron followed with a go-ahead two-run single in the fourth. The offense kept rolling. Virginia Tech, which had battled all weekend to earn a spot in the final, never had an answer for what LSU was doing at the plate. The Tigers are regional champions and headed to Tuscaloosa for their 11th Super Regional in program history.

How LSU Got Here

The Tigers opened the Baton Rouge Regional by blanking Akron 8-0, with pitcher Nat Heavener striking out 11. On Saturday, Cece Cellura threw the first complete-game shutout of her career in an 8-0 win over Virginia Tech, the most dominant single-game performance of the regional weekend at any site. Sunday's 7-2 final over VT sealed the sweep.

Across three games, LSU allowed two runs. Total. Their opponents didn't score until the fourth inning of the regional final. For a program seeded 16th nationally entering the tournament, the performance was a clear statement that the seed number did not reflect where this team actually stands in May.

The Alabama Challenge

Drawing No. 1 Alabama as a Super Regional opponent is exactly as difficult as it sounds. The Crimson Tide swept the Tuscaloosa Regional without allowing a run, won their three games by a combined 20-0, and rested ace Jocelyn Briski the entire weekend. Alabama is 49-7, owns an ERA well below 1.50, and will be hosting in front of one of the loudest crowds in college softball.

But here is the thing LSU can point to: they were also untouchable in their regional. They outscored three opponents 23-2. Their pitching staff delivered back-to-back shutouts through Saturday. The offense produced when it needed to. A team that plays the way LSU has played in this regional is not going to Tuscaloosa just to lose quietly. Alabama is the favorite, and a significant one. But this is a three-game series, not a prediction.

What's Next

LSU travels to Tuscaloosa to face No. 1 Alabama in the Super Regionals, beginning Friday, May 22 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. Game 2 is Saturday, May 23 at 3 PM ET on ESPN. A deciding third game, if necessary, would follow. The winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. LSU is the No. 16 seed in this matchup and considered a heavy underdog. Their regional resume suggests they won't see it that way.
