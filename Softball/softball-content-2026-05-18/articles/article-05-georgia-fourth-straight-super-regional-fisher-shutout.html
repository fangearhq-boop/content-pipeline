Fisher's Shutout Sends No. 10 Georgia to Fourth Consecutive Super Regional

By Emily Rawlings | May 18, 2026

Four consecutive Super Regionals. The number does not happen by accident. It takes pitching, offensive production, a coaching staff that wins in May, and a group of players who show up when the moment is the biggest. Georgia checked every box Sunday, using Addisen Fisher's complete-game shutout to beat Clemson 5-0 in the Athens Regional Final and book another Super Regional berth.

Fisher, a sophomore, allowed just two hits and two walks while striking out three Clemson hitters in a performance that never felt close. Senior Marisa Miller's home run in the fourth inning, her third of the season, got the scoring started. Georgia added two more runs in the fifth and never needed more. Final score: 5-0. The Athens Regional belongs to the Bulldogs.

Fisher's Moment

Addisen Fisher is a name that not enough people outside Georgia know yet. A sophomore throwing in a regional final, on the biggest stage of her career so far, she went nine innings and gave Clemson almost nothing to work with. Two hits. Two walks. The Clemson hitters who had gotten to pitchers throughout the regional weekend found Fisher to be a different kind of challenge.

The complete game mattered not just for the performance, but for what it means going forward. Georgia travels to Knoxville to face No. 3 Tennessee beginning Thursday. They will need Fisher pitching with that level of confidence. She showed Sunday in Athens that she can deliver it on the biggest day of the year.

The 10-Seed That Keeps Winning

Georgia arrived at this regional as the No. 10 national seed, which comes with a certain expectation of difficulty. Programs seeded 10th are supposed to scratch and fight for every run, hoping for favorable brackets and hot days from their aces. Georgia did something different. They swept through the Athens Regional with a shutout final and made it look practiced, not improvised.

This is what sustained program excellence looks like. Four consecutive Super Regionals is not a fluke. It is evidence of a coaching staff and player development pipeline that produces tournament performers. For Georgia fans watching Sunday's game, the win was validating. For the rest of the country paying attention, it was a reminder that this program has earned its place at the table regardless of seed number.

What's Next

Georgia faces No. 3 Tennessee in a Super Regional beginning Thursday, May 21 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 in Knoxville. Tennessee is the higher seed and hosts, but Georgia's four-year streak of making this stage suggests the Bulldogs are comfortable with the pressure. The series winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, starting May 28. Fisher is the name to watch when Georgia takes the circle in Knoxville.
