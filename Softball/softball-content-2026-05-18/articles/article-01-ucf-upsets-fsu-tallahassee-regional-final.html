UCF Knocks Off No. 9 Florida State 4-2, Claims Tallahassee Regional

By Sarah Mitchell | May 18, 2026

In the history of UCF softball's NCAA Tournament appearances, the Knights had never beaten Florida State. Not once. Sunday afternoon at Joanne Graf Field in Tallahassee, they did it on the biggest possible stage, beating No. 9 FSU 4-2 in the winner-take-all Tallahassee Regional Final to claim the regional title and punch a Super Regional ticket.

Two two-run home runs powered UCF's offense. Florida State never led in the game. The Seminoles, who entered the weekend as the No. 9 national seed with a 49-9 record and a program that had hosted 12 consecutive NCAA Regionals, had their season ended by a team playing in their own ballpark. Final score: UCF 4, FSU 2. UCF won the Tallahassee Regional without losing a game.

An Unbeaten Bracket Run

UCF's path through Tallahassee was convincing from the start. The Knights beat Jacksonville State in the opener, then absolutely dominated Stetson 10-1 on Saturday to advance to the regional final through the winners' bracket. All while FSU was fighting its way back through the elimination side after losing to Stetson on Friday.

By the time Sunday's championship game arrived, UCF had already beaten three opponents and showed no signs of fatigue or nerves. The Knights came out aggressive, hit the ball hard, and let their home-run power make the statement. Against the host team, on the host team's field, UCF played like the team that belonged there.

FSU's Season Ends in Tallahassee

For Florida State, the weekend was the rare tournament that didn't end with a trip north. The Seminoles finished 49-9 after what had been a strong season by any reasonable standard. They won 20 ACC Tournament championships and went 21-3 in conference play. A No. 9 national seed with that record entering the postseason deserved to be taken seriously.

But the 2026 Tallahassee Regional didn't go to script. The Stetson loss on Day 1 forced FSU into the loser's bracket, and while they fought back with a Saturday doubleheader sweep, Sunday's final against UCF was a step too far. The Knights had momentum, confidence, and the power to win. The 12-year streak of regional hosting ended alongside the season.

UCF Heads to the Super Regionals

UCF's reward is a Super Regional matchup against No. 8 seed UCLA, with Game 1 scheduled for Friday, May 22 at 9 PM ET on ESPNU and Game 2 on Saturday, May 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN. It will be just the second Super Regional appearance in program history, the first since 2024. But given what just happened in Tallahassee, UCF isn't traveling to Los Angeles just to make up the numbers.

The Knights beat a team that was never supposed to lose to them. They did it unbeaten, on the road, and with two home runs in the decisive game. UCLA made their third consecutive Super Regional this year and will be the heavy favorite. But UCF has now proven in the span of one tournament weekend that they can beat programs with bigger names and better seeds.

What's Next

UCF faces No. 8 UCLA beginning Friday, May 22 at 9 PM ET on ESPNU in a best-of-three Super Regional series. The winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, which begins May 28. For FSU, the offseason begins now, with 49 wins serving as a reminder that the program remains one of the country's best even after a painful ending to a strong season.
