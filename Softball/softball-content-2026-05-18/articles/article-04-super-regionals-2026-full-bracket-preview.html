2026 NCAA Softball Super Regionals: Full Bracket, Schedule, and Storylines

By Sarah Mitchell | May 18, 2026

Regional weekend is finished. The chalk held in some places, broke in others, and the result is a Super Regional field that has both dynasty matchups and genuine upset candidates. Eight best-of-three series run Thursday through Sunday, May 21-24. The eight winners head to Devon Park in Oklahoma City for the Women's College World Series, which begins May 28.

Here is every matchup, when it starts, and what to watch for.

The Full Super Regionals Bracket

No. 1 Alabama vs. No. 16 LSU — Tuscaloosa | Game 1: Friday, May 22, 7 PM ET (ESPN2). Alabama won the Tuscaloosa Regional going 3-0, outscoring opponents 20-0. LSU won the Baton Rouge Regional going 3-0, outscoring opponents 23-2. The seed difference is the largest in the bracket. Ace Jocelyn Briski hasn't thrown a pitch in three games. This is the marquee upset candidate of the round.

No. 3 Oklahoma vs. Mississippi State — Norman | Game 1: Friday, May 22, 1 PM ET (ESPN2). Oklahoma won their regional comfortably, beating Michigan 8-1 in Sunday's final. Mississippi State swept the Eugene Regional, including a walk-off win Friday and a Saint Mary's finish Sunday. Patty Gasso's squad is the heavy favorite, but Mississippi State comes in hot.

No. 2 Texas vs. Arizona State — Austin. The defending national champions advanced to their seventh consecutive Super Regional. Texas has been the standard of consistency in this sport. Arizona State earned their spot in the bracket and gets a road test against the toughest possible opponent.

No. 4 Nebraska vs. No. 13 Oklahoma State — Lincoln | Game 1: Thursday, May 21, 9 PM ET (ESPN2). Nebraska won the Lincoln Regional on a Hannah Camenzind solo home run in a 1-0 thriller over Grand Canyon. They'll host Oklahoma State in a battle that opens the Super Regional slate Thursday night.

No. 5 Arkansas vs. No. 12 Duke — Fayetteville | Game 1: Friday, May 22, 12 PM ET (ESPNU). Duke punched their Super Regional ticket after winning their regional. They travel to Arkansas, which has been one of the most consistent programs in the country over the past several years.

No. 3 Tennessee vs. No. 10 Georgia — Knoxville | Game 1: Thursday, May 21, 7 PM ET (ESPN2). Georgia earned their 4th consecutive Super Regional after Addisen Fisher threw a complete-game shutout to beat Clemson 5-0 in Athens. Tennessee is the higher seed and will host, but Georgia has made a habit of performing at this stage.

No. 8 UCLA vs. UCF — Los Angeles | Game 1: Friday, May 22, 9 PM ET (ESPNU). UCF just beat No. 9 Florida State on FSU's own field in Tallahassee to earn their Super Regional spot. UCLA made their third straight Super Regional. The Cinderella bracket run collides with a program that has been here before.

Florida vs. Texas Tech — Gainesville | Game 1: Friday, May 22, 11 AM ET (ESPN2). Two solid programs with plenty of postseason experience. Florida hosts and will be the favorite. Texas Tech made the Super Regionals for the second straight year.

Key Storylines to Watch

The Biggest Upset Candidate: LSU at Alabama. No. 16 vs. No. 1. The most asymmetric matchup in the bracket on paper. LSU swept their regional by 23-2 in scoring. Alabama rested Briski. If LSU can stay within a run through four innings of Game 1, the atmosphere in Tuscaloosa will become something else entirely.

UCF's Continued Run. The Knights beat FSU in their own stadium. They're an unbeaten regional champion. UCLA is a legitimate challenge, but UCF has already done the thing no one expected. Watch them closely.

Texas Staying on Top. Seven consecutive Super Regionals for the defending champions. Arizona State is not a pushover, but Texas at home has been nearly unstoppable in postseason play.

Georgia-Tennessee's SEC Flavor. Two SEC programs, No. 3 vs. No. 10. Georgia's Fisher is a name to watch after her shutout of Clemson. Tennessee hosts and has the better seed, but Georgia being at the Super Regional stage for the fourth straight year means this program knows how to win in May.

What's Next

Super Regional play begins Thursday, May 21 with Nebraska-Oklahoma State at 9 PM ET and Tennessee-Georgia at 7 PM ET, both on ESPN2. The full slate runs through Sunday, May 24 if third games are needed. All eight series winners advance to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, beginning May 28. Set your calendar for Thursday night. The WCWS field starts getting built this week.
