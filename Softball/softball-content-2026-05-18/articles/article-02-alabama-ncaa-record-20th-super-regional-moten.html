Alabama Sets NCAA Record with 20th Super Regional — Moten's Near-Perfect Game

By Emily Rawlings | May 18, 2026

Numbers tell the story of Alabama softball's place in history: 20 Super Regionals. No other program has reached that number. The Crimson Tide got there Sunday with a 9-0 win over USC Upstate in the Tuscaloosa Regional Final, and the performance itself was built to fit the milestone. Pitcher Vic Moten was perfect through four-plus innings. She didn't allow a hit until the sixth. Alabama's lineup produced four home runs. The No. 1 seed looked every bit like the team expected to win the whole thing.

Per the Alabama Athletics release, this is the program's 22nd all-time regional title and their 20th Super Regional appearance under the current postseason format. No program in NCAA softball history has made 20. Alabama just became the first.

Moten Carries the Load

Vic Moten came into the Tuscaloosa Regional as the No. 2 option behind ace Jocelyn Briski, who had dominated the conference season and won the SEC Pitcher of the Year. Coach Patrick Murphy held Briski in reserve all weekend and handed the regional to his supporting staff. Moten made that decision look easy.

Against USC Upstate on Sunday, Moten was perfect through the first four-plus innings and carried a no-hitter through five. The final box score read: one hit, zero runs, the kind of line that validates the depth Alabama's pitching staff has developed. When your second-best pitcher is dealing near-perfect games in regional finals, your No. 1 is rested and ready for the bigger rounds ahead.

The Offense Did Its Part

Four home runs in a 9-0 win against USC Upstate is not a particularly close game. But for Alabama's offense, regional weekend was about staying sharp and building momentum, not just winning. The Crimson Tide have the kind of lineup that can put up crooked numbers against anyone on a given day, and Sunday confirmed they came out of the regional healthy and hot.

Alabama didn't allow a single run across the entire Tuscaloosa Regional, winning their three games by a combined 20-0. They outscored opponents at every level. Briski never threw a pitch. The depth carried the load. That is what a No. 1 seed is supposed to look like heading into Super Regional weekend.

Next: No. 16 LSU in Tuscaloosa

The Super Regional matchup is set: No. 1 Alabama hosting No. 16 LSU, beginning Friday, May 22 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. The LSU Tigers swept the Baton Rouge Regional without a loss, outscoring their opponents 23-2 over three games. They're the No. 16 seed, but nothing about how they played in Baton Rouge suggested they intend to roll over in Tuscaloosa.

Alabama is the clear favorite. The seed differential is as large as it gets in the Super Regionals. But LSU carries the momentum of an unbeaten regional, a freshly dominant pitching performance from Cece Cellura, and an offense that has been quietly putting up runs all weekend. The combination makes for a first game that will be worth watching very closely.

What's Next

Alabama hosts No. 16 LSU beginning Friday, May 22 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2, with Game 2 on Saturday, May 23 at 3 PM ET on ESPN. The winner advances to the Women's College World Series at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, which opens May 28. For Alabama, this is the moment the program has been building toward all season: a record 20th Super Regional, an ace in reserve, and a home crowd ready to push them to the WCWS.
