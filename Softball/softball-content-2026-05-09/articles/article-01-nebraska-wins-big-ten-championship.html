Nebraska Claims 2026 Big Ten Softball Championship, Win Streak Reaches 21

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | May 9, 2026

The Huskers defeated UCLA to capture the Big Ten title, extending the nation's longest active winning streak and making a thunderous statement ahead of Sunday's Selection Show.

Nebraska is your 2026 Big Ten Softball Champion.

The Huskers defeated UCLA in the conference championship game Saturday at Maryland Softball Stadium in College Park, earning the tournament title and pushing their winning streak to 21 games — the longest active run in all of Division I softball.

It was the perfect capstone to one of the most dominant stretches any college softball program has put together this season. Nebraska entered the Big Ten Tournament as the No. 1 seed with a 44-6 record, rode the brilliance of ace pitcher Alexis Jensen all week, and left with the hardware.

Jensen Has Been Untouchable All Week

No player defined Nebraska's tournament run more than Jensen. In Friday's semifinal against Indiana, she was simply untouchable — throwing a complete-game shutout with 12 strikeouts. The win improved her record to 22-2, and it came in the most high-pressure moment of the Huskers' season.

Jensen's combination of velocity, movement, and composure makes her one of the elite pitchers in the country. When Nebraska needs a big game on the biggest stage, she delivers. That's the kind of reliability that wins championship weeks.

A Historic Win Streak

Twenty-one wins in a row. That's how Nebraska has finished the regular season and navigated the Big Ten Tournament. No team in D-I softball is playing better right now.

The streak stretches back weeks, encompassing a dominant run through the end of the Big Ten regular season, a conference title, and now the postseason tournament crown. Nebraska has not lost since before many softball fans started paying attention to the national seed race.

Compare that to the competition: Oklahoma — the longtime frontrunner for the No. 1 overall seed — was eliminated in the SEC Tournament quarterfinals last week by No. 9 seed Georgia. That stunning result opened the door for programs like Nebraska to stake their claim as the country's best team, and the Huskers have walked right through it.

A National Seed Résumé Like No Other

Nebraska's case for a top national seed is compelling by any measure. The Huskers won the Big Ten regular season title, claimed the conference tournament championship, play elite pitching, and have beaten teams across the country's toughest non-conference schedules.

Coming into the tournament, Nebraska held a record north of 44-6. After winning three more games this week — including the championship — the Huskers' final record heading into NCAA play is approximately 47-6, the kind of résumé that demands a hosting assignment and a spot among the top four national seeds.

The Selection Show airs tomorrow night — Sunday, May 10 — at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. When the committee reveals the bracket, Nebraska expects to be near the top of the board.

What's Next

Nebraska's next step is the NCAA Tournament. The Huskers will almost certainly host a regional (May 15-18) and potentially a super regional (May 22-24) before any potential trip to the Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City (May 28-June 3).

The path to the WCWS runs through teams like Alabama, Texas, Texas Tech, and Florida State — all of whom are vying for top seeds as well. But right now, on Championship Saturday, Nebraska has done everything right.

The 21-game win streak is a statement. The Big Ten championship is a statement. And the Huskers are ready to make one more when the NCAA Tournament begins.
