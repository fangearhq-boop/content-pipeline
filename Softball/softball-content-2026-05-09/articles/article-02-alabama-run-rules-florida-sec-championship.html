Alabama Run-Rules Florida 9-1, Reaches SEC Championship vs. Texas

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | May 9, 2026

Vic Moten was dominant and Ambrey Taylor went deep as the Crimson Tide cruised to a five-inning run-rule victory Friday, earning a date with Texas in today's SEC Tournament final.

Alabama came to Lexington with a purpose, and on Friday afternoon at John Cropp Stadium, the Crimson Tide made it clear they intend to leave as SEC Tournament champions.

Alabama run-ruled Florida 9-1 in five innings in the SEC Tournament semifinals, the kind of dominant performance that silences doubts and sends a message to everyone watching. Now 49-6 on the season, the Tide advance to the SEC Championship game against Texas — who edged Georgia 5-4 in the other semifinal — with a chance at their first conference tournament title since 2021.

Moten Delivers in the Big Moment

The story in the circle was freshman pitcher Vic Moten, who showed zero nerves in what was the biggest start of her young career. Moten went the full five innings, allowing just three hits and one run while striking out four Florida batters.

The win was Moten's 19th of the season. At 19-wins as a freshman in a high-stakes SEC Tournament environment, she has already carved out a remarkable legacy in a single year. Alabama coach Patrick Murphy has leaned on her all season, and the results have spoken for themselves.

Taylor Sends One Out

The offensive fireworks came early. Ambrey Taylor launched a home run to put the Tide ahead, and Alabama never looked back. The run-rule outcome — the game ending after five innings due to the 10-run mercy rule — showed just how dominant the Crimson Tide's attack can be when everything is clicking.

Alabama entered the SEC Tournament as one of the country's top teams at 48-6 before going 1-0 in quarterfinals and then 1-0 against Florida. The Tide have been a complete team all season — pitching, defense, and power in the lineup — and Friday was the clearest demonstration yet.

Texas Stands Between Alabama and the Title

The SEC Championship today features two programs that have played some of the most compelling softball in the country this spring. Texas earned its spot by defeating a surprising Georgia squad — the same No. 9 seed Georgia team that knocked out No. 1 Oklahoma — by a final score of 5-4. The Longhorns have shown grit all week and come in playing their best ball.

Alabama, meanwhile, has been building toward this moment. Friday's run-rule win over Florida — a program with its own elite pedigree — showed that Alabama's path to the championship wasn't luck. It was execution.

The winner earns the SEC's automatic NCAA Tournament bid, a massive boost to national seed positioning, and the conference's top billing heading into Selection Sunday. Expect a battle.

What's Next

The SEC Championship game is today in Lexington at John Cropp Stadium. One of these two programs — Alabama or Texas — will leave as SEC Tournament champion and in possession of one of the most coveted automatic bids in college softball.

Tomorrow night, Sunday May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2, the Selection Show reveals the full 64-team NCAA Tournament bracket. A win today against Texas would make Alabama a near-lock for one of the top two national seeds — and a strong case for No. 1 overall.

Alabama hasn't won the SEC Tournament since 2021. Today is the chance to change that.
