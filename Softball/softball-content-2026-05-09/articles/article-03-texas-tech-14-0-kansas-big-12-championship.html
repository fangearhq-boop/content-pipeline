Texas Tech Blasts Kansas 14-0, Advances to Big 12 Softball Championship

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | May 9, 2026

The Red Raiders put up a 14-run explosion in the Big 12 semifinals, keeping their tournament hopes alive and staying in the national seed conversation ahead of Sunday's Selection Show.

When Texas Tech is firing on all cylinders, the offense is breathtaking. The Red Raiders proved that again in devastating fashion on Friday at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, dismantling Kansas 14-0 in the Big 12 softball semifinals.

The run-rule victory sends Texas Tech to the Big 12 Championship game, scheduled for Saturday morning in OKC. It's the latest chapter in one of the most dominant seasons any college softball program has put together, and the Red Raiders aren't done yet.

An Offense That Can Erupt at Any Moment

Fourteen runs against a Big 12 program is not a fluke — it's a demonstration of just how talented the Texas Tech lineup is. The Red Raiders have been one of the country's premier offensive teams all spring, with the ability to score in bunches in any inning against any opponent.

Kansas couldn't handle the Texas Tech attack. The result was a mercy-rule finish that sent a clear message to anyone watching: this Red Raiders team is dangerous, and they are not going home until they have to.

Canady Sets the Table

NiJaree Canady has been the face of Texas Tech softball for the past two seasons, and her 2026 campaign has been nothing short of historic. Earlier this season she threw the first perfect game of her career — the sixth in program history — against Baylor. Since then, she's remained one of the country's most dominant pitchers.

In a tournament setting, Canady brings the kind of experience and poise that coaches dream about. Knowing she can take the ball in the Big 12 Championship and deliver a dominant performance gives Texas Tech a significant edge over any opponent.

A Season of Milestones

Texas Tech enters the Big 12 Championship game with more than 52 wins on the season, making them one of the winningest teams in college softball this year. They were the first program in the country to reach 50 wins, claimed the Big 12 regular season title outright, and have not stopped competing.

A Big 12 championship win Saturday would put a tournament crown on top of an already remarkable year. For the Selection Committee, Texas Tech's combination of record, pitching depth, and conference credentials makes a compelling case for a top-4 national seed — which could mean hosting both a regional and super regional on their home field.

What's Next

Texas Tech plays for the Big 12 Championship title today in Oklahoma City. A win would further strengthen their national seed profile ahead of Sunday's Selection Show (7 PM ET, ESPN2), where the 64-team NCAA Tournament bracket is revealed.

Regionals run May 15-18, and the Women's College World Series kicks off May 28 at Devon Park — the same venue where Texas Tech just knocked out Kansas. Playing on that field in June would feel very familiar. That's very much the goal.
