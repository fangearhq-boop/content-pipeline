Texas Claims Back-to-Back WCWS Titles With 4-1 Win Over Texas Tech in Game 2

By Sarah Mitchell June 5, 2026

Longhorns Sweep the Championship Series

Texas is the 2026 Women's College World Series national champion, and it didn't need three games to prove it. The Longhorns defeated Texas Tech 4-1 Thursday night at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, sweeping the championship series and claiming their second consecutive national title.

Combined with Game 1's 7-3 win on Wednesday, Texas outscored Texas Tech 11-4 across the two-game sweep. The Red Raiders never trailed in a series where the better team made the critical plays in the critical moments.

A Gutsy Coaching Decision Pays Off

Coach Mike White made a bold call heading into Game 2. Rather than sending ace Teagan Kavan back to the mound on one day of rest after her complete game, White started senior Citlaly Gutierrez and mapped out a plan to preserve Kavan for the final innings.

Texas Tech took advantage of the early matchup. The Red Raiders scored in the third inning to take a 1-0 lead, and the game stayed that way into the fifth. Then Texas responded the way championship teams do. Two Longhorn runners scored on a Texas Tech throwing error, giving Texas a 2-1 lead they would not relinquish.

Freshman Hannah Wells bridged from Gutierrez to Kavan, and in came the ace for the final two innings. Kavan retired six of the last seven batters she faced, striking out five. The threat was over before Texas Tech could mount it.

Kayden Henry Puts It Away

Kayden Henry put the game beyond reach in the seventh inning. Her opposite-field home run extended the Texas lead to 3-1, and the Longhorns added another run in the same frame to push the final to 4-1. Henry's blast was the kind of insurance swing that championship lineups produce when the moment is on the line.

Texas finished Game 2 with a performance that illustrated the depth of this roster. Gutierrez gave quality innings, Wells held the lead, and Kavan closed the door. Multiple contributors, one championship result.

Texas Tech's Remarkable Run Ends

The Red Raiders entered the championship series as a No. 11 seed with a 61-8 record, having knocked out No. 1 Alabama twice to reach the finals. NiJaree Canady — the most decorated pitcher in college softball — gave everything she had across this tournament. Texas Tech's season ends as national runners-up, a result that reflects how far this program has come and how much Canady poured into her final college campaign.

What's Next

Texas closes the 2026 season as back-to-back national champions and one of only five programs in WCWS history to win consecutive titles. Teagan Kavan earned Most Outstanding Player honors for the second straight year, becoming the first pitcher ever to accomplish that. The Longhorns will lose players to graduation and the AUSL Draft, but the program that Coach Mike White has built in Austin will begin 2027 as the team everyone is chasing.
