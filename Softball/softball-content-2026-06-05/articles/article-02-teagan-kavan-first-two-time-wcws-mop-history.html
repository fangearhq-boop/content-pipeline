Teagan Kavan Makes History as First Two-Time WCWS Most Outstanding Player

By Emily Rawlings June 5, 2026

A Record That Has Never Been Done Before

In the entire history of the Women's College World Series, no pitcher has ever been named Most Outstanding Player twice. Teagan Kavan just did it in back-to-back years. After Texas claimed the 2026 national championship with a 4-1 win over Texas Tech in Game 2, Kavan walked away with MOP honors for the second consecutive season — a first in tournament history.

The achievement sits alongside everything else she has built in her career at Texas: multiple program records, 10-plus career WCWS wins, and now two national championship rings. There is no comparable career in recent college softball history.

The 2026 WCWS by the Numbers

Kavan's 2026 Women's College World Series line tells the story of a pitcher who dominated across every role she was asked to fill. She finished the tournament 4-1 with two saves and a 1.47 earned run average. She struck out 30 batters in 33.1 innings pitched — maintaining the precision and effectiveness that made her an All-American all season.

In Game 1 of the championship series, she threw a complete game, allowing three hits and striking out six across all seven innings. In Game 2, coach Mike White held her in reserve — starting Citlaly Gutierrez — before bringing Kavan in for the final two frames. She responded by striking out five of the last six batters she faced and sealing the title.

Kavan in the Biggest Moments

What separates Kavan from any quantitative comparison is her consistency in elimination scenarios. When Texas needed shutout innings in the semifinal against Tennessee, she delivered. When the championship series was on the line, she closed it out. Her ability to perform without wavering when the season is on the line is what earns MOP awards — and it is what earns dynasties.

The White coaching staff trusted her completely, and she rewarded that trust every time she took the mound. Kavan does not pitch smaller in big moments. If anything, the opposite is true.

What the MOP Means in Historical Context

The WCWS Most Outstanding Player award has been given since the tournament's early years. Earning it once puts a pitcher among the elite. Earning it twice — consecutively — places Kavan in a category she created herself. No comparison exists because no one else has been here.

She arrived at Texas with enormous expectations. She has cleared every bar that was set for her and then raised the bar herself. Two championships. Two MOP awards. Whatever comes next in Kavan's career, her place in WCWS history is settled.

What's Next

Teagan Kavan is expected to be among the top selections when the AUSL College Draft takes place, carrying a resume that no collegiate pitcher has ever assembled. For Texas, retaining her in Austin for 2027 — if she has remaining eligibility — would be the goal, but the program has shown it can win with depth regardless. The two-time MOP era of Texas softball will be remembered long after this championship weekend ends.
