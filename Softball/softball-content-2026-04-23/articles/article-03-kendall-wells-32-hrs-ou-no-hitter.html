Five Away: Kendall Wells at 32 Home Runs as OU Throws Staff No-Hitter and Eyes the Record

By Jordan Reeves | April 23, 2026 | I Love Softball

Kendall Wells needs five home runs.

That is the number standing between her and Lauren Espinoza's all-time NCAA single-season home run record of 37 — a mark set at Arizona in 1995 that has stood for 31 years. Wells, a freshman at Oklahoma, enters Thursday at 32 home runs in 44 games. She is batting close to .380 and leads all of Division I softball in the home run category. The final stretch of the regular season has arrived, and the record is within reach.

Oklahoma reinforced its own health as a program Monday, when its pitching staff combined for a no-hitter in a 9-0 rout of Arkansas-Pine Bluff. The performance underscored what has become one of the Sooners' most important assets in the run toward the postseason: pitching depth. Oklahoma does not need Wells to carry the team. She is the story, but this is a complete program.

The Record in Historical Context

Lauren Espinoza hit 37 home runs for Arizona in the 1995 season, a record that has outlasted generations of college softball players and dozens of attempts to approach it. The all-time freshman record was more recently held at 30 home runs by previous record holders, and Wells broke that earlier this season when she hit her 31st during the Red River Rivalry series in April.

Wells already made NCAA history this season by setting the freshman single-season mark. Now she is chasing a record that has been untouched for more than three decades. The fact that she is doing it as a first-year player in the SEC — college softball's most competitive conference — makes the achievement all the more remarkable.

What makes the record chase particularly compelling at this stage of the season is the limited opportunity remaining. Oklahoma has 3-4 SEC series left before the conference tournament. The record requires five home runs across those games. It is achievable, but it is not guaranteed — Wells will have to earn it.

Oklahoma's Position: Tied at the Top

The Wells storyline plays out against a backdrop of genuine SEC urgency. Oklahoma is 42-6 overall and tied with Alabama at 15-3 in conference play — the two best records in the SEC. Texas, which had been projected as a top national seed, has slipped to 11-6 in the conference after Wednesday's 5-0 loss to Oklahoma State.

Oklahoma's staff no-hitter against Arkansas-Pine Bluff on April 21 was more than just a statistical novelty. It was evidence that the Sooners are pitching with purpose and sharpness heading into the most important stretch of the year. No-hitters don't happen by accident — they require execution from multiple arms and defense behind them.

For Oklahoma, the final weeks of the regular season carry dual stakes: chase down the SEC title and give Wells every possible opportunity to approach and break the all-time record. Both goals are in play. Both are achievable with the performances this team is capable of delivering.

What's Next

Every Wells at-bat from here carries weight. Watch for home run Nos. 33 through 37 over the coming weeks — each one is a step toward a landmark that would cement her legacy as one of the most accomplished freshmen in the history of college softball. Oklahoma's conference schedule will determine when and how often Wells gets her chances, and the Sooners will need her to perform at her best as the SEC title race reaches its conclusion. The WCWS selection show is May 10. The record chase, the SEC standings battle, and the national seed race are all converging at once.
