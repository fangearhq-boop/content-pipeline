Isa Torres Homers Twice as No. 12 FSU Defeats No. 5 Florida 3-1 in Rivalry Showdown

By Sarah Mitchell | April 23, 2026 | I Love Softball

Florida State needed a statement win. Isa Torres delivered two of them.

Torres homered twice on Wednesday night at JoAnne Graf Field in Tallahassee, powering No. 12 Florida State to a 3-1 defeat of No. 5 Florida in a rivalry game that carried genuine postseason weight. Ashtyn Danley entered in relief and delivered four scoreless innings to close it out, improving to 9-1 on the season. FSU improves to 40-7 and continues to build its case for a national seed at the May 10 WCWS selection show.

Florida struck first when Taylor Shumaker led off the game with a home run, giving the Gators a 1-0 lead before the crowd had settled in. Torres answered in the bottom half of the first inning with a solo shot of her own to tie the game at 1-1. That set the tone for what followed: a game decided by individual moments in big spots.

Torres Delivers the Defining Blow

With the game tied heading into the third inning, Torres stepped into the box and did it again. Her second solo home run of the night gave FSU a 2-1 lead, and the energy inside JoAnne Graf Field shifted immediately. The Seminoles had a lead against a top-5 program, and the belief in the dugout was palpable.

Shelby McKenzie made it 3-1 with an RBI single in the fourth inning, providing what proved to be the decisive insurance run. At that point, the game was in Danley's hands. The junior right-hander was electric — she worked four innings, allowed just two hits, and kept Florida's lineup off-balance throughout. Her performance in relief was every bit as critical as Torres's bat.

FSU's pitching held Florida's powerful offense to just one run, a testament to the depth and execution that has defined the Seminoles' season. Florida finished with limited offensive production against one of the ACC's most reliable staffs.

Rivalry History and National Context

FSU's win extended the Seminoles' run of dominance in this matchup to 8 of the last 9 meetings between the programs. The rivalry has increasingly tilted Florida State's direction, and Wednesday's result adds another chapter to that trend.

But the significance of this win goes well beyond the rivalry itself. FSU beat a No. 5 program on its home field, earning a marquee résumé win at a critical juncture of the season. Florida entered at 43-6 and ranked No. 5 in the NFCA poll — wins over teams of that caliber carry significant weight in the RPI and national seed calculations that will define the WCWS bracket.

FSU is now 7-0 against in-state opponents in 2026. The Seminoles had their 25-game win streak snapped in mid-April by Stanford, but they have responded with authority — sweeping UNC during Senior Weekend and now defeating Florida. This is a team that knows how to recover and how to perform in high-pressure games.

What's Next

FSU's 40-7 record and Wednesday's win over a top-5 program significantly strengthen the Seminoles' case for a national host seed at the WCWS. The selection show is May 10, with regional play beginning May 15. Watch for how the latest Softball America and NFCA bracketology updates reflect this result in the coming days. For Florida, the task is clear: the Gators are 43-7 with a strong enough season to remain in the national seed conversation, but they need quality wins in the final stretch to solidify their position. Both programs will be in the WCWS field — the question now is where they'll be seeded when the bracket is revealed on May 10.
