Texas Tech Runs It Up: Red Raiders Sweep UTEP 16-0, 11-1 in Doubleheader to Move to 44-4

By Sarah Mitchell | April 23, 2026 | I Love Softball

Texas Tech sent a clear message Thursday: the Texas State loss was an aberration, not a pattern.

The Red Raiders swept UTEP in a doubleheader at El Paso, winning 16-0 in the first game and 11-1 in the second. Both games ended in five-inning run-rules. Texas Tech improves to 44-4 overall and remains No. 2 in the NFCA rankings as NiJaree Canady and the Red Raiders push through the final road trip of the 2026 regular season.

The margin of victory in both games speaks to where Texas Tech's program stands heading into the postseason. This is a team that plays with relentless efficiency — it does not simply win, it dismantles opponents. The run-rule format required back-to-back dominant five-inning performances, and the Red Raiders delivered both without issue.

The Texas State Blip in Context

Texas Tech's 3-0 midweek loss to Texas State on April 15 dropped Canady to 17-3 and generated real attention about whether the Red Raiders had vulnerabilities that could be exploited in the postseason. The loss stung — being shut out by an unranked opponent in a midweek game is not the kind of result a top-2 program wants on its résumé.

But one loss is one loss. Texas Tech was 42-4 at the time of that defeat, and the program has since added two dominant run-rule wins to the record column. The response has been emphatic. The Red Raiders did not show lingering damage from the Texas State result — they showed they were focused on the next opponent and the next task.

That is the sign of a mature program. Elite teams do not allow midweek stumbles to become slides. Texas Tech's Thursday doubleheader was exactly the kind of response a No. 2-ranked team is expected to give after an unexpected loss.

Canady and the National Seed Picture

NiJaree Canady has been one of the central figures of the 2026 college softball season. Her overall record and ERA remain among the best in the country, and despite the Texas State setback, she continues to anchor one of the most complete pitching performances in recent program history. Her ability to be dominant in high-leverage moments is what separates Texas Tech from teams that have strong records but limited ceiling.

With 44 wins and just 4 losses, Texas Tech is in a strong position for a top-4 national seed at the WCWS. The selection show is May 10. The Red Raiders have been playing their best softball at the right time, and a strong finish to the regular season — including Big 12 Conference series — could push them to the No. 1 national seed conversation.

The final road trip continues after UTEP. Every win from here adds to a résumé that is already elite. Texas Tech has earned its No. 2 ranking, and the doubleheader sweep gives the program momentum heading into the final weeks of regular-season play.

What's Next

Texas Tech will finish the road trip before returning home for the final regular-season series. Watch for the NFCA Week 13 poll, which drops April 28, to see whether these dominant performances continue to strengthen the Red Raiders' position in the national rankings. A strong final stretch of the regular season would put Texas Tech firmly in the top-2 national seed conversation and could set up a high-profile regional hosting assignment. The Big 12 Conference title is also within reach — and adding a conference championship to this season's résumé would make the Red Raiders a complete package heading into the postseason.
