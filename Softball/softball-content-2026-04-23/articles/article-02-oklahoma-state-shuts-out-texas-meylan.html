Ruby Meylan Masterclass: Oklahoma State Shuts Out No. 4 Texas 5-0 on Just 2 Hits

By Emily Rawlings | April 23, 2026 | I Love Softball

A few weeks ago, Ruby Meylan walked into Love's Field in Norman and shut down Oklahoma. Wednesday night in Austin, she did it to Texas.

Meylan delivered a commanding complete-game performance, allowing just two hits over seven innings as No. 17 Oklahoma State defeated No. 4 Texas 5-0 at McCombs Field. Rosie Davis and Madison Hoffman each hit two-run home runs to account for all five runs, and Meylan was untouchable from start to finish. Texas managed just two hits in the entire game — two.

The result drops Texas to 35-8 overall and 11-6 in the SEC. For a program that many projected to be a national host seed at the WCWS, Wednesday's performance raises real questions about where the Longhorns stand heading into the final weeks of the regular season.

Meylan Commands the Night

Texas's Citlaly Gutierrez held Oklahoma State scoreless through the first five innings, giving the Longhorns every opportunity to scratch out some offense and stay in the game. But Texas could not solve Meylan. Through five innings, Oklahoma State hadn't scored, yet it also felt inevitable that Meylan would dominate long enough to force a breakthrough.

That breakthrough came in the sixth and seventh innings. Oklahoma State erupted for all five of its runs in those two frames, with Davis and Hoffman providing the power. The two-run home runs did the damage, and once the lead reached 5-0, the result was effectively sealed. Gutierrez took the loss, falling to 7-2 on the season after allowing five earned runs on five hits in those final two innings.

Meylan needed no such late drama. She was consistent and sharp throughout, working ahead in counts and keeping the Texas lineup off-balance all night. A two-hit complete game against a top-5 program is the kind of performance that defines a season for a pitcher — and Meylan now has two of them in quick succession.

Meylan's April Run Is Something Special

The context around Meylan's recent performances demands attention. Earlier this month, she threw a complete game in the Bedlam upset of No. 1 Oklahoma — a 6-4 Cowgirls win that shook up the entire national rankings picture. Less than two weeks later, she did it again to No. 4 Texas.

Oklahoma State is 30-11 overall — not a top-10 program in the traditional sense, but clearly a dangerous one. The Cowgirls have now defeated two of the top programs in the SEC, the league considered the deepest in college softball, within a matter of weeks. Meylan is the reason.

For Texas, the implications extend beyond a single midweek result. The Longhorns' national seed positioning was built on a foundation of quality wins and a strong schedule — but two midweek losses stack up when WCWS selection committees calculate RPI. Texas still has an elite enough body of work to be in the field, but the path to a regional hosting spot now requires strong performances down the stretch.

What's Next

Texas must respond in its final conference series and use the remaining regular season games to rebuild its RPI case. Oklahoma State, meanwhile, is proving it can compete with anyone in the country — Meylan's performance is the story of the month, and the Cowgirls should be on every bubble-watch and power-ranking list from here through the selection show. With the WCWS selection show on May 10, every remaining game carries maximum weight for teams on both sides of the national seed line.
