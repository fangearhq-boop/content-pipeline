Nebraska Extends Win Streak to 12, Remains No. 1 in Softball America at 37-6

By Emily Rawlings | April 23, 2026 | I Love Softball

Nebraska's 12-game win streak is not a fluke. It is a statement.

The Huskers beat Omaha on Wednesday night, hitting four home runs and drawing a crowd that set a record for the highest attendance in Omaha Maverick softball history — a testament to the growing national profile of this program. Nebraska improves to 37-6 overall and 17-1 in Big Ten play, retaining the No. 1 spot in Softball America's national rankings.

Coach Rhonda Revelle's program has been building toward this moment all season. But to truly understand what Nebraska's current run means, you need to look at the full body of work rather than just the recent win streak.

A Non-Conference Schedule That Proves the Point

Nebraska's case for a top national seed is not built on its Big Ten dominance alone — though a 17-1 conference record would be an elite mark in any league. The Huskers have beaten Power 4 opponents across the country: Texas, LSU, Texas Tech, Texas A&M, and South Carolina all appear on the Nebraska schedule as wins.

Those are five programs with legitimate WCWS aspirations of their own. Winning against that caliber of competition, away from home and on neutral fields, is precisely the kind of evidence that selection committees weigh when assigning national seeds. Nebraska has done the work to earn the No. 1 ranking it currently holds in Softball America.

The Huskers' 12-game win streak has not come against weakened competition either. It has included Big Ten conference games, midweek non-conference matchups, and a home game against Omaha that drew a record crowd — an indication of just how much this program has captured the attention of the local fanbase and the national softball community alike.

First-Ever Top-4 National Seed Conversation

Nebraska has never been in serious contention for a top-4 national seed at the WCWS — a placement that earns regional hosting rights and guarantees a favorable bracket position. That conversation is now happening.

The WCWS selection show is May 10. Regional play begins May 15. With three weeks of regular-season games remaining, Nebraska has time to strengthen its case further. The Huskers' current record, combined with the strength of their schedule and their win streak, makes them a genuine top-4 national seed candidate for the first time in program history.

A national seed would mean hosting an NCAA Regional in Lincoln, Nebraska. The record crowd Wednesday night against Omaha is a preview of what that could look like — a program on the rise, a fanbase hungry for it, and a team performing at the highest level of its history.

What's Next

Nebraska will continue Big Ten play over the coming weeks before the conference tournament. The Huskers need to maintain their current level and protect the 17-1 conference record as they close out the regular season. Watch the NFCA Week 13 poll (April 28) to see whether Nebraska's Softball America No. 1 ranking translates to the NFCA's poll, where Oklahoma currently holds the top spot. A strong finish sets up what could be a historic postseason for a program that has quietly built one of the most impressive résumés in college softball in 2026.
