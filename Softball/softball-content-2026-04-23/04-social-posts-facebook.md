## STORY 1: FSU Defeats Florida 3-1 — Torres Homers Twice

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET

Isa Torres is built for moments like this.

Wednesday night at JoAnne Graf Field in Tallahassee, No. 12 Florida State defeated No. 5 Florida 3-1 in a rivalry game that lived up to every ounce of hype. The result was decided by one player doing something remarkable: Torres homered twice.

Florida struck first when Taylor Shumaker led off with a home run to give the Gators an early 1-0 lead. It took Torres about one inning to respond — she answered with a solo shot of her own to tie the game at 1-1. Then in the third inning, she did it again. Another solo home run. FSU leads 2-1.

Shelby McKenzie's RBI single in the fourth pushed the lead to 3-1, and at that point it was Ashtyn Danley's game to close out. She delivered four scoreless innings in relief, allowing just two hits, to lock down the win and improve to 9-1 on the season.

FSU is now 40-7 and 7-0 against in-state opponents in 2026. That's 8 wins in the last 9 meetings with Florida — the Seminoles have owned this rivalry lately.

And the bigger picture matters here: FSU beat a top-5 team on their home field. That's exactly the kind of result that matters for the national seed race. The WCWS selection show is May 10. The Seminoles' hosting case is alive and well. 🥎

## STORY 2: Oklahoma State 5-0 Texas — Meylan Masterclass

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Ruby Meylan isn't just beating teams anymore. She's dominating them.

Wednesday night at McCombs Field in Austin, No. 17 Oklahoma State shut out No. 4 Texas 5-0 in a game that wasn't nearly as close as that score suggests. Meylan threw a complete game and held Texas to just two hits. Two. In seven innings.

The Cowgirls did all their damage via the home run ball. Rosie Davis launched a two-run homer. Madison Hoffman added another two-run shot. That was more than enough.

Texas RHP Citlaly Gutierrez was sharp through the first five innings — the Longhorns were actually in the game and she had held Oklahoma State scoreless. But the sixth and seventh innings were another story. Oklahoma State erupted for five runs, and just like that, it was over.

Here's the thing about Ruby Meylan you need to know: she beat Oklahoma in Bedlam earlier this month. Now she beats Texas. The two teams most people would consider the favorites to represent the SEC in the WCWS? Meylan's beaten both of them in the past two weeks.

Texas falls to 35-8 and 11-6 in the SEC. That's going to matter for national seeding. The Longhorns have some ground to make up in the final stretch. 🥎

## STORY 7: WCWS Selection Countdown

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM ET

Seventeen days.

That's how long until the 2026 Women's College World Series selection show on May 10. Regional play begins May 15. And the bracket is being written right now — game by game, result by result, with every win and loss filtering into the RPI calculations that determine who hosts and who travels.

This week alone has moved the needle significantly:

FSU's 3-1 win over No. 5 Florida in Tallahassee is a marquee résumé win that pushes the Seminoles higher in the hosting conversation. Texas's 5-0 loss to Oklahoma State complicates a national seed that many had assumed was locked in. Nebraska's 12th consecutive win keeps the Huskers at No. 1 in Softball America.

The format change matters too: 2026 marks the first year of 32-team seeding at the WCWS. All top 16 seeds earn regional hosting rights. The most recent Softball America bracketology named Stanford and Texas A&M as new host favorites, but these projections will shift several more times before May 10.

If your team is in the hunt, now is the time to watch closely. The 2026 WCWS field is taking shape. 🥎
