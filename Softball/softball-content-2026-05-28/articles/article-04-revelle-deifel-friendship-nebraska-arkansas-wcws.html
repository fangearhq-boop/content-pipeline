They'll Hug at Home Plate — Revelle, Deifel, and the Human Story Behind Nebraska-Arkansas

By Sarah Mitchell

May 28, 2026

The WCWS nightcap features two programs, two No. 1 seeds, and two coaches who have already made a promise: no matter who wins, they will find each other at home plate and hug.

Before the first pitch of tonight's nightcap is thrown, Nebraska head coach Rhonda Revelle and Arkansas head coach Courtney Deifel have already decided how it ends. Regardless of score, regardless of outcome, the two of them will meet at home plate when it is over — and hug.

It is one of the more human agreements you will find in a week when the stakes are as high as they get in college softball. Tonight at Devon Park, the friendship that produced that promise goes to war with itself.

The Relationship Behind the Matchup

Rhonda Revelle has built Nebraska softball into a program that reached 51-6 this season and returned to the WCWS for the first time since 2013. Her career reflects decades of sustained excellence at a program outside the sport's traditional power conferences. She is, by most measures of the profession, one of the best coaches who has ever done this job.

Courtney Deifel knows that better than most. Deifel has described Revelle as one of her mentors — someone whose example shaped how she approached building her own program at Arkansas. "She is one of the absolute best (coaches) to ever do it and one of the best humans also," Deifel said this week. The admiration is specific, not generic. It reflects a genuine professional lineage.

Revelle's feelings run in exactly the same direction. "As a friend, I just really admire and respect and adore Courtney Deifel," Revelle said. "I think she's a tremendous human being." These are the kinds of things people say when they mean them — not when they are filling airtime before a game.

The Roster Subplot That Makes It Stranger

The coaching connection is only part of what makes tonight's matchup unusual. One-third of Nebraska's everyday lineup are Courtney Deifel's former players.

Kacie Hoffmann was at Arkansas from 2022 to 2024. Twins Hannah and Lauren Camenzind were at Arkansas from 2023 to 2024. All three transferred to Nebraska, where they now play for Revelle. And the connection runs deeper: Hoffmann and the Camenzind twins were part of the same high school signing class as Arkansas starting pitcher Robyn Herron — who is expected to take the mound for the Razorbacks tonight against Frahm.

That means three players in Nebraska's lineup were once teammates of the woman who will try to get them out tonight. They know her mechanics. They know how she works in the circle. They also know her personally in a way that most opposing hitters do not know any pitcher they face.

Deifel has coached both sides of this game. Revelle has given those former Razorbacks a new home. The competition is real, but the human geography of the game is unlike most WCWS matchups.

The Competition Is Still Real

None of the friendship changes what tonight's game means. Nebraska is 51-6 and operating on a 26-game winning streak. Arkansas averages 7.7 runs per game and bats .345 as a team. Both programs are in the WCWS for very good reasons, and the loser goes to the elimination side of the bracket. Double-elimination forgives one loss, not two.

Jordy Frahm — 20-4, 1.14 ERA, the 2026 USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year — will take the mound for Nebraska. Robyn Herron will take it for Arkansas. Two elite pitchers, two programs with championship aspirations, and every reason for tonight's game to be one of the week's best.

Revelle said she thinks the teams are "really, on paper, really equal." That may be the most honest pre-game assessment offered at this tournament.

What's Next

Game 4 starts at 9:30 PM ET on ESPN2. It is the final game of WCWS opening day — the cap on twelve hours of softball at Devon Park. By the time the coaches meet at home plate, they will know which program advances with a win and which moves to the loser's bracket.

One thing is already settled: they will hug. Whatever happens on the field, the relationship between Rhonda Revelle and Courtney Deifel survives the scoreboard.
