Tennessee's Two All-Americans Enter the Circle — Pickens and Mardjetko Face Texas

By Emily Rawlings

May 28, 2026

Most WCWS teams have one All-American pitcher. Tennessee has two. Today at 2:30 PM ET, they take that depth into Game 2 against the defending national champion — with a very specific score to settle.

When you scout a starting pitcher, you prepare for a single player. You study tendencies, look for patterns in velocity and movement, and build a game plan around what that one pitcher does in the circle.

Tennessee offers Game 2's opposing lineup a problem with that approach. There is no single pitcher to prepare for. There are two — both named First-Team All-Americans by D1Softball in the same week, both capable of taking over a game, and both ready to go today against Texas at 2:30 PM ET on ESPN.

Karlyn Pickens: Four Times Over

Karlyn Pickens has earned a First-Team All-American nod from D1Softball four times. Three of those have come consecutively — she has been one of the best pitchers in the country in each of the last three seasons and the recognition has followed. Her 2026 line: 1.53 ERA across 31 appearances, 180 strikeouts in 132.1 innings pitched.

Those numbers reflect a pitcher at the peak of her college career. The ERA is elite. The strikeout total is the product of sustained dominance over an entire season. And the consistency of the All-American recognition signals something more than a single breakout year — it shows a pitcher who has maintained this level when it was hardest to do so.

Against Texas today, Pickens enters with a specific history. She watched from the mound as Tennessee lost to the Longhorns 2-0 in the 2025 WCWS semifinals. She knows what that scoreboard looked like. Today is the first opportunity to change the story.

Sage Mardjetko: Named This Week

Sage Mardjetko received her First-Team All-American designation from D1Softball on May 26 — two days before today's opening game. The timing is almost theatrical: the honor arrives, and then immediately the tournament begins.

Having two active First-Team All-Americans on the same WCWS pitching staff is exceptionally rare. The honor recognizes individual excellence, and two players on the same team reaching that threshold in the same season signals something larger about how Tennessee is built. Pitching depth is the most valuable commodity in double elimination, where a single pitcher can be asked to carry enormous workloads in a compressed bracket. Tennessee does not have to ask one pitcher to carry everything.

What It Means Strategically Against Texas

Texas enters today as the defending national champion. They are 51-6. Their lineup produced enough offense to win the 2025 national title, and they have had another strong season building on that foundation.

But Tennessee's dual-ace system creates a practical challenge: any lineup plan built around a first look at Pickens becomes partially obsolete the moment Mardjetko enters. The velocity profiles, release points, and pitch movement differ between the two pitchers. Adjusting in real-time to that kind of variation — at Devon Park, in a high-leverage WCWS game — is exactly the scenario Tennessee wants to create.

Last year's 2-0 loss in the semifinals is the backdrop that gives today's rematch its emotional weight. Tennessee has spent a full year with that score. The pitching staff that lost that game is not the same as the one that takes the field today — Mardjetko's addition to the All-American tier is new, Pickens' form is at its best — but the memory of the loss remains.

What's Next

Game 2 starts at 2:30 PM ET on ESPN. A Tennessee win puts the Lady Vols on the winners' bracket side of the double-elimination format and keeps Texas on the pressure side. A Texas win sends Tennessee to the loser's bracket, where every subsequent game is an elimination game.

For the team with two All-American pitchers, today is the first test of whether that advantage is as decisive as the résumés suggest. The answer arrives at Devon Park this afternoon.
