WCWS Day 1 Is Here — Devon Park Opens for the 2026 Women's College World Series

By Sarah Mitchell

May 28, 2026

Thirty-five years. One city. One diamond. The Women's College World Series is officially open for business — and it's only getting bigger.

The buildup is over. The rankings and projections and preview articles and power rankings have done their job. Now it's Devon Park's turn.

The 2026 Women's College World Series officially begins today in Oklahoma City, and for the 35th time in the tournament's history, Devon Park is the stage. Four games. Eight teams. First pitch at noon ET. The best college softball players in the country converge on a facility that has become synonymous with the sport's biggest moments — and in two years, it will host the world.

The Stage and What It Means

Devon Park holds 13,000 fans. City tourism officials project roughly 120,000 total attendees over the tournament's run and an estimated $25 million economic impact for the Oklahoma City metro area. Hotel occupancy rates are expected to exceed 90 percent this opening weekend. These are not just sports numbers — they are a reflection of how much college softball has grown, and how central this tournament and this venue have become to that growth.

Oklahoma City has hosted the WCWS every year since 1990, with one exception in 1996 when the event moved to Columbus, Georgia ahead of the Atlanta Olympics. That history gives today's opening a weight that goes beyond a single season. Devon Park is not just a ballpark. It is the home of this sport at its highest level.

And it is about to get a promotion. Devon Park has been confirmed as the venue for softball competition at the 2028 Los Angeles Olympics. The site that has hosted the WCWS through 2035 will host the world's best players on an Olympic stage. When fans fill those seats today, they are watching a venue that operates on two timelines simultaneously: the national championship right in front of them, and the global stage two years away.

Today's Four-Game Slate

The 2026 WCWS features an eight-team double-elimination format with all games at Devon Park. The opening-day schedule covers twelve hours of softball:

Game 1 — Noon ET, ESPN: No. 3 Texas Tech vs. No. 5 Mississippi State. NiJaree Canady takes the circle for Texas Tech against a Mississippi State program making its first-ever WCWS appearance.

Game 2 — 2:30 PM ET, ESPN: No. 1 Texas vs. No. 2 Tennessee. The defending national champion against the team they eliminated 2-0 in last year's WCWS semifinals. Both programs enter with First-Team All-American pitchers on their staffs.

Game 3 — 7 PM ET, ESPN2: No. 1 Alabama vs. No. 2 UCLA. The nation's No. 1 seed with 22 shutouts on the season faces Megan Grant, who set the all-time NCAA home run record this year with 40. Something will give.

Game 4 — 9:30 PM ET, ESPN2: No. 1 Nebraska vs. No. 2 Arkansas. Jordy Frahm — the 2026 USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year — makes her WCWS debut against a program she knows well. One-third of Nebraska's lineup are former Arkansas players.

Why This Field Is Special

The 2026 WCWS field has a character unlike most recent tournaments. Five of the eight teams are from the SEC — Alabama, Texas, Tennessee, Arkansas, and Mississippi State — meaning the national champion will almost certainly come from that conference regardless of what happens in the bracket. But the non-SEC teams carry some of the tournament's most compelling storylines.

Texas Tech's Canady is arguably the most decorated active pitcher in the country. Nebraska returns to Devon Park for the first time since 2013. UCLA's Grant carries the all-time home run record into the tournament. Mississippi State is here for the first time in program history, having upset No. 3 Oklahoma in the super regional round to earn the right to be here at all.

Double elimination means one loss is survivable. The first game is a statement, not a verdict. But the teams that take the field today — in the noon heat and the late-night ESPN2 slot — will carry whatever happens into everything that follows.

What's Next

Today's four games determine the Day 1 winners' bracket and set the loser's bracket matchups for Day 2. Teams that win today remain in the winners' side of the bracket and avoid elimination until much later. Teams that lose face an immediate pressure test. With the championship series scheduled to begin June 3, the teams that win on opening day are already buying themselves margin.

Devon Park is open. The 2026 Women's College World Series has officially begun.
