### STORY 2: Play-in Recap — Bandits 6, Cascade 5 (11 Innings)

#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET

Eleven innings. The longest game in AUSL history. The first play-in in the league's existence. And it ended on a walk-off single.

Thursday night at Davis Diamond, the Chicago Bandits and Portland Cascade played until nearly 1 AM in what became the defining game of the 2026 AUSL regular season — a 6-5 Chicago win in 11 innings that sent the Cascade home and the Bandits to the Championship.

The decisive moment: Bottom of the 11th. Two outs. Ailana Agbayani stepped in and singled down the first-base line, scoring Taryn Kern from second base. The stadium erupted. The Bandits had done it.

But the story of the game was Lexi Kilfoyl.

Kilfoyl entered the game in relief — not as a starter, which is the role she's played in every appearance this season. She had gone 6-0 as a starter. But the game was on the line, and Chicago's manager turned to their best pitcher. Kilfoyl responded. She allowed 2 hits and struck out 4 batters, keeping Portland scoreless through the late innings and extra frames. She held the lead — and the season — together.

Kat Sandercock started for Chicago and pitched 4 innings. For Portland, Carley Hoover (9-3 on the season) gave them everything she had. Megan Grant — who came in with 10 home runs and a 17-game on-base streak — was held in check when it mattered most.

Portland Cascade fought to the last out as a first-year expansion team in a sport that did not guarantee them anything. They beat the Bandits and the Talons in the regular season. They made it to the play-in game. They took it to the 11th inning.

That's a hell of a first season. But Chicago moves on.

Tomorrow is the day.

That is the date — 38 days away as of today — when Division I coaches can, for the first time, directly contact the current high school sophomore class, the Class of 2028, by phone, text, or email. Before September 1, coaches can evaluate, they can watch film, they can attend tournaments. But they cannot reach out.

The games happening this week at PGF Nationals are being played in front of coaches who are building their call lists for September 1. A championship run this week does not just win hardware. It puts players on lists that get dialed in the fall.

Championship day is Saturday. Tune in to ESPN+ and ESPNU and watch the best club softball in the country.

### STORY 4: Lexi Kilfoyl — Out of the Bullpen

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Lexi Kilfoyl has been a starter her entire professional career.

She came into last night's AUSL play-in game with a 6-0 record. Every one of those six wins came as a starting pitcher. And when the play-in game began, the plan was not for Kilfoyl to pitch — Kat Sandercock took the ball for Chicago.

But the game went long. It went very long. Eleven innings long.

And when the Chicago Bandits needed their best pitcher to close it out, the manager made the call. Kilfoyl entered the game in relief — a role she has not been asked to fill this season — with the Bandits' Championship berth on the line.

She delivered.

Four strikeouts. Two hits allowed. She shut Portland down through the critical late innings and extras until Ailana Agbayani ended it with a walk-off in the 11th. Kilfoyl's 6-0 record stays intact. The Bandits are in the Championship.

There's a specific reason why what Kilfoyl did matters beyond the box score. Relief pitching is a different mental game than starting. You come in cold, mid-game, with baserunners or the threat of them. The timing of your delivery is different. The hitters have already seen the bullpen. For a starter to step into that role in the biggest game of the season — and succeed — says something about what kind of pitcher Lexi Kilfoyl is.

She is in the rotation for the Championship Series. Saturday, 1 PM ET, ABC, Davis Diamond.

She is also the most important person in the Bandits' pitching plan for the next three days.

### STORY 5: Megan Grant's Season Ends

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM ET

Megan Grant's 2026 season is done.

The Portland Cascade's first base-line in the 11th inning was where it ended. A walk-off single. A 6-5 loss. An elimination. Grant walked off the field at Davis Diamond on Thursday night with 10 professional home runs — second-most in AUSL history — and a 17-game on-base streak, the longest active run in the league all season.

Let that number sit for a second. Ten home runs. Second-most in AUSL history. In her first professional season.

She came into this summer with 42 home runs from her senior year at UCLA — an NCAA single-season record. In her professional debut, she launched a 313-foot home run — the longest blast in AUSL history at the time. She never stopped hitting.

Grant's teammate Maya Brady set the new AUSL single-season home run record at 11 on July 22, the night before the play-in. Grant needed two home runs in the play-in game to tie it. The Portland bats didn't get there Thursday. But 10 is still more than anyone in the league except Brady. And the record Brady set at 11 — Grant will have a chance to break it next year.

What Grant built in 2026 is bigger than the stat line. Portland Cascade was an expansion team. They had no prior history, no franchise to build on. They went 14-11 in the regular season and made it to the postseason. Grant was the reason opposing coaches had to plan around Portland in every game.

She is 22 years old.

The 2027 AUSL season is going to look very different for whoever she plays for — and for whoever has to pitch to her.
