Lexi Kilfoyl Was 6-0 as a Starter. Last Night She Saved the Season as a Reliever.

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 24, 2026

Lexi Kilfoyl has been a starting pitcher in every game she has appeared in during the 2026 AUSL season.

She ended Thursday night's AUSL play-in game in a completely different role — and did it better than anyone could have reasonably expected.

Kilfoyl entered the Chicago Bandits' 6-5 walk-off win over the Portland Cascade as a mid-game reliever, with the season on the line, in the first play-in game in AUSL history. She allowed 2 hits and struck out 4 batters through the late innings and extra frames. When Ailana Agbayani walked off the Cascade in the eleventh, it was Kilfoyl who had kept Portland scoreless long enough to get there.

What Made It Work

Relief pitching is a different discipline than starting. When a starter enters a game in the first inning, the plate has been reset. The hitters have seen nothing. The pitcher sets the tone. When a reliever enters mid-game, the hitters have already logged innings against the Chicago bullpen. They know the rhythm of the game. They have seen velocities and sequencing. The entry point matters.

Kilfoyl came in with none of that in her favor — and she shut Portland down anyway.

Four strikeouts. Two hits. Zero runs. In the most pressure-loaded moment of the AUSL's postseason.

The ability to do that without a normal starting routine, without the built-in warmup structure of a rotation start, is what separates elite pitchers from very good ones. Kilfoyl has been elite all season. Thursday night confirmed it applies across contexts, not just when she is the planned starter with five days of preparation behind her.

Why This Matters for the Championship Series

The 2026 AUSL Championship Series is best-of-3. Games 1, 2, and potentially 3 are played over three consecutive days — Saturday, Sunday, and Monday. There is no off-day built in. Every pitching decision compounds.

Chicago used Kat Sandercock in Game 1 of the series equivalent. Kilfoyl came out of the pen. Those are decisions the Bandits now carry into Saturday.

What the play-in established is that Kilfoyl can handle either role. She can start Game 1 against Montana Fouts and Utah's lineup. She can also come in from the bullpen in a tight game and do exactly what she did on Thursday night. That flexibility is worth something in a three-game series where every arm is precious.

Utah's staff — led by Fouts — has had five full days of rest. Chicago's has not. But Chicago's most important pitcher just proved that fatigue and preparation are not the only factors that determine what a pitcher can do in October-stakes moments.

The Record Stays

Kilfoyl finishes the play-in with her 6-0 season record intact. She did not make a decision in which she was technically the winning or losing pitcher, but she did what she was asked to do and more.

The pitcher who has not lost a game all season now faces the pitcher who has not been fully tested since July 20. Saturday, 1 PM ET, ABC, Davis Diamond.
