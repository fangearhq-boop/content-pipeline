The AUSL Championship Is Here. Bandits vs. Talons. Saturday on ABC.

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 24, 2026

The Chicago Bandits survived eleven innings Thursday night to beat the Portland Cascade 6-5 and earn their spot. The Utah Talons have been waiting since July 20. Now it is settled.

The 2026 AUSL Championship Series begins Saturday, July 25. Game 1 is at 1 PM ET on ABC at Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. Sold out. Both Sunday and Monday are sold out too, if it comes to that.

This is a 2025 Championship rematch. Utah swept Chicago last year to win the title. The Bandits have had twelve months to think about it. They are here now.

What Makes Saturday Historic

Game 1 on ABC is the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television. Not cable. Not streaming. Network. ABC, which carries the Super Bowl, the NBA Finals, and college football, is airing softball on Saturday afternoon in prime placement.

This is what the AUSL has been building toward since the league launched. The Championship Series was always the showcase moment. Davis Diamond — the premium facility on the Texas A&M campus — was always the venue. Saturday is the delivery.

Whatever the viewership number is, it will set the benchmark. Every professional softball broadcast from here forward will be measured against it.

Utah Talons: Rested and Ready

The Talons locked up the No. 1 seed and received a bye directly to the Championship. They have had five full days to rest, prepare, and scout. Their pitching staff is fresh. Their lineup has been watching film.

Montana Fouts leads that staff. She finished the 2026 regular season at 7-4 with a 2.36 ERA, 63 strikeouts, and seven complete games. She holds the AUSL single-game strikeout record with 12 against the Texas Volts. She has been off since July 20. In a short series, a pitcher as dominant as Fouts carrying fully rested arms into Game 1 is a significant advantage.

Utah also knows what it is like to win this series. They swept the Bandits in 2025. That experience matters in a three-game Championship format where every at-bat in the first game shapes the entire series.

Chicago Bandits: Battle-Tested

The Bandits went eleven innings Thursday. They used Lexi Kilfoyl as a reliever. They survived a walk-off situation. They burned bullpen arms. They are coming into the Championship Series with less rest and deeper into their pitching depth than Utah.

That is also the case that Chicago is hardened in a way the Talons are not. The Bandits were tested at maximum pressure. Kilfoyl — their best starter — entered as a reliever in the middle of the biggest game of the season and delivered. That kind of moment either breaks a team or bonds it. Chicago is still standing.

Kilfoyl is 6-0 this season. She will be central to whatever Chicago does in this series. Whether that means a start in Game 1 or a relief role depending on the situation, the Bandits have their most important pitcher ready to go.

The Key Matchup

The last time Utah and Portland's offense tore through the Talons — the Cascade beat Utah 10-2 on July 15 — it was a run-rule result. The Talons know that kind of game is possible. But Portland is gone now. Chicago's lineup is different. Chicago's pitching is different.

If Fouts is elite in Game 1 and keeps the Bandits scoreless through six innings, Utah wins and takes control of the series. If Kilfoyl or Chicago's rotation keeps the Talons off the board while the Bandits scratch runs across, Chicago has a chance to steal home-field advantage in the series.

One game sets the frame. Saturday at 1 PM ET is the entire leverage point.

What's Next

The 2026 AUSL Championship Series between the Chicago Bandits and Utah Talons opens Saturday, July 25, with Game 1 at 1 PM ET on ABC at Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. Game 2 is Sunday, July 26, at 2 PM ET. If needed, Game 3 is Monday, July 27, at 7 PM ET. Davis Diamond is sold out for all three potential games. The winner is the 2026 AUSL Champion.
