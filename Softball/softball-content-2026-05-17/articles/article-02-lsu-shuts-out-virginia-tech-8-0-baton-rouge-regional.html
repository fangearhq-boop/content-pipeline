Cece Cellura's Career Day: LSU Shuts Out Virginia Tech 8-0, Tigers Enter Regional Final Undefeated

By Sarah Mitchell | May 17, 2026

Virginia Tech arrived in Baton Rouge as the story everyone was watching. The Hokies had dominated South Alabama 6-0 in the regional opener — Kylie Aldridge's two-out grand slam the exclamation point on a statement performance — and were labeled the consensus top upset pick of the entire 2026 NCAA Softball Championship. They were on LSU's field, in LSU's city, against a Tigers team that was supposed to be vulnerable.

Saturday, Cece Cellura made all of that narrative irrelevant.

Cellura threw the first complete-game shutout of her career, as LSU run-ruled Virginia Tech 8-0 in six innings. Maci Bergeron drove home two runs with a home run in the second inning to set the tone, added three hits total on the night, and Char Lorenz capped the scoring with a single through the left side that ended the game early. The Tigers are now undefeated in the Baton Rouge Regional — having outscored their opponents 16-0 through two games — and enter Sunday's regional championship needing just one win to advance to the Super Regionals.

Cellura's Defining Performance

Cece Cellura's Saturday outing was the kind of game that defines a pitcher's development arc. Entering the tournament as part of LSU's rotation rather than the headliner, Cellura handled the biggest stage of her career with poise. She navigated a Virginia Tech lineup that had looked dangerous all season and finished with a complete-game shutout — her first ever — against the team with the most to prove in Baton Rouge.

Virginia Tech (47-10 entering the regional) had been playing some of their best softball of the season. Kylie Aldridge had shown Friday night that the Hokies could hit with power. Cellura's answer was to not let it matter. The Tigers held VT scoreless for six innings, combining defensive efficiency with Cellura's command to produce a result that was never in doubt after Bergeron's second-inning swing.

LSU's Broader Statement

Context makes Saturday's result more striking. LSU entered the 2026 tournament as the No. 16 national seed — the lowest-seeded team to host a regional. The Tigers earned hosting rights despite their modest national seeding, and have spent the entire weekend playing like they belong at the top of the bracket rather than the bottom.

Two games. Sixteen runs scored. Zero runs allowed. That is not the performance profile of a No. 16 seed surviving on luck and home-field atmosphere. LSU has been dominant in every meaningful category, and Cellura's shutout was the signature performance that crystallized what this Tigers team is capable of when everything comes together.

Maci Bergeron's three-hit effort — including the two-run shot that opened the scoring — was the offensive centerpiece. When your catcher is providing that kind of production while your pitcher delivers a career game, the result is the kind of dominant showing that quiets a regional field.

Sunday's Baton Rouge Regional Final

Virginia Tech heads to the losers side of the bracket on Sunday and faces the prospect of needing to win twice to advance — a significant ask after Saturday's shutout loss. Meanwhile, LSU needs just one win in Sunday's regional championship to claim their regional title and advance to the Super Regionals.

The Tigers' path has been nearly perfect through the first two days. The only remaining variable is whether they can close it out with the same efficiency that has defined their regional run from the start.

What's Next

LSU's Baton Rouge Regional Final is scheduled for 1 PM CT Sunday at Tiger Park. The Tigers enter with an undefeated bracket record and the momentum of Saturday's dominant win. Virginia Tech would need to win the first game to force a second Sunday game — a significant challenge against a LSU team that hasn't allowed a run this regional weekend. The winner advances to the Super Regionals, which begin May 21. For LSU, one more signature performance would complete one of the most impressive regional runs of the 2026 tournament.
