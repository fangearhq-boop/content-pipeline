Loser's Bracket Heroes: Michigan and Grand Canyon Reach Regional Finals Against All Odds

By Sarah Mitchell | May 17, 2026

The loser's bracket in double-elimination tournament play is the sport's most unforgiving environment. One more loss and your season ends. Every game is an elimination game. Every inning requires total commitment to a run that, against the odds, might still end in a Super Regional berth.

On Saturday, Michigan and Grand Canyon both lived in that environment — and both came out the other side.

Michigan won two games in a single day to reach Sunday's Norman Regional Final against No. 3 Oklahoma. Grand Canyon needed extra innings and a walk-off single from Jada Cooper to survive South Dakota and reach Sunday's Lincoln Regional Final against No. 4 Nebraska. Both teams face top-seeded opponents. Both teams have already done things they weren't supposed to do. The loser's bracket had two unlikely heroes Saturday, and both of them are one win from the Super Regionals.

Michigan's Road Back in Norman

Michigan's Saturday was a test of stamina and resolve. The Wolverines had lost to Kansas 1-0 in eight innings on Friday — a gut-punch elimination game that would have ended lesser teams. Instead, Michigan responded with two complete Saturday victories.

First came Binghamton, dispatched 6-0 in an elimination game that allowed Michigan to clear the air and build confidence. Then came Kansas — a rematch in which the Wolverines turned a Friday loss into a Saturday statement. The two teams combined for 22 runs and a game that went down to the wire before Michigan pulled out a 12-10 victory, eliminating Kansas and advancing to Sunday's Norman Regional Final.

Michigan now faces No. 3 Oklahoma — a team that swept the winners' bracket without losing, run-ruling Binghamton 11-0 and shutting out Kansas 9-0 in five innings with a one-hitter from Guachino. The statistical mismatch is significant. But Michigan has already beaten a team Saturday that beat them Friday. They know how to win after a loss. That is precisely the mindset needed for Sunday.

Grand Canyon's Walk-Off Moment

In Lincoln, Nebraska, the story of the afternoon was a walk-off in the eighth inning that nobody outside of Anteater Athletic Park's most loyal fans predicted.

Grand Canyon faced South Dakota in a loser's bracket elimination game that stretched to extras. South Dakota had been one of the tournament's most compelling storylines — the first-ever NCAA Tournament participant in program history, a team that had led Nebraska 1-0 in its opening game before falling 4-1. But the Coyotes' magical run came to an end when Jada Cooper delivered a walk-off single through the center to give Grand Canyon a 5-4 victory in eight innings.

Cooper's moment was the culmination of a dramatic afternoon in which GCU refused to let their tournament end despite extraordinary pressure. The victory sends Grand Canyon to Sunday's Lincoln Regional Final against No. 4 Nebraska — a team that had already beaten GCU 2-0 in the Saturday winners' bracket game to reach the final undefeated.

For Grand Canyon to win Sunday, they need to beat Nebraska twice in one day. Nebraska beat them already Saturday. The Huskers are 48-6 and play at Bowlin Stadium, where they've been one of the most dominant home teams in college softball this season. The math is hard. Grand Canyon has already shown they don't care about the math.

Two Games, Two Definitions of Tournament Softball

Michigan and Grand Canyon arrived at their Saturday situations from different directions — Michigan through a close loss, GCU through a bracket that always promised a rematch with a team that had beaten them — but both demonstrated what makes college softball's postseason format special. The loser's bracket does not eliminate character. It reveals it.

Both teams showed they have it in abundance. Whether Sunday brings them to the Super Regionals or ends their runs against formidable top seeds, the Saturday performances from the Wolverines and the Lopes are among the best stories of the 2026 Regional Weekend.

What's Next

Michigan faces No. 3 Oklahoma at 3 PM CT Sunday in the Norman Regional Final. Grand Canyon faces No. 4 Nebraska at 3 PM CT Sunday in the Lincoln Regional Final. Both teams enter as underdogs, having already proven multiple times this weekend that being an underdog means very little in double-elimination tournament play. The Super Regionals begin May 21 — and both Michigan and Grand Canyon are one remarkable Sunday from being there.
