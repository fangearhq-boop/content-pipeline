Alabama Claims Tuscaloosa Regional Title — Briski's One-Hitter Leads Tide to Super Regionals

By Jordan Reeves | May 17, 2026

There is a formula to how Alabama runs an NCAA Regional. The No. 1 national seed has lived this script before — 21 consecutive regional appearances, a historically dominant all-time tournament record, and a coaching staff that understands exactly when to save its ace and when to unleash her.

This year, that formula worked exactly as designed.

On Thursday, freshman Kaitlyn Pallozzi threw a complete-game shutout to open the Tuscaloosa Regional, preserving SEC Pitcher of the Year Jocelyn Briski for the games that mattered more. On Saturday, Briski took the ball against Belmont — and was brilliant. One hit. Zero runs. A 3-0 win that sent Alabama straight to Sunday's regional championship. By Sunday, the Crimson Tide had claimed the Tuscaloosa Regional title and booked their spot in the Super Regionals.

Briski's Moment

Jocelyn Briski was the SEC Pitcher of the Year for a reason. Saturday's one-hit shutout against Belmont was the showcase performance of the Tuscaloosa Regional — a dominant, efficient, nearly unhittable outing that demonstrated exactly why Alabama coaches had held her out of the opener.

The game plan was patience. Let Pallozzi handle USC Upstate on Day 1, keep Briski fresh, and then let the ace perform at her best when the stakes are highest. The Crimson Tide's 3-0 win over Belmont gave Alabama a clear path to the regional championship without ever asking Briski to exceed her limits or pitch on short rest.

That is what elite program management looks like. That is the difference between teams that survive regionals and teams that win them.

The Alabama Standard

Alabama's consistency in the NCAA Tournament is one of the most remarkable ongoing stories in college softball. The program has now appeared in 21 consecutive regionals as a national seed — a run of postseason dominance that reflects sustained excellence at every level of program building. No matter how the season unfolds, Alabama finds a way to be among the nation's best when May arrives.

This year's team entered the Tuscaloosa Regional with the No. 1 overall national seed, the strongest pitching staff in the country anchored by Briski, and a lineup that put pressure on opponents from the first at-bat. The regional weekend validated every element of that profile. They never trailed. They never needed a dramatic comeback. They executed the game plan from start to finish and advanced — as Alabama programs so often do — with minimal drama and maximum results.

The Path to Super Regionals

Winning the Tuscaloosa Regional puts Alabama in the Super Regional round for the 21st consecutive tournament. The Crimson Tide will host their Super Regional matchup, continuing a home-field advantage that has defined their postseason approach for more than two decades.

Alabama's regional performance — clean, efficient, dominant — suggests a team that is peaking at the right time. Briski is at full strength. The lineup has been consistent all tournament. The coaching staff has shown again that they know how to manage a regional field better than almost anyone in the game.

What's Next

Alabama advances to the Super Regionals, which begin May 21. The Crimson Tide will host their Super Regional at Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa, where they've built one of the most formidable home-field environments in college softball. Their opponent will be determined by the results of other regional finals completing Sunday. For Alabama, the regional is finished — the Super Regionals and the road to Devon Park in Oklahoma City are what remain. This team has shown every sign of being built for the long postseason run.
