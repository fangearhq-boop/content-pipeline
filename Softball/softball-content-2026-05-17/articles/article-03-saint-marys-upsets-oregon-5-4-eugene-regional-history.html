Saint Mary's Makes Softball History: Gaels Shock No. 14 Oregon 5-4 in Program's First Postseason Win

By Emily Rawlings | May 17, 2026

Every program has a defining moment. For Saint Mary's softball, Saturday afternoon in Eugene was that moment.

The Gaels defeated No. 14 Oregon 5-4 in the Eugene Regional, recording the first postseason win in Saint Mary's softball program history. They did it on the road, against a ranked opponent, after falling behind 3-0 — answering with a four-run fourth inning that turned the entire game and the entire trajectory of their program in a single frame.

For a team that had never won a postseason game before arriving in Oregon, the scoreboard at Jane Sanders Stadium on Saturday afternoon will be remembered for a very long time in Moraga.

Oregon Jumped Out Early

The Ducks gave their home crowd exactly what they hoped for at the start. Elon Butler opened the scoring with a leadoff home run, and Oregon built the lead to 3-0 before Saint Mary's had found their footing. For most of the games across the country at the 16 regional sites, that kind of early deficit against a ranked host team ends predictably.

Saint Mary's was not interested in predictable.

The Gaels worked their way back into the game with patience and execution — nine hits across the contest — and when the fourth inning arrived, they were ready. Saint Mary's scored four runs in the frame, flipping a 3-0 deficit into a 4-3 lead with one of the most productive innings of the entire regional weekend. They added another run to cushion the advantage and then held on to win 5-4.

The Weight of What They Did

Saint Mary's had never won a postseason game before Saturday. Not in the program's entire history. That context makes the Eugene win more than a scoreline — it's a program-defining moment, the kind that coaches reference for recruiting classes and players remember for the rest of their careers.

To accomplish it by rallying from 3-0 down against a No. 14 national seed, on the road, in front of an opposing crowd, makes the victory even more meaningful. This wasn't a 16-over-1 where the math was always theoretically possible. Saint Mary's had to earn it the hard way — chasing a deficit, manufacturing runs, and then executing under the pressure of protecting a lead against a team with far more postseason experience.

Oregon's Disappointing Exit

For Oregon, Saturday's result was a tournament shock in its own right. The Ducks were the No. 14 national seed, hosting the Eugene Regional at Jane Sanders Stadium with home-field advantage and the support of a program that has been one of the Pac-12's most consistent competitors. A first-round postseason exit — especially at home — was not part of the plan for a program with Oregon's resources and expectations.

Butler's leadoff home run aside, the Ducks couldn't maintain their early advantage, and the four-run fourth inning proved impossible to answer. Oregon finished the 2026 season with a loss that the program will spend the offseason analyzing.

What's Next

Saint Mary's moves on to Sunday's Eugene Regional Final, where they face Mississippi State. It is an extraordinary position for a program that entered the weekend without a single postseason win. A victory Sunday would send the Gaels to the Super Regionals — an achievement that would have seemed impossible to even the most optimistic fans in Moraga before the weekend began. Whatever happens Sunday, Saint Mary's has already written the most important chapter in their program's history. And they wrote it in Eugene, against a ranked opponent, from 3-0 down.
