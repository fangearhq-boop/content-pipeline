FSU's Redemption Saturday: Seminoles Sweep Doubleheader, Force Regional Final vs. UCF

By Jordan Reeves | May 17, 2026

Twenty-four hours can change everything in college softball. On Friday evening at Joanne Graf Field in Tallahassee, Florida State suffered the biggest upset of Day 1 — a stunning 8-3 loss to Stetson that dropped the No. 9 national seed into the elimination bracket in front of their home fans. Saturday brought a completely different story.

Florida State swept a doubleheader, winning both games convincingly to force Sunday's Tallahassee Regional Final. First it was Jacksonville State, dispatched 11-0 in a morning run-rule. Then it was Stetson — the same Stetson team that had humiliated them less than 24 hours earlier — beaten 11-3 to send FSU into the regional championship. Brianna Danley delivered a career day in the Stetson rematch, providing the performance the Seminoles needed to complete one of the most dramatic one-day turnarounds of the entire regional weekend.

The Loser's Bracket Crucible

When FSU dropped to the elimination bracket on Friday, they faced the harshest reality of double-elimination tournament play: win every game or go home. There is no margin for error, no second chances, no waiting for a favorable matchup. The road back to the regional final required beating two teams on the same day — including the team that had already proven they could beat you.

Florida State (49-9) rose to the challenge. The 11-0 run-rule over Jacksonville State in the opening game established the tone. FSU's bats were alive and their pitching was sharp, dispatching an elimination game threat quickly and preserving energy for the afternoon's more important contest. Then came the harder ask: a rematch with Stetson, the ASUN team that had arrived on their field and won going away in the opener.

This time, the Seminoles gave a different answer. An 11-3 win over the Hatters closed out the day and put FSU back where they expected to be at the start of the tournament — in the regional championship game.

The Danley Factor

In any comeback story, there's a performance that anchors the narrative. For FSU's Saturday, that was Brianna Danley. The pitcher delivered what the Florida State athletic department called a "career day" in the Stetson rematch — a performance that steadied the Seminoles when they needed it most and helped them dominate a team that had, the day before, controlled them.

The margin of victory — 11-3 — told the full story. This was not a scrape-and-survive win. FSU came back stronger, more focused, and more disciplined than the team Stetson had beaten on Friday. That kind of response under elimination pressure speaks to the coaching staff's preparation and the veteran character of a program that has appeared in 12 consecutive NCAA Regionals.

Sunday: FSU vs. UCF

Florida State's reward for surviving the loser's bracket is an in-state rivalry game with UCF for a Super Regional berth. The Knights earned the other side of the bracket without a loss, including a dominant 10-1 win over Stetson on Saturday afternoon — the same Stetson team FSU had beaten twice. UCF has been the most consistent team of the regional weekend in Tallahassee.

Sunday's regional final puts two Florida programs against each other with a Super Regional spot on the line. For FSU, it's the opportunity to justify their No. 9 national seed and prove that Friday's loss was an aberration, not a preview. For UCF, it's a chance to knock off the host and claim one of the most satisfying upset victories in recent program history.

What's Next

The Tallahassee Regional Final is scheduled for noon Sunday at Joanne Graf Field. Florida State enters as the No. 9 national seed and the host team, carrying the weight of 12 consecutive regional appearances and the momentum of Saturday's dramatic comeback. UCF arrives undefeated through the bracket with nothing to lose and everything to gain. The winner advances to the Super Regionals, which begin May 21. One game decides it all — and after Friday's chaos, no one in Tallahassee will be making assumptions about how it ends.
