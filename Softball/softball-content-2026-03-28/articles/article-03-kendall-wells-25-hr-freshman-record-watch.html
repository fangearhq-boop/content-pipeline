Kendall Wells Is 5 Home Runs From the Freshman Record — And She's Not Slowing Down

By I Love Softball Staff March 28, 2026

The Number Is 25

Oklahoma freshman catcher Kendall Wells has 25 home runs on the season. She leads all of college softball. And she has at least 23 games remaining on the schedule to add to that total. For those doing the math: the NCAA freshman home run record is 30, a mark shared by three players who are now considered legends of the sport — Jocelyn Alo at Oklahoma (2018), Lauren Chamberlain at Oklahoma (2012), and Kelly Majam at Hawaii (2010). Wells needs five more to tie them. She appears very capable of getting there.

On Friday night in Baton Rouge against No. 20 LSU, Wells didn't add to the home run total — but she didn't disappear from the box score either. She delivered a clutch 2-run single in the seventh inning that tied the game and set up Oklahoma's eventual 3-2 extra-inning win. Even on nights when the long ball doesn't come, Wells finds a way to contribute in big moments. That's the mark of a special player.

The Record She's Chasing

The NCAA freshman home run record of 30 is one of college softball's most prestigious individual marks. Jocelyn Alo is arguably the greatest power hitter in the sport's history — she went on to set the all-time NCAA home run record with 122 career HRs. Lauren Chamberlain finished her career with 95. Both are Oklahoma legends. Kelly Majam's 30-HR freshman season at Hawaii in 2010 completed the record-sharing trio.

What makes Wells's chase remarkable is that she's a catcher — a position that demands tremendous physical exertion in every game. The wear of the position at the college level doesn't slow her down. Oklahoma has also shattered the SEC single-season team home run record as a program (previously 122 HRs in 32 games), averaging nearly four home runs per game as a team. Wells is the engine driving that historically unprecedented offensive output.

The Bigger Picture

Oklahoma is 33-2 overall and 7-0 in SEC play in their first year as a conference member. They are the best team in the SEC by record. Wells's individual pursuit of the freshman record is happening alongside one of the most dominant team seasons in conference history — and both storylines are feeding each other. When Wells launches a home run, it has the full weight of the program's national title chase behind it.

The all-time season home run record — 37, set by Laura Espinoza of Arizona in 1995 — is also within long-shot range if Wells stays on pace. That milestone would require 13 more HRs in 23+ remaining games, roughly one every other game. Given that Wells has already hit a home run in 20 of 33 games played, it's not a fantasy. It's a legitimate conversation.

What's Next

Oklahoma and LSU play Game 2 today at 11 AM CT on SEC Network in Baton Rouge. Every game from here forward is an opportunity for Wells to inch closer to the freshman record. Watch for her in the lineup whenever Oklahoma faces any ranked opponent — these are the games where she tends to rise to the moment. Five home runs. History is right there.
