Oklahoma Wins in Extras at LSU — 7-0 in SEC, Best Record in the Conference

By I Love Softball Staff March 28, 2026

A Road Win in Extras

Oklahoma's SEC debut season continues to produce moments that demand attention. On Friday night at Tiger Park in Baton Rouge, the Sooners won 3-2 in eight innings over No. 20 LSU — a comeback win on the road that showcases exactly the kind of resilience a title contender needs. Oklahoma improved to 33-2 overall and, more importantly, to 7-0 in SEC play. No other program in the conference has won every conference game this season.

LSU (22-11, 3-7 SEC) had the lead heading into the seventh inning, and a vocal Tiger Park crowd was sensing an upset. That's when Kendall Wells stepped to the plate and delivered a 2-run single, tying the game. LSU answered in the bottom of the seventh to make it 2-2. In the eighth, Gabbie Garcia came through with a go-ahead double followed by a sac fly that gave Oklahoma the lead for good. Pitcher Miali Guachino closed it out to improve to 9-0 on the season.

Oklahoma's Historic SEC Entry

The Sooners are in their first season as a member of the SEC, having transitioned from the Big 12 with the conference realignment. For context: no one expected Oklahoma to walk into the nation's most competitive softball conference and go undefeated through their first seven conference games. The SEC features No. 3 Florida, No. 6 Alabama, No. 9/10 Arkansas, LSU, and several other programs with legitimate tournament pedigree.

Oklahoma has beaten all of them. At 7-0, the Sooners have the best conference record of any SEC team — and they've done it with a combination of dominant offense (129 team home runs), solid pitching depth, and late-game clutch performances. Friday's extra-inning win at one of the most hostile road environments in softball is not a fluke. This team is genuinely the best team in the SEC right now.

Guachino: The Unsung Ace

Miali Guachino improved to 9-0 with the Friday win, a record that deserves more attention than it's getting. Oklahoma's pitching staff has been discussed primarily through the lens of Kendall Wells's home runs and the team's historic offensive output, but Guachino's 9-0 record in meaningful games is a real achievement. Winning nine games as a pitcher in the SEC — particularly on the road — requires elite command, composure, and competitiveness. Guachino has demonstrated all three.

What's Next

Game 2 of Oklahoma's series at LSU is today at 11 AM CT on SEC Network. An Oklahoma win would clinch the series and push their SEC record to 8-0. If LSU wins to force a Game 3 on Sunday, it would set up a series decider that could shift momentum in the conference standings. Watch for whether Guachino gets the ball again in Game 2 or if Oklahoma turns to another arm in the rotation. Either way, Wells and the offense will be the storyline worth tracking.
