Texas Survives A&M Scare, Win Streak Reaches 28 — Game 2 Today

By I Love Softball Staff March 28, 2026

Streak Survives a Wild One

No. 1 Texas needed every out on Friday night, but when the dust settled in Austin, the Longhorns had done it again. Texas defeated Texas A&M 9-8 to extend their program-record winning streak to 28 consecutive games. At 29-1 overall, the Longhorns have now surpassed every previous Texas team in terms of consecutive wins — a streak that has been building since late January.

The 9-8 final is not the margin Texas fans are used to seeing. This was not a run-rule blowout. The Aggies (23-9) pushed the nation's top program to the limit and came up one run short, a result that speaks to both A&M's quality and Texas's ability to grind through adversity. That combination — dominant when dominant and resilient when needed — is the hallmark of a championship-caliber team.

The Power Behind the Streak

Texas's offense has been fueled all season by a lineup with legitimate power from multiple spots. Outfielder Reese Atwood has 11 home runs on the year, and first baseman Katie Stewart has 15 — both among the national leaders at their positions. Junior pitcher Teagan Kavan, who has also served as the team's most reliable reliever, improved her record to 13-0 this season. When Texas needs to close a game, Kavan has been the answer.

The depth of Texas's roster is what separates them from programs that win 20 games but fade in big moments. Friday's 9-8 game required contributions up and down the lineup and arm-by-arm relief work in the circle — and the Longhorns delivered. That's not luck. That's a program firing on all cylinders.

What's at Stake Today

Game 2 of this SEC series is scheduled for 1 PM CT today on ESPN2 in Austin. Texas A&M has nothing to lose and everything to gain — snapping the nation's longest win streak against the No. 1 team in the country would be one of the signature wins in recent Aggie history. For Texas, this is simply the next game on a 28-game run that has already made history.

The series context adds another layer: this is an in-state SEC rivalry game with national implications. A Texas loss here would shake up the No. 1 ranking and potentially the entire top of the NFCA poll. A Texas win would push the streak to 29 and deepen a record that already has no modern precedent in Longhorns softball.

What's Next

Game 2 against Texas A&M is underway today at 1 PM CT on ESPN2. If Texas wins, they'll have the opportunity to sweep the series on Sunday and push the streak to 30 — a number that felt unthinkable at the start of the season. Watch for how Kavan and the rotation are deployed in Game 2, as Texas's pitching management will be a key factor down the stretch of this historic run.
