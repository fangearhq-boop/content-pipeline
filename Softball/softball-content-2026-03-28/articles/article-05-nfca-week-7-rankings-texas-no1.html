NFCA Week 7 Rankings: Texas Holds No. 1, Duke and Washington Return to Top 25

By I Love Softball Staff March 28, 2026

Texas Holds the Top Spot

The NFCA/GoRout Division I Top 25 Coaches Poll for Week 7 was released on March 24, and the top of the rankings looks like a who's-who of programs that have legitimate national championship expectations. Texas remains at No. 1 with a 28-1 record at time of polling, having recently reclaimed the top spot after defeating then-No. 1 Tennessee. Texas Tech is at No. 2. Florida is No. 3 — a ranking that will now be re-evaluated after Friday's stunning 6-2 loss at Arkansas. Oklahoma sits at No. 5 (31-2 at polling time, now 33-2), Alabama at No. 6, UCLA at No. 7, Florida State at No. 8, Nebraska at No. 9, and Arkansas at No. 10.

The week's notable entrants are Duke at No. 24 and Washington at No. 25. Duke returned to the poll after a two-week absence. Washington's ranking is their first appearance since February 11, 2025 — nearly 13 months away from the Top 25. Both programs earning their spots in a field this competitive is a meaningful achievement for programs outside the traditional power conferences.

Grand Canyon's Exit

The program that dropped out this week tells a different kind of story. Grand Canyon University, which had captured national attention with a perfect unbeaten start to the season, suffered their first loss and subsequently dropped out of the Top 25. GCU had been one of college softball's best storylines of 2026 — a mid-major program with a perfect record holding a Top 25 spot week after week. Their first loss ended that streak, and the rankings responded accordingly.

GCU's run was impressive and showcased how the sport has grown beyond just the traditional SEC and Big 12 powerhouses. They'll have the opportunity to re-enter the rankings if they win consistently down the stretch, but their Cinderella moment as an unbeaten program is over.

The Big Picture: A Loaded Top 10

The Week 7 Top 10 reads like a WCWS bracket preview. Texas, Texas Tech, Florida, Oklahoma, and Alabama are all legitimate national title contenders with rosters capable of winning in Oklahoma City in June. UCLA and Florida State are perennial powers. Nebraska has been one of the more consistent programs outside the Big 12 and SEC. Arkansas, ranked 10th, just beat No. 3 Florida — so if anything, these rankings are already outdated.

What this poll reflects is the incredible depth and competitiveness of college softball in 2026. There are at least eight to ten programs that, on any given weekend, could beat any other program in the country. That makes every series result relevant, every ranking position worth fighting for, and every weekend appointment viewing for fans of the sport.

What's Next

The Week 8 poll will incorporate the results of this weekend's series — including Texas-A&M, Arkansas-Florida, Oklahoma-LSU, and Florida State-Clemson. Given Friday's results, significant movement is possible, particularly at No. 3 Florida if they fail to win Games 2 and 3 against Arkansas. Watch for the updated poll next Monday to see how this weekend's action reshapes the national landscape heading into the home stretch of the regular season.
