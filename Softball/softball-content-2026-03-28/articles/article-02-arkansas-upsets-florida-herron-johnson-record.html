Arkansas Stuns No. 3 Florida 6-2 — Herron Deals, Reagan Johnson Breaks Career Hits Record

By I Love Softball Staff March 28, 2026

Herron Was Untouchable

It was supposed to be a measuring-stick game for No. 9 Arkansas. Instead, the Razorbacks aced the test in the biggest way possible. No. 9 Arkansas defeated No. 3 Florida 6-2 on Friday night at Bogle Park in Fayetteville, delivering the most significant upset of the weekend across college softball. The margin and the manner of the win were equally impressive.

Pitcher Robyn Herron was the story on the mound. She worked five innings, allowing just three hits while striking out eight batters — a performance that kept Florida's dangerous offense completely off-balance. Florida entered averaging one of the best offensive outputs in the SEC, but Herron neutralized it. Florida managed just three hits on the night, a season low (per the Florida Gators athletics department). Ace Keagan Rothrock, who entered the game at 17-2, was knocked out of the circle in a four-run Arkansas fifth inning, a development that will have Gators fans asking hard questions.

Reagan Johnson Makes History

In that decisive fifth inning, Arkansas centerfielder Reagan Johnson added a chapter to Razorback history. Johnson broke the Arkansas program's all-time career hits record with an RBI single — a milestone moment that arrived in the same inning that effectively put the game away. That's the kind of confluence of individual accomplishment and team success that defines memorable games.

Johnson's record came against a national top-3 opponent, on their home field, in a game that had SEC and WCWS-bracket implications. The record had stood as one of the program's most enduring benchmarks, and Johnson surpassed it with the entire conference watching. For Arkansas fans, it was one of the best Friday nights of the season.

What This Means for Florida — and the SEC

Florida entered this series at No. 3 in the country with genuine national championship aspirations. Losing Game 1 at home (as the visiting team in Fayetteville) — with a season-low three hits and their ace knocked out — is the kind of performance that raises real questions. Arkansas has now won eight of the last ten meetings between these programs since 2022, which means this is not an anomaly. The Razorbacks have figured something out against Florida's pitching.

Florida must win both Games 2 and 3 to take the series. Game 2 is today, Game 3 is Sunday. For the Gators to leave Fayetteville with a series win, they need to rediscover their offense in a hurry. For Arkansas, a series win over No. 3 Florida at home would be a statement performance that elevates their national profile heading into the stretch run.

What's Next

Game 2 of the Florida-Arkansas series is today at Bogle Park in Fayetteville. Florida needs this win to stay alive. If Arkansas wins, they take the series and deliver one of the program's most significant wins in recent memory. Game 3 (if necessary) is scheduled for Sunday. Watch for whether Florida goes back to Rothrock on short rest or turns to another arm — that decision could define the outcome of the series.
