Jazzy Francik Throws No-Hitter, Isa Torres Goes Deep Twice — FSU Blanks Georgia Tech 4-0

By Emily Rawlings | April 25, 2026 | I Love Softball

Florida State came to Atlanta on Friday night and left a statement on Georgia Tech's field.

Pitcher Jazzy Francik threw a complete-game no-hitter, and Isa Torres continued her remarkable week by hitting two home runs, as No. 13 FSU blanked Georgia Tech 4-0 in Game 1 of their final ACC road series of the regular season. The Seminoles improve to 41-7, 16-3 ACC — and they are playing the best softball of any program in the country right now.

Francik's no-hitter was her second of the 2026 season and the third of her career, an exceptional achievement that speaks to the level of consistency she has maintained all year. Torres's two home runs — including a leadoff blast off Georgia Tech pitcher Madalyn Johnson — provided the offensive backbone for a performance that was dominant in every phase.

Francik Makes History Again

Throwing a no-hitter once in a season is a career accomplishment. Throwing two in the same season elevates a pitcher into a conversation about the best arms in college softball history. Francik has done it — and in doing so, she has given FSU the ace-level performance it will need to advance deep into the NCAA Tournament.

Francik's ability to command the zone, change speeds, and locate her pitches under pressure is what separates her from merely being good. On Friday night, she did not allow a single hit against a Georgia Tech lineup that has been competitive in ACC play. The no-hitter was clean, efficient, and dominant.

FSU's pitching depth has been one of the underappreciated strengths of this team all season. Ashtyn Danley threw four scoreless innings of relief in Wednesday's win over Florida. Francik followed up with a no-hitter. The combination gives the Seminoles two genuinely elite arms heading into postseason play.

Torres Is Having a Week for the Ages

Isa Torres hit two home runs against Florida on Wednesday. She hit two more against Georgia Tech on Friday. That is four home runs in three days — against two different opponents — and it puts Torres in a tier of offensive performance that very few college softball players have ever achieved in a single week.

Her leadoff home run against Georgia Tech's Madalyn Johnson set the tone for the evening immediately. FSU was in control from the first pitch, and Torres's bat was the reason. She added a second home run later in the game to push the lead to 4-0 and render the result beyond doubt.

What makes Torres's production so valuable is where it comes in the lineup. As a leadoff-style presence who provides both contact and power, she creates scoring opportunities that FSU's lineup then capitalizes on. Her week has been one of the most impressive individual offensive performances of the entire 2026 season.

What's Next

Game 2 of the FSU-Georgia Tech series is Saturday, April 25, at 5:00 PM ET on ACCN. The Seminoles need to close this road series out strong. A series win gives FSU two road sweeps in three final weekends and a résumé that makes a compelling case for a national seed on May 10. After the way Francik and Torres performed Friday night, it is very hard to bet against them.
