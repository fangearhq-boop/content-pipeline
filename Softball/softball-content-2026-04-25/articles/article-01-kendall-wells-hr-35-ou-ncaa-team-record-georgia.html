Kendall Wells Blasts HR No. 35, Oklahoma Sets NCAA Single-Season Team Home Run Record in Win Over Georgia

By Sarah Mitchell | April 25, 2026 | I Love Softball

One swing. Two records.

Kendall Wells stepped into the batter's box in the first inning against No. 11 Georgia on Friday, took a single pitch, and launched a solo home run to center field at Love's Field. The blast was her 35th of the season — a new Oklahoma single-season program record, eclipsing the 34 set by Jocelyn Alo in both 2021 and 2022. And it was the swing that pushed Oklahoma's team total to 165 home runs on the year, shattering the NCAA single-season team home run record.

No. 1 Oklahoma finished the evening with a 10-2 win over Georgia in six innings, with Gabbie Garcia adding a stunning six-RBI night. The Sooners are now 43-6, 15-3 SEC — and Wells is two home runs from tying the most storied individual record in college softball history.

The Program Record Falls, Then the NCAA Record Falls

Wells entered Friday's series opener needing three home runs to break Alo's Oklahoma program record. She got one of them on the very first swing of the game. That single home run was enough to move the program record needle — and then some, as Oklahoma's team total surpassed the previous NCAA mark simultaneously.

The Oklahoma program has produced some of the greatest home run hitters in the history of the sport, and Wells's 35th of the season places her name alongside Alo's in the record books at just the beginning of her college career. She is a freshman who has already broken the NCAA freshman home run record and now holds the OU single-season mark. The pace she is on has no real historical comparison.

Garcia's six-RBI performance underscored how deep and dangerous this Oklahoma lineup is. Wells may be the headliner, but the Sooners have a lineup top-to-bottom that pitchers cannot simply work around the freshman slugger. The 10-2 run-rule win in six innings was clinical and commanding.

Two Away From the All-Time Record

The individual record Wells is now chasing belongs to Lauren Espinoza, who hit 37 home runs for Arizona in 1995 — a mark that has stood for more than 30 years. Wells needs just two more to tie it. With Game 2 of the Georgia series this afternoon at 1:30 PM on ESPN+, plus the SEC Tournament and a full NCAA Tournament run still ahead, the opportunities are there.

Espinoza set her record in 72 games. Wells, 49 games into her OU career, has already surpassed most of the marks that once seemed untouchable. The two-HR gap between where she sits and Espinoza's all-time record could close at any time — on any pitch, against any team.

What makes the chase so compelling is the context surrounding it. Wells is not hitting these home runs in blowouts against weak competition. She is doing it in the SEC, against top-25 opponents, in moments that carry weight for her team. The 35th came in the first inning of a critical series opener against a top-12 national team. The record chase has never felt more real.

What's Next

Game 2 of the OU-Georgia series is Saturday, April 25, at 1:30 PM CT at Love's Field, available on ESPN+. Oklahoma needs a win today to maintain separation in the SEC standings, where the Sooners remain tied with Alabama at 15-3. Wells entering the batter's box against Georgia's pitching staff with two home runs needed to tie Espinoza's record is the most compelling individual storyline in all of college softball. Watch every at-bat.
