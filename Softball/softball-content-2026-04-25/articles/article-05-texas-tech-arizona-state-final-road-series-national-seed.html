No. 2 Texas Tech Opens Final Road Series at Arizona State — National Seed Positioning Is the Prize

By Emily Rawlings | April 25, 2026 | I Love Softball

The end of Texas Tech's regular season is within sight — and the path to Oklahoma City runs through Tempe first.

No. 2/5 Texas Tech (45-4, 17-2 Big 12) opened its final road series of the 2026 regular season at No. 24/RV Arizona State (35-13, 8-10 Big 12) on Friday night. For a Red Raiders program that has spent most of the season in the top two of the national rankings, this trip to Tempe is more than a conference series — it is the last major test before the Big 12 Tournament and the WCWS field is finalized on May 10.

NiJaree Canady leads the Red Raiders into this final road stretch with an 18-3 record and a 1.29 ERA. The senior ace has spent the week fielding questions about her AUSL Golden Ticket — the first awarded in the 2026 college draft — but make no mistake: Canady's focus is on winning a national championship, and Arizona State is the next step toward that goal.

Why Arizona State Is a Real Test

Arizona State's 8-10 Big 12 record may not suggest a powerhouse, but the Sun Devils have been competitive in conference play and play significantly better at home than on the road. Their home environment in Tempe — warm weather, a passionate crowd, and familiar surroundings — gives them an edge that shows up in the final score more often than their record suggests.

Tech's opponents have tried all year to attack Canady early, disrupt her rhythm, and force the Red Raiders to rely on their lineup rather than their pitching. Arizona State is capable of manufacturing runs and putting pressure on opposing defenses. The Sun Devils are a dangerous opponent for a team looking past them to the postseason.

Texas Tech has been through enough late-season gauntlets this year to know that complacency is the enemy. The Red Raiders lost to Utah during a road trip in April — a reminder that any team can win any game on a given day. The Arizona State series is a must-win situation for Tech's national seed case.

The National Seed Picture for Texas Tech

Texas Tech sits No. 2 in the NFCA poll and No. 2 in the Softball America Power 10. A successful run through the final two weekends of the regular season — Arizona State this weekend, then one more series at home — followed by a strong Big 12 Tournament performance would put the Red Raiders in firm position for a top-2 national seed on May 10.

The top national seeds get the most favorable regional draws and host regionals at home, meaning Texas Tech's fans could watch the Red Raiders open the NCAA Tournament at Rocky Johnson Field in Lubbock. That home-field advantage is meaningful: familiar turf, a passionate crowd, and no travel fatigue for the first two rounds of the tournament.

Canady has been the engine of everything Tech has accomplished. Her combination of elite strikeout stuff, pinpoint control, and competitive fire makes her the best pitcher in college softball — and one of the best at any level. The AUSL Golden Ticket ceremony was a recognition of what she has meant to the game, but Canady is focused on what she has not yet won: a national title.

What's Next

Texas Tech continues its Arizona State series throughout the weekend. The Red Raiders need a series win to stay on track for a top-2 national seed when the WCWS selection show airs May 10 on ESPN2. If Tech closes out the weekend strong, attention shifts to their final home series of the regular season and then the Big 12 Tournament. For a program chasing a national championship, every win from here to the end of the year counts double.
