Nebraska Sweeps Iowa Doubleheader, Extends Win Streak to 14 in Front of Program Record Crowd

By Jordan Reeves | April 25, 2026 | I Love Softball

Nebraska's fans already knew something special was happening this season. On Friday night, they showed up to prove it.

A program-record crowd of 3,396 packed Bowlin Stadium to watch the No. 4 Huskers finish off a doubleheader sweep of Iowa, winning Game 1 by a 9-1 run-rule margin and taking Game 2, 5-2. Nebraska has now won 14 consecutive games, moved to 39-6 on the season, and improved to 19-1 in Big Ten play. Win No. 40 is one game away — the series finale against Iowa is Saturday at 1:00 PM CT on B1G+.

For a program that has never reached these heights before, the numbers feel almost surreal: No. 4 NFCA poll. No. 2 Softball America Power 10. Most wins in program history within reach. And the national seed conversation has moved from hypothetical to very real.

The Crowd Told the Story

The program-record attendance of 3,396 fans for Game 2 of the doubleheader was not a coincidence. Nebraska's fanbase has been building all year as the Huskers have climbed the national rankings and accumulated wins over elite programs — Texas, LSU, Texas Tech, Texas A&M, and South Carolina among them on the non-conference schedule.

When Nebraska swept Minnesota earlier this month and jumped to No. 4 in the NFCA poll — the highest ranking in program history — the local interest reached a new level. Friday's Game 2 crowd setting a Bowlin Stadium record is evidence that the fanbase has fully bought into what Coach Rhonda Revelle's program is building.

The team rewarded those fans with a dominant performance. The 9-1 run-rule in Game 1 was clinical, and the 5-2 victory in Game 2 showed the Huskers can win in tighter, more competitive environments as well. Both wins matter heading into the May 10 WCWS selection show, where RPI, strength of record, and overall record all factor into the seeding conversation.

The 40-Win Milestone Is Today

Nebraska enters Saturday's series finale at 39-6 — one win from the 40-win mark. In college softball, 40 wins is a significant threshold that signals a program operating at an elite level. For Nebraska, reaching it for the first time in program history would be another chapter in a historic 2026 season.

Iowa (24-17, 5-13 Big Ten) has had a difficult conference season, but the Hawkeyes play their final series with nothing to lose and everything to prove. Iowa's coaching staff and players have their own pride on the line. Nebraska has learned all season that taking opponents lightly in a doubleheader or series finale format is never safe — the Huskers will bring their full attention to Saturday's finale.

The 14-game win streak is the longest in the program's history at this stage of the season. Nebraska's pitching staff has been the foundation — limiting Iowa to just three runs in the two Friday games combined. The Huskers' ability to manufacture runs, play clean defense, and get quality pitching starts has been the formula for this winning stretch.

What's Next

The Nebraska-Iowa series finale is Saturday, April 25, at 1:00 PM CT, streaming on B1G+. A Nebraska win puts the Huskers at 40-6 — the first 40-win season in program history — and extends the win streak to 15. With the WCWS selection show 15 days away, Coach Revelle's team has every opportunity to close the regular season as one of the top seeds in the country. This is what historic looks like in real time.
