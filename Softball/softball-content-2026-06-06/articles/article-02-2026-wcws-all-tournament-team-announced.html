Kavan, Canady, Briski, Frahm: The 2026 WCWS All-Tournament Team Honors Twelve Across Five Programs

By Emily Rawlings June 6, 2026

The Complete 2026 WCWS All-Tournament Team

The 2026 Women's College World Series All-Tournament Team is official, and the 12-person roster reads like a greatest-hits collection from the most compelling softball tournament of this generation. Five programs are represented. Every player on the list earned their spot by performing at the highest level when the sport's brightest spotlight was shining.

Teagan Kavan of Texas earned Most Outstanding Player honors for the second consecutive year — the first time any pitcher has won the award back-to-back in WCWS history. Texas first baseman Katie Stewart joined Kavan on the All-Tournament Team, making the Longhorns a two-player contingent that perfectly represents a championship team's balanced excellence.

Texas Tech Earns Four Spots Despite Runner-Up Finish

The most striking feature of this year's All-Tournament Team is that Texas Tech placed four players on it despite losing the championship series to Texas. NiJaree Canady, Mihya Davis, Kaitlyn Terry, and Mia Williams all earned recognition — a testament to how legitimately good the Red Raiders were throughout the tournament and how hard they pushed the national champions in the final round.

Canady's selection is particularly meaningful. The two-time NFCA Player of the Year and AUSL No. 2 draft pick finished her college career on the All-Tournament Team for a second time, without the national title she came to Lubbock to win. Four Red Raiders on the list is not consolation. It is confirmation.

Three Tide, One Husker, Two Bruins

Alabama's Jocelyn Briski, Marlie Giles, and Jena Young represent a Crimson Tide squad that was dominant through the bracket before falling to Texas Tech in an unexpected elimination. Briski's selection as one of the tournament's best pitchers is no surprise — she was arguably the second-best pitcher in the field behind Kavan.

Nebraska's Jordy Frahm earned All-Tournament Team recognition in what was the Huskers' best WCWS run in years. The two-way star — a standout both in the circle and at the plate — gave Nebraska fans memories they'll carry for a long time. UCLA's Taylor Tinsley and Jordan Woolery round out the 12-person roster, representing a Bruins team that came to Devon Park with Megan Grant's historic home-run season and left having pushed deep into the tournament.

What's Next

For several of these players, the All-Tournament Team nod is a final collegiate honor. NiJaree Canady heads to the AUSL. Jordy Frahm concludes a legendary Nebraska career. The softball world now watches where these athletes go next and how the sport at large absorbs a 2026 class that was as talented as any in recent memory. For Kavan and Stewart, who both return to Texas in 2027, the All-Tournament Team is not a farewell — it is a promise.
