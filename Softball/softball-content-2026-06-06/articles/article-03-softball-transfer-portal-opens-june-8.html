Tennessee Already Losing Players as Softball's Transfer Portal Opens Monday June 8

By Jordan Reeves June 6, 2026

The Window Opens Monday

The Women's College World Series ended Thursday night. By Monday morning, college softball's offseason roster movement will be officially underway.

The NCAA Division I Council has approved a 15-day transfer portal window for softball, running from June 8 to June 22, 2026. The timing is designed to align with the end of the WCWS, giving programs and players the opportunity to assess their situations and make informed decisions about where they'll spend the next chapter of their careers.

Some players didn't wait for Monday. Across the country, athletes at programs that fell short of the national title have already begun signaling their intentions to enter the portal — and Tennessee is among the programs feeling it first.

Tennessee Faces a Significant Rebuild

Sophomore outfielder Saviya Morgan became the first Lady Vol to announce portal intentions for 2026-27. Shortly after, freshman left-handed pitcher Kailey Plumlee followed with her own announcement. Morgan appeared in 36 games for Tennessee in 2026 with two years of eligibility remaining. Plumlee, a highly regarded in-state recruit from Carthage, Tennessee, did not appear in a game as a freshman and carries four years of remaining eligibility.

The departures compound what is already a challenging offseason for head coach Karen Weekly. Tennessee's legendary pitcher Karlyn Pickens, whose three-time First-Team All-American career ended with the Lady Vols' WCWS semifinal loss to Texas, has exhausted her eligibility. Backup infielder Camryn Sarvis and backup catcher Jackie Kirkpatrick are also graduating.

Weekly has built Tennessee into a perennial WCWS program — 10 appearances in 11 seasons before 2026 — but the roster that pushed Texas to a tough semifinal will look significantly different in 2027.

Every Contender Faces Offseason Decisions

Tennessee isn't the only program navigating major changes. Alabama loses Jocelyn Briski and Jena Young to graduation, along with Marlie Giles. The Crimson Tide will need to replace three All-Tournament Team-caliber players. Texas Tech waves goodbye to NiJaree Canady — the most decorated pitcher in program history — who heads to the AUSL after her college career ends as a national runner-up. Nebraska's Jordy Frahm, one of the best two-way players in the 2026 WCWS, also concludes her eligibility.

Texas, the defending back-to-back champion, enters the offseason in the best position of any program. With Kavan and Stewart returning, the Longhorns have the core of their championship rotation and lineup intact heading into 2027.

What's Next

The 15-day portal window running through June 22 will produce a flurry of movement across college softball. For coaches, it is the most intense roster management period of the year — simultaneously working to retain current players, recruit portal entries from other programs, and map out the 2027 roster. For players, it is a chance to find the best fit for their remaining eligibility. Every decision made in the next two weeks will shape the landscape of the sport heading into next season.
