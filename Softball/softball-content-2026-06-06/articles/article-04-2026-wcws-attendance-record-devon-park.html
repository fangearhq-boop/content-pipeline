No Oklahoma, No Problem: 2026 WCWS Sets New All-Time Attendance Record at Devon Park

By Sarah Mitchell June 6, 2026

The Question Was Fair. The Answer Was Better.

When the 2026 NCAA tournament bracket was set and Oklahoma failed to reach the Women's College World Series — their first absence since 2015 — a legitimate question surfaced. The Sooners are one of the sport's biggest draws. They have won more national championships than any other program. Their fans travel in force and fill Devon Park with energy. Would losing Oklahoma hurt the tournament's attendance?

The answer arrived Thursday night when the final numbers came in: the 2026 WCWS set a new all-time attendance record. More than 105,000 fans came through the gates across the tournament's nine days of competition at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. The opening night session alone set a single-session record of 12,605 fans — a number that told the story before the championship series even began.

Oklahoma's Fans Showed Up Anyway

Part of the story behind the record is that OU fans didn't stay home. Many Oklahoma supporters made the trip to Devon Park even without the Sooners on the field, drawn by the traditions, friendships, and community that have grown up around the annual tournament over the years. The WCWS has been held in Oklahoma City long enough that it has become bigger than any single program — a gathering point for the broader softball community.

That dynamic speaks to something important about where the sport is. College softball's fan base has matured to the point where attendance at the WCWS doesn't depend entirely on which programs are in the field. The game itself draws people. The athletes draw people. Devon Park draws people.

Devon Park Is Becoming a Softball Cathedral

The 2026 WCWS was the 35th time Oklahoma City hosted the Women's College World Series. Devon Park, which was rechristened in 2022 when Devon Energy signed a naming-rights deal, has established itself as one of college sports' most beloved tournament venues. The sight lines are excellent. The atmosphere is electric. The 2028 Olympics will be hosted in Los Angeles, but Devon Park has its own kind of significance in softball's global footprint.

Record attendance in a year without the defending dynasty says something about the legitimacy of the 2026 field. Texas and Texas Tech playing a championship series for the second consecutive year proved the sport could captivate fans even in the absence of Oklahoma's familiar presence.

What's Next

The 2026 WCWS attendance record will likely stand as the standard that the 2027 tournament tries to break. With Oklahoma almost certain to return next year — and Texas defending what would be a three-peat bid — the 2027 edition of the Women's College World Series has the ingredients to shatter what was just set. Devon Park will be ready. So will the fans who make this the most passionate crowd in college softball every June.
