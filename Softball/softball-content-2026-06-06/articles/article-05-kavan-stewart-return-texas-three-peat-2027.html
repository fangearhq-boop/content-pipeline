The Three-Peat Talk Starts Now: Kavan and Stewart Return to Texas for 2027

By Emily Rawlings June 6, 2026

Both Come Back

The championship confetti had barely settled at Devon Park when the college softball world began processing what comes next for Texas. The answer was delivered in a few short words: the two best players on the two-time national champion Longhorns are both juniors. They both come back.

Teagan Kavan, the two-time WCWS Most Outstanding Player and the most decorated pitcher in the modern era of college softball, returns for her senior season in 2027. Katie Stewart, the SEC Player of the Year and the first player in WCWS history to homer in four consecutive games, also returns for her senior campaign. The core that won consecutive national championships is intact for one more run.

What Three in a Row Would Mean

No program has won three consecutive Women's College World Series titles in the modern era of the sport. Oklahoma's dynasty from 2022 to 2024 produced three consecutive championships — the most recent three-peat in the sport — before the Sooners missed the tournament entirely in 2026. Before that, you have to reach back to the early years of the WCWS, when programs like UCLA established the sport's original dynasties.

Texas winning in 2027 would make the Longhorns only the second program to win three straight in the last three decades and would establish Coach Mike White's program as the dominant force in college softball across a full three-year cycle. That is not a small thing. It would mean beating Alabama, Tennessee, Oklahoma — whoever returns healthy and hungry — for three straight seasons in the most competitive tournament in the sport.

Kavan's Legacy, Already Historic

Kavan comes into her senior season having already done something no pitcher in WCWS history had ever done: win the Most Outstanding Player award twice, in consecutive years. Her 2026 tournament line — 4-1, 1.47 ERA, 30 strikeouts in 33.1 innings — was a masterpiece on the game's biggest stage. She has 11 career WCWS wins, the most in Texas program history. A third title would give her an argument as the most impactful pitcher in the history of the Women's College World Series.

Stewart, for her part, enters 2027 carrying 30 home runs in her junior season — a Texas single-season program record — and the first player in WCWS history to reach deep beyond any one tournament's memory. Back-to-back SEC POY is within reach. First-team All-America is the expectation, not the ceiling.

What's Next

Texas heads into the 2027 offseason as the undeniable favorite. The three-peat will be discussed all summer, all fall, all spring — until Devon Park makes its verdict in June. For now, the Longhorns have earned the right to enjoy what they've built. The work resumes in the fall. The question of whether history is about to be made once more in Austin answers itself on the field.
