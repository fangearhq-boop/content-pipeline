Beachum Watch: Day 12 — Visited Florida, Three Days Left

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 19, 2026

One name left.

The 2026 transfer portal window is in its final days, and with Isa Torres now committed to Texas, the entire focus of college softball narrows to a single player: Jaysoni Beachum. She is the last elite transfer on the board. She is Softball America's No. 1 rated transfer. And on Day 12 of 15, she has visited the Florida Gators — FSU's biggest in-state rival — without making a formal commitment.

Three days remain before the June 22 window close. The decision is imminent.

Who Beachum Is

Jaysoni Beachum spent three seasons at Florida State, where she established herself as one of the most productive hitters in Seminole history. In 2026, her final season in Tallahassee, she posted a .417 batting average with 16 home runs and 66 RBIs. Her on-base percentage was .549. She won Second-Team All-American honors and was a two-time All-ACC selection.

Over her career at FSU, Beachum accumulated 37 home runs, 180 RBIs, and 202 hits — numbers that stand among the program's best. In 2024, when she was just a freshman, the NFCA named her Division I Freshman of the Year, a distinction that set the bar for what she would become.

She is a third baseman who hit for both average and power. Softball America ranked her as the No. 1 corner infielder available in the 2026 portal. With Torres now gone, she is the No. 1 available player, period.

The Florida Visit

The most dramatic moment of Beachum's portal process came when she posted a photo of herself in Florida Gators gear, signaling a visit to Gainesville. For a Tallahassee native who played three years at Florida State, it was a striking image — one that the rivalry context made immediately newsworthy.

Florida is not an unlikely destination on pure softball merit. The Gators have a strong program, a proven coaching staff under Tim Walton, and the infrastructure to compete at the national level. They have a legitimate need at third base with Kendall Grover's graduation, which makes Beachum an obvious fit both positionally and productively.

But no formal commitment has been announced. The visit happened. The decision has not.

The Market Around Her

Beachum entered the portal on June 2, before the official window opened June 8. When the window did open, she was immediately established as the top name, alongside Torres, in the available pool.

One by one, the other elite names came off the board. Torres went to Texas. Moriah Polar went to Texas Tech. Kasidi Pickering went to Texas Tech. Jayden Heavener went to Texas Tech. Katie Wetteland went to Arkansas. Madison Hartley went to Oklahoma State.

Beachum has outlasted all of them — still uncommitted on Day 12, still watching the market, still evaluating. Whether this is deliberate leverage-building, genuine indecision, or a careful process toward a specific destination, only she knows.

What is certain is that the programs still recruiting her are not passive. Every day she remains available is another day of pitches, relationships, and official visits.

What's Next

Three days. June 22 is the hard close for the Division I softball transfer portal window. Players who do not commit before that deadline lose access to the pool of available scholarship spots and program flexibility that the portal provides.

For Beachum, the decision is no longer abstract — it is happening this week. Whatever program has done the best job of showing her how she fits, what role she will play, and what that program can offer her final season of college softball, that is the program that gets her signature.

Florida visited. Other programs are in the mix. The answer is coming.
