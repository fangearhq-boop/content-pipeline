Portal Final Push: Yarnell to Arkansas, Three Days Remain

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 19, 2026

While the softball world watches Jaysoni Beachum's decision unfold, the rest of the 2026 transfer portal keeps moving.

With three days left before the June 22 close, the final wave of secondary market moves is reshaping rosters across college softball. Programs that did not land the top names are pivoting to depth. Coaches who identified quality players in the mid-tier are filling their boards now, before the window slams shut.

The most recent headline belongs to Arkansas: UCF utility player Kendall Yarnell has committed to the Razorbacks.

Yarnell to Arkansas

Yarnell is a sophomore who spent two seasons at UCF, where she was a versatile and productive presence in the Knights' lineup. In 2026, she batted .344 with 15 home runs and 46 RBIs as a sophomore, starting 54 of her team's 61 games as the designated player. She is a genuine multi-tool contributor who brings both power and on-base ability to Fayetteville.

For Arkansas, the addition is complementary. The Razorbacks already landed Katie Wetteland earlier in the window — the Mountain West Player of the Year who brings a proven track record from a successful mid-major program. Wetteland and Yarnell give Arkansas a productive portal class without the program having chased elite names they could not realistically have afforded.

That is roster construction with purpose. Identify what the team needs, find players who meet that need at a realistic tier, and commit to them before the window closes. Arkansas did exactly that.

The Secondary Market

Yarnell is not an outlier. Across the sport, the final days of the portal window are producing a quiet wave of commits — players and programs finding the right match after the headline names landed first.

Mountain West and Pac-12 programs have been especially active in recent days, with trackers showing continued movement across the secondary market. The pattern repeats across every portal window: the first week belongs to the elite names and the programs that can sign them. The final days belong to the programs that are smart enough to work the rest of the available pool.

Texas Tech, which landed three All-Americans in Polar, Heavener, and Pickering, is now experiencing the other side of portal reality — losing a star infielder to the portal itself. No class is without cost. Players who see elite transfers arriving sometimes reassess their own situations, creating ripple effects that continue through the window's close.

The result is a genuinely fluid final 72 hours. Roster spots that were locked last week may be reopened. Programs that closed their business may find themselves revisiting the board. That is the transfer portal in its final days — unpredictable, fast-moving, and consequential.

Who Is Still Out There

Beyond Beachum, the remaining pool is thinner than it was on June 8, but not empty. Mid-tier players who have not landed are still available. Pitchers, utility players, and depth pieces remain for programs that have not finished their business.

The window closing on June 22 is a real deadline. Players who have not committed by that date cannot access the portal without specific exemptions, and the scholarship opportunities that exist now will largely be filled. The urgency of the final 72 hours is not manufactured — it is structural.

For programs still working, the next three days are the last opportunity to upgrade their rosters for 2027 through the portal. Every phone call, every visit, every decision made in the next 72 hours has real consequences for how those programs open next season.

What's Next

The portal window closes Sunday, June 22. Between now and then, expect a final burst of commitment announcements as players and programs finalize their plans.

Beachum's decision will dominate the headlines. But the moves that happen around her — the Yarnells of the portal, the mid-level commitments that fill roster gaps quietly — are what ultimately build the depth of national title contenders.

In three days, the 2027 rosters will be largely set. The class is almost complete. The final wave is in motion.
