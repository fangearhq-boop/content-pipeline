Portal Tracker: Day 12 of 15 — Three Days Until the Window Closes

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 19, 2026

Day 12 of 15. The NCAA Division I softball transfer portal window — which opened June 8 and closes June 22 — is in its final stretch. Three days remain. Here is where the 2026 portal stands.

Confirmed Commitments

Texas: Isa Torres (FSU, .530 BA, D1 Player of the Year), Samantha Swan (Arizona State, catcher), Samantha Lincoln (Texas Tech, pitcher), Samantha Altmeyer

Texas Tech: Moriah Polar (Purdue, .555 BA, nation's top hitter), Jayden Heavener (No. 1 portal pitcher, 2.81 ERA / 128 K), Kasidi Pickering (Oklahoma, .370 BA, 20 HR, 61 RBI, career 50 HR)

Oklahoma State: Madison Hartley (All-Big 12)

Arkansas: Katie Wetteland (Mountain West Player of the Year), Kendall Yarnell (UCF, .344 BA, 15 HR, 46 RBI)

Texas A&M: Makena Azua

Alabama: Kailey Plumlee (Tennessee LHP, first left-handed pitcher for the Tide since Lauren Esman in 2024)

Tennessee: Karley Shelton (South Carolina, .345 BA, 40 RBI)

Still Uncommitted

Jaysoni Beachum (FSU, 3B): Softball America No. 1. Three seasons at Florida State: .417 BA, 16 HR, 66 RBI in 2026; career 37 HR, 180 RBI, 202 H. 2024 NFCA Freshman of the Year. 2026 Second-Team All-American. Has visited Florida. Decision expected before June 22.

What This Window Produced

The 2026 transfer portal window will be remembered for two dominant stories and one unresolved one.

The first dominant story is Texas. Mike White assembled the most impactful portal class of the two-time defending champion's recent tenure — bringing in the national Player of the Year, an elite catcher, and a proven pitcher within a 12-day window.

The second dominant story is Texas Tech. Jerry Glasco landed three All-Americans — Polar, Heavener, and Pickering — in a class that Softball America immediately ranked No. 1 nationally. The Red Raiders, already 61-8 in 2026 and WCWS finalists, become measurably more dangerous in 2027.

The unresolved story is Beachum. She is the No. 1 remaining player, and her decision — expected within the next 72 hours — is the final significant transaction of this window.

What's Next

Three days. The window closes June 22, and at that point, the 2027 rosters take their shape.

Watch Beachum's decision as the last major news event of this portal cycle. Watch the secondary market in the final 48 hours for depth additions and program adjustments. And watch Texas vs. Texas Tech on the 2027 schedule — because both programs just assembled the elite classes that will make that rivalry the most compelling matchup in college softball next spring.
