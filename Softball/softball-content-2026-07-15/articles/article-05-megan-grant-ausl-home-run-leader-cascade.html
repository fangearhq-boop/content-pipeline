Megan Grant Is Leading the AUSL in Home Runs — and She's Just Getting Started

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 15, 2026

On the night of the 34th ESPYs, it's worth pausing on something that often gets overlooked in the conversation about Megan Grant's historic season: she hasn't stopped breaking things.

The 42-home run NCAA record at UCLA. The ESPY nomination. Those are the anchors of the spring story. But Grant went pro, and the professional game has not slowed her down.

The Numbers

Through 20 games with the Portland Cascade, Grant has hit 8 home runs. That is the most of any player in the AUSL this season.

On June 10 — her professional debut — she took a pitch and launched it 313 feet. That home run is the longest in AUSL history. It happened in her first professional at-bat. The Cascade hadn't played a game yet and already their No. 4 overall pick was in the record book.

On June 28, she hit two home runs in a single game against the Texas Volts. She finished with 91 career home runs at UCLA — the program's all-time record — before walking into the AUSL with something left to prove.

The Cascade's Position

Portland Cascade is 11-9 heading into the final five games of the AUSL regular season. They're in playoff position — sitting third in the standings, three games behind the Talons (14-6) and 1.5 behind the Bandits (13-7). The math for the No. 1 seed is essentially closed. But the play-in spot on July 23 is still in reach.

A Cascade team that enters the play-in with Grant in this form is dangerous. She hit the longest home run in league history in her debut. She leads the AUSL in home runs. In a one-game elimination format, that kind of power is a program-altering variable.

What the Numbers Mean on a Night Like This

Tonight, Grant may or may not win an ESPY for her 42-home run spring. That outcome is real and meaningful, and the softball community has been rallying around her nomination for three weeks.

But the AUSL campaign she's building with the Cascade is a separate argument — and it's a strong one. The record-breaking didn't end at UCLA. She drafted into a new league and set a record there too. She's been the best power hitter in professional softball since June 10.

The 2026 AUSL Championship begins July 25 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas, with Game 1 airing on ABC — the first professional softball game ever on network television. If the Cascade makes it through the play-in and into the Championship Series, Grant will have a national stage to add one more chapter.

What's Next

Five games left in the regular season. Play-in July 23. Championship starts July 25. And tonight: the ESPYs at 8 PM ET on ABC.

Megan Grant is having one of the most complete seasons any softball player has had in years. The spring chapter is what the award show is about. The summer chapter is still being written.
