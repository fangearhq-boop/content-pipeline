PGF Nationals Is 4 Days Away — Rosters Are Set, the Field Awaits

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 15, 2026

Four days from now, the biggest travel softball showcase of the summer begins. PGF Nationals Week 1 opens Saturday, July 19 at Orange County Great Park in Irvine, California, featuring 18U and 16U divisions.

Rosters are locked. The freeze deadline passed July 13. Everything from here is the game itself.

What Week 1 Looks Like

The 18U and 16U divisions take the field Saturday at Great Park (8000 Great Park Blvd, Irvine, CA 92618). PGF Nationals draws some of the strongest travel ball programs in the country, with college coaches watching from the bleachers at every session.

For families with players in either division, this is the proving ground. The scholarship conversations that get started or accelerated at this tournament can define a recruiting timeline. The talent level at PGF 18U is consistently among the highest at any summer event in the country.

Week 2 follows July 26 through August 2, with 14U, 12U, and 10U divisions taking the field at multiple Orange County venues.

What Class of 2028 Families Need to Know

For families with Class of 2028 prospects — players who will graduate high school in 2028 — this week carries specific recruiting weight. The NCAA contact window for the Class of 2028 opens September 1. That's 47 days from today.

Until September 1, coaches cannot contact prospects directly. What they can do is watch. And PGF is exactly where they'll be watching. A strong performance at Nationals — particularly at the 16U level — puts players in front of programs that will be making contact decisions the moment the calendar turns to September.

Players don't need to deliver a perfect week to make an impression. They need to play with consistency, compete hard in pressure moments, and let the coaches evaluate what's actually there. The evaluation is the point.

What's Next

Games start Saturday, July 19. If your player is competing, the preparation window is effectively closed — rosters are locked, travel is booked, and the work that's been done since January is about to be tested.

If you're a fan, Great Park is open to the public and the caliber of play at PGF 18U rivals what you'll see at many college programs. If you're a coach evaluating the 2026-27 class for the final time before September 1, this week is why you're in Irvine.

Four days. Then it starts.
