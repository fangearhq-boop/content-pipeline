AUSL Championship at Davis Diamond — and Game 1 Will Air on ABC

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 15, 2026

The 2026 AUSL Championship will be held at Davis Diamond on the Texas A&M University campus in College Station, Texas. The play-in game between the No. 2 and No. 3 seeds is set for Thursday, July 23. The Championship Series begins Saturday, July 25.

Game 1 will air nationally on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television.

That is not a footnote. That is the most significant off-field moment in professional softball history.

Why This Matters Beyond the Schedule

The sport has existed at the professional level in various forms for years. But network television — the kind of broadcast that puts a game in front of millions of viewers who weren't already looking for it — has never carried a professional softball game. Until now.

ABC's decision to broadcast the AUSL Championship opener puts the sport alongside the NFL, the NBA, and college football in the only distribution model that truly reaches a mass audience. Someone who has never watched softball before will turn on the television on July 25, see the game, and have a chance to become a fan.

That is what growth looks like.

The Standings Race With 5 Games Left

For the teams competing, the stakes are clearer: the No. 1 seed earns a direct bye to the Championship Series, skipping the July 23 play-in entirely. The extra rest and the shorter bracket have real value, especially for pitching staffs that will be worn down by the end of a 20-game regular season.

Heading into the final five games of the regular season (ending July 20), the standings look like this:

  • No. 1 — Utah Talons: 14-6 (clinched postseason berth)
  • No. 2 — Chicago Bandits: 13-7 (1.5 games back)
  • No. 3 — Portland Cascade: 11-9 (3 games back)

The Talons clinched the first postseason berth of the 2026 AUSL season and hold the lead. But they haven't clinched the No. 1 seed. The Bandits — who started the season 0-4 before going on a seven-game winning streak — are very much alive.

For Portland Cascade, the math is tighter: three games back with five to play means they need help. But they're in playoff position, and with Megan Grant leading the AUSL in home runs (8 in 20 games), they have the firepower to make things interesting in the final week.

What's Next

Five regular-season games remain across the next five days. On July 20, the standings will be set. The play-in matchup will be confirmed. And on July 25, Game 1 of the AUSL Championship Series will air on ABC from Davis Diamond in College Station.

Professional softball will be on network television. The teams still need to finish earning their spots in the bracket. But the stage is built. The venue is confirmed. The broadcast is set.

This is what the AUSL was built toward.
