Two AUSL Games Tonight — Bandits Chase No. 1 Seed, Canady and Volts Play for Pride

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 15, 2026

Wednesday night brings two AUSL games, both with meaningful stories on the field.

At 6 PM ET, the Chicago Bandits (13-7) face the Carolina Blaze at Smith Family Stadium in Durham, North Carolina. At 8 PM ET, the Oklahoma City Spark take on the Texas Volts at Dell Diamond in Round Rock, Texas.

Both games are available on AUSL streaming.

The Bandits — Still Chasing

Chicago's story this season has been one of the more remarkable in AUSL history. They opened the year at 0-4, falling out of playoff contention almost immediately. Then came a seven-game winning streak — the longest in AUSL history — that turned the season around entirely.

Now they're 13-7, second in the standings, 1.5 games behind the Utah Talons (14-6) with 5 games remaining. The No. 1 seed earns a direct bye to the Championship Series, skipping the July 23 play-in. For a team that has won as many games as anyone in the league since mid-June, being forced into a play-in round would sting.

Tonight's game against the Blaze is a must-win in everything but official elimination math. The Bandits need to keep winning, and they need the Talons to slip. There are five games left to make that happen — tonight is one of them.

Canady and the Volts

The Texas Volts have had a difficult first season of AUSL existence. With their postseason chances essentially closed off, the final games of the regular season are about finishing with something to build on.

NiJaree Canady's professional debut has been a work in progress. She entered the season as one of the most anticipated players in the league — her college career at Stanford and then Texas Tech put her in the conversation for the best pitcher of her generation — but the professional transition has been harder than expected. Working with outside coaching help brought in from her Stanford contacts, she's been rebuilding mechanics that drifted during her collegiate years.

Her first AUSL win came on July 10 — a complete game, six strikeouts. Tonight's game against the Spark is a chance to build on that.

What's Next

With five regular-season games remaining for most teams, tonight's results will shape the final standings picture. The regular season ends July 20. After that: play-in on July 23, and the Championship Series beginning July 25 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas — with Game 1 on ABC, the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television.

Both games tonight matter. Turn on the stream and watch.
