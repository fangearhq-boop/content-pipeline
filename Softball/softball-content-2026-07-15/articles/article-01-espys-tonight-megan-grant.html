The ESPYs Are Tonight — And Megan Grant Is on the Ballot

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 15, 2026

After three weeks of voting, the moment has arrived. The 34th ESPYs air live tonight at 8 PM ET on ABC and the ESPN app — broadcast from the David H. Koch Theater at Lincoln Center in New York City, hosted by Marcello Hernández.

Megan Grant is nominated for Best Record-Breaking Performance. The vote is closed. The outcome will be announced on live television tonight.

What She Did to Get Here

In the 2026 softball season at UCLA, Megan Grant hit 42 home runs. That number is the new NCAA Division I record for home runs in a single season. The previous mark belonged to Laura Espinoza of Arizona, set in 1995 — 37 home runs that had stood for 31 years before Grant stepped to the plate in a Bruins uniform.

She didn't inch past the record. She surpassed Espinoza's mark by five. In 63 games. At the highest level of college softball competition. Against pitching that knew she was the most dangerous hitter in the country and tried everything to stop her.

That's what the ESPYs voters are judging tonight. And that's what the Best Record-Breaking Performance category was made for.

The Competition

The field for Best Record-Breaking Performance is the strongest version of itself in years. Grant shares the ballot with Myles Garrett, the Los Angeles Rams defensive end who broke the NFL single-season sack record; Johannes Høsflot Klæbo, the Norwegian cross-country skiing legend who became the first athlete to win six gold medals at a single Winter Games; and Sabastian Sawe, the Kenyan marathon runner who set a new world standard in long-distance running.

Each of those performances is extraordinary in its own right. But 42 home runs — at a position where power numbers are harder to post than in any other sport at the amateur level — represents something that the record books had resisted for three decades. That context matters when a voter has to choose between disciplines.

The Other Softball Nominee

Grant is not the only softball presence at tonight's ESPYs. The Texas Longhorns softball program is nominated for Best Team following their second consecutive national championship. The sport has two entries on a stage that reaches millions of viewers who have never watched a college softball game.

Both nominations represent something larger than individual votes. They signal where the sport stands in 2026 — visible, competitive, and earning a place at the biggest table in sports awards.

What's Next

Regardless of what happens in the Best Record-Breaking Performance category tonight, Megan Grant's 2026 has already produced two chapters: the 42-home run NCAA record, and a professional career that's added new entries to the record books at the AUSL level. She leads the league in home runs. Portland Cascade is in playoff position.

The 2026 AUSL Championship begins July 25 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas — with Game 1 airing on ABC. She might be on that network twice in the same month.

Tonight, though: 8 PM ET. ABC. Marcello Hernández at the podium. The ESPYs. And a softball player's name on the screen.
