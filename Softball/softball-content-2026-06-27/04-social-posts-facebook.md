### STORY 1: Tia Milloy Portal Week — Who's Winning the Race?

**Long-Form Post**
The Milloy recruitment just started — and every program in the country is paying attention.

Tia Milloy entered the transfer portal on June 26 with two years of eligibility remaining. She appeared in 41 games for Oklahoma in 2026 and can play multiple positions. She is, in every measurable way, exactly what struggling Power programs need right now.

FSU had been pursuing her through a separate pathway available outside the standard transfer window. That pursuit no longer needs to be quiet. Milloy is now fully on the open market, and FSU's head start in the relationship becomes one factor in a recruitment that has suddenly attracted national attention.

The programs with the strongest cases to land her share a few things: roster gaps at utility positions, scholarship availability, and 2027 ambitions that need a proven contributor to fulfill. South Carolina, which quietly added three targeted pieces through the portal, still has positional flexibility. Texas Tech, which landed arguably the strongest portal class in the country, may still have a spot that Milloy fills better than anyone available.

FSU's position is complicated by how the summer has gone. The Seminoles lost Isa Torres and Jaysoni Beachum. They added Ella Dodge and Nicole Edmiaston. Adding Milloy would give them the utility versatility they've been specifically targeting. Not landing her means finding that piece elsewhere — or trusting the existing roster to grow into the role.

The Milloy recruitment will be among the highest-profile uncommitted situations of the summer. Watch closely.

### STORY 2: Texas Softball 2027 — The Case for a Three-Peat

**Long-Form Post**
Texas didn't just win the portal. Texas is building something that hasn't existed in college softball in a long time.

The specific details matter. Katie Stewart, who set the Texas single-season home run record in 2026 with 31 home runs, is moving from the outfield to first base. That move creates room for Isa Torres — the former D1 Softball Player of the Year — to take her natural position in the outfield.

What that gives you is a lineup that starts with Stewart, Torres, and Teagan Kavan in the first three spots. No pitching staff in the country has seen that combination before.

Off the field, the Longhorns added Amber Freeman as an assistant coach. Freeman comes from Arizona, where she worked with hitters and catchers. Head coach Mike White now has the staff to match the roster.

Texas was also recognized as the 2026 ATEC/NFCA Division I National Coaching Staff of the Year — acknowledging what the entire sport has come to understand: the program in Austin is operating at a different level right now.

Three-peats are rare. But Texas has a starting rotation, a power lineup, and a coaching staff that has earned the right to believe.

### STORY 3: Jenny Topping Takes Bakersfield — Olympic Gold, First Head Coaching Role

**Long-Form Post**
On June 24, Cal State Bakersfield hired Jenny Topping as their new head coach.

For anyone who followed USA Softball at the Athens 2004 Olympics, that name is immediately recognizable. Topping was part of the gold medal team — one of the most celebrated runs in the sport's history. She is an Olympic champion, and now she is a first-time Division I head coach.

This is what the sport looks like when its best players stay in it.

Bakersfield has not historically been among the elite programs in the country. But the WAC is a conference where a transformative hire can change everything quickly. A head coach with an Olympic gold medal walks into every recruiting conversation with a credibility that took other programs years to build.

For a high school prospect evaluating options, the message is clear: the coach at Bakersfield played at the highest level the sport has ever reached. That matters.

### STORY 4: PGF Nationals Preview — The Showcase That Shapes the 2028 Recruiting Class

**Long-Form Post**
In 64 days, D1 coaches can make their first direct contact with the Class of 2028.

Before that date arrives, there is one event that determines who gets called first: PGF Nationals.

The tournament runs July 19–26 at Orange County Great Park in Irvine, California. The 18U Premier and 16U Premier divisions bring the highest concentration of Division I prospects under one roof at any event in the country. Coaches from Texas, Oklahoma, Florida, Arizona, and every other major program will have staff in the stands.

What they are watching: pitchers who hold velocity through seven innings, not just through the bullpen. Hitters who can handle spin. Shortstops and center fielders with elite range. Catchers who control the running game.

September 1 opens the communication window for the Class of 2028. But the phone calls coaches make on September 1 are not random — they are the product of months of evaluation, and PGF is the most important evaluation window of the summer.

For fans of college softball, PGF Nationals is worth paying attention to. The players on those fields in late July will be in college uniforms in two years.

### STORY 5: The 2027 Recruiting Race — Florida, Arizona, and UCLA Building Their Next Rosters

**Long-Form Post**
The transfer portal closed on June 22. The big portal announcements are done.

But recruiting — the part that shapes rosters three and four years from now — never stops.

Florida, Arizona, and UCLA have each been quietly building strong 2027 recruiting classes. Tim Walton at Florida has added multiple four-star prospects to a 2027 class that supplements an already-loaded returning roster. Arizona has made similar moves. Kelly Inouye-Perez and her staff at UCLA have added top-ranked 2027 players to a program working to return to national relevance.

The context that makes this more important than it looks: the House Settlement changed the rules. The 25-player D1 roster cap means programs cannot stockpile recruits as an insurance policy. Every scholarship commitment is now a calculated decision.

For the Class of 2028, September 1 is 64 days away. The players that coaches identify at PGF as must-have targets will define what that class looks like.

The portal gets the headlines. Recruiting is where the sport's future is actually decided.
