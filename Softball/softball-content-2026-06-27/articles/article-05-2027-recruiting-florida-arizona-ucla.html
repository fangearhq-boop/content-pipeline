The 2027 Recruiting Race Nobody Is Talking About: Florida, Arizona, and UCLA Are Building

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 27, 2026

The portal headlines are done. The power rankings have been written. The conventional wisdom about who won and who lost the 2026 transfer window has been established.

What has received less attention is the 2027 high school recruiting class — the players who will arrive at their programs in the fall of 2026 and begin contributing in 2027. Florida, Arizona, and UCLA have each been building strong classes in that cycle, and the programs that recruit most effectively at the high school level in the new NCAA landscape will compound advantages that portal acquisitions cannot fully replicate.

Florida: Tim Walton Adds Four-Star Power

Florida's 2027 class has been one of the more quietly strong builds in the country. Tim Walton has added multiple four-star prospects in a class that supplements a returning roster already loaded with All-SEC talent. The Gators return three first-team All-SEC players from 2026 — Keagan Rothrock, Taylor Shumaker, and Townsen Thomas — and Jaysoni Beachum, who transferred in from FSU, adds further to that core.

The 2027 recruiting class, arriving on top of that returning base, gives Walton both a competitive roster for 2027 and the developmental depth to sustain a run through 2028 and 2029. Florida's approach has consistently been to build through the transfer portal at the top of the roster while ensuring the pipeline of high school talent does not dry up beneath it.

Arizona and UCLA: Different Models, Same Goal

Arizona has historically used a combination of high school recruiting and portal additions to remain competitive. Their 2027 class has been active, with multiple prospects added to a program that has produced high-level players and assistant coaches — including Amber Freeman, now at Texas.

UCLA under Kelly Inouye-Perez has been one of the sport's most recognizable programs for more than two decades. The Bruins have added top-ranked 2027 players to a staff that carries institutional credibility in recruiting conversations. Inouye-Perez's track record as a developer of players remains a significant recruiting asset, and the 2027 class reflects active pursuit of the talent level required to compete nationally.

The House Settlement Context

Every recruiting decision now takes place inside a new constraint. The House Settlement established a 25-player Division I roster cap, which fundamentally changes how programs use scholarship commitments. Before the cap, programs could over-sign in the recruiting class and allow natural attrition to bring the roster to a manageable size. That option is gone.

Under the new structure, every scholarship commitment is a deliberate roster decision. Programs that recruit four-star players for a specific position need are making choices that are harder to reverse. The programs that recruit most effectively under these rules — evaluating need, not just talent — will have structural advantages that become clearer as the years progress.

What's Next

September 1 opens the D1 direct communication window for the Class of 2028. Before that date arrives, PGF Nationals (July 19–26) gives coaches their final major evaluation opportunity of the summer. The programs that leave Irvine with clear priority targets for the 2028 class will be the ones making the most confident calls on September 1. Florida, Arizona, and UCLA are all positioned to be active in both the 2027 class completion and the early stages of 2028 identification. The pipeline never stops. The programs that treat it that way are the ones that sustain.
