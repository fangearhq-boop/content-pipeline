Tia Milloy Is in the Portal — Now the Real Race Begins

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 27, 2026

The transfer portal window for Division I softball closed on June 22. The top programs made their moves, the rankings were reset, and most of the offseason's major decisions appeared settled.

Then Tia Milloy entered the portal on June 26, and the conversation started again.

Milloy is an Oklahoma utility player who appeared in 41 games in 2026 and has two years of eligibility remaining. She is on the open market now — available to any program that can offer a scholarship and a role. And the list of programs that want her is not short.

What Makes Milloy Valuable

The specific combination that drives Milloy's recruitment is not complicated to explain but is difficult to find. Two years of eligibility means any program that lands her gets a meaningful return on the scholarship investment — she is not a short-term rental. Utility designation means she can play multiple positions, which is exactly what programs with specific roster gaps need most.

Major-conference experience is the third piece. Milloy played her college career at Oklahoma — a program that has won multiple national championships and consistently operates at the highest level of the sport. That environment produces players who understand what winning looks like. Coaches recruiting Milloy are not evaluating a prospect. They are evaluating a player who has already competed at the top.

The late-June timing adds urgency. Programs that missed on their portal targets during the June 8–22 window now have a second opportunity at a player who fits what they were looking for. That is rare enough that every program director of operations with a remaining scholarship spot has her name on a board somewhere.

Who Has the Best Case

FSU had been in contact with Milloy through the pathway available outside the standard portal window. That relationship gives the Seminoles a head start in the information-gathering phase of the recruitment. They know her, she knows them, and that familiarity matters when a player is choosing among programs she may never have visited.

FSU's pitch is also specific. The Seminoles lost Isa Torres to Texas and Jaysoni Beachum to Florida during the portal window. They added Ella Dodge from Tennessee and Nicole Edmiaston from Stetson. What they have not fully addressed is the utility depth they need to be a legitimate national title contender in 2027. Milloy would close that gap directly.

South Carolina presents a different argument. The Gamecocks added three targeted pieces in the portal — catcher Brooke Bennett, utility player Lexie Shaver, and Gold Glove transfer Bella Foran — but remain a program with room to improve. A two-year utility player who adds positional versatility to an already-targeted class could push the Gamecocks into genuine title contention.

Texas Tech, which built what many analysts consider the strongest recruiting class of the 2026 portal window, may still have specific gaps that Milloy fills more cleanly than anyone else available at this stage of the calendar.

What's Next

Milloy's commitment will likely come within the next few weeks. Programs that move quickly in late-June situations tend to win them — the player has already decided she wants to move, and the first program to build a genuine relationship and present a clear role usually earns the commitment.

For FSU, the question is whether the relationship built during the pre-portal pursuit translates into a formal commitment now that the process is fully open. For South Carolina, Texas Tech, and the other programs in the mix, it is a matter of identifying need, communicating clearly, and being the first program that makes her feel like the answer — not just a recruit.

Whatever the result, Milloy's recruitment will be one of the most closely watched uncommitted situations of the summer. The portal closed. The market did not.
