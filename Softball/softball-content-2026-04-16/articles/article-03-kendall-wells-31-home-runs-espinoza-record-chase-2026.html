Kendall Wells Is 6 From History — Chasing the All-Time NCAA HR Record

By Jordan Reeves April 16, 2026

The Record That Has Stood for 31 Years

Lauren Espinoza hit 37 home runs for the Arizona Wildcats in 1995. It is the most home runs any player has hit in a single NCAA softball season, and it has stood for 31 years through every power surge, every transfer portal revolution, and every refinement in the game's approach to the long ball. No one has come close.

Kendall Wells has 31.

The Oklahoma freshman needs six more home runs to become the all-time single-season home run leader in NCAA softball history — a freshman from Georgia, in her first year of college softball, chasing a three-decade-old record. She is batting .383 with 60 RBIs and leads the entire country in home runs. Earlier this month, she broke the NCAA freshman single-season record, surpassing the mark of 30 shared by Jocelyn Alo, Lauren Chamberlain, and Kelly Majam. The freshman record was a milestone. Espinoza's is the summit.

The Next Opportunity: Arkansas at Love's Field

Oklahoma hosts No. 6 Arkansas at Love's Field in Norman starting April 17, a three-game series on ESPN2 that serves as the Sooners' most important test of the final regular season stretch. For Wells, it is also the next prime stage for the record chase. Three games. Three more chances to hit home runs against a tested pitching staff that will not hand her anything.

The Razorbacks have won series against both Mississippi State and Florida in April, which means their rotation will be stretched and ready. Every pitcher in the SEC has a plan for Wells by now — elevated pitches, off-speed sequences, anything to avoid putting a fastball in her zone. What makes her special is that she keeps hitting home runs anyway.

The Math Still Works

Oklahoma has 12-plus regular season games remaining after this weekend. Wells needs six. Even at a reduced pace from her record-setting spring — which saw her hit multiple home runs in some series — the math strongly favors her reaching the mark. The question is not whether she will get there, but when and on what stage. Every softball fan in the country has now learned to pay attention when she steps into the batter's box, regardless of the score or the inning.

Records this significant do not come around often. The last time the all-time home run record was seriously threatened before Wells, the sport was still decades away from the national platform it occupies today. Whatever Wells does with the next six, she is already part of the story of the 2026 season in a way that will not be forgotten.

What's Next

Wells and the Sooners open the Arkansas series Friday, April 17, on ESPN2. After that, Oklahoma has additional SEC series and the SEC Tournament before the WCWS. With 12-plus games still ahead, Wells' window to break the record is wide open. Every at-bat is now an appointment worth keeping.
