Oklahoma State Upsets No. 1 Oklahoma 6-4 — Meylan Delivers in Bedlam

By Sarah Mitchell April 16, 2026

Cowgirls Deliver at Devon Park

Devon Park was supposed to be Oklahoma's home away from home. The Sooners have dominated the venue since it opened, and Wednesday night they came in as the No. 1 team in the country with Kendall Wells on the roster and ESPN2 cameras rolling. Instead, No. 21 Oklahoma State left with a 6-4 victory that turned every assumption about this week's Bedlam game upside down.

Ruby Meylan threw a complete game on 133 pitches, striking out nine and keeping the nation's most dangerous lineup off-balance from the opening inning. When Abby Dayton hit a two-run double off the wall in the final frame to give Oklahoma a late threat, Meylan did not flinch. She retired the Sooners in order to close it out, earning Oklahoma State its first win over a No. 1 ranked team since the 2022 Big 12 Championship.

A Weather Warning Could Not Stop the Cowgirls

The game was weather-shortened when a tornado warning ended play after the seventh inning, but the outcome was already decided by then. Oklahoma State had built a lead that Meylan was not about to surrender. For a Cowgirls program that has been steadily building its credentials all spring — entering the game at 27-11 with a 9-6 Big 12 record — this win represents a major statement for postseason positioning.

Oklahoma State coach Kenny Gajewski has watched his program fight for respect in a season dominated by the Oklahoma-Texas-Texas Tech narrative at the top. A win over the No. 1 team in the country, in Oklahoma City, under the lights, rewrites the Cowgirls' storyline heading into the final weeks of the regular season. Oklahoma State improves to 28-11 and has genuine momentum heading into a critical postseason run.

Oklahoma's Rough Week Continues

For the Sooners, Wednesday's loss is the second in four days after a difficult stretch that saw Texas walk them off in Game 3 of the Red River Rivalry. Oklahoma falls to 40-5 — still leading the SEC, still ranked No. 1 in a poll that predates both of these losses — but the narrative has shifted. The Sooners are suddenly a team being tested, not just a team collecting wins.

The silver lining is that Oklahoma's core identity has not changed. Kendall Wells is still at 31 home runs, six away from Lauren Espinoza's all-time NCAA single-season record. The pitching staff is still capable of dominant performances. But the lessons from this week — two losses in four days against motivated opponents — will matter when the tournament arrives.

What's Next

Oklahoma shifts focus immediately to a weekend series that will feel like a reset. No. 6 Arkansas comes to Love's Field in Norman starting April 17 for a three-game series on ESPN2. The Sooners need wins, and they need them against a genuinely tough opponent. For Oklahoma State, the path forward includes a push for an at-large bid and momentum they can carry into the postseason. As for Devon Park — the WCWS is still eight weeks away, and Wednesday proved the venue does not automatically favor the favorites.
