NFCA Poll Fallout — OU's No. 1 Ranking in Jeopardy After Bedlam Loss

By Emily Rawlings April 16, 2026

The Poll That Already Feels Outdated

The NFCA Week 11 Coaches Poll was released on April 14. Two days later, it tells a story the sport has not heard in weeks: the top of college softball is genuinely unsettled.

Oklahoma sat at No. 1 with 747 points and 12 first-place votes. Texas Tech was No. 2 with 745 points and 12 first-place votes. Two points separated the best team in college softball from the second-best team. Alabama was No. 3 with 734 points and 7 first-place votes. Florida held No. 4, Texas was fifth, Nebraska was sixth, Tennessee seventh, and UCLA ninth. The spread at the top was razor-thin before Wednesday's Bedlam game had even been played.

Then Oklahoma lost to Oklahoma State 6-4 at Devon Park. The Sooners dropped to 40-5, their second loss in four days after Texas walked off OU in the Red River Rivalry finale. The Week 11 poll, already barely reflecting reality when it was released, now tells a very different story than the one coaches will vote on when Week 12 drops April 21.

Texas Tech's Case for No. 1

No team in college softball has made a stronger sustained argument for the top ranking than Texas Tech over the past six weeks. The Red Raiders sit at 40-3 overall — compared to Oklahoma's 40-5 — with NiJaree Canady at 17-2 in the circle and one of the sport's most dominant lineups backing her up.

Canady's ERA sits around 1.29, and she has posted complete-game performances in series clinchers throughout Big 12 play. Texas Tech won the Utah series 2-1 in their most recent road trip, showing they can handle adversity on the road. The Red Raiders have not lost in over a month. When 31 coaches sit down to fill out their ballots on April 21, it is difficult to construct a case for leaving Texas Tech at No. 2 after two Oklahoma losses in four days.

Alabama Is Also Watching

The Crimson Tide sit at No. 3 and received seven first-place votes in Week 11. Alabama has been one of the most consistent programs in the SEC throughout the spring — sweeping series, posting dominant performances, and building what looks like a genuine WCWS contender. If the coaches choose to reward consistency at the very top, Alabama has a case for climbing too.

The Week 12 poll becomes the defining document of the regular season's final stretch. With three or four weeks remaining before the NFCA polls close, the team sitting at No. 1 on April 21 will have a clear path to the top seed at the WCWS.

What's Next

Oklahoma hosts No. 6 Arkansas this weekend (April 17-19, ESPN2) in what amounts to an immediate chance to respond. Winning a series against a top-ten team would give the Sooners something tangible to show voters before Tuesday's deadline. Texas Tech continues its Big 12 run. The NFCA Week 12 poll drops April 21 — and for the first time since January, there is genuine drama at the top of the rankings.
