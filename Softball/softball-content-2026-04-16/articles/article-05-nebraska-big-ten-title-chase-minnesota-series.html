Nebraska Chases Big Ten Title at Minnesota — Huskers on an 8-Game Win Streak

By Emily Rawlings April 16, 2026

The Huskers Have Been Building All Spring

While the college softball conversation has centered on Oklahoma, Texas Tech, Alabama, and FSU, Nebraska has been quietly assembling one of the best seasons in the program's history. The Huskers sit at 33-6 overall, 14-1 in the Big Ten, ranked No. 6 in the NFCA Coaches Poll, and carrying an eight-game winning streak into this weekend's road series at Minnesota.

Head coach Rhonda Revelle has built something real in Lincoln. The Huskers are three games clear in the Big Ten standings, and a conference regular-season title that has eluded the program in recent years now sits within reach. This weekend's three-game series at Minnesota, April 17-19, is a meaningful step toward that goal.

What Has Made Nebraska So Good

The numbers tell the story. Nebraska has won 21 of their last 22 games and sits among the top four programs in the NCAA softball RPI rankings. Their offense has produced big innings at a consistent rate, and the pitching staff has posted shutouts and near-shutouts against quality Big Ten competition throughout the spring. This is a team built for the long haul, not just a hot streak.

Minnesota is not a pushover in the Big Ten this season, but Nebraska enters the series as a significant favorite. A sweep would extend the Huskers' conference lead and put them one step closer to clinching the title with multiple games still remaining. A series win, even without a sweep, continues the trajectory toward something the program has been working toward for years.

Nebraska in the National Seed Picture

At No. 6, Nebraska is squarely in the national seed conversation — the top eight programs in the final rankings host NCAA Regionals, providing home-field advantage through the Super Regionals. For a program that has made the WCWS before but has not consistently hosted the first two rounds, that would be a significant milestone.

Nebraska's RPI strength, combined with their 14-1 Big Ten record and overall schedule, gives them one of the best resumes in the country. Every win this weekend strengthens the case that should put them among the top regional hosts when the NCAA Selection Committee meets in May.

What's Next

Nebraska opens the Minnesota series Friday, April 17, and continues through Sunday. After that, the Huskers have the Big Ten Tournament and the postseason ahead. With the conference title in sight and a national seed firmly on the table, the final month of Nebraska's season could be the best stretch of softball this program has played in years. This weekend is where that push gets serious.
