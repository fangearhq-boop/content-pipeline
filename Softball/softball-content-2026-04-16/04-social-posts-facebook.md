### STORY 1: Oklahoma State 6-4 over No. 1 Oklahoma — Bedlam Upset

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET

Wednesday night at Devon Park, Oklahoma State pulled off something that barely anyone saw coming. 🥎

The No. 21 Cowgirls walked into the same venue that will host the Women's College World Series in May, stared down No. 1 Oklahoma, and left with a 6-4 win. Ruby Meylan threw a complete game on 133 pitches, struck out nine, and held the Sooners to a two-run double off the wall from Abby Dayton as the closest thing Oklahoma could manage to a comeback.

It was Oklahoma State's first win over a No. 1 team since the 2022 Big 12 Championship. A tornado warning ended the game after the seventh inning, but the result was not in doubt by then — Meylan had done her job.

For Oklahoma, it's a second loss in four days. The Sooners fell to Texas in a walk-off in the Red River Rivalry Game 3, then came out flat in Bedlam. They drop to 40-5, and the No. 1 ranking that seemed cemented just a week ago now feels very much in play heading into the NFCA Week 12 poll.

For Oklahoma State, it's a resume-defining win. The Cowgirls now sit at 28-11 and have genuine postseason momentum.

### STORY 2: NFCA Week 11 Fallout — OU's No. 1 in Jeopardy

#### Long-Form Post — 11:30 AM ET

Before Wednesday night's Bedlam result, the NFCA Week 11 Coaches Poll told a very interesting story. 🥎

Oklahoma was No. 1 — but only by two points. The Sooners had 747 points and 12 first-place votes. Texas Tech had 745 points and 12 first-place votes. Alabama was third at 734 points with 7 first-place votes. The margin at the top was essentially nonexistent before OU took the field against Oklahoma State.

Then the Sooners lost. Again.

Oklahoma is now 40-5 — two losses in four days, including Game 3 of the Red River Rivalry to Texas and Wednesday's Bedlam upset by Oklahoma State. Texas Tech, meanwhile, is 40-3 and has been one of the most dominant teams in the country over the past six weeks. NiJaree Canady is 17-2 in the circle. The Red Raiders have not lost since mid-March.

The NFCA Week 12 poll drops April 21. Unless something very unexpected happens between now and then, Texas Tech is about to take the No. 1 spot for the first time since OU claimed it two weeks ago. Alabama could also make a jump.

This is the first real top-of-the-rankings drama in college softball since February.

### STORY 3: Kendall Wells at 31 HRs — 6 from the All-Time NCAA Record

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

The most watched individual record chase in college softball history keeps moving forward. 🥎

Kendall Wells has 31 home runs this season. Lauren Espinoza hit 37 for Arizona in 1995 — a record that has stood for 31 years. Wells needs six more to become the all-time single-season home run leader in NCAA softball history.

The Oklahoma freshman is batting .383 with 60 RBIs and leads the entire country in home runs. She already broke the NCAA freshman single-season record earlier this month, surpassing the mark shared by Jocelyn Alo, Lauren Chamberlain, and Kelly Majam. Now she's chasing something even bigger.

This weekend, Oklahoma hosts No. 6 Arkansas at Love's Field in Norman for a three-game series starting April 17. Three more games. Three more chances to inch closer to Espinoza's record. Oklahoma has 12-plus regular season games remaining. The math is still very much in Wells' favor.

Even as the Sooners navigate a difficult stretch, Wells' record chase keeps the spotlight on every at-bat she takes.

### STORY 5: Nebraska at Minnesota — Huskers Are One of Softball's Best Stories

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

If you have not been paying attention to Nebraska softball this spring, now is the time to start. 🥎

The Huskers are 33-6 overall and 14-1 in the Big Ten — that's three games ahead of the next team in the conference standings. They have won eight straight games and 21 of their last 22. They are ranked No. 6 nationally. Head coach Rhonda Revelle has her program playing the best softball it has played in years, and this weekend, Nebraska heads to Minnesota for a three-game series that could bring them one step closer to a Big Ten regular-season title.

Nebraska's dominance in the conference this season has been built on consistent pitching and an offense that puts up big numbers in short order. They have outscored opponents by significant margins in the vast majority of their wins. With WCWS national seed positioning very much in the picture — the top eight programs host regionals — every conference win matters.

Minnesota is not a pushover in the Big Ten this season, but Nebraska enters as a heavy favorite. A sweep would extend their conference lead even further and put the Huskers on the doorstep of something special.

If Texas Tech does claim No. 1, Canady becomes the face of the top program in the country heading into the final month of the regular season and the WCWS push. That is a storyline the sport absolutely deserves.

### STORY 7: FSU Senior Weekend vs. UNC

#### Long-Form Post — 7:00 PM ET

Florida State's season reset begins this weekend. 🥎

The Seminoles dropped sharply in the national polls after Stanford swept them in Palo Alto, ending their 25-game win streak. FSU went from a top-five program to a team answering questions about national seed viability in the span of three games. Now they host North Carolina at JoAnne Graf Field for Senior Weekend — April 17 through 19 — in what amounts to a must-win series for their postseason positioning.

After North Carolina comes rival Florida on April 22, in what will be the regular-season home finale at JoAnne Graf. Two series, six games, and almost everything riding on the outcome.

FSU entered this season as one of the preseason favorites to compete deep into the WCWS. That ceiling has not changed — but the path there requires winning games they are expected to win. A series victory over North Carolina, combined with their record everywhere else, gives the Seminoles a legitimate case for a national seed. A loss turns the conversation very difficult very quickly.

Senior Weekend starts Friday. Time to answer the questions.
