### Long-Form Post The transfer portal gets a lot of attention when the biggest names move between Power Four programs. It gets much less attention when a player sets conference records at a mid-major, wins Player of the Year, and then waits quietly for the right program to notice. Kylie Doherty is waiting. The Boston University junior outfielder had a 2026 season that should not be overlooked. She hit 27 home runs — breaking the Patriot League single-season record. She drove in 89 runs — also a Patriot League single-season record. She was named 2026 Patriot League Player of the Year. And she is still in the transfer portal. The natural question is: why? Players with this production profile typically land quickly. There are a few reasonable explanations. First, mid-major power numbers don't always translate directly. Programs that might be the best fit have to do the evaluation work of projecting how her numbers hold up against Power Four pitching. That work takes time. Second, fit matters. If Doherty is waiting for a program where she is clearly the centerpiece rather than roster piece eight in a loaded transfer class, the wait makes sense. Third, geography and scholarship timing: programs in the right fit often need their scholarship space to clear before they can make a credible offer. But here's the bottom line: 27 home runs and 89 RBIs is not a sample size problem. That is a player with real power production over a full conference season, with two years of eligibility remaining. The program that takes a serious look at Doherty and decides the fit is right is going to look smart in about 18 months. [Posting Window: 11:00 AM ET]

PGF Nationals Is 19 Days Away — What Matters at Great Park Irvine
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post If you follow college softball recruiting, July 19 is the most important date on your summer calendar. PGF Nationals opens at Great Park Irvine in just 19 days. Week one covers 18U and 16U divisions. Week two is 14U. Every major college program sends evaluators. Coaches who can only contact prospects through phone and social media during the regular academic year watch players in person, watch them handle pressure, watch them compete against the best travel programs in the country. For the Class of 2027, this is the evaluation moment. College programs that are building their 2027 roster — the first year after the last House Settlement cycle fully takes effect — are filling their final spots right now. A big PGF weekend for an uncommitted junior or senior can open doors that were closed in the spring. A struggling week can slow a recruitment that was moving forward. The recruiting contact rules are nuanced, but the bottom line is: coaches can evaluate and communicate. They are present, they are watching, and they are forming opinions on prospects that will inform decisions through August. Texas, Oklahoma, Florida, Tennessee, and virtually every other Power Four program sends full evaluation staff to PGF Nationals. The list of coaches in the stands at Great Park Irvine next week will read like a Who's Who of college softball. For prospects: this is the opportunity to show up and play your best softball. Everything else follows from there. For fans who follow recruiting: start paying attention to what happens in Irvine. The commitment lists from August through October often trace directly back to who had a big week at PGF. [Posting Window: 5:30 PM ET]
