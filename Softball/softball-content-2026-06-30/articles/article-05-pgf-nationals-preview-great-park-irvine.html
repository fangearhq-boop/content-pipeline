19 Days to PGF Nationals — What to Watch at Great Park Irvine July 19-26

By Emily Rawlings June 30, 2026

Why This Event Matters

PGF Nationals is the premier national travel softball tournament of the summer. Every July, Great Park Irvine in California becomes the most-watched patch of softball real estate in the country for about two weeks. This year, the event runs from July 19 through July 26, with the 18U and 16U age divisions competing in week one and the 14U division in week two.

The reason college fans, coaches, and recruiting followers track this event so closely comes down to a simple reality: this is where in-person evaluation happens at the highest concentration of talent. During the college season, coaches are focused on their own programs. During the dead period in late May and early June, in-person contact is restricted. PGF Nationals opens in the evaluation window with the highest concentration of elite prospects in one location — and every major college program sends coaching staff.

What Coaches Are Watching

Coaches who evaluate at PGF Nationals are not watching stat lines. They can see those online. What they are watching is how a prospect handles the pressure of a national stage, how she moves between at-bats, how she responds after an error or a strikeout, and how her mechanics hold up over multiple games in a weekend.

For pitchers, it is the fastball velocity late in a game that matters most — not just the reading on a speed gun in the first inning, but what it says after 80 pitches under summer-afternoon heat. For infielders, it is first-step quickness and footwork on routine plays under fatigue. For hitters, it is the ability to identify pitch patterns across multiple appearances against unfamiliar arms.

None of that information comes from a stat sheet or a highlight video. It comes from a coach sitting in the stands at Great Park Irvine at noon on a Tuesday in July, watching a 16-year-old make a decision on a 0-2 count.

The 2027 Recruiting Class Context

For the Class of 2027, PGF Nationals arrives as the contact period allows coaches to communicate directly with recruits and their families. Programs that have been building interest through the spring evaluation period can convert that interest into serious conversations. Recruiting decisions that have been gestating for months can accelerate when a prospect has a strong PGF showing and a coaching staff that was in the stands to see it.

The 2027 class is the first fully formed in the post-House Settlement landscape. Every program is working through how the scholarship and roster structures affect their recruiting philosophy. PGF Nationals is the first major evaluation event where those new parameters are in effect in a meaningful way — programs are evaluating with precise knowledge of what they can offer and what they need.

Programs to Watch

Texas, Oklahoma, Florida, Tennessee, and Alabama historically send full evaluation staffs to PGF Nationals. Texas Tech, Nebraska, South Carolina, and Arizona are also reliably present. The coaches who just finished rebuilding their rosters through the 2026 portal cycle are already looking two years ahead, and PGF Nationals is where they begin forming opinions on the players they will pursue through the 2025-26 academic year.

For uncommitted prospects who had strong spring seasons, this is the moment to perform in front of the coaches who matter most. A big PGF weekend for an unrecruited junior can open a door that was closed in April. A standout 16U showing can begin a recruitment that lasts the next 18 months.

What's Next

PGF Nationals runs July 19-26. Watch for commitment announcements in the weeks that follow — August and September tend to see spikes in 2027 class commitments directly traceable to summer evaluation events. Programs that had outstanding PGF weekends from a talent identification standpoint will begin making formal offers through the fall. The 2027 class, which takes full shape by next January, has its most defining moment of the summer coming in 19 days at Great Park Irvine.
