27 Home Runs, 89 RBIs, Patriot League Records — Kylie Doherty Is Still in the Transfer Portal

By Emily Rawlings June 30, 2026

The Numbers That Should Have Moved Faster

The 2026 NCAA softball transfer portal moved fast for the biggest names in the sport. Isa Torres went from Florida State to Texas. Jaysoni Beachum landed at Florida. The Zache sisters transferred together to Michigan. When a high-profile portal name became available, programs moved quickly and the announcements followed within days.

Kylie Doherty has been waiting longer. The Boston University junior outfielder is still uncommitted, and the production she put up in 2026 makes that wait worth examining.

In the 2026 season, Doherty broke Patriot League single-season records in both home runs with 27 and RBIs with 89. She was named the 2026 Patriot League Player of the Year. She is a junior with two full years of eligibility remaining at her next program. And she has not committed anywhere.

The Mid-Major Translation Question

Every mid-major power hitter who enters the transfer portal runs into the same skepticism: how do the numbers hold up against better competition? Patriot League pitching is Division I, but the level of arms at a Power Four conference school — or a mid-major program like a Big West or Sun Belt member in a stronger softball region — is meaningfully different.

That gap is real and worth accounting for. But 27 home runs is not a gap that evaporates entirely in translation. The mechanics required to drive the ball 27 times over a full season, the ability to recognize pitches and make disciplined decisions under pressure, the sustained production across a 60-plus game schedule — these are skills that travel. The question is not whether Doherty can hit at a higher level. The question is how much of a margin her production creates when the competition improves.

Programs that do the evaluation work on a player like Doherty and conclude she can produce at 70 percent of her mid-major pace are still getting an elite offensive contributor. That calculation is worth making.

Why She Might Still Be Available

Several factors can slow a portal commitment that looks like it should be fast. Scholarship timing is the most common one — programs that make the most sense as a landing spot may have needed their financial aid picture to clear before making a credible offer. In a portal cycle where Power Four programs spent their early scholarship resources on established names from high-profile programs, a mid-major Player of the Year can end up waiting for the second wave of attention.

Fit also matters. A player with two years of eligibility and record-setting production has earned the right to be selective about where she goes. If Doherty is looking for a situation where she is unambiguously the featured bat — not the fifth piece of a loaded transfer class — her patience makes sense. The right program is one that genuinely needs her, can showcase her production, and gives her a realistic path to becoming the kind of impact player whose numbers draw even more attention by the time she finishes her career.

Who Could Use Her

Any program that finished 2026 with a hole in the outfield or a need for left-handed power should be running the numbers on Doherty. Mid-majors looking to make a statement hire — a single portal addition that changes their offensive ceiling — should be in active conversations. Even programs that finished the portal cycle with a complete class but lost a player to injury or attrition between portal windows could benefit from revisiting this name.

The production is real. Two years of eligibility is real. A player who set conference records and won the league's top individual award is not a risky bet — she is an underappreciated one.

What's Next

Doherty's commitment, when it comes, will probably make the receiving program look forward-thinking rather than lucky. The programs that passed on her — or never seriously evaluated her — will be the ones asking what they missed. A summer announcement lands before fall camp begins, giving a new program time to build her into the lineup plan for 2027. The window is still open. The right call is to make one.
