Nebraska's Jordy Frahm Makes History as USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year

By Jordan Reeves June 30, 2026

A First for Nebraska Softball

The USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year has been awarded annually since 2002. In those 24 years of the award's existence, no Nebraska player had ever won it. On May 27, 2026, Jordy Frahm changed that.

Frahm is only the sixth two-way winner in the award's history — a player recognized for excelling as both a pitcher and a hitter in the same season. That combination of skills is rare in any sport. In Division I softball, where the demands of pitching a full season and contributing meaningfully with the bat pull in opposite directions, it is genuinely exceptional.

What She Did in the Circle

Frahm's pitching numbers in 2026 were nationally elite across multiple categories. She posted a 1.15 ERA, 4th best in Division I. She recorded 12 saves — the most of any pitcher in all of Division I. Her strikeout-to-walk ratio of 3.08 ranked 3rd nationally.

The saves number is worth dwelling on. Saves in college softball work differently than in professional baseball, but the underlying demand is the same: come in with runners on base, a lead in the game, pressure at its highest, and strand those runners. Do it twelve times. Do it reliably enough that your coaching staff trusts you in every high-leverage situation across a 60-game season. That is a specific and high-demand role, and Frahm led every pitcher in the country in filling it.

Her ERA of 1.15 means that over a full season of pitching, she allowed less than one and a quarter runs per seven innings. That is not a stat that happens by accident. It is the product of command, deception, and the ability to put batters away in situations where they know exactly what's coming.

What She Did in the Box

A 1.15 ERA alone would make Frahm a standout. A .848 slugging percentage makes her a historic one.

Slugging percentage at .848 means that for every at-bat Frahm had, she averaged nearly one base. That is power production at a level that most designated hitters at the Power Four level never reach. For a pitcher carrying the workload Frahm carried in the circle, it is nearly impossible to explain unless the player is genuinely exceptional in both directions.

She was.

What It Meant for Nebraska

Nebraska went 52-8 in 2026. They reached the Women's College World Series for the first time in 13 years, finishing 5th nationally. The Huskers had added reinforcements through the transfer portal — including Boutté from Ohio State — but the engine of their run was Frahm.

Programs end 13-year WCWS droughts through rebuilding efforts, roster decisions, recruiting wins, and the gradual accumulation of program culture. Nebraska did all of those things. But droughts also end when a single player emerges who is better than anyone around her on a given day — someone whose presence makes wins possible that would not have happened otherwise. In 2026, that player was Jordy Frahm.

The USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year award is the recognition that follows from that kind of season. Nebraska has it now for the first time. Frahm earned it the hard way — from both sides of the diamond.

What's Next

Nebraska returns to 2027 as a program that has proven it belongs in the conversation at the top of the sport. Frahm's award adds an individual accolade to the program's résumé. Whether she can sustain her 2026 production — and whether Nebraska can build on a 52-8 season to make another deep tournament run — will be two of the most interesting stories in college softball when the spring season begins.
