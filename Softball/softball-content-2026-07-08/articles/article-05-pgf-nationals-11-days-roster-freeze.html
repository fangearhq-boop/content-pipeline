PGF Nationals in 11 Days: What Coaches and Families Need to Know Before Thursday's Roster Freeze

By Emily Rawlings | July 8, 2026

The calendar is moving fast. PGF Nationals begin July 19 at Great Park in Irvine, California — and there are only 11 days left. But the more urgent deadline is Thursday.

The final roster freeze for PGF Nationals is July 13, 2026 at 11:59 PM PST. That's five days from today. After that, rosters are locked and no additions can be made before the biggest travel softball showcase of the summer begins at Great Park in Irvine.

For coaches finalizing lineups, for families navigating last-minute player additions, and for players who might still be working their way into a program for the event — Thursday is the hard deadline.

What's on the Line at Great Park

PGF Nationals Week One covers 18U and 16U age groups and draws some of the best travel ball programs in the country. The event at Great Park in Irvine has become one of the premier college-evaluation showcases of the summer — coaches from Power Four programs, mid-majors, and everywhere in between put it on their calendar every year.

The stakes are particularly high in 2026 because the September 1 contact window — the first date coaches can directly contact prospective recruits in the 2028 class — opens in 55 days. That means PGF Nationals is one of the last significant evaluation opportunities before the contact window opens and recruiting shifts into a new gear. College coaches who see a player at Great Park can have a conversation with her and her family starting September 1.

This isn't just a tournament. It's a résumé event for the next generation of college softball players.

The Broader Recruiting Calendar

For families trying to keep track of the timeline: the NCAA transfer portal remains open through July 31 — which means current college players can still enter and commit to new programs after PGF. The post-PGF stretch through August is the quieter evaluation season, where coaches assess showcase film, finalize board rankings, and prepare their contact lists for September 1.

The summer is short. Every showcase appearance matters. Every coach who sees a player in the circle or at the plate this month is building a file that shapes a scholarship decision.

What's Next
