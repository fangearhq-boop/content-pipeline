Hallie Burns Heads to Baylor: The Southland's Best Pitcher Is Now a Big 12 Bear

By Emily Rawlings | July 8, 2026

Baylor head coach Glenn Moore has always had an eye for pitching talent, and the addition of Hallie Burns from Southeastern Louisiana may be his best portal move yet.

Burns — the 2026 Southland Conference Pitcher of the Year — officially committed to Baylor this week, bringing a résumé that reads more like a list of national statistical achievements than a typical transfer portal entry. In 2026, she went 20-5 with a 1.68 ERA across 112.2 innings pitched. She ranked No. 1 nationally in hits per seven innings (4.10), No. 5 in WHIP (0.86), and No. 10 in ERA (1.68).

"We are excited to welcome Hallie to Baylor and add another outstanding pitcher to our staff," Moore said. "She has proven herself as one of the top pitchers in the country and has been a big part of Southeastern's success over the last couple of seasons."

Building History at Southeastern Louisiana

Burns didn't just perform at the individual level at Southeastern Louisiana — she lifted the entire program. Her 2026 season helped guide the Lions to the program's first-ever Southland Conference regular-season championship and their first NCAA Tournament appearance. In a sport where mid-major programs rarely break through to the national stage, Burns was the engine that made it possible.

She was also the second Southeastern Louisiana player to commit to Baylor this cycle, a detail that speaks to the trust both players have placed in Moore and the Waco program.

A Long Road to Waco

Burns grew up in Booneville, Mississippi — a small town not typically associated with pipeline recruiting. She was named Mississippi Player of the Year and Class 3A Pitcher of the Year at Booneville High School, earned MaxPreps All-America honors, and helped lead her team to back-to-back state softball championships. She was also an all-state basketball player, an athletic background that suggests a physicality and competitiveness that translates across sports.

Her initial commitment out of high school was to Ole Miss. That path changed, she landed at Southeastern Louisiana, and now her trajectory has taken her to the Big 12 — a conference that consistently produces the best competition in college softball.

The Big 12 Challenge

The central question around every mid-major transfer to a power conference is simple: can the numbers translate? Burns dominated mid-level competition — can she sustain a 1.68 ERA against Oklahoma, Texas, and the rest of the Big 12?

The historical answer is that elite statistical profiles tend to hold up, even if there's some regression when the competition level jumps. And Burns' profile — nationally top-ranked in WHIP, contact suppression, and ERA — suggests she's not a product of a weak schedule. She was one of the best pitchers in the country in 2026 by the numbers that matter most.

Baylor's pitching staff just got significantly more dangerous.

What's Next

Burns will join Baylor's roster for the 2026-27 season. Head coach Glenn Moore and the Bears finished their 2026 campaign ahead of the portal deadline and are now assembling a roster for a potential deep postseason run. With Burns anchoring the pitching staff, Baylor enters the preseason conversation as a team capable of competing with the best in the Big 12. Watch for fall scrimmages and preseason rankings in October as the first indication of how this addition fits into Moore's rotation plans.
