Seven Days Left: The Softball World Must Vote for Megan Grant at the ESPYs

By Jordan Reeves | July 8, 2026

There are seven voting days left — and the softball community cannot afford to let them slip by.

Megan Grant is nominated for Best Record-Breaking Performance at the 2026 ESPYs, which air Wednesday, July 15, at 8 PM ET on ABC. Fan voting is open at ESPN.com through the night of July 15, with one vote allowed per day. If you haven't voted yet, you have seven chances remaining. If you have, you have seven more.

The record that earned Grant this nomination: 42 home runs in 63 games during her 2026 senior season at UCLA, breaking the NCAA Division I single-season mark of 37 set by Arizona's Laura Espinoza in 1995. That record stood for 31 years. Grant didn't just break it — she shattered it by five home runs and finished her UCLA career as the program's all-time leader with 91 home runs, surpassing Stacey Nuveman.

The Competition — And Why It's Close

Grant is not the only accomplished athlete in this category. Her competitors include Myles Garrett, the Los Angeles Rams defensive end who set an NFL record; Johannes Høsflot Klæbo, the Norwegian cross-country skier who won a sixth Winter Games gold medal; and Sabastian Sawe, the Kenyan marathon runner who ran a sub-two-hour marathon. These are legitimate, decorated athletes by any standard.

That's exactly the point. ESPY fan voting often comes down to which sport's community shows up — and softball has shown, over and over again, that it can mobilize its fanbase when it matters. This is one of those moments. Grant's achievement is extraordinary by any athletic measure. Forty-two home runs in a single college season is a number that stops people in their tracks, regardless of sport.

She deserves to be in that conversation. She deserves to win it. But "deserving" and "winning" are different things in a fan vote.

Grant in the AUSL

Grant was the No. 4 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL College Draft, selected by the Portland Cascade. She is currently competing in the AUSL regular season, which runs through July 20, and has already been one of the Cascade's most dangerous hitters since arriving. Her transition from college to professional softball is the next chapter in a career that has been defined by exceeding expectations.

The ESPYs on July 15 will be the 34th edition of the awards, hosted by Marcello Hernández and held at the David H. Koch Theater at Lincoln Center in New York. Whether Grant is recognized that night depends in part on whether her sport shows up for her.

What's Next

Vote at ESPN.com — once per day — through July 15. The 2026 ESPYs air that same night at 8 PM ET on ABC and the ESPN app. Grant is competing in the Best Record-Breaking Performance category. Seven days. Seven votes. Make them count.
