Softball Takes the MLB All-Star Stage: Four AUSL Stars Head to Philadelphia

By Sarah Mitchell | July 8, 2026

Softball has earned a prime-time slot at the biggest week on baseball's calendar — and it's arriving in style.

Four stars from the Athletes Unlimited Softball League will anchor teams in the inaugural MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 competition on Sunday, July 12, at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. NiJaree Canady, Rachel Garcia, and Tiare Jennings of the Texas Volts, along with Alyssa Brito of the Carolina Blaze, will each lead a team in a co-ed, power-hitting showcase that follows the All-Star Futures Game.

The team pairings pair each softball player with a Philadelphia-area MLB legend: Garcia leads Team Victorino (Shane Victorino), Jennings leads Team Rollins (Jimmy Rollins), Brito leads Team Howard (Ryan Howard), and Canady leads Team Jones (Chipper Jones). The format features two semi-finals and a final, with each game running 30 minutes in a fast-paced hitting and catching competition.

What This Moment Means for the Sport

The AUSL is in just its second season of professional softball, having launched in 2025 with a six-team format that runs through July 20 before a championship series in late July. Getting four players into MLB All-Star Week — not as sideline guests, but as team captains in their own event — is a significant milestone for the league and for women's softball at the professional level.

The MLBx platform is designed to be a co-ed athletic showcase, which is why the format works: it's not a pitching competition. It highlights the athletes' power, speed, and all-around skill in a format that resonates with a baseball audience already tuned in for the Home Run Derby and Futures Game. Softball fans will know these names; baseball fans are about to learn them.

For Rachel Garcia — an Olympic silver medalist with UCLA and one of the most decorated pitchers in softball history — the Philadelphia stage adds yet another highlight to a career defined by big moments. For Tiare Jennings, a dominant offensive presence for the Volts, it's a chance to show her bat on national television. For Alyssa Brito, the Blaze's standout, it's a breakout moment before a broader audience. And for Canady — more on that separately.

Softball's Footprint at All-Star Week

All-Star Week in Philadelphia runs through July 15, with the All-Star Game itself on Tuesday, July 14. The MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 on Sunday, July 12, slots in as one of the most anticipated alternative events — a crossover that gives baseball's biggest audience a direct window into what the AUSL is building.

Citizens Bank Park seats more than 43,000 people. Four AUSL softball players will be in front of them, competing as captains and showcasing what professional softball looks like in 2026.

The event is free to All-Star Week ticketholders and will be broadcast as part of the MLB All-Star coverage.

What's Next

Sunday, July 12, is MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. Tune in to watch Canady, Garcia, Jennings, and Brito take the stage. Following All-Star Week, the AUSL regular season resumes through July 20, with the play-in game on July 23 and the AUSL Championship Series on July 25-27 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. No. 1 seed earns a direct bye to the finals — and with just 12 days of regular-season play remaining, the race for that top spot is very much alive.
