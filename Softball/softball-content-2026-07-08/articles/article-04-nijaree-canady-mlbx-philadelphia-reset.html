OG NiJa at Citizens Bank Park: Why Canady's MLBx Moment Is Bigger Than a Stats Line

By Sarah Mitchell | July 8, 2026

When NiJaree Canady takes the field at Citizens Bank Park this Sunday, she won't be carrying the ball.

She'll be in the batter's box — and that changes everything about how 40,000 fans and a national broadcast audience will experience her for the first time.

Canady is one of four Texas Volts and AUSL stars heading to MLB All-Star Week for the inaugural MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 on July 12. The format is co-ed, fast-paced, and built around power hitting and athletic catching — which means the nation's most anticipated professional softball debut gets reframed in the best possible way. Not through the lens of her 6.87 ERA. Not through the 0-3 record. As an athlete. A complete one.

The Struggles in Context

It would be dishonest to write about Canady right now without acknowledging what this AUSL season has been. She arrived as the second overall pick in the 2026 AUSL Collegiate Draft — behind only Tennessee's Karlyn Pickens — after a contract standoff that delayed her debut until June 12. When she finally took the mound, the results were difficult: 0-3 with a 6.87 ERA, 17 earned runs allowed, and seven home runs surrendered in her early outings.

The Volts called in reinforcement in the form of Tori Nyberg, her former Stanford pitching coach. Nyberg's diagnosis was mechanical: during her time at Texas Tech, injuries had shifted Canady's body positioning from the "squared up" stance toward home plate that defined her dominant Stanford years — a 0.57 ERA, two USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year awards — to an "open" position that exposed her to more hard contact. The goal is to get back to what Nyberg calls "OG NiJa," the version of Canady that led Stanford to back-to-back national championships.

That reset is underway. It takes time.

A Different Kind of Stage

MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 doesn't care about ERA. It cares about bat speed, athleticism, and competitive fire — all things Canady has in abundance. She'll anchor a team alongside Chipper Jones in a co-ed format that showcases exactly the traits that made her one of the most sought-after players in AUSL draft history.

This isn't just a PR moment. It's a reset in the court of public perception. The players who remember Canady at Stanford — the ones who know what "OG NiJa" looks like at full speed — are about to get a reminder. And the fans who only know her 2026 AUSL numbers are about to see something different.

The pitching will come around. Nyberg and the Volts believe that. Sunday, at Citizens Bank Park, the world gets to see why they believe it.

What's Next

MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 takes place Sunday, July 12, at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, immediately following the All-Star Futures Game. After All-Star Week, the AUSL regular season returns with 12 days remaining in the regular season. The Volts — currently in a difficult stretch — need wins to improve their playoff positioning before the July 20 regular-season close. If Canady's mechanics reset clicks into place in the final weeks, the Volts could become a different team by the time the play-in game arrives July 23.
