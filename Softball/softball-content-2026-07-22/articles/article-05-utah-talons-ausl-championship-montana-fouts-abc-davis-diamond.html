Utah Talons, Davis Diamond, and the ABC Broadcast: What Championship Week Looks Like

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 22, 2026

While Tuesday's play-in picture was being sorted out in Durham, the Utah Talons were waiting.

They earned the right to wait. Utah finished the 2026 AUSL regular season 16-9 — tied with the Chicago Bandits for the best record in the league — and won the head-to-head tiebreaker 4-1 to claim the No. 1 seed and a direct bye to the AUSL Championship Series. The Talons do not play Thursday. They play Friday.

The Championship Series begins July 25-27 at Davis Diamond on the Texas A&M University campus in College Station, Texas. Game 1 airs on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television. This is what the Talons are resting for.

Montana Fouts and the Defending Champions

Utah won the 2025 AUSL Championship, sweeping the Chicago Bandits in two games. A repeat in 2026 would make them the first back-to-back champions in AUSL history. They are the team everyone else is trying to beat.

Montana Fouts led the AUSL this season in three categories: innings pitched (62.1), strikeouts (63), and complete games (7). She went 7-4 with a 2.36 ERA. She set the AUSL single-game strikeout record with 12 in one outing. Fouts is the pitcher the play-in winner will have to figure out, and no team in the AUSL has solved her consistently this year.

The Talons also led the league in team ERA (2.67), fewest home runs allowed (17), and fewest earned runs (66). This is a team built around preventing runs. When Fouts is at her best, the Talons are very difficult to score on.

The Wrinkle

On July 15, the Portland Cascade beat the Utah Talons 10-2 by run rule.

If the Cascade advances Thursday — and they have reason to, having also beaten the Bandits earlier in the season — the Talons will face a team that already beat them by nine runs in a five-inning game this year. That does not define what happens in the Championship Series. Regular-season results are not postseason results. But it does mean the Talons cannot assume Thursday's play-in winner arrives in College Station without a proven ability to beat them.

The ABC broadcast changes the stakes for everyone. Game 1 on July 25 will be the most-watched professional softball game in history by the nature of the broadcast platform alone. The Talons are the defending champions. They are the favorites. They are also the team that lost to one of the two possible Championship opponents by nine runs a week ago.

What the Next 72 Hours Look Like

Thursday's play-in game determines the opponent. Friday, that team flies to College Station and prepares for Game 1 on the Texas A&M campus. The Talons have been in College Station for days. Davis Diamond is their home for the next week.

One team will win the AUSL's second championship on Saturday night, July 27. It will air on ESPN. Before that, Game 1 will make professional softball history on ABC. This is what the sport has been building toward since the league launched in 2025.

What's Next

The AUSL Play-in Game — No. 2 Chicago Bandits vs. No. 3 Portland Cascade — is Thursday, July 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond, College Station, Texas. The winner faces the Utah Talons in the best-of-three Championship Series beginning Friday, July 25. Game 1 airs on ABC. Games 2 and 3 air on ESPN.
