Maya Brady Now Owns the AUSL Home Run Record. She Set It in the Bottom of the 5th of an Elimination Loss.

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 22, 2026

On Tuesday morning in Durham, North Carolina, Maya Brady came to the plate in the bottom of the fifth inning with the Oklahoma City Spark trailing the Carolina Blaze 11-0. The Spark needed five more outs to avoid a run-rule elimination. Brady needed one more swing to set a record.

She hit it 11th home run of the 2026 AUSL season.

The previous record for home runs in a single AUSL season was nine, set by Morgan Zerkle in the inaugural 2025 season. Brady's 11th surpassed it. She is now the greatest single-season power hitter in AUSL history.

Five outs later, the Blaze closed out an 11-1 run-rule win. The Spark's season ended. Brady's stat line froze. The Triple Crown chase — she had led the league in batting average, home runs, and RBIs for much of the second half — ended with it.

The Record That Came in Defeat

The Carolina Blaze had suspended a game Monday night when they led 8-0 after two innings. The Spark-Blaze game resumed Tuesday morning and ended in five innings by run rule. By the time Brady's solo home run landed in the outfield seats, the Spark's playoff chances were already gone mathematically. The run did not matter to the outcome. The record was the only thing left.

It is an unusual kind of milestone. Most records are set in moments of triumph, in games that still have something at stake, in innings where the hit might decide something. Brady's AUSL record came in the fifth inning of a season-ending loss. The record will stand regardless. History does not footnote the circumstance.

The Bruin Race, Now Over

Brady's final home run total places her one ahead of her former UCLA teammate Megan Grant, who finished the AUSL regular season with 10 home runs on the Portland Cascade. The two-Bruin home run rivalry was one of the storylines that defined the 2026 AUSL season. Both were former Bruins. Both were among the best hitters in professional softball's newest league. They were trading the league lead throughout the season.

Brady finished first at 11. Grant finished second at 10. The difference is that Grant's season is not over. The Cascade clinched the No. 3 seed by virtue of the Blaze's win over the Spark and will play in Thursday's play-in game against the Chicago Bandits. Grant is still playing. Brady is not.

Brady's Triple Crown case had been building for weeks. She led the league in batting average, home runs, and RBIs simultaneously — a rare achievement in any professional sport. The first Triple Crown in AUSL history was within reach. It ended when the Blaze completed the run-rule in the fifth inning.

What the Season Was

The OKC Spark finished 13-12 in fourth place in their second year in the AUSL. Brady, the No. 1 overall pick in the 2025 AUSL Expansion Draft, was the most decorated individual performer on a team that fell one game short of the final playoff spot. She hit for power, average, and production in a way the league has not seen before. She set the record in the building that last held an AUSL regular-season game.

The record is hers. The season is over. Megan Grant plays on.

What's Next

Maya Brady's AUSL single-season home run record of 11 now stands as the league's all-time mark, surpassing Morgan Zerkle's 9 from the inaugural 2025 season. The Oklahoma City Spark's season ended July 22 with a final record of 13-12.
