The Play-in Matchup Changed. Here's What You Need to Know About the Actual Game.

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 22, 2026

The Oklahoma City Spark are not playing Thursday. The Portland Cascade is.

On Tuesday morning, the Carolina Blaze completed a weather-delayed game that had been suspended Monday night, and finished off the OKC Spark 11-1 by run rule. The Spark's season is over. They finished 13-12 in fourth place. By virtue of that Blaze victory, the Portland Cascade — in their first year of AUSL existence — clinched the No. 3 seed and a spot in Thursday's play-in game.

So here is the actual matchup: No. 2 Chicago Bandits (16-9) vs. No. 3 Portland Cascade (14-11). Thursday, July 23. 9 PM ET on ESPN. Davis Diamond, Texas A&M University, College Station, Texas. Win-or-go-home. The winner advances to face the Utah Talons in the AUSL Championship Series beginning July 25.

That's the correction. Now here's why it matters.

What the Cascade Brings

Portland Cascade is an expansion team. This is their first season in the AUSL. They finished 14-11, which is not a number that demands respect on its own. But the opponents they beat along the way absolutely do.

On July 12, the Cascade beat the Chicago Bandits 5-4. That win was not just a regular-season data point. It snapped the Bandits' nine-game winning streak — the longest in AUSL history. When the Cascade walked off that field in mid-July, they were the team that ended the hottest run in AUSL history.

On July 15, the Cascade beat the Utah Talons 10-2 by run rule. Utah is the No. 1 seed. The Talons are the defending AUSL champions. They led the league in ERA, strikeouts, and complete games in 2026. The Cascade beat them by nine runs in a five-inning game.

Portland Cascade has beaten every other team remaining in this AUSL postseason during the regular season. That is not a coincidence. That is a data point.

Megan Grant and the Cascade's Star Power

The Cascade's marquee name is Megan Grant. The former UCLA All-American set the Division I single-season home run record with 42 this spring. She is UCLA's all-time career home run leader with 91. She hit a 313-foot blast in her AUSL professional debut — the longest measured home run in AUSL history. This season with the Cascade, she finished with 10 home runs, the second-highest total in the league behind only Maya Brady's record 11.

Grant enters Thursday's game having already beaten the team she is about to face. She is also one home run away from tying the AUSL record that her former Bruin teammate just set in defeat on Tuesday morning.

Portland also has Tori Vidales, an infielder who played her college ball at Texas A&M. Thursday's play-in game is at Davis Diamond — on the Texas A&M campus. Vidales goes home for the biggest game of her professional career.

What Chicago Brings

The Bandits are the No. 2 seed with good reason. Both Bandits and Talons finished 16-9 — the best record in the AUSL — and Utah earned the top seed by winning the head-to-head tiebreaker. Chicago's season included the longest winning streak in league history, and they feature Lexi Kilfoyl, who held AUSL opponents to a .163 batting average during her dominant second half of the season.

But Chicago lost to Portland on July 12. That game is on the record. They know who the Cascade is. They know what Grant is capable of. They have seen this team take away their best streak.

What's Next

The AUSL Play-in Game — No. 2 Chicago Bandits vs. No. 3 Portland Cascade — is Thursday, July 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. The winner advances to face the Utah Talons in the best-of-three AUSL Championship Series beginning July 25. Game 1 of the Championship airs on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television.
