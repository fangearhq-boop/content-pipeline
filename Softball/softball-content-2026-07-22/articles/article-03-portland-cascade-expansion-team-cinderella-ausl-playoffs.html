The AUSL's Most Dangerous Expansion Team Just Made the Postseason. They Already Beat Both Other Clubs.

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 22, 2026

Portland Cascade was supposed to be the team that gained experience in its first AUSL season.

They were one of two expansion franchises added for 2026. Their roster was built through the Expansion Draft, Allocation Draft, and College Draft — a process designed to give new teams the building blocks for future competitiveness. New teams learn first. They compete for championships later.

Portland Cascade apparently did not get that memo.

On Tuesday morning, when the Carolina Blaze finished a weather-delayed game and eliminated the Oklahoma City Spark 11-1 by run rule, the Cascade clinched the No. 3 seed in the 2026 AUSL Playoffs. Thursday night at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas, they will play the No. 2 Chicago Bandits in a win-or-go-home game on ESPN.

And they have already beaten this team. And the Talons. In the same regular season.

The Two Wins That Define the Cascade's Season

On July 12, Portland Cascade traveled to face the Chicago Bandits and won 5-4. That victory snapped Chicago's nine-game winning streak — the longest in AUSL history. The Bandits had won nine consecutive games, the best run in league history, and it ended in Portland.

On July 15, the Cascade beat the Utah Talons 10-2 by run rule. Utah is the No. 1 seed, the defending champions, and the team that led the AUSL in ERA, strikeouts, and complete games in 2026. Portland did not just beat them. They beat them by eight runs in five innings.

No other team in the AUSL postseason field has wins over both the No. 1 and No. 2 seeds during the regular season. Portland Cascade does.

Who Is on This Team

Megan Grant is the name. The former UCLA Bruin set the Division I single-season home run record with 42 this spring. She holds the UCLA career record with 91. She hit the longest measured home run in AUSL history — 313 feet — in her professional debut. This season, she finished with 10 home runs, second in the entire league.

But the Cascade is not just Grant. Infielder Tori Vidales is a Texas A&M alum who played her college ball in College Station. Thursday's game is at Davis Diamond — on the Texas A&M campus. Vidales plays the biggest game of her professional career at home.

Sierra Sacco-Ferrie, who led the SEC in batting average at Mississippi State before turning professional, has been a key contributor. Kenleigh Cahalan, from Florida, and Kenzie Brown, from Arizona State, round out a roster built from some of the most competitive college programs in the sport.

What Thursday Looks Like

This is not a team asking for credit for showing up. Portland Cascade has earned its place in Thursday's game twice already — once in Bandits territory, once against the defending champions. They are not a novelty act. They are an expansion team that has demonstrated, against the actual opponents they will now face, that they can compete at this level.

The Bandits are still the No. 2 seed for a reason. They have Lexi Kilfoyl, one of the most dominant pitchers in the AUSL. They had the longest win streak in league history. They finished tied with Utah for the best record in the league.

But the Cascade already beat them.

What's Next

The AUSL Play-in Game — No. 2 Chicago Bandits vs. No. 3 Portland Cascade — is Thursday, July 23 at 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. The winner advances to face the Utah Talons in the best-of-three AUSL Championship Series beginning July 25. Game 1 of the Championship airs on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television.
