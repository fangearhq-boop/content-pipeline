### STORY 1: AUSL Play-in — Tomorrow Night, Bandits vs. Cascade

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET

The play-in game we thought we knew has a different opponent.

Yesterday's pipeline had the Oklahoma City Spark in Thursday's play-in game. That was wrong. On Tuesday morning, the Carolina Blaze completed a weather-delayed game and beat the Spark 11-1 by run rule. OKC's season is over. The Portland Cascade — clinching the final playoff spot by virtue of that Blaze win — is the No. 3 seed.

So here's the actual game:

**No. 2 Chicago Bandits (16-9) vs. No. 3 Portland Cascade (14-11). Thursday, July 23. 9 PM ET on ESPN at Davis Diamond, Texas A&M University, College Station, Texas.**

On July 12, they beat the Bandits 5-4 and snapped Chicago's AUSL-record 9-game winning streak. On July 15, they beat the No. 1 Utah Talons 10-2 by run rule. Portland Cascade has beaten every other team in this AUSL postseason bracket during the regular season.

They are in their first year of existence. They are an expansion team. And they are the most dangerous No. 3 seed this league has ever had.

The winner of Thursday's game faces the Utah Talons in the Championship Series beginning July 25. Game 1 airs on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television.

### STORY 2: PGF Nationals — Bracket Play Continues

#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET

The easy games are gone.

PGF Nationals bracket play is in its final stretch across five Southern California venues. The 300-plus teams that came in from 34 states have been whittled down through pool play and bracket rounds to the programs still standing today — and every game is a win-or-go-home game now.

The championship stakes are coming into view. The 18U Premier Championship is scheduled for Friday on ESPNU. The 16U Premier Championship is on ESPN+ the same day. The PGF All-American Games follow Saturday.

And the number worth watching: September 1 is 40 days away. That's the date when Division I college coaches can, for the first time, directly contact current high school sophomores — the Class of 2028. The phones, texts, and emails that are not yet allowed go out the moment that window opens. Every coach watching this week's bracket play is deciding who those messages go to.

If your team is still alive today, you are playing in front of exactly the people you need to impress, at exactly the right time.

### STORY 3: Portland Cascade — Cinderella Expansion Team in the Postseason

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

Portland Cascade wasn't supposed to be here.

They're an expansion team. First year in the AUSL. One of two new franchises the league added for 2026. They finished 14-11 — not a dominant record. They clinched the final playoff spot only because the Carolina Blaze eliminated the OKC Spark on Tuesday morning.

But here's what makes the Cascade's story different from the average Cinderella narrative: they didn't back into this postseason. They earned it.

On July 12, the Cascade beat the Chicago Bandits 5-4. That win snapped Chicago's nine-game winning streak — the longest in AUSL history. On July 15, the Cascade beat the Utah Talons 10-2 by run rule. Utah is the No. 1 seed, the defending champions, the team every other team in this league is measured against.

Portland Cascade beat both of them.

Thursday, they face the Bandits again. Win-or-go-home. This is Megan Grant's team — she set the NCAA single-season home run record with 42 at UCLA this spring, finished second in the AUSL with 10 home runs, and launched a 313-foot blast in her professional debut. It's also Tori Vidales' team — a Texas A&M alum who will play Thursday's game at Davis Diamond, her home stadium.

The Cascade is not just here. They are here with a case.

### STORY 4: Maya Brady Sets AUSL HR Record — in the Elimination Game

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Maya Brady hit her 11th home run of the 2026 AUSL season on Tuesday morning.

The previous record was nine, set by Morgan Zerkle in the 2025 inaugural season. Brady's 11th broke it. She is now the greatest single-season home run hitter in AUSL history.

The home run came in the bottom of the fifth inning of a game the Oklahoma City Spark were losing 11-0. The Blaze completed a weather-delayed rout, 11-1, by run rule. Brady's solo shot was the only run OKC scored. The Spark's season was over five outs after she hit it.

The Triple Crown chase that defined the second half of OKC's season — Brady had led the league in batting average, home runs, and RBIs for weeks — is over. The stats are frozen. The milestone stays unclaimed.

Brady's former UCLA teammate Megan Grant holds the No. 2 position at 10 home runs. Grant's Portland Cascade is still in the postseason. Brady is not.

The two-Bruin rivalry that made the AUSL's home run race one of the season's best storylines ended the only way it could have: one record, two very different outcomes.

### STORY 5: AUSL Championship Week — Talons, ABC, Davis Diamond

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM ET

The Utah Talons have been waiting.

While the Bandits and Cascade finish their season, while the Spark went home Tuesday morning, the Talons have been resting with the No. 1 seed and a direct bye to the AUSL Championship Series — which begins Friday, July 25, at Davis Diamond on the Texas A&M campus in College Station, Texas.

Montana Fouts led the AUSL in innings pitched (62.1), strikeouts (63), and complete games (7) this season. She went 7-4 with a 2.36 ERA and set the league's single-game strikeout record (12 Ks in one outing). This is the pitcher the play-in winner will have to face.

Utah won the 2025 AUSL Championship by sweeping the Bandits. A repeat would make them the first back-to-back champions in AUSL history.

Game 1 of the Championship Series airs on ABC on July 25. This will be the first professional softball game ever broadcast on U.S. network television. The sport has not been here before. The audience is going to find it.

One additional detail for context: on July 15, the Portland Cascade beat the Utah Talons 10-2 by run rule. If Portland advances Thursday, they will face a team they have already beaten this season. Utah is good, but they have not been unbeatable in 2026. The play-in winner will know that walking in.
