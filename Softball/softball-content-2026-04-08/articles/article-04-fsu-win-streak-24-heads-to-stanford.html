Florida State Extends Nation's Longest Win Streak to 24 — Now Heads to Stanford

By Sarah Mitchell

April 8, 2026

Florida State trailed Notre Dame 4-0 on Saturday. The Seminoles didn't panic. They don't panic anymore.

No. 10 Florida State (35-4, 12-0 ACC) scored eight unanswered runs to beat Notre Dame 8-4 on April 4, completing the sweep and extending the nation's longest active winning streak to 24 consecutive games. It's tied for the fifth-longest winning streak in FSU program history and the longest since 2018-19.

Twenty-four games. Every single one won.

The Comeback Against Notre Dame

The Notre Dame series wasn't the easiest of the streak for FSU, but the Seminoles showed exactly what makes them dangerous: they can lose a lead and get it back. Trailing by four runs, they mounted a rally that grew from a run or two to an eight-run burst that left the Fighting Irish (16-21, 5-10 ACC) without an answer.

Isa Torres, who leads all of Division I softball with a .610 batting average and a .664 on-base percentage, was characteristically excellent in the series. She nearly hit for the cycle in one of the games, a performance that added another chapter to what is becoming one of the most compelling individual seasons in recent college softball history.

FSU is now 12-0 in ACC play — the only team in the conference without a conference loss.

The Torres Factor

It's impossible to write about Florida State's 2026 season without returning, again and again, to Isa Torres.

The junior is batting .610, leading the country. Her .664 OBP ranks second nationally. She was already one of the most accomplished hitters in FSU history before this season; in 2025, she batted .436 (a FSU program record) and was a unanimous first-team All-American. In 2026, she's playing at an even higher level.

Earlier this season, Torres set an NCAA record with 16 consecutive hits between March 8 and March 21. She reached base in 23 straight plate appearances at one point, the third-longest such streak in NCAA history. She is the frontrunner for National Player of the Year and arguably the best hitter in college softball since Alo.

The West Coast Road Trip

For all the home victories and comeback wins, Florida State hasn't truly been tested on the road against top competition in several weeks. That changes this week.

The Seminoles travel west for two stops:

Wednesday, April 8: FSU plays at Santa Clara — the first-ever meeting between the two programs. It's a midweek tune-up, but every game during a 24-game win streak carries weight.

Friday-Sunday, April 10-12: FSU plays a three-game ACC series at No. 24 Stanford. The Cardinal (22-12, 9-6 ACC) play at Sunken Diamond, and it marks the first time FSU has ever played a game on The Farm. Stanford is a capable opponent in a tough road environment.

If FSU can extend the streak to 27 or beyond in Palo Alto, the national seed projection discussion will shift from "likely No. 1 seed" to "how do you keep them out of the top national seed conversation."

What's Next

Florida State has built one of the great mid-season runs in recent college softball memory. With Torres leading the offense, a shutdown pitching staff, and 12-0 ACC play, the Seminoles enter the Stanford series with everything to gain and the nation's longest active winning streak on the line. This week's road trip will tell us how good this FSU team really is.
