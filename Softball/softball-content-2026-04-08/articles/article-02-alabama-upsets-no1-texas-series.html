Alabama Takes Down No. 1 Texas in Tuscaloosa — First Series Win Over the Top Rank at Home Since 2011

By Emily Rawlings

April 8, 2026

Nobody beats No. 1 easily. Alabama made it look easy in Games 2 and 3.

The Crimson Tide took down No. 1 Texas in a three-game series at Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa over the April 2-4 weekend, clinching the series with a 7-4 win in the decisive Game 3. It marked the first time Alabama had beaten the No. 1-ranked team at home since 2011, and it reshuffled the SEC standings and national polls with immediate and significant effect.

A Series in Three Acts

Game 1 on Thursday looked like exactly what you'd expect from a matchup between the country's top-ranked team and a program in fourth. Texas rolled to a 9-1 win, looking every bit the nation's best. The Longhorns scored early and often, and Alabama's pitching staff had no answers.

But Rhoads Stadium has a way of turning things around.

Game 2 on Friday was an altogether different story. Alabama's offense erupted for 11 runs in an 11-4 blowout, scoring in multiple innings and forcing the Longhorns into a must-win Game 3. It was the kind of performance that reminded everyone why Alabama entered the series ranked No. 4 nationally with a 33-2 record.

Game 3 on Saturday was the defining moment. Alabama won 7-4 to claim the series 2-1 — and with it, a piece of history that had been waiting since 2011.

The Poll Fallout

The aftermath of the Alabama series was immediate. Texas dropped from No. 1 in all four major polls for the first time in weeks. The Longhorns entered the week as the consensus top team in the country; they left Tuscaloosa ranked No. 4 or lower depending on which poll you consulted.

Alabama, meanwhile, climbed to No. 3 in the NFCA Coaches Poll — the program's highest ranking of the 2026 season. The series win validated the case that head coach Patrick Murphy's program was not merely a contender, but a genuine threat to win the national title.

Into the vacuum left by Texas's fall stepped Texas Tech, which returned to No. 1 in three of the four major polls. Oklahoma held at No. 2 in the NFCA rankings, buoyed by its own three-game sweep of Kentucky over the same weekend.

SEC Implications

The standings math was unambiguous after the weekend. Oklahoma (11-1 in SEC play) moved to a two-game lead over both Texas and Alabama (each at 9-3). For Texas, the series loss was costly — not just in the standings but in the narrative.

The Longhorns had spent much of the 2026 season as the consensus best team in the country. After losing their 29-game win streak to Texas A&M in late March and now dropping a home series at Alabama, Texas enters the back stretch of the conference schedule needing to win out to have any realistic shot at the SEC regular season title.

They start that bid at home. Against Oklahoma. On Friday.

What's Next

Texas hosts Oklahoma in the Red River Showdown beginning April 10 at Red and Charline McCombs Field in Austin. Oklahoma enters the series two games ahead of Texas in the SEC standings. A Texas sweep would cut the deficit to nothing; an Oklahoma sweep would effectively end the race. It's the biggest series in college softball this week — and possibly this month.

Alabama, meanwhile, returns home to face the rest of its schedule as a proven giant-killer. The Tide have already beaten one No. 1 team on their home field. The question now is what they do with the momentum.
