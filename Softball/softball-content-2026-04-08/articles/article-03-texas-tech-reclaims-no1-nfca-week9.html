Texas Tech Reclaims No. 1 in Three Polls After Texas's Fall — Canady's 1,000 Career K's Cap the Week

By Jordan Reeves

April 8, 2026

When No. 1 falls, someone has to step up. Texas Tech was already waiting.

The NFCA released its Week 9 Coaches Poll on April 7, and the Red Raiders (38-2) returned to the top spot in three of the four major national polls — NFCA, D1Softball, and ESPN/USA Softball. Only Softball America kept Texas Tech at No. 2. It's the latest chapter in a back-and-forth between Tech and Texas for the sport's top billing, one that started when the two programs emerged as the nation's clear class early in the 2026 season.

How Texas Tech Got Here

The Red Raiders haven't just been winning — they've been dominating. Texas Tech is 38-2 on the season with 23 run-rule victories, meaning more than half their wins haven't even required a full nine innings. They're riding a 13-game win streak entering this week, the second-longest active streak in the country behind Florida State's 24.

The engine driving that dominance is a two-pitcher rotation that most programs would take just one half of. Ace NiJaree Canady reached 1,000 career strikeouts on April 2 against BYU, striking out 11 Cougars in an 11-1 run-rule win. She became just the 102nd pitcher in DI history to reach the milestone. She also did something that had never been done at Texas Tech: she threw her third career no-hitter, making her the first pitcher in Red Raiders program history to accomplish the feat three times.

Canady compiled 555 strikeouts over two seasons at Stanford before adding to that total since transferring to Lubbock. She is, by most measures, the best pitcher in the country right now — and one of the most decorated in college softball history.

The Rest of the Week 9 Poll

The full Week 9 NFCA rankings reflected the seismic weekend results. Oklahoma held at No. 2 after sweeping Kentucky. Alabama jumped into the top three following its series win over Texas. Florida State cracked the top 10 for the first time in 2026 at No. 10, the recognition of a 24-game winning streak and dominant ACC play.

Two notable movers: Duke jumped five spots to No. 18 after a strong week of ACC play, and UCF returned to the rankings at No. 25 after winning their Big 12 series against Utah.

Texas dropped significantly after its series loss at Alabama but remained in the top tier of the rankings. The Longhorns' résumé still carries significant weight — they spent weeks at No. 1 and have been one of the three or four best programs in the country all season.

What's Left for the Red Raiders

Texas Tech has a case for a No. 1 national seed in the NCAA Tournament — but they haven't quite closed it yet. Canady and Kaitlyn Terry (batting .525, 4th nationally) are two of the best players in the sport. The team's 38-2 record is among the best in the country. Their run-rule percentage is extraordinary.

What they still need: continued wins in Big 12 play and, ideally, at least one more signature win over a top-ten opponent to cement the RPI case for a No. 1 seed.

What's Next

Texas Tech continues Big 12 play this week with another home series. Meanwhile, every eye in college softball will be on Love's Field in Norman on Friday, where Oklahoma hosts Texas in what may be the most consequential series of the regular season. The Red Raiders can only watch — and keep winning — as the standings around them get sorted out.
