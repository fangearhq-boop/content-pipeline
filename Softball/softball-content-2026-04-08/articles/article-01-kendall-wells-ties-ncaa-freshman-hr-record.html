Kendall Wells Ties the NCAA Freshman HR Record at 30 — And She Did It Faster Than Anyone

By Sarah Mitchell

April 8, 2026

Three Oklahoma legends. One Hawaii pioneer. And now, one freshman from Texas.

Kendall Wells joined the most exclusive club in college softball history on April 4, launching her 30th home run of the season against Kentucky to tie the NCAA single-season freshman home run record. In doing so, she became just the fourth freshman in Division I history to reach the mark — joining Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma, 2018), Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma, 2012), and Kelly Majam (Hawaii, 2010).

She did it in 41 games. That's fewer games than any of the three previous record-holders needed to reach the same milestone.

The Record-Tying Moment

Wells connected on a three-run shot in the fourth inning of Oklahoma's 12-2 run-rule win over Kentucky at Love's Field in Norman. The home run was vintage Wells — a towering drive that landed deep in the outfield and brought the crowd to its feet before she had even rounded first base.

Oklahoma swept Kentucky in all three games of the series, winning by run rule in two of them. The Sooners improved to 38-3 on the season and 11-1 in SEC play — the best record in the conference by two full games.

But the only number anyone was talking about was 30.

What 30 HRs Actually Means

To appreciate where Wells stands, it helps to understand who she's joining. Jocelyn Alo finished her freshman season at Oklahoma in 2018 with 30 home runs, setting the standard that held for over six years. Lauren Chamberlain reached 30 as a freshman back in 2012. Majam did it at Hawaii in 2010.

All three of them played more games to get there. Wells did it in 41 games.

Her full stat line entering this week reads like a first-round draft prospect's peak season: .384 batting average, 57 RBI, a 1.572 OPS that leads the nation. She has zero errors in the field. Head coach Patty Gasso called her "something new, something different" earlier this season — and that was before No. 30.

The Chase for History

The freshman record is tied. But Wells isn't done.

Ahead of her lies a bigger mountain: Laura Espinoza's all-time DI single-season home run record of 37, set at Arizona in 1995. Wells needs seven more home runs to break it. Oklahoma still has games remaining in the regular season, a conference tournament, and — almost certainly — a deep NCAA Tournament run.

At her current pace of roughly one home run every 1.4 games, Wells could approach Espinoza's record before the regular season even ends. No freshman in the sport's history has ever threatened the all-time record during the same season they tied the freshman mark. Wells isn't just doing something remarkable — she's doing something unprecedented.

What's Next

Oklahoma hosts Texas in a blockbuster three-game SEC series beginning Friday, April 10, at Love's Field. The Longhorns enter the series at 9-3 in conference play — two games behind the Sooners in the standings. Every Oklahoma game from here forward is a watch event.

Kendall Wells has 30 home runs and a record tied. The question now isn't whether she'll break the all-time mark — it's when.
