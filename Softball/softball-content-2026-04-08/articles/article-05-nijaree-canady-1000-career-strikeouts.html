NiJaree Canady Reaches 1,000 Career Strikeouts — And She's the First Texas Tech Pitcher to Throw 3 No-Hitters

By Emily Rawlings

April 8, 2026

NiJaree Canady arrived at Stanford as a highly recruited prospect with a devastating rise ball and a reputation for strikeouts. She leaves college softball with records stacked in two states.

On April 2, Canady became the 102nd pitcher in NCAA Division I softball history to reach 1,000 career strikeouts, recording the milestone during Texas Tech's 11-1 run-rule win over BYU. She struck out 11 Cougars in that game alone — vintage Canady, getting through a lineup three times and leaving them guessing every time.

The Journey to 1,000

Canady spent two seasons at Stanford before transferring to Texas Tech, where she has elevated an already elite Red Raiders program into a national championship contender. Her 555 strikeouts as a Cardinal were among the best in program history. Since arriving in Lubbock, she has added substantially to that total.

Reaching 1,000 career strikeouts is rare. Only 101 pitchers before Canady had accomplished it in Division I softball history. The milestone places her in a category with some of the greatest pitchers the sport has produced. For a pitcher who won't finish college for another two seasons, the number feels like a waypoint rather than a destination.

Three No-Hitters — A Texas Tech First

Alongside the strikeout milestone, Canady did something that has never been done before in Texas Tech softball history: she threw her third career no-hitter, making her the first pitcher in the program's history to accomplish the feat three times in a career.

No-hitters are rare enough at the Division I level — they require not just elite pitching but also flawless fielding behind the pitcher for a full seven innings. Throwing one in a career is an accomplishment. Throwing three makes a legacy.

The Red Raiders are 38-2 on the season, and Canady's three no-hitters are three of the reasons why. Combined with Kaitlyn Terry — who is batting .525 and ranks 4th nationally — Texas Tech has the most dangerous two-player combination in college softball right now.

The NIL Context

Canady's production comes with a high-profile off-field story as well. She is widely reported as the highest-paid NIL athlete in college softball, with a deal that reflects her status as the sport's most marketable player. The investment has been validated by results: Texas Tech has won at least one national title during her time at the program and enters 2026 as a legitimate contender for another.

For a sophomore who transferred across the country and entered a new conference, the adjustment has been seamless. The Red Raiders play in front of capacity crowds at Rip Griffin Park, and Canady has given them every reason to keep showing up.

What's Next

With 1,000 career strikeouts behind her and Texas Tech back at No. 1 nationally in three of four major polls, Canady's focus returns to conference play and the path toward a national seed. Her 2026 season stats — a sub-2.00 ERA and a dominant strikeout-per-inning rate — continue to build the case that she's the best pitcher in the country. Three no-hitters. 1,000 career strikeouts. And she's still playing.
