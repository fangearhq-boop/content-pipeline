Florida Heads to Stetson for a Midweek Reset After Tough Arkansas Series

By I Love Softball | April 1, 2026

College softball has a word for games like Wednesday night's Florida at Stetson matchup: necessary.

No. 5 Florida (33-4) travels to DeLand, Florida to face Stetson at 6 PM ET — a non-conference midweek game that, on paper, should be a comfortable win. But paper and reality have felt disconnected in Gainesville lately. The Gators just dropped two games in a series at Arkansas, including a walk-off three-run home run in the series finale, and the Week 8 NFCA poll punished them for it. Florida dropped from No. 3 to No. 5.

Tonight is not about reclaiming a ranking. It's about something more basic: can Florida remember what it looks like to play Gator softball?

What Went Wrong at Arkansas

The Arkansas series was Florida's worst stretch of the 2026 season. Losing two games in Fayetteville to the Razorbacks — who have been one of the more dangerous mid-tier SEC teams all year — was a surprise. The walk-off moment, Arkansas outfielder Dakota Kennedy's three-run home run in the series finale, was the kind of gut-punch loss that lingers.

Florida fell to 9-3 in SEC play — still very much in the conference title race, but now two full games behind Texas and Oklahoma at the top of the standings. There is no margin left for error if the Gators want to close that gap.

The Case for Keeping Faith in Florida

Context matters. Florida at 33-4 is still one of the best teams in the country. They have the pitching depth, the lineup, and the program pedigree to put a bad week behind them. A single rough series against a motivated team in Fayetteville does not define a season. What defines a season is the response.

Programs that win national championships are built to weather weeks like this. The question is whether Wednesday night in DeLand is the first step of a correction — or the beginning of a longer slide.

What's Next

After tonight's midweek game, Florida's attention will turn to their next conference series — where the stakes will be even higher. A win at Stetson tonight resets the emotional math, gets a pitcher extra work in a low-pressure environment, and sends the Gators into the weekend with a "W" in their pocket. That matters more than it sounds.

First pitch from DeLand is at 6 PM ET. Watch carefully. This is a team that still has everything it needs to make a deep postseason run — they just need to show it.
