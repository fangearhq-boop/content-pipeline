No. 1 Texas at No. 4 Alabama: The SEC Series That Could Define the 2026 Season

By I Love Softball | April 1, 2026

When the 2026 SEC softball schedule was released last September, one series stood out above all others: No. 1 Texas at Alabama, April 2-4, Tuscaloosa. That series begins tomorrow night at Rhoads Stadium — and the stakes have only grown larger since the calendar was set.

No. 1 Texas (31-2) arrives as the defending national champion, still the top team in the country, but with a fresh wound. The 29-game win streak they had built was snapped last Sunday when Texas A&M beat the Longhorns 9-7 in the series finale in Austin. Texas won the series 2-1, but the aura of invincibility they carried into Week 8 is gone. How they respond in Tuscaloosa will say everything about this team's championship pedigree.

Schedule and How to Watch

All three games are on national television:

  • Game 1: Thursday, April 2 — 6 PM CT on SEC Network+
  • Game 2: Friday, April 3 — 7:30 PM CT on SEC Network
  • Game 3: Saturday, April 4 — 5 PM CT on ESPN

Saturday's game on ESPN is the capstone — a prime-time stage for a matchup that has earned it.

The Teams

Texas head coach Mike White is in his eighth season with the Longhorns, holding a 345-94-2 (.785) overall record. The program won its first Women's College World Series title in 2025, finishing that season at 56-12. The Longhorns are 8-1 in SEC play entering this series and have been the standard by which every other team in the country has measured itself.

Alabama head coach Patrick Murphy is entering his 30th season leading the Crimson Tide. Last week, Alabama posted a dominant statement: four non-conference wins, a combined margin of 42-1, three shutouts. The Crimson Tide are 33-2 on the season. They have been quietly building a case for why they belong in the national title conversation, and now they get to make that case in front of a packed Rhoads Stadium with ESPN watching on Saturday.

History Favors Alabama

The all-time series between these two programs sits at 7-2 in Alabama's favor, though the head-to-head games predate their time as SEC conference opponents — this will be the first time Texas and Alabama meet as league rivals. That adds a layer to every pitch and every at-bat. Neither program has scouted the other in a high-stakes conference context before, and the tendencies that matter most in a three-game series are being evaluated in real time.

There is also a telling piece of recent history: last season, Alabama hosted then-defending champion Oklahoma at Rhoads Stadium and beat them two games to one. Murphy's program has shown it can deliver when the national spotlight is on. Texas would do well not to underestimate what they're walking into.

What's Next

The winner of this series will enter the final weeks of the regular season with a commanding position in the SEC standings and a powerful argument for a top national seed. The loser will face a more challenging path to Oklahoma City. With the NFCA poll tightening — Texas Tech is just nine points behind Texas at No. 2 — both programs know a loss here carries real consequences.
