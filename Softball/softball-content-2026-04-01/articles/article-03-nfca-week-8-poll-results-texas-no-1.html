NFCA Week 8 Poll: Texas Holds No. 1 as Florida State Rises to No. 6

By I Love Softball | April 1, 2026

The NFCA/GoRout Division I Top 25 Coaches Poll for Week 8 is final — and the coaches voted to keep Texas at No. 1.

That decision was not guaranteed. Texas (31-2) lost Sunday when Texas A&M beat the Longhorns 9-7 at McCombs Field, snapping a 29-game win streak. In another era, another sport, or on a different day, that might have been enough to knock them from the top spot. Instead, the coaching panel considered the full picture — Texas won the series 2-1, is 8-1 in SEC play, and is the defending national champion — and kept the Longhorns in the top position.

The margin, though, has never been thinner.

The Full Top 6

Texas held No. 1 with Texas Tech (34-2) in very close pursuit at No. 2 — just nine points separate the two programs. Texas Tech recorded four more run-rule wins last week (23 on the season total), outscoring Tarleton State and Iowa State by a combined 48 runs, and also set a single-season program record for home runs at 75. The Red Raiders have been as dominant as anyone — and the gap at the top has never been as tight as it is right now.

Oklahoma (34-3, now 35-3 after Tuesday's Wichita State win) sits at No. 3. Alabama (33-2) is No. 4 after a week in which they dominated four non-conference opponents 42-1. Florida (33-4) dropped two spots to No. 5 after losing two games in the Arkansas series. Florida State elevated to No. 6 — their highest ranking of the season — on the strength of their 21-game win streak and a sweep at Clemson that also gave coach Lonni Alameda her 1,000th career victory.

Elsewhere in the poll, Nebraska won a series against UCLA (capturing the finale 8-4), ending UCLA's 22-game win streak. The Bruins dropped to No. 10. Virginia (31-3) rose to No. 12, just behind in-state and ACC rival Virginia Tech (31-4) at No. 11.

What the Poll Means

The story beneath the rankings is Texas Tech. Nine points is nothing — a handful of coaches changing their top-five order could flip the No. 1 spot. The Red Raiders have a stronger resume in some respects (23 run-rule wins, no bad losses), and if Texas loses this weekend at Alabama, the Week 9 poll will force a real reckoning.

The other storyline is Florida State's climb. The Seminoles have been one of the best teams in the country for weeks — 9-0 in ACC play, 21-game win streak, Torres hitting .655 — and they're finally getting the poll recognition to match. At No. 6, they're a few weeks away from potentially pushing into the top four if the teams above them stumble.

The NFCA Week 9 poll will be released on Tuesday, April 7 — after three days of Texas-Alabama and Oklahoma-Kentucky results have reshaped everything.

What's Next

The biggest test of the poll's accuracy comes this weekend. No. 1 Texas plays at No. 4 Alabama (Thursday through Saturday, Saturday on ESPN). No. 3 Oklahoma hosts Kentucky at Love's Field (Thursday through Saturday). Whatever the results, the Week 9 poll — dropping Tuesday, April 7 — will be the most consequential rankings release of the season.
