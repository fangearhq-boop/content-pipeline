Oklahoma Returns Home to Host Kentucky With Wells Chasing History at Love's Field

By I Love Softball | April 1, 2026

After a seven-game road trip that ended with Kendall Wells breaking the SEC freshman home run record Tuesday night, No. 3 Oklahoma comes home. And the timing could not be better.

The Sooners (35-3) open a three-game SEC home series against Kentucky on Thursday at Love's Field in Norman, with first pitch at 6:30 PM. It's the first home series since late March, and it comes at the peak moment of one of the most compelling individual storylines in college softball history: Wells at 27 home runs, three away from the NCAA freshman record.

The Wells Effect

Wells broke the SEC single-season freshman home run record Tuesday in the Sooners' 12-3 run-rule win over Wichita State. The opposite-field shot in the sixth inning was vintage Wells — pure power, opposite way, with runners on base and the game already in hand. Isabela Emerling followed with a three-run shot (her 13th) to end it in six innings. Oklahoma hit four home runs total in the game, pushing their season total to 137 through 38 games.

Now Wells needs three more to tie the NCAA freshman record of 30 — shared by Jocelyn Alo, Lauren Chamberlain, and Hawaii's Kelly Majam. She'll chase that mark at home in front of Norman fans who have been following every at-bat from afar during the road stretch.

About Kentucky

Kentucky is led by 19th-year head coach Rachel Lawson and holds the honor of hosting the 2026 SEC Tournament later this season — a distinction that gives the Wildcats extra motivation to perform well in front of conference opponents. The last time Kentucky visited Love's Field was 2023, when Oklahoma won both games of that series by margins of 18-0 and 7-1.

Kentucky has earned its place in the SEC and is not a program to be dismissed. But facing Oklahoma at Love's Field, with Wells swinging for history and the Sooners fresh off a record-breaking performance, is about as difficult a road assignment as a mid-tier SEC team can draw.

SEC Standings Context

Oklahoma enters at 8-1 in SEC play. Three wins this weekend would push the Sooners to 11-1 in conference play — keeping maximum pressure on Texas regardless of the outcome in Tuscaloosa. Whatever happens at Alabama-Texas, Oklahoma can control its own destiny in the SEC title race by handling business at home.

What's Next

Thursday at 6:30 PM at Love's Field is where the next chapter of the Wells record chase gets written. If she hits one this week — or two — the NCAA freshman record will be within reach at exactly the moment when attention is highest. The Kentucky series runs through Saturday. Home crowds, home energy, and history on the line.
