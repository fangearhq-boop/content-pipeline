Kendall Wells Sets SEC Freshman HR Record With Her 27th Home Run of 2026

By I Love Softball | April 1, 2026

She needed one swing to rewrite the SEC record book. Oklahoma freshman Kendall Wells delivered it Tuesday night at Love's Field in Norman — an opposite-field home run in the sixth inning that landed in the right-field seats and made her the SEC's all-time leader in single-season freshman home runs.

It was HR No. 27. She did it in 37 collegiate games.

Wells' record-breaking shot came with runners on base in the sixth inning of the Sooners' 12-3 run-rule win over Wichita State. The drive down the right-field line put Oklahoma up 9-3. Isabela Emerling followed with a three-run home run — her 13th of the season — to end the game in six innings and push the Sooners' overall record to 35-3.

By the Numbers

The volume of what Wells is doing this season strains belief. She is hitting .384 with 57 RBI and a 1.572 OPS. She has a 1.107 slugging percentage. She is a catcher who has committed zero errors in her defensive chances. And she is the most dangerous power hitter in college softball — as a first-year player.

Oklahoma hit four home runs Tuesday, with Wells joined by Kasidi Pickering, Ailana Agbayani, and Emerling. The Sooners' season home run total reached 137 through 38 games — a number that leads the nation and puts them well clear of any other program in recent memory.

The SEC single-season freshman record had stood as a marker of the conference's power-hitting tradition. Wells erased it Tuesday in the 37th game of her career.

The NCAA Record Is Next

With the SEC mark in hand, the next target is clear: the NCAA freshman home run record of 30, shared among three celebrated Oklahoma sluggers — Jocelyn Alo, Lauren Chamberlain, and Hawaii's Kelly Majam. Wells needs three more to join that list.

To appreciate the context: Alo's record season came with her being one of the most hyped recruits in college softball history. Chamberlain defined an era at Oklahoma. Majam broke the mold for Hawaiian talent in a nationally elite program. All three hit 30 home runs in a freshman season — and none of them did it while playing catcher.

Oklahoma head coach Patty Gasso has coached Alo. She's coached Chamberlain. She has seen two of the three players who hold the record. When asked about Wells, Gasso offered the highest possible assessment: "Offensively, I've seen Jocelyn Alo. I've seen Lauren Chamberlain. This is something new, this is different. It is some of the most elite power I've ever seen from a young player and she came in like that."

Wells, characteristically, refuses to focus on the milestones. "It's a little surreal," she said. "I don't really look at the stats that much... If I try to hit home runs it's not gonna happen. So I try to push that off to the side."

What's Next

Oklahoma returns to conference play Thursday when it opens a home SEC series against Kentucky at Love's Field. Game 1 is at 6:30 PM, giving Norman fans their first look at Wells in person since the record was broken. With three more home runs needed to tie the NCAA freshman mark — and a schedule that still includes a full conference stretch and the postseason — every Wells at-bat carries the weight of history.

The Sooners are 35-3. They are No. 3 in the nation. And their freshman catcher is three home runs away from college softball immortality.
