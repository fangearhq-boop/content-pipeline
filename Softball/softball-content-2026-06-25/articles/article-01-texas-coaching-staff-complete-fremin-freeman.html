Texas Completes Its 2027 Coaching Staff: Rick Fremin and Amber Freeman Are In

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 25, 2026

When Ehren Earleywine left for Tennessee and Kristen Zaleski departed for Utah State, the task ahead of Mike White was clear: fill two vacancies on a coaching staff that had just won back-to-back national championships, and do it quickly enough to be ready for fall camp in August.

He got it done.

Texas announced Rick Fremin as Associate Head Coach on June 18. Amber Freeman was announced as hitting coach on June 24. Both vacancies filled. Both hires accomplished before the summer's first practice session. The most loaded roster in college softball history now has the coaching staff to match.

Who Is Rick Fremin?

The straightforward answer: one of the most accomplished coaches in program history to ever take an assistant role at a major program.

Fremin spent ten seasons as head coach at Southeastern Louisiana University, compiling a 348-197 record with a .638 winning percentage. He won Louisiana Coach of the Year three consecutive times — 2024, 2025, and 2026. In 2026, he led SLU to a 46-16 overall record, a 24-3 conference mark, a Southland Conference regular season title, and a third straight NCAA Tournament appearance.

That is the resume of a man who was building something special in Hammond — and chose to walk away from it to compete for national championships in Austin.

The decision says everything about the current state of Texas softball. When a head coach with 21 years of experience and a program on the rise takes an associate head coach position elsewhere, it means the destination program is operating at an entirely different level.

Who Is Amber Freeman?

Freeman brings two seasons of hitting coach experience from the University of Arizona, where she built one of the Big 12's most productive offenses.

In 2025, Freeman's Arizona lineup posted a .348 batting average — the best in the Big 12 — and led the conference in home runs, on-base percentage, RBIs, runs scored, and slugging percentage. She developed two Big 12 Players of the Year during her tenure: catcher Sydney Stewart and utility player Devyn Netz.

In 2026, she coached Arizona to a .340 batting average, third in the Big 12 and tenth in program single-season history.

That is the kind of offensive track record that Texas — a team that hit 99 home runs in 2026 with Katie Stewart setting a program record at 31 — needed to preserve in its coaching transition.

What This Means for the Three-Peat Bid

Coaching staff construction matters even when you have the best players in the country. Chemistry in the dugout, offensive philosophy, pitching development, and in-game decision-making all flow through the coaching staff. When Texas lost Earleywine and Zaleski, it created a real question about whether the program could maintain its culture while making such significant changes.

The Fremin and Freeman hires answer that question directly. White found an experienced strategist in Fremin and an elite offensive developer in Freeman — the two roles most critical to replace after the departures.

Pattie Ruth Taylor remains on the pitching staff, providing continuity in the most important area of the roster: Teagan Kavan's development and the overall pitching infrastructure that has powered two championship runs.

The full picture now in view: Kavan at ace. Torres, Stewart, and Swan in the lineup. Madi Doty and a top national recruiting class adding depth. Fremin and Freeman in the dugout beside White. Every piece assembled before August.

What's Next

Fall practice for Texas begins in August, giving the new coaching staff its first opportunity to work with the full 2027 roster in a team setting. The integration of Torres, Swan, and the incoming recruiting class will be the primary storyline to watch. Preseason polls — which will almost certainly place Texas at No. 1 — come out in January. The three-peat bid officially begins in the season opener next February.

The coaching staff is set. The roster is historic. The only thing left is to play the games.
