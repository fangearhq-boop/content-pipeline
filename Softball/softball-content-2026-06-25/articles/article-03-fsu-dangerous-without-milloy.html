Stop Counting Out FSU Softball — The Seminoles Are More Dangerous Than You Think

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | June 25, 2026

The narrative around Florida State softball this summer has been one of losses. Isa Torres to Texas. Jaysoni Beachum to Florida. Two of the most coveted transfers in the entire portal window — both of them former Seminoles — leaving for rival programs.

The story is accurate. The conclusion being drawn from it is not.

FSU entering 2027 as a program on the decline is the wrong read. What the Seminoles have assembled, even without those two players, is a roster capable of winning 40-plus games and making a legitimate run in the NCAA Tournament.

Jazzy Francik Is Still Here

Every conversation about FSU's 2027 outlook starts the same place: Jazzy Francik is coming back.

Francik won the 2026 ACC Pitcher of the Year award. She is a bonafide ace — the kind of starting pitcher around whom a national championship team can be built. When your No. 1 starter is that caliber, the margin for error everywhere else on the roster grows considerably. Programs win national titles with elite aces and functional offenses. They do not need elite aces and elite offenses simultaneously.

FSU has that anchor. The question was always whether they could build a functional offense around it — and the answer, looking at the full picture, is yes.

The Math Is Better Than the Headlines

Here is the fact that gets lost in the Torres-and-Beachum narrative: Florida State added more home run power through the portal than it lost.

Torres hit 16 home runs for FSU in 2026 before transferring to Texas. Beachum hit 16 home runs before committing to Florida. Combined: 32 home runs of departing power.

Ella Dodge committed to FSU from Tennessee, where she hit 23 home runs. Nicole Edmiaston committed from Stetson, where she hit 11. Combined: 34 home runs of arriving power.

The math does not tell the full story — Torres in particular was a transcendent player who contributed more than her home run total — but it corrects the assumption that FSU simply lost power and gained nothing. Dodge and Edmiaston add a combined 34 home runs to a lineup that still has Ashtyn Danley and additional returning players.

Tia Milloy Is Still Available

As of June 25, Oklahoma transfer Tia Milloy has not committed anywhere. Milloy — who hit .353 with a .706 slugging percentage at OU over two seasons — has two years of eligibility remaining. She entered the transfer portal following Oklahoma's Super Regional exit, but did not commit during the June 8-22 window.

The formal portal window is closed, but Milloy did not use it, which means she can continue to be pursued and can still commit to a program outside of the window. Florida State remains in contact. If Milloy commits to the Seminoles, the 2027 roster takes on an entirely different complexion.

That is not guaranteed. But it is possible. And in the meantime, FSU does not need Milloy to be a dangerous team.

What's Next

The Milloy situation remains worth monitoring as June turns to July. An FSU announcement would be significant news. Absent that, the Seminoles enter fall practice in August with Francik at ace, 34 combined portal home runs in their lineup, and Danley providing two-way value. Watch where FSU lands in the first preseason polls in January — a top-12 ranking would validate the roster construction. Programs built on elite pitching survive transfer portal losses. The Seminoles have elite pitching. Their 2027 story isn't finished being written.
