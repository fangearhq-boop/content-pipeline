The 2026 Transfer Portal Is Over — Here's Who Won

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 25, 2026

The 2026 transfer portal window ran June 8 through June 22. Fifteen days. More than 130 players entered. More than 103 committed — a commitment rate of approximately 79 percent. Rosters were overhauled, programs rebuilt, and the entire 2027 landscape was redrawn.

Now that every move has been made — or declined — it's time to score it.

No. 1: Texas

Texas entered the portal as the back-to-back defending champion. It exited with the player of the year.

Isa Torres was the most coveted transfer in the 2026 window. The D1Softball Player of the Year, ACC Player of the Year, and ACC Defensive Player of the Year hit .530 with a .978 slugging percentage, a .591 on-base percentage, and 16 home runs for Florida State in 2026. She set an NCAA record with hits in 16 consecutive at-bats. And she's from Georgetown, Texas — a homecoming that made the commitment feel inevitable once she entered the portal.

Layer Torres onto what was already there: Katie Stewart (31 HRs, Texas program record), Teagan Kavan (two-time WCWS Most Outstanding Pitcher), Samantha Swan (All-Big 12 catcher from Arizona State, .383/12 HR), a top national recruiting class featuring pitcher Madi Doty, and now a fully rebuilt coaching staff. Texas won the portal not because of a single move, but because every move made an already elite roster better.

No. 2: Texas Tech

If the portal were judged purely on incoming class quality, Texas Tech might be the winner. Softball America ranked the Red Raiders' 2026 class No. 1 nationally — three All-Americans in one window.

Kenna Polar arrives with a .555 batting average from her previous program. Jayden Heavener, a pitcher from LSU, went 13-9 with a 2.81 ERA. Kasidi Pickering was ranked the No. 2 portal player by On3, hitting .370 with 20 home runs at Oklahoma before making the move to Lubbock. Add Maestretti and Bernard, and the 61-8 WCWS runner-up added depth and talent at every level.

The difference between Texas and Texas Tech in these rankings is not the class itself — it's what the class is being added to. The Red Raiders are building toward a title. The Longhorns are defending two.

No. 3: Florida

Tim Walton made one move. It was the right one.

Jaysoni Beachum was ranked the No. 1 transfer in the entire 2026 portal by On3. She hit .417 with 16 home runs, 66 RBIs, and a .549 on-base percentage. She's a Tallahassee-area native who committed to Florida State's rival — a storyline that gave the entire announcement extra weight. Walton also added Duke transfer Kairi Rodriguez. The Gators hadn't been in the national title conversation for years. They are now.

No. 4: Nebraska

Nebraska's portal strategy was simple: don't need it. The Cornhuskers went 51-6 in 2026, reached the WCWS semifinals for the first time since 2013, and return the core of that squad minus Jordy Frahm. Frahm's departure is enormous — she was the USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year — but Nebraska's 51-win infrastructure doesn't evaporate with one transfer graduation. Head coach Rhonda Revelle-Deifel built something durable. The Cornhuskers ranked here because sustainability matters as much as off-season flash.

No. 5: South Carolina

The Gamecocks have been quietly building a program capable of deep WCWS runs, and the 2026 portal only reinforced that trajectory. Strong returning core, consistent coaching, and enough portal movement to address specific needs without overhauling a working formula.

Honorable Mentions

Florida State: 34 combined home runs in the portal (Ella Dodge + Nicole Edmiaston) and the return of Jazzy Francik (ACC Pitcher of the Year) make the Seminoles dangerous despite losing Torres and Beachum. Arizona State and LSU both made meaningful portal additions that improve their national standing heading into 2027.

What's Next

The portal rankings are settled. What comes next is fall practice — the first time these assembled rosters actually play together as teams. Texas opens camp in August with a coaching staff finalized yesterday. Texas Tech will be equally eager to test its record-setting class. The preseason polls in January will offer the first formal public rankings of the 2027 season. Everything that happened in the portal over the last 15 days built toward a single moment in Oklahoma City next June.
