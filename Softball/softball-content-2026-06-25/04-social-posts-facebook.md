### STORY 1: Texas Coaching Staff Complete — Fremin and Freeman Hired for 2027

**Long-Form Post**
Mike White's 2027 coaching staff is complete — and it happened faster than most expected.

When Ehren Earleywine left for Tennessee and Kristen Zaleski departed for Utah State, the question wasn't whether Texas would fill the vacancies. It was who White could attract to a program that just won back-to-back national championships.

The answers arrived this week.

Rick Fremin joins as Associate Head Coach. He spent the last ten seasons as head coach at Southeastern Louisiana, going 348-197 with a .638 winning percentage, three consecutive Louisiana Coach of the Year awards (2024, 2025, 2026), and three straight NCAA Tournament appearances. His 2026 SLU squad went 46-16. Fremin brings 21 years of head coaching experience to an assistant role — a remarkable hire that signals how attractive the Texas brand has become.

Amber Freeman joins as hitting coach. She comes from the University of Arizona, where she spent two seasons developing one of the Big 12's most productive offenses. In 2025, she coached Arizona to a .348 batting average — best in the Big 12 — and the conference led the league in home runs, on-base percentage, RBIs, runs scored, and slugging percentage. She developed Big 12 Players of the Year catcher Sydney Stewart and utility player Devyn Netz.

Both vacancies. Both filled. Before fall camp.

The most loaded roster in college softball history — Torres (.530 BA), Stewart (31 HRs), Kavan (two-time WCWS MOP), Swan (.383/12 HR), and a top national recruiting class — now has the complete coaching staff to match.

Three-peat. Fully staffed.

### STORY 2: Who Won the 2026 Transfer Portal? Final Power Rankings

**Long-Form Post**
The 2026 transfer portal window closed June 22. Fifteen days. 130 players. 103 commitments. And a very clear winner.

Here's the final post-portal power ranking:

No. 1 — Texas. The Longhorns entered the portal with the nation's best roster and added Isa Torres (.530 BA / .978 SLG, D1Softball Player of the Year) and Samantha Swan (.383 BA / 12 HR from Arizona State). Combined with returning players Teagan Kavan, Katie Stewart (31 HRs), and a top-four national recruiting class, Texas built a roster that has no obvious weakness.

No. 2 — Texas Tech. The Red Raiders assembled a class that Softball America ranked No. 1 nationally — three All-Americans in Kenna Polar (.555 BA), Jayden Heavener (LSU, 13-9 / 2.81 ERA), and Kasidi Pickering (.370 BA / 20 HR from Oklahoma). The 61-8 WCWS runner-up is loading up to finish the job in 2027.

No. 3 — Florida. Tim Walton landed Jaysoni Beachum (.417 BA / 16 HR / 66 RBI / .549 OBP), ranked the No. 1 transfer in the entire portal by On3. Beachum's Tallahassee-area roots made the commitment personal. Florida also added Duke transfer Kairi Rodriguez. The Gators are back in the national title conversation.

No. 4 — Nebraska. The Cornhuskers didn't reload through the portal — they rebuild through their returners from a 51-6, WCWS semifinal squad. Without Jordy Frahm, the question is sustainability. But this program has elite infrastructure and a proven staff.

No. 5 — South Carolina. Quietly dangerous, with strong returners and a coaching staff that consistently maximizes talent.

Honorable mention: FSU (34 combined portal HRs in Dodge + Edmiaston), Arizona State, LSU.

The portal closed. The rankings are set. Now the season itself will decide everything in Oklahoma City.

### STORY 3: FSU Still Waiting on Milloy — But the Seminoles Are Already Dangerous

**Long-Form Post**
The story coming out of Tallahassee this month has been one of losses.

Isa Torres to Texas. Jaysoni Beachum to Florida. Two of the biggest names in the entire portal — both gone.

But here's what the headlines are missing: Florida State may be more dangerous for 2027 than most people realize.

Jazzy Francik returns. The 2026 ACC Pitcher of the Year is the kind of anchor that lets a program survive major roster turnover. When your No. 1 starter is that good, your margin for error everywhere else gets wider.

Ella Dodge committed from Tennessee, bringing 23 home runs to Tallahassee. Nicole Edmiaston committed from Stetson, adding 11 more. That's 34 combined home runs from two portal additions — actually more power than Torres and Beachum brought to FSU's lineup together.

Ashtyn Danley is a proven two-way player who returns. The pitching staff behind Francik has depth. The offense has pop.

And Tia Milloy is still available.

Milloy — who hit .353 with a .706 slugging percentage at Oklahoma — didn't use the transfer portal window that closed June 22. That means she can still be recruited and committed to a program outside the formal window. FSU remains in contact.

If Milloy commits: a genuinely elite lineup.

If she doesn't: still a team that can win 40+ games and compete for an at-large.

The Seminoles are being underrated. Don't make that mistake heading into 2027.

### STORY 4: PGF Nationals Preview — July 19 Opens the Summer's Biggest Stage

**Long-Form Post**
The transfer portal closed. The WCWS is over. Texas hoisted the trophy in Oklahoma City.

Now the next wave arrives.

Premier Girls Fastpitch National Championship 2026 — July 19 through August 4. The biggest, most prestigious youth fastpitch event in the country. The event where college coaches travel from every program in the nation to find the next Isa Torres, the next Jordy Frahm, the next Teagan Kavan.

Here's how it works: Week 1 (July 19–26) features 18U and 16U competition. This is where most of the D1 recruiting happens — the players competing at 18U this summer are mostly 2027 high school prospects, the very class that current freshman will eventually share rosters with.

Week 2 (July 27–August 4) shifts to 14U, where programs begin identifying prospects three or four years out.

Why does this matter to college fans?

Because every program in the country that struck out in the portal is at PGF with a legal pad and a lot of questions. The 2028 recruiting class is being built right now. The player who wins 18U MVP in August could be signing a letter of intent in November.

For programs like Nebraska — which lost Jordy Frahm — and FSU — which needs to continue its rebuild — these events are critical.

For Texas and Texas Tech — who already have elite rosters — PGF is about adding depth and staying ahead of the competition.

The portal gave us the 2027 story. PGF Nationals begins writing the 2028 one.

### STORY 5: Oklahoma Rebuilds Around a History-Maker

**Long-Form Post**
In the middle of a summer dominated by Texas portal news and WCWS celebrations, a quiet piece of history was made at Oklahoma.

On June 9, freshman catcher Kendall Wells won the 2026 Johnny Bench Award — given annually to the top catcher in college softball. Wells became the first freshman in the award's history to win. She is only the second Oklahoma player ever to claim the honor.

Let that sink in: a freshman catcher just won the award named for one of baseball's greatest catchers. In her first college season.

Oklahoma didn't make the Women's College World Series in 2026. They exited in the Super Regional — a disappointment for a program accustomed to national stage appearances under head coach Patty Gasso.

But with Wells locked in as the cornerstone of the 2027 roster — and multiple years of eligibility ahead of her — the Sooners' timeline for returning to Oklahoma City looks shorter than the current standings might suggest.

Gasso is the winningest coach in college softball history. Oklahoma has won six national championships. They've navigated program transitions before.

The 2027 Sooners will be worth watching. Kendall Wells just told you why.
