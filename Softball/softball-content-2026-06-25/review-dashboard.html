### Long-Form Post The 2026 transfer portal window closed June 22. Fifteen days. 130 players. 103 commitments. And a very clear winner. Here's the final post-portal power ranking: No. 1 — Texas. Combined with returning players Teagan Kavan, Katie Stewart (31 HRs), and a top-four national recruiting class, Texas built a roster that has no obvious weakness. No. 2 — Texas Tech. Florida also added Duke transfer Kairi Rodriguez. The Gators are back in the national title conversation. No. 4 — Nebraska. The Cornhuskers didn't reload through the portal — they rebuild through their returners from a 51-6, WCWS semifinal squad. Without Jordy Frahm, the question is sustainability. But this program has elite infrastructure and a proven staff. No. 5 — South Carolina. Quietly dangerous, with strong returners and a coaching staff that consistently maximizes talent. Honorable mention: FSU (34 combined portal HRs in Dodge + Edmiaston), Arizona State, LSU. The portal closed. The rankings are set.
