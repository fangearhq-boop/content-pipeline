### STORY 2: FSU Softball's Painful Summer — Losing Torres and Beachum, but Not Done

**Long-Form Post**
There's no sugarcoating what happened to Florida State softball in the 2026 transfer portal window.

Isa Torres — D1 Player of the Year, .530 hitter, program all-time record holder in batting average and OBP — left for Texas. Jaysoni Beachum — Softball America's No. 1 transfer, .417 BA, 16 HR, 66 RBI — left for Florida. In a single 15-day window, the Seminoles lost two of the most decorated players in program history.

But here's the thing about FSU: they have rebuilt before.

ACC Pitcher of the Year Jazzy Francik is coming back. Two-way star Ashtyn Danley is coming back. The Seminoles added Nicole Edmiaston from Stetson and Ella Dodge from Tennessee — and those two combined for 34 home runs last season, more than Torres and Beachum combined (26).

Yes, Torres and Beachum brought more than just home runs. They drove runs, set the table, and anchored the lineup in ways stats don't fully capture. The losses are real. But the narrative that FSU is finished is premature.

Programs built on elite pitching survive roster turnover. Jazzy Francik is one of the best pitchers in the country. Head coach Lonni Alameda has rebuilt this program more than once. Count the Seminoles out at your own risk.

### STORY 5: The Offseason Begins — What's Next for College Softball After the Portal Closed

**Long-Form Post**
The portal is closed. Texas is the champion. The 15-day window that reshaped 2027 college softball is officially over.

So what comes next?

Here's the offseason calendar for fans who want to stay connected:

☀️ RIGHT NOW — SUMMER SHOWCASES
Premier Girls Fastpitch, Top Gun, and Triple Crown circuits are the proving grounds for high school prospects. College coaches are in the stands evaluating who to pursue for the 2027 and 2028 recruiting classes. If you're a high school player, summer is the most important evaluation period of the year.

📅 SEPTEMBER 1 — RECRUITING CONTACT OPENS
The single most important date in softball recruiting. D1 coaches can initiate direct contact with rising juniors (class of 2028) starting September 1. This is when verbal offers, phone calls, and campus visit conversations begin in earnest.

🏟️ AUGUST — FALL PRACTICE BEGINS
College programs return to campus. Portal additions meet returning rosters for the first time. This is when Isa Torres and Katie Stewart first practice together in burnt orange. When Polar, Heavener, and Pickering first take the field at Texas Tech.

📋 FALL — NFCA CONVENTION
The softball coaching community gathers. Coaching changes often get announced here — hires, departures, staff restructuring. Worth watching for fans of programs that had busy offseasons.

🏆 AFTER 2027 WCWS — NEXT PORTAL WINDOW
The cycle repeats. The next transfer portal opens after the 2027 national championship. Whatever happens between now and then, the clock is already running on the next rebuild season.

College softball never truly stops. It just changes what it looks like.
