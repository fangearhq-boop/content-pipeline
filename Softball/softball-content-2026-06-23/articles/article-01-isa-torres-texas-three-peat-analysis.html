Isa Torres to Texas: What the Transfer Really Means for the Three-Peat

By Sarah Mitchell

June 23, 2026

The transfer portal closed on Sunday evening, and when the final window shut, one name stood above every other name as the defining move of the 2026 cycle: Isa Torres, heading home to Texas.

Torres — the 2026 D1 Softball Player of the Year, the 2026 ACC Player of the Year, the 2026 ACC Defensive Player of the Year — signed with the University of Texas on June 18, joining the two-time defending national champions. She hit .530 this season. She slugged .978. She posted a .591 on-base percentage and scored 78 runs. She set an NCAA record with 16 consecutive at-bats reaching base safely.

And she still had a year of eligibility left.

Coming Home

Torres grew up in Georgetown, Texas — a city of about 90,000 people located roughly 30 minutes north of the UT campus. She chose Florida State out of high school and went on to become one of the most decorated players in Seminoles history. Now she's returning to the state she grew up in, joining the program that won back-to-back national titles in 2025 and 2026.

In her announcement, Torres used the phrase "I'm coming home." For a player who achieved everything there was to achieve at FSU, the choice carries both narrative weight and genuine strategic logic. Texas offered the platform for a championship run. She took it.

The Lineup She's Joining

To understand what Torres adds to Texas, you first have to understand the lineup she's walking into. This is not a program in need. The Longhorns went 61-4 in 2026 and won the Women's College World Series in two games over Texas Tech. They did it with a roster that Mike White and his staff assembled over years of elite recruiting and portal work.

Now they've added the best player in the sport.

Torres slots in at shortstop alongside a lineup that includes Katie Stewart — who hit a school-record 31 home runs in 2026 — and Teagan Kavan, a versatile offensive weapon heading into her senior season. Behind the plate, Samantha Swan, an All-Big 12 catcher acquired from Arizona State in the portal, handles the defense. Pitcher Madi Doty, rated the No. 2 prospect nationally for her class by Softball America, joins the pitching staff.

It's a lineup that doesn't have an obvious soft spot. Torres makes it even harder to find one.

What She Brings That Numbers Can't Fully Capture

Torres's statistics from 2026 are historic, but the numbers undersell her impact in one important way: she makes everyone around her better.

At .530, she was essentially impossible to retire in 2026. A lineup that already forced pitchers to work carefully around Stewart's power now has a .530 hitter in the middle of it. Pitchers cannot avoid both of them. Opposing coaches cannot construct a game plan that neutralizes either without leaving the other more dangerous.

Add Swan and Kavan and the top of the Texas order becomes a construction that no pitching staff in college softball has faced before — at least not at this level of depth.

The Three-Peat Conversation

Texas has already won two consecutive national championships. No program has won three in a row in the modern era of college softball. The Longhorns will be the first team in years walking into the 2027 season as the clear favorite in nearly every preseason ranking — with the portal class to back it up.

Texas Tech will be the primary challenger. The Red Raiders finished as WCWS runners-up in 2026 and spent the entire portal window assembling a class with three consensus All-Americans. That race will be the defining storyline of the 2027 season.

But for now, the portal is closed. Torres is in burnt orange. And the three-peat conversation has officially begun.

What's Next

Texas's 2027 roster is now largely set. The Longhorns will begin fall practice in August, where Torres integrates into the lineup alongside Stewart, Kavan, and Swan for the first time. Fans should watch for early scrimmages and fall ball reports to see how the new additions mesh with the returning core.

The 2027 schedule hasn't been released yet, but whenever Texas and Texas Tech meet in the regular season or the WCWS, it will be the most-watched matchup in college softball. That's what Torres joining the Longhorns — and the Red Raiders' own elite portal class — has created.

A rivalry. A rematch. And a race no one can look away from.
