Post-Portal Power Rankings: Who Really Won the 2026 Transfer Window?

By Jordan Reeves

June 23, 2026

The 2026 NCAA softball transfer portal opened June 8 and closed June 22. In those 15 days — the first condensed window of its kind in NCAA history — 122 players entered the portal and 89 committed to new programs, a commitment rate of 73 percent.

The window changed rosters across the country. It reshaped the 2027 national title race. It sent waves through programs from Florida to California and everywhere in between.

Now it's over. And we can finally score it.

No. 1: Texas — The Champion Got Better

Texas already won back-to-back national championships entering the portal window. Most programs in that position focus on maintenance — filling specific roster holes without overextending. Mike White and the Longhorns went after the best available player in the portal and landed her.

D1 Softball Player of the Year Isa Torres — .530 BA, .978 SLG, .591 OBP, 78 runs, NCAA record 16 consecutive at-bats with a hit — signed with Texas. She is a Georgetown, TX native returning home after four seasons at Florida State. She joins a lineup that already features school-record HR hitter Katie Stewart (31 HRs), Teagan Kavan, and All-Big 12 catcher Samantha Swan (from Arizona State). Pitcher Samantha Lincoln also joined in the portal.

Texas's portal class wins on overall talent level. The Longhorns added the single best player available and surrounded her with legitimate contributors. No other program can claim they got better than Texas did.

No. 2: Texas Tech — Three All-Americans, One Goal

If Texas won the portal on overall talent, Texas Tech won it on sheer volume of elite additions. The Red Raiders were the WCWS runner-up in 2026 with a 61-8 record. They came within two wins of a national title. And they responded by building the most All-American-loaded portal class anyone can remember.

Three additions tell the story: Kenna Polar (.555 BA, 101 hits from Purdue, No. 5 in the portal), Jayden Heavener (nation's top pitcher from LSU, 13-9 with a 2.81 ERA), and Kasidi Pickering (.370 BA, 20 HR, 61 RBI from Oklahoma, On3's No. 2 overall). All three were consensus All-Americans. Softball America rated Texas Tech's class as the No. 1 transfer class in the country.

The argument for Texas Tech over Texas: they added more impact players. The argument for Texas over Texas Tech: one of those players was Isa Torres. The debate will sort itself out on the field in 2027.

No. 3: Florida — One Move, Maximum Impact

The Gators made one portal move that defined their window: signing Jaysoni Beachum. Softball America's No. 1 transfer — .417 BA, 16 HR, 66 RBI, .549 OBP, and 54 walks — committed to Florida on the final day of the portal window, June 22. A Tallahassee native crossing the state to join FSU's biggest rival, she fills the immediate hole at third base left by Kendall Grover's graduation.

Florida head coach Tim Walton now has a true star to build around. The Gators don't have the depth of additions that Texas or Texas Tech assembled, but they have the headliner. Sometimes the right one move is all a program needs.

No. 4: Nebraska — The Quiet Builder

The Cornhuskers assembled a strong portal class without making headlines. Nebraska has consistently been among the better programs in the Big Ten and used this portal to build depth across their roster for 2027. Their additions may not generate national conversations the way Texas Tech or Florida's moves did, but they represent a program that knows how to build sustainably.

No. 5: South Carolina — Meaningful Moves in the SEC

The Gamecocks made several additions that position them to compete in the SEC in 2027. South Carolina has been a consistent postseason program, and their portal activity reflects a coaching staff that addresses needs thoughtfully rather than reaching for names.

Honorable Mention: Arkansas and Oklahoma State

Both programs made quality additions earlier in the portal window. Arkansas landed Mountain West Freshman of the Year Katie Wetteland. Oklahoma State added All-Big 12 veteran Madison Hartley. Neither class reaches the top five, but both programs are meaningfully better for the portal.

What's Next

These rankings will be tested when fall practice begins in August and programs integrate their portal additions with returning rosters. The first real preview of the 2027 season will come from fall scrimmage reports. The definitive test comes when the 2027 season opens in February.

One thing is certain: the gap between the top programs and the rest of college softball just widened. Texas, Texas Tech, and Florida used this portal window to separate themselves from the field. How the rest of the country responds — through recruiting, development, and the next portal window after the 2027 WCWS — will define college softball for years to come.
