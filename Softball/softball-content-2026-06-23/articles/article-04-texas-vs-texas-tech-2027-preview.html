Texas vs. Texas Tech: The 2027 National Championship Preview Starts Now

By Sarah Mitchell

June 23, 2026

On June 4, 2026, Texas defeated Texas Tech 4-1 in Game 2 of the Women's College World Series championship round to claim back-to-back national titles. The Longhorns had also won Game 1, 7-3. The Red Raiders, 61-8 on the season, came within two wins of the program's first national championship.

They went home. And they went to work.

What Texas Tech Did in the Portal

In the 15 days the transfer portal was open, Texas Tech assembled what Softball America called the No. 1 transfer class in the country. Three consensus All-Americans. Each one a program-altering addition on her own.

Kenna Polar arrived from Purdue. The .555 hitter with 101 hits and a .549 on-base percentage is one of the most consistent contact bats in the country. Jayden Heavener came from LSU, where she went 13-9 with a 2.81 ERA and earned recognition as the nation's top pitcher available in the portal. Kasidi Pickering transferred from Oklahoma — On3's No. 2 overall portal prospect, a .370 hitter with 20 home runs and 61 RBI.

All three of them were All-Americans. All three of them committed to the same school. The message was clear: Texas Tech intends to come back to Oklahoma City in 2027 and finish what they started.

What Texas Did in Response

Texas's response was precisely one player: Isa Torres.

Torres, the 2026 D1 Softball Player of the Year, hit .530 with a .978 slugging percentage, a .591 on-base percentage, and 78 runs scored at Florida State. She set an NCAA record with 16 consecutive at-bats reaching base safely. She won the ACC Player of the Year and Defensive Player of the Year awards. She has one year of eligibility remaining and will play it in burnt orange.

The Longhorns also added All-Big 12 catcher Samantha Swan from Arizona State and pitcher Samantha Lincoln. Texas's existing lineup already included Katie Stewart — who set the school record with 31 home runs in 2026 — and Teagan Kavan, heading into her senior year.

The Longhorns didn't build the deepest portal class. They built the best one.

The Case for Texas Tech

Three elite additions are harder to defend than one. A lineup that now has Polar (.555 BA) as a table-setter, Heavener on the mound, and Pickering providing power gives Texas Tech versatility that is genuinely difficult to game-plan against. You cannot pitch around three All-Americans the way you can manage one dominant presence.

Texas Tech also has the chip on their shoulder that comes from losing the final. Programs that reach the championship game and fall short typically come back with a ferocity that programs defending a title have to match. The Red Raiders will enter 2027 with something to prove. That motivation is real and it shows up in how teams compete.

The Case for Texas

Texas has two consecutive national championships. They know how to win the last game of the season. That experience, that culture, that coaching infrastructure — it doesn't disappear when the portal window closes.

Adding Torres to a lineup that already had Stewart, Kavan, and Swan is not simply adding a great player. It is adding the best player in college softball to a team that already has elite players. The sum matters. The 2027 Longhorns will be historically deep, and they will have Mike White — who has built this program into a dynasty — making the decisions.

The Storyline That Writes Itself

College sports is built on great rivalries, and the 2026 portal window may have just cemented this one. Texas and Texas Tech share a state, a history, and now a mutual obsession with the national championship game. Both programs spent the entire portal window building toward a 2027 rematch in Oklahoma City.

They may meet in the regular season. They may meet in the Big 12 tournament. But every Texas and Texas Tech softball fan is already thinking about June 2027 in Devon Park.

The rematch was written the day the portal opened. Now we just wait for it.

What's Next

Fall practice is the next real look at both programs. Texas Tech's new roster — Polar, Heavener, and Pickering integrating with returning players — will get its first test in fall scrimmages. Texas's lineup, now with Torres alongside Stewart and Swan, will face the same process of integration.

The 2027 season opens in February. The 2027 Women's College World Series is scheduled for late May and early June in Oklahoma City. Between now and then, the debate that the portal window started will play out in every practice, every scrimmage, and every regular-season game.

Who wins 2027? Ask the coaches in November, the statisticians in March, and the scoreboard in June.
