FSU Softball's Painful Summer: Losing Torres and Beachum, but the Seminoles Aren't Done

By Emily Rawlings

June 23, 2026

There is no sugar-coating what happened to Florida State softball in the 2026 transfer portal window.

In a 15-day span, the Seminoles lost Isa Torres — the 2026 D1 Softball Player of the Year, .530 hitter, FSU's all-time program record holder in batting average and on-base percentage — to Texas. They also lost Jaysoni Beachum — Softball America's No. 1 transfer, a .417 hitter with 16 home runs and 66 RBI this season, a two-time All-American — to Florida. Two of the most decorated players in program history, gone to conference rivals in the same window.

If you're a Seminoles fan, this summer hurt. That's real, and it deserves acknowledgment.

But FSU's story doesn't end at the portal's closing bell.

What the Seminoles Still Have

The single most important piece of Florida State's 2027 outlook isn't a portal addition or a new recruit. It's Jazzy Francik choosing to come back.

Francik won the 2026 ACC Pitcher of the Year award. She is one of the elite pitchers in the country, and her decision to return to Tallahassee means FSU enters 2027 with the kind of pitching anchor that allows programs to compete for titles even when rosters turn over. Elite starting pitching covers a multitude of roster sins. Francik is the most important returning piece in the ACC.

Two-way star Ashtyn Danley also returns for 2027, giving the Seminoles another proven contributor who can impact the game with both bat and glove.

The Portal Additions Tell a Different Story Than You'd Expect

Florida State moved quickly in the portal. The Seminoles added Nicole Edmiaston from Stetson and Ella Dodge from Tennessee. On the surface, neither name carries the marquee recognition of Torres or Beachum. But the production numbers are worth examining carefully.

Edmiaston and Dodge combined for 34 home runs in 2026. Torres and Beachum combined for 26. That's not a full picture — Torres and Beachum were also elite on-base threats, run scorers, and lineup anchors in ways that go beyond the home run column. But it does tell you that FSU's additions are not filler. They are legitimate offensive weapons who can contribute immediately.

FSU is also reported to be in conversations about further portal additions, with Oklahoma's Tia Milloy among the players the Seminoles are targeting. The roster is not yet final.

The Lonni Alameda Factor

Florida State head coach Lonni Alameda has built and rebuilt this program more than once. The Seminoles have been a national title contender in multiple eras under her leadership. Losing Torres and Beachum is a significant blow, but it is not a program-defining wound.

Programs built on elite pitching have a structural advantage when rosters turn over. You can rebuild a lineup faster than you can rebuild a pitching staff. FSU has the pitching. Francik's return means the foundation is intact.

The Seminoles also have an elite 2027 recruiting class coming in — and the losses of Torres and Beachum, paradoxically, may have freed up roster spots that make FSU's position more attractive for high school commits who want to play immediately.

Where FSU Stands Heading Into 2027

The Seminoles are not Texas or Texas Tech entering 2027. They don't have a portal class ranked No. 1 in the country. They don't have the Player of the Year or three All-Americans from the portal. They are a program that absorbed major losses and responded with a pitching-first rebuild strategy that is historically proven to work.

A realistic 2027 outlook for Florida State: WCWS contention, but not as a clear title favorite. The kind of team that nobody wants to face in the regional because Francik is on the mound. The kind of program that makes a deep run precisely because people underestimate them after a painful summer.

Count the Seminoles out at your own risk.

What's Next

FSU's roster situation may not be finalized. Watch for any remaining portal activity, including whether the Seminoles complete a deal for another impact player. The NFCA Convention in the fall often brings staffing and roster clarity across programs, so that's another date to mark for Seminoles fans. When fall practice begins in August, we'll get the first glimpse of how Alameda has structured the 2027 lineup without Torres and Beachum at its core.

This offseason stung. The 2027 season will tell us whether FSU responded the right way.
