No. 1 Texas at No. 4 Alabama: The Biggest Series in College Softball Opens Tonight

By Sarah Mitchell April 2, 2026

The Stage Is Set in Tuscaloosa

The most anticipated series of the 2026 college softball season begins Thursday night. No. 1 Texas travels to Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa to face No. 4 Alabama in a three-game set that could reshape the SEC standings, the national rankings, and the conversation about which program is the sport's best. Game 1 is at 6 PM CT on SEC Network+.

The Longhorns (31-2, 8-1 SEC) arrive as defending national champions, the consensus best team in the country, and — despite a rare stumble — still in command of the race they've led all season. Texas won 29 straight games to open conference play before Texas A&M ended the streak with a 9-7 win in the series finale on March 29. Texas still won the series two games to one, and head coach Mike White's program has answered every challenge thrown at it in his eighth season leading the program.

Alabama (33-2, 7-2 SEC) is not asking for Texas's mercy. Patrick Murphy's Crimson Tide enter the series in peak form, having outscored four non-conference opponents by a combined score of 42-1 last week. Three of those wins were shutouts. Rhoads Stadium, one of the most intimidating venues in college softball, will be packed and loud.

A Rivalry That Hasn't Been Played Before

For all the hype, tonight is technically the first time Texas and Alabama face each other as SEC conference rivals. The two programs have met nine times previously, and Alabama leads that all-time series 7-2. They've never played a game where SEC standings points were on the table. That changes tonight.

The historical implication matters for another reason. Alabama has a documented track record of rising to the moment on their home field when elite visitors arrive. In 2025, the Crimson Tide beat defending champion Oklahoma right at Rhoads — the kind of win that Murphy's program seems to produce every spring. Texas, for all its accomplishments, has not been tested in an environment like this.

Murphy is in his 30th season leading the Alabama program — a tenure that has produced national championships, consistent postseason runs, and a reputation for excellence that rivals any program in the sport. White, in his eighth season, built the Texas program into a national champion from scratch. These are two elite programs, two elite coaches, and tonight they meet for the first time in a game that truly counts.

What's at Stake in the SEC

The SEC standings heading into Thursday: Texas and Oklahoma are tied at 8-1, with Alabama two games back at 7-2 (one game behind each leader, with percentage slightly lower). Texas can create real separation with a series win. Alabama can climb into a tie for first with a series victory. The implications ripple outward — the NFCA Week 9 poll releases Tuesday, and the regular-season champion earns the No. 1 seed at the SEC Tournament in Lexington, Kentucky in May.

No team in college softball benefits more from home field advantage than Alabama in Tuscaloosa, and no team is better equipped to handle a road environment than a program that won the national championship on a neutral field last June. That tension — the Tide's fortress against the Longhorns' championship pedigree — is what makes this series so compelling.

Saturday's Game 3 is scheduled for ESPN at 5 PM CT, meaning if the series is still undecided entering the weekend finale, it will be played in front of a national television audience.

What's Next

Game 2 follows Friday night at 7:30 PM CT on SEC Network, and Game 3 airs Saturday at 5 PM CT on ESPN. The NFCA Week 9 poll drops Tuesday morning and will reflect this series' outcome. If Texas wins, the Longhorns at 10-1 would have daylight in the SEC standings. If Alabama wins, the Crimson Tide enters a three-way tie at the top — and the second half of conference play suddenly looks very different. Either way, the 2026 SEC softball race just officially began.
