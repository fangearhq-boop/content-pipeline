Kendall Wells Is Three Home Runs From History — The Chase Continues at Home

By Emily Rawlings April 2, 2026

27 Down. Three to Go.

Every time Kendall Wells steps to the plate, she carries a number with her: 30. That's the NCAA freshman home run record, shared by three celebrated Oklahoma sluggers — Jocelyn Alo, Lauren Chamberlain, and Hawaii's Kelly Majam. Wells sits at 27. She needs three more to join that list.

Thursday night, she opens a three-game home SEC series against Kentucky at Love's Field in Norman, with Game 1 at 6:30 PM CT on SEC Network+. Kentucky is 1-11 in SEC play, last in the conference. The matchup represents a favorable opportunity for an offense that went 17-1 in March and leads the nation in home runs.

Wells set the SEC single-season freshman home run record last Tuesday when she hit No. 27 in the Sooners' 12-3 run-rule win over Wichita State — an opposite-field shot in the sixth inning with runners on base. She did it in the 37th game of her collegiate career.

The Numbers Around a Historic Season

The home run chase is the headline, but the full statistical line is what separates Wells from being simply a power hitter. She's batting .384 with 57 RBI and a 1.572 OPS. Her slugging percentage is 1.107. She plays catcher — one of the most demanding defensive positions in softball — and has committed zero errors in her defensive chances this season.

Oklahoma head coach Patty Gasso has built one of the great dynasties in college softball. She coached Jocelyn Alo. She coached Lauren Chamberlain. Two of the three players who share the NCAA freshman record came through her program. When asked about Wells, Gasso said: "Offensively, I've seen Jocelyn Alo. I've seen Lauren Chamberlain. This is something new, this is different."

Oklahoma's season home run total stands at 137 through 38 games — sixth in NCAA single-season history. Wells accounts for more than 19 percent of those home runs herself. The Sooners hit four in Tuesday's win over Wichita State, with Kasidi Pickering, Ailana Agbayani, and Isabela Emerling (her 13th) joining Wells in the home run column.

Wells Isn't the Only Freshman Making History

While Wells dominates the power numbers, she's not even the only Oklahoma freshman doing something historically unusual. Kai Minor, a centerfielder, is batting .490 on the season — leading all Division I freshmen and the entire SEC in batting average. Ranking third in the SEC in hits (51) and fourth in stolen bases (13), Minor is quietly building a case for All-American consideration as a first-year player.

Oklahoma went 17-1 in March with Wells hitting 12 home runs and driving in 30 runs in that single month. The Sooners are 35-3 overall and tied with Texas atop the SEC standings at 8-1. Tonight's series opener against Kentucky — the conference's last-place team — is the kind of favorable matchup that could push Wells to No. 28 before the weekend is over.

Wells, characteristically, has tried to stay grounded amid the attention. "It's a little surreal," she has said. "I don't really look at the stats that much. If I try to hit home runs, it's not gonna happen. So I try to push that off to the side."

What's Next

Game 2 is Friday at 1 PM CT and Game 3 is Saturday at 11 AM CT, all on SEC Network+. Wells enters with 17 or more regular-season games remaining plus the postseason — more than enough runway to reach and potentially surpass 30. The all-time NCAA single-season freshman mark of 30 is three away. The OU single-season record of 34 (held by various players) is seven away. And the all-time NCAA single-season record of 37 is 10 away — theoretically within range if she maintains her current pace. The Love's Field crowd will be watching every swing.
