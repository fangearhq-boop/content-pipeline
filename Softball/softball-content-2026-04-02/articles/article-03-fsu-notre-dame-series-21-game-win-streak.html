Florida State Opens ACC Series vs Notre Dame With Nation's Longest Win Streak

By Jordan Reeves April 2, 2026

Seminoles Carry Historic Momentum Into April

Florida State's momentum hasn't slowed since the calendar flipped. The No. 6 Seminoles open a three-game ACC series against Notre Dame at JoAnne Graf Field on Thursday at 6 PM ET on ACC Network Extra, carrying with them the longest active win streak in college softball: 21 consecutive games.

The program is 32-4 overall and a perfect 9-0 in ACC play. That win streak is the program's longest since 2018 and the longest current run in Division I softball. It began after Florida State absorbed its fourth loss of the season and hasn't stopped since. The Seminoles have swept through every opponent placed in front of them with an offense and pitching staff that, on its best days, doesn't look beatable.

Notre Dame arrives at 16-19, 5-7 in the ACC. The Irish are 7-30 all-time against Florida State; their last win over the Seminoles was March 16, 2019. This is not a series where the opponent is expected to challenge the streak. But Notre Dame would love nothing more than to be the team that ends it.

The Numbers Behind the 21-Game Run

Florida State's 21-game win streak isn't a product of soft scheduling. The numbers that define it are remarkable. During this run, the Seminoles have hit .392 as a team and averaged 9.19 runs per game. They've drawn 99 walks against just 33 strikeouts — a rate that reflects elite pitch recognition at every spot in the lineup. They've hit 26 home runs and maintained the kind of plate discipline that coaches spend entire careers trying to build.

The streak also includes significant milestones. Last weekend at Clemson, head coach Lonni Alameda earned her 1,000th career win — an achievement that put her among just 12 active Division I coaches to reach that threshold. Alameda is in her 18th season leading the Seminoles program and has built it into a perennial national title contender. She won this series alongside some of the most talented players in the country.

The series at Notre Dame begins tonight and concludes Saturday at 2 PM ET on ACC Network. Games 1 and 2 are streamed on ACC Network Extra before the national television finale on Saturday.

Isa Torres and the Offensive Engine

No discussion of this Florida State team is complete without accounting for junior Isa Torres. She is batting .615 this season — first in all of Division I softball — with a .664 on-base percentage (second in the country). Over her last 15 games since returning from injury, Torres has gone 37-for-52 (.712) with five home runs and 22 RBI. She set the NCAA record for consecutive hits at 16 (March 8-21) and reached base in 23 straight plate appearances, the third-longest streak in NCAA history.

Torres did all of this after missing eight games. She returned from injury and immediately became the best hitter in the country. In 2025, she hit .436 — the FSU program record — and was a unanimous first-team All-American. In 2026, she's surpassed even that remarkable baseline.

What the Seminoles have in Torres is a player who makes every pitcher uncomfortable. When she's locked in — and she has been locked in for over a month — she's virtually impossible to get out.

What's Next

Game 2 is Friday at 6 PM ET on ACC Network Extra, and Game 3 airs Saturday at 2 PM ET on ACC Network. Florida State controls its own destiny in the ACC with a perfect conference record and roughly six weeks of regular-season play remaining. The program's goal is clear: win the ACC, earn a top national seed, and arrive at the Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City in June with a team that can compete for the title. If Torres and company keep performing like this, that scenario is entirely realistic.
