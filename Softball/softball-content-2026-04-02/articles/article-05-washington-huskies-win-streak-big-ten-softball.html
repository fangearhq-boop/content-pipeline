Washington Is 30-6 With a 20-Game Win Streak — and Nobody Is Talking About Them

By Emily Rawlings April 2, 2026

The Most Overlooked Team in College Softball

While college softball's national conversation revolves around the SEC — Texas, Oklahoma, Alabama — and the ACC's Florida State, a team in the Pac-12/Big Ten corridor has been winning quietly at an exceptional rate. Washington is 30-6 overall, on a 20-game win streak, and 12-0 in the Big Ten. They are the only team in any major conference without a conference loss.

The Huskies are ranked No. 21 in the ESPN/USA Softball poll. That number is almost certainly too low for a team with a perfect conference record and a 20-game winning streak. Their Big Ten-only mark of 12-0 is more dominant than any team in the SEC or Big 12 has managed through the same stretch of conference play.

Washington went 16-0 in March — eight wins at home, seven on the road, adding up to a month of dominance that any program in the country would claim. They've beaten ranked opponents, swept through conference weekends, and maintained the kind of consistency that translates to postseason success.

The Pitching Depth That Drives It

One of the clearest markers of Washington's quality is the depth of their pitching staff. In their win against Iowa, Sophia Ramuno and Rylee Rehbein combined for a no-hitter — nine innings of hitless softball as a tandem. The ability to combine two pitchers for a no-hitter speaks to the quality and trust invested in both arms.

That pitching depth has allowed Washington to navigate a full conference schedule without leaning on a single ace, which reduces wear and protects the staff heading into the postseason. Teams that rely heavily on one pitcher often see that pitcher hit a wall late in the season or in the first round of the tournament. Washington's approach avoids that risk.

On the offensive side, the Huskies batted .348 during their Midwest road trip — a series of games that included significant travel and adjustment. Key contributors include outfielder Kaycie Burdick (.467 on the trip), Giselle Alvarez (.429), Ava Carroll (.423), and Melody Acevedo (.400). That production across the lineup, not just one or two stars, defines what Washington is doing.

The Stanford Test

Washington heads to Palo Alto this weekend for the Cardinal Classic, where they'll face Stanford and Saint Mary's. Stanford is a perennial postseason program and will represent the Huskies' most significant test during the current win streak. Stanford's pitching and lineup are capable of ending runs like this one.

If Washington wins both weekend games — particularly if they beat Stanford — the narrative around this team will change. At 32-6 with a perfect conference record and wins over two nationally competitive programs in the same week, the Huskies would no longer be able to hide in the No. 21 bracket. The next ESPN/USA Softball poll releases Tuesday. A strong weekend performance could push them into the top 15.

The 20-game win streak sits second in the country behind Florida State's 21-game run. If FSU loses at any point this weekend and Washington wins both of theirs, the Huskies would own the nation's longest active streak heading into the second half of conference play.

What's Next

Washington plays at Stanford this weekend as part of the Cardinal Classic. The results will determine whether the Huskies get the national recognition their record demands. Beyond the weekend, the Big Ten conference schedule continues with no clear challenger to their undefeated conference mark — but that can change quickly in a competitive league. Watch for Washington's poll position after Tuesday's release, and watch even more closely as they approach a potential Women's College World Series push in June. This team is built for it.
