Tomorrow Night Is the ESPYs — Today Is Your Last Full Day to Vote for Megan Grant

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 14, 2026

The window is almost closed. And if you haven't voted yet — or if you missed a day — today is the last full day to cast your vote for Megan Grant before the 34th ESPYs air tomorrow night.

Grant is nominated for Best Record-Breaking Performance. Voting closes July 15 at ESPN.com, which is also the same night as the ceremony — 8 PM ET on ABC and the ESPN app. That means today is the last calendar day where you can guarantee your vote lands before the cutoff.

Cast one vote today. And if you can, share this article with someone who loves softball.

What Grant Did That Got Her Here

The nomination is not hard to justify. In 2026, Megan Grant hit 42 home runs in a single season at UCLA — setting a new NCAA Division I record for single-season home runs. The previous record belonged to Laura Espinoza, set in 1995. Thirty-one years it stood. Grant hit 42 and erased it.

She didn't just break the record by a small margin. She surpassed Espinoza's mark of 37 by five home runs. At UCLA's Jackie Robinson Stadium, in the Pac-12, against some of the best pitching in the country, she hit 42 in a single season.

That's the record the ESPY voters are judging. And the category is Best Record-Breaking Performance — which is exactly what this was.

Her Competition

The Best Record-Breaking Performance field is not an easy one. Grant's competitors include Myles Garrett, the Los Angeles Rams defensive end who holds the NFL record for career sacks at a young age; Johannes Høsflot Klæbo, the Norwegian cross-country skiing legend who rewrote yet another record at the World Championships; and Sabastian Sawe, the Kenyan marathon runner whose 2026 performance set a new world standard.

Each of those achievements is significant. But 42 home runs — breaking a 31-year-old record in a sport where single-season HR records are extremely hard to set — is something rare. It is the kind of mark that doesn't fall often and doesn't fall by much. Grant's margin of five is the sort of cushion that speaks to what kind of season she had.

What She's Done Since

Grant wasn't done after the NCAA season. She was selected No. 4 overall in the 2026 AUSL College Draft by the Portland Cascade. In her first week as a professional, she hit the longest home run in AUSL history — 313 feet. The record-breaking didn't stop at the college level.

The AUSL regular season ends July 20. Grant and the Cascade are in the thick of the postseason race. Meanwhile, the ESPYs air tomorrow at 8 PM ET.

What's Next

For Grant, tomorrow night is the culmination of a voting campaign that the softball community has been pushing for three weeks. The winner will be announced live on ABC. Whether she takes home the ESPY or not, the nomination itself is a milestone — it puts a softball player in front of an audience that has never watched a softball game, during one of sports' most-watched awards shows.
