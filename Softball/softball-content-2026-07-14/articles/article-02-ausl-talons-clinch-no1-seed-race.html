Utah Talons Clinch First AUSL Postseason Berth — Bandits Charge Into Final 6-Game Sprint

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 14, 2026

The Utah Talons became the first team to officially clinch a berth in the 2026 AUSL postseason. At 14-6, they hold the top spot in the standings heading into the final week of the inaugural regular season, which wraps up July 20.

But their lead is not comfortable. Not when the team right behind them started the year 0-4 and responded by going on the longest winning streak in AUSL history.

The Standings

With six games remaining in the regular season, the AUSL standings are:

  • No. 1 Utah Talons: 14-6
  • No. 2 Chicago Bandits: 13-7 (1.5 games back)
  • No. 3 Portland Cascade: 11-9

The Talons were first to clinch. Three spots remain for three teams — Bandits, Cascade, and at least one more. But the No. 1 seed question is the one that dominates this final stretch. And it has not been settled.

Why the No. 1 Seed Matters

In the 2026 AUSL postseason format, the No. 1 seed earns a direct bye to the Championship Series. Seeds 2, 3, and 4 compete in a play-in round before reaching the Championship. That means the difference between finishing first and finishing second isn't just bragging rights — it's one fewer game to win, played with more rest, in a short-format playoff where a single loss can end a season.

The AUSL Championship Series is set for July 26-28 at Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa, Alabama. Getting there fresh matters.

The Bandits' Run

The Chicago Bandits started the inaugural AUSL season 0-4. Then they went on a 7-game winning streak — the longest streak in AUSL history. That run built their record from a possible afterthought to genuine contenders for the top seed.

Morgan Zerkle and Sami Williams were central to the streak, with Zerkle contributing key multi-hit performances and Williams providing power. The Bandits finished the streak at 13-7, having gone 13-3 after the 0-4 start — an extraordinary turnaround in a 20-game regular season.

The math: if the Bandits win out (6-0 from here) and the Talons go 4-2, Chicago finishes 19-7 and Utah finishes 18-8. The No. 1 seed would flip.

What the Talons Need to Do

Utah does not need to sweep the final six games to hold the No. 1 seed. But they cannot afford to stumble significantly while the Bandits are playing as well as they have in the past three weeks. Montana Fouts, who pitched three scoreless innings at the MLBx 3-on-3 last Sunday at Citizens Bank Park, returns to regular AUSL duties with the Talons needing her best.

What's Next

Six games. July 14 through July 20. The No. 1 seed in the inaugural AUSL season will be determined by what happens in this final stretch. The Talons have the lead and the postseason berth in hand. The Bandits have the momentum and the history of a 7-game winning streak that already changed the season's narrative once.

Watch the standings. Watch the Bandits. The final week of the AUSL regular season may be its best.
