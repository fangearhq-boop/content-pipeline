PGF Nationals Rosters Are Locked — Five Days Until the Tournament Opens in Irvine

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 14, 2026

The deadline passed last night. As of 11:59 PM PST on July 13, the PGF Nationals rosters are officially locked. No more additions, no more transfers, no last-minute changes. Every team heading to Irvine has exactly the players they have right now.

PGF Nationals Week 1 opens this Saturday, July 19, at Great Park in Irvine, California. Five days from today.

The Freeze Is Done — What That Means

The PGF roster freeze was the final organizational hurdle before tournament play begins. Teams that needed to make roster moves — adding a transfer player, releasing a player, or completing any late paperwork — had to finish that work before the system maintenance window began last night at 10 PM PST.

PGF's systems were offline from 10 PM PST July 13 through 10 AM PST July 14 for scheduled maintenance. Any roster moves not completed before 10 PM last night were not processed. The official freeze time was 11:59 PM PST, but the practical window was 10 PM because of that maintenance downtime.

For most families, this is simply confirmation that the planning phase is over. The rosters they submitted are the rosters they'll compete with.

What Week 1 Looks Like

Week 1 of PGF Nationals features 18U and 16U divisions. Both are among the most competitive age groups in national travel softball, drawing elite programs from across the country. Great Park in Irvine, California is one of the best softball complexes on the West Coast, with multiple fields capable of hosting simultaneous games at the tournament's scale.

Week 2 follows July 26 through August 2 and features the 14U, 12U, and 10U divisions at venues across Orange County.

The Recruiting Dimension

PGF Nationals is not just a tournament. For 18U and 16U players, it is one of the last major showcases before recruiting timelines begin to matter significantly. September 1 is 48 days from today — that is the date the NCAA contact window officially opens for the Class of 2028.

College coaches will be on-site. They will be watching. Every at-bat, every pitch, every defensive play at Great Park starting July 19 matters in ways that go beyond the bracket.

What's Next

Five days. The preparation ends on July 19 when the first pitch is thrown at Great Park in Irvine. Teams will spend this week doing final practices, reviewing scouting, and making sure every player is healthy and ready to compete.

The roster freeze is in the past. The tournament is ahead. For the families who have been tracking this deadline since June — it arrived, it passed, and now only the game itself remains.
