Rachel Garcia's MLBx MVP Moment Was Softball's Biggest Stage of 2026 — Now She Needs to Carry It Home

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 14, 2026

On Sunday afternoon at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, Rachel Garcia was the best player at the inaugural MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3. She scored 18 points in the championship round — the high mark of any competitor in the event. She hit a walk-off home run in the semifinal to eliminate Team Jones. She was named MVP.

On Sunday night, her Texas Volts lost 3-1 to the Carolina Blaze.

That's professional softball in 2026. The regular season doesn't pause for the biggest individual moment of your career.

What Citizens Bank Park Showed

The MLBx 3-on-3 was professional softball's most-watched competitive moment of the year. It replaced the Celebrity Softball Game that MLB had hosted since 2001, and the format — points for home runs and catches, no separation by gender, hot-streak power-ups that could flip a game — put softball players in direct competition with MLB Hall of Famers and NFL stars on equal terms.

Garcia won that competition. She scored 18 points against a field that included Ryan Howard, Jimmy Rollins, Shane Victorino, Andruw Jones, Tiare Jennings, and Alyssa Brito. She won it on Peacock, in front of a Philadelphia crowd, during MLB All-Star Week.

If you wanted a moment to show what professional softball players are capable of on the biggest stage available, Sunday gave you that moment in Rachel Garcia.

Her Résumé

Garcia has earned moments like this. She was the pitcher on UCLA's 2019 Women's College World Series championship team and a two-time USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year. At the Tokyo 2020 Olympics, she earned a silver medal with the U.S. Women's National Team. She brought all of that into the 2026 AUSL season with the Texas Volts, selected in the inaugural AUSL Draft as one of the league's founding players.

Her MLBx performance didn't come out of nowhere. It came from a player with a long track record of rising to high-stakes competition.

What the Volts Need From Her Now

The Texas Volts sit outside the AUSL playoff picture. Portland Cascade is third at 11-9. Texas needs to climb past the Cascade — and do it in six games, with the regular season ending July 20.

Garcia isn't just a player on this roster. She is the player the Volts built around when the AUSL launched. Her presence at Dell Diamond in Round Rock was supposed to be the foundation for a playoff contender. The season has been rocky — NiJaree Canady's struggles on the mound have created headwinds — but Garcia at the plate and in the field is still what this team runs through.

The question is whether the Garcia who walked out of Citizens Bank Park as an MVP can bring that level of play back to the AUSL's final six games. The talent is clearly there. The stage at Citizens Bank Park proved it. The Volts just need it in Tuscaloosa on July 26 — but first they have to get there.

What's Next

Six games between now and July 20. The AUSL Championship is July 26-28 at Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa, Alabama. The Volts need wins to make it. Garcia is the player who can make those wins happen — the same player who just took down every co-ed competitor at Citizens Bank Park and walked away as the most valuable player in professional softball's biggest 2026 showcase.

She knows what she can do. Now she needs to do it one more time. Six more games. Get the Volts to Tuscaloosa.
