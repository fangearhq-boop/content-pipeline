Blaze 3, Volts 1: McKenzie Clark's Homer Stalls Canady's Comeback — Six Games Left

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | July 14, 2026

Three days after NiJaree Canady got her first professional win — a complete game, six strikeouts, 1-0 over these same Carolina Blaze — the Blaze came back and took the series back.

Carolina defeated Texas 3-1 on July 13 at Husky Softball Stadium. It was a steady, methodical win for the Blaze. And it leaves Canady and the Volts with six regular season games left to save their playoff chances.

How the Game Went

The Blaze built an early lead, putting up a 2-0 advantage through the first three innings. The Volts showed some fight in the fifth inning, cutting the lead to 2-1 and keeping the game alive.

Then McKenzie Clark stepped up in the sixth inning and hit a solo home run that pushed the Blaze lead back to 3-1. That run was the difference. The Volts could not respond, and the Blaze secured the win.

Clark's sixth-inning shot was the game's defining moment — an insurance run that came at exactly the right time for Carolina and took the air out of any Texas comeback.

Where Canady Stands

NiJaree Canady arrived in the AUSL as one of the most anticipated pitchers in the inaugural draft. The No. 2 overall pick out of Texas Tech, she rewrote Big 12 pitching records and was the most recognizable name in the 2026 class when the season began.

Her professional debut has been a mechanical recalibration. After struggling through her first several starts — posting a 6.87 ERA entering July 13 — the Volts brought in Tori Nyberg, the Stanford pitching coach who originally recruited and worked with Canady, to help reset her mechanics. The focus: getting Canady back to the "squared up" stance that made her so dominant in college, before a post-injury adjustment shifted her to an "open" stance that disrupted her release point.

The July 10 complete game win looked like the reset was working. The July 13 loss is a reminder that a single win doesn't close the book on an adjustment this significant.

What the Volts Need

The Texas Volts sit outside the playoff picture heading into the final six games. Portland Cascade holds the third postseason spot at 11-9. The Volts need to not only win games but also need help from the rest of the schedule to leapfrog Cascade before July 20.

In a 20-game regular season, every game is roughly 5% of your season. The Volts are down to 30% of their schedule remaining. Canady — the player they drafted No. 2 to anchor this team — needs to be at the level she reached on July 10, not the level she showed in her first three starts.

What's Next

The AUSL regular season ends July 20. The Championship Series is July 26-28 in Tuscaloosa. Canady's story is not over. She has six games left to write a different ending — and the Volts need her to do exactly that to make any of it matter.

The July 10 win showed what she is capable of. The July 13 loss is the obstacle. The next six games will determine which version of NiJaree Canady shows up for the first AUSL Championship in history.
