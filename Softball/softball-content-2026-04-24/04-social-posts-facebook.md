## STORY 1: Kendall Wells / OU-Georgia Series

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM ET
No. 1 Oklahoma opens a pivotal home series against No. 11 Georgia TODAY at Love's Field — and Kendall Wells is sitting five home runs away from tying the most storied record in college softball.

Wells has slugged 32 home runs this season, putting her within reach of Lauren Espinoza's all-time NCAA single-season record of 37, set at Arizona back in 1995. The Georgia native has been an absolute force all year: .383 batting average, 60 RBIs, and the SEC Freshman of the Week award under her belt. Her power surge has been the offensive engine behind Oklahoma's 42-6 season and 15-3 SEC record.

But this isn't just about individual milestones. Oklahoma is tied with Alabama at 15-3 in the SEC, and the race for the No. 1 seed at the SEC Tournament (May 5-9 in Lexington) is very much alive. Win this series over Georgia and OU stays on top. Lose it and the tiebreaker math gets complicated.

Game 1 is Friday afternoon at Love's Field on ESPN+. If Wells connects tonight, history is watching.

## STORY 2: Canady AUSL Golden Ticket

#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET
NiJaree Canady was already the best pitcher in college softball. Now she's officially the future of the professional game, too.

On Thursday night at Rocky Johnson Field, Texas Tech's ace became the first player to receive an AUSL Golden Ticket for the 2026 college draft. In a surprise on-field ceremony, AUSL Commissioner Kim Ng stepped onto the field and presented Canady with the honor in front of her team and the crowd — a moment that immediately went viral in the softball world.

And then? Canady went out and pitched the Red Raiders to a 9-1 run-rule win over Iowa State, throwing five innings, allowing just two hits and one run. Business as usual.

Texas Tech is 45-4 and holds the No. 2 ranking in the NFCA poll. Canady's Golden Ticket isn't just an individual honor — it's a signal that the AUSL is here, that professional women's softball is growing, and that the best players in college softball are now household names even before they turn pro.

This is a big deal. And this is what the future of softball looks like.

## STORY 6: WCWS Countdown

#### Long-Form Post — 5:30 PM ET
Sixteen days. That's how long it is until the 2026 WCWS selection show.

On Sunday, May 10 at 7:00 PM ET on ESPN2, the 32-team NCAA Tournament bracket will be unveiled. Regionals begin May 15 at 16 host sites. The Women's College World Series opens May 28 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City.

And for the first time in 2026, all 32 teams will be seeded — with all 16 top seeds earning the right to host their own regionals. That's a new format, and it changes the calculus for every program in the country. There's no longer a gap between "regional host" and "top 16 seed." They're the same thing.

This weekend's games are already filtering into the seeding math. Oklahoma-Georgia. Nebraska-Iowa. FSU-Georgia Tech. Every win or loss shifts RPI, strengthens or weakens national seed arguments, and determines whether teams host or travel in May.

Bracketology from Softball America projects the SEC taking roughly half of the regional hosting spots. The Big 12 (Texas Tech), ACC (FSU), and Big Ten (Nebraska) are expected to round out the field.

The next 16 days matter more than any stretch of the season.
