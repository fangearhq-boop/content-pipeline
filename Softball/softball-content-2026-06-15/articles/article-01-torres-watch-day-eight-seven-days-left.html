Torres Watch: Day 8, Seven Days Left and Still No Answer

By Sarah Mitchell

June 15, 2026

Eight days. No commitment. The silence from Isa Torres has become the defining story of the 2026 college softball offseason.

The D1 Softball Player of the Year — and the most coveted transfer portal target in the history of women's college softball — enters Day 8 of the 15-day window without a decision. The portal closes June 22. Seven days remain. And with the window now past the halfway point, the pressure is only intensifying for the programs waiting on her answer.

The Numbers Behind the Wait

To understand why the world is watching, start with the stats. In 2026, Isa Torres slashed .530/.591/.978 with 78 runs scored — every single figure a Florida State program record. She recorded a hit in 16 consecutive at-bats, setting an NCAA record that may stand for decades. The ACC named her conference Player of the Year. D1Softball named her the national Player of the Year. By any measure, she was the most dominant offensive player of the 2026 college softball season.

She has one year of eligibility remaining. Wherever she lands, that program becomes an instant national title contender.

The Window Is Past Halfway

The significance of Day 8 is more than just a number on a calendar. When the portal window crosses the midpoint, the dynamics shift. Programs that have been patient begin to press. Backup options start receiving more attention. Coaches who have held roster spots open for a specific target must weigh the cost of continued patience against the risk of missing on the second option too.

Torres, in theory, faces no such pressure — a player of her caliber will always have options. But the program she eventually chooses will need time to finalize roster logistics. Every day matters for everyone involved.

Texas or Texas A&M?

The recruiting picture has narrowed to two programs. Texas and Texas A&M have been linked to Torres throughout the window, and both have compelling cases.

Texas is the two-time defending national champion. Head coach Mike White returns Teagan Kavan — the only two-time Most Outstanding Player in Women's College World Series history — along with Katie Stewart and Kayden Henry as seniors for the 2027 three-peat bid. The Longhorns also just signed Samantha Swan, the Georgetown, Texas catcher who grew up in the same hometown as Torres. The geographic and cultural pitch is real: Torres is from Georgetown, TX, approximately 30 minutes north of the UT Austin campus.

Texas A&M offers a different proposition. Head coach Trisha Ford has the resources and the ambition. Landing Torres would immediately elevate the Aggies to national title conversation and give Ford a player capable of carrying a roster on her shoulders.

What's Next

Seven days remain before the June 22 deadline. Torres has given no public indication of a timeline. What is certain is that the decision — when it comes — will reshape the 2027 national title picture. A program with Torres in the lineup is a different program entirely. Texas and Texas A&M are both ready to make history with her. The wait continues.
