Texas Tech Is Quietly Winning the Portal: Heavener Commits, Pickering Expected

By Jordan Reeves

June 15, 2026

While the softball world has been fixated on Isa Torres's recruiting drama, Texas Tech has been methodically building what may be the strongest transfer class in the country — and doing it quietly.

On June 13, Jayden Heavener — ranked the No. 1 pitcher in the transfer portal — committed to the Red Raiders. It is a landmark addition for any program, and for a Texas Tech team that just finished as WCWS runner-up, it sends an unmistakable message: Jerry Glasco's program is not content to settle for second place.

Heavener: The No. 1 Arm Available

Heavener's commitment to Texas Tech gives the Red Raiders an elite arm to anchor their rotation heading into 2027. When the most coveted pitcher in the transfer portal chooses your program over every other school in Division I, it reflects the program's standing in the national landscape. For Texas Tech, which was one win away from the national title this past season, that standing has never been higher.

The Red Raiders return a deep and experienced roster. Adding Heavener at the top of the rotation gives Glasco one of the most dangerous pitching profiles in the country for 2027. A program that was already dangerous is now potentially elite.

Pickering: The DNT Signal Points to Lubbock

The Heavener addition becomes even more significant in the context of Kasidi Pickering's situation.

Pickering — the Oklahoma outfielder ranked No. 2 overall by On3 — entered the transfer portal on June 8 with a "Do Not Contact" tag. In the transfer portal ecosystem, a DNT designation carries a clear meaning: the player has already privately chosen her next program and is using the portal only as a procedural step. She is not taking calls. She is not taking visits. The decision has been made.

Everything in the public record points toward Texas Tech. Pickering publicly celebrated the Red Raiders' WCWS runner-up finish on social media with language that went well beyond a neutral fan's reaction. Multiple recruiting insiders have connected her to Lubbock. The circumstantial evidence is consistent and credible.

In 2026 at Oklahoma, Pickering hit .370 with 20 home runs and 61 RBIs. Over her OU career, she posted a .384 batting average with 50 home runs and 169 RBIs. She was a First-Team All-American in 2025. She is not a complementary player — she is an impact bat who would be the featured hitter in any lineup.

What This Would Mean for 2027

If Pickering's commitment to Texas Tech is confirmed as expected, the Red Raiders will have added the No. 1 pitcher and the No. 2 overall transfer in the same portal class — immediately following a WCWS runner-up finish. That combination puts Texas Tech firmly in the conversation for the 2027 national title, regardless of what happens with Torres, Beachum, or any other headline portal name.

Programs that chase the headliners and miss can sometimes end up with nothing. Programs that identify complementary targets and execute their recruiting plan cleanly often end up with the strongest class. Texas Tech appears to be doing the latter — and doing it at a level the rest of the country is only beginning to appreciate.

What's Next

Pickering's formal commitment announcement could come at any point before the June 22 portal deadline. Heavener's commitment is confirmed. The Red Raiders' 2027 roster is taking shape in a way that makes them a genuine national title threat. Jerry Glasco's program is moving with purpose and precision. The rest of the country is watching — and most of them are realizing they're already behind.
