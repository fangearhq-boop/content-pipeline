Portal Tracker: Day 8 of 15 — The Window Is Past Halfway, Seven Days to Go

By Emily Rawlings

June 15, 2026

The 15-day NCAA softball transfer portal window opened June 8 and closes June 22. Today is Day 8. The window has crossed its midpoint. Seven days remain. Here is a complete look at where things stand.

Committed

These players have announced their new programs:

Samantha Swan — Graduate transfer catcher from Arizona State (.383 BA / 12 HR / 55 RBI / .917 career OPS / First-Team All-Big 12) → Texas. Fills the biggest roster hole on the two-time defending champion Longhorns roster.

Kailey Plumlee — Left-handed pitcher from Tennessee (redshirt freshman / 4 years eligible) → Alabama. Joins an Alabama rotation that posted a 1.57 ERA (second nationally) in 2026.

Kaiah Altmeyer — Utility player from Arizona → Texas. Adds depth to the Longhorns' 2027 roster.

Maddy Azua — Sun Belt Conference Pitcher of the Year (1.95 ERA / 26-13 record / 244.2 IP in 2025) → Texas A&M. A marquee rotation addition for the Aggies who are simultaneously pursuing Torres.

Jayden Heavener — No. 1 pitcher in the transfer portal → Texas Tech. A program-defining commitment for the WCWS runner-up.

Madison Hartley — All-Big 12 slugger → Oklahoma State. Fills a run-producing hole in the Cowgirls' lineup.

Katie Wetteland — Mountain West Freshman of the Year → Arkansas. A high-ceiling young transfer to a program that can develop her further in the SEC.

Still Available

Isa Torres (On3 No. 1 / Softball America No. 1 / D1 Softball Player of the Year) — .530 BA / .978 SLG / .591 OBP / 78 runs. Florida State shortstop. Texas and Texas A&M pursuing. 7 days remain.

Jaysoni Beachum (Softball America No. 1 / On3 No. 3) — .417 BA / 16 HR / 66 RBI / .549 OBP. Florida State infielder. No commitment as of June 15. Uncommitted for the full 8 days.

Moriah Polar (On3 No. 5) — .555 BA / 101 hits / 2nd-Team All-American. Purdue outfielder. Nation's leading hitter still available at the midpoint of the window.

Appears Decided (Do Not Contact)

Kasidi Pickering (On3 No. 2) — .370 BA / 20 HR / 61 RBI at Oklahoma. Career: .384 BA / 50 HR / 169 RBI. Entered portal with "Do Not Contact" tag. Reporting and social media strongly linked to Texas Tech. Formal announcement expected before June 22.

What's Next

The second half of any portal window is where the major decisions come. Programs that have been patient through eight days are now approaching the point where their options narrow. The remaining headline names — Torres, Beachum, Polar — will each make their decisions in the coming seven days, and those announcements will define which programs enter the 2027 season as national title contenders. June 22 is the hard deadline. The countdown is on.
