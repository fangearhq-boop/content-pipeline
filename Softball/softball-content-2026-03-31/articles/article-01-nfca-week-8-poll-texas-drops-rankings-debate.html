NFCA Week 8 Poll Is Out: Does Texas Stay No. 1 After Sunday's Loss?

By I Love Softball Staff | March 31, 2026

Every Tuesday at 10 AM ET during the college softball season, 31 Division I head coaches submit their ballots to the NFCA/GoRout Top 25 Coaches Poll — and this Tuesday, for the first time in 2026, there is a genuine debate about who deserves the top spot.

Texas entered the week as No. 1 in the NFCA poll, riding what had been the nation's longest active win streak at 29 games. The Longhorns were the defending national champions, and entering the A&M series at 28-1, they looked bulletproof.

Then Sunday happened.

No. 15 Texas A&M walked into McCombs Field and beat No. 1 Texas 9-7, snapping the 29-game streak in a series finale that featured a six-run sixth inning and a head coach ejection. The Longhorns rallied with back-to-back solo home runs from Reese Atwood and Katie Stewart in the seventh, but it wasn't enough. Texas still won the series 2-1, improving to 31-2 overall and 8-1 in SEC play.

The question now: does one loss — in a series Texas ultimately won — cost the Longhorns the No. 1 ranking?

The Case for Keeping Texas at No. 1

There is a compelling argument to leave Texas at the top. The Longhorns are 31-2 overall — still one of the best records in the country. They are 8-1 in the SEC, the sport's most competitive conference. They are the defending national champions. They won the series against A&M 2-1.

One game does not define a season, and coaches who vote in the poll have historically been reluctant to penalize a team that loses one game in a series it ultimately won. The Longhorns remain one of the most complete teams in college softball — deep in the circle, powerful in the lineup, and experienced in the biggest moments.

In the Week 7 poll, Texas earned all but one first-place vote. That kind of consensus takes more than one loss to dismantle.

The Contenders Pushing for No. 1

But the coaches casting ballots this week have options — and some of those options made very compelling arguments over the weekend.

Texas Tech was No. 2 last week and has been the most consistent team in the Big 12. The Red Raiders won their series against UCF and have maintained an impressive record all season. If coaches decide Texas should drop, Texas Tech is the most straightforward replacement.

Florida State may be the most intriguing argument. The Seminoles swept Clemson on the road over the weekend — the third ACC sweep in a row — and are on a 21-game winning streak. They are 32-4 overall and a perfect 9-0 in ACC play. Head coach Lonni Alameda earned her 1,000th career win with Sunday's victory. At No. 8 last week, FSU would need to make a dramatic jump, but the metrics of this team are extraordinary.

Oklahoma won its series at LSU 2-1 and entered the week at No. 5. The Sooners are 34-3 and 8-1 in the SEC, tied with Texas at the top of the conference standings. Kendall Wells hit her 26th home run in the LSU win. Oklahoma has been as dominant as any team in the country over the last month.

Who Falls: Florida and Tennessee

While the debate at the top centers on who moves up, the bigger movement may come further down the poll.

Florida entered the week at No. 3 with a 31-2 record, but suffered a stunning series loss to Arkansas — capped by Dakota Kennedy's walk-off three-run homer on Sunday that gave the Razorbacks a comeback 6-4 win from a 4-0 deficit. The Gators are 33-4, 9-3 SEC, but losing two straight series to a team ranked outside the top 10 will cost them in the poll.

Tennessee has been sliding since losing a series to Florida two weeks ago and has not regained momentum. The Lady Vols will almost certainly fall further in the Week 8 poll.

The Bigger Picture: National Seeding Implications

Beyond the No. 1 debate, the Week 8 poll carries real implications for national seeding heading into the postseason. Programs hosting NCAA Regionals typically come from the top 16 in the national polls at the end of the regular season. The current reshuffling — with Florida State rising, Florida falling, and Oklahoma asserting itself in the SEC — will shape those seeding conversations for weeks to come.

The poll is a snapshot, not a verdict. But in a season this competitive, every position on the ballot matters.

What's Next

The Week 8 NFCA/GoRout Division I Top 25 Coaches Poll results are available at nfca.org. The next major rankings moment will come after this weekend, when Texas travels to Tuscaloosa to face Alabama in what shapes up as the most significant series of the regular season. That matchup, beginning April 2, could reshape the SEC standings and the national ranking picture simultaneously.

The 2026 college softball season is in full swing — and the rankings conversation is only getting more interesting.
