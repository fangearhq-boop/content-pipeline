She Turned Down the Money. Then She Struck Out 1,000 Batters. Maya Johnson's Story Is College Softball at Its Best.

By I Love Softball Staff | March 31, 2026

Maya Johnson had a decision to make.

The transfer portal offers had come in — real numbers, from programs with bigger stadiums, bigger rosters, and bigger national footprints. Sources reported the offers were in the range of "a couple hundred thousand" in NIL money, a significant sum in college athletics. For a redshirt senior pitcher who leads the nation in strikeouts, the market was eager.

She said no. She said no to all of it.

Johnson chose to stay at Belmont University, a mid-major program in Nashville playing in the Missouri Valley Conference, because she had unfinished business there and because the relationships she had built meant more than any dollar figure.

On Saturday, during Week 8 of the 2026 college softball season, she reached 1,000 career strikeouts — a milestone that D1Softball recognized as one of the defining moments of the weekend.

A Season That Started With a Statement

Johnson did not ease into the 2026 campaign. In her first start of the season, against Missouri State, she threw a perfect game — 83 pitches, 15 strikeouts, not a single baserunner. It was a statement delivered on Opening Day, a reminder that she had not lost anything from the version of herself that made second-team All-American a year ago.

Then, in a result that rattled the college softball world, Belmont beat No. 15 Georgia. A mid-major program, in Nashville, with Maya Johnson in the circle, went to Athens and won.

The 2026 season was underway, and Johnson was exactly who she'd always been.

The Career in Numbers

Going into the Week 8 strikeout milestone, Johnson led the entire country with 203 strikeouts on the season — not mid-major strikeouts, not conference-adjusted strikeouts, but more than any pitcher in Division I college softball. Her 2025 season produced 366 strikeouts in 207 innings pitched, with a 1.53 ERA, 24 wins, 23 complete games, and 11 shutouts. She won MVC Pitcher of the Year and the MVC Tournament MVP.

The career total of 1,000 strikeouts, reached during Week 8, is the cumulative product of all of it — every hitter she faced, every late-game jam she worked out of, every game she started knowing the opponent had watched film on her and was ready.

She got them anyway.

What Loyalty Looks Like in 2026

The NIL era has changed college softball in ways that are still being understood. Transfer portals, NIL deals, and the financial leverage of Power 4 programs have reshaped recruiting and roster-building across the sport. Players follow money and opportunity, and no one can blame them for it.

Which is what makes Maya Johnson's choice so striking. She had leverage — real leverage — and she used it to stay. She decided that 1,000 strikeouts at Belmont was worth more than the money that would have come with going somewhere else.

That kind of loyalty to a program, a community, and a group of teammates is rare. It is also exactly the kind of story that reminds fans why college sports still matter.

What's Next

Johnson and Belmont continue their Missouri Valley Conference schedule with the NAIA postseason positioning on the line. Johnson's career trajectory — 1,000 strikeouts, All-American credentials, and a reputation as the sport's most dominant mid-major pitcher — ensures that her influence on college softball will outlast her playing days. Whether that comes in coaching, development, or as an ambassador for the sport, the game will not lose her entirely when her senior season ends.

For now, she is still pitching. Still striking people out. One thousand down, and still counting.
