Gatorade Bath and a Thousand Wins: Lonni Alameda Makes History at Florida State

By I Love Softball Staff | March 31, 2026

The final out was recorded. The scoreboard read 7-3. And then Lonni Alameda was drenched in Gatorade.

On Sunday night at Doug Kingsmore Stadium in Clemson, South Carolina, Florida State's head coach became the 12th active Division I softball head coach to earn 1,000 career wins — a milestone she acknowledged with characteristic grace while trying to minimize its significance.

"I'm just grateful to be part of this game for a long time," she said on the ACC Network postgame broadcast.

Her players, judging by the Gatorade shower, felt differently.

A Career Built Over 18 Seasons

Lonni Alameda is in her 18th season as Florida State's head coach, and the numbers tell a story that speaks for itself. She holds a 1,000-388-3 record — a .720 winning percentage that ranks ninth-best among active coaches who have reached the 1,000-win plateau. Of her 1,000 victories, 843 came at Florida State, making her one of the most successful and loyal coaches in the sport's history.

She did not build this program overnight. When Alameda arrived in Tallahassee, Florida State was a solid ACC program with a history of solid seasons. What she created over the following 18 years was a consistent championship contender — a program that produces NFL draft picks, Olympians, and All-Americans on a regular basis, and that has become one of the sport's most recognizable brands.

One thousand wins is the validation of that work.

Sunday's Milestone Victory

The win itself was a reflection of everything Alameda's program has been doing right in 2026. Florida State came to Clemson for a three-game ACC series and swept it — three straight wins on the road, extending their season-long winning streak to 21 games and their ACC record to 9-0.

Sunday's game unfolded efficiently. The Seminoles got on the board early when Isa Torres and Jaysoni Beachum led off with consecutive infield singles, Ashtyn Danley drew a walk to load the bases, and Anna Hinde drove in a run on a groundout to give FSU a 1-0 lead. From there, Makenna Reid came on in relief and was dominant, retiring the first eight batters she faced. Reid improved to 5-1 on the season. Danley entered in the seventh and closed it out cleanly, retiring all three batters she faced.

The final score was 7-3, the win was the 1,000th of Alameda's career, and the Gatorade arrived on schedule.

The 2026 Season as Context

The timing of the milestone is fitting. Florida State is having one of its best seasons under Alameda's watch. The Seminoles are 32-4 overall, 9-0 in ACC play, and riding a 21-game winning streak that is the nation's longest active streak entering this week. Junior shortstop Isa Torres is having one of the greatest offensive seasons in NCAA softball history — batting .655 with an NCAA-record 16 consecutive hits earlier this month.

Torres contributed two home runs during the Clemson sweep. The team is deep in the circle, dangerous in the lineup, and playing with the kind of confidence that comes from sustained excellence.

In a season already defined by records and remarkable performances, Alameda's 1,000th win stands apart — not because of the number, but because of what it represents: two decades of dedication to a program, a sport, and the players who pass through it.

What's Next

Florida State returns home to Tallahassee to host Notre Dame starting Thursday at 6 PM at the Seminole Softball Complex. The Seminoles will look to extend their winning streak to 22 games and continue their case for a top national seed in the NCAA Tournament. Coach Alameda, win No. 1,001 awaits.
