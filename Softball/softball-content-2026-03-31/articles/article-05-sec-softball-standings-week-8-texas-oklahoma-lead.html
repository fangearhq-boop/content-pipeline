SEC Softball Standings After Week 8: Texas and Oklahoma Are Tied at the Top

By I Love Softball Staff | March 31, 2026

Eight weeks into SEC play, the conference softball race has developed exactly as the most optimistic observers hoped it would: competitive at the top, chaotic in the middle, and with every meaningful series carrying national implications.

After the full slate of March 29 results, here is where every major contender stands — and what it means for the race to come.

Texas (31-2 Overall, 8-1 SEC)

The defending national champions are still at the top of the conference standings despite Sunday's loss to Texas A&M that snapped their 29-game win streak. Texas's one conference loss came in a series they ultimately won 2-1. They are the deepest and most experienced team in the SEC, with a lineup that can erupt for double-digit runs on any given day and pitching depth that extends three deep.

The test this week: a trip to Tuscaloosa to face Alabama starting Thursday, April 2. It is the most significant series of the regular season — and Texas comes into it having just been reminded they can be beaten.

Oklahoma (34-3 Overall, 8-1 SEC)

Oklahoma is the other team tied at 8-1 in the SEC, and a case can be made that the Sooners are the nation's most complete program right now. They are 34-3 overall — the best record in the conference — and have won eight of their nine SEC series. Freshman Kendall Wells (26 home runs) headlines an offense that has shattered the SEC single-season home run record through 32 games. Miali Guachino, Jennifer Rocha, and others give the Sooners pitching depth that can match anyone in a postseason series.

Oklahoma hosts Missouri this week, a game that should be manageable before the schedule intensifies in late April.

Florida (33-4 Overall, 9-3 SEC)

Florida has played the most conference games of the top teams and has the most conference wins — but also the most losses. The Gators lost two straight series to Arkansas, including Sunday's stunning walk-off defeat in which they blew a 4-0 seventh-inning lead. Ace Keagan Rothrock remains one of the best pitchers in the SEC, and Taylor Shumaker's 16-game hitting streak is a legitimate weapon in the lineup.

But the path to a No. 1 national seed is narrowing. With more games to play against Texas and Oklahoma still on the schedule, Florida needs to stabilize. Losing a series to Arkansas — again — does not help the national seeding conversation.

Alabama (29-2 Overall, 6-1 SEC)

Alabama has played fewer conference games than the teams above them, but has just one conference loss. The Crimson Tide is coming off a bye week and arrives at the Texas series on Thursday rested and motivated. Head coach Patrick Murphy has managed his roster carefully, and ace Jocelyn Briski is one of the most difficult pitchers in the sport to solve.

The Alabama-Texas series starting April 2 is the biggest game on the schedule for both teams. A series win for Alabama could push them to the top of the conference standings. A series win for Texas would reassert their position after Sunday's stumble.

Arkansas (30-5 Overall, 5-4 SEC)

Arkansas has quietly become the conference's most dangerous disruptor. The Razorbacks have now won four straight series over quality opponents — including back-to-back series wins over Florida. Sunday's walk-off win, in which Dakota Kennedy hit a three-run homer off the first pitch to complete a comeback from 4-0 down in the seventh, was the kind of performance that builds program identity.

Arkansas at 5-4 in the SEC is not at the top of the standings, but their momentum is real, their pitching is solid with Saylor Timmerman at 7-0, and they have now established a clear pattern of success against Florida. If the bracket falls right in the postseason, they are a dangerous out.

What's Next

The coming weeks will define this conference season. Texas at Alabama (April 2) is the marquee event — two programs with different paths to this moment facing each other for the first time in 2026. Oklahoma, Florida, and Arkansas all have meaningful conference matchups that will continue to shape the standings and the national seeding picture.

The SEC is not just the nation's best softball conference — it is the most compelling storyline in the sport right now, and the race is far from over.
