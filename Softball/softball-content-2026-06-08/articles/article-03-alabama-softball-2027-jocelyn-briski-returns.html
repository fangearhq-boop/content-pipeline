Briski Is Back: Alabama's Best Pitcher Returns and the 2027 Title Race Gets Complicated

By Jordan Reeves June 8, 2026

The One Thing That Matters Most

When programs start their 2027 planning, the first question is almost always about pitching. You can fill an offense through the portal. You can develop position players. You can recruit your way into contention at almost every spot on the field. But elite pitching — the kind that carries a team through a tournament, that wins games when everything else is going wrong — does not arrive on demand.

Alabama already has it locked in for 2027. Jocelyn Briski, the SEC Pitcher of the Year, is expected to return for another season in Tuscaloosa. That one fact changes everything about where the Crimson Tide sits in the offseason calculus.

What Briski Built in 2026

Alabama's 2026 pitching staff was historically dominant by any meaningful measure. The Crimson Tide went 49-7 on the season, earned the No. 1 overall seed in the NCAA Tournament, and finished with a team ERA of 1.60. The staff threw 22 shutouts — a number that speaks not just to raw talent but to sustained, consistent execution over the course of a 50-game season.

Briski was the anchor of that staff. She was named the SEC Pitcher of the Year, the most meaningful individual pitching honor in the conference, and she earned it by being the kind of arm that opposing offenses plan entire weeks trying to solve. Alabama's tournament run ended when Texas Tech — a team built almost entirely of transfers — eliminated them in the WCWS bracket. But no one watched that program and concluded the problem was the pitching.

Why This Changes Alabama's 2027 Outlook

The difference between a rebuild and a reload is the starting point. Programs that lose their ace spend two or three years trying to find a replacement. Programs that keep their ace spend two or three weeks in the portal filling the roster around her.

Patrick Murphy has built Alabama into one of the most consistent programs in college softball over his tenure. He has won multiple national championships, recruited elite talent, and kept the Crimson Tide at or near the top of the sport for years. With Briski returning, his 2027 offseason is defined by one task: build an offense worthy of the pitching already in place.

That is a good problem to have. The transfer portal gives Murphy the tools to address it directly. Alabama has the brand, the facilities, and the track record to attract portal prospects. With Briski locked in as the selling point for any pitcher or position player considering Tuscaloosa, Murphy heads into the next 15 days with a compelling pitch.

The Reload Framework

Alabama has had years where everything clicked and years where they fell short at the wrong moment. The 2026 season was the latter — a historically strong regular season that ended too early in the bracket. But the foundation for a response is already in place. Briski gives the pitching staff a clear identity. Murphy gives the program a coaching staff with a history of winning. The portal gives both of them a mechanism to address whatever needs addressing.

The programs that should be watching carefully are the ones planning to use the transfer portal to improve in 2027. Alabama operating as a reload — not a rebuild — means they stay relevant faster than almost any other team in this conversation.

What's Next

Watch Alabama's portal activity over the next 15 days. Murphy will be targeting offensive additions to complement Briski's return, and any commitment that lands in Tuscaloosa carries the implicit message: this program is not starting over. It is building on what was already one of the best pitching foundations in the country.
