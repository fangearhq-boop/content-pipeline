Illinois Hires Jenna Hall. Minnesota Brings in a Dynasty Builder. The Big Ten Moves Fast.

By Emily Rawlings June 8, 2026

Two Vacancies, One Week, Two Different Solutions

When Illinois and Minnesota both parted ways with their head coaches in recent weeks, the question that followed was the same for each program: who comes next? Both programs had underperformed in the Big Ten. Both had work to do. And both found their answers — arriving at very different solutions through very different philosophies.

Illinois went home. Minnesota went hunting for a winner.

Jenna Hall Comes Home to Illinois

The Illinois hire is built around continuity and loyalty. Jenna Hall is a former Illinois softball All-American — she played in Champaign, she learned the program, and she carries that institutional knowledge back to her alma mater. After college, Hall built her coaching career at Ohio University, where she spent four years as head coach before the opportunity to return to Illinois became available.

Illinois Director of Athletics Josh Whitman made the hire official and signed Hall to a five-year contract — a clear signal that this is not a short-term fix but a committed, long-term investment in building the program. Hall becomes only the third head coach in Illinois softball program history, which speaks to the institutional weight of the moment.

The alumni hire carries both advantages and expectations. Hall understands Illinois because she lived it. The players she recruits and the culture she builds will be shaped by someone who genuinely belongs to this program. But alma mater hires are also evaluated against a standard that outside hires avoid — she will be measured against what the program has always said it could be.

Minnesota Imports a Proven Winner

Minnesota's hire tells a different story. The Gophers went looking for the best possible coaching credentials and found them at Augustana University in Sioux Falls, South Dakota, where Gretta Melsted had spent 20 seasons building one of the most accomplished programs in Division II softball.

Melsted's record at Augustana is difficult to argue with: 813 wins, 313 losses across two decades, a 2019 Division II national championship. She was named to the NFCA Hall of Fame. She is, by any reasonable measure, a program builder with a history of winning at every level she has competed.

The challenge for Melsted at Minnesota is different from what she built at Augustana. Division I recruiting, Big Ten competition, and the expectations of a Power Four fanbase operate on a different scale than D2 athletics. But coaches who build winning cultures tend to bring those cultures with them — and Minnesota is betting that Melsted's track record translates.

Two Hires, One Signal

What both hires share is a clear institutional message: Illinois and Minnesota are serious about turning these programs around. Neither search dragged out for months. Neither took a cautious, interim-first approach. Both programs identified their candidate, made the hire, and are now entering the transfer portal window with new coaches already in place and ready to recruit.

That timing matters more than it might seem. Coaches who are hired after the portal window closes miss the first — and often most important — cycle of roster building. Hall and Melsted are in position to be active from Day 1, and both will need to be aggressive with the transfers available over the next 15 days to begin reshaping their rosters for 2027.

What's Next

Both Hall and Melsted will be active in the transfer portal through June 22. Hall's familiarity with the Big Ten landscape and her existing recruiting relationships at Ohio give her a head start. Melsted's track record of developing talent over two decades means she knows how to evaluate players who fit her system. Watch both programs closely over the next 15 days — their portal activity will define how quickly each rebuild takes shape.
