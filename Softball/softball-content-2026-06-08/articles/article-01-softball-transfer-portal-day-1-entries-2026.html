The Portal Is Open: Day 1 Entries Confirm the 2027 Shuffle Has Begun

By Sarah Mitchell June 8, 2026

The Preview Is Over

The NCAA Division I softball transfer portal opened this morning, and the players everyone had been anticipating are already in. The 15-day window — running from June 8 through June 22 — is the most compressed and consequential stretch of the softball offseason, and it is underway right now.

The names that every program director had circled on their calendars are confirmed. Jaysoni Beachum. Tori Edwards. Samantha Swan. Tennessee's Saviya Morgan and Kailey Plumlee. The first wave of the 2026-27 portal cycle has arrived, and Day 1 announcements set the tone for everything that follows.

Beachum: The One-Year Headliner

Jaysoni Beachum entered the portal Monday via social media, and the announcement landed exactly as anticipated — with immediate, league-wide attention. The Florida State third baseman is ranked the No. 1 transfer in this cycle, and the numbers explain why. She hit .417 in 2026, drew 54 walks, and posted a .549 on-base percentage — the third-highest in FSU program history. She added 16 home runs and 66 RBI while earning NFCA Third-Team All-American recognition.

Beachum has one year of eligibility remaining. That means any program that lands her is getting an All-American contributor for exactly one season — a powerful, targeted addition for contenders that need one proven piece to push them over the top. Within hours of her announcement, coaches across the sport were in contact. The bidding, informal and unofficial, begins immediately in a 15-day window like this one.

Edwards: The Long-Term Investment

If Beachum is the highest-profile one-year rental in this class, Tori Edwards is the most valuable long-term piece. The LSU redshirt sophomore entered the portal as a center infielder with two full years of eligibility remaining. She set the LSU single-season program record with a .802 slugging percentage in 2026 and earned All-American honors — following up a 2025 campaign in which she was named the SEC Freshman of the Year.

The distinction matters: a program that signs Beachum is making a one-year bet. A program that signs Edwards is building around her for two full seasons. Her combination of proven production and remaining eligibility makes her arguably the most valuable asset available in this cycle, even if she doesn't draw the same immediate headlines as the No. 1 ranked transfer.

Swan and the Ambition Factor

Samantha Swan's entry carries a different kind of significance. The Arizona State outfielder hit .383 with 12 home runs, 55 RBI, and 21 doubles in 2026. She earned All-Big 12 First Team honors and landed on the NFCA All-West Region Third Team. Arizona State reached the Super Regionals. This is not a player leaving a program in decline.

Swan chose the portal because she wants more — a shot at a deeper tournament run, a different fit, a program where her final season aligns with her championship ambitions. Her entry signals something about the state of the portal: the window is no longer just for players escaping bad situations. It is now a tool for elite players seeking the best possible path to their goals.

Tennessee: Confirming What Was Expected

Sophomore outfielder Saviya Morgan and freshman pitcher Kailey Plumlee both confirmed their entries as soon as the window opened. Both had announced their intent to transfer before the portal was officially live, so their entries were expected — but the confirmation marks the official start of Karen Weekly's most consequential offseason in years.

Combined with the graduation of Karlyn Pickens, the portal departures mean Tennessee heads into the next 15 days needing to address multiple roster holes simultaneously. The program has the brand, the recruiting pull, and one of the most decorated coaches in the sport. The question is how quickly they can convert that equity into portal commitments.

What's Next

The portal window runs through June 22. Commitments can come quickly in a 15-day window — the compressed timeline creates urgency for both players and programs, and decisions that might take months in a normal recruiting cycle happen in days here. New entries are expected throughout the week. Watch the On3 and D1Softball transfer trackers daily for the latest additions. The 2027 season is being built right now.
