### STORY 4: NiJaree Canady Is 0-4. Here's What That Actually Means.

#### Facebook Long-Form Post

NiJaree Canady is 0-4 with a 6.87 ERA in the Athletes Unlimited Softball League.

Those numbers look wrong next to her college career: 104-24 record, 1.09 ERA, 1,127 strikeouts across 796 innings at Stanford and Texas Tech. She is, by most measures, the greatest pitcher in recent college softball history. The transition to professional ball has not been smooth.

But the Texas Volts are not giving up. And neither should anyone watching.

Here's what's happening. Canady came to the Volts after a 2025-26 season at Texas Tech in which she went 29-7 with 1.87 ERA and 253 strikeouts — elite numbers, but a departure from the 0.57 and 0.73 ERAs she posted at Stanford. The reason, according to Sports Illustrated, traces back to mechanics changes that came after injuries during her Tech tenure. Something in the delivery that made her historically unhittable in Palo Alto got altered. Professional hitters found it.

The Volts' response: they called Stanford. Specifically, they reached out to Tori Nyberg, the Stanford pitching coach who originally recruited Canady and coached her through her record-breaking seasons. Nyberg understands the mechanics that made Canady what she was. She's now working to rebuild them.

This is what professional development looks like when an organization believes in its player. It's not a quick fix, and it shouldn't be treated as a failure of potential. It's a pitching coach problem, and the Volts are treating it like one.

The AUSL regular season ends July 20. The Championship Series runs July 25-27. That's the timeline.

What happens between now and then matters. But so does what comes after it.
