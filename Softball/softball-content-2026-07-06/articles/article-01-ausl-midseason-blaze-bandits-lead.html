AUSL Midseason Report: Karlyn Pickens Is Running the League

By Sarah Mitchell

July 6, 2026

Two weeks remain in the 2026 AUSL regular season. The championship picture is forming — and one pitcher is defining it.

Karlyn Pickens, the No. 1 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL College Draft, is 2-0 with a 1.47 ERA for the Carolina Blaze. She has won both of her starts cleanly — a complete-game run-rule over the Portland Cascade, 11-2, and a 9-1 six-inning win over the Texas Volts. She was officially named to the 2026 AUSL All-Star Cup roster, leading the pitcher category in fan voting. That selection comes with a trip to Rosemont, Illinois, where the All-Star Cup runs August 8 through August 30 at Parkway Bank Sports Complex.

None of this has looked like an adjustment period. It has looked like the best pitcher in the league doing what the best pitcher in the league does.

The Bandits Are Right There

Carolina is not the only team with a case. The Chicago Bandits entered last week at 14-7 and demonstrated exactly why they belong at the top of the field — coming back from a 3-0 deficit against the Texas Volts on July 4 to win 4-3 on a fifth-inning four-run rally. They have won more games than any team in the league through this stretch. Their lineup produces runs, their defense has been reliable, and their experience in high-pressure games shows.

If the Carolina Blaze are the story of the AUSL's first half, the Chicago Bandits are the reminder that the story isn't finished.

The Defining Contrast

Professional sports runs on comparison, and the 2026 AUSL has produced the clearest one imaginable: Pickens versus NiJaree Canady.

Canady, the No. 2 overall pick, is 0-4 with a 6.87 ERA pitching for the Texas Volts. The numbers represent something genuinely unexpected for a pitcher who finished her college career at Texas Tech with a 104-24 record, 1.09 ERA, and 1,127 strikeouts. She is adjusting to professional ball after mechanics alterations caused by injuries during her Tech tenure. The Volts have brought in Tori Nyberg — the Stanford pitching coach who originally coached Canady to ERAs of 0.57 and 0.73 — to help reset those mechanics.

Pickens, at 2-0 and 1.47 ERA, requires no such intervention. The contrast is not mean-spirited — Canady's career remains extraordinary and the adjustment remains in progress — but it is honest. The two picks who were supposed to define the league's debut year have defined it. Just not in the ways anyone predicted.

The Rest of the Field

The six AUSL teams — Carolina Blaze, Chicago Bandits, Oklahoma City Spark, Portland Cascade, Texas Volts, and Utah Talons — each play a 25-game regular season that ends July 20. The Spark, Cascade, and Talons all have stories of their own to tell in the remaining two weeks. Portland's Megan Grant (No. 4 overall pick) is hitting for the Cascade while simultaneously running for a 2026 ESPY. Every team has players who came from dominant college careers and are proving what that translates to in professional competition.

What's Next

The AUSL regular season ends July 20. A play-in game follows on July 23. The Championship Series — best-of-three — runs July 25-27. The Blaze and Bandits are the early favorites, but two weeks is long enough for the picture to change. The league's first champion will be decided in a weekend series. Whether that team is the one that survived a midseason Pickens ERA or the one that figured out how to beat her is the question the second half of the season will answer.
