9 Days to Vote for Megan Grant at the ESPYS — Don't Waste Them

By Jordan Reeves

July 6, 2026

The ESPYS air on July 15 at 8 PM ET on ABC. Nine days from now, the biggest sports award show of the year will hand out its trophies in front of a national audience, and Megan Grant could be holding one of them.

Grant is nominated for Best Record-Breaking Performance at the 2026 ESPYS. Fan voting is open through July 15, one vote per day, at ESPN.com. The votes are not symbolic. They determine who wins.

What She Did

In 2026, Grant broke the NCAA Division I single-season home run record with 42. The previous record was 37, set by Patty Espinoza in 1995 — a mark that stood for 31 years before Grant erased it. She finished her UCLA career with 91 home runs, the most in Bruins program history.

She did all of this in 63 games while anchoring one of the most watched college softball programs in the country. She earned First Team All-American honors. She was a top-three finalist for USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year. And she is now playing professionally with the Portland Cascade in the AUSL, where she hit her first pro home run on June 10.

The record she broke had waited three decades for someone to reach it. Grant made it look possible in a single season.

The Competition Is Serious

Grant is not the heavy favorite here, and honesty requires saying so. Her competition includes Myles Garrett, the NFL defensive end who has become one of the most dominant players in the league's history. Johannes Høsflot Klæbo, a Norwegian cross-country skier with a World Championship resume. Sabastian Sawe, the Kenyan marathon runner who has been rewriting distance records.

These are athletes from sports that command enormous mainstream audiences and wall-to-wall media coverage throughout the year. Softball does not have that same built-in infrastructure. The voting gap between a sport's dedicated fan base and the casual sports audience can be wide.

That's exactly why the next nine days matter.

What a Win Would Mean

A Grant win at the ESPYS would mark the first time in years that college softball earned a major sports awards moment on national prime-time television. Not a mention. Not an honorable note. A winner, on stage, holding a trophy, at 8 PM on ABC.

Softball's growth over the past decade — in participation, viewership, and cultural recognition — has been real. An ESPY win does not define that growth, but it amplifies it. It puts the sport in front of people who may not have been paying attention to the WCWS, the AUSL, or the transfer portal. It is a chance to say: this is what softball looks like now.

What's Next

Vote at ESPN.com — once per day, every day, through July 15. The ESPYS air July 15 at 8 PM ET on ABC and stream on the ESPN App. Nine days. One vote per day. If every person who watched the NCAA tournament or followed the AUSL votes daily through July 15, Grant wins. The math is there. The motivation is there. What's needed is follow-through.
