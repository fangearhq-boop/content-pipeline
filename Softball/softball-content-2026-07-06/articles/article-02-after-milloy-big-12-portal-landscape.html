Milloy Is Gone. Here's What the Big 12 Portal Map Looks Like Now.

By Emily Rawlings

July 6, 2026

Tia Milloy committed to Arizona State on July 5. The 41-day portal saga is over, and the biggest name in the 2026 transfer market has a destination.

For the programs that spent weeks in active pursuit — Florida State, Tennessee, and Texas Tech among them — the answer arrived after the longest wait of the offseason. Now the question shifts: what does the landscape look like for everyone who came up short, and who moves next?

Florida State's Unfinished Business

No program entered this portal cycle with more urgency than FSU — and no program exits the Milloy chapter with more remaining work. The Seminoles lost Isa Torres (.530 batting average) to Texas in June. They lost Jaysoni Beachum (.417 BA, 16 home runs) to Florida on the final day of the portal window. And now Milloy — the left-handed utility bat they needed most — chose Arizona State.

What FSU still has is real. Francik returns, bringing back the reigning ACC Player of the Year. Ella Dodge and Lexi Edmiaston combine for 22 home runs from the outfield. The program's foundation is not broken. But the roster hole that opened with three portal departures is not fully closed, and the transfer portal contact window remains open through July 31.

Tennessee and Texas Tech

Both programs had active conversations with Milloy's camp and came away without a commitment. Tennessee's situation is further complicated by a coaching staff overhaul this spring that introduced new faces to a program still rebuilding from its WCWS absence. The Vols will direct their remaining portal energy toward depth pieces that fit the new staff's system.

Texas Tech is in a different position. The Red Raiders' portal class was already rated No. 1 by Softball America this offseason — the Polar, Heavener, and Pickering additions formed one of the most decorated transfer classes in the sport's history. Adding Milloy would have been an exclamation point on a class already standing on its own. Missing her does not change the foundation. It just means 2027 arrives with one fewer layer of depth than the program hoped for.

Arizona State, Newly Competitive

ASU's win of the Milloy sweepstakes has implications beyond roster construction. The Sun Devils brought in a two-year piece with Power Conference experience, proven postseason exposure through Oklahoma's program, and an OPS of .986. That's not a project — it's a contributor from Day 1.

In the Big 12, where Arizona State now competes alongside Texas, Texas Tech, Oklahoma, and others, landing a player of Milloy's caliber is a recruiting signal. The Sun Devils are in the conversation. The July 13 roster freeze — seven days away — puts a formal close on this chapter of their offseason.

Who's Still Available

With approximately 133 players remaining in the transfer portal and the window open through July 31, the post-Milloy market will move without the same headlines. Smaller names, quieter announcements, depth pieces rather than stars. But the commitments still reshape rosters, and the programs that execute in the final three weeks of the window will enter fall camp with advantages that compound over time.

What's Next

The transfer portal contact window closes July 31. PGF Nationals begins July 19, where coaches shift attention from portal evaluation to recruiting the Class of 2028. The September 1 contact window — 57 days away — opens direct outreach to 2028 prospects for the first time. The Milloy era of this offseason is finished. The next era starts immediately.
