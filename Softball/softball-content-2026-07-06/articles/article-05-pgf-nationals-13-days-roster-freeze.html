PGF Nationals Is 13 Days Away — And the Roster Freeze Comes First

By Emily Rawlings

July 6, 2026

The transfer portal's biggest story ended yesterday. The next chapter of the softball offseason begins today.

PGF Nationals 2026 — the nation's premier youth softball showcase — is 13 days away. The tournament runs July 19 through July 26 at the Great Park sports complex in Irvine, California. Week One brings 18U and 16U divisions. Week Two brings 14U. Between now and the first pitch, one critical deadline falls: the roster freeze.

The Freeze Comes First — July 13

Programs must finalize their rosters before July 13, the roster freeze deadline. Seven days from today. Tia Milloy's commitment to Arizona State on July 5 was exactly the type of move coaches needed to lock in before the freeze — a portal piece confirmed, a roster slot filled, a roster plan complete.

Under the House Settlement's 25-scholarship cap, roster construction decisions carry more weight than in any previous era of college softball. Every spot on a roster represents a scholarship allocation. Programs that filled their gaps before July 13 arrive at PGF with a clear picture of what they have and what they're evaluating. Programs that didn't — FSU remains the most prominent example — arrive with a different kind of urgency: building a list of prospects who might still fill a need through the July 31 portal close date or the incoming Class of 2028.

What PGF Nationals Is

For fans who haven't followed it closely, PGF Nationals is the most important evaluation event in youth softball. The nation's elite travel ball programs — the clubs that produce the players who will sign with Power Conference schools over the next four years — compete across multiple divisions at the same venue over two weeks. College coaches attend in force.

The players at PGF this year are the Class of 2028 — the athletes who will be freshmen at the schools they choose for the 2028-29 season. Coaches cannot contact them directly until September 1. But they can watch them, build rankings, and prepare the lists they'll work from when that window opens.

PGF Nationals is, in that sense, the homework. September 1 is when the real work begins.

September 1 Is 57 Days Away

The next major date after PGF Nationals is September 1 — 57 days from today. That's when the NCAA contact window opens for the Class of 2028. It's also when the informal recruiting race that started at PGF becomes a direct one: coaches calling families, players weighing offers, and the next wave of recruitment playing out in real time.

The programs that did their PGF homework — scouted thoroughly, built accurate rankings, identified the right targets — enter September 1 with advantages that are hard to make up later in the cycle.

What's Next

Roster freeze July 13. PGF Nationals July 19. September 1 contact window opens. The summer isn't over — it's transitioning from the season of portal moves to the season of evaluation and early recruitment. The biggest story of the 2026 offseason wrapped up yesterday. The next one is just getting started in Irvine.
