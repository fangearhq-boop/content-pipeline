### STORY 4: NiJaree Canady and the Road to Getting Right

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

Before NiJaree Canady threw a professional pitch, her college numbers looked like this: 104-24, 1.09 ERA, 1,127 strikeouts in 796 innings over a career at Stanford and Texas Tech. Her final college season: 29-7, 1.87 ERA. Time Magazine put her on their list of the Top 100 Most Influential Athletes in 2026.

Then she signed with the Texas Volts as the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL Draft and threw her first professional pitch.

Through the current AUSL season, Canady is 0-3 with a 6.87 ERA. The Texas Volts are out of playoff contention.

Sports Illustrated reported this week that the Volts reached out to former Stanford pitching coach Tori Nyberg to work with Canady. The reason: injuries Canady sustained during her time at Texas Tech altered her mechanics and body positioning. Those changes weren't always visible at the college level, where her stuff was so overpowering it didn't matter. At the professional level, where hitters have seen more and guess better, the mechanical inconsistencies are catching up.

The Volts calling Stanford isn't a panic move. It's an investment. They took Canady with a top-two pick because they believe she can be the best pitcher in the AUSL. Getting her there is a process.

College dominance doesn't travel automatically to professional baseball or professional softball. The first time through the pro adjustment is often the hardest. For Canady, it's happening in public — and the Volts are choosing to respond by bringing in help rather than running away from the challenge.

Keep watching. The talent is there.
