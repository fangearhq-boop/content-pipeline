Utah Talons Clinch 2026 AUSL Postseason Berth in 7th-Inning Comeback

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 16, 2026

The Utah Talons are the first team to clinch a 2026 AUSL postseason berth — and they did it the way you'd expect a first-place team to: down in the seventh, then not.

The Clinch Game

Facing the Oklahoma City Spark, the Talons trailed heading into the seventh inning. Then Jadelyn Allchin came up and hit her fourth home run of the season to tie the game. Sharlize Palacios followed with a 2-run blast to give Utah the lead for good.

Final: Utah Talons 5, Oklahoma City Spark 4.

Megan Faraimo pitched all seven innings. She struck out 10. The Talons clinched with a complete game from their ace and a late-inning lineup that knew what to do when the game was on the line.

The Standings Picture

The Talons sit at 14-6, the best record in the AUSL. They hold the No. 1 seed, and the No. 1 seed earns a direct bye to the Championship Series — which begins July 25 at Davis Diamond on the Texas A&M campus in College Station, Texas. Game 1 of the Championship Series airs on ABC, making it the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television.

The rest of the playoff picture: the Chicago Bandits dropped their July 15 game to the Carolina Blaze 12-6 and sit at approximately 13-8 in second place. The Portland Cascade hold the third and final playoff spot. Four regular-season games remain before July 20's end date. The play-in between the second and third seeds is July 23.

The OKC Spark beat the Texas Volts 7-2 on July 15 — Jailyn Ford threw a complete game with 8 strikeouts — but remain outside the playoff positions.

Why the Talons Have Led All Season

The Talons have combined pitching depth with a lineup that punishes mistakes. Faraimo is one of the most reliable arms in the AUSL. Allchin and Palacios have provided the power when the game was on the line — not just against OKC, but throughout a season that has kept Utah ahead of a Bandits team that never stopped chasing.

Four games remain. The Talons will spend them preparing for the Championship Series. The Bandits and Cascade will spend them fighting for position.

What to Watch

Tonight, the Bandits travel to Durham, NC to face the Carolina Blaze at Smith Family Stadium (7 PM ET). The Talons play the Cascade at Hillsboro Ballpark in Hillsboro, Oregon (10 PM ET). Both games have real consequences for the teams that need them most.

One team has already punched its ticket. Three more are still fighting for theirs.
