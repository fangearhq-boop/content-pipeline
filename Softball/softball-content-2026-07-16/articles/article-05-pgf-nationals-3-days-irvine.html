PGF Nationals Opens in 3 Days — What to Know Before Sunday at Great Park

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 16, 2026

The 2026 PGF National Championships open Sunday, July 19, at Orange County Great Park in Irvine, California. Three days out. Rosters locked since July 14. Teams are traveling.

Week 1: What's Playing and Where

Week 1 runs July 19 through July 25. The 18U Platinum, 16U Premier, and 16U Platinum divisions compete this week across three venues in Irvine: Orange County Great Park (8000 Great Park Blvd), Bill Barber Park, and Harvard Park.

All games stream live on PGF.TV. If you're not making the trip to Irvine, that's the place to watch.

Week 2: The Younger Divisions

Week 2 follows July 26 through August 2 and features the 14U, 12U, and 10U divisions. Opening ceremonies for Week 2 will be held at Huntington Beach Central Park. Games will be played across Irvine and Huntington Beach venues.

Teams to Watch in the 16U Bracket

Arizona Storm Appel finished third at last year's 16U championship and returns with one of the most-watched rosters in the 16U bracket. Their 2026 lineup includes commits to Alabama (Torynn Slaughter), Auburn (Peyton Valentine), Oklahoma (Payton Westra), and Northwestern (Goose Goodwin). They are not the only team arriving with Division I commitments, but they may be the most concentrated collection of Softball America Top 100 recruits in one program.

The Recruiting Context

For families with Class of 2028 prospects — the current crop of eighth-graders who will enter college in the fall of 2028 — the September 1 NCAA Division I recruiting contact window is 46 days away. PGF Nationals is the last major evaluation event before that window opens.

College coaches cannot make off-campus contact with Class of 2028 players until September 1. What they can do before that date is watch games and form their evaluations. PGF is the final showcase where those evaluations happen in a competitive, high-volume setting before the phone calls start.

The Bottom Line

If you are playing, coaching, or recruiting at PGF this year, you have three days. The rosters are set. The schedule is posted. The travel is booked.

The biggest travel ball showcase of the summer starts Sunday at Great Park.
