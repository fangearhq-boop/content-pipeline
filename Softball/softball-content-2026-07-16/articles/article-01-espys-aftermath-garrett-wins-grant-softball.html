Myles Garrett Wins the ESPY, but Softball Was on That Stage

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 16, 2026

The 34th ESPYs are over. Myles Garrett won Best Record-Breaking Performance. Megan Grant did not.

Those are the facts. Here's the rest of the story.

What Garrett Did

Myles Garrett, the defensive end for the Los Angeles Rams, set the NFL single-season sack record in 2025 with 23. That's a number that stood alone when the season ended, surpassing the previous mark in a league that has been tracking the statistic since 1982. The award was presented at Lincoln Center by Olympic snowboard champion Chloe Kim and comedian Tiffany Haddish. Garrett also won Best NFL Player — two ESPYs for the night.

That's a legitimate win for a legitimate record. There is no argument to be made that the award went to the wrong person.

What Grant's Nomination Meant

Here is what is also true: the Best Record-Breaking Performance category at the 34th ESPYs included a UCLA outfielder who hit 42 home runs in a single season of college softball, breaking a mark that had stood since 1995. Laura Espinoza's 37 home runs at Arizona survived 31 years of challengers before Grant stepped to the plate at Jackie Robinson Stadium and ended the record's run.

The people who put that ballot together decided that what Grant did — in a sport that receives a fraction of the broadcast time, the coverage, and the investment of the NFL — deserved to be weighed against a professional football sack record, six gold medals at a single Winter Olympics, and a world-class marathon performance.

That's the vote that matters for softball. Not the winner, but the nomination.

The Broadcast

The 34th ESPYs aired on ABC at 8 PM ET from the David H. Koch Theater at Lincoln Center in New York City, hosted by Marcello Hernández. Other major winners included Jalen Brunson of the New York Knicks for Best Male Athlete, A'ja Wilson of the Las Vegas Aces for Best Female Athlete, and Shohei Ohtani for Best Play.

Millions of viewers who will never watch a college softball game saw Megan Grant's name on the screen in that company. They may not know the numbers. They may not know who Laura Espinoza was. But they know that someone from softball was considered.

What's Next

Grant is still playing. The Portland Cascade are 11-9 in the AUSL with four games remaining in the regular season. She leads the league in home runs. The 2026 AUSL Championship begins July 25 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas, with Game 1 airing on ABC — the first professional softball game ever broadcast on network television.

She may be on that network again nine days from now. This time with a championship on the line.

The ESPYs were last night. The season is still going. And the sport is still moving forward.
