SEC Softball Semifinals: Florida Faces Alabama, Cinderella Georgia Takes On Texas

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | May 8, 2026

After a quarterfinal round that delivered multiple upsets and a 10-run game, the 2026 SEC Softball Tournament arrives at its semifinals Friday — and the bracket is nothing short of compelling. Four teams remain in Lexington. Two spots in Saturday's championship game are available. Hundreds of WCWS dreams are on the line.

Friday's schedule at John Cropp Stadium:

  • No. 3 Florida vs. No. 2 Alabama — 5:00 PM ET on ESPN
  • No. 9 Georgia vs. No. 6 Texas — Approximately 7:30 PM ET on ESPN

Florida vs. Alabama: The Marquee Matchup

Florida survived one of the wildest games of the tournament on Thursday, edging Auburn 10-9 in the quarterfinals. The Gators showed they can score in bunches — but also that they can be pushed. Alabama, meanwhile, dominated Arkansas 7-1 and looked like the cleanest team left in the bracket.

Alabama entered conference tournament week ranked No. 4 in the NFCA poll and has been one of the most consistent programs in the SEC all season. A win Friday would put them one game away from the SEC title and a strong case for a top national seed heading into Sunday's Selection Show.

Florida's offense is dangerous enough to beat anyone. But Alabama's pitching staff has been one of the conference's best, and after Thursday's clean win, the Tide carries momentum into this matchup.

Georgia vs. Texas: The Cinderella Game

Fresh off Thursday's stunning upset of No. 1 Oklahoma, Georgia comes into the semifinal as the tournament's most dangerous team — not because they're the most talented on paper, but because they've already proven they can beat anyone. The Bulldogs authored the largest comeback win in SEC Tournament history less than 24 hours ago. That kind of momentum doesn't disappear overnight.

Texas presents a very different challenge than Oklahoma. The Longhorns shut out Ole Miss 6-0 on Thursday behind ace Teagan Kavan, who was dominant. Texas is a balanced club with strong pitching, and they'll be motivated to avoid becoming another Georgia upset victim.

This is the game of the day from a narrative standpoint. Will the Bulldogs keep their magical tournament run alive? Or does Texas's pitching shut the door?

What's Next

The SEC Championship game is Saturday. The four remaining teams — Florida, Alabama, Georgia, and Texas — are all already assured of NCAA Tournament berths as SEC members. But a tournament title carries automatic qualifier status and, for some programs, could influence national seeding for hosting a regional.

The NCAA Selection Show is Sunday, May 10, at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. The committee will consider all conference tournament results through the weekend when making seeding decisions. Win Friday, and a team's national seed case gets significantly stronger.
