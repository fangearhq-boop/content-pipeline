Georgia Stuns No. 1 Oklahoma 10-5, Sooners Eliminated in Biggest SEC Tournament Upset

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | May 8, 2026

No. 9 seed Georgia did something Thursday night that almost nobody predicted: eliminated No. 1 Oklahoma from the SEC Softball Tournament quarterfinals, winning 10-5 in what officials called the largest comeback win in SEC Tournament history.

The game was played Thursday evening at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky. Georgia's Jayda Harrison had a career night, and the Bulldogs came through with clutch offense at every turn. Oklahoma — the top overall seed, the No. 1 ranked team in the country — simply could not match Georgia's energy or execution when it mattered most.

The loss ends Oklahoma's season at 48-8. The Sooners won the SEC regular season title and were among the most dominant teams in the country from wire to wire. But the SEC Tournament, played over a compressed week in Lexington, has a way of leveling the playing field — and Georgia took full advantage.

Wells's Record Chase Ends

The Oklahoma elimination carries a subplot that stings for Sooner fans: freshman catcher Kendall Wells entered the SEC Tournament with 36 home runs, one shy of the all-time NCAA single-season record held by Lisa Espinoza of Arizona since 1995. Wells never got her 37th. She finishes her freshman year at 36 — a number that already makes her one of the greatest single-season power hitters in the sport's history, even without the record.

The record will have to wait — at least for this season.

Georgia Makes History

For Georgia, Thursday's victory is historic in its own right. The Bulldogs not only pulled off one of the great upsets of the college softball season — they authored the largest comeback win in SEC Softball Tournament history. Head coach Tony Baldwin's squad came into the tournament as a No. 9 seed and took down the nation's No. 1 team. That is not a small thing.

The Bulldogs advance to Friday's semifinal, where they face No. 6 Texas — the Longhorns who shut out Ole Miss 6-0 on Thursday behind pitcher Teagan Kavan. Georgia, riding the momentum of Thursday's shocker, won't be anyone's underdog pick after what they just did to the Sooners.

What's Next

Georgia plays Texas in the SEC Tournament semifinals Friday at approximately 7:30 PM ET on ESPN. The winner advances to Saturday's SEC Championship game — and a berth in the NCAA Tournament is already secure for all remaining teams. But a conference title would mean automatic qualification and, potentially, a hosting opportunity.

The NCAA Selection Show airs Sunday, May 10, at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. With Oklahoma now out, the No. 1 national seed picture has been completely reshuffled — and the remaining conference tournaments, including Friday's SEC semis, will play a role in how the committee ranks the field.
