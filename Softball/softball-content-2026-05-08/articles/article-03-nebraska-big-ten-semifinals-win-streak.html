Nebraska Beats Michigan 4-2, Extends Win Streak to 19 Games Before Big Ten Semifinals

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | May 8, 2026

Nebraska softball hasn't lost in nearly a month. The Huskers beat Michigan 4-2 in the Big Ten Tournament quarterfinals Thursday at Maryland Softball Stadium in College Park, Maryland, extending the nation's longest active win streak to 19 consecutive games.

Pitcher Jordy Frahm was brilliant in the victory. She went all seven innings, struck out nine batters, and allowed just four hits and two runs — one earned. Nebraska jumped out to a 3-1 lead in the first inning and never surrendered that advantage.

The Huskers are now 44-6 on the season, and they advance to Friday's Big Ten Tournament semifinal against No. 4 Indiana at 5:00 PM ET on the Big Ten Network.

A Season for the Record Books

Nebraska came into this season with Big Ten title expectations — and they've delivered beyond what anyone could have anticipated. The Huskers won their first-ever Big Ten regular season title this year, finishing 23-1 in conference play, tying the Big Ten record for most conference wins in a season.

The 19-game win streak is the longest active in Division I softball. It is also the second-longest in program history. Nebraska has found a way to win through pitching — Frahm has been as consistent as any pitcher in the country over the last month — and through timely offense that puts runs on the board early.

National Seed Picture Brightens

Thursday's events nationally couldn't have gone better for Nebraska's national seed case. No. 1 Oklahoma was eliminated by Georgia in the SEC Tournament — a stunning development that reshuffled the top of the national seed picture. Nebraska, ranked No. 2 in the NFCA poll entering tournament week, now has a clear path to claiming the No. 1 national seed for the NCAA Tournament if the committee rewards their remarkable season.

A Big Ten Tournament championship would only strengthen that case. Nebraska has won every game they've played since mid-April. There's no indication they're slowing down.

What's Next

Nebraska faces Indiana in the Big Ten Tournament semifinals Friday at 5:00 PM ET on the Big Ten Network from College Park, Maryland. A win puts the Huskers in Saturday's conference championship game. The NCAA Selection Show is Sunday, May 10, at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 — and how Nebraska finishes this weekend could determine whether they host a regional and receive a top national seed.
