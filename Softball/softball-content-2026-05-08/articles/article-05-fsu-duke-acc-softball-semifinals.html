FSU and Duke Both Advance, Set for ACC Softball Semifinals in Charlottesville

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | May 8, 2026

Both of the ACC Softball Tournament's top seeds are still standing. No. 1 Florida State and No. 2 Duke each advanced out of Thursday's quarterfinals at Palmer Park in Charlottesville, Virginia, setting up a pair of marquee semifinal matchups on Friday.

FSU held off No. 9 Georgia Tech 2-1 in a tight defensive contest. Duke was far less tested, blowing out No. 10 North Carolina 16-8 in what became one of the most lopsided games of the tournament week nationally.

FSU: Tight Win, Big Stakes

Florida State's 2-1 win over Georgia Tech on Thursday was exactly the kind of grind that tournament softball often requires. The Seminoles, at 43-8, entered conference tournament week as one of the most consistent programs in the ACC. Getting through a resilient Georgia Tech squad with their pitching intact matters heading into today's semifinal.

FSU is one of the better national seed candidates in the ACC field, and a conference tournament title would reinforce that position heading into Sunday's Selection Show. In Friday's semifinal, they'll face the winner of Stanford vs. Louisville, which played Thursday in a quarterfinal.

Duke: Offense on Display

Duke didn't just win Thursday — they sent a message. The Blue Devils' 16-8 victory over North Carolina was dominant offensively, and it positions Duke as one of the more dangerous teams left at Palmer Park. Duke is the No. 2 seed in the tournament and one of the ACC's leading programs this season.

On Friday, Duke faces the winner of Virginia Tech vs. Virginia, with the semifinal scheduled at 3:30 PM ET on the ACC Network.

What's Next

Friday's ACC Tournament semifinals run in Charlottesville starting at 1:00 PM ET on the ACC Network, with the second game approximately 35 minutes after. The ACC championship game is Saturday, with the automatic qualifier and a conference title on the line.

The NCAA Selection Show follows Sunday, May 10, at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. The committee will factor all conference tournament results through the weekend. For FSU and Duke, winning the ACC outright would provide a significant boost to their national seeding arguments as the final weekend of the regular postseason schedule plays out.
