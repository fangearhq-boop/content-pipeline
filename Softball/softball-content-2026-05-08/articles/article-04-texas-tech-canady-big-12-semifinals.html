Canady Dominant Again: Texas Tech Shuts Out Baylor 7-0, Red Raiders Advance to Big 12 Semifinals at 51-5

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | May 8, 2026

Five days after throwing the first perfect game of her career, NiJaree Canady went right back to work. The Texas Tech ace took the circle Thursday in the Big 12 Tournament quarterfinals in Oklahoma City and made Baylor look helpless — five innings, one hit, six strikeouts, zero runs in a 7-0 Red Raiders shutout.

Texas Tech improves to 51-5 with the win. They advance to Friday's Big 12 Tournament semifinal at Devon Park, where they'll face No. 5 seed Kansas at 3:00 PM CT (4:00 PM ET).

Canady's Historic Stretch

What NiJaree Canady is doing right now is difficult to put into context. On May 2, Senior Day, she threw the first perfect game of her career — the sixth in Texas Tech program history — in an 8-0 win over Baylor. On Thursday, she matched up against that same Baylor lineup and allowed exactly one hit in five innings before giving way to Texas Tech's bullpen.

Canady's season statistics speak to how dominant she has been. The Red Raiders became the first Division I program to reach 50 wins when they hit that mark last week — and much of that success runs through Canady's performances in the circle.

For Baylor, facing Canady twice in less than a week was not a recipe for success. Thursday's result — 7-0 — was even more decisive than the perfect game shutout five days earlier.

Texas Tech's National Seed Case

At 51-5, Texas Tech holds the best record in the country. They are ranked No. 3 in the NFCA poll. With Oklahoma now eliminated from the SEC Tournament, Texas Tech's case for a top national seed — potentially the No. 1 overall seed — is getting stronger by the game. A Big 12 Tournament title would be the exclamation point on a historic regular season and a strong argument to the NCAA Selection Committee.

The Red Raiders have won 12 consecutive games. They have not lost since April 22. And with Kansas up next Friday, there is every reason to believe this streak continues.

What's Next

Texas Tech faces Kansas in the Big 12 Tournament semifinals Friday at 3:00 PM CT at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. The winner plays in Saturday's Big 12 championship game. The NCAA Selection Show is Sunday, May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 — and how Texas Tech finishes conference tournament week will factor heavily into seeding for the national tournament.
