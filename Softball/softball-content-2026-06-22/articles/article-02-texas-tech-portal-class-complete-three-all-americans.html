Texas Tech's Portal Class Is Complete — Three All-Americans and One Goal

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 22, 2026

The verdict is in, and it is historic.

With the transfer portal closing today, Texas Tech's 2026 recruiting haul is officially sealed — and Softball America has already rated it the No. 1 transfer class in the country. The Red Raiders, coming off a 61-8 season and a second consecutive trip to the WCWS championship game, assembled three consensus All-Americans in the span of 15 portal days. No program in the country came close.

This is what a program that just fell one game short does when given the chance to reload.

The All-American Trifecta

Texas Tech's three headlining additions enter Lubbock with the kind of pedigrees that programs spend years trying to recruit out of high school — let alone the portal.

Kenna Polar spent three seasons at Purdue, where she posted the best batting average in the nation at .555 with 101 hits. She is a corner outfielder with elite contact skills who makes hard contact to all fields. Her .555 average in 2026 was not a fluke — it is the continuation of a career .436 average that makes her one of the most reliable hitters in college softball.

Jayden Heavener comes from LSU, where she went 13-9 with a 2.81 ERA in 2026. The rising junior has two years of eligibility remaining, which means Texas Tech is getting at least two full seasons from the pitcher rated as one of the best in the transfer portal. She gives the Red Raiders an ace who can take the ball in the biggest games.

Kasidi Pickering arrives from Oklahoma, where she hit .370 with 20 home runs and earned All-American recognition. She was ranked the No. 2 overall transfer by On3 heading into the portal, and the Red Raiders held off considerable competition to land her. Pickering adds power and athleticism to an outfield that was already dangerous.

The Supporting Cast

Beyond the three headliners, Texas Tech made additional moves. Pitcher Hailey Maestretti arrived from Utah, where she earned All-Big 12 honors, giving the Red Raiders further depth in the pitching circle. Outfielder Zoie Bernard came over from Michigan State to add depth to the lineup.

The complete portal class — Bradi Gallaway, Polar, Heavener, Pickering, Maestretti, and Bernard — gives Texas Tech head coach Gerry Glasco a roster that looks capable of winning the national championship that twice slipped through their fingers.

The 2027 Mission

Texas Tech's motivation is simple and singular: Texas has beaten them in the WCWS championship game twice. The Red Raiders have NiJaree Canady — one of the greatest pitchers in college softball history — finishing her career with a national title still elusive. They have a program culture built around winning. And now they have a transfer class rated first in the nation.

The question is no longer whether Texas Tech is good enough to compete for a title. The question is whether anyone — including a Texas team that added D1 Softball Player of the Year Isa Torres — can stop them.

What's Next

With the portal now closed, Texas Tech shifts to integrating their new additions into the program. Fall ball will be the first test of how Heavener fits with Canady in the pitching rotation, and how Polar and Pickering complement each other in the lineup. The early read on 2027 power rankings puts the Red Raiders squarely in the top two — alongside the defending two-time champion Texas Longhorns. The rematch everyone wants is already on the horizon.
