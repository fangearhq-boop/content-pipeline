Portal Closes: The Final Scoreboard and 2027 College Softball Power Rankings

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | June 22, 2026

The 2026 NCAA softball transfer portal has officially closed.

The first-ever 15-day window — June 8 through today, June 22 — is complete. More than 120 players entered the portal, and more than 84 committed to new programs. The 2027 college softball landscape has been completely reshaped in two weeks. Now it is time for the final accounting: who won, who improved, and where every top program stands heading into next season.

The Winner: A Tie at the Top

Two programs emerged as the clear winners of the 2026 portal — and they happen to be the two teams that met in the WCWS championship game.

Texas built the most talented transfer class. Adding D1 Softball Player of the Year Isa Torres from FSU — a Georgetown, TX native who described her commitment as coming home — alongside All-Big 12 catcher Reagan Swan (also from Georgetown), plus utility pieces in Lincoln and Altmeyer, gives two-time defending champion Texas an obscene amount of talent. Teagan Kavan and Katie Stewart are returning for a potential three-peat. Torres is one of the most accomplished college softball players in recent history. The Longhorns may be better in 2027 than they were in 2026, which is a remarkable thing to say about a program that just won back-to-back national championships.

Texas Tech assembled the most All-American-loaded class. Kenna Polar (.555 BA), Jayden Heavener (LSU ace), and Kasidi Pickering (OU All-American) headline a haul rated No. 1 nationally by Softball America. The Red Raiders went 61-8 and lost the WCWS Final twice to Texas. They responded by signing three of the top transfers available. If Texas is the unstoppable force, Texas Tech is building the immovable object.

The Big Mover: Florida

Florida's portal window closed with one of the most impactful single acquisitions in the entire class. Jaysoni Beachum — the Softball America No. 1 transfer, a .417 hitter with 16 home runs — committed to the Gators on the final day of the portal. Paired with Duke transfer Kairi Rodriguez, Tim Walton's program enters 2027 as a genuine national title contender. Florida belongs in the top-three conversation.

The Steady Performers

Nebraska returns the core of a 51-6 squad that reached the WCWS for the first time since 2013, with Jordy Frahm leading one of the most dynamic offenses in the country. Portal additions supplement a foundation that is already built for sustained contention.

South Carolina made a strong, targeted haul: Gold Glove outfielder Bella Foran and Tennessee transfer Saviya Morgan give the Gamecocks an improved outfield and a legitimate path to the postseason in 2027.

Arkansas added Mountain West Freshman of the Year Madison Wetteland and UCF utility player Kendall Yarnell under head coach Dave Engel. The Razorbacks' first WCWS appearance in program history in 2026 was not a fluke — they are building something real in Fayetteville.

Tennessee played defense intelligently — adding South Carolina second baseman Courtney Shelton while retaining senior pitcher Hailey Mardjetko for a final season in Knoxville. The Vols stay relevant.

FSU started a difficult rebuild by landing Tennessee transfer Ella Dodge (No. 10 overall portal player) and Stetson first baseman Nicole Edmiaston. The losses of Torres and Beachum to rivals are significant. The rebuild will take time — but Alameda has a plan.

2027 Early Power Rankings

  1. Texas — Three-peat or bust. Torres + Kavan + Stewart is an all-time lineup.
  2. Texas Tech — Three All-Americans + Canady's final season. Built to win now.
  3. Florida — Beachum transforms a solid program into a title contender overnight.
  4. Nebraska — Frahm leads a WCWS-tested core back for another run.
  5. South Carolina — Quiet but effective portal work. Morgan + Foran make them dangerous.

What's Next

The portal window is closed. Programs can still finalize arrangements with players already entered, but the 15-day window for new additions is over. The focus now shifts to fall ball, 2027 preseason rankings, and early recruiting for the high school class. The next chapter of college softball's transfer portal era begins the moment today ends — with programs already thinking about June 2027 and the next window. For now, Texas and Texas Tech sit atop the mountain, with Florida and Nebraska right behind them.
