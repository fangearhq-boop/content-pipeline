Beachum to Florida: The Portal's Most Dramatic In-State Flip Is Complete

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 22, 2026

Jaysoni Beachum is headed to Florida.

On the final day of the 2026 NCAA softball transfer portal window — Day 15 of 15 — the Softball America No. 1 rated transfer has committed to the Florida Gators. The player every program in the country has been chasing for two weeks chose the one school that would sting the most for Seminoles fans: FSU's biggest in-state rival.

The transfer portal closes today, June 22. Beachum's commitment is the last major act of the 2026 window — and one of the most dramatic finales in recent college softball history.

The Numbers Behind the Name

Beachum's 2026 season at Florida State made her the most coveted transfer in the portal. She hit .417 with 16 home runs and 66 RBI. Her .549 on-base percentage ranked third in FSU program history. She drew 54 walks — a mark that reflects both her patience and the fear opposing coaches had of letting her swing freely.

She earned NFCA Second-Team All-American honors. Over her three-year career with the Seminoles, she accumulated 37 home runs, 180 RBI, and 202 hits. At just 20 years old, she enters her final season of eligibility with a resume that most college softball players never approach.

She plays premium defense at third base. She is a middle-of-the-order hitter with elite on-base skills. She is, in every measurable way, the best available player who entered the portal this offseason.

The Florida Fit

Florida needed exactly what Beachum provides. Kendall Grover's graduation left an immediate hole at third base — a premium defensive position where you cannot simply plug in a replacement and hope for the best. Head coach Tim Walton needed a proven, impact-level player.

He got the best one available.

Beachum visited the Gators, toured the program, and the visit left little doubt about where she was headed. She is a Tallahassee native — someone who grew up down the road from Florida State, played her college career under the Seminoles' brand, and now will spend her senior season wearing orange and blue in Gainesville.

The Gators also added Kairi Rodriguez from Duke during the portal window, giving Tim Walton additional positional flexibility heading into 2027. Florida's offseason has been built around filling specific needs with high-impact players — and nothing in the portal was higher impact than Beachum.

What It Means for FSU

For Florida State, this is a difficult offseason to summarize. The Seminoles lost Isa Torres — the D1 Softball Player of the Year — to Texas in the same portal window. And now they've lost Beachum to Florida. Two of the most talented players in FSU's recent history are gone, and one of them went to the program's biggest rival.

Head coach Lonni Alameda is working with what she has: Tennessee transfer Ella Dodge, Nicole Edmiaston from Stetson, and six incoming freshmen. The foundation for a rebuild is there. The 2027 season will be a rebuilding year for Florida State, and Alameda has enough talent to compete in the ACC. But reaching the heights of their recent seasons — which included trips to the WCWS and produced two All-Americans in the same year — will require time.

What's Next

With Beachum's commitment confirmed, the 2026 transfer portal is effectively closed. Programs can still finalize agreements with players who are already in the portal, but no new entrants are permitted after today. Florida now turns to integrating Beachum into their 2027 lineup and establishing their identity as a program ready to challenge for a national title. Watch for Florida to be among the preseason top-ten programs heading into next season — Beachum is that impactful.
