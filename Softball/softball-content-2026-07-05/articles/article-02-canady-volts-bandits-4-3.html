Bandits Complete 4-3 Comeback Over Volts: Canady's AUSL Turnaround Still Pending

By Emily Rawlings

July 5, 2026

The Texas Volts led 3-0. They finished 3-4.

The Chicago Bandits scored four runs in the fifth inning on July 4 to complete a 4-3 comeback win over the Volts in Rosemont, Illinois, dealing NiJaree Canady's team another loss in an AUSL debut season that has not gone as expected. Skylar Wallace, Morgan Zerkle, and Danielle Gibson Whorton led the Bandits' fifth-inning rally.

The Bandits, who entered at 14-7 — one of the AUSL's best records — remain in contention for a Championship Series berth. The Volts sit at the bottom of the standings.

Where Canady's Season Stands

NiJaree Canady carried a 0-3 record and a 6.87 ERA into Thursday's game. She allowed 17 earned runs and seven home runs in her first professional outings — a jarring departure from her college production, where she posted ERAs of 0.57 and 0.73 at Stanford and 1.11 and 1.87 at Texas Tech.

The Texas Volts moved to address the situation by bringing in Tori Nyberg — Canady's former pitching coach at Stanford, the coach who originally recruited her — to work on mechanics that changed after injuries Canady sustained at Texas Tech. The diagnosis: her arm path, release point, and body sequencing have shifted from what produced those sub-1.00 ERAs in Palo Alto.

Nyberg's stated goal is to get back to the "OG NiJa." Thursday's game was one of the early tests of whether that work is translating.

The Urgency Is Real

The AUSL regular season runs through July 20. The Championship Series is July 25-27. There are approximately 15 days of regular season remaining for Canady to find the form that made her the most dominant pitcher in recent college softball history.

Elite pitching mechanics don't vanish — they get disrupted. The question is how quickly a pitcher of Canady's caliber can get them back with focused coaching attention. Nyberg's track record with her is the best reason for optimism: this is not a coach who is meeting Canady for the first time. She knows what this pitcher looks like when she's right.

Losses like Thursday's make the road harder, but they don't change the underlying belief in what Canady can be. The next start will matter significantly for how the remainder of her first professional season is defined.

What's Next

Canady and the Volts have until July 20 to find enough wins to remain relevant heading into the AUSL postseason bracket. Her next scheduled start will be the most-watched game on the AUSL calendar — not because of its standings implications, but because the sport wants to see whether the mechanics work has started to show results. The Bandits, meanwhile, remain one of the AUSL's top teams and should be a legitimate Championship Series contender. A healthy, reset Canady is still the biggest wildcard in professional softball heading into the final weeks of the regular season.
