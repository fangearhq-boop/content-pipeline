Tia Milloy Commits to Arizona State: The 41-Day Portal Saga Is Over

By Sarah Mitchell

July 5, 2026

Tia Milloy is going to Arizona State.

One day after posting "forks up 😈" on X — the pitchfork gesture synonymous with the Sun Devils — Milloy committed to ASU on July 5, ending the most-watched transfer portal saga of the 2026 softball offseason. The 41-day wait is over.

The answer was Arizona State all along.

Who She Is

Milloy is a left-handed utility player and outfielder from Woodinville, Washington. She played two seasons at Oklahoma across 91 games, posting a .295 batting average with nine home runs, 35 RBIs, and a .986 OPS. Those numbers represent a player who hasn't scratched the surface of what she can do in the right program.

She entered the portal on May 25, 2026, after the Sooners were eliminated by Mississippi State in the Norman Super Regional. She had two years of eligibility remaining — making her a multi-year piece rather than a short-term rental. That distinction drove the recruiting interest that followed.

Her family's relationship with the sport runs deep. Her sister Kiki Milloy played at Tennessee. Her sister Amirah played at Washington. Tia grew up around high-level college softball before she ever played it.

Why Arizona State Won

Florida State was widely considered the most urgently-positioned suitor. The Seminoles lost Isa Torres (.530 batting average) to Texas and Jaysoni Beachum (.417 BA, 16 HR) to Florida in the portal — two of the program's most productive bats — and needed a replacement. FSU was in active conversations with Milloy's camp throughout the portal window.

Tennessee and Texas Tech were also engaged. Texas Tech, fresh off a Women's College World Series runner-up finish, had the momentum and recruiting capital to make a serious push.

None of them could close it. Arizona State could.

What ASU offered beyond football is unclear publicly, but the result tells the story: Milloy chose the Sun Devils. Her July 4 "forks up" post was the soft announcement; July 5 made it official.

What It Means for Arizona State

The Sun Devils are bringing in a high-upside, left-handed bat with two years of eligibility at a program working to re-establish itself among the sport's elite. Milloy gives ASU a proven Power Conference contributor who already understands the pressure of postseason softball.

With the roster freeze hitting July 13 — eight days away — her commitment arrives at the right moment for Arizona State's roster construction. Coaches can now finalize travel plans for PGF Nationals (July 19) knowing their portal class is complete.

What's Next

Milloy will suit up for Arizona State beginning in the 2027 season, with eligibility through 2028. The question now is where she plays in the Sun Devils' lineup and how quickly she develops into the impact bat coaches on multiple programs believed she could become. For FSU, Tennessee, and Texas Tech, the portal window is effectively closed — the remaining focus shifts to fall camp preparations and the 2028 recruiting class. The saga that kept the softball transfer portal in the conversation all summer long is finally finished.
