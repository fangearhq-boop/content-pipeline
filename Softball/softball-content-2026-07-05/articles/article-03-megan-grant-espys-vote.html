Megan Grant vs. the ESPYS Field: Softball's Biggest Mainstream Moment Is 10 Days Away

By Jordan Reeves

July 5, 2026

On July 15, Megan Grant could win the most visible individual award in the history of college softball recognition. The ceremony just happens to air on ABC in front of a national audience at 8 PM ET.

Grant is nominated for Best Record-Breaking Performance at the 2026 ESPYS — one of four finalists in a category that includes an NFL star, an Olympic skier, and a world-class marathon runner. To win, she needs votes. And the softball community votes.

What She Did

In 2026, Grant hit 42 home runs in 63 games for UCLA — breaking the NCAA Division I single-season record of 37 set by Laura Espinoza of Arizona in 1995. That record stood for 31 years. Grant didn't just break it; she surpassed it by five.

She finished her UCLA career as the program's all-time home run leader with 91. She earned First Team All-American honors and was a top-three finalist for USA Softball Collegiate Player of the Year. By any measure, her 2026 season was one of the greatest individual offensive performances in the sport's college history.

Her Competition at the ESPYS

The Best Record-Breaking Performance nominees alongside Grant are Myles Garrett (NFL), Johannes Høsflot Klæbo (cross-country skiing), and Sabastian Sawe (distance running). All three are accomplished athletes in high-profile sports with large mainstream audiences.

None of them broke a record that had been in place for 31 years.

Grant's achievement is specific, verifiable, and historically significant within her sport. The question is whether the ESPYS voting pool — which is driven by fan participation — knows enough about it to vote accordingly. Ten days of active campaigning from the softball community changes that equation.

She's Still Playing

Grant didn't stop when the college season ended. The No. 4 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL College Draft, she's suiting up for the Portland Cascade in professional softball's first season. She hit her first professional home run on June 10 in her debut game against the Carolina Blaze.

The ESPYS nomination comes while she's actively competing — a rare convergence of immediate relevance and historical achievement. The award would recognize what she did in college. The résumé is already being added to in the pros.

What's Next

Fans can vote once per day at ESPN.com through July 15. The ESPYS air Wednesday, July 15 at 8 PM ET on ABC and streaming on the ESPN app. Texas softball is also nominated in a separate category — Best Team — making this a potentially significant night for the sport if the voting community engages. Grant's nomination is a legitimate opportunity for softball to win a national award on a national stage. That opportunity closes at voting deadline on July 15.
