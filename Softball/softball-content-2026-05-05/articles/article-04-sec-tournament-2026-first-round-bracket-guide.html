2026 SEC Softball Tournament Opens Today — First Round Guide, Bracket, and What to Watch

By Sarah Mitchell | May 5, 2026 | I Love Softball

The 2026 SEC Softball Tournament is officially underway. John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky opens its gates today for the first round, and by Saturday evening, the conference will have a champion — and several teams will have locked in, or lost, their national seed aspirations heading into the Selection Show on Sunday.

Here is everything you need to know about the bracket, the schedule, and the stories to follow.

Today's First Round

The bottom of the bracket — seeds 10 through 15 — opens play today. No. 11 Missouri faces No. 14 Auburn at 1 PM ET on the SEC Network. No. 10 Mississippi State takes on No. 15 Kentucky approximately 35 minutes after the first game concludes, also on the SEC Network. The winner of the No. 12 South Carolina versus No. 13 Ole Miss matchup fills out the first-round slate.

These are survival games. One loss and you go home. Every team in today's first round is playing with their season on the line from the first pitch.

The Top Seeds Are Waiting

The tournament's four top seeds — No. 1 Oklahoma, No. 2 Alabama, Florida, and Tennessee — earned double byes and do not enter the bracket until Thursday. That gives those programs extra rest and preparation time, and removes any chance of an early-week upset knocking out the conference's best teams before the quarterfinal round.

Oklahoma (48-7) arrives as the undisputed favorite. The Sooners are the outright SEC regular season champions, and Kendall Wells — at 36 home runs, one shy of the all-time NCAA record — will be the most-watched player in Lexington all week. Alabama (No. 2 seed) brings a pitching staff that has been among the nation's best. Tennessee and Florida round out the double-bye group with strong regular season credentials.

Stakes Beyond the Conference Title

The SEC Tournament is not just about who wins the banner. The selection committee watches closely, and a strong tournament performance can move a bubble team into the national seed conversation — or push a confident team from a No. 3 seed to a No. 2.

Teams like LSU (No. 8 seed) and Georgia (No. 9 seed) are playing to prove they belong in the upper tier of the national bracket. A deep SEC Tournament run by an unseeded team can reshape the Selection Show projections dramatically.

What's Next

The second round features seeds 5 through 8 and plays tomorrow. Quarterfinals are Thursday when the top seeds enter. Semifinals run Friday and the championship is Saturday, May 9. The SEC Tournament champion earns an automatic national seed consideration and, historically, hosting rights for an NCAA Regional.

The Selection Show is Sunday, May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. The WCWS begins May 28 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. Five days from now, 64 teams will know their fate. This week in Lexington is how those fates are shaped.
