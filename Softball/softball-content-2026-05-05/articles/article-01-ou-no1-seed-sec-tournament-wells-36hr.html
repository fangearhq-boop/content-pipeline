Oklahoma Enters SEC Tournament as No. 1 Seed — Wells at 36 HR, One From All-Time NCAA Record

By Sarah Mitchell | May 5, 2026 | I Love Softball

No. 1 Oklahoma has done everything a team could possibly do in the regular season. The Sooners swept the Texas A&M doubleheader on May 2 — winning 4-3 and 6-4 — to claim the outright SEC regular season championship and enter the 2026 SEC Softball Tournament with a 48-7 overall record and a 20-4 mark in conference play.

They are the tournament's No. 1 seed with a double bye. They don't play until Thursday at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky. And when they do, the eyes of the college softball world will be on freshman phenom Kendall Wells — who sits at 36 home runs and is one swing away from tying the most hallowed individual record in the sport.

A Record Still Waiting

Lauren Espinoza set the all-time NCAA single-season home run record at the University of Arizona in 1995, hitting 37 home runs in a season that stood as a monument to power hitting for more than three decades. Wells has spent 2026 dismantling every record below it.

She broke the NCAA freshman home run record. She shattered the SEC single-season record. She surpassed Jocelyn Alo's Oklahoma program record. The only one left is Espinoza's. Wells enters the SEC Tournament at 36, one away from tying and two away from owning the record outright.

In the A&M series, opposing pitchers chose avoidance over confrontation: Wells drew six walks in three games. That discipline, while understandable from the other dugout's perspective, only deepens the tension around every plate appearance Wells takes in Lexington. The crowd will know. The broadcast will know. Wells will know.

The SEC Title Picture

Oklahoma's path to the outright SEC title was not without drama. Texas A&M stunned the Sooners in the weather-resumed Game 1 last week, rallying for an 8-5 win in College Station. But Oklahoma regrouped, returned to Davis Diamond on May 2, and delivered in both games of the doubleheader — gutting out a 4-3 win before finishing the job with a 6-4 result in the series finale.

The back-to-back SEC regular season titles affirm what the national rankings have been saying all year: Oklahoma is the standard. Their 48-7 record includes wins over every major opponent, and the Sooners' combination of pitching depth and Wells-anchored power is uniquely difficult to game-plan against.

Alabama enters as the No. 2 seed and will also have a double bye. Florida and Tennessee round out the top four. The tournament bracket sets up for potential all-SEC powerhouse matchups as the week progresses.

What's Next

Oklahoma does not play until Thursday in Lexington — but the tournament begins today with the first round, featuring seeds 10 through 15. The Sooners will watch, rest, and prepare as the bracket narrows around them.

When Wells steps into the batter's box this week, she'll do so with the weight of 31 years of softball history on her shoulders. Espinoza's record has lasted since 1995. It may not survive May 2026.

The SEC Tournament runs through Saturday, May 9. The Selection Show is Sunday, May 10, at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. The WCWS begins May 28 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. For now, the road runs through Lexington — and No. 1 Oklahoma will be ready.
