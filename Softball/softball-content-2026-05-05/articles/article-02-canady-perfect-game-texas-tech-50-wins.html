NiJaree Canady Throws First Career Perfect Game on Senior Day — Texas Tech First Program to 50 Wins

By Emily Rawlings | May 5, 2026 | I Love Softball

NiJaree Canady saved her best for last.

On May 2 — Senior Day at Rocky Diamond in Lubbock — the Texas Tech ace was perfect. Twenty-one Baylor batters came to the plate. Twenty-one sat back down. Canady threw the first perfect game of her career in an 8-0 Texas Tech victory, the sixth in program history, and sent the Red Raiders into the postseason as the first team in the country to reach 50 wins in 2026.

The Moment's Weight

Canady arrived at Texas Tech as one of the most heralded pitching prospects in the history of the sport. She has delivered on every expectation and then some. Through 2026, she surpassed 1,000 career strikeouts, threw multiple no-hitters, and emerged as the consensus best pitcher in college softball. She has anchored back-to-back Big 12 regular season title teams.

But a perfect game had eluded her — until Senior Day. In her final regular season start as a Red Raider, Canady was untouchable. Her fastball moved, her rise ball bit, and Baylor's lineup couldn't solve any of it. The result was a complete game, 8-0 shutout with no baserunners — perfection, in the most literal sense.

After the final out, the Texas Tech dugout sprinted onto the field. Canady, who has rarely let a moment rattle her in four years, was met with a celebration befitting of everything she has given the program.

First Program to 50 Wins

Canady's performance was the capstone to a historic regular season. Texas Tech enters the postseason at 50-5 overall — the first team in the country to reach 50 wins in 2026. Their 21-3 record in Big 12 play is a back-to-back conference title performance. The Red Raiders are the No. 1 seed in the Big 12 Tournament, which runs May 7-9 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City.

Devon Park, of course, is also the home of the Women's College World Series — a venue that feels almost destined to be Canady's final stage as a collegian. Texas Tech faces No. 8 seed Baylor in the first round, a team Canady just retired in order, which adds a poetic element to the rematch possibility.

What's Next

Canady and the Red Raiders head to Oklahoma City as the clear favorite to win the Big 12 Tournament. Nationally, Texas Tech is in the conversation for the No. 2 overall seed behind Oklahoma — a placement that would guarantee hosting rights for an NCAA Regional.

The Selection Show airs Sunday, May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2. If the Red Raiders win in OKC, their national seed case becomes nearly airtight. And if Canady pitches like she did on Senior Day, Texas Tech will be very hard to stop at any stage of this postseason.

The WCWS begins May 28 in Devon Park — the same diamond where Canady's career began. She has a chance to end it the same way she spent Senior Day: perfect.
