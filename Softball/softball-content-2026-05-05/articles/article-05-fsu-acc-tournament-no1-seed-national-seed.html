Florida State Enters ACC Tournament as No. 1 Seed — National Seed and WCWS Hosting Rights on the Line

By Emily Rawlings | May 5, 2026 | I Love Softball

Florida State arrives in Charlottesville with the highest stakes of the ACC Softball season.

The No. 10 Seminoles (43-8, 18-3 ACC) are the No. 1 seed in the 2026 ACC Championship, which begins Wednesday at Palmer Park in Charlottesville, Virginia and runs through Saturday, May 9. For FSU, the tournament is more than a conference title chase — it is the final audition before the Selection Show on Sunday, May 10, when the selection committee will determine whether the Seminoles host an NCAA Regional.

A National Seed Case Built Over a Full Season

Florida State has been one of the most consistent programs in the country in 2026. Their 43-8 record reflects a team that competed hard from the first weekend of the season, took on a challenging schedule, and came out of the regular season as the undisputed best team in the Atlantic Coast Conference.

The Seminoles' RPI sits inside the top 14 nationally entering the ACC Tournament — a figure that puts them squarely in the national seed conversation. The top 16 teams nationally receive hosting rights for an NCAA Regional, meaning FSU's opponents come to Tallahassee rather than the Seminoles traveling as lower seeds.

Isa Torres has been the engine of Florida State's offense throughout 2026. Her ability to drive runs and deliver in pressure situations has been a constant for a team that has navigated a competitive ACC schedule without losing sight of the bigger national picture.

Tournament Format and Path

The ACC Championship at Palmer Park in Charlottesville features a single-elimination bracket, with the Seminoles entering as the No. 1 seed. FSU will not play until the quarterfinal round on Wednesday. A title run through the bracket would almost certainly cement a national seed spot. A semifinal appearance would keep the conversation very much alive. A first-game exit would introduce uncertainty heading into Sunday's Selection Show.

The ACC has put multiple programs in the national seed conversation this year, creating some internal competition within the conference bracket. FSU's path will likely include at least one game against a team fighting to improve its own national standing.

What's Next

The ACC Tournament runs May 6-9 at Palmer Park. The championship game is Saturday. The Selection Show is Sunday, May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 — 24 hours after the ACC title game's final pitch.

For Florida State, everything points toward this week. Win the ACC, lock in the national seed, bring the postseason to Tallahassee. That is the path. It begins tomorrow in Charlottesville.

The WCWS starts May 28 at Devon Park in OKC. The road from Palmer Park to Devon Park runs through every game Florida State plays this week.
