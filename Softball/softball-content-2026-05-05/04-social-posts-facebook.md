### STORY 1:

#### Long-Form Post -- 9:00 AM
Oklahoma didn't just win the SEC regular season title on Saturday — they did it the hard way.

After dropping Game 1 to Texas A&M last week, the Sooners came back to win both games of the May 2 doubleheader in College Station: 4-3 and 6-4. It was a grind. It was fitting. And now No. 1 Oklahoma enters the 2026 SEC Tournament at 48-7, 20-4 in conference play — the outright regular season champions.

The Sooners are the No. 1 seed and own a double bye. They don't step back onto the field until Thursday in Lexington. And when they do, all eyes will again be on freshman Kendall Wells.

Wells finished the regular season at 36 home runs. She didn't homer in the A&M series — opposing pitchers issued her six walks in three games, choosing to work around her rather than challenge her. It didn't matter for the team result. But the math still stands: 36. One shy of Lauren Espinoza's 31-year-old all-time NCAA single-season record of 37, set at Arizona in 1995.

Wells has already broken the NCAA freshman HR record, the SEC single-season HR record, and the Oklahoma program record this season. The only one left is Espinoza's. She'll have chances in Lexington. Softball fans should have their notifications on.

### STORY 2:

#### Long-Form Post -- 11:00 AM
College softball's most decorated pitcher saved her best performance for last.

On May 2 — Senior Day at Rocky Diamond in Lubbock — NiJaree Canady took the mound for Texas Tech against Baylor and was simply untouchable. Twenty-one Baylor batters came to the plate. Twenty-one sat back down. Canady threw the first perfect game of her career in an 8-0 Red Raiders win, the sixth perfect game in Texas Tech program history.

That result also pushed Texas Tech to 50-5 on the season — making the Red Raiders the first program in the country to reach 50 wins in 2026. A remarkable achievement for a roster that also claimed back-to-back Big 12 regular season titles.

Canady arrived at Texas Tech as one of the most heralded pitching prospects in the sport's history, and her career has exceeded expectations. She crossed 1,000 career strikeouts this season. She has thrown multiple no-hitters. And now, in her final regular season start as a Red Raider, she added a perfect game.

Next up: the Big 12 Tournament, May 7-9 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City — the same venue that hosts the Women's College World Series. Texas Tech is the No. 1 seed. And Canady, pitching with house money, might be the scariest force in college softball right now.

A top-4 national seed — one of the 16 teams that host their own Regional — would allow the Huskers to welcome the postseason to Bowlin Stadium in Lincoln.

Nebraska doesn't have the historic name recognition of an Oklahoma or Texas Tech. But their numbers don't lie: 43-6, 23-1, first-ever conference title. The Huskers are a legitimate national championship contender, and tournament week is when quiet programs make loud statements.

For the Seminoles, winning the ACC Tournament would put an exclamation point on their résumé. Even falling short, their performance this week will be scrutinized by the selection committee. Every game at Palmer Park matters.
