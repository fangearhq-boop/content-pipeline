Texas Tech Lands Moriah Polar — Nation's Top Hitter Joins Historic Transfer Class

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 16, 2026

Moriah Polar is headed to Lubbock.

The nation's leading hitter in 2026 committed to Texas Tech on Sunday night and the Red Raiders officially confirmed the signing Monday morning. The move adds an extraordinary offensive piece to a transfer class that has already reshaped expectations for Jerry Glasco's program heading into the 2027 season.

Polar, a Purdue transfer from Pearland, Texas, led all of college softball with a .555 batting average in her junior season. She compiled 101 hits, 67 runs scored, 47 RBIs, and 14 doubles, earning NFCA Third-Team All-American recognition. She has one year of eligibility remaining and will be immediately eligible.

A Class Unlike Any Other

Polar's commitment does not arrive in isolation. On June 13, Texas Tech landed Jayden Heavener, who entered the portal as the No. 1 ranked pitcher available. Heavener went 13-9 with a 2.81 ERA and 128 strikeouts across 139.1 innings in 2026 and was on USA Softball's Player of the Year watch list before entering the portal.

Then there is Kasidi Pickering. The former Oklahoma standout — who hit .370 with 20 home runs and 61 RBIs in 2026 and holds a career line of .384 with 50 home runs and 169 RBIs over three seasons at OU — entered the portal June 8 with a "Do Not Contact" tag. That designation signals a player has already made a decision privately. Multiple reports and social signals from the transfer network have pointed to Lubbock since the day Pickering entered the portal. No official announcement has been made as of Monday.

If Pickering confirms what the DNT tag implies, Texas Tech's 2026 portal class will include the No. 1 pitcher, the No. 2 overall transfer, and the nation's No. 1 hitter.

Building From the Runner-Up Position

Texas Tech reached the 2026 Women's College World Series championship game before falling short of the title. That run changed the narrative around the program and, clearly, made it a destination for elite transfers.

What Glasco has assembled this offseason goes beyond filling holes. Polar replaces production at the plate with a player who averaged better than one hit per game all season. Heavener addresses pitching depth at the highest level the portal offers. Pickering, if confirmed, would add another proven power bat to an already dangerous lineup.

The Red Raiders are not just competing for a title in 2027. Based on the pieces they are adding, they are being built to win one.

What's Next

Kasidi Pickering's official announcement remains the last major domino for Texas Tech. While the DNT tag and widespread reporting points to the Red Raiders, no confirmation has come from Pickering or the program. That announcement could come at any time before the portal window closes June 22.

For Polar, the next step is arriving in Lubbock and integrating into a program that suddenly has a very bright horizon. For softball fans, the question shifts from "where is Polar going?" to "can anyone beat Texas Tech next spring?"
