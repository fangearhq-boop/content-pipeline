Torres Watch — Day 9: The Final Week Begins and the D1 Player of the Year Is Still Uncommitted

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 16, 2026

The clock is now inside six days.

Isa Torres — the D1 Softball Player of the Year, the ACC Player of the Year, and the most coveted position player in the 2026 transfer portal — enters Monday still uncommitted. The portal window closes June 22. Nine days in and the decision that will define this offseason has not been made.

Or if it has, Torres is not saying.

What She Brings

The numbers are worth repeating because they remain staggering. Torres hit .530 at Florida State in 2026, slugged .978, and posted a .591 on-base percentage while scoring 78 runs. Each of those figures broke school records. She also set an NCAA record with a hit in 16 consecutive at-bats.

She was not just the best player at Florida State. She was the best player in college softball. Every program in America that has the roster room and the program ambition to compete for a national title has reason to want Isa Torres in its lineup.

The Race Narrows

Texas and Texas A&M have been consistently identified as the frontrunners. Torres is a native of Georgetown, Texas, a small city about 30 miles north of Austin. Samantha Swan, who signed with Texas earlier in the portal window, is also from Georgetown — a detail that has not been lost on those watching the Torres recruitment closely.

Texas won the Women's College World Series this past spring. Adding Torres would give the Longhorns a chance to do something rarely accomplished in the modern era of college softball: repeat with a significantly upgraded roster. Texas A&M, meanwhile, has already added Maddy Azua — the Sun Belt Pitcher of the Year — and landing Torres would pair a dominant arm with an elite offensive weapon.

Both programs have compelling cases. Torres is taking her time making the call.

What's Next

The portal window closes June 22 — six days from today. Torres can announce at any point before then, and given the compressed timeline, an announcement this week feels increasingly likely.

The softball world has been watching this story for nine days. The final week begins now. Whatever Torres decides will be one of the defining roster moves of the 2026 portal cycle and will immediately shift the expectations for whichever program she chooses heading into 2027.
