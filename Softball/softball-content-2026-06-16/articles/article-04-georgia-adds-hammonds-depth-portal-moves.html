Georgia Adds Kylie Hammonds — How Programs Are Winning the Portal Without the Headline Names

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 16, 2026

The name Kylie Hammonds may not trend nationally the way Torres and Beachum do. But her commitment to Georgia on Monday is a reminder that the transfer portal is not just about the marquee signings. It is also about the programs that find value in the second week while everyone else focuses on the headlines.

Georgia added the Florida Atlantic transfer outfielder as the portal window enters its final six days. Hammonds spent her career at FAU before the Bulldogs identified her as a fit for their 2027 roster needs.

The Depth Market Is Moving Fast

The pattern in the second week of the portal is clear. The headline names — Torres, Beachum, Pickering — are still being pursued by a handful of programs each. But the depth market, the players who may not be ranked in the top five nationally but still bring meaningful production, is moving very quickly.

Madison Hartley chose Oklahoma State. Katie Wetteland went to Arkansas. Maddy Azua signed with Texas A&M. Jayden Heavener and Moriah Polar are now at Texas Tech. Georgia adding Hammonds fits the same pattern: programs identifying needs, acting decisively, and not waiting for the final days to secure the players they want.

Programs that wait too long in this second week risk losing the depth options they need even if the top names are still out there. The smart programs run parallel tracks — pursuing the elite players while also locking up the complementary pieces.

Georgia's Approach

Georgia has been one of the consistently competitive programs in the SEC, and adding an outfielder from the portal is a calculated move to fill a specific need. The Bulldogs have not been at the center of the Torres or Beachum pursuit, but they are building through the portal in the way that works for their roster construction.

That is, frankly, how most programs navigate this window. For every Texas chasing a D1 Player of the Year and every Texas Tech assembling a historic class, there are ten programs doing the quiet work of adding one or two pieces that will show up in the lineup when March arrives.

What's Next

The portal window closes June 22. Six days remain. The pace of commitments is expected to accelerate as the deadline approaches. Programs that have not secured their priority targets will need to make decisions quickly — both on who they are still chasing and who else in the depth market is still available.

Georgia's addition of Hammonds is a sign that some programs are choosing to lock in what they need rather than waiting on what they want. Not every team can land a Torres. But every team can make smart portal decisions — and the Bulldogs made one.
