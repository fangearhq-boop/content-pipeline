Beachum — Day 9: Softball's No. 1 Transfer Stays Patient as the Final Week Begins

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | June 16, 2026

With Moriah Polar now signed and Isa Torres still making programs wait, Jaysoni Beachum may be flying just slightly under the radar. She should not be.

Softball America's No. 1 overall transfer enters Monday — Day 9 of the 15-day portal window — without a commitment. Six days remain. And the player many scouts consider the most complete offensive transfer in this year's class is still available.

What Beachum Brings

The numbers from her junior season at Florida State tell the story: .417 batting average, 16 home runs, 66 RBIs, and a .549 on-base percentage. Those are not simply good transfer portal statistics — they are among the best numbers posted by any position player in college softball in 2026.

Across three seasons at FSU, Beachum compiled a .375 career batting average with 37 home runs, 180 RBIs, and 202 hits. The NFCA named her the Freshman of the Year in 2024. She earned Second-Team All-American honors in 2026 along with First-Team All-ACC and NFCA First-Team All-Region recognition, and she was named to the All-Tournament Team at the ACC Championship.

She is a third baseman with power, on-base skills, and a track record of production at every level of her college career. Softball America rated her No. 1 among all available transfers — ahead of every other player in the portal — for a reason.

The Context Has Shifted

When Beachum entered the portal, she was part of a group of elite FSU players that included Isa Torres. The two entering the market at the same time created a rare concentration of top-tier talent that drew every major program's attention.

Now, as the portal window enters its final stretch, the landscape has changed. Moriah Polar committed to Texas Tech on Sunday. The depth of available talent is thinning. Programs that want to make a significant offensive upgrade have two clear targets left: Torres and Beachum.

That means whoever lands Beachum is not just winning a transfer portal recruitment — they are winning the only other major offensive battle left in the 2026 cycle.

What's Next

The portal window closes June 22. Beachum has six days to announce, and given the compressed timeline, a decision this week is expected. The programs pursuing her know the deadline. The pressure of June 22 has a way of accelerating decisions.

Wherever Beachum lands, that program upgrades its lineup with one of the most productive hitters in college softball. The wait continues — but not for much longer.
