Portal Countdown — Day 9 of 15: Final Six Days Begin With Two Stars Still Uncommitted

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 16, 2026

Nine days in. Six days left. The 2026 college softball transfer portal window opened June 8 and closes June 22, and Monday marks the beginning of the final stretch with two of the biggest names in the sport still uncommitted.

Here is the full picture of where things stand as Day 9 begins.

Who Has Committed

The first nine days produced a significant number of major commitments. Samantha Swan (Arizona State, catcher) signed with Texas. Altmeyer also signed with Texas. Abby Plumlee (left-handed pitcher) chose Alabama. Maddy Azua — the Sun Belt Pitcher of the Year who went 26-13 with a 1.95 ERA and 244.2 innings pitched — signed with Texas A&M. Madison Hartley, an All-Big 12 slugger from Georgetown, chose Oklahoma State. Katie Wetteland, the Mountain West Freshman of the Year, committed to Arkansas.

And then Monday brought the biggest news of the portal's second week: Moriah Polar signed with Texas Tech. Polar led the nation in batting average (.555) and compiled 101 hits, 67 runs, and 47 RBIs in her junior season at Purdue. She joins Jayden Heavener — ranked the No. 1 pitcher in the transfer portal — who committed to the Red Raiders on June 13.

Who Is Still Uncommitted

Two names at the very top of the market remain uncommitted.

Isa Torres — the D1 Softball Player of the Year who hit .530 with a .978 slugging percentage and .591 on-base percentage while scoring 78 runs at Florida State — has not announced a destination. Texas and Texas A&M are the reported frontrunners. The portal closes June 22.

Jaysoni Beachum — Softball America's No. 1 overall transfer, who hit .417 with 16 home runs, 66 RBIs, and a .549 on-base percentage — also remains uncommitted. Her career numbers at FSU: .375 average, 37 home runs, 180 RBIs, 202 hits. The 2024 NFCA Freshman of the Year and 2026 Second-Team All-American has yet to reveal her next destination.

Also watching: Kasidi Pickering (On3 No. 2, .370 BA / 20 HR / 61 RBI from Oklahoma) entered the portal with a "Do Not Contact" tag June 8. Reports and social signals point strongly toward Texas Tech. No official announcement has come as of Monday.

What's Next

Six days remain before the portal closes. Every major player still uncommitted has until June 22 to decide — or risk having their options narrow further as programs fill their available spots.

Texas, Texas A&M, and multiple other programs are waiting on Torres and Beachum. Texas Tech may be a few days away from adding Pickering and completing one of the most remarkable transfer classes in softball history. The final week is here. The biggest decisions of the 2026 offseason are still ahead.
