The Road to OKC Begins: Day 1 of the 2026 NCAA Softball Regionals Is Here

By Sarah Mitchell | May 15, 2026

Today is the best day on the college softball calendar. The 2026 NCAA Division I Softball Championship officially opens across 16 regional sites, with 64 teams launching into double-elimination competition in front of packed stadiums from Tuscaloosa to Los Angeles. After months of regular season play, conference tournaments, and Selection Show anticipation, the road to Oklahoma City has arrived.

Every game today matters. In double-elimination format, a first-round loss doesn't end the tournament — but it immediately puts a team in the loser's bracket, one defeat away from going home. That urgency is what makes Day 1 of regionals unlike any other day in the sport. No team is comfortable. No lead is safe. And the upsets that define tournament legend start right now.

The Top Seeds Take the Field

All four national seeds open tournament play today. No. 1 Alabama (49-7) opens at Tuscaloosa's Rhoads Stadium against USC Upstate, with first pitch at Noon CT on SEC Network. The Crimson Tide have an all-time regional record of 74-12 — one of the most dominant tournament track records in softball history. Their 1.60 ERA and 22 shutouts this season make them the most feared team in the field.

No. 2 Texas, the defending national champions, hosts the Austin Regional at McCombs Field with a 3 PM CT first pitch against NEC Tournament champion Wagner on ESPN+. The Longhorns arrive with a chip on their shoulder that comes from winning everything — everyone is gunning for them in 2026. Pitcher Citlaly Kavan, who struck out 12 batters in the SEC Tournament championship game, leads one of the nation's most complete pitching staffs.

No. 3 Oklahoma (48-8) opens the Norman Regional at Love's Field against Binghamton at 2:30 PM CT. Patty Gasso's Sooners arrive with the nation's best batting average (.389) and 174 home runs on the season. After losing the 2025 WCWS to Texas, the Sooners are hungry. Michigan and Kansas round out the Norman field, squaring off at 5 PM CT in the opening round's second game.

No. 4 Nebraska (Big Ten champions) opens the Lincoln Regional against Summit League champion South Dakota at 5:30 PM ET on ESPN+. The Huskers are hosting at Bowlin Stadium for the first time since 2013 — a 13-year wait that their fans have circled on the calendar all season.

The Stories Beyond the Seeds

No. 9 seed Florida State (49-8) opens the Tallahassee Regional at noon ET against Stetson, bringing a 12-consecutive-regional hosting streak and a 20th ACC Tournament championship into the bracket. The Seminoles went 21-3 in ACC play and represent one of the deepest programs in the sport — despite a seeding that many feel undervalues their resume.

No. 8 seed UCLA opens the Los Angeles Regional tonight against Cal Baptist (10 PM ET, ESPN2). Senior outfielder Megan Grant enters tournament play with 38 home runs on the season — an NCAA single-season record she set in the Big Ten Tournament. Grant has won NCAA basketball championships at UCLA and seeks the one ring she still doesn't have: a softball title.

Virginia Tech (46-10), one of the most highly anticipated upsets picks of the regional weekend, opens the Baton Rouge Regional against South Alabama at 4 PM ET on ESPN+. The Hokies were ineligible to host due to graduation rate requirements and landed as the No. 2 seed in LSU's own backyard. Analysts and bracket watchers have circled Virginia Tech as the team most likely to knock off their No. 1 regional host.

The Format: What You Need to Know

Every regional runs May 15–17 in a four-team, double-elimination format. Two teams from each regional advance to the Super Regionals, scheduled for May 21–24. The eight Super Regional sites will each host best-of-three matchups, with the winners advancing to the Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City at Devon Park, May 28–June 5.

Coverage spans ESPN, ESPN2, ESPNU, ESPN+, SEC Network, and Big Ten Network across the weekend. Check ESPN+ for the majority of first-round games; many second-round matchups will move to linear networks as the bracket narrows.

What's Next

Day 1 results will reshape every regional picture by tonight. With games running from noon through midnight Eastern, the first wave of bracket clarity arrives quickly. Follow along for results, upsets, and analysis as the 2026 NCAA Softball Regionals unfold. The teams that survive the pressure of Day 1 in the winner's bracket arrive Saturday with momentum — and in double-elimination softball, momentum is everything. The Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City awaits the toughest 8 teams still standing when the dust settles Sunday night.
