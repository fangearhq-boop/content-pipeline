48 Years in the Making: South Dakota Makes History at the 2026 NCAA Tournament

By Emily Rawlings | May 15, 2026

The South Dakota softball program began in 1978. For 48 years, the Coyotes have never played in an NCAA Tournament — not in Division I, not in Division II, not at any level. Tonight, that all changes.

South Dakota (20-34-1) will take the field at Bowlin Stadium in Lincoln, Nebraska, for the first game in program history with a postseason bracket stake. They face No. 4 national seed Nebraska in the opening round of the Lincoln Regional at 5:30 PM ET on ESPN+. The record books will note only that they showed up. The people who were there will remember how they got here.

How South Dakota Got Here

The path was improbable. South Dakota entered the Summit League Tournament with a 20-34-1 overall record — a losing record that would seem to end any realistic hope of dancing. But softball's conference tournament format creates exactly the kind of opportunity the Coyotes seized in the final days of the regular season.

Over four days, South Dakota won four games. They beat the tournament's top seed, Omaha, not once but twice — both victories coming by a single run, the kind of margins that separate fortune from folly. Pitcher Madison Evans carried the team through every inning, throwing her arm into history with a performance that earned her the Summit League All-Tournament Team MVP award.

When the final out was recorded and South Dakota claimed the automatic qualifier berth, it was the kind of moment that makes coaching sports worth all the hard seasons. A team that had struggled all year found something — belief, execution, or just old-fashioned grit — at exactly the right time.

The Nebraska Connection

The Lincoln Regional matchup carries weight beyond the bracket. Seven members of South Dakota's roster are Nebraska natives — players who grew up in the state, who likely watched Nebraska play at Bowlin Stadium as kids, who chose to play for the Coyotes and now return for the biggest moment of their careers.

That is a dynamic no committee can engineer and no preview piece can fully capture. Tonight, nearly half the South Dakota lineup will step onto Nebraska's home field as visitors competing against a program their home state claims — and they'll do so in front of the Husker faithful who may not have circled this game on their calendar as a challenge worth worrying about.

Nebraska (No. 4 seed, 11th Big Ten championship) arrives as heavy favorites. The Huskers have elite pitching, a powerful lineup, and the home crowd advantage of a program that hasn't hosted a regional since 2013 and has been waiting for this weekend for years. On paper, this is not a close matchup.

Why It Matters Anyway

In college softball, every team in the field earned their spot. South Dakota did not receive a gift — they battled through four elimination games in four days against conference competition to get here. The 20 wins and 34 losses on their record tell one story. The Summit League Tournament tells another.

Madison Evans does not care about the record book. She is going to throw her best game. Seven Nebraska natives who grew up dreaming of moments like this are not going to roll over because the seed line says No. 4. This is the tournament, and the tournament belongs to the teams who show up ready to compete — regardless of what the preview pieces say.

Whether South Dakota wins a game or three in Lincoln, tonight they belong to a chapter of softball history they had never written before. The Coyotes are here. And that matters.

What's Next

South Dakota faces Nebraska tonight at 5:30 PM ET (ESPN+) in the opening game of the Lincoln Regional. If the Coyotes win, they advance to face the winner of Louisville vs. Grand Canyon on Saturday. A loss puts them in the loser's bracket, where they would need to win two consecutive games to survive the regional weekend. Whatever happens, South Dakota softball steps into an NCAA Tournament for the very first time tonight — and no result can erase the history they've already made.
