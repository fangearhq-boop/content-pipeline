The Hunt Is On: Texas Opens Title Defense at the 2026 Austin Regional

By Emily Rawlings | May 15, 2026

There is no harder position in college softball than defending national champion. Every team in the field circled your name on their calendar in the fall. Opposing coaches have spent the off-season building game plans specifically designed to beat your pitchers. Every opponent that takes the field against you wants to be the one who ended the reign. That is the reality Texas carries into the 2026 NCAA Tournament today.

The Longhorns (No. 2 national seed, 26th NCAA Tournament appearance) open the Austin Regional at McCombs Field this afternoon at 3 PM CT on ESPN+ against NEC Tournament champion Wagner. Texas arrives as one of the deepest programs in the country and one of the most hunted — the defending champions navigating a bracket where everyone has something to prove against them.

What Texas Won — and What That Means for 2026

In 2025, Texas won the Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City — their first national championship in program history. They did it in their first year as an SEC member, which added an exclamation point to the achievement that few programs ever earn. The Longhorns beat Oklahoma in the 2025 WCWS, the same Sooners who had won four consecutive national titles, and the statement was heard across college softball.

Texas did not stop there. In 2026, the Longhorns won their second consecutive SEC Tournament championship in May, defeating Alabama in the final behind Citlaly Kavan's 12-strikeout performance. That victory confirmed that Texas is not a one-year wonder. They are a program that has genuinely arrived at the highest level of the sport, with the pitching and the lineup to compete for a second championship.

Citlaly Kavan: The Ace Leading the Defense

Kavan is the engine of the Texas pitching staff and one of the premier arms in the tournament field. Her SEC Tournament championship performance — 12 strikeouts in a title-game victory — demonstrated why she is regarded as one of the top pitchers in the country heading into the postseason.

In regional play, Kavan's ability to control a game through strikeouts and ground balls is exactly what a defending champion needs: a pitcher who puts the team in a position to win early and often, limits the opponent's best hitters, and takes the pressure off the defense. The Austin Regional opens against Wagner, and while the Longhorns are heavy favorites, the tone Kavan sets in the first game sets the emotional temperature for the entire regional weekend.

The Austin Regional Bracket

Texas opens the Austin Regional against Wagner (NEC Tournament champion) at 3 PM CT. The bracket also features Baylor and Wisconsin, who clash in the day's first game at 12:30 PM CT on ESPN+. Both Baylor and Wisconsin represent legitimate secondary competition — programs with postseason experience that would be genuinely dangerous if either advances to face Texas after gaining momentum.

For Texas, the calculus in regional play is about winning clean. A two-game sweep through the winner's bracket, arriving Sunday in position to clinch a Super Regional berth — that is the ideal script. Each extra game taxes the pitching staff and reduces the margin before the Super Regionals, where the real competition arrives.

First to Win Back-to-Back?

Oklahoma's four-year dynasty (2021–2024) looms over the question of back-to-back champions, but they held a roster continuity across those years that is hard to replicate. Texas in 2026 is attempting something different: defending with a roster that has evolved since last June, against a field that has had an entire year to prepare for their strengths.

Whether that is motivation or pressure depends entirely on how the Longhorns approach each game. Texas's track record this season — the SEC title, Kavan's emergence, the consistent winning — suggests a program that hasn't coasted on their 2025 achievement. They have done the work to be here as a legitimate No. 2 seed. Now the tournament will decide if that preparation is enough to carry the trophy home again.

What's Next

Texas opens the Austin Regional today at 3 PM CT against Wagner at McCombs Field (ESPN+). The winner's bracket game on Saturday will follow, with Super Regionals on May 21–24 for teams who advance. Texas hosting a Super Regional in Austin would mark the continuation of one of the most compelling title-defense arcs in recent college softball history. The Women's College World Series begins May 28 in Oklahoma City — the city where the Longhorns celebrated last June and where they intend to celebrate again.
