Redemption Starts Today: Oklahoma Opens Norman Regional in Hunt for 9th National Title

By Jordan Reeves | May 15, 2026

In Norman, Oklahoma, the soil remembers championships. Love's Field has seen Patty Gasso's teams celebrate national titles, seen seniors lift trophies and freshmen watch with wide eyes and make promises to themselves. This afternoon at 2:30 PM CT, the 2026 version of that story begins against Binghamton — and everyone in that stadium knows the championship conversation that follows this team everywhere it goes.

Oklahoma enters the 2026 NCAA Tournament at 48-8 as the No. 3 national seed. The Sooners are the nation's leader in batting average (.389) and have launched 174 home runs this season. Their lineup is elite. Their tradition is unmatched. And their motivation is a very specific shade of Longhorn burnt orange.

The 2025 Loss That Fuels 2026

Oklahoma won four consecutive national championships from 2021 to 2024 — a dynasty run with no modern equal in college softball. Last June, that streak ended when Texas defeated the Sooners in the Women's College World Series. It was not a disqualifying loss. Oklahoma played and competed. But for a program built on winning, it was the kind of ending that doesn't sit still over an off-season.

Gasso, who has won 8 national titles — the second most in NCAA softball history — does not need to say much about this season's motivation. The players feel it. The coaching staff has channeled it. The 2026 regular season demonstrated a team that was sharp, hungry, and ready for a different outcome in OKC.

Except for one warning sign: the Sooners were eliminated in their SEC Tournament opener by Georgia (10-5 in the quarterfinals), a loss that raised some questions about the depth of their rotation heading into regional play. Those questions will need answers this weekend.

The Norman Regional Bracket

The Norman Regional features four teams: No. 3 Oklahoma, Binghamton, Michigan, and Kansas. Oklahoma faces Binghamton in the opener at 2:30 PM CT; Michigan and Kansas clash at 5 PM CT. All games at Love's Field.

Binghamton (America East champion) represents the tournament's classic small-program automatic qualifier — a team that earned their berth through conference championship play and arrives grateful for the moment, but unlikely to extend it beyond Game 1 against a program as deep as Oklahoma.

Michigan and Kansas represent more credible secondary threats. If either of those two programs advances out of the loser's bracket to face Oklahoma, the Sooners will need their pitching to perform. A healthy Oklahoma rotation shuts down any opposition this weekend. A shaky one against a team like Michigan creates exactly the kind of upset that defines tournament lore.

Gasso's Legacy and the 9th Title

Patty Gasso has spent her career redefining what consistency looks like at the highest level of college softball. Eight national titles. Every WCWS since 2015. The Norman Regional hosting streak. These are not just achievements — they are the standard she has set for every coach in the sport.

If Oklahoma wins the 2026 national championship, Gasso will own the most national titles in NCAA softball history. That milestone provides the kind of narrative gravity that turns a strong season into a historic run. It is the kind of thing players talk about in their pregame conversations, though they would never admit it publicly.

Today's game against Binghamton is the first step. In double-elimination play, every step matters — but the first one sets the tone. An emphatic Oklahoma win this afternoon sends a message to every team in the bracket. A comfortable, dominant performance from the Sooners' offense says: we are here, we remember last June, and we are not done.

What's Next

Oklahoma plays today at 2:30 PM CT (Love's Field, Norman, Okla.) against Binghamton, with the game streaming on ESPN+. A win sends them into the winner's bracket for Saturday competition. The Norman Regional continues through Sunday, May 17. If Oklahoma advances, they will host a Super Regional at Love's Field on May 21–24 — the next step on the road to Devon Park and the Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City. For a program that considers OKC a second home, this is simply the familiar route. The destination is what makes 2026 different.
