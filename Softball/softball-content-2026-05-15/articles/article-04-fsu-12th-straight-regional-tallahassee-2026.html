12 Straight and Still Hungry: Florida State Opens Tallahassee Regional as No. 9 Seed

By Sarah Mitchell | May 15, 2026

Florida State has hosted an NCAA softball regional for twelve consecutive years. That streak speaks for itself — it represents a program that, year after year, does the work necessary to be standing in the postseason and doing so on home soil. JoAnne Graf Field in Tallahassee is where the Seminoles raise their standards and where visiting teams arrive understanding they are in hostile territory.

On Friday at noon ET, FSU (49-8) opens the 2026 Tallahassee Regional against Stetson (36-20, 18-6 ASUN) on ESPN+. The Seminoles are the No. 9 national seed. A 49-8 record, a 20th ACC Tournament championship, and a 21-3 conference slate earned them nine fewer seeds than Alabama despite a resume that many in the softball community believe deserved better. Whether or not the frustration registers publicly, the motivation is real.

A Season Built for Postseason

Florida State went 49-8 in the 2026 regular season and conference tournaments — a win total that puts them among the most successful programs in the country this year. Their 21-3 ACC regular season record was dominant across one of the strongest conferences in the sport. The Seminoles swept through conference play with a physicality and depth that made them consistently dangerous from the lineup's top to bottom.

Earlier this month, FSU claimed their 20th ACC Tournament championship with a 2-1 victory over Virginia Tech — the same Virginia Tech team that finished 46-10 and is one of the most-discussed upsets picks in the entire regional field. The Seminoles beat that team in the ACC final and were seeded four national spots lower. That math is one reason Tallahassee will have an edge tonight.

The Opening Matchup: FSU vs. Stetson

Stetson arrives at the Tallahassee Regional as the ASUN Conference champion. The Hatters (36-20) earned their tournament berth the same way every automatic qualifier does — by winning their conference championship — and they deserve respect for that path. But the gap in talent between Stetson and Florida State on paper is significant.

For FSU, the key in a first-round opener like this is execution without complacency. The No. 9 seed is expected to win this game, and that expectation carries its own kind of pressure. A slow start or an uncharacteristic error can do more damage to a team's tournament psyche than any loss on the regular season schedule. The Seminoles' job today is to be clinical, efficient, and ready for the harder matchups to come.

The Tallahassee Regional also includes two other programs that provide the secondary challenges as the weekend develops. FSU's path to a Super Regional berth depends on staying clean in the winner's bracket.

The 12-Year Streak in Context

Twelve consecutive regional hosts is not a number that accumulates accidentally. It requires finishing in the top 16 of the national seed rankings every single year for over a decade — through coaching turnover, roster cycles, injury seasons, and conference realignments. Florida State under head coach Lonni Alameda has made regional hosting a baseline expectation, which is the highest compliment you can pay a program at this level.

The question that follows FSU into every tournament is whether they can convert the regular season excellence into a deep postseason run. The Seminoles have been close to national title games and experienced the heartbreak that comes with falling short. This 2026 roster, with the talent of a 49-win season behind it and the motivation of a perceived underseeding driving them, is positioned to be the year the Seminoles remind everyone what Tallahassee looks like on a full postseason run.

What's Next

Florida State opens the Tallahassee Regional today at Noon ET on ESPN+ against Stetson at JoAnne Graf Field. A win sets FSU up in the winner's bracket for Saturday competition. The Tallahassee Regional runs through Sunday, May 17. If FSU advances, they host a Super Regional at Tallahassee on May 21–24, with the Women's College World Series waiting in Oklahoma City starting May 28. The No. 9 seed has as good a path as any in this bracket — and a 12-consecutive-hosting streak to remind everyone that Florida State belongs at this stage every year.
