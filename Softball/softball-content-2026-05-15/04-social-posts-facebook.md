### STORY 2: South Dakota Makes History — Cinderella Faces Nebraska

**Long-Form Post:**

This is the softball story you have to know about today. ❤️

South Dakota (20-34-1) is making their first-ever NCAA Tournament appearance tonight. The Coyotes are competing in the Lincoln Regional against No. 4 seed Nebraska — and this is not just a first DI tournament appearance. It's the first time the South Dakota softball program has EVER been to an NCAA Tournament, at any level, since the program started in 1978.

They got here by winning four games in four days at the Summit League Tournament. They knocked out Omaha twice — the tournament's top seed — with consecutive one-run victories. Pitcher Madison Evans was named the All-Tournament MVP for that heroic run.

And here's the beautiful detail: seven of the Coyotes' players are Nebraska natives. Tonight, they're coming home.

Tonight at 5:30 PM ET (ESPN+), a 20-win team walks into the home park of a Big Ten powerhouse in front of their home crowd — and they've already earned their place in softball history just by being there.
