Florida State Clinches Share of 19th ACC Title, Sets Sights on National Seed

By Emily Rawlings | May 3, 2026 | I Love Softball

Florida State keeps adding to its legacy. No. 10 FSU (45-8, 20-3 ACC) clinched a share of its 19th ACC regular season championship on Saturday, further cementing the Seminoles' place as the most decorated program in conference history. It is another milestone in what has been a dominant 2026 season for a program that simply does not know how to rebuild — only reload.

FSU's 20-3 ACC record puts them at the top of the conference standings heading into the ACC Tournament. They are projected to host the Tallahassee Regional in the NCAA Tournament — a significant advantage that comes with being a national seed.

A Season of Consistency

Florida State's road to the title share was built on the kind of day-in, day-out execution that has defined this program under its current coaching staff. The Seminoles have been dominant at home and competitive on the road throughout conference play. At 45-8 overall, they have proven themselves capable of beating elite competition at every level.

FSU's Isa Torres has delivered one of the most productive offensive seasons in ACC history, and the Seminoles' pitching staff has been equally strong. It is the balance between those two facets that makes FSU a genuine national title threat heading into May.

The National Seed Picture

Florida State is firmly in the national seed conversation. The top 8 teams nationally receive national seeds in the NCAA Tournament, meaning they host both a Regional and — if they win — a Super Regional. For FSU, hosting in Tallahassee would give them a massive home-field advantage in the early rounds.

The ACC Tournament (May 6-9, Charlottesville, VA, Palmer Park) offers one more chance for FSU to strengthen its national seeding case. Winning the conference championship would add weight to their argument for a top-4 or top-8 seed. The Seminoles have proven they can win in pressure moments, and the tournament stage is where they have historically performed their best.

19 Times

Nineteen ACC regular season titles. That number is not an accident — it is the product of a recruiting philosophy, a development system, and a culture of winning that has been built over decades in Tallahassee. Each new class learns what it means to be a Seminole, and each new class adds to the title count.

Florida State's 19th title share is not the last chapter of the 2026 story. The ACC Tournament, the NCAA Tournament, and potentially the WCWS remain ahead. But it is another piece of evidence that this program remains one of the sport's enduring powers.

What's Next

Florida State opens the ACC Tournament on May 6 in Charlottesville, Virginia at Palmer Park. The tournament runs through May 9. The Selection Show on May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 will determine FSU's national seed and regional assignment. The Seminoles are projecting to host the Tallahassee Regional. An ACC Tournament title would further strengthen their case for a top-4 national seed. The WCWS runs May 28–June 3 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City.
