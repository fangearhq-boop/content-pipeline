SEC Tournament Bracket Set: Oklahoma No. 1, Alabama No. 2, Florida and Texas Round Out Double Byes

By Sarah Mitchell | May 3, 2026 | I Love Softball

The 2026 SEC Softball Tournament bracket is officially set, and the field is as deep as it has ever been. Oklahoma holds the No. 1 seed with a 20-4 conference record, Alabama is No. 2 at 19-5, Florida is No. 3 at 17-7, and Texas rounds out the top four at 16-8. All four teams earned double byes and will not play until Thursday at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky.

The tournament opens Tuesday, May 5 with the bottom half of the bracket. Seeds 5 through 15 will begin working through the single-elimination format until the No. 1 and No. 2 seeds enter on Thursday. The championship game is Saturday, May 9.

The Top Four — and What They're Playing For

Oklahoma arrives in Lexington fresh off a Saturday sweep of Texas A&M that clinched the Sooners their 14th consecutive SEC or Big 12 regular season title. With Kendall Wells at 36 home runs — one from tying the all-time NCAA record — Oklahoma is arguably the most must-watch team in the country right now. They are the clear favorite to win the SEC Tournament and leave Kentucky as the conference champion.

Alabama (19-5) has been one of the most consistent teams in the conference all season, boasting a pitching staff with an ERA among the SEC's best. The Crimson Tide are a legitimate threat to win the whole thing in Lexington.

Florida (17-7) has the offensive firepower to make noise in single-elimination play, and Texas (16-8) has been steadily improving since mid-season. Neither program should be underestimated in a format where one big performance can end any team's run.

What's at Stake Beyond the Trophy

The SEC Tournament has implications well beyond conference pride. The top 8 teams nationally after all conference tournaments conclude will earn national seeds in the NCAA Tournament — meaning they host a Regional and, if they win, a Super Regional. For teams on the bubble of that top-8 cut, SEC Tournament performance could be the deciding factor.

Oklahoma and Nebraska are near-locks for national seeds. Alabama, Texas, and Florida are all in the mix. A run to the SEC Tournament semifinals or final adds significant weight to any team's national seed case.

The Selection Show on May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 will reveal the full 64-team bracket. Teams are watching every game in Lexington closely.

What's Next

The SEC Tournament runs May 5-9 at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky, with games on SEC Network and ESPN networks throughout the week. Oklahoma (No. 1 seed), Alabama (No. 2), Florida (No. 3), and Texas (No. 4) all begin play Thursday. The bracket opens Tuesday with games involving seeds 5-15. The SEC champion earns an automatic bid to the NCAA Tournament, and the Selection Show on May 10 sets the full 64-team field. The WCWS runs May 28–June 3 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City.
