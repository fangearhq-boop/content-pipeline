Canady's Farewell: Perfect Game on Senior Day as Texas Tech Becomes First to 50 Wins

By Jordan Reeves | May 3, 2026 | I Love Softball

NiJaree Canady saved her best for Senior Day.

The Texas Tech ace threw the first perfect game of her career on Saturday at Rocky Johnson Field, retiring all 15 Baylor batters she faced in an 8-0 run-rule win in five innings. Nine strikeouts. Sixty-two pitches. Not a single baserunner. It was the sixth perfect game in Texas Tech program history, and the most emotionally charged of them all.

A Career Built for This Moment

Canady came to Texas Tech as the most anticipated recruit in the program's history, and she has delivered on every expectation. In four years, she surpassed 1,000 career strikeouts — a milestone she reached earlier this season. She has thrown multiple no-hitters. She has been the anchor of two consecutive Big 12 regular season titles.

But she had never thrown a perfect game. Until Saturday.

The performance was immaculate from the first pitch to the last. Canady mixed her fastball and rise ball with precision, never falling behind in the count, never allowing a competitive at-bat. The Baylor lineup — solid enough during the regular season — looked overmatched all afternoon. Canady's 21st win of the season was her most memorable.

First Team to 50 Wins

The win pushed Texas Tech to 50-4 on the season, making the Red Raiders the first team in the country to reach 50 wins in 2026. That number reflects not just Canady's brilliance but a program that has learned to win in multiple ways. Texas Tech has players who can mash, a pitching staff that can shut down elite lineups, and a coaching staff that has maximized this senior class.

The Red Raiders are the No. 1 seed in the Big 12 Tournament, which begins May 7 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. The same venue that will host the WCWS at the end of May. Familiarity with Devon Park could be an edge in the NCAA Tournament if Texas Tech earns a Super Regional trip to OKC.

A Legacy Sealed

Canady's place in Texas Tech softball history was secured long before Saturday. But the perfect game on Senior Day adds a final chapter that feels almost scripted. The program that recruited her, the fans who watched her grow, the teammates who have caught her pitches for four years — they all got to see the best version of her in one of the biggest settings she'll face in Lubbock.

The next time Canady pitches in a high-stakes environment, it will be at the Big 12 Tournament or the NCAA Tournament. She has shown in every stage of her career that the moment doesn't intimidate her. It brings out something else entirely.

What's Next

Texas Tech (50-4) opens the Big 12 Tournament as the No. 1 seed on May 7 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, facing the No. 8 seed at 1:30 PM CT. The tournament runs through May 9. A deep run here — with Canady leading the staff — would cement the Red Raiders as a true national title contender. The Selection Show on May 10 will likely make Texas Tech a national seed and a regional host. The WCWS is May 28–June 3, also at Devon Park.
