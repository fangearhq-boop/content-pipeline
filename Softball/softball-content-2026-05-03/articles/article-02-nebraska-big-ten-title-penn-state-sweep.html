Nebraska Makes History: First Outright Big Ten Title After Penn State Sweep

By Emily Rawlings | May 3, 2026 | I Love Softball

Nebraska softball has done something it has never done before. The No. 2 Huskers swept Penn State in a Saturday doubleheader — winning 5-1 in Game 1 and 10-4 in Game 2 — to clinch the outright Big Ten regular season title for the first time in program history.

It is the program's first regular season title of any kind since 2014, and never before have the Huskers owned it outright. On a sunny afternoon in State College, Nebraska (42-6, 22-1 Big Ten) made history.

Frahm Dominates Game 1

Jordy Frahm handled Game 1 start-to-finish. The ace went seven full innings, allowed one run, and struck out seven Penn State hitters. Jesse Farrell provided the offensive punch, going 2-for-3 with a double and a solo home run. Nebraska's defense and pitching staff were sharp all afternoon, and the 5-1 final was never really in doubt.

Frahm's performance was the latest entry in what has been one of the best seasons by a Big Ten pitcher in recent memory. She has been the engine of this Nebraska team, and in the biggest game on the biggest stage, she delivered exactly what the Huskers needed.

Comeback Clinches It in Game 2

Game 2 was a different kind of story. Penn State jumped out to a 4-1 lead heading into the sixth inning, and the Nittany Lions crowd was energized. Nebraska had other plans.

Kacie Hoffman struck first in the rally, driving in two runs to make it 4-3. Then Frahm — who had already thrown a complete game — came up with an RBI single to tie the game at 4-4. The knockout blow came from Hannah Coor, who lined a two-RBI double to give Nebraska the lead for good. The Huskers scored nine runs in the final two innings and cruised to a 10-4 victory.

The comeback is a reflection of everything Nebraska has been in 2026: resilient, deep, and capable of winning games multiple ways. This is a team that can beat you with pitching, with power, and now, with a late-inning rally on the road in a pressure situation.

A Number That Tells the Story

Nebraska's 22-1 Big Ten record speaks for itself. The Huskers went through the best conference in their league and lost only once. They are the No. 1 seed in the Big Ten Tournament (May 6-9, College Park, MD) and one of the front-runners for a national seed heading into the May 10 Selection Show.

A national seed would mean hosting a Regional at Bowlin Stadium in Lincoln — a chance for Nebraska fans to watch their team play in the NCAA Tournament on their home turf. The Huskers are on track to make that happen.

What's Next

Nebraska heads to College Park, Maryland next week as the No. 1 seed in the Big Ten Tournament (May 6-9). An outright Big Ten Tournament title would further cement their national seeding case. The Selection Show on May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 will determine whether Nebraska earns one of the top 8 national seeds and the right to host a Regional. The WCWS is May 28–June 3 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City, and the Huskers have every reason to believe they belong there.
