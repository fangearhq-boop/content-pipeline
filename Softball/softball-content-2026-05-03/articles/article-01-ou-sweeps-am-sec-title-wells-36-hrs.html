Oklahoma Sweeps A&M, Clinches 14th Straight SEC Title — Wells Ends Regular Season at 36 HRs

By Sarah Mitchell | May 3, 2026 | I Love Softball

No. 1 Oklahoma refused to let a stunning Game 1 loss define its season. The Sooners swept Texas A&M in Games 2 and 3 on Saturday at Davis Diamond in College Station, clinching the SEC regular season title for the 14th consecutive year and setting the stage for what promises to be a historic postseason run.

The hero of the series was junior outfielder Kai Minor. With the Sooners down to their final chances in a tied Game 2, Minor laced a two-out triple down the right field line in the sixth inning to plate the go-ahead run. Oklahoma held on for a 4-3 win and forced the rubber match.

Minor Sets the Tone in Game 3

Game 3 began on the fourth pitch. Minor turned on a fastball and sent it over the fence, giving OU an immediate 1-0 lead before the Aggies could settle in. Ella Parker and Kasidi Pickering added home runs as the Sooners built a cushion they would not surrender. Final score: 6-4, Oklahoma.

For Minor, the weekend was a statement performance. She bookended Saturday with a clutch triple and a leadoff home run, providing exactly the kind of aggressive play that has defined Oklahoma's championship culture. Head coach Patty Gasso has built a program where big-game moments bring out the best, and Minor delivered both times.

14 Straight — A Dynasty Still Rolling

Oklahoma's 14th consecutive year winning at least a share of the SEC or Big 12 regular season title is a number that doesn't get easier to contextualize with time. The Sooners (20-4 SEC) are the conference's No. 1 seed heading into the SEC Tournament, which begins Tuesday at John Cropp Stadium in Lexington, Kentucky. Oklahoma draws a double bye and will not play until Thursday.

The dynasty has absorbed roster turnover, conference realignment, and heightened competition from a deeper SEC field in 2026. It has also absorbed a Game 1 loss to a surging Texas A&M team — and then won two straight when the stakes were highest.

Wells at 36 — One From the Record

Kendall Wells did not homer against Texas A&M, but her place in history is no less assured. The Sooners freshman ends the regular season with 36 home runs — one short of tying Lauren Espinoza's all-time NCAA single-season record of 37, set at the University of Arizona in 1995. Two home runs would break the record outright.

Wells has already rewritten multiple records this spring: the NCAA freshman home run record, the SEC single-season record, and the Oklahoma program record all belong to her. The all-time mark is the last one standing, and she will have multiple SEC Tournament games to chase it down.

Every at-bat for Wells in Lexington will carry the weight of 31 years of history. The record has survived every single season since Clinton was president. It might not survive this one.

What's Next

Oklahoma begins the SEC Tournament on Thursday in Lexington with a double bye already in hand. The Sooners are the No. 1 overall seed and the favorite to win the conference tournament — though Alabama, Florida, Texas, and a resurgent Texas A&M are all capable of making a run. The Selection Show on May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2 will set the full NCAA Tournament bracket. The WCWS runs May 28–June 3 in Devon Park, Oklahoma City. Oklahoma's fourteenth straight championship gets its biggest tests yet.
