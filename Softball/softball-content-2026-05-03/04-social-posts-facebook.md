### STORY 1:

#### Long-Form Post -- 9:00 AM
No. 1 Oklahoma wouldn't let the season slip away.

After getting stunned by Texas A&M 8-5 in Game 1, the Sooners came back Saturday and swept Games 2 and 3 in College Station to clinch the SEC regular season title for the 14th consecutive year.

The difference? Kai Minor.

In Game 2, Minor delivered a two-out triple in the sixth inning to give the Sooners a lead they wouldn't relinquish — OU won 4-3. In Game 3, Minor went even deeper. She homered on just the fourth pitch of the rubber match, setting the tone immediately. Ella Parker and Kasidi Pickering added homers of their own as the Sooners pulled away 6-4.

And then there's Kendall Wells. The freshman phenom finished the regular season with 36 home runs — one from tying Lauren Espinoza's all-time NCAA single-season record (37, Arizona, 1995). Wells did not homer in the series but goes into the SEC Tournament with history still firmly in front of her.

Oklahoma is the No. 1 seed in the SEC Tournament, which begins Tuesday in Lexington, KY. The Sooners play Thursday with a double bye. Fourteen straight. And the record chase continues. 🥎

### STORY 2:

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:30 AM
Nebraska made history on Saturday.

The No. 2 Huskers swept Penn State in a doubleheader — 5-1 in Game 1, 10-4 in Game 2 — to clinch the outright Big Ten regular season title. For the first time ever, Nebraska owns the Big Ten regular season crown outright. The Huskers' last regular season title came in 2014 — and it wasn't outright.

Jordy Frahm was locked in from the first pitch of Game 1: seven innings, seven strikeouts, one run. Jesse Farrell added a solo home run and went 2-for-3.

Game 2 was the drama. Nebraska fell behind 4-1 heading into the sixth inning, and Penn State's crowd was alive. Then the Huskers answered: Kacie Hoffman drove in two runs, Frahm tied it with a single, and Hannah Coor delivered the knockout blow — a two-RBI double that put Nebraska ahead for good.

Final record: 42-6 overall, 22-1 in the Big Ten.

The Huskers are the No. 1 seed in the Big Ten Tournament next week (May 6-9, College Park, MD), and they're firmly in the national seed conversation heading into May. Bowlin Stadium could be hosting a Regional.

Nebraska. Outright. First time ever. 🌽

### STORY 3:

#### Long-Form Post -- 11:00 AM
NiJaree Canady's college career won't be forgotten.

The Texas Tech senior saved the best for last on Saturday, throwing the first perfect game of her career on Senior Day — a 8-0 run-rule win over Baylor in five innings. Nine strikeouts. Sixty-two pitches. Not a single baserunner.

It was the sixth perfect game in Texas Tech program history, and it pushed the Red Raiders to 50-4 — making them the first team in the country to reach 50 wins in 2026.

Canady entered the day with 1,000+ career strikeouts, 21 wins in her senior year, and a legacy as one of the most dominant pitchers in the history of the sport. Saturday's performance was a reminder of exactly what she is.

Texas Tech is the No. 1 seed in the Big 12 Tournament, which begins May 7 at Devon Park in Oklahoma City. Canady and the Red Raiders have one thing on their mind: a trip to the WCWS.

What a send-off for one of the greats. 🥎

The NCAA Selection Show is May 10 at 7 PM ET on ESPN2.

This tournament might be the best field in the history of the SEC. Don't miss it.
