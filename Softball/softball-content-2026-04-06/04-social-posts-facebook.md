This series will shape national seeds. It will shift rankings. It will create memories. Do not miss it.

### STORY 3: Kendall Wells — One HR From History

#### Long-Form Post -- 1:00 PM

She has done everything asked of her. And then some.

Kendall Wells, a freshman from Broken Arrow, Oklahoma, entered the 2026 season as one of the most hyped recruits in the country. She has delivered beyond any reasonable expectation.

On Saturday, April 4, Wells hit her 30th home run of the season against Kentucky — a 261-foot shot to right-center field. With that swing, she tied the NCAA single-season freshman home run record, a mark shared by three extraordinary players: Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma, 2012), Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma, 2018), and Kelly Majam (Hawaii, 2010).

Wells is now one home run away from sole possession of the record.

She is batting .383. She has 60 RBIs. She leads the nation in home runs. And she plays for a 38-3 Oklahoma team that is hungry for a national title.

The Red River Rivalry against Texas starts April 10. There is no bigger stage in college softball. If Wells is going to break the record, the stage will be worthy of the moment.

### STORY 4: FSU's 24-Game Win Streak

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 PM

It never gets old.

Florida State trailed Notre Dame 4-0 in the final game of their series Sunday. For most teams, that is a hole too deep to escape. For this year's Seminoles, it was just another obstacle to overcome.

Jaysoni Beachum started the comeback with a two-out triple in the fourth inning. Kennedy Harp doubled home the go-ahead run in the fifth. Then, in the sixth, Anna Hinde slapped a two-run single to push the lead to 8-4. FSU won the game 8-4 and completed the sweep of Notre Dame.

The win streak stands at 24 games — the nation's longest. Florida State is 34-4 overall and a perfect 12-0 in the ACC. They have not lost a conference game all season. Head coach Lonni Alameda, who recorded her 1,000th career win earlier this season, has this program operating at an elite level.

The path to the Women's College World Series in Oklahoma City goes through whoever earns a No. 1 national seed. Florida State is making the case that it should be them.

### STORY 5: Kylee Edwards LSU Cycle

#### Long-Form Post -- 5:30 PM

It had never been done in 46 years.

Kylee Edwards changed that on April 3.

The LSU junior stepped into the box four times in the Tigers' 16-4 win over Missouri and reached base four different ways, in four different innings. Home run in the second. Single in the third. Double in the sixth. Triple in the seventh. Four-for-six. Four RBIs.

And history.

Edwards became the first player ever to hit for the cycle in LSU softball history. The program was founded 46 years ago. She is the first. The moment was not lost on her teammates, who mobbed her at third base after the triple.

Edwards came to LSU as one of 11 newcomers on the 2026 roster, transferring from Mississippi State. She had already been on a tear — homering in six consecutive games before Friday's cycle. Now she is playing for the record books.

LSU improved to 16-4 in the game and secured the series over Missouri with the convincing win. Edwards has emerged as one of the SEC's most exciting offensive players. If you have not been watching, start now.

### STORY 6: Alabama's Statement — What's Next

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM

Alabama has sent its message.

The Crimson Tide walked into Rhoads Stadium last week, faced the No. 1 team in the country, and took the series 2-1. It was Alabama's first series win over a top-ranked team since April 2016 — the year they last went deep in the national tournament.

The difference this time was Katie Stewart. In the series clincher, Saturday's 7-4 win, Stewart went 3-for-3 with two home runs — a two-run blast and a solo shot, her 19th and 20th of the season. Reese Atwood added her 15th home run of the year. Together, they turned what had been a close game into a decisive statement.

Alabama is now 34-3 and positioned to make a significant leap in the NFCA poll on Tuesday. More importantly, they have answered the question that has followed this program all season: can they compete with the nation's best? The answer is yes.
