NFCA Week 9 Poll Preview: Alabama's Surge Sets Up Most Dramatic Rankings Drop of 2026

By Sarah Mitchell | April 6, 2026

Alabama stunned No. 1 Texas in a series. Oklahoma is 38-3. FSU is on a 24-game win streak. When the coaches' poll drops Tuesday morning, nothing will look the same.

The NFCA Week 9 poll drops Tuesday morning at 10 AM ET — and if the last week of college softball is any guide, it will be the most consequential rankings release of the 2026 season. The top of the sport just shifted on its axis, and the coaches who vote in this poll will have to grapple with what that means for a national title race that has suddenly become far more interesting.

Texas entered the weekend ranked No. 1 in the nation, as they had been for much of the spring. They left it having dropped two of three games to Alabama at Rhoads Stadium in Tuscaloosa. That result alone demands a response from the poll. Texas will fall. The only question is how far.

Alabama's Case for a Major Leap

Alabama's series win over No. 1 Texas on April 2-4 was their first over a top-ranked team since April 2016. The Crimson Tide were ranked No. 4 or No. 5 heading into the series, depending on which poll you consulted. Now, with a signature win on their resume, they have every right to demand a significant rise.

The story of the series was Katie Stewart. In the clinching 7-4 win on Saturday, she went 3-for-3 with two home runs — her 19th and 20th of the season. Reese Atwood added her 15th homer. Alabama won the rubber match convincingly, and the final impression was of a team that genuinely belongs among the nation's elite.

Alabama could realistically jump to anywhere from No. 2 to No. 4 in Tuesday's poll, depending on how the coaches weigh the body of evidence.

Texas's Drop — And Who Benefits

Texas falls to 32-4 overall and 9-3 in SEC play after the loss. That record is still excellent, but it no longer supports a No. 1 ranking in a field where other teams have fewer losses and more recent momentum. The Longhorns will drop — likely into the No. 3 to No. 5 range — and the ripple effects will push others up.

Oklahoma is the most obvious beneficiary. The Sooners are 38-3 overall and 11-1 in the SEC, with Kendall Wells sitting at 30 home runs after tying the NCAA freshman record last weekend. Oklahoma has not just won — they have run-ruled opponents and done it with flair. A jump to No. 1 or No. 2 would be well-deserved.

Texas Tech also deserves consideration. At 34-2, the Red Raiders have been one of the most consistent programs in the country all season. They were No. 2 in Week 8, just nine points behind Texas. A Texas drop could push them into or near the top spot.

FSU's Streak Demands Recognition

Florida State's 24-game win streak — the nation's longest active streak — should also factor into voters' minds. The Seminoles are 34-4 overall and a perfect 12-0 in ACC play, having not lost a conference game all season. They swept Notre Dame over the weekend, coming back from 4-0 down in the series finale. If the poll rewards sustained excellence, FSU could climb into the top five.

What's Next

The NFCA Week 9 poll releases Tuesday, April 7, at 10 AM ET at nfca.org. Whoever claims the top spot will carry a target into one of the biggest weeks of the regular season — headlined by the Red River Rivalry between Texas and Oklahoma beginning April 10 in Austin. The poll matters, but it will be tested immediately.
