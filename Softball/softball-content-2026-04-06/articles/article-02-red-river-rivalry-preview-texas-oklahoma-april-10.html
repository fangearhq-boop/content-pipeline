Red River Rivalry Preview: Texas and Oklahoma Set for Series That Could Define 2026

By Emily Rawlings | April 6, 2026

The most anticipated series of the regular season begins Friday in Austin. Texas arrives wounded. Oklahoma arrives with history on the line. Kendall Wells is one home run from a record.

Four days. That is the distance between now and the most important series in college softball this spring. Texas hosts Oklahoma at McCombs Field in Austin beginning Friday, April 10, with Game 1 on ESPN2 at 6 PM CT. Games 2 and 3 will follow on Saturday and Sunday on ESPN.

These are not just good teams meeting. These are the two programs that have defined college softball for a generation — a rivalry that has visited the Women's College World Series seven times between them in recent years. This spring, the stakes have only gotten higher.

Texas: A Team That Needs to Respond

Texas enters this series at 32-4 overall and 9-3 in SEC play, having just dropped two of three games to Alabama at Rhoads Stadium last weekend. For the Longhorns, who opened the season with 29 consecutive wins, back-to-back series losses would be a genuine crisis heading into the postseason.

Texas is still a WCWS contender — their overall numbers and strength of schedule remain elite. But they need to remind the country what they are capable of. Hosting Oklahoma in a primetime series on ESPN is the perfect opportunity. The question is whether the wounds from Alabama are deep enough to affect performance, or whether a rival as fierce as Oklahoma brings out the best in this group.

In 2025, Texas defeated Oklahoma 4-2 in the Women's College World Series on the way to a national championship. The Sooners remember that. So do the Longhorns.

Oklahoma: Red-Hot with History on the Line

Oklahoma arrives at 38-3 overall and 11-1 in SEC play. The Sooners have been rolling since the opening weekend and show no signs of slowing down. Their offense has been carried by Kendall Wells, whose home run production has been historic from the first week of the season.

Wells hit her 30th home run of the season last Saturday against Kentucky, tying the all-time NCAA single-season freshman home run record shared by Lauren Chamberlain (2012), Jocelyn Alo (2018), and Kelly Majam (2010). Home run No. 31 would give Wells the record outright — and if she hits it on the road at McCombs Field in front of a nationally televised audience, it will be one of the signature moments of the college softball season.

Oklahoma also boasts one of the strongest pitching staffs in the country, and their 11-1 SEC record reflects a team that wins both close games and blowouts. They are the hungriest team in Austin this weekend.

What to Watch

The first at-bat Wells gets with a runner on base will be the moment everyone watches. Each swing carries the potential for history. Beyond that individual narrative, watch how Texas responds on the mound in Game 1 — the Longhorns will need their ace to set the tone early if they want to steal the opener at home.

Both programs have combined to win five national championships in recent years. Both know that what happens this weekend echoes all the way to Oklahoma City in June. There are no neutral fans in Austin on Friday night.

What's Next

The Red River Rivalry begins Friday, April 10, at 6 PM CT on ESPN2. The series continues Saturday and Sunday on ESPN. The result will reshape national seed projections and the NFCA rankings, with the NFCA Week 9 poll already scheduled to drop Tuesday morning at 10 AM ET. This week in college softball does not have a slow moment.
