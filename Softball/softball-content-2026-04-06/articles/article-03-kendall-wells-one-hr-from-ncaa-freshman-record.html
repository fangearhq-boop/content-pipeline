Kendall Wells Is One Home Run From Making NCAA Freshman History Outright

By Jordan Reeves | April 6, 2026

She tied the record at 30. Now the outright mark belongs to whoever can break it. With the Red River Rivalry coming Friday, the stage could not be bigger.

The record has stood for 13 years in one form or another. Lauren Chamberlain set it at 30 in 2012. Jocelyn Alo matched it in 2018. Kelly Majam first reached that number in 2010. Three players, across more than a decade, have shared what was thought to be the ceiling for home runs by a college softball freshman.

Kendall Wells joined them on April 4, launching a 261-foot shot to right-center field against Kentucky in the fifth inning of Oklahoma's 12-2 run-rule win. Home run No. 30. The record tied.

Home run No. 31 is there for the taking. And the Red River Rivalry against Texas begins Friday.

A Season Beyond Expectations

Wells came to Oklahoma as a highly recruited freshman from Broken Arrow, Oklahoma. The expectations were substantial. The production has exceeded all of them.

She is batting .383 with 60 RBIs through the end of the Kentucky series. She leads the nation in home runs. She set the SEC single-season freshman home run record on March 31, when she hit her 27th home run of the year against Wichita State, breaking a record previously held by Bri Ellis, the 2025 USA Softball Player of the Year.

From there, the pace accelerated. She hit No. 28 and No. 29 in the first two games of the Kentucky series, then sealed the record-tying shot in the series finale. In the span of four days, she went from breaking an SEC record to tying the NCAA record.

The Next Level: 34

Breaking the freshman record is historic, but the conversation around Wells has begun to include an even more audacious number. The all-time NCAA single-season home run record belongs to Jocelyn Alo, who hit 34 in a season. Wells needs five more to tie that mark. With roughly two months of baseball remaining in the regular season and postseason, it is mathematically reachable.

Oklahoma is 38-3 and 11-1 in SEC play heading into the Red River Rivalry. The Sooners have no shortage of remaining games — or remaining opponents who will have to face Wells in the lineup.

The Red River Rivalry as the Stage

Oklahoma does not play again until Friday, April 10, when the Red River Rivalry opens at McCombs Field in Austin. Texas is the opponent — a Texas team that just dropped a series to Alabama and needs a win badly. The environment will be charged on both sides.

If Wells hits home run No. 31 at McCombs Field, in front of a nationally televised crowd on ESPN2, with history on the line, it will be one of the most memorable moments of the 2026 college softball season.

That is the opportunity in front of her. It is the kind of moment that comes along once in a generation. Wells has earned it.

What's Next

The Red River Rivalry begins Friday, April 10, at 6 PM CT on ESPN2. Games 2 and 3 follow on ESPN. The NFCA Week 9 poll, which will likely move Oklahoma up after their 38-3 mark and Wells' historic performance, drops Tuesday at 10 AM ET. Every Wells at-bat this week carries the weight of history.
