FSU's 24-Game Win Streak Is the Nation's Longest — and the Seminoles Are Just Getting Started

By Sarah Mitchell | April 6, 2026

Florida State rallied from 4-0 down to sweep Notre Dame and push their win streak to 24 — the longest active streak in the country. No ACC opponent has beaten them all season.

They trailed 4-0. In the stands, in the dugouts, and in the broadcast booth, the reasonable assumption was that the streak was in danger. Florida State had not faced many deficits during their remarkable run. Sunday's series finale against Notre Dame put them in unfamiliar territory.

They handled it the way this team has handled everything in 2026: by coming back and winning.

Jaysoni Beachum sparked the rally with a two-out triple in the fourth inning that tied the game. Kennedy Harp followed in the fifth with an RBI double that gave FSU its first lead. Then in the sixth, Harp added another RBI hit and Anna Hinde delivered a two-RBI single that extended the lead to 8-4. The final score: 8-4. The win streak: 24 games.

The Most Dominant Run of 2026

Florida State's win streak is the nation's longest active streak and the fifth-longest in program history — its best since 2018. The Seminoles are 34-4 overall and a perfect 12-0 in ACC play. They have not lost a conference game at any point in the 2026 season.

The streak encompasses wins over ranked and unranked opponents alike. FSU has won close games and run-ruled opponents. They have come back from deficits and built comfortable leads. The defining characteristic of this run is not any individual style of victory — it is the consistency and the refusal to lose.

Head coach Lonni Alameda recorded her 1,000th career coaching win earlier this season — a milestone that came at an appropriate time, with her team showing peak form. Alameda has built one of the most reliable programs in the sport over her tenure, and this version of the Seminoles is among her finest.

The Comeback That Defined the Weekend

The Notre Dame sweep was complete in three games: 7-2, 10-0 (run rule), and 8-4. Each win was comfortable, and yet Sunday's came with more drama than the scoreline suggests. Down 4-0, FSU needed their resilient brand of softball to surface — and it did, across four consecutive scoring innings.

Beachum's triple was the catalyst. Harp's double was the dagger. The stat line of the game is less impressive than the context: down multiple runs on the road in a sweep attempt, FSU simply refused to lose.

WCWS Credentials Building

Florida State's 12-0 ACC record puts them in position to claim the conference's automatic bid, but the bigger goal is a top national seed at the NCAA tournament. A No. 1 national seed would mean hosting a Regional and Super Regional — potentially keeping the path to Oklahoma City through Tallahassee for multiple rounds.

The NFCA Week 9 poll drops Tuesday, April 7. Whether the Seminoles move inside the top five — or crack the top three — will be determined in part by how voters weigh the streak against Alabama's signature win and Oklahoma's dominant record.

What's Next

FSU's next series assignment and schedule will test whether the streak can grow further. The NFCA Week 9 poll releases Tuesday, April 7, at 10 AM ET. Florida State's 24-game win streak has put them firmly in the national title conversation — and with the WCWS still months away, there is plenty of time for the streak to grow even longer.
