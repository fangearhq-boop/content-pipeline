Kasidi Pickering Signs with Texas Tech — Three All-Americans Complete Historic Portal Class

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 18, 2026

Texas Tech made it official.

Kasidi Pickering, the 2025 NFCA All-American outfielder from Oklahoma, has signed with the Texas Tech Red Raiders — completing what Softball America has called the No. 1 transfer portal class in the country and what many in college softball are calling the most dominant single-window recruiting haul in the sport's history.

Pickering is the third All-American to join the Red Raiders in this 15-day window, joining nation's top hitter Moriah Polar and No. 1 ranked portal pitcher Jayden Heavener. She is a rising senior with one year of eligibility and a career résumé that speaks for itself.

Who Pickering Is

Pickering spent three seasons at Oklahoma, where she was a cornerstone of one of the most successful programs in college softball. She helped the Sooners win a national championship in 2024 and left Norman with career totals that place her among the most accomplished hitters in recent OU history.

Three seasons. A .384 batting average. Fifty home runs. One hundred sixty-nine runs batted in. Those are the career numbers that Pickering brings with her to Lubbock.

Her 2026 season at Oklahoma was her most powerful individually. She set career highs with 20 home runs and 61 RBIs while posting a .370 batting average in 59 games. Opponents knew what she could do, and she did it anyway.

Pickering is from Humble, Texas — meaning she is returning to her home state to play the final chapter of her college career. For a program built in West Texas and competing against Texas in-state, adding a Texas-native Oklahoma power bat carries significance beyond the stat line.

The Class That Changed the Conversation

Pickering completes a portal class that began taking shape on June 13 when Jayden Heavener committed. Moriah Polar followed on June 15. Pickering, who had been tagged with a "Do Not Transfer" designation pointing to Texas Tech by multiple outlets, made it official as the portal window entered its final days.

The three players represent three distinct but equally elite contributions to what Texas Tech already had:

Polar, from Purdue, posted a .555 batting average with 101 hits in 2026 — the best average of any Division I player in the country. She is a contact machine who will change how pitchers approach the Red Raiders lineup.

Heavener is a power arm who brings a 2.81 ERA and 128 strikeouts to a pitching staff that already carried Texas Tech to a 61-8 record and the WCWS championship game in 2026. Adding another elite pitcher to that staff creates a depth that most programs in the country cannot match.

Pickering brings the power component. With 20 home runs in 2026 and 50 for her career, she is the bat in the lineup that opposing coaches will gameplan around every single time she steps to the plate.

What Texas Tech Already Has

The 2026 Red Raiders finished 61-8 and made the WCWS championship game, falling to two-time champion Texas in a hard-fought series. They were the No. 11 national seed who knocked out No. 1 Alabama twice on the way to Oklahoma City. NiJaree Canady, in her final college season, delivered a legendary performance in the tournament.

Canady has moved on to professional softball. But the core of that team — the players who went 61-8 and lived in the biggest moments — largely returns. Texas Tech head coach Jerry Glasco now layers three elite transfers onto that proven foundation.

Softball America named the class No. 1 nationally within hours of Pickering's signing becoming official. The framing was not about potential — it was about the certifiable, verified production each of these three players already brought into this program.

What's Next

Texas Tech's portal business is done. The Red Raiders have their class, and now the work shifts to integrating three All-Americans into a program that was already among the best in the country.

The matchup everyone will circle on the 2027 calendar: Texas vs. Texas Tech. The two-time defending champions who closed out the 2026 season in the WCWS championship game will be waiting, and Texas built its own elite portal class around D1 Player of the Year Isa Torres, catcher Samantha Swan, and pitcher Samantha Lincoln.

Two programs. Two historic portal classes. One rivalry that was already personal before either class was assembled.

For Texas Tech fans, Pickering's signing is the moment this offseason fully crystallized. The Red Raiders are not rebuilding. They are reloading — three All-Americans at a time.
