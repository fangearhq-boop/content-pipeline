Portal Countdown: Day 11 of 15 — Four Days Left, One Big Name Still Out There

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 18, 2026

Day 11. Four days remain.

The 2026 NCAA softball transfer portal opened on June 8 and closes on June 22. What began as a 15-day window filled with uncertainty — dozens of elite players available, dozens of programs rebuilding and reloading — has narrowed to a single final chapter: Jaysoni Beachum.

With Kasidi Pickering signing with Texas Tech on June 18, the last major card of this historic portal cycle has been played by everyone except Beachum. Here is where things stand.

The Committed List

Twelve significant transfers have found new homes since the window opened:

Texas: Isa Torres (D1 Player of the Year, .530 BA, FSU), Samantha Swan (No. 1 catcher, .383 BA, Arizona State), Samantha Lincoln (pitcher, Texas Tech), Savannah Altmeyer. Coach Mike White assembled the portal class built around the best player in the sport and the No. 1 catcher on the market. Two-time champions with room to spare.

Texas Tech: Moriah Polar (.555 BA, nation's top hitter, Purdue), Jayden Heavener (No. 1 pitcher, 2.81 ERA, 128 K), Kasidi Pickering (career .384 BA, 50 HR, 169 RBI, Oklahoma). Softball America's No. 1 class. The WCWS runner-up added three All-Americans.

Tennessee: Karley Shelton (2B, .345 BA, 40 RBI, South Carolina).

Texas A&M: Maddy Azua (pitcher, Sun Belt POY, 1.95 ERA, 244.2 IP, 26-13 record).

Alabama: Kailey Plumlee (pitcher, Tennessee).

Oklahoma State: Hannah Hartley.

Arkansas: Taylor Wetteland (Mountain West Freshman of the Year).

The One Name Still Out There

Jaysoni Beachum entered the portal on June 8. She has been uncommitted for 11 days. She is rated No. 1 on Softball America's transfer rankings and carries an ON3 rating of 97.00.

The profile that has every program in the country watching: three seasons at Florida State, .375 career batting average, 37 home runs, 180 RBIs, 202 hits. In 2026, she posted a .417 batting average with 16 home runs, 66 RBIs, and a .549 on-base percentage. She was the 2024 NFCA Freshman of the Year. She was named a 2026 Second-Team All-American.

Where she goes is unknown. When she announces is unknown. The only certainty is the deadline: June 22 at midnight. After that, the portal window closes.

What This Class Did to the Sport

Put into historical context: this 15-day window produced back-to-back All-American classes at two of the sport's premier programs. Texas added a D1 Player of the Year. Texas Tech added the nation's top hitter, the top portal pitcher, and a 50-HR power bat. Historically, one of those outcomes would be the story of an entire offseason.

In 2026, it happened simultaneously in the same two-week window — at schools 330 miles apart, in the same conference, with the same unfinished championship business driving the decisions.

College softball enters 2027 with a very specific rivalry at its center. Texas and Texas Tech have both made their cases. The preseason polls will have a field day sorting out which class is actually better on paper. The only poll that matters comes next May and June.

What's Next

Four days. June 19, 20, 21, and 22. Beachum has until June 22 to decide, and she appears in no hurry. When the announcement comes, it closes the book on the most remarkable transfer portal cycle in college softball history.

Every program remaining without a signature add should be treating these four days as critical. The window is almost closed. The players still available will not be available after June 22. For those who miss out, the recruiting cycle — high school and junior college — is where they turn next.

For softball fans, Day 11 ends with most of the drama resolved and one last headline still to come.
