Texas Tech's Historic Portal Class: What Three All-Americans Mean for 2027

By Jordan Reeves | I Love Softball | June 18, 2026

The numbers have been written. The signings are done. Texas Tech's 2026 transfer portal class is closed, and it is unlike anything college softball has seen.

In the span of five days — from June 13, when Jayden Heavener committed, to June 18, when Kasidi Pickering made it official — the Red Raiders assembled a class of three All-Americans from three different programs. Softball America ranked it No. 1 in the nation. Most observers ran out of appropriate comparisons quickly.

Now comes the question that matters for 2027: what does it actually mean?

The Pieces, Individually

Start with Moriah Polar. The former Purdue outfielder led Division I softball in 2026 with a .555 batting average and 101 hits — numbers that look more like a typo than a stat line. She is not a fluke performer. Her career average at Purdue was .436 over four seasons, which tells you that her 2026 breakout was the result of a player hitting her ceiling rather than an outlier season. At Texas Tech, she walks into a lineup that already had offensive depth. She makes it dangerous from the leadoff spot to the bottom of the order.

Jayden Heavener is the No. 1 ranked pitcher in this year's portal by D1Softball. A 2.81 ERA. One hundred twenty-eight strikeouts. She steps into a Texas Tech staff that lost NiJaree Canady to professional softball — the most difficult departure a pitching staff could absorb. Heavener does not replace Canady. No one does. But she is a No. 1-caliber arm who gives Jerry Glasco a genuine ace around whom a championship rotation can be built.

Kasidi Pickering is power. Three seasons at Oklahoma, 50 career home runs, 169 career RBIs, and a batting average that has never dipped below .370 in any season. Her 2026 at Oklahoma produced career highs in home runs (20) and RBIs (61). She won a national championship in 2024 with the Sooners. She knows what the biggest stages feel like. In Texas Tech's lineup, she is the power bat who changes game-planning for every pitcher who faces the Red Raiders.

What Texas Tech Returns

The context that makes this class extraordinary is what it is layered on top of.

The 2026 Red Raiders went 61-8 — a record that could carry most programs to a national title in most years. They ran through the WCWS bracket as the No. 11 national seed, defeating No. 1 Alabama twice in the process. They played for the national championship. They competed in the biggest games, on the biggest stage, against the best teams.

Head coach Jerry Glasco still has key pieces from that roster returning. The culture, the system, the experience of competing in Oklahoma City — that does not leave with graduation or the portal. Glasco is adding three elite players to a program that already knows how to win.

The combination is rare. Teams that add historic portal classes often do so because they are rebuilding. Texas Tech is not rebuilding. They are reinforcing.

The Texas Matchup Is Already Written

The natural conversation emerging from this portal cycle is Texas versus Texas Tech in 2027. Both programs built their rosters in the same 15-day window, and the results are exceptional on both sides.

Texas added D1 Player of the Year Isa Torres (.530 BA, .591 OBP), No. 1 catcher Samantha Swan (.383 BA, 1.151 OPS), and pitcher Samantha Lincoln. The two-time defending national champions are adding to an already deep roster.

Texas Tech added the nation's top hitter by average, the No. 1 pitcher in the portal, and a 50-HR power bat with a national championship on her résumé.

Whatever the Big 12 schedule looks like in 2027, the Texas-Texas Tech series is going to sell out. The rematch the sport has been building toward since the 2026 WCWS championship game is now loaded with more talent than anyone anticipated when the season ended.

What's Next

Texas Tech's portal class is closed. The three All-Americans are signed. Now comes the integration work — spring practice, fall scrimmages, the sorting-out period that turns portal additions into team chemistry. That process is invisible from the outside, but it is where good coaches earn their money.

Glasco has built Texas Tech into a program that no longer feels like a challenger. They are a contender. The 2026 season proved it. This portal class makes the case that they intend to remain one — with three All-Americans, a No. 1 ranked recruiting class, and a very specific unfinished piece of business from last June in Oklahoma City.
