Tennessee Adds Karley Shelton, Mardjetko Returns — Lady Vols Reload for 2027

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | June 18, 2026

Not every program's portal story is about three All-Americans and a No. 1 national ranking. Tennessee's story this cycle is quieter — but no less meaningful for a program with WCWS ambitions of its own.

The Lady Vols have added South Carolina transfer Karley Shelton to their 2027 roster, filling an infield need with a proven SEC bat. And ace pitcher Sage Mardjetko has announced she is returning to Rocky Top for her senior season — the piece of news that perhaps matters most for Tennessee's 2027 ceiling.

Karley Shelton Brings Proven SEC Production

Shelton is a rising senior who played second base at South Carolina during the 2026 season. She batted .345 with 68 hits, 40 RBIs, and 108 total bases — the kind of season that quietly builds a reputation in the SEC, even when the Gamecocks were not among the conference's elite teams in 2026.

Shelton graduated early from Lexington High School in Lexington, South Carolina, to join the Gamecocks program. That early graduation and three seasons of SEC experience make her a player who has been competing at the highest level of college softball for multiple years. She arrives at Tennessee as someone with situational awareness, postseason familiarity from the conference level, and the production profile to compete for a starting spot from day one.

At second base, Shelton fills one of the positions where Tennessee needed to address its 2027 roster after the transfer portal took three of its own players: outfielder Saviya Morgan, pitcher Kailey Plumlee (who committed to Alabama), and infielder Ella Dodge. The departures created holes. Shelton is one answer.

Mardjetko Is Staying — and That Changes Everything

If Shelton is the addition that stabilizes, Mardjetko's return is the news that elevates.

The ace pitcher was a central figure in Tennessee's 2026 postseason run, delivering key performances that carried the Lady Vols to the WCWS semifinals. She has built a reputation as one of the most reliable arms in the SEC — the kind of pitcher a program can give the ball to in elimination games and trust completely.

Mardjetko's return for her senior season gives Tennessee a genuine No. 1 starter heading into 2027. For a program that now needs to rebuild around the arms it has remaining, keeping its best pitcher is not a minor news item. It is the foundation on which the next season gets built.

Tennessee also elevated staffers Megan Rhodes Smith and Aubrey Leach-Gartner within the coaching structure, signaling continuity in the program's direction even as the roster transitions around them.

The Bigger Picture for Tennessee

The Lady Vols reached the WCWS semifinals in 2026 — the deepest run in a postseason that featured an extraordinary collection of talent. They beat Texas in the bracket before the Longhorns ultimately won it all. Tennessee is not a program in need of a rebuild. They are a program in need of a restock, and the early returns from this portal cycle suggest they are doing it intelligently.

Shelton adds an experienced bat to the infield. Mardjetko stays, anchoring the pitching staff. The coaching structure is stable. The framework for another WCWS run exists, even if this cycle did not produce the headline-grabbing portal class that Texas and Texas Tech built.

Not every program wins the portal. The ones that survive it — and come out with their most important pieces intact — often compete just as well when the season begins.

What's Next

The portal window closes June 22. Tennessee may still add to its class in the days remaining, though the two major announcements — Shelton's commitment and Mardjetko's return — provide a foundation to evaluate.

For the Lady Vols, the 2027 season preview starts with one question: can Mardjetko be the pitcher who carries Tennessee back to Oklahoma City? If the answer is yes, the rest of the roster around her gives the Lady Vols a legitimate path to the championship series — and another shot at a Texas program that is stacking its own class on the other side of the bracket.
