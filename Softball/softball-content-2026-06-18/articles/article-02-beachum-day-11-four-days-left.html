Beachum Watch: Day 11 — The Last Elite Transfer Has Four Days Left

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | June 18, 2026

Everyone else has decided. Jaysoni Beachum has not.

The Florida State third baseman enters Day 11 of the 2026 NCAA softball transfer portal window without a commitment — the last elite name in a class that has already sent Isa Torres to Texas, Moriah Polar and Kasidi Pickering to Texas Tech, and a host of other standout players to programs across the country.

Four days remain before the window closes on June 22. Beachum, ranked No. 1 among all 2026 transfers by Softball America, holds the final card of this remarkable portal cycle.

The Player at the Center of the Waiting Game

There is a reason the softball world has been watching Beachum's decision with such intensity. The numbers are exceptional.

In 2026 at Florida State, Beachum posted a .417 batting average with 16 home runs, 66 RBIs, and a .549 on-base percentage. She earned NFCA Second-Team All-American honors at the end of the regular season. She was named a Florida High School All-American before ever arriving in Tallahassee and immediately delivered on that billing, winning 2024 NFCA Freshman of the Year honors after hitting .417 with 16 home runs and 66 RBIs in her debut season.

Over three seasons at FSU, she compiled a .375 batting average, 37 home runs, 180 RBIs, and 202 hits. That level of offensive production from a third baseman — someone who also has the profile to play other infield positions — is the kind of thing that makes roster decisions for coaching staffs. You build around players like Beachum.

Day 11: What the Waiting Means

Beachum entered the portal on June 2, the day the window opened. She has been uncommitted for 11 days as of today. The patience is notable — and deliberate.

When elite players hold this long, it generally means one of two things: either multiple programs have made compelling offers and the decision is genuinely difficult, or the player is waiting for a specific circumstance to fall into place. In Beachum's case, the field of programs pursuing her has never been publicly confirmed, which itself is unusual for a portal class this high-profile.

What has changed around her in the last 24 hours: Pickering is now officially gone to Texas Tech. Torres committed to Texas on June 16. Polar committed to Texas Tech days earlier. The class has played out without Beachum deciding — which suggests her deliberation is intentional and not a matter of options running out.

Who Could Land Her

No program has been publicly identified as the frontrunner for Beachum. That stands in contrast to Pickering, whose Texas Tech destination was telegraphed by her "Do Not Transfer" tag and social media activity well before she signed.

Programs that have not yet completed a significant portal addition — those below the Texas and Texas Tech tier of this cycle — have the most immediate incentive to make a compelling offer. Alabama, which landed pitcher Kailey Plumlee from Tennessee, could absorb Beachum into an offense built around its own returning talent. Florida, which sends a consistent number of players to the portal and recruits heavily in the FSU recruiting region, is a perpetual candidate.

Oklahoma, navigating a roster rebuild after losing Pickering and other key pieces, would benefit significantly. Texas A&M, which already added Maddy Azua, could pursue Beachum as a second elite bat in the portal class.

But the specifics remain unconfirmed. Beachum is holding her announcement close, and four days remain for whatever program earns her commitment to make its case.

What's Next

June 22 is the hard close of the 2026 portal window. After that date, players may not enter or exit the portal, and commitments made within the window become official. Four days is enough time for an announcement to come at any hour — a social media post, a program announcement, a photo in a new team's colors.

Beachum's decision will close this remarkable portal cycle. She is the last major story in a window that started June 8 and has reshaped the rosters of the sport's most powerful programs.

Where does the best uncommitted transfer in college softball go? The answer is coming — and it will be worth the wait.
