Megan Grant ESPYS: 3 Days to Vote — The Record Stood 31 Years, and Wednesday Night Is Hers

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 12, 2026

Laura Espinoza set the NCAA Division I single-season home run record at Arizona in 1995. Thirty-one years passed. Multiple generations of softball players came through the game — All-Americans, Olympians, WCWS champions — and none of them touched it.

Then Megan Grant hit home run No. 38 at UCLA in 2026. Then 39. Then 40. Then 41. Then 42.

The record stood for 31 years. Grant broke it by five.

On Wednesday, July 15, the 34th ESPYs air at 8:00 PM ET on ABC and the ESPN app. Grant is nominated for Best Record-Breaking Performance alongside Myles Garrett, Johannes Høsflot Klæbo, and Sabastian Sawe. You can vote once per day at ESPN.com. You have three days left.

The Record

The previous mark of 37 home runs belonged to Arizona outfielder Laura Espinoza, set in the 1995 season. Espinoza's record outlasted Title IX growth spurts, the development of aluminum bat technology, the explosion of elite programs, and three decades of the sport becoming more competitive at every level.

Grant did not sneak past it. She built toward it across an entire senior season at UCLA, reaching 42 home runs in 63 games — a pace of one every 1.5 games. She also finished as UCLA's all-time home run leader with 91 career home runs, surpassing Stacey Nuveman's long-standing program record.

The Professional Chapter

Grant did not stop after the NCAA record. She was selected No. 4 overall in the 2026 AUSL Draft by the Portland Cascade and made her professional debut in June. In her debut week, Grant hit the first home run in AUSL history that was officially measured for distance: 313 feet. That distance became the league's first-ever recorded home run record.

She was also one of two Cascade players voted into the AUSL All-Star Cup by fans — an Aug. 8–30 event at Parkway Bank Sports Complex in Rosemont, Illinois. The recognition has followed her from college to the professional game without a gap.

The Competition

The Best Record-Breaking Performance category at the 2026 ESPYs does not lack for legitimate nominees. Myles Garrett set his record on the football field. Johannes Høsflot Klæbo broke world cross-country skiing marks. Sabastian Sawe is one of the top long-distance runners in the world. These are not soft competitors.

What Grant brings that the others cannot is the 31-year weight of the record she broke. The category is about breaking records. Her record had lasted longer than any of the others before it fell.
