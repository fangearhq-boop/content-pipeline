Tia Milloy Commits to LSU — The Summer's Most-Watched Portal Saga Finds Its Answer

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 12, 2026

It started May 25, when Oklahoma utility player Tia Milloy entered the transfer portal following the Sooners' loss in the Norman Super Regional. It wound through weeks of speculation — FSU, Arizona State, Tennessee, Texas Tech, all mentioned at various points. It produced a "Forks Up" social media post that sent ASU fans into a frenzy.

And it ends in Baton Rouge.

Tia Milloy has committed to LSU, choosing the Tigers over a reported final two that included Florida State. One of the most-watched uncommitted players of the summer has found her landing spot.

What Milloy Brings to LSU

Milloy is a left-handed hitter with versatility across multiple positions — the kind of utility player whose value exceeds a simple box-score read. She appeared in 91 games over two seasons at Oklahoma, making 16 starts, but was used primarily as a pinch-runner and late-game option in 2026 conference play.

Her numbers, when given plate appearances, were productive. In 2026 she hit .353 with four home runs and 22 RBI in 34 at-bats. Career at Oklahoma: .295 average, 9 home runs, 35 RBI. She comes from Redmond, Washington, was a three-time all-state selection in high school, and has two full years of eligibility remaining.

LSU's coaching staff saw enough in that production to make her a priority. And with the roster freeze deadline hitting tomorrow at 11:59 PM PST, the timing of this commitment closes the door on a summer that had been defined by her uncertainty.

The Road Not Taken

Florida State was reportedly the other finalist — a program with real urgency after losing Jordyn Torres (.530 average, 78 runs, to Texas) and Kalei Beachum (.417 average, 16 HR, to Florida) in the transfer portal. FSU's Lonni Alameda is one of the most respected coaches in the game, and Tallahassee had legitimate reasons to pursue Milloy hard.

The "Forks Up" post on Milloy's social media account — a gesture associated with Arizona State's Sun Devil hand sign — briefly pointed speculation in a completely different direction. It turned out to be misdirection, or at minimum, a sign that was misread by a lot of people who were watching closely.

What it was not: a commitment to Florida State, or Arizona State, or anywhere except LSU.

The Portal Closes Tomorrow

The PGF Nationals roster freeze hits tomorrow at 11:59 PM PST — and while that's a travel softball deadline rather than a college portal deadline, the proximity is not coincidental. The summer's uncommitted players have been making decisions under the pressure of rosters locking and fall practice schedules approaching. Milloy's commitment, with the roster freeze less than 36 hours away, follows the pattern of a market that is reaching its natural close.

For the programs that missed out, the window for additions is narrowing. For LSU, it's open and occupied.

What's Next

Tia Milloy will join the LSU program ahead of the 2027 season with two years of eligibility remaining. The PGF Nationals roster freeze hits July 13 at 11:59 PM PST. The transfer portal remains open through July 31. The Class of 2028 official contact window opens September 1, 2026.
