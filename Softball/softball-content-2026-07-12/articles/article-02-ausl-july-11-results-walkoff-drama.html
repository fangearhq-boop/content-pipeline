AUSL Saturday Results: Bandits Dominate Cascade, Spark Walk Off Defending Champion Talons

By Emily Rawlings | I Love Softball | July 12, 2026

Saturday's AUSL triple-header delivered something for every kind of fan: a dominant run-rule statement, a nail-biting one-run grind, and a walk-off that dropped the defending champions.

Three games. Three different endings. And with eight regular-season games remaining before the July 20 finale, none of them were throwaway.

Game 1: Texas Volts 4, Carolina Blaze 3

The afternoon opener at Dell Diamond in Round Rock, Texas, belonged to the teams that play there all summer — the Texas Volts and Carolina Blaze went back and forth in a game defined not by offensive explosions but by what did not happen. The Blaze out-hit the Volts 7-4 on the afternoon. They generated traffic. They put runners on base. And they could not push them across the plate.

The Volts held on for a 4-3 win — the kind of defensive, pitching-first victory that feels unsatisfying in the moment and important in the standings. Every game at this stage of the AUSL regular season matters, and Texas took one without their best offensive effort.

Game 2: Chicago Bandits 11, Portland Cascade 3 (Run Rule)

The 5:00 PM ET game at Hillsboro Ballpark in Hillsboro, Oregon was not close for long. Morgan Zerkle put Chicago in front early with a two-run triple in the first inning, setting a physical and psychological tone for the afternoon.

The Bandits kept building. Sami Williams capped a four-run third inning with a three-run home run, pushing the lead to 9-2 and ending the competitive portion of the game. The Bandits finished the Cascade via run rule in the fifth inning, 11-3.

Portland's Sydney Stewart hit her first professional home run in the loss — a moment worth noting even in a blowout. For a player making the transition from college ball to the professional game, the first professional homer is its own milestone regardless of the score.

But the game belonged to Chicago. The Bandits looked like a different team than the one playing before the AUSL break, and the margin of their win — 8 runs in a run-rule game — sent a message to the rest of the league.

Game 3: Oklahoma City Spark 4, Utah Talons 3 (Walk-Off)

The final game of the triple-header, starting at 8:00 PM ET at Tom Heath Field in Oklahoma City, was the one that will be remembered.

The Utah Talons — the defending AUSL champions, winners of the inaugural 2025 Championship Series — came into OKC having already dealt with the Lonni Alameda broadcast buzz from Talons-Spark Friday night. Saturday, they came back with a chance to even the short series.

It went to the bottom of the seventh inning tied at 3. And then Sydney Sherrill stepped to the plate and ended it. Her RBI double drove in the winning run and sent the OKC crowd home with a walk-off victory, 4-3.

The defending champions fell. In walk-off fashion. On the road. In back-to-back games against the Spark.

Tonight: Rematch at Tom Heath Field

The Spark and Talons do it again tonight at 8:00 PM ET at Tom Heath Field. The defending champions are now 0-for-2 in this weekend series against OKC. If the Talons are going to protect their bid for the No. 1 seed — which earns a direct bye to the Championship Series — they need a response tonight.

Can the Talons answer? Or does Oklahoma City make it three straight over the champions?
