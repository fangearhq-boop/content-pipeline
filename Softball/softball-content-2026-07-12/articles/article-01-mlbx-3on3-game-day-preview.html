MLBx All-Star 3-On-3 Is TODAY — The Celebrity Softball Game Is Gone, and What Replaces It Is Better

By Sarah Mitchell | I Love Softball | July 12, 2026

For 25 years, the Celebrity Softball Game was a staple of MLB All-Star Week. It ran from 2001 through 2025, and it gave fans a chance to see entertainers swing bats and mug for the cameras in a loosely organized softball game.

That era ended. And what replaced it starts today at 3:00 PM ET on Peacock.

The inaugural MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 Presented by Ito En takes the field this afternoon at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia — and for the first time in All-Star Week history, four professional softball players are competing in a co-ed power event broadcast nationally. NiJaree Canady. Alyssa Brito. Rachel Garcia. Tiare Jennings. Four of the most accomplished players in the AUSL's inaugural class, on the same field as Philadelphia Phillies legends and NFL stars, in front of a national audience.

The Teams and the Matchups

Team Howard is built around Hall of Famer Ryan Howard, the 2006 NL MVP who hit 58 home runs in his peak season and remains one of the most powerful left-handed hitters in Phillies history. His AUSL partner is Alyssa Brito of the Carolina Blaze — a three-time WCWS champion from Oklahoma and a member of the U.S. Women's National Team. Eagles offensive tackle Jordan Mailata completes the trio.

Team Rollins features Jimmy Rollins, the 2007 NL MVP and four-time World Series champion (2008, multiple NLCS appearances). Rollins partners with Tiare Jennings of the Texas Volts, a four-time WCWS champion whose offensive production at Oklahoma is among the best in program history. Terrell Owens — the Hall of Fame wide receiver and one of the most dominant pass catchers in NFL history — rounds out the team.

Team Victorino pairs Shane Victorino, the "Flyin' Hawaiian" and 2008 World Series champion, with Rachel Garcia of the Texas Volts. Garcia brings one of the most decorated resumes in amateur softball: an Olympic silver medal from Tokyo 2020 and a 2019 WCWS title from UCLA. Eagles wide receiver Eladio Carrion joins as the NFL representative.

Team Jones is the story of this event. Andruw Jones — the former Braves centerfielder and 10-time Gold Glove Award winner — was inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame in Cooperstown just this week. He competes today alongside NiJaree Canady of the Texas Volts, the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 AUSL Draft. Antonio Cipriano completes Team Jones as the third member.

How the Format Works

MLBx runs two 30-minute semifinal games followed by a championship game. The bracket: Team Howard faces Team Rollins in the first semifinal; Team Victorino faces Team Jones in the second. The two winners advance to the championship.

Each player gets a two-minute batting window per game. Home run equals one point. Catching a ball in the outfield also equals one point. There is a center-field target for bonus points, and each team has access to a Hot Streak power-up — a button that can be pressed once per game to grant five additional swings at double points. Pressing the button at the right moment can flip a game entirely.

The format is genuinely co-ed by design. A 450-foot blast from Ryan Howard and a line-drive homer from Alyssa Brito are worth exactly the same point. An outfield catch by DeVonta Smith counts the same as one from Tiare Jennings. The competition doesn't separate by gender — it competes.

Why This Matters for Softball

The shift from Celebrity Softball to MLBx 3-on-3 is not cosmetic. The Celebrity Softball Game put entertainers — actors, musicians, social media personalities — at the center of the broadcast. The AUSL stars today are not celebrity guest appearances. They are the competitive heart of the event.

Canady, Brito, Garcia, and Jennings are four of the most talented professional softball players on earth right now. They are competing at Citizens Bank Park on Peacock during the most-watched week of the MLB calendar. Every viewer who tunes in for Ryan Howard or Terrell Owens and stays to watch NiJaree Canady step into the box is a new AUSL fan who would not have found the sport otherwise.

That reach is the point. The Celebrity Softball Game was for the celebrity. MLBx 3-on-3 is for the sport.

What's Next

MLBx: All-Star 3-on-3 begins today at 3:00 PM ET at Citizens Bank Park on Peacock. After today's event, the AUSL regular season resumes Monday with eight games remaining before the July 20 regular-season finale. The No. 1 seed earns a direct bye to the Championship Series, set for July 25–27 at Davis Diamond in College Station, Texas. The play-in game is July 23.
