### STORY 1: Earleywine Leaves Texas for Tennessee

**FB Long-Form Post**
Window: 9:00 AM ET

Texas just lost the offensive mind behind its back-to-back national championships — and Tennessee just gained him.

Ehren Earleywine, who served as Texas's associate head coach for offense in 2026, is departing for Tennessee, where he'll join Kim Weekly's staff as associate head coach. It's the second major coaching departure from Austin in less than a week, following the exit of associate head coach Kristen Zaleski.

What Earleywine built in his one season at Texas was remarkable. The Longhorns hit a program-record 99 home runs in 2026 — a number that would have been eye-popping in any era. Katie Stewart, who led the team's offense, set a program record with 31 home runs while earning SEC Player of the Year honors. That offense was the engine behind Texas's second straight national championship.

Now that engine has a new home.

For Tennessee, this is a significant acquisition. The Lady Vols are in full rebuild mode after losing key pieces — starting pitcher Madison Morgan and outfielder Brooke Plumlee both entered the transfer portal. Head coach Kim Weekly is piecing together her staff and her roster at the same time, and bringing in an offensive coordinator who just engineered the most explosive offense in college softball sends a clear message: Tennessee is playing the long game, and she's playing to win.

For Texas, the question becomes: who builds the offense next? Head coach Mike White retains his core returning pieces — Teagan Kavan and Katie Stewart are both expected back — but the coaching continuity that helped Texas dominate now has a gap to fill. Three-peat bids require stability. Right now, Texas is in transition.

The 15-day transfer portal window runs through June 22. Programs are simultaneously rebuilding rosters and staffs. This offseason is moving fast — and the changes at the top matter.

### STORY 2: Torres Portal — Day 3

**FB Long-Form Post**
Window: 11:00 AM ET

Three days into the transfer portal window. The best player available in college softball still hasn't committed. And the programs chasing Isa Torres are only getting more serious.

Torres, the D1 Softball Player of the Year, entered the portal on June 8 after a season that can only be described as historic. She hit .530 at Florida State — a program record. Her .591 on-base percentage and .978 slugging percentage were also school records. She scored 78 runs and was named the ACC Player of the Year. She reportedly set an NCAA record with a hit in 16 consecutive at-bats.

And now she's available.

Texas and Texas A&M are the programs most frequently linked to Torres, who has one year of eligibility remaining. Both programs have the resources, the culture, and the ambition to win a national championship in 2027. For Texas, adding Torres would fill a power slot in a lineup that's already dangerous. For Texas A&M, landing Torres would be a program-defining acquisition — the kind of commitment that signals a genuine title contender.

The 15-day portal window opened June 8 and runs through June 22. Torres doesn't need to rush — and neither do the programs pursuing her. These decisions take time. Visits need to happen. Conversations need to happen. Financial aid packages get worked out.

But make no mistake: wherever Torres lands will be among the favorites to cut down the net in Oklahoma City in 2027. This is the biggest recruiting story in college softball right now, and it's just getting started.

### STORY 3: Swan Visits Texas

**FB Long-Form Post**
Window: 1:00 PM ET

The back-to-back national champions are wasting no time in the transfer portal.

Arizona State graduate transfer Samantha Swan visited Texas on Monday — and the fit looks good on every level.

Swan just completed a first-team All-Big 12 season at Arizona State, hitting .383 with 21 doubles, 12 home runs, 55 RBIs, and a .984 fielding percentage behind the plate. She owns a career .322 batting average and a .917 OPS across four seasons with the Sun Devils. She's a complete player — one of the best catchers available in the portal class.

And she's from Georgetown, Texas. A suburb of Austin.

For Texas, the timing works. Starting catcher Reese Atwood graduated in 2026, leaving a hole behind the plate that the Longhorns need to fill before the 2027 season. Swan is exactly the kind of player Mike White builds around — experienced, versatile, defensively sound, capable of making a lineup uncomfortable from the two-hole or the five-hole.

A visit isn't a commitment. But a visit is a first step. And the fact that it happened on the first Monday of the portal window tells you something about the level of mutual interest.

Texas is already navigating two coaching departures this week. Landing Swan would be the first concrete win of the portal cycle — and a signal that the dynasty machine is still operating at full speed even in transition.

Moriah Polar entered the portal on June 8 after a 2026 season that made her one of the most talked-about players in college softball. This is not a project. This is not a gamble. This is a proven, decorated hitter who was the best at doing what hitters do in all of Division I softball in 2026. You don't find those players very often. You don't often find them in the portal. When you do, you move fast.

And yet, three days into the window, Polar has not announced a commitment.

The reasons could be many. Programs are in the process of figuring out roster space, scholarship availability, and roster fit. Polar herself may be in the process of visiting and evaluating. But the clock is running.

The portal window closes June 22. Twelve days remain. Wherever Polar commits, that program just upgraded significantly.

### STORY 5: Coaching Carousel

**FB Long-Form Post**
Window: 3:00 PM ET

The transfer portal window opened June 8. The coaching carousel has been spinning since the WCWS ended. Both are happening simultaneously — and for programs building toward 2027, that combination defines everything.

Here's where things stand on the coaching side:

**Tennessee** grabbed the biggest prize, hiring Ehren Earleywine from Texas as associate head coach. He engineered Texas's record-setting 99-HR offense. Kim Weekly is building something.

**Colorado State** hired Michelle Gascoigne as its new head coach on June 8. Gascoigne spent 11 seasons as Northwestern's pitching coach and brings both coaching depth and championship pedigree to Fort Collins.

**Tulsa** hired DJ Gasso — son of OU legend Patty Gasso and most recently Arkansas's associate head coach — as its new head coach.

**Nebraska** added four-time first-team NFCA All-American Jordy Frahm as an assistant, continuing to build the Cornhuskers' staff as they pursue a national title of their own.

The carousel is always busy in the offseason. But this year, it feels especially consequential — because the biggest programs are simultaneously managing roster turnover through the portal window AND coaching staff turnover. Programs that nail both the roster side and the coaching side in the next 12 days will be the stories of the 2027 preseason.
