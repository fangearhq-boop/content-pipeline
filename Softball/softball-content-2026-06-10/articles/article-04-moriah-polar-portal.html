Moriah Polar Is Still Available — Nation's Leading Hitter Waits for the Right Fit

By Sarah Mitchell | June 10, 2026 | I Love Softball

There is a player in the transfer portal right now who led Division I softball in batting average and hits in 2026. She is a second-team All-American. She was among the 25 finalists for USA Softball Player of the Year. She has one year of eligibility remaining. And as of Wednesday, she still has not committed.

Moriah Polar, the Purdue outfielder who entered the portal on June 8, is one of the most impactful available players in this transfer portal class. Her final regular-season numbers were not close. A .555 batting average. 101 hits. Those are numbers that define a season, define a career, and define a player's value on the open market.

What Polar Brings to Any Roster

Polar's skillset is the kind that is immediately translatable. She is not a project. She is not a player whose numbers inflated because of a weak schedule or a favorable conference. She was the best hitter in the country, full stop, by the most direct measurement available.

For any program with national title aspirations, adding Polar to the lineup means adding one of the most reliable offensive threats in the sport. She drives up pitch counts, she gets on base, she puts pressure on opposing pitchers, and she does so consistently. The programs that win national championships are built around players who perform at exactly this level.

One year of eligibility remaining means any program that signs Polar is signing her for one season. But one elite season can be the difference between a good team and a great one. It can be the difference between a regional exit and a trip to Oklahoma City. The value proposition is clear.

Why the Decision Takes Time

The transfer portal process is not instantaneous. Programs need to evaluate whether they have roster space and scholarship availability. Polar needs to evaluate fit — playing time, lineup role, coaching staff, proximity to home, and the program's realistic championship contention level. Those conversations take days, not hours.

The portal window runs through June 22. There is no rush to commit in the first week if the right program hasn't emerged yet. Polar entering the portal on Day 1 signaled that she was ready to move on from Purdue. But moving on from Purdue does not mean taking the first offer that arrives. It means finding the right fit.

The programs that have the best combination of roster need, playing time to offer, facilities to show, and championship trajectory to sell are the ones that will be at the front of Polar's list. That narrows the field considerably — and suggests this could move fast once the right visits happen.

What's Next

Watch for visit announcements in the coming days. Once Polar begins taking visits, a decision typically follows quickly. The program that lands her will have made one of the best moves of the transfer portal cycle — adding the nation's leading hitter to a lineup that will become an immediate challenger for the 2027 national title. It is simply a matter of which program makes the right pitch.
