Coaching Carousel in Full Spin — Key Hires Shape the 2027 Landscape

By Emily Rawlings | June 10, 2026 | I Love Softball

The transfer portal window opened June 8 and will run through June 22. Parallel to that 15-day sprint, the coaching carousel is turning at full speed. Programs across Division I softball are simultaneously rebuilding rosters and staffs, and the moves made in the next two weeks will define the 2027 landscape as much as anything that happens during the season.

This week's most notable move: Tennessee head coach Kim Weekly hired Ehren Earleywine from Texas as associate head coach. Earleywine was the offensive coordinator who helped the Longhorns hit a program-record 99 home runs and win back-to-back national championships. It is a significant acquisition for a program with clear ambitions — and it is the most prominent example of a season-ending shakeup that has been felt at several programs simultaneously.

New Head Coaches: Gascoigne, Gasso, and More

On the head coaching side, Colorado State made its hire official on June 8, naming Michelle Gascoigne as the program's 13th head coach. Gascoigne spent 11 seasons as Northwestern's pitching coach and arrives with both substantial coaching experience and a championship background as a former collegiate player. It is an experienced hire for a program looking to climb in its conference.

Tulsa made waves by hiring DJ Gasso as head coach. Gasso was most recently Arkansas's associate head coach and comes with a pedigree that includes perhaps the most accomplished last name in college softball. His mother, Patty Gasso, built Oklahoma into one of the sport's all-time great programs. Now he gets his own stage at Tulsa, where expectations will be measured against that lineage whether he courts the comparison or not.

Staff Hires That Will Matter

Nebraska added a notable piece to its staff on June 5, hiring four-time first-team NFCA All-American Jordy Frahm as an assistant coach. Frahm's playing accomplishments speak for themselves, and Nebraska has been steadily building toward a national title run. Staff hires of this caliber tend to pay dividends in recruiting and player development.

Illinois added Yolanda McRae as an assistant coach on June 6, continuing a pattern of deliberate staff construction that program-builders pursue in the offseason. While these moves don't generate the same headlines as portal signings, they are often the quieter transactions that determine a program's ceiling.

The Texas Coaching Situation

The most scrutinized coaching story of the week remains at Texas, where head coach Mike White has lost both associate head coach Kristen Zaleski and offensive coordinator Ehren Earleywine within a matter of days. Texas enters the portal window managing dual openings at the top of its staff while the sport's best available players are making decisions about where to spend their final seasons.

White has built Texas into a two-time champion, and his recruiting networks and program infrastructure remain elite. But the offseason has been more turbulent than anyone in Austin expected. The replacements he identifies for Zaleski and Earleywine will be the first major test of whether the dynasty can sustain itself through turnover.

What's Next

The carousel will slow down as the portal window approaches its June 22 close. But before then, expect more announcements on both fronts — head coach hires for remaining vacancies and staff additions at programs trying to finalize their 2027 structures. The programs that get both sides right will enter next season with meaningful advantages. The ones that don't will be playing catch-up.
