Isa Torres Still Uncommitted — The Race for the Portal's No. 1 Player Intensifies

By Emily Rawlings | June 10, 2026 | I Love Softball

Three days into the 15-day undergraduate transfer portal window and the most consequential recruiting decision in college softball remains unresolved. Isa Torres — the D1 Softball Player of the Year, the ACC Player of the Year, and the holder of four Florida State program records — has not yet announced where she will spend her final year of eligibility.

Torres entered the portal on June 8, the first day the undergraduate window opened following the Women's College World Series. She was immediately considered the top available player in the class. Nothing that has happened in the three days since has changed that assessment. Texas and Texas A&M remain the programs most frequently connected to her by reports, but no program has yet landed a commitment from the player who had the best offensive season in Seminoles history.

What Torres Brings

To understand why this matters so much, consider the numbers. Torres hit .530 at Florida State in 2026 — a program record. Her .591 on-base percentage and .978 slugging percentage were also school records. She scored 78 runs, also a program record. She earned the top individual award in Division I softball and was named her conference's best player. She has one year of eligibility remaining.

Those aren't numbers from a good year. Those are numbers from one of the best offensive seasons anyone has had in recent memory. A player who performs at that level — in any sport, at any position — is a rare commodity. The transfer portal opening gives programs a chance to acquire exactly that kind of talent. No one has stepped up with a deal that has closed yet.

The Programs in Pursuit

Texas and Texas A&M have both been connected to Torres by multiple reports. For Texas, adding Torres would slot an elite hitter into a lineup that already features returning stars Teagan Kavan and Katie Stewart. The Longhorns are also navigating the departure of two key coaches this week, which creates some uncertainty — but Texas's track record of winning and its facilities make it a compelling destination for any player who wants to contend for a national title.

For Texas A&M, this would be a program-defining acquisition. The Aggies have been building steadily and have the resources and staff to be a serious contender. Landing Torres would vault them into the top tier of 2027 title conversations immediately.

Neither program has made a public announcement. The process takes time — visits need to be scheduled and completed, conversations need to happen, scholarship arrangements need to be finalized. The portal window does not close until June 22, which means Torres and the programs pursuing her have nearly two weeks to work through that process.

What's Next

This story is moving. Torres is a once-in-a-portal player, and the programs that recognize that are moving fast. Expect visits to be completed in the coming days and a potential commitment to follow shortly after. When it happens, it will be one of the biggest recruiting announcements in recent college softball history — and it will immediately reset the 2027 national title odds. Watch this space.
