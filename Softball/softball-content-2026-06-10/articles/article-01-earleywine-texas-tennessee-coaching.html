Texas Loses Earleywine to Tennessee — Dynasty's Coaching Staff Faces Overhaul

By Sarah Mitchell | June 10, 2026 | I Love Softball

The back-to-back national championship coaching staff that built the most explosive offense in college softball history is coming apart faster than anyone expected. Ehren Earleywine, Texas's associate head coach for offense, is departing for Tennessee — the second key staff member to leave Austin in less than a week.

Earleywine was the architect behind one of the most remarkable offensive seasons in college softball history. In his one year with the Longhorns, Texas hit a program-record 99 home runs. Junior Katie Stewart set a school record with 31 home runs and earned SEC Player of the Year honors. The Longhorns rode that offense to their second consecutive national championship, capping a dynasty run that has made Texas the undisputed standard-bearer of the sport.

Now Earleywine is taking that expertise to Knoxville, where Tennessee head coach Kim Weekly has hired him as associate head coach. Weekly is overseeing a significant rebuild — the Lady Vols lost key pieces to the transfer portal after a disappointing early exit from the 2026 postseason — and adding an offensive coordinator who just engineered the most powerful lineup in Division I sends a clear message about her ambitions.

Two Coaches Gone in One Week

Earleywine's departure follows the exit of associate head coach Kristen Zaleski, who also will not return to Texas's coaching staff for 2027. Together, the two departures represent a significant shakeup for a program that has been almost unstoppably dominant.

The Longhorns still have Teagan Kavan and Katie Stewart — two of the most decorated players in college softball — expected to return for 2027. Head coach Mike White built this program into a dynasty, and the infrastructure, recruiting pipeline, and facilities remain formidable. But coaching continuity matters. The staff that won back-to-back titles will look very different next season, and the person responsible for turning Texas into the hardest-hitting team in the country just walked out the door.

White now faces the task of replacing two coaches while simultaneously managing one of the most active transfer portal windows in recent memory. It is a significant challenge — even for a program of Texas's stature.

What Earleywine Brings to Tennessee

From a Tennessee perspective, this is a genuine coup. Earleywine arrives with an unimpeachable résumé: one season, one national championship, 99 home runs, a Player of the Year, and a program-record-setting hitter. He didn't just coach an offense — he transformed one. Texas's 2026 lineup was the kind teams nightmare about facing in the postseason, and Earleywine was at the center of it.

Tennessee has talent. Weekly has shown an ability to recruit and develop players. But the Lady Vols have struggled to reach the level of consistency that programs like Texas and Alabama have demonstrated. Adding an elite offensive mind changes the calculus in ways that can't be overstated. If Earleywine can help Tennessee's hitters develop the way he helped Stewart develop, the Lady Vols become a legitimate national title contender in 2027.

The coaching carousel is moving fast this offseason — Earleywine to Tennessee is the most significant move yet.

What's Next

All eyes are now on Mike White and who he brings in to replace both Earleywine and Zaleski. Texas's ability to retain or upgrade those roles will go a long way toward determining whether the Longhorns can make history with a three-peat in 2027. Meanwhile, Tennessee enters the fall with a meaningful new weapon — and a clear signal that Kim Weekly is done waiting for her program's moment.
