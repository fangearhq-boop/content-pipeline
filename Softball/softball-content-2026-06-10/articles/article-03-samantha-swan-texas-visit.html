Samantha Swan Visits Texas — All-Big 12 Catcher Could Fill a Key Void for the Champions

By Jordan Reeves | June 10, 2026 | I Love Softball

The back-to-back national champions are not waiting around in the transfer portal. Arizona State graduate transfer Samantha Swan visited Texas on Monday, according to reports, and the fit on every conceivable level is hard to ignore.

Swan is a first-team All-Big 12 catcher from Georgetown, Texas — a suburb of Austin — who just completed a standout 2026 season with the Sun Devils. She hit .383 with 21 doubles, 12 home runs, and 55 RBIs. Her .984 fielding percentage behind the plate speaks to her defensive reliability. She owns a career .322 batting average and a .917 OPS across four seasons at Arizona State. If there is a better available catcher in this portal class, it would be difficult to find one.

Texas Has a Clear Need

The Longhorns graduated starting catcher Reese Atwood in 2026. Atwood was an important piece of a lineup that hit 99 home runs and won a national championship, and replacing a catcher of that caliber requires targeting the best available option. Swan is exactly that.

The catcher position is one of the most difficult to fill through the portal because so few truly elite catchers become available. Swan's combination of offensive production and defensive excellence at an elite level makes her one of the more valuable players in the entire portal class — not just the most valuable catcher available.

Texas head coach Mike White is already navigating the departures of two key coaches this week. Landing Swan quickly would be the program's first meaningful portal win of the cycle — a signal that the dynasty continues to operate efficiently even during periods of transition.

The Homecoming Element

Swan's roots in Georgetown, Texas add a layer to this story that goes beyond roster math. She grew up near Austin, played her college career in Arizona, and now has the opportunity to finish it on one of the biggest stages in college softball — close to home, competing for another national title.

For a program like Texas, proximity to home is often a compelling recruiting factor. Players who already have ties to the area tend to transition smoothly. Swan would not be starting over in a new city; she would be returning to familiar ground, to family, to friends, to a community she knows.

A visit is not a commitment, and Swan is expected to weigh all of her options carefully before making a decision. But the combination of fit, need, opportunity, and geography makes this one of the more natural portal pairings of the early window.

What's Next

Swan still has other programs to consider, and the portal window runs through June 22. But Texas moved quickly to get her on campus, which tells you something. If the Longhorns can close this, they will have answered one of the most pressing roster questions of their offseason before the first week is out. An announcement could come as early as this week.
