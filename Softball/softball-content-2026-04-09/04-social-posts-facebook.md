```
Kendall Wells just made history — and she's not done yet.

The Oklahoma freshman slugger hit her 30th home run of the season on April 4 in a run-rule sweep of Kentucky, tying the NCAA single-season freshman home run record. She joins Jocelyn Alo (Oklahoma, 2018), Lauren Chamberlain (Oklahoma, 2012), and Kelly Majam (Hawaii, 2010) as the only freshmen in DI history to reach that mark.

What makes Wells' achievement even more remarkable is how fast she got there. She tied the record in just 40 games — 22 fewer games than any other player in Division I history. That pace is almost unimaginable for any level of college softball, let alone for a first-year player in the SEC.

Wells already set the SEC single-season home run record earlier this season (March 31 vs. Wichita State, HR No. 27). Now she has her eyes on an even bigger milestone: Jocelyn Alo's all-time NCAA single-season home run record of 37. With at least 25 or more regular-season games remaining, Wells needs just seven more to reach it.

This weekend, the Texas pitching staff gets to test her. The stakes don't get any higher — and Wells seems to thrive in exactly those moments.
```
