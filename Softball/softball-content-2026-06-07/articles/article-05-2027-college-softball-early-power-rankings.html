2027 Early Power Rankings: Texas Is No. 1, But Who Has the Best Shot to Dethrone the Longhorns?

By Emily Rawlings June 7, 2026

The Race for 2027 Starts Now

The confetti has settled. The champion has been crowned. And the 2027 Women's College World Series — still eleven months away — is already shaping up to have a clear frontrunner and a compelling field of challengers.

These early power rankings are based on returning personnel, coaching stability, program trajectory, and what we know about the transfer portal cycle that begins Monday. They will change as the offseason unfolds. But right now, this is where the race for Devon Park in May 2027 stands.

No. 1 — Texas Longhorns

This is not a close call. Teagan Kavan and Katie Stewart are both returning juniors heading into their senior seasons in 2027. Kavan is the two-time WCWS Most Outstanding Player — the first player in championship history to win the award twice. Stewart is the SEC Player of the Year and the first player in WCWS history to hit home runs in four consecutive games.

Texas is the defending back-to-back champion. Head coach Mike White has the program operating at a standard that produces national titles. The Longhorns are one of only five programs in history to win consecutive WCWS titles — and they return the two most important players from both of those championship runs.

For someone to beat Texas in 2027, they will need to be extraordinary. That is not impossible. But it is the standard that everyone else must chase.

No. 2 — Alabama Crimson Tide

Jocelyn Briski is a junior returning for her senior season. Patrick Murphy won Coach of the Year in 2026 after guiding Alabama to the No. 1 overall seed. The Crimson Tide have been to the WCWS in 16 of the last 17 seasons. Whatever Murphy rebuilds around Briski will be dangerous.

Alabama lost players to graduation and will lose others to the portal, but the program's foundation is strong enough to absorb those departures. They were one missed assignment away from the championship series in 2026. They will be back.

No. 3 — Texas Tech Red Raiders

Losing NiJaree Canady is significant. She was the best pitcher in program history — a 1,000-strikeout, 96-win generational talent. There is no replacing her. But Kaitlyn Terry was a first-team NFCA All-American and Mia Williams was second-team. Head coach Gerry Glasco built a program capable of reaching the championship series with Canady. He will build again without her.

Texas Tech's 2026 run to the championship series showed that this program belongs at the top of college softball. The Red Raiders are not done being a national contender. They're just starting a new chapter.

No. 4 — Nebraska Cornhuskers

Jordy Frahm is gone — and the two-way standard she set will not be replaced by any single player. But Nebraska made its first WCWS appearance in 13 years in 2026, and that is not a fluke. Head coach Rhonda Revelle has been building toward this moment for years and the program now has the experience of competing at Devon Park. Programs that learn how to win at the WCWS often come back with better performances the second time.

No. 5 — Oklahoma Sooners

Oklahoma missed the 2026 WCWS for the first time since 2015 — the first absence in a run that had included four consecutive national championships from 2021 to 2024. It is impossible to know exactly what went wrong in 2026, but one thing is certain: Patty Gasso is still coaching, the program has every resource it needs, and the Sooners will treat 2027 as a redemption year. Bounce-back bids from powerhouse programs tend to be uncomfortable for the rest of the field.

What's Next

These rankings will shift dramatically once the transfer portal window closes on June 22. A program that lands Jaysoni Beachum or Tori Edwards could jump significantly. A program that loses key players could fall. The 2027 race is already being run — in coaches' offices, in recruiting calls, and in the decisions players are making right now about where they want to spend their final years of eligibility. The next three months will tell us a great deal about who is ready to challenge Texas.
