The Post-WCWS Coaching Carousel: Firings, Retirements and Programs in Transition

By Sarah Mitchell June 7, 2026

The Offseason Moves Have Already Begun

The 2026 Women's College World Series champion has been crowned. The trophy is in Austin. And within days of the final out, the college softball coaching carousel was already spinning.

The period immediately following the championship is when athletic departments make decisions they've been weighing for months. Programs that had disappointing seasons move on from coaches. Coaches who have given decades to the game decide it is time to step away. And the combination of new openings and the transfer portal window creates the busiest stretch in the college softball offseason calendar.

Illinois Parts Ways with Tyra Perry

After 11 years at the helm, Tyra Perry and Illinois have parted ways. The 2026 season was a difficult one for the Illini — a 15-39 overall record and a 3-20 mark in Big Ten play. Perry spent more than a decade building the program and had stretches of real success, but this past season made a change inevitable. Illinois now searches for a coach who can restore the program's competitive standing in the Big Ten.

Minnesota Relieves Piper Ritter

Minnesota made a similar decision, relieving head coach Piper Ritter following a 16-36 season. The Gophers struggled throughout the 2026 campaign and the athletic department determined that a new voice was necessary to move the program forward. Like Illinois, Minnesota is now a head coaching opening in the Big Ten — a conference that is increasingly competitive and increasingly demanding at the coaching level.

Towson's Lisa Costello Steps Away After 33 Seasons

Not every departure is a firing. At Towson, the news is a retirement — and a significant one. Lisa Costello is stepping away after 33 seasons as the program's head coach, effective July 8. Her career record of 857-773-3 represents one of the most enduring coaching careers in college softball. Building a program over three decades, navigating the evolution of the sport, and stepping away having won more games than you lost — that is a remarkable legacy.

Towson now searches for its first new head coach in a generation.

Other Departures Across the Country

Morehead State head coach Megan Griffith resigned, effective June 10. Youngstown State's Brian Campbell retired after 18 seasons and 435 career wins — he spent 30 total years coaching the game. Longwood announced that Megan Brown will not return after six seasons.

The coaching market typically heats up this time of year, but the simultaneous arrival of the transfer portal window makes the next few weeks particularly intense. Prospective coaching candidates are evaluating programs while those same programs are also trying to navigate roster management in the portal. There are no easy weeks in this business.

What's Next

The programs with openings will move quickly. Coaching searches conducted in June and July allow new head coaches to be in place before the fall practice calendar begins, giving them the best possible chance to manage the transfer portal cycle and begin building toward 2027. The names that emerge as candidates for Illinois, Minnesota, Towson, and the other programs in transition will be among the most closely watched stories of the college softball offseason.
