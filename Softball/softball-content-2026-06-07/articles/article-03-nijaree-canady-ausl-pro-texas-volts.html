NiJaree Canady Turns Pro: The AUSL No. 2 Pick Brings Her 1,000-Strikeout Career to the Texas Volts

By Jordan Reeves June 7, 2026

The Next Chapter Begins

Her college career is over. Her professional career is about to begin. And anyone who watched NiJaree Canady pitch over the last four years at Texas Tech has every reason to be excited about what comes next.

Canady was selected No. 2 overall in the 2026 AUSL College Draft by the Texas Volts. The Athletes Unlimited Softball League, the premier professional softball league in the United States, adds one of the most accomplished college pitchers of her era to its roster. The career numbers Canady produced at Texas Tech were unlike anything the college game had seen from an active pitcher: more than 1,000 career strikeouts and 96 wins.

A Career That Defined an Era

Canady arrived in Lubbock from Topeka, Kansas and immediately announced herself as one of the best pitchers in the country. She was a consistent first-team All-American in each of her final three seasons at Texas Tech. In each of her four years, she finished in the top 10 nationally in ERA. Her combination of movement, command, and competitiveness made her a nightmare for opposing lineups at every level of competition.

The 1,000-strikeout mark is genuinely rare. The 96-win mark is rarer. Canady reached both simultaneously — something no other active college pitcher had accomplished. She was selected to represent the college game as the recipient of the AUSL's first-ever golden ticket, a distinction presented to her on March 27 by AUSL commissioner Kim Ng and Patrick Mahomes.

A Pioneer in the NIL Era

Canady was also a pioneer off the field. Before her college career ended, she signed a first-of-its-kind NIL deal with the AUSL that included profit participation units — giving her an equity stake in the league's future growth. No college player had previously negotiated that kind of arrangement. It reflected the AUSL's belief that Canady represented something bigger than just a great pitcher. She represented the kind of player who could grow the sport at the professional level.

What Happened in Her Final WCWS

Canady's final college appearance came in the 2026 Women's College World Series, where she competed through the championship series against Texas. Her performance was outstanding throughout the tournament — she was on the WCWS All-Tournament Team. Texas was simply better at the very end, and that was not a reflection of anything Canady failed to do. It was a reflection of how extraordinary the Longhorns were.

She leaves Texas Tech with a career that will be remembered as one of the best individual pitching runs in college softball history. The Red Raiders had four NFCA All-Americans in 2026 — and Canady was the engine that made Texas Tech a national championship contender every year she was in Lubbock.

What's Next

As a Texas Volt, Canady will compete against the best professional softball players in the world. The AUSL features elite competition, and the draft positioning suggests the league views her as one of the most impactful players entering the professional game. Her professional career is just beginning, and college softball fans who followed her for four years will now get to watch her compete at the highest level the sport has to offer.
