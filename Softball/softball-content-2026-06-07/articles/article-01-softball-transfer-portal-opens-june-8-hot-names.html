The Transfer Portal Opens Tomorrow — Here Are the Biggest Names to Watch

By Sarah Mitchell June 7, 2026

The Offseason Starts at Midnight

The Women's College World Series ended four days ago. The championship celebration in Austin is still fresh. And yet, the most consequential 15 days of the college softball offseason are about to begin.

The NCAA Division I transfer portal opens for undergraduate student-athletes on June 8 and runs through June 22. The window was approved by the NCAA DI Council as part of a structured calendar designed to protect the recruiting process. For players ready for a new chapter and programs hungry for roster upgrades, this is the moment they've been planning for since March.

Here are the biggest names expected to enter when the portal officially opens.

Jaysoni Beachum (FSU) — The Headliner

If there is one name that every program director in the country has circled, it is Jaysoni Beachum. The Florida State third baseman had a remarkable 2026 season — hitting .417 with 78 hits, 16 home runs, 66 RBI and a .509 on-base percentage. She was named a third-team NFCA All-American and led the Seminoles in virtually every major offensive category.

Beachum has one year of eligibility remaining, which means the program that lands her gets a proven All-American for exactly one season. For a contender trying to close the gap on Texas, she is the most impactful single addition available. Expect every top-ten program to be in contact before the portal window is 24 hours old.

Tori Edwards (LSU) — The High-Ceiling Returner

While Beachum represents a one-year rental, Tori Edwards offers something different: two full years of eligibility and a track record that suggests her ceiling is nowhere near reached. The LSU redshirt sophomore set a program record with a .802 slugging percentage in 2026. She was the 2025 SEC Freshman of the Year and a first-team All-American in her first full season.

Edwards is the kind of player who can anchor an offense for an entire recruiting cycle. The programs that land her in this portal window will not have to worry about the middle of their lineup for two seasons. That makes her among the most valuable assets available in the entire cycle.

Tennessee's Morgan and Plumlee Already Announced

Before the portal window even opened, Tennessee made news. Sophomore outfielder Saviya Morgan announced her intent to enter the portal last week, followed quickly by freshman pitcher Kailey Plumlee. Both will carry remaining eligibility into new programs for the 2027 season.

Their departures, combined with the graduation of legendary pitcher Karlyn Pickens, mean head coach Karen Weekly faces a significant roster rebuild. Tennessee is one of the most storied programs in college softball history, but the next two months will determine how quickly the Lady Vols can retool.

What Programs Will Be Most Active

This is not a portal cycle where only struggling programs are shopping. Programs at every level of the sport will use the 15-day window to upgrade specific positions or add depth. The programs that failed to make the WCWS this year are hungry to change their rosters. The programs that fell short in the tournament — like Tennessee or Alabama — will look to address whatever cost them a title.

On3.com and D1Softball are both tracking entries in real time. The next 15 days will reshape the landscape of college softball for 2027. The countdown is on.

What's Next

The portal opens June 8 and closes June 22. After that, programs must wait for the next transfer window or work with graduate transfer options. Players who enter the portal but do not commit to a new school can still withdraw and return to their original program. The pace of the next 15 days will be unlike anything else in the college softball calendar — and it starts tonight.
