The 2026 College Softball Season Awards: Grant, Frahm and a Historic Year Recognized

By Emily Rawlings June 7, 2026

A Season That Earned Its Recognition

The 2026 college softball season produced some of the most remarkable individual performances the sport has ever seen. A UCLA outfielder rewrote the home run record book. A Nebraska two-way player defied the limits of what one athlete can do. A Texas pitcher won the tournament's Most Outstanding Player award for the second consecutive season. When the awards came in, they reflected a year that will be discussed for a long time.

USA TODAY Sports Network All-Americans: Grant and Frahm at the Top

The USA TODAY Sports Network named Megan Grant (UCLA) and Jordy Frahm (Nebraska) as the top All-Americans of the 2026 season — and the case for each was nearly impossible to dispute.

Grant ended the regular season with more than 40 home runs, surpassing the all-time NCAA single-season record previously held by Arizona's Laura Espinoza, who hit 37 in 1995. Grant also hit .469 with 87 RBI. That combination of contact and power at the college level is extraordinary by any standard.

Frahm was the other kind of extraordinary — a two-way player whose contributions could not be measured on a single spreadsheet. As a pitcher, she went 20-4 with 12 saves, a 1.14 ERA and 234 strikeouts. At the plate, she hit .416 with 19 home runs and 50 RBI. To produce at either of those levels individually would be noteworthy. To do both simultaneously made Frahm one of the most unique college softball players in recent memory.

Alabama head coach Patrick Murphy was named the USA TODAY Coach of the Year after leading the Crimson Tide to the No. 1 overall seed in the 2026 NCAA Tournament.

NFCA All-Americans: The Full Picture

The National Fastpitch Coaches Association announced its 2026 Division I All-American teams before the WCWS, and the honorees reflected the depth of talent at the top of the college game.

Jordy Frahm was named to the NFCA First Team alongside Texas Tech's Kaitlyn Terry, who had one of the finest seasons for a pitcher in the Big 12. Terry was one of four Texas Tech players to earn NFCA recognition despite the Red Raiders falling in the championship series — a testament to how talented and deep that program was in 2026.

The NFCA Second Team included Texas's Teagan Kavan and Katie Stewart, along with Texas Tech's NiJaree Canady and Mia Williams. Kavan was the two-time WCWS Most Outstanding Player; Stewart was the SEC Player of the Year. Both earning second-team recognition reflects just how many elite players competed at the highest level this season.

Texas Tech's Jackie Lis earned Third Team honors, giving the Red Raiders four total NFCA All-Americans. Florida State also placed four players across the three All-American teams, with Isa Torres on the First Team and Ashtyn Danley on the Second.

A Season That Reset Expectations

The 2026 awards wrap a season that did more than deliver great games. It reset what fans expect from college softball. Grant showed what the hitting ceiling looks like when a player reaches her peak. Frahm showed that two-way dominance is still possible. Kavan and Stewart showed what a dynasty looks like from the inside.

Murphy's Coach of the Year honor recognizes what Alabama accomplished with one of the most demanding schedules in the country. The Crimson Tide was the last team many expected to lose — and they still fell short of the title. That is what the level of this sport looks like right now.

What's Next

With awards season wrapping up, attention now shifts to the transfer portal window opening June 8 and the 2027 recruiting cycle. The players honored this season will largely move on — some to professional careers, some to the portal — and a new group of competitors will begin building their cases for next year's recognition. The cycle of great college softball never stops.
