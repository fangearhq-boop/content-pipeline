All-Star Phase 1 Voting Results: Ohtani Leads All, Clement Tops the AL

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 25, 2026

Phase 1 of the 2026 MLB All-Star voting closed at noon ET today, and when the dust settled, Shohei Ohtani led all of baseball by a wide margin — while one of the most interesting results of the voting cycle came out of the American League.

Ohtani, the Dodgers designated hitter, finished Phase 1 with 2,310,735 votes — the most of any player in the game. He earned an automatic starting spot in the National League lineup for the July 14 All-Star Game at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. The only real question about Ohtani was never whether he'd make the All-Star Game; it was whether anyone could push him to a competitive vote total.

Nobody came close.

Clement Over Alvarez: The AL's Surprising Result

The more interesting Phase 1 story came from the American League, where Toronto Blue Jays infielder Ernie Clement finished as the AL's top overall vote-getter with 2,054,130 votes — approximately 80,000 votes ahead of Houston Astros slugger Yordan Alvarez.

That margin, while not insurmountable, makes Clement the Phase 1 leader in the AL. If it holds through the end of voting, Clement earns an automatic AL starting spot.

The result is a reminder of how effectively a passionate regional fan base can drive voting totals. Blue Jays fans mobilized for Clement throughout Phase 1. For Alvarez — one of the most feared hitters in the game — it was a rare moment of being outpaced in a popularity contest.

Schwarber Leads the DH Vote

In the National League, Kyle Schwarber of the Phillies is leading the DH vote with 1,540,000 votes. The context makes this particularly resonant: the All-Star Game is at Citizens Bank Park — Schwarber's home stadium. His fans are voting in enormous numbers, and his name is on a ballot for a game being played in his own backyard.

Schwarber currently leads MLB with 29 home runs. Whether he converts that to an automatic All-Star starting spot depends on where the final Phase 1 totals land across the NL DH race.

What Happens in Phase 2

Phase 2 voting begins June 29. In this round, the top vote-getters at each position from Phase 1 — in both leagues — compete head-to-head for the remaining starting spots on the All-Star roster. The final Starting Lineup announcement typically comes in early July.

The All-Star Game itself is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park. The Home Run Derby is the night before, July 13, at the same venue. Both events will be hosted in one of baseball's most energetic parks, in front of one of the sport's most passionate fan bases.

What's Next

Phase 2 voting runs from June 29 until its own deadline in early July. The full All-Star Game roster — starters and reserves — is typically finalized and announced in the week before the game. Bobby Witt Jr., currently recovering from a knee injury but expected back in the Kansas City Royals lineup within days, remains on the ballot and could see his vote totals affected by his return to action. Schwarber's Derby decision is also expected before the end of June. The All-Star Game, July 14 in Philadelphia, is 19 days away.
