Bobby Witt Jr. Available Off the Bench — Full Return to Royals Lineup Expected Within Days

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 25, 2026

Bobby Witt Jr. was available off the bench for the Kansas City Royals on June 24 — the clearest sign yet that the Grade 1 MCL sprain he suffered on June 18 is healing on schedule.

Manager Matt Quatraro offered the most encouraging language of the week: "Hopeful that in the next couple of days, he's available" for the full starting lineup.

Kansas City exhaled.

The Five Games He Missed

Witt suffered the knee injury against the St. Louis Cardinals and sat out five consecutive games — a stretch that tested the Royals' depth and reminded everyone watching how valuable he is to this team.

His numbers before the injury: .294 batting average, 10 home runs, 32 RBIs, 28 stolen bases. His fWAR at 4.5 led the entire American League. Those are the numbers of a player who, by advanced metrics, was the most valuable position player in the AL entering mid-June.

Replacing that production for five games is not possible. Managing without it for five games was the task, and Kansas City did well enough to stay competitive. But Witt's return to the starting lineup — expected within the next day or two — is the most significant positive development for this team since the injury happened.

The All-Star Timing

There is also an All-Star dimension to this. Phase 1 of All-Star voting closes today at noon ET. Witt has been on the ballot throughout his injury, but voters often respond to what they see — and five games of absence is not the visibility that helps a player's vote totals.

His return to the starting lineup, with strong play in the coming days, could matter for his final vote position heading into Phase 2 and the eventual roster selection. The All-Star Game is July 14 in Philadelphia. A healthy Witt, playing well in the days before the Phase 2 deadline, is the version of events that Kansas City wants.

What the Second Half Holds

The Royals are trying to compete in the American League. Their second-half success depends heavily on Witt's health and production. A team with 4.5 fWAR from its shortstop is a very different proposition than a team without it — and Kansas City has experienced both in a short window this month.

The Grade 1 classification of the MCL sprain is as encouraging as injuries get at this level. Grade 1 means partial tearing with good recovery timelines. Grade 2 and 3 are the more serious categories. Witt was available off the bench within a week of the injury. That tracks perfectly with the Grade 1 prognosis.

What's Next

Witt's return to the full starting lineup is expected within the next 48 hours, per manager Quatraro's comments. Watch the Royals game notes for his name in the starting lineup — that is the official signal. Once back, the immediate story becomes whether the knee holds up over a longer stretch of games. Assuming it does, the Royals' competitive picture for the second half gets considerably brighter the moment their AL fWAR leader is back in the field every day.
