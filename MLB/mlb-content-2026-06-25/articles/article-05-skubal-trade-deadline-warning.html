Skubal Gives the Tigers a Deadline Warning — And the Contenders Are Listening

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 25, 2026

Tarik Skubal said what most people were already thinking.

On June 23, the Detroit Tigers ace offered this assessment of his team's trade deadline situation: "The reality is we need to play better baseball or else, come the deadline, you give the front office an option to reassess where this team is."

It was a remarkably direct statement from a player who is, by any measure, one of the most valuable pieces on the trade market in all of baseball. Two-time AL Cy Young Award winner. Free agent after 2026. Anchoring a rotation for a team that is 30-44 and in last place in its division, 9.5 games behind the leader.

He said the quiet part out loud. And the contenders circling Detroit heard every word.

The Tigers' Situation

Detroit's record through 74 games — 30-44 — is not what anyone in the organization expected when the season began. The Tigers entered 2026 with hopes of contending, built around Skubal and a developing core of young position players. The season has not cooperated.

Forty days remain until the August 3 trade deadline. For the Tigers to reverse course in that window and become buyers — or even hold steady — they would need to play at a significantly higher level than they have all season. The math is harsh. Nine and a half games back with 40 days to the deadline is a steep climb in a competitive division.

Skubal knows this. His quote suggests he knows it better than anyone, and that he wants the front office to act accordingly.

What a Trade Would Mean

Skubal's market value as a rental is extraordinary. He is the best pitcher in the American League by most measures, coming off back-to-back Cy Young Awards, and he is healthy — he returned from a shoulder procedure approximately five weeks ago and has been building back to full strength since.

Every contender in baseball that lacks an elite starter has a reason to call Detroit. The Braves, Chicago Cubs, Dodgers, New York Yankees, San Diego Padres, and Toronto Blue Jays have all been linked to Skubal by reporters covering the deadline market. Each of those organizations would trade significant prospects to rent a two-time Cy Young winner for a postseason run.

For Detroit, the decision is painful but probably inevitable if the record stays where it is. A Skubal trade would generate one of the largest prospect returns in recent deadline history — a haul that could accelerate a rebuild if the Tigers decide that 2026 is not their year.

What's Next

The August 3 deadline is the date that matters. Between now and then, every Tigers win reduces the probability of a trade and every loss increases it. Watch Detroit's record in July as the clearest indicator of whether a deal gets done. If the Tigers approach the deadline 10-plus games out, Skubal almost certainly moves. If they somehow climb back to within five games, the calculus changes. Skubal's quote on June 23 should be understood as both a warning to his teammates and a signal to the market. Both audiences are listening.
