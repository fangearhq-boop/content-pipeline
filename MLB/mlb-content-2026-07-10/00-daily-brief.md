### STORY 4: Tyler Tolbert Ties 12-Consecutive-Hits MLB Record
- **Tier:** 2
- **Classification:** NEW STORY (occurred July 7-8; first coverage)
- **Summary:** Kansas City Royals outfielder Tyler Tolbert tied the MLB record for hits in consecutive plate appearances with 12, spread across two games on July 7 (5-for-5 vs. Phillies) and July 8 (5-for-6 in a 16-12 win over the Mets). His record-tying 12th hit was an infield single in the 7th inning. He flew out in the 9th to end the streak at 12. His bat is headed to the Baseball Hall of Fame. Previous holders: Johnny Kling (1902), Pinky Higgins (1938), Walt Dropo (1952), and Jose Miranda (2024). He also tied the MLB record for consecutive five-hit games (Roberto Clemente 1970, Hi Myers 1917).
- **Angle for X Post 1 (2:30 PM):** Tolbert tied a record that stood since 1952 — 12 straight hits, bat going to Cooperstown.
- **Angle for Article:** Full record breakdown, the two-game timeline, the list of predecessors, what makes this historically rare.
