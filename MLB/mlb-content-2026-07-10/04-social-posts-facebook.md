### STORY 1: All-Star Week Opens — HBCU Swingman Classic Tonight

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

The wait is over. MLB All-Star Week is in Philadelphia.

Catto — an HBCU alum, civil rights activist, and a co-founder of one of America's first organized Black baseball teams. That history is the reason this game exists.

Fifty years. One week. The city's ready.

### STORY 4: Tyler Tolbert Ties 12-Consecutive-Hits MLB Record

#### Long-Form Post — 2:30 PM ET

Tyler Tolbert just joined one of the most exclusive clubs in baseball history.

The Kansas City Royals outfielder recorded a hit in 12 consecutive plate appearances over two games — tying an MLB record shared by Johnny Kling (1902), Pinky Higgins (1938), Walt Dropo (1952), and Jose Miranda (2024). Tolbert is the fifth player all-time to reach 12.

Game 1, July 7 vs. the Phillies: Tolbert went 5-for-5. By the end of that game, he had already tied the record for consecutive hits in a single game and had started building his streak.

Game 2, July 8 vs. the Mets: The Royals won 16-12. In the 7th inning, Tolbert lined an infield single — his 12th consecutive hit, tying the all-time record. He came up in the 9th with a chance to break it outright, and flew out to right field. The streak ended at 12.

His bat is going to the Baseball Hall of Fame in Cooperstown.

On top of the consecutive-hit record, Tolbert also tied an MLB mark for back-to-back five-hit games — a company that includes only Roberto Clemente (1970) and Hi Myers (1917).

A performance for the history books, tucked into the week's biggest celebration.

### STORY 5: Skubal Trade Watch — 24 Days to the August 3 Deadline

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 PM ET

24 days until the August 3 trade deadline. Tarik Skubal's situation hasn't changed — he's still the most coveted starting pitcher available — but the context around it is evolving.

The Detroit Tigers are 30-44 overall, but they've had the best record in the American League since the start of June. The Tigers are good right now. That reality is making Detroit less eager to sell — and it isn't bringing the asking price down.

Skubal was direct about the situation in the Detroit News: "Play better baseball or else — come the deadline, you give the front office an option to reassess where this team is."

The Atlanta Braves remain the frontrunner, per ESPN, MLB.com, FanSided, Yahoo Sports, and Heavy.com. Their need is clear: Spencer Strider went on the 60-day IL on June 17 with right elbow inflammation. The MRI was clean, but his earliest return is around August 17 — after the deadline. A Braves insider told reporters last week that Strider may not pitch again in 2026 at all.

Who's out: The Yankees. A source told Heavy.com flat-out: "No chance. None."

Who's in the mix beyond Atlanta: The Brewers, Cubs, Phillies, Padres, Rays, Blue Jays, and Red Sox have all been linked at various points.

The asking price, per rival GMs quoted by FanSided's Robert Murray: Detroit wants two top-10 prospects in the acquiring team's system, with at least one ranked nationally in the top 100. The Braves' top nationally-ranked prospects are J.R. Ritchie (22-year-old RHP) and Cam Caminiti (19-year-old LHP) — both are what Atlanta would be trading away.

24 days. The price is real. Braves are the team to watch.
