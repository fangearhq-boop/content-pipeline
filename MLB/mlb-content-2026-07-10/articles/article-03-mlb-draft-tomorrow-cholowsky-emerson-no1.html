The MLB Draft Is Tomorrow and the No. 1 Pick Is Still Undecided

By Jake Torres | July 10, 2026

The 2026 MLB Draft begins tomorrow at 1:00 PM ET at the Pennsylvania Convention Center in Philadelphia — and the Chicago White Sox still have not signaled who they will take with the No. 1 overall pick.

This is not a mystery that will resolve before the moment itself. According to multiple outlets that have spoken with people inside the White Sox organization, conversations about all three finalists are "real and happening up to the minute" as of this week. The decision is expected to be made in the final hours before the pick. NBC and Peacock carry the first 10 selections beginning at 1:00 PM ET.

The Three Finalists

Every credible source has converged on three names:

Roch Cholowsky (SS, UCLA) has been the consensus top prospect for well over a year. Jonathan Mayo at MLB Pipeline gives him 50% odds at No. 1 — not because he is overwhelmingly dominant but because the alternative is essentially even. Cholowsky is the kind of shortstop evaluators have been drafting since baseball understood what shortstop production looks like at the major league level.

Grady Emerson (SS, Texas prep) moved to No. 1 on MLB Pipeline's rankings recently and has been described by some evaluators as the best player in the draft, if only marginally. Mayo gives him 45% odds. The Cholowsky vs. Emerson debate is the real question. CBS Sports has called it a two-man race. ESPN's mock draft treats it the same way.

Vahn Lackey (C, Georgia Tech) is the acknowledged wild card. Multiple reports confirm that discussions about Lackey are "real" and that he is genuinely in White Sox conversations. He is not, per most analysts, the front-of-mind choice — but he is in the room, which matters at this stage.

The Context Around the Pick

The White Sox last held the No. 1 overall pick in 1977, when they selected Harold Baines out of high school in Maryland. Baines played 22 seasons, made six All-Star teams, and was inducted into the Hall of Fame in 2019.

This year's irony: the White Sox earned this pick by going 41-121 in 2024 — the worst record in the American League. In 2026, they lead the AL Central. They will pick No. 1 overall tomorrow while occupying first place in their division. It is not a combination you see often.

The Draft takes place at the Pennsylvania Convention Center as part of All-Star Week festivities. CBS Sports notes seven total names that have appeared in White Sox discussions — including Jackson Flora (UC Santa Barbara) and Jacob Lombard (Florida prep) — but the decision is effectively between Cholowsky and Emerson, with Lackey as the potential disruptor.
