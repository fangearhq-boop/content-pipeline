24 Days to the Deadline: Why the Braves Are Still the Team to Beat for Tarik Skubal

By Marcus Cole | July 10, 2026

The MLB trade deadline is August 3. That is 24 days away. Tarik Skubal remains the most coveted starting pitcher on the market, the Atlanta Braves remain the frontrunner, and the Detroit Tigers are complicating everything by playing their best baseball of the season.

There has been no shortage of coverage on this front since the Braves' need crystallized in June. Spencer Strider went on the 60-day injured list on June 17 with right elbow inflammation. The MRI came back clean — no ligament damage — but his earliest return is projected around August 17, which is after the deadline. A Braves insider told reporters last week that Strider may not pitch again in 2026 at all.

That is the context behind Atlanta's urgency. The Braves have the best record in the National League. They need a starter. Skubal is available.

The Tigers Are Good Right Now

Detroit's overall record is 30-44. That looks like a seller. But the Tigers have had the best record in the American League since the start of June, and they have won seven of their last eight games. The team that was headed toward another disappointing season has found something over the past six weeks.

Skubal is aware of the dynamic. He told the Detroit News: "Play better baseball or else — come the deadline, you give the front office an option to reassess where this team is." The "or else" is the trade. The Tigers playing well means the front office is watching something that may shift the calculus — not because Detroit is suddenly a contender, but because player morale, win streaks, and confidence in a roster can make it harder to pull the trigger on a deal.

The asking price, per rival GMs cited by FanSided's Robert Murray, has not come down: Detroit wants two top-10 prospects in the acquiring team's system, with at least one ranked in the top 100 nationally.

Who's In, Who's Out

The Braves are the frontrunner across every outlet that has covered this consistently — ESPN, MLB.com, FanSided, Yahoo Sports, and Heavy.com. Their two nationally-ranked prospects, J.R. Ritchie (22-year-old right-hander) and Cam Caminiti (19-year-old left-hander), are the core of what a deal would cost. Nightengale reported last week that a Braves-Tigers deal would go to "the final days, if not hours" before the 6:00 PM ET August 3 deadline.

The New York Yankees are out. A source told Heavy.com: "No chance. None." Other teams that have been linked at various points include the Milwaukee Brewers, Chicago Cubs, Philadelphia Phillies, San Diego Padres, Tampa Bay Rays, Toronto Blue Jays, and Boston Red Sox. The Los Angeles Dodgers have been reported as interested in principle but not actively engaged.

What's Next

The MLB trade deadline is August 3, 2026, at 6:00 PM ET. Skubal is in the final year of his current contract, making him a rental. The market will sharpen in the final two weeks of July. The Braves are watching Detroit's win-loss record as closely as any Tigers fan.
