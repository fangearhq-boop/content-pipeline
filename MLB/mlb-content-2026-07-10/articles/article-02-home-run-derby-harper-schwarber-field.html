Bryce Harper Is In, Kyle Schwarber Is TBD, and the Home Run Derby Field Is Almost Set

By Marcus Cole | July 10, 2026

The Home Run Derby is three days away, six of eight spots are filled, and the best story in the field is playing at his home park.

Bryce Harper is confirmed for the 2026 Home Run Derby at Citizens Bank Park on Monday, July 13. Harper won the Derby in 2018. He will now get to swing under the lights in front of the same crowd that has watched him hit 298 career home runs as a Phillie. Citizens Bank Park is loud on a random Tuesday in April. On Derby night in front of a sellout, with Harper in the field, it will be something else entirely.

The Confirmed Field

Six of eight participants are set for Monday night (Netflix, 8:00 PM ET):

Junior Caminero (Tampa Bay Rays) was the 2025 Home Run Derby runner-up and returns with 26 home runs this season. Ben Rice (New York Yankees) will have his father Dan Rice pitching to him — one of the more charming wrinkles in the field. Jac Caglianone (Kansas City Royals) is a Derby first-timer with 14 home runs in his rookie season. Willson Contreras (Boston Red Sox) is a four-time All-Star making his first Derby appearance. Jordan Walker (St. Louis Cardinals) was confirmed July 9. And Harper makes six.

Two spots remain open.

The Schwarber Question

Kyle Schwarber leads all of Major League Baseball with 31 home runs. He is a two-time Derby participant. The Derby is at his ballpark. Every condition for his participation is met — with one exception: he has not officially said yes.

The situation has been clear for weeks. Schwarber told SI's Tom Verducci that he'd participate on two conditions: his back holding up, and an All-Star Game selection. Both conditions were met. He told the Philadelphia Inquirer on July 6 that he'd need to give the organization "an answer in the next day or so." Manager Don Mattingly appeared to settle the question after a recent Phillies win, saying "I thought Kyle was going to do it" — then quickly backed off when reminded that Schwarber hadn't officially confirmed. Mattingly later said "Not sure on Kyle yet."

The lean is clearly toward yes. An official announcement has not come.

How the Derby Works

The 2026 Derby uses the swing-based format: 20 swings in Round 1, 15 in the knockout round and final. Every swing counts against the allotment, whether or not it produces a home run. The exception: if a competitor homers on their final swing of a round, they continue until they fail to homer — creating the kind of walk-off tension the old timer format could never replicate.

What's Next

The Home Run Derby is Monday, July 13, at Citizens Bank Park, beginning at 8:00 PM ET on Netflix. The remaining two participant spots are expected to be announced before the weekend. Aaron Judge has already confirmed he will not participate. Kyle Schwarber's official decision is expected imminently.
