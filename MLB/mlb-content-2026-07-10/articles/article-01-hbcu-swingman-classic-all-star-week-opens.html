All-Star Week Is Here: The HBCU Swingman Classic Opens Philadelphia's 50-Year Wait

By Ryan Calloway | July 10, 2026

The last time Major League Baseball held its All-Star Game in Philadelphia, Gerald Ford was president, the country was celebrating its 200th birthday, and the venue was Veterans Stadium. That was 1976. Fifty years later, the city gets another turn — and this time, the opening act is playing tonight at Citizens Bank Park.

The 4th annual HBCU Swingman Classic begins at 7:00 PM ET, available on MLB Network and MLB.com. Gates open at 5:00 PM. Fifty players selected from 19 historically Black colleges and universities will take the field, with a selection committee that includes Ken Griffey Jr. The managers are Jimmy Rollins — the World Series champion shortstop who spent the most productive years of his career in Philadelphia — and Ricky Weeks, a 14-year major league veteran.

Philadelphia Mayor Cherelle Parker throws the ceremonial first pitch. Earlier today, participating players toured City Hall and paid tribute to Octavius V. Catto, an HBCU alum, civil rights activist, and a co-founder of one of America's first organized Black baseball teams in the years after the Civil War. The historical thread running through tonight's game is deliberate. This is the reason the Swingman Classic exists.

Why Philadelphia, Why Now

The 2026 All-Star Game carries unusual weight. America turns 250 years old this year, and Philadelphia — the city where the Declaration of Independence was signed and the Constitution was drafted — is the fitting host. The Semiquincentennial provides the backdrop; baseball's midsummer showcase provides the stage.

Philadelphia last hosted in 1976, during the Bicentennial. Veterans Stadium is long gone. Citizens Bank Park, which opened in 2004, will host the All-Star Game on Tuesday night for the first time. The Phillies have one of the loudest home crowds in the sport. That electricity is part of what the league was selling when it awarded the city this week.

The Full Week Ahead

Tonight's Swingman Classic is the first event of a week that builds toward Tuesday:

Saturday, July 11: The MLB Draft at the Pennsylvania Convention Center. First pick at 1:00 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. The Chicago White Sox hold the No. 1 selection, and the decision between Roch Cholowsky (UCLA) and Grady Emerson is genuinely unresolved going into tomorrow.

Sunday, July 12: The Futures Game and the MLBx All-Star 3-on-3 at Citizens Bank Park. The co-ed multi-sport event features AUSL softball stars alongside former MLB players and NFL names.

Monday, July 13: The Home Run Derby. Six of eight participants are confirmed, including Bryce Harper — the 2018 Derby champion — swinging at his home park. Kyle Schwarber, who leads MLB with 31 home runs, is expected to participate but has not officially confirmed. Coverage is on Netflix beginning at 8:00 PM ET.

Tuesday, July 14: The All-Star Game on FOX.
