Tyler Tolbert Tied an MLB Record That Stood Since 1952 — Then Flew Out in the 9th

By Ryan Calloway | July 10, 2026

Tyler Tolbert had one more chance to break it. He came up in the 9th inning of a 16-12 Kansas City Royals win over the New York Mets on July 8, with 12 consecutive hits already in the books. He flew out to right field. The streak ended at 12 — tied with four other men across 124 years of baseball history.

Tolbert's bat is headed to the Baseball Hall of Fame in Cooperstown.

How It Happened

The streak unfolded across two games. On July 7, against the Philadelphia Phillies, Tolbert went 5-for-5. By the final out of that game, he had accumulated seven straight hits and was closing in on territory that almost nobody reaches.

On July 8, against the Mets, the Royals won in a chaotic, 16-12 affair. In the 7th inning, Tolbert lined an infield single — his 12th consecutive hit, tying the MLB all-time record for hits in consecutive plate appearances. He had one more shot in the 9th. The fly ball ended it.

He finished the two-game stretch 10-for-11, with an RBI double and a two-run home run included in the July 8 performance.

The Company He Keeps

The MLB record for hits in consecutive plate appearances is 12. Before Tolbert, it had been reached four times:

Johnny Kling, Chicago Cubs, 1902. Pinky Higgins, Boston Red Sox, 1938. Walt Dropo, Detroit Tigers, 1952 — the most recent holder for 72 years until Jose Miranda of the Minnesota Twins tied it in 2024. Now Tolbert joins the list as the fifth player all-time.

Tolbert is, per reporting, the first player in the expansion era — after 1961 — to reach 12 consecutive hits. The record has existed across six decades of modern baseball without anyone touching it until Miranda two years ago and Tolbert now.

The Second Record

On top of the consecutive-hit record, Tolbert also tied the MLB mark for back-to-back five-hit games. Going 5-for-5 on July 7 and 5-for-6 on July 8 puts him alongside Roberto Clemente, who did it in 1970, and Hi Myers, who did it in 1917. Three players in history.

Both records arriving in the same 48-hour stretch, during a 16-12 win in which he had one more chance to own the consecutive-hit record outright — and flew out instead. Baseball is occasionally very good at this kind of story.

What's Next

Tolbert's bat from the record-tying game is going to the Baseball Hall of Fame in Cooperstown. The Royals continue their season with the All-Star break approaching. Tolbert bats in the No. 9 spot and plays outfield and infield for Kansas City.
