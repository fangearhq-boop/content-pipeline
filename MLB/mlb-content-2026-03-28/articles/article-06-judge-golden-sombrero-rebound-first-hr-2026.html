Judge Was 0-for-5 With Four Ks on Opening Day. He Hit a Home Run the Next Day.

By Ballpark Banter Staff March 28, 2026

The Golden Sombrero on Opening Day

Aaron Judge had one of the worst individual Opening Days in the history of reigning Most Valuable Players on Tuesday in San Francisco. The Yankees captain went 0-for-5 with four strikeouts — a golden sombrero — against the Giants at Oracle Park. Historical records show this was the first time a reigning AL or NL MVP had struck out four times on Opening Day. Max Fried's 6.1-inning shutout gem saved the team line: the Yankees won 7-0. But Judge's line was jarring on its own terms.

The strikeouts came against Logan Webb, who was sharp all evening. Webb's sinker movement was described afterward as the best it has been in any start this season. Judge acknowledged the performance simply and moved on — which is exactly what great hitters do. There was no panic, no media-driven crisis, no lineup shake-up. The Yankees captain struck out four times, tipped his cap to a well-pitched game, and came back the next day.

The Bounce-Back

On Wednesday, Aaron Judge hit the first home run of his 2026 season. The Yankees improved to 2-0. The golden sombrero was filed away as a single data point in what everyone — including Judge — expects to be a lengthy, productive season. That's the nature of 162 games: the sample size is large enough to absorb an anomaly, especially when the anomaly comes from the best pitcher in the Giants' rotation in a season-opening spot start.

Judge has hit 30 or more home runs in every healthy full season of his career. His on-base percentage has never dipped below .373 in a full season. His career OPS+ of 172 — meaning he is 72 percent better than the league average hitter, adjusted for park and era — is one of the highest active rates in the sport. One 0-for-5 game doesn't change any of that arithmetic.

What's Next

The Yankees continue their opening schedule with Judge back in the middle of the lineup and looking, by all accounts, to be in full health entering the season. The more interesting subplot to monitor is whether Webb's Opening Night performance was a sign of what the Giants might be capable of this year, or simply one elite start in a difficult season ahead for a San Francisco club without many confirmed answers in their rotation. For Judge, the story is already closed: he was bad on Tuesday and good on Wednesday. The 2026 season, for all practical purposes, begins now.
