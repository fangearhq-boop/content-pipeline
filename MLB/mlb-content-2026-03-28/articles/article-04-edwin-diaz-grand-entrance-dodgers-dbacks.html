Diaz, Live Trumpet, and a Ring Night Save — The Dodgers Are Already Must-Watch TV

By Ballpark Banter Staff March 28, 2026

The Entrance That Sold Out Dodger Stadium Twice

When the Dodgers added Edwin Diaz this offseason, they didn't just acquire one of the best closers in baseball — they acquired the most theatrical entrance in the sport. On Friday night at Dodger Stadium, in front of a sellout crowd gathered for the team's World Series ring ceremony, Diaz made that entrance official. A local Los Angeles trumpeter positioned just beyond the left-field wall played the opening notes of "Narco" as Diaz jogged in from the bullpen. The crowd went sideways.

Diaz said afterward he was surprised to hear a live trumpet — it's a detail the Dodgers kept from him. The moment was pure Hollywood, executed on the biggest possible stage: a ring night sellout in the entertainment capital of the world. The Dodgers, of course, have a gift for spectacle. This was a reminder that their new closer fits the brand perfectly.

The Save Itself — Not Just Theater

Beyond the pageantry, Diaz had a real job to do: protect a one-run lead against a dangerous Arizona Diamondbacks lineup in the ninth inning. He struck out Carlos Santana on three pitches. He walked Alek Thomas (who promptly stole second), then struck out Jordan Lawlar on a called third strike. With Ketel Marte — one of the most dangerous hitters in the National League — stepping in as the potential tying run, Diaz induced a groundout to Mookie Betts at first base. Dodgers win, 5-4.

The save wasn't Diaz's vintage dominant performance — the walk was a crack in the armor — but the strikeouts were crisp and the groundout was efficient when it mattered most. For a first appearance with a new team in a high-profile spot, it was a strong statement: the system works, the closer closes.

Kyle Tucker's Clutch Setup

The win was set up in the eighth inning, when Kyle Tucker delivered the go-ahead RBI. Alex Freeland led off with a double against Kevin Ginkel, moved to third on a Shohei Ohtani groundout, then scored on Tucker's single through a drawn-in infield. The sequence was exactly the kind of situational hitting that makes Tucker one of the most dangerous middle-of-the-order bats the Dodgers have ever assembled — patient, strong to the opposite field, and clutch in the moment. The Dodgers are 2-0, Tucker already has a game-winning hit, and Diaz has a save. The blueprint is clear.

What's Next

The Dodgers continue their opening homestand through the weekend, and the expectation is that every Diaz appearance this year will carry this same energy — the live trumpet is reportedly a plan the team has committed to through the season. The more interesting question is whether Diaz can maintain the elite strikeout rates that made him the most feared closer in the National League in New York. His fastball velocity and slider break will be closely watched in the coming weeks as he gets deeper into the regular-season grind. Two games in, two wins, and one unforgettable entrance. The 2026 Dodgers are already the team everyone is watching.
