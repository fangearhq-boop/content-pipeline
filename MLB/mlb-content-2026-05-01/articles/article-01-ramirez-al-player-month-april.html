José Ramírez Wins AL Player of the Month: .342, 7 HR, 28 RBI — and a Third 30-30 Season in Sight

By Marcus Cole | May 1, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

José Ramírez is baseball's most underrated superstar, and his April 2026 makes that harder to argue with each passing day. The Cleveland Guardians third baseman won the American League Player of the Month Award for April — and the numbers behind it are extraordinary: .342 batting average, .722 slugging percentage, 7 home runs, 28 RBI, 7 doubles, a triple, 12 runs, and 9 walks in 21 games.

Put that on a full-season pace and you get roughly 54 home runs and over 200 RBI. That's not what Ramírez is expected to deliver — it's what he delivered in April alone. And for a player who has somehow spent his entire career in the shadow of bigger markets and louder names, the award is a reminder that Cleveland has one of the greatest offensive players of his generation.

Setting a Franchise Mark a Century in the Making

Ramírez's April achievements went beyond the standard stats. On April 6, he played in his 1,620th game as a Guardian — breaking Terry Turner's franchise record for games played, a mark that had endured for more than a century. Turner played in the early 1900s, and his longevity record stood through decades of Hall of Famers and franchise cornerstones. Ramírez broke it with characteristic quiet: no ceremony, just another game, just another milestone.

He also began the season tracking toward the franchise's all-time extra-base hits record — a figure that reflects not just power but the kind of sustained excellence across multiple seasons that defines transcendent careers.

The 30-30 Chase

The storyline that may define Ramírez's 2026 season is his bid for a third consecutive 30-30 campaign. He has already achieved the rare feat in back-to-back years. A third consecutive 30-30 would make him one of just nine players in major league history to record at least two consecutive 30-30 seasons — a list that reads like a page from baseball's greatest hitters.

Ramírez turns 35 this month. Players at that age are supposed to be declining. His April suggested he did not receive the memo. The combination of power (7 HRs in April) and speed that a 30-30 season requires typically belongs to players in their late 20s — Ramírez is doing it while accumulating franchise records and award hardware in his mid-30s.

If Ramírez delivers a third 30-30 season in 2026, the conversation about his Hall of Fame credentials — already strong — becomes essentially closed. The combination of longevity, durability, offensive production, and consistent excellence at the highest level is the definition of what Cooperstown enshrines.

What's Next

Ramírez and the Guardians enter May with the AL Central standings in flux. The 30-30 chase will be a season-long storyline, with milestones likely falling throughout the summer. Cleveland's playoff case strengthens every time Ramírez delivers — and if April is any indication, May is going to feature more of the same from one of baseball's most consistent stars.
