One Week to Ten Days. That's How Long Ohtani Is Out. That's How Long His Cy Young Case Waits.

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | July 22, 2026

Dave Roberts gave the clearest public timeline yet on Wednesday: it will be "at least one week to 10 days" before the Los Angeles Dodgers even consider putting Shohei Ohtani back in the starting rotation. A bullpen session to test the left knee is expected around July 25 or 26. Until then, Ohtani is the Dodgers' designated hitter, and his Cy Young argument is collecting dust.

The knee issue — left knee inflammation, with fluid drained before the All-Star break — has now cost Ohtani at least two scheduled starts this month. He was scratched on July 10 and missed the game against the Phillies this week. He was selected to his sixth consecutive All-Star team and missed the Midsummer Classic entirely. The Dodgers expect him to pitch again in 2026. The question is how much time is left when he does.

What He Was Before the Knee

Ohtani entered his most recent absence at 8-2 with a 1.79 ERA, 95 strikeouts, and 85.2 innings pitched across 14 starts. Those are numbers that would have placed him squarely in the National League Cy Young conversation — possibly at the front of it. An ERA of 1.79 leads the majors by a significant margin. The strikeout total is elite for the innings pitched.

Those numbers are frozen. They are not getting better while he serves as a DH. Every day Ohtani is not pitching is a day another pitcher is building a case against him.

The Competition

The National League Cy Young race is not waiting for Ohtani to get healthy. Cristopher Sanchez of the Philadelphia Phillies has been making starts. The Milwaukee Brewers have multiple starters who are healthy and building innings. Every week Ohtani misses narrows the gap in the vote totals, even if his existing numbers remain strong.

Cy Young voting rewards durability and performance sustained over a full season. Ohtani is missing what may be a critical four- to six-week stretch. If he returns in late July or early August and pitches at his previous level for the rest of the year, the case is intact. If the return keeps slipping, the 1.79 ERA becomes a smaller piece of a shorter resume.

The Dodgers' Position

Los Angeles is 64-38 — the best record in the National League. They won Tuesday 2-1 against the Phillies behind seven strong innings from Bobby Wrobleski and a two-run home run from Max Muncy. The team does not need Ohtani on the mound to win games right now.

Roberts has been clear that the Dodgers expect Ohtani to pitch again in 2026 and that they are not considering placing him on the IL. The plan is to bring him back when the knee allows, not to rush a timeline that risks turning temporary inflammation into something worse. The July 25-26 bullpen session is the first real test of whether that timeline is tracking correctly.

What's Next

The bullpen session this weekend is the defining moment of Ohtani's second half. A successful session means a return to the rotation could follow within days. An unsuccessful one extends the timeline further and puts the Cy Young case in real jeopardy. Ballpark Banter will track every update on Ohtani's knee through the weekend and beyond.
