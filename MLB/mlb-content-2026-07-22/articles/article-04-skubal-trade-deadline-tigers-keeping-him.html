Detroit Is Hot in July. Skubal Wants to Stay. The Asking Price Is Outrageous. This Deal Might Not Happen.

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 22, 2026

For weeks, the most interesting question at the trade deadline was which team would pay the price to acquire Tarik Skubal. Multiple contenders were engaged. The Dodgers, Cubs, Braves, and Rays were all monitoring the situation. Rival executives described the asking price as "outrageous." The cost in prospects was deterring even the most aggressive buyers.

The question has changed. The "growing sense" among rival executives, per The Athletic's Ken Rosenthal, is now that the Detroit Tigers will hold Skubal through the August 3 deadline and make a run at the playoffs themselves. The biggest trade-deadline story of the summer is no longer about which team gets Skubal. It may be about whether any deal happens at all.

Why the Narrative Flipped

Detroit is 10-4 in July. Since June 1, the Tigers are 20-12 — the best record in the American League over that span. They are within 4.5 to 6.5 games of an AL Wild Card spot with 12 days remaining before the August 3 deadline. In a historically weak American League, where the divisional leaders include teams that are around .500, the math for a Tigers playoff run is not absurd.

Rosenthal reported that Tigers' management believes, barring a collapse, they will keep Skubal and push for October. Skubal himself has been public about wanting the organization to be buyers. When the ace of the staff is explicitly asking the front office to go for it, and the front office is watching the team go 10-4 in July, the calculus for a trade changes.

The Price Problem

The asking price has been a consistent obstacle throughout this process. Multiple rival National League executives have described it as "outrageous" — a prospect cost so high that most teams cannot make the math work even if they want Skubal badly enough. The Dodgers, who have the organizational depth to absorb such a trade, have been monitoring. So have the Cubs, Braves, and Rays.

But monitoring is not the same as closing. If Detroit's front office believes the Tigers can make the playoffs, the price only goes up. And if the Tigers are genuinely buyers at the deadline rather than sellers, the price becomes irrelevant — the pitcher is not available at any number.

What This Means for the Deadline

The trade deadline is August 3. Twelve days from now, one of two things happens: Detroit makes a move to bolster the roster around Skubal and goes for a Wild Card berth, or the Tigers decide the odds aren't favorable and deal him. The decision window is narrow.

The teams waiting for a Skubal trade now face a scenario where their top pitching target may not be available. If the deadline passes with Skubal in Detroit, the contenders who planned around him will need to pivot quickly to whatever remains on the market.

What's Next

The next two weeks will determine how this ends. If the Tigers lose six of their next eight, the calculus reverts and a trade becomes more likely. If they keep winning at the July pace, Skubal stays and the deadline's biggest story closes without a transaction. Ballpark Banter will track every development through August 3.
