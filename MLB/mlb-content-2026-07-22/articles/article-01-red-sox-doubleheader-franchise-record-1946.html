Two Games. Two Milestones. The Red Sox Franchise Record Is One Win Away.

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 22, 2026

The Boston Red Sox did not plan for this. A thunderstorm over Fenway Park on Tuesday night postponed their game against the Baltimore Orioles and rescheduled it as a split doubleheader on Wednesday. The franchise record they were already chasing is now available twice in the same day.

Boston enters Wednesday with 14 consecutive wins — the longest active winning streak in Major League Baseball and one of the most sustained stretches of dominance in franchise history. A win in Game 1 this afternoon ties the 1946 team record at 15. A win in Game 2 tonight breaks it outright at 16. The rainout was a delay. Inadvertently, it became the best possible setup.

The Record They're Chasing

The 1946 Red Sox won 15 consecutive games on the way to the American League pennant. That team featured Ted Williams, Johnny Pesky, Bobby Doerr, and Dom DiMaggio — a lineup that won the AL pennant and went to Game 7 of the World Series. It is the standard the franchise has lived under for 80 years.

The 2026 Red Sox have spent the last two weeks methodically approaching it. They have outpitched, outhit, and outlasted 14 consecutive opponents to get here. Now, through no planning of their own, they get two separate cracks at two separate milestones on the same afternoon-evening.

How Good This Team Has Been

The numbers during the 14-game streak define the run as one of the most complete stretches of pitching in recent franchise memory. Boston's staff has posted a 1.98 ERA across the winning streak. The bullpen is 7-0 with a 1.15 ERA and 8 saves — essentially untouchable over a two-week span.

The comparison point tells the full story. The 2018 Red Sox won 108 games — the most in franchise history. They never strung together more than 10 consecutive wins in that entire season. The 2026 team has already exceeded that by four games and is playing for two more.

This streak began after manager Alex Cora was fired in late April. Whatever dysfunction accompanied that decision has been replaced by the most cohesive pitching performance Boston has put together in a generation.

What Wednesday Looks Like

Game 1 of the split doubleheader is this afternoon. The opponent is the same Baltimore Orioles team that was rained out Tuesday. A win ties the 1946 record and forces Fenway to decide how to celebrate a milestone before Game 2 starts.

Game 2 is tonight. A win there breaks the record outright and makes this the longest winning streak in 120-plus years of Red Sox baseball. The 1946 team needed a full season to set the mark. The 2026 team has a chance to surpass it in the same day.

No Boston team has ever had two cracks at two different franchise milestones on the same calendar date. The rainout accidentally created something the schedule could never have planned.
