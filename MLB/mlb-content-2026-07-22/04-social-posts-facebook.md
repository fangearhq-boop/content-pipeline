### STORY 1: Red Sox Doubleheader — Two Cracks at Franchise History

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

The Boston Red Sox enter Wednesday with a 14-game winning streak and something they didn't plan for: two games instead of one.

Tuesday's game against the Baltimore Orioles at Fenway was postponed due to thunderstorms. It was rescheduled as a split doubleheader today. That means Boston now has two separate cracks at two separate milestones on the same day.

Game 1 this afternoon: a win ties the franchise record at 15 consecutive victories, matching the 1946 squad that featured Ted Williams, Johnny Pesky, Bobby Doerr, and Dom DiMaggio — a team that went to the World Series.

Game 2 this evening: a win breaks it outright. 16 in a row would be the longest winning streak in Red Sox history. Period.

During this 14-game streak, Boston's pitchers have posted a 1.98 ERA. The bullpen is 7-0 with a 1.15 ERA. The 2018 Red Sox — who won 108 games — never strung together more than 10 consecutive wins. This team has already blown past that.

The rainout was a delay. It accidentally became the best possible setup.

### STORY 2: Nolan Arenado 2,000th Hit

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

On July 20, Nolan Arenado hit a double against the Oakland Athletics in Phoenix for his 2,000th career hit.

The milestone placed him in a six-player club in baseball history: players with 2,000 or more hits, 350 or more home runs, and 10 or more Gold Gloves. The other five members of that club are Ken Griffey Jr., Willie Mays, Mike Schmidt, Johnny Bench, and Al Kaline.

Every single one of them is in the Hall of Fame.

Arenado finishes his 2,000th-hit milestone at .280/.337/.503 with 366 home runs, 1,228 RBI, 10 Gold Gloves, 5 Silver Sluggers, and 8 All-Star selections. He is the only third baseman in the club. He is the only active player.

Mike Schmidt — whom Arenado considers the greatest third baseman ever — was the first to say publicly that he loved how Arenado played the game. Standing next to Schmidt in this club now means something specific.

There is no more complete statistical argument for Nolan Arenado's Hall of Fame case than the six names in that club and what they all have in common.

### STORY 3: Ohtani Knee — Bullpen Session This Weekend, Cy Young at Risk

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

Shohei Ohtani will not pitch for at least another week to 10 days.

Manager Dave Roberts confirmed Wednesday that the Dodgers won't consider putting Ohtani back in the rotation until at least that window passes. The plan is for Ohtani to throw a bullpen session to test the knee around July 25-26. He is not on the injured list and continues to serve as the designated hitter.

Before the left knee inflammation forced him out, Ohtani was 8-2 with a 1.79 ERA and 95 strikeouts in 85.2 innings. He was selected to his sixth consecutive All-Star team but missed the game. He has now missed at least two scheduled starts this month.

The Cy Young argument was building. Now it's stalled. Every inning Ohtani sits out is an inning that goes to Cristopher Sanchez of the Phillies, the Brewers' staff, or whoever else is making starts in the NL.

The Dodgers are still 64-38 — the best record in the National League. They won Tuesday 2-1 behind seven innings from starter Bobby Wrobleski and a Max Muncy two-run home run. The team is fine. The question is whether Ohtani comes back in time to rebuild the case.

That answer comes this weekend.

### STORY 4: Skubal Trade Flip — The Biggest Deadline Story Just Changed

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

The biggest trade-deadline narrative of the summer has flipped.

Tarik Skubal was, as recently as a week ago, the most coveted pitcher at the deadline. Multiple contenders — the Dodgers, Cubs, Braves, and Rays — were engaged. The price was described as "outrageous" by rival executives. The question wasn't whether he'd be traded. It was who could afford the prospect cost.

Now the question is whether he's traded at all.

The "growing sense" among rival executives, per The Athletic's Ken Rosenthal, is that the Detroit Tigers will hold Skubal through the August 3 deadline and attempt a genuine playoff push. Detroit is 10-4 in July and 20-12 since June 1 — the best record in the American League over that span. The Tigers are within 4.5 to 6.5 games of an AL Wild Card spot in a historically weak American League. Skubal himself wants the organization to be buyers.

With 12 days until the deadline, the biggest deal of the summer might not happen. That is now the story.

### STORY 5: White Sox Lose Kyle Teel — The Rebuild Gets Its First Real Test

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

The Chicago White Sox have placed catcher Kyle Teel on the 10-day injured list with a left high ankle sprain. His projected recovery is 6 to 12 weeks — almost certainly the remainder of the regular season. Edgar Quero has been called up as the replacement.

Teel's absence removes a key offensive piece from a lineup that has been one of the best stories in baseball in 2026. The White Sox enter Wednesday 52-46, leading the AL Central.

Two seasons ago, this franchise went 121-41 — the worst record in major league baseball history. Last year they were 60-102. This year they lead their division in late July.

Colson Montgomery continues to make history at the plate — he is the first player ever to hit 20 or more home runs within his first 275 at-bats in each of his first two major-league seasons, and he now has 21 on the year.

Teel was part of what made this lineup work. He's not coming back this year. Now the question is whether the rebuild is deep enough to absorb the loss, or whether this is where the good story gets complicated.
