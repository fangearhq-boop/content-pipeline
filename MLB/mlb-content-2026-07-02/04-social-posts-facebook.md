### STORY 1: Cubs 23-3 Padres — Swanson 8 RBI, Suzuki 100th HR

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Tuesday night at Wrigley produced one of the wildest box scores of the 2026 season.

Cubs 23, Padres 3. Eight Chicago home runs. A final score that looked like a football game.

Dansby Swanson did something that almost never happens in baseball: he hit three home runs — including a grand slam — and drove in eight runs while batting ninth in the order. Eight RBIs from the 9-hole is the second most in MLB history by any player in that lineup spot. Back-to-back multi-homer games for Swanson, the first Cubs player to do that since Patrick Wisdom in August 2021.

Seiya Suzuki hit his 100th career MLB home run. He is the fourth Japanese-born player in major league history to reach that milestone, joining Shohei Ohtani, Hideki Matsui, and Ichiro Suzuki. The four of them now form a group in the record books that will not have another member added easily.

Michael Conforto, Pete Crow-Armstrong, and Michael Busch also went deep, helping the Cubs tie their franchise record of eight home runs in a single game. The 20-run winning margin was the largest in a Chicago home game since 1977.

The Cubs (49-38) extended their winning streak to five games and completed a sweep of the Padres (43-42), who came in close enough to a .500 record to believe they were heading somewhere. 🏟️

### STORY 3: deGrom Hits 100 MPH Again — Rangers Have a Second-Half Ace

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

For a long stretch, the question with Jacob deGrom was not whether he was great — it was whether great was possible anymore.

Multiple arm injuries. Long stretches on the injured list. Mechanics adjustments that came with the territory of someone rebuilding a delivery under constraint. The velocity that made deGrom one of the most feared starters in baseball was there in glimpses, but the 100+ mph readings that defined his prime had not shown up consistently since April 2023.

On July 1 in Cleveland, they were back.

deGrom threw five pitches above 100 mph against the Guardians, topping out at 100.5 mph — his fastest pitch since April 23, 2023. He gave up a two-run homer to Kyle Manzardo in the first inning, and then proceeded to retire 18 of the next 19 batters he faced. Final line: 7 innings, 2 earned runs, 4 hits, 0 walks, 9 strikeouts. Rangers won 4-2.

His 2026 season numbers — 7-5, 3.48 ERA, 0.99 WHIP, 115 strikeouts — are the production of a very good pitcher. But the velocity reading is the sign of something more. A deGrom who is throwing 100 mph into the seventh inning is not just very good. He is the version who won back-to-back Cy Young awards with the Mets and looked, for a time, like the best pitcher alive.

The Rangers have won four of his last five starts. The August 3 trade deadline is still over a month away. They don't need to make any moves to improve their rotation. The move is already on the mound. 🏟️

### STORY 5: The White Sox Are Leading the AL Central — And That's Not a Typo

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

Take a moment with this: the Chicago White Sox are currently leading the AL Central.

In 2024, the White Sox went 41-121 — one of the worst single-season records in modern major league history. The season was a generational low point for a franchise with an 11-time All-Star in the building and a fan base that had endured years of a rebuild that seemed to have no bottom.

In 2026, the White Sox are approximately 41-37. They lead the Cleveland Guardians by about a game in a division race that has been closer than most preseason models expected.

The turnaround has multiple contributors, but one stands out: Munetaka Murakami, the international signing from Japan, who hit seven home runs in his first 21 MLB games. No Japanese-born player in MLB history had hit that many through 21 games — surpassing the five that Shohei Ohtani managed through the same stretch.

The White Sox recently won a walk-off game against the Guardians 6-5, then split the remainder of a crucial series. They have not been swept away. They have not faded. As of early July, the team that seemed incapable of winning is leading a division.

Whether this holds matters less than what it represents: one of the most dramatic single-season turnarounds in recent memory is quietly unfolding in the middle of the American League. 🏟️
