The Skubal Market Has a Price: Tigers Set Their Ask, Three Teams Are In

By Marcus Cole July 2, 2026

The Market Is Real

With 32 days remaining until the August 3 trade deadline, the Tarik Skubal trade market has moved from speculation to specifics. The Detroit Tigers are listening to offers. Multiple reports this week have detailed both the structure of the Tigers' ask and the field of teams capable of meeting it. This is no longer a hypothetical. The conversations are happening.

Skubal is 4-4 with a 3.15 ERA through the first half of 2026 — solid production, slightly below the level that earned him back-to-back American League Cy Young Awards in 2024 and 2025. He is a free agent after the season. If the Tigers do not contend or commit to contending, trading him now maximizes their return. That calculus is what is driving the market.

What the Tigers Want

The Tigers' reported asking price, per CBS Sports and ESPN: one elite prospect at the top of a team's system, plus two to three additional pieces that should ideally include an MLB-ready starting pitcher and a power bat. It is a significant return requirement — appropriate for a player of Skubal's profile, but a price that immediately narrows the field of teams that can afford it in prospects rather than just dollars.

A two-time Cy Young winner in his age-28 season, with 32 days left before his last realistic trade window before free agency, commands that kind of market. Teams that miss on him cannot revisit the conversation. They either pay the price in July or they go into the second half with what they already have.

The Four Teams Ahead of the Field

The Dodgers, Yankees, Blue Jays, and Padres are the most frequently cited destinations with the combination of prospect depth and payroll flexibility to meet Detroit's ask.

The Dodgers have the deepest farm system in baseball and the financial resources to absorb Skubal's salary in both the current season and potential extension conversations. Adding a two-time Cy Young winner to a rotation already built around sustained playoff contention is a straightforward case to make. The Yankees have rotation needs and a system that has developed tradeable pieces at multiple levels. The Blue Jays are in a window with aging core pieces and a roster built to contend now — Skubal addresses their biggest need directly. The Padres have made aggressive deadline moves before and would again if the right piece becomes available.

Skubal's own public statement adds urgency to the timeline. He told reporters that Tigers management needs to demonstrate the team is World Series-caliber or "it all comes to an abrupt end." That is a pitcher signaling clearly that he will leave in free agency without a convincing organizational commitment — which means the Tigers have no middle option. They are either contending or trading him.

What's Next

August 3 is the hard deadline. Trade conversations typically accelerate in the final two weeks — the week of July 20 through August 3 is when most deals actually close, as teams finish their All-Star break evaluation and push into serious negotiation. The Skubal market will be one of the defining narratives of the deadline. Whether the Dodgers, Yankees, Blue Jays, or another team with the pieces to pay the price moves first is the question that will consume the second half of the month.
