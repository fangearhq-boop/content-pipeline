All-Star Voting Closes at Noon — Rosters July 6, Derby on Netflix July 13

By Jake Torres July 2, 2026

The Deadline Is Today

Phase 2 of the 2026 MLB All-Star ballot closes at noon ET today, July 2. The two-phase voting process began with Phase 1 determining the top vote-getters at each position, with Phase 2 pitting those leaders against one another in a final round of fan balloting. At noon today, the voting closes and the official selection process finishes its fan-driven component.

Full rosters — including the All-Star Game starters, reserves, and pitching selections — will be announced July 6. The 96th Midsummer Classic is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, the first All-Star Game in the city since 1996. Citizens Bank Park also hosts the Home Run Derby on July 13 and the Futures Game on July 12.

Who Is Already Locked In

Ernie Clement of the Toronto Blue Jays and Shohei Ohtani of the Los Angeles Dodgers are locked into starting positions as a result of Phase 1 results. Both received the highest vote totals in their respective leagues and have automatic All-Star starting berths regardless of Phase 2 outcomes.

The tightest Phase 2 competition heading into the final hours of voting was at first base in the American League, where Vladimir Guerrero Jr. and Ben Rice of the Yankees were separated by a margin that may not be clear until ballots are officially counted. Bobby Witt Jr. entered Phase 2 as the AL's leading position player by WAR at 4.3 and was a dominant vote-getter at shortstop in Phase 1.

The Home Run Derby Is on Netflix

The 2026 Home Run Derby will be broadcast exclusively on Netflix, airing July 13 starting at 8 PM ET. The move to a streaming-only platform for the Derby represents a significant rights deal for MLB and continues the league's strategy of diversifying its media footprint beyond traditional broadcast and cable.

Kyle Schwarber — the defending Derby champion and the current MLB home run leader with 30 through 84 games — told Sports Illustrated he intends to compete under two conditions: he makes the All-Star roster (a near-certainty given his production) and a back issue settles down in the next 11 days. Schwarber set a Phillies franchise record by reaching 30 home runs in 84 games, breaking Mike Schmidt's 1979 mark of 87 games. He is on pace to challenge the 60 home run threshold if he maintains anything close to his current rate through the second half.

The Significance of Philadelphia

The 2026 All-Star Game at Citizens Bank Park arrives at a moment of genuine organizational momentum for the Phillies. Philadelphia hosts the Derby and the Game with Schwarber as its most visible candidate and a roster built around postseason contention. The last Midsummer Classic at Citizens Bank Park — and in the city — was 30 years ago. The city's baseball appetite has been significant since the 2022 and 2023 postseason runs, and a full All-Star Weekend at Citizens Bank Park is the kind of event the franchise has positioned itself for.

What's Next

Rosters are announced July 6. The Futures Game is July 12. The Home Run Derby on Netflix is July 13. The All-Star Game itself is July 14. For the next 12 days, the competitive baseball calendar pauses in its most formal sense, but the trade deadline storylines — Skubal, the AL standings, the NL wild card race — continue to build underneath the All-Star Weekend coverage. By July 15, the second half is underway with 32 days remaining until the August 3 trade deadline.
