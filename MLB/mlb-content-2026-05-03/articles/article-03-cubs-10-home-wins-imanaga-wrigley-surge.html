10 Straight at Wrigley: How Imanaga and the Cubs Have Become One of Baseball's Best Teams

By Ryan Calloway | May 3, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Chicago Cubs are a dangerous baseball team right now, and the evidence is piling up. Saturday's 2-0 shutout of Arizona was their 10th consecutive home win at Wrigley Field — the longest such streak at the ballpark since 2008 — and pushed the Cubs to 21-12 on the season. They have gone 14-3 since April 14. They are playing the kind of baseball that playoff teams play in October, and they are doing it in May.

Shota Imanaga is the reason.

The Ace the Cubs Have Waited For

Imanaga lowered his ERA to 2.40 after Saturday's gem — seven scoreless innings, four hits, one walk, five strikeouts. It was his second scoreless appearance of six-plus innings this season. When the Cubs need to stop a slide, win a series, or send a message, Imanaga is the pitcher who gets the ball.

His value goes beyond individual starts. A true No. 1 starter changes how a rotation is constructed, how a manager manages a bullpen, and how an opponent prepares. Imanaga has done all of that for Chicago. He gives the Cubs a margin for error they did not have before, and he consistently delivers on the big-game stage.

The Diamondbacks, sitting at 0-for-the-day in terms of Ildemaro Vargas finally getting a hit, were the latest team to be completely neutralized. Ian Happ's three-hit day (homer, triple, double) provided just enough offense, and Imanaga made it look like more than enough.

A Division Where Everyone is Watching

The NL Central is a mess in the best possible way. All five teams entered May above .500 — a historical rarity in the divisional era. The Cubs are the hottest team in the division right now, but the Cardinals (powered by Jordan Walker's breakout), the Reds (who lead the standings), and the rest are all within striking distance.

Chicago's home record has become a genuine weapon. Ten straight wins at Wrigley means the Cubs go into every home series as a threat to extend the streak. The Wrigley crowd is loud, the Cubs' pitching staff is deep and capable, and Imanaga at the top makes it feel like Chicago belongs in any conversation about the NL's best teams.

What's Next

The Cubs (21-12) will look to maintain their momentum with more home games on the schedule and Imanaga anchoring the rotation. The NL Central race is still wide open with all five teams competing, and the next few weeks will start to separate contenders from pretenders. If Chicago can build on this 10-game home win streak and Imanaga continues pitching like an ace, the Cubs will be a factor in this race all the way to October.
