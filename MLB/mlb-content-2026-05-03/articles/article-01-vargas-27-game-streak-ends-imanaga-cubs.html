Imanaga and Cubs End Vargas' 27-Game Hitting Streak, Blank Diamondbacks 2-0

By Marcus Cole | May 3, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The streak is over. Ildemaro Vargas went 0-for-4 on Saturday, and Shota Imanaga made sure there was no chance of survival, throwing seven scoreless innings as the Chicago Cubs blanked the Arizona Diamondbacks 2-0 at Wrigley Field. The 27-game hitting streak that captivated baseball — a new record for Venezuela-born players — ended quietly in the eighth inning with a groundout against Ben Brown.

Imanaga was brilliant from first pitch to last: four hits, one walk, five strikeouts, 87 pitches. He lowered his ERA to 2.40. Ian Happ did the rest offensively, going 3-for-4 with a home run, a triple, and a double — accounting for both Cubs runs and, frankly, most of the action on a cool May evening in Chicago.

A Streak Worth Remembering

Vargas' streak will not fade quickly from memory. His 24-game run to open the 2026 season was the second-longest season-opening hitting streak since 1940, trailing only Ron LeFlore's 30-game start for Detroit in 1976. When you add three games from the end of the 2025 season, the overall streak reaches 27 — the longest by any Venezuela-born player in MLB history.

During the streak, Vargas raised his batting average to a major league-high .404. He led the NL in batting average and ranked among the league's best in slugging percentage and OPS. He passed Paul Goldschmidt for second place in Diamondbacks franchise history for consecutive games with a hit. He made the world stop and pay attention to a utility infielder who had spent years as a journeyman backup.

In baseball, streaks end. They always end. But they leave something behind, and Vargas' 27 games have left an imprint that will not disappear by the morning.

Cubs on a Roll

The Cubs needed neither streaks nor drama to win Saturday — they had Imanaga, and that was enough. Saturday marked Chicago's 10th consecutive home win at Wrigley, the longest such streak at the North Side ballpark since 2008. The Cubs improved to 21-12 overall and have gone 14-3 since April 14, one of the best marks in baseball over that stretch.

The win came at the expense of a Diamondbacks team that dropped its third straight game. Arizona is still searching for the consistency it showed earlier this spring, while Chicago has found a rhythm that feels sustainable. Imanaga is a true No. 1 starter — the kind of arm that changes how an entire season feels. When he's on, the Cubs look like a different team.

What's Next

The Cubs (21-12) continue to build one of the more compelling cases in the NL Central. With all five division teams above .500, every win matters — and Chicago has been collecting them at a remarkable rate. Vargas and the Diamondbacks will look to regroup as Arizona tries to rediscover form in a crowded NL West. The Cubs and Imanaga, meanwhile, will keep showing up at Wrigley and making it very difficult for visitors.
