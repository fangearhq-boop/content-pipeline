Judge and Rice Join Mantle and Berra in the Yankees Record Book

By Jake Torres | May 3, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The New York Yankees have a Mickey Mantle and Yogi Berra problem — in the best possible way. Aaron Judge and Ben Rice have become only the second pair of Yankees teammates to each hit 10 or more home runs before the team's 30th game, joining the legendary 1956 pair in a piece of franchise history that should not be possible to step into lightly.

Yet here they are. Judge with 12 home runs, an OPS of 1.010, a batting average of .252, and 19 RBIs. Rice with 10-plus home runs, an OPS of 1.192, and a batting average of .322 that leads most of the American League. The Yankees are 19-10 and have looked, at times, like the most dangerous lineup in the sport.

Judge's Path to 400

Judge entered the 2026 season needing approximately 32 home runs to reach 400 career. He has hit 12 in roughly 30 games. At his current pace, that 400th home run could arrive before the end of summer — a milestone that would cement his legacy as one of the greatest power hitters in franchise history and one of the best of his generation.

His OPS above 1.000 through April and into May suggests Judge is not simply a home run hitter grinding toward a counting milestone — he is performing at an elite level across the board. The Yankees need that from him, and he has delivered it consistently since the first day of the season.

The Rice Revelation

Ben Rice was not supposed to be this. The 24-year-old first baseman was expected to be a useful depth piece, a left-handed bat off the bench, a player who could fill in without embarrassing himself. Instead, he is hitting .322 with an OPS near 1.200 and has emerged as a genuine cleanup threat in the Yankees lineup.

Rice's emergence changes how opposing managers approach the New York order. You can no longer pitch around Judge to get to a weaker hitter — there is no easy out in this lineup. That forces pitchers to confront Judge, and Judge is very good at taking advantage when pitchers have no choice but to attack him.

The comparison to Mantle and Berra is obviously generous in terms of historical weight. But the statistical milestone — two teammates, 10-plus home runs each, before the 30th game — is identical. The 2026 Yankees have earned their place in that sentence.

What's Next

The Yankees (19-10) sit atop the AL East and are building the kind of first-half résumé that earns favorable postseason positioning. Judge and Rice will continue to get the most attention, but this is a deep and well-constructed roster top to bottom. As the season moves into May and beyond, the question is whether they can sustain this pace — or whether the AL East competition will tighten the race. Either way, the team that comes out of New York looking like this in April is one to take seriously in October.
