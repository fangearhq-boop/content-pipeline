### STORY 2: Schwarber HR Race

**FB Long-Form Post**
Window: 10:30 AM ET

The 60-home run club has exactly five members. Roger Maris did it in 1961 (61). Mark McGwire did it in 1998 (70) and 1999 (65). Sammy Sosa did it in 1998 (66), 1999 (64), and 2001 (63). Barry Bonds did it in 2001 (73). Aaron Judge did it in 2022 (62). That's it. That's the entire list.

Kyle Schwarber, as of June 11, 2026, has 23 home runs and the clearest path to that list of anyone in baseball.

Schwarber leads MLB with 23 home runs. Yordan Alvarez is second at 22. The next significant competition — Aaron Judge and Munetaka Murakami — are both on the injured list. Schwarber is pacing for a home run total that would make him the sixth player in history to reach 60. And he's doing it with a ferocity that makes every single at-bat worth watching.

He hit his 20th home run on May 15 — the earliest date anyone has ever reached that milestone in MLB history. That record alone tells you how fast this season has started for him. The question now is sustainability. Can he maintain this rate through the summer heat, through the grind of late June and July, through the dog days that separate the contenders from the pretenders in any single season?

The history says maintaining 60+ pace is extraordinarily difficult. Bonds had the peak of peaks. McGwire had the help. Maris had Roger Maris. These were rare, once-in-a-generation seasons. But Schwarber is in the conversation now, in real time, and that's something worth paying attention to.

Watch him tonight. Watch him tomorrow. Watch every game he plays. The 60-HR race has a real protagonist.
