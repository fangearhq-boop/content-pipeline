Thursday MLB Roundup — Yankees Win, Cardinals Hold, Athletics Blank Astros

By Jake Torres | June 11, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Thursday's MLB slate had four games worth discussing, and all four delivered something meaningful about where the 2026 season stands at this juncture. Here's the full picture.

Yankees Over Red Sox — The Judge-Less Machine Keeps Running

Cody Bellinger hit a solo home run. Jazz Chisholm Jr. added another. The New York Yankees beat the Boston Red Sox on Thursday, continuing to demonstrate a level of resilience that has surprised some observers who assumed Judge's IL absence would stall their season.

The Yankees are 40-26, sitting at or near the top of the AL East. Judge is expected to return in mid-to-late July from a rib fracture, and the bridge players have done their jobs. Bellinger, signed in the offseason as a complementary piece, has provided timely offense. Chisholm, whose energy and athleticism make him one of the more entertaining players in the AL, continues to produce. The Yankees look like a team that will be better when Judge returns — not a team that needs him just to function.

Cardinals 5, Reds 3 — NL Central Watch

St. Louis beat Cincinnati 5-3 in a game that has real implications for a division race that hasn't been fully decided. The Cardinals are positioning themselves as a team capable of staying competitive through the summer. The Reds, who have had an interesting 2026 season featuring several statistical anomalies (including a .203 batting average that somehow coexists with a 10-0 record in one-run games), dropped a game they needed.

The NL Central is one of the more interesting divisions in baseball right now, with no team separating itself decisively. The Cardinals' win keeps them in the conversation.

Athletics 5, Astros 0 — Gage Jump Delivers

Oakland's Gage Jump threw 6.1 scoreless innings in a 5-0 shutout of Houston. That performance matters for a few reasons. First, it's a quality start against a team that can hit. Second, it continues to demonstrate that the Athletics, long a punchline in AL West conversations this decade, have developed some legitimate pitching. Jump's performance was efficient, his command was sharp, and the zero in the Astros' column at the end of the night felt earned rather than lucky.

The Astros' inconsistency this season has been one of the less-heralded stories in the American League. Houston has not been the dominant force it was for much of the previous decade, and nights like Thursday against Oakland are evidence of that change.

What's Next

Friday is the big day: Tarik Skubal makes his highly anticipated return to the Tigers rotation at Cleveland, which will be the most-watched game on the MLB schedule. The standings picture continues to crystallize. The Braves lead the NL. The Yankees lead the AL East. The AL West remains genuinely competitive with the Mariners, Angels, Astros, and Rangers all involved. The midpoint of the season is approaching, and the separation between contenders and non-contenders will sharpen over the coming weeks.
