Angels Rout Dodgers 13-5 — Halos Close Season Series With Statement Win

By Marcus Cole | June 11, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Los Angeles Angels had a simple message for the defending World Series champions on Thursday night: we can beat you, and we can do it by a lot.

The Angels defeated the Los Angeles Dodgers 13-5 on Thursday, closing out the season series between the two Southern California franchises on a decisive note. The margin wasn't close. The performance wasn't ambiguous. The Halos put up 13 runs against the team that won the 2025 World Series, and the final score made for the kind of headline that gets attention around the league.

Mike Trout contributed in the win, as he has done when healthy throughout a season in which the former three-time AL MVP continues to demonstrate that, at whatever stage of his career this is, he is still one of the most dangerous offensive players in baseball when he steps into the box.

What the Win Means

Context matters here. The Dodgers are around 42-24 — they are still one of the better teams in baseball, with a deep rotation and the kind of roster depth that wins series over the long haul. A single 13-5 loss does not change that. But it does reveal something about the Dodgers' vulnerability on a night when their pitching wasn't sharp, and it does say something about what the Angels are capable of when their offense clicks.

The Angels are not a playoff team right now. But they are not a team to be dismissed either. A lineup that can put up 13 runs in a single game — against a quality opponent — has real offensive firepower. And in a division that features the Mariners, Astros, and Rangers all competing for position, the Angels' ability to beat teams like the Dodgers in head-to-head action could have indirect implications for the AL West standings as the season progresses.

The Dodgers' Perspective

From the Dodgers' side, Thursday was a game to forget. Getting beaten 13-5 in an interleague game against your city rival is embarrassing, and teams of the Dodgers' caliber are evaluated over 162 games, not individual performances. But the pitching questions that have surfaced throughout the 2026 season — including concerns about rotation depth — were on display in this game. The Dodgers have addressed some of those issues with bullpen moves and minor additions, but Thursday suggested there is still work to do.

The Dodgers will shake this off. They always do. The organization is too well-run and too deep in talent to spiral from a bad Thursday night in June. But the Angels served notice that the crosstown series, for at least one night, belonged to Anaheim.

What's Next

The Angels and Dodgers do not meet again in the regular season after closing out this series. For the Angels, the focus returns to the AL West and the question of whether this team can build momentum toward a competitive second half. For the Dodgers, it's back to the grind of the NL West race. For Trout, every game is a reminder that one of the greatest players of his generation still has something to say. Thursday, he said it loudly.
