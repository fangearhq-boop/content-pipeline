15. Not 16. The Orioles End the Red Sox Win Streak Before History Could Be Fully Rewritten.

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 23, 2026

The Red Sox needed two games on Wednesday to break the franchise record. They got one.

Boston beat the Baltimore Orioles 6-3 in Game 1 of the doubleheader at Fenway Park, extending their win streak to 15 games and tying the franchise record set in 1946 — the longest winning streak in Red Sox history before this week. Then the Orioles returned the favor in Game 2, winning 5-1 and ending the streak before it could become 16.

The Red Sox's 15-game win streak is over. The 1946 franchise record still stands — tied now, not broken. The season, however, is a different story.

What the Streak Did

When Boston began the streak, they were 37-48. A team in familiar territory for Red Sox fans in recent years — mid-July, buried in the standings, waiting for September to arrive. The streak moved them to 52-49. That is a 15-game swing in 15 days, and it pushed a team that had been 11 games under .500 to three games over it.

The Wild Card picture looks different at 52-49 than it did at 37-48. The Red Sox are not the favorites to make the postseason. But they are back in a conversation that didn't exist for them a month ago, and that conversation is entirely a product of what happened between the day the streak started and Wednesday afternoon at Fenway.

How It Ended

Game 1 gave Boston exactly what they needed. A 6-3 win, the streak reaches 15, the record is tied, and the 1946 squad with Ted Williams, Johnny Pesky, Bobby Doerr, and Dom DiMaggio has company in the franchise history books. Whatever happened in Game 2 wouldn't change that.

Then Game 2 happened. The Orioles scored five runs and held Boston to one. The Red Sox couldn't find the offense that had carried them through 15 wins. The streak ended cleanly — not because Boston collapsed, but because the Orioles played better on that afternoon in Game 2.

A 15-game win streak is still historically rare. It's the longest Red Sox streak since 2006. The fact that it ended one game short of outright franchise record status doesn't diminish what the team accomplished over those 15 wins. It just means the number in the books is 15 and not 16.

The Streak That Defined a Season

The 2018 Red Sox, who won 108 games and the World Series, had a 10-game winning streak as their longest of that season. This 2026 team matched that and then went 50 percent further. That is not a meaningless comparison. It says something about the quality of pitching and consistent offense that carried this team through mid-July.

The team ERA during the streak was 1.98. The bullpen went 7-0 with a 1.15 ERA in streak games. These are dominant performances sustained over a two-week stretch that doesn't happen by accident. The Red Sox pitched their way through a franchise-record-tying run. That rotation and bullpen combination will have to carry them through August if they are going to make the Wild Card case stick.

What's Next

The Red Sox are 52-49 and in the AL Wild Card picture after their 15-game win streak ended Wednesday. They face Baltimore today in the series finale. The trade deadline is August 3. Whether Boston acts as a buyer or seller over the next 11 days will depend on how they define their second half — a team that went 15-0 in mid-July, or a team at 52-49 that still has ground to make up.
