Kyle Teel Is Out. The White Sox Are Still Leading the AL Central. The Rebuild Keeps Going.

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 23, 2026

Kyle Teel is the player the Chicago White Sox's rebuild pointed toward. A top catching prospect who arrived earlier this season and immediately started making the team better. The proof of concept for a front office that made a calculated decision to lose — spectacularly, historically — in 2024 in order to have the assets and draft capital to compete by 2026.

Teel suffered a left high ankle sprain earlier this week while applying a tag in the second inning. He's on the injured list. He's expected to miss six to twelve weeks. His 2026 season is likely over.

The White Sox are still leading the AL Central.

The Quero Option

The organization's response to losing Teel was to call up 22-year-old Edgar Quero from Triple-A Charlotte. Quero is a switch-hitter who came up through the same development pipeline that produced Teel. He has not played an extended stretch at the major league level, but he is in Chicago because the system produced enough depth that the replacement isn't a placeholder from outside the organization — it's another prospect who was supposed to be here eventually.

That distinction matters for how the team evaluates the next two months. Losing Teel is a significant blow to what was working. But the White Sox are not scrambling on the open market for a journeyman to fill the position. They are deploying the next player in a line that the rebuild produced.

The Rebuild's Arc

In 2024, the Chicago White Sox went 41-121. That was the worst record in Major League Baseball history — worse than the 1962 Mets, worse than every expansion team's first season, worse than any of the other historically bad teams. It was a planned tear-down. The organization stripped the roster to its studs, accumulated top picks, and started over.

In 2025, the White Sox went 60-102. Still bad, but the trajectory shifted. Players who would eventually matter were coming through the system. The losing had a purpose that the 2024 season telegraphed.

In 2026, the White Sox are 52-49. AL Central leaders. With the second-best run differential in the American League.

Colson Montgomery is hitting 21 home runs this season, becoming the first player in MLB history to record 20 or more home runs in his first 275 at-bats in each of his first two major league seasons. That is the kind of statistic that makes the rebuild real rather than aspirational. Montgomery was in the pipeline the same way Teel and Quero were. He is producing in ways that franchise players produce.

What the AL Central Lead Means Now

The White Sox lead the Cleveland Guardians by one game in the AL Central. The margin is thin. The division has been competitive all season. Without Teel behind the plate, the team's offensive and defensive profile shifts. Quero is a capable replacement, but he is not yet proven at this level for an extended stretch.

The next month will tell the real story of this rebuild's first legitimately competitive season. A team that holds the AL Central lead despite losing its emerging star catcher to a season-ending injury is a team with genuine depth. A team that fades when Teel goes down would suggest the foundation is shallower than it appears.

The White Sox have a six-week sample size to add to the thesis.

What's Next

Edgar Quero takes over as the White Sox's primary catcher with Kyle Teel on the IL for six to twelve weeks. The White Sox lead the AL Central by one game over Cleveland. The trade deadline is August 3 — whether the organization acts as a buyer at this deadline will be one of the clearest signals yet about how confident the front office is in this team's ability to compete in October.
