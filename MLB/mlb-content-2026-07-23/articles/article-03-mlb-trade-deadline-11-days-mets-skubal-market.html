August 3 Is 11 Days Away. The Trade Market Is Heating Up and No One Knows What Happens With Skubal.

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | July 23, 2026

The MLB trade deadline is August 3 at 6 PM ET. Eleven days from today.

The early action is already done. The Kansas City Royals acquired right-hander Nate Pearson from the Houston Astros on July 22. The Dodgers picked up righty Seth Halvorsen from the Rockies on July 20. The Chicago Cubs grabbed Aaron Civale from Oakland on July 18. These are the kinds of moves that set up the roster architecture before the big-name deals come through. Teams that know what they need in October are filling the gaps while the headline names are still being negotiated.

The headline name, in case you haven't been paying attention, is Tarik Skubal.

The Skubal Situation

The Detroit Tigers ace is in the final year of his contract. The question of whether he gets traded before August 3 has been the central story of deadline season, and the answer keeps shifting depending on who you ask.

ESPN's Jeff Passan reported this week that the Tigers are "far more likely than not" to move Skubal before the deadline. That framing would have settled the debate if the reporting had stopped there. But rival executives around the league are telling other reporters a different story: the growing sense is that Detroit holds onto Skubal and makes a run at the playoffs themselves. Since June 1, the Tigers have the fourth-best record in Major League Baseball. As of this week, they're within striking distance of the AL Wild Card. Skubal has reportedly told people inside the organization that he wants to play meaningful baseball in Detroit.

The price tag is the other factor. Teams that would love to acquire Skubal — the Cubs, the Braves, the Rays, and the Dodgers are among those monitoring the situation — have seen the Tigers' asking price described as "enormous" by people close to negotiations. At that price, staying put in Detroit doesn't look like a bad decision for the organization.

The Sellers

The New York Mets have made the most dramatic shift of any team in the pre-deadline period. The Mets have moved into full sell mode, making the majority of their roster available to potential buyers. After a disappointing first half, the organization is taking the trade deadline as an opportunity to reshape the roster for next season rather than chase a long-shot postseason run.

The Los Angeles Angels are making starting pitchers Reid Detmers and Jose Soriano available. Teams looking for rotation help without paying the Skubal price have an alternative there. The Angels have been sellers in spirit all season, and making established starters available during the final week before the deadline is the last step in that process.

The Names Moving

Beyond Skubal, the players most widely expected to change teams in the next 11 days include second baseman Luis Arraez, who has been linked to playoff contenders seeking offensive upgrades, and closer Aroldis Chapman, whose velocity and experience make him an attractive target for teams with bullpen needs. Starting pitchers Sonny Gray, Sandy Alcantara, Kevin Gausman, and Joe Ryan have all appeared on reported trade lists from various contenders.

The next 11 days will determine whether Skubal stays or goes, whether the Mets find buyers for their available pieces, and whether any team acquires the kind of front-line starter that changes a postseason outlook. Last year the deals came in a rush in the final 48 hours before the deadline. There is no reason to expect anything different this time.

What's Next

The MLB trade deadline is August 3 at 6 PM ET. Tarik Skubal's status remains the centerpiece of the market. The Mets are actively seeking trade partners for their available roster pieces. Teams like the Cubs, Rays, and Braves continue to monitor the market for rotation upgrades. Eleven days is a long time in trade deadline terms — and usually not long enough.
