Chase DeLauter Has 5 HRs in His First 8 Games — One of the Most Historic Rookie Starts in Years

By Ryan Calloway | April 6, 2026

The Cleveland Guardians outfielder is batting .346 with an OPS above 1.290. The last player to hit 4 HRs in his first 3 MLB games was Trevor Story in 2016.

Trevor Story's first week with the Colorado Rockies in 2016 became one of the most referenced opening stretches in recent baseball history. Four home runs in three games. A statement so loud that it announced a new superstar before most fans had learned to spell his name correctly.

Chase DeLauter just matched it.

The Cleveland Guardians outfielder hit four home runs in his first three major league games — becoming the first player to accomplish that since Story a decade ago. Through eight games, he has five home runs, eight RBIs, a .346 batting average, and an OPS above 1.290. That last figure ranks fourth among all MLB hitters through the opening week of the season.

The Home Opener Moment

DeLauter's most impactful game of the week may have come on April 4, when the Guardians returned to Progressive Field for their home opener against the Chicago Cubs. In the seventh inning of a game Cleveland needed to win after a tough road stretch, DeLauter delivered a two-run home run to help the Guardians win 4-1.

He finished that game with three hits. It was his fifth home run in eight games and perhaps the clearest evidence yet that this is not a hot streak waiting to cool off — it is a talented player playing at the level that scouts always projected, arriving fully formed.

What Makes This Start Significant

The comparison to Story is instructive for two reasons. First, Story was historically productive in his debut stretch — so matching that production places DeLauter in elite historical company. Second, Story's career proved that opening week explosions do not always predict long-term greatness. Baseball is a long season, and DeLauter's first eight games represent less than five percent of a full year.

The more meaningful question is: what does DeLauter look like against breaking balls and pitchers who have adjusted? The league will. It always does. DeLauter's response to that adjustment will define whether this is a special season or a special start.

But there is nothing wrong with acknowledging that he has been one of the best hitters in baseball for eight games. That alone is worth celebrating.

What's Next

DeLauter and the Guardians continue their home schedule at Progressive Field, with Cleveland positioned at roughly 5-3 in the AL Central. DeLauter's at-bats will remain the most-watched in the Cleveland lineup as the season progresses, and the question of whether he can sustain the torrid pace will be one of the defining storylines of the first month of the year.
