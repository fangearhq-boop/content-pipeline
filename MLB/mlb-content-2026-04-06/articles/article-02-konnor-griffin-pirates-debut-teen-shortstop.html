Konnor Griffin's MLB Debut: RBI Double, Sellout Crowd, First Teen SS Since A-Rod in 1994

By Jake Torres | April 6, 2026

The 19-year-old Pirates prospect delivered an RBI double in his first at-bat at a sold-out PNC Park, becoming the first teenager to play shortstop in the major leagues since Alex Rodriguez debuted in 1994.

PNC Park held 38,986 people on April 3. They were there for the Pittsburgh Pirates' home opener against the Baltimore Orioles. And they were there to see Konnor Griffin.

The Pirates' top prospect — 19 years old, long considered one of the most gifted young infielders in a generation — walked to the plate for his first major league at-bat in front of the largest crowd PNC Park had seen in years. He lined an RBI double off the wall. The crowd erupted. The Pittsburgh Pirates had their first glimpse of what the future might look like.

Griffin finished the day 2-for-3 with a walk, scoring a run and driving in another. The Pirates won 5-4. ESPN described him in their postgame coverage as a "difference-maker." It was, by any measure, the most anticipated debut of the opening week.

The Historical Context

The last teenager to play shortstop in a major league game was Alex Rodriguez, who made his debut as an 18-year-old for the Seattle Mariners in 1994. That is more than three decades of baseball without a teenager manning the most demanding defensive position on the diamond at the big-league level.

Griffin is also the first Pittsburgh Pirate under 20 years old to reach base twice in a debut since Bobby Del Greco accomplished the same feat in 1952. The historical comparisons speak to the rarity of what Griffin represents — a teenage shortstop with the tools, the temperament, and apparently the skill to contribute at the major league level from the first day.

Why the Pirates Made the Call Now

The decision to start Griffin's major league clock before his 20th birthday signals that the Pirates believe he is ready — or at least ready enough that the cost of developing him in Triple-A no longer outweighs the benefit of having him on the major league roster. His offensive performance in the minors was advanced for his age, and his defensive reputation at shortstop drew comparisons to some of the game's best.

The sellout crowd for the home opener reflects a Pittsburgh fanbase hungry for reason to believe. Griffin, with an RBI double in his first at-bat and two hits in his debut game, gave them one. Whether the production continues is the question every fan will be tracking all season.

What's Next

Griffin continues his first MLB homestand at PNC Park, with the Pirates looking to build on their 5-4 home opener win. A teenager at shortstop is automatically one of the most compelling storylines in baseball. The first week of his career is already one to remember — RBI double, sellout crowd, historic age milestone. The story is just beginning.
