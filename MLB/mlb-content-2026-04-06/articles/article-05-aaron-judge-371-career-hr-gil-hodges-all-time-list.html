Aaron Judge Hits HR No. 371, Passes Gil Hodges on Baseball's All-Time List

By Jake Torres | April 6, 2026

Judge's third home run of the 2026 season moves him into sole possession of 83rd on the all-time list — passing a Hall of Famer — as the Yankees open at 6-1.

Gil Hodges played for the Brooklyn Dodgers and New York Mets across 18 big-league seasons. He was a seven-time All-Star, a two-time World Series champion, and the first baseman at the center of one of baseball's most storied dynasties. When he retired in 1963, he had hit 370 home runs — a number that stood for decades as a marker of a great power hitter's career.

Aaron Judge passed him on April 3.

Judge's 371st career home run — a slider from Miami Marlins right-hander Eury Pérez, launched into the left-field seats at Yankee Stadium in the home opener — moved him past Hodges for sole possession of 83rd on baseball's all-time list. It was Judge's third home run of the 2026 season and another entry in the lengthening list of milestones he has accumulated in a remarkable career.

The Home Opener Context

The milestone came in front of the Yankee Stadium crowd on Opening Night of the home schedule — as fitting a backdrop as Judge could have asked for. The Yankees won 8-2. Beyond Judge's homer, the game also featured one of the early defensive highlights of the season: Cody Bellinger sprinted 62 feet for a fly ball, had it pop off the heel of his glove, and then secured it with a blind 180-degree sweep. Multiple outlets called it a candidate for the best catch of 2026. It was that kind of night in the Bronx.

Judge is now 83rd all-time, one spot behind Paul Goldschmidt — his current Yankees teammate — who has 373 career home runs. If Judge continues at his recent pace, Goldschmidt's total on the all-time list may not stand for long either.

Judge's Place in the Conversation

At 33 years old and with 371 career home runs, Judge's trajectory on the all-time list depends on how many more seasons he plays at this level. The names above him in the coming years include a long list of Hall of Famers and legendary sluggers. Each one crossed in a post-August 2026 season becomes another chapter in the argument for his own Hall of Fame case — which, at this rate, is already an argument with very few counterpoints.

The Yankees entered Monday at 6-1, among the early leaders in the American League. Judge is the centerpiece. As long as he is healthy and hitting, New York looks like a legitimate AL contender.

What's Next

Judge and the Yankees continue their homestand this week. The all-time list offers plenty of names within reach. More immediately, the question of whether the Yankees can sustain their 6-1 start will be answered over the coming weeks. But for one night on April 3, a Hall of Famer's record fell — and Aaron Judge is still climbing.
