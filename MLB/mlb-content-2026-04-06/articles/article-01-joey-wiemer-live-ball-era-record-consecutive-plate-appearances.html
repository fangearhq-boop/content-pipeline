Joey Wiemer Ties Live-Ball Era Record With 10 Consecutive Plate Appearances Reaching Base

By Marcus Cole | April 6, 2026

The Washington Nationals outfielder, released by the Giants before the season, went 8-for-8 with two walks to start 2026 — tying a record dating back to 2002.

The San Francisco Giants let Joey Wiemer go before the season started. He was not part of their plans, released to make room for other pieces. He cleared waivers, signed with the Washington Nationals, and entered opening week with approximately zero national attention on him.

Then he went 8-for-8 with two walks in his first 10 plate appearances of the year.

That stretch tied Carlos Delgado's 2002 record for the longest out-free start to a season in the live-ball era — the period of baseball dating back to 1920. One hundred and six years of Major League Baseball, and only two players have reached base safely in each of their first 10 plate appearances to begin a season. Delgado was one. Wiemer is now the other.

What the Numbers Say

The statistics behind Wiemer's streak are not just impressive — they suggest the production was genuine rather than luck-driven. His exit velocity averaged over 100 mph across the streak. His hard-hit rate was 83.3%. These are not numbers that come from bloop singles and soft grounders finding holes. Wiemer was hitting the ball hard, and hard contact leads to results.

His first out came in his 11th plate appearance — a groundout to Phillies starter Taijuan Walker. By then, the record was already in the books, and the baseball world was paying attention to a player who had been an afterthought just weeks earlier.

The Unlikely Path

Wiemer's release by the Giants is the kind of organizational decision that teams make dozens of times each spring. A player does not fit the roster, the numbers do not support a spot, and they are let go. It happens constantly, and most players who clear waivers do so without incident.

What the Nationals saw when they signed Wiemer — whether it was a swing adjustment, a better approach at the plate, or simply a player who needed a change of scenery — has already paid dividends. A record-tying stretch in the first week of the season is a best-case scenario no team dares to project for a waiver claim.

What's Next

Joey Wiemer's start has put him on the national radar for the first time. The Washington Nationals, not a playoff team by most preseason projections, will be a storyline to monitor as long as Wiemer is producing at this level. The record is shared. The streak is over. And one of the more improbable stories of the young 2026 season is just getting started.
