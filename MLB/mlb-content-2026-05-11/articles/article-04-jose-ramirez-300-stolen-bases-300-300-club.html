José Ramírez Steals No. 300 — Nine Home Runs Away From Baseball's Elite 300-300 Club

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | May 11, 2026

The Cleveland Guardians third baseman became only the second player in franchise history to reach 300 career steals, and now sits nine home runs from joining one of the most exclusive groups in baseball history.

José Ramírez set his sights on a number that very few players in baseball history have ever reached — and he's nearly there.

On May 2, Ramírez swiped third base in the fifth inning of a 14-6 Cleveland Guardians win over the Athletics at Sutter Health Park, recording his 300th career stolen base. It was the AL-leading 13th steal of his 2026 season, and it made Ramírez only the second player in Cleveland franchise history to reach the milestone. Kenny Lofton was the first.

The immediate significance was clear. But the larger context made the moment historic: at the time of his 300th steal, Ramírez had 291 career home runs. Nine away from becoming the ninth member of the exclusive 300-300 club.

The Membership List

The 300-300 club — players who have hit 300 career home runs and stolen 300 career bases — is one of baseball's most selective groups. The members include Willie Mays, Bobby Bonds, André Dawson, Barry Bonds, Alex Rodriguez, Carlos Beltran, and a handful of others. It's not just a list of great players; it's a list of players who combined elite power with elite speed across a full career — a rare combination that requires both physical gifts and sustained excellence over many seasons.

Ramírez belongs in that conversation. He has been one of the most complete players of his generation at third base — a position not typically associated with power-speed combinations of this caliber. His ability to produce 20-plus home run seasons while consistently contributing on the bases makes him unusual at his position and in his era.

What He Said

Asked about the milestone after the game, Ramírez offered a characteristic response: "I set my standards a little higher than those numbers." It reflects a player who doesn't collect milestones as destinations but passes through them on the way to what he considers his actual standard — which is winning games for the Cleveland Guardians.

Guardians manager Stephen Vogt praised Ramírez after the milestone, highlighting what those 300 stolen bases mean for Cleveland's run production over the course of a season: speed on the bases creates pressure that doesn't show up in the box score but matters every game.

What's Next

Ramírez continues his pursuit of 300 career home runs across the 2026 Guardians schedule. At his current production pace, the milestone could arrive within weeks. Nine home runs from an elite player who is already at 291 career HRs is a reasonable expectation — not a guarantee, but an entirely realistic near-term target. When No. 300 arrives, Ramírez will become the ninth member of the 300-300 club and one of the more quietly celebrated milestones in baseball's recent history. The number is close. The moment is coming.
