Braves Win 6th Straight, Reach 28-13 — Atlanta Running Away With NL East Behind Elder, Olson

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | May 11, 2026

Bryce Elder struck out six in 6.2 innings and Matt Olson drove in three runs Monday night as Atlanta rolled past Washington 9-4 to extend the best streak of a dominant early season.

The Atlanta Braves won again Monday night. That's become a habit.

Bryce Elder struck out six batters in 6.2 innings and Matt Olson drove in three runs as Atlanta posted a 9-4 victory over the Washington Nationals, extending the Braves' winning streak to six games and pushing their record to 28-13 — the best mark in the National League.

This is what Atlanta's 2026 season has looked like at its best: a starting pitcher delivering quality innings, a middle-of-the-order bat making contact when it counts, and enough offensive depth to score runs even when individual contributors are off their peak. It worked against Washington. It's been working for weeks.

Elder's Efficient Night

Bryce Elder's line — 6.2 innings, 6 strikeouts — reflected a pitcher in command of a game from the first pitch. The Nationals are capable of generating offense, and Elder's ability to avoid the big inning while accumulating strikeouts speaks to the kind of pitching that wins games in a pennant race.

Elder has been a key piece of Atlanta's rotation throughout the season. His ability to eat innings at a high rate of quality takes pressure off a bullpen that needs rest during a long summer schedule. On Monday, he delivered exactly what the Braves needed.

Olson Provides the Offense

Matt Olson's three-RBI night was the offensive anchor. Olson has been one of the most consistent run producers in the National League this season, and his ability to drive in runs in situations where the game needs production is central to what makes Atlanta's offense reliable. The 9-4 final wasn't close — but it started with Olson creating separation when it mattered.

The Braves have the offensive depth to win when multiple contributors contribute and to win when one or two players carry the load. Both scenarios have appeared in this six-game winning streak.

The NL East Picture

At 28-13, the Braves aren't just leading the NL East — they're creating meaningful separation from the rest of the division. Earlier in the season, their lead was already approaching nine games; Monday's win continues building on that margin.

In a division that has historically been competitive, the Braves' record represents a statement about how they've approached 2026: playing consistently winning baseball from March through May, not waiting for the summer stretch to get serious. The National League's best record through approximately six weeks is a product of that consistency.

What's Next

The Braves continue their series in Washington before returning to Atlanta. At 28-13 with the NL's best record and a six-game winning streak, the question isn't whether Atlanta is a serious World Series contender — it's how serious. Elder, Olson, and the rest of the roster will continue making that case every time they take the field. The NL East race may already be tilting heavily in one direction.
