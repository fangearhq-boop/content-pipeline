Andrew Abbott Shuts Out Astros as Reds End 8-Game Losing Streak — De La Cruz Goes 3-for-3 in 5-0 Win

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | May 11, 2026

Abbott delivered six sharp innings, three relievers finished a combined shutout, and the Reds won the Houston series finale 5-0 Sunday after dropping eight games in a row.

Eight losses in a row will test a team's belief in itself. On Sunday afternoon in Houston, the Cincinnati Reds passed the test with a dominant 5-0 shutout of the Astros.

Andrew Abbott started and delivered exactly what the Reds needed: six innings of focused, efficient pitching. He allowed three hits, struck out five, and walked one. Fifty-five of his 85 pitches were strikes. It was the kind of outing that stops the bleeding — controlled, sharp, and never in jeopardy of turning into something larger.

Tejay Antone, Sam Moll, and Graham Ashcraft closed out the last three innings with perfect frames. The Astros didn't score once across all nine innings.

The Offense Woke Up in the Fourth

The Reds didn't score against Astros starter Kai-Wei Teng in the first three innings. Then the fourth inning opened and Cincinnati found its rhythm.

Elly De La Cruz singled. Sal Stewart doubled. Both scored on a triple to right field that put the Reds up 2-0. Tyler Stephenson followed with an RBI single, making it 3-0. Spencer Steer added a home run to extend the lead further. By the time the Astros replaced Teng, the game was in hand.

De La Cruz's contribution — a 3-for-3 day with two runs scored — was central to everything. He reached every time up, scored twice, and gave Cincinnati the spark its lineup needed after eight straight losses. His ability to get on base consistently isn't just a personal stat line; it creates pressure that forces opposing defenses to play differently for the rest of the order.

The Context: Snapping a Skid

Losing eight games in a row is a jarring stretch for any professional team. For the Reds, Sunday's win was the second consecutive — they had also won Saturday — capping a series win in Houston against a team with strong pitching and consistent offense. Coming into Houston with an eight-game losing streak and leaving with a series win is a significant result.

Abbott was the right pitcher for that moment. His start wasn't flashy — six innings, five strikeouts, no one dominant inning — but it was exactly what the team needed: calm, consistent pitching that kept the offense in reach and gave the relievers a game they could close.

What's Next

The Reds will look to carry the momentum from the Houston series into their next series on the schedule. Abbott moves to 2-2 on the season with Sunday's win. De La Cruz, who continues his emergence as the NL's best shortstop, will remain the offensive engine Cincinnati needs. For a team that spent the last 10 days in a spiral, winning two straight in Houston and taking the series represents a genuine recovery. The next step is showing it wasn't a one-series correction.
