Schwarber's 7-Game HR Streak Ends — But He Already Made the Most Historic Record of All

By Jake Torres | May 17, 2026

The streak is over. Kyle Schwarber did not homer on Saturday as the Phillies beat the Pirates 6-0 in a game dominated by Cristopher Sánchez's 13-strikeout masterpiece and Bryce Harper's 457-foot blast. The consecutive-games home run streak stopped at seven — one short of the MLB record of eight consecutive games with a home run.

That record, as every Phillies fan by now knows, is held by three players: Dale Long, Ken Griffey Jr., and Don Mattingly — Schwarber's own interim manager.

But here's what the week actually produced, stripped of the drama around the record chase: Kyle Schwarber's 2026 home run pace is one of the most historically dominant hitting stretches in modern baseball. And the streak ending is almost beside the point.

The Mattingly Subplot That Captivated Baseball

One of 2026's stranger storylines ran directly through the Phillies' dugout. Don Mattingly, the interim manager hired to stabilize a struggling team, holds a share of the MLB record for consecutive games with a home run — eight straight games in 1987 as a member of the Yankees. His own designated hitter was chasing that exact record.

Mattingly handled the week with characteristic grace. He praised Schwarber without adding pressure, kept the focus on winning games rather than individual milestones, and watched his team go 14-4 under his management while his DH made history in ways that extended beyond the streak conversation.

Saturday, Schwarber's game ended without a home run. The record remains at eight. Mattingly's name stays in the record book. And the story has a conclusion, even if it wasn't the one most people were rooting for.

The Record He Actually Broke

On Friday night, May 15, Schwarber hit his 19th and 20th home runs of the season in an 11-9 Phillies comeback win over Pittsburgh in extra innings. Home run No. 20 was the important one — not because of the scoreline, but because of the calendar.

May 15. With his 20th home run on that date, Schwarber set the MLB record for the earliest date in a season to reach 20 home runs. He broke the previous mark set by Arizona's Luis Gonzalez on May 17, 2001. Stats Perform confirmed the milestone as it happened: the earliest anyone in baseball history has ever reached 20 home runs in a season.

That's the record that matters more. The consecutive-game streak is thrilling, media-friendly, and narratively compelling. But reaching 20 home runs before anyone else in history — over nearly a century of the game — is a durability-of-production record that speaks to something sustained, not something streaky.

Still Leading Baseball at 20

Schwarber enters Sunday as the major league home run leader with 20. He's on a pace that would produce one of the most prodigious home run totals in recent memory if it holds. The streak ended without the record, but the broader performance is still extraordinary — and the Phillies, now 14-4 under Mattingly and at .500 for the first time since mid-April, are better for having him in that lineup every day.

The seven-game streak was remarkable. It's unlikely to be remembered as the most impressive thing Kyle Schwarber did in May 2026. That distinction belongs to the 20 home runs he'd already hit before most teams' cleanup hitters have adjusted to their summer routines.

What's Next

Schwarber heads into Sunday's games as the MLB home run leader — still comfortably, still historically — with no apparent intention of slowing down. The Philadelphia Phillies, riding a 14-4 run that has returned them to .500 and renewed genuine optimism around the franchise, will need every home run he can provide as the season's second quarter begins in earnest. The streak is over. The season is very much alive.
