### STORY 4: Cam Schlittler — Walter Johnson Numbers

#### FB Long-Form -- 3:00 PM ET

In 1913, Walter Johnson was the most dominant pitcher in baseball. He posted a 1.14 ERA over 36 starts and was the defining pitcher of his generation. No one came close to what he was doing.

Over 110 years later, Cam Schlittler is putting up numbers that reference that era.

Through his first nine starts of 2026, the 25-year-old New York Yankees right-hander has a 1.35 ERA, a 5-1 record, 59 strikeouts, just 9 walks, one home run allowed, and a 0.81 WHIP. He is the first pitcher in over a century to post that combination of metrics through the first nine starts of a season. The last time someone matched those numbers through nine starts? Walter Johnson. In 1913.

This is not an accident. Schlittler's command is exceptional — nine walks over nine starts is a rate that most starting pitchers aspire to for an entire season. His strikeout rate is elite. And he is not allowing pitchers' mistakes to turn into extra-base damage.

On Saturday in the Subway Series, Schlittler pitched into the eighth inning against the Mets, allowing Juan Soto's 250th career home run in the seventh while the Yankees won 5-2. Even in a start where he gave up a historic blast, he was dominant.

The 2026 Yankees didn't announce themselves as a juggernaut. They've simply kept winning — and Schlittler has been a primary reason why.

What is this kid doing? Something that hasn't been done since Wilson was president. And in New York, the biggest stage in baseball, he is rising to meet every moment.
