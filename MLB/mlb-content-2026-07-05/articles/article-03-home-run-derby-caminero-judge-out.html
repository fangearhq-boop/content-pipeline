Home Run Derby: Caminero Leads the Field, Judge Out With Rib Fracture

By Ryan Calloway

July 5, 2026

The 2026 T-Mobile Home Run Derby field is coming together — and its highest-profile absence will be the New York Yankees' Aaron Judge.

Judge, named an AL All-Star starting outfielder, has been on the injured list since June 5 with a stress fracture on the right side of his first rib. He will participate in the All-Star Game activities, but the Derby — scheduled for July 13 at 8 PM ET at Citizens Bank Park on Netflix — is off his calendar for 2026.

In Judge's absence, Junior Caminero is the event's most compelling figure.

Caminero: Hot Hand, High Stakes

The Tampa Bay Rays infielder is the first officially confirmed Home Run Derby entrant for 2026 — and he arrives in Philadelphia as scorching hot as any hitter in the sport. Caminero hit seven home runs in the past week, earned AL Player of the Week, and sits at 28 home runs on the season. He was last year's Derby runner-up.

If Caminero continues his current trajectory at the same velocity, the Derby will be his to lose. The combination of a hot streak and runner-up motivation makes him the early favorite, regardless of who fills out the remainder of the field.

Schwarber's Conditional Yes

Kyle Schwarber leads all of Major League Baseball with 30-plus home runs. He plays for the host Phillies. He was voted onto the All-Star roster (full reserve announcement July 6). And he told reporters he would participate in the Derby — "if this back deal calms down."

A Schwarber Derby appearance at Citizens Bank Park, in front of a home Phillies crowd, would be one of the marquee moments of All-Star week. His back health between now and July 13 is the only obstacle. The incentive to play through it is significant.

Yordan Alvarez of the Astros confirmed he will not participate — leaving one of the sport's most feared power hitters on the sidelines.

The New Format and the Netflix Move

The 2026 Derby introduces a swing-based format replacing the previous timed rounds: 20 swings in Round 1, 15 swings in Round 2 and the Final. The change emphasizes precision and strategic shot selection over pure volume within a clock window.

The broadcast is on Netflix for the first time in the event's history — a milestone in sports streaming that adds to the platform's growing sports portfolio. The broadcast team features Elle Duncan, Matt Vasgersian, and Lauren Shehadi, with analyst commentary from Barry Bonds, CC Sabathia, Hunter Pence, and Anthony Rizzo.

What's Next

The full Derby field will be announced as the remaining All-Star rosters are finalized on July 6. With Judge out and Alvarez declining, the competition opens up for players like Caminero, a potential Schwarber, and whoever else the field eventually includes. The Derby is July 13 at 8 PM ET on Netflix from Citizens Bank Park. For Caminero, who came within one round of winning last year, there may not be a better opportunity to complete the run.
