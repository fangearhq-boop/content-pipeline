2026 All-Star Starters: Dodgers Place Four, Freeman Earns His 10th, 26 First-Timers

By Marcus Cole

July 5, 2026

The Los Angeles Dodgers are sending four players to Philadelphia as 2026 MLB All-Star starters — and they will be doing it in the home ballpark of the team that beat them in the NLCS.

Shohei Ohtani (NL DH), Freddie Freeman (NL 1B), Max Muncy (NL 3B), and Andy Pages (NL OF) were all named to the starting lineup for the July 14 Midsummer Classic at Citizens Bank Park, revealed July 4 at 7:30 PM ET on FOX. No other franchise placed more than two starters on either squad.

The full starting lineups for the 96th MLB All-Star Game, presented by Mastercard, are now set.

The Complete Starting Lineups

American League starters: C Shea Langeliers (Athletics), 1B Vladimir Guerrero Jr. (Blue Jays), 2B Ernie Clement (Blue Jays), 3B Junior Caminero (Rays), SS Bobby Witt Jr. (Royals), OF Mike Trout (Angels), OF Byron Buxton (Twins), OF Aaron Judge (Yankees), DH Yordan Alvarez (Astros).

National League starters: C Drake Baldwin (Braves), 1B Freddie Freeman (Dodgers), 2B Ozzie Albies (Braves), 3B Max Muncy (Dodgers), SS CJ Abrams (Nationals), OF Brandon Marsh (Phillies), OF Juan Soto (Mets), OF Andy Pages (Dodgers), DH Shohei Ohtani (Dodgers).

The Numbers Behind the Selections

This year's class includes 26 first-time All-Stars — a figure that reflects the depth of the current generation of MLB talent and the degree to which rosters are turning over after the pandemic-era cohort ages out.

Freeman's 10th selection puts him among the franchise-era stalwarts for All-Star appearances. Ohtani's sixth, playing exclusively as a hitter this season, continues a streak of dominance as the Dodgers' offensive centerpiece. Ohtani was the top overall NL vote-getter — his All-Star status is now an annual constant.

The notable exclusion: Ronald Acuña Jr. did not make the roster, which speaks to the competitive depth in NL outfield voting this season rather than any decline in Acuña's production.

The Dodgers at Citizens Bank Park

There is a certain irony in the Dodgers — the best team in baseball at 58-31 — sending a starting lineup's worth of players to a game held in the home park of the Phillies, one of the NL East's main contenders. Phillies fans will have opinions.

For Freddie Freeman, it is his 10th time making the walk from the dugout to the All-Star photo. Chris Sale also earns his 10th selection. These are career milestones that reflect sustained excellence over a decade-plus in the sport.

What's Next

Full rosters — including all reserves and pitchers — are announced July 6. The 2026 MLB All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, airing on FOX at 8 PM ET. The Home Run Derby precedes it on July 13, streaming on Netflix for the first time. For the Dodgers, the All-Star break offers a chance to rest a rotation that has logged significant innings. For everyone else in the NL, it is a reminder of what the Western Division's dominant franchise looks like when it is operating at full strength.
