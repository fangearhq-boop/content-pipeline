Tigers Swept the Yankees — and They're Still Trading Tarik Skubal

By Marcus Cole

July 5, 2026

The Detroit Tigers swept the New York Yankees in three games at Yankee Stadium this week. It was the kind of series that generates "maybe they should keep him" takes about Tarik Skubal, the Tigers' two-time defending AL Cy Young Award winner who headlines the summer's most significant trade market.

The Tigers are 38-49. They're eight games behind the Chicago White Sox in the AL Central. No amount of Yankee-Stadium momentum changes that math. Detroit is selling — and Skubal is the prize.

The Braves Are the Frontrunner

USA Today's Bob Nightengale reports that rival executives around the league consider the Atlanta Braves the most likely landing spot for Skubal. Atlanta's urgency is clear: Spencer Strider, the Braves' ace, remains on the injured list. A rotation that expected Strider to be healthy has instead had to manage without him. Adding Skubal would give the Braves arguably the best 1-2 starting combination in the NL.

The price is steep. Detroit is asking for two top-50 prospects, a top-10 organizational arm, and MLB-ready depth. The Braves have the organizational inventory to assemble that kind of package. They also have the win-now urgency — Spencer Strider's status creates a rotation hole that the trade deadline is the natural mechanism to fill.

Who's In, Who's Out

The Yankees, despite their series loss to Detroit, have removed themselves from consideration. A Yankees source put it plainly: "No chance. None." The New York market may be the most natural trade partner for a player of Skubal's caliber and name recognition, but the Yankees aren't there.

The Dodgers, widely speculated as a natural fit given their trade-deadline history and roster depth, are currently "not in" — though that assessment comes with the caveat that Blake Snell or Tyler Glasnow missing significant time could change their calculus quickly.

Jon Heyman identified the Brewers, Cubs, Phillies, Padres, Rays, and Red Sox as teams also involved in Skubal conversations. ESPN placed the probability of a trade at 85%. ESPN also published nine specific Skubal trade proposals involving teams across both leagues.

Skubal's Situation

Skubal is 2-2 with a 2.22 ERA and 23 strikeouts in 2026. He is a Scott Boras client heading into free agency this fall — which means the Tigers have exactly one chance to maximize his trade return. Holding him through the August 3 deadline and watching him walk in November would be one of the least defensible front-office decisions in recent memory.

The deadline is August 3 at 6 PM ET. That's 29 days away from July 5.

What's Next

Skubal trade chatter will intensify between now and August 3 as the remaining interested teams sharpen their proposals. The Braves need to move before rival buyers sharpen their own bids. Detroit's leverage increases as the deadline approaches and the field potentially narrows. The likeliest outcome remains a trade — with Nightengale's Braves reporting representing the most credible insider signal available. Watch for a deal announcement anywhere from mid-July through the final 48 hours before the August 3 deadline.
