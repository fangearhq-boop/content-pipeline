Yamamoto Strikes Out 10, Dodgers Win 3-0: A Star-Spangled Sunday to Remember

By Jake Torres

July 5, 2026

All 15 Major League Baseball games were on the same company's platforms on Sunday, July 5, for the first time in the sport's history. NBC, Peacock, and NBCSN broadcast every game simultaneously in what the league branded Star-Spangled Sunday. The holiday weekend experiment was a success — and Yoshinobu Yamamoto made sure baseball's showcase day had a headliner worthy of the occasion.

Yamamoto struck out 10 batters in seven innings as the Dodgers shut out the San Diego Padres 3-0 in Sunday Night Baseball. Freddie Freeman, hours after being announced as a 10th-time All-Star starter, added a home run. The Dodgers improved to 58-31 — the best record in the National League by a comfortable margin.

Yamamoto's Gem

Ten strikeouts. Seven innings. Zero runs. The numbers for Yamamoto's July 5 start against San Diego were dominant in every category that matters — and they came on the most visible single day in the 2026 MLB regular season calendar.

Yamamoto has been one of the most valuable acquisitions in Dodgers history since arriving from Nippon Professional Baseball. The ability to pitch deep into games — seven innings against a lineup as dangerous as the Padres' — is the differentiator between his role and a high-leverage reliever's. The Padres, one of the NL West's contenders, were held scoreless.

The Star-Spangled Sunday Results

Beyond the Dodgers win, the holiday slate produced several notable results across the 15-game Star-Spangled Sunday card.

The Atlanta Braves dominated the New York Mets 10-3 in the noon showcase game on NBC — the free broadcast game available to all viewers without a Peacock subscription. Drake Baldwin, named an All-Star starter alongside Ozzie Albies at second base, contributed to a balanced Atlanta offensive performance.

In the AL Central, the Chicago White Sox defeated the Cleveland Guardians 3-1 at Progressive Field, trimming Cleveland's division lead to one game. That result sets up what may be the most compelling standings race in the American League heading into the trade deadline.

The Broadcasting Milestone

Star-Spangled Sunday was an MLB experiment in universal access: every game, every market, every team — no blackout restrictions for the day. The games aired across NBC (two free games), Peacock (streaming), and NBCSN (cable). For fans without a Peacock subscription, the two NBC games provided a window into the concept.

The initiative is part of MLB's ongoing effort to expand the sport's audience on nontraditional platforms — a push that also includes the Home Run Derby's move to Netflix on July 13 for the first time in the event's history.

What's Next

The Dodgers carry a 58-31 record and a four-game Midsummer Classic starting lineup into the All-Star break. Their rotation — Yamamoto anchoring, with Snell and Glasnow as the other arms — is healthy and productive. The All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park. After the break, the second-half pennant races begin in earnest, with the Dodgers as the NL's team to beat, the Brewers (NL Central leaders) as the closest competition, and the Braves as the team most likely to add a significant trade piece before August 3.
