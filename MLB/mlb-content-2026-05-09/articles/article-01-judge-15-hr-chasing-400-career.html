Aaron Judge First to 15 HR in 2026, Chasing a Yankees Legacy Milestone

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | May 9, 2026

The Yankees slugger is lapping the field in the home run race and quietly approaching 400 career homers — a feat only three Yankees legends have achieved.

Aaron Judge has become the first player in baseball to reach 15 home runs in the 2026 season, and if his current pace holds, this year could be historic by any measure. On pace for more than 60 home runs, Judge is not just winning the 2026 home run race — he may be building toward one of the great individual seasons in recent memory.

But the more interesting conversation isn't about 2026. It's about career.

Judge is nearing 400 career home runs — a milestone that would place him in the most exclusive company in New York Yankees history.

The 400 HR Club, Yankees Edition

Only three New York Yankees have ever hit 400 home runs in the franchise's storied history: Babe Ruth (659), Mickey Mantle (536), and Lou Gehrig (493). Three names that define not just baseball but American sports history. The Murderers' Row era, the golden age of the Yankees, the foundation of the most successful franchise in American sports.

Aaron Judge is about to join them.

At his current age and pace, reaching 400 HR this season is a realistic target. Barring injury, Judge appears to be on track to add his name to that list. For a player who already won back-to-back AL MVPs and set the American League single-season home run record, this is simply the next chapter in a remarkable career.

The 2026 Home Run Race

Judge didn't reach 15 HRs in a vacuum. Munetaka Murakami, the White Sox rookie who set an MLB record by hitting 14 home runs before recording his first double or triple, had been right on his heels. The two were tied at 14 just days ago, making for one of the more compelling individual races the sport has produced this spring.

Now Judge is ahead, and with the Yankees' lineup protecting him — and the team at 26-12 — there's no sign of a slowdown. The question isn't whether Judge will have a monster year. The question is how monstrous it will be.

The Bigger Legacy Picture

In terms of pure impact, Judge may already be the best right-handed hitter in Yankees history, and arguments can be made for his standing among the best power hitters of any era. His combination of walk rate, exit velocity, and raw power has never been more consistent than it is right now.

When 400 career home runs comes — and it appears it will come this season — it will simply be the next logical entry in a career full of them. Ruth. Mantle. Gehrig. Judge. The names feel right together.

What's Next

Judge and the Yankees will continue one of the sport's more entertaining stories as the 2026 season progresses. The HR race between Judge and Murakami gives fans a compelling head-to-head angle. The team's 26-12 record gives the Yankees genuine World Series aspirations.

And somewhere in the background, the career HR counter keeps ticking toward 400. When it gets there — when All Rise echoes through Yankee Stadium for that milestone homer — it will be one of the moments of the baseball year.
