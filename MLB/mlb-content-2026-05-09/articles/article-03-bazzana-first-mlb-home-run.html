Travis Bazzana Hits First MLB Home Run in Guardians Win Over Twins

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | May 9, 2026

The No. 1 overall pick in the 2024 MLB Draft is hitting milestones in rapid succession — first hit, now first home run — as Cleveland's most anticipated prospect makes his mark quickly.

Travis Bazzana is wasting no time.

The Cleveland Guardians' No. 1 overall pick from the 2024 MLB Draft hit his first career Major League home run on Friday night, helping the Guardians beat the Minnesota Twins. It's the latest milestone in a debut stretch that has been rapid even by the standards of elite prospects.

First MLB at-bat. First MLB hit. Now, first MLB home run. All in the span of roughly two weeks since his April 28 call-up from Triple-A Columbus.

Who Is Travis Bazzana?

Even casual baseball fans should know this name. Bazzana was the top pick in the entire 2024 MLB Draft — the most coveted amateur player in the world that summer. He arrives with two layers of historical distinction before he ever took a big-league at-bat.

First: he's Australian. Bazzana is the first Australian-born player ever taken with the No. 1 overall pick in the MLB Draft, a milestone for the game's international growth.

Second: he's a second baseman. No second baseman had ever been drafted first overall before Bazzana. For a position not traditionally associated with top-of-the-draft firepower, his selection represented a significant bet on both his abilities and how the modern game values middle infield play.

That bet is looking smart right now.

Quick Ascent

Bazzana's path to Cleveland was relatively fast for a top overall pick, and his early big-league numbers have been a mix of the expected adjustment period and flashes of the talent that made him the consensus No. 1 prospect. He showed a mature approach at the plate with above-average walk rates in his first games, and he's added stolen bases to go with his offensive production.

The first home run is a statement. Power from a second baseman was part of what made Bazzana such an unusual top pick, and seeing it show up at the big-league level this early — against major league pitching — is exactly what Cleveland hoped for.

The Guardians' Future

Cleveland has long been one of baseball's best organizations at developing young talent, and Bazzana fits their model perfectly. He's athletic, disciplined, and projects to be a cornerstone player rather than a one-dimensional slugger.

The Guardians won a division title not long ago with a collection of homegrown players who came up through the same system Bazzana just graduated from. If he continues developing at this pace, he could be the next franchise cornerstone in Cleveland — and one of the best second basemen in the sport within a few years.

What's Next

For Bazzana, the next steps are simply more of the same: adjusting to the speed of the game, working through opposing pitchers' tendencies, and building consistency. The Guardians won't rush him — they'll give him time to find his footing.

But the first home run is a symbol. It says: he belongs here. And the 2026 Cleveland Guardians, with Bazzana anchoring the future of their infield, suddenly look like a team worth tracking over the next decade.
