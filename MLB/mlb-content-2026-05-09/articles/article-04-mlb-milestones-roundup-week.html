MLB Milestones Roundup: The Week's Most Unusual and Historic Stats

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | May 9, 2026

From a Rockies outfielder chasing a 65-year-old record to a Yankees sweep for the history books, this week in baseball produced a remarkable collection of unusual milestones.

One of baseball's great virtues is that records are everywhere. Nearly every game, in some box score somewhere, a number appears that hasn't appeared quite this way in 20 or 40 or 65 years. This week produced an exceptional batch of them. Here's the rundown.

Mickey Moniak's Multi-Homer Barrage

Mickey Moniak of the Colorado Rockies recorded his fourth multi-homer game of the season through just the Rockies' 35th game. In the recorded history of Major League Baseball, only one player has had more multi-homer games through that early point in a season: Harmon Killebrew in 1959, who had five.

Moniak is doing something so rare that only a Hall of Fame slugger at the height of his powers has ever done more of it this early in a season. Colorado's hitter-friendly home park helps, but this is still a remarkable offensive achievement and a storyline that has flown under the national radar.

The Yankees' Historically Dominant Sweep

New York swept the Baltimore Orioles in a four-game series recently — and they didn't just win each game. They won every game of the series by at least five runs. That combination, sweeping a series of at least four games with every victory by a margin of five or more, has only happened twice in the Yankees' 100-plus years as a franchise.

The 2026 Yankees are doing this kind of thing with regularity. At 26-12, they are one of baseball's best teams, and performances like the Baltimore sweep explain why — this isn't a team that scrapes by. When they get rolling, they obliterate opponents.

Nick Kurtz's Walk Streak

Oakland Athletics first baseman Nick Kurtz walked in 20 consecutive games — a streak that ended in early May. The accomplishment put him in elite company: only Barry Bonds (also 20 games) has tied that mark in modern records. The all-time MLB record for consecutive games with a walk belongs to Roy Cullenbine, who walked in 22 straight games for the Cleveland franchise in 1947.

Kurtz came within two games of owning the most exclusive walk-streak record in baseball history. In an era when strikeouts dominate, a streak of 20 consecutive games with a base on balls is a testament to elite plate discipline.

The Pirates' Walk Inning

On a single May afternoon in Cincinnati, the Pittsburgh Pirates drew seven walks in one inning against the Reds — tying the all-time MLB record for most walks by a team in a single inning. It is the kind of statistical anomaly that will live in Baseball Reference footnotes forever: one inning, seven free passes, a moment that the Reds would prefer to forget.

What's Next

The statistical oddities will keep coming. Every week in the 162-game marathon of a baseball season produces new numbers, new records in danger, new moments to file away. Moniak's multi-homer record chase will be worth following. The HR race between Judge and Murakami produces new data points daily. The Cubs' walk-off total and win streaks are worth tracking.

Baseball rewards the fan who pays attention to the numbers. This week was a reminder of why.
