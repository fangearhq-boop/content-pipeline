Cubs Complete Second 10-Game Win Streak — First Team Since 1955 Dodgers to Do It

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | May 9, 2026

Chicago is 26-12 with a 15-game winning streak at Wrigley, having won 20 of 23. The 1955 Brooklyn Dodgers are the only team in the past 70 years to match this feat through 40 games.

The last time a team pulled off what the Chicago Cubs just did — completing two separate 10-game winning streaks in their first 40 games — an entire generation of Americans would grow up without ever seeing the Cubs win a World Series.

That team was the 1955 Brooklyn Dodgers, who broke their own championship drought that October. They are the only franchise since to match what the 2026 Cubs have done.

Let that context settle in as you consider this: the 2026 Chicago Cubs are playing historic baseball.

The Numbers

After completing their second 10-game win streak, Chicago sits at 26-12 — tied for the best record in all of baseball alongside the New York Yankees and Atlanta Braves. They have won 20 of their last 23 games. They are the class of the National League Central, a division they are making look like a minor league.

At Wrigley Field, the home winning streak stands at 15 games. It is the longest winning streak at Wrigley in nearly a century, and it has transformed Chicago's ballpark back into one of the sport's most feared home environments.

The Cubs have also had six walk-off wins in 38 games — the first Chicago team in 30 years to accomplish that feat in the early portion of a season. They are finding ways to win in every possible configuration.

Who Is Driving This?

The Cubs' rotation has been the foundation. Shota Imanaga continues to be one of the most overlooked elite starting pitchers in the sport, recently posting a 10-strikeout performance against the Reds. The depth behind him has provided length and quality on a nightly basis.

Offensively, Chicago scores runs from multiple spots in the lineup. There is no single player doing all the damage — this is a team that inflicts pain from top to bottom. When the offense goes quiet, the pitching saves them. When the pitching is tested, the offense erupts. Winning 20 of 23 does not happen any other way.

The NL Central Picture

For the NL Central, the Cubs have essentially ended the division race in mid-May. Whatever lead they hold is growing, and the teams behind them — the Cardinals, Reds, and others — are not closing the gap. Chicago is playing at a level that the rest of the division cannot match.

The bigger question now is whether this team can sustain this level deep into the summer and into October. The 1955 Dodgers were the last comparable reference point. And they won the World Series that year.

What's Next

The Cubs have a stretch of games ahead that will test whether this level of play can continue. No team maintains a .700-plus winning percentage for an entire season — the regression toward the mean always comes. But the Cubs have built enough cushion in the standings and enough roster depth that even a gradual step back won't threaten their division lead.

What they've accomplished in the first 38 games is legitimately historic. The 2026 Cubs belong in any conversation about the sport's best teams, full stop. Whether they're remembered as a special team depends on what they do in October. But right now, they're giving Chicago fans something to cheer about that rivals any stretch in the franchise's modern history.
