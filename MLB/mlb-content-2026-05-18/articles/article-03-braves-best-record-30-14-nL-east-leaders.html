Braves Are Baseball's Best Team at 30-14 — And the NL East Race Is Already Theirs to Lose

By Ryan Calloway | May 18, 2026

Thirty wins and 14 losses through 44 games. That's the Atlanta Braves entering Monday, and that's the best record in all of Major League Baseball. They're on pace for 103 to 107 wins. Their pitching has been anchored by a healthy Chris Sale. Their lineup, led by Austin Riley's recent surge, has produced when needed. And the NL East gap between the Braves and the field is getting wider.

Riley has been particularly dangerous recently, going 7-for-18 with three home runs over a four-game stretch that underlined why Atlanta's offense is not a one-man show. This is a lineup with depth throughout, capable of scoring from multiple spots. When the middle of the order is hitting, the Braves aren't just winning games — they're winning them convincingly.

The NL East Picture

The Braves' division rivals have had varying returns through the first 44 games. The most interesting storyline in the NL East right now is Philadelphia, where Don Mattingly has reversed a franchise going sideways. When Mattingly took over, the Phillies were 9-19. Under his management they've gone 14-4 — a remarkable turnaround that has pushed Philadelphia back to .500 at 23-23.

But .500 is not 30-14. The Braves are running away from the division, and the second half of their schedule through May includes a trip to Miami that represents a winnable series against a Marlins club hovering around .440 baseball. If Atlanta extends their lead to 7 or 8 games before June, the NL East will begin to look settled.

The Championship Conversation

The Braves' record invites the bigger question: Is this the team that wins the National League? Their pitching is deep. Their offense is balanced. Their manager has them playing focused baseball. At 30-14, they have earned the right to be discussed as the NL's best team.

The answer is that it's too early. 30-14 is excellent. 103 wins and a championship are different things. The Dodgers will factor in. Other NL teams will get healthier. But Atlanta is the benchmark that everyone else is measuring against, and they have not given any opponent a reason to stop thinking that way.

What's Next

The Braves open a four-game series at Miami on Monday at 6:40 PM ET. A series win would push their record to 31-14 or better heading into a week that will reveal whether they can maintain this pace through the second straight week of May. The NL East standings update each night. Right now, Atlanta is writing those numbers at the top.
