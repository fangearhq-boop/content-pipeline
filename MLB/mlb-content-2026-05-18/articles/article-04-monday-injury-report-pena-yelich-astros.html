Monday Injury Report: Astros Lose Pena and Imai, Yelich Misses Third Straight Game

By Marcus Cole | May 18, 2026

Monday's MLB transaction wire was busier than average, and the Houston Astros took the largest hit. Shortstop Jeremy Pena was placed on the 10-day injured list with a hamstring strain, and in the same transaction day, right-handed pitcher Tatsuya Imai was also placed on the 15-day IL with right arm fatigue. Losing two contributors on the same day puts Houston's depth to an immediate test.

Over in Milwaukee, Christian Yelich's return from a prior IL stint has hit a snag. The Brewers' outfielder and designated hitter missed his third consecutive game Monday with back tightness — a different issue from the adductor strain that kept him out for a month earlier in May. If the back tightness doesn't resolve quickly, Milwaukee could be facing a second IL trip for one of their most important offensive players.

The Astros' Double Hit

Pena's hamstring injury carries positional importance. Shortstop is a premium defensive position, and replacing an established fielder mid-season with a substitute creates a gap both in the field and in the lineup. His bat is a meaningful contribution to Houston's offense. The 10-day IL timeline is a minimum, not a ceiling — hamstring strains can extend if not fully healed before a player returns.

Imai's placement on the 15-day IL with arm fatigue is a different but related concern for the Astros' pitching depth. Arm fatigue at this stage of the season raises questions about workload management and whether the issue has been building for weeks. The 15-day minimum means he won't return until at least early June. Two players down on the same Monday is a significant roster challenge.

Yelich's Back Issue

The Yelich situation in Milwaukee deserves monitoring. He returned from an adductor strain on May 12, went 0-for-4 in his first game back, and then missed three straight days with a back issue. When a player returns from one injury only to immediately develop a second, it raises questions about whether the first rehab was complete or whether the player was protecting something that led to compensatory strain elsewhere.

The Brewers haven't announced an IL placement for Yelich as of Monday, but three missed games is a significant stretch in a season where Milwaukee needs production from every healthy starter. If this turns into another trip to the injured list, it will mark two separate stints in less than two weeks.

What's Next

Pena will be eligible to return from the 10-day IL after May 28. Imai's 15-day stint runs through at least June 2. Yelich's situation will be evaluated day-by-day, with an IL placement possible if he doesn't resume full activity. For fantasy baseball players and Houston and Milwaukee fans, the next 10-14 days will clarify the impact of today's transactions on each team's playoff trajectory.
