José Ramírez is 9 Homers From the 300-300 Club — One of Baseball's Most Exclusive Milestones

By Marcus Cole | May 18, 2026

Eight players in baseball history have hit 300 home runs and stolen 300 bases in their major league careers. Willie Mays did it. Barry Bonds did it. Bobby Bonds, Alex Rodriguez, Andre Dawson, Reggie Sanders, Steve Finley, and Carlos Beltran are the others. The club is that small, that exclusive, and that historically meaningful.

José Ramírez is nine home runs away from making it nine members.

He enters Monday with 291 career home runs and 303 career stolen bases. The stolen base threshold was crossed on May 2, when Ramírez swiped his 300th in the Guardians' 14-6 win over the A's — his AL-leading 13th steal of the season and a milestone that made him only the second player in Cleveland/Guardians franchise history to reach 300 career steals, behind Kenny Lofton. Half of the 300-300 equation is already done. Nine home runs stand between Ramírez and the other half.

What the 300-300 Club Means

The 300-300 combination is not a statistical quirk. It represents a specific kind of baseball player: elite power combined with elite speed, sustained over a long career. Home run totals above 300 belong to sluggers. Stolen base totals above 300 belong to speedsters and baserunning wizards. Players who reach both are genuinely rare — players who combined two skill sets that modern analytics often treat as mutually exclusive.

Look at the eight members of the current club. Mays and Bonds are two of the greatest players in baseball history. Rodriguez is a first-ballot Hall of Famer (complicated legacy aside). Beltran was a nine-time Gold Glover and 2023 Hall of Fame inductee. Andre Dawson won eight Gold Gloves and an MVP. These are not players who stumbled into a statistical category. They are players who defined generations.

Ramírez belongs in that company. He has led his position in WAR in multiple seasons. He has been Cleveland's franchise cornerstone for more than a decade. His 291 home runs with the Guardians rank second in franchise history, trailing only Jim Thome's 337. At 33 years old, in 2026, he is having another strong season with 6 home runs through roughly 45 games.

The Timeline

At his 2026 home run pace, Ramírez would reach the 300 HR mark sometime in early June — putting 300-300 within reach before the All-Star break. That's the optimistic projection. A hot May or June stretch could accelerate it. A slow patch could push it into July.

Either way, the math says it's coming. Nine home runs is not a distant goal for a 33-year-old power hitter who has consistently produced 20-25 home runs per season throughout his career. Ramírez is not approaching this milestone on fumes. He is approaching it as a healthy, productive player at the top of Cleveland's lineup — with a legitimate shot at delivering it on the Guardians' home field in front of fans who have watched this quiet legend build toward it for a decade.

The Quiet Legend Argument

Part of what makes Ramírez's story compelling is how little national attention it has received. He has played his entire career in Cleveland — not in New York, Los Angeles, or Boston. He doesn't have the marketing profile of a Shohei Ohtani or the home run spectacle of an Aaron Judge. He gets talked about primarily by baseball stat people, which is a category of fans who understand exactly how special he is but represent a fraction of casual viewership.

The 300-300 milestone has a way of changing that. It's a simple number that lands even with fans who don't follow WAR or xwOBA. Nine home runs from joining Mays, Bonds, and Rodriguez in a club of eight is a sentence any baseball fan can understand and appreciate. When Ramírez hits that ninth home run, it will be one of the season's most significant individual moments.

What's Next

Ramírez and the Guardians continue through the 2026 season with the milestone 9 home runs away. At a pace of roughly one home run every 7-8 games, the projection points to sometime in early June. Watch for the Cleveland home games particularly — few sports moments land harder than a franchise-defining milestone in front of a home crowd. The 300-300 club is about to gain its ninth member.
