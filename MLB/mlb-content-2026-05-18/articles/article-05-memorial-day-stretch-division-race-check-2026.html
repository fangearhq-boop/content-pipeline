Memorial Day Stretch Check: Three Teams Above .680 and the 2026 Division Race Update

By Jake Torres | May 18, 2026

Memorial Day is roughly a week away. In baseball, that means the standings are about to become real. The first 40 to 45 games of an MLB season are long enough to generate meaningful data but short enough to contain surprises. Teams that are five or more games above .500 at Memorial Day tend to stay that way, historically. Teams five or more games below .500 at this checkpoint almost never recover to a winning record.

So where does the 2026 season stand? Through approximately 43 games, three teams are running at or above a .680 win percentage. That's historically unusual — a sign that this is a year with genuine elites at the top of multiple divisions.

The Leaders

The Atlanta Braves own the best record in baseball at 30-14. They are the NL East frontrunner by a wide margin and have shown the ability to win both at home and on the road. Chris Sale anchors the rotation. Austin Riley is producing at a high level. The Braves are doing to the NL what the Dodgers have traditionally done — building a lead before most of the league figures out what's happening.

The Tampa Bay Rays are 29-14 in the AL East. This is the Tampa model operating at full capacity: a mix of openers, bullpen depth, and timely hitting that produces wins even without a traditional ace. The Rays have lost multiple rotation pieces to injury this season, yet their record remains elite. That resilience is the story of their franchise's modern era.

The Chicago Cubs are 28-16 in the NL Central. After a slow early stretch, the Cubs have been one of the game's most consistent teams in May. Their rotation is deep, their lineup is balanced, and they play in a division where winning consistently is enough to build a comfortable lead. At this pace, they are a strong postseason qualifier.

What the Barometer Tells Us

The Memorial Day barometer is not a lock. Teams that are 30-14 at Memorial Day sometimes collapse. Teams that are 15-28 sometimes make late pushes. But the historical correlation is real enough to take seriously. Teams above .600 at this point in the season make the postseason at rates around 60 to 65 percent. Teams above .680 — where all three of these clubs sit — push that number well above 70 percent.

The next meaningful marker is the All-Star break, approximately seven weeks away. Between now and then, the division races will crystallize further. But the teams leading today are carrying the weight of proof — each game adds to the case that they are genuine contenders and not statistical artifacts of a short sample.

What's Next

Memorial Day weekend arrives in approximately one week, which will put every team at roughly 48 to 50 games played. By then, the standings will be firm enough to start identifying real division races from formality. The trade deadline is nine weeks away. The All-Star break is seven. The 2026 standings, as of this Monday check, suggest the Braves, Rays, and Cubs have earned the right to be taken seriously as October players.
