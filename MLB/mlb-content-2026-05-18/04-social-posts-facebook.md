### STORY 2: Schwarber's Record-Setting HR Pace — 20 HRs on Earliest Date in MLB History

#### Long-Form Post
Kyle Schwarber has already made history. Whatever happens for the rest of the 2026 season, that part is locked in.

On May 15, Schwarber hit his 20th home run of the season. That made him the first player in MLB history to reach 20 home runs that early in the calendar year — breaking a record set by Luis Gonzalez of the Arizona Diamondbacks on May 17, 2001.

The seven-game consecutive home run streak that captivated baseball fans ended before it could break the all-time mark of eight consecutive games (shared by Dale Long, Don Mattingly, and Ken Griffey Jr.), but the pace story remains very much alive. At his current rate, Schwarber is on pace for approximately 67 home runs this season. Barry Bonds holds the all-time single-season record with 73, set in 2001.

We're not saying Schwarber will hit 67 home runs. We're saying that's what the numbers look like right now, in May, with the history books already open.

He still leads all of baseball with 20-plus home runs, four ahead of Aaron Judge in second place. The Phillies are getting healthier. The schedule sets up favorably. Don Mattingly, whose own 8-game consecutive HR record Schwarber was chasing, is now managing the team.

If you're not watching the Phillies right now, you're missing the season's most historically compelling individual story.
