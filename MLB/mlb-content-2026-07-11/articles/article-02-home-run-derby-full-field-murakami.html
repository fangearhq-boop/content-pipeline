2026 Home Run Derby Field Is Complete — Murakami's Return Makes It Must-Watch

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | July 11, 2026

The bracket is full.

With Munetaka Murakami's addition confirmed Saturday morning, all eight participants in the 2026 T-Mobile Home Run Derby are set for Monday night at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. First pitch — or first swing, rather — is at 8:00 PM ET on Netflix.

The Full Field

Here are all eight participants competing Monday:

  • Bryce Harper (Philadelphia Phillies) — The 2018 Derby champion returns to his home park. Harper won the Derby as a Washington National, but this is Citizens Bank Park, and the crowd will be something else entirely.
  • Kyle Schwarber (Philadelphia Phillies) — The MLB home run leader entering All-Star Week with 32 home runs. This is his third Derby appearance. Two Phillies in the field at home in Philadelphia is the kind of thing that happens once in a generation.
  • Jac Caglianone (Kansas City Royals) — One of the most anticipated young power hitters in the game, Caglianone makes his Derby debut as one of the headliners of the next wave of MLB sluggers.
  • Junior Caminero (Tampa Bay Rays) — The 2025 Derby runner-up is back looking to go one step further. Caminero was one of the best stories of last year's competition.
  • Willson Contreras (Boston Red Sox) — The veteran catcher brings one of the most complete power profiles in the field.
  • Munetaka Murakami (Chicago White Sox) — More on him below.
  • Ben Rice (New York Yankees) — The Yankees' first baseman makes his Derby debut with one of the most powerful left-handed swings in the American League.
  • Jordan Walker (St. Louis Cardinals) — The Cardinals outfielder rounds out a field that covers every corner of the league.

Murakami's Story

The Murakami piece is the one that makes this Derby field genuinely special.

The White Sox designated hitter missed more than a month with a strained hamstring. He returned to the lineup just this past Friday — two days before the Derby. His inclusion was far from certain even 72 hours ago. And then, almost immediately after getting back on the field, he confirmed he was in.

The historical footnote matters: Murakami becomes just the second Japanese-born player to participate in the Home Run Derby, after Shohei Ohtani did it in 2021. That context is not lost on anyone who follows baseball's international landscape. Ohtani's 2021 Derby appearance — on the same night he was named an All-Star starter at pitcher — was one of the most watched moments in Derby history. Murakami stepping into that company, especially after his injury scare, gives the field a dimension it wouldn't have had otherwise.

The Format Change

This year's Derby looks different from recent editions, and the format shift is worth understanding before Monday night.

MLB is scrapping the timed format and returning to a swing-based competition. Round 1: each player gets 20 swings. Round 2: 15 swings. The Final: 15 swings. There is one additional wrinkle: if your last swing of a round is a home run, you keep hitting until you don't hit one. That means a hot hitter can extend his own round in real time.

The change removes the clock-watching element that critics felt slowed the event down and replaced it with pure sequential drama. Every swing counts. Every miss ends a potential streak. The format puts the crowd back in the moment instead of watching a scoreboard countdown.

The Philadelphia Factor

Harper and Schwarber in the field at Citizens Bank Park is not a coincidence — it's the best possible version of home-field advantage the Derby format allows. The Phillies are the most All-Star-represented team in baseball this week with six selections, and Monday night gives the home crowd something specific to root for.

Harper won the Derby in 2018. He has never won it as a Phillie. Schwarber leads MLB in home runs and is competing in his home park for the first time. If either of them reaches the final, the noise level in Citizens Bank Park will be among the loudest crowds the Derby has ever seen.

What's Next

The 2026 T-Mobile Home Run Derby is Monday, July 14, at 8:00 PM ET on Netflix from Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. All-Star Week continues Sunday with the Futures Game, followed by the All-Star Game on Tuesday, July 15, at 8:00 PM ET on FOX.
