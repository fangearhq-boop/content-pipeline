MLB Draft Day 2026 — White Sox Select With the No. 1 Pick for the First Time Since 1977

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 11, 2026

The waiting is over.

The Chicago White Sox step to the podium today at the Pennsylvania Convention Center in Philadelphia to make the No. 1 overall selection in the 2026 MLB Draft — their first time picking at the top of the board since they selected Harold Baines in 1977. Picks 1 through 10 are live at 1:00 PM ET on NBC/Peacock, with coverage continuing on MLB Network and MLB.com through the evening.

After months of scouting, speculation, and a pre-draft week that offered no White Sox signal from the inside, the decision comes down to three candidates. All three are legitimate. Any of the three could go first.

The Three Finalists

Roch Cholowsky has been the consensus No. 1 overall prospect for most of the past year. The shortstop from UCLA hit .329/.448/.624 across three seasons, with 52 home runs and an advanced approach at the plate — he walked 105 times against 100 strikeouts at the college level. ESPN's Kiley McDaniel and Jesse Rogers, CBS Sports, and Baseball America all have him going No. 1 in their final pre-draft mocks. If selected, he becomes just the third college shortstop ever drafted first overall, joining Bill Almon in 1974 and Dansby Swanson in 2015.

Grady Emerson is the alternative. The Fort Worth Christian (Texas) high school shortstop was ranked No. 1 by MLB.com heading into draft week and is the 2026 Gatorade Baseball Player of the Year. He committed to the University of Texas but is draft-eligible. According to MLB Pipeline's Jonathan Mayo, Emerson has closed the gap on Cholowsky considerably, and the race is now at roughly 50-45 in Cholowsky's favor.

Vahn Lackey is the acknowledged wild card. The Georgia Tech catcher hit .397 with 20 home runs in 2026, and multiple sources have noted that he is the preference of certain voices within the White Sox front office. He would be a genuine surprise, but White Sox VP of Amateur Scouting Mike Shirley confirmed that all three conversations are "real and happening up to the minute."

The White Sox Context

Chicago enters this draft in a full rebuild. The team finished near the bottom of the AL the past two seasons, which is what put the No. 1 pick in their hands through the Draft Lottery. Their last No. 1 pick — Baines — went on to a Hall of Fame career. The pressure to repeat that outcome, or something close to it, is real.

Both top candidates are shortstops — a position of need and of organizational building-block value. If Chicago selects Cholowsky, they add a college-polished hitter who should move quickly through the system. If they select Emerson, they take the higher-upside high school prospect with a longer development timeline but a ceiling some evaluators see as higher than any player in the class.

The draft does not stop with the No. 1 pick. Rounds 1 through 4 run today, with Rounds 5 through 20 on Sunday. But every team in baseball will be watching at 1:00 PM ET to see which direction Chicago goes.

What's Next

The 2026 MLB Draft continues through Sunday at the Pennsylvania Convention Center in Philadelphia. Picks 1-10 aired at 1:00 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. Rounds 5 through 20 continue Sunday. The full draft tracker is available at ESPN.com and MLB.com.
