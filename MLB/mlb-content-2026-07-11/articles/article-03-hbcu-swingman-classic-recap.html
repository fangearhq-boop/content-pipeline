AL Wins 4th Annual HBCU Swingman Classic 6-1 — Radcliffe Takes MVP, Griffey Presents Trophy

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 11, 2026

The 4th Annual HBCU Swingman Classic is in the books.

The American League defeated the National League 6-1 Thursday night at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, in front of a crowd of approximately 17,000 fans. The game aired live on MLB Network and served as the unofficial kickoff to All-Star Week in Philadelphia.

Radcliffe Wins MVP

Southern University center fielder Jacoby Radcliffe was named the game's Most Valuable Player after a performance that showcased exactly the kind of talent the Swingman Classic was designed to put on baseball's biggest platform.

Radcliffe scored a run, stole two bases, and was hit by a pitch across his appearances at the plate. The combination of speed, instinct, and situational awareness made him the standout player of the game and the clear MVP choice.

Southern University, a member of the Southwestern Athletic Conference and one of the flagship HBCU institutions in Louisiana, had reason to celebrate with Radcliffe's recognition at Citizens Bank Park. For Radcliffe, the moment on a nationally-televised stage at MLB's premier annual event represented exactly the kind of visibility the Swingman Classic was built to create.

The Griffey Moment

The image that will last from Thursday night did not come from the scoreboard.

Ken Griffey Jr. personally walked onto the field at Citizens Bank Park to present the MVP trophy to Radcliffe. The Hall of Fame center fielder — one of the most beloved players in the history of the game — handing the award directly to a young HBCU player on national television was the kind of moment that transcends the game itself.

Griffey's connection to the HBCU Swingman Classic goes beyond trophy presentation. His presence at the event reflects a broader commitment from former players of his generation to use their platform to support and amplify HBCU baseball. For a 22-year-old college player like Radcliffe, the handoff from Griffey in that setting is a career moment.

The Game and the Managers

The American League broke the game open with a three-run first inning and never let the National League back into it. The final score of 6-1 was not particularly close, though the event's value was never primarily about the margin.

The managerial matchup added a Philadelphia layer to the night. Jimmy Rollins, the former Phillies shortstop and 2007 National League MVP, managed the National League squad. Rollins managing at Citizens Bank Park — the building where he spent his prime years and where he remains one of the most celebrated players in franchise history — was a moment of local resonance that the crowd recognized throughout the night.

Rickie Weeks managed the American League. Weeks, a former Milwaukee Brewers second baseman who played 12 seasons in the majors, guided the AL to the victory.

50 Players, 19 Schools

The Swingman Classic featured 50 players from 19 HBCU programs across the country. The event, now in its fourth year, has grown into one of the genuine can't-miss moments on the All-Star Week calendar — not just for HBCU communities, but for any baseball fan who wants to see elite college talent on the biggest stage.

The 17,000 fans at Citizens Bank Park Thursday night represented one of the strongest attendance figures in the event's history. The All-Star Week setting in Philadelphia, with the stadium accessible and buzz building throughout the city, helped drive the crowd to a level that reflected how much the Swingman Classic has grown since its inaugural edition.
