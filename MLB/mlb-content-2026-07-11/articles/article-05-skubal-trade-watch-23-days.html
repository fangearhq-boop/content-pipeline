Tarik Skubal Trade Watch: Tigers Are Winning, and the August 3 Decision Just Got Complicated

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | July 11, 2026

Twenty-three days until the August 3 MLB Trade Deadline. And the Tarik Skubal situation just changed.

A month ago, the narrative was simple: the Detroit Tigers were struggling, Skubal was heading to free agency after the season, and a rental trade of the back-to-back AL Cy Young winner felt like a matter of when, not if. Contenders were circling. The asking price was steep but understood. The Tigers were going to sell.

Then Detroit got hot.

The Tigers Have the Best Record in the AL Since June 1

Since June 1, the Detroit Tigers have gone 19-12 — the best record in the American League over that stretch. They have won seven of their last eight games. As of Saturday morning, they sit just four games out of a Wild Card spot.

That is not a team that looks like a seller. That is a team that has spent six weeks convincing itself — and the rest of the league — that it belongs in a playoff race.

The shift in Detroit's record has not changed the fundamental facts of the Skubal situation: he is still heading to free agency, the Tigers still need to make a decision, and the deadline is still August 3. But it has changed the conversation inside the organization about what that decision should be.

Skubal Wants to Stay — and He's Said So

The most unusual dimension of this story is Skubal himself. He is not passively waiting for the front office to determine his fate. He is actively making the case for Detroit to buy at the deadline instead of sell.

"We need to play better baseball or else, come the deadline, you give the front office an option to reassess where this team is," Skubal told the Detroit News this week.

That is a pitcher publicly lobbying for his own team to make a push. That framing — that the team's performance on the field determines the front office's options — is a direct appeal to his teammates and, through the press, to the organization itself. Skubal is not resigned to being traded. He wants the Tigers to make a run, and he is saying so out loud.

The Buyers Are Still Circling

None of this means the trade market has cooled. The Yankees and Phillies remain the most-named frontrunners for Skubal. The Braves have been cited as a sleeper. All three are genuine contenders who would immediately benefit from adding a back-to-back Cy Young winner to their rotation.

The reported asking price has not changed: at minimum, one top-100 MLB prospect and another team top-10 prospect. That is an aggressive ask that reflects both Skubal's dominance and his free agency status — teams acquiring him are getting a rental, which makes the prospect cost the entire return on the deal.

The buyers will not stop calling. The Tigers' front office will field those calls up to the deadline. But what has changed is that the Tigers now have a reason to say no — a record that makes keeping Skubal a defensible baseball decision, not just a sentimental one.

The Decision

Every day that passes without a trade is a day closer to the Tigers convincing themselves to run it back. If they stay four games out of a Wild Card spot entering the final week of July, the calculus shifts dramatically. If they fall back to eight or ten games out, the math reasserts itself and a trade becomes likely again.

Twenty-three days. No one inside baseball knows which way this goes. That uncertainty — a genuine open question at the top of the trade deadline market — makes the Skubal watch the most compelling subplot running through August 3.
