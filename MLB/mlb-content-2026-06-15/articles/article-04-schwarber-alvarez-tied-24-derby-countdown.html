Schwarber and Alvarez Both Go Cold Sunday — Still Tied at 24, Derby Countdown Begins

By Marcus Cole

June 15, 2026

Sunday offered no resolution to the question everyone in baseball is asking. The Phillies lost to the Brewers 4-0, with Kyle Schwarber held without a home run. The Astros lost to the Royals 4-0, with Yordan Alvarez similarly shut out. Both men head into Monday still tied at 24 home runs apiece — the most in baseball and a pace that is drawing serious conversations about the 60-homer threshold.

How the Race Got Here

Schwarber entered the season as the primary favorite after leading the National League with 56 home runs in 2025. He reached 24 homers at one of the earliest paces in NL history, drawing immediate comparisons to record-setting seasons past.

Alvarez entered the conversation dramatically. On June 12, he hit a home run and a grand slam in the same first inning of a game against the Royals — the first player in major league history to accomplish that specific feat. The two-homer inning pushed him to 24, pulling even with Schwarber and immediately transforming a one-man race into a genuine duel.

They have been tied ever since. Sunday's scoreless afternoon from both only deepened the drama.

The Derby as Backdrop

The timing of the 2026 Home Run Derby cannot have been scripted more perfectly. The Derby is Monday, July 13 — four weeks from today — at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. That is Schwarber's home ballpark, in his city, in front of the fans who chant his name during every home run swing. He won the NL award at Citizens Bank Park last season. He knows every dimension of that outfield fence by heart.

Bryce Harper, the other face of Phillies baseball, has also said he is considering participating in the Derby if selected as an All-Star. Philadelphia hosting the All-Star Game has become a month-long celebration of their roster — and the HR race between Schwarber and Alvarez is the centerpiece of that narrative.

The 60-Homer Question

The projection math has been done by every baseball analyst following this race. At their current pace, both players are tracking toward home run totals that enter historical territory. The 60-homer club is an exclusive group: only Barry Bonds, Mark McGwire, Sammy Sosa, and Roger Maris have done it (the latter in a now-asterisked expansion season). Aaron Judge came closest in recent memory with his 62-home run 2022 season.

Whether Schwarber or Alvarez can sustain their pace through the second half is a question that will not be answered until September. But the fact that both of them are tied at 24 in mid-June, with the Derby looming, with the All-Star Game in Philadelphia, with Aaron Judge on the IL — this is the exact scenario that turns a regular season into something people remember.

What's Next

Both Schwarber and Alvarez will look to break the tie this week. Every at-bat now carries the weight of the race. A Schwarber lead changes the story. An Alvarez lead changes it differently. A continued tie going into the Derby in Schwarber's home park would be almost too cinematic to believe. The race is exactly that good. It continues Monday.
