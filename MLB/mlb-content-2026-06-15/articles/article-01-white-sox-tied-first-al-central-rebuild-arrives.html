The White Sox Are Tied for First Place — The Rebuild Has Arrived

By Marcus Cole

June 15, 2026

Two years ago, the Chicago White Sox went 41-121. The worst record in franchise history. One of the worst records in modern MLB history. They were beyond rebuilding — they were a cautionary tale.

On Sunday, they beat the Los Angeles Dodgers 6-4 and are now tied for first place in the American League Central.

The Six-Run Sixth

The game turned in the sixth inning. The White Sox entered it trailing and left it with a lead that held the rest of the way. In a span of three half-innings, Chicago completely unraveled the Dodgers' bullpen with three consecutive home runs — Colson Montgomery, Sam Antonacci, and Chase Meidroth all going deep in what became a six-run frame.

Montgomery, one of the cornerstones of Chicago's rebuild, also doubled in the game. Andrew Benintendi added two hits to keep the pressure on. Erick Fedde delivered 2⅔ scoreless innings in relief to secure the win, and Seranthony Dominguez sealed it with his 12th save — striking out Freddie Freeman with two runners on in the late innings to end any Dodger comeback threat.

The Dodgers got solo home runs from Freddie Freeman and Mookie Betts, but that was all they could muster against a White Sox team that seemed to have a different kind of energy on Sunday.

First Series Win Over the Dodgers Since 2014

The series win was meaningful beyond the standings. Chicago had not beaten Los Angeles in a series since 2014 — twelve years of trying and failing against one of the premier franchises in the sport. Sunday's win changed that.

The White Sox have now won eight consecutive home series and nine of their last 13 games overall. They are tied with Cleveland for first place in the AL Central — the latest in the season they have led their division since 2021. For a franchise that spent the last two years in the wilderness, these numbers represent something real.

The Rebuild Is Working

The 2024 White Sox were historically bad. The 2025 White Sox were still rebuilding, but the pieces were coming together. The 2026 White Sox are first in their division in mid-June.

Colson Montgomery represents what Chicago built toward — a young, electric shortstop who made the MLB Top-100 player list entering 2026 and has delivered on that billing. He is surrounded by other young contributors finding their footing at the major league level. The offense is not just functional; on Sunday, it was spectacular.

The skeptics will note that first place in June does not equal October baseball. They are right. But being tied for first in June is an enormous step for a franchise that had to tear everything down and start over. The White Sox are no longer a punchline. They are a contender.

What's Next

The White Sox enter Monday tied with Cleveland for first place in the AL Central. The schedule ahead will test whether this is a sustainable position or a hot stretch in an otherwise difficult season. But the evidence from Sunday — three home runs in one inning, a series win over the defending champions, and a tied first-place standing — suggests Chicago is for real. The rebuild has arrived. The baseball world is paying attention.
