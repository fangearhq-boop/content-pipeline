Sunday Baseball Roundup: Rangers Win SNB, Cubs Split, Mize's Return Delayed Again

By Jake Torres

June 15, 2026

Sunday's baseball card featured fifteen games and a postponement, the Rangers winning the featured national broadcast, the Giants salvaging a series split at Wrigley Field, and the Detroit Tigers watching rain cancel their comeback story — again. Here is what happened.

Rangers 6, Red Sox 4 — Sunday Night Baseball

Nathan Eovaldi delivered the outing his team needed. The right-hander threw seven innings for the Texas Rangers in the Sunday Night Baseball feature game at Fenway Park, allowing four runs while keeping Boston off-balance through the late innings. Wyatt Langford hit a home run over the Green Monster to give Texas early momentum, and the Rangers' lineup was able to withstand the Red Sox's late push for the 6-4 final.

The win keeps Texas in the conversation in the American League wild card picture. Eovaldi has been one of the more consistent starters in the AL this season, and a seven-inning quality start against a competitive Boston lineup on a national stage is the kind of performance that earns attention.

Giants 5, Cubs 1 — Series Split in Chicago

The Chicago Cubs came into Sunday looking to take the series against the Giants after Pete Crow-Armstrong launched a first-pitch leadoff home run on Saturday in a 6-1 Chicago win. Sunday went differently. San Francisco won 5-1 and the series ended in a split — not the outcome the Cubs needed as they try to build momentum in the NL Central.

Crow-Armstrong was held in check on Sunday, and the Cubs' offense could not replicate Saturday's energy. A series split at home is not ideal, but Chicago's young core showed enough on Saturday to suggest the pieces are there for stretches of sustained winning.

Tigers-Guardians Postponed — Mize Delayed to Wednesday

Rain in Cleveland washed out Sunday's Tigers-Guardians game, which was supposed to feature Casey Mize's return to the Detroit rotation. The postponement pushes Mize's next start to Wednesday in Houston — the latest delay in what has been a frustrating year for the Tigers' rotation to get healthy.

Mize posted a 2.27 ERA over nine starts before returning to the injured list with an adductor strain. He went 2-3 with 49 strikeouts in 47.2 innings — elite production when available. His return alongside Tarik Skubal's recent activation gives Detroit a potential one-two punch at the top of their rotation heading into the second half, if both pitchers can stay healthy.

The Tigers have gone 7-2 in June despite the rotation injuries. If Mize and Skubal are both active and effective in July, Detroit becomes a legitimate contender in the AL Central — already tied for first by the White Sox and Cleveland.

What's Next

All-Star Game voting for Phase 1 remains open through June 25. The results — top two vote-getters per position revealed on MLB Network — will follow on June 25, with Phase 2 running June 29 through July 2. The All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, preceded by the Home Run Derby on July 13. The baseball summer is fully in motion.
