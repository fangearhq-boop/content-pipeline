Rockies 23, Athletics 9 — Colorado Sets Franchise Record for Runs in a Game

By Jake Torres

June 15, 2026

Twenty-three. That is how many runs the Colorado Rockies scored on Sunday at Las Vegas Ballpark. It is a franchise record — the most runs Colorado has ever scored in a single game — and it came in a 23-9 blowout of the Las Vegas Athletics that featured performances that will be talked about in Denver for years.

Willi Castro's Afternoon

If there was one name to remember from Sunday's record-setting performance, it was Willi Castro. The Rockies infielder went 4-for-something with seven RBIs, the centerpiece of which was a grand slam off Scott Barlow in the eighth inning. With the game already well in hand, Castro's bases-clearing shot was the exclamation point on a performance that cannot be overstated. Seven RBIs in a single game is extraordinary production by any standard.

Goodman, Karros, and the Rest of the Lineup

Castro was not the only Rockies hitter who had a day to remember. Hunter Goodman went 5-for-5 with two home runs and four RBIs — a perfect game at the plate in the most literal sense of the term. Kyle Karros added four hits of his own. Troy Johnston and TJ Rumfield also connected for home runs, contributing to Colorado's total of six home runs on the afternoon.

The final hit total was 24. Twenty-four hits in a nine-inning game. The Rockies put up a crooked number in essentially every inning they batted, and the Athletics' pitching staff had no answer. By the time Barlow surrendered the Castro grand slam in the eighth, the game had long since been decided — it was just a matter of how historic the numbers would get.

The Context

The Rockies are not a good baseball team in 2026. Their record reflects the challenges that come with competing in the National League West while also playing in the thin air of Coors Field, which complicates roster construction in ways that no other team in baseball faces. On most days, Colorado does not look like this.

But Sunday was not most days. Sunday was the kind of game that reminds you why baseball is the most statistically unpredictable of all the major sports. The worst offensive team can have an historically great offensive day. The most struggling lineup can put up a franchise record. The Athletics, for their part, are also struggling — but 23 runs is not a statement about the opponent. It is a statement about the sheer variety of baseball outcomes, even in a 162-game season.

What's Next

The Rockies return home after their road series in Las Vegas. Sunday's franchise record will sit in the history books regardless of what happens in the games that follow. For Castro and Goodman, it was the kind of afternoon that defines a season — a moment where everything went right in the most emphatic possible way. Colorado may not do this again in 2026. But they did it once, and that is enough to remember.
