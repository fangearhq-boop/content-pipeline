## STORY 1: White Sox Tied for First

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

The Chicago White Sox are tied for first place in the AL Central. Let that sink in for a moment.

Two years ago, the White Sox went 41-121 — the worst single-season record in franchise history, one of the worst in modern MLB history. They were the cautionary tale about what happens when a rebuild goes wrong. They were punchlines. They were the team every contender scheduled as a rest week.

On Sunday, they beat the Los Angeles Dodgers 6-4. They won a series against the defending World Series champions — their first series win against the Dodgers since 2014. And they did it with a six-run sixth inning in which three of their young core players — Colson Montgomery, Sam Antonacci, and Chase Meidroth — all hit home runs.

The White Sox have now won 8 consecutive home series and 9 of 13 games overall entering the week. They moved into a tie with Cleveland for first place in the AL Central — the latest they have led their division since 2021.

Erick Fedde pitched 2⅔ scoreless innings for the win. Seranthony Dominguez earned his 12th save, striking out Freddie Freeman with two runners on to end the threat. Even the Dodgers' contributions — solo homers from Freeman and Mookie Betts — weren't enough.

The White Sox rebuild isn't just working. It has arrived.

## STORY 2: Rockies 23, Athletics 9

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Colorado put up 23 runs on Sunday. That is a franchise record. That is also one of those numbers you have to read twice.

The Rockies beat the Las Vegas Athletics 23-9 at Las Vegas Ballpark in a game that started getting out of hand early and never came back. Willi Castro was the story: four hits, a grand slam off Scott Barlow in the eighth inning, and seven RBIs on the day. Seven. Hunter Goodman went 5-for-5 with two home runs and four RBIs. Kyle Karros added four more hits. Troy Johnston and TJ Rumfield also connected for home runs.

Final hit count: 24. Final home run count: 6.

The Rockies have one of the worst records in baseball this season, but for one afternoon in the Nevada desert, they were unstoppable. Twenty-three runs in a nine-inning game is not something that happens in modern baseball. The Rockies franchise had never done it before Sunday.

Castro's grand slam in the eighth was the capstone — at that point the game was already decided, but the pinch in the gut from a Barlow bases-clearing blast was a fitting exclamation point on an afternoon that defied expectations at every turn.

And they have lost three games in a row.

And what does the trade deadline picture look like if they need to add reinforcements?

Right now, Atlanta is still the best team in baseball — by record, by run differential, by any measure. The three-game skid is a warning, not a crisis. The response starts today.

## STORY 4: Schwarber-Alvarez Tied at 24 HR

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Sunday was not the day either of them made a move.

The Phillies lost to the Brewers 4-0, with Kyle Schwarber shut out. The Astros lost to the Royals 4-0, with Yordan Alvarez similarly held without a home run. Both went into the quiet of Sunday evening still tied at 24 home runs apiece — the most in baseball.

Which means the race enters Monday exactly where it ended Saturday: knotted.

The timing of the 2026 Home Run Derby cannot be more perfect for this storyline. The Derby is Monday, July 13, at Citizens Bank Park — Schwarber's home ballpark, in his city, in front of his fans. He led the NL with 56 home runs last season. He is on pace for a number that could challenge the 60-HR plateau. And now Bryce Harper — the other face of Phillies baseball — has said he is considering competing in the Derby if selected as an All-Star.

The All-Star weekend in Philadelphia is shaping up to be one of the most anticipated in recent memory. And it begins with this question: who breaks the tie first?
