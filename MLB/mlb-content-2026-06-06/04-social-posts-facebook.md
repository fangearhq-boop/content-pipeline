### STORY 3: Cristopher Sanchez's 50.2 Scoreless Innings

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

It started as a hot streak. It became franchise history. Then it became one of the most remarkable stretches of pitching in the history of Major League Baseball.

Philadelphia Phillies left-hander Cristopher Sánchez recently completed a scoreless innings streak of 50.2 innings — the fifth longest in MLB history since 1893. The run ended with the Phillies winning 3-2, and while the streak is over, what Sánchez accomplished will stand in the record books long after the 2026 season is done.

Along the way, Sánchez broke the Phillies' franchise scoreless innings record that Grover Cleveland Alexander had held for 115 years. Alexander — a Hall of Famer who won 373 career games — set his mark in 1911. For over a century, no Phillies pitcher had matched it. Then Sánchez not only matched it but blew past it, reaching 44⅔ innings before the franchise record was safely in hand.

He didn't stop there. The streak eventually reached 50.2 innings, surpassing Hall of Famers Carl Hubbell, Bob Gibson, and Cy Young on the all-time list. He also passed Clayton Kershaw's expansion-era mark for left-handed pitchers.

The company Sánchez kept: Orel Hershiser (59 IP in 1988, the all-time record), Don Drysdale (58⅔ IP in 1968), and a small handful of others. Fifth place is extraordinary.

This is a performance for the ages — and a reminder that special things can happen even in a sport that often feels like it's seen everything.
