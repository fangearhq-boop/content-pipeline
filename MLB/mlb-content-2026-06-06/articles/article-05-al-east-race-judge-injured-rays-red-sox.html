Judge on the IL, Red Sox Win Friday: The AL East Race Just Got Wide Open

By Jake Torres June 6, 2026

The Math Just Changed

Before Friday, the American League East was close but manageable for the New York Yankees. The Tampa Bay Rays had pulled to within one game in the loss column. The Boston Red Sox were fighting. But the Yankees had Aaron Judge — the three-time AL MVP and the most dangerous hitter in the American League — and that meant New York could absorb a rough stretch and still be the team to beat.

Judge is on the injured list with a stress fracture in his right rib. Four to six weeks minimum. The Yankees lost to the Red Sox 5-2 on Friday night with Judge out. The AL East race just changed.

Who Benefits Most

Tampa Bay is the most natural beneficiary. The Rays entered Friday at 36-22 — playing efficient, well-rounded baseball with quality pitching and a lineup that doesn't need to be dangerous to be effective. They were already within striking distance. Six weeks of Yankees baseball without Judge is six weeks to build a lead they may be able to protect.

Boston, which won Friday, also stands to gain. The Red Sox have the pitching and the lineup depth to keep themselves in the picture, and the Yankees just removed their most threatening bat from the equation. Toronto is not out of it either — a sustained run over the next several weeks could push the Blue Jays into the conversation as well.

The Yankees Without Their Best Player

New York recalled Spencer Jones from Triple-A to fill the vacancy left by Judge. The rest of the roster — which includes capable pieces up and down the lineup — will need to perform with more consistency during Judge's absence than they did while leaning on him as the lineup's anchor.

Six weeks is a long time in a divisional race this tight. The Yankees have proven throughout the season that they can compete without perfect circumstances. But losing the AL's home-run leader for a month and a half while division rivals are healthy and hungry is a different test than anything New York has faced so far in 2026.

What's Next

The Yankees' best-case scenario is a clean recovery that brings Judge back in late July, giving New York several weeks to make a push before the August 3 trade deadline and a full September at full strength. The worst case is complications that push the return into August or beyond. In the meantime, the Rays, Red Sox, and Blue Jays will play with urgency — because this kind of opportunity doesn't arrive often in a division this competitive.
