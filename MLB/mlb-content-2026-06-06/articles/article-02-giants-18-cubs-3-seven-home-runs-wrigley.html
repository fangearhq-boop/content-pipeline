Matt Chapman Hits a Grand Slam and a Three-Run Shot as Giants Blast Cubs 18-3 with Seven Home Runs at Wrigley

By Jake Torres June 6, 2026

Seven Home Runs, One Statement

The San Francisco Giants hit seven home runs in a 18-3 victory over the Chicago Cubs at Wrigley Field on Friday, delivering one of the most overwhelming offensive performances of the 2026 season. The Giants scored in multiple innings, sent several balls over the ivy-covered brick walls at Wrigley, and left no doubt about the final outcome from the middle innings on.

Willy Adames hit two home runs. Casey Schmitt hit two more. Seven different players reached base with power, and the Giants turned what could have been a competitive series opener into a statement about the danger their lineup poses to any opponent on any given afternoon.

Chapman's Career Day

The centerpiece of the performance was Matt Chapman. The third baseman hit his fourth career grand slam in the fourth inning, putting the Giants up early and giving San Francisco immediate runway. He returned in the sixth inning — when the Giants scored seven runs — to hit a three-run home run that capped the half-inning and pushed the lead beyond any possibility of a Chicago comeback.

Seven runs batted in from one player in a single game is an extraordinary output. Chapman was 2-for-4 at the plate with 7 RBI, and his two home runs accounted for nearly half the team's runs. When his bat is this dangerous, the Giants are a different team.

The Giants' Full Offensive Power on Display

The 18-3 final and seven home runs speak to a broader truth about this Giants roster: when the lineup is functioning at full capacity, San Francisco has as much offensive potential as any team in the National League. Adames and Schmitt added depth to the output beyond Chapman's performance, and the seven-run sixth inning came against what was already a beaten-down Cubs bullpen.

For Chicago, the 18-3 result at home serves as a difficult reminder of where the franchise stands relative to the NL's contenders. The Cubs must find more consistent pitching to protect leads and keep games competitive against lineups this dangerous.

What's Next

The Giants continue their series at Wrigley through the weekend. A dominant opener doesn't guarantee a series win, but San Francisco heads into Saturday's game having reminded the NL that this offense is capable of erupting on any night. The Cubs will need to answer — and Chicago's pitching staff faces a difficult task containing the Giants lineup that just hit seven times through their ballpark in a single game.
