Aaron Judge on IL with Rib Stress Fracture — Yankees Lose Their Best Player Through At Least August

By Marcus Cole June 6, 2026

The Diagnosis Is Confirmed

The New York Yankees placed Aaron Judge on the 10-day injured list Friday after an MRI confirmed a stress fracture in his first right rib. The three-time American League Most Valuable Player will miss at least four to six weeks, a timeline that puts his expected return date somewhere in mid-to-late July at the earliest — and potentially beyond the August 3 trade deadline in a less favorable scenario.

Judge initially reported discomfort during the Yankees' series against the Oakland Athletics earlier in the week. The team initially characterized the issue as a possible bone bruise near the right rib cage, but further imaging revealed the more serious diagnosis. He did not start Friday's game against Boston, a game the Yankees went on to lose 5-2.

What New York Loses

Judge came into Friday having hit 20 home runs — the first player in the American League to reach that mark in 2026. He was on pace for what would have been an unprecedented fifth career season with 50 or more home runs, a milestone no player in major league history has reached. Ruth, Griffey, Sosa, and Judge himself each did it four times. A fifth was in sight. Now that pursuit is suspended for at least six weeks, and the math gets considerably harder with each missed game.

Beyond the power numbers, Judge anchors the Yankees' lineup in a way that affects every player around him. With him out, pitchers can pitch around New York's other threats more aggressively. Lineup construction becomes a more difficult puzzle for manager Aaron Boone throughout the upcoming weeks.

Spencer Jones Gets His Chance

To fill the roster vacancy, the Yankees recalled outfielder Spencer Jones from Triple-A Scranton/Wilkes-Barre. Jones, one of New York's top prospects, arrives in the major leagues ahead of the timeline most had projected. The opportunity is significant. Top prospects don't often get this kind of unplanned extended look, and how Jones performs over the next several weeks will shape how the Yankees view their roster composition both this season and beyond.

What's Next

Judge has not been ruled out for the remainder of the 2026 season. A four-to-six-week recovery places his best-case return around late July, which would give him several weeks before the trade deadline and a full push in August and September. The critical factor is whether the rib heals without complications. The Yankees will re-evaluate with imaging in approximately four to six weeks and adjust the timeline based on his progress. Until he returns, New York faces its stiffest test of the season — and the rest of the AL East is paying attention.
