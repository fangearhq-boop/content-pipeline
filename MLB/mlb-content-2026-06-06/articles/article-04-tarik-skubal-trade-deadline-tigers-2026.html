The Skubal Deadline: Detroit's 6-21 Collapse Sets Up the Most Important Trade Decision of the Summer

By Marcus Cole June 6, 2026

The Tigers Are Falling

Detroit has gone 6-21 since May 4. That is not a slump. That is a collapse. The Tigers entered the season with legitimate playoff aspirations, built in large part around the presence of Tarik Skubal — the back-to-back American League Cy Young Award winner who was scheduled to anchor their rotation as one of the best pitchers in baseball.

Skubal underwent elbow surgery in early May and has been working his way back, targeting a return to the rotation in mid-June. His path back has been encouraging — a 64-pitch simulated game went well, and a rehab start is expected soon. But the team around him has not held up in his absence.

Every Contender Is Watching

The August 3 trade deadline is already being called "The Skubal Deadline" by national analysts, and the characterization is appropriate. Skubal is a free agent after the 2026 season. He is the most accomplished and available starting pitcher in baseball. Any contender that adds him instantly improves its rotation for a playoff run.

The list of interested teams is effectively every team with legitimate championship ambitions. The Dodgers would love to add Skubal behind Shohei Ohtani. The Braves — baseball's best record entering this weekend — could use an additional frontline arm for October. The Yankees, suddenly more desperate with Aaron Judge on the injured list, have an obvious need. The Brewers and Phillies are deep in their respective division races.

Detroit's Painful Calculation

For the Tigers, this is not a comfortable position. Skubal is the face of the franchise. Trading the two-time defending AL Cy Young Award winner is an acknowledgment that the team built around him has not been good enough to make a run. But the alternative — holding Skubal, watching him become a free agent in November, and receiving a qualifying offer pick in return — represents a significantly worse outcome for a franchise with an uncertain path forward.

A trade done correctly could return a package of premium prospects that restarts Detroit's rebuild with real organizational depth. The Tigers' front office is facing one of the highest-stakes decisions in franchise history, and the next six weeks will test their nerve.

What's Next

Skubal's mid-June return will be closely watched by every team in baseball. His first few starts back will set the market price and clarify whether the Tigers are willing to move him. Every start is a showcase — and every Tigers loss makes a trade more likely. By the time August 3 arrives, the Skubal decision will be among the most consequential moves of the entire baseball season.
