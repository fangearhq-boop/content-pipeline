Sanchez Passes Alexander: 50.2 Scoreless Innings Is the 5th Longest Run in MLB History Since 1893

By Ryan Calloway June 6, 2026

The Streak That Rewrote Phillies History

At some point in the spring of 2026, Philadelphia Phillies left-hander Cristopher Sánchez stopped being hot and started being historic. His scoreless innings streak built quietly, inning by inning, start by start, until it reached a number that placed him among the most accomplished pitchers of any era: 50.2 consecutive scoreless innings.

When the streak finally ended — in a 3-2 Phillies victory — Sánchez had already done things that will be written about in Philadelphia for decades. He had broken a franchise record that stood for 115 years. He had surpassed Hall of Famers. He had placed himself fifth on the all-time list in MLB history going back to 1893.

Breaking Alexander's Untouchable Record

Grover Cleveland Alexander set the Phillies' scoreless innings record in 1911 — a streak that stretched to 41 innings and endured through two world wars, multiple expansion eras, and more than a century of pitching evolution. Alexander won 373 career games and is enshrined in the Baseball Hall of Fame. His franchise record had every reason to last forever.

Sánchez passed Alexander when he extended his streak to 41⅓ innings, and the franchise record was broken. But that wasn't where the story ended. Sánchez continued adding scoreless innings, pushing to 44⅔ innings, then further still. By the time he allowed a run, his streak had grown to 50.2 innings — a number that places him fifth in MLB history since 1893.

The Company He Keeps

The names above Sánchez on the all-time list are among the most celebrated pitchers in the sport's history. Orel Hershiser's 59 consecutive scoreless innings in 1988 remains the all-time record, set during one of the most dominant single-season pitching runs anyone has ever produced. Don Drysdale reached 58⅔ innings in 1968. A small handful of others fill the top positions on the list.

Along the way to 50.2 innings, Sánchez also surpassed Hall of Famers Carl Hubbell, Bob Gibson, Cy Young, and Sal Maglie. He passed Zack Greinke. And he set the expansion-era record for left-handed pitchers, surpassing Clayton Kershaw's previous mark of 41 consecutive scoreless innings from 2014.

What's Next

The streak is over but Sánchez's season is not. He continues as one of the most effective starters in the National League, and a scoreless run of this magnitude can only accelerate the conversation about where he fits in the broader context of the Phillies' rotation and their championship window. Sánchez took a franchise record that stood 115 years and made it his own. That's not something that fades easily.
