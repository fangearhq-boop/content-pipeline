Cristopher Sánchez Just Broke a 115-Year-Old Phillies Record

By Jake Torres

May 28, 2026

Seven scoreless innings against San Diego on Wednesday put Phillies left-hander Cristopher Sánchez past Grover Cleveland Alexander on the franchise's all-time scoreless innings list. The record that stood since 1911 now belongs to 2026.

Grover Cleveland Alexander pitched for the Philadelphia Phillies from 1911 to 1917. He is in the Hall of Fame. His 1911 season was one of the greatest debut seasons any pitcher has ever had, and the franchise record he set for consecutive scoreless innings stood for 115 years.

On Wednesday night in San Diego, Cristopher Sánchez threw seven shutout innings in a 3-0 Phillies win over the Padres, extended his scoreless innings streak to 44⅔ innings, and passed Alexander into franchise history.

It is a record that carries the weight of over a century.

How the Streak Happened

Sánchez's 44⅔-inning scoreless run did not arrive through dominance in a single stretch — it was built over five consecutive starts. In each of those starts, he threw at least seven innings without allowing a run. That kind of sustained shutdown performance, start after start, is what separates elite pitching stretches from hot weeks.

His Wednesday line: seven innings, zero runs, eight strikeouts. The Phillies swept the Padres series. Sánchez improved his 2026 ERA to 1.62 while extending his MLB-leading innings pitched total to 72⅓. His bWAR of 3.7 leads all pitchers in the major leagues.

He is also now the owner of a list he probably did not know existed. The Phillies franchise record for consecutive scoreless innings — held since before World War I, before most of American baseball history as the sport is currently understood — now belongs to a 29-year-old left-hander from the Dominican Republic pitching in 2026.

Where He Stands All-Time

Sánchez's 44⅔ innings currently rank seventh on the all-time single-season scoreless innings list. The record belongs to Orel Hershiser, who threw 59 consecutive scoreless innings in 1988 for the Los Angeles Dodgers — a mark that has stood for 38 years and is widely considered one of the least assailable records in baseball.

Sánchez needs another 14⅓ scoreless innings to pass Hershiser. That would require roughly two more complete shutout starts. Both are theoretically achievable. Neither is guaranteed. Pitching does not work on guarantee.

What matters for now is the franchise record — a real, tangible milestone — and what it says about the season Sánchez is having. He is not having an anomalous hot stretch. He is having a season that the Phillies have not seen from any pitcher since the Deadball Era.

The NL Cy Young Picture

Sánchez's combination of MLB-leading innings, league-best ERA among NL starters, and now a franchise record for scoreless innings has moved him to the center of the National League Cy Young conversation. A second early-season poll placed him among the frontrunners.

For a team built around Kyle Schwarber's home runs and a lineup that ranks among the NL's most productive, the addition of a 1.62 ERA anchor at the top of the rotation is what makes the Phillies a genuine World Series contender. Schwarber can hit 60 home runs. Sánchez has to hold leads. Through 72⅓ innings, he is doing exactly that — and doing it in a way no Phillies pitcher has since Alexander himself.

What's Next

Sánchez's next start will come with the entire city of Philadelphia watching the zero count in the official record. Every plate appearance until he allows a run will be tracked in real time. That kind of attention can be a weight — or a fuel. So far, in five consecutive shutout-or-better starts, Sánchez has shown no signs of running out of gas.

The Hershiser record is 59 innings. Sánchez is at 44⅔. The math is real. The possibility is real. The question is whether he wants to be part of the conversation or just a very good pitcher having a very historic season.
