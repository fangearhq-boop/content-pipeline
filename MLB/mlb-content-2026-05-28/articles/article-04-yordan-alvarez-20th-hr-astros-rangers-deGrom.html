Alvarez Hits 20th HR Off deGrom — Ties Murakami for AL Lead in Wild Night

By Marcus Cole

May 28, 2026

Yordan Alvarez went deep twice against Texas on Wednesday night, with his 20th home run of the season coming in the eighth inning to seal a 4-3 Astros win. Murakami hit his 20th the same night in Chicago. The AL race is now a tie.

When Yordan Alvarez comes to the plate twice in a game and the Astros need runs both times, something tends to happen. On Wednesday night in Arlington, it happened twice.

Alvarez hit a 428-foot solo home run off Jacob deGrom in the fourth inning to tie the game. He hit his 20th home run of the season in the eighth inning to put the Astros in front for good. Houston won 4-3 over the Texas Rangers. And for the first time in his eight big-league seasons, Alvarez homered in consecutive games.

The Hit That Tied the Game

The first home run was the kind of offensive statement that gets clipped and replayed. Jacob deGrom, when healthy, is one of the most respected arms in the game. A 428-foot solo shot off his delivery in the fourth inning is not a product of pitcher weakness — it is a product of elite bat speed meeting a well-located fastball and losing.

The Astros trailed when Alvarez stepped in. They did not trail when he reached home plate.

The 20th Home Run

His second home run of the night came in the eighth inning and carried the number that changes the conversation. His 20th home run of the 2026 season tied him with Munetaka Murakami of the Chicago White Sox for the American League lead — and for second place in MLB, behind Kyle Schwarber of the Phillies, who also sits at 20.

Alvarez arriving at 20 home runs is different from Murakami arriving at 20. Alvarez is 29 years old, a two-time All-Star, a World Series champion, and one of the most productive hitters in the American League since he debuted in 2019. For him, 20 home runs before the end of May represents an excellent start to an excellent season. It is also worth noting that he was already hitting .432 wOBA through the first weeks of May — the number is not a surprise for anyone who has been watching.

What is different about Wednesday's performance is the timing relative to Murakami's simultaneous milestone in Chicago. Both players hit their 20th on the same night. The AL home run race has two co-leaders as of May 28.

Consecutive Games for the First Time

The consecutive-game home run achievement is worth noting. Eight big-league seasons. Multiple All-Star selections. A World Series ring. And Wednesday was the first time Alvarez homered in back-to-back games. It is the kind of specific milestone that gets lost in the broader story of his night — but it signals that he is in a productive stretch that goes beyond a single good week.

What's Next

The Astros are mid-season and fighting for AL West positioning. Alvarez's continued production is central to their offensive identity. His first consecutive-homer game in eight seasons is either an anomaly or the sign of a player who has entered an elevated zone for May and June.

With Murakami also at 20 home runs and Schwarber sitting at the same number, the race for the MLB home run title has arrived at its most compelling moment well before the All-Star break. Three players. Three very different stories. All at 20.
