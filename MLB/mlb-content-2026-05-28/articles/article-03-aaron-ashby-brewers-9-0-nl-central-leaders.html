Aaron Ashby Is 9-0 — And the Brewers Are Running Away With the NL Central

By Ryan Calloway

May 28, 2026

Milwaukee's bullpen ace earned his ninth win of the season in a 2-1 Brewers victory over St. Louis on Wednesday, completing a home sweep of the Cardinals — their first at home since 2011. No other pitcher in baseball has more than eight wins.

Aaron Ashby did not start Wednesday's game. He came in from the bullpen, as he has all season, threw two innings, allowed one base hit and a walk, and kept the Brewers ahead.

Milwaukee won 2-1. The Brewers completed their first home sweep of the St. Louis Cardinals since June 10-12, 2011. And Ashby improved to 9-0 on the season — the first pitcher in Major League Baseball to reach nine wins in 2026. No one else has more than eight.

What 9-0 From the Bullpen Looks Like

Relief pitchers do not typically lead the major leagues in wins. The structure of baseball — relievers pitching in high-leverage situations for one or two innings rather than carrying the starter's usual five-or-six — makes the traditional win accumulation model much harder for pitchers who never take the mound first.

Ashby has navigated that math to 9-0 and 2.06 ERA through late May. The Brewers Fanatic described his season as "the pursuit of quirky baseball history" — which is an accurate if understated framing for a reliever approaching double-digit wins before June.

His path to nine wins reflects two things working in tandem: he is pitching well in high-leverage moments, and the Brewers are winning games when he is pitching in them. Both are required. A reliever who pitches brilliantly in blowout losses does not collect wins. Ashby has been in the game when games are decided.

The Brewers' Bigger Picture

Milwaukee is 33-20. They have won 20 of their last 27 games and 15 of their last 19. That is not a hot streak in the traditional sense — it is a sustained period of dominance that has moved them to the top of the NL Central standings.

The Wednesday win over St. Louis completed a three-game home sweep, which sent a message about the division's current hierarchy. The Cardinals entered the series with postseason aspirations. They left having been swept at American Family Field for the first time in 15 years.

The Brewers are built for exactly this kind of extended run. Their pitching staff is deep enough to win games that their offense does not dominate — Wednesday's 2-1 final is a case study in that architecture. You do not sweep the Cardinals without being able to win tight games. The Brewers can win tight games.

The Record No One Expected

When the 2026 season began, Aaron Ashby was an interesting name in Milwaukee's bullpen. By late May, he is the winningest pitcher in baseball. That sentence does not parse the way it normally would — because it normally would not apply to a reliever — and yet the sentence is true.

The history Ashby is chasing, as Brewer Fanatic framed it, is quirky. Relief pitchers do not appear on win-leader lists. They do not headline the conversations about who is pitching the best baseball in the sport. But 9-0 is 9-0, and no framing changes the number on the ledger.

What's Next

Ashby will continue in his bullpen role as the Brewers push through the second month of the season. Their next challenge is to hold the NL Central lead against a Cardinals team that will not stay quiet and a Cubs team that has been playing competitive baseball all season. For Milwaukee, the question is sustainability — whether 15-of-19 represents a new floor or a peak they are about to descend from.

Ashby's 9-0 record is the symbol of where the Brewers are right now. The number will only change in two directions: it will grow if they keep winning, or it will stay the same if he stops pitching in winning situations. At 33-20 and climbing, Milwaukee appears determined to keep adding to both columns.
