### STORY 2: Cristopher Sánchez 44⅔ Scoreless Innings — Breaks 115-Year Phillies Record

#### FB Long-Form — 10:30 AM ET

In 1911, Grover Cleveland Alexander was the best pitcher in Philadelphia. He put together a scoreless innings streak that became the standard for Phillies franchise excellence for the next century and beyond. His record stood for 115 years.

On Wednesday night, Cristopher Sánchez ended it.

Sánchez threw seven scoreless innings against the San Diego Padres on May 27, completing a three-game sweep and extending his consecutive scoreless innings streak to 44⅔. That figure surpasses Alexander's Phillies franchise record, a mark that had survived every Philadelphia rotation that came after — Steve Carlton, Curt Schilling, Cole Hamels, Roy Halladay. None of them broke it.

It has taken five consecutive starts of at least seven shutout innings for Sánchez to reach this point. Five starts in a row where he walked off the mound without allowing a run for at least seven innings. That kind of consistency is not a hot streak — it is dominance, sustained and compounded.

Where does this streak stand in the broader history of baseball? His 44⅔ ranks seventh all-time in a single season. The all-time record — 59 scoreless innings — was set by Orel Hershiser of the Los Angeles Dodgers in 1988. That mark has stood for 38 years. Sánchez, if he continues at this pace, will have a chance to approach it as the summer unfolds.

His 2026 numbers already tell the story without the streak: a 1.62 ERA going into Wednesday's game, 85 strikeouts in 72⅓ innings, a 3.7 bWAR that leads all of baseball, and the lowest ERA of any pitcher in the National League.

Grover Cleveland Alexander would understand what this kind of run looks like. He's just no longer the one who owns the Phillies record. ⚾

### STORY 5: Kyle Schwarber Makes MLB History With 20-HR Milestone

#### FB Long-Form — 6:00 PM ET

Luis Gonzalez hit his 20th home run of the 2001 season on May 17. That date became a record — the earliest in MLB history for any player to reach 20 home runs in a season.

Gonzalez's record stood for 25 years.

Kyle Schwarber broke it.

Schwarber now has 20 home runs through the Philadelphia Phillies' first 49 games of 2026 — the earliest date in MLB history to reach that milestone. He is only the 18th player in the history of the game to hit 20 home runs through a team's first 49 games, and the first to do it since Josh Hamilton in 2012.

The numbers around the milestone are worth reading carefully. His slash line: .237/.365/.645 with 35 RBI. His pace: 66 home runs by the end of the 2026 season, if he keeps this up. The most home runs through 49 games in MLB history belong to Mark McGwire (1998) and Barry Bonds (2001), both with 25. Schwarber, at 20, is in elite company while still trailing the all-time leaders in this specific measure.

This is also not happening out of nowhere. Schwarber hit his 350th career home run on April 30. He hit his 200th home run as a Phillie on May 10. He re-signed with Philadelphia last December for five years and $150 million. The Phillies made a large bet on a large bat. Through 49 games, that bet looks like the most prescient contract in the sport.

Cristopher Sánchez broke a 115-year-old Phillies record last night. Kyle Schwarber broke an MLB record tonight. Philadelphia is having quite a week. ⚾
