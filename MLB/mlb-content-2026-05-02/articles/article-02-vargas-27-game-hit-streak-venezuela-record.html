Ildemaro Vargas Extends Streak to 27 Games — Breaks Venezuela Record, Chases LeFlore's 1976 Mark

By Jake Torres | May 2, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Before 2026, most casual baseball fans couldn't have placed Ildemaro Vargas on a roster. He was a journeyman second baseman with a modest career track record — not the kind of player you'd associate with history-making. That was before May 1, 2026, when Vargas extended his hitting streak to 27 games and broke the all-time MLB record for Venezuela-born players.

The Arizona Diamondbacks infielder is now just three games from matching Ron LeFlore's 30-game season-opening hitting streak for the 1976 Detroit Tigers — the second-longest streak to start a season in the last 90 years. He is currently leading the National League in batting average at .378.

A Historic Streak Inside a Streak

Of Vargas's 27-game hitting streak, 24 came consecutive to open the 2026 season. That season-opening portion alone is historic — tied for the second-longest since 1940. Only LeFlore, who opened 1976 with 30 straight hits, has a longer season-opening streak in that span.

The Venezuela record fell on May 1 as Vargas went 1-for-4 in the D-backs' 6-2 victory over the Milwaukee Brewers — a game Arizona won behind four team home runs. Vargas wasn't the only star that night, but he was the one making history.

The Numbers Don't Lie

It's not just the streak that stands out. Vargas leads the NL in batting average at .378 and ranks in the top three in slugging percentage and OPS. His production has been consistent, not just hot — he's been hitting with authority all season and only rarely has the streak been in serious jeopardy.

Arizona's lineup has provided good run support around him, and the team's home run production has been steady. The D-backs could become a genuine wild card contender if Vargas continues to anchor the offense at this level.

What's Next

Three games. That's all that stands between Vargas and sole possession of the record for the longest season-opening hitting streak since 1976. Whether he catches LeFlore or not, Vargas has already written his name into MLB record books — and reminded everyone that breakout seasons can come from any direction.

Watch the D-backs' next series closely. History could be made in teal and red at Chase Field.
