Braves Enter May With 6.5-Game Lead — Third-Largest Division Lead at This Point Since 1969

By Marcus Cole | May 2, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Atlanta Braves spent the early weeks of the 2026 MLB season doing something that defied the offseason predictions: winning. A lot. After a winter in which their roster raised questions about age and sustainability, the Braves have silenced critics by becoming the first team to 20 wins and building the largest division lead in baseball.

Atlanta enters May at 20-9, holding a 6.5-game cushion in the NL East. According to historical data, that lead is tied with the 2012 Texas Rangers for the third-largest division lead entering May since divisional play began in 1969. Only two teams in 55+ years of divisional baseball have entered May with a bigger advantage.

Best ERA and Most Runs

What makes Atlanta's start so impressive isn't just the record — it's the balance. The Braves have posted the best ERA in baseball through April while also scoring the most runs. That kind of two-way dominance is rare, and it usually signals a team that isn't running on luck or unsustainable BABIP variance.

Pitching and defense have been the backbone of Atlanta's early success, with the rotation delivering consistently deep outings that have protected a bullpen doing everything asked of it. Meanwhile, the lineup has been dangerous every night, scoring runs in bunches against pitching staffs that expected to hold their own against this group.

A Three-Team Race at the Top

Atlanta isn't alone at the top of baseball. The Los Angeles Dodgers are also 20-9, tied for the best record in the majors. The New York Yankees are right behind at 19-10. All three franchises entered the season as World Series contenders, and all three have delivered on those expectations through April.

But the Braves hold one advantage neither the Dodgers nor Yankees can claim: a 6.5-game division lead. That cushion means Atlanta can rest key players, develop pitching depth, and enter June with more margin for error than anyone else in the sport.

What's Next

May will be the true test of whether Atlanta's early lead is a precursor to a dominant season or an unsustainable early burst. The schedule gets tougher, the opposition more familiar with Atlanta's patterns, and injuries can always scramble any team's plans.

But right now, the Braves look like the team to beat in the National League — and possibly all of baseball. The 2026 postseason picture is still being drawn, but Atlanta is holding the pen.
