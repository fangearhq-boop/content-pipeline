Judge and Rice Join Mantle-Berra: First Yankees Duo With 10+ HRs Each Before Game 30 Since 1956

By Ryan Calloway | May 2, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

In 1956, Mickey Mantle and Yogi Berra each reached double-digit home runs before the New York Yankees' 30th game. It was a mark of a ferocious lineup — two of baseball's greatest players hitting in tandem at the top of their powers. Nobody had replicated it in the 70 years since.

Until now. Aaron Judge (12 HRs) and Ben Rice (10 HRs) have become only the second pair of Yankees teammates to each reach 10 home runs before game 30 of the season. The Yankees are 19-10 and lead the AL East. The Bronx Bombers look like they mean business.

Judge's Historic Chase Continues

Judge entered the 2026 season with approximately 388 career home runs, needing roughly 32 more to join the exclusive 400-HR club. Through April, he already has 12 — putting him on pace to potentially reach that milestone well before September.

The 2022 AL Triple Crown winner and AL MVP has been particularly lethal in the first inning, reportedly batting .450 with a 1.726 OPS in first-inning at-bats this season. He has been a one-man wrecking crew in the leadoff spots of the batting order, setting the tone from the first pitch.

Ben Rice: More Than a Supporting Role

Ben Rice has emerged as more than a complementary piece in 2026 — he's become a genuine power threat in a lineup already loaded with dangerous bats. His 10 home runs before game 30 weren't fluky — they reflected quality contact, consistency in the zone, and an ability to drive the ball to all fields.

Having two hitters at that kind of pace takes real pressure off the rest of the lineup. Pitchers can't simply navigate around Judge when Rice is right behind him in the order. The historical connection to Mantle and Berra is striking — two legends sharing a stage — and Rice is proving the comparison has merit.

What's Next

The Yankees are expected to get Gerrit Cole and Carlos Rodon back from the injured list in the coming weeks. A healthy rotation behind this lineup would make New York a formidable playoff contender. The AL East race is young, but the Yankees appear to be the team to beat.

And if Judge keeps hitting at this pace, that 400th career home run isn't just possible — it could be a moment that stops the baseball world cold sometime in late summer. Watch and wait.
