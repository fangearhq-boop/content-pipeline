Max Muncy's Home Run Off Gerrit Cole's 103rd Pitch Gives Dodgers a 2-1 Win at Yankee Stadium

By Marcus Cole

July 18, 2026

Gerrit Cole gave the New York Yankees everything Friday night at Yankee Stadium.

Six-plus innings. Eight strikeouts. One walk. Four hits. A hundred and three pitches. He was the best pitcher on the field, and it wasn't close.

He still lost.

Max Muncy stepped in to face Cole's 103rd pitch in the seventh inning and hit a two-run home run into the right-field second deck. The Los Angeles Dodgers won 2-1 — the first time these two franchises had played since the 2024 World Series — and Aaron Boone stood at his postgame podium and told reporters that the decision to leave Cole in for one more batter was his, and that it was wrong.

What Cole Did Right

This is important context: Gerrit Cole was excellent. Through six innings, he had worked past a few baserunners but kept the Dodgers off the scoreboard. His strikeout-to-walk ratio was dominant. His stuff was crisp. On most nights against most teams, 103 pitches into the seventh inning with a lead would be exactly the right time to leave your ace on the mound.

The problem was not Cole's performance. The problem was that Max Muncy was the next hitter, and Muncy punished the decision the way only elite hitters can — with a swing that made the result look inevitable in retrospect.

The Moment the Game Changed

The Dodgers entered the seventh trailing 1-0. Roki Sasaki had started for Los Angeles and kept the Yankees off the board for most of the game, but New York had scratched a run across to take the lead.

Then Muncy connected. Two runs scored. A 1-0 Yankees lead became a 2-1 Dodgers lead in a matter of seconds. Tanner Scott came on to close it out in the ninth and earned his 14th save of the season.

Jack Dreyer picked up the win for Los Angeles.

Ohtani's Return

Shohei Ohtani played designated hitter in his return from a knee lubricant injection administered during the All-Star break. He went 0-for-4. Ohtani is expected to return to the mound July 22 against the Philadelphia Phillies as the team manages his workload carefully around the knee.

The Yankees, meanwhile, continue to navigate the season without Aaron Judge, who remains out with a rib injury. Imaging during the All-Star break showed minimal healing, and the best-case timeline for Judge's return is August.

What's Next

The Dodgers-Yankees series continues Saturday at Yankee Stadium. For New York, the hole gets slightly deeper — the Yankees fall to 54-43 and sit 2.5 games back in the AL East. Ohtani's return to pitching July 22 is the next notable marker for Los Angeles. Boone's lineup decisions will be under renewed scrutiny after owning Friday's mistake publicly. The series is the most-watched of the weekend, and Game 2 gives both teams a chance to reset the tone.
