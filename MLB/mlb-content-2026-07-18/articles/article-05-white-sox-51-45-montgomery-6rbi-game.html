Colson Montgomery's 6-RBI Game Highlights White Sox 12-4 Win Over Blue Jays

By Ryan Calloway

July 18, 2026

Two years ago, the Chicago White Sox lost 121 games. It was the most losses in a 162-game major league season in history. Last year they lost 102.

On Friday, they beat the Toronto Blue Jays 12-4 at Rogers Centre and moved to 51-45, leading the American League Central.

The main reason was Colson Montgomery, who had one of the best individual games of this White Sox season — and possibly of his career so far.

Montgomery's Night

In the fifth inning, with the bases loaded, Montgomery lined a bases-clearing double that drove in three runs. In the seventh inning, with more runners on base, he hit a triple — the first of his major league career — and cleared the bases again for three more.

Six RBI. Two extra-base hits. One night.

Sam Antonacci had already given Chicago a foundation in the second inning, when his two-run homer capped a five-run frame and put the White Sox ahead to stay. But Montgomery's performance in the middle innings turned a win into a statement.

The Power Trio

Montgomery is not alone in what he's doing this season. The White Sox's lineup features three players on pace to exceed 38 home runs — Montgomery, Murakami, and Miguel Vargas. If all three reach that mark, it would be the first time in franchise history that three Chicago teammates have done it in the same season.

The combination of those three in the middle of the order has made the White Sox genuinely dangerous in a way that no one predicted heading into 2026. A team coming off back-to-back historically bad seasons should not have the best power trio in the AL Central. But here they are.

What This Team Has Become

Chicago entered Friday having won three straight. The 12-4 win extended that streak to four games. The White Sox are above .500, leading their division, and carrying the kind of momentum that makes opponents take notice.

The AL Central does not need to be won by anyone dominant. The White Sox do not need to be a historic contender. They need to stay in front, play the way they have been playing, and give Montgomery, Murakami, and Vargas at-bats in meaningful games down the stretch.

They are doing exactly that.

What's Next

The White Sox continue their series at Toronto on Saturday, with a chance to extend the win streak to five games. A healthy lead in the AL Central through July is the kind of cushion this roster will need heading into August and a tough second-half schedule. The power production from the top of the order has been the story of this season — and Friday night, Montgomery made sure everyone knew it was still very much ongoing.
