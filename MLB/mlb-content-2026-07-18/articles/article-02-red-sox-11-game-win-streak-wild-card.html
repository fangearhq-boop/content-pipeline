Red Sox Win 11 Straight — From 14 Under .500 to the Wild Card Picture

By Ryan Calloway

July 18, 2026

The Boston Red Sox swept a Friday doubleheader against the Tampa Bay Rays and now own the longest winning streak in baseball. Eleven consecutive wins. The franchise's longest since September 2016.

On June 24, Boston was 14 games under .500. Sellers. A team the trade market was sizing up for parts.

On July 18, the Red Sox are 48-48, back to .500 for the first time since March 28, and sitting in the American League's third Wild Card spot ahead of the Seattle Mariners and Minnesota Twins.

That is not a narrative. That is a fact. The numbers say it happened.

How Friday's Sweep Happened

Game 1 was not close. Jake Bennett gave Boston six sharp innings, and Masataka Yoshida and Carlos Navarez both homered as the Red Sox rolled to a 10-0 shutout.

Game 2 required more. The Red Sox won 5-3 behind Wilyer Abreu, who homered twice in the same game. Willson Contreras made his return from a five-game suspension, giving interim manager Chad Tracy a full lineup to work with.

Two games. Two wins. Eleven in a row.

The Scale of the Turnaround

The math here is worth stating clearly. Boston went 16-2 over their last 18 games to climb from 14 games under .500 to exactly .500. That rate of play over a full season would project to a 118-win pace. They are not sustaining it over a full season. But they are sustaining it long enough to matter.

The Rays entered Friday at 56-38. The Yankees are near the top of the AL East. Boston is not chasing a division title. But a Wild Card spot — the third and final — is genuinely within reach, and the Red Sox own it right now.

Chad Tracy's Imprint

Tracy took over as interim manager earlier this season and has quietly built something cohesive in the clubhouse. The roster has not changed dramatically. The results have.

What Tracy has done is make this team believe it belongs in a pennant race. A roster that looked like it was heading for a fire sale on June 24 is now preparing for a series with the belief that it can win. That shift does not happen without someone in the dugout making it real.

What's Next

The Red Sox were considered sellers heading into August 3. That calculus is now reversed — Boston will stand pat or potentially add, rather than dismantle. The winning streak continues into the weekend, and each game adds another data point to one of the more remarkable second-half stories the American League has produced in years. The Wild Card spot is real. The question now is whether the Sox can hold it through the final two months of the season.
