### STORY 1: Chase Burns — 7-Year, $105M Extension

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

The Cincinnati Reds were supposed to be sellers this summer.

Instead, they just signed Chase Burns — their 23-year-old All-Star right-hander — to a seven-year, $105 million extension that runs through 2033. No options. No deferrals. Just $15 million flat per year, and a declaration that Cincinnati is done rebuilding and ready to compete.

Burns has a 2.54 ERA with 118 strikeouts across 18 starts in 2026. He's been one of the most coveted names on the trade market for months. Now he's not available. The deal is the largest extension ever given to a pitcher with fewer than four years of MLB service time — a bet on a young ace before he's even reached arbitration.

The Reds are officially out of sell mode. The deadline landscape just changed.

### STORY 2: Red Sox 11-Game Winning Streak

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

On June 24, the Boston Red Sox were 14 games under .500 and looked like textbook sellers heading into the August 3 trade deadline.

Then something shifted.

Eleven wins later — including a Friday sweep of the Tampa Bay Rays — the Red Sox are 48-48, back to .500 for the first time since March, and sitting in the American League's third Wild Card spot. The franchise hasn't won 11 straight since September 2016.

Interim manager Chad Tracy has built something nobody expected. Wilyer Abreu homered twice in Friday's Game 2. Jake Bennett gave Boston six sharp innings in the shutout opener. The team has gone 16-2 in its last 18 games and climbed past the Mariners and Twins in the Wild Card race.

The same roster that looked like a teardown a month ago is now in the postseason picture. That's the kind of story that defines a summer.

### STORY 3: Dodgers 2, Yankees 1

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

Gerrit Cole gave the Yankees everything Friday night.

Six-plus innings. Eight strikeouts. One walk. Four hits. One hundred and three pitches. He was the best pitcher on the field by a wide margin.

He still lost.

Max Muncy stepped in against Cole's 103rd pitch in the seventh inning — with Aaron Boone having decided his ace could handle one more batter — and hit a two-run home run into the right-field second deck at Yankee Stadium. Final: Dodgers 2, Yankees 1.

It was the first time these teams had met since last October's World Series. Shohei Ohtani played designated hitter in his return from a knee injection and went 0-for-4. Tanner Scott earned his 14th save. Boone was self-critical after the game — and he should be.

Cole pitched a gem. One pitch too many cost him the win.

### STORY 4: Tarik Skubal Trade Deadline

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

A few weeks ago, Tarik Skubal's trade felt like a formality.

Now it isn't.

Skubal has told people close to him that he badly wants to stay in Detroit. He believes this Tigers team can win the World Series. And looking at what Detroit has done since June 1 — playing outstanding baseball and climbing into the AL picture — it's hard to say he's wrong.

An NL executive called Detroit's early asking price "outrageous." The Cubs, Braves, and Rays remain active suitors. But Skubal — who owns a 3.09 ERA, 0.95 WHIP, and 89 strikeouts in 75.2 innings across 13 starts — is not just a rental. He's a franchise piece on a team that has suddenly started playing like one.

Sixteen days until August 3. The deadline picture on this one is genuinely complicated.

### STORY 5: White Sox / Colson Montgomery

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

Two years ago, the Chicago White Sox lost 121 games. Last year, 102.

On Friday night, they beat the Toronto Blue Jays 12-4 and moved to 51-45 at the top of the AL Central.

Colson Montgomery was the story. He hit a bases-clearing double in the fifth inning — three RBI — and then capped his night with the first triple of his big-league career in the seventh — three more. Six RBI total in a performance that felt like a statement.

The White Sox are now riding a four-game winning streak with Montgomery, Murakami, and Miguel Vargas all on pace to top 38 home runs this season. If all three hit that mark, it would be the first time in franchise history three White Sox teammates have reached it in the same year.

Nobody had the Chicago White Sox leading the AL Central in 2026. They don't care.
