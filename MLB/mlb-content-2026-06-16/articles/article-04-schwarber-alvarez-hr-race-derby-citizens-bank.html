Tied at 24: Schwarber and Alvarez Are Running the Best Race in Baseball — and the Derby Is at Schwarber's Park

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 16, 2026

Through June 15, Kyle Schwarber and Yordan Alvarez each have 24 home runs. They have been tracking each other for weeks. Every day one of them connects, you check to see what the other did. Most days, the answer is: the same thing.

The 2026 MLB Home Run Derby is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. That is Schwarber's home stadium. The setup is almost too good to be scripted.

How the Race Stands

Schwarber has 24 home runs as the Phillies' cleanup hitter in a lineup that also includes Bryce Harper and a cast of powerful bats. He is slugging the ball to the pull side and the opposite field with equal authority in 2026, which is what makes him so difficult to pitch around — there is no safe quadrant of the strike zone.

Alvarez is putting together another typically dominant season for Houston. The Royals shut out the Astros 4-0 on Sunday, holding Alvarez in check, but that is the kind of day that happens across a long season. He will be back in the lineup Monday swinging for his 25th.

Behind them: Byron Buxton of Minnesota is third at 22. Ben Rice of New York is at 19. Aaron Judge, who might normally be in this conversation, has been on the injured list since May 31 with a right rib stress fracture and has no confirmed return timeline.

The Derby Calculus

The Home Run Derby at Citizens Bank Park will be one of the summer's marquee events regardless of the participants. But if Schwarber is in the field — and he should be, as the home team's star and the co-leader in home runs — he will carry an advantage that goes beyond batting practice swings.

Schwarber knows every inch of Citizens Bank Park. He knows where the ball carries in July heat. He knows how the crowd reacts to different parts of the field. He has hit home runs in nearly every section of that ballpark during his time in Philadelphia. The crowd will be unconditionally on his side in a way that creates real energy. Home run derbies are won and lost on momentum as much as anything else.

If Alvarez participates, he brings his own brand of power — the grand slam type, the kind that clears the batters' eye with room to spare. He has done it in playoff games. He will not be intimidated by a Philadelphia crowd, however loud they get.

What's Next

Twenty-eight days to the Derby. Both men will add to their totals before then. The race could break open — one could get hot and pull away — or it could stay perfectly even all the way to July 14 when they both step into the cage and start swinging.

Either way, the home run race of summer 2026 has its two protagonists locked in. This is the best non-playoff baseball argument in the sport right now.
