Braves Sliding Without Acuña — NL East Lead Holds at 8, But the Losing Streak Warrants Watching

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | June 16, 2026

The Mets beat the Braves 8-1 on Sunday. It was a lopsided result that fit the pattern Atlanta has been in since Ronald Acuña Jr. went back on the injured list June 10 with a strained left hamstring — his second IL stint this season, both involving the same injury to the same leg.

The Braves are 46-25. They have the best record in the National League East and an 8-game lead over the second-place Phillies. The lead is comfortable. The slide is not catastrophic. But it is real, and the team is not the same without its franchise cornerstone.

What the Numbers Say

Before he left the game June 10, Acuña was slashing .251/.373/.421 with 7 home runs and 22 RBIs in 53 games. He started the season slowly, then heated up after returning from his first IL stint. Now he is out again.

The supporting cast remains talented. Chris Sale has been one of the NL's best pitchers in 2026. Michael Olson has 20-plus home runs. Ozzie Albies is leading NL second basemen in All-Star voting, which reflects how well he has played. The Braves have the roster depth to weather a short Acuña absence. They proved that after the first IL stint.

But there is a difference between one short absence and two absences to the same injury in the same season. The second recurrence raises legitimate questions about Acuña's hamstring and how long Atlanta will be without him.

The Division Picture

With the Phillies at 38-33 and Zack Wheeler pitching the way he did Sunday, the gap is not insurmountable — not over a full season — but it is large enough that Atlanta would have to truly collapse and Philadelphia would have to surge simultaneously for a real NL East race to materialize.

New York's Mets, despite beating Atlanta Sunday, are 32-39 and 14 games back. They are not a contender. Washington and Miami are further behind. This remains Atlanta's division to lose.

But losing is easier without Acuña. That much is clear.

What's Next

The Braves need Acuña healthy. The team's doctors have characterized the hamstring strain as fairly mild, with hopes for a short absence. When Acuña returns — and the club remains optimistic he will — Atlanta will again be the clear favorite to represent the NL East in October. Until then, Sale, Olson, Albies, and Drake Baldwin will need to keep the ship steady.

The NL East race is not over. But the Braves' margin for extended inconsistency is narrower than it looked two weeks ago, before the second hamstring trip to the injured list.
