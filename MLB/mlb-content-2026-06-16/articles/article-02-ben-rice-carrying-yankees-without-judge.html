Ben Rice Is Carrying the Yankees — And an AL MVP Case Is Taking Shape

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 16, 2026

Aaron Judge went on the injured list May 31 with a stress fracture of the right rib. No official return timeline exists. Manager Aaron Boone has expressed confidence that Judge plays again in 2026, but the Yankees' captain has not appeared in a game in more than two weeks.

None of that has stopped New York from leading the American League East.

The Yankees are 43-27. They beat the Blue Jays 8-3 on Monday. The reason, more than any other single factor: Ben Rice.

The Numbers Are Real

Rice's 2026 line through June 15: .300 batting average, .393 on-base percentage, .638 slugging percentage, 1.031 OPS. He has hit 19 home runs, driven in 44 runs, scored 46 times, and collected 62 hits including 15 doubles. He is leading the Yankees in every major offensive category.

On June 14, with the Yankees and Blue Jays tied late, Rice hit a two-run home run in the ninth inning to seal the series. It was his 19th home run of the season and a representative snapshot of what he has become: the most reliable producer in New York's lineup on a day-to-day basis.

The AL MVP conversation has historically been dominated by outfielders and designated hitters with massive power numbers. Rice checks every box the voters look for — batting average, on-base skills, home runs, run production, and team success. If the Yankees win the AL East, Rice will have been the central reason why.

Filling a Titan's Absence

It is worth being clear: Rice is not replacing Aaron Judge. Nobody does that. But he is filling the lineup hole Judge's absence created and then some. When a team's best player misses two months of the season and the team continues winning, you look for the player who stepped into the void. In New York's case, that is Rice, who at 27 is now in the prime of a career that has rapidly accelerated from promising prospect to legitimate star.

The Yankees' front office made investments this winter to build depth around Judge. Rice is the return on those investments, plus his own considerable development. He has arrived at the right moment.

What's Next

Judge will be re-evaluated in the coming weeks, and there is hope he returns before the All-Star break or shortly after. When he does, New York will have a legitimate claim to the most dangerous lineup in the American League. For now, the Yankees are winning behind Rice, and the AL East lead over Tampa Bay (41-27) is two games and holding.

The All-Star vote closes June 25. If Judge cannot participate due to injury, Rice — with his .300 average and 19 home runs — has an argument to represent New York at Citizens Bank Park. It would be a fitting stage for his breakout year.
