All-Star Voting: Ohtani Leads All Players, Phase 1 Closes June 25 — and Judge's Injury Opens the Door for Rice

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 16, 2026

The 2026 MLB All-Star Game is coming to Philadelphia on July 14, and the vote to determine who plays in it is entering its final stretch.

Phase 1 of fan voting closes June 25 at noon ET. Players who lead their position in Phase 1 earn automatic starting spots. The game is 28 days away. The rosters are beginning to take shape, with a few storylines worth tracking closely.

The Vote Leaders

Shohei Ohtani is leading all players in the 2026 ballot with 1,165,133 votes. If the vote holds, it would mark the first time the Los Angeles Dodgers' two-way superstar topped the overall vote totals. He is well ahead of the field.

In the American League, Yordan Alvarez leads all position players with 1,015,768 votes. Bobby Witt Jr. of Kansas City is in front of AL shortstops with 890,575. Witt has been one of the AL's most valuable players in 2026 and the vote reflects it.

In the National League, Ronald Acuña Jr. and Ozzie Albies of Atlanta are both tracking toward starting spots. Drake Baldwin, the Braves' catcher, has 972,813 NL catcher votes and leads his position. Atlanta fans voted in force.

Judge's Absence and the Rice Opportunity

The most interesting subplot heading into the July 14 game involves Aaron Judge. The Yankees' captain and AL MVP candidate went on the injured list May 31 with a stress fracture of the right rib. No official return timeline has been set. He has not played in more than two weeks.

Judge's injury casts doubt on his All-Star availability. If he cannot participate, attention turns naturally to Ben Rice — who has led the Yankees in nearly every offensive category during Judge's absence and is hitting .300 with a 1.031 OPS and 19 home runs. Rice would be a deserving participant based purely on 2026 performance. The absence of the sport's biggest star creates the vacancy.

What's Next

The Phase 1 vote closes June 25 at noon ET. Position leaders become automatic starters. Phase 2 follows, allowing fans to vote between the top two finishers at each position. Managers, players, and the Commissioner's Office fill remaining roster spots.

The game itself — July 14, Citizens Bank Park, Philadelphia — will host the Home Run Derby the night before (July 13) with Kyle Schwarber and Yordan Alvarez both at 24 home runs and almost certain to participate. The All-Star week in Philadelphia is shaping up to be a memorable one.

You have nine days to vote before Phase 1 closes. Use them.
