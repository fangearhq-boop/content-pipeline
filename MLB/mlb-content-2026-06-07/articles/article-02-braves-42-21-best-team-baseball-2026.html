The Braves Are Baseball's Best Team — And It Isn't Close

By Jake Torres June 7, 2026

The Best Record in Baseball Belongs to Atlanta

At the two-month mark, one team has established itself as the clear measuring stick for everyone else in Major League Baseball. The Atlanta Braves are 42-21 — the best record in the sport. They lead the NL East by 8.5 games over the Philadelphia Phillies. ESPN has ranked them No. 1 for the fourth consecutive week. And there is no credible argument against the premise: the Braves are the best team in baseball right now.

How They Got Here

The Braves built their roster to win now, and the investment is paying off. Chris Sale has anchored the starting rotation — his presence at the top of the depth chart sets the tone for everything that follows. Ronald Acuña Jr. came through a thumb bone bruise concern without significant missed time and has been playing his usual dynamic game. Matt Olson and Austin Riley give the middle of the lineup legitimate protection at both corners.

The depth matters, too. When any team sustains the best record in baseball for two months, it is not doing it with just five starters and three relievers performing well. The Braves' roster has had contributions across 25 spots, and the organizational depth behind them has limited the damage when individual players have been unavailable.

The NL Picture Around Them

The Los Angeles Dodgers are 40-22 — excellent, and the team that most observers consider the Braves' most likely World Series opponent. The Milwaukee Brewers are 37-22. For the first time this season, the top three teams in ESPN's power rankings are all from the National League, which reflects how well the NL's best programs have outperformed expectations in 2026.

The NL East gap tells the story most clearly. At 8.5 games, Atlanta has built the kind of cushion that allows a manager to rest players, limit high-leverage bullpen usage, and prepare for October without burning out key contributors. The Phillies at 33-29 are playing .530 ball, which would typically position a team to compete — but they're still 8.5 back because the Braves have been so consistently excellent.

Is This a World Series Team?

The honest answer is: probably yes, barring significant injury. The Braves check the boxes that October contenders need. They have an elite starter at the top of the rotation. They have a lineup without obvious holes. They play solid defense. And they have the kind of winning culture that comes from sustained success over multiple seasons.

The Dodgers are the other team that checks those same boxes. If both teams hold their positions and meet in the NLCS, that series will be worth watching for every baseball fan regardless of team affiliation. But for now, the Braves own the best record — and they've earned it.

What's Next

The Braves enter the second week of June with the largest division lead in baseball and no reason to panic about anyone catching them. The trade deadline in August and the playoff expansion format are both working in their favor — a team with their record will be heavily favored to finish with a top seed and home field advantage throughout the postseason. For now, they play, they win, and the rest of baseball watches.
