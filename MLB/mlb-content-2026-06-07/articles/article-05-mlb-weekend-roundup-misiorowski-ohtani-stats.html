Weekend in Baseball: Misiorowski's Velocity, Ohtani's Historic ERA and the Week That Was

By Jake Torres June 7, 2026

The Stats Worth Carrying Into Monday

A full baseball weekend means a full baseball weekend's worth of individual performance worth cataloguing. From a velocity record tour in Milwaukee to a two-way season in Los Angeles that keeps defying historical precedent, here are the numbers and moments that deserve attention as the week begins.

Misiorowski Continues Velocity Record Run

Jacob Misiorowski pitched for the Milwaukee Brewers in their 7-1 win over the Colorado Rockies on Saturday, and the young right-hander continued his reputation as one of the hardest-throwing starters in the game. Misiorowski set an all-time velocity record for a starting pitcher when he reached 103.6 miles per hour on May 27 — a performance that included 12 strikeouts. This weekend's outing added to the file of evidence that his velocity is not a fluke or a brief peak.

What makes Misiorowski special is not just the raw speed. At 100-plus miles per hour, pitchers can get away with location mistakes because hitters do not have enough time to process the pitch properly. But Misiorowski also changes speeds and locates the fastball in different quadrants of the zone, which makes it genuinely difficult to time. The Brewers have something rare in him.

Ohtani: The Two-Way Numbers Keep Getting Better

Shohei Ohtani's 2026 season continues to be the kind of performance that requires historical context to fully appreciate. Through his first ten starts, he has posted a 0.74 ERA. For reference, that is the third-lowest ERA for any pitcher through their first ten starts of a season in the live-ball era since earned runs became an official statistic in 1913. Only Jacob deGrom — who posted a 0.56 ERA through his first ten starts in 2021 — and Juan Marichal — who posted a 0.59 ERA in 1966 — have been better.

He is also the National League on-base percentage leader at .420. The same man who is rewriting pitcher ERA history at the start of his season is also one of the best hitters in the league. The conversation about where Ohtani fits in the all-time rankings will have to wait until his career is over. For now, it is enough to appreciate what is happening in real time.

Mariners Blank Tigers on Sunday

The Seattle Mariners shut out the Detroit Tigers 4-0 on Sunday. It is one result among dozens this weekend, but it carries additional context given Tarik Skubal's rehab start happening simultaneously. The Tigers were held scoreless while their ace was testing his elbow in the minor leagues. That combination — a blanked offense and a potential ace returning — captures the state of Detroit's season as succinctly as any single day could.

The Week Ahead

The AL East race is half a game with Judge out. The Braves at 42-21 are the undisputed best team in baseball. Skubal's potential return to the Tigers rotation could come as early as June 14. Murakami's AL Rookie of the Month award was announced. The week ahead promises more of what the first two months of 2026 have delivered: an unusually rich season of baseball with genuine historical performances happening regularly.

What's Next

Monday starts a fresh set of series across both leagues. The Skubal situation resolves one way or another by next weekend. The AL East standings will be watched daily. Ohtani's next start will draw a full house in Los Angeles and a full audience across the country. Misiorowski's next appearance will have velocity sensors pointed at his release point. This is what mid-June baseball looks like in 2026 — compelling in almost every direction you look.
