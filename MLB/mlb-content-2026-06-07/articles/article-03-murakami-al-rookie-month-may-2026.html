Murakami Wins AL Rookie of the Month: The Historic Start That Earned the Hardware

By Ryan Calloway June 7, 2026

The Award Is Official

Munetaka Murakami of the Chicago White Sox has been named the American League Rookie of the Month for May 2026. The official recognition formalizes what baseball observers had been watching all month: one of the most extraordinary early-career offensive performances in the history of the game.

In 26 games during May, the 26-year-old first baseman hit .244 with 8 home runs, 18 RBI, 4 doubles, 19 walks, 21 runs scored, a .382 on-base percentage and a .556 slugging percentage. That is a .938 OPS for the month.

The Historical Context

Across his first 55 career MLB games, Murakami hit 20 home runs — the fourth-fastest player in MLB history to reach that mark. He is the fastest Japanese-born player to reach 20 career MLB home runs, surpassing Shohei Ohtani, who needed 101 games to accomplish the same milestone.

The advanced numbers paint an equally compelling picture. His 2026 average exit velocity of 94.2, hard-hit percentage of 58.7, wOBA of .400, and barrel rate of 20.7 percent are all marks that identify elite contact hitters with genuine power. These are not stats produced by a player getting lucky. They are the results of someone who knows exactly what he is doing at the plate.

Murakami also tied the White Sox franchise record with a five-game home run streak earlier in May — one of those moments that reminded the entire baseball world that something genuinely special was happening on the South Side of Chicago.

The IL Pause Does Not Erase What He Did

Murakami was placed on the injured list around May 31 with a Grade 2 hamstring strain. The projected recovery timeline is approximately four weeks, which puts his return in the late June to early July window — potentially before the All-Star Game. The injury is unfortunate but not structurally serious, and the White Sox expect him back contributing in the second half.

The Rookie of the Month award covers his May performance, not his current availability. What he did in that 26-game stretch was remarkable on its own terms, and the recognition reflects what the voters saw.

The Bigger Story: The White Sox Rebuild Is Working

In 2024, the Chicago White Sox lost 121 games — one of the worst single-season records in modern Major League Baseball history. The rebuilding plan that followed was supposed to take years to produce results. In 2026, the White Sox are a Wild Card contender, and the engine of their transformation is a 26-year-old Japanese import who is making the adjustment to North American pitching look effortless.

The turnaround is not solely attributable to Murakami. Other players have contributed to this team's competitive resurgence. But he is the face of it. And when he comes back from the hamstring injury, the AL ROY race and the White Sox playoff push both get dramatically more interesting.

What's Next

Murakami's return is projected for late June or early July. When he comes back, the question is whether he picks up where he left off. At 20 home runs in 55 games, an extrapolated full-season pace would put him in historically elite territory. The hamstring is a legitimate concern, but the offensive profile that earned the Rookie of Month award is real. The second half of 2026 may tell us whether what he did in the first two months was a preview or a peak.
