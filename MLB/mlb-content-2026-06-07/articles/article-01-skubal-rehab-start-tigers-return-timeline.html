Skubal's Comeback Starts Today: Rehab Start Puts Tigers Ace on Track for June 14 Return

By Marcus Cole June 7, 2026

The Left Arm Is Active Again

Less than five weeks after surgery, Tarik Skubal is back on a mound. The Detroit Tigers placed their two-time Cy Young Award winner on a rehab assignment with the High-A West Michigan Whitecaps, and Skubal made his first live competitive start of the 2026 post-surgery era on Sunday against Dayton.

If all goes according to plan, this is the last stop before the major leagues. The Tigers have targeted the June 14 series at Cleveland as a realistic return date. The entire organization — and much of the baseball world — is watching closely.

The Nano-Scope Difference

The surgery Skubal underwent on May 6 was a nano-scope procedure to remove a loose body from his left elbow. It is a newer, less invasive approach compared to traditional arthroscopic surgery, which historically has required two to three months of recovery time. Skubal's rapid progress is, in part, a testament to how much the procedure has evolved.

Scott Boras, Skubal's agent, expected a short rehab. What "short" meant in practical terms was confirmed when Whitecaps manager AJ Hinch announced the rehab assignment: Skubal would face professional hitters before the week was out. That's remarkable for a starting pitcher who was on the operating table less than a month and a half ago.

The decision to use West Michigan rather than Triple-A Toledo came down to weather — the forecast in Grand Rapids was more favorable than in Iowa this weekend. The baseball itself doesn't change. The conditions for evaluating his recovery are the priority.

What the Tigers Have Been Without Him

The numbers tell the story plainly. Since Skubal went on the injured list around May 4, the Tigers went 6-21. The rotation without their ace has been unable to compensate. Detroit has fallen significantly in the AL Central standings during that stretch, and every game without Skubal has emphasized how much one elite arm can mean to a 26-man roster.

His return matters beyond 2026, too. Every start Skubal makes before the August 3 trade deadline affects his market value. He becomes a free agent at the end of the season. A healthy Skubal pitching well in late June and July is the best possible advertisement for what a trading partner would be getting. The teams that need a frontline starter are paying close attention.

What to Watch From Today's Start

For Tigers fans and observers, the metrics that matter today are velocity, movement, and command. Skubal's fastball before surgery was elite. If he's working at full effort without hesitation, that's the best possible sign. If the Tigers manage his pitch count conservatively — as expected — an exit after two or three innings still constitutes a successful return.

There will not be a formal declaration of "all clear" today. Today is an information-gathering exercise. But the fact that the exercise is happening five weeks after the scalpel is a strong statement about how well the recovery has gone.

What's Next

If today's start goes well, Skubal returns to the Tigers rotation for the June 14 series at Cleveland. From there, the rest of the season plays out with Detroit's most important player back in the mix. What the Tigers do at the trade deadline — keep him and compete, or deal him to the highest bidder — remains the most consequential front-office decision they will face this summer.
