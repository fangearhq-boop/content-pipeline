### STORY 3: Murakami Named AL Rookie of Month

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

The award was official. The season was historic. And the best part? He's coming back.

Munetaka Murakami of the Chicago White Sox was named the American League Rookie of the Month for May 2026 — and the numbers explain why it wasn't a close call. In 26 games during May, the 26-year-old hit .244 with 8 home runs, 18 RBI, 19 walks, a .382 on-base percentage and a .556 slugging percentage. That's a .936 OPS for the month.

Across his first 55 career MLB games, Murakami hit 20 home runs — the fourth-fastest player in MLB history to reach that milestone. He's the fastest Japanese-born player to reach 20 career MLB home runs, surpassing Shohei Ohtani who needed 101 games to get there.

He's currently on the injured list with a Grade 2 hamstring strain that landed him there around May 31. The projected recovery is approximately four weeks, putting his return window at late June to early July. That means he could be back before the All-Star Game.

The broader story around Murakami is what he represents for the White Sox. This is a franchise that lost 121 games in 2024. The rebuild was supposed to take years. Now they're a Wild Card contender with a legitimate AL ROY frontrunner and one of the most compelling offensive players in the league. The turnaround has been remarkable.

When Murakami comes back from the IL, watch for the home run race to heat up again. He's only going to get better as he continues adjusting to MLB pitching.
