Shohei Ohtani's Next Pitching Start Is April 8 — Two-Way Season on Full Display

By Marcus Cole | April 4, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Shohei Ohtani's 2026 season is already moving at a breathtaking pace, and the week ahead only adds to the theater. The Los Angeles Dodgers' two-way superstar is scheduled to make his second pitching start of the year on April 8 against the Toronto Blue Jays, following a first week that featured a dominant mound performance and his first home run of the season.

On March 31, Ohtani took the ball for his 2026 pitching debut and threw six scoreless innings on 87 pitches. The message was clear: last year's Cy Young-caliber pitcher is back, healthy, and ready to win games. Then, on April 3 against the Washington Nationals, Ohtani picked up his bat and launched a 3-run homer in the third inning — 401 feet, 109.5 mph exit velocity — as the Dodgers won 13-6. He finished the night 2-for-5 with four RBIs.

How the Dodgers Are Managing His Workload

Los Angeles is operating Ohtani in a six-man rotation, giving him extra rest between starts while keeping his body fresh for the full 162-game haul and an inevitable deep postseason run. Manager Dave Roberts has spoken about targeting the sixth inning at approximately 75 to 80 pitches per start — a conservative approach designed to protect Ohtani's arm through a full two-way season without the limitations that kept him off the mound in 2024.

The Blue Jays, who got on the wrong end of Dylan Cease's Toronto debut this week, will now face Ohtani on the mound Tuesday. Toronto enters the series having lost Kirk to injury and with questions about their catching situation — adding an Ohtani pitching start to the mix doesn't make things easier for the Blue Jays.

The Historic Context

Ohtani's pursuit of a full two-way season has no true historical precedent in modern baseball. The Dodgers built their rotation around his schedule. The lineup builds around his presence in the heart of the order. When he pitches, he's among the best arms in the game. When he hits, he's among the most dangerous sluggers. That both things happen in the same season is something baseball has never seen at this level.

The first week of results — one pitching gem, one multi-RBI offensive explosion — suggests 2026 could be the most complete two-way season of Ohtani's career and, by extension, of any player who has attempted this at the major league level.

What's Next

April 8 vs. Toronto is the next appointment on Ohtani's pitching calendar. Watch his pitch count and inning progression as the Dodgers fine-tune their workload management. If the first two starts are any indication, the Dodgers — and the rest of baseball — are in for a remarkable year of watching what Ohtani can do when nothing stands in his way.
