Ohtani Hits First HR of 2026, Dodgers Demolish Nationals 13-6

By Ryan Calloway | April 4, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

It had been seven games, but Shohei Ohtani finally went yard in 2026 — and the result was vintage Dodgers.

Ohtani launched a 3-run home run in the third inning against Washington on Friday, sparking a 10-run Dodgers response that turned a 3-3 tie into a 13-6 rout. Los Angeles finished with five home runs on the night, a full-lineup demonstration that this offense is capable of putting up crooked numbers at any moment.

Starter Emmett Sheehan got the win, going 5.2 innings. On the other end, Miles Mikolas had a rough afternoon, surrendering a career-high 11 runs in just 4.1 innings as Washington's pitching staff had no answer for the Dodgers' depth.

The Ohtani Number to Know: 38

Ohtani's on-base streak extended to 38 consecutive games with Friday's performance, a streak that began during the 2025 season and has carried unbroken into 2026. Getting on base in 38 consecutive games is not an accident — it speaks to plate discipline and contact ability that complements the power.

Kyle Tucker also had a landmark night, homering for the first time as a member of the Los Angeles Dodgers. Tucker was one of the offseason's most significant acquisitions, and his first homer in Dodger blue is a moment Dodger fans will remember. Mookie Betts and Freddie Freeman also went deep, making Friday an emphatic statement about what this lineup can do.

Dodgers 4-2 in First Week

Los Angeles has navigated its opening week at 4-2, a strong start for a team with World Series expectations. The early road series against Cleveland produced mixed results, but the Dodgers appear to be finding their footing at the plate. If Ohtani is hitting home runs and Tucker is contributing, the NL West competition will face significant challenges all season.

Washington fell to 3-3 with the loss, and Mikolas' nightmare start will be something the rotation needs to move past quickly. The Nationals are in a rebuilding phase and losing by seven runs to the Dodgers is not unexpected — but a career-worst start by their veteran right-hander made the afternoon especially difficult.

What's Next

The Dodgers and Nationals play their series finale on Saturday in Washington. Ohtani's pitching start will come later in the week when the Dodgers return home. Watch for more big production from Tucker and the rest of the Dodger lineup as the two-way star continues to grow into his 2026 offensive rhythm.
