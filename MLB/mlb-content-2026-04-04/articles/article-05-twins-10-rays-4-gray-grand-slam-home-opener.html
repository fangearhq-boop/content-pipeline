Twins Hammer Rays 10-4 in Home Opener — Gray Grand Slam Caps 7-Run 7th

By Jake Torres | April 4, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Minnesota's Target Field gave their home opener crowd exactly what they were hoping for on Friday — a comeback, a big inning, and a convincing final score.

The Twins trailed Tampa Bay entering the seventh inning, then erupted for seven runs in the frame, capped by a Tristan Gray grand slam, to pull away and beat the Rays 10-4. It was the kind of offensive explosion that generates early-season momentum, and the packed home opener crowd was on its feet for the entire stretch.

Gray's Grand Slam Moment

Tristan Gray's grand slam was the signature moment of the Minnesota home opener. A bases-loaded shot in a seven-run inning against a Tampa Bay bullpen that simply couldn't hold the game in the seventh put everything out of reach and gave the Twins a victory that the standing-room crowd will remember into May.

Early-season home opener moments matter differently than a mid-July game. They set the tone for how a fanbase experiences the first months of the campaign, and Minnesota gave their fans a statement performance.

Rays Fall to 2-4

Tampa Bay is now 2-4 after seven games, a troubling start for a franchise that has consistently punched above its payroll weight over the past decade. The Rays' early-season stumble mirrors the Red Sox, who sit at 1-5, and both teams will need to find their footing before the AL East race becomes difficult to reverse.

A 2-4 record through seven games is not a crisis — 155 games remain, and teams have recovered from far worse starts. But the Rays have built their reputation on consistency and efficient winning, and these first seven games have not reflected that standard.

What's Next

The Twins and Rays conclude their series with a Saturday matchup at Target Field. Minnesota will be looking to win the series and establish some early momentum in the AL Central, where Cleveland (4-3) and Kansas City (3-3) are the early pace-setters. Tampa Bay needs a win to stop the early slide before it becomes a defining narrative for their April.
