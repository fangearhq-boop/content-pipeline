Week 1 MLB Standings: Marlins Lead NL East, Yankees Top AL East — The Early Surprises

By Jake Torres | April 4, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

After the first full week of the 2026 MLB season, the standings are beginning to take shape — and they include at least one major surprise. Here's where every division stands heading into the final games of the opening weekend.

American League

The New York Yankees (5-1) lead the AL East by a game over the Toronto Blue Jays (4-2). Aaron Judge's 371st career home run opened the Yankees' home opener in dramatic fashion, and New York has been everything a 5-1 record suggests: deep lineup, reliable rotation, and no apparent weaknesses in the early going. The Yankees enter Week 2 as the clear AL East favorites.

In the AL West, the Houston Astros (5-2) lead Texas (4-2) by half a game, with the two programs establishing themselves as the division's frontrunners while the Athletics (1-5) struggle in the opposite direction. The AL Central is tightly bunched with Cleveland (4-3) holding a slim lead.

National League — Where the Surprises Are

The biggest early story in baseball might be the Miami Marlins sitting at 5-1 and leading the NL East. The Marlins were projected as a rebuilding team entering the year, but Sandy Alcantara and a veteran pitching staff have delivered wins that nobody predicted would come this early or this consistently. Miami leads Atlanta (5-2) by half a game in a division that was expected to be Philadelphia's to lose.

In the NL Central, the Milwaukee Brewers (5-1) are right alongside Miami as one of the league's best records. The Brewers have long been one of baseball's most efficiently run organizations, and their typical strong start suggests another competitive year in the Central.

The Los Angeles Dodgers (4-2) lead the NL West, with Arizona and San Francisco both sitting at 3-4 in a competitive division. The Dodgers' depth advantage — particularly once Ohtani hits full two-way stride — figures to assert itself as the season progresses.

What's Next

Sunday closes out the opening series weekend, and the standings will shift again before the second week of play begins Monday. The NL East race between Miami, Atlanta, and Philadelphia is shaping up as one of baseball's most compelling early stories. The Yankees' AL East lead will be tested as Blue Jays, Rays, Orioles, and Red Sox find their footing over the next month. Week 2 gets underway Monday with Ohtani's April 8 pitching start against Toronto as the marquee event on the horizon.
