Judge HR No. 371 in First At-Bat of Home Opener — Yankees Beat Marlins 8-2, Now 6-1

By Marcus Cole | April 4, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Aaron Judge needed exactly one at-bat to announce that the 2026 Yankees are very much open for business.

On the first pitch he saw at Yankee Stadium's home opener on Friday night, Judge drove a 1-1 slider from Marlins right-hander Eury Perez 387 feet to left field — 101.2 mph off the bat — for a 2-run home run. It was the 371st home run of his career, moving him past Hall of Famer Gil Hodges into sole possession of 83rd place on baseball's all-time list.

New York went on to defeat Miami 8-2, improving to 6-1 on the season. The Yankees own the best record in the American League.

A Milestone on Opening Night at The Stadium

Judge arrived at Yankee Stadium with 369 career home runs. He reached 370 earlier this road trip, and 371 came in the most theatrical way possible — swinging on the first pitch of his first home at-bat of the year. The crowd erupted well before the ball landed.

The historical company at 371 home runs speaks to where Judge is in his career. At 34 years old, he has hit 50 or more home runs twice in his career (including 62 in 2022, the AL single-season record). If he sustains even a modest pace, the 400-home run milestone — an elite threshold — is firmly in view at some point during the 2026 or 2027 season. He needs 29 more to reach 400.

Judge has now homered three times in seven games to open 2026. He is driving the ball at the same exit velocity and launch angle that have defined his career peak, and Miami had no answer for it on Friday.

Yankees 6-1, AL's Best

New York's hot start has been built on pitching and timely power. The 8-2 win over the Marlins gave the Yankees their sixth win in seven games, and their 6-1 record leads the AL East by two games over the Blue Jays. The Marlins fell to 5-2 after the loss — still a strong record, but the gap between the two teams on Friday was apparent.

The home opener atmosphere at Yankee Stadium amplified everything. A full crowd, Judge's leadoff blast, and a convincing wire-to-wire win sent New York's fanbase home satisfied.

What's Next

The Yankees and Marlins wrap up their series with a Game 3 on Saturday in New York. Judge will keep climbing the all-time home run list with every swing — watch for milestone No. 375 and eventually the chase toward 400 to become one of the year's signature storylines. The Yankees' early 6-1 record has genuine AL East contention written all over it.
