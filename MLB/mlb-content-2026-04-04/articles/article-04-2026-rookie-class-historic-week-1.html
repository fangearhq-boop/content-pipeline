The 2026 MLB Rookie Class Had One of the Most Historic Opening Weeks in Baseball History

By Marcus Cole | April 4, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

After seven games, the 2026 MLB rookie class has already done things that have never been done before. Not "rarely done." Never done.

Chase DeLauter of the Cleveland Guardians has 5 home runs in 7 career games — only the 4th player in MLB history at that pace. Munetaka Murakami of the Chicago White Sox homered in each of his first three major league games, joining only a handful of players ever to accomplish that. Aaron Stewart of the Cincinnati Reds hit .700 in Week 1 and earned the NL Player of the Week award. DeLauter won the AL honor. That made this only the second time in history that both first weekly awards of a season went to rookies.

The other time? 2016, when Trevor Story and Tyler White did it.

The Full Rookie Ledger

Kevin McGonigle made his MLB debut for the Detroit Tigers with four hits — the third-youngest player to accomplish that in the last 100 years. Jackson Wetherholt delivered a walk-off home run for the St. Louis Cardinals, the kind of moment that typically defines an entire young career in a single swing. Carson Benge homered and stole a base in his debut for the New York Mets, checking every box for a lead-off prospect.

Six rookies. Six remarkable stories. All in the opening week of the season.

Why It Matters

Opening-week statistics are notoriously unreliable over 162 games. Hot starts fade. Pitchers make adjustments. The sample size problem is real. But what this rookie class has done in seven days is historically significant regardless of what comes next, and it sets up a Rookie of the Year race that will be one of the most competitive in recent memory.

DeLauter's pace — if sustained even partially — would put him among the most productive young sluggers in the modern era. Murakami brings international star power and a power-plus-discipline combination that is rare at any age. Stewart, Wetherholt, and Benge all project as legitimate contributors for competitive teams.

The talent is real. The history is already made. The question is just how far they go from here.

What's Next

The Rookie of the Year race will develop through April and May as the sample size grows and the league adjusts. Watch DeLauter vs. Murakami as the two leading power stories in each league. The first AL/NL monthly award announcements will arrive in early May and give the first real indication of how the race is shaping up.
