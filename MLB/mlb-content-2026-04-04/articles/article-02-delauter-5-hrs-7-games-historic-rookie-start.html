DeLauter Hits 5th HR in 7th Career Game — Only 4th Player in MLB History to Do It

By Jake Torres | April 4, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Chase DeLauter's MLB career is entering uncharted territory — and he has not yet completed his first full week.

The Cleveland Guardians rookie outfielder crushed his 5th home run in just his 7th career regular-season game on Friday, a 2-run blast off Hunter Harvey that traveled 402 feet, lifting Cleveland to a 4-1 home opener victory over the Chicago Cubs at Progressive Field. DeLauter went 3-for-4 on the night and is now tied for the American League home run lead.

He is only the 4th player in Major League Baseball history to homer five or more times in his first seven career regular-season games, joining Trevor Story (Colorado Rockies, 2016), Mark Quinn (Kansas City Royals, 1999), and Rece Hinds (Cincinnati Reds, 2024).

An Instant Home Opener Hero

Progressive Field was packed for Cleveland's first home game of the season, and DeLauter gave the crowd everything they could have wanted. The 402-foot blast off Harvey landed in the left field seats and capped a three-hit performance that had the sold-out stadium buzzing from the third inning on.

DeLauter entered Friday's game already generating national attention after his first six career games. Going 3-for-4 with a home run in the home opener only intensified that spotlight. At this pace, he is not just a feel-good story — he is a genuine statistical anomaly.

Historical Context

The players DeLauter is joining in the record books are not afterthoughts. Trevor Story went on to win the NL Rookie of the Year in 2016. Rece Hinds was just doing this last year. Quinn's 1999 debut remains one of the most electrifying short-career starts in Royals history. DeLauter has now placed himself in a category that only three players have occupied over the past century of baseball.

The Guardians are 4-3 on the season. DeLauter's production has been the brightest early storyline in Cleveland's campaign, and the team is winning games behind his bat.

What's Next

The Guardians and Cubs wrap their series with Game 3 on Saturday at Progressive Field. DeLauter will be the most watched hitter in every game for the foreseeable future. If he reaches 6 home runs in 8 career games, the historical comparisons will only grow louder. Cleveland's fanbase — and the entire baseball world — is watching.
