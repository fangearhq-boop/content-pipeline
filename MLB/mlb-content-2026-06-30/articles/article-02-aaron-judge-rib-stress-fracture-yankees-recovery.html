Aaron Judge Played With a Broken Rib for Six Weeks — Here's What Comes Next

By Jake Torres June 30, 2026

The Injury and the Reveal

Aaron Judge was diagnosed with a stress fracture in his right first rib on June 5, 2026, and placed on the injured list. That date is the most important one in this story. The second most important date is April 26.

April 26 is when Judge believes the injury occurred — diving for a fly ball during a series against the Houston Astros. Nearly six weeks passed between that moment and the diagnosis that sent him to the IL. During those six weeks, he kept playing. He kept hitting. He kept showing up in the Yankees lineup while a bone in his rib cage was fractured.

"I fought as long as I could," he said after the IL announcement. It was not a boast. It was a plain statement of fact from a player who is built around the idea of staying on the field.

The Recovery Timeline

A stress fracture in the rib does not heal through rest alone — it heals through rest with monitored progress. Manager Aaron Boone said after the IL placement that there is no firm timetable on re-imaging, which is the key evaluation step that will determine the return timeline.

Re-imaging is expected approximately 4-6 weeks from the June 5 diagnosis. That puts the evaluation window in early to mid-July. Based on that timeline, the earliest realistic return to game action would be sometime in August.

What that means practically: Judge will miss the All-Star Game entirely. He will miss at least the first two weeks of the second half. The Yankees will need to manage a shortened lineup through a stretch of the schedule that matters for the AL East race.

The Yankees Without Their Three Starters

Judge is not the only New York regular currently unavailable. Giancarlo Stanton and Austin Wells are also on the injured list. That is three starters — a significant portion of the lineup — unavailable at the same time during a period when the Boston Red Sox just swept them in a four-game series, with Jarren Duran delivering a walk-off single in the tenth inning of the finale.

The Yankees are navigating a stretch that tests depth, roster construction, and managerial creativity in ways that most AL East teams would not face. Boone has said there is "no firm timetable" on any of the three injured players, which means the Yankees are running a rolling inventory of who is available rather than a set lineup card.

What This Means for the AL MVP Race

Before the injury sent him to the IL, Judge was among the leading candidates for the 2026 American League MVP Award. His absence removes the Yankees' most visible presence from that conversation and reshuffles the race.

Yordan Alvarez has been consistent and productive for the Houston Astros all season. Bobby Witt Jr. — who returned from his own injury, a Grade 1 MCL sprain that cost him six games — is putting up the numbers that have made him one of the most dynamic players in the AL. With Judge sidelined and the award race open, Alvarez and Witt Jr. are the names at the center of the conversation.

If Judge comes back in August and produces at a high level over the final six weeks, he can still make a case. But a two-month absence in the middle of a season changes the math on an award that weighs performance over the full 162 games.

What's Next

The key date is mid-July, when the first post-IL evaluation gives a clearer picture of Judge's healing timeline. If the re-imaging shows normal fracture progression, an August return becomes a firm target rather than a best-case estimate. If it does not, the timeline extends further. The Yankees need him back not just for the lineup but for the second-half narrative of their season. The worst-case scenario is a return so late that the AL East race has already been decided without him.
