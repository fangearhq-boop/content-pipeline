The Skubal Dilemma: How Detroit Decides Whether to Trade Its Best Pitcher

By Marcus Cole June 30, 2026

The Situation

Tarik Skubal won the AL Cy Young Award in 2024. He won it again in 2025. He returned from bone chip surgery this year and is hitting 99.9 miles per hour with his fastball, answering the health question that loomed over his market value.

His team is 34-46.

The August 3 trade deadline is 34 days away. ESPN's Jeff Passan has him at 85 percent trade probability — meaning nearly every credible analyst and source expects him to be traded. The Dodgers, Yankees, Braves, Blue Jays, Brewers, and Rays are among the teams in discussions.

And yet. The Detroit Tigers are only 5 games out of the American League Wild Card.

The Case for Trading

The straightforward version of the trade argument runs like this: Skubal is the most valuable trade chip in baseball this deadline. His market is enormous. The asking price — comparable analysts have referenced the CC Sabathia 2008 trade as a baseline, meaning two or three top-100 prospects and likely a top-10 prospect — would represent a generational haul for Detroit. A team that is 12 games below .500 trades its best pitcher for the future, accepts the sunk cost of 2026, and builds toward a window where they can actually compete for a title.

The historical argument supports this too. Teams that held ace pitchers on the trade deadline out of Wild Card ambition and then missed the playoffs anyway often look back at that decision as the wrong one. The two-time Cy Young winner does not come around twice for any organization. When the market is at its peak, the return is at its peak.

The Case for Holding

Five games. That is the gap between Detroit and the Wild Card as of June 30. In baseball's expanded playoff format, five games in late June is a real distance — but it is not an impossible one. A team playing .600 ball over the next three weeks closes that gap before the deadline conversation becomes irreversible.

Skubal pitching on a team that surges into the Wild Card race looks very different from Skubal pitching on a 34-46 team. The narrative changes, the front office's calculus changes, and suddenly the most valuable pitcher in the market is pitching October baseball in Detroit. That outcome is unlikely — but the probability is not zero.

What the Next 20 Days Determine

The practical reality is that the Tigers have approximately 20 games between now and the point where a trade becomes necessary to complete the deal before the August 3 deadline. If Detroit plays .600 or better baseball over those 20 games, the math on holding improves meaningfully. If they play .400 or below, the math on trading becomes almost irresistible.

The front office is watching the same standings every night that everyone else is. They have the information. The question is which number moves the needle — whether it is the gap to the Wild Card closing fast enough, or the prospect haul available for Skubal growing more attractive as buyers compete for his services.

What's Next

Watch Detroit's record over the next 20 days. That is the most direct indicator of whether the Tigers are genuine sellers or reluctant participants at the deadline. If the gap to the Wild Card holds below 5 games by mid-July, this conversation becomes more complicated. If it expands to 8 or 10 games, the outcome becomes more predictable. The August 3 deadline is the finish line. Everything between now and then is the race to reach it.
