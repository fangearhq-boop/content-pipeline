All-Star Phase 2 Voting Closes Thursday — Here's Who's Fighting for What

By Marcus Cole June 30, 2026

What Phase 2 Is and Why It Matters

All-Star Phase 2 voting opened Monday, June 29 at noon ET. It closes Thursday, July 2 at noon ET. Full rosters, including pitchers and reserves, will be announced July 4 on FOX. The game itself is July 14, 2026 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia.

The mechanics of Phase 2 are different from Phase 1 in one important way: vote totals do not carry over. Phase 1 determined which two players at each position advanced to Phase 2. When Phase 2 opened, every player started at zero. The player who led Phase 1 by two million votes begins Phase 2 tied with the player who barely edged out third place. That reset creates genuine competition that did not exist in Phase 1, where leaders at some positions built insurmountable margins early.

The Automatic Starters

Two starters are already decided. Shohei Ohtani earned an automatic NL DH start by leading all Phase 1 vote-getters at 3.34 million — a margin so large that Phase 2 could not have overturned it even if it ran twice as long. Ernie Clement locked in his AL 2B start in Phase 1 as well. Neither player needed Phase 2 to secure their roster spots.

Every other starting spot, at every other position in both leagues, is being decided between now and Thursday at noon.

The Philadelphia Factor

Three Phillies are among the finalists to start the All-Star Game. The significance of that is straightforward: the game is being played at Citizens Bank Park, in front of a Phillies fanbase that fills that stadium with volume and votes at rates that track with the intensity of their support.

Kyle Schwarber is the most compelling Phillies ballot case. He has 29 home runs entering July — one of the highest totals in the NL — and is having a first half that would be newsworthy on any team. The Home Run Derby is also July 13, the night before the game, at Citizens Bank Park. Schwarber has not committed to participating, but a fan base pushing him into the All-Star Game is also the fan base that wants to see him swing in the Derby at home.

The competitive logic of having three hometown players on a Phase 2 ballot in your own city is not lost on a fanbase that has been among the most vocal in baseball for years. Those three finalists will get Philadelphia's full attention for the next 72 hours.

The AL Race Without Judge

Aaron Judge's absence — he was placed on the injured list June 5 with a right first rib stress fracture — removes what would have been the most prominent AL ballot driver in the New York market. Judge was leading the AL MVP conversation before the injury. His absence shifts the AL voting narrative toward Yordan Alvarez, Bobby Witt Jr. (recently returned from his MCL sprain), and the other position players competing in Phase 2.

The AL position races without a dominant figure at every spot create genuine uncertainty about who ends up starting July 14. When the rosters are announced on the Fourth of July, there will be at least one or two surprises — the Phase 2 format guarantees it.

What's Next

Voting closes Thursday, July 2 at noon ET. Starters, pitchers, and reserves will all be announced July 4 on FOX. The 2026 All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park. If you haven't voted and there is a player you want to see in the starting lineup, the window is closing. Three days of Phase 2 have already passed. You have two left.
