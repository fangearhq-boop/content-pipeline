Ohtani Threw 30 Pitches Wednesday. The Next One Is Saturday. A Return Is Still Weeks Away.

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | July 24, 2026

Shohei Ohtani threw approximately 30 pitches at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia on Wednesday afternoon. Pitching coach Mark Prior described it as similar to a normal between-start side session.

That is where the good news ends for now.

Manager Dave Roberts made clear after the session that a pitching return is not imminent. "It's going to be at least a week to 10 days" before the club even begins to talk about a return to the rotation, Roberts said. He added that he "can't foresee (Ohtani) going out there and throwing 100 pitches on his first outing" — making clear that even a first return start would be a shortened appearance, not a normal start.

The Injury

Ohtani's left knee — the landing knee in his pitching delivery — was first reported as problematic in June. He received an Orthovisc injection during the All-Star break, a lubricating agent designed to reduce inflammation and restore cushioning to the joint. The injection takes time to take full effect.

The pitching motion is hard on the knee in a specific way. When Ohtani lands on his left leg after his stride, the force of his full body weight and velocity travels through that joint. A knee that is inflamed or lacking cushioning cannot absorb that force properly. The Orthovisc injection was meant to restore what the inflammation had taken away.

Wednesday's 30-pitch session was the first test of whether the injection has worked well enough to allow Ohtani to progress. Prior said it looked normal. That is encouraging. It is not, however, a clearance.

The Schedule

The next bullpen session is expected Saturday at Citi Field in New York, where the Dodgers open a series against the Mets. If Saturday goes as well as Wednesday, the Dodgers can begin an actual conversation about when to reintroduce him to game action — likely a single-inning appearance, then a two-inning appearance, then a progression toward a normal starting workload.

That process takes time. Given Roberts' "week to 10 days" comment, the earliest any honest return projection gets to is early to mid-August. With the playoffs starting in late September, that still gives the Dodgers a meaningful window to have Ohtani as a starting pitcher in October — if everything goes to plan.

What He Was Before the Injury

Before his last start on July 3, Ohtani was 8-2 with a 1.79 ERA, 95 strikeouts, and 85.2 innings pitched. He was one of the best pitchers in baseball and a front-runner for the NL Cy Young Award. He continues to DH and has not missed time with the bat.

The pitching version of Ohtani, however, is a different weapon — one the Dodgers have built their rotation around all season. Getting him back before October is the goal. Saturday's bullpen session is the next step.

What's Next

Shohei Ohtani is expected to throw his second bullpen session Saturday, July 25, at Citi Field in New York. If it goes well, the Dodgers will begin discussing a return to the rotation timeline. Manager Dave Roberts said a return is "at least a week to 10 days" away as of Thursday, meaning the earliest realistic return start is early August. Ohtani continues to DH for Los Angeles in the meantime.
