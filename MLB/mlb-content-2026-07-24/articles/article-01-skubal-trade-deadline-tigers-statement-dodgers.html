Skubal Says He Wants to Stay. The Dodgers Still Want Him. August 3 Is 10 Days Away.

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 24, 2026

Tarik Skubal made his preference known on Thursday.

"My preference is to finish the season with the Tigers and win the World Series," the back-to-back AL Cy Young winner said publicly, while acknowledging that his next start could be his last in a Detroit uniform.

That sentence did not stop the trade talks. It may have made them more complicated. It almost certainly made the next ten days more interesting.

What the Market Looks Like

ESPN's Jeff Passan and Kiley McDaniel put the odds of a Skubal trade at 60 percent. The Dodgers have emerged as the lead suitor, according to multiple reports, and the fit makes obvious sense: Los Angeles can offer a controllable starting pitcher plus quality prospects — precisely the package Detroit has said it needs to move the best arm on the trade market.

The Angels are also selling. Their shortstop Zach Neto, right-handers Reid Detmers and Jose Soriano are all available. The Mets are expected to shed salary. The Phillies are looking for a back-end starter. The trade market is active, and Skubal sits at the center of it.

What Skubal's statement did Thursday is clarify the human element. Teams negotiating for a pending free agent know the player in question will choose where to sign in the off-season. If Skubal is dealt to a team he does not want to be at, that matters for his eventual free-agent decision. The Dodgers presumably know this and believe they can make their pitch in the off-season just as well if not better than Detroit can.

Detroit's Math

The Tigers are 6.5 games back in the AL Central and 4.5 out of the Wild Card. Those numbers are not hopeless. They are also not the numbers of a team that walks into the deadline as a clear-cut buyer.

Detroit has been playing better ball since June 1. They were 20-12 in that stretch. The Skubal want-to-stay statement from Thursday reflects a player who sees a team that is moving in the right direction and wants to be part of it. The question the Tigers front office has to answer is whether 6.5 games back in mid-July, with the trade market as hot as it is, is the right moment to hold the best pitcher available and bet on their own run.

If they hold Skubal and the Tigers miss the playoffs, the off-season conversation about that decision will be loud. If they trade him, the roster they get back could accelerate the next wave of the rebuild.

Neither choice is obviously wrong. That is what makes this the best deadline story in baseball.

The Clock

August 3 is the deadline, at 6 PM ET. Ten days remain. Skubal's next start is within that window. He said it himself — it could be his last in Detroit.

For the Tigers, the choice is now public. For the Dodgers, the chase continues. For everyone else watching baseball right now, the countdown is on.

What's Next

The MLB trade deadline is August 3 at 6 PM ET — 10 days from today. Tarik Skubal is expected to make at least one more start for Detroit before the deadline. The Dodgers are the identified lead suitor. All trades must be completed by 6 PM ET on August 3. Skubal is a pending free agent, meaning whichever team trades for him will need to sign him to a long-term extension to get full value — or move him again next winter.
