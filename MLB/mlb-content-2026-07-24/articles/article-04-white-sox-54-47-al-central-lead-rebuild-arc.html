The White Sox Are 54-47 and Leading the AL Central. Two Years Ago They Lost 121 Games.

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 24, 2026

The Chicago White Sox are 54-47 and sitting in first place in the AL Central.

In 2024, they went 41-121. That was the worst record in modern MLB history.

In 2025, they went 60-102. Still losing 100 games, but the total came down by 19. The development was working, slowly.

Now, in 2026, they lead their division in late July. The 30-win improvement from 2025 to 2026 matches the single-season improvement rate of only seven franchises in baseball history. This is not a hot stretch. This is a fully constructed rebuild that has arrived on schedule.

The Players Building It

Colson Montgomery has 20 home runs. He came into the 2026 season as the team's top prospect, and the home run total reflects what he was always projected to do. Teammates Murakami and Vargas are also hitting for power, putting the White Sox in a position where they may be the first team in franchise history to have three players hit 38 or more home runs in the same season.

When Kyle Teel — the young catcher who had been part of the team's core — went on the injured list with a high ankle sprain that will likely end his season, the White Sox called up Edgar Quero. Quero is 22 years old. He came from Triple-A Charlotte. The White Sox handled the loss of a key piece by replacing them with another piece from their own system.

That is what an organization that has properly rebuilt looks like. You do not panic. You call up your next guy. He plays.

The Race

The Cleveland Guardians are 54-49 — one game back. The Minnesota Twins defeated Cleveland 3-1 on Thursday, which kept Chicago's one-game lead intact. The AL Central race is real, and the White Sox will need to keep winning to hold it.

The trade deadline is in 10 days. With a first-place record and a system that has more players arriving, the White Sox are firmly in buyer territory heading toward August 3. A team that was 121-41 in 2024 buying at the trade deadline in 2026 is the most improbable sentence in baseball this week — and it is completely true.

The Larger Story

Rebuilds are supposed to take this long. You lose for multiple years, you accumulate draft picks and prospects, you develop them, and eventually the young players arrive and the wins accumulate. The timeline on the White Sox rebuild was always going to end around now.

What is unusual is how complete the rebuilding has been. They did not anchor the rebuild to one or two prospects and hope everything else figured itself out. They built a farm system deep enough that when Montgomery is in the lineup, Quero is ready to step in as the catcher, and multiple pitchers have developed behind him. Depth breeds consistency. Consistency breeds winning.

The 2024 White Sox lost 121 games. The 2026 White Sox are in first place. The math tracks.

What's Next

The Chicago White Sox (54-47) lead the AL Central by one game over the Cleveland Guardians (54-49). The MLB trade deadline is August 3. Chicago is expected to be active buyers. Colson Montgomery's home run chase and the AL Central race will define the second half. The Guardians and Twins are the primary competitors for the division.
