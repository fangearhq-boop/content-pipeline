The Red Sox Went from 37-48 to Buyers. The Deadline Is in 10 Days.

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 24, 2026

Six weeks ago, the Boston Red Sox were 37-48. The conversation around them at the trade deadline was about selling — which veterans had value, what prospects they could accumulate, what the off-season plan looked like.

That conversation is over. Boston is 52-49, on a 20-3 run over their last 23 games, and reports indicate they have interest in Zach Neto, the shortstop from the Los Angeles Angels. The Red Sox are buyers.

What Built the Streak

The 15-game win streak that ended Thursday — when the Orioles beat them 5-1 in Game 2 of a split doubleheader — was built on pitching. During the streak, the Red Sox posted a 1.98 team ERA. The bullpen went 7-0 with a 1.15 ERA. A team does not sustain a 15-game win streak on offense alone; it requires the kind of collective pitching execution that Boston found in mid-to-late June and rode for the better part of a month.

Even as the streak ended, the winning kept coming. They are now 20-3 in their last 23 games. The team that was two games under .500 in late June is now three games over .500 in late July, back in the AL Wild Card race, and playing the kind of baseball that justifies going after a difference-maker at the deadline.

The Zach Neto Connection

The Angels are selling. Neto, their starting shortstop, is one of the primary assets available. The Red Sox need a shortstop who can play at a high defensive level in a playoff environment. The fit is real.

It also fits with the moment. Boston does not have the payroll flexibility to add in multiple spots, so a single impactful addition at a position of need is the right play. A shortstop who helps them play better defense while the pitching staff — which clearly has the capacity to be special — keeps performing is exactly the formula a team in their position should be looking for.

The Angels are listening. The price will reflect what the market will bear. The Red Sox have 10 days to decide how much they believe in this version of themselves.

The Real Question

Every team that goes on a prolonged win streak faces the same inflection point at the deadline: is this who we are, or was this a run?

The honest answer with the Red Sox is that the pitching — specifically, the bullpen's 7-0, 1.15 ERA stretch — is not guaranteed to continue at that rate. But the underlying roster improvement over the past calendar year is real. This is not a team that fell into a 15-game win streak from a 37-48 position by accident. Something changed.

If Boston believes that — and they should have evidence to evaluate internally — then the deadline is a moment to take advantage of being in a race nobody expected them to be in. A shortstop from the Angels could be the move that turns 52-49 into October baseball.

What's Next

The MLB trade deadline is August 3 at 6 PM ET. The Red Sox (52-49) are reportedly interested in Zach Neto of the Los Angeles Angels. The Angels are in sell mode and have Neto, Reid Detmers, and Jose Soriano available. Boston's AL Wild Card position and deadline activity will be the story to watch over the next 10 days.
