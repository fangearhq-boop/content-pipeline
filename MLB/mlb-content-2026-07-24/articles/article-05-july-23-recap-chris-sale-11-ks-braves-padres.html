Chris Sale Struck Out 11 Thursday. Atlanta Hit Four Home Runs. The Braves Won 6-5.

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 24, 2026

Chris Sale matched his season-high with 11 strikeouts on Thursday afternoon at Petco Park. The Atlanta Braves hit four home runs. They beat the San Diego Padres 6-5.

That is a good day to be a Braves fan.

Sale is 37 years old. He has pitched in parts of 14 Major League seasons across Chicago (White Sox), Boston (Red Sox), and Atlanta (Braves). He has survived elbow surgery, Tommy John surgery, and a rib fracture. He has been to a World Series. He has led the American League in strikeouts twice.

Thursday was a reminder that Sale is still very much capable of putting up the kind of line that stops you when you're scrolling through the box scores.

How Thursday Unfolded

Sale's 11 strikeouts were his best performance by that measure this season. The Braves' offense supported him with four home runs across the game to back his effort. The final score of 6-5 suggests the Padres made a game of it late, but Atlanta controlled enough of the game to secure the win behind Sale's performance.

For a pitcher of Sale's vintage, the strikeout number matters not just as a one-game stat but as evidence that the stuff is holding up. A 37-year-old left-hander matching his season-high with 11 strikeouts in late July means the arm is healthy and the pitch mix is working. For a playoff team, that is the kind of performance that validates the choice to run a veteran starter through the second half of the season.

Around the Majors on Thursday

The Detroit Tigers beat the Kansas City Royals 4-3 on a comeback in the late innings. James Outman hit a three-run home run in the seventh to put Detroit ahead. Dillon Dingler followed in the eighth with a tiebreaking double that turned out to be the margin of victory. The Tigers win while trade deadline speculation about Tarik Skubal continues to dominate the conversation around the franchise.

In Toronto, the Blue Jays beat the Tampa Bay Rays 3-1 in a quiet game at Rogers Centre. The Rays, who have been inconsistent this summer, fell to another AL East opponent in what is becoming a familiar pattern.

The Minnesota Twins defeated the Cleveland Guardians 3-1 in a result with AL Central implications. The Guardians, one game behind the Chicago White Sox in the division, lost ground Thursday. The Twins win keeps them in the Wild Card picture as well.

And in a makeup game from June 25, the Arizona Diamondbacks routed the St. Louis Cardinals 10-6 in a game that had little playoff impact but produced a memorable offensive output from the Arizona lineup.

Friday Ahead

Thursday's slate set up a Friday with plenty of storylines still moving. The trade deadline is 10 days out. The AL Central race remains one game. The Red Sox are buyers for the first time in months. And somewhere in New York, Shohei Ohtani is preparing for a bullpen session on Saturday.

What's Next

Friday's MLB action continues the push toward the August 3 trade deadline. Chris Sale and the Atlanta Braves remain in NL playoff contention. The Tigers (Skubal deadline decision), White Sox (AL Central lead), and Red Sox (Neto trade interest) are the three teams driving the most headline activity in the days ahead.
