### STORY 1: Red Sox Win 14 Straight — One Win from Franchise History

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

In 1946, the Boston Red Sox went on a run that nobody in the franchise has matched since. Fifteen consecutive wins. Ted Williams, Johnny Pesky, Bobby Doerr, Dom DiMaggio. That team went to the World Series.

Eighty years later, the 2026 Red Sox are one win away from tying it.

Boston extended their winning streak to 14 games on Monday night, beating the Baltimore Orioles 6-5 at Fenway Park. Caleb Durbin hit a go-ahead solo home run over the Green Monster in the 8th inning to break a 5-5 tie. The Orioles had been on their own 7-game winning streak. Not anymore.

The Red Sox haven't been this hot since 2006. They are alive in the AL Wild Card race. And tonight — Tuesday, July 21 — they host the Orioles again. One win matches a record that has stood since Truman was in the White House.

The 1946 team played in the World Series. The 2026 team is playing for October.

### STORY 2: Ohtani Knee — Start Canceled, Cy Young in Jeopardy

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Shohei Ohtani will not be pitching this week.

The Los Angeles Dodgers have formally canceled his scheduled start, citing left knee inflammation that began before the All-Star break. Ohtani had fluid drained from his knee during the break. He told Dodgers training staff that the knee hadn't progressed enough to go. His next start — against the Philadelphia Phillies — is off.

Dodgers manager Dave Roberts addressed it directly: "It's gonna be some time, and I'd say that it's not gonna be a day-to-day thing."

There is no timetable. There is no IL placement, because Ohtani says he feels no pain when hitting and the Dodgers are keeping him in the lineup as the designated hitter. Background: Ohtani had surgery for a bipartite patella in 2019, a congenital condition. The current issue is a separate inflammation and fluid problem from the repeated stress of pitching — landing on that knee more than 100 times per start, once a week, all season.

His Cy Young Award push is in jeopardy. The arm is fine. The bat is fine. The knee isn't there yet. And nobody in Los Angeles is willing to say when it will be.

### STORY 3: Skubal Trade — Braves Frontrunner, 13 Days

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

Thirteen days.

That's what's left between now and the August 3 trade deadline. And in those 13 days, the Tarik Skubal situation has to reach a conclusion that nobody is quite sure is actually coming.

The Detroit Tigers are ~44-52. They are clearly sellers. Skubal is the biggest chip on the board — a two-time Cy Young Award winner, controllable through this season, and arguably the most coveted arm on any trade market in recent memory. The asking price from Detroit is described as "outrageous" by one rival NL executive. That hasn't stopped teams from calling.

Per Bob Nightengale, the Atlanta Braves have emerged as the frontrunner. The Cubs, Rays, Dodgers, and Astros are all described as engaged. The Braves need rotation help. The Cubs need rotation help. The Rays always need rotation help but somehow never stop finding it.

Here's the twist: Skubal has told close friends he wants to stay in Detroit. He believes the Tigers can win the World Series. And the Tigers — which entered this week's stretch of reporting with an arm that any playoff-bound team in the league would give up significant prospect capital to acquire — are now described as "inclined to keep" him.

That could change tomorrow. It could change at midnight August 2. Thirteen days is a long time in trade deadline season. It is also, occasionally, not long enough.

### STORY 4: White Sox 52-46 — One of Baseball's Greatest Turnarounds

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

In 2024, the Chicago White Sox lost 121 games. It was the worst single-season record in the history of major league baseball.

In 2025, they went 60-102. Third consecutive season of 100 or more losses. The rebuild was ongoing. The calendar kept moving.

Today, the Chicago White Sox are 52-46. They are leading the AL Central.

What's happening in Chicago is one of the most improbable stories in baseball. A 30-win improvement from where they were last year would match only seven teams in the history of the sport. Their team batting wRC+ — a measure of offensive production relative to league average — improved from 88 in 2025 to 104 in 2026. That is the biggest year-over-year offensive improvement in the major leagues.

Rookie shortstop Colson Montgomery has 21 home runs. He was passed over for the All-Star Game. Per the Chicago Sun-Times, the snub is fueling him heading into the second half.

The White Sox are not a fluke. They are not getting lucky. The numbers, the record, and the standings are all saying the same thing, at the same time. That doesn't happen by accident.

### STORY 5: Diamondbacks Walk Off Cardinals from 7-Run Deficit

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

The Cardinals led 7-0. The Cardinals had been winning like that all day. Then the Arizona Diamondbacks went to work.

On Sunday, July 19, the Diamondbacks rallied from seven runs down to beat the St. Louis Cardinals 8-7 in 10 innings — matching the largest comeback in franchise history. What made it hit differently: the player at the center of it used to wear the red.

Nolan Arenado spent 10 seasons in St. Louis before signing with Arizona. On Sunday he came up in the 8th inning with the Cardinals ahead 7-5 and the game on the line. He hit a 2-run home run against his former team to tie it. It was career hit No. 1,999.

Three runs in the 6th, two in the 7th, the tying home run in the 8th. Gordon Graceffo allowed the late damage for St. Louis. Then Max Kepler came up in the 10th inning with an automatic runner on base. He hit a walk-off RBI double. Jorge Barrosa scored. Arizona 8, Cardinals 7.

The Cardinals had not blown a 7-run lead since July 6, 2010. The numbers, the history, the ex-Cardinal who swung the biggest hit — this one had everything.
