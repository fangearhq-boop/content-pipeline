From 121 Losses to Leading the Division: The White Sox Are Real

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 21, 2026

Let's start with where the Chicago White Sox were two years ago.

In 2024, they lost 121 games. That is the single-season record for losses in major league baseball history. Not a bad year. Not a rebuilding year with some growing pains. The worst record any team has ever posted in the history of the sport. In 2025, they went 60-102. A third consecutive season of 100-plus losses. The rebuild was still ongoing. The calendar kept moving.

Today, the Chicago White Sox are 52-46. They lead the AL Central.

The Numbers

The turnaround is not ambiguous. The numbers are not flattering them. A 30-win improvement over their 2025 record — which is what they are tracking toward — would match only seven teams in baseball history. Their team offensive wRC+, a park and league-adjusted measure of offensive production, improved from 88 in 2025 to 104 in 2026. That is the biggest year-over-year offensive improvement in the major leagues.

The roster is genuinely better. The development is real. The results are real.

Colson Montgomery

No player represents the turnaround more directly than Colson Montgomery, the rookie shortstop who has hit 21 home runs in his second major league season. Montgomery was not selected to the All-Star Game. Per the Chicago Sun-Times, the snub is fueling him heading into the second half. He is hitting with power at a rate that few players have matched through the first two seasons of a major league career, and he is doing it for a franchise that spent two years at the bottom of the sport.

There is a version of this story in which Montgomery is the face of a White Sox team that competes for the AL Central title in August and September. That version of the story has supporting evidence. It also has 70-plus games of results to back it up.

The Skeptics

The White Sox haven't entirely escaped doubt. After 121 losses two years ago, skepticism is a reasonable default. Teams run hot stretches in July. Records sort themselves out by October. Any team that wins at a .530 pace in a weak division gets a second look from people who remember where they started.

The counterargument is the underlying numbers. This team is not winning games it shouldn't be winning. The offensive improvement is real. The development is real. And at some point, a team that is 52-46, leading its division, and matching historical turnaround benchmarks earns the right to be taken at face value.

That point is now.

What's Next

The Chicago White Sox lead the AL Central at 52-46 heading into the second half of the 2026 season. Colson Montgomery has 21 home runs. The club's wRC+ is 104, up from 88 a year ago. They are on pace for one of the greatest one-year turnarounds in baseball history. The remainder of the season determines whether this is a real contender or a good story that runs out of runway in September.
