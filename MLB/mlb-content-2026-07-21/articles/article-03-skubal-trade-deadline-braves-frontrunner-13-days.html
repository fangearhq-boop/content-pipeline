The Pitcher Who Wants to Stay, the Team That's Thinking About It, and 13 Days Left to Decide

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 21, 2026

The August 3 trade deadline is 13 days away. The most coveted starting pitcher on the market has told close friends he doesn't want to be traded. His team is now described as inclined to keep him. And somehow, half a dozen front offices are still on the phone making their case.

This is the Tarik Skubal situation on July 21, and it may stay the Tarik Skubal situation right up until midnight August 2.

The Market

The Detroit Tigers are ~44-52. They are not in playoff contention. They are, by any reasonable definition, sellers heading into the deadline. Skubal is a two-time Cy Young Award winner, controllable through this season, and the kind of arm that legitimate World Series contenders would part with significant prospect capital to acquire.

Per Bob Nightengale of USA Today, the Atlanta Braves have emerged as the frontrunner. Atlanta's rotation has been depleted by injuries, and a pitcher of Skubal's caliber addresses their most pressing need in the most direct way possible. Also engaged, according to multiple reports: the Chicago Cubs, Tampa Bay Rays, Los Angeles Dodgers, and Houston Astros. The asking price from Detroit has been described as "outrageous" by one rival NL executive. That has not stopped teams from engaging.

The Twist

Here is what complicates the picture: Skubal has told close friends he wants to stay in Detroit. He believes the Tigers can win the World Series. That belief — from a pitcher looking at a team sitting 8 games under .500 at the midpoint of the season — might sound like optimism disconnected from the standings. But it's the kind of thing that matters when the player has any influence over the situation, and it changes the calculus for the Tigers.

The organization is now described as "inclined to keep" Skubal heading into the final stretch before the deadline. That doesn't mean the calls have stopped. That doesn't mean the Braves have given up. What it means is that the final two weeks of this trade window will run simultaneously with an internal Tigers debate about whether winning now — or preserving the foundation that Skubal represents — is the better path.

The Clock

Thirteen days is long enough for three things to happen: the Tigers can dig in and keep him, one team can make an offer too large to refuse, or Skubal can tell the organization directly that he'd prefer to stay and that preference carries real weight. Any of those endings is plausible right now. None of them is certain.

The Braves are the frontrunner today. The Cubs are still engaged. The Rays always find a way. And somewhere in the middle of all of it is a two-time Cy Young winner who says he wants to stay in a city that hasn't won a World Series since 1984 — and who pitches like someone who might help them get there.

What's Next

The MLB trade deadline is August 3. The Tigers, Braves, Cubs, Rays, Dodgers, and Astros are all involved at some level. Skubal's preferences and the Tigers' inclination to keep him make this more complicated than a straightforward sale. The next 13 days determine whether the market wins or the relationship does.
