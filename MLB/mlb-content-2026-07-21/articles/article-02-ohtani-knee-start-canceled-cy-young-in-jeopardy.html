Ohtani's Next Start Is Off. The Timeline Is Unknown. His Cy Young Push Is in Jeopardy.

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | July 21, 2026

Shohei Ohtani will not be pitching this week. His scheduled start has been formally canceled by the Los Angeles Dodgers after the left knee inflammation that sent him to training staff over the All-Star break failed to progress enough for him to take the mound.

Dodgers manager Dave Roberts offered this on Tuesday morning when asked about a return timeline: "It's gonna be some time, and I'd say that it's not gonna be a day-to-day thing."

There is no IL placement. There is no target date. Ohtani says he feels no pain when hitting, so he will continue as the designated hitter. The bat remains in the lineup. The arm is on hold. And the Cy Young Award push that had made 2026 a historically compelling season is now in genuine jeopardy.

What Happened

Ohtani dealt with left knee inflammation in the weeks leading up to the All-Star break. The Dodgers had fluid drained from his knee during the break, hoping the procedure would allow him to return to the rotation quickly. When Ohtani checked in with training staff ahead of this week's scheduled start against the Philadelphia Phillies, the knee hadn't responded the way the Dodgers needed it to. The start was canceled.

There is relevant medical history here. In 2019, Ohtani underwent surgery for a bipartite patella — a congenital condition in which the kneecap forms in two pieces rather than one. The 2026 knee issue is a separate problem: inflammation and fluid buildup believed to be caused by the accumulated stress of pitching, specifically the landing mechanics on the left knee that occur more than 100 times per start. When he pitches every fifth day, that stress compounds. The All-Star break offered a window to address it. The window didn't produce the results the Dodgers needed.

The Cy Young Dimension

Before the knee became the story, Ohtani's 2026 season had been one of the most decorated individual campaigns in the sport. The pitching side was strong enough to put him in Cy Young conversations. The hitting side, as always, needs no additional context — he continues to carry that production as the DH without restriction.

The canceled start doesn't end the Cy Young bid mathematically. He could return in a week or two and accumulate enough innings to remain in the conversation. But with no timeline and a Roberts quote that explicitly walks back any expectation of a quick return, the realistic path to a Cy Young Award just got significantly harder. Award voters weight the full body of work, and a stretch of DH-only production in late July and August is not what any Cy Young campaign needs.

The Dodgers have the depth to absorb the rotation shortfall. What they can't manufacture is more time in Ohtani's pitching season. Every day the knee isn't ready is a day he can't get back.

What's Next

The Dodgers have not placed Ohtani on the injured list and are not required to with the next start canceled rather than a formal IL move. He will continue as the designated hitter while the knee is monitored. No specific return date has been given. Dave Roberts has said it will take time. The Dodgers continue without their best starting pitcher while one of the most compelling Cy Young races in recent memory plays out without its most prominent candidate in the rotation.
