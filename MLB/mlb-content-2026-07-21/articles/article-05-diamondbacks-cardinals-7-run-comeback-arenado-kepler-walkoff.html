Arenado Beat His Old Team. Then Kepler Finished It Off. Arizona Had Never Done This Before.

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | July 21, 2026

The Cardinals led 7-0. That felt safe. It was not safe.

On Sunday, July 19, the Arizona Diamondbacks rallied from seven runs down to beat the St. Louis Cardinals 8-7 in 10 innings — matching the largest comeback in franchise history. The player at the center of it used to call Busch Stadium home. And the Cardinals have not blown a lead this large since 2010.

The Comeback

Arizona chipped away. Three runs came home in the 6th inning. Two more in the 7th. St. Louis still led 7-5 heading into the 8th, a two-run cushion with six outs to go.

That's when Nolan Arenado stepped in against the Cardinals' bullpen.

Arenado spent 10 seasons in St. Louis. He knows the pitching staff, the ballpark, the sounds. He hit a 2-run home run against his former team to tie the game at 7-7. It was career hit No. 1,999. Gordon Graceffo allowed the damage. The Cardinals had come too far to let it end there, but it did end there — eventually.

The game went to the 10th inning. Under current rules, each extra inning begins with an automatic runner on second base. Jorge Barrosa started the 10th at second. Max Kepler came up and hit an RBI double, scoring Barrosa. Final: Diamondbacks 8, Cardinals 7.

The History

The St. Louis Cardinals had not blown a 7-run lead since July 6, 2010, when they lost 12-9 to the Colorado Rockies in Denver. That was 16 years ago. The franchise has held leads this large on hundreds of occasions since then and converted every single one of them. Until Sunday.

For the Diamondbacks, the comeback matches the largest in franchise history — previously set on August 29, 2022, when Arizona also rallied from 7 down to beat the Philadelphia Phillies. The fact that they've now done it twice, with the same deficit, in this era, suggests something about the character of the club rather than just the circumstances of one bad night for a rival.

The Arenado Angle

No part of this story lands harder than Arenado. He gave the Cardinals the best years of his career in St. Louis. He is now in Arizona. The 8th-inning home run off his former team, for career hit No. 1,999, with the game on the line — it would be almost too neat to manufacture. But it happened. The ball went over the wall. The score was tied. Career hit No. 2,000 is coming, and the context around 1,999 will always be this one.

What's Next

The Arizona Diamondbacks improved their NL Wild Card positioning with the win and return to action later this week. Nolan Arenado is one hit away from career hit No. 2,000. The Cardinals continue to carry one of the most embarrassing blown leads in franchise history — and the memory that it took 16 years to happen again, which means they had done something right for a very long time before Sunday night.
