One Win from 80 Years of Franchise History

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 21, 2026

In the spring of 1946, the Boston Red Sox won 15 games in a row. That team had Ted Williams, Johnny Pesky, Bobby Doerr, and Dom DiMaggio. They went to the World Series. And for 80 years, that winning streak has stood as the longest in franchise history.

The 2026 Red Sox need one win to tie it.

Boston extended their current winning streak to 14 games on Monday night, beating the Baltimore Orioles 6-5 at Fenway Park. Tonight — Tuesday, July 21 — the Orioles are back. One win matches the record. A 15th-straight victory puts the 2026 Red Sox in the same sentence as the greatest team in franchise history.

Monday Night

The game was tied 5-5 when Caleb Durbin stepped in against the Orioles' bullpen in the bottom of the 8th. He hit the ball over the Green Monster. The Red Sox led 6-5. That's where it stayed.

The win was notable for reasons beyond the streak itself. The Baltimore Orioles entered Monday on a 7-game winning streak of their own — and Boston ended it while extending theirs. According to reports, this was only the fifth time in the past 65 years that two teams with at least 7-game winning streaks faced each other. The Orioles got four errors against them in the game. Christian Encarnacion-Strand homered and drove in a run. None of it was enough.

How They Got Here

The Red Sox were not a team anyone considered dangerous at the start of July. They entered the month grinding for respectability, sitting near the bubble of the AL Wild Card race and doing nothing to suggest they were about to become the hottest team in baseball.

Then the streak started. Fourteen games later, they are alive in October. They are not just playing for wins — they are playing to enter a record book that hasn't moved on this particular line in eight decades.

The streak is the longest for Boston since 2006. It's the longest since their own franchise reached 13-plus in 1948. The context matters: this isn't a team running over weak opponents in a meaningless stretch. These wins count. They have moved the Red Sox into the AL Wild Card picture. They ended a rival's winning streak on Monday night to keep the run going.

What Tonight Means

The franchise record of 15 consecutive wins was set between April 25 and May 10, 1946. That team had four Hall of Famers in the everyday lineup. They are one of the most decorated collections of baseball talent in American League history. The fact that 80 years have passed without anyone in Boston matching what they did says something about how rare a moment this is.

The 2026 Red Sox are not the 1946 Red Sox. But one win tonight puts them in the same place in the record book. And the 1946 team that set this record went to the World Series. The 2026 team is trying to get back to October for the first time in years. That starts tonight.

What's Next

The Boston Red Sox host the Baltimore Orioles on Tuesday, July 21 at Fenway Park. A win ties the franchise record of 15 consecutive wins, set in 1946. A loss ends the streak at 14. The Red Sox are in the AL Wild Card race and have 14 wins in a row to show anyone who doubted them. Tonight adds the next chapter.
