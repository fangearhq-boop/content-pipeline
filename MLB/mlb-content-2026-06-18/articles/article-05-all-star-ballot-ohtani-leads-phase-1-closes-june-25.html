All-Star Ballot: Ohtani Leads All Players, Phase 1 Closes June 25 — Here's What's at Stake

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 18, 2026

The 2026 MLB All-Star voting window is entering its final week of Phase 1, and the leaderboard reflects what most fans already knew: Shohei Ohtani is the most popular player on the planet.

Phase 1 of fan voting for the 2026 All-Star Game closes at noon ET on Thursday, June 25. After that, Phase 2 begins on June 29. The stakes for Phase 1 are meaningful: the player with the most votes in each league at the conclusion of the first phase earns an automatic starting spot in the lineup for the July 14 game at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia.

The Leaderboard

Ohtani's vote total leads all players in Major League Baseball by a significant margin. The Dodgers two-way star has become a cultural touchstone far beyond the game of baseball, with an international fanbase that casts ballots in numbers that North American-only stars struggle to match. His automatic starter status in the NL is essentially secure barring an extraordinary circumstance.

On the American League side, Yordan Alvarez leads the DH position in voting. The Astros slugger, who is also one of the NL's leading home run hitters, has a substantial following that has sustained his position at the top of the AL ballot throughout the voting period.

Drake Baldwin, the Atlanta Braves catcher who has been one of the breakout performers of 2026, leads catchers in overall voting with more than 972,000 ballots cast as of the last official update on June 15. Baldwin's All-Star case is built on more than just popularity — his offensive production this season has been among the best in baseball at his position, and the Braves' consistent relevance in the NL East has kept his name in the national conversation.

First base in the NL presents an interesting race: Freddie Freeman of the Dodgers leads among NL first basemen, while Matt Olson of the Braves sits close behind.

Why Phase 1 Matters

The automatic-starter rule for Phase 1 leaders gives fan voting genuine stakes that a system with purely advisory influence would lack. A player who wins Phase 1 does not need to survive Phase 2 competition — the spot is secured. That creates urgency for fanbases backing players in competitive positions and a meaningful outcome for the candidates themselves.

For players who are competing for All-Star recognition in a crowded position, the next week is the window that matters most for fan-based selection. Phase 2, beginning June 29, will narrow each position to finalists who face a second round of voting before the roster is completed ahead of the All-Star Game.

The Philadelphia Setting

The 2026 All-Star Game is at Citizens Bank Park on July 14 — the day after the Home Run Derby on July 13. Philadelphia is one of the most passionate baseball markets in the country, and Citizens Bank Park's reputation as a hitter-friendly environment makes it a natural fit for a mid-summer showcase that prizes offense.

For the Phillies' own players — including Kyle Schwarber, who leads the NL in home runs and OPS — having the game in their home park creates an additional incentive for fans to get involved in the voting process. Schwarber as a starting designated hitter at his home park, in the Derby and the game in the same week, would be a complete narrative package.

What's Next

The Phase 1 deadline is June 25. One week remains to vote. After that, the process shifts to Phase 2 (June 29) and eventually to the final roster selections. The All-Star Game itself is July 14 in Philadelphia. Between now and then, the voting window is open and the automatic starter spots are still being decided.

Go vote. The deadline is real and the stakes are clear. Whoever leads at noon ET on June 25 earns something the rest of the players on the ballot do not: a guaranteed spot in the starting lineup without needing a second vote to confirm it.
