Schwarber Leads NL with 23 HRs — The Derby at Citizens Bank Is the Setting He Was Made For

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 18, 2026

Kyle Schwarber is the National League's home run leader. The Home Run Derby is coming to his home ballpark on July 13. The setup feels less like coincidence and more like a script someone wrote in December.

Schwarber leads the NL with 23 home runs and a .949 OPS, adding a .598 slugging percentage that leads the league at his position. On the American League side, Yordan Alvarez of the Houston Astros continues to pace the junior circuit. The two have been locked in a slugging conversation for most of the season, with neither able to put significant daylight between themselves and the other.

The Citizens Bank Setting

The 2026 Home Run Derby is scheduled for July 13 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia — the same stadium where Schwarber has spent his career as a Phillie, the park where he has sent baseballs deep into the night with the regularity of a man who treats the left-field seats as a personal address.

Citizens Bank Park is one of the most hitter-friendly parks in the National League. Its dimensions and the atmospheric conditions that prevail in Philadelphia summer nights create an environment where home run hitters thrive. Schwarber playing in his home Derby — with his home crowd, in his home conditions, off pitches from batting practice arms in a format specifically designed to maximize his singular skill — is the kind of narrative that writes itself.

CBS Sports has already assembled its ideal Derby field: Schwarber, Alvarez, Bryce Harper, Mike Trout, and Ben Rice. That is a formidable collection of power. If the field comes anywhere close to that list, the July 13 Derby will be a genuine television event rather than a pre-All-Star appetizer.

The Alvarez Question

Yordan Alvarez brings his own profile to the HR race and the Derby conversation. The Astros first baseman and designated hitter is one of the most dangerous left-handed bats in baseball — a player who hits the ball with the kind of frequency and distance that makes pitchers uncomfortable before they even take the sign.

Alvarez and Schwarber matching up in the Derby would be the matchup the format was designed to produce: two genuinely elite power hitters going round-for-round at a ballpark that rewards exactly what they do. The best-of format does not always produce the most artistic baseball, but it produces moments — and Schwarber versus Alvarez at Citizens Bank would produce plenty of them.

The Ballot Connection

All-Star voting Phase 1 closes June 25. Both Schwarber and Alvarez are involved in their respective league's DH races, with Alvarez running well in AL voting and Schwarber making the NL case for a player whose offensive numbers speak louder than any campaign. The Derby field announcement typically comes after the All-Star rosters are more fully determined — meaning the next two weeks will be significant for understanding exactly who shows up at Citizens Bank in July.

Schwarber has a choice in whether he participates. Derby performance has historically been linked — fairly or not — to second-half performance. Some sluggers decline the invitation for exactly that reason. Given that the Derby is at his home park and his production entering the event is among the best in the sport, the argument for participating has rarely been stronger.

What's Next

The Phase 1 All-Star vote closes June 25. The roster and Derby field picture will sharpen significantly in the days that follow. For Schwarber, the immediate priority is continuing to hit — and with 23 home runs already and a .949 OPS that suggests the pace will not slow dramatically, the argument that he will enter the Derby as the NL's best home run hitter is not hypothetical.

July 13 is coming. Citizens Bank is the venue. Schwarber is the NL HR leader. All that is left is the official invitation.
