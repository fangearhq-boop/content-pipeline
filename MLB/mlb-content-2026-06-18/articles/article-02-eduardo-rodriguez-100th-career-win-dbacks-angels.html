Eduardo Rodriguez Earns Career Win No. 100 — 9th Venezuelan Pitcher to Reach the Milestone

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 18, 2026

The number 100 carries weight in baseball. It is a threshold that separates career pitchers from pitchers who had careers. Eduardo Rodriguez reached it Wednesday night.

The Arizona Diamondbacks left-hander threw seven innings against the Los Angeles Angels, allowing just one run on six hits, walking three batters and striking out five, as Arizona won 8-1. Rodriguez lowered his season ERA to 2.45. More importantly, he earned career win No. 100 — a milestone that places him in rare company among Venezuelan-born pitchers in the history of the major leagues.

The Milestone in Context

Rodriguez became the ninth Venezuelan-born pitcher in MLB history to win 100 career games. The list of his predecessors includes celebrated arms who defined their own eras — a short but distinguished roll call that E-Rod now joins permanently.

The path to 100 was not straightforward. Rodriguez spent time with the Boston Red Sox, where he was a rotation fixture and fan favorite. He moved on to the Detroit Tigers, then had a stint with the New York Yankees before landing in Arizona. Along the way, injuries cost him time — including a significant stretch away from the mound that tested his durability and his resolve.

None of that is visible in the 2026 version of Eduardo Rodriguez. A 2.45 ERA heading into June is the kind of number a team builds its rotation plans around. He is not just accumulating innings — he is winning them.

The Game That Got Him There

Wednesday's start against Los Angeles was representative of what Rodriguez has been this season: efficient, composed, and effective without being flashy. Seven innings from a starting pitcher, with one run allowed, is exactly what a team needs when it wants to win a baseball game. The Diamondbacks provided plenty of run support around him — led by a grand slam from Corbin Carroll that blew the game open.

Carroll's slam, combined with the D-Backs' overall offensive performance, turned what was a pitching story into a complete team effort. Arizona beat the Angels 8-1. Rodriguez got the win. The milestone was secured with Diamondbacks fans watching from Chase Field and Rodriguez teammates congratulating him in a dugout that had plenty of reason to celebrate.

What's Next

Win No. 100 is the kind of milestone that comes with perspective. Pitchers who reach it have navigated enough seasons, enough roster changes, and enough physical setbacks to understand exactly what the number represents. Rodriguez has navigated all of those things and come out on the other side still doing it at a high level.

The Diamondbacks, with Rodriguez at the front or middle of their rotation, are a team with postseason ambitions in 2026. His 2.45 ERA gives Arizona exactly what it needs from a veteran starter: quality starts, competitive innings, and the kind of consistency that makes a pitching staff credible.

The career total stands at 100. The work is not finished. But for one night in Anaheim, Eduardo Rodriguez earned the right to let a number speak for itself.
