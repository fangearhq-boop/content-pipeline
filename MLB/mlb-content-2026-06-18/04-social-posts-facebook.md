### STORY 1: Pete Crow-Armstrong Cycle

**Long-Form Post**

Monday night at Wrigley — well, technically it started in the afternoon — Pete Crow-Armstrong did something no Chicago Cub had ever done before.

The 24-year-old center fielder hit for the cycle against the Colorado Rockies on June 15. That's impressive enough on its own. But he did it in reverse order: home run in the first inning, triple in the third, double in the fifth, and single in the seventh.

Not just the first cycle of the 2026 MLB season. Not just the 13th cycle in Cubs franchise history. The first reverse-order cycle in Cubs history. And only the fourth player in the entire divisional era — going back to 1969 — to accomplish the feat in reverse order.

Crow-Armstrong, who went 4-for-4 with two RBI, said afterward he felt upset with himself for getting picked off immediately after recording the single that completed the cycle. "I did everything I could to help the team," he told reporters, "but I also had a real lapse in focus."

The game still needed him. The Cubs trailed 4-3 entering the ninth before Matt Shaw drew a bases-loaded walk to send Chicago home with a 5-4 victory. The win was part of a stretch that has Cubs fans starting to wonder: is this the year MVPETE puts it all together?

At 24, he is the youngest Cubs player to hit for the cycle since catcher Randy Hundley did it in 1966. Whatever frustrations come with the inconsistency, performances like Monday's are a reminder of what this player can do when everything clicks.

### STORY 2: Eduardo Rodriguez 100th Win

**Long-Form Post**

Eduardo Rodriguez has been to Boston, Detroit, and New York. He's had injuries that kept him from the mound for entire seasons. He's switched teams, rebuilt his craft, and earned his place at the front of a rotation more than once.

On Wednesday night in Anaheim, all of that came together in one number: 100.

E-Rod threw seven innings against the Los Angeles Angels, allowing just one run on six hits and walking three while striking out five. The Arizona Diamondbacks won 8-1, with Corbin Carroll providing a grand slam for good measure. And Eduardo Rodriguez picked up career win No. 100 — his most significant milestone as a professional.

The achievement places Rodriguez in rarefied company: he is the ninth Venezuelan-born pitcher in the history of Major League Baseball to win 100 games. The list of predecessors includes some celebrated names in the sport's history.

His 2026 season has been quietly excellent. A 2.45 ERA across his starts for Arizona is the work of a pitcher who has found consistency at the back end of his prime years. He was good in Boston, had his moments with Detroit, but in Arizona he has found a home and a rotation where he belongs.

Win No. 100 is the proof.
