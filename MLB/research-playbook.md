# Ballpark Banter — Research Playbook

How to research, tier, and fact-check MLB stories for daily content.

## Research Categories

Run **8-10 web searches** across these categories before writing content.

| # | Category | Example Search Queries |
|---|----------|----------------------|
| 1 | Scores & Results | "MLB scores last night", "MLB box scores [date]", "walk-off home run MLB", "no-hitter MLB" |
| 2 | Standings & Races | "MLB standings", "AL wild card standings", "NL Central standings", "MLB magic numbers" |
| 3 | Individual Performances | "MLB home run leaders", "MLB batting leaders", "MLB milestone watch", "3000 hits", "500 home runs" |
| 4 | Trades & Transactions | "MLB trades today", "MLB Trade Rumors", "free agent signings", "MLB DFA", "MLB waiver claims" |
| 5 | Pitching Performances | "MLB no-hitter", "MLB perfect game", "dominant pitching performance", "MLB strikeout leaders" |
| 6 | Injury & Roster | "MLB injury report", "MLB IL moves", "[star player] injury update", "MLB call-ups today" |
| 7 | Coaching & Front Office | "MLB manager fired", "MLB coaching change", "GM hire MLB", "front office shakeup" |
| 8 | Preview & Lookahead | "MLB probable pitchers today", "MLB schedule today", "rivalry series preview", "weekend MLB matchups" |
| 9 | Unusual/Milestone Stats | "MLB unusual stat", "MLB milestone last night", "MLB all-time record [date]", "MLB streak record", "[player] passes [legend] all-time" |

### Category 9: Unusual & Milestone Stats (IMPORTANT)

Run 1-2 dedicated searches for genuinely interesting stats from the most recent games. These are the kind of stats that make fans stop scrolling — things like:

- A player passing someone on an all-time leaderboard (e.g., "LeBron passes Kareem" equivalent for MLB)
- A crazy stat line that's historically rare (e.g., "first player to hit 3 HR and steal 2 bases in the same game since 1990")
- A weird team streak or record (e.g., "The Orioles have now won 12 straight Tuesday games")
- A player doing something for the first time in franchise history

**Rules for stat posts:**
- The stat MUST be from the team's or player's most recent game — not from weeks ago
- It MUST be 100% factually verified from a primary source (Baseball Reference, official box score, MLB.com)
- It must be genuinely interesting — the "would a fan share this?" test. If it's not remarkable, skip it.
- Do NOT force a stat post if nothing interesting exists. An empty slot is better than a boring stat.
- These make great Tier 2-3 standalone posts or can be woven into game recaps as bonus content

## Story Tiering

### Tier 1 — Lead Stories (8:00 AM, 10:30 AM slots)

Must-cover content that anchors the day. Gets the earliest available slot.

| Story Type | Why Tier 1 |
|-----------|-----------|
| No-hitters and perfect games | Rare historic achievements — always the lead, no exceptions |
| Walk-offs (especially postseason) | Peak fan emotion — the kind of moment people remember for years |
| Major trades and blockbuster signings | Roster-altering moves affect multiple teams and the entire league landscape |
| Star player injuries (season-ending, major) | A torn ACL for an MVP candidate reshapes a team's season and division race |
| Milestone achievements (3000 hits, 500 HR, 300 wins) | Historic — these happen a handful of times per decade |
| Playoff clinching / elimination | The highest stakes in regular season baseball |
| Opening Day | Once a year — always the lead |
| Postseason games (all rounds) | October baseball is always Tier 1 |

### Tier 2 — Developing Stories (12:30 PM, 3:00 PM slots)

Strong content that builds out the day.

| Story Type | Why Tier 2 |
|-----------|-----------|
| Regular game results (contender/rivalry matchups) | Yankees-Red Sox, Dodgers-Giants, Cubs-Cardinals — rivalry games always matter |
| Notable pitching performances (15+ K, CGSO) | Dominant starts that don't quite reach no-hitter status |
| Standings shifts (new division leaders, wild card changes) | Playoff race movement is always worth covering |
| Prospect call-ups (top prospects debuting) | A top-5 prospect making his MLB debut is a genuine event |
| Trade deadline rumors (late July) | Speculation drives engagement 2 weeks before the deadline |
| All-Star selections and snubs | Fans have strong opinions — engagement gold |
| Win/loss streaks (7+ games) | Extended streaks in either direction are story-worthy |
| Monthly/weekly awards | Player of the Week/Month, Pitcher of the Month |

### Tier 3 — Supporting Content (6:00 PM, 10:00 PM slots)

Fills out the schedule with analysis, evergreen, and engagement content.

| Story Type | Why Tier 3 |
|-----------|-----------|
| Tonight's pitching matchup previews | Sets the stage for evening viewing |
| Weekend series previews | Lookahead at marquee matchups |
| Stats deep dives | WAR leaders, Statcast leaderboards, advanced metric explainers |
| Historical comparisons | "Best starts to a season since..." type features |
| Fun facts and engagement | Trivia, "this day in baseball history", debate prompts |
| Minor league roundups | System-wide top prospect updates |
| Rule change analysis | Pitch clock impact, shift ban effects, rule proposals |

## Posting Priority

This order is non-negotiable:

1. **Last night's scores and walk-offs** — always first. A game recap that posts after the next game starts is useless.
2. **Today's pitching matchups and previews** — must post BEFORE first pitch. If a 1:05 PM ET game, preview goes no later than 10:30 AM.
3. **Breaking news** — trades, major injuries, transactions. Post as early as confirmed.
4. **Everything else** — analysis, evergreen, historical deep dives fill remaining slots.

If multiple game results exist, order by tier: walk-offs and no-hitters first, then rivalry/contender matchups, then notable individual performances.

## Fact-Check Requirements

Every post must be verified before publishing. Priority order:

### Priority 1 — Scores and Box Scores

| Check | How to Verify |
|-------|--------------|
| Final score | Cross-reference ESPN box score AND MLB.com game recap — verify both |
| Innings pitched / line score | Verify from box score — never assume 9 innings (extras, rain delays, suspended games) |
| Winning/losing/save pitcher | Box score decision column — do not guess from starter |
| Key play details (HR distance, exit velo) | Verify from Baseball Savant if citing Statcast data |
| Walk-off details | Confirm batter, pitch count, inning from box score |

### Priority 2 — Records and Milestones

| Check | How to Verify |
|-------|--------------|
| Career stats / milestones | Baseball Reference player page — the definitive source |
| Franchise records | Baseball Reference franchise page or team media guide |
| Season stats (BA, ERA, HR) | Baseball Reference or FanGraphs — stats update daily, verify same-day |
| Win-loss records / streaks | Baseball Reference team schedule page — count manually if claiming a streak |
| All-time records | Baseball Reference or Baseball Almanac — cross-reference |

### Priority 3 — Player Stats (Advanced)

| Check | How to Verify |
|-------|--------------|
| WAR | FanGraphs (fWAR) or Baseball Reference (bWAR) — specify which version |
| OPS+ / wRC+ / FIP | FanGraphs for FIP/wRC+, Baseball Reference for OPS+ — stats are park/league adjusted |
| Statcast metrics (xwOBA, barrel rate, exit velo) | Baseball Savant — the only primary source for Statcast |
| Contract values (AAV, total, years) | Spotrac or MLB Trade Rumors — never estimate from memory |
| Player age | Look up birth date on Baseball Reference, calculate from today's date |

### Priority 4 — Game Times and Schedule

| Check | How to Verify |
|-------|--------------|
| Game time | MLB.com schedule page — ALWAYS use ET |
| TV/streaming broadcast | ESPN or MLB.com schedule — broadcasts change, especially nationally |
| Day of week | Cross-reference the calendar — never say "tonight's game" for an afternoon start |
| Series length | Verify 3-game vs 4-game vs 2-game from official schedule |
| Doubleheaders | Confirm 7-inning vs 9-inning format and start times for both games |

### Priority 5 — Contract and Financial Data

| Check | How to Verify |
|-------|--------------|
| Contract values (AAV, total, years) | Spotrac — the definitive payroll source |
| Arbitration projections | MLB Trade Rumors arbitration tracker |
| Luxury tax / payroll figures | Spotrac team payroll page |
| Free agent contract comparisons | Spotrac or MLB Trade Rumors — never estimate from memory |

## Coverage Balance Check

Before finalizing any day's content, verify coverage breadth:

- Do NOT default to Yankees/Dodgers/big-market teams every day
- AL and NL should get roughly equal coverage over any given week
- Small-market stories (Guardians, Rays, Royals, Pirates) deserve attention when earned
- If 3+ posts are about the same team, look harder at other storylines
- West Coast games (late results) are easy to miss — check them in the morning
- International players and storylines deserve coverage — MLB is a global game

## Key Dates Reminder

- **Trade deadline**: Late July / early August — rumor coverage starts 2 weeks before
- **All-Star rosters announced**: Early July — snub debates are automatic Tier 2 content
- **September 1**: Roster expansion — call-up previews start in late August
- **Award voting deadlines**: After regular season — prediction content in late September
- **Free agency opens**: 5 days after World Series — watch for opt-outs and early signings
- **Non-tender deadline**: Early December — DFA and non-tender stories
- **Winter Meetings**: Early December — peak offseason content week
