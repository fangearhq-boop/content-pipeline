Bobby Witt Jr. — Grade 1 MCL Sprain, Sunday Evaluation, All-Star Race at Risk
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The news on Bobby Witt Jr. is about as good as it could be given the circumstances — and the calendar is working in his favor. The Royals confirmed Witt has a Grade 1 MCL sprain in his right knee. He exited Thursday night's Cardinals game with right knee discomfort, and an MRI revealed the sprain the next morning. Kansas City manager Matt Quatraro called it "best case scenario" — and in the context of an MCL injury to your best player, he's right to say that. Grade 1 MCL sprains are the mildest on the MCL injury spectrum. They typically respond well to conservative treatment — rest, ice, compression, and a brace to stabilize the knee. Surgery is not required. The Royals indicated they can manage this without going under the knife, and they're evaluating Witt again on Sunday to determine whether an IL stint is necessary. Here's the key number: the All-Star Game is July 14 in Philadelphia. If Witt misses 10 to 14 days — a reasonable timeline for Grade 1 MCL recovery — he returns well before the Midsummer Classic. The risk is a setback or additional swelling that pushes the recovery into the All-Star window. That would be a blow both to the Royals and to fans who expected to see Witt starting at shortstop in his first All-Star Game. For now, the message from Kansas City is cautious optimism. Grade 1. Best case. Not surgical. Those are the words you want to hear on a Saturday morning when your franchise player has a knee issue. ### Image Post Caption Bobby Witt Jr. — Grade 1 MCL sprain, "best case scenario" per the Royals. Evaluating Sunday. All-Star Game is July 14. The timeline still works. 🙏 #Royals #BobbyWittJr
