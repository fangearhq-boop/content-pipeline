Twins Crush Diamondbacks 16-8 — Buxton Grand Slam in 10-Run 5th Inning

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 21, 2026

Byron Buxton hit a grand slam on Sunday. It was part of a 10-run inning. The Twins won 16-8.

That's the kind of sentence that makes you read it twice — and it accurately describes what happened when Minnesota visited Arizona for Sunday's game. A 10-run 5th inning with a Buxton grand slam at its center turned a manageable game into a statement. The Twins scored 16 runs. The Diamondbacks allowed them.

Buxton at His Best

Byron Buxton is one of the most talented players in baseball. He is also one of the most injury-prone — a combination that has defined his career and frustrated the Minnesota fanbase for years.

When Buxton plays, the Twins are a different team. He provides elite center field defense, speed on the bases, and power at the plate that few players in the sport can match. A grand slam in a 10-run inning is exactly the kind of game-changing performance that makes his presence in the lineup so valuable — and his absences so difficult to absorb.

Right now, he's healthy. And Minnesota is benefiting accordingly.

The 10-Run Inning

10-run innings are a baseball rarity. They require a combination of hits, walks, errors, and timely power — the kind of confluence that happens a handful of times per season league-wide. When they happen, they're usually accompanied by a Buxton-type moment: a grand slam that blows a game open and turns a close contest into a rout.

Minnesota scored 10 runs in the 5th inning on Sunday. The game was effectively over before the 6th inning started. A 16-8 final is not a reflection of a competitive game — it's what happens when one team's offense gets loose and the other's pitching can't stop the bleeding.

The Diamondbacks are a quality team. Minnesota beat them badly. That's a statement about where the Twins are right now.

Minnesota in the Wild Card Picture

The American League Wild Card race is competitive, and the Twins are right in the middle of it. Days like Sunday — 16 runs, a 10-run inning, a Buxton grand slam — are the best version of what Minnesota can be: a dangerous offensive team with the kind of power that can change a series in an inning.

The question for the Twins is always sustainability. Can they stay healthy? Can they maintain this production through the second half? Games like Sunday build the case that yes, they can — at least for now.
