Albies Walk-Off, Two-Homer Game — Braves Beat Brewers 4-3, Lead NL

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 21, 2026

The Atlanta Braves have the best record in the National League. They're doing it without Ronald Acuña. And on Saturday, they did it with a walk-off.

Ozzie Albies hit two home runs on Saturday, including a walk-off blast in the 9th inning to give Atlanta a 4-3 comeback win over Milwaukee. The Braves trailed heading into their final at-bats and needed someone to deliver. Albies stepped up in the moment — the same way he has been stepping up all season with Acuña on the injured list.

Albies the Offensive Engine

When Acuña went back on the IL with a hamstring injury — his second stint in 2026 — Atlanta needed other players to carry more of the offensive load. Albies has answered that call.

The second baseman has long been one of the more underrated hitters in the National League. He's a switch-hitter with legitimate power from both sides of the plate, a player who can hit in the middle of any lineup and not look out of place. In a season where Acuña's absence has tested Atlanta's depth, Albies has been a consistent presence in the heart of the order.

Two home runs in a game, one of them a walk-off, is a reminder of what Albies can do when he's swinging well. He is not a player who gets the national spotlight by default. Moments like Saturday give him a reason to have it.

The Braves Without Acuña

Atlanta was 45-23 before Acuña's most recent IL stint — the best record in the NL. Since losing him, the Braves have continued to win, which speaks to the organization's roster depth and to the contributions of players like Albies, Chris Sale, Michael Olson, and others who have held the fort.

Beating the Brewers — a legitimate playoff contender in the NL Central — with a 9th-inning walk-off is the kind of win that builds confidence and maintains a winning culture. Atlanta isn't coasting to a division title. They're earning it, night by night, even without their best player on the field.

What's Next

Atlanta continues to build toward postseason positioning while monitoring Acuña's recovery. With Chris Sale providing dominant starting pitching and Albies anchoring the middle of the lineup, the Braves are built to compete at a high level regardless of when Acuña returns.
