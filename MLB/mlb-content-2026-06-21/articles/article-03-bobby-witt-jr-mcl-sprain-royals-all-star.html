Bobby Witt Jr. — Grade 1 MCL Sprain, "Best Case Scenario," Sunday Evaluation

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | June 21, 2026

The news on Bobby Witt Jr. could have been much worse. It wasn't.

The Kansas City Royals confirmed Friday that Witt has a Grade 1 MCL sprain in his right knee after exiting Thursday night's game against the Cardinals with discomfort. An MRI revealed the injury, which is the mildest classification on the MCL injury spectrum. Manager Matt Quatraro described it as the "best case scenario" — and relative to the alternatives, he's right.

Kansas City is taking a measured approach. The club will evaluate Witt again on Sunday before determining whether an injured list stint is necessary. The Royals can backdate an IL stint up to three days from Witt's last game, giving them flexibility to wait and assess.

What Grade 1 Means

MCL sprains are graded on a scale of 1 through 3. A Grade 1 sprain involves stretching of the ligament without significant tearing. It is the least severe classification and typically heals with conservative treatment — rest, a stabilizing brace, and time to reduce swelling.

Surgery is not required for Grade 1 sprains. The Royals indicated as much, noting they can put a brace on the knee and manage the injury without an extended absence. Recovery timelines vary, but 10 to 21 days is a common range for Grade 1 MCL injuries in athletes.

For Witt and the Royals, the injury timeline matters for one specific reason: the All-Star Game.

The All-Star Picture

The 2026 MLB All-Star Game is set for July 14 in Philadelphia. Witt has been the leading vote-getter among American League shortstops throughout the Phase 1 ballot period. He is the kind of player the All-Star Game is built around — young, electric, and playing at an MVP level.

A Grade 1 MCL sprain with an evaluation on Sunday and a potential 10-to-14-day recovery timeline puts Witt back on the field well before July 14. The window is tight but manageable, assuming no setbacks or additional inflammation during the healing process.

If Witt's recovery goes smoothly, he plays in the All-Star Game. If there are complications, the timeline gets more difficult. The Royals aren't projecting anything dramatic — but they are being appropriately cautious.

Witt's 2026 Season

Witt was having an outstanding season before the injury. He has established himself as one of the premier shortstops in baseball and one of the most complete players in the American League. Losing him for even a week significantly impacts Kansas City's outlook in a competitive AL Wild Card race.

The Royals were optimistic enough to play Sunday without him and reassess. That's not panic — that's a team being cautious with its most important player while keeping a clear head about what the medical situation actually is. Grade 1. Not surgical. Evaluating Sunday. Those are the facts.

What's Next

Sunday's evaluation will determine whether Witt goes on the injured list or attempts to play through the injury with a brace. Follow-up MRIs or additional imaging could be ordered depending on how he feels. The All-Star ballot closes June 25 (Phase 1) — Witt's vote totals should ensure his selection regardless of a brief IL stint.
