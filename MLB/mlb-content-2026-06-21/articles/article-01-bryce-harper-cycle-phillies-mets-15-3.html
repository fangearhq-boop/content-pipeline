Bryce Harper Hits for the Cycle — First Career Cycle, Completed in Five Innings

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 21, 2026

Bryce Harper now has something he'd never had before Saturday night: a cycle.

In a 15-3 rout of the New York Mets at Citizens Bank Park, Harper completed the first cycle of his career and did it in just five innings — making it the second-fastest cycle completion in MLB history. The Phillies' designated hitter went deep in the 1st inning, added a double and a single during Philadelphia's eight-run 3rd inning, and completed the feat with a triple in the 5th. He slid into third, pumped his right arm, and raised both arms skyward as the crowd erupted.

Harper became the 11th player in Phillies history to hit for the cycle.

The Sequence

Hitting for the cycle requires collecting a single, double, triple, and home run in the same game — in any order. The challenge of the triple is what makes cycles rare. The vast majority of MLB players never record one in their careers, let alone in five innings.

Harper's cycle followed a natural rhythm of the game. The homer in the 1st set the tone. His contributions to the 3rd-inning outburst — a double and a single — helped Philadelphia build the kind of lead that makes a game effectively over by the middle innings. The triple in the 5th completed history.

The only player to complete a cycle faster was Mike Lansing, who accomplished the feat in four innings in 2000. Harper's five-inning completion sits alongside the very best in that category.

The Game Around Him

Harper wasn't the only Phillie making history on Saturday. Kyle Schwarber hit three home runs in the same game — going from 25 to 28 on the season to extend his MLB home run lead. The combination of Harper's cycle and Schwarber's three-homer game in the same 15-3 blowout makes this one of the more remarkable individual stat-line nights a single team has produced in recent memory.

The Mets, by contrast, had no answer for the Philadelphia offense. A 15-3 final is a statement. The Phillies played the kind of complete game that makes clear why Philadelphia is one of the favorites to represent the National League in October.

Harper in Context

Harper was named the National League Most Valuable Player in 2021 and 2025. He has been one of the most decorated and scrutinized players in baseball for over a decade. The cycle is one of those individual accomplishments that had eluded him through his entire career — a combination of luck, timing, and performance that hadn't aligned until Saturday night.

He has it now. And he got it in five innings, in a 15-3 win, in his own park, in front of a crowd that watched Schwarber hit three more home runs in the same game. There are better venues for a career highlight. It's hard to think of many.

What's Next

Philadelphia continues their homestand and remains one of the top contenders in the National League. With Schwarber approaching historic home run totals and Harper adding a cycle to his resume, the Phillies offense is operating at a level few teams in baseball can match.
