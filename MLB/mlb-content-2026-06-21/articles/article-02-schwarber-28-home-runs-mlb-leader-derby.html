Schwarber Hits 3 HRs in Harper Cycle Game — Now MLB-Leading 28 Home Runs

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 21, 2026

Bryce Harper had the most memorable individual night. But Kyle Schwarber made history of his own.

In the same 15-3 blowout of the Mets that featured Harper's first career cycle, Schwarber went deep three times to jump from 25 home runs to 28 in a single game. He now holds the major league lead — and the gap between him and the field is growing.

One of his blasts traveled 456 feet. This is what Schwarber's best version looks like: a left-handed power hitter who hits baseballs into places that force outfielders to turn and watch rather than run.

The Pace

The conversation around Schwarber has moved past "is he having a great season" and into territory that a lot of people weren't expecting to occupy in 2026. At his current pace, Schwarber is tracking toward 60 or more home runs on the season — a number that would place him in territory that very few hitters in the history of baseball have reached.

The single-season home run record belongs to Barry Bonds, who hit 73 in 2001. Reaching those heights is a different conversation. But 60 home runs? That's Roger Maris territory. That's the kind of season that gets discussed for decades.

We are in June. There is still half a season to play. Paces change. Pitchers make adjustments. Players get hurt. None of that has happened to Schwarber yet in 2026, and he is swinging as freely and as well as he has at any point in his career.

The Derby Setup

The Home Run Derby is scheduled for July 13 at Citizens Bank Park — Schwarber's home stadium. If he continues on this pace through Derby day, the event will be appointment television.

A left-handed power hitter in his own park, in front of a crowd that has watched him hit missiles all season, swinging without consequence in a home run competition. The conditions for a historic Derby performance are all there. The only thing standing in the way is sustaining this production for three more weeks.

Given what he's doing right now, that doesn't seem like a significant obstacle.

The Same Game as Harper's Cycle

The oddity of Saturday night is that neither Harper's cycle nor Schwarber's three-homer game would have been the lead story in isolation. Together, they produced one of the most remarkable individual double-billing performances in recent MLB history — two star players having landmark games on the same night for the same team against the same opponent.

The Mets gave up 15 runs. Both Harper and Schwarber had nights for the record books. When a lineup this talented gets going, the results can be difficult to fathom.

What's Next

Schwarber's home run total will be tracked daily through the All-Star break and beyond. The Home Run Derby on July 13 at Citizens Bank Park is the next major landmark event.
