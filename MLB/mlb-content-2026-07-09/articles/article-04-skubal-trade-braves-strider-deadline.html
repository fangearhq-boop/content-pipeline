Skubal Trade Watch: The Real Reason the Braves Are Desperate

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 9, 2026

The Atlanta Braves want Tarik Skubal. But the more you understand what happened to Spencer Strider, the more "want" feels like the wrong word. The Braves need Tarik Skubal.

That distinction is driving one of the most consequential trade negotiations in baseball.

What Happened to Strider

Spencer Strider was Atlanta's No. 2 starter heading into 2026 — the kind of arm that gives a rotation depth and gives an organization confidence in its rotation from top to bottom. In mid-June, he went to the injured list with right elbow inflammation.

On June 17, he was transferred to the 60-day IL. The MRI came back clean — no ligament damage, which was the feared outcome. But he was shut down for at least four weeks from that date. The earliest possible return puts him back around August 17.

The August 2 trade deadline is 15 days before that.

A Braves insider went further this week, suggesting Strider may not pitch again in 2026 at all. That's speculation until it's not, but the implication is clear: Atlanta cannot count on Strider being part of their postseason rotation.

JR Ritchie and Reynaldo Lopez have been filling the vacancy. They are not Spencer Strider.

Why Skubal

Tarik Skubal is a two-time Cy Young Award winner pitching for a Tigers team that is 37-49 and firmly in sell mode. ESPN's Jeff Passan and Kiley McDaniel estimate the probability of a Skubal trade at 85%. He is the best available arm at the deadline by a considerable margin.

USA Today's Bob Nightengale has named the Braves as the frontrunner, describing a negotiation expected to go "to the final days, if not hours" before the August 2 deadline at 6 PM ET.

The cost, per multiple reports: at least two of Atlanta's top-10 prospects, with one likely ranking in the top 100 overall. That is a significant price for a rental — and Skubal is a rental. He is a pending free agent, and acquiring him without extending him would mean paying two elite prospects for a partial-season contribution.

Unless the Braves also sign him, which would mean giving a pitcher the largest contract in MLB history.

The Calculus

This is not a situation where Atlanta is looking to upgrade at the margins. This is a situation where a team that believes it is a legitimate World Series contender — with Ronald Acuña, Chris Sale, Matt Olson, and a deep lineup — has a meaningful hole in its rotation and the deadline approaching fast.

The Dodgers have a superior prospect pool and are also in the Skubal conversation, as are the Yankees, Cubs, and others. ESPN published nine separate blockbuster trade proposals involving Skubal in different destinations. This is the most contested deal of the deadline cycle.

But the Braves' urgency has a clarity that the other teams cannot fully match. Strider being out until at least August 17 — or potentially done for the year — is the difference between an upgrade and a necessity.

What Happens Next

All-Star Week runs through July 14. The next two weeks will see activity in the Skubal sweepstakes intensify as the August 2 clock ticks down. Nightengale's "final hours" prediction suggests this won't be resolved quietly.

The Braves are the team with the most to lose if they don't get him. Which means they're probably the team most likely to blink first.

Watch for movement in the final week of July.
