MLB Draft 2026: Two Days Out, No. 1 Pick Is Still Anyone's Guess

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 9, 2026

The 2026 MLB Draft is Saturday, and ESPN's Kiley McDaniel has a description that says everything you need to know about the top pick: "a three-way coin flip."

Two days from the first selection, the Chicago White Sox — who hold the No. 1 overall pick — still haven't made their choice public. Sports Illustrated reported this week that the internal decision is "a bit of a mess."

That's not a knock on the franchise. It's a testament to the quality of this draft class at the top. Three players are legitimately in play for the most coveted spot in amateur baseball, and any of the three could be the right answer.

The Three Candidates

Roch Cholowsky, SS, UCLA

Cholowsky has been the consensus No. 1 prospect in this class for more than a year. Baseball America considers him the best overall prospect in the draft, citing advanced offensive tools and the most polished skill set of any player available. He's a shortstop who projects to hit at every level, with plate discipline well beyond his age. For teams looking for a sure thing, Cholowsky has been the answer since before the season started.

Grady Emerson, SS, Fort Worth Christian HS (TX)

Emerson just leapfrogged Cholowsky to the No. 1 spot on MLB Pipeline's most recent Top 250 update. The Texas prep shortstop has the kind of upside that makes scouts dream — raw tools, elite athleticism, and a ceiling that may exceed anyone else in this class. High school picks carry more developmental risk, but the upside can also be unmatched. Emerson's late surge to the top of the list reflects a growing belief that his ceiling is simply the highest.

Vahn Lackey, C, Georgia Tech

Lackey is the dark horse who has become loud in recent weeks. The catcher from Georgia Tech brings a skillset that is rare at his position — genuine power combined with the defensive profile teams covet. His name has gotten progressively louder as the draft approaches. He is a legitimate No. 1 candidate, not just a name to consider as an afterthought.

The White Sox Context

The Chicago White Sox picking No. 1 in 2026 requires a moment of appreciation for the absurdity of the situation.

In 2024, the White Sox went 41-121. One of the worst records in modern baseball history. They won the draft lottery based on that performance and entered 2026 holding the top selection.

As of this week, the White Sox are 47-42 and leading the AL Central. One of the most improbable first-half turnarounds in recent memory.

This Saturday, while the baseball world descends on Philadelphia for All-Star Week, the same White Sox — first-place team, two-year rebuild, one of sport's great reclamation stories — will walk to the podium and add what they believe is a franchise cornerstone to their already-improving roster.

It's the right kind of problem to have. And it's a story that unfolds live on NBC and Peacock when the first 10 picks are announced.

The Rest of the Class

Beyond the top three, this class is notably shortstop-heavy. Jackson Flora (RHP, UC Santa Barbara) and Jacob Lombard (SS, Gulliver Prep, FL) are among the names expected to go in the first handful of picks regardless of what the White Sox decide.

Flora represents the top pitching prospect in a class that is significantly stronger at position players up top. If a team decides to break the shortstop run early, he's the name to watch.

Saturday's Setting

One more detail worth noting: the draft takes place at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, in the middle of All-Star Week. The stage is absurd in the best way — the biggest event in the amateur baseball calendar, happening simultaneously with the biggest event in the professional baseball calendar, in the same building, in the same city.

Cholowsky or Emerson or Lackey will hear their name called Saturday. Then they'll shake hands and go stand somewhere near the same field where the All-Star Game will be played four days later.

Two days out. Still a coin flip. Still worth watching every second.
