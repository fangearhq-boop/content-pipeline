White Sox Stumble Before the Break, But the AL Central Race Is Still Wide Open

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 9, 2026

The Chicago White Sox did not go into the All-Star break with the kind of momentum they were hoping for.

In their final series before the break, the White Sox faced the Boston Red Sox at home — and dropped both games. Tuesday's loss was an 8-1 rout. Wednesday's was a 5-0 shutout, with Davis Martin absorbing the loss after allowing runs via wild pitch. Boston's contributors included Tsung-Che Cheng, Ceddanne Rafaela, and Carlos Narváez — none of them household names, which makes the whitewash sting even more.

It was a rough closing statement for a team that is otherwise living one of baseball's most compelling second-act stories.

The Context

The White Sox went 41-121 in 2024. One of the worst records in MLB history. A season that prompted front-office upheaval and a complete reassessment of the rebuild timeline.

This year, they're 47-42 and have been leading the AL Central for stretches of the first half. The turnaround isn't a fluke — it's built on improved pitching, better bullpen management, and a lineup that is actually scoring runs. This is a team that has earned its position.

The losses to Boston don't erase any of that. They do, however, introduce some tightness in the standings heading into the break.

The Twins Are Quietly Surging

While the White Sox were losing to Boston, the Guardians were falling too — a 6-5 walk-off loss to the Minnesota Twins on Wednesday. Alan Roden delivered the game-winning RBI single in the bottom of the ninth. Kody Clemens went 3-for-4. Yoendrys Gómez picked up his first win of the season with a scoreless ninth inning.

The Twins have now won four straight and five of their last six. They are applying pressure from below in the AL Central, and after the break, this race will be a three-team story rather than a two-team one.

Brayan Rocchio and Rhys Hoskins both homered for Cleveland in the loss — two players who have been part of what was supposed to be a Guardians surge. The walk-off defeat is the kind of game Cleveland will look back on when the final standings are calculated.

The Draft Weekend Wrinkle

One more layer to the White Sox narrative before the break: they hold the No. 1 overall pick in Saturday's MLB Draft.

They earned that pick by going 41-121. They're now using it while leading the AL Central. The juxtaposition is something baseball writers will be citing in the "greatest turnarounds" conversation for years. A team bad enough to win the No. 1 pick is now good enough to be in first place by the time they make it.

What Comes After the Break

The All-Star break resets the calendar. When games resume, the AL Central race will be exactly what it was before the Boston series — close, competitive, and genuinely unresolved.

The White Sox lead. The Guardians are close. The Twins are rising. Three teams, 70-ish games remaining, no clear favorite for the division title.

Two losses to the Red Sox do not change that. But they do serve as a reminder that the White Sox are not a finished product — they're a work in progress, same as every other team in this race.

The break comes at the right time. When baseball comes back, so does the chase.
