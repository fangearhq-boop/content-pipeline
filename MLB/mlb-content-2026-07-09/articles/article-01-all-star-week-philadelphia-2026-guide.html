All-Star Week in Philadelphia: A 50-Year Wait, a Nation's Birthday, and Baseball's Biggest Stage

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 9, 2026

Philadelphia has been waiting for this for fifty years.

The last time the city hosted the MLB All-Star Game was 1976 at Veterans Stadium, in the heart of the nation's Bicentennial celebration. On Tuesday night, the Midsummer Classic returns to Citizens Bank Park — and this time, it arrives alongside America's Semiquincentennial, the 250th anniversary of independence.

The symmetry is not accidental. MLB awarded the 2026 game to Philadelphia years ago with this exact milestone in mind. Baseball and America's birthday, reunited in the same city where both have deep, complicated, beloved roots.

All-Star Week officially begins Friday, July 10, and runs through Tuesday, July 14. Here is the complete breakdown of what's coming.

The Schedule

Friday, July 10: Opening events begin at Citizens Bank Park and the Pennsylvania Convention Center. The week kicks off quietly before the weekend's events escalate.

Saturday, July 11: The 2026 MLB Draft opens at Citizens Bank Park with the first 10 picks airing live on NBC and Peacock. The Capital One All-Star Village also opens Saturday, and the HBCU Swingman Classic takes place in the afternoon.

Sunday, July 12: The All-Star Futures Game showcases baseball's top prospects. MLBx — the All-Star 3-on-3 home run and catch competition — follows in the evening.

Monday, July 13: The T-Mobile Home Run Derby at Citizens Bank Park, broadcast live on Netflix at 8 PM ET. The first Derby on Netflix, featuring a new swing-count format.

Tuesday, July 14: The 96th MLB All-Star Game presented by Mastercard, broadcast on FOX at 8 PM ET. American League versus National League in the game that, even in a non-home-field-advantage era, carries its own weight of history and pageantry.

The Entertainment

MLB announced an entertainment lineup that matches the occasion.

Patti LaBelle — the Philadelphia legend whose voice is as much a part of this city's identity as the Liberty Bell — will perform the National Anthem with the Military District of Washington Joint Chorus and the West Point Band.

Jennifer Hudson, winner of Emmy, Grammy, Tony, and Oscar awards, will perform "America the Beautiful" alongside the U.S. Army Old Guard Fife & Drum Corps and the Philly Pops.

Miles Teller, born in Pennsylvania and a lifelong Phillies fan, will narrate a tribute to baseball's impact on American life — a piece that will likely hit harder in Citizens Bank Park than it would anywhere else.

Boyz II Men, one of the most celebrated R&B groups in music history and Philadelphia's own, will perform during the fifth-inning Stand Up to Cancer moment.

The Phillies Storyline

Phillies players Bryson Stott, Brandon Marsh, and Alec Bohm all advanced to the final round of All-Star fan voting. Any of them starting in front of their home crowd would add another layer to an already emotional week.

Kyle Schwarber, the 2025 All-Star Game MVP, brings a defense-of-title subplot. He won the Ted Williams Award last year with a performance that is already the stuff of baseball legend. If healthy — his back has been an issue this week — he could stand in the same batter's box where it happened and do it again.

Only Mike Trout has ever defended an All-Star MVP on his home field. Schwarber has a chance to join that list.

The Bigger Picture

Baseball's relationship with Philadelphia is long and often beautiful. The city has given the game Schmidt, Carlton, Roberts, Hamels, Howard, Utley, Rollins, Harper, and Schwarber. Citizens Bank Park, opened in 2004, has established itself as one of the sport's best environments — loud, knowledgeable, and relentlessly partisan.

Fifty years between hostings is a long time. But the timing in 2026, landing exactly at America's 250th birthday, makes this feel less like a scheduling coincidence and more like something that was always going to happen this way.

The All-Star Game comes to Philadelphia every half-century, apparently. And every time, the nation is celebrating something.

Baseball's biggest week is here. It runs through Tuesday night. Don't look away.
