Amed Rosario Hits Two Home Runs as Yankees Rally From 3-1 Deficit to Stay at 8-2

By Marcus Cole

April 8, 2026

The Yankees went 0-for-12 with runners in scoring position on Tuesday night. Then Amed Rosario picked up a bat.

In what became one of the more unexpectedly dramatic victories of their early 2026 run, New York rallied from a 3-1 deficit against Oakland in the 8th inning, with Rosario delivering a go-ahead three-run home run to power a 5-3 win. The victory improved the Yankees to 8-2, a record built on the back of historically elite pitching and, apparently, a bench player with a flair for late-innings heroics.

The Night Rosario Took Over

Rosario had already made his mark in the 1st inning with a solo home run. But the real moment came in the 8th. The Yankees had been thoroughly stymied with runners on base — zero hits in 12 tries. The scoreboard read 3-1 Athletics. Former Yankee Mark Leiter Jr. had the ball on the mound.

Rosario worked the count to 0-1, then turned on a splitter and lifted it over the wall for a three-run shot. Yankees lead, 4-3. A sacrifice fly tacked on one more run. Final: 5-3, Yankees.

It was the 3rd multi-homer game of Rosario's career, and his first since August 31, 2021, when he was still playing for Cleveland. He drove in all 5 of New York's runs.

A Complicated Night for the Rotation

Tuesday's game was notable for another reason: it ended Cam Schlittler's remarkable scoreless innings streak. The Yankees rookie had gone 21 consecutive scoreless innings — a streak that dated back to the 2025 season — before Oakland's Nick Kurtz doubled to right-center in the 3rd inning, scoring two runs. It was a reminder that no streak lasts forever, even from a pitcher who had been effectively historic over his past five appearances.

Schlittler still left the game before the deficit grew larger, and the bullpen held until Rosario's 8th-inning eruption completed the comeback.

The Bigger Picture: 8-2 and Counting

The Yankees' 8-2 start is part of an early-season run that has drawn comparisons to historically great pitching teams. Through nine games, New York has allowed just 8 runs — tying the 2002 San Francisco Giants and 1993 Atlanta Braves for the fewest runs allowed through nine games in modern MLB history. They've done it without Gerrit Cole and Carlos Rodon, both currently on the injured list.

Max Fried has pitched 13.1 scoreless innings across his first two starts — the first Yankee to accomplish that since Mel Stottlemyre in 1967. The staff has been, simply, one of the most statistically dominant early-season rotations in baseball history.

On nights when that pitching gets hit — and last night, it did — the Yankees still find a way. This time, it was a journeyman whose last multi-homer game came during the pandemic era.

What's Next

The Yankees continue their series against Oakland on Wednesday, with Will Warren taking the mound against the Athletics. Will Warren faces a lineup that has shown some ability to get on base but also leads the AL in strikeouts per game. New York enters the week looking to push toward 9-2 and further solidify their position atop the AL East standings.
