The Milwaukee Brewers Are 8-2 and Nobody Predicted This

By Marcus Cole

April 8, 2026

PECOTA projected the Milwaukee Brewers to finish the 2026 season at 81-81. Dead even. An average team in an average division.

Through 10 games, the Brewers are 8-2 with a +30 run differential — the second-best differential in all of baseball. They entered the year ranked No. 11 in national power rankings. They are now ranked No. 3.

Nobody saw this coming.

It Started on Opening Day

The tone was set immediately by a 22-year-old pitcher named Jacob Misiorowski. On Opening Day, he struck out 11 batters in just 5 innings — a new Brewers franchise record for Opening Day strikeouts. He became the youngest player to start on Opening Day for Milwaukee since Ben Sheets in 2002, and the youngest pitcher to record 10+ strikeouts on Opening Day since Felix Hernandez did it at age 20 in 2007.

Misiorowski didn't just make history. He announced that the Brewers' young pitching core was ready for something bigger than an 81-81 season.

The Offense Has Shown Up Too

If the pitching was the headline, the offense wrote the supporting story. In the first week of the season, Garrett Mitchell posted 5 hits — including 3 doubles and a home run — with 8 RBI. William Contreras and Christian Yelich each led the team with 6 hits. Gary Sánchez launched 2 home runs.

That combination — young arms dominating and veterans contributing — gave the Brewers a +30 run differential in just 10 games. Only the Los Angeles Dodgers have been better, and the Dodgers are the defending World Series champions with a $400 million payroll.

Milwaukee's payroll is considerably more modest.

Why This Matters

Early-season samples are small, and projections exist for a reason. The Brewers will not finish 8-2 through 10-game blocks all year. But there's something real here. A 22-year-old ace setting franchise records. A lineup with legitimate contributors at multiple positions. A run differential that suggests this isn't a product of luck — teams don't outscore opponents by 30 in 10 games by accident.

Milwaukee's NL Central competition isn't particularly fearsome. The Cubs are dealing with injuries. The Cardinals are inconsistent. The Pirates and Reds are young and unpredictable. The Brewers' window to win the NL Central may be right now — and right now, they look like the best team in their division.

What's Next

The Brewers' April schedule continues with a mix of divisional and interleague games. If Misiorowski can sustain even a fraction of his Opening Day performance, and if the lineup continues to produce at this rate, Milwaukee may be the NL Central's biggest story well into the summer. For now, they're simply the biggest surprise in baseball. Nobody told them they were supposed to be average.
