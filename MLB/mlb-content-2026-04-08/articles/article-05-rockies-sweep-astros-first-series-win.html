Rockies Sweep Astros — An 8-Run Inning and a Shutdown Performance for Colorado's First Series Win in Nine Months

By Jake Torres

April 8, 2026

Colorado's first series win since mid-June 2025 wasn't supposed to come against the Houston Astros. But the Rockies apparently didn't care about the script.

Over two games at Coors Field, the Rockies beat the Astros 9-7 and 5-1 to complete the sweep and improve to 5-6 on the season. The method was different each night — a fireworks show followed by a surgical shutdown — but the result was the same. Colorado walked away with a series they had no business winning on paper, their first series win of any kind since the middle of last summer.

Monday: The 8-Run Inning

Game 1 on Monday had all the makings of a loss. The Astros are a better team on paper. The Rockies were playing at home, but Coors Field giveth and taketh away. The Astros had the lead late.

Then the 5th inning happened.

Colorado scored 8 runs in the 5th, turning a deficit into a blowout. Troy Johnston contributed a home run, double, and single across the game. Edouard Julien, T.J. Rumfield, and Willi Castro all recorded multiple hits. The Rockies finished with 12 hits on the night in the 9-7 final.

Tuesday: The Shutdown

Game 2 was the mirror image. Kyle Freeland gave the Rockies seven effective innings, holding Houston to just 1 run on 3 hits through 6.1 innings of work. Willi Castro and Mickey Moniak each hit two-run home runs to provide the offense in a 5-1 win. Antonio Senzatela came in for the final outs and retired all 8 batters he faced on just 26 pitches, earning his first career save in the process.

It was the kind of complete performance — starter goes deep, big inning in the middle, bullpen closes it out — that winning teams put together consistently. The Rockies haven't put together many of those in recent years.

Context and Caveat

The Astros were missing Hunter Brown, who went on the injured list this week with elbow discomfort. Houston was also sorting through early rotation kinks that most contenders deal with in the first two weeks of the season. None of that diminishes what Colorado accomplished, but it's part of the picture.

What the Rockies showed in this series: they can score in big bursts, and they have at least one starter (Freeland) who can give them a chance to win on any given night. For a franchise that hasn't made the postseason in years, back-to-back wins over a playoff team with a series-clinching 5-1 final is the kind of small confirmation that maybe 2026 could look different.

What's Next

Colorado now turns its attention to the rest of April with an improving record and some genuine momentum. A 5-6 start doesn't make them a contender, but it's better than last year's pace at this point in the season. Their first series win since mid-June 2025 didn't come against a weak opponent. That's worth something in a long season.
