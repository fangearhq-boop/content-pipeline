The April MLB Report Card: Braves and Dodgers Lead the Way, Mets and Phillies Already in Trouble

By Marcus Cole | April 30, 2026

April 2026 is over. The standings have spoken. Here is what a month of baseball has revealed about which teams are real and which ones need to ask serious questions.

At the top of the leaderboard, the Atlanta Braves and Los Angeles Dodgers are tied with baseball's best record at 20-9. Atlanta reached 20 wins first, and they have done it the right way — with the best ERA in baseball and the most runs scored in the sport. Their pitching staff has been the anchor, and the offense has more than kept pace. The Dodgers match the record on the strength of Shohei Ohtani's historic two-way start and one of the deepest rotations in baseball.

The AL Picture

The New York Yankees are 19-10 and lead the AL East, paced by Aaron Judge's torrid start. The surprise team of April in the American League is the Oakland Athletics, who sit atop the AL West at 15-13 despite a negative run differential — a mathematical signal that some regression may be coming, but a record is a record and Oakland has earned it.

The Houston Astros are the most glaring team-level disappointment in the junior circuit. At 11-18, they are the worst team in the AL West, a development made all the more striking by the fact that Yordan Alvarez has put up one of the most dominant offensive Aprils by any player in recent memory. The Astros have failed to support their best player, and the standings reflect it.

The NL's Best and Worst

In the National League, the story is the NL Central — every team in that division is over .500, a historically rare occurrence. The Cincinnati Reds lead at 16-9.

The NL East has produced baseball's most striking disappointments. The New York Mets and Philadelphia Phillies are both 9-19, tied for the worst record in the National League. Both franchises entered 2026 with postseason expectations; both are playing catch-up after a catastrophic April. At nine games below .500 with roughly 130 games remaining, neither team is mathematically eliminated from a comeback — but the window is narrowing.

What's Next

May is where the season's shape begins to solidify. Teams with strong April foundations — the Braves, Dodgers, Yankees, Cubs, Reds — can deepen their advantages. Teams that spent April treading water or falling behind need to make moves, find internal solutions, or begin the painful process of accepting where the season is headed. The Mets and Phillies have a closing window. The Astros have Alvarez. Everyone else has April behind them and 130 games ahead.
