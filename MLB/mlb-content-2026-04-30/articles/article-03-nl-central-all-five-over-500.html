Every NL Central Team Is Winning — And If It Stays That Way, It Would Be an MLB First

By Ryan Calloway | April 30, 2026

There are five teams in the NL Central. As of April 28, all five of them are over .500. The Reds are 16-9 and lead the division. The Cubs, Cardinals, Pirates, and Brewers are all winning games, too.

This has almost never happened.

According to Sportradar, April 28 is the second-latest date in a season that every NL Central club has been simultaneously above the break-even mark, trailing only May 29, 2004 — when the division still had six teams and each one sat at 23-22 or better. In the modern configuration of five teams, a date this late in the season with all five clubs in positive territory is essentially unprecedented in the division's history.

The History Behind the Rarity

Baseball has been played in a divisional format since 1969. In that entire span — more than 50 years of data — no entire division has ever finished the full season with every team above .500. There have been two instances of all teams finishing at exactly .500 or better: the 2005 NL East and the 1991 AL West. But finishing above .500? It has never happened. The 2026 NL Central has a chance to make history in September if the current standing holds.

The reality of the race makes this even more compelling. Only three of these five teams will make the playoffs: the division champion plus two NL wild card spots. That means at minimum two teams with winning records — potentially two teams with 85 or 90 wins — are going to be watching the postseason from home. The NL Central could produce one of the most painful eliminations in baseball history this October.

The Division Breakdown

The Cincinnati Reds (16-9) lead the way and have been one of the early surprises of the season, playing well in close games and getting strong performances from their pitching staff. The Cubs built a lengthy winning streak earlier in the month that fueled attention on the North Side. The Cardinals, Pirates, and Brewers have all found ways to win in April despite different personnel profiles and different expectations coming into the year.

The cumulative run differential across the division speaks to its strength — these are not five teams winning fluky games. They are genuinely competitive, with the kind of depth and starting pitching that can sustain winning records over 162 games.

What's Next

The NL Central race is only going to get more intense. As May begins, the division will start playing meaningful intra-division series that will shift the standings quickly. Every series loss within the division carries double weight — your loss is also their gain. For baseball fans who want to watch the most competitive single division in the sport, the NL Central in 2026 is exactly that. The history-making potential — a full division above .500 at season's end — is real. Whether it holds is the best storyline in baseball right now.
