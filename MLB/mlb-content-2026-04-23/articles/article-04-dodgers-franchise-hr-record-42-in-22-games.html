Dodgers Set Franchise Home Run Record with 42 in First 22 Games — Even in a Loss

By Marcus Cole | April 23, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Los Angeles Dodgers hit their 42nd home run of the 2026 season on Wednesday night against the San Francisco Giants.

The Dodgers lost the game 3-0.

Those two facts together say everything about what the Dodgers have built offensively: a franchise record with 42 home runs through the first 22 games of the season, achieved on a night when the offense couldn't push a single run across. The power depth is so extraordinary that the record was set even in one of the team's most complete shutout losses of the young season.

Max Muncy leads Los Angeles with eight home runs. Dalton Rushing, a name that wasn't on casual fans' radar as a power threat, has contributed seven. The Dodgers are not relying on a single player to carry the offensive load — they have built a lineup where multiple hitters can deliver the long ball on any given night, and the collective production has been record-setting.

What the Record Means

Setting a franchise home run record through 22 games is not a small accomplishment for a franchise with the history of the Dodgers. This organization has featured some of the most decorated power hitters in baseball history — names that span generations and eras. The 2026 team has hit more home runs in its first 22 games than any previous Dodgers team, and it has done so with a combination of players rather than a single generational talent carrying the count.

Los Angeles is 16-7, the second-best record in baseball, and the home run rate is a direct contributor to that position. Teams that hit the ball over the fence frequently tend to score runs efficiently, and the Dodgers have been doing exactly that throughout April.

The context of Wednesday's game is worth noting: Shohei Ohtani's on-base streak ended in the same 3-0 loss in which the franchise HR record was set. Ohtani himself did not contribute to the home run total that night — he was 0-for-4 at the plate. The record was set despite, not because of, Ohtani's bat. That is a testament to how deep and capable this lineup is beyond its most famous player.

Muncy and Rushing Driving the Count

Max Muncy's eight home runs lead the team and reflect the kind of consistent left-handed power that has made him one of the most valuable offensive contributors in the National League. Dalton Rushing's seven home runs represent one of the early breakout stories of the 2026 season — a young player delivering power production well ahead of schedule.

The combination of veterans like Muncy, emerging talents like Rushing, and the overall depth of the Dodgers' lineup create a situation where the home run totals will continue to accumulate regardless of any individual player's daily performance.

What's Next

The Dodgers are positioned to remain one of the most productive power-hitting teams in baseball throughout 2026. Watch for the home run total to climb as the season progresses and the full lineup — including Ohtani when he performs at his two-way best — operates at maximum capacity. A franchise chasing a third consecutive World Series title has the personnel to make the case every night. The first 22 games have offered the most tangible early evidence.
