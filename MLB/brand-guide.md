# Ballpark Banter — Brand Guide

## Brand Identity

- **Brand Name:** Ballpark Banter
- **Parent Company:** FanGear HQ
- **Coverage:** League-wide MLB (all 30 teams, AL and NL)

## Colors

| Name | Hex Code | Usage |
|------|----------|-------|
| Classic Red | #C62828 | Headlines, breaking news, key stats, alert moments |
| Navy Blue | #1A237E | Text overlay backgrounds, secondary headlines, division standings |
| Grass Green | #2E7D32 | Accent highlights, positive stats, win streaks |
| Midnight | #1A1A2E | Semi-transparent overlay backgrounds (40-50% opacity) |
| White | #FFFFFF | Body text on dark overlays, secondary text |

## Typography

| Role | Font | Usage |
|------|------|-------|
| Title/Headlines | Bebas Neue | All caps, bold. Used for main text overlays on images |
| Subtitle | Montserrat | Clean, professional. Used for stat lines and sub-text |
| Body | Open Sans | Readable. Used for article body text |

## Image Dimensions

| Platform / Use | Width | Height |
|----------------|-------|--------|
| Social (Instagram/FB) | 1080 | 1350 |
| Article Hero | 1200 | 630 |

## Image Composition Rules

1. Semi-transparent midnight bar (#1A1A2E at 40-50% opacity) behind all text overlays
2. Text placement: top-left or bottom-center preferred
3. Keep 50-65% of image clean for action photography
4. Logo placement: bottom-right corner when space allows
5. Action shots preferred over posed shots — diving catches, bat flips, strikeout reactions
6. Horizontal/landscape orientation for article heroes; vertical OK for social

## Social Media Voice

### X/Twitter Voice
- Concise, punchy, stat-driven
- ALL CAPS for emphasis on key stats or moments (1-2 words max)
- Dashes and periods for dramatic pacing
- Strong opinions backed by data
- Exactly 3 hashtags per tweet
- Primary hashtag: #BallparkBanter (every tweet)

### Facebook Voice
- More explanatory — assume reader needs context
- Narrative storytelling across 3-4 paragraphs
- Opinionated but less aggressive than X
- One emoji per caption OK, no emojis in long-form
- No hashtags in Facebook posts

### Article Voice
- Professional sports journalism
- Accessible to casual fans
- Stats woven into narrative, not listed
- Always end with "What's Next" forward-looking section
