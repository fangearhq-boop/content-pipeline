The reason comes down to urgency: Spencer Strider, Atlanta's projected No. 1 starter, remains out with injury. The Braves need an elite arm at the top of their rotation, and Skubal — a two-time Cy Young winner — is exactly that.

The pitch is straightforward: Skubal paired with Chris Sale at the top of the Braves' rotation would create what analysts are calling the best 1-2 in baseball. For a team with playoff ambitions in the National League, that kind of addition is transformative.

The Tigers' asking price remains steep. The earlier reports suggested a top-100 prospect plus two-to-three top-10 organizational pieces. The Braves, with one of the deeper farm systems in the NL, can theoretically meet that price — but it requires giving up significant future value for a two-month rental.

The trade deadline is August 3. The market won't resolve until late July at the earliest. But if Nightengale's reporting is accurate, Atlanta is the team to watch in the Skubal sweepstakes.
