Guardians Take the AL Central Lead: 4-3 Win Over White Sox in 10 Innings Flips the Division

By Marcus Cole

July 4, 2026

The AL Central standings just changed, and the holiday weekend has a new division leader.

The Cleveland Guardians beat the Chicago White Sox 4-3 in 10 innings on July 3, moving into first place in the American League Central for the first time this summer. The standings entering July 4: Guardians 46-42, White Sox 45-41. One game separates two teams that have been the most competitive in a division where the Tigers, Twins, and Royals have all stumbled.

What Changed

The White Sox entered this series having held the AL Central lead for much of the early summer — a genuine and somewhat surprising development from a team that went 41-121 in 2024. Their turnaround was real. Jacob Gonzalez's walk-off run against the Royals in late June was the kind of moment that crystallized how far the franchise had come. But the Guardians, who play a different style of baseball — grinding, bullpen-forward, built for 10-inning games — just demonstrated why Cleveland is never out of a pennant race.

The 10-inning game tells a story. The White Sox had enough to tie it in regulation but not enough to win in extras. That gap — a single run over 10 innings — is exactly the kind of marginal difference that separates a first-place team from a second-place team in a division race this tight.

The AL Central Race in Context

One game between the Guardians and White Sox, with the Tigers, Twins, and Royals all at varying distances behind, means this division race is genuinely open through August. Neither Cleveland nor Chicago has built a cushion that would make the August 3 trade deadline a simple decision.

For the Guardians: do they trust the current roster through the deadline, or do they add to a group that is 46-42 and one game ahead? For the White Sox: does the standings position change their buyer posture, or does one loss remind the front office that this roster has limits?

Both teams have to make that call before August 3. The division race makes both calls harder — and more interesting for everyone watching.

The Second Half of the AL Central Race

The teams are playing a four-game July series, with results through July 3 showing the Guardians winning at least twice. The remaining games of this series will go a long way toward establishing who enters the All-Star break with momentum. All-Star break begins after the July 14 game — meaning there are approximately 10 days before the league stops for the Midsummer Classic.

Both the Guardians and White Sox have earned the right to be taken seriously. The division race between them is the best story in the American League Central this summer.

What's Next

Watch the remainder of this July series and the standings heading into the All-Star break (July 14). Both teams' trade deadline postures — buyer, seller, or stand-pat — will be clarified over the next two weeks as the standings either stay tight or one team separates. For the first time this summer, the Guardians are in first. Whether they're still there at the break is the question worth tracking.
