Braves Emerge as Skubal Trade Frontrunners: Strider's Absence Makes the Need Urgent

By Jake Torres

July 4, 2026

The Tarik Skubal trade market has a clear frontrunner — and it is the Atlanta Braves.

USA Today MLB insider Bob Nightengale reported that rival executives around the league consider Atlanta the favorites to land the Detroit Tigers' ace ahead of the August 3 trade deadline. The market has been developing since late June, but the Braves have moved to the front of the pack as July begins.

Why the Braves Make Sense

The explanation starts with Spencer Strider. The Braves' projected No. 1 starter remains out with injury. Atlanta is a team built to compete in the National League — their depth and offensive talent make them a legitimate postseason contender — but a rotation missing its top arm is a significant vulnerability. Skubal fixes that.

The pairing with Chris Sale is the other piece of the argument. Analysts have noted that Skubal and Sale at the top of Atlanta's rotation would represent the best 1-2 starting combination in baseball. That's not a small thing. In a deep National League where multiple teams can hit, a rotation advantage can be determinative in October.

From a financial standpoint, Skubal's salary for the remainder of the 2026 season is approximately $11 million. For a franchise with Atlanta's resources and a short window, that's a manageable number for what he brings.

What It Would Cost

The Tigers have been holding firm on their asking price — as they should. Detroit is not a team that needs to sell Skubal. They could hold him through the deadline if they don't receive the package they want. Earlier reporting established their price as a top-100 prospect plus two-to-three top-10 organizational pieces from any given team. For the Braves specifically, the need for at least one top-100 prospect would require Atlanta to move significant future value.

That's the tradeoff every Skubal suitor faces: a two-month rental of the best pitcher available in exchange for significant organizational future. ESPN has placed the trade probability at 85%. The market is real.

Who Else Is Still In?

The Braves being the frontrunner does not mean the market has closed. The Dodgers, Yankees, Cubs, Blue Jays, Phillies, Padres, Rangers, and Rays have all been connected to Skubal in previous reporting. These teams don't disappear because Nightengale names Atlanta — they recalibrate. The Tigers will continue gathering offers through late July before making a decision.

The Dodgers in particular remain a formidable alternative. They have organizational depth to meet Detroit's asking price and are always constructed for October. If Atlanta flinches at the prospect cost, Los Angeles will be there.

What's Next

The Skubal trade is unlikely to be completed until very close to the August 3 deadline. Teams typically wait for maximum leverage and minimum risk before pulling the trigger on a deal of this magnitude. The next substantive reporting will likely come in the final week of July. Watch for movement on the Braves' prospect package discussions — if those break down, the market reopens. If they hold, Atlanta gets their ace.
