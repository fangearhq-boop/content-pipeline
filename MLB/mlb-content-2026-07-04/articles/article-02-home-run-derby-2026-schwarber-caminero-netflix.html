Schwarber and Caminero Head a 2026 Home Run Derby Field Built for a Big Moment

By Ryan Calloway

July 4, 2026

The 2026 Home Run Derby has two confirmed power hitters — and both are exactly who you'd want for the event's biggest stage in years.

Kyle Schwarber of the Philadelphia Phillies, MLB's home run leader, has confirmed his participation contingent on making tonight's All-Star roster. Junior Caminero of the Tampa Bay Rays is the first official confirmed competitor, announced June 30. The field is still being finalized as tonight's All-Star broadcast releases rosters, but the framework is in place for a Derby worth watching.

Schwarber: A Home Run Derby at Home

Schwarber leads all of Major League Baseball in home runs on the season with 30 or more entering July 4. He's a two-time prior Derby participant who knows the format and has competed at the highest level of the competition before.

What makes his 2026 appearance different is the venue. Citizens Bank Park is the Phillies' home ballpark, which means Schwarber will be launching balls in front of the Philadelphia crowd that has watched him hit all season. That's a home-field dynamic the Derby rarely produces — a hitter who is not a visitor, but the fan favorite performing in front of his own stadium.

His conditions were health and All-Star roster selection. Given his production, the roster selection is almost certain to be confirmed tonight.

Caminero: The First Confirmed, With a Score to Settle

Caminero entered the 2026 Derby as the first official confirmed participant. He has motivation: in 2025, he was the Derby runner-up, losing 18-15 in the final to Cal Raleigh. His home run total in the competition — and in the regular season — established him as one of the game's legitimate power threats. He returns in 2026 with the energy of a player who came close and wants to finish the job.

Netflix: The Derby's Streaming Debut

The 2026 Home Run Derby airs on Netflix — the first time the competition has appeared on a streaming platform. This is a genuine milestone for the event's distribution. Netflix brings the Derby to audiences who don't have traditional cable or satellite access to sports broadcasts, potentially expanding the viewer base significantly beyond prior years.

For an event that runs roughly three hours on a Monday evening in July, Netflix is a natural fit. The Derby is social-media-friendly, crowd-driven, and built for the kind of viewing experience that streaming platforms have optimized over the past decade.

July 13. Citizens Bank Park. Netflix. That's the event.

What's Next

Tonight's All-Star Selection Show (FOX, 7:30 PM ET) will trigger additional Derby participant announcements as rosters are finalized. Expect the full Derby field to be confirmed in the next 24-48 hours. Schwarber's participation becomes official once he appears on the All-Star roster, likely confirmed tonight. July 13 is the date — one week out — and the field has the quality to make this year's competition one of the most memorable in years.
