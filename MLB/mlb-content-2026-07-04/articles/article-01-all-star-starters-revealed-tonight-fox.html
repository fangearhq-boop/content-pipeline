2026 All-Star Starters Revealed Tonight: Witt Jr., Alvarez, Freeman Lead Phase 2 into FOX Broadcast

By Jake Torres

July 4, 2026

The wait is almost over. At 7:30 PM ET tonight, FOX will broadcast the 2026 MLB All-Star Game Selection Show — the live reveal of every starting lineup, pitcher, and reserve for the Midsummer Classic on July 14 in Philadelphia.

Phase 2 voting closed July 2 at noon ET. Here's what the last public update showed, and who is expected to walk away as a starter tonight.

The Automatic Starters: Already Decided

Two spots were locked in before Phase 2 even began. Shohei Ohtani received 3,341,257 Phase 1 votes — the highest NL total — to earn automatic NL designated hitter status. Ernie Clement of the American League won 3,232,932 Phase 1 votes and is the automatic AL second baseman. Their All-Star spots are confirmed regardless of tonight's broadcast.

The Phase 2 Races: What to Expect Tonight

At shortstop in the American League, Bobby Witt Jr. of the Kansas City Royals held a commanding 72% advantage over Andrés Giménez of the Blue Jays in Phase 2 voting. If that lead holds, Witt would become the first Royals shortstop to start the All-Star Game in the modern era — and would finally end a streak of near-misses that has become its own storyline. He lost Phase 2 to Gunnar Henderson in 2024 and to Jacob Wilson in 2025. A 72% lead is large enough that a reversal would be a genuine surprise.

At designated hitter in the National League, Yordan Alvarez of the Houston Astros posted an OPS of 1.031 — the highest mark in the league — and was expected to win the DH vote as the clear statistical leader at the position. Alvarez came approximately 300,000 votes shy of an automatic Phase 1 bid, which appears to have merely delayed the result rather than changed it.

At first base in the National League, Freddie Freeman of the Los Angeles Dodgers led Phase 2 at 59%. A win would extend his consecutive All-Star appearance streak to 10 — one of the most consistent honor streaks in the modern game.

What Happens After Tonight

Tonight's broadcast reveals starters. Full 32-player rosters — starters plus reserves plus pitchers for both leagues — are announced on July 6. The complete picture of who made the All-Star team for each franchise, including which players received their first selections and which were famously left off, comes in that announcement.

Cam Schlittler, confirmed as the AL starting pitcher, has already been named ahead of tonight's broadcast. His inclusion is set regardless of tonight's Phase 2 announcements.

The 2026 MLB All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, carried on FOX. Home Run Derby precedes it on July 13 at Citizens Bank Park, airing for the first time on Netflix.

What's Next

Tonight at 7:30 PM ET on FOX — that's the only next step that matters right now. Watch for Witt Jr.'s starting announcement, which would be the signature moment of the broadcast. Full rosters follow on July 6, which is when the conversation shifts to who was snubbed. And mark July 13 and 14 on the calendar: Philadelphia is hosting the most anticipated All-Star Weekend in years.
