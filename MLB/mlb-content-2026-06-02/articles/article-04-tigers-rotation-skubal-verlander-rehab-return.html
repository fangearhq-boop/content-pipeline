Skubal and Verlander Are Rehabbing — Detroit's Rotation Recovery Is Finally Underway

By Marcus Cole | June 2, 2026

The Detroit Tigers rotation has been a walking wounded unit since April. Tarik Skubal, Justin Verlander, and Casey Mize — three of the five starters from Opening Day — have spent significant portions of the season on the injured list, leaving the team to piece together starts from depth arms while waiting for their true staff to reconstitute.

Tuesday brought the clearest sign yet that the waiting may be coming to an end.

Verlander is rehabbing at Triple-A Toledo today, testing his left hip in a live game setting for the first time in months. Skubal, who has not pitched in a major-league game since April 29, could make a rehab start against Triple-A competition as soon as this Saturday. Both pitchers are tracking toward what the Tigers hope will be mid-to-late-June returns.

Skubal's Road Back

When Tarik Skubal was shut down in late April for NanoScope surgery — a minimally invasive procedure to remove loose bodies from his elbow — the natural concern was a long-term absence. NanoScope procedures, while less invasive than traditional elbow surgery, still require recovery time and a careful progression before a pitcher can return to game situations.

The encouraging news is that the procedure appears to have gone well. The Tigers have been monitoring Skubal's throwing program closely, and as of Tuesday the reports suggest he is ready for live competition. A rehab start allows both the pitcher and the organization to evaluate real game mechanics, pitch mix, and command before clearing him for a return to the major-league rotation.

If all goes to plan, Skubal returns to Detroit's rotation sometime in the last two weeks of June. A healthy Skubal — who was a Cy Young Award winner in recent seasons — transforms the Tigers' starting pitching from a liability into a genuine strength.

Verlander at 43, Still Trying

Justin Verlander's career arc has included more comebacks than any pitcher of his generation, and the 2026 version of that story is still being written. Left hip inflammation has limited him to one major-league start all season — a March 30 outing — before he was shut down again. He has since been rehabbing toward a return that now appears closer than it did even a few weeks ago.

Verlander rehabbing at Triple-A Toledo on Tuesday represents a meaningful step. It is a live game environment, against real hitters, with real stakes — the kind of test that reveals whether an older pitcher's stuff is still where it needs to be to compete at the highest level.

Whatever he shows in Toledo will shape the conversation about his role when he returns. There is no question that a healthy Verlander brings something to the rotation that a depth arm cannot replicate — both the effectiveness and the presence that comes with his career history. But a healthy caveat is that 43-year-old pitchers coming off hip procedures do not always return to their prior form. The rehab appearance is the first real data point in that evaluation.

What About Casey Mize?

The third member of Detroit's injured rotation remains on the IL without a confirmed return timeline. While Skubal and Verlander are actively working through rehab progressions, Mize's situation is less developed. The Tigers have not provided a specific target date for his return.

Even without Mize, two of the three injured starters returning by late June would represent a significant upgrade over what the Tigers have been throwing out there through the first two months of the season.

What's Next

Watch for Skubal's expected rehab start this Saturday — the results will set the timeline for his return to Detroit's active roster. Verlander's Toledo outing on Tuesday will be evaluated by team medical and coaching staff before any decision on his next step.

The AL Wild Card picture shifts noticeably if both pitchers return healthy by late June. Detroit has the roster to compete; what they have been missing is the pitching to hold games close and manufacture wins consistently. That picture changes when Skubal walks back out to a major-league mound. The countdown is on.
