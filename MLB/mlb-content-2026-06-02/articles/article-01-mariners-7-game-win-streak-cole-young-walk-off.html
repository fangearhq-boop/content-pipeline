Mariners Win Seven Straight — Cole Young's Walk-Off Caps Third Dramatic Finish in Four Days

By Marcus Cole | June 2, 2026

They keep finding ways to win. Monday night in Seattle, Cole Young's broken-bat single into left field in the bottom of the tenth inning scored Randy Arozarena with the winning run, giving the Mariners a 3–2 victory over the New York Mets. Seven consecutive wins. Three walk-offs in four days. Seattle is officially hot.

The Mariners improved to 32–29, their best record of the season and three games above .500 for the first time all year. After a slow start raised questions about whether this roster could deliver on its preseason expectations, the answer — over the past week-plus — has been a resounding yes.

How the Walk-Off Happened

The game was tied 2–2 heading into the bottom of the tenth when Arozarena reached third base on a stolen base. Young came up as the potential walk-off hitter in a situation that defined the Mariners' recent run: high-pressure, late-inning, everything on the line.

He broke his bat on the swing but still found left field with the ball. Arozarena jogged home. The Mariners erupted. It was the kind of moment that happens to teams in the middle of a roll — the baseball finds open grass no matter what.

Emerson Hancock set the tone from the start, handling six strong innings for Seattle. He allowed only two hits, both solo home runs to Jacob Young and Marcus Semien. The bullpen — José A. Ferrer, Matt Brash, and Andrés Muñoz — locked it down with three scoreless innings in relief.

Julio Rodriguez's Career Month

Behind the individual result sits a broader story about where this team is headed. Julio Rodriguez hit 10 home runs in May — the best single month of his career. Through the first few days of June, he has 12 on the season with a .263 batting average and a .319 on-base percentage.

After a 2025 season that raised questions about his development trajectory, Rodriguez has reasserted himself as one of the most dangerous center fielders in baseball. Ten home runs in a single month is not just a career best — it puts him in a category alongside the best performers in the AL for that span.

Young players delivering when they're supposed to is how franchises turn the corner. Rodriguez doing it, with the team in the middle of a win streak, is exactly the kind of signal that gives Mariners fans reason to believe this summer is going to be different.

The Walk-Off Factory

Three walk-off wins in four games is an extraordinary stretch of late-game execution. It requires everything to go right — clutch hitting, clean baserunning, a defense that does not give anything back, and bullpen arms that keep the game within reach. Seattle has delivered all of it.

The Mariners are not a team that wins every game in a blowout. They find ways to win games by one run in extra innings, which is both more stressful and more indicative of a club with genuine competitive character. You cannot manufacture walk-offs through roster construction alone. They happen when a team is playing with confidence and finds its best players in the best moments.

What's Next

The Mariners continue play Tuesday with a chance to extend their win streak. Heading into June with momentum, favorable matchups ahead, and a lineup producing clutch results at a remarkable rate, Seattle has positioned itself as a genuine factor in the AL West race.

The question now is not whether the Mariners can get hot. They clearly can. The question is whether they can sustain it. Seven games is a sample. What happens over the next 30 games tells the real story. Based on the last week, the answer might surprise a few people.
