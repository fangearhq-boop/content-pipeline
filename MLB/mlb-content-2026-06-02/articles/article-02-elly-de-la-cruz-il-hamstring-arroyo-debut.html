Elly De La Cruz to IL With Grade 1-2 Hamstring Strain — Reds Call Up Edwin Arroyo for MLB Debut

By Jake Torres | June 2, 2026

The Cincinnati Reds lost their most important player Monday when Elly De La Cruz was placed on the 10-day injured list with a right hamstring strain. An MRI confirmed what the crowd feared when they saw him pull up after reaching first base: a Grade 1-2 strain that carries a 2-to-4 week recovery timeline.

Cincinnati could not have picked a worse time to lose him. The Reds sit six games behind the Milwaukee Brewers in the NL Central, and De La Cruz had been one of the most productive players in the league heading into the injury.

A Season Interrupted

Before Monday, Elly De La Cruz was making a legitimate MVP case. His season numbers: .280 batting average, .855 OPS, 12 home runs, 37 RBI. Those are numbers that put a player in the top tier of NL position players, and they come with the added dimension of De La Cruz's baserunning and defensive abilities that no statistic fully captures.

He is the kind of player who makes everything harder for the opposition. His speed on the bases — the threat of a steal, the pressure he puts on catchers and pitchers alike — elevates every other batter in the lineup. When he is not in the lineup, the Reds are a different team.

The injury happened during a game against the Braves when De La Cruz reached first base and immediately signaled to his dugout. Manager Terry Francona confirmed the MRI result shortly after, and the 2-to-4 week estimate represents the best-case timeline if recovery proceeds without complication.

Edwin Arroyo Gets His Moment

In every injury story, there is a player who gets the opportunity he might have waited a year for. For the Reds, that player is Edwin Arroyo.

The top prospect was called up to make his MLB debut in the wake of De La Cruz's placement on the IL. Arroyo will start at second base, with Matt McLain sliding over to shortstop to cover De La Cruz's position. For the organization, it is a glimpse of the future arriving ahead of schedule — and for Arroyo, it is the moment every minor league player works toward.

Whether Arroyo can approximate any of what De La Cruz provides is a question that will take weeks to answer. The more realistic expectation is that he plays competent, steady baseball while De La Cruz heals — and that the Reds find a way to tread water in a division that was already pulling away.

The NL Central Picture

Milwaukee has been the class of the NL Central for most of 2026. A six-game lead is not insurmountable, but it is significant — especially with De La Cruz now out. The Reds need their best player to return healthy and quickly if they want to stay in the conversation through summer.

The Reds front office will monitor the hamstring closely, wary of the tendency for Grade 1-2 strains to recur if a player returns before the muscle is fully healed. A cautious approach in the next two weeks might mean a 3-to-4 week absence rather than two; rushing him back increases the risk of a longer setback.

What's Next

De La Cruz will begin his recovery with rest and physical therapy immediately. The Reds have a series against the Royals in Cincinnati to navigate this week without their star shortstop. Edwin Arroyo's debut will be watched closely, both as a performance indicator and as a scouting opportunity for a prospect many believe will be a key piece of Cincinnati's next contending window.

For De La Cruz, the goal is simple: get healthy, come back right. The season is long enough that his absence, if limited to two or three weeks, does not have to define the Reds' summer. But every game matters when you're trying to chase down a six-game deficit — and this team needs its best player on the field as soon as safely possible.
