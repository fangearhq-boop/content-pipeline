### STORY 3: Schwarber's Record Chase

**Long-Form Post**

Kyle Schwarber has 22 home runs. The season started in late March. Let that math work itself out for a second.

The Philadelphia Phillies slugger leads all of MLB and is on a pace — through roughly 55 games — that projects to 66 home runs for the full season. That number hasn't been reached by a clean player in modern baseball. The last players with more through 49 games were Mark McGwire in 1998 and Barry Bonds in 2001, who finished with 70 and 73, respectively.

Schwarber is the 18th player in MLB history with at least 20 home runs through a team's first 49 games. The last one was Josh Hamilton in 2012.

To put it plainly: what he is doing right now has almost never been done. And he's showing no signs of slowing down.

Does this end with 60 home runs? 65? More? We don't know. But it is the story of the MLB season, and right now, Kyle Schwarber owns it. 💥
