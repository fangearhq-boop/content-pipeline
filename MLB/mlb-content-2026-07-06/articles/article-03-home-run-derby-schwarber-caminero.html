Derby Field Taking Shape: Schwarber Is In, Judge Is Out, Caminero Is Ready

By Ryan Calloway

July 6, 2026

The 2026 Home Run Derby is one week away, and the field is almost set. Two names define the event's storyline heading into Monday, July 13, at Citizens Bank Park: one player who desperately wants to be there, and one who won't make it.

Schwarber: The Conditions Are Met

Kyle Schwarber laid out two conditions for participating in the Home Run Derby: he needed to make the All-Star Game, and he needed to be healthy. The first condition was just cleared — Schwarber was named to the 2026 NL All-Star roster. He leads all of Major League Baseball with 30 home runs. He plays for the Phillies, who are hosting the event at their own ballpark.

The second condition — health — is the remaining question. Schwarber has dealt with back issues this season. His participation is not yet official, but the barriers are now down to one. If his back cooperates over the next few days, Philadelphia is going to see its own first baseman in the event at Citizens Bank Park. That would be one of the better Derby storylines in recent memory.

Caminero: The Confirmed Returner

Junior Caminero of the Tampa Bay Rays is in. The 22-year-old third baseman announced his participation on Instagram and comes in as the field's known commodity — he was the runner-up in the 2025 Derby and has spent this season reminding everyone why. Caminero has 28 home runs on the year and hit seven in a single week in June. He is one of baseball's most electric young power hitters, and he will be competing on the biggest stage in mid-season baseball.

Judge Is Out

The most notable absence is Aaron Judge. The Yankees outfielder was placed on the injured list in June with a stress fracture in his right first rib. He will not be at the Derby, and his All-Star appearance remains uncertain. Judge is one of the game's most compelling power hitters and one of the most watched names in any Home Run Derby conversation. His absence is a real blow to the event's marquee factor.

Ben Rice, also of the Yankees, told reporters in June that he wants to participate. He has 22 home runs on the season. Whether he gets an invitation as the confirmed field comes together remains to be seen. Yordan Alvarez has opted out.

A New Format

The 2026 Derby uses a new format that changes the strategic calculus. Instead of a timed round, hitters get 20 swings in Round 1 and 15 swings each in Round 2 and the Final. The clock is gone. Swing count is the constraint now. That means pacing matters — a hitter who is selective and efficient has an advantage that the old timed format didn't reward in the same way.

For Caminero, who is built for sustained power output, the format could suit his style. For Schwarber, whose pull-side power at Citizens Bank Park is genuinely fearsome, home field may matter more than format.

What's Next

The 2026 Home Run Derby streams live on Netflix on Monday, July 13, at 8 p.m. ET from Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. Caminero is confirmed. Schwarber's health will determine whether the home crowd gets the moment it's waiting for. The full field announcement is expected in the days ahead. One week from now, the best power hitters in baseball take the cage at a park that was built for this.
