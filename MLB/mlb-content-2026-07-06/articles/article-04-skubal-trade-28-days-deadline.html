Twenty-Eight Days: The Tarik Skubal Trade Clock Is Running

By Marcus Cole

July 6, 2026

The 2026 MLB trade deadline is August 3. That is 28 days from today. And for the next four weeks, one name is going to appear in more trade columns, more league-source reports, and more front-office discussions than any other: Tarik Skubal.

The Number That Matters Most

ESPN's Jeff Passan and Kiley McDaniel, the sharpest baseball trade analysts in the business, have Skubal's trade probability at 85 percent. That figure is not a projection or a guess — it reflects the convergence of everything pointing toward a deal. The Detroit Tigers are 38-49. Skubal is a pending free agent worth approximately $32 million on a contending team's terms. He has won two Cy Young Awards. The math on keeping him in Detroit is very difficult to make.

When a team has a player of Skubal's caliber and is 11 games under .500 at the All-Star break, holding him is not a strategy. It is a choice to leave value on the table. The Tigers have given no indication they intend to do that.

The Braves Are the Frontrunner

Multiple reports, including from Bob Nightengale, have pointed to the Atlanta Braves as the frontrunner in the Skubal market. The case for Atlanta is straightforward: Spencer Strider is on the injured list, Bryce Elder is not pitching like a rotation anchor, and the Braves have a complete roster capable of a deep October run if they can stabilize their starting pitching.

Adding Skubal to a rotation that already includes Chris Sale — who earned his 10th All-Star selection this season — would give Atlanta a No. 1 and No. 2 that no other team in the National League could match. The Braves have the financial flexibility and the prospect capital to make the deal. They have the urgency. The fits are not hypothetical.

A Deep Market

The Braves are the frontrunner, but they are not alone. Reported interested parties include the Milwaukee Brewers, Chicago Cubs, Philadelphia Phillies, San Diego Padres, Tampa Bay Rays, and Boston Red Sox. Every team on that list would benefit from acquiring a pitcher of Skubal's caliber. The depth of the market works in Detroit's favor — more bidders means higher return.

The Tigers have leverage. Skubal is pitching at the peak of his value. The market understands what he is. Detroit's job between now and August 3 is to extract the best return available from whoever wants him most.

What's Next

The trade deadline clock runs for 28 more days. The Skubal conversation is going to accelerate as August approaches. Every Tigers start between now and the deadline is a data point that either strengthens or complicates the case. The Braves are the team to watch as frontrunners, but a deal of this size rarely goes from rumor to wire without surprises. August 3 is the deadline. Watch closely between now and then.
