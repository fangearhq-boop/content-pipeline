Bryce Harper Gets the Legend Pick: Full 2026 All-Star Rosters Are Out

By Marcus Cole

July 6, 2026

Commissioner Rob Manfred made a call. Bryce Harper is going to the All-Star Game.

Harper, who did not earn a selection through fan voting or player/manager balloting, was designated as the Commissioner's "Legend Pick" — a mechanism that allows Manfred to add one player to each league's roster. Harper's selection sends him to Citizens Bank Park for his ninth All-Star appearance. His reaction was exactly what you'd expect from him: "I deserve to be in the game."

He's probably right. And the story gets better: this is his home park. The Phillies are hosting the All-Star Game, which means Harper plays the Midsummer Classic in the stadium where he plays 81 regular-season games. Fans in Philadelphia are going to love it.

Three Teams Lead With Five All-Stars Each

The Atlanta Braves, Los Angeles Dodgers, and Philadelphia Phillies each placed five players on the 2026 All-Star rosters — tied for the most of any club. The Dodgers bring Shohei Ohtani (sixth career selection), Freddie Freeman (10th), Max Muncy, Andy Pages, and a fifth representative. The Braves send Chris Sale (also reaching his 10th career selection) and four others. The Phillies get five, including Harper via the Legend Pick, Brandon Marsh as the NL right field starter, Kyle Schwarber, Cristopher Sanchez, and reliever Jhoan Duran.

Two milestones intersect on the same night: Freeman and Sale both earning their 10th All-Star selections in the same game, at the same venue. That combination hasn't happened since — well, there may not be a comparable moment. Both are career long-haul players being honored for cumulative excellence.

Langeliers Starts, Kurtz Plays First

The most surprising element of the AL lineup is the starting catcher. Shea Langeliers of the Oakland Athletics — yes, the franchise still trying to establish itself in Sacramento — earned the AL starting catcher spot. He becomes the first Athletics catcher to start the All-Star Game since Terry Steinbach in 1988. Langeliers has 20 home runs and an .860 OPS this season. The vote validated what his numbers have been saying all year.

Nick Kurtz starts at first base for the AL, stepping in for Vladimir Guerrero Jr. Brandon Marsh starts in right field for the NL — a first-time All-Star doing it in front of the Philadelphia faithful.

Twenty-Six First-Timers

The depth of the 2026 All-Star class is measured in this number: 26 players making their first Midsummer Classic appearance. That reflects the extraordinary talent pipeline entering the game right now. Among the first-timers: Cam Schlittler (Yankees) earns the AL starting pitching assignment. Dillon Dingler (Tigers) — a catcher with 19 home runs and a 4.0 WAR — makes the AL roster. Jacob Misiorowski (Brewers) comes in leading all of MLB with a 1.47 ERA. The list reads like a Who's Who of baseball's next generation.

What's Next

The 2026 MLB Home Run Derby takes place Monday, July 13, at 8 p.m. ET on Netflix. The All-Star Game follows on Tuesday, July 14, at 8 p.m. ET on FOX at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. After that, the second half of the season begins — and with it, the August 3 trade deadline, playoff races, and the final push toward October. But first: Philly gets to host the biggest stage in mid-season baseball. And Bryce Harper gets to play the starring role in his own house.
