Home Run Derby Is Tomorrow — Two Phillies, Their Home Park, and Netflix's Big Night

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 12, 2026

Monday night at Citizens Bank Park belongs to the Home Run Derby, and for the first time in the event's history, the audience watching at home will be doing so on Netflix.

The 2026 T-Mobile Home Run Derby begins at 8:00 PM ET, streaming live on Netflix from Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. The field is set, the format has changed, and the two players with the loudest crowd behind them are both wearing Phillies red.

Philadelphia's Night

Bryce Harper and Kyle Schwarber are both in the eight-man field, and both will be competing in front of a home crowd at the park where they play every night. Harper won this event in 2018 as a member of the Washington Nationals — which means he won it here, at this park, before he was even a Phillie. Now he gets to try to do it again in a Phillies uniform with the city fully behind him.

Schwarber enters as the competitive favorite. He leads MLB in home runs this season and is built for an event where strength is the only variable that matters. The format change this year removes the shot clock — for the first time in a decade, batters can reset between swings without a timer running. Round 1 gives each participant 20 swings, with 15 apiece in rounds 2 and 3. That setup rewards pure raw power over swings-per-minute efficiency, and there is no one in this field with more of it than Schwarber.

The Rest of the Field

Harper and Schwarber are the headliners, but the bracket has legitimate threats throughout. Jac Caglianone of the Kansas City Royals was the runner-up in last year's Derby and has the power to win it again. Junior Caminero of the Tampa Bay Rays was the first confirmed participant for the 2026 field. Willson Contreras (Boston Red Sox) and Jordan Walker (St. Louis Cardinals) round out the field as proven bats who can put together a big round on any given night.

Munetaka Murakami of the Chicago White Sox brings his own layer of historical significance. He is just the second Japanese-born player to compete in the Home Run Derby, following Shohei Ohtani in 2021. Murakami entered the All-Star break as the White Sox's designated hitter on a first-place team — his participation in the Derby adds one more thread to what has been a remarkable All-Star Week for Chicago. Ben Rice of the New York Yankees completes the eight-man field.

The Netflix Debut

Monday night marks the first time the Home Run Derby has aired on Netflix. The streaming platform's entry into live sports broadcasting has been a gradual process, and the Derby is one of the higher-profile events in that ongoing expansion. For fans without a traditional cable subscription, this year's Derby is available through a Netflix login — no cable package, no blackout complications, no regional restrictions. The broadcast experience will be closely watched as an indicator of how streaming and live sports events can coexist at scale.

What's Next

The 2026 T-Mobile Home Run Derby airs Monday, July 13, at 8:00 PM ET, live on Netflix from Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. The MLB All-Star Game follows on Tuesday, July 14, at 7:30 PM ET on FOX. Regular-season play resumes Thursday, July 17, when all 30 teams return to action for the second half.
