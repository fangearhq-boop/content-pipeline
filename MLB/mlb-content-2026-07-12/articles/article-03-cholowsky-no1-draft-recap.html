White Sox Select Roch Cholowsky No. 1 Overall — The Third College Shortstop Ever to Go First

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 12, 2026

The pick is in. The Chicago White Sox selected UCLA shortstop Roch Cholowsky No. 1 overall in the 2026 MLB Draft on Saturday afternoon at the Pennsylvania Convention Center in Philadelphia.

Cholowsky's selection, announced at 12:43 PM CT, confirmed what the consensus had been projecting for most of the past year. He becomes just the third college shortstop ever taken with the No. 1 overall pick, joining Bill Almon (San Diego Padres, 1974) and Dansby Swanson (Arizona Diamondbacks, 2015) in an extremely short list.

What Cholowsky Brings

The numbers from his 2026 season at UCLA made the decision difficult to argue against. Cholowsky hit .320/.452/.636 with 21 home runs and 60 RBI — a combination of contact, plate discipline, and over-the-fence power that evaluators rate as rare at the college level. The slash line tells the story on its own: a .320 average paired with a .452 on-base percentage indicates someone who doesn't chase, walks when he should, and still hits for power when he swings.

MLB Pipeline's assessment placed him in elite historical company. The evaluation service called him "the best all-around college shortstop prospect since Troy Tulowitzki" and described him as "big league ready in a hurry." Tulowitzki, selected 7th overall in 2005, played 14 major league seasons, made five All-Star appearances, and won a Gold Glove. Putting Cholowsky in that conversation signals that the expectation is not just that he reaches the majors but that he contributes immediately when he does.

The Historical Company He Joins

College shortstops going No. 1 overall is genuinely rare. In the 60-year history of the MLB Draft, it has happened exactly three times now. Almon's 1974 selection came in the early years of the draft. Swanson, taken first in 2015, went on to win a World Series with Atlanta and has established himself as a reliable major league contributor. What all three have in common is that evaluators believed their floors were high enough and their ceilings were high enough to make the risk of picking first worthwhile.

Cholowsky's profile fits that pattern. College players — particularly college position players — arrive with more advanced skills and shorter development timelines than high school prospects. "Big league ready in a hurry" is not a guarantee, but it does suggest the White Sox may not be waiting until 2029 to see what they've got.

The White Sox Rebuild in Context

The White Sox's last No. 1 overall pick was Harold Baines in 1977 — a player who went on to a Hall of Fame career. The franchise finished 2024 with a 41-121 record, the worst single-season mark in modern MLB history. The losses were painful, but they produced the draft position. The White Sox enter the All-Star break at 45-40 and in first place in the AL Central — a turnaround that has been faster than almost anyone predicted. Adding the draft's consensus top prospect to a roster that is already competing accelerates the timeline further.

The rebuild has always had a finish line. With Cholowsky in the system and the White Sox in first place, that finish line looks closer than it did 18 months ago.

What's Next

Rounds 5 through 20 of the 2026 MLB Draft continue Sunday at the Pennsylvania Convention Center in Philadelphia. Cholowsky and the other top draft picks are expected to be introduced at the Home Run Derby on Monday before the All-Star Game on Tuesday. MLB teams typically sign their draft picks over the weeks following the draft, with Cholowsky expected to be assigned to the White Sox minor league system after signing.
