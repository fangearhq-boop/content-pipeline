Ohtani's Knee, Judge's Rib, and Skubal's Future: The Three Questions That Define the Second Half

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | July 12, 2026

The All-Star break is the natural pause where the questions are all still open. Heading into the second half of 2026, three stories are unresolved in ways that will shape the rest of the season.

Skubal: 22 Days to the Deadline

Tarik Skubal has 22 days left before the August 3 trade deadline, and the question is not whether he gets traded — it's what the Tigers get back for him.

Detroit is 37-49, six and a half games out of the Wild Card. The math on a playoff run is essentially closed. Skubal is the two-time reigning AL Cy Young Award winner and is in the final year of his contract before reaching free agency. He has publicly lobbied the Tigers to buy rather than sell — "if they don't think what we have is a World Series-caliber team, the whole team is going to look different," he told reporters in early July — but the record makes it difficult to argue that case.

The Yankees have emerged as the frontrunners to land him. Carlos Rodón and Max Fried are both on the injured list with elbow issues, leaving New York with a rotation in crisis at exactly the wrong moment in a tight AL East race. The Phillies are also linked, with the possibility of pairing Skubal alongside Cristopher Sánchez as a historically dominant one-two combination. The Dodgers, Cubs, and Brewers round out the reported market per ESPN.

Someone is going to pay a significant price before August 3. The Yankees, with two aces down and a playoff spot to protect, may have the greatest urgency.

Ohtani: The Knee Procedure

Shohei Ohtani was the leading vote-getter for the AL All-Star roster and will not be at the All-Star Game. He missed his final start of the first half — against the Arizona Diamondbacks — due to left knee irritation, and the Dodgers confirmed he had fluid removed from the knee and likely received an injection during the All-Star break.

The Dodgers' official position is that the procedure is not expected to affect Ohtani's second-half pitching availability. That is the message that matters for Los Angeles's playoff hopes — Ohtani as a two-way player changes their ceiling in the postseason in ways that Ohtani as a designated hitter alone does not. He remains available to DH during the break, which limits the concern to some degree. But "expected" and "confirmed" are different things, and the Dodgers' second-half picture is significantly different depending on when and whether Ohtani's knee is fully right.

Judge: The Rib Situation

Aaron Judge has been on the injured list since June 5 with a fractured right rib. His rib was being reimaged during the All-Star break — a standard midpoint evaluation, though not one that inspires confidence about the timeline.

Yankees GM Brian Cashman offered an update that landed as cautiously optimistic at best. "I don't think we're anticipating it's coming back clean," Cashman said. "I think we're anticipating and hopeful that it's showing the healing process." The best-case return remains August. Until Judge is back in the lineup, the Yankees cannot fully assess what they are — or whether the desperation to acquire Skubal is the only move that keeps their season alive.

The Yankees Problem Summarized

New York has three major variables that are all unresolved simultaneously: Rodón on the IL, Fried on the IL, and Judge on the IL. Any one of those is manageable. All three at once, with 22 days to the deadline and a Wild Card spot to protect, creates a level of urgency that will drive them to overpay for Skubal — or watch the window close.

What's Next

The MLB trade deadline is August 3 at 6:00 PM ET. Second-half play begins Thursday, July 17. Ohtani's pitching availability for the second half is expected to be confirmed in the coming days as the Dodgers provide updates on his knee procedure. Judge's rib reimaging results are expected this week; the Yankees have not set a return date. Follow trade deadline coverage at ESPN.com and MLB.com for the latest on the Skubal market and other major transactions.
