AL Central at the Break: White Sox Hold 1-Game Lead, Guardians Ready to Surge

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 12, 2026

Two years ago, the Chicago White Sox lost 121 games. They enter the 2026 All-Star break in first place in the AL Central.

At 45-40, Chicago holds a one-game lead over the Cleveland Guardians heading into the second half. The race is real, the margin is thin, and the White Sox's turnaround from the worst record in modern MLB history to a first-place team is one of the more compelling stories in baseball this season.

The Standings

The AL Central at the break looks like this: White Sox at 45-40, Guardians at 45-42, Twins at 42-46, Tigers at 37-49. Chicago leads by one game. Cleveland leads Minnesota by three and a half. The Tigers, six and a half games back, are sellers — Tarik Skubal's trade market will define their second half more than the standings will.

The gap between first and second is small enough that a single series can shift the division lead. The White Sox have played to exactly two games above .500 — they are not a dominant team running away from the field. They are a competitive team in a division where everyone else has their own limitations. That is a legitimate path to October if they can maintain it.

Why the White Sox Story Matters

The 2024 White Sox went 41-121. The rebuild was painful, deliberate, and unapologetic. They lost games to accumulate draft picks and prospect talent, stayed patient through the kind of losing that empties ballparks, and built toward a roster that could compete. The 2026 version of this franchise is in first place at the All-Star break with Munetaka Murakami — their designated hitter — competing in the Home Run Derby at Citizens Bank Park on Monday night.

The rebuild is not complete. Roch Cholowsky, their No. 1 overall pick from yesterday's draft, is not in Guaranteed Rate Field yet. There are gaps in the roster that will take time to fill. But 45-40 and in first place is not a mirage. It is a team that has figured out how to win games while still adding pieces for the future. That combination is rare.

The Guardians Aren't Going Away

Cleveland is one game back and has been one of baseball's best-kept secrets all season. The Guardians play efficient, well-managed baseball — they limit mistakes, use their bullpen intelligently, and grind out wins in games they have no business winning. They have been at or near the top of the AL Central all year without generating the national attention that a New York or Los Angeles team would command in the same position.

The second half of the AL Central race will be determined in head-to-head games between Chicago and Cleveland. Both teams know it. Both teams are built for a grind. This division will not be decided until late September.

Elsewhere: Wetherholt Locks In With St. Louis

On the transaction front, the St. Louis Cardinals made a significant commitment on Thursday when they signed second baseman JJ Wetherholt to an eight-year, $112.5 million extension. The deal was announced July 10, with the timing of All-Star Week providing maximum visibility for a franchise signaling that it is building rather than selling.

Wetherholt is one of the more promising young bats in the National League. The Cardinals locking him in through his prime years is a franchise-building move that stands in contrast to teams like Detroit, which are preparing to move assets at the deadline. St. Louis is choosing the longer arc.

What's Next

Second-half play begins Thursday, July 17, for all 30 teams. The White Sox and Guardians will meet in head-to-head series throughout the second half — those games will define the AL Central title race. The Twins, at three and a half games back, remain in the picture. The MLB trade deadline is August 3. Follow standings updates and second-half coverage at MLB.com.
