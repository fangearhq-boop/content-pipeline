Dodgers Take Game 1 Over Phillies, 4-2 — MLB's Best Series Continues Tonight

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter

The NL's best series got off to the start the Dodgers wanted. Los Angeles beat Philadelphia 4-2 on Friday night at Dodger Stadium, taking Game 1 and extending a winning streak to five games. Game 2 is tonight at 7:10 PM PT.

The Dodgers entered this series at 37-20, the best record in the National League. The Phillies came in at 29-27 — a team that has played well enough on the road (15-11) to give any opponent trouble. Friday night, it wasn't enough.

Starter Jack Wrobleski went for Los Angeles while Zack Wheeler took the ball for Philadelphia. The result: Dodgers by two. The details of how they got there matter less than what it sets up for Saturday night.

The Game 2 Pitching Matchup

Roki Sasaki gets the ball tonight for the Dodgers, and his recent form makes this compelling. His overall ERA sits at 4.93 — not the number anyone in Los Angeles envisioned when he signed. But over his last four starts, Sasaki has posted a 3.52 ERA. Something is clicking. The electric stuff that made him a coveted international signing appears to be finding its footing.

On the other side, Andrew Painter takes the mound for Philadelphia. Painter is 1-5 with a 5.40 ERA — numbers that tell the story of a young pitcher working through the inevitable bumps of establishing himself at the major league level. The talent is not in question. The results have been inconsistent.

It's a contrast that makes for interesting theater: a pitcher trending up versus a pitcher still searching for his level. At Dodger Stadium, on Saturday night, with a series on the line.

The Ohtani-Sánchez Subplot

Here's the wrinkle that adds a layer to this whole series: Shohei Ohtani pitched on Wednesday, the day before this series started. Ranger Suárez — who has thrown 44 and two-thirds consecutive scoreless innings, one of the most remarkable stretches of pitching in recent memory — is scheduled to pitch Monday, the day after the series ends.

Two of the most dominant arms in baseball this season will have appeared in the same series without facing each other. Ohtani just missed the opener. Suárez misses the finale by one day in the other direction. Sometimes baseball constructs its own dramatic irony.

It also means: this series will end without that showdown. Which makes a future meeting all the more anticipated.

What This Series Means

The Dodgers' five-game winning streak coming into this series speaks to where they are right now — not just talented, but locked in. A series win over the Phillies, a team with legitimate playoff aspirations, would be a statement heading into June.

For Philadelphia, dropping Game 1 isn't fatal. Their 15-11 road record suggests they know how to win away from Citizens Bank Park. But coming back in a series against the NL's best team, in their home park, requires a big performance from Painter tonight.

Game 2. Tonight. 7:10 PM PT. The NL's marquee series is still being decided.
