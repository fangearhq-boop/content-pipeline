First Career Multi-HR Game: J.P. Crawford Powers Mariners Past D-Backs 7-6 in 10

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter

Nine seasons in the big leagues. More than 800 career games. And J.P. Crawford had never hit two home runs in the same game.

Friday night, that changed.

Crawford went deep twice off Zac Gallen — a solo home run in the first inning and a two-run blast in the fifth — to fuel Seattle's offense in a game they ultimately won 7-6 over Arizona in 10 innings. Randy Arozarena provided the finish, lining a walk-off double off Juan Morillo in the tenth.

It was the Mariners' fourth straight win, and it returned them to .500 — 29-29 — for the first time since April 29.

The Career-First Milestone

Crawford is 31 years old. He was drafted in 2013, made his MLB debut in 2017, and has been a fixture in the Mariners' lineup for years. He's known primarily as a defensive player — a shortstop with sure hands and a reliable arm — whose bat has grown progressively more useful over time.

But no multi-homer game. Not once, until Friday.

His first shot off Gallen, a solo blast leading off the first inning, set the tone early. The second, a two-run HR in the fifth, pushed Seattle's lead. Crawford finished the night with his career-high ninth home run of the season.

Gallen, the Arizona ace, absorbing that kind of damage from a player not usually associated with power output is itself a notable development. Crawford is becoming a more complete offensive player, and a nine-HR pace through late May is the evidence.

Arozarena's Walk-Off

The game went to extras tied, and Seattle needed a hero in the tenth. Arozarena stepped up against Juan Morillo and delivered — a double into the gap that ended it and sent the Mariners' dugout into celebration mode.

Arozarena has been one of the better free-agent stories of the offseason, and moments like this are why Seattle wanted him. The speed, the contact, the tendency to come through when games are on the line — it all showed up in the tenth inning of a back-and-forth affair.

Back to .500, Back in the Conversation

The Mariners have been grinding through an inconsistent season. Getting back to .500 on April 29 and then sliding back below it was a deflating stretch. But four straight wins — capped by Crawford's career night and a walk-off — changes the feel of things.

Seattle at 29-29 is a team that still has time to make the AL West a real competition. The division won't wait, but the Mariners appear to be finding their rhythm at the right moment. Friday was the kind of night that teams point to later when they talk about when things started to turn around.

One of those nights. For Crawford, it was also a first.
