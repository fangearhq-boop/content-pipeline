Cardinals' Velazquez Launches 3-Run HR in 6-5 Win Over Cubs — NL Central Gets Interesting

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter

The Milwaukee Brewers lead the NL Central at 33-20. That part isn't changing anytime soon. But the St. Louis Cardinals are doing something important: they're winning.

Friday night, the Cardinals beat the Cubs 6-5 behind a three-run home run from Nelson Velazquez, plus solo shots from Iván Herrera and Thomas Saggese. Three different Cardinals contributed home runs. The Cubs couldn't hold on. St. Louis stays in the race.

Velazquez as the Difference-Maker

Velazquez's three-run blast was the kind of swing that changes a game's texture. The Cardinals outfield had a night, and Velazquez led the way. He's been one of the more interesting players in St. Louis's lineup this season — a player who can change outcomes with one swing, and on Friday, he did exactly that.

Herrera and Saggese added solo home runs to round out the Cardinals' offensive production. Three different players going deep in a 6-5 win is a balanced attack, the kind that suggests an offense clicking rather than one relying on a single star to carry the load.

The Cubs' Struggles

Chicago came into this series with its own playoff aspirations, and dropping Game 1 to St. Louis is not how those ambitions advance. The Cubs' 2026 season has been one of potential not yet fully realized — they have the pieces, but the consistency that separates contenders from the rest of the pack has been elusive.

Game 2 of this series is today. The Cubs need a response.

The NL Central Picture

Milwaukee's 33-20 record gives the Brewers a comfortable cushion at the top of the NL Central. But "comfortable" is different from "insurmountable." The Cardinals, by continuing to win games like this one, ensure the division race has competitive life heading into June.

June is when these races take shape. Teams that enter the month with momentum and a reasonable gap to close have shown, year after year, that divisions can be turned upside down between June 1 and August 1. The Cardinals aren't conceding anything.

For now, St. Louis takes Game 1. The NL Central conversation continues.
