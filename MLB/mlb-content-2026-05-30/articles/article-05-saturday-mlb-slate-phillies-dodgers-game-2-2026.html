Saturday MLB: Phillies-Dodgers Game 2, Cubs-Cardinals, and More

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter

Saturday's MLB schedule gives you everything you could want from a weekend of baseball: the sport's best current series continuing, a division rivalry with real stakes, and a milestone chase that could happen at any moment.

Here's what to watch.

Phillies at Dodgers, Game 2 — 7:10 PM PT

The week's marquee series picks up at Dodger Stadium tonight. Los Angeles took Game 1 on Friday, 4-2, extending their winning streak to five. The Phillies head into Game 2 needing to answer.

The pitching matchup: Roki Sasaki for the Dodgers, who has a 3.52 ERA over his last four starts after a slow overall season start. Andrew Painter for Philadelphia — 1-5, 5.40 ERA — a young arm working through his first significant stretch of major league struggles.

This is one of the better games of the weekend, regardless of sport. The Dodgers are the best team in the NL. The Phillies are hot enough to be a genuine threat. Game 2 in a short series, with LA up 1-0, is exactly the kind of pressure situation that defines what October contenders are made of. First pitch at 7:10 PM PT.

Cubs at Cardinals, Game 2

St. Louis took Game 1 6-5 on Friday behind Nelson Velazquez's three-run home run and solo shots from Iván Herrera and Thomas Saggese. The Cubs need a response.

The NL Central context: Milwaukee leads at 33-20, but the Cardinals are making sure this race doesn't become a formality. A Cardinals series win here would be a meaningful statement for St. Louis's June outlook. For the Cubs, falling behind two games to a divisional rival on their home schedule would be a step backward they can't afford.

The Ramírez Watch

Keep the Cleveland Guardians game in your peripheral vision today. José Ramírez is approaching home run No. 300 in his career — one of the most decorated offensive players of his generation sitting on the doorstep of a round number milestone.

Ramírez doesn't chase stats, but his sheer consistency over a long career means milestones arrive whether he's thinking about them or not. Watch the Guardians game. It could happen at any moment.

The Full Picture

Saturday's slate is exactly what June-approaching baseball should look like. Division races tightening. Marquee matchups in the evening. A milestone chase adding a subplot to an otherwise ordinary regular season afternoon.

The best game on the board is tonight in Los Angeles. But the afternoon has plenty to offer before that first pitch.
