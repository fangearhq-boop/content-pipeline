Yankees Roll Past Oakland 8-2 — AL East Race Shaping Up as a Big One

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter

New York made it look routine. Yankees 8, Oakland 2 on Friday night, and the Yankees enter Saturday at 36-22 — a team built to chase down a division lead, doing exactly that.

Tampa Bay still holds the AL East lead. But the Yankees are right there, and the race heading into summer is shaping up to be one of the better storylines in the American League.

The Rotation Anchor

Any conversation about the Yankees this season runs through Cam Schlittler. His numbers are remarkable: 7-2, 1.50 ERA. In a league where a sub-3.00 ERA earns you Cy Young consideration, Schlittler is lapping that standard.

The 1.50 ERA sits among the best in the American League — possibly in all of baseball. His 7-2 record reflects run support and a winning team behind him, but the ERA is a number Schlittler earns every fifth day regardless of what his teammates do. You don't pitch to a 1.50 ERA through this point in the season by accident.

The Cy Young conversation is real, and it should be. Schlittler has given the Yankees exactly what they needed when they needed it — a legitimate ace to build around.

Chasing Tampa

The AL East is not a two-team race yet, but the Yankees and Rays are positioning themselves as the class of the division. Tampa Bay's lead is real, but a 36-22 New York team with Schlittler at the front of the rotation is built to make up ground.

June will tell the story. The Yankees have the lineup to score runs, and with Schlittler pitching every fifth day, they have a guaranteed impact start built into their rotation. For a team chasing a lead, that consistency matters more than almost anything else.

Friday's 8-2 win over Oakland was efficient and convincing. The Yankees didn't need anything dramatic — they just won cleanly. That's what good teams do.

What June Holds

The AL East race is the kind of storyline that builds across a summer. There's no decisive moment in late May — it's all accumulation. Each win inches a team closer or falls back in the rearview mirror.

At 36-22, the Yankees are clearly in this. Tampa leads, but not by a margin that makes catching them an unreasonable task. Schlittler, the lineup, and a team that just beat Oakland by six runs heading into Saturday all point toward a New York team with plenty left to say about this division.

The East race is on. The Yankees aren't going away.
