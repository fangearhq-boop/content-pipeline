Jose Ramirez Sets Guardians All-Time Games-Played Record — Now Chasing the 300/300 Club

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | April 9, 2026

There was no ceremony. No pause in the game. No grand announcement over the public address system. On April 6, Jose Ramirez appeared in his 1,529th game as a Cleveland Guardian, surpassing the franchise's all-time record for games played. The man who held it before him gave way quietly to the man who has spent every day of his major league career in the same uniform.

That is the most distinctive part of what Ramirez accomplished: he has never played for anyone else. In an era when franchise loyalty is frequently a casualty of free agency, arbitration, and trade demands, Ramirez has simply shown up to play baseball in Cleveland, year after year, and become the most durable player the organization has ever produced.

What Comes Next: The 300/300 Club

The games-played record is remarkable on its own. The chase that follows it may be even more significant.

Ramirez enters the 2026 season with 285 career home runs and 288 career stolen bases. To join the 300 HR/300 SB club, he needs 15 more home runs and 12 more stolen bases. The math is straightforward. The accomplishment, if he reaches it, would place him in company that has been earned by only eight players in the history of Major League Baseball: Bobby Bonds, Barry Bonds, Steve Finley, Carlos Beltran, Reggie Sanders, Jeff Bagwell, Larry Walker, and Alex Rodriguez.

Ramirez would become the ninth.

At 33 years old and showing no signs of physical decline — Cleveland is 8-5 and leading the AL Central, with Ramirez at the center of the lineup as always — the production and athleticism required to reach both milestones appears to be there. The question is simply whether he stays healthy and keeps producing at the level he has sustained for the better part of a decade.

Other Milestones Stacking Up

The 300/300 chase is the headline, but it is not the only number Ramirez is approaching. He enters the season with 399 career doubles — one away from 400. He also has 951 career RBIs, closing in on the 1,000 mark that would give him another round number to celebrate in what is already shaping up as a milestone-heavy season.

These are not manufactured milestones — the kind of inflated counting stats that accumulate when a player stays in the league past his prime. Ramirez's career doubles total reflects elite contact production maintained over an entire career. His RBI total reflects a player who has consistently hit in situations where runs come in. And the stolen base number is perhaps the most unusual of all — 288 career steals for a third baseman with significant power is a combination that does not appear in the record books alongside many players.

The Quiet Legend Angle

Baseball has its share of famous franchise cornerstones — players who spent their careers in one city and became synonymous with the team's identity. Ramirez belongs in that conversation, even if he has not always attracted the same national attention as some of his peers. He has never demanded a trade. He has not spent time chasing a larger market or a wealthier contract in another city. He signed extensions, played games, and produced at an All-Star level every season.

The franchise record for games played is a measure of that commitment expressed in pure numbers. It takes years of availability, durability, and consistent performance to appear in more games than anyone in a franchise's history. Ramirez has done that without fanfare, and now he is closing in on one of the sport's rarest statistical achievements.

What's Next

The Guardians are in the middle of an AL Central race that has them in first place at 8-5, and Ramirez remains the engine of the lineup. His 400th double could come at any point in April, possibly within the next few games. The 300/300 milestone, if he sustains his production, could be achieved before the end of the season. Watch the stolen base total closely — getting to 12 steals in a season is realistic for Ramirez at this stage of his career, and the home run number at 15 is well within his range.
