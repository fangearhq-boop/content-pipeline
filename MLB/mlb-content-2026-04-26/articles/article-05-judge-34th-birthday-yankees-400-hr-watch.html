Aaron Judge Turns 34 — 9 HRs, Yankees Lead AL East, and 400 Career HRs Are in Sight

By Jake Torres | April 26, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Aaron Judge wakes up on April 26, 2026, as a 34-year-old man. He also wakes up as the most feared hitter in the American League, nine home runs into a season that could push him past one of baseball's most storied milestones.

Through 22 games, Judge has nine home runs and the New York Yankees are 16-9, leading the AL East. At his current early-season pace — a rate that no hitter sustains for a full 162 games, but one that illustrates how well he is hitting — Judge would reach somewhere in the 65-home run range. What matters more than the pace extrapolation is the number he is quietly chasing: 400 career home runs.

The 400 Watch

Judge has approximately 377 career home runs heading into Sunday's game. He needs 23 more to reach 400 — a milestone that only 57 players in the history of the sport have achieved. At his current 2026 pace of nine HRs in 22 games, reaching 400 in the late June or July window is plausible. A more conservative projection — say, 40-50 home runs over the full season — still gets him there by September, with several years of prime production potentially remaining.

The significance of 400 is contextual. In the steroid era, the number was reached by many players whose totals were later asterisked in the minds of fans. In the modern era, with more stringent testing and different physical profiles, 400 is a rare achievement. Judge, at 34 and hitting at this level, is a legitimate Hall of Fame argument even if he stops today. Reaching 400 adds another layer to the resume that is already extraordinary.

His age-34 season matters because several of the game's great power hitters showed continued production well into their mid-30s. Mickey Mantle, Babe Ruth, and more recently players like Jim Thome produced significant power numbers at 34 and beyond. There is nothing in Judge's physical profile or durability record suggesting an imminent decline. He has been among the most careful and conditioned athletes in the sport.

The Yankees at 16-9

New York is 16-9 after a strong April. The AL East remains competitive — the Yankees lead Tampa Bay by 2.5 games — but the Bombers have established themselves as the class of the conference so far. The rotation has been a strength, with Max Fried continuing to provide the stability the Yankees acquired him for. The lineup features a depth that makes pitching around Judge difficult.

For the Yankees, the best-case scenario in 2026 is a deep playoff run that happens to coincide with Judge reaching 400 home runs somewhere memorable — Yankee Stadium, in October, in a moment befitting the franchise. That possibility is not far-fetched. It is, in fact, quite within the range of what this team and this player are capable of in the months ahead.

What's Next

The Yankees continue their AL East schedule this week, with Judge chasing 400 career home runs through every game. The milestone could arrive as early as mid-June at his current pace, or as late as September in a more conservative scenario. Either way, it is coming — and it will be one of the signature moments of the 2026 MLB season. Today, on his 34th birthday, Judge is healthy, leading his team, and closer to history than he has ever been.
