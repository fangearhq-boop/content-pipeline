Elly De La Cruz Steals Home for 2nd Career Time, Reds Win 2-1 Over Rangers

By Marcus Cole | April 26, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The steal of home is among the rarest plays in baseball. The courage, speed, and timing required to pull it off at the major league level make it an event when it happens. Elly De La Cruz has now done it twice.

The Cincinnati Reds shortstop stole home Sunday against the Texas Rangers, giving the Reds a critical run in a 2-1 victory. It was the second steal of home of De La Cruz's MLB career — the first came July 8, 2023, against the Milwaukee Brewers. He finished the day 2-for-5 with an RBI and a run scored as Cincinnati stayed tied for first in the NL Central at 16-9.

The Company He Keeps

De La Cruz is now the fifth Cincinnati Reds player since the stat was first tracked in 1974 to steal home twice in their career — joining Joe Morgan, Dan Driessen, Gary Redus, and Spencer Steer in that company. Morgan, a Hall of Famer and one of the greatest second basemen in the history of the sport, is the headliner of that group. Being mentioned alongside him in a Reds baserunning historical context is not a trivial distinction.

De La Cruz is 23 years old. The fact that he has already stolen home twice — a feat most players never accomplish once — is an indicator of elite athleticism and an aggressive, instinct-driven approach on the bases that few players in the sport possess. His combination of home run power (eight HRs through late April) and stolen base ability makes him one of the most dangerous players in the game to have on the basepaths.

The steal of home Sunday was not a fluke or a wild pitch. It was De La Cruz reading a situation, trusting his speed, and executing. That is the difference between a player with raw athleticism and a player who has developed the baseball instincts to weaponize it.

Reds in the Race

The 2-1 win over Texas keeps Cincinnati tied with the Cubs at 16-9, the two best records in the NL Central. The Reds' rotation — built around Hunter Greene and Andrew Abbott — has been one of the pleasant surprises of the early season, and the offense features enough speed and power to put pressure on opposing teams in multiple ways.

De La Cruz is the engine. His on-base ability, his home run production, and his steals — including one straight from the pitcher's windup to home plate — give the Reds a dimension that most teams in the National League cannot match. A team led by De La Cruz is a team that can manufacture runs when the home runs aren't falling, and can change a game in a single play regardless of the score.

Sunday was a reminder of what Cincinnati has at shortstop. The NL Central is going to be a long-season battle, and Elly De La Cruz is exactly the player you want fighting that fight with you.

What's Next

The Reds continue their schedule this week with several NL Central matchups that will further shape the division race. De La Cruz heads into May on a strong pace — his combination of home run production and stolen base numbers mirrors the historic season that made him a household name in 2025. For context on his future trajectory: he became one of only five players in MLB history to hit 20 home runs and steal 60 bases in a single season in 2025. If 2026 follows that path, the Reds have a generational player locked in for the long haul. Watch every De La Cruz at-bat — and every time he reaches base — with full attention.
