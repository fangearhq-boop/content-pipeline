Chicago Cubs Beat the Defending Champions for 10th Straight Win — The Best Streak in Baseball Right Now

By Marcus Cole | April 26, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

There are win streaks, and then there are statements. What the Chicago Cubs have been doing in April 2026 is both.

The Cubs rallied from four runs down to beat the Los Angeles Dodgers 6-4 on Friday night at Dodger Stadium, claiming their 10th consecutive victory — the longest active win streak in Major League Baseball. Dansby Swanson delivered yet another clutch moment for a Chicago team that simply refuses to lose. The Cubs are 16-9, tied for first in the NL Central with the Cincinnati Reds, and heading into Sunday's finale at Dodger Stadium with all the momentum in the series.

This is not a soft-schedule streak. The Cubs are beating the two-time defending World Series champions on their home turf.

The Numbers That Define This Run

During the eight-game portion of this win streak, the Cubs have outscored opponents 58-20 while posting a 2.22 team ERA. The offense has hit .318/.399/.514 as a unit with 7.3 runs scored per game. These are not numbers that look like a lucky run. They look like a very good team operating at full capacity.

The significance of Friday's win over the Dodgers is hard to overstate. Chicago came to Los Angeles carrying a nine-game streak — their longest run since the 2016 championship season — and faced a Dodgers team that has won consecutive World Series titles and owns the best record in baseball. The Cubs trailed 4-0 heading into the late innings behind a season-best outing from Emmet Sheehan. Then they took the game over.

Swanson has been a recurring theme throughout this stretch. The Cubs shortstop has authored several of the biggest at-bats of the run — a pattern that speaks to his value beyond the counting stats. He is the kind of player who tends to make his mark when the game is on the line.

The Cubs' win on Friday also marked one of the most historically significant portions of the streak: a run that, per Bleacher Nation, hadn't been replicated in nearly 100 years for this franchise. That context places this April in company the Cubs haven't been in since well before any current roster member was born.

Measuring Stick Passed

Every team with a win streak eventually faces a measuring stick. The Cubs' was this series in Los Angeles. Beating the Dodgers in a hostile environment, rallying from a four-run deficit, validating the streak against the best team in the sport — Chicago passed.

The Cubs are 16-9 through April 26. The NL Central is historically competitive — all five teams are over .500 — and the race is tight. Cincinnati, tied with Chicago at the top, is not going anywhere. The Cardinals and Pirates are within striking distance. Chicago does not have the luxury of coasting.

But right now, in late April, the Cubs are playing better baseball than anyone. That matters. Teams that play this way in April often find themselves in October.

What's Next

Game 3 of the Cubs-Dodgers series is tonight, Sunday April 26, at 8:10 PM PT at Dodger Stadium. A Chicago win would complete the series win at Dodger Stadium — one of the loudest statements any team can make at this stage of the season. The Cubs return home after Los Angeles for a stretch that could further solidify their position atop the NL Central. For the Dodgers, Sunday is a chance to respond to a bullpen performance that cost them Friday's game. Expect both teams to bring everything they have in this finale.
