All 5 NL Central Teams Are Over .500 — The First Time in Divisional Era History

By Ryan Calloway | April 26, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The National League Central has done something that has never been done before in the history of modern baseball.

Through Sunday, April 26, every team in the NL Central is over .500. The Cincinnati Reds and Chicago Cubs are tied for first at 16-9. The St. Louis Cardinals are 14-10. The Pittsburgh Pirates are 14-11. The Milwaukee Brewers are 13-11. Combined record: 73-48. This is the first time since the Divisional Era began in 1969 that all five NL Central franchises have been over .500 this deep into an April.

In more than half a century of baseball, this division has never collectively looked like this in the season's opening month.

Top to Bottom

What makes this remarkable is not just that two teams are good — there are always strong teams in competitive divisions. What makes it historic is the depth. The Milwaukee Brewers, who lost several regulars to the injured list earlier this month and were projected by multiple systems to finish below .500, are still 13-11. They have absorbed significant roster disruption and remain above break-even.

The Pittsburgh Pirates at 14-11 is genuinely unexpected. Pittsburgh has been in active rebuild mode for several years, and while the fanbase has watched promising young players develop, the expectation was not a 14-win April. Konnor Griffin, the franchise's top prospect, made his MLB debut earlier this month. The Pirates have simply been playing better baseball than anyone anticipated.

The Cardinals at 14-10 reflect a St. Louis team that added productive pieces in the offseason and has their veteran core contributing. The Cardinals are built to win — that has never been in question — but being 14-10 in a division as strong as this one requires execution at a high level.

At the top, the Reds and Cubs are engaged in a genuine two-team race for the division lead. Cincinnati has gotten strong rotation work from Hunter Greene and Andrew Abbott while the offense produces runs in bunches. Chicago is riding a 10-game win streak. Both teams are as good as their records suggest.

The Best Division in Baseball

On any given night through late April, every NL Central game has been a competitive contest between teams that can beat anyone. That context makes the division must-watch baseball for the rest of the season. There is no easy series. There is no assumed outcome. Every team in this division can — and has — beaten division leaders from other groups.

The interplay between five competitive teams over the course of 162 games is going to be one of the most compelling storylines of the 2026 season. By September, multiple teams in this division will have a legitimate playoff argument. The race could go down to the final weekend.

What's Next

The NL Central race is just beginning. The Cubs and Reds each have significant series coming up against division opponents in May. The Cardinals, Pirates, and Brewers have ample time to build on their strong starts. For the first time since the sport's modern era, this division has no clear bottom. Every fan base in the NL Central has genuine reason to believe — and that is a remarkable thing to say about any division in baseball in late April.
