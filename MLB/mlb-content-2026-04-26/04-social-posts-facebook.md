## STORY 3: NL Central Historic

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET
The NL Central just made history — and nobody in baseball is talking about it enough.

Every single team in the division is over .500 through late April 2026. The Cincinnati Reds and Chicago Cubs are each 16-9. The St. Louis Cardinals are 14-10. The Pittsburgh Pirates are 14-11. The Milwaukee Brewers are 13-11. Combined record: 73-48.

This is the first time in Divisional Era history — going back to 1969 — that all five NL Central teams have been over .500 this far into a season. This division is producing elite baseball across the board, from last to first. There are no easy games in the NL Central right now.

The Cubs and Reds are chasing each other for the top of the division. The Cardinals are right there. The Pirates — a team many had penciled in for last place — are four games above .500. The Brewers, with several regulars on the IL earlier this month, are still above break-even.

This is the most balanced and competitive division in baseball. And it's not close.

## STORY 4: Elly De La Cruz Steals Home

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET
Elly De La Cruz stole home today.

The Reds shortstop pulled off one of baseball's rarest plays in Cincinnati's 2-1 victory over the Texas Rangers on Sunday — becoming only the second player in the last three seasons to steal home. It was the second steal of home in De La Cruz's MLB career, with the first coming on July 8, 2023 against the Brewers.

De La Cruz is now the fifth Reds player since the stat was first tracked in 1974 to steal home twice in their career. He joins Joe Morgan, Dan Driessen, Gary Redus, and Spencer Steer in that company.

He finished the day 2-for-5 with an RBI and a run scored as the Reds won 2-1 and stayed tied for first in the NL Central at 16-9. De La Cruz is 23 years old.

The ability to steal home at the highest level requires a perfect read, pure speed, and absolute fearlessness. Elly has all three.

## STORY 5: Judge Birthday / Yankees

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET
Aaron Judge turns 34 today. His 2026 season so far is the perfect birthday present — for himself and for Yankee fans.

Nine home runs in 22 games. The Yankees are 16-9 and leading the AL East. At his current early-season pace, Judge is on track for somewhere in the 65-home run range over a full season — though sustained paces like that are extraordinarily rare. What matters more is the milestone lurking ahead.

Judge has approximately 377 career home runs heading into Sunday's game. He needs 23 more to reach 400. At his current 2026 pace, he's looking at late June or July as the potential window. That would make Judge the 58th player in MLB history to reach 400 home runs — and at 34, still one of the youngest in recent decades to get there.

New York is 16-9 and in first place. Judge is healthy. The 400 watch is officially open.

Happy birthday, Big Fella.

The Dodgers had one of their best starting performances of the season on Friday night.
