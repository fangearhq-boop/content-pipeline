Derby in 5 Days: Schwarber Has 31 Home Runs and Citizens Bank Park in His Sights

By Ryan Calloway | July 8, 2026

Kyle Schwarber hit his 31st home run of the season last night. It landed in the seats at Citizens Bank Park.

The Home Run Derby is at Citizens Bank Park in five days.

Baseball is really good at setting up its own narrative, and the 2026 T-Mobile Home Run Derby is not leaving anything to chance. The July 13 event — streaming live on Netflix at 8 PM ET from Philly — is shaping up to be one of the most compelling Derby editions in recent years, and the storylines are already writing themselves.

The Confirmed Field

Two participants are officially confirmed: Junior Caminero of the Tampa Bay Rays and Ben Rice of the New York Yankees. Caminero enters as the 2025 Derby runner-up, bringing the experience and the power that make him a legitimate threat to go all the way this time. Rice is a first-time All-Star in his third Major League season — and a first-time Derby participant, which carries its own kind of unpredictability.

Aaron Judge, who would have been a frontrunner, is out with a rib fracture. His absence creates an opening for someone else to claim the Derby's biggest stage.

Schwarber, leading MLB with 31 home runs and demonstrating his current form at Citizens Bank Park on Tuesday night, is the obvious headliner if his health holds. He was listed as conditional coming into the week, but Tuesday's performance suggests he's locked in.

New Format, New Drama

After years of the timer-based format — where hitters got a set number of minutes per round to slug as many balls as possible — MLB is switching to a swing-based system in 2026. Each participant gets a specific number of swings per round, not a time limit. The change alters the strategy and removes some of the manufactured late-round drama from the countdown, replacing it with more straightforward at-bat tension.

It also levels the playing field slightly for hitters who might tire under the previous format's pace demands. Under the swing format, every cut matters, and efficiency — hitting home runs early in your allotment rather than swinging and missing under time pressure — becomes a different kind of skill.

The Netflix Angle

This is the first Home Run Derby to stream exclusively on Netflix, part of MLB's expanding partnership with the platform. The Home Run Derby historically draws its largest audiences through television, and the Netflix format will test whether the streaming-only model can replicate those numbers. For international audiences, the streaming approach may actually expand the Derby's reach beyond its traditional broadcast footprint.

What's Next

The 2026 T-Mobile Home Run Derby airs July 13 at 8 PM ET on Netflix, streaming live from Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. The All-Star Game follows the next night, July 14, at 8 PM ET on FOX. The full Derby field will be announced before the event. If Schwarber participates — with 31 home runs and Citizens Bank Park literally being his home stadium — the narrative could not be more perfectly constructed.
