Wheeler's 14-K Masterpiece at the All-Star Site: The Snub That Became a Statement

By Jake Torres | July 8, 2026

If you're going to make a statement about being left off the All-Star roster, Zack Wheeler found the most poetic possible venue.

On Tuesday night at Citizens Bank Park — the stadium hosting the 2026 MLB All-Star Game in exactly one week — Wheeler tied his career high with 14 strikeouts across seven innings. The Philadelphia Phillies beat the Cincinnati Reds 4-1. Wheeler threw 104 pitches, allowed four hits and one earned run, and did not walk a single batter.

He is not on the All-Star roster. He pitched that game on the All-Star site anyway.

The Performance

Wheeler has been elite for years, and Tuesday's outing was a reminder of what the 36-year-old still brings at this stage of his career. He navigated 25 batters with precision — the lone blemish coming when Eugenio Suárez led off the seventh inning with a solo home run to make it a 3-1 game. Wheeler's response was to get Tyler Stephenson swinging on a splitter for strikeout No. 14, closing out a dominant inning and his night simultaneously.

Seven innings. Fourteen strikeouts. No walks. That's a master class in control and deception.

For context: in an era where starters are increasingly managed with pitch counts and workload limits, throwing seven innings and striking out 14 with no free passes is the kind of line that would have been celebrated no matter the season. In the week before the All-Star Game, at the game's own venue, with a snub narrative fully in place? It becomes the day's story.

Schwarber Adds the Exclamation Point

Kyle Schwarber hit his 31st home run of the season in Tuesday's win, extending his MLB lead in the category. He hit it at Citizens Bank Park. The Home Run Derby is at Citizens Bank Park in five days. If the Phillies are putting on a preview of All-Star Week's storylines, they're doing a thorough job.

Schwarber's 31st ties him with his best home run pace in years and sets up what could be a home-park Derby appearance — or at minimum, the knowledge that the league's leading home run hitter competed at the Derby site the week before the event.

What's Next

Wheeler will take his non-All-Star rotation to the back half of the schedule. The Phillies, hosting All-Star Week starting July 12, will have their stadium full of the game's biggest names while one of their own starts will not be included. The All-Star Game itself is July 14 at 8 PM ET on FOX at Citizens Bank Park. Next up on the Phillies' schedule after the break: continuing to chase the NL East. Wheeler continues every five days, with or without an All-Star nod.
