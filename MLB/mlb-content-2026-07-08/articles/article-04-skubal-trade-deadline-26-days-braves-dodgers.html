26 Days: The Skubal Trade Clock Is Running and the Braves and Dodgers Are Watching

By Marcus Cole | July 8, 2026

There are 26 days until the August 3 MLB trade deadline, and the question that's defined most of baseball's offseason speculation still doesn't have an answer.

Where does Tarik Skubal land?

The Detroit Tigers are sellers. They're sitting at approximately 38-49, outside the playoff picture, with a roster that isn't competitive enough to challenge the AL's top teams. Skubal — a two-time Cy Young Award winner and one of the best pitchers in the sport — is entering his final contract year. The Tigers have to decide whether to trade him now or risk losing him as a free agent for nothing.

According to ESPN's Jeff Passan and Kyle McDaniel, there's an 85% probability he gets traded. That number has been hovering around that range for weeks. It's not if. It's when and to whom.

The Race to Land Him

Two teams dominate the discussion. The Atlanta Braves have been identified as the frontrunner by veteran baseball reporter Bob Nightengale — and the fit makes intuitive sense. The Braves need starting pitching, have a competitive window open, and the baseball logic of adding Skubal to an Atlanta rotation is compelling on the surface.

The complication is the Los Angeles Dodgers. Los Angeles has the superior prospect capital — the kind of depth in their farm system that gives them leverage in any negotiation because they can match or exceed whatever Atlanta offers in future assets. The Tigers' GM Scott Harris is making the biggest call of his tenure, and the prospect package he can extract from the Dodgers may simply be too valuable to pass up, regardless of which team makes more baseball sense for Skubal's immediate future.

The Deadline Day Setup

One quirk of this year's deadline: August 3 falls on an off-day for the Tigers. That gives the Detroit front office maximum time on deadline day to finalize the deal without the distraction of a game schedule — a small but meaningful logistical advantage. The organization has noted the date on their calendar since the schedule was released.

The asking price is being compared to CC Sabathia-level returns when Sabathia was traded from Cleveland to Milwaukee in 2008. That's the tier of deal Skubal's value demands — and the tier of return the Tigers need to justify the trade.

What's Next

The trade deadline is August 3, 2026. Skubal will continue pitching for Detroit through the deadline — and every strong start adds to the asking price. The Braves and Dodgers, and potentially other suitors, have until 6 PM ET on August 3 to close a deal. The next major inflection point will be if any team formally submits a package — but those conversations typically stay quiet until the final days. Set a reminder: August 3 could be the biggest day of the 2026 baseball season.
