The White Sox Are in First Place. No, Really.

By Jake Torres | July 8, 2026

Go check the AL Central standings. The Chicago White Sox are at the top.

At 47-42, the White Sox have moved into first place in the American League Central, ahead of the Cleveland Guardians, as the league heads into the All-Star break. It is, depending on your familiarity with recent White Sox history, either a surprise or one of the great baseball comebacks of the modern era.

In 2024, the Chicago White Sox went 41-121. Let that sit for a moment. Forty-one wins. One hundred and twenty-one losses. It was one of the most catastrophic single-season records in the history of professional baseball — a season so difficult it reshaped the entire conversation about the franchise, its ownership, its management, and what it would take to rebuild from the ground floor.

Two years later, they lead their division.

What Changed

The specifics of how a team goes from 41 wins to first place in 24 months are worth studying, because this kind of turnaround doesn't happen by accident. Player development, roster construction, and organizational culture all factor into the equation. What the White Sox have demonstrated is that the rebuild — however painful it was to watch in 2024 — was working in ways that weren't visible in the standings.

The front office made moves. Players developed. The organization stayed the course. And the result is a team that, entering All-Star Week, sits at 47-42 and leads one of the more competitive divisions in the American League.

The AL Central Race

The Guardians, who have been one of the league's most analytically interesting teams in 2026 — leading their division for stretches despite a slugging percentage that would be historically low for a contender — are right behind Chicago. The race for the AL Central is genuinely compelling entering the second half, with multiple teams believing they have a path to the postseason.

For the White Sox, the task now shifts from proving they can play .500 ball to proving they can sustain it — and potentially win — in August and September, when the divisions get sorted out and the Wild Card race tightens.

What's Next

The second half of the MLB season begins after the All-Star Game on July 14 at Citizens Bank Park. The White Sox's AL Central lead going into the break is a statement about where the organization is — and a challenge to the teams behind them. September 2026 will tell us whether this is a true contender or a first half mirage. Based on how far they've already come from 2024's low point, it would be unwise to bet against them.
