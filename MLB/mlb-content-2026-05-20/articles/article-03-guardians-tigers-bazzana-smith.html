Guardians 4-3 Tigers: Bazzana's Homer, Rocchio's RBI, Smith's 15th Save

By Ryan Calloway

May 20, 2026

Cleveland won their fourth consecutive game Tuesday, continuing a pattern that is starting to define both teams heading into the AL Central race.

For the fourth consecutive night, the Cleveland Guardians found a way to win. And for the fourth consecutive night, the Detroit Tigers found a way to lose.

Tuesday's final was 4-3, another close game with a familiar Cleveland ending: a timely hit, a clutch at-bat, and Cade Smith retiring the final two batters in the ninth inning without incident. The Guardians have developed a formula for winning tight games, and it is working.

How It Happened

The Guardians trailed 2-0 through the early innings before rallying. Kyle Manzardo walked to open the fourth inning against Keider Montero. Two pitches later, Travis Bazzana drove a two-run home run to give Cleveland a 3-2 lead.

Bazzana's shot was his second homer of the series and was exactly the kind of decisive swing that the second-year player has shown the ability to produce in clutch situations. He finished the game 2-for-3 with two RBI.

The Guardians added their fourth run in the seventh inning when Brayan Rocchio grounded out, allowing Steven Kwan to score the go-ahead run. That 4-2 lead held long enough for Smith to close out the 4-3 final.

Smith struck out Jahmal Jones and Dillon Dingler in the ninth for his 15th save in 17 opportunities. The Guardians closer has been one of the most consistent late-inning options in the American League this season.

Detroit's Struggles Continue

Spencer Torkelson hit a two-run homer for the Tigers, and Detroit had opportunities throughout the game. But the Tigers have now dropped four consecutive games, a stretch that threatens to cost them ground in the AL Central standings.

Riley Greene scored twice for Detroit. The Tigers' offense was not the issue — allowing Cleveland to execute late-inning offense was.

What's Next

The Guardians and Tigers play Game 3 of their series on Wednesday. Cleveland can extend their winning streak to five games; Detroit can stop the bleeding at four consecutive losses. The AL Central race continues to take shape one close game at a time.
