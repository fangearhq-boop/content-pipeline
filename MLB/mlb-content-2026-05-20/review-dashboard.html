James Wood gave baseball fans that moment Tuesday night in Washington. The 22-year-old Nationals outfielder sent a ball to left-center field that caromed off Morabito's glove and rolled toward center. By the time the Mets fielders tracked it down and got the ball home, Wood was sliding across the plate — not with a single, not with a double, not with a triple. With a grand slam. It was the first inside-the-park grand slam in Major League Baseball since 2022. Wood's first career grand slam. And it arrived when it mattered most: the Nationals were trailing the Mets 5-0. Washington then did what baseball teams do when something rare and remarkable happens on their side of the diamond: they kept going. The Nationals rallied to win 9-6. Wood went 3-for-4 on the night, with Jose Tena adding another home run to the Washington offensive explosion. Inside-the-park grand slams are the baseball equivalent of lightning: incredibly rare, impossible to predict, and once you see one, you never forget exactly where you were.
