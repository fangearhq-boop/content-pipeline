Sasaki, Ohtani, Yamamoto: Dodgers Complete the First Japanese Pitcher Trilogy in MLB History

By Ballpark Banter | April 1, 2026

When the Dodgers built their rotation, they built it like no team has ever built one. Tonight's game is the proof.

Yoshinobu Yamamoto takes the mound at Dodger Stadium in Los Angeles on Wednesday, April 1 — the final game of a three-game series against the Cleveland Guardians, and the final piece of a pitching sequence that has never occurred in 150 years of Major League Baseball. According to Elias Stats, this will be the first time any team has started three consecutive Japanese-born pitchers: Roki Sasaki pitched Monday, Shohei Ohtani pitched Tuesday (six scoreless innings in a 4-1 Dodgers win), and Yamamoto goes tonight.

This isn't a trivia footnote. These are three of the best pitchers on the planet.

The Sequence

Sasaki opened the series Monday, setting the tone. Ohtani followed Tuesday night with one of the most anticipated starts in recent memory — six innings, zero runs, one hit, 98 miles per hour — and the Dodgers won 4-1. Now it falls to Yamamoto to close it out.

Yamamoto, who won two World Series rings with the Dodgers in 2024 and 2025, enters the 2026 season as one of the most reliable arms in the National League. He pairs a devastating slider with elite command, and his track record at Dodger Stadium is one of consistency and professionalism. He is the kind of pitcher who makes this look routine, even when it isn't.

The Dodgers enter tonight at 4-1. A win would give them a 5-1 start and a statement to the rest of the National League about what defending champions look like in early April.

Why It Matters Beyond the Numbers

The three-pitcher sequence matters not just statistically but culturally. Sasaki, Ohtani, and Yamamoto all came to American baseball from Japan — three transcendent talents who took different paths to Los Angeles and found each other in the same rotation at the same time. The fact that this is a first in MLB history reflects how rare it is for any one team to accumulate this kind of talent, let alone talent with this particular international dimension.

It is also a statement about how the global development of baseball has arrived at Dodger Stadium. Japan has produced Hall of Fame talent for two decades. Three consecutive starters from the same country — all in their prime, all on the same team — represents the high-water mark of that pipeline.

What's Next

After tonight, Yamamoto's next start will come mid-next week, when the Dodgers move on to their next series. The rotation then cycles back to Sasaki, then Ohtani again — and the sequence will continue, week after week, for a full 162-game season. For Dodger fans, that is an embarrassment of riches. For everyone else in the National League, it is something closer to a problem.

Watch tonight. This is history being made on a Wednesday in April.
