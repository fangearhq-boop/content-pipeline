Aaron Judge Is 32 Home Runs From 400 — and the Season Just Started

By Ballpark Banter | April 1, 2026

The 2026 season began five days ago, and Aaron Judge is already on the clock.

The New York Yankees captain entered the year with 368 career home runs — 32 away from joining the 400-home run club, one of baseball's most exclusive groups. Thirty-two home runs in a season is well within Judge's reach: he has hit 37 or more home runs in multiple seasons, and even a modest year by his standards would put 400 within sight before the postseason begins.

The question isn't whether Judge reaches 400 in 2026. It's when.

How No. 1 Happened

Judge hit his first home run of 2026 in the Yankees' second game — and he did it in a way that only makes sense in 2026. An at-bat was extended by an ABS challenge reversal: a strike call was overturned, the count continued, and Judge punished the next pitch. The ball was gone. His first home run of the season came with an assist from Hawk-Eye tracking technology — and Judge, typically stoic about such things, rounded the bases as if it had always been the plan.

The Yankees, off to a 3-0 start, needed no more proof that their captain is operating at full strength. But the home run was a useful reminder: he's here, he's healthy, and the count to 400 has officially begun.

The Company He's In

Judge is not alone in chasing 400. His Yankees teammate Paul Goldschmidt is also approaching the milestone, making New York potentially the first team in recent memory with two players on the 400-HR watch list simultaneously. Across the league, Manny Machado, Freddie Freeman, and Bryce Harper are all closing in on the mark as well.

There is a real possibility — not guaranteed, but real — that 2026 could see multiple players reach 400 career home runs. The depth of talent in this generation of power hitters makes it a reasonable projection rather than a fantasy.

The Historical Weight

Four hundred home runs places a player in permanent company. It is not the automatic Hall of Fame threshold it once was — the game has changed too much for a single number to carry that weight definitively — but 400 HRs is still a benchmark that separates the great from the generational. Judge is on his way there. The 2026 season is the vehicle.

What's Next

Every Judge home run from here forward carries the accumulating weight of the chase. The Yankees have 157 games remaining in the regular season. If Judge averages even 35 home runs this year — below his career peak — he reaches 403 and the milestone is complete before October.
