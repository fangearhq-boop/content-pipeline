Shohei Ohtani Is Back: Six Scoreless Innings in His 2026 Pitching Debut

By Ballpark Banter | April 1, 2026

Shohei Ohtani made his 2026 regular-season pitching debut Tuesday night at Dodger Stadium — and he answered every question that had been asked of him since his second Tommy John surgery with the only evidence that matters: results.

Six innings pitched. One hit allowed. Zero runs. Six strikeouts. Three walks. Eighty-seven pitches, fifty-four for strikes. His fastball touched 98 miles per hour. His breaking pitches were responsible for every strikeout. The Los Angeles Dodgers won 4-1 over the Cleveland Guardians.

It was, by any measure, a dominant performance from a pitcher who had every reason to be cautious — and chose instead to pitch like himself.

What Happened on the Mound

Ohtani was in command from the first inning. He got first-pitch strikes against 14 of the 23 batters he faced. The Guardians managed one base runner per inning at most, and the only real threat came in the fourth, when Rhys Hoskins laced a two-out double to left-center. Ohtani responded by getting Bo Naylor to pop out, ending the inning with Hoskins stranded at second. That was as close as Cleveland got.

The fastball velocity — touching 98 miles per hour — was the first physical confirmation of what scouts and medical staff had been suggesting in spring training: Ohtani's arm is healthy. His mechanics are clean. His release point is consistent. And when he is working with that kind of velocity behind his breaking stuff, he is one of the hardest starting pitchers to solve in baseball.

The Two-Way Reality

Ohtani did not simply pitch on Tuesday. He also batted — part of the universal DH structure that allows him to do what no other player in the modern game does. He went 0-for-1 but drew two walks, ensuring he reached base multiple times in a game he also started on the mound. He is the only player in Major League Baseball who participates in games this way, and the 2026 season is shaping up to be his first full two-way campaign since his surgeries.

History Around Him

Tuesday's start was also historically significant beyond Ohtani's individual line. The Dodgers have now deployed three consecutive Japanese-born starting pitchers for the first time in MLB history: Roki Sasaki started Monday, Ohtani Tuesday, and Yoshinobu Yamamoto is scheduled for tonight (April 1). According to Elias Stats, this three-game sequence has never happened before in 150 years of Major League Baseball.

The Dodgers entered tonight's Yamamoto start at 4-1. Their three-peat bid is very much alive.

What's Next

Ohtani's next pitching start will come in roughly five days, when the Dodgers' rotation cycles around again. Between now and then, he will continue to bat daily. The 2026 season has just begun, but the message from Tuesday night was clear: Shohei Ohtani is back, he is healthy, and he is still one of the best pitchers alive.

For baseball fans, that is very good news. For the rest of the National League, it is a problem.
