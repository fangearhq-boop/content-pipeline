April 1 MLB Recap: Astros Blow Out Red Sox, Rays Hand Brewers Their First Loss

By Ballpark Banter | April 1, 2026

Wednesday's MLB schedule was short on drama at the top of the lineup — Houston battered Boston, Texas battered Baltimore, Chicago battered the Angels — but long on significance at the bottom, where Tampa Bay's 3-2 win over Milwaukee handed the Brewers their first loss of the 2026 season.

Let's walk through what happened.

The Astros Serve Notice

Houston's 8-1 win over the Boston Red Sox was a statement game for a team that enters every season as a World Series contender. Eight runs, one allowed — a dominant performance that speaks to the kind of depth the Astros bring to every series. Houston started the season 3-2, and Wednesday's blowout will help them find their footing after a slightly uneven opening week.

Boston, at 1-3 after Wednesday's loss, is facing early questions about whether their roster construction can compete in the AL East alongside the Yankees (3-0) and Blue Jays (3-1).

Rangers Win Again

The Texas Rangers continued their strong early start with an 8-5 win over the Baltimore Orioles. Texas entered the week at 3-1, showing that last season's AL West competition has not slowed them down. The eight-run output is a sign of an offense that can do damage against any pitching staff.

Baltimore fell to 2-2 — a disappointing result for a team that opened the week with expectations of competing at the top of the AL East.

Brewers Fall to Tampa Bay

The most consequential result of the night: the Tampa Bay Rays beat the Milwaukee Brewers 3-2, ending Milwaukee's perfect 3-0 start. The Brewers had been one of a handful of teams with an unbeaten record through the first five days of the season, and the Rays — never to be overlooked regardless of payroll — showed why they are never a safe out.

Milwaukee drops to 3-1. They are still in excellent position in the NL Central alongside the Cincinnati Reds, but the 3-0 narrative is over.

The Rest of Wednesday's Results

The Cubs beat the Angels 7-2, with Chicago's offense continuing to show early signs of life. The Braves shut out the Athletics 4-0 — another win for a team managing a severe rotation injury crisis with quiet effectiveness. The Mets topped the Cardinals 4-2. The White Sox battered the Marlins 9-4 in the most lopsided game of the day.

What's Next

Thursday brings another full slate of MLB games. The picture is beginning to sharpen: the NL East is a four-way tangle at 3-1, the Phillies are in last place, the Yankees are still unbeaten, and the Dodgers — with Yamamoto on the mound Wednesday night — are making a case for the best rotation in baseball. April is just getting started, but the 2026 season is already providing a compelling opening chapter.
