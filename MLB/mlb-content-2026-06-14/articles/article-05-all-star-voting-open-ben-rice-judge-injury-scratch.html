All-Star Voting Is Open: Ben Rice's Moment, Judge's Situation, and Derby Intrigue

By Marcus Cole

June 14, 2026

Phase 1 of MLB All-Star voting is underway, and this year the process comes with more storylines than usual. The game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. The starting lineups will be determined by July 2. And between now and then, there are genuine questions about who shows up, who wins a vote they cannot cash, and who emerges as the most unexpected first-time selection of the cycle.

Ben Rice: The Yankees' Surprise All-Star

The most compelling individual story heading into the ballot is Ben Rice. The Yankees first baseman has had one of the most surprising offensive seasons of any young player in baseball this year. In 57 games, Rice has slashed .300/.393/.638 with 17 home runs and a 181 wRC+ — a figure that indicates he has been roughly 81 percent better than the average major league hitter over that period.

He was not supposed to be this story. Aaron Judge was supposed to be this story. But Judge is on the injured list with a right rib stress fracture, and Rice stepped into a leadership role in the Yankees' lineup and refused to look out of place. He may earn the starting spot at American League first base in his first All-Star selection, which would be one of the feel-good moments of the midsummer.

Judge's Complicated Situation

Aaron Judge is expected to receive significant fan support in the voting — Yankees fans tend to show up for their captain, injury or no injury. That creates a scenario where Judge wins a starting spot but has to withdraw due to his rib stress fracture, which projects a mid-August return at the earliest.

That outcome would not be unusual — players withdraw from the All-Star Game every year for various reasons. But it would make Rice's path to the starting lineup cleaner, and would give him a platform in his first year of national recognition.

The Derby Side

Parallel to the All-Star vote is the Home Run Derby on July 13, also in Philadelphia. With Kyle Schwarber and Yordan Alvarez tied for the major league lead in home runs at 24 apiece — and the Derby being held at Schwarber's home park — the exhibition could produce one of the most anticipated matchups in Derby history. Both players are likely participants. Both are at a pace that could threaten 60 home runs for the season.

What's Next

Phase 1 voting runs through June 25 at noon Eastern time. Phase 2 begins June 29 and concludes July 2. Roster selections for pitchers and reserves will follow. The game itself is July 14. Between now and then, every good performance by a bubble player is an argument for inclusion. Ben Rice needs to keep hitting. Kyle Schwarber needs to keep homering. Voters, as always, need to pay attention.
