Schwarber and Alvarez Tied at 24 HR — Derby at Schwarber's Home Park Coming Fast

By Jake Torres

June 14, 2026

This home run race just found its shape.

Yordan Alvarez hit two home runs Friday night against the Royals — a grand slam and a solo shot — in the Houston Astros' 10-8 victory. The two-home run night moved him into a tie with Kyle Schwarber at 24, both players deadlocked at the top of the major league leaderboard. Byron Buxton sits third with 21.

And the timing could not be better: the Home Run Derby is July 13 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. Kyle Schwarber's home park.

The Race Takes Shape

For most of the first two months of the season, Schwarber was the story — hitting home runs at a pace that drew genuine comparisons to the elite single-season marks in baseball history. He reached 20 home runs on May 15, the earliest any player had hit that benchmark in the modern era. The conversation around a 60-homer season started before Memorial Day.

Now there are two contenders. Alvarez's back-to-back season has been consistent and devastating — his combination of power and plate discipline makes him one of the most complete offensive hitters in the game. On any given night, he can hit the ball as far as anyone. Friday night was the reminder.

Buxton at 21 is not out of the conversation, but the gap from third to first is now three home runs — significant enough that this is realistically a two-man race unless Buxton goes on a sustained run.

The Derby Context

Only five players in MLB history have ever hit 60 home runs in a single season: Barry Bonds (73), Mark McGwire (70 and 65), Sammy Sosa (66, 64, and 63), Roger Maris (61), and Mickey Mantle (54 — close but not there). Both Schwarber and Alvarez are currently on a pace that would put them in or near that company if sustained through the full season.

The Derby at Citizens Bank Park adds an aesthetic layer that makes this race more compelling. Schwarber has hit some of the longest home runs of his career in that building, feeding off a Philadelphia crowd that understands power hitting the way few fan bases do. If Alvarez is invited to the same event — and his home run total makes him one of the most obvious selections — the Derby alone could become appointment viewing.

What's Next

Both players are in action Sunday. Every home run from this point forward carries additional weight — not just in the standings, but in the Derby and All-Star narratives that will dominate the baseball conversation through July. The race is tied. The setting is perfect. Watch closely.
