Braves' Best Record in Baseball Under Pressure as Acuña Hits the IL

By Ryan Calloway

June 14, 2026

The Atlanta Braves have the best record in baseball. They also no longer have Ronald Acuña Jr.

Acuña has been placed on the injured list with a left hamstring strain, the result of an injury suffered while running to first base in a recent game against the White Sox. The Braves were 45-23 before the news hit — the best mark in either league, carrying an eight-game NL East lead. They have since dropped three consecutive games.

The Scale of the Loss

Acuña is not simply a player in the Braves' lineup. He is the reason opposing pitchers make different decisions about how to work the rest of that order. When he is in the box, teams cannot pitch around the middle of Atlanta's lineup without paying a price. When he is not — and the last three games have illustrated this — those same pitchers have more room to maneuver.

What the Braves still have is a rotation anchored by Chris Sale, who has been one of the best pitchers in baseball in 2026, leading the majors with 92 strikeouts and eight wins. Matt Olson is providing consistent production with 20 home runs and 51 RBIs. Michael Harris II is hitting .304 with 13 home runs. The roster is not bare without Acuña — but a hamstring strain that sent him to the IL is not a day-to-day issue.

Deadline Math Changes

Six weeks ago, the Braves were unambiguously buyers at the August 3 trade deadline — a team with the best record in baseball adding a final piece to a roster that looked capable of a deep October run. That calculus has shifted.

If Acuña's hamstring requires extended time, the Braves now have a health question at the center of their lineup projection. Buying for a team that might be without its best player at the most important time is a different proposition than buying for a team at full strength. The deadline market will be watching Atlanta's medical reports as closely as their standings position.

What's Next

The Braves have the depth to absorb a short absence — the roster that went 45-23 is not suddenly mediocre. But hamstring injuries in the summer heat are unpredictable. The three-game skid needs to end, and Sale's next start will be the best indicator of whether Atlanta can stabilize without Acuña in the lineup. Every team in the NL East will be watching the Braves closely this week.
