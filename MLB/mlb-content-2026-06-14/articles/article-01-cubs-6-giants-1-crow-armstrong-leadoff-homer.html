Cubs 6, Giants 1: Crow-Armstrong Homers on First Pitch, Three-Game Streak

By Ryan Calloway

June 14, 2026

Pete Crow-Armstrong did not wait for anyone's permission. The Cubs centerfielder launched the first pitch he saw Saturday into the stands for a leadoff homer — his 12th of the season — and Chicago never looked back in a 6-1 victory over the San Francisco Giants at Wrigley Field.

It was not just the home run. It was the statement attached to it. First pitch, first inning, the longest ball of the night before the crowd had fully settled in. This is what a confident baseball player looks like.

Crow-Armstrong and Brown Set the Tone

Crow-Armstrong finished 3-for-5 with the homer, a double, and two runs scored. But the story he shared with Ben Brown, who turned in one of his cleanest starts of the season: 5.2 innings pitched, just one hit allowed. Brown was efficient and precise, giving the Cubs' bullpen a night off and building on the momentum from the previous two wins.

The Cubs have now won three consecutive games — their longest winning streak in recent weeks — and they did so against a Giants team that came in with legitimate postseason aspirations.

The "Back" Question

Every Cubs winning streak in 2026 has come with the inevitable external question: Are the Cubs back? Manager Craig Counsell addressed it directly after Saturday's win.

"We'll be 'back' when we stop asking if we're 'back,'" Counsell said.

The answer contains its own philosophy. The Cubs have been inconsistent in 2026 — stretches of dominant play interrupted by prolonged slumps, a frustrating pattern for a team with the talent on this roster. But something about the last three games has felt different: consistent starting pitching, the offense producing in the early innings, and Crow-Armstrong providing the kind of spark that changes a team's entire psychological posture.

The victory also delivered a statistical footnote worth noting: it was the Cubs' first series win in 11 tries. Winning series is the bedrock of successful seasons; getting one in the bank matters.

What's Next

The Cubs continue their homestand Sunday with an opportunity to complete the sweep of San Francisco. A sweep would make three series wins in the last three series — and that would be the moment where the "back" question might earn a different answer. Saturday's game was the kind that momentum is built from. Crow-Armstrong on the first pitch. Brown through five-plus. Three in a row. The North Side is paying attention.
