Marlins 12-0 Braves: Sanoja Grand Slam, Meyer Dominant as Miami Shuts Out Baseball's Best Team

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | May 19, 2026

The Atlanta Braves arrived in Miami as the team with the best record in Major League Baseball. They left Monday night without a single run on the board, shut out 12-0 in one of the most lopsided results involving a first-place team in recent memory.

Javier Sanoja hit the first grand slam of his career. Max Meyer was dominant on the mound. And the Marlins — playing in front of fewer than 9,000 fans at loanDepot Park — delivered a statement that the standings can't fully measure.

Meyer's Masterpiece

Max Meyer was the difference-maker from the first pitch. The Miami starter held Atlanta to just three hits over six innings, allowing zero earned runs, striking out six, and walking two. Against a lineup that had been producing offense at the highest level in baseball all season, Meyer made the Braves look ordinary.

Sanoja's grand slam — his first in the major leagues — blew the game open early. Joe Mack drove in four runs on three hits. Miami's offense was relentless throughout, producing a dozen runs against a Braves pitching staff that had been among the best in the National League.

Atlanta starter JR Ritchie absorbed the punishment: six hits, six earned runs, and a loss in four innings of work. The Braves managed just four hits as a team all night.

Perspective on the Loss

One blowout does not define a season. The Braves are 30-15 and remain in first place in the NL East by a comfortable margin. The best teams in baseball lose occasionally by ugly scores — that is the nature of a 162-game season.

What the game revealed is that Miami's pitching, when it's right, can compete with anyone in the league. Meyer has the stuff to neutralize elite lineups. On a Monday night in mid-May, he showed exactly that.

What's Next

The Braves and Marlins continue their series Tuesday night, with Martin Perez starting for Atlanta against Braxton Garrett for Miami. Don't expect another 12-0 performance from either side. Atlanta is too good, and they'll have had a full night to process a performance that didn't match their standard. Game 2 is the response game.
