Ramírez Hits No. 8, Now 8 HRs Away From the 300-300 Club

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | May 19, 2026

José Ramírez led off the ninth inning with a solo home run Monday night. His 8th of the 2026 season. His 292nd of his career. And eight more now stand between him and one of baseball's most exclusive milestones.

The Cleveland Guardians rolled past the slumping Detroit Tigers 8-2 at Comerica Park, and while the outcome was never in doubt, that ninth-inning homer from Ramírez was the signature moment. A statement swing that tightened his grip on a place in baseball history.

The 300-300 Club

Eight players have ever hit 300 home runs and stolen 300 bases in the same career. Willie Mays. Barry Bonds. Bobby Bonds. Alex Rodriguez. Andre Dawson. Reggie Sanders. Steve Finley. Carlos Beltran. Hall of Famers, five-tool legends, and the defining athletes of their generations.

Ramírez crossed the stolen base threshold in early May, reaching 303 career steals against the Athletics. Now he is methodically working through the home run side of the equation. At 292 career home runs, the math is simple: eight more and he joins that club as only its ninth member ever — and its first primary infielder alongside Alex Rodriguez.

At his current 2026 pace, with 8 HRs through roughly six weeks of the season, Ramírez could reach 300 career home runs within a matter of weeks. The summer stretch of June and July has historically been his most productive offensive period.

Monday's Performance

Ramírez did more than just the home run on Monday. He also contributed a two-run double earlier in the game, driving in runs the conventional way before ending his night with the solo shot in the ninth. Chase DeLauter and Rhys Hoskins each drove in two runs as Cleveland built its lead.

On the mound, Slade Cecconi was excellent — going 7.1 innings while allowing just two runs and four hits, picking up his third win. Detroit's Framber Valdez had a difficult night by contrast, giving up four runs in five innings as the Tigers extended their recent misery to a 2-11 mark over their last 13 games.

What's Next

The Guardians have now won six of their last seven games and head into the week with genuine momentum. Ramírez carries the chase for 300 career home runs with every plate appearance, and Cleveland's fans have settled in for what could be one of the most memorable individual milestones in franchise history. The 300-300 club is within reach. Eight home runs away.
