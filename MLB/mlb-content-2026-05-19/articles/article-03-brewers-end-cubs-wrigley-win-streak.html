Brewers 9, Cubs 3: Milwaukee Ends Chicago's 15-Game Wrigley Winning Streak

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | May 19, 2026

The Cubs had not lost at Wrigley Field since April 11. Fifteen consecutive home wins, one of the most remarkable stretches of home dominance in recent baseball history. It ended Monday night, when the Milwaukee Brewers walked out of the Friendly Confines with a 9-3 victory and Chicago's invincibility at home very much in question.

The Third Inning That Decided It

Chicago starter Shota Imanaga had a disaster on his hands almost from the first pitch. Christian Yelich — whose status had been uncertain in recent days due to back tightness — homered in the second inning to put Milwaukee on the board.

Then came the third. Jake Bauers hit a three-run home run. Sal Frelick followed with a two-run double. Jackson Chourio added an RBI single. By the end of that inning, the Brewers led 5-0 and Imanaga's night was already effectively over. He finally exited in the fifth after allowing a three-run homer to Bauers — that is, another Bauers homer — finishing the evening with eight runs and nine hits allowed in four and a third innings. It was his worst start of the 2026 season by a wide margin.

Cubs Fight Back Briefly

Chicago did show fight. In the fifth inning, Dansby Swanson launched a two-run homer and Michael Busch added an RBI double, cutting the deficit to 8-3. But Milwaukee's lead was simply too large, and their bullpen handled the final innings without drama.

For the Cubs, the loss is a reminder that streaks end. Chicago still sits at 28-16 overall and remains atop the NL Central standings — a single loss does not change the story of this team's season. But after 15 straight wins in the building they call home, it still stings.

What's Next

The Brewers and Cubs play Game 2 of their series Tuesday night at Wrigley. After a performance like Monday's — Imanaga's roughest outing, a 9-3 drubbing in front of the home fans — Chicago will be motivated to respond quickly. The NL Central race between these two division rivals is very much alive, and Tuesday night has the feel of a statement game in the making.
