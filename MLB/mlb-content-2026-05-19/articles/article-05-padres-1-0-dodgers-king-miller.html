Padres 1-0 Dodgers: King's Nine-Strikeout Gem and Miller's 15th Save Win NL West's First Showdown

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | May 19, 2026

The first Padres-Dodgers series of 2026 opened Monday night at Petco Park, and San Diego answered the moment: a 1-0 victory built on seven dominant innings from Michael King and sealed by an edgy ninth from Mason Miller, who earned his MLB-leading 15th save of the season.

One run was all it took. Miguel Andujar provided it in the first inning with a solo home run off Yoshinobu Yamamoto. Everything else was pitching.

King at His Best

Michael King was exceptional from his first pitch. He struck out nine batters over seven innings — season highs in both categories — while allowing just four hits, zero earned runs, and two walks. Against a Dodgers lineup that includes Shohei Ohtani and one of the deepest collections of talent in baseball, those numbers are remarkable.

Ohtani had two hits on the night, reminding everyone that even on a struggling offensive day, he is impossible to fully neutralize. But King limited the damage and kept Los Angeles off the board every time the Dodgers put runners on base.

Miller Survives the Ninth

It was not easy at the end. Mason Miller walked the first two batters he faced on nine pitches, threatening to give back the lead that King had protected for seven masterful innings. But Miller gathered himself, got three consecutive outs, and induced a game-ending groundout from Andy Pages to close out San Diego's 1-0 win.

It was Miller's 15th save of the season — the most in Major League Baseball — and a reminder that even the best closers can manufacture drama before getting the job done.

For Los Angeles starter Yamamoto, Monday was a tough-luck outing. He threw quality baseball and kept his team in the game, but Andujar's first-inning blast was the only offense either team could generate. Yamamoto was not the story. King and Miller were.

What's Next

The Padres and Dodgers play Game 2 of their three-game series Tuesday night at Petco Park, with Griffin Canning starting for San Diego against Emmet Sheehan for Los Angeles. The Dodgers will need to even this series — falling two games back to the Padres in the first series of the year between these NL West rivals would be a meaningful early-season statement. Both teams know the weight of this series, and Tuesday night will raise the stakes further.
