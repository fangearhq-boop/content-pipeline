José Ramírez Steals No. 300 — Now 9 HRs From Joining Baseball's Most Exclusive Club

By Marcus Cole | May 4, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

José Ramírez slid into third base on Saturday and slid into history at the same time.

The Cleveland Guardians third baseman swiped his 300th career stolen base in the fifth inning of Cleveland's 14-6 victory over the Oakland Athletics at Progressive Field, joining Kenny Lofton as the only two Guardians players in franchise history to reach that milestone. But the stolen base was only the headline. The real story is what it means for the next chapter of one of baseball's most decorated careers.

The 300/300 Club — One of Baseball's Most Exclusive Addresses

Ramírez enters this week with 291 career home runs. He needs nine more to reach 300 — which, combined with his newly achieved 300 career steals, would place him in a club that currently has only eight members: Barry Bonds, Alex Rodriguez, Willie Mays, Andre Dawson, Carlos Beltrán, Bobby Bonds, Reggie Sanders, and Steve Finley.

Consider that list for a moment. Four Hall of Famers, one surefire future inductee in Beltrán, and three others who combined some of the rarest athletic gifts in baseball history. Ramírez would become the ninth member — and the first active player to join the club since Beltrán crossed 300 stolen bases in 2012.

With the Guardians on pace for another strong season and Ramírez showing no signs of slowing down — he won the AL Player of the Month award for April after posting a .342 batting average with seven home runs and 28 RBIs — the milestone could arrive by midsummer.

The Context Behind the Stolen Base

Ramírez's 300th stolen base came in the fifth inning, when he swiped third base after leading off with a two-run double. The Guardians had already built a significant lead over the Athletics, but the steal mattered regardless of the scoreboard. Ramírez has been one of the most complete players in baseball for nearly a decade — one of the rare athletes who can change a game with his bat, his glove, and his legs.

On the same afternoon, Ramírez joined Starling Marte, José Altuve, and Trea Turner as the only active players with 300 career stolen bases. That's a short list. And Ramírez is the only one of the four who is also closing in on 300 career home runs.

The 30-30 Chase in Parallel

While the 300/300 milestone gets the attention, Ramírez is simultaneously chasing a third consecutive 30-30 season. He entered the 2026 season having posted 30-30 numbers in each of the previous two years — a feat accomplished by only a handful of players in baseball history. A third consecutive 30-30 campaign would put him among the rarest company imaginable for sustained elite dual-threat production.

He currently has 13 stolen bases on the season with a home run pace of approximately 30. The math is achievable. The player is proven. Cleveland has reason to believe May and June will bring a historic announcement.

What's Next

The Guardians are back in action Monday as the AL Central race continues to take shape. Ramírez remains one of the most watched position players in baseball as he approaches career home run No. 300. At his current pace, the milestone could arrive within the next six to eight weeks. If it lands before the All-Star break, Ramírez enters July as one of the most compelling storylines in the sport — a Hall of Fame-caliber player quietly adding his name to a list that begins with Willie Mays and ends, so far, with only eight names.
