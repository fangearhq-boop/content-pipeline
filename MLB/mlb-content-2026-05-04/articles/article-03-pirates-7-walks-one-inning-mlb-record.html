Pittsburgh Pirates Walk Seven Times in One Inning — Ties MLB Record from 1909 and 1983

By Ryan Calloway | May 4, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Baseball's record books have been around for a long time. Some entries look like they will never be touched. Saturday in Cincinnati, the Pittsburgh Pirates touched one of them.

The Pirates drew seven walks in a single inning against the Cincinnati Reds on May 2, tying the MLB record for most walks by a team in a single inning — a record that had only been set twice before in the history of professional baseball, in 1909 and 1983. That's 117 years of MLB games, roughly 200,000 team-innings played, and a mark that had been equaled a grand total of three times.

The Rarity Behind the Number

Seven walks in one inning sounds improbable. It requires a pitcher — or pitchers — to lose the strike zone almost entirely for an extended period, with batters disciplined enough to lay off borderline pitches despite the unusual situation. The 1909 occurrence predates the live ball era. The 1983 instance is a memory for only a handful of people still actively following baseball.

What made Saturday's episode notable was not just the number itself, but the conditions. The Pirates came into the game without any particular reputation for elite plate discipline — this was not a lineup packed with walk leaders. And yet, for one inning, seven batters in a row (or close enough to it) found their way to first base without putting a ball in play.

Baseball is like that. It delivers the unexpected, often on a random Saturday afternoon, and the record books quietly add another asterisk to a number that most fans never knew existed.

The Pirates' 2026 Backdrop

Pittsburgh enters May with one of the more unusual team profiles in baseball. The Pirates are on pace for a franchise-record number of home runs and carry one of the better ERAs in the sport. Despite their numbers, they have struggled to translate individual production into wins at the rate their underlying stats would suggest. It is the kind of team where strange, standout moments — like seven walks in a single inning — feel somehow fitting.

The Pirates are not in the NL Central race. But they have produced some of the season's more compelling individual and statistical storylines, and Saturday added one more to the list.

What's Next

The Pittsburgh Pirates continue their 2026 schedule this week with an NL Central matchup on the road. The seven-walks-in-one-inning record now ties 1909, 1983, and 2026 — a span of 117 years of Major League Baseball. The record remains at seven. And somewhere in the back of an MLB record book, a statistician has quietly updated the entry with a new date.
