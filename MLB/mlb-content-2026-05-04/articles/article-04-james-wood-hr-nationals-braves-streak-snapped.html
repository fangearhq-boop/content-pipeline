James Wood HR, García's 4 Hits Snap Braves' 6-Game Streak — Washington 11, Atlanta 4

By Marcus Cole | May 4, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Atlanta Braves' winning streak had to end sometime. On Sunday, James Wood made sure of it.

The Washington Nationals outfielder blasted a 412-foot opposite-field home run — his eighth of the season — and reached base five times as Washington delivered an emphatic 11-4 victory to snap Atlanta's six-game winning streak at Nationals Park. Luis García Jr. added four hits and scored twice in what became one of the Nationals' most complete offensive performances of the 2026 season.

Wood's Growing Power Signature

The home run was not just notable for its distance — 412 feet to the opposite field — but for what it represents in Wood's development. The 22-year-old is now leading MLB with five opposite-field home runs on the season, a sign that his power is authentic and spread across the field, not manufactured by pulling everything. Pitchers cannot simply pitch him away and expect to win.

Wood reached base five times in the game — homer, walks, and hits — in a performance that showcased the full offensive toolkit that made him one of the most anticipated young Nationals in recent memory. He entered May with eight home runs and is on pace for a genuinely impressive freshman full-season campaign at the big-league level.

García's Steady Presence

While Wood's power drew headlines, Luis García Jr.'s four-hit game was equally important to Washington's offensive output. García is quietly one of the more consistent contact hitters on the Nationals roster, and Sunday's performance was a reminder that the Nationals' lineup depth — when it clicks — can challenge anyone.

The 11-4 final was not particularly close after the early innings. Washington sent the Braves' starters for multiple runs before the bullpen took over, and the lead grew to a point where the outcome was never in genuine doubt over the final few innings.

The Braves Remain Dominant Despite the Loss

One loss does not shake Atlanta's grip on the NL East. The Braves still hold an 8.5-game lead over the Marlins — the largest division lead in Major League Baseball — and have been the consensus best team in the National League through the first month of the season. Their six-game winning streak was the longest in MLB at the time it was snapped.

The loss will not be remembered as a turning point for Atlanta. But for Washington, and specifically for James Wood, Sunday afternoon may be looked back on as one of the early landmark performances of what is shaping up to be a breakout year.

What's Next

Washington hosts its next series this week at Nationals Park, with Wood expected to continue hitting near the top of the order. The Braves return to Atlanta for a home series beginning Monday. Atlanta's NL East lead of 8.5 games remains the largest divisional cushion in baseball, and the defending division champions show no sign of surrendering it. For the Nationals, the mission is continuing to give Wood the platform to develop into the franchise anchor they believe he can be.
