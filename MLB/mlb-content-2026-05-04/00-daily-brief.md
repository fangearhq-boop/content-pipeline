### STORY 2:
**Headline:** Nick Kurtz Walk Streak Ends at 20 — Tied Bonds, 2 Shy of Roy Cullenbine's 1947 MLB Record
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** NEW
**Priority Score:** 9
**Source Searches:** mlb.com, si.com, bolavip.com, yardbarker.com

Nick Kurtz's streak of consecutive games drawing a walk ended at 20 on May 2, when the Oakland first baseman failed to walk in a 14-6 loss to the Guardians. During the streak, Kurtz tied Barry Bonds (20 games) and passed Ted Williams (19 games), but fell two games short of Roy Cullenbine's all-time MLB record of 22 consecutive games with a walk, set in 1947. Kurtz entered May leading the majors with 33 walks — more than any hitter before May since Bryce Harper's 38 in 2018.

### STORY 3:
**Headline:** Pirates Walk 7 Times in One Inning — Ties MLB Record from 1909 and 1983
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** NEW
**Priority Score:** 8
**Source Searches:** wfin.com, mlb.com, baseball-reference.com

The Pittsburgh Pirates drew seven walks in a single inning against the Cincinnati Reds on May 2, tying the MLB record for most walks by a team in a single inning. The only other times this occurred were in 1909 and 1983. The bizarre statistical event underscores one of baseball's most unusual inning-level records.

Munetaka Murakami enters May 4 leading MLB with 13 home runs, on pace for 65 on the season. With 26 RBIs, 33 walks, and a .967 OPS, the 26-year-old Japanese star is rewiring what expectations look like for international players joining MLB.
