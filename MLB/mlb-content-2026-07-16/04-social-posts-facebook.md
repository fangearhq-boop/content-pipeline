### STORY 2: Cristopher Sanchez Breaks a 115-Year Phillies Record

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Here's a first-half story that deserves more attention than it got.

Cristopher Sanchez, the Phillies starter, set the franchise all-time record for consecutive scoreless innings at 44 2/3. He broke a record that had stood since 1911 — set by Grover Cleveland Alexander, who is in the Baseball Hall of Fame.

Let that number sink in. Alexander's record lasted 115 years. Two world wars. The Great Depression. The end of segregated baseball. The DH. The Wild Card. Interleague play. Replay review. The pitch clock. Through all of it, Alexander's 41 innings stood as the longest scoreless streak in Phillies history.

Sanchez erased it in late spring 2026.

His first-half line: 5-2, 1.62 ERA, 1.15 WHIP, 86 strikeouts in 72.1 innings pitched. He was named the NL starter at the All-Star Game at Citizens Bank Park on July 14 — pitching at his home park in front of the fans who watched him make history this spring.

If you haven't been following Sanchez closely, start now. He's one of the best starters in the National League, and the second half hasn't started yet.
