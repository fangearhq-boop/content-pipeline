Ryan O'Hearn Drove In 10 Runs in One Game — and the Pirates Record Books Had to Be Rewritten

By Ryan Calloway

Ryan O'Hearn drove in 10 runs in a single game just before the All-Star break, setting a Pittsburgh Pirates franchise record that had stood since 1939.

If you missed it — and given the All-Star week noise, that is entirely understandable — it is worth taking a moment now to understand what happened.

Ten RBIs, One Game

Ten RBIs in a single game is among the rarest individual achievements in baseball. It is not a number that appears often in the record books, and when it does, it tends to belong to players in the middle of a career-defining performance. O'Hearn's game set the Pittsburgh Pirates all-time franchise record for RBIs in a single contest, surpassing a mark that had not moved since 1939 — 87 years ago.

What made the performance more notable: O'Hearn accounted for every one of those runs himself. He was not the beneficiary of errors or wild pitches that inflated the box score. He drove each one in personally. That distinction — the first player to account for his team's first 10-plus RBIs individually in a single game — adds another layer of historical significance to what was already a franchise-record performance.

The Context

The game occurred approximately July 12 to 14, right in the window before the All-Star break pulled every major news outlet toward Midsummer Classic coverage. The timing was brutal for O'Hearn and the Pirates from a visibility standpoint. A 10-RBI game in early June would have been the sports story of the day. In the window immediately preceding All-Star week, it competed with roster announcements, travel logistics, and the steady pre-break storylines that dominate coverage.

It shouldn't have been buried. It won't stay buried.

O'Hearn and the Pirates

Ryan O'Hearn has carved out a career as a first baseman who can hit for power when healthy and in a favorable lineup situation. His name is not among the first called when power hitters are discussed nationally. That is the context that makes a 10-RBI game even more unexpected — this was not a superstar putting up a mega-performance. This was a player having the game of his life at the right moment.

For the Pittsburgh Pirates, who have had limited individual performance milestones to celebrate in recent years, it was a genuine bright spot — a franchise record that eclipsed a mark set before World War II.

Giving It Its Due

The second half of the 2026 MLB season begins today. The trade deadline is 15 days out. There are big stories in every direction right now, and they will continue to accumulate.

But before the second half fully takes over, the O'Hearn game deserves at least a moment of proper recognition. Ten RBIs. Pirates franchise record. He did it himself. The previous record was set 87 years ago.

Now you know.
