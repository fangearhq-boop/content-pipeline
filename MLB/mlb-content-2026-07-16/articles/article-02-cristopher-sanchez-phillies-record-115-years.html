Cristopher Sanchez Just Broke a 115-Year-Old Phillies Record

By Jake Torres

Cristopher Sanchez set the Philadelphia Phillies all-time franchise record for consecutive scoreless innings this spring at 44 and two-thirds innings. The record he broke had stood since 1911 — set by Grover Cleveland Alexander, who was inducted into the Baseball Hall of Fame in 1938.

That mark survived two World Wars. The Great Depression. The integration of Major League Baseball. The designated hitter. The Wild Card. Interleague play. Instant replay. The pitch clock. For 115 years, no Phillies pitcher had matched Alexander's 41 consecutive scoreless innings.

Sanchez erased it in the spring of 2026.

The Numbers

Sanchez's scoreless streak ran from late April through early June, a stretch in which he simply did not allow an earned run across nine-plus starts. The 44 and two-thirds innings represents a record that may stand for another century.

His full first-half line: 5-2, 1.62 ERA, 1.15 WHIP, 86 strikeouts in 72 and one-third innings pitched. Those are not just good numbers — they are historic ones for a pitcher in a Phillies uniform. Sanchez has been as good as anyone in the National League this year, and the scoreless streak is the headline act of a broader performance that deserves recognition.

The All-Star Game

On July 14, Sanchez started the MLB All-Star Game at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia — his home park, in front of the fans who watched him make history this spring. He was named the NL's starting pitcher, the league's recognition of a first half unlike almost any in Phillies pitching history.

Sanchez was acquired from the Miami Marlins in a trade. He has become one of the most quietly dominant starters in the National League, the kind of pitcher whose excellence builds across a calendar without making daily headlines. Until this spring, when the streak started attracting national attention, many casual fans had not yet absorbed how good he had become.

Alexander and History

Grover Cleveland Alexander was one of the greatest pitchers in the history of the game. He won 373 career games, struck out more than 2,000 batters across an era when pitching conditions were entirely different from today, and compiled one of the most sustained stretches of excellence any pitcher has ever managed. His 1916 season with the Phillies — 33 wins, 16 shutouts — remains one of the most remarkable individual pitching seasons in Major League history.

His scoreless innings record for the franchise stood for 115 years. Sanchez's name is now above his in the Phillies record book.

What Comes Next

The second half of 2026 begins today. Sanchez enters it with a 1.62 ERA, a record-breaking streak behind him, and — if the Phillies make their expected moves at the trade deadline — a team built to contend deep into October.

He is one of the best starters in the National League. The record he set this spring makes that case better than any argument could.
