MLB Second Half Begins: Dodgers Lead the Way as Trade Deadline Looms

By Marcus Cole

The All-Star break is over. The second half of the 2026 MLB season starts today, and for every team still in contention, the calendar just got very short.

Here is where things stand as of this morning.

The National League

The Los Angeles Dodgers are the best team in baseball. Their record of 61-36 leads every other team in the majors, and they enter the second half as the clear favorite in the National League West. The Dodgers have had no real division race to speak of — they have built a cushion that makes the NL West story about the playoffs, not the pennant race.

In the NL Central, the Milwaukee Brewers are playing well at 59-37. Their division lead has held all first half. The NL East belongs to the Atlanta Braves at 55-40, though that division will be more competitive in the second half.

The American League

The AL East leader is the Tampa Bay Rays at 56-38. They've been one of the stronger stories in the first half — a team that doesn't spend like the Yankees but has repeatedly found ways to compete. At 56 wins through 94 games, they're on pace for a strong playoff position.

The Chicago White Sox hold the AL Central at 50-45 — a lead, but not a comfortable one. That division will likely be decided in August. The Texas Rangers lead the AL West at 49-47, a mark that reflects a genuinely competitive division where nothing has been settled.

The Trade Deadline: 15 Days

The 2026 MLB trade deadline is July 31 at 6 PM ET. That is 15 days from today. Every team in the standings above knows what they need; the question is who acts and who talks.

The biggest name on the market is Tarik Skubal of the Detroit Tigers. The two-time AL Cy Young Award winner is in a contract year with no extension talks currently underway. The Yankees, Phillies, Rays, Cubs, and Braves are all expected to pursue him seriously. Acquiring Skubal does not just improve a rotation — it changes the World Series math entirely for the team that lands him.

Other names in play include Mason Miller, the San Diego Padres closer, who has been linked to the Yankees for weeks. The Padres are leaning toward selling, which makes Miller available. The Houston Astros are reportedly finalizing a trade that would send left-handers Lance McCullers Jr. and Colton Gordon to the Milwaukee Brewers. The New York Mets are the most likely seller in the NL East.

Second Half Begins Tonight

The post-break schedule resumes tonight across both leagues. For fans, the second half is always when the standings tighten and the stakes become real. For front offices, it's the two weeks before the deadline that will define whether 2026 ends in October or not.

The Dodgers are the team everyone else has to catch. The deadline is the mechanism by which contenders try.

Second half is here. Time to find out who wants it.
