The 2026 MLB Trade Deadline Is 15 Days Away — Here's What You Need to Know

By Marcus Cole

The 2026 MLB trade deadline is July 31 at 6 PM ET. The second half of the season starts today. That means every front office in baseball has exactly 15 days to decide what they are.

The Name That Matters Most: Tarik Skubal

Tarik Skubal is the trade deadline. Every other conversation exists in the shadow of this one.

Skubal is the Detroit Tigers ace and a two-time AL Cy Young Award winner. He is in a contract year. Extension talks between him and Detroit have been described as non-existent. The Tigers have made clear they want elite prospects in return, and the teams willing to pay that price — in players, not money — are forming a short but serious list.

The Yankees, Phillies, Rays, Cubs, and Braves are the five organizations most consistently linked to Skubal. Every one of them has rotation needs. Every one of them has a realistic playoff path. And every one of them understands what adding a front-of-rotation, strikeout-heavy, command-elite ace does to a postseason series.

The team that lands Skubal does not just improve. It becomes the World Series favorite in its league. That is not an overstatement. In a short playoff series, the ability to deploy an ace twice in a seven-game series can be the entire difference. Skubal is that pitcher.

Other Names in the Market

Mason Miller, the San Diego Padres closer, has been connected to the Yankees for several weeks. He throws hard, misses bats at an exceptional rate, and fits the profile of a dominant late-inning piece for a contender. The Padres are leaning toward selling at the deadline, which makes Miller available and puts the Yankees in a position where they could add both a closer and potentially more rotation depth.

The Houston Astros are reportedly finalizing a trade to send left-handers Lance McCullers Jr. and Colton Gordon to the Milwaukee Brewers. That deal, if completed, would address the Brewers' pitching needs while giving Houston a return on pitchers who no longer fit their timeline. The Astros' window has been scrutinized this year; this move suggests they are pivoting toward a rebuild rather than chasing a final run.

The New York Mets are the clearest seller in the National League East. Their position in the standings makes the decision straightforward. The question is which pieces they move and which buyers are willing to pay.

Who Is Buying and What They Need

The Yankees need bullpen depth and have been in the market for a catcher upgrade alongside the closer conversation. The Cubs are looking at both starting pitching and relief help. The Phillies are targeting relievers and potentially a starter to complement their existing rotation — particularly if Skubal ends up elsewhere. The Rays have been described as aggressive Skubal suitors who are willing to trade from a strong farm system to make the acquisition.

Fifteen Days

July 16 to July 31. That is the window in which this deadline will be defined. The second half begins tonight with the deadline conversation already running in the background of every game played.

The moves that happen in the next two weeks will determine which teams are built for October and which are built for next year. Watch the Skubal situation most closely. Everything else follows from that.
