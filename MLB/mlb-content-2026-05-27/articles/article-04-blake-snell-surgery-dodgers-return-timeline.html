Blake Snell's Surgery Went 'As Expected' — But His Return Timeline Has a Wide Range

By Marcus Cole

May 27, 2026

The Dodgers removed three bone spurs from Snell's left elbow. The GM says it went well. The return could be as fast as 12 days or as long as 3 months. Los Angeles is planning for both.

The Los Angeles Dodgers have cautious good news on Blake Snell after a week of uncertainty around the two-time Cy Young Award winner's elbow. The surgery went well. The team expects him back this season. The only remaining question — and it is a significant one — is when.

General manager Brandon Gomes confirmed this week that Snell's procedure to remove loose bodies from his left elbow went "as expected," with three bone spurs removed during the operation performed by renowned sports orthopedic surgeon Dr. Neal ElAttrache. Gomes expressed optimism about Snell's eventual return but stopped short of committing to a specific timeline.

Two Models, Two Very Different Timelines

The uncertainty around Snell's return stems from the range of possible recovery trajectories. The surgery is a known procedure — removing loose bodies from an elbow is a fairly well-understood operation — but what is discovered during the procedure and the individual body's response to healing can shift the timeline significantly in either direction.

The optimistic scenario for Snell and the Dodgers points to Tarik Skubal of the Detroit Tigers, who had a similar procedure performed by Dr. ElAttrache and threw his first bullpen session just 12 days after surgery. If Snell's recovery tracks that path, a return to the Dodgers rotation before the end of June or by the All-Star break is within the realm of possibility.

The more conservative comparison is Edwin Díaz, the New York Mets closer whose timeline after a related procedure ran closer to three months. That model would push Snell's return to late July or August — still this season, but a longer absence from the Dodgers' plans during a stretch of the schedule that matters.

Gomes indicated that the preferred hope is the shorter timeline, and that the Dodgers' expectation is "the hope and expectation right now." The optimism is real, even if the commitment to a specific return date is not yet possible.

Dodgers Move to Shore Up the Depth

While Snell works through recovery, the Dodgers have not been passive. The front office acquired lefty reliever Eric Lauer from the Toronto Blue Jays in exchange for cash considerations or a player to be named later — a move that adds experience and a quality left arm to a bullpen managing multiple absences.

The Dodgers have also had to manage Jack Dreyer going to the injured list in the same window, adding to a bullpen situation that has required active management. Brusdar Graterol is also on the 60-day IL.

Snell made his 2026 season debut before the surgery, giving up four earned runs in three innings after missing the start of the year with shoulder fatigue and inflammation. The elbow issue emerged as he attempted to build on that debut, and the decision to address it surgically now — rather than attempt to pitch through it — reflects the Dodgers' preference to get a clean resolution and bring Snell back healthy.

What's Next

Blake Snell will follow a recovery program overseen by the Dodgers' medical staff as he works through the post-surgical process. The team will provide updates on his throwing progress as they become available. Los Angeles continues to navigate its pitching depth through a combination of roster additions and internal options while Snell recovers, with the expectation that he will contribute to the Dodgers' 2026 postseason push when he returns to health.
