Corbin Carroll Is One Triple From D-Backs Franchise History — and He's Playing in San Francisco This Week

By Ryan Calloway

May 27, 2026

Carroll smacked two triples against the Rockies on May 24 to reach 51 career three-baggers — one away from Stephen Drew's D-backs all-time record of 52. Oracle Park awaits, and Carroll is scorching hot.

There are exactly two ways to view what Corbin Carroll has been doing this season. The first is to say he is on the verge of breaking the Arizona Diamondbacks' all-time franchise triples record — and that is accurate. The second is to say he is probably already past it by now, given the rate at which he accumulates three-baggers and the three-game series at San Francisco's Oracle Park that started Tuesday.

Either way, history is close. Carroll has 51 career triples. Stephen Drew's franchise record is 52.

How He Got Here

Carroll reached the 51-triple mark with a memorable game against the Colorado Rockies on May 24, going 4-for-4 at the plate with two triples. It was exactly the kind of performance that has defined his career: pure, aggressive speed that turns what would be doubles for most outfielders into three-baggers with room to spare.

The pace at which Carroll has accumulated his 51 career triples is extraordinary even by his own standards. He reached that total in 537 career games. Stephen Drew, who held the franchise record at 52, needed 773 games to get there. Carroll is arriving at these milestones faster than anyone expected, and he is doing it against better competition across a longer season than most comparison points would suggest.

In 2026 alone, Carroll has eight triples — twice as many as any other player in baseball. He has led the league in triples in each of the last three seasons. This is not a fluke of ballpark dimensions or lineup protection. This is what he does.

The Record in Context

Stephen Drew played for the Diamondbacks from 2006 to 2011, accumulating his 52 career triples over parts of six seasons and 773 games. He was a quality shortstop who played hard and ran well. The fact that his franchise record has stood for more than a decade is a testament to how rare the combination of speed and instinct required to hit triples consistently really is.

Carroll is about to erase that mark, and when he does — whether it happens Tuesday night, Wednesday, or sometime this weekend — he will own it by a margin that grows every week. He already holds the D-backs' top two single-season triples records (14 in 2024, 17 in 2025). When he breaks Drew's career mark, he will have all three.

Among all active MLB players, only Mike Trout and Starling Marte have more career triples than Carroll. Carroll is 25 years old. He has more seasons of this pace ahead of him than behind.

Oracle Park and the 12-Game Hit Streak

The Diamondbacks are at San Francisco for three games starting May 26. Oracle Park, situated on the bay, has right-field dimensions that can play deep on certain wind days but has produced plenty of extra-base hits across its history. It is not the worst place in baseball to be a speed-first player looking for gaps.

Carroll enters the series with a 12-game hit streak — the longest active streak in the major leagues at the time he reached 51 career triples. He is seeing the ball well, running at full speed, and playing the best baseball of what has already been a very good career.

One triple. That is all that stands between Corbin Carroll and the D-backs franchise record. Oracle Park has three opportunities to deliver it.

What's Next

The Diamondbacks play at San Francisco through Thursday before returning to the road. Carroll's pursuit of Stephen Drew's franchise record is the most immediate storyline in Arizona's season, but it also previews what could be a larger record chase: at his current pace, Carroll is not just approaching the franchise mark — he is on track to eventually own the MLB record for career triples by an active player, a conversation that begins once he breaks Drew's mark and keeps running.
