Jacob Misiorowski Sets Statcast Era Record: 57 Pitches at 100+ MPH Against the Cardinals

By Marcus Cole

May 27, 2026

The 24-year-old Brewers righty broke the record by 10 pitches on Memorial Day while also matching his career high in strikeouts. Nobody in the Statcast era has come close to this number — and he shrugged it off.

On the afternoon of Memorial Day, Jacob Misiorowski walked into American Family Field in Milwaukee and proceeded to do something that has never happened before in the era of MLB pitch tracking. He threw 57 pitches at 100 miles per hour or faster against the St. Louis Cardinals — shattering the previous Statcast era record by ten pitches, matching his career high with 12 strikeouts, and leading the Brewers to a 5-1 victory.

The previous record was 47. It was set by Hunter Greene of the Cincinnati Reds on September 17, 2022. Misiorowski erased it without ceremony.

The Numbers That Define the Record

To understand the scale of what Misiorowski accomplished, here is the breakdown of his 96-pitch outing against St. Louis:

Fifty-seven of those 96 pitches crossed the plate at 100 mph or faster. Forty reached 101 mph. Twenty-two touched 102. Nine exceeded 103. His top velocity of 103.4 mph was reached three separate times — not once, not as a single adrenaline spike, but as a consistent output that appeared regularly throughout a full afternoon of pitching.

That kind of sustained elite velocity across more than 90 pitches is what makes this record different from a single high-end reading. Greene's 2022 record was already remarkable. Misiorowski's performance was in a different category entirely.

12 Strikeouts, 100 on the Season

The velocity figures get all the attention, but Misiorowski's command and results completed the picture. He matched his career high with 12 strikeouts and finished with Milwaukee's fifth win, built on an outing where St. Louis could not put together any sustained offensive pressure.

The 12-strikeout game pushed Misiorowski to 100 strikeouts in just 11 starts in 2026. That pace — a strikeout rate above 9 per start — is the kind of production that earns consideration for year-end awards. He is 24 years old and appears to be pitching his best baseball at the point in the season when the schedule gets harder and the weather gets hotter.

The Brewers sit in strong position in the NL Central, and Misiorowski is a central reason for that standing. On days he takes the mound, opposing managers face a fundamentally different problem than they do against most starters: elite velocity that does not diminish with the pitch count, paired with strikeout ability that eliminates the contact game as an option.

The Durability Question

The reaction from inside the Brewers' organization has been measured. The record is one thing; the sustainability of this approach is another. Throwing this kind of velocity at this volume is something the sport has not seen before, and the long-term implications for Misiorowski's arm are a legitimate topic of conversation.

For now, the Brewers are watching closely and trusting that a pitcher who has accumulated 100 strikeouts in 11 starts without apparent discomfort is doing something right. The record-setting game was a single data point in an ongoing process — and Misiorowski gave the clearest possible indication of his own perspective when asked about it afterward. Four words. Casual. Moving on.

That reaction says something about who he is as a pitcher, and perhaps about the Brewers' organization that developed him. The record is registered. The next start comes next.

What's Next

Jacob Misiorowski will continue to take his turn in the Brewers' rotation on a schedule that will carry Milwaukee through the summer stretch of the 2026 season. Every time he takes the mound, the 100-mph threshold and what he does with it will be part of the story. The Statcast era record is already his — the question now is what comes next.
