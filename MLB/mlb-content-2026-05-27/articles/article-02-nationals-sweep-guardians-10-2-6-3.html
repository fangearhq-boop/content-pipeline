16 Runs in Two Days: Nationals Take Two From Cleveland's AL Central Leaders

By Jake Torres

May 27, 2026

Washington walked into Cleveland and scored 10 runs on Monday, then 6 more on Tuesday. The AL Central leaders are still in front, but the Nationals just reminded everyone that no lead in baseball is safe.

The Washington Nationals went to Progressive Field in Cleveland with nothing to lose and a lot of runs to score. Over two days, they did exactly that — winning 10-2 on Monday night and 6-3 on Tuesday, putting together one of the more unexpected offensive performances of the 2026 season.

The opponent was the Cleveland Guardians, the leaders of the AL Central and one of the steadier teams in the American League this season. The Guardians entered the series with every advantage on paper. The Nationals proceeded to score 16 runs over two games.

Monday's Opener: Six Home Runs, 10 Runs, No Mercy

The series-opening 10-2 win by Washington was built on power. The Nationals hit six home runs in the game, turning what could have been a competitive series opener into something closer to a statement. Six home runs against a Cleveland staff that ranks among the better pitching units in the American League is not a number that happens often.

Luis García Jr. nearly added to the highlight reel by finishing a triple shy of hitting for the cycle — a performance that captured the quality of Washington's offensive night in a single line. The Nationals were not scraping for runs. They were hitting the ball over the fence repeatedly and with authority.

Washington's 10-2 victory gave the Nationals a winning record on the season, a threshold worth noting for a team that has spent much of the year building toward it. The win came at the expense of the AL's best team in the Central, which made it more meaningful than the standings column would suggest.

Tuesday's Follow-Up: Six More, Two in a Row

Tuesday's 6-3 win confirmed that Monday was not an accident. The Nationals scored six more, put four runs on Cleveland starter Joey Cantillo in the early innings, and held their lead the rest of the way.

Taking two straight from the AL Central leaders on the road — across two separate days, with two separate starting pitchers on both sides — is not a fluke. Washington produced something real at Progressive Field, and the Guardians, for whatever reason, did not have an answer for it.

What It Means for the Guardians

For Cleveland, the two losses represent a moment to recalibrate rather than a crisis. The Guardians entered this series with one of the American League's best records and a substantial division lead. Two games against the Nationals does not erase that — but it does serve as a reminder that no team in baseball has earned the luxury of treating any opponent as a guaranteed win.

The Guardians have been one of 2026's pleasant surprises at the top of the standings. Their rotation has been solid, their bullpen reliable, and their consistency across the schedule has built a comfortable cushion in the AL Central. Wednesday's rubber game gives them the chance to close out the series with a split and refocus on the larger picture.

What's Next

The series concludes Wednesday in Cleveland. The Nationals will look to finish the sweep; the Guardians will look to salvage the final game and move forward. For Washington, these two wins — 16 runs, six home runs, two road victories against a good team — represent the kind of performance that changes the story about what a team is capable of. The Nationals scored 16 runs in 48 hours against the AL Central's best. That is worth remembering.
