Three NL Teams Are Separating From the Pack — the 2026 Postseason Picture Is Taking Shape

By Jake Torres June 5, 2026

Three Dominant Teams, Three Different Divisions

Through approximately one-third of the 2026 MLB season, three National League teams have distinguished themselves as the clear class of their respective divisions. The Atlanta Braves are 42-20 and lead the NL East by 9.5 games. The Los Angeles Dodgers are 40-22 and lead the NL West by seven games over the San Diego Padres. The Milwaukee Brewers are 37-22 atop the NL Central. All three teams have built leads that are not safe — leads in June rarely are — but that reflect sustained excellence across nearly two months of baseball.

The Braves: Best Record in the NL

At 42-20, Atlanta has the best record in the National League and is on a pace that would translate to more than 110 wins over a full 162-game season. The Braves have been here before — they have made the postseason in consecutive years and have built a roster designed to sustain this level of play. Chris Sale's stumble on Thursday against the Blue Jays does not change the overall picture. This is a team that absorbs the bad nights and keeps moving forward.

The Dodgers: Relentless in the NL West

Los Angeles at 40-22 is running away from a division that was supposed to be competitive. The Padres at 32-28 and the Diamondbacks at 32-29 are both seven-plus games back. The Dodgers have the roster depth and rotation quality to maintain this kind of dominance without sustaining it at unsustainable levels — they can coast to a reasonable record in the second half and still arrive in October as a legitimate World Series contender.

The Brewers: Quietly Building in the NL Central

Milwaukee at 37-22 is the least-discussed of the three NL leaders, which may be the most Milwaukee thing possible. The Brewers consistently build rosters that win more than expected and find ways to compete into October without receiving the national attention that the coasts command. Their position atop the NL Central with Pittsburgh and St. Louis both five-plus games back suggests another season where Milwaukee is relevant until the end.

What's Next

The second third of the season, from June through July, will test whether these leads hold. Trade deadline decisions will reshape rosters. Injuries will alter rotations. The NL picture heading into June has these three teams at the top, and all three have the talent to still be there at the end of September. For fans of October baseball, the 2026 National League is shaping up to deliver exactly the kind of postseason field worth watching.
