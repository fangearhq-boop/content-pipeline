Blue Jays 7, Braves 2: Chad Dallas Debuts, Sale Stumbles as Toronto Hands Atlanta a Rare Beating

By Marcus Cole June 5, 2026

A Debut Worth Remembering

Chad Dallas walked into Truist Park on Thursday night having never thrown a pitch in the Major Leagues. He walked out with a debut that the Toronto Blue Jays will tell stories about for years. Dallas allowed just one earned run in 3.2 innings, then combined with five Toronto relievers to hold the Atlanta Braves — the best team in the National League — to four hits all game. The final score: Blue Jays 7, Braves 2.

For context on what four hits means: the Atlanta Braves entered Thursday at 42-20, leading the NL East by 9.5 games, with one of the most consistent offenses in baseball. Holding that lineup to four hits is a statement regardless of who is pitching. For a pitcher making his MLB debut, it is remarkable.

Sale's Rare Stumble

On the other side of the battery, Chris Sale had the kind of night that reminders you that even Cy Young contenders have bad days. Sale, who entered Thursday at 8-3 with an ERA that had kept him in award conversations all season, allowed 10 hits in 5.2 innings and left with his record at 8-4.

Myles Straw drove in three runs for Toronto on two hits, accounting for the bulk of the damage. The Blue Jays scored early and maintained a lead that Sale's outing never gave Atlanta a realistic path to close.

Context on the Braves

Atlanta's reaction to one loss at this point in the season should be approximately zero concern. The Braves are 42-20. Their lead in the NL East is 9.5 games. Sale has been one of the better pitchers in the National League all season. One rough start does not change any of those things.

What makes this loss noteworthy is not what it says about the Braves' season — it says very little — but what it says about the Blue Jays, who ended a four-game losing streak with their best pitching performance of the month. Dallas's debut added a compelling storyline to what could have been just another forgettable score.

What's Next

The Braves move forward with Sale's next start on the horizon and a rotation that has carried the team all season. For Toronto, the question is whether Thursday's performance was a sign of something sustainable or a night where everything broke right. Dallas gets to carry the memory of a debut the Blue Jays needed. Atlanta gets to forget a night that will not define their season, because they have not let anything define their season yet.
