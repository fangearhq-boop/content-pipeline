Schwarber Is Chasing the 60-HR Club — And It's Not as Crazy as It Sounds
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Let's talk about what Kyle Schwarber is doing — and what it would mean if he keeps doing it. Schwarber has 22 home runs on the season. He hit No. 20 on May 15, the earliest date in Major League Baseball history that anyone has reached that milestone. He is currently on pace for 66 home runs over a full season. Here is why that number matters: the 60-home run club has exactly three members. Barry Bonds hit 73 in 2001 and is the all-time single-season record holder. Mark McGwire hit 70 in 1998 and 65 in 1999. Sammy Sosa hit 66 in 1998, 64 in 2001, and 63 in 1999. That is it. No one else. Ever. Schwarber is in that conversation right now. In early June. He is not just on a 50-HR pace — he is on the kind of pace that puts him in one of the most exclusive clubs in baseball history. Obviously, sustaining this pace for five more months is incredibly difficult. But for right now, in June, what Schwarber is doing is genuinely unprecedented territory and worth paying attention to every single day. Are you watching Schwarber's at-bats this season? You should be. ### Image Post Caption Kyle Schwarber: 22 HRs, on pace for 66. The 60-HR club has exactly 3 members. He's in the conversation. ⚾ #Phillies #MLB
