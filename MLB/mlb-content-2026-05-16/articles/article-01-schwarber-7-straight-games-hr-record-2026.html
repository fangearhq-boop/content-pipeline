Schwarber Hits Nos. 19 and 20, Extends Streak to 7 Straight — Tonight He Can Tie the MLB Record

By Marcus Cole | May 16, 2026

Kyle Schwarber hit two home runs in a comeback win over the Pittsburgh Pirates on Friday night, and now he is one game away from something that has happened only three times in the history of major league baseball.

Seven consecutive games with a home run. Twenty home runs in 45 games this season. And tonight — in game 8 of the streak — Schwarber has the chance to tie the all-time MLB record of eight consecutive games with a home run, a mark achieved by Dale Long in 1956, Don Mattingly in 1987, and Ken Griffey Jr. in 1993.

Don Mattingly is the current manager of the Philadelphia Phillies. Kyle Schwarber can tie his own manager's record tonight.

What Happened Friday

The Phillies trailed the Pirates and needed a rally. Schwarber delivered two swings that will look different by Sunday depending on how tonight's game goes. Home run No. 19 extended the streak to six — already a rare achievement. Home run No. 20 extended it to seven and pushed Schwarber into company almost no one occupies.

The Phillies won 11-9 in 10 innings. The comeback was a team effort. But the two-homer night from Schwarber was the individual performance that rewrites the storyline of this week.

The Record He's Chasing

Eight consecutive games with at least one home run. Only three players have done it. Dale Long did it first, for the Pittsburgh Pirates, over a stretch that ran from May 19-28, 1956. Mattingly matched it with the New York Yankees in July 1987. Griffey Jr. tied it with the Seattle Mariners in 1993.

Mattingly's streak came before the All-Star break that year. He homered in five straight games before the Midsummer Classic, then in three more coming out of the break. The baseball world remembers it as one of the great individual offensive stretches of the late 1980s.

Now his own player — 39 years later — is knocking on the door of his record.

"It's one of those things where you just try to keep it going," Schwarber has said of hot streaks throughout his career. For this particular run, keeping it going means making history.

The Context: A Season Like No Other

Before this seven-game stretch, Schwarber was already having the best power season of his career. He is the first player in the majors to reach 20 home runs this season, having done so in his team's 45th game. Kyle Schwarber and Cy Williams in 1923 are the only Phillies to hit 20 home runs in the first 45 games of a season.

His current pace projects to somewhere in the mid-60s for home runs over a full 162-game season — a number that would place him in elite historical company. Whether the pace holds is another question. Whether the streak continues tonight is the only question that matters right now.

What's Next

Tonight, the Phillies play game 2 of their current series. Schwarber will be in the lineup. Don Mattingly will be in the dugout. The all-time consecutive-game home run record will be one swing away from being tied. If Schwarber connects, he joins Long, Mattingly, and Griffey Jr. as the only players to accomplish this feat. Game 9 would mean breaking it outright. Baseball at its most compelling happens one at-bat at a time, and right now every at-bat Kyle Schwarber takes is appointment viewing.
