Cubs Erupt for 10 Runs at Rate Field, Ending White Sox Winning Streak in Crosstown Classic Opener

By Ryan Calloway | May 16, 2026

The 2026 Crosstown Classic opened on the South Side, and the Chicago Cubs came in and claimed it immediately.

Carson Kelly drove in four runs, delivering a tiebreaking single in the seventh inning that broke a competitive game open as the Cubs defeated the White Sox 10-5 at Rate Field on Friday night. The win ended Chicago's 5-game White Sox winning streak and gave the North Siders the early edge in a rivalry series that had genuine energy on both sides of the city entering the weekend.

Kelly Delivers the Deciding Blow

The Crosstown Classic wasn't a blowout from the start. The White Sox had won five straight games and were playing at home in front of their own fans. The game stayed competitive into the middle innings before Kelly's tiebreaking single in the seventh inning changed the trajectory for good.

Kelly's four-RBI night carried the Cubs offense at the moments when it mattered most. The Cubs lineup accumulated 10 runs total, a strong output for a team that had been working through a difficult recent stretch in the rotation and at the plate.

What This Rivalry Means in 2026

The Crosstown Classic has not always carried the weight its name implies. For much of the last decade, one side of Chicago or the other was so far out of playoff contention that the series became more civic curiosity than genuine competition. Block Club Chicago captured the sentiment well heading into this series: both sides of the city arrived with real investment in the outcome — "the most alive the rivalry has felt in years," as one reporter put it.

The Cubs, at 28-plus games into a solid season, are a team with legitimate NL Central aspirations. The White Sox's 5-game winning streak entering Friday showed a team capable of sustained momentum. That context makes Game 1 feel like more than just a regular series opener.

The Cubs took it. That matters.

What's Next

The Crosstown Classic continues Saturday with Game 2 at Rate Field. The Cubs hold a 1-0 series lead after Friday's win. The White Sox will need to bounce back in front of their home fans to keep the series competitive before the finale. Game 3 rounds out the weekend before both teams return to divisional play. For a Rivalry Weekend that was already delivering across the league, the Crosstown Classic opener set the stage for what could be a genuinely compelling three-game series. Saturday's pitching matchup will determine whether this stays close or turns into a North Side statement weekend.
