Braves Win 3-2 Over Red Sox; Matt Olson's Iron Man Streak Reaches 826 Consecutive Games

By Jake Torres | May 16, 2026

The Atlanta Braves beat the Boston Red Sox 3-2 on Friday night, adding another win to a season that has been defined by consistency, depth, and quiet dominance at the top of the National League East.

And while the final score was close, the most significant number attached to the Braves on Friday had nothing to do with runs or innings. Matt Olson played in game No. 826 of his consecutive-games streak, a run that dates to May 2, 2021 and stands as one of the longest active consecutive-games streaks in major league history.

826 and Counting

Five-plus years of showing up every day. That's what Olson's streak represents. Through lineup decisions, minor injuries that would have kept other players out, road trips, extra-inning games, and the full grind of modern baseball, Olson has been in the Braves starting lineup for 826 consecutive games.

The consecutive-games played record in major league history belongs to Cal Ripken Jr., whose streak of 2,632 games with the Baltimore Orioles is one of the most revered records in the sport. Ripken's standard is unlikely to be approached in the modern era. But 826 consecutive games is significant — a top-10 mark in major league history among all players who have ever done this, according to the 2026 MLB Milestones tracker.

Olson plays first base for a team that competes for the playoffs every year. He hits for power, drives in runs consistently, and provides one of the most reliable defensive presences at his position in the game. And he has done it without missing a start since May 2021.

The Braves' Bigger Picture

Atlanta's 3-2 win over Boston keeps them at the top of the NL East standings. They remain among the most accomplished teams in the National League through the first six weeks of the season — leading in run prevention and producing consistently across their roster.

The NL East race is one of the more competitive divisional races in baseball as the Phillies continue to surge under manager Don Mattingly and the Cubs push from the NL Central. But the Braves are the benchmark — the team everyone else is measuring themselves against.

What's Next

The Braves continue their road series against the Red Sox on Saturday. Olson will take the field again — as he has every day for more than five years — and the streak will grow by one more. Most fans won't notice. That might be the point. Durability, unlike the dramatic, rarely announces itself. But 826 consecutive games into an ongoing streak, Olson's presence in the lineup is both a record worth celebrating and a daily reminder of what sustained excellence over years actually looks like.
