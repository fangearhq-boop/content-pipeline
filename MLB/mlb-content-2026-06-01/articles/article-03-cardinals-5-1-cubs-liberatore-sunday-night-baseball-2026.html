Liberatore Silences the Doubters as Cardinals Top Cubs 5-1 on Sunday Night Baseball

By Ryan Calloway | June 1, 2026

Matthew Liberatore had questions to answer. He had allowed at least three runs in each of his previous three starts, and Sunday Night Baseball on NBC brought the brightest spotlight of the regular season. He answered the questions definitively.

Liberatore worked 5.1 scoreless innings, allowing three hits and a walk, and the St. Louis Cardinals beat the Chicago Cubs 5-1 to win the weekend series. The Cardinals entered Sunday's game on a four-game losing streak. They left with a series win and some renewed confidence in their rotation.

Liberatore's Bounce-Back Performance

The 24-year-old left-hander moved to 3-3 on the season after Sunday's outing — and the manner in which he pitched was as important as the result. He kept the Cubs' lineup off-balance all evening, working efficiently and avoiding the big inning that had derailed his previous appearances.

Hunter Dobbins, who had been recalled from Triple-A Memphis earlier in the day, came on in relief and threw 3.2 innings — allowing one run on four hits with four strikeouts. For a pitcher making his first big-league appearance in circumstances like that, the outing was impressive. He earned his first professional save.

The Cardinals' Young Core Delivers

Offensively, this was a game where the Cardinals' developing young players made the plays that mattered. Masyn Winn drove in two with a single. JJ Wetherholt and Iván Herrera each collected two hits and scored twice. Alec Burleson added two more runs batted in.

The Cardinals entered the series on a four-game losing streak, which made Sunday's series win feel significant. This is a team that has the pieces to compete in the NL Central when they put things together, and Sunday's performance showed the best version of how they can operate — pitching holding, the young hitters delivering, and a bullpen arm coming through on short notice.

The Cubs' Frustrating Night

Alex Bregman homered in the seventh for Chicago — one of the few encouraging moments in the Cubs' loss. Jordan Wicks (0-2) was charged with three runs and four hits before getting the hook after two-plus innings, and the Cubs' offense couldn't get enough going to offset the early deficit.

The Cubs are 3-2 in their last five games after a 10-game losing skid. That's progress, but Sunday's loss means they gave back a game in the NL Central standings. Bregman's homer keeps him relevant in the Cubs' lineup, but the rotation and offense have been too inconsistent to generate sustained momentum.

What's Next

The Cardinals take a series win and a rotation boost into the week ahead. If Liberatore can build on Sunday's performance and the young offense keeps producing, St. Louis has the profile of a team that can move in the standings.

For the Cubs, the NL Central race remains the defining context. They need the rotation to stabilize and the lineup — particularly Bregman — to produce consistently. Sunday was another reminder that neither is guaranteed.
