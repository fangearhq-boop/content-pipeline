Seven Years in the Making: Ryan Ward Hits First Career Homer in Dodgers' 9-1 Rout

By Jake Torres | June 1, 2026

Ryan Ward has spent seven years in the Los Angeles Dodgers' organization. He is 28 years old. He has been called up, sent back down, and called up again — the kind of career path that most baseball fans never think about, sustained by the belief that the moment would eventually come.

On Sunday afternoon at Dodger Stadium, with Yoshinobu Yamamoto dominant on the mound and the Phillies unable to generate anything against the Los Angeles lineup, that moment arrived. Ward stepped in against Andrew Painter in the fourth inning and drove a pitch 400 feet to right field — an exit velocity of 108.4 mph — for the first home run of his major league career.

The Dodgers won 9-1. They took the series 2-1 from Philadelphia. And Ryan Ward finally had the moment he had been building toward for seven years.

Yamamoto's Best Outing of 2026

The game was never really in doubt once Yamamoto found his rhythm. The Dodgers' ace worked 5.1 scoreless innings and struck out 10 — a season high — finishing with a 2.48 ERA for the year. His command was excellent, his secondary pitches worked consistently, and he gave the offense every opportunity to build a cushion.

The offense obliged. The Dodgers scored in seven consecutive innings, collecting contributions from Ward, Alex Freeland (who homered and drove in two runs), and Max Muncy (another long ball). The nine-run total was the kind of offensive display that reflected a team firing on all cylinders after a Saturday setback.

Bryson Stott homered in the ninth inning for Philadelphia to prevent a shutout — a consolation note in a game that was decided long before.

The Ward Story

Ward had appeared in only two major league games before Sunday, going 2-for-6. His most recent callup came because injuries to Teoscar Hernandez (hamstring) and Kiké Hernandez created an opening in the Dodgers' outfield, and the organization turned to the player who had developed quietly in their system for years.

Hitting his first career homer at Dodger Stadium — in his home ballpark, in a meaningful game, in front of the home crowd — is the kind of debut moment that carries weight for any player. For Ward, who had made seven years of his baseball career in the organization's minor league system, it carried more weight than most.

The Series Context

The win gave the Dodgers the series 2-1 after Saturday's blown save by Tanner Scott in Game 2, which had ended a six-game Dodgers winning streak. Sunday's dominant outing served as the corrective — a reminder that the Dodgers' rotation and lineup are capable of imposing their will when everything is functioning.

The Phillies leave Dodger Stadium with a split record — Tanner Scott's blown save in Game 2 cost them the series — and Kyle Schwarber's home run pace remains the defining storyline of their season regardless of this road trip result.

What's Next

The Dodgers return to their home schedule with momentum from Sunday's win and Yamamoto's best start of the year. Ryan Ward's role in the roster going forward will depend on the health of the players he is covering for — but Sunday showed why the organization kept him in their system through seven seasons of waiting.

As for Yamamoto: a 10-strikeout, 5.1-inning, zero-run outing is the kind of start that anchors a rotation and sets the tone for a month. The Dodgers will take it.
