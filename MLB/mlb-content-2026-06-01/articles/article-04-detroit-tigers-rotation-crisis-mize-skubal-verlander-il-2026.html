Three Starters, One IL: Detroit's Rotation Crisis Deepens With Mize Injury

By Marcus Cole | June 1, 2026

The Detroit Tigers have Casey Mize, Tarik Skubal, and Justin Verlander on the injured list. At the same time. The three pitchers who were supposed to anchor a competitive rotation are now all unavailable, and the franchise is navigating a pitching emergency that could define its season.

Mize was placed on the 15-day IL on Friday, retroactive to May 28, with right adductor inflammation. He joins Skubal (elbow surgery, conservatively out through July) and Verlander (hip, eligible to return after a Triple-A rehab start) as the three starters currently unavailable to the major league roster.

Each Injury in Context

Tarik Skubal's situation is the most serious in terms of timeline. The ace underwent surgery to remove a loose body from his left elbow. The procedure used a NanoNeedle — a device smaller than traditional arthroscopy that is expected to minimize swelling and recovery time. Skubal is the first MLB player to have the surgery performed with this technology. The conservative timeline puts him out through July, though the hope is that the smaller procedure allows a faster recovery than standard elbow operations.

Justin Verlander is closer to a return. The future Hall of Famer was eligible to come off the 60-day IL at the end of May and is scheduled for a rehab assignment with the Triple-A Toledo Mud Hens before returning to the rotation. Detroit is hoping to get him back on the major league mound in June, which would provide a significant boost to a rotation that desperately needs it.

Mize's adductor injury adds a new layer of complexity. The right-hander's timeline is uncertain, and losing him while the team waits for Verlander and Skubal means the 2-through-4 spots in the rotation are currently covered by depth options.

What It Means for Detroit's Season

The Tigers entered 2026 with genuine playoff aspirations. A rotation anchored by Skubal, Verlander, and Mize was supposed to be a strength. Instead, injuries have rendered that group unavailable for an extended stretch of the schedule, and the team is relying on alternatives while waiting for reinforcements.

Verlander's return is the most important development to watch. Even at this stage of his career, a healthy Verlander stabilizes a rotation and changes the calculus for opposing offenses. If Detroit can get him back in June as expected, and if Mize's recovery is manageable, the franchise still has a path through its current difficulties.

Skubal's absence through July is the harder problem. He was the centerpiece of Detroit's pitching plans, and a mid-summer return at best means the Tigers will need to compete without their best starter for a significant portion of the first half.

What's Next

All eyes are on Verlander's rehab timeline with Toledo. If the start goes well and there are no setbacks, Detroit could have their veteran back in the rotation within weeks. That would shift the rotation crisis from critical to manageable.

For now, Detroit is playing with a depleted rotation while asking their offense to carry more of the load. How they navigate the next 6 to 8 weeks will go a long way toward determining what this team's 2026 actually becomes.
