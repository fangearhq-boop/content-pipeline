NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post J.T. Ginn threw 8 no-hit innings last night. 105 pitches. 10 strikeouts. A 1-0 lead. He fanned the side in the seventh. He was in complete control. Then the ninth inning happened. Adam Frazier led off with an infield single — the first hit of the game, killing the no-hitter in an instant. Three pitches later, Zach Neto drove a 2-0 sinker 413 feet to center field. Two-run walk-off homer. Angels 2, Athletics 1. Ginn's masterpiece evaporated in the span of one inning. What makes this so remarkable: according to baseball historians, this is only the third time since 1961 that a team has been no-hit through at least 8 innings and still won — let alone on a walk-off homer. The previous instances were Don Lock of the Washington Senators in 1963 and Josh Harrison of the Pittsburgh Pirates in 2017 (extras). Ginn became just the sixth MLB pitcher since 1974 to allow no hits and no runs through 8 innings and still take the loss. For the Angels, the win snapped a six-game losing streak. And it preserved a streak that most fans don't know about: Los Angeles hasn't been no-hit since September 11, 1999 — the longest active no-hit drought in the majors. Zach Neto's walk-off blast. One of the most remarkable single-game moments of the 2026 season. ⚾ ### Image Post Caption J.T. Ginn was 8 innings into a no-hitter with a 1-0 lead. The Angels won 2-1 on a Zach Neto walk-off homer. Baseball doesn't get more dramatic than this. ⚾ #Angels #Athletics #MLB

NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Let's talk about what Kyle Schwarber is doing — because the numbers are historically rare. Schwarber hit his 20th home run of the 2026 season in the Phillies' 49th game. That makes him just the 18th player in MLB history to reach 20 home runs that quickly. The last player to do it was Josh Hamilton in 2012. And here's where the comparison gets truly staggering: the only players in the modern era who have been BETTER than Schwarber's current pace through 49 games are Barry Bonds in 2001 (25 HRs) and Mark McGwire in 1998 (25 HRs). Those are the two seasons that produced the single-season home run records of 73 and 70. Schwarber's current pace projects to 66 home runs for the 2026 season. With Munetaka Murakami (who had tied Schwarber at the top of the HR leaderboard before going down with a hamstring injury) expected out until mid-June, Schwarber stands alone as the MLB home run leader. The nearest challengers are nowhere close. Can he sustain it? That's the only question. Big power starts don't always hold. But right now, Kyle Schwarber is doing something that only two players in baseball history have done better — and those two players are in the record books. ⚾ ### Image Post Caption 20 HRs in 49 games.
