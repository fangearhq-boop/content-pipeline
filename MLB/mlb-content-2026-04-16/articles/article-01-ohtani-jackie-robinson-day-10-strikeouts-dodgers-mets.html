Ohtani's Jackie Robinson Day Masterpiece — 10 Ks, Pitcher Only, Dodgers Sweep Mets

By Marcus Cole April 16, 2026

A Different Kind of Ohtani Start

There was something slightly unusual about Shohei Ohtani's Jackie Robinson Day start at Dodger Stadium on Wednesday. He was on the mound — and nowhere else. The two-way phenom who has spent the last several years redefining what a baseball player can be was limited to his pitching role only, the result of a hit-by-pitch on his right shoulder from Mets starter David Peterson earlier in the series.

It was the first time since May 28, 2021, that Ohtani had pitched in a game without also appearing in the lineup as a designated hitter. Manager Dave Roberts acknowledged the simple logic: absent the injury, Ohtani would have been both pitching and hitting. "If it weren't for the hit by pitch, he would've been DHing and pitching tonight," Roberts said.

Ohtani responded to his one-dimensional limitation the only way he knows how: by being dominant on the mound. Six innings, one run, two hits, two walks, 10 strikeouts on 95 pitches. In the sixth inning, he struck out the side on just 14 pitches. The Dodgers won 8-2, completing the sweep of the New York Mets.

The Supporting Cast Came Through

While Ohtani anchored the game on the mound, the Dodgers' offense delivered around him. Hyeseong Kim provided early offense with a two-run home run, and Teoscar Hernandez added another blast to extend the lead. Dalton Rushing and Kyle Tucker blew the game open late with additional run support, turning a competitive ballgame into a comfortable final margin.

The combination of Ohtani's pitching and a lineup that can run up the score on any given night is what makes the Dodgers feel different from other championship contenders. Even without Ohtani contributing at the plate, Los Angeles found a way to win by six runs. The depth is genuine, and it shows in margins like this.

What the Start Means

Wednesday's performance was Ohtani's third start of the 2026 season. He has now completed six innings in each of his three outings. The question of what his role will look like this year — two-way player with full DH duties, or primarily a pitcher during stretches where his body requires it — has no clean answer yet. This was an unusual circumstance, not a change in philosophy.

For the record, a pitcher-only Ohtani striking out 10 batters in six innings is a reminder that even when you remove half of what makes him historically unique, the other half is still elite. The 10 strikeouts were a statement on a day that had plenty of them.

What's Next

Ohtani is expected to return to his full two-way role once his shoulder recovers from the hit-by-pitch. His fourth start of the season will come in approximately five days. The Dodgers, meanwhile, have swept a series against the Mets and strengthened their case as the league's best team in the early weeks of 2026. They will continue their homestand before heading on the road later this month.
