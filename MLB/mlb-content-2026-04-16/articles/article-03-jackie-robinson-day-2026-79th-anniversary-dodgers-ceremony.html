Jackie Robinson Day 2026 — 79 Years, No. 42, and a Ceremony at Dodger Stadium

By Ryan Calloway April 16, 2026

A Date That Still Carries Weight

April 15, 1947. Jackie Robinson walked onto a major league field for the first time in a Brooklyn Dodgers uniform, breaking a color barrier that had stood for more than six decades. Seventy-nine years later, Major League Baseball stopped to remember.

Every player, manager, and coach across all 30 teams wore No. 42 on April 15, 2026 — the annual ritual that turns the entire sport into a tribute to what Robinson meant to the game and to the country. The gesture is no longer novel; it has been part of the baseball calendar long enough that younger fans have grown up with it as a fixture. But familiarity has not diminished the significance. Every year, No. 42 is everywhere, and every year it means something.

The Ceremony at Dodger Stadium

Dodger Stadium is the right place for the most prominent Jackie Robinson Day game. The Dodgers are Robinson's franchise — his name is on the field, his statue stands outside the stadium, and his legacy is woven into the identity of the organization. Wednesday's ceremony before the Dodgers-Mets game honored that connection.

Bob Kendrick, president of the Negro Leagues Baseball Museum in Kansas City, participated in the pregame ceremony at the Jackie Robinson statue in Centerfield Plaza. Robinson's granddaughters, Sonya Pankey Robinson and Ayo Robinson, were also part of the tribute. Joe Buck, Ron Darling, and Orel Hershiser called the nationally televised game on ESPN, bringing the broadcast gravitas appropriate for the occasion.

The Game Itself

The Dodgers completed the Mets sweep 8-2, with Shohei Ohtani delivering a 10-strikeout performance on the mound. Hyeseong Kim and Teoscar Hernandez homered. Dalton Rushing and Kyle Tucker added late run support. It was, by any measure, a dominant home performance on a day when home was the place to be.

Robinson debuted on April 15, 1947, in a 5-3 Brooklyn Dodgers victory over the Boston Braves. Seventy-nine years later, his franchise won again on the date that bears his memory. There is no clean symmetry in sports, but sometimes the calendar offers something close.

What's Next

The annual Jackie Robinson Day celebration continues to grow as a cultural event within the sport. The 80th anniversary, in April 2027, will be another major milestone. For now, the 2026 edition served as a reminder that honoring history and playing excellent baseball are not mutually exclusive — the Dodgers demonstrated both on the same night.
