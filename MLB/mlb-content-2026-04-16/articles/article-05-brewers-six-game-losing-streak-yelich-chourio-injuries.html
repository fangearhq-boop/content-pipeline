Brewers Spiral — Six-Game Skid, Three Starters on IL, Longest Losing Streak Since 2003

By Jake Torres April 16, 2026

Injuries Are Compounding

The Milwaukee Brewers entered 2026 as a competitive NL Central franchise with a roster capable of contending for a division title. Six games into a losing streak, with their core offensive players sitting in the training room, that projection looks shaky.

Christian Yelich suffered an adductor strain and is not expected to return until mid-to-late May. Jackson Chourio broke his hand and is targeting an early May return. Andrew Vaughn also broke his hand and is on track for mid-May. Starter Quinn Priester is dealing with thoracic outlet issues, and Jared Koenig has an elbow problem. When you remove those five players from a roster, the team looks fundamentally different than what the front office assembled.

Six Losses and Counting

The Brewers' six-game losing streak is their longest since 2003 — a stretch that predates much of what Milwaukee has built in the modern era of their franchise's competitive run. Six losses in a row tests the depth of any organization, and the Brewers are navigating that test without their franchise player, their starting center fielder, and multiple pieces of their rotation.

The NL Central, which was expected to feature a genuine multi-team race, is watching Milwaukee's slide with interest. Every game the Brewers lose in April is a deficit that becomes harder to overcome as the season advances, particularly when the return timeline for their injured players pushes toward Memorial Day.

What the Path Forward Looks Like

The Brewers have organizational depth — they build rosters in ways that account for attrition, and their minor league system has produced contributors in situations like this before. But replacing Yelich's production, Chourio's speed and potential, and Vaughn's bat in the same three-week window is an enormous ask.

The front office will need to decide whether to stay patient and wait for the roster to heal, or pursue external help through trades or free agency. The pitching market still has Lucas Giolito available, which is one option if the rotation continues to struggle. Whatever the approach, the Brewers' competitive window in 2026 is being tested earlier than anyone anticipated.

What's Next

The Brewers continue their schedule while waiting on injury returns. Chourio and Vaughn are targeting early May, which means Milwaukee needs to stay close enough to the NL Central leaders over the next three weeks that they can make a run when the lineup is closer to full strength. At six losses in a row and counting, that margin for error is getting thin.
