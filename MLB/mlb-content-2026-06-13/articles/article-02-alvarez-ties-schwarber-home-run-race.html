Alvarez Homers Twice, Ties Schwarber at 24 — Home Run Race Officially On

By Jake Torres

June 13, 2026

The home run race needed a jolt, and Yordan Alvarez provided one in Kauffman Stadium on Saturday.

The Houston Astros designated hitter crushed two home runs against the Kansas City Royals — a first-inning grand slam and a solo shot — as the Astros won 10-8. The offensive eruption moved Alvarez to 24 home runs on the season, tying Philadelphia's Kyle Schwarber atop the major league leaderboard. Byron Buxton sits third with 21.

The Grand Slam Changed Everything

Alvarez's first-inning grand slam was the play that shifted momentum, both in the game and in the home run race narrative. He entered the game one behind Schwarber. He left the first inning already tied. The solo shot later in the game put an exclamation point on one of the better offensive performances of any player this season.

Going into June 13, Schwarber had led the race for much of the season, posting 23 home runs through June 12 while Alvarez sat at 22. In the span of one afternoon, the entire storyline reset.

The Derby in Schwarber's House

The Home Run Derby is scheduled for July 13 in Philadelphia. Citizens Bank Park — one of the more homer-friendly venues in the National League — is Kyle Schwarber's home stadium. The crowd will be roaring for the Phillies' left fielder on the game's biggest stage for power hitters.

Now Alvarez is tied with him, and the race into the Derby just became genuinely compelling. Aaron Judge, who held 20 home runs before his rib injury in late May, is sidelined. His absence cleared the way for a two-man race that is now perfectly even entering the final weeks before the All-Star break.

What the Numbers Say

Schwarber entered June 13 slashing .239/.358/.575 with a .933 OPS, 24 home runs, and 42 RBIs in 65 games. He has been consistent and patient, with a walk rate that keeps his on-base percentage elevated even when home runs aren't falling. His .575 slugging percentage ranks third in the major leagues.

Alvarez's power is more explosive. His grand slam Saturday showcased the raw strength that makes him one of the most dangerous hitters alive when he gets a pitch to hit. The 10-8 final score suggests the game was not a pitcher's duel, but Alvarez's two contributions were the deciding difference.

What's Next

Eighteen games remain before the All-Star break. Both Schwarber and Alvarez are on pace for seasons that could challenge 55 or 60 home runs if they maintain current rates. The Derby on July 13 will be the most-watched event of All-Star Week, and for the first time in years, there is a genuine race — not a coronation — heading into it. The tie at 24 makes the next three weeks worth watching every night.
