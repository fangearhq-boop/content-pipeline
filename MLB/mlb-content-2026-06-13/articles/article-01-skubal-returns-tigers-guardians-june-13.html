Skubal Returns: Tigers' Two-Time Cy Young Ace Back from IL Against Guardians

By Marcus Cole

June 13, 2026

Thirty-eight days after elbow surgery shut him down, Tarik Skubal is back on the mound. The two-time American League Cy Young Award winner returns to Detroit's rotation today against the Cleveland Guardians at Progressive Field, completing one of the season's most anticipated comeback arcs.

The Tigers entered 2026 as a team with a genuine question mark: could they compete in the American League without their best pitcher? Through June 12, the answer was an encouraging 7-2 run in June alone. But the real test starts today.

The Rehab Report Card

Skubal's rehab start delivered everything Detroit needed to see. Five innings, zero earned runs, six strikeouts. His command was sharp, his velocity held, and he came out of the outing without any setbacks. The Tigers moved quickly after that: he was activated from the IL, and the Saturday afternoon start at Cleveland was confirmed.

He enters the game at 3-2 with a 2.70 ERA from his work before going down. That's essentially the same Skubal — the pitcher who posted historically low ERAs en route to back-to-back Cy Young Awards. The elbow procedure appears to have fixed the issue without costing him anything in terms of performance.

The Stakes Are Larger Than the Standings

Context matters here. Tarik Skubal is a free agent after the 2026 season. The trade deadline is August 3. Every contending team in baseball has a Detroit folder on their trade board, and Skubal is the centerpiece of every conversation.

His opponent today is Joey Cantillo (4-3, 4.57 ERA), Cleveland's left-handed starter. The Guardians are a legitimate AL Central competitor. A strong performance by Skubal advances two narratives simultaneously: it tells the Tigers they can compete, and it tells every potential trade partner exactly what they'd be acquiring.

Detroit's performance without him — 7-2 in June — complicated the calculus. A team that wins without its ace is a harder sell to its own fanbase when it comes time to discuss trading him. But the Tigers' front office knows what Skubal means both to their ceiling and to the prospect capital they could acquire.

What to Watch

Pitch count management will be the first data point. Coming off an IL stint, managers typically limit starters to 80-90 pitches on their first start back. How Skubal looks through four or five innings — velocity, breaking ball sharpness, command — will tell you more than the final line.

The Tigers have Casey Mize starting Sunday in the series finale. Mize (2.27 ERA) has been excellent in Skubal's absence, which is its own interesting subplot — Detroit potentially has two elite trade candidates on the same staff.

What's Next

Today is step one of what promises to be a seven-week storyline that ends at the August 3 deadline. If Skubal is healthy and dominant, the Tigers have a decision to make about their future. If today's start raises any concerns, the calculus changes. Either way, every start Skubal makes from here through the deadline will be scrutinized at the highest level. Welcome back.
