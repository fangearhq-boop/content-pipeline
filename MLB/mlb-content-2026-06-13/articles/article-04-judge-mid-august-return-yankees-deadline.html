Judge Update: Mid-August Return Expected — and What It Means for Yankees' Deadline Math

By Marcus Cole

June 13, 2026

Aaron Judge will not be available for the August 3 trade deadline. He will likely return about two weeks after it. That gap — the space between the Yankees' deadline decisions and Judge's return — is the most interesting organizational dilemma in baseball right now.

Judge has been on the injured list since May 31 with a right rib stress fracture. Imaging will determine when the bone has healed enough for him to restart baseball activities. Manager Aaron Boone has been consistent on one point: he believes Judge comes back this season, and the organization shares that confidence. The timeline just doesn't line up with the deadline.

The Mid-August Math

A two-month recovery timeline from a late-May injury puts Judge's return somewhere in the latter half of August. The trade deadline is August 3. That is a 10-to-14-day window where the Yankees have to make potentially significant financial and prospect commitments without knowing exactly what they have when their best player gets back.

If Judge returns fully healthy and at his 2025-2026 standard, the Yankees become a different team immediately — one capable of matching any lineup in baseball. If his return is delayed or his production is limited, the deadline additions carry more weight.

The Yankees Without Judge

The case for buying at the deadline is obvious: New York is 41-26 without their franchise player and leads the AL East by a game over Tampa Bay (40-25). This is a team with genuine World Series aspirations, and they have proved they can win without their best hitter. Adding to a roster that's already winning is the smart play.

The case for patience is equally coherent. Judge's return is essentially a mid-season trade acquisition — the equivalent of landing an elite bat without giving up a single prospect. The Yankees get Judge back and the roster improves dramatically without anyone else having to join.

What New York Needs

The obvious area of addition is bullpen depth for a potential October run. Starting pitching has been serviceable, but an elite reliever — the kind that shuts down innings in high-leverage playoff situations — would make the Yankees more complete. Aroldis Chapman (0.46 ERA, 17 saves in Boston) is the most discussed closer on the market. Whether New York pursues him or other options depends heavily on whether they decide to buy aggressively or stand pat with their current core.

What's Next

Seven weeks to August 3. The Yankees will continue winning games, continue monitoring Judge's recovery, and continue evaluating the trade market. When the deadline arrives, general manager Brian Cashman will have to make a call with incomplete information. But that is the nature of the deadline — and teams that hesitate waiting for perfect clarity often miss the window entirely. New York's 41-26 record says they are one of the best teams in baseball. What they do on August 3 will say something about what they think their ceiling is.
