The Braves Are 45-23 and Baseball's Best Team — One Year After Going 76-86

By Ryan Calloway

June 13, 2026

One year ago, the Atlanta Braves were in the middle of a 76-86 season. Today, they own the best record in baseball at 45-23.

That reversal — 22 games better in the standings over the same span of the season — is not the product of an injury bouncing back or a single superstar emerging. The Braves have been collectively excellent for two solid months, and the gap between them and the rest of the National League East has grown to more than 8 games.

The Walt Weiss Effect

This is Walt Weiss's first full season managing in Atlanta. The organization handed him a roster that had clearly underperformed its talent level in 2025, and the early results under his direction have been striking. The Braves have played well on the road, won close games, and shown the kind of consistent execution that separates good teams from great ones.

Weiss played for the Braves during their dynasty years in the 1990s, and the institutional knowledge he brings — understanding what winning culture at this organization looks like — appears to have translated into the clubhouse faster than anyone outside Atlanta expected.

The Pieces That Make It Work

Chris Sale is anchoring the rotation at 36 years old in one of the better stories of the 2026 season. Sale's durability was a year-to-year question for most of his career, and the fact that he is delivering at the top of Atlanta's staff this deep into the season speaks to both his commitment and the organization's investment in his health management.

Ronald Acuña Jr. has returned to the form that made him a perennial MVP candidate. The combination of elite pitching and a lineup anchored by Acuña gives Atlanta both floors and ceilings that most teams cannot match.

The Historical Context

A 45-23 record at this point in the season extrapolates to approximately 110 wins over a full 162-game schedule. The Braves are not just good — they are playing at a historically excellent pace. Whether they sustain it is the only real question facing this team. The talent is there. The execution has been there. The next test is October.

What's Next

With an 8-plus game NL East lead, Atlanta's primary focus has shifted to maintaining health and fine-tuning for the postseason. The Braves have the best record in the sport and are the clear favorite in the National League. If the pitching holds, they will be playing meaningful baseball in October — and their 2025 collapse will be nothing more than the backstory that makes the 2026 story better.
