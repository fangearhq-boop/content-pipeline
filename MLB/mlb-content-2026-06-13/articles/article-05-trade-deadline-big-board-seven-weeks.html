Trade Deadline Big Board: Seven Weeks Out, Chapman and Mize Lead a Strong Seller's Market

By Jake Torres

June 13, 2026

The August 3 trade deadline is seven weeks away, and the early framework is taking shape. The seller's market in 2026 is notably strong — multiple teams with controllable or soon-to-be-free-agent players are likely to move pieces as the deadline approaches. Here is where the landscape stands today.

Aroldis Chapman, Boston Red Sox (Closer)

Chapman is the most impactful reliever on the market and the most likely to generate a bidding war. In 19.2 innings this season, he has posted a 0.46 ERA with 17 saves. Those numbers are elite by any standard — Chapman is performing at the level of a true shutdown closer, the kind that contenders pay dearly for heading into October.

Boston is not in the playoff race, which makes them a motivated seller. Chapman will almost certainly be wearing a different uniform after August 3. The question is who pays the most prospects to get him.

Casey Mize, Detroit Tigers (Starter)

Mize is the less-discussed half of Detroit's potential deadline haul, but his numbers warrant attention: 2.27 ERA in nine starts, with a career-best 26.5 percent strikeout rate. Like Tarik Skubal, he is a free agent after 2026. The Tigers could be in a position to move two controllable rotation pieces, which would be an unusual and profitable set of circumstances.

Mize has dealt with injuries throughout his career, but when healthy, he profiles as a legitimate No. 2 or No. 3 starter for a postseason contender. Teams preparing for October rotations will take a hard look at what it costs to acquire him.

Logan Webb, San Francisco Giants (Starter)

The Giants occupy an interesting position. They are carrying significant contracts and are in a period of organizational transition. Webb has been connected to trade discussions as the Giants evaluate their competitive window. He would be one of the more interesting acquisitions on the market — a durable starter with ace potential — if San Francisco decides the timing is right to move him.

The Buyers and What They Need

The Braves (45-23), Yankees (41-26), Dodgers, and Astros all have World Series ambitions and the prospect capital to make moves. Each of them needs pitching — either bullpen depth, starting rotation upgrades, or both. The overlap between strong buyer need and strong seller availability makes 2026 look like one of the more active deadlines in recent years.

What's Next

Seven weeks is enough time for standings to shift, injuries to change calculations, and new names to emerge. The teams that identify their needs now and begin building relationships with seller front offices will be positioned best when the final days before August 3 arrive. The market is strong. The buyers are motivated. The deadline conversations have already started.
