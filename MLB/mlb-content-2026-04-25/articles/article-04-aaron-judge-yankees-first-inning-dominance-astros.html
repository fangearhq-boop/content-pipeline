Aaron Judge's First-Inning Dominance Is the Yankees' Secret Weapon — New York Hosts the Astros This Weekend

By Marcus Cole | April 25, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Of Aaron Judge's nine home runs through the first 22 games of the 2026 season, five have come in the first inning.

That is not a coincidence or a statistical quirk. It is a pattern with real implications — for opposing pitchers trying to plan their approach, for the Yankees' own game strategy, and for what the American League's best team looks like when everything is clicking. In first-inning plate appearances this season, Judge is batting .450 with a 1.726 OPS, nine hits in 20 at-bats, a walk, a double, and eight RBIs before the lineup has even turned over once.

The Yankees are 11-3 when they score first. The reason they score first so often is Aaron Judge hitting a baseball into the seats before anyone else on the team has settled into the game.

What the First-Inning Pattern Reveals

Judge's early-count aggression in 2026 reflects a conscious adjustment. Pitchers who attacked him in the first inning in previous seasons often tried to establish a fastball up before introducing breaking balls, betting that a cold hitter in his first at-bat of the game would miss on something hard. Judge has apparently decided that sitting on that early fastball and turning on it is the right approach at the start of games.

The result has been catastrophic for opposing pitching staffs. Five first-inning home runs in 22 games is an extraordinary early-count production rate. When Judge's first at-bat results in a home run, the psychological weight it places on both the opposing team and its pitcher is significant. The Yankees score, take the lead, and force their opponent to play from behind against a lineup that is capable of expanding any lead quickly.

The Yankees swept the Boston Red Sox at Fenway Park in three games during the week of April 21-23, with Judge providing early offense that the team's pitching staff then protected. At 16-9, New York holds the best record in the American League. Their offense ranks third in the sport in home runs through 22 games, with 32 long balls.

This Weekend: Judge vs. Alvarez

The Yankees host the Houston Astros this weekend in what is the marquee American League series of the final days of April. Yordan Alvarez — who is tied with Munetaka Murakami for the MLB home run lead at 11 — anchors the Houston offense, and the Astros have been one of the AL's most productive teams through the early weeks of the season.

A Yankees-Astros series in late April carries playoff preview energy. These are two teams that have met in the postseason in recent years, with history and familiarity on both sides. For Judge, a great series against Alvarez and the Astros would add another chapter to what is shaping up as a spectacular individual season — one where his first-inning production has already become a conversation point across baseball.

If the current pace holds, Judge is on track for 66 home runs across a full 162-game season. That is not a record that will fall — Roger Maris's 61 in 1961, which Judge broke in 2022, remains the standard for single-season home runs — but a pace approaching 60 home runs in the second half of a full season reminds everyone why Judge remains the most feared right-handed hitter in the sport.

What's Next

The Yankees-Astros series runs through this weekend at Yankee Stadium. Judge's first-inning at-bats will be must-watch television. If the pattern continues — five first-inning HRs in 22 games — opposing pitchers will need to find a new approach before the first pitch of the game, not after. The AL East race, with the Red Sox struggling at 9-16, is beginning to look like a Yankees division for the taking. Watch the first inning.
