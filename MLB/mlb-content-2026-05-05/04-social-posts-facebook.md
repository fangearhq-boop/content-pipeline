### STORY 2:

#### Long-Form Post -- 9:00 AM
Baseball paused on Monday to remember a legend — and then delivered a moment that felt scripted for the ages.

John Sterling, the legendary voice of the Yankees for over four decades, passed away on Monday, May 5. His home run calls — each player getting their own unique signature phrase — were as much a part of the Yankees experience as pinstripes and October baseball. "Bern Baby Bern" for Bernie Williams. "The Giambino!" for Jason Giambi. For decades, Sterling's exuberant voice was synonymous with winning.

Hours after his passing, Aaron Judge stepped into the batter's box in the first inning against the Baltimore Orioles and hit a two-run home run — his 14th of the season. Yankees broadcaster Michael Kay paused, honored his longtime partner, and delivered "THEEEE YANKEES WIN!" in tribute to Sterling.

The Yankees won 12-1, completing a four-game sweep of the Orioles in which they outscored Baltimore by 29 total runs. Judge finished 2-for-4 with four RBI. The Yankees improved to 24-11 on the season.

There will be many ways to honor John Sterling in the days and weeks ahead. But Monday night's home run, and the broadcast call that followed, was the most fitting farewell possible.

Rest in peace, John Sterling. The stadium lights never dim for a voice like his.

For now, the focus is on surgery and recovery. Skubal has shown elite resilience throughout his career. The goal: back on a big-league mound by late summer.

On May 2, Ildemaro Vargas went 0-for-4 against the Cubs at Wrigley Field, and just like that, one of the most unlikely hitting streaks in years was over.

The Arizona Diamondbacks utility player's 27-game hitting streak — baseball's longest since Trea Turner's 27-gamer that spanned the end of the 2021 season and the start of 2022 — came to an end on a Saturday afternoon in Chicago. Vargas went quietly into the night, going out the same way he spent much of the streak: without drama, without flair, as though a 27-game hitting run was simply what you did when you showed up every day.

But let's be clear about what Vargas accomplished. He broke the MLB record for the longest hitting streak by a Venezuela-born player. His streak is the second-longest in Diamondbacks franchise history, behind only Luis Gonzalez's 30-game run. He won the NL Player of the Month award for March and April, slashing .378/.398/.689 with six doubles, two triples, and six home runs. On his final streak game — May 1 — he went 4-for-4 with a walk and pushed his batting average to .404.

Nobody predicted this. Vargas entered 2026 as a depth piece, a utility man who fills in gaps. Instead, he put together one of the most memorable runs of the young season. Baseball always finds a way to surprise you.

The NL Pitcher of the Month award is just an award. But it confirms what anyone who's watched him pitch this spring already knows: Shohei Ohtani is in the middle of one of the most remarkable individual pitching stretches in baseball history.
