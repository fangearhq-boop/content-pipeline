### STORY 4:
**Headline:** Vargas Hit Streak Ends at 27 — Season's Longest Run Is Over
**Tier:** 2
**Type:** FOLLOW UP
**Priority Score:** 8
**Source Searches:** arizonasports.com, washingtonpost.com, cbssports.com

Ildemaro Vargas's 27-game hitting streak for the Arizona Diamondbacks ended on May 2 when he went 0-for-4 against the Cubs at Wrigley Field. The streak was baseball's longest since Trea Turner's 27-gamer in 2021-22, and the longest ever by a Venezuela-born player. Vargas won the NL Player of the Month award for March/April with a .378 batting average. On May 1 (his final streak game), he went 4-for-4 with a walk and raised his average to .404. The 27-game streak is second all-time in Diamondbacks history behind Luis Gonzalez's 30 in 2006.
