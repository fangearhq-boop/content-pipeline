Murakami's 5-Game HR Streak Ties White Sox Record — Tied with Judge at 14 for MLB Lead

By Marcus Cole | May 5, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Munetaka Murakami hit his 14th home run of the season on Monday — a 429-foot, two-run blast off a 98 mph fastball in the White Sox's 6-0 shutout of the Los Angeles Angels — and in doing so, extended his active home run streak to five consecutive games, tying the Chicago White Sox franchise record.

He is now tied with Aaron Judge of the New York Yankees for the MLB lead in home runs. The home run race for 2026's most remarkable individual offensive season has officially begun.

Numbers That Don't Exist

The statistics around Murakami are not normal. His first 12 extra-base hits in Major League Baseball have all been home runs — a record that no player has matched as far back as comprehensive records go, dating to at least 1900. That means Murakami has hit exactly zero doubles, zero triples, and zero non-home-run extra-base hits in his entire MLB career to this point. Every ball he's hit with extra-base power has gone over the fence.

He's on pace for more than 65 home runs. That figure, held as a near-mythic barrier in baseball history, is no longer a thought experiment. It is a realistic outcome if Murakami sustains his current rate through October.

On Monday, Murakami went 3-for-4 with two RBI and three runs scored. The five-game home run streak ties the White Sox franchise record. He finished the night with Judge, who hit his own 14th homer against the Orioles in New York.

Davis Martin's Quiet Excellence

While Murakami drew the spotlight, Davis Martin delivered one of his best outings of 2026. The right-hander worked seven shutout innings against the Angels, striking out 10 batters, walking none, and lowering his ERA to 1.64 on the season. He improved to 5-1. In a year when White Sox pitching was not projected to be a strength, Martin has emerged as a legitimate staff anchor — and the understated co-star of a White Sox team that may be more dangerous than anyone anticipated.

What's Next

The question now is whether Murakami or Judge pulls ahead first. Judge's 14th came in a 12-1 demolition of the Baltimore Orioles, a statement performance in its own right. The Yankees are 24-11. The White Sox are making their own statement from the South Side of Chicago.

Baseball has not seen a home run race with this level of intrigue this early in a season in years. Two players, two cities, two historic profiles — and the season is barely six weeks old. Keep watching.
