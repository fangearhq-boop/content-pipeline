Braves 25-10 With MLB's Best Record — Atlanta Is Building Something Special in May

By Jake Torres | May 5, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Atlanta Braves are the best team in baseball. That is not a projection or an optimistic take. It is the current state of the Major League standings.

Atlanta is 25-10 — the best record in MLB — with an 8.5-game lead in the National League East. They were the first team to reach 25 wins. They have the best ERA in the NL. They are scoring the most runs in baseball. In virtually every relevant metric that describes winning baseball teams, the Braves are at or near the top.

The Names Driving It

What makes Atlanta's start particularly impressive is that the performance is coming from multiple directions. This is not a one-star team carried by a single outlier performance.

Matt Olson has been dominant at first base, providing the kind of consistent middle-of-the-order production that makes an entire lineup function. Drake Baldwin has been the season's most surprising performer in Atlanta — an offensive force that almost nobody saw coming. Ozzie Albies has provided the steady, professional production at second base that Atlanta has come to rely on. Michael Harris II has continued his development in center field and is contributing at a genuine impact level.

That is four position players, off to MVP-caliber starts, on the same roster. The Braves don't need any one of them to be perfect. They need all of them to be very good — and that is exactly what they are delivering.

The NL East is Already a Formality

At 8.5 games back entering May, the rest of the NL East faces a significant challenge. For context, the Dodgers — the other team with World Series-caliber aspirations — finished April at 20-9, which is an excellent record and five wins behind Atlanta's pace. The Braves are not just winning. They are winning at a rate that is outpacing the rest of the league's elite teams.

May tells us very little about what October will bring. Injuries happen. Hot streaks cool. September is a different game than April. But a team that has built an 8.5-game division lead before the calendar turns is a team that has spent down its margin for error and stored advantage for later.

What's Next

The Braves will continue to play baseball while the rest of the league tries to answer a question they haven't solved yet: how do you beat Atlanta right now?

The answer, through 35 games, has been that you mostly don't. The Braves are 25-10, they have the personnel to maintain it, and they play in a division that is currently no match for them. If this holds, the Braves will have an October date circled before the rest of baseball figures out June.
