Judge vs. Trout: Two Three-Time MVPs, Four Home Runs, and One Historic Game

By Jake Torres April 15, 2026

The Game That Happened Monday

Aaron Judge hit a 456-foot two-run home run in the first inning. That set the tone. Mike Trout answered with a three-run blast in the sixth to tie things. Judge hit his second of the game in the bottom of the sixth to retake the lead. Trout hit his second in the eighth to put the Angels back ahead 10-8. Trent Grisham tied it with a two-run homer in the ninth. Jose Caballero scored on a wild pitch to end it. Yankees 11, Angels 10.

What unfolded on April 13 at Yankee Stadium was more than a wild scoring game. It was the fourth time in Major League Baseball history that two players who had each won multiple MVP awards hit at least two home runs in the same game. The three previous instances: Roy Campanella and Stan Musial in June 1956, and Roger Maris alongside Mickey Mantle twice in July 1962. Judge and Trout joined that specific list two nights ago.

What It Means for Judge

Judge's two home runs were his 46th and 47th career multi-home run games, surpassing Mickey Mantle (46) for second-most in Yankees history. Only Babe Ruth (68) has more multi-homer games in the franchise's record books. For a player already tracking toward Cooperstown, this is another data point in a career full of them.

His six home runs through 16 games project to a pace of roughly 61 over a full 162. That pace is not sustainable — no one has hit 61 home runs in the modern era — but it tells you where his swing is right now. He had a slow start by his own standards in early April, then accelerated. This was the statement game. He's locked in.

Trout's Counterargument

The best part of Monday's game, from a baseball history standpoint, is that Trout matched him. There is a narrative around Trout in his mid-30s that centers on injury and decline. His two-HR game on April 13 did not support that narrative at all. A 3-run homer that tied the game and then another in the eighth that put the Angels ahead — both were legitimate, meaningful baseball events.

Judge and Trout have been the consensus two best players in the American League for most of the past decade. Watching them trade home runs in an 11-10 game is the sport functioning at its highest level. The walk-off ending kept the drama going right through the last out.

What's Next

Judge enters the week with 6 home runs and 372 career, closing in on the 400 HR milestone that has been a background storyline all season. Trout and the Angels (currently in the AL West standings race) host the Yankees later this month for a potential rematch. If Monday set a template, that series is must-watch television. Keep an eye on the schedule.
