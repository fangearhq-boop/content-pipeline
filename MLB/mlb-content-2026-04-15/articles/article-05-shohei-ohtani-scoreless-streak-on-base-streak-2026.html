28.2 Scoreless Innings and Matching Ichiro — Ohtani's 2026 Start Is Historic on Both Sides

By Jake Torres April 15, 2026

The Pitching Numbers

Shohei Ohtani returned to the mound in 2026 after missing the 2024 pitching season following Tommy John surgery. Two starts into his comeback, he has not allowed an earned run in 12 innings this year. His career scoreless innings streak as a pitcher currently stands at 28 and two-thirds innings, the longest active streak in Major League Baseball. He has been the most difficult pitcher to score against in the sport for a sustained period that includes the last month of his pre-surgery work and now the start of his return.

The first start of 2026 came on April 1 against Cleveland: 6 innings, 1 hit, 0 earned runs, 6 strikeouts, 3 walks. The second came at home later in April: another 6 innings, 0 runs, continuing the streak. Both starts came with two months of rust theoretically on his mechanics. He didn't look rusty.

The On-Base Streak

As a hitter, Ohtani's on-base streak has reached 43 consecutive games. That matches Ichiro Suzuki's 2009 mark for the longest on-base streak ever recorded by a Japanese-born player in Major League Baseball. Ichiro hit safely in over 200 games multiple times and owns the single-season hits record. His 43-game on-base streak was considered a benchmark that might stand for a very long time. Ohtani is at that number right now.

The on-base streak reflects Ohtani's remarkable combination of power and contact ability as a hitter. He is not manufacturing on-base appearances through walk-first approaches or bunt singles. He is hitting for power, taking walks when pitchers go around him, and refusing to go hitless for more than six weeks of baseball.

The Two-Way Context

Every elite pitcher produces dominant stretches. Every elite hitter has on-base streaks. What makes Ohtani's numbers different is that he is doing both at the same time, in the same season, for the same team. No other active player comes close to this profile. The Dodgers, who entered the day with a 12-4 record and the best mark in baseball, are performing at this level with Ohtani serving as both their ace on his pitching starts and a top-of-the-order threat in every lineup he is in.

His third start of 2026 will come within the next week. The scoreless streak will be tested then by a lineup that has had time to analyze his mechanics and sequencing. But through two starts, there is nothing on film that suggests a scoring opportunity is coming soon.

What's Next

Ohtani's third pitching start of 2026 is scheduled for the coming week, with the Dodgers continuing to manage his workload through the first half of the season. As a hitter, his on-base streak will be one of the most-watched numbers in baseball through the summer. Ichiro's record is tied. Whether Ohtani extends it depends on what happens in the next handful of games. Watch the box scores closely.
