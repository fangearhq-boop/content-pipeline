Yelich, Chourio, Vaughn — The Brewers' April Injury Crisis Is Getting Worse

By Marcus Cole April 15, 2026

The Injury List

Christian Yelich went on the injured list Tuesday, retroactive to Monday, with a second-degree adductor strain. The Brewers expect him to miss at least a month and return sometime in mid-to-late May. Yelich was batting .314 with 10 RBIs through his first 15 games. He was good. Now he is gone for four-plus weeks.

Yelich is not the only piece missing. Jackson Chourio, who represented the future of the Milwaukee lineup, has a broken bone in his left hand and is expected back in early May. Andrew Vaughn also has a broken hand — his could keep him out until mid-May. Quinn Priester, a starting pitcher, is dealing with thoracic outlet syndrome with no clear return date. Reliever Jared Koenig is out with an elbow issue. Milwaukee is navigating a roster that looks nothing like the one they planned to run in April.

Six Straight Losses

The losing streak has reached six games, the longest for Milwaukee since 2003. That was 23 years ago. This is not how the Brewers drew it up. They came into 2026 as a genuine NL Central contender with an exciting young core around Chourio and a veteran leader in Yelich. Now both are on the IL, and the team is playing its worst stretch in over two decades.

The timing compounds the damage. April is when pace-setters in division races establish leads that are hard to overcome in August and September. Every game Milwaukee loses now while their stars are out is a deficit they have to erase later. The NL Central does not have an obvious runaway favorite, but the window for the Brewers to be in that conversation is narrowing by the day.

Can They Survive Until May?

The roster they are running right now lacks the punch of the team built over the offseason. The pitching staff, even without Priester, has capable arms. But a lineup missing Yelich, Chourio, and Vaughn simultaneously is not the lineup that was supposed to carry Milwaukee through the first month. The bench depth and options in Triple-A Nashville will determine whether the Brewers tread water or fall further behind.

Early May is the target date for the return of Chourio and, in theory, the beginning of a recovery phase. Yelich should be back around Memorial Day. The two-week window between now and Chourio's return is the critical stretch — if Milwaukee can limit the damage to three or four games lost, the season is still very much in play. If they lose eight of ten during this stretch, the math gets harder.

What's Next

The Brewers host the Toronto Blue Jays for a three-game series starting Tuesday. These are the games that define early-season character. Without their core, Milwaukee needs pitching performances and unexpected contributions from their bench. Chourio and Vaughn both targeted early May for return. Yelich points to mid-to-late May. Watch the standings in the NL Central over the next two weeks to see how much ground Milwaukee gives back before reinforcements arrive.
