### STORY 1: Jackie Robinson Day 2026

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Today is Jackie Robinson Day. ⚾

On April 15, 1947 — 79 years ago today — Jackie Robinson ran onto Ebbets Field in Brooklyn and changed Major League Baseball forever. He was the first Black player in the modern era of the sport. He endured abuse, hostility, and pressure that no player should have to bear. And he excelled through all of it, winning Rookie of the Year in 1947, batting champion, two-time stolen base champion, and the 1949 NL MVP.

In 2004, MLB made it official: April 15 is Jackie Robinson Day. Every player, coach, manager, and umpire wears No. 42 today. It is the only number retired across the entire league. It belongs to everyone now.

Tonight, the most appropriate stage in baseball is set: the Los Angeles Dodgers — the franchise that signed Robinson, that first integrated the national pastime — host the New York Mets exclusively on ESPN at 10 PM ET. Joe Buck, Ron Darling, and Orel Hershiser are in the booth.

The Dodgers are 17-4 on April 15 since this became a league-wide event. That number feels right.

Take a moment today to remember why No. 42 means what it means.

### STORY 2: Judge-Trout HR Duel

#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET

If you didn't watch the Yankees-Angels game on April 13, you missed one for the ages. ⚾

Aaron Judge and Mike Trout, both three-time MVPs, each hit two home runs in the same game. Judge opened with a 456-foot two-run blast in the first inning. Trout answered with a three-run bomb in the sixth. Judge hit his second to reclaim the lead immediately after. Trout hit his second in the eighth to put the Angels back on top 10-8. Then Trent Grisham hit a two-run homer in the ninth to tie it, and the Yankees won on a wild pitch.

What happened was historically rare. It was only the fourth time in MLB history that two players who had won multiple MVP awards each hit two or more home runs in the same game. The previous instances: Roy Campanella and Stan Musial in June 1956, and Roger Maris with Mickey Mantle twice in July 1962.

For Judge specifically, it was his 47th career multi-home run game, surpassing Mickey Mantle (46) for second-most in Yankees history. Only Babe Ruth has more, with 68. Judge now has six home runs through 16 games, a pace of 60-plus for the season.

Two of the best players in the game, going back and forth, in an 11-10 thriller. That's the game.

### STORY 3: Orioles Manager Albernaz

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

Here is the toughest-man-in-baseball story of the 2026 season. ⚾

During the Orioles game against Arizona on Monday, Baltimore manager Craig Albernaz was struck in the face by a 70.6 mph line drive off the bat of his own hitter, Jeremiah Jackson. He was standing on the top step of the dugout, away from the protective netting. The ball caught him directly on the right cheek.

Medical evaluation revealed the damage: a broken jaw and seven facial fractures. No surgery needed. His jaw did not need to be wired shut. But he is on a baby-food and liquid diet for the foreseeable future.

Albernaz returned to the dugout that same game. Baltimore beat Arizona 9-7. The next day, he came back to manage again. He stood in the exact same spot on the top step.

That is the energy that filters down to a clubhouse. The team sees that, and they know exactly what kind of manager is running the show in Baltimore.

### STORY 5: Ohtani Pitching

#### Long-Form Post — 2:00 PM ET

The Shohei Ohtani story in 2026 continues to read like something a screenwriter made up. ⚾

He returned to the mound this season after missing 2024 following Tommy John surgery — and picked up right where he left off. Through two pitching starts, Ohtani has thrown 12 scoreless innings. His career scoreless innings streak as a pitcher now stands at 28 and two-thirds innings, the longest active streak in Major League Baseball. He has not allowed an earned run in a very long time.

As a hitter, his on-base streak has reached 43 consecutive games, which matches Ichiro Suzuki's 2009 mark for the longest on-base streak by a Japanese-born player in MLB history.

He is doing this while the Dodgers own the best record in baseball at 12-4. The two-way performance is not a gimmick or a marketing story. It is real, it is sustained, and it is historically unprecedented.
