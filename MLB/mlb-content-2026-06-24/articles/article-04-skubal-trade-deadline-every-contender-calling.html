The Skubal Trade: Every Contender Is Calling Detroit — Who Wins the Sweepstakes?

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 24, 2026

There are 40 days until the August 3 MLB trade deadline. And there is one transaction that is going to define the summer.

Tarik Skubal is available.

The Detroit Tigers left-hander — a two-time Cy Young Award winner and the most coveted starting pitcher at the deadline — is going to be traded. The organization has crashed fast enough that a deal now appears more likely than not, and multiple reports confirm that essentially every major contender has been in contact with the Tigers about acquiring him.

The Skubal sweepstakes has begun in earnest.

Why the Tigers Are Selling

After a promising 2024 and 2025, the Tigers' 2026 season has gone sideways quickly. The contention window that scouts and executives pointed to has not materialized, and the organization faces a clear decision: pay a premium to keep Skubal and compete for a wild card, or trade him for a haul of prospects and begin rebuilding for 2028 and beyond.

The case for trading is compelling. Skubal is a free agent after the 2026 season, and his agent Scott Boras is expected to command a deal that Detroit will not match. If the Tigers hold him, they lose him for nothing at the end of the year. A trade gets them the prospect capital to build the next wave.

Skubal returned from a minor elbow procedure in mid-June and has given potential trade partners time to see him healthy before making a significant offer.

The Suitors

The list of teams linked to Skubal reads like a who's-who of baseball contenders: the Atlanta Braves, Toronto Blue Jays, Chicago Cubs, Los Angeles Dodgers, San Diego Padres, and New York Yankees have all been connected. That is seven franchises, spanning both leagues, covering markets from New York to Los Angeles.

What makes the competition fierce: every team on that list has a genuine postseason window and a genuine need for pitching. For teams with deep farm systems, the calculation is whether the prospect cost is worth a rental ace who could be the difference between a first-round exit and a World Series appearance.

The Tigers, for their part, have made their position clear. They want an elite prospect plus additional pieces — not a package built around depth, but a package headlined by a top-ten prospect with star ceiling.

Why Skubal Changes Everything

A two-time Cy Young Award winner mid-season, still in his prime, available at the trade deadline is an extraordinarily rare event. The combination of proven postseason capability and ace-level stuff makes Skubal one of the most impactful rental acquisitions any team could make.

The team that lands him adds not just a starter but a rotation anchor who can pitch the pivotal Game 4 or Game 5 in a series when everything is on the line. That kind of certainty has a price that most contenders will at least consider paying.

What's Next

The Skubal chatter will intensify through July. Watch for evaluators from multiple teams to attend Tigers games in the coming weeks. The first substantive offers will likely be exchanged in mid-July after the All-Star break. If the Tigers get what they want, a deal could close well before the August 3 deadline. If negotiations stall, expect a final push in the last 48 hours. Either way, this is the deal that defines the 2026 deadline.
