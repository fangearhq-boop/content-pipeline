Kyle Tucker Update: Dodgers' $240M Star Is 'Pretty Confident' He'll Avoid the IL

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 24, 2026

The news on Kyle Tucker keeps getting better.

The Los Angeles Dodgers outfielder, who left Monday's game against the Minnesota Twins in the second inning with lower back spasms, has been moving in the right direction. Tucker told reporters Tuesday that he is "pretty confident" he will avoid the injured list. He noted the spasms were familiar — he has dealt with similar episodes before — and that he felt noticeably better on Tuesday than he did Monday night.

Dodgers manager Dave Roberts reinforced the message. "Not overly concerned," Roberts said of Tucker's status. The team has no plans to send Tucker for imaging, which would indicate they believe the situation does not warrant further investigation beyond monitoring his daily progress.

What Happened

Tucker exited the June 23 game against the Twins in the top of the second inning after experiencing lower back spasms between innings. The discomfort began as the team took the field for defense, and Tucker told the training staff before he stepped into the batter's box. The Dodgers won the game 2-1.

Dodgers catcher Dalton Rushing was also out of the lineup on June 23 with a concussion he sustained during the same game, creating a two-player absence for an already taxed roster. Both players were held out of the starting lineup the following day as a precaution.

Tucker's situation appears to be the more straightforward of the two. Back spasms, while painful, are typically a short-term issue when not associated with structural damage — and Tucker's comfort with the scenario, based on his own previous experience, suggests this falls into that category.

What Tucker Means to the Dodgers

Tucker signed a contract worth $240 million to join the Dodgers, and the investment reflects what the franchise believes he brings to the roster. He is a reliable 30-plus home run bat with a strong throwing arm, above-average outfield defense, and the kind of consistent plate discipline that fits well within Los Angeles's lineup construction.

For a team with legitimate World Series aspirations in 2026, Tucker's health in the second half is essential. The Dodgers have the depth to absorb short-term absences, but extended Tucker unavailability would create a meaningful offensive gap that is difficult to fill from within the organization.

What's Next

If Tucker's progress continues — which the early signs suggest it will — expect him back in the Dodgers' starting lineup within the next few days. Roberts and the training staff will manage his workload carefully, but the internal expectation is that this is a days issue, not a weeks issue. For Dodgers fans, the combination of Tucker's own optimism and Roberts's calm demeanor is the best available signal that the immediate concern has passed.
