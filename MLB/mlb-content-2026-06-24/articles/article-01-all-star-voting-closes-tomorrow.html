All-Star Voting Closes Tomorrow — Current Leaders, Close Races, and Why It Matters

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 24, 2026

You have one day left.

MLB All-Star voting Phase 1 closes Thursday, June 25 at noon ET. After that, the fan ballot closes, and the process shifts to player and manager votes to fill out the roster. If your favorite player is in a close race, today is the most important moment in the voting cycle.

Here is where things stand going into the final 24 hours.

Ohtani Leads Everyone

Shohei Ohtani leads all players in baseball with 2,310,735 votes. He has led every All-Star voting update since balloting opened, and there is no realistic path to catching him at this point. Barring a dramatic shift in the final hours, Ohtani will be an automatic starter for the National League when the final Phase 1 results are announced.

That would make this Ohtani's third consecutive All-Star selection. He remains the most popular player in the sport by a significant margin — his voting total is more than 250,000 ahead of the second-highest vote-getter in either league.

The Clement Surprise in the AL

The most notable storyline in this year's voting is the emergence of Ernie Clement of the Toronto Blue Jays, who leads the American League with 2,054,130 votes. That total is the second-highest of any player in either league, behind only Ohtani.

Clement's presence at the top of the AL ballot has been one of the more discussed — and debated — developments of the voting cycle. Whether that reflects a genuine performance surge or a coordinated fan effort, his name will be among the first called at the All-Star Game if he holds the lead through noon tomorrow.

Schwarber and the Philadelphia Factor

Kyle Schwarber is leading All-Star voting at designated hitter with 1,540,202 votes. The reason that number matters: the 2026 All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. Schwarber's home park.

The Phillies faithful have clearly been voting. Schwarber also has the statistical production to back up the fan support — he leads all of baseball with 29 home runs, hitting .255/.369/.603. He has been the best power hitter in the sport for the first half. The All-Star selection would be both fan-driven and performance-justified.

Close Races Across Positions

MLB.com has flagged several close races in the Phase 1 standings. The difference between an automatic starter and a reserve can be hundreds of thousands of votes. In a voting cycle that draws tens of millions of ballots, the final day often matters more than any other day in the process.

If your team has a player in a tight race for a starting position, the window to act is today. Phase 1 closes at noon ET tomorrow. There is no Phase 2 fan vote. This is it.

What's Next

After Phase 1 closes, the process shifts to player balloting (for All-Star reserves) and managerial selections. Final rosters for the July 14 game at Citizens Bank Park will be announced in the first week of July. The Home Run Derby, held the night before the game, has its own participant selection process — and at least one Phillie with 29 home runs is currently on the fence about entering. More on that in a moment.
