Bobby Witt Jr. Has an Encouraging Workout — The Royals Are Optimistic He'll Avoid the IL

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | June 24, 2026

Kansas City Royals shortstop Bobby Witt Jr. took a significant step toward returning to the lineup on Monday.

Witt, who has been sidelined with a Grade 1 MCL sprain in his right knee, worked out at Tropicana Field before Monday's game in St. Petersburg. He ran the bases. He completed defensive drills. He hit in the batting cage. He was wearing a brace on the injured knee throughout the workout.

Manager Matt Quatraro watched. When it was done, his message was clear: "Very encouraging what we saw today."

The Royals expect Witt to avoid a stint on the injured list. The team's optimism has shifted from guarded to genuinely confident since Monday's workout. Witt himself said he is "accustomed to wearing the brace" and expects to be back in the lineup soon.

What It Means for the Royals

Bobby Witt Jr. is not simply a contributor for Kansas City — he is the engine of the offense and, by the numbers, one of the most valuable players in the American League this season.

Through June, Witt is hitting .294 with 10 home runs, 32 RBIs, and 28 stolen bases. He leads American League position players in Wins Above Replacement. He is leading All-Star voting at shortstop in the AL. He is, by multiple metrics, in the conversation for the AL Most Valuable Player Award.

The knee sprain last week — suffered while fielding a ground ball against the Cardinals — threatened to derail both his All-Star chances and Kansas City's playoff positioning. The encouraging workout changes the calculus considerably.

Timeline and Recovery

A Grade 1 MCL sprain is the least severe category of the injury — it involves stretching of the ligament without significant tearing. The typical recovery timeline for a Grade 1 sprain ranges from a few days to two weeks, depending on the player, the sport, and how aggressively they push the return.

Witt's Monday workout suggests he is on the faster end of that range. The Royals have not given a specific date for his return, but the language from Quatraro — "very encouraging" — combined with Witt's own optimism points toward a return within the next few days rather than weeks.

The All-Star Game on July 14 remains a realistic target, assuming this trajectory holds.

The Bigger Picture

Witt's season has been one of the best in Kansas City since the franchise's last championship window. His combination of speed, power, and defense at shortstop makes him one of the most complete players in the game. His AL fWAR lead reflects both his offensive production and his defensive contributions.

For a Royals franchise still building toward sustained contention, keeping Witt healthy and productive for the second half is paramount. A quick return from this MCL sprain would allow him to maintain his MVP-caliber pace — and give Kansas City its best chance at a meaningful postseason run.

What's Next

Watch for daily injury updates from the Royals. If Witt's symptoms continue to improve, expect an activation from the injured list — if he went on it at all — within the next few days. The Royals' front office will want him back in the lineup as soon as medically reasonable. Monday's workout was the best evidence yet that "soon" means what they hope it means.
