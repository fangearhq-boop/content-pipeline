NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Let's talk about what Kyle Schwarber is doing — and what he should do next. Through June 24, Schwarber leads all of Major League Baseball with 29 home runs. He is hitting .255 with a .369 on-base percentage and .603 slugging percentage. He is on pace for more than 60 home runs — a threshold reached by only a handful of players in the history of the game. Now add this: the 2026 Home Run Derby is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park. Philadelphia. His home park. The crowd would be electric. The storyline would be perfect. The pace he's on already makes him the most compelling participant the Derby could have. And he still hasn't committed. When asked about participating, Schwarber said: "If that happens, we'll see what happens for the Home Run Derby." That's not a no. But it's not a yes either. There are real reasons to skip. The Derby can mess with a hitter's swing. Some players prefer to rest during the All-Star break. Some feel the added pressure of a home crowd is more burden than boost. But 29 home runs. A pace most players never approach. The Derby at his house. The city that adopted him after he followed Bryce Harper to Philly. It would be one of the great All-Star moments in recent memory. Come on, Kyle. #Phillies #HomeRunDerby #MLB [Posting Window: 10:30 AM ET]

NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post There are 40 days until the August 3 trade deadline. And there is one trade that is going to define it. Tarik Skubal is available. The Detroit Tigers have crashed quickly after their promising 2024 and 2025 seasons, and a Tarik Skubal trade now appears more likely than not. Skubal is a two-time Cy Young Award winner who is a free agent after the 2026 season — and according to multiple reports, the Tigers are not expected to meet his asking price for an extension. He returned from a minor elbow procedure in mid-June and has given potential trade partners time to evaluate him before the deadline. The list of teams calling Detroit is long: the Braves, Blue Jays, Cubs, Dodgers, Padres, and Yankees have all been linked. That is essentially every major contender in both leagues. The Tigers are not panicking — they are holding out for what they want: an elite prospect plus additional pieces. For whichever team lands Skubal, the calculus is simple: a proven ace in the postseason is worth almost anything. For contenders watching their pitching staffs wobble heading into August, Skubal is the clearest solution on the market. The question is not whether a Skubal trade happens. It is who pays the price to make it happen. Watch this space. #MLB #TradeDeadline #TarikSkubal #Tigers [Posting Window: 3:00 PM ET]

Kyle Tucker Update — Dodgers' $240M Star Expects to Avoid IL
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Kyle Tucker is optimistic. The Dodgers outfielder, who signed a $240 million contract to join Los Angeles, left Monday's game against the Minnesota Twins in the second inning with lower back spasms. He was out of the lineup again on Tuesday. But as of today, the prognosis is encouraging. Tucker said he is "pretty confident" he will avoid the injured list. He noted he felt better than the previous day and has dealt with similar spasms before. The Dodgers and manager Dave Roberts echoed the calm — Roberts said he is "not overly concerned" and the team has no plans to send Tucker for imaging. For a Dodgers team with legitimate World Series aspirations, keeping Tucker healthy is priority one. He is one of the most well-rounded outfielders in the game — a 30-plus home run threat with a strong arm and above-average defense. Losing him, even short-term, would be significant. The plan is to get him back in the lineup as soon as he feels ready. No timeline has been given, but the expectation within the organization is that this is a matter of days. Good news for LA. #Dodgers #MLB #KyleTucker [Posting Window: 6:00 PM ET]
