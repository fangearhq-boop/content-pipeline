Judge is out with a right rib stress fracture — gone for 4-6 weeks. Murakami is recovering from a Grade 2 hamstring strain that cost him approximately four weeks beginning around May 31. Both of the Phillies slugger's closest rivals are sidelined, and Schwarber is now the uncontested leader of the 2026 HR race.

He hit No. 20 on May 15 — the earliest date in Major League Baseball history that any player had reached the 20-HR mark. Since then, he has continued to add to the total. He is on pace for a season that could reach historic territory.

The 60-HR club exists in baseball's record books with three names: Barry Bonds (73, 2001), Mark McGwire (70 in 1998 and 65 in 1999), and Sammy Sosa (66 in 1998, 64 in 2001, 63 in 1999). No player has reached 60 home runs since Bonds. No National League player has done it since Sosa in 2001. The numbers are rarefied air.

Schwarber is not there yet. He has a long season still to play. And historical HR pace races tend to change dramatically as the summer progresses, fatigue accumulates, and pitchers make adjustments. But for right now, as of this Monday in June, there is one player in Major League Baseball on a pace worth tracking — and his name is Kyle Schwarber.
