Acuña Is on Fire and the Braves Are Running Away With the NL East

By Marcus Cole

June 3, 2026

Ronald Acuña Jr. has homered in four consecutive games with five home runs in that stretch. The Atlanta Braves are 40-20 and lead the NL East by 9.5 games. This team is not waiting for anyone.

Acuña Is Back — and He Looks Dangerous

After dealing with early-season injury concerns that tempered expectations heading into June, Ronald Acuña Jr. has sent a clear message: he is healthy, he is locked in, and he is the most dangerous hitter in the National League when he plays like this.

Acuña has homered in four consecutive games, connecting for five home runs in that stretch. For the 2023 NL Most Valuable Player — who became one of only four players in history to hit 40 home runs and steal 70 bases in the same season — this kind of production is not surprising. It is, however, exactly what the Atlanta Braves needed to see entering June.

The Braves' Dominant Start

Atlanta enters June 3 at 40-20, giving them the best record in the National League and a 9.5-game lead over the Phillies in the NL East. The Braves first crossed 40 wins faster than any team in recent memory, and their lead in the division is large enough that daily fluctuations barely register.

The pitching staff has been effective throughout. Chris Sale, who continues to defy age-related expectations, delivered a strong eight-strikeout performance recently. Atlanta has the combination of quality pitching and elite offense that separates genuine World Series contenders from divisional winners who fade in October.

What a Healthy Acuña Means

When Acuña is at full strength, the calculus changes for opposing teams. His speed forces defenders to play differently. His power threat in the middle of the lineup changes how the rest of Atlanta's hitters see pitches. And his energy — his visible joy and intensity on the field — affects how the Braves play as a unit.

The 2023 version of Acuña — 41 HRs, 73 stolen bases, unanimous MVP — was the best player in baseball. He has been working back toward that level, and a four-game HR streak with five home runs suggests he may be arriving.

For the NL East's other teams, that is not good news.

The Rest of the NL East

Atlanta's lead is built on more than just being good — it's built on the rest of the division not being nearly good enough. The Phillies, at 30-29, are nine and a half games back despite the league's home run leader in Kyle Schwarber. The Nationals, Mets, and Marlins are all well below .500.

Unless something breaks dramatically for the Braves — a wave of injuries, a catastrophic pitching collapse — the NL East is settled. The question that actually matters for Atlanta is whether they can maintain this level of performance through July and August to position themselves for a deep October run.

What's Next

Ronald Acuña Jr. and the Atlanta Braves continue their 2026 season. With a 9.5-game lead in the NL East and the best record in the National League at 40-20, Atlanta appears on track for another division title. Monitor whether Acuña's hot streak extends and whether the Braves maintain their pace through the upcoming road trips in June and July.
