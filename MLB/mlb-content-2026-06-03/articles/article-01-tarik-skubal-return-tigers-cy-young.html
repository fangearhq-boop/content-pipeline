Tarik Skubal Is Almost Back — and the Tigers Need Him More Than Ever

By Marcus Cole

June 3, 2026

Manager A.J. Hinch called Tarik Skubal's recent simulated game "awesome," and reports indicate the back-to-back AL Cy Young winner could return to the Detroit Tigers rotation within two weeks. Here's what to expect.

"Not Rehabbing — Pitching"

There is a difference between a pitcher going through the motions of a recovery and a pitcher who has found his stuff again. According to Detroit Tigers manager A.J. Hinch, Tarik Skubal has clearly crossed that line.

After Skubal's most recent simulated game, Hinch described what he saw in emphatic terms. "What I saw yesterday was an athlete free and pitching and competing and not rehabbing," the manager said. He added that the team was at "a very good step to advance him if all reports are good today."

For Tigers fans who have been waiting since May 6 — when Skubal underwent a nano-scope procedure to remove a loose body from his throwing elbow — those words land like a runway cleared for takeoff.

The Timeline

According to reports, Skubal is expected to begin a minor league rehab assignment in the coming days, with a projected return to the major league roster approximately two weeks from now. That would put his comeback somewhere in mid-to-late June, depending on how the rehab assignment goes.

Before going on the injured list, Skubal had been sharp. In seven starts, he posted a 2.70 ERA with 45 strikeouts and a 3-2 record. He was doing exactly what you'd expect from the back-to-back American League Cy Young Award winner — making it look routine when it is anything but.

What the Tigers Have Been Missing

Skubal's absence has been felt in every rotation turn. Detroit has struggled with the gap his starts leave, and the organization's ability to compete for a playoff spot is directly tied to his health and effectiveness. His name surfacing in trade speculation — though the Tigers have resisted making any such move — speaks to how this season has unfolded without their anchor.

When Skubal returns, he changes the calculus entirely. A 2.70 ERA starter at the top of the rotation reconfigures how Detroit pitches every five days. The lineup is capable of producing runs. The question has always been whether the pitching can hold them.

Back-to-Back Cy Young, Back on the Mound

Skubal's back-to-back AL Cy Young Award wins are the most decorated back-to-back starting pitcher achievements in the AL in recent memory. Two consecutive Cy Youngs is an exclusive club, and Skubal earned both with sustained dominance — not a one-year wonder, but a pitcher who has become one of the most reliable aces in the sport.

Elbow procedures, even minor ones, carry inherent uncertainty for pitchers. But Hinch's confidence in what he saw — an athlete "free" and pitching at full competitive intensity, not nursing his way through an exercise — suggests Skubal has come out the other side in good shape.
