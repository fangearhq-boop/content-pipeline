Aaron Judge Is on Pace for His 5th Career 50-HR Season — No Player Has Ever Done That

By Jake Torres

June 3, 2026

Through approximately 55 games of the 2026 season, Aaron Judge has 17 home runs and is tracking toward another 50-homer campaign. If he gets there, he would become the first player in Major League Baseball history to hit 50 or more home runs in five different seasons.

The Record Nobody Talks About Yet

Everyone knows Aaron Judge can hit home runs. He has proven it in ways that were previously unimaginable, finishing the 2022 season with 62 — the new American League record — and reaching 50 or more in three seasons since. But the record Judge is currently chasing is quieter, and potentially more significant, than any single-season total.

No player in Major League Baseball history has ever hit 50 or more home runs in five different seasons. Babe Ruth came the closest, with four such campaigns: 54 in 1920, 59 in 1921, 60 in 1927, and 54 in 1928. Barry Bonds hit 73 in 2001 but never reached 50 again. Sammy Sosa had multiple 60-plus seasons but only a handful of 50-plus. Nobody has ever hit five.

Judge already has four: 52 in 2017, 62 in 2022, 58 in 2024, and 53 in 2025. With 17 home runs through approximately 55 games in 2026, he is on pace for 50 again.

The Math and the History

Projecting 17 HRs over roughly 55 games gives a per-game rate that projects to approximately 50 home runs over 162 games. Judge has shown a consistent ability to sustain his power production through full seasons — his injury concerns in earlier parts of his career have largely faded, and he has appeared in 150-plus games in each of his four 50-HR seasons.

The timing in relation to baseball's history adds another layer. Ruth's four 50-HR seasons were all produced in the 1920s, when the game was transforming around the live-ball era he helped create. Judge is doing it in a modern era with different pitching, different parks, and a different level of competition. The context matters, but the number is the number: five would be unprecedented.

What Would Make This Different

Judge's 2022 season — 62 home runs, the new AL record — is the obvious crown jewel of his career. But a fifth 50-homer season would speak to something different: longevity. Consistency over years. The ability to maintain elite power production not just in one transcendent year, but across a decade of baseball.

Ruth's four 50-HR seasons spanned 1920 to 1928 — eight years. Judge would achieve his fifth in just nine years of his career, at age 34, which would make it one of the most durable elite power performances in the sport's modern history.

The Competition

Kyle Schwarber leads MLB with 22 home runs and is on pace for 66. Judge, at 17, trails but is by no means out of contention for the HR title. Two players tracking toward 50-plus seasons simultaneously is rare — the last time two players hit 50+ in the same season was Bonds and Sosa in 2001. Whether Judge and Schwarber can both get there will be one of the sport's defining storylines this summer.

For Judge, though, the individual milestone matters more than the title. Five 50-HR seasons would be a record all his own — and it would belong to him alone, forever.
