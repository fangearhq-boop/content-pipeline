### Long-Form Post We may be watching baseball history in slow motion, and it barely has anyone's full attention. Aaron Judge already has four career seasons with 50 or more home runs: 52 in 2017, 62 in 2022, 58 in 2024, and 53 in 2025. Through approximately 55 games of the 2026 season, he has 17 home runs and is on pace to reach 50 again. Here's why that matters: No player in Major League Baseball history has ever hit 50+ home runs in five different seasons. Babe Ruth had four. Judge already has four. A fifth would be a record that stands completely alone — a milestone nobody has ever reached. It's June. There are more than 100 games to go. But Judge has been one of the most consistent power hitters in the sport for nearly a decade. He's not fading. He's not slowing down. He is, if anything, exactly the kind of player who does what nobody has ever done before. Keep watching. ### Long-Form Post Here's a fact: Kyle Schwarber reached 20 home runs on May 15. That is the earliest date in Major League Baseball history that any player has hit 20 home runs in a season. Here's another fact: Through Tuesday, Schwarber leads all MLB hitters with 22 home runs, five ahead of Aaron Judge (17) in second place. Schwarber is on pace for 66 for the season. Judge is on pace for 50+. Two players on 50+ home run paces in early June. The last time MLB had that? Bonds and Sosa in 2001. Bonds finished with 73. Sosa had 64. We're not predicting anything close to that. But these are the two most electric power hitters in the sport right now, on the same calendar, competing for the same record. The Phillies under new manager Don Mattingly are 10-3, with Schwarber as the engine. The Yankees are circling in the AL East. This race is just getting started.
