Walker vs. Judge — Baseball's April Home Run Race You Weren't Expecting

By Jake Torres | April 17, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Aaron Judge at the top of the home run leaderboard in April is expected. Jordan Walker at the top of the home run leaderboard in April is one of the better surprises in baseball so far this year.

Through the first 16 games of the St. Louis Cardinals' season, Walker had hit 8 home runs — tying the franchise record for the most homers in the team's first 16 games. The Cardinals legends who share that mark: Stan Musial, Mark McGwire, Scott Rolen, and Albert Pujols. Walker is 23 years old. He had a combined 11 home runs across his entire first two major league seasons (2024 and 2025). In the first 16 games of 2026, he has matched his prior career total in fewer than a hundred at-bats.

On Thursday, Walker helped the Cardinals defeat the Cleveland Guardians 5-3, extending his hitting streak to 11 games in the process. His 2026 numbers are eye-catching: a wRC+ of 219 (elite even by MVP-candidate standards), and an average exit velocity of 97.1 mph that leads all of Major League Baseball. These are not fluky power numbers — they are the product of a player generating consistent, hard contact with every swing.

Judge Ties Him

Then came Thursday in the Bronx. Aaron Judge hit a 410-foot solo home run to left-center field in the first inning against the Los Angeles Angels — his 8th of the season — pulling even with Walker for the MLB home run lead. Judge was coming off a stretch in which he hit five home runs over 15 at-bats, and his 2026 pace projects to roughly 60 home runs over a full season.

Judge's 47th career multi-home run game this April passed Mickey Mantle (46) for second place in Yankees franchise history, trailing only Babe Ruth's 68. On a career level, the Yankees' captain is building one of the great power résumés in franchise history. On a seasonal level, his April is simply doing what April Judge does.

What makes the Walker-Judge tie so compelling is the contrast in the narratives. Judge is the known commodity — one of the best power hitters in baseball, performing at his ceiling. Walker is the question mark made tangible. Nobody projected this. Nobody put Walker in their preseason AL- or NL-MVP conversation. He was the third outfielder on a Cardinals team rebuilding toward contention.

The Question Going Forward

Walker's career trajectory from this point depends almost entirely on whether the power and contact rates sustain themselves against the adjustments opposing pitchers will now make. Every team in the NL has three weeks of scouting data on what Walker is doing mechanically, and the league will attack. The question is whether Walker adjusts back.

The history of April breakout hitters suggests regression is more common than continuity. But Walker's exit velocity and contact quality suggest this is not noise — it is a real mechanical or approach change that is translating to results. Cardinal fans have been waiting for him to be this player since he was the No. 5 overall pick in the 2022 draft. He may finally be arriving.

What's Next

Watch how Walker handles the April-to-May transition as pitchers begin to throw his weaknesses more aggressively. Judge is the baseline — consistent excellence. Walker is the story. The race to see who finishes April with more home runs is one of the most engaging sub-plots in the early 2026 season.
