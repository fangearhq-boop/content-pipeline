Trevor Story Is Back — Homer, 5 RBIs, and a Comeback Nobody Saw Coming

By Jake Torres | April 17, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Baseball has a long tradition of comeback stories, and many of them deserve the attention they receive. Trevor Story's 2026 season, now quietly building in Boston, is starting to look like one of them.

On Tuesday, Story homered and drove in five runs as the Red Sox defeated the Minnesota Twins. Five RBIs in a single game is not a routine occurrence for any player — it is the kind of performance that carries an entire offense and provides the kind of cushion that pitchers and managers remember. For Story specifically, it carried added meaning: a demonstration that the power, the plate presence, and the competitive instincts are all back and functioning.

The context for those who haven't followed Story's path: after signing a massive contract with the Red Sox prior to the 2022 season, he required UCL surgery that wiped out most of that year. The recovery stretched into 2023 with limited effectiveness. Then more injury concerns in 2024 and 2025 kept Story from ever fully establishing the consistency that made him one of the better shortstops in the National League during his Colorado years. He turned 32 this past August.

What Made Tuesday Different

When Story was healthy in Colorado, he was a genuine five-tool threat — power that led the NL in home runs in 2021, a plus defender at shortstop, and a 20-steal speed profile. The version that has shown up in limited Boston action over the past few seasons was a reduced version of that player — still capable, but with the injury questions hanging over every game.

The five-RBI game was not just impressive by the number. It was the combination of a home run, which requires full power generation, and multiple RBI opportunities, which requires being in the lineup and healthy enough to be a dependable presence. Story was both. The Red Sox, who entered the 2026 season with a legitimate AL East case if their roster stayed healthy, got exactly what they needed from their veteran infielder.

Boston entered the early weeks of the season with Tampa Bay and the Yankees as the early pace-setters in the AL East. Story's emergence as a full-time contributor gives them another dimension they will need to compete in a tight division race.

The Broader Comeback Picture

Stories like this one are part of why April baseball carries its own unique energy. The first three weeks of the season are still revealing which players are healthy, which teams are as good as advertised, and which surprises will define the summer. Story's return to form is not guaranteed to last — the injury history makes that clear — but a five-RBI game demonstrates what he looks like when everything is working.

For a player who gave up multiple full seasons to injury and recovery, games like Tuesday matter on a level beyond the box score.

What's Next

Watch how Story's production line continues over the next few series. The Red Sox need sustained health from him to compete in the AL East. If Tuesday's game is a sign of what the 2026 season holds for Story, it is a very good sign — both for Boston and for a player who has been waiting four years to feel like himself again.
