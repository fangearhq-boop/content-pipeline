Dodgers Shut Out Cubs 6-0, End 10-Game Win Streak — Ohtani Delivers the Exclamation Point

By Marcus Cole | April 27, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Chicago Cubs' MLB-best 10-game winning streak is over. The defending World Series champions made sure of it.

On Sunday night at Dodger Stadium, Los Angeles shut out the Cubs 6-0 to win their three-game series, with starter Justin Wrobleski delivering six scoreless innings and Shohei Ohtani providing the defining moment with his sixth home run of the season — a 109.8 mph rocket in the seventh inning that sealed the outcome.

The Cubs had arrived in Los Angeles riding one of the best stretches in the NL this season — 10 straight wins, outscoring opponents 58-20 during an eight-game portion of the streak at a 2.22 team ERA. They beat the Dodgers twice earlier this week. On Sunday, they ran into a wall.

A Game Chicago Would Rather Forget

The Cubs left 12 runners on base and went 0-for-8 with runners in scoring position. The first number is a headache. The second is a nightmare. Against a pitching staff like Los Angeles runs, that kind of inefficiency gets punished every time.

Wrobleski navigated multiple jams without breaking. The left-hander gave the Dodgers exactly what they needed: enough innings to let the offense take over when opportunity arrived, and enough composure to keep the Cubs from stringing any damage together in traffic.

Ohtani's seventh-inning home run was the capstone. Exit velocity 109.8 mph, launched inside-out to left-center field. It pushed the score to 6-0 and was the kind of play that the Dodgers have produced regularly since the start of September 2025 — which is to say, it felt inevitable.

What This Means for Both Teams

For the Dodgers, winning this series was important for more reasons than wins and losses. They had lost to the Cubs twice earlier in the week — one of those losses the result of a four-run bullpen collapse in a game Emmet Sheehan was dealing in. Sunday restored the series and, more importantly, confirmed that Los Angeles remains the NL's standard to beat at 18-8.

For the Cubs, a 10-game win streak is an accomplishment regardless of how it ends. Chicago is still 16-9 and tied with Cincinnati atop the NL Central. The streak proved this is a legitimate contending team, not a mirage. That the streak ended against the defending champions, in Dodger Stadium, against Ohtani — there's no shame in that.

The NL West race is tied: Dodgers and Padres both at 17-8. The NL Central is the sport's most chaotic division, with all five teams over .500 for the first time in Divisional Era history. Sunday's game raised questions but answered just as many.

What's Next

The Dodgers remain locked in a dead heat with San Diego in the NL West. The Cubs return home to face the next challenger in their NL Central gauntlet. The next time these two clubs meet, it may carry even bigger weight — because a late-October matchup between Chicago and Los Angeles is looking more likely every week.
