Paul Skenes Went 20-Up, 20-Down Before Jake Bauers Spoiled It — The Cy Young Winner Is Back

By Marcus Cole | April 27, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

On Friday night in Pittsburgh, Paul Skenes was pitching a perfect game.

Twenty consecutive Milwaukee Brewers hitters stepped up to the plate against the reigning NL Cy Young Award winner and went back to the dugout having done nothing. No hits. No walks. No base runners of any kind. Through six innings and two outs into the seventh, Skenes had set down every batter he faced.

Then Jake Bauers ripped a single to center field to end it.

The hit stings if you're a Pittsburgh fan, but the rest of the night more than made up for it. Skenes finished seven innings — 1 hit, 0 runs, 0 walks, 7 strikeouts on 93 pitches. The Pirates won 6-0 in a one-hit combined shutout. And the baseball world was reminded of something important: when Skenes is right, he's among the best pitchers alive.

The Redemption Arc

The contrast with Opening Day could hardly be more dramatic. On March 26 in New York, Skenes allowed five runs before recording two outs against the Mets. He was pulled before completing the first inning of the 2026 season — a brutal start for a pitcher carrying the highest expectations in the NL.

Since then, Skenes has been excellent. His record sits at 3-1, his ERA at 3.27. The near-perfect game against Milwaukee was his best outing of the year — perhaps his best since early 2025. His command was electric, his velocity was up, and his ability to sequence hitters through multiple at-bats was exactly what separates good starters from elite ones.

The Pittsburgh Pirates made Skenes their Opening Day starter specifically because they believe this team can contend. At 14-11, they're alive in the most fascinating divisional race in baseball — the NL Central, where all five teams are currently over .500 for the first time in Divisional Era history.

What the Numbers Say

Through his first five 2026 starts, Skenes has struck out 23 batters with 7 walks across 22 innings before the Milwaukee start — a game that pushed him past 29 innings and brought his 2026 ERA from 3.27 down further after seven scoreless innings. He owns a 2.10 ERA for his career across his first 57 starts, the fourth-best mark by any starter since 1920 — elite company by any measure.

Friday's start against Milwaukee was Skenes' longest outing of the year and a statement game. When he sets down 20 consecutive major-league hitters, the perfect game discussion is deserved — even if Bauers had other ideas.

What's Next

Skenes' next scheduled start comes later this week for Pittsburgh, which remains very much in the NL Central hunt. The Pirates haven't been legitimate October contenders in years, but Skenes at the top of the rotation makes them legitimately dangerous heading into May. If he can sustain Friday's level of performance through the next two months, the NL Cy Young conversation won't just be about protecting his title — it'll be about extending his legacy.
