### Long-Form Post *Posting Window: 5:30 PM ET* If you're still sleeping on Jose Ramirez, April 2026 should be your wake-up call. The Cleveland Guardians' third baseman was named the American League Player of the Month for April after one of the best single-month stretches of his career: a .342 batting average, .722 slugging percentage, seven home runs, and 28 RBIs in 21 games. Those are MVP-level numbers for a full month. But the bigger story is where Ramirez sits historically. He entered the 2026 season with 285 career home runs and 287 career stolen bases. With seven April home runs, he's closing in on 292 career HRs. He needs just 13 stolen bases to reach 300 for his career. The 300/300 club — 300 home runs and 300 stolen bases — is one of the rarest achievements in baseball history. Only eight players have done it: Bobby Bonds, Barry Bonds, Steve Finley, Carlos Beltran, Reggie Sanders, Jeff Bagwell, Larry Walker, and Alex Rodriguez. Ramirez is chasing history with every game he plays. He's already one of the best players in franchise history. A healthy 2026 season could put him in conversation with some of the greatest switch-hitters to ever play the game. ### Image Post Caption *Post with Ramirez at bat photo* Seven HRs. 28 RBIs.
