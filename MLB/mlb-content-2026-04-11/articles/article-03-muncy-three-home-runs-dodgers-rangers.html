Max Muncy Slugs Three Home Runs as Dodgers Dominate Rangers

By Ryan Calloway | April 11, 2026

Shohei Ohtani was intentionally walked on Friday night at Dodger Stadium. The message was clear: Texas would rather deal with someone else.

Max Muncy made them pay.

The Los Angeles Dodgers first baseman slugged three home runs in a single game as the Dodgers defeated the Texas Rangers in the opener of their three-game series. On a night that was Ohtani Bobblehead Night at Dodger Stadium — a tribute to the most captivating player in baseball — it was Muncy who became the actual story. Three home runs from one player in one game is rare at any level; in the first month of the season, against a legitimate AL West contender, it was extraordinary.

What Muncy's Night Tells You About the Dodgers

The fact that opponents are intentionally walking Shohei Ohtani says everything about how the baseball world perceives the Dodgers' lineup. Ohtani is the most dangerous hitter on the planet when locked in — his exit velocity numbers and expected stats make him the player most pitchers would prefer to avoid with runners on base.

But intentionally walking Ohtani in the Dodgers' lineup means pitching to Muncy. And on Friday, that calculation backfired spectacularly.

Muncy has always been one of the more underrated power threats in the National League. His ability to work counts, take walks, and then punish fastballs makes him the kind of hitter who can have a three-homer game on any given night. Friday was that night, and the Rangers have the unfortunate distinction of having been on the wrong side of it.

Tyler Glasnow and the Pitching Side

While Muncy made the headlines, Tyler Glasnow handled the pitching duties for Los Angeles. Glasnow has been the anchor of the Dodgers' rotation in seasons when he's been healthy, and 2026 has seen him take the mound consistently in the early going.

The Dodgers' ability to pair elite offense with an above-average pitching staff is what makes them the most complete team in baseball. They don't need a single player to be perfect every night because the depth ensures someone else will step up. Friday was Muncy's night. Tomorrow it could be Ohtani or Freddie Freeman or someone else entirely.

The Rangers' Perspective

For Texas, traveling to Los Angeles is never easy. The Rangers entered as a team looking to rebound early in the season, but a three-homer performance from Muncy — bookended by an intentional pass to Ohtani — is a difficult outing to recover confidence from. The bullpen is typically what defines how well a team can compete in a long series, and the Dodgers will keep applying pressure across all three games.

What's Next

The Dodgers and Rangers play Saturday and Sunday in Los Angeles to complete the three-game series. A Dodgers series win would further cement their status as the team with the best record in baseball.

For the rest of the National League, Friday's Muncy game is a reminder: the Dodgers are not a team you win a World Series by avoiding. You have to go through them. Right now, getting through them means dealing with Ohtani, Muncy, Freeman, and a rotation deep enough to handle whatever lineup they face.

Three home runs by Max Muncy on Ohtani Bobblehead Night. The 2026 Dodgers don't do anything small.
