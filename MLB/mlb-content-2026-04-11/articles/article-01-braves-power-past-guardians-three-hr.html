Acuña, Olson, Harris All Homer as Braves Power Past Guardians

By Marcus Cole | April 11, 2026

Atlanta's lineup delivered a message Friday night: the Braves are built for the long haul.

Three separate sluggers sent balls over the fence at Truist Park as the Atlanta Braves handled the Cleveland Guardians with authority. Ronald Acuña Jr., Matt Olson, and Michael Harris II each homered off Cleveland starter Slade Cecconi, powering Atlanta to a win that lifted their record to 9-5. The Guardians fell to 8-6 with the loss.

Three-home-run nights from three different players don't happen by accident. They happen when a lineup has genuine depth from top to bottom — when opposing pitchers can't find a soft spot to attack because every station in the order has a dangerous hitter.

Acuña and the Big Three

Ronald Acuña Jr. is the centerpiece of the Braves' lineup for a reason. The outfielder has become one of the most electrifying players in baseball, capable of affecting games with his bat, his legs, and his range in the outfield. His home run Friday continued what has been an excellent early-season stretch for a player who suffered through injury concerns in recent years.

Matt Olson has established himself as one of the most reliable left-handed power hitters in the National League. His presence in the lineup forces pitchers to make a difficult choice: be careful to Acuña (who punishes mistakes) or be careful to Olson (who also punishes mistakes). Cecconi learned that lesson Friday night when Olson drove a pitch deep to add to Atlanta's growing lead.

Michael Harris II adds a third dimension to the Braves' offensive attack. The outfielder provides speed, athleticism, and the kind of power that doesn't just come from the middle of the lineup. When Harris homers in the sixth inning to drive in two runs, it turns a comfortable lead into a blowout. That's what Atlanta got on Friday night.

The Baldwin Factor

Don't overlook what rookie Drake Baldwin brings to this lineup. Through his first 13 games, Baldwin leads all of Major League Baseball in RBIs with 15. He's scored a run in each of the Braves' first seven games of the season — the first Brave to accomplish that since Hank Aaron did it in 1957.

When an opposing team maps out how to attack Atlanta's lineup, they now have to account for Acuña, Olson, Harris, and Baldwin. That's a lot of danger concentrated in one batting order. Teams that try to pitch carefully to one hitter will often walk the tightrope right into another one.

Cleveland's Concern

For Cleveland, Friday's loss points to a pitching vulnerability that can't be ignored. Slade Cecconi is a young pitcher being asked to perform against elite competition, and three home runs in one outing is a tough mark to overcome. The Guardians at 8-6 are still a competitive team — Jose Ramirez was named AL Player of the Month for April, and their lineup is capable — but the pitching staff has questions that Friday deepened.

The good news for Cleveland is that one bad outing doesn't define a staff. The Guardians have enough talent to recover. But the Braves exposed some cracks that other teams will study on film.

What's Next

Atlanta and Cleveland continue their series on Saturday and Sunday before heading into their respective division schedules. For the Braves, a series win would push their record to 10-5 and establish them as the clear early-season leader in the NL East.

After just two weeks, Atlanta is making the case that the preseason projections placing them in the middle of the NL East race were wrong. Three Braves hitting home runs in the same game is the kind of statement that gets a fanbase excited — and rightfully so.
