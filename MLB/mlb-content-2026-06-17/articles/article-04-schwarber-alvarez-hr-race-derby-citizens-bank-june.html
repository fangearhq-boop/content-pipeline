Schwarber and Alvarez Tied at 24 — Derby Field Takes Shape as Citizens Bank Countdown Begins

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 17, 2026

Twenty-four home runs apiece. Kyle Schwarber of the Phillies and Yordan Alvarez of the Astros are locked in the most compelling individual race in Major League Baseball, and neither has blinked.

The backdrop gets better: the Home Run Derby is scheduled for July 13 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia — the place where Schwarber hits his home games. His house. His crowd. His short porch in right field.

The Race

Schwarber and Alvarez have been running together at the top of the MLB home run leaderboard for weeks. The gap between them and third place has remained wide enough to make this a genuine two-man competition. Both players have produced through difficult stretches — road trips, tough pitching matchups, the grind of a long season — and neither has separated himself.

Entering the week, the number is 24 for each player. That number will change, possibly tonight. But the framing of this race — two elite left-handed sluggers, one from Philly and one from Houston, matching each other swing for swing — is the kind of storyline that keeps baseball fans checking the scores every night.

The Derby Context

The Home Run Derby takes place July 13 at Citizens Bank Park, the night before the All-Star Game. Schwarber plays his home games in that ballpark. He knows every inch of the sightlines, every acoustic quirk of the crowd noise, the exact feeling of the left-handed batters box on the afternoon before a summer night game in South Philadelphia.

If he competes in the Derby — and the conversations around the event strongly suggest his name is on the short list — the combination of home crowd, home park, and leading the NL home run race would make for one of the better Derby storylines in recent memory.

CBS Sports recently assembled an ideal 2026 Derby field that included Schwarber, Alvarez, Bryce Harper, Mike Trout, and Ben Rice of the Yankees. Whether or not the official invitation list matches that vision exactly, the power narrative of 2026 has produced exactly the kind of players that make the Derby worth watching.

Alvarez's Side of the Ledger

Yordan Alvarez's summer is not defined solely by the home run race. He is also leading the American League among designated hitters in All-Star Phase 1 voting, and his June has included the kind of production that cements MVP-caliber seasons. He hit a home run and a grand slam in the same inning earlier this month — a first in MLB history — and has been as consistently dangerous as any hitter in the league.

The Derby would also give Alvarez a national stage beyond the AL leaderboard. In the home run competition, the park doesn't play favorites. It just measures distance.

What's Next

All-Star Phase 1 voting closes June 25. The Derby field will be announced in the days that follow. Schwarber and Alvarez will keep swinging in the meantime, and the number that sits at 24 today will look different by then.

The Derby on July 13 in Philadelphia is 26 days away. The race to that night starts tonight, and the two men leading the sport in home runs are perfectly positioned to make it unforgettable.
