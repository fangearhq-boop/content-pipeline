All-Star Ballot Update — Baldwin Leads Catchers, Ohtani and Alvarez Lead DH as Phase 1 Closes June 25

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 17, 2026

Eight days. That is what remains of Phase 1 of the 2026 MLB All-Star ballot. The deadline is June 25 at midnight. The All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. Between now and then, the races at several positions remain close enough that every vote counts.

Here is where things stand as of June 15.

Catcher: Baldwin Running Away

Drake Baldwin of the Atlanta Braves leads all catchers in Phase 1 voting with 972,813 votes. The second-place finisher at the position is Will Smith of the Dodgers with 662,883 votes — a gap of more than 300,000. Baldwin's lead is not the kind that collapses in a week without a dramatic reversal of fortune at the position.

Baldwin's season has backed up the vote totals. His 473-foot home run on Tuesday against Giants starter Adrian Houser — measured as the longest in MLB this season — is the type of moment that crystallizes a ballot case. He is power, he is defense, and he is the kind of catcher that makes a lineup more dangerous than the sum of its parts.

First Base: Freeman vs. Olson

The first base race carries extra texture. Freddie Freeman of the Dodgers leads with 870,606 votes. Matt Olson of the Braves is in second with 802,848. The gap is under 70,000 — a genuine contest with eight days remaining.

Freeman left Atlanta for Los Angeles. Olson came to replace him. Now both men are on the All-Star ballot, one of them wearing a Braves uniform and one of them wearing Dodger blue, running within 70,000 votes of each other for a starting spot at the same position.

Designated Hitter: Ohtani and Alvarez Lead

Shohei Ohtani leads the NL designated hitter voting. Yordan Alvarez leads the AL. Both results reflect seasons that have made the case for those selections without needing a promotional push. Ohtani continues to hit at the highest level in baseball; Alvarez is tied at 24 home runs with Phillies slugger Kyle Schwarber and is competing for what may be an MVP award when the season is done.

Earlier ballot reports placed Ohtani's total at over 1.1 million votes across all positions — a reflection of his visibility that spans far beyond casual baseball fans.

What's Next

Phase 1 voting closes June 25. Phase 2 begins June 29, where the ballot narrows to the leading candidates from Phase 1 and fans vote again. Final selections are announced shortly after Phase 2 closes, in advance of the July 14 game in Philadelphia.

The Home Run Derby takes place July 13 — the night before the game — also at Citizens Bank Park. With Schwarber and Alvarez leading the home run race, the Derby field announcement in the days after June 25 will be worth watching closely.

Vote at MLB.com. Eight days left. The rosters for Midsummer Night in Philadelphia are being written right now.
