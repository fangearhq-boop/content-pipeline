Drake Baldwin Hits 473-Foot Blast — MLB's Longest Homer of 2026 as Game Is Suspended

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 17, 2026

The Braves are in the middle of a difficult stretch. The pitching staff is short. The best player on the team is in the training room.

And then Drake Baldwin stepped to the plate Tuesday night and hit a baseball 473 feet.

The leadoff home run against Giants starter Adrian Houser in the first inning at Truist Park was measured as the longest home run hit in Major League Baseball so far in the 2026 season. It cleared the fence with room to spare and landed in a place that reminded everyone in the building that Atlanta has not run out of capable hitters.

The Home Run

Baldwin connected off Houser on the leadoff plate appearance, sending the ball on a towering arc into the Atlanta night. The 473-foot reading puts it in the conversation not just for the 2026 season, but for the kind of home runs that stay in ballpark lore — the ones fans describe to people who were not there.

The Giants-Braves game did not reach a conclusion on Tuesday. The contest was suspended in the bottom of the second inning. MLB rules for suspended games require them to be resumed from the point of suspension, which means Wednesday, June 17's schedule at Truist Park will include the completion of Tuesday's game before the regularly scheduled matchup. Braves and Giants fans are getting a doubleheader.

When Tuesday's game does reach its conclusion, it will carry the full context of Braves fans needing something to cheer about — and Baldwin provided exactly that in the first inning.

Baldwin's All-Star Case

The home run added another chapter to what has been an impressive 2026 season for Baldwin at the position where the Braves have needed reliability most. He has been one of the better offensive catchers in the NL this year, and the voters have noticed.

As of June 15, Drake Baldwin leads all catchers in Phase 1 of the 2026 MLB All-Star voting with 972,813 votes — more than 300,000 ahead of second-place Will Smith of the Dodgers. The vote closes Phase 1 on June 25, and Baldwin's lead entering the final week is substantial.

The All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. If Baldwin holds his lead and earns the starting spot, he will represent a Braves team that has been battered by injuries but has kept competing. A 473-foot home run from the catcher is the kind of moment that builds a case.

What's Next

Wednesday's schedule at Truist Park begins with the resumption of Tuesday's suspended game — starting from the bottom of the second inning, with the Giants carrying whatever score was established before the stoppage and Baldwin's first-inning blast already in the books for the Braves.

For Baldwin, the trajectory of the summer runs through the final week of All-Star voting, the July 14 game in Philadelphia, and the question of whether he can sustain his performance through the second half. Based on Tuesday evening, the answer seems straightforward.

He has the power. He has the votes. He just hit the longest home run in the sport this year.
