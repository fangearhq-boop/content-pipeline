Braves Lose Strider to Elbow Inflammation — MRI Clean, But Double Injury Crisis Deepens

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 17, 2026

The Atlanta Braves are holding their breath.

Spencer Strider has been placed on the 15-day injured list with right elbow inflammation, shutting down the Braves starter from throwing for four weeks. An MRI on the right elbow returned the best possible news under the circumstances: no ligament damage. But a four-week absence for one of Atlanta's most important pitchers — on top of Ronald Acuña Jr.'s ongoing hamstring injury — puts the NL's best team in a position no one expected entering June.

How It Happened

Strider exited an early June start against the Mets when the velocity on his fastball dropped noticeably during the game, falling as low as 88 miles per hour on a pitch that in peak form sits considerably higher. The Braves removed him from the game and sent him for imaging. The MRI results, released shortly after, showed no structural ligament damage in the right elbow — meaning Strider avoids the surgery discussion, at least for now.

The four-week shutdown is precautionary and restorative. No throwing for a month. After that, a gradual ramp-up that may bring Strider back to action in late July or early August. The Braves have not specified a return date.

In eight starts in 2026, Strider posted a 5.31 ERA and struck out 46 batters. He was not at his historically dominant levels this season, but a front-of-rotation arm going on the IL is a front-of-rotation absence regardless of the ERA number next to his name.

The Broader Picture

The Strider news lands less than a week after Ronald Acuña Jr. went on the injured list with a hamstring strain — his second IL stint of the season. When Acuña was placed on the IL on June 14, the Braves held a 46-25 record, the best mark in the National League.

Now Atlanta is navigating a significant portion of June without both its most dynamic position player and one of its most important pitchers.

The rotation still has Chris Sale, who leads all of Major League Baseball in strikeouts this season and has been one of the best pitchers in the NL. The lineup still features Matt Olson, who has 20 home runs, and Drake Baldwin, who has emerged as an All-Star-caliber catcher with a monster season at the plate.

But the margin for error has shrunk in a way that the standings at the time of Acuña's injury did not suggest. The NL East lead that felt comfortable a week ago is now something that must be protected every day.

What's Next

Atlanta's immediate focus is rotation depth. With Strider unavailable, the Braves need to rely on their back-end starters and bullpen in ways they had not planned heading into the summer. The trade deadline is August 3 — and if Strider's return timeline pushes toward that date, the front office may look at starting pitching options on the market.

The good news is that the MRI result closes the door on the worst-case scenario. No ligament damage is not a guarantee against further complications, but it is the result that says this is inflammation and rest rather than a structural problem. For Atlanta fans, that distinction matters enormously.

Strider will throw again. The question is when, and whether the Braves can hold what they have built while they wait.
