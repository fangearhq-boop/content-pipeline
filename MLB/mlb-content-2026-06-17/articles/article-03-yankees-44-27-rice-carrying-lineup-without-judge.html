Yankees 44-27 — Ben Rice Carries the Lineup as New York Rolls On Without Judge

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | June 17, 2026

The New York Yankees were 44-27 after Tuesday's win over the White Sox. Aaron Judge had not played in more than two weeks.

This is not a team in crisis mode. This is a team with depth — and a first baseman who has made the past month look effortless.

Ben Rice, the 27-year-old who emerged as a genuine power threat last season and has taken the next step in 2026, is leading New York's offense through the most critical stretch of the summer. His slash line of .300/.393/.638 would be remarkable in any context. In the context of replacing one of the best hitters in baseball, it is something else entirely.

Judge's Absence

Aaron Judge has been sidelined since June 4 with a stress fracture in his rib. The Yankees announced at the time of the injury that the timeline was four to six weeks, placing his return somewhere in mid-to-late July. There is no surgery involved, and the injury is expected to heal with rest. Every update since the announcement has been consistent: the captain is progressing and will return within the expected window.

In the meantime, the Yankees have played some of the best baseball of their season.

At 44-27, New York holds the AL East lead and has built a cushion that suggests they are not simply surviving without Judge — they are thriving. The pitching has remained solid. The lineup, anchored by Rice, has continued to score runs. The formula that produced a championship-caliber team earlier in the season has not changed.

The Rice Emergence

Ben Rice's season has been one of the better stories in the AL this year, and it predates Judge's injury. He entered June already putting together an outstanding offensive performance, and the pressure of holding down the middle of the order in Judge's absence has not rattled him.

Nineteen home runs. Forty-four RBIs. A .638 slugging percentage that ranks among the best in the American League. Rice is doing damage from the left side with a combination of bat speed and pitch recognition that has made him one of the harder outs in the division.

The All-Star conversation is no longer hypothetical. With voting Phase 1 closing June 25, Rice's numbers make the case on his behalf. If he continues at this pace through the vote closing, the argument for including him in the midsummer classic in Philadelphia on July 14 is difficult to ignore.

What's Next

Judge's return, when it comes, will give New York a lineup that looked formidable before the injury and will look even deeper with Rice in the fold as a proven contributor. The question of whether Rice maintains his starting role when Judge returns is a good problem to have.

For now, the Yankees are holding what they have, adding wins, and watching Rice do things their offense needs done. At 44-27, they are in the business of expanding the lead and keeping it. Based on the first two weeks of June, there is little reason to think they cannot do exactly that.
