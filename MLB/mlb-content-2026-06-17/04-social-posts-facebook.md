### STORY 1: Braves Lose Strider

#### FB Long-Form — 8:00 AM ET

The Atlanta Braves are now managing a genuine injury crisis.

Spencer Strider has been placed on the injured list with right elbow inflammation and will be shut down from throwing for four weeks. Strider exited a recent start against the Mets when the velocity on his fastball dropped noticeably, and the Braves sent him for imaging. An MRI on the right elbow showed no ligament damage — the result everyone was hoping for and the injury's best-case outcome. No surgery. No Tommy John. A four-week pause.

But the impact is still significant. Strider made eight starts in 2026 with a 5.31 ERA and 46 strikeouts before the inflammation knocked him out. He was not at his dominant best this season, but he is still a top-of-rotation arm in a league where those are irreplaceable.

And he is not the only Brave who is absent.

Ronald Acuña Jr. was placed on the injured list June 14 with a hamstring strain — his second IL stint this season. Before the injury, the Braves held the best record in the National League at 46-25. Since that point, they have been navigating without their most electric player.

Now they are without Acuña and Strider simultaneously.

Chris Sale, who leads all of MLB in strikeouts, remains healthy and at the front of the rotation. Matt Olson, who has 20 home runs, anchors the lineup. The Braves are not a broken team. But the margin for error that a 46-25 record implies has shrunk considerably.

The next month will tell us whether this team's depth is genuine.

### STORY 2: Drake Baldwin 473-Foot HR

#### FB Long-Form — 10:30 AM ET

In the first inning of Tuesday night's Giants-Braves game at Truist Park, Drake Baldwin stepped to the plate against Giants starter Adrian Houser and did something remarkable.

He hit the ball 473 feet.

The leadoff home run was described by trackers as the longest home run in Major League Baseball this season. In a year of power hitters and launch-angle awareness, 473 feet is still a number that makes people stop and look at the scoreboard.

The game did not get far. Atlanta's matchup with San Francisco was suspended in the bottom of the second inning — and will be completed Wednesday, June 17, before the regularly scheduled game between the two teams. Atlanta fans will get a doubleheader today, with the suspended game's conclusion setting the table.

For Baldwin, the home run was a reminder of why he is one of the more compelling stories in the National League this season. He is not simply a catcher with power — he is leading all catchers in Phase 1 of the 2026 All-Star voting with 972,813 votes as of June 15. The gap between Baldwin and second-place Will Smith of the Dodgers (662,883 votes) is substantial.

There is a lot wrong in Atlanta right now, between the Strider IL and Acuña's absence. Baldwin gave the home crowd a reason to cheer.

### STORY 3: Yankees Without Judge

#### FB Long-Form — 12:30 PM ET

The New York Yankees were supposed to struggle without Aaron Judge.

They have not.

New York beat the White Sox on June 16 to move to 44-27 in the AL East — continuing one of the more impressive stretches of baseball a depleted roster has produced this season. Judge has been out since June 4 with a rib stress fracture that is expected to sideline him through mid-August. The four-to-six week timeline, announced at the time of the injury, still holds.

In Judge's absence, Ben Rice has been the story.

Rice, the 27-year-old first baseman who broke out in a big way last season, has taken the vacancy in the middle of the Yankees' lineup and made it his own. He is slashing .300/.393/.638 with 19 home runs and 44 RBIs. He leads the Yankees in every major offensive category. He is fast becoming the argument for why New York can hold on to first place through the summer.

The case for Rice in the All-Star Game is building with every at-bat. The Phase 1 ballot closes June 25. The way he is hitting, the votes will follow.

New York enters Wednesday looking every bit like an AL East team capable of running this out through August. And when Judge comes back? That lineup becomes something else entirely.

### STORY 4: Schwarber-Alvarez HR Race

#### FB Long-Form — 3:00 PM ET

There is no more compelling subplot in Major League Baseball right now than the home run race between Kyle Schwarber and Yordan Alvarez.

They enter the week tied at 24. The gap between them and third place has not closed materially. This is a two-player race, and both players are aware of it.

The context makes it richer. The Home Run Derby is July 13 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. That is Schwarber's home ballpark. He plays his home games in the very place where the Derby will be held this summer. The prospect of Schwarber competing in the Derby in front of his own fans — in his own park, at his own plate, swinging from the left side into the short porch in right — is one of the best storylines the All-Star break could offer.

CBS Sports recently built out an ideal Derby field for 2026: Schwarber, Alvarez, Bryce Harper, Mike Trout (potentially making his Derby debut), and Ben Rice among the names. Whether or not the official invitations match that wish list, the conversation is well underway.

All-Star voting Phase 1 closes June 25. The Derby field will crystallize around the same time. Eight days of home run production by Schwarber and Alvarez could determine a lot.

### STORY 5: All-Star Ballot Update

#### FB Long-Form — 6:00 PM ET

Phase 1 of 2026 MLB All-Star voting closes June 25 — eight days from today. Here is where the races stand as of June 15.

At catcher, Drake Baldwin of the Braves leads all players at the position with 972,813 votes. Will Smith of the Dodgers is in second place with 662,883, a gap of more than 300,000 votes. Baldwin's massive home run on Tuesday only reinforces the case.

At first base, Freddie Freeman of the Dodgers leads with 870,606 votes, with Matt Olson of the Braves close behind at 802,848. Two former Atlanta first basemen — Olson still with the Braves, Freeman having departed for Los Angeles — are running almost neck-and-neck.

In the designated hitter races, Shohei Ohtani leads the NL and Yordan Alvarez leads the AL. Ohtani's total (1.165 million votes per prior reporting) continues to place him among the overall leaders across both leagues.

The All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. Phase 2 voting begins June 29, with selections finalized shortly after. Between now and then, every at-bat matters — both for the performance itself and for the votes it generates.
