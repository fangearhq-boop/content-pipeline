Jose Ramirez Becomes First Guardian to Homer Against All 29 Teams — Two Franchise Records in Six Days

By Marcus Cole | April 13, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Baseball has no shortage of milestones. Players chase hits records, strikeout records, consecutive games, consecutive saves. But there is one achievement that requires not speed, not durability alone, but a combination of power, longevity, and the unique circumstance of facing every opponent in the sport. On Saturday at Truist Park, José Ramírez completed it.

Ramírez pulled an 82 mph pitch from Atlanta Braves starter Martin Perez 403 feet down the left-field line for a solo home run — his first career home run against the Braves, and in doing so became the first player in Cleveland Guardians franchise history to hit at least one home run against all 29 other MLB teams.

The Milestone in Context

The 29-team home run challenge is not an official stat tracked by MLB, but it is a feat celebrated in the baseball community for what it represents: sustained excellence across more than a decade. A player who hits one home run against every team in baseball has to stay healthy, stay productive, and stay in one place long enough to face the full rotation of opponents across multiple seasons.

Ramírez has done all of that — exclusively as a Guardian. In 13-plus seasons, he has never been traded and never signed elsewhere. Every one of his more than 1,600 games has been played in a Cleveland uniform. That loyalty, combined with his offensive production, makes the achievement feel different from a similar feat by a player who bounced around the league.

Two Records in Six Days

Saturday's milestone came just six days after another piece of Guardians history. On April 6, Ramírez appeared in his 1,620th career game as a Guardian, breaking a franchise games played record held by Terry Turner for nearly 108 years. Two franchise records in less than a week, and neither one in a category that typically makes front-page headlines — which somehow makes them more meaningful.

At 33, Ramírez is still an everyday player who makes every team he faces plan around him. His career numbers as of this writing: approximately 286 home runs and 288 stolen bases — totals that have him on course to potentially join baseball's 300/300 club, one of the most exclusive in the sport's history. Only eight players have ever hit 300 home runs and stolen 300 bases: Bobby Bonds, Barry Bonds, Steve Finley, Carlos Beltran, Reggie Sanders, Jeff Bagwell, Larry Walker, and Alex Rodriguez.

The Hall of Fame Argument

The conversation about Ramírez and Cooperstown is not new. But weeks like this one — stacking franchise record on top of franchise record — add another layer to an argument that was already compelling. His career slash line, his doubles production (approaching 400 career), his reputation as one of the best third basemen of his generation, and now his status as the first Guardian to homer against every opponent: the file is getting thicker.

Ramírez has never been a player who chased numbers for the sake of personal glory. He plays the same way every night, gets on base, hits for extra bases, and does the small things that show up in box scores and in the way his teammates talk about him. The milestones are a byproduct of consistency, not a goal in themselves.

What's Next

Ramírez needs approximately 14 more home runs to reach 300 career and 12 more stolen bases to reach 300 SBs. If both happen this season, he joins a club of eight. More immediately, Cleveland continues to push toward the AL Central's upper tier, and Ramírez remains the engine of the lineup. Watch the stolen base total as the weather warms — the 300/300 chase could become the next conversation.
