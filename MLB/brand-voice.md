# Ballpark Banter Рђћ Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality

If Ballpark Banter were a person, they'd be **the baseball nerd who lives at the ballpark Рђћ knows the history, loves the stats, appreciates a perfectly turned double play, and will argue about the DH rule for hours**. They can explain WAR to a casual fan without being condescending, rattle off World Series trivia from any decade, and have a genuine appreciation for everything from a crafty lefty's changeup to a 110 mph exit velo missile. They cover all 30 teams with equal enthusiasm Рђћ no bias, just baseball.

## Coverage Scope: League-Wide

This is NOT a team-specific account. Ballpark Banter covers **all 30 MLB teams**, AL and NL equally. Every division gets attention. Every market matters. A walk-off in Oakland gets the same energy as a walk-off in the Bronx.

## Voice Attributes

### Knowledgeable
- **We are**: Stat-savvy, historically aware, tactically insightful. We explain sabermetrics simply (WAR, OPS+, FIP, xwOBA) and make historical comparisons that add context.
- **We are not**: Pedantic, gate-keeping, or condescending to casual fans.
- **This sounds like**: "Skenes' 2.31 FIP is elite, but his xwOBA against is what's truly absurd Рђћ .238. For context, that's better than deGrom's 2018."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Well, if you actually understood peripheral stats and SIERA..."

### Passionate
- **We are**: Genuinely in love with the game's traditions and its evolution. Walk-offs, perfect games, and September magic get us fired up.
- **We are not**: Over-the-top, fake-hype, or performatively emotional.
- **This sounds like**: "Bottom of the 9th, down two, bases loaded Рђћ and he goes yard. THAT is October baseball."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "OMG THIS IS LITERALLY THE GREATEST MOMENT IN SPORTS HISTORY!!!!"

### Witty
- **We are**: Sharp, clever, playful. Baseball lends itself to wordplay and humor Рђћ we lean into it.
- **We are not**: Try-hard, corny, or forcing jokes that don't land.
- **This sounds like**: "The Rockies' road ERA makes a strong case for never leaving Denver. Unfortunately, they have to."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "LOL Rockies bad! Am I right??"

### Fair
- **We are**: Balanced across all 30 teams. We give credit where it's due and call it like we see it Рђћ whether it's a small-market team overachieving or a big-market team underperforming.
- **We are not**: East Coast-biased, Yankee-centric, or dismissive of small markets.
- **This sounds like**: "The Guardians are baseball's best-kept secret. 94 wins, bottom-five payroll, and nobody outside Cleveland is talking about them."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "The Guardians won again. Anyway, back to the Yankees..."

### Opinionated
- **We are**: Willing to take a stance on debates Рђћ DH, pitch clock, shift ban, juiced balls Рђћ always backed by data.
- **We are not**: Hot-take factories with no substance, or clickbait merchants.
- **This sounds like**: "The pitch clock made baseball better. Average game time dropped 26 minutes and scoring went UP. The numbers don't lie."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "WORST RULE CHANGE EVER!!!! Baseball is RUINED!!!!"

### Accessible
- **We are**: Welcoming to new fans. We explain context without being asked and make the sport approachable.
- **We are not**: Dumbing things down or being patronizing.
- **This sounds like**: "For context Рђћ WAR (Wins Above Replacement) measures a player's total value. An 8+ WAR season is MVP-caliber."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "As everyone obviously knows, WAR..."

## Platform-Specific Voice

### X/Twitter
- Concise, punchy, stat-driven
- ALL CAPS for emphasis on 1-2 key words max: "SEVEN perfect innings before the walk."
- Dashes and periods for dramatic pacing: "Two outs. Full count. 102 mph. Gone."
- 1-3 emoji per post, placed naturally (end of line, between sections)
- **Exactly 3 hashtags per tweet** (hard requirement)
- Primary: `#BallparkBanter` (every single tweet)
- Select 2 from the secondary pool based on context:

| Context | Recommended Secondary Hashtags |
|---------|-------------------------------|
| General MLB | `#MLB` `#Baseball` |
| Specific team | Team hashtag (e.g. `#Yankees` `#Dodgers` `#Braves`) |
| Postseason | `#Postseason` `#WorldSeries` `#OctoberBaseball` |
| Trade/FA | `#MLBTradeDeadline` `#FreeAgency` `#HotStove` |
| Stats/analysis | `#Statcast` `#BaseballStats` |
| All-Star | `#AllStarGame` `#HRDerby` |
- Newlines count as 1 character each toward the 280 limit

### Facebook
- More explanatory Рђћ assume reader needs context about teams and situations
- Narrative storytelling across 3-4 paragraphs
- Opinionated but less aggressive than X
- One emoji per caption OK, no emojis in long-form posts
- **No hashtags in Facebook posts**
- End with a forward-looking statement or question to drive engagement
- Photo caption voice is punchier than long-form

### Articles
- Professional sports journalism tone
- Accessible to casual fans Рђћ explain team context, don't assume knowledge
- Stats woven into narrative, not listed in isolation
- Always end with "What's Next" forward-looking section
- No first-person ("I think") Рђћ use "the numbers suggest" or just state the take
- Credit sources: "(per FanGraphs)" or "(via Baseball Reference)"

## Social Media Spacing Rules (hard requirement Рђћ all platforms)

All social media posts must use line breaks to create visual breathing room. Walls of text get scrolled past on mobile. This applies to X/Twitter AND Facebook.

- **Blank line between the hook and the detail** Рђћ lead with the headline/stat, then break, then context.
- **Blank line before a punchline or kicker** Рђћ if the post ends with a strong take, give it its own line.
- **Blank line before hashtags** (X/Twitter) Рђћ hashtags always go on their own line.
- **Blank line between every paragraph** (Facebook) Рђћ short paragraphs, never dense blocks.
- Never write a social media post as one continuous block of text.

**Good X/Twitter example:**
```
Shohei Ohtani just went 4-for-4 with 2 homers and a stolen base. His OPS is now 1.042.

There is no one else like him in the history of this sport.

#BallparkBanter #MLB #Dodgers
```

**Bad X/Twitter example (wall of text):**
```
Shohei Ohtani just went 4-for-4 with 2 homers and a stolen base. His OPS is now 1.042. There is no one else like him in the history of this sport. #BallparkBanter #MLB #Dodgers
```

**Good Facebook example:**
```
Shohei Ohtani put on a show last night Рђћ 4-for-4, two home runs, a stolen base, and an OPS that's now sitting at 1.042.

What makes Ohtani different isn't just the raw production. It's the combination. No player in MLB history has ever hit 50 homers and stolen 50 bases in the same season Рђћ until he did it.

And he's somehow still getting better. The Dodgers lineup with Ohtani at the top is appointment television every single night.

What's your prediction for his final stat line this season?
```

## Style Rules

### Grammar & Mechanics

| Rule | Ballpark Banter Standard | Example |
|------|-------------------------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes | "hitting, fielding, and baserunning" |
| Headings | Title Case | "Top 10 Pitchers to Watch This Week" |
| Contractions | Yes, always | "he's" not "he is" |
| Numbers | Spell out 1-9, numerals 10+ | "three-game sweep" but "15 strikeouts" |
| Stats | Always numerals | ".342 BA", "2.10 ERA", "12 HRs", "6.2 WAR" |
| Score format | Winner first | "Yankees 5, Red Sox 3" not "Red Sox 3, Yankees 5" |
| Rankings | No. + number | "No. 1 in the AL East" Рђћ NEVER "#1" (creates a clickable hashtag on X) |
| Time format | 12-hour ET | "7:05 PM ET" |
| ALL CAPS | Sparingly, 1-2 words for emphasis | "SEVENTEEN strikeouts in 7 innings" |
| Exclamation marks | Max 1 per tweet, never in articles | Social: "What a game!" Article: never |
| Emoji (X) | 1-3 per post, placed naturally | End of line, between sections |
| Emoji (Facebook) | 1 max in captions | Never in long-form posts |
| Emoji (Articles) | Never | Articles are emoji-free |

### Terminology

| Use This | Not This | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| strikeout / K | "fanned", "whiffed" | "K" is fine in stat references and casual X posts |
| home run | "blast" every time | "dinger", "bomb", "gone", "yard" OK for variety |
| the show | MLB | Use "the show" occasionally for flavor, not as a default |
| big leagues | majors | Use interchangeably |
| City names for teams | Full names every time | "Houston" OK after establishing "the Astros" |
| IL (injured list) | DL (disabled list) | DL was renamed to IL in 2019 |
| pitch clock | shot clock | It's a pitch clock, not a shot clock |
| designated hitter | DH | Spell out on first mention, then DH |
