The All-Star Starters Come Out Tomorrow. Here's What We Know.

By Marcus Cole July 3, 2026

The Announcement Is Tomorrow Night

Phase 2 of MLB All-Star voting closed Thursday, July 2, at noon ET. After six weeks of campaigns, vote pushes, and position battles, the waiting is almost over. The complete All-Star starting lineups for both leagues will be announced live tomorrow night — July 4 at 7:30 PM ET on FOX, right before the Cardinals-Cubs game begins at 8:08 PM.

Full rosters, including reserves and pitchers, follow on Sunday, July 6. The All-Star Game itself is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia.

The Two We Already Know

Two starters are already determined. Ernie Clement of the Toronto Blue Jays locked up the American League DH spot in Phase 1, earning 3,232,932 votes — the most of any AL player. Shohei Ohtani of the Los Angeles Dodgers locked up the National League DH spot with 3,341,257 votes, leading all players in baseball.

Cam Schlittler of the Milwaukee Brewers was already named the AL starting pitcher, making him potentially the first rookie pitcher to start an MLB All-Star Game in recent memory. Schlittler has been one of the best young pitchers in the league this season and his selection reflects the Brewers' dominant run in the NL Central.

The Battles That Were Closest

At American League shortstop, Bobby Witt Jr. of the Kansas City Royals was tracking at 72% of Phase 2 votes against Andrés Giménez of the Guardians. Witt Jr. entered Phase 2 having already led all AL position players other than the locked-in Clement, with 2,513,432 Phase 1 votes. If those vote margins held, Witt Jr. becomes the first Royals infielder to start an All-Star Game in several years.

At National League outfield, the competition was much tighter. Brandon Marsh of the host Philadelphia Phillies was at 23% in the crowded five-way Phase 2 battle — giving him a genuine shot at becoming a first-time All-Star starter on his home field. Marsh was joined in Phase 2 by Andy Pages, Ronald Acuña Jr., Teoscar Hernández, Juan Soto, and Michael Harris II. Phase 2 snapshots showed Aaron Judge leading the AL outfield race at 19%, with Mike Trout at 23% and Byron Buxton at 20%.

At American League first base, Ben Rice of the Yankees and Vlad Guerrero Jr. of the Blue Jays were nearly split heading into the final stretch, making that position one of the last genuine unknowns of the reveal.

What the Announcement Means

For players, being named an All-Star starter carries a different weight than being named to the roster as a reserve. Starters earn the postgame attention, the ceremonial first-pitch introductions, and the narrative of being voted in by fans rather than selected by coaches or players. Brandon Marsh as a first-time starter on his home field in Philadelphia would be one of the better stories of the All-Star break. A Witt Jr. start would cap a first half in Kansas City that has already generated significant buzz around what the Royals are building.

The starting pitcher matchup — AL's Schlittler against whoever the NL selects — sets the tone for the game itself on July 14.

What's Next

Tune in Friday night at 7:30 PM ET on FOX for the live All-Star starting lineup announcement. Full rosters for both leagues come Sunday, July 6. The Home Run Derby at Citizens Bank Park airs July 13 on Netflix. The All-Star Game follows July 14. Tomorrow night's reveal is the first domino.
