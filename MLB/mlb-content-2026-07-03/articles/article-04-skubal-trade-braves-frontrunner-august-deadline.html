The Braves Just Became the Frontrunner for Tarik Skubal

By Marcus Cole July 3, 2026

Why the Braves Are Ahead Now

The Atlanta Braves entered 2026 with one of the deepest rotations in baseball. Spencer Strider was healthy. Chris Sale was back. The plan was airtight. Then Strider went down with right elbow inflammation in the early months of the season, and the calculation shifted. The Braves are 50-34, tied for the second-best record in the National League behind the Brewers, but they are doing it with a rotation that has a significant gap where Strider used to pitch.

That gap is why the Braves have moved to the front of the Tarik Skubal market. According to MLB insider Steve Phillips, Atlanta is now the team most likely to land Skubal before the August 3 trade deadline. They are ahead of the Dodgers. They are ahead of the Yankees. Their need is more urgent, and their ability to construct a competitive package is real.

What the Tigers Are Asking For

The Detroit Tigers made clear early in the summer that they were serious about their price. Their baseline demand starts with two top-50 prospects plus a top-10 organizational arm and major-league-ready depth. ESPN's Jeff Passan and Kiley McDaniel have estimated the odds of a Skubal trade at 85% before August 3 — and that figure reflects how inevitable the market looks, not just how interested the teams are.

Skubal signed his $32 million arbitration contract through this season and becomes a free agent in the fall. His agent is Scott Boras, and the Tigers have no realistic path to an extension at a number that works for both sides. This trade is happening. The only question is which organization assembles the right package.

What Skubal Brings

Tarik Skubal is a two-time Cy Young Award winner. He missed time early in 2026 after a bone spur procedure — he is 2-2 with a 2.22 ERA and 23 strikeouts in his available starts — but his health is not the question it was a year ago. When he pitches, he is the same pitcher who won back-to-back Cy Young awards: elite command, three-pitch mix, ability to pitch deep into games without losing effectiveness.

For the Braves, adding Skubal does not just replace Strider — it gives them a rotation featuring Sale and Skubal at the top, two of the best pitchers in the National League, entering the second half of a pennant race. That combination, with the offense the Braves already have, makes them a legitimate World Series contender. Without a frontline add, they are a strong team in a conference that also includes the Dodgers and Brewers.

The Competition

The Dodgers are still in. They are the favorites at prediction markets, and their farm system has the depth to meet the Tigers' asking price. The Yankees are involved. The Cubs, Padres, and Rays have all been mentioned. The difference between the Braves and the rest of the field is injury-driven urgency — teams that are contenders but not missing a starter have more flexibility on when to commit. The Braves are committed now.

What's Next

The August 3 deadline is 31 days away. The Tigers' official posture has been that Skubal is off limits for now, but that stance is expected to soften as the calendar tightens. The Braves are building their offer. The Dodgers are building theirs. A trade of this magnitude, for a pitcher of Skubal's caliber, typically closes in the final days before a deadline. The month of July is when the conversations happen. August 3 is when someone wins.
