Caminero Is In. Schwarber Is Close. The Netflix Derby Field Is Coming Together.

By Jake Torres July 3, 2026

The First Name On the Board

Junior Caminero is the first player to officially confirm his participation in the 2026 Home Run Derby. The Tampa Bay Rays third baseman, 21 years old, has 28 home runs on the season and was the runner-up at last year's Derby. He knows what the stage is. He said yes immediately.

The Derby is July 13 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. It airs on Netflix — the first time in the event's history it will not air on traditional cable television. Eight hitters compete in a bracket format. One confirmed. Seven spots still being filled.

The Hold-Out Everyone Is Watching

Kyle Schwarber leads all of MLB with 30 home runs. He is on pace for 58 HRs. He has now hit 30 or more home runs in six consecutive seasons — the longest active streak in baseball. The Home Run Derby is at Citizens Bank Park. His park. His fans.

Schwarber told Sports Illustrated he will participate under two conditions: his back issue has to calm down, and he has to make the All-Star team. "Yeah, if everything lines up, I'm there," he said. "If this back deal calms down, then I'll probably do it."

Both conditions are pointing toward yes. His back has not been severe enough to miss games. All-Star voting heavily favors him. But he has not formally committed, and until he does, Caminero remains the only confirmed name in an event that is now 10 days away.

Who Said No

Yordan Alvarez of the Houston Astros leads the American League with 25 home runs. He told The Athletic he will not participate in the 2026 Derby. "I'm still open, but not this year," he said. Alvarez has been in the Derby before and chose to pass this time.

Gunnar Henderson of the Baltimore Orioles — who made a first-round exit at the 2024 Derby in Arlington — has not committed or declined. He is being mentioned as a potential participant but has not made a decision.

The Netflix Angle Changes Everything

Whatever combination of eight hitters ends up on the field July 13, the broadcast context is unlike anything the Derby has seen. For the first time, viewers need a Netflix subscription to watch it. The event moves from ESPN — where it has aired for most of its history — to a streaming platform with a global reach that dwarfs traditional cable audiences.

That shift means the Derby draws an audience that will discover it fresh rather than find it on a familiar channel. If Schwarber commits, the story writes itself: the MLB home run leader competing in front of his own fans, on the biggest new platform in sports television, with Caminero — the 21-year-old who pushed him to the edge last year — back for a rematch.

What's Next

The All-Star roster announcements Sunday, July 6, will determine whether Schwarber's second condition is met. The Derby field will come into focus in the days between now and July 13. If Schwarber is in, this becomes one of the most compelling Derbies in years. If he's out, Caminero enters as the headliner — and the 21-year-old with 28 home runs is a headliner who can carry it.
