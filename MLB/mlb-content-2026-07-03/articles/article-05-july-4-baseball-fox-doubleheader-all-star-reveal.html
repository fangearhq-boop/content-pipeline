Your July 4 Baseball Guide: FOX Doubleheader, All-Star Reveal, and All 15 Games on Peacock Sunday

By Ryan Calloway July 3, 2026

The Saturday Anchor: All-Star Reveal and Two Games on FOX

The Independence Day schedule this year has a centerpiece that no July 4 broadcast has had before. At 7:30 PM ET on FOX, the complete MLB All-Star starting lineups for both leagues will be announced live. Phase 2 voting closed Thursday. Ernie Clement and Shohei Ohtani are already confirmed Phase 1 auto-starters. The rest of the names — the Bobby Witt Jr. vs. Giménez AL shortstop battle result, the crowded NL outfield fight, the AL first base race — come out in real time during a national broadcast on the night most of the country is watching fireworks.

Immediately after the reveal, FOX carries two games. Cardinals at Cubs begins at 8:08 PM ET nationally. Mets at Braves airs at the same time on FOX in regional markets. For Braves fans specifically, the Mets-Braves game carries trade deadline subtext: if the Braves are negotiating for Skubal, what they look like against a rival NL East team on July 4 matters for how the second half is framed.

Friday Night Preview

The holiday weekend began Thursday night on Apple TV+ Friday Night Baseball, with the Rays hosting the Astros at 8:15 PM ET and the Brewers visiting the Diamondbacks at 9:45 PM ET. Both games featured playoff-contending teams in live standings implications — the Rays sit atop the AL East at 50-33, and the Brewers bring the NL's best record at 53-31 into their Arizona series.

Sunday: Star-Spangled Sunday and All 15 Games on Peacock

Sunday, July 5 is where the weekend becomes historically notable. NBC Sports is presenting what it calls "Star-Spangled Sunday" — every one of the 15 scheduled MLB games streaming nationally on Peacock. This is the first time a single media company has broadcast all 15 MLB games on a single day. The traditional Sunday cable patchwork of regional sports networks, local broadcasts, and ESPN Sunday Night Baseball is replaced by one platform.

The Mets at Braves game serves as the Sunday Leadoff on Peacock at noon ET. Padres at Dodgers is Sunday Night Baseball at 7 PM ET — a matchup between two NL West rivals, both above .500 and in the wild card picture. The Dodgers are 56-31, the best record in baseball, and a Sunday night showcase against the Padres is the right closing act for what figures to be the biggest baseball weekend of the summer.

Why This Weekend Matters

July 4 weekend is the traditional halfway point of the baseball season. Teams use it as a marker — who is above or below .500 at the midpoint tends to predict postseason participation better than any other single checkpoint. This year, 25 of 30 MLB teams are within six games of a playoff spot, making the midseason marker unusually meaningful. The standings on the morning of July 5 will clarify, for every front office watching, exactly how aggressive they need to be before August 3.

For the broadcast side, the combination of a live All-Star reveal on FOX July 4, two national games immediately after, and then all 15 games on one streaming platform July 5 gives this weekend a scale it has not had in recent years. It is, by design, a showcase for what major league baseball looks like with multiple broadcast partners all putting their best content forward at the same time.

What's Next

After the July 4 reveal, full All-Star rosters follow July 6. The Home Run Derby is July 13 on Netflix at Citizens Bank Park. The All-Star Game is July 14. And when the regular season resumes after the break, 25 teams that consider themselves in the hunt will have 30 days to decide what they want to look like by the time August 3 arrives. The holiday weekend is the last moment before everything accelerates.
