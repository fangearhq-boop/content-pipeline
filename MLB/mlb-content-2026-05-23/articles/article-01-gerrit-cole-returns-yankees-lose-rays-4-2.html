Cole Returns, Throws 6 Shutout Innings — Yankees Still Lose to Rays 4-2

By Marcus Cole

May 23, 2026

Gerrit Cole made his first start since Game 5 of the 2024 World Series and was brilliant across 6 shutout innings. Tim Hill and the Yankees bullpen gave it all back in the 8th. Tampa Bay improved to 33-15.

Gerrit Cole walked to the mound Friday night at Yankee Stadium for the first time in 569 days, and he looked like the pitcher who had been worth every dollar of his nine-year, $324 million contract. He was precise. He was powerful. He held Tampa Bay to 2 hits and no runs across 6 innings. His fastball averaged 96.1 mph and peaked at 98.6 mph. He threw 50 of 72 pitches for strikes.

The Yankees still lost, 4-2.

Cole's last appearance had been Game 5 of the 2024 World Series. A UCL issue ended his season shortly after and cost him the entirety of 2025. The road back was long — rehab, setbacks, cautious progressions through the minor leagues. When he was activated from the injured list earlier this week, it felt like one of the more significant baseball moments of the spring. Friday night in the Bronx was supposed to be a celebration.

For six innings, it was. Cole was everything the Yankees could have hoped for. His stuff was sharp, his command was precise, and the velocity that once made him one of the game's best starters was very much present. Tampa Bay, the AL's best team at 33-15, could do almost nothing against him.

The 8th Inning

Cole departed after six frames with a 2-0 lead. The Yankees bullpen was tasked with finishing the job. Tim Hill entered in the 8th inning and did not record a single out before allowing 4 runs. The lead evaporated. Tampa Bay took a lead they never relinquished, and the final score — Rays 4, Yankees 2 — did not reflect how well Cole had actually pitched.

It is a cruel twist that one of baseball's most anticipated returns in recent memory ended in a loss, particularly one so directly tied to late-game bullpen failure. Cole pitched to win. His teammates in the bullpen could not protect what he built.

The Rays Keep Rolling

Tampa Bay is 33-15. They have beaten the Yankees in all four meetings this season. Their AL East lead stands at 5.5 games. The Rays entered Friday having already proven themselves the AL's most complete team, and Cole's debut did nothing to slow them down.

For the Yankees, the box score loss stings — but the performance from Cole is the story that matters most going forward. He is healthy. He is throwing hard. He is competitive. The bullpen problems are real and fixable. Cole's health status is what has kept New York fans holding their breath for 18 months, and Friday resolved that question with an emphatic answer.

What's Next

The Rays and Yankees play Game 2 of their series Saturday afternoon in the Bronx. For the Yankees, a win would be a chance to prevent a sweep and respond after Friday's painful loss. For Tampa Bay, a sweep would push their AL East lead even further. Cole is not scheduled to pitch Saturday — his next turn would come later in the week — but his return shifts the Yankees rotation outlook considerably.

When healthy, Gerrit Cole is one of the three or four best pitchers in baseball. Friday reminded everyone of that. The Yankees bullpen is a separate conversation. One problem at a time.
