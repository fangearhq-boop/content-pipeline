The Rays Are 33-15 and Not Slowing Down — Even Cole's Return Couldn't Stop Them

By Marcus Cole

May 23, 2026

Tampa Bay is 33-15, leads the AL East by 5.5 games, and went 4-0 against the Yankees in 2026 — the latest win coming Friday against Gerrit Cole on his highly anticipated return. The Rays are the class of the American League.

There was a story in the Bronx on Friday night about one pitcher's triumphant return after 569 days away. It was a good story. Gerrit Cole threw 6 shutout innings. The fastball touched 98.6 mph. The command was there. Everything New York fans had hoped for came true.

The Rays won anyway.

Tampa Bay is 33-15. That is the best record in the American League. They lead the AL East by 5.5 games. They have won all four of their meetings with the Yankees in 2026. They took Friday's game 4-2 on a 4-run 8th inning that came at the expense of New York's bullpen. Cole was everything the Yankees needed. The bullpen was not. Tampa Bay cashed in.

That is the story of the 2026 AL East so far: the Rays find a way.

Winning Against the Best

The Yankees were one of the more anticipated opponents on Tampa Bay's schedule in late May. Cole's return added a layer of intrigue that made Friday's game appointment viewing across the league. A healthy Cole — even in his first start back — is a legitimate challenge for any lineup.

The Rays solved him for two hits in six innings and ultimately won the game. That is a statement. Not about Cole — he was excellent — but about a Tampa Bay team that is equipped to beat quality pitching. They are patient at the plate, disciplined with their approach, and capable of breaking games open late.

The 4-run 8th inning against the Yankees bullpen was a microcosm of how the Rays operate. They wait for an opening and they take it. Tim Hill provided that opening, and Tampa Bay walked out of the Bronx with another win.

The AL East Picture

At 33-15 with a 5.5-game lead, Tampa Bay has positioned themselves as a heavy favorite to win the AL East division. Their schedule over the next month includes several series against division rivals, and the gap they have built gives them meaningful cushion.

The Yankees, Red Sox, and Blue Jays are all capable of making runs. The AL East has historically been one of baseball's most competitive divisions. But the Rays are not a team that collapses under pressure. Their roster construction, bullpen depth, and analytical approach to roster management have made them consistently competitive, and this year's group appears to be one of their better ones.

The Bigger AL Picture

Beyond the AL East, the Rays' 33-15 record makes them a legitimate American League Championship Series and World Series contender. Only a handful of teams in baseball have a comparable record at this point in the season. Tampa Bay deserves more national conversation than they typically receive.

They beat Cole's return. They beat the Yankees for the fourth time in four tries. They keep adding to a lead that was already comfortable. The Rays are not sneaking up on anyone — this is simply what they do. Saturday's game at Yankee Stadium gives New York a chance to respond. But it will require beating a team that, right now, looks like the best in the American League.
