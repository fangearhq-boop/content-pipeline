Acuña Exits with Left Thumb Bone Bruise — X-Rays Negative, Day-to-Day

By Jake Torres

May 23, 2026

Ronald Acuña Jr. fouled a pitch off his left hand in the 7th inning of Friday's 9-3 win over Miami and did not return. X-rays came back negative. He is day-to-day. The Braves are 35-16.

The Atlanta Braves routed the Miami Marlins 9-3 on Friday night at Truist Park. Spencer Strider struck out nine batters across six innings. Michael Harris II and Mike Yastrzemski both went deep. The offense was clicking and the Braves improved to 35-16, one of the best records in the National League.

None of that is what anyone who follows the Braves was focused on after the game.

Ronald Acuña Jr. fouled a pitch off his left hand in the 7th inning and left the game. The diagnosis that followed: a left thumb bone bruise. X-rays came back negative. He is listed as day-to-day.

Another Health Scare

Acuña's production before exiting was excellent — he went 2-for-3 with a 2-run single. He was not slumping, not forcing things. A pitch got away from a Marlins pitcher, he fouled it off his hand, and another health scare was born.

Context matters here. Acuña tore his ACL in 2024 and missed a significant portion of that season. He had returned to form this spring, looking every bit like the player who won the 2023 NL MVP award. He was placed on the 10-day injured list earlier this month with a left hamstring strain, missed 14 games, and had only recently returned to the lineup.

Two IL stints in May — even with negative X-rays on the most recent — will raise concern among a fan base that has already watched their franchise player go through substantial injury adversity. Day-to-day status is better than the alternative. But the Acuña watch has become a constant in Atlanta, and no outcome other than "cleared to play" will fully ease the anxiety.

Strider Dominant, Offense Erupts

The Braves won this game convincingly without Acuña finishing it. Strider was excellent — six innings, nine strikeouts, minimal traffic — and looked like the frontline starter Atlanta needs him to be after his own injury-interrupted stretch earlier this year. The offense backed him up with nine runs, including home runs from Harris II and Yastrzemski.

The Braves are deep enough to win without Acuña for a few days. At 35-16, they have proven that much. But their ceiling — and their World Series aspirations — are meaningfully higher when he is in the lineup and healthy.

Saturday Status

Acuña's status for Saturday's game at Washington will be one of the day's most-watched injury updates in baseball. If X-rays were negative and the bone bruise is minor, he could be available. If there is lingering pain or grip-strength concerns, another IL stint — even a short one — is possible. The Braves play at Nationals Park in the afternoon. Atlanta will update his status before first pitch.
