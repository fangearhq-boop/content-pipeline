Jacob Misiorowski — The Case for a First-Year Cy Young
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The conversation about the 2026 National League Cy Young Award has to include Jacob Misiorowski, and it has to take him seriously. The Milwaukee Brewers right-hander is 9-3 with a 1.45 ERA, a 0.768 WHIP, and 146 strikeouts — which leads all of Major League Baseball. On June 26, he threw a pitch measured at 105.5 miles per hour, one of the fastest pitches ever recorded in MLB history. The Brewers are 52-31, the best record in the National League. The historical precedent for a first-year pitcher winning the Cy Young Award is thin. The award has almost always gone to established veterans with a track record of dominance. But the argument for precedent giving way to performance gets considerably more compelling when the candidate leads the league in strikeouts, has a sub-1.50 ERA in the second half of the season, and plays for the team with the NL's best record. Misiorowski is not making an argument by reputation. He is making it with the numbers, and the numbers are this good. [Posting Window: 5:30 PM ET]
