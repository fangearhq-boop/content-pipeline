Skubal's First Win in Two Months Comes With a Trade Deadline Five Weeks Away

By Marcus Cole July 1, 2026

Two Days, Two Masterpieces

On June 29, Tarik Skubal threw seven innings against the Minnesota Twins. He allowed one hit, no earned runs, and struck out 13 batters — eight of them in a three-inning stretch. The Tigers won 3-0. Two days later, on June 30, he went to Yankee Stadium and threw six innings against New York. One hit. One earned run. Nine strikeouts, including five consecutive in the fourth inning. Tigers won 9-3.

Twenty-two strikeouts in two consecutive starts. Both one-hitters. His first win since April 18.

The trade deadline is August 3.

The Injury and the Return

Skubal left his April 29 start against the Braves shaking his left arm. He underwent surgery May 6 — a minimally invasive procedure to remove loose bodies from his elbow using a 1.9mm arthroscopic device that significantly reduces recovery time. The initial projection was two to three months. He was back in 38 days.

His June 13 return start against Cleveland was shaky — 4.2 innings, 3 runs. What followed was a month of steady improvement, culminating in the twin performances against Minnesota and New York that looked indistinguishable from his 2025 Cy Young form.

That form: 13-6, 2.21 ERA, 241 strikeouts, a 0.891 WHIP — the best in the major leagues that season. He is the reigning AL Cy Young Award winner. He was on a path toward a third consecutive AL Cy Young before the elbow surgery interrupted the campaign. The back-to-back one-hitters suggest he has found that path again.

The Deadline Math

Detroit is not a playoff team. The Tigers entered July sitting well outside Wild Card contention. At the August 3 trade deadline, contending teams will look to add starting pitching, and the available market will feature a handful of names that command the league's attention. Skubal's name will be at the top of that list.

ESPN's Jeff Passan reported an 85% trade probability before Skubal's late-June surge. That number is not going down after two consecutive one-hitters. The Los Angeles Dodgers have been identified as the frontrunner in trade discussions. Skubal is earning $32 million in 2026 and is a pending free agent — a team acquiring him would have him for the final two months of this season plus a playoff run before his market opens in the offseason.

His projected open-market value is expected to rival the largest contracts ever given to a starting pitcher. For a contending team looking at the next several years, trading for him is not just a rental — it is an audition for what comes next. The Dodgers understand that better than anyone.

What's Next

Skubal will make his next start before the All-Star break. Every outing between now and August 3 is both a performance and a market signal. The Tigers have no incentive to rush negotiations. Skubal, for his part, has said he will address contract matters after the season. The trade talks are happening between front offices, not between player and team. What matters now is that the player at the center of those talks is healthy, dominant, and making the case for his own market value in real time — on a national stage, against the Yankees, in the final days of June.
