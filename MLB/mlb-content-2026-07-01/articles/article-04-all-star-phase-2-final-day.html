All-Star Phase 2 Closes Tomorrow: What You Need to Know Before the Polls Shut

By Marcus Cole July 1, 2026

The Deadline

All-Star Phase 2 voting closes tomorrow — July 2 — at noon Eastern Time. Tonight is the last real window for fans to vote in any meaningful volume. The polls close midday, which means the overnight hours and early morning of July 2 represent the final sustained opportunity to influence the starting lineups for the 2026 All-Star Game at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia.

What's Already Set

One major piece of the All-Star puzzle has already been resolved. Cam Schlittler has been announced as the American League starting pitcher — a selection that makes him the first rookie to start the Midsummer Classic as a pitcher in recent memory. That designation came through the commissioner's selection process rather than the fan vote, but it is now confirmed. Schlittler will take the mound for the American League on July 15.

The Phase 2 vote is determining the remaining starters — the position players who will round out both leagues' starting lineups. The margins in close races can be decided in the final hours of voting, and tonight through early tomorrow morning is when fans of players in tight battles have the most leverage.

What Comes After the Vote

The full All-Star rosters will be announced on July 4, airing on FOX. That announcement will include the complete starting lineups (reflecting the Phase 2 vote results), the pitching staffs, the reserve selections, and any final commissioner's picks. July 4 is the first time the complete picture of both leagues' rosters will be public.

The Home Run Derby is July 13, eight days before the All-Star Game. This year's Derby will air on Netflix — a first for the event and a significant change in how the audience for the Derby will be reached. Citizens Bank Park is the venue, and the field for the Derby has not yet been finalized. The most prominent open question in Derby participation involves Kyle Schwarber, the Phillies' designated hitter and the major league home run leader at 30 on the season, who has not publicly committed to competing at his home stadium.

The Setting

Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia is the host venue for All-Star Week. The park has a reputation as a hitter-friendly environment, and a home crowd for the Phillies' players who earn starting spots will be among the louder All-Star audiences the game has seen in recent years. Philadelphia sports fans have a well-documented relationship with their teams, and All-Star Week at Citizens Bank will give them a chance to show up for a national event.

The All-Star Game itself is July 15. The week runs from the Derby on July 13 through the game two days later, with various events and activities filling the schedule around those two anchor events.

What's Next

Vote before noon Eastern on July 2. Rosters drop July 4 on FOX. The Derby is July 13 on Netflix. The All-Star Game is July 15 at Citizens Bank Park. After the break, the second half of the season begins — and the trade deadline follows on August 3, which will reshape rosters across the league before the final push toward October. The All-Star break is the midpoint. Tonight is the last chance to have a say in who represents each league when the game is played.
