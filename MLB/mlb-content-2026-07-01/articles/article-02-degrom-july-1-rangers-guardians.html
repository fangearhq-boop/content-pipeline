Jacob deGrom Threw 7 Innings Today. The Return Is Complete.

By Jake Torres July 1, 2026

The Line

Jacob deGrom, July 1, 2026, against the Cleveland Guardians: 7 innings, 2 earned runs, Rangers win 4-2. The damage was a Jose Manzardo two-run home run. Everything else was deGrom doing what deGrom does. The Texas Rangers won their sixth consecutive game. deGrom went 4-0 during the month of June.

That is the line. Here is what it means.

The Background

Jacob deGrom is one of the most talented pitchers of his generation. He is also one of the most injured. He missed significant portions of the 2023 and 2024 seasons with elbow and back injuries that, in a baseball context, raised legitimate questions about whether he would ever return to the form that made him a two-time Cy Young Award winner in New York. The Rangers signed him to a historic contract and watched him spend more time in the training room than on the mound in the two years that followed.

The return that was talked about for so long began earlier this season and has now accumulated enough sustained evidence to move from "promising" to "confirmed." A pitcher does not go 4-0 in a calendar month, throwing 7 innings per start, on luck or circumstance. He does it by being healthy and effective.

What 4-0 in June Means

Four wins in a single month, with no losses, represents a sustained stretch that removes the qualifiers from the comeback narrative. This is no longer "deGrom looked good in a start last week." This is deGrom as a constant — as a pitcher who is reliably available, reliably deep into games, and reliably keeping his team in contention.

The Rangers' six-game winning streak is not incidental to deGrom's performance. Texas is competing in the American League West. Every deGrom start is a game where the bullpen gets rest and the offense needs to produce only enough to win a game where the starting pitcher is controlling the opposition. On July 1, that meant needing four runs against the Cleveland Guardians. Texas scored four. Rangers win.

The Manzardo Homer

Jose Manzardo hit a two-run home run that accounted for both of deGrom's earned runs. That is the only blemish in an otherwise dominant afternoon. In the context of a full recovery from serious injury, a two-run allowed over seven innings against a competitive lineup is not a flag — it is a footnote. The arc of today's game belongs to deGrom.

What's Next

deGrom will pitch again before the All-Star break. The Rangers are buyers at the trade deadline, not sellers, and the pitching staff around their ace will be the subject of offseason scrutiny if Texas finishes the first half in a strong position. For now, the most relevant baseball story about Jacob deGrom is this: he is healthy, he went 4-0 in June, and today he threw 7 innings. For a player whose career has included more injury updates than pitching lines in recent years, that is a story that deserves to be told plainly.
