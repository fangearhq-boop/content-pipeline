**Long-Form Post**
*Posting Window: 3:30 PM ET*

Konnor Griffin — 19 years old, the No. 1 overall prospect in baseball, the ninth pick in the 2024 draft — was called up for the Pirates' home opener. In his first MLB at-bat, he hit an RBI double. Then, within days of his debut, Griffin signed a 9-year, $140 million contract extension.

$140 million. For a player who hadn't played a single big league game when negotiations started. That's the largest contract ever given to a pre-debut player. It broke the record set just the week prior — when the Mariners gave Colt Emerson 8 years and $95 million.

The message from Pittsburgh ownership is clear: they believe this kid is a franchise cornerstone. They're not waiting for him to prove it over multiple seasons. They're betting now, before the market figures out what he's worth.

Griffin had posted a .438 average in five Triple-A games before the call-up. At 19, he's playing with the poise of someone years older.

For a Pirates fan base that has endured a lot of rebuilds and false starts, Griffin's arrival — and the contract commitment behind it — feels like something genuinely different.
