Best in Baseball: Dodgers 11-3 After Ohtani Leadoff Homer and Sheehan's Gem

By Marcus Cole | April 12, 2026

Shohei Ohtani hit a leadoff home run on Friday night. 104.5 miles per hour off the bat. 390 feet to right field. First batter. First inning. Fourth home run of the season.

Then Teoscar Hernandez hit a three-run shot immediately after, and by the time the Texas Rangers recorded their first out, the Dodgers were ahead 4-0 and the game was effectively decided. Emmet Sheehan took it from there — six innings, four hits, six strikeouts — and Los Angeles won 6-3 to move to 11-3, the best record in Major League Baseball.

Two weeks into the 2026 season, the Dodgers are doing something that hasn't been done since 1900.

The Historic Start

Los Angeles has scored 45 runs in its first four road games of the season — the most by any team in their first four road games since 1900. That figure covers more than a century of Major League Baseball, through expansion and rule changes and the designated hitter and everything else that has altered the game. Nobody has done it in the first four road games like this Dodgers team has.

The offensive depth goes well beyond any single player. Ohtani leads the charge with four home runs. Max Muncy hit three home runs in Thursday's walk-off win over Texas alone, including the decisive solo shot in the ninth inning. Hernandez has been productive in the middle of the order. Andy Pages has contributed across multiple games.

And all of this is happening without Mookie Betts, who has been on the 10-day injured list since April 4 with a right oblique strain. Manager Dave Roberts reported on April 10 that Betts is "symptom-free" and on track to return before the end of the month. Hyeseong Kim has filled in at shortstop since Betts went down.

Ohtani's Two-Way Season Watch

Friday's leadoff homer was part of a broader statement Ohtani is making in his second full two-way season. His Opening Day pitching debut on March 31 against Cleveland was exceptional — six innings, zero runs, one hit, six strikeouts. His batting has been equally consistent: a 45-game consecutive on-base streak dating back through the 2025 season remains the longest active streak in the majors.

The leadoff home run was the kind of swing that changes the energy of a game before it really starts. Rangers pitching had no chance to settle in, no chance to establish early momentum. Ohtani's ability to do that as the first batter of an inning — with the kind of exit velocity that most hitters only generate in their best moments — is something opposing teams have to plan around every single time he steps to the plate.

His third pitching start of the season is coming soon. The Dodgers have been careful managing his workload as he ramps up to full two-way capacity. When he takes the mound again, the national attention will be significant.

Sheehan Builds His Case

While Ohtani and the offense generated the headlines, Emmet Sheehan's performance on Friday deserves recognition. He improved to 2-0 with a quality start — six innings, four hits, six strikeouts — against a Rangers lineup that had come to Dodger Stadium believing they could compete in this series.

The Dodgers' rotation depth has been a quiet part of their exceptional start. When the top of the rotation needs a rest, when Ohtani's pitching appearances are still being managed carefully, arms like Sheehan provide the bridging performances that keep a team's record intact. Going 6-3 against Texas in back-to-back nights, after Thursday's walk-off drama, required the kind of professionalism Sheehan provided.

What's Next

The Dodgers close out the Rangers series Sunday before moving on to their next opponent. The Betts return will be the most-watched storyline of the next two weeks — plugging the best defensive shortstop in the National League back into a lineup already producing at a historic clip will only make an already-frightening team more dangerous.

At 11-3 with the best record in baseball, the Dodgers are not just a preseason favorite living up to expectations. They're exceeding them. Two weeks in, they look like exactly the team most analysts predicted — plus a few additional statistical notes that suggest the ceiling might be even higher than anyone imagined in March.
