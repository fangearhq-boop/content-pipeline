Four-Way HR Race: Judge, Murakami, Álvarez, Rice All in Contention

By Jake Torres May 7, 2026

Baseball's Best Story Gets Better

The MLB home run race heading into May's second week is not a two-man contest anymore. Aaron Judge (Yankees) and Munetaka Murakami (White Sox) sit atop the leaderboard tied at 14, where they've been for several days. But now Yordan Álvarez (Astros) and Ben Rice (Yankees) have both climbed to 12 home runs — and each represents a legitimate threat to the leaders if the gap does not close over the next few weeks.

All four men are on pace for 50-plus home runs over a full season. All four have been among the most productive hitters in baseball in 2026. The combination of a legitimate four-way race, historically unprecedented individual performances, and a cast of characters from three different teams makes this the most engaging power story in the sport since the 2019 surge.

Murakami's Impossible Season

The number that keeps standing out when you look at Munetaka Murakami's 2026 season is this: of all his extra-base hits this year, every single one has been a home run. That number is now over 14. The previous MLB record for most consecutive extra-base hits that were all home runs, dating to records that go back to 1900, was 10 — set by Dae-ho Lee in 2016. Murakami has already shattered that mark, and the season is not yet two months old.

What does that mean, practically? Murakami has not doubled off the wall. He has not legged out a triple. When he has made hard contact that went past the outfielders, it has flown over the fence. His five-game home run streak earlier in the season tied the MLB rookie record. The White Sox, despite their modest overall record, have a player who is doing something the sport has no modern frame of reference for.

Judge and the 400-HR Path

Aaron Judge is the reigning home run king, the face of the AL East's best team, and the reason the Yankees are the most-watched franchise in baseball. At 14 home runs through about 40 games, he is on a pace that would project to 55-plus over a full season. More relevantly for the individual arc: Judge is closing in on 400 career home runs, needing approximately 20 more to join that club. If he maintains his current pace, he could reach that milestone in late July or August.

What makes Judge's situation particularly interesting is what is sitting two spots behind him in the Yankees' own lineup. Ben Rice is also at 12 home runs. The Yankees have the race's No. 1 seed and the No. 4 seed on the same roster — a dynamic that mirrors the Mantle-Berra pairings of Yankees lore.

Álvarez: The Quiet Threat

Yordan Álvarez leads all of MLB in OPS (1.245), slugging percentage (.779), and RBIs. He is not simply a power hitter in the traditional definition — he is the most complete offensive player in baseball right now by the numbers. At 12 home runs, he is two behind the leaders. At his current offensive pace, two home runs is a week of games. Álvarez does not draw the same attention as Judge or Murakami, but every scout in baseball has him circled as the player most likely to catch fire and make this a three-way tie at the top very quickly.

What To Watch

The race will turn on streaks. Murakami had five home runs in five games earlier this season. Judge hit 11 in his last 21 games entering this week. Power hitters go through phases, and the first player to string four or five together in a week can build a lead that lasts. With all four men clustered within two home runs of each other, that opportunity is there for anyone who gets hot. The May stretch run of this HR race is going to be appointment viewing.
