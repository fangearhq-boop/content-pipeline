Skubal Recovery Update: Best Case August — Tigers' Rotation in Crisis

By Marcus Cole May 7, 2026

The Best-Case Math

The best news about Tarik Skubal's elbow surgery is that it went as planned. The procedure to remove loose bodies from his left elbow is an established surgery with a known recovery path. An orthopedic surgeon who reviewed the timeline this week put early August as the optimistic end of the spectrum: six to eight weeks of physical therapy and range-of-motion work, followed by another six to eight weeks of throwing buildup, and you are looking at the beginning of August if everything proceeds without complications.

The more realistic forecast is late August or early September. Recovery does not always follow the minimum timeline, and with a two-time Cy Young Award winner's career involved, the Tigers are going to be appropriately cautious about returning him before his arm is fully ready. A setback from rushing Skubal back early would be far more damaging to the organization than another month of his absence.

What the Rotation Looks Like Without Him

Detroit's rotation around Tarik Skubal was already a question heading into the season. Now that question has been answered in the most difficult way possible. The active rotation consists of Framber Valdez, Jack Flaherty, Keider Montero, and Ty Madden — Madden having stepped into Skubal's slot immediately after the injury. This is a serviceable rotation, but it is not one built to contend in the American League Central with the depth it had before Skubal went down.

The picture is actually worse when you account for the full list of unavailable arms. Casey Mize is on the injured list. Justin Verlander is not available. Reese Olson underwent shoulder surgery in February and will not pitch in 2026 at all. The Tigers entered the season expecting to build their rotation around one of the best pitchers in baseball — and now that pitcher will not return until the final weeks of the regular season at best.

The Trade Deadline Becomes Critical

Detroit's situation between now and the August 1 trade deadline is going to be a defining test for the organization. The choices are straightforward but not easy: they can absorb the losses, play the rotation they have, and wait for Skubal to return while accepting whatever record that approach produces. Or they can identify a starting pitcher upgrade available at the deadline and pay the prospect cost to add one.

The argument for patience is that Skubal returning by August 1 still leaves two months of playoff chase — if Detroit is in contention. The argument for action is that without intervention, the Tigers' rotation depth does not give them a reasonable chance to be in contention by August. That question will define their July.

Skubal's Longer Context

Tarik Skubal won the AL Cy Young Award in back-to-back seasons. He is one of the three or four best pitchers in baseball when healthy. Detroit's front office built the team's competitive window around his presence in the rotation for the next several years. None of that changes based on one elbow surgery. But the 2026 season for the Tigers is now a race against a clock that is counting down to August — and the rotation they have in the meantime is not the one they planned.
