Cubs Walk Off for the Third Straight Night — 14 Wins in a Row at Wrigley

By Marcus Cole May 7, 2026

The Cubs Keep Finding a Way

Wednesday night at Wrigley Field followed the same script as Tuesday, and Monday before that. Michael Busch came to the plate in the bottom of the 10th inning with the bases loaded, the game tied at six, and the Reds' bullpen on the hook. Busch worked a bases-loaded walk out of Brock Burke. The Cubs won 7-6. Wrigley erupted.

Three consecutive walk-off wins over the same opponent. The last time the Cubs pulled that off was 1943. Chicago's Wrigley Field win streak now stands at 14 games — the longest since 2008 — and the overall winning streak is at 8. The Cubs are 24-12, lead the NL Central by 2.5 games, and have won 18 of their last 21 games. The 2026 Cubs at the Friendly Confines are one of the great home runs in recent franchise history.

What This Streak Means

Numbers tell part of the story. The Cubs' 17-5 start at Wrigley is the best the franchise has posted through 22 home games since 1985. They have six walk-off wins in 37 games overall, which is a 30-year franchise first. When these Cubs need a run, they are finding it — in the 9th inning, in the 10th inning, and in every dramatic form the game offers.

The walk-off frequency at Wrigley this season isn't just luck. It reflects a team that understands how to grind out at-bats, that has depth in the lineup capable of producing in high-leverage moments, and that has the bullpen depth to stay in games that do not resolve cleanly in nine innings. All three of those qualities are present in Chicago's 2026 roster.

Busch as the Recurring Hero

Michael Busch does not lead the Cubs in home runs, RBIs, or batting average. He is not the player whose name gets attached to a headline when you think of Chicago's lineup. But he keeps turning up in the moments that matter most. His walk-off contribution on Wednesday was not a home run — it was a patient at-bat that identified a ball on a full count and took the free base. That is precisely the kind of plate discipline that separates winning teams from ones that go on nine-game win streaks and then fall apart.

The Cubs have a version of Busch at multiple spots in the lineup. That is the point. This is not a one-or-two-star team with filler around them. This is a roster that generates production from multiple angles — and at Wrigley Field, that production has been lethal for visiting teams.

NL Central and Beyond

Chicago's 2.5-game NL Central lead over the Cardinals is real but not insurmountable. The Cardinals are playing well — Jordan Walker's breakout season has given St. Louis a genuine offensive threat — and they will be in this race through June at minimum. But the Cubs have the advantage of the hottest home-field stretch in baseball and a brand of play that does not seem to be slowing down.

If the Cubs finish Thursday's series finale against Cincinnati and extend the Wrigley streak to 15, they will be within range of historical franchise records at home. The 2026 summer at Wrigley is shaping up to be something fans who attend regularly will remember for a long time.
