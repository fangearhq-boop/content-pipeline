José Ramírez Rewrites the Cleveland Record Books — Again

By Jake Torres | April 14, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Two franchise records in ten days. That's what José Ramírez delivered to the Cleveland Guardians in early April — a pair of history-making moments that cement his place as the greatest player in franchise history.

The first came on April 6, when Ramírez appeared in his 1,620th career game with the Guardians — breaking the franchise all-time games played record held by Terry Turner since 1918. The mark had stood for nearly 108 years. Ramírez erased it in the middle of a Tuesday night game, the way great players make history: quietly, then undeniably.

The second was louder. On Saturday, April 11, at Truist Park in Atlanta, Ramírez stepped in against Braves starter Martín Pérez and launched a 108.1 mph rocket 408 feet over the left-field fence. The home run against Pérez was the last item on Ramírez's list — completing an achievement no Guardians or Indians player had ever accomplished: a home run against all 29 other MLB teams.

The 29-Team Club

Hitting a home run against all 29 opponents requires years of opportunity, plate appearances across every ballpark in the game, and consistency in production that holds up regardless of the pitcher, the stadium, or the stakes. For Ramírez, the Braves were the final holdout — his career had simply never produced a home run off their pitching staff until that April afternoon.

The moment was documented across the MLB milestone tracker maintained by FOX Sports, which confirmed Ramírez as the first player in Cleveland franchise history to complete the 29-team set. The 33-year-old entered the game knowing the record was there to claim, and Pérez's 0-2 changeup over the heart of the plate made the moment possible.

Ramírez reacted with characteristic calm. He's 33 years old, has been through six playoff runs, and has learned to let the moments find him rather than chasing them.

A Career Unlike Any Other in Cleveland

Ramírez signed his first professional contract with the Guardians out of the Dominican Republic in 2009. He has never played for another team. In an era defined by free agency and franchise-hopping, his loyalty to Cleveland is as unusual as it is admirable — and the franchise has reciprocated with long-term commitments that have kept him in a Guardians uniform through at least the late 2020s.

His résumé is historic by any standard: four All-Star selections, multiple Silver Slugger awards, an MVP runner-up finish, and now sole possession of both the franchise games played record and the 29-team home run distinction. His career home run total keeps climbing. His defense at third base remains elite.

The question of whether Ramírez belongs in the conversation for the best player of his generation is worth asking. His counting stats and advanced metrics both suggest he does.

What's Next

Ramírez will keep doing what he does — hitting line drives into the gaps, launching home runs into the bleachers, and making plays at third base that make highlight reels. The 2026 season offers more milestones as he climbs the all-time lists in multiple categories. Cleveland's playoff hopes rest significantly on his production. For Guardians fans, watching Ramírez play is the privilege of watching one of the franchise's all-time greats at what may be his peak — every game another entry in a history that belongs entirely to Cleveland.
