Shohei Ohtani Is Back on the Mound — and Matching Ichiro's Records at the Plate

By Ryan Calloway | April 14, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

There is no template for evaluating Shohei Ohtani. He pitches every five days and bats every day. He has been the most dangerous hitter in the American League and one of the most overpowering pitchers in the majors in the same season. Normal baseball comparisons don't apply.

That point was reinforced again in 2026 when Ohtani made his season debut on the pitcher's mound. Six innings. Zero runs. Six strikeouts. One hit allowed. Three walks. The Los Angeles Dodgers won. And Ohtani's scoreless innings streak — dating back to the 2025 regular season — extended to 28 2/3 innings, the longest active streak in all of Major League Baseball and the longest of his career.

At the plate, meanwhile, Ohtani reached base for the 43rd consecutive game, dating back to last season — tying Ichiro Suzuki's 2009 mark for the longest on-base streak by a Japanese-born player in MLB history.

He did all of this in the same week.

The Pitching Return

Ohtani missed pitching time in 2024 while recovering from Tommy John surgery performed in September 2023. His full return as a two-way player was one of the defining storylines of the 2025 season. In 2026, he returns healthy, experienced, and with a refinement in his arsenal that has made him even more effective.

The 28 2/3-inning scoreless streak is built on a foundation of elite command and a pitch mix that continues to confound hitters. His fastball velocity has held. His splitter remains one of the most lethal pitches in the game. When Ohtani is on the mound, opposing lineups approach each plate appearance differently — there is no comfortable at-bat against him.

His debut outing against the Cleveland Guardians — a lineup that ranks among the better offensive units in the American League — demonstrated that the offseason did nothing to diminish his effectiveness. Six innings of one-hit ball against that lineup is a statement start.

Matching Ichiro

The 43-game on-base streak that ties Ichiro Suzuki's 2009 mark is a reminder that Ohtani's offensive production is not just about power numbers. Yes, he hit 44 home runs in 2023 and continues to produce at a high rate. But an on-base streak of 43 games — which encompasses hits, walks, and hit-by-pitches over a span beginning in the 2025 season — speaks to consistency and patience at the plate across two separate calendar years.

Ichiro Suzuki's 2009 season was legendary. He finished with a .352 batting average and walked enough to match his already remarkable on-base total. Tying his Japanese-born player record is not a trivial achievement — it places Ohtani in a category that includes one of the most disciplined hitters in baseball history.

The Dodgers signed Ohtani to a 10-year, $700 million contract beginning in 2024 because they believed he would be the defining player of the era. The early returns on that investment have been extraordinary.

What's Next

Ohtani will make his next pitching start in approximately five days, while continuing to bat daily in the Dodgers' lineup. His next milestones are already being tracked: the scoreless innings streak, the on-base streak, and the ongoing conversation about what kind of season he's building toward. For baseball fans, the 2026 season offers the privilege of watching the most unique player in the sport's history do his best work. That's not something to take for granted.
