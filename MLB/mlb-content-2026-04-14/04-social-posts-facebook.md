⚾ TOMORROW IS JACKIE ROBINSON DAY — BASEBALL'S MOST SACRED DATE ⚾

On April 15, 1947, a man put on a Brooklyn Dodgers uniform, walked onto the field at Ebbets Field, and changed American history.

Jackie Robinson didn't just break baseball's color barrier. He did it with dignity, grace, and excellence that transcended the sport. He was spat at, threatened, and targeted — and responded by becoming Rookie of the Year, a six-time All-Star, and a Hall of Famer. He later called it the greatest of his life's accomplishments.

Tomorrow, April 15, 2026, every player, manager, coach, and umpire across all of Major League Baseball will wear No. 42 — Robinson's number, the only number retired by every franchise simultaneously. It's not just a gesture. It's the league saying: this one moment was bigger than all of us.

The Jackie Robinson Celebration Game takes place tomorrow at the Jackie Robinson Training Complex in Vero Beach, FL — the site where Robinson trained with the Dodgers' minor league organization in 1946.

This year marks the 79th anniversary of his debut. The games will all be good. But none of them will be the main event.

The main event is remembering what one person's courage can do.

Happy Jackie Robinson Day. 🖤⚾
