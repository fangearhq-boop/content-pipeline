### STORY 1: Murakami Sets Japanese-Born HR Record

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM ET
Baseball has a new record holder — and it has nothing to do with the player you'd expect.

Munetaka Murakami, the White Sox's Japanese import and three-time NPB batting champion, hit his 4th home run in his first 8 MLB games on Saturday. That surpasses Shohei Ohtani's 3 HRs in his first 8 games in 2018 — the most ever by a Japanese-born player at that point in their career.

Murakami, who makes his MLB debut at 31 after years as one of the best hitters in NPB, is off to a statement start in Chicago. The White Sox, fresh off a historically bad stretch of seasons, are finally getting something to cheer about.

And yes, breaking Ohtani's record — even this one — is very much a big deal.

### STORY 3: Chase DeLauter Historic Start

#### Long-Form Post -- 12:30 PM ET
Only one player in MLB history had ever done what Chase DeLauter just did.

The Cleveland Guardians outfielder hit 4 home runs in his first 3 career MLB regular-season games during Opening Week. The only other player to accomplish that? Trevor Story in 2016. And DeLauter didn't do it against soft competition — his four blasts came off three different All-Stars: Logan Gilbert, George Kirby, and Andrés Muñoz.

DeLauter's performance earned him the AL Rookie of the Week for the first week of the season. He and Colt Stewart (NL) both won their respective ROTW awards — the first time two rookies had done that in the same week in MLB history.

The 2026 rookie class is already something special. And DeLauter may be the headliner.

### STORY 4: Misiorowski Opening Day K Record

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM ET
Jacob Misiorowski's Opening Day was historic — and most people outside of Milwaukee might have missed it.

The 23-year-old Brewers right-hander struck out 11 batters in 5 innings in the season opener against the White Sox, a 14-2 Milwaukee win. That's the most Ks by any pitcher on Opening Day in Brewers franchise history, breaking the record of 8 set by Ben Sheets in 2002 and later tied by Freddy Peralta.

At 23 years, 357 days old, Misiorowski was the youngest pitcher to record 10+ strikeouts on Opening Day since Felix Hernandez struck out 12 batters at age 20 in 2007. Nine of his 11 Ks came on his fastball; two came on his curveball.

The Brewers combined for 20 strikeouts in the game — tying the modern MLB record for Ks in a nine-inning game since 1900.

Milwaukee's rotation was already considered a strength. After Opening Day, it looks like a legitimate ace threat is in the building.

### STORY 5: Paul Skenes Opening Day

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM ET
Nobody expected this from Paul Skenes on Opening Day.

The Pittsburgh Pirates ace — NL Cy Young runner-up in 2025, one of the most anticipated arms in baseball — lasted just two-thirds of an inning in the season opener against the Mets. He allowed 5 runs on 37 pitches. The first seven Mets batters all had productive at-bats. Brett Baty hit a 3-run triple on a ball that center fielder Oneil Cruz misjudged.

It was the shortest career start of Skenes' young career, and the kind of game that every pitcher goes through — but rarely on the biggest stage of the regular-season calendar.

The good news? Skenes has bounced back before. His spring was strong. One Opening Day implosion doesn't define a season.

The Mets won 11-7 in what turned into a slugfest.
