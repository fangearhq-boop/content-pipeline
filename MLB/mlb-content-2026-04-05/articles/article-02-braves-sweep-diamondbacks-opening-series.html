Braves Sweep Diamondbacks, Cap Dominant Home Opening Series

By Jake Torres | April 5, 2026

Atlanta closed out a 3-0 series sweep with a 6-4 victory Sunday, two days after a 17-2 blowout sent a message to the rest of the National League.

The Atlanta Braves needed just one weekend to make their intentions clear for 2026. After dismantling the Arizona Diamondbacks 17-2 on Saturday — the most lopsided margin of the young season — Atlanta finished the sweep Sunday with a 6-4 victory at Truist Park. The Braves won their home opening series 3-0 without ever being seriously threatened.

Three games. Three wins. A statement made before the end of the first week.

The Dominant Weekend

The tone was set early. In Game 2 on Saturday, Atlanta's offense exploded for 17 runs — powered by home runs from Matt Olson, Dominic Smith, Mauricio Dubon, and Jorge Mateo — overwhelming Arizona's pitching staff and sending the Truist Park crowd home thoroughly entertained.

Sunday's 6-4 win was more measured, but no less convincing. The Braves controlled the game throughout, building an early lead and withstanding Arizona's late push. The sweep was never in genuine doubt.

NL Implications

The Braves enter Week 2 as one of baseball's hottest teams. Their lineup features consistent power up and down the order, their rotation has depth that few teams in the NL can match, and their bullpen has been reliable early on.

Arizona, the defending NL champions, will need to regroup quickly after getting dominated at Truist Park. Their pitching staff struggled to contain Atlanta's lineup, and the 17-2 loss will linger in team meetings for a while.

The Bigger Picture

It's only seven games into the season, and early results are often misleading. But the Braves have looked like a team playing with urgency — the kind that comes from years of playoff experience and the hunger to compete for a championship. Atlanta has the pieces. The question is whether they can stay healthy over 162 games.

What's Next

The Braves move forward into Week 2 with momentum and confidence. Arizona heads home to regroup and face the early reality of their season. The NL standings are still being written, but after this weekend, Atlanta has staked its claim as a serious contender. The regular season is a marathon — but the Braves just ran the first mile faster than almost everyone else.
