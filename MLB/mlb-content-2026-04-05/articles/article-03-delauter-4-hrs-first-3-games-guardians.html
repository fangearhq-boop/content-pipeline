Chase DeLauter Joins Trevor Story as Only Players to Hit 4 HRs in First 3 MLB Games

By Ryan Calloway | April 5, 2026

The Cleveland Guardians outfielder's historic opening week earned him AL Rookie of the Week and put him in rarefied company in baseball history.

In 2016, Trevor Story announced his arrival in the major leagues by hitting 4 home runs in his first 3 career games. That performance became the stuff of legend — a jaw-dropping debut that immediately put the baseball world on notice.

In 2026, Chase DeLauter did it again.

The Cleveland Guardians outfielder hit 4 home runs in his first 3 career MLB regular-season games during Opening Week, becoming just the second player in MLB history to accomplish the feat. He's now in the same sentence as Story — and that's not a sentence many players get to inhabit.

The Quality of the Competition

What makes DeLauter's performance even more remarkable is who he hit those home runs against. All four blasts came off pitchers who had appeared in All-Star Games: Logan Gilbert, George Kirby, and Andrés Muñoz. This wasn't a young player feasting on soft competition — DeLauter was beating elite arms.

The home runs came in sequence across three games, each one seemingly more emphatic than the last. By the time DeLauter connected off Muñoz, Cleveland fans were already treating Opening Week like a pennant race.

Hardware and History

DeLauter's performance earned him the AL Rookie of the Week award for the first week of the 2026 season. He shared the rookie spotlight with Colt Stewart, who won the NL honor — the first time two rookies had simultaneously claimed their respective Rookie of the Week awards in MLB history.

The 2026 rookie class has generated significant buzz even before the season started. DeLauter, along with Stewart and a handful of other highly-touted prospects, is making the case that the class will live up to the hype.

What's Next

DeLauter and the Guardians move forward into Week 2 with one of the most exciting young storylines in baseball. Whether he can sustain this production over 162 games remains to be seen — sustaining any Opening Week pace is difficult — but the foundation is clearly there. Keep an eye on DeLauter in the AL Rookie of the Year race. After three games, his name should be at the top of any conversation about 2026's best young players.
