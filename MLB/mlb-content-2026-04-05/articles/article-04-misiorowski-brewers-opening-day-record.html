Misiorowski Strikes Out 11, Sets Brewers Opening Day Record — Youngest to Do It Since King Felix

By Marcus Cole | April 5, 2026

The 23-year-old right-hander dominated the White Sox in a 14-2 win, setting a franchise record and joining elite historical company.

On Opening Day, Jacob Misiorowski wanted to make a statement. He made history instead.

The Milwaukee Brewers right-hander struck out 11 batters in five innings against the White Sox, earning a 14-2 Milwaukee win and setting the franchise's Opening Day strikeout record. The previous mark was 8, set by the great Ben Sheets in 2002 and later tied by Freddy Peralta. Misiorowski didn't just break it — he shattered it.

Putting It in Historical Context

At 23 years and 357 days old on Opening Day, Misiorowski became the youngest pitcher to record 10 or more strikeouts on Opening Day since Felix Hernandez struck out 12 batters in 2007. Hernandez was just 20 years old that day. Misiorowski has a bit more mileage — but the company is exceptional.

Nine of his 11 strikeouts came on his fastball; two more came on his curveball. He allowed just one run, two hits, and three walks across his five innings. His command was sharp, his stuff was electric, and his composure on the biggest stage of the regular season was evident throughout.

A Team Effort for the Ages

When Misiorowski exited after five innings, the Brewers' bullpen kept piling up strikeouts. The team finished with 20 strikeouts combined in the nine-inning game — tying the modern MLB record for strikeouts in a nine-inning game since 1900. It was one of the most dominant pitching performances by any team on any Opening Day in memory.

The Brewers finished with a 14-2 win — their largest margin of victory in an Opening Day game in franchise history.

Milwaukee's Rotation Outlook

The Brewers have been known for developing pitching year after year, and Misiorowski is the latest product of that system. His Opening Day performance validated the organizational belief in his potential and gave Milwaukee's fanbase a clear picture of what the rotation could look like at its best.

What's Next

Misiorowski takes the mound again in Week 2, and the expectations are suddenly very high. Milwaukee's rotation will be one of the most-watched storylines in the NL Central as the season progresses. If Misiorowski can perform close to this level consistently, the Brewers could be a serious postseason threat. One Opening Day is a small sample — but it was a very large sample of what this pitcher is capable of.
