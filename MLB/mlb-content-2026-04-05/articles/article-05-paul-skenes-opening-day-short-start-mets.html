Paul Skenes Lasts 2/3 of an Inning in Opening Day Disaster Against the Mets

By Jake Torres | April 5, 2026

The Pirates ace was chased before the first inning was over, allowing 5 runs on 37 pitches in the shortest start of his young career.

Paul Skenes walked to the mound on Opening Day as one of the most anticipated pitchers in baseball — a dominant right-hander coming off an NL Cy Young runner-up season, making his second career Opening Day start. He walked off less than an inning later, the victim of a Mets lineup that wouldn't let him catch his breath.

Skenes lasted two-thirds of an inning, allowing 5 runs on 37 pitches before being pulled in what became the shortest career start of his life. The Mets won 11-7 in what turned into a high-scoring slugfest.

How It Unraveled

The first seven Mets batters all had productive at-bats. Whether it was a hit, a walk, or a hard-hit ball that found a gap, New York's lineup refused to give Skenes any easy outs. The knockout blow came from Brett Baty, who launched a three-run triple on a ball that center fielder Oneil Cruz misread and lost in the outfield grass. By the time Skenes was pulled, Pittsburgh was already in a deep hole.

The 37 pitches he threw in two-thirds of an inning illustrated how hard it was for him to retire anyone. Command issues, contact, traffic on the bases — it all combined for the worst outing of his career.

Context and Perspective

This is not the Paul Skenes who pitched deep into games and dominated lineups through the 2025 season. Every great pitcher has one game like this — a day when nothing works, when the opposing lineup is hot, when the ball just won't cooperate.

Skenes finished 2025 as one of the five best pitchers in the National League. His spring training was strong. His stuff hasn't changed. Opening Day was an anomaly, not a trend.

The Mets' Offense Impresses

New York's 11-run output was impressive by any measure, and it didn't come easy — a lineup that can rough up Paul Skenes is capable of beating anyone. The Mets won 11-7 in a contest that turned into a back-and-forth game after Pittsburgh's bullpen kept them in it.

What's Next

Skenes will get the ball again in his next scheduled start, and the Pirates will need him to be the ace they know he is. One bad Opening Day does not define a season — not for a pitcher of Skenes' caliber. The bounce-back will be the more important storyline. Pittsburgh's playoff hopes depend on him performing at his best, and if history is any guide, he will.
