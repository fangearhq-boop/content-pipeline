### STORY 1: Misiorowski Throws Fastest Pitches in MLB History for a Starter

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

The radar gun has never lied quite like this.

On Friday night, Milwaukee Brewers right-hander Jacob Misiorowski stepped to the mound at Yankee Stadium and did something no starting pitcher in the Statcast era — which dates back to 2008 — had ever done. He threw seven pitches faster than any starting pitcher had ever thrown one. And then he did it again. And again.

His top velocity: 103.6 mph. The previous record for a starting pitcher was 103.2 mph, set by Cardinals reliever Jordan Hicks in 2022. Misiorowski didn't just break it — he shattered it, throwing SEVEN pitches at 103.2 mph or higher in a single outing.

In total, he threw 43 pitches at 100 mph or faster. That's the third-most in any single game under Statcast tracking, behind Hunter Greene's 47 (September 2022) and 44 (March 2023).

And none of this velocity came at the expense of results. Misiorowski struck out 11 Yankees and allowed nothing — zero runs, just 2 hits and 2 walks in 6 innings. It was, by any measure, one of the most dominant individual pitching performances of the 2026 season and one of the most historically significant velocity performances the sport has ever seen.

He's 24 years old.

At some point, we're going to have to stop being surprised when Misiorowski does something unimaginable. But we're not there yet.

### STORY 2: Aaron Judge at 383 Career HRs — 400 Club in Sight for 2026

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Aaron Judge has 383 career home runs.

That number matters because 400 is the milestone that separates the great from the historically elite — only 59 players in MLB history have reached it. Judge will almost certainly become the 60th this season, at his current pace of 15 home runs through just 38 games.

To appreciate how fast he's moving: he reached 15 home runs in his 37th game this year, the fastest any Yankee has done so since Alex Rodriguez in 2007, when A-Rod took 31 games. Judge is two years removed from a 58-homer season and one year removed from a 53-homer season. A 50-homer season, for Aaron Judge, is becoming almost routine.

Except no one has ever done it five times.

If Judge maintains anything close to his current pace — he's on track for approximately 64 home runs — he would become the first player in MLB history to have five 50-homer seasons. The list of players with four 50-HR seasons is not long. Nobody has five. Judge is knocking on that door right now.

And there's a bonus Yankees subplot: first baseman Paul Goldschmidt is also tracking toward his 400th career home run this season. It's possible — though not guaranteed — that both Yankees hit their 400th homer in the same season. That has never happened for a franchise, either.

Judge hitting No. 384 to tie Harold Baines (69th all-time) could come any day now. The 400 milestone is the next mountain. He's going to climb it.
