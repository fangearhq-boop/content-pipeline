Blue Jays Rout Angels 14-1 — Valenzuela's Four-Hit Game Nearly Includes a Cycle

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | May 10, 2026

Toronto erupted for one of its most dominant offensive performances of the season on Saturday, with Luis Valenzuela delivering his first career four-hit game and coming one triple shy of hitting for the cycle.

There are blowout wins, and then there are the kinds of nights where everything clicks for a lineup at once.

Saturday in Anaheim was the latter for the Toronto Blue Jays. They beat the Los Angeles Angels 14-1 in one of their most complete offensive performances of the 2026 season, and at the center of it was Luis Valenzuela — who went 4-for-5 with 4 RBI in his first career four-hit game.

Valenzuela came within a triple of hitting for the cycle. He collected every type of extra-base hit except the hardest one to get, and he drove in nearly a third of Toronto's 14 runs in a game that was over early.

Valenzuela's Career Night

Four hits in a game is a milestone for any hitter, and Valenzuela had never done it in his MLB career before Saturday. His 4-for-5, 4-RBI performance was the engine of Toronto's offensive explosion — a night where he looked every bit the offensive player the Blue Jays envisioned when they gave him a spot in the lineup.

The near-cycle adds another layer of significance to the night. Hitting for the cycle — a single, double, triple, and home run in the same game — is one of baseball's rarest individual achievements, happening fewer than 20 times per season across the entire league. Valenzuela was one triple away from joining that exclusive group. He'll take the four hits and 4 RBI and move on, but the near-miss of something truly rare makes the performance even more memorable.

Toronto's Offensive Depth on Display

The Blue Jays have had an inconsistent 2026 season, with moments of offensive brilliance separated by stretches where the lineup has gone quiet. Saturday showed what Toronto is capable of when the bats get going collectively.

Fourteen runs is not a product of one player having a good night. It's a product of the entire order contributing, pitchers struggling to find answers, and a game that snowballs in the home team's favor — or in this case, the visiting team's favor. The Angels had no answer for Toronto's lineup as runs kept piling up throughout the game.

What's Next

For Valenzuela, Saturday's breakout performance is the kind of night that builds confidence heading into the week. After a four-hit, 4-RBI game with a near-cycle, the at-bats that follow tend to come with a different energy.

For the Blue Jays, the 14-1 win is a confidence-builder as they continue to find their footing in 2026. The talent has always been there in Toronto. Games like Saturday are a reminder of what they can do when everything comes together.

For the Angels, the search for consistent pitching continues after one of their roughest games of the season. Fourteen runs allowed at home is not the kind of performance that builds momentum into the next series.
