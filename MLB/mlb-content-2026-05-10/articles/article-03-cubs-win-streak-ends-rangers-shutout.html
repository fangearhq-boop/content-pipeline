Rangers Shut Out Cubs 6-0, Ending Chicago's Second 10-Game Win Streak — But the Season Story Holds

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | May 10, 2026

The Texas Rangers held the Cubs scoreless for the fourth time in 2026, snapping one of the most historically rare stretches in early-season baseball history — but Chicago remains 27-13 and in command of the NL Central.

Streaks end. That's baseball.

On Saturday in Arlington, the Texas Rangers beat the Chicago Cubs 6-0, ending the Cubs' second 10-game win streak of the 2026 season. Josh Jung opened the scoring with a second-inning home run, and Evan Foscue — hitting his first career homer — pushed the lead to 5-0 in the fifth inning and effectively settled the game. Chicago's bats went quiet for the fourth time this season.

But let's put this in perspective: the Cubs are 27-13. They are leading the National League Central. They have accomplished something that hadn't been done through this point in a season since the 1955 Brooklyn Dodgers — two separate 10-game win streaks before the 40th game. One cold afternoon in Arlington doesn't change any of that.

The Streak Was Historically Rare

The Cubs' first 10-game win streak ran from April 14 through April 24. After three straight losses, they launched the second streak with a win over the Padres on April 28 and ran it all the way through Friday, May 8. The streak ended at 10 games on Saturday night.

Two separate win streaks of 10+ games within a team's first 40 games of a season is a feat that has happened only once in modern MLB history before 2026. The 1955 Brooklyn Dodgers — one of the most celebrated teams of their era — did it, and they went on to win the World Series that year. It is not common company for the Cubs to be keeping.

Being held scoreless for the fourth time in a season that has otherwise been defined by timely hitting and clutch performances is a reminder that baseball humbles everyone eventually. The Cubs have hit more than enough walk-off homers, strung enough consecutive wins, and done enough unusual things offensively to earn the benefit of the doubt after one quiet night.

The Wrigley Home Streak

The Cubs' 15-game home winning streak at Wrigley Field entered the weekend intact — and since Saturday's loss was on the road, it remains intact. Chicago's performance at home has been one of the more remarkable stories within their already-remarkable season. A 15-game streak at Wrigley is the kind of run that builds mythology around a franchise, and it hasn't ended yet.

The road game in Arlington was exactly that: a road game. The Cubs will return to Wrigley soon, and when they do, the streak will still be waiting for them.

What's Next

The Cubs remain in first place in the NL Central with a record that puts them among the best teams in baseball. The loss on Saturday is a data point in a 162-game season, not a narrative shift. A third win streak — or a different kind of run — could begin at any point.

At 27-13, the Cubs are exactly where any team would want to be heading into the heart of May. Whatever the Rangers did on Saturday, it didn't change the trajectory of Chicago's 2026 season. It just reminded everyone that nothing in baseball comes easy — or lasts forever.
