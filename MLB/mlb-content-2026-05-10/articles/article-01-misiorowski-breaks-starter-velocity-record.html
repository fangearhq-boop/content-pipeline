Misiorowski Throws Fastest Pitches in MLB History for a Starter — 103.6 MPH in Dominant Gem vs. Yankees

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | May 10, 2026

The Brewers' 24-year-old righty rewrote the Statcast record books with seven pitches at 103.2 mph or faster in a single outing, adding 11 strikeouts and six scoreless innings against New York.

The radar gun had never seen a starting pitcher do this before Friday night.

Milwaukee Brewers right-hander Jacob Misiorowski stood on the mound at Yankee Stadium on May 8 and threw the fastest pitches ever recorded by a starting pitcher in the Statcast era. His top velocity reached 103.6 mph — breaking the previous record of 103.2 mph set by Cardinals reliever Jordan Hicks in 2022. And then he did it again. And again. And again. Seven times in one game, he topped what had been an untouchable benchmark for a starting pitcher.

The performance was as dominant as it was historically significant: 6 innings, 0 runs, 2 hits, 2 walks, 11 strikeouts. Misiorowski didn't just break a velocity record — he delivered a performance worthy of one.

The Numbers Behind the Performance

The velocity story goes deeper than the single top number. Misiorowski threw 43 pitches at 100 mph or faster during Friday's outing — the third-most in any single game under Statcast tracking. Only Hunter Greene's 47 (September 17, 2022) and 44 (March 30, 2023) rank ahead of him.

For context: it's common for power relievers to light up the radar in short bursts. Throwing triple digits for an inning or two is one thing. Throwing 43 pitches at 100+ mph across six innings — while still getting outs, commanding the strike zone, and maintaining the kind of movement that generates swings and misses — is something else entirely.

Misiorowski is 24 years old. He is doing things on a baseball mound that simply hadn't been done before.

Historical Context

The Statcast era, which began in 2008, has given baseball fans unprecedented data on exactly how hard pitchers throw. Jordan Hicks, the Cardinals reliever, owned the starter velocity record at 103.2 mph before Friday. Hicks set it as a reliever who had been converted to a starter — a pitcher specifically deployed for his elite velocity.

Misiorowski's 103.6 mph isn't a fluke of a single delivery — it's a sustained velocity floor across an entire outing. The fact that he can maintain 100+ mph deep into games while generating strikeouts at an elite rate makes the comparison to even the hardest throwers in recent history stand up.

Eleven strikeouts against one of the most dangerous lineups in the American League. Zero runs allowed. Six innings of work. The velocity was the headline, but the results were the story.

What This Means for Milwaukee

The Brewers have built their franchise around pitching development, and Misiorowski represents the latest — and perhaps most extreme — example of that philosophy. A 24-year-old ace who can throw 103 mph while maintaining command and generating strikeouts at an elite rate is exactly what a pitching-first organization dreams about.

This wasn't an aberration. Misiorowski has been one of the most electric starting pitchers in the NL all season. Friday night against the Yankees was the performance that put the national baseball audience on notice in the most emphatic way possible.

What's Next

Misiorowski will take the mound again in five days or so, and the baseball world will be watching to see if Friday was a one-time peak or a new baseline. Based on what he's shown all season, a repeat performance isn't out of the question.

For the record books: 103.6 mph is the number. Seven pitches above the old record. Forty-three pitches at triple digits. The fastest starting pitcher the Statcast era has ever seen, and he's just getting started.
