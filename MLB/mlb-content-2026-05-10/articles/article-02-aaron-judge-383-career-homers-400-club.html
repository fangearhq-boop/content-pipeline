Aaron Judge at 383 Career Home Runs — The 400 Club Is a 2026 Achievement Waiting to Happen

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | May 10, 2026

The Yankees slugger leads MLB with 15 home runs this season and is on pace for what could be an unprecedented fifth 50-homer campaign — while just 17 away from joining one of baseball's most exclusive clubs.

Aaron Judge is doing it again.

Through 38 games of the 2026 season, the New York Yankees right fielder has 15 home runs, giving him 383 for his career. He needs just 17 more to join the 400 home run club — a milestone only 59 players in MLB history have achieved. At his current pace, he reaches it before the All-Star break.

The sprint to 400 isn't the only milestone worth tracking. Judge is on pace for approximately 64 home runs this season — a number that, if reached, would represent an American League record. And perhaps more staggeringly, it would be his fifth career 50-home run season. No player in MLB history has ever done that five times.

The Fastest Yankee Since A-Rod

Judge reached 15 home runs this season in his 37th game, the fastest any Yankee has hit that mark since Alex Rodriguez in 2007, when A-Rod reached 15 dingers in 31 games. The company Judge keeps in the Yankees' historical home run conversation has shifted dramatically in the last few years.

He's already fourth on the Yankees' all-time home run list with 383, ahead of Joe DiMaggio and Yogi Berra. The only players ahead of him in the franchise record book are Babe Ruth (659), Mickey Mantle (536), and Lou Gehrig (493). If Judge maintains his current pace for a few more seasons, he'll eventually challenge all three.

His next individual milestone comes at 384, when he ties Hall of Famer Harold Baines for 69th on the all-time list.

The 400 Club Race

Reaching 400 career home runs is a milestone that separates the generational power hitters from everyone else. It's not just a round number — it's a marker of sustained, elite production over a career. Judge would become just the 60th player in history to reach it.

There's an added Yankees subplot worth watching: first baseman Paul Goldschmidt is also tracking toward his 400th career home run in 2026. The possibility of two Yankees joining the 400 club in the same season would be a unique franchise achievement — a milestone pairing that hasn't been done before.

Judge's pace makes his arrival at the milestone nearly certain. The question isn't if — it's when, and how many he'll hit after.

The Unprecedented Five-Peat

Four 50-home run seasons: 52 in 2017, 62 in 2022, 58 in 2024, 53 in 2025. No player in MLB history has had five of them. Judge is on pace to get there in 2026.

The record for most 50-home run seasons in a career is four, and Judge already owns it alongside a handful of historical legends. A fifth would be his alone — a record that, by its nature, could stand for a very long time.

His combination of elite bat-to-ball skills, exceptional plate discipline, and raw power at 6-foot-7 makes him the rarest kind of slugger: one who produces prodigiously without sacrificing contact quality. That's why he can sustain 50-homer seasons year after year when most power hitters are inconsistent.

What's Next

The 2026 season has a long way to go, and Judge has proven he can maintain elite production from April through October. If the Yankees stay healthy and contending, he'll have every at-bat available to him — and at his current clip, career home run No. 400 could arrive before summer's halfway point.

Keep watching the scoreboard. Every Judge at-bat this season has historical weight attached to it.
