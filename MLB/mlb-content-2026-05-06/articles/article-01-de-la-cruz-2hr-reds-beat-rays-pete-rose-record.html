Elly De La Cruz Hits Two Home Runs, Passes Pete Rose in Reds History — Cincinnati Beats Tampa Bay 12-6

By Marcus Cole | May 6, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Elly De La Cruz had one of those nights in Cincinnati on Tuesday — the kind that gets added to the highlight reel and the record books at the same time.

De La Cruz went 3-for-6 with two home runs, five RBI, a stolen base, and three runs scored as the Cincinnati Reds beat the Tampa Bay Rays 12-6. The Reds scored the first nine runs of the game, turning what could have been a competitive series game into a statement performance. It was the Reds' fifth consecutive win and their seventh in the last eight games.

A Franchise Record, and the Name It Surpassed

The number that will follow De La Cruz's performance on Tuesday is six. That's how many career multi-home run games he now has — the most by any switch-hitter in Reds franchise history, surpassing Pete Rose.

Pete Rose. The Hit King. The man with 4,256 career hits. One of the most prolific offensive players in the history of baseball played his best years in Cincinnati, and De La Cruz has now passed him in a category that speaks to the sheer power of his swing.

De La Cruz is still in his mid-twenties and already a fixture in conversations about the most exciting players in baseball. Tuesday's performance added another chapter: power, speed, and now a franchise record. He is the full package.

A Rookie Who Keeps Producing

Cincinnati's NL Rookie of the Month, Sal Stewart, added three RBI in the win, pushing his season total to 24. Stewart was named NL Rookie of the Month for March and April on Monday — having hit .281 with nine home runs and 29 RBI in 31 games during the period. He has been one of the best rookies in baseball since Opening Day, and Tuesday was another reminder that the Reds didn't just get good, they got historically good at a position of need.

The Reds' offense is built around multiple dangerous weapons. That combination — De La Cruz's athleticism and Stewart's power production — is giving Cincinnati's lineup a depth that opposing pitchers can't navigate around.

First Place, Best Road Record in Baseball

The Reds are in first place in the NL Central. Their road record — 10-2 — is the best in Major League Baseball. Five consecutive wins, seven of the last eight. This is a team playing with genuine confidence and producing results to match.

Tampa Bay, despite the lopsided final score, came in as a capable opponent. The Reds are simply outplaying everyone in their path right now.

What's Next

The Reds continue their road trip against Tampa Bay. The win Tuesday extended their hold on first place and reinforced their credentials as a legitimate NL Central contender. De La Cruz's six multi-home run games set the franchise switch-hitter standard. Stewart continues to make the NL Rookie of the Year race his own. Cincinnati's early-season surge is no fluke — it's built on real talent, real production, and nights like Tuesday in Tampa.
