Washington Ends Atlanta's 6-Game Winning Streak — James Wood Homers in 11-4 Nationals Win

By Ryan Calloway | May 6, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Atlanta Braves have the best record in baseball. They were on a six-game winning streak heading into Tuesday. They are built, top to bottom, for sustained dominance.

Washington didn't care.

The Nationals beat the Braves 11-4 Tuesday night, snapping Atlanta's streak and reminding a national audience that over 162 games, everyone gets beaten eventually — even the most complete team in the sport. James Wood hit a home run. Luis García Jr. collected four hits. The Nationals' offense delivered a complete performance against a team that had been rolling.

James Wood Is the Real Deal

If Tuesday's game served any purpose beyond the standings, it was to put a wider spotlight on James Wood. The young Washington outfielder has been one of the more exciting players in the early going of 2026 — a five-tool talent with the kind of physical tools that make scouts use superlatives.

His home run Tuesday came against Atlanta pitching that had been dominant for the better part of two weeks. Wood didn't flinch. He's the kind of player that teams build futures around, and the Nationals are building theirs around him right now.

Luis García Jr. added four hits — a reminder that Wood isn't the only weapon in Washington's lineup. When the Nationals' offense is clicking, they have multiple dangerous contributors. Tuesday, it all came together.

The Braves Perspective

Atlanta entered May 6 at 25-11, with an NL East lead measured in games plural. Their core — Matt Olson, Ozzie Albies, Drake Baldwin, Michael Harris II — has been outstanding on both sides of the ball. The rotation has been reliable. The bullpen has been efficient.

One bad night against Washington doesn't change the picture. The Braves are still the best team in the National League. They are still on a pace that, if sustained, would deliver one of the top records in franchise history. Their division lead is comfortable enough that a Tuesday stumble barely registers in the standings column.

What it does is add a small, necessary note of context: this is a long season. The Braves will lose games. They will have nights like Tuesday. The question is whether those nights accumulate into something concerning or remain isolated events in an otherwise dominant run.

History suggests the latter. But Washington had a say Tuesday, and they made it count.

What's Next

The Braves and Nationals continue their series in Atlanta. The Braves are focused on their larger mission — sustaining the best record in baseball through the long middle stretch of the season and into October. Washington, coming off this performance, is playing with the kind of confidence that can carry a young team through an important stretch.

Atlanta bounces back. They always have. But the Nationals sent a message Tuesday that they're not a team anyone should pencil in as an automatic loss — not even the best team in baseball.
