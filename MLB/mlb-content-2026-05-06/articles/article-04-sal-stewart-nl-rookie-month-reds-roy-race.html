Sal Stewart Named NL Rookie of the Month — Reds Third Baseman Is Running Away With the ROY Race

By Marcus Cole | May 6, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

When the Cincinnati Reds drafted Sal Stewart, the expectation was that he'd need time. Most third basemen drafted out of high school need years of minor league refinement before they're ready to contribute at the major league level. Stewart apparently didn't read the manual.

The Reds' rookie third baseman was named the National League Rookie of the Month for March and April on Monday — and the numbers that earned him the award are extraordinary by any standard, not just rookie ones.

The Numbers That Made the Choice Easy

In 31 games during March and April, Stewart hit .281 with nine home runs and 29 RBI. He led all rookies in RBI during that stretch and, at certain points, led the entire major leagues. His slugging percentage and OPS ranked among the best at any position across baseball.

Those aren't numbers you throw around casually. For a first-year player still adjusting to major league pitching, speed, and the daily grind of the big leagues — posting 29 RBI in a month and a half is historically significant for the Reds franchise.

And it hasn't slowed down in May. Stewart added three RBI in Tuesday's 12-6 win over Tampa Bay, pushing his season total to 24 and counting. He's hitting in the middle of an already dangerous Cincinnati lineup, and the Reds have built their first-place standing in the NL Central around his consistent production.

The Rookie of the Year Race

The NL Rookie of the Year award has been Stewart's race to lose since Opening Day. Through May 6, no other NL rookie has matched his combination of power, RBI production, and daily impact. He is the frontrunner by a significant margin.

Other names will emerge as the season progresses — the ROY race is a marathon, not a sprint, and it tends to shift as injuries, slumps, and breakout performances reshape the landscape. But Stewart has done everything he needs to do to establish himself as the player to beat. Any other contender for the award will have to unseat him.

The Reds, for their part, look like a team built to sustain his impact. With Elly De La Cruz as the dynamic centerpiece and Stewart providing thunderous production in the heart of the lineup, Cincinnati's offense has legitimate star power at multiple positions. That's a recipe not just for first-place positioning in May, but for a serious run through October.

What's Next

Stewart will continue to be the central story of the Reds' season going forward — both as a team contributor and as a Rookie of the Year candidate whose numbers demand attention. The award for March and April was the first recognition. It will not be the last.

Cincinnati continues its road trip against Tampa Bay. The Reds are in first place, five games into a winning streak, with the best road record in baseball. And their rookie third baseman is putting up numbers that make the entire league take notice. The NL Rookie of the Year race is Sal Stewart's to lose.
