Tyler O'Neill's 6 Consecutive Opening Day HRs: The Most Quietly Historic Record in Baseball

By Ballpark Banter | March 29, 2026

There's a stat from Thursday's Opening Day that almost nobody is talking about, and it deserves more attention.

Tyler O'Neill homered on Opening Day for the sixth consecutive season. Per Elias Sports Bureau, that's an MLB all-time record — no player in the history of the sport had ever accomplished the feat six years in a row. The previous best was four consecutive Opening Day home runs, a mark tied by Yogi Berra (1955-1958), Gary Carter (1977-1980), and Todd Hundley (1994-1997).

Two of those three players are in the Baseball Hall of Fame. All three were elite offensive performers at their respective positions. And none of them managed to do what Tyler O'Neill just did on Thursday afternoon.

What Makes This Record So Remarkable

Opening Day is a single game. It happens once a year. For a hitter to reliably deliver in that specific, high-pressure moment — year after year, regardless of team, opponent, ballpark, or personal circumstance — is a form of clutch consistency that doesn't have an obvious statistical explanation.

Berra was one of the greatest catchers in baseball history. Carter was the most complete catcher of his generation. Hundley had a 41-home-run season in 1996 that made him briefly the face of Mets power hitting. Their four-year Opening Day streaks were remarkable, embedded in otherwise excellent careers.

O'Neill has been a quality outfielder for the better part of a decade — productive, durable, underrated by broader baseball conversation. But the Opening Day record is now his, alone. No one else in the 150-year history of organized baseball can claim six in a row.

A Record That Keeps Getting Extended

What's striking about O'Neill's streak is how little fanfare it has received along the way. Each March, O'Neill goes yard. Each March, the broader baseball conversation moves on to bigger names and bigger stars. And each March, the record gets longer.

The streak now stands at six. The natural question is how long it can continue. Opening Day home runs, by definition, require a healthy player who starts every year, a baseball in the right part of the strike zone at the right moment, and the ability to deliver under the spotlight. O'Neill has done it six years running. The seventh — whenever it comes — would only make the record more surreal.

What's Next

Tyler O'Neill will continue his 2026 season with the long runway of 159 remaining games. But the opening act — homer on Opening Day, sixth consecutive year — is already complete. No one in baseball history has ever done it more often. That's worth taking a moment to appreciate, even as the calendar moves past March and into summer.
