Will Smith's Birthday Walk-Off Completes Dodgers' Sweep of Diamondbacks: LA Now on 8-Game Win Streak

By Ballpark Banter | March 29, 2026

Will Smith turned 31 on Saturday. He celebrated by hitting the go-ahead home run in the eighth inning, completing the Los Angeles Dodgers' three-game sweep of the Arizona Diamondbacks and extending LA's winning streak to eight consecutive games — a run that dates back to last year's World Series victory.

The final score: Dodgers 3, Diamondbacks 2. The Dodgers are 3-0 to start 2026. The Diamondbacks are 0-3.

The Smith Birthday HR

With the Dodgers trailing 2-1 in the eighth inning, Will Smith worked a 2-run home run that gave Los Angeles their first lead of the game. It was a decisive swing — the kind that a catcher hitting in the heart of a championship lineup is supposed to provide. Smith's blast chased Arizona reliever and gave Edwin Diaz a clean ninth inning to work with.

Diaz handled it. Three up, three down, second consecutive save. The Dodgers were done in three games.

Tyler Glasnow Keeps the Foundation Intact

Starting pitcher Tyler Glasnow gave the Dodgers six innings, allowing 2 runs on 4 hits with 6 strikeouts. His performance kept the game manageable and left the outcome in the hands of a loaded bullpen. Kyle Tucker added a spectacular moment in the sixth, leaping at the wall to rob Geraldo Perdomo of what would have been a home run — a play that will make highlight compilations for weeks.

Freddie Freeman also had a strong afternoon, going 3-for-4 to snap a rough 0-for-8 start to the season. Freeman entering his full form is a significant development for the Dodgers' lineup depth going forward.

The Series No One Will Forget in Arizona

The most remarkable detail of this series may be how Arizona lost it. The Diamondbacks jumped out to a 2-0 lead in every single game — Game 1, Game 2, and Game 3. And in every single game, they lost. Three times Arizona had the lead. Three times Los Angeles answered.

It's an early-season series, so its long-term significance is limited. But the pattern — Dodgers down, Dodgers responding — reinforces everything about what made this team a three-peat contender heading into 2026.

What's Next

The Dodgers have an off day on Sunday before welcoming the Cleveland Guardians to Dodger Stadium on Monday. That series will include Shohei Ohtani's first pitching start of the 2026 season on Tuesday, March 31 — one of the most anticipated events on the baseball calendar. The 3-0 start sets the stage perfectly.
