JJ Wetherholt Is 2 for 2 in Career-Defining Moments: HR on Debut, Walk-Off RBI in Game 2

By Ballpark Banter | March 29, 2026

Two games into his major-league career, JJ Wetherholt already has a home run and a walk-off to his name.

The Cardinals' No. 1 prospect entered Opening Day with high expectations — as the highest-drafted Cardinals position prospect in years — and has already exceeded them. On Thursday (Game 1), he homered in his first career at-bat. On Saturday (Game 2), he lined a walk-off RBI single in the tenth inning to give St. Louis a 6-5 win over the Rays. Two games. Two moments that will be in every Cardinals highlight reel for years.

The Debut HR

Wetherholt's first major-league at-bat came against Drew Rasmussen in the third inning on Opening Day. He fell behind 0-2, and then — with Busch Stadium holding its breath for their newest prospect — he launched a 425-foot home run to dead center field at 101.7 mph off the bat. It wasn't a cheapie. It wasn't a cheap pull-shot off a hanging breaking ball. It was a dead-center bullet off a quality pitcher's fastball, hit with the kind of exit velocity that makes scouts stop typing and just watch.

The crowd gave him a standing ovation before he finished his home run trot. That moment doesn't happen very often.

The Walk-Off

Saturday's Cardinals game featured Michael McGreevy throwing six no-hit innings in his own 2026 debut — before the bid ended in the seventh, the Cardinals surrendered the lead, and the game went to extras. Down to their last options in the tenth, Wetherholt stepped in against Griffin Jax with runners on base.

He lined a two-run single to right field. Cardinals win. Two games, two defining moments.

Wetherholt's approach at the plate was as notable as the result. He wasn't swinging at borderline pitches. He wasn't chasing off-speed below the zone. He was working counts, identifying pitches he could hit hard, and delivering when the moment called for it. That composure, that early in a major-league career, is what separates top prospects from stars.

What He Brings to the Cardinals

Wetherholt was the fifth overall pick in the 2023 draft, selected out of West Virginia where he had one of the most decorated amateur careers in recent memory. The Cardinals' philosophy has long been to develop homegrown middle-of-the-order talent — and Wetherholt slots naturally into that system as a potential two or three hitter with power, plate discipline, and the kind of baseball instincts that translate quickly.

Two games is too small a sample for sweeping conclusions. But certain players arrive in the major leagues looking like they belong, and Wetherholt belongs.

What's Next

The Cardinals enter Sunday's series finale at 2-0, with Wetherholt already a fan favorite after two games. His development over the next 160 games will be one of the more compelling individual narratives of the 2026 season — and based on the first 48 hours, it's off to a memorable start.
