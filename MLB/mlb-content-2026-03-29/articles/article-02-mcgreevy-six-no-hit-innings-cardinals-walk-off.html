McGreevy Throws 6 No-Hit Innings, Wetherholt Delivers Walk-Off: Cardinals 6, Rays 5 in Ten

By Ballpark Banter | March 29, 2026

Saturday afternoon at Busch Stadium featured two rookies putting on a performance that neither side of the Rays dugout will forget anytime soon.

Michael McGreevy made his 2026 Cardinals debut and threw six no-hit innings — neutralizing a Tampa Bay lineup that had torn apart Cardinals pitching in 2025, particularly against left-handed hitters. JJ Wetherholt, playing in just the second game of his major-league career, then walked it off in the tenth with an RBI single off Griffin Jax. Cardinals 6, Rays 5.

McGreevy's Six Brilliant Innings

McGreevy was sharp from the first pitch. He worked through 6 innings without allowing a hit — 2 walks, 5 strikeouts, 96 pitches total — using a mix of sink and arm-side movement to exploit the same Rays left-handed hitters who had punished him in 2025 (where they hit .318 with a .911 OPS against him). On Saturday, that group went 0-for-13.

The defense helped. Iván Herrera handled a tricky pop-out behind the plate. Wetherholt made a leaping grab at second base that preserved the no-hit bid in the fifth. The effort was collective — and McGreevy was the engine of it.

The no-hit bid ended in the seventh when Junior Caminero singled off reliever Riley O'Brien. By then, McGreevy was in the dugout. But the line — 6 IP, 0 H, 2 BB, 5 K — stands on its own as one of the better debut pitching performances in recent Cardinals history.

Wetherholt Delivers the Finish

After the Cardinals gave up the lead in the seventh and eighth, the game headed to extras tied. JJ Wetherholt — who had homered in his first career at-bat on Opening Day just two days earlier — stepped up in the tenth with runners on base and lined a two-run single to right field off Griffin Jax, walking off the Rays and giving the Cardinals a 2-0 series record.

Wetherholt is now 2-for-2 in terms of career-defining moments. The 425-foot homer in his first at-bat. The walk-off single in his second game. The Cardinals' No. 1 prospect is delivering exactly what the organization hoped for — just faster than anyone expected.

Cardinals at 2-0 and Ahead of Schedule

St. Louis enters Sunday's finale of their opening series at 2-0, with the possibility of a sweep against a Rays team that opened the year 0-2. McGreevy and Wetherholt have given Cardinals fans a reason to pay close attention to this team in 2026 — and a performance like Saturday's is the kind that builds a fanbase's belief in a roster's ceiling.

What's Next

McGreevy will take his next turn in the rotation in approximately five days. Wetherholt will continue to run with the opportunity in front of him. For both rookies, Saturday was a statement. For the Cardinals, it was a sign that the future they've been building toward may be arriving ahead of schedule.
