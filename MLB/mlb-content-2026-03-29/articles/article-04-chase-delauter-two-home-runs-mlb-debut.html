Chase DeLauter Hits 2 HRs in His MLB Debut — Including the Go-Ahead Shot in the 9th

By Ballpark Banter | March 29, 2026

The 2026 Opening Day rookie class was historic for a number of reasons, and Chase DeLauter belongs near the top of the conversation.

The Cleveland Guardians outfielder made his MLB debut on Thursday against the Seattle Mariners and hit two home runs — including the go-ahead solo shot off Cooper Criswell in the ninth inning. The Guardians won 6-4. DeLauter's performance stands alongside the other remarkable debut moments of Opening Day 2026 as one of the best individual newcomer performances in years.

A Minor-League Career Defined by Absence

Context makes DeLauter's debut more remarkable. He was drafted 16th overall in the 2022 MLB Draft by the Guardians — a high-ceiling outfielder with a quick left-handed swing and advanced plate discipline for his age. The expectation was a fast track to Cleveland.

Instead, injuries intervened. Hamstring. Oblique. Ankle. Between June 2022 and Opening Day 2026, DeLauter played in exactly 138 minor-league games — a number that reflects how often he was unavailable, not how slowly he was developing. Most top-10 picks accumulate 300 or more minor-league appearances before their big-league debut. DeLauter arrived at 138.

And then he played his first regular-season game and hit two home runs.

The Go-Ahead Shot in the 9th

The second homer was the one that will be remembered longest. With the Guardians trailing in the ninth inning, DeLauter stepped in against Cooper Criswell and connected on a pitch that left the park for a lead-changing solo home run. Cleveland protected the lead and won 6-4.

Two home runs in a debut is rare. The go-ahead nature of the second one, in the ninth inning, in your first professional game — that's the stuff that defines a career's narrative before it's truly begun.

An Opening Day Debut Class for the Ages

DeLauter joined Cardinals rookie JJ Wetherholt (425-foot HR in his first career at-bat), Mets outfielder Carson Benge (HR and stolen base), White Sox signing Munetaka Murakami (HR in debut), and Tigers prospect Kevin McGonigle (4-for-5 with 2 doubles and 2 RBI) as part of a debut-day class that may be the most impressive group of Opening Day rookies in modern memory.

Peyton Pallette, a Rule 5 pickup for the Guardians, also pitched two perfect innings in his first MLB appearance on March 28 — the first Cleveland pitcher to debut with two perfect frames in nearly 60 years. Cleveland's week was full of moments worth noting.

What's Next

DeLauter will continue earning playing time in Cleveland's outfield, with the Guardians set to host the Los Angeles Dodgers beginning Monday — including Shohei Ohtani's first pitching start of the season on Tuesday. DeLauter just became the player every Dodgers scouting report now includes. He'll get his chance against the biggest stage quickly.
