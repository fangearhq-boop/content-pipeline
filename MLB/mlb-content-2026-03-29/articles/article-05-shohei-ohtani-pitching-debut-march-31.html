Shohei Ohtani's First 2026 Pitching Start Is Tuesday — Here's What to Expect

By Ballpark Banter | March 29, 2026

Shohei Ohtani has been in the Dodgers lineup since Opening Night, doing what he does as a hitter — batting cleanup, taking walks, driving in runs, being the best player on the field. But Opening Week served as a reminder that in 2026, Ohtani is fully two-way again. And Tuesday is when the pitching side of that equation begins.

Ohtani is scheduled to make his first 2026 pitching start on March 31 against the Cleveland Guardians at Dodger Stadium. It will be the most anticipated individual pitching performance of the early season and, depending on how it goes, possibly one of the most significant starting-pitcher debuts in recent baseball history.

The Spring Numbers Set the Stage

Ohtani made two Cactus League appearances this spring, capped by a final start that left very little room for concern. Four innings. Eleven strikeouts. Fastball sitting at 99 mph. Manager Dave Roberts offered a concise assessment: "The intensity was there, the focus was there, and the execution, all that stuff was there."

Roberts also stated, without hedging, that he believes Ohtani can compete for the NL Cy Young Award in 2026. That's a bold prediction for a pitcher working on a ~70-75 pitch workload in a flexible rotation — but it's a prediction grounded in Ohtani's track record. In 2025, he made 14 pitching starts around his position-player duties and posted strong numbers throughout. The spring suggests his velocity and stuff are fully intact.

The Opponent Adds to the Storyline

Ohtani's Opening Day opponent, Cleveland, enters Tuesday's game having already provided one of the week's memorable moments: Chase DeLauter hit two home runs in his MLB debut on Opening Day, including the go-ahead shot in the ninth. The Guardians aren't a pushover lineup.

Facing a rotation led by the defending three-time MVP on his 2026 pitching debut will be a significant test for a young Cleveland lineup still finding its rhythm. For Ohtani, it's the kind of stakes he's been delivering in since he first walked onto a professional field.

The 2026 Dodgers Rotation Context

Los Angeles opened the season with Yoshinobu Yamamoto on the mound for Opening Day, followed by Tyler Glasnow in Game 2 (6 IP, 2 R, sweep-completing effort against Arizona). Ohtani slots in as the No. 3 starter — a rotation that, when fully healthy, is the deepest in baseball. Roki Sasaki and Emmet Sheehan round out the back end.

The caveat is health. The Dodgers enter 2026 with multiple pitchers on the injured list, including Kiké Hernández (position player on 60-day IL), Gavin Stone, Brusdar Graterol, Evan Phillips, Jake Cousins, and Brock Stewart at various stages of recovery. The front three — Yamamoto, Glasnow, Ohtani — carry the weight until reinforcements arrive.

What's Next

Tuesday's start will generate the kind of attention that follows Ohtani everywhere. He'll be pitching on the same days he's also batting — a concept that still feels vaguely impossible when you say it out loud. He hit 55 home runs in 2025, won his third consecutive MVP award, and now wants to win a Cy Young. First step: Tuesday, March 31, against the Guardians. First pitch is expected to be well past bedtime on the East Coast.
