Bobby Witt Jr. Return Expected Tonight vs. White Sox After Six-Game MCL Absence

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | June 26, 2026

Bobby Witt Jr. has been sitting. That may end tonight.

The Kansas City Royals shortstop has missed six consecutive games after suffering a Grade 1 MCL sprain in his right knee on June 20 during a game against the St. Louis Cardinals. Manager Matt Quatraro expressed hope Thursday that Witt would be cleared for Friday's series opener against the Chicago White Sox — a return that would give the Royals their most important player back entering the final stretch before the All-Star break.

Why This Injury Was Manageable

Grade 1 MCL sprains sit at the most favorable end of ligament damage. Grade 1 means the fibers stretched under stress but did not tear — a distinction that changes the recovery timeline significantly. While Grade 2 and Grade 3 MCL injuries often require weeks of healing and rehabilitation, Grade 1 sprains typically resolve in one to three weeks with appropriate rest and treatment.

Quatraro described the injury as a "best case scenario" when it was first diagnosed, and the six-game absence lands within the expected one-to-three-week recovery window. Witt wore a knee brace during practice drills Thursday — a cautious indicator that the knee is still being managed, but a sign of active progress toward return rather than continued rest.

The Royals have played 6 games without their starting shortstop. That the team managed during his absence is a testament to roster depth, but anyone watching those games knows what was missing.

What Witt's Absence Actually Cost Kansas City

Witt's 4.5 fWAR this season leads all American League position players. That number reflects the full weight of his contributions: the defense at a premium position, the on-base skills, the power, the speed. Teams lose fWAR when key players sit — it's unavoidable — but not all fWAR is equal. Witt's fWAR comes at shortstop, with production distributed across every offensive category and a glove that anchors the infield.

Six games without him created a deficit that backups absorbed with admirable effort. Maikel Garcia covered the position during the absence. But the production gap between Garcia and Witt at shortstop is significant enough that every game Witt missed was a game played below the team's capability.

All-Star Implications

Witt's position in All-Star voting adds another dimension to his return timeline. The voting period is ongoing and closes soon. Six games of absence during a visible stretch of the season could affect fan engagement with his name on the ballot — every game he plays is also a game he can remind voters he belongs in Philadelphia on July 14. A healthy Witt playing well before the break is both a baseball outcome and a public relations outcome for his All-Star candidacy.

His 4.5 fWAR makes the argument without any additional campaigning: he is one of the best players in the American League and belongs on the All-Star roster whether he starts or not. Getting back on the field makes that case every game.

What's Next

The Royals open their series against Chicago tonight. If Quatraro clears Witt to play, Friday's lineup will include the most important return in the American League this week. If the medical staff needs one more day, the Royals' Saturday game becomes the next target. Either way, the window for his return is narrow — and the team's second-half push benefits significantly from having him healthy and active before the All-Star break resets the calendar.
