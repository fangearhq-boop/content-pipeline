All-Star Voting Phase 1 Results: Ohtani Leads All of MLB, Schwarber at 1.5 Million

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 26, 2026

All-Star voting Phase 1 closed June 25, and the results are familiar at the top and compelling in the middle.

Shohei Ohtani leads all of Major League Baseball with 3,341,257 votes. That number is not a surprise. Ohtani's combination of on-field production, two-way stardom, and global fan base makes him the sport's most recognizable ballot name regardless of the season's specifics. He is the standard-bearer for what MLB All-Star voting looks like in the current era.

The more interesting number is Kyle Schwarber's. The Philadelphia Phillies first baseman ranks second in the National League with 1,540,202 votes — and his position on the ballot lines up precisely with his position in the home run race.

Schwarber's Ballot Position and the HR Chase

Schwarber leads the major leagues with 29 home runs. He is on pace for a 60-home run season — a number achieved only five times in the sport's history, by Bonds, McGwire, Sosa, Maris, and Ruth. That pace, sustained into late June, creates a feedback loop between the ballot and the box score: fans who watch Schwarber hit choose him on the All-Star ballot, and the ballot numbers reflect the production fans see.

The timing also matters. The All-Star Game is scheduled for July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia — Kyle Schwarber's home stadium. If Schwarber is named a starter, he will play the midsummer classic in front of the crowd that has watched him hit 29 home runs in the first half. That is a narrative baseball could not script better if it tried.

The American League Picture

Ernie Clement of the Toronto Blue Jays leads the American League in Phase 1 voting. Phase 2 of voting opens June 29, providing another opportunity for fans across both leagues to influence the final starter selections.

The starters announced after Phase 2 closes will represent the player selections that fans made across both phases. All-Star starters have increasingly reflected a combination of fan enthusiasm, production, and name recognition — in most years, those three factors align closely enough that the starters are defensible if not universally agreed upon.

The Game Itself

The 2026 MLB All-Star Game at Citizens Bank Park on July 14 puts one of the sport's most electric venues at center stage for its signature summer event. Philadelphia's ballpark, one of the best in the sport for player and fan experience, hosts an All-Star weekend that will include the Home Run Derby on July 13.

Schwarber, with 29 home runs at his home park, is the natural candidate to consider for Derby participation — though Derby invitations involve logistical negotiation between the player, the team, and the league. His presence in the field would make the Derby's setting match the contestant with a poetic precision that rarely happens in sports.

What's Next

Phase 2 voting opens June 29 and runs through the end of the month. Final starter selections will be announced shortly after Phase 2 closes. Players not selected as starters by fans are eligible for reserve selection by the All-Star coaching staffs and player ballots. The Home Run Derby is July 13. The All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park. For Schwarber, the next three weeks will be defined by two numbers: how many home runs he adds to his total, and how many votes add to his 1.5 million.
