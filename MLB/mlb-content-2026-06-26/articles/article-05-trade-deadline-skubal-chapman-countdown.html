Trade Deadline Countdown: Skubal, Chapman, and the Moves That Could Define August

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 26, 2026

Thirty-eight days until August 3.

The trade deadline is the point on the baseball calendar where front offices stop theorizing and start executing. The organizations that have been building prospect pools and clearing payroll space for months finally deploy those resources. The sellers accept that the second half is not their season to win. The buyers bet that one acquisition can be the difference between a first-round exit and a World Series run.

This year's deadline has a headliner, and everyone in baseball knows it.

Tarik Skubal: The Move Every Contender Wants

Tarik Skubal is a two-time back-to-back Cy Young Award winner. He has explicitly declined to discuss a contract extension with the Detroit Tigers, signaling his intention to reach free agency after the 2026 season. The Tigers are 13 games under .500 and selling. The combination of those three facts makes Skubal the most consequential trade candidate in the sport.

There is no ambiguity about whether Skubal will be traded. The only live questions are where he goes and what the Tigers receive in return. Detroit's asking price — one elite prospect plus two to three additional pieces — reflects a front office that understands it is moving an asset that organizations would surrender significant value to acquire.

The teams most aggressively linked to Skubal include the Los Angeles Dodgers, New York Yankees, Atlanta Braves, Milwaukee Brewers, Toronto Blue Jays, and San Diego Padres. Each of those organizations is in genuine playoff contention, and each has the prospect depth to make a credible offer. The Dodgers are frequently named as the frontrunner, given their financial resources and demonstrated willingness to acquire top-of-rotation pitching under Dave Roberts.

The organization that lands Skubal gets two months of the sport's best pitcher. In a competitive American League or National League race, that addition changes October odds in a way that few single acquisitions can match.

Aroldis Chapman: The Bullpen Prize

The Boston Red Sox are sellers, and their most tradable asset is closer Aroldis Chapman. As one of the sport's most electric bullpen arms, Chapman provides a contender with a late-inning option that can change game outcomes from the seventh inning on. Red Sox selling Chapman signals an organizational decision that the second half is better used rebuilding prospect depth than chasing a playoff spot that is not materializing.

For buyers, Chapman represents a lower-acquisition-cost alternative to the Skubal conversation — a team that cannot afford the prospect price for a starting ace can add Chapman's impact at a different tier of cost. The organizations most likely to pursue Chapman are those one bullpen piece short of a championship-caliber unit, not those who need a rotation overhaul.

The Broader Deadline Landscape

Beyond the marquee names, the August 3 deadline will produce a wave of relief arms, complementary starters, and utility players that reshape roster construction across the sport. Every contending organization is evaluating multiple targets simultaneously — Skubal at the top of the board, with a list of depth pieces that round out the impact of a major addition.

The sellers — Detroit, Boston, and others assessing their positions — face the calculation of optimizing prospect return against the optics of formal surrender. Every general manager who sells is betting that the prospects they receive will produce more value over the next five years than the veteran they traded could have produced in the next two months.

History suggests the buyer wins more championships than the seller. But the seller often produces the next buyer, if the prospects develop correctly.

What's Next

The deadline is August 3 at 6 p.m. ET. Between now and then, formal trade discussions accelerate through July, mock trade evaluations publish constantly, and front office calls increase in frequency and specificity. The next major signal to watch: which organizations formally classify themselves as buyers vs. sellers over the July 4 holiday weekend, when win totals and gap to the division leaders become difficult to dispute. Skubal's destination will be the trade deadline's defining story. The race to land him has already started.
