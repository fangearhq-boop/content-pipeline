### Long-Form Post The 2026 MLB Draft Combine wraps up today at Chase Field in Phoenix — and over four days, the baseball world got its best look yet at the players who will define the next decade of the sport. The headliners came in exactly as expected. Roch Cholowsky, the UCLA shortstop who slashed .338/.463/.707 with 21 home runs this spring, remained the consensus No. 1 overall prospect. The Chicago White Sox, holding that pick, face a genuine choice: Cholowsky at 55-60% probability in most mock drafts, or Grady Emerson — the 6'2" left-handed prep shortstop from Fort Worth Christian High School in Texas — at 40-45%. Both have the tools to go first overall. The White Sox's decision will define how the first round falls. Below the top two, several prospects moved their stock significantly: Jack Beck, a shortstop from Columbia Central High School in Tennessee, hit a ball 465.6 feet — the longest drive of the event. He also registered 20 batted balls at 100 mph or harder, with a peak exit velocity of 113 mph. Beck entered the Combine as a high-variance prospect. He exits as one of the most physically impressive hitters in this class. Brady Snow, a right-handed pitcher from American Heritage High School in Plantation, Florida, threw 97.7 mph — the fastest velocity recorded by any high school pitcher at this Combine. He averaged 96 mph and spun his slider at 2,990 rpm at 83 mph. Snow arrived as a first-round talent. He left as a potential top-ten pick. Collin Bland registered multiple exit velocities at 112.5 mph or higher. Logan Reddemann, a right-handed pitcher from UCLA, threw 92-94 mph with carry. The MLB Draft begins July 11 in Philadelphia, with Day 2 on July 12. The 2025 Combine saw 82.8% of attendees eventually drafted. The 2026 class looks deep. #MLBDraft #Baseball [Posting Window: 8:00 AM ET]

Kyle Tucker — Back Spasms Keeping Dodgers' $240M Star Day-to-Day
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Kyle Tucker is day-to-day, and the Dodgers are managing it carefully. Tucker, who signed a $240 million contract in the offseason to join Los Angeles, exited Monday's game against the Minnesota Twins in the second inning after feeling low back spasms going out for defense between innings. The timing — between the first and second — minimized the total playing time lost, but left the Dodgers with a visible health question heading into what should be a first-half sprint to the All-Star break. Manager Dave Roberts offered measured reassurance. The team is not "overly concerned," he said. Tucker himself was more specific: he was "pretty confident" he would avoid the Injured List, and reported feeling better by Tuesday when he remained out of the lineup as a precaution. The current target window for Tucker's return is the Dodgers' upcoming San Diego series. A day-to-day designation with a short target window is the best-case scenario when dealing with back stiffness — a condition that can escalate quickly if a player is rushed back. The broader context: the Dodgers are managing a championship roster through the stretch of the season where fatigue accumulates and small injuries compound. Tucker's $240 million deal was built on the assumption he would be available and productive through October. A brief back spasm absence is manageable. A longer absence would create lineup construction challenges for a Dodgers team built around his production in the outfield. Back spasms are the kind of injury that often look worse on paper than they are in practice. Tucker's confidence and Roberts' tone both point toward a short absence. For a Dodgers roster this deep, that is probably all it is. #Dodgers #KyleTucker [Posting Window: 3:00 PM ET]

Trade Deadline Countdown — Skubal, Chapman, and the Moves That Could Define August
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Thirty-eight days until the August 3 trade deadline. The chessboard is already in motion. The marquee piece is Tarik Skubal. The Detroit Tigers' ace is a two-time back-to-back Cy Young Award winner who has explicitly declined to discuss a contract extension, signaling that he will reach free agency after the 2026 season. The Tigers are 13 games under .500. There is no longer any reasonable case for keeping Skubal in Detroit for the second half of a lost season. The teams most aggressively linked to Skubal include the Dodgers, Yankees, Braves, Brewers, Blue Jays, and Padres. Every one of those organizations is in legitimate contention and looking for starting pitching upgrades. The asking price — one elite prospect plus two to three additional pieces — reflects Detroit's understanding that Skubal is the most valuable trade chip in the sport. The other significant piece is Aroldis Chapman. The Boston Red Sox closer is available as the organization shifts into seller mode. Chapman remains one of the sport's most electric bullpen arms, and any contender that can add him without surrendering a top prospect has improved its October bullpen dramatically. For buyers, the calculus is familiar: you can win without an in-season trade, but you can rarely win a championship without one. The teams that land Skubal have two months of the sport's best pitcher added to their rotations. The teams that don't have to beat him. The deadline is August 3. The negotiations are already happening behind closed doors. #MLB #TradeDeadline #TarikSkubal [Posting Window: 6:00 PM ET]
