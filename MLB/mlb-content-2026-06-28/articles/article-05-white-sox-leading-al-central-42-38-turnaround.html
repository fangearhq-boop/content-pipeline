Wait, the White Sox Are Leading the AL Central? Yes, and Here's Why It's Real

By Jake Torres June 28, 2026

The Standings

The Chicago White Sox are 42-38, first place in the American League Central, and the most underrated story in baseball this season. Jacob Gonzalez hit a walk-off RBI single in the ninth inning Friday night to beat the Kansas City Royals 2-1. That is the kind of moment -- a young player delivering a division-race win in a late inning -- that did not exist in Chicago's 2025 season.

The White Sox had one of the worst records in recent MLB history the previous year. The 2026 turnaround is not a hot streak. Through approximately 80 games, it is a sustained performance that has the team above .500 and in first place in their division. The facts are the facts.

The Division Picture

The AL Central standings as of June 27 are genuinely competitive. The White Sox lead at 42-38. The Guardians are at 42-40, within striking distance and in wild card position. The Royals are in the series tonight with Bobby Witt Jr. returning from a knee injury, meaning their best player is back after six days away. Three teams within range, one divisional series starting tonight, and a deadline in five weeks that could reshape any of these rosters.

For context on the broader AL: the Rays are 46-33 in the wild card and the Yankees lead the East at 48-33. The White Sox are not simply the best team in a weak division -- they are holding their own in a competitive conference.

What the Turnaround Is Built On

Walk-off wins in tight division games require execution in high-leverage moments. Gonzalez's ninth-inning single Friday was not a long ball off a bad pitch -- it was a professional at-bat that produced a run when the game was on the line. Teams that win games like that consistently are not getting lucky. They are making plays.

The second half will test whether the White Sox have the roster depth to sustain a division lead against teams that may acquire additional pieces before August 3. Their pitching and lineup construction will face harder scrutiny in July and August than they have through the first half. That is the standard test for every team in first place at the midpoint.

What's Next

Tonight's game against the Royals is the most immediate test. Kansas City gets Witt back in the lineup as designated hitter, which changes what their offense can do. The AL Central race will be defined by how the White Sox perform in divisional series through July. Whether they buy or sell at the deadline -- a decision that will clarify their front office's read on how real this contention window is -- will be the clearest signal of their second-half intentions. The most interesting story in the AL right now is playing in Chicago.
