Phase 2 Opens Monday: Ohtani and Clement Are In, and Here's What Happens Next

By Marcus Cole June 28, 2026

The Automatic Starters

Shohei Ohtani earned 3,341,257 votes in Phase 1 of All-Star voting and is the automatic NL starter at designated hitter. Ernie Clement of the Toronto Blue Jays earned 3,232,932 votes and is the automatic AL starter at second base. Both spots are locked -- no Phase 2 voting required. Whatever happens from Monday forward, those two players will be in the starting lineup at the 2026 All-Star Game.

Phase 2 opens Monday, June 29 at noon ET and runs through July 2 at noon ET. Winners are announced July 4 on FOX, along with pitchers and reserves who are selected separately. Fans vote at MLB.com/vote, with one vote allowed per person per 24-hour period.

What Phase 2 Actually Means

The most important detail about Phase 2: the votes from Phase 1 do not carry over. It is a completely fresh election. The top two finalists at each position -- plus the top six outfielders -- advance to the new ballot. A player who finished second in Phase 1 by a large margin has the same standing as one who finished first by a narrow margin. Everything resets.

That structure creates genuine competition. Fan bases that were energized during Phase 1 need to stay organized through July 2. Momentum from one phase does not automatically transfer. The team that gets its fans voting every day from Monday through Thursday will have a real advantage over the team that celebrated Phase 1 and then went quiet.

The Clement Story

Ernie Clement winning Phase 1 is the most notable organizational fan campaign in the All-Star voting era. The Blue Jays fan base coordinated in a way that produced 3,232,932 votes for a player who, under a standard awareness-driven voting process, would not have generated that number. The effort worked. Clement is now the automatic AL second baseman.

The precedent matters beyond 2026. Other fan bases will study what Toronto's supporters did and try to replicate it in future cycles. Organized campaigns have always been part of All-Star voting, but the coordination required and the result achieved here is a benchmark. Smaller markets now have a proven model for getting their player onto the field.

What's Next

The races at catcher, shortstop, third base, and the outfield spots are the ones to watch through Phase 2. First base and the AL/NL pitching spots will be determined on July 4. The full All-Star roster -- starters, pitchers, and reserves -- will be public before the end of the holiday weekend. Phase 2 voting is the last point where fans directly determine who starts. Vote at MLB.com/vote starting Monday at noon ET.
