Witt Jr. Is Back: Bobby Returns as DH in Series With Division-Leading White Sox

By Ryan Calloway June 28, 2026

Six Days, No IL

Bobby Witt Jr. is back in the Kansas City Royals lineup tonight. He suffered a Grade 1 right MCL sprain on June 18 against the Cardinals -- the lowest severity level for that type of injury -- missed six games, and never went on the injured list. He is starting as designated hitter against the Chicago White Sox, wearing a knee brace, with Josh Rojas handling shortstop.

The fact that Witt has never been placed on the injured list in five years in the major leagues is relevant context. Grade 1 sprains are the kind of injury that sends most players to the IL for a week or two. Witt's willingness to play through discomfort, combined with the Royals' confidence that the knee was stable enough to return without further risk, accelerated a timeline that had been described as "very encouraging" as recently as Friday.

Why the DH Designation Makes Sense

Starting Witt at designated hitter rather than shortstop on the first night back is the right call. Six days after a knee sprain, the demand of playing shortstop -- the lateral movement, the pivots on double plays, the throws from the hole -- is significantly higher than standing in the batter's box. The Royals are protecting their best player while getting his bat back in the lineup immediately. That is the right balance of caution and aggression given the situation.

If tonight goes well and the knee responds without setback, expect Witt to be back at shortstop within a game or two. The team has been careful without being overly conservative, which reflects both the nature of the injury and the stakes of the second half.

What It Means for the Royals

Kansas City enters tonight's series against a White Sox team that is 42-38 and leading the AL Central. The White Sox beat the Royals 2-1 Friday night on a Jacob Gonzalez walk-off RBI single in the ninth inning. The series continues tonight with Witt available for the first time since June 18.

The AL wild card race is tight. Having Witt back, even as DH for one game, changes what the Royals can do offensively against a division rival. His presence in the lineup affects how opposing pitchers approach every lineup card from the first pitch forward. Kansas City needed him back.

What's Next

Watch for Witt to return to shortstop in the next series if the knee holds up without any setback tonight. The Royals are in a position where every series in the AL Central matters, and having their best player available and healthy is the single most important factor in their second-half picture. The DH designation tonight is a one-game bridge. The goal is to have him at shortstop for the rest of the season.
