Skubal Is Almost Certainly Getting Traded -- Here's Who Makes the Most Sense

By Jake Torres June 28, 2026

The Odds and the Math

ESPN's Jeff Passan and Kiley McDaniel put the probability of a Tarik Skubal trade at 85 percent before the August 3 deadline. The Kalshi prediction market has it at 64 percent. The gap between those numbers reflects genuine disagreement about whether the Tigers will actually move their ace, but both figures point in the same direction: Skubal is most likely leaving Detroit before the trade deadline.

The asking price is what makes this trade historic. Detroit wants a top-100 prospect from the acquiring team's farm system plus two to three additional top-10 organizational prospects. That is a package comparable in scope to what Cleveland received in the CC Sabathia deal. For any specific team, the math is painful. The Braves, for example, would need to send two of their top-10 prospects, with one required to be a top-100 overall name. These are the kinds of pieces organizations build around for years.

The Market

The list of teams linked to Skubal is nearly every contender in baseball: Dodgers, Braves, Cubs, Yankees, Blue Jays, Phillies, Padres, Rangers, Rays. The breadth of that market is itself a signal. Teams that need pitching and have the prospect capital to make a run are all involved, which is exactly the situation Detroit wants. More bidders means a higher final price and more leverage for the Tigers to wait.

At approximately $11 million for two months of service, Skubal represents the most cost-efficient ace available at any deadline in recent memory. The salary is not the obstacle. The prospect cost is. The teams that can afford the asking price are the ones with deep systems -- organizations that have not spent their best chips already and can still absorb the loss of two to three high-end prospects without gutting their pipeline.

Who Makes the Most Sense

The Dodgers have the prospect depth and the payroll flexibility to absorb both the prospect cost and the salary. They are the betting favorite for most deadline acquisitions in any given year, and Skubal would fit a rotation that already has high-end talent but could use a front-line addition for a playoff push.

The Braves case is compelling from a need standpoint. They are 49-31, leading the NL East, and have a rotation that could benefit from a premium addition. Their farm system has the pieces Detroit wants. The question is whether Atlanta's front office is willing to pay the full asking price in a year where their window remains open.

The Yankees sit at 48-33 and have a pitching need that has been visible all season. Their farm system has been depleted by previous moves, which complicates their ability to match the Tigers' ask without compromising their pipeline in ways that affect 2027 and beyond.

What's Next

The Tigers are in no hurry. They control the timeline and benefit from letting contenders grow more desperate as August 3 approaches. Expect the trade to accelerate in the final 10 days before the deadline, when teams have clarity on their standing and less time to find alternatives. The landing spot will reshape the playoff race. Whatever team adds Skubal adds a No. 1 starter who changes the math in a short series. That is why the price is what it is.
