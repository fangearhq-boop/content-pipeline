Mets Fire Carlos Mendoza — Andy Green Steps In as Interim
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The Mets fired Carlos Mendoza on Friday, June 27. The record was 34-47 -- second-worst in the National League, ahead of only the Rockies and Giants. Andy Green, the Mets' VP of Player Development, takes over on an interim basis. Green will return to the front office after the 2026 season, and the permanent manager search begins then. The context matters here. Mendoza was not a bad manager. He led the Mets to the 2024 NLCS in his first season. In 2025 the team started 45-24 before collapsing to finish below .500. In 2026, they have been worse. The Mets lost six consecutive games before the front office made its move, including a Cubs doubleheader sweep that featured a 6-error game in the nightcap. The problem was not Mendoza. It was a roster that underperformed its payroll for two straight seasons, and a situation where the manager became the only lever the front office could pull publicly. Green inherits a team that needs more than a new voice in the dugout to fix what is wrong. The August 3 trade deadline is five weeks away. What Steve Cohen's front office does with it will tell us more about this organization's direction than the managerial change ever could. [Posting Window: 9:00 AM ET]

Skubal Trade Deadline Heat — 85 Percent Odds and a Historic Price Tag
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ESPN has the odds of a Tarik Skubal trade at 85 percent before August 3. The Kalshi prediction market puts it at 64 percent. The gap between those two numbers reflects genuine uncertainty about whether the Tigers will actually pull the trigger -- but the direction is clear. The asking price is historic. Detroit wants a top-100 prospect plus two to three additional top-10 organizational prospects. That is a package comparable to what Cleveland received in the CC Sabathia trade. The Braves would need to move two of their top-10 prospects, with one being top-100 overall. For reference, those are the kinds of pieces teams protect for years. The market is deep. Dodgers, Braves, Cubs, Yankees, Blue Jays, Phillies, Padres, Rangers, and Rays have all been connected. At approximately $11 million for two months of service, Skubal represents the most cost-efficient ace available at any trade deadline in recent memory. Every contender with pitching questions has a reason to make the call. The Tigers are not in a hurry. They control Skubal through this season and can wait for the deadline to approach before accepting their best offer. The longer they wait, the more desperate contenders become. That leverage is real. ### Long-Form Post Bobby Witt Jr. is back in the Kansas City Royals lineup tonight. He suffered a Grade 1 right MCL sprain on June 18 against the Cardinals -- the lowest severity classification for that type of injury. He missed six games. He never went on the injured list. In five years in the major leagues, Witt has never been placed on the IL. Tonight he starts as designated hitter against the White Sox, wearing a knee brace, with Josh Rojas handling shortstop. All-Star Phase 2 Opens Monday — Ohtani and Clement Are In, Everyone Else Fights for Their Spot
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Phase 2 of MLB All-Star voting opens Monday, June 29 at noon ET. Shohei Ohtani earned 3,341,257 votes in Phase 1 and is the automatic NL starter as designated hitter. Ernie Clement of the Blue Jays earned 3,232,932 votes and is the automatic AL starter at second base. Both spots are locked -- Phase 2 voting determines the remaining starters. Here is the key detail most fans miss: Phase 1 votes do not carry over. Phase 2 is a completely fresh election. The top two finalists at each position advance to a new ballot, plus the top six outfielders. One vote per day, per person, through July 2 at noon ET. Winners are announced July 4 on FOX along with pitchers and reserves. The Clement story alone is worth watching. The Blue Jays fan base organized around him in Phase 1 in a way that hadn't been seen since the aggressive vote campaigns of the 2010s. Now he is the automatic starter and has set a benchmark that other franchises will study heading into 2027. The Phase 2 races at shortstop, catcher, and the outfield spots are the ones to watch. The White Sox are 42-38, leading the AL Central, after what was one of the most difficult seasons in recent baseball memory in 2025.
