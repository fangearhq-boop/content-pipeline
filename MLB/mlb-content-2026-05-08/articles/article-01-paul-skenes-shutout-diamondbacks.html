Paul Skenes Throws 8 Shutout Innings Against Diamondbacks in Best Start of 2026 Season

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | May 8, 2026

The defending NL Cy Young Award winner served notice on Thursday night: he is still the best pitcher in the National League, and he is just getting started.

Paul Skenes threw eight shutout innings against the Arizona Diamondbacks on Thursday, allowing just two hits while striking out seven batters. By any measure, it was the best start of his 2026 season, and a performance that will generate serious attention in the early NL Cy Young Award discussion.

The line — 8 IP, 0 ER, 2 H, 7 K — tells part of the story. The efficiency is what makes it particularly impressive. Skenes worked deep into the game, sparing the bullpen on a night when the team needed it.

Defending His Crown

Skenes won the NL Cy Young Award in 2025, putting together a season that established him as the premier young arm in the National League. There was always going to be a target on his back in 2026 — other pitchers would be chasing him, and skeptics would be watching for any sign of regression.

Thursday's outing against Arizona was not a sign of regression. It was Skenes at his best: dominant, controlled, nearly unhittable. The two-hit shutout performance is the kind that reminder fans why he won the award in the first place.

The Arizona Diamondbacks are not an easy opponent. They feature a lineup capable of doing damage against any pitcher. That made Skenes' efficiency — eight innings on what likely wasn't an excessive pitch count — all the more impressive.

NL Cy Young Watch Begins

It is early in May, and award predictions are inherently premature. But Thursday's performance puts Skenes firmly in the conversation as the early NL Cy Young frontrunner. A defending champion having what observers called his "best start of the season" in the first week of May is not something to dismiss lightly.

The National League features several elite pitchers who will contend for the award all season. Skenes, however, enters the summer as the standard by which others will be measured.

What's Next

Skenes will continue his 2026 season with his next start likely coming in four or five days, depending on rotation scheduling. The national attention from Thursday's gem will only increase expectations — and based on what he delivered against Arizona, that appears to be exactly where he wants to be.
