Cubs 24-12, Franchise Home Win Record: Chicago Is Having a Historic Start to 2026

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | May 8, 2026

The Chicago Cubs are one of baseball's best stories in 2026, and the numbers back it up at every turn.

At 24-12, the Cubs hold a 3.5-game lead in the NL Central and rank among the elite teams in the National League. They've won 18 of their last 21 games. At Wrigley Field, they've won 14 consecutive home games — a new franchise record. The Cubs, who not long ago seemed to be in a rebuilding phase, are playing like a legitimate pennant contender.

Walk-Off Magic and Home Dominance

This week alone, the Cubs authored a walk-off win against Cincinnati in three consecutive games — a feat so rare it has only happened a handful of times in franchise history. The offense has been timely, the bullpen has been reliable in close games, and the atmosphere at Wrigley has reflected a fanbase that senses something special is happening.

The 14-game home winning streak is more than a number — it's a statement. Wrigley Field has historically been a neutral-or-worse environment for Cubs teams that weren't built to take advantage of the park. This team is. They're winning at home at an extraordinary clip, and they're doing it against a variety of opponents.

NL Central Command

The Cubs' 3.5-game lead over the St. Louis Cardinals in the NL Central is a comfortable but not commanding buffer. St. Louis has its own compelling story — Jordan Walker's breakout season (.308, 10 HR, .962 OPS) has the Cardinals playing competitive baseball. But right now, Chicago is the division's best team by record and by feel.

The Cubs are also just a half game behind the Atlanta Braves (26-12) for the best record in the National League. If they continue at this pace, a top seed in the NL playoffs is not an unrealistic outcome.

What's Next

The Cubs will continue to defend their NL Central lead through the weekend, with the pennant race heating up as May progresses. The franchise home win streak at Wrigley is a record that fans should celebrate — it reflects a team that is genuinely dominant at home. If Chicago keeps winning at this rate, the North Side might have the most entertaining team in baseball in 2026.
