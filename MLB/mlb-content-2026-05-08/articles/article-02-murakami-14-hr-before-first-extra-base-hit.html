Munetaka Murakami Sets MLB Record: 14 Home Runs Before His First Career Double or Triple

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | May 8, 2026

Munetaka Murakami plays baseball in a way that has no historical precedent — and that is not an exaggeration.

The Chicago White Sox rookie slugger hit 14 home runs before recording a single double or triple in his MLB career, setting a record that no player in the sport's history had previously achieved. When Murakami finally hit his first career double on May 4, the statistical ledger he had built was extraordinary: 14 HRs, 0 doubles, 0 triples.

He is also tied with Aaron Judge of the New York Yankees for the MLB home run lead at 14.

An Unprecedented Pattern

In baseball, extra-base hits come in three forms: doubles, triples, and home runs. Virtually every power hitter in the sport's history has accumulated doubles alongside their home runs. That's simply how the game works — balls hit hard often end up as doubles when they don't clear the fence, and line drives in the gaps become extra-base hits in their own right.

Murakami apparently didn't get that memo. His first 14 career extra-base hits were all home runs. Not some of them — all of them. He also became just the fourth player in MLB history to homer in each of his first three career games, and the first White Sox player ever to accomplish that feat.

Murakami signed a two-year, $34 million contract with Chicago and made his debut on March 26, 2026. At his current pace, some projections suggest he could finish the season with over 50 home runs. That would be one of the great power seasons in baseball history.

The HR Race

Tied with Judge at 14 home runs, Murakami occupies the top spot on the MLB home run leaderboard alongside one of baseball's greatest power hitters. Judge won the AL MVP in 2022 with 62 home runs — the AL single-season record. Murakami, in his first MLB season, is keeping pace.

Yordan Álvarez of the Houston Astros and others are clustered behind them, making the 2026 home run race one of the more compelling storylines of the early season.

What's Next

The next milestone to watch: whether Murakami can sustain this pace and challenge for the single-season home run record as the summer progresses. Fifty-plus home runs is an extraordinary bar, but early-season projections make it a genuine possibility. For White Sox fans — a franchise that hasn't had a lot to celebrate in recent years — Murakami is the most exciting player to watch in baseball right now.
