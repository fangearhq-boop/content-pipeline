Yankees Sweep Red Sox at Fenway — Improve to AL-Best 16-9 on Five-Game Win Streak

By Ryan Calloway | April 24, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Yankees came to Fenway Park and swept the house.

New York finished a three-game series against the Boston Red Sox on Thursday, winning all three games at Fenway Park (April 21-23) and improving to 16-9 — the best record in the American League. The Yankees have now won five consecutive games and are establishing themselves as the clear class of the AL East after the first month of the season.

The series was notable not just for the result but for how it was accomplished. The Yankees pitched brilliantly, hit timely, and executed in Boston's most storied environment with the calm of a team that feels it belongs at the top.

How the Sweep Unfolded

Game 1 on April 21 began with Giancarlo Stanton making his presence felt immediately. The Yankees designated hitter hit a solo home run and added a two-run double as New York won 4-0. The Red Sox managed little offensively against the New York pitching staff, and the final score understated the margin of dominance.

Game 2 belonged to Max Fried. The left-hander delivered eight scoreless innings, allowing just three hits across the entire outing in a 4-1 Yankees victory. Fried's performance was the kind of statement start that pitching coaches draw up when their team is trying to win a series on the road against a rival: go deep, use no bullpen, give your team a win without any drama. He did exactly that.

Game 3 on April 23 completed the sweep in the series finale. The Yankees sent Payton Tolle to the mound for his 2026 debut on Boston's behalf, and New York navigated the game successfully to close it out. Three games, three wins, all in Fenway.

AL East Picture Takes Shape

The Boston Red Sox fall to 9-16 with Thursday's result, sitting seven games behind New York in the AL East standings — firmly in last place. This is the first Yankees-Red Sox meeting since New York eliminated Boston in last year's Wild Card Series, and the Yankees left no ambiguity about the current hierarchy in the rivalry.

The AL East has not become a runaway yet — Baltimore, Toronto, and Tampa Bay remain factors — but New York's 16-9 start puts them in command. The Yankees' pitching depth, from Fried to the back of the rotation, combined with a lineup that can generate runs through power and situation hitting, gives them a complete profile that other AL contenders will need to match to challenge for home-field advantage in October.

What's Next

The Yankees will return home for a series this weekend before heading back on the road. The five-game win streak has New York riding momentum into one of the season's most productive stretches — the stretch from late April through mid-May when team identities crystallize and early-season tendencies become confirmed trends. If the Yankees extend this run through the next homestand, the conversation about AL pennant contention will start in earnest. For the Red Sox, the challenge is structural: a seven-game deficit in late April with a .360 winning percentage is a difficult hole, though not an insurmountable one given how early the season is.
