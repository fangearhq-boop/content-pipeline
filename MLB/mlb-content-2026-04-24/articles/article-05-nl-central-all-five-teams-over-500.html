All Five NL Central Teams Are Over .500 — Baseball's Most Competitive Division Has No Floor

By Jake Torres | April 24, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Milwaukee Brewers are in last place in the National League Central. They are 12-9. They have a winning record. They would lead four of baseball's other five divisions.

That is the state of the NL Central in late April 2026, and it has produced the most tightly contested divisional race in the major leagues.

All five teams are above .500. The Cincinnati Reds lead at 15-8. The Pittsburgh Pirates, Chicago Cubs, and St. Louis Cardinals are each 13-9. The defending NL Central champions, the Brewers — who posted the best record in all of baseball in 2025 at 97-65 — are in last place despite a winning record, just three games back. No team in the division is more than three games behind the leader.

Why the Reds Are Leading

Cincinnati's 15-8 record is driven in significant part by Corbin Burns, who has been pitching like a legitimate Cy Young candidate through the first month of the season. Burns, who Cincinnati acquired with the intention of making him the anchor of a rebuilt rotation, has delivered on that promise with precision and velocity. The Reds have made a series of smart organizational decisions that have paid off in the early standings, and their position atop the division reflects both their talent and their ability to execute early.

The Pirates, Cubs, and Cardinals at 13-9 each represent a different kind of story. Pittsburgh is outperforming most preseason projections, playing with the energy of a young roster that believes in itself. Chicago is winning without their top starter, who has been sidelined with an injury, demonstrating the rotation depth that manager Craig Counsell has cultivated. St. Louis is managing expectations carefully while finding ways to win close games.

The Brewers Are Fine

Milwaukee's position in last place should not generate concern — yet. The Brewers were the best team in baseball last year and they know how to navigate a long season. Their 12-9 record is a winning record, they have faced no significant injuries to their core, and the talent that powered a 97-win season is still present. The NL Central schedule will give them multiple opportunities against division rivals over the coming months, and the Brewers' organizational experience at winning games in September is as reliable as any team in the league.

The deeper point about the NL Central is that its balance produces compelling baseball through all six months of the season. In a division where the "last place" team is 12-9, every series carries weight. There are no easy weeks. Winning a road trip against a division rival feels like genuine progress because it probably is.

What's Next

The NL Central race will be determined through head-to-head division games, which accelerate in frequency through May and June. The Reds' early lead is real but fragile — three games is not an insurmountable advantage with five months remaining. The division is positioned to deliver one of the most competitive title races in recent NL history. Watch for how the Brewers respond over the next 30 games, whether the Reds can maintain Burns's health, and whether the Pirates' early-season performance is sustainable. All three questions will shape the story of this division's second half.
