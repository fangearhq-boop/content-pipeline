Aaron Judge Records 500th Career Extra-Base Hit — First in MLB History With 5 HR and 15 RBI in Team's First 6 Games

By Marcus Cole | April 3, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Aaron Judge is doing something no player in Major League Baseball history has ever done.

The New York Yankees slugger hit a 3-run home run off Arizona's Merrill Kelly in the first inning on Friday, April 3 — his fifth home run of the young 2026 season. With it, he became the first player in MLB history to record five home runs and 15 RBIs in his team's first six games. The home run also served as his 500th career extra-base hit, reached in his 999th game wearing Yankee pinstripes — making him the third-fastest player in Yankees franchise history to reach that milestone, measured by games played.

The Yankees are 5-1. Their best player is making history every time he steps to the plate.

AL Player of the Week and a Slow Start Forgotten

Judge's opening week began inauspiciously — he went 0-for-7 with five strikeouts on Opening Day. What followed was one of the most explosive stretches in recent baseball memory. Named the American League Player of the Week for the season's first frame, Judge finished the opening week 6-for-11 with four home runs, 11 RBIs, and a 1.818 slugging percentage.

His first home run of the season came via an ABS challenge reversal — an early marker of how baseball's automatic ball-strike system is already changing the game. The home run was overturned from a called strike, and Judge responded by sending the pitch over the wall. By the end of the week, the story was no longer about the ABS system. It was about Judge.

The 400 HR Chase

Judge entered the 2026 season with 368 career home runs — needing 32 to reach 400. That countdown has accelerated faster than anyone expected. With five home runs in six games, Judge has shortened the chase to 27 or fewer remaining. If he sustains even a fraction of this pace, the 400 home run milestone will arrive in August at the latest.

Saturday's game, April 4, will be Judge's 1,000th as a Yankee. If he homers in that game, he would tie Babe Ruth's record of 321 home runs in a player's first 1,000 games in Yankee pinstripes. It is a measure of where Judge stands in the franchise's history that the comparison is even relevant.

What's Next

The Yankees play Saturday in what will be Aaron Judge's 1,000th game as a member of the franchise. The 1,000-game milestone, the Ruth comparison, and the ongoing 400 HR chase all converge this weekend in New York.

The AL East race is still six games old. But the Yankees have the best record in the division, and they have the best player in the sport playing the best baseball of anyone in the major leagues right now. That combination has defined New York's championship windows before. It appears to be defining this one too.
