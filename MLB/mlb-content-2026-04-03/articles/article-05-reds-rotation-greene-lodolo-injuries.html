Reds' Rotation Already in Trouble — Greene Out Until July, Lodolo Rehabbing Blister

By Jake Torres | April 3, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Cincinnati's 3-3 start looks solid until you examine the rotation behind it.

The Reds have opened 2026 without Hunter Greene and Nick Lodolo — their two most talented starting pitchers — and that combination of absences will define how Cincinnati's season is written in the months ahead. Six games into the year, the organization already faces a meaningful rotation challenge that has nothing to do with performance and everything to do with injury.

Greene Out Until July

Hunter Greene underwent right elbow surgery and will not return to the Reds until at least July. The 25-year-old right-hander was entering this season as the unquestioned anchor of Cincinnati's starting rotation — a power pitcher with triple-digit fastball velocity, elite strikeout rates, and a ceiling that scouts consistently rated as one of the highest in the sport.

Without Greene for the first three-plus months of the season, the Reds are asking their rotation to perform without its best arm. Whatever Greene brings back when he returns — and the timeline assumes a July return at the earliest — Cincinnati will have played roughly 80 games before they see him.

Lodolo Rehabbing a Blister

Nick Lodolo is on the injured list with a blister on his left index finger. The left-hander is not as immediately alarming a concern as Greene — blisters are manageable and he appears to be progressing — but his absence further depletes a rotation that was already missing its ace.

Lodolo threw a 60 to 65 pitch rehab start on Thursday for Single-A Daytona against Jupiter. The team is targeting a potential mid-April return, with one scenario placing him back in a Miami series. If the start goes well and the blister cooperates, Lodolo's absence may be relatively brief. But "relatively brief" still means missing additional starts, and the Reds cannot afford to lose more innings from their best arms.

What's Next

Cincinnati is 3-3 — which means the rest of the rotation has held up so far. How long that continues without Greene determines whether the Reds remain competitive into May or begin to fall behind in the NL Central.

Lodolo's return is the more immediate variable to track. If he comes back healthy in mid-April, the Reds get at least one of their two best starters back in a reasonable timeframe. Greene's July return, if on schedule, would give the team a potential second-half boost at exactly the point when the playoff race typically begins to sharpen.

The 2026 Reds season will be defined in part by how much baseball they can win before those two starters return. Three wins and three losses through six games is a reasonable foundation. The test comes now.
