Aaron Judge's First HR of 2026 Had an Unexpected Assist — And It Kicks Off a Historic Chase

By Ballpark Banter Staff | March 31, 2026

Aaron Judge's 2026 season started with a thud. He went 0-for-5 in the Yankees' Opening Night win over the San Francisco Giants — no hits, a handful of strikeouts, the kind of night that would concern anyone if they weren't watching Aaron Judge.

Then he hit a home run in each of the next two games.

His first home run of the season came with a twist that only 2026 could provide: an ABS challenge. Under the new semi-Automated Ball-Strike system that debuted this week, Judge challenged a ball-strike call in the same at-bat — successfully — and then made the corrected count count, depositing a pitch over the wall for his first home run of the year.

It was a fittingly modern beginning to what could be one of the most historic individual milestone chases in the 2026 season.

The 400 Home Run Chase

Judge enters 2026 with 368 career home runs. He needs 32 more to reach 400, and he's chasing more than the milestone itself. Mark McGwire holds the record for the fewest career games to reach 400 home runs, getting there in game No. 1,412. Judge has played 1,145 career games. If he reaches 400 this season, he breaks McGwire's record with significant margin to spare.

The pace is achievable. Judge hit at least 50 home runs in two of the last four seasons. Even if he manages 35-40 this year — a conservative projection for a player of his caliber — he reaches the milestone before September. If he approaches his peak pace, the record could fall by midsummer.

Career Context: Already the Greatest Living Slugger

The 400 HR chase is not happening in isolation. Judge already holds the record for the fewest career games to reach 300 home runs — 955 games, surpassing Ralph Kiner by 132 games. He has won back-to-back Most Valuable Player awards (unanimous in 2024, adding his first batting title that year) and won again in 2025. He captained Team USA at the 2026 World Baseball Classic, hitting two home runs in the tournament.

He is, by almost any measure, the best hitter in baseball — and possibly the best pure power hitter the Yankees have had since Mickey Mantle.

A New Era Assists the Chase

The ABS challenge system adds an interesting dimension to the chase this season. For a hitter like Judge — who draws walks at a high rate and has exceptional plate discipline — the ability to challenge incorrect ball-strike calls could help him protect favorable counts and get better pitches to hit. His first 2026 home run coming after a successful challenge is both a coincidence and a preview of what this technology might enable for elite hitters.

Through six days of the 2026 season, the system has already overturned numerous calls across multiple games. If hitters get more accurate strike zones, power hitters who drive pitches out of specific zones benefit most.

What's Next

The Yankees are 3-0 after sweeping the Giants on the road to open the season. Judge's bounce-back after Opening Night is exactly the kind of narrative that builds over a long season — bad day, reset, production resumes. The 400 HR chase will be the most-tracked individual milestone of the 2026 AL season. The only question is when, not if.
