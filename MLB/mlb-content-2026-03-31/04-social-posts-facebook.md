### STORY 4: ABS Challenge System Debuts

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:30 PM ET

```
Baseball changed this week.

For the first time in Major League history, the semi-Automated Ball-Strike (ABS) challenge system is officially in use during the regular season. The rules: batters, pitchers, and catchers can challenge a ball-strike call by tapping their helmet or cap. Each team starts with two challenges per game.

If the challenge is upheld — meaning the call was wrong — the original challenge is returned and the game continues. If the challenge fails, the team loses one of its two challenges.

The debut could not have been more dramatic. In one of the first games using the system, veteran umpire CB Bucknor had 8 calls challenged — and 6 of them were overturned. The system has been pushed for years by analytics advocates, hitters who felt penalized by inconsistent strike zones, and pitchers who wanted to protect their borderline pitches. Whether you love it or hate it, what happened this opening weekend confirmed the core argument: traditional ball-strike calling was, in many cases, wrong.

This is the beginning of a new era for baseball. And it's going to be talked about all season long.
```
