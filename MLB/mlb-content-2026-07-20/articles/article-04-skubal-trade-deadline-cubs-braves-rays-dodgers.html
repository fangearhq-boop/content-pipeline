Five Teams Are In On Tarik Skubal. Detroit's Price Tag Is Still "Outrageous."

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | July 20, 2026

The Tarik Skubal trade deadline situation has more buyers in the room.

The Chicago Cubs, Atlanta Braves, and Tampa Bay Rays had been the established suitors. Now the Los Angeles Dodgers and Philadelphia Phillies are confirmed active as well. That is five teams in pursuit of the same pitcher with 14 days remaining before the August 3 deadline.

The asking price has not changed.

The Buyer Pool

Each of the five teams has a different motivation and a different level of urgency.

The Cubs have the most pressing need. Their rotation has been a problem and the deadline is their clearest chance to fix it. The Braves want to pair Skubal with Chris Sale and give themselves two elite starters for a postseason run. The Rays are described as aggressive buyers — a departure from their usual posture that signals how seriously they view their window. The Dodgers are considered a wild card, with the farm system depth and financial resources to make a significant offer even after adding at the deadline. The Phillies round out a group that reflects just how rare Skubal's profile is.

A two-time Cy Young winner. Twenty-eight years old. Posted in a contract year with no extension talks on the horizon. Teams with a chance to win now are willing to pay for that combination even knowing they get him for one playoff run.

The Asking Price

Per Jon Heyman of the New York Post, the Tigers are seeking controllable pitching and athletic position players close to big-league ready. That is a high bar. Skubal is a rental — he becomes a free agent after the 2026 season — and Detroit is asking for pieces that would contribute to the franchise beyond the current year.

One NL executive described the asking price as "outrageous." That characterization has appeared in multiple reports. It reflects the frustration of teams that want Skubal but are being asked to pay a premium that does not align with the one-year return they would receive.

The Tigers' position is rational. Skubal represents an asset they have developed from the beginning. Trading him without certainty in return makes no sense unless the return is spectacular. They have set a price that reflects that conviction and are waiting for someone to meet it.

Skubal's Credentials

Two AL Cy Young Awards in 2024 and 2025. A 2026 season that has reinforced his standing as one of the best pitchers in the sport. His ERA, strikeout rate, and WHIP have all been consistent with his award-winning campaigns. For any team that acquires him, the argument is simple: add the best available pitcher, give him a postseason stage, and see what happens.

What's Next

Fourteen days remain before the August 3 deadline. The five confirmed suitors will each need to decide whether the asking price is worth the one-year rental. The Tigers will need to decide whether to move at all if no one meets their number — holding Skubal through the deadline and into free agency is a possibility, though one that carries its own risks. The deadline will resolve this. Until then, five teams are calling Detroit, and Detroit has not answered the way any of them wants yet.
