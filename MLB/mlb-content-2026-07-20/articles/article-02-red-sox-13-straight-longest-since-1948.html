Thirteen Straight. The Boston Red Sox Have Not Lost Since July 3.

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 20, 2026

The Boston Red Sox beat the Tampa Bay Rays 6-1 Sunday to complete a four-game sweep and extend their winning streak to 13 games.

Thirteen consecutive wins. The franchise's longest win streak since 1948. The second-longest in Red Sox history.

The franchise record is 15. They are two wins away.

The 13th Win

Sunday's game was controlled from the beginning. The Red Sox led comfortably against the Rays, gave the starting pitcher a lead to work with, and got contributions from multiple lineup spots to keep the margin wide. The 6-1 final was a clean win — not a comeback, not a late-inning rally, not a one-run grinder. Just a baseball game won the way good teams win baseball games.

What made it significant was not the manner of the win but the count. Thirteen in a row. The same franchise that went 11-9 in the last three weeks before the streak feels like a completely different team than the one that started it.

The Historical Context

The last time the Red Sox won 13 consecutive games was 1948. That team finished the season tied for the American League pennant before losing a one-game playoff to the Cleveland Indians. It had Dom DiMaggio in center field and Bobby Doerr at second base and Ted Williams in left.

This team has a 13-game winning streak and a record of 50-48.

The streak ties the second-longest in franchise history. The record is 15 consecutive wins. The Red Sox have a game Monday against the Baltimore Orioles. Then another. The record is within reach in a way that felt impossible two weeks ago.

How This Happened

The Red Sox are doing this without a superstar carrying them through. The contributions have been distributed — the kind of winning that comes from depth and consistency rather than one elite performer. Every game of the streak has been different. The cumulative result is the same: a win column that has moved 13 times in a row without interruption.

Boston is 50-48. Even. For a team that was multiple games below .500 when this run began, that is not a modest accomplishment. It is a genuine turnaround that has reshaped the AL Wild Card race and put the Red Sox back in conversations they had no business being in two weeks ago.

What's Next

The Orioles start a series in Boston on Monday. The Red Sox need two wins to tie the franchise record of 15. Three wins to break it. Whether the streak reaches that number or not, it has already produced something meaningful: a 50-48 baseball team that looked like a selling candidate a month ago and now holds a Wild Card spot. The 13 is the number. The number behind it matters more.
