Ohtani's July 22 Start Is Canceled. Left Knee Irritation. No Timeline.

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 20, 2026

Shohei Ohtani is not pitching Wednesday.

The Los Angeles Dodgers announced Sunday that Ohtani's scheduled July 22 start against the Philadelphia Phillies has been canceled. The injury is left knee irritation. Ohtani received a lubricant injection in the knee before the All-Star break and has not felt fully healthy when throwing since.

Manager Dave Roberts offered no specific return date. He said it would be "some time." He suggested two to three weeks as a possible window but framed the absence as a precaution against a larger problem later in the season rather than a reaction to something acute.

What Happened

The left knee irritation has been managed rather than treated in the sense of being resolved. Ohtani received the injection before the break. He attempted to play catch and throw a bullpen session in the days that followed and did not feel 100 percent. The Dodgers made the decision to remove him from his next scheduled start rather than push through and risk a more significant setback.

Roberts explained the thinking directly: if the issue costs Ohtani two to three weeks now, the team would rather absorb that cost in July than have it become a problem in September or October. The Dodgers have too much invested in their ace being healthy for the postseason to gamble with his availability now.

The Hitting Is Separate

Ohtani continues to play. He was in the lineup Sunday as the designated hitter in Game 1 of the Dodgers-Yankees doubleheader at Yankee Stadium and went 2-for-5 with two RBI. There is no pain when hitting. The knee issue is specific to the mechanics and load of pitching, not the swing.

That is the best news in an otherwise uncertain announcement. The two-way career is not in question. The mound return is on pause. Ohtani will keep contributing as a hitter while the knee settles and the Dodgers' medical staff determines when he is ready to pitch again.

What This Means for the Dodgers

The Dodgers have the best record in baseball. They have depth in their rotation. The absence of Ohtani on the mound for the next two to three weeks does not put their postseason position at risk.

What it does is create a gap in their rotation that someone else has to fill. And it extends the period of uncertainty around the thing the Dodgers need most by October: a fully healthy Ohtani on the mound. His last full season as a two-way player before his elbow surgery showed what a postseason-ready Ohtani means. The Dodgers know that better than anyone.

Roberts' framing of the absence as a long-term investment is the right one. Two to three weeks in July is a reasonable trade for a fully healthy Ohtani in October. But there is no guarantee the two weeks becomes three, or that the three becomes four. That is what no timeline means.

What's Next

The Dodgers will announce rotation adjustments for the coming weeks. Ohtani will continue as the designated hitter in the meantime. Roberts said the club will reassess when Ohtani feels better throwing and will set a return date at that point. The August 3 trade deadline is two weeks away. The Dodgers' rotation depth and the Ohtani mound timeline will both be relevant to how the front office approaches the deadline — though the team has the flexibility to address either need from a position of strength.
