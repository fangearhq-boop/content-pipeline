Colson Montgomery Hits No. 24. White Sox Blank Toronto 3-0.

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 20, 2026

Colson Montgomery hit his 24th home run of the season Sunday, and the Chicago White Sox shut out the Toronto Blue Jays 3-0 at Rogers Centre.

Sean Burke started and combined with the bullpen on a four-hit shutout. Montgomery provided the power. Chicago left Toronto with a win and remained in first place in the AL Central with a record of 51-46.

Montgomery at 24

The numbers are not a projection anymore. Colson Montgomery — the No. 1 overall pick from the 2023 draft — has 24 home runs in mid-July. He is among the power leaders in the American League and is doing it in his first full season in the major leagues.

When the White Sox selected Montgomery with the top pick, the expectation was that he would be a cornerstone of whatever rebuild they were undertaking after a historic 2024 collapse. He is doing more than that. He is being a cornerstone of a division leader in the present tense, right now, in 2026.

The 24 home runs represent a pace that very few rookies sustain. Montgomery has sustained it into the final stretch before the trade deadline. Every home run he hits is another data point confirming that the evaluation was right and that the development has been faster than most rebuilds allow.

The Pitching

Sean Burke handled the Blue Jays for the bulk of the game before turning it over to the bullpen. The combined shutout held Toronto to four hits across nine innings. Burke's line extended a strong run for a Chicago staff that has quietly been one of the more effective units in the AL Central this season.

The 3-0 shutout is the kind of game that builds winning percentage quietly. No drama, no late-inning heroics. Just a clean game won by good pitching and one home run.

The AL Central Picture

The White Sox are 51-46 and lead the AL Central. That sentence would have been unthinkable entering the 2026 season for most outside observers. This team went 41-121 in 2024. They are 51-46 now, in first place in their division, with the trade deadline two weeks away and Montgomery hitting home runs at a pace that would have made any AL team want to draft him first overall.

What's Next

The White Sox return home from the Toronto series and continue their schedule through the August 3 trade deadline. Whether the front office acts as a buyer or stays the course with their current roster will define the next chapter of what is already a remarkable season. Montgomery's 24th home run Sunday did not change anything about that decision. It just made it more enjoyable to watch.
