Mariners Sweep A's and Take First Place in the AL West for the First Time in 2026

By Ryan Calloway

May 29, 2026

In the most wide-open division in baseball — where every team has spent time under .500 — the Mariners finally claimed the top spot by sweeping Oakland this week.

The American League West has been one of the most genuinely unpredictable divisions in baseball in 2026. All five teams have spent time below .500 at some point this season. No team has separated meaningfully from the pack. The standings page has changed shape almost daily.

After sweeping the Oakland Athletics in a three-game series — capping the series with a 9-1 win at Sutter Health Park — the Seattle Mariners are in sole possession of first place in the AL West for the first time this season. They are the leaders of a division that doesn't yet know who its leader is supposed to be. That is both a compliment and a caveat.

The Sweep and What It Means

The A's, currently operating out of Sacramento while their Las Vegas facility is completed, did not offer much resistance in the series finale. The Mariners won 9-1 and completed the three-game sweep with authority. It was the kind of series that doesn't feel like a defining moment when it happens but looks more significant in hindsight — because when Seattle landed in first place after that final out, it was the first time all season they could make that claim.

The Mariners entered 2026 with expectations. Their roster was regarded as one of the more complete in the AL West. The preseason analysis favored them to contend. What they couldn't control was a division where nobody ran away and left them room to breathe. Every week the standings shuffled, and Seattle couldn't find enough separation to sit comfortably above the rest.

For now, they are above the rest. The question is whether they can stay there.

A Division That Refuses to Separate

The five AL West teams — Seattle, Houston, Texas, the Angels, and the A's — have all experienced extended stretches of competitive mediocrity in 2026. No team has gone on a 15-5 run that reshapes the standings. No team has fallen out of contention through consistent losing. The result is a division where winning first place in late May requires only moderate competence sustained over a few weeks.

That context matters when evaluating what the Mariners have accomplished. They are not leading a dominant division; they are leading a tight one. The distance between first place and the bottom of the division is likely measured in single digits. Any of the other four teams could be playing first-place baseball within the next two or three weeks if the Mariners cool off.

The most accurate way to describe Seattle's position is this: they are the best team in the AL West right now, and nobody yet knows who the best team in the AL West will be in September.

What's Next

The Mariners' schedule in the coming weeks will tell us a great deal about whether this first-place moment is the beginning of a sustained run or a temporary high point in a season that continues to refuse resolution. If they can hold the division lead through June, the conversation about Seattle as a genuine postseason contender becomes much more serious.

For a franchise and fanbase that has been waiting for this kind of season for years, being in first place — even for a day — feels like something. It is proof that the direction is right. Whether it's also proof that the destination is within reach is a question that will take months to answer.
