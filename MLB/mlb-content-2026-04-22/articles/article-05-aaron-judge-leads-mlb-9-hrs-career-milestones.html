Aaron Judge Leads MLB with 9 Home Runs — Career Milestones Accumulating as He Climbs Yankees History

By Jake Torres | April 22, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Aaron Judge leads Major League Baseball with 9 home runs through 22 games, and the milestones are arriving in clusters. His career total stands at 377 home runs, he has accumulated franchise records the way other players accumulate doubles, and he turns 34 years old on Saturday, April 26 — still in what most analysts consider his prime production years.

The individual milestones are arriving with regularity. Earlier this season, Judge hit his 90th career home run in the first inning of a game — placing him third in Yankees franchise history in that specific category, behind Babe Ruth (126) and Mickey Mantle (103). He also moved past Mantle on the Yankees' all-time list for multi-home run games, where he now sits second with 47, trailing only Ruth.

The 400 and 500 Milestones

Judge needs 23 more home runs to reach 400 for his career, a threshold he could reasonably reach during the 2026 season if his current pace holds. At 9 HRs through 22 games, he is on a pace that projects to 60 or more over a full 162-game season — a rate that would make the 400-home run milestone achievable by summer and potentially position him to chase the single-season franchise record he set in 2022 (62 HRs).

Looking further ahead, analysts project 500 career home runs could be within reach as early as the 2028 season if Judge maintains a pace of 45 to 50 per year. That milestone — 500 career home runs — has historically guaranteed Hall of Fame consideration and places a player among the most productive long-ball hitters in the sport's history.

The Yankees Context

The Yankees have won without Judge in the past, but his production is central to the team's offensive identity. Through 22 games, his 9 home runs lead the majors and give New York a clear offensive anchor even as the team navigates its own early-season ups and downs. The pitching staff has been one of the team's stronger early-season stories, and Judge's bat gives the offense what it needs to be competitive against any opponent.

The Mantle comparisons arise naturally because of the Yankees' franchise context and because Judge has now passed his career contemporary on multiple franchise lists. These are not empty comparisons — they reflect genuine historical achievement within the most storied franchise in American professional sports.

Turning 34 on Saturday

Age adds context to everything Judge is doing. At 34, most hitters have already reached their statistical peak and are managing decline. Judge's production through the first three weeks of the 2026 season shows no evidence of decline — the power is present, the plate discipline remains, and the approach that made him an MVP-level player has not eroded.

For comparison, Babe Ruth hit 46 home runs at age 34. Mickey Mantle's production declined significantly after his early 30s. Judge, who has dealt with significant injuries in earlier seasons, appears to be aging differently — maintaining elite production into his mid-30s in a way that separates him from most historical comparisons.

What's Next

Judge's next milestone targets are relatively clear: 400 career home runs (23 away) and continuing to climb Yankees franchise lists for first-inning home runs and multi-homer games. Watch for whether his pace through the first month can be sustained, and pay attention to the 400 home run moment when it arrives — it will represent a significant career benchmark for one of the sport's most accomplished active power hitters.
