Friday MLB Roundup: Dodgers Edge Pirates, Orioles Survive, and the Weekend Storylines Set Up

By Jake Torres

June 12, 2026

A full slate of Friday night baseball produced several competitive games and standings movement on both sides of the diamond. Here's everything you need to know heading into the weekend — including the story that will dominate Saturday's baseball conversation.

Dodgers 5, Pirates 4

Los Angeles edged Pittsburgh 5-4, continuing to grind out wins in a season where the Dodgers have demonstrated repeatedly that they don't need blowout victories to stay in first place. Without Tyler Glasnow — who has been on the IL since early in the season — the Dodgers' pitching depth has been tested every week. Friday's one-run win keeps them in position entering the weekend.

Orioles 6, Mariners 5

Baltimore survived a close one against Seattle, holding on for a 6-5 win in what has been a characteristically competitive AL series. The Orioles continue to navigate the AL East race, staying relevant as teams above them try to extend their leads heading into the weekend.

Padres 5, Reds 3

San Diego defeated Cincinnati 5-3, an important NL West win for a Padres team that has been building consistency through June. The Reds, who made noise earlier in the season with a historically strong record in close games, fall in a Friday night contest that tightens the NL landscape.

Nationals 6, Giants 3

Washington took down San Francisco 6-3 in an interleague matchup. The Nationals, rebuilding for the future, took advantage of a Giants team that has had difficulty sustaining offensive consistency through the first two months.

What's Next

The headlining story of the weekend begins Saturday afternoon: Tarik Skubal makes his return to the Detroit Tigers' rotation in Cleveland, 38 days after elbow surgery. It's the most anticipated individual start of the 2026 season, and it sets the stage for a weekend of baseball that could reshape the AL Central standings. Friday's results were the appetizer. Saturday is the main event.
