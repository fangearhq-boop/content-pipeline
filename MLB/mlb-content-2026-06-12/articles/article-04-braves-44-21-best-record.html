From 76-86 to 44-21: The Atlanta Braves Are Baseball's Best Team and Nobody Talks About It

By Marcus Cole

June 12, 2026

The Atlanta Braves have the best record in Major League Baseball at 44-21. They lead the NL East by a margin that has effectively closed the division race before June's halfway point. Their pitching staff is elite. Their offense is back. And yet the national conversation about the Braves is almost nowhere to be found.

Part of that invisibility is the nature of a team doing exactly what they're supposed to do. Great records attract attention only when they're surprising. The Braves surprise you when you remember where they were a year ago.

The Context: From 76-86 to 44-21

Last season, the Atlanta Braves finished 76-86. Eighty-six losses. For a franchise that had won division titles for five consecutive years and made deep postseason runs, it was a jarring fall. Key players were injured. The rotation was inconsistent. The chemistry that had defined the championship-caliber Braves felt broken.

Walt Weiss took over as manager and began his first full season with the club. Chris Sale, whose health had been the subject of constant anxiety, stepped into a rotation anchor role and has delivered. Ronald Acuña returned to the lineup. The pieces assembled during the rebuild clicked together almost immediately.

At a 44-21 pace, the Braves are on pace for 109 wins. Last year's team won 76. The single-season swing of 33-plus wins is one of the most dramatic turnarounds in recent baseball memory.

The Rotation Is the Foundation

Chris Sale has been everything the Braves needed him to be. The left-hander, who has spent years fighting injury, is finally pitching a full season — and the results have been outstanding. His presence at the front of the rotation provides stability that cascades through every other roster decision.

Atlanta's run differential reflects a team that doesn't just win — it wins decisively. The plus-100 run differential from earlier this season positioned the Braves as not just the best record team, but the most dominant team in terms of quality of wins.

The NL East Is Not a Race

With a lead that has consistently grown through May and early June, the NL East division title is functionally over. The Braves' lead has reached double digits at points this season, and nobody in the division has shown the consistency to mount a challenge.

The real question for Atlanta is not whether they win the NL East — it's whether they can build toward October with the depth and health required to advance deep in the playoffs. The regular season record is a foundation; the postseason tests will determine the legacy of this remarkable turnaround.

What's Next

The Braves continue through June and into the trade deadline with the best record in baseball and a pitching staff built to hold it. For Weiss, Sale, Acuña, and the rest of this roster, the regular season is proceeding exactly as planned. The test will come in October — when the 76-86 nightmare is either completely erased or becomes a permanent asterisk on a season that deserved better. Everything points toward the former.
