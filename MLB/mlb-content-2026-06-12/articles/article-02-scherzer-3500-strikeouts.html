Max Scherzer Is the 11th Pitcher to Reach 3,500 Strikeouts — And He May Be the Last

By Jake Torres

June 12, 2026

Wednesday night at Rogers Centre in Toronto, Blue Jays pitcher Max Scherzer wound up and threw a changeup that froze Philadelphia Phillies slugger Kyle Schwarber for a called third strike. It was, by most measures, a routine out in a baseball game. But the number attached to it made it anything but routine.

It was Scherzer's 3,500th career strikeout — making him just the 11th pitcher in Major League Baseball history to reach the milestone. Sports Illustrated's projection is even more sobering: he may be the last.

The Club He Joined

The list of pitchers with 3,500 career strikeouts is as follows: Nolan Ryan (5,714), Randy Johnson (4,875), Roger Clemens (4,672), Steve Carlton (4,136), Bert Blyleven (3,701), Tom Seaver (3,640), Don Sutton (3,574), Justin Verlander (3,554), Gaylord Perry (3,534), Walter Johnson (3,509), and now Max Scherzer.

Look at those names. Every single one is in the Baseball Hall of Fame — with the exception of Scherzer, who has not yet been eligible. Look at the workload behind those numbers. Nolan Ryan threw 5,386 innings. Randy Johnson threw 4,135 innings. These were men who pitched like workhorses during an era when 250-inning seasons were normal and starting pitchers finished what they started.

Max Scherzer has thrived in a different era. At 42, he's still pitching effectively for the Blue Jays. His continued production is a testament to preparation, mechanical repeatability, and the kind of competitive intensity that doesn't diminish with age.

Why He May Be the Last

Getting to 3,500 strikeouts requires roughly 3,500 quality innings of starting pitcher work — typically the product of a 17 to 20-year career during which a pitcher throws 200-plus innings per season with regularity. The modern game has moved decisively away from that model.

Today's starters average fewer innings per start, face strict pitch count limits, and are pulled earlier regardless of performance. A pitcher who maxes out at 180 innings per season would need nearly 20 years to accumulate what Scherzer has done. The development pipeline, health management protocols, and organizational philosophies that govern pitcher usage today make the 3,500-strikeout milestone increasingly unreachable.

Nobody currently active is close. The window for the next pitcher to realistically challenge 3,500 career strikeouts is likely decades away — if it ever opens at all.

The Poetic Details

That the milestone came against Kyle Schwarber — the current MLB home run leader — added a layer of symmetry. The best hitter of this particular week in baseball, frozen on a changeup by a 42-year-old who has been doing this since 2008. The crowd at Rogers Centre gave Scherzer an ovation. Even Scherzer, a pitcher who rarely acknowledges anything except the next batter, paused to take it in.

What's Next

Scherzer continues pitching for the Blue Jays, presumably adding to his strikeout total with every start. His Hall of Fame candidacy, when he becomes eligible, will be one of the most straightforward cases in recent memory. And for now, the record books have a new entry in a list that has not grown since Justin Verlander made it in 2024 — and may not grow again for a very long time.
