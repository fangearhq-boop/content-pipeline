Tarik Skubal Starts Saturday: The 38-Day Wait Ends in Cleveland

By Marcus Cole

June 12, 2026

The wait is almost over. Tarik Skubal, the two-time American League Cy Young Award winner, will return to the mound tomorrow — Saturday, June 13 — for the Detroit Tigers against the Cleveland Guardians. It will be his first major league start in 38 days, since undergoing arthroscopic surgery to remove loose bodies from his left elbow in early May.

The Tigers announced Wednesday that Skubal would be activated from the injured list, with Casey Mize expected to follow from the IL on Sunday. Detroit's rotation, which has been pieced together with duct tape and prayers since early May, could be whole again by Monday morning.

The Rehab Report Was Spotless

Any concerns about the elbow holding up were quieted in a June 7 rehab appearance with Detroit's High-A affiliate in West Michigan. Skubal worked five innings, surrendering two hits and zero runs, with six strikeouts and no walks on 54 pitches, 44 of which were strikes. Sixteen swinging strikes.

That's not a pitcher easing back into action. That's a pitcher who's ready.

Manager A.J. Hinch confirmed the activation on Wednesday, and the matchup is set: Skubal versus Cleveland, the division rival that the Tigers trail by eight games in the AL Central. His first start back comes with immediate stakes.

What the Tigers Have Done Without Him

The numbers from the Skubal-less era were difficult to look at. The Tigers went 6-21 during his absence — a stretch that erased any realistic division-title aspirations and turned the trade deadline conversation from "will Detroit compete?" to "what will Detroit sell?"

But June has been different. The Tigers have won seven of their first nine games this month, somehow finding wins without their ace even as the rotation struggled. When Skubal rejoins that June momentum tomorrow, the calculus changes entirely.

The Trade Deadline Subplot

The trade deadline is August 3, and every contending team in baseball has had a standing alert on Skubal's return date since his surgery was announced. A healthy, dominant two-time Cy Young winner entering free agency — the Tigers hold the most valuable asset in the sport right now.

If Skubal starts Saturday and looks like himself — 95-plus velocity, the slider working, double-digit strikeout potential — every contending front office will be on the phone with Detroit before the series ends. If he struggles, the deadline conversation looks very different.

Tomorrow's start is, in the most literal sense, the most watched individual outing in baseball right now.

What's Next

Skubal faces the Guardians on Saturday afternoon in Cleveland. Casey Mize is expected to start Sunday's series finale, giving the Tigers two major rotation reinforcements in back-to-back days. The AL Central standings will look different on Sunday night. And regardless of outcome, the August 3 trade deadline watch on Tarik Skubal officially begins with first pitch tomorrow.
