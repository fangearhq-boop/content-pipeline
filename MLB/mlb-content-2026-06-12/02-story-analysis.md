### STORY 2: Max Scherzer 3,500 Strikeouts

**Primary Angle:** The 11th pitcher in MLB history. Possibly the last one ever. At 42 years old, Scherzer has outlasted almost every era of the sport and produced enough strikeouts to stand in the same company as Ryan, Clemens, and Walter Johnson. The fact that he struck out Schwarber — the current MLB HR leader — to get there is perfect storytelling.

**Headlines:**
- "Scherzer Joins the 3,500-K Club — And He May Be the Last to Do It"
- "11th Pitcher Ever: Max Scherzer Reaches 3,500 Strikeouts for Blue Jays"
- "The Last of His Kind: Scherzer's 3,500th K and the Club That May Never Grow Again"

**Key Hook:** SI's projection that Scherzer might be the last pitcher to ever reach 3,500 strikeouts is the long-form hook. The modern bullpen era, load management, and shortened starts have made 200+ innings a season increasingly rare for starting pitchers. The days of 300-K seasons are over. Scherzer's career is a bridge to another era.

**Audience Appeal:** Baseball history fans; Blue Jays followers; MLB legacy/record trackers; fans who appreciate generational greatness.

### STORY 3: Schwarber HR Race

**Primary Angle:** 23 home runs. One of the five Cy Young Award winners who's ever hit in the same sentence as the 60-HR club. The Home Run Derby happens in Philadelphia. His home park. If he's still near this pace in July, that Derby will be one of the best-attended events in the history of the All-Star break.

**Headlines:**
- "Schwarber Leads MLB with 23 HRs — And the 60-HR Conversation Has Already Started"
- "23 and Counting: Schwarber vs. Alvarez, the Home Run Race Nobody Expected to Be This Good"
- "Home Run Derby in Philadelphia, Schwarber at 23 — The Universe Is Setting This Up Perfectly"

**Key Hook:** The contrast with the rest of the HR landscape. Judge is on IL. Murakami was on IL. Ohtani has been inconsistent. Schwarber has been the most consistent power producer in baseball for months. The Phillies HR race has found its unexpected star.

**Audience Appeal:** Phillies fans; fantasy baseball; HR-chasing fans; historical record trackers; casual fans who love the Home Run Derby.

### STORY 4: Braves 44-21

**Primary Angle:** The most underappreciated story in baseball. This team went 76-86 last year — and now has the best record in the sport. Walt Weiss's first full season managing. The rebuild-to-champion pipeline hasn't been this fast in recent memory.

**Headlines:**
- "44-21 and Running: The Braves Are the Best Team in Baseball and Nobody Is Talking About It"
- "From 76-86 to Best Record in MLB: The Atlanta Braves' Remarkable 2026 Flip"
- "Braves 44-21: Chris Sale, Ronald Acuña, and a Team the Rest of the NL Should Fear"

**Key Hook:** 76-86 to 44-21 in one season. The numbers don't lie — this is one of the most dramatic single-season reversals in recent baseball history. For a franchise that spent years at the top before a painful rebuild year, this is validation for the roster construction choices made in the offseason.

**Audience Appeal:** Braves fans; national baseball audience tracking best records; NL East followers; fans interested in team-building stories.
