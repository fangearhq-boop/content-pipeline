## STORY 2: Max Scherzer 3,500 Strikeouts

#### FB Long-Form — 10:30 AM ET

On June 10 at Rogers Centre in Toronto, Max Scherzer struck out Philadelphia Phillies slugger Kyle Schwarber with a changeup.

It was the 3,500th strikeout of his career.

Scherzer became just the 11th pitcher in Major League Baseball history to reach that number, joining a list that includes Nolan Ryan (5,714), Randy Johnson (4,875), Roger Clemens (4,672), Steve Carlton (4,136), Bert Blyleven (3,701), Tom Seaver (3,640), Don Sutton (3,574), Justin Verlander (3,554), Gaylord Perry (3,534), and Walter Johnson (3,509).

For context: Sports Illustrated projected that Scherzer may be the last pitcher to ever achieve this milestone. The modern bullpen era has eliminated the kind of workload that allows a pitcher to accumulate 3,500 strikeouts. Nobody currently in the game is close. It's the kind of record that may simply stop growing at 11 names.

At 42 years old, still pitching for the Blue Jays, Scherzer has outlasted every era since his Washington days. His 3,500th strikeout came against the current MLB home run leader. The two legends of their eras met for one called third strike — and history was made.
