Aaron Judge Homers in the Yankees Home Opener — The Career HR Chase Is On in the Bronx

By Ryan Calloway | April 7, 2026

Judge hit his third home run of 2026 in an 8-2 win over the Marlins at Yankee Stadium. He entered the season 32 HRs from 400 in his career. The Yankees lead the AL East.

Aaron Judge hit a home run in the Yankees' home opener. That sentence has become so reliable it almost feels like it should be built into the schedule — Yankee Stadium, April, Judge at the plate, ball leaving the yard.

On April 5, in front of a packed Yankee Stadium, Judge hit his third home run of the 2026 season — a two-run blast off Marlins pitcher Eury Pérez that traveled 387 feet at 101.2 miles per hour exit velocity. The Yankees won 8-2 in a game that was never particularly close. Judge went 2-for-4 with the home run, a walk, a hit-by-pitch, a stolen base, and three RBIs. It was a complete performance from a player who has made complete performances look routine.

The Numbers Behind the Chase

Judge entered the 2026 season with 368 career home runs. Three games in, after Monday's game, he stands at 371. He needs 29 more to reach 400 career home runs — a milestone he could reach before the end of the 2026 season if he stays healthy and productive at even a modest version of his recent pace.

Last season, Judge hit 53 home runs on his way to a third consecutive AL MVP Award. If he approaches that kind of production in 2026, he would not just reach 400 — he would blow past it and put numbers on the board that begin to reshape how we think about his career arc and eventual Hall of Fame case.

Through seven games, his advanced metrics are in line with what you would expect: a 26.1 percent barrel rate, strong hard-hit rate numbers, and an approach at the plate that generates both walks and damage on contact. The home run pace is sustainable at his current level.

The Yankees' Bigger Picture

The home opener win moved New York to roughly 7-2 on the early season, placing them at the top of the AL East standings. The Yankees built this roster to win now — with Judge at the center, surrounded by depth that can carry the team through the inevitable rough stretches of a 162-game season.

Max Fried, who signed with New York this offseason, has been contributing from the rotation. The bullpen has been functional. The lineup, built around Judge, has scored runs consistently in the early going. Everything the Yankees preseason projections suggested would happen has, so far, happened.

What's Next

Judge and the Yankees will continue their AL East schedule through April, building toward the midpoint of the season where the career HR chase will get meaningful national attention. With 371 career home runs as of Monday and a season that has barely begun, Judge is tracking toward one of the more compelling individual milestones in the sport. The question is not whether he reaches 400 — it is when, and who is watching when he does. If the Yankees remain in contention and Judge stays healthy, that moment should come before September. Yankee Stadium will be ready for it.
