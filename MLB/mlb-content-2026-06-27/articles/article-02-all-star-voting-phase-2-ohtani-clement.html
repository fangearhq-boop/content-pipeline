All-Star Voting Phase 2 Opens Sunday: Ohtani and Clement Are In, but the Race Isn't Over

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 27, 2026

Two players have already been decided. For everyone else, Sunday is where the real race begins.

Phase 2 of All-Star voting opens June 29 and runs through July 2 at noon ET. Shohei Ohtani, who received 3,341,257 Phase 1 votes to lead all of baseball in either league, earns an automatic starting assignment at designated hitter in the National League. Ernie Clement secured the American League's automatic starting assignment with 3,232,932 Phase 1 votes.

For everyone else at every other position, Phase 2 resets the counters and runs a new race among the top two finalists at each spot.

How Phase 2 Works

The Phase 2 format is specifically designed to tighten races that Phase 1 may have settled by large margins. Vote totals from Phase 1 reset to zero when Phase 2 opens. The top two vote-getters at each position — and the top six outfielders — advance as finalists. Fans can vote once per day through July 2 at noon ET.

The players who dominated Phase 1 with wide margins will likely carry their momentum into Phase 2. But a player who entered Phase 2 with a narrow Phase 1 lead faces a legitimate challenge from a motivated fanbase behind the second-place finalist. The format was designed to give Phase 2 stakes that Phase 1 did not always produce.

Starters will be announced in the days following the July 2 voting deadline. The All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia.

The Derby Night Before

The Home Run Derby is July 13 at Citizens Bank Park — the night before the All-Star Game. Kyle Schwarber, who leads all of baseball with 29 home runs, has not committed to participating in the Derby at his home park. The Derby field typically finishes taking shape in the week before the event.

The combination of the Derby and the All-Star Game in one of the louder baseball environments in the sport makes All-Star week 2026 in Philadelphia a genuinely anticipated event. Citizens Bank Park does not do quiet, and a sellout crowd for both nights is a certainty.

What's Next

Phase 2 voting runs through July 2 at noon ET. Fans can vote daily at MLB.com. Starters will be announced shortly after the voting deadline. The Derby field and rosters will take shape through the week following. For the players on the Phase 2 ballot who are not locked in as automatic starters, the next five days are the last real opportunity to make their case directly through fan voting before the manager selections and player selections round out the roster.
