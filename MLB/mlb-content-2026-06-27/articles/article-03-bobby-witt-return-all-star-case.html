Bobby Witt Jr. Is Back — and He Has a Lot of Baseball Left to Play

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | June 27, 2026

Bobby Witt Jr. is expected to return to the Royals' lineup after a Grade 1 MCL sprain kept him out of six games. Manager Matt Quatraro called the recovery "very encouraging" following a full pregame workout that included base running, defensive drills, and batting cage work.

The injury happened on June 18. The Royals avoided placing Witt on the injured list, which was both a roster decision and a statement about how they assessed the severity of what they were dealing with. Grade 1 is the least severe MCL classification — the ligament is stretched, not torn — and the six-day absence is consistent with what a Grade 1 sprain typically requires.

The more interesting question is what his return means, both for the Royals and for the conversation about the best players in the American League.

The fWAR Number

Bobby Witt Jr. was leading the American League in fWAR at 4.5 entering June. That number accounts for offense, defense, and baserunning relative to position, and at 4.5 through mid-June, Witt was on pace for one of the elite individual seasons in the AL in recent years.

The six-game absence did not fundamentally change the season. But it created a gap in the argument. A player who misses games — even six of them, even for a ligament strain — hands a talking point to the voters who prefer players who accumulate counting statistics continuously. Witt's return allows him to rebuild that argument over the remaining regular season and, specifically, over the 17 days between now and the July 14 All-Star Game.

The Royals' Second-Half Picture

Kansas City's position heading into the second half is shaped significantly by whether Witt can sustain his first-half production. He is not just the best player on this team — he is the player who makes the Royals capable of outcomes that their raw roster might not otherwise support.

A Grade 1 MCL typically carries no elevated reinjury risk once the player is cleared to play. Witt has completed the checklist. The Royals are not managing him through a condition that is likely to recur. He is back, and the expectation is that he picks up where he left off.

What's Next

Witt's return to the lineup gives the Royals a functional starting shortstop and a top-five AL position player back in their lineup card. The All-Star Game is July 14 in Philadelphia. Voting for Phase 2 opens June 29. Witt needs to stay healthy and produce — neither of which should be complicated, given both the nature of the injury and the player involved — and the argument for his All-Star Game spot becomes self-evident. He was the best player in the league before the injury. There is no reason to believe that changes once he is back in the lineup.
