37 Days to the Deadline: Skubal's 85% Trade Probability and What Comes Next

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 27, 2026

August 3 is 37 days away. The trade deadline market is already visible.

The centerpiece is Tarik Skubal. Two Cy Young Awards. A fastball that has returned to 99.9 mph after coming back from midseason bone chip surgery. A Tigers organization that sits in last place with no realistic path to contention and a pitcher who has made clear he will not sign an extension in Detroit. The math points in one direction, and every front office in the sport has done it.

An 85 percent trade probability estimate has circulated around Skubal from credible analytical outlets. That number reflects both the Tigers' situation and the market's appetite — when a two-time Cy Young winner becomes available at the deadline, the bidding starts expensive and escalates quickly.

The Suitors

Four teams have emerged as the early frontrunners in the Skubal market: the Dodgers, the Padres, the Blue Jays, and the Yankees. Each brings a specific case.

The Dodgers have the depth and the payroll flexibility to include multiple prospects in a package without depleting the system. They also have the urgency of a team built to win now with a roster that requires the kind of front-line starting pitching that Skubal provides. The Padres, meanwhile, are a team capable of trading from organizational depth for a top starter at the right price.

The Blue Jays and the Yankees add AL competition to what might otherwise become a National League bidding war. Any team acquiring Skubal also acquires him for the remainder of the season, through the postseason, and through free agency. The rental value of a two-time Cy Young winner on a team competing in October is substantial — and the price the Tigers expect in return reflects that.

The Chapman Variable

Aroldis Chapman adds a separate market dynamic. He has converted 28 consecutive saves — one shy of his career record — since returning to elite form in Boston. A closer of that caliber becomes the most attractive bullpen addition available if the Red Sox decide they are selling rather than buying.

Chapman carries a significant contract, which complicates the acquisition calculus. But a proven, high-leverage closer at the trade deadline is exactly the piece that contenders lack and that October baseball requires. His availability depends on the Red Sox's second-half decision, which is still being made.

What's Next

The next 37 days will clarify which teams are genuine buyers and which are sellers waiting for the right offer. By mid-July, teams within five games of a wild card spot will have enough information about their trajectories to make firm decisions. Skubal's market will likely move quickly once that clarity arrives — deals of this magnitude rarely drag to the literal deadline. For the Tigers, the calculation is straightforward: maximize the return on a pitcher who is leaving after the season, and rebuild with whatever package comes back. For the contenders, the window to acquire the pitching staff piece that makes the difference in October runs 37 days. The clock is running.
