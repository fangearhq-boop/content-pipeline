### Long-Form Post August 3 is 37 days away. The trade deadline market is already forming. Tarik Skubal is the centerpiece. Two Cy Young Awards. A fastball that is back at 99.9 mph after returning from midseason bone chip surgery. A Tigers team sitting in last place and carrying no realistic path to a second-half collapse of the deficit between them and contention. Skubal will not extend his contract with Detroit. He is a rental, and every team that knows what good pitching costs at the deadline is already doing the math. An estimated 85 percent trade probability is circulating around Skubal from credible outlets. Four teams have emerged as the early suitors: the Dodgers, the Padres, the Blue Jays, and the Yankees. If that list narrows to a two-team race, the bidding for one good pitching package — a top prospect plus multiple pieces — will get expensive quickly. If the Red Sox decide to sell, Chapman becomes the best closer available, with the durability and the contract that makes him worth pursuing. Thirty-seven days is a long time in baseball. Teams that are buyers in late June sometimes become sellers by mid-July.
