### STORY 2: All-Star Voting Phase 2 — Two Days Away, Ohtani and Clement Locked In

**Long-Form Post**
Phase 1 of All-Star voting ended on June 25. Phase 2 opens Sunday, June 29.

Two automatic starters are already decided. Shohei Ohtani received 3,341,257 Phase 1 votes — the most of any player in baseball, NL or AL — and earns an automatic DH starting assignment for the National League. Ernie Clement received 3,232,932 Phase 1 votes in the American League, locking in his automatic start.

Phase 2 changes the format. The top two vote-getters at each position advance to Phase 2, and the counters reset to zero. The players who dominated Phase 1 by wide margins will likely extend their leads, but the format gives challengers a legitimate path to overturn a close Phase 1 race.

Voting runs June 29 through July 2 at noon ET. Starters are announced shortly after. The All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia — the same park hosting the Home Run Derby the night before.

The 2026 All-Star week in Philadelphia is already shaping up as one of the louder settings in recent years. A building that makes noise for every Phillies home game does not suddenly go quiet for an exhibition.

### STORY 3: Bobby Witt Jr. — Back in the Lineup, Eyes on the All-Star Game

**Long-Form Post**
Bobby Witt Jr. is expected to return to the Royals' lineup after missing six games with a Grade 1 MCL sprain.

The injury happened on June 18, when Witt slid awkwardly while fielding and felt immediate discomfort. A Grade 1 sprain is the least severe classification — ligament stretched but not torn. The Royals avoided placing him on the injured list, which is itself a signal of how confident they were in a quick recovery.

Manager Matt Quatraro described Witt's preparation as "very encouraging." He ran the bases, completed defensive drills, and hit in the batting cage — the checklist a player completes before being cleared to play.

What makes Witt's return significant beyond the box score is where his season stands. He was leading the American League in fWAR at 4.5 before the injury. That number is not just a statistic. It is the clearest single-number argument for why Witt should be starting at shortstop at Citizens Bank Park on July 14.

The All-Star Game is 17 days away. Witt has time to make the final case that his first half — before and after the six-game absence — is the best in the AL.

### STORY 4: Dansby Swanson's 15-RBI Series — The Stat That Puts His Season in Context

**Long-Form Post**
Dansby Swanson drove in 15 runs in a four-game series against the Mets.

That is not a number that happens very often. A four-game series is a finite opportunity — maybe 20 plate appearances, maybe fewer. Accumulating 15 RBIs in that window requires the right combination of timing, lineup support, and situational hitting. Swanson produced all three against the Mets.

The production puts his 2026 season in a different frame. Swanson has been a solid, reliable contributor for Atlanta throughout his career. But a 15-RBI series is not solid and reliable. It is dominant.

The Braves are 48-31, the best record in the NL East, and they are doing it without Acuña — who missed significant time earlier in the season. The team's ability to find production from players like Swanson and Ozzie Albies in the middle of the lineup is what makes them a credible postseason threat rather than a wild card afterthought.

Swanson's name rarely shows up in the MVP conversation. After a four-game, 15-RBI performance against the division's top competition, it should.

### STORY 5: 37 Days to the Trade Deadline — Skubal and the Market Taking Shape

**Long-Form Post**
August 3 is 37 days away. The trade deadline market is already forming.

Tarik Skubal is the centerpiece. Two Cy Young Awards. A fastball that is back at 99.9 mph after returning from midseason bone chip surgery. A Tigers team sitting in last place and carrying no realistic path to a second-half collapse of the deficit between them and contention. Skubal will not extend his contract with Detroit. He is a rental, and every team that knows what good pitching costs at the deadline is already doing the math.

An estimated 85 percent trade probability is circulating around Skubal from credible outlets. Four teams have emerged as the early suitors: the Dodgers, the Padres, the Blue Jays, and the Yankees. If that list narrows to a two-team race, the bidding for one good pitching package — a top prospect plus multiple pieces — will get expensive quickly.

Aroldis Chapman adds a different dynamic to the market. He has converted 28 consecutive saves — one shy of his career record — since rediscovering his elite form in Boston. If the Red Sox decide to sell, Chapman becomes the best closer available, with the durability and the contract that makes him worth pursuing.

Thirty-seven days is a long time in baseball. Teams that are buyers in late June sometimes become sellers by mid-July. But the Skubal market is going to be one of the louder storylines between now and August 3.
