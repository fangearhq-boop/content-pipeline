### STORY 1: Witt Jr. Grade 1 MCL Sprain — 'Best Case Scenario'

**Long-Form Post**
Royals fans got the news they were hoping for on Saturday morning.

Bobby Witt Jr. has a Grade 1 MCL sprain in his right knee — and manager Matt Quatraro called it exactly what it is: "best case scenario."

Witt exited Thursday's 14-6 win over the Cardinals in the fourth inning with right knee discomfort. He had already hit a home run before leaving. An MRI Saturday morning showed the sprain, and medical guidance puts Grade 1 MCL sprains in the 1-3 week recovery window. The knee can typically heal on its own without surgery.

The Royals aren't rushing. They plan to wait until Sunday to evaluate how Witt feels before deciding whether to place him on the injured list or keep him day-to-day. The All-Star Game is July 14 — right in that 1-3 week window from Sunday's decision point.

The news isn't all-clear, but it's as close to it as anyone could realistically hope for after watching him exit early. Grade 1 is the mildest MCL classification. The concern is real, but so is the optimism. ⚾

### STORY 2: Ranger Suarez Takes No-Hitter Into 7th

**Long-Form Post**
Friday night in Seattle, Ranger Suarez was three outs from history.

The Boston Red Sox lefthander carried a no-hitter into the seventh inning at T-Mobile Park before Josh Naylor hit a one-out double to break up the bid. Suarez had retired the first 10 batters he faced, walked Josh Naylor's teammate in the fourth, and otherwise dominated a Mariners lineup that couldn't touch him for six-plus innings.

His final line: 6⅔ innings, one hit, five strikeouts, three walks. The Red Sox won 6-2.

It was Suarez's first win since April 27. The 30-year-old lefty has had a difficult stretch, but Friday night looked like a different pitcher — aggressive, commanding, keeping Seattle off-balance from the first batter to the last.

The no-hitter didn't happen. But what Suarez did Friday night was still one of the best single-game pitching performances of 2026. And sometimes that's enough. 🔥

### STORY 3: Schwarber's Historic HR Pace

**Long-Form Post**
Let's stop and appreciate what Kyle Schwarber is doing in 2026.

Twenty-five home runs. The first player in baseball to get there this season. And when you do the math on a 162-game pace, the number that comes out the other end lands in historically rare territory — approximately 65+ home runs for the full year.

That would place Schwarber's 2026 season third on the all-time single-season home run list, behind only Barry Bonds (73, 2001) and Mark McGwire (70, 1998).

It's June. There's half a season left. Schwarber has done this before — he's always been a home run hitter — but this version of him is operating at a different level.

And here's the thing that makes the next month appointment television: the Home Run Derby is July 13, and it's at Citizens Bank Park. Schwarber's home. His fans. His field. The MLB HR leader getting to swing in front of his own crowd at his own ballpark.

Yordan Alvarez is chasing. The race is real. And the Derby is in 23 days. 🔥

### STORY 4: All-Star Ballot — Ohtani Leads, 5 Days Left

**Long-Form Post**
Five days left in Phase 1 of MLB All-Star voting — and the leaderboard looks like you'd expect, with one name towering above everyone else.

Shohei Ohtani leads all of baseball with 1,165,133 votes. Not just the DH category. Not just the National League. All of baseball.

Yordan Alvarez tops the American League at 1,015,768 votes. Aaron Judge sits at 977,460. There are real races at multiple positions — Ben Rice has quietly built a strong case for the AL first base spot with Aaron Judge sidelined. In the NL, Freddie Freeman leads with 870,606 votes at first base over Matt Olson's 802,848.

Phase 1 voting closes June 25 at noon ET. The top two vote-getters at each position and the top six outfielders advance to Phase 2, which opens June 29. The All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia.

With the Derby also at Citizens Bank on July 13, Philly fans have a week of baseball history coming their way. Get your votes in. 🗳️
