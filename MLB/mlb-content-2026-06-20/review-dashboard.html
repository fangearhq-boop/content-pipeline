### Long-Form Post Royals fans got the news they were hoping for on Saturday morning. Bobby Witt Jr. has a Grade 1 MCL sprain in his right knee — and manager Matt Quatraro called it exactly what it is: "best case scenario." Witt exited Thursday's 14-6 win over the Cardinals in the fourth inning with right knee discomfort. He had already hit a home run before leaving. An MRI Saturday morning showed the sprain, and medical guidance puts Grade 1 MCL sprains in the 1-3 week recovery window. The knee can typically heal on its own without surgery. The Royals aren't rushing. They plan to wait until Sunday to evaluate how Witt feels before deciding whether to place him on the injured list or keep him day-to-day. The All-Star Game is July 14 — right in that 1-3 week window from Sunday's decision point. The news isn't all-clear, but it's as close to it as anyone could realistically hope for after watching him exit early. Grade 1 is the mildest MCL classification.
