Ohtani Leads All-Star Voting at 1.165M, Phase 1 Closes in Five Days

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 20, 2026

Five days remain in Phase 1 of All-Star voting, and the leaders are pulling away.

Shohei Ohtani of the Los Angeles Dodgers leads all of baseball with 1,165,133 votes — a total that reflects his status as the sport's most popular player and, arguably, one of the most popular athletes in the world. Phase 1 closes June 25 at noon ET. The top two vote-getters at each position in each league and the top six outfielders advance to Phase 2, which opens June 29. The All-Star Game itself is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, the same venue hosting the Home Run Derby on July 13.

Ohtani is headed to Philadelphia. The only question is whether anyone makes it close in the final five days.

The Leaders

Behind Ohtani, the American League is shaped by two of its most recognizable sluggers. Yordan Alvarez leads the AL with 1,015,768 votes, the best total in the league and a figure that reflects his consistent production and Houston's substantial fan base. Aaron Judge sits at 977,460 votes — still drawing enormous support from the most engaged fanbase in baseball despite the Yankees' variable 2026 season.

The gap between Ohtani (1,165,133) and Alvarez (1,015,768) is a reminder of how thoroughly Ohtani occupies a different tier of public attention. He leads the second-place vote-getter in all of baseball by roughly 150,000 votes.

The First Base Races

The most competitive position battles in Phase 1 are at first base in both leagues, where the margins are real and a final-week surge could change the outcome.

In the AL, Vladimir Guerrero Jr. leads with 603,014 votes, with Ben Rice at 509,830 and Murakami at 437,107. Guerrero's lead is meaningful but not insurmountable — Rice and Murakami both have engaged fanbases that could close ground with five days of organized voting.

The NL first base race looks different: Freddie Freeman leads with 870,606 votes, Matt Olson sits at 802,848, and Bryce Harper is at 651,792. Freeman and Olson are separated by fewer than 70,000 votes — close enough that the outcome is not decided, though Freeman's Dodgers fan base is among the most active in the sport. Harper's 651,792 votes keep him in the Phase 2 conversation regardless of where he finishes in Phase 1.

The Philadelphia Angle

With the All-Star Game and the Home Run Derby both landing at Citizens Bank Park, the host city's players carry an added dimension in the voting. Kyle Schwarber, who leads MLB with 25 home runs and is the central figure in the NL Home Run Derby conversation, has become as much a part of the All-Star week story as the voting itself.

Philadelphia fans voting in the final five days of Phase 1 are not just voting for their players — they are voting for the players who will represent the host city in one of baseball's biggest weeks. That local identity adds energy to the final stretch of the ballot.

How the Process Works

After Phase 1 closes June 25, the top two finishers at each position and the top six outfielders in each league advance to Phase 2. Phase 2 opens June 29, and a second round of fan voting determines the remaining starters. Pitchers and additional roster spots are selected by players and coaches.

Fans can vote up to five times per 24-hour period at MLB.com, through team websites, or on the MLB app. Five days is enough time for an organized push to move the needle at any contested position — the AL first base race in particular has enough separation to be interesting without being settled.

What's Next

Phase 1 voting closes June 25 at noon ET. Barring a significant late surge, Ohtani, Alvarez, and Judge all appear to hold their positions. The battles to watch in the final five days are the AL first base race — where Guerrero, Rice, and Murakami are within range of each other — and the NL first base contest between Freeman and Olson.

Phase 2 opens June 29, bringing another round of fan voting before the finalists are announced. The All-Star Game is July 14 in Philadelphia. The roster will take shape over the next two weeks, but the foundation of who makes it starts with the five days remaining in Phase 1.

Vote accordingly.
