Witt Jr. Diagnosed With Grade 1 MCL Sprain — Royals Receive 'Best Case Scenario' News

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 20, 2026

Kansas City exhaled Saturday morning.

Bobby Witt Jr. underwent an MRI on his right knee, and the results came back as well as the Royals could have hoped. He's been diagnosed with a Grade 1 MCL sprain — the mildest classification on the ligament injury scale. Manager Matt Quatraro called it "best case scenario," and for once, that's not spin. A Grade 1 is exactly what it sounds like: the ligament is stretched, not torn.

The Cleveland Clinic puts Grade 1 MCL recovery at roughly one to three weeks. For a team staring at the All-Star Game on July 14 in Philadelphia, that window matters enormously.

How Witt Got Here

The injury happened Wednesday night in the fourth inning of Kansas City's 14-6 rout of the St. Louis Cardinals — a game that had already produced one of the more remarkable sequences in recent Royals history. Kansas City had just set a club record with five doubles in a single inning. Witt himself had gone deep before the knee flared up. It was, by every measure, a good night for the Royals — until No. 7 walked off the field.

What made the exit more concerning than the average knock is that this is the same right knee that sent Witt to the bench on June 7 against Minnesota. He missed two games that time, came back, and kept playing. Quatraro said after that incident there was nothing structurally wrong with the knee. The June 18 exit was, at minimum, a reminder that the joint needed a closer look.

The MRI delivered an answer. Grade 1 means the structural integrity of the MCL is intact. There's no tear. No surgery conversation. No season-ending scenario.

What Grade 1 Actually Means

MCL sprains are graded on a three-level scale. Grade 1 is a stretch — ligament fibers stressed but not significantly damaged. Grade 2 involves a partial tear and a longer recovery. Grade 3 is a complete rupture, often requiring surgery and months of rehabilitation.

Grade 1 sprains in professional athletes tend to resolve quickly with rest, ice, and controlled activity. The one-to-three-week timeline is standard guidance, though high-performance athletes and aggressive medical staffs have been known to bring players back on the shorter end of that range, especially when there's no structural damage to protect.

What it does mean in the immediate term: Witt won't be playing Saturday. The Royals have said they'll wait until Sunday before making any decision about an IL placement — a deliberate approach that gives them a full day of observation before committing to a roster move.

The All-Star Clock

The timing of this injury sets up one of the more intriguing storylines of the Kansas City summer. The All-Star Game is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia — exactly 24 days away. If the three-week end of the Grade 1 recovery window holds, Witt could theoretically be back and healthy in time to participate, assuming his midsummer candidacy survives the voting process while he's sidelined.

Phase 1 All-Star voting closes June 25. Witt's absence from the lineup during that window could affect his vote totals, but the case for his All-Star status is built on what he's done all season — not the next five days.

The Royals will need to navigate their lineup in the meantime. Kansas City has been one of the better teams in the American League in 2026, and while the offense showed Thursday night that it can produce without Witt (14 runs is 14 runs), the shortstop is the engine of this team's identity. Every day he's not at short, the lineup has a different shape.

What's Next

Sunday is the next key date. The Royals will evaluate Witt's knee and determine whether an IL stint is necessary or whether they can manage him day-to-day while the ligament settles down. An IL placement would require a 10-day minimum absence — longer than the expected recovery window — but teams frequently use the IL to guarantee rest rather than rush a player back prematurely.

The one-to-three-week recovery timeline puts Witt's potential return date somewhere between June 29 and July 11. Both of those dates land before the All-Star break. If the Royals are conservative and place him on the IL Sunday, he could still be back and playing meaningful baseball before Philadelphia.

Kansas City got the result it needed Saturday morning. "Best case scenario" is not a phrase that teams use often when their best player exits clutching a knee. This time, it's accurate.
