Thursday Night Roundup: Royals 14-6, Mariners 3-0, and the Night Witt Went Down

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 19, 2026

Thursday night in baseball was defined by two results and one injury. Here is what happened:

Royals 14, Cardinals 6 — Witt Exits With Knee

Kansas City produced one of its most impressive offensive outings of the season, scoring six runs in the second inning — setting a club record with five doubles in that single frame — on the way to a 14-6 win over St. Louis.

But the story of the night was not the club record or the blowout win. Bobby Witt Jr. hit a home run in the early innings, then left in the fourth with right knee discomfort after making a sliding stop on an infield ground ball. Manager Matt Quatraro confirmed an MRI is scheduled and results are pending.

It is the same right knee that sidelined Witt briefly on June 7 during a game against Minnesota. He returned after two days that time, and Quatraro said there was nothing structurally wrong. Whether Thursday's recurrence changes that assessment is the question hanging over Kansas City heading into Friday.

The Royals won by eight runs. It will not feel like enough until they know Witt is okay.

Mariners 3, Orioles 0 — Woo's Seven Shutout Innings

Bryan Woo pitched the kind of game Seattle needs from its rotation to remain competitive: seven innings, three hits, zero runs, five strikeouts. Colt Emerson knocked in two of Seattle's three runs in a first-inning burst that gave Woo all the cushion he needed.

The Mariners scored all three runs in the first and then let their pitching hold Baltimore scoreless the rest of the way. A clean, efficient victory — the type that gets noted in the standings long after the game story fades.

Seattle continues to operate as one of the AL's less-heralded but consistently performing teams. Woo's night is a good example of why.

Around the League

Thursday's full slate also included games between the Guardians and Brewers, and the Twins and Rangers — matchups that will shape the AL Central and wild card pictures heading into the weekend.

The Guardians and Brewers game featured a competitive back-and-forth, while the Twins and Rangers were active in games that carried division implications. Full results continued to come in through the evening.

What's Next

Friday brings a full schedule of games, with the Bobby Witt Jr. MRI results as the most anticipated non-game development of the morning. Beyond that, the standings races in each division continue to tighten as teams approach the halfway point of the season.

The Royals win. The Mariners win. And one of the best players in the American League is waiting on MRI results. That is where Thursday night left things.
