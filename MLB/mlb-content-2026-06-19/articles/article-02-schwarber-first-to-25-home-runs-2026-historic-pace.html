Schwarber First to 25 Home Runs in 2026 — Historic Pace Continues

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | June 19, 2026

Kyle Schwarber has done what no other player in baseball has done in 2026: he has hit 25 home runs.

The Philadelphia Phillies designated hitter became the first player in the major leagues to reach the 25-homer mark this season — a milestone that lands him in genuinely historic company when you trace the pace back to seasons that mattered most in the record books.

Through 315 plate appearances, Schwarber is batting .249 with 35 extra-base hits, 43 RBIs, 46 walks, and 44 runs scored. He is leading the National League in home runs and the conversation around a number that keeps getting louder: 60.

What 25 Means Historically

The only times a player has been to 25 home runs this early in a season and gone on to have a truly historic year, the results were the 1998 and 2001 seasons — Mark McGwire hit 70 in 1998, and Barry Bonds hit 73 in 2001. Both were at a comparable pace point when they reached 25.

Schwarber's current rate projects to approximately 66 home runs on the full season. That would be the third-highest single-season total in MLB history, behind only Bonds (73) and McGwire (70). Sammy Sosa hit 66 in 1998, so Schwarber's projection ties the third-highest total ever recorded.

Those comparisons carry significant asterisks from the steroid era. But the mathematical reality of where Schwarber is sitting right now is undeniable — his pace is historically elite, and the summer has not yet arrived.

The Competition

Schwarber is not running away from the home run race. Yordan Alvarez has been right behind him for much of the season, and the Astros slugger has shown he is capable of matching Schwarber homer for homer in stretches. The Schwarber-Alvarez race has been one of the best storylines in baseball in 2026, two elite power hitters pushing each other without being in the same league.

Their divergent league platforms mean they will never directly match up except during the All-Star Game — and the Home Run Derby, which brings the 25-homer angle to its most immediate consequence.

The Derby Factor

The Home Run Derby is set for July 13 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. Schwarber's home park.

It is not a stretch to say the Derby is setting up to be the most compelling version of the event in years. The player who has hit more home runs than anyone else in baseball in 2026 competing in front of a Phillies crowd, in the park where he has spent years launching balls into the stands — the theatrical conditions are ideal.

Schwarber has not formally committed to the Derby, but a player in the middle of a historic home run pace almost certainly participates when the event comes to his home stadium.

What's Next

Schwarber's next target is 30. No player has hit 30 home runs before the All-Star break in recent memory — achieving that would push the historical conversation further and cement this as one of the most watched individual statistical pursuits of the 2026 season.

He has three weeks between now and the All-Star break. At his current pace, 30 home runs before the break is entirely plausible.

Twenty-five is just the latest number. The next one will get here before the calendar has fully processed this one.
