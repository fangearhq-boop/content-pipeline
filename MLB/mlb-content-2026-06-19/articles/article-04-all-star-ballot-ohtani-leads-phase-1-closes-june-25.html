All-Star Ballot Update: Ohtani Leads All of Baseball, Phase 1 Closes June 25

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | June 19, 2026

Six days remain in Phase 1 of All-Star voting, and the picture is coming into focus.

Shohei Ohtani leads all of baseball with 1,165,133 votes, putting him on pace for an automatic starting spot in the 2026 MLB All-Star Game when Phase 1 closes June 25 at noon ET. The automatic starting spots go to the top vote-getter at each position in each league — if Ohtani holds his lead as the AL's designated hitter option, that slot is his without further deliberation.

The game itself is July 14 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, and the city is gearing up for one of baseball's biggest showcases in one of the sport's most passionate markets.

The Leaders

Ohtani's 1,165,133 votes lead all of baseball by a significant margin. He is the consensus face of the sport and a draw that transcends any single position or league — his two-way capability and personality have made him one of the most popular athletes in the world, and the All-Star voting reflects that.

Yordan Alvarez follows at 1,015,768 votes, leading the AL's designated hitter position. The Houston Astros slugger has been one of the AL's most dangerous bats all season, and his vote total reflects the combination of performance and market size that drives All-Star balloting.

Aaron Judge is at 977,460 votes, continuing to attract significant support even while managing injury. The Yankees' first baseman has been one of the sport's most compelling figures throughout his career, and the voting suggests his fanbase remains engaged regardless of his on-field availability.

The NL Picture

In the National League, Kyle Schwarber leads at the designated hitter position. His 25-home-run pace has been the most compelling individual offensive story in the NL this season, and the voting reflects his dominance and his profile in one of baseball's most historically vocal fan markets.

That Schwarber will be voting for himself — metaphorically, as Phillies fans pack Citizens Bank Park for All-Star week — adds another layer to what is shaping up to be a Philadelphia celebration of its most prominent player.

How the Game Works

The Phase 1 winner at each position automatically earns a starting spot. After Phase 1 closes June 25, a second phase of voting for remaining positions determines the rest of the starters. Players and coaches select the pitchers and additional position players for each roster.

The All-Star Game is July 14 in Philadelphia, airing live on FOX and streaming on FOX One. Fans can vote up to five times per 24-hour period at MLB.com, on team sites, or through the MLB app.

What's Next

Six days of voting remain before Phase 1 closes. Ohtani, Alvarez, and Judge all appear to hold comfortable enough leads that their positions are unlikely to change significantly, barring an unusual surge from competitors.

More interesting may be the players on the bubble — the positions where the top vote-getter and the second-place player are close, where organized fan campaigns in the final days can make the difference between a starting spot and a reserve selection.

The ballot closes June 25. Cast your votes.
