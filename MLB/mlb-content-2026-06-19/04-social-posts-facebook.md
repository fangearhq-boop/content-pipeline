### STORY 1: Bobby Witt Jr. Knee Injury

#### ### Facebook Long-Form — 8:00 AM ET

The Royals won Thursday night. It barely felt like it.

Bobby Witt Jr., Kansas City's All-Star shortstop and one of the best players in the American League, left Thursday's 14-6 win over the St. Louis Cardinals in the fourth inning with right knee discomfort. He had hit a home run before exiting — just as he did in a previous injury scare — but the concern that followed was impossible to set aside.

Witt's departure stemmed from a sliding stop on an infield ground ball. He fielded it cleanly, but landing awkwardly on his right knee left him unable to continue. Manager Matt Quatraro confirmed after the game that Witt would undergo further testing — an MRI pending — before the team had any further information.

What makes this particularly worrying is the history. It is the same right knee that sidelined Witt earlier this month, on June 7, during a game against the Minnesota Twins. He missed only two games at that point, and Quatraro said then that there was nothing structurally wrong. He returned and continued to play at his usual level.

But now the same knee is in the news again.

Witt is the engine of the Royals offense. He is an MVP-caliber talent who has turned Kansas City into a legitimate American League contender. The Royals were 14-6 winners Thursday night — but the only number the team was watching was the one on Witt's back.

The MRI results will determine everything that comes next.

### STORY 2: Schwarber 25 HRs

#### ### Facebook Long-Form — 10:30 AM ET

Kyle Schwarber is doing something that doesn't happen very often in baseball.

The Philadelphia Phillies designated hitter became the first player in the major leagues to hit 25 home runs in 2026, a milestone that puts his name alongside some of the most historic sluggers in the sport's history. Only two other players — Mark McGwire in 1998 and Barry Bonds in 2001 — were at this pace point in their respective record-breaking seasons.

Schwarber's 2026 numbers are astonishing in their totality. Through 315 plate appearances, he is batting .249 with 35 extra-base hits, 43 RBIs, 46 walks, and 44 runs scored. His home run pace projects to approximately 66 on the full season — a total that would be the third-highest single-season mark in MLB history, behind only Bonds (73) and McGwire (70).

At 25 home runs, he has already outpaced where most players finish in an entire season.

The competition in the home run race is real. Yordan Alvarez has been right on his heels all year — their back-and-forth in the chase has been one of the best storylines in baseball. But Schwarber holds the milestone of being first to 25, and he holds something else: the Home Run Derby is set for July 13 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia.

His house.

For Phillies fans, watching Schwarber launch baseballs into the seats from the first row at Citizens Bank Park has been one of the experiences of the season. Now imagine that in July, in the Derby, at peak attention.

The pace is real. The moment is building.
