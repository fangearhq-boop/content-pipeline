Sandy Alcantara Throws the First Maddux of 2026 as Marlins Rout White Sox 10-0

By Marcus Cole | April 2, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

There is pitching efficiently and then there is what Sandy Alcantara did on Tuesday night. Nine innings. Three hits. Zero walks. Zero runs. Seven strikeouts. Ninety-three pitches. The Miami Marlins beat the Chicago White Sox 10-0, and the only story was the man on the mound.

Alcantara threw what baseball calls a Maddux — a complete game shutout in fewer than 100 pitches, named for the Hall of Famer who made pitching economy his defining art. It is one of the rarest accomplishments in the sport. Alcantara became the first pitcher to throw one in the 2026 season.

It was his second career Maddux and 13th career complete game. His 5th career shutout. And the exclamation point on an opening week that has reminded baseball fans exactly what this pitcher is capable of when healthy and locked in.

The Streak and the Context

Alcantara's scoreless inning streak now stands at 15 innings to open the 2026 season. He entered Tuesday's start having not allowed a run through his first six innings as a Marlins starter this year, and he extended that streak through a nine-inning masterpiece against a White Sox lineup that could not find a foothold against him.

The 2022 National League Cy Young Award winner is 2-0 with a 0.00 ERA. His command was precise — not a single walk issued. His efficiency was the story: 93 pitches to retire 27 batters is the kind of output that makes opposing managers wince and pitching coaches smile.

For context on just how rare the Maddux is: in an era where starters average fewer than six innings per outing and pitch counts routinely hit 100 before the seventh inning, completing a game on 93 pitches while allowing no runs is an achievement that belongs in a different category entirely. Alcantara now has two of them on his career resume.

The Offense Did Its Part

Alcantara had plenty of support. Liam Hicks went 3-for-4 with a home run and four RBI, pushing his MLB-leading total to 12 RBI through six games. The 26-year-old catcher, acquired by Miami in the Rule 5 Draft in December 2024, continues to be the early offensive surprise of the 2026 season. He leads all of baseball in RBI by a comfortable margin.

Otto López also homered and Graham Pauley doubled twice, adding to what became a comfortable margin against a White Sox team that has now lost all six games to open the year. Chicago starter Shane Smith, who is 0-2 on the season, allowed 12 runs over just 4⅔ innings in his first two starts combined.

The 10-0 margin was not a fluke. The Marlins have outscored opponents by a significant margin over their first six games, and the combination of Alcantara's brilliance and the lineup's timely production has given Miami its best start since 2020.

Miami's Quiet Surge

The Marlins are 5-1. In a National League that features the defending champion Dodgers and perennial contenders in Atlanta and Philadelphia, that record may fly under the radar. But it shouldn't.

This is a Miami team built around pitching, and Alcantara is the anchor. When he is dealing at this level — efficient, command-forward, dominant through nine — the Marlins become a different kind of problem for opposing lineups. The rest of the rotation needs to hold up, and the offense needs to continue producing runs at this clip. But through six games, the early evidence suggests this team is worth watching.

What's Next

Alcantara will make his next start on a standard rotation schedule, likely Sunday or Monday. With a 15-inning scoreless streak and his Maddux now in the books, the conversation will only grow louder: is the 2022 NL Cy Young winner pitching at that level again in 2026?

The White Sox, meanwhile, will try to snap a six-game losing streak in upcoming series play. For a rebuilding franchise, the early skid is not a crisis — but it is a reminder of how far the rebuild still has to go.
