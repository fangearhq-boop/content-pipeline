The 2026 Rookie Explosion: DeLauter and Stewart Deliver Historic Opening Week

By Marcus Cole | April 2, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The 2026 MLB season has barely reached a week old, and two first-round picks from the same draft class are already rewriting what Opening Week can look like for a rookie.

Chase DeLauter of the Cleveland Guardians and Sal Stewart of the Cincinnati Reds have been named the first AL and NL Players of the Week of the 2026 season, respectively. It is only the second time in Major League Baseball history that rookies have swept both of the first weekly Player of the Week awards in the same season.

Both players were selected in the first round of the 2022 MLB Draft. DeLauter went 16th overall. Stewart went 32nd. They are now, four years later, producing at a level that neither scout nor projection system could have reasonably anticipated.

Chase DeLauter: Four Home Runs in Three Games

DeLauter hit four home runs in his first three Major League games. He is only the second player in MLB history to accomplish that. The first was Trevor Story, who did it in 2016.

His triple-slash through four games: .353/.353/1.059, with five RBI in 17 plate appearances. He was the first Guardians player since 1901 to hit multiple home runs in a regular season debut. At 21 years old, he is playing like a veteran who has been waiting for this opportunity.

The backstory makes the production even more striking. DeLauter played only 138 minor league games before reaching the majors — an unusually small sample that resulted from a series of injuries that threatened to derail his development timeline. The Guardians believed in him through all of it. That patience has produced one of the most explosive major league debuts in recent memory.

Sal Stewart: .700 and Hitting Cleanup

Stewart's numbers are equally difficult to contextualize. He batted .700 with a 1.300 slugging percentage through his team's first three games — seven hits in 13 plate appearances, including three doubles and a home run.

He batted cleanup for the Reds on Opening Day, becoming the first Cincinnati rookie to hit in that lineup spot on Opening Day since 1958. He is the sixth rookie since 1961 to reach base three or more times in each of his team's first three games — the previous one was Steven Kwan in 2022.

Stewart, a first baseman, is 22 years old. The Reds selected him with the 32nd pick in the 2022 draft expecting him to be a cornerstone of their rebuild. He is arriving earlier, and at a higher level, than the timeline suggested.

The 2022 Draft Class Takes Center Stage

The significance of both players coming from the 2022 draft class is not lost on the front offices that invested in that class. Four years after the picks were made, the returns are arriving simultaneously.

For the Guardians, DeLauter represents the kind of home-grown power threat that Cleveland has traditionally struggled to develop. For the Reds, Stewart fits the profile of the position player rebuild Cincinnati has been executing — young, patient at the plate, with a power ceiling that matches his production through the first week.

The fact that they are doing it at the same time, in the same week, for different leagues, makes the story richer. It is only the second time in baseball history that two rookies have won the first AL and NL POTW awards of a season. The 2026 rookie class has announced itself.

What's Next

Both players will face the inevitable adjustment that every rookie encounters — opposing scouts who have now watched several at-bats and are building a book. The question is not whether DeLauter and Stewart will eventually face adversity. It is how they respond to it.

The early evidence suggests they are ready. Week 1 gave them the stage. The rest of the season will determine whether this is a preview of long, productive careers or simply an electrifying sample that fades into context. The smart money says there is more to come from both of them.
