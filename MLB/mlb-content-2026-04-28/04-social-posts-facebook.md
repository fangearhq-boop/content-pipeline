Window: 1:00 PM ET

It's official: the Boston Red Sox have fired manager Alex Cora — and the decision has sent shockwaves through the baseball world.

The Red Sox let Cora go alongside five members of his coaching staff on Saturday with the team sitting at a dismal 10-17. Chad Tracy, who had been managing Triple-A Worcester, was named interim manager and made his debut Sunday against the Baltimore Orioles at Camden Yards.

Here's what makes this firing so striking:
⚾ Alex Cora won the 2018 World Series with the Red Sox — one of the most dominant championship runs in recent memory

⚾ Multiple players reportedly cried when news of the firing broke
⚾ Craig Breslow, the Red Sox's chief baseball officer, called the decision "painful but necessary"
⚾ The 10-17 record reflects struggles across all areas — pitching, offense, and defense

The Red Sox came into 2026 with legitimate postseason expectations. Something has gone very wrong — and now Tracy has to figure out how to fix it.
