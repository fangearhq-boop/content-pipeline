Ohtani Goes 3-for-3 With a Home Run as Dodgers Shut Out Cubs to Win Series

By Jake Torres

April 28, 2026

Shohei Ohtani had been quiet by his standards — 13 games without a home run, an eternity for a player who bends the rules of what is possible in baseball. On Sunday at Dodger Stadium, he offered a sharp reminder that a quiet Ohtani is still the most dangerous hitter in the game. Ohtani went 3-for-3 with a walk, a home run, two runs scored, and an RBI as the Los Angeles Dodgers shut out the Chicago Cubs 6-0 to win the series two games to one.

Ohtani's home run — his sixth of the 2026 season, struck with a 109.8 mph exit velocity — was the kind of ball that goes quiet off the bat and loud on the way out. It came in a game where Justin Wrobleski was excellent, tossing six scoreless innings to give the Dodgers' bullpen a much-needed rest. Los Angeles moves to 19-9 on the season.

Context of the HR Drought

Thirteen games without a home run is not a crisis for Shohei Ohtani — it is a blip that the baseball world nonetheless tracks obsessively, because the expectation for Ohtani at the plate has been permanently recalibrated by what he has done over the past three seasons. His pace in 2026 remains formidable. His offensive profile — combining elite on-base skills with historic power potential — means that even in stretches without home runs, he remains one of the most productive hitters in the game.

Sunday's performance was a reset. Three hits, a walk, nearly a cycle — it was exactly the kind of multi-faceted offensive display that defines what Ohtani brings to every lineup. He was robbed of the cycle by a triple, making his final stat line all the more impressive for what it almost was.

The Broader Dodgers Picture

The Dodgers at 19-9 are consistent with preseason expectations as one of baseball's elite teams. Their depth — starting pitching, bullpen, and an offense that can score from anywhere in the lineup — has been their hallmark. Wrobleski's strong outing Sunday was an example of the organizational pitching depth Los Angeles has built. When Roki Sasaki and the top rotation starters are performing, the Dodgers are nearly unbeatable. When the back of the rotation steps up, as Wrobleski did, it reinforces why Los Angeles is the consensus NL favorite.

The Cubs, fresh off having their 10-game win streak snapped on Saturday, left 12 runners on base in the series finale — a frustrating end to a weekend that had started so promisingly for Chicago. The Dodgers won the series 2-1, affirming their place atop the NL rankings.

What's Next

Ohtani and the Dodgers continue their 2026 schedule with a full week of games ahead. The AL and NL West races are far from settled, and Los Angeles will need sustained performance from all cylinders — including Ohtani's bat — to maintain their dominant position. For now, the message from Sunday's game is clear: the drought is over, and Ohtani is back in full force.
