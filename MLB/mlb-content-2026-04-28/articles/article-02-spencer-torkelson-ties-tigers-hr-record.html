Spencer Torkelson Ties Detroit Tigers Record With Home Run in Fifth Straight Game

By Jake Torres

April 28, 2026

Seven days ago, Spencer Torkelson was hitting under .200 with zero home runs in 2026. On Sunday, the Detroit Tigers first baseman homered for the fifth consecutive game, tying a franchise record shared by five legendary sluggers: Hank Greenberg, Rudy York, Vic Wertz, Willie Horton, and Marcus Thames. Detroit defeated the Cincinnati Reds 8-3 behind Torkelson's 421-foot blast to left-center field, capping one of the most remarkable week-long turnarounds the sport has seen in recent memory.

The record milestone has placed the 24-year-old in extraordinary company. Greenberg is arguably the greatest hitter in Tigers history, a Hall of Famer and two-time MVP. Willie Horton was a cornerstone of Detroit's 1968 World Series championship team. The fact that Torkelson now shares their record — however temporarily — is a testament to the sustained explosiveness he has shown over the past week.

The Turnaround

There are few statistical reversals in baseball as dramatic as what Torkelson has done since breaking out of his early-season slump. The streak began Wednesday when he finally connected against Milwaukee's Chad Patrick at Comerica Park, snapping a prolonged drought. His first career walk-off home run came Thursday, off a 99.2 mph fastball from Abner Uribe. From there, the power kept coming — home runs in four consecutive days that built into the franchise-record-tying fifth.

Torkelson was a highly touted prospect when the Tigers selected him first overall in the 2020 MLB Draft. His 2026 season had started slowly, leaving questions about his development trajectory. This five-game stretch has silenced those questions loudly.

What Comes Next

The baseball world is now watching every Torkelson plate appearance with elevated attention. One more home run would give him sole possession of the all-time Tigers franchise record for consecutive games with a home run — a mark he would own outright, separating himself from the franchise legends he currently shares it with. It would also mark the longest such streak in Major League Baseball since Rafael Devers accomplished the same feat in 2024.

Detroit's season has been modest overall, but Torkelson's explosion has given Tigers fans something to celebrate and a compelling individual storyline to follow. The city of Detroit has loved watching its sluggers make history — and Torkelson could be on the verge of writing a new page in that tradition.

What's Next

Spencer Torkelson and the Detroit Tigers continue their 2026 season with the entire baseball world focused on his next at-bat. The next time he steps to the plate with a chance at history, it will be among the most-watched moments of the young MLB season. A 421-foot blast to left-center and a franchise record already secured — what comes next for the 24-year-old is anyone's guess, but the expectation is another home run isn't far off.
