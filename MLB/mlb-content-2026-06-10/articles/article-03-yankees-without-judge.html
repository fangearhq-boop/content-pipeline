Yankees 7-5 in 10 Innings — Bellinger Walks It Off, New York Goes 2-0 Without Judge

By Ryan Calloway | June 10, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The New York Yankees are proving, at least early on, that they can win without Aaron Judge. Cody Bellinger delivered a walk-off two-run single off Shawn Armstrong in the 10th inning Monday night, lifting New York to a 7-5 victory over the Cleveland Guardians. It was the Yankees' second consecutive win since Judge was placed on the 10-day injured list with a right rib stress fracture.

Paul Goldschmidt and Ryan McMahon both homered for the Yankees. Cleveland actually had the lead heading into the later innings — Angel Martinez hit a two-run home run in the fifth to give the Guardians a 5-4 advantage — but Goldschmidt's RBI grounder in the eighth tied it at five and sent the game to extras. In the 10th, the automatic runner turned into the winning run when Bellinger's opposite-field hit fell in against a drawn-in infield.

The Yankees Without Their Best Player

Two wins is not a trend. But it is a statement of sorts. The Yankees have enough depth and experience to string together victories in the short term, and the most encouraging part of Monday's win was who produced it: Goldschmidt, McMahon, and Bellinger are three veteran bats who have been around big moments before. They did not shrink when the team needed them to step up.

Spencer Jones, recalled from Triple-A to fill out the roster, is adjusting to his first meaningful big-league opportunity. Ben Rice has provided steady production in the middle of the lineup. The Yankees are managing, and managing well — for now.

The question is whether they can keep doing it for two months. Judge is expected to miss approximately eight weeks with the rib stress fracture, which puts his return timeline somewhere around late July or the early days of August. The August 3 trade deadline lands in that window, which means the Yankees will be making roster construction decisions before they know exactly when their franchise player is coming back.

The AL East Context

The Yankees are not playing in a forgiving division. The Tampa Bay Rays and Boston Red Sox have both shown the ability to put together competitive stretches, and every game in June has implications for where teams stand come July. New York needs Judge healthy and productive in August and September to win the AL East. Two extra-inning wins over Cleveland are encouraging, not conclusive.

But momentum matters in a long season. The Yankees could have spiraled after losing their best player. Instead, Bellinger is walking it off and Goldschmidt is homering and the team is fighting. That character, more than any individual performance, is what contending teams need when the stars are unavailable.

What's Next

The Yankees continue their series at Cleveland before returning to face the AL East competition they'll need to beat all summer. The most important number to watch over the next several weeks isn't the win-loss record — it's how many games back New York falls (or doesn't fall) while Judge recovers. If they can hold their position, he returns to a team that still has a shot. If the division slips away, the deadline calculus changes dramatically.
