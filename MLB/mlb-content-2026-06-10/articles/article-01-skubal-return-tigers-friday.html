Skubal Confirmed to Start Friday — Two-Time Cy Young Returns to Detroit Rotation

By Marcus Cole | June 10, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The moment every Tigers fan has been waiting for is finally here. Tarik Skubal is set to return to the Detroit starting rotation this Friday at Cleveland, ending a month-long absence following arthroscopic left elbow surgery that was supposed to cost him two to three months. He's coming back in five.

The confirmation came after Skubal's dominant rehab start on June 7 with High-A West Michigan, where he threw five scoreless innings, struck out six, allowed just two hits, and needed only 54 pitches to do it. Manager A.J. Hinch did not hide his enthusiasm. "Very encouraged with Tarik," Hinch said. "He looked exactly kind of how he's looked in the live BPs. The velocity, the execution was good."

Those are not the words of a manager talking about a pitcher who needs more time. That is a manager describing someone who is ready.

What Skubal's Return Means for the Tigers

The Tigers went 6-21 while Skubal was on the injured list. That record is not a coincidence. Skubal is not just their best pitcher — he is the pitcher who gives the entire organization confidence that a winning baseball team is worth believing in. With him in the rotation, Detroit becomes a different team.

His return also means the rotation that had been cobbled together through his absence gets to reset. Every start he makes is one fewer start the Tigers have to construct around arms not designed for front-line work. He changes the daily math of the bullpen, the opponent's lineup construction, the feel in the dugout, and the momentum that can build over a series.

The Trade Deadline Angle

Skubal is eligible for free agency after this season. The Tigers are not in playoff position. Those two facts create an obvious and consequential equation: if Detroit falls further out of the race between now and August 3, every team with a championship window becomes a potential landing spot for the two-time Cy Young winner.

ESPN recently published nine separate blockbuster trade proposals involving Skubal — the Yankees, Dodgers, Braves, Brewers, Phillies, and others all featured prominently. The interest is real. The question is whether the Tigers choose to be sellers, and if so, what kind of return they demand for a pitcher who just proved he can dominate Triple-A hitters five weeks after elbow surgery.

Teams have to start answering these questions now. Every team with a playoff push needs to decide: do you go get the two-time Cy Young, or do you let someone else do it?

What's Next

Friday's start at Cleveland will be scrutinized more closely than almost any regular-season start in recent memory. How many innings does Skubal pitch? What does the velocity look like? Does the command from the rehab start translate? The answers will shape conversations about both the Tigers' short-term plans and the entire trade deadline landscape heading toward August 3.
