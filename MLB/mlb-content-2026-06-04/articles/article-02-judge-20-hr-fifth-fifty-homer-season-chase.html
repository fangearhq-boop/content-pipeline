Judge First to 20 HRs, On Pace for Unprecedented 5th Career 50-Homer Season

By Jake Torres June 4, 2026

First in the Game to 20

Aaron Judge is the first player in Major League Baseball to reach 20 home runs in 2026. He did it in roughly 56 team games — ahead of his own pace from the 2022 season when he hit 62 and set the American League record. Kyle Schwarber is at 22 and leads the majors, but reached that total slightly earlier in the calendar, meaning Judge hit No. 20 first among players measured against the same game count.

This is the third time Judge has been the first player in the majors to 20 HRs in a season (2017, 2022, 2026). The only player in Yankees history who has been first to 20 more often: Babe Ruth, who did it seven times. Judge is at three and counting.

The Five 50-HR Seasons Nobody Has Had

Here's the record that matters most: four players in baseball history have hit 50 or more home runs in four different seasons. Babe Ruth. Ken Griffey Jr. Sammy Sosa. And Aaron Judge, who did it in 2017, 2022, 2024, and 2025. The record is tied at four.

If Judge reaches 50 HRs in 2026 — at his current pace projecting to 56 over 162 games, that is a real possibility — he would be the first player in the history of baseball to do it five times. That's a sentence that takes a moment to absorb. Five seasons. Five times. A record that has stood at four since Sosa hit 63 in 2001 and never got another one.

What 56-Plus HRs Means for the Yankees

New York is in a pennant race. The Yankees and the Tampa Bay Rays have been the two best teams in the AL East, and Judge is the engine. His presence in the lineup changes how opposing pitchers approach every inning. With the Yankees looking to return to the World Series after their 2024 championship, Judge having another historically great offensive season is exactly what they need.

He is also the AL MVP frontrunner. The market for that award closed at minus-135 (FanDuel) as of early June — as short as it's been since his unanimous MVP season in 2024. If the pace holds, the award conversation won't be close.

What's Next

Judge is 20 HRs into a pace that projects to 56 over 162 games. The next meaningful milestone is No. 25, which he could reach before the All-Star break. If he stays healthy through September, the record for most 50-HR seasons becomes his to take alone. Watch for the Yankees' schedule in the coming weeks as Judge navigates the AL East competition.
