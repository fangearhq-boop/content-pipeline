Only deGrom and Marichal Did It Better: Ohtani's 0.74 ERA Is the Third-Best Through 10 Starts in MLB History

By Marcus Cole June 4, 2026

Six More Scoreless Innings Wednesday

Shohei Ohtani pitched six scoreless innings against the Arizona Diamondbacks on Wednesday, struck out six, and allowed two hits. The Dodgers won 7-0. Kyle Tucker hit a two-run home run into the Chase Field pool for good measure. But the number that matters most is the one that didn't move: zero earned runs, for the sixth time in 10 starts this season.

Ohtani's ERA is now 0.74. Through 10 starts since 1913, only Jacob deGrom (0.56, 2021) and Juan Marichal (0.59, 1966) have posted a lower figure. Ohtani is third. His 6-2 record, 67 strikeouts, 0.79 WHIP, and 61 innings pitched sit at a level that, in any normal year, would be the unquestioned story of the entire season.

Then He Picked Up a Bat

After the last out of his sixth inning, Ohtani headed to the dugout and eventually to the plate. He went 3-for-4 with two walks, reached base five times, and scored a run. His batting average is back above .300. His on-base percentage leads the National League at .420.

No player in Major League Baseball history has qualified for the season leaderboards as both a hitter and a pitcher in the same season. Ohtani is doing it in 2026. The pitching ERA title requires 162 innings; he's on pace to get there. The batting titles require 502 plate appearances; he's on pace for that too. He may qualify for both simultaneously. Nothing like it has happened at this level of performance.

The Historical Context Around 0.74

DeGrom in 2021 was not merely the best pitcher in baseball, he was historically dominant — and he finished the season with a 1.08 ERA because the second half did not match the first. Marichal's 1966 season is considered one of the greatest pitching years of the post-deadball era. Ohtani is in that company after 10 starts, and he's also hitting .300 with 12 home runs from the designated hitter spot on the days he doesn't pitch.

The Dodgers are the frontrunner in the NL West and the broad NL pennant race. Ohtani is the reason. Two other Cy Young-caliber starters back up a rotation that should carry Los Angeles deep into October. But none of it happens without Ohtani at the top.

What's Next

Ohtani's next start is expected in four to five days. If he maintains anything close to this ERA, he'll finish in the top three of all-time ERA through 10 starts regardless of what happens in the second half. The Dodgers continue their road trip, and Ohtani's next outing will again be must-see baseball for anyone who follows the game.
