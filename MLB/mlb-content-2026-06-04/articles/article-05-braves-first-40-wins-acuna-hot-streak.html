First to 40 Wins: Braves Lead NL East by 9.5 as Acuña Breaks Out With 4 HRs in 3 Games

By Jake Torres June 4, 2026

Baseball's Best Record, and an Accelerating Offense

The Atlanta Braves were the first team in Major League Baseball to reach 40 wins in 2026. They're now 40-20 through early June, with a 9.5-game lead in the NL East. Chris Sale is anchoring the rotation. Austin Riley and Matt Olson have been consistent. The bullpen has been reliable. And now the player who was supposed to make this offense elite is finally looking like himself.

Ronald Acuña Jr. had two home runs over his first 42 games of 2026. Not the impact anyone expected from the reigning NL MVP. Then, in a three-game stretch against Cincinnati, he hit four home runs — including a 438-foot blast — and also contributed stolen bases, making him one of three players since 1900 with 4-plus HRs and 4-plus steals in a three-game span. The others: Shohei Ohtani in 2024 and Max Carey in 1922.

Why This Matters for Atlanta's October Outlook

The Braves have been winning without Acuña at full power. That's the most impressive part of their 40-20 record. Their run differential is strong, their pitching has been elite, and the lineup has enough depth to cover for a star having a slow start.

But when you add a fully engaged Acuña to a team already playing at this level, the ceiling gets significantly higher. Acuña's combination of power, speed, and on-base ability is the engine that makes the Braves' lineup go from very good to genuinely dangerous for any opponent in any round of the playoffs.

The NL East Picture

A 9.5-game lead in early June is substantial. The Braves don't need to be perfect from here — they just need to be consistent. Their schedule through the summer includes stretches against the Mets, Phillies, and Marlins, which will test whether this lead holds. But with Sale healthy and Acuña breaking out, Atlanta is built to absorb a stretch of inconsistency better than most teams at this point in the season.

No team in baseball has a better record. No NL team has a larger division lead. The Braves are the clear NL pennant frontrunner going into June.

What's Next

The Braves continue their regular-season schedule with a series schedule that tests the NL East. The next major marker for Atlanta is the midseason record: if they're above .600 at the All-Star break with Acuña in form, they're the team nobody in the NL wants to face in October. Keep an eye on Acuña's HR total over the next few weeks to see if the hot stretch holds.
