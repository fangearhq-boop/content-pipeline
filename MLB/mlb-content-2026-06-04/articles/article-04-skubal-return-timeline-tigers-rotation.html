Rehab Start Coming, Return in Sight: Tarik Skubal's Road Back to the Tigers Rotation

By Marcus Cole June 4, 2026

The Simulated Game and What Comes Next

Tarik Skubal threw a 64-pitch simulated game this week, describing the session as "awesome." A minor-league rehab start is expected this weekend, which would put him on track for a return to Detroit's major-league rotation around mid-June. Manager A.J. Hinch has been careful not to commit to a specific return date, but multiple reporters have pointed to June 17 at Cleveland as the target if Skubal comes through the rehab start without issue.

Skubal went on the injured list May 6 for a nano-scope procedure to remove a loose body from his throwing elbow. The procedure was described as minor, and his recovery has moved faster than initial projections suggested. A one-month absence for a loose-body removal, if it holds, would be a relatively quick turnaround for a procedure of that type.

What the Tigers Have Been Without Him

Detroit has been navigating the AL Central without its best pitcher for a month. The Tigers went through a stretch without Skubal, Jack Flaherty, and a healthy bullpen simultaneously, which stretched the rotation thin. The pitching depth has been tested. Having Skubal back in the fold — assuming no setbacks in the rehab process — gives the Tigers their ace back in time for the back half of a competitive division race.

The AL Central has not been dominated by any one team. Detroit is in the thick of a Wild Card race and needs pitching to stay relevant into August and September. Skubal is not just the best pitcher on the staff, he's the difference between a playoff contender and a fringe team.

The Cy Young Conversation

Skubal won the AL Cy Young in 2024 and 2025. Back-to-back. Going into the 2026 season, he was among the early favorites for a third consecutive award, which would be the first time any pitcher has done that since Randy Johnson won four straight from 1999-2002. A month-long absence complicates the candidacy on the innings front, but if Skubal comes back and pitches as he did before the injury, the conversation picks up right where it left off.

His 2026 numbers before going down were what you'd expect: ERA under 2.00, strikeout rate elite, and the Tigers winning a high percentage of his starts. The arm was fine; it was the loose body that caused the layoff. None of this was structural. That's the detail Tigers fans needed to hear, and they heard it.

What's Next

The rehab start this weekend is the key milestone. If Skubal throws 60-80 pitches without issue, the mid-June return target becomes concrete. The Tigers open a series at Cleveland around that timeframe, which has been identified as the likely return spot. Watch for official roster moves and activation news from Detroit over the next two weeks.
