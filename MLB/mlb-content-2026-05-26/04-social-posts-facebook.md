### STORY 1: Astros Combined No-Hitter

#### FB Long-Form — 9:00 AM ET

Baseball waited 630 days for a no-hitter. When it finally arrived, it came with a twist nobody expected.

On Monday night in Arlington, the Houston Astros combined to no-hit the Texas Rangers 9-0. The three pitchers who made history: Tatsuya Imai (6 innings), Steven Okert (1 inning), and Alimber Santa (2 innings).

The Astros' ninth was already blank. Their bullpen just needed to keep it that way.

What makes this no-hitter remarkable is how improbable it felt in the early going. Imai, who signed a three-year, $54 million deal with Houston in January after three All-Star seasons in Japan's Pacific League, walked three of the first four batters he faced. The bases were loaded immediately. A hit would have ended everything.

Instead, he got a double play. Then another. Then eight consecutive ground-ball outs. He went six innings, allowed nothing, and handed the ball to Okert and then to a 23-year-old pitcher named Alimber Santa.

Santa had never thrown a pitch in the major leagues before Monday. He retired all six batters he faced in two perfect innings to seal the no-hitter.

Alimber Santa is the first pitcher since 1900 to appear in a no-hitter in his MLB debut. He threw two perfect innings in his first major league game, in a no-hitter, on the road in Texas.

The last MLB no-hitter was September 4, 2024 — the Cubs' Shota Imanaga, Nate Pearson, and Porter Hodge against Pittsburgh. That was 630 days ago. Monday's gem was the 17th no-hitter in Astros franchise history.

Imai looks like a steal. Santa looks like a future closer. And the Rangers had no idea what hit them. ⚾️

### STORY 2: Blue Jays Rotation Crisis

#### FB Long-Form — 10:30 AM ET

There is a full-blown pitching emergency in Toronto.

On Sunday, Dylan Cease left the field in the fifth inning of the Blue Jays' game against the Pittsburgh Pirates. On Monday, the news was official: 15-day injured list, left hamstring strain, mild severity. He will miss multiple starts.

Cease, in his first season with Toronto on a seven-year, $210 million contract, has been excellent. He's 3-3 with a 3.05 ERA, and his 92 strikeouts are the most in the major leagues. He was the centerpiece of a pitching staff Toronto believed could compete with anyone in the American League.

But Cease joining the injured list doesn't tell the full story of what Toronto is dealing with right now.

Also on the Blue Jays' injured list: José Berríos. Max Scherzer. Shane Bieber. Cody Ponce.

Five starting pitchers, including the $210M ace, are all unavailable. At the same time, Vladimir Guerrero Jr. is day-to-day after being struck by a pitch on his elbow.

The Blue Jays built this roster to compete in the AL East. The plan depended on keeping expensive arms healthy and getting production from Vladdy at the plate. Right now, neither is happening.

Toronto still has time to recover if Cease comes back healthy. But the depth has been exhausted before June, and the path back is going to require players nobody expected to matter this much, this early. ⚾️

This is going to be one of the most-watched trade deadline situations in baseball. ⚾️

### STORY 4: Dodgers Bullpen Franchise Record

#### FB Long-Form — 3:00 PM ET

Here is the list of Dodgers pitchers who are not available right now: Tyler Glasnow. Blake Snell. Jack Dreyer. Edwin Diaz — on the IL since early in the season with a loose body in his elbow.

The Dodgers were supposed to have one of the most formidable pitching staffs in baseball. Instead, they've spent the first two months of the season patching together a rotation and hope their bullpen holds.

The bullpen has not just held. It has made history.

Los Angeles relievers have posted **38 consecutive scoreless innings** — a franchise record in the modern era, and the longest bullpen scoreless streak in MLB since the 2017 Cleveland Indians.

Twelve different pitchers have contributed. The standout is Kyle Hurt, who has made 14 appearances this season without allowing a single run, posting a 0.60 ERA with an 0.87 WHIP. Tanner Scott has been the veteran presence, scoreless in his last 10.1 innings since April 24.

None of this was in anyone's projections. The Dodgers signed Diaz to be their elite closer and lost him before summer. They built a rotation around Glasnow and Snell and lost them both.

The bullpen decided that losing stars wasn't a reason to stop competing.

Sometimes the best baseball stories come from the places nobody expected. Right now, in Los Angeles, a dozen relievers are writing one of the most unexpected stories of the 2026 season. ⚾️
