Elly De La Cruz Is Having the Best Shortstop Season in Baseball — And It's Not Close

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | May 12, 2026

De La Cruz leads all MLB shortstops in WAR at 1.9, with 10 home runs and 8 stolen bases. He's the first shortstop since 1900 to reach 5 HR and 5 SB through his team's first 16 games. The Reds have something special.

When the Reds gave Elly De La Cruz their shortstop job, the question was whether his tools would translate into sustained production or whether the raw power and speed would come with enough strikeouts and inconsistency to limit his value. Forty games into 2026, the answer is in. De La Cruz leads all major league shortstops in WAR at 1.9, one tick ahead of Bobby Witt Jr. at 1.8. He is hitting .265/.337/.510 with 10 home runs and 8 stolen bases.

Those aren't just good numbers for a shortstop. They are historically unusual numbers for a shortstop at any point in his career, let alone at age 24.

The Historical Context

De La Cruz is the first MLB shortstop since 1900 to reach 5 home runs and 5 stolen bases through his team's first 16 games of a season. That combination requires elite power at a position where power has historically been a secondary skill, paired with elite speed at a position where base-stealing is expected but rarely accompanies the kind of slugging percentage De La Cruz is posting.

The 1900 cutoff matters because it places the record in the modern era of baseball, after the sport had standardized its rules and the shortstop position had become what it is today. Every shortstop who has played meaningful games in the past 125 years is in the comparison pool. None of them did what De La Cruz is doing through 16 games.

What the Numbers Look Like in Full

A .510 slugging percentage from a shortstop is power-hitter territory. Combined with a .337 on-base percentage, De La Cruz is producing run value at every phase of the offensive game. He gets on base at a rate above league average. When he makes contact, he hits it hard and far. When he reaches, he runs. The stolen base number compounds the offensive impact because he turns singles into doubles and doubles into threats that defenses have to account for on every pitch.

The 10 home runs through roughly 40 games put him on pace for a 40-plus home run season if he sustains it. Eight stolen bases put him on pace for 30-plus. A shortstop finishing a season at 40 home runs and 30 stolen bases would be one of the more remarkable individual performances of the decade.

De La Cruz in the Shortstop Landscape

The competition at shortstop in 2026 is legitimate. Bobby Witt Jr. in Kansas City is a two-time All-Star with a Gold Glove and a multi-year track record as one of the best players in the American League. Witt at 1.8 WAR is not having a bad year. He is simply sitting one position below De La Cruz in a ranking that will matter for All-Star selections and, eventually, for award voting.

De La Cruz is a two-time All-Star himself. His first two seasons in the majors established that his tools were real and that his development curve was trending in the right direction. The 2026 season looks like the year those tools have converged into consistent, dominant production. The Reds play in Cincinnati, not New York or Los Angeles, which means the national coverage will lag the actual performance. The numbers are there regardless of which market produces them.

What's Next

De La Cruz and the Reds continue their schedule with games through the week. Every at-bat adds to the historical ledger he is building. The shortstop WAR race between him and Witt Jr. will be a story worth tracking all season — two elite players at the same position, both capable of the kind of performance that makes the other's season look ordinary by comparison. For now, De La Cruz has the lead, the numbers, and a place in the record books that no shortstop since 1900 can claim. That is where the 2026 season stands for the best shortstop in baseball right now.
