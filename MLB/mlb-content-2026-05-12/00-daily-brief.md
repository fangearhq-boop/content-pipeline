## Key Facts Summary

- **Judge vs. Murakami HR race:** Aaron Judge (Yankees) and Munetaka Murakami (White Sox) are tied for the MLB home run lead at 15. Murakami hit a solo HR on May 9 to tie Judge. Judge has a career total of 383-384 HRs and is batting .273. Murakami is in his first MLB season, having signed a 2-year $34M deal in December 2025 after winning the NPB Triple Crown in Japan with 56 HRs in 2022. Murakami owns multiple MLB records for a Japanese-born player, including most HRs before first double or triple in MLB history.
- **Cubs at Braves tonight:** Cubs (27-14) visit Braves (28-13) at Truist Park, 7:15 PM ET on TBS. These are the two best records in the NL. Cubs are the first team since the 1955 Brooklyn Dodgers to have multiple distinct 10+ game win streaks in their first 40 decisions. Braves have the NL East's best record and a large division lead.
- **Henry Bolte debut:** Henry Bolte was recalled by the Oakland A's on Monday, May 11, for his first MLB stint. In his debut, Bolte hit safely in 12 straight plate appearances with 9 extra-base hits — a feat nobody has accomplished at the Major League level in the Expansion Era (since 1961). He was a top prospect in the A's system.
- **Ramírez 300-300 chase:** Ramírez stole his 300th career base on May 2, becoming only the second player in Cleveland franchise history to reach that milestone (Kenny Lofton was first). He now has 291 career HRs, needing 9 to join the 300-300 club, which would make him the 9th member ever. Club includes Willie Mays, Barry Bonds, Alex Rodriguez, Andre Dawson, Carlos Beltran, Bobby Bonds, and others.
- **Elly De La Cruz:** De La Cruz (Reds) is slashing .265 with 10 HRs and 8 stolen bases on the year. He leads all MLB shortstops in WAR (1.9) ahead of Bobby Witt Jr. He is the first MLB shortstop since 1900 to post 5+ HR and 5+ SB through 16 games. He is 24 years old and already a two-time All-Star.
- **Tuesday MLB:** Braves (28-13) host Cubs (27-14) in the NL's featured matchup. Other games: Yankees at Orioles, Nationals at Reds, Rays at Blue Jays, Tigers at Mets.
