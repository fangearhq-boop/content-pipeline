Skubal Tells Tigers to Be Buyers — The Ace With Zero Appetite to Be Traded

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 14, 2026

Trade deadline season produces a reliable set of characters every July. The star who demands a trade. The executive who won't confirm or deny anything. The rival GM who's been "closely monitoring the situation." Tarik Skubal is a different character entirely.

The Detroit Tigers' ace is telling his organization to be buyers. Not sellers. Buyers.

Skubal has told friends he "badly wants to stay in Detroit" and has "zero appetite to be traded." But he went further than that in his most recent public comments, addressing the August 3 deadline directly and saying he wants the Tigers front office to add at the deadline — not subtract. To build around this run they're on, not sell it off.

Why He Thinks Detroit Has a Chance

The Tigers have gone 19-12 since June 1 — the best record in Major League Baseball over that span. They have climbed to within 4 games of an AL Wild Card spot heading into the second half. Skubal believes in what he's seeing in the clubhouse and in the standings.

For context on what he is: a two-time AL Cy Young Award winner. The 2024 award was unanimous, with all 30 voters selecting him No. 1. In 2025, he went 13-6 with a 2.21 ERA and 241 strikeouts. The 2026 season was progressing along the same lines before the All-Star break. He is one of the two or three best pitchers in the American League by any reasonable metric.

The Dilemma

This is where the story gets complicated, because Skubal's desire to stay in Detroit is in some tension with the front office's calculus. He is in a contract year. Following the season, he is expected to command $400 million or more in free agency — a range that has historically been beyond what the Tigers spend.

The organization has received overtures from contenders, with the New York Yankees and Philadelphia Phillies reported as early frontrunners among potential suitors. The logic of trading him is simple: if you cannot afford to re-sign him and he's going to walk anyway, extracting top prospect talent before he leaves makes the rebuild faster and the future cleaner.

The logic of keeping him is also simple: you have a 19-12 run since June 1 and your best player is asking you to go for it. Extension talks have been described as "nil," but "nil" at the start of July is not necessarily the same as "nil" in October.

What's Next

August 3 is 20 days away. The Tigers enter the second half of the season with a clear message from their ace and an unclear plan from ownership. Skubal's public push for the organization to be buyers gives the front office something they do not often get: a superstar asking to stay and asking for teammates.

Whether that message changes the calculus, only the next 20 days will tell. The second half of the 2026 season begins tonight after the All-Star Game. Skubal's regular turn would have him on the mound in Detroit on Wednesday or Thursday. He is ready to make the case at 95 miles per hour.
