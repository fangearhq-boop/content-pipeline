White Sox Lead the AL Central Into the Second Half — The Most Surprising Story in Baseball

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 14, 2026

Two years ago, the Chicago White Sox went 41-121. That is the worst record in the history of modern Major League Baseball. It was a season so comprehensively lost that the only thing left to discuss was the prospect pipeline and whether the rebuild would eventually produce something worth watching.

Today, the White Sox are 48-45 and leading the American League Central Division.

The second half of the 2026 MLB season begins tonight after the All-Star Game at Citizens Bank Park. When it does, the Chicago White Sox will enter it as division leaders. That sentence was not imaginable two years ago.

What the Division Looks Like

The AL Central is a genuine three-team race heading into the second half. The White Sox lead at 48-45. Behind them are the Cleveland Guardians, who have been in this race all year and represent a consistent, well-run organization that knows how to compete in this division. Behind the Guardians are the Detroit Tigers, who have gone 19-12 since June 1 — the best record in baseball over that span — and are just four games out of a Wild Card spot.

Three teams. One division. The second half will determine a lot.

What Made the White Sox Turnaround Happen

There isn't a single explanation for a 48-45 record after going 41-121. Rebuilds rarely work this cleanly or this quickly. The organization brought in coaching changes, made player development adjustments, and benefited from a wave of young talent beginning to arrive. The roster is not the same one that lost 121 games. The culture, reportedly, is not either.

Roch Cholowsky — the No. 1 overall pick in the 2026 MLB Draft, a shortstop out of UCLA — is not yet in Chicago. He joins a system that now has several Top-100 prospects: Montgomery, Schultz, Bonemer, Smith, and Carlson all rank among the best in the organization. The pipeline that was supposed to take years to pay off is starting to pay off sooner.

The Second Half Stakes

If the White Sox hold on and win the AL Central, it would rank among the most dramatic single-season turnarounds in MLB history. The delta between 41-121 in one season and division champion two seasons later has no clear precedent.

But they have to hold on. The Guardians and Tigers are both capable of catching them. The deadline is August 3. The White Sox's posture as buyers or sellers — and who they add or subtract — will shape the second half significantly.

What's Next

Regular season play resumes Wednesday. The White Sox's next games will tell the early story of the second half. If they continue at their current pace, they are a division contender through September. If the Tigers' recent surge continues and the Guardians stay healthy, this race goes down to the final weekend.

The most surprising team in baseball in 2026 is the Chicago White Sox. That they lead a division in July is remarkable enough. That they might win it is almost unbelievable.

Watch anyway. This one is worth it.
