Jordan Walker Silences Philadelphia — Cardinals Outfielder Wins 2026 Home Run Derby

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 14, 2026

The Philly crowd wanted Kyle Schwarber. Jordan Walker did not care.

The 24-year-old Cardinals outfielder defeated Schwarber 12-11 in the final of the 2026 MLB Home Run Derby on Monday night at Citizens Bank Park, becoming the first Cardinal in the event's history to take home the title. He also walked away with $1 million in prize money on a $799,400 season salary.

The way he won it will be remembered for a while.

How the Final Played Out

Walker hit 8 home runs on his first 13 regulation swings in the championship round while Schwarber was sitting at 11. With the Philadelphia crowd already celebrating in its collective mind, Walker activated what the format calls "final ball" swings — bonus swings that come after a final-round HR — and hit 4 consecutive home runs to reach 12. Schwarber had 11. That was the margin.

Cardinals 12, Phillies hometown crowd 11. Silence in the seats.

The Rest of the Bracket

The semifinal matchups set the stage. In the first semi, Schwarber defeated Willson Contreras 9-8, with the Citizens Bank Park crowd fully behind their designated hitter in what amounted to a home-game atmosphere. In the second semi, Walker defeated Junior Caminero 6-5.

That Caminero semifinal produced the night's most memorable single swing. The 23-year-old Rays third baseman launched a home run measuring 491 feet — the longest HR of the entire night. Caminero fell short, but he had the crowd wide-eyed for a moment.

In the first round, Bryce Harper — the 2018 Derby champion who won the event at this same ballpark eight years ago — was eliminated after hitting 8 home runs. Schwarber hit 10 in the same round to advance and send Harper home early in front of his own fans.

Walker's Credentials

Walker entered the Derby as a legitimate power hitter on a Cardinals team looking to build around him. His average exit velocity in the competition was 107 mph, his hardest was 115 mph, and his average distance was 428 feet in the semifinals. He has shown this kind of power potential in the regular season, and Monday night was the first time a national audience saw it on the sport's biggest regular-season stage.

His age and contract status make the $1 million payday particularly notable. Pre-arbitration players at his salary level don't typically walk away from a single exhibition event with more money than they made all season. Walker does.

What's Next

The 2026 Home Run Derby was the inaugural edition on Netflix, which took over broadcast rights from ESPN for the event. Walker's walk-off comeback finish was exactly the kind of moment Netflix needed for the debut. Tonight, the 96th MLB All-Star Game takes place at the same venue — Citizens Bank Park — at 8 PM ET on FOX. For Walker, last night was spectacular. Now the focus shifts to the regular season, the Cardinals' second half, and the question of whether Monday night marked the emergence of one of the National League's best young power hitters.
