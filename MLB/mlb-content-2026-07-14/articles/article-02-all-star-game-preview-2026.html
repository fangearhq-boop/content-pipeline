2026 MLB All-Star Game Preview — Citizens Bank Park's First Midsummer Classic

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 14, 2026

Tonight at 8 PM ET on FOX, the 96th MLB All-Star Game takes place at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia — the first time in the ballpark's 22-year history that it has hosted the Midsummer Classic. Here is what you need to know before first pitch.

The Starting Lineups

The American League will field a lineup with Mike Trout leading off from center field. Trout is making his 12th All-Star appearance and playing approximately 40 miles from his hometown of Millville, New Jersey. The rest of the AL order features Ben Rice (1B), Ernie Clement (2B), Junior Caminero (3B), Bobby Witt Jr. (SS), Riley Greene and Cody Bellinger in the outfield alongside Trout, and Yordan Alvarez as the designated hitter. Shea Langeliers handles the catching duties.

The National League will bat Kyle Schwarber first — as the designated hitter — less than 24 hours after Schwarber finished as the runner-up in Monday night's Home Run Derby. That kind of back-to-back scheduling is rare in any sport. Schwarber hit 11 home runs in the Derby to get to the final, then fell 12-11 to Jordan Walker. Tonight he turns the page. The NL order also includes Freddie Freeman (1B), Ozzie Albies (2B), Max Muncy (3B), CJ Abrams (SS), Brandon Marsh, Juan Soto, and Andy Pages in the outfield, with Drake Baldwin catching.

Cristopher Sánchez, the Phillies lefthander, starts on the mound for the National League — pitching in his home park, for the city that has six All-Stars on the roster.

The Absences

Two of baseball's biggest names are not here. Shohei Ohtani (Los Angeles Dodgers) withdrew with left knee irritation — fluid was drained and an injection administered, with expectations that he returns to pitch in the second half. Aaron Judge (New York Yankees) is out with a fractured rib that has kept him sidelined since late May, with his re-imaging results still pending. Front Office Sports called the dual absence "a sizable blow for MLB." The replacements are genuinely accomplished All-Stars; the absences simply underscore how much those two players define the current era.

The Story Behind Marsh

Brandon Marsh of the Phillies is making his first All-Star appearance. At .315 with an .871 OPS and 15 home runs, his season has been the kind of breakout that makes a player an All-Star for the first time. Playing at his home park, in a city that has followed his development closely, his All-Star debut is one of the more satisfying storylines of this game.

What Else to Watch

Junior Caminero is playing third base for the AL tonight. Caminero hit a 491-foot home run in the Derby semifinals just 24 hours ago — the longest swing of the entire night. He lost to Walker 6-5, then bounced back to take the field tonight. He's 23. The future is starting to arrive.

What's Next

The 96th MLB All-Star Game tips the second half of the 2026 MLB season. When the final out is recorded tonight at Citizens Bank Park, the regular season resumes Wednesday and the August 3 trade deadline is 20 days away. Watch FOX at 8 PM ET. The game's first appearance at Citizens Bank Park deserves the audience.
