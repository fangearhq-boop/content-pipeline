1.45 ERA in June — Jacob Misiorowski Is Having the Best Pitching Season Nobody Is Talking About
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post You should know who Jacob Misiorowski is. The fact that many people don't is entirely a function of where he pitches. The 24-year-old Washington Nationals starter leads Major League Baseball in ERA (1.45), WHIP (0.75), and strikeouts (138) entering the final week of June. He's also responsible for the only shutout on that list of leading statistics. The NL Cy Young betting markets have him at -140 — the clear frontrunner heading into the second half. The problem is that Misiorowski pitches for Washington. The Nationals are not in contention. They are not competing for a playoff spot. The games he pitches matter for his individual legacy but not for October baseball. And in a sport where the narrative engine runs on October stakes, a dominant pitcher on a non-contending team gets a fraction of the coverage he would receive if he were doing these things in Atlanta or Los Angeles or New York. That does not make the numbers less real. A 1.45 ERA is elite by any historical standard. A 0.75 WHIP means fewer than one baserunner per inning. 138 strikeouts in late June is a pace that, maintained over a full season, would produce one of the best strikeout totals in recent history. Misiorowski will make the All-Star team. He should win the Cy Young if these numbers hold through September. He deserves the coverage that his performance has earned — even if the team behind him doesn't. [Posting Window: 12:30 PM ET]
