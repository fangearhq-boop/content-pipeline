Aaron Judge Goes on the IL with a Stress Fracture. The AL MVP Race Just Changed.

By Jake Torres June 29, 2026

The Injury

The New York Yankees placed Aaron Judge on the injured list with a stress fracture in his rib. No specific return timeline has been announced, and rib stress fractures are not injuries that resolve on a predictable schedule — recovery depends on the severity of the fracture, the player's healing response, and how the team manages the return process. Significant time missed is the operative phrase coming from the organization.

The timing makes this worse than it already is. Judge's IL placement comes one day after the Yankees were swept in four games by the Boston Red Sox — a result that, on its own, was damaging enough to the team's second-half outlook. Now New York heads into July having lost its most important offensive player to an injury with an uncertain timeline.

The Yankees' Roster Situation

Judge is the third Yankees regular to hit the IL in recent weeks. Austin Wells, the team's catcher, was placed on the 10-day IL on June 6 with cervical headaches and neck stiffness. Giancarlo Stanton has been managing his own IL stint. The simultaneous absence of Judge, Stanton, and Wells removes significant production from one of the league's largest payrolls.

This is the kind of injury cluster that changes deadline calculations. The Yankees were expected to be buyers in August — a team with their payroll and postseason history rarely approaches the deadline without adding. But the calculus shifts when you're assessing a depleted roster. Do you buy aggressively to compensate for missing players, accepting that you might be adding to a team that looks different in September than it does now? Or do you wait until the full roster picture clarifies before committing top prospects?

The answer the front office arrives at will reveal a great deal about how much confidence they have in the surrounding roster to compete even without Judge in the lineup.

The AL MVP Race

The immediate consequence of Judge's IL placement is a complete reshuffling of the AL MVP market. Judge had been considered a strong candidate based on his first-half production, and his absence from the lineup for what could be a month or more removes him from serious contention unless he returns, stays healthy, and produces at an elite level down the stretch.

Yordan Alvarez (+105 betting odds) is now the frontrunner. Alvarez's first half has been exactly what Houston needs — a middle-of-the-order presence who makes the Astros offense function in a way that the roster cannot replicate with anyone else. His on-base skills, power, and RBI production give him the counting stats and rate stats that MVP voters respond to.

Bobby Witt Jr. is the other name that immediately benefits. Witt returned to the Kansas City Royals lineup on June 28 after missing six days with a Grade 1 MCL sprain — wearing a knee brace, starting at designated hitter, and wearing the unmistakable look of a player who simply refused to give the injury more time than it absolutely required. He has never been placed on the injured list in his five-year MLB career. His first-half production at shortstop has been exceptional, and if the Royals remain in playoff contention through August, Witt's MVP case will grow with each week.

What's Next

Judge will be re-evaluated as the stress fracture heals. There is no timeline being communicated publicly beyond "significant time," which could mean four weeks or it could mean longer. The Yankees' medical staff and the organization will manage this carefully given both the immediate playoff implications and the long-term significance of not rushing one of the game's most valuable players back too quickly. Watch for a more specific return window to emerge in the next week or two as the initial evaluation period concludes.
