Luis Arraez Has a 90 Percent Chance of Being Traded. Here's Who Makes Sense.

By Jake Torres June 29, 2026

The Number at the Top

The August 3 trade deadline is 35 days away. The two players generating the most deadline conversation are familiar from recent coverage, but their probability figures are worth leading with: Luis Arraez now sits at 90 percent trade probability per the latest deadline tracking — the highest of any player currently on the market. Tarik Skubal follows at 85 percent.

Those two numbers represent different kinds of players and different kinds of impact. Skubal is the elite starting pitcher every contender wants; the Tigers are extracting maximum value from a market they know is desperate. Arraez is different. He is the best contact hitter in baseball, a .330-plus average producer who has also quietly improved his defense at second base to the point where his value is no longer purely offensive. The contender that adds him gets a player who makes an entire lineup harder to retire.

What Arraez Brings

At his best, Arraez does something that almost no other hitter in modern baseball does consistently: he puts the bat on the ball. His strikeout rates are historically low, his walk rates are solid, and his ability to foul off pitches and drive the ball to all fields forces pitchers to work differently than they do against everyone else. In a lineup full of power hitters, Arraez becomes the piece that shifts the entire offensive profile — he is the table-setter and on-base machine that gives lineup construction flexibility.

His defense has been a question mark at various points in his career, but the recent improvement at second base makes him a more complete piece. A team that adds Arraez is not sacrificing a defensive liability to gain an offensive asset. They're adding a player who contributes in both directions.

Landing Spots That Make Sense

The contenders that benefit most from Arraez are the ones whose biggest lineup weakness is contact rate and on-base percentage rather than raw power. A team that already hits for power but loses too many plate appearances to strikeouts becomes significantly more dangerous when Arraez is hitting second or third in the order. He does not need to hit home runs to change what an offense looks like against elite pitching in October.

The Dodgers, Braves, and Phillies all have the prospect capital to absorb the asking price for a player of Arraez's caliber. Philadelphia has been publicly identified as a team looking to add, particularly in the outfield, but lineup versatility improvements are on the table for any of the NL contenders. In the AL, the Astros are the natural fit for a patient, high-contact hitter who makes a lineup function differently.

The Broader Deadline Picture

Beyond Arraez and Skubal, the deadline market has additional names worth tracking. Oakland's Brett Harris is facing a 5-day window to find a trade partner before the team would need to place him on outright waivers — a ticking clock situation that gives potential acquirers leverage. Philadelphia is actively looking for outfield help, creating demand that could be filled by several available pieces across the league.

The context that makes this deadline especially active is straightforward: 23 of 30 MLB teams are either leading their division or within four games of a playoff spot as of late June. That is an unusually wide buyer pool, and it creates competition for available pieces that drives asking prices up and accelerates the timeline of deal-making. By the time August 3 arrives, the majority of significant moves will have been completed — and the conversations to get there are happening now.

What's Next

The deadline picture will become more defined as July progresses and teams clarify their second-half positioning. Arraez's situation will resolve before the deadline — at 90 percent trade probability, the question is not whether he moves but where he lands and what it costs the acquiring team. Skubal's situation will run closer to the deadline as the Tigers hold out for the top-of-market package they are demanding. Watch for the first significant deadline move to happen in the next two to three weeks as the conversations that started in June produce signed agreements in July.
