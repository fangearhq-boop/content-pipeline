Matt Chapman's 10th-Inning Single Lifts Giants Over Cubs 2-1 in Sunday Night Baseball

By Marcus Cole June 9, 2026

One Swing, One Run, One Win

In the 10th inning of Sunday Night Baseball, with Jonah Cox on second as the automatic runner, Matt Chapman stepped in and lined a single to give the San Francisco Giants a 2-1 win over the Chicago Cubs. The stadium-ready moment was the kind of Sunday night baseball produces at its best — a clean, satisfying finish to a tightly pitched game that stretched into extras.

For Chapman, it was a signature at-bat in a season where he has quietly been one of the steadier presences in the Giants' lineup. For San Francisco, the win was their fourth in the last five games — a stretch that has lifted the team's confidence heading into what figures to be a critical run of summer baseball.

The Pitching Carried the Night

Before Chapman's deciding moment, the story of the game belonged to the pitchers on both sides. San Francisco held Chicago's offense to a single run through nine innings, a performance that required composure in high-leverage situations all evening. The Giants navigated the Cubs' lineup with minimal damage, limiting them to one crossing of home plate despite multiple threats across the regulation frame.

In a season when runs are flowing freely across the league — with players like Schwarber and Murakami setting home run pace records — a 1-1 tie through nine innings felt almost out of time. That was not an accident. Both teams' pitching staffs were good enough to make it happen.

What It Means for the NL Picture

San Francisco's win is important in context. The National League Wild Card race is genuinely crowded below the Braves — Atlanta has constructed such a dominant lead in the NL East that the rest of the league is effectively competing for five remaining playoff spots. The Giants staying competitive gives them options heading into the summer. A four-win stretch, including a Sunday Night showcase victory, builds momentum that a young roster can carry.

For Chicago, the loss is a reminder that June is not forgiving. The Cubs are a playoff-caliber club, but they cannot fall into a pattern of dropping winnable games against division rivals and Wild Card competitors. The Braves sit at 45-21 with a 9.5-game lead — the NL is essentially playing for the field below them. Every Cubs loss matters in that context.

What's Next

Both teams continue their respective schedules with Monday doubling as a travel day in some cases. Chicago needs to respond quickly — their next stretch of games will define where they stand in the Wild Card picture heading into the All-Star break. San Francisco looks to build on momentum with a rotation that performed well Sunday and a lineup finding its footing in June.
