Tarik Skubal Is Targeting June 12 at Cleveland — The Tigers' Ace Is Almost Back

By Ryan Calloway June 9, 2026

The Return Is Coming

Tarik Skubal is days away from walking back onto a major league mound. According to the Detroit News, the Tigers are targeting June 12 as his return date — with a start planned against the Cleveland Guardians. Five weeks after arthroscopic left elbow surgery, the two-time Cy Young Award winner appears to be healthy, sharp, and ready to pitch.

The recovery has moved faster than most expected when Skubal went under the knife on May 6. Initial projections called for a two-to-three month absence. What actually happened was something considerably more efficient: a simulated game on June 1, a High-A rehab start on June 7, and now a target date that is just five days away.

What the Rehab Start Showed

Skubal's June 7 outing for the West Michigan Whitecaps removed whatever uncertainty remained about his readiness. He pitched five innings, allowed zero runs, gave up two hits, struck out six, and did not issue a single walk. His 54-pitch outing produced 44 strikes — an 81 percent strike rate that reflects both command and confidence, not the tentative approach you'd expect from a pitcher testing an elbow fresh off surgery.

The 16 swings-and-misses were perhaps the most telling number. Hitters were not just getting out — they were getting beaten. The stuff is there. The competitive sharpness is there. Manager A.J. Hinch is now deciding whether to add one more warmup start or activate directly. Either path leads to the same destination.

What His Return Means for Detroit

The Tigers have gone 6-21 in starts without Skubal this season. That number explains both why his return matters so much for the team's day-to-day record and why the trade deadline conversation around him is so charged. A rotation with Skubal at the top can win games in October. A rotation without him is fighting to stay above .500.

Detroit's front office faces a consequential decision: compete around Skubal in the second half and hope the win total is enough to justify keeping him through August 3, or engage with the growing market of contenders who want him. The answer may depend entirely on how he performs in his first two or three starts back.

The Trade Deadline Is Listening

Every contender with rotation needs has been tracking Skubal's recovery. ESPN's Jeff Passan documented the breadth of interest in a detailed 30-team trade deadline preview published this week — the Dodgers, Yankees, Brewers, and Braves are among the organizations with reported interest. Skubal is a free agent after the season. That makes him a rental. And a rental Cy Young winner with elite stuff is the rarest commodity at any deadline.

The June 12 date against Cleveland is not just about Skubal's health. It is the starting gun for the most significant trade deadline storyline in baseball this summer.

What's Next

Watch for an official activation announcement from Detroit in the next 48-72 hours. If Hinch opts for one more warmup start, Skubal could pitch in the minors as early as Wednesday before returning by the weekend. If he goes directly to the roster, his first start back in Cleveland on June 12 will be nationally watched. Follow the Tigers' official injury report and beat reporters for the confirmation.
