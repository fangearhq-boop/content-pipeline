Yankees Adapting Without Judge: Spencer Jones Recalled, Ben Rice Producing as New York Holds Pace

By Ryan Calloway June 9, 2026

The Loss

Aaron Judge — the three-time MVP, the face of the franchise, the player who hit 62 home runs in 2022 — is on the injured list. The diagnosis is a stress fracture in his upper right rib, an injury that came without a single dramatic play, revealed instead through persistent discomfort that medical evaluation eventually identified. The timeline is 4-6 weeks before reevaluation, which means his earliest possible return is early-to-mid July.

The Yankees placed him on the IL on June 5, and immediately recalled Spencer Jones from Triple-A Scranton. The transition from three-time MVP to 25-year-old prospect was jarring in its bluntness — but it is the reality the organization has to manage.

The Replacement: Spencer Jones

Jones is a genuine prospect. The Yankees have been patient with his development, and he has the physical profile — power, speed, outfield versatility — that scouts have valued since he was drafted. His previous brief MLB stint this season produced modest results: a .167/.259/.167 slashline in limited appearances. That line is not the reason to disqualify him. It is the sample that defines the challenge.

Playing every day in Judge's absence is a fundamentally different experience than a call-up cameo. Jones has the organizational support and the coaching infrastructure to perform at a higher level with consistent plate appearances. Whether he rises to the moment or struggles until Judge returns will be one of the more interesting subplot stories of the Yankees' summer.

Ben Rice and the Supporting Cast

Rice has been a stabilizing presence in the Yankees' lineup. His production through the first half has helped offset the pressure on the lineup's other components and given the team a threat who was contributing before Judge's injury and will continue to contribute through his absence. He has been one of the more pleasant surprises in New York's lineup depth this season.

The AL East Math

New York sits at 37-26, one game behind the Tampa Bay Rays at 37-25 in the AL East. Both teams are in a legitimate race in one of baseball's tightest divisions, and every game through June carries postseason weight. The Yankees must hold their position while managing the Judge void — and the next several weeks will reveal whether the supporting cast is deep enough to do so.

The trade deadline adds another dimension. Judge's expected return arrives just before the August 3 deadline. If he's back and healthy, the Yankees' posture at the deadline looks one way. If his recovery runs longer, the calculus changes. New York does not have the luxury of waiting to find out — they must evaluate now.

What's Next
