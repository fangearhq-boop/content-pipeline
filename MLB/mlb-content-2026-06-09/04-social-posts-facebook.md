### STORY 3: Schwarber HR Race

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

Kyle Schwarber is alone at the top of the MLB home run leaderboard — and the company he's keeping is historically rare.

With Aaron Judge on the injured list (rib stress fracture) and Roki Murakami sidelined (hamstring), Schwarber has no competition for the MLB HR lead. That might sound like a convenient circumstance. But the pace he is on was historic before either player went down.

On May 15, Schwarber hit his 20th home run of the season. That was the earliest date in MLB history that a player has reached 20 home runs. Not the earliest this decade. Not the earliest in the last generation. The earliest in the history of the sport. When Barry Bonds hit 73 home runs in 2001 — the all-time record — he reached 20 on May 19. Schwarber was four days ahead of Bonds.

He is currently on pace for somewhere between 65 and 70 home runs.

The 60-home-run club in MLB history has exactly three members: Barry Bonds (73 in 2001), Mark McGwire (70 in 1998, 65 in 1999), and Sammy Sosa (66 in 1998, 64 in 2001, 63 in 1999). No player in the sport has reached 60 since Sosa in 2001. If Schwarber maintains his current pace, he would join that club.

That's not a projection. That's just the math.
