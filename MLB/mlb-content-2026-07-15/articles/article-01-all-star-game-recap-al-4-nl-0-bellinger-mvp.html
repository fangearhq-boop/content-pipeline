All-Star Game Recap: AL Shuts Out the NL 4-0 — Bellinger Wins MVP in Historic CBP Debut

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 15, 2026

The 2026 MLB All-Star Game at Citizens Bank Park was supposed to be a neutral-ground showcase. Instead, it became one of the most one-sided Midsummer Classics in recent memory.

The American League defeated the National League 4-0 on Monday night, posting the first All-Star Game shutout in 13 years — and only the eighth in the event's history. Cody Bellinger of the New York Yankees earned MVP honors. The NL managed three hits. Not one NL runner reached second base.

How It Happened

The AL took control in the first inning and never let go. Dylan Cease started for the AL; Cristopher Sanchez took the ball for the NL. Sanchez ran into immediate trouble. With runners on base, Bellinger drove in two runs with a single to center field. Ben Rice followed to drive in Bobby Witt Jr., and the AL led 3-0 before the stadium had fully settled in.

The score stayed at 3-0 until the eighth inning, when Miguel Vargas of the Chicago White Sox connected off Justin Wrobleski for the game's only home run — a solo shot that made it 4-0. That was also the only extra-base hit of the entire game.

The Pitching Performance

The story of the night was what the AL pitching staff did to the NL lineup. Eleven pitchers combined for a three-hit shutout and 15 strikeouts. The three NL hits: Juan Soto singled in the fourth, Pete Crow-Armstrong singled in the eighth, and Otto Lopez singled in the ninth. None of them advanced past first base.

It was the eighth shutout in All-Star Game history and the first since 2013. Statistically, it was one of the more dominant defensive performances in a Midsummer Classic since the format settled into its current structure.

Absences and Acknowledgments

The game was missing two of the biggest names in baseball. Shohei Ohtani (Los Angeles Dodgers) was unavailable with a left knee injury; Aaron Judge (New York Yankees) sat out with a fractured rib. Both were voted starters by fans but could not participate.

Justin Verlander, 43, attended as the AL Legend Pick — his 10th All-Star selection. He did not pitch; he's been on the 60-day injured list since the spring. But he was present in Philadelphia for what figures to be his last All-Star appearance before he retires following the 2026 season.

What's Next

The All-Star break is over. The second half of the 2026 MLB season begins this week. The AL leads the series in All-Star Game wins with 48. The trade deadline is August 3 — 19 days away — and the market for deadline-day pitching headlined by Tarik Skubal is already in full motion. Philadelphia got its first All-Star Game. The AL got its first shutout in 13 years. Cody Bellinger got a trophy.
