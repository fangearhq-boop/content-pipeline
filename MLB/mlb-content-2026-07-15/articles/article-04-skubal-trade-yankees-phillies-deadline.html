The Trade Deadline Clock Is Running — Skubal Market Opens With Yankees and Phillies in Front

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 15, 2026

The All-Star break gave baseball a brief pause. That pause is over. August 3 is 19 days away, and the most consequential trade deadline in years is now the primary conversation in the sport.

The centerpiece of that conversation is Tarik Skubal.

Where the Market Stands

The New York Yankees and Philadelphia Phillies are the frontrunners to acquire the Tigers' ace, according to multiple reports. The Chicago Cubs, Atlanta Braves, and Tampa Bay Rays are also expected to make significant pushes as the deadline approaches.

Skubal is a two-time reigning AL Cy Young Award winner — unanimous in 2024, dominant again in 2025. He's in a contract year. His expected open-market value in free agency is widely cited at $400 million or more. Extension talks with Detroit have been described by an insider as "nil."

The Tigers' asking price is understood to include near-MLB-ready position players and at least one elite prospect. That's a steep ask for a one-year rental, but Skubal is not an ordinary pitcher, and the market for ace-level starting pitching at the deadline is rarely clean.

The Complication: Skubal Wants to Stay

What makes this more complicated than a typical deadline deal is Skubal's reported position. Multiple outlets have noted that he has expressed a preference to remain in Detroit — that he wants the Tigers to be buyers rather than sellers. The Tigers have been one of the hottest teams in baseball since June 1 (19-12 in that stretch, the best record in baseball), are four games out of a Wild Card spot, and are very much in the conversation for a postseason push.

The front office faces a decision that organizations hate making: honor a franchise player's preference and push to compete, or move the most valuable trade asset in baseball and guarantee a significant return. The clock is ticking.

What's Next

The 19 days between today and August 3 will define multiple teams' trajectories. For the Yankees — who lost Aaron Judge (fractured rib) and have been searching for pitching depth — acquiring Skubal would represent a massive upgrade in their rotation and a statement of World Series intent. For the Phillies, who play in a market that hosted the All-Star Game this week and has expectations that come with it, adding a two-time Cy Young winner would be their most aggressive move in years.

For Skubal himself, the outcome of the next 19 days will determine whether he pitches in Detroit for the second half or puts on a new uniform for a team that has made a calculated bet on his ability to get them to October.
