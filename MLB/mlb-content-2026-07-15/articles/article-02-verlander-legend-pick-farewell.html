Verlander's Last All-Star Night — 21 Seasons End With a Legend Pick at Citizens Bank Park

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 15, 2026

Justin Verlander has pitched in All-Star Games. He won them. He started them. He was the reason the opposing manager changed his lineup before the first pitch. On Monday night at Citizens Bank Park, he walked out onto the field one more time — not to pitch, but to be honored.

It was his 10th All-Star selection, his first as the AL Legend Pick. It was his last All-Star appearance. He's retiring after the 2026 season.

The Career That Earned the Night

Verlander's statistical case for the Hall of Fame requires almost no argument. In 21 seasons with the Detroit Tigers (twice), Houston Astros, New York Mets, and San Francisco Giants, he compiled a record of 266-159 with a 3.33 ERA and 3,554 strikeouts.

He won three Cy Young Awards: 2011 with Detroit, 2019 and 2022 with Houston. He was the AL Most Valuable Player in 2011 — one of only a handful of pitchers to win the award in the live-ball era. He was the AL Rookie of the Year in 2006. He won two World Series rings. He was, at multiple points in his career and across three different franchises, the best pitcher in the American League.

He has not pitched since March 30, 2026 — a start against the Arizona Diamondbacks in which he lasted 3⅔ innings before hip and hamstring issues ended his evening and, ultimately, his season. That will be his final career outing. One start. 3⅔ innings. Five earned runs in a 9-6 loss. It's not the ending the career deserved, but the career itself was enough.

The Night in Philadelphia

Verlander attended All-Star week in Philadelphia with his wife, Kate Upton. He walked the red carpet. He stood on the field at Citizens Bank Park — the park where the 96th MLB All-Star Game was played, the first ever hosted there — and received the honor that comes with a decade of selections at the game's highest level.

"The opportunity to attend once again is something I'll cherish," he said.

He was surrounded by players who are still doing what he spent two decades doing — winning games, building legacies, earning their own selections. He started where they are. He finished where he is. That's the arc of a 21-year career, visible in one All-Star night.

What's Next

Verlander will retire following the conclusion of the 2026 season. He becomes eligible for the Baseball Hall of Fame in 2031. First-ballot status is widely expected — the résumé of 266 wins, 3,554 strikeouts, and three Cy Youngs is not a complicated case.

For now, the career is over. Monday night was the send-off. Philadelphia gave it a proper stage.
