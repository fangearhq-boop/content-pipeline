### STORY 1: All-Star Game Recap — AL 4, NL 0, Bellinger MVP

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

The American League beat the National League 4-0 on Monday night at Citizens Bank Park — the first All-Star Game shutout in 13 years, and the first time the Midsummer Classic has ever been held in Philadelphia.

Here's how it happened:

Dylan Cease started for the AL, Cristopher Sanchez for the NL. In the first inning, Sanchez ran into trouble immediately. Cody Bellinger — who would go on to win MVP — ripped a two-run single to center field. Ben Rice then drove in Bobby Witt Jr. to make it 3-0 after one. The game was effectively over before most viewers had finished their first inning hot dog.

Shohei Ohtani (left knee) and Aaron Judge (fractured rib) missed the game. Justin Verlander attended as the AL Legend Pick. First shutout in the All-Star Game since 2013.

This was the 96th MLB All-Star Game. It won't be remembered for closeness.

### STORY 5: Jordan Walker — What Happens After You Win the Derby

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

The 2026 Home Run Derby ended in one of the better finishes the event has ever produced.

Jordan Walker — 24 years old, Cardinals outfielder — was trailing Kyle Schwarber 11-6 heading into his final three swings in the championship round. At Citizens Bank Park. In front of a crowd that had been cheering for the hometown Phillies legend all night.

Then he hit six home runs in a row. Six. Three in the regular round, three in the bonus period. Final: Walker 12, Schwarber 11.

He is the first Cardinals player to win the Home Run Derby since the event began in 1985. The $1 million prize he took home exceeded his entire 2026 Cardinals salary. After the Derby, he told ESPN that he hoped his performance would inspire Black kids to take up baseball — a statement that went beyond the event itself.

For context on the field: Caminero of the Guardians hit the longest home run of the night — 491 feet — and still lost to Walker in the semifinals 6-5. Harper was eliminated in the first round (8 HR, lost to Schwarber 10). The format removed the shot clock and returned to swing counts: 20 in the first round, 15 in the semis, 15 in the final.

Now the second half begins. Walker returns to the Cardinals outfield with a trophy, a check, and a story. What he does with that momentum over the next two months is the follow-up question.
