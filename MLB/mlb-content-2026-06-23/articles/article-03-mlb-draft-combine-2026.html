MLB Draft Combine 2026: 335 Prospects, the Top Names, and What to Watch

By Ryan Calloway

June 23, 2026

The 2026 MLB Draft Combine opens today at Chase Field in Phoenix, and it's the most concentrated showcase of baseball talent you'll find outside of an actual MLB game. Four days, 335 prospects, and the eyes of every major league organization's scouting department on the same field at the same time.

This is the fourth consecutive year Chase Field has hosted the event, and the format has become a reliable preview of the MLB Draft that follows. In 2025, 256 of the 309 players who participated were drafted — an 82.8 percent rate, including 87 of the first 100 picks. If you want to understand who will be shaping big league rosters five years from now, this week is where you start.

By the Numbers

335 total prospects are attending: 195 collegiate players and 140 high school athletes. Of MLB's top 200 draft prospects, 192 are in Phoenix this week. Seven of the top 10 are participating.

Live coverage starts today on MLB Network at 12 PM ET, with the event also streaming on MLB.TV, MLB.com, and the MLB App. For fans who've never watched a Combine before, it's a mix of athletic testing (60-yard dash, arm strength measurements, fielding drills) and batting practice — a showcase format that often reveals things game tape can't capture about a prospect's tools.

The Top Prospects to Watch

No. 1 on Baseball America's BA 500 is UCLA shortstop Roch Cholowsky. He's the kind of premium shortstop prospect that generates multi-team bidding wars at the top of the draft — elite glove, plus arm, with a bat that projects to produce at the major league level. The team with the No. 1 overall pick will spend this week confirming what they already expect to see.

No. 2 on the board is Texas prep shortstop Grady Emerson — a high school prospect with a premium defensive profile and the bat speed scouts drool over. High school shortstops at this tier are high-risk, high-reward selections. Emerson represents the kind of ceiling-based bet that teams in rebuild mode are willing to make.

The top pitcher in this class is UC Santa Barbara right-hander Jackson Flora. He carries the power arm and advanced feel for pitching that separates top pitching prospects from the rest. His Combine performance will be closely watched by every contender that needs starting pitching — and also by every rebuilder who wants to draft him and develop him.

Why the Combine Matters Beyond the Names

The biggest impact of the Combine isn't always on the players who are already No. 1 on the board. It's on the players who move up or down based on what scouts see in person this week.

A player who runs a great 60-yard dash time, shows arm strength that exceeds what was on tape, or demonstrates bat speed that jumps off the testing equipment can see his draft stock rise significantly. Conversely, a prospect whose physical tools don't match his college production — or who shows inconsistency in athletic testing — might see teams re-evaluate his slot.

The 2025 Combine had several examples of players who weren't projected as first-round picks entering the event and left as first-rounders based on what they showed. Those are the stories to watch this week: not just the names that were already famous, but the ones that might surprise you by the time the draft arrives.

What's Next

The Combine runs through Friday, June 26. The MLB Draft itself follows later this summer — the specific dates and broadcast details are expected to be announced shortly. The players who impress most this week will see their draft stocks finalized in the days that follow, as teams meet internally to discuss what they observed and recalibrate their boards.

Today's MLB Network coverage starting at noon ET is the best way to watch. Follow along and see who emerges as the week's standout performers — they're the ones you'll be tracking on rosters in two to five years.
