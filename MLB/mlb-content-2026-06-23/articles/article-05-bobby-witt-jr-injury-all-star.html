Bobby Witt Jr.: IL Decision Looms, All-Star Hopes in Limbo

By Jake Torres

June 23, 2026

Bobby Witt Jr. was expected to be reevaluated Monday before the Royals' series opener against the Tampa Bay Rays. The 26-year-old shortstop has been dealing with a Grade 1 MCL sprain in his right knee since sliding for a ball in a game against the St. Louis Cardinals last week. Manager Matt Quatraro called the diagnosis "best case scenario." He's been listed as day-to-day.

None of that changes the reality: the Kansas City Royals' most important player has been on the bench, and every day that continues is a day closer to a potential 10-day IL stint.

The Injury in Context

A Grade 1 MCL sprain is the mildest form of an MCL injury — a ligament stretch without a tear, with the medial meniscus intact. Recovery typically runs one to three weeks with limited activity. For most players, it's the kind of injury you can play through with pain management. For a shortstop who covers ground aggressively on every play, every lateral movement reopens the risk.

Witt was injured sliding for a ball in the hole. His game is built on athleticism, range, and explosiveness — exactly the physical attributes that put stress on a knee. Even day-to-day status requires the medical staff to clear him for the movements that make him the player he is, not just walking from the dugout to the batter's box.

Maikel Garcia has covered at shortstop in Witt's absence. The Royals are not lost without him — they're a functional team — but they're not quite the same threat either.

The All-Star Picture

Phase 1 of MLB All-Star voting closes Thursday, June 25. Witt was building an AL MVP-caliber season entering the injury: 4.5 fWAR heading into mid-June, which put him at or near the top of the AL position player leaderboard. He's an established All-Star who should be a lock under normal circumstances.

But voters don't always separate "deserving" from "healthy." A player who misses a week or more heading into voting season can lose ballot momentum to players who are producing visibly right now. It's an imperfect system, and Witt may be on the wrong side of it this week.

If the Royals put him on the 10-day IL starting today, his return window would be July 3. The All-Star Game is July 14. He could play and still make the team if the IL timeline holds — and managers have some discretion in selecting injured players who are recovering. But it requires everything to go well from here.

The MVP Race Implications

Witt was positioned as one of the legitimate AL MVP candidates entering this week. In a race that hadn't clearly crowned a frontrunner by mid-June, his 4.5 fWAR and the package of skills he brings — elite shortstop defense, plus bat, plus baserunning — made him the kind of complete-value player voters notice.

Missing games in June doesn't disqualify anyone from the MVP race if the season as a whole is strong enough. But it does allow competitors to gain ground in the counting stats — home runs, RBI, hits — that some voters weight heavily regardless of the broader context. The next two weeks matter for Witt's candidacy in a way they wouldn't if he were healthy.

What's Next

The Royals' decision on the IL will tell us how serious they are about this injury. Day-to-day suggests they think he can return before the 10-day threshold. IL placement suggests they're prioritizing health over a narrow window of games. Either way, Witt himself will be the best indicator of where this is headed — his body knows before the medical staff officially announces anything.

For Royals fans: watch the injury report updates daily this week. For All-Star voters with two days left: cast a ballot now before the situation gets more complicated. For anyone tracking the AL MVP race: Witt's health through the second half will be a major variable in how that conversation develops.
