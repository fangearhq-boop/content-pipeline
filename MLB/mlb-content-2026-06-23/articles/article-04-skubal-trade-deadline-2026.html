Skubal Trade Buzz Heats Up: Tigers Want One Elite Prospect, Dodgers Lead the Race

By Marcus Cole

June 23, 2026

The August 3 MLB trade deadline is 41 days away. Every year, the deadline has a defining centerpiece — the trade that frames everything else. In 2026, that centerpiece is Tarik Skubal, and the conversation around what it will take to land him is already consuming the league's front offices.

Skubal is a Cy Young Award winner pitching for a Detroit Tigers team that is going to sell at the deadline. He will be a free agent after this season. His rental value — already high given his résumé — has made him the most talked-about trade piece in baseball.

What the Tigers Want

Multiple reports indicate the Tigers' asking price for Skubal is one elite prospect plus two to three additional pieces. This pricing mirrors what other top-tier rental ace trades have commanded in recent years — the Juan Soto precedent looms large in every comparable discussion, though pitchers typically move for different prospect profiles than position players.

One elite prospect means a top-10 type in a given organization's system — a player that teams are reluctant to move. The Tigers know they have leverage: a healthy Cy Young-caliber starting pitcher who is available is exceedingly rare, and the organizations bidding for him have the prospect depth to make the price.

The Dodgers as Frontrunners

The consensus across reporting is that the Los Angeles Dodgers are the favorites to land Skubal. This is both intuitive and alarming depending on your rooting interest. The Dodgers have the prospect capital, the financial flexibility, the competitive window, and the front-office infrastructure to execute a trade of this complexity quickly.

A Skubal trade to Los Angeles would give a Dodgers rotation that already includes Yoshinobu Yamamoto and Tyler Glasnow yet another elite arm. It would be the kind of "adding a fourth ace to a rotation that already has three" move that makes a team essentially unbeatable in a short series.

Whether the baseball gods allow that kind of concentration at one franchise is a separate question from whether the Dodgers will try.

The Rest of the Field

The New York Yankees, Tampa Bay Rays, San Diego Padres, and Milwaukee Brewers are all reportedly in on Skubal. That's five legitimate bidding organizations for one pitcher, which is exactly the kind of competition that drives prices higher and makes deadline coverage appointment viewing.

The Yankees need a starter to complement Gerrit Cole. The Rays are perpetual contenders who find ways to acquire pitching. The Padres have shown a willingness to trade significant prospects for impact talent. The Brewers — who perennially outperform their market — would see Skubal as a chance to go from good to championship-caliber.

Any of them landing Skubal would reshape the second-half picture.

The Skubal Wild Card

One element that distinguishes this deadline trade from a typical rental: Skubal reportedly "warned" the Tigers amid trade chatter. The specific nature of that warning isn't fully public, but it adds an element of player agency to a situation that organizations sometimes treat as purely transactional.

Players have no-trade clauses, contractual protections, and informal leverage in negotiations. If Skubal has a preference for where he plays — or where he doesn't — that becomes a variable the Tigers and potential acquiring teams need to navigate. It's not a dealbreaker, but it's a factor.

The Red Sox also appear to be moving into seller mode, with closer Aroldis Chapman emerging as their most likely trade chip. Every contender needing bullpen help will be evaluating Chapman as a complementary piece to whatever rotation addition they pursue.

What's Next

The next six weeks will be defined by this race. Watch for which teams make aggressive trade moves — acquiring depth pieces now signals that a team is building toward a bigger move closer to August 3. Watch for injury news on any of the major contenders, since a key injury can turn a buyer into a seller or change which position of need is most urgent.

The Skubal trade will likely happen in the final week before the deadline. When it does, it will be the largest single roster transaction of the 2026 season — and it will tell us a lot about which team is most serious about winning a World Series this October.
