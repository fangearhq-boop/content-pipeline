27 Days to the Deadline: Why the Braves Are the Frontrunner for Tarik Skubal

By Jake Torres | July 7, 2026

The August 3 trade deadline is 27 days away. Tarik Skubal is 85 percent likely to be traded. And the Atlanta Braves — who already have Chris Sale at the top of their rotation — are the frontrunner to acquire him.

If it happens, Atlanta's starting rotation becomes the most dangerous in baseball.

Why Skubal Is Available

The Detroit Tigers are 38-49. They are not a playoff team, they do not project to become one, and their best starting pitcher is heading into free agency at the end of this season. Skubal is a Scott Boras client. The Tigers are not going to extend him. The choice for Detroit is simple: trade Skubal now for a substantial return, or watch him walk for nothing in the offseason.

ESPN's Jeff Passan puts the trade probability at 85 percent. That is not a number that leaves much room for doubt. Skubal is going somewhere — the only question is where.

Why the Braves

According to Bob Nightengale, the Braves are the frontrunner, a conclusion confirmed by rival executives around the league. Atlanta has the prospect capital to meet Detroit's asking price, the organizational conviction to move quickly, and the most obvious need in a pitching market: a No. 2 starter capable of carrying weight behind Sale in a playoff rotation.

Chris Sale made his 10th All-Star selection this season. He is still one of the best pitchers in the National League. But Sale is 37 years old, and Atlanta's postseason aspirations require depth behind him. Skubal — 29, in his prime, with a full season of dominance behind him — would give the Braves exactly what they need.

Sale plus Skubal would be one of the most formidable 1-2 starting combinations assembled in the recent history of the sport. For a team built to win now, that is the kind of move that defines an October run.

Who Is Out

The field of potential suitors is narrowing fast. The Los Angeles Dodgers, despite obvious pitching depth concerns, are believed to be not in on a Skubal deal. One source confirmed the Yankees are out with more finality: "no chance, none."

That leaves Atlanta with less competition for Detroit's attention than the initial speculation suggested. A two-team negotiation is more tractable than a bidding war, and the Braves know it.

The Price

Nothing about this deal will come cheap. Multiple reports have characterized the asking price as equivalent to the CC Sabathia-level acquisition — one elite prospect plus two or three additional top-10 prospects from the acquiring team's system. Detroit is not going to sell their ace at a discount, especially when they know the Braves need him more than any other team and have the system to pay full price.

Atlanta's farm system is deep enough to absorb the cost without gutting their future. That is precisely why they are positioned as the frontrunner.

27 Days

The All-Star break buys both sides a moment to breathe before the final stretch of deadline negotiations begins in earnest. But the clock is running, and everyone in baseball knows Atlanta and Detroit are the most likely endpoints of this deal.

Twenty-seven days until Sale and Skubal could be teammates. Twenty-seven days until one of the most significant pitching acquisitions of the trade deadline era either happens — or doesn't.

The Braves are the frontrunner. The price is known. Now it comes down to whether Atlanta pulls the trigger.
