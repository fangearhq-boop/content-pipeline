From Replacement Slot to First Starter: Nick Kurtz Is Ready for His All-Star Moment

By Jake Torres | July 7, 2026

Vladimir Guerrero Jr. needed to rest his back. Nick Kurtz needed an opening. Both found what they were looking for.

The Oakland Athletics first baseman will start the 2026 MLB All-Star Game on July 14 at Citizens Bank Park — his first All-Star selection in what is only his second Major League season. He is 23 years old. He is one of the most complete offensive players in the American League. And now he gets the national stage to prove it.

How He Got Here

Guerrero, a six-time All-Star, was selected to the 2026 roster before making the decision to step aside ahead of the break. A lower back injury required rest rather than risk, and the veteran made the right call. His withdrawal promoted Kurtz from All-Star reserve to starter — from a roster spot to first in the lineup.

It is the kind of opening that changes careers, and Kurtz has more than earned it.

What the Numbers Say

Through approximately 87 games this season, Nick Kurtz has put together a first half that speaks for itself:

  • 20 home runs
  • 66 RBI — leads the American League
  • 76 walks — leads the American League
  • .424 on-base percentage
  • .949 OPS
  • 61 runs scored — leads the American League

Leading the AL in both RBI and walks is an unusual combination. It means Kurtz is doing damage when pitchers challenge him and taking his base when they do not. He is forcing outcomes either way. That is the kind of hitter who does not have weaknesses a defense can exploit over 162 games.

A Quiet Team Making Noise

The Oakland Athletics are having a very good season — one that has not always made the front pages in a year dominated by trade deadline speculation and All-Star storylines elsewhere. Kurtz is a primary reason why.

The A's drafted Kurtz second overall in the 2023 draft. He arrived quickly and made an immediate impression in his first season. This year, in his second, he has taken the next step. At 23, he is building toward what could be a long run as one of the best first basemen in baseball.

The Stage

Citizens Bank Park on July 14. Fox. 8 PM ET. A Phillies crowd that will be loud and engaged for everything the night has to offer. This is the stage Nick Kurtz gets.

He did not ask for it, exactly — but he made himself impossible to pass over, and circumstances delivered the rest. Now the only thing left to do is play.

Guerrero will be watching. So will the rest of the American League. So will Oakland, quietly proud of the player they built.

Nick Kurtz gets his moment on Tuesday. He has been building toward it all year.
