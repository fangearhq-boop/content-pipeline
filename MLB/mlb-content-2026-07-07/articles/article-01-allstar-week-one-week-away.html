One Week to All-Star: Your Complete Guide to MLB's Midsummer Classic in Philadelphia

By Marcus Cole | July 7, 2026

One week from today, Citizens Bank Park will host the best players in baseball. The 2026 MLB All-Star Week is here — and for the first time since 1996, Philadelphia gets the Midsummer Classic.

Here is everything you need to know before the first pitch.

The Schedule

The Home Run Derby takes place on Monday, July 13, at Citizens Bank Park. For the first time in history, the Derby streams live on Netflix. Start time to be confirmed, but expect a prime-time slot for what figures to be one of the most-watched sporting events of the summer.

The All-Star Game follows on Tuesday, July 14, also at Citizens Bank Park. First pitch is at 8 PM ET on FOX. Philadelphia is the backdrop; 90,000 eyeballs from across the country will be watching.

The Rosters

The 2026 rosters feature 26 first-time All-Stars — one of the freshest fields in years. The game belongs as much to the next generation as to the established names.

The teams leading the way in representation: the Los Angeles Dodgers, Atlanta Braves, and Philadelphia Phillies each sent five players to Midsummer Classic. In the American League, the Toronto Blue Jays, Tampa Bay Rays, and New York Yankees each have four.

The Starters

Shohei Ohtani of the Dodgers led all players in voting and is an automatic starter. Ernie Clement of the Toronto Blue Jays led the American League ballot.

There is one significant change in the AL infield. Vladimir Guerrero Jr., a six-time All-Star, made the decision to step aside and rest a lower back injury rather than play through the All-Star break. His withdrawal opened the starting slot at first base for Nick Kurtz of the Oakland Athletics — a 23-year-old in just his second MLB season who has put together one of the best offensive first halves in the American League.

The Storylines

The starting pitching matchup is set to feature Ernie Clement as the AL's top vote-getter, but the pitching headline belongs to Tarik Skubal. With the August 3 trade deadline 27 days away, every All-Star Game appearance this week carries an extra layer of context for players who might be wearing different uniforms by August.

The Home Run Derby sets the table. Kyle Schwarber leads MLB with more than 30 home runs this season — and on July 13, he gets to swing at his own home ballpark. Junior Caminero, the 2025 runner-up, is confirmed and back to compete. Aaron Judge is out due to a rib fracture.

A new swing-bracket format replaces the traditional Derby structure, adding another layer of intrigue to a competition that needed no additional drama.

Philadelphia's Moment

The last time Philadelphia hosted the All-Star Game was 1996 — the year before Citizens Bank Park existed. Veterans Stadium was the venue that year. This city has grown, this ballpark has aged into one of the sport's best venues, and the Phillies faithful are ready to make noise.

The 2026 Phillies are one of the most All-Star-laden teams in baseball, with five representatives earning their place. The home crowd will have plenty to cheer for.

Seven days. The countdown is on.
