Jacob Misiorowski Is Putting Together One of the Best Pitching First Halves in MLB History

By Ryan Calloway | July 7, 2026

The Milwaukee Brewers are the fastest team in franchise history to 50 wins. Their starting pitcher has the best ERA in baseball, the best WHIP in baseball, and the highest single-pitch velocity ever recorded for a starter in the Statcast era.

Jacob Misiorowski may be the best story in the sport right now — and the second half of 2026 has not even started.

The Numbers Are Historic

Through 15 starts this season, Misiorowski has built a first half that stands alongside the best in recent memory:

  • 1.45–1.50 ERA — best in all of Major League Baseball, best in Brewers franchise history through 15 starts
  • 0.81 WHIP — best in all of Major League Baseball
  • 105.5 mph max velocity (June 26) — the highest ever recorded for a starting pitcher in the Statcast era
  • 146 strikeouts — among the MLB leaders

Leading baseball in both ERA and WHIP simultaneously is rare. Leading baseball in both ERA and WHIP while also generating the fastest pitch any starter has thrown in the history of modern pitch-tracking is extraordinary. Misiorowski is doing all of that, and the Brewers are reaping the results.

A Team Ahead of Schedule

Milwaukee reached 50 wins faster than any team in franchise history. Their division lead reflects the depth of the roster — William Contreras is also an All-Star selection this year — but Misiorowski is the engine.

In an era of bullpen management and limited pitch counts, the Brewers have found a pitcher who can take the ball every fifth day and dominate. That scarcity is what makes Misiorowski so valuable, and why his All-Star status is clouded by workload concerns heading into the break. Milwaukee is not going to risk their best asset for an exhibition.

The Turang Subplot

There is a storyline adjacent to the Misiorowski celebration that has Milwaukee fans less than thrilled. Brice Turang, the Brewers' best position player by advanced metrics, was not selected to the 2026 All-Star Game. He ranks No. 7 in NL bWAR — a top-ten performance that the All-Star selection process passed over entirely.

Turang's omission has been widely called one of the biggest snubs of the year. It is a legitimate argument. The process is imperfect, and sometimes the best players in a given half get left off the roster. It does not diminish what Misiorowski has built — but it is a reminder that the Brewers are deeper than even their All-Star representation suggests.

What Comes Next

Misiorowski's All-Star status remains in doubt as the Brewers manage his workload. Whether he pitches in Philadelphia or not, the second half begins shortly after the break — and Milwaukee will need every start they can get from him to hold their division lead and make a postseason push.

He is 24 years old, still in the early chapters of what could be a very long career. If he maintains even a shadow of his first-half performance into August and September, the Brewers will be a serious October team.

One pitcher. One historically dominant first half. And a franchise that may be right on the edge of something special.
