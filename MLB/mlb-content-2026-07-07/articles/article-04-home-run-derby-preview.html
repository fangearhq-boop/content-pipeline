Home Run Derby 2026: Schwarber, Caminero and a New Format on Netflix

By Marcus Cole | July 7, 2026

Six days until the Home Run Derby. Kyle Schwarber leads Major League Baseball with more than 30 home runs. The Derby is at his home ballpark. This is not a coincidence — this is the setup for a moment.

The 2026 MLB Home Run Derby takes place July 13 at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia, and for the first time in history, it streams live on Netflix. Everything about this event is different this year — the platform, the format, and the electricity in the stands at Citizens Bank Park when Schwarber steps into the box.

The Headliner

Kyle Schwarber has been the most dangerous home run hitter in baseball this season. His 30-plus long balls lead the Majors, and the left-handed slugger has spent all season hitting them at the same ballpark where he will compete on July 13.

No competitor in recent Derby history has entered the event as the active league leader while swinging in his home park. The setup writes itself: a Phillies faithful crowd that will roar at every ball that leaves the yard, and one of the sport's most prolific power hitters getting to feed off that energy all night.

The Returner

Junior Caminero is confirmed and back for more. The Tampa Bay Rays slugger finished as the 2025 Derby runner-up and enters this year's competition as one of the most explosive young power hitters in baseball. He did not win in his first attempt, and that is the kind of unfinished business that makes for a compelling follow-up chapter.

The Absence

Aaron Judge will not compete. The New York Yankees slugger suffered a rib fracture and is out of the Derby entirely. Judge's absence is a loss for the event — he is one of the most anticipated participants whenever he enters — but it also creates opportunity for the field to step forward.

The New Format

The traditional Home Run Derby format is gone. A new swing-bracket structure takes its place, rewarding different skills than the old round-based format and adding strategic dimension to what has historically been a straightforward power display.

The specifics of the swing-bracket will be explained to fans ahead of the event, but the concept shifts the Derby toward something closer to a head-to-head competition with more variables in play. It is a risk for an event that has worked for decades — and a potential reward for viewers looking for something fresh.

Netflix and the Global Stage

This is the first Derby ever aired on Netflix. The streaming platform reaches more than 300 million households globally, and the Derby has never had this kind of international distribution. For fans in markets that have not historically had easy access to the Mid-Summer Classic, July 13 represents a genuine first look at the event.

It is the right moment for the Derby to take that step. The combination of Schwarber in his home park, a new format, and a streaming platform that can deliver the spectacle to the world is exactly what the event needed to feel fresh again.

Six Days

Citizens Bank Park. July 13. Netflix. One of the best power hitters in baseball swinging in front of his home crowd.

The Home Run Derby does not need to be more complicated than that — and this year, it is not.
