The Braves Have Beaten the Cubs Twice. Game 3 Today Will Tell Us Who the NL's Best Team Actually Is.

By Jake Torres | May 14, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Chicago Cubs entered Atlanta this week with one of the most historically impressive starts in the National League in years. They had already posted two separate 10-game winning streaks in the season's first 40 decisions — a feat the NL had not seen since the 1955 Brooklyn Dodgers. They had 27 wins. They were playing as well as any team in baseball in April.

The Braves were not impressed. Atlanta has won both games of the series, holding the Cubs to a combined 3 runs over 18 innings, and now sit at approximately 30-13 — the best record in the National League. Game 3 is scheduled for today at Truist Park.

Two Games Tell the Story

Game 1 was a declaration. Grant Holmes and three Atlanta relievers combined to hold the Cubs to just one hit in a 5-2 Braves victory. Mike Yastrzemski provided the offensive damage with a two-run home run in the fifth inning. Austin Riley added another. The Cubs, who had been generating offense consistently all season, were neutralized entirely.

Game 2 on Wednesday built on that foundation with a 4-1 Atlanta win. The Braves controlled the game from the early innings, adding to a lead that the Cubs could never overcome. What was a tight NL standings race two days ago has opened up, at least temporarily, in Atlanta's favor.

What Makes the Braves This Good

Atlanta's case for being the NL's best team runs through their pitching. The Braves lead the National League in fewest runs allowed per game at 3.44 — the best mark in the senior circuit. That number is built from a combination of starting pitching depth and a bullpen that consistently protects leads.

When you allow fewer than 3.5 runs per game against a full NL schedule, you win a lot of baseball games. The Braves' offense has enough depth — Riley, Yastrzemski, Ronald Acuña Jr., Matt Olson — to provide support when the pitching staff delivers a solid effort. The combination has produced a 30-13 record that reflects a team operating without significant weaknesses.

The Cubs' April brilliance was real. Their two 10-game win streaks represented genuine quality, not schedule manipulation or statistical noise. But the Cubs' offense — dependent on consistent run production from a specific lineup configuration — is not immune to quality pitching.

What Today's Game Decides

A third Atlanta win would complete the sweep of a top-tier opponent and send a clear message about where the NL's hierarchy actually stands at this point in the season. A Cubs win would protect their record, avoid the sweep, and maintain the argument that Chicago's April was meaningful, not just a favorable schedule run.

The NL playoff picture in May is still entirely malleable. Three months of regular season remain, and neither team will look in June exactly as they do in May. But series like this one — two of the NL's best records meeting in a three-game test — produce useful signal. Through two games, the Braves are sending it clearly.

What's Next

Game 3 of the Braves-Cubs series takes place today at Truist Park in Atlanta. The Cubs will need a win to avoid the sweep and protect a record that, while still excellent, has taken a dent this week. Following the series, both teams return to their respective schedules before likely crossing paths again in the second half. Today's result will be a reference point in the NL playoff conversation for the next several weeks.
