### STORY 1: Schwarber's Quest — Going for 6 Straight with His Manager's MLB Record in Sight

**Tier:** 1
**Angle:** Kyle Schwarber owns the Philadelphia Phillies franchise record outright tonight — if he can hit a home run for the sixth consecutive game at Fenway Park. The previous Phillies record of five straight was shared with legends Dick Allen, Mike Schmidt, Bobby Abreu, Chase Utley, Rhys Hoskins, Odúbel Herrera, and Trea Turner. He tied it on May 12. Now he chases solo possession. But there's a more compelling layer: the MLB record for consecutive HR games is eight, set by Ken Griffey Jr. (1993), Dale Long (1956), and Don Mattingly (1987). Mattingly is now Schwarber's interim manager — the man whose record could eventually be in Schwarber's sights is writing the lineup card. A different angle from yesterday's "tied the record" coverage: today is about the trajectory, the narrative, and the Mattingly connection.

### STORY 2: Rico Garcia — The Expansion Era's Most Untouchable Reliever

**Tier:** 1
**Angle:** Somewhere in Baltimore's bullpen, there is a reliever who has allowed one hit to the first 64 batters he has faced in 2026. That hit was a home run. Every ball in play since then has been an out. Rico Garcia is the first pitcher in the Expansion Era to allow at most one hit over 64 consecutive batters. He is also the first pitcher in MLB history to allow at most one total hit in his first 20 appearances in a season. His 0.47 ERA and .018 batting average allowed are the kinds of numbers that look like typos. The backstory makes the performance more remarkable: Garcia was a 30th-round pick in 2016 who had Tommy John surgery, was a free agent seven times, and was claimed off waivers four times — three of them in 2025 alone.
