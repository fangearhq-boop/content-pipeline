### STORY 1: Schwarber's Quest — Manager's Record in Sight

#### Long-Form Post

There's a moment in sports where the story writes itself so cleanly that you almost have to check if it's real.

Kyle Schwarber just tied the Philadelphia Phillies franchise record for consecutive games with a home run — five in a row, matching the mark held by Mike Schmidt, Dick Allen, Bobby Abreu, Chase Utley, Rhys Hoskins, Odúbel Herrera, and Trea Turner. He tied it Tuesday night at Fenway with a first-inning blast off Brayan Bello, propelling the Phillies to a 2-1 win. He now has 17 home runs on the season, the most in Major League Baseball.

Tonight he goes for six straight. If he gets there, he stands alone in Phillies history.

And then the real question begins: can he chase down the MLB record?

The record is eight consecutive games with a home run. Three men have done it: Dale Long of the 1956 Pirates, Don Mattingly of the 1987 Yankees, and Ken Griffey Jr. of the 1993 Mariners.

Don Mattingly is currently Kyle Schwarber's manager.

The same man who set the record 39 years ago as a Yankee is now writing the lineup card for the player who could eventually break it. Baseball is not subtle about its storytelling.

Tonight at Fenway: Schwarber, sixth game, sole possession of the Phillies record in reach. ⚾
