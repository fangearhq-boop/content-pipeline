### Long-Form Post ⚾ ARE WE REALLY DOING THIS? ⚾ Kyle Schwarber hit his 20th home run on May 15. Not a big deal on its own — except that it's the earliest any player in the history of Major League Baseball has reached 20 home runs. Earlier than Barry Bonds in his 73-home run season. Earlier than Mark McGwire. Earlier than Sammy Sosa. Earlier than all of them. Through his first 49 games of 2026, Schwarber is on pace for 66 home runs. Let's put that in context. The 60-HR club has three members: Bonds (73), McGwire (70), and Sosa (66). No player has joined the club since 2001 — 25 years ago. The current generation of fans has essentially never watched a player seriously threaten 60 home runs in a clean season. Schwarber is doing it. The Phillies outfielder isn't just a power hitter running hot. He set a milestone on the calendar that no one in the history of baseball has cleared before. The numbers are not just impressive. They are unprecedented in when they arrived. Is the pace sustainable? Maybe not. But the conversation is legitimate. The math is real. And Schwarber is the reason you should be watching the Phillies every night. [Will Schwarber reach 60?
