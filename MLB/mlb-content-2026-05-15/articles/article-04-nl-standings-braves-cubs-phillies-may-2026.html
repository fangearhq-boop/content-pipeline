Braves Lead, Cubs Lurking, Phillies Rising: The NL Race in May 2026

By Marcus Cole | May 15, 2026

The National League is sorting itself out in May, and the picture is worth examining carefully. Three franchises with large fan bases, deep histories, and genuine 2026 ambitions are writing very different stories right now — and all three of them are pointing toward a summer that could define the NL postseason race.

Atlanta, Chicago, and Philadelphia are the three NL narratives you need to know.

The Braves: Baseball's Best Record

The Atlanta Braves are approximately 30-14 — the NL's best record and arguably baseball's most complete team. They lead the major leagues in fewest runs allowed per game, which means their pitching staff is performing at an elite level while their lineup, anchored by Austin Riley, Ozzie Albies, and the rest of a dangerous batting order, generates enough offense to support it.

They lost to the Cubs 2-0 on Thursday night — a combination shutout where Chicago's Ben Brown was brilliant for four innings and Ian Happ provided the only home run of the game. It was a loss that ended a series the Braves had been winning 2-0 after taking the first two games. But one series finale loss against a very good opponent does not dent what Atlanta has built. The Braves have been the most consistent team in the NL all spring, and the standings reflect it.

The Cubs: 28-16 and Closing the Gap

The Chicago Cubs went through a rough stretch — four consecutive losses, including two to Atlanta before Thursday's turnaround — but they are still 28-16 and only two games behind the NL's best team. When you consider that two separate 10-game win streaks marked their first 40 decisions (the first team to achieve that since the 1955 Brooklyn Dodgers), this is an organization that has demonstrated the capacity for sustained elite performance.

The emergence of Ben Brown as a starter is the most interesting development in the Cubs' recent run. A pitcher who spent the first 12 appearances of 2026 working out of the bullpen has now thrown 8 scoreless innings in his two rotation starts, against quality competition. If Brown maintains this level into June, Chicago's rotation depth improves significantly — and a team that is already 28-16 with room to grow is a genuine NL title contender.

The Phillies: A Team Reborn Under Mattingly

The Philadelphia story is the most dramatic of the three. On April 28, the Phillies fired manager Rob Thomson after a 9-19 start that looked like the beginning of a lost season. Thomson had guided them to back-to-back NL East titles and four straight playoff appearances. His dismissal was difficult for the organization and the fan base.

Then Don Mattingly took over as interim manager, and something changed. Phillies 12-4 under Mattingly. Kyle Schwarber, now leading baseball with 18 home runs and a six-game consecutive HR streak, looks like the best hitter in the sport. The lineup is producing. The pitching, including Jesus Luzardo's six shutout innings Thursday night, is performing to its talent level.

At 22-22, the Phillies are a .500 team — not yet in the wild card picture by comfortable margin. But the trajectory under Mattingly is undeniable. A team that was heading toward a disappointing collapse in April is now playing winning baseball, and every night Schwarber is out there chasing a record adds fuel to the emotional momentum that powers stretches like this.

What This Means for the Rest of May

The Braves, Cubs, and Phillies are not all in the same division — Atlanta and Philadelphia share the NL East; Chicago is in the NL Central — so their competition is indirect until the postseason. But the quality of these three teams shapes the entire league. If the Phillies continue their run, they become a Wild Card threat that complicates the NL East picture. If the Cubs maintain their pace, the Central belongs to them. If the Braves keep winning — and there is no evidence yet that they will stop — they will enter the second half of the season with the NL's most formidable record and the clearest path to the pennant.

What's Next

All three teams open Rivalry Weekend Friday. The Cubs visit the White Sox in the Crosstown Classic, carrying their Thursday momentum into a series against South Side rivals. The Braves host another series at Truist Park. The Phillies continue their run with Schwarber's record chase front and center. The NL standings in May are worth following closely — by July, these three franchises may be the entire conversation.
