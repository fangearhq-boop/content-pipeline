Elly De La Cruz Is Baseball's Best Shortstop Right Now — A Mid-May Reality Check

By Jake Torres | May 13, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The conversation about the best shortstop in baseball tends to orbit the same familiar names — Francisco Lindor, Corey Seager, Trea Turner. But through the first six weeks of the 2026 season, one player has separated himself from the field by every available measure, and his name is Elly De La Cruz.

The 24-year-old Cincinnati Reds shortstop entered Wednesday hitting .288 with 10 home runs, 29 runs scored, 29 RBI, and 9 stolen bases. He leads all shortstops in Baseball-Reference Wins Above Replacement. The advanced metrics are equally convincing: a 93.9 mph average exit velocity, 50.9% hard-hit rate, 15.5% barrel rate, and a .383 wOBA. These are not the numbers of a player having a hot week. They are the numbers of a player having an extraordinary season.

A Historical Pace

What makes De La Cruz's start historically significant is the combination of power and speed he's produced simultaneously. He became the first shortstop since 1900 to hit five or more home runs and steal five or more bases in his team's first 16 games of a season. That combination — the kind that scouts talk about as a "five-tool" profile — is genuinely rare at any position, and at shortstop it's nearly unprecedented in the modern era.

The power numbers command respect on their own. Ten home runs and a .658 slugging percentage through mid-May, from a shortstop, while also leading the position in WAR — that's an MVP-level contribution. But pair it with nine stolen bases and you have a player who changes games in ways that don't fit neatly into any single statistical category.

The Quiet Case for National Attention

De La Cruz plays in Cincinnati. The Reds are not a team that dominates national coverage, and the 2026 home run race narratives have centered on Judge, Murakami, and now Schwarber. Meanwhile, De La Cruz has been building something quietly in the Great American Ball Park that deserves a wider audience.

His hard-hit rate of 50.9% places him among the top contact-quality hitters in baseball. His barrel rate of 15.5% is elite regardless of position. When you produce those numbers from the six spot in the batting order, on a team that doesn't get marquee national coverage, it's easy for the work to go unnoticed. But the WAR leaderboard doesn't care about market size.

What's Next

The question for De La Cruz and the Reds is whether the team around him can get into postseason contention. The Reds are not a lock to make the playoffs, and without that team context, the individual performance can feel like a footnote in someone else's pennant race. But if the Reds remain competitive through the summer, De La Cruz's name will inevitably work its way into award conversations that have so far overlooked him.

Mid-May is the right moment to say it clearly: Elly De La Cruz is the best shortstop in baseball right now. The numbers say so. The history says so. The only thing still catching up is the recognition.
