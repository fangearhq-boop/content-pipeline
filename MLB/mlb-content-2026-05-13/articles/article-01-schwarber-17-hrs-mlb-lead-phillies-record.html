Schwarber Seizes MLB HR Lead With 17th, Ties Phillies Franchise Record of 5 Straight Homer Games

By Marcus Cole | May 13, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

Kyle Schwarber hit his 17th home run of the 2026 season Tuesday night to give the Philadelphia Phillies a 2-1 win over the Boston Red Sox — and in doing so, he leapfrogged Aaron Judge and Munetaka Murakami to take the MLB home run lead while tying one of the most exclusive marks in Phillies franchise history.

The five-consecutive-game home run streak places Schwarber alongside Mike Schmidt, Dick Allen, Bobby Abreu, Rhys Hoskins, Chase Utley, Trea Turner, and Odúbel Herrera as the only Phillies players to accomplish the feat. Six home runs across five games is a personal best for the 33-year-old slugger.

A Race Transformed

For much of the early 2026 season, the American League's Aaron Judge and the White Sox's Munetaka Murakami dominated the home run conversation, trading the lead back and forth as both chased what looked like a potential 50-HR season. Schwarber's recent surge has changed that narrative entirely.

With 17 home runs, Schwarber stands alone atop the MLB leaderboard. His pace, if sustained, projects to one of the better single-season home run totals in recent franchise history. But Schwarber's hot stretches are well-documented across his career — the question is always whether he can maintain the elevation when the cold periods inevitably arrive.

Tuesday's game-winning shot off Boston's pitching was vintage Schwarber: left-center field power, a high launch angle, and the kind of swing that looks unhurried even when it's decisive. The 2-1 final gave the Phillies their third consecutive victory.

Context: The Phillies' Revival

Schwarber's surge doesn't exist in isolation. The Phillies are 11-3 since manager Rob Thomson was fired on April 28 and Don Mattingly took over as interim manager. Philadelphia was 9-19 at the time of the change — the worst record in baseball. Now at 20-22, the team has reversed course dramatically.

The talent level hasn't changed. The lineup that looked broken in April is the same lineup performing now. What has changed is the performance, and Schwarber's home run tear is the most visible expression of a team that has suddenly found its form.

What's Next

Schwarber and the Phillies continue their series with the Red Sox on Wednesday. If he can extend the home run streak to six consecutive games, he would stand alone with the outright Phillies franchise record. Beyond the individual milestone, a continued Phillies run through mid-May could push a team that looked dead in April back into legitimate NL East contention.

The home run race has a new leader. And in Philadelphia, for the first time in weeks, there's reason to believe things are going in the right direction.
