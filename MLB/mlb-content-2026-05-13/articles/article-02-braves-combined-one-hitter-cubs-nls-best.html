Braves Hold Cubs to One Hit in 5-2 Win — Holmes' Dominant Start Opens NL's Marquee Series

By Jake Torres | May 13, 2026 | Ballpark Banter

The Atlanta Braves sent a message in the opener of Tuesday's National League showcase series: their pitching staff is the best in baseball, and the Chicago Cubs found that out firsthand.

Grant Holmes combined with three relievers on a one-hitter Tuesday night at Truist Park, and the Braves defeated the Cubs 5-2 in the opening game of a three-game series between the NL's top two records. Austin Riley homered, and Mike Yastrzemski went 2-for-2 with a two-run shot in the fifth inning and three RBI to power Atlanta's offense.

Pitching Built for Playoff Runs

Atlanta enters Wednesday leading the NL with a 29-13 record and allowing just 3.44 runs per game — the fewest in the major leagues. Tuesday's combined one-hitter against one of the National League's most dangerous offenses was the perfect encapsulation of what makes this Braves team formidable: depth across the pitching staff that doesn't collapse when the ace isn't scheduled to pitch.

Holmes, who shared the workload with three relievers, kept Chicago's lineup off-balance throughout. The Cubs entered the series having accomplished something the 1955 Brooklyn Dodgers were the last team to do — posting two separate 10-game winning streaks in their first 40 games of the season. Against Atlanta's pitching on Tuesday, none of that recent history mattered.

Yastrzemski and Riley Provide the Offense

While the pitching dominated the narrative, the Braves' offense did its part. Yastrzemski's two-run home run in the fifth inning — a shot that landed in front of the Chop House restaurant in right field — gave Atlanta a cushion that never felt threatened. His three-RBI night provided more than enough support for a staff that would have made 2-1 look comfortable anyway.

Riley's home run added to a total that had the Braves leading the NL in runs scored per game as well. Atlanta is winning games both ways, which is what separates genuine pennant contenders from teams that are simply hot.

What's Next

The series continues Wednesday night with Game 2 at Truist Park. The Cubs (27-15) need a response after being held to a single hit in the opener. Their season-long pattern of going on extended winning runs suggests they're capable of bouncing back quickly — but doing it against this Braves pitching staff will require a different approach than Tuesday's game.

The NL's two best teams are in the same building for three nights. Tuesday belonged to Atlanta. The question now is how Chicago answers.
