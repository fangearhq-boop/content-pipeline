MLB Second Half, Day 2: The Standings, the Stories, and the White Sox

By Jake Torres

The second half of the 2026 MLB season has started. Two days in, the standings are clear. Here is where every division race stands, and why the most interesting story in the American League has been building all year.

The National League

The Los Angeles Dodgers are the best team in baseball. Their record of 61-36 leads every organization in the majors, and they enter the second half with a comfortable lead in the NL West. For the Dodgers, the division race is not the story — the October destination is the story, and what they do before August 3 to prepare for it.

The Milwaukee Brewers lead the NL Central at 59-37 and have been one of the steadiest teams in the National League all season. They are expected to be buyers at the deadline.

The NL Wild Card race is the most competitive element of the National League right now. Multiple teams across the NL East and Central are close enough to the playoffs that the next 17 days of performance — combined with what each front office does before August 3 — will determine the final wild card picture.

The American League

The Tampa Bay Rays lead the AL East at 56-38. They have been one of the more durable contenders in the American League despite the economic realities that typically constrain their off-season spending. They are expected to add before August 3.

The Texas Rangers are at 49-47 in the AL West, leading a division that hasn't settled into a clear hierarchy. The AL West remains genuinely competitive heading into the second half.

And then there are the Chicago White Sox.

The White Sox Story

The White Sox are 50-45, leading the American League Central.

In 2024, the Chicago White Sox lost 121 games — the most losses in a single 162-game season in Major League Baseball history. It was a record that no team would choose to hold. The 2025 season was an improvement, but they were still clearly in rebuild mode.

Now it is 2026, and they are in first place in a division that includes the Cleveland Guardians and Detroit Tigers. They have a positive win-loss record in the second half of the season's calendar. They are, by any measure, a legitimate contender for a playoff spot.

Whether they can hold on through the final 17 days before August 3 — and through the rest of the season — is the most interesting question in the American League. They will face pressure from the Guardians and Tigers in the division. Their trade deadline direction (buyers to hold the lead? sellers to stock up for the future?) is genuinely unclear. Whatever they decide will say a lot about how seriously they take what they've built.

The Early Market Move

The Washington Nationals signaled their deadline direction on Thursday, trading outfield prospect Robert Hassell III to the Pittsburgh Pirates for cash considerations. It is a minor transaction in the context of what August 3 will bring, but it is a direction statement. The Nationals are sellers. The Pirates picked up a piece. The market is moving.

What's Next

The second half has 17 days until the trade deadline and the rest of the regular season after that. The Dodgers will add. The White Sox will have to decide what they are. The Phillies will be urgently shopping for a reliever after Brad Keller's UCL tear. And Tarik Skubal's future will be decided before August 3 at 6 PM ET. The second half is here. The interesting part starts now.
