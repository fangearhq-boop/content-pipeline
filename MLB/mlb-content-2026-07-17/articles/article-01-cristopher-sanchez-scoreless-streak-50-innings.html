Cristopher Sánchez's 50 2/3-Inning Scoreless Streak Is the Fifth-Longest in Baseball History

By Marcus Cole

It ended on a single.

Wednesday night at Citizens Bank Park, Jackson Merrill put a ball into left field in the seventh inning, Ty France crossed the plate from second base, and Cristopher Sánchez's consecutive scoreless innings streak came to a close. The final number: 50 2/3 innings.

That number is the fifth-longest in Major League Baseball history since the pitching mound was set at its current distance in 1893.

Where It Ranks

The all-time list of consecutive scoreless innings in a single season looks like this:

Orel Hershiser threw 59 scoreless innings for the Dodgers in 1988, breaking Don Drysdale's record from 1968. Drysdale threw 58. Walter Johnson threw 55 2/3 in 1913. Jack Coombs threw 53 in 1910. Cristopher Sánchez threw 50 2/3 in 2026.

He is the only left-handed pitcher on that list. No southpaw in the recorded history of the major leagues has ever thrown more consecutive scoreless innings in a single season. Not Sandy Koufax. Not Warren Spahn. Not Randy Johnson. The list of great left-handers in baseball history is long, and Sánchez has put his name at the top of this particular category without anyone else close enough to be mentioned in the same breath.

The Game Itself

Sánchez did not leave the mound after the run scored. He finished the seventh inning, collected his eighth strikeout of the night, and walked off to a standing ovation from Citizens Bank Park that lasted more than a minute.

The Phillies won 3-2. It matters, both for the standings and for the record — Sánchez's streak ended on a loss, but his team won. His ERA dropped to 1.46 with the performance.

He started the 2026 season quietly, emerging through the spring as one of the most dominant starters in the National League. His scoreless streak began in early June, crossed the Phillies' franchise record held by Grover Cleveland Alexander — a mark set in 1911 — at 44 2/3 innings last week, and continued until Wednesday night.

The Historical Weight

The names above Sánchez on the all-time list span more than a century of baseball. Hershiser's record has stood since 1988 and is widely considered one of the most durable marks in the sport. Drysdale's 58-inning run in 1968 — the year that was — came in one of the greatest pitching environments the modern game has ever seen. Walter Johnson pitched his 55 2/3 innings in 1913, when the game was a different sport. Jack Coombs pitched his 53 innings in 1910.

Sánchez put his name next to all of them on Wednesday night. The streak ended, but the position in the record books does not.

What's Next

Sánchez takes a 1.46 ERA into his next start. The Phillies are a World Series contender with the August 3 trade deadline 17 days away. They just lost Brad Keller to a UCL tear, which has made their bullpen situation urgent. But their rotation — anchored by the most dominant pitching stretch in Phillies history — is not a concern. What Sánchez did over the last seven weeks will be discussed for a long time. He is back to being a normal pitcher starting his next turn.

That streak belongs to history now.
