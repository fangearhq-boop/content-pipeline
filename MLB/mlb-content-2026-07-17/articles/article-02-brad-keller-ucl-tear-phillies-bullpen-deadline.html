Brad Keller's UCL Tear Turned the Phillies' Trade Deadline Into a Bullpen Emergency

By Jake Torres

The Philadelphia Phillies were already going to address their bullpen before the August 3 trade deadline. They had identified the need before the All-Star break. The market was already being mapped.

And then Brad Keller's elbow ligament tore.

What Happened

Keller was placed on the 15-day injured list Thursday with a torn ulnar collateral ligament in his right elbow, retroactive to July 13. He is 30 years old. He will seek a second opinion before what is broadly expected to be Tommy John surgery — a procedure that typically requires 12 to 14 months of recovery. His 2026 season is over.

Seth Johnson was recalled from the minor leagues to fill his roster spot.

Keller was a leverage reliever in the Philadelphia bullpen — the kind of arm teams use in the seventh and eighth innings when leads are on the line. His absence creates a gap that the Phillies cannot fill internally, and they are now entering the August 3 deadline with a need that has moved from "important" to "urgent."

The Deadline Context

The 2026 MLB trade deadline is 17 days away. For a team with World Series aspirations, that is enough time to solve a bullpen problem — but not enough time to be patient about it.

The Phillies came into the second half hosting the All-Star Game at Citizens Bank Park and watching their ace, Cristopher Sánchez, finish what turned out to be the fifth-longest scoreless innings streak in baseball history. The rotation is not a concern. The lineup has Bryce Harper, Kyle Schwarber, and the rest of one of the best offensive groups in the National League.

What they needed before July 17 was a reliever who could protect leads. What they need now is the same thing — except with more urgency and 17 days to find it instead of the relaxed timeline they might have operated on before Keller's diagnosis.

The Market

Multiple names are in play for teams seeking bullpen help. Mason Miller of the San Diego Padres has been linked to the Yankees and others; the Padres are among the teams leaning toward selling this deadline. Boston closer Aroldis Chapman is estimated at approximately 90% probability to be dealt. Several other high-leverage arms are expected to be available from teams moving toward seller mode.

The Phillies will be at the front of that conversation now. A team that was already going to buy has been handed a specific, urgent need — the clearest possible mandate going into the deadline market.

What's Next

Keller will get his second opinion and is expected to undergo Tommy John surgery, with a recovery timeline that makes a 2027 return the earliest realistic target. The Phillies have 17 days to address the bullpen before August 3 at 6 PM ET. The names they call and the deals they make in the next 17 days will define how their 2026 postseason run unfolds — if they make it there.
