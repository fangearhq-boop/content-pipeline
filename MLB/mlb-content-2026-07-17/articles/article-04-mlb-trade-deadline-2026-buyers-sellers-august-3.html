The 2026 MLB Trade Deadline Is 17 Days Away — Here's the Full Market Picture

By Marcus Cole

The 2026 MLB trade deadline is August 3 at 6 PM ET. The second half of the season is underway, and every team in baseball is now operating with a hard date on the calendar. Here is where the market stands.

The Headline Name: Tarik Skubal

The Detroit Tigers ace is the defining figure of this trade deadline. Skubal is a two-time AL Cy Young Award winner in a contract year, having returned from bone chip surgery to throw at 99.9 mph. He does not want to be traded. But extension talks are described as "nil," his expected free agent ask is more than $400 million, and multiple analysts put his trade probability at 85%.

The teams identified as best fits include the Los Angeles Dodgers, New York Yankees, Atlanta Braves, Milwaukee Brewers, Toronto Blue Jays, and Tampa Bay Rays. Any one of them would be fundamentally altered by adding Skubal to their rotation heading into the postseason.

Skubal said Thursday that the Tigers "need to play better baseball or else" — giving the organization 17 days to make the trade question irrelevant. If they play well enough to be buyers, he stays. If they don't, "or else" becomes the most important phrase in the trade deadline conversation.

Other High-Probability Names

Luis Arraez — the contact machine who has put up the best batting average marks in baseball in recent seasons — is estimated at 90% probability to be dealt. Aroldis Chapman out of the Boston Red Sox bullpen is estimated at approximately 90% as well.

The starting pitcher market behind Skubal is deep: Sandy Alcantara, Reid Detmers, Sonny Gray, Kevin Gausman, Joe Ryan, and Michael Wacha are all names that contending teams are circling. Wandy Peralta is also available, though his 4.81 ERA means teams will need to evaluate carefully.

The Buyers

The Los Angeles Dodgers remain the class of baseball at 61-36 and are expected to add rotation or bullpen depth. The Milwaukee Brewers at 59-37 are buyers in the NL Central. The Chicago Cubs have been identified as a team with an extensive pitching shopping list — starters, relievers, right-handed, left-handed, rental, controllable.

The Philadelphia Phillies entered the deadline already as buyers, and Brad Keller's UCL tear — announced Thursday — has made bullpen help their most urgent priority. The Tampa Bay Rays and Red Sox are also expected to be active.

The most interesting potential buyers are in the crowded NL Wild Card race: the Florida Marlins, Washington Nationals, St. Louis Cardinals, and Pittsburgh Pirates are all close enough to the playoffs that any of them could become unexpected buyers, adding four more organizations to the demand side of an already active market.

The Sellers

The New York Mets are confirmed sellers. Manager Carlos Mendoza was fired recently, and the organization's direction is clear. The San Diego Padres are leaning toward selling, with closer Mason Miller as their headline available asset — the Yankees have been linked to him for weeks.

The Athletics, Kansas City Royals, Los Angeles Angels, Cincinnati Reds, and San Francisco Giants are all also expected to move pieces before August 3. The Nationals signaled their position early by trading outfield prospect Robert Hassell III to the Pirates for cash considerations — a minor deal, but an early statement of direction.

What's Next

The August 3 deadline is 17 days from today. The contenders are identified, the sellers are positioned, and the names are on the board. What happens between now and 6 PM ET on August 3 will define which teams are still playing in October — and which ones wish they had made a different call.
