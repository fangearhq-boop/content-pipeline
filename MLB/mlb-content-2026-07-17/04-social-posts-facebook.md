### STORY 1: Cristopher Sánchez Scoreless Streak Ends

**FB Long-Form (8:00 AM ET)**

It ended on a single.

Wednesday night at Citizens Bank Park, Jackson Merrill hit a ball to left field in the seventh inning, Ty France scored from second base, and Cristopher Sánchez's consecutive scoreless innings streak came to a close at 50 2/3 innings.

The number needs context. In all of Major League Baseball history since the pitching mound was moved to its current distance in 1893, only four pitchers have thrown more consecutive scoreless innings in a single season:

- Orel Hershiser: 59 innings (1988)
- Don Drysdale: 58 innings (1968)
- Walter Johnson: 55 2/3 innings (1913)
- Jack Coombs: 53 innings (1910)

Sánchez is fifth. And he is the first left-handed pitcher in baseball history to appear on this list. No southpaw has ever gone longer without allowing a run in a single season — not Sandy Koufax, not Randy Johnson, not Warren Spahn. Cristopher Sánchez holds the record for left-handers by 50 2/3 innings to zero, because nobody else was ever close enough to have a number put next to their name.

The Phillies won 3-2. He threw seven innings, allowed one run, and struck out eight batters. When he walked off the mound at Citizens Bank Park, the crowd gave him a standing ovation that lasted more than a minute.

His ERA is now 1.46.

The streak is over. The legacy isn't going anywhere.

### STORY 2: Brad Keller UCL Tear — Phillies Bullpen Emergency

**FB Long-Form (10:30 AM ET)**

The Philadelphia Phillies were already making calls about bullpen help before the August 3 trade deadline. Now they're making those calls differently.

Right-hander Brad Keller was placed on the 15-day injured list Thursday with a torn UCL in his right elbow — retroactive to July 13. The injury is season-ending. Keller, 30, will seek a second opinion before what is expected to be Tommy John surgery, which typically requires 12 to 14 months to recover from.

Seth Johnson has been recalled from the minors to take his roster spot.

The Phillies came into the second half of the season as one of the most complete teams in the National League. They hosted the MLB All-Star Game at Citizens Bank Park on July 14. Their rotation features Cristopher Sánchez, whose scoreless streak of 50 2/3 innings just ended with one of the best single-season pitching performances in the modern era. They have offense. They have a park their players love.

What they now urgently need is a reliever who can hold leads in the seventh and eighth innings — a leverage arm. Keller was part of that solution. Now he's out.

The Phillies have 17 days before the August 3 deadline. A team that was going to add help regardless now has a specific hole that needs to be filled first. The names in play include every high-leverage arm that a selling team might move. The Phillies will be at the front of the line.

### STORY 3: Tarik Skubal Warning to Tigers

**FB Long-Form (12:30 PM ET)**

And then he said this: "We need to play better baseball or else."

"Or else" is the part of that sentence that matters. Skubal is giving the Tigers a window. It is a real window, with a closing date — August 3, when the MLB trade deadline shuts at 6 PM ET. If the Tigers can get themselves into a legitimate contention position in the next 17 days, the two-time AL Cy Young Award winner would be the first to say the deadline passed without drama.

But if they can't?

Skubal's bone chip surgery is behind him. He returned to the mound hitting 99.9 mph with his fastball. The stuff is there. The contract situation is straightforward: he's in the final year of a $32 million arbitration deal, and no extension talks are currently underway. His expected price on the free agent market is estimated at more than $400 million.

Multiple analysts put the probability of Skubal being traded before August 3 at 85%. The Dodgers, Yankees, Braves, Brewers, Blue Jays, and Rays have all been identified as teams that want him.

Skubal wants the Tigers to make him irrelevant to the trade deadline by being good enough that they don't have to sell. That is the best possible scenario for everyone involved. He has 17 days to find out if the Tigers can make it happen.
