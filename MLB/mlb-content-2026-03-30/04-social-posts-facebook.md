### STORY 1: Giants 20 Scoreless Innings — Franchise Record

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM ET

The San Francisco Giants just experienced one of the most historically rough offensive starts in franchise history — and it dates back more than a century.

Through their opening three games against the New York Yankees, the Giants went 20 consecutive innings without scoring. That matches a franchise record set all the way back in 1909, when New York was scoreless for 13 innings in the opener and the first 7 innings of Game 2.

They were shut out 7-0 in Game 1. Shut out 3-0 in Game 2. Only four hits over the first two games combined.

In Game 3, the Giants finally scored in the third inning. Jung Hoo Lee doubled to right. Matt Chapman followed with an RBI single. The crowd at Oracle Park reportedly erupted — for a single run that came in a game they were already losing.

Final: Yankees 3, Giants 1. Series: Yankees sweep, outscoring San Francisco 13-1.

The Giants are now the 11th team in MLB history to score one or fewer runs over their opening three games. The only team shut out all three times? The 2016 San Diego Padres, who went on to finish 68-94.

New manager Tony Vitello — who arrived with no prior professional playing or coaching experience — has his work cut out for him. But it's three games. Baseball is long.

Still, San Francisco fans have every right to be concerned. The question heading into next week: is this a matchup problem, or is the Giants offense actually this thin?

### STORY 2: Munetaka Murakami — HR in First 3 MLB Games

#### Long-Form Post -- 9:30 AM ET

The Chicago White Sox introduced their new Japanese slugger to the American League this weekend — and he responded by making history.

Munetaka Murakami hit a home run in each of his first three MLB games, becoming just the fourth player in baseball history to do so. The feat was confirmed by Sportradar. The other three: Chase DeLauter of the Cleveland Guardians (who did it this same week), Trevor Story of the Colorado Rockies (2016), and Kyle Lewis of the Seattle Mariners (2019).

Trevor Story owns the all-time record with home runs in his first four career MLB games. Murakami needs just one more to tie him.

Let's be clear about what Murakami brings to this league. In eight seasons with the Yakult Swallows of Japan's Central League, he hit 246 home runs. In 2022, he hit 56 — breaking the single-season record previously held by Sadaharu Oh, one of the most revered hitters in baseball history. He signed a two-year, $34 million contract with Chicago in December.

He's not just a power hitter either. In three games, Murakami also drew four walks and posted a .538 on-base percentage. Pat Murphy, the Brewers manager who watched all three losses from the other dugout, put it simply:

"The park doesn't seem big enough to hold him, you know what I mean? He's impressive."

The White Sox may be a rebuilding team. But Murakami? He's here to mash.

### STORY 3: Emerson Hancock — 6 No-Hit Innings

#### Long-Form Post -- 11:00 AM ET

Emerson Hancock was something special on Sunday afternoon in Seattle.

The 26-year-old right-hander took the mound against the Cleveland Guardians and proceeded to throw six hitless innings — not a single ball left the infield. His only baserunners came on a first-inning walk to José Ramírez and a sixth-inning hit-by-pitch.

Hancock struck out nine batters — a career high — in a dominant 97-pitch performance. Mariners manager Dan Wilson pulled him after six and chose not to test the no-hit bid further. Cleveland's Chase DeLauter then singled off reliever Cooper Criswell to lead off the seventh, officially ending Seattle's bid for a combined no-no.

But here's the company Hancock now keeps: he joins Félix Hernández — the man who threw the Mariners' only perfect game in 2012 — as the only pitchers in franchise history to strike out nine or more batters in a hitless outing of at least six innings.

Hancock spent part of his offseason in rural southern Georgia refining his sweeper slider without any advanced technology. It showed. His fastball generated nine swings-and-misses, and his new sweeper was largely why the Guardians couldn't put anything in play.

Final: Mariners 8, Guardians 0. Brendan Donovan hit his second home run of the series — a three-run blast — to power the offense. Seattle took a 2-2 series split to open the season.

### STORY 7: Early Standings + Aaron Judge at 400 HR

#### Long-Form Post -- 7:00 PM ET

Three games into the 2026 MLB season, three teams are undefeated.

The Los Angeles Dodgers swept the Arizona Diamondbacks. The New York Yankees swept the San Francisco Giants. The Milwaukee Brewers swept the Chicago White Sox. Three familiar names, all 3-0, all looking the part of legitimate contenders.

Now for the number that's going to follow Aaron Judge for the next few months: 32.

That's how many home runs he needs to reach 400 for his career. And according to the math, if he stays healthy and hits at his typical pace, Judge could reach the milestone faster than any player in Major League Baseball history. Mark McGwire is the current record holder — he reached 400 home runs in 1,412 games. Judge, who hit 53 home runs and won the batting title at .331 last season, has the power output to eclipse that mark before the All-Star break.

He would be the fourth Yankee ever to reach 400 home runs — joining Babe Ruth (659), Mickey Mantle (536), and Lou Gehrig (493).

Not a bad club.

Elsewhere: the Kansas City Royals are 1-2 but have gotten back-to-back strong starts from Michael Wacha and Seth Lugo. The Atlanta Braves are 1-2 with a rotation full of injury question marks. The Phillies dropped a series to Texas. Early days for everyone.

But Dodgers, Yankees, Brewers? They're starting exactly where everyone expected them to.
