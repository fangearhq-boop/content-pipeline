Giants Go 20 Straight Innings Without Scoring to Open 2026 — Matching a 117-Year-Old Franchise Record

The San Francisco Giants were outscored 13-1 in their opening series against New York, matching the worst offensive start in franchise history.

Twenty Innings of Silence

The San Francisco Giants opened the 2026 MLB season by going 20 consecutive innings without scoring — matching a franchise record that dated back to 1909. The streak began with a 7-0 shutout loss to the New York Yankees in Game 1, continued through a 3-0 defeat in Game 2, and finally ended in the third inning of Game 3 when Jung Hoo Lee doubled to right and Matt Chapman followed with an RBI single.

The crowd at Oracle Park erupted for a single run in a game the Giants were already losing. San Francisco still fell 3-1 and was swept, outscored 13-1 in the series.

The original franchise record came in 1909, when the Giants were scoreless for 13 innings in their opener and the first seven innings of Game 2. The 2026 Giants matched that streak across three games against a Yankees rotation that gave them nothing to work with.

Historical Context

The Giants became the 11th team in MLB history to score one or fewer runs over their opening three games. The bar for company is grim: the only team to be shut out all three times to start a season was the 2016 San Diego Padres, who went on to finish 68-94.

San Francisco managed only four hits over the first two games combined. The lineup — which included shortstop Willy Adames, outfielder Jung Hoo Lee, and veteran third baseman Matt Chapman — produced almost nothing against Yankees starters and a bullpen that allowed the Giants to barely breathe.

Adames acknowledged the struggle without panic: "The first three games haven't gone the way we wanted to, but that's how baseball goes. We're going in the right direction."

Tony Vitello's Introduction to MLB Managing

The Giants' 0-3 start came under first-year manager Tony Vitello, who arrived in San Francisco after a successful college coaching career but with no prior professional playing or coaching experience. The opening series handed him a baptism by fire.

Vitello's lineup construction and in-game decisions will face heightened scrutiny if the Giants' offense doesn't show improvement quickly. San Francisco's front office made offseason additions, but the results through three games raised immediate questions about whether the lineup has enough power and run-scoring ability to contend in the NL West.

The Yankees' Dominant Start

Credit is due to New York. The Yankees swept the Giants 3-0, outscoring them 13-1, in what amounted to a statement-making start to the 2026 season. Aaron Judge hit a home run in the series, continuing his chase toward 400 career home runs — a milestone he could reach faster than any player in baseball history. New York is one of three teams that entered Monday at 3-0 alongside the Dodgers and Brewers.

What's Next

The Giants open their home schedule against new opponents this week with an opportunity to reset. San Francisco needs offensive production from its middle of the order — particularly from Lee and Chapman, who had the team's only signs of life in Game 3. The 162-game season is long, and three games in March tell only a fraction of the story. But for Giants fans, the 20-inning drought was not the start anyone envisioned for the Vitello era.
