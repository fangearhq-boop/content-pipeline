Full Two-Way Mode: What to Expect from Ohtani's First Regular-Season Start of 2026

Shohei Ohtani takes the mound at Dodger Stadium on Tuesday — his first regular-season pitching appearance since returning from Tommy John surgery as a full two-way player.

The Most Anticipated Start of the Early Season

When Shohei Ohtani walks to the mound at Dodger Stadium on Tuesday, March 31, he will do something he hasn't done in a regular-season MLB game in over two years: pitch. His opponent will be the Cleveland Guardians. His supporting cast will be a Dodgers team that has already gone 3-0 to open 2026. His expectations will be nothing short of extraordinary.

This is Ohtani's first season as a full two-way player after returning from Tommy John surgery. In his first two seasons with Los Angeles, he was limited on the mound as his elbow healed. Now, he enters 2026 with no restrictions — manager Dave Roberts confirmed there will be no limitations going forward once the season is underway.

The Spring Training Evidence

The two spring starts suggested Ohtani is ready. In his Cactus League debut against the Giants, he threw 4.1 scoreless innings with just one hit allowed. His final spring outing against the Angels was even more impressive: four-plus innings, one run allowed, 11 strikeouts on 86 pitches.

Roberts was unambiguous about what he saw: "He's ready to go. I thought the two outings that we saw, the intensity was there, focus was there, execution and all that stuff was there. A lot of punchouts tonight."

Ohtani himself has said publicly that he plans to pitch without any limitations this season — a departure from the careful management that defined his first two Dodger years.

What to Expect Tuesday

Roberts said Ohtani won't go past the seventh inning in his first regular-season start, but that six or seven innings are possible. "When you're talking about the first game of the season, could he get through six? I mean, could he touch the seventh? Yes, but he won't touch the eighth inning," Roberts explained.

The pitch count will be managed, but the intent is for Ohtani to start accumulating innings quickly. With Blake Snell recovering from left shoulder inflammation and Gavin Stone also dealing with shoulder trouble, the Dodgers are using a modified rotation that has Justin Wrobleski in a swingman role. Yoshinobu Yamamoto started Opening Day. Ohtani goes Tuesday. The depth behind them is thinner than usual.

The Cy Young Conversation

Roberts went as far as mentioning the NL Cy Young Award in the context of Ohtani's 2026 pitching season — a statement that would have seemed outlandish before the spring. But the form is there. Ohtani pitching without restrictions for a full season, backed by a Dodgers lineup that leads the NL in run-scoring expectations, is a legitimate recipe for historic two-way numbers.

He signed his $700 million contract largely on the promise of this: Shohei Ohtani, full two-way player, every fifth day on the mound and every day in the middle of one of the best lineups in baseball.

What's Next

Tuesday will be the first test of what the full version of this experiment looks like. If the spring form holds — 11 Ks in his final tune-up, elite velocity and a well-refined arsenal — the Guardians will face a pitcher the baseball world has been anticipating for over two years. The NL is watching. The Cy Young race may already be starting to take shape.
