MacKenzie Gore Nearly No-Hits the Phillies in His Rangers Debut — 5 Hitless Innings, 7 Ks

The left-hander acquired from Washington struck out Bryce Harper with the bases loaded as Texas took the series from the defending NL East champions.

A Debut to Remember

MacKenzie Gore stepped into his first game as a Texas Ranger on Sunday in Philadelphia and nearly made franchise history. The left-hander retired the first 15 Phillies he faced, carrying a perfect game through five innings before Justin Crawford's infield single in the sixth ended the no-hit bid.

Gore finished with five-plus innings, one hit, one hit-by-pitch, and seven strikeouts in the Rangers' 8-3 victory. He earned the win, improving to 1-0 in his Texas debut.

The last no-hitter in Rangers history was Kenny Rogers' perfect game in 1994. Gore didn't get there — but the debut was a resounding statement from a pitcher who had spent his career in Washington before the offseason trade.

The Harper Strikeout

The signature moment came in the sixth inning. After Crawford's single ended the no-hit bid, the Phillies loaded the bases with one out — setting up Bryce Harper, the two-time NL MVP and face of Philadelphia's franchise, in a situation that defines seasons.

Gore struck him out.

The bases-loaded strikeout of Harper was the exclamation point on a debut that already had Rangers fans talking. Gore left after the hit-by-pitch of Alec Bohm later in the inning, having established himself as a legitimate front-of-rotation arm in the American League.

New Rangers Providing Power

The offense backed Gore up convincingly. Brandon Nimmo — who received boos from Phillies fans all weekend after years as a Met — answered them with a two-run home run off starter Jesús Luzardo in the second inning to give Texas an early lead. Andrew McCutchen added a three-run home run in the fourth for a 5-0 cushion. Kyle Higashioka had an RBI single in the sixth on a flyball that Adolis Garcia — a former Ranger himself — lost in the sun.

The final was 8-3. The Rangers took the series 2-1 from the defending NL East champion Phillies in a strong early-season statement. Luzardo dropped to 0-1, allowing three runs on Nimmo and McCutchen's homers.

Offseason Additions Making an Impact

Texas made significant acquisitions before 2026: Gore arrived in the Nationals trade, while Nimmo and McCutchen were brought in as offensive reinforcements. All three delivered meaningful contributions in their first series as Rangers. The opening weekend suggested that Texas's offseason investment has the potential to pay dividends in an AL West division race.

What's Next

Gore will make his second Rangers start after the team opens a new series in Baltimore. His debut performance — especially the strikeout of Harper with the bases loaded — provides immediate credibility in the American League. The Phillies will look to reset against Washington while adjusting to the loss of their series opener. For Rangers fans, the introduction of Gore, Nimmo, and McCutchen in Week 1 has been exactly what they hoped for.
