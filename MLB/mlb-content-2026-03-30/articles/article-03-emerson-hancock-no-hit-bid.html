Emerson Hancock Throws Six No-Hit Innings as Mariners Rout Guardians 8-0

The 26-year-old right-hander struck out a career-high nine batters and joined Félix Hernández in exclusive Mariners pitching history.

Zero Hits, Nine Strikeouts

Emerson Hancock was extraordinary on Sunday in Seattle. The right-hander threw six hitless innings against the Cleveland Guardians, striking out a career-high nine batters while allowing zero hits and zero batted balls that left the infield. His only baserunners came on a walk to José Ramírez in the first inning and a hit-by-pitch to CJ Kayfus in the sixth.

Hancock needed 97 pitches — 62 strikes — to complete six innings. Manager Dan Wilson pulled him after the sixth without any consideration of extending the no-hit bid. Chase DeLauter then led off the seventh inning with a clean single to right off reliever Cooper Criswell, officially ending Seattle's bid for the seventh no-hitter in franchise history.

The final score was 8-0. The Mariners took a 2-2 series split against Cleveland to open the season.

Joining Félix in the Record Books

With his nine-strikeout performance in a hitless outing of six or more innings, Hancock joined Félix Hernández — who threw the Mariners' only perfect game against Tampa Bay in 2012 — as the only pitchers in franchise history to accomplish the feat. It is rarefied air for any Mariners pitcher, let alone one who entered the rotation largely because of a Bryce Miller injury.

Hancock, the sixth overall pick in the 2020 draft, has spent parts of several seasons working his way through Seattle's system. He entered 2026 as a rotation piece by circumstance but performed like one by design.

The Offseason Work Behind the Performance

Hancock spent a significant portion of his offseason in rural southern Georgia refining his sweeper slider — without the benefit of advanced pitching technology. The results were apparent on Sunday. His four-seam fastball generated nine swings-and-misses. The sweeper gave Guardians hitters a second look that they couldn't solve.

He also increased his fastball velocity, which contributed to the groundout and popout heavy contact profile on Sunday. Not a single ball carried past the infield.

Offense Provides Comfortable Cushion

Brendan Donovan provided the biggest offensive moment, hitting his second home run of the series — a three-run blast — to give Seattle an early lead against Guardians starter Slade Cecconi. Randy Arozarena added an RBI double in the fifth, Luke Raley had a run-scoring single, and Cal Raleigh drove in another run with a ground-rule double. The Mariners scored six runs off Cecconi before the bullpen finished out the game.

The 8-0 final was one of the most dominant wins of the opening weekend across the entire league.

What's Next

Hancock's next scheduled start will come in Seattle's second homestand of the season. With his sweeper now fully integrated and his velocity up, he has established himself as a legitimate rotation piece for a Mariners team that entered 2026 with postseason aspirations. The Guardians move on after a 2-2 split — their ace Emerson Hancock problem now in the rearview, but not forgotten.
