Yelich's Pinch-Hit Blast Caps 6-Run Rally as Brewers Complete Season-Opening Sweep

Christian Yelich hit the first pinch-hit homer of his career as Milwaukee rallied from a 7-2 deficit to beat the White Sox 9-7 and go 3-0.

The Comeback

Milwaukee trailed 7-2 after three innings. Their starter, Brandon Sproat, had surrendered a grand slam before recording an out and eventually gave up seven runs over three innings with four walks. The Brewers' bullpen then threw six consecutive scoreless innings to keep the game within reach.

In the eighth inning, the Brewers loaded the bases with one out against reliever Chris Murphy. Brice Turang singled to make it 7-4. Seranthony Dominguez came in for Chicago and got William Contreras on a popout. Then Luis Rengifo lined a two-run single to center — 7-6.

Christian Yelich was announced as the next pinch hitter. The crowd of 32,737 rose to their feet at American Family Field.

Yelich Delivers

On a 2-2 splitter from Dominguez that caught too much of the plate, Yelich turned and drove it 421 feet to right field — a three-run home run. Exit velocity: 111.1 mph, the hardest-hit ball by any Brewer in 2026. Yelich dropped his bat immediately, turned to the dugout, and didn't even watch it land.

It was the first pinch-hit home run of Yelich's career, which began in 2013. Over twelve seasons, he had never hit one until Sunday. Milwaukee led 9-7.

Trevor Megill closed the ninth for the save, and the Brewers completed their season-opening three-game sweep of the White Sox.

A Messy Start, A Brilliant Finish

Sproat's implosion — in which he walked the first two batters, watched a routine flyball drop for a single to load the bases, and then surrendered Colson Montgomery's grand slam — could have derailed Milwaukee's week. Instead, it set up one of the more dramatic early-season comebacks in recent memory.

The bullpen combination of Garret Anderson, Jared Koenig, Jake Woodford (who earned the win), and Trevor Megill gave up nothing over six innings after Sproat departed. That kind of sustained relief pitching allowed the Brewers to stay in a game they had no business winning entering the eighth.

Murakami's Consolation

Munetaka Murakami hit his third home run in three games for the White Sox — a solo shot in the second inning off Sproat. The milestone moment came in a losing cause as Chicago was swept to open the season at 0-3. Murakami's three straight opening-game home runs were historic in their own right, but his White Sox couldn't complement the power with a win.

What's Next

The Brewers travel to face their next opponent at 3-0, having already shown the resilience and late-game execution that defined their best recent seasons. Christian Yelich — whose pinch-hit moment will be replayed on highlight shows all week — appears motivated and healthy. Milwaukee's starting pitching will need to improve after Sproat's rocky debut, but the bullpen and comeback ability are early statements from a team with serious playoff ambitions in 2026.
