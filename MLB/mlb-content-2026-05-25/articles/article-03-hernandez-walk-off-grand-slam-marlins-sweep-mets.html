Hernandez's Walk-Off Grand Slam in a 0-0 Game Gives Marlins Mets Sweep

By Ryan Calloway

May 25, 2026

A 0-0 game, a pinch-hitter, bases loaded in the ninth, Devin Williams on the mound — and then a 416-foot walk-off grand slam that hadn't been seen in a scoreless game since 2013. Heriberto Hernandez gave Miami a moment to remember.

There are games that end quietly. A single, a sac fly, a blown save — three runs crossing the plate somewhere in the eighth and the home team running out the clock. And then there are games like Sunday in Miami, where both starters go deep, both bullpens hold, and the whole nine innings build toward a single moment of extraordinary baseball.

Heriberto Hernandez provided that moment.

Coming off the bench as a pinch-hitter in the bottom of the ninth, with the bases loaded and a 0-0 game on the line against Mets closer Devin Williams, Hernandez drove a 416-foot grand slam to center field for a walk-off victory — final score: Marlins 4, Mets 0. Miami completes a three-game sweep of New York in the process.

A Rare Piece of Baseball History

This wasn't just a walk-off win. According to MLB records, Hernandez's slam was the first walk-off grand slam in a 0-0 game since Justin Maxwell hit one for the Kansas City Royals on September 22, 2013 — a span of nearly 13 years. That context makes Sunday's moment more than a highlight. It's a genuinely rare slice of baseball history.

To manufacture a walk-off grand slam in a scoreless game requires the precise convergence of multiple circumstances: a game that plays tight through nine, a team that can get three runners on base in the ninth without scoring, and a hitter capable of hitting one over the wall on command. All three aligned on Sunday in Miami.

Hernandez didn't even start the game. He replaced Owen Caissie in the seventh inning as a pinch-hitter. Then he waited. When the ninth inning arrived and the bases were loaded, the Marlins turned to him again — and he came through in the most dramatic fashion possible.

Williams Takes the Loss

Devin Williams is one of the better closers in the National League — which makes the moment harder. Williams has been good all season, but Hernandez hit a 416-foot missile to center that cleared the fence with authority. Some nights, even a good pitch becomes a grand slam when the contact is perfect.

The Mets have had a difficult stretch, and a three-game sweep at the hands of Miami — on a walk-off grand slam in a 0-0 game — is the kind of series loss that stings. New York has work to do in the NL East.

Miami Bounces Back

The Marlins needed this. They had dropped three of four to the Atlanta Braves earlier in the week — a rough stretch that put their NL East position in sharper focus. Sweeping the Mets improves Miami to 25-29. They are not out of the playoff conversation. And a series win built around starting pitching and one of the most memorable endings in recent baseball history provides a jolt of confidence heading into the Memorial Day games.

What's Next

The Marlins host the Toronto Blue Jays on Memorial Day at 7:07 PM ET, looking to carry momentum from the sweep. The Mets travel to Cincinnati to face the Reds in an afternoon Memorial Day game at 4:10 PM ET. For Miami, Sunday's win is the kind of night that gets replayed, tweeted, and remembered. Hernandez wrote his name into the record books — and the Marlins swept along with him.
