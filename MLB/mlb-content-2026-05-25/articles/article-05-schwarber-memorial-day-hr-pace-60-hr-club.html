Kyle Schwarber's Memorial Day Checkpoint: Still on the Most Historic Home Run Pace Since Bonds

By Jake Torres

May 25, 2026

At Memorial Day, Kyle Schwarber has hit 20 home runs in 2026 — a total he reached on May 15, the earliest any player has done so in MLB history. His pace projects to 66-72 by season's end. Nobody has been here since Barry Bonds in 2001.

Memorial Day in baseball is more than a date on the calendar. It is the sport's first real checkpoint — the moment at which teams and players have enough games on the record to start making reasonable projections about where the season is going.

For Kyle Schwarber, the Memorial Day checkpoint looks like this: he has already hit 20 home runs in 2026. He reached that total on May 15 — the earliest any player in Major League Baseball history has reached the 20-homer mark in a single season.

Barry Bonds, in 2001, hit his 20th homer on May 19. He finished that season with 73. Schwarber beat that date by four days.

The Numbers

The projections vary slightly by methodology, but the range is consistently between 66 and 72 home runs if Schwarber maintains his current pace for a full 162-game season. The 60-homer threshold — joining Bonds (73), Mark McGwire (70), and Sammy Sosa (66, 65, and 63) — has not been reached by any player since Sosa hit 63 in 2001. Schwarber is not just threatening that club; he is, by the numbers, currently ahead of every player who ever entered it.

This is not a small sample-size aberration. Schwarber has hit 20 home runs in less than 50 games. He is in his prime years as a hitter. And the Phillies are built around their lineup's ability to protect him and maximize his plate appearances. Everything points toward continued production.

Context and Caution

The history of dramatic pace projections in May is also the history of regression. Players get hurt. Pitchers adjust. Stretches of poor contact happen. Schwarber himself hit a career-high 56 home runs in 2025 — a season that exceeded most projections — and he remains a threat to match or exceed that in 2026.

But 73? The projection says it's within reach. The honest answer is that nobody knows whether Schwarber will sustain this pace across an entire season. What's known is that he's on a trajectory that baseball has not seen in 25 years, and that is worth paying attention to regardless of where the final total lands.

In 2026, Schwarber hit 56 home runs in 2025, and he is already ahead of that pace in May. The leap from 56 to 60+ is significant. The leap from 60 to 70 is historic. He has the power. The swing. The mechanics. Memorial Day says: stay tuned.

What's Next

The Philadelphia Phillies play the San Diego Padres on Memorial Day at 6:40 PM ET. Every Schwarber at-bat comes with a crowd watching the scoreboard. At some point this season — whether it is home run No. 25, No. 40, or No. 57 — the conversation will move beyond comparisons to Schwarber's own previous seasons and firmly into the territory of baseball history. Memorial Day's checkpoint suggests that moment is coming sooner than anyone expected.
