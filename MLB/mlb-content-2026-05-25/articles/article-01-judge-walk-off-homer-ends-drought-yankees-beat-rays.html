Aaron Judge's Walk-Off Homer Ends 11-Game Drought, Yankees Beat Rays 2-0

By Marcus Cole

May 25, 2026

Ryan Weathers gave New York seven scoreless innings, and then Aaron Judge took the first pitch from Kevin Kelly and sent it over the right-center field wall for his 17th homer of the season — ending an 11-game drought in the most theatrical way possible.

The slump was real. Eleven games without a home run. One hit in 24 at-bats. No RBIs for nearly two weeks. The stories were writing themselves, and none of them were kind to Aaron Judge.

Then came Sunday night in the Bronx, and the first pitch of the ninth inning, and the kind of moment that makes baseball worth watching.

Judge turned on a sinker from Tampa Bay reliever Kevin Kelly and drove it into the right-center field seats — a two-run walk-off home run that gave the New York Yankees a 2-0 victory over the first-place Rays. His 17th homer of the season. His fourth career walk-off. The slump, officially, is over.

Weathers Sets the Stage

The walk-off overshadows what was an equally impressive performance from Ryan Weathers, who held the Rays scoreless across seven innings. Tampa Bay managed just two hits against Weathers — a complete pitching performance from a starter who gave his lineup every chance to win the game.

The Yankees had threatened but couldn't score through the first eight innings. Everything was set up for a ninth-inning moment, and Judge delivered it on the first pitch he saw. That precision — seeing the offering and making contact immediately — suggests his timing was sharp in a way that the 11-game slump had obscured.

The Yankees ended a three-game losing streak with the win, and Weathers' performance gives their rotation another option beyond their ace starts.

What the Drought Meant — and Didn't Mean

Context matters with slumps. Judge was 1-for-24 during the 11-game stretch without a home run, but the sample is small relative to a 162-game season, and he had otherwise remained on base at a respectable clip. The conversation around whether something was mechanically wrong was premature — this is Aaron Judge, and Aaron Judge goes quiet sometimes before reminding you what he's capable of.

HR No. 17 in May keeps him in the conversation for another monster season. His walk-off was his fourth career walk-off homer — a number that speaks to his comfort in high-leverage situations.

AL East Implications

With the victory, the Yankees improve to 31-22 on the season. Tampa Bay's lead in the AL East is now 4.5 games. A week earlier, it was 5.5 games. The Rays (approximately 34-17) have been the AL's most dominant team all season, but the Yankees — with their lineup finally healthy and Judge heating up — remain a legitimate threat.

The Memorial Day checkpoint is traditionally where analysts start projecting playoff pictures. The Yankees are positioned in the race, and Sunday's win provides momentum heading into a full week of games.

What's Next

The Yankees play the Kansas City Royals on Monday in the ESPN Memorial Day feature game at 3:30 PM ET — Judge vs. Bobby Witt Jr. is as compelling an individual storyline as any in the AL on Memorial Day. Tampa Bay continues their homestand. The AL East race that has defined early-season baseball in 2026 shows no signs of resolution, and Sunday's result made sure of it.
