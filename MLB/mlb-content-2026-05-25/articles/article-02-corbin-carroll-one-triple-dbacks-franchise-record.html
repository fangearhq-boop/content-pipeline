Corbin Carroll Goes 4-for-4 With Two Triples, Now One Away From D-Backs Franchise Record

By Jake Torres

May 25, 2026

Carroll's perfect Sunday against Colorado pushed his career triple total to 51 — one away from tying Stephen Drew's franchise record of 52. At the pace Carroll is moving, the record isn't just within reach — it's nearly inevitable.

Baseball in the modern era has been remade by exit velocity and launch angle, by three-run homers and strikeout leaderboards. The triple — the hardest play to manufacture in the sport, requiring contact, speed, and a read on the outfield all at once — has largely become an afterthought.

Corbin Carroll didn't get that memo.

On Sunday against Colorado, Carroll went 4-for-4 with two triples, pushing his career total to 51. Stephen Drew holds the Arizona Diamondbacks franchise record at 52 career triples. Carroll needs one more to tie it, two more to own it outright.

The Pace of the Chase

Numbers matter here. Drew reached his franchise-record 52 triples over 773 games across parts of seven seasons in Arizona. Carroll has 51 triples through 537 games — parts of five major league seasons. He is 26 years old.

The differential is striking: Carroll has accumulated nearly the same number of triples in 236 fewer games. His rate of triple production is faster than any player in D-Backs history, and there's no reason to expect it to slow. Carroll's game is built around speed and contact — he can turn any ball in the gap into an extra-base hit, and he reads outfield terrain better than almost anyone in the game.

Among active players in career triples, only Mike Trout and Starling Marte stand ahead of Carroll with 51 and counting. Both are significantly older. Carroll's ceiling in this category hasn't been reached.

Leading the League in 2026

Carroll already leads MLB in 2026 with eight triples on the season — more than double the second-place active total among current leaders. The gap-to-gap doubles that become triples in his hands are not accidents; they're the product of elite first-step speed and the instinct to push for the extra base when others would settle for two.

Sunday's performance — 4-for-4, two triples, against a Colorado pitching staff — showed Carroll at his best. Perfect contact, legs fully extended, pulling a 4-for-4 day out of a game that might otherwise be a footnote in the season.

Stephen Drew's Legacy

Stephen Drew spent parts of seven seasons in Arizona from 2006 to 2011, a shortstop with premium left-handed bat speed and the ability to drive balls into the alleys. His 52 triples as a Diamondback stood as the franchise record for over a decade — a number that seemed safe given how the game has evolved toward the fly ball.

Carroll is about to make it not safe.

What's Next

The Diamondbacks host the San Francisco Giants today in a Memorial Day afternoon game. Carroll will take the field needing one triple — a single well-struck ball to center or left-center — to tie Drew's record. Watch for it on the D-Backs broadcast, because when it happens, it will be one of the season's best individual moments.
