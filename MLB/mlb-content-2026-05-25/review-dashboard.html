### Long-Form Post Ask a casual baseball fan what the most exciting play in the sport is and they'll say a home run. Ask a purist. They'll say a triple. Corbin Carroll has been rewriting the history of the three-bagger all season — and now he's one triple away from franchise immortality. On Sunday, Carroll went 4-for-4 with two triples against Colorado, pushing his career total to 51. Stephen Drew holds the Arizona Diamondbacks franchise record at 52. One triple. That's it. What makes this chase even more remarkable is the pace. Carroll has played 537 career games to reach 51 triples. Drew needed 773 games to get to 52. Carroll is getting there in fewer at-bats, at a younger age (26), with a legitimate shot at blowing past the record entirely. He also leads MLB in 2026 with 8 triples, and trails only Mike Trout and Starling Marte among active players in career total triples. The home run era has squeezed out the triple. Carroll doesn't care. Watch for the record to fall this week. --- ### Image Post Caption Corbin Carroll is one triple from the D-Backs franchise record.
