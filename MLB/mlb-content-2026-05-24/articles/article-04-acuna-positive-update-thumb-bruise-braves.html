Acuña Gets Positive Injury Update After Left Thumb Bone Bruise

By Ryan Calloway

May 24, 2026

Ronald Acuña Jr. exited Friday's game with a bone bruise in his left thumb. X-rays were negative. Manager Walt Weiss said Acuña was day-to-day — and reportedly trying to get back on the field before the game was over.

The Atlanta Braves got the update they needed on Saturday.

Ronald Acuña Jr., who exited Friday's 9-3 win over the Marlins in the sixth inning with pain in his left thumb, received X-rays that came back negative. Manager Walt Weiss reported a positive prognosis and a day-to-day designation. And in the most on-brand moment of Acuña's 2026 season, Weiss told reporters that the star outfielder was "trying to talk his way into the lineup" before the game had even finished.

A bone bruise is not a structural injury. Negative X-rays confirm no fracture. Day-to-day means the timeline is measured in days, not weeks or months. The Braves' most important player should be available sooner than later.

Context: Acuña's 2026 Injury History

Friday's thumb bruise was the third injury concern for Acuña in the first two months of the season. He was placed on the injured list May 2 with a left hamstring strain and returned just three games before the thumb issue emerged. The combination of injuries — each individually manageable but collectively frustrating — has limited the amount of time Acuña has spent in full health this season.

That said, the Braves are 35-16 with the best record in the National League. Their performance with and without Acuña in the lineup reflects the depth of a roster built to withstand disruptions. The team has not faltered in his absence. But the ceiling of this Braves team is clearly higher when their best player is healthy and available every day.

Acuña's Response

Acuña said in Spanish after the game that he felt tightness in his thumb during play and that it was the first time something like that had happened to him. His competitive instinct — immediately trying to return — is consistent with the mentality that makes him one of the best players in the game. Bone bruises can be managed with rest and treatment; the negative X-rays remove the most serious concern.

What's Next

The Braves continue their road series at Washington Nationals on Sunday (1:35 PM ET). Acuña's availability for Sunday will be evaluated before game time; the day-to-day designation leaves the door open. For Atlanta at 35-16, with the NL East lead and the best record in the National League, the most important near-term goal is getting Acuña back healthy without risk of aggravation. A bone bruise with negative X-rays gives the Braves every reason to be optimistic.
