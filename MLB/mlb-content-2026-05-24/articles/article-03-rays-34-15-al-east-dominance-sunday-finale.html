Rays at 34-15: How Tampa Bay Built the AL's Best Record — And Sunday's Finale

By Jake Torres

May 24, 2026

Tampa Bay is 34-15, leading the AL East by 5.5 games over the Yankees. Saturday's game was rained out. Sunday's series finale features Drew Rasmussen against Ryan Weathers at 1:05 PM ET at Yankee Stadium.

The Tampa Bay Rays are 34-15.

That is the best record in the American League. It is a pace of approximately 110 wins over a full 162-game season. It represents a 5.5-game lead over the New York Yankees, who are themselves at 30-22 and playing competitive baseball. The gap is real.

Saturday's game at Yankee Stadium was postponed due to rain and rescheduled as part of a September 22 doubleheader. Sunday's finale brings Drew Rasmussen (4-1) to the mound for Tampa Bay against Ryan Weathers (2-2) for New York, with first pitch at 1:05 PM ET.

Tampa Bay's Model, Playing Out Again

The Rays are doing what the Rays do. Their organizational approach to roster construction — prioritizing pitching depth, defensive versatility, and analytical identification of undervalued players over high-priced free agents — keeps producing competitive teams at a fraction of the cost of their AL East competitors.

Rasmussen at 4-1 is one component of a pitching staff that has been one of the best in the league through the first third of the season. The Rays have not relied on one ace to carry their rotation; they have built depth that absorbs injuries and down stretches without significant performance decline. That organizational philosophy is reflected in the 34-15 record.

The Yankees' Gap Problem

The New York Yankees at 30-22 are playing winning baseball by any reasonable standard. Most teams would take that record heading into Memorial Day weekend. The problem for New York is not that they are playing poorly — it is that Tampa Bay is playing so well that the gap has grown to 5.5 games despite the Yankees' own competitive season.

Ryan Weathers (2-2) gets the ball Sunday against a Tampa Bay lineup that has been one of the most consistent offensive units in the AL. Closing a 5.5-game gap with 117 games remaining is possible but requires sustained excellence while the division leader falters. At 34-15, Tampa Bay has not shown signs of faltering.

What's Next

Sunday's game at Yankee Stadium (1:05 PM ET) is the series finale for this three-game set. The rainout consolidates the series to two games — and whatever the outcome, Tampa Bay goes home with the AL East lead firmly in hand. The Rays next visit New York for the September 22 doubleheader that makes up Saturday's rainout. Between now and then, the AL East race will either develop into a real pennant competition or confirm that Tampa Bay's 2026 season is already headed toward something historic.
