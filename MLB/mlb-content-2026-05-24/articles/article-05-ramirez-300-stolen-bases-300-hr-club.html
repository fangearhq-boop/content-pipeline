Ramírez at 300 Stolen Bases — Nine Home Runs from the 300-300 Club

By Marcus Cole

May 24, 2026

José Ramírez became just the second Cleveland player in franchise history to steal 300 bases in May. With 291 career home runs, he is nine away from the 300-300 club — one of baseball history's most exclusive lists.

José Ramírez doesn't do things quietly. He doesn't show up on highlight reels as often as the game's biggest stars. He doesn't attract the national coverage his production warrants. And yet, in May of 2026, he quietly reached one of the most significant milestones in Cleveland franchise history — his 300th career stolen base — while also sitting nine home runs away from one of the most exclusive lists in all of baseball.

Kenny Lofton was the only Cleveland player to ever steal 300 bases before Ramírez joined him. That is the company Ramírez keeps, and it reflects what makes him one of the most complete players of his generation.

The 300-300 Club

The 300-300 club — players who have hit at least 300 home runs and stolen at least 300 bases during their MLB career — is one of the most difficult combination milestones to achieve in baseball history. It requires sustained power and sustained speed across a career that spans 15 or more years without catastrophic injury interruption. The number of players who have accomplished it fits on one hand with some fingers to spare.

Achieving 300 stolen bases requires elite speed, timing, and baserunning instincts. Achieving 300 home runs requires consistent power production across a career. Achieving both requires a rare combination of athleticism that has produced fewer than ten career members.

Ramírez has 300 stolen bases. He has 291 career home runs. Nine more puts his name on that list permanently.

Still Elite at 33

What makes Ramírez's chase credible is not just that he is close to the number — it is that he is still performing at a level that suggests reaching it is a matter of when, not if. At 33 years old, he leads the American League in stolen bases in 2026 and continues to hit for power with a consistency that most players his age have lost.

His career arc is one of the game's most impressive stories of sustained excellence from a player who arrived with limited fanfare and built his reputation over a decade of consistent production at the highest level. The 300-300 club, if he reaches it, would be a capstone to a career that deserved more attention than it received.

What's Next

Ramírez and the Cleveland Guardians continue their series against the Philadelphia Phillies on Sunday. Every home run Ramírez hits moves him one step closer to a historical milestone that has always been within reach but never quite close enough to feel imminent. At 291 career home runs with his current pace, the 300-300 club could arrive before the 2026 All-Star break. The milestone is no longer something to project years into the future — it is happening now.
