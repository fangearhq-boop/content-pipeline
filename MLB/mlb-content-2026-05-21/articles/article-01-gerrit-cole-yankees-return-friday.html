Gerrit Cole's 569-Day Wait Ends Friday — Yankees Ace Returns Against the Rays

By Ryan Calloway

May 21, 2026

Cole hasn't thrown an MLB pitch since Game 5 of the 2024 World Series. After Tommy John surgery and 14 months of recovery, he starts Friday night in New York.

There is a moment every pitcher who undergoes Tommy John surgery thinks about during the recovery. It is not the physical moment of throwing again — the throws come gradually, incrementally, in bullpen sessions that feel like practice rather than games. The moment they think about is this one: the first time they walk out of the dugout in a real game, with a real crowd, and throw their first real pitch.

For Gerrit Cole, that moment arrives Friday night.

Cole will start for the New York Yankees against the Tampa Bay Rays on May 22 — his first major league appearance in 569 days. The last time he pitched in an MLB game was Game 5 of the 2024 World Series at Yankee Stadium. He underwent Tommy John surgery in March 2025. Fourteen months later, the 35-year-old right-hander is ready to pitch again.

What the Triple-A Starts Showed

Evaluating a pitcher's return from Tommy John surgery requires looking past the results and focusing on the arm. What are the velocity readings? How does the mechanics look? Is there discomfort?

Cole's Triple-A tune-up appearances answered those questions in the most encouraging way possible. In his most recent start for Triple-A Scranton/Wilkes-Barre, Cole averaged 97 miles per hour on his four-seam fastball and reached 99.6 mph. He threw 86 pitches across 5 1/3 innings. The arm is not just functional — it is explosive.

The velocity numbers matter because they tell you something about the surgery's success. Cole was an elite velocity pitcher before the procedure. The fact that he is throwing in the high 90s again in a Minor League rehab setting suggests his elbow has healed fully and his mechanics have been restored. That is not guaranteed after Tommy John surgery. Cole has done the work.

What New York Has Been Missing

The Yankees have been competing without their ace since the fall of 2024. New York is 30-20 through mid-May — a winning record, a playoff contender, but a team that has operated with a visible gap at the top of their rotation.

Cole was one of the most dominant starting pitchers of the last decade before the injury. His ability to pitch deep into games, to post elite strikeout numbers, to front a rotation with authority — none of that has been available to the Yankees for nearly a year and a half. Adding that back to a 30-win team changes the ceiling of what they can become.

Friday night is not just a comeback story. It is a potential turning point in the Yankees' 2026 season.

What's Next

Cole starts Friday, May 22 against the Tampa Bay Rays at Yankee Stadium. The start will be watched nationally — both for the performance and for the health of Cole's arm as he makes his first appearance since Tommy John surgery. If he is sharp, New York's rotation enters a different tier. If there are early signs of discomfort, the questions begin again. Either way, the 569-day wait is over.
