The Mets Are Selling. Francisco Lindor — Signed Through 2031 — Is Available.

By Ryan Calloway

July 19, 2026

When a team shops depth pieces and rental veterans, they're selling. When a team shops their franchise shortstop — who is signed through 2031 — they're doing something different.

The New York Mets are doing something different.

Francisco Lindor is reportedly available heading into the August 3 trade deadline. The Mets have fully committed to a sell posture, and Lindor — the face of the franchise for the last several seasons, the player who was supposed to anchor the organization's window — is on the table.

What the Mets Are Protecting

The Mets are not selling everything indiscriminately. They have a clear picture of what they're building around: Juan Soto stays. Pitching prospects Benge and Ewing are protected. Christian Scott, who showed flashes of becoming a legitimate rotation piece, is off limits. So is outfielder McLean.

Everything else is available. And in the context of what the Mets have, "everything else" includes Lindor.

That's the signal. You don't protect your young pieces and offer your franchise shortstop if you're halfheartedly trimming payroll. The Mets have made a decision about what era of Mets baseball is over and what the next one needs to look like.

The Lindor Question

Lindor is signed through 2031. Any trade would require a team to take on years of that contract and be comfortable doing so. That limits the buyer pool — only organizations with both payroll flexibility and a genuine need at shortstop make sense as destinations.

Fifteen days remain before the August 3 deadline. The window for a deal is narrow, and the contract complexity means any trade would require significant back-and-forth before it could close.

What's Next

The Mets have 15 days to execute their vision of a deadline. If Lindor moves, it signals the most complete teardown the organization has attempted in years — and sets up a rebuild around Soto and a young pitching core. If no deal gets done, New York still finishes the season with clarity about what they will and won't do. Either outcome defines the next chapter of Mets baseball. The August 3 deadline is the date that changes what the next few years look like.
