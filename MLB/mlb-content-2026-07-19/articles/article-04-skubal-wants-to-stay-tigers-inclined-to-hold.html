Tarik Skubal Wants to Stay in Detroit. The Tigers Might Actually Let Him.

By Marcus Cole

July 19, 2026

A week ago, Tarik Skubal was nearly certain to be traded before August 3. The only questions were the destination and the price.

Today, neither of those questions has a clear answer — because Skubal doesn't want to leave, and the Detroit Tigers are starting to listen.

What Skubal Said

Skubal has told people close to him that he wants to remain in Detroit. He believes the Tigers can win the World Series. That is not a statement crafted to manage his trade market or protect his leverage. That's a pitcher who is invested in what his team is building and wants to be part of what happens next.

That kind of statement changes how a front office has to think about a trade. You can move a player who'd prefer another city without much internal friction. Moving a player who wants to stay — especially one of Skubal's stature — requires a different conversation.

The Tigers' Position

Detroit's asking price for Skubal has been described as "outrageous" by rival executives. That characterization has functioned as a natural brake on the market. The Cubs, Braves, and Rays are all still engaged. But none of them has apparently met the Tigers' threshold — and the threshold keeps getting harder to reach as Detroit's own season progresses.

The Tigers are increasingly inclined to keep Skubal. That inclination is informed by both his personal wishes and by the organization's honest assessment of where they stand. If the team believes it can compete, trading its best starting pitcher becomes harder to justify regardless of what comes back in return.

The Deadline Math

Fifteen days remain before August 3. The conventional wisdom in deadline coverage is that most trades happen in the final 72 hours — the pressure of the clock forcing decisions that weeks of negotiation couldn't produce. That dynamic still applies here. A team could meet Detroit's price at 11:59 PM on August 3 and Skubal could be gone.

But for the first time since this story began, the base case is not a trade. The base case is that Skubal stays, the Cubs call someone else's number, and the Tigers find out what they're capable of with their ace in the rotation.

What's Next

The deadline is August 3. Every day between now and then is a day the Cubs, Braves, and Rays can make their case — and a day the Tigers can decide they've heard enough. If Skubal is still in Detroit at 12:01 AM on August 4, the story becomes about what the Tigers do with him in the second half. That's the story Skubal wants to be in. The question is whether the Tigers let it happen.
