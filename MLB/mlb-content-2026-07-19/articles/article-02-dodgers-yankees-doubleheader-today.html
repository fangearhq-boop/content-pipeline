The Rain Made It Better. Dodgers-Yankees Doubleheader Is Today.

By Jake Torres

July 19, 2026

Saturday's Dodgers-Yankees game at Yankee Stadium never happened. Rain pushed it off the schedule before first pitch, and now Sunday carries the weight of two games instead of one.

Game 1 starts at 12:35 PM ET. Game 2 is at 7:20 PM ET on NBC and Peacock. The best team in baseball plays the most storied franchise in the sport — twice, in the same day, for the first time since the 2024 World Series put them in the same building.

What's At Stake

The Los Angeles Dodgers are 62-36, the best record in Major League Baseball. They've been operating at that level for months. The Yankees entered the series at 54-43, holding the first AL Wild Card spot in the American League East — a position they need to protect without their most important player.

Aaron Judge remains on the injured list with a rib injury. The Yankees went to their first scheduled game of this series without him, lost 2-1 on a seventh-inning home run by Max Muncy, and now face two more games against the team that beat them in October with the same limitation. Judge's best-case return is August, and August is not today.

The Series Context

This is the first time the Dodgers and Yankees have met since the 2024 World Series. Los Angeles won that series. The Dodgers have carried that into 2026 — the roster that won the championship is largely intact, reinforced and healthy, and running at a pace that suggests another October appearance is the expectation, not the aspiration.

For New York, this series is a test of where they stand against the best. Playing without Judge against the Dodgers' full rotation and lineup is not a comfortable position for any team. The Yankees need their pitching staff to keep games close and their offense to manufacture runs without their most dangerous bat.

The Broadcast

Game 2 tonight at 7:20 PM ET airs on NBC and Peacock — national broadcast, prime time, the kind of game that lands in front of casual fans who don't watch regularly. Two famous franchises, a World Series rematch, and a Sunday night window. The rain that erased Saturday made this game matter more.

What's Next

Two games today. Game 1 at 12:35 PM ET is the makeup of the rained-out Saturday contest. Game 2 at 7:20 PM ET on NBC/Peacock is the original Sunday night slate. After today, the Dodgers return to Los Angeles; the Yankees stay home and prepare for what comes next in a pennant race they're navigating without their anchor. Shohei Ohtani is expected to take the mound for Los Angeles on July 22 against the Phillies — his return to the rotation after a knee procedure. Today is a doubleheader. Tomorrow the baseball calendar keeps moving.
