The Red Sox Trailed by Three in the 7th and Won Anyway. That's 12 in a Row.

By Marcus Cole

July 19, 2026

There is a version of what the Boston Red Sox did Saturday night that could be explained away. A bullpen implosion. A hot hand. A team that gets lucky on the right nights.

Then you look at the scoreboard: Red Sox 7, Rays 6. Boston trailed 6-3 entering the seventh inning. They scored four runs in three innings against a Tampa Bay team fighting for its playoff life.

That's 12 consecutive wins. The franchise's longest streak since June 2006.

How the Comeback Happened

The moment that changed the game came off the bat of Wilyer Abreu. With Boston trailing by three, Abreu drove a two-run home run off Evan Cleavinger to cut the deficit to one. The swing had the quality of a player who has been locked in for days — which Abreu has been.

He now has four home runs in his last two games.

Jahmai Jones added another home run in his first start since being traded to Boston. Newly acquired players don't always contribute immediately. Jones didn't wait. He went deep in his debut in a Red Sox uniform, and the offense piled on from there to take the lead and hold it.

The Streak in Context

On June 24, the Boston Red Sox were 14 games under .500. The trade deadline conversations happening in Boston involved which veterans to move, not which prospects to acquire. The season, for all practical purposes, looked over.

Twelve wins later, Boston is 49-48. One game over .500 for the first time since April. They're in the American League Wild Card picture, tied for the third and final spot with the Minnesota Twins but holding a one-game edge in the loss column.

The franchise record for consecutive wins is 18, set in June 2006 during the era when they were defending World Series champions. They're not approaching that. But the 2006 team had different expectations. This team was supposed to be gone by now.

What This Means for the Deadline

The Red Sox were sellers two weeks ago. That calculus has been reversed. Boston is now a team that needs to protect what it has, not dismantle it — and the front office will need to decide whether to add at the margins or stand pat and let the streak carry them.

Interim manager Chad Tracy has built something cohesive enough to rally from three runs down in the seventh inning of a game they could have lost. That doesn't happen by accident, and it doesn't happen without belief.

What's Next

The Red Sox enter Sunday holding a Wild Card spot and owning the longest winning streak in baseball. Each game now is a referendum on whether this is real — and 12 straight wins is a compelling answer to anyone still asking the question. The August 3 deadline is 15 days away. For the first time all season, Boston's trade posture is that of a team going somewhere.
