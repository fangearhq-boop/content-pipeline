Four Teams. Three Spots. The AL Wild Card Race Just Got Real.

By Jake Torres

July 19, 2026

Two weeks ago the American League Wild Card race had three teams in three spots with a clear gap behind them. One of those spots has since been occupied by a team that wasn't in the conversation at all on July 5.

The Boston Red Sox have won 12 consecutive games. The AL Wild Card picture has changed completely.

The Standings

Here is where things stand entering Sunday, July 19:

The New York Yankees hold the first Wild Card spot at 54-43. They're the most comfortable team in this group — enough of a cushion that Sunday's games don't change their positioning dramatically. The Cleveland Guardians are at 51-46 in the second spot, similarly secure for now.

The third and final Wild Card spot is a fight. The Minnesota Twins (49-49) and Red Sox (49-48) are essentially tied, with Boston holding a one-game edge in the loss column. The Twins have been here for weeks. The Red Sox arrived on a 12-game winning streak.

The White Sox (51-45) lead the AL Central and would enter the Wild Card bracket as the three-seed if the season ended today — which means the Central race and the Wild Card race are directly connected. The Rays (56-40) lead the AL East. The Rangers (49-48) sit at the edge of the AL West picture.

How the Red Sox Got Here

On June 24, Boston was 37-50, fourteen games under .500. The trade deadline conversations in the front office were about which veterans to move. The team looked like one that had run out of season.

Twelve wins later — including a Saturday night comeback from 6-3 down in the seventh inning — the Red Sox are 49-48, tied for the final Wild Card spot and holding a one-game edge in the loss column over the Twins.

That's not a correction. That's a reversal. The same roster that was heading for a fire sale is now a team that makes comebacks when trailing by three with three innings left.

The Deadline Implications

Every team in the Wild Card picture is now a holder or a buyer at the August 3 deadline. None of them can afford to add to a deficit by trading veterans. The Red Sox — who were sellers two weeks ago — are now looking at the deadline as an opportunity to add, not subtract. The race directly impacts how the market shapes up in 15 days.

What's Next

The third Wild Card spot is a coin flip between two teams with nothing to separate them. The Red Sox are winning with momentum; the Twins are holding on with a roster that hasn't found a gear. Every game between now and August 3 matters in the standings and at the trade deadline. The AL Wild Card race is, for the first time all season, a legitimate four-team fight with no obvious outcome.
