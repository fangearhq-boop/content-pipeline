Two games, two different outcomes, one left to play.

The Phillies and Dodgers wrapped up a split Saturday night in Los Angeles and are now set for a rubber game Sunday at Dodger Stadium. Game 1 went to the Dodgers, 4-2. Game 2 went to the Phillies, 4-3, after Tanner Scott blew his first save of 2026 and Edmundo Sosa crushed a two-run home run in the eighth inning. Now it comes down to one game.

Rubber game baseball in May with playoff implications carries a particular weight. Neither team can afford to walk away from this series with a loss. Both have enough on the line that winning it would mean something real heading into June.

How the Series Got Here

The Dodgers won Game 1 comfortably. Their offense did the work in a 4-2 result, and the series opened in their favor. Entering Game 2, the Dodgers had won six straight games and looked like the most dangerous team in the National League West by some distance.

Roki Sasaki gave them 5.1 innings Saturday — seven strikeouts, one earned run, a performance that should have been the foundation of a series-clinching win. Then the eighth inning arrived. Scott, who had not allowed a run in 13 straight appearances, was touched for a pair of singles, then Sosa's home run, then Harper's RBI single. The 3-1 Dodgers lead became a 4-3 Phillies lead in a matter of minutes. The six-game winning streak was over.

The Phillies took the game on the kind of two-out clutch hitting that defines their best moments. Schwarber and Harper in the middle of a late-inning rally is a dangerous combination, and the Dodgers experienced exactly why Saturday night.

What the Dodgers Need

The Dodgers need to bounce back — emotionally from the blown save and practically from the uncertainty it created in their bullpen. Scott will presumably be available Sunday; how he is deployed, and whether manager Dave Roberts leans on him in a high-leverage situation this quickly, will be one of the storylines heading into the game.

Sasaki won't be available to start Sunday after going 5.1 innings the day before. The Dodgers will need another starting option or a bullpen game — the decision they make will define their pitching approach for the series finale.

Their offense was capable in Game 1. In Game 2, they took a 3-1 lead and didn't extend it when they had chances. Squandering leads against good bullpens is how teams lose close series, and the Phillies' bullpen is good. The Dodgers can't give the Philadelphia hitters extra opportunities.

What the Phillies Need

The Phillies arrive at Sunday with momentum from Saturday's comeback. Momentum is real in baseball, and taking a lead through grit in the late innings is the kind of win that carries over. The bench and bullpen feel better. The hitters approach their next game with the memory of what they just did.

Schwarber leads MLB in home runs. Harper has been one of the most consistent hitters in the NL. The Phillies have a lineup capable of making any pitcher uncomfortable at any point in a game. They don't need to do anything differently than what worked Saturday — stay patient, execute two-out situations, and trust that their talent gives them a chance to win the series.

What they cannot do is get into a situation where Dodger Stadium's crowd and environment put them on their heels. Road rubber games are hard. The Phillies have the roster to handle that pressure; they just need to play their game.

The Series Implications

For the Dodgers, taking a series at home against one of the NL's best lineups affirms their status as the NL West frontrunner and a legitimate World Series contender. Dropping it at home — after a blown save, with a home crowd expecting more — would be a different kind of result.

For the Phillies, winning this series in Los Angeles would be a statement. Kyle Schwarber on pace for 66 home runs, Bryce Harper in the middle of the lineup, and a road series win over the Dodgers would cement them as the team to beat in the NL East.

Rubber game baseball produces the clearest answers. Sunday will give both teams exactly what they're looking for — or exactly what they've been dreading.

First pitch is today at Dodger Stadium.
