Sunday, May 31. The last day of May. Fifteen MLB games on the schedule, standings about to roll over into June, and a full day of baseball for anyone willing to commit to the couch.

Here's everything you need to watch.

Cubs vs. Cardinals — 7 PM ET, Sunday Night Baseball

The headliner of the day is an NL Central rivalry game that feels bigger this season than it has in recent years. The Cubs have been one of the better stories in the National League in 2026, putting together the kind of consistent, well-rounded performance that suggests they're serious about their division. The Cardinals have been fighting to stay relevant in a Central that was supposed to belong to them.

Cubs-Cardinals is one of the genuine blood rivalries in baseball. The history between these franchises runs deep, and both sets of fans treat every series as meaningful regardless of what the standings say. When the standings actually matter, the games carry extra weight.

This one has implications for how the NL Central shapes up heading into June. Watch it.

Phillies vs. Dodgers Game 3 — Dodger Stadium

The series is tied at one game each. Saturday night's Phillies comeback win — courtesy of Edmundo Sosa's two-run home run and Bryce Harper's RBI single off Tanner Scott in the eighth — set up exactly the kind of rubber match Sunday that baseball fans want to see.

Two of the NL's best teams, one game to decide the series, at one of baseball's most iconic venues. Kyle Schwarber comes in leading MLB in home runs. Harper has been delivering in clutch situations all year. The Dodgers need to bounce back at home after giving away a 3-1 eighth-inning lead.

If there's a game to watch before 7 PM, this is it.

Mariners vs. Diamondbacks — 4:10 PM ET, Peacock

The Mariners have been building quietly in the AL West in 2026. Streaming on Peacock at 4:10 PM, this series finale matters for their positioning as the division race intensifies. The Diamondbacks have been inconsistent but remain dangerous — they're a team with enough talent to go on a run at any point.

This is the kind of afternoon series finale that sometimes produces great games because both teams need a win. The Mariners are a team worth investing attention in if you haven't already.

Rays vs. Angels — Afternoon Game

Tampa Bay has been trying to hold their ground in the AL East, a division that never stops being competitive. The Rays hosting the Angels in a Sunday afternoon game matters for their positioning — the AL East standings are tight, and every series has implications for who's genuinely in the hunt.

The Bigger Picture Entering June

Before the calendar flips, here's where the major storylines stand:

Munetaka Murakami of the White Sox is on the injured list with a Grade 2 hamstring strain and will miss approximately four weeks. He had 20 home runs — tied for the AL lead — before going down. His injury reshapes both Chicago's Wild Card hopes and the shape of the home run race.

Kyle Schwarber now leads MLB in home runs alone. He hit his 20th on the earliest date in major league history, surpassing Luis Gonzalez's 2001 record, and is currently on pace for approximately 66 home runs. The last time anyone hit 60 in a season was Barry Bonds in 2001.

The AL East, AL Wild Card, NL Central, and NL Wild Card races are all tight. June is when the contenders start to separate from the pretenders, and today's games are the beginning of that process.

Fifteen games. One full Sunday. Pick your poison and settle in.
