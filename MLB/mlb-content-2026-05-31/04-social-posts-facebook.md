### STORY 2: Tanner Scott Blown Save — Phillies 4-3 Dodgers

**Long-Form Post**

For 13 consecutive appearances, Tanner Scott was untouchable. 1.14 ERA. 0 runs allowed since April 24. The Dodgers' closer was one of the best relievers in baseball.

Then came the eighth inning on Saturday night.

Roki Sasaki had been tremendous — 5.1 innings, 7 strikeouts, just the one Alec Bohm homer in the second. The Dodgers led 3-1 entering the eighth and Scott jogged in to close it out.

He allowed a pair of singles. Then, with two outs, Edmundo Sosa crushed a 2-run homer just over the left field wall. Tied. Bryce Harper added an RBI single to finish it off. Phillies 4, Dodgers 3.

First blown save of 2026 for Scott. Dodgers' 6-game win streak, snapped. Series, tied at 1-1.

This was a great game by any measure — Sasaki looked like a legitimate ace, and the comeback required two-out clutch hitting at its finest. But the one thing everyone will remember is Scott's first blown save of the year, and the brief flashback it created to his 10-blown-save nightmare in 2025.
