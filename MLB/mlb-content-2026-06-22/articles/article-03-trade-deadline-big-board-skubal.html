Trade Deadline Big Board: Six Weeks Out, the Skubal Sweepstakes Have Already Begun

By Ryan Calloway

June 22, 2026

The August 3 trade deadline is six weeks away. The biggest conversation in every front office with championship aspirations has already started. And it has one name at the center of it: Tarik Skubal.

The two-time American League Cy Young Award winner is pitching for the Detroit Tigers — a team that is going nowhere — and will be one of the most coveted trade assets in recent memory. The teams that want him read like a who's-who of National and American League contenders. The price will be extraordinary. And someone is going to pay it.

Skubal: The Centerpiece

Understanding why Skubal is the defining piece of this deadline requires understanding how rare elite starting pitching is on the trade market. Teams rarely make true No. 1 starters available. When they do, it is almost always because circumstances — a rebuilding effort, a contract dispute, a catastrophic record — force the issue. In Detroit's case, all of those factors are converging.

Skubal returned from bone chip surgery on his elbow this season and has been pitching as expected: as one of the best starters in the American League. His stuff hasn't diminished. His track record — two consecutive Cy Young Awards — is the strongest available on any market in years. And the Tigers, sitting among the teams at least 5.5 games out of a playoff spot as of mid-June, have every incentive to sell.

The teams linked to Skubal include the Atlanta Braves, New York Yankees, Los Angeles Dodgers, Chicago Cubs, Toronto Blue Jays, and San Diego Padres. That list spans both leagues and covers franchises at different stages of their competitive windows. What they have in common is the belief that adding a pitcher of Skubal's caliber gives them a meaningful upgrade in October.

The price will reflect his value. Whoever lands Skubal will have to give up top prospects — multiple of them — and quite possibly a package that includes MLB-ready talent. Acquiring a 29-year-old ace under team control is not cheap, and no one in those front offices is pretending otherwise.

The Sellers

Beyond Skubal, the trade market is shaping up to be more active than usual. Seven teams are at least 5.5 games out of a playoff spot as of mid-June: the San Francisco Giants, Colorado Rockies, New York Mets, Los Angeles Angels, Detroit Tigers, Kansas City Royals, and Boston Red Sox.

The Giants represent the most significant organizational pivot on the seller side. San Francisco entered the season with legitimate playoff aspirations and was categorized as a buyer heading into the summer. That has changed. After a difficult first half, the Giants have shifted toward selling, which opens up their roster for trade conversations. Their movable assets — veterans on expiring contracts, high-value bullpen pieces — are drawing interest.

Detroit has additional inventory beyond Skubal. Casey Mize, who carries a 2.27 ERA, is another arm that contenders have noticed. The Tigers' bullpen, including Aroldis Chapman — who entered the season with a 0.46 ERA and 17 saves — represents real upgrade potential for teams in need of late-inning depth.

Boston, also among the sellers, has inventory to move. The Red Sox's position at or near the bottom of a competitive AL East makes trading for the future an obvious path.

The Buyers

On the buyer side, the landscape is defined by the obvious contenders and a few surprising names. The Atlanta Braves, sitting at 46-25 as of mid-June and carrying the best record in the National League, are the clearest aggressive buyer on the market. Acquiring Skubal would give them a rotation that could compete with any staff in baseball through October.

The Yankees and Dodgers are perennial deadline buyers who have the prospect capital to make significant moves. Both have playoff expectations regardless of how their regular seasons unfold, and adding elite starting pitching is the most reliable way to improve October odds.

The surprising names on the buyer side are worth noting. The Chicago White Sox, Washington Nationals, and St. Louis Cardinals have each positioned themselves as unexpected contenders in their respective divisions or wild card races. A deadline that seemed likely to be dominated by the usual elite franchises now has some underdog energy. Those organizations may not be able to afford Skubal, but they will be active — adding pieces to sustain or extend their competitive windows.

The Timeline

Six weeks is a long time in baseball. Teams that are 5.5 games out in mid-June have come back to win divisions before. Teams that look like dominant buyers can stumble and reassess. The deadline calculus is always more complex in late August than it looks in late June.

But the Skubal conversation is happening now, not in late July. When a player of his caliber becomes available, the evaluation process — scouting, prospect valuations, contract projections — begins months before the deadline. Teams don't scramble at the last minute for a two-time Cy Young winner. They prepare.

Mark August 3 on the calendar. The trade deadline is six weeks out. The most consequential name on the market is already clear.

What's Next

The standings over the next three to four weeks will define who is buying aggressively and who is buying cautiously. If the Giants fall further back, their fire sale accelerates. If any of the surprise contenders — the White Sox, Nationals, or Cardinals — fade, they pivot to seller mode and add more inventory to a market already rich with options.

Skubal's performance between now and the deadline will also matter. Every good start strengthens his value. Every question about his elbow health — he is returning from surgery — creates leverage in negotiations. The Detroit front office will be watching his outings just as closely as every rival scout in the press box.

The deadline is August 3 at 6 p.m. ET. The sweepstakes have already begun.
