NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The Royals find out today whether Bobby Witt Jr. goes on the injured list or returns for the Tampa Bay series. The Kansas City shortstop was held out Sunday after exiting Friday with a Grade 1 MCL sprain in his right knee. Manager Matt Quatraro described the diagnosis as a "best case scenario" — but Witt is now missing his second game in a row, and the Royals said he'd be re-evaluated before Monday's series opener against the Rays. Here's what makes this more than just an injury update: Witt is the best player in the American League right now. He leads the AL with 4.5 fWAR. He leads AL shortstops in All-Star voting. His MVP case is one of the strongest in baseball. And the All-Star Game is July 14 in Philadelphia — right in the middle of the recovery window for a Grade 1 sprain. A Grade 1 MCL sprain is the mildest classification — no ligament tearing, just minor fiber damage. The typical recovery window is 1-3 weeks. If Witt goes on a minimum 10-day IL, he could be back in late June or early July, giving him time to recover and play in the All-Star Game. If the Royals keep him on the active roster and manage his workload, he might return as soon as this week. The stakes are real. Witt missing meaningful time would hurt an already struggling Royals team — and could impact his All-Star participation and MVP standing. Today's evaluation is the answer. ### Image Post Caption Bobby Witt Jr.'s status is decided today. Grade 1 MCL sprain. IL or return vs Tampa Bay? Schwarber at 28 — On Pace for One of the Greatest HR Seasons in History
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Kyle Schwarber is doing something that barely anyone in baseball history has ever done. After hitting three home runs Saturday in the Phillies' 15-3 demolition of the Mets — pushing his total to 28 — Schwarber is now on pace for 60+ home runs on the season. To put that in perspective: fewer players have ever hit 60 home runs in a season than you can count on one hand. It's one of the rarest individual achievements in baseball. His three-homer game Saturday was remarkable in its own right. He launched his 26th at 456 feet. He went deep twice in the third inning alone. His 28th — a two-run shot that barely stayed fair inside the right-field foul pole — stood as his season total heading into Monday. He leads Yordan Alvarez and Byron Buxton (both at 24) by four. And there's a subplot worth monitoring: the Home Run Derby. It's July 14. At Citizens Bank Park. Schwarber's home stadium. The Phillies' power-hitter having the season of his life, competing in the Derby in front of his home crowd, is a scenario the sport and the city of Philadelphia are buzzing about. He hasn't committed to entering — but he hasn't ruled it out either. There's also the teammate angle. Saturday's performance made Schwarber and Bryce Harper just the second pair of teammates in MLB history to hit 3+ home runs and complete a cycle in the same game. The first? Lou Gehrig and Tony Lazzeri. Yankees. June 1932. Schwarber is 28 home runs into one of the most historic individual offensive seasons in recent memory. The summer has barely started. ### Image Post Caption Kyle Schwarber: 28 HRs, on pace for 60+, Derby at his home park in 3 weeks. This Phillies slugger is having one for the ages. ⚾ #Phillies #MLB

Rice has become the offensive engine for a Yankees team that was expected to struggle without Judge. The Rays are no pushover. Tampa Bay has been competitive all year and entered Monday one series win away from reclaiming first place. This is the kind of division race that will go down to the final week of the regular season. Aaron Judge returns in August. Ben Rice keeps the ship sailing until then.
