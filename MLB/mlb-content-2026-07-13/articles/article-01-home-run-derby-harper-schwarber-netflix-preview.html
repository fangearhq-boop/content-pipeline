The Home Run Derby Is Tonight: Harper, Schwarber, and the Rematch Nobody Forgot

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 13, 2026

In 2018, the MLB All-Star Home Run Derby was held at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. Bryce Harper won it. Kyle Schwarber was in the final. Philadelphia went into that summer night with two of its own competing on its own field, and Harper walked out with the trophy.

It is 2026. The Derby is back at Citizens Bank Park. Harper and Schwarber are both in it. One of them is going to win it tonight.

The 2026 Home Run Derby begins at 8 PM ET on Netflix — the first time the event has ever aired on the platform, and the first time in eight years that Citizens Bank Park has hosted it.

The Field

Eight players will compete tonight: Bryce Harper (Philadelphia Phillies), Kyle Schwarber (Philadelphia Phillies), Junior Caminero (Tampa Bay Rays), Munetaka Murakami (Chicago White Sox), Jordan Walker (St. Louis Cardinals), Ben Rice (New York Yankees), Jac Caglianone (Kansas City Royals), and Willson Contreras (Boston Red Sox).

Schwarber enters as the favorite. He leads Major League Baseball with 30 home runs through 82 games this season and was the NL home run leader last year with 56. His raw power is the obvious headline. But the more interesting number is the context: he reached the final of the 2018 Derby at this exact venue and lost to the man standing across the bracket from him tonight.

Harper is chasing history. He won here in 2018, making him one of only three players ever to win the Derby in their home ballpark. No player has ever won it twice in their home park. Tonight he has a chance to be the first.

The New Format

The format is different this year. The Derby has moved away from the timed structure that defined the previous era and toward a swing-count model. Round 1 gives each player 20 swings. Round 2 gives the top four a head-to-head bracket with 15 swings each. The Final is 15 swings. Ties in any head-to-head round are settled by a 3-swing swing-off.

One additional rule worth noting: if a player hits a home run on their final swing of a round, they continue swinging until they do not hit a home run. This rewards the player who is the hottest at the end of a round rather than the one who was hot at the start.

The result is a format that rewards pure, sustained power. There is no clock to run out. There is no counting-down drama that ends on a non-homer. Hit it out or your round is over.

Who Else Could Win

The event is not simply a two-man contest. Junior Caminero, 23 years old and one of the most dynamic power hitters in the Tampa Bay Rays organization, could become the youngest Derby champion in history if he wins. Jordan Walker (Cardinals) hit a home run pace that got him here for a reason. Munetaka Murakami's inclusion is itself a storyline — a Japanese-born rookie competing in one of baseball's marquee showcase events in his debut season.

Ben Rice and Jac Caglianone both entered the season as names to watch. Willson Contreras, now in Boston, is the veteran presence in a field that trends young.

What's Next

The 2026 MLB All-Star Home Run Derby starts tonight at 8 PM ET, streaming on Netflix from Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. The All-Star Game follows tomorrow, July 14, at 8 PM ET on FOX. For the Phillies faithful in Philadelphia, this is two consecutive nights of baseball's biggest platform arriving at their home — and for at least one of the two hometown entries, tonight is a chance to repeat a moment the city hasn't forgotten since 2018.
