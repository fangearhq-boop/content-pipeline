AL 6, NL 1: Nathan Flewelling Wins the Futures Game MVP — and the White Sox Deserve a Closer Look

By Ryan Calloway | Ballpark Banter | July 13, 2026

The American League defeated the National League 6-1 in the 2026 MLB Futures Game yesterday at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. Nathan Flewelling, a catcher in the Tampa Bay Rays organization, was named MVP after hitting a 365-foot, two-run home run in the sixth inning that extended the American League lead to 4-1.

Flewelling is the third Rays prospect to win the Futures Game MVP award. Tampa Bay's pipeline continues to produce names worth tracking.

How the Game Played Out

The American League scored first on an RBI groundout by Franklin Arias that drove in Leo De Vries for a 1-0 lead. The National League tied it when Jesus Made drove in Edward Florentino to make it 1-1. The American League retook the lead on a Theo Gillen RBI single — a throwing error on the play advanced Gillen — and then Flewelling's home run made it 4-1.

JoJo Parker added an RBI double that scored Kevin Alvarez to push it to 5-1, and Ralphy Velazquez drove in Parker for the final margin. The final: AL 6, NL 1.

Other standouts from the game included Jesus Made, Kade Anderson, Seth Hernandez, and Leo De Vries on the NL side. The American League controlled the contest from the third inning onward.

The White Sox Story Nobody Is Telling

The Futures Game spotlight also lands — indirectly — on a team that deserves more attention than it's getting: the Chicago White Sox.

White Sox rookie Munetaka Murakami is competing in tonight's Home Run Derby at Citizens Bank Park — the first night of All-Star festivities. The inclusion of a White Sox player in the Derby spotlight reflects something that has been building quietly all year: this organization is ahead of schedule.

The White Sox entered 2026 as a team most observers expected to continue rebuilding. Instead, they sit at 48-45, leading the AL Central at the All-Star break. They drafted shortstop Roch Cholowsky from UCLA with the No. 1 overall pick in the 2026 MLB Draft. They have five prospects in MLB Pipeline's 2026 Top 100 — tied for fourth most in baseball, alongside the Dodgers, Marlins, Pirates, and Cardinals — led by outfielder Braden Montgomery at No. 36.

The full Top 100 White Sox contingent: Braden Montgomery (OF, No. 36), Noah Schultz (LHP, No. 49), Caleb Bonemer (INF, No. 61), Hagen Smith (LHP, No. 72), and Billy Carlson (SS, No. 73). Add Cholowsky to the incoming pipeline and Murakami to the current roster, and the infrastructure is real.

This is a team with a young core in the majors, an elite prospect pipeline, and a first-place position at the break. Nobody wrote them up in April. They're still standing in July.

What's Next

The 2026 MLB season resumes after the All-Star break. The White Sox will return to action defending their AL Central lead with 69 games remaining. The trade deadline is August 3, and Chicago's position as an unexpected contender could make them buyers rather than sellers — a scenario no one projected at the start of the year. Roch Cholowsky and the rest of the incoming pipeline give this team a runway that extends well beyond 2026. The first chapter of this story may already be the best part.
