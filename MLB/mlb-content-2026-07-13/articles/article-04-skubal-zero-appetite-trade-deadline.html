Tarik Skubal Says He Has "Zero Appetite to Be Traded." The Tigers Have Three Weeks.

By Jake Torres | Ballpark Banter | July 13, 2026

Tarik Skubal told friends this week that he "badly wants to stay in Detroit the rest of the season" and has "zero appetite to be traded."

The trade deadline is August 3 — 21 days away.

Skubal is the two-time reigning AL Cy Young Award winner. He won it in 2024 unanimously — all 30 first-place votes — and repeated in 2025 when he went 13-6 with a 2.21 ERA, 241 strikeouts, and a 0.891 WHIP. He is in a contract year. Extension talks with the Tigers are described by one league insider as "nil." He is expected to seek at least $400 million on the open market in free agency after this season. The Yankees and Phillies have already been identified as early leaders in any potential trade sweepstakes.

He is 28 years old. He is the best pitcher available at the deadline by a significant margin. And he does not want to go.

The Business Case Against His Preference

The Tigers are not a playoff team. They sit near the bottom of the AL standings. The window that Skubal believes exists — he reportedly feels they have "a legitimate shot at the World Series" — is not a window the front office can realistically act on before August 3.

The business case for trading him is straightforward. A two-time defending Cy Young winner in a contract year will command the largest return of any player at this deadline. The Tigers will not re-sign him at $400+ million. If they hold on to him and he walks in free agency, they get nothing. If they trade him now, they get a significant package of prospects or major league pieces that can actually accelerate whatever rebuild or transition is underway.

The team that acquires Skubal gets one of the most dominant pitchers in baseball for the second half of the season and potentially a playoff run. The bidding will be real.

What His Preference Does and Does Not Mean

Players say they want to stay. Players get traded. These things are not mutually exclusive. The front office controls this decision, not the player. Skubal making his feelings known to friends — and those feelings becoming public — is not the same as the Tigers committing to keep him.

That said, a player's preference matters on the margins. If the Tigers can find an extension that works, Skubal's stated desire to stay in Detroit makes the conversation easier. But extension talks are "nil" right now. Three weeks is not a lot of time to go from "nil" to a nine-figure deal that satisfies both sides.

The more likely scenario is a trade. Not because Skubal's feelings don't matter, but because the math is what it is.

What's Next

The August 3 MLB trade deadline is the forcing function. The Tigers will make a decision before that date. Whatever the outcome — trade, extension announcement, or the unlikely hold — this is the central story of the second half. Skubal pitching for Detroit in July is the same Skubal who wins Cy Youngs. Skubal pitching for the Yankees or Phillies in October would look like a different kind of story entirely. Three weeks. Something has to happen.
