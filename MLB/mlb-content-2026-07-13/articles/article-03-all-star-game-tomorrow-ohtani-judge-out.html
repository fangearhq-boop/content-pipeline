Ohtani Is Out. Judge Is Out. The All-Star Game Is Tomorrow.

By Marcus Cole | Ballpark Banter | July 13, 2026

The 96th MLB All-Star Game is tomorrow night — July 14, 8 PM ET, at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. FOX and FOX One will carry the broadcast. And for the first time since the park opened in 2004, Philadelphia is hosting the Midsummer Classic.

There is a lot to look forward to. The event carries a USA 250 theme marking the 250th anniversary of the signing of the Declaration of Independence, with special patriotic jerseys featuring red, white, blue, and stars-and-stripes graphics. Citizens Bank Park has hosted Phillies postseason runs, World Series games, and — last night — the Home Run Derby. The All-Star Game is the capstone.

But the biggest storyline going into tomorrow is an absence, not a presence.

Ohtani and Judge Are Both Out

Shohei Ohtani will not play. He is dealing with left knee irritation — fluid has been drained from the knee, and the pain is aggravated when he pitches. He skipped his final scheduled start before the All-Star break. The Dodgers are managing his condition carefully.

Aaron Judge will not play either. Judge has been out since late May with a fractured rib. He is using the All-Star break to have the rib re-imaged. There is no timeline for his return to the lineup.

The two biggest stars in baseball — the two players who most define the current era of the game — will both be watching from home tomorrow night. Front Office Sports described their joint absence as "a sizable blow for MLB" and noted the potential ratings impact for FOX.

The blow is real. Ohtani and Judge are not just popular names; they are the anchors of the All-Star conversation for casual fans. Without them, tomorrow's game requires a different marketing pitch.

What the Game Still Has

The absence of two players does not empty a roster. The All-Star Game brings together the best players from across the league. The pitching lineups remain formidable. Position players from both leagues were selected based on performance, not names, and the game will feature players who earned their spots through the first half.

The USA 250 theming is a genuine spectacle. The jerseys are the kind of limited-edition aesthetics that create moments people photograph and share. Philadelphia as a host city, on the 250th anniversary of the Declaration of Independence, in the week when Citizens Bank Park also hosted the Derby — there is an energy to this setting that goes beyond any individual player's status.

Justin Verlander's presence as the AL Legend Pick adds a meaningful subplot. His retirement announcement this week, paired with a 10th All-Star appearance as the League's honored retiree, gives the night its emotional center: the end of one of the great careers of the modern era, acknowledged on the All-Star stage.

What's Next

The 96th MLB All-Star Game is tomorrow, July 14, at 8 PM ET on FOX and FOX One, at Citizens Bank Park in Philadelphia. The game follows last night's Home Run Derby and marks the midpoint of the 2026 MLB season. After the break, all 30 teams return to action with the second half of the schedule. The August 3 trade deadline follows shortly after.
