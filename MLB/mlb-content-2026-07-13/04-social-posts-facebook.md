### STORY 2:

**Long-Form Post:**

Justin Verlander is retiring at the end of the 2026 season.

The announcement came this week, with Verlander currently on the 60-day injured list with hip and hamstring injuries. Commissioner Rob Manfred honored him with the AL "Legend Pick" designation — a special selection to give him his 10th All-Star appearance.

The numbers are staggering: 266 wins. 3,554 strikeouts. 3.33 ERA. 21 seasons. 3 Cy Young Awards. 1 unanimous MVP. 2 World Series titles.

What separates Verlander is the decade span. He won Cy Youngs in 2011, 2019, and 2022 — three awards across three different decades of baseball. He won the 2011 AL MVP unanimously and took the Pitching Triple Crown that same season. He was the dominant ace of his generation through his 20s and 30s, then had Tommy John surgery in his mid-30s, returned, and won his third Cy Young at age 39.

He came to the big leagues with the Detroit Tigers in 2005. He played in Houston and New York in between. And he is finishing where he started, in Detroit, one more time.

"I've realized that time has come." 

That's the whole statement. That's enough.

Here is the situation: Skubal won the AL Cy Young in 2024 (unanimously — all 30 first-place votes) and again in 2025. He's in a contract year. Extension talks with the Tigers are described by one league insider as "nil." He's expected to seek at least $400 million in free agency after this season. The Yankees and Phillies are already identified as early leaders in any potential trade.

The Tigers aren't in playoff contention.

So you have a two-time Cy Young winner, in a contract year, on a losing team, with three weeks to the August 3 trade deadline — and he's publicly saying he wants to stay.

The front office will weigh this. They always do. His preferences matter and so does the return package. But the business case for trading him is overwhelming. Teams don't often get a two-time defending Cy Young winner on the market.

Three weeks. Something has to happen.
