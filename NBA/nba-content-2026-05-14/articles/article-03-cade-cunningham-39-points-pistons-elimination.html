Cade's 39-Point War Goes to Waste: Pistons Face Elimination After Heartbreaking OT Loss

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 14, 2026

There may not be a more accurate summary of what happened in Detroit on Wednesday night than this: Cade Cunningham scored 39 points and 9 assists and the Pistons still went home losers.

The Cleveland Cavaliers won 117-113 in overtime, taking a 3-2 series lead that leaves Detroit one loss from the end of its season. And for a city that has embraced this young, hungry team all year long, the manner of this defeat will cut especially deep.

A Performance That Deserved to Win

Cunningham was at his absolute best. He attacked the rim with authority, made pull-up jumpers with defenders draped on him, and distributed the ball unselfishly throughout a night when he clearly had it going. His 39 points were the highest scoring output of his 2026 playoff run, and his 9 assists showed a command of the offense that has become his hallmark as Detroit's unquestioned leader.

The Pistons built a lead of 15 in the first half. They still led 103-94 with under three minutes remaining in the fourth quarter. In basketball terms, that is a very comfortable position. But as any Cleveland fan can tell you — after Mitchell's 24-0-run game in Game 4 — this series is not playing by comfortable rules.

The Play That Ended Regulation

As the final seconds of regulation drained away, Ausar Thompson appeared to have secured the basketball and was moving toward the basket. Then his legs tangled, the ball came loose, the clock hit zero, and the referees signaled nothing. Thompson and the Detroit bench believed Jarrett Allen had tripped Thompson while in possession — a foul that would have sent Detroit to the line with a chance to win the game without overtime.

Referee Tony Brothers confirmed no foul was assessed. Cunningham addressed reporters afterward without hesitation: "We all saw the play. I think it's pretty clear it was a foul." Detroit coach J.B. Bickerstaff shared the frustration, though no outcome will change the result.

The non-call sent the game to overtime. In OT, Cleveland — powered by James Harden's clutch scoring and free throw execution — put the game away.

Detroit's Season on the Line

The Pistons have been here before in this very series. They trailed 0-1, came back. They trailed 0-2, came back to make it 2-2 before Mitchell's explosion changed everything. Now, trailing 2-3 with a must-win Game 6 on the road in Cleveland, Detroit needs to find resilience one more time — and do it in a building that has been electric for the Cavaliers all series long.

Cunningham will be the reason it's possible if it happens. He is playing at a level that proves he belongs among the upper tier of young stars in this league. Wednesday's loss didn't diminish that — it illustrated it. He gave Detroit everything it needed to win.

What's Next

Game 6 is Friday at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in Cleveland. The Cavaliers need one win. The Pistons need to win two straight — Friday on the road, and potentially Game 7 back in Detroit next week — to advance. No team in this series has won on the road yet. That streak will have to end for Detroit to stay alive.

Cade Cunningham will be ready. The question is whether his teammates can match what he showed Wednesday and whether the basketball gods are done testing Detroit's resolve.
