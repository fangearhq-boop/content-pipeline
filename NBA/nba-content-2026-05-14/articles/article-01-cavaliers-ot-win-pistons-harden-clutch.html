Cavaliers Survive OT Thriller 117-113, Harden's Clutch 30 Points Lead Comeback Over Pistons

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 14, 2026

Down nine points with under three minutes remaining in regulation, the Cleveland Cavaliers refused to go home. James Harden orchestrated one of the gutsiest clutch stretches of his long career, and the Cavs pulled off a stunning 117-113 overtime victory over the Detroit Pistons in Game 5 on Wednesday night, taking a 3-2 series lead in the Eastern Conference semifinals.

The game was heading toward a decisive Detroit moment. The Pistons led 103-94 and the clock was draining. Then Cleveland turned the entire building upside down.

Harden Takes Over When It Mattered Most

Harden finished with 30 points, 8 rebounds, and 6 assists, shooting 8-of-21 from the field but a lethal 11-of-14 from the free throw line when the Cavs needed every point. He carried a plus/minus of +11 — second on the team — and was the engine of Cleveland's 13-0 run that stretched from late in the fourth quarter through the midpoint of overtime.

The pivotal sequence came with 24.4 seconds left in regulation. Harden made one of two free throws, then — almost unbelievably — grabbed his own miss off the rim, drew another foul, and converted one of two more, giving the Cavs a 115-111 advantage they wouldn't relinquish. The crowd inside Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse had been deathly quiet for most of the second half. It didn't stay that way.

Donovan Mitchell added 21 points and Evan Mobley's pair of free throws knotted the score at 103 with 45.2 seconds left to initially keep Cleveland alive in regulation.

The No-Call That Rocked Detroit

As the final seconds of regulation ticked away, Detroit's Ausar Thompson appeared to have the basketball and a clear path — until he stumbled, the ball came loose, and the clock hit zero. Replay showed Jarrett Allen's leg making contact with Thompson, a moment the Pistons believed was an obvious foul. No whistle came.

Referee Tony Brothers confirmed after the game that no call was made, saying officials determined the contact did not rise to a foul. Cade Cunningham, who finished with 39 points and 9 assists in a performance that deserved a win, was unambiguous in his frustration: "We all saw the play. I think it's pretty clear it was a foul. He has the ball in his possession, he's running forward, he gets tripped up."

Detroit coach J.B. Bickerstaff echoed the sentiment, though the Pistons now face an even harder reality: they must win Game 6 on the road or their season ends.

Cade's Heroic Night Defined by the Wrong Result

Cunningham was extraordinary. He scored from every area of the floor, facilitated Detroit's offense with efficiency, and nearly willed his team to a series-altering win. His 39-point, 9-assist performance against a defense that knows his tendencies better than anyone at this point should serve notice to the rest of the league — this Pistons team has a genuine star.

Detroit led by 15 in the first half and led by nine with under three minutes left. Losing this game, in this fashion, will sting for a long time in the Motor City.

What's Next

Game 6 returns to Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in Cleveland on Friday night. The Cavaliers have won three consecutive games in this series and are one win from their first Eastern Conference Finals appearance since 2018. If they advance, they'll face the New York Knicks, who swept the Philadelphia 76ers and have been resting since completing that series.

Detroit, meanwhile, must become the first team in this series to win on the road. Cade Cunningham will need to play another game of his life — and this time, the calls will need to go Detroit's way.
