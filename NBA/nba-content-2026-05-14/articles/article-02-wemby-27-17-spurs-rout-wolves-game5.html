Wemby Explodes for 27/17/5/3, Spurs Rout Timberwolves 126-97 to Lead Series 3-2

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 14, 2026

Victor Wembanyama didn't need a warmup period. He needed one quarter to settle the conversation.

One game after being ejected for a Flagrant 2 foul on Naz Reid, the 22-year-old San Antonio Spurs superstar returned on Tuesday night and delivered the most complete performance of his 2026 postseason run: 27 points, 17 rebounds, 5 assists, and 3 blocks as the Spurs crushed the Minnesota Timberwolves 126-97 in Game 5. San Antonio leads the Western Conference semifinal series 3-2.

18 Points Before Most People Had Settled In

Wembanyama scored 18 of his 27 points in the first quarter alone. By the time the opening 12 minutes had concluded, the Timberwolves were already chasing a deficit they would never recover. Minnesota had no answer for Wemby's size, his shooting range, or the confidence with which he attacked every possession.

He shot 9-of-16 from the field and 2-of-5 from three-point range, adding 17 rebounds — nine of them on the offensive glass — and distributed for five assists while altering multiple Minnesota looks with his three blocks. The final margin of 29 points told the complete story: the Spurs were the better team from the opening tip.

The Bounce-Back Story

Context matters. In Game 4, Wembanyama was ejected in the first half after an elbow made contact with Reid's face and neck, drawing a Flagrant 2 from officials. Without their centerpiece, San Antonio struggled, and Anthony Edwards — who finished Game 4 with 36 points and 16 in the fourth quarter — dragged Minnesota to a series-tying win.

Wembanyama addressed the ejection and acknowledged the stakes heading into Game 5. Then he went out and produced his answer on the floor. Bounce-back games rarely look this complete.

At 22 years old, Wembanyama is producing at a level that has few comparisons at his age in playoff history. His combination of size, skill, and basketball intelligence sets him apart in any era.

Team Performance Amplifies the Star

The Spurs' supporting cast played their role. De'Aaron Fox and Dylan Harper contributed in transition and in the halfcourt, and San Antonio's defense — suffocating without Wemby and downright elite with him — held Minnesota to a season-low scoring output in a playoff game this series. The Timberwolves, who had looked dangerous in Games 3 and 4 with Edwards in top form, could never find their footing.

Edwards was held well below his series averages as San Antonio's defensive schemes rotated and helped on every drive. Wemby's presence as a shot-alterer changes what opposing offenses can attempt, and Tuesday's game was a master class in that reality.

What's Next

Game 6 is Friday, May 15 at Target Center in Minneapolis at 9:30 PM ET on Prime Video. The Timberwolves, facing elimination on their home floor, will need an Anthony Edwards performance for the ages to extend this series. Edwards has shown he's capable — but so has Wemby, and right now the Spurs star is playing with an edge that is very hard to cool.

If San Antonio wins Friday, the Western Conference Finals begin Monday, May 18 against the Oklahoma City Thunder — a team that is a perfect 8-0 in the 2026 playoffs and has been resting and waiting since sweeping the Los Angeles Lakers in Game 4.
