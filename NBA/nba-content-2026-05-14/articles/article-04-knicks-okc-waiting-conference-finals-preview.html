Knicks and OKC Are Watching: Two Game 6s Friday Will Complete the Conference Finals Bracket

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 14, 2026

Two teams have already made it. Two more will join them on Friday.

The New York Knicks and the Oklahoma City Thunder have been resting, watching, and preparing while the rest of the second round plays out. By Friday night, they will know who they're playing — and the conference finals will be set.

The East: Knicks Wait for Cavs or Pistons

New York swept the Philadelphia 76ers in emphatic fashion and has had extended time off. The Knicks advanced behind Jalen Brunson, Julius Randle, and a balanced offensive attack that wore down Philadelphia without relying on a single hero. They've been practicing, staying sharp, and watching Cleveland and Detroit trade haymakers.

The Eastern Conference Finals Game 1 is slated for as early as Sunday, May 17 if the Cavs-Pistons series wraps Friday. If it requires a Game 7, the schedule shifts accordingly. New York will host the series opener regardless of who comes out of the east semifinal — the Knicks earned the No. 1 seed and Madison Square Garden is ready.

Cleveland leads Detroit 3-2 after an overtime thriller Wednesday. The Cavaliers, despite their talent and clutch wins, would be entering the ECF with less rest than New York. Detroit, if it can somehow win two straight, would arrive as a heavy underdog — a team nobody expected to even push the Cavs this far.

The West: Thunder Ready, Awaiting Spurs or Wolves

Oklahoma City is the most dominant team in the 2026 playoffs. An unbeaten 8-0 run through the first two rounds — including a sweep of the Los Angeles Lakers featuring Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, Chet Holmgren, and a deep supporting cast — has made the Thunder the betting favorites to win the NBA title.

The Western Conference Finals will start May 18 at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City if the San Antonio Spurs advance Friday. San Antonio leads their series against the Timberwolves 3-2 after Victor Wembanyama's 27-point, 17-rebound demolition in Game 5. Game 6 is Friday at Target Center in Minneapolis.

A Spurs-Thunder WCF would be the matchup many imagined at the start of the season — two transcendent young stars in Wembanyama and Gilgeous-Alexander, squaring off with a Finals berth on the line. If Minnesota wins Friday and forces a Game 7, the WCF start date shifts slightly, but OKC would still host throughout.

Double-Header Friday Could Set the Whole Bracket

Both Game 6s tip off Friday evening — Cleveland-Detroit at an evening tip, and Minnesota-San Antonio at 9:30 PM ET on Prime Video. If both series end in six games, the entire conference finals bracket — all four participants — will be known before midnight Friday.

The Knicks and Thunder have done their part. Now Cleveland, Detroit, San Antonio, and Minnesota will determine who joins them.

What's Next

Game 6 — Cavaliers at Detroit (series: Cleveland leads 3-2) — Friday at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse. Game 6 — Timberwolves at San Antonio (series: Spurs lead 3-2) — Friday at Target Center, 9:30 PM ET, Prime Video. ECF Game 1 as early as Sunday, May 17. WCF Game 1 at OKC as early as Monday, May 18. The conference finals are almost here.
