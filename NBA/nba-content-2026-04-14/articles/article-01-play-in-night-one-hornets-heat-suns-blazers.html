Play-In Night 1: LaMelo's Hornets Host Heat, Suns Face Blazers in Elimination Showdowns

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 14, 2026

Two elimination games tip off Tuesday night as the 2026 SoFi NBA Play-In Tournament gets underway — and both carry enormous stakes for the teams involved.

The Improbable Hornets

On January 2, the Charlotte Hornets held a 11-23 record and looked like a team destined to spend another April watching from home. Fourteen weeks later, LaMelo Ball and the Hornets are hosting an elimination Play-In game at the Spectrum Center, a 7:30 p.m. ET showdown against the Miami Heat that will send one team home for good.

The turnaround is one of the better stories of the 2025-26 NBA season. Charlotte went from long-shot Play-In hopeful to legitimate home-floor Play-In host by winning 33 of its final 57 games. LaMelo Ball, when healthy and locked in, is one of the most creative offensive forces in the league — averaging approximately 20.1 points and 7.1 assists per game over the course of the year. His ability to make plays out of nothing gives Charlotte a ceiling that most teams at this seeding simply don't have.

The path gets harder from here: the winner must then beat either the Philadelphia 76ers or Orlando Magic on Wednesday to earn the eighth seed. But just getting to tonight — hosting a game, playing at home — is a statement for a franchise that's been rebuilding for years.

Miami's Defense Problem

The Miami Heat finished the season with a 43-39 record and the No. 10 seed, qualifying for the Play-In for the fourth time in the tournament's brief history. Tyler Herro has been the engine of the offense, averaging 20.5 points per game, and his ability to create his own shot in late-clock situations remains one of the more reliable weapons the Heat own.

But the concern heading into tonight is on the other end of the floor. Miami allowed more than 120 points in 13 games over the final month of the regular season — a rate that spells trouble against a Charlotte team that can push pace and manufacture offense in a variety of ways. If the Hornets get the game into the open court, Miami's defensive vulnerabilities could be exposed quickly.

For the Heat, this is a must-win in the truest sense. Lose tonight and the season is over.

Out West: Booker vs. Avdija at 10 p.m. ET

The second game of the night carries even higher immediate stakes. The No. 7 Phoenix Suns host the No. 8 Portland Trail Blazers at PHX Arena beginning at 10 p.m. ET, and the winner earns an automatic berth in the first round of the playoffs as the No. 7 seed, where they'll face the No. 2 San Antonio Spurs.

Devin Booker has been Phoenix's constant this season, averaging 26.1 points per game in a year when the Suns navigated significant roster upheaval following Kevin Durant's departure. That they won 45 games under those circumstances is a testament to Booker's growth as a franchise cornerstone. The Suns enter as 4.5-point favorites.

Portland's Deni Avdija is the wild card. The young forward has erupted for 24.2 points per game this season, one of the most improved performances of any player in the league. Donovan Clingan provides an interior anchor — his 11.6 rebounds per game lead the team and give the Blazers a presence in the paint that can wear on opponents over 48 minutes.

The Bigger Picture

Tuesday night's results set the stage for Wednesday's second round of Play-In action: the East loser of the 9-10 game gets another chance against the winners of either the No. 7 or No. 8 matchup. The full Play-In bracket concludes Friday, April 17, with all four playoff spots from the 7-10 seeds determined before the first round opens April 18.

Both games air exclusively on Amazon Prime Video — the first time the entire Play-In Tournament has shifted to a streaming-only platform.

What's Next

After tonight's results, the Play-In picture will clarify significantly. A Hornets win sends Charlotte to face the 76ers or Magic on Wednesday; a Heat win means they get another shot Friday. In the West, whoever wins the Suns-Blazers game learns their first-round matchup immediately: a date with Victor Wembanyama and the Spurs beginning April 18. Wednesday brings the final two Play-In games (76ers-Magic, Clippers-Warriors), and by Friday night the full 16-team playoff bracket will be locked in.
