SGA Breaks Wilt Chamberlain's Record — A Season That Belongs in History

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 14, 2026

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander's 2025-26 season wasn't just great — it set records, drove Oklahoma City to the best record in the NBA, and all but locked up back-to-back MVP honors.

Breaking a Record That Stood for Decades

Some individual seasons announce themselves quietly, through consistency and efficiency. Others write themselves into the history books. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander's 2025-26 campaign belongs in the second category.

The Oklahoma City Thunder star broke Wilt Chamberlain's NBA record for consecutive games with 20 or more points during the season — a mark that had stood for decades and was widely considered untouchable. It's the kind of achievement that puts a player's name in a sentence with the all-time greats, and SGA earned every letter of it.

He finished the regular season averaging 31.6 points, 6.5 assists, and 4.4 rebounds per game while shooting 55 percent from the floor. In March alone — when MVP races typically tighten and fatigue sets in — Gilgeous-Alexander posted five 30-point games and three 40-point performances. The great ones don't show up in the big moments. They manufacture them.

The MVP Case Is Closed

The numbers make the argument, but the votes sealed it. ESPN's NBA MVP straw poll returned 88 of 100 votes in SGA's favor. The Thunder finished 64-16 — the best record in the entire league — and will carry home-court advantage throughout the Western Conference playoffs. No other player this season combined individual brilliance with franchise impact at this level.

This would be Gilgeous-Alexander's second consecutive MVP award. Back-to-back MVP seasons have happened only a handful of times in NBA history — Kareem Abdul-Jabbar ran six in a row, LeBron James did it twice, Stephen Curry did it once. SGA is 27 years old.

OKC: The Team Built Around Him

Oklahoma City's 64-16 record isn't just a product of SGA's individual greatness — it's a reflection of how well the organization has built around him. The Thunder's depth, defensive identity, and coaching under Mark Daigneault have created one of the most complete teams in the league.

The first round of the playoffs begins April 18, with OKC awaiting the Play-In winner to round out their first-round opponent at No. 8. On a court where SGA can operate with confidence, the Thunder aren't just a favorite to advance — they're a legitimate title contender.

For a franchise that spent years rebuilding through the draft, this is the payoff. And SGA is just getting started.

The Historical Context

At 31.6 points per game, SGA becomes one of only a handful of players in NBA history to average more than 30 points per game while also averaging at least six assists — a combination that requires elite scoring ability and elite playmaking simultaneously. The list of players who have pulled that off in a single season is very, very short.

His 55 percent field goal efficiency while attempting and making this volume of shots removes any argument about quality of shots taken. This wasn't a season built on volume. It was built on precision.

What's Next

The MVP award announcement is expected sometime in late April or early May, during the first round of the playoffs. By that point, SGA and the Thunder will already be deep into postseason competition. The trophy will catch up with the player eventually — but OKC's goals don't stop at an individual award. This team wants a championship, and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander intends to be the reason they get one.
