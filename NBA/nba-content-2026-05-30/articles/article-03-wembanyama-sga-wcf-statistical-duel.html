Wembanyama vs. SGA: The Statistical Duel That Has Decided Every WCF Game

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 30, 2026

Six games into the Western Conference Finals, the pattern is unmistakable. In every contest between San Antonio and Oklahoma City, the result has been determined by a single variable: which of the two franchise superstars outscored the other. No exceptions. No noise. Just the cleanest possible signal in an otherwise messy and unpredictable sport.

Victor Wembanyama outscores Shai Gilgeous-Alexander — the Spurs win. SGA outscores Wembanyama — the Thunder win. Three times each way. Series tied 3-3. And tonight, in Game 7 at the Paycom Center, the same rule will almost certainly apply one final time.

Wembanyama: The Series Numbers

Wembanyama is averaging 28.2 points, 11.5 rebounds, and 3.0 blocks per game in this series. Those numbers are extraordinary in isolation. In context — given the quality of opponent and the playoff pressure — they are among the most impressive statistical lines produced by a 22-year-old in modern NBA postseason history.

His Game 1 performance set the tone: 41 points and 24 rebounds in a double-overtime victory. That line places him alongside Wilt Chamberlain as the only players to record 40-plus points and 20-plus rebounds in a Conference Finals debut — a claim that should be used carefully, as historical comparisons of this kind require verification across full box score databases. What is not in dispute: the numbers were extraordinary, the performance was dominant, and it announced to the country that San Antonio was a genuine title contender.

Wembanyama won the Defensive Player of the Year award unanimously this season — receiving all 100 first-place votes. He led the NBA in blocks for the second consecutive year. He is, at this moment, the most complete two-way player in professional basketball.

SGA: Two-Time MVP in Peak Form

Gilgeous-Alexander won back-to-back Most Valuable Player awards after averaging 31.1 points in the regular season. He is the singular engine of Oklahoma City's offense — a methodical, precise scorer who creates points through angles and contact that most guards cannot even identify, let alone execute.

In this series, SGA is averaging 24.3 points and 8.8 assists. His distribution numbers reflect how much the Thunder rely on him to set teammates up when defenses collapse. In the three Oklahoma City wins, Gilgeous-Alexander was at his best: controlling tempo, hitting big shots, and making the passing decisions that give OKC's role players clean looks.

His Game 6 was the outlier — 15 points on 6-of-18 shooting, a minus-28 rating as the Spurs built a 27-point blowout. Great players have bad games. SGA's resume gives him every right to consider Game 6 an anomaly and come back tonight with something to prove.

The Pattern and What It Means for Game 7

When a statistical pattern holds across six consecutive playoff games, it stops being a coincidence and becomes an operational truth. Both San Antonio and Oklahoma City are structured around making their star the focal point of everything. When that star dominates, the team around them is elevated. When they struggle, the supporting cast rarely compensates at this level of competition.

For Game 7, the question becomes one of will and circumstance. Wembanyama will have the energy that comes from playing a home crowd on the road — his calm under pressure has been notable all series. SGA will have 20,000 Oklahoma City fans at his back for the biggest game of the year. Jalen Williams is out, which removes a defender who made Wembanyama work for his touches at times during the series.

The math is simple. The basketball is not. But tonight, one of these extraordinary players will determine not just who wins, but who goes to the Finals.

What's Next

Game 7 tips tonight at 8:15 PM ET on NBC and Peacock from the Paycom Center in Oklahoma City. The winner advances to the NBA Finals against the New York Knicks, beginning June 3 at 8:30 PM ET on ABC. The Knicks swept the Cleveland Cavaliers and have been waiting 13 days. Whoever emerges from tonight will need to find a gear to match a rested, hungry New York team.

One more game. One more duel between two of the best players on the planet. The Western Conference Finals have been a masterclass in playoff basketball, and tonight's Game 7 is its final chapter.
