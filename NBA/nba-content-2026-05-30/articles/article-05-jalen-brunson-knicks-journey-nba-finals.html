Jalen Brunson's Journey: From Jersey Kid to Knicks Franchise Savior

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 30, 2026

Jalen Brunson grew up 40 miles from Madison Square Garden in Union, New Jersey. He watched the Knicks as a kid, grew up in a basketball household shaped by his father Rick, who played professionally including a stint with the Knicks in the early 2000s. There are childhood photos of Brunson in Knicks gear, dreaming the kind of dream that most kids never get to live.

Today, Jalen Brunson is taking the New York Knicks to the NBA Finals for the first time in 27 years. Some stories write themselves. This is one of them.

The Signing That Changed Everything

When Brunson left Dallas in the summer of 2022 to sign with New York, the basketball world had questions. Was he a second-tier guy given too big a contract? Could someone who'd been Luka Doncic's supporting act actually carry a franchise? The Knicks believed. Most of the media was skeptical.

The answers arrived quickly. Brunson made the All-Star team in his first full Knicks season. He became the engine around which an entire offensive system was built — not by deferring or spot-shooting, but by making decisions at the speed of thought, manufacturing points in the mid-range, finishing through contact at a rate that defied logic for someone his size, and leading fourth quarters like he'd been born for them.

Then the Knicks added Karl-Anthony Towns, and the ceiling raised considerably higher.

The Playoff Numbers

In the 2026 playoffs, Brunson has averaged 27.8 points and 6.7 assists. Against Cleveland in the Eastern Conference Finals sweep, he posted 25.5 points and 7.8 assists per game — carrying the Knicks through a Cleveland team that won more regular-season games than New York and had home court in the series.

He has been their engine in the clutch, their voice in the huddle, and their best player in the moments that mattered most. His combination of usage rate and efficiency in these playoffs is remarkable — he's not just taking shots, he's taking the right ones at the right times.

There is an argument — not a frivolous one — that Brunson has been the best guard in this postseason. The back-to-back MVPs of the Western Conference have attracted more headlines, but no player has done more to put his team in the Finals on the Eastern side.

The Full Circle

Rick Brunson played for the New York Knicks. He raised his son on basketball, on the city, on the idea that the right opportunity combined with the right preparation produces something lasting. When Jalen signed with New York four years ago, Rick understood the weight of what was happening. His son was going home.

Knicks fans have sensed that connection from the beginning. The way the crowd at MSG reacts to Brunson goes beyond normal appreciation for a player — there's something more personal about it. He feels like one of them. He grew up dreaming about the Garden the way the people in the Garden grew up dreaming about moments like this one.

Twenty-seven years in the making. One man who happened to grow up rooting for the team he now leads to the biggest stage in basketball.

What's Next

The Knicks learn their NBA Finals opponent tonight when Game 7 between Oklahoma City and San Antonio concludes. Game 1 of the NBA Finals begins June 3 at 8:30 PM ET on ABC — on the road, in the Western Conference city. The Knicks will open as underdogs, the road team, the team without home court.

They have been here before. They've been underestimated before. And Jalen Brunson — the kid from New Jersey who always believed — has never seemed too bothered by long odds.

Twenty-seven years of waiting ends now. The Finals are here. And Brunson is leading the charge.
