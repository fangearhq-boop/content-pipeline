Knicks in the NBA Finals: New York's 27-Year Wait Is Finally Over

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 30, 2026

The New York Knicks are going to the NBA Finals. For the first time since 1999, Madison Square Garden will host Finals basketball. The Garden will be loud in a way it has not been loud in a generation — filled with fans who spent 27 years wondering if they would ever see this again.

The Knicks swept the Cleveland Cavaliers 4-0 in the Eastern Conference Finals, capping an extraordinary 11-game postseason winning streak. Jalen Brunson, Karl-Anthony Towns, and a roster that quietly assembled itself into something championship-caliber are heading to the greatest stage the sport offers. And tonight, they'll learn which Western Conference team will meet them.

How the Knicks Got Here

The Knicks' path to the Finals was built on two pillars: Brunson's clutch brilliance and a supporting cast that elevated when the moment demanded. Brunson averaged 27.8 points and 6.7 assists in the playoffs, playing with a poise that silenced every "can he carry a team?" debate before it could fully form.

In the ECF sweep against Cleveland, Brunson averaged 25.5 points and 7.8 assists. He was the series' best player by a wide margin, orchestrating an offense that looked unstoppable at its peak. The Cavaliers' top-seeded defense had no answer for how the Knicks moved the ball or how Brunson manufactured points from nothing.

Karl-Anthony Towns was the force inside that transformed this roster. In the clinching Game 4, Towns had 19 points and 14 rebounds — a reminder that when he and Brunson are clicking together, few teams in the league can match New York's offensive versatility. The trade that brought Towns from Minnesota has been fully justified by these playoffs.

The Finals Matchup and Home Court Reality

The Knicks will have home-court disadvantage in the NBA Finals regardless of which team emerges from the Thunder-Spurs Game 7 tonight. Oklahoma City finished 64-18, the best record in the NBA. San Antonio finished 62-20, second-best. New York's 53-29 record was excellent — but not as excellent as its opponents.

That means the Western Conference representative will host Games 1, 2, 5, and 7 of the Finals. The Knicks get Games 3, 4, and 6 at Madison Square Garden — which, when the time comes, will be among the loudest sporting environments in the country.

The NBA Finals begin Wednesday, June 3, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC. Game 2 follows Friday, June 5. The Knicks host Games 3 and 4 on June 8 and June 10. If the series extends, Game 5 is June 13, Game 6 at MSG on June 16, and a potential Game 7 on June 19.

The Rest Equation

The Knicks have been resting for 13 days since sweeping Cleveland, waiting and watching while San Antonio and Oklahoma City exhaust each other in seven grueling games. Rest in the NBA playoffs is a complicated commodity. The time off is valuable for healing bodies — OG Anunoby's hamstring, Brunson's accumulated wear — but the extended break can also introduce rust into a rhythm that had been flowing.

History suggests the break is mostly a net positive. Teams with extended Finals waiting periods perform roughly as well as teams that came out of a short series. Brunson and the coaching staff have used the time for detailed film study. By the time Game 1 tips, New York will know its opponent's tendencies with surgical precision.

What's Next

Game 7 between OKC and San Antonio tips tonight at 8:15 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. The Knicks will learn their opponent sometime this evening. Both potential matchups present challenges — the Thunder won 64 games and are the defending Finals representatives, while the Spurs have Wembanyama, who may be the most complete player in basketball at age 22.

Regardless of what happens in Oklahoma City tonight, the Knicks are prepared. Twenty-seven years of waiting ends here. New York is going to the Finals, and Madison Square Garden will never be louder.
