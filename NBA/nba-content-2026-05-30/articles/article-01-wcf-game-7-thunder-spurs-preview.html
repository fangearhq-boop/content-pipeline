WCF Game 7: Thunder vs. Spurs — Everything at Stake in Oklahoma City Tonight

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 30, 2026

The Western Conference Finals end tonight. Victor Wembanyama and the San Antonio Spurs walk into the Paycom Center in Oklahoma City for a winner-take-all Game 7, tipping off at 8:15 PM ET on NBC and Peacock. The team that walks out earns a trip to the NBA Finals. The one that doesn't goes home and wonders what might have been.

It is one of the most anticipated games in recent playoff memory — a six-game series that has been decided, in every single contest, by which of the two transcendent stars outperformed the other. The Thunder win when Shai Gilgeous-Alexander outscores Wembanyama. The Spurs win when Wembanyama outscores SGA. The series is 3-3. The pattern will almost certainly hold one more time tonight.

Williams Out: OKC's Biggest Problem

The Thunder enter Game 7 with a significant wound. Jalen Williams, their second-best scorer and the player teams must account for when scheming against Gilgeous-Alexander, is out with a left hamstring injury. Williams averaged 14.6 points per game in this series and his absence removes one of the Thunder's most effective secondary options.

Without Williams, San Antonio's defensive focus narrows. The Spurs can devote more attention to containing SGA while trusting their wings to handle OKC's remaining threats. Oklahoma City's role players — Isaiah Hartenstein, Luguentz Dort, Aaron Wiggins — will need to step up in ways they haven't been asked to deliver all series.

For Gilgeous-Alexander, the Williams absence is a two-edged sword. It will demand more from him offensively, which can be taxing over 48 minutes. But it also means the ball stays in his hands — which is where it needs to be in a Game 7.

The Formula That Has Decided Every Game

No WCF series in recent years has produced a more elegant analytical observation. In Games 1, 4, and 6, Wembanyama outscored SGA — and the Spurs won all three. In Games 2, 3, and 5, SGA outscored Wembanyama — and the Thunder won all three. Every game. No exceptions.

This isn't coincidence. It reflects the reality that both teams are built around their superstar to a degree that makes the supporting cast largely reactive. When Wembanyama's 7-foot-4 frame is attacking the basket, pulling up in the mid-range, and protecting the rim at the other end, San Antonio is simply a different team. When SGA is getting to his spots with that hypnotic stop-start rhythm and finishing through contact, OKC looks unbeatable.

Wembanyama enters Game 7 averaging 28.2 points, 11.5 rebounds, and 3.0 blocks in the series. SGA comes in averaging 24.3 points and 8.8 assists. His lowest scoring output in a Thunder win was 25 points. His Game 6 — 15 points on 6-of-18 shooting, a minus-28 rating — was the ugliest performance of his playoff run.

OKC's Home Advantage

Oklahoma City was the best home team in the NBA this regular season at 34-7. They're 6-1 at Paycom Center these playoffs. The energy inside that building when the Thunder are at full volume is a genuine force — the kind of crowd that has beaten teams before a ball is tipped.

The Spurs are 5-3 on the road in these playoffs, so this is not unfamiliar territory. They won Game 1 at Paycom — the series opener — in double overtime. San Antonio knows how to play through the noise. Wembanyama, in particular, has shown an ability to detach from external atmosphere and operate within his own calm.

The Thunder enter as 3.5-point favorites with a moneyline at -155. The Spurs are +130 underdogs — a significant gap, but one that reflects home court rather than any dramatic talent differential between the two rosters.

What's Next

The winner advances to the NBA Finals to face the New York Knicks, who have been resting for 13 days after sweeping the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Finals. The Knicks last appeared in the Finals in 1999, and the city of New York is ready to explode regardless of which Western team arrives.

Because both the Thunder (64-18) and the Spurs (62-20) had better regular-season records than New York (53-29), the Western Conference representative will hold home-court advantage throughout the Finals. Game 1 tips Wednesday, June 3, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC.

For Oklahoma City, a victory would make them the first back-to-back Finals team since the Golden State Warriors reached five consecutive Finals from 2015 to 2019. For San Antonio, it would be the Spurs' first Finals appearance since 2014 — and a validation of the rebuild that produced Wembanyama.

Game 7. Paycom Center. 8:15 PM ET. Everything on the line.
