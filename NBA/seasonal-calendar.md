# Hoop Heroes — Seasonal Calendar

Use this to calibrate story selection and tiering throughout the season.

## Season Timeline

| Period | Dates (approx) | What's Happening | Tiering Notes |
|--------|----------------|------------------|---------------|
| Preseason | Oct | Training camp, preseason games, roster battles | Roster news Tier 2; preseason games Tier 3 |
| Regular Season (Early) | Late Oct – Dec | Season openers, early storylines, Christmas Day games | Opening week Tier 1; Christmas games always Tier 1 |
| Regular Season (Mid) | Jan – Feb | All-Star voting, trade deadline buildup, standings taking shape | Trade rumors Tier 1-2; All-Star Tier 2 |
| All-Star Break | Mid Feb | All-Star Weekend (Rising Stars, Skills/3PT/Dunk, All-Star Game) | All-Star Weekend Tier 1-2 |
| Trade Deadline | Mid Feb | Always Tier 1 — deals, non-deals, fallout | Every trade Tier 1; deadline day coverage is all Tier 1 |
| Playoff Push | Mar – mid Apr | Standings tighten, play-in race, clinch scenarios | Standings movement Tier 1-2; clinch/elimination always Tier 1 |
| Play-In Tournament | Mid Apr | Seeds 7-10 battle for final playoff spots | All play-in games Tier 1 |
| Playoffs (First Round) | Late Apr – early May | Best-of-7 series (1v8, 2v7, 3v6, 4v5) | Every game Tier 1-2; eliminations Tier 1 |
| Playoffs (Conference Semis) | May | Best-of-7 second round | All Tier 1 |
| Conference Finals | May – early Jun | Final four teams, best-of-7 | All Tier 1 |
| NBA Finals | Jun | Best-of-7 championship series | Everything Tier 1 |
| NBA Draft | Late Jun | Draft Lottery (May), Draft Night (Jun) | Lottery Tier 1; Draft night Tier 1 |
| Free Agency | Jul | Signings, trades, opt-ins/opt-outs | Major signings Tier 1; mid-tier Tier 2 |
| Summer League | Jul | Las Vegas Summer League, rookie showcases | Rookie highlights Tier 2-3 |
| Offseason | Aug – Sep | Contract extensions, international play (FIBA), training camp previews | Extensions Tier 2; FIBA Tier 2-3 |

## Key Annual Events

### October
- Training camp opens (early Oct)
- Preseason games (2 weeks)
- Opening Night (late Oct) — always Tier 1
- Early-season storylines: new rosters, coaching debuts, revenge games

### November – December
- In-Season Tournament / NBA Cup (Nov-Dec)
- Thanksgiving slate (featured games)
- Christmas Day games — always Tier 1 (5-game showcase)
- Early MVP and award race narratives

### January
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day showcase games — Tier 1
- All-Star voting opens
- Trade rumors intensify
- Halfway point of season — standings check

### February
- All-Star Weekend (Rising Stars Friday, Skills Saturday, All-Star Game Sunday)
- Trade Deadline (always mid-Feb) — Tier 1 all day
- Post-deadline roster evaluations
- Second-half storylines begin

### March
- Playoff race intensifies
- Play-In Tournament race takes shape
- MVP/awards narratives crystallize
- Rivalry games with playoff implications — Tier 1

### April
- Final week of regular season — clinch/elimination scenarios
- Play-In Tournament (7v8 and 9v10 games)
- Playoffs begin (late April)
- First Round matchups set

### May
- First Round concludes
- Conference Semifinals
- Conference Finals begin (late May)

### June
- Conference Finals conclude
- NBA Finals (best-of-7)
- NBA Draft (late June)
- Awards show (MVP, DPOY, ROY, etc.)

### July
- Free Agency opens (July 1 negotiation, July 6 signings)
- Las Vegas Summer League (2 weeks)
- Trade activity continues

### August – September
- FIBA international tournaments (World Cup years, Olympics years)
- Contract extensions
- Preseason previews and power rankings
- Training camp previews

## Story Selection Reminders

- **Playoff/elimination games always Tier 1** — any win-or-go-home game is automatic lead story
- **Buzzer beaters and OT thrillers always Tier 1** — regardless of teams involved
- **50+ point games always Tier 1** — historic individual performances
- **Triple-doubles by stars are Tier 2** — unless it's a milestone (e.g., most in a season)
- **Trade deadline day is ALL Tier 1** — every deal, every report, full coverage
- **Market-size bias awareness** — cover all 30 teams, not just Lakers/Celtics/Warriors/Knicks
- **Conference balance** — East and West get equal coverage; don't default to one conference
- **Times always in ET** — NBA is a national league, ET is the standard
