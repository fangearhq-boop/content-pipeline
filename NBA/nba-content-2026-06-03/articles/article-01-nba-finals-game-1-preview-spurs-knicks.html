The Wait Is Over: Knicks vs. Spurs NBA Finals Game 1 Preview — Tonight, 8:30 PM ET

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 3, 2026

Twenty-seven years is a long time to hold a grudge.

The New York Knicks and San Antonio Spurs last met in the NBA Finals in 1999, when a lockout-shortened season ended with Tim Duncan lifting the trophy and New York heading home empty-handed. Tonight, on the floor of Frost Bank Center in San Antonio, the rematch finally tips off. Game 1 of the 2026 NBA Finals starts at 8:30 PM ET on ABC.

This is not just a basketball series. It is a generational story playing out in real time.

The Storylines That Define This Matchup

Jalen Brunson's father, Rick, was a reserve guard on the 1999 Knicks team that lost to San Antonio in five games. Jalen grew up in Union, New Jersey, listening to stories from that series. Now he is the franchise player, the ECF MVP, and the reason New York is here for the first time since his father's era.

Brunson averaged 27.8 points and 6.7 assists per game in the Eastern Conference Finals, earning MVP honors after sweeping the Boston Celtics 4-0. His ability to create offense out of nothing — floaters off screens, pull-up jumpers in traffic, lobs to bigs — makes him the most difficult offensive player to game-plan against in the East.

Victor Wembanyama is the counter-argument. At 22 years old, the Spurs' franchise centerpiece won Western Conference Finals MVP after averaging 27.3 points, 10.9 rebounds, and 2.7 blocks per game against the Oklahoma City Thunder in seven games. He is the two-time All-Star who earned unanimous Defensive Player of the Year honors this season, and he has never looked more ready for a moment than this one.

Numbers That Tell the Story

The Knicks enter tonight riding an 11-game playoff win streak with a +271 combined point differential — the best mark in NBA history for a team entering the Finals. New York's offense has been relentless, shooting 40.0% from three-point range, the best mark in this postseason.

San Antonio had to earn it the hard way. The Spurs pushed through a seven-game Western Conference Finals against the Thunder — a series that required everything Wembanyama and his teammates had. That battle may have sharpened them or it may have worn them down. Tonight is the first evidence either way.

The Spurs enter as home favorites, playing in front of their crowd at Frost Bank Center. New York has nine days of rest after the ECF sweep. San Antonio is coming off the emotional drain of a Game 7.

The Key Matchup to Watch

Stephon Castle against Jalen Brunson is the chess match within the chess match. Castle, the second-year guard who has defended All-Star guards all postseason, will be tasked with slowing one of the league's most relentless scorers. Castle's combination of length, IQ, and quickness has made him San Antonio's most important defensive piece on the perimeter.

On the other end, watch what Karl-Anthony Towns and OG Anunoby do to Wembanyama. Both are shooting better than 48% from three in these playoffs, creating a perimeter threat that forces defensive decisions no other team has faced this postseason.

What's Next

Game 1 tonight sets the tone for what promises to be one of the most compelling Finals in years. A Spurs win gives San Antonio early home-court leverage going into Game 2 on Thursday. A Knicks win and New York could take the building's energy entirely out of play for the rest of the trip to San Antonio.

Game 2 follows on June 5 at Frost Bank Center. The series shifts to Madison Square Garden for Games 3 and 4 on June 8 and June 10. Watch the first quarter tonight — both coaches will telegraph their adjustments early, and the team that wins that opening frame has won this series starter in both of their regular-season matchups this year.

The rematch is here. Tip-off is at 8:30 PM ET on ABC. Don't miss a second.
