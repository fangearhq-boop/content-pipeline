Wizards Win 2026 NBA Draft Lottery — No. 1 Pick for First Time Since John Wall

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 3, 2026

While the Knicks and Spurs prepare to battle for the Larry O'Brien Trophy tonight, the Washington Wizards are already planning the future.

On May 10, the Wizards won the 2026 NBA Draft Lottery, securing the No. 1 overall pick for the first time since the franchise selected John Wall at the top of the 2010 draft. It is the most significant piece of good fortune Washington has experienced in years — and it arrives at a moment when the franchise desperately needed it.

The full top four shook out as follows: Washington Wizards (No. 1), Utah Jazz (No. 2), Memphis Grizzlies (No. 3), Chicago Bulls (No. 4).

How Washington Got Here

The Wizards entered the 2026 lottery with a 14.0% chance at the top selection — tied with the Brooklyn Nets and Indiana Pacers for the best odds in the field. In lottery terms, 14% is not overwhelming, but it was enough. The ping-pong balls fell Washington's way, and suddenly a franchise that has been rebuilding slowly now holds the most valuable asset in next month's draft.

This is Washington's first No. 1 pick since Wall, who went on to become a five-time All-Star before injuries derailed his career in Washington. The organization will be hoping this selection follows a different trajectory — particularly with the draft date now confirmed for June 23 (Round 1) and June 24 (Round 2).

The Broader Rebuild Landscape

The four teams in the top four positions represent four different stages of NBA reconstruction. The Wizards, at No. 1, are the most prominent. The Utah Jazz hold No. 2 — a franchise that has methodically accumulated draft capital and young talent under Danny Ainge's patient leadership. The Memphis Grizzlies, at No. 3, are still navigating a post-Ja Morant rebuild with considerable young pieces already in place. The Chicago Bulls at No. 4 have been hovering in the middle of the Eastern Conference standings for years, and this pick represents a genuine opportunity to accelerate their timeline.

For context: the team winning tonight's NBA Finals will have either the 29th or 30th pick in this draft, depending on series outcome. The gap between No. 1 and No. 30 is the difference between a franchise cornerstone and a depth piece — and it is the entire story of where teams are in their cycles.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Draft takes place June 23-24. The Wizards will spend the next three weeks evaluating prospects and conducting workouts — a process that, with the No. 1 pick, includes visits from every top player in the class. Washington's front office will be doing homework on a player who could define the franchise for the next decade.

Meanwhile, the team that wins the NBA Finals tonight will begin their own offseason process from a very different vantage point — thinking not about rebuilding but about how to repeat. The lottery and the Finals are happening simultaneously this week, which is one of the things that makes basketball's June calendar unlike any other month in sports.
