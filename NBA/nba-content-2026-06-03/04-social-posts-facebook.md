### STORY 2: Castle Admits He "Sells Calls"

**Long-Form Post (11:00 AM ET)**

Stephon Castle walked into NBA Finals Media Day on Tuesday afternoon and delivered the most honest answer any defender has given in years.

When asked about flopping — embellishing contact to draw fouls — Castle didn't deny it. He leaned in. "I sell calls too sometimes. I mean, I can't lie," the 21-year-old second-year guard said.

He wasn't done. Castle explained his philosophy: "It's really just a feel thing, especially in the playoffs. If it's too egregious, the refs are not going to bail you out... I think we talk to the refs a lot, especially me in particular, but most of the time they're right."

This is the young man the Spurs are deploying to stop Jalen Brunson — a perennial All-Star who averaged 27.8 points per game in the Eastern Conference Finals. Castle's size, athleticism, and IQ have neutralized bigger scoring guards all postseason. His willingness to work the officials may be just as important as his footwork.

In a Finals defined by elite talent, Castle's transparency is a reminder that championship basketball is also a mind game — played against opponents and officials alike.

### STORY 3: Wembanyama's Defensive Dilemma

**Long-Form Post (11:00 AM ET)**

The New York Knicks have spent this entire playoff run setting a trap — and in the NBA Finals, they're about to spring it on the best defensive player in the world.

Karl-Anthony Towns is shooting 48.9% from three-point range in these playoffs. OG Anunoby is shooting 48.3%. Both are dead-eye shooters who draw defenders to the perimeter. Both demand Victor Wembanyama's attention.

Here is the problem for San Antonio: if Wembanyama steps out to contest perimeter shots, he vacates the paint — the area where he's most devastating as a shot-blocker. That creates driving lanes for Jalen Brunson, who is the most dangerous floater-and-finisher guard in the league.

Stay home on KAT and OG, and they'll fire wide-open threes at 49%. Climb out to contest, and Brunson goes straight to the basket with no protection. The Knicks are shooting a playoff-best 40.0% from deep, and they've built their entire system around this dilemma.

How head coach Mitch Johnson schemes around it will likely determine who wins this series.
