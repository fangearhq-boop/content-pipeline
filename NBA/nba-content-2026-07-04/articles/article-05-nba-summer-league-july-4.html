NBA Summer League Tips Off on July 4: The Next Generation Takes the Court

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 4, 2026

While LeBron James weighs his future and blockbuster trades wait to become official, a new generation of NBA players is already playing basketball today. The 2026 NBA Salt Lake City Summer League tipped off on Independence Day at the Jon M. Huntsman Center on the campus of the University of Utah.

Four teams are competing in Salt Lake City: the Utah Jazz, Atlanta Hawks, Memphis Grizzlies, and Oklahoma City Thunder. Saturday's slate features two games — the Grizzlies vs. the Thunder at 3 PM ET and the Hawks vs. the Jazz at 5 PM ET.

Why Summer League Matters

Summer League is the laboratory of the NBA. Draft picks get their first taste of professional-level competition. Undrafted free agents fight for roster spots that could define their careers. Coaches and front office staff evaluate which young players can make the jump from college or overseas to the NBA level.

The Salt Lake City league is one of two pre-Las Vegas summer events — the California Classic, held in Sacramento and San Francisco, began July 3 and features a separate six-team bracket. Both serve as warm-up acts for the main event.

Las Vegas Is the Main Stage

The marquee showcase is the Las Vegas Summer League, which begins July 9 and runs through July 19. All 30 NBA teams participate, and the games are televised nationally on ESPN and NBA TV. This is where the biggest storylines unfold — top rookies in head-to-head matchups, viral moments, and breakout performances that can change a player's trajectory overnight.

No. 1 overall pick AJ Dybantsa of the Washington Wizards, who signed a 4-year, $66.91 million rookie deal, will be among the most-watched players in Las Vegas. The 18-year-old out of BYU was the most-hyped prospect since the early days of Zion Williamson, and NBA fans are eager to see how his game translates.

What's Next

The Salt Lake City Summer League wraps up July 7. The California Classic concludes that same week. Then on July 9, Las Vegas takes center stage for 11 days of basketball that will set the tone for what is already shaping up to be one of the most transformational NBA seasons in memory.

While the veterans navigate free agency drama, the rookies are just getting started. July 4, 2026 — the NBA is celebrating Independence Day in the best way it knows how: with basketball.
