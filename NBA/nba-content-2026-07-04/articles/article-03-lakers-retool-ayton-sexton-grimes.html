Post-LeBron Lakers Move Fast: Ayton to Washington, Sexton and Grimes Signed

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 4, 2026

LeBron James hasn't even chosen his next team yet, and the Los Angeles Lakers are already deep into rebuilding without him. The front office has wasted no time reshaping the roster around a new identity — and the moves are coming fast.

The Lakers traded center Deandre Ayton to the Washington Wizards in exchange for guard Jaden Hardy and two future second-round picks in 2031 and 2032. Combined with the earlier agreement to sign Walker Kessler from the Utah Jazz on a 4-year, $130 million deal, Los Angeles has completely overhauled its frontcourt.

The New Backcourt Takes Shape

While the big-man situation is sorted, the Lakers have added perimeter pieces to complement the interior. Collin Sexton, a dynamic scoring guard who averaged over 20 points per game in multiple seasons, agreed to a deal with Los Angeles. Wing Quentin Grimes, known for his three-point shooting and defensive versatility, also signed with the team. Center Sandro Mamukelashvili rounds out the new additions.

The core that is taking shape: Kessler as the defensive anchor at center, with Sexton and Grimes providing perimeter punch and versatility. It is not a championship roster — but it is a functional, competitive team built for player development and future flexibility.

What the Ayton Move Means

Ayton, the No. 1 overall pick in the 2018 NBA Draft, never fully delivered on expectations in Los Angeles. Moving him to Washington clears cap space and roster room while the Lakers receive a promising young guard in Jaden Hardy — who averaged 11.4 points per game last season for the Wizards as a developing scorer.

For the Wizards, landing Ayton gives them a veteran big man to pair alongside No. 1 pick AJ Dybantsa as they begin their own youth movement.

What's Next

The Lakers' rebuild is real and it's happening quickly. Once LeBron's destination becomes official on July 6, Los Angeles will know what it is working with long-term. If James chooses the Warriors or Cavaliers, the Lakers fully commit to the youth movement around Kessler, Sexton, and Grimes. If the conversation ever returns to Los Angeles — highly unlikely but never fully off the table — all these pieces remain tradeable assets.

For now, the Purple and Gold are pivoting with purpose. The post-LeBron era in Los Angeles has officially begun.
