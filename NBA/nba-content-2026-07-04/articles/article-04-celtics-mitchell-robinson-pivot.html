Celtics Sign Mitchell Robinson: Boston's Post-Brown Pivot Is Taking Shape

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | July 4, 2026

When the Boston Celtics traded Jaylen Brown to the Philadelphia 76ers for Paul George and a package of draft picks, the reaction across the NBA was shock. Brown had been with Boston for a decade. He was a Finals MVP. He was, for many fans, the face of the franchise alongside Jayson Tatum.

But the Celtics are not standing still in grief. They are building — quickly and deliberately.

Boston agreed to sign center Mitchell Robinson to a 3-year, $47 million contract. The move brings one of the league's best rim protectors to the Garden to anchor the interior.

What Robinson Brings

Mitchell Robinson is a specialist — but a rare one. The 7-foot center is among the elite shot-blockers in the NBA and one of the most efficient offensive rebounders in the game. He does not need the ball to be impactful. In a system built around Tatum and George, Robinson's job is clear: protect the rim, control the glass, and stay out of foul trouble.

For a Celtics team that ranked among the league leaders in defense under Ime Udoka and Joe Mazzulla, adding Robinson keeps the defensive identity intact even as the offensive star power evolves.

The New-Look Celtics Core

Boston's projected roster is taking shape around Jayson Tatum, Paul George, Mitchell Robinson, and veteran guard Mike Conley — who signed a 1-year deal earlier this offseason. Tatum remains a perennial All-NBA player entering his prime. George, a three-time All-Star, gives the Celtics a second star capable of carrying offensive load.

The Celtics also hold substantial draft capital from the Brown trade — future Philadelphia first-round picks in 2028 and 2031, plus two second-rounders — giving Brad Stevens the flexibility to add pieces or make a move if the right opportunity arises.

What's Next

The Celtics won't be a favorite going into 2026-27. Losing Brown hurts, and the Eastern Conference will be dramatically more competitive with Giannis in Miami and Brown in Philadelphia. But Boston has a star in Tatum, a veteran in George, a defensive anchor in Robinson, and a front office in Stevens that has consistently made smart decisions.

Don't write them off yet. The Celtics pivot is a work in progress — and it may surprise some people come October.
