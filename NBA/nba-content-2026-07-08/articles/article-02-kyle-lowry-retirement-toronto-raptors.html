Kyle Lowry Retires as a Toronto Raptor: 'I Always Said I Would Do This Here'

By Damon Pierce | July 8, 2026

NBA Retirement

Kyle Lowry closed the greatest chapter of his career the way he always promised he would: in Toronto, wearing Raptors red and black, with his family beside him and a city at his feet.

On July 7, 2026, Lowry signed a ceremonial one-day contract with the Toronto Raptors and officially announced his retirement from professional basketball. His No. 7 jersey — worn through nine seasons, six All-Star games, and one championship parade — will be retired in a ceremony at Scotiabank Arena during the 2026-27 season.

Toronto Mayor Olivia Chow proclaimed July 7 "Kyle Lowry Day" in the city. It was the kind of civic honor that does not happen by accident. It happens when a player earns it over thousands of possessions, countless comeback wins, and a championship that permanently altered what a city believes about itself.

The Moment That Defined Everything

Everything about Kyle Lowry's career in Toronto points back to June 13, 2019. Game 6 of the NBA Finals. Oracle Arena, Oakland. The Raptors trailed the Golden State Warriors three games to two. Lowry, who had averaged just 10.2 points through the first five games while battling injuries and defensive pressure, erupted for 26 points on 7-of-14 shooting. The Raptors won 114-110. They became champions. Canada became an NBA city in the truest sense.

It is the single greatest moment in Canadian sports history. And Lowry was at the center of it.

Nine Seasons, One City, One Legacy

Lowry arrived in Toronto in 2012 from Houston, an undersized point guard with a mean streak and a relentless motor. He left nine years later as the defining Raptor — six All-Star selections, a championship ring, and the franchise's all-time leader in games played. In between, he rebuilt the Raptors' culture from the ground up, challenged teammates to toughen up, and refused to let the organization be satisfied with second-round exits.

He did not do it alone. DeMar DeRozan was his partner for most of that era. Kawhi Leonard played one transformative season and then left. Pascal Siakam grew up around him. But Lowry was the constant — the point guard who defined the Raptors identity for nearly a decade.

At his retirement press conference on Tuesday, Lowry was emotional and direct about what Toronto meant to him:

"This place has just been special for me since the day I got here. I always said I would retire as a Toronto Raptor."

He meant it. He kept his word.

Life After Basketball in His Adopted City

Lowry's connection to Toronto does not end at the Scotiabank Arena. He and his wife, Ayahna Cornish-Lowry, have become co-owners of the WNBA's Toronto Tempo, the franchise that launched in 2026 as part of the league's expansion into Canada. The investment keeps Lowry tied to professional basketball and to the city that made him a household name north of the border.

The jersey retirement ceremony — date to be announced but expected during the 2026-27 season — will bring him back to the Scotiabank Arena one more time. When No. 7 goes to the rafters, it will hang alongside the names of the greatest players in franchise history: Vince Carter, Chris Bosh, Tracy McGrady.

No one will argue about whether it belongs there.

What's Next

The ceremony celebrating Lowry's jersey retirement is expected to take place during the 2026-27 NBA season, with the Raptors expected to announce a date once the schedule is finalized. As a co-owner of the Toronto Tempo, Lowry will remain a visible figure in the city's sports scene. And in Toronto, where the 2019 championship still echoes in conversations about what the Raptors can be, his legend is only going to grow.
