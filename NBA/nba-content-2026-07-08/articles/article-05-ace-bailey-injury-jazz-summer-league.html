Ace Bailey Out for Vegas Opener — Hip Flexor Concern Clouds Jazz Rookie Debut

By Damon Pierce | July 8, 2026

NBA Summer League / Injury

The Utah Jazz came to the 2026 NBA Summer League with one of the most celebrated rookie classes in the league's draft history. Through the lens of the first week, one of those rookies — Darryn Peterson — has been everything advertised and then some. Another — Ace Bailey — has not played a single meaningful minute since going down in Game 1 in Salt Lake City.

CBS Sports confirmed Tuesday that Bailey will not play in the Jazz's Las Vegas Summer League opener on July 9. The latest medical evaluation describes a hip flexor concern — the injury began as back spasms in SLC's opening game against the Hawks — and Bailey's status is being monitored day-to-day by the Jazz's medical staff.

It is Summer League, so the long view applies. But Jazz fans had reason to hope for more.

What Happened in Salt Lake City

Bailey, one of the Jazz's 2026 draft selections, suffered back spasms during Game 1 of the Salt Lake City Summer League against Atlanta. He exited the game and did not return. The Jazz went on to win that game in overtime — largely on Peterson's 28-point performance — but Bailey's absence cast a shadow over what was otherwise a triumphant debut for the rookie class.

He sat Games 2 and 3 as well, with the team reporting he was being evaluated. By the time the medical staff upgraded the diagnosis to a hip flexor concern, the Jazz were already packing for Las Vegas. Clutch Points ran a headline noting that Bailey's injury "takes some luster off Darryn Peterson's Jazz Summer League dominance" — a slightly harsh way of framing a situation that is still early and evolving.

What It Means for the Jazz in Vegas

The practical effect of Bailey's absence is that Peterson will carry the Jazz's Summer League load with considerably less support. Third rookie Cody Williams — the Jazz's other high-profile draft pick — is expected to play and will receive additional responsibility with Bailey on the sideline.

Peterson has shown he can dominate against Summer League competition. His 28-point debut and 25-point/12-assist follow-up in SLC put him in the national conversation as the most impressive rookie of the summer so far. His opening-night matchup against AJ Dybantsa — the No. 1 overall pick, making his pro debut with the Wizards — will draw the most attention of any Summer League game this year.

But the Jazz's long-term vision involves all three of their 2026 rookies developing on the court together. Bailey's absence, however temporary it proves to be, delays that picture.

Keeping It in Perspective

Summer League games do not count. None of what happens in Las Vegas or Salt Lake City appears in any official box score, standings column, or statistical database that matters for the regular season. Bailey has plenty of time to get healthy before training camp opens in late September.

Still, the concern is real because the muscle group is real. Hip flexor injuries can be nagging and slow-healing. The worst-case scenario — which there is no current reason to assume — would be an injury that lingers into preseason. The Jazz and their medical staff will manage Bailey carefully, and Tuesday's "not playing" update is the cautious and appropriate response.

What's Next

The Jazz will monitor Bailey's hip flexor on a daily basis during Las Vegas Summer League. If he is able to play later in the two-week event, expect the Jazz to get him some minutes — carefully — to maintain his development timeline. The more important date is September, when training camp begins and the 2026-27 season starts taking shape. Bailey's health heading into camp will be the real measure of what Tuesday's news ultimately means for the Jazz rebuild.
