No. 1 vs. No. 2: Dybantsa and Peterson Open Las Vegas Summer League in the Most Anticipated Rookie Matchup of the Summer

By Marcus Cole | July 8, 2026

NBA Summer League

AJ Dybantsa has never played a professional basketball game. He is about to play his first one against the hottest rookie in the country.

Wednesday night at 9 PM ET, Dybantsa — the No. 1 overall pick in the 2026 NBA Draft, selected by the Washington Wizards — makes his professional debut at the Las Vegas Summer League against Darryn Peterson and the Utah Jazz. The game is on ESPN at Thomas & Mack Center, and NBA.com has already listed it among the five must-see matchups of the entire Summer League.

This is the game of the summer.

What Dybantsa Brings

At 6-foot-9, Dybantsa is a wing who can do nearly everything on a basketball court. Last season at BYU, he led all of college basketball in scoring at 25.5 points per game — adding 6.8 rebounds, 3.7 assists, 51% shooting from the field, and 77.4% from the free throw line. He was the consensus No. 1 pick from early in the college season, and the Wizards selected him without hesitation.

He has not played a professional game yet. Las Vegas is his debut. There is no tape from any summer league — no SLC games, no warm-up, no soft introductory matchup. His first game is against the No. 2 pick, who has already put up back-to-back dominant performances and received national feature coverage from NBC Sports.

That is a lot to absorb for your first professional moment. Dybantsa's camp clearly is not concerned about easing him in.

What Peterson Has Already Done

Peterson, the Jazz's No. 2 overall selection, spent three games in Salt Lake City turning heads. In Game 1 against the Hawks, he scored 28 points on 11-of-21 shooting (4-of-7 from three), grabbed 5 rebounds, and delivered a game-winning play in overtime. In Game 2 against the Grizzlies — with No. 3 pick Cameron Boozer on the other side — Peterson posted 25 points and 12 assists. The Jazz won that one too.

The Jazz closed out SLC Summer League 3-0, with Peterson and the other key rookies (Cody Williams, Ace Bailey) rested for Game 3 to prepare for the bigger stage. NBC Sports ran a feature headlined: "It may be just Summer League, but Darryn Peterson looks special."

He arrives in Vegas as the story everyone is already talking about.

The Storylines Within the Storyline

Beyond the simple No. 1 vs. No. 2 matchup, there are layers to tonight's game. Dybantsa grew up in a different recruiting ecosystem — he committed to BYU as part of the new era of name, image, and likeness agreements that reshaped college basketball. Peterson is a product of a different path, and the Jazz are counting on him as the centerpiece of their rebuild.

Both players will be under extraordinary scrutiny. Every possession will be dissected. Every shot charted. Every defensive breakdown amplified. That is the nature of being the top two picks, and that is the nature of Summer League when the matchup is this good.

Cameron Boozer — the No. 3 pick from Memphis — will also play in Vegas, with the Grizzlies squaring off against the Bulls in a separate game. The battle of the top three picks will be the defining storyline of the 2026 Las Vegas Summer League.

What's Next

Wednesday's game is just the first chapter. Both Dybantsa and Peterson are expected to play multiple games in Las Vegas through July 19. Ace Bailey — Peterson's Jazz teammate, dealing with a hip flexor concern — remains day-to-day and is not expected to play in the opener. His availability as the Summer League progresses will affect how the Jazz's rookie showcase is evaluated. For now, the spotlight belongs to Peterson and Dybantsa. Tune in at 9 PM ET on ESPN.
