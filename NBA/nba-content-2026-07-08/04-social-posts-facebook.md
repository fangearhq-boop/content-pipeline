### STORY 2: Kyle Lowry Retires as a Toronto Raptor

**Long-Form Post (11:00 AM ET)**

💛❤️ The Toronto Raptors — and all of Canadian basketball — said goodbye to a legend today.

Kyle Lowry signed a ceremonial one-day contract and officially retired as a Toronto Raptor on July 7, 2026. His No. 7 jersey will be raised to the rafters at Scotiabank Arena during the 2026-27 season.

Toronto Mayor Olivia Chow proclaimed July 7 "Kyle Lowry Day" in the city.

At the press conference, Lowry said what his fans always believed he'd say:

"This place has just been special for me since the day I got here. I always said I would retire as a Toronto Raptor."

He meant every word. In 2019, Lowry and the Raptors did the unthinkable — they beat the Golden State Warriors for the franchise's first and only NBA championship. It is the single greatest moment in Canadian sports history, and Lowry was at the center of it.

He spent nine seasons in Toronto (2012-2021), becoming a six-time All-Star and the most beloved Raptor of all time. In retirement, Lowry and his wife Ayahna have become co-owners of the WNBA's Toronto Tempo — staying connected to the city that made him.
