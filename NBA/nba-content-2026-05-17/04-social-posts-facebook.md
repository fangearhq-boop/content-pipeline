### STORY 3: Wembanyama Unanimous DPOY

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

Victor Wembanyama made NBA history — and he hasn't even started the Western Conference Finals yet.

The 22-year-old San Antonio Spurs center became the first player in NBA history to win the Defensive Player of the Year Award unanimously, claiming all 100 first-place votes for the 2025-26 Kia NBA DPOY Award. He is also the youngest player ever to win the award.

Wembanyama led the NBA in blocked shots for the third consecutive season, finishing the regular year with 197 blocks and 66 steals. His footwork, length, and court awareness at 7-foot-4 are simply unlike anything the league has seen.

He also joins an exclusive club — only David Robinson and Michael Jordan had previously won both the Rookie of the Year and the Defensive Player of the Year in their careers.

The other finalists? Chet Holmgren of OKC and Ausar Thompson of Detroit — both excellent players — but neither of them was close.

Wemby already has Rookie of the Year (2024) and DPOY (2025, 2026) on his résumé. He's 22. The Western Conference Finals start tomorrow.

The scary thing is, he's still getting better.
