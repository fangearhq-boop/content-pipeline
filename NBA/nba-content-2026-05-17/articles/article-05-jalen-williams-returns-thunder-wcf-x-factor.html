Jalen Williams Expected Back for OKC: The X-Factor That Could Define the WCF

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 17, 2026

The Oklahoma City Thunder were already the favorites in the Western Conference Finals. Getting Jalen Williams back for Game 1 makes them more dangerous still.

Williams, who averaged 24.0 points per game during the regular season, is expected to return for OKC when the Thunder host the San Antonio Spurs on Monday (8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock). His availability is the subplot that could fundamentally change how this series unfolds — and how San Antonio tries to slow down a defending championship team.

Why Williams Changes the Equation

Victor Wembanyama is one of the most versatile and gifted defenders in NBA history. The Spurs' 7-foot-4 center and unanimous 2026 Defensive Player of the Year can guard multiple positions, protect the rim, and change the trajectory of shots he doesn't even touch. He is a legitimate individual force capable of bottling up elite offensive players.

But even Wembanyama cannot guard two people at once.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander averaged 31.6 points per game in the regular season on 55% shooting. He led the NBA in clutch points. He is an MVP finalist who has been one of the two or three best players on the planet all year. Any opposing defense has to build its scheme around limiting him first.

Now add Williams — 24.0 points per game, a second scorer who can punish any double-team or defensive rotation that collapses on SGA. The Thunder have two players who, on different nights, could be the best offensive player on the floor. Defending that combination with any individual defensive player, however gifted, is extraordinarily difficult.

The Regular Season Context

San Antonio went 4-1 against Oklahoma City in the regular season, including a victory in the NBA Cup semifinal. That edge is real, but context matters. The conditions of those matchups — including whether Williams was fully available and healthy for all five games — need to be accounted for when projecting how the WCF will unfold.

The postseason is also a different environment. OKC has swept two opponents and played with a level of focus and execution that regular-season film may not fully capture. A team operating at 8-0 in the playoffs builds confidence and cohesion that is difficult to model.

What It Means for the Spurs

San Antonio will need more than Wembanyama to win this series. De'Aaron Fox's ability to create in transition and generate easy buckets for the Spurs will matter enormously if OKC's half-court defense pins the Spurs in place. Stephon Castle, who had a brilliant closeout performance against Minnesota (32 points, 11 rebounds, 6 assists), needs to sustain his form against a more formidable defensive opponent.

The Spurs are a well-built team with legitimate title aspirations. But the return of Williams shifts the burden of proof back onto San Antonio. Beating OKC once in the regular season is one thing. Winning four games in a seven-game series against a fully healthy defending championship roster is another challenge entirely.

What's Next

Game 1 of the Western Conference Finals tips off Monday, May 18 at 8:30 PM ET at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City on NBC and Peacock. Whether Williams is at full strength — and how San Antonio's defense adjusts to having both him and SGA to account for — will be the defining question of the series opener. This WCF has the makings of something memorable. Don't miss Game 1.
