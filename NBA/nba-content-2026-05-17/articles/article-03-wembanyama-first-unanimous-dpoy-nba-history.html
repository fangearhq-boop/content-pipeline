Wembanyama Wins First Unanimous DPOY in NBA History at Age 22

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 17, 2026

In over six decades of the Defensive Player of the Year Award, no player had ever received every single first-place vote. Victor Wembanyama changed that in 2026.

The San Antonio Spurs center was named the 2025-26 Kia NBA Defensive Player of the Year with all 100 first-place votes — the first unanimous winner in the award's history. He is also the youngest player ever to claim the honor, at 22 years old. The accolades keep stacking up for the most extraordinary young talent the NBA has seen in a generation.

What Wembanyama Did Defensively This Season

The numbers begin to tell the story, even if they fall short of capturing what Wembanyama actually looks like on a basketball court. He led the NBA in blocked shots for the third consecutive season, finishing the regular year with 197 blocks. He added 66 steals — remarkable for a center.

But the impact extends far beyond box-score statistics. At 7-foot-4 with an extraordinary wingspan, Wembanyama can contest shots at the rim, switch onto guards and wings in pick-and-roll coverage, and alter shots he doesn't even reach. The threat of his presence compresses driving lanes and forces opposing offenses into uncomfortable decision-making before the play has fully developed.

The Spurs' team defense was anchored by Wembanyama's presence. Opposing offenses game-planned around avoiding him entirely — a defensive impact that doesn't register in traditional statistics but is visible to anyone watching.

Historic Company

Wembanyama joins Michael Jordan and David Robinson as the only players in NBA history to win both the Rookie of the Year Award and the Defensive Player of the Year Award in their careers. Wembanyama won Rookie of the Year in 2024, his first season in the league. He then won DPOY in 2025 and again unanimously in 2026.

He is the fourth Spurs player to win the Defensive Player of the Year: Alvin Robertson took the award in 1986, David Robinson in 1992, and Kawhi Leonard in both 2015 and 2016. San Antonio now has more DPOY winners than any franchise in NBA history.

The other finalists for this year's award were Chet Holmgren of the Oklahoma City Thunder and Ausar Thompson of the Detroit Pistons — both outstanding defensive players. Neither was close to Wembanyama in the voting.

And the WCF Starts Monday

What makes this recognition particularly striking is the timing. Wembanyama accepted this distinction with the Western Conference Finals beginning tomorrow night at Paycom Center. He is not just the league's best defender in theory — he is about to prove it again against the Oklahoma City Thunder and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, an MVP finalist who averaged 31.6 points per game this season.

At 22, Wembanyama has already built a résumé that most players spend entire careers chasing. His upward trajectory suggests this is not a ceiling — it is a floor.

What's Next

Wembanyama and the Spurs face the Thunder in Game 1 of the Western Conference Finals on Monday, May 18 (8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock) at Paycom Center. The unanimous DPOY will be tested immediately against the league's top offense. He has faced this kind of challenge before — and made history. Don't be surprised if he does it again.
