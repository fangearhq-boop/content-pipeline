Knicks ECF Schedule Set: Game 1 Tuesday After Long Wait Ends

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 17, 2026

The New York Knicks swept their way to the Eastern Conference Finals and then waited. Tonight, the wait finally ends.

The Knicks will learn their ECF opponent after the Cleveland Cavaliers and Detroit Pistons play a decisive Game 7 tonight (8:00 PM ET, Amazon Prime Video) in Detroit. Whoever wins that game faces New York in Game 1 of the Eastern Conference Finals on Tuesday, May 19, with the full schedule set and all games airing on ESPN and ABC.

New York hasn't played since May 11, when Miles McBride's 25-point, seven-for-nine shooting performance — including four three-pointers in the first quarter, the first Knick to accomplish that feat since 1997 — sealed a sweep of the Philadelphia 76ers in the second round.

The ECF Schedule

Game 1 is Tuesday, May 19. Game 2 follows on Thursday, May 21. The series shifts for Game 3 (Saturday, May 23) and Game 4 (Monday, May 25). If the series extends, Games 5 through 7 are scheduled for May 27, May 29, and May 31, respectively. Every game airs on ESPN or ABC.

The Knicks have had six days off before the ECF begins. That rest could be a significant advantage against either potential opponent, both of whom played a seven-game series — the full distance — just to get here.

Home-Court Advantage: The Unanswered Question

Who holds home court in the ECF will not be determined until Game 7 is final. The Detroit Pistons are the No. 1 seed in the Eastern Conference — meaning if they beat the Cavaliers tonight, they would own home-court advantage against the Knicks, who are the No. 2 seed.

A Cleveland victory flips that equation. The Cavaliers are the No. 4 seed. In a Cavs-Knicks series, New York would hold home court at Madison Square Garden. For a franchise and a city that have spent years dreaming of ECF basketball at MSG, that detail matters.

How the Knicks Got Here

New York's postseason run has been a statement of depth and execution. They swept the Atlanta Hawks in the first round and followed by sweeping Philadelphia in five games, with Jalen Brunson, Karl-Anthony Towns, Josh Hart, OG Anunoby, and McBride all contributing at different moments.

Brunson has been the anchor throughout — the steady presence in the fourth quarter who has made New York impossible to rattle in close games. Towns has brought the interior size that forces opponents to adjust. McBride's emergence as a genuine offensive threat has given head coach Tom Thibodeau another reliable option in key moments.

The Knicks have not looked like a team conserving energy. They have looked like a team locked in and building toward something. The long rest period between the second round and the ECF gives them time to reset and focus entirely on whoever comes out of tonight's Game 7.

What's Next

Game 7 between Cleveland and Detroit tips off tonight at 8:00 PM ET. New York will know its ECF opponent by 11:00 PM. Game 1 of the Eastern Conference Finals is Tuesday, May 19, on ESPN. The Knicks have done their part. Now they watch and wait for one more night.
