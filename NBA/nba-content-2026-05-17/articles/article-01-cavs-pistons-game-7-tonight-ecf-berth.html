Cavs-Pistons Game 7 Tonight: One Game, One Eastern Conference Finals Berth

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 17, 2026

The No. 1 seed has to go on the road tonight and fight for its playoff life. The series no one expected to go this deep has arrived at its ultimate destination.

The Cleveland Cavaliers and Detroit Pistons play a decisive Game 7 tonight at Little Caesars Arena in Detroit (8:00 PM ET, Amazon Prime Video), with the winner advancing to the Eastern Conference Finals against the New York Knicks beginning Tuesday, May 19. The loser goes home.

Cleveland entered this postseason as the East's top seed — expected to navigate the bracket with relative ease. Detroit had other ideas. After the Pistons won the first two games of the series on Cleveland's floor, the Cavaliers rallied to win three straight and take a 3-2 lead. Then came Game 6.

How Detroit Flipped the Series Again

On Friday night in Cleveland, the Pistons delivered the most dominant road performance of their postseason run — and perhaps of this entire 2026 playoff field. Detroit won 115-94, a final margin that doesn't do justice to how complete the victory was.

It was the Cavaliers' first home loss of the 2026 playoffs. Every other time Cleveland played at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse this postseason, they won. Detroit ended that streak emphatically, and now they head home with the series advantage.

Cade Cunningham was central to Detroit's blowout. The Pistons' star guard finished with 21 points, connecting on 5-of-10 three-point attempts in a composed, controlled performance. He didn't force anything and made every right decision.

Donovan Mitchell, by contrast, had one of the worst shooting nights of his 2026 playoff run. The Cavaliers' cornerstone finished with 18 points on 6-of-20 from the field and 2-of-6 from three — a far cry from the Mitchell who put up 43 points in Game 2 or scored 18 in OT to help Cleveland win Game 5. James Harden chipped in 21 points for the Cavs, but the secondary pieces couldn't compensate for the star's off night. Jalen Duren added 15 points, 11 rebounds, and 3 blocks for Detroit, and Daniss Jenkins contributed 15 more in a full-team effort.

The Injury Picture Tonight

Cleveland enters Game 7 with Larry Nance Jr. listed as doubtful due to illness — a minor roster note, though Nance's role off the bench is not inconsequential. On the Detroit side, Huerter, Caris LeVert, and Duncan Robinson are listed as questionable. All three could potentially factor into the game depending on rotations.

Neither Cunningham nor Mitchell is on the injury report. The stars will be available. This game will be decided by who plays better.

Stakes and Context

The Cavaliers have now been in this exact spot before — needing to win on the road in this series. They won Game 2 away from Cleveland to stay in it early on. The road hasn't been insurmountable for this team.

But Detroit's crowd tonight will be different from anything Cleveland has encountered. A Game 7 at home — one that puts the Pistons in the Eastern Conference Finals for the first time in years — will generate a level of energy that Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in a closeout game simply cannot match.

The Knicks have been watching. New York swept both the Atlanta Hawks and the Philadelphia 76ers to reach the ECF without needing a seventh game themselves. They've been resting since May 11. From a preparation standpoint, they are ready for either opponent.

Home-court in the ECF is also at stake. The Pistons, as the East's No. 1 seed, would hold home-court advantage in any Eastern Conference Finals series they reach. The Cavs are the No. 4 seed — meaning a Cleveland win would give the No. 2 seed Knicks home court at Madison Square Garden.

What's Next

Tip-off is at 8:00 PM ET tonight at Little Caesars Arena on Amazon Prime Video. Mitchell needs to rediscover the form that carried Cleveland through multiple deficits in this series. Cunningham needs to stay in control and let his home crowd fuel his focus. The winner faces the Knicks in Game 1 on Tuesday, May 19.

Everything this series has built toward ends tonight. One game. One winner. One Eastern Conference Finals spot.
