Thunder vs. Spurs WCF Preview: Defending Champions Face Wemby's Rising Spurs

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 17, 2026

The Oklahoma City Thunder are 8-0 in the 2026 playoffs. They are the defending NBA champions. They have the league's MVP finalist as their best player, and their second-leading scorer is coming back healthy for the Conference Finals.

None of that intimidated the San Antonio Spurs during the regular season.

San Antonio went 4-1 against Oklahoma City in the 2025-26 regular season, including a win in the NBA Cup. Now the teams meet in the Western Conference Finals, with Game 1 set for Monday at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City (8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock). It is the most compelling series of the 2026 postseason.

Oklahoma City's Case for Repeating

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander had one of the great regular seasons in recent memory. He averaged 31.6 points, 6.5 assists, and 4.4 rebounds per game while shooting 55% from the field. He scored at least 20 points in 127 consecutive regular-season games — a streak of consistency that is almost impossible to fathom.

In the playoffs, SGA has been the Thunder's engine at every turn. Against the Los Angeles Lakers in the second round, he averaged 24.5 points and 6.3 assists per game. In the first round versus the Phoenix Suns, he set a new playoff high with 42 points in Game 3, shooting 15-of-18 from the field. He led the NBA in total clutch points during the regular season with 175 on 6.5 per game in clutch time.

Oklahoma City also gets wing Jalen Williams back for this series. Williams averaged 24.0 points per game during the regular season and adds a second first-option-caliber scorer to an already loaded OKC lineup. The Thunder enter the Western Conference Finals healthier and deeper than at any point in their postseason run.

The supporting cast is formidable. Chet Holmgren remains one of the most versatile big men in the game. Luguentz Dort is one of the league's elite defensive wings. This organization is built to win another championship.

San Antonio's Case for the Upset

The Spurs earned their spot here. They were the No. 2 seed in the Western Conference — 62 wins, one of the most striking turnarounds in NBA history for a franchise that won fewer than 25 games just two seasons ago.

Victor Wembanyama was named the 2025-26 Defensive Player of the Year unanimously — all 100 first-place votes — making him the first ever to receive that distinction. He led the NBA in blocked shots for the third consecutive season (197 blocks) and adds 66 steals to his résumé. At 7-foot-4, he can contest shots at the rim and switch onto perimeter players in ways that leave offenses with no good options.

De'Aaron Fox provides the veteran floor general presence this young team needs. In the Game 6 closeout win over Minnesota, Fox posted 21 points and 9 assists. Stephon Castle, the No. 4 pick in 2024, put together one of the most electric closeout performances of the playoffs — 32 points on 11-of-16 shooting, 11 rebounds, 6 assists. Castle is 20 years old and apparently doesn't know what pressure is.

The regular-season series edge is meaningful context, not a guarantee — but it shows that the Spurs have cracked OKC's defensive code before. Wembanyama guarding SGA while the rest of San Antonio disrupts the rhythm has worked at least four times this season.

The Key Matchup

The core question of this series: Can Wembanyama guard both SGA and a healthy Jalen Williams? During the regular season, Williams was limited or absent for several of those Spurs wins. The math changes significantly when OKC has two first-option scorers operating simultaneously.

Wembanyama is remarkable, but defense is a five-man game. If the Spurs' perimeter help defenders can contain SGA enough to force someone else to beat them — and Williams is that someone — it will test San Antonio's scheme in ways the regular season didn't fully reveal.

What's Next

Game 1 tips off Monday, May 18 at 8:30 PM ET at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City, airing on NBC and Peacock. This is the series the Western Conference was built toward all season. OKC with the defending-champion pedigree versus San Antonio with the young talent and the statistical edge. Watch closely — this one could go the distance.
