LeBron James Leaves the Lakers: Warriors Are -500 Favorites to Land the King

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 1, 2026

The era is over. LeBron James has informed the Los Angeles Lakers that he will not return for the 2026-27 season — the most consequential free agency announcement in years, and the definitive close of a chapter that began when he arrived in Hollywood in 2018.

LeBron instructed agent Rich Paul to open conversations with all interested teams. The Golden State Warriors are the immediate and overwhelming frontrunners, listed at -500 on DraftKings — meaning bettors need to risk $500 to win $100. No other team is close. The Cleveland Cavaliers check in at +300, the Miami Heat at +1,000, and the San Antonio Spurs at +2,000.

Why LeBron Is Leaving

The 2025-26 season provided the clearest answer. The Los Angeles Lakers were swept 4-0 by the Oklahoma City Thunder in the playoffs — a humbling exit for a team that could not match the athleticism and depth of the Western Conference's new elite. At 41 years old, LeBron James cannot afford to spend his final seasons in rebuild mode. The Lakers have no clear path back to contention on the immediate horizon.

The decision comes as LeBron prepares for his record-breaking 24th NBA season. No player in league history has appeared in more. Playing somewhere with a genuine championship window is almost certainly a factor in his thinking — the same logic that drove his move from Cleveland to Miami in 2010, his return to Cleveland in 2014, and his move to Los Angeles in 2018.

The Warriors' Case

Golden State's pursuit of LeBron is real and well-organized. The Warriors have financial flexibility, a franchise that understands how to win at the highest level, and a fanbase that would embrace a title run with the all-time great. The -500 odds suggest the betting market believes this landing spot is nearly settled — even before any official negotiations begin.

No contract can be signed until the moratorium lifts on July 6 at 12:01 PM ET. That means five more days before anything is official. But LeBron's direction of intent appears clear.

Cleveland, Miami, and San Antonio Remain in Play

The Cavaliers at +300 represent a genuine alternative — LeBron built two championships there, and the franchise has remained a competitive team in the Eastern Conference. The Heat at +1,000 carry the emotional weight of LeBron's two championship rings won in South Beach, and the Giannis Antetokounmpo trade to Miami means that roster is suddenly a contender. The Spurs at +2,000 are the longest shot among the field but represent the appeal of one final challenge — pairing LeBron with San Antonio's emerging young talent.

The End of the Laker Era

LeBron arrived in Los Angeles in 2018 and won the franchise's 17th championship in the 2020 NBA bubble. That title came without fans, in a singular pandemic season that nonetheless counted as much as any other. He gave the Lakers relevance, national attention, and a championship. What he could not give them was a sustainable championship contender.

The franchise now faces a meaningful rebuild. LeBron's departure opens a new chapter for Los Angeles — but it's the chapter ahead for LeBron himself that has the entire basketball world riveted.

What's Next

The moratorium lifts July 6. That's when deals become official, when Rich Paul can finalize terms, and when LeBron James formally changes his address. Until then, the Warriors remain -500 favorites to land one of the greatest players the sport has ever produced, heading into his 24th season. The league's new map is still being drawn.
