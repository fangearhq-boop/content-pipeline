Jaylen Brown's Market Keeps Moving: Portland Still Engaged, But Celtics Can't Find a Taker

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 1, 2026

The trade market has reshuffled dramatically in 48 hours. Portland landed Ja Morant. Toronto got Kawhi Leonard. And Jaylen Brown is still in green.

The Boston Celtics have been actively shopping their All-NBA Second Teamer since early in the offseason, and the asking price — four or more unprotected first-round picks — has not moved. What has moved is everything around it. The field of willing suitors is narrowing even as the timeline tightens.

Where the Market Stands

Portland remains in Brown discussions despite completing the Ja Morant trade from Memphis, according to reports. The Trail Blazers, now with Morant and Deni Avdija as building blocks, may be willing to add another star alongside their new acquisition if the package can be structured correctly. The Celtics, for their part, continue to push for maximum value.

ESPN's Brian Windhorst has been consistent on his read of the situation: Brown "is going to get traded." The Celtics have made a franchise-level decision about the direction of their rebuild. The question is whether any team matches Boston's price before the summer window closes.

What Brown Brings

The 2025-26 season was the best of Jaylen Brown's career. He averaged 28.7 points, 6.9 rebounds, and 5.1 assists per game — career highs across the board. He was named to the All-NBA Second Team and finished sixth in MVP voting. His two-way ability and contract status — three fully guaranteed years remaining on a five-year, $285.4 million extension signed in 2023 — make him one of the most coveted players available in any format.

Brown has not requested a trade. He is in the final years of a long-term commitment to Boston, playing at the peak of his career, and the decision to move him is the Celtics' call to make. He has handled the situation professionally.

The Extension Wrinkle

On July 26, Brown becomes eligible to sign a two-year, $141.9 million extension. That date could accelerate the trade timeline — Boston may want resolution before extending him becomes a conversation that further complicates any deal. Teams acquiring Brown inherit his current contract; an additional extension would require a new negotiation entirely.

What's Next

The Celtics are not in a hurry, but the market is moving. Every deal that gets done — every Kawhi, every Morant — shifts who has assets and who has need. Brown's value is not diminishing. Boston's patience is the real variable. With the summer window open and teams building their rosters around Giannis, LeBron, and Kawhi, the pressure to act is building on both sides. Expect the Brown saga to reach its conclusion before the summer is out.
