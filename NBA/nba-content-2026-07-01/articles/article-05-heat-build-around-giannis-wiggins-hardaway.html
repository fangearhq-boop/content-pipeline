Miami Doesn't Wait: Wiggins and Hardaway Jr. Are Incoming as Heat Build Around Giannis

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 1, 2026

The moratorium doesn't lift for five more days. The Giannis Antetokounmpo trade doesn't officially execute until July 6. But the Miami Heat are already building the team they intend to run.

Andrew Wiggins intends to sign a three-year, $64 million contract with Miami. Tim Hardaway Jr. has agreed to a one-year, $6.5 million deal. Both pieces fit exactly what the Heat need around their incoming superstar — wing versatility, positional defense, and perimeter shooting to complement the Giannis-Bam Adebayo frontcourt.

What Wiggins Brings

Andrew Wiggins is a proven commodity — a two-way wing who defends multiple positions, hits threes at an acceptable rate, and fits seamlessly into winning basketball environments. He was part of the Golden State Warriors' 2022 championship team, and his skill set is exactly the type that Pat Riley's organizations have always valued: versatile, unselfish, and coachable.

At $64 million over three years, Wiggins is being paid at a level commensurate with his value. Miami is not overpaying. They're building a contender on reasonable terms, which is the Riley way — maximize talent acquisition without creating roster-crippling commitments.

Hardaway Jr. and the Shooting Floor

Tim Hardaway Jr. on a one-year, $6.5 million deal is a smart, low-risk addition. He provides reliable three-point shooting and the kind of veteran bench scoring that championship teams need. At that price point, he adds value without constraining future flexibility.

The Core Coming Together

The Giannis trade — agreed June 22, official July 6 — sent Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., Kasparas Jakucionis, the No. 13 pick, two first-round picks, a pick swap, and a second-rounder to Milwaukee. Miami gave up enormous depth to land Giannis. The signings of Wiggins and Hardaway Jr. are the first steps in refilling the roster around what is now the most dominant frontcourt in the Eastern Conference.

Giannis Antetokounmpo and Bam Adebayo as a pairing is physically terrifying. Both are capable of guarding multiple positions, finishing at the rim, and operating in high-level half-court offense. Add Wiggins's wing versatility and shooting, Hardaway Jr.'s bench scoring, and Pat Riley's system, and Miami's formula begins to take shape.

The Heat's championship odds have already moved from 30-1 to 18-1 in anticipation of the Giannis trade. Expect that number to tighten further as the full roster picture comes into focus.

What's Next

Five days. July 6 is when the moratorium lifts, Giannis officially becomes a Miami Heat player, and the signings of Wiggins and Hardaway Jr. can be finalized on paper. The Eastern Conference is about to look different. The Heat are building fast, building smart, and building around a two-time MVP. South Beach's summer is just getting started.
