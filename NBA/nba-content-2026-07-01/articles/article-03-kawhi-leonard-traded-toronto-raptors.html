Kawhi Leonard Is Going Back to Toronto: The Clippers Trade the King of the North Home

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | July 1, 2026

Seven years later, Kawhi Leonard is heading back to Toronto.

The Los Angeles Clippers have agreed to trade Leonard to the Toronto Raptors in a blockbuster deal that sends the franchise's defining player home to the city that first truly embraced him as a superstar. In return, the Clippers receive Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, unprotected first-round picks in 2031 and 2033, second-round picks in 2030 and 2033, and a 2027 first-round pick swap.

The Reunion That Toronto Dreamed About

The 2018-19 Raptors season remains the most celebrated moment in Canadian basketball history. Leonard arrived from San Antonio and delivered a singular performance — the Game 7 buzzer-beater against Philadelphia that bounced four times on the rim before dropping in, the Eastern Conference Finals demolition of Milwaukee, and the championship triumph over Golden State. He was named Finals MVP. He was, for one extraordinary season, the best player in the world.

Then he left. One year in Toronto, one championship, and he was gone to Los Angeles. The departure stung, but it could not erase what he had given the franchise and the country. Now, at 34, he's coming back.

The Trade Breakdown

The Raptors paid a significant price. Brandon Ingram is a quality scorer — a player who averaged over 20 points per game at his best — but Toronto determined that a reunion with Kawhi was worth the asset expenditure. Gradey Dick, a young wing with three-point shooting upside, heads to Los Angeles alongside the pick package.

The Clippers, for their part, receive a rebuilding package that sets them up for future flexibility. Without Leonard, they enter a new phase — younger, draft-rich, looking toward the next window.

Can Kawhi Still Lead a Contender?

The question that has followed Leonard for the back half of his career is simple: how many games can he play? His body requires careful load management, and his availability has been inconsistent over the past several seasons. When healthy, Kawhi Leonard remains one of the most complete two-way players in the NBA — a lockdown defender, an elite shot creator, and a player who elevates in the biggest moments.

Paired with Scottie Barnes and a Raptors supporting cast that will be reshaped around this acquisition, Kawhi gives Toronto a legitimate contender in the Eastern Conference. The potential is real. The durability question remains open.

What's Next

The deal is done. Kawhi Leonard will be a Raptor again when the moratorium lifts on July 6. Toronto will rebuild its roster around his return, and the city's most beloved basketball moment will get a sequel. "We The North" has its cornerstone back — and the NBA is better for it.
