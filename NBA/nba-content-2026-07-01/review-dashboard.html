### Long-Form Post The Knicks won the championship. Now comes the hard part. Knicks owner James Dolan has publicly stated that New York will not operate in the second tax apron in the 2026-27 season. That statement carries enormous consequences for Karl-Anthony Towns, who is eligible for a four-year extension potentially worth $273 million. The message is clear: the Knicks want Towns to take a $7-10 million haircut on his max extension. The team also needs Josh Hart — also extension-eligible — to accept a similar discount. If both agree, the championship core of Towns, Jalen Brunson, OG Anunoby, Mikal Bridges, and Hart stays intact for multiple more years. The precedent is right there in the locker room. Jalen Brunson took approximately $113 million less than his market value, built the greatest Knicks season since 1973, and walked away from the 2026 NBA Finals as the series MVP. The Brunson Blueprint is the ask — a financial sacrifice in exchange for a championship window and legacy in New York. Towns has publicly said he's "ready to make a financial sacrifice." Now Dolan is calling that bluff. The extension window doesn't stay open forever, and the math only works if players buy in. If KAT takes the deal, the Knicks repeat. If he doesn't, the dynasty may end before it truly begins. ### Image Post Caption Owner James Dolan has drawn the line: no second apron, $7-10M discount on KAT's extension. ### Long-Form Post Seven years after he gave Toronto its only NBA championship, Kawhi Leonard is coming home. The Los Angeles Clippers have traded Leonard to the Toronto Raptors in a deal that reshapes both franchises. The Raptors are sending Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, unprotected first-round picks in 2031 and 2033, second-round picks in 2030 and 2033, and a 2027 first-round pick swap to Los Angeles. The Clippers get a rebuilding package of young talent and future capital. The Raptors get their culture-defining player back, at age 34, for what should be the final act of a Hall of Fame career. Kawhi's 2018-19 run with Toronto remains one of the most legendary single seasons in franchise history — the shot against Philadelphia, Game 6 against Milwaukee, and the championship triumph over Golden State. He left after one year, but Toronto never forgot what he brought. This is a reunion with legitimate championship upside. Paired with Scottie Barnes and a supporting cast, Kawhi gives the Raptors a genuine contender — assuming his availability holds. That's always been the question with Leonard, whose body requires careful management. When he's on the court, he's still one of the most complete two-way players in the game. The North has its cornerstone back. ### Image Post Caption Kawhi Leonard is returning to Toronto. The man who gave the Raptors their only championship is coming back at 34.
