Eight Teams, Four Spots: Your Complete 2026 NBA Play-In Tournament Preview

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 13, 2026

The 2026 SoFi NBA Play-In Tournament tips off tomorrow, April 14, and over the next four nights on Prime Video, eight teams will fight for four remaining playoff spots. One slip and your season is over. Welcome to the most intense basketball of the year — and the playoffs haven't even started yet.

Here is everything you need to know about who's playing, when they're playing, and what's at stake for every franchise still alive.

How the Play-In Works

The No. 7 and No. 8 seeds in each conference meet first. The winner advances directly to the playoffs as the No. 7 seed. The loser gets a second chance — they face the winner of the No. 9 vs. No. 10 game on April 17. The final two games on Friday determine who rounds out the playoff field as the No. 8 seeds. No. 9 and No. 10 teams are playing pure elimination basketball from night one.

Eastern Conference

No. 9 Charlotte Hornets vs. No. 10 Miami Heat

Tuesday, April 14 | 7:30 PM ET | Prime Video

This is the East's opening-night elimination game. Both the Hornets and Heat enter knowing that a loss means their offseason begins immediately. Charlotte, led by second-year sensation Kon Knueppel — the Rookie of the Year frontrunner who shattered the NBA record for three-pointers in a rookie season — has been one of the surprise teams of the second half. Miami has used its championship culture as a survival tool all season long.

No. 7 Philadelphia 76ers vs. No. 8 Orlando Magic

Wednesday, April 15 | 7:30 PM ET | Prime Video

This is the game with the most drama attached to it. Joel Embiid is out indefinitely for Philadelphia, stripping the 76ers of their most important player heading into a win-or-advance situation. The Orlando Magic enter as slight favorites — DraftKings has them at -1.5 — with Paolo Banchero leading their charge. A Magic victory sends them directly to the No. 7 seed and a first-round date with the Boston Celtics. A 76ers win has the same result for Philadelphia.

Western Conference

No. 7 Phoenix Suns vs. No. 8 Portland Trail Blazers

Tuesday, April 14 | 10 PM ET | Prime Video

The Suns have the seeding advantage and the chance to punch their ticket to the playoffs directly. Phoenix has been inconsistent all season but still earned the No. 7 spot in a crowded Western Conference. Portland, the No. 8 seed, brings a young and hungry team with nothing to lose. A Blazers upset here would be one of the stories of the tournament.

No. 9 Los Angeles Clippers vs. No. 10 Golden State Warriors

Wednesday, April 15 | 10 PM ET | Prime Video | Intuit Dome

This is the marquee matchup of the Play-In round. Kawhi Leonard is averaging a career-high 27.9 points per game and has been one of the most unstoppable offensive players in the NBA this season. His partner Darius Garland, acquired from Cleveland in a midseason trade, adds 20.2 points and 6.7 assists per game. The Clippers won three of four regular-season meetings, including a 115-110 win over Golden State just this past Sunday.

The Warriors, meanwhile, are battling adversity. Jimmy Butler is out for the season with a knee injury, and Moses Moody is also sidelined. Stephen Curry is still averaging 26.6 points per game, but even the two-time MVP can only do so much on a depleted roster. If this is indeed Curry's final act with Golden State, a Play-In exit would be a heartbreaking chapter in a legendary story.

Final Games — Friday, April 17

The losers of the 7/8 games return on Friday to face the winners of the 9/10 games. In the East, the 76ers or Magic loser gets one more shot. In the West, the Suns or Blazers loser gets another chance. Win and you're in the playoffs. Lose and go home. The Friday finales are always among the most emotionally charged games of the entire basketball calendar.

What's Next

After the Play-In concludes on April 17, the NBA Playoffs officially tip off on Saturday, April 18. The first-round matchups involving non-Play-In teams are already set: in the East, the New York Knicks host the Toronto Raptors and the Cleveland Cavaliers face the Atlanta Hawks. In the West, the Denver Nuggets take on the Minnesota Timberwolves and the Los Angeles Lakers battle the Houston Rockets. Eight days of Play-In drama set the stage for what promises to be one of the most compelling postseasons in recent memory.
