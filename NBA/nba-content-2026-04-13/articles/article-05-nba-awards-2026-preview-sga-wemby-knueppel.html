The 2026 NBA Awards Are Coming: SGA's Back-to-Back, Wemby's DPOY, and the Year's Best Rookie

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 13, 2026

The regular season is over. The Play-In begins tomorrow. And while the on-court drama is about to reach its peak, there is a parallel storyline unfolding just off the court: the 2026 NBA Awards season.

Award announcements begin in late April and run through May, dovetailing with the playoffs in classic NBA fashion. Based on the season's results, here is a breakdown of who is expected to win each major award — and why each race ended up the way it did.

Most Valuable Player: Shai Gilgeous-Alexander

This is not a race. It is a coronation.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander led the NBA in scoring for the second consecutive year at 31.6 points per game, while adding 6.5 assists, 4.4 rebounds and 1.4 steals per game. His Oklahoma City Thunder finished with the best record in the league. In ESPN's annual straw poll of media voters, SGA received 88 of 100 first-place votes.

The competition never really materialized. Victor Wembanyama made a compelling case based on historical defensive numbers and a spectacular offensive season, but even his supporters largely conceded that SGA's combination of individual production and team success was too strong to overcome. Nikola Jokic continued to do Jokic things — triple-doubles, elite efficiency — but a third MVP in three years faced significant resistance from voters. SGA is the clear and deserving winner of a second consecutive award.

Defensive Player of the Year: Victor Wembanyama

If SGA's MVP is a coronation, Wembanyama's DPOY is something rarer: a unanimous verdict that borders on unanimous acclaim.

At 22 years old, Wembanyama led the NBA in blocks at 3.1 per game. He is the most disruptive defensive presence the league has seen in a generation — a seven-foot-four center who moves like a guard and jumps like nobody's business, erasing shots that players were already proud of themselves for attempting. His 62-20 Spurs had one of the best team defensive ratings in basketball, and Wembanyama was the reason.

The expected unanimous selection carries additional historical weight. Wembanyama would become only the second San Antonio Spur to win the Defensive Player of the Year award, joining the legendary David Robinson. At 22, he likely has a decade of this ahead of him.

Rookie of the Year: Kon Knueppel

The Charlotte Hornets selected Kon Knueppel with the No. 4 pick in the 2025 NBA Draft, and he immediately made the pick look like a steal.

Knueppel finished the 2025-26 season with 265 three-pointers — obliterating the previous NBA record for a rookie, held by Keegan Murray at 206, by a margin of 59. That is not a record he narrowly broke. He shattered it by a full 28 percent. He did it while shooting 43 percent from three on 7.9 attempts per game, adding 18.8 points, 5.4 rebounds and 3.4 assists per night.

Knueppel's Hornets are still alive in the Play-In Tournament, facing the Miami Heat on Tuesday in an elimination game. The rookie record-breaker may well still be adding to his totals this week.

Coach of the Year: Mark Daigneault

The Oklahoma City Thunder head coach guided his team to the NBA's best record in one of the quietest dominant seasons in recent memory. Daigneault has developed SGA into a perennial MVP, built a deep roster that plays at both ends, and kept OKC consistently elite through the grind of a full 82-game season. He is the frontrunner and the deserving choice.

What's Next

Award announcements are expected to begin around late April, with the first major awards typically unveiled in the third week of the playoffs. The Most Valuable Player announcement traditionally comes last — likely in May — giving SGA one more moment in the spotlight after what has been a brilliant individual season. In the meantime, several of these award favorites are still playing basketball. Wemby's Spurs await a first-round opponent. Knueppel's Hornets face elimination on Tuesday. The hardware can wait — the hardwood comes first.
