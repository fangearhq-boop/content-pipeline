Kawhi vs. Curry: Clippers-Warriors Play-In Is the Best Game Nobody Is Talking About

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 13, 2026

Everyone is talking about Joel Embiid's absence from the 76ers-Magic game. Nobody seems to be paying enough attention to the Western Conference Play-In matchup happening at the same time — and that is a mistake.

Wednesday night at 10 PM ET from Intuit Dome in Los Angeles, the No. 9 Clippers host the No. 10 Golden State Warriors in a win-or-go-home showdown. Kawhi Leonard's career-best season meets Stephen Curry's depleted Warriors in what could be one of the most compelling single-elimination games in Play-In history.

The Kawhi Leonard Renaissance

The basketball world has spent years speculating about whether Kawhi Leonard was still capable of being a top-five player. This season settled the debate — loudly.

Leonard is averaging a career-high 27.9 points per game. Not a career high for a healthy season. Not a career high excluding injury years. A career-high period, surpassing his previous best and doing it at 34 years old. He is an offensive force night after night, combining his trademark mid-range mastery with improved creation off the dribble, and the results have been stunning.

His supporting cast has evolved too. The Clippers acquired Darius Garland from the Cleveland Cavaliers in a midseason trade, and the move transformed their offense. Garland is contributing 20.2 points and 6.7 assists per game in Los Angeles — giving Leonard the kind of secondary playmaking that takes pressure off his every possession. The Clippers began this season 6-21 and in deep trouble. They are now one game away from extending their year.

The Warriors Are Limping In

The Golden State Warriors have one of the great stories in NBA history. But this season has tested even the most loyal fans.

Stephen Curry is still doing his part. He's averaging 26.6 points, 3.5 rebounds and 4.8 assists this season — a season that any other player would proudly call his best. But the Warriors around him have crumbled. Jimmy Butler, who came to Golden State hoping to chase a ring, is out for the entire postseason with a knee injury. Moses Moody is also sidelined. The Warriors have been essentially locked into the No. 10 seed for weeks, playing games that mattered only in terms of survival.

Curry has carried this team as far as he can. Wednesday night, he'll need everyone still standing to play the game of their lives.

The Season Series Favors Los Angeles

The regular-season history between these two teams tells one story: the Clippers have the Warriors' number this year. Los Angeles won three of four regular-season matchups, including a decisive 115-110 victory over Golden State in the final game of the regular season this past Sunday. The Clippers are at home and arrive with momentum and a clear tactical advantage based on how the season series played out.

Golden State did show competitive fire in a come-from-behind 103-102 win earlier in the year, but that result required both a Kerr ejection and a late Curry foul-out to go their way. Clean results favor the Clippers.

What's Next

If the Clippers win Wednesday, they advance to the playoffs as the No. 8 seed in the West and face the No. 1 Oklahoma City Thunder in the first round. An underdog role would suit Kawhi's current form — he has always performed his best when the pressure is highest. If the Warriors pull the upset, they would also advance to face OKC. Either way, the winner earns the right to keep playing in the most difficult conference in basketball. The loser heads home. Wednesday night at 10 PM ET on Prime Video, one dynasty ends and another writes its next chapter.
