2026 NBA Playoff Bracket Is Set: Nuggets Clinch No. 3, Full First-Round Matchups Confirmed

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 13, 2026

The 2026 NBA regular season is officially in the books — and the playoff bracket is locked. Sunday's final day of action delivered the last pieces of the seeding puzzle, highlighted by the Denver Nuggets clinching the No. 3 seed in the Western Conference with a 128-118 win over the San Antonio Spurs, sending the Los Angeles Lakers to the No. 4 spot.

With the Play-In Tournament set to determine the final No. 7 and No. 8 seeds in each conference, here is a full breakdown of the bracket and the first-round matchups you need to circle on your calendar.

Western Conference Bracket

The Oklahoma City Thunder enter the postseason as the team with the NBA's best record, led by back-to-back MVP favorite Shai Gilgeous-Alexander. They'll await the winner of the Western Conference Play-In for their first-round opponent.

The San Antonio Spurs secured the No. 2 seed with a 62-20 record — the best in franchise history in a very long time — and will await the seventh-seeded Play-In winner for their first-round series. Victor Wembanyama, 22 years old and preparing for his first NBA Playoff run, gives San Antonio a weapon no opponent wants to face.

The Denver Nuggets, the defending Western Conference regulars, clinched No. 3 in the most fitting way possible — an efficient 128-118 win over a Spurs team resting its stars on the final day. Denver, at 54-28, earned a reward that comes with teeth: a first-round matchup with the No. 6 seed Minnesota Timberwolves. The Wolves are no pushover, having reached the Western Conference Finals just two seasons ago.

The No. 4 Los Angeles Lakers, finishing at 53-29, face the No. 5 Houston Rockets in the first round. Kevin Durant leads a Rockets squad that has been one of the best stories of the second half — and LeBron James will be challenged immediately in a matchup with KD.

Eastern Conference Bracket

The Detroit Pistons claimed the No. 1 East seed in one of the most remarkable turnarounds in modern NBA history — just two years removed from a franchise-worst 14-68 season. Cade Cunningham leads a young, hungry team that will host a Play-In winner in the first round and play every game with the confidence of a team that has already exceeded every expectation.

The Boston Celtics hold the No. 2 seed and will also await a Play-In opponent. Boston has the experience and depth to make a deep run, and the Eastern Conference title feels very much within reach for Brad Stevens' squad.

The No. 3 New York Knicks host the No. 6 Toronto Raptors in the first round — a matchup with early-round drama potential. Jalen Brunson and company have been one of the Eastern Conference's most consistent teams, and Madison Square Garden will be rocking for every home game.

The No. 4 Cleveland Cavaliers take on the No. 5 Atlanta Hawks. Donovan Mitchell and the Cavs have legitimate title aspirations, and the Hawks — led by Trae Young — are built to push the pace and create chaos.

What the Seedings Mean

The four seedings decided in the Play-In (No. 7 and No. 8 in each conference) will complete the bracket picture by Friday, April 17. The No. 1 seeds — OKC and Detroit — get the benefit of facing those teams first. But in a post-Play-In world, No. 7 and No. 8 seeds are not pushovers. Teams like the Clippers (if they survive) would arrive in the playoff bracket red-hot and with nothing to lose.

What's Next

The NBA Playoffs officially begin on Saturday, April 18. Non-Play-In first-round series will tip off across Saturday and Sunday, while the Play-In No. 7/8 seeds begin play as early as Monday, April 20 after the tournament concludes April 17. The race for the 2026 NBA championship starts in less than a week — and with two compelling conference favorites and a loaded Play-In field, this postseason is already shaping up to be one for the ages.
