Spurs Back in the Playoffs at 62-20 — And Wembanyama's First Postseason Run Begins April 18

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 13, 2026

The San Antonio Spurs are back in the NBA Playoffs for the first time since 2019 — and they didn't sneak in. They earned it, emphatically, with a 62-20 final record that stands as the No. 2 seed in the Western Conference and includes the Spurs' first Southwest Division title since 2017.

For a franchise that endured a painful rebuild after the Gregg Popovich dynasty era concluded, this return is significant. But what makes it historically special is what it represents for the player leading the charge: 22-year-old Victor Wembanyama, preparing to make his first-ever NBA Playoff appearance.

A Season of Historic Dominance

Wembanyama sat out Sunday's regular-season finale against the Denver Nuggets, protecting a sore left ribcage that had nagged him late in the year. It was the right call — he had already done everything he needed to do. On April 11, appearing in his 65th game against the Dallas Mavericks, he scored 40 points in just over 26 minutes and secured the minimum game requirements for NBA award eligibility. Defensive Player of the Year and, for some voters, an outside MVP shot are both on the table.

His statistical dominance this season has been staggering. Wembanyama leads the entire NBA in blocks at 3.1 per game. He is a seven-foot-four center who closes out on shooters like a guard, contests every interior attempt, and anchors a Spurs defensive system that finished among the best in basketball. At 22, he is doing things that players a decade older have never accomplished.

The Road Ahead

As the No. 2 West seed, the Spurs will face the winner of the Suns-Blazers Play-In game on Tuesday, April 14. Either Phoenix or Portland will earn the No. 7 seed and set up a first-round matchup with San Antonio starting April 18.

Neither the Suns nor the Blazers should strike fear into San Antonio's locker room. The Spurs have home-court advantage and a roster depth that few teams in the conference can match. Their 62-20 record is not the product of luck or scheduling — it is the result of a team that has learned to play together and win in multiple ways.

And at the center of it all is Wembanyama, walking into the playoffs for the first time. The French phenom has spent two professional seasons proving he belongs among the elite. Now he gets to prove it in the environment where legends are made.

What's Next

The Suns-Blazers Play-In game takes place Tuesday, April 14, at 10 PM ET on Prime Video. The winner becomes San Antonio's first-round opponent, with the series beginning Saturday, April 18. If the Spurs advance through the first round — something their talent and depth makes very plausible — they could face the Oklahoma City Thunder in the second round, setting up one of the most compelling potential matchups in this postseason: SGA vs. Wemby, in a series that would settle, at least temporarily, the question of who is the best young player in the NBA.
