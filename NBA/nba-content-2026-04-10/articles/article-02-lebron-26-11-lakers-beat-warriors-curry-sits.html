LeBron's 26 and 11 Carries Lakers Past Warriors 119-103 as Curry Sits Out

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 10, 2026

LeBron James sent a message to the rest of the Western Conference on Thursday night, and he sent it without his co-star by his side.

With Luka Doncic sidelined for the remainder of the season due to a Grade 2 hamstring strain, James carried the Los Angeles Lakers to a 119-103 win over the Golden State Warriors at Chase Center. He finished with 26 points on 11-of-17 shooting (including three three-pointers), 11 assists, and 8 rebounds — a complete, vintage LeBron performance that left little doubt about his readiness for the postseason.

Stephen Curry did not play Thursday. Golden State rested him due to right knee injury management, a decision that also carried symbolic weight: Thursday was the Warriors' final home game of the regular season, and the organization chose to shield its franchise player from any unnecessary risk.

LeBron in Command Without Luka

The concern entering the night was valid. Without Doncic, the Lakers were leaning entirely on James — a 41-year-old leading scorer carrying a playoff team's offense in the regular season's final week. Thursday put those concerns to rest, at least temporarily.

James controlled tempo, manufactured easy looks for teammates, and attacked the Warriors' defense at will. His 11-of-17 shooting line included shots from all three zones — mid-range jumpers, pull-up threes, and finishes at the rim. It was not just efficient; it was authoritative.

The win keeps the Lakers at 51-28 and locked into the No. 4 seed in the Western Conference. Whether Doncic is available for the playoffs remains the central question around this team, but LeBron's Thursday performance offers a compelling alternate scenario: a James-led Lakers team that enters April 18 playing its best basketball.

Warriors Playing for April 14

Golden State's decision to rest Curry underscores the reality of their situation. The Warriors (38-42) are locked into the No. 10 seed in the Western Conference play-in tournament. They will need to win two consecutive play-in games starting April 14 just to reach the first round of the NBA Playoffs.

Thursday marked the final regular-season home game at Chase Center. The atmosphere carried the kind of wistfulness that comes with uncertainty — no one knows if this is the last time this roster plays together in San Francisco before a summer of potential roster changes. The regular season will conclude Sunday, April 12, with the Warriors' last road game before the play-in.

A Season Without a Curry-LeBron Showdown

Thursday's result means the two all-time greats never shared a healthy, competitive regular-season matchup this season. Curry missed extended time with a right knee injury, and with LeBron the focal point in Thursday's win without Doncic while Curry rested, their paths never properly crossed. It is a minor footnote to an otherwise packed regular season, but it is worth noting for the history books.

What's Next

The Los Angeles Lakers have two regular-season games remaining before the season closes April 12. They are locked into the No. 4 seed in the West and are projected to face the No. 5 Houston Rockets in the first round of the NBA Playoffs beginning April 18. The Golden State Warriors, meanwhile, enter the Western Conference Play-In Tournament on April 14 as the No. 10 seed, needing two wins to reach the postseason's real bracket.
