SGA's Second MVP Is Two Games Away — and Wemby's Award Eligibility Hangs in the Balance

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 10, 2026

The 2026 NBA Most Valuable Player race is over in everything but name. Two regular-season games remain, and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is the most overwhelming MVP favorite the league has seen in years.

ESPN's straw poll of 100 media members handed SGA 88 first-place votes. DraftKings lists his odds at -400 — the kind of number that signals a done deal, not a horse race. Gilgeous-Alexander has averaged 31.6 points per game on 55.3% shooting, with 4.4 rebounds, 6.5 assists, and 1.4 steals across Oklahoma City's remarkable season. The Thunder finished with the NBA's best record for the second consecutive year, and SGA's fingerprints are on every win.

The Stats That Sealed It

The case for SGA starts with volume and efficiency existing in the same sentence. Averaging 31.6 points at 55.3% from the floor is historically rare — it places him among the most efficient high-volume scorers in the modern era. Add six-plus assists per game and nearly 1.5 steals, and you have a player who is dominant on both ends of the floor.

What has pushed SGA past the competition is context. Oklahoma City will close the regular season among the top two teams in the league by record. The Thunder are the defending champions, and SGA's two-way excellence has been the engine behind another potential title run. Voters tend to reward the player who most lifted his team, and by that standard, there is no close second.

Wembanyama's Award Eligibility Drama

The only drama remaining in the awards race surrounds Victor Wembanyama. The San Antonio Spurs center — who averages 24.9 points, 11.6 rebounds, and 3.1 assists per game, and leads the league in blocks for the third straight season — is day-to-day with a rib contusion he suffered in the Spurs' April 7 game.

NBA eligibility rules require players to appear in a minimum number of games or minutes to qualify for major awards. Wembanyama must play 20 or more minutes in at least one of the Spurs' final two regular-season games to preserve his eligibility for MVP, Defensive Player of the Year, and All-NBA team considerations.

The Spurs received encouraging news when Wembanyama's MRI came back clear following the rib injury. He is listed as day-to-day, and the organization has indicated he will play if medically cleared. San Antonio (61-19) is locked into the No. 2 seed in the West regardless of results; the games have stakes for Wemby personally, but not for the team's playoff positioning.

The Historical Significance

A second consecutive MVP for SGA would cement his place among the game's all-time greats. Back-to-back MVP awards are rare; they have been earned by a short list of players including Bill Russell, Wilt Chamberlain, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Moses Malone, Larry Bird, Magic Johnson, Michael Jordan, LeBron James, and Nikola Jokic. SGA would join that company at age 27.

The Thunder's dynasty narrative is just beginning. Oklahoma City is the defending champion, has the league's best record, and is headlined by a back-to-back MVP in his prime. The rest of the league had two seasons to slow them down. The regular season ends April 12, and then the real basketball starts.

What's Next

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander and the Oklahoma City Thunder have two regular-season games remaining before April 12. SGA's MVP award will be officially announced during the NBA's awards cycle following the postseason, but his second straight trophy is a formality. The spotlight now shifts to Victor Wembanyama's health and whether he can get on the floor long enough to maintain his award eligibility heading into what should be a deep Spurs playoff run starting April 18.
