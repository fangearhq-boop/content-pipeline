Knicks Beat Celtics 112-106: Hart 26, Brunson 25/10, No. 3 Seed Within Reach

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 10, 2026

The New York Knicks are one win away from locking up the No. 3 seed in the Eastern Conference, and Josh Hart made absolutely sure of it Thursday night at Madison Square Garden.

Hart erupted for 26 points — 15 of them in a pivotal fourth quarter — as New York defeated the Boston Celtics 112-106. Jalen Brunson orchestrated the win with 25 points and 10 assists, and all five Knicks starters reached double figures in a performance that underlined just how dangerous this team can be heading into the playoffs.

The Celtics entered the game without Jaylen Brown, who was held out due to left Achilles tendinitis. Boston is safely locked into the No. 2 seed at 54-26 and was clearly protecting its best player with the playoffs two weeks away. The Knicks (52-28) had no such luxury — this game meant everything for their seeding, and they rose to the moment.

Hart Takes Over When It Mattered

The game was tight through three quarters before Hart took it over. His fourth-quarter outburst was the kind of performance that defines a postseason run — physical, relentless, and executed under pressure. While Brunson did what Brunson does (slice through defenses, create for teammates, and knock down critical shots), Hart provided the hammer blow that closed it out.

The Knicks' depth showed throughout the contest. New York didn't need a hero from the bench — the starting five handled it. Every starter in double figures means the Celtics couldn't key on any single player, and Boston's defense, already diminished without Brown directing plays at the perimeter, never found a clean answer.

Seeding Implications

The win moves the Knicks to 52-28, one full game ahead of the Cleveland Cavaliers (51-29) for the No. 3 seed in the East. Crucially, New York holds the head-to-head tiebreaker over Cleveland, meaning a single Knicks win in their final two games — or a single Cavaliers loss — officially seals the slot.

The difference between No. 3 and No. 4 is meaningful. The No. 3 seed earns home-court advantage in the first round and a more favorable projected path through the bracket. With the postseason starting April 18, Knicks fans have every reason to be counting wins rather than calendar days.

Celtics Protecting Brown

Boston's decision to rest Jaylen Brown was entirely understandable, even if it drew some boos from the Garden crowd. Brown has been battling Achilles tendinitis, and the Celtics — locked into their seed — have nothing to gain by playing him through pain in a meaningless regular-season game. The Celtics are a legitimate title contender, and keeping Brown healthy through a deep playoff run is more valuable than any regular-season positioning.

For the Knicks, the result counts the same regardless. A win is a win, and this one carries weight.

What's Next

The New York Knicks have two games remaining before the regular season closes Sunday, April 12. One more win clinches the No. 3 seed in the Eastern Conference. Following the regular season, the NBA Play-In Tournament runs April 14-17, with the first round of the playoffs tipping off April 18. The Knicks — with Brunson healthy and Hart ready to explode — will be a nightmare matchup for anyone they face.
