Six Teams, Two Spots: The East's Play-In Race Comes Down to the Wire

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 10, 2026

With two to three games remaining in the 2025-26 NBA regular season, the Eastern Conference play-in race is the tightest it has been all year — and the stakes could not be higher.

Six teams are battling for two precious spots. The Atlanta Hawks (45-35) and Toronto Raptors (44-35) lead the pack at No. 5 and No. 6, but they hold those positions by the thinnest of margins. Behind them: the Orlando Magic (44-36), Philadelphia 76ers (43-36), Charlotte Hornets (43-37), and Miami Heat (41-38). Only 2.5 games separate fifth from ninth. The regular season ends Sunday, April 12.

Teams finishing fifth and sixth earn direct seeds in the first round of the NBA Playoffs beginning April 18. Teams finishing seventh through tenth must survive the play-in tournament (April 14-17), where they have to win at least one of two games to advance. For teams already playing undermanned rosters, the extra games represent both risk and opportunity.

The Leaders: Hawks and Raptors

Atlanta and Toronto each control their own destinies. The Hawks sit at 45-35, and with two to three games remaining, they have enough cushion to absorb a loss and still likely hold the No. 5 seed. Their concern is staying focused through a season-ending stretch without letting the pack close the gap.

Toronto at 44-35 is in a similar position at No. 6, though the margin is slimmer. The Raptors' final push will determine whether they face a first-round series starting fresh or have to grind through a play-in game first. Both teams carry the head-to-head tiebreakers and game-by-game results that will decide final positioning if records end in a tie.

The Chasers: Magic, Sixers, Hornets, Heat

Orlando (44-36) is the most dangerous threat to jump into the top six. The Magic have won four straight games entering this week and are playing their best basketball of the second half. A strong final weekend could realistically push them past one of the leaders.

Philadelphia (43-36) and Charlotte (43-37) are mathematically alive but will need help. The Sixers have enough talent to win in critical moments; the Hornets, led by Knueppel's record-breaking shooting, have surprised throughout the season. Miami (41-38) faces the longest path, but the Heat have repeatedly shown the ability to perform well when the pressure is highest — their playoff pedigree demands respect even at No. 10.

What the Seeds Actually Mean

The difference between No. 5 and No. 7 is not just two spots on a page. It is the difference between entering the playoffs rested versus grinding through a play-in game, and it is the difference in first-round opponents. Teams seeded fifth and sixth are projected to face opponents who came through the play-in tournament — opponents who may be momentum-hot or significantly depleted from extra games. That cuts both ways.

The bottom line: every game between now and Sunday counts double for these six franchises.

What's Next

The NBA regular season concludes Sunday, April 12, and the Eastern Conference's final seeding picture will emerge that night. The Play-In Tournament runs April 14-17, with teams seeded 7 through 10 competing for the final two playoff spots in each conference. The first round of the NBA Playoffs begins Saturday, April 18. For the Hawks and Raptors, every game this weekend is a chance to close the door on the field behind them — and for the Magic, Sixers, Hornets, and Heat, it is a final opportunity to force the issue.
