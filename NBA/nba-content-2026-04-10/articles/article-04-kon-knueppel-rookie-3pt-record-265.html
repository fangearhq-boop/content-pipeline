Kon Knueppel Obliterates NBA Rookie Three-Point Record With 265 Makes and Counting

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 10, 2026

Charlotte Hornets rookie Kon Knueppel did not just break the NBA record for three-pointers made by a first-year player. He shattered it so completely that it may stand for a generation.

Knueppel enters the final two games of the regular season with 265 made three-pointers, demolishing the previous rookie record of 206 set by Keegan Murray of the Sacramento Kings in the 2022-23 season. That is a margin of 59 additional threes — not a squeaker, not a close call, but an obliteration. Knueppel is also converting at a 43% clip on 7.9 attempts per game, an efficiency rate that would be elite for any player in the league regardless of experience level.

Record-Breaking Numbers

Knueppel reached the franchise record first, surpassing Kemba Walker's all-time Charlotte Hornets record for three-pointers in a season on April 2. Then came the league-wide rookie record. Then the gap between his total and the old mark grew to numbers that seem almost implausible in hindsight.

The trajectory of his season tells the story. The No. 4 overall pick in the 2025 NBA Draft entered the season as a projected contributor, a college shooter expected to need time to adjust to the NBA's speed and length. Instead, he thrived from opening night and kept accelerating. His 3.4 three-point makes per game are not the result of volume shooting — they are the result of volume shooting married to accuracy, which is a far rarer combination.

Rookie of the Year Race

Knueppel's record-setting season has earned him the No. 1 spot on the NBA's official Kia Rookie Ladder in its final edition of the season, published April 8. He edges Dallas Mavericks forward Cooper Flagg, who made headlines earlier in the season by becoming the first teenager in NBA history to score 50 or more points in a game.

Flagg's ceiling is arguably higher, and the individual brilliance of his 51-point performance for Dallas gave him an extraordinary highlight. But Knueppel's consistency — game after game of high-volume, high-efficiency three-point shooting — represents a different kind of rookie excellence. The Rookie of the Year award will come down to voter preference between explosive ceiling moments and sustained statistical dominance.

Charlotte's Silver Lining

The Hornets missed the playoffs this season, but their fans have Knueppel's historic campaign to celebrate. His season is already being discussed as one of the most impressive rookie shooting performances in NBA history. At 43% from three on 7.9 attempts, he did not just break a record — he rewrote expectations for what a first-year player can accomplish from beyond the arc.

With two games remaining and the record already obliterated, Knueppel is playing for additional milestones. Every three-pointer he makes pushes the new record further out of reach for whoever might chase it next.

What's Next

Kon Knueppel and the Charlotte Hornets play their final two regular-season games before the season concludes April 12. The Hornets have been eliminated from playoff contention but remain in the East's play-in picture at 43-37 — two wins and favorable results elsewhere could pull them into the 9-10 play-in seeding. For Knueppel personally, the regular season finale offers a final opportunity to add to his record total. The Rookie of the Year award will be announced during the NBA's postseason awards cycle.
