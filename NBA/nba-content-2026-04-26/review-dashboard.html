### Long-Form Post Window: 9:00 AM ET Shai Gilgeous-Alexander put on the kind of performance that defines a player's legend. The Thunder guard finished with a playoff career-high 42 points Saturday afternoon, shooting 15-of-18 from the field and 11-of-12 from the free throw line to go with eight assists, as Oklahoma City demolished the Phoenix Suns 121-109 and took a commanding 3-0 series lead. The efficiency alone made history. SGA became only the second player in NBA playoff history to score 40 or more points while shooting at least 83.3 percent from the field — joining Terry Porter. It wasn't just an individual performance; it was a systematic demolition. The Thunder, already playing without Jalen Williams (left hamstring), did not need him against a Suns team that had no answer for SGA's relentless pressure. Phoenix now faces elimination in Game 4. No team in NBA history has come back from a 3-0 deficit to win a playoff series. ### Long-Form Post Window: 11:00 AM ET Ayo Dosunmu was not supposed to be the hero. With Anthony Edwards sidelined by a knee injury and Donte DiVincenzo also unavailable, the Minnesota Timberwolves needed someone to step into the void against the defending champion Denver Nuggets in Game 4. What they got was one of the most remarkable reserve performances in playoff history. Dosunmu scored 43 points off the bench — shooting 13-of-17 from the field, going a perfect 5-of-5 from beyond the arc and 12-of-12 from the free throw line in 42 minutes. According to NBA records, it is the highest-scoring playoff game by a reserve in 50 years, since Fred Brown poured in 45 for Seattle against Phoenix on April 15, 1976. Minnesota won 112-96 and now leads 3-1, placing Denver one loss from an early exit. Naz Reid supported with 17 points and 9 rebounds, but this was Dosunmu's night. The Nuggets, who have leaned on Nikola Jokic all season, could not manufacture enough offense to keep pace. Denver must now win three straight — starting with Game 5 — to stay alive. #HoopHeroes | #NBAPlayoffs
