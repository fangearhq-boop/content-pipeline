Banchero and Bane Each Score 25 as Magic Hold Off Pistons 113-105, Take 2-1 Series Lead

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 26, 2026

The Orlando Magic have spent the entire season navigating adversity, and they did it again Saturday morning. Paolo Banchero and Desmond Bane each scored 25 points as Orlando held off the Detroit Pistons 113-105 in Game 3 to take a 2-1 series lead — surviving a late push by the Pistons to secure the upper hand in a matchup between two of the Eastern Conference's most exciting young teams.

Detroit came into the game looking to take the series lead after splitting the first two games in Orlando. The Pistons pushed hard in the final minutes and made it a genuine contest, but the Magic made the plays they needed to close it out. Banchero, the 2022 No. 1 overall pick who has developed into a clear franchise cornerstone, delivered in a pressurized moment. Bane, playing with the poise of a veteran, provided exactly the secondary scoring punch Orlando needed alongside him.

Banchero and Bane: A Formidable Pairing

The combined 50-point effort from Banchero and Bane illustrated why the Magic have been such a difficult team to game-plan against. Banchero creates his own shot — from the post, off the dribble, off ball screens — in ways that defenses cannot simply scheme away. Bane stretches the floor and punishes any attention that concentrates on Banchero. When both are scoring simultaneously, Orlando becomes very hard to beat.

Saturday was proof of that formula working under pressure. Detroit needed a win on the road to swing momentum. The Magic did not give it to them, relying on their two best scorers to come through when the game was in the balance. It was exactly the kind of gut-check performance a young playoff team needs in its development.

Pistons Must Respond

Cade Cunningham and the Pistons have shown they can compete in this series — their Game 2 victory was convincing, and Detroit is a team built on talent and hunger. But falling behind 2-1 adds urgency to Game 4. The Pistons cannot afford to let Orlando go up 3-1 without facing a near-impossible climb to advance.

Detroit's path forward involves finding more consistent offense beyond Cade and generating the defensive pressure it takes to slow Banchero and Bane simultaneously — a task that has so far proven elusive.

What's Next

Game 4 is Monday in Orlando, where the Magic will have the chance to push the series to 3-1 and put the Pistons in a desperate situation. If Orlando wins, it would mark a significant moment in the Magic's development as a legitimate playoff contender in the Eastern Conference. For Detroit, Monday represents an opportunity to prove the series is far from over.
