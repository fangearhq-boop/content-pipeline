KAT's Triple-Double Carries Knicks to 114-98 Win in Atlanta, Evens Series 2-2

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 26, 2026

The New York Knicks came to Atlanta looking for a response. They found one — a decisive, physical, unambiguous statement that removed all doubt about whether McCollum's Game 3 buzzer-beater had shaken them. Karl-Anthony Towns recorded a triple-double and OG Anunoby dominated on both ends as New York dominated the Atlanta Hawks 114-98 and evened the first-round series at 2-2.

Towns finished with 20 points, 10 rebounds, and 10 assists — a triple-double that reflected how the Knicks played collectively. He was not just a scorer; he was the engine of the offense, the hub through whom New York's movement and spacing flowed. Anunoby added 22 points and 10 rebounds, the kind of two-way performance that makes him one of the most valuable players on either team in this series.

The Knicks came in with a purpose and a plan, and it worked from the first quarter. The physicality that head coach Tom Thibodeau demands was present from the opening tip. New York brought intensity and didn't let it waver, turning what could have been a fragile moment into a dominant road statement.

Defense as the Foundation

What most distinguished this Knicks performance was their defensive engagement. After allowing McCollum's dramatic game-winner in Game 3, New York came into State Farm Arena and suffocated the Hawks. Atlanta could not generate clean looks, could not find rhythm, and could not put together the kind of run that had defined their approach in the first three games.

New York's size and switching ability created problems all night. When the Hawks tried to attack the paint, Anunoby and Towns were waiting. When they tried to create off the dribble from the perimeter, the Knicks' perimeter defenders stayed attached. The 16-point margin was a direct product of commitment on that end, not a gift from Atlanta's miscues.

The Knicks have now won three of the five playoff series games they have played since McCollum's Game 2 winner last week — their resilience a credit to the culture that Thibodeau has built in New York.

Hawks Must Regroup

Atlanta entered Game 4 with a chance to go up 3-1 and effectively end the series. Instead, they are now back to even with the Knicks holding some measure of momentum. Jalen Johnson and the Hawks will need to rediscover the offensive creativity that powered their wins in Games 1 and 3 if they want to steal a Game 5 in New York.

What's Next

Game 5 heads to Madison Square Garden, where the Knicks will have the benefit of a home crowd that has been passionate and loud throughout the playoffs. A win there puts New York one game from advancing. For Atlanta, the challenge is flipping momentum in one of the loudest arenas in basketball — not an easy task, but one this young Hawks team has shown it isn't afraid of.
