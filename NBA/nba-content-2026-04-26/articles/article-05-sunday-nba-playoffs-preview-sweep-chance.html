Sunday NBA Playoffs Preview: Lakers Can Sweep, Three Series at Pivotal Moments

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 26, 2026

Sunday's NBA playoff schedule delivers the most consequential day of the first round. Four games across two networks, four series in varying states of urgency, and one overriding storyline: LeBron James and the Los Angeles Lakers can complete a first-round sweep of the Houston Rockets at 9:30 p.m. ET on NBC/Peacock — capping a postseason opener that has been all about age-defying brilliance and historic moments.

Before LeBron gets his moment, three other series offer their own drama: the Cavaliers visit Toronto with a chance to go up 3-1 in the early afternoon, the Spurs and Blazers play Game 4 in Portland with the lingering question of Victor Wembanyama's health, and the Celtics head to Philadelphia looking to extend their 2-1 series lead with Joel Embiid still absent from the Sixers' lineup.

The Sweep Attempt: Lakers at Rockets, 9:30 PM ET

LeBron has been the dominant story of the first round. He hit a tying 3-pointer with 13 seconds left in Game 3's regulation, then watched Marcus Smart take over overtime in a remarkable 112-108 Lakers victory. Los Angeles leads 3-0, and Kevin Durant remains out with a left ankle sprain, leaving the Rockets without their best weapon for a third consecutive game.

No team has ever recovered from a 3-0 deficit in an NBA playoff series. That history looms over Houston's Sunday night in a way that makes the game feel more like a sendoff than a contest. The Lakers, should they complete the sweep, would earn extended rest before the second round — a significant benefit for a team managing LeBron's workload deep into a long career.

The Other Three Games

At 1 p.m. ET, Cleveland and Toronto play Game 4 in the Cavaliers' series with the Cavaliers holding a 2-1 lead. The Raptors have shown they can compete, but Cleveland's depth has proven difficult to match through three games. A 3-1 lead would give the Cavs enormous breathing room.

At 3:30 p.m. ET, San Antonio visits Portland for Game 4 with the Spurs ahead 2-1. The pressing question: Is Victor Wembanyama healthy enough to play? He entered concussion protocol after Game 2 and missed Game 3, but the Spurs staged a stunning comeback anyway — with Stephon Castle and Dylan Harper combining for 60 points. His return would reshape this series dramatically.

At 7 p.m. ET, Boston and Philadelphia play Game 4 with the Celtics leading 2-1. Embiid's absence from appendectomy surgery continues to define Philadelphia's ceiling in this series. Tyrese Maxey is fighting to keep the Sixers alive, but without their center, the path forward is narrow.

What's Next

By Sunday night, the first round picture will be significantly clearer. A Lakers sweep would be the biggest outcome, but a Wembanyama return in Portland or a Sixers comeback in Philadelphia would each add their own layer to what has already been a compelling start to the 2026 NBA playoffs. Four games. Four chances for something memorable.
