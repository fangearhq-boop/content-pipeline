SGA Scores Playoff Career-High 42 as Thunder Rout Suns 121-109, Take 3-0 Series Lead

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 26, 2026

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander does not perform under pressure — he thrives in it. The Oklahoma City Thunder point guard delivered the most dominant playoff performance of his career Saturday afternoon, scoring 42 points on 15-of-18 shooting with 11-of-12 free throws and eight assists as OKC destroyed the Phoenix Suns 121-109 and pushed the first-round series to a 3-0 lead.

It was not just a career playoff high. The efficiency made history. SGA became only the second player in NBA postseason history to score 40 or more points while shooting better than 83 percent from the field, joining Terry Porter in that exclusive company. From the moment he touched the ball in Game 3, it was clear the Suns had no answer. He attacked off the dribble, hit from the mid-range, and converted at the line with ice-cold consistency.

Oklahoma City won without Jalen Williams, their second-best player, who has been sidelined all series with a left hamstring injury. The Thunder did not need him Saturday. SGA was enough — and then some.

A Performance for the Record Books

What separated SGA's Game 3 from a typical great playoff performance was the shot selection — or rather, the lack of bad shots. Shooting 15-of-18, he barely missed, and none of the makes were fortunate. He created his own offense with methodical pick-and-roll attacks, used hesitation moves to carve open space against the Phoenix defense, and repeatedly drew fouls on drives to the basket, finishing 11-of-12 from the line.

He added eight assists, meaning his fingerprints were on virtually every Oklahoma City possession. The Thunder offense flowed through him with a precision that spoke to how comfortable SGA has become running a team in the postseason — not just scoring, but dictating pace, reading the defense, and making the pass that produces the bucket.

The Suns, meanwhile, had no way to contain him without fouling or conceding. Phoenix threw multiple defenders at him and it barely mattered. The final margin of 12 points does not fully capture how complete the Thunder's control was for most of the game.

Suns Face the Impossible Climb

Phoenix now faces elimination in Game 4, and history offers no comfort. No team in NBA history has ever overcome a 3-0 series deficit to win a playoff series. The Suns will need to produce four consecutive victories — beginning with a win at home — to do what has never been done.

What makes their situation more difficult is that the Thunder are playing their best basketball without Jalen Williams. OKC's supporting cast has filled in admirably, and SGA is playing at an MVP level that even the most optimistic Thunder supporter could not have scripted before the series. Phoenix must now decide whether to fully commit to a comeback or begin looking at the offseason.

What's Next

Game 4 is Monday in Phoenix, where the Suns will try to avoid becoming the latest team swept by Shai Gilgeous-Alexander and the Thunder. For Oklahoma City, the question shifts to who they might see in the second round and whether Williams can return to health before that series begins. If SGA continues at this level — and nothing about Saturday suggests he will slow down — the Thunder are one of the most dangerous teams remaining in the Western Conference playoffs.
