Celtics Now Emerge as Serious Rival to Heat in Race for Giannis With 11 Days to Draft

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 12, 2026

The Giannis Antetokounmpo trade situation just became considerably more complicated. For months this appeared to be a straightforward story: Giannis wanted to go to Miami, the Heat were willing to deal, and Milwaukee was ready to move on. Then Friday happened.

New reports indicate the Boston Celtics have had a significant change of heart. Bill Simmons of The Ringer reported on his podcast that Boston is now "in on Giannis," a characterization that lines up with additional reporting suggesting Giannis has been actively comparing the Heat and Celtics rosters. Eleven days remain before the June 23 NBA Draft — the unofficial deadline set by Bucks owner Jimmy Haslam for a resolution.

Heat Still Lead — But Boston Is Now Real

Miami retains the pole position. Multiple sources close to the process have described the Heat as the top item on Giannis's wish list, and the Heat have been assembling package components for weeks. The two-time MVP's desire to play for a franchise built to compete in a warm-weather city with a championship culture is well-documented.

But the Celtics have not been ruled out. Reports indicate that a move to Boston remains "in his thoughts" — a meaningful phrase, given how closely these situations are parsed when NBA insiders choose their words. The Celtics' championship infrastructure, defensive identity, and roster depth make them an appealing destination for a player of Giannis's caliber.

The Athletic's Sam Amick offered a dissenting view Friday, saying there are "no strong signs at all" that Boston is actively pursuing a deal. The conflicting reports are a sign of genuine uncertainty — even those closest to the situation disagree on where this is heading.

Nets Emerge as a Dark Horse

The Brooklyn Nets have surfaced as a surprising third party in this trade drama. In rebuilding mode and flush with draft capital, the Nets could offer Milwaukee a compelling picks-and-players package that includes pieces like Nic Claxton. Whether Giannis has any interest in Brooklyn remains unclear, but the Nets' ability to construct an attractive offer for the Bucks keeps them relevant in any three-team scenario.

Why Milwaukee Is Ready to Move

The Bucks finished the 2025-26 season at 32-50, snapping a nine-year playoff streak. Owner Jimmy Haslam reportedly wants this resolved before the draft — and the NBA Finals outcome has accelerated the timeline. The Bucks were waiting to see how the Finals played out before finalizing their approach; with the Knicks one win away from a title, the calculus has settled.

Giannis also has a $275 million contract extension eligible on October 1. The trade happens before that date, or it becomes substantially more complicated.

What's Next

The next 11 days will be among the most consequential in NBA history. Wherever Giannis lands — Miami, Boston, or somewhere unexpected — the move will reshape the Eastern Conference for years. Watch for developments to accelerate after the Finals conclude Saturday night. The offseason dominoes are about to fall fast.
