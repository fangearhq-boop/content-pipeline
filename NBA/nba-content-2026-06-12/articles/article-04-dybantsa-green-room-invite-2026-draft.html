Dybantsa, Peterson and Boozer Confirmed for Draft Green Room — But No. 1 Pick Still Wide Open

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 12, 2026

The NBA Draft green room at Barclays Center in Brooklyn is taking shape, and the names on the list confirm what draft watchers have expected: AJ Dybantsa, Darryn Peterson, and Cam Boozer have all received invitations to sit in the seats reserved for the first calls on the night of June 23.

The green room invite is a signal from the league — these players expect to hear their names called quickly. But it does nothing to answer the question that has consumed NBA circles for months: who goes No. 1 overall?

Dybantsa's Case for the Top Pick

Dybantsa arrives with the most momentum. The former BYU standout averaged 25.5 points, 6.8 rebounds, and 3.7 assists per game this season while shooting 51.0% from the floor — production that earned him consensus All-American honors. Analysts noted that he is the first player since Larry Bird in 1978-79 to average those numbers and earn that distinction.

His athleticism at the Combine further strengthened his case: a 42-inch vertical leap and a 7-foot-0.5 wingspan for a 6-foot-9 forward. The Washington Wizards, who hold the top pick, have scheduled a pre-draft visit with Dybantsa in the coming days, per David Aldridge and Josh Robbins of The Athletic.

Peterson Remains in the Mix

Yet the Wizards have been deliberately non-committal. Former Kansas guard Darryn Peterson has not been ruled out for the top selection. His green room invitation signals that Washington — and potentially other teams — still views him as a top-tier prospect capable of going in the top handful of picks.

Multiple reports in recent weeks have characterized the No. 1 choice as less settled than it appeared at the start of the draft cycle, when Dybantsa seemed like a near-certain selection. CBS Sports' mock draft currently projects Washington taking Dybantsa, but acknowledges that "debate rages" internally.

Boozer Rising

Cam Boozer, the Connecticut center, rounds out the headline green room group. His inclusion reflects a rising draft stock that has him viewed as a top-five prospect by many boards heading into draft week.

What's Next

The Wizards will meet with prospects in the days ahead, and their pre-draft process is expected to conclude the week before June 23. The first round takes place Tuesday, June 23, with the second round on Wednesday, June 24, both at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. Whatever happens in Game 5 of the NBA Finals on Saturday, the draft conversation will accelerate immediately after. Eleven days. The next franchise cornerstones are about to be revealed.
