Knicks Can Clinch First Title Since 1973 in Game 5 Saturday Night in San Antonio

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 12, 2026

The New York Knicks are one victory away from ending 53 years of anguish. Game 5 of the 2026 NBA Finals tips off Saturday night at 8:30 PM ET on ABC from Frost Bank Center in San Antonio, and the Knicks arrive with a commanding 3-1 series lead and a closing pedigree that should terrify the Spurs.

New York has been perfect in elimination games this postseason — three wins, all on the road, by an average of 39.3 points. More stunning still: two of those wins came right here at Frost Bank Center. The Knicks opened this series by winning Games 1 and 2 in San Antonio, proving early that the atmosphere in South Texas holds no power over them.

History Is Almost Entirely on New York's Side

The numbers tell a near-definitive story. Teams with a 3-1 Finals lead win the championship approximately 96% of the time. In more than 80 years of NBA Finals history, only one team has ever mounted a comeback from that deficit — the 2016 Golden State Warriors, who overcame the Cleveland Cavaliers. That is one team, one time, in the entire history of the sport.

The San Antonio Spurs are 5.5-point home favorites at FanDuel and ESPN/DraftKings, the total sitting at 216.5 points. The odds reflect the Spurs' home-court advantage and their desperate urgency, but they do not reflect history.

What the Knicks Must Execute

Jalen Brunson has been the engine of this run. The point guard poured in 36 points in the Game 4 comeback win — one of the most astonishing performances in Finals history — and has been the steadying force every time New York has needed a basket or a stop. SportsLine's model projects him for 24 points in Game 5, suggesting another efficient outing against a Spurs defense that hasn't found an answer.

Karl-Anthony Towns and OG Anunoby — whose tip-in with 1.2 seconds remaining won Game 4 — give the Knicks weapons that spread the floor and attack in different ways. Josh Hart remains a pest on the glass. This is a complete team, and it has shown it can win ugly or win decisively.

The key matchup will again be managing Victor Wembanyama. The Spurs' 22-year-old star logged 44 minutes in Game 4 and projects for 29 points in Game 5 according to SportsLine. A rested and motivated Wemby is a serious threat. But the Knicks have limited him to 10.5 rebounds per game in this series, which is manageable, and their combination of size and length has forced inefficient shooting nights more often than not.

What's Next

If the Knicks win Saturday night, New York will celebrate its first NBA Championship since 1973, when Walt Frazier and Willis Reed closed out the Lakers. The city hasn't seen this kind of moment in over half a century.

If the Spurs extend the series, Game 6 returns to Madison Square Garden on Tuesday, June 17. But based on everything this Knicks team has shown in three road closeout wins, the smart money says New York finishes what it started. Game 5. Saturday. Frost Bank Center. The moment has arrived.
