Dybantsa Pre-Draft Visits: Meetings With Wizards and Jazz Went Well

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

June 16, 2026

AJ Dybantsa is making the rounds — and he's leaving good impressions. The BYU phenom and projected No. 1 overall pick in the 2026 NBA Draft completed pre-draft visits with two lottery teams over the weekend, and his father confirmed everything went according to plan.

Dybantsa visited the Washington Wizards in the nation's capital on Friday, June 13, then flew to Salt Lake City on Saturday for a session with the Utah Jazz. His father Ace Dybantsa told reporters that both meetings "went well," offering the clearest public signal yet that the process is on track heading into next week's draft.

Washington Still the Expected Landing Spot

The Wizards hold the No. 1 overall pick after finishing with a league-worst 17-65 record, giving them 14.0% lottery odds. Dybantsa remains the -450 betting favorite to be selected first — a number reflecting near-certainty in the market's eyes. Mock drafts across ESPN, CBS Sports, and NBC Sports all project him to Washington.

Darryn Peterson, the second most frequently projected top-two pick, also visited the Wizards over the same stretch, giving Washington a direct comparison between the two players being discussed most seriously for their slot. The Wizards appear to be doing their due diligence, but the expectation around the league is that Dybantsa is their pick.

What Dybantsa Brings

The 6-foot-9 forward averaged 25.5 points per game and shot 51.0% from the field at BYU — numbers that drew comparisons to some of the game's best scoring forwards. His combination of size, skill, and efficiency is rare at any level, and scouts have consistently rated him as the best prospect in the 2026 class.

If he lands in Washington as expected, Dybantsa joins a young core that is already more competitive than the Wizards' record suggests. Trae Young was acquired from the Hawks in January. Anthony Davis came over from the Mavericks at the February trade deadline. Add Alex Sarr, Bilal Coulibaly, and Kyshawn George, and a No. 1 pick of Dybantsa's caliber could accelerate the Wizards' timeline significantly.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Draft takes place June 23 (Round 1) and June 24 (Round 2) at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. Seven days out, Dybantsa has done his visits, made his impressions, and positioned himself for the moment his name is called. Washington is waiting. The Canyon of Heroes belongs to the Knicks this week — but Barclays Center belongs to Dybantsa on June 23.
