Dynasty Mode: How the Knicks Plan to Defend Their NBA Championship

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

June 16, 2026

The Canyon of Heroes parade is Thursday. But the work of defending a championship starts now. The New York Knicks enter the 2026 offseason as NBA champions for the first time since 1973 — and the organization is already thinking about how to make it happen again.

The good news? The foundation is exceptional. Jalen Brunson is locked in at $156.5 million through 2028, the team-friendly extension he signed in 2024 that gave the Knicks the cap flexibility to build the roster around him. That contract — famously $113 million less than what he could have demanded — remains the financial cornerstone of everything the Knicks are doing.

The Key Offseason Decisions

Three decisions will define New York's offseason. The first is Karl-Anthony Towns. The versatile center is a pivotal part of what makes the Knicks' offense function — his ability to stretch the floor and operate in pick-and-roll with Brunson was central to their Finals run. The Knicks will need to prioritize his extension timeline before the market for big men gets complicated.

The second decision is OG Anunoby. The lockdown defender finished the Finals as one of the most impactful two-way players on either roster, and his ability to guard the opponent's best perimeter player gave head coach Tom Thibodeau enormous flexibility in his defensive schemes. Keeping Anunoby should be a priority.

Third, GM Scott Perry will monitor the trade deadline landscape in February. The Knicks have assets — picks and depth — that could allow them to add one more piece at the right price without disrupting the championship core.

How the East Looks Now

The Eastern Conference the Knicks will need to navigate in 2026-27 depends heavily on what happens in the next seven days. If Giannis Antetokounmpo lands in Miami, the Heat become an immediate contender. If he goes to Boston, the Celtics could be the most dangerous team in the East. Either way, the Knicks will face serious competition.

But entering as defending champions matters. The team has championship experience now — not just organizational knowledge, but the actual feeling of winning in the Finals, of closing out a series, of managing playoff pressure at its highest level. That is an asset that cannot be replicated in any front office transaction.

What's Next

Thursday's parade is a celebration — and it should be fully savored. But beginning June 23, when the NBA Draft kicks off at Barclays Center, the business of basketball resumes. The Knicks need to make their moves, navigate the free agent market, and set the table for a run at back-to-back titles. If any team in the East has the roster, the coaching, and the financial structure to do it, it's this one.
