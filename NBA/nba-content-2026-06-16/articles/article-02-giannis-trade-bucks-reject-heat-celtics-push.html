Giannis Trade War: Bucks Reject Heat Package as Celtics Push Jaylen Brown Offer

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

June 16, 2026

The Giannis Antetokounmpo trade market has shifted. With seven days left before the Bucks' self-imposed June 23 deadline, the Milwaukee front office has made a pivotal move: they are rejecting the Miami Heat's current offer and widening their search for a better deal. According to league insiders, the Bucks "do not love" what Miami is putting on the table — and they want more.

NBA insider Marc Stein reported this weekend that a trade is "indeed drawing near," adding urgency to a situation that has already consumed the entire post-Finals news cycle. Bucks co-owner Jimmy Haslam wants the deal finalized before the NBA Draft on June 23, where the Bucks hold the No. 13 pick — a pick that could be part of any trade package.

Heat's Offer Falls Short

The Heat entered the summer as the clear frontrunner, and Giannis reportedly prefers Miami as his destination. But the Bucks are in the business of maximizing return, and Miami's proposed package — Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jacquez Jr., and the No. 13 pick — has not cleared the bar Milwaukee is looking for.

The sticking point appears to be the lack of a proven, established star in Miami's offer. Herro is a capable scorer but not an All-Star-level return for a two-time MVP. Kel'el Ware is a promising second-year big, and Jacquez is a young wing, but neither constitutes the kind of blue-chip asset that typically accompanies a blockbuster trade of this magnitude. The Bucks are signaling they need either a more established name or significantly more picks.

Celtics Enter With Jaylen Brown

Boston has entered the conversation with a formal offer built around Jaylen Brown. On paper, it addresses the Bucks' request for star-level talent — Brown is a two-time All-Star and former Finals MVP who averaged over 23 points per game during last season's playoff run. But the offer comes with its own complications.

Trading Brown means ending the Tatum-Brown partnership that has defined the Celtics for nearly a decade. NBA insiders have noted that "stumbling block" directly — there is real organizational reluctance in Boston about dismantling a core that came within two wins of a title in 2024. Whether the Celtics can truly commit to that trade remains unclear.

Other teams in the mix include the Timberwolves, Rockets, and Nets, though none have emerged as serious frontrunners. The Warriors were reported to be out of the running earlier this week.

What's Next

Seven days remain before the June 23 deadline, and the Bucks appear determined not to blink first. If Miami wants Giannis before the draft, they will need to sweeten their offer. If they don't, the Celtics' Jaylen Brown package becomes increasingly attractive — complicated as it is. Whatever happens, this trade will reshape the Eastern Conference for years to come. The clock is running.
