Austin Reaves: Lakers Hold Exclusive Window but Nets Are Waiting With Max Offer

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

June 16, 2026

The Los Angeles Lakers have a window — but it is closing. Between now and June 30, when NBA free agency officially opens, the Lakers hold an exclusive negotiating period with their own free agents. That means they have a head start on re-signing Austin Reaves before the Brooklyn Nets or anyone else can make a competing offer. The question is whether they use it.

Because the Nets will be ready the moment free agency starts. Reports indicate Brooklyn is prepared to offer Reaves a 4-year, $178.5 million max contract on June 30. The Lakers can counter with a 5-year, $239 million deal — a larger total but also a longer commitment for a 28-year-old wing.

Why Reaves Matters So Much

Reaves finished the 2025-26 season averaging 23.3 points, 5.5 assists, and 4.7 rebounds per game on 49.0% shooting from the field. He has developed into one of the most complete two-way players at his position in the league, combining elite shot creation with the defensive awareness to guard multiple positions.

More importantly, Reaves is the ideal complement to Luka Doncic. His ability to play off the ball, space the floor, and make decisions in pick-and-roll situations gives Los Angeles something very specific — a secondary playmaker who never competes with Doncic for the rock, but can absolutely carry offensive possessions when needed. Losing that would set the Lakers back significantly.

The Nets' Play

Brooklyn finished last season outside the playoff picture but has been aggressive in building toward a competitive future. Reaves would be the centerpiece of that effort — a player entering what should be his prime years, with a championship-level game already established. The Nets offering max money makes sense strategically, even if the match between Reaves and Brooklyn's current roster is less clear than his fit in Los Angeles.

The Atlanta Hawks and Detroit Pistons have also been mentioned as interested parties, giving Reaves a genuine market if he reaches unrestricted free agency. All of that gives him leverage — leverage the Lakers have to respect.

What's Next

The NBA's exclusive negotiating window runs through June 30. If the Lakers want to lock up Reaves before the chaos of open free agency, they need to get a deal done in the next two weeks. Reports suggest Reaves wants to stay in Los Angeles — but wanting to stay and accepting the Lakers' terms are two different conversations. The Nets' offer will be out there on July 1. The Lakers need to make this right before that clock expires.
