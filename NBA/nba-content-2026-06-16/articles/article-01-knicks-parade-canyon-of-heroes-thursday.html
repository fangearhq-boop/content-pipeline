Knicks Parade Thursday: The First Canyon of Heroes Celebration in Franchise History

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

June 16, 2026

Here is a fact most New York fans don't know: the Knicks won the NBA championship in 1970 and again in 1973, but they never received an official Canyon of Heroes ticker-tape parade for either title. Thursday, June 18 changes that. After 53 years, the New York Knicks will make their debut in the most storied celebration corridor in the world.

Mayor Zohran Mamdani has confirmed all the details. The parade begins at 10:00 AM at Battery Park and travels north along Broadway through the Canyon of Heroes to City Hall Plaza, where a Key to the City ceremony will immediately follow. City Hall and municipal buildings across the five boroughs will be illuminated blue and orange for the occasion.

What Makes This Parade Unprecedented

The Canyon of Heroes, the famous stretch of Broadway through Lower Manhattan, has hosted some of the most iconic celebrations in American history — from the return of Apollo 11 astronauts to Super Bowl champions to World Series winners. But despite winning two NBA titles, the Knicks have never marched those streets as champions. Thursday's parade is not just the first since 1973 — it is the first Canyon of Heroes celebration in franchise history.

That distinction matters to the generations of fans who have waited. The Knicks won in 1970 under Red Holzman with Willis Reed's iconic limping return in Game 7. They won again in 1973 with Walt Frazier and Earl Monroe. Yet somehow, the full Broadway ticker-tape experience eluded them both times. Fifty-three years later, the 2026 champions finally get their moment.

The Route and Details

The parade route covers approximately one mile through the heart of Lower Manhattan. It begins at Battery Park at the southern tip of Manhattan and follows Broadway north through the Financial District's glass-and-steel canyon to City Hall Plaza. Multiple outlets are already calling Thursday's event one of the biggest anticipated parades in New York City history.

The Key to the City ceremony at City Hall Plaza will be hosted by Mayor Mamdani and will feature the Knicks players and coaching staff. The ceremony follows immediately after the parade procession arrives, giving fans who position themselves at City Hall one final celebration moment with their champions.

NYPD is expected to line the full route, and city transportation officials have already posted guidance on street closures in Lower Manhattan. Fans traveling from outside the city are advised to use public transit, as parking in the area will be extremely limited.

What's Next

Thursday's parade is the ceremonial close of an extraordinary championship run — but the business of basketball doesn't wait long. After the confetti settles, the Knicks front office turns its attention to the offseason: Karl-Anthony Towns' extension timeline, OG Anunoby's re-signing priority, and how to navigate the Giannis Antetokounmpo trade market that could reshape the Eastern Conference. For Thursday, though, all of that waits. Battery Park. 10 AM. The first Canyon of Heroes parade in Knicks history.
