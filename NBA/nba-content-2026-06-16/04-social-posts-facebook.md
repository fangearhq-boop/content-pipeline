### STORY 4: Austin Reaves — Lakers' Clock Is Ticking

**Long-Form Post** | 4:00 PM ET
The Los Angeles Lakers have a closing window — and they cannot afford to miss it.

Austin Reaves, the Lakers' 28-year-old star who averaged 23.3 points, 5.5 assists and 4.7 rebounds per game this season on 49% shooting, is an unrestricted free agent when the market opens June 30. Between now and then, the Lakers hold an exclusive negotiating window with their own free agents.

The problem? The Brooklyn Nets are waiting.

The Nets are expected to offer Reaves a 4-year, $178.5 million max contract the moment free agency opens. The Lakers can respond with a 5-year, $239 million deal — a larger package that should be the winning bid. Reports indicate Reaves wants to remain in Los Angeles, where he has built a partnership with Luka Doncic that figures to be the core of the franchise for years.

But leverage is leverage. And Reaves has plenty of it.

The Lakers front office knows the stakes. Losing Reaves to Brooklyn would be a massive blow — not just for this season, but for the long-term vision of what they are trying to build around Doncic. The exclusive window is their best chance to get a deal done before the market chaos begins.

June 30 comes fast. The clock is running.

### STORY 5: Knicks Offseason — Building a Dynasty

**Long-Form Post** | 7:00 PM ET
The parade is Thursday. The offseason is already here.

After 53 years without a title, the New York Knicks did what seemed impossible — they won it all. But in the NBA, winning once is never enough. The question now is how do you do it again?

The foundation is extraordinary. Jalen Brunson is locked in at $156.5 million through 2028 — one of the most team-friendly superstar contracts in the league — and he just won Finals MVP while dropping 45 points in a closeout. The coaching staff, front office structure, and roster depth that got the Knicks here are all intact.

Now comes the hard part.

General Manager Scott Perry will need to make decisions on Karl-Anthony Towns' extension, OG Anunoby's re-signing priority, and whether to use the trade deadline next February to add another piece to an already deep squad. The Knicks enter the offseason with strong salary cap flexibility — a direct result of Brunson's sacrifice two years ago.

The Eastern Conference knows what's coming. With the Giannis trade saga unresolved and Boston potentially blowing things up for a piece of the Greek Freak, the East could look very different in October.

But for now? Enjoy the parade Thursday. Because this Knicks team earned every second of it — and they're not done yet.
