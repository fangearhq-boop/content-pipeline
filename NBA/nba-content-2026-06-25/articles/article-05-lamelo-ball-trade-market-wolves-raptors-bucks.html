LaMelo Ball Trade Market Is Open: Wolves, Raptors, and Bucks Are Calling Charlotte

By Damon Pierce June 25, 2026

Charlotte Is Ready to Deal

The Charlotte Hornets have made it known they are open to trading LaMelo Ball — and the NBA offseason now has its first major trade storyline to replace the Giannis conversation. Per HoopsHype and NBC Sports, two teams have already emerged as serious suitors: the Minnesota Timberwolves and the Toronto Raptors. The Milwaukee Bucks, newly in rebuild mode after trading Giannis Antetokounmpo to Miami, have long-standing interest as well.

Ball is 24 years old and carries a $40.8 million salary. He is one of the most gifted passers and creative offensive players in the league when healthy and engaged. The trade window opens July 6 — the same day the Giannis trade formally executes — and the LaMelo market is expected to move quickly once that window opens.

The Suitors and What They Want

Minnesota is the most aggressive pursuer. The Timberwolves missed out on Giannis Antetokounmpo in the offseason race and are now looking for a star to pair alongside Anthony Edwards. LaMelo Ball next to Anthony Edwards would be one of the most electric backcourts in the NBA — two of the most creative offensive players under 25 in the same starting lineup. The complication is structural: Ball's $40.8 million salary is larger than the Rudy Gobert-centered trade packages Minnesota typically assembles, creating cap math challenges that require creativity to solve.

Toronto has a different angle. The Raptors are building around Scottie Barnes and Brandon Ingram and need a genuine playmaker to complete the trio. Ball at 24 would be the missing piece in what could become a young, talented roster with a legitimate ceiling. Barnes, Ingram, and Ball as the core of a Toronto team would make the Raptors relevant in ways they have not been since the championship era.

Milwaukee's interest is more circumstantial. The Bucks just completed the Giannis trade to Miami and received Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., Kasparas Jakucionis, and significant pick equity in return. In some timelines, they pivot from a pure rebuild toward contention by adding Ball as the centerpiece of a new direction around those assets.

Charlotte's Position

The Hornets are not in a rush to give Ball away. They watched him develop into one of the most watched players in the league — and his entertainment value, regardless of the win-loss record in Charlotte, has kept the franchise visible in a market that could easily get lost. If they are moving him, the asking price needs to justify the departure: young players, picks, and potentially a player who can anchor the next phase of Charlotte's rebuild.

The question of whether Charlotte's asking price matches what teams are willing to give is the variable that defines this story going forward. Ball being available is news. The deal getting done is the outcome — and that gap can be enormous.

What's Next

July 6 is the date that matters. When the trade window opens, the Timberwolves, Raptors, and Bucks are expected to begin formal discussions with Charlotte about what a LaMelo Ball trade looks like in practice. Ball is 24, healthy, and — if properly motivated — a player who can change the trajectory of a franchise. Wherever he lands, the receiving team gets one of the most electrifying passers in the game. The NBA offseason is just getting started, and the LaMelo Ball market is already its most compelling open story.
