Michigan's Historic Draft Night: Three Top-12 Picks, One Night, Never Done Before

By Marcus Cole June 25, 2026

The Wolverines Rewrote the Record Books

No school in NBA Draft history had ever placed three players in the top 12 selections in a single draft. Michigan did it Tuesday night — and did it less than two months after winning the 2026 NCAA Championship. Coach Dusty May went 34-3, beat UConn in the title game, then departed to become the new head coach of the Dallas Mavericks. His players followed him directly into the history books.

The sequence: Morez Johnson Jr. went No. 9 to the Dallas Mavericks, where he will play alongside 2025 Rookie of the Year Cooper Flagg. Yaxel Lendeborg went No. 11 to the Golden State Warriors. Aday Mara — the 7-foot-3 Spanish center who won Big Ten Defensive Player of the Year — went No. 12 to the Oklahoma City Thunder. Three Wolverines. Top 12. First time in NBA Draft history.

The Broader Draft Class That Set Multiple Records

Michigan's night was historic within a draft class that was itself historically unusual. Eight of the first nine selections were freshmen — tying a record set in 2017 and matched in 2025. The first 20 picks were all college players, the first time that had happened since 1994. The draft class announced a generational shift in how NBA-ready talent develops and where it comes from.

AJ Dybantsa, the No. 1 pick from BYU going to Washington, set the tone for all of it. He averaged 25.5 points per game on 51 percent shooting and skipped every pre-draft workout before the draft. "They know what I can do," he said. His confidence was validated the moment his name was called first. Darryn Peterson from Kansas went No. 2 to Utah, having averaged 20.2 points per game. Cameron Boozer went No. 3 to Memphis — the Naismith Award winner who averaged 22.5 points and 10.5 rebounds per game at Duke.

The Boozer Family Story

Cameron Boozer's selection deserves its own moment. His father, Carlos Boozer, was selected No. 35 overall in 2002 — a second-round pick who built an All-Star NBA career. His son went No. 3 in 2026, winning every major college award in the process: the Naismith Player of the Year, consensus All-American, the works. The Memphis Grizzlies, beginning life after Ja Morant, landed the best player in college basketball with the third pick. The Boozer family went from second round to top three in one generation.

Morez Johnson Jr. landing in Dallas is another subplot worth tracking. He joins a Mavericks team with Cooper Flagg and a new head coach in Dusty May — the same coach who developed Johnson at Michigan. The continuity is unusual for a rookie entering a new organization, and it gives Dallas a built-in advantage in the player-coach relationship that typically takes years to develop.

What's Next

Michigan's three top-12 picks will spread across Dallas, Golden State, and Oklahoma City, adding to rosters that were already building toward something. For the program itself, the combination of an NCAA Championship and an unprecedented draft night puts the 2025-26 Wolverines in the conversation for the greatest single-season college basketball program in recent history. The records are set. Now comes the part where the players prove they belong — and the 2026-27 NBA season will begin to answer whether Michigan built a championship team or just a draft class.
