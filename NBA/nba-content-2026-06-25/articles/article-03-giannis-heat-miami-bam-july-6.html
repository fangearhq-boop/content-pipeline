July 6 Can't Come Fast Enough: How Giannis and Bam Are About to Reshape the Eastern Conference

By Jake Torres June 25, 2026

The Trade That Changed Everything

In the early hours of June 23, the NBA world learned what was happening in Milwaukee. Giannis Antetokounmpo and Bobby Portis were going to Miami. The Bucks were receiving Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., Kasparas Jakucionis, three first-round picks, a pick swap, and a second-round pick. The deal was agreed to at Draft Night, two weeks before it formally executes — that happens July 6, when the trade moratorium lifts and the Greek Freak officially becomes a Miami Heat.

The Eastern Conference has not looked like this in years. The defending champion New York Knicks are the standard. Behind them, as of July 6, comes something the East has never seen: Giannis Antetokounmpo and Bam Adebayo, side by side, in Heat colors.

Why Giannis + Bam Changes the Conversation

Adebayo has spent years as one of the NBA's most versatile defensive big men — a center who can guard one through five, switch pick-and-rolls, and anchor a top-tier defensive scheme. Antetokounmpo is a two-time MVP, a champion from 2021, and one of the most physically dominant forwards in the history of the game. He is 31 years old and at the peak of his powers.

The frontcourt they form together has a credible case as the most dangerous defensive pairing in the modern game. What the Knicks will have to game-plan for in 2026-27 is something very few teams have ever had to navigate — a pair of elite two-way forwards who can anchor a defense, rebound at a championship level, and punish smaller lineups on the offensive end.

Giannis is eligible for a $275 million extension beginning October 1. The expectation across the league is that it gets signed before the calendar flips. Heat and Bucks officials discussed his long-term commitment as part of the trade framework. He wanted to be here.

How It Happened — And Who Came Away Empty

Giannis had signaled for over a year that he wanted out of Milwaukee. He reportedly pushed for the New York Knicks as his preferred destination — but the Knicks won the 2026 NBA Championship on June 11, complicating the calculus. When the Bucks set a Draft Night deadline and began soliciting final offers on June 23, Miami was the team that had the most complete package prepared.

The Boston Celtics made their pitch: Jaylen Brown plus two first-round picks. Milwaukee said no. The Bucks co-owner Jimmy Haslam preferred the volume of young talent and future picks that Miami was sending — Herro as a proven scorer, Ware as a developmental center with upside, Jakucionis as a draft-night wild card, and enough pick equity to rebuild a competitive roster faster than most expected. Celtics fans are still processing what did not happen.

What's Next

The formal date is July 6. That is when Giannis puts on a Heat uniform for the first time, when his agent and the Heat finalize the extension framework, and when the Eastern Conference race for 2026-27 truly takes shape. The Knicks, who won the championship three weeks ago, are no longer the only team building something worth watching. The Heat with Giannis and Bam are now the most credible challenger in the East — and the race between South Beach and New York is the storyline that will define the NBA season before it even begins.
