Giannis to Miami: 9 Days Until the Trade That Reshapes the East Becomes Official

By Marcus Cole

June 27, 2026

Hoop Heroes

The moratorium lifts on July 6 at noon. That is when the Giannis Antetokounmpo trade officially executes, when Tyler Herro becomes a Milwaukee Buck, when Bobby Portis becomes a Miami Heat, and when the Eastern Conference fundamentally reorganizes itself around a new power structure. Nine days. The league is holding its breath.

The trade agreed on June 22 is one of the largest transactions in NBA history by raw asset value. Miami sent Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., Kasparas Jakucionis, a 2030 first-round pick swap, an unprotected 2031 first-round pick, an unprotected 2033 first-round pick, and a 2033 second-round pick to Milwaukee. The Bucks sent Giannis Antetokounmpo and Bobby Portis back. Four players and three first-round picks for one of the most decorated players of his generation.

Why the Bucks Chose Miami Over Boston

The Bucks had two serious offers on the table. The Boston Celtics constructed a package centered on Jaylen Brown — an All-Star with a championship pedigree — plus at least two first-round picks. Miami built theirs around younger, more versatile pieces and a deeper haul of draft capital.

Milwaukee chose the Heat's offer. According to reporting from SI.com, the Bucks preferred Miami's package because it offered a broader combination of young contributors and future flexibility. The Celtics' offer was headlined by a bigger name but fewer picks.

What drove the decision, at least in part, was the state of the Giannis-Milwaukee relationship itself. ESPN described the final chapter of Giannis's time with the Bucks as genuinely difficult: "things got really yucky," one insider put it. The breakdown took 13 months — from May 2025, when Giannis first informed the organization he wanted to move on, through the June 22 agreement — and the relationship had deteriorated to the point where a clean break was preferable to any resolution that kept everyone in Milwaukee.

Giannis spent 13 seasons with the Bucks. He is a 10-time All-Star, a two-time league MVP, a Defensive Player of the Year, and the player who delivered Milwaukee its first championship since 1971 in 2021. The city gave him everything. He gave it back. But things ended badly, and the trade brings closure to both sides.

Herro Goes Home

Tyler Herro grew up in Milwaukee. When the trade was announced, he posted his hometown's 414 area code on social media. He did not need to say anything else.

Herro has been one of Miami's most consistent offensive weapons since the Heat drafted him in 2019. His relocation to Milwaukee means the Bucks are getting exactly what they needed in the short term: an established scorer with playoff experience who can help stabilize a team that will spend the next several years rebuilding around the picks they received.

Portis also joins Miami — a familiar, physical presence who can provide energy off the bench while the Heat's primary frontcourt of Giannis and Bam Adebayo takes shape.

The Eastern Conference Ripple Effect

The Celtics lost the sweepstakes and are now reportedly exploring a Jaylen Brown trade — though Brown has not requested one. ESPN's Brian Windhorst has said Brown "is going to get traded." Boston is asking for four first-round picks in return.

Miami's title odds moved from 30-1 before the trade to 18-1 after. Their Eastern Conference odds moved to 6-1 — behind the Knicks and Celtics. Giannis alongside Bam Adebayo in South Beach is a pairing that requires every competitor in the conference to reconsider their defensive schemes, their rosters, and their timelines.

The Knicks, the defending champions, will be watching closely. The Heat were already a dangerous team. The Heat with Giannis are a genuine title contender.

What's Next

July 6 is when everything becomes real. The Giannis trade executes. Free agency signings begin at noon. The league's offseason fully opens.

Before that, the free agency negotiating period opens Monday at 6 PM. Teams will be working simultaneously on multiple fronts — finalizing deals, managing cap space, navigating the rosters they have while engineering the ones they want. Miami already has Giannis locked in. Their focus will shift to filling out the roster around him and Bam at the right price.

Giannis has said he wants to win. Miami has constructed around winning. Nine days from now, the most anticipated partnership in this offseason gets its official start.
