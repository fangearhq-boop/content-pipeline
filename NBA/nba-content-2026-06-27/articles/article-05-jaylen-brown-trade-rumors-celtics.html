Jaylen Brown Hasn't Asked Out — But the Celtics Are Already Working the Phones

By Jake Torres

June 27, 2026

Hoop Heroes

Jaylen Brown has not requested a trade. That is where this story starts, because it is easy to lose in the noise. ClutchPoints sources confirmed it. Brown's own camp has not signaled any desire to leave Boston. He is not the one pushing for this.

The Celtics are.

Multiple outlets confirmed this week that Boston is "actively engaged" in trade discussions around Brown. ESPN's Brian Windhorst, one of the league's most reliably wired reporters, stated plainly that Brown "is going to get traded." The asking price, per multiple sources, is at least four unprotected first-round picks. The NBA offseason, already the most chaotic in recent memory, just got another seismic storyline.

What Triggered This

The Giannis Antetokounmpo sweepstakes. Boston assembled what sounded like a competitive offer for Milwaukee — Jaylen Brown, plus at least two first-round picks. The Bucks chose Miami instead, attracted by a deeper haul of draft capital and younger role players.

When the Celtics lost that bid, it left them with a roster that won a championship in 2024 and reached the conference finals in 2026, but also one that is not getting any younger and that has real questions about whether it can be sustained under current financial constraints. Brown is 29 years old and on a supermax contract. He is also someone teams around the league would almost certainly overpay to acquire.

The Celtics know this. They are reportedly willing to explore what that market looks like — with or without Brown initiating anything. In the NBA, sometimes the front office makes the move before the player does.

The Asking Price and Who Could Meet It

Four unprotected first-round picks is a franchise-reshaping ask. Only a handful of teams have both the cap flexibility and the pick inventory to meet it, and most of them are in various stages of rebuilding.

The Detroit Pistons have been mentioned prominently. A sign-and-trade centered on Jalen Duren — a young, mobile center with upside — would give Boston something to work with while clearing salary and injecting youth into a roster that has been built for the present. Detroit has picks from previous tank seasons and the cap room to make it work.

Portland appears on the list as well. The Trail Blazers have been deliberately accumulating future assets for years, and Brown at 29 would represent a legitimate star for a team that has lacked one since Damian Lillard's departure.

Minnesota was involved in early conversations before landing LaMelo Ball from Charlotte. With Ball now on the way, the Timberwolves' calculus changed. But if a team suddenly finds itself with the assets and a gap at star forward, they tend to call Boston.

What Brown Brings

Whatever team trades for Brown is acquiring one of the better two-way wings in the league. He averaged roughly 26 points per game in 2025-26 on efficient shooting, and his playoff track record — two championships with Boston in 2024, four Finals appearances — is legitimate. He can defend multiple positions, attack off the dribble, and handle the ball in pick-and-roll situations that matter in the fourth quarter.

The concern, for any team considering the deal, is whether they can build around him rather than alongside him. Brown has been a co-star for most of his career. The question of whether he can be the anchor of a contender has never been fully answered.

What's Next

Nothing official can happen until July 6 when the moratorium lifts, but conversations are happening right now. The free agency negotiating period opens Monday at 6 PM ET, and teams exploring the Brown market will be doing it simultaneously with their own free agency work.

Windhorst has been right before when he stakes a claim this clearly. If he says Brown is going to be traded, the burden of proof is on the skeptics. Boston has won two championships in the last three years and still chose to explore blowing it up after losing a single sweepstakes bid. It says something about where the franchise believes it is in its cycle.

Brown has not asked out. He may never publicly say a word about this. But in the NBA offseason, the player does not always drive the car — and right now, the Celtics are at the wheel.
