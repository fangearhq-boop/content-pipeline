2026 NBA Playoffs First Round Preview: Full Bracket, Schedule, and Key Storylines

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 17, 2026

The 2026 NBA Playoffs tip off Saturday, April 18, and the field is nearly complete. Pending the results of tonight's two play-in games — Magic vs. Hornets in the East and Warriors vs. Suns in the West — the full 16-team bracket will be set. What is already confirmed is a first round loaded with compelling matchups, franchise narratives, and individual rivalries that should make for one of the most watchable postseasons in recent memory.

Eastern Conference First Round

No. 1 Detroit Pistons vs. No. 8 TBD — April 19, 6:30 PM ET (NBC)

The Pistons made NBA history this season, finishing 60-22 for their first 60-win campaign since 2005-06 and only the third such season in franchise history. Cade Cunningham missed 11 games mid-season with a collapsed lung, returned to finish strong, and helped deliver Detroit its first No. 1 seed in two decades. Their opponent — either the Magic or the Hornets — arrives battle-tested from the play-in and potentially dangerous despite the seeding gap. The Pistons were 3-0 against Charlotte during the regular season, while Detroit had a different dynamic against Orlando. Regardless of who shows up Sunday, the Pistons enter as heavy favorites.

No. 2 Boston Celtics vs. No. 7 Philadelphia 76ers — April 19, 1 PM ET

This matchup carries an asterisk the size of Philadelphia. Joel Embiid, who underwent appendicitis surgery around April 9, will not play in the first round. The 76ers' entire championship ceiling rested on Embiid's dominance, and without him, Tyrese Maxey is being asked to carry the franchise. Maxey has been brilliant in the play-in — he scored 31 points in a Night 2 victory over the Magic — but facing a Celtics team of this caliber is a different test entirely. Boston enters as a significant favorite but is not invulnerable; a hobbled 76ers team playing with nothing to lose can be unpredictable.

No. 3 New York Knicks vs. No. 6 Atlanta Hawks — April 18, 6 PM ET

The Knicks clinched the No. 3 seed and finished one of their most complete regular seasons in recent memory. The Hawks, led by a young and athletic core, are an awkward matchup for anyone. Expect physicality, defensive intensity, and at least one nationally televised classic in Madison Square Garden. The Knicks' experience advantage should ultimately prevail, but the Hawks can make this series uncomfortable.

No. 4 Cleveland Cavaliers vs. No. 5 Toronto Raptors — April 18, 1 PM ET

The most evenly matched East first-round series on paper. The Cavaliers went 52-30, finished No. 4, and found new life after adding Donovan Mitchell and Chris Harden to the same lineup, going 16-6 when both played together. Toronto has steadily improved and enters with the momentum of a team playing above expectations. This one could easily go six or seven games.

Western Conference First Round

No. 1 Oklahoma City Thunder vs. No. 8 TBD — April 19, 3:30 PM ET

The Thunder finished 64-18 — the best record in the NBA — and are the consensus title favorites. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander won his second consecutive MVP award, averaging 31.6 points per game on 55 percent shooting. Oklahoma City is healthy, deep, and playing in front of a home crowd that has become one of the most intimidating atmospheres in basketball. Their first-round opponent — Suns or Warriors — will arrive exhausted from back-to-back elimination games. The Thunder are the class of the league.

No. 2 San Antonio Spurs vs. No. 7 Portland Trail Blazers — April 19, 9 PM ET (NBC)

Billed as one of the most intriguing first-round matchups in years. The Spurs' Victor Wembanyama — the unanimous Defensive Player of the Year, averaging 25 points, 11.5 rebounds, and 3.1 blocks per game — faces a Trail Blazers team that has been on the most remarkable run in basketball. Portland's Deni Avdija scored 41 points, added 12 assists, and seven rebounds in the Night 1 play-in win over the Suns — one of the highest-scoring performances in play-in history. The Blazers went 42-40 in the regular season but have clearly arrived at the right moment. San Antonio (62-20) is still the heavy favorite, but Avdija and the Blazers will make this a series.

No. 3 Denver Nuggets vs. No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves — TBD

Nikola Jokic and the Nuggets — three-time champion Jokic leads a team built around Finals experience — face a hungry Minnesota team looking to recapture the momentum from their conference finals run two years ago. The Nuggets have championship institutional knowledge that few teams can match. Expect slow, methodical basketball at high altitude with one of the sport's best players controlling tempo.

No. 4 Los Angeles Lakers vs. No. 5 Houston Rockets — April 18, 8:30 PM ET

The headlining injury story of the entire postseason. The Lakers open without Luka Doncic (Grade 2 hamstring strain, in Madrid for treatment) and Austin Reaves (Grade 2 oblique), both of whom were injured on April 2. Anthony Davis will lead a shorthanded roster against a Rockets team that has suddenly become far more dangerous than the seedings suggest. Alperen Sengun and Jalen Green will test Los Angeles's depth from the opening tip.

What's Next

Tonight's play-in games complete the bracket. The first-round schedule begins Saturday afternoon and runs through early May, with the second round beginning once series are decided. Keep an eye on the Spurs-Blazers series for the best basketball in Round 1, the Thunder's path to repeat as champions, and the Lakers' survival story without their stars. The 2026 playoffs are already unlike anything we've seen in recent years — and the games haven't even started yet.
