Lakers Enter Playoffs Without Luka Doncic or Austin Reaves — Both Out for Rockets Series

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 17, 2026

The Los Angeles Lakers will open their 2026 NBA Playoffs campaign Saturday night without their two most important players. Luka Doncic and Austin Reaves both suffered significant injuries in the same game — an April 2 loss to the Oklahoma City Thunder — and neither is expected to be available for the first-round series against the Houston Rockets. Game 1 tips off at 8:30 PM ET on Saturday, April 18.

Luka Doncic: Grade 2 Hamstring Strain, In Madrid for Treatment

Doncic sustained a Grade 2 hamstring strain during the April 2 game against the Thunder and has since traveled to Madrid, Spain to receive specialized stem cell treatment. A Grade 2 hamstring strain indicates a partial muscle tear — a serious injury that typically requires three to six weeks of recovery with a high risk of reinjury if rushed. Injury experts have been blunt about the timeline: a return in the first round is not considered realistic.

Even with the aggressive treatment in Madrid, the realistic target for Doncic's return is the second round of the playoffs — meaning early May at the earliest. Head coach JJ Redick confirmed at an April 14 press conference that both Doncic and Reaves are out indefinitely, offering no specific timeline. For a franchise that built its playoff identity around a 27-year-old superstar who averaged over 28 points per game this season, the absence is seismic.

Austin Reaves: Grade 2 Oblique Strain, Also Sidelined

Reaves suffered a Grade 2 oblique strain in the same April 2 loss — a partial tear of the muscle group running along the core that is aggravated by nearly every basketball movement. ESPN's Shams Charania reported that the earliest Reaves could return is the first week of May, which essentially rules him out for Round 1 as well.

There is a slight silver lining in Reaves's case: injury experts have noted that oblique strains carry lower reinjury risk than hamstring strains, creating a theoretical window for him to return in the back half of the first round if the series extends to five or six games and his recovery progresses ahead of schedule. That possibility remains speculative at this point. As of Friday, both players are out.

Who Steps Up for Los Angeles?

The Lakers' remaining roster will be asked to fill an enormous void. Anthony Davis anchors the frontcourt and is capable of dominant playoff performances, but the perimeter scoring and playmaking that Doncic and Reaves provided — particularly in late-game situations — cannot simply be replaced by committee. The supporting cast will need to collectively exceed what anyone reasonably expected of them before this season began.

Houston, meanwhile, enters as the No. 5 seed in the West at 45-37. The Rockets feature Alperen Sengun as a versatile big man and Jalen Green as a high-volume scorer. They are a team that Los Angeles was expected to handle comfortably with a full roster. Without Doncic and Reaves, the matchup looks considerably more balanced — or potentially lopsided in the other direction.

What's Next

Game 1 of Lakers vs. Rockets tips off Saturday night at 8:30 PM ET. If the Lakers can survive the first round — perhaps with one or both stars returning late in the series — the championship picture shifts dramatically. For now, the questions are simple and the answers are uncertain: How good are these Lakers without their stars, and can Davis will them through a round? The NBA playoffs have been defined by unexpected turns, and Los Angeles is counting on another one starting tomorrow.
