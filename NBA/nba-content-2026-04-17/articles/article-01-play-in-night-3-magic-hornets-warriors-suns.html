Play-In Night 3: Magic vs. Hornets and Warriors vs. Suns — Final Two Seeds Decided Tonight

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 17, 2026

The 2026 NBA Play-In Tournament reaches its climax on Friday night with two win-or-go-home games that will complete the playoff bracket. In the East, the Charlotte Hornets travel to Orlando to face the Magic at 7:30 PM ET on Amazon Prime Video. Two and a half hours later, the Golden State Warriors head to Phoenix to face the Suns at 10 PM ET. In both matchups, the winner earns a No. 8 seed and a first-round assignment. The loser goes home for the summer.

East: Charlotte Hornets vs. Orlando Magic

The Hornets carry a powerful narrative into Orlando. Charlotte last made the NBA playoffs in 2016 — a full decade ago — and the franchise has spent the intervening years cycling through rebuilds, injuries, and near-misses. This season, behind the backcourt of LaMelo Ball and Kon Knueppel, the Hornets turned that cycle around. They finished 44-38, secured the No. 9 seed in the East, and earned their play-in spot.

Ball set the tone for their postseason bid in Night 1, delivering a vintage performance against the Miami Heat. He finished with 30 points and 10 assists, including the go-ahead layup with 4.7 seconds left in overtime, before Miles Bridges swatted away a potential Heat buzzer-beater to seal a 127-126 victory. That win pushed Charlotte into Friday's game, where the prize is even bigger: the No. 8 seed and a series against the No. 1 Detroit Pistons.

Orlando arrives in this spot after dropping Night 2's matchup against the Philadelphia 76ers, 97-109. The Magic had the home-court advantage in that game and couldn't hold off Tyrese Maxey, who scored 31 points. Now they host the Hornets with their season on the line. The Detroit Pistons went 3-0 against Charlotte during the regular season, and a February brawl between the franchises — which led to suspensions for Jalen Duren and Isaiah Stewart — adds a layer of drama to what awaits the winner.

West: Golden State Warriors vs. Phoenix Suns

Everything about the Warriors' play-in run has defied logic. Golden State came into the postseason as the No. 10 seed in the West and needed two play-in wins just to reach the first round. Their Night 2 performance against the Clippers delivered both the win and the signature moment of the entire tournament thus far.

Stephen Curry, 38 years old and averaging 26.6 points per game this season, scored 35 points against Los Angeles, including 27 after halftime. He hit seven three-pointers and buried a dagger three with 50.4 seconds remaining to put the game out of reach in a 126-121 final. At his age, Curry's output is historically anomalous — it belongs alongside the greatest late-career performances in league history.

The Suns present a different kind of challenge. Phoenix finished the regular season 45-37 as the No. 7 seed in the West before losing to the Portland Trail Blazers on Night 1, 110-114. Devin Booker, playing his 11th season and still only 29 years old, is averaging a career-high 26.1 points and 6.0 assists. The Suns have home court and a motivated star who has watched his team's championship window slowly close in recent years. For Booker, Friday is a chance to extend the season and prove Phoenix can still compete. For Curry, it is one more opportunity to remind a younger generation that the legend never really ends.

What's Next

The winner of tonight's East game faces the No. 1 Detroit Pistons in Game 1 of the first round on Sunday, April 19, at 6:30 PM ET on NBC. The winner out West earns a first-round series against the No. 1 Oklahoma City Thunder, tipping off Sunday at 3:30 PM. Both games represent steep climbing challenges — the Pistons finished 60-22 and the Thunder finished 64-18 with SGA as back-to-back MVP — but someone has to take the court. By midnight tonight, the 2026 playoff bracket will be fully set.
