LaMelo and Knueppel Make NBA History: New Splash Brothers Match Curry and Klay's 3-Point Record

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 17, 2026

When Steph Curry and Klay Thompson were lighting up arenas from downtown in the mid-2010s, it seemed impossible that any backcourt duo would replicate their volume-shooting legacy. Then came Kon Knueppel and LaMelo Ball. In the 2025-26 NBA season, the Charlotte Hornets guards finished first and second in the entire league in three-pointers made — Knueppel with 273, Ball with 272 — becoming the first teammates to accomplish this feat since the Splash Brothers themselves.

The Numbers That Stunned the League

Curry and Thompson achieved the 1-2 three-point finish twice in their careers: first in the legendary 2015-16 season when Golden State went 73-9, and again in 2022-23. Both times, basketball observers treated it as a once-in-a-generation achievement. The idea that two players on the same roster would collectively fire enough attempts, at high enough efficiency, to claim both the top spots in the entire NBA simultaneously was considered a Splash Brothers-only phenomenon.

Charlotte's backcourt just proved otherwise. Knueppel's 273 three-pointers led the league. Ball's 272 finished immediately behind. The margin between them was a single made shot. Their combined output — 545 three-pointers between two players on the same team — represents one of the most remarkable individual and shared achievements in a single season in NBA perimeter history.

The Story Behind Kon Knueppel

Knueppel is 20 years old. This was his rookie season. Those two facts, placed alongside the three-point record, are almost disorienting. Rookies are not supposed to lead the NBA in three-pointers made. They are supposed to adjust, struggle, grow — not immediately produce at a historical level alongside one of the league's established stars.

Knueppel came in with a reputation as a shooter, but nobody projected this kind of volume and efficiency from Day 1. He has steadily built one of the strongest Rookie of the Year cases in recent years, with the three-point record serving as the defining statistic of his campaign. His arrival gives Charlotte a long-term identity as one of the premier perimeter-shooting teams in basketball — and gives LaMelo Ball a partner capable of creating a genuine Splash Brothers-level backcourt conversation.

What It Means for Charlotte's Playoff Run

The Hornets finished 44-38 and earned the No. 9 seed in the Eastern Conference — a result that exceeded most preseason projections. Ball contributed 30 points and 10 assists in Charlotte's Night 1 play-in victory over the Miami Heat, delivering the go-ahead layup with 4.7 seconds remaining in overtime. Tonight, the Hornets face the Orlando Magic at 7:30 PM ET with the No. 8 East seed on the line and a first-round series against the Pistons awaiting the winner.

Charlotte has not made the playoffs since 2016. If Ball and Knueppel deliver tonight the way they have delivered all season, that drought ends — and the Splash Brothers comparison becomes even more irresistible.

What's Next

The immediate next chapter for Ball and Knueppel comes tonight in Orlando. Beyond that, the bigger picture is clear: regardless of tonight's result, Charlotte's backcourt has established itself as one of the most exciting young shooting duos in the NBA. Knueppel will only get better. Ball is still in his prime. If the Hornets can build around this two-man nucleus, the comparison to the Splash Brothers era may eventually be measured not just by a single stat line, but by a dynasty.
