Steph Curry at 38: One More Game, One More Reminder That the Legend Never Fades

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 17, 2026

There was a moment on Wednesday night when the Golden State Warriors were trailing the Los Angeles Clippers late in the second half of a do-or-die play-in game, and a 38-year-old man — one who has won four championships, two MVP awards, and reshaped how basketball is played — simply refused to let his season end. Stephen Curry scored 27 of his 35 points in the second half, hit seven three-pointers, and buried a dagger three with 50.4 seconds remaining to seal a 126-121 Warriors victory and keep their postseason hopes alive.

For anyone who needed a reminder of why Curry occupies a singular place in basketball history, Wednesday provided a 36-minute refresher course.

The Performance in Context

Curry finished the night 12-of-23 from the field and 7-of-12 from three-point range — shooting percentages that reflect both elite efficiency and a volume of activity that would be demanding for a player 15 years younger. He also added four assists, a rebound, and a steal in 36 minutes. What makes the second-half surge particularly remarkable is the composure involved: the Warriors were trailing, the season was on the line, and Curry methodically dismantled the Clippers' defense sequence by sequence.

His supporting cast contributed — Al Horford hit four three-pointers, and Jordan Porzingis added 20 points — but this was, unambiguously, a Curry performance. The Clippers had Kawhi Leonard (who finished scoreless in the fourth quarter) and James Harden (23 points) and they still lost by five because one man decided it was not their night.

What 38 Means in NBA Terms

Most NBA careers are over by 35. Some elite players extend into their mid-30s with reduced roles. Very few are still producing at a level that would place them among the best scorers in the league. Curry's 2025-26 season average of 26.6 points, 3.6 rebounds, and 4.7 assists per game is not a diminished veteran hanging on — it is an All-Star caliber season by any historical measure.

Curry signed a one-year, $62.6 million extension with the Warriors in August 2024, becoming the first NBA player in history to earn $60 million in a single season. The market was not wrong. He has been worth every dollar and then some. Whether this is his final season or there are more chapters ahead, the 2025-26 campaign has added meaningfully to one of basketball's most complete legacies.

Tonight in Phoenix

Friday night brings Curry and the Warriors to the Footprint Center in Phoenix for a 10 PM ET showdown with Devin Booker and the Suns — Game 4 of Curry's play-in existence and the most important shot at the playoffs he may have left. Booker, 29, is averaging 26.1 points and 6.0 assists this season, playing the best basketball of his career and fighting to extend a campaign that nearly ended on Tuesday when Phoenix fell to Portland.

The Suns have home court. They are younger. They have more to prove. And yet, anyone watching Wednesday night understands that none of those advantages fully neutralize what Curry can do with a basketball in his hands and his back against the wall. History says bet against him with caution.

What's Next

If the Warriors win tonight, they earn the No. 8 West seed and face the No. 1 Oklahoma City Thunder — a team with the NBA's best record — in Game 1 on Sunday. It would be one of basketball's most improbable stories of the year: a No. 10 seed, led by a 38-year-old legend, advancing to the first round. If Curry falls short in Phoenix, he exits one of the most remarkable late-career stretches in sports history with his reputation fully intact. Tonight, though, the game is still to be played — and Stephen Curry is still on the court.
