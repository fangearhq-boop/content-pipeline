Jokic's Worst Playoff Night in Memory Puts Nuggets in Crisis — Down 2-1 to Minnesota

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 24, 2026

There is no gentle way to say it: Nikola Jokic had one of the worst shooting performances of his career in Game 3, and Denver's season is now teetering on the edge because of it.

Jokic reportedly shot 7-for-26 from the field in Minnesota's 113-96 victory, a Game 3 result that gave the Timberwolves a 2-1 series lead. For a player who has built his legacy on efficiency and feel, the kind of shooting night he endured Thursday was deeply out of character. The three-time MVP who almost never wastes a possession was turned into a volume chucker by Minnesota's methodical defensive scheme — and Denver had no answers when their best player couldn't get his game going.

This Is a Pattern, Not an Accident

What happened in Game 3 wasn't an isolated bad night — it was a continuation of the challenge Minnesota has given Jokic throughout this series and in previous postseason meetings. In Game 2, Jokic posted elite counting numbers but went just 2-for-12 in the fourth quarter, unable to impose his will when the game was on the line.

The Timberwolves' defensive approach is specifically designed to take away Jokic's preferred spots and force him into difficult midrange and three-point attempts. Their switching and physical defensive rotations compress his space, and their length forces him into awkward angles in the paint — the same domain where he was held to a 68-34 disadvantage in overall points in the paint in Game 3. Minnesota held Denver to just 11 first-quarter points, the lowest total allowed in a Timberwolves postseason game.

Supporting Cast Has Gone Missing

Jokic's struggles would be manageable if Denver's supporting cast was picking up the slack. But through three games, the Nuggets have yet to find a consistent third or fourth option to take pressure off their superstar. Jamal Murray's fourth-quarter production — he combined with Jokic for just 2-for-12 in the decisive stretch of Game 2 — has been a persistent concern.

While Anthony Edwards' knee injury may limit Minnesota's own offensive capabilities in Game 4, the Timberwolves demonstrated in Game 3 that they can win without him at full strength, with Ayo Dosunmu and Jaden McDaniels absorbing the load. Denver does not have that kind of depth insurance.

What's Next

Game 4 is scheduled for Sunday in Denver, where the Nuggets will have the crowd and the home court advantage to try to even the series. But history is working against them: teams that fall behind 2-1 in the first round after losing Game 3 at home win the series less than 30 percent of the time. For Jokic and Denver to avoid an early exit, they need a vintage performance — patient, efficient, dominant in the post — from their franchise cornerstone starting Sunday. Denver's dynasty-level expectations demand no less.
