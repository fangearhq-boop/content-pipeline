Wolves Dominate 113-96, Lead Series 2-1 — But Anthony Edwards Exits With Knee Injury

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 24, 2026

The Minnesota Timberwolves won Game 3 convincingly, defeating the Denver Nuggets 113-96 on Thursday night to take a 2-1 series lead. But the celebration inside Target Center was tempered by a significant concern: Anthony Edwards left the game in the fourth quarter with a knee injury and did not return.

Edwards, who was listed as questionable before tip-off due to right knee maintenance, played 24 minutes and scored 17 points on 6-for-15 shooting before heading to the locker room. He was visibly in discomfort, and his departure — which included a visible limp and a chair being kicked on the bench — immediately overshadowed a dominant team performance. He had already missed 11 of the Timberwolves' final 14 regular season games with the knee issue.

Bench Steps Up in a Big Way

Minnesota's depth was on full display throughout Game 3. Ayo Dosunmu came off the bench to deliver 25 points and 9 assists, providing the Timberwolves with a secondary playmaker who kept the offense moving even as Edwards was limited. Jaden McDaniels was dominant at both ends, finishing with 20 points and 10 rebounds. Donte DiVincenzo added 15 points and 4 steals, turning Denver's ball-handlers into turnover machines.

The tone was set in the first quarter, when Minnesota held Denver to just 11 points — a franchise postseason record. The Timberwolves outscored the Nuggets 68-34 in the paint across the entire game, and built a lead as large as 27 points in the third quarter before coasting to the finish.

Jokic Unrecognizable

Nikola Jokic, arguably the most skilled big man in NBA history, was held to an extraordinary level of inefficiency in Game 3, reportedly shooting 7-for-26 from the floor. It was a performance completely out of character for the three-time MVP, who had posted 24 points, 15 rebounds, and 8 assists in Game 2. Minnesota's switching defense, length, and physicality disrupted his rhythm in ways Denver struggled to counter.

The Nuggets were outplayed in every facet of the game. Jokic's poor night, combined with their inability to protect the paint or manufacture quality shots for their supporting cast, put Denver in a deep hole that they could not climb out of.

What's Next

The most pressing question ahead of Game 4 is Edwards' availability. The Timberwolves have not offered a timeline for his return, and how they manage his knee in the coming days will shape the rest of this series. The good news for Minnesota is that they demonstrated on Thursday they can win without him at full strength — Dosunmu's bench performance was one of the best in this year's playoff field. For Denver, the challenge is enormous: revive Jokic's shooting, find answers for Minnesota's paint dominance, and extend a season that is now on the edge. The Nuggets must win Game 4 or head back to Minnesota needing to win three straight.
