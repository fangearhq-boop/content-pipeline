McCollum Does It Again: Hawks Edge Knicks 109-108, Lead Series 2-1

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 24, 2026

CJ McCollum has turned clutch time into his personal stage in this series, and the New York Knicks are running out of answers.

With 12.3 seconds remaining in Game 3, McCollum caught the ball on the wing, sized up his defender, and drained a fadeaway jumper that gave the Atlanta Hawks a 109-108 victory at State Farm Arena. Atlanta now leads the first-round series 2-1, and the Knicks are looking at a must-win situation heading into Game 4 on Saturday.

New York had appeared to steal the momentum. Jalen Brunson's and-one with 1:03 remaining gave the Knicks a 108-105 lead and had the energy shifting in New York's favor. But McCollum, who finished with 23 points, answered with a shot that could define Atlanta's postseason run.

Johnson Sets the Table

Beyond McCollum's heroics, Jalen Johnson was the most complete player on the floor all night. The Hawks forward recorded 24 points, 10 rebounds, and 8 assists — a line that underscores his growth into one of the Eastern Conference's most versatile young talents. Jonathan Kuminga added 21 points off the bench to give Atlanta a formidable supporting cast around the veteran McCollum.

Atlanta's first quarter was commanding. The Hawks erupted with an 11-0 run and closed the period with three consecutive three-pointers to take a commanding early lead.

Knicks Couldn't Finish

New York's trio gave everything they had. OG Anunoby led all scorers with 29 points, showing his elite two-way value throughout the night. Brunson contributed 26 points and nearly authored the comeback before McCollum shut the door. Karl-Anthony Towns added 21 points in a strong interior performance.

The problem for the Knicks was simple: they had no answer for McCollum in the decisive moment. He has now delivered a go-ahead or game-winning shot in each of Atlanta's two wins in this series, and the Knicks have yet to find a defensive solution for the veteran guard late in games.

What's Next

Game 4 is scheduled for Saturday in Atlanta, where the Hawks will have another home crowd and another chance to push New York to the brink of elimination. For the Knicks, the challenge is clear: find a way to contain McCollum in clutch situations, execute when they have the lead, and figure out how to slow down a Hawks team that has now won back-to-back games with the series on the line. A loss Saturday would put New York in a 3-1 hole — a deficit from which only a handful of teams in playoff history have ever recovered.
