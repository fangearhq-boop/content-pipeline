Friday Playoff Triple-Header: Celtics-76ers, Thunder-Suns, Lakers-Rockets All on Tap

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 24, 2026

After three gripping Game 3 results Thursday night, the 2026 NBA playoffs roll right back into action on Friday with a three-game slate that has must-watch written all over it. Here's everything you need to know.

Thunder vs. Suns — Game 3 (3:30 PM ET, NBC)

Oklahoma City opens the day's action looking to extend their series lead to 3-0 against Phoenix. The Thunder have been the dominant force in the West all season — Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is operating at back-to-back MVP level, averaging 31.6 points per game — and the Suns have not found an answer in either of the first two games.

Phoenix's situation is complicated further by the ongoing absence of Jalen Williams, who remains out with a hamstring injury. Williams, one of the Suns' top scorers, was hurt before the series began and has been unable to provide the secondary scoring Oklahoma City's defense was forced to stop. With the Thunder at home and SGA in full command, the Suns need a miracle performance to avoid going down 3-0.

Celtics vs. 76ers — Game 3 (7 PM ET, Prime Video)

This is the matchup with the most intrigue on the slate. Boston and Philadelphia split the first two games — the Celtics won Game 1, the 76ers answered in Game 2 — and neither team has established a clear series edge. Game 3 in Philadelphia is the first chance for either side to gain control of what has the potential to be the Eastern Conference's most entertaining first-round series.

The Sixers will have the home crowd behind them at Xfinity Mobile Arena, and Philadelphia's home crowds have been electric in this playoff push. Boston, meanwhile, has the experience and depth to win on the road. The winner of this game controls the series' narrative heading into the weekend.

Lakers vs. Rockets — Game 3 (8 PM ET, Prime Video)

The short-handed Los Angeles Lakers have defied expectations, winning both games in Houston to take a 2-0 series lead — all without Luka Doncic, who remains out with a Grade 2 hamstring injury. LeBron James has been the story, posting 28 points, 8 rebounds, and 7 assists in Game 2. Role players like Luke Kennard and Marcus Smart have stepped into the void as well.

Austin Reaves is listed as questionable for Game 3 as he recovers from an oblique strain. Kevin Durant is also questionable for Houston with an ankle issue — if Durant is limited or out, the Rockets' chances of breaking their series slide drop significantly. Los Angeles, with a 2-0 lead, will look to push Houston to the brink of elimination on the road.

What's Next

Friday's results could dramatically reshape the playoff picture. If Oklahoma City goes up 3-0, the Suns' season is effectively over. If the Lakers take a 3-0 lead on the road without Luka Doncic, it would be one of the most remarkable stories of this postseason. And in Boston-Philadelphia, the winner of Game 3 will be the heavy favorite to advance. Saturday brings more first-round action as well, with the Hawks hosting the Knicks in a pivotal Game 4. The bracket is moving fast.
