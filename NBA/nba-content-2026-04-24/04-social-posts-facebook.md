### STORY 1: McCollum Does It Again — Hawks Walk Off Knicks 109-108, Lead Series 2-1

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 9:00 AM ET

CJ McCollum has officially become the Atlanta Hawks' playoff boogeyman — and the New York Knicks simply have no answer for him.

With 12.3 seconds left on the clock and Atlanta trailing by a point, McCollum caught the ball on the wing, faded away from his defender, and drained the jumper that gave the Hawks a 109-108 victory in Game 3. Atlanta now leads the first-round series 2-1.

The Knicks had battled back from early adversity. Atlanta opened the game with an 11-0 run and three consecutive three-pointers to close the first quarter. Jalen Brunson's three-point play with 1:03 remaining gave New York a 108-105 lead — and then McCollum answered.

Jalen Johnson was Atlanta's engine all night: 24 points, 10 rebounds, and 8 assists. Jonathan Kuminga added 21 points. For New York, OG Anunoby's 29-point effort and Brunson's 26 weren't enough. Karl-Anthony Towns chipped in 21.

Game 4 is Saturday in Atlanta. The Knicks need a win or they return home staring at a 3-1 deficit.

### STORY 2: Raptors 126, Cavaliers 104 — Barnes and Barrett Drop 33 Each, Series 2-1 CLE

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 11:00 AM ET

Down 2-0 and playing in front of a home crowd that needed a reason to believe, the Toronto Raptors delivered one of the most stunning performances of the 2026 playoffs.

Scottie Barnes and RJ Barrett each scored 33 points — combining on 23-for-36 from the field and 9-for-13 from beyond the arc. And when the game was in doubt, the Raptors simply refused to stop scoring.

### STORY 3: Edwards Exits With Knee Scare, But Wolves Roll Nuggets 113-96 — Minnesota Leads 2-1

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 1:00 PM ET

Minnesota dominated Denver in Game 3, winning 113-96 to take a 2-1 series lead. But the bigger story isn't the win — it's what happened to Anthony Edwards in the fourth quarter.

Edwards was listed as questionable before tip-off with right knee maintenance, played 24 minutes and scored 17 points on 6-for-15 shooting before heading to the locker room without returning. He was visibly in discomfort. This is a continuation of a knee issue that caused him to miss 11 of the Timberwolves' final 14 regular season games.

The good news for Minnesota: the team didn't need him to seal the victory. Ayo Dosunmu came off the bench for 25 points and 9 assists. Jaden McDaniels finished with 20 points and 10 rebounds. Donte DiVincenzo added 15 points and 4 steals.

The Nuggets were the problem. Nikola Jokic reportedly shot 7-for-26 from the floor, and Minnesota outscored Denver 68-34 in the paint while holding the Nuggets to just 11 first-quarter points — a franchise postseason record. Denver built nothing against Minnesota's defense.

The full impact of Edwards' injury will be clearer before Game 4. But Minnesota has proven it can win without him at full strength.

### STORY 5: Jokic's 7-for-26 Nightmare — Nuggets Face Elimination Pressure Down 2-1

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 4:00 PM ET

It was not the Nikola Jokic anyone expected to see in the playoffs.

In Game 3, Jokic reportedly shot 7-for-26 from the field as Denver fell 113-96 and dropped to a 2-1 series deficit. It was a performance completely at odds with his regular season dominance — the three-time MVP rendered inefficient while Minnesota smothered everything Denver ran in the half-court.

It's not the first time Minnesota's defense has gotten the better of Jokic this series. In Game 2, he finished with 24 points, 15 rebounds, and 8 assists but went just 2-for-12 in the fourth quarter when the game was on the line. The Timberwolves' switching defense and physicality disrupt his rhythm in ways most teams cannot.

Denver also got outscored 68-34 in the paint — Jokic's primary domain — and allowed Minnesota to build a 27-point lead in the third quarter.

Denver's path forward is complicated further by Anthony Edwards' knee injury. If the Wolves have their full arsenal available, the Nuggets will need Jokic to find his vintage form immediately. Denver's season depends on it.
