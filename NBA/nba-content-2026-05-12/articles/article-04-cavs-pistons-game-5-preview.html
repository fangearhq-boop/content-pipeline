Cavs-Pistons Game 5 Preview: Detroit Must Respond After Mitchell's Historic Explosion

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

May 12, 2026

Twenty-four hours ago, the Detroit Pistons held a 2-1 series lead and appeared to be firmly in control of their second-round matchup with the Cleveland Cavaliers. Then Donovan Mitchell walked back out of the halftime locker room and rewrote the night — and quite possibly the series.

Mitchell's 39-point second half tied an NBA playoff record and powered Cleveland's stunning 24-0 run that swung momentum entirely. The Cavaliers won 112-103 and tied the series at 2-2. Now Game 5 heads to Little Caesars Arena in Detroit on Wednesday, May 13, with the Pistons desperately needing to reclaim their footing.

Detroit's Path Back

The Pistons are the No. 1 seed in the Eastern Conference, and this is still their arena. They have home-court advantage in this series and the talent to win a deciding game at home. Cade Cunningham has been excellent — he averaged 25 and 10 in Game 2 with a clutch fourth quarter, and he's capable of taking over a game just as Mitchell did on Monday.

Detroit's challenge isn't just responding to what happened — it's preventing it from happening again. Mitchell was held to 4 points in the first half on poor shooting. What changed at halftime? The Pistons will pour over film looking for answers. They'll need to be more physical with him in pick-and-roll coverage and limit his trips to the free-throw line, where he went 12-of-13 in the second half of Game 4.

Cleveland Riding Unreal Momentum

From Cleveland's perspective, there is no better story to ride into a road game than what just happened. The Cavaliers know they can produce 24 unanswered points with Mitchell leading the charge. They know their best player can go from ice cold to historically hot within the span of a halftime break.

Cleveland's task in Game 5 is simple in concept: play with the confidence of a team that just produced one of the great halves in playoff history, and trust that Mitchell will bring that same energy regardless of an early shooting slump.

The Stakes

The winner of Game 5 takes a 3-2 series lead and puts themselves one win from the Eastern Conference Finals, where the New York Knicks are waiting. The Knicks swept the Philadelphia 76ers in four games and have been resting while the Cavs-Pistons series has been played out. Whoever survives will then have to quickly regroup for a fresh and energized New York squad.

For the Pistons, losing Game 5 at home after squandering a 2-1 series lead would be a devastating outcome for a franchise that has worked hard to rebuild its playoff credibility. For the Cavaliers, winning in Detroit would put them one step from a third ECF appearance in recent memory.

What's Next

Game 5 tips off Wednesday, May 13 from Little Caesars Arena in Detroit. Tip time and network broadcast will be confirmed. The series winner will advance to face the New York Knicks in the Eastern Conference Finals, tentatively slated to begin May 17 or May 19 depending on how quickly the second round wraps up.
