Thunder Sweep Lakers, Advance to WCF With Perfect 8-0 Record

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

May 12, 2026

Chet Holmgren rose up and emphatically slammed home a tiebreaking dunk with 32.8 seconds left on the clock, and the Oklahoma City Thunder held on to defeat the Los Angeles Lakers 115-110 Monday night, completing a four-game sweep and advancing to the Western Conference Finals with a perfect 8-0 record in the 2026 NBA Playoffs.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander led all scorers with 35 points, and Ajay Mitchell added 28 — including 10 in a furious fourth quarter — as Oklahoma City survived the Lakers' most competitive performance of the series. The Thunder, who entered the game having won their first three by an average of 21 points, needed every last second to close this one out.

Holmgren Delivers When It Mattered Most

With the score tied and 32.8 seconds remaining, Holmgren caught a lob near the rim and threw down the decisive dunk to put Oklahoma City ahead for good. The Thunder then got to the free-throw line to seal the victory, finishing off a sweep that cements their status as the clear favorite to defend their NBA championship.

Mitchell, the Oklahoma City guard who had already impressed earlier in these playoffs, delivered in the fourth quarter when the pressure was highest. His 10 fourth-quarter points helped OKC stave off a Lakers squad that refused to quit despite trailing the series 3-0.

LeBron Fights to the End

LeBron James gave everything he had. The Lakers' all-time scoring leader scored 24 points and hauled in 14 rebounds in what may have been the final game of his remarkable 23rd NBA season. With the Lakers trailing by three and roughly 20 seconds remaining, James drove the lane and launched a banking shot that would have cut the deficit — but it bounced away, and Oklahoma City sealed the win at the free-throw line.

It was a moment that encapsulated what the 41-year-old means to the sport: still in the fight, still capable of the big play, but just a hair short in a series where his team was consistently outmatched by a younger, deeper Oklahoma City squad.

OKC's Historic Dominance

Oklahoma City is now a perfect 8-0 in the 2026 postseason, having beaten the Phoenix Suns in the first round and now dispatching the Lakers in four straight. Their margin of victory over the series — winning Game 1 108-90, Game 2 125-107, Game 3 131-108, and now Game 4 115-110 — shows a team that can win comfortably or grind it out in a close game.

SGA has been the driving force all postseason, but this Thunder team's depth is what separates it. Multiple contributors can rise on any given night — and on Monday, it was Mitchell and Holmgren who delivered when the spotlight was brightest.

What's Next

The Thunder will now wait to see who emerges from the San Antonio Spurs versus Minnesota Timberwolves series, which is tied 2-2 heading into Game 5 on Tuesday night. Oklahoma City will face the winner of that series in the Western Conference Finals, with home-court advantage firmly in OKC's favor.

As for LeBron and the Lakers — the future is uncertain. No retirement announcement has been made, but the questions about what comes next for the NBA's greatest scorer will only intensify in the days ahead.
