Mitchell's 39-Point Half Ties 1987 Record as Cavs Even Series 2-2 With 24-0 Run

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

May 12, 2026

Donovan Mitchell scored just 4 points in the first half on 1-of-8 shooting, went back to the locker room, and came out a completely different player. Mitchell's 39-point second half tied the 1987 NBA playoff record for most points in a half — set by Eric "Sleepy" Floyd of the Golden State Warriors — and powered the Cleveland Cavaliers to a 112-103 victory over the Detroit Pistons in Game 4 on Monday night, evening their second-round series at 2-2.

Mitchell finished with 43 points in total, making 12-of-18 shots in the second half, hitting 3-of-7 from three-point range, and converting 12-of-13 free throws. In a performance that will be remembered for years, he erased a halftime deficit and helped the Cavaliers go on a stunning 24-0 run that completely swung the momentum of the series.

The 24-0 Run That Changed Everything

The Cavaliers' decisive run began in the final 12 seconds of the first half and carried through the first six minutes of the third quarter. In those unforgettable few minutes, Cleveland turned what had been a competitive game — one in which the Pistons held a clear edge — into a rout. Mitchell personally accounted for 15 of those 24 unanswered Cleveland points.

For Pistons fans watching the second half unfold, it must have felt like the floor disappeared. Detroit had been in complete control, riding a 2-1 series lead with a chance to push toward the conference finals. Instead, they were outscored 24-0 over a span that bridged two quarters.

A Record With Deep History

Mitchell's 39-point second half ties the performance by Floyd on May 10, 1987, when the Warriors guard erupted against the Los Angeles Lakers in the Western Conference Semifinals. Floyd scored 39 points in the second half of that game, a record that stood unchallenged for nearly four decades before Mitchell matched it Monday night.

The historical irony is not lost: Mitchell entered the half with just 4 points — the kind of first-half performance that makes coaches nervous and fans anxious. What followed was one of the greatest individual halves in NBA playoff history.

Pistons Face a Steep Challenge

For Detroit, the result represents a stunning reversal. The Pistons had won Game 3 behind Cade Cunningham's clutch play and entered Monday with a 2-1 series lead. Cunningham — who had scored 23 and 25 points in Games 1 and 2 respectively — and the Pistons will need to find a way to reset and respond in a must-control Game 5 on Wednesday in Detroit.

The task is not impossible. The Pistons have home-court advantage as the No. 1 seed in the East, and their roster is capable of a bounce-back. But they will need to figure out how to solve Mitchell — or at the very least, make the game less dependent on one player's off night flipping into a historic performance.

What's Next

Game 5 of the Cavaliers-Pistons series is scheduled for Wednesday, May 13 at Little Caesars Arena in Detroit. With the series tied 2-2 and Cleveland riding a historic momentum wave, the next game looms as one of the most important in both franchises' recent playoff histories. The winner will hold a 3-2 series lead heading into the final stretch.

Donovan Mitchell has delivered performances before. But Game 4 of the 2026 playoffs may stand as the defining moment of his Cleveland chapter — and possibly the defining game of a second-round series that just got turned upside down.
