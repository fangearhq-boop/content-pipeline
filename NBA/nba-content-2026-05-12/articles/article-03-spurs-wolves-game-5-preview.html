Spurs-Wolves Game 5 Tonight: Wemby Returns to San Antonio With Series on the Line

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

May 12, 2026

Victor Wembanyama is heading back home, and he has something to prove. Game 5 of the San Antonio Spurs versus Minnesota Timberwolves second-round series tips off Tuesday at 8:00 PM ET on NBC and Peacock from the AT&T Center in San Antonio, with the series locked at 2-2 and everything to play for.

The last time Wembanyama was on the floor in this series, he lasted just 12 minutes and 29 seconds before being ejected for a Flagrant 2 foul — an elbow to Timberwolves forward Naz Reid's face and neck. He scored just 4 points. The Timberwolves rallied behind Anthony Edwards' 36-point explosion to win 114-109 and knot the series.

Wemby's Redemption Opportunity

Wembanyama has been one of the most dominant players in these playoffs. Before his ejection in Game 4, he had posted a 39-point, 15-rebound, 5-block performance in Game 3 that had fans reaching for the history books — joining Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Hakeem Olajuwon, and Shaquille O'Neal as the only players ever to record those numbers in a single playoff game.

Tuesday's Game 5, back at home where the AT&T Center crowd will be loud and behind him, is his chance to remind the basketball world what he's capable of when he's in full control and not in foul trouble. San Antonio is 2-0 at home this series, having won Games 1 and 3 convincingly.

Edwards Is Just as Dangerous

Anthony Edwards is the other side of the coin. The Timberwolves star scored 36 points in Game 4, including 16 in the fourth quarter, as Minnesota turned an 8-point deficit into a 5-point victory in the final 8-plus minutes. Edwards was not just effective — he was clutch, scoring when it mattered most and dragging the Wolves back from what looked like an insurmountable position.

Through four games, Edwards is averaging over 35 points per game in this series. The Spurs held him under 30 in Game 1 and dominated in Game 2, but he's risen to the level of star performance when the pressure has been highest. The Spurs' defense will need to find new answers for him Tuesday night.

The Bigger Picture

The winner of this series will face the Oklahoma City Thunder — now a perfect 8-0 after sweeping the Lakers — in the Western Conference Finals. OKC is the defending champion and the clear favorite in the West regardless of opponent, but the Spurs or Wolves will not be easy outs.

For San Antonio, this is the deepest playoff run Wembanyama has led. For Minnesota, Edwards has already proven himself a playoff performer. Both teams know Tuesday's game is the pivotal moment of their series — and potentially, their entire season.

What's Next

Game 5 tips off at 8:00 PM ET on NBC and Peacock. If the series remains alive after tonight, Game 6 is scheduled for May 15 in Minnesota, with a potential Game 7 on May 17 back in San Antonio. The Western Conference Finals cannot start until this series is resolved — and OKC is watching.
