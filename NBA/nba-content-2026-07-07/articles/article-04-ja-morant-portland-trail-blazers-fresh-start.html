Ja Morant Gets His Fresh Start in Portland — and the Trail Blazers Got Him for Almost Nothing

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | July 7, 2026

The Portland Trail Blazers just made one of the most quietly remarkable acquisitions of the 2026 offseason. They landed Ja Morant — a 26-year-old former All-Star, former MVP candidate, one of the ten most electric players in basketball when healthy — and it cost them Jerami Grant, Kris Murray, and cash. Not a single draft pick changed hands.

For Memphis, it was a necessary reset. For Portland, it might be a franchise-defining gamble that pays off in a way nobody is currently predicting.

The Morant File

The context behind Morant's trade value is worth understanding, because it's the context that made this deal possible. Over the past three seasons, Morant has appeared in just 79 games due to a combination of injuries and suspensions. His 2024-25 season was largely lost to a knee issue. His 2023-24 season involved a disciplinary suspension that cost him significant time. In between, there were flashes of the player who averaged 26 points and made the All-Star team — but not enough of them, and not consecutively enough, to override the risk calculation.

The Grizzlies chose to move on. For a franchise in transition after trading Morant's closest ally Jaren Jackson Jr. to Atlanta, the Morant era in Memphis was closing regardless. The trade to Portland at least gave them expiring contracts to work with as they continue rebuilding around their young pieces.

Portland's Calculation

The Trail Blazers have been patient. They rebuilt from scratch after the Damian Lillard era ended, accumulating cap flexibility and building through the draft without rushing toward false contention. Acquiring Morant on a contract that runs through 2027-28 — with a potential three-year, $178 million extension available — represents a calculated swing: if Morant is healthy, if the suspensions are behind him, if Portland can give him the low-pressure environment he needs to rediscover himself, they've landed a genuine star for bargain-bin assets.

That's a lot of "ifs." But Portland's roster situation — still in early rebuild mode — is exactly the kind of environment where a motivated reclamation project can thrive. There's no championship pressure. There's no scrutiny from a demanding veteran roster. There's an opportunity for Morant to remind everyone what he looked like in that 2021-22 season when Memphis won 56 games and he was a serious MVP candidate.

What's Next

Morant's Portland debut will be the most-watched moment of the Trail Blazers' preseason. The question isn't whether he still has the tools — the athleticism, the court vision, the finishing ability around the rim that was genuinely unprecedented at his position. The question is availability. If Morant can play 65 games, Portland has something real. If he plays 40, they've absorbed a worthwhile risk. Either way, the Blazers didn't give up a pick to find out. That calculus, for a rebuilding franchise, makes all the sense in the world.
