Kawhi Leonard Hires New Agent — and the Message to Toronto Is Unmistakable

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 7, 2026

Kawhi Leonard communicates in actions, not words. After seven years with the LA Clippers — a tenure marked by intermittent brilliance, persistent injuries, and the perpetual question of whether he'd ever fully recover the dominance of his peak — Kawhi is back in Toronto. The team he led to their only NBA championship in 2019. The city where 72,000 people crammed Jurassic Park to watch a Finals game on an outdoor screen.

Now comes the next action. Kawhi Leonard has hired Harrison Gaines of SLASH Sports as his new agent, specifically to handle extension negotiations with the Toronto Raptors. He is eligible for a two-year extension worth $123.7 million. And multiple reports indicate he has no interest in exploring extensions elsewhere — this is Toronto or nothing.

For Kawhi Leonard, that's a standing ovation.

The Trade That Brought Him Home

The Raptors paid significantly to bring Kawhi back when the NBA moratorium lifted Monday. Toronto sent Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, unprotected first-round picks in 2031 and 2033, second-round picks in 2030 and 2033, and a 2027 first-round swap to the LA Clippers. That's a franchise-altering package — the kind of trade a front office makes when it believes the player on the other end is worth reshaping the team's long-term future.

Toronto clearly believes Kawhi Leonard, even at this stage of his career, is worth it. The 2019 championship changed everything for the Raptors franchise, transformed how the world viewed Canadian basketball, and created memories that Raptors fans will carry for the rest of their lives. The player at the center of all of it — the one who hit the four-bounce buzzer-beater against Philadelphia, the one who averaged 28 points in the Finals — is back.

What the New Agent Signals

Hiring an agent specifically to negotiate an extension with one team is an unusual move. Players typically work through existing representation on whatever deal emerges from the market. The specificity of Kawhi's move — a new agent, brought in for a single defined purpose — says something important about the intentionality behind his return to Toronto.

He isn't testing the waters. He isn't using the Raptors as leverage to drive up offers elsewhere. He made clear, through the structure of his own agent search, that this is where he intends to finish his career or at least the next significant chapter of it. At $123.7 million over two years, the extension would give Toronto long-term certainty about their cornerstone and give Kawhi financial security that reflects his star status even at age 34.

What's Next

Extension negotiations can be finalized at any time, but there's no artificial deadline pressing either side. The Raptors want Kawhi. Kawhi wants to stay. Gaines' job is to structure the contract in a way that works for both parties within the CBA's framework. Assuming good faith — and there's every reason to believe it exists here — the extension should come together before training camp begins. When it does, Toronto will have its cornerstone locked in for the years ahead. The Klaw isn't just visiting. He's home.
