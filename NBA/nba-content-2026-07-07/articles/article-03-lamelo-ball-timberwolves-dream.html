LaMelo Ball on Joining the Timberwolves: "It's a Dream, Man"

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 7, 2026

Athletes who get traded somewhere they didn't ask to go have a script. You've heard it before: "I'm excited to be part of this organization." "I'm looking forward to the opportunity." "I'm going to give everything I have." It's polished, professional, and tells you almost nothing about how the player actually feels.

LaMelo Ball did not use the script.

"It's a Dream, Man," Ball said when asked about joining the Minnesota Timberwolves and teaming up with Anthony Edwards. Not corporate. Not guarded. Just honest and enthusiastic, from a player who could have hidden behind the standard playbook and chose not to.

The Trade That Shook Charlotte

The Minnesota Timberwolves acquired LaMelo Ball from the Charlotte Hornets when the NBA moratorium lifted Monday at 12:01 PM ET. Minnesota's return to Charlotte: Naz Reid, an unprotected 2033 first-round pick, three pick swaps in 2028, 2029, and 2030, and three second-round picks. It's a significant return for a franchise rebuilding around a young nucleus — but Minnesota was willing to pay it because LaMelo Ball, when healthy and engaged, is a top-ten talent in this league.

For Ball, the trade ends his time in Charlotte, where he spent five seasons, made two All-Star teams, and became one of the most entertaining players in the NBA. The Hornets never surrounded him with enough talent to compete, and the relationship between the franchise and its star had reportedly become strained. Minnesota is a new chapter — and if LaMelo's words are any indication, it's a chapter he's genuinely excited to start.

The Edwards Dynamic

Anthony Edwards has quietly become one of the three or four best players in the NBA. The Timberwolves' recent championship run was built on his back, and his combination of elite scoring, defensive intensity, and magnetic personality has made him one of the league's most popular stars. For LaMelo, who brings a completely different skill set — elite playmaking, gravity as a shooter, and 6-foot-7 vision from the point guard position — the pairing with Edwards on paper is one of the most exciting backcourt combinations in the league.

LaMelo also mentioned the possibility of eventually reuniting with his brother Lonzo Ball, adding another emotional dimension to his Minnesota arrival. Lonzo, who has dealt with significant knee injuries over the past few years, would need to return to full health — but LaMelo's mention of it signals that the brothers remain close and that family is woven into his thinking about his basketball future.

What's Next

The Timberwolves have already made the moves to clear space for LaMelo in their rotation, having previously traded Julius Randle to the Brooklyn Nets. Training camp is where the real work begins — the Ant-LaMelo dynamic, which looks extraordinary on paper, needs to be built into an actual functional offense. But if early enthusiasm matters, Minnesota's new co-star is as eager as any franchise could hope for. When your new star calls his new team "a Dream," that's a better start than most teams get.
