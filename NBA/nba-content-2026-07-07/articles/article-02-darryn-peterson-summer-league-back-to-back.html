Darryn Peterson's Back-to-Back 25/12 Performances Are Turning Summer League Into Must-Watch TV

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 7, 2026

Darryn Peterson wasn't supposed to make you this excited in early July. Salt Lake City Summer League is where young players audition, where rookies get their first taste of professional basketball, and where cautious observers remind themselves to keep expectations in check. Summer League stats don't translate. Every year, analysts say this.

Then Peterson goes out and posts 25 points and 12 assists. Twice. Back-to-back. Against the same opponent. With the Utah Jazz winning both games.

At some point, even the cautious observers have to admit: something is happening here.

The Stat Lines

Peterson, the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 NBA Draft, opened Salt Lake City Summer League against the Memphis Grizzlies and their No. 3 pick, Cameron Boozer. The Jazz won 109-100. Peterson finished with 25 points and 12 assists — a double-double that would be notable from any player at any level. Boozer answered with 18 points, 7 rebounds, and 4 assists in the loss.

Then came the rematch on Tuesday. Same opponent. Same Peterson. The Jazz won again, pulling away in the fourth quarter 32-25, with Peterson delivering an identical 25 points and 12 assists. Blake Hinson added 15 points in support. Memphis shot just 42 percent from the floor and 33.3 percent from three, but even a cleaner shooting night might not have been enough. Peterson was that good.

Yahoo Sports assessed him simply: "It may be just Summer League, but Darryn Peterson looks special." That's not hyperbole from a hot-take factory. That's an accurate read of what the numbers and film are showing.

What Sets Peterson Apart

The raw scoring is one thing. Rookies score in Summer League all the time, sometimes in ways that never transfer to the regular season. What's different about Peterson is the assist numbers alongside the scoring — and more specifically, the way he's creating those assists. His court vision at 19 years old has been the subject of genuine coach and analyst praise. He's not just running pick-and-roll and finding the open man. He's making reads before defenders have settled into position.

Peterson was selected by the Jazz out of a class that featured AJ Dybantsa as the consensus No. 1 pick — the Washington Wizards chose Dybantsa with the first pick, and Cameron Boozer went third to Memphis. Peterson at No. 2 was never questioned as a prospect, but the early returns from Salt Lake City are making the case that the Jazz may have gotten the best player available at any position.

What's Next

The Las Vegas Summer League is next on the calendar, and the matchup everyone wants to see is already circled: Peterson's Jazz against Dybantsa's Wizards on opening night. The No. 1 versus the No. 2 pick, in a real game, with both players having now established summer baselines. After back-to-back 25/12 performances, Peterson walks into Las Vegas as the hype story of the summer. Dybantsa will have something to say about that. For Utah Jazz fans, who have waited patiently through years of patient rebuilding, these early summer days are suddenly very interesting.
