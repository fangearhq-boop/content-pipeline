### STORY 4: Ja Morant to Portland

#### Long-Form Post — 4:00 PM ET

One year ago, the conversation around Ja Morant was whether his career was salvageable at all.

Now the Portland Trail Blazers have him — and it cost them zero draft picks.

The Memphis Grizzlies finally parted ways with their former franchise cornerstone on Monday, sending Morant to Portland in exchange for Jerami Grant, Kris Murray, and cash. No first-round picks. No second-round picks. The Blazers essentially acquired a former All-Star, a player who was a legitimate MVP candidate before injuries and suspensions derailed three consecutive seasons, for expiring contracts and money.

Morant has played just 79 games over the past three years. That context explains the trade return. But Portland, a team in an early stage of rebuilding, has the patience — and the roster situation — to absorb that risk. Give him a year in a low-pressure environment, let him rediscover his athleticism and joy for the game, and Ja Morant might remind the league exactly why Memphis once built their future around him.

He's under contract through 2027-28 and eligible for an extension that could reach $178 million over three years. Portland didn't just trade for a player. They traded for a reclamation project that could be the center of their next competitive era.

The fresh start begins now. 🌹

### STORY 5: Kawhi Leonard Hires New Agent

#### Long-Form Post — 9:30 PM ET

Kawhi Leonard doesn't say much. He never has. After seven years with the LA Clippers — a tenure defined more by injuries than championships — Kawhi is back in Toronto, the city where he became a legend, where he hit one of the most famous shots in NBA history, where 72,000 fans packed Jurassic Park to watch the Raptors finally win it all.

Now there's news. Kawhi has hired Harrison Gaines of SLASH Sports as his new agent — specifically to handle extension negotiations with the Raptors. He's eligible for a two-year, $123.7 million extension. And according to reports, Kawhi has made clear he has no interest in extending anywhere else. Toronto or bust.

This is about as close to a public statement as Kawhi Leonard gets.

The Raptors gave up a significant package to get him back: Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, unprotected first-round picks in 2031 and 2033, second-round picks in 2030 and 2033, and a 2027 first-round swap. That's a franchise-altering commitment to bringing Kawhi home.

If Kawhi signs the extension, Toronto has a legitimate cornerstone for years to come. If the basketball gods cooperate, a second championship in Toronto is not a fantasy.

Maple Leaf fans, the Klaw is home. 🦖
