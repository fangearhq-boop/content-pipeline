Jokic vs. Elimination: Denver's Season Depends on Another Miracle Tonight

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 30, 2026

Nikola Jokic has made a habit of doing the undoable. The Denver Nuggets have been backed into corners before — pressed against the wall with nothing left but one game and Jokic's will — and they have survived. Tonight, they need to survive again.

Denver trails Minnesota 3-2 after a series that has not gone according to plan. The Nuggets have lost three of their last four games, including two at home. Their bench depth has been tested by injuries, their defense has been physically bullied by a Timberwolves team that has played with extraordinary toughness, and their 3-1 lead — which they held earlier in the series — is now a 3-2 deficit with their season on the line.

Game 6 is tonight at Ball Arena in Denver. 9:30 PM ET on ESPN. The home crowd will be electric. The stakes couldn't be higher. And Jokic will have the ball.

What Jokic Did in Game 5

When the Nuggets faced elimination earlier in this very series — down 3-1 heading into Game 5 — Jokic gave one of the most complete performances of his career. He finished with 27 points, 12 rebounds, and 16 assists. Not a good game. Not a great game. A transcendent game, one where every element of his historically unique skill set was on display simultaneously. Denver won to force this Game 6.

Jamal Murray contributed 24 points that night, and Spencer Jones — stepping in for injured Aaron Gordon — added 20 off the bench. The supporting cast delivered. But the foundation of everything Denver does runs through Jokic, and it is Jokic who Minnesota must solve if they want to close this series out tonight.

Minnesota's Advantage

The Timberwolves have been relentless this series. Anthony Edwards has played with an infectious ferocity — attacking the basket, defending with length and aggression, and demanding the ball in critical moments. Minnesota's frontcourt has given Denver problems by matching Jokic's physicality with their own brand of toughness, and their wing defenders have made life difficult for Murray when he's tried to take over games.

Minnesota leads 3-2 and needs just one more win to advance to the second round. They can do it in Denver tonight, or they can force it back to Minneapolis for a potential Game 7. Either way, the Wolves have shown they have enough to win this series. They have shown it on the road and they have shown it at home.

What's Next

If Denver wins tonight, this series goes to a Game 7 in Minneapolis — and at that point, anything can happen. Jokic in a seventh game, with a season on the line, is not an opponent anyone wants to face. The Timberwolves know this, which is why they will be trying to end it Thursday rather than give Jokic one more chance.

If Minnesota wins, the Wolves advance to face either the San Antonio Spurs — who already punched their Round 2 ticket by eliminating Portland — in the Western Conference Semifinals. For Denver, a loss tonight ends one of the most turbulent playoff runs in Nuggets history. For Jokic, a win is simply the next chapter in a postseason story that keeps refusing to end.
