Rockets Win Again, Trim Lakers Lead to 3-2 — Is an Upset Brewing?

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 30, 2026

Teams that fall behind 3-1 in the playoffs almost never come back. Statistically, historically, the math is merciless. The Houston Rockets don't seem to care about statistics.

For the second consecutive night, the Rockets beat the Los Angeles Lakers — this time 99-93 in Game 5 — to trim the series deficit to 3-2. Houston has now won back-to-back games after falling into a 3-1 hole with their season on the ropes. The Lakers still lead the series and can still close things out in Game 6, but the narrative has changed completely. What felt like a Los Angeles coronation last week now feels genuinely unresolved.

LeBron James gave the Lakers everything he had. He finished with 25 points, doing his job and then some in the absence of Kevin Durant, who remains sidelined with a left ankle injury. But without Durant beside him, the supporting cast couldn't generate enough on either end of the court. The Rockets' defense was physical and disciplined, and Houston converted enough possessions to come away with the win.

Jabari Smith Jr. Seizes the Moment

With Durant out, the expectation was that Houston would struggle to find enough secondary scoring to keep pace with a Lakers team that, even without its co-star, still has LeBron in peak form. Jabari Smith Jr. answered that question emphatically. The young big man scored 22 points, was effective in the mid-range and at the rim, and provided the defensive resistance the Rockets needed to contain the Lakers' interior game.

Tari Eason added 18 and continued a run of postseason performances that have drawn attention league-wide. Eason plays with a relentless motor, and on both ends of the court, his energy has become one of Houston's defining traits in this series. Together with Smith, they gave the Rockets two legitimate scoring threats that the Lakers couldn't neutralize simultaneously.

Can Houston Complete the Comeback?

The math is still against them. But the momentum is real. The Rockets have won back-to-back games against a team that featured LeBron James. Their defense has found its identity. Their young players are performing on the biggest stage without flinching. And they play Game 6 at home in Houston, where the atmosphere will be the loudest it's been all year.

The one wildcard remains Kevin Durant's injury status. KD has not played since suffering the left ankle injury, and there has been no firm timeline for his return. If Durant is somehow available for Game 6, the dynamic shifts dramatically. If he remains out, this comes down to whether LeBron can will the Lakers to the road win that ends the series before things get desperate.

What's Next

Game 6 is Thursday in Houston. The Rockets will be loud and focused, playing with the confidence of a team that has already done what most said they couldn't. The Lakers need one win. Houston needs two.

If the Rockets win Thursday, this goes to a Game 7 in Los Angeles — and at that point, all conventional wisdom about who should win this series goes out the window. Houston has earned the right to dream about it. Whether they can finish it is the story of the week.
