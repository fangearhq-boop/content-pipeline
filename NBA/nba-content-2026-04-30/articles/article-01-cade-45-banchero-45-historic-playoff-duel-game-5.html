Cade 45, Banchero 45: The Most Historic Playoff Duel of 2026

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 30, 2026

There are playoff games you watch and forget by morning. Then there are nights that belong to history — nights you'll still be talking about a decade from now. Wednesday's Game 5 between the Detroit Pistons and Orlando Magic was the second kind.

Cade Cunningham scored 45 points in a 116-109 Detroit victory, setting a franchise record for the Pistons in the playoffs. Paolo Banchero, determined not to be outdone, also finished with 45 — going 17-of-31 from the field, making six three-pointers, and adding nine rebounds and seven assists. Two No. 1 overall picks. Two 45-point nights. One playoff game.

The last time two former No. 1 picks each scored 40 or more points in the same playoff game was the 2001 NBA Finals, when Allen Iverson and Shaquille O'Neal put on a combined scoring performance that became the stuff of legend. Cade and Banchero joined them Wednesday night in Detroit.

Cunningham's Franchise Record Night

Cade Cunningham was perfect where it mattered most. He converted all 14 of his free throw attempts, made five three-pointers, and shot 13-of-23 from the field on his way to the franchise's highest-scoring playoff performance. His 27 points in the first half alone were the most by a Pistons player in any postseason half in the play-by-play era — an astonishing mark for a game that was still anybody's ballgame at halftime.

What made Cunningham's performance more than a stat line was its timing. Detroit was staring at elimination, trailing the series 3-1 coming into Wednesday night. The Pistons — the No. 1 seed in the East — were on the verge of being bounced by an 8-seed before reaching the second round. Cunningham refused to let that happen. He was aggressive in the first quarter, relentless in the second, and then closed the game out in the fourth with the poise of someone playing the biggest game of his career because he was.

Banchero Gave Detroit Everything He Had

Paolo Banchero's 45-point night would have been the headline of almost any other playoff game. The Magic star was creative, relentless, and brilliant — 17-of-31 from the field, 6-of-11 from three-point range, and active across the stat sheet with nine rebounds, seven assists, and two steals. He scored at every level, drew contact, and repeatedly pulled the Magic back into a game where Detroit threatened to pull away.

But the Pistons held on. Detroit's depth was a factor — the team's supporting cast made enough plays around Cunningham to keep Orlando from sustaining any run. Banchero couldn't do it alone, even with one of the best individual playoff performances you'll see this postseason.

What's Next

Orlando still leads this series 3-2, and Game 6 is Friday night in Orlando. The Magic will play in front of a home crowd with a chance to advance to the second round and complete the upset of the No. 1 seed. If Banchero can replicate anything close to Wednesday's production on his home floor, the Magic will be very difficult to stop.

For Detroit, everything rides on one more Cunningham performance of this magnitude. The Pistons have shown they can match Orlando's best player point for point. The question is whether they can do it on the road, in an arena that will be rocking, with their backs against the wall. Game 6 in Orlando is the definition of must-watch basketball — because Game 7 is not guaranteed, and for one of these teams, the season ends Friday.
