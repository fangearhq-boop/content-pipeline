Thursday Triple-Header: Three Series Could End Tonight

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 30, 2026

There are nights when the NBA hands you everything at once. Thursday is one of those nights. Three series are alive. Three teams face elimination. Three games will be played, each with the potential to end a team's playoff run before midnight.

Clear your schedule. Here is what's on the line tonight.

Knicks at Hawks — 7 PM ET, ESPN (New York leads 3-2)

Jalen Brunson scored 39 points in Game 5 to give the Knicks a 3-2 lead, and New York now has a chance to advance to the second round with a win in Atlanta. The Hawks have played competitively all series and have found ways to keep games close — but Brunson has been unguardable in his best moments, and Karl-Anthony Towns has provided the complementary production that makes New York difficult to stop with two defenders.

The Hawks will need a monster performance from Trae Young and a hostile home crowd to steal this one. Atlanta has stayed in the series through individual brilliance, but the Knicks' depth and experience have been the difference. Tip-off is 7 PM ET on ESPN.

Celtics at 76ers — 8 PM ET, Peacock (Boston leads 3-2)

Joel Embiid's 33-point, 18-point-in-the-second-half performance in Game 5 kept Philadelphia alive and sent this series back to the Xfinity Mobile Arena. The Celtics still lead 3-2 and have the deeper, healthier roster — but Embiid has made clear that he is not going to go away quietly.

Jayson Tatum had 24 points and 16 rebounds in Game 5 and was not enough. Jaylen Brown added 22. That level of production typically wins games. The Celtics need to find a way to prevent Philly's fourth-quarter surges and eliminate this series before an Embiid-led Game 7 in Boston becomes a genuine nightmare. Tip-off is 8 PM ET on Peacock.

Timberwolves at Nuggets — 9:30 PM ET, ESPN (Minnesota leads 3-2)

This is the game with the highest drama potential of the three. Denver is down 3-2, playing at home, with Nikola Jokic in a mode that only shows itself when everything is on the line. In Game 5, with the Nuggets facing a 3-1 deficit and near-certain elimination, Jokic delivered 27 points, 12 rebounds, and 16 assists. He refused to let his team's season end.

Minnesota leads the series and has been exceptional — their defense has made Denver uncomfortable all series, and Anthony Edwards has played like someone who wants to be the face of this playoff run. But Jokic in an elimination game at Ball Arena, with a crowd that knows what's at stake, is one of the most formidable forces in basketball. Tip-off is 9:30 PM ET on ESPN.

What's Next

If the Knicks, Celtics, and Timberwolves all close out their series tonight, the Eastern Conference second round bracket will be set, and the Western Conference will have three of four teams determined — with only the Lakers-Rockets series still to be decided. If any of the three trailing teams steal a win, the drama extends into the weekend.

On a night like this, keep the remote close and the notifications on. The NBA playoffs have delivered remarkable individual performances and dramatic swings all first round. Tonight, three of those stories reach their potential conclusion. Don't miss a minute of it.
