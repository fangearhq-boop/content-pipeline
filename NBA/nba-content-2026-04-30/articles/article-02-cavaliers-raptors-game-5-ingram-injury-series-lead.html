Cavaliers Take 3-2 Series Lead as Raptors Lose Ingram to Injury

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 30, 2026

For four games, the Cavaliers-Raptors series had been one of the most bizarre in the first round: every single game was won by the visiting team. Cleveland won in Toronto, Toronto won in Cleveland, and neither team could defend its home floor. Game 5 on Wednesday night finally broke the streak — but not in the way Toronto was hoping.

The Cleveland Cavaliers won at home for the first time in the series, 125-120, to take a 3-2 lead. The Raptors fought back, led at halftime, and tried to rally in the fourth quarter — but a historically cold shooting performance down the stretch undid everything. Toronto was 7-of-28 from the field in the fourth quarter. Cleveland, which made 9-of-19 in the same period, seized control and never let go.

The result: the Raptors now face elimination in Game 6. And they may face it without their best player.

The Ingram Injury

Brandon Ingram left the game in the second quarter with right heel inflammation and did not return. His status for Game 6 is listed as uncertain. For a Toronto team that has been one of the most competitive upsets of the first round, losing Ingram — who has been critical to their offensive attack all series — would be a devastating blow.

Even without full Ingram production in Game 5, the Raptors got plenty from their secondary options. RJ Barrett finished with 25 points and remained aggressive throughout. Ja'Kobe Walter added 20, and Jamal Shead delivered 18 off the bench in what has become a reliable pattern of depth contributions from the Toronto roster. But depth can only go so far when a team's star is sidelined, and the Cavs are a formidable foe that punishes mistakes in the open court.

Cleveland Handles Business When It Counts

Cleveland's performance in the fourth quarter was decisive. The Cavaliers controlled the tempo, converted their opportunities, and outscored Toronto 9-to-7 from the field in the final frame while the Raptors went cold. Donovan Mitchell has been the engine for Cleveland throughout — even with Toronto throwing different coverages at him, he finds ways to make plays when the game is on the line.

The Cavaliers came into this series as the higher seed and the more experienced playoff team. After dropping two games on their home floor in Games 3 and 4, they answered in Game 5 with composure. They outran Toronto on the break, were disciplined with the ball late, and made the Raptors pay for every missed shot down the stretch.

What's Next

Game 6 is Saturday night in Toronto. The Raptors will return home to a crowd that has been their fuel all series — and Toronto fans know what's at stake. Elimination games in Canada, with the Scotiabank Arena atmosphere at full volume, are some of the most electric moments in playoff basketball.

But Brandon Ingram's health is the story between now and tip-off. If he can play, the Raptors have enough firepower to extend the series and force a Game 7 back in Cleveland. If he can't, Cleveland will carry a 3-2 lead into an already difficult road environment and will be favored to close it out. The Raptors have defied expectations all series. The question is how much they have left — and who will be on the floor to lead them.
