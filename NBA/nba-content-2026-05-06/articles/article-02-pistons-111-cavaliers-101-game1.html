Pistons Beat Cavaliers 111-101 in Game 1, Snap 12-Game Playoff Losing Streak vs. Cleveland

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 6, 2026

Cade Cunningham and Tobias Harris combined for 43 points as Detroit continued its remarkable postseason run with another commanding home victory.

The Detroit Pistons keep making history — and they're making it look easy.

Cade Cunningham scored 23 points with 7 assists, Tobias Harris added 20 points and 8 rebounds, and the Pistons beat the Cleveland Cavaliers 111-101 on Monday night in Game 1 of the Eastern Conference Semifinals. The victory snapped a 12-game playoff losing streak against Cleveland and marked Detroit's first Game 1 playoff win since May 2008, when they defeated the Orlando Magic in the Eastern Conference Semifinals.

A team that wasn't even expected to make the postseason is now two wins into the second round — and playing like a contender.

Cade Runs the Show

Cunningham was efficient and composed throughout, never forcing the action while still getting to his spots. His 23 points came in a manner that made Cleveland defenders look helpless — off the pick-and-roll, in the mid-range, and from the free throw line. His 7 assists kept Detroit's offense flowing and ensured Harris had room to operate on the weak side.

Harris, fresh off his career playoff-high 30 points in the Game 7 victory over Orlando, was just as important here. His 20-point, 8-rebound line gave the Pistons a second, reliable scoring option that the Cavaliers simply couldn't double-team without leaving someone else open.

Detroit's balanced attack — with four players scoring in double figures — made it nearly impossible for Cleveland to make any defensive adjustment stick.

Mitchell and Harden Rally Late, But Fall Short

The Cavaliers were not without fight. Donovan Mitchell scored 23 points and remained aggressive throughout, and James Harden turned in a 22-point performance that included 13 points in the fourth quarter. Cleveland made a push late, closing within single digits at one point, but Detroit held firm and didn't let the Cavs make it a one-possession game down the stretch.

The Pistons outrebounded Cleveland and limited turnovers — two areas where their opponent had struggled even during their first-round run. Detroit controlled the pace and made the Cavaliers play from behind for much of the night.

A Franchise Moment

Context matters for this one. The last time the Pistons won a playoff series against Cleveland was in 2008 — when this current core of players was in elementary school. The 12-game losing streak in the playoffs against the Cavs encapsulated years of Cleveland dominance in the rivalry.

Tuesday's win didn't just deliver a Game 1 result. It delivered a message: Detroit is not intimidated by history, by seedings, or by opponents coming off back-to-back Game 7 victories. They have won every game they've played on their home court in these playoffs.

What's Next

Game 2 is Thursday, May 7, at 7:00 PM ET at Little Caesars Arena, airing on Prime Video. The Pistons have a chance to take a commanding 2-0 series lead before the series shifts to Cleveland. For the Cavaliers, Mitchell and Harden must carry more of the offensive burden, and the team must find a way to slow down the Cunningham-Harris two-man game that dismantled their defense in Game 1.
