Luka Doncic Injury Update: ESPN Targets May 7, But Sources Point to Game 4 or Later

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 6, 2026

The Lakers star has been out since April 2 with a Grade 2 hamstring strain, and conflicting timelines are adding to the anxiety around Los Angeles' postseason hopes.

The most talked-about absence in the NBA playoffs remains just that — an absence.

Luka Doncic has not played since April 2. He is dealing with a Grade 2 left hamstring strain that has kept him out of the Los Angeles Lakers' entire first-round run and Game 1 of the Western Conference Semifinals, a 108-90 loss to the Oklahoma City Thunder on Tuesday night. As of Wednesday morning, there is still no firm timetable for his return.

ESPN updated their internal return target to as early as May 7, reflecting what sources describe as gradual but real progress. However, sources closer to the Doncic camp are more conservative, pointing to Game 4 on May 11 at Crypto.com Arena as a more realistic target based on where his recovery actually stands.

Where the Recovery Stands

The challenge with a Grade 2 hamstring strain — which involves partial tearing of the muscle fibers, unlike the milder Grade 1 — is that rushing the return creates significant risk of re-injury. Athletes who come back before the hamstring has fully healed often re-aggravate the injury, sometimes more severely.

As of this week, Doncic has progressed to light on-court work but has not yet engaged in full-speed running or one-on-one contact drills, according to multiple reports including ESPN's Shams Charania. That gap — between light movement and full basketball activity — is not a small one.

Lakers head coach JJ Redick said before Game 1 that Doncic was "not close" to returning, though the language around his status has softened slightly in recent days.

LeBron Holding the Fort

In Doncic's absence, LeBron James is doing everything in his power to keep the Lakers competitive. He scored 27 points on 12-of-17 shooting in Tuesday's Game 1 loss, adding 6 assists. But the Thunder's depth is overwhelming — Oklahoma City had four players score in double figures and won by 18 even without their own All-Star in Jalen Williams.

The Lakers' situation is precarious. Without Doncic, they lack the ball-handling, shot-creation, and playmaking to challenge a team as complete as Oklahoma City. LeBron at age 41 is still an elite player, but he cannot replicate what Doncic brings as a No. 1 option in a half-court offense.

The Stakes Are Rising

Every game the Lakers fall further behind, the pressure to play Doncic before he's fully ready intensifies. If Los Angeles drops Game 2 on Thursday — another likely outcome given the Thunder's current form — the team will face a 0-2 hole. That is a deficit from which very few teams recover in a playoff series.

The tension between medical prudence and playoff urgency is one of the most compelling subplots in the entire postseason. Push Doncic back too early and risk a potentially season-ending re-injury. Wait too long and the series slips away anyway.

What's Next

Game 2 of Thunder-Lakers is scheduled for Thursday, May 8, in Oklahoma City. If Doncic's availability for that game is ruled out, the next potential window is Game 3, May 10, at Crypto.com Arena in Los Angeles. Game 4, if necessary, would be May 11 at the same venue — the date sources close to Doncic have identified as the most realistic return target. For the Lakers' season to survive long enough for that to matter, they need a miracle or two without him first.
