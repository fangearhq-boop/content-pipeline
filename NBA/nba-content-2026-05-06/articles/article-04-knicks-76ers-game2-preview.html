Knicks vs. 76ers Game 2 Preview: Embiid Needs a Statement Performance at MSG

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 6, 2026

The 76ers were dismantled in Game 1. To avoid falling into a 0-2 hole tonight at Madison Square Garden, Joel Embiid needs to be what he was built to be.

The New York Knicks have not just been winning. They have been embarrassing people.

Their last four playoff victories have come by margins of 16, 29, 51, and 39 points. The 76ers got the 39-point treatment in Game 1, losing 137-98 on Sunday while Jalen Brunson torched them for 35 — 27 in the first half alone. Tonight, Philadelphia gets a chance to respond at Madison Square Garden, where the Knicks are 7:10 PM ET favorites on ESPN and listed as 6.5-point favorites.

The task for the 76ers is enormous. But it is not impossible — if Embiid shows up.

Embiid's Must-Perform Moment

Joel Embiid shot 3-for-11 from the field in Game 1 for just 14 points. That is not who he is. In the regular season, Embiid averaged 28.5 points and 9.1 rebounds on 52 percent shooting. He has been one of the most dominant postseason performers of his era.

If the 76ers are going to steal a game at MSG — and they must — Embiid needs to establish position early, demand the ball on the low block, and force the Knicks defense into difficult decisions. When he is getting to his spots and drawing fouls, Philadelphia becomes a completely different team. He shot 34 free throws as a team in Game 1 — that volume shows the 76ers know how to get to the line. They just weren't converting enough opportunities to matter.

The Knicks' interior defense — anchored by Mitchell Robinson at center — will present challenges. But Embiid is one of the few players in the league big enough and skilled enough to overcome Robinson's rim protection with pure post skill.

The Knicks' Relentless Machine

New York's dominance goes beyond Brunson. OG Anunoby, Mikal Bridges, and the entire defensive rotation give the Knicks one of the most disruptive defensive identities in the league. The 76ers turned the ball over 19 times in Game 1 and shot just 41 percent from the field.

Brunson is playing the best basketball of his career. His combination of pull-up shooting, floater game, and playmaking has the Knicks operating with incredible efficiency. He does not need 35 every night — the supporting cast takes enough pressure off him that a 22-point, 8-assist performance is just as dangerous.

Madison Square Garden on a playoff night is also a factor that cannot be understated. The atmosphere there has rattled visiting teams all postseason, and Philadelphia will need to block out the noise from the opening tip.

What's Next

Tonight's game tips off at 7:10 PM ET at Madison Square Garden on ESPN. If the Knicks go up 2-0, the series shifts to Philadelphia for Games 3 and 4 on Friday, May 8, and Sunday, May 10 — but with the 76ers already playing from a significant deficit. A loss tonight would put Philadelphia in an extremely difficult position given how dominant New York has looked throughout the postseason. Everything Philadelphia has worked toward this season comes down to how Embiid responds at MSG.
