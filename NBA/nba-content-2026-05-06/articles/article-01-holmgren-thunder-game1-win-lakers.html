Holmgren's 24/12/3 Carries Thunder to Game 1 Win Over Shorthanded Lakers

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 6, 2026

Oklahoma City's big man stepped into the spotlight Tuesday with a dominant double-double, proving the Thunder are deep enough to win even when Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has an off night.

The Oklahoma City Thunder didn't need their MVP to be at his best Tuesday night. They had Chet Holmgren instead.

Holmgren posted 24 points, 12 rebounds, and 3 blocks in 31 minutes as the Thunder beat the Los Angeles Lakers 108-90 in Game 1 of the Western Conference Semifinals, improving to 5-0 in the 2026 playoffs without Jalen Williams — who missed his third consecutive game with a Grade 1 left hamstring strain — and facing a Lakers team missing its own star in Luka Doncic.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander contributed 18 points and 6 assists in what the NBA itself called a "rare off night" for the reigning MVP candidate. On most nights, 18 and 6 from SGA would be the headline. Tuesday, it barely registered.

Holmgren Dominates on Both Ends

Holmgren was a problem from the opening tip. He shot 9-of-17 from the field and converted all four of his free throw attempts, finishing the night one assist short of a triple-double scare. His three blocks disrupted multiple Lakers possessions in the paint, and his 12 rebounds — a playoff-season high — gave Oklahoma City extra possessions at key moments in the second half.

Ajay Mitchell added 18 points off the bench to round out Oklahoma City's balanced attack. The Thunder had four players score in double figures — a theme of this postseason run — and their defense held the Lakers to just 90 points, their lowest total of the playoffs.

LeBron Fights, But Lakers Fall Short

LeBron James did everything within his power to keep Los Angeles competitive. He scored 27 points on an efficient 12-of-17 from the field and added 6 assists, but without Doncic — out since April 2 with a Grade 2 left hamstring strain — the supporting cast simply couldn't keep up with Oklahoma City's depth.

The Lakers trailed for the final three quarters after getting off to a fast start. The Thunder eventually pulled away in the third period and never looked back, winning by 18 despite SGA's quiet-by-his-standards performance.

Doncic's return timeline remains unclear. ESPN listed May 7 as a possible target, but sources closer to the situation point to Game 4 on May 11 as more realistic. He has not yet begun full-speed running or contact drills.

Thunder Depth Is Simply Unfair

The frightening part for Western Conference opponents is what the Thunder look like at full strength. Oklahoma City's net rating with Jalen Williams on the bench during the regular season was plus-10.9 — meaning even in a worst-case injury scenario, this roster outperforms most teams in the league.

They entered the series as 15.5-point favorites for Game 1. They covered. They are 5-0 in the 2026 playoffs, having not dropped a game since the regular season ended.

No other team in either conference can match their combination of MVP-caliber star play and 10-deep roster construction.

What's Next

Game 2 of Thunder-Lakers is scheduled for Thursday, May 8, in Oklahoma City. The Lakers face growing urgency with each passing game Doncic misses. If Los Angeles falls to 0-2, the pressure to return the star point guard — perhaps before he's fully ready — will become immense.

For Oklahoma City, the blueprint is clear: stay disciplined, keep rotating defenders, and trust the depth. It has worked for five straight games, and with Holmgren playing like this, there's little reason to expect it to stop now.
