NBA Draft 2026: Wizards "Torn" Between Dybantsa and Peterson With Two Days to Go

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 21, 2026

For months, the NBA Draft's No. 1 pick felt settled. AJ Dybantsa was going to Washington. Darryn Peterson was going to Utah. The Wizards were comfortable, the oddsmakers agreed, and the rest of the league had moved on to debating who would fall to which contender in the mid-lottery.

Two days before the draft, none of that is true anymore.

The Washington Wizards have reportedly given "increased consideration" to selecting Peterson over Dybantsa with the top pick, with ESPN insiders characterizing the decision as a "tough call." The consensus has cracked at exactly the wrong time for everyone who thought this was resolved.

Peterson's Chess Move

The story behind the story is tactical. Darryn Peterson, the Kansas freshman who averaged 20.2 points per game in a season interrupted by 11 missed games due to cramping issues, made a bold pre-draft decision: he visited only the Washington Wizards and refused meetings with every other team. In a draft cycle where prospects typically work the circuit — visiting teams, running through workouts, creating bidding wars — Peterson's single-destination strategy was a direct message to Washington's front office.

The message: take me first, or I'm not available. Peterson isn't pretending he wants to fall to No. 2 or No. 3. He's putting himself in position to be the top pick and betting on his relationship with the only team that matters right now. According to reports, Shams Charania confirmed only the Wizards were granted access to visit with Peterson, reinforcing the exclusivity of the arrangement.

Whether that gambit helps or hurts his chances is the question plaguing draft analysts. Some see it as confidence and clarity. Others wonder if a player with a question-mark season and injury concerns can afford to be so selective heading into draft night.

Dybantsa's Counter: Confidence Over Workouts

AJ Dybantsa took the opposite approach — and was just as deliberate about it. The BYU standout revealed this week that he didn't work out for a single franchise before the draft. In his own words: "I didn't work out for anyone; I just visited the cities. Take me to the facility, take me to dinner, talk. I don't gotta work out. They know what I can do."

That confidence is not entirely without foundation. Dybantsa's freshman season at BYU — 25.5 points, 6.8 rebounds, and 3.7 assists per game on 51% shooting in 35 games — stands as one of the most statistically dominant college freshman performances in recent memory. He was a consensus All-American and drew comparisons to Larry Bird based on his point-per-game and field-goal efficiency combination. The argument for Dybantsa is simple: the tape is the workout.

The argument against him at No. 1 is less obvious but real: scouts want to see Dybantsa compete in a controlled setting, gauge his conditioning and coachability, and probe for weaknesses. By declining workouts entirely, he handed the Wizards less information than they might have wanted two days before making the most consequential selection in franchise history.

What Washington Is Weighing

Washington's front office faces a genuine dilemma. Dybantsa offers the higher ceiling by most measures — a versatile wing who can score from anywhere on the floor and project as a multi-year All-Star. Peterson offers elite athleticism and defensive potential, but comes with the baggage of an interrupted season and health questions his camp has worked to explain away.

The Wizards already have Trae Young and Kyle Kuzma on the roster. The No. 1 pick will be expected to anchor a long-term rebuild alongside those veterans. Both Peterson and Dybantsa fit that timeline — the question is which one fits the roster construction better, and which front office trusts more.

As of Sunday evening, the answer remains genuinely unknown. The draft is Tuesday. The Wizards will pick first.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Draft kicks off Thursday, June 23, at 8:00 PM ET at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. The Wizards hold the first and most scrutinized pick of the night. Cameron Boozer, the third major prospect in the class (23 PPG, 11 RPG, 4 APG at Duke), is projected to fall no further than No. 3 to the Memphis Grizzlies regardless of how the Dybantsa-Peterson decision shakes out. Expect front-office leaks and insider reports through Monday and Tuesday as teams finalize their boards and the Wizards' decision becomes clear.
