Austin Reaves Free Agency: Lakers in Exclusive Window With 9 Days to Decide

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 21, 2026

The clock on Austin Reaves' future in Los Angeles is running — and the Lakers are making the most of the time they have.

Los Angeles holds an exclusive negotiating window with Reaves before the NBA's free agency period officially opens on June 30. Reaves must formally decline his $14.9 million player option by June 29 to enter the market. By all accounts, that's what he'll do — turning down the guaranteed money to bet on himself in what figures to be a significant payday.

The Offer Gap

The financial difference between the Lakers and their competition is substantial. Los Angeles can present Reaves with a five-year maximum contract worth $239 million — a number that reflects the new collective bargaining agreement's supermax provisions for players of his caliber. The Brooklyn Nets, currently considered the most credible alternative suitor, are limited to a four-year deal worth approximately $178.5 million.

That gap — roughly $60 million over the length of the contracts — would seem to make the decision straightforward. But multiple league insiders caution that simplifying this to dollars misses the point. Sources close to the situation say Reaves' decision "will not come down solely to a dollar figure," a phrase that has appeared in multiple credible outlets covering the negotiations.

The Intangibles

The case for Reaves staying in Los Angeles goes well beyond the financial advantage. Having averaged 23.3 points per game last season, Reaves has proven himself as a legitimate first option — but he's also built an elite partnership alongside Luka Doncic, the generational talent the Lakers acquired to anchor their championship window. The chemistry between Reaves and Doncic on the court is visible; the trust they've developed as teammates doesn't rebuild overnight in a new city.

Beyond basketball fit, Reaves has been open about his comfort in Los Angeles. He's one of the team's most marketable players, beloved by the Laker faithful, and operating in the largest market in professional basketball. Those factors carry real weight for a player in his prime years.

The Nets, for their part, represent a legitimate change-of-scenery alternative. Brooklyn is in the early stages of a rebuild and would position Reaves as the unquestioned centerpiece of their franchise — a different kind of appeal than complementing an established star.

What the League Expects

Despite the headline-grabbing contract disparity and Nets interest, the prevailing sentiment around the league is that Reaves stays in Los Angeles. Multiple front-office sources from competing teams — the same people who would benefit from Reaves leaving — still predict a Lakers reunion. "The only question is the final number," one source told NBC Sports, suggesting negotiations will focus on the precise contract structure rather than the destination itself.

Minnesota is also said to be monitoring the situation as a contingency plan, but has not emerged as a serious threat to either the Lakers or Nets for Reaves' services.

What's Next

Austin Reaves has until June 29 to formally decline his player option and enter free agency. Free agency opens league-wide on June 30 at 6:00 PM ET. The Lakers' exclusive window ends when the market opens, meaning Brooklyn and any other interested teams can make formal offers beginning June 30. Expect the Reaves decision to come quickly after free agency opens — this isn't a player who typically lets situations drag. By July 4, the picture should be clear.
