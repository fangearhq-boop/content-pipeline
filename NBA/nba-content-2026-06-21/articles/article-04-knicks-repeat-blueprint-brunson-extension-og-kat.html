Knicks' Repeat Blueprint: Brunson Extension, OG, KAT, and a No. 17 Pick

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 21, 2026

A week after the last pieces of confetti settled on Broadway, the New York Knicks are pivoting from celebration to construction. The 2026 NBA championship is theirs — the franchise's first since 1973, won in five games over the San Antonio Spurs with Jalen Brunson earning unanimous Finals MVP honors. The parade through Canyon of Heroes is a memory. The offseason is the present.

And the Knicks' offseason to-do list is not short.

With the franchise officially out of the Giannis Antetokounmpo sweepstakes and two days until the NBA Draft, New York's front office is executing on a compressed but clear offseason strategy built around three pillars.

Pillar One: The Brunson Extension

Jalen Brunson's next contract will be the most consequential piece of business the Knicks conduct this offseason. The 30-year-old point guard is the engine of this franchise — and the story of how he got here only deepens the emotional weight of getting the extension right.

In 2022, Brunson made the unconventional decision to sign a four-year deal worth significantly below his market value to give New York the cap flexibility needed to build around him. That selflessness paid off in a championship ring this June. His next contract will carry no such discount — and it shouldn't. Brunson's performance on and off the court has earned him every dollar of a maximum extension, and the Knicks are expected to make that deal a priority before free agency opens June 30.

Pillar Two: OG Anunoby and Karl-Anthony Towns

The two other critical re-signings define whether the Knicks can seriously threaten a repeat or simply hope lightning strikes twice. OG Anunoby's two-way ability — his elite defense paired with a reliable outside shot — gives New York a wing the championship core cannot replace cheaply on the open market. His tip-in to win Game 4 of the Finals after a 29-point Knicks comeback will live in franchise lore forever.

Karl-Anthony Towns, at center, provides the rim protection and interior scoring the Knicks need to remain one of the conference's premier offenses. Re-signing Towns comes with luxury tax implications that will test ownership's commitment to the championship window — but there's every indication the Dolan family is willing to pay what it takes.

Both players are expected to receive maximum offers from the Knicks before free agency opens, though the final cap math will require careful maneuvering.

Pillar Three: The No. 17 Pick

Thursday's NBA Draft brings the Knicks to the table with the No. 17 overall selection — a pick that, in isolation, might seem peripheral amid the extension negotiations and title defense. But smart franchises understand that depth picks are championship insurance. The Knicks need a player who can develop into a reliable rotation piece within two years, either as a starter backup or a specialist. At No. 17, the value is real; the question is identification.

What's Next

The NBA Draft is Thursday, June 23, at Barclays Center — two days away. The Knicks pick at No. 17 in the first round. Free agency opens June 30, when Brunson's extension window and the re-signing conversations with OG and Towns go into overdrive. If the Knicks execute on all three pillars, they enter next season as legitimate back-to-back contenders. If the roster fractures — even in small ways — the Eastern Conference will come after New York with everything it has. The work starts now.
