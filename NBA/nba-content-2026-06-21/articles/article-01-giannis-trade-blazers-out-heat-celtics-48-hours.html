Giannis Trade Endgame: Blazers Out, Heat vs. Celtics With 48 Hours to Go

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 21, 2026

The Giannis Antetokounmpo sweepstakes just got simpler — and more urgent. Two days before the NBA Draft convenes at Barclays Center in Brooklyn, the Portland Trail Blazers have been officially ruled out as a likely destination for the two-time MVP, leaving exactly two franchises standing: the Miami Heat and the Boston Celtics.

Marc Stein has reported that a trade is "drawing near," and sources across multiple outlets confirm the momentum remains with Miami. The Heat's package — Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., and the No. 13 pick in Thursday's draft — has been on the Bucks' table for weeks. Boston counters with Jaylen Brown and a collection of draft capital. Both packages represent significant franchise-altering moves for Milwaukee regardless of which deal gets done.

What Giannis Wants

The most important factor isn't the asset value — it's Antetokounmpo's willingness to commit long-term. Sources have indicated Giannis would agree to a long-term extension with either Miami or Boston, removing the "one-year rental" concern that typically depresses a star's trade value. That assurance gives both the Heat and Celtics significant leverage in their pitches: they're not just buying one more crack at a championship, they're building around a 31-year-old who still has years at an elite level.

The Celtics' pitch centers on roster fit. Adding Giannis alongside their existing core creates one of the most formidable frontlines in modern NBA history. Miami's appeal leans on culture, climate, and Pat Riley's proven ability to attract and retain stars. Both are compelling arguments. The Bucks, for their part, are not rushing toward one side.

Milwaukee's Additional Angle

While the Giannis drama dominates the narrative, Milwaukee is working a separate angle that could define what the post-Giannis Bucks look like. According to multiple reports, the Bucks are pursuing an additional first-round pick on top of their existing No. 10 selection, and have signaled to rival front offices that they would like to trade up in the lottery if possible.

That pursuit of extra draft capital reflects a Bucks organization thinking beyond Thursday night. The rebuild begins the moment Giannis lands elsewhere, and Milwaukee wants as many high-upside assets as possible to begin that process. The combination of a Giannis trade return — which would already include significant talent and picks — plus additional lottery selections gives them a fighting chance to remain competitive sooner than most torn-down franchises.

Heat vs. Celtics: The Final Accounting

Breaking down the two remaining packages honestly reveals why this decision has taken so long. Miami's offer is headlined by Tyler Herro, a proven scorer averaging more than 20 points per game, but Herro has never won a championship and the Heat's supporting cast raises questions about immediate contention. The addition of the No. 13 pick gives Milwaukee another potential building block, but it won't protect itself in terms of draft-pick strength.

Boston's Jaylen Brown-centered offer is arguably the higher-floor basketball asset. Brown is a proven Finals performer, a two-way wing who fits the modern NBA template. The Celtics' offer also comes with draft capital attached. The tradeoff for Milwaukee: Boston is an established competitor, meaning the picks coming back won't be lottery selections anytime soon.

The Bucks have a decision that has no wrong answer — only different paths forward. Draft night starts the clock on what comes next.

What's Next

The NBA Draft begins Thursday, June 23, at 8:00 PM ET at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. All indications point toward the Giannis trade being resolved on or around draft night to allow the receiving team to maximize its first-round picks. If a deal closes before picks are made, it could trigger significant reshuffling across the entire top 15. Watch for Thursday evening announcements from Milwaukee, Miami, or Boston in the hours leading up to tip-off. The 48-hour window has begun.
