Ja Morant Trade Market: Memphis Is Waiting on Giannis, and Tuesday Night Changes Everything

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 21, 2026

The biggest trade in the NBA offseason hasn't happened yet — and it might not be the Giannis deal everyone is watching.

While the Milwaukee Bucks and their suitors finalize a Giannis Antetokounmpo trade ahead of Tuesday's NBA Draft, another earthquake is loading in Memphis. The Grizzlies are prepared to listen to serious offers for Ja Morant, but they've made a deliberate organizational decision: no meaningful talks until the Giannis situation resolves on draft night.

The strategy is not passive. It is calculated.

Why Memphis Is Waiting

The teams most seriously interested in Morant — led by the Miami Heat — are the same franchises currently chasing Giannis. Memphis understands this. By forcing interested parties to make their Giannis decision first, the Grizzlies avoid two failure scenarios: losing their top trade asset in a deal only to watch the buyer pivot to a different marquee target, or being undersold because a team split its attention between two major acquisitions.

Morant averaged 24.4 points per game last season. At 26 years old, he remains one of the most explosive point guards in the league when healthy. Memphis trading him is about rebuilding around youth — they also hold the No. 3 pick in Thursday's draft — not discarding damaged goods. The Grizzlies want full market value, and full market value requires a focused buyer.

The Miami Heat's Plan B

For the Heat, Ja Morant isn't second prize — he's a legitimate franchise-altering swing. If Giannis chooses Boston or stays in Milwaukee, Miami will pivot quickly. The fit alongside Bam Adebayo is a natural one: a rim-running center who finishes above the break paired with the most athletic lead guard in the Eastern Conference. Pat Riley has never been afraid to build around players who carry uncertainty, and Morant's off-court past doesn't appear to be a dealbreaker for the Heat's front office.

Minnesota's interest adds competitive pressure to the Morant market. The Timberwolves have been mentioned as a team monitoring the situation, with Morant potentially fitting alongside their young core. More bidders mean more leverage for Memphis — exactly the position they want to be in when talks begin in earnest.

The No. 3 Pick Wild Card

Memphis holds the No. 3 selection in Thursday's draft. Cameron Boozer — the Duke freshman who averaged 23 points and 11 rebounds per game — is widely projected to land there regardless of how the Dybantsa-Peterson debate at No. 1 resolves. That pick represents a decision point for Memphis: take Boozer and pair him with Morant as a short-term competitive piece, or use the pick as currency in a Morant blockbuster trade.

The second scenario is the more likely one given the organizational direction signals Memphis has sent. A Morant trade package that includes the No. 3 pick would be one of the richest asset combinations to hit the market in years.

What's Next

Draft night is Tuesday, June 23. Once Giannis' destination is announced — which all parties expect to happen around or during the first round — the Morant market opens. Teams that missed on Giannis will have clarity on their direction and budget. Teams that never entered the Giannis race will have been watching Memphis, waiting to call. The 48 hours after Thursday's draft could be the most active stretch of NBA trade activity since the blockbuster deadline deals of 2024. Morant's next chapter begins when Giannis' is settled.
