Twenty-seven years later, Rick's son Jalen is leading the Knicks back to the NBA Finals. Same franchise. Same opponent.

The story writes itself. Jalen Brunson grew up watching his father play professional basketball. He watched those 1999 Knicks fight through the lockout-shortened season to reach the Finals against Duncan and the Spurs. Now, as a franchise cornerstone and ECF MVP, Jalen has a chance to do something his father could not — win the championship against San Antonio.

For long-suffering Knicks fans, this Finals has layers upon layers. Madison Square Garden has not hosted a Finals game since 1999. The city has waited through 27 years of heartbreak and rebuilds.

Now they're back. And the question on every New York fan's mind: Can Jalen finish what his father started?
