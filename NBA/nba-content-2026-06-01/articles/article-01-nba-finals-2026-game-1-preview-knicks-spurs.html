NBA Finals 2026 Game 1 Preview: Knicks vs. Spurs Tips Off Wednesday in San Antonio

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 1, 2026

The wait is almost over. The 2026 NBA Finals begin Wednesday, June 3, when the New York Knicks travel to San Antonio to face the Spurs at the AT&T Center (8:30 PM ET, ABC). It is, remarkably, the same matchup from 1999 — but almost everything else about this series is different.

The Spurs are -205 series favorites, backed by a 62-20 regular season record and home-court advantage throughout. San Antonio has not been to the championship round since 2014. The franchise returns now with Victor Wembanyama and a supporting cast built around young guards Stephon Castle and Dylan Harper.

The Knicks arrive riding one of the most dominant postseason runs in NBA history. New York has won 11 straight playoff games, accumulating a +271 total point differential and a +19.8 net rating — the best in postseason history. They swept the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Finals and have not played a close game in more than a month.

The Full Finals Schedule

Game 1 tips off Wednesday, June 3, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC from the AT&T Center in San Antonio. Game 2 follows Friday, June 5, at the same venue. The series shifts to Madison Square Garden for Game 3 on Monday, June 8, and Game 4 on Wednesday, June 10. If needed, Game 5 is scheduled for Saturday, June 13. All games air on ABC at 8:30 PM ET.

How the Spurs Got Here

San Antonio's path was not easy. The Spurs survived a first-round scare in which Wembanyama briefly entered concussion protocol, then navigated a grueling Western Conference Finals against the Oklahoma City Thunder that went the distance. They fell behind 3-2 before winning Games 6 and 7, with Wembanyama posting 28 points and 10 rebounds in the clinching Game 6 blowout and being named WCF MVP.

San Antonio's 62-20 regular season record gives them home-court advantage over the Knicks' 53-29 mark. The Spurs are deep, well-coached, and playing their best basketball of the year.

How the Knicks Got Here

New York's postseason has been one long highlight reel. Jalen Brunson has averaged 26.9 points per game across 14 playoff games, ranking third among all postseason scorers. Karl-Anthony Towns has been the interior force. Mikal Bridges has been the stopper. OG Anunoby and Josh Hart provide the grit and versatility that define this team.

The Knicks swept the Cavaliers in the ECF, winning Game 4 by 37 points. Brunson earned ECF MVP honors. The team has not trailed in a series since their first-round matchup.

What's Next

Game 1 on Wednesday sets the tone for what figures to be a memorable series. The Spurs have the home crowd, the star power, and the defensive infrastructure anchored by Wembanyama. The Knicks have the momentum, the historical streak, and the desire of a city that has waited 27 years for this moment.

Do not miss Game 1. Wednesday, June 3, 8:30 PM ET on ABC.
