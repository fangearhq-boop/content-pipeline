The Knicks Are Having the Most Dominant Postseason Run in NBA History

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 1, 2026

There are dominant postseason runs. There are historically dominant postseason runs. And then there is what the New York Knicks have done in the 2026 NBA playoffs.

Through 11 consecutive playoff victories, the Knicks have accumulated a total point differential of +271 and a net rating of +19.8 — the most dominant point differential in NBA postseason history. They have not played a close game in over a month. They are averaging a 23.8-point margin of victory. Over 11 games. In the playoffs.

The Numbers in Full

Championship teams typically post net ratings around plus-10 to plus-14 during dominant playoff runs. The Knicks, through 11 games, are operating at a level that surpasses those benchmarks by a historically wide margin.

Jalen Brunson, the engine of this machine, has averaged 26.9 points per game across 14 playoff appearances. He earned ECF MVP honors. Karl-Anthony Towns has been a double-double force. Mikal Bridges has been the defensive stopper and secondary scorer. OG Anunoby and Josh Hart provide the physicality and IQ that define the Knicks' identity.

How Did They Get Here?

The Knicks swept the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Finals, winning Game 4 by 37 points. They had outscored opponents in every round, building the historic differential game by game without a single slip. This is not a one-man show — New York has scoring threats at every position and depth that allows them to impose their style regardless of opponent.

Can the Spurs End the Run?

That is the central question heading into the Finals. The Spurs have Victor Wembanyama — the unanimous DPOY — waiting to disrupt whatever New York tries to do in the paint. San Antonio's layered defense, featuring Castle and Harper as frontline guard defenders, is specifically built to challenge teams like the Knicks. The Spurs went 62-20 in the regular season and are -205 series favorites.

Eleven straight wins is extraordinary. But the Knicks have not yet faced a defense like San Antonio's, anchored by a 7-foot-4 rim protector who changes the geometry of the paint on every possession.

What's Next

Game 1 tips off Wednesday, June 3, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC in San Antonio. The Knicks will carry their 11-game streak and historic point differential into the AT&T Center. Whether this run continues or finally meets its match, the Knicks have already written themselves into the history books. The question now is whether they can write one more chapter: a championship.
