The 1999 Rematch: How the Knicks and Spurs Found Each Other Again, 27 Years Later

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 1, 2026

In the summer of 1999, the San Antonio Spurs and New York Knicks met in the NBA Finals. Tim Duncan was cementing his legacy. The Spurs won 4-1, and the result seemed, at the time, inevitable. What did not seem inevitable was that these two franchises would find each other again in the championship round, 27 years later — with the son of a player from those 1999 Knicks now leading New York's charge for redemption.

Rick Brunson and the 1999 Knicks

Rick Brunson was a journeyman guard on those 1999 Knicks. He was part of a team that made an improbable run to the Finals — the eighth-seeded Knicks becoming the first and only eight-seed to reach the championship round in NBA history, defeating the Indiana Pacers in a memorable Eastern Conference Finals.

The Spurs were too much. Duncan's dominance was overpowering. San Antonio took the series 4-1 and earned the first championship in franchise history.

Rick Brunson's son Jalen was born in 1996 and grew up watching his father play basketball. He understood what the franchise meant to New York.

Jalen Brings It Full Circle

Now Jalen Brunson is a franchise cornerstone, an ECF MVP, and the best player on the Knicks in decades. Through 14 playoff games in 2026, he is averaging 26.9 points per game. He is the engine of an offense that has posted the best point differential in playoff history through 11 consecutive wins.

And his opponent in the Finals? The San Antonio Spurs. The same franchise that defeated his father's team 27 years ago. The storyline is almost too perfect to be real.

A City's Long Wait

For Knicks fans, the past 27 years have been defined by near-misses, disappointments, and the constant reminder that 1999 was the last time their team competed for a championship. Madison Square Garden has not hosted a Finals game since that series. The city has waited through multiple rebuilds, draft busts, and coaching carousels.

Now they are back. The Garden will host Games 3 and 4 (June 8 and June 10), and the noise inside the building figures to be unlike anything the arena has produced in a generation.

What's Next

The rematch begins June 3 in San Antonio. The Spurs, now led by Victor Wembanyama rather than Tim Duncan, still have home-court advantage and are still the favorites. The Knicks, now led by Jalen Brunson, enter with an 11-game win streak and the belief that the story ends differently this time.

Game 1 is Wednesday. The Brunson family narrative continues.
