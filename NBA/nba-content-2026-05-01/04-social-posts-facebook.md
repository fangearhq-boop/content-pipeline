### STORY 5: Anthony Edwards Injury Analysis

**Long-Form Post (11:00 AM ET)**

The Timberwolves may be celebrating their second-round berth, but a major question looms over their next series: when will Anthony Edwards return?

Edwards suffered a left knee hyperextension and bone bruise in Game 4 against the Nuggets. The team listed him week-to-week, and he missed both Games 5 and 6. Despite his absence — along with three other key rotation players — Minnesota won both games to close out Denver.

A bone bruise with hyperextension is one of the more unpredictable injuries in basketball. The encouraging news is there appears to be no structural ligament damage, which would be a far more serious issue. With rest and treatment, the hyperextension component often resolves within 1-2 weeks. The bone bruise typically takes a bit longer and causes lingering soreness even after swelling subsides.

The Wolves will face San Antonio in the Western Semis. Victor Wembanyama and De'Aaron Fox are rested and ready. Minnesota proved this week that they can compete without Edwards — Jaden McDaniels (32 pts) and Terrence Shannon Jr. (24 pts) filled the void brilliantly. But getting Ant back transforms this team from a competitive Round 2 participant to a legitimate Western Conference Finals threat.

The timeline remains unclear. Watch for official practice reports as the series approaches.
