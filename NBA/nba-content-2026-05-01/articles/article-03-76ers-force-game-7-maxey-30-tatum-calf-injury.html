Maxey 30 Forces Game 7 as Tatum Exits with Calf — 76ers Top Celtics 106-93

By Jake Torres | May 1, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Tyrese Maxey refused to let Philadelphia die.

Behind Maxey's 30-point masterclass and a near triple-double from Joel Embiid, the Philadelphia 76ers defeated the Boston Celtics 106-93 in Game 6 on Thursday night, forcing a winner-take-all Game 7 this Saturday at TD Garden. The victory is all the more significant given where this series stood just days ago — the 76ers were staring down the barrel of a 3-1 deficit and nearly universal elimination predictions.

Maxey Takes Over

Maxey was at his dynamic best, finishing with 30 points on 11-for-22 shooting. He was especially lethal in the paint, going 5-for-6 at the basket while also shooting 3-for-5 from three-point range and converting all 5 of his free throw attempts. His ability to create at multiple levels gave Boston's defense no clean answers.

"He's the engine of this team," said Embiid, who nearly recorded a triple-double with 19 points, 10 rebounds, and 8 assists. Paul George added 23 points as Philadelphia was firing from every angle.

The Sixers dominated the fourth quarter, a pattern that has emerged throughout this series as Philadelphia has repeatedly refused to buckle when the pressure is highest.

The Tatum Situation

Boston's night was complicated by the departure of Jayson Tatum in the third quarter. The Celtics' superstar exited with left calf discomfort, visibly favoring his leg on the way to the locker room as Philadelphia was building its lead. Tatum finished with 17 points before the exit.

Post-game, Tatum sought to downplay concerns. "I wasn't overly concerned," he said, describing the issue as tightness rather than anything structural. He clarified it was his left leg — not the leg he dealt with issues in last postseason — and indicated he expects to play in Game 7.

Jaylen Brown was hampered by foul trouble, picking up three fouls in the first half and finishing with 18 points. Boston's star duo managing health heading into Saturday is now the dominant subplot.

A Series for the Ages

What has made this 76ers-Celtics series so compelling is the momentum swings. Boston controlled Games 1 through 4, but Philadelphia has now won back-to-back games with Embiid clearly gaining his legs under him as he continues working back from an appendectomy he had earlier in the postseason. Embiid's near-triple-double in Game 6 was his best performance of the series.

The storylines heading into Game 7 are almost too rich: Tatum's calf vs. Embiid's recovery from surgery. A two-time defending champion trying to close out vs. a franchise in pursuit of its first title in decades. Home-court advantage in front of the TD Garden faithful vs. the Sixers' relentless belief.

What's Next

Game 7 is Saturday at 7:30 PM ET at TD Garden in Boston. Jayson Tatum's status is the most pressing question heading into one of the most anticipated winner-take-all games in recent playoff memory. For the 76ers, winning two consecutive on the road to force a Game 7 has already been an achievement — now they need one more in Boston's building.
