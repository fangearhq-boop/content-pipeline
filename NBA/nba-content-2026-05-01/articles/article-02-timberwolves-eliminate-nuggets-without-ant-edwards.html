Wolves Eliminate Nuggets Without Ant: McDaniels 32, Shannon 24 Fuel 110-98 Game 6 Win

By Damon Pierce | May 1, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

They were already missing their franchise player. Then three more rotation contributors went down. Most teams would have crumbled. The Minnesota Timberwolves won.

Without Anthony Edwards (left knee), Donte DiVincenzo (Achilles), Ayo Dosunmu (calf), and Kyle Anderson (illness), the Timberwolves defeated the Denver Nuggets 110-98 in Game 6 on Thursday night to win the series 4-2. It is the second time in three seasons that Minnesota has eliminated Denver from the playoffs — and perhaps their most remarkable win of the bunch.

McDaniels and Shannon Fill the Void

Jaden McDaniels was extraordinary. The forward filled the offensive void left by Edwards with 32 points, 4 rebounds, and 5 assists, carrying the load in a way that demonstrated exactly why Minnesota's roster depth has been one of the league's best-kept secrets.

Terrence Shannon Jr. was equally important, pouring in 24 points and hauling in 6 rebounds in a performance that showed why the Wolves trusted their depth even when it was tested to the extreme. Together, the two combined for 56 points and provided the offensive firepower Minnesota needed to close out a defending champion in their home arena.

"We believe in every guy in this locker room," McDaniels said after the game. "It doesn't matter who's out there."

A Pattern of Denver Dominance

For the second time in three years, the Timberwolves have sent the Nuggets home. It is a remarkable run of success against Denver for a franchise that struggled for much of its history. Minnesota now heads to its third consecutive Western Conference Semifinals appearance — a level of sustained success that puts them firmly in the conversation as an elite Western Conference program.

The Nuggets, for their part, fought to the end, but without the kind of secondary firepower needed to overcome Minnesota's team defense and the contributions of their depth players. Nikola Jokic, Aaron Gordon, and Jamal Murray were not enough to overcome a collective Wolves effort.

Edwards' Shadow Looms Large

The win is remarkable in context. Edwards suffered a left knee bone bruise and hyperextension in Game 4, and the Wolves proceeded to win the final two games of the series without him. That resilience is a testament to the coaching and roster construction — but the elephant in the room is the second round.

Minnesota will face the San Antonio Spurs in the Western Conference Semifinals. Victor Wembanyama, De'Aaron Fox, and the Spurs fresh off their own first-round sweep present a formidable challenge. The Wolves' chances improve dramatically if Edwards returns healthy, but the week-to-week designation provides no guarantees.

What's Next

The Timberwolves advance to the Western Conference Semifinals to face the Spurs, with Anthony Edwards' return timeline uncertain. McDaniels and Shannon have proven they can carry the load, but Minnesota's full-strength roster is significantly more dangerous. The question for the next round is simple: when does Ant come back?
