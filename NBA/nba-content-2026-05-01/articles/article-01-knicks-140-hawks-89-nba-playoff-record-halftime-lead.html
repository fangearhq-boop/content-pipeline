Knicks Set NBA Playoff Record with 47-Point Halftime Lead, Rout Hawks 140-89

By Marcus Cole | May 1, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The New York Knicks didn't just win a basketball game on Thursday night. They rewrote the record books.

Behind a historic first half and OG Anunoby's incandescent 29-point performance, the Knicks destroyed the Atlanta Hawks 140-89 in Game 6 at State Farm Arena, clinching the series 4-2 and earning a berth in the Eastern Conference Semifinals. Their 47-point halftime advantage — the Knicks led 83-36 at intermission — is the largest halftime lead in NBA playoff history.

A Historic First Half

The Knicks came out of the gates like a freight train, building a 40-15 lead after the first quarter — the largest first-quarter lead in the shot clock era. From there, a 43-6 run essentially ended the game before halftime. When the buzzer sounded at intermission, Atlanta's crowd was stunned into silence. The margin: 47 points.

Anunoby was the engine of the explosion. The forward knocked down 4-of-4 from beyond the arc in the first half alone, finishing with 26 of his 29 points before the break. He added 7 rebounds and 4 steals in one of the most dominant half-game performances of this postseason.

"OG just got it going," Knicks guard Mikal Bridges said after the game. "When he's locked in like that, it's hard to stop."

Bridges and KAT Close It Out

Bridges had an efficiency showcase in his own right, going 10-for-12 from the field for 24 points. The efficiency numbers told the story of a Knicks squad operating at a completely different level than Atlanta on this night.

Karl-Anthony Towns sealed the celebration in style with a triple-double: 12 points, 11 rebounds, and 10 assists. Jalen Johnson did what he could for Atlanta with 21 points, 8 rebounds, and 6 assists, but there was nothing to be done once New York found its rhythm.

The final margin of 51 points stands as one of the most lopsided playoff wins in recent memory.

What It Means for New York

The Knicks enter the Eastern Conference Semifinals on a wave of confidence. Anunoby has established himself as a two-way weapon capable of game-changing performances. Towns's playmaking from the center position adds a dimension few teams can match. And Bridges continues to be among the most reliable high-efficiency scorers in the postseason.

New York went 4-2 in the first round against an Atlanta team that fought hard in Games 4 and 5 before being overwhelmed in the finale. The Knicks' next opponent will be announced once the other Eastern bracket results are settled.

What's Next

The Knicks advance to the Eastern Conference Semifinals and will await their opponent, with the bracket still being determined. For Atlanta, the season is over after a competitive but ultimately outmatched first-round run. As for New York, after a performance like Thursday's, the entire Eastern Conference is on notice.
