Friday Night Triple-Header: Magic, Raptors, Rockets All Fight to Stay Alive

By Marcus Cole | May 1, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Thursday night delivered three unforgettable playoff moments. Friday is trying to one-up it.

Three Game 6s are on tap tonight, all streaming on Prime Video, and in each case a team is fighting for its playoff life. The Magic are trying to eliminate the No. 1 seed in the Eastern Conference. The Raptors are riding a bizarre road-team trend that has defined their series. The Rockets are hoping to stun LeBron James and the Los Angeles Lakers. Here's everything you need to know.

Magic at Pistons — 7:00 PM ET, Prime Video (Orlando Leads 3-2)

The Orlando Magic, seeded No. 8 in the East, are one win away from eliminating the top-seeded Detroit Pistons. It would be one of the most significant upsets of the first round — and entirely possible given how this series has gone.

Detroit did show life in Game 5, which featured one of the most extraordinary individual performances of the first round: Cade Cunningham and Paolo Banchero both scored 45 points in a 116-109 Pistons victory. It was the first time in NBA playoff history that two No. 1 overall draft picks both scored 40-plus points in the same game since Allen Iverson and Shaquille O'Neal in the 2001 NBA Finals. But Orlando still holds the series edge, and the Magic have been the more consistent team over six games.

Detroit's season is on the line in front of its home crowd.

Raptors at Cavaliers — 7:30 PM ET, Prime Video (Cleveland Leads 3-2)

The strangest series trend of the first round has defined the Cavaliers-Raptors matchup: the road team has won every single game through the first five contests. Every single one.

That pattern puts Toronto in an interesting position heading into Cleveland. Logic says the streak has to end. History says follow the trend. The Cavaliers hold a 3-2 series lead and will be playing in front of their home crowd with a chance to close out the series.

Brandon Ingram left Game 5 with right heel inflammation, adding a key injury subplot to an already compelling series. Cleveland's defense will be tested, but the Cavs, led by Donovan Mitchell and Darius Garland, have been one of the best home teams in the NBA all season.

Rockets at Lakers — 9:30 PM ET, Prime Video (L.A. Leads 3-2)

LeBron James and the Los Angeles Lakers need one win to advance, but the Houston Rockets are not cooperating. Without Kevin Durant, who has been sidelined throughout the series, the Rockets stunned the Lakers in Game 5, 99-93, with Jabari Smith Jr. leading the way with 22 points. Jordan Eason contributed 18 off the bench.

LeBron had 25 in that Game 5 loss, but Houston's defense and the collective effort of their young core proved too much in a hostile environment. Now the Lakers must close it out at home, where they've been more comfortable. The question is whether Houston's momentum from Game 5 carries over or if LeBron shuts it down on the Crypto.com Arena floor.

What's Next
