Thunder Crush Lakers 139-96 as SGA Stars in 43-Point Blowout

By Jake Torres April 3, 2026

A Message to the Entire Western Conference

When Shai Gilgeous-Alexander and the Oklahoma City Thunder decide to turn it on, the gap between them and the rest of the Western Conference becomes undeniable. Thursday night was one of those games.

Oklahoma City dismantled the Los Angeles Lakers 139-96, a 43-point blowout broadcast on Prime Video that served as a statement win for a team that has operated at the top of the West for most of the 2025-26 season. SGA scored 28 points in what was a controlled, efficient performance — less a showcase of brilliance and more a steady exhibition of dominance from a team that simply refused to let the Lakers get comfortable at any point in the game.

The No. 1 Seed Race Tightens for San Antonio

The immediate consequence of Thursday's result is felt in the standings. OKC improves to 61-16. San Antonio, which currently sits at 58-18, is now 3 games back with just 5 games remaining in the regular season. Mathematically, the Spurs' path to the No. 1 Western Conference seed still exists — San Antonio holds a 4-1 head-to-head record against OKC this season, meaning the tiebreaker belongs to the Spurs if both teams finish level — but the margin for error has nearly evaporated.

For the Spurs to claim the No. 1 seed, they would need to win all 5 remaining games and watch OKC go 2-3 or worse down the stretch. Both outcomes are unlikely. OKC has been one of the most consistent teams in the NBA this season. Yet the Spurs, who have gone 23-2 since February 1, know better than most that improbable runs are possible.

SGA and the MVP Subplot

The 43-point margin obscures something worth noting: Gilgeous-Alexander continues to play at a level that commands MVP consideration even when the game is not close. Scoring 28 points efficiently in a blowout is a sign of a player who does not coast — who raises his performance regardless of scoreboard pressure.

SGA remains the season-long MVP favorite at -300 in current betting markets, averaging 31.5 points per game. Victor Wembanyama recently surged to No. 1 on the official NBA.com MVP Ladder behind a historic run of 40-point double-doubles, but SGA's combination of volume, efficiency, and team success remains the most compelling overall case. Thursday's blowout win — the latest reminder of OKC's dominance — only reinforces that argument.

What's Next

Oklahoma City will close the regular season with 5 games remaining. Their lead over San Antonio means the No. 1 seed is theirs to lose, and nothing about their recent play suggests they are in danger of losing it. For the Lakers, who remain safely seeded in the No. 3 West position, Thursday's loss is a bump in the road ahead of what they hope will be a deep playoff run. For everyone watching the Western Conference, the message from OKC was clear: SGA and the Thunder are ready for April.
