Curry Completes Second Consecutive 5-on-5 Scrimmage — Sunday Return Still the Target

By Jake Torres April 3, 2026

The Strongest Signal Yet

For the second consecutive day, Stephen Curry stepped onto a practice court and participated in full 5-on-5 contact. That fact alone — back-to-back scrimmages without incident — is the most positive development Golden State Warriors fans have seen since Curry went down January 30 with patellofemoral pain syndrome and a bone bruise in his right knee.

Warriors head coach Steve Kerr delivered an encouraging report after Thursday's 118-111 loss to the Cleveland Cavaliers: "Everything went well. He looked good." Curry himself was characteristically measured in his own assessment. "I don't know how the knee will respond," he said, "because it hasn't responded well in past attempts to get to this five-on-five level." The caution is understandable given the stops and starts that have defined his recovery. But completing two consecutive scrimmages is the threshold no one was sure Curry could clear — and he has now cleared it twice in a row.

Sunday vs. Houston: The Target

The Warriors have identified Sunday's home game against the Houston Rockets as Curry's most likely return date, with Tuesday against the Sacramento Kings as the backup option if additional caution is warranted. Kerr has declined to officially confirm the Sunday return, calling Curry day-to-day, but the tone from the Warriors' organization has shifted noticeably this week — from uncertainty to cautious optimism.

The stakes for Golden State could not be higher. The Warriors are 36-40 and hold the 10th spot in the Western Conference — the final Play-In position — by a narrow margin over the Portland Trail Blazers. With 4 games remaining after Thursday's loss, Golden State needs victories, and the math of their season makes one point abundantly clear: they are a different team when Curry plays. Their record with Curry this season is 23-16. Without him, they are 12-22. That differential is the starkest statistical argument for any single player's value in the league this year.

The Larger Playoff Context

Beyond Golden State's immediate situation, Curry's potential return carries storyline weight for the broader postseason picture. A healthy Curry changes the Warriors' ceiling significantly. In the Play-In format, Golden State at full strength is a dangerous opponent for any team. A banged-up, Curry-less version of this team is a different proposition entirely.

The final week of the regular season is also a week of preparation for playoff opponents. Teams like Houston, which Curry would face first on Sunday, are watching Golden State's health situation closely. A Curry return does not simply affect Golden State's odds of making the postseason — it changes the risk calculation for any team that might face them in April or May.

What's Next

The Warriors will monitor Curry's knee response to Thursday's scrimmage over the next 48 hours. If he shows no setbacks, Sunday's return becomes official. For a franchise and a fanbase that spent most of January and February watching their season spiral without its cornerstone player, the news this week represents the first real reason for optimism. Curry healthy and playing is a different Golden State. The question is whether there are enough games left to make it matter.
