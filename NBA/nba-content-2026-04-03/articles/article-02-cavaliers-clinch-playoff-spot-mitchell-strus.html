Cavaliers Clinch 2026 Playoff Berth Behind Mitchell and Strus in 118-111 Win Over Warriors

By Marcus Cole April 3, 2026

Cleveland Is Officially In

The Cleveland Cavaliers are headed to the 2026 NBA Playoffs. The result that confirmed it came Thursday night: a 118-111 victory over the Golden State Warriors, secured behind Donovan Mitchell's 25 points and a sharp-shooting 24-point effort from Max Strus.

Cleveland becomes the fourth team confirmed in the Eastern Conference playoff bracket, joining the Detroit Pistons, Boston Celtics, and New York Knicks. After weeks of playoff-positioning basketball, the Cavaliers' postseason spot is now official — and the manner of the win, against a short-handed Golden State squad playing without Stephen Curry, was a workmanlike display of the kind of depth and execution Cleveland will need in April.

Mitchell Leads When It Counts

Donovan Mitchell has spent the 2025-26 season establishing himself as one of the Eastern Conference's most reliable scoring threats. Thursday's performance — 25 points in a game with playoff-clinching stakes — is the kind of moment that reinforces that status. Mitchell has a history of playing his best basketball in high-leverage situations, and the Cavaliers will need that tendency to carry into the first round.

Strus's 24-point contribution was equally important. Cleveland's ability to generate scoring from multiple sources beyond Mitchell is precisely what gives them a realistic chance of advancing deep in the bracket against Eastern Conference heavyweights. On a night that clinched their postseason entry, both players delivered.

The Warriors' Play-In Problem

For Golden State, Thursday's loss underscores the danger of their current situation. The Warriors fell to 36-40, maintaining an uncomfortable grip on the final Play-In spot in the Western Conference. Playing without Curry — who has missed more than 25 consecutive games with patellofemoral pain syndrome — Golden State's offense lacks the gravitational pull that makes their system work. Their record without Curry this season is 12-22. With him, it is 23-16.

Stephen Curry is targeting Sunday's home game against the Houston Rockets as his return date. Kerr confirmed after Thursday's loss that Curry's second consecutive 5-on-5 scrimmage went well. But the Warriors have five games left, and the Play-In zone is not secure.

What's Next

Cleveland heads into the final stretch of the regular season with their playoff berth confirmed and a seeding battle ahead — they are currently 4th in the East and will be looking to hold or improve that position before April 12. The Warriors, meanwhile, turn their attention to Sunday and the most anticipated return of the regular season's final week. Golden State's postseason fate likely hinges on what happens when Curry steps back on the floor. Cleveland already knows where they're going. Golden State is still fighting to find out.
