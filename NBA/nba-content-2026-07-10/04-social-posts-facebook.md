### STORY 3: Kawhi Leonard Trade on Hold — NBA Investigation

**Long-Form Post (1:00 PM ET)**

Toronto and Los Angeles had a deal. Kawhi Leonard was going home.

And then the NBA froze everything.

The Raptors and Clippers agreed to a trade sending Kawhi back to Toronto, with the Raptors giving up Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, two first-round draft picks, two second-round picks, and pick swaps. It was a significant package for a 34-year-old coming off injury-filled seasons. But for the Raptors, it was a franchise moment — the champion coming home.

Now that deal is stuck in place, and nobody knows for how long.

The NBA is conducting an investigation into whether the Clippers circumvented salary cap rules to sign Kawhi in 2019. At the center of the probe is an alleged $28 million endorsement deal between Leonard and Aspiration Fund Adviser LLC — a company that has since filed for bankruptcy. If the Clippers improperly paid Leonard benefits to lure him from San Antonio, that could violate league rules, and the trade would need to be adjudicated before it can close.

Outside counsel is handling the investigation. There is no timetable for its completion.

The Clippers say they "did not funnel money to Kawhi Leonard." Until the NBA concludes otherwise, or clears them, the trade sits in limbo. Raptors fans who were starting to imagine the jersey retirement ceremonies and the roar of Scotiabank Arena have to keep waiting.

This is the NBA offseason working at its messiest.

Is $112M the right price for Dosunmu? The market says yes. The Game 4 tape says definitely.

### STORY 5: Jokić Delays Extension — Targeting $359M NBA-Record Deal Next Summer

**Long-Form Post (7:00 PM ET)**

Nikola Jokić could have signed a $278 million contract extension with the Denver Nuggets this summer. He is going to wait instead.

The three-time MVP told reporters Thursday that he plans to hold off on signing until after the 2026-27 season. By waiting one year, he becomes eligible for a five-year, $359.5 million maximum contract — the largest deal in NBA history — rather than the four-year, $278 million extension available now. The math is simple: patience is worth roughly $81 million.

"My idea and desire is to stay in Denver. I'll probably sign next year," Jokić said.

This is not about leverage, and it is not about uncertainty. Jokić has never asked out of Denver. He won three MVP awards and a championship there. He has repeatedly said he wants to finish his career in Colorado. The delay is purely financial strategy, and it is entirely rational.

What it means for the Nuggets: they head into 2026-27 without a signed contract extension from their franchise player. That creates a small but real layer of roster-building risk. Teams cannot treat an unsigned Jokić exactly the same as a locked-in Jokić when planning long-term.

What it means for the NBA: if and when Jokić signs next summer, the $359.5 million deal will reset every benchmark in the sport.

Until then, the best player in basketball is playing out the year, committed to Denver, just not yet signed.

Jokić said it himself: "I'll probably sign next year." At $359M, can you blame him? 🐐
