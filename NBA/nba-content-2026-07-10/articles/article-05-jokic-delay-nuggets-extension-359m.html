The $81 Million Wait: Jokić Confirms He's Targeting $359M NBA-Record Deal Next Summer

By Jake Torres July 10, 2026

The Most Valuable Delay in Sports

Nikola Jokić has a very simple answer for why he is not signing a contract extension with the Denver Nuggets this summer. Waiting one year will earn him approximately $81 million more.

The three-time MVP confirmed Thursday that he intends to pass on the four-year, $278 million maximum extension currently available and wait until after the 2026-27 season. By doing so, he will become eligible for a five-year, $359.5 million maximum deal — the largest contract in NBA history. His commitment to Denver is not in question. The timeline on the paperwork is purely strategic.

In His Own Words

"My idea and desire is to stay in Denver. I'll probably sign next year," Jokić told reporters Thursday. The quote is everything a Nuggets fan could want: clear intent to stay, acknowledgment that the deal is not happening yet, and no drama attached to either part of it.

Jokić has never asked out of Denver. He won the MVP award in 2021, 2022, and 2024 in Colorado. He won a championship there in 2023. He has been repeatedly linked to potential exits during contract cycles, and every time the conclusion has been the same — he stays, he signs, and he plays. There is no reason to expect this cycle to be different. The delay is financial, not relational.

The Financial Math

Current maximum extension available: four years, $278 million. If Jokić signs after next season: five years, $359.5 million, plus the likelihood of a no-trade clause. The difference is roughly $81 million in guaranteed money and an additional year of contract security. For context, $81 million is more than many NBA players earn in their entire careers.

Jokić's current deal runs through 2026-27. He can play out next season, enter negotiations in the summer of 2027, and sign the largest contract the NBA has ever issued. The only risk in that scenario is an injury or a dramatic performance decline that changes how the Nuggets or the market values him. Given his durability and the nature of his game — based on skill and basketball IQ rather than athleticism that diminishes quickly — that risk is considered low.

What the Nuggets Must Navigate

Denver enters 2026-27 with their franchise player on the final year of his deal, unsigned. That is an unusual situation for a title contender. Teams typically cannot recruit effectively in free agency or trades when the centerpiece of the roster has no long-term commitment. Other teams will not approach this Nuggets roster the same way they would approach one with a locked-in Jokić.

The Nuggets' front office has publicly accepted this dynamic. Jokić's verbal commitment and his track record are treated as sufficient assurance. The organization is betting that he is who he has always been — the player who wins MVPs, helps bring the title, and eventually signs the extension. That bet has paid off before. There is every reason to believe it does again next summer.

What's Next

Jokić heads into the 2026-27 season as the best player in basketball, playing out the final year of his current deal. If the Nuggets remain a legitimate title contender — and with Jokić healthy, they always are — the extension conversation next summer will be a formality, just a very large one. The $359.5 million deal would reset every contract benchmark in the sport the moment it is signed. Denver fans have to get through one more season of unsigned-but-committed. After what Jokić has delivered, that seems like a trade worth making.
