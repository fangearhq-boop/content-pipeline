Dosunmu Stays in Minnesota: Timberwolves Lock Up Playoff Hero With 5yr/$112M Deal

By Damon Pierce July 10, 2026

Minnesota Moves Fast

Tim Connelly and the Minnesota Timberwolves are not in the habit of letting playoff performers reach open market. On Thursday, they moved again. Guard Ayo Dosunmu agreed to a five-year, $112 million contract to remain in Minnesota, with a player option built into the fifth year. Several teams had interest in Dosunmu as a free agent. Connelly never gave them the chance to pitch.

Dosunmu appeared in 24 regular-season games for the Timberwolves in 2025-26, averaging 14.4 points, 4.2 rebounds, and 3.5 assists per game. Those numbers are solid for a complementary guard on a contending roster. But they are not the reason Minnesota is paying him $112 million.

The Moment That Changed His Market

The moment that made Dosunmu worth a maximum-range commitment happened in Game 4 of Minnesota's first-round playoff series against the Denver Nuggets. In that game, Timberwolves starting guard Donte DiVincenzo suffered a torn Achilles tendon. The season felt like it was in jeopardy. Dosunmu came off the bench and scored 43 points.

Minnesota won the game. Dosunmu averaged 15.6 points in 29.2 minutes across a 10-game playoff run, stepping up every time his team needed him. He is the kind of player contending rosters need: capable of blending in during the regular season and elevating when it matters most in the postseason.

What He Brings to the 2026-27 Roster

The Timberwolves' roster is in transition. Julius Randle was traded to the Brooklyn Nets. LaMelo Ball arrived via trade from Charlotte. Anthony Edwards and Karl-Anthony Towns anchor the core while the surrounding pieces continue to shift. Dosunmu gives Minnesota a proven scorer and defender who fits the contender timeline without requiring the ball to be productive.

His defensive effort is what separates him from comparable offensive creators at his position. Dosunmu competes on that end of the floor at a level that earns him trust in closing lineups. Connelly paid him for the complete picture — regular-season floor-raising, playoff ceiling-raising, and the defensive reliability to keep him on the court in critical moments.

The Strategy Behind the Signing

Connelly worked with Dosunmu's agents, Mike Lindeman and Jeff Schwartz of Excel Sports, to complete the deal before free agency officially allowed competing offers to land. The principle is consistent with how the Timberwolves have operated: identify the players who fit the culture, move early, and avoid the auction process that can drive prices to uncomfortable levels.

Whether $112 million is the right valuation for a player whose peak performance came in a 10-game sample is a fair question. The market suggests it is — other teams were ready to make their pitches. Connelly's answer was not to find a lower price elsewhere but to prevent the conversation from happening at all.

What's Next

Dosunmu's deal makes him a Timberwolf through the 2030-31 season with the player option. Minnesota continues building toward a championship window centered on Anthony Edwards, with LaMelo Ball now added to the perimeter mix and Dosunmu providing a proven playoff contributor behind them. The Wolves are not done reshaping their roster — LeBron James' free agency could pull more secondary pieces in unexpected directions — but locking up Dosunmu was a clear priority, and it's done.
