Kawhi's Toronto Homecoming Is Frozen: NBA Investigation Puts Clippers Trade on Hold

By Marcus Cole July 10, 2026

A Trade on Ice

The Toronto Raptors and Los Angeles Clippers agreed to a trade last month that would send Kawhi Leonard back to the city where he won an NBA championship. It was one of the emotionally loaded stories of the entire offseason — the champion coming home. Now that trade is frozen indefinitely, blocked by an NBA investigation into whether the Clippers ever paid Leonard impermissible benefits to sign with Los Angeles in the first place.

The NBA announced that the Kawhi Leonard trade will not be processed until the league's investigation into the Clippers concludes. No timetable has been provided for the probe's completion. Outside counsel is conducting the inquiry, and neither the NBA nor the teams involved have indicated when a resolution is expected.

What the Investigation Is About

The NBA opened its investigation in September into an alleged $28 million endorsement deal between Leonard and Aspiration Fund Adviser LLC, a company that has since filed for bankruptcy. Investigators are examining whether that deal represented impermissible payments from the Clippers — or parties connected to the Clippers — that were used to induce Leonard to leave the San Antonio Spurs and sign with Los Angeles ahead of the 2019-20 season.

Salary cap circumvention is among the most serious violations the NBA polices. Teams are prohibited from directing outside money to free agents as part of negotiations. If the Clippers or their associates routed $28 million to Leonard as a hidden benefit during the 2019 free agency period, the league's rules would have been violated — and the penalties can be severe, including fines, loss of draft picks, or voiding of subsequent contracts.

The Clippers' Position

The Clippers have denied all wrongdoing. The organization has stated that they "did not funnel money to Kawhi Leonard" and have maintained that position throughout the investigation. Whether the NBA's outside counsel concludes otherwise remains to be seen.

The complication for the trade is not necessarily that the Clippers will be found guilty. It is that the investigation must conclude — one way or another — before the league will allow the trade to move forward. The NBA cannot process the deal while the Clippers remain under active scrutiny for conduct that predates it.

What This Means for the Raptors

Toronto gave up a significant package for Leonard: Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, two first-round draft picks, two second-round picks, and pick swaps. The Raptors agreed to those terms believing they were getting Kawhi back for what would likely be the final meaningful chapter of his NBA career. Now that deal sits unsigned, and the players and picks the Raptors agreed to trade are also in a kind of administrative limbo.

Raptors fans who watched Leonard lift the championship trophy in 2019 — and who chanted his name through that entire run — had started imagining a farewell chapter worthy of the moment. Kyle Lowry's jersey was retired at Scotiabank Arena last week. A Kawhi return was supposed to be the next act. That story now has an uncertain timeline.

What's Next

The investigation has no publicly stated deadline, and the league has not indicated urgency. Until outside counsel delivers its findings and the NBA makes a determination about the Clippers, the trade cannot close. If the Clippers are cleared, the trade moves forward and Kawhi heads to Toronto. If violations are found, the situation becomes significantly more complex — with possible punishment for the organization and questions about what happens to the agreed trade terms. For now, both franchises wait on a decision that is entirely outside their control.
