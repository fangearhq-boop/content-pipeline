Draymond Clears the Way: Warriors Go All-In on LeBron as Cavaliers Hold Their Lead

By Jake Torres July 10, 2026

A $27.7 Million Statement

Draymond Green turned down $27.7 million this week. Not because he didn't want the money. Because he wants to play with LeBron James more than he wants the security of the deal that was already on the table.

Green declined his Golden State Warriors player option for 2026-27, clearing financial space for the franchise to pursue LeBron James and, potentially, Anthony Davis. The Warriors and Green had been discussing this scenario for weeks: Green takes a restructured deal at a lower number, the franchise creates flexibility, and they go after the biggest free agent on the market. That plan is now in motion.

LeBron Left the Lakers — Now Comes the Hard Part

LeBron James informed the Los Angeles Lakers that he will not return for the 2026-27 season. After eight years in Los Angeles — including a 2020 championship — he is leaving as a free agent. The decision did not come with a destination announcement. That is still pending, and Rich Paul, LeBron's agent, has made clear it will not come for at least a few more weeks. Mid-July is the working timeline.

What is known: six teams are in legitimate contention. The Warriors, Cavaliers, 76ers, Heat, Timberwolves, and Nuggets all have paths to landing him. Each has built a distinct pitch based on roster talent, financial structure, and personal relationships with James and his camp.

Who Has the Best Case

ESPN's Brian Windhorst describes the Cleveland Cavaliers as the front-runner. The evidence is circumstantial but real: LeBron recently posed for a photo with his Akron high school teammates, including Brandon Weems, who works in the Cavaliers' front office. Donovan Mitchell signed a four-year, $273 million extension with Cleveland last week. The roster is built, the city wants LeBron back, and James has never publicly distanced himself from a Cleveland return.

The Golden State case is built on relationships. LeBron and Stephen Curry won an Olympic gold medal together in 2024 in Paris and have spoken openly about wanting to play meaningful basketball in the final chapters of their careers. Green declining $27.7 million specifically to help create LeBron room is the kind of sacrifice that sends a message directly to James and Paul about how serious the Warriors are.

The Philadelphia 76ers emerged as a genuine factor earlier this week when ESPN's Shams Charania reported that LeBron is taking the 76ers "very seriously." The Sixers have Tyrese Maxey, a Klutch Sports client like James, and Jaylen Brown, who arrived in Philadelphia via trade from Boston. A roster featuring Maxey, Brown, and LeBron would be a threat to anyone in the Eastern Conference immediately.

The Waiting Game

Every team building its offseason around LeBron's decision is, to some degree, frozen. James Harden declined his $42.3 million Cavaliers player option to renegotiate a new deal, and that process is tangled up in the LeBron uncertainty. Anthony Davis trade discussions involving the Lakers have similarly stalled because teams evaluating what Golden State can offer depend on what space remains after LeBron.

This is how LeBron's free agency has always worked. The rest of the league reorganizes itself around his decision, and then fills in the gaps after he signs. It is reasonable to expect that within days of LeBron's announcement, three or four significant secondary moves follow. Until then, everyone waits.

What's Next

Rich Paul has communicated that a decision is not imminent. Mid-July is the stated timeline, which gives teams until roughly July 14-21 to continue their pitches and roster-building. For Golden State, the immediate priority is completing Draymond Green's new contract at a reduced salary. For Cleveland, it is keeping their roster flexible while projecting confidence. For Philadelphia, it is making the case that Jaylen Brown plus LeBron plus Maxey is the most dangerous lineup in the East. Whoever lands LeBron will be the immediate favorite to win the 2026-27 NBA championship.
