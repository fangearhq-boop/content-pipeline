Stephon Castle's 32-Point Breakout: The Spurs' Emerging Star Is Ready for the WCF

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 16, 2026

Somewhere in the middle of Stephon Castle's 32-point, 11-rebound, 6-assist performance in San Antonio's Game 6 blowout win over the Minnesota Timberwolves, you started to wonder: does this kid realize how hard this is supposed to be?

He was playing in a closeout game. In a hostile environment. In just his second NBA season. And he was the best player on the floor.

Castle finished 11-of-16 from the field, including 5-of-7 from three-point range. He picked up double-digit rebounds. He distributed with 6 assists. And he did all of it with a composure that belies his age. The San Antonio Spurs are heading to the Western Conference Finals, and Castle is a major reason why.

Who Is Stephon Castle?

Castle entered the NBA from UConn, where he was a key piece of the Huskies' run to back-to-back national championships. The Spurs selected him with their lottery pick in the 2024 NBA Draft, adding him to a team already featuring Victor Wembanyama.

In his rookie season, Castle showed the defensive instincts and athleticism that made scouts excited. He was raw offensively — a work in progress. But this postseason, the work has paid off. He has emerged as a legitimate third option alongside Wembanyama and De'Aaron Fox, and in Game 6, he was the best of all of them.

The Perfect Supporting Cast

What makes Castle's emergence even more remarkable is who surrounds him in San Antonio. Victor Wembanyama commands the double-teams, the attention, and the respect of every defense in the league. De'Aaron Fox attacks in transition, creates on the ball, and puts constant pressure on opposing guards.

Castle operates in the spaces those two create. He's long enough to shoot over closeouts, quick enough to finish in traffic, and instinctive enough to find the right play in pick-and-roll situations. Against Minnesota in Game 6, the Timberwolves simply had no answer for the combination of Fox and Castle working off of Wembanyama's gravity.

De'Aaron Fox added 21 points and 9 assists. Rookie Dylan Harper contributed 15 off the bench. Wembanyama posted 19 points in just 27 minutes. The entire Spurs machine was clicking at maximum efficiency.

What Comes Next

The Western Conference Finals against Oklahoma City will be Castle's biggest challenge yet. The Thunder went 8-0 in the first two rounds with one of the best defenses in basketball. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is one of the premier individual defenders in the league, and OKC's wing rotation is deep and athletic.

But Castle has answered every challenge thrown at him in these playoffs. There's no reason to think he'll back down from the biggest stage he's played on.

What's Next

The Spurs open the Western Conference Finals on Monday, May 18, in Oklahoma City with Game 1 tipping off at 7:30 PM ET on NBC and Peacock. After his 32-point breakout, Castle has earned the right to go head-to-head with the Thunder's elite defense. The basketball world will be watching — and if Game 6 against Minnesota is any indication, Stephon Castle won't be intimidated by the moment. He'll rise to it.
