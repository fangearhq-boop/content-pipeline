Cavs-Pistons Game 7 Is Set: Everything on the Line Sunday in Detroit

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 16, 2026

It was supposed to be a formality. The No. 1 seed, at home, needing one win to close out the series. The Cleveland Cavaliers had it in their hands on Friday night — and the Detroit Pistons took it away.

After Detroit's dominant 115-94 Game 6 victory, the Cavaliers-Pistons Eastern Conference Semifinals is going to a Game 7. The decisive game is scheduled for Sunday, May 17, in Detroit. The winner advances to face the New York Knicks in the Eastern Conference Finals beginning May 19.

Cleveland's Back Is Against the Wall

The Cavaliers were the prohibitive favorites in this series. They finished the regular season as the top seed in the Eastern Conference. They have established stars in Donovan Mitchell and James Harden. And they had home court advantage throughout the series.

Now, after losing Game 6 by 21 points — their first home defeat of the entire 2026 playoffs — they must win a road Game 7 in a hostile environment. History is clear on what happens when the No. 1 seed loses home court in a Game 6 blowout: the momentum is nearly impossible to recover.

Mitchell finished with just 18 points on 6-of-20 shooting in Game 6. He'll need to be a completely different player Sunday if Cleveland has any hope of advancing.

Detroit's Momentum Is Real

The Pistons have earned this moment. They took down the No. 1 seed in Games 1-2 on the road, answered Cleveland's comeback with resilience, and then delivered a crushing blow in Game 6 that silenced an entire arena.

Cade Cunningham has been exceptional throughout this series. His 21-point, 5-of-10 from three performance in Game 6 showcased both his individual brilliance and his ability to perform in big moments. Daniss Jenkins and Jalen Duren have provided critical supporting production. And now Detroit heads home for the biggest game in their young franchise's recent history.

The crowd at Little Caesars Arena on Sunday will be deafening. The Pistons' fans know what's at stake, and this team has given them every reason to believe.

The Knicks Wait

New York has been watching this series very carefully. The Knicks swept the 76ers in the first round and have had a long rest period — plenty of time to prepare for either opponent.

A Cavaliers matchup would pit superstar against superstar — Mitchell and Harden vs. the Knicks' deep roster. A Pistons matchup would be a battle between one of the East's most experienced franchises and one of its most hungry young teams. Either way, the Knicks know their ECF begins May 19.

What's Next

Game 7 tips off Sunday, May 17, in Detroit. One team's season ends; one team advances to the Eastern Conference Finals. The Pistons have momentum, home court, and a belief system that has carried them to this point against all expectations. The Cavaliers have the stars, the experience, and the motivation of a team that knows it underperformed when it mattered. Sunday will decide which factor matters more.
