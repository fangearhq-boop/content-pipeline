Spurs Blow Out Wolves 139-109, Advance to WCF Behind Castle's 32-Point Masterpiece

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 16, 2026

Stephon Castle left no doubt on Friday night.

The 20-year-old San Antonio Spurs guard erupted for 32 points, 11 rebounds, and 6 assists on 11-of-16 shooting — including 5-of-7 from three — as the Spurs dismantled the Minnesota Timberwolves 139-109 in Game 6 to clinch the Western Conference Semifinals. San Antonio wins the series four games to two and advances to face the Oklahoma City Thunder in the Western Conference Finals, beginning Monday, May 18.

It was a dominant, complete performance from a Spurs team that has quietly become one of the most exciting rosters in basketball.

Castle Takes Center Stage

Before Friday's game, Stephon Castle had shown flashes of brilliance throughout the 2026 playoffs. In Game 6, he put it all together in the most important moment of his young career.

He scored from every level — attacking the rim, knocking down mid-range jumpers, and connecting on five three-pointers — all while facilitating with 6 assists and cleaning the glass with 11 rebounds. In a closeout game, on the road, with the season on the line, Castle played like a seasoned veteran.

De'Aaron Fox was characteristically brilliant alongside Castle, finishing with 21 points and 9 assists. And rookie Dylan Harper — who continues to surprise throughout this playoff run — added 15 points off the bench. Victor Wembanyama contributed a quiet 19 points in just 27 minutes, a reminder of how deep and balanced this Spurs team truly is.

Timberwolves Couldn't Keep Up

Anthony Edwards gave everything he had — 24 points — but 9-of-26 shooting was the story of Minnesota's night. The Wolves simply couldn't find an answer for San Antonio's relentless pace, spacing, and ball movement.

The final score of 139-109 — a 30-point blowout in a closeout game — underscored just how complete the Spurs' series win was. Minnesota had their moments earlier in the series, but San Antonio's talent and depth ultimately proved to be too much to handle.

A Franchise Moment

The San Antonio Spurs are heading back to the Western Conference Finals. After years of rebuilding following their dynasty era, this young core — anchored by Wembanyama, energized by Fox, and now supercharged by Castle's emergence — has taken the franchise to a new level.

The Western Conference Finals matchup against Oklahoma City is a generational collision. SGA and the Thunder have been the league's best team all season and went 8-0 through the first two rounds. The Spurs have their own version of brilliance in Wembanyama. And now Castle has proven he belongs in this conversation too.

What's Next

Game 1 of the Western Conference Finals tips off Monday, May 18 at 7:30 PM ET in Oklahoma City on NBC and Peacock. It's the first time these two young franchises have met in the postseason, and it shapes up as one of the most compelling WCF matchups in recent memory. San Antonio enters with momentum and a healthy, confident roster. OKC enters with an undefeated playoff record and home court. Something has to give — and fans won't want to miss a second of it.
