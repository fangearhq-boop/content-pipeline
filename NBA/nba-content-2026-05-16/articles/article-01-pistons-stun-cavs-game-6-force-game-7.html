Pistons Stun Cavs 115-94 in Game 6, Force Decisive Game 7 in Detroit

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 16, 2026

Cleveland had been unbeatable at home during the 2026 NBA playoffs. On Friday night, Detroit finally changed that.

The Detroit Pistons delivered one of the most dominant road performances of the postseason, blasting the Cleveland Cavaliers 115-94 at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse to even the Eastern Conference Semifinals at 3-3. It was the Cavaliers' first home loss of the entire 2026 playoffs, and it came at the worst possible moment — with a series on the line.

Game 7 is set for Sunday in Detroit. The winner advances to face the New York Knicks in the Eastern Conference Finals starting May 19.

Cade Cunningham Delivers, Mitchell Struggles

Cade Cunningham has been the engine of Detroit's surprising run, and Friday night was no different. The Pistons' star guard finished with 21 points, drilling 5-of-10 attempts from three-point range. He was in complete control throughout, orchestrating Detroit's offense and consistently making the right play.

On the other end, Donovan Mitchell had one of the worst shooting nights of his playoff career. The Cavs' star was held to 18 points on just 6-of-20 from the field and 2-of-6 from three. James Harden chipped in 21 points for Cleveland, but the Cavs' supporting cast couldn't compensate for Mitchell's off night.

Detroit got contributions throughout the lineup. Daniss Jenkins added 15 points, while Jalen Duren was dominant in the paint with 15 points, 11 rebounds, and 3 blocks. The Pistons were simply the better team in every phase on Friday night.

A Historic Road Win

The magnitude of what Detroit accomplished cannot be understated. Entering Game 6 as the No. 4 seed visiting the No. 1 seed's court in an elimination game — on a night when Cleveland needed only one win to advance — the Pistons played with none of the pressure that typically grips a road team in that spot.

The final margin of 21 points was emphatic. This wasn't a lucky bounce or a fortunate shooting night — Detroit controlled this game from early on and never let the Cavaliers get comfortable. It was a statement that this young Pistons group belongs on the same floor as anyone in the Eastern Conference.

The Knicks Are Watching

While Cleveland and Detroit prepare for their winner-take-all game, the New York Knicks have been waiting in the Eastern Conference Finals for more than a week. After sweeping the Philadelphia 76ers in the first round, the Knicks have had a long rest period and will face the Cavaliers or Pistons starting May 19.

Both potential opponents present different challenges for New York. The Cavaliers, led by Mitchell and Harden, have the star power to go toe-to-toe with anyone. The Pistons, with Cunningham emerging as a legitimate No. 1 option, have the momentum and the hunger of a team that nobody believed in.

What's Next

Game 7 is set for Sunday, May 17 in Detroit. The Pistons carry all the momentum heading home, where their crowd will be electric for a winner-take-all game. The Cavaliers must regroup and find the Mitchell of Games 3-5 — the one who carried Cleveland through multiple deficits. Cleveland has won on the road this series, so the ability is there. But can they flip the script one more time?

Tip-off Sunday in Detroit. The Eastern Conference's final playoff spot is at stake.
