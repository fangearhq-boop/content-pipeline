Thunder vs. Spurs Western Conference Finals Preview: SGA Meets Wemby in Monday's Game 1

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 16, 2026

The Oklahoma City Thunder have been waiting. Watching. Winning.

After going a perfect 8-0 through the first two rounds of the 2026 NBA playoffs, the Thunder finally get their Western Conference Finals opponent: the San Antonio Spurs. Game 1 tips off Monday, May 18 at 7:30 PM ET in Oklahoma City on NBC and Peacock, and it marks the collision of two of the most electrifying young rosters in basketball.

OKC's 8-0 Playoff Machine

Oklahoma City has been the most dominant team in the 2026 playoffs. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has been sensational throughout — producing big scoring nights, controlling tempo, and making the right plays at every turn. The Thunder swept their first-round opponent and then swept the Los Angeles Lakers in the second round, with SGA dropping 35 points in the clinching Game 4.

The Thunder's defense has been suffocating. Their offense has been efficient. And SGA has looked every bit like an MVP candidate performing at the highest level. Going 8-0 in the postseason is a rare achievement, and Oklahoma City has made it look almost routine.

Now, after sitting for days watching the Western Conference Semifinals unfold, the Thunder are fully rested and ready for their biggest challenge.

The Spurs' Dynamic Trio

San Antonio arrives with momentum and star power of their own. Victor Wembanyama — the No. 1 pick in the 2023 draft and one of the most unique players in league history — is the centerpiece. His combination of length, shooting, shot-blocking, and playmaking creates problems that no defense has figured out for more than a game at a time.

De'Aaron Fox gives the Spurs blazing speed and backcourt scoring, and Stephon Castle emerged as a legitimate third star with his 32-point, 11-rebound, 6-assist performance in the Game 6 clincher over Minnesota. The Spurs are young, athletic, and coming in hot after a 30-point blowout win.

Matchup to Watch

SGA vs. Wemby is the headline matchup, even though they'll rarely guard each other directly. Both players have the ability to dominate a game in entirely different ways. SGA with his on-ball creation, finishing at the rim, and free-throw efficiency. Wemby with his unguardable post game and perimeter shooting from seven feet tall.

De'Aaron Fox will test OKC's perimeter defense, while San Antonio will have to find ways to contain SGA without fouling. Fox vs. OKC's wing defenders could prove to be a decisive chess match throughout the series.

What's Next

Game 1 tips off Monday, May 18 at 7:30 PM ET in Oklahoma City on NBC and Peacock. The Thunder hold home court throughout the series as the No. 1 seed in the West. OKC's record 8-0 playoff run is remarkable — but the Spurs proved against Minnesota that they're capable of dominant, complete performances when it matters most. This WCF shapes up as one of the best in recent memory. Don't miss Game 1.
