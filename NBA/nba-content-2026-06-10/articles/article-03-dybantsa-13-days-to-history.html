AJ Dybantsa Is 13 Days From Making History — and He Knows It

By Marcus Cole | June 10, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Ask AJ Dybantsa how certain he is that the Washington Wizards will take him No. 1 overall in the 2026 NBA Draft, and he will give you the clearest possible answer: 100 percent. No hedging, no qualifications, no talk of process. He is certain, and he is not afraid to say so.

The betting markets agree. Dybantsa sits at -450 to go first overall — an overwhelming probability that reflects not just his talent but the reality that this draft class, despite its depth, has a clear top prospect who has been at the apex of evaluations all season.

In 13 days, at Barclays Center in Brooklyn, that certainty will either be confirmed or overturned. The smart money says it gets confirmed.

What Dybantsa Brings

The case for taking Dybantsa first overall starts with the combination of skills that is extraordinarily rare for a player his age. The 6'9" BYU forward averaged 25.5 points per game on 51.0% shooting this past season — numbers that reflect elite scoring efficiency, not just volume. He can create off the dribble, hit from range, and finish at the rim against length. He projects as a versatile, two-way wing who can guard multiple positions at the NBA level.

The comparison most frequently made is to a player who combines scoring creation with defensive versatility — the modern template for a franchise cornerstone. At 19 years old, Dybantsa is not a finished product. He is a starting point that Washington can build an era around.

What Washington Gets

The Wizards finished 17-65 this season — one of the worst records in the league. They earned the No. 1 pick in the lottery, and now they get to use it on the player most regarded as this draft's best prospect.

For Washington, this would be the first No. 1 overall selection since John Wall in 2010. Wall's arrival started a promising era that eventually was cut short by injury and circumstance. The hope in Washington is that Dybantsa represents something more durable — a player with the physical tools and the mentality to anchor a franchise through its rebuilding years and into contention.

The Wizards have not officially confirmed their pick — which is standard draft operating procedure. But no one around the league believes they are drafting anyone else. Dybantsa himself believes it. The market believes it. The only question now is how quickly Washington can build the right pieces around him.

Behind Dybantsa

The class behind the clear No. 1 pick also has clarity at the top. Darryn Peterson is expected to go No. 2 to the Utah Jazz. Cameron Boozer rounds out the third slot with the Memphis Grizzlies. According to multiple reports, Dybantsa, Peterson, and Boozer have separated themselves from the rest of the field and represent the draft's clear tier-one prospects.

The Chicago Bulls at No. 4 and the Los Angeles Clippers at No. 5 will have their own decisions to make, and the debate over that range of picks figures to dominate draft coverage over the next 13 days. But the top story — Dybantsa to Washington — has been settled for weeks.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Draft takes place June 23-24 at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. The first round begins at 8:00 PM ET on June 23. Dybantsa's moment — the handshake, the commissioner, the jersey, the first step into the professional chapter of his basketball life — is 13 days away.

For a player who has carried the weight of this kind of expectation all season, the certainty he projects is either the sign of an athlete who belongs at this level — or one who is about to learn what pressure really looks like. Everything in his career so far suggests it is the former.
