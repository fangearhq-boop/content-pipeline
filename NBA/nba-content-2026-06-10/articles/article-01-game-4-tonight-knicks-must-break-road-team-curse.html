Game 4 Tonight: Can the Knicks Finally Win at Home in the NBA Finals?

By Damon Pierce | June 10, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Madison Square Garden has given the 2026 NBA Finals everything it promised. The noise. The energy. The electricity of a crowd that has been waiting 53 years for a championship. What it has not given the New York Knicks is a win.

Tonight, in Game 4, that changes — or the entire complexion of the series does.

The Spurs won Game 3 at MSG four days ago, 115-111, making this only the second NBA Finals in history where the road team won each of the first three games. The Knicks still lead 2-1. They are still 1.5-point favorites for tonight. But the pressure has shifted, quietly and unmistakably, to the home side.

The Free-Throw Controversy

Knicks coach Mike Brown did not stay quiet after Game 3. He publicly questioned the free-throw disparity between the two teams, a comment that drew attention across the league and set the tone for Wednesday's pregame conversation.

It was an unusual move — coaches rarely invite that kind of spotlight in the Finals — but it speaks to the frustration in the Knicks' locker room after a loss that felt, to many inside the building, like a game they should have won. New York had the crowd, the momentum of a historic streak, and two of its best players at their best. They still lost.

Whether the officiating scrutiny generates any tangible difference in Game 4 is uncertain. What it does accomplish is keeping the Knicks' fire burning and giving their team a focal point beyond simply "play better."

What the Knicks Need

The answer starts with Jalen Brunson. He put up 32 points in Game 3 — an extraordinary individual output — but the Spurs' defensive scheme disrupted his rhythm throughout and he was less efficient than New York needed. Against a San Antonio defense anchored by Victor Wembanyama at the rim, the Knicks' entire offensive structure runs through Brunson's ability to create cleanly and make the right decision in the pick-and-roll.

OG Anunoby gave the Knicks 28 points in Game 3. Karl-Anthony Towns has been a key presence on both ends. The pieces are there. The Knicks are not a team lacking talent. What they need tonight is sharper execution in the moments that decide Finals games — the final four minutes of the fourth quarter, the free-throw line, the ball-security plays that San Antonio has been winning.

What the Spurs Need

For San Antonio, the formula is straightforward: replicate Game 3. Move the ball. Defend with length and effort. Let Wembanyama operate at the level he reached Monday. Get Stephon Castle big shots when the moment demands them.

A Spurs win tonight would level the series at 2-2 with Game 5 in San Antonio on Saturday. In a series where the road team has won every game, that would mean the Spurs head home with momentum and a legitimate chance to take the lead for the first time.

A Knicks win puts them one win from the title, with Games 6 and 7 (if necessary) at home. The stakes for both sides tonight could not be higher.

The Road-Team Pattern

The pattern that has defined this series adds an unusual subplot to every tip-off. Road teams winning is not random in basketball — visitors play looser, without the weight of expectation pressing down on them, while the home crowd sometimes raises the pressure on the team it came to cheer.

Both the Knicks in San Antonio and the Spurs at MSG have used that dynamic to their advantage. Tonight, the Knicks need to be the team that breaks it. They need to carry the crowd's energy, not be carried away by it.

What's Next

Game 4 tips off tonight at 8:30 PM ET on ABC at Madison Square Garden. A Knicks victory would push them to within one game of their first championship since 1973. A Spurs victory would even the series and send it back to San Antonio for Game 5 on Saturday.

The road-team streak, the free-throw controversy, Brunson's efficiency, and Wembanyama's ability to deliver back-to-back masterpieces — all of it gets resolved tonight. One way or another, the 2026 NBA Finals takes its decisive turn in the next 48 minutes of basketball.
