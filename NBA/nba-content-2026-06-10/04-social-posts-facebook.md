### STORY 5: The Road-Team Curse — Historical Analysis

#### Long-Form Post — 4:00 PM ET

Something historically unusual is happening in the 2026 NBA Finals — and no one has an explanation.

The road team has won all three games played so far. The Knicks went to San Antonio and took Games 1 and 2. The Spurs came to Madison Square Garden and took Game 3. All three visiting teams. All three wins.

This has only happened once before in the entire history of the NBA Finals. The pattern has a disorienting effect: the roaring MSG crowd Monday night didn't give the Knicks an edge — it might have worked against them. The same thing happened in San Antonio in Games 1 and 2. The noise, the expectation, the crowd wanting a win so badly — none of it translated to results on the court.

Tonight is Game 4, again at MSG. If the Spurs win, it would be the first time in NBA Finals history that the visiting team won the first four games. No team has ever gone 4-0 on the road to start a Finals.

For the Knicks, breaking this pattern isn't just a nice-to-have. It's a necessity. They need to win tonight to preserve their 2-1 series lead and avoid heading back to San Antonio for Game 5 in a 2-2 series.

For the Spurs, the pattern is their friend. They haven't needed home court. They've shown they can win anywhere.

Tonight we find out if the curse has any logic behind it — or if it just ends.
