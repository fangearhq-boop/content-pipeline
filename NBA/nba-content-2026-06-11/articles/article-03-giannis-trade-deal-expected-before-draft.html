Giannis Trade Expected Before June 23 Draft as Bucks' Timeline Clears

By Damon Pierce | June 11, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The NBA's biggest offseason domino is about to fall — and the timeline just got a lot clearer.

League sources now have increasing belief that a trade involving Giannis Antetokounmpo will be completed before the June 23 NBA Draft. With the Knicks leading the Finals 3-1 and a championship clinch on Saturday looking increasingly likely, the waiting game the Milwaukee Bucks had been playing is over.

Why the Bucks Were Waiting

According to reports, the Milwaukee front office was monitoring the NBA Finals closely — specifically whether the Knicks would blow their 2-0 series lead and extend the Finals deep into June. A longer series would have given the Bucks more time to work a deal and potentially more leverage, with teams spending more time focused on basketball and less on offseason roster moves.

Instead, New York is three wins in with Game 5 on Saturday. The Finals will almost certainly be over before the week ends, leaving the full attention of the league — and every trade partner — pointed squarely at Milwaukee for the final stretch before the draft.

Miami Still Leads

The Miami Heat remain the frontrunner. Multiple league sources continue to point toward Miami as Giannis's preferred destination, with the expected package centered on Tyler Herro, Jaime Jaquez Jr., Kel'el Ware, and multiple first-round picks. The Heat have been consistent throughout this process — aggressive and focused in a way that other teams have not matched.

The complication remains the same one that has shadowed Miami's pursuit for weeks. Giannis has genuine concerns about what the Heat's roster looks like on the other side of a deal that would require them to gut their depth. Playing alongside Bam Adebayo, Andrew Wiggins, and role players assembled on the edges of the cap is not the same as playing alongside the kind of co-stars he would need to compete for a championship immediately.

The Rest of the Field

The Oklahoma City Thunder have taken themselves out of the market. The Boston Celtics have shown interest but have not engaged in direct talks, and NBA insider Brian Windhorst has publicly questioned whether Giannis is even on their radar. The broader market has been described as cooling — "much more tepid than expected" — but Miami's position at the front has not changed.

The alternative for Giannis is staying in Milwaukee. A four-year, $275 million extension will be available to him on October 1. Bucks co-owner Jimmy Haslam has made clear he wants this resolved before the draft. If no deal materializes by June 23, the landscape looks very different.

What's Next

The clock on the Giannis trade is now ticking in real time. Twelve days remain until the June 23 draft. A resolution — trade or extension — should arrive in that window. Miami remains the team most likely to land him. But in a process that has taken more turns than expected, nothing is final until it is announced.

Watch for activity to accelerate as soon as the NBA Finals ends. Bucks general manager Jon Horst will have fewer distractions and more urgency. So will every team in the market.
