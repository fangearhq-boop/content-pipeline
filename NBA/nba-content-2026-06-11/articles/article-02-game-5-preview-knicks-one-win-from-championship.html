Game 5 Preview: Knicks One Win From First NBA Title Since 1973

By Marcus Cole | June 11, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The New York Knicks have been waiting 53 years for this. On Saturday night in San Antonio, they could end the wait.

Game 5 of the 2026 NBA Finals tips off at 8:30 PM ET on ABC at Frost Bank Center, and the Knicks carry a 3-1 series lead into the building where they also won Games 1 and 2. The math and history are overwhelmingly in their favor. Only one franchise has ever blown a 3-1 lead in the NBA Finals — the 2016 Golden State Warriors, who needed the all-time performance of LeBron James to lose what had seemed like a certain championship.

For New York, Saturday is about completing the job. For San Antonio, it is about survival.

The Market Still Believes in the Spurs

Despite facing elimination, the San Antonio Spurs opened as 5.5-point favorites for Game 5. That number reveals what the market knows: the Spurs are playing in their building, their best player is capable of being the best player on the floor in any game, and Game 4's second-half collapse was unusual enough that projecting it to repeat feels unwise.

Victor Wembanyama went 9-of-25 in Game 4. He still finished with 24 points and 13 rebounds over 44 minutes. He told reporters afterward that it "just hurts." That is not the language of a player who has given up. He has already reset after adversity in this series — after the errant pass in Game 2, he delivered 32 points in Game 3. The question is whether he can do it again with the season on the line.

The Knicks' Road Formula

New York knows how to win in San Antonio. They did it in Game 1 and Game 2, setting the tone for a series they have controlled more often than not. Jalen Brunson was the engine in both wins — attacking the rim, drawing fouls, creating for teammates. OG Anunoby has now found an entirely different gear, coming off a playoff career-high 33 points in Game 4.

Karl-Anthony Towns has been the steady force throughout — a presence on both ends who frees up Brunson and gives the Spurs a second elite threat to account for. If those three are playing at their recent levels in Frost Bank Center on Saturday, the Knicks' formula is simple enough: defend, run, attack, and close.

The Historical Weight

The 1973 Knicks won the title on May 10 in Los Angeles. In the more than five decades since, New York has been close — the 1994 Finals loss to Houston is the most painful near-miss — but never close enough. This roster, this coach, and this moment represent the best chance the Knicks have had in a generation.

Winning in San Antonio would write an additional chapter to the narrative: the Knicks opened this series as road underdogs in Games 1 and 2, won both, then came back from 29 down in Game 4 to put themselves here. A championship clinched on the road, in the building of the team that ended their dynasty in 1999, would be fitting.

What's Next

Game 5 tips off Saturday, June 13, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC. A Knicks win sends Madison Square Garden into the kind of celebration that has not happened since Willis Reed. A Spurs win sends the series back to New York for Game 6 on Monday and keeps Wembanyama's dream alive.

History says the Knicks should close it out. History also said a 29-point Finals halftime lead was safe. In this series, no lead has been final and nothing has been certain until the final seconds. Saturday night will be no different.
