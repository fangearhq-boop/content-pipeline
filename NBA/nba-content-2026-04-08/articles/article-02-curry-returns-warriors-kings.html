Curry Makes His Return Count — Two Four-Point Plays Power Warriors Past Kings 110-105

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 8, 2026

It's only been two games. Stephen Curry is still shaking off rust from a right knee injury that kept him off the floor for weeks. And yet, when the Golden State Warriors needed him most on Tuesday night, he delivered a sequence that felt entirely vintage.

Down to Sacramento in the final minutes, Curry hit a tying 3-pointer with 2:38 remaining, then drew a foul — converting the four-point play. Moments later, he set up Brandin Podziemski for the go-ahead triple. Two four-point plays. One gutted-out, badly-needed win. Golden State 110, Sacramento 105.

The Numbers and the Narrative

Curry finished with 17 points in the victory. De'Anthony Melton led the Warriors with 21 points, and Podziemski added 20. But the stat line only tells part of the story — Curry's impact arrived in the clutch moments that determine playoff-caliber games.

Golden State had lost four in a row heading into Tuesday. Sacramento has now fallen in 6 of their last 8 games. The Kings' Killian Hayes contributed 18 points off the bench, and Maxime Raynaud added 17 points and 8 rebounds in what happened to be his 23rd birthday. It wasn't enough.

For the Warriors, snapping the skid matters. But snapping it with Curry showing these kinds of flashes is what really gets the fan base optimistic.

Play-In Stakes

Golden State currently sits in the No. 10 spot in the Western Conference play-in picture. To advance to the playoffs, they'll need to navigate the play-in tournament, which begins April 14.

The Warriors with Curry healthy are a legitimately dangerous team. His shooting gravity alone forces defenses to make impossible choices. Add a capable supporting cast around him — Melton as a defensive-minded contributor, Podziemski developing his offensive game — and Golden State has the pieces to pull off an upset.

The question has always been about Curry's knee. How close is he to full strength? Tuesday was a positive step. Two four-point plays off the bench of a comeback win suggest the machinery is warming up.

What's Next

Golden State has a handful of regular season games remaining before the play-in begins. Curry will almost certainly get additional reps to build toward full health, and the Warriors will want to enter the play-in with momentum.

Their first play-in game will be a home-or-away matchup depending on seeding, facing one of the teams in the 7-10 range. Given what Curry showed on Tuesday, that conversation is now a lot more interesting than it was a week ago.

The defense of Golden State's dynasty may be on life support after this season. But Steph Curry making the most of his return is a reminder: write them off at your own risk.
