Dosunmu Shines as Timberwolves Dominate Pacers 124-104 in Late-Season Statement

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 8, 2026

The Minnesota Timberwolves continue to find ways to win, and Tuesday night's 124-104 dismantling of the Indiana Pacers was another example of a team operating with purpose in the season's final week.

Ayo Dosunmu was the standout performer, scoring 24 points on 10-of-17 shooting while adding 5 rebounds and 3 steals in 33 minutes. He was the engine of an offense that controlled the game from the early minutes and never let Indiana get comfortable.

Dosunmu's Steady Rise

Ayo Dosunmu doesn't always get the headlines — that's what happens when you share a roster with legitimate star power. But performances like Tuesday's are why Minnesota values him. His efficiency (10-of-17 from the field), his willingness to get into passing lanes (3 steals), and his ability to make plays both in the half-court and in transition make him exactly the kind of player playoff teams need.

Dosunmu's 24 points weren't flash. They were the product of smart basketball — working screens, getting to the rim, and not forcing shots from outside his comfort zone. That kind of composure doesn't develop overnight, and Minnesota has benefited from it all season.

Timberwolves Locking In

Minnesota sits at No. 6 in the Western Conference and is focused on finishing the regular season strong before the playoff push begins. The Timberwolves have depth and versatility — multiple players capable of leading them on any given night — which is the hallmark of a team that can navigate a long playoff run.

Indiana's season is winding down without the postseason appearance they would have hoped for. The Pacers have talent to build around, but Tuesday's performance showed the gap that can exist between teams at different points in their trajectories.

What's Next

Minnesota wraps up the regular season before turning its attention fully to first-round playoff preparations. The Timberwolves are a dangerous team — physical, deep, and capable of winning games multiple ways. They are not the most discussed team in the West entering April, but their résumé suggests they should be.

Performances like Dosunmu's on Tuesday are the reason. They have contributors at every level of the rotation, and they're playing their best basketball heading into the postseason. That combination — depth plus timing — is what playoff runs are built on.

Minnesota's first-round opponent will be determined in the final days of the regular season. Whoever it is will know they're in for a physical, well-prepared series.
