Rockets Run It to Seven — Kevin Durant and Houston Surge to No. 5 West Seed

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 8, 2026

Seven consecutive victories. That is what the Houston Rockets have put together in the closing stretch of the 2025-26 NBA regular season, and nobody in the Western Conference is celebrating that number more than the Rockets themselves.

Tuesday night's 119-105 win over the Phoenix Suns was Houston's latest statement. Kevin Durant led the way with 24 points and 5 three-pointers, and the Rockets barely broke a sweat. The result pushed Houston to the No. 5 seed in the West — and with four days left in the regular season, their momentum is undeniable.

Durant Continuing to Deliver

Kevin Durant at 37 is still a weapon. His ability to score from anywhere on the floor — mid-range, the three-point line, driving to the basket — makes him one of the most difficult covers in the game regardless of age. Tuesday's 5-of-however-many from three was a reminder that defenders cannot sag off him, which opens the floor for Houston's entire offense.

The Rockets have built a complete team around Durant. It's not a one-man show. Their defense has been stout, their depth consistent, and their ability to close games has been on display throughout this win streak. Seven straight wins doesn't happen by accident — it's the product of a team that has figured something out.

The Seeding Implications

Houston at No. 5 matters enormously in the Western bracket. The No. 5 seed faces the No. 4 seed in the first round — which, depending on how the final games shake out, could mean a matchup with the Los Angeles Lakers.

A Lakers-Rockets first-round series would be one of the most marketable playoff matchups possible: LeBron and company against an experienced Houston squad riding a seven-game wave of momentum. That's the kind of first-round matchup that draws national attention.

Phoenix, meanwhile, is heading in the opposite direction. The Suns gave up 119 points and find themselves in play-in territory with very little time to recover.

What's Next

The Rockets have a few regular-season games remaining to either extend the streak or rest their key players heading into the playoffs. Given the momentum they've built, don't expect Houston to hit the brakes early.

If they enter the playoffs on an 8 or 9-game win streak, the Rockets will be the most dangerous team nobody is fully talking about. The West is loaded — OKC, San Antonio, Denver, the Lakers. But seven straight wins entering April says Houston deserves to be in that conversation.

Watch the Rockets. They are not done surprising people.
