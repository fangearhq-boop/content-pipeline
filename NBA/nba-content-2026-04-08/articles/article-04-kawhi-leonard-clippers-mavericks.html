Kawhi Leonard Erupts for 34 Points, Clippers Top Mavericks 116-103 in Play-In Tune-Up

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 8, 2026

When Kawhi Leonard is healthy and locked in, the LA Clippers have a different identity. On Tuesday night against the Dallas Mavericks, he was both — and the result was 34 points, 6 three-pointers, and a 116-103 victory that keeps the Clippers squarely in the Western Conference play-in picture.

Darius Garland contributed 22 points in support as the Clippers controlled the game throughout. The win reinforces Los Angeles's spot in the postseason bracket — a position they've been fighting to protect as the regular season winds down.

Kawhi in Full Form

There is no manual for guarding Kawhi Leonard. His combination of size, footwork, and shooting touch from the mid-range and beyond the arc makes him a problem for any defensive scheme. On Tuesday, his 6 three-pointers were the exclamation point on a complete offensive performance.

He got to his spots, he hit shots, and the Clippers offense flowed around his gravity. When Kawhi is producing at this level, Los Angeles is a legitimate threat to cause problems in the play-in and potentially beyond it. The challenge has always been keeping him on the floor, and right now, he appears healthy and motivated.

Cooper Flagg Keeps Fighting for Dallas

Tuesday's loss was a tough one for Dallas, but Cooper Flagg gave the Mavericks faithful something worth watching. The rookie posted 25 points and 9 rebounds in the defeat — a strong individual line in a game his team couldn't win.

Flagg has been one of the more compelling stories of the 2025-26 season. His development has been real and consistent, and even in losses, he competes at a level that suggests he'll be the centerpiece of whatever Dallas builds going forward. Tuesday was another data point in that conversation.

What's Next

The Clippers carry their play-in positioning into the final days of the regular season. Los Angeles needs to finish strong to maintain or improve their seeding. The play-in field is competitive — Golden State, Portland, and Phoenix are all jostling for position.

For Dallas, the season's final chapter isn't about playoff positioning. It's about evaluating what they have in Flagg, building chemistry for next year, and finishing with some dignity. Tuesday showed the roster has fight left. That's not nothing heading into an offseason where the Mavericks will have decisions to make.

The play-in begins April 14. The Clippers are a team to watch — especially when Kawhi shows up like he did on Tuesday.
