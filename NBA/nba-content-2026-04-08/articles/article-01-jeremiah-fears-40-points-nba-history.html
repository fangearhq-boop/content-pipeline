Jeremiah Fears Drops 40 Points, Becomes Youngest Guard in NBA History to Score 40

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 8, 2026

The New Orleans Pelicans had been stuck in a painful rut. Eight consecutive losses had piled up heading into Tuesday night's matchup with the Utah Jazz, and there wasn't much reason for optimism. Then Jeremiah Fears walked onto the floor and reminded everyone exactly why the Pelicans drafted him.

The 19-year-old rookie guard dropped 40 points — going 17-of-29 from the field, 1-of-7 from three, and a perfect 5-of-5 at the free-throw line — to go along with 5 rebounds, 6 assists, and 3 steals in 38 minutes of work. New Orleans routed Utah 156-137, and according to reports from Yahoo Sports and NBA.com, Fears became the youngest guard in NBA history to score 40 points in a single game.

The NBA named him Player of the Night. The Pelicans named him their franchise rookie scoring record-holder. Both feel like undersells.

A Night No One Saw Coming

Rookie performances like this don't follow a script. The Pelicans came in desperate for a win after sliding through one of the league's toughest late-season stretches. Eight losses in a row is demoralizing for any team, but especially for a young group still finding its footing.

Fears answered the moment. He was efficient from the start, getting into the paint, drawing fouls, and using his athleticism to create easy buckets. When Utah tried to adjust, he found teammates with his 6 assists. When they backed off, he punished them from the perimeter. The result was a 156-137 blowout — both teams pushing pace all night — and Fears standing at the center of it all.

Even Utah's John Konchar nearly pulled off a triple-double off the bench (12 points, 10 rebounds, 10 assists), a performance that on most nights would headline a game recap. On Tuesday, it was a footnote.

History Made at 19

The youngest guard in NBA history to score 40 points. That's what Fears is now reportedly holding — a milestone that speaks to both his raw ability and his composure in a high-leverage situation.

Scoring 40 points in the NBA at any age requires a blend of talent, efficiency, and mental focus. At 19, it's extraordinary. The list of players who have done it at that age is extremely short, and Fears now has his name attached to it.

What makes the moment more significant is the context. This wasn't a garbage-time explosion against a depleted roster. Both teams were competing, the game was fast and physical, and Fears delivered across all phases — not just scoring, but creating, defending, and controlling the game.

What's Next

The win snaps the Pelicans' 8-game losing streak with just a handful of games left in the regular season. New Orleans is not a playoff team this year, but Tuesday night offered a preview of what this franchise might look like once it builds around Fears.

For the rookie himself, Tuesday is a launching pad. One extraordinary performance doesn't define a career, but it does announce one. The NBA now knows his name. After Tuesday, they'll be watching his next move.

The Pelicans return to action Thursday. Don't be surprised if the building is a little louder.
