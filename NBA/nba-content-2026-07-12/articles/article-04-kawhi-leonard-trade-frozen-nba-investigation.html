Kawhi Leonard Trade Still Frozen — Commissioner Silver Calls for Finality as Clippers Investigation Drags On

By Jake Torres | July 12, 2026

NBA Offseason

The trade that would send Kawhi Leonard to the Toronto Raptors has been agreed upon by both franchises for weeks. It is still not done.

The NBA's investigation into the Los Angeles Clippers' alleged use of an Aspiration Fund to make impermissible payments to Leonard continues without a formal resolution, leaving the deal frozen and both organizations unable to finalize their offseason plans. Commissioner Adam Silver acknowledged the situation this week, stating that he believed the league was "close to the point where we need to wrap this up, because you also need finality."

But close to the point and at the point are different things — and for now, Kawhi Leonard is in an unusual state of professional suspension, officially belonging to neither team.

What the Investigation Involves

The NBA is investigating claims that the Clippers circumvented the salary cap by funneling money to Leonard through the organization's Aspiration Fund, a vehicle that had previously been framed as an internal charitable initiative. If the allegations are proven true, it would represent one of the most significant cap violations in recent league history.

The Clippers have denied the allegations directly. The organization released a statement declaring that they "did not funnel money to Kawhi Leonard" and that "we have not done what we are accused of doing." The league is evaluating evidence that contradicts that characterization, which is what has prolonged the investigation.

The trade terms — which would send Leonard to Toronto along with related compensation — were agreed to as part of the broader summer trade window. The Raptors have been preparing to build around Leonard as a franchise centerpiece, a plan that has been in a holding pattern ever since the investigation began.

Silver Signals Urgency

Silver's public comments this week marked the first time the Commissioner directly acknowledged the need for resolution in specific terms. "I think we're close to the point now where I think we need to wrap this up" is the strongest statement from league offices since the investigation became public.

His reference to "finality" carries particular weight. The NBA's ability to enforce the salary cap depends in part on investigations proceeding with some degree of predictable timing. Franchises cannot plan effectively when the status of a major trade remains legally uncertain for extended periods.

For the Raptors, the limbo is especially difficult. Toronto has operated with the expectation that Leonard would be joining the roster, and has likely limited certain other roster decisions accordingly. If the trade is ultimately cleared, their offseason unfolds one way. If it is not, an entirely different rebuild begins.

Leonard's Unusual Position

Leonard himself has been largely silent throughout the investigation. He is contractually associated with the Clippers pending the NBA's decision, which means he cannot participate in any Raptors activities, cannot appear in Toronto's facilities, and cannot begin building relationships with what may be his next team.

It is a strange position for a player who agreed to a trade and expected his professional situation to be settled by now. Leonard is not responsible for the investigation's existence, but its consequences fall on his offseason timeline as much as anyone's.

The Clippers, meanwhile, have continued operating as an organization in transition — they agreed to the trade knowing the investigation was ongoing, and they have maintained their denial throughout the process.

What's Next

Silver's language suggests a resolution could come within weeks rather than months. The question is what that resolution looks like: a full clearance of the trade, a finding against the Clippers with penalties attached, or some form of negotiated settlement that allows the trade to proceed under modified terms.

Any of those outcomes would allow both franchises to finally move forward. Until then, the Kawhi Leonard trade remains one of the most consequential unresolved items in a summer that has already seen enormous movement across the league.
