Spurs Lock Down Defending Champion Knicks 70-49 — Carter Bryant Stars in Vegas Defensive Clinic

By Jake Torres | July 12, 2026

NBA Summer League

The defending NBA champions had one of their worst offensive performances of the year on Saturday — even if it only happened in Summer League.

San Antonio held New York to just 26.5 percent shooting from the field and rolled to a 70-49 victory at Las Vegas Summer League Day 4, one of the most dominant defensive performances of the tournament so far. Carter Bryant led all scorers with 19 points as the Spurs made a statement at the Thomas & Mack Center.

Bryant Runs the Show

Carter Bryant entered Las Vegas as one of the quieter names on San Antonio's Summer League roster. After Saturday, that changes.

Bryant's 19-point game-high was the product of efficient scoring at both ends of the offensive spectrum — he found openings off the ball, finished at the rim, and knocked down shots when the defense scrambled to close out. For a Spurs team that prides itself on developing young talent within a structured system, Bryant fit the mold precisely.

He won't carry the type of hype that follows Victor Wembanyama — nobody on the roster will — but Spurs coaches will have taken note. San Antonio's ability to cultivate overlooked prospects into reliable rotation pieces is well-documented, and Bryant's Saturday performance suggests he might be the next name to move in that direction.

Knicks Couldn't Find the Basket

For New York, 26.5 percent shooting represents a hard night regardless of the competition. The Knicks couldn't establish any consistent offensive rhythm against a Spurs unit that stayed disciplined in its rotations, communicated well on switches, and contested every shot at the perimeter without fouling.

The 49-point total is a product of efficiency woes compounding across four quarters. The Knicks' Summer League group — composed largely of developmental prospects and two-way candidates — was unable to generate clean looks against San Antonio's scheme. It was 70-49 at the final buzzer, and the margin was not misleading.

None of this impacts what the Knicks' core group accomplished in the regular season. Jalen Brunson's Finals MVP run and the franchise's championship are safe in the record books. But Summer League is about roster construction evaluation, and Saturday's performance left questions unanswered.

San Antonio's Developing Core

The Spurs agreed to a five-year, $252 million max extension with Wembanyama last week, locking in their franchise centerpiece through the 2031-32 season. The Summer League group won't feature Wembanyama, but it offers a look at the supporting cast taking shape around him.

Saturday's defensive performance showed organizational identity. The Spurs play connected basketball on that end — they don't gamble for steals, they don't leave the paint exposed, and they make opponents earn every possession. That structure doesn't appear by accident in Summer League. It comes from coaching and a buy-in to organizational culture.

Bryant's 19 points were the highlight, but the defensive box score tells the story San Antonio wanted to tell. Twenty-one-point margin. 26.5 percent opponent shooting. If that's what the Spurs' Summer League rotation can produce, the pieces around Wembanyama are more interesting than expected.

What's Next

The Spurs and Knicks will continue at Las Vegas Summer League, which runs through July 19. Bryant will have more opportunities to build on Saturday's performance as scouts continue to evaluate both rosters through the week.

San Antonio's next assignment will test whether Saturday's defensive display represents a sustainable identity or a single-game peak. Given the Spurs' history of replicating consistent performances regardless of opponent, the early evidence points toward the former.

For the Knicks, the focus is simply finding some offensive rhythm before the roster finalizes. The championship run is the measure of success for this franchise right now — not Summer League results — but the development of supporting pieces matters heading into the 2026-27 title defense.
