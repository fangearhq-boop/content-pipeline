Rich Paul Says LeBron Decision Not Coming 'Anytime Soon' — Cavaliers Remain Frontrunners at 40%

By Marcus Cole | July 12, 2026

NBA Free Agency

If you were hoping for a LeBron James announcement this week, Rich Paul has some news for you: keep waiting.

James' agent and longtime confidant told reporters that a decision on where LeBron will play next season is not imminent, stating plainly that it "won't be in the next few days" and that he doesn't think it happens "anytime soon." The comments, which came during a week that included Paul appearing on a podcast with Philadelphia 76ers president Bob Myers, offered the clearest public timeline guidance yet — and that guidance is essentially no timeline at all.

Cavs Still Lead, But the Race Is Open

The Cleveland Cavaliers remain the frontrunners, holding approximately 40 percent odds on prediction markets as of Saturday. The Golden State Warriors and 76ers are each hovering around 15 percent. The Miami Heat, Minnesota Timberwolves, Denver Nuggets, and other teams round out a six-team field that Paul confirmed as the active consideration set.

Cleveland's case is straightforward: Donovan Mitchell signed a four-year, $273 million extension that signals a commitment to sustained excellence. The Cavaliers came off arguably their best non-LeBron season in three decades. And LeBron spent his summer in Akron — which, if you're reading tea leaves, says something.

But Paul went to significant lengths to emphasize that this process is different. "This is the first time LeBron James is making a decision completely pressure-free," Paul said. "This is strictly for his happiness." After two decades of decisions shaped by championship windows, roster construction, and player-movement calculus, James is apparently approaching this one on his own terms.

27 Teams, No Deadline

Paul has spoken to representatives from 27 of the 30 NBA teams on LeBron's behalf. That number is remarkable — it suggests that the recruiting process has been thorough almost to the point of exhaustion, with very few doors left unexplored.

James, who will turn 42 during the 2026-27 season, averaged 20.9 points, 7.2 assists, and 6.1 rebounds last season on 51.5 percent shooting. He remains a productive, valuable player. The question is not whether a team can use him — virtually all 30 could — but whether the fit is right enough to justify his attention.

Paul described the situation as a "blank slate" for LeBron — a rare opportunity to define his own narrative without external pressure. Previous decisions, most notably "The Decision" in 2010 and the 2018 move to Los Angeles, carried enormous contextual weight. This one, Paul suggests, is more personal.

What the Odds Actually Tell Us

At 40 percent, the Cavaliers have a substantial lead in prediction markets — but 40 percent also means there is a 60 percent chance LeBron goes somewhere else. Those numbers matter. The 76ers' recruitment — symbolized by Bob Myers appearing on Paul's podcast — signals that Philadelphia is actively working its angle. A roster featuring Tyrese Maxey, Joel Embiid, and now Jaylen Brown is a genuine contender, and LeBron would know exactly where he stood in the pecking order there.

The Warriors present a different kind of appeal. Stephen Curry remains one of the most beloved teammates in league history, and Golden State has roughly $15 million in mid-level exception money to offer. A LeBron-Curry pairing would be historically unprecedented and culturally massive.

Cleveland offers something else: home. The narrative of a championship return, the community connection, the closing chapter written in the city where the most famous chapter of his career was already written. Paul's "pressure-free" framing might ultimately point in that direction — this being LeBron's chance to choose what resonates most deeply rather than what optimizes his odds of a ring.

What's Next

There is no deadline. The NBA season doesn't begin until October, and teams can afford to wait for the most consequential free agent decision of the summer to play out on its own schedule. Paul's comments suggest the waiting will continue for weeks, not days.

Watch for any change in the signals: where LeBron trains, which team facilities he visits, what Paul says next. In a process this open-ended, small details tend to become outsized news. Until then, the Cavaliers lead, the race is real, and LeBron James is taking his time.
