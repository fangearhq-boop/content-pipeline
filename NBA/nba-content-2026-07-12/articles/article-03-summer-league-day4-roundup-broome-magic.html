Summer League Day 4: Johni Broome 23/11 in OT Heroics, Magic Escape Heat, Pelicans Run Down Hornets

By Damon Pierce | July 12, 2026

NBA Summer League

Johni Broome is starting to look like the most consistent performer at Las Vegas Summer League.

The Philadelphia 76ers rookie posted 23 points and 11 rebounds in a 100-93 overtime win over the Indiana Pacers on Saturday, his second straight strong performance of the tournament. Meanwhile, the Orlando Magic edged the Miami Heat 93-88 on a late defensive stop, and the New Orleans Pelicans rallied to beat the Charlotte Hornets 95-91 behind Hunter Dickinson and a closing 15-2 run. Day 4 delivered on multiple fronts.

Broome Making His Case

Before Las Vegas, Johni Broome was a lottery pick who had people intrigued but not quite sold. After two games of Summer League play, there is a building consensus: the 76ers selected someone who can produce at the pro level right away.

Saturday's 23-point, 11-rebound outing came in an overtime game, which means Broome played through a full 45 minutes of action and still finished with a double-double. His second game has been stronger than his first, which itself was strong — the trajectory is pointing up.

What makes Broome appealing is his combination of size, skill, and competitive temperament. He finishes in traffic, he rebounds on both ends, and he doesn't disappear when the game gets physical. Philadelphia's front office has to feel good about what they've seen this week.

Magic Edge Heat on a Late Stop

The Orlando Magic's 93-88 win over the Miami Heat came down to a single defensive play in the final seconds. Izaiyah Nelson denied Jahmir Young's game-tying three-point attempt with seven seconds left, and Orlando held on for the five-point victory.

Close wins in Summer League carry limited predictive value, but defensive awareness and clutch-moment composure are traits that scouts prioritize at every level of competition. Nelson's read on Young's shot attempt — understanding the situation, identifying the shooter, contesting without fouling — showed exactly that.

The Heat, who added Giannis Antetokounmpo to their roster earlier this month, are in a different situation than teams using Summer League to evaluate rookies and fringe prospects. Miami's Summer League group is entirely about development, not about reflecting what the regular-season roster will look like.

Pelicans Close Strong, Dickinson Stands Out

New Orleans defeated Charlotte 95-91, but the score doesn't capture how the game finished. The Pelicans closed on a 15-2 run to escape what appeared to be shaping up as an upset for the Hornets. Hunter Dickinson was the catalyst with 21 points — a productive performance from a big man whose future in New Orleans has been discussed as one of the more interesting offseason storylines around the team.

Dickinson's size and skill set as a floor-spacing center has appealed to coaches and front offices looking for modern big men who can do multiple things. Saturday's output — 21 points, presumably with efficient shooting — only adds to the case for a larger role.

Rest of the Day 4 Slate

The Denver Nuggets defeated the Minnesota Timberwolves behind KJ Simpson's 19-point effort, with the Nuggets pulling away in the fourth quarter. Simpson showed quickness in transition and a comfort level on the offensive end that suggests he's been prepared well for this setting.

The Atlanta Hawks closed out the Brooklyn Nets with a clutch fourth-quarter stretch, capping a Saturday that saw several games decided in the final minutes. Tight games in Summer League test composure in ways that blowouts don't — and more than a few players took advantage of the stage.

What's Next

Las Vegas Summer League continues through July 19, and the slate of games will grow increasingly meaningful as teams near the elimination rounds. Broome and the 76ers will be one of the most-watched groups as he pushes toward Summer League Rookie of the Year consideration.

Wilson and Boozer — the two highest picks in the tournament — will also have upcoming games that attract outsized attention. After Wilson's 35-point debut on Thursday and Boozer's 23-point winner, the early days of Vegas Summer League have delivered the theater the opening schedule promised.
