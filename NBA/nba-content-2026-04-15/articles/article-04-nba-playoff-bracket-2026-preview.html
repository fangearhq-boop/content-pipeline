2026 NBA Playoff Bracket Preview: 12 Teams Set, Four Spots Remain After Play-In Night 1

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 15, 2026

With the Blazers locked into the No. 7 West seed and Wednesday's games deciding two more seeds, the 2026 NBA Playoffs field is nearly complete ahead of the April 18 first-round start.

Eastern Conference: Four Seeds Confirmed, Four to Be Determined

The Eastern Conference's top six playoff seeds were set before the play-in began, and two matchups are already confirmed for the first round.

The No. 3 New York Knicks face the No. 6 Atlanta Hawks — a matchup that pits New York's playoff experience against Atlanta's hot second-half play. The No. 4 Cleveland Cavaliers draw the No. 5 Toronto Raptors — Donovan Mitchell and James Harden versus Scottie Barnes and one of the top-five rated defenses in the NBA.

The No. 1 Detroit Pistons and No. 2 Boston Celtics each wait for their play-in opponents. Detroit's 60-22 record — their first 60-win season since 2005-06 — earns them home-court advantage through the East bracket. Boston, which won the championship two seasons ago, is the most experienced team in the field.

Wednesday's 76ers-Magic game decides the East No. 7 seed. The loser gets a second chance Friday against Charlotte. By Saturday, the No. 8 seed will be set and the Pistons and Celtics will know exactly who they're playing.

Western Conference: Confirmed Matchups and the Remaining Pieces

The West is where the most compelling first-round matchup is already confirmed.

The No. 2 San Antonio Spurs — 62-20, owners of the NBA's second-best record, winners of 24 of their last 27 games — will face the No. 7 Portland Trail Blazers beginning April 18. That means Victor Wembanyama, who is expected to win the Defensive Player of the Year award in near-unanimous fashion, matches up against Deni Avdija, who just posted one of the most electric performances in play-in tournament history. The Spurs are the heavy favorites. The Blazers just demonstrated they are not to be overlooked.

The No. 3 Denver Nuggets — led by Nikola Jokic — face the No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves in what should be one of the most physical series of the first round. The No. 4 Los Angeles Lakers face the No. 5 Houston Rockets in a Western Conference rivalry renewed with high stakes.

The No. 1 Oklahoma City Thunder — SGA's 64-16 team — hold the top seed and home-court advantage throughout the West. They await a play-in opponent; whoever it is will be outmatched on paper. The Warriors-Clippers loser-goes-home game tonight will determine who faces the Suns in Friday's final West play-in game.

The Matchup to Circle: Spurs vs. Blazers

On paper, this is the most lopsided matchup in the bracket — a 62-win powerhouse against a team that needed an overtime-era play-in win to get into the field. But Avdija's 41-point performance on Tuesday reframed what Portland is capable of when their best player is locked in. Wembanyama is exceptional, but Avdija's combination of scoring and playmaking — 41 points and 12 assists in one game — gives Portland a blueprint. How San Antonio adjusts to that threat will define the opening games of the series.

What's Next

Wednesday night's two play-in games — 76ers-Magic at 7:30 ET and Warriors-Clippers at 10 ET on Prime Video — determine two more seeds and eliminate two teams. Friday's games clear the final spots. Then, on Saturday, April 18, the 2026 NBA Playoffs officially begin. The bracket, through the chaos of the play-in, is nearly set. The actual championship conversation is about to start.
