Deni Avdija Drops 41 in Playoff Debut as Blazers Stun Suns 114-110 to Earn No. 7 Seed

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 15, 2026

The Portland forward erased an 11-point fourth-quarter deficit with a performance that ranks among the greatest in play-in tournament history.

A Debut for the Ages

Deni Avdija had never played in an NBA postseason game before Tuesday night. He picked an interesting time to have the best game of his life.

Avdija posted 41 points, 12 assists, seven rebounds, and two blocks on 15-of-22 shooting from the field as the Portland Trail Blazers overcame an 11-point fourth-quarter deficit to beat the Phoenix Suns 114-110 and claim the No. 7 seed in the Western Conference playoffs. His 41 points are the fifth-highest total in play-in tournament history, joining an elite group that includes Jayson Tatum, Coby White, Zion Williamson, and Anthony Davis.

What separated Avdija's performance wasn't just the volume — it was the timing. With 16.1 seconds remaining and Portland trailing by a possession, Avdija drove to the basket, drew contact, finished through it, and converted the free throw. That 3-point play gave Portland a lead it wouldn't surrender.

"Deni was unreal," Jrue Holiday said afterward. "There's no other word for it."

A 17-5 Closing Run That Changed Everything

The Blazers did not look like the better team for most of Tuesday night. Phoenix's Jalen Green was exceptional, scoring 35 points on 14-of-29 shooting and keeping the Suns competitive through the midgame stretches that might otherwise have given Portland breathing room. Devin Booker added 22 and Dillon Brooks contributed 20 off the Suns bench, giving Phoenix multiple sources of offense.

Portland trailed by 11 in the fourth quarter. The margin felt significant — the Blazers are not built to come from behind against a team with Phoenix's offensive firepower. But Avdija refused to accept the situation as settled. His scoring in the fourth quarter anchored a 17-5 Portland closing run that turned what looked like a Suns win into a Blazers celebration.

Jrue Holiday provided 21 points and the veteran leadership that helped Portland stay composed when it would have been easy to fold. Jerami Grant's two clutch three-pointers in the closing minutes — both of which stopped Phoenix from answering — proved as important as anything else.

The Blazers Are Back

Portland last appeared in the NBA Playoffs in 2021. Five years is a long time in professional basketball — careers end, rosters turn over completely, fan bases lose patience. The Trail Blazers' return to the postseason is built on a core that nobody outside of Portland was paying much attention to entering this season.

Avdija's 24.2 points per game this regular season was a revelation — a Most Improved Player-caliber season that put the league on notice. Tuesday's performance suggested that regular-season number might have been underselling him. Donovan Clingan's interior presence gave Portland something to build around defensively. Holiday's experience tied the group together.

The reward for all of it is a first-round matchup against the No. 2 seed San Antonio Spurs — Victor Wembanyama, a 62-20 team, and one of the most formidable defenses in the league. Portland is the heavy underdog. After Tuesday night, nobody should be confident about exactly how heavy.

What's Next

The Portland Trail Blazers open the first round of the 2026 NBA Playoffs against the San Antonio Spurs on April 18. Avdija against Wembanyama — in what would be two of the most intriguing young talents in the league — is a matchup that deserves more attention than a 7-versus-2 underdog-versus-favorite framing typically generates. The Spurs had the best record in the Western Conference. The Blazers just showed they can beat anyone when Avdija is locked in. The series should be fascinating.
