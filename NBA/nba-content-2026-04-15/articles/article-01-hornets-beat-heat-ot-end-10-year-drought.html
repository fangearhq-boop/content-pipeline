LaMelo's Layup, Bridges' Block: Hornets Beat Heat in OT to End 10-Year Playoff Drought

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 15, 2026

A go-ahead layup with 4.7 seconds left in overtime and a buzzer-beating block sent Charlotte to the postseason for the first time since 2016.

One Play. Ten Years.

LaMelo Ball drove baseline, laid the ball off the glass, and watched it drop through with 4.7 seconds left in overtime. Miles Bridges stepped up on the ensuing possession and swatted Davion Mitchell's desperation attempt out of the air at the buzzer. Charlotte Hornets 127, Miami Heat 126 — and a decade of waiting was over.

The Hornets hadn't been to the postseason since 2016. In a league where rosters turn over rapidly and rebuild timelines stretch from season to season, ten years is an eternity. Tuesday night at Spectrum Center, in front of a crowd that had waited through a lot of hard basketball, this particular eternity came to an end in the most dramatic fashion the play-in tournament has produced yet.

LaMelo Ball finished with 30 points and 10 assists. He was the best player on the floor for most of the night, and then — for a few unbearable moments in regulation — he wasn't. He missed free throws that could have put the game away. He let the Heat claw back. And then Coby White hit a turnaround three with 10.8 seconds left to send it to overtime, and Ball got one more chance to be the hero.

He took it.

The Improbable Run That Got Them Here

What makes Tuesday's result so meaningful is what preceded it. Charlotte opened the 2025-26 season inconsistently, falling to 11-23 on January 2. They weren't just bad — they looked like a team going nowhere fast, their lottery odds climbing with each loss. Then the schedule softened, health returned, and something shifted.

The Hornets went 33-16 over their final 49 games. The finished 44-38, which was good enough for the No. 9 seed in the Eastern Conference play-in. It was a run powered almost entirely by LaMelo Ball's playmaking — he averaged approximately 20.1 points and 7.1 assists across the season — and by a supporting cast that found its roles late. Miles Bridges provided the physicality and secondary scoring the team needed. Coby White gave them a reliable shot-creator off the bench.

Tuesday night was the payoff for all of it.

Miami Pushed Charlotte to the Edge

The Heat — who finished the regular season 43-39 — weren't supposed to make this easy, and they didn't. Davion Mitchell scored 28 points and created consistent problems for Charlotte's defense throughout. Andrew Wiggins added 27, including buckets in key moments when Miami needed them. Tyler Herro, who averaged 20.5 points per game in the regular season, finished with 23 and kept the Heat in the fight every time Charlotte tried to pull away.

The game went back and forth in the fourth quarter, the lead changing hands multiple times. With the Hornets unable to put Miami away, the Heat closed the gap in regulation. Coby White's turnaround three with 10.8 seconds remaining saved Charlotte from an immediate exit, forcing the extra period that ultimately became Ball's moment.

For Miami, the season is over. The Heat made the play-in for the fourth time in the tournament's brief history, and for the second consecutive year they leave without advancing.

What's Next

Charlotte now waits to find out which team it will face in the second play-in game on Friday. Tonight, the No. 7 Orlando Magic host the No. 8 Philadelphia 76ers at Wells Fargo Center in a winner-advances matchup — the loser of that game faces the Hornets on Friday for the East's No. 8 seed. Whoever Charlotte gets, the Hornets will enter as the team riding momentum and the emotion of a historic night. A win Friday locks in their first-round opponent — either the No. 1 Detroit Pistons or the No. 2 Boston Celtics. The postseason feels possible now. It felt impossible in January.
