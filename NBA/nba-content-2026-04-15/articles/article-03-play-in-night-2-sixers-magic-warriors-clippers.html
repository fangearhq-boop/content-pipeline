Play-In Night 2: Embiid-Less 76ers Host Magic, Kawhi vs. Curry in West Elimination Game

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 15, 2026

Wednesday brings two more play-in games — including one missing a franchise cornerstone and another featuring two of the best scorers in the league.

The Question in Philadelphia: Can Maxey Lead the Way?

Joel Embiid will not play tonight. Philadelphia's franchise center required surgery to have his appendix removed after missing the final three games of the regular season with appendicitis. The timetable for his return remains unclear, though the 76ers are hopeful he could be available once the first round begins. For tonight's play-in game, Philadelphia must figure out how to beat the Orlando Magic without him.

That responsibility falls to Tyrese Maxey. The 25-year-old guard has been one of the more consistent offensive performers on a Philadelphia team that has dealt with injury disruptions throughout the season, and this is his moment to step into the franchise cornerstone role that Embiid typically occupies.

The 76ers enter as slight home favorites despite Embiid's absence — a reflection of Philadelphia's advantage at Wells Fargo Center and their 2-1 record against Orlando in the regular season. The Magic come in at nearly full strength and have shown throughout the season that their defense is capable of slowing down higher-seeded opponents.

The winner advances directly to the first round as the No. 7 East seed. The loser gets a second chance Friday night against Charlotte — the Hornets will host after their overtime victory over Miami on Tuesday.

Kawhi Leonard's Career Year Meets Stephen Curry's Playoff Pedigree

At 10 p.m. ET, the Western Conference's ninth and tenth seeds meet at Intuit Dome in a straightforward proposition: win or go home.

Kawhi Leonard has had the best scoring season of his career in 2025-26 — 27.9 points per game, shooting 50.5 percent from the field and 38.7 percent from beyond the arc. The Clippers (42-40) have leaned on Leonard throughout, and his health and availability through this stretch of the season have been one of the few things Los Angeles has been able to count on. He enters Wednesday as the clear best player in this particular game.

But Stephen Curry — and the context of everything he's accomplished in high-stakes moments — is not a player anyone can simply dismiss. Curry averaged 26.6 points per game this season and shot 41.7 percent from three in his last four games. The Warriors (37-45) are underdogs, and appropriately so. But Curry's ability to turn a game on its head with a single stretch of three-point shooting gives Golden State a ceiling that their record does not reflect.

Los Angeles is a 5.5-point favorite. The Clippers had a 3-1 regular-season series edge over Golden State, including a victory in the final regular-season meeting.

The Big Picture

After Tuesday's games, the play-in field has clarified significantly. The Hornets are alive in the East and the Blazers have secured the No. 7 West seed. Tonight's two games complete the picture for the No. 7 and No. 8 seeds in the East and eliminate one more team from the West. Friday brings the final two play-in contests — Charlotte against the 76ers or Magic loser, and the Warriors or Clippers loser against whoever falls out of the West's 7-8 game. By Friday night, the full 16-team playoff bracket will be set for first-round action beginning Saturday, April 18.

Both games air exclusively on Amazon Prime Video. Tip-off for the 76ers-Magic game is 7:30 p.m. ET. The Warriors-Clippers game follows at 10 p.m. ET.

What's Next

Wednesday's results will determine the final structure of Friday's Play-In games and, by extension, who fills the last two playoff spots before the first round begins. The East No. 7 seed — either Philadelphia or Orlando — will be confirmed tonight. The West survivor game Friday pits the Warriors or Clippers loser against the Suns. After that, 16 teams enter the tournament bracket, and the real games begin. The full first round is set to begin April 18.
