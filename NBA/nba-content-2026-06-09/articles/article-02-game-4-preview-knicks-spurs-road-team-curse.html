Game 4 Preview: Can the Knicks Finally Win at Home in the 2026 NBA Finals?

By Marcus Cole | June 9, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Three games into the 2026 NBA Finals, the basketball world is grappling with the same strange question: why does the road team keep winning?

The Knicks won Games 1 and 2 in San Antonio. The Spurs answered by winning Game 3 at Madison Square Garden. Every game has gone to the visiting team. This is only the second time in NBA Finals history that the road team has won each of the first three games of a series. Nobody predicted it. Nobody has a clean explanation for it. And now Game 4 tips off Wednesday at 8:30 PM ET on ABC — still at MSG — and everyone is wondering whether the pattern continues.

What the Numbers Say

The Knicks entered this series as 2.5-point favorites in Game 3 and lost by four. They were favored in both San Antonio road games and won. The market's predictive power has been essentially zero. What has mattered is execution, and in Game 3 the Spurs executed at a higher level than anyone expected — 28 assists, a 21-7 advantage in points off turnovers, and a pair of 22-year-olds who combined for 55 points in the biggest moments.

The series lead still belongs to New York at 2-1. The Knicks remain the team with the easier path to the championship. But the comfortable margins they enjoyed through 13 consecutive postseason wins are gone. Victor Wembanyama showed Monday night that he can be the best player in any game he plays in, and Stephon Castle showed he can be the decisive one.

What the Knicks Need

The most pressing issue for New York is Jalen Brunson's efficiency. He scored 32 points in Game 3, but the Spurs' defensive scheme disrupted his rhythm all night, forcing him into contested mid-range looks and keeping him off the free throw line. Brunson shooting a high volume of jumpers without drawing fouls is a formula that gives Wembanyama time to dominate the other end.

Karl-Anthony Towns, who had 21 points and 13 rebounds in Game 2, needs to reassert himself as the Knicks' interior anchor. In Game 3, Towns was held to 11 points and 8 rebounds — productive by most standards, but insufficient when Wembanyama is operating at 32-8-6 on the other end. The matchup between the two big men is the most important individual battle in the series.

The Knicks also need to stop giving the Spurs easy transition opportunities. Fourteen turnovers leading to 21 Spurs points is a recipe for losing, especially at home where the pressure to perform invites the kind of mistakes that get you beat.

What the Spurs Need

San Antonio needs two things: more of what they got in Game 3, and the discipline not to waste it. Wembanyama at his Game 3 level is virtually unguardable, but sustaining that performance requires support. Castle delivered in the clutch on Monday night, but the Spurs cannot afford to be entirely reliant on two 22-year-olds closing every game.

The Spurs also need to continue winning the battle of ball movement. Their 28 assists against the Knicks' 18 in Game 3 is not an accident — it reflects a team that has been trained to pass out of pressure and find the open look rather than force individual creation. If they maintain that discipline in Game 4, they give themselves every chance to win.

A Spurs win on Wednesday levels the series at 2-2 and shifts the series back to San Antonio for Game 5 on Saturday, June 13. That scenario gives San Antonio home court in a pivotal game. It also, essentially, resets the entire Finals.

What's Next

Game 4 tips off Wednesday, June 10, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC at Madison Square Garden. The Knicks are fighting to maintain their series lead and stay on track for their first championship since 1973. The Spurs are fighting to level a series they were supposed to be losing badly at this point.

The road team has won every game so far. The home team is due. But in this series, nothing has gone according to form — and that is precisely what makes Game 4 must-watch television.
