Wembanyama and Castle Silence Madison Square Garden as Spurs Win Game 3, 115-111

By Jake Torres | June 9, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The crowd at Madison Square Garden was ready for a coronation. The New York Knicks had won 13 consecutive playoff games. They had won Games 1 and 2 on the road in San Antonio. They were two wins from ending a 53-year title drought. The Garden was ready to celebrate.

Victor Wembanyama had other plans.

The San Antonio Spurs stunned the Knicks 115-111 on Monday night in a Game 3 that belonged entirely to their 22-year-old franchise player — and to another 22-year-old who has been waiting quietly for his own defining moment. The series is 2-1 Knicks. The Spurs are alive. And the NBA Finals have become exactly the kind of series the sport needed them to be.

Wembanyama's Masterclass

The final stat line read 32 points, 8 rebounds, 6 assists, 3 blocks, and 2 steals. Those numbers would be extraordinary from any player in any Finals game. From a 22-year-old at the most hostile arena in professional basketball, against a team that had been unstoppable for three weeks, they were historic.

According to CBS Sports, Wembanyama became only the second-youngest player to post a 30-5-5 stat line in an NBA Finals game, behind Magic Johnson. He was the best player on the floor for 48 minutes, something that could not be said of him in Games 1 and 2.

More importantly, Wembanyama delivered with the game in balance. His scoring runs kept the Spurs close when the Knicks made their runs. His defensive presence — three blocks and constant deterrence at the rim — frustrated a New York offense that had torched everyone in its path through the postseason. The errant pass that defined Game 2 was replaced by sharp decision-making and composure that belied his age.

Castle's Clutch Execution

If Wembanyama was the dominant force, Stephon Castle was the decisive one. The second-year guard had 23 points on the night, but two plays defined his performance and defined the outcome.

With just under two minutes remaining and the game still in doubt, Castle drained a go-ahead 3-pointer that gave San Antonio the lead. Then, with six seconds left and the Knicks pressing, Castle stepped to the free throw line and calmly converted both shots to seal the victory.

Together, Wembanyama and Castle made NBA Finals history. Per CBS Sports, they became the first Finals teammates both aged 22 or younger to each score 20 or more points in the same game. It is the kind of record that speaks to the extraordinary youth and talent of this Spurs roster.

The Knicks Had Their Own Heroes

New York did not go quietly. Jalen Brunson matched Wembanyama's 32 points in a performance that on any other night would have been the story of the game. OG Anunoby added 28 points in perhaps his finest game of the postseason. The Knicks had scoring. They had effort. They had the crowd.

What they did not have was ball security or collective execution at Wembanyama's level. The Spurs finished with 28 assists to the Knicks' 18, showing a team executing as a unit even while their superstar was playing at an individual level the Knicks could not match. San Antonio also outscored New York 21-7 in points off turnovers — a number that tells the story of the game as cleanly as any single statistic.

The Knicks' 13-game win streak is over. Their aura of invincibility has been tested. They still lead the series, but Game 3 proved the Spurs are not here to be swept.

A Pattern Nobody Expected

This is now only the second NBA Finals where the road team has won each of the first three games. The pattern has given the series a strange, disorienting quality — the home crowd and the home court advantage have meant nothing. San Antonio went to New York and won. New York had already gone to San Antonio and won twice.

Whatever the explanation — elite road mentality on both sides, crowd noise becoming a liability when it raises the home team's nerves — the pattern will be the defining subplot of Game 4.

What's Next

Game 4 tips off Wednesday, June 10, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC at Madison Square Garden. The Knicks need a win to stay within one game of the championship. The Spurs need a win to even the series at 2-2 and head back to San Antonio for Game 5 on Saturday.

For Brunson and the Knicks, the challenge is clear: protect home court for the first time in this series and give themselves a chance to close things out in New York. For Wembanyama, Castle, and the Spurs, the challenge is equally plain: prove that Monday night was not an aberration and that this young San Antonio team can sustain its performance for another 48 minutes in the most demanding environment in the game.
