14 Days to History: AJ Dybantsa Is Going No. 1, and Washington Has Been Waiting 16 Years

By Marcus Cole | June 9, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The last time the Washington Wizards held the No. 1 pick in the NBA Draft, John Wall was the name on everyone's lips. It was 2010. LeBron James had not yet made his Decision. The league looked very different.

Sixteen years later, the Wizards are back in that position — and AJ Dybantsa is very much the name on everyone's lips. The BYU forward says he is "100% sure" Washington will select him No. 1 overall at the June 23 draft in Brooklyn. The betting market agrees, installing him at -450 odds. Mock drafts across the industry show him going first. And with 14 days left until draft night, the question is not whether it will happen — it is what it will mean.

The Prospect

Dybantsa made a compelling case in his lone college season at BYU. The 6-foot-9, 210-pound wing led all of NCAA Division I in scoring with 25.5 points per game, shooting 51.0% from the field across the entire season. He combined positional size with advanced footwork — the kind of movement skill that allows a forward to create his own shot against any coverage. His scoring range extended from the post to the perimeter.

The comparisons to generational prospects have followed him since high school. The scouting consensus heading into the draft is that Dybantsa is a rare scoring prospect, the type who comes along once every several years — a player whose combination of size, touch, and competitive instinct gives him the ceiling of a No. 1 overall pick in the fullest sense of the term.

The Washington Context

The Wizards finished 17-65 this season, giving them the highest lottery odds and the opportunity that came with them. They earned the top pick and are now preparing to build around the player they believe can transform their franchise.

For a franchise that has drafted well at times — Wall in 2010, Bradley Beal in 2012 — but has struggled to build around its stars consistently, Dybantsa represents a different kind of opportunity. He arrives into a roster that doesn't have the same veterans demanding winning now. He will have time and space to develop within a rebuild that is being planned around him, not despite him.

The first Washington No. 1 pick since John Wall is not just a draft selection. It is the opening move of a multi-year project to rebuild an NBA franchise from the ground up.

The Rest of the Lottery

Behind Dybantsa, the Jazz are expected to select Darryn Peterson at No. 2, and Cameron Boozer is projected at No. 3. The top of this draft has solidified considerably since the lottery, with Dybantsa's destination appearing settled and the rest of the top five taking clearer shape.

The June 23 date also coincides with the Bucks' stated deadline for resolving the Giannis Antetokounmpo trade situation, meaning draft night could feature the dual resolution of the two biggest NBA offseason stories. Both carry franchise-altering weight. The league's attention will be undivided.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Draft takes place on June 23-24 at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. Dybantsa and the Wizards are expected to make it official on the first night. Whether Washington is also watching a Giannis trade finalize on the same evening remains to be seen.

For now, the countdown continues. Fourteen days to draft night. Fourteen days to the moment AJ Dybantsa becomes the face of a franchise. Washington has been waiting 16 years for this. The wait is almost over.
