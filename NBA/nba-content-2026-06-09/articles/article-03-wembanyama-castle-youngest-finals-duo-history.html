Young Guns: Wembanyama and Castle Make NBA Finals History at MSG

By Damon Pierce | June 9, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

They have been teammates for two years. They were both drafted in the top five. Neither is old enough to have watched the last time the Spurs played a Finals at Madison Square Garden.

On Monday night, Victor Wembanyama and Stephon Castle did something that has never been done in NBA Finals history: two teammates both aged 22 or younger, each scoring 20 or more points in the same Finals game. Wembanyama had 32. Castle had 23. Together, they led the San Antonio Spurs past the New York Knicks 115-111 in Game 3, keeping the series alive and announcing, in the loudest possible venue, that the Spurs' future is very much their present.

The Wembanyama Milestone

Wembanyama's stat line was not just good. It was historically rare. Per CBS Sports, his 32-point, 8-rebound, 6-assist performance makes him just the second-youngest player to post a 30-5-5 in an NBA Finals game, behind Magic Johnson. The comparison is one that should be made carefully — Johnson is one of the greatest players in basketball history — but it reflects the reality of what Wembanyama did Monday night.

What separated this performance from his Game 2 showing — where he scored 29 points but gave the ball away with 13 seconds left — was his decision-making. He was aggressive without being reckless. He made difficult shots but also found open teammates. He anchored the defense on the other end, blocking three shots and altering countless others with his 7-foot-4 wingspan presence. When the MSG crowd tried to rattle him with every possession, he answered.

At 22, Wembanyama is already operating at a level of Finals poise that most players spend their entire careers searching for.

Castle Steps Into the Spotlight

Stephon Castle was always going to get his moment. The question was when and where.

The second-year Spur has spent much of this postseason as a complementary piece — the player who keeps the offense humming, defends multiple positions, and doesn't need the ball to be effective. On Monday night at MSG, he needed it. And he delivered.

Castle's go-ahead 3-pointer with just under two minutes remaining gave San Antonio a lead they would not relinquish. His two free throws with six seconds left closed the door. He finished with 23 points in a performance that required him to be the clutch executor while his more famous teammate absorbed every defensive scheme the Knicks threw at him.

Together, Wembanyama and Castle did something that none of the legends who wore Spurs uniforms before them had done. Tim Duncan never had a Finals partner under 23 also scoring 20 points. David Robinson certainly didn't. This was a new generation writing its own record — right in the middle of the most pressure-packed game of their careers.

What Comes Next for This Duo

The most important context around Monday night's milestone is the context of time. Wembanyama and Castle are 22 years old. If the Spurs win this series, they will have won a championship with two of the youngest dual stars in Finals history. If they don't, they will have two players in their mid-twenties who have already experienced Finals pressure in hostile environments and know exactly what it takes to win.

For Spurs fans, the feeling walking away from Game 3 should be twofold: relief that the series is alive, and something deeper — the recognition that this young team has arrived. The record they set Monday night is one they can build on for the next decade.

What's Next

Game 4 is Wednesday, June 10, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC at Madison Square Garden. If Wembanyama and Castle repeat what they did Monday, the series will be even at 2-2 heading back to San Antonio. If Brunson and the Knicks reassert themselves on home court, New York moves to within one win of the championship.

Either way, the NBA Finals now belong to the young. The Wembanyama-and-Castle era has officially announced itself on the biggest stage in basketball.
