The Giannis Trade Market Is Quieter Than Expected — And He Might Just Stay in Milwaukee

By Jake Torres | June 9, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

A week ago, the story was simple: Giannis Antetokounmpo was going to be traded, the Miami Heat were closing in, and the Milwaukee Bucks were heading into a rebuild. The NBA offseason felt predictable in that way only the biggest stories ever do.

Then the market moved — or rather, it didn't. According to multiple league insiders, the trade market for Giannis has come back "much more tepid than expected." The teams that were supposed to jump into the bidding haven't materialized. The frenzy never arrived. And the story that seemed straightforward now has a genuinely surprising ending in play: Giannis Antetokounmpo staying in Milwaukee.

The Frontrunner Has Stalled

The Miami Heat remain the frontrunner. That hasn't changed. Multiple sources around the league believe the Heat-Giannis connection is louder than any other, and their willingness to put together a player-and-pick-heavy package — reportedly centered around Tyler Herro and multiple first-round picks — makes them the most credible option.

But it is not a done deal. According to reports, Giannis himself has questions about what the Heat roster would look like after a trade, given how many assets would need to leave Miami to acquire him. He doesn't want to be traded into a situation where he's the only star left standing on a team that can't compete for a championship.

That hesitation changes the calculus significantly. Giannis isn't simply waiting to be moved — he has genuine input into where he goes and what that destination looks like. If Miami can't convince him the roster will be competitive, the "frontrunner" label may not mean as much as it sounds.

Celtics Intrigue, But Skepticism Too

The Boston Celtics have been linked to Giannis in recent weeks, with reports suggesting he is "very intrigued" by the idea of playing in Boston. The Celtics' championship culture, their organizational stability, and the prospect of pairing Giannis with their existing core make the scenario appealing on paper.

However, ESPN's Brian Windhorst offered a note of skepticism, saying he has not heard that Boston is genuinely on Giannis's list or that Giannis is prepared to sign an extension there. That cooling qualifier matters. Intrigue is not the same as a trade. And the Bucks are not going to deal their franchise player to a conference rival without certainty that the player will stay.

The Extension Option Grows Louder

The quieter the trade market gets, the more the $275 million option comes into focus. Giannis is eligible to sign a four-year, $275 million extension to remain in Milwaukee — the largest contract available to him from any team, including a potential sign-and-trade destination. No trade package can match it in pure dollar terms.

The Bucks co-owner Jimmy Haslam has said publicly he wants the Giannis situation resolved before June 23, which is both the NBA Draft date and the hard deadline for trade discussions. If the market doesn't materialize in the next two weeks, the extension becomes the path of least resistance — and possibly the most rational decision Giannis can make.

What's Next

The June 23 deadline is 14 days away. Between now and then, every quiet day in the trade market is a day that brings Giannis closer to staying in Milwaukee. If a team wants to change his calculus, they need to move quickly and convincingly.

The NBA's biggest offseason story may end not with a blockbuster trade but with a superstar choosing to stay where he has been — because nowhere else made a compelling enough case to leave. Keep watching the Giannis market. The next two weeks will determine everything.
