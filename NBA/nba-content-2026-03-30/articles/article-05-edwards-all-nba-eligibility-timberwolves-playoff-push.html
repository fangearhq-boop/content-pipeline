Anthony Edwards Upgraded to Questionable — All-NBA Eligibility, Wolves Seeding Both at Stake

By Hoop Heroes Staff | March 30, 2026

The stakes could not be higher for Anthony Edwards — in a game against the Dallas Mavericks that most fans outside Minnesota might overlook.

The Timberwolves star has been upgraded to questionable for Monday's contest after missing six straight games with patellofemoral pain syndrome in his right knee. Edwards was cleared for on-court activities Friday, and according to Jon Krawczynski of The Athletic, "all signs point to" the 24-year-old playing Monday night. His return would address two separate urgencies: his team's seeding battle and his own individual award candidacy.

The Eligibility Clock

Edwards has appeared in 58 games this season. Under the NBA's 65-game eligibility rule, players must appear in at least 65 regular season games to qualify for individual awards — All-NBA, MVP, Defensive Player of the Year. With eight games remaining in the regular season, Edwards must play in every single one to reach the threshold.

The implications are significant. Edwards is averaging a career-high 29.5 points per game — a mark that clearly places him in All-NBA territory. A full All-NBA selection carries real financial stakes as well: qualifying for the All-NBA team can trigger roster status and contract extension eligibility under the collective bargaining agreement's supermax provisions. Missing the 65-game cutoff would cost him that designation regardless of his production.

The NBPA has been vocal about reforming the 65-game rule, particularly after several high-profile cases this season — including Detroit's Cade Cunningham, who also faces eligibility questions following a collapsed lung injury. But rule reform is a future concern; Edwards' situation requires a solution now.

Minnesota's Seeding Battle

Beyond the individual stakes, Minnesota needs Edwards to compete for seeding. The Timberwolves (44-28) are tied with the Houston Rockets for the No. 5 seed in the Western Conference with eight games remaining, both sitting 1.5 games behind No. 4 Denver.

The seeding difference between No. 4 and No. 5 carries significant playoff consequences: the No. 4 seed gets home-court advantage in a first-round series. In a sport where home-court has proven impactful in close series, Minnesota has every reason to fight for the highest seed possible.

Teammate Jaden McDaniels has been ruled out for Monday's game with patella tendinopathy in his left knee, leaving Minnesota's perimeter even more shorthanded entering what has become a must-win situation with or without Edwards.

What's Next

If Edwards plays Monday against Dallas, it will be his first game since March 23. His conditioning and sharpness after six days off will be closely monitored — both by the team's medical staff and by anyone watching the Timberwolves' playoff prospects.

Minnesota's final eight games include a mix of winnable matchups and potential traps. With a healthy Edwards running the offense and Rudy Gobert anchoring the defense, the Wolves have the profile of a team that can compete deep into the postseason. Getting ANT back in rhythm with two weeks left before the Playoffs begin on April 18 is the most important thing this franchise can do right now.
