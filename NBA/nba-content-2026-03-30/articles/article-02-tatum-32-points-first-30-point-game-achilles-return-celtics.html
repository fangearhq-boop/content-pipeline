Tatum Drops 32 in First 30-Point Game Since Achilles Return — Celtics Clinch Playoff Spot

By Hoop Heroes Staff | March 30, 2026

This is what a full comeback looks like.

Jayson Tatum scored 32 points on Sunday night to lead the Boston Celtics to a 114-99 victory over the Charlotte Hornets, clinching Boston's 2026 playoff spot in the process. It was the first 30-point performance of Tatum's return from a torn Achilles — a comeback from an injury that ended his 2025-26 season after just one game and kept him sidelined for the first 62 contests this year.

The performance drew immediate comparisons to the Tatum of old: punishing drives, stepback 3-pointers, midrange jumpers that dropped from impossible angles. He made 12-of-23 shots, hit five 3-pointers, and added eight assists and five rebounds in his most complete showing since returning.

"He was special tonight," Celtics coach Joe Mazzulla said. "That's the Jayson Tatum we know. The motor, the confidence — he had it all."

A Historic Night at the Garden

Beyond the 32-point outburst, Sunday delivered a milestone. Tatum became the youngest Celtic in franchise history to reach 14,000 career points. At 27 years old, he reached that mark having missed the better part of a full season — a testament to the elite level he played at before the Achilles tear and the player he is already again on his return.

Payton Pritchard delivered one of his better performances of the season alongside Tatum, finishing with 28 points on 10-of-18 shooting with a perfect 6-of-6 from the foul line. With both Jaylen Brown (Achilles tendinitis) and Derrick White (knee) sidelined Sunday, Pritchard's scoring punch was essential to Boston's comfortable margin.

Neemias Queta rounded out Boston's contributions with 17 points and eight rebounds off the bench.

Charlotte Goes Cold

The Hornets entered Sunday having led the NBA all season in made 3-pointers, with a 38.2% clip from deep. Charlotte's perimeter attack — fueled by LaMelo Ball, Brandon Miller, and Kon Knueppel — has been one of the most potent in the league. On Sunday night, Boston neutralized it entirely.

Charlotte went just 12-of-43 from 3-point range, with Ball, Miller, and Knueppel combining to go 7-of-27 from deep. Ball finished with 19 points, Miles Bridges added 14, but no one else could generate consistent offense. The Celtics' defense — long one of the league's best before injuries decimated the roster — reasserted itself in a way that will concern Play-In-bound opponents.

The Hornets' loss sealed the end of their seven-game homestand on a sour note, dropping them to two straight losses after winning the first five.

What's Next

The Celtics travel to Atlanta on Monday to face the Hawks with their playoff spot secured. Coach Mazzulla said he was uncertain whether Brown or White would return for that game, though both are progressing in their respective recoveries.

Boston is now 9-2 in games featuring Tatum, and the prospect of eventually adding Brown back into a lineup that already looks this capable has opposing coaches taking notice. The Celtics hold the No. 2 seed in the Eastern Conference and are separated from the No. 3 Knicks by a comfortable margin.

With seven games remaining in the regular season, Boston's focus shifts to seeding and health. A fully healthy Celtics team entering the playoffs — with Tatum playing at this level — may be the most dangerous team in the Eastern Conference.
