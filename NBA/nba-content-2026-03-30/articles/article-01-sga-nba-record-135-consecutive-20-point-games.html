SGA Extends NBA Record to 135 Consecutive 20-Point Games as Thunder Defeat Knicks 111-100

By Hoop Heroes Staff | March 30, 2026

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander was quiet for three quarters. That only made the fourth quarter more impressive.

SGA finished with 30 points — including 10 in the final frame — as the Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the New York Knicks 111-100 on Sunday night at Paycom Center. More importantly, he extended his NBA record for consecutive games with at least 20 points to 135, a streak that now stands alone in league history.

For three quarters, the Thunder's superstar was just 5-of-15 from the field. New York mounted a 13-3 run midway through the fourth quarter to cut a comfortable OKC lead all the way to one point — 92-91. Then SGA took over. He hit all three of his fourth-quarter field goal attempts and scored nine straight Thunder points to rebuild the lead and put the game away.

"That's what great players do," Thunder coach Mark Daigneault said after the game. "The shots weren't falling early, but he never stopped competing. He found a way."

Free-Throw Dominance

SGA's efficiency on Sunday was defined at the foul line: he converted 13-of-16 free throws as Oklahoma City attempted 38 free throws compared to just 17 for the Knicks. That disparity speaks to SGA's ability to draw contact and convert under pressure — a skill that keeps his scoring line reliable even on off nights from the field.

Jalen Williams provided crucial support with 22 points on 7-of-11 shooting, including a perfect 7-of-7 performance from the free-throw line. Williams, who missed significant time earlier this season with a wrist and hamstring injury, has been increasingly reliable as a secondary option for OKC down the stretch.

Karl-Anthony Towns led New York with 15 points and 18 rebounds, while Josh Hart and Mikal Bridges each added 15 points. The Knicks played without a fully healthy roster but had enough chances to steal the win before SGA's fourth-quarter takeover sealed the result.

West Race Heats Up

The win moved Oklahoma City to 59-16 on the season — the best record in the Western Conference and tied for best in the NBA with the Detroit Pistons. The Thunder now lead the San Antonio Spurs by 2.5 games for the No. 1 seed with seven games remaining.

OKC has won 14 of their last 15 games. At this rate, locking up the No. 1 seed — and the home-court advantage that comes with it throughout the playoffs — appears inevitable.

SGA's record-breaking streak has been a constant backdrop to OKC's dominant run. He surpassed Wilt Chamberlain's previous standard for consecutive 20-point games earlier this season and has shown no signs of slowing down. His season scoring average remains among the league leaders, and he continues to do it while managing his rest and protecting his health.

What's Next

The Thunder host the Detroit Pistons on Monday — a preview of a potential Eastern Conference Finals matchup between the two best-record teams in the NBA. Oklahoma City's remaining schedule is manageable, meaning an OKC clinch of the No. 1 overall seed could come as early as this week.

For the Knicks (47-27), the loss means they failed to clinch a playoff spot Sunday night. They will visit the Houston Rockets on Tuesday. New York still holds the No. 3 seed in the East, but the margin between them and the fourth-seeded Cleveland Cavaliers has tightened. With Jayson Tatum and the Celtics looking dangerous ahead of the postseason, every Eastern Conference seed matters.

And SGA? His record-breaking season continues — one game at a time, one streak at a time, one championship run building toward what figures to be a historic spring in Oklahoma City.
