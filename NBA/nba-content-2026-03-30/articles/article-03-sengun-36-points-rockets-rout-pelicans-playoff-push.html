Sengun's 36-Point Masterclass Rockets Past Pelicans — Houston Ties for No. 5 West

By Hoop Heroes Staff | March 30, 2026

Numbers like these deserve to be read twice.

Alperen Sengun posted 36 points, 13 rebounds, 7 assists, 3 steals, and 3 blocks in just 32 minutes on Sunday as the Houston Rockets dominated the New Orleans Pelicans 134-102. The 23-year-old Turkish center committed zero turnovers while doing all of it, going 12-of-23 from the floor, 5-of-7 from 3-point range, and 7-of-8 from the foul line.

It was the kind of performance that puts a player on the national radar — and it was not a one-time occurrence. Sengun has scored 30 or more points in three of Houston's past four games since returning from a back injury on March 18. In those seven games, he is averaging 24.9 points, 9.3 rebounds, 7.1 assists, and 1.6 blocks while shooting 62.1% from the field.

"He's special," said Kevin Durant, who added 20 points Sunday alongside Jabari Smith Jr.'s matching 20-point effort. "You watch him work and you understand why people talk about him the way they do."

A Complete Weapon

What makes Sengun uniquely dangerous is the variety of ways he can hurt an opponent. On Sunday, he operated from the post, stepped out to hit 3-pointers, found cutters and roll men on the pick-and-roll, and defended the rim. His 5-for-7 performance from deep — matching his career high for makes in a single game — showed the Pelicans couldn't simply sag off him and protect the paint.

Houston out-rebounded New Orleans 66-51, with Sengun easily leading both teams on the glass. Tari Eason contributed 15 points off the bench as the Rockets built a double-digit lead by halftime and never looked back.

Durant's steady 20-point contribution was a reminder that this team has a genuine two-headed offensive threat. When Sengun and KD are both operating at this level, Houston looks like a different franchise than the one that entered the season with modest expectations.

Playoff Stakes

The win moved Houston to 45-29, tied with the Minnesota Timberwolves for the No. 5 seed in the Western Conference with eight games remaining. Denver sits 1.5 games ahead of them in the No. 4 spot — a gap the Rockets can close if they keep winning and the Nuggets stumble.

The seeding race is not abstract: No. 4 vs. No. 5 will determine home-court advantage in a potential first-round series between Houston and Minnesota — one of the most compelling potential matchups in the West bracket given how similar their trajectories have been this season.

For a Rockets team that has gotten excellent production from Durant while Sengun emerged as a co-lead, the 45-win mark with a month of the season still to play represents genuine progress. Houston's previous best this century was 55 wins in 2017-18, and while they won't approach that, the Rockets are clearly built to compete deep into May.

What's Next

The Rockets welcome the New York Knicks on Tuesday for a nationally televised game with clear playoff implications — New York needs the win to lock down seeding in the East, while Houston needs every win to hold or improve its West spot. With Sengun playing at this level, Houston will be a tough out for anyone.

For the Pelicans, the loss leaves them 30-45 and on the outside looking in at the Play-In picture as the regular season nears its conclusion.
