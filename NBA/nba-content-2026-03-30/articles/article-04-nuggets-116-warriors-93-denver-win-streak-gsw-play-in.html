Nuggets Run Past Warriors 116-93 — Denver's Sixth Straight Win, Golden State Locked Into Play-In

By Hoop Heroes Staff | March 30, 2026

For two quarters, it was a game. Then Denver turned it on.

The Denver Nuggets outscored the Golden State Warriors 70-40 in the second half to cruise to a 116-93 victory on Sunday Night Basketball, extending Denver's winning streak to six games. Nikola Jokic anchored the effort with 25 points, 15 rebounds, and 8 assists — falling just short of a 10-assist mark that would have made it yet another triple-double.

Jamal Murray added 20 points in a balanced Nuggets effort that featured strong contributions throughout the roster. Even without Aaron Gordon, Denver had too much firepower and too much depth for a depleted Golden State roster to handle over 48 minutes.

A Tale of Two Halves

The first half was competitive. Golden State kept pace and trailed by a manageable margin at the break. But the third quarter was where the game was decided — Denver dominated it and continued applying pressure in the fourth. The Warriors, who have been fighting for their playoff lives without Stephen Curry all season, simply ran out of answers.

Golden State's offensive limitations without their franchise player were on full display. The Warriors have talented pieces, but without Curry's gravity and shooting threat creating space for everyone else, opposing defenses have been able to scheme more aggressively against them. The result has been the Warriors trending toward the Play-In for the past month.

Warriors' Play-In Fate Confirmed

With Sunday's loss and Houston's simultaneous 134-102 rout of New Orleans, Golden State's position is mathematically confirmed: the Warriors will participate in the Play-In Tournament as the No. 10 seed in the Western Conference. They'll face the Portland Trail Blazers in the first round of the Play-In, with only a win advancing them to a second chance against the No. 9 Suns-Clippers game loser.

The bigger question for Golden State is whether Stephen Curry will suit up at all. The Warriors' star has been out since late January with runner's knee and reports this week indicated swelling has continued to delay his return. Without Curry, even the Play-In looks like a steep hill to climb.

What's Next

Denver hosts the Sacramento Kings on Tuesday. The Nuggets (47-28) hold the No. 4 seed in the Western Conference, 1.5 games ahead of the fifth-place Rockets and Timberwolves. A continued win streak could allow Denver to lock down that seed before the final week of the regular season.

Jokic has been in brilliant form — his triple-double streak ended Sunday at four, but the near-miss (25/15/8) still represents elite play from one of the best players in the league. With the playoffs beginning April 18, Denver looks ready to make a deep postseason run. Their second-half performance against Golden State — an NBA Finals finalist as recently as three seasons ago — was a statement of intent.
