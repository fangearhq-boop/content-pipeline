### STORY 2: Tatum First 30-Point Game Since Achilles Return

**Long-Form Post** [9:30 AM ET]
Jayson Tatum looked like himself again — and the Boston Celtics look like a real threat.

Tatum dropped 32 points on Sunday in Boston's 114-99 victory over the Charlotte Hornets, marking his first 30-point performance since returning from a torn Achilles that cost him the first 62 games of the season. He shot 12-of-23 from the field, knocked down five 3-pointers, and added eight assists and five rebounds in his most complete game since coming back.

Along the way, Tatum became the youngest Celtic in franchise history to reach 14,000 career points — a milestone that speaks to both his talent and his longevity, even at just 27 years old.

This game was played without Jaylen Brown (Achilles tendinitis) and Derrick White (knee), meaning Tatum carried the offensive load largely alone. Payton Pritchard stepped up with 28 points and Neemias Queta added 17 points and eight rebounds. The Celtics held Charlotte — who entered leading the NBA in made 3-pointers — to just 12-of-43 from beyond the arc.

Boston improved to 9-2 in games with Tatum and clinched their 2026 playoff spot. With Tatum playing like this, the Celtics' defense, and the prospect of eventually getting Brown and White back, this team could be the East's biggest sleeper for a Finals run.

### STORY 3: Sengun 36/13/7 — Rockets Rout Pelicans

**Long-Form Post** [11:00 AM ET]
The Alperen Sengun show continues — and the Houston Rockets are making noise in the Western Conference playoff race.

Sengun was absolutely unstoppable on Sunday, finishing with 36 points, 13 rebounds, 7 assists, 3 steals, and 3 blocks in just 32 minutes during Houston's 134-102 blowout win over the New Orleans Pelicans. He shot 12-of-23 from the floor, went 5-of-7 from 3-point range, and committed zero turnovers. At 23 years old, the Turkish center is playing like an MVP candidate.

This wasn't a one-off. Since returning from a back injury on March 18, Sengun has averaged 24.9 points, 9.3 rebounds, 7.1 assists, and 1.6 blocks per game while shooting 62.1% from the field over seven games. He has scored 30 or more points in three of Houston's past four contests.

Kevin Durant and Jabari Smith Jr. each added 20 points, and Tari Eason contributed 15 off the bench.

With the win, the Rockets improved to 45-29 — tied with Minnesota for the No. 5 seed in the Western Conference with eight games remaining. They're 1.5 games behind Denver for No. 4. Houston entered the season with questions about their identity. Sengun has answered them all.

### STORY 5: Edwards Upgraded to Questionable

**Long-Form Post** [11:30 AM ET]
Anthony Edwards appears ready to return — and both his team and his award candidacy need him.

The Minnesota Timberwolves star has been upgraded to questionable for Monday's game against the Dallas Mavericks after missing six straight games with patellofemoral pain syndrome in his right knee. Edwards was cleared for on-court activity Friday, and per The Athletic's Jon Krawczynski, "all signs point to" him playing Monday night.

His return can't come soon enough for Minnesota — the Timberwolves are locked in a tight race with Houston for the No. 5 seed in the West, both sitting at 45-29.

But there's also an individual stakes element here that makes this one of the most compelling storylines of the week. Edwards has appeared in just 58 games this season. Under the NBA's 65-game eligibility rule for individual awards, he must play in all eight remaining regular season games to qualify for All-NBA consideration. He's averaging a career-high 29.5 points per game — numbers that easily put him in All-NBA territory.

Teammate Jaden McDaniels has been ruled out for Monday with knee tendinopathy, making Edwards' availability even more important for the Wolves.

### STORY 7: Steph Curry Knee Update

**Long-Form Post** [4:00 PM ET]
The question isn't whether Stephen Curry will play in the NBA Playoffs — it's whether he'll play at all before the season ends.

Curry has been sidelined since late January with runner's knee (chondromalacia patella), and recent reports indicate that swelling in the knee has continued to delay his return. There is no established timeline for when he will play again. The Warriors star, who turned 38 this February, has been working to manage the injury, but the lack of progress is a concerning sign.

The timing could not be worse. Golden State locked up a Play-In berth Sunday after losing to Denver, meaning their postseason starts in mid-April at the absolute earliest — and they'll be facing teams who have been playing competitive basketball while Curry has been sidelined for more than two months.

The Warriors entered this season with hope that a healthy Curry-led squad could make a deep run. That hope has been replaced with uncertainty. Even if Curry somehow returns in time, asking him to go from months of inactivity to playoff basketball raises serious concerns about his health and effectiveness.
