### Long-Form Post Sometimes sports write the most beautiful stories. In 1999, the New York Knicks made a miraculous run to the NBA Finals as a No. 8 seed. They were gritty, tough, and represented New York with everything they had. They lost to the San Antonio Spurs in five games. On the roster that year was a reserve guard named Rick Brunson — a journeyman who worked his way into the league and played his part for the Knicks. That reserve guard had a son named Jalen. Twenty-seven years later, Jalen Brunson — who grew up in Union, New Jersey, who watched his father play for the Knicks, who signed with New York from Dallas in 2022 and immediately became the face of a franchise — has led those same New York Knicks back to the NBA Finals. Against the same San Antonio Spurs. Jalen Brunson averaged 27.8 PPG and 6.7 APG through this playoff run. He was named ECF MVP after the Knicks swept the Cavaliers. And when he was asked about being in the Finals, he said: "It's an honor to be here in this city and this organization. I wouldn't trade it for the world. We're still writing our story, but I like the journey that we're on right now." Rick Brunson lost that 1999 Finals to the Spurs. Now it's Jalen's turn to rewrite the ending.
