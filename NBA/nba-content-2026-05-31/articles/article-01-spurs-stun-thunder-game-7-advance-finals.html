Wemby and the Spurs Stun Thunder in Game 7, Advance to NBA Finals

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 31, 2026

The Oklahoma City Thunder hosted a Game 7 at Paycom Center on Saturday night, with the defending NBA champions and the crowd firmly behind them. The San Antonio Spurs didn't care. Behind seven players in double figures and a composed 22-point performance from Victor Wembanyama, San Antonio stunned the Thunder 111-103 to advance to the NBA Finals for the first time since 2014.

OKC entered as -3.5 home favorites. The Spurs played like the favorites. And with that, the Wembanyama era is officially Finals-bound.

Seven Spurs Show Up When It Matters Most

The defining difference in Game 7 wasn't one superstar performance — it was depth. Julian Champagnie lit up the Paycom Center crowd like a house fire from three-point range, scoring 20 points on six made threes. Stephon Castle contributed 16 points, six rebounds, and six assists in a mature display of two-way play. De'Aaron Fox added 15 points and five assists. Seven San Antonio players finished in double figures.

Wembanyama himself was efficient rather than spectacular — 22 points on 7-of-15 shooting, seven rebounds, two assists, and 42 minutes of anchor defense. He didn't need to carry the team. He just needed to be him, and the supporting cast did the rest. That's a championship-caliber formula.

SGA Couldn't Do It Alone

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, the back-to-back MVP, was magnificent. Thirty-five points, nine assists, two steals, two blocks — a line that wins most playoff games. But with Jalen Williams sidelined by a hamstring injury for the second straight game, SGA lacked the secondary firepower to match the Spurs' collective effort. Cason Wallace contributed 17 points off the bench, but the depth gap proved fatal.

"They were just the better team tonight, from start to finish," SGA said after the game. "And every time we tried to cut into it and take control of the game, they had an answer." He was asked about the Thunder's offseason plans and gave an answer that revealed his character: "I will give zero input. I will let Sam Presti, the greatest GM ever, do his job."

A New Spurs Era Announces Itself

The last time San Antonio appeared in the NBA Finals was 2014, the season Tim Duncan, Tony Parker, and Manu Ginobili won their final championship together. That team was a dynasty built over 15 years. This team is a dynasty just beginning.

Wembanyama, named WCF MVP after the series, averaged 27.3 points, 10.9 rebounds, 2.7 blocks, and 1.4 steals per game throughout the Western Conference Finals. He is 22 years old and is already breaking Tim Duncan's Spurs postseason records. That context is staggering.

Coach Gregg Popovich's successor has coached this group to a Finals berth through team basketball, defensive discipline, and an emerging superstar who makes everyone around him better. The Spurs are not just a good story — they are a legitimate championship threat.

What's Next

The San Antonio Spurs will host the New York Knicks in Game 1 of the 2026 NBA Finals on Wednesday, June 3 at 8:30 PM ET on ABC. San Antonio holds home court advantage as the higher seed (62-20 regular season). The Knicks arrive with an 11-game playoff winning streak and fresh legs after sweeping the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Finals.

The matchup is a rematch of the 1999 NBA Finals — a meeting that carries its own layers of history. The Spurs won that series in five games. Whether San Antonio can repeat the result against a Knicks team led by a player with deep personal roots in that 1999 series will be the story of June. Game 1 cannot come soon enough.
