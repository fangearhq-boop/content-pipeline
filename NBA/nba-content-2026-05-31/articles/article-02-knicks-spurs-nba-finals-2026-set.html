Knicks vs. Spurs: The 2026 NBA Finals Is a 27-Year Rematch

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 31, 2026

In 1999, the San Antonio Spurs defeated the New York Knicks in five games to win the NBA Championship. Tim Duncan was the cornerstone of a dynasty just beginning. The Knicks were a scrappy No. 8 seed who shocked the basketball world just by reaching the Finals.

Twenty-seven years later, these two franchises are meeting again. The 2026 NBA Finals tips off on Wednesday, June 3 at 8:30 PM ET on ABC — at the AT&T Center in San Antonio, where the Spurs enter as -196 favorites.

It's the series basketball didn't know it needed. And it's going to be spectacular.

The Spurs: Wemby's Moment

Victor Wembanyama earned Western Conference Finals MVP honors after leading San Antonio past the defending champion Oklahoma City Thunder in seven games. His series averages — 27.3 points, 10.9 rebounds, 2.7 blocks, and 1.4 steals — were historic for a player his age. He is 22 years old and plays like a veteran who has seen everything.

The Spurs enter the Finals as the No. 2 seed from the West, carrying a 62-20 regular season record and home court advantage. They play their best basketball at the AT&T Center, where the crowd brings an energy forged by five championship banners. San Antonio isn't just a team — it's a basketball culture.

The Knicks: Brunson's Charge

The New York Knicks arrive in the Finals on one of the most dominant playoff streaks in recent memory. They have won 11 consecutive playoff games, sweeping the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Finals behind Jalen Brunson's ECF MVP performance of 27.8 points and 6.7 assists per game.

Brunson was named ECF MVP and delivered a statement when the Knicks punched their ticket: "It's an honor to be here in this city and this organization. I wouldn't trade it for the world. We're still writing our story, but I like the journey that we're on right now."

The Knicks are the No. 3 seed from the East at 53-29. They will be the road team for Games 1 and 2 in San Antonio before MSG hosts Games 3 and 4. No team in the Finals' modern history has a home court advantage that quite compares to Madison Square Garden at full volume — and New York will be deafening.

The Schedule

The Finals follows a 2-2-1-1-1 format:

  • Game 1: June 3 — at San Antonio, 8:30 PM ET, ABC
  • Game 2: June 5 — at San Antonio, 8:30 PM ET, ABC
  • Game 3: June 8 — at New York, 8:30 PM ET, ABC
  • Game 4: June 10 — at New York, 8:30 PM ET, ABC

A potential Game 7 would be scheduled for June 19 if needed.

What's Next

The Knicks have 13 days of rest since their last game. They will be fresh, prepared, and ready to make history in a city that hasn't celebrated an NBA championship since 1973. The Spurs have slightly less rest but the benefit of home court and a crowd that knows how to will its team through a Finals.

San Antonio is favored. But this Knicks team has done nothing but confound expectations all postseason. The 2026 NBA Finals promises to be one for the ages — a generational superstar against a city's favorite son, two storied franchises, and 27 years of history waiting to be settled. Game 1 is Wednesday, June 3 on ABC. Don't miss a second.
