SGA Dropped 35 in Game 7 and It Still Wasn't Enough

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 31, 2026

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander played one of the great individual performances of this postseason on Saturday night. He scored 35 points, handed out nine assists, pulled down four rebounds, blocked two shots, and recorded two steals. The Oklahoma City Thunder lost anyway, 111-103, and were eliminated from the Western Conference Finals in Game 7.

That is the brutal math of team sports: sometimes your best isn't enough. And on this particular night, in this particular arena, without Jalen Williams alongside him, SGA's extraordinary effort fell short against a San Antonio Spurs team that simply had more answers.

What Went Wrong for OKC

The Thunder's structural problem in Game 7 was depth. With Williams sidelined by his hamstring injury — he had averaged 14.6 points per game in the series before his injury — OKC had no reliable second option to take the pressure off SGA. Cason Wallace contributed 17 points from the bench, but the Spurs had seven players reach double figures. That imbalance was the decisive factor.

Every time Oklahoma City tried to cut into the Spurs' lead, San Antonio had a counter. Julian Champagnie hit a three. Stephon Castle made a play. Victor Wembanyama was there in the paint. The Thunder's one-man approach simply couldn't match San Antonio's collective depth, and by the fourth quarter, the game was settled.

SGA's Grace in Defeat

After the loss, Gilgeous-Alexander faced the cameras with the composure that has defined his tenure in Oklahoma City. "They were just the better team tonight, from start to finish," he said, without deflection or excuse. "And every time we tried to cut into it and take control of the game, they had an answer."

Asked about whether he would offer input on the Thunder's offseason decisions — roster moves, trades, the questions that will dominate Oklahoma City's summer — SGA didn't hesitate: "I will give zero input. I will let Sam Presti, the greatest GM ever, do his job."

That level of trust in an organization, delivered in the rawest moment of a postseason exit, speaks to the partnership SGA and the Thunder have built. This is not a franchise in crisis. This is a franchise that has won back-to-back championship berths, secured a title last year, and has their generational star fully committed to seeing it through.

What's Next

The Oklahoma City Thunder will enter the offseason with one of the most enviable rosters in the NBA. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, Jalen Williams, and a roster of young contributors remain in place. The Thunder have won 64-18 over the past two regular seasons combined, reached back-to-back Western Conference Finals, and have a championship banner from 2025.

The Spurs were simply a step ahead this time. But the Thunder will be back — and when Williams is healthy alongside a fully focused SGA, they will once again be legitimate contenders. Saturday night's elimination does not diminish what Oklahoma City has built. It only adds fuel to the fire for what comes next.
