His Father Lost the 1999 Finals to the Spurs. Now Jalen Brunson Gets His Shot.

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 31, 2026

The 2026 NBA Finals is a rematch of 1999 — and that fact carries more weight for Jalen Brunson than almost anyone in basketball.

Twenty-seven years ago, Rick Brunson was a reserve guard on the New York Knicks roster when they made their improbable run to the NBA Finals as a No. 8 seed. They were gritty, passionate, and beloved by a city that adores that combination. They lost to the San Antonio Spurs in five games. Tim Duncan was 22 years old and announced himself as the next great basketball dynasty.

Now Jalen Brunson — Rick's son, who grew up in Union, New Jersey watching his father play for the Knicks — is leading those same New York Knicks back to the Finals. Against those same San Antonio Spurs. The connection is real, verifiable, and carries the kind of narrative weight that makes sports something more than competition.

Growing Up Brunson

Jalen Brunson was born in 1996, making him just three years old when the Knicks ran their 1999 Finals campaign. He was too young to watch it with full comprehension, but the stories shaped him. His father's connection to New York — the city, the Garden, the culture — was something passed down at the dinner table, in the gym, in the hours spent working on his game alongside a father who knew exactly what it took to make it in this league.

Brunson attended Villanova, won two national championships under Jay Wright, then was drafted by the Dallas Mavericks in 2018. He developed into one of the best point guards in the NBA during his years in Dallas, earning All-Star status and elevating the Mavericks alongside Luka Doncic. In 2022, the Knicks signed him.

It felt like fate the moment it happened. A Brunson was back in New York.

The Journey to the Finals

Brunson has been the Knicks' best player and their emotional leader in these 2026 playoffs. He averaged 27.8 points and 6.7 assists per game through the postseason, earning Eastern Conference Finals MVP honors after the Knicks swept the Cleveland Cavaliers. His 25.5 points and 7.8 assists in the ECF were the driving force behind New York's 11-game playoff winning streak.

When asked about finally reaching the Finals, Brunson didn't reach for the historic connection. He talked about the present, the journey, the people around him. "It's an honor to be here in this city and this organization," he said after the Knicks punched their ticket. "I wouldn't trade it for the world. We're still writing our story, but I like the journey that we're on right now."

We're still writing our story. That sentence lands differently when you know the first chapter involved his father losing this exact series 27 years ago.

What's Next

Game 1 of the 2026 NBA Finals is Wednesday, June 3 at 8:30 PM ET on ABC, at the AT&T Center in San Antonio. The Spurs are the -196 favorites and hold home court. The Knicks arrive as 11-game winners with a city that hasn't celebrated an NBA championship since 1973.

Jalen Brunson will walk into San Antonio knowing exactly what those courts have meant to his family. Whether that history motivates him or adds pressure is something only he can answer — and he'll answer it in June, in the most important series of his career. His father played in this series and came home without a ring. The 2026 Finals gives Jalen the chance to write a different ending to the story. The city of New York will be watching every second.
