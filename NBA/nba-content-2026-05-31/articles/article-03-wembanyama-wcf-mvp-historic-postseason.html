Wembanyama Named WCF MVP: A Historic Postseason at Age 22

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 31, 2026

Victor Wembanyama has been officially named the 2026 Western Conference Finals MVP — an honor that feels both inevitable and staggering when you consider the numbers behind it.

Over seven games against the defending champion Oklahoma City Thunder, Wembanyama averaged 27.3 points, 10.9 rebounds, 3.1 assists, 2.7 blocks, and 1.4 steals per game while playing 37.7 minutes per night. He connected on 2.3 three-pointers per game while guarding the best players on the opposing team at the other end.

He is 22 years old. He is already rewriting Spurs postseason history books. And he just led San Antonio to the NBA Finals.

Breaking Into the Record Books

Victor Wembanyama's 2026 postseason has already produced performances that place him alongside Tim Duncan in the Spurs' all-time playoff lore. According to multiple reports, Wemby has surpassed several of Duncan's Spurs postseason statistical benchmarks — a remarkable feat given that Duncan spent 19 seasons piling up playoff numbers and five championships.

Wembanyama's combination of scoring, rebounding, and shot-blocking at his size and age has no historical precedent. He stands 7 feet 4 inches, but moves with the fluidity of a wing player and handles defensive assignments that would challenge guards. When the Spurs needed stops late in close games, Wembanyama was the answer. When the Spurs needed buckets in transition, he was the answer. He is the answer to almost any question a defense can pose.

How He Operates

What separates Wembanyama from other dominant big men isn't just the athleticism — it's the basketball intelligence. In the WCF, he consistently found teammates when double-teamed, averaging 3.1 assists per game. He made the right play rather than the flashy play, and that discipline created opportunities for Stephon Castle, De'Aaron Fox, and Julian Champagnie to flourish alongside him.

In Game 7, the defining moment came not from one of his highlight plays but from his poise. With OKC making a fourth-quarter push, Wembanyama stayed composed, made his shots, and let his teammates do the rest. Champagnie hit six threes. Castle ran the offense. Wemby anchored the defense. That's the blueprint.

What's Next

Victor Wembanyama now faces the New York Knicks in the 2026 NBA Finals, beginning with Game 1 on Wednesday, June 3 at 8:30 PM ET on ABC at the AT&T Center in San Antonio. He will be matched up against Jalen Brunson — one of the most technically complete point guards in the game — in what promises to be the most fascinating individual matchup of the Finals.

Brunson averaged 26 points, 4 rebounds, and 8 assists in three regular season and NBA Cup meetings against San Antonio this season. He knows what Wemby does, and he is willing to go at him. The chess match between two of basketball's most intelligent players will define the championship series. If the WCF is any indication of what Wembanyama is capable of, the Knicks have a generational challenge ahead of them in June.
