Knicks Open as NBA Finals Underdogs: What +215 Odds Mean for New York's Title Chances

By Damon Pierce | May 28, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The New York Knicks are heading to the NBA Finals for the first time since 1999. They swept the Cleveland Cavaliers. They won 11 consecutive games. Jalen Brunson averaged 27.8 points and 6.7 assists in 13 playoff games. OG Anunoby's hamstring is healing with more than a week of rest before Game 1 on June 3.

And yet: DraftKings opened the Knicks as underdogs against both potential Western Conference opponents. Against the Oklahoma City Thunder, New York opened at +215 with OKC at -265. Against the San Antonio Spurs, the line sits at Knicks +185, Spurs -225.

That's the market speaking. Here's why the number is what it is — and why the Knicks may have something to say about it.

Why the Knicks Are Underdogs

The odds reflect some genuine structural disadvantages. Oklahoma City posted a 64-18 regular-season record — the best in the NBA — versus the Knicks' 53-29 mark. The Thunder are the defending champions, led by a reigning Finals MVP candidate in Shai Gilgeous-Alexander. OKC went 2-0 against New York in regular-season matchups this year, winning both games with relative comfort. The Thunder also hold home-court advantage and have been to the Finals before. Their experience gives them an edge the Knicks don't have.

San Antonio represents a different kind of challenge. Victor Wembanyama, when at his best, is the most uniquely gifted player any team in the Finals would face — a 7-foot-4 center who can guard all five positions, shoot over any defense, and affect the game in ways that do not show up in a single stat line. The Spurs are 5-2 at home in the 2026 playoffs and built around one of the most significant talents the sport has seen in a generation.

The Knicks' Case

The Knicks bring their own advantages — and several of them are substantial.

Start with rest. New York has not played since sweeping the Cavaliers on May 26, meaning they will have 8-plus days of rest before Finals Game 1. Their opponents — whichever team survives the WCF — will have been playing through May into June, with less recovery time and more wear on their bodies.

OG Anunoby was averaging 21.4 points and 7.5 rebounds per game before suffering a right hamstring strain. He played through it during the ECF and was less than 100%, but the extended rest gives him a real chance to be at or near full capacity for Game 1. An OG Anunoby operating at his peak is one of the best two-way wings in the NBA — a player who can guard SGA or Wembanyama's supporting cast and hit 50-plus percent from three in critical moments.

Brunson has been the best player in the Eastern Conference playoffs. He has driven to the rim, created shots for teammates, made big plays in close games, and stayed healthy. His 27.8-point average in the postseason places him comfortably among the top performers on either side of the bracket.

The Knicks also play exceptional team defense. Their rotations are crisp, their communication is elite, and their size at every position gives them matchup flexibility that few teams can match.

The MSG Factor

New York will host Games 3 and 4 of the NBA Finals at Madison Square Garden on June 8 and June 10. Madison Square Garden in a playoff environment is one of the most electric arenas in professional sports, and the Knicks will be playing for the city's first championship since 1973.

The Knicks have not been to the NBA Finals since the 1999 lockout-shortened season, when they fell to the San Antonio Spurs. A 27-year drought is ending. The crowd at MSG will be unprecedented in recent memory. That kind of home environment does not show up in betting lines.

What's Next

The Knicks wait while the Western Conference Finals concludes tonight. Thunder versus Spurs, Game 6, 8:30 PM ET, AT&T Center in San Antonio. New York will know their opponent by tomorrow morning. Finals Game 1 is June 3 on ABC, either in Oklahoma City or San Antonio depending on who advances.

The +215 odds represent the market's honest read on New York's chances. They also represent the Knicks' latest opportunity to prove everyone wrong — which, if their playoff run is any indication, is exactly the kind of situation this team embraces.
