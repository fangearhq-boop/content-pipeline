OKC Thunder One Win from Back-to-Back NBA Finals: A Dynasty Taking Shape

By Marcus Cole | May 28, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Oklahoma City already has a championship ring. They won the 2025 NBA title, ending years of patient rebuilding and delivering the city of Oklahoma City its first title. Now, less than a year later, the Thunder are one win away from returning to the NBA Finals for the second consecutive season.

That kind of sustained excellence is rare. Consecutive Finals appearances are the benchmark of a true dynasty — a sign that a team isn't just peaking at the right moment but operating at an elite level over multiple seasons. Tonight at AT&T Center in San Antonio, Oklahoma City can take another step toward that legacy.

SGA's Playoff Dominance

The engine of this Thunder run is Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, who has been the most consistent performer on the most dominant team in the NBA. In Game 5, he delivered 32 points and 9 assists — a vintage SGA performance on the road in a pivotal moment. Over the course of the 2026 playoffs, he has averaged well over 30 points per game while maintaining the defensive intensity and playmaking vision that make him the rare player who can take over a game from either end of the floor.

SGA entered the league as a first-round pick and developed steadily under Sam Presti's direction, eventually becoming not just the best player on a Finals team but a genuine MVP-level talent in back-to-back postseasons. His growth from promising young player to championship engine is one of the best development stories in recent NBA history.

The Depth That Makes OKC Different

What separates this Thunder team from other SGA-led squads in the early part of his career is the depth Sam Presti has assembled around him. Alex Caruso has been a revelation this postseason — 47.7% from three in the playoffs compared to 29.3% in the regular season, averaging 17.0 points per game in the WCF series. That kind of output from a bench piece makes Oklahoma City nearly impossible to game-plan against.

Jared McCain made his first career playoff start in Game 5 and delivered 20 points, with 18 of them coming in the second half when San Antonio was searching for a foothold. Chet Holmgren continues to be Oklahoma City's defensive anchor, capable of guarding centers and perimeter players alike. Isaiah Hartenstein has been a physical force against Wembanyama and the Spurs' frontline.

This is a team where the seventh and eighth rotation players can post 20-point games. That combination of superstar leadership and genuine roster depth is what makes back-to-back runs possible in today's NBA.

The Road to Two Consecutive Finals

Oklahoma City's path this postseason has not been easy. They dispatched their early opponents efficiently, but the San Antonio Spurs pushed them in a series that has gone five games and could go six or seven. Wembanyama is a legitimate challenge — the kind of talent that complicates every defensive scheme and makes every game unpredictable.

The fact that OKC leads 3-2 despite Wembanyama's best efforts — 41 points in Game 1, 33 in Game 4, an NBA-wide defensive blueprint being written against him — speaks to how good this Thunder team actually is. They have multiple answers for every problem the Spurs throw at them.

Tonight, in an elimination game on the road, they have the opportunity to close it out and begin preparing for the Knicks. If they succeed, the conversation about OKC as a dynasty will grow considerably louder.

What's Next

A Thunder win tonight sends them to the NBA Finals against the New York Knicks starting June 3 on ABC. Oklahoma City would hold home-court advantage based on their 64-18 regular-season record. They would enter the Finals as significant favorites — roughly -265 at DraftKings — against a Knicks team making their first Finals appearance since 1999.

The AT&T Center crowd will be hostile. Wembanyama will be motivated after his Game 5 struggles and the NBA warning that followed. De'Aaron Fox's availability is uncertain. None of those factors have stopped this Thunder team all season long.

Oklahoma City plays for history tonight. The ring from 2025 is in the case. The 2026 trophy is one win away.
