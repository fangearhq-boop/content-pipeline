De'Aaron Fox Questionable for WCF Game 6: Spurs' Biggest Wildcard

By Jake Torres | May 28, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The question that has defined the Western Conference Finals since before the opening tip is back again on Thursday: will De'Aaron Fox play? And if he plays, how much will he have?

Fox is listed as questionable for WCF Game 6 tonight at AT&T Center (8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock) with the right high ankle sprain that has shadowed him all series. The San Antonio Spurs guard missed Games 1 and 2 entirely, returned for Games 3 through 5, and remains one of the most important injury storylines in the 2026 playoffs.

The Series-Long Subplot

Fox's absence in Games 1 and 2 shaped the series early. Without their second-best offensive weapon, the Spurs leaned entirely on Victor Wembanyama — and it worked in Game 1, when Wembanyama posted 41 points and 24 rebounds in a double-overtime victory. In Game 2, Oklahoma City adjusted, and the Spurs lost without the Fox-led secondary attack to keep the Thunder honest.

Fox returned in Game 3 and the Spurs responded, finding a different level of offensive flexibility with a two-headed threat that forced OKC to make difficult defensive decisions. San Antonio won Game 4 in a blowout, with Fox and Wembanyama operating in tandem to expose the Thunder's defense.

Game 5 was different. Fox played but appeared limited, and Wembanyama had his worst game of the series on 4-of-15 shooting. The two-threat advantage that had made San Antonio competitive in the series collapsed, and Oklahoma City ran away with a 127-114 win.

What Fox Means to San Antonio's Offense

In the regular season, Fox averaged 25.8 points and 6.5 assists — production that placed him among the elite guards in the Western Conference. He is a blur in transition, a reliable mid-range scorer, and one of the best players in the league at converting in the paint through contact.

More important than his scoring is his playmaking function. Fox's ability to get into the paint and collapse defenses creates kick-out opportunities for shooters like Stephon Castle, Harrison Barnes, and Julian Champagnie. When Fox is right, the Spurs run through both him and Wembanyama in pick-and-roll and isolation actions that are genuinely difficult to guard.

When Fox is limited or absent, every Thunder defensive rotation becomes more predictable. They can front Wembanyama's catches, pack the paint, and dare San Antonio's role players to beat them from the outside. That strategy worked in Game 5. The Spurs know they cannot let it work again tonight.

The Ankle's History in This Series

Fox suffered the right high ankle sprain ahead of the series and was ruled out immediately for Games 1 and 2. He was cleared to return for Game 3 but has been on the injury report every day since. In Game 5, there was an incident late in the game where Fox appeared to reaggravate the ankle after a dive from an OKC defender. He returned to play but was clearly managing the injury.

The Spurs have eight days of rest after tonight's game if they survive — but first they have to survive. If Fox can play at somewhere near full capacity for 30 to 35 minutes tonight, San Antonio has a fighting chance. If he cannot, the Thunder need only replicate the Game 5 blueprint.

What's Next

Tonight's game tips at 8:30 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. The Spurs are listed as 3.5-point home favorites despite trailing the series 3-2, reflecting both their excellent home record (5-2 in the 2026 playoffs) and the wildcard of Wembanyama in an elimination game on his home floor.

Fox's official status will be updated on the NBA injury report approximately 90 minutes before tipoff. Whether he is available, active, or limited will shape the entire flow of the game and the series narrative heading into whatever comes next — another game in Oklahoma City, or the end of San Antonio's run.
