WCF Game 6 Tonight: Spurs Face Elimination as Thunder Hunt NBA Finals Berth

By Damon Pierce | May 28, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Western Conference Finals comes down to Thursday night in San Antonio. The Oklahoma City Thunder, fresh off a commanding 127-114 victory in Game 5, arrive at AT&T Center carrying a 3-2 series lead and a clear blueprint for controlling Victor Wembanyama. The Spurs, facing elimination for the first time in these playoffs, will need their transcendent talent to deliver — and then some.

Game 6 tips off at 8:30 PM ET on NBC and Peacock. A Thunder victory sends OKC to the NBA Finals against the New York Knicks beginning June 3. A Spurs victory forces a winner-take-all Game 7 back at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City.

Wembanyama Must Respond

The central question of Game 6 is the same question that has defined this entire series: what version of Victor Wembanyama shows up? In Game 5, it was the worst version — 4-of-15 from the floor, 20 points accumulated almost entirely through 12 free throws, a performance the Spurs described afterward as unacceptable.

The Thunder threw everything at him. Isaiah Hartenstein used his mass to push Wembanyama away from his preferred catch spots. Chet Holmgren disrupted his shooting angles with his length. Jaylin Williams and Alex Caruso harassed him in transition and on the perimeter. The rotating wall, deployed methodically by Thunder head coach Mark Daigneault, produced Wembanyama's worst shooting night in the entire WCF.

But history suggests Thursday's crowd at AT&T Center gets the bounce-back version. Wembanyama posted 33 points and 8 rebounds in Game 4 after being physically challenged in Game 2. He put up 41 points and 24 rebounds in Game 1 when the series was still being defined. San Antonio is 5-2 at home in the 2026 playoffs, and much of that success was built on Wembanyama feeding off the energy of a full building.

The Fox Factor

Alongside the Wembanyama question, no storyline has shadowed this series longer than the health of De'Aaron Fox. The 28-year-old guard — who averaged 25.8 points and 6.5 assists in the regular season — has been battling a right high ankle sprain since before Game 1, which he missed entirely. He also missed Game 2, returned in Game 3, and has been listed on the injury report in every game since.

For Game 6, Fox remains questionable.

The difference Fox makes cannot be understated. When he plays, San Antonio has a credible secondary creator who can attack the rim, draw fouls, and force Oklahoma City's defense to guard two threats simultaneously. Without Fox at full capacity, the Thunder can load help toward Wembanyama, disrupt his rhythm, and reduce him to the kind of performance that unfolded in Game 5.

If Fox plays and plays well, Game 6 is a genuine contest. If he's diminished or absent, the Thunder's path to closing out the series becomes considerably easier.

Oklahoma City's Formula

The Thunder do not need to reinvent themselves for Game 6. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander was transcendent in Game 5 — 32 points, 9 assists — and Alex Caruso gave Oklahoma City an impossible problem to solve off the bench with 22 points in 22 minutes. Jared McCain added 20 points in his first career playoff start, with 18 coming in the second half when San Antonio was searching for answers.

The blueprint is in place. OKC's bench depth is a genuine weapon that San Antonio's coaching staff has yet to solve. If Caruso and McCain are available and productive again Thursday, the Thunder can absorb whatever version of Wembanyama shows up and still manage the game through SGA's experience.

OKC won the 2025 NBA Championship, making them the defending champions entering Game 6. A trip back to the Finals would make this one of the most accomplished two-year stretches in recent franchise history.

What's Next

If the Thunder win tonight: Oklahoma City advances to the NBA Finals for the second consecutive season, where they will face the New York Knicks starting June 3 on ABC. The Knicks, resting since their May 26 sweep of the Cavaliers, enter as underdogs at +215 (DraftKings) but bring an 11-game win streak and a recovering OG Anunoby.

If the Spurs win tonight: the series shifts back to Oklahoma City for Game 7 on Saturday. San Antonio would carry the momentum of a home playoff survival win, and Wembanyama — motivated and at home — would be the most dangerous player on either floor.

Either way, Thursday night at AT&T Center is must-watch basketball. San Antonio's season is on the line. Oklahoma City's dynasty ambitions are a win away from the next stage. And 21,000 fans in San Antonio are ready to make history.
