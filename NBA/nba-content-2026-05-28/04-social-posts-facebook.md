### STORY 3: De'Aaron Fox Ankle — Spurs' Biggest Wildcard

#### Long-Form Post

All series long, the biggest subplot has been the same: will De'Aaron Fox play?

Fox has been battling a right high ankle sprain since the start of the Western Conference Finals. He missed Game 1 and Game 2 entirely, returned in Game 3, and has been on the injury report every day since. He played through the discomfort in Games 4 and 5, but his explosiveness — the thing that makes Fox one of the most dangerous guards in the NBA — hasn't been 100%.

For tonight's Game 6 at AT&T Center, Fox is listed as questionable.

That word — questionable — has defined this series. When Fox plays well, the Spurs are a different team. He averaged 25.8 points and 6.5 assists in the regular season and gives San Antonio a secondary playmaker who can take pressure off Wembanyama, get into the paint, and create easy buckets for teammates.

Without Fox at full strength, Oklahoma City's defense can key on Wembanyama, load up on double-teams, and force the Spurs' role players to beat them — which is exactly what happened in Game 5.

Tonight, the Spurs need Fox. And Fox needs to be healthy enough to deliver.

If he plays and performs? Game 6 becomes a game. If he doesn't? Thunder make the Finals.

It's that simple.
