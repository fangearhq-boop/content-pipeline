NBA Free Agency Opens in 2 Days — KAT, LeBron, Harden Headline the Class

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

June 28, 2026

The waiting is almost over. NBA free agency negotiations officially open on Tuesday, June 30 at 6:00 PM ET, and this year's class may be the most star-studded in recent memory. Karl-Anthony Towns, LeBron James, James Harden, Draymond Green, Zach LaVine, Norman Powell, Andrew Wiggins — the list of players who could change addresses runs well past 20 intriguing names.

Contracts cannot be signed until July 6 at 12:01 PM ET, per league rules, but teams and agents can begin negotiating when the moratorium window opens on June 30. Expect the rumor mill to go into overdrive the moment that clock strikes six.

The Three Stories That Will Define This Offseason

While the full free agent class is deep, three decisions stand above the rest.

Karl-Anthony Towns, fresh off winning the NBA Championship with the New York Knicks, faces the defining financial decision of his career. Towns is eligible for a four-year, $272 million maximum extension or a three-year, $208 million deal — both options beginning June 30. The fact that he has publicly declared he is "ready to make a financial sacrifice for the right team" has every Knicks fan holding their breath and every rival front office paying close attention.

LeBron James' future with the Los Angeles Lakers remains the league's most watched storyline. James has not indicated any immediate plans to leave Los Angeles, but with the Lakers still searching for a championship-contending core, his decision carries massive weight for both the franchise and the broader Western Conference landscape.

James Harden holds a player option with the Cleveland Cavaliers. The Cavaliers have positioned themselves as a legitimate contender, and Harden's choice to exercise or decline that option will shape Cleveland's roster construction heading into next season.

The Supporting Cast

Beyond the headliners, the depth of this free agent class makes it genuinely unpredictable. Norman Powell has established himself as one of the league's premier scorers off the bench and will attract serious max-level interest. Andrew Wiggins, a proven two-way wing, gives teams another high-upside target. Zach LaVine's combination of athleticism and scoring ability ensures he will draw a rich contract regardless of where he lands. Draymond Green, even at this stage of his career, is a defensive anchor and playmaker that winning teams covet.

The secondary tier of free agents — role players, veterans on minimum deals, and mid-level exception targets — will also move quickly once the big dominoes begin to fall on June 30.

The Brunson Factor

Jalen Brunson's contract decision last cycle has reset expectations across the entire league. Brunson took approximately $113 million less than his maximum contract value, prioritized winning, and proceeded to average 32.6 points per game while winning the 2026 NBA Finals MVP. That performance — championship jewelry, Finals MVP hardware, and universal respect — has made the "take less, win more" argument significantly more persuasive for players contemplating their next deals.

KAT is not the only player who has noticed. The Brunson model is now a template that agents and players are actively discussing across the league, and it could quietly shift several negotiations this summer in ways that benefit teams willing to build genuine contenders.

One More Wildcard: Giannis

Adding another layer of chaos to what is already a frenetic period: the Giannis Antetokounmpo trade between the Milwaukee Bucks and Miami Heat officially executes on July 6 — the same day contracts can be signed. That alignment means the league will see simultaneous roster moves across multiple fronts, with front offices managing free agent signings and the ripple effects of a blockbuster trade happening in the same window.

Front offices have known this date was coming. The teams that have done their homework will move decisively. The ones that have not will be scrambling.

What's Next

June 30 at 6 PM ET is the start gun. The negotiation window gives teams and players the opportunity to agree on terms, but no ink goes on paper until July 6. With more than 20 free agents generating legitimate market interest and three franchise-altering decisions still unresolved, this summer is going to move fast.
