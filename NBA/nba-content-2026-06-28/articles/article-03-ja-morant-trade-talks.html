Ja Morant Trade Talks Heat Up — Memphis Ready to Move On

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

June 28, 2026

When Brian Windhorst speaks about a player's future, the league listens. And what Windhorst said about Ja Morant this week did not leave much room for interpretation.

"I think we've probably seen the last moments of Ja Morant in a Grizzlies uniform."

That is a direct quote from the ESPN insider, and it has set off a wave of trade speculation that shows no signs of slowing. Memphis appears to be turning a page — and with the Phoenix Suns reportedly leading the race to acquire Morant, the destination picture is starting to take shape.

The Numbers Behind the Move

Morant's 2025-26 season raised legitimate questions about where his game is heading. He appeared in just 20 games last season, averaging 19.5 points and 8.1 assists per game. The scoring and playmaking are still there. The shooting numbers, however, hit career lows — 41.0% from the field and 23.5% from three-point range. For a player whose explosiveness and shot creation are central to his value, those efficiency numbers matter.

His contract is the other complicating factor. Morant is owed $42.2 million in 2026-27 and $44.9 million in 2027-28. Any team acquiring him is taking on a major commitment for a player who has struggled to stay healthy and whose shooting numbers have declined. That is a real risk, and it is shaping the trade market dynamics considerably.

The Trade Market Dynamics

The Suns are out front, per Bleacher Report. Phoenix has been aggressive in its pursuit of star talent, and Morant's playmaking ability fits the kind of offense they want to build. The fit makes sense on paper — Morant's downhill attack would put constant pressure on defenses, and if his shooting can return to career norms, the upside is enormous.

The Boston Celtics are circling as well, though their interest comes with an asterisk. Boston missed out on acquiring Giannis Antetokounmpo and is actively looking to upgrade. Morant would represent a significant swing at a different kind of star — younger, cheaper over the long term, but with a different risk profile than the Greek Freak would have carried.

The Milwaukee Bucks and Toronto Raptors have also been mentioned as interested parties, adding to the competition for Morant's services if Memphis does move him.

There is a catch, though. The trade negotiation has reached a standoff point. Memphis is asking acquiring teams to attach picks to sweeten the deal from their end. The interested teams, meanwhile, want Memphis to include additional assets to offset the risk of Morant's contract and injury history. Neither side has blinked yet — which is what makes this one of the more fascinating standoffs of the offseason.

Adding another wrinkle: most teams that identified a point guard need as a priority went into the 2026 NBA Draft and filled it there. The pool of teams that both need a lead guard and are willing to pay the price Memphis is asking is smaller than it might appear on the surface.

Memphis Is Building for Tomorrow

The Grizzlies are not sitting around waiting for someone to meet their asking price. They drafted Darius Acuff Jr. with the No. 7 pick in the 2026 NBA Draft, and by all indications, he is the player they intend to build around moving forward. Acuff represents the kind of young, cost-controlled talent that makes rebuilding a viable and even exciting proposition.

The Morant era in Memphis was defined by highlight-reel moments and playoff promise. It did not end with a championship, and the combination of availability concerns, efficiency questions, and contract weight has clearly pushed the organization toward a new direction. Windhorst's comment did not come out of nowhere — it reflected a sentiment that appears to be shared inside the Grizzlies' front office.

What's Next

This trade will not close immediately. The standoff over asset distribution needs to be resolved, and at least one of the interested teams needs to blink first. Watch for the Suns to either make a concrete offer or walk away as free agency consumes attention over the next ten days. If Memphis does not find a deal it likes before the July 6 signing window opens, the team may reset expectations, potentially accepting less than what it originally sought in exchange for moving Morant's contract and committing fully to the Acuff era. A trade announcement before the end of July feels realistic. Hoop Heroes will track every development.
