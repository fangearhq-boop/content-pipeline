KAT's $272M Decision Is the Knicks' Championship Retention Test

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

June 28, 2026

The New York Knicks are NBA champions. They have the rings, the banner, and the Finals MVP in Jalen Brunson. Now comes the harder part: keeping the team together.

Beginning June 30, when free agency negotiations officially open, Karl-Anthony Towns will be eligible for either a four-year, $272 million maximum extension or a three-year, $208 million deal from the Knicks. The number is staggering. The stakes are even larger. And KAT's own words suggest he already understands exactly what this moment means for New York's championship window.

"Ready to make a financial sacrifice for the right team." That is what Towns said publicly. In a league where max extensions are treated as entitlements, that statement is not nothing.

The Brunson Blueprint

Jalen Brunson wrote the playbook. When Brunson agreed to take approximately $113 million less than his maximum contract to stay with the Knicks, it was met with a mix of admiration and skepticism. Critics called it naïve. Brunson called it a bet on himself and his team.

He won that bet. Brunson averaged 32.6 points per game in the 2026 NBA Finals and walked away with the Finals MVP trophy. He has a championship. He has legendary status in New York. And he made his money work for the franchise in a way that kept the team competitive rather than hamstringing it under the luxury tax.

KAT saw all of it. Every Knick saw all of it. The question is whether KAT believes his own situation — his own legacy, his own career arc — merits the same kind of calculated sacrifice.

The Knicks' Cap Reality

This is where the numbers get tight. James Dolan and the Knicks front office are acutely sensitive to the NBA's second apron rules. The team is currently estimated to be $13 to $16 million below the second apron threshold — a narrow buffer that could disappear quickly depending on how KAT's deal is structured and what else the organization does this offseason.

The existing commitments are massive. OG Anunoby is locked in at $212.5 million over five years. Mikal Bridges is locked in at $150 million over four years. Add KAT at full max money and the second apron becomes an immediate reality, with all the roster-building restrictions that come with it.

Those restrictions are not trivial. Teams above the second apron cannot use the mid-level exception to sign free agents, cannot receive players in sign-and-trade deals, and face compounding penalties. For a championship team trying to add depth and address needs, being locked above that threshold is a serious competitive disadvantage.

Mitchell Robinson, a UFA this summer, has indicated he is very open to re-signing with New York. Whether the Knicks can make that work depends heavily on where KAT's deal lands.

What KAT Brings to the Table

The context for this negotiation is that Towns delivered in his first season as a Knick alongside Brunson. He was the frontcourt anchor the team had needed, his rim protection and rebounding complemented Brunson's guard play, and his ability to stretch the floor opened lanes that the Knicks had not had access to in prior seasons. He did not just fit — he was central to why New York won.

That production is why the max extension is on the table and why the Knicks cannot afford to let him walk. A Towns departure would leave the champion roster without its interior pillar and set off a rebuilding process that no Knicks fan wants to discuss right now.

What's Next

Negotiations officially open June 30 at 6 PM ET. KAT's camp and the Knicks front office are expected to move quickly once that window opens. The critical date is July 6 at 12:01 PM ET, when contracts can actually be signed. If KAT commits to a number below the full max — following the path Brunson blazed — the Knicks have a real opportunity to re-sign Robinson, address depth needs, and defend their championship. If KAT takes every dollar the max allows, the math becomes significantly more difficult. Dolan's comfort with the second apron — or lack thereof — will shape everything. Expect clarity on this one before July 6 arrives.
