NBA Summer League 2026 Preview: Dybantsa vs. Peterson Headlines Las Vegas

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

June 28, 2026

Las Vegas is always the center of the basketball world for one stretch every summer. Starting July 9 and running through July 19, the Thomas & Mack Center and the Pavilion at UNLV will host the 2026 NBA Summer League — and this year's edition has a marquee opening night matchup that looks more like a marketing department's dream than a random scheduling quirk.

AJ Dybantsa of the Washington Wizards against Darryn Peterson of the Utah Jazz. The No. 1 overall pick versus the No. 2 overall pick. Right out of the gate, on opening night, in Las Vegas.

This one is going to draw eyeballs.

AJ Dybantsa: The No. 1 Pick Arrives

The hype around AJ Dybantsa is not manufactured. He averaged 25.5 points per game and shot 51 percent from the field at BYU — those are dominant numbers by any measure, and they came against quality competition from a player who scouts believe has the tools to translate his production immediately to the professional level.

Washington took him with the first pick for a reason. The Wizards have been building toward a moment like this — landing a transformational young talent and giving him the runway to develop in a low-pressure environment. Summer League is Dybantsa's first professional stage, and the basketball world will be watching every possession to see whether the college numbers hold up as a preview of things to come.

Darryn Peterson: The No. 2 Responds

Darryn Peterson was the No. 2 overall pick and he lands with the Utah Jazz — a franchise that has shown real commitment to building through the draft. Peterson heads into Summer League with something to prove: in a draft where Dybantsa received the lion's share of the pre-draft attention, Peterson has every motivation to use these games to establish that the gap between No. 1 and No. 2 is smaller than the public narrative suggests.

Opening night matchups like this one are rare in their directness. No. 1 and No. 2 do not usually end up on opposite sides of the court this early in their careers. This is a genuine event.

Cameron Boozer and the Rest of the Top Picks

The third piece of the top-of-the-draft conversation is Cameron Boozer, the No. 3 overall pick out of Memphis heading to his new team. Boozer is expected to make his Summer League debut in Las Vegas as well, giving fans an early look at three of the top prospects from the 2026 class in a compressed ten-day window.

Depth does not come only from the top of the draft, either. Summer League rosters will be filled with players fighting for two-way contracts, roster spots, and a chance to prove they belong in the league. That competition is real, and the under-the-radar stories that emerge from Summer League every year are a reminder that the talent pipeline in professional basketball runs deeper than the top ten picks.

Two-Way Deals Already Signed

Before Summer League even tips off, several two-way contracts have already been locked in. Josh Dix, out of Creighton, has signed with the OKC Thunder. Tre Donaldson, out of Miami, is staying in Florida with the Miami Heat. AK Okereke, out of Vanderbilt, heads to the Los Angeles Lakers.

Two-way players occupy a unique space in the NBA ecosystem — they spend time bouncing between the NBA roster and the G League, getting game reps and development time that traditional roster spots do not always provide. These signings are worth tracking; two-way players who excel in Summer League often find their way onto guaranteed contracts faster than expected.

What's Next

July 9 is less than two weeks away. The Dybantsa-Peterson matchup on opening night is the headlining event, but Summer League is ten full days of basketball featuring every NBA franchise's next wave of talent. Cameron Boozer's debut will draw its own crowd of observers. The two-way signings of Dix, Donaldson, and Okereke give those franchise fan bases early looks at young players who could contribute this season. Hoop Heroes will be covering the top performances, the surprise standouts, and the storylines that will carry into training camp. Set your calendars for July 9 — Las Vegas is about to be the center of the basketball universe.
