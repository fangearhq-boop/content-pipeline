### STORY 2: KAT $272M Decision — Knicks' Championship Retention Test

**Long-Form Post:**

The New York Knicks just won the NBA Championship. Now comes the hardest part — keeping the team together. 🏆

Karl-Anthony Towns is eligible for either a 4-year, $272 million maximum extension or a 3-year, $208 million deal starting June 30. And according to KAT himself, money isn't the only factor: he's said he's "ready to make a financial sacrifice for the right team."

Sound familiar? Jalen Brunson took about $113 million less than his max contract, then turned around and won the 2026 Finals MVP while averaging 32.6 points per game. The Brunson model is now the blueprint.

But the Knicks' situation is complicated. Owner James Dolan is highly sensitive to the NBA's second apron rules — New York is reportedly just $13-16 million below that threshold. OG Anunoby is locked in at $212.5 million over 5 years. Mikal Bridges is locked in at $150 million over 4 years. Mitchell Robinson is also a free agent, though he's reportedly very open to returning.

The window to build on this championship is real, but it's narrow. KAT's decision — max money or a hometown discount — could define how long this Knicks dynasty runs.

What would you do if you were KAT? 👇

### STORY 3: Ja Morant Trade Talks Heat Up — Memphis Ready to Move On

**Long-Form Post:**

It sounds like Ja Morant's time in Memphis may be coming to an end.

ESPN's Brian Windhorst put it bluntly this week: "I think we've probably seen the last moments of Ja Morant in a Grizzlies uniform."

The numbers tell part of the story. Morant appeared in just 20 games last season, posting 19.5 PPG and 8.1 APG, but his shooting numbers were at career lows — 41.0% from the field and just 23.5% from three. And he's due $42.2 million in 2026-27 and $44.9 million in 2027-28.

Per Bleacher Report, the Phoenix Suns are the leading trade destination. The Boston Celtics are also circling after missing out on Giannis Antetokounmpo. The Milwaukee Bucks and Toronto Raptors have expressed interest as well.

There's a complication though: most teams that need a point guard already drafted one in the 2026 NBA Draft. Memphis wants incoming teams to attach picks — but other teams reportedly want Memphis to sweeten the offer first. Classic offseason standoff.

Meanwhile, the Grizzlies appear ready to hand the keys to Darius Acuff Jr., the No. 7 pick in the 2026 NBA Draft. A new era in Memphis is beginning.

### STORY 4: LaMelo + Edwards — Timberwolves' Dynamic New Backcourt

**Long-Form Post:**

Anthony Edwards just got a running mate — and the Western Conference should be on notice. 🐺

The Minnesota Timberwolves completed a blockbuster trade on June 25, acquiring LaMelo Ball and Josh Green from the Charlotte Hornets. In return, Minnesota sent Naz Reid, a 2033 unprotected first-round pick, three pick swaps (2028, 2029, 2030), and three second-round picks (2029, 2032, 2033) to Charlotte.

LaMelo Ball, 24, averaged 20.1 points and 7.1 assists per game last season on a $40.8 million annual salary. Pair him with Anthony Edwards, who had been carrying Minnesota as the team's clear alpha, and you suddenly have one of the most exciting backcourts in the NBA.

The trade officially executes July 6, but the pieces are in place. The Timberwolves are betting big — giving up a lot of future assets — to compete right now in the West.

For Charlotte, this is the beginning of a rebuild. Naz Reid is a solid young piece, and the haul of picks gives the Hornets ammunition to rebuild around their draft assets going forward.

Edwards and LaMelo. Are the Wolves for real heading into next season? 🏀
