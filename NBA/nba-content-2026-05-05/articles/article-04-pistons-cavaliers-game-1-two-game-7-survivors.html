Battle-Tested: Pistons Host Cavaliers in Game 1 After Both Teams Survived Back-to-Back Game 7s

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 5, 2026

The No. 1 seed Detroit Pistons open the Eastern Conference Semifinals tonight at Little Caesars Arena against the Cleveland Cavaliers — two squads that won Game 7s on the same afternoon just two days ago.

In a postseason already defined by dramatic finishes and unexpected heroes, Tuesday night's Game 1 between the Detroit Pistons and Cleveland Cavaliers carries a specific kind of emotional weight. Both teams just came through the most pressure-filled game possible. Both did it on the same day. Now they have to face each other.

The Pistons host the Cavaliers tonight at 7:00 PM ET at Little Caesars Arena, broadcasting on NBC and Peacock. It is the opening of the Eastern Conference Semifinals, and both franchises arrive here having earned every ounce of their right to be on this floor.

The Pistons' Historic Journey

Detroit's story this postseason is one of the most compelling in recent memory. The Pistons fell behind the Orlando Magic 3-1 in the first round — a deficit that historically eliminates nearly every team that faces it. Instead, Detroit fought back, winning three consecutive games to take a series that most observers had already written off.

Game 7 on May 3 was the exclamation point. Cade Cunningham delivered 32 points and 12 assists. Tobias Harris matched a career playoff high with 30 points, shooting 11-for-18 from the field and connecting on five three-pointers. The Pistons won 116-94, completing just the 15th comeback from a 3-1 deficit in NBA history and ending an 18-year postseason series drought.

The crowd at Little Caesars Arena that night was deafening. Tonight, they will be louder.

The Cavaliers Answered in Kind

Cleveland's path to this round was equally demanding. The Cavaliers and Toronto Raptors traded wins through six games before a decisive Game 7 in Cleveland on May 3 — on the same afternoon the Pistons were completing their comeback in Detroit.

Jarrett Allen was exceptional in that game, scoring 22 points and hauling in 19 rebounds. His dominance in the third quarter — when Cleveland outscored Toronto 38-19 — was the difference. Donovan Mitchell added 22 points and James Harden contributed 18. The Cavaliers won 114-102 and have now traveled to Detroit as the No. 4 seed looking to pull off a road win in Game 1.

Keys to the Series

For Detroit, the home floor advantage matters — but so does sustaining the offensive output Cade Cunningham and Harris showed in the closeout game. Jalen Duren's interior presence (15 points and 15 rebounds in Game 7) will be tested against Allen's physicality in what figures to be a physical battle in the paint.

For Cleveland, the challenge is executing a road game plan immediately after an emotionally exhausting Game 7. Mitchell and Allen need to replicate their Game 7 form quickly, and Harden's ability to control pace and find open teammates will be critical against a Detroit team that defends with intelligence and effort.

The matchup at point guard — Cade Cunningham against Darius Garland — may ultimately prove to be the series' defining individual battle.

What's Next

Game 1 tips off tonight at 7:00 PM ET at Little Caesars Arena in Detroit on NBC and Peacock. Both teams enter without the luxury of rest, having played Game 7 less than 48 hours ago. That shared exhaustion levels one field, but home court gives Detroit a distinct edge in the opener. The Cavaliers have shown they can win on the road in this postseason. Tonight they get their first chance to prove it against the Eastern Conference's No. 1 seed.
