No Mercy: Knicks Become First Team in NBA History to Win Three Straight Playoff Games by 25+ Points

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 5, 2026

Jalen Brunson scored 35 points, OG Anunoby was nearly perfect from the field, and the Knicks dispatched the 76ers 137-98 to set an unprecedented postseason record.

The New York Knicks are not just winning in the 2026 NBA Playoffs. They are rewriting the record books, one blowout at a time.

On Monday night at Madison Square Garden, New York crushed the Philadelphia 76ers 137-98 in Game 1 of the Eastern Conference Semifinals — their third consecutive playoff victory by a margin of at least 25 points. That has never happened in NBA history. Not once. The Knicks are the first team ever to accomplish it.

Combined across the three games, New York's point differential stands at 119 — the largest in any three-game stretch in postseason history.

Brunson Sets the Tone Early

Jalen Brunson finished with 35 points, but the damage was largely done before halftime. The Knicks point guard poured in 27 of those points in the first two quarters, systematically dismantling Philadelphia's defensive game plan before the 76ers could even attempt to adjust.

OG Anunoby was surgical alongside him. The versatile wing shot 7-for-8 from the field and finished with 18 points. Karl-Anthony Towns and Mikal Bridges each added 17 as New York spread the ball and the misery in equal measure.

The Knicks shot 63 percent from the field and at one point led by 40 points. It was the kind of performance that makes opponents question whether they belong on the same floor.

Embiid Silenced, Maxey Contained

Philadelphia arrived with legitimate offensive weapons. Joel Embiid — one of the most dominant scorers in the game — had averaged 28 points, nine rebounds and seven assists in playoff games this season. He had just completed a gritty seven-game series against Boston that showcased his resilience.

None of that translated on Monday night. Embiid shot 3-for-11 from the field and managed only 14 points. Tyrese Maxey, who had scored 30 in the Celtics series, was held to 13. Paul George provided 17, but even that bright spot could not dent a Knicks team that seemed to anticipate every Philadelphia possession before it developed.

The 76ers completed a 3-1 comeback to reach this round. Against New York, they never found their footing for even a quarter.

The Record Nobody Expected

To put the three-game run in context: the Knicks beat the Hawks by 51 points in Game 6 of the first round, building a 47-point halftime lead that set its own record. They then dispatched Atlanta's hopes in that series. Now they have opened the second round with another 39-point hammering.

No team in NBA playoff history had ever produced three consecutive victories with each margin exceeding 25 points — not the 1996 Bulls, not the 2001 Lakers, not the dynasties of any era. The 2026 New York Knicks own that distinction now.

"They're playing at a level I've never seen in a postseason," one analyst noted after the final buzzer. The numbers support that assessment without any need for embellishment.

What's Next

Game 2 of the Eastern Conference Semifinals between the Knicks and 76ers returns to Madison Square Garden. Philadelphia must find answers on both ends of the floor, starting with getting Embiid into better offensive rhythm and finding some way to slow Brunson in the early minutes.

For New York, the challenge is simpler to articulate and harder to achieve: maintaining this unprecedented level of focus and execution. History, however, suggests the Knicks have earned every bit of confidence they carry into the next game.
