Knicks Rolling, Wolves Stealing, Luka Out: The 2026 NBA Conference Semifinals Are Already Historic

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 5, 2026

Two days into the second round of the 2026 NBA Playoffs, the record books have already been touched. Here is everything you need to know as four series play out toward the Conference Finals.

The first round of the 2026 NBA Playoffs produced game-winners, 3-1 comebacks and a historic 61-point blowout. Two days into the second round, the Conference Semifinals are already delivering at the same pace — and in some cases, surpassing it.

Two series are now 1-0. Two more tip off tonight. Luka Doncic is on the sideline. Victor Wembanyama is shattering records and still losing. The New York Knicks are rewriting NBA history every time they take the floor. Here is where everything stands.

East: Knicks Making History

The No. 3 seed New York Knicks crushed the Philadelphia 76ers 137-98 in Game 1 on Monday, their third consecutive playoff victory by at least 25 points. That has never happened in NBA history. The combined margin across those three games — 119 points — is the largest in any three-game stretch in postseason history.

Jalen Brunson scored 27 points in the first half alone, finishing with 35. OG Anunoby shot 7-for-8. The Knicks shot 63 percent from the field. Joel Embiid managed just 14 points on 3-for-11 shooting. Philadelphia, which completed its own 3-1 comeback to reach this round, looked overmatched from the opening tip.

Game 2 in New York looms. The question facing Philly is not just how to adjust — it is whether any adjustment can neutralize a team playing at this level of offensive and defensive efficiency.

East: Pistons and Cavaliers Tip Off Tonight

The other Eastern Semifinals matchup opens tonight at 7:00 PM ET at Little Caesars Arena in Detroit. The No. 1 seed Pistons host the No. 4 seed Cavaliers — two teams that both won Game 7s on the afternoon of May 3.

Detroit completed one of the great comebacks in recent playoff memory, rallying from a 3-1 deficit against Orlando before Cade Cunningham (32 points, 12 assists) and Tobias Harris (30 points, career playoff-high) closed it out in Game 7. Cleveland answered with Jarrett Allen's 22-point, 19-rebound masterpiece to eliminate the Raptors the same evening.

Both franchises earned this moment the hard way. Neither has a rest advantage. The home crowd in Detroit will be operating on pure playoff electricity.

West: Wolves Steal One in San Antonio

The No. 2 seed San Antonio Spurs suffered a stunning home defeat Monday when the Minnesota Timberwolves won Game 1, 104-102 — despite Victor Wembanyama's historic 12-block performance that shattered the all-time NBA playoff record for blocks in a single game.

Wembanyama's triple-double (11 points, 15 rebounds, 12 blocks) was one of the most extraordinary individual defensive efforts in postseason history. It was not enough. Anthony Edwards, returning 10 days early from a hyperextended knee, came off the bench and scored 11 of his 18 points in the fourth quarter to seal Minnesota's road win.

San Antonio hosts Game 2. The Spurs need their offense to match Wembanyama's defense if they want to reclaim home-court advantage in this series.

West: Lakers Face the Thunder Without Luka

Tonight's late game in Oklahoma City represents the most unusual storyline of the second round. Luka Doncic, officially ruled out for Game 1 with a Grade 2 hamstring strain, has not played in more than 32 days and according to coach JJ Redick has not yet begun his contact ramp-up. A return in Game 3 or Game 4 is possible but not guaranteed.

The No. 1 seed Thunder enter as 15.5-point favorites — and that spread may undersell the challenge Los Angeles faces. Oklahoma City beat the Lakers by an average of 29.3 points per game during the regular season. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is operating at MVP level. LeBron James will carry the load for a Lakers team that needs to stay competitive long enough for Doncic's potential return to matter.

Game 1 tips off at 8:30 PM ET on NBC and Peacock from Paycom Center in Oklahoma City.

What's Next

Tonight's double-header — Pistons-Cavaliers at 7:00 PM ET and Thunder-Lakers at 8:30 PM ET — completes the opening-game rotation for all four Conference Semifinals series. By tomorrow morning, every second-round series will have played at least one game, and the picture of how the path to the Conference Finals is shaping up will be considerably clearer. Four series, two months' worth of basketball remaining, and every game from here forward matters more than the last.
