### STORY 2: Wembanyama Sets NBA Playoff Blocks Record — But Wolves Steal Game 1

**Long-Form Post**
Victor Wembanyama put on a defensive performance last night that may never be matched.

In Game 1 of the Western Conference Semifinals in San Antonio, the 22-year-old Spurs center recorded 12 blocks — shattering the all-time NBA playoff record for blocks in a single game. He matched the previous record of 10 in just three quarters.

He also had 11 points, 15 rebounds and 12 blocks — one of the rarest triple-doubles in basketball history, with blocks as the third stat.

The Timberwolves still won, 104-102.

That's where the night gets surreal. Despite Wembanyama's historic effort, Anthony Edwards made sure the storyline wouldn't be clean. Edwards — who was expected to miss the first two games of this series after hyperextending his left knee — came off the bench and scored 18 points, including 11 in the fourth quarter, closing out a narrow Wolves victory.

More blocks than points for Wembanyama. A record-breaking individual game. And still a loss.

The Spurs are still very much alive in this series, but it's a brutal start when your franchise player has arguably the greatest defensive game in playoff history and you come up two points short.
