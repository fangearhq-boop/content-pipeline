### Long-Form Post Victor Wembanyama put on a defensive performance last night that may never be matched. In Game 1 of the Western Conference Semifinals in San Antonio, the 22-year-old Spurs center recorded 12 blocks — shattering the all-time NBA playoff record for blocks in a single game. He matched the previous record of 10 in just three quarters. He also had 11 points, 15 rebounds and 12 blocks — one of the rarest triple-doubles in basketball history, with blocks as the third stat. The Timberwolves still won, 104-102. That's where the night gets surreal. Despite Wembanyama's historic effort, Anthony Edwards made sure the storyline wouldn't be clean. Edwards — who was expected to miss the first two games of this series after hyperextending his left knee — came off the bench and scored 18 points, including 11 in the fourth quarter, closing out a narrow Wolves victory. More blocks than points for Wembanyama. A record-breaking individual game. And still a loss. The Spurs are still very much alive in this series, but it's a brutal start when your franchise player has arguably the greatest defensive game in playoff history and you come up two points short. ### Image Post Caption Wembanyama: 11 pts, 15 reb, 12 blocks. ### Long-Form Post The Lakers-Thunder series was already a massive mismatch on paper. Now it's even more lopsided. Luka Doncic has officially been ruled out for tonight's Game 1 in Oklahoma City, per the official injury report. The Slovenian superstar has been sidelined for more than 32 days with a Grade 2 hamstring strain — and according to his own coach, JJ Redick, he hasn't even begun the contact ramp-up phase of his recovery yet. That leaves LeBron James to take on the Oklahoma City Thunder — the No. 1 seed in the West — as 15.5-point underdogs tonight. The Thunder beat the Lakers by an average of 29.3 points per game during the regular season. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is playing at an MVP level, and OKC's depth makes them dangerous even when opponents game-plan specifically for their best player. LeBron James, at this stage of his career, has proven he can carry teams in the playoffs. But keeping pace with a complete, healthy Thunder squad in Oklahoma City — without Doncic drawing defensive attention and creating spacing — is a different kind of challenge.
