NBA Draft Is 4 Days Away: Dybantsa Visits Done, Peterson Medicals Clear

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 19, 2026

With four days until the 2026 NBA Draft at Barclays Center in Brooklyn, the top of the board is coming into sharp focus. AJ Dybantsa is a -450 odds-on favorite to go No. 1 overall to the Washington Wizards, and Darryn Peterson got positive news this week when his medical evaluations came back clean.

The top-two storyline that has dominated pre-draft coverage since March is now approaching its resolution. Here is where things stand heading into draft week.

Dybantsa's Visits: Washington and Utah — Done

Dybantsa scheduled exactly two pre-draft visits: Washington and Utah. He completed both. That level of precision isn't accidental — top prospects generally only schedule visits to teams with realistic intent to draft them, and visit No. 1 went to the Wizards while visit No. 2 went to the Jazz, holding the No. 2 pick.

Sources around the league indicate Dybantsa's profile — a 6-foot-9 wing who averaged 25.5 points and shot 51.0 percent from the field in his lone college season at BYU — aligns well with what Washington has valued in building their roster. The Wizards already have Trae Young, Anthony Davis (acquired at the February trade deadline), Alex Sarr, Bilal Coulibaly, and Kyshawn George. Dybantsa has reportedly told people close to him that he expects to be the No. 1 pick.

His comparisons run generational. Scouts have noted his scoring instincts and size create a profile not seen since the late 1970s for a consensus No. 1 prospect — a comparison that first emerged during his BYU season and has not been walked back.

Peterson Medicals: No Red Flags

Darryn Peterson, the Kansas guard projected to go No. 2 to the Utah Jazz, got the news his camp needed this week. His medical evaluations, a source of quiet concern after a season slowed by physical issues, came back without major red flags.

Peterson's talent was never in question — the question was whether the physical issues that affected his 2025-26 college season at Kansas would give teams pause. With medical clearance now in hand, the path to No. 2 looks unobstructed.

Utah has been in need of a foundational piece since trading Lauri Markkanen. Peterson, with his combination of size, shooting, and playmaking at 6-foot-6, fits that need precisely.

The Wizards Roster Picture

Washington's situation is genuinely fascinating. They acquired Trae Young from Atlanta in January and Anthony Davis at the February deadline, building aggressively around a projected No. 1 pick. Trae Young is expected to reject his $49 million player option and renegotiate with Washington at a lower per-year rate — a sign of his commitment to what the Wizards are building.

Young plus Davis plus Dybantsa creates one of the more intriguing young cores in the Eastern Conference. Whether they can develop into a playoff team within two or three years will be among the more interesting storylines of the next half-decade.

What's Next

The draft begins June 23, with Round 2 continuing June 24. Both take place at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. Given the Giannis trade deadline also falls on June 23, it could be one of the more eventful nights in recent NBA history — two major franchise-altering moments potentially happening simultaneously.

Dybantsa's name will almost certainly be called first. Peterson's should follow immediately after. Four days.
