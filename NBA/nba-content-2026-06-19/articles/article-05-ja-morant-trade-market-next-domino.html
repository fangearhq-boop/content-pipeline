After Giannis, the Next Big Domino: Why the Ja Morant Trade Market Is About to Open

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 19, 2026

The NBA's offseason earthquake is coming in waves. Wave one — the Knicks' championship — has already washed through. Wave two — the Giannis Antetokounmpo trade — is expected before the June 23 draft. But sources suggest wave three may be building quietly in Memphis: the Ja Morant trade market.

Reports indicate the Grizzlies have been deliberate about their timing: they are waiting for the Giannis situation to resolve before entering serious discussions about trading their franchise point guard. The reasoning is straightforward. Once Giannis lands somewhere, the entire landscape reshapes — and Memphis wants to negotiate from a position of full information.

The Heat Connection

The Miami Heat are among the teams monitoring Morant's availability. Critically, sources have identified the Heat as a team that views Morant as their Plan B if they miss on Giannis. Given that the Heat are currently the team with momentum in Giannis talks but face real resistance from the Bucks over their offer structure, that fallback position may become relevant sooner than Miami would prefer.

Morant, 26, averaged 24.4 points per game last season and remains one of the most electrifying guard-level talents in the league when healthy. A Morant-Butler core in Miami would create a very different kind of offensive threat than a Giannis-Butler combination — more explosive off the dribble, less imposing physically — but it would give the Heat genuine star power.

Memphis's Calculus

Why would Memphis trade Morant? The Grizzlies have had internal discussions about their long-term direction, and Morant's injury history — he has missed significant time in multiple recent seasons — has complicated the franchise's ability to build around him consistently. Memphis went 41-41 last season, missing the playoff play-in by two games.

The Grizzlies have assets: young players and picks accumulated over a competitive window that has produced mixed results. Trading Morant would be a reset — potentially painful, but potentially accelerating the next rebuild if they can maximize the return from a marquee asset.

A Second Wave of Deals

NBA observers tracking the offseason have noted that the June 23 draft date is not just a Giannis deadline — it is the pivot point for nearly everything happening across the league. Multiple teams are waiting for the draft to clarify pick values, Giannis's destination, and the broader trade market before making their own moves.

That means the post-draft window — roughly June 24 through the July 6 free agency start — could be unusually active. If Giannis resolves and the Morant market opens simultaneously, it would create a layered set of negotiations unlike anything in recent memory.

What's Next

Watch June 23 as the reset date for the Morant market. If Giannis lands in Miami, that likely closes the Heat's interest in Morant. If the Giannis deal goes elsewhere, the Heat's interest in Memphis could accelerate quickly. Either way, the Grizzlies are watching, waiting, and ready to make their move.

This offseason has multiple layers. The first wave broke over New York. The second is building in Milwaukee. The third may be gathering in Memphis.
