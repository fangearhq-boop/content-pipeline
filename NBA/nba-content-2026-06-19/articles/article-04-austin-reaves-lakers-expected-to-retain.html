Why Austin Reaves Is Staying in LA: The Case for a Lakers Homecoming

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 19, 2026

Among the many moving parts of this NBA offseason, Austin Reaves' free agency has developed a quiet clarity: league sources widely expect him to remain with the Los Angeles Lakers.

Reaves holds a $14.9 million player option for the 2026-27 season and is expected to decline it before the June 29 deadline, entering the open market as an unrestricted free agent. But the open market, in this case, is expected to lead him right back to Los Angeles — likely on a deal in the $200 million range.

The Structural Advantage

The math is significant. Under current collective bargaining rules, the Lakers can offer Reaves a five-year maximum contract worth approximately $239.3 million. Rival teams — including the Brooklyn Nets, who are widely expected to submit a competing offer — are limited to four-year maximum contracts worth approximately $178.5 million.

That's not a small difference. Over a five-year deal, the Lakers can put roughly $60 million more on the table than any outside team. The Nets have been the most prominently linked competitor, but recent reports indicate Brooklyn is fading as a credible threat. The Pistons and Hawks have also expressed interest, though neither appears positioned to meaningfully challenge the Lakers' structural edge.

League sources expect the final number to land around $200 million over five years — below the hard maximum but well above what any outside team can offer on a four-year deal.

The Luka Factor

Reaves averaged 23.3 points, 5.5 assists, and 4.7 rebounds per game last season while shooting 49.0 percent from the field. Those numbers make him one of the most efficient scoring guards in the league, but the context around them matters: Reaves developed that production within the Lakers system, alongside Luka Doncic.

The Doncic-Reaves partnership has become one of the more effective backcourt combinations in the Western Conference, and that partnership doesn't exist anywhere else. Sources close to Reaves indicate his affection for Los Angeles and his desire to continue building with Luka are significant factors in his decision-making. Money matters. Chemistry matters too.

Timeline

Reaves must decide on his player option by June 29. Free agency officially opens June 30 at 6 p.m. ET. Teams cannot officially sign players until July 6, but deals can be agreed upon and announced from June 30 onward.

The Lakers will have exclusive negotiating rights until Reaves formally opts out. If he opts out, there will be a window — likely brief — before the sides reach an agreement. Multiple front-office sources indicate they believe the two sides will not let this linger into late July.

What's Next

Watch June 29 as the first major marker: Reaves declines his option. Then watch June 30 for reports of deal framework. The expectation throughout the league is that by the time free agency opens, Reaves will already have a path back to the Lakers mapped out. For LA fans, one of the few clear wins in what could be a complicated offseason appears to be taking shape.
