4 Days to Decide: Giannis Trade Race Is Now Heat vs. Celtics

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 19, 2026

The Knicks parade is over. The Canyon of Heroes has been swept. And now, the most consequential transaction in the NBA offseason is staring down a four-day deadline.

Giannis Antetokounmpo's trade market has narrowed to two teams: the Miami Heat and the Boston Celtics. The Milwaukee Bucks want a deal done before the June 23 NBA Draft. Marc Stein has reported the situation is "drawing near." What happens over the next four days could reshape the Eastern Conference for the next decade.

Heat: Momentum, With an Asterisk

Miami entered this week as the team with the most momentum in Giannis talks. Their package — Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., and the No. 13 pick in next week's draft — has been the "presumed certainty" of what a Heat offer would contain, according to multiple reports.

But there is an issue. Multiple outlets have reported that the Bucks are not impressed with what Miami is putting on the table. ESPN's Brian Windhorst has characterized the organization's reaction to the Heat package as underwhelming. The specifics of that "issue" haven't been fully disclosed, but the framing makes clear this isn't a done deal simply because the Heat want it to be.

For Miami, acquiring Giannis would represent a dramatic reset — pairing the two-time MVP with Jimmy Butler (who is returning from injury) and a young core. But the Bucks aren't desperate, and they know it.

Celtics: On the List, Offering Brown

Boston is very much alive. According to reports, Giannis has the Celtics on his list of preferred destinations, and Boston has reportedly made a formal offer that includes Jaylen Brown as the centerpiece.

That creates a fascinating dilemma for both franchises. For the Celtics, trading Brown — a cornerstone of back-to-back Finals appearances — would be a seismic shift in philosophy. For the Bucks, Brown would represent an arguably better basketball trade return than Herro. He's younger, more versatile, and under contract at a controlled rate.

The Celtics have also indicated a willingness to sign Giannis to a contract extension in the event of a trade — a meaningful offer given that Antetokounmpo has two years remaining on his deal and is eligible for a $275 million extension starting October 1.

The Knicks Are Out

Multiple reports confirmed this week that the Knicks — who won the championship just days ago and whose parade still echoes through Lower Manhattan — are not a factor in the Giannis sweepstakes. Despite widespread speculation and fan hopes of pairing Giannis with Jalen Brunson, the Knicks do not appear on Milwaukee's radar at this stage.

Whether that changes after the draft remains to be seen, but the current trade conversation is firmly between Miami and Boston.

What's Next

The June 23 NBA Draft serves as an unofficial deadline. The Bucks want clarity before draft night — both because the No. 13 pick is part of the Heat's offer and because Milwaukee may want to use additional picks strategically. Expect the next 96 hours to produce significant reporting movement on this story.

Heat or Celtics. Four days. The next chapter of Giannis Antetokounmpo's career is about to be written.
