Knicks vs. Cavaliers — ECF Game 4 Preview: New York One Win From First Finals Since 1999

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 25, 2026

The New York Knicks arrive in Cleveland tonight needing one win to do something they have not done in 27 years. A victory in Game 4 of the Eastern Conference Finals at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse sends them to the NBA Finals for the first time since 1999. Tip-off is 8 PM ET on ABC.

They lead the series 3-0. No team in NBA playoff history has ever recovered from a 3-0 deficit. The mathematics are brutal for Cleveland, the narrative is irresistible for New York, and the stage is set for one of the most significant games the Knicks franchise has played in decades.

What New York Has Built

The Knicks have won 10 consecutive playoff games and are operating at a 130.5 offensive rating during that stretch — the best mark among any remaining team in the 2026 postseason. They are not winning these games with one superstar. They are winning them with five contributors who each know their role and execute it without deviation.

Jalen Brunson leads the charge, averaging 26 points in the ECF. Mikal Bridges shot 11-of-15 from the floor in Game 3 for 22 points, adding 3 steals and 2 blocks. OG Anunoby posted 21 points and 7 rebounds in that same game. Karl-Anthony Towns ran the offense from the high post with 7 assists alongside 13 points. Josh Hart contributed 12 points, 9 rebounds, 4 assists, and 5 steals. There are no nights off on this roster. Cleveland has been unable to guard all of them simultaneously.

Cleveland's Last Stand

The Cavaliers were 6-1 at home earlier in these playoffs, which makes Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse a natural setting for their best effort. Donovan Mitchell has been individually strong — 29 points in Game 1, 23 in Game 3 — but strong individual performances have not translated into wins when the rest of the supporting cast falters.

The issue begins and ends with James Harden. He shot 1-of-8 from three-point range in Game 3 and committed 6 turnovers in Game 1. The Knicks have specifically targeted Harden on defense, funneling ball-handlers toward him in pick-and-roll situations and exploiting every moment of hesitation. Evan Mobley's 24 points in Game 3 showed Cleveland's secondary scoring potential; the Cavs need Harden to match that energy, not undermine it.

Both rosters enter Game 4 with clean injury reports. This will be decided by execution, not availability.

The Line and the Moment

The Knicks open as 2.5-point favorites at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, with the total set at 217.5. The spread is tight enough to suggest oddsmakers believe Cleveland is capable of winning tonight — but not tight enough to suggest it is likely.

History creates the emotional weight. The 1999 Finals appearance ended in a five-game loss to the San Antonio Spurs. Since then, New York has rebuilt, retooled, and regressed many times over. This run — anchored by Brunson, assembled intelligently over four offseasons, and executed with remarkable poise through 10 straight postseason wins — represents something different. This team has earned every game of this run.

What's Next

If the Knicks win tonight, they advance to the NBA Finals, which begin Wednesday, June 3, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC. New York would face the winner of the Thunder-Spurs series. If Cleveland extends the series, Game 5 would be scheduled for Thursday, May 28, in New York. ECF Game 4: Tonight, Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland, 8 PM ET, ABC. Series: Knicks lead 3-0.
