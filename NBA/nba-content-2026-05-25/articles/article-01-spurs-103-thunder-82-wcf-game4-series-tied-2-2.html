Wembanyama 33 Points and a Half-Court Buzzer Beater: Spurs Blowout Thunder 103-82, WCF Tied 2-2

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 25, 2026

Victor Wembanyama needed three dribbles and 40 feet to summarize exactly what kind of player he is. As the first-half clock expired at Frost Bank Center on Sunday night, the San Antonio Spurs center caught the ball near the bench, drove to center court, and launched a shot that went in — a half-court buzzer beater that pushed San Antonio's lead to 50-38 and effectively ended Oklahoma City's hopes before the night was over.

The final score was Spurs 103, Thunder 82. The Western Conference Finals is tied at 2-2, and one of the dominant stories of the 2026 postseason — the Thunder's relentless depth — was completely dismantled in a single game.

Wembanyama finished with 33 points, 8 rebounds, 5 assists, and 3 blocks. He scored 22 of those points in the first half. He blocked a Chet Holmgren dunk attempt within the game's first two minutes. He drained a 27-footer 24 seconds in to set the offensive tone. And then, at halftime, came the 40-foot bomb that lit up every highlight package in sports.

A Rout From the Opening Tip

This was not a close game that got away from Oklahoma City late. San Antonio imposed its will from the jump, racing out to a 16-0 run in the first quarter and refusing to let the Thunder climb back. The Spurs led for 96 percent of the contest and built their advantage to as many as 25 points.

Oklahoma City's offensive system, which averaged 76 bench points per game in Games 2 and 3, was entirely neutralized. The Thunder shot just 33 percent from the field and a stunning 18 percent from beyond the arc. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, the two-time MVP, finished with 19 points on 6-of-15 shooting — a quiet night against a defense that seemed to know every read he wanted to make.

San Antonio's Supporting Cast Delivered

Wembanyama was the story, but this was a complete team performance from San Antonio. De'Aaron Fox posted 12 points and 10 rebounds, quietly stuffing the stat sheet without needing a single signature play. Stephon Castle ran the offense with maturity beyond his years, finishing with 13 points and 6 assists. Devin Vassell added 13 points and 6 rebounds.

The Spurs starting five played connected basketball throughout — sharing the ball, attacking weak points in OKC's coverage, and rotating sharply on the other end. Wembanyama anchored the defense in ways the raw numbers don't fully capture, altering shots and clogging the paint even when he wasn't recording blocks.

The Buzzer Beater That Changed Everything

Wembanyama's halftime heave deserves its own paragraph. According to GeniusIQ tracking data, it was the longest field goal by a Spurs player in the playoffs since tracking began in 2014. It was also the longest made shot of Wembanyama's career. In a series where San Antonio needed to assert itself after dropping Games 2 and 3, the buzzer beater arrived at the perfect psychological moment.

"I feel like with who we are, we need to start games like this," Wembanyama said after the game. The sentiment applied to halves as much as full contests — his shot sent San Antonio into the locker room with a lead and a momentum that Oklahoma City simply couldn't overcome.

What's Next

The Western Conference Finals shifts back to Paycom Center in Oklahoma City for Game 5 on Tuesday, May 26, at 8:30 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. The Thunder enter as 5.5-point home favorites. SGA and the OKC offense must rediscover their rhythm — a bounce-back performance at home is well within reach for a team that went 8-0 in the postseason before losing Game 1. For San Antonio, a road win in Game 5 would give the Spurs a chance to close out the series in Game 6 back in San Antonio on May 28.
