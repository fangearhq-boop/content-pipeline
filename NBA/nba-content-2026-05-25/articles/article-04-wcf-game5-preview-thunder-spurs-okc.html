WCF Game 5 Preview: Thunder Must Respond at Home as Series Resets at 2-2

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 25, 2026

Twenty-four hours after getting blown out at home in Game 4, the Oklahoma City Thunder will need to find answers — fast. The Western Conference Finals is tied 2-2, and Game 5 tips off Tuesday at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City at 8:30 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. The Thunder open as 5.5-point home favorites against a San Antonio Spurs team that just delivered the most dominant performance of the series.

The Thunder went 8-0 before losing Game 1 of this series. Paycom Center has been a fortress. The crowd, the altitude, the familiarity — all of it has historically worked in Oklahoma City's favor. But Game 5 will test something more fundamental: whether this team can respond after being completely disassembled for 48 minutes.

What Went Wrong for OKC in Game 4

The numbers tell a harsh story. Oklahoma City shot 33 percent from the field and 18 percent from beyond the arc in Game 4. The bench unit — which had poured in 76 combined points over Games 2 and 3 — was neutralized. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander managed just 19 points on 6-of-15 shooting, a performance that stood in stark contrast to his MVP-level output earlier in the postseason.

Victor Wembanyama dominated every phase of the game, but the structural failure for OKC was defensive. San Antonio's guards and wings found openings repeatedly, and the Spurs' passing rotations created easy looks throughout. This was not bad shooting luck — it was a system failure.

What OKC Needs to Fix

SGA bouncing back is the obvious starting point. He has shown all postseason that elite scoring nights are well within his capability. The question is whether the offensive infrastructure around him — the spacing, the ball movement, the role-player contributions — can recover. Jared McCain delivered 24 points off the bench in Game 3; repeating that output in Game 5 would change the tenor of the entire night.

Defensively, Oklahoma City must contain Wembanyama more effectively. Giving him clean looks from 27 feet and 40 feet in the first half of Game 4 set an impossible tone. The Thunder will need to bring a body to Wembanyama early and force him to create offense from the post rather than from pull-up range.

What San Antonio Is Playing For

A Spurs win on the road in Game 5 would give San Antonio a chance to close out the series in Game 6, scheduled for May 28 in San Antonio. Wembanyama averages 41.7 minutes per game in this series and has been the best player through four games. De'Aaron Fox's 10-rebound performance in Game 4 showed the Spurs have depth beyond their star. If the Spurs can manage OKC's home crowd early and prevent a 16-0 run in the other direction, they will be in this game.

What's Next

Game 5 is the pivot point of this series. Oklahoma City holds home-court advantage and carries the motivation of Sunday's loss. San Antonio has the momentum of a dominant performance and the confidence of knowing they have already won on the road once this series. The winner controls the series. WCF Game 5: Tuesday May 26, Paycom Center, Oklahoma City, 8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock. Series: Tied 2-2.
