Kawhi Investigation Expands to Second Endorsement Deal — Trade to Toronto Still Frozen

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 18, 2026

The NBA's investigation into Kawhi Leonard's trade eligibility has grown in scope, with a second previously undisclosed endorsement deal now added to the inquiry, according to a HoopsHype report from earlier this week.

The agreed deal that would send Leonard to the Toronto Raptors — where he won a championship in 2019 before departing in free agency — remains frozen with no timeline for resolution.

A Case That Keeps Growing

The original investigation centered on a $28 million endorsement agreement with a company called Aspiration, which the NBA alleged was structured in ways that constituted impermissible benefits to Leonard during his time under contract. The new development adds a second deal to the review — increasing the complexity and likely the timeline of the investigation.

Former Clippers executive Steve Sanberg, who was a central figure in the original endorsement arrangement, was convicted on related charges and sentenced to 14 years in prison. The Clippers have claimed they were defrauded as part of the scheme. The criminal proceedings have added another layer to what is already an unprecedented investigation in league history.

The Trade That Can't Close

The agreed framework — Leonard returning to Toronto in exchange for Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, first-round picks in 2031 and 2033, second-round picks in 2030 and 2033, and a 2027 first-round swap — was reached weeks ago. But it cannot become official until the NBA completes its review and determines whether any penalties apply.

The Toronto Raptors declined to assume liability for any potential NBA-imposed penalties against Leonard or the deal. The league has given no estimate for when its investigation will conclude. Commissioner Adam Silver has previously said the league wants the investigation fully resolved before allowing a trade to proceed.

Leonard's Situation

Kawhi Leonard is 35 years old. The Clippers chose not to offer him an extension, making this trade — if and when it clears — his path to a new team. He has remained out of the public eye throughout the investigation and has not made public comments on the status of the case.

With each passing week of legal and league scrutiny, Leonard's offseason limbo continues. He is not a Clipper, but he is not yet a Raptor either.

What's Next

The NBA has not given a timeline for the investigation's conclusion. Watch for league communications regarding the Sanberg case and any updates on the second endorsement deal. Until the investigation closes, the Kawhi-to-Toronto trade remains on indefinite hold.
