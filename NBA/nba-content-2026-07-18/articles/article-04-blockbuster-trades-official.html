It's Official: Giannis to Miami, LaMelo to Minnesota, George to Boston — The NBA's Offseason Is Complete

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 18, 2026

With the NBA moratorium ending Friday, every major trade agreed upon during the freeze period became official Saturday. The league's most dramatic offseason in memory has produced a fully reshuffled landscape — and it is now on the books.

Five blockbuster transactions, each agreed upon during the moratorium period, cleared simultaneously. Here is the new reality:

Giannis Antetokounmpo: Miami Heat

The Greek Freak is officially a Miami Heat player. Antetokounmpo, a two-time MVP and 2021 NBA champion with Milwaukee, heads south in what Heat president Pat Riley has long positioned his franchise to pursue. Miami adds a generational talent to a roster built around pace, spacing, and championship-caliber culture.

LaMelo Ball: Minnesota Timberwolves

LaMelo Ball joins the Timberwolves in a multi-piece deal that sent Josh Green alongside Ball to Minnesota in exchange for Naz Reid and picks. Ball gives Minnesota a dynamic playmaking presence alongside their existing core.

Paul George: Boston Celtics

Paul George is now a Celtic. Boston adds a third star to a roster that already features championship DNA from their 2024 title. The Celtics, who have remained one of the East's premier franchises, add another wing scorer and veteran presence.

Jaylen Brown: Philadelphia 76ers

Jaylen Brown joins Joel Embiid in Philadelphia. Brown was the All-Star swingman departing Boston as part of the complex trade web centered on Paul George, giving the 76ers a proven co-star to build around their franchise center. Philadelphia now has two of the most talented players in the Eastern Conference on the same team.

Ja Morant: Portland Trail Blazers

Ja Morant heads to Portland in a trade that brought Jerami Grant, Kris Murray, and cash considerations back to Memphis. The Grizzlies part with the franchise player who defined their recent identity; Portland gains a star guard around whom to rebuild. The deal included no draft picks, an unusual structure for a transaction of this magnitude.

What's Next

The NBA's offseason landscape is now set — with one enormous asterisk. LeBron James has not yet announced his destination, and his decision (expected next week) will be the final piece that determines which conference has the most imposing new power structure. Every team in contention for LeBron has been watching these deals become official while preparing their own roster accordingly.
