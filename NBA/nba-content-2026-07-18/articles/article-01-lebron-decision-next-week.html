LeBron Said "Won't Hold You Up Much Longer" — Sources Say Decision Comes Next Week

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 18, 2026

LeBron James gave the basketball world the tease of the summer on Thursday night at Fanatics Fest NYC — and then promptly walked away without delivering.

Appearing on a live taping of the "Mind the Game" podcast alongside Tyrese Haliburton, James looked out at a packed audience and offered his most pointed words yet: "I won't hold you guys up too much longer." When Haliburton pressed him for more, James shut it down: "We literally talked about this in the back."

He then left the stage without naming a team. The internet imploded anyway.

The Wait Is Almost Over

While Thursday's appearance yielded no announcement, multiple league sources confirmed to reporters Friday that LeBron's decision is expected during the week of July 20. The summer's most anticipated domino is finally about to fall.

James has already told the Los Angeles Lakers they can move on without him for the 2026-27 season. His agent, Rich Paul, has spoken to representatives from 27 teams since the start of free agency. The field, however, has narrowed considerably.

The Golden State Warriors, Philadelphia 76ers, Cleveland Cavaliers, and Miami Heat are the remaining serious contenders. Multiple Western Conference executives told HoopsHype earlier this week that the Warriors have emerged as the frontrunner, though league sources caution that nothing is finalized and James has made no commitments.

What LeBron Has Said

James has kept his public comments deliberately vague throughout the process. "I want to compete at a high level," he told the Fanatics Fest crowd. "I want to join a franchise that shares the same values as myself."

He is 41 years old entering the 2026-27 season — a remarkable age for any professional athlete, let alone one who remains among the most sought-after free agents in the sport. Whichever franchise lands him instantly transforms its championship outlook. Every front office not in the running has already begun adjusting its offseason accordingly.

The cap maneuvering has been visible across the league. In Cleveland, James Harden this week declined a $42.3 million player option to work on a restructured deal — widely interpreted as a move to give the Cavaliers flexibility to pursue LeBron. The 76ers secured Jaylen Brown to pair with Joel Embiid. The Warriors have been aggressive in creating the roster infrastructure to support a James signing.

What's Next

By all accounts, the decision is coming. LeBron's "won't hold you up much longer" may have been the last major tease before the announcement that reshapes the NBA. Sources expect clarity during the coming week — possibly as early as Monday or Tuesday.

For a league that has spent two months holding its collective breath, the end of the wait is in sight. The question is simply where LeBron James will play next — and that answer, for the first time all summer, feels genuinely close.

What's Next

Monitor league sources and LeBron's social media closely. A signing announcement is expected during the week of July 20. Once official, a press conference and jersey reveal would follow quickly for the receiving franchise.
