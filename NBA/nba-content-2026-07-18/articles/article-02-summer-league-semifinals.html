Summer League Semifinals Tonight: Lakers (4-0) Face Warriors, Grizzlies Battle Rockets

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | July 18, 2026

The NBA's showcase of next-generation talent reaches its penultimate night on Saturday, with two Summer League semifinal matchups set to deliver the most competitive basketball yet in Las Vegas.

The Los Angeles Lakers, the tournament's lone unbeaten team at 4-0, face the Golden State Warriors in the second game of a Prime Video doubleheader beginning at 8:30 PM ET. In the opener, the Memphis Grizzlies take on the Houston Rockets at 6:30 PM ET. The two winners advance to Sunday's Summer League Championship.

Lakers the Team to Beat

Los Angeles earned the No. 1 seed with the only unblemished record in pool play. Cameron Carr has been the offensive catalyst, turning heads with his performance in Day 8's Battle of LA matchup. The Lakers' depth and chemistry have translated into a dominant pool-play run, and they'll be heavy favorites to advance to Sunday's title game.

Standing in their way are the Warriors, who secured the No. 4 seed on the strength of LJ Cryer's play throughout the tournament. Golden State's brand carries weight even in Summer League — the chance to hand the unbeaten Lakers their first loss will bring extra edge to tonight's matchup.

Grizzlies vs. Rockets Opens the Night

The first semifinal features two teams that clawed through a crowded six-team tiebreaker situation to lock down their seeds. Memphis held the No. 2 spot on a point differential of plus-11.5, while Houston earned No. 3 at plus-10.3. The Warriors edged into the fourth and final spot at plus-6.3.

Memphis's postseason pedigree — even in Summer League — gives them an edge in knowing how to handle high-pressure moments. Houston, meanwhile, has the talent and athleticism to push back. Expect a competitive first game before the Lakers-Warriors showdown takes center stage.

More Than a Summer Game

With the NBA's historic free agency period still unresolved — LeBron James's decision is expected next week — Summer League carries an unusual backdrop of anticipation. A Lakers-Warriors semifinal carries accidental narrative weight: two of the teams most heavily linked to James are competing tonight in the Nevada desert.

For the players on the court, however, the bigger picture is simpler: win and play again Sunday, or pack up and watch. These are careers being shaped in real time, in front of NBA front offices with roster decisions to make before training camps open in the fall.

What's Next

The two winners meet in the Summer League Championship on Sunday, July 19 in Las Vegas. Both semifinal games air on Prime Video — Grizzlies vs. Rockets at 6:30 PM ET, Lakers vs. Warriors at 8:30 PM ET.
