NBA Draft Countdown: Withdrawal Deadline Arrives as Dybantsa Leads 10-Day Sprint to June 23

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 13, 2026

The 2026 NBA Draft is now 10 days away, and the class is officially set. Today, June 13, is the withdrawal deadline for early entry players — the final moment for anyone to pull their name and return to college. The top prospects are staying in.

AJ Dybantsa. Darryn Peterson. Cam Boozer. All three are confirmed for the green room at Barclays Center in Brooklyn on the night of June 23, and all three are competing for their spot at the top of the board.

The No. 1 Picture

Dybantsa enters the final stretch as the heavy odds-on favorite. BetMGM has him at -500 to be selected No. 1 overall by the Washington Wizards — a price that reflects his dominance at BYU and the consensus view across the league that he is the safest, highest-ceiling prospect in the draft.

The numbers from his freshman season at BYU are historic. He averaged 25.5 points, 6.8 rebounds, and 3.7 assists per game while shooting 51.0 percent from the floor — a statistical combination that made him the first player since Larry Bird in 1978-79 to achieve that output while also earning consensus All-American honors. At the NBA Draft Combine, he measured in with a 42-inch vertical and a 7-foot-0.5 wingspan.

The Wizards have a confirmed pre-draft visit with Dybantsa scheduled for this week, according to The Athletic's Shams Charania and Fred Katz. Washington hasn't tipped its hand publicly.

Peterson and Boozer Still Alive

Darryn Peterson is not going away quietly. The Kansas standout is at +375 to be taken No. 1 overall, which is live money in any sportsbook. Multiple front-office sources have indicated Peterson remains "very much in the mix" for the top pick, offering a different skill set and style than Dybantsa that some teams find appealing.

Cam Boozer checks in at +1500 and is universally regarded as a top-five selection. His green room invite reflects how highly he is viewed as a prospect, even if he trails Dybantsa and Peterson in the No. 1 conversation.

The Wizards' Decision

Washington has been deliberate and measured in this process. They were reportedly non-committal after the lottery, and their decision to schedule visits with multiple prospects rather than immediately declaring for Dybantsa suggests they are doing their due diligence. But the odds — and the general consensus of scouts and executives across the league — point to the BYU star walking to the stage first.

What's Next

Ten days. No more withdrawals. The stage is set in Brooklyn. On the night of June 23, Commissioner Adam Silver will walk to the podium at Barclays Center and announce the No. 1 overall pick in the 2026 NBA Draft. All indications point to AJ Dybantsa hearing his name first — but in the NBA Draft, nothing is certain until the commissioner speaks.
