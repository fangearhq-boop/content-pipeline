Knicks Can End 53 Years of Waiting Tonight: Game 5 of the 2026 NBA Finals

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 13, 2026

Fifty-three years of waiting could end tonight in San Antonio. Game 5 of the 2026 NBA Finals tips off at 8:30 PM ET on ABC from Frost Bank Center, where the New York Knicks arrive with a 3-1 series lead and one of the most remarkable road-game records in recent postseason history.

The San Antonio Spurs are 5.5-point home favorites at FanDuel and ESPN/DraftKings, with the total set at 216.5 points. They have Victor Wembanyama, who is rested and healthy after a two-day break. And they have a Frost Bank Center crowd that will bring a level of noise and urgency befitting a franchise staring down elimination.

None of it may matter. The Knicks have already been here — twice. New York won Games 1 and 2 in this same building to open the series, proving early that San Antonio's atmosphere holds no power over them.

The Numbers Are On New York's Side

The Knicks have been perfect in road closeout games this postseason: three wins, all away from Madison Square Garden, by an average margin of 39.3 points. Not three points. Not thirteen points. Thirty-nine-point-three. This team doesn't close out series — it ends them with authority.

History backs up that dominance at the macro level. Teams with a 3-1 lead in the NBA Finals win the championship approximately 96 percent of the time. In more than 80 years of Finals history, only one team has ever mounted a comeback from that deficit — the 2016 Golden State Warriors, who rallied against the Cleveland Cavaliers. That is one team, in the entire history of the sport.

The Spurs are not the 2016 Warriors. And this Knicks team, with its identity built on road toughness and Jalen Brunson's steady leadership, is not going to fold in San Antonio.

What to Watch

Jalen Brunson is the engine of everything. He scored 30 points in Game 1 and 36 in Game 4, and has been the source of calm and offense every time the Knicks needed a basket or a steadying possession. SportsLine's model projects him for approximately 24 points tonight — another efficient outing, another night of finding ways to make the right play.

Karl-Anthony Towns and OG Anunoby — whose tip-in with 1.2 seconds remaining saved Game 4 — give the Knicks versatility that San Antonio has no clean answer for. Josh Hart continues to be an absolute pest on the glass.

The critical battle will be managing Wembanyama. The Spurs' 22-year-old star comes in healthy after two days of rest, which is the most recovery time he's had all postseason. He averaged 40.3 minutes per game during these playoffs and looked gassed at points in Game 4, shooting just 9-of-25 from the floor. SportsLine projects him for 29 points tonight — a significant bounce-back from Game 4's 24. When Wemby is locked in, as he was in Game 3 when he scored 32, the Spurs are a completely different team. The Knicks will need to make his night difficult early.

What's Next

If the Knicks win tonight, they will bring a championship to New York for the first time since Willis Reed and Walt Frazier closed out the Los Angeles Lakers in 1973. The city has been waiting through generations of near-misses, heartbreak, and mediocrity for precisely this moment.

If the Spurs find a way to extend the series, Game 6 returns to Madison Square Garden on Tuesday, June 17. But based on everything New York has shown in three road elimination wins — and based on 80-plus years of Finals history — tonight is the Knicks' night to claim. The wait ends in San Antonio. That's the story. And it tips off at 8:30 PM ET.
