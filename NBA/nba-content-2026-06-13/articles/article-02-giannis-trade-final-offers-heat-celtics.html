Bucks Seeking Final Offers for Giannis as Heat-Celtics Trade Race Reaches Its Endgame

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 13, 2026

The Milwaukee Bucks are now actively soliciting final offers for Giannis Antetokounmpo, and the trade that will reshape the Eastern Conference is coming into sharp focus. With the NBA Draft just 10 days away, co-owner Jimmy Haslam wants this resolved before June 23 — and two teams are competing head-to-head for the two-time MVP.

The Miami Heat and the Boston Celtics. The championship contenders at opposite ends of the landscape. The deadline is the same for both of them.

Heat: The Frontrunner

Miami has led this race from the beginning, and according to multiple reports, Giannis prefers South Beach as his destination. The Heat's reported offer is substantial: Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., the No. 13 overall pick in this month's draft, and multiple future first-round picks.

It's a package that addresses what Milwaukee needs — young players, immediate contributors, and draft equity to rebuild around. The Heat have been the most organized and aggressive team in this process since Milwaukee first opened Giannis to trade discussions.

One important factor: Giannis is reportedly "determined to stay in the Eastern Conference," a stipulation that Miami satisfies. He has ruled out Western Conference destinations entirely.

Celtics: The Wild Card

The Boston Celtics' emergence has complicated everything. Multiple reports indicate Boston is now willing to trade "anyone besides Jayson Tatum" in pursuit of Antetokounmpo. The likely foundation of a Celtics offer centers on Jaylen Brown — who won a Finals MVP just two years ago — plus significant draft capital.

A Brown-for-Giannis trade would be one of the most jaw-dropping exchanges in league history. Two All-Stars, both with championship pedigrees, switching franchises in one deal. Tatum and Giannis as Boston teammates. Brunson and Giannis as... wait. This is where it gets complicated — if the Knicks finish off the Spurs tonight, they'll be champions. The Celtics would be adding a co-star without a ring.

The Nets remain the dark horse. Brooklyn possesses the draft capital to construct a compelling offer and has enough flexibility to be dangerous. But this is shaping up as a two-team finale between Boston and Miami.

The Bucks' Position

Milwaukee's leverage is real but time-sensitive. The $275 million supermax extension Giannis is eligible for on October 1 changes the calculus dramatically — if he signs that, the Bucks could keep him. But if Giannis is serious about moving on, the window for Milwaukee to extract maximum value is closing fast.

Final offers being requested now suggests Haslam's timeline is genuine. The Bucks want this done before the chaos of draft night, before teams get distracted by picks and new assets, and before the league officially turns to next season.

What's Next

The next 10 days will determine whether Giannis Antetokounmpo stays in Milwaukee, lands in Miami, or shocks the world by becoming a Celtic. The deadline is real, the offers are being made, and the two-time MVP's next chapter is about to be written. Check the NBA news feed frequently — this could break at any moment.
