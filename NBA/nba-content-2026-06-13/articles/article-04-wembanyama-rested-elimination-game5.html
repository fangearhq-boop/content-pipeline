Victor Wembanyama Rested and Ready: The 22-Year-Old Gets His Best Shot to Save the Spurs

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 13, 2026

For the first time all postseason, Victor Wembanyama enters a game with his body as close to fully recharged as it has been since the playoffs began. He is healthy. He is cleared. And tonight, with the San Antonio Spurs facing elimination in Game 5 against the New York Knicks, he gets the best chance of this series to announce himself on basketball's biggest stage.

The two-day break between Game 4 and tonight's Game 5 might not sound significant. For Wembanyama, it is enormous.

The Load He Has Carried

Wembanyama has averaged 40.3 minutes per game this postseason — a career high for usage and a workload that would tax any player, let alone a 22-year-old in his first NBA Finals. In Game 4, he logged 44 grueling minutes. He shot 9-of-25 from the floor (36 percent), finished with 24 points and 13 rebounds, and watched helplessly as the Spurs' 27-point second-half lead vanished in one of the most stunning collapses in Finals history.

"It just hurts," he said afterward. It was one of the most honest and telling quotes of the series.

The challenge for Wembanyama hasn't been talent — it has been sustaining that talent deep into high-usage games against a physical Knicks defense that applies constant pressure. In Game 4, he was a different player in the fourth quarter than he was in the first. The legs were heavy. The decisions were slower.

What Two Days Means

The gap between Game 4 (Wednesday, June 10) and Game 5 (tonight, June 13) is the longest rest period of Wembanyama's postseason. He doesn't need to be convinced to take care of his body — he will have done everything in his preparation to be as explosive and effective as possible for 48 minutes.

When Wembanyama is at full energy — as he was in Game 3 in New York, when he scored 32 points — the Spurs become a legitimately dangerous team. They won that game, 115-111. They can win tonight if he replicates that version of himself.

SportsLine's predictive model projects him for 29 points tonight — a significant bounce-back from Game 4 and a reflection of what a rested Wemby can do at Frost Bank Center, where his home crowd will be at absolute full volume for an elimination game.

The Legacy Question

Wembanyama is 22 years old and already in the NBA Finals. That fact alone separates him from virtually every player in league history. But his performance in this series — 24 points on 36 percent shooting in Game 4, a loss from a commanding position — will follow him for years if the Spurs lose tonight.

The chance to change that narrative exists right now. Fresh legs. Home court. A city that needs him to deliver. This is the moment that will define how people remember his first Finals.

What's Next

If Wembanyama delivers and the Spurs win tonight, Game 6 shifts to Madison Square Garden on Tuesday, June 17. If the Knicks close it out, a remarkable chapter ends for San Antonio's young core — but it would still represent a season that far exceeded expectations for a 22-year-old leading a team to the Finals. Tonight's answer starts at 8:30 PM ET on ABC.
