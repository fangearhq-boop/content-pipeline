Kawhi Leonard Trade Probe Expands to Second Endorsement Deal, Raptors Still Waiting

By Marcus Cole | July 15, 2026

NBA investigators examining a previously unreported arrangement between Leonard and a second company, widening a probe that has already kept the blockbuster Clippers-Raptors trade on hold for two weeks.

The investigation surrounding Kawhi Leonard's trade to the Toronto Raptors just got bigger — and that means the wait just got longer.

As of Tuesday, NBA investigators working through outside counsel Wachtell Lipton are examining a previously unreported endorsement deal Leonard had with a second company, separate from the $28 million Aspiration arrangement that originally triggered the salary cap circumvention probe, according to HoopsHype. The expansion of scope adds another layer of complexity to an already tangled situation that has left the trade in limbo since late June.

The Clippers and Raptors agreed June 30 to send Leonard to Toronto in exchange for Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, and a package of future picks. But the deal cannot be finalized until the NBA's investigation concludes — and the league has made clear to Toronto that they would need to assume the risk of any penalties that result from the probe. The Raptors declined.

A Growing Investigation

The probe's origins trace to Aspiration, a now-bankrupt green banking company that held both a $28 million personal endorsement deal with Leonard and a $300 million, 23-year naming rights partnership with the Clippers franchise. Investigators have been examining whether those dual arrangements constituted improper cap circumvention — a mechanism to funnel additional compensation to a player outside of his official contract.

The Clippers have maintained that they were defrauded. Former Aspiration CEO Joe Sanberg, convicted on separate charges and sentenced to 14 years in prison, is central to the probe. The Clippers say they cooperated fully with investigators, providing access to dozens of interviews and tens of thousands of documents.

Now investigators are looking at a second company arrangement that was not previously reported. The expansion of the investigation's scope makes a near-term resolution less likely, not more.

The Raptors Stuck in Limbo

Toronto's front office has been unable to plan around this trade for two weeks. They cannot integrate Kawhi into their systems, cannot adjust their roster plans around his arrival, and cannot project their competitive standing for the 2026-27 season with any certainty. The NBA essentially told them to decide whether to accept the risk — and they said no.

That is a reasonable position. Accepting unknown financial penalties on a trade that may or may not survive scrutiny is not a business decision a front office can responsibly make. But the result is a franchise stuck in place while the league investigates the team they're trying to trade with.

Kawhi Leonard's situation is not much better. The former Finals MVP has been unable to commit to a city, work with teammates, or begin any meaningful preparation for next season. At 35, those are not trivial concerns.

What's Next

No timetable has been set for the conclusion of the investigation. The second endorsement deal now under examination suggests the probe still has material ground to cover. Until investigators complete their work and the league makes a determination, the Kawhi Leonard trade remains frozen — and both franchises remain in an uncomfortable holding pattern that shows no signs of ending anytime soon.
