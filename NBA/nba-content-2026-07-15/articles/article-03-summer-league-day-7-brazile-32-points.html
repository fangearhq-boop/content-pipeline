Brazile Erupts for 32 as Nuggets Edge Thunder; Hansen Nearly Triples in Portland Blowout

By Damon Pierce | July 15, 2026

Trevon Brazile delivered the performance of Day 7 while Yang Hansen's all-around game carried Portland to a dominant 27-point win over Minnesota in NBA Summer League action.

Day 7 of NBA Summer League in Las Vegas did not disappoint. Trevon Brazile turned in one of the week's best individual performances, and Yang Hansen gave Portland fans something genuine to get excited about in a Wednesday slate that had no shortage of compelling moments.

Here's how it all unfolded.

Brazile Puts on a Show

The Nuggets forward was the story of the day. Brazile poured in 32 points to lead Denver to a 106-103 win over Oklahoma City in a game that went down to the wire. His scoring burst carried a Nuggets team that needed every bit of it in a three-point escape that kept the crowd engaged until the final buzzer.

Brazile has been one of the more intriguing developmental players in Denver's pipeline, and a 32-point Summer League performance does nothing to quiet that conversation. The Nuggets, coming off a deep playoff run, are always looking for ways to add depth, and Brazile's scoring in high-pressure moments is exactly the kind of thing evaluators pay attention to even in the low-stakes Summer League environment.

This was his statement game. He delivered.

Hansen's Three-Dimensional Night Buries Minnesota

The Trail Blazers handled the Timberwolves 111-84 with Yang Hansen doing a little bit of everything. The forward finished with 18 points, 10 rebounds, and 5 assists in what was a genuinely dominant all-around performance against a Minnesota team that had no answers.

Portland's margin of victory — 27 points — tells the full story. The Blazers controlled this game from start to finish, and Hansen's ability to contribute as a scorer, rebounder, and playmaker in the same game is the kind of versatility that draws attention from front offices looking to project a player's long-term ceiling.

The rebuild in Portland is ongoing, but performances like Hansen's give the organization concrete evidence of what they're building toward.

Around the Rest of Day 7

The Phoenix Suns edged the Milwaukee Bucks 95-88, with Koa Pete scoring 19 points and adding 2 steals. Pete has been a bright spot throughout Summer League, and another double-digit scoring night keeps his name in conversations that matter.

The Utah Jazz defeated the Chicago Bulls 80-63, with Jonas Aidoo recording a double-double of 15 points and 11 rebounds. Aidoo's interior presence has been consistent all week, and the Jazz coaching staff has seen enough to feel good about what he brings to the frontcourt rotation.

What's Next

Summer League continues in Las Vegas as the tournament heads toward its final days. Brazile's 32-point effort will earn him headlines in Denver media, and Hansen's complete performance deserves wider recognition as one of the week's most impressive outings. For teams still evaluating their rosters ahead of training camp, every game in this environment matters — and Day 7 gave multiple front offices some encouraging data points to work with.
