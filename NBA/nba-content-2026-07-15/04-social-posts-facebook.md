#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET

The NBA's investigation into the Kawhi Leonard/Clippers situation just got more complicated — and that means Toronto's wait just got longer.

As of Tuesday, investigators are now examining a previously unreported endorsement deal Leonard had with a second company, separate from the $28 million Aspiration deal that first triggered the probe. The scope of the investigation continues to widen, with outside counsel Wachtell Lipton still working through a trove of documents and interviews.

No timetable has been set.

Meanwhile, Kawhi Leonard waits. The former Finals MVP hasn't been able to plan his offseason, work with his new teammates, or even know which city he'll be playing in come October. At 35, every lost preparation day matters.

This is one of the most unusual situations in recent NBA history — a completed trade that cannot be completed.

### STORY 4: James Harden Opts Out of $42.3M

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

James Harden is walking away from $42.3 million.

That's the value of the player option he's declining with the Cleveland Cavaliers for the 2026-27 season. But don't read this as Harden heading for the exits — he and the Cavs are working on a new multiyear deal.

The move is a player-friendly restructure, not a departure. Harden opts out of a single large year and re-signs for a deal that works better for both sides going forward. It's a common offseason maneuver for players who want to get off of a contract structure that no longer reflects market value.

What makes this interesting from a Cleveland perspective: the Cavs are currently in the mix for LeBron James, working on a new contract structure for Harden, and dealing with their James Harden opt-out situation — all at the same time. If all of it breaks right, Cleveland could emerge from this offseason as one of the most dangerous teams in the East.

The pieces are moving. Cleveland fans should be paying close attention.
