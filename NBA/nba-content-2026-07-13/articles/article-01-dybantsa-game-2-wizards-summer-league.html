AJ Dybantsa Delivers Again: 23 Points, 7 Rebounds as Wizards Stay Perfect in Vegas

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | July 13, 2026

NBA Summer League | Washington Wizards | AJ Dybantsa | Rookies

AJ Dybantsa is not going to let anyone forget why Washington made him the No. 1 pick.

In his second Las Vegas Summer League game on Sunday, the 19-year-old BYU product posted 23 points, 7 rebounds, 3 blocks, and 2 steals as the Wizards cruised to a 104-85 victory over the Sacramento Kings. Two days after his eye-opening 27-point debut against the Utah Jazz, Dybantsa showed that first performance was no fluke — adding elite defensive instincts to an already polished offensive game.

Through two Vegas games, Dybantsa is averaging 25.0 points and 7.0 rebounds while keeping opponents honest on the other end. The Wizards have not lost a game.

A Different Kind of No. 1 Pick

What separates Dybantsa from many top picks who arrive in Summer League is the two-way effort. It is easy for prospects to chase buckets and light up the scoreboard against reserves and undrafted rookies. It is another thing entirely to anchor a defense with three blocks and two steals while still doing the offensive work.

The Wizards drafted Dybantsa expecting him to anchor their franchise for the next decade. Through two Summer League games, those expectations look entirely justified. He has shown patience on offense — not forcing shots — and a sense of defensive timing that usually takes years for young players to develop.

Sunday's Full Summer League Scoreboard

Dybantsa was the headliner, but Sunday provided plenty of action across the Las Vegas bracket:

  • Golden State Warriors 104, Oklahoma City Thunder 79
  • LA Clippers 104, Utah Jazz 82
  • New Orleans Pelicans 81, Phoenix Suns 75
  • Washington Wizards 104, Sacramento Kings 85

The Warriors' blowout win over the Thunder featured strong play from their young roster, while the Clippers used a balanced attack to beat the Jazz comfortably.

Rookie Class Starting to Take Shape

Dybantsa is not the only rookie turning heads. His former rival from the summer circuit, Darryn Peterson of the Jazz — the No. 2 pick out of Kansas — opened his Salt Lake City Summer League with 28 points and followed it up with a 25-point, 12-assist performance. The Peterson-Dybantsa competition for rookie supremacy is just beginning.

Meanwhile, No. 4 pick Caleb Wilson of the Bulls scored 35 points in his Vegas debut against the Grizzlies — a remarkable outburst. No. 3 pick Cameron Boozer of the Grizzlies won that game, answering Wilson's performance with 23 points of his own.

This 2026 draft class is looking like a genuine generational group of talent at the top.

What's Next

Dybantsa and the Wizards will look to extend their Summer League winning streak as the Las Vegas bracket continues through mid-July. With each performance, the buzz around Washington's rebuild grows louder. If Dybantsa keeps this up — and there is no reason yet to think he won't — the Wizards may have found their face of the franchise for years to come.
