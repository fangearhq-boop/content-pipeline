LaMelo Ball Is a Timberwolf: Minnesota Lands Its Running Mate for Anthony Edwards

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | July 13, 2026

NBA Trades | LaMelo Ball | Minnesota Timberwolves | Anthony Edwards | Charlotte Hornets

Anthony Edwards is getting a backcourt partner worthy of his star power.

The Minnesota Timberwolves have acquired LaMelo Ball and Josh Green from the Charlotte Hornets in a four-team trade, pairing two of the league's most electric young guards on the same roster. In return, Charlotte received Naz Reid, three first-round pick swaps, and three second-round picks. Julius Randle was moved to the Brooklyn Nets as part of the deal's structure.

The move signals Minnesota's aggressive intent this offseason. After battling deep into the playoffs in recent years, the Wolves are going all-in on the Edwards era — and LaMelo Ball is the most high-upside piece they could have added.

Why This Pairing Makes Sense

On paper, the Edwards-Ball backcourt is one of the most exciting offensive pairings in the Western Conference. Both players are gifted playmakers, both create their own shot at a high level, and both have shown the kind of scoring versatility that modern NBA defenses struggle to contain. Edwards brings downhill explosiveness and elite athleticism. Ball is a 6-foot-7 point guard who sees the floor as well as anyone in the game.

The fit questions will revolve around the defensive end — neither player has been a lockdown defender by reputation — but in today's NBA, offensive juice can cover a lot of ground.

Josh Green arrives as well, giving the Wolves another versatile wing with defensive energy and athleticism that complements the backcourt's scoring.

What Charlotte Gets

The Hornets received Naz Reid — a proven rotation big who has been one of the league's better off-the-bench contributors — along with a significant haul of future draft capital. Charlotte is in rebuild mode, and the picks give them flexibility to accumulate assets and shape the next chapter of their roster.

The Randle-to-Brooklyn portion of the four-team structure allowed Minnesota to complete the deal without taking on all of the salary obligations themselves.

What's Next

LaMelo Ball will arrive in Minnesota with plenty of motivation. He is one of the league's most naturally talented players, and joining a legitimate contender alongside Edwards could unlock a version of his game that Charlotte's rebuilding rosters never allowed. Training camp will tell how quickly the two stars gel — but the potential is undeniable.

For the Timberwolves and their fans, the summer just got a lot more exciting. Keep it locked on Hoop Heroes as Minnesota continues to reshape its roster heading into 2026-27.
