Summer League Day 5: Dillon Mitchell's 24-Point Game Leads Celtics; Chaz Lanier Hits 7 Threes for Pistons

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 13, 2026

NBA Summer League | Boston Celtics | Detroit Pistons | Dillon Mitchell | Chaz Lanier

Day 5 of NBA Summer League in Las Vegas delivered two convincing wins and a pair of standout individual performances on Monday.

The Boston Celtics defeated the Charlotte Hornets 87-75 behind 24 points from Dillon Mitchell, while the Detroit Pistons handled the Cleveland Cavaliers 103-94 with Chaz Lanier connecting on seven three-pointers. Both players made strong cases for expanded roles with their respective organizations.

Dillon Mitchell Stars for Boston

Mitchell's 24-point effort was the high-water mark of the day in Las Vegas. The young forward showed the kind of scoring efficiency that has made him an intriguing prospect for the Celtics, finding his spots within the offense and converting at a high clip. Boston controlled the game from early on, pulling away in the second half to hand Charlotte a decisive defeat.

For a Celtics franchise that values two-way players and system fit, Mitchell's ability to contribute offensively while playing within structure was an encouraging sign.

Chaz Lanier Catches Fire in Detroit's Win

Chaz Lanier provided the most memorable individual sequence of Day 5, knocking down seven three-pointers in the Pistons' 103-94 victory over the Cavaliers. When a shooter gets that hot from range in a Summer League setting, it creates a buzz that carries beyond the box score — and Lanier's performance will have the Pistons' coaching staff taking notice.

Cleveland had a chance to make a game of it in the fourth quarter, but Detroit's lead proved too substantial. The Cavaliers' Summer League group will look to regroup ahead of their next outing.

The Bigger Picture

With five days of Summer League now in the books, the 2026 class is establishing a pecking order at the top. AJ Dybantsa (Washington) leads the way at 25 points per game through two appearances. Caleb Wilson (Chicago) announced himself with a 35-point debut. Darryn Peterson (Utah) has been dominant in Salt Lake City action. And now Dillon Mitchell and Chaz Lanier are adding their names to the list of prospects worth tracking.

The Summer League runs through mid-July, giving the rest of the field time to make their mark.
