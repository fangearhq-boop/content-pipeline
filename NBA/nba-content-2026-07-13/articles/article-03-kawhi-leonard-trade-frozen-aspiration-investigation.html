The Kawhi Leonard Trade Is Still Frozen — Here's Why the Investigation Is So Complicated

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 13, 2026

NBA Trades | Kawhi Leonard | LA Clippers | Toronto Raptors | NBA Investigation

The Kawhi Leonard trade to Toronto is agreed upon. The trade package is set. The players know where they are going. But the deal cannot be completed — because the NBA is still investigating something that has nothing to do with basketball.

At the center of the frozen transaction is a $28 million endorsement deal between Kawhi Leonard and Aspiration, a green banking company that has since gone bankrupt. The NBA is probing whether the Los Angeles Clippers used that deal to compensate Leonard in a way that circumvented league salary cap rules. Until the investigation concludes, the trade stays in limbo.

A Web of Financial Connections

What makes the Aspiration angle so complicated is the depth of the Clippers' financial involvement with the company. It was not simply a player endorsement deal — Aspiration also held a $300 million, 23-year naming rights deal on the Clippers' arena. The company was deeply woven into the Clippers' financial ecosystem.

More striking still: Clippers owner Steve Ballmer personally invested $60 million in Aspiration. Ballmer has publicly denied having knowledge of Leonard's $28 million endorsement arrangement. But the overlap between a team owner's personal investment, the arena naming rights deal, and a star player's endorsement income has raised the exact kind of questions the NBA's front office is obligated to investigate.

The investigation was originally triggered by journalist Pablo Torre's podcast series last offseason, which laid out the financial connections in detail.

What the Raptors Are Being Told

The NBA's position has put Toronto in an uncomfortable spot. The league has told the Raptors that they can proceed with the trade — but that they would have to "assume the risk" of whatever the investigation ultimately finds. If the Clippers are found to have violated salary cap rules, there could be consequences that affect the trade's validity or the compensation involved.

The Raptors have chosen to wait. In the meantime, the pieces they would be sending out — Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, and a package of first-round picks — remain frozen, leaving Toronto's roster planning in an awkward state heading into the offseason.

The Leonard Side

Kawhi Leonard himself has said little publicly through the process. The trade — sending him back to the Raptors organization that he helped lead to the 2019 championship — was reportedly something Leonard was open to. But without a completed transaction, he remains technically a Clipper while the league does its work.

The NBA has provided no timetable for when the investigation might conclude. Commissioner Adam Silver has indicated he wants the probe wrapped up responsibly, but league investigations of this nature can extend for months.

What's Next

Until the NBA completes its investigation, the Kawhi-to-Toronto trade will remain in a holding pattern. Both franchises are watching closely. If the Clippers are cleared, the deal could complete quickly. If violations are found, the fallout could be significant — for the trade and potentially for the organization.

Hoop Heroes will continue to track this story as it develops. The intersection of sports and financial misconduct investigations rarely moves quickly, but the stakes for both franchises are enormous.
