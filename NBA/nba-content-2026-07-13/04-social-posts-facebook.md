**Long-Form Post:**

Miami is boosted by the Giannis trade, creating an eye-opening LeBron-Giannis-Bam frontcourt possibility. Philadelphia has Rich Paul connections, though Jaylen Brown's arrival complicates the wing picture.

No decision is expected this week. The summer is still young.

### STORY 3: Kawhi Leonard Trade Frozen by NBA Investigation

**Long-Form Post:**
The Kawhi Leonard trade to Toronto is agreed upon — but it cannot be completed, and the reason goes far beyond basketball.

The NBA is investigating whether the LA Clippers violated salary cap rules by compensating Kawhi through a $28 million endorsement deal with Aspiration, a green banking company that has since gone bankrupt. But the connections run even deeper: Aspiration also held a $300 million, 23-year naming rights deal on the Clippers' arena. And Clippers owner Steve Ballmer personally invested $60 million in Aspiration, though he says he had no knowledge of Leonard's deal.

The investigation was originally triggered by journalist Pablo Torre's podcast reporting last offseason. The NBA has told the Raptors they can proceed with the trade but would have to assume the risk of whatever the probe concludes. Toronto is waiting it out. No timetable has been given.

Meanwhile, Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, and a haul of picks sit in limbo.

The Minnesota Timberwolves acquired LaMelo Ball and Josh Green from the Charlotte Hornets in a four-team trade, sending Naz Reid and a substantial draft package back to Charlotte. Julius Randle was also moved to Brooklyn as part of the deal's structure.

LaMelo Ball joining Anthony Edwards creates one of the most electric backcourts in the NBA on paper.
