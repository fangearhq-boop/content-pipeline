Day 24: LeBron James Still Unsigned as NBA Holds Its Breath

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 23, 2026

Twenty-four days after leaving the Los Angeles Lakers, LeBron James remains one of the most consequential free agents in NBA history — still unsigned, still deciding, still making every team in the league wait.

As of Thursday morning, no announcement had come. Rich Paul, LeBron's longtime agent, gave the clearest signal available on the "Game Over" podcast earlier this week: "We're not going to be rushed." No timetable. No hints. Just patience.

The four finalists are unchanged: the Miami Heat, Cleveland Cavaliers, Golden State Warriors, and Philadelphia 76ers. The betting markets on Kalshi have the Heat as heavy favorites at roughly 48%, with the Cavaliers at about 27%, the Warriors at 14%, and the 76ers somewhere in the mix.

The Heat's YouTube Slip

The most tantalizing development of the week came when the Miami Heat's YouTube account briefly published a livestream event titled "LeBron James Introductory Press Conference" — dated July 27. The video was removed quickly. The Heat clarified that it was prepared in advance in case he signed, not a confirmation of anything. But screenshots circulated before the page came down, and the internet did what the internet does.

Whether that July 27 date means anything at all remains unknown. What it did accomplish was briefly putting Miami's fingerprints front and center in one of the most-watched free agency sagas in recent memory.

What Each Team Offers

LeBron has been clear about his priorities: "I want to compete at a high level. I want to join a franchise that shares the same model as myself. That's like practicing championship habits every day."

Miami already has Giannis Antetokounmpo — acquired this summer in the Tyler Herro trade package — and Bam Adebayo anchoring the front court. A LeBron-and-Giannis pairing would be the most star-studded Miami roster since the original Big Three.

Cleveland has Donovan Mitchell locked in on a four-year, $273 million extension, plus Evan Mobley, Jarrett Allen, and James Harden — who declined his $42.3 million option specifically to restructure a deal that could create LeBron cap space. This would be LeBron's third stint in Cleveland.

Golden State has Stephen Curry, a freshly minted Summer League title, and Curry's own direct pitch: "Do you want to play good basketball and be around people that know how to play the game?" Curry and James share Olympic gold — chemistry is established.

Philadelphia has Jaylen Brown (acquired from Boston for Paul George), Joel Embiid, and Tyrese Maxey. Bob Myers, the 76ers' GM, made his case directly on Rich Paul's podcast.

The League Waits Too

Commissioner Adam Silver has publicly applied his own kind of pressure: "I would say this can be wrapped up — and needs to be wrapped up — before next season." As of July 23, the NBA has still not released its 2026-27 schedule. The league is holding the calendar for one player.

It's an unusual situation even by LeBron's standards. But then, few decisions in NBA history have had this much riding on a single signature.

What's Next

The moment LeBron signs, the NBA's offseason calendar begins moving again. A press conference, a jersey reveal, and a wave of roster adjustments from the three teams he doesn't choose will follow immediately. Every GM in the league is watching their inbox.

For now, Day 24 ticks over to Day 25. And the answer still hasn't come.
