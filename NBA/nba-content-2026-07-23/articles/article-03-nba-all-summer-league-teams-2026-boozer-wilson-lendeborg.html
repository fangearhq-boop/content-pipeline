2026 NBA All-Summer League Teams: Lendeborg, Wilson, Boozer Lead the Class

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 23, 2026

With the Las Vegas Summer League officially in the books, NBA.com has announced the 2026 All-Summer League First and Second Teams. These are the names that stood out over 76 games across Thomas & Mack Center and Cox Pavilion — the prospects who will arrive at training camp in October with something already proven.

The 2026 First Team: Cameron Boozer, Brayden Burries, Yaxel Lendeborg, Meleek Thomas, and Caleb Wilson.

Yaxel Lendeborg — The MVP Who Made History

No player in Las Vegas had a bigger summer than Yaxel Lendeborg. The Golden State Warriors' No. 11 overall pick won the Championship MVP after posting 21 points, 10 rebounds, 2 assists, and 2 steals in 23 minutes during Golden State's 94-90 comeback win over the Memphis Grizzlies in the final.

But the most remarkable part of Lendeborg's summer isn't the stat line. He became only the second player in history to win an NCAA championship and an NBA Summer League championship in the same calendar year. The first was Davion Mitchell, who won with Baylor in spring 2021 and then led the Sacramento Kings to the Summer League title later that summer. No one else has done it since — until now.

Warriors fans will want to track how Lendeborg carries this momentum into the regular season. He's the kind of prospect whose Summer League success directly overlaps with his team's current pitch to another famous free agent.

Caleb Wilson — The Record-Setting Debut

Caleb Wilson's 35-point performance in his Summer League debut was the second-highest debut total in the history of the Vegas event. It announced him immediately as a prospect worth watching in a draft class that generated enormous excitement.

Wilson's summer didn't end with a championship — his team fell short — but he earned First Team honors on the strength of consistent scoring and the attention-grabbing opening statement that put him on the radar of every coaching staff evaluating their rosters.

Cameron Boozer — The Championship Game Standout

Cameron Boozer had 19 points and 8 rebounds in the championship game for Memphis — in a loss. The Grizzlies fell to Golden State in a game that came down to the final minutes after the Warriors rallied from 12 down in the fourth quarter. Boozer, the son of former NBA big man Carlos Boozer, showed the interior presence and scoring instincts that made him a high pick.

His All-SL selection caps a summer that demonstrated the Grizzlies made a sound investment in their frontcourt future.

What's Next

Summer League hardware is a first step, not a destination. But for the five players on this First Team, October's training camps arrive with something meaningful already attached to their names. Coaches and front offices pay attention to these results, and the players who performed under the lights in Las Vegas will get the benefit of the doubt when roster decisions get made.

The 2026 NBA regular season tips off in October. These are the names worth bookmarking before then.
