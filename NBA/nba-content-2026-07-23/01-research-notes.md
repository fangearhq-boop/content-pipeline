## STORY 2: Stephen Curry Hall of Fame Exhibit

- Basketball Hall of Fame unveiled "Stephen Curry: Beyond the Arc" — an immersive exhibit honoring Curry while he is still an active NBA player (Source: NBA.com)
- Developed in partnership with Thirty Ink, Curry's business team (Source: NBA.com)
- Exhibit covers his basketball legacy, entrepreneurship, philanthropy, and family life
- This breaks with Hall of Fame tradition: typically the Hall honors retired players — Curry has not yet been inducted (Source: NBA.com)
- Described as a "living-legend recognition" — first time the Hall has done this for an active player in basketball
- Curry has won four NBA championships (2015, 2017, 2018, 2022), two MVP awards (2015, 2016 — unanimous), and revolutionized the game through three-point shooting
- Curry holds the all-time three-point record by a wide margin
- Also in current news context: Curry made his pitch to LeBron with the Olympic gold-medal chemistry angle — "Do you want to play good basketball and be around people that know how to play the game?" (Source: Yahoo Sports)

## STORY 4: Victor Wembanyama Extension Decoded

- Agreement reached July 10, 2026: 5-year, $252 million maximum rookie-scale extension with San Antonio Spurs (Source: HoopsHype, ESPN, Yahoo Sports, SI.com)
- Wembanyama chose the 25% max rate, NOT the 30% supermax escalator
- Supermax would have been approximately $303 million — Wembanyama left roughly $50 million on the table (Source: ESPN)
- Deal includes a player option in Year 5 (Source: HoopsHype)
- Third-largest rookie extension in NBA history by total value — behind Cade Cunningham (Detroit Pistons) and Evan Mobley (Cleveland Cavaliers), both at $269 million (Source: ESPN)
- Wembanyama is 22 years old (Source: Wikipedia)
- San Antonio won 38 games in 2025-26 with Wemby averaging elite numbers — his commitment is a long-term signal for the franchise
- The $50M discount suggests possible CBA preference or roster-building strategy by the Spurs front office

## STORY 5: Kawhi Leonard Investigation

- Investigation approximately 11-12 months old as of July 23 (started September 2025) (Source: CBS Sports, HoopsHype)
- Conducted by law firm Wachtell Lipton on behalf of the NBA (Source: CBS Sports)
- Original allegation: Aspiration Fund Adviser LLC paid Kawhi Leonard a $28M endorsement deal as disguised compensation / cap circumvention — Aspiration has since filed for bankruptcy (Source: CBS Sports)
- Expanded scope (as of July 14-16 reports): now also covers a second, previously undisclosed endorsement deal with an unnamed company + personal expense coverage (Source: HoopsHype July 14, July 16)
- Alex Sanberg (linked to the probe) was sentenced to 14 years in prison (Source: pipeline history)
- Commissioner Silver: investigation "needs to be wrapped up before next season" — but HoopsHype (July 16) reports conclusion "still weeks away" (Source: HoopsHype, NBA.com)
- Clippers-Raptors trade on hold: Toronto to receive Kawhi Leonard in exchange for Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, and multiple draft picks — officially frozen (Source: ESPN, NBA.com)
- Clippers deny wrongdoing; cite Sanberg's fraud
- Kawhi is 35 years old — every month of delay has competitive implications
- Raptors cannot finalize summer plans around a player they may not receive
- Potential punishments include suspension, contract voiding
