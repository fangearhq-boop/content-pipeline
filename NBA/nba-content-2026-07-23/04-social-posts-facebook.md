### STORY 2: Stephen Curry Hall of Fame Exhibit

#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET

Stephen Curry is still playing basketball.

And the Basketball Hall of Fame just gave him his own exhibit.

"Beyond the Arc" opened this week at the Hall in Springfield, Massachusetts. Built in partnership with Curry's business team — Thirty Ink — the immersive exhibit covers his career, his entrepreneurship, his philanthropy, and his family.

Here's what makes this remarkable: the Basketball Hall of Fame does not typically honor active players. Induction and recognition come after retirement, after the career is over, after the legacy is sealed. Curry hasn't retired. He's still running pick-and-rolls and draining contested threes.

But the Hall said, essentially: we can't wait.

The résumé, if you need a reminder: four NBA championships, two MVP awards (the 2016 unanimous), the all-time three-point record by a wide margin. Stephen Curry didn't just succeed in the NBA — he changed how basketball is played, at every level, from the pros down to youth recreation leagues.

Now the Hall of Fame has said it officially, in exhibit form, while he's still lacing up his sneakers.

That's a different kind of honor.

### STORY 4: Wembanyama Extension Decoded

#### Long-Form Post — 4:00 PM ET

Victor Wembanyama left $50 million on the table.

Here's the context: Wemby's 5-year, $252 million extension with the San Antonio Spurs — agreed July 10 — is the third-largest rookie extension in NBA history. It's a massive commitment from one of the most generational talents the league has seen.

But under the current CBA, a player of Wembanyama's stature was eligible for the 30% supermax escalator. That would have pushed the deal to approximately $303 million. Instead, Wemby chose the 25% maximum rate. The difference: roughly $50 million.

Why would he do that?

There are a few credible theories. One: the Spurs may have structured it this way to preserve roster flexibility and build around him properly. Two: Wembanyama, at 22, may genuinely believe in San Antonio's direction and isn't chasing maximum money as his primary signal. Three: the player option in Year 5 gives him a future off-ramp regardless.

Whatever the reason, the message sent is clear: Wembanyama is committed to San Antonio. At age 22, with a generational talent anchoring the franchise, the Spurs now have a defined window to build.

The other number to know: Cade Cunningham and Evan Mobley each signed $269M rookie extensions — both ahead of Wemby by value. Wemby chose to sign for $17M less than either of them.

### STORY 5: Kawhi Leonard Investigation

#### Long-Form Post — 7:00 PM ET

Here is where things stand with the Kawhi Leonard investigation on July 23.

It has been approximately 12 months since the probe began.

The NBA hired law firm Wachtell Lipton to investigate whether the Los Angeles Clippers provided Kawhi Leonard with impermissible benefits — specifically, a $28 million endorsement deal with Aspiration Fund Adviser LLC that functioned as disguised compensation in violation of salary cap rules. Aspiration has since filed for bankruptcy. Alex Sanberg, who was connected to the investigation, has been sentenced to 14 years in prison.

Since then, the probe has grown. HoopsHype reported in mid-July that the scope now includes a second, previously undisclosed endorsement deal with an unnamed company, plus personal expense coverage.

Commissioner Adam Silver has been clear in public statements: this investigation "needs to be wrapped up before next season." But as of this week, HoopsHype reports the conclusion is "still weeks away."

Meanwhile, the Clippers-Raptors trade remains officially on hold. Toronto was set to receive Kawhi Leonard in exchange for Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, and multiple draft picks — a blockbuster haul. But they cannot execute the trade until the investigation closes.

Kawhi Leonard is 35 years old. Every month of limbo erodes the trade's value. And the Raptors are operating this summer without knowing whether their biggest potential move is coming or not.

For a franchise that has been rebuilding since the 2019 championship, this is a particularly painful kind of uncertainty.
