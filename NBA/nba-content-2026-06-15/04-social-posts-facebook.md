### STORY 2: Brunson's $113M Paycut Built a Championship

**Long-Form Post** | 11:00 AM ET
Two years ago, Jalen Brunson made a decision that most star athletes simply don't make.

He left $113 million on the table.

In 2024, Brunson signed a 4-year, $156.5 million extension with the Knicks — a team-friendly deal designed to give New York the salary cap flexibility to build a true championship roster around him. Had he waited a year, he would have been eligible for a 5-year, $269 million max deal. He chose the team over the money.

That decision didn't just pay off. It paid off with a championship ring.

On Saturday night in San Antonio, Brunson scored 45 points — including 15 in the fourth quarter — to close out the Spurs 94-90 in Game 5 of the NBA Finals. He was unanimously named the 2026 NBA Finals MVP, receiving all 11 media votes. And his father, Rick Brunson — a Knicks assistant coach — was right there on the court for the celebration.

A son. A father. A championship.

The money Brunson left behind bought New York depth, flexibility, and ultimately, the team that ended a 53-year drought. The $113 million sacrifice became the best investment in NBA history.

Some decisions make you rich. Some decisions make you a champion.

Jalen Brunson made the right one. 🏆

### STORY 4: Austin Reaves Free Agency

**Long-Form Post** | 4:00 PM ET
The Lakers' offseason could hinge on one decision: can they keep Austin Reaves?

The 28-year-old guard is an unrestricted free agent when free agency opens June 30, and multiple front-office sources expect the Brooklyn Nets to offer a 4-year, $178.5 million max contract to try to pry him away.

But the Lakers have a major advantage. They can offer a 5-year, $239 million deal — nearly $61 million more than anyone else can put on the table.

Reaves is coming off the best season of his career: 23.3 points, 5.5 assists, 4.7 rebounds per game on 49% shooting. He has become the perfect co-star alongside Luka Doncic and has publicly expressed a desire to stay in Los Angeles.

The Atlanta Hawks and Detroit Pistons are also expected to show interest. But with a $61 million gap in their favor, the Lakers are in the driver's seat — as long as they commit to the full five years.

Reaves has earned every dollar. The question is whether the Nets can make LA blink.
