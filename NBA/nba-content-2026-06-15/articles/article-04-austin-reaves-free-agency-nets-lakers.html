Austin Reaves Free Agency: Nets Expected to Offer $178.5M Max as Lakers Fight to Keep Him

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

June 15, 2026

The Los Angeles Lakers have a championship window built around Luka Doncic — and the player who makes that window work is about to become the most coveted unrestricted free agent of the 2026 offseason.

Austin Reaves, 28, is heading into free agency when the market opens June 30, and multiple front-office sources expect the Brooklyn Nets to offer him a four-year, $178.5 million maximum contract. The Lakers' response: a five-year, $239 million deal — nearly $61 million more than Brooklyn can put on the table.

Reaves has publicly said he wants to stay in Los Angeles. The question is whether Brooklyn's offer forces the Lakers to fully commit — or whether other suitors complicate the math.

A Career Year That Changed Everything

The 2025-26 season was the best of Reaves' five-year career by every meaningful measure. He averaged 23.3 points, 5.5 assists, and 4.7 rebounds per game while shooting 49.0 percent from the field and 36.0 percent from three. He became a legitimate No. 2 option alongside Doncic and emerged as a locker room leader — the kind of player teams build around, not around the edges of.

Reaves arrived in the NBA as an undrafted free agent. He has earned everything the hard way. Now he is about to be rewarded for it.

The Lakers' Advantage — and Their Risk

The Lakers hold a structural edge that no competing team can match. As Reaves' current team, they can offer him a fifth year and a higher starting salary — pushing the total value of a max extension to $239 million over five years versus $178.5 million over four that Brooklyn can offer. That is a $60.5 million gap in guaranteed money.

For a player who has expressed loyalty to the Lakers and values the Doncic partnership, that gap could be decisive. But the Atlanta Hawks and Detroit Pistons have also been mentioned as teams expected to show interest, which means Los Angeles cannot take the outcome for granted.

Losing Reaves would be a significant blow. He is not just a complementary piece — he is the engine that makes the Lakers' half-court offense functional alongside Doncic, and his defensive versatility gives head coach JJ Redick options he does not have with any other player on the roster.

What's Next

Free agency opens June 30. The Lakers have an exclusive negotiating window with Reaves before other teams can submit offers — giving them the first, best chance to lock him in before Brooklyn can make its pitch.

Reaves has all the leverage. He has the championship-contending team, the better offer on the table, and a co-star in Doncic that no other franchise can replicate. If the Lakers make the right offer, this could be resolved quickly. If they hesitate, Brooklyn will be ready.
