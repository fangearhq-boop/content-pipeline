Giannis Trade: Heat Still Frontrunner With 8 Days to the June 23 Deadline

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

June 15, 2026

The NBA Finals are finished. Now the offseason's defining drama moves to center stage: where does Giannis Antetokounmpo play next?

As of Sunday, the Miami Heat remain the frontrunner to acquire the two-time MVP, according to insiders Marc Stein and Shams Charania. The Milwaukee Bucks are "comfortable" with what Miami has put on the table — a package centered on Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, and Jaime Jacquez Jr., plus a significant haul of prime draft picks including firsts through 2032. The deadline is the June 23 NBA Draft — now eight days away.

What Miami Is Offering

The Heat's proposed package is one of the richest trade offers the league has seen in years. Herro is a proven 20-point scorer. Kel'el Ware, the second-year center, is one of the most intriguing developmental bigs in the East. Jaime Jacquez Jr. provides two-way versatility. And the draft capital — multiple first-round picks at prime positions — gives Milwaukee the rebuilding runway it needs.

The Bucks co-owner Jimmy Haslam has made no secret of his preferred timeline: resolve the Giannis situation before teams are locked into draft-night decisions on June 23. That urgency has turned the next eight days into one of the most consequential weeks in Bucks franchise history.

Who Else Is in the Picture

The Boston Celtics have maintained interest and remain engaged, according to multiple reports from Bleacher Report. A Celtics package would likely center on Jaylen Brown and draft picks — a different profile than what Miami is offering, but one that gives Giannis a chance to compete for a title immediately.

The Portland Trail Blazers have made a push, though their appeal is less obvious given where they are in their rebuild. The Golden State Warriors, once mentioned as a possible suitor, have dropped out of the sweepstakes entirely.

One complication that has emerged: Giannis has reportedly expressed questions about the roster he would inherit wherever he lands. That concern could influence whether he provides any preference signal to the Bucks before a deal is finalized.

What's Next

Giannis has two years remaining on his current contract and becomes eligible for a four-year, $275 million supermax extension on October 1. That extension eligibility is the leverage point that defines the Bucks' timeline — they want a deal done before draft night so both sides can move forward with clarity.

Eight days. The Heat are in front. The Celtics are watching. And the rest of the Eastern Conference is holding its breath to find out which team just became the new championship contender.
