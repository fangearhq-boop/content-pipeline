Brunson Left $113 Million on the Table. Saturday Night, He Got His Return.

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

June 15, 2026

In the summer of 2024, Jalen Brunson made a decision that defied logic in the modern NBA. He walked away from approximately $113 million.

Instead of waiting until 2025 to sign a five-year, $269 million maximum contract, Brunson signed a four-year, $156.5 million extension with the New York Knicks — a deliberate team-friendly deal designed to give the franchise the salary cap flexibility to surround him with real talent. He accepted less money so the Knicks could be more than a star and a supporting cast.

On Saturday night in San Antonio, Jalen Brunson scored 45 points, won the 2026 NBA Finals MVP award unanimously, and watched his father — Rick Brunson, a Knicks assistant coach — wrap him in an embrace on the floor of Frost Bank Center as confetti fell in orange and blue.

The $113 million paid off. Every single dollar of it.

The Math Behind the Sacrifice

The numbers are stark when laid out directly. A star of Brunson's caliber, coming off his 2023-24 season, would have been eligible for a designated maximum extension of five years and $269 million if he waited until 2025. His agent knew it. The league knew it. Brunson knew it.

He signed anyway — for four years and $156.5 million. The gap between those two contracts: roughly $113 million in guaranteed money, spread across one additional year and a higher annual salary he chose not to pursue.

General manager Leon Rose and team president Steve Mills used that flexibility to build the roster that won the 2026 championship. OG Anunoby, who delivered a clutch tip-in in Game 4 to prevent a Spurs comeback and logged 11 points, 8 rebounds, and 3 steals in the clincher, was part of that roster construction. So was the depth that allowed the Knicks to absorb double-digit deficits in every one of their four series wins and claw back each time.

Finals MVP: 45 Points and No Doubts

Brunson's performance in Game 5 was a summation of everything he is as a player. He shot 14-of-27 from the field and went to work in the fourth quarter — scoring 15 of his 45 points when the margin was thinnest and the pressure was highest.

All 11 media voters named him the Bill Russell Trophy winner. His series averages: 32.6 points, 4.6 assists, and 4.2 rebounds per game across five games against a Spurs team that had Victor Wembanyama playing as well as any player in the Finals since 2016.

Brunson was better. He was better because he had a team, and he had a team because he chose to build one rather than cash in on his own leverage.

What's Next

The Knicks are NBA champions, and Jalen Brunson is their Finals MVP. The parade comes Thursday in the Canyon of Heroes. The ring ceremony will follow in the fall when the new season tips off.

As for the $113 million? Brunson has said nothing about it publicly. He doesn't need to. The ring says everything that needs to be said about whether he made the right call.
