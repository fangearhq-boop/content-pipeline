AJ Dybantsa's Playoff Promise to the Wizards: The Roster Behind the Bold Claim

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

June 15, 2026

AJ Dybantsa visited the Washington Wizards earlier this month and made a promise that most 18-year-old basketball prospects would never dare to make before they had played a single NBA game.

"If I go to DC, I'll make the playoffs," Dybantsa said. "I'm gonna make sure I make it."

It was a bold claim from a player who has not yet been drafted. But when you look at what the Wizards have quietly assembled around their No. 1 pick, the promise does not sound nearly as audacious as it might have a year ago.

A Roster That Already Has Pieces

Washington had an unusually active trade deadline in early 2026. In January, the Wizards acquired Trae Young from the Atlanta Hawks — a legitimate All-Star point guard who averaged over 25 points and 10 assists per game during his peak seasons. At the trade deadline, they added Anthony Davis from the Dallas Mavericks, giving them an elite defensive anchor and interior presence.

Those additions join an existing core of young talent: Alex Sarr, Bilal Coulibaly, and Kyshawn George — players who were selected in recent drafts and have been developing with competitive minutes. The Wizards finished 17-65 this past season, but the roster is meaningfully different from what those results suggest.

Now picture Dybantsa in the mix. The 6-foot-9 BYU prospect averaged 25.5 points per game and shot 51.0 percent from the field in his lone college season. He is listed at -450 odds to go No. 1 overall at the June 23-24 draft at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. If those projections hold, Washington would have a starting lineup of Trae Young, Kyshawn George, Dybantsa, Bilal Coulibaly, and Anthony Davis — a group that has legitimate size, playmaking, and defensive capability.

The First Washington No. 1 Since John Wall

If Dybantsa goes No. 1 to the Wizards, it will be the first time Washington has held the top pick since John Wall went first overall in 2010. That selection began a promising era in Wizards history — Wall and Bradley Beal gave the franchise its best basketball in decades. The hope is that Dybantsa, paired with a more established supporting cast than Wall ever had entering the league, can accelerate that timetable.

Darryn Peterson of Kansas remains in the No. 1 conversation, and the Wizards have reportedly conducted meetings with multiple prospects. But the odds and the insider reporting point overwhelmingly toward Dybantsa landing in DC.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Draft is eight days away — June 23-24 at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. For Dybantsa, the next step is the podium. For the Wizards, it is the beginning of a rebuild that suddenly looks a lot more like a contender in the making.

The playoffs may be a year or two away. But for a franchise that won 17 games last season, Dybantsa's promise is not just bravado. It is a target. And the roster he is walking into gives him a real chance to keep it.
