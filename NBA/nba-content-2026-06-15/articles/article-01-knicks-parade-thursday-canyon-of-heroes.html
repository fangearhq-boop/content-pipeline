Knicks Parade Thursday: Canyon of Heroes Route, Time, and Everything You Need to Know

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

June 15, 2026

New York City is ready to throw the biggest basketball party in 53 years. Mayor Zohran Mamdani has officially confirmed all the details for the New York Knicks' 2026 NBA Championship ticker-tape parade — and it is everything fans have been waiting for.

The celebration takes place this Thursday, June 18. The parade begins at 10:00 AM at Battery Park and travels north along Broadway through the Canyon of Heroes, ending at City Hall Plaza where Mayor Mamdani will host a Key to the City ceremony for the players and coaching staff.

The Route: Broadway's Canyon of Heroes

The Canyon of Heroes has hosted some of the most iconic celebrations in New York City history — from moon landings to Super Bowl champions. On Thursday, for the first time since 1973, it will be covered in blue and orange confetti for the Knicks.

The full route runs from Battery Park at the southern tip of Manhattan, north along Broadway's famous corridor of glass and steel skyscrapers, through the Financial District to City Hall Plaza. City Hall and other municipal buildings across the five boroughs will be illuminated in blue and orange on parade day.

The Key to the City ceremony at City Hall Plaza will follow the parade immediately, giving fans who make it to the end of the route one final celebration alongside their champions.

53 Years in the Making

The last Knicks championship parade was on May 9, 1973. Willis Reed, Walt Frazier, and Earl Monroe led the team to the franchise's second title, and the city went wild. Then came 53 years of nothing.

A majority of New York's current population has never witnessed a Knicks title celebration. For lifelong fans who endured the Patrick Ewing near-misses of the 1990s, the Isiah Thomas mess, the decade of mediocrity and the slow rebuild under Leon Rose — Thursday is the payoff for all of it.

The Knicks defeated the San Antonio Spurs 94-90 in Game 5 on June 13 at Frost Bank Center to win the NBA Finals four games to one. Jalen Brunson scored 45 points in the closeout — 15 in the fourth quarter alone — and was unanimously named Finals MVP. All night in New York City, fans poured into the streets. The NYPD reported 63 arrests citywide, a testament to a celebration that simply could not be contained.

What's Next

Thursday's parade is the official crescendo of an extraordinary championship run. After that, the Knicks will face the work of defending their title — beginning with a critical decision on whether to extend Karl-Anthony Towns, and how to navigate a cap sheet that Brunson himself helped keep manageable with his 2024 team-friendly contract.

For now, though, none of that matters. Set your alarm. Get to Battery Park by 10:00 AM. And welcome to the Canyon of Heroes — the Knicks have finally earned their place on that list.
