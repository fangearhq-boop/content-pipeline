Houston Rockets Clinch Playoff Berth — Sengun's Surge Is Carrying Them In

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 4, 2026

The Houston Rockets are going to the playoffs. After years of rebuilding, developing young talent, and absorbing the growing pains that come with a full-scale roster overhaul, Houston has officially clinched a postseason berth in the Western Conference.

The clinch came this week, and it arrived on the back of one of the most dominant individual stretches produced by any player in the league's second half. Since returning from injury on March 18, Alperen Sengun has averaged 24.9 points, 9.3 rebounds, and 7.1 assists per game while shooting 62.1% from the field across seven games. Those are not rotation-player numbers. They are MVP-candidate numbers sustained over multiple weeks when the games matter most.

Sengun's Emergence

When Houston selected Alperen Sengun in the 2021 draft, the franchise believed they had found a rare interior talent — a big man who could score at multiple levels, pass from the high post, and serve as the foundational piece of a rebuilt franchise. Three years later, that projection is looking conservative.

Sengun's 62.1% field-goal shooting since returning from injury is the product of elite footwork, advanced positioning, and a finishing package that makes him extremely difficult to guard consistently. His 7.1 assists per game over that stretch reflect a passing vision that is genuinely unusual for a center — he reads defenses, creates for shooters, and exploits help rotations with a level of sophistication that typically takes veterans years to develop.

The Rockets have built around Sengun's two-way versatility, surrounding him with spacing and defensive role players who allow him to operate freely. The results speak for themselves.

The Western Context

Houston earns their playoff berth as a top-six seed in the Western Conference — no Play-In required. They face a first-round opponent from a conference where every remaining team is legitimate. Oklahoma City, San Antonio, the Lakers, Denver, and now Houston make the West's bracket as challenging as it's been in years.

The Rockets will not be an easy out. Their depth, physicality inside, and Sengun's peak form entering the postseason give them a genuine chance to win a first-round series against almost any opponent. Teams that came in expecting an easy bracket slot with Houston as their first-round opponent may want to reconsider.

The Rebuild Is Done

Houston's path to this point required patience from an organization and a fan base that watched their team deliberately dismantle itself to rebuild correctly. The franchise endured years of lottery positioning, developmental growing pains, and the kind of uncertainty that tests anyone's faith in a long-term plan. The payoff is here. A playoff berth with a young, ascending core and a center playing at an all-conference level.

This is not a team that has come to simply participate in the postseason. With Sengun this hot, Houston represents exactly the kind of dangerous fifth or sixth seed that disrupts bracket projections and forces top seeds to prepare with full seriousness.

What's Next

Houston will learn their first-round opponent when the regular season concludes April 12. Their seeding will determine whether they face OKC, San Antonio, or the Lakers — three very different matchup challenges. Watch Sengun's final few regular-season games as a form guide heading into Round 1. If he maintains this level of efficiency, the Rockets are capable of upsetting anyone in a seven-game series.
