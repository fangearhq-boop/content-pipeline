Luka Doncic Reaches 15,000 Career Points — Third Youngest in NBA History

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 4, 2026

Add another milestone to the list. Luka Doncic crossed 15,000 career points this week, reportedly becoming the third-youngest player in NBA history to reach the mark — joining LeBron James and Kevin Durant in extraordinarily rare company. For a player who has been rewriting records since his first NBA season, it is another data point in what is shaping up to be one of the great individual careers the league has ever seen.

Doncic has averaged 35.8 points per game over his last 21 contests, with the Los Angeles Lakers going 16-5 in that stretch. The numbers align almost perfectly — when Luka is engaged, efficient, and healthy, the Lakers are among the better teams in the Western Conference. The correlation is direct and consistent.

The Historical Context

Reaching 15,000 career points at Doncic's age requires two things simultaneously: scoring volume and staying healthy enough to play the games. LeBron James and Kevin Durant cleared the milestone in their mid-twenties, each operating as the primary offensive option for playoff-caliber teams. Doncic — who turns 27 later this year — joins them in the conversation that matters: not just great players, but historically great players measured against the standards of all time.

The pace of Doncic's accumulation suggests his career scoring total could be exceptional. He entered the league as a 19-year-old and has been a starter logging heavy minutes since his first season in Dallas. His usage rates have been among the highest in basketball year over year. If he stays healthy through his prime, the scoring ledger will continue to grow in ways that place him in discussions about the all-time leaders.

Lakers' Playoff Positioning

The individual milestone comes at an important team moment. The Los Angeles Lakers are locked in as the No. 3 seed in the Western Conference, having clinched their playoff position with their Pacific Division title. Luka's 16-5 run with the team suggests the Lakers enter the postseason in genuinely strong form — not simply relieved to have qualified, but capable of making a deep run if Doncic stays at this level.

The No. 3 seed sets up a first-round matchup against either the No. 6 seed or a Play-In survivor, depending on how the final standings shake out. Either way, the Lakers will be expected to advance, and Luka's scoring output in the last month of the regular season makes them a team that opponents in any bracket position will take seriously.

What's Next

Doncic will finish the regular season with a career-high scoring average and a milestone that only two players in the history of the sport have reached at his age. The Lakers' first-round playoff matchup will be announced when the regular season concludes April 12. For Doncic, the milestone is noted — and then the focus turns to the only thing remaining that would make the season complete: a deep postseason run.
