Wembanyama Tops the MVP Ladder — Can He Flip the Vote in the Final Week?

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 4, 2026

With one week left in the regular season, the NBA's most compelling debate has reached its crescendo. Victor Wembanyama sits at No. 1 on the NBA.com Kia MVP Ladder. His odds have surged from +1000 to +225. The Spurs have won 11 consecutive games and 23 of their last 25. And yet Shai Gilgeous-Alexander remains the -300 MVP favorite with 88 of 100 first-place media votes.

This is what happens when one player has had a dominant year-long season and another player has produced the most historically unusual individual stretch anyone can remember. The award appears decided on one side of the argument and genuinely open on the other.

What Wemby Has Done

The statistics require no embellishment. Wembanyama posted back-to-back games with 40+ points and 15+ rebounds — becoming the youngest player in NBA history to accomplish the feat at just 22 years old. His Spurs have gone from a fringe postseason team to legitimate Western Conference contenders with a 59-18 record. He has done this across every facet of the game: scoring, rebounding, shot-blocking, and the kind of defensive gravity that forces opposing offenses to build their entire game plan around avoiding him.

Over his last 10 games, Wembanyama averaged 27.9 points and 12.3 rebounds per game. The consistency of his production down the stretch — a stretch when other stars have shown wear from a long season — has given him the closing argument many voters will hear before casting their final ballots.

SGA's Case Hasn't Weakened

None of this diminishes what Gilgeous-Alexander has done. SGA has led the Thunder to the Western Conference's best record at 61-16, averaging 31.6 points, 6.5 assists, and 4.4 rebounds on 55.3% shooting from the field. His efficiency at that volume of usage is genuinely exceptional — he produces like a volume scorer while shooting like a sniper.

SGA has held first-place votes at the top of the ballot across the most recent media polling. Consistency over 77-plus games is what many voters prioritize over a late-season surge. He has played at or near an elite level every month of the season. His team has the best record in the West. The case for SGA is not a close one by traditional standards.

The Final Week Matters

MVP ballots are submitted after the regular season concludes. That means anything that happens in the final week of play — strong performances, additional milestones, team results — can influence how writers who haven't yet submitted their ballots vote. Recency bias is real. So is narrative momentum.

Wembanyama has the narrative and the ladder ranking. SGA has the votes and the favorite's odds. The closing act is set. Both players will perform in high-stakes, meaningful games as the final week plays out. What happens between now and April 12 may not change the outcome — but it will define how this chapter of basketball history is remembered.

What's Next

Watch Wemby's performance in San Antonio's four remaining home games. If he posts two or three more 30+ point efforts while the Spurs chase OKC for the No. 1 seed, his odds will continue to move. Watch SGA's road games this week — if he stays efficient under difficult conditions, his vote count won't move.

The MVP announcement will come shortly after the regular season ends April 12. Regardless of who wins, the 2025-26 race between Wembanyama and Gilgeous-Alexander — two generational players in their prime — will be studied and debated for years to come.
