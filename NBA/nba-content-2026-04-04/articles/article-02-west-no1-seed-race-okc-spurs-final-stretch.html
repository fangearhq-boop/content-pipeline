West No. 1 Seed Race: OKC vs. Spurs — Five Games Left and Nothing Is Settled

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 4, 2026

The Oklahoma City Thunder are 61-16. By any reasonable measure, they should feel comfortable. Instead, the final stretch of the 2025-26 Western Conference regular season has the feel of a genuine race — because the San Antonio Spurs have made sure of it.

San Antonio is 59-18 with five games remaining, trailing Oklahoma City by two in the standings. The gap is two games. The tiebreaker belongs to the Spurs, 4-1 in head-to-head matchups this season. The schedules favor San Antonio. The momentum belongs entirely to the Spurs, who have won 23 of their last 25 games.

The Western Conference No. 1 seed is not decided. Not even close.

The Tiebreaker Changes Everything

The head-to-head record is the most underreported element of this race. If Oklahoma City and San Antonio finish with identical records, the Spurs claim the No. 1 seed by virtue of their 4-1 regular-season advantage. That means the Thunder don't just need to maintain their two-game lead — they need to outperform the Spurs by at least two games net over the final five contests. In basketball terms, that's far from guaranteed.

The scenario is simple: if OKC goes 3-2 and San Antonio goes 4-1, they'd finish tied and the Spurs get the nod. If San Antonio runs the table at 5-0 while OKC goes 2-3, the Spurs take the lead outright. The Thunder need to outrun a team playing the best basketball in the league right now.

Schedule Divergence

Oklahoma City's closing schedule presents real challenges. The Thunder face three road games against playoff-caliber opponents in their next four nights — a stretch that would test any team, let alone one trying to protect a lead in a high-stakes race. Road games in April, against teams fighting for their own positioning, rarely go as planned.

San Antonio, by contrast, closes with four consecutive home games with rest days between each. The Frost Bank Center has been one of the toughest road environments in the West all season. With Wembanyama at peak form and the crowd behind them, the Spurs' home slate is arguably the most favorable closing schedule any contender could ask for.

The Thunder's strength of schedule ranks 12th toughest in the league over their final five; the Spurs' rates 10th. Neither team is playing for seeding against pushovers. But San Antonio has the conditions to win every game if they play their best basketball.

The Stakes

The No. 1 seed carries enormous practical value. Home-court advantage throughout the Western Conference playoffs means Oklahoma City or San Antonio would host every round — critical in a conference this deep, where road wins against elite teams are extraordinarily difficult.

For Oklahoma City, a third consecutive Western Conference No. 1 seed would cement this era as the start of a dynasty. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander's league-leading season demands the hardware that comes with it. For San Antonio, claiming the top seed would validate Wembanyama's MVP campaign and signal that the Spurs' rebuild is complete — not just good, but great.

The final five games of the regular season rarely carry this much collective weight in the Western Conference. Enjoy them while they last.

What's Next

Oklahoma City's road games this week represent the clearest test of whether the Thunder can close. San Antonio tips off their home stand Friday, with the crowd at full playoff-caliber intensity. Track both teams' results nightly — the race could be decided by the weekend, or it may come down to the final day of the regular season on April 12.

For bettors and fans alike: check the tiebreaker column, not just the standings. If you see the teams within one game of each other by April 9, the Spurs effectively hold the lead.
