Stephen Curry Cleared to Return Sunday — Warriors' Playoff Survival Is On

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 4, 2026

The waiting is over. Stephen Curry has been cleared to return to game action Sunday when the Golden State Warriors host the Houston Rockets — his first appearance since January 30, when a persistent right knee injury knocked him out of the lineup for what turned into a 27-game absence.

Head coach Steve Kerr confirmed the news Friday, telling reporters that Curry completed two successful 5-on-5 scrimmages this week and showed no signs of regression. "He looked good," Kerr said. "He looks like Steph Curry." For a fan base that has watched their team stumble without him, those six words carried everything.

The Numbers Don't Lie

The Warriors without Curry are a different team — and the record proves it in black and white. Golden State is 23-16 when Curry takes the floor and 13-25 when he doesn't. That 24-game swing in winning percentage is nearly unmatched for a single player's impact in the modern NBA. Remove Curry and the Warriors are a lottery team. Add him back and they're a legitimate playoff threat.

Golden State finished the regular season at 36-41, locked into the No. 10 seed in the Western Conference. They'll face the LA Clippers in the Play-In Tournament — a one-game situation that, without Curry, would have felt like a formality. With him healthy, the calculus changes entirely.

The Play-In Stakes

The Western Conference Play-In Tournament begins April 14. As the No. 10 seed, the Warriors must win two games to advance to the first round of the playoffs — a brutal format where one loss sends you home. Their first opponent is the LA Clippers, the No. 9 seed.

Curry's return gives the Warriors an ace they haven't had all winter. At 38 years old, he remains one of the most capable offensive players in basketball — a gravitational force who transforms defensive schemes the moment he steps on the court. Curry's shooting and off-ball movement alone forces defenses to cover ground that no other player in the game demands.

The question isn't whether Curry can play Sunday. The question is how quickly rust gives way to rhythm. Two scrimmages tell the coaching staff his body is ready; only real game speed will reveal whether his shot is where it needs to be for the Warriors to make a postseason run.

A Season-Defining Stretch

Golden State has two regular-season games remaining before the Play-In — Sunday vs. Houston and Tuesday vs. Sacramento. For Curry, these aren't meaningless tune-ups. They're the only game reps he'll get before facing elimination pressure. Kerr is expected to manage his minutes carefully in the opener, gradually ramping up his workload before the Play-In begins.

The timing is nearly perfect. Curry returning Sunday gives him two regular-season games to find his footing, then a full week of practice before the Play-In tips off April 14. If the Warriors advance past the Clippers, they'd face the winner of the Suns-Trail Blazers matchup. A healthy Curry would make that second Play-In game equally compelling.

What's Next

Sunday's game vs. Houston (7:00 PM PT, NBC Sports Bay Area) will be the most-watched Warriors regular-season game in months. Curry's return marks the official start of the Warriors' postseason push — and depending on how the next two weeks unfold, it may be the moment the 2025-26 season is remembered for.

Keep an eye on his first quarter minutes (Kerr will likely limit him early), his shot selection from deep (the primary rust indicator), and his defensive engagement (a sign of full physical confidence). If all three look sharp, the Warriors are a team nobody in the West wants to play come April 14.
