Pistons on the Doorstep: Detroit Is One Win From Their First No. 1 East Seed Since 2007

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 4, 2026

Two seasons ago, the Detroit Pistons won 14 games. They were historically bad in ways that felt almost impossible — a franchise with four championship banners reduced to a lottery fixture. Today, their magic number to clinch the No. 1 seed in the Eastern Conference stands at one.

A Pistons win Saturday, or a Boston Celtics loss on Friday, officially seals Detroit's place atop the East. The last time this franchise held the conference's premier seed was 2007 — the Chauncey Billups era, the prime of a team that made five consecutive Eastern Conference Finals. Nearly two decades later, Detroit is there again, built differently, but playing with the same collective identity that made those teams special.

The Turnaround in Numbers

The Pistons lead Boston by 4.5 games with five games remaining. Their record reflects a team that has been dominant for months, not one that stumbled into the top spot by default. Detroit's defense-first approach has been the foundation — a coaching staff that maximizes effort and discipline across a deep roster without relying on a single star carrying the full offensive load.

Jalen Duren has become the anchor the Pistons envisioned when they drafted him. His 22-point, 14-rebound performance in Thursday's 113-108 win over Minnesota — without Cade Cunningham in the lineup — underscored exactly how this team has operated all season: players step up, the system holds, and Detroit finds a way to win. Ausar Thompson, Isaiah Stewart, and Malik Beasley have all contributed in the moments that mattered most.

Cade and the Road Ahead

Cade Cunningham has been out since mid-March with a collapsed lung — a significant injury that could have derailed a lesser team's hopes. The Pistons have responded by going about their business without complaint. The coaching staff never made excuses. The roster absorbed the absence and kept winning.

Cunningham remains day-to-day as the postseason approaches. He is optimistic about returning for the playoffs, and the Pistons are cautious about rushing him back. The calculus is straightforward: with a potential No. 1 seed and home-court advantage throughout the East, there's no reason to risk Cade's health in regular-season games that the team is winning without him.

If Cunningham returns healthy for the first round, the Pistons become a genuinely frightening matchup. Even without him, they've proven they can win high-leverage basketball games against quality opposition.

What the No. 1 Seed Means

Home court throughout the Eastern Conference playoffs is not a small thing. The Pistons would host every round of the East bracket, playing in front of a Detroit crowd that has fully invested in this team's resurrection. In the modern NBA, where road wins in the playoffs are genuinely difficult against elite teams, home advantage can be the difference between a first-round exit and a deep run.

Boston, Cleveland, and New York are all capable of beating anyone in a seven-game series. Detroit will likely face one of them in the first round. Playing those games at home changes the competitive equation significantly.

What's Next

Watch for the clinch scenario to play out Friday night (Celtics game) or Saturday (Pistons game). If Cade Cunningham suits up in the next two weeks, it will be the biggest development of the Pistons' postseason preparations. Regardless, Detroit enters the playoffs as the East's top seed — a sentence that would have seemed impossible 24 months ago. The greatest turnaround in recent NBA history is nearly complete.
