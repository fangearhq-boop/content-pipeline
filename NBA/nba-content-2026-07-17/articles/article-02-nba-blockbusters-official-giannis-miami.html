It's Official: Giannis to Miami, Brown to Philly, and More as Moratorium Ends

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 17, 2026

The NBA free agency moratorium ended Friday, making the summer's biggest moves final and transforming the Eastern Conference landscape.

The waiting is over. The NBA's free agency moratorium has ended, and the summer's blockbuster deals are now officially on the books. In the span of a few weeks, the Eastern Conference was redrawn — and Friday made all of it official.

Giannis Antetokounmpo is a Miami Heat player. Jaylen Brown is a Philadelphia 76er, with Paul George heading to Boston in exchange. LaMelo Ball has become a Minnesota Timberwolf. Ja Morant is officially a Portland Trail Blazer. And Victor Wembanyama is locked into San Antonio on a five-year, $252 million extension.

The NBA looks significantly different than it did on June 30.

Giannis in Miami

The Antetokounmpo trade is the headline move of the summer. The two-time MVP joins a Miami Heat franchise that has long been associated with attracting elite talent. The combination of Giannis' physical dominance and Miami's championship culture creates one of the most compelling storylines heading into the 2026-27 season. The specific terms of the deal made Miami's front office one of the most aggressive in the league this summer.

Brown to Philly, George to Boston

The Jaylen Brown and Paul George swap reshapes two of the East's most interesting franchises. Brown joins Joel Embiid in Philadelphia, giving the 76ers a second high-level scorer who can operate in the half-court alongside their franchise center. George, for his part, returns to a role alongside Jayson Tatum in Boston, reuniting two star wings in pursuit of another championship run.

LaMelo Ball's arrival in Minnesota pairs the guard with Anthony Edwards to create one of the most electric backcourts in the league. The Timberwolves, who have been building steadily, are now a genuine playoff contender.

Wembanyama Stays Home

Victor Wembanyama's extension with the San Antonio Spurs is as significant as any of the trades. The 20-year-old reportedly left approximately $50 million on the table to stay in San Antonio rather than wait for an even larger offer in the future. The deal sends an unambiguous message: Wemby is a Spur, and he is committed to building something in Texas.

It is the kind of franchise-defining moment that San Antonio has seen before — a generational talent choosing loyalty over maximum dollars, betting on himself to elevate the organization around him.

What's Next

The NBA offseason is now in its final stretch. Rosters are taking shape, the secondary free agency market is filling out, and training camps open in September. The one giant question mark remaining? LeBron James, who is still deciding where he will play in 2026-27. His announcement could reshape the Eastern Conference picture all over again — especially if it involves Cleveland, which has been quietly assembling a very impressive supporting cast.
