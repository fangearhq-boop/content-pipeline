Kawhi Leonard Trade Frozen by Two Separate NBA Investigations

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 17, 2026

A second previously undisclosed endorsement deal tied to Alex Sanberg has been added to the NBA's investigation, complicating an already stalled trade and offering no clear timeline for resolution.

The Kawhi Leonard trade is not just stalled — it is now frozen under the weight of two separate NBA investigations. According to a report from HoopsHype, a second previously undisclosed endorsement deal connected to convicted fraudster Alex Sanberg has been added to the league's review, on top of the original Aspiration Sports investigation that has blocked the trade for weeks.

The agreed deal — Kawhi Leonard to the Toronto Raptors in exchange for Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, two first-round picks, two second-round picks, and a pick swap — sits in limbo. Both the Clippers and Raptors want to close it. Commissioner Adam Silver must first conclude both investigations before granting approval.

What's Being Investigated

The original investigation centers on Aspiration Sports, the agency run by Alex Sanberg. The NBA found evidence that Sanberg provided Leonard with impermissible benefits — specifically through a $28 million endorsement deal that was structured in a way that raised league concerns. Sanberg was convicted and received a 14-year prison sentence.

The second deal, now under review, was not previously reported and adds a new layer of complexity to an investigation that was already difficult to resolve. The league must determine whether and to what degree the additional deal affects any potential penalties related to the Clippers organization, and whether that creates risk exposure for any franchise that trades for Leonard.

The Raptors had previously stepped back from risk assumptions related to potential penalties — a stance that has not changed, per reports.

What It Means for Both Teams

For the Clippers, the freeze extends their organizational uncertainty. Los Angeles is in the early stages of a rebuild, and having Kawhi's contract and situation unresolved makes planning significantly harder. The assets they are set to receive — Ingram, Dick, and draft capital — are not officially theirs yet.

For Toronto, the situation is equally complicated. The Raptors are trying to bring back the player who delivered their only championship in 2019, and every passing week without resolution delays their ability to plan around him. Kawhi has one year remaining on his current deal, which means the clock is ticking on how long the uncertainty can last before it affects the trade's viability entirely.

What's Next

There is no public timeline for when the NBA investigation will conclude. Silver has been deliberate in his approach, refusing to move faster than the evidence allows. The addition of a second investigation makes a quick resolution less likely. For Kawhi Leonard, one of the best players in the league when healthy, it is a strange summer — his fate determined not by the court but by the investigation file on Silver's desk.
