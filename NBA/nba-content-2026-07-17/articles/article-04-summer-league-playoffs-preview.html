NBA Summer League Playoffs Begin Saturday — Here's What to Watch

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | July 17, 2026

The top four teams in Las Vegas advance to the semifinals on July 18, with the tournament wrapping up Sunday and the roster implications running deep heading into September camps.

The Las Vegas NBA Summer League is entering its final stretch. After more than a week of group stage basketball, the top four teams will meet in the playoff semifinals on Saturday, July 18, with the championship game to follow on Sunday, July 19. Non-playoff teams are playing their final 5th games today before bowing out of the tournament.

This year's Summer League has offered a richer-than-usual cast of characters. The combination of a deep rookie class, intriguing second-year players, and veterans battling for roster spots created games that felt more meaningful than the exhibition label might suggest.

Names to Watch in the Playoffs

LaBaron Philon Jr. of the Philadelphia 76ers has been one of the standout performers all week, consistently demonstrating the offensive creation and defensive instincts that made him a highly anticipated summer league prospect. If Philadelphia advances, Philon will be the focal point of their attack.

AJ Dybantsa, the Washington Wizards' No. 1 overall pick, was shut down after two electric performances as a precaution, meaning his Summer League story ended early but left a lasting impression. Dybantsa scored 27 points in his debut and followed with 23 more before the Wizards chose to protect their investment — two games that set an expectation bar for his rookie season.

Cameron Carr stepped up for the Los Angeles Lakers on Day 8, leading them to a Battle of Los Angeles win over the Clippers and establishing himself as a name to remember heading into the fall.

What the Playoffs Mean

For the players involved, the Summer League playoffs represent one more chance to make an impression before rosters are finalized. Coaches and front offices are watching closely, and a strong performance in a high-stakes environment can be the difference between a two-way contract and a standard deal.

For fans, the playoffs compress the talent into a single-game elimination format — where every possession matters and the best young prospects get their final showcase before disappearing into training camp preparations.

What's Next

The Summer League championship game is scheduled for Sunday, July 19, which will be the final game of the 2026 Las Vegas event. After that, the next major milestone is training camp in September, when coaches and veterans return and the real roster battles begin. For the players who shined in Vegas, the pressure to carry that performance into the regular season starts now.
