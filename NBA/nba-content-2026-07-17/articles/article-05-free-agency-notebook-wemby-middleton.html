Free Agency Notebook: Wembanyama Locks In, Middleton Finds New Home

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 17, 2026

While LeBron's decision dominates the headlines, the NBA offseason has been busy with meaningful secondary moves that will shape the 2026-27 season.

Every NBA offseason has a headliner and a supporting cast. This summer, LeBron James is the headliner — his free agency decision has consumed the basketball media for weeks. But behind the noise, a number of significant deals have been quietly taking shape, and with the moratorium now over, those moves are officially on the books.

Wembanyama: Commitment Over Maximum Dollars

Victor Wembanyama made the defining financial decision of his young career. The San Antonio Spurs center signed a five-year, $252 million rookie max extension — reportedly leaving approximately $50 million on the table compared to what he could have eventually commanded on the open market.

The choice reflects Wembanyama's stated commitment to building something in San Antonio rather than waiting for free agency. The Spurs, who have not been to the playoffs since the Kawhi Leonard era, are betting that the most unique young player in the league can restore their championship tradition. Wembanyama, for his part, is betting on the organization's ability to build around him.

At 20 years old, Wembanyama's ceiling remains essentially untested. But his decision to stay — and to take slightly less money to stay — is the kind of franchise cornerstone commitment that builds dynasties.

Middleton's Next Chapter

Khris Middleton agreed to a three-year, $17.6 million deal with the Washington Wizards in a sign-and-trade. Middleton, a three-time All-Star who won a championship with Milwaukee in 2021, joins a Wizards team that is in the early stages of building around No. 1 pick AJ Dybantsa.

At 34, Middleton is no longer the high-usage player he was in his prime. But his midrange game, basketball IQ, and championship experience make him exactly the kind of veteran presence a young team needs. Washington is not expecting to contend immediately, but pairing Middleton with Dybantsa gives the rebuild some credibility and a proven winner in the locker room.

Harden and the Cavs

James Harden's situation with Cleveland remains active. He declined his $42.3 million player option for 2026-27, but the move is widely understood to be a restructuring rather than a departure. Harden and the Cavaliers are working toward a new multiyear deal, with the structure designed to give Cleveland financial flexibility — particularly relevant if the team makes a major free agency signing before the deadline.

What's Next

The secondary free agency market will continue to fill out over the next few weeks as teams finalize their rosters. The biggest remaining storyline is LeBron's decision — which, once announced, will trigger a fresh round of adjustments across the league. Until then, front offices are managing their cap space carefully and watching Vegas Summer League closely to identify late-round roster additions for training camp.
