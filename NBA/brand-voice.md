# Hoop Heroes — Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality

If Hoop Heroes were a person, they'd be **your basketball-obsessed friend who watches every game, knows the advanced stats, and has the hottest takes** — the one who fires off a group chat message at 11 PM saying "Jokic just dropped a 30-point triple-double like it was nothing" before you even checked the score. They're passionate, knowledgeable, opinionated (backed by receipts, not just hot air), and genuinely love great basketball. They respect the game and the players, never talk down to casual fans, and cover all 30 teams without hometown bias.

## Voice Attributes

### Knowledgeable
- **We are**: Advanced-stats-aware, historically informed, tactically insightful. We know the game beyond the box score — PER, TS%, Net Rating — and explain it accessibly.
- **We are not**: Gatekeeping, elitist, or drowning readers in numbers without context.
- **This sounds like**: "Shai's 32.5 PER is absurd, but what really separates him is his 64% true shooting — he's efficient from everywhere."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Well, if you actually understood usage-adjusted offensive rating per 100 possessions..."

### Passionate
- **We are**: Genuinely excited about great basketball — a clutch shot, a dominant performance, a rivalry game that delivers.
- **We are not**: Over-the-top, fake-hype, or resorting to empty superlatives every post.
- **This sounds like**: "Wemby just blocked THREE shots in a single possession. The NBA has never seen anything like this kid."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "OMG BEST PLAY EVER!!! NBA IS THE GREATEST THING IN THE WORLD!!!!"

### Opinionated
- **We are**: Willing to take a stance, make predictions, and argue a point — always backed by evidence.
- **We are not**: Reckless, trolling, or controversial for controversy's sake.
- **This sounds like**: "Hot take: the Knicks' defense is fool's gold. They're 2nd in DRTG but haven't faced a healthy top-10 offense since January."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "The Knicks are trash and everyone knows it."

### Fun
- **We are**: Appreciative of highlights, nicknames, rivalries, and the drama that makes the NBA compelling.
- **We are not**: Reducing everything to memes or treating the sport as a joke.
- **This sounds like**: "The Nuggets-Wolves rivalry just hits different. Two MVP-caliber bigs going at each other every possession."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "lmaooo ratio + L + poverty franchise"

### Fair
- **We are**: Full-league coverage across all 30 teams. Small-market wins get the same energy as big-market drama.
- **We are not**: Defaulting to Lakers/Celtics/Warriors every day just because they drive clicks.
- **This sounds like**: "The Pacers are the most fun team nobody's talking about — 118 offensive rating in March."
- **This does NOT sound like**: (silence on every team outside the top 6)

### Respectful
- **We are**: Supportive of players and the game itself. We critique performance, not character.
- **We are not**: Tearing down players personally, body-shaming, or making attacks beyond the court.
- **This sounds like**: "Anthony Edwards went 4-for-19 in a must-win game. That's a tough night — he'll want that one back."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Edwards choked AGAIN. Dude is not that guy."

## Platform-Specific Voice

### X/Twitter
- Enthusiastic, fast-paced, like live game commentary
- ALL CAPS for emphasis on key stats or moments (use sparingly, 1-2 per post)
- Dashes and periods for dramatic pacing: "40 points. 12 assists. Zero turnovers. On the road."
- Strong opinions backed by stats
- Max 3 hashtags per tweet
- #HoopHeroes always included + 2 contextual hashtags (team-specific, event-specific)
- 1-3 emoji max per post (fire, basketball, eyes most common)

### Facebook
- More explanatory — assume the reader needs context
- Narrative storytelling across 3-4 paragraphs
- Opinionated but less aggressive than X
- One emoji per caption OK, no emojis in long-form posts
- No hashtags in Facebook posts
- End with a forward-looking statement or question to drive engagement
- Photo caption voice is punchier than long-form

### Articles
- Professional sports journalism tone
- Accessible to casual fans — explain context, don't assume knowledge
- Stats woven into narrative, not listed in isolation
- Always end with "What's Next" forward-looking section
- No first-person ("I think") — use "the numbers suggest" or just state the take
- Credit sources: "(per Basketball Reference)" or "(via NBA.com/stats)"

## Style Rules

### Grammar & Mechanics

| Rule | Hoop Heroes Standard | Example |
|------|---------------------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes | "scoring, passing, and rebounding" |
| Headings | Title Case | "Top 10 Players Under 25" |
| Contractions | Yes, always | "he's" not "he is" |
| Numbers | Spell out 1-9, numerals 10+ | "three assists" but "15 rebounds" |
| Stats | Always numerals | "32.5 PER", "64.1% TS", "12 APG" |
| Score format | Winner first | "Celtics 112, Lakers 105" not "Lakers 105, Celtics 112" |
| Rankings | No. + number | "No. 1 seed Celtics" — NEVER "#1" (creates a clickable hashtag on X) |
| Time format | 12-hour with timezone | "7:30 PM ET" |
| ALL CAPS | Sparingly for emphasis | "FORTY points on 15 shots" — max 1-2 per post |
| Exclamation marks | OK in social, never in articles | Social: "What a finish!" Article: never |
| Emoji (X) | 1-3 max per post | Common: fire, basketball, eyes, 100 |
| Emoji (Facebook) | 1 max in captions | Never in long-form posts |
| Emoji (Articles) | Never | Articles are emoji-free |

### Terminology

| Use This | Not This | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| three-pointer | trey | "three" or "three-ball" OK in casual X posts |
| the Association | the league | Use "the Association" sparingly for flavor |
| city name + team | just nickname | "the Boston Celtics" or "the Celtics" — not just "Boston" unless context is clear |
| free throw line | charity stripe | "charity stripe" OK in casual X posts |
| assist | dime | "dime" OK in casual X posts |
| double-double / triple-double | DD / TD | Spell out, never abbreviate in posts |
| play-in tournament | play-in | Capitalize "Play-In Tournament" on first mention |
| NBA Finals | the Finals | Capitalize; "NBA Finals" on first mention, then "the Finals" |
| All-Star | allstar, all star | Always hyphenated |
| mid-range | midrange | Hyphenated |
