Jokic Has 31 Triple-Doubles This Season — The Most Statistically Complete Player in the NBA

By Hoop Heroes Staff April 1, 2026

The Quiet Genius at Work

While Shai Gilgeous-Alexander and Victor Wembanyama trade headlines in the MVP race, Nikola Jokic has quietly assembled the most statistically complete season of any player in the NBA. He has now recorded 31 triple-double games — the most by any player in the current season — and since returning from injury on March 18, he has been operating at a level few players in basketball history have ever reached.

Over seven games since his return, Jokic is averaging 26.2 points, 13.4 rebounds, and 9.9 assists. His full-season averages hover near a triple-double, a feat made all the more extraordinary given that he plays center — a position not traditionally associated with elite playmaking.

What the Numbers Actually Mean

Triple-doubles are a benchmark of all-around contribution — they require scoring, physicality, and court vision simultaneously. Jokic achieves them at a rate that puts him in the conversation with Oscar Robertson and Russell Westbrook as the most prolific triple-double producers in NBA history. But what separates Jokic is the efficiency with which he produces those numbers.

His style is deceptively calm. Jokic rarely looks like he is trying — there are no thunderous dunks, no theatrical celebrations, no highlight-reel athleticism by traditional definition. Instead, there is an unmatched feel for the game: anticipating passing lanes before they open, attacking mismatches with the footwork of a guard, and reading defenses as if he already knows what they plan to do three passes ahead.

The Nuggets' Playoff Picture

Denver has clinched a playoff spot and sits fourth in the Western Conference — a position that likely sets up a first-round matchup with the fifth or sixth seed from the opposite bracket. With Jokic playing at this level heading into the postseason, the Nuggets are nobody's idea of an easy out. Murray has been contributing around him, and the team's winning culture from their 2023 championship run still flows through this roster.

Jokic's ranking of No. 3 in most MVP analyses does not diminish what he is doing. It simply reflects a season so extraordinary — with SGA's record streak and Wembanyama's defensive and offensive brilliance — that being third in the MVP race in 2025-26 would be first in virtually any other season.

What's Next

Denver enters the final seven games of the regular season with a playoff berth secure and seeding to protect. Jokic's health and rhythm heading into the postseason are more important than any individual statistical milestone — though 31 triple-doubles in a single season is a number that will stand as a monument to this extraordinary player's 2025-26 campaign. If Denver makes a deep run, Jokic will be the reason. He always is.
