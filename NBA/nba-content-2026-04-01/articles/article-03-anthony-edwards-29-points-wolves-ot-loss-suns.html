Edwards Scores 29 on a Minutes Restriction — But Late Turnovers, Booker's Winner Sink Wolves in OT

By Hoop Heroes Staff April 1, 2026

Brilliant But Not Enough

Anthony Edwards scored 29 points against the Phoenix Suns on Tuesday night — and he did it while playing on a minutes restriction following six games missed with right knee patellofemoral syndrome. The individual performance was electric. The team result was devastating.

Late turnovers in overtime handed Phoenix the momentum, and Devin Booker — who scored 35 points on the night — hit the game-winner as the Suns escaped with a 110-107 overtime victory. The loss drops the Minnesota Timberwolves to a precarious position: half a game ahead of Houston for the West's fifth seed, with every game from here forward carrying playoff seeding implications.

The Context Behind the Limitation

Edwards' minutes restriction is not a precaution taken lightly. After six games away from right knee patellofemoral syndrome — the same diagnosis that has plagued Stephen Curry this season — the Timberwolves are easing their franchise cornerstone back into action. A full return is the goal. A setback would be catastrophic.

The complication is that without a fully unleashed Edwards, Minnesota is vulnerable. Jaden McDaniels is week-to-week with a left knee ailment, robbing the Wolves of one of the league's best defensive wings. Without that combination of Edwards and McDaniels operating at full strength, the team that was blowing out opponents in January and February is a different animal in March and April.

What's at Stake for Edwards Personally

The personal stakes for Edwards are significant. Averaging 29.5 points per game this season — third in the league behind Luka Doncic and SGA — Edwards is in line for All-NBA honors that would represent a landmark in his career development. But he needs to appear in all seven of Minnesota's remaining regular-season games to reach the NBA's 65-game threshold for postseason award eligibility.

That means playing through a minutes restriction, through a knee that is still being managed, while also fighting for playoff seeding. The Wolves and Edwards are threading a narrow needle.

What's Next

Minnesota sits half a game ahead of Houston for fifth in the West with seven games left. A fifth seed keeps the Wolves away from the Thunder and Spurs until the second round of the playoffs — a significant advantage. The next week of basketball will test the Wolves' depth, Edwards' health, and whether this team has enough to compete without McDaniels at full strength. With the right pieces in place, this roster is capable of reaching the conference finals. Getting there starts with winning these final regular-season games.
