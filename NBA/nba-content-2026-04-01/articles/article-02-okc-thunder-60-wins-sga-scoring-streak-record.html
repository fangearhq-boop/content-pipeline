Thunder Hit 60 Wins Again — SGA's NBA-Record Scoring Streak Reaches 128-Plus Games

By Hoop Heroes Staff April 1, 2026

A Dynasty Keeps Building

Back-to-back 60-win seasons. An NBA-record scoring streak. A two-and-a-half-game lead for the Western Conference's top seed. The Oklahoma City Thunder are doing things that only a handful of teams in league history have done — and they are doing it with a quiet, methodical excellence that the rest of the league has not yet found an answer for.

The Thunder's record stands at 60-16 as of April 1, and in their most recent marquee performance, Shai Gilgeous-Alexander delivered 47 points in a 114-110 overtime win over the Detroit Pistons, extending his NBA-record streak of consecutive games with 20 or more points past 128.

The Most Consistent Player in Basketball

SGA's scoring streak is the most underappreciated record in professional sports. Every single night, without exception, he has found a way to reach 20 points — through double teams, through injury scares, through tight defensive schemes designed specifically to stop him. The streak has now stretched across more than a full calendar year of basketball.

His season averages reinforce the narrative: 31.6 points per game, 55.3% shooting from the field, 6.5 assists. He is the reigning MVP and the odds-on favorite to win the award again at -300 — though Victor Wembanyama's recent surge has compressed those numbers from what was once -850. SGA's response to the growing MVP competition has been consistent: more of the same, more efficient, night after night.

The West's Top Seed Is Theirs to Lose

OKC leads San Antonio by 2.5 games with the regular season winding toward its April 12 conclusion. The Spurs — 57-18 and winners of 24 of their last 26 games behind Wembanyama — are the only team with a realistic path to catching the Thunder. But OKC's depth, scheduling, and home-court advantage make them the clear favorites to finish first in the West.

The significance of the No. 1 seed cannot be overstated. Home court throughout the Western Conference Playoffs gives the Thunder a massive advantage, particularly in what projects to be a highly competitive bracket. For a young team that has already won two consecutive postseason appearances, the step from contender to champion runs through this seed.

What's Next

With six games remaining, OKC needs just a handful of wins to clinch the No. 1 seed outright and rest their starters for the Play-In and Playoff stretch. SGA's scoring streak will be one of the season's final compelling storylines to track — each game adds another entry to a record that may stand for decades. The Thunder's destiny appears set: the West's best team, playing their best basketball, heading into a playoff run with everything to prove and no ceiling in sight.
