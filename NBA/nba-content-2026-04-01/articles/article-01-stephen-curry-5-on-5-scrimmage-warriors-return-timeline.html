Curry Participates in 5-on-5 Scrimmage — Return Sunday vs. Houston Reportedly on the Table

By Hoop Heroes Staff April 1, 2026

The Moment Warriors Fans Have Been Waiting For

Stephen Curry stepped onto the practice court Wednesday and did something he had not done since January 30 — he played basketball in a live, competitive setting. The Golden State Warriors star participated in a full 5-on-5 scrimmage, the most significant step in his recovery from patellofemoral pain syndrome, an injury that has kept him sidelined for 26 consecutive games.

Coach Steve Kerr confirmed the scrimmage after practice: "He went through a full practice, but it was very light. We didn't do anything live. He's going to scrimmage right now, five on five. It's a good step for him." The progression from individual workouts to full contact represents the threshold the Warriors medical staff had been watching for before authorizing a return to game action.

What Draymond Green Said

Warriors veteran Draymond Green was characteristically direct when asked about the possibility of a season shutdown for his longtime teammate. "There's no shutdown coming for Steph at the moment. He wants to play," Green said. The quote matters because speculation about a precautionary shutdown had grown louder as Curry missed game after game without a clear return timeline.

Reports now indicate a possible return as soon as Sunday against the Houston Rockets is on the table — contingent on Curry showing no setbacks between now and tip-off. The Warriors coaching staff has not officially confirmed a date, but the scrimmage represents the green light they needed before making any announcement.

The Stakes for Golden State

The Warriors have gone 13-23 without Curry, a record that has dragged their overall mark to 36-39 — the 10th seed in the Western Conference and the last available Play-In spot. They are clinging to that position over a Portland Trail Blazers team at 39-38, meaning a slip of even two games in the wrong direction could knock Golden State out of the postseason picture entirely.

Before his injury, Curry was averaging 27.2 points, 4.8 assists, and 3.5 rebounds per game across 39 appearances, shooting 46.8% from the field and 39.1% from three. Those numbers suggest a player in the prime of his efficiency, and even a partial Curry — one on a minutes restriction for the first few games back — fundamentally changes how opponents must defend the Warriors.

Complicating matters, Golden State is also without Moses Moody (torn patellar tendon, out for the season) and has dealt with various other injury absences throughout the campaign. With seven games remaining, every win has multiplied importance.

What's Next

If Curry returns Sunday against Houston, the Warriors would have six games remaining to cement a Play-In berth or potentially climb into the traditional bracket. The Play-In Tournament runs April 14-17, with the playoffs beginning April 18. A healthy Curry — even for a few games — gives Golden State a puncher's chance against any opponent. The NBA's most dramatic return story of the 2025-26 season is just beginning to unfold.
