From 14-68 to Division Champions: Pistons Clinch Central Title in NBA's Greatest Turnaround

By Hoop Heroes Staff April 1, 2026

The Moment It Became Official

With a 127-116 win over the Toronto Raptors, the Detroit Pistons clinched the Central Division title and cemented one of the most improbable turnaround stories in NBA history. Detroit is now 55-21 — a record that leads the entire Eastern Conference and puts the No. 1 seed within five wins.

Two seasons ago, this same franchise went 14-68, tying the NBA record for most consecutive losses in a single season with a 28-game losing streak. The gap between that low and this championship is not measured in years — it is measured in something more profound: organizational belief, draft precision, and the emergence of a generational talent.

Built the Right Way

The Pistons' rebuild followed the blueprint that winning franchises execute but rarely complete cleanly. Detroit accumulated premium draft picks during its lean years, used them to select Cade Cunningham, and surrounded him with complementary pieces that fit his style of play. The result was a team that improved incrementally, then dramatically, and now stands atop the Eastern Conference with legitimate championship aspirations.

The bittersweet element of this division title is that Cunningham — the architect of Detroit's resurgence, averaging 24.5 points and 9.9 assists this season and an MVP candidate before his injury — is watching from the bench with a collapsed lung. His absence would have buried most teams. The Pistons kept winning anyway, a testament to the depth and culture built around him.

The No. 1 Seed Is Realistic

Detroit holds a four-game lead over the Boston Celtics for the East's top seed, with a magic number of five wins to clinch it outright. Home court throughout the Eastern Conference Playoffs would be a massive advantage for a team that has played exceptionally well in front of its home crowd this season.

The Pistons lead the East at 55-21 — a pace that would represent one of the best single-season turnarounds in NBA history by win differential. From 14-68 to 55-21 across two seasons is a swing of roughly 70 wins, unprecedented in the modern era.

What's Next

With the division title secured, the Pistons will focus on two objectives over the final seven games: clinching the No. 1 seed and getting Cade Cunningham healthy for a potential playoff return. Detroit's regular season ends April 12. If Cunningham can return for the postseason, the Pistons transform from a very good team into a legitimate Finals threat. Even without him, they have proven this season that no obstacle is too large. The greatest turnaround story in recent NBA history is entering its most important chapter.
