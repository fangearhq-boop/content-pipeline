Jokic's 27/12/16 Triple-Double Keeps Nuggets Alive in Game 5

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 28, 2026

When the Denver Nuggets needed their best player to be his absolute best, Nikola Jokic answered in the most Jokic way imaginable. With elimination looming and their season hanging by a thread, the three-time MVP recorded 27 points, 12 rebounds, and 16 assists as the Nuggets defeated the Minnesota Timberwolves 125-113 on Monday night, cutting the series deficit to 3-2.

Sixteen assists in an elimination game. That number deserves its own moment of reflection. While other stars respond to pressure by hunting their own scoring, Jokic plays chess when everyone else is playing checkers. He found cutters, spotted shooters, and unlocked angles that the Wolves had no preparation for. If there was a moment that encapsulated why he remains arguably the most skilled offensive player in NBA history, Monday night was it.

Jamal Murray complemented his All-Star partner with 24 points, giving Denver the two-headed attack that has powered three championship runs in recent years. But the true wild card was Spencer Jones, who stepped in for the injured Aaron Gordon and delivered 20 points along with the energy that sparked a pivotal third-quarter run. When Jones caught fire and Denver began pulling away, you could see the belief return to the Nuggets' bench.

Minnesota's Response Falls Short

Ayo Dosunmu, who had exploded for 43 points in Game 4 in one of the great reserve performances in recent playoff history, was held to a quieter 18 Monday night — still respectable, but not enough to power Minnesota over the top on the road. The Wolves had carried their 3-1 series lead into the game expecting to celebrate an advancement. Instead, they head home for Game 6 knowing the Nuggets are still very much alive and very much dangerous.

The key question hanging over Minnesota heading into Thursday is Anthony Edwards' health. The Wolves' franchise star has been managing a knee injury, and Denver's ability to extend this series likely depends on whether Edwards is at full capacity. If he is, Minnesota's depth and athleticism should be enough to close it out. If not, a Jokic-led Nuggets team on a survival run is capable of anything.

What's Next

Game 6 takes place Thursday in Minneapolis at Target Center, where the Timberwolves hold home-court advantage. Minnesota needs one win. Denver needs two consecutive — one Thursday on the road and then a winner-take-all Game 7 back at Ball Arena if they can survive.

The Nuggets have shown before that they are never truly dead. With Jokic operating at this level, it would be unwise to count them out. But the window is narrow, the margin for error is zero, and the Wolves have their fans at their backs for what should be a raucous closeout attempt. Few games this weekend will match the intensity of Game 6 in Minneapolis.
