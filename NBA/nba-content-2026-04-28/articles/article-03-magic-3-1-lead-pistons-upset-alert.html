8-Seed Magic One Win Away From Eliminating No. 1 Pistons

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 28, 2026

The Orlando Magic beat the Detroit Pistons 94-88 on Monday night, and with that victory the No. 8 seed did something that nearly every basketball analyst said would not happen: they grabbed a 3-1 series lead over the top seed in the Eastern Conference. Orlando is one win away from completing one of the more stunning first-round upsets in recent playoff memory.

Desmond Bane was the steadiest hand on the court for Orlando, finishing with 22 points and five made three-pointers. The veteran shooter made the big shots when they counted, and his calm presence gave the young Magic a blueprint for how to play in tense moments. Paolo Banchero contributed across the board, and the Magic's collective defense made life difficult for Detroit throughout the night.

Cade Cunningham put up 25 points, 9 rebounds, and 6 assists — numbers that look fine until you notice the turnover column. Eight turnovers. In a game decided by six points. Those eight giveaways were not just costly mistakes; they were momentum killers that repeatedly bailed out an Orlando team that was hanging on at various stretches. Tobias Harris added 20 for the Pistons, but Detroit's execution was simply not clean enough against a Magic team that has proved it belongs in this series.

The Giant-Killing Storyline

Detroit finished the regular season at 60-22, the best record in the Eastern Conference. They were expected to cruise through Round 1, begin their championship campaign in earnest, and show the conference that the new era of Pistons basketball had arrived. Instead, they are staring at elimination after four games.

The Magic's path to a 3-1 lead has been built on defense, ball movement, and the kind of unselfish basketball that can make experienced rosters uncomfortable. Orlando does not have a singular superstar who demands game plans — they have five players who can hurt you in different ways, and that collective approach has made Detroit's defensive rotations look confused night after night.

There is historical precedent for teams recovering from 1-3 deficits, but it requires winning three consecutive games, and two of those would need to come on the road in Orlando. The Magic crowd is electric right now. That is not a friendly environment for a team in crisis.

What's Next

Game 5 is Thursday in Orlando, where the Magic will host what could be a historic series-clinching game. If Orlando wins, they become the latest 8-seed to eliminate a No. 1, a result that would reshape the Eastern Conference bracket conversation and send a message to every team remaining in the playoffs.

For Detroit, the challenge is straightforward but difficult: limit turnovers, get Cade going early, and find a way to steal one on the road before bringing the series back to Little Caesars Arena. The Pistons have the talent to do it. The question is whether they have the composure. With their season on the line Thursday night, they will find out quickly what this group is made of.
