Playoff Week Ahead: Lakers, Cavs, Magic, Nuggets All in Action Wednesday-Thursday

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 28, 2026

The Oklahoma City Thunder are through. Every other team still standing in Round 1 has something left to prove. As Tuesday night's three-game slate unfolds, the rest of the 2026 playoffs schedule takes shape with four more critical games arriving on Wednesday and Thursday that could bring the first round to a dramatic close.

Wednesday: Two Series on the Brink

The Los Angeles Lakers host the Houston Rockets in Game 5 on Wednesday at Crypto.com Arena, with Los Angeles holding a 3-1 series lead. LeBron James has been the defining force in this series despite playing without Luka Doncic (hamstring) and Austin Reaves (oblique), and a home closeout would send the Lakers to Round 2 for the first time since their depleted squad surprised the league. Kevin Durant has been sidelined by a left ankle sprain for Houston, and the Rockets will need their full collective effort — led by Amen Thompson, Alperen Sengun, and the veteran presence of the rest of the roster — to steal a win in Los Angeles.

Also Wednesday, the Cleveland Cavaliers host the Toronto Raptors in Game 5 of a series that is tied 2-2. Cleveland holds home-court advantage for this one, with the series split evenly after Scottie Barnes and Brandon Ingram both played brilliantly in Game 4 to level things up in Toronto. Donovan Mitchell's ability to take over in the fourth quarter has been Cleveland's trump card throughout the series, and a win at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse would put the Cavs in the driver's seat to advance for the first time this round.

Thursday: History Possible in Orlando, Survival in Minneapolis

Thursday's doubleheader features two of the most compelling remaining storylines in Round 1. In Orlando, the Magic host the Pistons for Game 5 with Orlando leading 3-1 and on the verge of a historic upset. An 8-seed eliminating a No. 1 seed is always a seismic moment in the NBA, and if Desmond Bane and company can close it out at home in front of a raucous crowd, this Magic team will announce itself as a genuine Eastern Conference dark horse for years to come.

Meanwhile in Minneapolis, the Timberwolves host the Nuggets in Game 6 (MIN leads 3-2) after Nikola Jokic's 27/12/16 masterpiece kept Denver alive Monday night. Target Center will be deafening as Minnesota tries to finish off a championship contender. Jokic's performance showed that Denver is never truly out of a series, but winning in Minneapolis — especially if Anthony Edwards is healthy — is a different challenge than playing in Denver. The Nuggets will need their best basketball to survive and force a Game 7.

What's Next

By Thursday night, the 2026 NBA Playoffs first round could be largely decided. With the Thunder already through and several series nearing their conclusions, the field for Round 2 is coming into focus. The Western Conference semifinals are shaping up to be fiercely competitive — OKC awaiting the Lakers-Rockets winner, Spurs potentially facing Nuggets or Wolves depending on how things shake out. In the East, Celtics, Knicks, and the Magic's potential upset run are the stories everyone will be tracking. Round 1 is almost done. Round 2 cannot get here soon enough.
