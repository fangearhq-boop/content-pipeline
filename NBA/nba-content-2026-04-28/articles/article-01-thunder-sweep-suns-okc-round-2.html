Thunder Sweep Suns, Become First Team Into Round 2

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 28, 2026

The Oklahoma City Thunder completed their first-round sweep of the Phoenix Suns on Monday night, winning 131-122 to advance to the Western Conference Semifinals as the first team to punch their ticket in the 2026 NBA Playoffs. The performance was a masterclass in balanced, team-driven basketball — the kind of statement that should put every remaining contender on notice.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander delivered a characteristically brilliant 31 points and 8 assists in the closeout, orchestrating OKC's offense with the poise of a player who has done this before. But what made Game 4 special was the supporting cast rising to the occasion. Chet Holmgren was exceptional, finishing with 24 points on 9-of-16 shooting and 12 rebounds — a double-double that underscored his emergence as one of the most versatile bigs in the league. Center Isaiah Hartenstein did the unglamorous work, recording 18 points and 12 boards including 7 offensive rebounds, giving OKC valuable extra possessions when they needed them most.

The Thunder also made the Suns pay from distance, knocking down 17 of 34 three-point attempts for an even 50 percent clip. In a closeout game, that kind of efficiency is ruthless. Phoenix simply had no answer for OKC's spacing and movement, and without Jalen Williams — who has been sidelined by a hamstring injury — the Thunder showed a depth and cohesion that hints at something special.

A Dynasty Pattern Takes Shape

With the series win, Oklahoma City is now 12-0 in the first round over the last three seasons. That is not a coincidence. That is a culture. Coach Mark Daigneault has built an organization that treats April basketball as an opportunity, not an obstacle, and the players respond accordingly. SGA's back-to-back MVP campaign was defined by regular-season dominance, but these playoffs are where legacies get written.

Jalen Green led the Suns with 23 points in their final game, and Phoenix showed flashes throughout the series of what their young roster could become. But they were outmatched in every phase against the defending champions, and the margin between these two franchises is significant right now. The Suns will spend the offseason reassessing.

What's Next

The Thunder now wait for their Round 2 opponent. The Los Angeles Lakers lead the Houston Rockets 3-1 heading into Game 5 on Wednesday in Los Angeles. If the Lakers close it out, OKC will face a LeBron James-led squad depleted by the absence of Luka Doncic (hamstring) and Austin Reaves (oblique) — a matchup Thunder fans would relish. Should the Rockets somehow extend the series and pull off a comeback, OKC would instead face an inspired Houston squad riding momentum.

Either way, the Thunder are rested and ready. In a deep, competitive Western Conference, being the first team through to Round 2 is a genuine advantage. With SGA healthy and the supporting cast firing, Oklahoma City has the look of a team built to go all the way.
