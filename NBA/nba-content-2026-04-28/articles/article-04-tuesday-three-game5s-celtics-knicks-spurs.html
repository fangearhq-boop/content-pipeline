Three Game 5s Tuesday Night: Celtics, Knicks and Spurs All in Action

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 28, 2026

Tuesday delivers one of the great single-night slates of Round 1, with three Game 5s packed into a primetime window that stretches from 7 PM to midnight. Two teams can clinch. One series — the most evenly matched of the bunch — tips at the most iconic venue in basketball. Here is what you need to know heading into a pivotal Tuesday night.

Celtics-76ers, 7 PM ET (ESPN) — Boston Can Close It Out

The Boston Celtics enter Game 5 with a 3-1 series lead and the chance to advance on their home floor at TD Garden. After Payton Pritchard's remarkable 32-point bench performance in the Game 4 blowout, the Celtics carry significant momentum. Jayson Tatum added 30 in that game, and Boston's franchise-record 24-of-53 shooting from three in Game 4 demonstrated just how unguardable this offense can be when it is flowing.

Joel Embiid returned from his appendectomy in Game 4 and posted 26 points, 10 rebounds, and 6 assists in 34 minutes — a remarkable effort under the circumstances that was not enough to prevent the blowout. Whether Embiid's conditioning holds up for 40-plus minutes in a must-win Game 5 is the central question for Philadelphia. If the former MVP is at anything close to full strength, this series is not over. If he is running on fumes, the Celtics will end it quickly in Boston.

Knicks-Hawks at MSG, 8 PM ET (NBC) — Series Tied 2-2

Madison Square Garden hosts Game 5 of a back-and-forth series that has refused to give either team more than a one-game cushion. The New York Knicks won Game 4 in Atlanta with Karl-Anthony Towns recording a 20/10/10 triple-double and Julius Randle contributing alongside OG Anunoby's 22/10 performance. The Knicks won that game with defense and physicality — and now they bring that same approach to MSG, where the crowd will be at maximum volume.

For the Atlanta Hawks, CJ McCollum has been the heartbeat of their postseason run. His 32-point game-winner in Game 3 at MSG had the series tilted Atlanta's way before the Knicks responded. McCollum is averaging nearly 19 points per game in this series, and Jalen Johnson has been dangerous when his motor is running at full speed. But road wins at the Garden in a tied series are never easy, and Atlanta will need their best collective effort to escape New York with a victory.

Spurs-Blazers, 9:30 PM ET (ESPN) — San Antonio Can Advance

The San Antonio Spurs enter Game 5 holding a 3-1 series lead over the Portland Trail Blazers, and with Victor Wembanyama healthy and dominant — 27 points, 11 rebounds, 7 blocks in his concussion return in Game 4 — they are heavy favorites to clinch tonight on their home floor. A series win would mark San Antonio's first playoff series victory since 2017 and the first for nearly the entire current roster, including coach Mitch Johnson.

Portland needs a road win in San Antonio to stay alive, and the Trail Blazers have shown they can compete in this series. Anfernee Simons and Scoot Henderson have moments of brilliance, and the Blazers are not built to go quietly. But Wemby's ability to change possessions on both ends — his seven-block game in Game 4 was a reminder of just how unique a defender he is — makes this an incredibly difficult assignment for a Portland team that needs to execute perfectly to extend the series.

What's Next

If the Celtics and Spurs both close out their series Tuesday, the East and West brackets become significantly clearer. Boston would await a Knicks-Hawks winner in the semifinals, while San Antonio would face whoever survives Nuggets-Wolves in the West.

The night will be long, the games will be intense, and multiple seasons could end before midnight. That is playoff basketball at its finest.
