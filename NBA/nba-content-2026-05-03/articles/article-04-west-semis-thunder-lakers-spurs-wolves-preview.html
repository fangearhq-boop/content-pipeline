West Semis Set: Thunder-Lakers and Spurs-Wolves Deliver Star Power, Drama in Round 2

By Jake Torres | May 3, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Western Conference second round is officially set, and the matchups that have emerged are as compelling as anyone could have drawn up. Two series involving some of the most recognizable names in basketball are about to collide — and both promise to be worth staying up late for.

The No. 1 Oklahoma City Thunder face the No. 4 Los Angeles Lakers. The No. 2 San Antonio Spurs face the No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves. If you're a basketball fan, your calendar is already blocked off.

Thunder vs. Lakers: The Marquee Matchup

This is the series that has been building all spring. Game 1 is Tuesday night in Oklahoma City, where Shai Gilgeous-Alexander and the Thunder — the team that swept the Phoenix Suns out of the first round without breaking a sweat — will host LeBron James and a Lakers team that just dispatched the Rockets in six games.

Oklahoma City has looked like the best team in basketball for stretches of this season and postseason. Gilgeous-Alexander is playing at an MVP level, surrounded by Chet Holmgren (24 points and 12 rebounds in the series-clinching sweep game against Phoenix) and Isaiah Hartenstein, who has been a menace on the offensive glass. The Thunder went 12-0 in first-round series over three straight seasons before this one — a run of dominance that speaks for itself.

The Lakers counter with LeBron, who put up 28 points, 8 assists, and 7 rebounds in the Game 6 clincher against Houston, and a fully healthy Austin Reaves back from a three-week absence with an oblique injury. Deandre Ayton was a force on the boards throughout the first round. Los Angeles has the veteran experience and star power to compete with anyone — but Oklahoma City is hungry, young, and as deep as any team in the conference.

Spurs vs. Wolves: The Rising Stars Clash

The other Western semifinal matchup may ultimately be the better series. San Antonio eliminated Portland to set up a showdown with a Timberwolves team that knocked out the defending champion Denver Nuggets without Anthony Edwards for their final two wins — an extraordinary achievement that speaks to Minnesota's depth.

Victor Wembanyama has been the most physically dominant player in his position since his rookie year, and the Spurs have built a system around his ability to protect the rim, score from anywhere, and control defensive possessions. If Edwards can return from the left knee bone bruise and hyperextension he suffered in Game 4 against Denver, Minnesota has the star power to challenge any team in the bracket.

If Edwards cannot play at full capacity, this becomes an extremely difficult task for the Wolves. Their depth was impressive against Denver, but sustaining that level without their No. 1 option against a team as well-constructed as the Spurs is a different challenge entirely.

What's Next

Game 1 of Thunder-Lakers tips off Tuesday in Oklahoma City. The Spurs-Wolves schedule will follow shortly. Both series carry enormous stakes — for LeBron, whose championship window narrows each year; for SGA, who is building toward his first conference finals appearance; and for Wembanyama, who has already begun to look like the generational center his pre-draft hype always promised.

The Western Conference second round is going to be spectacular. Block off your evenings accordingly.
