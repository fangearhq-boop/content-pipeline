Cavaliers vs. Raptors Game 7 Preview: Home Team Has Won Every Game — Will Cleveland Close It Out Tonight?

By Damon Pierce | May 3, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

In the six games played between the Cleveland Cavaliers and the Toronto Raptors this first round, one pattern has been absolutely consistent: the home team wins. Every single time. The Cavaliers have not lost at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse. The Raptors have not lost at Scotiabank Arena. Both teams own their buildings.

Tonight, Game 7 goes back to Cleveland. That pattern says the Cavaliers win. But RJ Barrett made NBA history just 48 hours ago, and the Raptors have proven they don't follow patterns.

Game 7 tips off at 7:30 PM ET on NBC. It is the most unpredictable game of this entire first round.

Barrett's Historic Shot Changed Everything

The setup going into Game 6 was straightforward: the Cavaliers led the series 3-2, they had won Game 5 in Toronto to take control, and they were heading home with a chance to close out the series. Cleveland had every advantage. Then RJ Barrett happened.

With 1.2 seconds remaining in overtime and the Raptors trailing by one, Barrett launched a 29-foot three-pointer that bounced off the back of the rim and fell through. Toronto won 112-110. The series was tied at three. And Barrett became the first player in NBA history to hit a game-winner in the final two seconds of overtime to avoid playoff elimination.

Scottie Barnes was brilliant throughout Game 6, finishing with 25 points and 14 assists in a performance that showcased exactly why Toronto believes in his star potential. James Harden quietly ran the offense with precision: 16 points, 9 rebounds, and 9 assists — veteran leadership that kept the Raptors organized when the moment demanded it most.

Cleveland's Counter-Argument

For all of Toronto's drama and resilience, the Cavaliers have a strong case of their own. Evan Mobley put up 26 points and 14 rebounds in Game 6 — a performance that would have won most playoff games — and still came out on the losing end of Barrett's miracle shot. Donovan Mitchell added 24 points and has been the consistent offensive engine Cleveland needs him to be throughout this series.

The Cavaliers are 8.5-point favorites tonight, and that line reflects real basketball logic. The home pattern is genuine. Cleveland won Games 1 and 2 at home to open the series, won Game 5 in Toronto in the anomaly that gave them control of the series, and their fanbase has been one of the most energetic in this postseason. Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse tonight will be as loud as any building in basketball.

The Raptors Are Playing With House Money

Toronto entered this series as a team that few analysts expected to advance. They pushed it to seven games. They forced overtime on the road. They have a player in Barrett who has already made history once in this series, and a player in Barnes who is developing into one of the most complete two-way contributors in the Eastern Conference.

If the Raptors win tonight in Cleveland, it will stand as one of the great first-round upsets of the past decade. The home pattern will be broken. And Barrett's name will be mentioned in the same breath as legendary clutch performers.

If the Cavaliers win — which the numbers, the history, and the home-court advantage all suggest they will — then Mitchell, Mobley, and a deep Cleveland roster head into the second round with momentum and validation.

What's Next

The winner tonight faces either the Detroit Pistons or the Orlando Magic in the Eastern Conference Semifinals, depending on the outcome of the afternoon's Game 7 at Little Caesars Arena. Either way, they will be playing meaningful basketball well into May.

Game 7. Cleveland. 7:30 PM ET on NBC. One of the most entertaining first-round series in recent playoff memory comes to its conclusion tonight — and if the last six games have taught us anything, it's that nothing about this matchup is predictable.
