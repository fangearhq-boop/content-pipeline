76ers vs. Knicks Is Set — Embiid Heads to MSG in the East's Marquee Second-Round Matchup

By Marcus Cole | May 3, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Joel Embiid is going to Madison Square Garden, and that is the kind of basketball sentence that makes the rest of the second round feel even more important.

After completing their remarkable comeback from a 3-1 series deficit against the Boston Celtics on Saturday night, the Philadelphia 76ers have been confirmed as the opponent for the No. 3 New York Knicks in the Eastern Conference Semifinals. One of the East's most anticipated second-round matchups is officially on — and based on what Embiid just did in Boston, the Knicks have reason to be wary.

Philadelphia Arrives With Momentum and Muscle

The 76ers didn't just advance — they advanced in the most impressive way possible. Down three games to one against the Celtics, they won three straight, including a Game 7 on the road at TD Garden where Embiid delivered 34 points despite playing through post-appendectomy recovery. Tyrese Maxey added 30 points to close out the game, and Philadelphia emerged as one of the hottest teams in the East heading into the second round.

That psychological edge matters. Teams that come back from 3-1 deficits carry a sense of collective belief that is difficult to manufacture and even harder to break. The 76ers now know, in a way they didn't before this series, that they can survive and advance through virtually any adversity.

New York Has Been Dominant and Rested

The Knicks had one of the most lopsided first rounds in recent memory. Their 4-2 series win over the Atlanta Hawks included a record-setting Game 6 performance in which they took a 47-point halftime lead — the largest halftime margin in NBA playoff history. OG Anunoby delivered 29 points in that game, including 26 in the first half alone. Karl-Anthony Towns contributed a triple-double, and Mikal Bridges finished with 24 points on near-perfect shooting.

New York is rested, confident, and playing at Madison Square Garden. That building has a way of elevating the Knicks and rattling opponents in equal measure. For Embiid, who has delivered some of his most memorable performances on the biggest stages, the Garden crowd will be more of a challenge than a welcome.

The Matchup to Watch

Embiid against whatever defensive coverage New York throws at him is the central question of this series. The Knicks have multiple capable defenders and a depth that allows Tom Thibodeau to rotate fresh bodies at the 76ers' center. But Embiid just showed in Boston that he can deliver at a superstar level even when physically compromised — and a week of rest before Game 1 will only help his recovery.

Maxey versus the Knicks' backcourt is another compelling subplot. He has been one of the most reliable closers in this postseason, and New York will need to find an answer for his ability to create late in games.

What's Next

The schedule for the 76ers-Knicks East Semifinal has not yet been announced. The other side of the East bracket is being determined today by two Game 7s: Pistons at home vs. Magic at 3:30 PM ET, and Cavaliers at home vs. Raptors at 7:30 PM ET.

Whatever happens in those games, the 76ers have earned their place in the second round by demonstrating the kind of mental toughness that playoff basketball ultimately rewards. Embiid is going to Madison Square Garden. The East Semis are going to be must-watch basketball.
