Pistons vs. Magic Game 7 Preview: Can Cade Finish It at Home After the Most Dramatic Comeback of the Round?

By Marcus Cole | May 3, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Detroit Pistons are the No. 1 seed in the Eastern Conference. They have home court. They have Cade Cunningham, who just put together one of the most electrifying fourth-quarter performances of this playoff round. And they just survived one of the most historically dramatic collapse-and-comeback sequences in recent postseason memory.

All they have to do now is close it out.

Game 7 between the Pistons and the Orlando Magic tips off at 3:30 PM ET on ABC at Little Caesars Arena — and the stakes could not be more straightforward. Win and Detroit validates their season. Lose and the No. 1 seed goes home to one of the most stinging early exits in franchise history.

Cade's Comeback Performance Set the Stage

Let's revisit what happened Friday night in Orlando, because it still seems almost unreal in retrospect.

The Pistons trailed 62-38 early in the third quarter — a 24-point deficit that looked like a knockout blow for a team already down 3-2 in the series. Then Cade Cunningham did something that hasn't been done in Detroit in a very long time. He scored 19 of his 32 points in the fourth quarter alone, orchestrating a furious rally as the Magic suddenly and completely forgot how to shoot.

Orlando missed 23 consecutive shots in a stretch that represented the longest such drought by any team in a playoff game during the play-by-play era. The Pistons outscored the Magic 55-19 in the second half. A 24-point hole became a 14-point victory. The series came back to Detroit.

Home Court Has Meant Everything

Detroit went into Orlando for Game 6 and stole it. Now they come home for Game 7, and the home-court advantage that should have made this series easy in the first place has one final chance to pay off.

Cade Cunningham became the first Pistons player since Isiah Thomas to record at least 30 points and 10 rebounds in an elimination game. That is the kind of historical company that resonates in Detroit — a city that has always measured its basketball identity against the legend of the Bad Boys era. What Cade did Friday was worthy of that legacy.

Tobias Harris (22 points in Game 6) gives Detroit a reliable secondary option, and the team's defensive intensity that held Orlando to historic shooting futility suggests their defensive motor is running at full capacity heading into this decider.

Can Orlando Recover?

The Magic have been the great overachievers of this first round. As the No. 8 seed, they were not expected to push Detroit to this point. Paolo Banchero has played brilliantly in stretches — his 45-point performance in Game 5 remains one of the best individual playoff games by any player in this postseason. Franz Wagner and the supporting cast have contributed consistently.

But Game 6 in Orlando was a psychological gut punch. Missing 23 consecutive shots doesn't just happen on a neutral day — it reflects a team that lost its composure under pressure. The question for the Magic is whether they can shake that off in a hostile environment in just 48 hours.

What's Next

The winner of today's game faces the survivor of tonight's Cavaliers-Raptors Game 7 in the Eastern Conference Semifinals. For the Pistons, it would represent their first second-round appearance in years and a confirmation that Cade Cunningham is ready to carry a playoff team into May.

For the Magic, an upset victory today would be one of the most remarkable first-round wins in recent memory — a No. 8 seed knocking out the top-seeded Pistons after coming back from the Game 6 nightmare. Basketball has no script. Tune in at 3:30 PM ET to see which story gets written.
