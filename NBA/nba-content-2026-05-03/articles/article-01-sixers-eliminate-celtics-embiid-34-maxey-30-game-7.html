76ers Eliminate Celtics 109-100 in Game 7 — Embiid 34, Maxey 30, Historic 3-1 Comeback Complete

By Jake Torres | May 3, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Joel Embiid walked into TD Garden on Saturday night with a surgically repaired abdomen and a probable tag on his injury report. He walked out with 34 points, a playoff series victory, and a permanent place in the conversation about the most gutsy performances in recent NBA postseason history.

The Philadelphia 76ers eliminated the Boston Celtics 109-100 in a winner-take-all Game 7, completing one of the more stunning first-round reversals the Eastern Conference has seen in years. Three weeks ago, Philadelphia was down three games to one and seemingly on the brink of collapse. On Saturday, they stood in the middle of Boston's home floor and celebrated.

Embiid Silences Every Doubt

It had been less than a month since Embiid underwent an appendectomy on April 9. He was listed as probable before tip-off, and there were genuine questions about whether he could deliver at the level this moment required. He answered those questions quickly and emphatically.

Embiid's 34 points weren't just a statistical achievement — they were a statement. He kept Boston honest on the interior, drew foul after foul, and refused to let the Celtics settle into any defensive rhythm that might have slowed Philadelphia's attack. For a player who had been managing pain and limited practice time for weeks, the performance was extraordinary.

Maxey Closes the Door

If Embiid was the engine that kept the 76ers competitive, Tyrese Maxey was the closer who finished the job. His 30 points included clutch baskets down the stretch as Boston made one final push to stay alive, and his ability to create his own shot under pressure proved to be the difference that sent the Celtics home.

Maxey entered Game 7 listed as probable himself with a right tendon finger strain — and like Embiid, he played through it as if nothing were wrong. The Celtics had no reliable answer for Philadelphia's one-two punch, and by the time the final buzzer sounded at TD Garden, the 76ers had finished what looked like an impossible comeback.

Boston's Shooting Woes Prove Fatal

The Celtics had been 8.5-point favorites heading into Game 7, and they had home court, a raucous crowd, and a roster that had looked sharp for stretches of this series. But the shooting that had carried them to victories earlier in the postseason deserted them when they needed it most, and Philadelphia's defensive pressure gave Boston no easy looks in the moments that mattered.

Jayson Tatum had returned to the injury report in Game 6 with a left calf issue but was fully cleared and available — yet Boston as a team could not find the offensive consistency that would have required Philadelphia to play their very best. The 76ers took full advantage of every opportunity the Celtics gave them.

What's Next

Philadelphia now faces the No. 3 New York Knicks in the Eastern Conference Semifinals. The Knicks had one of the most dominant first rounds in recent memory, and Madison Square Garden will be a hostile environment for any opponent. But the 76ers just showed they can come back from any deficit and deliver in the most pressure-packed moments imaginable.

Embiid heading to New York is appointment basketball. The schedule for Game 1 has not yet been announced, but the matchup is set — and based on what Embiid did on Saturday night in Boston, opposing fan bases should be very concerned.
