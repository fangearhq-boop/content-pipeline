AJ Dybantsa Signs Rookie Deal with Washington Wizards — No. 1 Pick's Journey Begins

By Marcus Cole July 3, 2026 Hoop Heroes

The Washington Wizards took the first step toward a new era on July 2 when AJ Dybantsa, the No. 1 overall pick in the 2026 NBA Draft, signed his rookie scale contract. The deal is four years and $66.91 million — standard rookie scale compensation for the top selection in the draft — and it officially makes Dybantsa a Washington Wizard. The 6-foot-8.5 forward from BYU is the consensus best prospect in his class, and the Wizards are betting everything on him becoming the cornerstone of their franchise for years to come.

Dybantsa arrives in Washington as part of a dramatic organizational overhaul. The Wizards also signed veteran star Trae Young to a 4-year, $212 million deal, giving the organization two new building blocks in a matter of days. One is a proven offensive force who can generate points and playmaking opportunities at an elite level. The other is the most heralded young prospect to enter the draft in recent memory. Together, they give Washington something it has not had in a long time: genuine reasons for optimism.

Who Is AJ Dybantsa?

Dybantsa came to BYU as the most anticipated college freshman in years. At 6 feet 8.5 inches, he possesses the frame of a modern NBA forward — long, athletic, and versatile enough to guard multiple positions while creating offense in a variety of ways. He can put the ball on the floor and attack closeouts, shoot with confidence from the perimeter, and make plays in traffic that demonstrate a level of physical and basketball intelligence well beyond his years.

His college production at BYU generated the kind of excitement that builds around players who appear to be inevitable. NBA scouts who tracked him throughout the season noted his competitive intensity, his motor, and his ability to take over games in the fourth quarter when opponents were at their most fatigued. The Wizards selected him No. 1 with the full expectation that what scouts saw in college is just the beginning of what Dybantsa will become at the professional level.

The Trae Young Partnership

Washington's $212 million commitment to Trae Young is the other half of this story. Young is one of the most skilled offensive players in the NBA — a wizard with the ball (fitting, given the franchise) who can pull up from anywhere, create for teammates in pick-and-roll situations, and manufacture points in ways that leave defenders frustrated and coaches searching for answers. He is also a proven veteran who has carried the offensive load for a franchise and knows what it takes to lead.

The combination of Young's veteran playmaking and Dybantsa's emerging two-way skill set gives Washington a legitimate foundation. Young can absorb defensive attention and create easy looks for Dybantsa early in his career, allowing the rookie to develop his game with less pressure than he would face as a franchise's sole offensive option. As Dybantsa grows into his role, the balance of the partnership will shift, and Washington hopes to have built something that can sustain success across multiple seasons.

Washington's New Chapter

The Wizards have invested significantly in their future: $66.91 million for Dybantsa, $212 million for Young — nearly $279 million in committed salary to two players who represent the franchise's new identity. That is not a team that is tanking or waiting for better days. That is an organization that has decided the time for building is now, and it has staked its financial future on two players it believes can deliver.

Washington fans have endured lean years and have reason to be cautiously excited about what management has assembled. The nation's capital has never lacked for passion when a franchise gives fans something to believe in. Dybantsa and Young could be the players who finally give Wizards fans a sustained reason to show up and make noise on a nightly basis.

What's Next

AJ Dybantsa will spend the coming months preparing for his first NBA training camp. The Wizards will use the remainder of the free agency period to round out their roster, adding complementary pieces around their two cornerstones. The NBA world will watch closely when the 2026-27 season tips off to see how quickly Dybantsa adjusts to the professional game. If his talent and work ethic translate the way Washington expects them to, the Wizards' path from the lottery to the playoffs could happen faster than anyone outside the organization is currently predicting.
