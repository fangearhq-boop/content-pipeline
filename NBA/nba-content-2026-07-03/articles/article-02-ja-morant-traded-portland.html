Ja Morant Traded to Portland Trail Blazers as Memphis Enters Full Rebuild

By Marcus Cole July 3, 2026 Hoop Heroes

The Ja Morant era in Memphis is over. The Grizzlies traded their two-time All-Star to the Portland Trail Blazers on Thursday in a multi-team deal that signals Memphis is committed to a full rebuild. Portland sent Kris Murray and Jerami Grant to Memphis in return, while the Dallas Mavericks received Santi Aldama and draft rights to Tarik Biberovic as part of the broader transaction. The deal officially executes on July 6, when the NBA's moratorium lifts.

Morant's departure brings to an end one of the most exciting runs a young star has had in recent NBA history. He was the face of a Memphis team that captured the imagination of basketball fans with their high-flying, fearless style of play. Two All-Star appearances, countless highlight-reel moments, and a city that fell completely in love with him — all of it now belongs to history. Ja Morant is a Portland Trail Blazer, and Memphis is starting over.

The Trade Breakdown

Memphis received Kris Murray and Jerami Grant from Portland in the core exchange. Murray, a forward out of Iowa, gives the Grizzlies a young piece with defensive upside and shooting ability at the wing. Grant is a proven veteran scorer who provides immediate stability but also has enough value that Memphis could move him again as they continue reshaping the roster.

The Mavericks' involvement adds a layer of complexity to the deal. Dallas acquiring Santi Aldama — a versatile forward who showed flashes during his time with the Grizzlies — and draft rights to Tarik Biberovic suggests Dallas had a stake in how this transaction came together. Multi-team trades in the NBA have become increasingly common as front offices look for creative ways to reshape rosters while satisfying the needs of multiple parties. This deal checks all those boxes.

What This Means for Portland

The Trail Blazers have not had a player this dynamic since their last legitimate Western Conference contender era. Morant brings exactly what Portland needs: star power, energy, and a personality that can fill an arena and command national attention. The Rose Garden — known now as Moda Center — is about to get loud again.

Portland has the bones of a team that can grow around Morant. He has proven that he can elevate a franchise, make teammates better, and put butts in seats on a nightly basis. The Trail Blazers will now build their offensive system around his ability to attack the rim and create chaos in transition. If the roster around him develops properly, Portland could go from afterthought to playoff contender faster than most expect.

Memphis Says Goodbye

For Grizzlies fans, this is a genuinely painful moment. Ja Morant was not just a basketball player in Memphis — he was a symbol of the city's identity on the national stage. His arrival breathed life into a franchise that had been searching for direction, and his best moments were among the most thrilling in recent NBA memory. The path forward in Memphis will require patience, and the fanbase will have to trust that the front office is building toward something worth waiting for.

The Grizzlies are not the first team to trade a franchise cornerstone in the name of rebuilding, and they will not be the last. What matters now is what comes next. A full rebuild means draft capital, development, and the kind of unglamorous process that eventually produces winners. Memphis has done it before. With the right picks and the right patience, they can do it again.

What's Next

The trade becomes official on July 6 when the moratorium lifts. At that point, Ja Morant will put on a Trail Blazers jersey and begin the work of building something new in Portland. Memphis will turn their attention to the 2026 NBA Draft class and the next wave of young talent. For both franchises, July 6 is the start of a new chapter. For the Trail Blazers, they cannot wait to get started.
