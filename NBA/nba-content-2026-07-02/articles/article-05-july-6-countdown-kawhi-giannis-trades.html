July 6 Countdown: Giannis, Kawhi and the Trades That Will Change the NBA Forever

By Jake Torres | July 2, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Four days. That's all that stands between the current NBA landscape and a version of the league that will look dramatically different than what fans watched through May and June. July 6 at 12:01 PM ET is when the moratorium lifts, contracts execute, and everything that's been agreed to in hushed phone calls and board rooms becomes official.

For anyone trying to keep track: the moves are breathtaking in scope.

Giannis Arrives in Miami

The Milwaukee Bucks are sending Giannis Antetokounmpo and Bobby Portis to the Miami Heat. In return, Milwaukee receives Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., Kasparas Jakucionis, the 2026 No. 13 overall pick, unprotected 2031 and 2033 first-round picks, a 2030 pick swap, and a 2033 second-round pick.

The trade was first reported on June 22. Miami's championship odds moved from 30-1 to 18-1 following the announcement. The Heat also agreed to deals with Andrew Wiggins on a three-year, $64 million contract and Tim Hardaway Jr. on a one-year, $6.5 million deal, building the supporting cast around Giannis and Bam Adebayo in what could become the league's most formidable defensive frontcourt.

When July 6 arrives, Pat Riley's vision becomes reality. The Heat franchise, which has never been content to simply compete, will take the floor next season as one of the NBA's most feared teams.

Kawhi Comes Home to Toronto

The Kawhi Leonard story has a narrative arc that belongs in a Hollywood screenplay. He was traded from San Antonio to Toronto in 2018. He won the Raptors their only NBA championship in 2019, then left for the Los Angeles Clippers in free agency. Seven years and a complicated journey later, he is coming back to Toronto.

The Clippers declined to exercise Leonard's option for 2026-27, and the 34-year-old center signaled to teams around the league that he would only sign an extension with Toronto. The Raptors sent Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, two 2031 and 2033 unprotected first-round picks, two second-round picks, and a 2027 first-round pick swap to acquire him.

HITC noted that Toronto gave up more this time than they did in the original 2018 trade — a reflection of how much the Raptors want their hero back and what the market demands for a veteran of Leonard's caliber, even at 34. Toronto plans to offer a two-year extension when the deal is official.

The Rest of the Landscape

Beyond the headliners, the moratorium has been full of moves. Ja Morant is heading to Portland after the Grizzlies completed his trade to the Trail Blazers. James Harden declined his $42.3 million player option with Cleveland and is working out a new multi-year deal to stay with the Cavaliers. Norman Powell agreed to a two-year, $45 million deal with the Chicago Bulls. Tobias Harris is going to San Antonio on two years and $31 million. Mike Conley Jr., in his 20th NBA season, will play for the Boston Celtics.

None of it is official until July 6. All of it will change the landscape on July 6.

What's Next

The final chapter of this extraordinary offseason will be written on July 6. Where does LeBron James officially sign? Does KAT ink his extension with the Knicks? Do Kawhi and Giannis put pen to paper on the extensions that would cement their new homes?

Four days. The NBA's summer of chaos has one more act to deliver, and it promises to be the loudest one yet.
