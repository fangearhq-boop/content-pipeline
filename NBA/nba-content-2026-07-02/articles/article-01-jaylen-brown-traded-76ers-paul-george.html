Jaylen Brown Traded to 76ers for Paul George in Stunning Celtics Reset

By Damon Pierce | July 2, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Ten seasons. Five All-Star appearances. One NBA championship. One Finals MVP. And it ended not with a parade or a farewell speech, but with a trade that sent shockwaves through the NBA on Wednesday.

The Boston Celtics have agreed to send Jaylen Brown to the Philadelphia 76ers in exchange for Paul George, a 2028 first-round pick, a 2031 first-round pick via Philadelphia, a 2028 second-round pick, and a 2030 second-round pick. The deal is set to execute July 6 when the moratorium lifts.

One anonymous NBA general manager already had an opinion: Bleacher Report reported the exec called the deal "highway robbery" in favor of Philadelphia.

What Philadelphia Gets

For the 76ers, this is a franchise-altering acquisition. Joel Embiid now has a co-star capable of guarding any player on the floor, scoring from anywhere, and providing the championship pedigree that Philadelphia has chased for years.

The backcourt pairing of Brown and Tyrese Maxey may be the most dangerous in the Eastern Conference. Maxey is an elite scorer and playmaker. Brown is a two-way bulldozer who can impose his will on both ends. Add VJ Edgecombe's athleticism and Embiid's dominance in the post, and the Sixers have constructed a legitimate title contender.

At 29 years old with three guaranteed seasons remaining on his contract, Brown gives Philadelphia a long-term cornerstone at the cost of a relatively modest pick package — at least by the standards of what other teams were reportedly asking.

What Boston Gets — And What It Means

The Celtics receive Paul George, now 36, as the headline return piece alongside two first-round picks and two second-round picks. George is a future Hall of Famer, but his best seasons are firmly behind him. The picks are the more meaningful long-term assets.

This move accelerates what has already been a turbulent offseason for Boston. The Celtics were reportedly among the finalists for Giannis Antetokounmpo — offering Brown and two unprotected first-round picks for the two-time MVP — and came up short when Milwaukee chose Miami's package instead. Now Brown is gone anyway, for considerably less in return.

The Celtics appear to be choosing a controlled rebuild over a win-now scramble. George can provide veteran leadership and scoring off the bench or in a featured role, while the picks give Boston the flexibility to pursue future stars through the draft.

The End of a Celtic Dynasty Run

Brown's departure closes a remarkable chapter. Drafted No. 3 overall in 2016, Brown grew alongside Jayson Tatum, won a title in 2024, and became one of Boston's most beloved players. The image of Brown hoisting the championship trophy was supposed to symbolize a new dynasty beginning. Instead, it may have been its peak.

The Celtics are now in unfamiliar territory: younger, less certain, building toward a future that doesn't include the All-NBA forward who anchored their most successful stretch in a generation.

What's Next

The trade becomes official July 6. The 76ers will then turn their attention to extension conversations — Brown is eligible for a max extension that would keep him in Philadelphia long-term. On the Celtics' side, all eyes will shift to Jayson Tatum and what the franchise's new identity looks like heading into 2026-27.

One thing is certain: the Eastern Conference just got more complicated, and the Philadelphia 76ers just made their loudest statement of the decade.
