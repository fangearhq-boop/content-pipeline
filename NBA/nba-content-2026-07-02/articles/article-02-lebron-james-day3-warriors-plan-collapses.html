LeBron James Free Agency Day 3: Warriors' Anthony Davis Plan Falls Apart

By Jake Torres | July 2, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Golden State Warriors had a vision: bring LeBron James to the Bay, trade for Anthony Davis, and rebuild an instant super team around Steph Curry for one final championship run. On Day 3 of NBA free agency, that vision officially died.

The Washington Wizards have told teams they will not trade Davis to Golden State, and the Warriors cannot sweeten their offer enough to change that position. Without the Davis piece, LeBron's path to the Warriors becomes dramatically different — and perhaps less appealing.

The King still hasn't committed to anyone. July 6 is the first day contracts can be signed, and with four days left before the moratorium lifts, the suspense around the most high-profile free agent decision in years is reaching its peak.

The Warriors' Revised Pitch

Golden State remains the -500 favorite at DraftKings to land LeBron, down from the earlier wave of optimism but still heavy favorites over the Cleveland Cavaliers (+300), Miami Heat (+1000), and San Antonio Spurs (+2000).

The Warriors are offering the Non-Taxpayer Mid-Level Exception, worth approximately $15.1 million. It's not the max contract LeBron could theoretically earn — at 41, he's playing on something closer to a prove-it deal at MLE money — but the appeal was always roster construction, not salary.

With Davis off the table, the pitch to LeBron includes Steph Curry, Kristaps Porzingis, and whatever else Golden State can assemble around that core. It's a compelling supporting cast, but it's also a different calculation than what LeBron's camp originally heard when Warriors conversations reportedly began.

The Other Suitors

Cleveland represents LeBron's most compelling alternative. The Cavaliers have a young core and real cap flexibility. LeBron played some of his best basketball in Cleveland and won the franchise's only championship in 2016. There would be a symmetry to ending his career where it truly began.

Miami at +1000 is a longer shot, but Pat Riley is Pat Riley. The Heat just acquired Giannis Antetokounmpo in a blockbuster trade, so their roster construction makes a LeBron fit more complicated — but Riley has never been accused of thinking small.

San Antonio (+2000) remains the long-shot story of the week. Victor Wembanyama is the next great NBA player. LeBron alongside Wembanyama would be a storyline the league has never seen: the greatest player in history guiding the player who might succeed him. It's compelling. It's also unlikely.

What LeBron Actually Wants

The details of LeBron's priorities have remained frustratingly vague throughout the process. He informed the Lakers he would not return for 2026-27 after Los Angeles was swept 4-0 by Oklahoma City in the 2026 playoffs — a clean, decisive break from his eight-year run in Southern California.

Since then, his representatives have spoken with multiple teams. No team has been granted a formal meeting. No verbal commitment has been made public. LeBron James is playing this one close to the vest until the very end.

What's Next

The countdown to July 6 continues. If no agreement is announced before then, LeBron could wait until the moratorium lifts to sign simultaneously with a team's cap space clearing. He could also signal a commitment before July 6 — verbal agreements are permissible, just not legally binding.

The Warriors remain the favorite. But without Anthony Davis, the question is whether LeBron still sees Golden State as the right fit — or whether the Cavaliers, the Heat, or a dark horse makes its move in the final days before July 6.
