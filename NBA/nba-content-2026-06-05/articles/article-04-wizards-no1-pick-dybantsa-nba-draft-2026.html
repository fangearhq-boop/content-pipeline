Washington Wins the Lottery: AJ Dybantsa and the Wizards' New Beginning

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 5, 2026

The Washington Wizards have the No. 1 pick in the 2026 NBA Draft, and AJ Dybantsa is ready to be their franchise cornerstone.

Washington won the 2026 NBA Draft Lottery with a 14.0 percent chance — the first time the Wizards have held the top selection since they took John Wall in 2010. The draft itself takes place June 23–24 in Brooklyn, and the overwhelming expectation is that Dybantsa will hear his name called first.

He has left no doubt about how he feels about it. "I'm 100 percent sure I'll be the Wizards' pick," Dybantsa said this week. "I fill seats."

The Case for Dybantsa

Dybantsa, a 6'9", 210-pound forward from BYU, averaged 25.5 points per game this season on 51.0 percent shooting — the highest scoring average in Division I college basketball. He can score at every level of the court, handles the ball with point-guard ease for his size, and projects as a versatile wing who can defend multiple positions at the next level.

Draft analysts who have covered this class describe him as a rare combination of safe floor and enormous ceiling. His skill set is polished enough to contribute immediately; his athleticism and shot-creation ability give teams a reason to project him as a franchise player long term. In a draft class that is widely praised as one of the deepest in recent memory — with multiple players holding legitimate cases for the first overall pick — Dybantsa remains the consensus top choice.

The mock drafts reflect the reality: virtually every projection has Dybantsa landing at No. 1 in Washington. The Athletic's post-lottery mock draft confirmed Dybantsa to the Wizards, with Darryn Peterson of the Jazz picking second.

A Small Wrinkle

Not everything about the Dybantsa-to-Washington pipeline is locked in. NBA Draft Combine reports out of Chicago indicated that Dybantsa was open to working out for more than one team and was reportedly hoping to remain in the Utah area. He and his camp appeared to be doing what smart draft prospects do — maintaining leverage and keeping all options open before the June 23 deadline.

CBS Sports' combine intelligence suggested the Dybantsa-to-Washington pairing is "not quite the lock everyone assumed." That is a far cry from saying it is unlikely. It is an acknowledgment that the Wizards' front office has its own evaluation to complete before making the pick official.

The complete top five after the lottery: Wizards (No. 1), Jazz (No. 2), Grizzlies (No. 3), Bulls (No. 4), Clippers via a Pacers trade (No. 5).

Washington's Long Wait

The last time the Wizards selected No. 1 overall was a different era for the franchise. John Wall was drafted in 2010 — before years of injury, before the organizational transition, before a rebuild that has taken the better part of a decade to produce. Washington finished 17-65 this season. They have not been to the playoffs since the Wall era ended.

A 14.0 percent lottery ball ending in a No. 1 pick is a franchise-defining moment. The Wizards have been through enough difficult seasons to know exactly how rare this opportunity is — and Dybantsa's talent gives Washington a genuine reason to believe this rebuild ends differently from the ones before it.

Sixteen days until the draft. Washington is ready. And apparently, so is Dybantsa.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Draft takes place June 23–24 in Brooklyn, New York. Round 1 is on June 23, with Round 2 following on June 24. Pre-draft workouts and official visits will continue over the next two weeks as teams finalize their evaluations. The Wizards are expected to make AJ Dybantsa the first overall selection, beginning a new chapter for a franchise that has been waiting sixteen years for a night like this.
