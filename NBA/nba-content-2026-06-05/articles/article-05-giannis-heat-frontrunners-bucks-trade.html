Miami Heat Are the Frontrunners for Giannis as Bucks Trade Market Takes Shape

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 5, 2026

The NBA's most consequential offseason trade is taking shape, and multiple people around the league are pointing in one direction: Miami.

The Milwaukee Bucks are "open for business" when it comes to Giannis Antetokounmpo, according to reporting from Shams Charania. And as the trade market has developed over the past week, the Miami Heat have emerged as the frontrunners to land the two-time MVP, with agents, executives, and sources close to the situation increasingly expecting Antetokounmpo to end up in South Beach.

The timeline is tight. A deal is expected to coincide with the June 23 NBA Draft proceedings in Brooklyn — just 18 days away.

Miami's Position

The Heat have made Antetokounmpo their primary offseason target and are willing to offer nearly everything in their roster to get him. There is one exception: Bam Adebayo remains off-limits in any trade package Milwaukee receives. Every other Heat asset — including Jimmy Butler successor Tyler Herro, defensive forward Haywood Highsmith, and draft picks extending through 2032 — is reportedly on the table.

The Heat's appeal to Giannis is multifaceted. Miami offers an elite coaching system under Erik Spoelstra. They have a reputation for player development and maximizing talent around a superstar. And perhaps most practically: Florida's lack of state income tax has a real financial impact on any player's take-home earnings over a max contract.

Giannis is eligible to sign a four-year, $275 million extension starting October 1. A team that lands him in a trade has the inside track on locking him up before any other suitor can make a competing offer.

The Portland Question

The Portland Trail Blazers are also in pursuit, but insiders are skeptical. Giannis has a well-documented preference to remain in the Eastern Conference, and Portland is as far west as it gets in the NBA. The Blazers' young core — built around Scoot Henderson and a collection of draft assets — could theoretically assemble an attractive package, but the geography problem is not a small one.

ClutchPoints reported that multiple insiders have "dumped cold water" on Portland's chances, with the Eastern preference being the primary barrier. Unless the Trail Blazers can somehow change Antetokounmpo's preference about conference, they are competing from behind.

Other teams — ESPN reported up to 10 potential trade contenders, including the Warriors, Rockets, and Celtics — are involved in various stages of interest and evaluation, but none have the same level of reported certainty around their pursuit as Miami.

The Bucks' Position

Milwaukee owner Wes Edens made the situation clear in March: "Either he will be extended or he'll be traded." The Bucks are not going to wait out Giannis's contract and risk losing him for nothing in free agency. They want either a long-term commitment or maximum return in a trade.

Giannis has two years left on his current deal — the second is a player option — so the Bucks have leverage. They are not selling into a vacuum. But the trade market has been described internally as "tepid" so far, which suggests the return package for a two-time MVP may not reach the levels Milwaukee hopes for.

The Bucks are navigating a difficult moment: maximizing value for a player who has defined the franchise, while also acknowledging that the player himself may have more say in where he ends up than their leverage implies.

What's Next

The NBA Draft on June 23 in Brooklyn is the expected pivot point for the Giannis situation. Teams will have clarity on their draft selections, their offseason budgets, and their ability to assemble trade packages once draft night begins — making the days immediately surrounding June 23 the most likely window for a deal to get done.

For now, Miami is in the lead. The Bucks are ready to deal. And Giannis Antetokounmpo's future — whether it ends in a Miami Heat jersey or somewhere else entirely — is being written in real time.
