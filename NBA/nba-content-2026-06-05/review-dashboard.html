Josh Hart's Larry Bird Moment — 15 Reb, 6 Ast, 4 Stl in Game 1
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 11:00 AM ET Josh Hart did something in Game 1 of the NBA Finals that hadn't been done since Larry Bird. Hart finished with 15 rebounds, six assists, four steals and a block in 27 minutes — scoring just three points on five shots. It is the first time any player has posted 15-plus rebounds, six-plus assists, and four-plus steals in an NBA Finals game since Bird went off for 25 points, 15 rebounds, 11 assists and four steals in Game 3 of the 1986 Finals against the Houston Rockets. And the Knicks were +22 in Hart's minutes. Not +22 for the game — just in the 27 minutes Hart was on the floor. When Jalen Brunson was asked about Hart after the game, he called him "a kid." Not as an insult — as the highest possible compliment. Hart plays with the unbothered, relentless energy of someone who has nothing to prove and everything to give. He corralled Wembanyama's critical late-game turnover and helped drain the clock. He clawed for every loose ball. He guarded whoever the Spurs needed guarded. He made the Knicks +22 in 27 minutes without making a single basket that will show up on a highlight reel. There's no award for what Josh Hart does. There's no way to explain it to someone who only looks at scoring columns. You just watch and feel it — and last night, the entire city of New York felt it. The Hart era in New York is real. And it started with 40 years of waiting for a moment like this. 🌟 ### Image Post Caption 3 points. 15 rebounds. 6 assists. 4 steals. Josh Hart in Game 1 of the NBA Finals. ### Long-Form Post Window: 1:00 PM ET Victor Wembanyama shot 6-of-21 in Game 1. He had six turnovers. He limped at one point and the crowd in San Antonio gasped. An MRI cleared him. And when reporters asked him about it afterward, Wembanyama was calm. "I just have to play normal," he said. "I'm not worried. We just need to approach the game with a better mental state." That's not bravado. That's a 22-year-old who has seen enough adversity in his career to understand that one bad shooting night isn't a career-defining moment. He knows what he is. But NBA Hall of Famer Shaquille O'Neal had some advice this week that goes a step further: Wemby needs to stop settling for jump shots. His length and footwork are most effective in the paint — not chasing contested threes over Knicks wings who can match his wingspan. Getting to the rim forces the Knicks to foul him. It changes the game. The Spurs shot only 25.6 percent from three in Game 1 and managed just 16 assists — neither of those numbers is going to beat a Knicks team playing at its current level. Game 2 is tonight at 8:30 PM ET. We're about to find out whether Wemby's calmness was confidence or denial.
