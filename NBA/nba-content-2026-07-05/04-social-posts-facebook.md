### STORY 2: Darryn Peterson Announces Himself in Summer League

**Long-Form Post**

🏀 THE FUTURE ARRIVED EARLY — DARRYN PETERSON EXPLODES IN SUMMER LEAGUE

The Utah Jazz selected Darryn Peterson with the No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 NBA Draft, and on July 4th in Salt Lake City, Peterson delivered the most jaw-dropping Summer League debut of the weekend.

Peterson finished with 28 points in the Jazz's 103-102 overtime victory over the Atlanta Hawks — and the way he got there was even more impressive than the number. He scored just 10 points in the first half, then erupted for 18 points in the second half alone, taking over the game when it mattered most. With Utah trailing in overtime, Peterson hit a go-ahead 3-pointer to put the Jazz ahead for good.

The 19-year-old showed everything that made him a top-two pick: athleticism, shot-making, composure in pressure moments, and the ability to take over when the lights brighten.

Peterson was not the only 2026 draft pick to shine on July 4th. Cameron Boozer — the No. 3 overall pick selected by the Memphis Grizzlies — dropped 15 points as Memphis dominated the OKC Thunder 111-74. Olivier-Maxence Prosper led that Memphis squad with 17 points.

The 2026 NBA Draft class is already delivering on the hype, and Summer League has barely begun.

→ MITCHELL ROBINSON signed — 3 years, $47 million. One of the best rim protectors in the NBA, Robinson gives Boston a defensive anchor in the paint they lacked.

The Celtics also retained offensive key cogs and now hold a war chest of future picks from the 76ers. They went from "missed on Giannis" to "actually positioned themselves better for the next three years."
