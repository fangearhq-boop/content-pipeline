LeBron James Day 6: Cavaliers Take the Lead as Moratorium Eve Arrives

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 5, 2026

The waiting is almost over. LeBron James, the greatest player of his generation, will become officially eligible to sign a contract on July 6 when the NBA moratorium lifts at 12:01 PM ET. And for the first time in this free agency saga, one team has separated itself from the field.

The Cleveland Cavaliers are now the frontrunner to land LeBron James, according to prediction markets on Polymarket and Kalshi. Cleveland sits at 40 to 56 percent odds, while the Golden State Warriors have fallen to 18 to 32 percent. Miami, Philadelphia, and San Antonio remain distant possibilities at single-digit odds.

The shift is significant. Through much of the week, the Warriors had occupied the top spot in most market tracking tools. The Cavs moving in front on the final day before the moratorium lifts reflects a broader sentiment among informed observers: the homecoming story is too powerful to ignore.

The Homecoming Narrative

LeBron James grew up in Akron, Ohio, 40 miles from Cleveland. He played seven seasons with the Cavaliers across two stints and in 2016 led them to the NBA Championship — completing a comeback from 3-1 down against these same Golden State Warriors in one of the greatest finals in league history. The city of Cleveland had waited 52 years for a major sports title. LeBron delivered it.

Now at 41, entering his 24th NBA season, the chance to finish his career where he first became a legend carries a weight that no Bay Area pitch can fully match. For Cleveland, landing LeBron a second time would be not just a basketball move — it would be a moment.

Rich Paul Manages Expectations

LeBron's agent and close friend Rich Paul gave a candid assessment of the timeline on Saturday. "I don't think this happens anytime soon," Paul told reporters, cautioning against expecting a July 6 announcement the moment the moratorium ends.

Paul also revealed the extraordinary breadth of LeBron's options. "He's heard from every team in the entire league except one or two," Paul said. That includes the Warriors, who have been vocal and credible throughout the process, and the Cavaliers, who have made a compelling financial and emotional pitch.

The Warriors Still in It

Golden State remains a real option. Stephen Curry and LeBron James forged a genuine friendship as teammates on the 2024 Paris Olympics gold medal squad — a partnership that went far beyond two rivals sharing a bench. The Warriors have constructed a roster designed to make a final championship run attractive. Their pitch is simple and powerful: come win one more ring alongside the best shooter who ever lived.

But the market has spoken. Cleveland is leading, and unless something changes in the final hours before July 6, LeBron James could be heading home to Ohio.

What's Next

The NBA moratorium lifts Monday, July 6 at 12:01 PM ET. LeBron James can officially sign with any team at that moment. Per Rich Paul, the decision may not come immediately — but the world will not be waiting long. Once LeBron chooses, the ripple effects across the entire league will follow instantly, reshaping roster construction, title odds, and the narrative of the 2026-27 season before it has even begun.
