July 6 Is Here: Giannis, Kawhi, Brown and the Trades That Reshape the NBA Go Official Tomorrow

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 5, 2026

One hour. That is all it takes for the NBA to look completely different. Monday, July 6 at 12:01 PM ET, the moratorium ends and what has been one of the most chaotic, historic, and consequential offseasons in league history becomes official. Four transformative trades execute simultaneously, and the power map of professional basketball is redrawn from scratch.

Giannis to Miami

The biggest move of the summer becomes real on Monday. Giannis Antetokounmpo, the two-time MVP who spent 13 seasons in Milwaukee, is officially a Miami Heat player the moment the clock strikes 12:01. The Greek Freak joins Bam Adebayo in what will instantly become one of the most dominant frontcourts in the NBA.

The price was steep: Milwaukee receives Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., Kasparas Jakucionis, the No. 13 pick in the 2026 draft, three future first-round picks, a 2030 pick swap, and a 2033 second-rounder. It is a comprehensive package that sets the Bucks up for a rebuild with significant assets.

For Miami, the transformation is immediate. Their championship odds at DraftKings moved from 30-1 to 18-1 the moment the deal was reported. Andrew Wiggins, who agreed to a 3-year, $64 million deal with the Heat this week, adds an additional weapon to a team that suddenly looks like a genuine Eastern Conference threat.

Kawhi Returns to Toronto

Kawhi Leonard, who delivered the Raptors their only NBA Championship in 2019 and whose buzzer-beater against Philadelphia remains the defining moment in franchise history, is coming back to Toronto. At 34, Leonard chose to return to the city that embraced him most — agreeing to a two-year extension with the Raptors after the Clippers made no long-term commitment.

Toronto sent Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, two unprotected first-round picks, two second-round picks, and a 2027 swap to Los Angeles. It is a significant price for a 34-year-old, but the Raptors are betting on what Kawhi brings to their culture and their fanbase.

Brown to Philly, George to Boston

Jaylen Brown, the 2024 NBA Finals MVP, officially becomes a Philadelphia 76er on Monday. Brown joins Tyrese Maxey, Joel Embiid, and rookie VJ Edgecombe on a 76ers team that suddenly has championship-level talent on paper. Paul George heads to Boston with a collection of future draft picks, joining Jayson Tatum on a retooled Celtics squad that refused to accept the Giannis miss as the end of its contention window.

What's Next

Once the moratorium lifts at 12:01 PM ET on Monday, all four trades become official and LeBron James becomes eligible to sign. The cascade of decisions, roster announcements, and press conferences that follows will define the shape of the 2026-27 NBA season. Watch for early title odds adjustments, the official announcement of LeBron's destination, and the first wave of dominoes from teams now scrambling to adjust to a league that looks nothing like it did a week ago.
