Darryn Peterson's 28-Point Explosion Announces the 2026 Draft Class Is Already Here

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | July 5, 2026

NBA Summer League is supposed to be a scouting exercise, a low-stakes proving ground for rookies finding their footing. Nobody told Darryn Peterson.

The Utah Jazz's No. 2 overall pick in the 2026 NBA Draft delivered one of the most electric Summer League debuts in recent memory on July 4th in Salt Lake City, finishing with 28 points in Utah's 103-102 overtime victory over the Atlanta Hawks. The 19-year-old did not just score — he took over the game at its most critical moments.

A Second-Half Takeover

Peterson entered halftime with a manageable 10 points. What happened next was something else entirely. He erupted for 18 points in the second half alone, turning a close game into his personal showcase. When the game reached overtime tied, Peterson delivered the decisive blow — a go-ahead 3-pointer that put the Jazz ahead for good and sealed the 103-102 victory.

The performance showcased the full range of what made Peterson a consensus top-two pick: his explosiveness getting to the basket, his composure in pressure situations, and a shooting touch that looks ready for the next level on Day 1. For a Jazz franchise looking to build around young talent, this was exactly the debut they were hoping for.

Boozer Announces Himself Too

Peterson was not the only 2026 draft pick to turn heads on Independence Day. Cameron Boozer, selected No. 3 overall by the Memphis Grizzlies, scored 15 points in Memphis's commanding 111-74 blowout of the OKC Thunder. Olivier-Maxence Prosper led the Grizzlies with 17 points, but Boozer's presence was unmistakable at 18 years old going against Summer League competition.

The 2026 draft class entered the league with significant hype. Two games into Summer League, that hype looks justified. Peterson and Boozer both demonstrated the foundational skills that make draft analysts optimistic: shot-making, basketball IQ, and the physical tools to compete at the highest level.

A Full Summer League Slate

The July 4th games also featured strong play from veterans trying to make rosters. In the California Classic, the Sacramento Kings edged the Brooklyn Nets 79-76 with Nique Clifford hitting a late 3-pointer to seal it. The Milwaukee Bucks topped the Warriors Blue squad 97-83 with Brandon Boston Jr. leading the way with 17 points.

Summer League runs concurrently across Salt Lake City and the Bay Area, with dozens of games scheduled through mid-July. The early read on this class is promising across the board.

What's Next

Darryn Peterson and the Utah Jazz continue their Summer League schedule through mid-July in Salt Lake City, where Peterson will face increasingly experienced competition as the event progresses. Cameron Boozer and the Memphis Grizzlies are also scheduled to play multiple additional games. Summer League results do not always translate directly to NBA production, but a player who can perform the way Peterson did in his first game — taking over in overtime at 19 years old — gives every indication that the Jazz made the right call at No. 2.
