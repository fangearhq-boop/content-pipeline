Kawhi Leonard Investigation Drags Into Month 11 With No End in Sight — Silver Demands Resolution

By Damon Pierce

July 21, 2026

Eleven months after the NBA launched its investigation into the Los Angeles Clippers' relationship with Kawhi Leonard and the carbon-credits startup Aspiration, the league remains weeks away from any resolution. What began as a focused probe into a single endorsement deal has expanded into a multi-faceted investigation touching two separate endorsement arrangements and questions about personal expense coverage — with no timetable for conclusion.

HoopsHype reported on July 16 that the investigation's conclusion is still "weeks away." Commissioner Adam Silver, who has grown visibly impatient, made his most direct public statement yet: the probe "needs to be wrapped up before next season." That ultimatum, delivered publicly, is the strongest signal yet that Silver is personally pressuring law firm Wachtell Lipton — which is conducting the investigation — to accelerate its work.

What the Investigation Has Expanded to Include

The original allegation centered on the Clippers allegedly routing compensation to Leonard through a no-show endorsement deal with Aspiration, circumventing the NBA's salary cap rules. That allegation has since been joined by two additional areas of investigation.

First, investigators are examining a second previously undisclosed endorsement deal that Leonard allegedly had — a deal that was not reported to the league at the time it was structured. Second, there are questions about whether the Clippers covered personal expenses for Leonard that he did not reimburse, which could represent additional undisclosed compensation in violation of league rules.

The Clippers have consistently denied wrongdoing, and their position is complicated by the arrest and conviction of Alex Sanberg — a figure connected to the Aspiration deal — who was sentenced to 14 years in prison, giving the Clippers a fraud narrative they continue to invoke in their defense.

The Raptors Wait in Limbo

The immediate victim of the investigation's length is the Toronto Raptors. A trade that would return Leonard — who won Toronto its only NBA championship in 2019 — to the franchise has been agreed upon in principle but remains officially frozen while the investigation continues. Toronto was informed that if the trade went through before the probe concluded, the Raptors would assume the risk of any punishment levied against Leonard.

That condition has made Toronto reluctant to finalize the deal. The Raptors cannot build their roster around a player whose status is in legal jeopardy, whose trade eligibility is unresolved, and whose potential punishments range from a long-term suspension to a complete voiding of his contract.

The situation is particularly acute heading into free agency season. With every other franchise in the league actively restructuring — five blockbuster trades have already reshaped the landscape — Toronto is among the few teams unable to plan for 2026-27 with any certainty.

What Could Happen

The NBA has broad investigative authority to impose significant penalties when it finds evidence of salary cap circumvention. Potential outcomes include franchise penalties for the Clippers, a suspension for Leonard, or, in the most extreme scenario, a voiding of Leonard's contract — which would make him an unrestricted free agent but would also represent an unprecedented outcome in league history.

Silver's public timeline demand suggests he expects the investigation to conclude before training camps open in late September. If it does not, the NBA would face the prospect of entering a new season with an unresolved cloud over one of its most prominent players and a trade that has been in purgatory for nearly a year.

What's Next

The ball is in Wachtell Lipton's court — and, ultimately, in Silver's hands. The investigation must conclude before teams can finalize rosters, and Toronto specifically cannot make meaningful offseason moves while the Leonard situation remains open. Whether the probe wraps in weeks as sources suggest, or stretches further into the summer, the damage to both the Raptors' planning and the league's competitive integrity is already significant.

When the investigation does conclude, the fallout will be immediate. Whatever ruling the NBA hands down, it will reshape the Clippers, the Raptors, and potentially Leonard's entire future in the league.
