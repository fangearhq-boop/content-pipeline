### STORY 1: Caleb Wilson 35-Point Summer League Debut

**Long-Form Post:**
Caleb Wilson just put the NBA on notice.

The No. 4 overall pick — playing his first competitive game since breaking his wrist at North Carolina back in February — dropped 35 points in his Las Vegas Summer League debut on Friday. He shot 12-of-21 from the field and a scorching 7-of-11 from three, adding 5 rebounds, 2 steals, and 3 blocks.

That's the second-highest single-game scoring debut in Las Vegas Summer League history. Only Marco Belinelli's 37-point night in 2007 ranks higher.

The problem? It wasn't enough. Cameron Boozer — the No. 3 pick out of Memphis — answered with 23 points, 6 rebounds, and 4 assists to lift the Grizzlies to a 97-96 win. The top picks delivered the theater Summer League promised.

Wilson was openly emotional after the game, referencing his long road back from injury. The talent is undeniable. The Bulls have a genuine star in the making.

What a way to start. 🏀

### STORY 3: Victor Wembanyama Extension

**Long-Form Post:**
Victor Wembanyama just left $50 million on the table. On purpose.

The Spurs and Wembanyama have agreed to a 5-year, $252 million max extension — locking him in through the 2031-32 season with a player option in year five. But here's what makes the deal remarkable: Wembanyama chose the 25% max over the 30% supermax. The supermax would have been worth approximately $303 million. He took less.

It's only the third-largest rookie extension in NBA history, behind Cade Cunningham and Evan Mobley's deals at $269 million each.

Why the discount? Because Wembanyama believes in San Antonio's rebuild. He's choosing legacy over money at 22 years old — the same move Jalen Brunson made before helping the Knicks reach the Finals.

The Spurs haven't had a cornerstone like this since Tim Duncan. And their franchise player just bet on them right back.

### STORY 4: Giannis + Jaylen Brown Trades Official

**Long-Form Post:**
The Eastern Conference just got a whole lot more interesting.

Giannis Antetokounmpo is officially a Miami Heat. The blockbuster deal completed July 6, with Milwaukee receiving Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., Kasparas Jakučionis, a package of future first-round picks, and pick No. 13. Giannis is eligible for a 4-year, $275 million extension as early as January 2027. Heat title odds improved dramatically from 30-1 to 18-1.

And in Philadelphia, Jaylen Brown is officially a 76er. The Celtics parted with their All-Star wing for Paul George (who has two years left on his deal), plus two firsts and two seconds. Brown joins Tyrese Maxey and Joel Embiid to give Philly one of the East's most imposing trios.

Boston gets George and a mountain of draft capital. They're rebuilding and reloading at the same time.

Meanwhile, LeBron James is still watching all of this unfold while picking his next team.
