Summer League Day 3 Recap: Heat Dominate, Nets Handle Knicks, Lakers Cruise Past Thunder

By Damon Pierce | July 11, 2026

NBA Summer League

Eight games. Multiple breakout performances. And a top-pick showdown that ended with 35 points on the losing side. Friday's Las Vegas Summer League slate was everything the format promises — raw talent, unexpected results, and storylines that will follow these players into October and beyond.

Heat Blow Out Bucks, 119-86

The Miami Heat's rebuilt roster carries an air of confidence even in exhibition basketball. Despite Giannis Antetokounmpo's absence from Summer League action — veterans rarely participate — Miami dominated Milwaukee 119-86 in a performance that signals genuine organizational depth. The Bucks, reshaping their identity post-Giannis, absorbed the blowout as part of a longer rebuilding process.

Nets Handle Knicks in NYC Rivalry, 91-65

Brooklyn's Mikel Brown Jr. made a statement in the crosstown rivalry, scoring 20 points on 3-of-6 shooting from three and adding 2 steals in the Nets' convincing 91-65 win. The margin was unexpected — the Knicks' Summer League roster carries organizational talent — but Brooklyn was sharper and more efficient from start to finish. Brown Jr. will be a name worth tracking as camp approaches.

Lakers Roll Past Thunder, 96-84

Los Angeles looked comfortable from the opening tip. Adou Thiero led the way with 20 points while Cameron Carr added 18 in the Lakers' 96-84 win over Oklahoma City. The Thunder are among the league's most intriguing organizations heading into 2026-27 — their Summer League roster reflects genuine depth — but Friday belonged to LA. Thiero has been one of the more quietly impressive performers in the early Vegas slate.

Celtics Edge Raptors in OT, 83-80

Boston needed overtime to put away Toronto, with Amari Williams providing the difference. Williams finished with 23 points and 13 rebounds — a dominant double-double performance that earns attention regardless of context. The Celtics won 83-80, but the bigger takeaway was Williams's ability to control the paint on both ends through 45 minutes of play.

Grizzlies Beat Bulls 97-96 in the Night's Main Event

Caleb Wilson's historic 35-point debut deserved a win. Instead, Cameron Boozer's Grizzlies held on for a 97-96 victory that denied Wilson the complete debut script. The No. 4 pick shot 12-of-21 from the field and 7-of-11 from three in his first competitive game since a college wrist injury in February; the No. 3 pick responded with 23 points, 6 rebounds, and 4 assists. The top picks delivered exactly what the marquee billing promised.

Other Results

The Houston Rockets defeated the Denver Nuggets 97-86, the Indiana Pacers held off the Cleveland Cavaliers 99-93, and the Phoenix Suns edged the Portland Trail Blazers 81-79 in a close finish that went down to the wire.

In the day prior, Charlotte's Liam McNeeley continued his breakout Summer League stretch with 28 points on 7-of-8 shooting from three in an 86-74 win over Orlando. McNeeley averaged just 4.3 points per game as a rookie; his current stretch suggests that number has no room to survive the season ahead.

Minnesota's Zyon Pullin also turned heads with a 24-point, 8-rebound, 12-assist line in the Timberwolves' opener — one of the more complete Summer League performances by a player not in the top-five draft conversation.

What's Next

Las Vegas Summer League runs through July 19, with games continuing daily. The narrative threads — Wilson's development, Boozer's consistency, McNeeley's push for a rotation spot, Brown Jr.'s emergence in Brooklyn — will deepen with each additional contest.

The first week of Summer League has produced more compelling basketball than many expected. The draft class is talented, the teams are engaged, and the performances have genuine implications for October rosters. There's more to come.
