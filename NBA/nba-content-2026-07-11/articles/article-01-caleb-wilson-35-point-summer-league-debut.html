Caleb Wilson Scores 35 in Vegas Debut, But Cameron Boozer's Grizzlies Win 97-96

By Marcus Cole | July 11, 2026

NBA Summer League

Caleb Wilson made the kind of first impression that gets talked about for years — and still ended up on the losing side.

The Chicago Bulls' No. 4 overall pick scored 35 points in his Las Vegas Summer League debut Friday, shooting 12-of-21 from the field and a stunning 7-of-11 from beyond the arc while adding 5 rebounds, 2 steals, and 3 blocks in 32 minutes. It was the second-highest scoring debut in Las Vegas Summer League history, behind only Marco Belinelli's 37-point night in 2007. And it wasn't enough.

Cameron Boozer — the No. 3 pick, drafted by the Memphis Grizzlies — responded with 23 points, 6 rebounds, 4 assists, a steal, and a block of his own to lift Memphis to a 97-96 victory. The result delivered exactly the kind of top-pick theater that Summer League was built to provide.

A Long Road Back

Wilson's debut carried extra meaning beyond the box score. Friday's game was his first competitive action since February 10, when a broken wrist cut short his lone college season at North Carolina. Months of recovery, rehabilitation, and uncertainty preceded his pro debut.

The 35-point performance suggested none of it slowed him down. Wilson was visibly emotional after the game — grateful, relieved, and clearly motivated to make a statement. He did exactly that.

His shooting from deep was the standout. Seven three-pointers on 11 attempts against live NBA competition, in his first pro game, returning from a wrist injury, represents a level of shot-making confidence that doesn't show up in a scouting profile — it has to be witnessed.

Boozer Answers Every Time

The story of the night wasn't solely Wilson's performance. Boozer was relentless. Having already posted 15 points in his Salt Lake City debut against Oklahoma City and 18 against Utah, the Grizzlies' top pick kept finding ways to produce. His 23-point, 6-rebound effort in Las Vegas came without the luxury of being the bigger scorer — Wilson was scorching from the perimeter all night — and yet Memphis held on.

Boozer's game is predicated on intelligence and timing rather than raw athleticism. He finds angles, finishes through contact, and distributes efficiently. His four assists helped Memphis stay connected when Wilson's hot hand threatened to make it a blowout.

Bulls Get the Marquee Performance — and a Loss

Chicago will take the trade-off. Wilson is the centerpiece of a franchise rebuild, and his Summer League audition confirmed that the Bulls didn't miss with their selection. The 97-96 loss stings, but Summer League wins don't translate to October. The performance does.

Wilson's combination of perimeter shooting and defensive activity — three blocks from a guard-sized wing — gives Chicago a versatile weapon on both ends. He's not just a scorer; he's a player who impacts the game in multiple ways.

Liam McNeeley of the Charlotte Hornets went for 28 points and 7-of-8 from three the night before in a separate game, making the early Summer League stretch an extraordinary showcase of draft-class shooting talent. But Wilson's debut stands alone in terms of historical context.

What's Next

Wilson and the Bulls return to action over the next several days as Las Vegas Summer League continues through July 19. His next outing will tell a different story — teams will have had time to prepare, and the initial element of surprise will be gone.

The real question is whether Wilson can build on Friday's debut or whether it represents a ceiling in the short term. Based on his shot selection, defensive awareness, and composure in a high-profile moment, the early evidence suggests it's a floor.

Boozer and the Grizzlies, meanwhile, carry momentum into their next contest after a hard-fought win. The No. 3 pick proved he can compete with the best the draft class has to offer — even when they're scoring 35 points.
