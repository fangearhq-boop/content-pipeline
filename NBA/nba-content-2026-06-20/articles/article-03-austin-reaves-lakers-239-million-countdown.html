The $239 Million Countdown: Why Austin Reaves Is Expected to Stay in Los Angeles

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 20, 2026

Austin Reaves has nine days to make one of the biggest financial decisions of his career — and the expectation in NBA circles is that he makes it easy on himself.

By June 29, Reaves must decide whether to exercise his $14.9 million player option for the 2026-27 season or decline it and become an unrestricted free agent. He is expected to decline. The reasons aren't complicated: the open market will pay him far more than $14.9 million, and the Lakers have the richest offer available.

Los Angeles can offer a five-year maximum contract worth approximately $239.3 million. Outside teams — including the Brooklyn Nets, who had been mentioned as potential suitors — are capped at four-year deals worth roughly $178.5 million. That's a $60 million gap before factoring in the fifth year. The money advantage alone makes this decision relatively straightforward.

Beyond the Dollars

The more interesting part of the Reaves situation isn't the contract structure — it's what the latest reporting says about his decision-making. According to multiple sources, Reaves' choice "will not come down solely to a dollar figure."

That framing matters. It suggests Reaves isn't simply maximizing a paycheck — he's evaluating where he wants to play, who he wants to play with, and what he wants his career to look like. On all three fronts, Los Angeles scores well.

Reaves has become one of Luka Doncic's most important running mates in the two seasons they've played together in LA. The two have a genuine on-court chemistry — Reaves as the lethal off-ball scorer and two-way player who complements Doncic's creation — that both players have spoken about publicly. Building on that feels like unfinished business.

The Nets Factor

Brooklyn had been the most credible outside threat to land Reaves. The Nets had cap flexibility and were willing to move aggressively. But the latest reporting suggests Brooklyn is fading as a realistic competitor.

That's partly because the Nets' four-year offer simply can't match what LA puts on the table. And it's partly because the vision — joining a rebuilding franchise in Brooklyn versus partnering with Doncic in Los Angeles — isn't a close comparison for a 28-year-old player entering the prime years of his career.

The league's expectation, per multiple reports, is that Reaves signs with the Lakers "widely expected" to reach a deal around $200 million. That range would represent a slight discount below the maximum — something Brunson famously did before last season — and would give the front office flexibility to retain other roster pieces.

What Reaves Brings

The 28-year-old averaged 23.3 points per game last season, making him one of the most productive scorers in the league not considered a No. 1 option. He is elite at off-ball movement, three-point shooting, and defending multiple perimeter positions. He doesn't need the ball to impact a game, which makes him the ideal complement to a usage-heavy star like Doncic.

For a team trying to build around Doncic and contend for a championship, keeping Reaves isn't just desirable — it's essential. Letting him walk to Brooklyn would be a franchise mistake the Lakers can easily avoid.

What's Next

The June 29 player option deadline starts the clock on NBA free agency. The league expects Reaves to decline the option and open negotiations with the Lakers shortly after. If a deal gets done quickly, it could be announced before free agency officially opens June 30.

Nine days from now, one of the best restricted free agency sagas of recent years should have a resolution. And the resolution everyone expects is: Reaves stays a Laker.
