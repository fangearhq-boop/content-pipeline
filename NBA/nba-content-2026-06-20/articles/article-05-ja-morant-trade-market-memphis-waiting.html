The Second Domino: Memphis Is Waiting on Giannis Before Opening the Ja Morant Market

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 20, 2026

The Ja Morant trade market exists — it's just frozen right now, and it'll stay that way until Monday.

The Memphis Grizzlies have made a deliberate, strategic decision: they are not opening serious Morant trade discussions until the Giannis Antetokounmpo situation resolves. With draft night (June 23) serving as the Bucks' internal deadline to get the Giannis deal done, Memphis is essentially pressing pause until Tuesday morning at the earliest.

The reasoning is straightforward. Multiple teams potentially interested in Morant — most notably the Miami Heat — are currently consumed by the Giannis sweepstakes. Having trade conversations with the Heat right now, when Miami's front office is laser-focused on Antetokounmpo, would be a waste of time. Memphis is smart enough to wait for the room to clear.

The Heat's Plan B

Of all the teams reported to be interested in Morant, the Heat connection is the most compelling. Miami is the frontrunner for Giannis and has been for weeks. But if that deal falls through — if the "issue" with their package can't be resolved, or if Giannis ultimately chooses Boston — the Heat need to pivot quickly.

Reports indicate that Morant is Miami's Plan B. That pairing makes intuitive sense: a dynamic, highlight-reel point guard in a South Beach uniform is exactly the kind of star player the Heat would want to build around. Morant averaged 24.4 points per game last season and is 26 years old — entering the prime years of what has been an already spectacular career.

Whether the Heat could construct a package that satisfies Memphis is a separate question. But the interest is real.

Memphis's Position

The Grizzlies hold significant leverage. They have the No. 3 overall pick in Monday's draft — a top-3 selection that is a coveted asset in any trade negotiation. If they want to include the pick in a Morant deal, it dramatically increases what they can command in return. If they prefer to keep the pick and use it Monday night, they walk away with a potential franchise cornerstone regardless of what happens with Morant.

Multiple teams beyond the Heat are said to be monitoring the Morant situation. Any team that misses on Giannis — or that isn't in that conversation to begin with — could pivot to Memphis once the first domino falls. The post-draft trade market could be active within 48 hours of Monday night's conclusion.

Morant's Status

The 26-year-old guard has said publicly that he wants to win. Memphis is a young team with talented pieces — Jaren Jackson Jr., Desmond Bane — but has not made a deep postseason run. If ownership and management decide that rebuilding around a different core makes more sense than continuing to build around Morant, a trade becomes the logical outcome.

The fact that Memphis is holding — not actively shopping him, not closing the door — suggests the front office is treating this as a deliberate process rather than a fire sale. They want to see what the market looks like after the dust settles Monday night.

What's Next

The Giannis situation resolves (one way or another) by Monday night at the latest. The moment it does, the Morant market unfreezes. Memphis will start taking calls. The Heat — either flush with the Giannis they wanted or pivoting off a failed pursuit — will be among the first on the phone.

The NBA offseason runs in waves. The first wave is Giannis. The second wave could be Ja Morant. If the pattern holds, the league is about to get very interesting for the rest of June.
