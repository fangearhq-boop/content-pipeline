The Giannis Trade Endgame: Heat vs. Celtics With Three Days to Draft Night

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 20, 2026

The Milwaukee Bucks have a deadline in three days, and the trade that has consumed the NBA offseason is no closer to being done than it was a week ago — with a new complication now in the mix.

According to multiple reports, there is a significant "issue" with the Miami Heat's trade package for Giannis Antetokounmpo. The offer that had been circulating — Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., and the No. 13 pick in Monday's NBA Draft — has stalled. The Bucks are not satisfied. Whether that means they want more assets, different assets, or something else entirely, the gap has not closed.

That creates a window for the Boston Celtics, who have never truly gone away in this conversation. The Celtics' package centers on Jaylen Brown — a more polished All-Star than anyone in Miami's offer — and comes with a significant upside that the Heat cannot match: Giannis could sign a contract extension if he lands in Boston, locking in his future while the Celtics build around him.

The Heat's Situation

Miami has been the frontrunner for weeks. Reports have consistently stated that Giannis still wants to be in South Beach — the weather, the organization, the Pat Riley legacy that drew him. But wanting something and getting it are different things, and right now the Heat's offer has hit a wall.

The "issue" reported Friday hasn't been spelled out publicly. It could be the quality of Kel'el Ware, who is a promising big but not an established star. It could be the No. 13 pick, which falls in the middle of the first round. Or it could be Milwaukee asking for more: a second lottery pick, an additional role player, a sweetener of some kind.

What's clear is that if Miami wants Giannis, they will likely have to add to what they've put on the table. The question is whether they have the assets to do it.

The Celtics' Offer

Boston's pitch is structurally different. A package built around Jaylen Brown — a two-time All-Star and former Finals champion — gives Milwaukee a proven commodity rather than a collection of younger pieces and picks. The Celtics are offering an established player for an established player, the kind of trade the Bucks' front office could defend publicly.

The contract extension wrinkle adds another dimension. If Giannis joins the Celtics, he can sign an extension that secures his future with the organization. That option isn't on the table in Miami, where the financial structure of the deal limits what extensions can be offered.

The catch: Giannis has reportedly preferred Miami throughout this process. Getting him to choose Boston over his preferred destination would require convincing him that winning — not lifestyle — is the priority. The Celtics went to the Finals two years ago. They're a title contender. The basketball argument is real.

The Bucks' Timeline

Milwaukee has treated draft night — June 23 — as an internal deadline for getting this done. NBA Draft night is the one moment every offseason when teams can execute complex multi-player trades simultaneously, with picks and players moving in concert. It is logistically the easiest night to complete a deal of this magnitude.

Marc Stein, who has been tracking this situation as closely as anyone, said this week that a Giannis trade is "drawing near." Whether that means hours or days remains unclear. But the clock is running.

If no deal gets done Monday, the Bucks would be looking at free agency (which opens June 30) as the next realistic window. Giannis has one year remaining on his contract. The risk of not trading him now is significant: if he plays out the season and leaves in free agency, Milwaukee gets nothing.

What's Next

The next 72 hours will answer a question the NBA world has been asking since the Bucks were eliminated from the playoffs: Where does Giannis Antetokounmpo play next season?

The Heat need to resolve the "issue" with their package before Monday — either by adding assets or convincing Milwaukee the offer is fair. The Celtics need to convince Giannis that Boston is the right basketball fit. And the Bucks need to decide whether to take the best basketball offer or the package that checks more of their boxes.

Three days. The NBA offseason's biggest domino is about to fall.
