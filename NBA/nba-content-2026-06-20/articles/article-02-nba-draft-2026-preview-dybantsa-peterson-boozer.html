Three Days to Draft Night: AJ Dybantsa, the 2026 NBA Draft's Generational Prospect

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 20, 2026

Three days from now, the lights go up at Barclays Center in Brooklyn, and AJ Dybantsa walks across the stage to shake the commissioner's hand as the No. 1 overall pick in the 2026 NBA Draft. That's the expectation. That's what the -450 betting odds say. And that's what nearly every mock draft published in the last month projects.

The Washington Wizards won the lottery. They have the first pick. And barring something extraordinary, they are taking the 6'9", 217-pound freshman from BYU who averaged 25.5 points, 6.8 rebounds, and 3.7 assists per game in 35 games this past season while shooting 51% from the field.

Put those numbers into context: Dybantsa led the nation in scoring. He did it at 51% efficiency. He did it at 6'9" with the handle of a guard and the finishing ability of a power forward. NBA executives have broadly aligned on him as the best prospect in this class — not by a little, but by enough that the margin at the top of the draft is essentially settled.

Who's Behind Him

The No. 2 pick goes to the Utah Jazz, and the story there is Darryn Peterson. Medical evaluations came back clean this week — no red flags of any kind — which kept his projected stock exactly where it has been: No. 2 overall. Peterson is a different player than Dybantsa: more of a playmaking guard who scores efficiently and defends multiple positions. Jazz fans should be excited.

At No. 3, the Memphis Grizzlies are looking at Cameron Boozer, the Duke freshman who averaged 23 points, 11 rebounds, and 4 assists per game for the Blue Devils. Boozer's ceiling debates have been the most active conversation in draft circles — teams disagree on whether he projects as a power forward or undersized center at the next level — but the production is undeniable.

The Chicago Bulls hold No. 4 and have options. The rest of the lottery fills in from there, with a deep draft class that extends well into the first round.

The Giannis Variable

The one wildcard that could scramble the draft order is a Giannis trade. The Milwaukee Bucks have been involved in trade talks with both the Miami Heat and Boston Celtics, and multiple reports indicate a deal could happen before or on draft night. The Bucks hold the No. 10 pick — and starting draft night, they gain the ability to trade two additional first-round picks (in 2031 and 2033).

A Giannis trade would almost certainly involve pick swaps, additional first-rounders from the acquiring team, and potentially a reshuffling of where several players end up on draft night. The ESPN mock draft already projected six potential blockbuster trades involving 11 lottery picks. If Giannis moves Monday, all bets are off on the mid-to-late lottery.

Why This Draft Feels Different

The 2026 class is being talked about in the same breath as some of the modern era's elite drafts because of the gap between the top prospects and the average. Dybantsa isn't just good — he produced at a level rarely seen from a college freshman. Peterson's combination of size and skill has scouts drawing comparisons to players who became stars. Boozer's production at Duke is simply hard to ignore.

For the Wizards, who have been rebuilding for years, Dybantsa represents the franchise-altering pick that lottery systems are designed to deliver. Washington hasn't had a pick this consequential in a long time.

What's Next

Monday night at Barclays Center is going to be must-watch television. Beyond the top three, there are potential trades, surprise picks, and an entire second round of prospects that teams have scouted obsessively since November. The Giannis situation could reshape Monday night entirely.

Three days. The draft begins June 23. The NBA is about to look very different.
