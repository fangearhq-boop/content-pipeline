Tatum and Brown Each Score 25, Celtics Beat 76ers 108-100 in Game 3

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 25, 2026

The Boston Celtics went to Philadelphia and won Game 3 the way championship teams are supposed to — with their best players making big plays when the game was on the line. Jayson Tatum and Jaylen Brown each scored 25 points Friday night as Boston defeated the Philadelphia 76ers 108-100 to take a 2-1 series lead in the Eastern Conference first-round playoff series.

It was Brown who delivered the killing blow. With the Celtics clinging to a narrow lead in the fourth quarter, he rattled off 8 consecutive personal points — a run that pushed Boston's advantage to 96-92 and effectively ended Philadelphia's hopes of stealing the game on their home floor. Tatum, meanwhile, was surgical from beyond the arc, going 5-for-9 on 3-pointers in just his 19th game of the season following Achilles surgery in May 2025.

"We trust each other in big moments," Brown said afterward. "That's what this team is built for."

Payton Pritchard provided a massive boost off the bench, connecting on five 3-pointers for 15 points. The Celtics shot 20-of-47 from downtown as a team — a testament to the spacing and ball movement that has defined Boston's offense all postseason. Tatum and Brown combined for 19 of the team's 29 fourth-quarter points to close things out.

Maxey Fights But Can't Carry Sixers Alone

Tyrese Maxey gave Philadelphia everything he had. The 76ers guard scored 31 points and was relentless in his attempts to keep Philly competitive, but Joel Embiid's absence — the center is still recovering from an appendectomy performed on April 9 and has not yet been cleared to practice — left the Sixers without the interior presence needed to truly challenge Boston.

Paul George added 18 points for Philadelphia, but outside of Maxey and George, the Sixers failed to generate consistent offense against Boston's suffocating defense. The team that stole Game 2 with a dominant performance in Boston now finds itself in a familiar hole, trailing a series to a team with more firepower, more depth, and more experience winning in big moments.

Embiid has not been given a timeline for return, with the team describing his recovery as progressing but not yet ready for game action. The Sixers' path forward without him is narrow.

Celtics' Championship DNA on Display

What made this win particularly telling was that Boston didn't dominate from start to finish. The 76ers pushed them throughout, and Philadelphia's home crowd kept the energy high well into the fourth quarter. Yet when the game tightened, the Celtics found a way.

Tatum's shooting from 3 was a statement in itself — he entered the game with the Sixers' defense specifically designed to push him away from the basket and make him work off the dribble. Instead, he beat them at their own game with ball movement and off-ball reads that produced clean looks all night. His Achilles recovery has now reached a point where opposing defenses can no longer count on him being a half-step slow.

Brown's scoring run was almost visceral — a reminder that despite the analytics-driven, system-first reputation Boston has built, this team still has players who can simply take over a game through sheer will and talent.

What's Next

Game 4 is Sunday in Philadelphia, where the 76ers will need a win to stay alive in the series. Joel Embiid's status remains the central question — if there is any possibility of him returning, it would be Game 4 or later. Without him, the Sixers must hope Maxey can continue his dominant individual play while getting more production from the rest of the roster. For the Celtics, a win Sunday would put them one game from advancing and ease what has been a cautious approach to Tatum's minutes all season.
