NBA Playoff Saturday: Thunder-Suns, Knicks-Hawks, Wolves-Nuggets Headline Loaded Slate

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 25, 2026

After a Friday night that featured overtime thrillers and stunning comebacks, Saturday offers another packed playoff slate with four games across four active first-round series. The action runs from midday to late night on NBC and ABC, and every game carries real stakes for how the remainder of the first round shakes out.

1 PM ET — Detroit Pistons at Orlando Magic (Game 3, NBC)

The Pistons and Magic have split the first two games of their series, with Orlando winning Game 1 wire-to-wire and Detroit bouncing back with a dominant Game 2 victory. Cade Cunningham had 27 points, 11 assists, and 6 rebounds in that Game 2 win, and the Pistons showed the defensive intensity that made them the No. 1 seed in the East. Now the series shifts to Amway Center in Orlando, where Paolo Banchero and the Magic will look to reclaim home-court advantage.

3:30 PM ET — Oklahoma City Thunder at Phoenix Suns (Game 3, NBC)

The Thunder have won both games in Oklahoma City by a combined 48 points, and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has been a monster throughout the series. OKC holds a 2-0 lead, but the series now moves to Phoenix, where the Suns will play before a home crowd for the first time. The Thunder's biggest challenge remains the absence of Jalen Williams, who left Game 2 with a left hamstring injury that could keep him out for weeks. Devin Booker and the Suns' offense will be looking to make OKC pay for the roster shortfall.

6 PM ET — Atlanta Hawks at New York Knicks (Game 4, NBC)

The most emotionally charged game of the day. The Knicks went to Atlanta and lost Games 1 and 3 on a combined last-second margin — including C.J. McCollum's now-famous fadeaway with 12.3 seconds left in Game 3 that gave the Hawks a 109-108 win and a 2-1 series lead. New York is under pressure at home: lose this game and face the prospect of elimination games in Atlanta. Jalen Brunson and OG Anunoby will need to perform at their highest level if the Knicks want to even the series.

8:30 PM ET — Denver Nuggets at Minnesota Timberwolves (Game 4, ABC)

The late game is the one everyone will be talking about. Anthony Edwards left Game 3 in the fourth quarter with a knee injury and played just 24 minutes, yet the Timberwolves still dominated the Nuggets 113-96 — holding Denver to just 11 points in the first quarter, a franchise postseason record. Edwards' status for Game 4 is uncertain, but if he plays, Minnesota will enter with an opportunity to take a commanding 3-1 series lead. Nikola Jokic went just 7-for-26 in Game 3, and the Nuggets have yet to figure out how to attack Minnesota's size and physicality. A 3-1 deficit would put Denver in serious trouble.

What's Next

Saturday's results will go a long way toward clarifying which teams are serious contenders and which are facing early exits. Monday's schedule is expected to include games for series that need them, and by the end of the weekend, the first round's landscape should be much clearer. Watch for Edwards' health, Jokic's response, and whether Booker can deliver a signature moment in front of a desperate Suns crowd.
