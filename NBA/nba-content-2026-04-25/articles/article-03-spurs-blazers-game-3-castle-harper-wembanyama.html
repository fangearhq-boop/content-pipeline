Castle 33, Harper 27: Spurs Stun Blazers 120-108 Without Wembanyama to Take 2-1 Lead

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 25, 2026

The San Antonio Spurs were down 15 points in the third quarter, on the road in Portland, without their franchise cornerstone Victor Wembanyama — and they found a way to win anyway. Stephon Castle exploded for 33 points and Dylan Harper added 27 points and 10 rebounds as the Spurs staged an epic comeback and defeated the Portland Trail Blazers 120-108 on Friday night to take a 2-1 lead in their first-round series.

Wembanyama sat out after entering concussion protocol following Game 2, when he tumbled face-first to the floor after contact from Jrue Holiday and struck his jaw on the hardwood. The fall was alarming, and while the 22-year-old superstar traveled with the team to Portland, he was unable to play. In his absence, the Spurs turned to their next generation of stars — and got the best performance they could have possibly hoped for.

The Comeback No One Saw Coming

Portland led 65-59 at halftime and extended that lead to 82-67 in the third quarter — a 15-point advantage that seemed to have the Blazers in full control. The home crowd at Moda Center was electric, sensing a chance to take a commanding series lead against a depleted San Antonio team.

And then the Spurs went on a 21-5 run.

Castle ignited the charge with his aggression and scoring — he attacked the basket, created mismatches, and refused to let the lead hold. Harper matched him with playmaking and shot creation that belied his rookie status. Luke Kornet, starting at center in Wembanyama's absence, contributed 14 points and 10 rebounds. By the time the third quarter ended, San Antonio had turned a 15-point deficit into an 88-87 lead — and they never relinquished it. The final score was 120-108, a victory that no one outside the Spurs' locker room had predicted was possible in this building without their best player.

Jrue Holiday led Portland with 29 points and played his heart out, but the Blazers' supporting cast could not match the energy or talent the Spurs brought from their two first-round picks.

Castle and Harper: The Future Arriving Early

Stephon Castle was the No. 4 overall pick in the 2024 NBA Draft. Dylan Harper was the No. 1 overall pick in 2025. On Friday night, they combined for 60 points in one of the most impactful performances by a young duo in playoff history. For a franchise that built its identity around Wembanyama's generational talent, seeing Castle and Harper rise to this occasion with him watching from the bench was both remarkable and deeply encouraging.

The Spurs have been built patiently, with an eye on sustained success rather than quick results. Friday's win showed that the infrastructure around Wembanyama is not merely complementary — it is competitive on its own terms.

What's Next

Game 4 is Sunday afternoon in San Antonio, and the biggest question surrounding the Spurs is whether Wembanyama will be available to play. He must clear every step of the NBA's return-to-participation protocol before being cleared, and while there are encouraging signs — he completed cardio and shooting work Thursday in San Antonio — there is no confirmed timeline. If he returns, San Antonio will be a heavy favorite to close out the series. If he cannot play, the Spurs have proven they can compete either way. The Trail Blazers, meanwhile, must find a way to generate more from their supporting cast if they hope to extend the series and force things back to Portland.
