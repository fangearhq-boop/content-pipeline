Tatum & Brown Each Score 25, Celtics Beat 76ers 108-100 in Game 3
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 9:00 AM ET The Boston Celtics showed exactly why they're considered championship contenders — even on the road and without Joel Embiid on the other side, they found a way to get it done. Jayson Tatum and Jaylen Brown each finished with 25 points Friday night in Philadelphia as the Celtics won Game 3, 108-100, and took a 2-1 series lead over the Sixers. Tatum was brilliant from beyond the arc, going 5-for-9 on 3-pointers in just his 19th game of the season following Achilles surgery. But it was Brown who delivered the knockout blow — he rattled off 8 consecutive points late in the fourth quarter to push Boston's advantage to 96-92 and take the wind out of Philly's sails. Payton Pritchard was a revelation off the bench, knocking down five 3-pointers for 15 points. Tyrese Maxey gave Philadelphia everything he had with 31 points, but Paul George (18 pts) was the only other Sixer to provide real offensive support. Joel Embiid continues to recover from his April 9 appendectomy and is not yet cleared to practice, much less play. The Sixers showed fight — winning Game 2 in Boston — but with their star center out, the margin for error against this Celtics team is razor thin. Game 4 is Sunday in Philadelphia. #HoopHeroes | #NBAPlayoffs

LeBron's Tying 3 + Smart's Historic Line — Lakers Beat Rockets 112-108 OT, Lead 3-0
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 11:00 AM ET LeBron James refused to let the Los Angeles Lakers lose. With Houston leading by six points and only 30 seconds remaining in regulation, the 41-year-old legend — who had already logged more than 45 minutes — stepped back and buried a tying 3-pointer with 13 seconds on the clock. The Toyota Center fell silent. The Lakers were going to overtime. And then Marcus Smart took over. Smart finished with 21 points, 10 assists, 5 steals, and 2 blocks — the first 20/10/5-steal/2-block playoff game since Michael Jordan accomplished it in 1991. He scored 8 of the Lakers' 11 overtime points, including a go-ahead 3-pointer to push the lead to 107-103. Rui Hachimura added 22 points as the Lakers won 112-108 and took a 3-0 series lead. Kevin Durant was ruled out with a left ankle sprain he suffered in Game 2, leaving Houston without its best player for the third time in four playoff games this series. The Rockets are now on the brink of elimination with no clear timeline on Durant's return. #HoopHeroes | #NBAPlayoffs

### Long-Form Post Window: 4:00 PM ET When Victor Wembanyama tumbled to the floor after contact from Jrue Holiday in Game 2, his jaw hitting the hardwood, the stakes of his absence became immediately clear. He entered the NBA's concussion protocol, missed Game 3 entirely on Friday night, and watched from the bench as the Spurs beat Portland 120-108 anyway. For one game, San Antonio answered the question nobody wanted to face: Can the Spurs survive without him? Castle and Harper combined for 60 points in a remarkable display. But concussion protocol isn't just a one-game thing. Wembanyama must clear every step of the NBA's return-to-participation program. He completed some cardio and shooting Thursday before the team departed — encouraging, but not definitive. Game 4 is Sunday afternoon in San Antonio. Wembanyama averaged 25 points, 11.5 rebounds, and 3.1 blocks this season and was the near-unanimous choice for Defensive Player of the Year. He is the most important player on this team, and San Antonio's ceiling in this series depends on his health. #HoopHeroes | #NBAPlayoffs
