Pritchard's 32, Franchise-Record 24 Threes Fuel Celtics Blowout as Embiid Returns

By Jake Torres | April 27, 2026

Joel Embiid returned from an appendectomy and put up 26 points, 10 rebounds and 6 assists in 34 minutes. It was an impressive performance for a player 17 days removed from surgery. It also didn't come close to being enough. The Boston Celtics set a franchise playoff record and demolished the 76ers 128-96, taking a commanding 3-1 series lead.

Payton Pritchard wrote the story of Game 4 off the Boston bench, erupting for a career playoff high 32 points and making six of the team's 24 three-pointers. That number — 24 made threes — is a Boston Celtics franchise record in a playoff game, eclipsing previous highs on 53 attempts from the field.

The Pritchard Explosion

Pritchard had scored as many as 23 points in a playoff game before Sunday. He matched that total before the first quarter ended, attacking the 76ers defense relentlessly and converting from long range. The Celtics took a 22-5 run to close the opening period, and once Boston established that kind of margin, Philadelphia was chasing all night.

The Celtics never led by fewer than 14 points after that first-quarter surge. By the time the fourth quarter arrived, the game was a formality. Pritchard's 32 points came through three quarters, and the Celtics had already put the contest away.

Jayson Tatum complemented Pritchard perfectly, finishing with 30 points and 11 assists, while Jaylen Brown added 20 points. Boston had three players eclipse 20 points while setting the franchise three-point record.

Embiid's Return Falls Flat

The Embiid storyline dominated the pregame conversation. Philadelphia's All-NBA center had been absent since having his appendix removed on April 9, missing seven games in the process. His return brought hope to a 76ers team that had won Game 2 at the TD Garden.

Embiid looked serviceable — 26 points and 10 rebounds is nothing to dismiss for a player on limited prep time — but the Celtics' shooting was simply too much to overcome. Boston drained threes from every position on the floor, and Philadelphia's defense had no consistent answer.

The 32-point margin of defeat was a sobering reality for a team now facing elimination. Philadelphia must win three straight games beginning with Game 5 to advance.

Boston's Championship Pedigree

The Celtics finished the regular season as the No. 2 seed in the Eastern Conference. Defending champion Boston has championship expectations, and a performance like Game 4 — setting franchise records while a rival's star player returns — is exactly the kind of statement a title contender makes.

The three-point barrage (24-for-53) will be remembered as one of the dominant shooting performances in recent playoff history. For context, the Celtics had gone 20-for-47 from three in Game 3, meaning they've made 44 threes in the last two games alone.

What's Next

Game 5 of the Celtics-76ers series will shift back to Boston, where the Celtics can close out the series and advance to the second round. Philadelphia will need a near-miraculous turnaround, starting with a healthier Embiid and a dramatically better defensive effort. Boston, meanwhile, enters the game with all the momentum — and a three-point record that will be hard to top any time soon.
