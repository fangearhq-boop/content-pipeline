Monday Playoff Preview: OKC Chases Sweep, Denver Faces Elimination, Magic Host Pistons

By Jake Torres | April 27, 2026

Sunday delivered four must-watch playoff games. Monday brings three more — and each one carries series-altering stakes. From Orlando to Oklahoma City to Minneapolis, tonight's slate may determine which teams advance and which go home.

Magic vs. Pistons — Game 4 (8 PM ET, NBC)

Orlando leads this series 2-1 after taking Game 3 on the road behind 25 points from Paolo Banchero and 25 from George Bane. The Magic host Detroit on Monday and have a chance to take a commanding series advantage before the matchup returns to Michigan.

The Pistons won Game 2 at Little Caesars Arena to level the series, proving they can compete — but Orlando's size and athleticism have been the dominant factor when the Magic are firing on all cylinders. Cade Cunningham will need another big performance to keep Detroit's season alive.

Thunder vs. Suns — Game 4 (9:30 PM ET, Peacock)

Oklahoma City is three wins deep and one win away from a first-round sweep. The Thunder won Games 1, 2 and 3 against the Phoenix Suns, with Shai Gilgeous-Alexander putting up monster numbers all series. SGA had a playoff career-high 42 points in Game 3 — shooting 15-of-18 from the field — and has been the most dominant player in this year's playoffs.

For Phoenix, this is a must-win game in a season that has already fallen below expectations. Devin Booker and the Suns will need a complete team performance to simply extend the series. OKC lost Jalen Williams to a hamstring injury early in the series, but it hasn't slowed them down at all.

This series has felt one-sided since the opening tip. The question is whether Phoenix has enough to force a Game 5.

Nuggets vs. Timberwolves — Game 5 (10:30 PM ET, NBC)

The Minnesota Timberwolves lead this series 3-1 and have a chance to eliminate the defending champion Denver Nuggets on Monday night. After Naz Reid's consistent contributions and a historic 43-point bench performance from Ayo Dosunmu in Game 4 — the best reserve playoff game in 50 years — Minnesota is playing its best basketball of the season.

The concern for the Timberwolves is health. Anthony Edwards left Game 4 with a knee injury, and Dario DiVincenzo also exited that game hurt. Their availability for Game 5 will be closely monitored before tip-off.

For Denver, Nikola Jokic must respond to a difficult Game 3 shooting performance and carry the Nuggets to a victory that keeps their championship defense alive. In Jokic's playoff history, elimination-game performances have been among his best. If there's a team that can trust their superstar to deliver when everything is on the line, it's Denver.

What's Next

Monday's results will significantly shape the rest of the first round. If OKC sweeps the Suns and Minnesota eliminates Denver, the Western Conference field becomes clearer with the Thunder and Timberwolves waiting for their next opponents. The Magic-Pistons series, meanwhile, could see one team take decisive control with a win tonight. All three games tip off in prime time — don't miss any of it.
