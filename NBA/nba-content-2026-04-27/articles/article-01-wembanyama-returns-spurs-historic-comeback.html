Wembanyama Returns, Spurs Make NBA Playoff History with Stunning Comeback

By Damon Pierce | April 27, 2026

Victor Wembanyama cleared concussion protocol and started Game 4 against the Portland Trail Blazers — then proceeded to put together one of the most dominant individual performances in recent playoff memory. The San Antonio Spurs didn't just win on Sunday. They made history.

Wembanyama posted 27 points, 11 rebounds, 7 blocks, 4 steals and 3 assists in the Spurs' 114-93 victory to take a 3-1 series lead. That stat line made him only the 10th player ever — and the youngest — to record those kinds of combined numbers in an NBA playoff game.

The Most Unlikely Comeback

Before Wemby's individual brilliance took center stage, the Spurs had a massive problem. At halftime, San Antonio trailed 58-41 — a 17-point deficit on the road in a crucial Game 4. The Trail Blazers had controlled the first half and looked ready to tie the series.

What followed in the second half was extraordinary. The Spurs outscored Portland 73-35 over the final two quarters, completing a comeback that had never been accomplished in NBA playoff history. San Antonio became the first team ever to trail by 15 or more points at halftime and win by 15 or more points, finishing with a 21-point margin of victory.

De'Aaron Fox played a central role in the turnaround. The veteran guard put up 28 points on 11-of-17 shooting, adding 6 rebounds, 7 assists, a steal and 2 blocks — a stat combination that had never been accomplished by a guard in Spurs franchise history.

Wemby's Statement Game

Wembanyama's concussion had sidelined him in Game 3, a game the Spurs still won 120-108 thanks to Stephon Castle's 33 points and Jeremy Sochan's contributions. But there's no replacing a generational talent, and Wemby's return gave San Antonio a completely different dimension.

The 7-foot-4 Frenchman dominated on both ends from the opening tip. His 7 blocks were a reminder of why he was the near-unanimous Defensive Player of the Year during the regular season, when he averaged 3.1 blocks per game. On offense, Wemby found his rhythm from the midrange and at the rim, converting efficiently for his 27 points.

Together, Fox and Wembanyama formed an unstoppable two-headed attack that Portland had no answer for in the second half.

Series Context

The Spurs now lead the first-round series 3-1 and will look to close it out in Game 5. San Antonio finished the regular season with the No. 2 seed in the Western Conference at 62-20, and this team has shown remarkable depth throughout the playoffs even when Wembanyama wasn't available.

Portland has now lost three of four games in the series and faces a steep climb to extend the year. The Trail Blazers will need to find answers they haven't had for most of this matchup.

What's Next

Game 5 of the Spurs-Trail Blazers series is scheduled for later this week. If San Antonio closes it out, they will advance to face the winner of the Oklahoma City Thunder-Phoenix Suns series, where OKC is on the verge of completing a sweep. The Western Conference is shaping up to be a historically competitive bracket, and the Spurs — fully healthy with Wembanyama — look like one of the most dangerous teams remaining.
