Rockets Rout Lakers 115-96 to Avoid Sweep as LeBron Struggles

By Damon Pierce | April 27, 2026

LeBron James rescued the Lakers in Game 3 with a tying three-pointer in the final 13 seconds of regulation and a memorable overtime performance. Game 4 was a different story entirely. LeBron shot 2-for-9 from the field for just 10 points, and the Houston Rockets responded with a dominant 115-96 victory to stave off elimination and cut the series to 3-1.

Without Kevin Durant — who has been sidelined by a left ankle sprain — the Rockets leaned on collective firepower to deliver the kind of performance that keeps their season alive. This was a team win in every sense of the phrase.

Rockets' Starting Five Delivers

Every Houston starter reached at least 16 points as the Rockets overwhelmed the Lakers with depth and energy. Amen Thompson led the team with 23 points, while Tari Eason added 20. Alperen Sengun contributed 19 points and Reed Sheppard finished with 17 in a collective statement game.

Houston led by nine at halftime and built the advantage to 90-65 entering the fourth quarter. By that point, the outcome was decided. The Rockets controlled tempo, generated easy looks and shot efficiently throughout, refusing to let the Lakers find any rhythm.

A Rough Night for the Lakers

Beyond LeBron's 2-of-9 shooting, the Lakers saw their three-point shooting completely abandon them. Los Angeles made just five threes in Game 4 after combining for 35 in the first three games. Without any perimeter pop, the Lakers' offense was predictable and easy to defend.

DeAndre Ayton was ejected in the third quarter for a Flagrant Foul 2, leaving the Lakers shorthanded in the paint for much of the second half. James finished with 9 assists and 8 turnovers alongside his 10-point night — a far cry from the takeover performance the team needed.

The silver lining for Los Angeles is that they still hold a 3-1 series advantage. The Lakers have not been swept out of a first-round series in LeBron's time with the franchise, and that track record provides some comfort.

Houston's Identity Without Durant

Kevin Durant's absence has forced the Rockets to play through their young core, and on Sunday that group responded emphatically. Thompson, Eason, Sengun and Sheppard collectively outplayed one of the league's most storied franchises by 19 points. If Houston can maintain that level of play and get Durant back healthy, they become a genuinely dangerous team.

What's Next

Game 5 is scheduled for Wednesday night at Crypto.com Arena in Los Angeles. The Lakers will play with the chance to close out the series at home — but they must rediscover the shooting touch and LeBron's form that made them so competitive in the first three games. For Houston, Wednesday is an opportunity to extend the series further and see how much fight this young roster truly has. The Rockets made a statement Sunday. Now they have to back it up.
