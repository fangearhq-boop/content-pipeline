Wembanyama Returns, Spurs Rally from Down 17 to Beat Blazers 114-93 (Game 4, Tier 1)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 9:00 AM ET Victor Wembanyama didn't just return from a concussion — he made NBA history. Wemby cleared concussion protocol and started Game 4 against Portland, then put up 27 points, 11 rebounds, 7 blocks, 4 steals and 3 assists in San Antonio's 114-93 victory. That stat line made him the youngest player — and only the 10th — ever to record those numbers in an NBA playoff game. The story, though, wasn't just about Wemby. The Spurs were down 17 points at halftime, trailing 58-41, and faced elimination pressure on the road. What happened in the second half defied playoff history: San Antonio outscored Portland 73-35 over the final two quarters, completing a comeback that had never been done before. The Spurs became the first team in NBA playoff history to trail by 15 or more at halftime and win by 15 or more. De'Aaron Fox was right there alongside Wemby — 28 points on 11-of-17 shooting, 6 rebounds, 7 assists, a steal and 2 blocks. That combination had never been accomplished by a guard in franchise history. San Antonio leads 3-1 and is one win away from the second round. With Wembanyama healthy and Fox at his best, this team is a real threat in the Western Conference. #HoopHeroes | #NBAPlayoffs

### Long-Form Post Window: 1:00 PM ET Joel Embiid came back from his appendectomy after 17 days. He scored 26 points, grabbed 10 rebounds and dished 6 assists in 34 minutes. And it didn't matter at all. The Boston Celtics absolutely dismantled the 76ers 128-96 in Game 4, setting a franchise playoff record in the process. Boston made 24 of 53 three-point attempts — the most threes in a single playoff game in Celtics history. Payton Pritchard was the catalyst. The reserve guard erupted for a career playoff-high 32 points, converting six of those record-breaking threes. He had 13 points by the end of the first quarter alone as the Celtics opened the game with a 22-5 run. After that, Boston never led by fewer than 14 points. Jayson Tatum added 30 points and 11 assists. Jaylen Brown chipped in 20. Three Celtics topped 20 points while their bench player set the franchise record. Boston leads 3-1 and needs one win to advance. Philadelphia, even with its star player back, has no answer for this Celtics machine right now. #HoopHeroes | #NBAPlayoffs
