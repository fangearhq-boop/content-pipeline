Jalen Brunson Named 2026 ECF MVP: First Knick Since Patrick Ewing in 1999

By Marcus Cole | May 26, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Jalen Brunson has been named the 2026 Eastern Conference Finals Most Valuable Player, capping one of the most complete series performances in recent Knicks playoff history. Brunson averaged 25.5 points, 7.8 assists, and 3.3 rebounds on 48.7 percent shooting across four games against the Cleveland Cavaliers — numbers that led New York to its first NBA Finals appearance in 27 years.

He is the first New York Knick to earn this award since Patrick Ewing in 1999, the last time the franchise reached the Finals.

A Series for the Ages

Brunson's four-game run against Cleveland told the story of a player who rises to the occasion. In Game 1, trailing 93-71 with 7:52 left, he scored 15 points in the fourth quarter and overtime — including the floater that tied the game — to ignite a 44-11 Knicks run in one of the great comebacks in ECF history. The Knicks won in overtime, 115-104.

Game 2 brought a different dimension of his game. Brunson distributed a career-high 14 assists while adding 19 points in a 109-93 victory. He manipulated the Cavaliers' defense as a passer, finding cutters and shooters for easy looks all night long.

In Game 3, he shouldered more of the scoring load with 30 points in a 121-108 road win. By Game 4, with a sweep in reach, Brunson contributed 15 points in a 130-93 blowout where the entire roster shared the load — a sign of a team so confident that its star could step back and let the depth shine.

The Ewing Connection

The last time the Knicks went to the Finals, Patrick Ewing was their franchise cornerstone and the ECF MVP. Ewing never won a championship. Brunson, 13 years younger than Ewing was during that 1999 run, now has the chance to finish what his predecessor could not.

The parallels extend beyond the award. Both players were the unquestioned engines of their respective teams. Both faced skepticism before the playoffs — Brunson's ceiling was questioned coming into this postseason after regular-season struggles. He answered with the best basketball of his career.

The difference is that the 2026 Knicks are entering the Finals having swept three consecutive playoff series on an 11-game win streak, with the best offensive rating in the 2026 postseason at 130.5. The 1999 Knicks were a No. 8 seed who scraped to the Finals. These Knicks are a different team.

What's Next

Brunson and the Knicks now await the winner of tonight's Game 5 between the Oklahoma City Thunder and San Antonio Spurs (8:30 PM ET, Paycom Center, NBC/Peacock). The NBA Finals begin June 3 at 8:30 PM ET at the Western Conference champion's home arena.

If the Knicks' ECF run is any indication, Brunson is not done adding to his legacy. The question is whether he can extend it all the way to a championship — and give New York a moment that Patrick Ewing never could.
