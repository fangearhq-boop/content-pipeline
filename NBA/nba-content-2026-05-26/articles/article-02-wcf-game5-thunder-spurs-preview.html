WCF Game 5 Preview: Thunder vs. Spurs Tonight at Paycom Center

By Jake Torres | May 26, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Western Conference Finals returns to Oklahoma City on Tuesday night for what figures to be a decisive Game 5. The Oklahoma City Thunder host the San Antonio Spurs at Paycom Center with tip-off set for 8:30 PM ET on NBC/Peacock, the series knotted at two games apiece after the Spurs' stunning 103-82 victory in San Antonio on Sunday.

Oklahoma City enters as 5.5-point favorites with moneyline odds of -218. The Spurs, who dominated from the opening tip in Game 4, come in as +180 underdogs. The over/under sits at 215.5 combined points.

What makes tonight critical beyond the series situation: the winner is headed to the NBA Finals to face the New York Knicks, who swept the Cleveland Cavaliers on Monday night.

The Injury Factor

Oklahoma City's path back to the NBA Finals may depend on the health of Jalen Williams. The third-year forward, already dealing with a hamstring injury that has bothered him throughout the series, is listed as questionable for Game 5. Ajay Mitchell remains out.

Williams is not the team's leading scorer — that's Shai Gilgeous-Alexander — but his two-way versatility and rebounding presence matter enormously. In games where Williams has been limited or absent, the Thunder's margin for error against Wembanyama shrinks considerably.

San Antonio enters healthy and with the confidence of a team that executed its best game of the series on the road. Victor Wembanyama (33 points, 8 rebounds, 5 assists, 3 blocks) and De'Aaron Fox (12 points, 10 rebounds) led an effort that held OKC to 33 percent from the field and 18 percent from three-point range.

SGA's Bounce-Back Opportunity

The series narrative in Game 5 starts and ends with Shai Gilgeous-Alexander. The two-time NBA MVP managed just 19 points on 6-of-15 shooting in Sunday's loss — his worst performance of the 2026 playoffs. The Spurs defended him with disciplined man-to-man coverage and light help, taking away his driving lanes while daring him to settle for mid-range jumpers.

Expect OKC coach Mark Daigneault and his staff to have adjustments ready. The Thunder are 8-0 in the pre-series portion of these playoffs, and SGA averaged 31.1 points per game during the regular season. Games this close tend to hinge on stars playing like stars.

The home crowd at Paycom Center, which has been one of the louder environments in the league all season, gives OKC a real advantage. The Spurs, however, have already shown they don't wilt in hostile arenas — they've won on the road in this series and know what it takes to survive in Oklahoma City.

What's Next

A Thunder win tonight sets up a Game 6 in San Antonio on Thursday, May 28, before a potential Game 7 returns to OKC. A Spurs victory would give San Antonio a commanding 3-2 series advantage, with their home crowd potentially closing things out Friday.

Either way, the NBA Finals begin June 3 at 8:30 PM ET at the Western Conference champion's home arena. The Knicks are waiting. The question is who joins them.

Game 5 tips off at 8:30 PM ET tonight. Watch on NBC or stream on Peacock.
