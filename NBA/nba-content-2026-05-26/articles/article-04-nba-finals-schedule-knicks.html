2026 NBA Finals: Full Schedule, Matchup Preview and What to Expect

By Damon Pierce | May 26, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The New York Knicks are headed to the 2026 NBA Finals. Now comes the waiting — and the previewing. While the Knicks swept the Cleveland Cavaliers Monday night to complete the Eastern Conference Finals, the Western Conference is still being decided. Oklahoma City and San Antonio play Game 5 tonight with the series tied 2-2. Whoever survives will meet New York starting June 3.

Here is everything you need to know about the 2026 NBA Finals.

Full Finals Schedule

Game 1: Wednesday, June 3 at 8:30 PM ET — At Oklahoma City or San Antonio

Game 2: Friday, June 5 at 8:30 PM ET — At Oklahoma City or San Antonio

Game 3: Monday, June 8 at 8:30 PM ET — At Madison Square Garden, New York

Game 4: Wednesday, June 10 at 8:30 PM ET — At Madison Square Garden, New York

Game 5: Saturday, June 13 at 8:30 PM ET — At Oklahoma City or San Antonio (if needed)

Game 6: Tuesday, June 16 at 8:30 PM ET — At Madison Square Garden (if needed)

The Home-Court Question

The Knicks will be road underdogs in the NBA Finals regardless of their opponent. New York finished the regular season 53-29 — a strong record, but trailing both the Oklahoma City Thunder (64-18) and San Antonio Spurs (62-20). Home-court advantage goes to the Western Conference champion.

That means Games 1, 2, and a potential Game 5 will take place in Oklahoma City or San Antonio, with New York hosting Games 3, 4, and a possible Game 6 at Madison Square Garden. Madison Square Garden hosting Finals games for the first time in 27 years will be one of the defining moments of the 2026 NBA season regardless of the outcome.

Who Would the Knicks Prefer?

Both potential opponents present significant challenges. The Thunder are the defending regular-season leaders and boast a two-time MVP in Shai Gilgeous-Alexander. Their defense is elite and their depth — particularly off the bench — has been a problem for every team they've faced.

The Spurs bring Victor Wembanyama, the 22-year-old phenomenon who was named the 2026 Defensive Player of the Year unanimously and is putting up historic numbers in the playoffs. Their playoff run has been defined by Wembanyama's dominance on both ends, with de facto leadership from De'Aaron Fox running the offense.

The Knicks have the 11-game win streak and the confidence. The West has the home court and the talent. This Finals shapes up to be one worth watching.

What's Next

The Western Conference Finals continues tonight with Game 5 at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City at 8:30 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. The series is tied 2-2. The NBA Finals begin June 3 — and for the first time since 1999, New York will be there.
