11 Straight: The 2026 Knicks' Historic Playoff Run by the Numbers

By Jake Torres | May 26, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

What the New York Knicks have accomplished over the last few weeks deserves more than a bracket update. Their 130-93 win over the Cleveland Cavaliers in Game 4 of the Eastern Conference Finals extended their postseason winning streak to 11 consecutive games — a figure that places them in rarefied NBA air.

Only three other teams in NBA history have won 11 or more consecutive games during a single postseason run. The 2026 Knicks are the fourth.

The Numbers Behind the Run

The 2026 Knicks' postseason numbers are not just good — they are historically rare. Their offensive rating of 130.5 points per 100 possessions is the best among all teams in the 2026 playoffs. They have swept three consecutive opponents, dispatching Atlanta, Philadelphia, and Cleveland without a single defeat.

In the closeout game alone, the Knicks' numbers were staggering. They outscored the Cavaliers 32-5 in second-chance points — a complete dominance of the offensive glass that reflected how Cleveland had mentally and physically checked out by the final minutes. The fast-break advantage was 33-9. The final margin was 37 points.

These are not numbers from a team that got lucky in a close series. These are the numbers of a team operating at peak efficiency across an entire postseason.

How They Got Here

The streak did not begin quietly. In Game 1 of the Eastern Conference Finals, the Knicks trailed Cleveland by 22 points in the fourth quarter before Jalen Brunson and company produced a 44-11 run to force overtime — and ultimately win. That comeback was as much a statement about this team's mental fortitude as any blowout could be.

From there, it was dominance. Game 2 saw Brunson distribute a career-high 14 assists in a 16-point win. Game 3 was a road win in Cleveland, 121-108. Game 4 was the sweep-clinching blowout.

The offense that Jalen Brunson, Karl-Anthony Towns, and Mikal Bridges have built together is the foundation of this run. Towns stretches the floor and rebounds at an elite rate. Bridges defends at a high level and converts when needed. Brunson drives the entire system.

What's Next

The Knicks now wait to learn their Finals opponent. Oklahoma City and San Antonio play Game 5 tonight (8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock) with the WCF tied 2-2. The NBA Finals begin June 3.

No NBA team has ever entered the Finals on an 11-game win streak and failed to win the championship. Whether or not that holds true in 2026 remains to be written — but the 2026 Knicks have given New York every reason to believe that streak does not stop until they raise the trophy.
