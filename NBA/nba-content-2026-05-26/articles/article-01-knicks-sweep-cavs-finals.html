Knicks Sweep Cavaliers 130-93, Advance to First NBA Finals Since 1999

By Damon Pierce | May 26, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Twenty-seven years of waiting ended Monday night in Cleveland. The New York Knicks routed the Cavaliers 130-93 in Game 4 of the Eastern Conference Finals, completing a four-game sweep and punching their ticket to the NBA Finals for the first time since 1999.

The margin said everything. New York led by as many as 45 points, entering halftime ahead 68-49 and never looking back. Karl-Anthony Towns anchored a dominant performance with 19 points and 14 rebounds, while Landry Shamet added 16 off the bench. Jalen Brunson and Mikal Bridges each contributed 15 points in what became a six-man scoring outburst.

Donovan Mitchell poured in 31 for Cleveland — a valiant effort on a night when virtually nothing else worked for the Cavaliers. The loss sent Cleveland to a humiliating finish: swept in a playoff series for the first time since the 2018 NBA Finals when LeBron James' squad fell to the Golden State Warriors.

A Night New York Will Never Forget

The Knicks dominated in every category that matters for a closeout game. They outscored Cleveland 32-5 in second-chance points and held a 33-9 advantage in fast-break points. Those numbers don't happen by accident — they reflect a team playing with overwhelming confidence and physicality at the most important moments of the season.

The win extended New York's playoff winning streak to 11 games, making the Knicks just the fourth team in NBA history to win 11 consecutive games during a single postseason run. Along the way, they swept the Atlanta Hawks in the first round, dispatched the Philadelphia 76ers in the second, and now leave no doubt about their ECF dominance.

The Knicks' 130.5 offensive rating this postseason stands as the best among all 2026 playoff teams — a number that signals not just health and momentum, but a system operating near its ceiling.

Brunson Seals the Legacy Moment

Jalen Brunson, who was later named the 2026 Eastern Conference Finals MVP, was the heartbeat of this series even when the box score didn't demand it. He averaged 25.5 points, 7.8 assists, and 3.3 rebounds on 48.7 percent shooting across four games against one of the East's best defensive teams.

In Game 1, he willed New York out of a 22-point deficit with 15 points in the fourth quarter and overtime, capping the comeback with a floater that tied the game. In Game 2, he distributed a career-high 14 assists. In Game 3, he posted 30. And in Monday's closeout, he did what great players do — managed the moment, let teammates shine, and got his team home.

He became the first New York Knick to earn Eastern Conference Finals MVP honors since Patrick Ewing in 1999. The symmetry with the previous Finals run is not lost on a fanbase that has waited a generation for this.

What's Next

The Knicks now await the winner of the Western Conference Finals between the Oklahoma City Thunder and San Antonio Spurs, who play a pivotal Game 5 on Tuesday night at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City (8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock) with the series tied 2-2.

The 2026 NBA Finals are scheduled to begin Wednesday, June 3 at 8:30 PM ET at either Oklahoma City or San Antonio. Games 3 and 4 will be held at Madison Square Garden on June 8 and June 10. The Knicks will enter as road underdogs — both the Thunder (64-18) and Spurs (62-20) posted better regular-season records than New York's 53-29.

That won't matter to a team that has won 11 straight. The Knicks are going to the Finals, and they look every bit like a championship contender.
