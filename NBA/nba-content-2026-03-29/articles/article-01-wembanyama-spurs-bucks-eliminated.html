Wembanyama Dominates as Spurs Crush Bucks 127-95, Milwaukee Officially Eliminated

March 29, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The San Antonio Spurs didn't just beat the Milwaukee Bucks on Thursday night. They ended their season.

Victor Wembanyama delivered another dominant performance as San Antonio rolled to a 127-95 victory over Milwaukee at Frost Bank Centre, a 32-point blowout that officially eliminated the Bucks from playoff contention. With the loss, Milwaukee joins the growing list of teams that will be watching April basketball from home.

San Antonio improves to 54-18, the No. 2 seed in the Western Conference — a position the organization has held with remarkable consistency since Wembanyama began asserting himself as the best player in the league.

Wembanyama Builds His MVP Case

The performance came one day after Wembanyama jumped to No. 1 on the NBA's official Kia MVP Ladder, surpassing Oklahoma City's Shai Gilgeous-Alexander. The timing was not accidental.

Wembanyama is averaging 24.2 points, 11.2 rebounds, 3.1 blocks, and 1.1 steals per game this season — the most complete two-way stat line in the NBA. His plus-minus rating of plus-16.9 leads the league among top candidates, dwarfing SGA's plus-7.8.

Dylan Harper, Stephon Castle, and Gary Trent Jr. contributed to a balanced Spurs attack, but the evening belonged to Wembanyama — a performance that gave MVP voters even more to think about with three weeks remaining in the regular season.

The Bucks' Disappointing Exit

For Milwaukee, the ending was painful. The Bucks — NBA champions just five seasons ago — finish their 2025-26 campaign without a playoff appearance, a collapse that will define a difficult offseason of reflection.

Giannis Antetokounmpo's knee injury, sustained on March 15 and described as a bone bruise from left knee hyperextension, robbed Milwaukee of its cornerstone for a critical stretch run. But the injury was the final chapter of a longer story: a roster that couldn't find the right supporting cast around its franchise player and a front office that ran out of answers.

The questions now will come fast. What does the Bucks' future look like? Is this the end of the Giannis era in Milwaukee? Those answers will take months to sort out — but Thursday's loss ensured there are no more chances to change the narrative this season.

San Antonio's Remarkable Rise

The other side of this story belongs to the Spurs. Two seasons ago, San Antonio was a lottery team rebuilding around Wembanyama's extraordinary talent. Today, they are the No. 2 seed in the West with the youngest MVP frontrunner in the league.

The Spurs' 22-2 record since February is the best stretch in the league over that span. Their net rating with Wembanyama on the court is the highest of any player-team combination in basketball. They've beaten OKC's Thunder four times in five meetings — the same Thunder team sitting at 57-15 with the No. 1 overall seed.

This franchise's turnaround is complete, and it has happened faster than anyone imagined.

What's Next

The Spurs will close the final two weeks of the regular season aiming to lock in their playoff bracket position and keep Wembanyama healthy. With the No. 2 seed in hand, San Antonio's first-round opponent will be determined by how the rest of the West shakes out — likely either a Play-In survivor or one of the teams battling for the No. 7 seed.

For Milwaukee, the offseason begins immediately. The Bucks face pivotal decisions about Giannis's contract, the roster's direction, and whether the current front office and coaching staff remain in place to begin the next phase of the franchise's rebuild.

The regular season ends April 12. The Play-In Tournament runs April 14-17. Playoffs begin April 18.
