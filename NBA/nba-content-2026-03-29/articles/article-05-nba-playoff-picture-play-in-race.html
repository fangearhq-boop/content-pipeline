NBA Playoff Picture: Bucks Eliminated, Play-In Fields Set — Who's In, Who's Out

March 29, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

With 14 games remaining in the regular season, the 2026 NBA playoff picture is coming into sharp focus. The Milwaukee Bucks were officially eliminated from contention Thursday night after a 127-95 loss to San Antonio, while the top three seeds in both conferences have already punched their playoff tickets.

What's left is a compelling collection of seeding battles, Play-In races, and elimination scenarios that will play out over the final two weeks of the regular season.

Western Conference

Three teams have clinched postseason berths in the West: the Oklahoma City Thunder (57-15, No. 1 seed), the San Antonio Spurs (54-18, No. 2 seed), and the Los Angeles Lakers (46-26, No. 3 seed). Below them, the race is tight.

The Denver Nuggets (44-28) and Minnesota Timberwolves (44-28) are tied for the No. 4-5 seeds, with the Houston Rockets (43-28) just one game behind. Anthony Edwards' knee injury — out 1-2 weeks — gives Denver a potential opening to gain ground. Jokic's four-game triple-double streak has Denver clicking at the right time.

The West Play-In is shaping up as a battle between the Phoenix Suns (40-32, No. 7) and the LA Clippers (36-36, No. 8), with the Portland Trail Blazers and Golden State Warriors (both around 34-38) fighting for the No. 9-10 spots. The Warriors' 36-game journey through the Play-In qualifier is a stark reminder of how far that organization has fallen from its dynasty peak.

Eastern Conference

The East is equally stratified at the top. The Detroit Pistons (52-19, No. 1 seed) have had a remarkable season — a franchise-altering campaign that ended a long playoff drought and restored pride to the Motor City. The Boston Celtics (47-24, No. 2) and New York Knicks (47-25, No. 3) are separated by a single game as they battle for East seeding positioning.

The Cleveland Cavaliers (44-27) and Toronto Raptors (40-31) have staked out the No. 4-5 positions, while a fascinating three-team scrum for the No. 6-8 seeds involves the Atlanta Hawks (40-32), Philadelphia 76ers (39-33), and Charlotte Hornets (approximately 38-33).

The East Play-In race is the most competitive in recent memory. The 76ers face the Hornets for the No. 7-8 matchup, while the Orlando Magic (38-33) and Miami Heat (38-34) are fighting for No. 9-10. A healthy Joel Embiid makes Philadelphia dangerous; a Charlotte team that has exceeded expectations all season is the sentimental favorite among neutral fans.

Milwaukee's Exit

The Bucks' elimination on March 28 closed a painful chapter for one of the league's most celebrated recent franchises. Milwaukee won the NBA championship in 2021 behind Giannis Antetokounmpo's iconic Finals performance, but the years since have been a gradual decline — roster turnover, injuries, and an inability to build a sustainable supporting cast around their superstar.

Giannis' knee injury in March, which ended his availability for the season's final stretch, was the final blow. Milwaukee will enter the offseason facing fundamental questions about the organization's direction — and whether the Giannis era in Wisconsin has reached its natural end.

What's Next

The NBA regular season concludes April 12. The Play-In Tournament begins April 14, with games through April 17, determining the No. 7 and No. 8 seeds in each conference. First-round playoff games begin April 18 and run through early May.

The next two weeks will see every game carry playoff seeding weight. Teams fighting for home-court advantage, better matchups, and Play-In survival will be playing with maximum urgency — making late March and early April some of the most compelling basketball of the entire season.
