Edwards Out 1-2 Weeks: Minnesota's Seeding Battle Gets Complicated

March 29, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Minnesota Timberwolves received unwelcome news ahead of their Sunday games: Anthony Edwards is out with right knee inflammation, with a re-evaluation scheduled in one to two weeks.

The timing is difficult. Minnesota sits at 44-28, tied with the Denver Nuggets for the No. 4-5 seeds in the Western Conference — a seeding gap that carries enormous playoff implications. With 14 games remaining in the regular season and the postseason beginning April 18, the Timberwolves will need to navigate the next stretch without their most important player.

The Seeding Stakes

The difference between the No. 4 and No. 5 seeds in the West will determine who the Timberwolves face in the first round of the playoffs. The No. 4 seed plays the No. 5 seed's theoretical opponent — meaning the distinction between facing a first-round matchup against a Play-In survivor versus a higher seed matters enormously.

The Rockets (43-28) are just one game behind both Minnesota and Denver, adding further urgency. A soft stretch of scheduling without Edwards could see the Wolves drop from No. 4 to No. 5 or even No. 6, fundamentally changing their playoff path.

Rudy Gobert's defensive anchor becomes even more critical in Edwards' absence. The Timberwolves are one of the better defensive teams in the league regardless of personnel, but their offensive generation — without Edwards driving, shooting off the dribble, and creating for others — will be compromised.

The Silver Lining

A 1-2 week timeline, if accurate, puts Edwards' return date anywhere from April 5 to April 12 — the final day of the regular season. That window would give him limited regular season games to re-acclimate, but could have him healthy in time for Game 1 of the playoffs.

The Timberwolves have shown they can win without Edwards before. Earlier this season, they went 52-19 through a stretch in which Cade Cunningham's Pistons were the talk of the East — partly because Minnesota had proved capable of maintaining their defensive identity regardless of who was on the floor.

Whether that level of play can be sustained in a meaningfully competitive stretch of games against playoff-caliber opponents remains to be seen.

What It Means for Denver

The Nuggets are the direct beneficiaries of Edwards' absence. Denver enters March 29 with zero players on its injury report — a remarkable fact at this stage of the season — and plays the Golden State Warriors on Sunday night. If the Nuggets go on a run while Minnesota treads water without Edwards, they could emerge from this stretch with sole possession of the No. 4 seed.

Nikola Jokic's four-game triple-double streak (25.3 PPG, 16.8 RPG, 15.5 APG) has Denver playing as well as any team in the league right now. The confluence of Jokic's dominance and Edwards' absence could define the West's mid-seeding race.

What's Next

The Timberwolves are scheduled to play the Detroit Pistons on March 28 (without Edwards) and will face a string of games in the next two weeks that will test their depth. Minnesota's next marquee matchup is a critical game against the Rockets, with No. 5 vs. No. 6 seeding implications.

Edwards' formal re-evaluation date will be closely watched league-wide. A healthy return before the playoffs would reset Minnesota's ceiling. An extended absence — or, worse, a setback — would fundamentally change the Wolves' outlook for the 2026 postseason.
