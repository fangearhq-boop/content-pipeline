Jokic's Efficiency Masterclass: 33/15/12 on 13-of-16 Shooting, 4th Straight Triple-Double

March 29, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

There are no superlatives left for Nikola Jokic. The numbers have to speak for themselves.

On Thursday night, the Denver Nuggets center went 13-of-16 from the floor, finishing with 33 points, 15 rebounds, and 12 assists as Denver defeated the Utah Jazz 135-129. It was Jokic's fourth consecutive triple-double — a stretch that has produced averages of 25.3 points, 16.8 rebounds, and 15.5 assists per game with no real statistical precedent in NBA history.

The efficiency was the story within the story. Thirteen made field goals on 16 attempts is a 81.3 percent clip — the kind of number you associate with layup drills, not 33-point performances against professional NBA defenders.

A Season Unlike Any Other for a Big Man

Jokic's full-season averages of 28.0 points, 12.6 rebounds, and 10.6 assists per game represent something that has never been done in NBA history: a player averaging a triple-double for an entire season from the center position, with a scoring average north of 25 points.

For context, the greatest triple-double seasons in history belong to Oscar Robertson, Russell Westbrook, and Jokic himself — but none of them came attached to Jokic's combination of scoring and interior dominance. Robertson averaged 30.8 points per game during his 1961-62 triple-double season, but he was a guard. Westbrook's 2016-17 triple-double season came with averages of 31.6 points — but with a field goal percentage that paled next to Jokic's career-long consistency.

Jokic is a center who can run a point guard's offense, a scorer who can finish at every level, and a rebounder who cleans the glass with 12-plus per game. The combination should be impossible. It is not.

The Four-Game Stretch in Focus

Over his past four games — March 23 through March 28 — Jokic has produced the following:

March 23 vs. Portland: 22 points, 14 rebounds, 14 assists. March 25 vs. Phoenix: 23 points, 17 rebounds, 17 assists. March 26 vs. Dallas: 23 points, 21 rebounds, 19 assists — coming within one assist of his second career 20-20-20 game. March 28 vs. Utah: 33 points, 15 rebounds, 12 assists on 13-of-16 shooting.

Denver has won all four games. The Nuggets sit at 44-28, tied with Minnesota for the No. 4-5 seeds in the Western Conference with two weeks of regular season remaining.

The MVP Conversation

Jokic's statistical dominance has not been enough to wrestle the MVP conversation away from Wembanyama and SGA, primarily because Denver has not separated itself in the West standings. The Spurs and Thunder have earned their top seeds through team-wide excellence; the Nuggets have relied on Jokic's almost singular brilliance more than any title contender should have to.

That reliance is a double-edged argument. It demonstrates how irreplaceable Jokic is to Denver's playoff viability — and also how difficult it is for a team to win 44 games when it needs one player to average 28-12-10 just to compete.

Whether he wins the award or not, Jokic's 2025-26 season will be studied for decades as one of the most statistically extraordinary individual campaigns in the history of professional basketball.

What's Next

Denver has 14 games remaining and needs to resolve its seeding battle with Minnesota. The Nuggets (44-28) and Timberwolves (44-28) are tied for the No. 4-5 spots, with the Rockets (43-28) just a game behind. Anthony Edwards' knee injury (out 1-2 weeks) gives Denver an opening to claim the No. 4 seed outright.

The Nuggets play the Golden State Warriors on March 29, entering with zero players on the injury report — a rarity at this point in the season and a sign that Denver is healthy and hungry heading into the stretch run.
