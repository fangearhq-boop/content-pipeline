Thunder Dominate Spurs 127-114 in WCF Game 5, Take 3-2 Series Lead

By Marcus Cole | May 27, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Oklahoma City reclaimed control of the Western Conference Finals on Monday night, defeating the San Antonio Spurs 127-114 at Paycom Center to take a 3-2 series lead. The Thunder answered their worst performance of the series — a 103-82 blowout loss in Game 4 — with their most complete team effort of the WCF.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander was the engine with 32 points and 9 assists, but the night belonged to OKC's supporting cast. Alex Caruso exploded for 22 points in just 22 minutes off the bench, and Jared McCain provided 20 points with 18 of them coming in the second half when San Antonio desperately needed a stop. The Thunder bench produced 27 first-half points, a number that set the tone before Wembanyama could even get the Spurs into their rhythm.

The Thunder's Rotating Wall

The defining storyline of Game 5 was not Oklahoma City's offense — it was how they contained Victor Wembanyama. The Spurs' 22-year-old superstar, who had posted 41 points in Game 1 and 33 in Game 4, shot 4-of-15 from the field for a series-low 20 points.

OKC's approach was deliberate: rotate a fresh defender at Wembanyama every few possessions. Isaiah Hartenstein used his size and strength to push the 7-foot-4 Frenchman off his preferred catch spots. Chet Holmgren deployed his wingspan to contest shots without fouling. Jaylin Williams hounded him in transition. Alex Caruso applied pressure with his reading of passing lanes. Jared McCain described it simply as "team defense."

Wembanyama reached 20 points almost entirely via the free-throw line, making 12 of 12 attempts. Without those free throws, he would have finished with 8 points on field goals in a conference finals game. He still finished with 6 rebounds, 3 blocks, and 2 steals — a reminder that even a bad offensive night from Wembanyama is never quiet — but the Thunder neutralized the one thing that had been beating them.

Caruso and McCain Change the Game

If the Thunder needed a second narrative beyond SGA's performance, they got it. Caruso's 22 points in 22 minutes were a masterclass in efficiency: 5-of-10 from the field, 4-of-8 from three, 8-of-8 from the foul line, 6 assists, and 3 steals. His plus-minus was felt every time he was on the floor.

The Caruso performance was no accident. He is shooting 47.7 percent from three this postseason, compared to 29.3 percent in the regular season — a gap that is on pace to be the largest regular-season-to-playoffs three-point percentage jump in NBA history. His clean catch-and-shoot looks, created by SGA's gravity, are converting at an elite rate.

McCain's second-half burst gave San Antonio nothing to grab onto. After the Spurs mounted a small push midway through the third quarter, McCain scored 12 consecutive Thunder points in a stretch that sealed OKC's grip on the game. The Thunder lead swelled past 20 and never returned to single digits.

Spurs Remain Dangerous

San Antonio was not without contributors. Stephon Castle posted 24 points on 7-of-11 shooting, providing another reminder that the Spurs have legitimate scoring depth beyond Wembanyama. Julian Champagnie added 22 points off the bench on four made three-pointers — the kind of performance that keeps a team competitive even when its star struggles.

The Spurs shot 50 percent from the field and actually out-scored the Thunder from three-point range. The problem was Oklahoma City's pace and bench production — the Thunder's supporting cast outperformed San Antonio's reserves by a wide margin, and that gap proved decisive.

What's Next

The Thunder are one win from the NBA Finals. Game 6 shifts to AT&T Center in San Antonio on Thursday, May 28 (8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock), where the Spurs are 5-2 in the 2026 postseason. Wembanyama is expected to respond after his series-low performance — he has shown throughout his young career that difficult nights are typically followed by dominant ones.

If the Thunder close out the series Thursday, they will face the New York Knicks in the NBA Finals starting June 3 on ABC. New York won the Eastern Conference by sweeping the Cavaliers and has been resting since Monday night — over eight days off before Game 1.

Oklahoma City is one win away from completing one of the most anticipated Finals matchups in recent memory. The question is whether the Spurs can force a Game 7 at Paycom Center first.
