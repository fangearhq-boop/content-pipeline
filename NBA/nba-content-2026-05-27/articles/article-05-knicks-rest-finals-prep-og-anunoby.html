Eight Days Off and a Healthy OG: The Knicks Are Ready for Their Championship Moment

By Marcus Cole | May 27, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The New York Knicks finished sweeping the Cleveland Cavaliers on Monday night — and then they sat down. They will not play again until June 3, when the NBA Finals begin on ABC. Between now and Game 1, the Knicks have over eight full days to rest, recover, and prepare for the most important series of their generation.

That may be the best news New York has received since the final buzzer on Monday. Not because the Knicks need rest after sweeping their way through the Eastern Conference — they look as fresh and confident as any team in recent playoff memory — but because one player in particular needs this time more than most.

OG Anunoby's Recovery Window

OG Anunoby suffered a right hamstring strain in the second round against the Philadelphia 76ers, missing several games before returning for the Eastern Conference Finals. He played through the injury — logging 34 minutes in ECF Game 1, contributing defensively, and finishing the series as an active presence — but he was clearly not at full capacity.

Before the injury, Anunoby was averaging 21.4 points and 7.5 rebounds per game in the 2026 playoffs. Those are No. 2 option numbers on a Finals-caliber team. The Knicks won the ECF without a fully healthy OG, which speaks to their depth. But a completely recovered Anunoby heading into the Finals changes the matchup math against either Oklahoma City or San Antonio.

Against the Thunder, a healthy OG could shadow either SGA or Jalen Williams on defense — the exact kind of versatile assignment OKC's wing-heavy offense forces. Against the Spurs, his combination of defensive length and ability to score in isolation would directly challenge a San Antonio team that allows opponents to score in the mid-range. Eight days of legitimate rest could be the difference between Anunoby playing at 70 percent and playing at 100.

Brunson's Absurd Postseason Run

Whatever happens with OG, the Knicks' championship case is built on Jalen Brunson. He has averaged 27.8 points and 6.7 assists across 13 playoff games in 2026. In the ECF against Cleveland, he posted 25.5 points and 7.8 assists on 48.7 percent shooting — numbers that earned him unanimous Eastern Conference Finals MVP honors.

Brunson has not simply been statistically dominant. He has made the right play in the right moments throughout this run. He led the Knicks back from a 22-point deficit in ECF Game 1 with 15 fourth-quarter and overtime points. He distributed 14 assists in Game 2 while enabling Hart, Bridges, and Towns to find rhythm. He scored 30 in Game 3 when the Cavaliers tried to force a series adjustment. By Game 4, the sweep was a formality.

Since the 2025-26 regular season ended, Brunson has been playing the best basketball of his career. The rest will allow him to maintain that edge — not regain it.

The Home-Court Reality

The Knicks will face an unusual challenge heading into the Finals: they will not have home-court advantage against either remaining opponent. New York finished 53-29 in the regular season. Oklahoma City went 64-18. San Antonio went 62-20. In a Finals matchup with either team, the Knicks will open the series on the road, playing Games 1 and 2 away from Madison Square Garden.

Games 3 and 4 return to Madison Square Garden on June 8 and June 10, and the Garden has been one of the loudest buildings in basketball all postseason. The Knicks are 7-0 at home in the 2026 playoffs.

Still, road underdog status fits this team's identity. The Knicks have won 11 consecutive playoff games. They swept the No. 6 seed Atlanta Hawks, dispatched the No. 5 Philadelphia 76ers, and then demolished the No. 4 Cleveland Cavaliers, who had home-court advantage in the ECF. The Knicks have not played like a lower seed since the playoffs began.

Waiting to Learn the Opponent

Before the Knicks can fully commit to Finals preparation, they need to know who they are playing. Oklahoma City and San Antonio play Game 6 on Thursday, May 28 at AT&T Center (8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock). Thunder lead 3-2.

If Oklahoma City closes out the series Thursday, the Knicks face a team they have not played against since the 2024-25 regular season — a Thunder squad with the best record in the Western Conference, a two-time MVP in SGA, and a bench that has been one of the surprises of the entire postseason. If San Antonio forces a Game 7, the Knicks get another day or two to prepare while watching closely.

Either way, June 3 is circled. The Knicks are rested, healthy — or getting there — and ready. The longest Finals drought in New York history will end one way or another. The only question remaining is which team will try to stop them.
