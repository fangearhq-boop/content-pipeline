AJ Dybantsa Says He's "100%" Going No. 1 to Washington — The 2026 Draft Is 16 Days Away

By Jake Torres | June 7, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

AJ Dybantsa is not interested in pretending there is suspense. The BYU wing — the most talked-about prospect to enter the NBA Draft in years — says he is "100%" certain the Washington Wizards will select him with the No. 1 overall pick when the draft begins June 23 in Brooklyn.

The Wizards won the 2026 NBA Draft Lottery, their first No. 1 selection since choosing John Wall in 2010. The city of Washington is ready to believe that this pick is the foundation of something new.

Who Is AJ Dybantsa?

The BYU wing finished his season with 25.5 points, 6.8 rebounds, and 3.7 assists per game while shooting 51% from the field. At his size and age, those numbers represent the kind of production that scouts spend careers searching for. He can score at all three levels, has the athleticism to defend multiple positions, and the feel for the game that typically takes years to develop.

The betting markets agree with Dybantsa's self-assessment. He is the -450 favorite to go No. 1 overall — meaning you would need to bet $450 to win $100 on the outcome. That kind of one-sided market reflects a near-universal consensus.

The Wizards' Perspective

CBS Sports has noted that Washington has not officially committed publicly to selecting Dybantsa. In the business of the NBA Draft, that kind of public restraint is standard — teams rarely announce their intentions weeks in advance, both out of negotiating protocol and genuine organizational caution.

But the expectation around the league is virtually unanimous. The Wizards won the lottery for a reason. Washington is in the early stages of a rebuild, and a player of Dybantsa's caliber — one who could anchor a franchise for a decade — is precisely the kind of selection that changes an organization's trajectory.

The last time Washington had a No. 1 pick, they chose John Wall. Wall became one of the most exciting point guards of his generation before injuries derailed his career in Washington. The Wizards hope this pick has a different ending.

The Broader Draft Picture

The 2026 NBA Draft coincides with one of the most significant trade deadlines in recent league history. The Milwaukee Bucks have set June 23 as the informal deadline for resolving the Giannis Antetokounmpo situation — meaning the biggest No. 1 pick in years and the biggest trade of the summer could both land on the same night.

For the Wizards, that convergence could be significant. If Washington selects Dybantsa on June 23, they will immediately become a more attractive trade partner for teams looking to move star players. The question is whether they want to build organically around Dybantsa or accelerate the rebuild by adding a veteran star alongside him.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Draft takes place June 23-24 at the Barclays Center in Brooklyn. Dybantsa is expected to be the No. 1 pick, with the remainder of the top 10 still being finalized in the weeks ahead.

For Washington, for Dybantsa, and for every team holding a pick that night, June 23 cannot come soon enough. The future of multiple franchises gets written in Brooklyn — and the Wizards are convinced they already know their chapter's opening line.
