Giannis Trade Race: Celtics Emerge, Luka Complicates Lakers, Heat Still Lead

By Jake Torres | June 7, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Giannis Antetokounmpo trade saga took a significant turn over the weekend, with two new major players entering a race that now has three serious contenders and a deadline that is closing fast.

The Miami Heat remain the frontrunner — that has not changed. But new reports indicate that Giannis is "very intrigued" by the idea of playing in Boston, and the Los Angeles Lakers are very much in the mix despite a complicating factor that threatens to derail their pursuit entirely.

Heat: Still Leading, But Not Comfortable

Sources around the league continue to describe the noise surrounding Miami as "louder than all the rest." The Heat are the clear frontrunner for Antetokounmpo's services, and their pursuit has been the most aggressive of any team in the market.

The sticking point is the roster. Miami's trade package would require gutting significant assets, and Giannis reportedly has concerns about what the Heat's team would look like after completing a deal of that magnitude. He wants to win — and a stripped-down roster around him in Miami is not necessarily the contender he is envisioning.

The one non-negotiable for Miami: Bam Adebayo does not move. The All-Star center is off the table in any deal. Everything else is theoretically available.

The Luka Problem in Los Angeles

The Lakers are "very much in the mix," according to multiple reports, and their interest in Antetokounmpo is genuine and substantial. But there is a significant obstacle standing in their way: Luka Doncic.

Doncic has reportedly made it known to the Lakers' front office that he will not support Austin Reaves being included in any potential Giannis trade package. For Milwaukee, Reaves is a coveted asset — a proven two-way wing with a reasonable contract. His inclusion would make the math work more cleanly.

Without Reaves in the deal, the Lakers' trade package becomes more complicated. They have assets, but the path narrows considerably when one of their most attractive pieces is taken off the table by their franchise player.

Boston Emerges as a Wild Card

The most surprising development this weekend: Giannis is "very intrigued" by the possibility of going to Boston, according to a report from NBC Sports. The Celtics have the infrastructure — an existing contender, strong ownership, and a culture of winning — that checks many of Giannis's boxes.

Critically, Antetokounmpo wants to stay in the Eastern Conference. That preference eliminates most Western teams and keeps the Celtics squarely in the conversation. However, there have been no direct talks between Boston and Milwaukee yet, and the Celtics would need to construct a serious trade package to compete with Miami's offer.

The Oklahoma City Thunder, previously mentioned as a possibility, are out. OKC has no interest in pursuing Antetokounmpo at this stage of their rebuild.

What's Next

Milwaukee has set the NBA Draft — June 23-24 in Brooklyn — as its informal deadline for a resolution. Bucks owner Jimmy Haslam wants clarity before the league's biggest offseason event begins. The Bucks have indicated they are also open to extending Antetokounmpo if he commits to staying, but that option appears unlikely.

The Finals themselves are a factor. Milwaukee has been watching the Knicks and Spurs closely, hoping to identify potential trade partners from the championship round. With Game 3 set for Monday at MSG and the series still very much alive, the Bucks are in no rush — but the clock is ticking. Sixteen days until Brooklyn.
