By the Numbers: The 2026 Knicks' Historic Playoff Run That Nobody Can Explain Away

By Damon Pierce | June 7, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The New York Knicks have won 13 consecutive playoff games. They have outscored opponents by over 270 points in this postseason. They have swept through the Eastern Conference and won the first two games of the NBA Finals on the road. ESPN Analytics gives them a 58.2% win probability in Game 3 at their own home building.

At some point, the numbers stop looking like a hot streak and start looking like evidence of something genuinely extraordinary.

The Streak in Context

Thirteen consecutive playoff wins is one of the longest runs in the modern NBA Finals era. The Knicks swept their Eastern Conference Finals opponent and then took both games in San Antonio — becoming only the third team in NBA history to win the first two Finals games on the road.

Their point differential of +270 across the playoff run translates to a net rating over +19.8. That number is not merely good. It is historically dominant. For context, the best regular season teams in NBA history typically post net ratings around +12 to +14. This Knicks team has been playing at a level in the playoffs that exceeds the best regular season performances of most championship teams.

Why the Depth Is Their Superpower

The most dangerous thing about this Knicks team is that there is no single player to stop. Jalen Brunson shot 7-for-25 in Game 2 — the worst shooting performance of his postseason — and the Knicks still won by one point. Karl-Anthony Towns stepped up with 21 points and 13 rebounds. Josh Hart was active and physical. The bench contributed.

San Antonio has thrown multiple defensive schemes at Brunson, and the Knicks have still found ways to win. Their ball movement, defensive versatility, and physical toughness make them genuinely difficult to game-plan against. You can slow one player down; you cannot slow all of them down at once.

This is the product of Tom Thibodeau's system — grueling, physical, unrelenting — and a roster that bought into it completely.

What the Numbers Say About Game 3

ESPN Analytics gives the Knicks a 58.2% win probability in Game 3, even accounting for the matchup and Victor Wembanyama's capability to dominate a single game. The Knicks are -490 to win the series, a dramatic shift from +164 underdogs to start the Finals.

None of this means the Spurs are finished. Wembanyama averaged 27.3 points and 10.9 rebounds in the Western Conference Finals and won the WCF MVP award. He is capable of carrying a team to a win in any single game. But the pattern over 13 games suggests that when the Knicks face a challenge, they typically find a way through it.

What's Next

Game 3 is Monday, June 8 at 8:30 PM ET at Madison Square Garden on ABC. It will be the first Finals game at MSG since 1999. A Knicks win would put them on the brink of their first championship since 1973 — and make history as the first team to complete the modern postseason with a 16-0 record since the Spurs in 1999.

One more win at home. The crowd will be at a decibel level not heard in that building in decades. The numbers say New York is the better team. Monday night is the chance to prove it again.
