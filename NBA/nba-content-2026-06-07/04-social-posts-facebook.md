### STORY 3: Wembanyama Mental Reset — "We Can't Change the Past"

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 1:00 PM ET

Victor Wembanyama had 29 points and shot 11-of-21 from the floor in Game 2 of the NBA Finals.

He was exceptional for 47 minutes and 47 seconds.

And then came the final 13 seconds.

His errant pass bounced off Stephon Castle's back. Jalen Brunson scooped it up, drove hard, and drew a foul. Brunson made one of two free throws — 105-103 with 2 seconds left. Wembanyama caught the inbound pass, turned, and launched a 20-footer at the buzzer.

It rattled out. Knicks win, 105-104. Series: 2-0.

The 22-year-old, making his first ever NBA Finals appearance, was left standing at center court.

His response afterward? "We can't change the past. We're already thinking about Game 3."

That is the right attitude. The only productive attitude. If the Spurs are going to pull off the impossible, Wembanyama's mental reset will be a major reason why.
