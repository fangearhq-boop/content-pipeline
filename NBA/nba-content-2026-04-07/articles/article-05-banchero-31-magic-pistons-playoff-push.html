Banchero 31, Bane 25: Magic Rout Pistons 123-107 in Playoff Push

By Marcus Cole April 7, 2026

Orlando Makes a Statement

The Orlando Magic needed a win Monday and got a dominant one. Paolo Banchero poured in 31 points and Desmond Bane added 25 as Orlando rolled past the Detroit Pistons 123-107, a performance that should raise eyebrows about what this Magic team is capable of in the final week of the regular season.

Beating the No. 1 seed in the East by 16 points is not a fluke, regardless of where the Pistons' heads were in a late-season game where they may have been managing minutes and protecting key players for April. Orlando came out with purpose and played like a team that knows exactly what is at stake in the next five days.

Banchero Is Arriving at the Right Time

Paolo Banchero at his best is one of the more complete offensive players in the Eastern Conference. The 23-year-old forward has the combination of size, skill, and basketball IQ that makes him difficult to contain in the half court, and Monday night he looked like the best player on the floor for extended stretches against a Detroit squad that has been among the league's best all season.

Thirty-one points is a number that matters in any context. In a game with playoff implications for Orlando in the final week, it is the kind of performance that gives a team momentum and gives a coaching staff confidence heading into the most important games of the year.

Bane Provides the Balance

Desmond Bane's 25 points alongside Banchero's 31 is what separates a good offensive night from a dominant one. When both are productive, Orlando becomes difficult to defend — Banchero demands attention in the paint and Bane punishes teams that over-help, connecting from three with the efficiency that has made him one of the league's better catch-and-shoot options. The Magic's offense on Monday was a showcase of what their ceiling looks like when both stars show up.

What's Next

Orlando's push for a favorable position in the East continues with games remaining before the April 12 regular-season close. The Magic have shown in the final stretch that they are not simply going through the motions — they want the best possible seeding and are willing to earn it. Monday's win over Detroit sends a message: Orlando is a team to take seriously when the playoffs arrive on April 18.
