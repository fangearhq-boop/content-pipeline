NBA Regular Season Finale: West No. 3 Seed Goes Down to the Wire

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 12, 2026

After 82 games and six months of basketball, the 2025-26 NBA regular season comes to a close on Sunday with a 15-game slate — the largest single-day conclusion in league history. And remarkably, several of the most consequential questions of the year remain unanswered heading into the final hours.

At the center of it all is the battle for the No. 3 seed in the Western Conference. The Denver Nuggets (53-28) hold a half-game lead over the Los Angeles Lakers (52-28). Denver faces the San Antonio Spurs at home. The Lakers travel to Salt Lake City to take on the Utah Jazz. Whatever happens in those two games will determine how the entire right side of the Western bracket falls.

What's at Stake in the West

The No. 3 vs. No. 4 distinction in the Western Conference matters enormously. The No. 3 seed draws the No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves in the first round. The No. 4 seed plays the No. 5 Houston Rockets — a team that won seven straight to close the regular season and enters the postseason playing its best basketball.

The Nuggets have been one of the hottest teams in basketball, riding a 10-game winning streak heading into the final day. Nikola Jokic has been his usual triple-double self, and Denver's depth has been exceptional. The Lakers, without Luka Doncic (Grade 2 hamstring strain, out for the season), are relying heavily on LeBron James — who delivered 28 points and 12 assists in Friday's dominant win over the Phoenix Suns. LeBron at 41 is still carrying this team.

The scenario is straightforward: if the Nuggets win and the Lakers lose — or if results are mixed but favor Denver — the Nuggets lock in No. 3. If Los Angeles wins and Denver loses, the Lakers leap into the third seed. Any split creates a potential for a photo finish decided by the final margins.

The Eastern Conference Final Day

In the East, the top four seeds are confirmed: the Detroit Pistons (No. 1), Boston Celtics (No. 2), New York Knicks (No. 3, clinched Friday), and Cleveland Cavaliers (No. 4). But the seeds from No. 5 through No. 10 are still being settled.

The Atlanta Hawks (No. 5 entering Sunday) host the Miami Heat. The Toronto Raptors (No. 6) host the Brooklyn Nets — a must-protect game for Toronto, which wants to stay in the top six and out of the play-in bracket. The Orlando Magic (No. 7), Philadelphia 76ers (No. 8), Charlotte Hornets (No. 9), and Miami Heat (No. 10) are all vying for play-in positioning.

Teams seeded 5-6 receive a guaranteed first-round playoff berth. Seeds 7-10 enter the SoFi Play-In Tournament, which begins Tuesday, April 14, and runs through April 17 — all on Prime Video.

What's Next

When the final buzzer sounds tonight, the full 2026 NBA Playoffs bracket will be set. The SoFi Play-In Tournament starts April 14, with East and West matchups airing exclusively on Prime Video. The first round of the NBA Playoffs begins April 18. It has been one of the most compelling regular seasons in recent memory — and the postseason promises to be even better.
