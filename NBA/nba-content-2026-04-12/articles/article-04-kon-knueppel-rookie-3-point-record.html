265 Threes: Kon Knueppel's Rookie Record Season Changes the Conversation

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 12, 2026

Keegan Murray's rookie record of 206 three-pointers, set with the Sacramento Kings in 2022-23, seemed like a number that could last for years. It was a legitimate outlier — a first-year player making threes at a rate that approached the output of established veterans. Three seasons later, Kon Knueppel made it look modest.

Knueppel, the No. 4 pick in the 2025 NBA Draft, finished the 2025-26 regular season with 265 three-pointers — shattering Murray's record by 59. He shot 43 percent from behind the arc on 7.9 attempts per game. He also broke Kemba Walker's Hornets franchise record for three-pointers made in a single season along the way. The Charlotte Hornets, searching for an identity and a star, found both in one rookie campaign.

The Numbers Behind the Record

What separates Knueppel's three-point record from pure counting-stat accumulation is the efficiency behind it. Shooting 43 percent from three on nearly eight attempts per game is not a lucky hot streak — it is elite shooting at an elite volume. Most NBA sharpshooters dream of that combination. Very few achieve it in their first season.

Beyond the three-point line, Knueppel posted full averages of 18.8 points, 5.4 rebounds, and 3.4 assists per game — a complete freshman profile that suggested he was not simply a specialist deployed in spot-up situations. He created his own shots, handled the ball in pick-and-roll actions, and functioned as a secondary playmaker in Charlotte's offense.

The record-breaking moment against the Phoenix Suns earlier this month drew celebration from around the league, not just from Hornets fans. Murray, whose record Knueppel broke, acknowledged it graciously on social media. That kind of generational handoff — where a previous record-holder applauds the new standard — says something about the magnitude of what Knueppel accomplished.

Rookie of the Year Conversation

The Kia Rookie of the Year award is rarely a close race when one player outperforms the field by this margin. Knueppel ranked No. 1 on the final Kia Rookie Ladder, and the three-point record only reinforces the case. His season produced the kind of historic footprint that voters consistently reward.

The Charlotte Hornets have not had a player generate this kind of immediate impact in years. The franchise is building around Knueppel's arrival, and what he showed in 82 games suggests the investment will pay returns for a long time.

What's Next

The Charlotte Hornets enter the SoFi Play-In Tournament on Wednesday as the No. 9 seed, facing the No. 10 Miami Heat. A victory keeps their season alive and earns them a chance to fight for the No. 8 spot — and an NBA Playoffs berth — in the following round. With Knueppel playing the best basketball of his young life, Charlotte has a genuine shot at extending their season. The ROY award will be formally announced during the playoffs. It is already Knueppel's to claim.
