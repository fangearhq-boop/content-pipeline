Back-to-Back: SGA's Historic Season Makes Him the Obvious MVP Choice

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 12, 2026

There were two generational talents in the MVP race. There was the defending champion of three straight awards. There was a superstar reviving a storied franchise. And then there was Shai Gilgeous-Alexander — who rendered the entire conversation moot by simply being better than everyone else, every single night.

As the 2025-26 NBA regular season concludes Sunday, SGA wraps up his campaign with averages of 31.6 points, 6.5 assists, 4.4 rebounds, and 1.4 steals per game — his second consecutive scoring title. His Oklahoma City Thunder finished with a league-best 64-16 record, securing the No. 1 seed in the Western Conference. He received 88 of 100 first-place votes in ESPN's final MVP straw poll. DraftKings set his odds at -400. The award is his.

Why the Race Was Never Really a Race

Victor Wembanyama had an extraordinary season. Nikola Jokic averaged a triple-double. Both were remarkable cases. But the gap between SGA and the field was defined less by raw numbers and more by context: the Thunder were the best team in basketball, and he was the reason why.

Oklahoma City's 64-16 finish is the franchise's best record since the Kevin Durant era, when SGA was not yet old enough to vote. The Thunder went from a lottery team to the top of the NBA in three seasons — and the common denominator the entire way was SGA's relentless, elegant improvement. He added playmaking, sharpened his shot selection, and turned himself into one of the most complete offensive players in the game.

A back-to-back MVP is not automatic even for great players. It requires not just sustained excellence but continued evolution. LeBron James did it in 2009-10 and 2010-11. Stephen Curry did it in 2015-16 (unanimously). Giannis Antetokounmpo did it in 2018-19 and 2019-20. Now Shai Gilgeous-Alexander joins that club.

The Season in Context

The Thunder's 64-16 record was built on balance, depth, and SGA's ability to take over games when it mattered. OKC posted one of the league's best point differentials, went on multiple double-digit winning streaks, and clinched the No. 1 seed with several games remaining. This was not a team that needed SGA to score 40 every night — and yet, when the games were on the line, he almost always delivered.

His 31.6 scoring average led the NBA and represents one of the most efficient high-volume scoring seasons in modern history. His 6.5 assists show a player who has fully developed as a creator for others. His 1.4 steals per game make him a threat at both ends.

What's Next

The formal MVP announcement will come during the playoffs, but there is nothing left to announce — the voters have spoken. OKC now turns its full attention to the postseason, where the Thunder will enter as the favorite to represent the Western Conference in the NBA Finals. SGA has never been past the first round. This year, with the league's best team and his best basketball, that is likely to change. The greatest individual season in Thunder history is complete. The postseason is just beginning.
