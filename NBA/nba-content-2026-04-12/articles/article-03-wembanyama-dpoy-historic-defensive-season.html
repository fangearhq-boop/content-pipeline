Wembanyama's DPOY Is a Lock — And It's Only the Beginning

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 12, 2026

Betting markets set Victor Wembanyama's odds to win the 2026 NBA Defensive Player of the Year at -5000. That translates to an implied probability exceeding 98 percent. It is not the kind of number you argue about — it is the kind of number you acknowledge as a reflection of reality.

Wembanyama secured his award eligibility Friday, playing 26 minutes and scoring 40 points through a rib contusion in the Spurs' win over Dallas — his 65th game of the season, meeting the league's minimum threshold for postseason honors. With that box checked, the path to DPOY is clear. It always was.

What He Did Defensively

The raw numbers begin and end with blocks. Wembanyama led the NBA in blocks per game this season, an accomplishment that understates his full impact. His 7-foot-4 frame, combined with a wingspan that extends well beyond that, allows him to alter shots that other defenders can only hope to contest. He does not just protect the rim — he controls an entire zone around it, forcing opponents to adjust their drives, floaters, and post moves before they even reach the basket.

Coaches around the league spent the year designing plays to avoid him rather than attack him. That kind of spatial influence does not show up in standard box scores, but advanced metrics capture it clearly: the Spurs' defensive rating with Wembanyama on the court was among the best in the league. When he rested, it showed.

What makes him historically unusual is the combination of length, mobility, and instinct. Most elite rim protectors are one-dimensional — they stay near the basket and dare you to get there. Wembanyama can switch onto guards, hedge on pick-and-rolls, and still recover in time to erase a mid-range attempt. He is both a shot-blocker and a scheme disruptor.

The Award in Context

The DPOY Award has a history of multi-year dynasties. Ben Wallace won it four times between 2002 and 2006. Dwight Howard won it three straight years from 2009 to 2011. Rudy Gobert won it four times. When a center arrives with the combination of size and skill Wembanyama possesses, the voters tend to take notice — and keep noticing.

Wembanyama is 22 years old. His season — rib injury included — produced the kind of defensive performance that inspires that kind of long-term appreciation. San Antonio coach Gregg Popovich's successors built this franchise around his development, and DPOY would be the first major individual recognition of what he has become.

What's Next

The Spurs enter the 2026 NBA Playoffs as the No. 2 seed in the Western Conference. Their first-round opponent will be determined by today's results, but Wembanyama's health heading into the postseason is the most important variable for San Antonio's championship hopes. He played through the rib contusion Friday; how he feels in the coming days matters far more than the DPOY trophy, which is already effectively won. The award announcement will come during the playoffs. The defense starts April 18.
