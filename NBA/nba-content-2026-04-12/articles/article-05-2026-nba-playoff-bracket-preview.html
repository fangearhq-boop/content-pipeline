2026 NBA Playoffs: Your Complete First-Round Bracket Guide

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 12, 2026

The 2026 SoFi NBA Play-In Tournament begins Tuesday, April 14. The NBA Playoffs tip off April 18. After one of the most eventful regular seasons in years — featuring two generational sophomores, a historic turnaround franchise, a back-to-back scoring champion, and a rookie who rewrote the record books — the real competition is finally about to begin.

Here is your complete guide to the playoff bracket as it stands entering the final day of the regular season.

Eastern Conference

The Detroit Pistons enter as the No. 1 seed — one of the most remarkable single-franchise reversals in NBA history. Two seasons ago, Detroit went 14-68. This year, they are the top seed in the East and Central Division champions. The rise of Jalen Duren, Cade Cunningham's return from injury, and a coaching staff that maximized the roster make Detroit a genuine threat.

The Boston Celtics are No. 2 — a familiar position for a franchise that has owned the East's upper echelon in recent years. Jayson Tatum's return from an Achilles procedure limited his early-season output, but Boston remained dangerous throughout. Jaylen Brown's playoff pedigree and depth across the roster keep them in the championship conversation.

The New York Knicks clinched No. 3 on Friday with a victory over Toronto. Jalen Brunson and Josh Hart anchored a resilient group that plays its best basketball under pressure. Home games at Madison Square Garden in the first round will be an enormous advantage. The Cleveland Cavaliers (No. 4), led by Donovan Mitchell, are equally capable of a deep run.

The Play-In bracket has the Orlando Magic (Paolo Banchero, No. 7) facing the Philadelphia 76ers (Paul George, No. 8) in what could be the most entertaining of the opening Play-In games. The Charlotte Hornets (Kon Knueppel, No. 9) face the Miami Heat (No. 10) in the second East game.

Western Conference

The Oklahoma City Thunder (64-16) are the No. 1 seed and the best team in basketball. SGA-led, deep, and experienced, OKC will enter the first round as the most complete squad in the West. Their opponent will emerge from the play-in bracket.

The San Antonio Spurs are No. 2, riding the extraordinary two-way play of Victor Wembanyama. The Spurs have been one of the most consistent teams in the league over the second half of the season and enter the postseason healthy — relatively speaking, given Wembanyama's recent rib contusion.

The No. 3 seed between the Denver Nuggets and Los Angeles Lakers is determined today. Denver (53-28, 10-game winning streak, Nikola Jokic triple-double machine) would draw the No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves if they hold. The Lakers (52-28, LeBron at 41 in playoff form, no Luka) would get the same matchup if they jump Denver.

Houston Rockets (No. 5) enter on a 7-game winning streak. The Western Play-In features the Phoenix Suns (Devin Booker, No. 7) against the Portland Trail Blazers or LA Clippers (No. 8), with the Golden State Warriors (Stephen Curry, No. 10) lurking as the most dangerous low seed in the bracket.

What's Next

The SoFi Play-In Tournament runs April 14-17, all exclusively on Prime Video. The NBA Playoffs first round begins April 18. The Finals are scheduled to begin in June.
