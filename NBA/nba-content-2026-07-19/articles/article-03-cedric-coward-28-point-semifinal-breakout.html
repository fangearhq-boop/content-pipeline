Cedric Coward Just Had the Summer League Performance of the Year

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 19, 2026

Before Saturday, you might have been forgiven for not knowing Cedric Coward's name. After Saturday, that excuse is gone.

The Memphis Grizzlies' 2025 No. 11 pick delivered one of the most complete Summer League performances of the 2026 season: 28 points, 6 rebounds, 5 assists, and 4 three-pointers in Memphis's semifinal win over the Houston Rockets. He was the best player on the floor in a game featuring multiple first-round picks, and he looked the part at every stage.

Who Is Cedric Coward?

Coward is 22 years old, drafted out of Eastern Washington — a mid-major program that rarely produces lottery picks. That draft slot — No. 11 overall — reflected the Grizzlies' faith in his projectability: a long, versatile wing with shooting ability, playmaking instincts, and the kind of feel for spacing that modern NBA offenses demand.

His first NBA season was largely a developmental year. He appeared in 31 games, averaged modest numbers in limited minutes, and was never a headline name. The Summer League was supposed to be a chance to build confidence. Instead, it became a coming-out party.

What He Did Saturday

Coward's 28-point semifinal line broke down this way: 4-of-7 from three-point range, consistent pressure in pick-and-roll actions, and a willingness to attack closeouts that kept Houston's defense perpetually off-balance. His 5 assists showed a secondary skill set that doesn't always surface in Summer League environments where players are rightly prioritizing their own scoring opportunities.

He carried momentum from prior performances throughout the tournament, but Saturday was his peak. "He's ready," one Western Conference scout told multiple outlets after the game. The Grizzlies' front office would not disagree.

The Memphis Future Core

The most compelling takeaway from Coward's performance isn't the box score — it's what it suggests about the Grizzlies' future alongside Cameron Boozer. Boozer, the 2026 No. 3 pick, has been the most consistent Summer League performer in Las Vegas this year: 18.8 points, 6.5 rebounds, 3.5 assists per game across four appearances. He added a 12-12-5 line in Saturday's semifinal.

Coward and Boozer complement each other cleanly. Boozer is the high-usage interior presence — a face-up four who can play-make from the elbow. Coward is the wing who spaces the floor, creates off cuts, and can take over a quarter when his shot is falling. Memphis's developmental staff has been building toward this kind of modularity for three years. These two are the result.

The Grizzlies play for the Summer League championship tonight at 9:00 PM ET on ESPN. If Coward brings another performance like Saturday's, Memphis will be champions — and his name will be firmly established heading into his second NBA season.

What's Next

Coward's Summer League run positions him for a meaningful role in Memphis's 2026-27 rotation. Watch for his preseason usage to expand off what he showed in Las Vegas. After tonight's championship game, Grizzlies training camp in October becomes the next stage for the Coward-Boozer partnership. If the summer is any preview, that Memphis future is arriving on schedule.
