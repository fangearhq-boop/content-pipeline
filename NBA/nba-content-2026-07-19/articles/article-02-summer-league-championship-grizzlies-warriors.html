Summer League Championship Tonight: Grizzlies vs. Warriors Brings the NBA's Future to ESPN

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 19, 2026

The 2026 NBA Summer League ends tonight — and it ends with a championship game worthy of the best July basketball has to offer.

The Memphis Grizzlies and Golden State Warriors meet at 9:00 PM ET on ESPN in the Las Vegas Summer League title game, a matchup loaded with first-round picks, proven summer performers, and enough youth movement to excite fans of both franchises heading into the fall.

How They Got Here

Saturday's semifinals delivered genuine drama. The Golden State Warriors — seeded fourth — knocked off the Los Angeles Lakers, who entered the day 4-0 and the No. 1 seed, 92-88 in an upset that ended the Lakers' run. Yaxel Lendeborg, the Warriors' 2026 No. 11 pick, delivered the standout performance: 15 points (5-for-8 from the field, going 6-for-10 on two-pointers), 9 rebounds, and 2 steals. It was the kind of efficient, physical showing that scouts have been waiting to see from the 20-year-old forward.

In the other semifinal, the Memphis Grizzlies handled the Houston Rockets behind Cedric Coward's 28-point explosion. Coward — the 2025 No. 11 pick — added 6 rebounds, 5 assists, and hit 4 three-pointers, proving he belongs in conversations about the league's most intriguing second-year wings. Cameron Boozer contributed a double-double: 12 points, 12 rebounds, 5 assists.

The Championship Storylines

Cameron Boozer has been the headliner of this Summer League. The 2026 No. 3 overall pick has averaged 18.8 points, 6.5 rebounds, and 3.5 assists in 27.2 minutes over four games — a production rate that few high picks match in their Summer League debut. At 6-foot-9 with genuine playmaking instincts and an advanced feel for the game, Boozer has looked like exactly the kind of big the Grizzlies envisioned drafting him for.

For the Warriors, Lendeborg's semifinal performance makes him the one to watch. Drafted 11th overall this past June, the big man showed polished footwork and excellent off-ball positioning — skills that translate to the regular season far more reliably than raw athleticism. If Lendeborg brings Saturday's version of himself to tonight's game, he gives Golden State a genuine chance.

Worth noting for historical context: when these teams met in pool play on July 14, the Grizzlies won 106-85, with Javon Small leading Memphis with 26 points. The Warriors will be hungry to flip that result on the biggest stage.

Why It Matters

Summer League titles carry no official weight in the NBA record books, but they matter to development staff and the young players competing for roster spots. For Boozer and Coward in Memphis, winning tonight adds a statement to an already impressive summer. For Lendeborg in Golden State, another strong showing cements his standing as a potential rotation contributor by October.

This is also the closing note of the league's most frenetic offseason in recent memory — a summer that featured Giannis to Miami, Paul George to Boston, Jaylen Brown to Philly, and LeBron James still undecided. Tonight's championship offers a brief, uncomplicated reprieve: two teams, one trophy, and a gym full of rookies playing their hearts out.

What's Next

Tip-off is at 9:00 PM ET on ESPN from Las Vegas. After tonight's championship, attention shifts fully to LeBron James's expected announcement early this week — and then to training camp preparations as all 30 teams begin building rosters for 2026-27. Watch for Boozer, Coward, and Lendeborg's Summer League numbers to factor into their respective teams' preseason rotation discussions.
