Kawhi Leonard Investigation Expands Again — Raptors Trade Still Frozen With No Timeline

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | July 19, 2026

The Kawhi Leonard saga is not ending soon.

The NBA's investigation into the Los Angeles Clippers' alleged salary cap circumvention has expanded in scope, now encompassing a second previously undisclosed endorsement deal in addition to the original Aspiration arrangement that triggered the probe in September 2025. As of July 16, league sources told HoopsHype that a conclusion is still "weeks away" — with the agreed trade sending Leonard to Toronto remaining frozen indefinitely.

What the Investigation Covers

The probe, being conducted by outside law firm Wachtell Lipton on behalf of the NBA, centers on whether the Clippers circumvented the salary cap by routing payments to Leonard through third-party channels. The original allegation — surfaced by reporting on Pablo Torre's podcast — involved Aspiration, a financial services company that has since gone bankrupt. Investigators allege that Clippers owner Steve Ballmer used the company to pay Leonard through a "no-show" sponsorship arrangement.

The investigation has since expanded in two additional directions. A second previously undisclosed endorsement deal is now under the microscope, and investigators are also examining whether the Clippers improperly covered personal expenses for Leonard without reimbursement — a separate potential violation of league rules.

Former Clippers executive Steve Sanberg, a central figure in the case, has already been convicted on separate charges and sentenced to 14 years in prison. His cooperation — or lack thereof — with NBA investigators has reportedly complicated the timeline.

Commissioner Silver's Position

Commissioner Adam Silver said publicly that he wants the investigation concluded before the start of the 2026-27 season. That remains the stated goal, but the July 16 HoopsHype report makes clear the league is not close to that outcome — at least not as of mid-July. With training camps scheduled to open in late September, the window for the investigation to conclude before the season is narrowing.

The NBA's options, if violations are confirmed, range from fines and loss of draft picks to voiding the trade entirely. The Clippers and their ownership have maintained that the arrangements were legitimate business dealings.

The Raptors Are Stuck

Toronto agreed to acquire Leonard — in exchange for Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, and draft picks — before the investigation began. The Raptors declined to assume penalty risk, a reasonable position given the investigation's scope, but they now sit in limbo: unable to finalize the trade, unable to move on, and waiting for a process that keeps expanding rather than concluding.

Leonard himself is 35 years old. Every week of delay is a week less of whatever remains of his playing prime. Whether the trade eventually clears — or collapses entirely — the situation has already cost him months of offseason preparation with his new team. There is no obvious resolution on the immediate horizon.

What's Next

Monitor HoopsHype, The Athletic, and ESPN for updates as the investigation progresses. The key markers to watch: any announcement of an investigation conclusion, any league ruling on the Clippers, and any indication the Raptors trade is moving forward or being unwound. Until the NBA completes its inquiry, Kawhi Leonard's basketball future remains in official suspension.
