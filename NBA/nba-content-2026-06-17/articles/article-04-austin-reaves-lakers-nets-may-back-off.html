Austin Reaves Likely Stays in LA as New Reports Suggest Nets May Back Off

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 17, 2026

Lakers fans may be able to exhale.

New reports circulating around the NBA suggest that the Brooklyn Nets — previously the most-discussed rival suitor for Austin Reaves — may not pursue the Los Angeles guard as aggressively as earlier reports indicated. That shift puts the Lakers firmly in the driver's seat to retain one of the league's most efficient scorers before free agency opens June 30.

The Financial Landscape

Reaves holds a $14.9 million player option for the 2026-27 season and is widely expected to decline it before June 29, triggering his entry into free agency. Under NBA salary cap rules, the Lakers hold an exclusive negotiating window until free agency officially opens — giving Los Angeles a head start on any conversation.

The numbers heavily favor a Lakers deal:

  • Lakers maximum offer: 5 years, approximately $239 million
  • Nets maximum offer: 4 years, approximately $178.5 million

That is a gap of roughly $60 million and one guaranteed year. For a player who has publicly indicated he wants to remain in Los Angeles alongside Luka Doncic, the financial disparity makes it difficult for Brooklyn to compete even if they make the offer.

What Reaves Brings

Reaves had a breakout 2025-26 season by any measure. He averaged 23.3 points, 5.5 assists, and 4.7 rebounds per game while shooting 49.0% from the field. At 28 years old, he is entering his prime years, making the five-year commitment the Lakers can offer an even more appealing investment.

His chemistry with Doncic — built through a full season of playing alongside the Slovenian superstar — is a significant factor that money alone cannot replicate. Reaves has become the ideal secondary ball-handler and shot creator next to an elite lead guard, and rebuilding that partnership elsewhere would take time neither player wants to spend.

The Other Teams

The Atlanta Hawks and Detroit Pistons had also been mentioned as potential suitors with cap space to offer a competitive bid. But with the Nets possibly stepping back, those teams are unlikely to present offers that can match what the Lakers can put on the table both financially and in terms of winning opportunity.

The Pistons are rebuilding. The Hawks are in a transitional phase. The Lakers are competing for championships. For a player at Reaves's stage of career, the choice is not complicated.

What's Next

Reaves's player option deadline is June 29. Expect him to formally decline and begin exclusive negotiations with the Lakers immediately. If the Nets do not escalate their pursuit, a new deal between Reaves and Los Angeles could be announced within hours of free agency opening on June 30. For the Lakers, retaining their second-best player at a fair max contract would be the ideal start to a critical offseason.
