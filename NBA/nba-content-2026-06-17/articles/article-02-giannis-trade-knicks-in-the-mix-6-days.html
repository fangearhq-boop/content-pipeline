Giannis Trade: Knicks Now in the Mix With Six Days Until the Draft Deadline

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 17, 2026

The Knicks won the championship six days ago. Now they might be in the running for Giannis Antetokounmpo.

NBA insider Jake Fischer reported that the Milwaukee Bucks deliberately waited until the NBA Finals concluded before requesting final offers for their two-time MVP — which means the real Giannis trade market is only just beginning. With the June 23 draft serving as the de facto deadline, teams have six days to make their best case to Milwaukee.

And if multiple league reports are accurate, the Knicks may have a significant edge on their competition.

What the Reports Say

Multiple sources circulating in NBA circles indicate that Giannis Antetokounmpo "only wanted the Knicks" in a trade scenario last offseason. That preference has not definitively changed.

Now New York is a championship organization. The pitch writes itself: come win with Jalen Brunson, Karl-Anthony Towns, and OG Anunoby in the city that is about to host a championship parade. Play in Madison Square Garden. Compete immediately for another title.

The Miami Heat and Boston Celtics remain serious contenders. Heat insider Marc Stein had been reporting that Miami was "closing in" on a deal before the Finals began, though the Bucks rejected a package centered on Tyler Herro plus picks. The Celtics have put Jaylen Brown's name into discussions — a move complicated by the optics of breaking up the Tatum-Brown partnership.

The Bucks' Position

Milwaukee co-owner Jamie Haslam has consistently indicated that the Bucks want the Giannis situation resolved before draft night. With Giannis having two years remaining on his contract and eligible for a four-year, $275M extension starting October 1, the clock pressure is real.

The Bucks' ask has reportedly centered on a package of players and picks — not just draft capital. Any deal involving the Knicks would require New York to part with significant assets from a championship roster, which is a meaningful ask from a front office that just built something rare.

That calculus will drive the Knicks' decision in the next six days. Champion teams rarely blow up their cores for one more star. But Giannis Antetokounmpo is not one more star — he is one of five players in NBA history to win consecutive MVP awards.

The Timeline

The Bucks have not yet asked teams for formal final offers, per Fischer's reporting. That process begins now, with the June 23 draft as the hard stop. A deal done on draft night allows Milwaukee to potentially trade back into the first round with acquired picks — which is exactly why the Bucks have tied their own draft strategy to this timeline.

The league is watching. For now, the Knicks have the momentum of a championship and, if reports are to be believed, Giannis's own preference on their side.

What's Next

The next 48 to 72 hours are critical. Expect Bucks GM Jon Horst to formally engage with suitors before the weekend. If the Knicks are serious, they will need to present a package that convinces Milwaukee they are getting a better deal than the Heat or Celtics can offer. The answer — if it comes — likely arrives before Thursday's parade ends.
