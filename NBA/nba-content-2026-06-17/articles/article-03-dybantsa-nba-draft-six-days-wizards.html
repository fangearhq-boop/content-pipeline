Six Days to the Draft: Why AJ Dybantsa's Larry Bird Comparison Is Earned

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 17, 2026

AJ Dybantsa's college season at BYU was so good that comparisons to Larry Bird are not hyperbole — they are statistical fact.

The 2026 NBA Draft is six days away at Barclays Center in Brooklyn (June 23-24), and Dybantsa sits at -450 odds to go No. 1 overall to the Washington Wizards. The heavy favorite billing is backed by a resume that has not been replicated in nearly five decades.

The Bird Comparison Explained

In his lone college season at BYU, Dybantsa averaged 25.5 points per game with 51.0% shooting from the field, 6.8 rebounds, and 3.7 assists per game. He earned consensus All-American honors.

The last player to average 25-plus points per game on over 51% shooting from the field AND be named a consensus All-American was Larry Bird at Indiana State in 1978-79 — the same season Bird led the Sycamores to the national championship game and lost to Magic Johnson's Michigan State team in one of the most-watched games in college basketball history.

That is the tier Dybantsa is operating in. His combination of scoring volume, efficiency, and all-around game has scouts projecting him as an immediate NBA contributor with All-Star upside within three seasons.

The Wizards' Situation

Washington went 17-65 this season and won the draft lottery for the second time in franchise history, securing the right to select first on June 23. Their roster already features recent acquisitions Trae Young (acquired from Atlanta in January) and Anthony Davis (acquired from Dallas at the trade deadline), alongside homegrown pieces Alex Sarr, Bilal Coulibaly, and Kyshawn George.

Dybantsa himself said publicly: "If I go to DC, I'll make playoffs." The confidence is not misplaced. A lineup featuring Dybantsa, Trae Young, and Anthony Davis would be one of the more intriguing young ensembles in the Eastern Conference.

Who Else Is in the Conversation

Darryn Peterson remains in the mix for the No. 1 pick, though most scouts have Dybantsa clearly ahead. Cameron Boozer rounds out the top tier. The rest of the top five picks belong to the Jazz (No. 2), Grizzlies (No. 3), Bulls (No. 4), and Clippers (No. 5).

Barring a last-minute surprise, Dybantsa's name will be called first at Barclays Center in six days — a moment that will officially launch one of the most anticipated NBA careers since the draft class that included Luka Doncic and Trae Young.

What's Next

Pre-draft workouts conclude this week. Dybantsa has completed visits with the Wizards and Jazz, with both meetings described as going well. Expect the Wizards to make their selection official barring a trade that reshuffles the top pick — which the Giannis trade saga could conceivably trigger before June 23.
