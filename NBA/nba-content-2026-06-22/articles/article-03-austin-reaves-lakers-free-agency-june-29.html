Austin Reaves' $239M Decision: Lakers in Exclusive Window as June 29 Deadline Approaches

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 22, 2026

The NBA calendar turns to draft night tomorrow, but in Los Angeles, a different kind of deadline is quietly approaching. Austin Reaves has until June 29 — one week from today — to opt out of his $14.9 million player option and enter the free agent market that opens June 30.

The Los Angeles Lakers are in their exclusive negotiating window right now, and the decision sitting in front of Reaves is one of the biggest of his career: stay in LA on a five-year, $239 million max deal, or test the open market where the Brooklyn Nets wait with a four-year, $178 million offer and an opportunity to be the face of a franchise rebuild.

Why the Lakers Are Favored

Multiple league executives believe the Lakers remain the strong favorites to retain Reaves — but only if they're willing to pay near his market value. The good news for Lakers fans is that every indication points to Los Angeles offering exactly that.

The retention pitch has multiple layers. Reaves has spoken publicly about his desire to remain a Laker for his entire career. The partnership with Luka Doncic — who arrived in LA this past season — gives Reaves a generational playmaking partner who makes everyone around him better. And the city of Los Angeles itself represents a platform that small-market alternatives simply cannot match.

Reaves' numbers last season made the pitch easy to justify. He averaged 23.3 points, 5.5 assists, 4.7 rebounds, and shot 49.0% from the field at age 28 — peak value, peak market, peak leverage.

The Nets' Offer and Other Suitors

Brooklyn has emerged as Reaves' most credible alternative. The Nets can offer a four-year max deal worth approximately $178 million — less than what LA can offer in both years and total dollars, but a significant contract by any measure. For a franchise still looking for its identity in the post-rebuild era, landing a player of Reaves' caliber would be a major statement.

The Atlanta Hawks and Detroit Pistons have also registered interest, though both are considered longshots compared to the Lakers-Nets competition at the top.

What separates Los Angeles's offer is the fifth year that Bird rights allow — no other team can match five seasons. That gap compounds significantly over time, potentially reaching a $60 million total difference between the best Lakers offer and the best anyone else can provide.

The "Not Just About Dollars" Factor

Reaves himself has been direct about his priorities. "It will not come down solely to dollars," he said when discussing his upcoming decision — a quote that multiple outlets have highlighted as the single most revealing statement in the Lakers' favor.

That framing matters. If this were purely a financial decision, Reaves could reasonably take the Lakers' max and call it done. The fact that he's flagged non-financial factors suggests his roots in LA, his relationships with teammates, and his buy-in to the Lakers' championship vision are real factors — not just PR talking points.

What's Next

The June 29 opt-out deadline is seven days away. If Reaves declines his option, free agency opens June 30 and negotiations can proceed with any team. If he doesn't opt out, he returns to the Lakers on his current $14.9M deal — an unlikely outcome given where his market value sits.

For now, the Lakers hold the leverage and the dollars. The question is whether Reaves' love of LA translates into a signature on a five-year max. One week to find out.
