Ja Morant Trade Market Frozen Until Draft Night — Then It Could Explode

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 22, 2026

The Ja Morant trade market hasn't stalled because of a lack of interest. It has stalled on purpose.

Memphis Grizzlies have deliberately pumped the brakes on trade discussions involving their star point guard, choosing to wait until tomorrow's NBA Draft resolves the Giannis Antetokounmpo sweepstakes before formally entering the market. The strategy is calculated: once Giannis lands somewhere, a cascade of dominoes follows — and Memphis wants to know exactly where those dominoes fall before naming a price for Morant.

Why Memphis Is Waiting

The Giannis trade connection is direct. The Miami Heat are the most likely destination for Morant as a Plan B scenario — but only if they miss out on Giannis. If Heat president Pat Riley gets his first choice, he won't be calling Memphis about Morant. If he doesn't, the phone rings immediately.

For the Grizzlies, running a Morant trade process while Giannis remains undecided means potentially selling at a discount to teams that don't yet know what their roster looks like. Waiting costs nothing — and could cost everything if they jump too soon.

"The feeling is that the Grizzlies are still likely to trade Morant," per SI, but the timing is everything.

The Suitors

Beyond Miami, the field of interested teams is growing. The Phoenix Suns have been named an ambitious target for Morant, seeking a dynamic point guard to pair with their young core as they attempt to return to contention. The Sacramento Kings were actively pursuing Morant at the trade deadline and remain engaged months later. The Chicago Bulls have also been floated in trade projections, though at lower confidence levels.

What makes Morant appealing is obvious on the court — 24.4 points per game last season, elite athleticism, and highlight-reel playmaking that elevates teammates. He is one of the most marketable players in the league.

What makes the trade complicated is the contract. Morant has two years and $87 million remaining — $42.1 million next season and $44 million in 2027-28. That's a significant commitment for any acquiring team, and it means any trade package back to Memphis needs to be substantial.

The No. 3 Pick Factor

Memphis adds another layer of complexity: the Grizzlies hold the No. 3 overall pick in tomorrow's draft. That asset could be packaged with Morant to sweeten a deal, used independently to add a building block, or held back entirely.

If Memphis lands a top prospect at No. 3 AND moves Morant for a significant return of picks and players, they could be engineering one of the more impressive full resets in recent NBA history — clearing Morant's contract, upgrading their draft capital, and resetting their timeline.

What's Next

Tomorrow's draft is the trigger. Once picks start going off the board and the Giannis situation resolves, the Morant market unfreezes almost instantly. The Heat, Suns, Kings, and others will know their rosters, their needs, and their budgets — and Memphis will be ready to listen.

The post-June 23 NBA is going to move fast. Morant is among the most significant pieces yet to find a new home. That changes by the end of this week.
