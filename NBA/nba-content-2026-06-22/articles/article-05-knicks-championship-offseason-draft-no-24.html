Knicks Championship Offseason Begins: No. 24 Pick Tomorrow, Extensions to Follow

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 22, 2026

The confetti has been swept off the Canyon of Heroes. The two million fans who lined Broadway have gone home. And now the New York Knicks face the hardest part of winning a championship: doing it again.

Tomorrow's NBA Draft marks the opening chapter of New York's first offseason as reigning champions — the first time in 53 years the franchise has entered a summer with a title to defend. The Knicks have three picks in the 2026 draft: No. 24 in the first round, plus No. 31 (acquired via Washington) and No. 55 in the second round. None of those picks are expected to be rotation players on Day 1. But in a championship window built on depth, every pick counts.

The Picks: Finding the Right Pieces

At No. 24, the Knicks are looking for affordable contributors — players who can develop behind the veteran core without putting strain on an already ballooning payroll. Champions rarely find stars in the mid-to-late first round, but they regularly find role players who help sustain dynasties.

With two second-round picks as well, New York's draft night is about restocking depth on minimum contracts. The Knicks' front office, which engineered the championship core with creative roster building, will be looking for the same kind of value that turned Austin Reaves from an undrafted player into a max-contract candidate.

The Real Work: Free Agency Starts June 30

Draft night is preamble. The true test begins when free agency opens June 30, one week from tomorrow.

New York's offseason checklist is daunting:

OG Anunoby — The lockdown wing who was instrumental in the championship run needs to be retained. Anunoby is entering unrestricted free agency and will command a max or near-max contract. Losing him would be a massive blow to a defense built around his ability to guard the opponent's best player.

Karl-Anthony Towns — The big man who gave the Knicks elite spacing and interior scoring is also a free agent. Towns is max money after his championship performance. The question isn't whether he deserves it — it's whether the Knicks can absorb the luxury-tax consequences of paying both him and Anunoby at max level.

Jalen Brunson Extension — The Finals MVP and undisputed face of the franchise famously left roughly $113 million on the table when he signed a team-friendly extension in 2024, giving the Knicks the cap space to build a winner. Now it's the organization's turn to reward that sacrifice with a market-rate commitment.

The Luxury-Tax Math

Retaining Anunoby, Towns, and negotiating Brunson's extension while carrying existing roster contracts is going to push the Knicks deep into luxury-tax territory. The bill will be staggering — potentially making New York one of the highest-spending teams in league history on an annual basis.

But winning changes the calculus. Owner James Dolan and the organization now have championship proof that the investment works. And Knicks fans, who waited 53 years for a title, aren't going to tolerate a teardown of a proven winner.

What's Next

Tomorrow night at No. 24, the Knicks make their first pick as champions. Then free agency opens, the real negotiations begin, and New York finds out whether championship goodwill is enough to convince OG, KAT, and Brunson to stay together for another run.

The first repeat attempt in franchise history doesn't happen automatically. It gets built — one decision at a time, starting tonight.
