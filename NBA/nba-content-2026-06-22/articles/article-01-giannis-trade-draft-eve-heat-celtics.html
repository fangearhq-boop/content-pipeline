Giannis Trade — Draft Eve: Heat Remain Frontrunner as Celtics Make Late Push

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | June 22, 2026

The NBA world wakes up on draft eve with one question consuming every front office, every fan, and every trade analyst: where will Giannis Antetokounmpo play basketball next season?

With the 2026 NBA Draft set for tomorrow night at Barclays Center in Brooklyn — tip-off at 8 PM ET on ABC and ESPN — the pressure on the Milwaukee Bucks and rival suitors has never been higher. Bucks co-owner Jimmy Haslam made it clear weeks ago that he wanted the two-time MVP's situation resolved before draft picks started flying. That window is now hours away from closing.

Heat in the Driver's Seat

League sources continue to characterize the Miami Heat as the frontrunner for Giannis's services. The motivation is simple: Giannis "wants to be in Miami," according to multiple reports. The Heat's trade package — centered on Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Jaime Jaquez Jr., and the No. 13 overall pick in tomorrow's draft — has been on the table for weeks and remains Milwaukee's most compelling offer.

Miami's pitch goes beyond just assets. The franchise offers a proven winning culture, a major market, and a warm-weather lifestyle that Giannis has reportedly found appealing. The Heat previously reached the Finals under Erik Spoelstra's system, and adding a generational talent like Giannis would instantly make them a championship contender again.

For the Bucks, the appeal is clear: Herro gives them a proven offensive creator and All-Star-caliber guard, Ware is one of the most intriguing young bigs in the league, and the No. 13 pick adds another draft asset to an already rebuilding roster.

Celtics Intensify Their Push

Don't write off the Boston Celtics. Multiple reports Monday confirmed that Boston has "intensified" its Giannis trade efforts in the final 24 hours, with sources indicating the Celtics are exploring a multi-team deal that could involve moving star forward Jaylen Brown to a third team.

The Celtics' calculus is different from Miami's. Rather than leading with a package centered on their best players, Boston appears willing to restructure its roster in ways that would maximize what they can offer Milwaukee. Giannis is reportedly on the Celtics' list of preferred destinations, and the franchise would commit to signing him to a long-term max extension upon arrival.

A multi-team deal involving Jaylen Brown would be seismic — Brown is a max player and one of the most accomplished players of his generation. But if it unlocks Giannis for Boston, the Celtics might view it as a price worth paying.

The Bucks' Calculation

Milwaukee has been methodical throughout this process. The Bucks simultaneously pursued an additional first-round draft pick beyond their No. 10 selection, signaling to rival teams they wanted to maximize returns. The organization has been patient, but tomorrow's draft creates a hard deadline.

Once picks begin changing hands on draft night, the trade calculus shifts dramatically. Teams that land impact players could change their free agency plans. The post-draft landscape will look different — which is exactly why Haslam wanted this done beforehand.

Giannis has two years remaining on his current deal and becomes eligible for a four-year, $275 million supermax extension on October 1. Whichever team lands him holds enormous leverage to keep him long-term — a significant factor in Milwaukee's decision to move now rather than risk losing him for nothing.

What's Next

The next 24 hours will be among the most consequential in recent NBA history. A Giannis deal could drop at any moment — whether tonight before draft coverage begins, or in a dramatic draft-night announcement that reshapes the entire 2026 lottery picture.

Heat or Celtics? A dark horse? Or does Giannis reach October 1 still in Milwaukee, eligible to negotiate that $275M extension? Draft night answers are coming — sooner than anyone expected.
