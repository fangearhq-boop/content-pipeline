### Long-Form Post Shai Gilgeous-Alexander answered back. Oklahoma City answered back. And the Western Conference Finals is now tied. The Thunder defeated the San Antonio Spurs 122-113 in WCF Game 2 at Paycom Center on Wednesday night, with SGA delivering 30 points, 9 assists, and a +14 finish on 50 percent shooting. It was the kind of performance that reminded everyone why he's a two-time MVP — focused, decisive, and in command. But the margin between these two teams wasn't just about stars. The Spurs committed 21 turnovers — the Thunder only 9. Those 12 extra possessions told the story of San Antonio's second-half frustrations, and OKC made every one of them count. Still, the Spurs showed fight. Victor Wembanyama went for 21 points and 17 rebounds, and Stephon Castle — stepping in for the injured De'Aaron Fox — posted 28 points and 8 assists. The foundation is there. The injury news looms large. Both Jalen Williams (hamstring) and Dylan Harper (right leg) exited Game 2 early. Combined with Fox's ankle injury, the Spurs are heading to San Antonio for Game 3 with serious roster questions. Series tied 1-1.
