### STORY 2: Thunder 122-113 Spurs — WCF Game 2

**Long-Form Post (9:30 PM ET)**
Shai Gilgeous-Alexander answered back. Oklahoma City answered back. And the Western Conference Finals is now tied.

The Thunder defeated the San Antonio Spurs 122-113 in WCF Game 2 at Paycom Center on Wednesday night, with SGA delivering 30 points, 9 assists, and a +14 finish on 50 percent shooting. It was the kind of performance that reminded everyone why he's a two-time MVP — focused, decisive, and in command.

But the margin between these two teams wasn't just about stars. The Spurs committed 21 turnovers — the Thunder only 9. Those 12 extra possessions told the story of San Antonio's second-half frustrations, and OKC made every one of them count.

Still, the Spurs showed fight. Victor Wembanyama went for 21 points and 17 rebounds, and Stephon Castle — stepping in for the injured De'Aaron Fox — posted 28 points and 8 assists. The foundation is there.

The injury news looms large. Both Jalen Williams (hamstring) and Dylan Harper (right leg) exited Game 2 early. Combined with Fox's ankle injury, the Spurs are heading to San Antonio for Game 3 with serious roster questions.

Series tied 1-1. Game 3: Friday May 22, 8:30 PM ET, AT&T Center, San Antonio, NBC/Peacock.

### STORY 3: WCF Injury Watch — Harper, Williams, Fox

Then it got worse. Jalen Williams, the Thunder's second-best player, left Game 2 in the first half with tightness in the same left hamstring that had cost him six games earlier in the playoffs. He did not return.

Stephon Castle — already filling in for Fox — lost his backcourt partner Dylan Harper in the third quarter. Harper landed awkwardly on his right leg twice while challenging SGA at the rim and going for a rebound. He was ruled out for the fourth quarter and did not return.

Neither team gave post-game injury updates. Nobody knows who plays Friday.

What we do know: Wembanyama has averaged 31 points and 20.5 rebounds through two WCF games. He will be asked to carry even more. The question is whether the humans around him are healthy enough to help.

**Long-Form Post (4:00 PM ET)**
The Western Conference Finals were billed as a heavyweight showdown between two of basketball's most gifted young stars. Through two games, Victor Wembanyama and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander have delivered on that promise — but the injury cloud over San Antonio is threatening to change the series entirely.

The Spurs head home to AT&T Center for Game 3 on Friday night with three key contributors in various degrees of doubt. De'Aaron Fox has been out with his ankle since the second round. Dylan Harper left Game 2 with a right leg injury. Jalen Williams — who missed six playoff games earlier this season — exited with hamstring tightness.

Wembanyama averaged 31 points and 20.5 rebounds through the first two games. In a healthy series, that might be enough to keep things interesting. Against a Thunder team that had only 9 turnovers in Game 2 while San Antonio had 21, the margin for error is thin.

Oklahoma City comes in with confidence and a tied series. SGA just had his best game of the WCF. The Thunder defense is sharp.

But San Antonio has home court for Game 3. The crowd will be loud. Wembanyama will be motivated. And playoff basketball has a way of defying rosters.
