ECF Game 2 Preview: Cavaliers Must Fix the Harden Problem or Fall Into a 0-2 Hole at MSG

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 21, 2026

The Cleveland Cavaliers blew a 22-point lead in four minutes of real time Monday night. They know exactly why it happened. They have roughly twelve hours before it happens again.

Game 2 of the Eastern Conference Finals tips tonight at Madison Square Garden — same building, same hostile crowd, same Jalen Brunson standing at the other end of the floor with the same formula Cleveland could not solve in Game 1. The Cavaliers need answers, and they need them before 8 PM ET.

The Harden Diagnosis

Knicks coach Mike Brown confirmed after Game 1 that his team targeted James Harden defensively in the fourth quarter. They attacked him on every switch, every help rotation, every moment he was in a position to be exploited. And Harden — finishing with 1-of-8 from three-point range and six turnovers — validated the strategy completely.

This is not a new problem for Cleveland's offense. Harden's postseason decline has been a subject of league-wide conversation for years, but in game contexts where he controls tempo and operates against passive defense, the veteran can still produce. What happened Monday was different: Brunson forced the issue, the Knicks got into running pace, and Harden's ball-handling became a liability rather than an asset.

The Cavaliers backed their player publicly after the game. That loyalty is admirable and probably strategically sound — alienating Harden in the middle of a playoff series would be worse than whatever Game 1 produced. But the scheme needs to change. Harden must be better protected or removed from situations where Brunson can hunt him repeatedly.

Mitchell's Late-Game Role

Donovan Mitchell scored 29 points in Game 1 on efficient shooting. His last basket came at the 8:19 mark of the fourth quarter. That's the problem.

Mitchell's production in the first three quarters of Game 1 was outstanding, but basketball playoff series are frequently decided in those final eight minutes when the game tightens and every possession matters. Cleveland's offensive structure in crunch time funneled through Harden, effectively removing Mitchell from being the decision-maker when pressure peaked. Brunson had no such limitation for the Knicks.

Against a building and a moment that will demand Cleveland's best player perform at his best, Mitchell cannot disappear late. His 29 first-three-quarter points are meaningful, but a Game 2 performance that mirrors Game 1's fourth-quarter silence will almost certainly produce the same result.

What the Knicks Will Do Again

Brunson will attack Harden. New York will push pace in transition. The MSG crowd will be a factor from the first minute. The Knicks lead this series and have established a formula that produced one of the largest comebacks in recent Conference Finals history.

That does not mean Cleveland cannot win tonight. Evan Mobley's 15-point, 14-rebound presence around the basket is a real asset. Mitchell at his best — engaged, aggressive, and involved late in games — is still one of the Eastern Conference's most dangerous offensive players. And the Cavaliers have every incentive to respond with the desperation that losing Game 2 on the road would demand.

What's Next

A Cavs win tonight ties the series 1-1 and shifts the ECF landscape entirely. A Knicks win puts Cleveland in the exact position they feared going into this series — down 2-0 with Games 3 and 4 returning to New York after a brief road trip.

The moment is Cleveland's to define. They had the Game 1 result within their grasp for three and a half quarters. The difference between a 22-point lead and a 1-0 series deficit tells you everything about how quickly these things can change — and how tonight could change them again. ECF Game 2: May 21, tonight, 8:00 PM ET, Madison Square Garden, ESPN. Series: Knicks lead 1-0.
