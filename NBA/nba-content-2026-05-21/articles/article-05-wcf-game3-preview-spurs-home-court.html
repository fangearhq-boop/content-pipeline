WCF Game 3 Preview: Spurs Head Home With Injury Questions and Wembanyama's Back Against the Wall

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 21, 2026

Victor Wembanyama averages 31 points and 20.5 rebounds through the first two games of the Western Conference Finals. He has put up 62 points and 41 rebounds against a team that finished 64-18 in the regular season. He has been, by any reasonable measure, the best player in the series.

And his team is about to play Game 3 without knowing for certain whether its next three most important contributors will be available.

The Spurs return to AT&T Center on Friday night for Game 3 against the Oklahoma City Thunder, tied 1-1, with De'Aaron Fox (ankle), Dylan Harper (right leg), and Jalen Williams (hamstring) all in various states of uncertainty. The crowd will be loud. The atmosphere will be electric. And Wembanyama may be playing with whatever remains of a rotation that looked depleted even before Wednesday night's injuries.

Home Court Means Something

The Spurs won the regular-season series against Oklahoma City 4-1, and AT&T Center was a significant reason why. San Antonio's crowd generates a specific kind of energy that has historically elevated Wembanyama's performances — the anticipation in the building whenever he touches the ball in the post, the collective gasp when he rises for a jump shot from a position no other center in the world attempts.

Game 3 represents a chance for San Antonio to reclaim momentum before the series goes sideways. A win Friday would put the Spurs back up 2-1 and restore a level of control that a healthy OKC roster makes extremely difficult. A loss would put them down 2-1 heading into Game 4 — still manageable, but the kind of deficit that requires the full rotation to recover from.

SGA's Momentum Is Real

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander's 30-point, 9-assist performance in Game 2 was a reminder that he remains one of basketball's elite offensive weapons. In a game where Williams exited early and the Thunder still won by nine points, SGA showed he can be a one-man solution when the roster around him is incomplete.

He is coming to San Antonio on a high. The question for the Spurs is whether Stephon Castle — who posted 28 points and 8 assists in Game 2 while already covering for Fox — can continue matching that production while also managing the defensive challenge of guarding SGA for extended stretches. That is an enormous ask for a second-year player, regardless of how impressive his postseason has been.

What a Wembanyama Carry Job Looks Like

Wembanyama's performance through two games has been extraordinary by any comparison — but he has had at least some support in both games. In Game 1, Harper's 24 points and 7 steals were critical. In Game 2, Castle's 28 points held the offense together despite the turnover hemorrhage.

A Wembanyama performance in Game 3 without Fox, without Harper, and potentially without a fully healthy supporting cast would test something that has not been fully tested in this series: how much can one player do when the margin for error around him is essentially zero? History suggests it is possible to win a playoff game that way. Sustaining it over a series is the harder question.

What's Next

Game 3 represents the first real fork in this series. Both teams have proven they can win; both teams now carry injury concerns that could flip the balance on any given night. Wembanyama will play. SGA will play. Everything else is uncertain.

The AT&T Center will be sold out and loud. San Antonio has home court for two games. For a Spurs team that has answered every challenge this postseason — winning the regular-season series, upsetting OKC at Paycom in Game 1, losing Game 2 to an SGA performance while shorthanded — the next challenge is the hardest yet. WCF Game 3: Friday May 22, 8:30 PM ET, AT&T Center, San Antonio, NBC/Peacock.
