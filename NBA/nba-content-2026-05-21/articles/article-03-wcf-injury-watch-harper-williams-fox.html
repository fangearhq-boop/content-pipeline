WCF Injury Crisis: Harper, Williams, and Fox All Uncertain for Game 3 in San Antonio

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 21, 2026

The Western Conference Finals were supposed to be a showcase for the NBA's next generation. Through two games, they have become something else entirely: a medical waiting room, with three of the series' most important players in various stages of doubt heading into Friday's Game 3 in San Antonio.

De'Aaron Fox. Dylan Harper. Jalen Williams. None of them played the full game Wednesday night. Two of them did not finish it. And as the series shifts to AT&T Center, the Spurs may be asking Victor Wembanyama to carry the heaviest load of his young career.

Fox: Out Since the Second Round

De'Aaron Fox's ankle has been the defining injury of this postseason for San Antonio. He suffered a right high ankle sprain in Game 4 of the second-round series against the Minnesota Timberwolves — an awkward landing on a loose-ball play — and has not played since. He sat out both WCF games.

Spurs coach Mitch Johnson has framed Fox's status as a series of day-to-day evaluations, avoiding any specific timeline. There is optimism within the organization that he can return at some point in the series, but that optimism has not translated into availability. Stephon Castle has handled the playmaking duties admirably in Fox's absence, but losing a player of Fox's caliber against Oklahoma City's defense is a structural handicap that no amount of individual effort fully covers.

Harper: A Concerning Exit

Dylan Harper's WCF Game 2 injury is the one that has generated the most immediate concern. The Spurs rookie — already starting in place of Fox and coming off a 24-point, 7-steal performance in Game 1 — landed awkwardly on his right leg twice in quick succession in the third quarter. First while challenging Gilgeous-Alexander at the rim. Then while contesting a rebound against Chet Holmgren.

Harper did not return. He was ruled out for the fourth quarter. The mechanism of the injury — two separate awkward landings in rapid succession — drew concern from observers and medical professionals who watched the broadcast. Neither the Spurs nor their medical staff offered any public assessment after the game.

The series without a healthy Harper looks fundamentally different. In two games, he had accumulated 31 points, 8 assists, and 7 steals — the kind of disruptive, multi-dimensional contribution that made him the Spurs' second most important player regardless of draft position or experience level.

Williams: The Same Hamstring

Jalen Williams' situation carries its own layer of anxiety because of what preceded it. The Thunder's forward missed six playoff games earlier in the postseason with left hamstring tightness. He had returned and appeared healthy for most of the WCF opener. Then, in the first half of Game 2, the NBC broadcast caught him being wrapped around the same left hamstring as he came to the bench.

He did not return. Oklahoma City won anyway, with SGA carrying the offensive load through 30 points and 9 assists. But a Williams absence for Games 3 and 4 in San Antonio would change the Thunder's defensive assignment considerably — and would give Wembanyama a slightly less complicated matchup than he faced when Williams was available.

What's Next

The next 48 hours will clarify — or fail to clarify — what the Spurs have available for Game 3. Johnson has managed Fox's return timeline carefully, and there is reason to think the same measured approach will govern Harper and Williams evaluations.

What is not in question is this: Wembanyama has already posted 62 points and 41 rebounds across two WCF games. He is playing at an extraordinary level regardless of roster. The question is whether he gets enough help — from whoever is healthy enough to suit up — to give San Antonio a real chance on their home floor. WCF Game 3: Friday May 22, 8:30 PM ET, AT&T Center, San Antonio, NBC/Peacock.
