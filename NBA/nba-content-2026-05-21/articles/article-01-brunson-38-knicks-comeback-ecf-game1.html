Brunson's 38-Point Masterpiece Completes Knicks' 22-Point ECF Game 1 Comeback Over Cavaliers

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 21, 2026

With 7:52 left in the fourth quarter and the score 93-71, Madison Square Garden was quiet. The Cleveland Cavaliers had just spent three quarters methodically dismantling the New York Knicks, and the Eastern Conference Finals looked like a formality. Donovan Mitchell had scored 29 points. The Cavaliers were in total control.

Then Jalen Brunson took over, and nothing was under control anymore.

The Knicks erupted for a 44-11 run over the final minutes of regulation and overtime, completing one of the most improbable comebacks in recent playoff history and defeating the Cavaliers 115-104 in overtime Monday night. Brunson finished with 38 points, 6 assists, 5 rebounds, and 3 steals — including 15 points on 7-of-9 shooting in the fourth quarter alone.

The Run That Changed Everything

The Knicks didn't make a subtle shift in momentum. They made a violent one. With the crowd at MSG growing increasingly restless, Brunson attacked every switch, forced contact at the rim, and began converting at a rate that made the Cavaliers' 22-point lead feel like a fiction.

Mitchell scored his final basket of the night at the 8:19 mark of the fourth quarter. After that, Cleveland got almost nothing from its primary scorer when it needed him most. Evan Mobley had contributed 15 points and 14 rebounds throughout, but the Cavs had no answer for what Brunson was doing to their defense in those final minutes.

With 20 seconds remaining in regulation, Brunson launched a floater off the backboard that rattled through to tie the game. Overtime belonged entirely to New York — the Knicks outscored Cleveland 14-3 in the extra period, capping a night that will be replayed on this city's highlight reels for years.

The Harden Problem

As much as this game was about Brunson's brilliance, it was equally about James Harden's collapse. The veteran guard was targeted by Knicks coach Mike Brown from the moment the fourth quarter began — and Brown was direct about it afterward, confirming the team went after Harden on every possession they could.

Harden finished with 15 points on 5-of-16 shooting, 1-of-8 from three-point range, and six turnovers. In the fourth quarter, as the Knicks mounted their comeback, he became a liability on both ends. The isolation dribbling that Cleveland had used to control tempo throughout the night broke down completely, and the defensive attention Brunson demanded created even more confusion for Harden to navigate.

The Cavaliers backed their guard publicly after the game, and there's reason to believe Harden can be more disciplined in Game 2. But what the Knicks uncovered Monday night — that attacking Harden creates a chain reaction of offensive breakdowns — is not something Cleveland can simply talk away before tonight's rematch.

Mitchell's Disappearing Act

Donovan Mitchell is one of the best offensive players in basketball. His 29 points on 12-of-23 shooting were a reminder of that. His silence in the final eight minutes was a reminder of something else: the Cavaliers' late-game offense has a vulnerability in high-pressure moments when Mitchell gets separated from the ball.

Cleveland had a near-certainty of winning with seven minutes left in the fourth. They did not win. Mitchell's production was front-loaded in a way that didn't account for what would happen when the Knicks began their push. The Cavaliers' offensive structure in crunch time — with Harden handling the ball and isolating in a way that froze Mitchell — is a structural issue that Game 2 will test.

What's Next

The Knicks hold a 1-0 series lead with Game 2 happening tonight at Madison Square Garden. Cleveland arrives knowing exactly what broke them in Game 1, which gives them the ability to fix it — but also knowing that Brunson can attack the same way again and produce the same results if they don't.

For New York, the challenge is sustaining this level without Brunson needing to be supernatural. He cannot drop 38 every night, but the framework he established — attack Harden, attack switches, force the issue in transition — is repeatable. ECF Game 2: May 21, tonight, 8:00 PM ET, Madison Square Garden, ESPN. Series: Knicks lead 1-0.
