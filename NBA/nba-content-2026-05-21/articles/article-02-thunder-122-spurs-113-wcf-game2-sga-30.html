SGA's 30-Point Response Lifts Thunder Past Spurs to Even WCF Series at 1-1

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 21, 2026

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander had one bad night against Victor Wembanyama in WCF Game 1. He made sure it was just the one.

Gilgeous-Alexander delivered 30 points, 9 assists, 2 blocks, and a plus-14 finish on 50 percent shooting as the Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the San Antonio Spurs 122-113 on Wednesday night at Paycom Center, evening the Western Conference Finals at 1-1. The two-time MVP was relentless, decisive, and in command of a game that the Thunder needed to win to prevent falling into an early 0-2 hole on their home floor.

SGA Takes Control

The difference between SGA in Game 1 and SGA in Game 2 was sharp. He attacked the rim with more aggression early, found his pull-up jumper at the mid-range and beyond, and got teammates involved with nine assists that kept the Thunder offense fluid enough to absorb the Spurs' resistance.

His plus-14 rating tells the story: when Gilgeous-Alexander was on the floor and engaged, Oklahoma City was the better team. The Spurs had no reliable answer for him without De'Aaron Fox, and the rotating coverage they tried only opened corners for his teammates.

On the defensive end, his two blocks and one steal contributed to a turnover differential that swung dramatically in OKC's favor. The Thunder committed nine turnovers all night. The Spurs committed 21.

The Turnover Problem

Twenty-one turnovers is a crushing number for any team in a Conference Finals game, and the Spurs paid for every one. Oklahoma City converted those extra possessions into transition opportunities that kept the Spurs from establishing any consistent offensive rhythm in the second half.

The numbers behind Stephon Castle's 28-point, 8-assist performance are genuinely impressive, but they also reflect the reality of what Castle is being asked to do: run an offense with two starters missing (Fox and Harper) while matching up against a defense built around neutralizing him. He kept the Spurs functional, but 21 turnovers as a team tells you the effort came at a cost.

Victor Wembanyama's 21 points and 17 rebounds — another monster line in a series that has already seen him post 41 and 24 in Game 1 — were largely a product of his own will. The infrastructure around him was leaking.

Injuries Cloud the Result

The series may be tied 1-1, but the bigger story from Wednesday night is the health of both rosters. Jalen Williams, Oklahoma City's second-best player, left the game in the first half with tightness in his left hamstring — the same hamstring that kept him out for six games earlier in these playoffs. He did not return.

On the Spurs' side, Dylan Harper — who had been heroic with 24 points and 7 steals in Game 1 — landed awkwardly on his right leg twice in the third quarter. Once while challenging Gilgeous-Alexander at the rim. Once while going up for a rebound against Chet Holmgren. He was ruled out for the fourth quarter.

Neither team provided post-game injury updates. The cloud heading into Game 3 is thick on both sides of the court.

What's Next

The series is tied and moving to San Antonio for Games 3 and 4. The AT&T Center crowd will be the loudest it has been all season, and Wembanyama will feed off that energy. But the Spurs need to know who is available before they can plan how to use them.

For OKC, the concern is whether Williams can return for Game 3 or whether the Thunder are operating at diminished capacity as well. A healthy SGA is still a 30-point performer on his off night. The rest of the series depends on how many bodies both coaches have to work with. WCF Game 3: Friday May 22, 8:30 PM ET, AT&T Center, San Antonio, NBC/Peacock.
