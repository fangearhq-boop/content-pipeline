Murray Erupts for 37 Points, 10 Threes, and a Half-Court Buzzer-Beater as Nuggets Roll Jazz

By Marcus Cole April 2, 2026

A Night in Rare Company

There are nights when a shooter simply cannot miss, when every release feels perfect before the ball leaves the fingertips. Jamal Murray had one of those transcendent evenings Wednesday against the Utah Jazz, finishing with 37 points on 10 made three-pointers as the Denver Nuggets rolled to a 130-117 victory.

Ten threes in a single game places Murray in rare company. It is a number that belongs in highlight packages and record books, not a mid-week regular-season result. But the box score was only half the story.

The Half-Court Moment

As the first quarter's final seconds ticked away, Murray collected the ball near midcourt and launched a half-court shot at the buzzer. It went in. The shot was not the kind that changes the final score or decides a playoff series, but it captured something real about where Murray and the Nuggets stand heading into the postseason — loose, confident, and playing like a team that has nothing to prove and everything to gain.

Denver won 130-117, a convincing margin that belied how effortlessly the Nuggets dispatched a Utah team that had been competitive at home. Murray's shooting display set the tone from the opening minutes and never relented.

Denver's Dangerous Combination

The Nuggets enter the final week of the regular season with Nikola Jokic posting his customary near-triple-double numbers — 31 triple-double games on the season — alongside a Murray who has found his rhythm at precisely the right moment. Denver is fourth in the Western Conference and has clinched a playoff berth, meaning the games remaining are about positioning, momentum, and health.

But the Nuggets at full strength, with Jokic operating as the most statistically complete center in NBA history and Murray capable of exploding for 37-point, 10-three nights, represent a matchup nightmare for any playoff opponent. Wednesday was not just a regular-season performance. It was a warning.

What's Next

Denver will finish the regular season looking to solidify their seeding before the Play-In Tournament begins April 14. Murray's current form, combined with Jokic's consistent excellence, gives the Nuggets as complete a one-two punch as any team remaining in the Western Conference. If this version of Jamal Murray shows up in the playoffs, Denver's path to a deep run is very much alive.
