Wembanyama Delivers Back-to-Back 40-Point Double-Doubles as Spurs Win 10th Straight

By Jake Torres April 2, 2026

A Franchise First on the Road

Victor Wembanyama had never done it before. No San Antonio Spur had. On Wednesday night at Chase Center, in one of the loudest arenas in the Western Conference, the 22-year-old phenom made franchise history — again.

Wembanyama posted 41 points and 18 rebounds as the San Antonio Spurs defeated the Golden State Warriors 127-113, extending their winning streak to 10 consecutive games. It was his second straight 40-point double-double, a performance combination no Spur has ever produced on back-to-back nights. He finished the regular season's penultimate week as the most dominant player on the planet, and the numbers backed it up completely.

The No. 1 Seed Math

The Spurs' win carried seismic implications beyond the scoreboard. San Antonio improved to 58-18, closing to within 2 games of the Oklahoma City Thunder (60-16) in the race for the Western Conference's top seed. With seven games remaining, the gap is narrow — but the more important number for the Spurs is 4-1.

That is San Antonio's record against Oklahoma City this season. In the NBA's tiebreaker system, head-to-head record is the first tiebreaker applied when two teams finish with identical win-loss records. If the Spurs can close the gap and both teams finish level, San Antonio claims the No. 1 seed by virtue of winning four of five head-to-head matchups. The Spurs know the math. Their opponents know the math. Every remaining game is played with that calculation looming.

The Most Sustained Run in the NBA

San Antonio has gone 23-2 since February 1 — the best stretch of sustained excellence by any team in the league over that span. The Spurs have not just been hot; they have been historically consistent, absorbing a schedule that included marquee road games and responding with a level of defensive and offensive execution that observers have compared to the franchise's dynasty years under Gregg Popovich.

Wembanyama is the engine. His season averages of 24.5 points, 11.4 rebounds, and 3.1 assists per game don't fully capture his impact on both ends of the floor. His shot-blocking changes how opponents approach the paint, and his emerging ability to take and make 40-point games on demand elevates San Antonio's ceiling in a way that was only theoretical when he entered the league.

What's Next

The Spurs close the regular season with seven games remaining, each one a potential step toward either the No. 1 seed or a comfortable playoff position that sets up favorable first-round matchups. If Wembanyama can maintain even a fraction of this form into the postseason, San Antonio's emerging case as a dark-horse championship contender will only grow louder. The NBA's most compelling story of the 2025-26 season isn't in Oklahoma City or Boston — it's in San Antonio, and it's only two decades old.
