Curry Targeting Sunday Return as Warriors Cling to Play-In Spot With 7 Games Left

By Jake Torres April 2, 2026

The Moment Is Almost Here

After 26 games missed, multiple setbacks, and weeks of cautious optimism followed by quiet concern, Stephen Curry appears finally close to returning. Reports indicate the Golden State Warriors star is targeting Sunday's game against the Houston Rockets as his comeback, with Tuesday against Sacramento serving as a contingency if the next few days of practice reveal any lingering discomfort.

The news follows Wednesday's milestone: Curry's participation in a live 5-on-5 scrimmage — the first time he had been cleared for full-contact practice since injuring his knee on January 30. Coach Steve Kerr cautioned that the next few days will be critical, but the scrimmage was the green light the Warriors organization had been waiting for before any return announcement.

The Numbers Tell the Story

Golden State's record with Curry this season is 23-16. Without him, the Warriors have gone 12-22 — a .353 winning percentage that would represent one of the worst full-season marks in the league. The resulting overall record of 36-39 has the Warriors in 10th place in the Western Conference, holding the final available Play-In spot by a thin margin over Portland.

Before his injury, Curry was averaging 27.2 points per game on 46.8% shooting from the field and 39.1% from three-point range across 39 appearances. Those are numbers that fundamentally change how opposing coaches prepare their game plans — and their absence has left Golden State without the defensive attention-drawing that opens the offense for everyone else.

Seven Games to Save the Season

The Warriors' season is compressed into seven remaining regular-season games, each of which now carries outsized weight. Portland sits just one game behind Golden State in the race for that final Play-In berth, meaning a single losing streak without corresponding wins from San Antonio or Houston could push the Warriors entirely out of the postseason picture.

Even a partial Curry — on a minutes restriction, working his way back to full conditioning — changes the calculus dramatically. His presence on the floor forces opposing defenses to account for the possibility of a step-back three from anywhere on the court. That spacing opens driving lanes and second-chance opportunities that simply do not exist in the current Warriors lineup.

What's Next

If Curry returns Sunday against Houston, the Warriors will have six games remaining to nail down a Play-In berth. The Play-In Tournament begins April 14, with the playoffs tipping off April 18. A fully healthy Curry, even playing 25-28 minutes per game in the first few outings, gives Golden State a legitimate chance to advance deep into May. The question is whether his knee cooperates with the urgency of the moment. If it does, the Warriors' long-suffering fans may have their moment after all.
