The West No. 1 Seed Race Has a Tiebreaker Twist: Spurs Went 4-1 Against the Thunder

By Marcus Cole April 2, 2026

Two Games. One Tiebreaker.

Oklahoma City enters the final week of the NBA regular season at 60-16, two games ahead of the San Antonio Spurs (58-18) in the race for the Western Conference's No. 1 seed. On the surface, the race looks comfortable for the Thunder. But there is a detail embedded in the season series that changes the entire complexion of the competition.

The Spurs went 4-1 against Oklahoma City during the 2025-26 season. Under the NBA's tiebreaker rules, head-to-head record is the first criterion applied when two teams finish with identical win-loss records. If San Antonio can close the 2-game gap over the remaining seven games — meaning the Spurs would need to finish roughly two games better than OKC — the No. 1 seed goes to San Antonio, not Oklahoma City.

The Spurs' Unprecedented Run

San Antonio has gone 23-2 since February 1 — the best sustained run by any team in the Western Conference over that span. Victor Wembanyama just delivered back-to-back 40-point double-doubles, including Wednesday's 41-point, 18-rebound performance against Golden State. The Spurs are not just hot. They are playing at a level that is making longtime NBA observers reach for historical comparisons.

ESPN analysts have openly discussed whether this young Spurs group, led by a 22-year-old who appears to be reaching his ceiling faster than anyone anticipated, could be one of the most surprising championship contenders the league has seen in a generation. San Antonio's tiebreaker advantage over OKC means they have not just been beating everyone else — they have been beating the team directly in front of them.

The Thunder Are No Slouches

Oklahoma City's 60-16 record is not the mark of a team that will simply yield. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has extended his NBA-record streak of consecutive 20-point games past 128, and the Thunder's depth and defensive cohesion make them one of the most complete teams in the league. The race will require the Spurs to run down a genuinely excellent team, not simply a stumbling front-runner.

The Thunder's 17-2 record since the All-Star break tells its own story. Both teams are playing extraordinary basketball. Seven games will determine which of them enters the 2026 playoffs as the top seed and earns home-court advantage through the Western Conference bracket.

What's Next

Both teams play out the remaining week of the regular season before the playoffs begin April 18. The No. 1 seed carries home-court advantage through the Western Conference Finals and represents the clearest path to the NBA Finals for either franchise. For the Spurs, it would also signal something larger — that the rebuild is not just complete, but that this team is a genuine contender for the league's ultimate prize. The next seven games will answer that question.
