### STORY 2: Josh Hart's Historic Night

#### Long-Form Post — 11:00 AM ET

Josh Hart finished Game 1 of the NBA Finals with 3 points.

That tells you almost nothing about what he actually did.

In 27 minutes, Hart grabbed 15 rebounds, dished out 6 assists, and recorded 4 steals. He also added a block. He shot 1-for-5 from the floor — the low scoring total that would normally get a player benched in a Finals game.

Instead, Hart was arguably the most important player on the court.

He is only the second player in NBA Finals history to post at least 15 rebounds, 5 assists, and 4 steals in a single game. The other player? Larry Bird. In 1986.

In the final minute, Hart corralled a Wembanyama turnover and held the ball to drain the clock on the fastbreak, effectively putting the game away for New York.

The Knicks won the minutes Hart was on the court by 22 points.

3 points. Larry Bird company. Biggest win of the postseason.

That is what Josh Hart gives the New York Knicks.

### STORY 5: Giannis Trade Buzz

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

While the NBA Finals are playing out in San Antonio, the league's biggest offseason story is already taking shape.

ESPN's Shams Charania reported that the Milwaukee Bucks are "open for business on trade calls and offers" for Giannis Antetokounmpo. Two teams have moved immediately: the Miami Heat and the Portland Trail Blazers are both described as "already in pursuit."

Miami's pitch is significant. The Heat can construct a deal around Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, and multiple first-round picks — all of their own selections from 2026 through 2032. Antetokounmpo would join a franchise with a culture of winning and a track record of maximizing star players.

Portland's chances, though, appear slim. League sources tell NBC Sports that Giannis prefers the Eastern Conference, and the Trail Blazers are geographically outside his preference zone.

The Cleveland Cavaliers — fresh off a playoff exit at the hands of the very Knicks who now lead the Finals — were briefly linked to Giannis, with Evan Mobley as the centerpiece. But Cleveland's front office is described as "vehemently" opposed to trading Mobley.

Insiders caution the market is "tepid so far." The expectation is that Antetokounmpo's future gets resolved around the June 23 NBA Draft — when teams have maximum clarity on their assets.

The Finals aren't over. But the next era of the NBA is already starting to form.
