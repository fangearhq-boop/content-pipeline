Shams: Bucks Open for Business on Giannis — Heat and Blazers Lead the Chase

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 4, 2026

The NBA Finals are not finished. Game 2 between the Knicks and Spurs is still two days away. And yet the league's biggest offseason story is already taking shape.

ESPN's Shams Charania reported this week that the Milwaukee Bucks are "open for business on trade calls and offers" for Giannis Antetokounmpo, the 10-time All-Star who has spent his entire career in Wisconsin. Two teams have already made their interest known: the Miami Heat and the Portland Trail Blazers are both described as "already in pursuit."

How this trade market resolves will shape the next several years of the NBA more than any other single transaction. Giannis is 31 years old, entering the prime-to-peak stretch of his career, and still widely considered one of the five best players in the league. The team that lands him — if anyone does — becomes an immediate championship contender.

Miami's Case

The Miami Heat appear to be the most credible suitor. Miami can construct a deal around Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, and multiple first-round picks — the Heat control all of their own first-round selections from 2026 through 2032, an extraordinarily valuable collection of assets.

There is also reported mutual interest. Antetokounmpo, who has publicly stated his preference for winning and franchise stability, views Miami as a franchise that has a proven track record of developing and extending star players' careers. The Heat's culture, built under Pat Riley and now under Jimmy Butler's successors, aligns with how Giannis describes his ideal environment.

Milwaukee would receive a young cornerstone in Ware, a proven offensive contributor in Herro, and enough draft capital to rebuild quickly. It is a package that makes basketball sense for both sides.

Portland's Slim Chances

The Trail Blazers are aggressive, but insiders have poured cold water on their chances. Per NBC Sports, league sources indicate Antetokounmpo prefers to stay in the Eastern Conference — a preference that immediately limits Portland's appeal regardless of what assets they might offer. The Blazers are a Western Conference team in a smaller market, which works against them on both geography and preference grounds.

Portland's interest is genuine, and they have assets to offer after several years of rebuilding. But "unlikely" is the word most insiders are using when asked about a Blazers-Giannis pairing.

Cleveland's Internal Debate

The Cleveland Cavaliers were briefly linked to the Antetokounmpo trade market — fresh off a playoff exit at the hands of the Knicks in the Eastern Conference, a Giannis acquisition would instantly re-insert Cleveland into title contention. Evan Mobley is the name most frequently attached to a hypothetical package heading back to Milwaukee.

But Cleveland's front office is described as "vehemently" opposed to trading Mobley. The Cavaliers view their young defensive anchor as a cornerstone piece, not a trade chip, and there appears to be genuine internal conflict about whether any external acquisition is worth parting with him.

What's Next

The expectation across the league is that Antetokounmpo's future gets resolved around the June 23 NBA Draft — when teams have maximum clarity on their assets, picks, and roster construction going into free agency. The draft is already significant this year with the Washington Wizards holding the No. 1 overall pick. Adding a Giannis trade to the proceedings would make it one of the most consequential single days in recent NBA history.

For now, insiders describe the overall market as "tepid" — teams are interested, but no one has submitted anything close to a formal offer. The real negotiations begin once Milwaukee signals it is ready to move and the Finals are concluded.

The question is not whether Giannis gets traded. The question is whether any offer meets the Bucks' standard for what a franchise player of his caliber is worth. That bar is high. And Miami, more than anyone else right now, appears closest to clearing it.
