Game 2 Friday: What the Spurs Must Fix Before Brunson and the Knicks Put This Away

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 4, 2026

The San Antonio Spurs are down 1-0 in the 2026 NBA Finals and hosting a team that has won 12 consecutive playoff games by an average of more than 22 points per game.

They have 48 hours to figure out how to win one.

Game 2 tips off Friday, June 5 at 8:30 PM ET on ABC from Frost Bank Center. San Antonio enters as the -218 favorite, with the Knicks at +180. The market still believes in the Spurs at home. After Wednesday's performance, the question is whether San Antonio believes in itself enough to make the necessary adjustments before the series slips completely out of reach.

The Three Adjustments Spurs Must Make

The first adjustment is the simplest and the hardest: Victor Wembanyama needs to shoot better. His 6-of-21 mark in Game 1 was an aberration by his standards. The Knicks contested every mid-range pull-up and closed out hard on every three-point attempt. San Antonio's offense functioned adequately around his 26 points, but when those 26 came on 21 attempts, the efficiency cost was severe. Game 2 requires a Wembanyama who converts at closer to his postseason average.

The second adjustment involves Wembanyama's defensive load. Head coach Mitch Johnson asked his franchise player to cover enormous defensive space in Game 1 — guarding Karl-Anthony Towns on the perimeter while simultaneously protecting the rim from Brunson drives. That dual responsibility is what appears to have drained Wembanyama by the fourth quarter. The Spurs need to design more help rotations that take some of that coverage off Wemby's shoulders, even if it means giving up some open looks from role players.

The third adjustment is role-player production. Devin Vassell shot nearly 40% from three during the Western Conference Finals against Oklahoma City. Julian Champagnie contributed significant stretch scoring throughout the playoffs. Neither made a sustained impact in Game 1. If those two players shoot well in Game 2, the spacing opens for Wembanyama in the pick-and-roll and makes life considerably more difficult for the Knicks defensively.

What the Knicks Must Maintain

New York's goal in Game 2 is straightforward: do not change what got them here. The Knicks' 12-game winning streak was built on consistent execution, late-game poise, and a roster depth that does not require any single player to carry the load on any given night.

The most important element heading into Game 2 is health. Josh Hart, Mitchell Robinson (playing with a broken right pinkie), and OG Anunoby will all need to be available and effective. Robinson's physical presence against Wembanyama at the rim — drawing fouls and competing for offensive rebounds — was a difference-maker on Wednesday night even if it did not show in his final numbers.

Brunson's late-game form is particularly encouraging. He has now scored 13+ points in the fourth quarter of a Finals game, which means the Spurs must plan for a version of Brunson who gets better as the game goes on. Stephon Castle's assignment — slowing Brunson all night while managing his own foul trouble — becomes more complicated when the player he is guarding peaks in the final 12 minutes.

The Historical Context

Teams that fall behind 2-0 in the NBA Finals win the championship approximately 5% of the time. San Antonio cannot afford to let the series shift to New York with the Knicks holding a commanding lead. The Frost Bank Center crowd will be energized and desperate on Friday. The Spurs know how to channel that energy — they have done it throughout this postseason.

But the Knicks have proven, as recently as Wednesday night, that they can quiet a building and take a lead regardless of the environment. Brunson's corner three with two minutes left silenced Frost Bank Center completely.

What's Next

Game 2 tips off Friday at 8:30 PM ET on ABC. If San Antonio wins, the series returns to its expected competitive form heading into two Madison Square Garden games on June 8 and June 10. If New York wins, the Knicks will head home 2-0 with a chance to close out the series in front of the loudest building in basketball.

The Spurs' adjustments in the next 48 hours will define whether this is a series or a sweep. For a franchise that prides itself on preparation and adaptability, Friday night is the first real test of what this young Spurs team is truly made of when their backs are against the wall.
