### Long-Form Post Oklahoma City came into Los Angeles and did exactly what great teams do — they let the Lakers believe for a half, then took over and destroyed them. In Game 3 on Saturday night, the Thunder beat the Lakers 131-108 to take a 3-0 series lead. Oklahoma City is now 7-0 in the 2026 playoffs and looking every bit like a championship team. The story of the night was Ajay Mitchell. The 22-year-old guard posted 24 points, 10 assists, 3 steals, and zero turnovers while shooting 10-of-17. According to ESPN, that combination makes him just the fourth player since 1990 to record 20+ points, 10+ assists, 3+ steals, and zero turnovers in a playoff game — joining Magic Johnson, Chris Paul, and Tyrese Haliburton. Here's the wild part: the Lakers actually led at halftime. They hit 11 three-pointers in the first two quarters and held a 2-point edge. Then Oklahoma City — SGA (23 pts, 9 ast), Holmgren (18 pts, 9-14 FG), Wallace (16 pts) — turned it on and outscored Los Angeles by 25 in the second half. LeBron James finished 7-of-19 from the field for 19 points. Hachimura led the Lakers with 21. Reaves had 17 with 9 assists. It wasn't enough. Without Luka Doncic, who remains uncleared for contact, LA simply doesn't have the firepower to sustain a run against this Thunder team. ### Image Post Caption Oklahoma City 7-0. ### Long-Form Post Cleveland's season isn't over — and Donovan Mitchell made absolutely sure of that on Saturday night. Detroit still leads, but the Cavs got a crucial home win to avoid falling into an almost certain elimination hole. The milestone: Mitchell reached 2,000 career postseason points during this game, achieving the mark in his 73rd playoff appearance.
