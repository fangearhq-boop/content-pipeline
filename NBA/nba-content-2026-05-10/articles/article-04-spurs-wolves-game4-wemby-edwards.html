Wemby vs. Edwards: Game 4 Tonight as Spurs Aim for Commanding Series Lead Over Timberwolves

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 10, 2026

Every time Victor Wembanyama and Anthony Edwards share a playoff floor, the basketball world stops and watches. Tonight, they do it again. Game 4 of the Western Conference Semifinals between the San Antonio Spurs and Minnesota Timberwolves tips off at 7:30 PM ET on NBC/Peacock at Target Center in Minneapolis. The Spurs lead the series 2-1 and can take a commanding 3-1 advantage; the Wolves need a win to level the series and breathe life back into their season.

Wemby's Game 3 Was Something Else

Victor Wembanyama put on one of the most dominant individual playoff performances of the young season in Game 3, scoring 39 points on 13-of-18 shooting, grabbing 15 rebounds, and blocking 5 shots. He was virtually unstoppable — and 18 of his 39 points came in the fourth quarter alone, when San Antonio needed a knockout punch.

Stephon Castle added 13 points and 12 assists in that 115-108 victory. The Spurs are doing it with system basketball, but Wembanyama's individual excellence is the engine. At just 22 years old, he has already established himself as one of the most unguardable players in playoff history when locked in.

Edwards Won't Back Down

Anthony Edwards countered with 32 points, 14 rebounds, and 6 assists in Game 3. The game wasn't enough to save Minnesota, but it showed what Ant is capable of when motivated. Back at home, with a season potentially on the line if the Wolves fall 3-1, Edwards is likely to come out with maximum intensity.

The Timberwolves need their star to play the kind of game that changes a series. They've seen what happens when Wemby is rolling; the only answer is for Edwards to match that energy and find ways to get his teammates involved.

What's Next

San Antonio is favored by 4.5 points tonight, according to multiple betting markets. A Spurs win would put them one victory from the Western Conference Finals in just Wembanyama's second playoff appearance. A Wolves win resets the series to 2-2 and turns Game 5 into a must-win for San Antonio. Either way, tune in tonight at 7:30 PM ET on NBC for what promises to be another unforgettable chapter in this rivalry.
