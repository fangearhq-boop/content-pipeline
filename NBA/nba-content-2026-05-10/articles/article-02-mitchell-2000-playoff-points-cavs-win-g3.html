Mitchell Hits 2,000 Career Playoff Points, Cavaliers Beat Pistons 116-109 in Game 3

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 10, 2026

When the Cleveland Cavaliers needed their best player to show up in a must-win game, Donovan Mitchell delivered — and then some. Mitchell scored 35 points on Saturday night as Cleveland held off the Detroit Pistons 116-109 in Game 3 of the Eastern Conference Semifinals at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, cutting the series deficit to 2-1.

Buried inside that remarkable performance was an equally remarkable milestone: Mitchell reached 2,000 career postseason points in his 73rd playoff game. That pace places him tied for third-fastest among active players and ninth in NBA history to reach the milestone, according to NBC Sports — a testament to how consistently elite he has been in big moments throughout his career.

Cleveland Finds Clutch Plays at the Right Time

The Cavaliers had squandered fourth-quarter leads in both Games 1 and 2 on the road. Saturday was different. With the game on the line in the final three minutes, James Harden delivered timely buckets when the Pistons threatened to pull away. Max Strus provided key contributions off the bench, and Mitchell simply refused to let his team lose.

Detroit had every reason to believe they could steal the home-court advantage game. The Pistons still lead the series 2-1 and return home for Game 4 with the chance to push Cleveland to the brink of elimination. But the Cavaliers showed resilience they had lacked in the opening two games, protecting their home floor when it mattered most.

Mitchell's Legacy Moment

The 2,000-point milestone is more than a number — it's a snapshot of what Mitchell has meant to the Cavaliers since arriving in Cleveland in 2022. He has been the franchise's engine in the postseason, capable of carrying the scoring load against any level of competition. Reaching the milestone in the 73rd game speaks to the efficiency and volume of his playoff production.

Only a select group of active players have arrived at 2,000 career playoff points more quickly. Mitchell's place in that company reflects a career trajectory that puts him among the best postseason performers of his generation.

What's Next

Game 4 of the Pistons-Cavaliers series shifts back to Detroit, where Cade Cunningham and the Pistons will look to push their lead to 3-1 and put Cleveland on the verge of elimination. For the Cavaliers, extending Mitchell's strong form and finding the same clutch execution they showed on Saturday will be essential. After two late-game collapses on the road, Cleveland will need to prove Saturday wasn't an outlier.
