History on the Line: Knicks Seek First Best-of-Seven Sweep Since 1999 in Game 4 vs. 76ers

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 10, 2026

The New York Knicks step into Wells Fargo Center on Sunday afternoon with an opportunity to make NBA history. A Game 4 win over the Philadelphia 76ers — today at 3:30 PM ET on ABC — would give New York a best-of-seven sweep and push them into the Eastern Conference Finals. No team in NBA history has ever come back from an 0-3 series deficit to win.

Philadelphia has one final chance to change that — or at least extend their season. The 76ers, who led their series 3-0 at this stage in 2023 only to blow that lead against the Celtics, now find themselves on the wrong side of history. This time it's their turn to face impossible odds.

Knicks: Deep, Relentless, Complete

In Game 3, New York was without OG Anunoby (hamstring strain) and still won comfortably. Jalen Brunson scored 33 points with 9 assists. Mikal Bridges went 8-of-14 from the floor for 23 points. Karl-Anthony Towns posted an 8-point, 12-rebound, 7-assist triple-double line. Josh Hart had 12 points and 11 rebounds. And off the bench, Landry Shamet scored 15 points in 26 minutes while finishing a stunning plus-20 on the night.

That is not a team running on fumes. That is a team operating at peak efficiency with contributors at every position. OG's status for Game 4 remains uncertain, but given what the Knicks showed without him, Philadelphia faces a stacked deck regardless of the injury report.

76ers' Last Stand

Joel Embiid returned for Game 3 after missing Game 2 with ankle and hip soreness, scoring 18 points in 35 minutes. His availability and health for Game 4 is once again listed as questionable. Even at full strength, Embiid and the 76ers haven't found an answer for New York's combination of ball movement, defensive intensity, and depth.

For Philadelphia to extend their season, they need a career performance from Embiid, a perfect shooting night from Tyrese Maxey and Paul George, and the Knicks to have one of their worst games of the season — all in the same building, on the same afternoon.

What's Next

If the Knicks close out today, they await the winner of the Thunder-Lakers series (OKC leads 3-0) in the Eastern Conference Finals — well, the Western Conference Finals, sorry, that's OKC. New York would face either the Pistons or Cavaliers in the East Finals. A sweep would send a message to the entire conference: the Knicks are the team to beat. Tune in to ABC at 3:30 PM ET today to see if they can make history.
