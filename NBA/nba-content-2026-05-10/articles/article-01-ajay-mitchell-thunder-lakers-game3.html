Ajay Mitchell's Historic Line Fuels Thunder's 131-108 Rout of Lakers in Game 3

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 10, 2026

Oklahoma City came into Los Angeles and did what no one expected — they let the Lakers build a halftime lead, then destroyed them in the second half to win Game 3 of the Western Conference Semifinals 131-108 on Saturday night. The Thunder are now 3-0 in the series and 7-0 in the 2026 playoffs.

The story of the night belonged to Ajay Mitchell. The 22-year-old guard delivered one of the most statistically rare playoff performances in the modern era: 24 points on 10-of-17 shooting, 10 assists, 3 steals, and zero turnovers. According to ESPN, that combination puts Mitchell alongside Magic Johnson, Chris Paul, and Tyrese Haliburton as the only players since 1990 to post at least 20 points, 10 assists, 3 steals, and zero turnovers in a single playoff game.

"That's a very specific list," Thunder head coach Mark Daigneault noted after the game. "And it fits exactly the kind of player Ajay is becoming."

The Second Half That Decided Everything

Los Angeles had reason for optimism at halftime. The Lakers shot 11 three-pointers in the opening two quarters and led by two points heading to the locker room. LeBron James, despite a rough shooting night overall, was still directing traffic. The Wells Fargo Center was buzzing.

Then Oklahoma City took over. The Thunder outscored the Lakers by 25 points in the second half, suffocating Los Angeles defensively, attacking the glass, and playing with a controlled pace that had no counter. Mitchell orchestrated the surge with pinpoint passing and aggressive attacks off the dribble.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander contributed 23 points and 9 assists. Chet Holmgren went 9-of-14 from the field for 18 points and 9 rebounds. Isaiah Wallace added 16 points off the bench. Oklahoma City had five players in double figures and turned the ball over just six times as a team.

Lakers Run Out of Answers

LeBron James finished 7-of-19 from the field for 19 points, 6 rebounds, and 8 assists. Rui Hachimura led the Lakers with 21 points and five rebounds. Austin Reaves added 17 points and 9 assists, and Luke Kennard scored 18 off the bench. But the effort was disjointed, and OKC's defensive rotations erased every advantage Los Angeles tried to manufacture.

Luka Doncic remains sidelined with a Grade 2 hamstring strain and is still not cleared for contact. Without their second star, the Lakers lack the dynamic creation needed to keep pace with one of the deepest rosters in the league.

What's Next

The Thunder return home for potential Game 4, where they can complete a sweep of the Los Angeles Lakers. No team in NBA history has ever come back from an 0-3 series deficit — a mark that has been tested more than 150 times across the league's history. Oklahoma City, at 7-0 in the 2026 playoffs with no signs of slowing down, looks like a freight train with no brakes.

For Los Angeles, survival requires three consecutive wins against a team that has won every game this postseason. That task may be impossible without Luka — and possibly even with him.
