### Long-Form Post Two playoff career-highs in the same game. That's what it took to run the Cleveland Cavaliers out of Madison Square Garden again. Josh Hart scored 26 points — the best postseason performance of his career — going 10-of-21 from the field and 5-of-11 from three-point range while adding 7 assists, 4 rebounds, and 2 steals. Jalen Brunson chipped in 19 points and 14 assists, the most dimes he has ever dished in a single playoff game. Together, they were unstoppable. New York won 109-93 and now leads the Eastern Conference Finals 2-0 with Game 3 heading to Cleveland on Saturday. The Knicks are two wins away from the NBA Finals, and they are playing with the kind of confidence that comes from winning ugly (Game 1 comeback) and winning comfortably (Game 2 blowout) in back-to-back nights. Cleveland's defense had no answer for the two-man game between Hart and Brunson. When they overloaded on Brunson, Hart made threes. When they played Hart straight up, Brunson found his way to the rim or freed up Karl-Anthony Towns in the post. The Cavaliers tried adjustments, and none of them worked. For the Cavs, the situation is now mathematically desperate. No NBA team has ever come back from an 0-3 deficit. Donovan Mitchell and James Harden need a completely different performance on Saturday in Cleveland — and they need their home crowd to help them find it. WCF Game 2 Recap — Thunder 122-113 Spurs (May 20) — SGA 30, Series Even
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Shai Gilgeous-Alexander would not let the Thunder fall behind 0-2. And he made sure everyone knew it. SGA finished with 30 points, 9 assists, 2 blocks, and a +14 differential in Oklahoma City's 122-113 Game 2 win over San Antonio on Wednesday night at Paycom Center. He shot 50 percent from the field, attacked the paint when the Spurs sagged back, and found cutters when they ran him off the three-point line. It was a 48-minute masterclass. But this game wasn't just about SGA. It was about turnovers. San Antonio committed 21 of them; OKC committed 9. That 12-possession advantage translated directly into OKC transition points that kept the Thunder comfortably in control. Victor Wembanyama did everything he could — 21 points, 17 rebounds, playing the kind of game that makes you realize this person has no ceiling. Stephon Castle, already stepping in for the injured De'Aaron Fox, posted 28 points and 8 assists in a remarkable individual performance. But the injuries haunted San Antonio again. Jalen Williams, OKC's second star, exited in the first quarter with left hamstring soreness and did not return. Dylan Harper left in the third quarter with a right leg issue later diagnosed as a right adductor strain. When the final buzzer sounded, both teams were looking at injury reports instead of box scores. Series tied 1-1. WCF Game 3: TONIGHT at AT&T Center, San Antonio, 8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock. ### Image Post Caption 30 points. 9 assists. +14. SGA was locked in. Thunder 122, Spurs 113. WCF tied 1-1. No NBA team has ever rallied from an 0-3 deficit to win a playoff series. That's the cliff edge Cleveland is standing on if they lose Saturday at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse. Donovan Mitchell needs to be the player he knows he can be — the one who put up 41 points in Game 7 against Detroit, the one who can carry a series on his back when the moment demands it. Mitchell scored well in Games 1 and 2, but the moments that defined both those games were controlled by Brunson. On Saturday, Mitchell needs to flip that equation. For the Knicks, the challenge is simpler: don't let the environment rattle you. Brunson has been exceptional. Hart has been exceptional. Karl-Anthony Towns and Mikal Bridges have contributed. Josh Hart — Playoff Career-High 26 Points, The Heartbeat of the Knicks
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post When the New York Knicks selected Josh Hart in a trade deadline move years ago, the analytics were fine and the fit made sense. Nobody was predicting he'd be posting playoff career-highs in the Eastern Conference Finals. And yet there he was Thursday night — 26 points, 7 assists, 4 rebounds, 2 steals. Going 10-of-21 from the field, 5-of-11 from three. Doing it in a game where both he and Jalen Brunson set personal playoff career records on the same night. Hart is the anti-star star. He doesn't dominate highlights or get in the MVP conversation. He sets screens, chases loose balls, sprints back on defense, and does every single thing a basketball team needs its third or fourth option to do — then turns around and drops 26 when the moment calls for it. His three-point shooting evolution has been the key. At 47.6 percent from deep in this ECF, he has become a real floor-spacing threat, which forces Cleveland to make choices it doesn't want to make. Every time they cheat toward Brunson or Towns, Hart is there to punish them. What's also remarkable: Hart did this in Game 2 of the Eastern Conference Finals. In a building that was still buzzing from one of the greatest comebacks in recent NBA playoff history. After a game where the Knicks drained every ounce of energy from the building. Hart came back 48 hours later and played even better. The Knicks have a lot of good players. Josh Hart is the heartbeat. ### Image Post Caption 30th pick. Playoff career-high 26 points. Eastern Conference Finals. Josh Hart is not a surprise to anyone who has watched this Knicks team — but the number on the scoreboard still makes you look twice. That's just what he does. #HoopHeroes #Knicks
