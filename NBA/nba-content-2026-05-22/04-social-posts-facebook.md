### STORY 1: Knicks 109-93 Cavaliers — ECF Game 2

**Long-Form Post (9:00 AM ET)**

Jalen Brunson chipped in 19 points and 14 assists, the most dimes he has ever dished in a single playoff game.

Cleveland's defense had no answer for the two-man game between Hart and Brunson. When they overloaded on Brunson, Hart made threes. When they played Hart straight up, Brunson found his way to the rim or freed up Karl-Anthony Towns in the post. The Cavaliers tried adjustments, and none of them worked.

For the Cavs, the situation is now mathematically desperate. No NBA team has ever come back from an 0-3 deficit. Donovan Mitchell and James Harden need a completely different performance on Saturday in Cleveland — and they need their home crowd to help them find it.

### STORY 3: WCF Game 2 — Thunder 122-113 Spurs (May 20)

**Long-Form Post (9:30 AM ET)**
Shai Gilgeous-Alexander would not let the Thunder fall behind 0-2. And he made sure everyone knew it.

SGA finished with 30 points, 9 assists, 2 blocks, and a +14 differential in Oklahoma City's 122-113 Game 2 win over San Antonio on Wednesday night at Paycom Center. He shot 50 percent from the field, attacked the paint when the Spurs sagged back, and found cutters when they ran him off the three-point line. It was a 48-minute masterclass.

But this game wasn't just about SGA. It was about turnovers. San Antonio committed 21 of them; OKC committed 9. That 12-possession advantage translated directly into OKC transition points that kept the Thunder comfortably in control.

Victor Wembanyama did everything he could — 21 points, 17 rebounds, playing the kind of game that makes you realize this person has no ceiling. Stephon Castle, already stepping in for the injured De'Aaron Fox, posted 28 points and 8 assists in a remarkable individual performance.

But the injuries haunted San Antonio again. Jalen Williams, OKC's second star, exited in the first quarter with left hamstring soreness and did not return. Dylan Harper left in the third quarter with a right leg issue later diagnosed as a right adductor strain.

When the final buzzer sounded, both teams were looking at injury reports instead of box scores.

Series tied 1-1. WCF Game 3: TONIGHT at AT&T Center, San Antonio, 8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock.

### STORY 5: Josh Hart — Playoff Career-High 26, The Heart of the Knicks

**Long-Form Post (11:00 AM ET)**
When the New York Knicks selected Josh Hart in a trade deadline move years ago, the analytics were fine and the fit made sense. Nobody was predicting he'd be posting playoff career-highs in the Eastern Conference Finals.

And yet there he was Thursday night — 26 points, 7 assists, 4 rebounds, 2 steals. Going 10-of-21 from the field, 5-of-11 from three. Doing it in a game where both he and Jalen Brunson set personal playoff career records on the same night.

Hart is the anti-star star. He doesn't dominate highlights or get in the MVP conversation. He sets screens, chases loose balls, sprints back on defense, and does every single thing a basketball team needs its third or fourth option to do — then turns around and drops 26 when the moment calls for it.

His three-point shooting evolution has been the key. At 47.6 percent from deep in this ECF, he has become a real floor-spacing threat, which forces Cleveland to make choices it doesn't want to make. Every time they cheat toward Brunson or Towns, Hart is there to punish them.

What's also remarkable: Hart did this in Game 2 of the Eastern Conference Finals. In a building that was still buzzing from one of the greatest comebacks in recent NBA playoff history. After a game where the Knicks drained every ounce of energy from the building. Hart came back 48 hours later and played even better.

The Knicks have a lot of good players. Josh Hart is the heartbeat.
