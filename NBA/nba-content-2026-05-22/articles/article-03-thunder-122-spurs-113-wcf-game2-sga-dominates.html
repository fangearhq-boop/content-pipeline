Thunder 122, Spurs 113: SGA's 30-Point, 9-Assist Performance Evens WCF at 1-1

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 22, 2026

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander had a simple message for the Western Conference Finals: it is not over.

Coming off San Antonio's double-overtime Game 1 upset, SGA delivered 30 points, 9 assists, 2 blocks, and a +14 differential as the Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the San Antonio Spurs 122-113 in Game 2 on Wednesday night at Paycom Center. The Thunder evened the series at 1-1 and reclaimed home court advantage heading into Friday's Game 3 in San Antonio.

SGA in Command

The two-time MVP shot 50 percent from the field, attacked the paint when the Spurs tried to switch him onto smaller defenders, and found cutters when San Antonio sent extra coverage. His 9 assists came without a single turnover, a detail that encapsulates the kind of precision that defines his game at its best.

SGA had gone for 25 points in the double-overtime loss in Game 1. His Game 2 response was to put up 30 and do it with less effort than that sentence makes it sound — not because he was reckless, but because he was controlled. He made the right decision nearly every time he touched the ball.

The Turnover Margin Told the Story

San Antonio committed 21 turnovers. Oklahoma City committed 9. That 12-possession advantage was not an accident — the Thunder's pressure defense targeted the Spurs' backcourt depth all night, and with Stephon Castle playing heavy minutes at point guard in De'Aaron Fox's continued absence, ball security became the game's defining variable.

Castle was still exceptional on both ends — he finished with 28 points and 8 assists — but sustaining that production while also protecting the ball for 40-plus minutes is a difficult ask for a player in only his second NBA season.

Wembanyama posted 21 points and 17 rebounds and was his usual otherworldly self, but when your team gives the opponent 12 extra possessions, even the most dominant center in basketball cannot fully compensate.

Injuries Complicate the Picture

Game 2 ended with both teams watching injury reports with more urgency than stat lines. Jalen Williams, the Thunder's second star, exited in the first quarter with left hamstring soreness — his fourth hamstring issue in five months. Dylan Harper, who had been San Antonio's second engine in Game 1, left in the third quarter with a right adductor strain and will be evaluated for Game 3.

Combined with Fox's continued absence, both teams are heading into the second half of this series at uncertain health. Oklahoma City won Game 2 without Williams for the majority of the game. San Antonio's margin for error shrinks dramatically if Harper joins Fox on the sideline.

What's Next

The series shifts to San Antonio for Game 3 tonight at AT&T Center, 8:30 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. Injury updates for Harper, Fox, and Williams will be released in the hours before tip-off. WCF tied 1-1. Game 3: Friday May 22, AT&T Center, San Antonio, 8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock.
