Hart's 26, Brunson's 14 Assists Lead Knicks Past Cavaliers 109-93 in ECF Game 2

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 22, 2026

Two playoff career-highs. Same night. Same team.

Josh Hart scored 26 points — the best postseason performance of his career — and Jalen Brunson delivered 14 assists, the most he has ever distributed in a single playoff game, as the New York Knicks defeated the Cleveland Cavaliers 109-93 in Game 2 of the Eastern Conference Finals on Thursday night at Madison Square Garden. New York leads the series 2-0.

The Knicks are two wins away from the NBA Finals, and they are playing with a balanced, confident, and increasingly dominant style that the Cavaliers have found no answer for in either game of the series.

Hart Takes Over

If Game 1 was Brunson's night — 38 points, a 22-point comeback, overtime — then Game 2 belonged to Hart. He went 10-of-21 from the field and 5-of-11 from three-point range, the latter number underlining a shooting evolution that has transformed his role in this offense. Hart added 7 assists, 4 rebounds, and 2 steals in a complete two-way performance.

Hart's presence created exactly the spacing problems Cleveland couldn't solve. When the Cavaliers committed extra attention to Brunson, Hart knocked down shots from the arc. When they adjusted to shade toward Hart, Brunson found interior options or made his way to the rim. The two-man game between them was lethal, and Cleveland had no defensive configuration that adequately addressed both threats at once.

After the game, Hart credited the Knicks' system — but the system only works when the players within it execute at this level. Hitting 5-of-11 threes in an ECF game is not routine. It is remarkable.

Brunson as Orchestrator

With 14 assists and 19 points, Brunson's Game 2 was defined not by his scoring — though that was effective — but by his ability to put every teammate in position to succeed. He attacked the Cavaliers' defense methodically, drew the attention of two defenders, and found the open man with precision that kept Cleveland's scheme in a perpetual state of adjustment.

The 14 assists set a new playoff career-high for Brunson. It also signaled a willingness to trust his teammates — something the Knicks needed after Game 1 required him to carry 38 points of scoring burden. In Game 2, the load was shared, and the result was a 16-point blowout that never felt as close as it may read on the line score.

Cleveland's Crisis Deepens

The Cavaliers are staring at a 2-0 hole that has historically been impossible to climb out of. No team in NBA history has recovered from an 0-3 deficit, meaning one more Knicks win Saturday would effectively end Cleveland's season. The urgency could not be higher.

Donovan Mitchell has been a willing scorer in this series — he contributed points in both games — but the Cavaliers' offense has not functioned well enough around him to generate sustained runs against New York's defense. James Harden, targeted mercilessly in Game 1, has yet to rediscover the playmaking rhythm that made him an elite performer in the regular season.

The Cavs will return to Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse for Game 3 with the crowd behind them and a desperate need to prove they belong in this series. Whether Mitchell can elevate his game in the same way Brunson has — and whether the supporting cast around him can give him the kind of help Hart has given Brunson — will determine whether this becomes a series or an elimination.

What's Next

The Eastern Conference Finals shifts to Cleveland for Game 3 on Saturday, May 23. Tip-off is scheduled for 8 PM ET on ABC. The Knicks arrive with a 2-0 lead, two players who both just set playoff career-highs, and the momentum of back-to-back victories.
