WCF Game 3 Tonight: Three Spurs Stars Questionable as Series Returns to San Antonio

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 22, 2026

The Western Conference Finals returns to San Antonio tonight with more question marks than answers.

Game 3 tips off at 8:30 PM ET at AT&T Center with the series tied 1-1, and the Spurs are heading into it without certainty about the availability of three of their key players. De'Aaron Fox, Dylan Harper, and Jalen Williams — the Thunder's Williams — are all listed as questionable, creating a scenario in which both teams may be competing at significantly less than full strength.

Victor Wembanyama, as ever, is healthy. He will play. And he may need to be superhuman again.

The Three Question Marks

De'Aaron Fox has not played a single minute of the Western Conference Finals. The Spurs' starting point guard has been sidelined since suffering a right high ankle sprain in the second round against the Minnesota Timberwolves. Spurs coach Mitch Johnson has offered little optimism about his timeline, describing the injury as "tough" and one Fox "wouldn't be playing with in the regular season." Friday's game is a game-time decision.

Dylan Harper stepped into the starting lineup in Fox's absence and was brilliant in Game 1 — 24 points, 7 steals, a rookie breaking through on the biggest stage in the Western Conference. Then he suffered a right adductor strain in the third quarter of Game 2 on Wednesday and did not return. An MRI confirmed the diagnosis. He is listed as questionable for tonight.

On the Thunder's side, Jalen Williams — Oklahoma City's second-best player — exited Game 2 in the first quarter with left hamstring soreness. It is his fourth hamstring injury in five months. He traveled with the team to San Antonio and has been receiving treatment around the clock, but his status for tonight's game remains uncertain.

What Wembanyama Faces

Through two games of this series, Wembanyama has averaged 31 points and 20.5 rebounds. He scored 41 points and grabbed 24 rebounds in a double-overtime Game 1 win. He followed that with 21 points and 17 rebounds in the Game 2 loss. Neither performance was enough to carry San Antonio to the win without adequate support around him.

If Fox and Harper are both unavailable, Wembanyama will be leaning on Stephon Castle — who has been excellent, with 28 points and 8 assists in Game 2 — and a bench rotation that becomes thinner with each injury. Castle at point guard and Wembanyama as the offensive engine can win games. The question is whether that combination can defeat an Oklahoma City team that is healthy and hungry after SGA's 30-point Game 2 performance.

Home Court and Momentum

There is a reason series tied 1-1 heading into a road game are considered neutral — the home team almost always gets a boost. AT&T Center in San Antonio will be electric tonight, with a crowd that has watched one of the most watchable young players in the history of basketball take the sport somewhere new every single game.

In the regular season, the Spurs went 4-1 against Oklahoma City. They won Game 1 of this series in double overtime. The building will not be quiet. Wembanyama will not be quiet. And whichever Spurs players are available — however many that turns out to be — will compete.

The team that wins Game 3 takes a 2-1 series lead with two of the next three games on their home floor. That context alone makes tonight's outcome enormous.

What's Next

WCF Game 3 tips off tonight at 8:30 PM ET from AT&T Center in San Antonio, live on NBC/Peacock. The series is tied 1-1. Game 4 will follow Sunday, May 24, in San Antonio. If the Spurs take Games 3 and 4, the series would return to Oklahoma City for Game 5.
