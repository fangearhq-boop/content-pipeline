ECF Game 3 Preview: Mitchell Must Carry Cavaliers as Knicks Look to Go Up 3-0 in Cleveland

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 22, 2026

The Cleveland Cavaliers go home Saturday night carrying an 0-2 series deficit, a two-game confidence crisis, and the weight of a simple historical fact: no team in NBA history has come back from 0-3.

ECF Game 3 tips off Saturday, May 23, at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse at 8 PM ET on ABC, and the Cavaliers need something they haven't had yet in this series — a complete game from Donovan Mitchell and a functioning offense that doesn't collapse in the moments that matter most.

The New York Knicks are flying into Cleveland having won back-to-back games against the Cavaliers in ways that exposed Cleveland's roster at multiple levels. The question for Saturday is whether a home crowd and a desperate team can change the series trajectory before it's too late.

What Cleveland Must Fix

In Game 1, the Cavaliers led by 22 with 7:52 remaining and still lost in overtime. The collapse was structural — Mitchell went quiet after the eight-minute mark, Harden was hunted defensively and finished 1-of-8 from three with six turnovers, and the offense had no secondary creator to maintain pressure once both of those players were neutralized.

Game 2 was a more complete New York performance that started earlier in the game. The Knicks pulled away in the second half and won by 16. The Cavaliers never threatened to repeat the dominant first half they had in Game 1.

Cleveland's path back into this series runs through Mitchell playing 40 minutes of sustained excellence — attacking mismatches, creating for teammates in the half-court, and staying aggressive in clutch moments rather than deferring. It also runs through Harden making better decisions: cutting turnover opportunities down, hitting open threes, and managing the game without forcing plays that the Knicks have shown they can take advantage of.

The Knicks' Challenge

Road games in the playoffs test teams differently than home games. The crowd noise disrupts communication, fatigue accumulates, and the small adjustments that come easily at home become harder on the road. The Knicks have the momentum, but Cleveland's arena will be as loud as it's been all season.

Josh Hart and Jalen Brunson both set playoff career-highs in Game 2. The realistic expectation is not that they repeat those specific numbers in Cleveland, but that they continue playing the kind of basketball that makes the Knicks impossible to guard. The system — moving the ball, attacking switches, spacing the floor with Hart's three-point shooting — should function in any environment if the players execute.

Knicks coach Mike Brown will need to address any in-game adjustments Cleveland makes, which will likely come in the form of doubling Brunson earlier and testing Hart off the dribble rather than just as a catch-and-shoot threat.

What's Next

ECF Game 3 tips off Saturday, May 23, at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in Cleveland. The game airs at 8 PM ET on ABC. A Knicks win puts the series in a virtually unrecoverable position for Cleveland; a Cavaliers win keeps the series alive and sets up Game 4 Monday. ECF Game 3: Saturday May 23, 8 PM ET, ABC, Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland. Series: Knicks lead 2-0.
