Warriors Rally From 12 Down to Win 2026 NBA Summer League Championship

By Marcus Cole

July 20, 2026

Golden State needed every second of the fourth quarter — and then some — to close out the 2026 NBA Summer League Championship. Down 12 points entering the final frame against the Memphis Grizzlies, the Warriors outscored Memphis 28-12 over the final period to claim a 94-90 victory Saturday night at Thomas & Mack Center in Las Vegas.

The hero of the comeback was Yaxel Lendeborg, the Warriors' No. 11 overall pick in the 2026 NBA Draft. Lendeborg posted 21 points, 10 rebounds, 2 assists, and 2 steals in just 23 minutes of action and was named Championship Game MVP. Off the bench, Deivon Smith matched him point for point with 21 on efficient 9-for-14 shooting.

Memphis made Golden State earn it. Cedric Coward, who had exploded for 28 points in the semifinal win over Houston, scored 19 in the championship loss. Cameron Boozer, the Grizzlies' prized young prospect, matched him with 19 of his own. The Grizzlies controlled much of the first three quarters and looked poised to deny Golden State before the Warriors' fourth-quarter burst.

Lendeborg's Validation

When the Warriors selected Lendeborg at No. 11 in June, there was skepticism. The pick was viewed as a reach by several draft analysts who had him projected outside the top 15. Seven Summer League games later, Lendeborg answered that skepticism with a championship trophy and an MVP award.

His 21-point, 10-rebound performance in a high-pressure championship game — at age 19, in his first extended professional action — showed a player comfortable operating under the spotlight. Lendeborg's two-way impact, reflected in his two steals alongside the offensive output, aligns with what the Warriors covet in big men: versatility, communication, and the ability to impact the game without dominating the ball.

Golden State's player development record over the past decade gives context to what Lendeborg's Summer League run might mean. The organization has consistently turned first-round picks into rotation contributors and eventually into starters. If the pattern holds, the No. 11 pick may prove prescient.

Grizzlies' Strong Run Ends in Defeat

Memphis should not leave Las Vegas disappointed. The Grizzlies reached the championship game and pushed a well-constructed Warriors squad to the final minutes. Coward's emergence as a Summer League standout — 14.7 points per game in Vegas, including a monster semifinal — gives the organization a real piece in their developing rotation.

Boozer, meanwhile, continues to show flashes of the upside that made him a coveted young talent entering the league. A 19-point showing in a championship game indicates he belongs in these conversations about Memphis's future.

The Grizzlies, navigating a significant roster reconstruction this offseason, now have clarity on two players who project as genuine contributors at the next level. That is a meaningful takeaway even in defeat.

What's Next

Golden State's Summer League title is a morale boost heading into a pivotal free agency period. The Warriors are one of the four teams in the conversation for LeBron James, and Lendeborg's emergence gives the organization a compelling story to sell: the franchise is developing new talent while positioning itself for championship-level additions.

For Memphis, the focus shifts to roster construction decisions around a young core that showed real promise in Vegas. How the Grizzlies build around Coward, Boozer, and their existing pieces this offseason will define their competitive outlook for the next several seasons.
