Rockets 119, Grizzlies 109: KD's 25-10 Leads Houston, Prosper Sets Career High

By Hoop Heroes Staff | March 28, 2026

Kevin Durant delivered another vintage performance on Thursday night, and it was exactly what the Houston Rockets needed.

Durant posted 25 points and 10 assists as Houston defeated the Memphis Grizzlies 119-109 on March 27, a win that keeps the Rockets at 43-28 and safely inside the top-six seeds in the Western Conference. But the most memorable moment of the night came from the losing team's bench: Memphis rookie Olivier-Maxence Prosper torched the Rockets defense for a career-high 31 points in a performance that had scouts reaching for their notebooks.

Durant's Double-Double Provides the Foundation

Durant's 25-point, 10-assist line exemplified the kind of versatile veteran leadership he's brought to Houston since arriving. He didn't need to force shots or carry the offense alone — instead, he orchestrated the Rockets' attack as both a scorer and a playmaker, distributing efficiently while dropping in exactly the points the team needed when defensive attention shifted away from him.

For Houston, the win matters in the larger context of a crowded Western Conference playoff race. The Rockets at 43-28 have been one of the most consistent teams in the second half of the season, and their grip on the No. 6 seed keeps them out of the Play-In Tournament — a crucial advantage with the schedule winding down.

Prosper Announces Himself

The performance of the game, in many ways, belonged to the player in the losing locker room.

Olivier-Maxence Prosper, a Grizzlies rookie who has developed quietly in Memphis throughout the season, surpassed his previous career high by a significant margin, pouring in 31 points in what may be remembered as his breakout performance. The kind of game where a young player looks around at the end and realizes he belongs at this level.

Memphis is firmly in rebuild mode, and results like this — even in losses — are the fuel that keeps a franchise's long-term vision alive. Prosper's arrival as a consistent offensive threat gives the Grizzlies an interesting piece to build around alongside their other developing talent.

Houston's Playoff Position

The Rockets' playoff positioning is a quiet success story of the 2025-26 NBA season. KD arrived in Houston without massive expectations of a contending run, yet the Rockets have built a team that is genuinely fighting for a top-six seed — not just sneaking into the Play-In. At 43-28, they are two full games ahead of the No. 7 seed and have the cushion to manage their roster through the final two weeks without panic.

That matters. Play-In basketball is an extra round of high-stakes elimination games that takes a physical and mental toll. If Houston can hold the No. 6 seed and enter the postseason properly rested, Durant and the Rockets become a legitimate threat to cause problems for whoever draws them in the first round.

What's Next

Houston heads into the final stretch of the regular season with a clear goal: hold the No. 6 seed and avoid the Play-In. Durant's health and production will be the primary variable in whether the Rockets can deliver on that objective. Meanwhile, Prosper and the Grizzlies continue their development cycle with a talented young roster that showed flashes of its ceiling on Thursday night.

Keep an eye on Prosper. Thursday may have been the first of many highlights for the Memphis rookie.
