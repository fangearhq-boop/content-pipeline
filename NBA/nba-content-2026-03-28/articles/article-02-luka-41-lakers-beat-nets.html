Luka Drops 41 as Lakers Beat Nets Without LeBron

By Hoop Heroes Staff | March 28, 2026

Luka Doncic did not need help on Thursday night.

With LeBron James sidelined by an ankle sprain, Doncic delivered 41 points and 6 three-pointers to lead the Los Angeles Lakers past the Brooklyn Nets on March 27. The win keeps Los Angeles at 46-26 and firmly seated at No. 3 in the Western Conference with roughly two weeks remaining in the regular season.

For Doncic, the performance was simply the latest entry in one of the most remarkable individual stretches of basketball the league has seen this season. He is averaging 39.5 points per game over his last 11 games — a run that has included a 60-point game, a 50-point game, and multiple 40-point outings.

A One-Man Show That Keeps Getting Better

Doncic came into Thursday's game having already won Western Conference Player of the Week for his 42.3 points-per-game average. He left the arena having raised the bar again. His full-season averages of 33.6 points, 7.8 rebounds, and 8.3 assists per game place him among the elite in every major statistical category.

Brooklyn's Nic Claxton provided the only meaningful resistance in the paint, finishing with 16 points and 7 rebounds. The Nets were operating short-handed with Sharpe and Demin also sidelined, limiting their options for stopping a player who was in complete command of the game from start to finish.

The margin of victory was commanding. Doncic orchestrated the offense with precision, creating open looks for his teammates while also exploiting every defensive mistake the Nets made. His six three-pointers gave him the range advantage that forced Brooklyn's defense to extend and created the driving lanes he exploited all night.

LeBron's Ankle and the Road Ahead

The subplot behind the box score carries genuine weight for the Lakers' playoff ambitions. James, who turned 41 this season and surpassed Kareem Abdul-Jabbar for the most career field goals made in NBA history earlier this month, was held out with an ankle sprain. The injury is described as manageable, and the Lakers will likely use the final games of the regular season — particularly against depleted opponents — to ensure James arrives at the postseason fully healthy.

Austin Reaves and Jarred Vanderbilt absorbed additional minutes in James's absence and provided solid support. Neither player is capable of carrying the load that James does over a full game, but their contributions helped keep the margin comfortable enough that Doncic didn't need to be perfect in the fourth quarter.

The Lakers enter the postseason as a legitimate Western Conference contender. At 46-26, they have earned a home-court advantage in the first round and positioned themselves to avoid the No. 1 seed in OKC until at least the conference semifinals. Their path to the Finals runs through Luka staying healthy and LeBron being able to contribute his veteran leadership and defensive presence alongside him.

What's Next

The Lakers have approximately two weeks of regular-season basketball remaining before the playoffs begin on April 18. The priority in that window is rest and health — specifically for LeBron, whose ankle should be closely monitored. Doncic, meanwhile, appears to be in the kind of form that could carry the team through a playoff run even if James is on a minutes restriction.

If Doncic continues this trajectory, the Los Angeles Lakers may be the most dangerous No. 3 seed in recent playoff memory. The Western Conference bracket is about to get very interesting.
