NBA Playoff Picture: Knicks Fail to Clinch, Bulls Eliminated, Boston Leads East Race

By Hoop Heroes Staff | March 28, 2026

The NBA's final two weeks are shaping up to be must-watch basketball across both conferences.

On March 27-28, several significant developments reshaped the standings: the Chicago Bulls were officially eliminated from playoff contention, the New York Knicks failed to clinch despite having a chance, and the Boston Celtics extended their lead over New York for the No. 2 seed in the East to two games — with one head-to-head matchup still remaining between them.

Eastern Conference: The Race Nobody Wants to Miss

The Detroit Pistons have clinched the No. 1 seed in the East at 52-19, but everything below them is a tightly contested battlefield. The Celtics sit at 47-24 with Boston leading the Knicks (47-25) by 2 games for the No. 2 seed. That differential, combined with one remaining head-to-head game on the schedule, transforms a regular-season matchup into essentially a playoff game for seeding purposes.

The Knicks' failure to clinch on March 27 — dropping a game to the Charlotte Hornets — handed the Celtics a crucial game back. New York cannot afford more slip-ups at this stage. A second-round matchup against the Pistons, versus a potential first-round matchup against a Play-In qualifier, could depend entirely on whether the Knicks or Celtics close the year more strongly.

Further down the Eastern Conference seedings, the race from No. 5 through No. 8 is equally compelling. Toronto (40-31) and Atlanta (40-32) are separated by one game for seeds five and six. Philadelphia at 39-33 and Orlando at 38-33 are battling for the No. 7 seed, which goes directly to the playoffs, while Miami at 38-34 is fighting to avoid dropping out of even Play-In position.

Western Conference: Clinched at the Top, Chaotic Below

Oklahoma City (57-15) and San Antonio (54-18) have clinched their playoff spots as the No. 1 and No. 2 seeds respectively, and the gap between them and the rest of the Western Conference is significant. The Lakers at 46-26 have secured the No. 3 seed, while Denver (44-28) and Minnesota (44-28) are tied for the No. 4 and No. 5 seeds with games to play.

Houston at 43-28 occupies the No. 6 seed, the final spot that avoids Play-In basketball. Below them, Phoenix (40-32) at No. 7 and Golden State (36-36) at No. 8 are currently in the Play-In picture, with teams fighting for every available position through No. 10.

The Bulls' elimination removed one variable from the Eastern Conference standings — though Chicago was competing for an Eastern spot, their departure doesn't directly affect the Western picture. What it does confirm is that the middle tier of the NBA has been sorted; the remaining uncertainty is about order, not about who makes it in.

The Key Dates

The regular season ends on April 12, giving every team in both conferences approximately two more weeks to finalize their seedings. The Play-In Tournament runs from April 14 to 17, with the first round of the playoffs beginning April 18.

For teams on the bubble, those five days between the regular season finale and the Play-In games are the window for rest and preparation. For teams already clinched — including the Pistons, Thunder, and Spurs — the remaining games are about momentum, health management, and potential home-court maintenance.

What's Next

The final two weeks of the regular season are appointment viewing. The Celtics-Knicks head-to-head will be circled on every basketball fan's calendar. The Play-In race in the West features enough teams and enough storylines to fill a full week of coverage on its own. And with LeBron James managing an ankle sprain and multiple playoff-bound rosters managing their veterans' workloads, every box score through April 12 carries context beyond the final score.
