Wembanyama Tops MVP Ladder, Surpasses Shai Gilgeous-Alexander

By Hoop Heroes Staff | March 28, 2026

Victor Wembanyama is now the official frontrunner for the NBA's Most Valuable Player award — at least according to the Kia MVP Ladder.

The San Antonio Spurs center surged to No. 1 on the weekly ladder released on March 27, 2026, overtaking Oklahoma City Thunder guard Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, who had held a firm grip on the top spot for much of the season. The jump came on the back of a dominant three-game stretch in which Wembanyama recorded 17 blocks — an output that few players in NBA history could match over an entire week of basketball.

The Spurs, sitting at 54-18 and holding the No. 2 seed in the Western Conference, have been on a 22-2 run since February. Wembanyama has been the primary engine of that surge.

The Case for Wembanyama

Wembanyama presents an unprecedented argument for MVP consideration. At just 22 years old, he is operating as an elite scorer, a dominant rebounder, and a historically impactful shot-blocker simultaneously. The 17 blocks in three games is the kind of figure that turns heads even among basketball historians.

Earlier this season, Wembanyama delivered one of his signature performances: 26 points, 15 rebounds, and 5 blocks as the Spurs demolished the Miami Heat 136-111. His combination of scoring efficiency and defensive impact makes him genuinely difficult to compare to any previous MVP winner.

"There isn't a player in the league right now who changes a game the way Wembanyama does," the NBA.com ladder summary noted, citing his ability to alter opponent shot selection even when he doesn't record a block.

SGA's Historic Season Isn't Over Yet

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander's claim to the award remains compelling. In March, SGA surpassed Wilt Chamberlain's record for consecutive games scoring 20 or more points, extending his streak to 127 straight — a mark of offensive consistency that has no modern parallel. The Thunder at 57-15 are the best team in basketball by record, and SGA is unquestionably their driving force.

What makes the debate so rich is that both players are doing something historic in different dimensions of the game. SGA is rewriting what sustained offensive output looks like. Wembanyama is redefining defensive dominance. The voters — a media panel — will have to decide which kind of greatness weighs more in the final tally.

The Final Two Weeks

With the regular season ending April 12, both players have approximately 10 to 12 games left to make their case. The Spurs and Thunder, the No. 2 and No. 1 seeds respectively, are playing meaningful games — both have already clinched their playoff spots and are now jockeying for seeding and momentum.

If the two teams meet in the Western Conference Finals, which the bracket structure makes possible, the MVP winner may be setting the stage for one of the most compelling playoff storylines in years: the award recipient facing the runner-up in a best-of-seven series.

What's Next

The Kia MVP Ladder updates weekly, and the final announcement will come at the end of the regular season. Wembanyama's immediate schedule includes several games against playoff-caliber opponents where he can continue to build his case. SGA and the Thunder face similar high-stakes matchups as OKC attempts to maintain the best record in basketball through the final stretch.
