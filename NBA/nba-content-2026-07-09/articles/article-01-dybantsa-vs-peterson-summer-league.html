Dybantsa vs. Peterson: The Summer League Showdown NBA Fans Have Been Waiting For

By Jake Torres July 9, 2026

Two Picks, One Court, Tonight

AJ Dybantsa has been the most talked-about prospect in basketball since long before the 2026 NBA Draft. The Washington Wizards made him the No. 1 pick, and the basketball world has been waiting to see what that looks like against real competition. Thursday night at 9 PM ET on ESPN, the wait ends. The Utah Jazz come to the Thomas and Mack Center with Darryn Peterson, the No. 2 pick, who has already made clear he's not planning to play the bridesmaid role quietly.

Peterson's early case is strong. In his Salt Lake City Summer League opener against the Grizzlies, he went for 25 points and 12 assists. Two days later against the Hawks, he poured in 28 points, shot 11 for 21 from the field and 4 for 7 from three, and delivered the overtime win. There are turnovers to clean up, eight in that second game, but the Jazz have seen enough to feel good about their pick.

What Dybantsa Has to Answer

Dybantsa averaged 25.5 points per game during his freshman season at BYU, leading all of Division I in scoring. He's a player built around volume creation at the rim and the free throw line, with the size and strength to overwhelm defenders before they're ready. Summer League won't give him the full picture, but it gives fans the first real look in an NBA context.

Peterson's hot start changes the dynamic of tonight's game. Dybantsa isn't just debuting against a Jazz team. He's debuting against a counter-narrative that the second pick might already be ahead. That kind of pressure, showing up in the first Vegas game, is something most top picks never face at this stage. Dybantsa will have to produce, and he'll have to do it against a team whose best rookie just had two of the best Summer League openers in years.

The Bailey Wildcard

There's one more factor worth noting. Ace Bailey, the Jazz's third lottery pick, was listed out of Thursday's game after a hip flexor strain. Then, Wednesday morning, the Jazz updated his status to available. Bailey had scored 18 points, grabbed 7 rebounds, and added 3 assists before the injury, and his return turns this from a two-man showcase into something messier and more interesting for the Wizards.

If Bailey is healthy and active, the Jazz have three legitimate high-ceiling prospects on the floor in one Summer League game. The Wizards, with Dybantsa in his debut, have a lot to manage defensively. The matchup within the matchup matters here. Which rookie, on which side, handles the moment best will tell us more about the next 15 years of both franchises than almost anything that happens in the regular season until November.

What's Next

The Jazz and Wizards tip off Thursday at 9 PM ET on ESPN from the Thomas and Mack Center in Las Vegas. Both teams continue Vegas Summer League through the round-robin stage, with the top teams advancing to the championship bracket. For Dybantsa and Peterson, tonight is the first chapter of what figures to be a long rivalry. Watch for how both players handle turnovers, late-game moments, and transition defense, the three things that separate Summer League standouts from players who carry it into October.
