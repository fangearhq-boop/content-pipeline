PG13 Is Back — Paul George Waives Trade Bonus, Claims No. 13 in Boston

By Marcus Cole July 9, 2026

The Number Says Something

Paul George wore No. 8 in Philadelphia. It was fine. It was a number. But No. 13 is the Paul George number, the one that made PG13 a household name in Indiana, the number associated with his best basketball and his best version as a two-way wing. Thursday, Bleacher Report confirmed George is taking No. 13 with the Boston Celtics. And that matters more than a jersey reveal usually would.

George also waived his $3.9 million trade bonus, the payment built into the Jaylen Brown deal that he was entitled to collect. He left it on the table. His cap hit with Boston stays at $54.1 million. He has a $56.6 million player option for 2027-28, so the long-term picture is complicated, but the short-term signal is not: George is invested in this working.

Why Boston Made This Trade

Celtics general manager Danny Ainge went on record explaining the thinking behind trading Jaylen Brown for George. The explanation was direct: Paul George, in last spring's first-round playoff series, dominated Boston. The 76ers beat the Celtics in Round 1, and George was a primary reason why. The Celtics couldn't stop him, so they decided to stop trying.

That kind of logic, signing the player who just beat you, has a long history in the NBA. It's not sentimental. It's practical. Boston enters this season with George and Jayson Tatum as the primary one-two, and Neemias Queta, who signed a four-year, $56 million extension Thursday, as the long-term center solution. The roster is being rebuilt around the George-Tatum partnership, and the front office is clearly not treating this as a short-term rental situation.

What the Player Option Means

The $56.6 million option for 2027-28 is real money, and George will have a choice to make next summer. If Boston is winning and the situation is working, he exercises the option and stays. If it's not, he can opt out and pursue a different path. But the waived trade bonus removes the one signal that would have suggested George was hedging. Players who waive money they don't have to waive are telling you something. He wants this to work.

PG13 has been many things at many stops. Indiana, Oklahoma City, the Clippers, Philadelphia. At 35, he's at an age where the legacy considerations start to weigh alongside the basketball ones. A championship with Boston, pairing with Tatum and whatever the front office builds around them, is the kind of outcome that changes how a career gets remembered. George knows that. The number choice and the waived bonus both say the same thing: he's not here for a visit.

What's Next

Celtics training camp opens in late September. The Boston roster is largely assembled now, with the George acquisition as the anchor and Queta's extension providing frontcourt depth. The main remaining question is whether Boston adds a veteran creator or shooter before camp. Watch for any additional signings in the $10-20 million range between now and early August, as Ainge typically fills out rosters methodically once the major pieces are confirmed.
