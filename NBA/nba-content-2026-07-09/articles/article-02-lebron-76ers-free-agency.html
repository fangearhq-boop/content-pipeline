LeBron Is Taking the 76ers 'Very Seriously' — The Free Agency Race Just Changed

By Marcus Cole July 9, 2026

Philadelphia Just Entered the Conversation for Real

The phrase "very seriously" carries weight when it comes from Shams Charania about LeBron James and the Philadelphia 76ers. That's exactly how ESPN's NBA insider framed the situation Thursday, and it shifts the entire free agency picture. For the past week, the Cavaliers and Warriors had been the understood frontrunners, with the Heat as a longer shot. The 76ers were a name in the conversation but not a real destination. That framing no longer applies.

Philadelphia's pitch has a few concrete angles. Mike Gansey, the 76ers' team president, spent years in the Cavaliers front office and knows LeBron's world. Tyrese Maxey is a Klutch client, putting him in the same agency orbit as LeBron himself. And the 76ers just acquired Jaylen Brown, giving the roster two capable perimeter threats alongside a potential LeBron signing. The pitch isn't theoretical. It has structure.

Where the Other Teams Stand

ESPN's Brian Windhorst, who has followed LeBron's career as closely as any reporter in the country, described the Cavaliers' position using a different lens: "The vibes point to Cleveland." That read has held up for over a week. Cleveland has the Donovan Mitchell pairing, the familiarity factor, and a front office that's been patient through the process. Rich Paul, LeBron's agent, confirmed the decision is not coming in the next few days, and mid-July remains the working timeline.

The Warriors are taking this seriously enough that Draymond Green opted out of his $27.7 million player option Thursday. That's not a move you make unless the front office has told you there's a real plan in place. Golden State has also been pursuing an Anthony Davis trade, trying to build the full picture of what a LeBron landing could look like on paper. And in Cleveland, James Harden declined his $42.3 million player option to renegotiate a new deal, a move that keeps the Cavaliers' roster construction flexible depending on LeBron's choice.

What a 76ers Choice Would Mean

LeBron to Philadelphia would be the most unpredictable move of his career. He went to Miami to win, to Cleveland to return home, to the Lakers as a legacy statement. Philadelphia would be something different: the most urgent possible situation, a team built to compete right now, in a city starving for a championship. Maxey is 25 and playing the best basketball of his career. Brown is a proven All-Star-level wing. LeBron, at 41, picking the situation with the shortest runway and highest ceiling is not as far-fetched as it sounded two weeks ago.

But four teams is four teams. Each has a different argument. Each has a different version of what it offers. The decision, whenever it comes, will require LeBron to answer questions about legacy, competition level, money, and fit that don't all point in the same direction.

What's Next

Rich Paul set the expectation clearly: mid-July is the timeline. That means between now and July 19, the four teams, Cavaliers, Warriors, Heat, and now the 76ers, are all making their final pushes. Watch for any reports of LeBron taking formal meetings, a step he had reportedly not yet taken as of mid-week. The moment meetings happen, the decision is close. Whoever gets the call before that happens knows they've done their job.
