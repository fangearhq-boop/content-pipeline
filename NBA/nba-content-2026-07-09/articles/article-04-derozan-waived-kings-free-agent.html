DeMar DeRozan Waived by Kings — A Six-Time All-Star Is Now on the Market

By Jake Torres July 9, 2026

Sacramento Moves On

The Sacramento Kings waived DeMar DeRozan on Thursday, making the six-time All-Star a free agent at the age of 36. DeRozan averaged 19.2 points per game in 39 appearances last season before a hand injury cut his year short. When he was healthy, he was the same player he's been for the last decade: a midrange creator who draws fouls, plays within the offense, and provides a floor that teams with younger rosters often can't manufacture.

Sacramento's decision makes sense from a roster-building standpoint. The Kings have pieces to develop and cap flexibility to protect going into a transitional period. DeRozan, who signed with Sacramento to stay competitive, ends up caught in the middle of a franchise shifting its timeline. That's not a knock on him. It's just the way roster construction goes when a team decides to reset.

What He Still Brings

The midrange game doesn't go away. DeRozan has built his entire career on a shot that analytics departments spent a decade declaring unworkable, and he's made it work anyway because his pull-up off the dribble and his ability to get to his spots under any defensive pressure make it as reliable as a well-designed play. At 36, with 39 games of last-season tape, he still posted 19.2 points on strong shooting efficiency.

The hand injury is a concern. Any 36-year-old coming off a mid-season injury requires evaluation. But DeRozan has always been durable, and the hand is not a structural issue that signals a longer decline. A contender that signs him on a short deal, one or two years, gets an experienced scorer who can function as a sixth man or starter without needing the ball to run through him all night.

The Contender Fits

Three teams stand out immediately. The Celtics just traded Jaylen Brown and need proven scoring depth alongside Paul George and Jayson Tatum. The Heat are building their first full season around Giannis Antetokounmpo and Bam Adebayo, and they need shooting and creation on the perimeter. The Cavaliers, depending on whether LeBron James shows up, have roster spots and a culture that values veterans. Any of those three landing pages fit DeRozan's game and his stage in a career.

The price will be a veteran minimum or close to it, given where he is in his career and the market. That's actually a selling point for teams managing tight caps. Six-time All-Star production on a veteran minimum deal is not a small thing. DeRozan is going to get calls this week. The question is which situation he chooses.

What's Next

Teams have until July 15 to make offers before the waiver wire clears and DeRozan can sign anywhere without restriction. Expect the process to move quickly. A player of his caliber and age doesn't wait long on the open market during an active free agency period. Watch for reports of meetings or interest by the weekend, and likely an announcement within the next 7 to 10 days.
