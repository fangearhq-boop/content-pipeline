No. 34 in Milwaukee, No. 7 in Miami: Giannis Starts a New Chapter

By Damon Pierce July 9, 2026

The Number That Changes Everything

Giannis Antetokounmpo wore No. 34 for 12 NBA seasons in Milwaukee. Two MVP awards, a Defensive Player of the Year, a Finals MVP, and a championship all came under that number. In a lot of ways, 34 and Giannis were the same thing. Then the Heat traded for him in early July, and the first real signal that this chapter is genuinely new came Thursday: he's wearing No. 7 in Miami.

It's the first time in his professional career Giannis has worn a number other than 34. The choice is small in the grand scheme, but it tells you something about how he's approaching this. He's not coasting. He's not a player who requested a trade out of Milwaukee and is now biding his time. He changed his number. That's a deliberate act of committing to a new identity.

The Extension Timeline Explained

Giannis is eligible to sign an extension with the Heat starting October 1, but per reports, he plans to wait until January 6, 2027. That six-month mark from the trade date is the trigger for the full four-year, $275 million maximum extension. The math is simple: waiting 97 more days costs him nothing and gains him the maximum possible contract value.

This is not a delay that should concern Heat fans. The extension timeline is standard procedure for players who want to maximize their earnings after a mid-season or mid-offseason trade. Giannis isn't using the January window as leverage or as a statement about his commitment. He's using it because it's the financially correct thing to do. He's 31 years old, still in the middle of his prime, and the Heat have made clear they see him as the foundation of the franchise going forward.

What This Pairing Looks Like

Bam Adebayo is one of the most versatile defensive centers in the league. Giannis is the most physically imposing wing-to-center hybrid the sport has produced. Putting them in the same frontcourt creates problems that most teams do not have answers for in half-court defense. The Heat's question this summer is whether Pat Riley and Erik Spoelstra can build enough shooting around those two to space the floor properly in playoff conditions.

Miami's front office has been publicly bullish about Eastern Conference title contention. They gave up a significant package to get Giannis, including Bam's long-term contract value as part of the trade consideration. The commitment is real, and the January extension will make it permanent. No. 7 is not a placeholder. It's the beginning.

What's Next

Giannis reports to Heat training camp in late September. The extension becomes signable on January 6, 2027, and barring something unforeseen, that's when the paperwork gets done. In the meantime, watch for how the Heat build out the rest of the roster around him and Bam this summer. The pieces they add between now and training camp will determine how serious the October-through-June version of this team really is.
