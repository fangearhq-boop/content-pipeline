### Long-Form Post For 12 years in the NBA, Giannis Antetokounmpo wore one number: 34. In Milwaukee, No. 34 became one of the most recognizable jerseys in basketball. Two MVP awards, a Defensive Player of the Year, a championship. Everything was built under that number. Now it's No. 7. The Miami Heat confirmed Thursday that Giannis has chosen No. 7 for his new chapter in South Beach. It's the first time in his professional career he's worn anything other than 34. The change is deliberate. He's committing to the identity of a new team. On the contract side: Giannis is eligible to sign his extension as early as October 1, but per reports, he'll wait until January 6, 2027, which is the six-month mark from the trade date. That unlocks the full four-year, $275 million maximum. It's a formality. He's staying. Paired with Bam Adebayo in the frontcourt, the Heat are building something the Eastern Conference hasn't seen before. A legitimate second big alongside arguably the most physically imposing player in the sport. No. 7. New city. Same mission. ### Image Post Caption No. 34 in Milwaukee. No. 7 in Miami. ### Long-Form Post The Sacramento Kings have waived DeMar DeRozan, and somewhere right now, a contender's front office is picking up the phone. DeRozan, a six-time All-Star, averaged 19.2 points per game in 39 appearances last season before a hand injury cut his year short. When he was healthy, he was the same player he's been for the last decade: a midrange creator who draws fouls, plays within the offense, and provides scoring that teams with younger rosters often can't manufacture. Sacramento is moving in a different direction. The Kings have young pieces to develop and cap flexibility to protect. DeRozan, who signed with Sacramento to stay competitive, ends up caught in the middle of a franchise shifting its timeline. Now every contender with a roster spot and a need for reliable scoring should be in conversation. The Celtics just traded Jaylen Brown. The Heat are building around Giannis and Bam. The Cavaliers have LeBron's arrival pending. Any of those teams could use a proven, shot-creating veteran who doesn't need the ball to be dominant. DeRozan won't cost a fortune on a short deal. Six-time All-Star production on or near a veteran minimum is not a small thing. This call gets made fast. ### Image Post Caption Six-time All-Star. 19.2 points a game last season.
