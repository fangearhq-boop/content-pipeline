Austin Reaves Free Agency: Why the Lakers Are Favored to Keep Their $239 Million Man

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 18, 2026

The Austin Reaves free agency story was supposed to be a bidding war. A week out from the June 29 player option deadline, it's starting to look less like a war and more like the Los Angeles Lakers walking away with what they want.

Multiple reports indicate the Brooklyn Nets may not pursue Reaves as aggressively as previously anticipated. League executives surveyed by HoopsHype's Michael Scotto believe the Lakers remain the frontrunners to retain Reaves, with a simple reason: if Los Angeles offers him something close to the full max, he is expected to stay.

"There's a strong belief within league circles that if the Lakers offer Reaves close to or the full max, he'll stay in Los Angeles," Scotto wrote.

The Contract Math

The Lakers can offer Reaves a projected five-year, $239 million contract. The Nets' ceiling — as an outside team — is a four-year deal in the range of $177 million. That $62 million gap is significant on its own. But the gap in years — five versus four — means additional security and guaranteed income that goes beyond the annual salary figure.

Reaves holds a $14.9 million player option for the 2026-27 season. He is widely expected to decline that option before June 29 and enter free agency. The Lakers hold the exclusive negotiating window before July 1, which gives them the first shot at a deal before other teams can formally offer.

The Atlanta Hawks and Detroit Pistons are also reported to have interest, but neither is considered a serious threat to land Reaves given the financial disparity and Reaves' stated preference.

What Reaves Brings

The 28-year-old guard had a career-best season in 2025-26, averaging 23.3 points, 5.5 assists, and 4.7 rebounds per game while shooting 49 percent from the field. He is one of the most efficient mid-volume scorers in the league — a player who does not require the ball for extended possessions but can create his own shot when needed.

His value alongside Luka Doncic is particularly clear. Reaves functions effectively as the secondary ball-handler, taking off-ball possessions, creating in the mid-range game, and providing the second-unit scoring punch that keeps LA competitive across 48 minutes. Replacing that production in free agency would cost the Lakers either comparable money or a significant drop in quality.

The Risk of Letting Him Walk

Bleacher Report's league-executive survey offered a sobering perspective on what the Lakers face if Reaves departs. One executive described it as a "disaster scenario" — a team losing its second-best player with no obvious in-house replacement and limited cap flexibility to address it given the Doncic contract.

The Lakers' financial structure limits their options. Allowing Reaves to leave without replacement would force Los Angeles into the mid-level exception market for his backup, a significant step down from what he provides.

What's Next

Reaves' player option deadline is June 29. Expect the Lakers to engage seriously before the option window closes. If they present something near the max, the expectation around the league is that Reaves accepts and stays in Los Angeles.

For a team that just watched the Knicks win a championship while building around their franchise player, the lesson is clear: you lock up the pieces around your star when you have the chance. Letting Reaves walk would be a difficult decision to justify.

The Lakers hold all the leverage here. The Nets may be stepping back. The market appears to be trending toward a return to LA.
