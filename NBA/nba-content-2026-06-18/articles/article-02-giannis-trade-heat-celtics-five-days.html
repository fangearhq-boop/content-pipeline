Giannis Trade Race: Heat vs. Celtics, 5 Days to the June 23 Deadline

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 18, 2026

While New York celebrates a parade, the rest of the NBA is watching a clock. Five days until the June 23 NBA Draft — the de facto deadline for the Giannis Antetokounmpo trade — and the race has narrowed to two teams.

The Miami Heat and the Boston Celtics are the final contenders for the most anticipated trade in the NBA offseason. Multiple reports confirm the Heat remain the frontrunner, but nothing is close to settled. The Bucks have not been impressed by what Milwaukee has received so far, and the Celtics face structural challenges that make their path complicated.

Marc Stein reported this week that a deal is "indeed drawing near." Five days is not much time. The market is about to move.

The Heat's Offer — and Why It's Stalling

Miami's package centers on Tyler Herro as the primary asset, paired with draft picks and additional role players. The Heat have made multiple additions and adjustments to their offer since initial discussions at the trade deadline.

ESPN's Brian Windhorst reported that the Bucks have not been wowed by the package. Milwaukee's front office is aware of its leverage — Giannis has two years remaining on his contract, and a $275 million supermax extension becomes eligible October 1 — and is not in a rush to accept the first strong offer they receive.

Some teams have reportedly pressed the Bucks to come to them with a specific price they would accept, rather than continuing back-and-forth negotiations. Milwaukee's posture suggests they believe more can be extracted before the deadline closes.

Boston's Dilemma

The Celtics' interest is real, but their path involves a painful decision: any competitive offer for Giannis would almost certainly require including Jaylen Brown as the centerpiece.

Brown just led the Celtics to a conference finals appearance and remains one of the better two-way wings in the league. Breaking up the Tatum-Brown partnership — one of the most successful duos in recent Boston history — is not a move the Celtics front office makes lightly.

The calculus is straightforward: a healthy Giannis Antetokounmpo is arguably a better player than Brown right now. But Giannis is 31, has dealt with injury concerns, and gives the Celtics two years of certainty before the extension window opens. Whether Boston views that as sufficient upside to break up what they have remains the core question.

The Deadline and What Happens Next

Co-owner Jimmy Haslam set the June 23 NBA Draft as the Bucks' preferred timeline for resolving Giannis's future. That date is not an arbitrary choice — it allows Milwaukee to potentially trade into draft picks as part of a deal, and it ensures clarity before the league-wide offseason dominoes begin to fall.

If no trade materializes by June 23, the Bucks are not obligated to move Giannis. He still has two years on his contract. But the dynamic shifts — it becomes a slower burn, more likely resolved in the summer or at the February trade deadline. Most observers still expect something to happen before June 23.

FanSided reported this week that the Bucks are also exploring trading up in the draft as part of an overall Giannis deal, adding another dimension to the negotiation.

What's Next

Expect activity over the next 72 hours as the deadline pressure intensifies. Both the Heat and Celtics will need to finalize their best offers. The Bucks will decide whether either package clears their bar.

For the two teams in the race: Miami would pair Giannis with Bam Adebayo on a roster built for defense and toughness. Boston would install Giannis alongside Jayson Tatum on one of the deepest rosters in the East. Either way, the conference landscape changes dramatically.

The Knicks just won. Now the race to stop them from doing it again begins in earnest.
