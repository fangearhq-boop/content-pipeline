Morant Trade Market Post-Draft: Pelicans Out, Suns Lead, Celtics Sniffing Around

By Jake Torres June 24, 2026

Two Teams Out — And Why It Matters

The Ja Morant trade market came into sharper focus on draft night. New Orleans drafted Jeremiah Fears with the No. 7 pick, installing the guard from Arkansas as the franchise's point guard of the future. That selection made the Pelicans' position on Morant official: they're not a fit, and they don't need to be. Minnesota had already rejected pursuit before the draft. Two of the league's most realistic landing spots are gone, and the field narrows considerably.

What remains is a complicated asset sitting inside a franchise trying to figure out who they are without him. Memphis used the No. 3 pick on Cameron Boozer from Duke, giving them a frontcourt piece. Whether Boozer is paired with Morant's replacement or folded into a trade package for a different kind of star, the Grizzlies are in a transitional phase. Their patience in the Morant market is a strategic move, not a lack of options.

Who's Actually in the Running

The Phoenix Suns lead current betting markets, though at odds that reflect just how complicated a Morant trade would be to execute. Phoenix needs a star and has been aggressive in the trade market in recent seasons. They have assets and a timeline that could work with Morant's contract, which runs through 2027-28 at escalating salaries ($42.1M next season, $44.8M in 2027-28, per his five-year $197.2M deal).

The Boston Celtics are circling, which makes sense given the context. Boston came into draft night hoping to land Giannis, lost that race to Miami, and now needs an alternative path to competing with the Heat next season. Morant at 26 represents a different kind of bet than Giannis at 31, but the talent ceiling is still star-caliber when healthy. The Milwaukee Bucks and Toronto Raptors round out the known field of interested parties.

The Contract Problem Is Real

Any honest analysis of the Morant trade market has to account for the injury history. He played 20 games in 2025-26. Over the last three seasons combined, he's appeared in 79 games. For a player whose entire game is built around the kind of explosive athleticism that puts stress on ankles and knees, that number creates real risk for any team trading significant assets to acquire him.

The talent floor remains high — Morant averaged 24.4 points per game in the games he played, and the combination of speed, finishing ability, and playmaking has no analogue in the league. But a team handing over future first-round picks for a player owed $44.8 million in 2027-28 is betting on health, and that's a harder sell when recent history suggests it's fragile. Memphis knows this, which is why they're patient rather than desperate.

What's Next

Free agency opens June 30, which will reshape the market for Morant as teams clarify their roster situations. The Suns will be clearest on their needs and available assets once they know which free agents they've retained or lost. Expect Morant trade talks to accelerate in late July and early August, once free agency settles. If Phoenix or Boston makes a serious offer, Memphis will listen. They just won't be rushed into accepting the wrong one.
