Austin Reaves Has Until June 29: The $62.5M Question Every Lakers Fan Is Asking

By Damon Pierce June 24, 2026

The Numbers That Frame Everything

Austin Reaves has a $14.9 million player option for 2026-27 and until June 29 to decide whether to decline it and enter free agency. If he does, the Los Angeles Lakers can offer a five-year maximum contract worth approximately $241 million. Every other team in the league — including the Brooklyn Nets, who have shown the most serious interest — is limited to a four-year deal worth roughly $178.5 million. That's a $62.5 million gap in Los Angeles's favor, and it's the central fact shaping every conversation about Reaves's future.

Reaves earned a max discussion. He averaged 23.3 points, 5.5 assists, 4.7 rebounds, and shot 49% from the field in 2025-26 — numbers that put him firmly in the tier of players teams build around. He went from undrafted in 2021 to one of the most productive shooting guards in the Western Conference in five seasons, which is one of the better self-made arcs in recent NBA history.

What "Not Solely About Dollars" Actually Means

Reaves made one comment that's been chewed on constantly since: his decision "won't come down solely to dollars." At first read, that sounds like a player hedging, keeping leverage alive. But there's a more charitable interpretation. A $62.5 million gap doesn't close no matter how much a competitor wants it to. Saying the decision isn't purely financial is probably true — it just doesn't mean the financial gap is ignorable.

What other factors matter? Winning comes first. The Lakers have LeBron James in year 23 and a roster that can compete for a title. Brooklyn is in a rebuild, which means better contract terms potentially come at the cost of playoff contention for years. Reaves is 28. He's in his prime now. The math of taking less money to spend your best seasons on a losing team is hard to make work, whatever the contract number says.

Who Else Is Watching

The Nets have shown interest and can offer the max available to outside teams. The Detroit Pistons are also monitoring the situation, though they're further back in the conversation. Neither team has the same combination of max money, championship infrastructure, and organizational stability that Los Angeles can offer.

Multiple sources with knowledge of the situation expect Reaves to re-sign with the Lakers. That consensus has been consistent for weeks. But "expected" and "done" are different things in NBA free agency, and until June 29 passes without a surprise, the speculation runs on. The Lakers can begin exclusive negotiations before the moratorium lifts, meaning conversations between the sides are almost certainly already happening.

What's Next

Reaves's deadline to decline the player option is June 29. Free agency officially opens June 30. If he declines the option and signs with the Lakers during the moratorium period — which teams can agree to deals but not officially execute them until July 6 — that announcement would come sometime in the final days of June. Watch for a report from Adrian Wojnarowski or Shams Charania between now and the 29th. That's when this story ends, one way or the other.
