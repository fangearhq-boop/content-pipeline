AJ Dybantsa Goes No. 1 to Wizards — Full 2026 NBA Draft First-Round Recap

By Damon Pierce June 24, 2026

The Night's Biggest Moment: Dybantsa to Washington

The 2026 NBA Draft opened at Barclays Center on June 23 with a pick that felt inevitable for months and delivered exactly what Washington needed. AJ Dybantsa, the 6'9" wing from BYU who averaged 25.5 points and shot 51% from the field this season, became the first player from BYU ever selected No. 1 overall. The Wizards had been the heavy favorites for months, and they didn't waver.

What made Dybantsa's path unusual was his refusal to participate in any pre-draft workouts. While most prospects spent the spring flying city-to-city impressing front offices, Dybantsa stayed home. When asked about it, his response was blunt: "They know what I can do." It was the kind of confidence that either reads as arrogance or as a player so secure in his own tape that proving himself in a gym felt redundant. Washington bet on the tape.

Top of the Board: Peterson, Boozer, and a Loaded Top Three

Darryn Peterson went No. 2 overall to the Utah Jazz, ending weeks of speculation about whether the Wizards might pivot. Peterson had only visited Washington during his pre-draft tour — he refused meetings with every other franchise — but the Jazz saw enough on film to pull the trigger. The Kansas guard was projected as a franchise cornerstone and Utah didn't need much convincing.

Cameron Boozer landed at No. 3 in Memphis, giving the Grizzlies a frontcourt piece to build around. Boozer, out of Duke, gives head coach Taylor Jenkins a young post presence to complement whatever roster Memphis is building around the Ja Morant situation that remains unresolved. Darius Acuff Jr. went No. 7 to Sacramento, while Golden State grabbed Michigan's Yaxel Lendeborg at No. 11 and Philadelphia took Alabama guard Labaron Philon Jr. at No. 12.

The Surprise: Meleek Thomas Falls Out of Round One

The night's biggest shock wasn't any of the top picks. It was who didn't hear his name called. Arkansas guard Meleek Thomas, one of the most talked-about prospects heading into draft week and widely projected to go inside the top 20, went undrafted through all 30 first-round selections. He's now available when the second round continues on June 24.

Thomas falling isn't necessarily a death sentence for his NBA future. Second-round picks who show up in the right system often outperform their draft slot. But it's a reminder that scouting is inexact, team needs shift in real time, and draft night rarely plays out the way mock drafts suggest it will.

What's Next

Round two continues June 24, and Thomas will be among the most-watched names available. For the first-round picks, the work starts now: summer league rosters will be announced shortly, and for a class headlined by Dybantsa and Peterson, the real test comes in October when the games count. Watch Dybantsa's NBA debut against NBA-caliber athleticism and length — that's the moment that settles months of draft-night debate.
