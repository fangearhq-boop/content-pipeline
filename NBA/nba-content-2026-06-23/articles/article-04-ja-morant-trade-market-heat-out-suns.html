Ja Morant Trade Market Reshuffles: Heat Out After Giannis Deal, Suns Move to the Front

By Jake Torres | June 23, 2026

The Ja Morant trade market entered June 23 with one major assumption baked in: the Miami Heat were the most likely landing spot if they missed on Giannis Antetokounmpo. That assumption is now off the table. Miami landed Giannis on Monday night, removing themselves entirely from the Morant sweepstakes and reshuffling a market that the Memphis Grizzlies have been carefully managing for weeks.

With the Heat eliminated, the conversation shifts. The Phoenix Suns now hold the clearest path, according to Kalshi prediction markets — which peg Phoenix at an 18 percent chance of landing Morant, the highest of any team now that Miami is off the board. The Sacramento Kings and New Orleans Pelicans have also been in contact with Memphis, as has Minnesota.

Memphis' Calculated Patience

The Grizzlies have been deliberate throughout this process. Memphis' front office chose to pause trade discussions while the Giannis situation played out, recognizing that the Heat would have been the most aggressive bidder for Morant. That decision — to hold and wait — now means the Grizzlies face a different set of suitors, and likely a different class of return package.

Morant is 27 years old and entering the final two years of his five-year, $197.2 million extension. He is owed $42.1 million in 2026-27 and $44.8 million in 2027-28. His value as a tradeable asset remains high — he averaged 24.4 points per game last season — but the market has narrowed.

Memphis Holds the No. 3 Pick Tonight

One leverage point the Grizzlies carry into draft night: the No. 3 overall pick. Memphis could use the pick as a standalone asset or package it alongside Morant to increase the appeal of a deal for a team that may not otherwise have the cap structure to absorb his contract. A three-team trade involving the No. 3 pick and Morant has been floated in league circles as one mechanism to unlock the market.

The Suns' Position

Phoenix's interest in Morant is built around a straightforward basketball logic: a high-flying point guard who can create offense in transition and pick-and-roll situations would immediately elevate a Suns team searching for its identity. The Suns' ability to offer a package with young assets and future picks makes them a credible bidder, even without the Heat's depth.

Kings and Pelicans interest suggests teams that see Morant as a franchise-altering talent worth paying the price — in both contract and assets — to acquire. Neither has been publicly confirmed as making a formal offer to Memphis.

What's Next

The Grizzlies are expected to accelerate trade discussions post-draft now that the Giannis uncertainty has been removed. League observers expect the Morant market to move quickly — possibly within days of tonight's draft. Memphis' leverage is real but time-limited: the longer the situation lingers into free agency, the more complicated roster management becomes. Watch the next 48 to 72 hours for movement from either Phoenix or Sacramento as the league's attention shifts from draft night to the summer's next big deal.
