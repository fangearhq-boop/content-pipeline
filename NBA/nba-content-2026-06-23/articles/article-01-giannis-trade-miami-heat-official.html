Giannis Antetokounmpo Traded to Miami Heat: Bucks End 13-Year Era in Blockbuster Deal

By Jake Torres | June 23, 2026

After weeks of speculation, late-night negotiations, and competing trade packages from across the league, it is official: Giannis Antetokounmpo is a member of the Miami Heat. The Milwaukee Bucks announced the deal on the eve of the 2026 NBA Draft, sending the two-time MVP to South Beach in one of the most consequential trades in league history.

The Heat land Antetokounmpo and veteran forward Bobby Portis in the deal. Milwaukee receives a youth-and-picks return: guard Tyler Herro, center Kel'el Ware, forward Jaime Jaquez Jr., guard Kasparas Jakucionis, the No. 13 overall pick in tonight's 2026 NBA Draft, and two additional future first-round selections.

The Trade That Beat Boston

The Boston Celtics made a serious push to land Antetokounmpo, offering 2024 Finals MVP Jaylen Brown and two first-round picks. It was a compelling package — and ultimately not enough. Milwaukee opted for youth and draft capital over a marquee veteran, a choice that signals a long-term rebuild rather than a quick pivot to another star.

The Heat's winning offer centered on Herro's proven scoring ability, Ware's two-way upside, and a bounty of future picks. With Jakucionis — a creative playmaker who can contribute immediately — also included, Milwaukee has the building blocks to compete sooner than many rebuilding teams.

End of an Era in Milwaukee

Antetokounmpo's tenure with the Bucks spanned 13 seasons and produced one of the most decorated individual careers in franchise history. He won the NBA MVP award in both 2019 and 2020, captured Defensive Player of the Year honors in 2020, and led Milwaukee to its first NBA title since 1971 in 2021 — earning Finals MVP in a six-game series over the Phoenix Suns.

He leaves Milwaukee as arguably the greatest Buck since Kareem Abdul-Jabbar. For the organization, the trade represents a deliberate reset — accepting that the championship window had closed and extracting maximum value while Antetokounmpo remained one of the most coveted players in the sport.

What the Heat Get

In Miami, Antetokounmpo joins a roster anchored by All-Star center Bam Adebayo. The pairing of two elite, physical frontcourt players gives head coach Erik Spoelstra one of the most formidable lineups in the Eastern Conference. The Heat — who built their culture on maximum effort and adaptable basketball — now have the star power to back it up.

Miami also enters tonight's draft holding the No. 13 pick, acquired as part of this very deal. That additional asset gives the Heat immediate flexibility and roster construction options heading into the new season.

What's Next

For the Heat, the offseason focus shifts immediately to surrounding Antetokounmpo with the right pieces. Free agency opens June 30, and Miami now has both the star power and the draft asset to shape an aggressive offseason push. For the Celtics, the Giannis miss raises urgent questions about the next direction — do they pivot toward another big trade, or build around their existing core? And in Milwaukee, the Bucks begin a new chapter tonight at Barclays Center, drafting at No. 13 with a fresh young roster already taking shape.
