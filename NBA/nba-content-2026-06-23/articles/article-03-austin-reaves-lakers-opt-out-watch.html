Austin Reaves Opt-Out Watch: Lakers' $241M Offer vs. Nets' $178.5M as June 29 Looms

By Damon Pierce | June 23, 2026

The Austin Reaves free agency drama is building toward a June 29 deadline, and every indicator points to one conclusion: he is opting out of his $14.9 million player option to test the market. What happens after that is where the real intrigue lies.

Reaves comes off the best statistical season of his career, averaging 23.3 points, 5.5 assists, 4.7 rebounds, and 1.1 steals per game while shooting 49.0 percent from the field. He is 28 years old, proven, and entering what could be the most lucrative contract of his life.

The Two Offers on the Table

The Los Angeles Lakers have made clear they intend to offer Reaves the maximum contract available to them: five years at up to $241 million. The Brooklyn Nets, meanwhile, are prepared to offer four years at approximately $178.5 million. That gap — nearly $62.5 million — is substantial by any measure.

On paper, this should be a simple decision for Reaves. It rarely is. The guard has been consistent in framing his choice in terms beyond pure dollars, saying publicly that his decision "will not come down solely to dollars." Multiple league sources expect him to re-sign with Los Angeles, citing his preference for winning and his comfort in the organization that drafted him and turned him from an undrafted prospect into an All-Star-caliber guard.

The Lakers' Case

Beyond the financial advantage, the Lakers offer Reaves something the Nets cannot: championship-level infrastructure. The Atlanta Hawks and Detroit Pistons have also expressed interest, but neither is expected to match the Lakers' offer or roster appeal.

The Lakers have leveraged a $61.9 million contract advantage in their favor — the result of being able to offer a fifth year under their Bird Rights. That edge matters significantly over the life of the deal, and league executives widely project it to be decisive.

The Nets' Play

Brooklyn's pitch to Reaves hinges on a different kind of promise: a leading role on a young, rebuilding team with significant cap space and a front office committed to building around him. For a player who came up through the system in Los Angeles, that narrative of stardom may hold some appeal — but the gap in money and winning track record is a significant barrier.

What's Next

Reaves has until June 29 to formally decline his player option. NBA free agency opens June 30, at which point the Lakers can sign him to the full maximum deal immediately. If Reaves opts out and the Lakers make their offer official on June 30, the expectation is that a deal gets done quickly. A prolonged courtship that stretches into July would signal something unexpected — either a change of heart or an outside suitor making a serious run. For now, all signs point toward Reaves staying in purple and gold.
