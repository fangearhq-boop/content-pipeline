NBA Draft Night 2026: AJ Dybantsa on the Clock as Giannis Trade Reshapes the Board

By Marcus Cole | June 23, 2026

The 2026 NBA Draft gets underway tonight at 8 p.m. ET at Barclays Center in Brooklyn, and for the first time all offseason, the board is completely set. AJ Dybantsa is the overwhelming favorite to go No. 1 overall to the Washington Wizards. The Miami Heat — fresh off acquiring Giannis Antetokounmpo — enter the night picking at No. 13 with the Milwaukee pick in hand. And the New York Knicks, NBA champions, wait patiently at No. 24.

Draft night 2026 promises to be one of the most eventful in recent memory, with multiple trade scenarios already in play before the first name is called.

Dybantsa: The Presumptive No. 1

AJ Dybantsa spent his only college season at BYU putting up numbers that few freshmen in the modern era have matched. He averaged 25.5 points per game on 51.0 percent shooting from the field, adding 6.8 rebounds and 3.7 assists per game from the wing. At 6-foot-9, he combines elite scoring instincts with the size to defend multiple positions at the next level.

Perhaps as notable as his production is his pre-draft posture. Dybantsa did not work out for a single team before the draft — a decision he defended directly. "I don't gotta work out," he said. "They know what I can do." His odds reflect that confidence: -750 on FanDuel, with 80 percent probability on prediction markets like Polymarket and Kalshi for the No. 1 slot.

Washington has been widely reported to have settled on Dybantsa. The Wizards hold the top pick and have been in active dialogue with his camp throughout the pre-draft process.

Peterson at No. 2 and Beyond

Kansas guard Darryn Peterson is projected to go No. 2 to the Utah Jazz, followed by Cameron Boozer at No. 3. Peterson averaged 20.2 points per game at Kansas and is considered one of the most complete two-way guards in the class, despite missing 11 games during the season.

The remaining top 10 promises plenty of intrigue. The Heat's addition of the No. 13 pick in the Giannis deal gives them a chance to add another promising young piece to an already revamped roster.

Knicks Make Their Picks at 24 and 31

The defending champions enter draft night with modest draft capital — No. 24 in the first round and No. 31 via Washington in the second — a natural consequence of trading for pieces that helped them win the championship in June. The front office has been transparent: the real offseason work involves Jalen Brunson's extension, re-signing OG Anunoby, and bringing back Karl-Anthony Towns, not unearthing franchise talent in the late first round.

Still, the Knicks have a history of unearthing contributors in the late first round. Tonight's picks will add depth to a roster already built for a championship run.

What's Next

Round 1 tips off tonight at 8 p.m. ET on ABC and ESPN. Round 2 follows on June 24. After the picks are made, the league's attention will shift quickly to free agency, which opens June 30. For the Wizards, the Dybantsa era begins tonight — assuming he goes where all signs point. For the Heat, they add yet another asset on what has already been one of the most consequential nights in franchise history.
