Knicks Enter Draft Night as Champions: Picks No. 24 and No. 31 as Bigger Offseason Priorities Loom

By Marcus Cole | June 23, 2026

Nine days after winning the NBA championship, the New York Knicks take their seat at the 2026 NBA Draft table — and it is a humble one. The defending champions hold the No. 24 pick in the first round and the No. 31 pick (via Washington) in the second round, the natural consequence of being good enough to trade away early selections along the road to a title.

That is not a complaint. It is a reality of the NBA's competitive machinery: championships are built through trades that cost draft capital, and the Knicks spent wisely. Two million fans turned out for the Canyon of Heroes parade on June 18. Now the front office shifts its focus from celebration to roster construction.

The Real Offseason Agenda

Knicks president Leon Rose and head coach Tom Thibodeau enter this offseason with a clear priority list. At the top: a Jalen Brunson extension. The point guard who orchestrated New York's championship run has earned max contract consideration, and the Knicks are expected to move aggressively on an extension that will anchor the franchise for years to come.

Beyond Brunson, the front office must navigate the re-signing of two critical pieces: forward OG Anunoby and center Karl-Anthony Towns. Both are essential to what the Knicks do offensively and defensively, and losing either would fundamentally alter a championship-caliber roster. The luxury tax bill looming over the organization is substantial, but ownership has signaled a willingness to spend to keep the core together.

What Late First-Round Picks Can Provide

Picks at No. 24 and No. 31 are not franchise-altering selections — but they are not irrelevant either. The late first round has produced meaningful contributors across NBA history, and a Knicks team that already has its stars in place can afford to let a rookie develop with low pressure in a winning environment. The No. 24 pick will be used on the best available player, whether a wing, a backup point guard, or a developing big man who can compete for a roster spot.

The No. 31 pick, acquired via Washington in an earlier deal, represents an additional chip. It could be used or packaged in a future trade as the Knicks continue to manage their roster depth.

Repeat or Reload?

The New York Knicks have not won back-to-back championships since, well, ever. The franchise's two titles in the early 1970s were not consecutive. If Brunson, Anunoby, and Towns are re-signed and the roster structure remains largely intact, the Knicks will enter next season as legitimate favorites to make a deep playoff run again.

That is the bar they have set for themselves. One championship validated the blueprint. A second would cement a dynasty in Manhattan — and put this front office and coaching staff among the most celebrated in league history.

What's Next

The Knicks draft at No. 24 tonight beginning at 8 p.m. ET on ABC and ESPN. The No. 31 pick follows in Round 2 on June 24. Free agency opens June 30, at which point the real Knicks offseason begins. Expect movement on the Brunson extension quickly — both sides are motivated to get it done. OG Anunoby and Karl-Anthony Towns negotiations will follow. Keep watch: if those three deals get completed in the first two weeks of July, the Knicks will have made their statement about what this offseason is truly about.
