LeBron James Decision Day: Moratorium Lifts, But the Wait Isn't Over Yet

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 6, 2026

The NBA's free-agent moratorium lifted at 12:01 PM Eastern today — and LeBron James, the greatest player of his generation, is officially eligible to sign a contract with any team in the league. The world has been waiting for this moment for six days. If you were hoping for an instant answer, Rich Paul has a message: keep waiting.

"I don't think this happens anytime soon," Paul, LeBron's agent and closest confidant, told reporters over the weekend. He indicated the decision would not come in the next few days, suggesting fans and front offices should prepare for a period of continued suspense. "He's heard from every team in the entire league except one or two," Paul added — which, when you pause to think about it, is an extraordinary statement. Every franchise in the NBA is pitching LeBron James.

Two Frontrunners, One Wild Card

The race has consolidated around two teams. The Golden State Warriors remain a credible frontrunner, having spent the past week actively constructing the roster they hope will convince LeBron to choose San Francisco over Cleveland. The Warriors signed Kristaps Porzingis to provide floor-spacing size and are aggressively pursuing Anthony Davis from Washington in a trade that would reshape their frontcourt into a legitimate championship core alongside Stephen Curry and Draymond Green.

The Cleveland Cavaliers offer something the Warriors cannot: history, identity, and the emotional weight of a homecoming. LeBron grew up in Akron, Ohio. He played seven seasons in Cleveland across two stints. In 2016, he orchestrated the most improbable Finals comeback in NBA history — erasing a 3-1 deficit against these same Golden State Warriors — to deliver the city its first major sports championship in 52 years. The pull of finishing a career where it all began is real.

And then there is the surprise entrant: the Minnesota Timberwolves reportedly made a pitch over the weekend that turned more than a few heads. Details of that pitch have not been fully reported, but the fact that Minnesota is even in the conversation at this stage speaks to the genuine open-endedness of a decision everyone assumed was already made.

The Warriors Are Building

Golden State is not sitting still while LeBron deliberates. The Porzingis signing gives the Warriors a 7-foot-3 stretch big who can shoot over any defense and protect the rim — a profile that fits seamlessly alongside Curry's spacing demands. The Davis pursuit is bolder still: league sources say the trade would need to include Jimmy Butler, on his expiring $57 million contract while recovering from a torn ACL, plus draft capital.

Washington has been resistant. The Wizards just locked up Trae Young on a four-year, $212 million extension and are signaling long-term commitment to building around Davis. Whether the Warriors' asset package — two future first-round picks and four first-round swaps — is enough to change that calculation remains to be seen. But Golden State's willingness to engage at this level tells LeBron something important: the Warriors are all in.

What's Next

LeBron James can sign anytime from this moment forward. Rich Paul's "not anytime soon" framing suggests the formal announcement could come days or even weeks from now. The league will continue to hold its breath. The Warriors will continue building. The Cavaliers will continue waiting. And somewhere, LeBron James — 41 years old, entering his 24th NBA season, with every championship option in front of him — is making the most consequential call of his final chapter.
