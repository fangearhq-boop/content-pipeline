The Warriors' Grand Plan: Porzingis Signed, Anthony Davis Trade in Pursuit

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 6, 2026

Golden State isn't just waiting for LeBron James to make a phone call. The Warriors are building a roster that makes their pitch undeniable — and Day 1 of official free agency revealed just how aggressive that strategy has become.

Kristaps Porzingis is now officially a Golden State Warrior. The 7-foot-3 Latvian stretch big — a proven floor spacer and rim protector who can make any offense run more efficiently — gives head coach Steve Kerr a versatile frontcourt anchor alongside Stephen Curry's three-point gravity. The fit makes immediate sense: Porzingis's shooting and interior presence opens the exact kinds of cuts and driving lanes that Curry and Draymond Green have been exploiting for a decade.

The Anthony Davis Gambit

Porzingis is the confirmed move. The Anthony Davis pursuit is the ambitious one.

Multiple sources confirm that Golden State has engaged the Washington Wizards on a trade for Davis, the franchise cornerstone who has spent the past two seasons rebuilding his reputation after years of injury and trade drama in Los Angeles. A deal would reportedly require Jimmy Butler — on an expiring $57 million contract while still recovering from a torn ACL — plus draft capital from the Warriors' reserve of two future first-round picks and four first-round swaps.

Washington's resistance has been notable. The Wizards just signed Trae Young to a four-year, $212 million extension — a clear signal that management is committed to building something meaningful in the nation's capital. Packaging Young with Davis as the foundation of a legitimate contender is their stated vision. Whether the Warriors' asset offer is compelling enough to change that calculus entirely remains uncertain.

Why the Sequence Matters

The Warriors' strategic logic is transparent and bold: sign Porzingis to show roster intent, pursue Davis to demonstrate franchise commitment, and present LeBron James with a completed championship core — not a promise, but a reality. Curry. Green. Porzingis. Davis. LeBron. That lineup, if assembled, would be the most formidable collection of talent since the 2017 Warriors added Kevin Durant.

LeBron's camp has been clear that the decision will not come quickly. Rich Paul said over the weekend that the answer is still days or weeks away. In that window, every roster move Golden State makes is part of the pitch. The Cavaliers are offering history and emotion. The Warriors are offering construction and proof.

What's Next

The Davis trade pursuit will either resolve or collapse in the coming days. If Washington holds firm, Golden State will need to recalibrate around the Porzingis signing and whatever LeBron decides on his own terms. If the Wizards move, the Warriors would instantly become the prohibitive title favorites — and potentially remove all doubt from LeBron's calculus. The next 48 to 72 hours in Golden State are as consequential as any stretch in the franchise's recent history.
