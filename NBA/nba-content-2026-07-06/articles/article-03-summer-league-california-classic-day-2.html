California Classic Day 2: Lakers Edge Heat in OT, Kings Stay Perfect

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | July 6, 2026

While the rest of the NBA world was watching clocks tick toward 12:01 PM Eastern and the moratorium lift, the California Classic Summer League was busy producing genuinely exciting basketball on Saturday. Day 2 delivered overtime drama, a dominant rout, and an undefeated Kings team continuing to build buzz in Sacramento.

The Los Angeles Lakers escaped with a 93-91 overtime win over the Miami Heat, the Golden State Warriors Gold crushed the San Antonio Spurs 98-69, and the Sacramento Kings improved to 2-0 by edging Warriors Blue 91-85 in a late-charging comeback.

Cameron Carr Leads Lakers in OT Thriller

Cameron Carr was the story of the day. The Lakers guard exploded for 26 points — matching the energy and skill that drew pre-draft attention — and Anton Watson delivered the decisive blow, drilling the go-ahead basket in overtime to seal the 93-91 victory over Miami.

It was the kind of Summer League game that sticks in a general manager's memory: pressure situation, final possession, late-game execution. Carr handled it. For a Lakers organization still sorting out its post-LeBron identity, finding dependable young pieces in summer play carries real weight.

Kings Go 2-0, Warriors Gold Rolls

The Sacramento Kings are the early California Classic story. Emanuel Sharp was the catalyst in a second-half comeback against Golden State Warriors Blue, leading Sacramento to a 91-85 victory and an unblemished 2-0 record in the tournament. The Kings — who were lottery-bound last season — are using Summer League to showcase young depth and generate momentum heading into a crucial offseason.

For the Warriors Gold side, Yaxel Lendeborg was the headliner in a lopsided 98-69 demolition of the San Antonio Spurs. The performance reinforced what scouts already suspected: Golden State's development pipeline remains one of the league's best, even as the organization builds toward a championship-or-bust LeBron push at the top.

The Bigger Picture

Coming off the explosive Salt Lake City debut performances by No. 2 pick Darryn Peterson — 28 points, go-ahead OT 3 for the Utah Jazz — and No. 3 pick Cameron Boozer's solid 15-point, 4-assist outing for the Memphis Grizzlies, the 2026 class continues to exceed early expectations in competitive summer settings. The California Classic runs through the week, with more names set to emerge from the bracket play.

What's Next

The California Classic continues through the week, with the Kings looking to stay perfect and the Lakers chasing momentum after their overtime win. The Salt Lake City Summer League runs in parallel, where Peterson and Boozer will look to build on their strong debuts. With free agency news dominating headlines, Summer League provides the NBA's younger audience with actual basketball to watch — and this year's class is making the most of the spotlight.
