Day 1 Official: Drummond Joins Knicks, Powell Signs with Bulls, Eason Rockets Deal Finalized

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 6, 2026

LeBron James may be dominating the headlines, but free agency is about far more than one man's decision. As the NBA's moratorium lifted at 12:01 PM Eastern today, dozens of agreed-upon deals officially became binding contracts — and a handful of veteran signings reshaped rosters in meaningful ways that the blockbuster chatter tends to obscure.

Andre Drummond Brings Size to New York

The New York Knicks have added Andre Drummond on a one-year, $3.8 million deal. The veteran center — a two-time All-Star in his prime who averaged double-digit rebounds for five consecutive seasons earlier in his career — brings interior presence and energy to a Knicks roster still searching for the right frontcourt configuration.

Drummond at this stage of his career is a depth signing, not a franchise-altering move. But the Knicks are a team that needs bodies, needs rebounding, and needs a veteran capable of providing minutes when the starters need rest. At under $4 million, the risk is minimal and the upside — a motivated Drummond in a contract year with something to prove — is real.

Norman Powell Heads to Chicago

Norman Powell is a Chicago Bull on a two-year deal. The sharpshooting wing, who spent his most recent seasons with the Los Angeles Clippers, brings exactly the thing Chicago needs most: reliable three-point shooting from a player who doesn't need the ball in his hands to be effective.

Powell shot consistently from beyond the arc during his time in Los Angeles, and his ability to get off his shot quickly in catch-and-shoot situations makes him ideal alongside Chicago's younger ball-dominant pieces. The Bulls are in a transitional period — not yet rebuilding, not yet a true contender — and Powell's two-year deal provides structure without locking up cap space beyond a manageable commitment window.

Tari Eason Officially a Rocket for Five More Years

The biggest of the Day 1 official signings is arguably the one that was least surprising: Tari Eason's five-year, $81.5 million extension with the Houston Rockets is now fully signed and binding. Eason, the Rockets' defensive enforcer and energy forward, agreed to the deal pre-moratorium but could not make it official until today.

At 23, Eason represents exactly the kind of long-term investment Houston is making across its roster — locking up young, high-upside players before they reach the open market at full premium. The Rockets are not rebuilding. They are loading up, methodically assembling a roster they believe will compete for the Western Conference within two seasons.

What's Next

Day 1 of official signings is just the beginning. The real flood of contracts will come over the next 48 to 72 hours as teams finalize deals on players across the entire salary spectrum. The veteran market — always active once the blockbuster names are settled — will produce another wave of roster-shaping moves before the week is out. And somewhere above all of it, LeBron James will eventually pick up the phone and call his new team. When that happens, the rest of the league will instantly adjust.
