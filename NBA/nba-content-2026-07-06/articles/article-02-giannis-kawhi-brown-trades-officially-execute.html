It's Official: Giannis in Miami, Kawhi in Toronto, Brown in Philly — the NBA's Offseason Becomes Real

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 6, 2026

At 12:01 PM Eastern on Monday, the NBA's free-agent moratorium lifted — and in that same moment, three of the most consequential trades in recent league history officially became binding contracts. Giannis Antetokounmpo is a Miami Heat. Kawhi Leonard is a Toronto Raptor. Jaylen Brown is a Philadelphia 76er. Paul George is a Boston Celtic. Everything that had been agreed upon, whispered about, and debated for the past week is now real.

The Eastern Conference looks nothing like it did seven days ago.

Giannis Goes to Miami

The blockbuster of all blockbusters is complete. Giannis Antetokounmpo, the two-time MVP and one of the five best players on the planet, is officially a member of the Miami Heat. The Bucks received a staggering return: guard Tyler Herro, center Kel'el Ware, forward Jaime Jaquez Jr., guard Kasparas Jakučionis, the No. 13 overall pick in the 2026 draft, first-round picks in 2031 and 2033, a 2030 pick swap, and a 2033 second-round pick.

Milwaukee got assets. Miami got Giannis. The Heat's transformation from a perpetual contender into a genuine championship-level threat is complete the moment No. 34 suits up in South Beach. Pat Riley's vision — assembled piece by piece through the Wiggins and Hardaway signings earlier in free agency — now has its centerpiece.

Kawhi Returns to Toronto

Kawhi Leonard is a Toronto Raptor for the second time in his career, and this homecoming carries an entirely different emotional weight than the first. At 34, Leonard is coming back to the city where he won his only championship, where he played the most dominant Finals performance of his career in 2019. The Clippers receive a rebuild package: forward Brandon Ingram, guard Gradey Dick, two first-round picks, two second-round picks, and a pick swap.

Toronto gets Kawhi, and with it the chance to write a second chapter around one of the most uniquely gifted two-way players the league has ever produced — when healthy. The durability question has never fully gone away for Leonard, but a motivated Kawhi at 34 in a familiar city remains an elite NBA player.

Brown to Philly, George to Boston

The Celtics-76ers swap that rocked Boston's fanbase a week ago is now fully official. Jaylen Brown, the 2024 NBA Finals MVP, is a Philadelphia 76er. Paul George is a Boston Celtic — along with a 2028 unprotected first-round pick, an unprotected 2031 first-round pick, and second-round picks in 2028 and 2030.

For the Celtics, it is a calculated bet: Brown's max contract was a financial burden; George at his ceiling is a proven All-NBA player who gives Jayson Tatum the secondary star he has lacked. Boston also stockpiled the pick currency for future flexibility. Whether it works depends entirely on George finding the right version of himself in a new city, alongside a different superstar, in a different offensive system.

What's Next

The Eastern Conference title conversation has at least five legitimate contenders for the first time in years: Miami with Giannis, Boston with George and Tatum, Philadelphia with Brown and whoever else they add, Milwaukee in rebuild mode, and Toronto banking on Kawhi's health. Championship odds will shift dramatically once LeBron James makes his decision and the Western Conference picture firms up. But today, July 6, is the day the league's most transformative offseason stopped being hypothetical and became permanent.
