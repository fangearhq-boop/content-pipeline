Play-In Night 3 Preview: Magic, Suns Playing For Their Playoff Lives Tonight

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 16, 2026

Wednesday gave us Stephen Curry's second-half takeover and Tyrese Maxey's masterclass. Thursday brings something even higher-stakes: two elimination games that will decide the final playoff seeds — and end two more NBA seasons.

Tonight's Play-In Night 3 schedule features the Orlando Magic against the Charlotte Hornets at 7:30 PM ET, followed by the Golden State Warriors visiting the Phoenix Suns at 10:00 PM ET. Both games operate under the same brutal rule: win and you're in the playoffs, lose and your summer starts now.

East: Magic vs. Hornets — 7:30 PM ET

Orlando's path to this game ran through a 12-point loss to the Philadelphia 76ers on Wednesday. Desmond Bane kept the Magic alive with 34 points, but Tyrese Maxey was simply better, and now Orlando must win a second consecutive game just to reach the first round.

Charlotte arrives on the other side of what might be the Play-In Tournament's defining moment so far. LaMelo Ball scored 30 points and delivered the go-ahead layup with 4.7 seconds left in overtime against the Miami Heat on April 14, sending the Hornets to tonight's game. Miles Bridges ended it with a clutch block at the buzzer. The moment sent the Spectrum Center into a frenzy and gave Charlotte something this franchise has needed for a decade: a signature postseason moment.

The Hornets haven't made the playoffs since 2016. If LaMelo Ball and Bridges can hold off a desperate Magic team tonight, that drought ends. For a franchise that has spent most of the last decade rebuilding around young talent, this would be a meaningful milestone — and a statement that Charlotte is ready to be taken seriously in the East.

Orlando has the talent to be dangerous. Jalen Suggs is a high-level defender who will make Ball work for everything. But the Hornets have the momentum, the crowd, and the layup that everyone is still talking about.

West: Warriors vs. Suns — 10:00 PM ET

Phoenix comes in humbled. The Trail Blazers knocked out the Suns 114-110 in the No. 7 seed game on April 14, ending what many expected to be the Suns' easier path to the playoffs. Now they face a Golden State team riding one of the postseason's great individual performances.

Stephen Curry's 35-point, 27-in-the-second-half eruption against the Clippers will still be fresh in everyone's memory when tip-off arrives Friday. The Warriors have all the psychological edge, and Curry has proven in recent days that his playoff instincts remain as sharp as they've ever been.

The Suns are not without weapons — they still have established veterans and a roster built for moments like this. Home court gives Phoenix an advantage. Desperation can be a motivator. But facing a Warriors team carrying this much momentum, with Curry showing no signs of slowing down, is a significant ask.

The No. 8 West seed will face the Oklahoma City Thunder in the first round — a matchup that appears daunting on paper for either survivor. But to get there, one of these teams has to navigate the NBA's most unforgiving single-elimination format.

What's Next

Tonight's winners claim the final two playoff seeds. The 2026 NBA Playoffs' first round begins Saturday, April 18. East: the winner of Magic-Hornets joins the Detroit Pistons, Boston Celtics, New York Knicks, and Cleveland Cavaliers in an eight-team field. West: the survivor of Warriors-Suns joins the Thunder, Spurs, Nuggets, and Lakers. The bracket is nearly complete. Tonight, it finishes.
