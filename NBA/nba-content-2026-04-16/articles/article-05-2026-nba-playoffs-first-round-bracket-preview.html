2026 NBA Playoffs First Round Preview: Breaking Down Every Matchup

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 16, 2026

Two names will be added to the bracket tonight when Play-In Night 3 concludes. But 14 of the 16 first-round participants are already set — and the 2026 NBA Playoffs first round has everything a basketball fan could want: a dynasty chasing another title, a franchise making its first playoff run in two decades, a superstar playing without his co-star, and LeBron James still finding new milestones to chase.

The first round begins Saturday, April 18. Here is what to watch in every matchup.

Eastern Conference

No. 1 Detroit Pistons vs. No. 8 TBD (Winner of Hornets-Magic)

The Pistons finished 64-18, their first 60-win season since 2005-06 and only the third 60-win season in franchise history. Cade Cunningham returned from a collapsed lung to help close the regular season, and Detroit is the team everyone in the East is quietly trying to avoid. Their opponent will be either Charlotte or Orlando — two teams that have been playing elimination basketball all week and will arrive either exhausted or emotionally primed. Detroit is a heavy favorite regardless of who survives tonight.

No. 2 Boston Celtics vs. No. 7 Philadelphia 76ers

Boston is built for this moment. The Celtics finished 56-26 and feature Jayson Tatum leading one of the Eastern Conference's most experienced playoff rosters. The Sixers arrive without Joel Embiid — whose appendectomy has left him with no confirmed return date — leaning entirely on Tyrese Maxey. Philadelphia beat Orlando in the Play-In when Maxey was magnificent, but the Celtics present a much more demanding test. Series opens April 19 at TD Garden.

No. 3 New York Knicks vs. No. 6 Atlanta Hawks

This series will have an edge to it. The Knicks finished 55-27 and have the kind of physical, defensive identity that gives the Hawks problems. Atlanta finished with one of the league's stronger second halves and will bring a pace-and-space attack that challenges New York's ability to defend in space. The Knicks are favored but the Hawks have the tools to be unpredictable.

No. 4 Cleveland Cavaliers vs. No. 5 Toronto Raptors

The Cavaliers went 52-30 and feature one of the East's most balanced rosters. Donovan Mitchell and Chris Harden form a combination that Cleveland has built around effectively in the second half of the season, with the duo posting a 16-6 record when both played. Toronto, in its first playoff appearance after several rebuilding years, is a live underdog that plays fast and can shoot. The Cavs are the more complete team but shouldn't overlook a Raptors squad playing without pressure.

Western Conference

No. 1 Oklahoma City Thunder vs. No. 8 TBD (Winner of Warriors-Suns)

The Oklahoma City Thunder went 64-18 — tied with Detroit for the NBA's best record — and Shai Gilgeous-Alexander is the consensus MVP frontrunner with 31.6 points per game. OKC is the Western Conference's most dominant team, and their opponent tonight is either a Warriors team riding Curry's brilliance or a Suns squad desperate to redeem a Play-In stumble. Either way, the Thunder will be heavily favored in the first round.

No. 2 San Antonio Spurs vs. No. 7 Portland Trail Blazers

The Spurs are back. San Antonio returned to the playoffs for the first time since 2019, winning the Southwest Division and posting a 62-20 record. Victor Wembanyama is the near-unanimous DPOY candidate and the Spurs' gravitational center on both ends. Portland earned the No. 7 seed by knocking out the Suns in the Play-In, behind Deni Avdija's remarkable season. The Trail Blazers will compete, but the Spurs are in a different class.

No. 3 Denver Nuggets vs. No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves

This is the West's most competitive first-round matchup on paper. Denver went 54-28 and enters with one of the deepest rosters in the conference. Minnesota has built a defensive identity around Anthony Edwards and a strong supporting cast. These teams have met in the postseason before and know each other well. Nuggets are the slight favorite, but expect this to go six or seven games.

No. 4 Los Angeles Lakers vs. No. 5 Houston Rockets

LeBron James and the Los Angeles Lakers (53-29) host the Houston Rockets in a first-round matchup loaded with narrative. LeBron has been adding to his playoff record book all season and brings the kind of pedigree that always elevates a series' watchability. Houston is young, hungry, and finished 51-31 with one of the West's more improved rosters. The Rockets won't be intimidated by the Lakers' history — and this series could go deeper than the seedings suggest.

What's Next

Tonight's Play-In Night 3 games at 7:30 and 10:00 PM ET complete the bracket. The 2026 NBA Playoffs' first round tips off Saturday, April 18, with games running through approximately early May. Detroit and Oklahoma City enter as the conference's top seeds and favorites, but in a field this loaded — with Curry, LeBron, Tatum, SGA, and Wemby all in action — every series has the potential to surprise. The postseason is here.
