Maxey's 31-Point Masterclass Lifts Sixers Past Magic, 109-97

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 16, 2026

The question hanging over Wednesday night's NBA Play-In Tournament game in Philadelphia was simple and enormous: could the 76ers compete at a playoff level without Joel Embiid? Tyrese Maxey answered it with a performance that left no room for debate.

Maxey scored 31 points as the Philadelphia 76ers defeated the Orlando Magic 109-97, securing the No. 7 seed in the Eastern Conference and booking a first-round series against the Boston Celtics. He capped the night with seven consecutive points in the fourth quarter, turning a competitive game into a statement. It was exactly the kind of performance that defines a player's postseason reputation — and Maxey had it without his franchise center by his side.

Embiid, who underwent an appendectomy around April 9 following an emergency surgery in Houston, missed the game entirely. Coach Nick Nurse acknowledged this week that Embiid hasn't been around the team "at all" since the procedure. No timeline for his return has been set. The 76ers, at least on Wednesday, didn't seem to need him.

Edgecombe Steals the Subplot

If Maxey was the night's headliner, rookie V.J. Edgecombe was its most compelling subplot. The 20-year-old guard showed no signs of a first postseason jitter, finishing with 19 points and 11 defensive rebounds — the latter total particularly vital against a Magic frontcourt that stands three players at 6-foot-10 or taller.

Edgecombe also gave the night its most memorable moment of bravado. After scoring over All-Defensive guard Jalen Suggs, the rookie allegedly walked Suggs into the basket stanchion and stared him down, earning a technical foul. It was overzealous, maybe even counterproductive — but it was also the act of someone completely unintimidated by the playoff stage. Edgecombe had averaged 16.0 points, 5.6 rebounds and 4.2 assists per game during the regular season, and Wednesday was his proof-of-concept moment.

Orlando's Desmond Bane put up a tremendous individual effort, leading all scorers with 34 points. But the Magic couldn't find a second consistent contributor to match Philly's depth, and the deficit proved insurmountable as Maxey took over in the closing minutes.

What It Means for the Celtics Series

The 76ers, who finished the regular season 45-37, will face the No. 2 seeded Boston Celtics in the first round. Game 1 is scheduled for April 19 at TD Garden. Boston is a heavy favorite, and the Celtics have Jayson Tatum leading a team that has reached the Eastern Conference Semifinals for five straight years.

The Embiid question will dominate the series narrative. Based on the average NBA recovery time for an appendectomy — 23 days, according to injury database "In Street Clothes" — the earliest Embiid could theoretically return is around May 2. That window falls during the first-round series, which could extend to early May if it goes deep.

Maxey proved Wednesday that he can lead this team in high-pressure moments. Whether that ceiling is high enough to steal games from a healthy Celtics squad is the question the 76ers must now answer — with or without Embiid in the lineup.

What's Next

The Philadelphia 76ers open their first-round series against the Boston Celtics on April 19 at TD Garden. Game 2 is set for April 21. If Joel Embiid can return before the series ends, it completely transforms the matchup. If not, Tyrese Maxey and V.J. Edgecombe will need to pull off one of the postseason's signature upsets. Either way, the Sixers have shown they're alive — and that Maxey is ready for the biggest stage.
