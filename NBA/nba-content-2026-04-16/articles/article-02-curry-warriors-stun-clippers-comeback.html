Curry Erupts for 35, Warriors Stun Clippers in Epic Second-Half Comeback

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 16, 2026

Down 13 points in the fourth quarter with a season on the line, the Golden State Warriors did what they have always done in desperate moments: they gave the ball to Stephen Curry, got out of the way, and watched history happen.

Curry scored 35 points — 27 of them in the second half alone — as the Warriors defeated the Los Angeles Clippers 126-121 in the NBA Play-In Tournament on Wednesday night. He drilled his seventh three-pointer of the game with 50.4 seconds remaining to break a tie and put Golden State ahead for good. The Warriors closed the game on a 16-6 run, eliminating the Clippers and advancing to a winner-take-all game Friday against the Phoenix Suns.

It was one of the great individual clutch performances in Play-In Tournament history — and it came from a player who has given the basketball world so many of these moments that it's easy to take them for granted.

The Comeback Unfolds

The Warriors trailed for most of the evening. The Clippers built a 13-point lead in the fourth quarter with Bennedict Mathurin (23 points) and Darius Garland (21 points, 8 assists) keeping the deficit comfortable. Kawhi Leonard added 21 points across the game and appeared to be guiding Los Angeles toward the postseason.

Then Curry started hitting shots — the kind of escalating crescendo that Warrior fans know by feel now. He wasn't just scoring; he was scoring in the exact moments when the Clippers most needed a stop. Al Horford, who turned 37 in June, nailed four three-pointers during the fourth-quarter surge, proving that Golden State's supporting cast could rise to the moment when Curry created the opening.

Kristaps Porzingis contributed 20 quiet points on 8-of-12 shooting, including three three-pointers, giving the Warriors a functional second scorer when they needed points in the paint and on the perimeter both. But this game will be remembered for one player.

Leonard Goes Cold at the Worst Moment

For all that went right for Golden State, the Clippers' collapse had a specific shape: Kawhi Leonard, who scored 21 points in the first three quarters, went scoreless in the fourth quarter until the final 16 seconds of the game. By then, the Warriors had already taken control.

It is an uncomfortable data point for a Clippers franchise that bet heavily on Leonard's ability to deliver in postseason environments. Garland played well throughout, and Mathurin was the best Clipper on the night statistically. But when Los Angeles needed its veteran superstar to make a play, Leonard had nothing to offer against a fully locked-in Warriors defense.

The Clippers' season is over. Golden State moves on.

What's Next

The Golden State Warriors travel to Phoenix on Friday night for a winner-take-all game against the Suns, with the No. 8 West seed on the line. The Suns lost to the Trail Blazers in the first round of the Play-In and are desperate. The Warriors, coming off one of the postseason's signature individual performances, have all the momentum. If Curry plays Friday the way he played Wednesday, the Suns should be very worried — and so should whoever the Warriors face in the first round.
