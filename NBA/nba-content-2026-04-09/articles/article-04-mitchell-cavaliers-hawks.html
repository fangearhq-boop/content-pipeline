Donovan Mitchell Scores 31 as Cavaliers Top Hawks 122-116, Atlanta Misses Playoff Clinch

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 9, 2026

Donovan Mitchell had 31 points on 12-of-19 shooting Wednesday night, and Cleveland needed every one of them. The Cavaliers escaped Atlanta 122-116 in a game that carried real stakes — the Hawks had a chance to clinch a playoff berth and couldn't get it done.

Mitchell has been Cleveland's engine all season, and this performance was a reminder of why. He scored at a high rate, didn't force the issue when the defense clamped down, and kept the Cavaliers in front when the Hawks made their runs. This is what a go-to scorer looks like in crunch time.

Atlanta's Missed Opportunity

The Atlanta Hawks entered Wednesday with a chance to officially clinch their playoff spot and put the stress of the final week behind them. Instead, Mitchell and the Cavaliers had other plans.

Atlanta's inability to close out the win keeps their postseason positioning live — and uncertain — heading into the final days of the regular season. The East's lower bracket has been one of the more compelling races of the second half, with multiple teams bunched together and every game carrying outsized consequences.

The Hawks will have another shot before the season ends April 12. But missing Wednesday's clinching opportunity adds pressure to each remaining game on their schedule. One loss, and the drama continues.

Mitchell and Cleveland's Postseason Readiness

For Cleveland, the win was about more than seeding. It was a quality performance against a team fighting hard, with Mitchell showing the kind of individual scoring ability that playoff opponents will need to account for. On 12-of-19 shooting, he was efficient without being reckless — the ideal combination for a team preparing to make a deep postseason run.

The Cavaliers have been steady all season, and Mitchell has been their consistent bright spot. As the playoffs approach, his ability to take over a game when it matters most could be the difference between an early exit and a long run.

What's Next

The regular season ends April 12. Atlanta still has games to play and a playoff spot to secure. Cleveland, meanwhile, has a clear picture of its seeding and will focus on staying healthy and sharp entering the postseason.

The East's bracket will look different in a few days than it does today. For the Hawks, Wednesday was a missed chance — but not the last one. For the Cavaliers and Mitchell, it was another reminder that this team knows how to win games that count.
