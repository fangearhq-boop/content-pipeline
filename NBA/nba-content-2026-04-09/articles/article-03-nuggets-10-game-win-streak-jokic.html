Nuggets Win 10th Straight as Jokic Posts Triple-Double, Murray Scores 26

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 9, 2026

The story of the Denver Nuggets' season will need a second act. Because this late-season run — 10 consecutive wins, a 136-119 beatdown of the Memphis Grizzlies on Wednesday, and a 1.5-game cushion in the race for the West's No. 3 seed — looks nothing like the team that was fighting for its playoff life a month ago.

Nikola Jokic posted another triple-double: 14 points, 15 rebounds, and 10 assists. Jamal Murray led Denver with 26 points. The Nuggets were in command from start to finish, and their winning streak has quietly become one of the most significant storylines of the final week of the regular season.

Ten in a Row

Ten consecutive wins in the NBA isn't an accident. It requires a combination of health, execution, and the kind of internal chemistry that doesn't come from a front office meeting — it comes from surviving hard games and building trust in one another. Denver has both.

During the streak, the Nuggets have been led by the version of Nikola Jokic that has won him multiple MVP awards — not dominating with points, but controlling games through rebounding and passing in a way that no other player in the league can replicate. His 15 rebounds and 10 assists against Memphis were vintage: he didn't need to score 30 because he created so many good looks for teammates that the Grizzlies couldn't account for everyone.

Murray, meanwhile, has been a force. His 26 points against Memphis showed the All-Star guard is fully healthy and operating as the perfect complement to Jokic's orchestration. When Murray is scoring and Jokic is distributing, Denver is extraordinarily difficult to defend.

The No. 3 Seed and What It's Worth

Denver sits 1.5 games ahead of both the Los Angeles Lakers and Houston Rockets in the race for the No. 3 Western Conference seed. That gap sounds small, but with only three games remaining and Denver having won 10 straight, the Nuggets control their own fate.

The No. 3 seed isn't just a number on a bracket. It means Denver avoids the Oklahoma City Thunder — the defending champion and No. 1 overall seed — until at least the Western Conference Finals. A team seeded No. 3 faces the No. 6 in the first round, then theoretically meets the No. 2 San Antonio Spurs in the second round. That's a navigable path to the conference finals for a team this hot.

Compare that to finishing at No. 5 or No. 6, where a potential first-round date with OKC looms. The seeding race matters enormously for Denver's championship outlook, and right now they're in the driver's seat.

What's Next

Three games remain for Denver in the regular season. If the Nuggets keep winning, the No. 3 seed is essentially locked. The more intriguing question is whether this momentum translates into the playoffs — a team that has won 10 straight entering the postseason is carrying real psychological momentum alongside its statistical hot streak.

The regular season ends April 12. The playoffs begin April 18. Denver, having spent portions of this season looking like a team in decline, now enters the postseason looking like a threat. The late-season surge is real, the stars are healthy, and the Nuggets have remembered what they look like when everything clicks.

Whoever draws Denver in the first round has a problem.
