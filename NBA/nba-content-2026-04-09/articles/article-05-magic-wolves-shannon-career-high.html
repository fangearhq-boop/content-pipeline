Magic Win Fourth Straight 132-120 Over Wolves; Shannon Jr. Posts Career-High 33 in Loss

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 9, 2026

Terrence Shannon Jr. gave everything he had Wednesday night. Thirty-three points — a new career high — for the Minnesota Timberwolves against a surging Orlando Magic team. It was an outstanding individual performance. It just wasn't enough.

The Orlando Magic won 132-120 for their fourth consecutive victory, with Paolo Banchero leading the way with 20 points and 8 rebounds and Desmond Bane contributing 18 points and 6 assists. Orlando's win streak is no longer a quiet story — four straight wins heading into the final days of the regular season will get noticed.

Shannon Jr.'s Breakout Night

For Minnesota, the lasting image from Wednesday is Shannon Jr. putting up a career-high performance on a night when the Wolves needed a lift. He scored 33 points and looked every bit capable of being a meaningful contributor when given minutes and opportunities.

Career-high nights like this carry real weight for young players. Shannon Jr. will remember this game — and so will Minnesota's coaching staff when they're building their postseason rotation. Teams need more than seven or eight reliable players in a deep playoff run, and Wednesday showed that Shannon Jr. can be part of that fabric.

The Timberwolves still lost, and that's the painful part. But individual development moments don't stop being meaningful just because the team came up short.

Orlando's Quiet Surge

The Orlando Magic don't get the same national attention as the Celtics, Pistons, or Cavaliers when discussing Eastern Conference contenders. Maybe they should. Four consecutive wins, a Banchero-led offense that's clicking, and a Bane pickup that has added perimeter scoring and playmaking — this is a team that is difficult to match up with.

Banchero at 20 points and 8 rebounds was in command. Bane's 18 points and 6 assists showed how the trade has elevated Orlando's offensive versatility. This team can hurt you from multiple spots, and they're gaining confidence heading into the postseason.

Whoever faces Orlando in the first round should not take them lightly. Four straight wins heading into the playoffs is the kind of form that carries over.

What's Next

The regular season concludes April 12. The Magic will look to close out the final week in strong form, while Minnesota will try to shake off a tough loss and refocus before the playoffs begin April 18.

For Shannon Jr., the goal is simpler: build on Wednesday. A career-high 33 is a launching pad, not an endpoint. For Orlando, the goal is maintaining the energy and execution they've shown through four straight wins — and carrying it into a postseason where they could make some serious noise.
