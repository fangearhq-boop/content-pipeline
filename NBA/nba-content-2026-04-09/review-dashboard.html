### Long-Form Post Window: 9:00 AM ET The Oklahoma City Thunder are officially the team to beat. On Wednesday night, Chet Holmgren delivered one of the most complete performances of his career — 30 points, 14 rebounds, 5 assists, 2 steals, and 4 blocks while shooting 10-of-13 from the field and 3-of-4 from three. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander added 20 points and 11 assists. Jalen Williams contributed 18. The Thunder handled the LA Clippers 128-110, and in doing so, clinched the NBA's best regular-season record at 64-16. That means OKC, the defending NBA champion, has homecourt advantage locked in throughout the entire postseason. The San Antonio Spurs had pushed to within two games at 61-19 with an 18-2 run since February, but Wednesday's win put it out of reach. The No. 1 seed, and with it the right to host every playoff series, belongs to Oklahoma City. What made Wednesday's win notable wasn't just the clinching — it was how dominant it looked. The Thunder shot 58.1% from the field and held the Clippers to zero fast-break field goals. Oklahoma City forced 13 turnovers (nine off steals) and scored 23 points off those miscues. This is a team playing at a championship level. Holmgren, in particular, looked like the second-best player on the planet Wednesday night. After SGA, obviously. The big man is putting up the kind of two-way production that makes OKC a nightmare matchup for anyone. --- ### Image Post Caption Window: 9:30 AM ET Chet Holmgren. 30 points. 14 rebounds. 4 blocks.
