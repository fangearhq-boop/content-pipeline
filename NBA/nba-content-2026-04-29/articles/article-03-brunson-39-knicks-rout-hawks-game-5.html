Brunson Drops 39 as Knicks Rout Hawks 126-97, Take 3-2 Series Lead

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 29, 2026

Jalen Brunson is playing playoff basketball at a different level right now, and Tuesday night the New York Knicks made it impossible to look away. Brunson scored 39 points, the Knicks shot 58.5 percent in the first half, and New York routed the Atlanta Hawks 126-97 at Madison Square Garden to take a 3-2 series lead. Game 6 is Thursday in Atlanta, and the Knicks can advance to the second round with a win.

This was not a tense, back-and-forth game. The Knicks made eight of their first twelve shots and orchestrated a 9-0 run late in the first quarter that essentially decided things early. New York led 35-22 after the first period and 64-48 at halftime. Atlanta spent the second half trying to manage the deficit rather than challenge it, and the Knicks simply kept pouring it on.

Brunson's 39 points were a reminder that when he is completely locked in, he is one of the most efficient playoff scorers in the entire league. He attacked mismatches relentlessly, got to his spots without hesitation, and converted at a high rate from every zone on the floor. The Garden crowd fed off his energy and the Knicks' shooting advantage created open looks for teammates all night long.

The Knicks as a Contender

There have been fair questions about whether New York has the depth to compete with the East's top teams. After Tuesday night's performance, those questions feel less pressing. The Knicks as a unit were exceptional — every starter contributed, the spacing was crisp, and the defense held Atlanta to a manageable total despite their own offensive explosion.

Atlanta fought hard in Games 3 and 4 to tie the series at 2-2, and credit is due for their resilience. But Game 5 exposed the gap between these two teams when the Knicks are operating at full efficiency. When Brunson is scoring at will and the team is shooting above 55 percent, New York becomes a very difficult opponent for anyone in this conference.

What's Next

Game 6 is Thursday night at State Farm Arena in Atlanta. The Hawks will be desperate in front of a home crowd that will pack the building and push the noise level to the rafters. Atlanta has shown it can win this series — they tied it at 2-2 — so writing them off entirely would be premature. But the Knicks head south with momentum, a confident Brunson, and a 3-2 series advantage that gives them room to absorb a road loss if necessary before a potential decisive Game 7.

New York is playing like a team that genuinely believes it can win a championship. If Brunson stays locked in and the team keeps shooting like this, the Knicks could be celebrating a Round 2 berth by Thursday night.
