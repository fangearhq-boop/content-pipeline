Embiid's 33-Point Second-Half Takeover Stuns Celtics, Keeps Sixers Alive

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 29, 2026

TD Garden thought it was over. With 7:42 left in the third quarter, the Boston Celtics led the Philadelphia 76ers by 13 points, and the crowd was loud enough to rattle the rafters. A series-ending 4-1 lead felt inevitable. Instead, Joel Embiid had other ideas — and the 76ers delivered one of the most stunning second-half performances of the 2026 NBA Playoffs.

Philadelphia won Game 5 on Tuesday night, 113-97, and the Celtics' series lead has been trimmed to 3-2. Game 6 is Thursday at the Xfinity Mobile Arena in South Philadelphia, where the Sixers will have home-court advantage and the weight of a comeback story behind them.

Embiid finished with 33 points and 8 assists, scoring 18 of those points after halftime. He attacked the Celtics' defense relentlessly in the fourth quarter, drawing fouls and finishing at the rim while Philadelphia outscored Boston 28-11 in the final frame. Jayson Tatum had 24 points and 16 rebounds for the Celtics, and Jaylen Brown added 22 — production that would have been enough to win in almost any other game. It simply wasn't enough against a Sixers team that refused to go home.

A Collective Effort Built Around Embiid

What made Philadelphia's comeback so convincing was not just Embiid — it was the ensemble performance around him. Tyrese Maxey delivered 25 points and 10 rebounds, providing the kind of complementary brilliance that gives the Sixers a legitimate second star in these moments. Paul George added 16 points, steady as ever. Quentin Grimes came off the bench and scored 18 points, giving the Sixers the depth they needed to weather the Celtics' push.

Boston's collapse in the fourth quarter was jarring. The Celtics had led comfortably and appeared to be managing the game efficiently. But when Embiid found his rhythm, Philadelphia's offense became impossible to corral. The Sixers shot efficiently down the stretch and made the Celtics pay for every defensive miscommunication. When Boston waved the white flag with 2:16 remaining — holding a 109-95 deficit — it felt like a different team than the one that had been in control all night.

What's Next

Game 6 arrives Thursday evening in Philadelphia, and the atmosphere at the Xfinity Mobile Arena will be unlike anything the Sixers have produced this postseason. The crowd will be desperate, the energy will be deafening, and Embiid will be locked in. Boston still holds the series lead and needs one more win to advance, but they will need to find answers for the Sixers' depth and Embiid's second-half surges.

For the 76ers, this is the kind of victory that defines playoff runs. Philadelphia kept its season alive at the hardest possible venue. If they can carry this momentum into Game 6 and even the series, a decisive Game 7 back in Boston becomes a genuine opportunity. The Sixers are not done yet — and Embiid made that perfectly clear Tuesday night.
