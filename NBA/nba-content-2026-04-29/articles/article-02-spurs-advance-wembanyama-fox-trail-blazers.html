Spurs Advance — Wembanyama and Fox Dominate Trail Blazers in Game 5 Closeout

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

April 29, 2026

The San Antonio Spurs are moving on, and they are sending a warning to the rest of the Western Conference. Victor Wembanyama posted 17 points, 14 rebounds, and 6 blocks. De'Aaron Fox scored 21 points. The Spurs led the Portland Trail Blazers by as many as 28 points in a 114-95 Game 5 clinching win Tuesday night that was far more authoritative than the final score suggests.

San Antonio wins the first-round series 4-1 and advances to the Western Conference Semifinals, where they will face the Oklahoma City Thunder — the defending champions who swept Phoenix in four games and have already been resting and preparing. The matchup is set. The anticipation is building.

But first, appreciation: this Spurs team has earned every bit of the attention they're getting. Wembanyama's line — 17-14-6 — understates just how disruptive he was on defense. He altered shots he didn't block. He forced Portland's guards to think twice before driving. He made the Blazers uncomfortable at every level of the floor, which opened up Fox and the San Antonio perimeter attack to do damage without resistance.

Portland Had No Answer

The Trail Blazers were competitive in this series — they stole Game 2 and pushed the Spurs in moments — but they were ultimately overmatched. Portland's young roster has real pieces, but they weren't ready to compete with a San Antonio team that has both a generational talent and veteran-savvy contributors. The 28-point margin at one stage of Tuesday's game reflected the size of that gap on this particular night.

Fox, acquired this past offseason to give the Spurs exactly this kind of two-way veteran presence, was everything they needed him to be throughout the series. He scored efficiently, facilitated the offense when Wembanyama attracted double-teams, and provided the kind of clutch decision-making that playoff basketball demands. The partnership between Fox and Wembanyama is beginning to look genuinely dangerous over a seven-game stretch.

What's Next

The Western Conference Semifinals matchup between the No. 1 Thunder and No. 2 Spurs is one of the most compelling second-round series anywhere in the 2026 playoffs. Oklahoma City swept Arizona in four and is rested and battle-tested. San Antonio won in five and has momentum. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander against Wembanyama — two of the five best players in the NBA on opposite sidelines — is a marquee matchup that will draw national attention.

The Spurs are young, fearless, and well-coached. They are not intimidated by the Thunder's pedigree. In San Antonio, there is a belief that this team is built to compete for a championship right now — not in two or three years, but this spring. After Game 5's dominant performance, it is very hard to argue with that.
