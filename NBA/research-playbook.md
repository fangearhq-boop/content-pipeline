# Hoop Heroes — Research Playbook

How to research, tier, and fact-check NBA stories for daily content.

## Research Categories

Run **8-10 web searches** across these categories before writing content.

| # | Category | Example Search Queries |
|---|----------|----------------------|
| 1 | Scores & Results | "NBA scores last night", "NBA box scores [date]", "[team] game score" |
| 2 | Standings & Playoff Race | "NBA standings today", "NBA playoff picture", "NBA play-in race", "[conference] seed clinch scenarios" |
| 3 | Individual Performances | "NBA triple-double last night", "NBA career high tonight", "50 point game NBA [date]", "NBA player of the week" |
| 4 | Trades & Transactions | "NBA trade rumors today", "NBA trade deadline [year]", "NBA waiver claims", "NBA 10-day contract signings" |
| 5 | Injury Updates | "NBA injury report today", "NBA injuries [date]", "[player] injury update", "[team] injury report" |
| 6 | Coaching & Front Office | "NBA coaching change", "NBA coach fired", "NBA front office moves", "[team] coaching search" |
| 7 | Rivalry & Storyline Watch | "NBA rivalry games this week", "[team] vs [team] history", "NBA revenge game", "NBA grudge match" |
| 8 | Preview & Lookahead | "NBA games tonight", "NBA schedule this weekend", "NBA marquee matchups", "NBA nationally televised games" |
| 9 | Unusual/Milestone Stats | "NBA unusual stat last night", "NBA all-time record [date]", "NBA milestone", "NBA streak record", "[player] passes [legend] career" |

### Category 9: Unusual & Milestone Stats (IMPORTANT)

Run 1-2 dedicated searches for genuinely interesting stats from the most recent games. These are the kind of stats that make fans stop scrolling — things like:

- A player passing someone on an all-time leaderboard (e.g., Jokic joins Wilt and Oscar as the only players to average a triple-double)
- A crazy stat line that's historically rare (e.g., "first player with 40/15/10 since 1975")
- A weird team streak or record (e.g., "The Nuggets have won 10 straight Thursday games")
- A player doing something for the first time in franchise or league history

**Rules for stat posts:**
- The stat MUST be from the team's or player's most recent game — not from weeks ago
- It MUST be 100% factually verified from a primary source (Basketball Reference, NBA.com/stats, official box score)
- It must be genuinely interesting — the "would a fan share this?" test. If it's not remarkable, skip it.
- Do NOT force a stat post if nothing interesting exists. An empty slot is better than a boring stat.
- These make great Tier 2-3 standalone posts or can be woven into game recaps as bonus content

## Story Tiering

### Tier 1 — Lead Stories (top time slots)

Must-cover content that anchors the day. Gets the 9:00 AM or 11:00 AM slot.

| Story Type | Why Tier 1 |
|-----------|-----------|
| Playoff clinch or elimination | Win-or-go-home and clinch scenarios are the biggest stories in basketball |
| Major trades | Star player trades reshape the league — always lead-worthy |
| Star player injuries (major) | ACL, Achilles, or season-ending injuries to stars change the playoff picture |
| Buzzer beaters and OT thrillers | Dramatic finishes are the most shareable content in sports |
| 50+ point games | Elite individual performances that define a season |
| Triple-doubles by star players | Especially milestone triple-doubles (career record, most in a month, etc.) |
| Trade deadline day deals | Every deadline-day trade is Tier 1 on that day |
| NBA Finals / Conference Finals results | Postseason results are always the top story |

### Tier 2 — Developing Stories (mid-day slots)

Strong content that fills the 1:00 PM or 4:00 PM slots.

| Story Type | Why Tier 2 |
|-----------|-----------|
| Regular season results (contenders) | Games between top-6 seeds or playoff implications always matter |
| Notable individual performances | 40+ points, 20+ rebounds, 15+ assists, near triple-doubles |
| Standings movement | Team clinches playoff spot, drops out of play-in, takes over a seed |
| Coaching news | Coach of the Month, mid-season firings, extension announcements |
| Trade rumors (credible) | Reports from Woj/Shams with real substance, not speculation |
| All-Star selections and snubs | Annual debate content |
| Regular triple-doubles | Strong but not historic unless context makes it bigger |

### Tier 3 — Supporting Content (later slots)

Fills out the schedule. Gets the 7:00 PM or 9:30 PM slot (pre-game or late recap).

| Story Type | Why Tier 3 |
|-----------|-----------|
| Tonight's game previews | Lookahead at marquee matchups and nationally televised games |
| Evergreen analysis | "Best defensive teams in the league" type features |
| Stat deep dives | Advanced stats breakdowns, historical comparisons |
| Milestone watch | Player approaching but not yet reaching a record |
| Power rankings | Weekly or mid-season power rankings content |
| Historical comparisons | "Best sophomore seasons ever" type content |

## Posting Priority

This order is non-negotiable:

1. **Last night's scores and results** — always first. A game recap after tonight's games start is useless.
2. **Today's game previews** — marquee matchups, rivalry games, or elimination games happening tonight.
3. **Breaking news** — trades, major injuries, coaching changes.
4. **Everything else** — analysis, evergreen, previews for future days.

If multiple game results exist, order by tier: buzzer beaters/OT first, then upsets, then star performances, then contender results.

## Fact-Check Requirements

Every post must be verified before publishing. Priority order:

### Priority 1 — Scores and Box Scores

| Check | How to Verify |
|-------|--------------|
| Final score | Cross-reference ESPN box score AND NBA.com game summary |
| Overtime | Verify if game went to OT — never assume regulation |
| Winner stated first | Score format: "Celtics 112, Lakers 105" — winner always first |
| Stat line accuracy | Verify points/rebounds/assists from official box score, not social media |

### Priority 2 — Standings and Records

| Check | How to Verify |
|-------|--------------|
| Current standings | Verify against NBA.com/standings — standings change nightly |
| Seed numbers | Confirm current seed, not projected or previous |
| Win/loss records | Cross-reference NBA.com — records change daily during season |
| Streak data | Count manually from schedule/results — don't trust secondary sources |
| Clinch scenarios | Verify against NBA.com or ESPN playoff clinch tracker |

### Priority 3 — Player Stats and Contracts

| Check | How to Verify |
|-------|--------------|
| Season averages | Verify against Basketball Reference or NBA.com/stats — averages change nightly |
| Career stats/milestones | Basketball Reference player page — the gold standard |
| Contract values | Spotrac for exact figures — never round or estimate |
| Player ages | Look up birth date, then calculate — never trust a secondary source's stated age |
| Advanced stats (PER, TS%, etc.) | Basketball Reference or Cleaning the Glass — specify the source |

### Priority 4 — Schedule and Logistics

| Check | How to Verify |
|-------|--------------|
| Game times | Verify against NBA.com schedule — times change for national TV |
| Day of week | Cross-reference the calendar — "Saturday's game" must actually be Saturday |
| Arena/location | Confirm from schedule — some games move venues |
| TV/streaming info | Check NBA.com or ESPN schedule — broadcasts get added/changed late |
| All times in ET | Convert if sourcing from local team sites that use local time |

## Market Bias Check

Before finalizing any day's content, verify coverage breadth:

- Do not default to Lakers/Celtics/Warriors/Knicks every day just because they drive engagement
- Small-market contenders (OKC, Denver, Milwaukee, Sacramento, etc.) produce Tier 1 stories too
- If 4+ posts feature the same team or conference, look harder at the other side
- Small-market upsets over big-market teams are always worth covering
- Balance East and West conference coverage

## Key Dates Reminder

- **Trade Deadline**: Mid-February (exact date announced each season)
- **All-Star Weekend**: Third weekend of February
- **Play-In Tournament**: Mid-April (after regular season ends)
- **Playoffs Begin**: Late April
- **NBA Finals**: June
- **Draft**: Late June
- **Free Agency Opens**: July 1 (negotiation moratorium), July 6 (signings official)
