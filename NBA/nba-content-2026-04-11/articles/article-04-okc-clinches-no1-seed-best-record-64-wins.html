Oklahoma City Thunder Clinch No. 1 Seed, Best Record in NBA with 64th Win

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 11, 2026

The Oklahoma City Thunder have the best record in basketball. It is no longer a projection or a probability — it is a fact. With their 64th win of the season Friday over the Los Angeles Clippers, OKC officially clinched the No. 1 seed in the Western Conference and the top mark in the entire NBA, setting up a home-court advantage that runs through the entire playoff bracket.

The Thunder's 64-16 record is the franchise's best since the Kevin Durant era, a remarkable achievement for a young team that has ascended faster than almost anyone in the league predicted. And at the center of it all — as he has been all season — is Shai Gilgeous-Alexander.

SGA's Historic Season

Gilgeous-Alexander's 2025-26 campaign will be remembered as one of the great individual seasons in recent NBA history. Averaging 31.1 points, 6.6 assists, and 4.3 rebounds, he has been the most complete offensive player in the league from wire to wire. His second consecutive MVP award is essentially a formality at this point — ESPN's voter panel gave him 88 of 100 first-place votes, and he sits at -400 at DraftKings, as close to a lock as these things get.

The last OKC player to win back-to-back MVP awards was Kevin Durant, who captured the honor in 2013-14 with his legendary "You are the real MVP" speech to his mother. What SGA is doing belongs in that same conversation — and this Thunder team, unlike Durant's, appears built to sustain this level for years.

OKC's Postseason Path

With the No. 1 seed comes home-court advantage through every round of the Western Conference playoffs. The Thunder will not have to play a true road game until the NBA Finals, assuming they get there. Their first-round opponent will be determined after the Play-In Tournament (April 14-17), when the No. 7 and No. 8 seeds in the West are finalized.

Oklahoma City has shown all year that they can beat anyone, anywhere. The question entering the postseason is not whether they are good enough — it is whether the weight of expectations can rattle a roster that has handled pressure with remarkable composure all season long.

What's Next

The Oklahoma City Thunder play the final game of their regular season Sunday, April 12. Their seeding and home-court status are locked. The SoFi NBA Play-In Tournament runs April 14-17 on Prime Video. The NBA Playoffs tip off April 18, with OKC hosting the No. 8 seed from the West in the first round. The Thunder are the Western Conference favorites, and SGA's MVP coronation is imminent. The real work — the part that matters most — begins in less than two weeks.
