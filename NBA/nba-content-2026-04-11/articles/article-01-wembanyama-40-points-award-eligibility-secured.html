Wembanyama Plays Through Rib Injury, Scores 40, Secures Award Eligibility

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

April 11, 2026

Victor Wembanyama had every reason to stay on the bench Friday night. His ribs ached. A 65th regular-season game was at stake — the threshold needed to remain eligible for MVP, Defensive Player of the Year, and All-NBA awards. The easy decision would have been to rest, protect the body, and let the awards chips fall where they may.

Wembanyama played anyway. And he scored 40 points in 26 minutes to make absolutely sure the decision mattered.

The San Antonio Spurs center appeared in his 65th game of the 2025-26 season Friday in a win over the Dallas Mavericks, ending the suspense over whether he would meet the NBA's minimum games requirement for end-of-season award eligibility. He needed 20 minutes of action in the game to qualify — and he delivered far more than that, reaching 40 points in a performance that had Spurs fans exhaling one of the deepest collective sighs of relief in recent memory.

A Candid Admission

Postgame, Wembanyama offered a revealing glimpse into his mindset. "If I had had 65 [games] before, I would, for sure, not have played," he said. The honesty was striking. Wembanyama did not pretend the awards were irrelevant — but he also made clear that health and team needs came first. Friday's situation put both values into alignment: San Antonio needed him, the eligibility threshold demanded him, and he answered.

The rib contusion that threatened all of this came April 6, when Philadelphia 76ers forward Paul George fouled Wembanyama hard in the second quarter of San Antonio's 115-102 win. Wembanyama finished the first half but did not return. He was listed as day-to-day for the Spurs' remaining games, leaving his 65th appearance in genuine doubt.

What the Eligibility Means

With 65 games now on the ledger, Wembanyama retains his candidacy for multiple awards. While the MVP race belongs to Shai Gilgeous-Alexander — who has received 88 of 100 first-place votes in ESPN's straw poll and sits at -400 on DraftKings — Wembanyama's presence in the DPOY and All-NBA discussions is fully intact.

His numbers this season — 24.9 points, 11.6 rebounds, and the league's top mark in blocks — make him one of the most complete players in the game. A DPOY award would be a historic acknowledgment of his defensive brilliance and a fitting capstone to a season that, even with the injury hiccup, was remarkable by any measure.

What's Next

The San Antonio Spurs enter the 2026 NBA Playoffs as the No. 2 seed in the Western Conference. Their first-round matchup will be determined after the final day of the regular season Sunday, April 12. The NBA Play-In Tournament runs April 14-17, with the playoffs tipping off April 18. A healthy Wembanyama gives San Antonio one of the most dangerous players in the postseason — and after Friday's performance through pain, the rest of the West has reason to take notice.
