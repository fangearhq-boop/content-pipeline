Knicks Clinch No. 3 East Seed with 112-95 Win Over Raptors

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

April 11, 2026

The New York Knicks are officially locked in. A 112-95 victory over the Toronto Raptors on Friday sealed the No. 3 seed in the Eastern Conference for New York, delivering home-court advantage in the first round of the playoffs and completing a week in which the Knicks turned late-season pressure into late-season clarity.

At 53-28, the Knicks finish one game ahead of the Cleveland Cavaliers and hold the head-to-head tiebreaker that made the math clean. After Thursday's hard-fought win over the Boston Celtics — a game that put New York in the driver's seat — Friday's result against a Raptors team that still has something to play for on Sunday was a statement of consistency. The Knicks handled business when the spotlight was on.

What the No. 3 Seed Means

The difference between the No. 3 and No. 4 seeds in the Eastern Conference is more than just optics. The No. 3 seed earns home-court advantage in the first round, meaning the Knicks will open the playoffs at Madison Square Garden — one of the most intimidating arenas in professional basketball when the crowd is fully engaged. It also shapes the projected bracket path, giving New York a more favorable first-round matchup.

Toronto, despite the loss, is not done. The Raptors fall to No. 6 with the defeat but remain in the driver's seat for a guaranteed playoff spot — they host the Brooklyn Nets on Sunday and will be heavy favorites to protect that position. A win for Toronto on Sunday keeps them directly in the bracket and out of the play-in tournament.

The Engine Behind the Knicks' Run

The story of the Knicks' late-season push has been Jalen Brunson's steadiness and Josh Hart's energy. Brunson has been the team's primary ball-handler and scoring anchor, delivering big moments when the team needed them most. Hart's explosive fourth-quarter performance against the Celtics on Thursday — 15 of his 26 points in the final frame — was the kind of momentum swing that defines playoff contenders. The Knicks don't have one dominant star; they have a roster full of players who understand their roles and execute under pressure.

That is a formula that can go deep in May and June. Madison Square Garden is going to be deafening when the playoffs begin.

What's Next

The New York Knicks play their regular-season finale Sunday, April 12, though their seeding is now set. The NBA Play-In Tournament runs April 14-17, determining the seventh and eighth seeds in each conference. The first round of the NBA Playoffs tips off April 18, with the Knicks hosting Game 1 in New York. The bracket on the East side will come fully into focus after Sunday's final results. One thing is certain: the Knicks will be a tough out for any opponent at Madison Square Garden.
