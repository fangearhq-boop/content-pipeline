Detroit Pistons Clinch No. 1 East Seed for First Time Since 2007 — The Greatest NBA Turnaround in Recent Memory

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 5, 2026

The Detroit Pistons are the No. 1 seed in the Eastern Conference. Let that sit for a moment.

Detroit beat the Philadelphia 76ers 116-93 on Friday night, clinching the top seed — and home-court advantage throughout the Eastern Conference playoffs — for the first time since the 2006-07 season. With 57 wins and four games remaining, the Pistons have completed one of the most stunning organizational turnarounds in modern professional sports.

Where They Were Two Years Ago

Context is everything with this Pistons team. Two seasons ago, Detroit went 14-68 — one of the worst records in the league. They lost 60 or more games in back-to-back seasons. There was no obvious path forward, no franchise cornerstone in place, and no sense that a team this far removed from competitiveness could rebuild in any reasonable timeframe.

And yet. Here they are. Fifty-seven wins. Top seed. Best record in the Eastern Conference.

How They Did It

The Pistons' ascent has been built on development, depth, and defensive identity. The front office made calculated decisions during their rebuilding years — prioritizing draft capital, developing young players with patience, and assembling a coaching staff that could extract performance from a roster not yet built around any single superstar.

The result is a team that wins in multiple ways. They can grind opponents in half-court sets. They can run in transition. They have wing depth that most teams can't match, and they have held opposing offenses below league-average efficiency on a consistent basis throughout the season.

The Historical Significance

The last time the Pistons held the No. 1 seed in the East was 2007 — a year in which they won 53 games and fell in the Eastern Conference Finals to the Cleveland Cavaliers. Nearly two decades later, they're back. And they have significantly more wins this time around.

With four games remaining, Detroit has a chance to add to their win total and push toward historic franchise territory. The 1988-89 championship team won 63 games. The 2005-06 team won 64. The current Pistons, at 57-21 with four to play, are within striking distance of joining those teams in the conversation.

What's Next

The real work begins April 18, when the NBA Playoffs tip off and the Pistons open at home against their first-round opponent. They will have Chase Center — excuse us, their own arena — rocking for every home game, and they enter the postseason as legitimate Eastern Conference title contenders.

Detroit has not won an NBA championship since 2004. This team has the talent, the depth, and now the top seed to make a run. The city is ready. The team is ready. Playoff basketball in Detroit is about to be very, very loud.
