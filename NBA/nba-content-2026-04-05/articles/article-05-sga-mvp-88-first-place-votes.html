SGA Is the MVP — 88 of 100 Media Voters Have Him No. 1, and the Numbers Back Them Up

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 5, 2026

The NBA MVP debate is over. In ESPN's annual media straw poll, Shai Gilgeous-Alexander received 88 of 100 first-place votes. Victor Wembanyama got 8. Nikola Jokic got the remaining 4. There is no debate.

What SGA has done this season — individually and as the engine of the Oklahoma City Thunder — has been the story of the 2025-26 NBA year. His numbers are elite. His team is elite. His impact on both ends of the floor is elite. At some point, the conversation stops being about who deserves it and starts being about how to properly appreciate what he's doing.

The Numbers

SGA is averaging 31.7 points and 6.6 assists per game, shooting 55.1 percent from the field and 38.3 percent from three. Those shooting percentages, on the volume he's attempting, are exceptional. He is not a volume scorer padding a stat line — he is an efficient, decisive offensive weapon who forces defenses to make impossible choices on every possession.

The Oklahoma City Thunder are 61-16 on the season. They have the best defensive rating in the NBA (106.2) and the best net rating in the NBA (+10.8). SGA is the centerpiece of a team that dominates on both ends of the floor — a combination that, historically, produces MVP winners.

The Competition

Wembanyama and Jokic both have legitimate statistical cases in other years. Wembanyama is averaging 24.7 points, 11.5 rebounds, and 3.1 blocks per game — the most blocks in the league — on a Spurs team that has won 27 of its last 29 games and is the No. 2 seed in the West. His two-way impact is historically rare.

Jokic is averaging 27.7 points, 13.0 rebounds, and 10.8 assists — on pace for one of the most statistically remarkable seasons any player has ever had. He is threatening territory that Russell Westbrook previously occupied in the record books, and he may become the first player ever to lead the league in both rebounds and assists in the same season.

In most years, either of them would be the favorite. This is not most years.

The Historical Context

An 88-of-100 first-place vote total in ESPN's straw poll is a landslide. It reflects the near-universal view among basketball media that SGA has been the best player on the best team in an era when both matter enormously to the award.

The Thunder's 61-16 record is the best in the NBA. Their defense has been suffocating all season. And their offense runs through a 26-year-old who has emerged as the league's most dominant two-way guard since the prime years of players his own generation grew up watching.

What's Next

The formal MVP announcement comes later this month, but the outcome has been clear for weeks. The playoff run begins April 18, and SGA will have the opportunity to add a championship run to his MVP season — which would cement his place among the game's all-time greats in one clean stroke. Oklahoma City is ready. So is he.
