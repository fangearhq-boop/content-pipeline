Steph Curry Returns Tonight After 27-Game Absence — Warriors Host Rockets in Play-In Warmup

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 5, 2026

Stephen Curry is back. After 27 games, nearly three months of rehabilitation, and what he described as "long, dark days," the Golden State Warriors' superstar is confirmed to play Sunday when the Warriors host the Houston Rockets at Chase Center.

It is Curry's first game since January 30, when he injured his right knee against the Detroit Pistons and was subsequently ruled out for an extended stretch. The return has been anticipated for weeks, and it arrives with the Warriors in a precarious spot: 36-41 overall, locked into the 10th seed in the Western Conference, and needing two Play-In wins just to reach the postseason for the first time without home-court advantage in years.

What the Warriors Looked Like Without Him

The numbers tell the story clearly. Golden State went 13-25 without Curry — a pace that, over a full season, would produce fewer than 25 wins. They were competitive in some stretches and outright outclassed in others, a reflection of how much the team's identity, spacing, and offensive rhythm depend on his presence.

Before the knee injury, Curry was averaging 27.2 points, 3.5 rebounds, and 4.8 assists while shooting 39.1 percent from three-point range. The Warriors looked like a team capable of making a playoff push. After January 30, that possibility evaporated quickly.

What He Changes

The most immediate impact of Curry's return is floor spacing. Golden State's offense became predictable and stagnant without him — opposing defenses could sag off shooters and dare others to beat them, a defensive adjustment that no one consistently punished. With Curry back, every set runs differently. The defense has to account for him at all times, and that opens the floor for everyone else.

The Play-In Tournament begins April 14, and the Warriors — as the 10th seed — would host the 9th seed in the first game, needing to win to advance to a second game, then needing to win that game to reach the postseason. It is a two-win requirement from a standing start. With Curry healthy, it is achievable. Without him, it likely was not.

Curry's Perspective

Curry has spoken candidly about the difficulty of the rehab process. He acknowledged the mental toll of extended absences at this stage of his career, describing days of uncertainty and frustration. He kept those moments largely private, but his acknowledgment of "long, dark days" offered a window into how seriously he takes each opportunity to compete.

At 37 years old, Curry is not playing for legacy at this point — the legacy is established. He is playing because he wants to compete at the highest level for as long as his body allows. Tonight is the next chapter in that story.

What's Next

The Warriors have a handful of regular-season games remaining before the Play-In Tournament begins. Tonight's game against Houston is Curry's chance to shake off the rust, find his rhythm, and remind everyone — himself included — that he still belongs among the game's elite. The Chase Center crowd will be ready. The question now is how sharp Curry looks after nearly three months away.
