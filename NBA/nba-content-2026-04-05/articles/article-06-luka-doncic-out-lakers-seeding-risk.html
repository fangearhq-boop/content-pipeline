Luka Doncic Out for Rest of Regular Season — Lakers Need to Go 5-0 to Protect No. 3 Seed

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 5, 2026

The Los Angeles Lakers are in a bind. Luka Doncic is officially out for the remainder of the regular season with a Grade 2 hamstring injury, and Austin Reaves remains sidelined as well. With a handful of games remaining, the Lakers need a perfect run just to hold onto the No. 3 seed in the Western Conference.

The injury to Doncic comes at the worst possible time. He had been one of the best players in the NBA this season, forming a terrifying combination with LeBron James that made the Lakers look like genuine title contenders. Without him for the final stretch, the team enters an uncertain stretch before the postseason begins.

What a 5-0 Run Requires

The math is unforgiving. The Lakers must win their remaining games without two of their most important players to protect the No. 3 seed. Slipping to a lower seed in the Western Conference has significant implications for the playoff bracket — a lower position likely means a harder first-round matchup and potentially no home games until deeper in the postseason.

LeBron James, D'Angelo Russell, and the rest of the Lakers' supporting cast will need to step up in a major way to close out the regular season at full effort. LeBron, at 41 years old, is still one of the most capable players on the floor — but running a contender short-handed through the final week of the regular season is not an ideal preparation for a playoff run.

The Injury and What Comes After

A Grade 2 hamstring strain is a soft-tissue injury that typically requires two to four weeks of recovery, though the timeline varies considerably by individual. The Lakers' medical staff will manage Doncic's recovery aggressively in the hope that he returns fully healthy for the playoffs, which begin April 18.

The best-case scenario for Los Angeles is that Doncic rests fully during the final regular-season games, recovers on schedule, and returns to the lineup healthy by the time the first-round series begins. Hamstring injuries, however, have a history of lingering — and returning to full explosiveness too quickly can risk re-injury or compromise performance at the most critical time of the year.

The Seeding Stakes

The No. 3 seed in the West currently puts the Lakers on a bracket path that could include a second-round matchup with either Oklahoma City or San Antonio — both formidable opponents. Slipping to No. 4 or lower opens the door to even more challenging bracket scenarios.

Western Conference seeding in 2026 matters enormously. The conference is deep, the margins are thin, and home-court advantage in a seven-game series is genuinely impactful. The Lakers are fighting to protect the best possible position — and they're doing it short-handed.

What's Next

The Lakers play out their final regular-season games without Doncic and Reaves, hoping the rest of the roster rises to the occasion. Then, if Doncic returns healthy in time for April 18, the conversation shifts entirely — a fully equipped Lakers team with Luka, LeBron, and a healthy supporting cast is a genuine championship threat. The regular-season stumble will be forgiven quickly if the playoffs go well. But first, they have to survive the next few days.
