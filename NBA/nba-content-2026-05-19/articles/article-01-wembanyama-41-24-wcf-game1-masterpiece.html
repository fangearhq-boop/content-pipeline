Wembanyama's 41-Point, 24-Rebound Night Sends Spurs Past Thunder in WCF Game 1 Classic

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 19, 2026

Victor Wembanyama entered Monday night's Western Conference Finals opener as the unanimous Defensive Player of the Year. He left Paycom Center as something more — a performer of Wilt Chamberlain–caliber dimensions, and the architect of one of the most remarkable playoff performances in NBA history.

Wembanyama posted 41 points, 24 rebounds, 3 assists and 3 blocks across 49 minutes of play as the San Antonio Spurs shocked the Oklahoma City Thunder 122-115 in double overtime to take a 1-0 lead in the Western Conference Finals. The 22-year-old Frenchman played every second of the fourth quarter, both overtime periods, and nearly the entire game — his minutes total the most of his NBA career — and never looked tired.

The Logo 3 That Changed Everything

With the Spurs trailing by one in the final minute of the first overtime period, Wembanyama caught the ball roughly 27 feet from the basket. No hesitation. He pulled up from logo range, launched, and watched the ball drop cleanly through the net with 26.3 seconds remaining. The building fell silent. San Antonio had a chance.

The Spurs held on to force a second overtime, and Wembanyama proved just as dominant in the extra session. With the game hanging in the balance, Dylan Harper found his teammate cutting toward the basket and threw a perfect lob. Wembanyama caught it above the rim, absorbed contact from Chet Holmgren, and finished the and-one slam that effectively iced the 122-115 victory.

Into the Company of Wilt

The final stat line — 41 points, 24 rebounds in a Conference Finals debut — placed Wembanyama alongside exactly one other player in NBA history. Only Wilt Chamberlain has ever recorded 40 or more points and 20 or more rebounds in his Conference Finals debut. Chamberlain did it in an era when the sport was a different animal. Wembanyama did it against a team that went 64-18 in the regular season and had gone 8-0 in the playoffs before Monday night.

The age context makes it more staggering: Wembanyama is 22 years and 134 days old. He won the DPOY award just weeks ago — unanimously, with all 100 first-place votes, another unprecedented achievement. Now he is rewriting the Conference Finals record book as well.

Harper's 7 Steals Set the Tone

In a game defined by Wembanyama's brilliance, it would be easy to overlook Dylan Harper's contribution — which is exactly why it deserves emphasis. Harper finished with 24 points and 7 steals, the latter total a Spurs team playoff record. His defensive activity disrupted Oklahoma City's ball movement throughout the night, generated fast-break opportunities at critical moments, and put constant pressure on SGA's rhythm in a way the Thunder clearly had not fully prepared for.

Harper also made the smart decision late in the second overtime, threading the pass to Wembanyama that led to the decisive and-one. The assist does not do justice to the read — Harper understood where his teammate was, where the defense was, and what would happen when those two things intersected.

OKC's Difficult Position

The Thunder entered Monday as heavy favorites. They had every statistical reason for confidence: back-to-back MVP in Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, a 64-18 regular season record, and an 8-0 playoff mark. They had swept both the Suns and the Los Angeles Lakers to reach this point. Their depth was unmatched. Their home crowd had not seen a loss since the playoffs began.

All of that is now behind them. Home court is gone in Game 1. Game 2 is tonight at 8:30 PM ET on NBC/Peacock — still in Oklahoma City — and San Antonio will arrive with the confidence of a team that just proved it can win anywhere against anyone.

What's Next

The Spurs have a narrow but real chance to put OKC in a devastating 0-2 hole tonight at Paycom Center. SGA remains one of basketball's most capable players when his back is against the wall, and the Thunder will not roll over. But the Spurs showed Monday what they're capable of, and Wembanyama gave every indication he can sustain this level of performance across a seven-game series.

The Western Conference Finals were expected to be a showcase for Shai Gilgeous-Alexander. Through one game, they have become something different entirely. WCF Game 2: May 20, 8:30 PM ET, Paycom Center, Oklahoma City, NBC/Peacock.
