Steve Kerr Signs New 2-Year Deal to Return as Warriors Head Coach for Year 13

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 2, 2026

The Golden State Warriors and Steve Kerr have reached an agreement on a new two-year contract, keeping the 60-year-old coach with the franchise into what will be his 13th season. The deal maintains Kerr's status as the highest-paid coach in the NBA. He earned $17.5 million in the final year of his previous contract.

The announcement came roughly three weeks after Kerr and the Warriors began substantive talks, with the signing made official on May 12, 2026.

The Season That Led Here

Golden State's 2025-26 season was difficult. The Warriors finished 37-45, plagued by injuries that limited their star players through stretches of the season. They entered the postseason as the No. 10 seed in the Western Conference and were eliminated by the Phoenix Suns in the play-in tournament.

It was not the ending anyone in the Bay Area wanted. At the beginning of training camp, Kerr had announced that he would not seek an extension, choosing instead to let the final year of his contract run out before deciding his future. That left the question genuinely open through the season.

How the Deal Got Done

According to reports, Kerr had multiple meetings with controlling owner Joe Lacob and general manager Mike Dunleavy over a three-week period. The conversations covered everything from offensive philosophy to long-term roster direction to the broader vision for the franchise.

After three weeks, Kerr signed. The choice to commit for two more years signals that both sides see a realistic path back to contention — not a farewell tour dressed up as a contract.

What It Means for Golden State

Kerr has been one of the most successful coaches in NBA history during his time with the Warriors, guiding the franchise to four championships. The question heading into Year 13 is whether the roster can be rebuilt into a genuine contender. That work is primarily the job of Dunleavy and the front office. Kerr's responsibility is to be ready when the pieces are in place.

Warriors fans who grew up watching the dynasty years — the dynasty Kerr helped build — have reason to believe he is not done yet.

What's Next

With the 2026 NBA Draft set for June 23-24 at Barclays Center in Brooklyn, and free agency following shortly after, the Warriors' offseason takes shape under a head coach who is now committed through at least 2028. What Golden State does with its draft position and cap space will determine whether Year 13 looks like a rebuild or a resurgence.
