KAT and OG's Three-Point Barrage: The Knicks' Hidden Weapon Against Wembanyama

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 2, 2026

The basketball world is focused on Jalen Brunson versus Victor Wembanyama. That matchup is real, and it will be decisive. But the deeper weapon the New York Knicks bring into the 2026 NBA Finals may be two players standing behind the arc — and the impossible choice they force on the best defender in the world.

Karl-Anthony Towns is shooting 48.9 percent from three-point range in the 2026 playoffs. That is a career best and one of the most efficient postseason shooting performances from range in recent memory. OG Anunoby is right beside him at 48.3 percent from three.

Together, they present a dilemma that Wembanyama's defensive architecture cannot solve cleanly.

Why This Creates a Real Problem for Wemby

Wembanyama's defensive system is built around paint protection. He sags off the action, reads passing lanes, and erases mistakes at the rim. It is the most effective rim-protection scheme in the league when deployed against typical five-out offenses, because most big men who anchor such schemes are not genuine three-point threats. If Wemby ignores them, the cost is acceptable.

Karl-Anthony Towns is not a typical big man. He is now starting at center in Tom Thibodeau's five-out lineup, which places a seven-footer with a 48.9 percent three-point conversion rate at the position Wembanyama is designed to protect. If Wemby sags to the paint to guard the drive, Towns is open from 25 feet with one of the hottest shooting streaks of any player in these playoffs.

If Wembanyama chooses to close out on Towns, Brunson has an open lane. If he stays in the paint, KAT has the shot. There is no correct answer — only a choice about which risk to accept.

OG Anunoby on Both Ends

Anunoby contributes from behind the arc at 48.3 percent, but his value in this series extends to the other side of the ball. Per defensive efficiency metrics, OG Anunoby has allowed the fewest points per 100 matchup possessions as a primary Wembanyama defender of any player in the league who has guarded him more than 100 half-court possessions this season.

That is a rare combination: a player who can shoot Wembanyama off the arc and guard him effectively in return. The Knicks did not build their roster by accident.

The Blueprint

New York's system asks Brunson to find the crack. Towns and Anunoby create the crack by forcing Wembanyama to make a choice he cannot make correctly. This is the architecture behind an offense that has outscored opponents by 271 points in 14 playoff games.

The Spurs have the best defensive player in basketball. The Knicks have figured out how to use that against him.

What's Next

Game 1 on Wednesday will be the first real test of San Antonio's adjustments. Head coach Mitch Johnson and his staff have had the full postseason to study how other teams have tried and failed to stop this Knicks offense. Whether they have found a solution no previous team could find is the tactical question that could determine the series.
