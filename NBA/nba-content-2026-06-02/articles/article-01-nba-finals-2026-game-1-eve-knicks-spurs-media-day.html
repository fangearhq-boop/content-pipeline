NBA Finals 2026 Game 1 Eve: Knicks and Spurs Finish Media Day, Tips Off Tomorrow in San Antonio

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 2, 2026

The interviews are done. The cameras have been packed up. And tomorrow night, the basketball begins.

The New York Knicks and San Antonio Spurs wrapped up NBA Finals Media Day on Monday at the Frost Bank Center in San Antonio, completing the last formality before one of the most anticipated championship series in recent memory. Game 1 tips off Wednesday, June 3, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC.

Two franchises who have not shared a Finals stage since 1999. Two superstars the league has spent years anticipating. One city that has waited 27 years, and one that has waited 12.

The Stakes and the Schedule

The full series schedule: Game 1 on Wednesday, June 3, in San Antonio; Game 2 on Friday, June 5, in San Antonio; Game 3 on Monday, June 8, at Madison Square Garden; Game 4 on Wednesday, June 10, at MSG; Game 5, if necessary, on Saturday, June 13. All games tip at 8:30 PM ET on ABC.

San Antonio opens as a 5.5-point home favorite in Game 1. The Spurs finished the regular season 62-20 — the NBA's best record — and have home-court advantage throughout.

The Knicks' Historic Arrival

New York arrives as perhaps the most dominant team to ever enter an NBA Finals. The Knicks have won 11 consecutive playoff games, posting a +271 total point differential — the best ever by a team entering the championship round. Their average margin of victory in the postseason is 23.8 points per game.

They swept the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Finals, winning Game 4 by 37 points. Jalen Brunson earned ECF MVP honors after averaging 25.5 points and 7.8 assists per game in the series. The Knicks have not faced a close game in over a month.

There is one advantage the Spurs hold that cannot be quantified in point differential: rest. The Knicks have not played a game in nine days. San Antonio just closed out a seven-game Western Conference Finals battle against the Oklahoma City Thunder on Saturday. Whether that fatigue factors in on Wednesday remains to be seen.

Injury Updates

Knicks center Mitchell Robinson is dealing with a broken right pinkie finger but plans to play in the series. Robinson's interior presence — his rebounding and rim protection alongside Karl-Anthony Towns — is a meaningful component of New York's interior defense.

On the Spurs side, forward David Jones Garcia is out for the season with an ankle injury. His absence narrows San Antonio's depth, though the core of Victor Wembanyama, Stephon Castle, De'Aaron Fox, and Dylan Harper remains intact.

What's Next

Game 1 on Wednesday sets the tone for what is expected to be a competitive series. The Spurs have the home crowd, the best record in basketball, and the most dominant defender on the planet. The Knicks have the momentum, the three-point shooting, and a guard in Brunson who has not been stopped all postseason.

One thing is certain: this Finals has been worth the wait. Wednesday, June 3, 8:30 PM ET on ABC.
