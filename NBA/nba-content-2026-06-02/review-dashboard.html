Gregg Popovich's Shadow — The Legend Still Drives These Spurs
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Before Victor Wembanyama, before the 62-20 record and the Western Conference title — there was Gregg Popovich. Pop is 77. In November 2024, he suffered a stroke that ended his coaching career. He has not stood on an NBA sideline in an official capacity since. But ask anyone in the Spurs organization, and they will tell you: he never really left. Popovich now holds the title of President of Spurs Basketball. In practice, he is something closer to a coach emeritus. He attends practices, sometimes walking with the assistance of a cane. He works in the gym alongside Tim Duncan during rehab sessions. He watches film. He advises head coach Mitch Johnson. Victor Wembanyama has spoken about Popovich with visible emotion. The relationship between the legend and the generational phenom is one of the defining threads of this franchise's current story. The Spurs are in the NBA Finals because of what Popovich built — the culture, the system, the standard of professionalism. Mitch Johnson did not create those values. He inherited them, tended to them, and brought them to the championship round for the first time since 2014. Gregg Popovich's shadow hangs over this Finals. It is the shadow of someone who built something worth watching. ### Image Post Caption He built this. 🙏 Gregg Popovich, 77, is still in the building — attending practices, advising the coaching staff, cheering on a team he poured his life into. The Spurs are in the Finals. Pop helped make it possible.

Steve Kerr Returns to Warriors on New 2-Year Contract
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post While the NBA Finals steal the spotlight, a significant piece of coaching news has landed: Steve Kerr is coming back to Golden State. The Warriors and Kerr have agreed to a two-year contract, confirming his return for what will be his 13th season coaching the franchise. The deal keeps Kerr as the highest-paid coach in the NBA — he earned $17.5 million last season. This comes after a difficult year. Golden State finished 37-45 and was eliminated by the Phoenix Suns in the play-in tournament. Kerr had announced in training camp he would not seek an extension, choosing instead to let the final year of his contract run out. After three weeks of deliberation — including meetings with owner Joe Lacob and GM Mike Dunleavy — Kerr signed. The choice to come back says something. Kerr believes in this franchise. He believes there is more to accomplish. For Warriors fans who watched four championships under his leadership, that belief is worth something. ### Image Post Caption He's back. 🏆 Steve Kerr signs a new 2-year deal to remain as Golden State Warriors head coach. Year 13 in the Bay. The work continues. #Warriors

KAT and OG's Three-Point Barrage — The Hidden Weapon Against Wemby
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Victor Wembanyama is the best defensive player on the planet. But the New York Knicks have built something that creates a genuine problem for him — and it starts from behind the arc. Karl-Anthony Towns is shooting 48.9 percent from three in these 2026 playoffs. That is a career playoff best. OG Anunoby is at 48.3 percent from three. When two of the five players on the floor are converting at that clip, something has to give. Wembanyama's defensive system is built around protecting the paint. But that system has a flaw against a lineup that includes both KAT and OG as genuine long-range threats. If he sags to protect the paint, one of them buries a three. If he closes out, Brunson has an open lane. There is no correct answer. Anunoby also provides a defensive answer on the other end. Per efficiency metrics, he has allowed the fewest points per 100 matchup possessions as a primary Wembanyama defender of any player who has guarded him more than 100 half-court possessions this season. The Knicks have an offense that exploits Wembanyama's scheme and a defender who can neutralize him on the other end. Whether it works over seven games is the question Game 1 begins to answer. ### Image Post Caption KAT: 48.9% from three in the 2026 playoffs. OG Anunoby: 48.3%. Wembanyama's defensive system doesn't have an answer for this. Tomorrow night, we find out how the Spurs adjust. #NBAFinals #Knicks
