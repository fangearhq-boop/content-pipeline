Knicks Clinch 2026 Playoff Berth After Heat Defeat 76ers 119-109

By Hoop Heroes Staff March 31, 2026

The Knicks Are Going to the Playoffs

The New York Knicks officially punched their ticket to the 2026 NBA Playoffs on Monday night — and they did it without playing. The Miami Heat defeated the Philadelphia 76ers 119-109, a result that eliminated the mathematical possibility of New York being passed for a playoff spot. The Knicks, now 48-27, will be in the postseason for the second straight year.

Bam Adebayo powered Miami with 23 points, 16 rebounds, and 6 assists — one of the Heat center's best performances of the season. For Philadelphia, the loss continued a troubling stretch that has complicated their playoff positioning. The 76ers remain 39-34 overall, holding the No. 7 seed in the East, but their grip on a guaranteed playoff spot is tenuous with the East Play-In bracket compressing.

New York's Locked-In Position

The Knicks enter April as one of the more difficult outs in the Eastern Conference. At 48-27, they sit among the confirmed top seeds alongside the Detroit Pistons (54-20) and Boston Celtics. New York's combination of Jalen Brunson's playmaking, solid two-way wings, and an improving defense gives them legitimate upside in a playoff run.

The clinching also frees the Knicks' coaching staff to experiment and manage minutes over the season's final weeks, particularly with players who have been carrying heavy loads through the second half. The priority now shifts to seeding — specifically, whether New York can improve their position and secure favorable first-round matchup positioning. Every game remaining in the East has seeding consequences.

The East Bracket Taking Shape

The upper half of the Eastern Conference is largely settled: Detroit holds the No. 1 seed, Boston and New York are in, and the Cleveland Cavaliers have been one of the conference's best teams through March. Below the top four, however, the bracket remains volatile. Seeds 5 through 10 in the East are separated by just 3.5 games, with the Toronto Raptors, Atlanta Hawks, Philadelphia 76ers, Miami Heat, and others all still fighting for position.

The Play-In Tournament — scheduled for April 14-17 — will decide seeds 7 through 10 in each conference. For teams outside the guaranteed top six, every remaining game carries elimination-bracket stakes. The chaos in the lower East seeds makes the Knicks' comfortable position all the more valuable heading into the final stretch.

What's Next

With their playoff spot secured, the Knicks will focus on the seeding battle over the final 10 games of the regular season. The first round of the NBA Playoffs begins April 18, with bracket matchups determined by seeding order at the end of the regular season on April 12. New York fans who have been through some lean years will be watching closely — this Knicks team has the depth and the playmaker to do real damage in the postseason.
