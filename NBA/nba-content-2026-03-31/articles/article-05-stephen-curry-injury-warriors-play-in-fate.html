Curry Still Sidelined 25-Plus Games: Warriors' Play-In Future Hangs in the Balance

By Hoop Heroes Staff March 31, 2026

The Long Wait Continues

Stephen Curry left a January 30 game against the Detroit Pistons with what was later diagnosed as patellofemoral pain syndrome — runner's knee, an inflammation condition around the kneecap that is notoriously difficult to predict in terms of recovery time. More than 25 consecutive games later, there is still no return timeline. Curry has not been cleared for five-on-five scrimmaging, the threshold before a player is typically considered for game return.

The four-time NBA champion has been candid about the unusual nature of the injury. "This is a weird one," Curry said. "It's kind of unpredictable how it'll heal. It's going to be a little longer." That uncertainty has hung over the Golden State franchise since February, coloring every game result and every practice report from Chase Center.

Warriors Surviving — Barely

Without Curry, the Warriors are 35-38 overall, occupying the No. 10 seed in the Western Conference. That position locks them into the Play-In Tournament bracket — where they would need to win two consecutive games to reach the playoffs. Golden State's situation is further complicated by the season-ending injuries to Jimmy Butler III and Moses Moody, both of whom were lost to knee injuries, leaving the roster thin in key areas.

Coach Steve Kerr has acknowledged publicly that a season shutdown for Curry — ending his regular season early to protect him for a potential playoff run — is a real possibility if no return timeline materializes soon. The tension is real: pushing Curry back too quickly risks aggravating the injury, but keeping him out through the Play-In might mean he returns for a three-game playoff run at best. Neither scenario is ideal for a franchise that has built its modern identity entirely around Curry's health and brilliance.

What a Return Could Mean

Even a healthy Curry arriving for the Play-In Tournament would represent an enormous upgrade for Golden State's chances. His shooting gravity alone — the way defenses are forced to extend and account for him on every possession — creates open looks for teammates that simply do not exist without him on the floor. The Warriors have shown flashes of competitiveness without him, but they are a different team, with a different ceiling, the moment No. 30 is in the starting lineup.

For the fan base that has watched four championships built on Curry's shoulders, this stretch has been an uncomfortable reminder of how fragile even the greatest careers can be. The Warriors have been here before — Curry missed significant time in 2019-20 and 2021 — and found ways back. The hope is that the medical staff, the coaches, and Curry himself can navigate this one the same way.

What's Next

The Golden State Warriors have approximately nine games remaining in the regular season, ending April 12. Their No. 10 seed position in the West means they will participate in the Play-In Tournament (April 14-17), where they would need to win to advance to the first round of the playoffs beginning April 18. The coming days of practice reports will be the most closely watched in the Warriors' season — and Curry's participation in five-on-five scrimmaging would be the clearest sign that a return is imminent.
