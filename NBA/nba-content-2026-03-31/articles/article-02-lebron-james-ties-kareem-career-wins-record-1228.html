LeBron James Ties Kareem Abdul-Jabbar's All-Time Career Wins Record at 1,228

By Hoop Heroes Staff March 31, 2026

History, Again

LeBron James has spent more than two decades rewriting the NBA record books. On Monday night, he added another entry. The 41-year-old Lakers star recorded career win No. 1,228, tying Kareem Abdul-Jabbar for the most victories by any player in the history of the league. LeBron delivered a triple-double in the process — 21 points, 10 rebounds, and 12 assists — because of course he did.

Kareem Abdul-Jabbar spent 20 seasons building his wins total, playing from 1969 through 1989 on six championship teams. LeBron has now matched that number in his own 23-year journey — and at 41 years old, he has shown no credible signs of stepping away. Each time the league expects the record-setting to stop, LeBron finds another milestone to chase.

A Father-Son Moment for the Ages

Monday's game provided more than just a historic wins milestone. Late in the contest, LeBron James dished an assist to his son Bronny James, who connected on a three-pointer. The moment is being reported as the first father-son assist in NBA history — a footnote that adds an extraordinary personal dimension to an already remarkable night.

Bronny James, who joined the Lakers organization after being selected in the 2024 NBA Draft, has seen expanded minutes recently with Marcus Smart sidelined. With that additional opportunity, the younger James has responded — scoring in double figures in back-to-back games heading into Tuesday. For LeBron, who has spoken publicly about his desire to play alongside his son, Monday night was a realization of something he envisioned years ago when the idea seemed more aspiration than reality.

Still Chasing Goals at 41

The Lakers have been one of the more surprising stories of the second half of the NBA season. They've won 12 of their last 13 games, with LeBron contributing at a level that would be remarkable for a player a decade younger. Luka Doncic has been the offensive engine, but LeBron's supporting role — leaning on playmaking, rebounding, and leadership — has been perfectly calibrated for a team trying to peak at the right moment.

Los Angeles currently has an opportunity to clinch a playoff berth and the Pacific Division title Tuesday night with a win plus a Phoenix Suns loss. With Luka suspended for one game (after earning his 16th technical foul), the burden of leading the team falls squarely on LeBron — which feels appropriate on the night after he tied the all-time career wins record.

What's Next

LeBron James now holds the all-time career wins record outright should he earn one more victory — a milestone that could come as early as Tuesday night if the Lakers secure a win. Beyond the personal records, the basketball calendar is accelerating: the regular season ends April 12, with the Play-In Tournament April 14-17 and the first round of the playoffs beginning April 18. For LeBron, the wins record is a beautiful moment in a career full of them — but his focus, as always, is on what comes next.
