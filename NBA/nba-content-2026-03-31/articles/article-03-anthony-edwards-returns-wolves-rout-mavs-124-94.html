Edwards Returns, Dosunmu Triples: Wolves Rout Mavs 124-94 in Statement Win

By Hoop Heroes Staff March 31, 2026

ANT Is Back

Anthony Edwards missed six consecutive games with right knee patellofemoral syndrome — a nagging inflammation condition that forced Minnesota's star off the floor at the worst possible time in the playoff race. On Monday night, he returned, and the Timberwolves looked nothing like a team missing their best player. Minnesota blew out the Dallas Mavericks 124-94, one of their most commanding wins of the season.

Edwards came off the bench and contributed 17 points, easing back in carefully. Julius Randle led the offense with 24 points, and Rudy Gobert added 14 points and 10 rebounds in a game that was never close after the first quarter. The Wolves moved to 46-29 on the season, claiming sole possession of fifth in the Western Conference — a half-game ahead of the idle Houston Rockets.

Dosunmu's Breakout Moment

The individual performance of the night belonged to Ayo Dosunmu, who recorded his first triple-double since being acquired at the NBA trade deadline. The Chicago-native-turned-Wolf finished with 16 points, 15 rebounds, and 12 assists — a stat line that defied his role as a secondary contributor. Dosunmu's ability to impact games across all three categories has been a quiet addition to a Wolves team that needed depth.

The trade deadline addition has been integrating steadily, but Monday's performance was a declaration. With Edwards managing his workload carefully down the stretch, Dosunmu's expanded playmaking keeps Minnesota from over-relying on their star. The team chemistry on display — crisp passing, shared ball movement, and defensive intensity — reflected the cohesion of a team that has been building through a difficult mid-season stretch.

All-NBA Stakes for Edwards

Beyond the win itself, Edwards' return carries enormous individual significance. He entered the season averaging a career-high 29.5 points per game — a figure that put him firmly in All-NBA consideration. But All-NBA eligibility requires a player to appear in at least 65 games, and Edwards had played in only 58 before his six-game absence. He now needs to appear in every remaining game to reach that threshold.

Monday's return set the clock ticking in the right direction. With approximately nine games left in the regular season and the Wolves still fighting for seeding in the West, Edwards has every motivation to stay healthy and active. The All-NBA implications aren't just about an award — they also trigger contract provisions that can significantly impact player compensation. For a player of Edwards' caliber and trajectory, every game remaining matters on multiple levels.

What's Next

The Timberwolves continue their push for the best possible playoff seed, with the West's fifth and sixth spots still in flux as the Denver Nuggets and Houston Rockets remain within striking distance. Edwards will continue to ramp up his minutes carefully, and the Wolves' medical staff will monitor his knee throughout the final stretch. If Edwards stays healthy, Minnesota's playoff ceiling is considerably higher — and after Monday's 30-point blowout, the rest of the West has reason to pay attention.
