Conference Finals Countdown: Knicks and OKC Waiting as Two Elimination Games Loom

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 15, 2026

One Friday night could determine the entire landscape of the 2026 NBA playoffs. Two Game 6 matchups tonight — Cavaliers vs. Pistons at 7:00 PM ET and Spurs vs. Timberwolves at 9:30 PM ET — have the potential to set both Conference Finals matchups before midnight. And waiting at the finish line are two teams who have earned the right to rest: the New York Knicks and the Oklahoma City Thunder.

Both top seeds swept their first-round opponents and have been waiting for more than a week. Tonight, they find out who they'll play next.

Knicks vs. Cavaliers — The Eastern Conference Finals Preview

If Cleveland closes out Detroit tonight, the Eastern Conference Finals begin as early as Saturday, May 17. The Knicks, who swept the Philadelphia 76ers in the first round, are rested and ready. Jalen Brunson, Karl-Anthony Towns, and OG Anunoby lead a team that won 60 games in the regular season and enters the ECF with enormous confidence.

Cleveland would bring a different challenge: Donovan Mitchell's ability to erupt for 40-point second halves, James Harden's veteran playmaking, and a defensive identity built around Evan Mobley. The Cavs-Knicks ECF would be the most compelling East matchup in years, featuring two teams that genuinely believe they can reach the NBA Finals.

Mikal Bridges has been quietly excellent for New York all season. His ability to guard Mitchell — one of the hardest assignments in the league — could be the series' defining factor.

OKC vs. Spurs — The Western Conference Finals Everyone Wants to See

The Oklahoma City Thunder have been as dominant as any team in this playoffs. Their 8-0 record includes a sweep of the Los Angeles Lakers in the second round, with Shai Gilgeous-Alexander averaging over 30 points per game. Chet Holmgren hit a tiebreaking dunk with 32.8 seconds left in the series-clinching Game 4. Ajay Mitchell dropped 28 points off the bench. This team has every weapon and no apparent weakness.

And then there's Wemby. If San Antonio closes out Minnesota tonight, the WCF matchup of Wembanyama vs. SGA becomes the story of the basketball world. Two 22-and-under superstars, two legitimate MVP candidates, facing off in a Conference Finals for the first time. The WCF would start May 18 at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City.

This is the matchup the league has been building toward all season.

Double-Elimination Friday

The structure of tonight matters as much as the individual games. Both Game 6s on the same night creates a playoff marathon — fans can watch Cleveland try to close out at 7:00 PM and immediately flip to Minnesota's elimination game at 9:30 PM. It is the kind of scheduling that produces communal, shared sports moments.

If both road teams (Detroit and Minnesota) win tonight, the drama continues into next week. If both home teams win, the Conference Finals are set by Saturday morning. Either outcome produces good television and compelling sports debate.

What's Next

Cavaliers vs. Pistons tips off tonight at 7:00 PM ET on Prime Video, followed by Spurs vs. Timberwolves at 9:30 PM ET, also on Prime Video. The Eastern Conference Finals could begin Saturday, May 17. The Western Conference Finals could begin Monday, May 18. Tonight is the pivot point of the entire 2026 NBA postseason.
