Spurs vs. Timberwolves Game 6: Wemby One Win From the WCF

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 15, 2026

Victor Wembanyama is 48 minutes away from the Western Conference Finals. The San Antonio Spurs take their 3-2 series lead into Target Center in Minneapolis tonight for Game 6 against the Minnesota Timberwolves, with tipoff scheduled for 9:30 PM ET on Prime Video. A win tonight sends Wemby's Spurs to face the Oklahoma City Thunder — an 8-0 juggernaut — in what would be one of the most anticipated Conference Finals matchups in recent memory.

San Antonio earned its series advantage with a dominant Game 5 performance. Wemby posted 27 points, 17 rebounds, 5 assists, and 3 blocks, and did most of his damage in the opening quarter — 18 of his 27 points came in the first 12 minutes as the Spurs raced out to a lead the Wolves never challenged. The final score was 126-97.

Wemby's Most Complete Playoff Game

Game 5 was a statement for Wembanyama. He had been ejected in Game 4 after a Flagrant 2 foul on Naz Reid's face — his first career ejection — and returned in Game 5 without suspension. The way he played, it's possible the brief absence sharpened his focus. He was in command from his first possession, scoring on a variety of moves, protecting the rim, and reading Minnesota's defense with a fluidity that is genuinely unprecedented for a player his size.

Wemby is 7-foot-4 with guard-level footwork, a 3-point shooting ability, and shot-blocking instincts that don't fit any prior template. In his second NBA postseason, he is already making the conversation about the greatest young playoff performers in league history a legitimate one.

Edwards Must Deliver or Minnesota Goes Home

Anthony Edwards kept the Wolves alive in Game 4 with 36 points and a furious second-half effort. His 16-point fourth quarter helped Minnesota outscore San Antonio 28-15 over the final 8:51 to level the series. But Game 5 was a complete reversal — Minnesota scored just 97 points, their worst output of the postseason, as Wemby's first-quarter explosion deflated the home crowd before it could really engage.

Edwards is 24 years old and playing in the second round of the playoffs for the first time in what looks like a deep career. If he's going to push back and extend this series tonight, he needs to set the tone immediately. Let Wemby hit his first two shots without a fight, and this series could be over by halftime. But Edwards at his best — aggressive, explosive, fearless — is one of the few players capable of changing the energy of an entire building.

The Wolves are 2-1 at Target Center in this series. The crowd will be loud. Minnesota has something to play for.

The Prize: OKC and a Dream Matchup

If the Spurs advance, they draw the Oklahoma City Thunder, who are a perfect 8-0 in the 2026 playoffs after sweeping the Los Angeles Lakers. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has been the best player in the Western Conference playoffs, and the Wemby-vs-SGA matchup in a WCF would generate enormous national interest.

The WCF would begin Monday, May 18, at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City if the Spurs close out tonight. San Antonio has an opportunity to take a massive step toward the biggest stage in basketball — and Wemby is the reason they're here.

What's Next

Game 6 tips off tonight at 9:30 PM ET at Target Center in Minneapolis, broadcast on Prime Video. A Spurs win sends San Antonio to the Western Conference Finals to face Oklahoma City. A Minnesota win forces a decisive Game 7 back in San Antonio. Either way, this series has delivered playoff basketball at its finest — and tonight's elimination game promises more of the same.
