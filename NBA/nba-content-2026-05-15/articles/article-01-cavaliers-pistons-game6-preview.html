Cavaliers vs. Pistons Game 6: Cleveland One Win From Conference Finals

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 15, 2026

The Cleveland Cavaliers return home to Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse tonight with a 3-2 series lead and one win standing between them and the Eastern Conference Finals. Game 6 of the second-round matchup against the Detroit Pistons tips off at 7:00 PM ET on Prime Video, and Cleveland will look to close out a series that has been more competitive — and more dramatic — than almost anyone predicted.

The Cavs earned their series lead the hard way. They fell behind 2-0 in Detroit before stringing together three straight wins, including a 117-113 overtime victory in Game 5 on Wednesday that required a stunning 13-0 run in the final minutes of regulation just to force overtime.

Harden's Clutch Performance Sets the Stage

James Harden has been Cleveland's most important player in this series at the moments that matter most. His Game 5 line — 30 points, 8 rebounds, 6 assists, and an 11-of-14 performance from the free throw line — doesn't fully capture how critical his late-game production was. When the Cavs trailed 103-94 with under three minutes remaining, Harden orchestrated the entire comeback. He made free throws, grabbed his own miss off the rim, and drew fouls when Cleveland could least afford to miss a possession.

His plus/minus of +11 in Game 5 told the story. At 36 years old, Harden continues to produce in the highest-pressure moments of the postseason, and Cleveland will need more of the same tonight on their home floor.

Donovan Mitchell added 21 points in Game 5 after his historic 43-point second-half performance in Game 4. Mitchell is averaging 29.2 points over the last three games of this series, and the Pistons have yet to find a consistent answer for his combination of pull-up shooting and downhill drives.

Detroit Has Nothing to Lose

The Pistons arrived at this series as a team many expected to exit quickly. Instead, they won the first two games on the road, took commanding leads in multiple games, and pushed Cleveland to the absolute edge. Cade Cunningham's 39-point, 9-assist performance in Game 5 — a loss, on the road, in overtime — was the performance that announced him definitively as a franchise-caliber talent.

Detroit also has unfinished business from Wednesday's no-call controversy. With the clock expiring in regulation, Ausar Thompson appeared to be tripped by Jarrett Allen while holding the ball, a moment that replays showed clearly but officials deemed not a foul. The play cost the Pistons a chance to win in regulation, and it has motivated an already hungry city. Expect Detroit to come out with enormous energy tonight, even on the road.

The Pistons have won twice at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in this series — both in the first two games — so a road environment is nothing new for them. If Cunningham plays at his Game 5 level and the Detroit defense gets stops early, this series could go to a deciding Game 7 in Detroit on Sunday.

The Stakes for Cleveland

A Cavaliers win tonight sets up a marquee Eastern Conference Finals matchup against the New York Knicks, who swept the Philadelphia 76ers in the first round and have been resting since. The Knicks, led by Jalen Brunson, Karl-Anthony Towns, and a deep supporting cast, would be formidable opponents — but Cleveland has the star power to compete.

The Cavs last appeared in the Eastern Conference Finals in 2018 in their LeBron James-era run. This version of Cleveland is building its own identity, and Mitchell, Harden, and Evan Mobley have the potential to make a deep run. But they have to win tonight first.

What's Next

Game 6 tips off tonight at 7:00 PM ET at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in Cleveland, broadcast on Prime Video. If the Cavaliers win, they advance to the Eastern Conference Finals to face New York, with the series beginning as early as Saturday, May 17. If Detroit wins, the series shifts to a decisive Game 7 on Sunday in Detroit — and the Pistons' remarkable run continues.
