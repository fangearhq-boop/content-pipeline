Anthony Edwards and the Moment Minnesota Has Been Waiting For

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 15, 2026

Anthony Edwards has spent three NBA seasons proving he can be a franchise player. Tonight, in a Game 6 elimination game at Target Center against Victor Wembanyama and the San Antonio Spurs, he gets to prove it on the biggest stage of his career so far.

The Timberwolves trail the series 3-2 after a brutal Game 5 blowout loss — 126-97 — in which Wemby scored 18 of his 27 points in the first quarter. Minnesota had no answer for him. The question tonight is whether Edwards can set a different tone before the Spurs can establish their dominant rhythm.

What Edwards Has Already Done This Series

The 24-year-old's Game 4 performance is the best argument that Minnesota belongs in this series. Edwards scored 36 points and helped lead the Wolves back from an eight-point deficit in the fourth quarter. He was relentless, attacking downhill, making pull-up jumpers, and drawing fouls when the game was on the line. Minnesota outscored San Antonio 28-15 over the final 8:51 of regulation — that was almost entirely Edwards.

Those moments matter. They demonstrate the kind of player Edwards is becoming — a performer who rises when the stakes are highest. But a single dominant game only carries a team so far. The Spurs responded with their own statement: a first-quarter blitz that neutralized everything Minnesota had done to level the series.

The Wemby Problem

Defending Victor Wembanyama is unlike defending anyone else in the league. His combination of height, wingspan, footwork, and shooting range creates coverage problems that no single scheme can solve. Minnesota tried switching, tried zone, tried hard hedges. In Game 5, none of it mattered — Wemby was making his first shots regardless of the coverage and building momentum that carried through four quarters.

Edwards isn't a Wemby stopper — no one is, really — but his ability to create early offense for the Wolves might be the only counterbalance Minnesota has. If Edwards attacks immediately, forces the Spurs into early foul trouble, gets the crowd into it, and disrupts San Antonio's defensive rhythm, the Wolves give themselves a chance to dictate the game's pace instead of chasing it.

Passive Edwards doesn't beat this Spurs team. Assertive, aggressive, downhill Edwards gives Minnesota a legitimate path to Game 7.

The Home Floor Advantage

Target Center has been a genuine home court advantage in this series. The Wolves are 2-1 at home and have shown they can make things difficult for the Spurs in Minneapolis. The fan base is engaged, the building gets loud, and Minnesota has the kind of roster — athletic, physical, capable of long defensive possessions — that plays well in front of a home crowd.

Edwards has said publicly that he plays differently when Target Center is rocking behind him. If this crowd shows up the way it did in Games 2 and 4, he'll have the energy he needs to deliver his best performance of the season.

What's Next

Game 6 tips off tonight at 9:30 PM ET at Target Center in Minneapolis, broadcast on Prime Video. An Edwards-led Minnesota win forces Game 7 in San Antonio. A Spurs win ends the Wolves' season and sends Wemby's team to the Western Conference Finals to face Oklahoma City. Tonight is the most important game of Anthony Edwards' career to date — and one of the most anticipated elimination games of the 2026 playoffs.
