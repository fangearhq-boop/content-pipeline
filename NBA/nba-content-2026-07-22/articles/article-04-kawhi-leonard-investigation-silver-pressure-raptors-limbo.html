Kawhi Leonard Investigation: Silver Says It Must End Before Season, But Resolution Still Weeks Away

By Damon Pierce

July 22, 2026

Nearly eleven months into the NBA's investigation of Kawhi Leonard and the Los Angeles Clippers, Commissioner Adam Silver is running out of patience — but the probe is running out of runway even faster than Silver would like.

Speaking at Summer League media availability, Silver repeated the statement he has made publicly on multiple occasions: "I would say this can be wrapped up — and needs to be wrapped up — before next season." The regular season is less than three months away. As of mid-July, league sources confirmed the investigation remains weeks from its conclusion.

The math is getting uncomfortable.

How the Investigation Expanded

The probe began in September 2025 with a focused allegation: that the Clippers had funneled compensation to Leonard through an endorsement deal with Aspiration Partners, a now-defunct carbon-credits startup. If true, the arrangement would constitute illegal salary cap circumvention — paying a player off the books in violation of collective bargaining rules.

Since then, the investigation conducted by law firm Wachtell Lipton has expanded significantly. A second, previously undisclosed endorsement deal has been added to the scope of the inquiry. Personal expense coverage by the Clippers on Leonard's behalf is also under review. Each expansion adds complexity to an already sensitive investigation and pushes the resolution timeline further into the future.

Alex Sanberg, a figure connected to the probe, was sentenced to 14 years in prison. His case added a criminal dimension to what was already a serious league compliance matter.

The Raptors' Impossible Position

Toronto's situation deserves particular attention. The Clippers and Raptors negotiated a trade involving Leonard, and the deal reached the point of finalization before the NBA intervened. League officials informed Toronto that clearing the trade would require the Raptors to assume the risk of any penalties Leonard faces when the investigation concludes — including potential contract voiding or suspension.

The Raptors declined. Their calculus was straightforward: acquiring a player who might be suspended or whose contract might be voided provides no roster stability. Better to wait for resolution.

So Toronto waits. The Raptors are in their third consecutive offseason of roster uncertainty tied to this situation. They cannot finalize their team, cannot commit to a direction, and cannot give their coaching staff a clear picture of what the 2026-27 roster looks like — all because a trade that was agreed upon months ago remains legally frozen.

What a Resolution Looks Like

The range of outcomes is broad. If Wachtell Lipton concludes that cap circumvention occurred, the penalties could include fines, loss of draft picks, and potentially voiding Leonard's contract. In a severe finding, Leonard himself could face a lengthy suspension.

If the investigation concludes with insufficient evidence — or with a finding of no violation — the Clippers-Raptors trade would likely be cleared to complete, and Leonard could play in Toronto as originally planned. That scenario gets increasingly complicated as the months stretch on: Leonard is 35 years old, and his window as a meaningful contributor is not unlimited.

Silver's repeated public statements suggest he views this as more than a procedural matter. The integrity of the salary cap system depends on teams believing that agreements to circumvent it carry real consequences. The scope expansion to a second deal suggests there may be more to find here than the original allegation indicated.

What's Next

Silver's "before next season" deadline is rapidly approaching. If the investigation cannot conclude before training camps open in late September, the Commissioner faces an uncomfortable choice: delay the start of the season, allow a team to operate with illegal roster uncertainty, or make a ruling with incomplete information.

None of those options is clean. The pressure on Wachtell Lipton and the NBA's legal team is real. The Raptors, Clippers, and Leonard himself are all waiting for an answer that has been "weeks away" for most of the summer.
