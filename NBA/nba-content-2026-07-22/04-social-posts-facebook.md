### STORY 4: Kawhi Investigation — Silver Wants It Done

Commissioner Adam Silver said again this week what he's been saying for months: "I would say this can be wrapped up — and needs to be wrapped up — before next season."

Law firm Wachtell Lipton is conducting the probe, which centers on allegations that the LA Clippers funneled compensation to Leonard through a sponsorship deal with Aspiration Partners (a now-defunct carbon-credits startup). Alex Sanberg — a figure connected to the probe — has been sentenced to 14 years in prison.

For the Raptors, every week without resolution is a week of summer planning lost. For the Clippers, the legal and competitive implications of a major ruling could reshape their franchise. For the league, this is an integrity issue that Commissioner Silver needs to close.

### STORY 5: What LeBron's Decision Means for Each Team

#### Long-Form Post — 7:00 PM ET

LeBron James is about to make a decision that changes four NBA rosters, the conference power structure, and the 2026-27 championship picture all at once.

Here's what each destination actually means:

**Miami Heat (48% odds)**
The Heat already gave up Tyler Herro, multiple picks, and their entire young core to get Giannis Antetokounmpo. If LeBron comes, they become the most star-laden team since the 2011-12 Heat with Giannis + LeBron anchoring everything. The downside: no depth, no draft capital, no margin for error.

**Cleveland Cavaliers (27% odds)**
A third LeBron stint in Cleveland — and this time he'd be arriving to a team that's already ready. Donovan Mitchell signed a four-year, $273 million extension. Evan Mobley and Jarrett Allen are young and ascending. James Harden declined his $42.3M option to restructure specifically to create cap room for LeBron. This might be his easiest landing spot competitively.

The emotional storyline: LeBron and Stephen Curry, Olympic gold medal teammates, closing out their careers together. Yaxel Lendeborg just made NBA history. The franchise culture is elite. But at 14%, the market doesn't believe it happens.

Bob Myers' front office just added Jaylen Brown (from Boston for Paul George + picks). Add LeBron to Embiid and Maxey and Brown and you have an instant Eastern Conference powerhouse. Myers appeared on Rich Paul's own podcast to make the pitch.

One city is about to find out it's getting LeBron. Three cities are about to find out they're not.
