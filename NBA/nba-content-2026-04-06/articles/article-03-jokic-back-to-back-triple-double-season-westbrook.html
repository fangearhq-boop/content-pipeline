Jokic on the Verge of Joining Westbrook With Back-to-Back Triple-Double Season Averages

By Damon Pierce April 6, 2026

The Numbers Nobody Else Can Match

With six regular-season games remaining, Nikola Jokic is averaging 27.7 points, 13.0 rebounds, and 10.8 assists per game. Those averages will hold — they aren't going to fall below 10 in any category in six games. Which means that when the final buzzer sounds on the 2025-26 NBA regular season, Jokic will have averaged a triple-double for the second consecutive full season — something only Russell Westbrook has done in NBA history.

Westbrook achieved the feat during the 2016-17 and 2018-19 seasons, breaking Oscar Robertson's single-season record in the process. Those back-to-back achievements launched Westbrook into a conversation that consumed the basketball world for years. Jokic is about to join him — quietly, efficiently, and without the kind of fanfare that surrounded Westbrook's run. That's how Jokic operates.

What Makes This Even More Remarkable

Context is everything when evaluating Jokic's triple-double averages. This isn't Westbrook in 2017 playing on a Thunder team that was built to funnel the ball to him after Kevin Durant left. Jokic is doing this on a Denver Nuggets team with legitimate complementary players — Jamal Murray, Michael Porter Jr. — who demand their own offensive involvement. And yet Jokic still finds a way to average more than 10 assists per game as a center.

His 32 triple-doubles this season lead the NBA by a significant margin, and earlier this season he passed Oscar Robertson on the all-time career triple-doubles list, climbing to No. 2 behind Westbrook. For a player who came into the league in the 41st pick of the 2014 draft and was famously announced during a World Cup game broadcast in Serbia, the historical arc is one of basketball's great stories.

The MVP Conversation He Keeps Losing

Despite these statistics, Jokic is not the frontrunner for the 2026 MVP award. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander (-260 in betting markets) and Victor Wembanyama (+250) are running far ahead of him in voter preference — and Jokic's case at +600 or longer reflects the basketball world's familiarity with his brilliance. Three MVP trophies in recent years have perhaps made his dominance feel too routine to generate the same excitement as SGA and Wemby's battle.

But the triple-double average removes any ambiguity about where Jokic stands historically. Wilt Chamberlain, Jerry West, Bill Russell, LeBron James, Magic Johnson — none of them averaged a triple-double in back-to-back full seasons. Westbrook did it, and now Jokic is about to. That is an exclusive club of exactly one, soon to be two.

What's Next

Jokic and the Denver Nuggets (No. 4 West seed) have six regular-season games remaining before the playoffs begin April 18. Their first-round matchup will be determined by how the final week of seeding shakes out, with Minnesota and Houston both within striking distance of Denver's position. Regardless of the seed, Jokic enters the postseason as one of the most dangerous players in the bracket — a three-time MVP who is, statistically, having the best all-around season of his career.
