Luka Doncic Reportedly Heads to Europe for Hamstring Treatment With Playoffs 12 Days Away

By Damon Pierce April 6, 2026

Racing Against the Postseason Clock

Luka Doncic was officially ruled out for the remainder of the Los Angeles Lakers' regular season after suffering a Grade 2 left hamstring strain. Now, with the NBA playoffs beginning April 18 — just 12 days away — reports indicate the Lakers superstar is traveling to Europe for specialized rehabilitation in hopes of accelerating his recovery timeline enough to play in the first round.

Grade 2 hamstring strains typically require four to eight weeks of standard recovery. The 12 days between now and the start of the playoffs places Doncic well outside the typical recovery window. The European treatment option, which NBA players have pursued before for various injuries, is designed to compress that timeline using intensive rehabilitation protocols not always available in standard team settings. Whether it works is far from guaranteed.

What the Lakers Lose Without Luka

The stakes for Los Angeles are enormous. Doncic entered his injury averaging 33.5 points per game — the highest in the NBA this season — alongside 7.7 rebounds and 8.3 assists. He had played 64 games (one short of the 65-game threshold for award eligibility) and was in the midst of the most explosive individual stretch of his career, averaging more than 35 points per game over his last several weeks.

Saturday night's 134-128 loss to Dallas showed exactly what the Lakers face without him. LeBron James delivered 30 points and 15 assists against Cooper Flagg's historic performance — a superhuman individual effort that still resulted in a loss. LeBron can carry games. He can't replicate what Luka provides as a first-option scorer, shot creator, and offensive hub across a seven-game playoff series at 41 years old.

The Questions That Won't Go Away

Even if Doncic returns for Game 1 or Game 2 of the first round, the more pressing question is whether he can be effective. A hamstring injury that barely healed in 12 days is not a player who operates at full capacity. The risk of re-injury in a high-intensity playoff environment is significant, and the Lakers will face a first-round opponent built for exactly this kind of scenario — preparing for a star who may or may not be 100%.

Los Angeles is the No. 3 seed in the West and will likely face either the No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves or a Play-In survivor in the opening round. None of those matchups are easy. Doncic's status transforms the Lakers from a legitimate championship contender into a team with a ceiling that depends almost entirely on whether he recovers in time — and how well he plays when he does.

What's Next

The Lakers' regular season ends April 12, and the NBA Playoffs begin April 18. Luka Doncic's status will be the most-watched injury update in basketball over the next two weeks. If European treatment accelerates his recovery to a potential Games 2 or 3 return, Los Angeles remains dangerous. If he misses the first round entirely, the Lakers face a must-win series without their best player against an opponent fully prepared for their backup plan. Updates on his rehab timeline are expected in the coming days.
