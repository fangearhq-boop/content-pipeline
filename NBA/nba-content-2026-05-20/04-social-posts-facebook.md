### STORY 5: Wembanyama's 40/20 — Historical Context — Long-Form Post
**Posting window:** 4:00 PM ET
**Tier:** 2

**Post:**
Let's put what Victor Wembanyama did Monday night in its full historical context.

In WCF Game 1 against the Oklahoma City Thunder, Wembanyama posted 41 points and 24 rebounds in a double-overtime victory. He hit a 27-foot three-pointer with 26.3 seconds left in the first overtime to force the second extra session. He finished with an and-one dunk over Holmgren that sealed the win. In 49 minutes.

Only six other players in NBA history have posted 40+ points and 20+ rebounds in a Conference Finals game:

- Wilt Chamberlain (1960)
- Elgin Baylor
- Moses Malone
- Kareem Abdul-Jabbar
- Charles Barkley
- Shaquille O'Neal

And now Victor Wembanyama — at 22 years and 134 days old — is the youngest ever to join that list.

Think about the names around him. Hall of Famers. Legends of the sport. Players who defined eras of basketball.

Wembanyama may be in the middle of doing the same thing. We are watching something extraordinary unfold, and we're only in Year 3.
