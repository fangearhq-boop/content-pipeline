Wembanyama's 41/24 Joins the Seven Greatest Conference Finals Games in NBA History

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 20, 2026

When Victor Wembanyama posted 41 points and 24 rebounds in WCF Game 1 on Monday night, he was not just playing a great game. He was joining a list that spans six decades of NBA basketball — a list of performances so extraordinary that only seven have ever qualified.

In the entire history of the NBA Conference Finals, only seven players have posted 40 or more points and 20 or more rebounds in a single game. Wembanyama, at age 22 years and 134 days, is the youngest player ever to do it, and he did it in his Conference Finals debut.

The List

Before Monday night, the exclusive club of 40/20 Conference Finals performances included Wilt Chamberlain, Elgin Baylor, Moses Malone, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Charles Barkley, and Shaquille O'Neal. These are not simply Hall of Famers. They are the players most basketball historians consider among the 15 or 20 greatest to ever play the sport.

Chamberlain's name appears on the list from 1960 — the same year that Wembanyama joined him as the only players to post 40 and 20 in a Conference Finals debut. That is a specific subset within an already extraordinary list.

Wembanyama's line from Monday: 41 points, 24 rebounds, 3 assists, 3 blocks in 49 minutes. He hit a 27-foot three-pointer with 26.3 seconds left in the first overtime to force a second extra session. He sealed the Spurs' 122-115 double-overtime victory with an and-one dunk over Chet Holmgren in the second overtime.

Age and Context

The historical weight of Wembanyama's performance is amplified by his age. At 22 years and 134 days old, he is the youngest player in NBA history to record 40 or more points and 20 or more rebounds in a single playoff game. He is also the unanimous Defensive Player of the Year — all 100 first-place votes — for the 2025-26 season, the first unanimous winner of that award in NBA history.

This is Year 3 of Wembanyama's NBA career. He has already posted one of the seven greatest Conference Finals games ever played, the most dominant defensive award season in history, and a 40/20 debut in a round that only legends have touched at that level.

The Spurs are in the Western Conference Finals. Wembanyama is 22. The trajectory of this career — if he stays healthy — is something basketball has not seen since LeBron James, and before that, since the legends on that 40/20 list were making their own marks.

What's Next

WCF Game 2 is tonight at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City (8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock). The Thunder, adjusting from Monday's historic loss, will have had a full day to study Wembanyama's movements, post-up tendencies, and three-point range. Whether OKC can find an answer for a player who may genuinely not have one at this point in the series is the defining question of the Western Conference Finals.

De'Aaron Fox, who missed Game 1 with a right ankle injury, is a game-time decision and trending toward playing tonight. If Fox returns, the Spurs' offensive arsenal expands even further. At 22 years old, with a unanimous DPOY, a 40/20 Conference Finals debut, and a potential NBA Finals appearance within reach, Victor Wembanyama is having a season that basketball will talk about for a very long time.
