Knicks Erase 22-Point Deficit, Beat Cavaliers 115-104 in OT for Biggest ECF Comeback Since 1997

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 20, 2026

Madison Square Garden erupted into chaos on Tuesday night as the New York Knicks pulled off the most stunning comeback in Conference Finals history in nearly three decades. Down 22 points with 7:52 remaining in the fourth quarter, New York outscored the Cleveland Cavaliers 44-11 over the final 7:40 of regulation and overtime to win ECF Game 1, 115-104.

It is the largest fourth-quarter comeback in Conference Finals history since 1997 and the biggest Knicks playoff comeback since 1970. At the center of it all was Jalen Brunson, who delivered one of the great individual clutch performances in MSG playoff history.

Brunson Takes Over

Brunson finished the night with 38 points, 6 assists, 5 rebounds, and 3 steals. Those numbers alone tell a remarkable story. But the specifics of how he got there tell a better one.

In the fourth quarter alone, Brunson scored 15 points on 7-of-9 shooting. As the Cavaliers' defense scrambled to cope with the MSG crowd and New York's defensive pressure, Brunson was surgical — pick-and-rolls, mid-range jumpers, and critical free throws — all delivered without hesitation under maximum pressure. By the time overtime ended, he had scored or assisted on 23 of the Knicks' final 32 points.

For a player who has built his reputation as a clutch performer across multiple playoff runs, Tuesday's performance at MSG was his defining moment in a Knicks uniform.

Cleveland's Collapse

The story of the Cavaliers' meltdown begins with how dominant they were before it happened. Donovan Mitchell was outstanding through most of the evening, finishing with 29 points and a career-high 6 steals. Cleveland looked well in control at 93-71 — the kind of lead that typically ends games.

Then the Knicks' defense clamped down in a way that Cleveland had no answer for. Mitchell went scoreless over his final 14 minutes on the floor. As a team, the Cavaliers shot just 22 percent during the Knicks' comeback run. The offense that had been flowing freely froze at the worst possible moment.

Karl-Anthony Towns contributed 11 points, 10 rebounds, and 5 assists for New York but turned the ball over 6 times. OG Anunoby was held to just 3 points on 5 shots. For this comeback, it was Brunson's stage, and the rest of the cast played supporting roles.

What's Next

The Cavaliers now face a 0-1 series deficit and must come back to Madison Square Garden for Game 2 on Thursday. For Cleveland, the central question is whether Tuesday's collapse was an anomaly — an unlikely combination of Mitchell fatigue and Knicks defensive brilliance — or something that can be replicated. Mitchell has been one of the league's best clutch performers throughout his career, and Game 2 at MSG will be a direct answer to that question.

For the Knicks, confidence is sky-high, the Garden is the loudest building in basketball right now, and Jalen Brunson just reminded the entire NBA why New York believed in him when they handed him the keys. The largest lead in MSG playoff history just became fuel.
