WCF Game 2 Preview: Thunder Must Respond as De'Aaron Fox Eyes Return Against Spurs

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 20, 2026

Oklahoma City entered the Western Conference Finals as the clear favorite — the No. 1 seed with a 8-0 playoff record and two-time MVP Shai Gilgeous-Alexander leading the charge. Then Monday night happened. Victor Wembanyama dropped 41 points and 24 rebounds in double overtime and the San Antonio Spurs walked out of Paycom Center with a shocking 122-115 victory and a 1-0 series lead.

Now, Wednesday night at Paycom Center, the Thunder face a must-not-fall-behind-0-2 situation in WCF Game 2 (8:30 PM ET, NBC/Peacock). And the Spurs may be getting an upgrade: De'Aaron Fox, who missed Game 1 with a right ankle injury, is listed as a game-time decision and trending toward playing, according to multiple reports.

The Fox Factor

De'Aaron Fox's potential return is the biggest story heading into tonight's game. Fox missed the entire WCF Game 1 due to a right ankle injury, and the Spurs still managed to win in double overtime behind Wembanyama's historic performance and Dylan Harper's 24 points, 11 rebounds, and 7 steals.

If Fox is healthy enough to play meaningful minutes tonight, San Antonio's offensive ceiling rises dramatically. Spurs head coach Mitch Johnson said Fox "will do everything he can" to be available and that optimism exists throughout the organization about his status. A final determination will come closer to tip-off.

For Oklahoma City, the prospect of defending both Wembanyama and a healthy Fox — on top of Harper — represents a defensive challenge that is genuinely difficult to game-plan for.

SGA Needs His Best

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander won his second consecutive Most Valuable Player award this season, averaging 31.1 points per game and leading the Thunder to the league's best record. In WCF Game 1, he played well enough statistically but was ultimately outperformed on the night by Wembanyama's all-time performance.

Oklahoma City was 8-0 in the playoffs before Monday's loss, the most dominant start to a postseason in Thunder franchise history. They have the depth, the defensive identity, and the home court advantage tonight. A bounce-back performance from SGA — and a better defensive scheme against Wembanyama — are the two critical adjustments that determine whether OKC evens the series at 1-1.

The Thunder's supporting cast, including Jalen Williams (who returned from injury for the WCF), will need to match the energy of the Spurs' collective effort from Game 1. Harper's 7-steal performance in particular disrupted Oklahoma City's offensive flow in ways that compounded Wembanyama's dominance.

What's Next

Tonight's game carries enormous weight for the Thunder. An 0-2 deficit heading to San Antonio would put Oklahoma City in a position that only a handful of teams have escaped from in playoff history. The home crowd at Paycom Center will be the loudest it has been all season. SGA has earned the right to be trusted in big moments, and this is the biggest moment of his career yet.

For the Spurs, winning on the road in Game 2 would be one of the great upsets in recent WCF history. Fox's potential return tonight adds another dimension to an already historic series. WCF Game 3 shifts to San Antonio, where Wembanyama and the Spurs will have their home crowd for the first time. But all of that only matters after tonight.
