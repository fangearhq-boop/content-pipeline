### Long-Form Post Today is the day NBA fans have been waiting for since the Knicks won their first championship in 53 years. At 6 PM ET, the free agency negotiating window officially opens — and the 2026 offseason goes into overdrive. The headline storyline is Karl-Anthony Towns and his $272 million extension with New York. The Knicks are exploring a middle-ground offer rather than the full max due to second-apron concerns, but KAT has said he's "ready to make a financial sacrifice." With Jalen Brunson's blueprint ($113 million less than max, Finals MVP) still fresh, this is Towns's moment to set the tone for the repeat bid. James Harden has already opted out of his $42.3 million deal with the Cleveland Cavaliers and is negotiating a new contract to stay in Cleveland. LeBron James — 41 years old and coming off a 4-0 sweep at the hands of Oklahoma City — is the biggest name in the UFA pool, with most analysts expecting him to return to the Lakers. Contracts can be agreed upon starting tonight but won't officially execute until July 6 at 12:01 PM ET when the moratorium lifts — meaning the next 24 hours are about positioning, not paperwork. The league's new map starts taking shape in the next few hours. This is Free Agency Day One. ### Image Post Caption Free agency opens at 6 PM ET. ### Long-Form Post The Ja Morant era in Memphis is officially over. The Grizzlies have traded their franchise point guard to the Portland Trail Blazers in a blockbuster deal. Per ESPN's Shams Charania, the Morant trade is Portland's "big move as of right now" — a clear signal that the Blazers chose this path over the Jaylen Brown sweepstakes. Morant averaged 19.5 points per game in just 20 games during the 2025-26 season before the trade rumors began dominating the headlines. The Suns were reported as the frontrunner for Morant's services, with the Celtics also circling in the wake of missing on Giannis Antetokounmpo. Portland swooped in and made the deal before free agency could reset everyone's plans. For Memphis, the rebuild is now fully underway.
