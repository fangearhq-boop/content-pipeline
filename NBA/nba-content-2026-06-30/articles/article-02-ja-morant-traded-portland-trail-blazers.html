Ja Morant Traded to Portland Trail Blazers: Blazers Make Their Big Swing

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | June 30, 2026

The Memphis Grizzlies have traded Ja Morant to the Portland Trail Blazers, delivering one of the most significant transactions of the 2026 offseason. Per ESPN's Shams Charania, the deal is Portland's "big move as of right now" — a declaration that the Blazers made their franchise-defining choice and it wasn't Jaylen Brown.

The move reshuffles the Western Conference and officially closes the book on Morant's tenure in Memphis.

Morant's Path to Portland

Morant's market had been one of the offseason's most closely watched. The Phoenix Suns were considered the frontrunner for his services, while the Boston Celtics were also monitoring the situation after falling short in the Giannis Antetokounmpo sweepstakes. Portland entered late and won.

Morant averaged 19.5 points per game in 20 appearances during the 2025-26 season before the trade discussions accelerated. His two-year, $87 million contract — $42.1 million next season and $44 million in 2027-28 — arrives in Portland at a price that gives the Blazers significant value relative to the impact he can provide when healthy.

The raw ability has never been in question. At his peak, Morant is among the most electric and dangerous guards in the NBA — a player capable of carrying a franchise, elevating teammates, and single-handedly willing teams to wins. Portland is betting on that version of Morant arriving in Oregon.

What Portland Gets

The Trail Blazers have been searching for their next cornerstone since Damian Lillard's departure to Milwaukee. Morant represents a high-upside, high-variance bet — the kind of player who can transform a franchise's identity and fan base almost immediately.

Portland now has a marquee name to build around heading into free agency. The Blazers made clear they were willing to compete for major trade targets — the Jaylen Brown framework they'd been involved with demanded four or more first-round picks. Morant's contract structure offers similar upside at a lower asset cost.

Memphis Resets

For the Grizzlies, the Morant trade represents a full commitment to the rebuild. Memphis has been patient in navigating Morant's injury history and trade rumors, but with free agency opening, they chose to move forward rather than invest further in an uncertain situation.

Memphis still holds the No. 3 overall pick from the 2026 NBA Draft — Cam Boozer, who went to the Grizzlies from Duke. The combination of that young talent, cap space from the Morant deal, and the assets they receive from Portland gives them a foundation to rebuild around youth rather than trying to compete in the short term.

The Grizzlies' best years under Morant were genuine and memorable. Grit and Grind gave way to Young and Explosive. Now they turn the page toward something new.

The Western Conference Gets Wilder

Morant in Portland changes the math in the West. The Oklahoma City Thunder won the Western Conference title this spring. The Denver Nuggets, Los Angeles Lakers, and Golden State Warriors all carry their own storylines into free agency. A Morant-led Blazers team won't challenge for a title immediately, but they become an immediate watchlist team — and in two years, when Morant is in his prime window, Portland could be a playoff threat.

What's Next

Morant's official Blazers debut comes when the moratorium lifts July 6. Portland will now turn its attention to rounding out the roster around their new star through free agency. The trade is done. The rebuild in Memphis begins. The Blazers have their guy.
