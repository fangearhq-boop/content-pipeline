Jaylen Brown's Trade Market Resets: Portland's Gone, Boston Needs a New Partner

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | June 30, 2026

The trade market for Jaylen Brown just changed dramatically — and not in Boston's favor.

The Portland Trail Blazers, who had been among the most frequently cited frontrunners to acquire the Celtics' star, completed the trade for Ja Morant from Memphis instead. Per ESPN's Shams Charania, Portland is now "not involved" with Brown — a pivot that forces the Celtics back to the drawing board with one of the NBA's most valuable trade assets.

The Player Boston Is Selling

Brown's market value is undeniable on paper. He averaged 28.7 points per game last season, earned All-NBA Second Team honors, and has three fully guaranteed seasons remaining on his contract at age 29. He is one of the few players in the league who can legitimately lead a team to a playoff series victory.

Brown has not requested a trade. He has remained professional and committed throughout the rumor cycle. But ESPN's Brian Windhorst has been direct: Brown "is going to get traded." The decision reflects Boston's front office reading the competitive window after falling short in the Giannis sweepstakes and reassessing what comes next.

Boston's Asking Price and Why Nobody's Met It

The Celtics have consistently sought four or more unprotected first-round picks for Brown. That asking price is staggering by league standards — and reflects how seriously Boston values what they're giving up. Brown is not a rental. He's not injury-prone. He's in his prime.

The Portland framework that circulated before the Morant trade would have sent Brown and Sam Hauser to the Blazers for Anfernee Simons, Scoot Henderson, Shaedon Sharpe, and four first-round picks. Brown would have been paired with Donovan Clingan and Toumani Camara as potential Boston targets. With Portland out, that entire framework is off the table.

The Celtics now need a team willing to meet that price — and do so quickly, before free agency reshuffles rosters in ways that change what teams have available to offer.

Who Steps In?

The short list of teams with the assets and motivation to pay four first-round picks for Brown has always been limited. Detroit was mentioned as a possibility in sign-and-trade scenarios. Minnesota was in preliminary conversations before the LaMelo Ball deal closed that door. With Portland now settled on Morant, the field of active suitors has narrowed considerably.

Teams in championship windows who feel one elite wing away from contention are the most logical fits. The next 48 hours — with the negotiating window now open — may reveal who's willing to step up and meet Boston's number.

What's Next

The Celtics are not under immediate pressure to move Brown. His contract is long-term and his value only grows if they hold him. But the front office has signaled a willingness to trade, the market knows it, and with free agency now underway, new partnerships and roster needs are emerging by the hour. Watch for new suitors to emerge before July 6, when trades officially execute and the league's offseason picture comes fully into focus.
