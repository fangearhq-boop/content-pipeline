Pistons-Cavaliers Game 3 Preview — Cleveland Needs a Win Before Cade Cunningham Takes Over the Series

By Damon Pierce | May 9, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Somewhere in Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse today, the Cleveland Cavaliers' season-long investments in veteran experience, defensive intensity, and Donovan Mitchell's brilliance will be tested in the most consequential setting of the year. Detroit Pistons-Cleveland Cavaliers Game 3 tips off at 3:00 PM ET on NBC, and the Cavaliers need a win badly — not just to avoid going down 0-3, but because Cade Cunningham is playing some of the best basketball of his career and the window to slow him down may be closing fast.

The Pistons lead the series 2-0 after wins of 111-101 and 107-97. Detroit's performances have been consistent: Cade takes over when it matters, the defense refuses to give ground, and the complementary players make enough plays to protect leads late.

Cade Cunningham's Series Has Been Exceptional

Cade Cunningham's stat lines through two games — 23 points and 7 assists in Game 1, 25 points and 10 assists in Game 2 — don't tell the complete story of how good he has been. His Game 2 fourth quarter is what Cleveland's coaching staff is studying relentlessly right now: 12 points, 8-for-8 from the free throw line, and the ice-blooded composure of a player who wanted the ball precisely when the pressure was highest.

Tobias Harris has been reliable alongside him, scoring 21 points in Game 2. Daniss Jenkins has contributed off the bench. Detroit's supporting cast doesn't need to star — it just needs to execute, because Cunningham does the heavy lifting in critical moments.

The Cavaliers Have Firepower — They Need to Use It Smarter

Cleveland is not without resources. Donovan Mitchell scored 31 points in Game 2 and has been brilliant. Jarrett Allen contributed 22 points in that same loss, working effectively in the paint. James Harden added 22 points off a veteran playmaking performance that nearly dragged Cleveland to a Game 2 win.

The problem isn't individual talent. It's the moments when the Cavaliers go cold at the worst times. In the fourth quarter of Game 2, Cleveland shot 0-for-11 from three-point range. Against a Detroit team that executes properly in transition and never gives up extra possessions, missing eleven straight three-pointers in the final period is a death sentence. Cleveland has to fix that today.

Home Court Should Matter

The Cavaliers won 64 games during the regular season. They were one of the Eastern Conference's best teams. Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse has been a fortress all year, with a crowd that generates genuine defensive noise and makes opponent communication difficult. Today, that crowd will be at its loudest and most urgent.

Whether Cleveland can channel that energy into a basketball advantage is the central question of the afternoon. Detroit has shown it can handle adverse road environments — the Pistons won a Game 7 in the first round and followed it by winning two straight against one of the East's elite teams. Noise alone doesn't slow this Detroit team down.

What's Next

A Cleveland win today makes this a 2-1 series with momentum potentially shifting back to the Cavaliers. A Detroit win puts Cleveland in an almost-impossible 0-3 hole and would essentially end the Cavaliers' season. The stakes could not be more clearly defined.

Game 4, if necessary, would follow Monday. For now, Cleveland needs to give Mitchell the right kind of looks early, fix the three-point woes, and find a way to make Cade Cunningham uncomfortable in a fourth quarter where he has thrived in both previous games. None of those are simple tasks — but all of them are necessary.
