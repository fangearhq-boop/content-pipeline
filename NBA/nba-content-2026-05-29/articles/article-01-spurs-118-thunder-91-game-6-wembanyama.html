Spurs Rout Thunder 118-91 in Game 6 as Wembanyama Dominates, Forces Game 7

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 29, 2026

Victor Wembanyama had 28 points, 10 rebounds, three blocks, and two steals, and the San Antonio Spurs delivered a statement blowout — routing the Oklahoma City Thunder 118-91 in Game 6 of the Western Conference Finals on Wednesday night at AT&T Center — evening the series at three games apiece and forcing a winner-take-all Game 7.

It was a performance that silenced every doubt about whether the 22-year-old Wembanyama could carry the Spurs in their biggest moments. He shot 4-of-9 from three-point range, managed the game with poise beyond his years, and played a dominant 28 minutes before Spurs coaches sat him with the outcome long decided and 9:20 remaining.

The Collapse That Broke OKC

The game turned on one catastrophic stretch in the third quarter. The Thunder went scoreless for seven straight minutes as San Antonio mounted a ferocious 20-0 run, turning a competitive game into an embarrassment. By the time the third quarter ended, the Spurs had outscored Oklahoma City 32-13 in the period and the game was effectively over.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander — the reigning back-to-back MVP — was at the center of the storm. He finished with 15 points on 6-of-18 shooting, committing 2 turnovers, and posting a game-worst minus-28 rating. In a series where SGA had been magnificent — including a 32-point masterpiece in Game 5 — Game 6 was his most difficult night as a professional.

"They made us uncomfortable," Thunder coach Mark Daigneault said. "When you go through a stretch like that in the third, it's hard to recover in a playoff game."

Wembanyama's Quiet Devastation

What made Wembanyama's performance so remarkable was how effortless it looked. He wasn't forcing anything. He attacked mismatches in the mid-post, made the right reads in the pick-and-roll, and guarded everything at the other end of the floor. His 2 steals and 3 blocks were just the visible part of his defensive impact — the Paycom Center crowd that will be rocking on Saturday was already silenced on this night in San Antonio.

Wembanyama's numbers this series have been extraordinary. In Game 1, he erupted for 41 points and 24 rebounds in a double-overtime victory — a performance that put him alongside Wilt Chamberlain as the only players to record 40-plus points and 20-plus rebounds in a Conference Finals debut. In Game 6, he was more controlled but equally decisive.

"Vic was just locked in from the beginning," Spurs guard De'Aaron Fox said. "When he plays like that, we're a different team."

What's Next

Game 7 takes place Saturday, May 30, at 7 PM ET at the Paycom Center in Oklahoma City on NBC/Peacock. The Thunder enter as 3.5-point favorites at home, where they went 34-7 in the regular season and are 6-1 during these playoffs. The Spurs are 5-3 on the road in the postseason.

The winner advances to the NBA Finals to face the New York Knicks, who have been resting for more than 12 days after sweeping the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Finals. Because both the Thunder and Spurs had better regular-season records than the Knicks, the Western Conference representative will have home-court advantage throughout the Finals.

Game 1 of the NBA Finals is scheduled for Wednesday, June 3, at 8:30 PM ET on ABC.

For the Spurs, one win separates them from their first Finals appearance since 2014. For the Thunder, one win secures back-to-back Finals trips. Both teams know what's at stake. Saturday night, only one goes home happy.
