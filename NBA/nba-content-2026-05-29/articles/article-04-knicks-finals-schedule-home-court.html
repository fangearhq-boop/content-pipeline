Knicks Finals Bound: Full Schedule, Home Court Picture, and the Long Wait Is Almost Over

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 29, 2026

While Oklahoma City and San Antonio prepare for a winner-take-all Game 7 on Saturday night, the New York Knicks are doing what they've spent the last 27 years dreaming about — waiting for the NBA Finals to begin.

The Knicks swept the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Finals, extending their playoff winning streak to 11 consecutive games. Since May 17, they've been resting, recovering, and watching — and by the time they tip off Game 1 of the NBA Finals on June 3, they'll have had more than 12 days to get healthy and hungry.

The Full NBA Finals Schedule

Regardless of whether Oklahoma City or San Antonio wins Game 7, the NBA Finals calendar is locked in. Here are the dates and times New York fans need to know:

  • Game 1: Wednesday, June 3 — 8:30 PM ET on ABC
  • Game 2: Friday, June 5 — 8:30 PM ET on ABC
  • Game 3: Monday, June 8 — 8:30 PM ET on ABC
  • Game 4: Wednesday, June 10 — 8:30 PM ET on ABC
  • Game 5: Saturday, June 13 (if needed) — 8:30 PM ET on ABC

The Home Court Question

One significant fact in the Knicks' Finals picture: New York will not have home court advantage, no matter who wins Game 7. The Thunder finished 64-18 in the regular season and the Spurs finished 62-20 — both superior to the Knicks' 53-29 record. The Western Conference representative earns home court for the duration of the series.

That means Games 1 and 2 will be played in the West — either Oklahoma City's Paycom Center or San Antonio's AT&T Center. The Knicks would host Games 3, 4, and 5 (if necessary) at Madison Square Garden before the series potentially returns to the West for Games 6 and 7.

The lack of home court is significant, but the Knicks' playoff résumé suggests they aren't intimidated by road environments. New York won on the road multiple times in this postseason run, including a pivotal road win that helped them put away the Cleveland Cavaliers.

The Knicks' Strengths Heading In

Rest is the most obvious advantage New York carries. Jalen Brunson — the Knicks' heart and engine — averaged 27.8 points and 6.7 assists in the ECF sweep of Cleveland. OG Anannoby, who had been managing a hamstring issue, has had over 12 days to recover. The Knicks' defensive identity — the thing that made them the best team in the Eastern Conference — should be sharp and ready.

New York is returning to the Finals for the first time since 1999, when Patrick Ewing and Allan Houston led them past the Indiana Pacers in the East. That team was swept by the San Antonio Spurs in the Finals. The basketball universe doesn't care about symmetry, but it certainly notes it.

What's Next

The Knicks find out their opponent Saturday night when Oklahoma City hosts San Antonio in WCF Game 7 at 7 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. By Sunday morning, the full Finals picture will be clear — including which city hosts Games 1 and 2 and how MSG will factor in.

For New York, the message is simple: 27 years of waiting, 11 straight playoff wins, and a city that has been dreaming of this moment since the turn of the millennium. Now the Finals are six days away. The Knicks' long wait is almost over.
