WCF Game 7 Preview: Thunder vs. Spurs — One Game for the NBA Finals

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 29, 2026

It all comes down to this. After six remarkable games — including a 2-overtime thriller in Game 1, a blistering Game 5 comeback by the Thunder, and a stunning 27-point blowout by the Spurs in Game 6 — the Western Conference Finals will be decided in a single winner-take-all game Saturday night.

Game 7: Oklahoma City Thunder vs. San Antonio Spurs. Saturday, May 30. 7 PM ET. Paycom Center, Oklahoma City. NBC/Peacock. The New York Knicks are watching from MSG, waiting to learn who they'll face in the NBA Finals.

The Home Court Factor

The Thunder enter as 3.5-point favorites at home, with a moneyline of -155. The Spurs are +130 underdogs, and the game total sits at 212.5 points. These odds reflect one overwhelming reality: the Paycom Center is one of the most hostile environments in basketball.

Oklahoma City finished the regular season 34-7 at home — the best home record in the entire NBA. In these playoffs, the Thunder are 6-1 at Paycom Center, with their lone loss coming in the first round. Every time they've played at home in this WCF series, they've won.

San Antonio counters with genuine playoff road experience. The Spurs are 5-3 away from AT&T Center this postseason, and they have already proven they can silence hostile crowds — including in Game 1 of this series, when they went into OKC and stole a double-overtime victory.

The Key Matchup: SGA vs. Wembanyama

Every Game 7 in history comes down to superstars. This one features two of the most remarkable players of their generation.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander was historically bad in Game 6 — 15 points, 6-of-18 shooting, -28 rating. For context, SGA averaged 31.1 points per game in the regular season and just won back-to-back MVP awards. His WCF series has seen him swing from dominant (32 points in Game 5) to invisible (Game 6). At home in Oklahoma City, with everything on the line, expect the real SGA to show up.

Victor Wembanyama, meanwhile, has been the defining force of this entire series. He scored 41 points and grabbed 24 rebounds in a 2-overtime Game 1 win and was dominant again in Game 6 with 28 points, 10 rebounds, and 3 blocks. The question for Game 7 isn't whether Wembanyama will be good — it's whether the Spurs can get enough from the rest of their roster on the road.

What Each Team Needs

Thunder: SGA must recapture his MVP form. The Thunder also need contributions from Jalen Williams, Chet Holmgren, and Isaiah Joe. Oklahoma City's defense — which strangled the Spurs in Games 4 and 5 — must be the identity they return to. They cannot afford another seven-minute scoreless stretch like they suffered in Game 6.

Spurs: Wembanyama needs support. Dylan Harper and Stephon Castle must be aggressive early to keep pressure off of Wembanyama one-on-one. San Antonio's effort to exploit the Thunder's transition defense — something they did brilliantly in Game 6 — must translate to a hostile road environment.

What's Next

The winner of Game 7 advances to face the New York Knicks in the NBA Finals starting June 3 (8:30 PM ET, ABC). The Knicks have been resting for more than 12 days after their ECF sweep of Cleveland, riding an 11-game winning streak entering the Finals.

Crucially, both the Thunder (64-18) and the Spurs (62-20) had superior regular-season records to the Knicks (53-29), meaning whichever team wins Game 7 will have home-court advantage in the NBA Finals. Games 1 and 2 will be in the Western city — whether that's Oklahoma City or San Antonio.

This is the game that determines legacies. Victor Wembanyama trying to send a 22-year-old Spurs squad to its first Finals since 2014. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander trying to defend his championship and prove his back-to-back MVPs mean something in the biggest moments. Saturday night, one of these stories ends, and the other one gets a Finals stage.
