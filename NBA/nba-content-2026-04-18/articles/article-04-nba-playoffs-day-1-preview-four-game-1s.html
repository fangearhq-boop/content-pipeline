NBA Playoffs Day 1: Your Complete Guide to Four First-Round Game 1s

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 18, 2026

Championship basketball is officially here. Saturday's slate features Raptors-Cavs, Wolves-Nuggets, Hawks-Knicks, and the highly anticipated Lakers-Rockets showdown — here's what to watch for in each series opener.

The 2026 NBA Playoffs begin Saturday with four first-round Game 1s spread across the afternoon and evening. With the play-in tournament complete — the Phoenix Suns and Orlando Magic earned the final two spots Friday night — all 16 postseason teams are locked in and ready to go. Here is your guide to every matchup on today's slate.

No. 5 Toronto Raptors at No. 4 Cleveland Cavaliers — 1 PM ET (Amazon Prime Video)

Toronto enters this series as a mild upset threat after going 3-0 against Cleveland during the regular season by an average of 11 points. Raptors basketball has evolved into one of the East's more unpredictable propositions — they move the ball, defend with discipline, and don't back down from big moments.

But the Cavaliers have responded to adversity all season. Cleveland went 13-3 at home in its last 16 regular-season games, and Donovan Mitchell — averaging 27.9 points per game this year — enters the postseason as one of the most dangerous offensive players in the East. Mitchell averaged 24.0 points per game against Toronto specifically, so he knows this opponent well.

The series hinges on whether Cleveland can impose home-court advantage on a Raptors team with nothing to lose and a roster built for playoff basketball. Game 1 set the tone for everything that follows.

No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves at No. 3 Denver Nuggets — 3:30 PM ET (Amazon Prime Video)

This is a rematch that requires no introduction for anyone who watched the 2022-23 and 2023-24 playoffs. Nikola Jokic and Anthony Edwards have developed one of the NBA's most compelling postseason rivalries over the past three years, and Saturday's Game 1 at Ball Arena will be the latest chapter.

Denver brings interior dominance and championship pedigree. Minnesota brings explosive athleticism and a defensive identity that has proven capable of slowing even the league's best offenses. Both teams finished above .500 and earned high seeds — this is legitimate playoff basketball from the opening possession.

No. 6 Atlanta Hawks at No. 3 New York Knicks — 6 PM ET (Amazon Prime Video)

This is the matchup that most surprised NBA fans when the bracket was finalized. The Atlanta Hawks reached the playoffs after trading Trae Young to the Washington Wizards and replacing him with veteran guard CJ McCollum, a move that stabilized the locker room and added the kind of calm leadership that plays well in April and May.

The Knicks, seeded third at 53-29, are the favorites with good reason. Karl-Anthony Towns averaged 28.5 points and 15.5 rebounds in his two meetings with Atlanta this season, and the Madison Square Garden crowd will be one of the loudest environments in the entire first round. New York is installed as -205 Game 1 favorites and -280 to win the series.

But Atlanta's underdog identity is real. OG Anunoby — the Knicks' defensive X-factor — was nursing an ankle sprain entering this series. If he's not fully healthy, Atlanta has the shooting and guile to make New York uncomfortable at home.

No. 5 Houston Rockets at No. 4 Los Angeles Lakers — 8:30 PM ET (ABC)

The marquee game of the entire first-round slate, and not entirely for the right reasons. Luka Doncic (Grade 2 hamstring) and Austin Reaves (Grade 2 oblique) are both out for Game 1, leaving LeBron James — at 41 years old — as LA's primary offensive engine against a fully loaded Rockets squad led by Kevin Durant.

Houston finished just one game back of Los Angeles in the standings but has been the more complete team in all three regular-season matchups, consistently dominating the paint. Doncic and Reaves combined for 56.8 points per game this year. Without that production, the Rockets' path to a first-round victory becomes dramatically clearer.

The drama, of course, is LeBron. He has defied age, injury, and circumstance throughout his 22-year career. Whether he can do it again — without his two best teammates, against a healthy opponent — is the most compelling question on the board tonight.

What's Next

Today's four Game 1s set the narrative trajectory for the entire first round. Sunday brings four more openers: Pistons vs. Magic, Celtics vs. 76ers, Thunder vs. Suns, and Spurs vs. Blazers. The 2026 NBA playoffs are officially underway — and based on the play-in tournament drama that ended just last night, this postseason has every ingredient for memorable basketball.
