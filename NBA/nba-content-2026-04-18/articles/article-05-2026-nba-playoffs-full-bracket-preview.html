2026 NBA Playoffs Full Bracket Preview: Eight Series, One Championship

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 18, 2026

All 16 teams are set after the play-in tournament concluded Friday. Here is every first-round series broken down — from the Thunder's title defense to the Suns' record-breaking upset bid.

The 2026 SoFi NBA Play-In Tournament is complete, and the full postseason bracket is locked. Jalen Green's eight-three-pointer night gave the Phoenix Suns the final Western Conference spot, while Paolo Banchero's dominant performance secured Orlando as the No. 8 seed in the East. Now the real competition begins: eight best-of-seven series, a 16-team field, and one championship at the end.

Eastern Conference First Round

No. 1 Detroit Pistons vs. No. 8 Orlando Magic

Detroit (60-22) had the best record in the Eastern Conference this season — a 10-win improvement over the previous year that has established the Pistons as the East's most complete team top-to-bottom. Orlando arrives in this series as the No. 8 seed, but no one who watched Friday's 121-90 play-in demolition of Charlotte will mistake them for a pushover. The Magic went 2-2 vs. Detroit in the regular season, and Banchero is playing his best basketball right now. Series begins Sunday.

No. 2 Boston Celtics vs. No. 7 Philadelphia 76ers — April 19, 1 PM ET

Boston's title defense begins Sunday against a 76ers team still clouded by Joel Embiid's health situation. Embiid missed the play-in entirely following appendix surgery and has provided no public timeline for his return. The Celtics, built to repeat and reinforced over the offseason, are substantial favorites to advance — but the 76ers with a healthy Embiid are a different animal entirely.

No. 3 New York Knicks vs. No. 6 Atlanta Hawks

The Knicks are +600 to represent the East in the Finals, a reflection of the belief in New York's core: Jalen Brunson (29.3 PPG vs. Atlanta in regular season), Karl-Anthony Towns, and OG Anunoby form one of the East's deepest trios. Atlanta's transformation — built around CJ McCollum after trading Trae Young to Washington — is this postseason's great curiosity. The Hawks reached the playoffs against expectations; now they have to win once they're here. Game 1 tips tonight at 6 PM ET.

No. 4 Cleveland Cavaliers vs. No. 5 Toronto Raptors

Cleveland's Donovan Mitchell (27.9 PPG) faces a Raptors team that beat him three times this season. Toronto's ball movement and defensive structure gives them a legitimate path to an upset, but the Cavs' 13-3 home record in the final stretch of the season suggests they know how to protect home court when it matters. Game 1 begins at 1 PM ET today.

Western Conference First Round

No. 1 Oklahoma City Thunder vs. No. 8 Phoenix Suns — April 19, 3:30 PM ET (ABC)

The defending NBA champions and three-time No. 1 seed host a Suns team that 48 hours ago was in elimination territory. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander — back-to-back MVP, 31.6 points per game this season — leads an Oklahoma City roster built for sustained dominance. The Thunder are -3000 series favorites, the largest first-round spread in recent memory. But Jalen Green just tied an all-time record. Don't touch that remote on Sunday afternoon.

No. 2 San Antonio Spurs vs. No. 7 Portland Trail Blazers — April 19, 9 PM ET (NBC)

Victor Wembanyama vs. Scoot Henderson: two generational talents drafted back-to-back at No. 1 and No. 3 overall, now meeting in the postseason for the first time. Wembanyama (San Antonio's 22-year-old anchor) nearly won Defensive Player of the Year this season. Henderson (Portland's point guard) led the Blazers to the No. 7 seed in a loaded Western Conference. The youth movement in the West is on full display Sunday night.

No. 3 Denver Nuggets vs. No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves

Jokic. Edwards. Mountains and timberwolves and the best rivalry the Western Conference has produced in years. Denver brings three-time MVP Nikola Jokic and home-court advantage at altitude. Minnesota counters with the electric Anthony Edwards and a defensive system capable of slowing anyone. This series should go the distance, and Game 1 tips today at 3:30 PM ET.

No. 4 Los Angeles Lakers vs. No. 5 Houston Rockets

Luka Doncic and Austin Reaves are out. Kevin Durant is almost certainly in. LeBron James, 41 years old, is expected to play through illness. The Lakers are +550 underdogs. Game 1 is tonight at 8:30 PM ET on ABC. There is no story in these playoffs with more dramatic potential than what happens inside Crypto.com Arena over the next two weeks.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Playoffs could run through late June if all eight series go the maximum seven games. Four Game 1s tip today (April 18), and four more follow on Sunday (April 19). The road to the championship begins right now — and based on everything the play-in tournament demonstrated, this postseason will not lack for drama, upsets, or unforgettable moments. Pick your team, clear your schedule, and settle in.
