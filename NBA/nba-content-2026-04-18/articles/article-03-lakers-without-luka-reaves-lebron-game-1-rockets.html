Lakers Without Luka Doncic and Austin Reaves: Can LeBron Carry LA Past the Rockets in Game 1?

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 18, 2026

Both starters suffered Grade 2 injuries on April 2 and won't play tonight. LeBron James, 41, faces the Rockets alone — and Vegas has LA as a +550 underdog for the series.

The Los Angeles Lakers are entering the 2026 NBA Playoffs as a wounded team. When Game 1 tips off tonight against the Houston Rockets at 8:30 PM ET on ABC, two of the team's three best players will be watching from the bench in street clothes — and the franchise's hope rests entirely on the shoulders of its 41-year-old superstar.

Luka Doncic suffered a Grade 2 left hamstring strain on April 2 in a game against the Oklahoma City Thunder. He flew to Madrid, Spain for specialized stem cell treatment in the weeks that followed, and while he has returned to Los Angeles to be with the team, the Lakers do not expect him to play a single minute in the first round. The most realistic target for his return is the second round — if Los Angeles can get there.

Austin Reaves sustained a Grade 2 left oblique muscle strain in the same April 2 game against Oklahoma City. His recovery timeline runs parallel to Doncic's: out for the majority of Round 1, with his earliest possible return in the first week of May. Together, Doncic and Reaves averaged 56.8 points per game during the regular season. Both of those contributions evaporate tonight.

LeBron's Moment — Again

LeBron James is 41 years old and has been battling an illness this week. He is expected to play. He always plays.

This is not new territory for LeBron — carrying impossible loads in the playoffs is something he has done on six separate finals trips, winning four championships along the way. But this may be the steepest climb yet: a shorthanded roster, a healthy opponent, and an age that no NBA player has ever overcome at this level of the game.

The Lakers will lean on Luke Kennard for additional ball-handling, Jake LaRavia for defensive assignments, and Rui Hachimura to knock down open looks created by whatever gravity LeBron can generate. The margin for error is essentially zero — every possession will require perfect execution from a roster that has had limited time to practice without its two starting contributors.

Houston's Opportunity

The Rockets enter Game 1 with their own injury concerns — Steven Adams (left ankle surgery) and Fred VanVleet (ACL surgery) are both out — but Kevin Durant, listed as questionable with a knee contusion, is fully expected to play. Durant, acquired from Phoenix in last summer's blockbuster seven-team trade that sent Jalen Green to the Suns, has been Houston's offensive anchor throughout the season.

Houston has owned the paint against Los Angeles in all three regular-season meetings, winning the rebounding battle each time. Without Doncic's playmaking and Reaves' off-ball movement, LA's spacing and interior defense become more predictable — and the Rockets have the personnel to exploit exactly that.

Vegas reflects the reality: the Lakers are +550 underdogs for the series. That number implies a roughly 15 percent chance of Los Angeles advancing. For most teams, those would be insurmountable odds. For a team with LeBron James, they are merely steep ones.

What's Next

Game 1 tips off tonight at 8:30 PM ET on ABC. If the Lakers can somehow steal a game in the first round, both Doncic and Reaves become more realistic options as the series progresses into early May. The optimistic scenario has LA stealing Game 1 or 2 on energy and LeBron's greatness, then welcoming back at least Reaves as a reinforcement for the back half of the series.

That is the best-case scenario. The more sobering reality is that Houston is better-equipped, deeper, and healthier entering this series. But if LeBron James taught the NBA anything across three decades, it is that the scoreboard doesn't care about injury reports — and neither does he.
