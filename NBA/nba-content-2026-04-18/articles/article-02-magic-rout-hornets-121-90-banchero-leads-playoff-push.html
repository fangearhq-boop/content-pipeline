Magic Rout Hornets 121-90: Banchero Leads Playoff Clinch as Charlotte's Decade of Darkness Continues

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 18, 2026

Orlando dominated from tip to buzzer, outscoring Charlotte 64-28 in the paint — and the Hornets have now missed the playoffs for 10 consecutive seasons, the longest active drought in the NBA.

Paolo Banchero scored 12 of his 25 points before the first quarter ended Friday night, and the Orlando Magic never stopped accelerating. By halftime they led by 35. By the final buzzer, they had secured the No. 8 seed in the Eastern Conference with a 121-90 demolition of the Charlotte Hornets that was never remotely in question.

Banchero finished with 25 points, 5 rebounds, 6 assists, 2 steals, and a block — the kind of complete, high-efficiency outing that has defined his ascent from lottery pick to legitimate franchise cornerstone. Franz Wagner added 18 points, 7 rebounds, and 6 assists. Wendell Carter Jr. chipped in 16 points and 6 boards. All five Orlando starters scored in double figures, a testament to a balanced attack that has been building toward this moment all season.

Dominance From the Opening Tip

The Magic shot 67 percent from the field in the first quarter, going 16-of-24 as they raced to a 38-16 lead. Charlotte's defense had no answer for Banchero's combination of size, skill, and vision — he was operating at a level that recalled the best stretches of his young career.

Orlando's advantage in the paint was particularly crushing. The Magic outscored the Hornets 64-28 in the paint, a nearly three-to-one margin that reflected an enormous physical and tactical edge. Charlotte turned the ball over 20 times, and Orlando converted those mistakes into 26 easy points, turning each Hornets possession into a fast-break opportunity at the other end.

LaMelo Ball did his best against the tide, finishing with 23 points in a losing effort. Miles Bridges added 15, and Brandon Miller contributed 14 on 4-of-7 from three-point range. But individual performances became largely academic once the Magic's lead passed 30 points early in the second quarter.

A Decade Without the Postseason

The defeat extended a deeply painful chapter for the Charlotte Hornets franchise. With this loss, Charlotte has now missed the NBA playoffs for 10 consecutive seasons — the longest active drought in professional basketball. Even with LaMelo Ball representing one of the league's most exciting young talents, the Hornets have been unable to break through to the postseason in what has become a frustrating organizational cycle.

Ball, 24, has been the face of Charlotte's attempts to rebuild around a generational talent. His 23-point effort Friday was emblematic of the problem: individually brilliant, collectively insufficient. The questions about roster construction, coaching, and front-office vision will dominate the offseason for a franchise that has now watched 10 consecutive spring tournaments begin without them.

What's Next

For the Magic, the reward is a first-round date with the Detroit Pistons — the No. 1 seed in the Eastern Conference with a 60-22 regular-season record, the best mark in the East. Game 1 begins Sunday, and the Pistons will carry home-court advantage through at least the conference finals if they advance.

The Magic went 2-2 against Detroit during the regular season, which suggests this won't be a mismatch. Banchero's performance Friday was a statement: Orlando arrives in the postseason at full strength, with their leader operating at an elite level. The Pistons will be a formidable test, but after Friday's clinic, it's impossible not to feel optimistic about where this Magic team is headed.
