Jalen Green's 36-Point Record Night Sends Suns Past Warriors 111-96

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 18, 2026

Green tied the all-time play-in record with 8 three-pointers as Phoenix locked up the No. 8 seed — and ended Stephen Curry's season in stunning fashion.

Jalen Green had a message for the NBA on Friday night, and he delivered it with eight three-pointers. The Phoenix Suns guard tied the all-time play-in tournament record — matching Keegan Murray's mark — on the way to 36 points, powering Phoenix past the Golden State Warriors 111-96 and securing the No. 8 seed in the Western Conference.

The Suns never trailed. Phoenix jumped to a 13-2 lead before Golden State could blink, and the Warriors spent the rest of the night trying — and largely failing — to find any answer for Green's relentless offensive arsenal.

Green shot 14-of-20 from the field in total, a remarkable efficiency mark for a player who had come under scrutiny for stretches of inconsistency earlier in the season. His three-point barrage — 8-of-14 from beyond the arc — left the Paycom Arena faithful in awe even before fans realize this was a road performance.

A Career-Defining Moment

Green arrived in Phoenix last summer as part of a blockbuster seven-team deal that sent Kevin Durant to Houston. The transition wasn't seamless — Green admitted publicly that he was "hurt" by his departure from the Rockets — but Friday's performance underscored exactly why Phoenix was willing to make him a cornerstone of their rebuild.

Jordan Goodwin delivered a secondary performance that nearly went unnoticed alongside Green's heroics, recording 19 points on 7-of-11 shooting with 9 rebounds and 6 steals. The Suns' defensive intensity proved particularly decisive: Phoenix forced 20 Golden State turnovers and converted them into 30 points, a swing that effectively ended the game before halftime.

Curry's Nightmare

For Stephen Curry, Friday night was a sobering reminder that basketball's greatest shooters are not immune to cold nights on the biggest stages. The 38-year-old Golden State icon shot just 4-of-16 from the field, finishing with 17 points, 4 assists, and 4 turnovers in what may have been the last game of his 2025-26 season.

The Warriors' $62.6 million man was never able to find rhythm, partly because Phoenix's defense — led by Goodwin's 6-steal night — disrupted Golden State's ball movement from the opening tip. The Warriors, who had defeated the Los Angeles Clippers 126-121 in Night 2 of the play-in behind Curry's 35-point explosion, could not replicate that magic against a better-prepared Suns defense.

Golden State's season ends here, their last realistic path to the postseason blocked by a team that arrived in Phoenix without its original superstar and has spent the year rebuilding an identity around Green's scoring and Goodwin's energy.

What's Next

The Suns' reward is the most daunting possible first-round opponent: the Oklahoma City Thunder, defending NBA champions and the No. 1 seed in the Western Conference for the third consecutive season. Game 1 tips off Sunday, April 19 at 3:30 PM ET on ABC at Paycom Arena in Oklahoma City.

Oklahoma City is installed as a -3000 favorite to win the series, reflecting an enormous talent gap on paper. But Jalen Green arrived at this play-in as a near-afterthought and tied a record that has stood across multiple postseasons. In the NBA playoffs, particularly in a first round that has produced historic upsets in years past, nothing is guaranteed. Green's Friday night suggests Phoenix is more dangerous than anyone expected — and SGA's Thunder should take that seriously.
