### STORY 1: Pistons Complete Historic 3-1 Comeback — Cade 32/12, Harris 30, First Series Win Since 2008

**Long-Form Post**
The Detroit Pistons are going to the second round of the NBA Playoffs for the first time since 2008 — and they did it in the most dramatic fashion imaginable.

Down 3-1 to the Orlando Magic, with their season seemingly over, Detroit won three consecutive elimination games to complete one of the rarest comebacks in NBA history. Only 14 other teams had ever done it before. On Sunday, the Pistons made it 15 with a dominant 116-94 rout in Game 7.

Tobias Harris was the unexpected hero, pouring in a playoff career-high 30 points on 11-of-18 shooting, including five three-pointers, adding nine rebounds and three steals. Then Cade Cunningham took over with 32 points and 12 assists — joining Isiah Thomas as the only Pistons in franchise history with 20-plus points and 10-plus assists in a Game 7. Together they became the first Detroit teammates to each score 30 points in a playoff game since Bob Lanier and Howard Porter in 1977.

Paolo Banchero gave everything he had — 38 points on the losing end — but the rest of the Magic shot just 10-of-37 from the field. Cade and Harris were simply too much.

The Pistons now face the Cavaliers in Round 2, starting Tuesday in Detroit. After 18 years, the city of Detroit has a playoff team worth believing in again.

It was his third quarter that decided this series. With the Cavaliers needing a momentum shift after trailing by 10 in the second quarter, Allen grabbed 10 rebounds — five of them offensive — as Cleveland outscored Toronto 38-19 in the period to take complete control.

The Cavaliers advance as the No. 4 seed to face the No. 1 Detroit Pistons, who won their own Game 7 earlier in the day.
