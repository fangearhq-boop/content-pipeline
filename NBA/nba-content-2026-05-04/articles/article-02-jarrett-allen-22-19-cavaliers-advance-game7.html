Allen's 22 Points and 19 Rebounds Carry Cavaliers to Game 7 Victory, East Semifinals Await

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 4, 2026

CLEVELAND — When the Cavaliers needed someone to step up in Game 7, it was not Donovan Mitchell who seized the moment. It was not Evan Mobley. It was Jarrett Allen — tying his career playoff best with 22 points and grabbing 19 rebounds as Cleveland dispatched the Toronto Raptors 114-102 to advance to the Eastern Conference Semifinals.

For three quarters, the game hung in the balance. Toronto led by as many as 10 points in the second quarter, and the arena was tense. Then Cleveland began to find its rhythm, closing the half on an 11-2 run. When the third quarter opened, the Cavs continued that momentum immediately with an 11-1 burst — and at the center of all of it was Allen, his massive frame crashing the glass time and again.

Allen scored 14 of his 22 points in the third quarter alone. He hauled in 10 of his 19 rebounds in that single period — five of them on the offensive glass — as Cleveland outscored Toronto 38-19 in the quarter. It was the kind of performance that does not end up in NBA highlight packages, but it is the kind that wins playoff games.

"Big fella was a monster tonight," Mitchell said after the game. "We needed him and he was there."

A Resilient Cavaliers Squad Survives

Mitchell finished with 22 points to complement Allen's effort, while James Harden added 18 points in the victory. The Cavaliers, who at various points in this series had to survive a 29-foot overtime buzzer-beater from RJ Barrett in Game 6 just to force a Game 7, showed they can handle adversity when it matters most.

The Raptors fought hard to the end. Scottie Barnes scored 24 points with nine rebounds, and Barrett added 23 in a tremendous individual effort. But once Cleveland built its lead to 19 in the third and eventually to 22 in the fourth, the outcome was no longer in doubt.

Toronto had been one of the most surprising stories in this first round — a team many expected to be easy prey that pushed Cleveland all the way to a deciding seventh game. The Raptors' young stars earned respect throughout this series. It just wasn't enough on Sunday.

The No. 4 Seed Goes North

Cleveland advances as the No. 4 seed to face the No. 1 Detroit Pistons, who won their own Game 7 earlier Sunday with a 116-94 victory over the Orlando Magic. The Pistons, in the midst of their own historic moment — their first playoff series win since 2008 — will host the Cavaliers beginning Tuesday.

It is a matchup of contrasting stories. Detroit is the top seed, a team that won 60-plus games during the regular season, with Cade Cunningham establishing himself as one of the premier players in the Eastern Conference. Cleveland is a battle-tested unit that has overcome adversity at every turn to get here.

What's Next

Game 1 of the Cavaliers-Pistons Eastern Conference Semifinal series tips off Tuesday, May 5, at Little Caesars Arena in Detroit. Tip-off is scheduled for 7 p.m. ET on Peacock/NBC. For Jarrett Allen, whose performance on Sunday was a reminder of what he can do when locked in, Tuesday represents the next opportunity to keep being the unsung engine that keeps Cleveland's run alive.
