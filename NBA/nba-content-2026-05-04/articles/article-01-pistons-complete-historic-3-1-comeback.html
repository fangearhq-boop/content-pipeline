Cade and Harris Power Detroit's Historic Comeback — Pistons Advance for First Time Since 2008

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 4, 2026

DETROIT — The Detroit Pistons are going to the second round, and they did it the hard way.

Down 3-1 to the Orlando Magic and seemingly headed toward a first-round exit, Detroit won three consecutive must-win games — capping the run with a dominant 116-94 victory in Game 7 on Sunday to clinch the series. It is the franchise's first playoff series win in 18 years and only the 15th time in NBA history that a team has recovered from a 3-1 deficit to advance.

Cade Cunningham delivered the finest performance of his playoff career: 32 points on 10-of-18 shooting, 12 assists, and two blocks. In doing so, he joined Isiah Thomas as the only players in Detroit franchise history to record at least 20 points and 10 assists in a Game 7. That is the company Cunningham is now keeping.

But the real surprise hero was Tobias Harris, who had a first half that will be talked about in Detroit for years. Harris finished with a playoff career-high 30 points on 11-of-18 from the field, including five three-pointers, and added nine rebounds, three steals, and a block. He and Cunningham became the first Detroit teammates to each score 30 points in a playoff game since Bob Lanier (33) and Howard Porter (30) turned in that feat against the Golden State Warriors on April 17, 1977.

Orlando Ran Out of Magic

Paolo Banchero gave the Magic everything he had. The No. 1 overall pick from 2022 finished with 38 points and nine rebounds on 12-of-22 shooting, an enormous individual effort on a losing night. But he had almost no help. The rest of the Magic roster shot a combined 10-of-37 from the field, unable to generate consistent offense against Detroit's swarming defense.

Daniss Jenkins contributed 16 points off the Pistons' bench and Jalen Duren added 15 points and 15 rebounds, giving Detroit a physical advantage in the paint that Orlando simply could not overcome. The Pistons separated in the third quarter, and by the time the final buzzer sounded, a 22-point margin told the full story.

Desmond Bane added 16 points for Orlando, but it was not nearly enough. The Magic's season is over. Detroit's just got a lot more interesting.

18 Years in the Making

The Pistons' last playoff series win came in 2008, when the franchise was still operating on the residual reputation of the 2004 championship team. Since then, there have been rebuilds, lottery picks, coaching changes, and a painful decade-plus of irrelevance. Now, with Cunningham as the face and a deep supporting cast built around him, Detroit has re-established itself as a genuine postseason threat.

The comeback from 3-1 also cannot be understated. When Orlando took that series lead after Game 4, the numbers were brutal: teams that go up 3-1 win the series at a 96.3% clip in NBA playoff history. Detroit became one of the rare exceptions, joining some elite historical company.

"This team doesn't quit. That's what I've always believed about this group," Cunningham said after the game. "We knew what we were capable of."

What's Next

The Pistons now face the Cleveland Cavaliers in the Eastern Conference Semifinals. Cleveland, the No. 4 seed, also needed a Game 7 to advance, beating the Toronto Raptors 114-102 on Sunday evening. Game 1 of the Cavs-Pistons series tips off Tuesday night at Little Caesars Arena in Detroit at 7 p.m. ET on Peacock/NBC.

For a franchise that has waited 18 years to celebrate a playoff series victory, the celebration will be brief. There is more basketball to play — and this Detroit team has every intention of going further.
