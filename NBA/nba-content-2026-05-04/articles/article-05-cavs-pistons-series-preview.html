Both Needed a Game 7 to Get Here: Cavaliers vs. Pistons Is the Eastern Conference's Most Compelling Semifinal

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 4, 2026

DETROIT — On Sunday afternoon, the Detroit Pistons won a Game 7. A few hours later, the Cleveland Cavaliers won one too. On Tuesday night, they will play each other.

The Eastern Conference Semifinals matchup between Cleveland and Detroit is loaded with story lines, and it begins at Little Caesars Arena at 7 p.m. ET. Both teams are still riding the emotional high of their respective Game 7 victories — Detroit over Orlando, Cleveland over Toronto — and both will have to quickly reset and find another gear for a series that figures to be physical, tactical, and intensely competitive.

The Case for Detroit

The Pistons are the No. 1 seed in the Eastern Conference, and they earned that status with one of the most convincing regular seasons in franchise history. Cade Cunningham is playing at an All-NBA level — he posted 32 points and 12 assists in Game 7 against Orlando, a performance that places him in franchise historical company alongside Isiah Thomas. Tobias Harris has been a revelation, turning back the clock at age 34 with a playoff career-high 30 points in the series-clinching win.

Jalen Duren is one of the most physical young big men in the East — he posted 15 points and 15 rebounds in Game 7 — and provides Detroit a frontcourt presence that will test Cleveland's interior. The Pistons also have the depth to apply pressure in waves, with Daniss Jenkins emerging as a reliable bench contributor.

At home in Detroit, where the city's basketball energy has not been this alive since the Chauncey Billups era, the Pistons will have a genuine crowd advantage. Their defense is built for playoff intensity, and they know how to respond when things get hard — they just proved that over three consecutive elimination games.

The Case for Cleveland

The Cavaliers do not have the top seed or home court, but they have something arguably more valuable right now: momentum and mental toughness. This is a team that has stared down elimination multiple times in this postseason — trailing 3-2 to a Toronto squad that had momentum on its side — and found a way to advance every single time.

Donovan Mitchell is one of the most reliable scorers in the East in high-pressure moments. Evan Mobley has developed into a legitimate two-way star, capable of guarding multiple positions and attacking the rim on offense. And Jarrett Allen just reminded everyone, with his 22-point, 19-rebound Game 7 performance, that when he is engaged and energetic, he changes what a defense can do against Cleveland.

James Harden's playmaking and ability to set tempo give Cleveland another dimension that not every team can match. At 36, he is not the Harden of a decade ago, but in a half-court playoff setting where possessions are precious, his experience and IQ are invaluable assets.

What's Next

Game 1 tips off Tuesday, May 5, at Little Caesars Arena in Detroit at 7 p.m. ET on Peacock/NBC. Game 2 follows Thursday at the same venue. The series then shifts to Cleveland for Games 3 and 4. After the drama of the first round, this matchup has every ingredient for a series that goes the distance. Do not miss the start.
