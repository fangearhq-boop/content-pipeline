Western Conference Semifinals Are Here: Wembanyama-Edwards Tonight, SGA-LeBron on Tuesday

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 4, 2026

The Western Conference semifinals begin Monday night with two series that could not be more different in their setup — and both of them promise to be spectacular.

Game 1 of Spurs versus Timberwolves tips off in San Antonio at 9:30 p.m. ET, featuring one of the most fascinating individual matchups in basketball: the 7-foot-3 Victor Wembanyama against a Minnesota squad that proved its toughness by winning a playoff series in Round 1 without Anthony Edwards (knee) and Dillon DiVincenzo (Achilles). The Wolves, led by Jaden McDaniels' 32 points in the series-clinching Game 6, are a deep and resilient group. But Wembanyama — averaging 17 points and 14 rebounds in the Spurs' five-game first-round win over Portland — is unlike any player Minnesota has faced this postseason.

De'Aaron Fox was the Spurs' second force in Round 1, adding 21 points per game. San Antonio's combination of Wembanyama's all-around dominance and Fox's scoring ability gives them answers for almost any defensive scheme Minnesota can design. If the Wolves get Edwards back at full speed, this series becomes genuinely difficult to predict. Without him, they lean heavily on their depth and defensive identity.

The Main Event: Thunder vs. Lakers on Tuesday

If Spurs-Wolves is fascinating, Thunder-Lakers is appointment television. Game 1 in Oklahoma City tips off Tuesday at 8:30 p.m. ET on Peacock/NBC, and the spread of 16 points in OKC's favor tells the story of the regular season. What it cannot account for is what LeBron James and a team with nothing to lose might do.

Oklahoma City is the No. 1 seed in the West for a reason. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander won the regular-season scoring title and has been extraordinary in the playoffs. Chet Holmgren's length and shot-blocking create problems for every offense in the league. Isaiah Hartenstein anchors a defense that is built to suffocate opponents in five-on-five basketball. The Thunder have gone 12-and-0 in the first round over the previous three postseasons — they know how to close out.

Los Angeles comes in riding the momentum of eliminating Houston in six games, with LeBron posting 28 points, eight assists, and seven rebounds in the series-clinching Game 6. Austin Reaves returned from an oblique injury for that game. Anthony Davis anchors a physical frontcourt that can challenge Holmgren and Hartenstein inside.

The Lakers are double-digit underdogs in a series against the best team in the West. That is exactly the kind of situation LeBron James has historically turned into something memorable.

A Week That Defines the Conference

By Thursday, all four second-round series in the Western Conference will be underway. The results of these opening games will set the tone for two to three weeks of basketball that will ultimately determine who represents the West in the NBA Finals. The field is as competitive as it has been in years — and it starts tonight in San Antonio.

What's Next

Spurs vs. Timberwolves, Game 1: Tonight (May 4), 9:30 p.m. ET. Thunder vs. Lakers, Game 1: Tuesday (May 5), 8:30 p.m. ET on Peacock/NBC. Watch both — the Western Conference bracket is genuinely open, and these series will go a long way toward answering the biggest questions remaining in the 2026 playoffs.
