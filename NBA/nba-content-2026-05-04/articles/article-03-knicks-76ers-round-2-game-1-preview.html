The Garden Is Ready: Knicks-76ers Round 2 Begins Tonight, Embiid Probable at 8PM ET

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 4, 2026

NEW YORK — The second round of the 2026 NBA Playoffs opens at the most famous arena in basketball. Madison Square Garden will host Game 1 of the New York Knicks versus the Philadelphia 76ers tonight at 8 p.m. ET, and the matchup has all the ingredients for a memorable series.

Both teams had to scratch and claw just to make it this far. The 76ers spent most of their first-round series against the Celtics playing from behind, falling into a 1-3 hole before engineering one of the more remarkable comebacks in recent postseason memory. Joel Embiid, recovering from an appendectomy earlier in the playoffs, willed the team through elimination games — and he is listed as probable tonight despite a right hip contusion.

The Knicks, for their part, were dominant in closing out the Atlanta Hawks in six games — including a historic Game 6 in which New York led by 47 points at halftime, an NBA record. It is the kind of efficiency that sends a message to the rest of the East: this team is not messing around.

The Central Matchup: Brunson vs. Embiid

Jalen Brunson has been the soul of this Knicks team all season and through the first round. He averaged 26.3 points through the Hawks series, consistently attacking in clutch moments and finding teammates when defenses collapsed. Karl-Anthony Towns averaged 18.7 points alongside him, giving New York one of the more dangerous one-two punches in the East.

On the other side, Embiid is simply one of the most dominant players in basketball when healthy. He averaged 28 points, nine rebounds, and seven assists in his playoff appearances this spring. Even at something less than 100 percent, he is a matchup problem that no center in the league has solved. His ability to operate in the post, step out to the three-point line, and facilitate out of double-teams gives Philadelphia a versatility that the Knicks will have to account for on every possession.

Tyrese Maxey, who averaged 30 points in the Game 7 win over Boston, provides the dynamic second option that makes the 76ers dangerous even on nights when Embiid is not at full strength.

Home Court and the MSG Factor

New York holds home-court advantage in this series, which means tonight's game carries real weight. The Garden crowd has been one of the most electric atmospheres in these playoffs — it was the backdrop for Brunson's 39-point Game 5 performance against Atlanta — and the 76ers will need to find a way to quiet it early if they want to steal a road win.

Philadelphia has shown it can win tough games in hostile environments during this postseason. The comebacks against Boston were not all in the building on South Broad Street. But MSG in May, with a series on the line, is a different kind of challenge.

What's Next

Game 1 tips off tonight at 8 p.m. ET from Madison Square Garden. The full series schedule has the first four games of Knicks-76ers penciled in over the next two weeks, with games alternating between New York and Philadelphia. For a fan base that has waited years for a team worth watching, tonight is exactly what playoff basketball is supposed to look like.
