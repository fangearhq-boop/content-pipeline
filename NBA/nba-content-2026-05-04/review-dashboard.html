Pistons Complete Historic 3-1 Comeback — Cade 32/12, Harris 30, First Series Win Since 2008
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The Detroit Pistons are going to the second round of the NBA Playoffs for the first time since 2008 — and they did it in the most dramatic fashion imaginable. Down 3-1 to the Orlando Magic, with their season seemingly over, Detroit won three consecutive elimination games to complete one of the rarest comebacks in NBA history. Only 14 other teams had ever done it before. On Sunday, the Pistons made it 15 with a dominant 116-94 rout in Game 7. Tobias Harris was the unexpected hero, pouring in a playoff career-high 30 points on 11-of-18 shooting, including five three-pointers, adding nine rebounds and three steals. Then Cade Cunningham took over with 32 points and 12 assists — joining Isiah Thomas as the only Pistons in franchise history with 20-plus points and 10-plus assists in a Game 7. Together they became the first Detroit teammates to each score 30 points in a playoff game since Bob Lanier and Howard Porter in 1977. Paolo Banchero gave everything he had — 38 points on the losing end — but the rest of the Magic shot just 10-of-37 from the field. Cade and Harris were simply too much. The Pistons now face the Cavaliers in Round 2, starting Tuesday in Detroit. After 18 years, the city of Detroit has a playoff team worth believing in again. ### Image Post Caption CADE. HARRIS. NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Jarrett Allen is not going to make a highlight reel you'll see on SportsCenter. He doesn't dunk over people or drain half-court shots. But on Sunday night, he was the single most important player on the court. Allen finished with 22 points and 19 rebounds in Cleveland's 114-102 Game 7 win over the Toronto Raptors — but the numbers don't tell the full story. It was his third quarter that decided this series. With the Cavaliers needing a momentum shift after trailing by 10 in the second quarter, Allen grabbed 10 rebounds — five of them offensive — as Cleveland outscored Toronto 38-19 in the period to take complete control. Donovan Mitchell added 22 points and James Harden contributed 18. Scottie Barnes put up 24 for the Raptors in a valiant losing effort. But once Cleveland took that lead, they never let it go — building it to 19 in the third and 22 in the fourth. The Cavaliers advance as the No. 4 seed to face the No. 1 Detroit Pistons, who won their own Game 7 earlier in the day. Both teams are coming off emotional, draining wins — and it sets up one of the most intriguing Eastern Conference Semifinals in years. Game 1 is Tuesday night in Detroit at 7 p.m. ET. ### Image Post Caption 22 points. 19 rebounds. That's JARRETT ALLEN in Game 7. Cavaliers advance to Round 2 to face the Pistons. The Cleveland Cavaliers and Detroit Pistons both punched their Round 2 tickets on May 3 in the most dramatic way possible — and they meet in the Eastern Conference Semifinals starting Tuesday night in Detroit at 7 p.m. ET on Peacock/NBC. The Pistons are the No. 1 seed for a reason — Cade Cunningham posted 32 points and 12 assists in his Game 7, Tobias Harris added 30, and Jalen Duren is one of the most physical young big men in the East.
