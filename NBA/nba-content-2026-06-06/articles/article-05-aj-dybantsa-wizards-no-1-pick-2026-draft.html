AJ Dybantsa Says He's "100%" Going No. 1 to Washington — The Wizards Are Still Deciding

By Marcus Cole | June 6, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The 2026 NBA Draft is 17 days away, and BYU wing AJ Dybantsa is not leaving anyone in doubt about where he expects to be on the night of June 23 in Brooklyn.

When asked recently how certain he was that the Washington Wizards would select him with the No. 1 overall pick, Dybantsa's answer was simple: "100%." He has been equally direct in other interviews, saying he expects to be a Wizard — and his case on the court is difficult to argue with.

The Stats That Build the Case

Dybantsa spent his one college season at BYU putting together one of the most dominant freshman campaigns in recent memory. He averaged 25.5 points, 6.8 rebounds, and 3.7 assists per game while shooting 51 percent from the field.

At 6-foot-9 with a 7-foot wingspan, Dybantsa can score from all three levels, facilitate out of pick-and-roll situations, and defend multiple positions. The combination of physical tools and feel for the game at 18 years old has drawn comparisons to some of the best wings in basketball history.

Oddsmakers have him at -450 to be selected first overall — reflecting just how heavily consensus has swung toward a Washington selection.

The Uncertainty the Wizards Created

Despite Dybantsa's confidence, the picture is not entirely settled. CBS Sports reported that sources around the league do not view the Dybantsa-to-Washington selection as the lock many have assumed, noting that the Wizards' internal evaluations have remained open through the pre-draft process.

Washington officially won the 2026 Draft Lottery in May, claiming the No. 1 pick for the first time since John Wall in 2010. The Wizards have not publicly committed to any prospect, and their front office has made a point of running a thorough evaluation process through workouts and the draft combine.

The other names in the conversation — particularly Darryn Peterson of Kansas — have supporters within league circles who believe the Wizards could pivot. Peterson is viewed as a stronger defender and a more polished all-around prospect by some evaluators.

What a Dybantsa Selection Would Mean

Washington is a franchise in full rebuild mode. After missing the playoffs for multiple consecutive seasons, the Wizards are building around a young core that includes Alex Sarr, their own No. 2 pick from the previous draft. Adding Dybantsa would give them a potential cornerstone duo of elite two-way big men.

The Wizards' challenge is not finding the talent — it is building the culture and continuity needed to develop that talent into a winner. A generational prospect like Dybantsa at No. 1 would accelerate that timeline significantly and give the franchise a marketable star capable of driving interest in the nation's capital.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Draft takes place June 23-24 at Barclays Center in Brooklyn, New York. Washington holds the No. 1 pick and is expected to make their selection known in the days before the draft. AJ Dybantsa continues to express certainty about his destination. The Wizards, as of this writing, have not officially committed to any prospect.
