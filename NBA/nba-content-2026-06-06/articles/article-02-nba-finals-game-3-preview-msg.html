The Garden Awaits: NBA Finals Returns to MSG for First Time Since 1999

By Marcus Cole | June 6, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Madison Square Garden has not hosted an NBA Finals game in 9,845 days. The last time? Game 5 of the 1999 NBA Finals. Against these exact same San Antonio Spurs — a series the Spurs won, leaving New York's fans to wait 27 years for another chance at a championship.

That wait ends Monday, June 8, when Game 3 of the 2026 NBA Finals tips off at 8:30 PM ET. The Knicks hold a 2-0 series lead. The atmosphere at MSG will be unlike anything the building has produced in decades. And the Spurs arrive knowing that if they lose, their championship dream is likely over.

The Seismic Shift in Series Odds

When the NBA Finals began, oddsmakers had the San Antonio Spurs as -196 favorites to win the championship. The Knicks opened as +164 underdogs — a team many expected to be overmatched by Wembanyama's two-way brilliance.

After two games, the landscape looks nothing like that opening picture. The Knicks are now -490 series favorites, with the Spurs having shifted to +380. New York hasn't just won two games — they've won two games in San Antonio, at the Spurs' home arena, with a combined margin of 11 points (105-95 in Game 1, 105-104 in Game 2).

That means the Knicks accomplished the hardest part of winning a championship on the road. Now they get to play the most important game of their season at home, in front of their own crowd, in the building that defines New York basketball.

What the Spurs Need

San Antonio has never faced a 3-0 deficit in a Finals series — and no team in NBA Finals history has ever come back from one. If the Spurs fall behind 3-0 Monday night, the mathematical reality of their situation becomes overwhelming.

Victor Wembanyama has been good but not great in this series. He scored 29 points in Game 2 but his most memorable moment was the final 13 seconds — a bad pass, a costly foul, and a missed buzzer shot that ended the Spurs' best chance of stealing a win. His performance in Games 3 and 4 in New York will define his first Finals experience.

Stephon Castle and the Spurs' supporting cast have contributed, but the Knicks' defensive versatility has disrupted San Antonio's offensive rhythm throughout the series. Head coach Gregg Popovich, coaching in an advisory capacity following his 2024 stroke, will need to find solutions that haven't materialized in Texas.

The Historical Weight

For Knicks fans, the symbolism of facing the Spurs at MSG is powerful. In 1999, Rick Brunson played for that New York team that fell to San Antonio in five games. Now his son Jalen leads the Knicks back against the same franchise — and this time, New York holds the series lead.

The Knicks' 11-game postseason win streak heading into the Finals was the second longest ever recorded by a team entering the championship round. Their point differential through the playoffs was the best in league history. This team is not just good — it is historically dominant in its execution.

Game 3 at MSG is the moment this city has waited 27 years for. The last championship parade in New York happened in 1973.

What's Next

Game 3 tips off Monday, June 8 at 8:30 PM ET on ABC from Madison Square Garden in New York. If the Knicks win, they will be one game away from the franchise's first NBA championship since 1973. Game 4 is scheduled for Wednesday, June 10, also at MSG. A potential Game 5 would return to San Antonio on June 13 if needed.
