### STORY 1: Knicks 105-104 Spurs — Game 2 Recap

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 9:00 AM ET

The Knicks just won their ugliest game of the postseason — and somehow it might be their most impressive.

How? Karl-Anthony Towns stepped up when his backcourt partner went cold. KAT finished with 21 points, 13 rebounds, and 4 assists on highly efficient 8-of-12 shooting. He was the reason the Knicks had any lead to protect going into the final minute.

But the game came down to 13 wild seconds. The Spurs had just gone on a 14-0 run to cut the deficit to one. Victor Wembanyama had the ball. He tried to find a teammate — but his pass hit Stephon Castle in the back. Brunson swooped in for the steal, got fouled going out of bounds, and made the crucial free throw to push the lead to two.

Then came the final shot. Wemby had a clean 20-foot look at the buzzer. It was long. The Knicks won 105-104.

The Spurs were right there. The Knicks barely survived. And now the series heads to Madison Square Garden.
