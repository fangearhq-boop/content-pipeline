New York's Machine: Knicks Sweep 76ers to Return to the Eastern Conference Finals

By Damon Pierce

May 13, 2026

The New York Knicks swept Philadelphia out of the playoffs and did it with a performance that put the rest of the Eastern Conference on notice.

A 144-114 rout in Game 4 on Saturday night completed New York's second-round sweep of the 76ers. The Knicks' average margin of victory across the four games was 19.4 points — the largest margin through two rounds since the playoffs expanded to 16 teams in 1984. New York is back in the Eastern Conference Finals.

Brunson Runs the Show

Jalen Brunson was the engine of this entire run, averaging 29.0 points and 6.0 assists per game throughout the sweep. Game by game, he either took over in the fourth quarter or set teammates up when defenses collapsed on him. Against a Philly team without Joel Embiid for most of the series, there was simply no way to slow him down.

Brunson's combination of scoring efficiency and playmaking has made him the centerpiece of what New York is building. Through two rounds, he has been everything the Knicks needed him to be.

McBride Steals the Spotlight in the Clincher

The story of Game 4 belonged to reserve guard Deuce McBride. He erupted for 25 points on 7 three-pointers — going 4-for-4 from deep in the first quarter alone — tying the NBA postseason record for made 3-pointers in a single game.

The 144-114 final is the kind of performance that sends a message. This wasn't a team grinding out close wins — it was a team finding another gear when the series was already in hand and turning a sweep into a statement.

The Philadelphia Factor

Context matters here: Philadelphia was without Joel Embiid for the bulk of the series due to ankle and hip issues, and the Sixers were always going to be undermanned. But dominant teams win dominant games regardless of who's on the other side, and the Knicks didn't let up when they had the chance to bury their opponent.

The four-game series scores tell the story: Knicks 137-98, Knicks 108-102, Knicks 108-94, Knicks 144-114. Even in the tighter games, New York controlled the margin and closed like a veteran team that knows what it takes to win in May.

What's Next

The Knicks advance to the Eastern Conference Finals, where they will face the winner of the Cavaliers-Pistons series. Game 5 of that series tips tonight in Detroit, with Cleveland and Detroit knotted at 2-2. Madison Square Garden is ready for another playoff run — and this Knicks team looks ready to make a deep one.
