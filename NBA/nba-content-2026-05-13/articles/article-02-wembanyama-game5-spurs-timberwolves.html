Wemby Writes a New Chapter: 27/17/5 Carries Spurs Past Timberwolves in Game 5

By Marcus Cole

May 13, 2026

Victor Wembanyama was ejected in Game 4. He returned in Game 5 and put on one of the most dominant individual performances of the 2026 playoffs.

Wembanyama recorded 27 points, 17 rebounds, 5 assists, and 3 blocks in a 126-97 San Antonio blowout of the Minnesota Timberwolves on Tuesday night, giving the Spurs a 3-2 series lead. They are now one win from the Western Conference Finals. Game 6 goes Friday in Minneapolis.

Eighteen Points in the First Quarter

The tone was set before Minnesota even had a chance to settle in. Wembanyama scored 18 of his 27 points in the first quarter, single-handedly outpacing the Timberwolves' entire team at one point. When Minnesota called timeout with 5:44 remaining in Q1, Wemby had outscored the entire Wolves roster 16-11 on his own.

The final stat line — 27 points on 9-of-16 shooting, including 2-of-5 from three and 7-of-9 from the free-throw line — only tells part of the story. The bigger picture was how completely Wembanyama controlled the game at both ends. San Antonio outscored Minnesota 68-36 in points in the paint, a margin that speaks to how thoroughly the Spurs dominated the physical battle.

A Historic Statline

Wembanyama's Game 5 performance placed him in historic company. He became just the third-youngest player in NBA postseason history to record at least 25 points, 15 rebounds, and 5 assists in a playoff game, joining Magic Johnson and Luka Doncic on that short list.

The performance came just two days after Wembanyama was hit with a Flagrant 2 foul ejection in Game 4 for an elbow on Naz Reid. League officials reviewed the play but did not suspend him. He came out with a purpose.

Edwards Neutralized, Wolves in Survival Mode

Anthony Edwards had been the Timberwolves' lifeline throughout the series, going for 36 points and 16 rebounds in Game 4 to bring Minnesota back from a 2-1 deficit. In Game 5, however, he was largely contained as Wemby's gravitational pull altered everything Minnesota tried to do.

Now the Wolves go home knowing that their season depends on a Game 6 win at Target Center. They were 2-1 at home in this series before the blowout, and the crowd in Minneapolis will need to be at full volume to give Edwards and his teammates a fighting chance.

What's Next

Game 6 tips Friday, May 15 at the Target Center in Minneapolis. If Minnesota wins, the series goes to a decisive Game 7 in San Antonio. If the Spurs close it out, they'll face the Oklahoma City Thunder — a team that has yet to lose a single game in the 2026 playoffs — in the Western Conference Finals.

Either way, Wembanyama's performance in Game 5 made one thing clear: at just 22 years old, he is already one of the most unguardable players the league has seen in the postseason in a very long time.
