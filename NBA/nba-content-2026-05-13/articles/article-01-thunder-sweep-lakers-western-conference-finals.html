Thunder Are 8-0 and Just Getting Started After Sweeping the Lakers

By Jake Torres

May 13, 2026

The Oklahoma City Thunder aren't just winning playoff games — they're making a statement to every team left standing. A 115-110 victory over the Los Angeles Lakers in Game 4 on Sunday night completed a four-game sweep and sent OKC to the Western Conference Finals with a perfect 8-0 record in the 2026 postseason.

In a series that never felt close despite a few tighter final scores, Oklahoma City controlled the Lakers from start to finish. The Thunder won Games 1 and 2 by 18 points each before routing Los Angeles 131-108 in Game 3. Game 4 was the tightest of the bunch, but Shai Gilgeous-Alexander made sure there was no drama with a 35-point performance in the clincher.

Holmgren's Emergence Is the Biggest Story in the West

While SGA remains the face of this franchise, the most compelling storyline from the Lakers series was the ascent of Chet Holmgren. The Thunder's center averaged 18.6 points, 9.1 rebounds, and shot 60.0 percent from the floor across the four games — numbers that would make him the best player in most series.

Holmgren's best performance came in Game 1, where he posted 24 points, 12 rebounds, and 3 blocks. He followed that with 22 points, 9 rebounds, 4 steals, 3 assists, and 2 blocks in Game 2 — a statline that looked more like a highlight reel than a box score. By the time Game 4 arrived, the Lakers had no answer for him, and his 16-point, 9-rebound effort helped OKC hold off a late push.

At just 24 years old, Holmgren is posting the kind of two-way dominance that makes championship runs possible. Oklahoma City's front office looks prescient for building around both him and SGA.

SGA Seals It, LeBron's Questions Begin

Gilgeous-Alexander was measured for most of the series, allowing his teammates to carry scoring loads and picking his spots. But when the Lakers threatened to steal Game 4, SGA turned it on — 35 points, efficient and relentless, the kind of performance that reminds you why he's one of the game's best players.

For LeBron James, 41 years old and completing his 23rd NBA season, this early exit will only intensify questions about his future. James was productive throughout the series but couldn't drag the Lakers past a Thunder team that simply outclassed them at every position. Whether he hangs up his sneakers or returns for one more run remains the offseason's defining question.

What's Next

The Thunder now wait for the conclusion of the Spurs-Timberwolves series, which San Antonio leads 3-2 after Tuesday's 126-97 blowout. Game 6 goes Friday in Minneapolis. OKC, rested and perfect, gets to watch two battle-tested opponents wear each other down before the Western Conference Finals begin.

With an 8-0 record and the deepest roster in the West, Oklahoma City enters the Conference Finals as the clear favorite. For a franchise that has been building toward this moment for years, the next two rounds will define whether this Thunder team belongs in the conversation with the great teams in league history.
