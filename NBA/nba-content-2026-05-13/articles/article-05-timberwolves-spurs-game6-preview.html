Can Minnesota Force a Game 7? Wolves Face Elimination Friday vs. the Spurs

By Marcus Cole

May 13, 2026

The Minnesota Timberwolves came into this series as the underdogs, stole a game when Victor Wembanyama was ejected, and seemed to have momentum. Then Tuesday happened.

San Antonio's 126-97 demolition of Minnesota in Game 5 was a reminder of what this series is really about: a generational talent on the Spurs side versus a team desperately searching for a way to match him. Game 6 goes Friday, May 15 at the Target Center in Minneapolis. A Wolves loss ends their season.

Edwards Has to Be the Answer Again

Anthony Edwards gave Minnesota exactly what it needed in Game 4 — 36 points and 16 rebounds, a performance that forced a split in San Antonio and breathed life back into the Wolves' season. Game 5 was a different story. Wembanyama controlled the paint, scored 18 points before the first-quarter buzzer, and never let Minnesota find a foothold.

If there is a reason for Minnesota fans to believe, it starts with Edwards. At his best, he's one of the few players in the league who can match Wemby's energy and force a game to go through him. At Target Center, in front of a crowd that has seen the Wolves through this entire incredible run, he'll need to be at his absolute best.

The Home Floor Hasn't Always Been Enough

Minnesota was 2-1 at home entering Game 6, so there is precedent for the Wolves playing their best basketball in front of their own fans. But the Spurs have shown throughout this series that they don't wilt on the road. De'Aaron Fox and Stephon Castle have both been consistent contributors, and San Antonio's depth allows them to maintain pressure across four quarters.

The Spurs are one win from the Western Conference Finals, a place this franchise hasn't been since their dynasty days. For a team that was in full rebuild mode two years ago, this run has been remarkable — and Wembanyama has been the reason for all of it.

The Western Conference Finals Picture

If San Antonio advances, they will face the Oklahoma City Thunder, who swept the Lakers to improve to 8-0 in the 2026 playoffs. An OKC-Spurs series would be a fascinating matchup of the West's two youngest franchise cornerstones — Wembanyama against SGA and Holmgren, with both teams built to win now and for years to come.

If Minnesota survives, they'd bring a hungry and unpredictable team to face a Thunder group that hasn't been pushed to the limit yet this postseason.

What's Next

Game 6 of the Spurs-Timberwolves series tips Friday, May 15 at Target Center in Minneapolis. If the Wolves win, Game 7 would be scheduled for Sunday in San Antonio. Everything rides on Friday night. Can Edwards and Minnesota hold off the Wemby freight train one more time?
