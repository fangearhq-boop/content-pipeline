Cade vs. Mitchell, Game 5: Everything on the Line Tonight in Detroit

By Jake Torres

May 13, 2026

The Cavaliers-Pistons series has turned into exactly the kind of playoff battle nobody expected — and Game 5 tonight in Detroit will determine which team seizes momentum heading into the final stretch.

Tip-off is at 8:00 PM ET on ESPN from Little Caesars Arena, with the series knotted at two games apiece. Cleveland came back from a 0-2 deficit by winning back-to-back games, including Donovan Mitchell's 43-point explosion in Game 4. Now the Pistons have to answer on their home floor.

Mitchell Is Playing the Best Playoff Basketball of His Career

Donovan Mitchell has been a revelation in these playoffs. His Game 4 performance — 43 points in a 112-103 Cavaliers win — was the kind of statement that changes a series's narrative. James Harden added 24 points as Cleveland erased a big deficit with a 24-0 run in the second half, completing one of the more stunning momentum swings of the entire postseason.

Mitchell has never been more confident or more dangerous. If he's locked in tonight the way he was in Game 4, Detroit will need to find another level.

Cade Cunningham at Home Is a Different Beast

The counter-argument for Detroit centers entirely on Cade Cunningham and the home crowd at Little Caesars Arena. Cunningham has averaged 31.8 points and 8.2 assists per game across his six home playoff games this postseason on 50 percent shooting. He is a completely different player in front of his own fans, feeding off the energy and imposing his will when it matters most.

If any player in the East has the ability to single-handedly neutralize what Mitchell is doing, it's Cade at home. The Pistons went into this series as the No. 1 seed in the East, and they've shown the resilience to win close games when the stakes are highest.

What Each Team Needs

Cleveland needs Mitchell to continue his offensive onslaught and the supporting cast to make enough shots to keep Detroit's defense honest. The Cavaliers' second-half run in Game 4 showed this team can go on massive scoring streaks — but sustaining that over 48 minutes in a hostile environment is the challenge.

Detroit needs Cade to assert himself early, settle the crowd's nerves, and avoid the kind of run that ended Game 4. The Pistons also need their role players to protect the home court — the last place this series should be decided is in a Game 7 on Cleveland's floor.

What's Next

The winner of this series advances to the Eastern Conference Finals to face the New York Knicks, who swept the 76ers in convincing fashion. A Game 6 would go Saturday in Cleveland; a potential Game 7 would return to Detroit. Tonight's winner controls their own destiny. This one won't be over early.
