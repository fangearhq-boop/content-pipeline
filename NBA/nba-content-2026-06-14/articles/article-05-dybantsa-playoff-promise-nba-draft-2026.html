Dybantsa's Playoff Promise: AJ Makes Bold Vow to Wizards With Draft 9 Days Away

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

June 14, 2026

Nine days before the 2026 NBA Draft, the No. 1 prospect has already told the team that plans to pick him exactly what he plans to deliver.

AJ Dybantsa visited the Washington Wizards ahead of the June 23 draft at Barclays Center in Brooklyn, and he did not leave any ambiguity about his intentions. "If I go to DC, I'll make the playoffs," Dybantsa said. "I'm gonna make sure I make it."

Bold words for a 19-year-old. But Dybantsa has earned the right to say them.

The Prospect

In his one collegiate season at BYU, Dybantsa averaged 25.5 points and shot 51.0% from the field. At 6 feet 9 inches and 210 pounds, he projects as a versatile forward with the scoring instincts of a franchise player. NBA scouts have spent months debating where the ceiling is — most of them have stopped debating.

The Wizards hold the No. 1 pick after finishing 17-65 this season, winning the lottery with 14.0% odds. It is Washington's first top selection since John Wall in 2010, and the franchise has not reached the playoffs in years. Dybantsa knows the rebuild he is walking into. He is promising to accelerate it.

Draft Day Is Also Giannis Day

The NBA Draft on June 23 comes with an unusual overlay: it is also the day the Bucks want to resolve the Giannis Antetokounmpo trade saga. Both events will dominate headlines simultaneously when Barclays Center opens its doors in Brooklyn.

For Wizards fans, Dybantsa's promise is the story. The top five picks are expected to go Wizards, Jazz, Grizzlies, Bulls, and Clippers — a draft class that could reshape multiple franchises in one evening.

Darryn Peterson remains in the mix for the No. 1 conversation, though Dybantsa's odds at the position are overwhelming. His visit with Washington appears to have gone well. The pre-draft process rarely produces public statements like the one he made — which suggests he knows exactly where he is going and is already thinking past draft night.

What's Next

The draft is June 23-24 at Barclays Center. Assuming the Wizards select Dybantsa first overall — as expected — his NBA career begins with a pledge he made publicly before he even laced up for Washington. He will be held to it. He seems to want it that way.

For the Wizards, the combination of a generational prospect and his stated commitment to winning fast is the best-case scenario heading into the franchise's most pivotal offseason in years.
