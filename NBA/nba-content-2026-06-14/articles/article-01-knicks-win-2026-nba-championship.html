Knicks Win 2026 NBA Championship: First Title Since 1973

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

June 14, 2026

The wait is over. After 53 years, the New York Knicks are NBA champions.

Jalen Brunson poured in 45 points — including 15 in the final quarter — as the Knicks defeated the San Antonio Spurs 94-90 in Game 5 Friday night at Frost Bank Center, claiming the 2026 NBA championship and winning the Finals series 4-1. It is the third championship in franchise history and the first since 1973.

The Knicks did it the only way they know how: by fighting back. New York trailed by double digits at some point in all four of its victories, clawing back each time behind Brunson's relentless fourth-quarter brilliance. Friday was no different. The Spurs pushed hard in the fourth, but Brunson would not let the moment slip away.

A Night for the History Books

Victor Wembanyama gave San Antonio everything he had — 19 points, 14 rebounds, and 5 blocks — but it was not enough. OG Anunoby contributed 11 points, 8 rebounds, and 3 steals for New York, providing the defensive anchor that kept Wembanyama from taking over in the closing minutes.

The Knicks, who were 5.5-point underdogs entering Game 5, have now proven that championships are not always won by the favorite. They are won by teams that refuse to quit.

The celebration inside Frost Bank Center was surreal — orange and blue confetti raining down on a San Antonio floor, New York players embracing in disbelief and joy. For a franchise that last hoisted the Larry O'Brien Trophy when Richard Nixon was in office, this was more than a game. It was generational vindication.

53 Years of Waiting

The 1973 championship team — Willis Reed, Walt Frazier, Earl Monroe, Dave DeBusschere — is the stuff of New York legend. Every Knicks fan who grew up in the intervening decades knows those names, because those names were all they had.

Now there are new names to celebrate. Brunson. Anunoby. A roster built through the draft, trades, and sheer front-office determination. A team that won 13 straight playoff games at one stretch and never once looked like it would fold under pressure.

The 2026 Knicks are champions. And New York will never forget it.

What's Next

The celebration is just beginning. Mayor Zohran Mamdani has already announced a ticker-tape parade through the Canyon of Heroes on Thursday, June 18. Full details — route, timing, viewing areas — will be released today.

Off the court, the NBA offseason machine kicks into gear immediately. The Giannis Antetokounmpo trade saga — with the Heat as frontrunners — must be resolved before the June 23 NBA Draft. And in Brooklyn in nine days, the Wizards will take AJ Dybantsa first overall, beginning a new chapter for a franchise that has spent years in the wilderness.

But first, New York. Fifty-three years is a long time to wait. Thursday's parade will be the party to end all parties.
