Brunson's 45-Point Masterpiece: A Unanimous Finals MVP Performance for the Ages

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes

June 14, 2026

There was no debate. All 11 media members tasked with selecting the 2026 NBA Finals MVP cast their votes for the same person. Jalen Brunson walked out of Frost Bank Center on Friday night with 45 points, a championship trophy, and the Bill Russell Trophy in hand.

What he did in Game 5 against the San Antonio Spurs — and what he did throughout this entire Finals run — belongs in the conversation with the greatest individual performances in NBA championship history.

The Fourth Quarter Was His

The Knicks needed a hero in the fourth quarter with the Spurs refusing to fold. They got one. Brunson scored 15 of his 45 points in the final frame, willing New York past San Antonio in a 94-90 series-clinching victory.

His performance drew immediate comparisons to Michael Jordan. Brunson matched Jordan's record for most points scored in a road Finals-clinching game — a statistical line that puts him in genuinely elite territory.

For the entire Finals series, Brunson averaged 32.6 points, 4.6 assists, and 4.2 rebounds per game. He did not have a bad game. Not once. When the Spurs made adjustments, he adjusted faster. When they doubled him, he found the right play. When they gave him space, he made them pay.

A Father-Son Moment

After the final buzzer, amid the chaos of confetti and celebrating teammates, Brunson did not seek out the cameras. He sought out his father.

Rick Brunson serves as an assistant coach on the Knicks' staff. The embrace between father and son after the championship — captured by cameras throughout the arena — was the defining image of the night. A basketball family, working together, reaching the summit together.

It was a reminder that behind every great NBA player is a story. Jalen Brunson's story is one of perseverance, development, and an unwavering belief that his best was good enough.

His Legacy, Defined

Brunson was not a consensus top pick. He was not handed anything. He built himself into one of the most clutch players of his generation through years of craft, and Friday was the culmination of all of it.

When people talk about the 2026 Knicks — and they will, for decades — Jalen Brunson will be the first name mentioned. Not because he had to be; because he earned it.

What's Next

Brunson will be the guest of honor at Thursday's ticker-tape parade through the Canyon of Heroes. Endorsements, legacy discussions, and the inevitable contract extension conversations will follow. At 29, he is in the prime of his career with a championship already in hand.

The 2026 NBA Finals MVP was unanimous. In 20 years, people will ask: how was it ever in doubt?
