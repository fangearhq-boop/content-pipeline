Knicks Championship Parade Set for Thursday, June 18 — NYC Goes to the Canyon of Heroes

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

June 14, 2026

New York City does not do celebrations small. And for the first Knicks championship in 53 years, Mayor Zohran Mamdani is making sure of it.

The city announced a ticker-tape parade through the Canyon of Heroes on Thursday, June 18, honoring the 2026 NBA champion New York Knicks. The route will follow the traditional path — from Battery Park north along Broadway to City Hall in Lower Manhattan. Full timing and logistics are expected to be released later today.

A Parade 53 Years in the Making

The last time the Knicks made this trip was 1973. Willis Reed, Walt Frazier, and company received a hero's welcome after defeating the Los Angeles Lakers for the franchise's second championship. Nobody who attended that parade imagined the city would wait more than half a century for another one.

Now, June 18 is the day. Jalen Brunson, OG Anunoby, and the rest of the 2026 Knicks will ride through the Canyon of Heroes as champions — the same route used by the 1969 Mets, the 1986 Mets, the 1994 Rangers, and the 2009 Yankees. New York knows how to welcome a champion. It has been patient enough.

The City That Waited

Knicks fans are among the most passionate — and most tested — supporters in professional sports. Madison Square Garden has hosted some of the greatest moments in basketball history, but a championship night was not among them in the modern era. Every season that ended short was another year added to the drought.

Now the drought is over. And the party Thursday is for every fan who kept believing through the lean years, the near-misses, and the heartbreaks.

Thursday, June 18 will see Broadway lined with orange and blue, confetti falling from office buildings, and the sound of a city that has been waiting five decades finally letting go.

What's Next

Full parade details — including start time, designated viewing areas, and security information — will be released by the mayor's office today. Plan accordingly if you are heading to Lower Manhattan on Thursday.

After the parade, the Knicks offseason begins in earnest. The Giannis Antetokounmpo trade saga and the June 23 NBA Draft will dominate NBA headlines for the next nine days. But for one Thursday in June, all of that can wait.
