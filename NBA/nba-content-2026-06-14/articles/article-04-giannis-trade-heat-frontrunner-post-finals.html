Giannis Trade Countdown Begins: Heat Still Frontrunner as Bucks Set June 23 Deadline

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

June 14, 2026

The NBA Finals are over. The Milwaukee Bucks have run out of reasons to wait.

With the Knicks crowned champions Saturday night, the biggest remaining story of the 2026 NBA offseason moves to center stage: where will Giannis Antetokounmpo play next season? The Bucks have been clear about their timeline — a deal done before the June 23 NBA Draft. That gives the league's front offices nine days.

Heat Lead the Race

The Miami Heat remain the frontrunners according to NBA insiders Marc Stein and Jake Fischer. Giannis's preference reportedly has Miami at the top of the list, and the proposed trade package — featuring Tyler Herro, Kel'el Ware, Nikola Jovic, a 2029 first-round pick, and a 2031 first-round pick — is one the Bucks are reportedly comfortable with in principle.

That does not mean a deal is done. It means the two sides are close enough that the conversation is real. The Celtics, Timberwolves, and Magic have also engaged the Bucks, ensuring Milwaukee has leverage as it evaluates its options.

The Clock Is Real

Giannis has two years remaining on his current contract. He becomes eligible for a four-year, $275 million supermax extension on October 1. The Bucks are not going to let those final two years play out without either committing to him or trading him — and everything this offseason has pointed toward a move.

The draft on June 23 is not just a deadline for picks. It is a natural gathering point for league executives. A trade at or before the draft allows both sides to restructure around new players and picks in one motion. Miss that window and the offseason calculus changes significantly.

What's Next

Nine days. That is all that stands between the current uncertainty and a resolution. If the Heat and Bucks are as close as reported, expect league sources to signal a deal in the days ahead — possibly as soon as this week.

For Miami, landing Giannis would be the most significant roster move in franchise history since acquiring LeBron James in 2010. For Milwaukee, the return package could shape the Bucks' competitive window for the next decade. Everything is on the table. The countdown is real.
