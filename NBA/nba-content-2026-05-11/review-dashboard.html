Joel Embiid, fighting through injury all postseason, couldn't stop the bleeding. The final margin was 30 points. NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The 2026 playoffs delivered their most dramatic night yet on Sunday, and it had nothing to do with the final score. Victor Wembanyama — the reigning Defensive Player of the Year and the face of the San Antonio Spurs' rebuild — was ejected from Game 4 of the Western Conference Semifinals early in the second quarter after elbowing Minnesota's Naz Reid in the face and neck. After a video review, referee Zach Zarba upgraded the call to a Flagrant 2, automatically disqualifying Wembanyama for the remainder of the game. He finished with 4 points in just 13 minutes. According to ESPN Research, it was the earliest an All-Star had been ejected from a playoff game since play-by-play tracking began in 1997. Target Center was electric, fans chanting "Kick him out!" as the call was reviewed. And yet — the Spurs nearly won anyway. San Antonio led by 8 points in the fourth quarter, seemingly on course to take a commanding 3-1 series lead despite losing their star. Then Anthony Edwards happened. The 24-year-old Wolves superstar scored 16 of his 36 points in the final quarter, turning a Minnesota deficit into a 28-15 closing run over the last 8:51 of the game. Edwards finished 13-of-22 from the field, 3-of-5 from three, and 7-of-8 from the free-throw line — a complete and dominant performance. The final score was Minnesota 114, San Antonio 109. The series is tied at 2-2. Game 5 heads back to San Antonio, where the Spurs will hope to have Wembanyama and the crowd advantage working in their favor. Wembanyama's ejection also creates a potential cloud over Game 5: the Flagrant 2 automatically places him under enhanced review, and the NBA's Basketball Operations department could add additional flagrant points that would trigger an automatic suspension. ### Image Post Caption Wemby ejected (Flagrant 2), Edwards erupts for 36 (16 in Q4).
