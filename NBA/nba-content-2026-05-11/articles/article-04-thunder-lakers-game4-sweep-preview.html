Thunder vs. Lakers Game 4: Oklahoma City Aims to Complete Sweep as LeBron Fights Without Luka

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 11, 2026

Oklahoma City has not lost a single game in the 2026 NBA Playoffs. Through seven contests across two rounds, the Thunder have beaten every opponent by double digits and dismantled the Los Angeles Lakers in all three games of the Western Conference Semifinals. On Monday night, they travel back to Los Angeles for Game 4 with the chance to complete a sweep and advance to the Western Conference Finals.

Tip-off is set for 10:30 PM ET on Prime Video from Crypto.com Arena. The Thunder lead the series 3-0.

Oklahoma City's Suffocating Dominance

The numbers tell the story bluntly. Oklahoma City has won the three games in this series by 18, 18, and 23 points respectively. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has been masterful, directing the offense with precision and attacking the Lakers' defense in ways Los Angeles has had no answer for. Chet Holmgren has dominated the interior, shooting over 60 percent from the field across the series and providing rim protection that smothers Los Angeles' attempts to get into the paint.

The Thunder's depth has been equally important. Ajay Mitchell's 24-point, 10-assist, 3-steal, zero-turnover Game 3 performance was one of the most statistically rare playoff lines in the modern era, according to ESPN. Isaiah Wallace, Tre Mann, and the rest of the Thunder bench have contributed cleanly when called upon. Oklahoma City has turned the ball over a combined 18 times in three games — an almost unfathomable number across a playoff series.

The Lakers' Nearly Impossible Task

LeBron James is 41 years old and averaging 21 points in this series. In Game 3, he finished 7-of-19 from the field — the effort was clearly there, but the execution has not been enough. Rui Hachimura, who led the team with 21 points in Game 3, has been the most consistent offensive contributor. Austin Reaves has remained feisty, generating 17 points and 9 assists in that contest.

The absent variable is Luka Doncic. The Lakers' second star remains sidelined with a Grade 2 hamstring strain and is not cleared for contact. Without Doncic, Los Angeles cannot generate the dynamic two-man creation needed to manufacture quality looks against Oklahoma City's switching, well-coordinated defense. The Lakers have tried. They've had moments. But they have not found a way to string together the kind of sustained offensive production needed to beat this team.

No team in NBA history has ever rallied from an 0-3 series deficit to win. In over 150 opportunities across the league's history, it has never been done. Tonight is Los Angeles' first chance to make that 0-4 instead of 0-3 in attempts.

What's Next

If Oklahoma City wins tonight, they advance to the Western Conference Finals and await the winner of the Spurs-Timberwolves series, which is now tied at 2-2 after Wembanyama's ejection in Game 4 and the Wolves' comeback win. That matchup could set up one of the more compelling WCF pairings in recent memory — a young, ascending Thunder team against either an explosive Wolves squad led by Anthony Edwards or a Spurs side built around the most unique talent in basketball.

For the Lakers, tonight is about pride, legacy, and perhaps one last moment of defiance before a series that has been decided since the opening tip. LeBron James, for everything he has accomplished, deserves a send-off worthy of his career — even if that send-off is fighting hard against a team that is simply better than everyone else right now.
