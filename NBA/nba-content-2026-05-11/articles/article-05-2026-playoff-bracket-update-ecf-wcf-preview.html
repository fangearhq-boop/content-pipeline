2026 NBA Playoff Bracket Update: Knicks Wait, Wembanyama's Status in Doubt, Finals Picture Emerging

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 11, 2026

The 2026 NBA Playoffs are entering their most critical stretch. Four series are still being decided. One team is already resting and watching. And the biggest individual question in the entire postseason — will Victor Wembanyama play in Game 5? — is hanging over a tied series headed back to Texas.

Here's where everything stands heading into Monday evening's action.

The Knicks Are Waiting

New York swept Philadelphia 4-0 on Sunday with a 144-114 demolition at Wells Fargo Center, completing their first best-of-seven sweep since 1999. The Knicks are resting, recovering, and watching as the Pistons and Cavaliers decide who will be their Eastern Conference Finals opponent.

Detroit leads Cleveland 2-1 in that series, with Game 4 on Monday night at 8:00 PM ET at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse. A Knicks opponent could be determined before the end of the week, or the series could extend. Either way, New York heads into the ECF with seven straight postseason wins and no signs of slowing down.

Wembanyama's Suspension Watch

The most consequential off-court development in the playoffs right now is what happens to Victor Wembanyama following his Flagrant 2 ejection in Game 4 against Minnesota. The NBA's Basketball Operations department will review his flagrant foul points record after Sunday's ejection.

In the NBA's system, a player who accumulates a certain number of flagrant foul points across the playoffs faces an automatic suspension. Whether Wembanyama's points total puts him at risk depends on his prior record in the 2026 postseason. The league's decision, expected before Game 5 in San Antonio, could be the single most important ruling of this year's playoffs.

The Spurs-Wolves series — now tied at 2-2 — is already one of the most dramatic in the bracket. Game 5 returns to San Antonio with or without Wembanyama. Anthony Edwards, who scored 36 points in Game 4 to lead Minnesota's comeback from eight points down in the fourth quarter, has demonstrated that his team can win without Wembanyama helping them through his own errors. The Wolves proved they can close out a game, and they now hold as much momentum as the team with home court.

The Western Bracket Is Shaping Up

Oklahoma City enters Game 4 against the Lakers on Monday night as a near-certainty to advance to the Western Conference Finals. The Thunder have gone 7-0 in the postseason, and the Lakers — without Luka Doncic — have been outmatched by at least 18 points in every contest. When OKC closes out this series, they will face the Spurs-Wolves winner.

Either WCF matchup is compelling. Edwards and the Wolves against a Thunder team that has been flawless would be a generational talent showdown with championship stakes. The Spurs, if Wembanyama plays, represent the most unique defensive challenge any offense would face — a 7-foot-4 center who can guard one through five and alter every shot in the paint.

What's Next

Monday night brings two marquee games: Pistons-Cavaliers at 8:00 PM ET on NBC and Thunder-Lakers at 10:30 PM ET on Prime Video. The playoff bracket will be significantly clearer by Tuesday morning. Watch for the NBA's ruling on Wembanyama's suspension eligibility, which shapes the most dramatic ongoing series in either conference. The road to the 2026 NBA Finals runs through all of it.
