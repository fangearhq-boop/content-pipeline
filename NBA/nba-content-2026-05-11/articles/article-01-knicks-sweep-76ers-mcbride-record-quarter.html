Deuce McBride's Record Quarter Fuels Knicks' 144-114 Demolition of 76ers, Completing the Sweep

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 11, 2026

Miles "Deuce" McBride did something no Knick had done in a playoff game since statistical tracking began in 1997: hit four three-pointers in a single quarter. He did it Sunday afternoon in Philadelphia, and by the time the dust settled, New York had beaten the 76ers 144-114 to complete a stunning 4-0 sweep and book their ticket to the Eastern Conference Finals.

McBride's explosion off the bench — 25 points, seven-for-nine from beyond the arc — was the defining performance of a game that was never really a contest after the first quarter. The Knicks hit 11 three-pointers in those opening 12 minutes, tying the NBA playoff record for made threes in a quarter, and turned what could have been a tense elimination game into a coronation.

"He was just locked in from the moment he touched the floor," Jalen Brunson said afterward. "That's Deuce. He's been ready for a moment like this."

The Quarter That Decided Everything

Philadelphia had something to play for. The 76ers were at home, Joel Embiid was in the lineup, and the crowd at Wells Fargo Center was ready for a fight. Four minutes into the game, with New York holding a modest four-point lead, McBride caught fire.

He made four consecutive three-pointers across four straight possessions, extending a lead that the 76ers never recovered from. According to NBA records, no Knick had accomplished that feat in a playoff game since play-by-play tracking began nearly three decades ago.

New York's collective three-point barrage in that first quarter tied a league postseason record. When the buzzer sounded at the end of the opening frame, the Knicks led by a margin that made the next three quarters a formality.

The final score, 144-114, marked New York's seventh double-digit victory of the 2026 playoffs. This team has not won a single postseason game by a narrow margin. They are punishing opponents from start to finish.

The Full Lineup Delivered

While McBride grabbed the headline, the rest of the Knicks rotation performed exactly as expected. Brunson finished with 22 points and continued his steady command as the team's primary ball-handler. Josh Hart added 17 points with the physicality and hustle that have defined his playoff run. Karl-Anthony Towns matched Hart at 17 points, operating efficiently in limited minutes.

New York hit 25 three-pointers as a team in the game. It was a display of spacing, movement, and execution that Philadelphia had no answer for across any of the four games in the series.

The 76ers got 25 points from Tyrese Maxey and contributions from Paul George, but Embiid — battling through ankle and hip issues — could not provide the interior dominance needed to shift the game's architecture. OG Anunoby, who missed the second half of the series with a hamstring strain, was not available to defend on the other end.

Philadelphia's season is over. New York's run continues.

What's Next

The New York Knicks will wait for the winner of the Detroit Pistons-Cleveland Cavaliers series to determine their Eastern Conference Finals opponent. Detroit leads that series 2-1, with Game 4 scheduled for Monday night in Cleveland.

Whatever opponent emerges, the Knicks will enter the ECF with more rest than their opponent, seven straight playoff wins, and a first-quarter firepower capability that has yet to be extinguished. After their first best-of-seven sweep since 1999, this team has a genuine case as the most dangerous squad left in the Eastern bracket.
