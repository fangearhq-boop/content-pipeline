Wembanyama Ejected in Historic First, Edwards Leads Wolves' Rally Past Spurs 114-109 to Tie Series 2-2

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 11, 2026

Victor Wembanyama had never been ejected from a professional basketball game. That changed on Sunday night at Target Center, and in the most dramatic fashion possible.

With 8 minutes and 39 seconds remaining in the second quarter of Game 4 between the San Antonio Spurs and Minnesota Timberwolves, Wembanyama was assessed a Flagrant 2 foul for driving his right elbow into the face and neck of Naz Reid while being sandwiched in a double-team. After a video review, referee Zach Zarba upgraded the call and disqualified the 7-foot-4 center from the rest of the game. He left with 4 points in 13 minutes — and the Spurs, somehow, still nearly won.

According to ESPN Research, it was the earliest an All-Star had been ejected from a playoff game since play-by-play tracking began in the 1997-98 season. The record, and the circumstances surrounding it, will define Game 4 regardless of whatever else unfolded.

The Play That Changed the Night

The sequence was violent but somewhat understandable in context. Wembanyama had gathered an offensive rebound when Reid and Jaden McDaniels swarmed him simultaneously outside the paint. Feeling trapped, the 22-year-old center swung his elbow and connected flush with the right side of Reid's jaw and neck, sending Reid spinning to the floor.

The initial call was an offensive foul. After review, it became a Flagrant 2 — excessive contact to the head and neck area — and Wembanyama was gone. Target Center erupted with chants of "Kick him out!" during the review.

Spurs coach Mitch Johnson was asked afterward if he was upset with his star. "I'm glad he took matters into his own hands," Johnson said, stopping short of apologizing for the play. The league's Basketball Operations department will review the flagrant points on Wembanyama's record — the Flagrant 2 could push him into suspension territory for Game 5 if additional points are added.

The Spurs Held On — Then Didn't

Without their franchise player, San Antonio fought. The Spurs built a lead and carried it into the fourth quarter. With roughly nine minutes remaining, they led by eight points. A win without Wembanyama would have been one of the more remarkable results in this young postseason.

Then Anthony Edwards decided the game. The 24-year-old Wolves star, who finished with 36 points on 13-of-22 shooting, turned his fourth quarter into a personal highlight reel. He scored 16 of Minnesota's points in those final 12 minutes as the Timberwolves outscored the Spurs 28-15 over the game's final 8:51. His final line: 36 points, six rebounds, two assists, and one steal.

The Wolves won 114-109. The series is tied at 2-2.

What's Next

Game 5 returns to San Antonio on Tuesday. The Spurs will be home, energized, and — they hope — with their franchise player available. Whether Wembanyama plays will depend on the NBA's review of his flagrant foul record following Sunday's ejection. Uncertainty around his availability adds a significant variable to what is suddenly a series where anything can happen.

Edwards, for his part, made clear that his fourth-quarter heroics were no accident. This series is now a best-of-three with enormous stakes for both franchises. The winner advances to the Western Conference Finals; the loser goes home. It doesn't get bigger than this.
