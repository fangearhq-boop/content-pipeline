Pistons vs. Cavaliers Game 4 Preview: Cleveland Seeks to Even Series at Home

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 11, 2026

The Detroit Pistons came into this Eastern Conference Semifinal as a surprise, and they have played the role perfectly. They won Game 1 in Cleveland, won Game 2 in Cleveland, then watched the Cavaliers come home and win Game 3 to cut the deficit to 2-1. Now they come back to Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse on Monday night for Game 4, and the stakes could not be clearer: Cleveland wins to even the series, or Detroit takes a 3-1 stranglehold that would put the Cavaliers in serious trouble.

Tip-off is scheduled for 8:00 PM ET on NBC/Peacock.

What Detroit Has Done Right

This Pistons team has leaned on Cade Cunningham, and Cunningham has delivered. Through the first three games, the young star has averaged 24 points and 9 assists. In Game 2, he scored 25 on 8-of-8 from the free-throw line and dropped 12 of those points in the fourth quarter to put the game away.

Detroit has also gotten contributions from Jalen Duren, Malik Beasley, and Jalen Williams, a depth that has allowed the Pistons to sustain their defensive intensity across 48 minutes. They came into Cleveland twice and took control of the series. They have answered every question about whether this young roster is ready for postseason basketball.

Cleveland's Response and What It Needs Tonight

Donovan Mitchell was spectacular in Game 3. His 35 points came with a side of history: he reached 2,000 career postseason points in his 73rd playoff game, one of the fastest marks for active players in NBA history. James Harden provided the clutch baskets Cleveland needed in the closing minutes.

For the Cavaliers to even the series tonight, Mitchell and Harden must deliver again. Cleveland has the crowd, the momentum, and a recent win to build on. But Detroit's defensive structure and Cunningham's ability to generate late-game offense makes this far from a given.

The winner of Game 4 holds significant momentum heading into Game 5. If Cleveland wins, this is a coin flip heading into the back half of the series. If Detroit wins at 3-1, Cleveland's back is against the wall.

What's Next

The winner of this series faces the New York Knicks in the Eastern Conference Finals. New York swept the 76ers 4-0 on Sunday and is resting, watching, and waiting. Whichever team advances will carry significant battle scars into that matchup — but they will also carry the confidence that comes from winning a tough second-round series.

Game 4 tips off at 8:00 PM ET on NBC/Peacock from Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in Cleveland. This is must-watch basketball.
