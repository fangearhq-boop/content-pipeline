Knicks Survive 76ers Scare 108-102 — Brunson, KAT, and OG Power New York to 2-0 Series Lead

By Jake Torres | May 8, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Twenty-five lead changes in a playoff game is a story about chaos. But the New York Knicks didn't need order throughout — they just needed it at the end. A 9-0 closing run gave them a 108-102 victory over the Philadelphia 76ers in Game 2 Wednesday night, sending New York home with a 2-0 series lead and leaving a Sixers team playing without Joel Embiid wondering how much longer it can survive.

This was the kind of ugly, grinding, back-and-forth game that Madison Square Garden was built for. The Knicks weren't beautiful — but they were better when it counted, and that's all that matters in May.

Brunson Delivers When It Gets Tight

Jalen Brunson's numbers — 26 points on 9-for-21 shooting — don't tell the full story of his value in a game like this. In a contest with 25 lead changes where neither team ever felt truly in control, the Knicks needed a player who could make the right decisions under maximum pressure on the final few possessions. Brunson was that player, delivering key buckets down the stretch as New York closed the game.

OG Anunoby added 24 points and provided the kind of two-way effort that makes him so difficult to defend. When Philadelphia keyed in on Brunson, OG was there to punish them. When the Sixers tried to get into a transition game, Anunoby was there to cut it off.

KAT Does More in 27 Minutes Than Most Do in 40

Karl-Anthony Towns spent the night in foul trouble, and yet he still finished with 20 points, 10 rebounds, and 7 assists in just 27 minutes. That's a triple-double's worth of production in limited time, and it reflects why the Knicks' acquisition of Towns has been such a massive upgrade for this franchise. When he's on the floor, New York's offense operates at a different level of efficiency — he creates spacing, draws double teams, and reads the game quickly enough to find open teammates before defenses can recover.

The fact that he's accumulating this kind of production while managing foul trouble speaks to both his game IQ and his physical conditioning. Towns knows how to be impactful without gambling on defense, and he's been one of the most efficient players in these playoffs.

Philadelphia Fights Without Its Star

The 76ers are going to be a very good team when Joel Embiid is healthy. This series is proving it in the most frustrating possible way — by showing how much Philadelphia can compete even without him. Tyrese Maxey led the Sixers with 26 points and played like the franchise player this team needs him to be. Paul George added 19. Kelly Oubre Jr. contributed another 19. Rookie VJ Edgecombe impressed with 17.

That's a legitimate offensive rotation built around a common theme: none of them are Joel Embiid. Without their center's ability to anchor the defense, draw fouls in the post, and collapse the lane on every drive, the Sixers are playing a different style of basketball — one that requires everything to go right simultaneously. Against the Knicks, it almost did. But "almost" earns a 0-2 hole in a playoff series.

What's Next

Game 3 is tonight at 7:00 PM ET on Amazon Prime Video in Philadelphia. Joel Embiid has been listed as questionable with a right ankle sprain and right hip soreness — his status could define the rest of this series. If he plays, the Sixers get back their most important player and suddenly the dynamic changes completely. If he sits out again, New York has a chance to take a 3-0 lead from which very few playoff teams have ever recovered.

OG Anunoby and Josh Hart are also listed as questionable for the Knicks. Regardless of injuries, the Sixers have home court advantage tonight and a crowd desperate to see their team mount a comeback. Whether Embiid is in uniform or watching from the bench is the only question that really matters heading into Game 3.
