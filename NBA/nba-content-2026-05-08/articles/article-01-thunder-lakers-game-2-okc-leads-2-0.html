Thunder Roll Lakers 125-107 in Game 2 — OKC Takes 2-0 Series Lead

By Jake Torres | May 8, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Oklahoma City Thunder have done something the Los Angeles Lakers are going to have a very hard time explaining: they won a second consecutive playoff game by double digits while Shai Gilgeous-Alexander wasn't even trying his hardest. Thursday night's 125-107 Game 2 victory puts OKC up 2-0 in the Western Conference Semifinals, and the margin of victory undersells just how comfortable the Thunder looked throughout.

This isn't a team that wins because of one superstar. It's a machine, and that machine hummed perfectly at Paycom Center in front of a crowd that smelled a knockout from the opening tip.

The Third Quarter Said Everything

With SGA resting on the bench, Oklahoma City went on a 32-15 third-quarter run to stretch what had been a competitive game into a comfortable lead. That kind of damage from your depth chart — against a team featuring LeBron James — is the definition of organizational superiority. The Thunder can hurt you at any moment, with any lineup, in any quarter.

When Gilgeous-Alexander returned, he finished with 22 points to match Chet Holmgren's 22. But the real story wasn't the two stars — it was the complementary players who refused to let up. Ajay Mitchell added 20 points. Jared McCain chipped in 18. Oklahoma City's bench outperformed Los Angeles's starters for stretches of this game, and that's what a 2-0 series lead looks like when it's built on depth rather than luck.

Holmgren's Two-Way Masterpiece

Chet Holmgren finished with 22 points, 9 rebounds, 4 steals, 3 assists, and 2 blocks, and those numbers still undersell his impact on the game. He was a menace defensively — forcing turnovers, disrupting driving lanes, and communicating defensive rotations with the calm of a veteran. His ability to guard four or five positions while also being a legitimate offensive threat makes him one of the most difficult players in basketball to game-plan against.

For the Lakers, Holmgren is a nightmare matchup. He's too quick for their big men and too big for their wings, and his combination of skills ensures that no single defender can contain him.

Reaves and LeBron Run Out of Gas

Austin Reaves delivered 31 points on 10-for-16 shooting — arguably one of his finest individual playoff performances — and it still wasn't close to enough. LeBron James added 23 points and the Lakers briefly cut Oklahoma City's lead to five in the fourth quarter, giving their fans a sliver of hope. But the Thunder answered every run, every momentum shift, and every Laker attempt to wrestle control of the game.

That's been the consistent narrative of this series: Los Angeles makes a move, Oklahoma City responds with something bigger. The Lakers' turnover issues have compounded the problem, handing OKC easy transition opportunities and keeping the Thunder offense in rhythm even in moments when Los Angeles could have stolen momentum.

What's Next

Game 3 moves to Los Angeles, where the Lakers will have home crowd support and a desperate need to find an answer for Oklahoma City's versatile defense. Down 0-2 in a series against the No. 1 seed, the path for Los Angeles to come back is narrow but not impossible — the NBA has seen stranger things.

But the Thunder have shown nothing that suggests they're ready to let this series get complicated. They have depth, two-way stars, home court for Games 1 and 2 already in hand, and a defensive system that takes away the exact kinds of open looks the Lakers rely on. LeBron James may have something to say about the rest of this series — but OKC is playing basketball at another level right now.
