Pistons Lock Down Cavaliers 107-97 — Cade's Clutch Fourth Quarter Seals 2-0 Series Lead

By Marcus Cole | May 8, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Detroit Pistons had a message for anyone who thought their first-round heroics were a fluke: they came back to Little Caesars Arena in Game 2 and did it again. A 107-97 victory over the Cleveland Cavaliers gives Detroit a 2-0 lead in the Eastern Conference Semifinals, and the defining moment came in the fourth quarter when Cade Cunningham simply refused to let his team lose.

When the game was tightest and the crowd was loudest and the Cavaliers were making their most serious push, Cunningham produced 12 of his 25 points in the final frame while going 8-for-8 from the free-throw line. That's the stat line of a franchise player who has fully arrived in the postseason.

Cade in Control

The 25 points and 10 assists tell part of the story. The 8-for-8 from the line in crunch time tells the rest. Free-throw shooting under pressure in a playoff game is one of the purest measures of a player's composure, and Cunningham passed that test with complete authority on Thursday night.

He was already the best player on the court for most of the game — his vision, his pick-and-roll execution, and his ability to draw contact created openings for Detroit all night. But when Cleveland mounted its most dangerous run and the margin started to shrink, Cunningham didn't shrink with it. He got to his spots, made his shots, and closed the game out the way franchise players do.

Cavaliers Freeze When It Matters

Donovan Mitchell gave Cleveland everything he had. Thirty-one points is a remarkable output for a player carrying such a heavy offensive load, and for three quarters he kept the Cavaliers in a game they probably had no business staying close in. Jarrett Allen was excellent as well, finishing with 22 points and providing interior resistance all night.

But the fourth quarter was a different story. Cleveland shot 0-for-11 from three in the final frame — missing every single three-point attempt when they needed them most. When the paint was locked up by Detroit's defense and the mid-range wasn't falling, the Cavaliers had nowhere to turn. Mitchell can't manufacture a comeback alone, and the supporting cast couldn't deliver when the pressure peaked.

Detroit's Depth Holds the Line

Tobias Harris added 21 points for the Pistons, continuing a postseason run in which he's been far more impactful than his regular-season reputation suggested. Daniss Jenkins came off the bench to contribute 14 important points, underscoring Detroit's ability to sustain quality play at multiple positions without burning out their starters.

This Detroit team is not surviving on talent alone. They are executing a coherent defensive system, making the right basketball decisions under pressure, and leaning on their depth in moments when other teams would be scrambling. The Pistons feel like a team that knows exactly who they are and what they're capable of.

What's Next

Game 3 moves to Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in Cleveland, where the Cavaliers will have their home crowd and a desperate need to reset this series. Down 0-2, Cleveland cannot afford another slow fourth quarter — they need Mitchell to get help early and often, and they need their shooters to find the three-point shooting touch that went cold late in Game 2.

As for Detroit, they're heading into enemy territory with the most dangerous kind of confidence — the kind built on two consecutive wins against a capable team. Cade Cunningham is playing like a legitimate star. The Pistons are playing like a legitimate contender. Game 3 will reveal whether Cleveland can reverse course or whether Detroit is ready to take a commanding 3-0 series lead.
