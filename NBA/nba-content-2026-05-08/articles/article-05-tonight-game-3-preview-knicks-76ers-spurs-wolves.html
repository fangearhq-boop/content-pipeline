Friday Night Preview: Embiid Questionable for Game 3 as Knicks-76ers and Spurs-Wolves Tip Off

By Marcus Cole | May 8, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Friday night brings two playoff games that could each shift the momentum of their respective series. The evening opens with the Knicks heading to Philadelphia for Game 3 at 7:00 PM ET on Amazon Prime Video — and the question hanging over that game is whether Joel Embiid will finally take the court. Later, at 9:30 PM ET on the same network, the Spurs head to Minneapolis for Game 3 in a series tied 1-1, where the Timberwolves desperately need a bounce-back performance at home.

Both games are on Amazon Prime Video, and both carry enormous stakes for teams that cannot afford to fall further behind.

The Embiid Question

Joel Embiid has been listed as questionable for Game 3 with a right ankle sprain and right hip soreness — the same combination of injuries that kept him out of Games 1 and 2. After being ruled out on Wednesday afternoon just hours before tip-off, coach Nick Nurse said that Embiid had woken up that morning with significant soreness and the team decided he couldn't go after shootaround.

Down 0-2 in a series they cannot afford to lose, the Sixers need their franchise center. The difference in Philadelphia's offensive and defensive profile with Embiid on the floor versus without him is the difference between a team capable of competing for a title and a team trying to survive with its secondary options. Tyrese Maxey, Paul George, and the supporting cast have been admirable, but they can only go so far against this Knicks defense.

OG Anunoby and Josh Hart are also listed as questionable for New York, meaning both teams may be working through injury concerns heading into tonight.

Knicks Seeking a Stranglehold

The New York Knicks have won two straight playoff games against an opponent that nearly beat them in Game 2 through 25 lead changes and a fierce second-half push. If New York can win Game 3 in Philadelphia — with or without Embiid in the lineup — they would hold a 3-0 series lead, a position from which no NBA team has ever come back to win a series.

Jalen Brunson, OG Anunoby, and Karl-Anthony Towns have all shown they can handle the load. The Knicks' defense has been stifling even when the offense has been inconsistent. Winning on the road in a playoff atmosphere in Philadelphia won't be easy, but this Knicks team has done difficult things all spring.

Minnesota's Bounce-Back Moment

The Minnesota Timberwolves are in desperate need of a reset after Wednesday's 133-95 obliteration in San Antonio — the worst postseason loss in franchise history. Heading home to Target Center, the Wolves will lean on their crowd and their home-court energy to find a better version of themselves.

Anthony Edwards needs to be the aggressor from the opening tip. After Game 2, where he finished with just 12 points as part of a collective shooting collapse, Edwards has to attack downhill and force the Spurs to make decisions early rather than letting Victor Wembanyama dictate defensive terms. The series is tied 1-1, which means Minnesota is not yet in trouble — but another performance like Game 2 in the Wolves' own building would change everything.

What's Next

Tonight is a pivotal moment in both series. The games are on Amazon Prime Video starting at 7:00 PM ET (Knicks-76ers) and 9:30 PM ET (Spurs-Wolves). The weekend slate will round out with additional playoff action as the Thunder-Lakers and Pistons-Cavaliers series also continue.

By Saturday morning, we may know whether this is a two-series race heading toward the conference finals or whether the Sixers and Wolves have found ways to make their respective matchups competitive again. Either way, Friday night basketball in the NBA Playoffs delivers maximum stakes — and that's exactly what the postseason is built for.
