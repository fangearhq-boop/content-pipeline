Portland Emerges as Frontrunner in Jaylen Brown Trade Market

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes

June 29, 2026

The summer's most significant trade may be approaching its conclusion — and the destination is becoming clearer. The Portland Trail Blazers have emerged as the most frequently cited frontrunner to land Jaylen Brown, according to Marc Stein and multiple reports, as the Boston Celtics continue to field serious interest from at least seven teams willing to pay a steep price for one of the best two-way wings in basketball.

Brown averaged 28.7 points, 6.9 rebounds, and 5.1 assists per game last season, earning All-NBA Second Team recognition in the process. Boston's asking price to move him: four or more first-round picks. So far, they have not budged.

Why Portland Is Leading

The Trail Blazers have been mentioned in connection with this trade more than any other team, and the reason is straightforward: Portland may be the team most willing to meet Boston's price. For a franchise that has been in a long rebuilding cycle, acquiring a 28.7-PPG All-NBA player would represent a seismic shift in their competitive timeline.

The other teams reportedly in the mix are a who's who of organizations with varying degrees of draft capital and competitive urgency: the Los Angeles Clippers, Denver Nuggets, Atlanta Hawks, Brooklyn Nets, Charlotte Hornets, Toronto Raptors, and Detroit Pistons. The breadth of that field is important context. It means Boston has genuine competition — and genuine leverage.

When seven or more teams are in serious pursuit of the same player, the seller does not have to settle. Brad Stevens knows this. That is why Boston has maintained its asking price without showing any sign of softening.

The Key Detail: Brown Did Not Ask for This

The most underreported element of this trade situation is that Jaylen Brown has not requested a trade. The Celtics are driving this process entirely. Brown is under contract. The team is exploring the market on their own initiative, and — critically — from a position of strength.

ESPN's Brian Windhorst has stated publicly that he believes Brown "is going to get traded," and Shams Charania has reported that the Celtics are "actively engaged" with multiple suitors. But none of that urgency is coming from the player. It is coming from the front office.

That dynamic changes the entire calculus. Teams pursuing Brown cannot use any leverage related to player preference. There are no back-channel conversations where Brown's camp signals a preferred destination. The Celtics are going to extract maximum value, or they are going to keep one of the best players in the league. Full stop.

What Four First-Round Picks Actually Means

Boston's asking price of four or more first-round picks is not a negotiating bluff — it is a genuine statement about how the Celtics value Brown and what it would take to move him. Four unprotected first-round picks from a non-contending team represents more than a decade of lottery-range selections. That is franchise-altering currency.

Portland is the team most often cited as willing to approach that price. The Blazers have accumulated draft capital over their rebuild, and they have roster flexibility that most teams do not. Whether they can actually satisfy Boston's full demand — four or more firsts with acceptable protections — is the question that will determine whether this trade happens or whether Brown stays in green.

What's Next

Free agency negotiations open June 30 at 6 PM ET, and while Brown's situation is a trade rather than a free agency decision, the timing matters. Once teams begin committing resources to free agents, their draft capital and roster flexibility become encumbered in new ways. The window for Portland — or any other team — to put together the package Boston requires may be narrower than it appears.

Stevens and the Celtics front office are in no rush. They hold all the cards. But the trade market tends to move fast once free agency opens and teams begin to feel the pressure of missing out. If Portland is serious about meeting Boston's price, the next few days will tell us everything we need to know.
