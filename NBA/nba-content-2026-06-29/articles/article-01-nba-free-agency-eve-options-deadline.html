NBA Free Agency Eve: Harden Opts Out as the Offseason Officially Begins

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

June 29, 2026

Today is the day the NBA offseason truly begins. Player option deadline day — the last chance for veterans to lock in guaranteed money or bet on themselves in open market negotiations — sets the table for one of the most anticipated free agency periods in recent memory. And the early returns suggest this summer is going to be chaotic in the best possible way.

James Harden is expected to opt out of his $42.3 million option with the Cleveland Cavaliers, making himself an unrestricted free agent just as negotiations officially open tomorrow evening. Draymond Green is going the other direction, opting in to his $27.67 million deal with the Golden State Warriors. And Jose Alvarado faces a decision of his own: a $4.5 million option with the New York Knicks, the defending NBA champions.

Harden Bets on Himself — Again

This is not the first time James Harden has turned down guaranteed money. The veteran guard has a long history of structuring deals in ways that preserve his leverage, and opting out of $42.3 million with Cleveland is entirely consistent with that approach.

The decision carries real risk. Harden will turn down a substantial, guaranteed salary to compete for a new contract in a market where his value is genuinely uncertain. He is no longer the offensive juggernaut who once averaged 36.1 points per game in Houston, but he remains a dangerous playmaker and shot creator who can change a team's offensive ceiling when motivated and healthy.

By opting out, Harden becomes an unrestricted free agent — free to negotiate with any team once the window opens. What he finds on the other side of that negotiation will tell us a great deal about how the league values him at this stage of his career.

Draymond Stays Put

If Harden represents one end of the veteran spectrum — the player willing to roll the dice — Draymond Green represents the other. The four-time champion is expected to opt in to his $27.67 million deal with Golden State, choosing the certainty of a known role over the uncertainty of the open market.

For Draymond, the math is simple. He is one of the most unique defensive and playmaking talents in the league, but he is also a player whose value is deeply tied to system, roster, and chemistry. The Warriors are the system he knows best. Opting in preserves everything he has built in Golden State — his legacy, his role, his comfort — while removing the risk that the open market does not value that as highly as his current deal does.

It is the pragmatic choice, and for Draymond Green, pragmatism has always served him well.

The Timeline Every NBA Fan Needs to Know

Here is what happens next, and when:

Free agency negotiations officially open tomorrow, June 30, at 6 PM ET. Teams and players can talk, reach agreements in principle, and announce deals. But no contracts can actually be signed until July 6 at 12:01 PM ET — a full week after the negotiating window opens.

That gap between "agreed in principle" and "signed and sealed" is the most volatile stretch of the NBA offseason. Handshake deals fall apart. Players change their minds. Medical reviews raise questions. Teams pivot to backup options. Every reported agreement during that window comes with an asterisk until the ink is dry.

July 6 is going to be a historic date for another reason, too. The Giannis Antetokounmpo trade to the Miami Heat also executes on that date — meaning the league will simultaneously see blockbuster free agency signings formalized and a franchise-defining trade land at the same moment. The July 6 moratorium lift may be the single most consequential day in recent NBA history.

What's Next

Tonight is quiet. By this time tomorrow, it will not be. As free agency negotiations open at 6 PM ET on June 30, the calls will be made, the offers will come in, and the decisions that reshape rosters will begin to cascade across the league.

Harden's landing spot will be among the most closely watched developments. With Cleveland now potentially out of the picture, the question becomes which teams have the cap space, the roster fit, and the appeal to convince the veteran guard to commit. Expect names and teams to emerge quickly once that 6 PM clock ticks over.

The calm is almost over. The storm is 24 hours away.
