Kawhi to Dallas? Why the Masai Ujiri Connection Makes This Real

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes

June 29, 2026

There is one name that changes everything about the Dallas Mavericks' pursuit of Kawhi Leonard — and it is not Kawhi's. It is Masai Ujiri's.

Ujiri, now the Mavericks' team president, is the architect of the 2019 Toronto Raptors championship — the team that ran through Kawhi Leonard's superhuman performance to bring the franchise its first and only title. He knows exactly what Kawhi looks like when he is locked in and at full strength. And he is making a serious push to bring that version of Kawhi Leonard to Dallas as free agency opens tomorrow at 6 PM ET.

Why Ujiri Changes the Calculus

When a random team throws a max contract at a superstar free agent, it is noise. When the man who built your greatest professional moment comes calling with a championship offer, it is something different.

Ujiri is not just a team president making a pitch. He is the executive who understood Kawhi better than almost anyone — who put the right roster around him, managed his workload through a grueling playoff run, and watched him deliver one of the most iconic individual postseason performances in league history. The buzzer-beater against Philadelphia. The sweep of Milwaukee. The Finals win over Golden State.

Kawhi knows Ujiri builds winning teams. And Ujiri knows what Kawhi needs to succeed. That shared history is not nothing. In a free agency process where multiple teams will be competing for the same signature, the personal connection between player and team executive can be the difference-maker.

What Dallas Could Offer

Reports have indicated that the package Dallas could put together includes Klay Thompson and P.J. Washington alongside draft picks. That kind of offer would reshape both franchises involved in any potential sign-and-trade structure, and it signals that the Mavericks are treating this as a legitimate priority rather than a speculative inquiry.

Thompson, a four-time champion himself, brings shooting and defensive credibility. Washington has developed into a versatile, reliable piece. The combination, alongside picks, gives Dallas real trade assets to work with if a sign-and-trade structure is needed to make the deal work for all parties.

The Toronto Raptors have also been mentioned as interested in Kawhi — a homecoming narrative that adds a layer of competition for his services. But Dallas has the Ujiri connection, the Western Conference positioning, and the resources to make a serious run.

The Honest Questions About Kawhi at 34

This is not a pursuit without risk. Kawhi Leonard is 34 years old, and his injury history is real. He has missed significant stretches across multiple seasons due to knee issues, and the load management that has defined much of his career has occasionally strained his relationships with franchises that paid him to be present.

But here is the equally honest counter-argument: when Kawhi is healthy and motivated, he remains one of the two or three most complete two-way players in basketball. His shot-making, his defense, his ability to perform on the biggest stages — none of that has abandoned him. He is simply a player who requires careful management to stay at full capacity.

In Dallas, under Ujiri — who demonstrated in Toronto that he understands how to build around a superstar without burning him out — the conditions may be as favorable as anywhere in the league for Kawhi to produce at an elite level for two or three more meaningful seasons.

What's Next

Free agency negotiations open June 30 at 6 PM ET, and the Kawhi watch officially begins then. Dallas is in position to move quickly. Expect outreach from Ujiri's camp almost immediately once the window opens.

The Raptors will also make their pitch. There may be other teams in the mix that have not yet been publicly reported. Kawhi is a notoriously deliberate decision-maker — this could extend beyond the first few days of the negotiating window.

But if Ujiri is leading this chase, dismiss it at your own peril. He has done this before. He won a championship doing exactly this. And he is trying to do it again in Dallas.
