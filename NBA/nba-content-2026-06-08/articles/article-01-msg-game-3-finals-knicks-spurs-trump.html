MSG Hosts Its First NBA Finals Game Since 1999 — A President, a Sweep, and 53 Years of Waiting

By Marcus Cole | June 8, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The last time an NBA Finals game was played at Madison Square Garden, Patrick Ewing was exhausted and the San Antonio Spurs were champions. That was June 25, 1999. The Knicks lost the series in five games. The drought that followed would become the defining narrative of one of the NBA's most storied franchises.

Twenty-seven years later, Game 3 of the 2026 NBA Finals tips off tonight at 8:30 PM ET on ABC — and the story has completely flipped. The New York Knicks hold a 2-0 series lead over those same San Antonio Spurs. The Garden is full. And this time, New York is the team with everything to gain.

An Unprecedented Night at the Garden

Tonight's game carries layers that go well beyond basketball. President Donald Trump is attending Game 3, bringing with it the full apparatus of a presidential security detail. The New York City Police Department has banned outdoor watch parties near the arena. Fans entering MSG must comply with a strict no-bag policy and TSA-style screening procedures. Anyone planning to attend is strongly advised to arrive at least two hours before tipoff.

"It's going to be crazy," Knicks forward O.G. Anunoby said during media day Sunday. He wasn't wrong. The combination of a potential series-clinching push, a presidential appearance, and the building's historic significance creates something New York has not seen in a generation.

The Knicks are 2.5-point favorites at FanDuel for Game 3, with the total set at 216.5 points. ESPN Analytics gives New York a 58.2% win probability.

The 1999 Parallel and What It Means

There is a poetic brutality to the matchup. The Knicks and Spurs were the two franchises who met in the 1999 NBA Finals — the shortened, post-lockout season that produced one of the more unusual championship runs in league history. San Antonio won the title at MSG in Game 5. For Knicks fans who lived through it, the memory is vivid and painful.

Now those same franchises are back, and the roles have reversed. The Knicks are the ones with the commanding lead. The Spurs, anchored by 22-year-old phenom Victor Wembanyama, are the team that needs a miracle. They would need to win four consecutive games — three of them, starting tonight, at Madison Square Garden or another road venue — to claim the championship.

No NBA team has ever recovered from a 3-0 Finals deficit. The record is 0-for-the-history-of-the-league.

How New York Got Here

The Knicks arrived at this moment by building something methodical and deep. They have won 13 consecutive playoff games entering tonight, compiling a point differential of plus-270 across this entire postseason run. They swept their Eastern Conference Finals opponent in four games, then traveled to San Antonio and won Games 1 and 2 on the road — only the third team in NBA Finals history to accomplish that feat.

Game 1 was a statement: 105-95, with Jalen Brunson scoring 30 and Karl-Anthony Towns adding 18 points and 12 rebounds. Game 2 was messier — Brunson shot just 7-for-25 from the field — but KAT delivered again with 21 points and 13 rebounds, and Brunson came through on the free throw line in the final seconds to seal a 105-104 victory.

Wembanyama gave everything in Game 2. He finished with 29 points on 11-of-21 shooting, keeping the Spurs competitive even as their offense sputtered around him. But with 13 seconds left, his errant pass became a turnover, Brunson drew a foul, and the Knicks walked out of San Antonio with a stranglehold on the series.

What Each Team Needs Tonight

For the Knicks, the formula is straightforward: play their game, protect the ball, and feed the Garden's energy. Their depth has been a defining advantage all postseason. When Brunson struggled in Game 2, Towns stepped up. When Wembanyama threatened to take over, Josh Hart — who had one of the great defensive performances in Finals history with 15 rebounds, 6 assists, and 4 steals in Game 1 — was there to disrupt.

For the Spurs, the challenge is enormous. They need Wembanyama to deliver 48 mistake-free minutes in one of the loudest arenas in professional sports. They need their role players to stop disappearing. They need to fix the turnover issues that have plagued them all series. And they need to do it while 20,000 Knicks fans and a sitting president create an atmosphere unlike any in recent NBA Finals memory.

What's Next

Game 3 tips off tonight at 8:30 PM ET at Madison Square Garden on ABC. A Knicks win puts them in position to complete the first sweep of an NBA Finals in over two decades. A Spurs win, however unlikely, keeps the series alive and sets up a fascinating Game 4 scenario.

For Knicks fans who have waited 53 years for another title, tonight is the most important home game in a generation. The Garden will be ready. And for one night, basketball and history and the presidency of the United States all collide at 33rd Street in Manhattan.
