AJ Dybantsa Says He's Going No. 1 to Washington. The NBA Draft Is 15 Days Away.

By Damon Pierce | June 8, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Two weeks from today, the 2026 NBA Draft begins at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. And if AJ Dybantsa is to be believed, the opening moments will be very short: Washington Wizards on the clock, AJ Dybantsa walking to the podium.

"I'm 100% sure I'm going No. 1," Dybantsa told reporters recently.

The confidence is not misplaced. Draft analysts who cover the league more closely than anyone else have spent the better part of a year arriving at the same conclusion. Dybantsa is the consensus top prospect — a 6-foot-9 wing from BYU who averaged 25.5 points per game on 51.0% shooting from the field this season. The comparisons to Kevin Durant and Jayson Tatum are not hyperbole. They are the framework of the conversation.

Washington's Lottery Win

The Wizards earned the No. 1 pick the hard way: by going 17-65, the worst record in the NBA. Their 14.0% lottery odds were the highest available, and the ping-pong balls cooperated.

It is Washington's first No. 1 pick since John Wall in 2010. That era — Wall and Bradley Beal, built around the idea of two elite backcourt players — produced playoff appearances but never a championship. Wall's ACL injury in 2018 started an unraveling that eventually led to a complete rebuild. The Wizards have spent years at the bottom, accumulating picks and patience.

Now the patience pays off. The franchise that was searching for an identity has a once-in-a-decade prospect falling directly into their lap.

What Dybantsa Brings

The scouting reports on Dybantsa read like a wish list. At 6-foot-9 and 210 pounds, he has NBA-caliber athleticism already. His shot creation — the ability to get his own shot against any coverage — is rare at any level and nearly unheard of for a college freshman. His 51.0% field goal percentage at BYU was not the product of easy attempts; it was the product of an offensive arsenal that covers every zone of the floor.

His physical profile projects to a primary wing defender in the NBA, capable of guarding multiple positions. His ceiling, per scouts who have watched him closely, is a perennial All-Star with the potential to become more.

The oddsmakers have him at -450 to be the No. 1 selection — meaning you would need to bet $450 to win $100. In other words: it is already priced as a near certainty.

June 23 — Where Two Stories Converge

The NBA Draft is not the only major event scheduled for June 23. That date has also emerged as the informal deadline for the Giannis Antetokounmpo trade saga, with Milwaukee Bucks co-owner Jimmy Haslam wanting the situation resolved before draft night.

The result: June 23 in Brooklyn may be one of the most consequential single nights in recent NBA history. The league's most coveted draft prospect walks to one podium. The league's most coveted trade target potentially agrees to a new home hours away. The entire landscape of the Eastern Conference could shift before the second pick is announced.

What's Next

The 2026 NBA Draft takes place June 23-24 at Barclays Center in Brooklyn. AJ Dybantsa is expected to be selected No. 1 by the Washington Wizards. The second through fifth picks are projected to go to the Utah Jazz, Memphis Grizzlies, Chicago Bulls, and Los Angeles Clippers — each beginning their own version of the rebuild the Wizards have been living for years.

For Washington fans who have endured years of losing, the draft is not just about adding talent. It is a reset button. The Wizards are about to start over with the most promising prospect the franchise has ever added — and they are about to do it on national television with the entire league watching.
