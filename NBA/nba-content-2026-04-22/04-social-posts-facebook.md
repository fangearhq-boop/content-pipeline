### STORY 1: Wembanyama Playoff Debut

**Long-Form Post:**
Victor Wembanyama's playoff debut was everything the basketball world hoped for — and more.

The 22-year-old Spurs center dropped 35 points in San Antonio's 111-98 Game 1 win over Portland, making it the most productive playoff debut in franchise history. He broke Tim Duncan's previous record of 32 points set years ago, shattering it on 13-for-21 shooting including a remarkable 5-for-6 from beyond the arc. His 21 first-half points were the most by any player in the first half of a playoff debut since 1997.

What made this performance even more remarkable was the context. This was the first Spurs playoff game without Gregg Popovich on the bench since 1999 — the franchise was entering a new era without its legendary coach. Wembanyama looked like he'd been waiting for this moment his entire career.

Stephon Castle added 17 points, De'Aaron Fox contributed 17 of his own, and Devin Vassell chipped in 15 as San Antonio controlled the game throughout. Portland's Deni Avdija was brilliant in a losing effort with 30 points and 10 rebounds.

Game 2 brought a different result — Scoot Henderson's 31-point performance powered Portland to a 106-103 comeback win, tying the series 1-1. But regardless of how this series ends, Wembanyama has served notice: the future of the NBA is happening right now, and it wears Spurs silver and black.

### STORY 2: Lakers vs Rockets

**Long-Form Post:**
No Luka. No Reaves. No problem — at least not yet for the Los Angeles Lakers.

LeBron James delivered another vintage playoff performance Tuesday night, scoring 28 points with 8 rebounds and 7 assists as the Lakers downed Houston 101-94 in Game 2 to take a commanding 2-0 first-round lead. Luke Kennard contributed 23 points off the bench, and Marcus Smart delivered a clutch fourth-quarter three-pointer that effectively sealed the game.

This Lakers team is missing its two biggest stars — Luka Doncic is sidelined with a Grade 2 hamstring injury and Austin Reaves is out with a Grade 2 oblique. The two combined for over 56 points per game during the regular season. None of that has mattered in this series.

For Houston, Kevin Durant returned after missing Game 1 with a sore right knee and showed why he's still dangerous, scoring 23 points on 6-for-9 shooting with 6 rebounds and 4 assists. But Los Angeles held the Rockets to 40.4% shooting from the field and kept them under 100 points — a clear sign of defensive dominance.

The series returns to Houston for Games 3 and 4. The Rockets need a win — and fast.

### STORY 3: Magic vs Pistons

**Long-Form Post:**
The Orlando Magic just served notice to the entire Eastern Conference.

The No. 8 seed Magic walked into Little Caesars Arena on Sunday and beat the top-seeded Detroit Pistons 112-101 — wire-to-wire, never trailing for a single possession. It was one of the most complete wire-to-wire playoff upsets in recent memory.

Paolo Banchero was magnificent. The former No. 1 overall pick scored 23 points with 9 rebounds and 4 assists, putting up 17 of those points in the first half before Orlando's bench and role players took over. Every single Pistons starter scored 16 or more: Wendell Carter Jr. had 17, Desmond Bane had 17, and Jalen Suggs contributed 16 points.

Cade Cunningham did everything he could for Detroit, erupting for 39 points on 13-for-27 shooting — but his teammates couldn't support him, and Orlando's defense suffocated the rest of the Pistons' offense. Detroit has now lost 11 consecutive home playoff games, a streak dating all the way back to 2008.

Game 2 is tonight in Detroit. The Pistons need a response.

### STORY 4: Hawks vs Knicks

**Long-Form Post:**
The Atlanta Hawks pulled off one of the most memorable moments of Round 1 Tuesday night.

Trailing by 12 points after three quarters at Madison Square Garden — one of the most intimidating atmospheres in basketball — the Hawks staged a complete fourth-quarter takeover. CJ McCollum was the catalyst, finishing with 32 points on 12-for-22 shooting and leading a 28-15 final-quarter run that turned a comfortable Knicks lead into a stunning 107-106 Atlanta victory.

New York had won Game 1 comfortably 113-102 and looked to be in control heading into the fourth quarter. But McCollum had other ideas, and Atlanta's collective will to fight proved too strong for the Knicks to overcome on their own floor.

The series heads back to Atlanta tied at 1-1, with the Hawks suddenly holding all the momentum. New York will need to reset quickly before Game 3 shifts the home court advantage conversation in a serious direction.
