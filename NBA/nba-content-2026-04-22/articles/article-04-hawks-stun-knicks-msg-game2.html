CJ McCollum's 32-Point Fourth Quarter Fuels Hawks' Stunning Comeback at MSG

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 22, 2026

Madison Square Garden has witnessed plenty of heartbreak across its storied history. Monday night added another chapter — this time at the expense of the New York Knicks.

The Atlanta Hawks erased a 12-point third-quarter deficit to stun the Knicks 107-106 at MSG, tying the first-round Eastern Conference series at 1-1. CJ McCollum was the instrument of New York's destruction, finishing with 32 points on 12-for-22 shooting and taking complete ownership of the fourth quarter when the game was on the line.

A Fourth Quarter Takeover

The numbers tell a story that is almost hard to believe. Atlanta trailed by 12 points heading into the final 12 minutes of play on the Knicks' home court, in front of one of the most electric playoff atmospheres in professional basketball. The Hawks then proceeded to outscore New York 28-15 in the fourth quarter — a staggering 13-point swing that transformed a comfortable Knicks advantage into a one-point Atlanta win.

McCollum, a veteran guard who has seen playoff pressure in multiple organizations, showed the composure and shot-making ability that have defined his career. He attacked the Knicks' defense in the fourth quarter with a mix of pull-up jumpers, floaters, and drives that New York's defense simply could not contain. By the time the final buzzer sounded, McCollum had cemented his status as the latest villain to haunt MSG.

Context and Series Implications

New York had taken Game 1 comfortably, winning 113-102 to grab early home court advantage. The Knicks looked composed and in control for three quarters of Game 2, seemingly on their way to a dominant 2-0 series lead. Atlanta's ability to respond, especially on the road and from a double-digit deficit, suggests a mental toughness that New York will need to respect going forward.

The Knicks entered the playoffs as one of the Eastern Conference's most anticipated teams, seeded No. 3 with legitimate championship aspirations. A series with the No. 6 seed Hawks was considered manageable. Suddenly, that series is a 1-1 coin flip with Games 3 and 4 scheduled in Atlanta — meaning New York could face a situation where they need to win a road game to avoid falling behind.

What's Next

The series shifts to Atlanta for Games 3 and 4. The Hawks now have home court advantage in the immediate term, and after the way McCollum and company performed at MSG, they'll arrive in State Farm Arena with full confidence.

New York needs to identify what broke down defensively against McCollum in that fourth quarter and fix it quickly. The Knicks have the talent to respond — but Atlanta just showed they have the mentality to seize a series when the moment presents itself.

The Knicks-Hawks series just became one of the most intriguing in the first round. Don't look away.
