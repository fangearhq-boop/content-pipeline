Wembanyama's Historic Playoff Debut: 35 Points, Franchise Record, and a New Era in San Antonio

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 22, 2026

Victor Wembanyama had been waiting for this moment since the day he arrived in San Antonio as the No. 1 overall pick in 2023. When it finally came, he didn't just meet the moment — he obliterated it.

The 22-year-old center dropped 35 points in the Spurs' 111-98 Game 1 victory over the Portland Trail Blazers on April 19, shattering the franchise record for most points in a playoff debut. Tim Duncan had held that mark at 32. It lasted two decades. Wembanyama erased it in a single half.

His final line — 13-for-21 from the field, 5-for-6 from three-point range, 5 rebounds, 2 blocks, 1 assist — told the story of a player who was not rattled by the weight of expectations or the magnitude of the moment. He was clinical, efficient, and at times simply unstoppable.

A Record Before Halftime

What made Wembanyama's debut even more remarkable was the speed with which he established himself. He poured in 21 points in the first half alone, setting a league record for the most points scored in the first half of an NBA playoff debut since 1997. When the Spurs jogged to the locker room at halftime, it was already clear that something historic was unfolding.

San Antonio's supporting cast rose to the occasion around their star. Stephon Castle contributed 17 points, De'Aaron Fox added 17 of his own, and Devin Vassell chipped in 15. Luke Kornet provided 10 as the Spurs received contributions throughout the lineup. For Portland, Deni Avdija was brilliant in a losing effort, finishing with 30 points and 10 rebounds.

A New Era Begins

This game carried weight beyond the box score. It was the first Spurs playoff contest without Gregg Popovich coaching on the sideline since 1999 — a stretch that had produced 170 playoff victories and five NBA championships. Under new coach Mitch Johnson, San Antonio's culture and identity are being rebuilt around Wembanyama, and Game 1 offered a powerful preview of what that future could look like.

It was also the Spurs' first playoff appearance since 2019 — their first postseason victory in seven years. For a city and franchise that built its identity on championship pedigree, getting back to relevant playoff basketball matters deeply. Wembanyama made sure the return was unforgettable.

Characteristically, Wembanyama himself was understated afterward. "It's not that much," he reportedly said of his record-setting performance, deflecting credit to his teammates. That humility, paired with a 35-point debut, only added to the legend-in-progress narrative.

The Series Tightened in Game 2

Portland refused to fold. In Game 2 on April 21, Scoot Henderson delivered a 31-point performance that powered the Trail Blazers to a 106-103 road win, evening the series at 1-1. The resilience of the Blazers — who were the No. 7 seed, fresh off their own play-in run — was a reminder that this series is far from decided.

San Antonio lost despite Wembanyama's continued strong play. The series now shifts back to San Antonio for Game 3, with both teams knowing a win could provide major psychological momentum.

What's Next

Game 3 of the Spurs-Blazers series is scheduled for later this week in San Antonio. With the series knotted at 1-1, home court advantage shifts back to the Spurs — but Portland has already shown it can win on the road. Watch for Wembanyama to continue drawing double teams and elevated defensive attention, which should open opportunities for Castle, Fox, and the rest of the supporting cast.

Beyond this series, Wembanyama's playoff debut has already answered the most important question: this is a player who rises to the biggest moments. The NBA has a new generational talent, and he just introduced himself to the playoffs in the most emphatic way possible.
