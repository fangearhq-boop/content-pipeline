Magic Stun No. 1 Pistons Wire-to-Wire: Banchero Leads Stunning Game 1 Upset

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 22, 2026

The Detroit Pistons finished the 2025-26 regular season with a franchise-best 60 wins since 2005-06, claiming the No. 1 seed in the Eastern Conference with one of the most impressive turnarounds in recent NBA history. On Sunday, the No. 8 seed Orlando Magic walked into Little Caesars Arena and never gave them a chance to enjoy it.

Orlando won Game 1 wire-to-wire, 112-101 — leading from the opening tip through the final buzzer without ever relinquishing the advantage. It stands as one of the most complete first-round upsets in the early portion of the 2026 playoffs.

Banchero and a Balanced Attack

Paolo Banchero, the former No. 1 overall pick who has quietly blossomed into one of the Eastern Conference's most reliable stars, set the tone early. He finished with 23 points, 9 rebounds, and 4 assists, including 17 points in the first half as Orlando built its lead and never looked back.

What was equally impressive was the depth of Orlando's scoring. Every single Magic starter reached double figures with 16 or more points: Wendell Carter Jr. had 17, Desmond Bane contributed 17, and Jalen Suggs added 16. Head coach Jamahl Mosley deployed a game plan that spread the floor, moved the ball, and refused to let Detroit focus its defense on any single player.

Cunningham Couldn't Do It Alone

Cade Cunningham was extraordinary for Detroit, finishing with 39 points on 13-for-27 shooting. The Pistons' star, who missed significant time earlier in the season recovering from a collapsed lung, showed no signs of physical limitation and clearly left everything on the floor. But Cunningham's teammates couldn't match Orlando's collective effort, and the Magic's defense smothered the rest of the Pistons' rotation.

Detroit's home playoff struggles have now reached historic territory. The Pistons have lost 11 consecutive home playoff games, a streak that dates all the way back to 2008 — a reminder that despite their excellent regular-season record, postseason success has eluded this franchise for nearly two decades.

Game 2 Tonight

Game 2 tips off tonight (April 22) in Detroit, and the Pistons face a pivotal juncture. A 0-2 hole against a team that already dominated them at home in Game 1 would be enormously difficult to overcome. Detroit needs Cunningham to get more support and needs to find defensive answers for Banchero's combination of size, skill, and footwork.

For Orlando, the goal is simple: replicate what worked. The Magic had the Pistons on their heels for 48 minutes in Game 1. Another disciplined, balanced effort could send shockwaves through the entire Eastern Conference bracket.

What's Next

This series has quickly become one of the most compelling in the first round. A Magic victory in Game 2 would represent one of the defining upsets of this playoff cycle and validate everything Orlando has built around Banchero. Detroit, meanwhile, must find a way to channel their regular-season dominance before this series escapes their grasp entirely.

The basketball world will be watching Little Caesars Arena closely tonight. Can the No. 1 seed recover, or is the Magic about to write the chapter that defines their season?
