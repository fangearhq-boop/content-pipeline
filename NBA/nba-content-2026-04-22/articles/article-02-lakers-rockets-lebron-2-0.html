LeBron James Carries Shorthanded Lakers to 2-0 Lead Over Rockets

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 22, 2026

The Los Angeles Lakers entered the 2026 playoffs with a shorter injury report that read more like a casualty list. Luka Doncic — the team's leading scorer and orchestrator — is sidelined for the majority of Round 1 with a Grade 2 hamstring injury. Austin Reaves, their second-best player, is out with a Grade 2 oblique strain. The two combined for over 56 points per game during the regular season.

None of it has mattered. LeBron James is here.

At 41 years old, James delivered his second straight dominant playoff performance Tuesday night, scoring 28 points with 8 rebounds and 7 assists to lead Los Angeles to a 101-94 Game 2 victory over the Houston Rockets. The Lakers now hold a commanding 2-0 first-round series lead, having also won Game 1 by a score of 107-98.

James Sets the Tone, Defense Does the Rest

LeBron's scoring was essential, but the real story in Game 2 was Los Angeles's collective defensive effort. The Lakers held Houston to just 40.4% shooting from the field and kept the Rockets under 100 points — no small feat given that Kevin Durant was back in the Houston lineup after missing Game 1 with a sore right knee.

Durant showed his class upon return, finishing with 23 points on 6-for-9 shooting, adding 6 rebounds and 4 assists. But the Lakers smothered the Rockets' supporting cast, and Marcus Smart delivered a critical fourth-quarter three-pointer with 2:23 remaining that stretched the lead to eight and effectively sealed the victory. Luke Kennard provided an outstanding 23 points off the bench, giving Los Angeles a legitimate scoring threat beyond James.

Defying the Circumstances

The Lakers entered this series as underdogs. Missing Doncic and Reaves, Los Angeles was viewed by many as potentially vulnerable against a Rockets team led by Durant and a young, athletic roster. Instead, the Lakers have looked like a team playing with a clear purpose, executing coach JJ Redick's system with discipline on both ends of the floor.

James has embraced the role of team elder-statesman-turned-primary-weapon. His ability to operate in the mid-range, attack closeouts, and distribute when defenses collapse has given Houston's coaching staff no clean answers. Every time the Rockets have tried to key on James, someone else has stepped up — and when they've left James alone, he's made them pay.

What's Next

The series shifts to Houston for Games 3 and 4, where the Rockets will look to regroup on their home floor. A return to the Toyota Center typically energizes Houston's fan base and will be a genuine test for Los Angeles's road performance.

The Rockets need to find answers defensively against James while simultaneously unlocking their offensive ceiling with Durant healthy. A 3-0 deficit would be historically difficult to overcome. Houston must win Game 3 to keep the series alive in any meaningful sense.

For the Lakers, the goal is simple: stay the course. Two wins from Round 2, and one of the NBA's most remarkable shorthanded playoff runs would be complete.
