NBA Playoffs Round 1 Roundup: Cavs Cruise, 76ers Even It in Boston, Thunder Look Dominant

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 22, 2026

The 2026 NBA Playoffs first round has delivered exactly what fans hoped for: drama, upsets, and breakout performances across every corner of the bracket. With the week's first wave of games now in the books, here's where every series stands heading into today's action.

East: Cavaliers Roll, 76ers Even It Up

The Cleveland Cavaliers have been the model of efficiency in the early going. After taking Game 1 from the Toronto Raptors 126-113, Cleveland followed with a 115-105 win in Game 2 to take a commanding 2-0 series lead. The Cavs' combination of Donovan Mitchell's scoring and their collective defensive intensity has given Toronto no answers in the opening week.

The Celtics-76ers matchup delivered the drama everyone expected. Boston dominated Game 1, winning 123-91 in a statement performance that seemed to put the series in their control. But Philadelphia responded emphatically in Game 2, winning 111-97 in Boston to level the series at 1-1.

VJ Edgecombe was the story of Tuesday's Game 2, putting together a remarkable 30-point, 10-rebound performance on the road in one of the NBA's most difficult playoff environments. Jaylen Brown did everything he could for Boston — finishing with 36 points and 7 rebounds — but Edgecombe and Tyrese Maxey's backcourt combination proved too much for the Celtics to handle. The series is now a genuine toss-up heading into Game 3 in Philadelphia.

West: Thunder Issue a Statement

Oklahoma City left absolutely no doubt in Game 1 against the Phoenix Suns, routing them 119-84 in a performance that served as a reminder of why the Thunder entered the playoffs as defending champions. The 35-point margin of victory against a veteran Suns team underscored OKC's depth, defensive intensity, and SGA's continued excellence.

Game 2 tips off tonight at 9:30 PM ET on ESPN. The Suns need a dramatic response after being humiliated in Game 1 — and they'll need to do it with the Thunder at full strength. Phoenix faces a near-must-win situation in a hostile environment if they want to keep this series meaningful.

Tonight's Schedule

In addition to Thunder-Suns at 9:30 PM ET, the Pistons and Magic tip off their Game 2 tonight as well. Detroit faces enormous pressure after the No. 1 seed was beaten wire-to-wire by No. 8 Orlando in Game 1. Going down 0-2 at home would put the Pistons in uncharted, deeply uncomfortable territory.

What's Next

The first round of the 2026 playoffs is already defining itself as one of the more compelling opening acts in recent memory. Upsets (Magic over Pistons), fourth-quarter drama (Hawks at MSG), dominant two-game stretches (Lakers, Cavaliers), and veteran playoff moments (LeBron, McCollum, Edgecombe) have all appeared in the first week.

As series continue to develop, watch for the teams leading 2-0 — Cleveland and Los Angeles — to push for the close. And keep an eye on Atlanta and Orlando, two lower seeds that have already showed they belong on this stage.
