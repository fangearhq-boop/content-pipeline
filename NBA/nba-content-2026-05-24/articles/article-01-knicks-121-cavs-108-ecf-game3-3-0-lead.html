Brunson 30, Bridges 22, OG 21: Knicks Crush Cavaliers 121-108, Take 3-0 ECF Lead

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | May 24, 2026

One win. That is all that separates the New York Knicks from the NBA Finals.

Jalen Brunson scored 30 points, Mikal Bridges added 22, and OG Anunoby contributed 21 as New York routed the Cleveland Cavaliers 121-108 in Game 3 of the Eastern Conference Finals on Saturday night at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse. The Knicks now lead the series 3-0 — and no team in the history of the NBA has ever recovered from a 3-0 series deficit.

Karl-Anthony Towns posted 13 points, 8 rebounds, and 7 assists. Josh Hart recorded 12 points, 9 rebounds, 4 assists, and 5 steals. Five Knicks in double figures, each contributing in ways that went beyond their raw numbers, in a performance that illustrated exactly why this team has now won 10 consecutive playoff games.

Brunson Commands the Stage

Brunson shot 10-of-19 from the field and 10-of-12 from the free throw line, operating with the calm efficiency of a player who has done this before — even though reaching the verge of the NBA Finals is new territory for him and his franchise.

His 30 points came in all phases: mid-range pull-ups against coverage that sagged, drives that drew fouls, and floaters that barely grazed the net on their way through. Cleveland had no consistently effective method of containment. When the Cavs sent two defenders, Brunson found his teammates. When they played him straight up, he scored. Through three ECF games, he has averaged 26 points while controlling tempo on every possession that matters.

Bridges and Anunoby Put It Away

While Brunson set the table, Bridges and Anunoby did the damage. Bridges went 11-of-15 from the floor for 22 points to go with 6 rebounds, 3 steals, and 2 blocks — a complete two-way performance that silenced anyone who wondered whether his regular-season form would carry into the postseason. Anunoby added 21 points and 7 rebounds, attacking the paint and converting at a rate that stretched Cleveland's defense thin.

The combination of three 20-plus-point scorers alongside Towns' playmaking (7 assists) and Hart's defensive havoc (5 steals) left the Cavaliers scrambling throughout. Cleveland played hard — Evan Mobley delivered 24 points and Donovan Mitchell scored 23 with characteristic determination — but the Knicks simply had too many weapons working at once.

The Weight of History

The Knicks are playing at the best offensive clip among all remaining 2026 playoff teams — a 130.5 rating per 100 possessions across their current 10-game winning streak. They are shooting 60% from the field and closing to 40% from beyond the arc during that run. These are not the numbers of a team that is peaking too early or operating on borrowed time. These are the numbers of a team that has found its ceiling and is performing there consistently.

Since sweeping the Philadelphia 76ers in the Eastern Semifinals, the Knicks have not needed an emergency performance from Brunson. Every game has been won with contributions spread across the roster. That balanced approach — unusual for a team this deep in a playoff run — is what makes New York genuinely difficult to game-plan against.

What's Next

The Eastern Conference Finals returns to Cleveland for Game 4 on Monday, May 25. Tip-off is scheduled for 8 PM ET on ABC. The Cavaliers need to win to extend their season. The Knicks need one more victory to advance to the NBA Finals for the first time since 1999 — a 27-year absence that has defined the franchise's most painful era. ECF Game 4: Monday May 25, Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland, 8 PM ET, ABC. Series: Knicks lead 3-0.
