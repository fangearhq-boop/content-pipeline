ECF Game 4 Preview: Cavaliers Face Elimination, History, and an Unbeatable Knicks Streak

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 24, 2026

In the entire history of the NBA playoffs, no team has ever won a series after trailing 3-0.

The Cleveland Cavaliers will try to change that on Monday night at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, when they host the New York Knicks in ECF Game 4 at 8 PM ET on ABC. But history is not the only obstacle they face. They are also staring down a Knicks team that has won 10 consecutive playoff games, plays the best offense in these 2026 playoffs, and has two players — Brunson and Bridges — operating at peak form at exactly the wrong time for Cleveland.

The odds of survival are nearly nonexistent. But basketball games still have to be played, and the Cavaliers have too much pride to concede anything before the final buzzer sounds.

What Cleveland Needs

Donovan Mitchell has been a consistent producer in this series: 23 points in Game 3, reliable scoring throughout. He is doing his part. The question has always been what happens around him, particularly when the Knicks shift their attention his way in the fourth quarter.

Evan Mobley's 24-point performance in Game 3 was the most encouraging sign Cleveland has produced in the series. If Mobley can maintain that level — and if James Harden can find the playmaking rhythm that made him valuable during the regular season — then the Cavaliers have a theoretical path to a competitive game. Harden's ability to facilitate and score in clutch situations will be the swing variable on Monday night.

Cleveland was 6-1 at home in the 2026 playoffs before this series. Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse will be as loud as it has been all season. That environment matters, and it could give the Cavs the early energy needed to establish themselves before the Knicks' offense finds its rhythm.

The Knicks' Problem for Cleveland

New York does not have one defining weakness the Cavaliers can exploit. Brunson adapts when teams try to take him away. Bridges has emerged as a genuine secondary scorer who doesn't need setups. OG Anunoby disrupts offenses before they develop. Karl-Anthony Towns stretches the defense and creates passing angles. Hart rebounds, steals, and never stops competing.

The Knicks are also well-rested, having swept their previous opponent and managed this series with enough efficiency to avoid exhausting their rotation players. They arrive in Cleveland with an advantage in conditioning, confidence, and momentum that is difficult to overcome regardless of the venue.

What's Next

ECF Game 4 tips off Monday, May 25, at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in Cleveland. Start time is 8 PM ET on ABC. If the Knicks win, they advance to the NBA Finals for the first time since 1999. If Cleveland wins, the series continues with Game 5 back in New York on Wednesday. ECF Game 4: Monday May 25, Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse, Cleveland, 8 PM ET, ABC. Series: Knicks lead 3-0.
