WCF Game 4 Preview: Can San Antonio's Home Crowd Slow OKC's Dominant Bench?

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | May 24, 2026

Tonight at AT&T Center, the San Antonio Spurs need to find an answer they have not found across three games of this series.

The Oklahoma City Thunder lead the Western Conference Finals 2-1 and will be playing Game 4 in San Antonio starting at 8 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. The Spurs, favored by 2.5 at home, have the crowd advantage — but through three games, Oklahoma City's bench has been so dominant that home-court comfort alone will not be sufficient.

The Thunder's reserves have outscored San Antonio's backups by a combined margin of more than 50 points per game in this series. OKC's bench averages 61 points per game; San Antonio's averages 21.3. That gap does not emerge from circumstance. It reflects a roster construction advantage that the Spurs must somehow neutralize tonight.

The Wembanyama Workload Problem

Victor Wembanyama has been exceptional in this series. He is averaging north of 25 points per game and playing 41.7 minutes per night. The problem is structural: San Antonio is plus-21 with him on the floor and minus-38 when he sits. Gregg Popovich's replacement coaching staff has no good option. Play Wembanyama less and the game gets away. Play him at current rates and risk cumulative fatigue that compounds across a potential seven-game series.

The Spurs need Stephon Castle to step up as a consistent secondary scorer. Castle has moments in this series but has not sustained the kind of scoring production that makes him a genuine defensive priority. If he can find that rhythm — particularly in the first half, when OKC's starters are still managing their own footing — San Antonio could build a lead worth protecting.

OKC's Roadmap Has Worked Twice

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander controls the pace, exploits any defensive mismatch he finds, and gets to the free throw line with mechanical regularity. Behind him, Jared McCain and Jaylin Williams provide explosiveness that exhausts opposing coaches' adjustment windows. The Thunder erased a 15-point deficit in Game 3 — showing they are comfortable playing from behind and capable of sustained runs regardless of circumstance.

Oklahoma City is not yet in finish-line territory. Trailing 2-1 rather than 3-0, San Antonio has more agency than Cleveland does in the East tonight. A Spurs win resets the series and gives Wembanyama and company a chance to claim home-court advantage heading into Games 5 and 7 if the series extends. The stakes are genuine on both sides.

What's Next

WCF Game 4 tips off tonight, Sunday May 24, at AT&T Center in San Antonio. Start time is 8 PM ET on NBC and Peacock. A Thunder win would put them up 3-1 and on the verge of the NBA Finals. A Spurs win ties the series and completely changes the tenor heading into Game 5. WCF Game 4: Sunday May 24, AT&T Center, San Antonio, 8 PM ET, NBC/Peacock. Series: Thunder lead 2-1.
