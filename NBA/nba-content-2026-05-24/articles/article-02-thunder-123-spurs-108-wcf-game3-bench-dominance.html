OKC's Bench Erupts for 76 Points as Thunder Top Spurs 123-108, Lead WCF 2-1

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | May 24, 2026

The story of the Western Conference Finals is written in bench points.

Oklahoma City's reserves outscored San Antonio's backup unit 76-23 in Game 3 on Friday night at AT&T Center, as the Thunder rallied from a 15-point deficit to defeat the Spurs 123-108 and take a 2-1 series lead. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander delivered 26 points and 12 assists, going a perfect 12-for-12 from the free throw line, but it was the depth behind him that determined the outcome.

Game 4 is tonight in San Antonio. Oklahoma City leads the series and has a structural advantage the Spurs have not been able to solve.

McCain Changes Everything

Jared McCain's 24 points off the bench were the most consequential individual contribution of the night. OKC outscored San Antonio by 28 points with McCain on the floor — a differential so large it reframed the entire game. He went 10-of-21 from the field, hit timely shots across all three quarters he dominated, and created the kind of second-unit energy that forced the Spurs to rotate their starters back in earlier than desired.

The impact extended beyond his own scoring. McCain's movement pulled defenders out of position, opened lanes for SGA in pick-and-roll, and put the Spurs' coaching staff in a situation they had not prepared for: a Thunder reserve who could genuinely hurt you from the perimeter.

SGA's Flawless Night

Gilgeous-Alexander's line — 26 points, 12 assists, 12-for-12 from the free throw line — was a masterclass in controlled aggression. He attacked the basket to draw fouls, picked apart San Antonio's zone with precision passing, and made every big-moment decision correctly when the game was on the line in the fourth quarter.

Jaylin Williams added 18 points in just 22 minutes, including a four-point play in the fourth quarter that pushed OKC's lead from four to eight and effectively ended any Spurs rally. The Thunder's depth is not a narrative: it is a measurable, consistent advantage that has appeared in every game of this series.

Wembanyama's Impossible Position

Victor Wembanyama scored 26 points on 8-of-15 shooting with 4 rebounds, 3 assists, and 2 blocks. By any objective measure, it was an excellent performance. By the standard required to overcome OKC's depth, it was not enough.

Wembanyama is averaging 41.7 minutes per game in this series — a workload that cannot be reduced because the Spurs are plus-21 with him on the floor and minus-38 when he rests. The math creates a no-win scenario for San Antonio's coaching staff: play him into exhaustion, or watch the game slip away when he sits. Through three games, the Spurs have not found a second player capable of sustaining sequences against OKC's second unit.

What's Next

The Western Conference Finals resumes tonight in San Antonio for Game 4. Tip-off is 8 PM ET on NBC/Peacock. The Spurs are favored by 2.5 at home and need a win to even the series. Oklahoma City is looking to take a commanding 3-1 lead and push toward the NBA Finals. WCF Game 4: Sunday May 24, AT&T Center, San Antonio, 8 PM ET, NBC/Peacock. Series: Thunder lead 2-1.
