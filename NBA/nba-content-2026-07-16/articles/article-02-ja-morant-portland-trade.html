Ja Morant Traded to Portland Trail Blazers in Blockbuster Deal With Memphis Grizzlies

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 16, 2026

The two-time All-Star guard heads to the Pacific Northwest for Jerami Grant and Kris Murray in a deal that reshapes Portland's roster and gives Morant a fresh start.

The Ja Morant era in Memphis is over. The Portland Trail Blazers and Memphis Grizzlies completed a blockbuster trade that sends the two-time All-Star guard to the Pacific Northwest in exchange for Jerami Grant, Kris Murray, and $1 million in cash considerations. No draft picks changed hands.

Morant called the move "like starting all over again" — and that framing captures exactly what Portland is betting on. The 26-year-old was one of the most electric players in the NBA during his peak years with Memphis, averaging 22.4 points per game for his career. But injuries and off-court suspensions cost him nearly three full seasons — just 79 games played over a three-year stretch — leaving serious questions about what version of Ja Morant shows up in a new city.

Portland's Bold Swing

The Trail Blazers are sending a clear message: the rebuild is over. Portland already had a guard-heavy roster before this trade, and adding Morant to a group that includes Damian Lillard, Scoot Henderson, Jrue Holiday, Deni Avdija, and Shaedon Sharpe creates one of the most crowded backcourts in the league.

The challenge for Portland's coaching staff will be fitting all of these ball-handlers and creators into a coherent offense. Lillard and Morant can both function as primary playmakers and need the ball in their hands. Henderson and Sharpe are at-their-best as scoring threats off the dribble. The Blazers are gambling that talent wins out — and that Morant's explosive playmaking will elevate the whole group.

Morant has two years remaining on his contract at approximately $43 million per year average. At that number, Portland is clearly treating this as a long-term investment in the player Morant was — and could be again.

Memphis Moves On

From Memphis' perspective, the trade signals a commitment to a new direction. Grizzlies fans have seen Morant go from Rookie of the Year in 2020 to one of the most controversial figures in the league, and the front office decided a fresh start served both sides.

In return, Memphis gets Jerami Grant, a versatile forward who can defend multiple positions and provide veteran leadership during a transition period. Kris Murray adds youth and potential. The Grizzlies got out of Morant's contract without giving up any future draft capital — a win by any measure if you believe Morant's best basketball is behind him.

What's Next

The pressure is now on Ja Morant to prove he belongs in this conversation. Portland's front office is betting heavily on the player who averaged 27.4 points and 8.1 assists in 2022-23. If that Ja Morant shows up in Portland, the Trail Blazers are an instant Western Conference contender. If his injury and off-court issues follow him, it becomes one of the more expensive gambles in recent league history. Training camp will be the first real indicator of which version of Morant the Blazers are getting.
