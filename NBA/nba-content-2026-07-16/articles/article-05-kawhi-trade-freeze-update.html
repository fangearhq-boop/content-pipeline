Kawhi Leonard Trade Remains Frozen as NBA Investigation Into Aspiration Fraud Case Continues

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | July 16, 2026

With one year left on his contract and multiple teams interested, Leonard can't be moved until Commissioner Silver clears the way — and there is no public timeline for that to happen.

The Kawhi Leonard trade situation remains one of the most unusual in recent NBA history — not because of anything related to basketball, but because of a sprawling fraud case that has entangled the Los Angeles Clippers organization in a legal nightmare.

Leonard has one season remaining on his contract, multiple teams have expressed genuine interest in acquiring him, and yet no deal can be finalized. Commissioner Adam Silver has made clear the league's investigation into the Aspiration Sports fraud case must be resolved before any Kawhi Leonard transaction moves forward.

The Scandal at the Center

Alex Sanberg, a former executive connected to Aspiration Sports, was convicted of fraud and sentenced to 14 years in prison. The Clippers organization alleges it was defrauded through a series of transactions including a $28 million Kawhi Leonard endorsement deal and a $300 million arena naming rights agreement. Owner Steve Ballmer has stated he personally invested $60 million based on representations later found to be fraudulent.

The NBA's investigation is examining the full scope of those transactions and their relationship to the Clippers organization's finances and basketball operations. Until that investigation concludes, Silver's office will not approve a trade that could be later challenged or complicated by the ongoing legal fallout.

The Basketball Problem

From a purely basketball perspective, the freeze is costly for everyone involved. Leonard has one guaranteed year remaining on his deal, meaning any team that acquires him must decide whether to trade for a one-year rental or negotiate an extension. The longer the freeze continues, the shorter the window teams have to make that decision and plan accordingly.

For Leonard himself, the uncertainty is difficult. He has no control over when the investigation concludes, no ability to choose his next team, and is stuck in organizational limbo. Multiple reports have confirmed league-wide interest in his services, but none of that interest can be converted into a deal while the freeze holds.

What's Next

There is no public timeline on when the NBA's investigation will conclude. Silver's office has not set a deadline, and the legal proceedings surrounding Sanberg's case remain active. The Clippers will continue to operate in this holding pattern, unable to fully reshape their roster, until the league gives the green light. For the teams waiting on the sidelines, patience is the only option — and for Leonard, the wait continues.
