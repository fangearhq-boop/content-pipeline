Summer League Day 8: Cameron Carr Shines for Lakers, Magic Hold Off 76ers in Las Vegas

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | July 16, 2026

Cameron Carr led the Lakers to a Battle of Los Angeles victory while the Orlando Magic edged Philadelphia 99-92 as the Vegas tournament enters its final stretch.

The 2026 NBA Summer League in Las Vegas reached Day 8 on Wednesday, with the tournament nearing its July 19 conclusion and young players making their final pitches for roster spots. Two games stood out from Thursday's slate: a Battle of Los Angeles that saw Cameron Carr take command for the Lakers, and a closely contested Orlando-Philadelphia game that ended with the Magic holding off LaBaron Philon Jr. and the 76ers 99-92.

Carr Leads Lakers

Cameron Carr has been one of the emerging names of this year's Summer League, and he delivered another impressive performance to lead the Los Angeles Lakers past the Clippers in the Battle of Los Angeles. Carr has shown the kind of multidimensional game that generates genuine roster interest — he can score, facilitate, and defend, which is exactly what organizations look for in evaluating Summer League prospects.

The Clippers had their own standout moments this week. Kobe Sanders scored 26 points in a Clippers 108-94 win over the Washington Wizards on Day 7, continuing to build his case for a significant role. The organizational depth on display from LA's two teams has been impressive throughout the tournament.

Philon Keeps Impressing

Philadelphia's LaBaron Philon Jr. has been one of the most-talked-about prospects throughout the Summer League run. Even in a 99-92 loss to Orlando, Philon showcased the skills that have made him one of the event's standout performers. His ability to create off the dribble and make plays in traffic has drawn consistent praise from coaches and scouts on the scene in Las Vegas.

The 76ers' Summer League squad has been competitive throughout, and Philon's development storyline has given Philadelphia fans something positive to focus on during a complex offseason. Whether he earns a significant role heading into training camp remains to be seen, but his summer performance has been genuinely encouraging.

The Bigger Picture

With the Summer League wrapping on July 19, the evaluations are nearly complete. This year's field has featured top prospects including AJ Dybantsa (whose 27-point, 23-point back-to-back performances earlier this week drew enormous attention), Caleb Wilson, Darryn Peterson, and Koa Pete. These players are the next wave coming into the league, and the Summer League has given fans a compelling early look.

What's Next

Three days remain in the 2026 NBA Summer League. The tournament concludes July 19, after which teams will shift their full attention to free agency and training camp preparation. For prospects like Philon, Carr, and the dozens of other players fighting for roster spots this week, the final days in Las Vegas are their last chance to make a lasting impression before rosters are finalized ahead of the new season.
