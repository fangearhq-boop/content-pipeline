Down 24 and Out? Not Cade Cunningham — Pistons Storm Back 93-79 to Force Game 7

By Marcus Cole | May 2, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

By halftime Friday night in Orlando, the Detroit Pistons looked finished. The Magic led 62-38, their home crowd was electric, and a stunning first-round upset of the No. 1 seed was 24 minutes away from becoming reality.

Then Cade Cunningham reminded everyone that he is not an ordinary player — and that this series is very much not over.

Cunningham erupted for 32 points and 10 rebounds, with 24 of those points coming in the second half and a jaw-dropping 19 in the final quarter alone. The Pistons outscored the Magic 55-19 after halftime to win Game 6, 93-79, forcing a winner-take-all Game 7 back in Detroit on Sunday afternoon.

A Collapse for the Record Books

Whatever happened to the Orlando Magic in the second half may haunt their organization for years. At one point, Orlando missed 23 consecutive field goal attempts while Detroit embarked on a 35-5 run that turned what looked like a death blow into an absolute stunner.

The Magic became the first team since 1996-97 to lose at home while leading by 24 or more points with a chance to win a playoff series. Context rarely makes those numbers feel less brutal.

Tobias Harris added 22 points for Detroit, providing the secondary scoring punch that kept the pressure on while Orlando was searching for answers it never found. Paolo Banchero and Desmond Bane each scored 17 for the Magic, but in the final 24 minutes, those contributions might as well have been invisible.

Cade's Isiah Moment

Cunningham's fourth-quarter performance was the kind that sticks in the collective memory of a franchise. He became the first Pistons player since Isiah Thomas to record at least 30 points and 10 rebounds in an elimination game — a comparison that carries enormous weight in Detroit basketball history.

Whether Cunningham can carry that form into a Game 7 environment at home, in front of a crowd that will be shaking Little Caesars Arena, remains the central question of the series. If Friday night is any indication, the Magic made a serious mistake letting him breathe in the second half.

What's Next

Game 7 tips off Sunday at 3:30 PM ET on ABC at Little Caesars Arena in Detroit. The Pistons will have home court and a narrative on their side — the NBA's No. 1 seed that has survived everything thrown at them. The Magic, meanwhile, must process a historically bad second half and find a way to play 48 focused minutes in the most hostile environment of their young stars' careers.

The Pistons-Magic series has been the most surprising of the first round. Sunday will be the most dramatic chapter yet.
