The 2026 NBA Playoffs Second Round Is Taking Shape — Here's Everything You Need to Know

By Marcus Cole | May 2, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Through the chaos, the comebacks, the overtime shots, and the Game 7s, the 2026 NBA Playoffs second round is beginning to emerge — and the bracket looks exceptional.

Two Western Conference Semifinals matchups are confirmed. The Eastern Conference is a few days from being fully set. Here's the full picture as it stands heading into Saturday, May 2.

West: Thunder vs. Lakers

The most anticipated matchup in the Western bracket is confirmed: the No. 1 Oklahoma City Thunder will host the No. 4 Los Angeles Lakers, with Game 1 scheduled for Tuesday in Oklahoma City.

The Thunder swept the Phoenix Suns in four first-round games and have looked like the West's dominant force all season. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has been the most consistent superstar in the conference, and OKC's depth makes them dangerous on every possession.

The Lakers arrive fresh off eliminating Houston 4-2, with LeBron James playing some of the best basketball of his late-career run. Austin Reaves is healthy. The team is clicking. LeBron has faced and defeated greater challenges, and the No. 4 seed should not be taken lightly when it contains a player who has been in this position before and thrived.

West: Spurs vs. Timberwolves

The other Western Semifinal is equally compelling: the No. 2 San Antonio Spurs against the No. 6 Minnesota Timberwolves. San Antonio closed out Portland in five games behind Victor Wembanyama and De'Aaron Fox — a pairing that has proved every bit as dynamic as expected in the postseason. Minnesota upset the defending-competitive Nuggets in six games, holding together through key injuries to advance.

Wembanyama vs. the Wolves' frontcourt — including Anthony Edwards, Karl-Anthony Towns, and Rudy Gobert — is one of the premier individual matchups of Round 2.

East: Knicks Are Waiting

The New York Knicks eliminated Atlanta in six games, including an NBA playoff record 47-point halftime lead in the clincher. They've been resting since and will host Games 1 and 2 against whoever emerges from tonight's Celtics-76ers Game 7 at TD Garden.

A Celtics win sets up a matchup between two East powers that have history. A 76ers win would bring Joel Embiid and a motivated Philadelphia squad into Madison Square Garden — one of the most electric environments in professional basketball.

What's Next

The final two first-round series will be decided this Sunday. The Pistons host the Magic at 3:30 PM ET on ABC in what promises to be a feverish atmosphere in Detroit. An hour later, the Cavaliers host the Raptors at 7:30 PM ET in Cleveland, where RJ Barrett's 29-foot overtime game-winner from Friday night is still fresh in everyone's mind.

By Sunday night, the entire 2026 second round bracket will be set. Based on how the first round has unfolded, the Conference Semifinals may be even better than what we've already seen.
