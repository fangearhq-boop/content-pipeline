Lakers Eliminate Rockets 98-78 — LeBron 28, Reaves Back, LA Heads to West Semis vs. Thunder

By Jake Torres | May 2, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Los Angeles Lakers are through to the Western Conference Semifinals, and LeBron James sent Houston home with exactly the kind of performance that reminds the basketball world why he still belongs in every star-studded conversation.

Los Angeles dismantled the Rockets 98-78 on Friday night to win their first-round series 4-2, and the margin of victory barely captures how thoroughly dominant the game really was. The Lakers didn't just beat Houston — they broke them in the first half, building a 27-3 run that turned a competitive playoff game into a coronation.

LeBron Sets the Tone

James finished with 28 points, 8 assists, and 7 rebounds in 10-of-25 shooting, orchestrating one of the most efficient offensive performances of the series when it mattered most. By halftime, Los Angeles led 49-31, and the Rockets never mounted a credible threat in the final two quarters.

This was the LeBron formula at its finest — probing the defense, finding weaknesses, and then letting gravity do the rest as his presence opened up the floor for everyone around him.

Reaves' Return Changes Everything

Austin Reaves had been sidelined for more than three weeks with an oblique injury, and his absence was felt throughout the series. His return in Game 5 immediately shifted the team's dynamic, and in Game 6 he delivered 15 points to underscore just how important his health is to this Lakers squad.

Deandre Ayton added 7 points and 16 rebounds, providing the interior presence that controlled the glass and kept Houston from finding second-chance opportunities late in the game.

Houston Falls Short Again

The Rockets entered this series as a scrappy, energetic team capable of pushing any opponent. They pushed Los Angeles to six games, and that alone is a credit to their resilience. But without Kevin Durant — who missed the series with an injury — and without a reliable answer for LeBron James on either end of the floor, they ultimately fell short of the breakthrough their young core is still building toward.

What's Next

The Lakers now face the most formidable obstacle in the Western bracket: the No. 1 seed Oklahoma City Thunder. OKC swept the Phoenix Suns in four first-round games and will host Game 1 on Tuesday night with full home-court advantage.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander and the Thunder haven't shown a weakness all spring. But LeBron James has won championships against long odds before, and with Reaves healthy and the team playing with momentum, the Lakers will head to Oklahoma City believing they can pull off another statement series win.
