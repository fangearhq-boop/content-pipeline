Celtics vs. 76ers Game 7 Preview — Embiid Probable, Tatum Cleared, Everything on the Line Tonight

By Jake Torres | May 2, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Tonight at TD Garden in Boston, the most gripping first-round series of the 2026 NBA Playoffs reaches its conclusion. The Celtics and 76ers — two franchises bound by one of basketball's greatest rivalries — play Game 7 at 7:30 PM ET on NBC, with an Eastern Conference Semifinals berth on the line.

Boston enters as 8.5-point favorites. Philadelphia enters with the momentum of back-to-back wins and a player who, when healthy, can control any basketball game on the planet.

Embiid's Status Is the Story

Joel Embiid is listed as probable for tonight's game due to his abdomen, still not fully healed from the appendectomy he underwent on April 9. That he has played at all is remarkable. That he has played well — 19 points, 10 rebounds, and 8 assists in a Game 6 that Philadelphia won 106-93 — is something beyond remarkable.

His mentality ahead of Game 7 tells you everything you need to know about what the 76ers are walking into Boston with. "I tell my brain I feel amazing," Embiid told reporters Friday. "So I feel amazing." Whether his body can match that intention for 48 minutes against Boston's physical frontcourt will be the defining storyline of the night.

Tatum Gets the All-Clear

Boston's best news came Friday when Jayson Tatum was removed entirely from the injury report after showing calf discomfort during Thursday's Game 6. Tatum's full availability is a significant development for the Celtics, who need their franchise player operating at maximum capacity if they're going to close out Philadelphia on their home floor.

Tyrese Maxey, who leads Philadelphia's offensive charge alongside Embiid, is listed as probable with a right tendon finger strain. Maxey has been brilliant in the second half of this series — he scored 30 in Game 6 — and the Celtics would welcome any limitation on his explosiveness.

A Series of Momentum Swings

Boston led this series 3-2 heading into Thursday's Game 6, with a chance to advance on Philadelphia's home floor. The 76ers won 106-93. Paul George had 23 points, Embiid had 19 points, 10 rebounds, and 8 assists, and suddenly the momentum had completely reversed. Now it's a neutral game in the sense that both teams have proven they can beat the other, and home court may matter less than who executes under pressure.

The winner faces the New York Knicks in the Eastern Conference Semifinals. New York has been resting since their 4-2 series win over Atlanta, and they'll have several days of preparation on whoever emerges from TD Garden tonight.

What's Next

Tip-off is at 7:30 PM ET tonight on NBC. If Boston wins, they host Games 1 and 2 of the second round against the Knicks beginning Monday. If Philadelphia advances, they'll head into hostile Knicks territory at Madison Square Garden with an emotional win fueling one of the more improbable playoff runs in recent memory.

This series has had everything: comebacks, injuries, brilliant individual performances, and a rivalry that never disappoints. Tonight is the final chapter — and it promises to be exceptional.
