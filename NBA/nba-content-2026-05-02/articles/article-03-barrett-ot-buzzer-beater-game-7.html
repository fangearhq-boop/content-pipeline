RJ Barrett's 29-Foot OT Miracle Keeps Raptors Alive — Cavaliers-Toronto Headed to Game 7

By Damon Pierce | May 2, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

You can describe exactly what happened at the end of overtime in Cleveland on Friday night, but the description will never fully capture what it felt like to watch it.

The Toronto Raptors trailed by one point. Time was running out. RJ Barrett caught the ball 29 feet from the basket with the clock ticking toward zero — and he launched a three-pointer into the Cleveland night that changed the entire series.

It went in. With 1.2 seconds remaining. The Raptors won 112-110 in overtime, tied their first-round series with the Cavaliers at 3-3, and forced a Game 7 that will be played Sunday night in Cleveland.

The Shot That Made History

Context makes Barrett's moment even more staggering. He became the first player in NBA history to hit a game-winning shot in the final two seconds of overtime to prevent his team's elimination in a playoff series. That is not hyperbole. That is a fact recorded in the league's record books as of Friday, May 1, 2026.

Barrett finished with 24 points, 9 rebounds, and 3 assists in 43 minutes of basketball. He was not even necessarily the best player on the floor for Toronto — but he was the right player with the right shot at the right moment.

Barnes Elevates to Another Level

While Barrett provided the most dramatic moment, Scottie Barnes may have been the most complete performer on either side. Barnes posted 25 points and 14 assists — a combination that reflects both the scoring punch and the playmaking vision of a player who has elevated throughout this postseason. Add 7 rebounds and Toronto's all-time-great Game 6 comes into full focus.

James Harden was quietly excellent as well, finishing with 16 points, 9 rebounds, and 9 assists to anchor the offense through regulation and overtime.

Cleveland Had the Answers — Almost

Cleveland did not play a bad game. Evan Mobley was outstanding with 26 points and 14 rebounds, and Donovan Mitchell responded to every Toronto surge with 24 points of his own. The Cavaliers had the lead. They had their crowd. For two seconds, they had a series win.

Now they must regroup and prepare to close it out at home in a Game 7 environment against a Raptors team that has proven, twice now, that it can win in Cleveland.

What's Next

Game 7 tips off Sunday at 7:30 PM ET at Rocket Mortgage FieldHouse in Cleveland. Toronto has won there before and has a superstar who just made one of the most remarkable shots in playoff history. Cleveland has home court, the better roster on paper, and something to prove after letting this series slip.

Whoever advances will face the winner of the Pistons-Magic Game 7 earlier that same afternoon. One of the most chaotic first rounds in recent memory is about to have its final chapter written.
