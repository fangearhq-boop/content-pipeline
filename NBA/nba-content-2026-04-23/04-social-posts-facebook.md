### STORY 1: Thunder 120, Suns 107 — SGA 37 + Williams Injury

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 9:00 AM ET

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander didn't just accept the NBA Clutch Player of the Year award Wednesday night — he performed his acceptance speech on the hardwood.

SGA dropped 37 points on 13-of-25 shooting with 9 assists as the Oklahoma City Thunder dominated the Phoenix Suns 120-107 in Game 2, taking a commanding 2-0 first-round series lead. Chet Holmgren added 19 points in what looked like another easy evening for the defending champions.

But the night ended on an anxious note. Jalen Williams, who had 19 points through three quarters, left the game with a left hamstring injury in the third and never returned. Thunder head coach Mark Daigneault said the team believes it was "aggravated" — significant, since Williams already missed 30 games this season with a right hamstring strain.

For Phoenix, Dillon Brooks fought hard with 30 points before fouling out in the fourth quarter. Devin Booker added 22 and Jalen Green contributed 21, but the Suns couldn't overcome SGA's brilliance.

The Thunder face a real question heading into Game 3 on Sunday: Can they keep rolling without their second-best player?

### STORY 2: Pistons 98, Magic 83 — Cade's Bounce-Back

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 11:00 AM ET

Cade Cunningham showed Detroit exactly why he's their franchise cornerstone — and then some.

After pouring in 39 points in Game 1 of their first-round series against Orlando and watching it result in a loss, Cunningham flipped the script entirely in Game 2. He finished with 27 points, 11 assists and 6 rebounds, but the most important stat was the third-quarter score: Detroit 38, Orlando 16.

That quarter was Cade at his most complete — creating for others, attacking the rim, making the Pistons impossible to guard. When it was over, the Pistons had an 98-83 victory and a series tied at 1-1.

But perhaps the most meaningful number is 11. As in, 11 consecutive home playoff losses for the Detroit Pistons before Wednesday night. A streak that started in 2008 and became part of the franchise's identity — over. Done. Erased.

Paolo Banchero had 18 points for Orlando and Jalen Suggs added 19, but neither could match the collective energy Detroit brought in front of a delirious home crowd.

The series heads to Orlando tied up. Advantage: anyone's guess.

### STORY 3: Timberwolves 119, Nuggets 114 — Edwards 30/10 Comeback

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 1:00 PM ET

Nineteen points down. Most teams fold. Anthony Edwards is not most players.

On Monday in Denver, the Minnesota Timberwolves found themselves trailing the Nuggets 44-25 in the second quarter — on the road, in a building where Denver rarely loses in the postseason. It looked like the Wolves might get blown out and fall into a 0-2 series hole.

Then Edwards decided enough was enough.

By the time the final buzzer sounded, Edwards had 30 points (10-for-25, 7-for-9 from the line) and 10 rebounds. Donte DiVincenzo hit a massive three-pointer with 1:05 left to seal it. Minnesota won 119-114 and tied the series at 1-1.

The other MVP of the night: Rudy Gobert. The big man physically challenged Nikola Jokić all night, holding the three-time MVP to 24 points on 8-of-20 shooting. Jokić and Jamal Murray combined to shoot just 2-for-12 in the fourth quarter, when it mattered most.

Murray finished with 30 points for Denver, but it wasn't enough to overcome Minnesota's collective will.

Game 3 is tonight in Denver. The Wolves are alive — and SGA's defending-champion squad just lost Jalen Williams. Nothing about these playoffs is set in stone.
