Down 19, Anthony Edwards Would Not Let Minnesota Lose — Wolves Stun Nuggets 119-114 in Game 2

By Damon Pierce | Hoop Heroes | April 23, 2026

The score read 44-25 early in the second quarter at Ball Arena in Denver. The Minnesota Timberwolves were staring at a 0-2 series deficit, already in trouble on the road against a Nuggets team that thrives in pressure situations. It was the perfect moment for a collapse.

Anthony Edwards had other ideas.

The Timberwolves' star poured in 30 points, grabbed 10 rebounds and willed Minnesota back into the game, as the Wolves completed one of the more stunning early-playoff comebacks in recent memory — a 119-114 victory in Game 2 on April 20 that evened the first-round series at 1-1.

The Comeback Unfolds

Denver opened Game 2 with an overwhelming first quarter, outscoring the Wolves 39-25 and building the kind of lead that typically ends a road team's competitive spirit. But Minnesota matched that exact margin in the second period — outscoring the Nuggets 39-25 in the second to cut it to a manageable gap by halftime.

Edwards finished with 10-of-25 shooting from the field and 7-of-9 from the free-throw line. He made himself felt across every phase, particularly in transition and at the rim in the fourth quarter. Donte DiVincenzo delivered the dagger — a three-pointer with 1:05 remaining that created the separation Minnesota needed. DiVincenzo finished with 16 points. Jaden McDaniels added 14, Bones Hyland 13 and Naz Reid 11.

Gobert Locks Down Jokić When It Mattered

As impressive as Edwards was offensively, Rudy Gobert's defensive performance may have been the most important factor in Minnesota's win. Nikola Jokić, the three-time MVP, finished with 24 points on 8-of-20 shooting — numbers that look respectable until you see that he and Jamal Murray combined to shoot just 2-for-12 in the fourth quarter.

Gobert challenged Jokić at the rim repeatedly, disrupting the easy post touches and forcing the Nuggets star to work harder than he's accustomed to. It was a performance that reminded the basketball world that rim protection remains one of the most undervalued skills in playoff basketball.

Murray scored 30 for Denver, but even his prolific night couldn't stop the Wolves' collective momentum.

What's Next

Game 3 is tonight (April 23) in Denver at 9:30 PM ET on ESPN. The Nuggets host back on their home floor after giving up a 19-point lead — a scenario that tends to produce motivated basketball from a franchise with Jokić's competitive fire. But Minnesota has shown it belongs in this series, and Edwards has shown he can be the best player on the floor when it matters. The Wolves-Nuggets series just became the most intriguing matchup of the first round.
