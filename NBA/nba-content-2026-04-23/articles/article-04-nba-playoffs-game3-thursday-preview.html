NBA Playoffs Game 3 Thursday: Knicks-Hawks, Wolves-Nuggets and Cavs-Raptors All on the Line

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 23, 2026

Thursday delivers a three-game playoff slate that could reshape several first-round series in a single evening. Two series that are knotted at 1-1 get their decisive Game 3s, and a third gives one team the chance to complete a sweep.

Knicks at Hawks — 7 PM ET (ESPN)

New York and Atlanta share a 1-1 series after one of the first round's most dramatic moments: CJ McCollum's 32-point eruption helped the Hawks erase a 12-point third-quarter deficit and steal Game 2 at Madison Square Garden, 107-106. The Knicks won Game 1 at home 113-102, so neither team has won on the road yet.

Tonight in Atlanta, the Hawks get their first home game of the series. State Farm Arena will be loud. McCollum will look to build on his Game 2 momentum. The Knicks, meanwhile, need a road win to reclaim series control — and they'll need to fix whatever defensive breakdowns allowed McCollum to go nuclear in the fourth quarter.

Cavaliers at Raptors — 8 PM ET (Prime Video)

Cleveland leads this series 2-0 after comfortable wins of 126-113 and 115-105. The Cavs have been dominant and show no signs of slowing down. For Toronto, this is a must-win game — teams that lose Game 3 to go down 0-3 historically have almost no chance of advancing.

The Raptors will lean on home crowd energy and the desperation that comes with facing elimination. Cleveland has all the advantages on paper. Whether Toronto can manufacture a miracle at Scotiabank Arena is tonight's great unknown.

Wolves at Nuggets — 9:30 PM ET (ESPN)

The best series in the first round — and arguably the best game of the night — closes the Thursday slate. Minnesota stunned Denver with a 19-point comeback in Game 2, leaning on Anthony Edwards' 30-point, 10-rebound performance and Rudy Gobert's interior defense to even the series at 1-1.

Now Denver gets a chance to respond at home. Nikola Jokić, who struggled in the fourth quarter of Game 2, will be motivated. The Nuggets haven't lost consecutive home playoff games often under coach Michael Malone. But the Wolves have momentum, and Edwards is playing the best basketball of his career.

Game 3 of Wolves-Nuggets could be the defining game of the entire first round.

What's Next

All three games are essential viewing tonight. Friday brings more: Celtics-76ers Game 3 in Philadelphia at 7 PM ET, and Spurs-Blazers Game 3 in Portland at 10:30 PM ET. The 2026 first round is delivering the goods — every night feels like it matters.
