SGA Drops 37 as Thunder Top Suns in Game 2, But Jalen Williams Exits With Hamstring Injury

By Jake Torres | Hoop Heroes | April 23, 2026

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander received the NBA Clutch Player of the Year award before tip-off on Wednesday, then reminded everyone why he won it. SGA scored 37 points on 13-of-25 shooting with 9 assists as the Oklahoma City Thunder rolled past the Phoenix Suns 120-107 in Game 2, taking a commanding 2-0 first-round series lead.

But the victory came with a major asterisk: Jalen Williams left the game in the third quarter with a left hamstring injury and never returned, casting a shadow over what should have been an uncomplicated Thunder celebration.

SGA at His Brilliant Best

Gilgeous-Alexander picked up right where Game 1 left off, putting together a complete performance that made the Suns look helpless at times. He had 17 points on 7-of-13 shooting at the half, then pushed the advantage home in the third quarter before the Williams injury shifted the mood inside Paycom Center.

Chet Holmgren contributed 19 points in what was becoming a familiar Thunder formula — SGA orchestrates everything, Holmgren handles the interior, and everyone else fills a role. It worked again. Oklahoma City held a 65-57 halftime lead and pulled away in the second half.

Williams' Hamstring Scare

The most pressing storyline leaving Game 2 has nothing to do with the scoreboard. Jalen Williams, who had 19 points through three quarters, missed a contested layup mid-third quarter, took a few steps, then clutched his left leg. He exited immediately and did not return.

Thunder coach Mark Daigneault said after the game: "We think he aggravated his left hamstring. We'll take a look at it in the next couple of days, and we'll update you guys appropriately."

The context makes this all the more alarming. Williams already missed 30 games this season with a right hamstring strain, and also missed time at the start of the year recovering from offseason wrist surgery. A Grade 2 hamstring strain typically sidelines a player for three to six weeks — a timeline that would put Williams at risk of missing significant playoff rounds if OKC advances.

Phoenix Fights, But Not Enough

The Suns were not without effort. Dillon Brooks was aggressive all night, finishing with 30 points before fouling out in the fourth quarter — a costly disqualification that left Phoenix without its most physical wing defender in the game's closing minutes. Devin Booker added 22 points and Jalen Green chipped in 21, but Phoenix could never find consistent offensive rhythm against Oklahoma City's smothering defense.

Oklahoma City won Game 1 by 35 points and followed it up with this 13-point victory. The Suns face a steep climb to get back in the series.

What's Next

The Thunder's biggest question heading into Game 3 on Sunday in Phoenix isn't matchup-related — it's medical. An MRI on Williams' hamstring will determine severity. If Oklahoma City gets good news, they are the clear favorite to close out this series quickly. If Williams is out for multiple weeks, SGA will need to shoulder even more offensive responsibility against a Suns team that still has Brooks, Booker and Green.

Game 3 is Sunday at Footprint Center in Phoenix. Tip-off time to be confirmed. For now, the Thunder hold the edge in the series — and the suspense around Williams' status.
