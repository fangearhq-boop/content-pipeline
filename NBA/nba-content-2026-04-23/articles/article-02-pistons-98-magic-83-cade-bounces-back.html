Cade's 27/11 and a 38-16 Third Quarter: Pistons Even Series, End 18-Year Home Playoff Curse

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 23, 2026

He scored 39 points in Game 1 and lost. So Cade Cunningham came back for Game 2 and became a different kind of player entirely — one who makes everyone around him better. The result: Detroit's most dominant playoff performance in years.

Cunningham finished with 27 points, 11 assists and 6 rebounds as the Detroit Pistons defeated the Orlando Magic 98-83 on Wednesday to even their first-round series at 1-1. The victory ended Detroit's 11-game home playoff losing streak, a remarkable streak of futility that had stretched all the way back to 2008.

The Third Quarter That Said Everything

Game 2 was competitive enough through the first half. But when the teams came out for the third quarter, Detroit announced its intentions clearly: this was not going to go to the wire. The Pistons outscored the Magic 38-16 in the third period — a run that turned a game into an argument.

Cunningham was the conductor throughout. He found cutters, initiated the fast break, pulled up for mid-range jumpers when the defense sagged, and ran the offense with a maturity that belied his age. After shooting his way through the Magic in Game 1, he showed something more valuable in Game 2: the ability to dominate a game without needing to be the primary scorer.

The Streak Ends

Eleven consecutive home playoff losses. The streak began in 2008 and became a franchise footnote — a symbol of the Pistons' long journey through irrelevance. The crowd at Little Caesars Arena knew exactly what was at stake, and when the final buzzer sounded on a 15-point Detroit victory, the celebration was less about advancing in a series and more about closing a chapter.

For a franchise that has been rebuilding for the better part of a decade, making the playoffs as the Eastern Conference's top seed and winning a home playoff game in the same year counts as genuine progress.

Orlando's Response

Paolo Banchero had 18 points for the Magic — a step down from his 23-point Game 1 performance. Jalen Suggs was the bright spot with 19 points off the bench, but Orlando couldn't match Detroit's third-quarter eruption or Cunningham's overall performance. The Magic were outworked on the glass and outpaced in the half-court offense once Cunningham got going.

Orlando's Game 1 win showed they can beat Detroit. But Detroit's Game 2 performance showed they can handle adversity and respond with force.

What's Next

The series shifts to Orlando for Game 3 on Friday, giving the Magic the home-court advantage they used to steal Game 1. Banchero will need a more dominant performance, and Orlando will need to find an answer for Cunningham's two-way impact. Detroit, meanwhile, plays with confidence — a No. 1 seed that has answered its first real postseason challenge. Game 3 in Orlando is Friday. The Pistons are ready.
