2026 NBA Playoffs Round 1: Three Teams on the Brink of a Sweep, Five Series Perfectly Tied

By Marcus Cole | Hoop Heroes | April 23, 2026

Two games into eight first-round series, the 2026 NBA Playoffs have already produced plenty of surprises and clarity in equal measure. Three favorites are in commanding control. Five series are anyone's to win. And one star player's health suddenly puts the defending champion's title run in doubt.

Here is where everything stands heading into a packed Thursday slate.

The 2-0 Leaders

Three teams have won both their games and look positioned to close their series quickly:

The Oklahoma City Thunder lead the Phoenix Suns 2-0 after wins of 119-84 and 120-107. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has been unguardable, but the Thunder are holding their breath over Jalen Williams, who left Game 2 with a left hamstring injury.

The Los Angeles Lakers lead the Houston Rockets 2-0 after wins of 107-98 and 101-94. LeBron James has delivered with both Luka Doncic and Austin Reaves sidelined by injury, including a 28-point, 8-rebound, 7-assist performance in Game 2.

The Cleveland Cavaliers lead the Toronto Raptors 2-0 after wins of 126-113 and 115-105. Cleveland has looked dominant in both games and hosts Game 3 tonight in Toronto — a potential knockout blow.

The Five Tied Series

The parity in five other matchups is striking:

The Detroit Pistons and Orlando Magic are tied 1-1. Orlando stole Game 1 in Detroit (112-101); Detroit dominated Game 2 (98-83) behind Cade Cunningham's 27-point, 11-assist showing and a 38-16 third quarter.

The Minnesota Timberwolves and Denver Nuggets are tied 1-1. Denver won Game 1 (116-105); Minnesota came back from 19 points down to win Game 2 (119-114), with Anthony Edwards delivering 30 points and 10 rebounds.

The Atlanta Hawks and New York Knicks are tied 1-1. New York won Game 1 at MSG (113-102); Atlanta's CJ McCollum erupted for 32 points to steal Game 2 (107-106) with a 28-15 fourth quarter.

The Boston Celtics and Philadelphia 76ers are tied 1-1. Boston blew out Philly in Game 1 (123-91); VJ Edgecombe's 30-point, 10-rebound Game 2 led Philadelphia to a 111-97 win.

The San Antonio Spurs and Portland Trail Blazers are tied 1-1. Victor Wembanyama's record 35-point debut led the Spurs to Game 1 (111-98); Portland bounced back in Game 2 (106-103, Scoot Henderson 31 pts).

What's Next

Thursday features three Game 3s: Knicks at Hawks (7 PM ET, ESPN), Cavs at Raptors (8 PM ET, Prime Video), and Wolves at Nuggets (9:30 PM ET, ESPN). Friday adds Celtics-76ers in Philadelphia (7 PM ET) and Spurs-Blazers in Portland (10:30 PM ET). Weekend Game 3s include Lakers-Rockets in Houston and Thunder-Suns in Phoenix. The 2026 NBA Playoffs are only beginning — and the first round is already delivering exactly what fans hoped for.
