### STORY 3: Kawhi Leonard Trade Still Frozen — Clippers Say They Were "Defrauded"

**Long-Form Post:**
The Kawhi Leonard trade to Toronto is still not done. And the reason just got more complicated.

The deal agreed June 30 — Leonard heading to the Raptors for Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, and future picks — has been on hold pending an NBA investigation into a $28 million endorsement deal between Kawhi and Aspiration, a financial tech company that also held a $300 million arena naming rights deal with the Clippers.

Today, a new layer: the Clippers say they were defrauded. They have cooperated fully with the investigation — dozens of interviews, tens of thousands of documents. Their argument is that Joe Sanberg, Aspiration's founder, acted independently and without their knowledge. Sanberg has now been convicted and sentenced to 14 years in prison.

The NBA told the Raptors to "assume the risk" of whatever the probe concludes. Toronto is still waiting. No timetable for the investigation to conclude has been given.

### STORY 5: Ace Bailey Back Spasms — Jazz's No. 5 Pick Uncertain

**Long-Form Post:**
The Utah Jazz are hoping what happened to Ace Bailey on Sunday is a temporary setback and nothing more.

Bailey, the Jazz's No. 5 overall pick in the 2026 draft, left his Summer League game at halftime on July 12 with back spasms and did not return. He was visibly stiff throughout before being ruled out for the second half. His status for the remainder of Summer League is uncertain.

Back spasms in young players are typically not long-term concerns — often the result of overexertion or muscle fatigue. But they are worth monitoring, especially when the player is one of the five most important pieces of a franchise's future. Utah needs Bailey healthy and developing. Every Summer League game matters.

No official update from the Jazz has been announced. The expectation is that he will be evaluated day-to-day.
