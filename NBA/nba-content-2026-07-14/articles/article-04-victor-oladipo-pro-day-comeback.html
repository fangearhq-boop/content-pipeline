Victor Oladipo Holds Las Vegas Pro Day in Bid for NBA Comeback

By Marcus Cole | July 14, 2026

The former two-time All-Star is working out in front of NBA decision-makers today, looking to earn his way back into the league after years of battling injuries.

Victor Oladipo has never stopped believing he belongs in the NBA. On Tuesday in Las Vegas, he's doing something about it.

The former two-time All-Star is holding a pro day workout today, putting his skills in front of team personnel who are in town for NBA Summer League. The timing is deliberate — when the league's decision-makers are all in one place, that is exactly the moment to remind them what you can still do.

Oladipo's path to this point has been anything but smooth. A devastating quad tendon rupture in January 2019 altered the trajectory of what had been one of the league's most exciting careers. Knee issues followed. There were stretches where his future in the NBA felt genuinely uncertain.

The Long Road Back

At his peak, Oladipo was not just a scorer — he was a problem. Long arms, quick hands, and the kind of defensive intensity that made him one of the better two-way guards in the game. In Indiana, he blossomed into an All-Star twice over and became the face of a franchise that believed it was building something real around him.

The injury changed that trajectory. Recovery timelines stretched. Expectations were managed. And eventually, the question became whether he could regain any meaningful version of the player he used to be.

Every comeback attempt since has carried that weight. Today's workout is the latest chapter in a story that, for Oladipo, refuses to end on anything other than his own terms.

What Teams Are Looking For

NBA teams evaluating a veteran presence in free agency this summer are looking for one thing above all else: reliability. Can Oladipo stay healthy? Can he contribute in a defined role without needing the ball in his hands to be effective?

If today's workout answers those questions convincingly, there will be teams interested. The market for experienced, defensively capable guards never fully disappears. The question is always whether the player in question can hold up over a full season.

Oladipo, by all reports, has been training with purpose. Today is the day he shows it.

What's Next

If his workout generates interest, the expectation is that Oladipo would work out individually with one or more teams before signing. The summer signing window is open, and teams looking to add veteran depth have until training camp to fill out their rosters.

Whether Oladipo gets another NBA contract depends on what scouts see today in Las Vegas. He has done the work. The rest is out of his hands.
