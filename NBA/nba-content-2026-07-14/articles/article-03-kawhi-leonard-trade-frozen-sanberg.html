Kawhi Leonard Trade Remains Frozen — Clippers Claim They Were Defrauded as Sanberg Gets 14 Years

By Jake Torres | July 14, 2026

The Clippers say they cooperated fully with the NBA's investigation and believe they were victimized by Joe Sanberg. The trade to Toronto is still officially on hold with no timeline for resolution.

The Kawhi Leonard trade to the Toronto Raptors was agreed on June 30. More than two weeks later, it is still not official, and the investigation behind the hold just got a new chapter.

Joe Sanberg, the founder of Aspiration — the financial technology company at the center of the NBA's probe — has been convicted and sentenced to 14 years in prison. And the Los Angeles Clippers are now making clear: they believe they were the ones defrauded.

The Clippers say they cooperated fully with the NBA's investigation, turning over dozens of interviews and tens of thousands of documents. Their position is that Sanberg acted independently, that the $28 million endorsement deal between Kawhi Leonard and Aspiration was done without the team's knowledge or authorization, and that the organization was itself deceived in the process.

What the Investigation Is Looking At

The NBA launched its probe after questions surfaced about the relationship between the Kawhi-Aspiration endorsement deal and the $300 million, 23-year arena naming rights agreement Aspiration held with the Clippers. The concern: did the Clippers effectively provide Kawhi with additional compensation above the allowable maximum by steering a major business partner toward him?

Clippers owner Steve Ballmer has said publicly that he personally invested $60 million in Aspiration but denies having any knowledge of the endorsement arrangement with Kawhi. The league takes these arrangements seriously — any connection between player compensation and team business relationships falls into murky territory under the collective bargaining agreement.

The NBA told the Raptors to "assume the risk" of whatever outcome the investigation produces. Toronto agreed the trade in principle with that understanding. Whether that means they take on Leonard regardless of findings, or whether certain outcomes could void the deal entirely, remains unclear from public statements.

The Raptors Wait

For the Raptors, the limbo is real. The agreed trade package — Brandon Ingram, Gradey Dick, and a portfolio of future draft picks heading to Los Angeles — represents a significant organizational commitment to acquiring Leonard. Toronto cannot fully plan its offseason around him until the deal is official, and no timetable for that has been provided.

Sanberg's 14-year prison sentence adds a layer of clarity to the legal picture, even if it does not immediately accelerate the NBA's internal process. The league is conducting its own investigation separate from any criminal proceedings and will reach its own conclusions on a timeline it controls.

What's Next

The NBA has not indicated when its investigation will conclude. Both teams continue to wait. If the league clears the trade, it moves forward with the original terms. If it finds violations, the consequences for the Clippers could range from fines and draft pick penalties to the trade being altered or voided entirely.

Every day the investigation continues is a day the Raptors cannot fully operate as the team they intend to be — and a day the Clippers cannot close a chapter they would rather put behind them.
