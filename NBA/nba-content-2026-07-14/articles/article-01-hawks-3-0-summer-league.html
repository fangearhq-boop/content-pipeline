Atlanta Hawks Are Summer League's Biggest Story at 3-0 After Day 6 Wins

By Marcus Cole | July 14, 2026

Kobe Johnson's 30-point performance headlines a dominant Hawks run in Las Vegas, while Day 6 delivers clutch finishes and standout performances across the board.

Nobody circled Atlanta on their Summer League preview sheet. That oversight is looking more embarrassing by the day.

The Atlanta Hawks improved to a perfect 3-0 in NBA Summer League play on Sunday, rolling past the Boston Celtics 102-90 in Las Vegas. Kobe Johnson led the way with 30 points, while Henri Veesar added 20 in what was an authoritative performance from a team that has simply refused to lose.

Three games in, Atlanta is not just winning — they're winning convincingly. Their margin of victory suggests a roster with real depth and a system that's already clicking, the kind of early-summer cohesion that coaches and front offices notice even when the casual fan is watching other stories unfold.

The Hawks Look Like the Real Deal

Johnson's 30-point night was the type of performance that forces teams to update their internal scouting notes. Playing with the kind of assertiveness that coaches talk about when they say "playing free," he attacked the paint, got to the line, and knocked down shots from the perimeter when Boston tried to sag off him.

Veesar's 20 points provided complementary production that gave Atlanta's offense multiple options — the definition of a well-constructed Summer League roster. The 102-90 final score doesn't fully capture how in control the Hawks looked for most of the afternoon.

Day 6: A Full Slate of Summer League Action

Sunday's full card had no shortage of compelling moments.

In New York's 86-75 win over Detroit, Chaz Lanier continued the most consistent shooting performance of Vegas so far. The guard knocked down 8 of his 12 three-point attempts, finishing with 24 points in a game the Knicks controlled throughout. Lanier has become one of the tournament's most watchable players, and his efficiency numbers are making opponents pay for every contested closeout.

In Dallas, Cameron Boozer did everything he could for the Grizzlies in a losing effort. The forward finished with 21 points, 8 rebounds, and 3 steals in a 96-88 loss to the Mavericks, with Kaodirichi Akobundu-Ehiogu contributing 16 points and 2 blocks for Dallas in the win. Boozer's box score was impressive for a player who couldn't change the outcome — the kind of performance that keeps his development on the right track regardless of the final score.

The most dramatic finish of the day came in Toronto's 94-93 escape over Indiana. Seth Lundy hit the game-winner with 22 points to lift the Raptors, denying a remarkable effort from Rienk Mast, who nearly willed the Pacers to victory with 23 points and 10 rebounds. Indiana had enough chances to win the game; Lundy made sure they didn't.

What's Next

The Atlanta Hawks are the Summer League story nobody is writing enough about. At 3-0, they're the last undefeated team still standing in Vegas, and the question now is whether anyone can end the run before the tournament's knockout stage. Atlanta has two more games in pool play, and if Johnson and Veesar keep producing, teams are going to have to bring significantly better game plans to stop them.

Summer League results don't predict regular-season success — but they do tell you something about player development and roster culture. Right now, the Hawks are building both.
