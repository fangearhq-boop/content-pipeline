Wembanyama Wins Unanimous DPOY — The First in NBA History, at Age 22

By Jake Torres | May 18, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

Nobody has ever done it the way Victor Wembanyama just did it.

The San Antonio Spurs center was named the 2025-26 Kia NBA Defensive Player of the Year after receiving all 100 first-place votes — making him the first player in the history of the award to win it unanimously. In a league where 100 voters find 100 reasons to disagree, Wembanyama generated complete consensus: he is the best defensive player on the planet.

He is 22 years old.

Numbers That Demanded Unanimity

The case for Wembanyama was overwhelming enough that argument felt unnecessary. He led the NBA in blocks for the second consecutive season, finishing with 197 total rejections — an average of 3.1 per game. He grabbed 11.5 rebounds per game and logged 66 steals, all while serving as the centerpiece of a Spurs defense that helped San Antonio finish 62-20 and hold the No. 2 seed in the Western Conference.

His combination of length, timing, and basketball intelligence produces a defensive effect that goes beyond raw statistics. Opposing guards change their floater trajectories. Forwards take off-balance jumpers rather than drive baseline. Post players abandon plans entirely when they feel his presence behind them. His court-altering impact — the shots teams choose not to take because he's there — cannot be fully measured. What can be measured is extraordinary enough on its own.

History's Context

The DPOY award has been given since 1983. In those 43 years, no player had ever received every single first-place vote. Players who won the award multiple times — Dikembe Mutombo, Ben Wallace, Rudy Gobert — never achieved unanimity. The award, by its nature, generates disagreement. Wembanyama erased it.

At 22, he's also the youngest DPOY winner in NBA history, claiming the award in just his third professional season. The trajectory the award follows — a player who becomes legendary in their prime — is one Wembanyama is just beginning. He's not yet the player he'll be at 25 or 28. That is the part that should make the rest of the league genuinely uncomfortable.

Playoffs and What Comes Next

Wembanyama's playoff resume has matched his regular-season dominance. In nine games, he's averaging 20.4 points, 11.2 rebounds, and 4.2 blocks per game. He scored 35 points in his playoff debut — a Spurs franchise record. He helped San Antonio eliminate the Minnesota Timberwolves in six games, setting up tonight's Western Conference Finals opener against SGA and Oklahoma City.

Tonight's matchup is the most anticipated defensive assignment of the playoffs: Wembanyama trying to limit the player who just won his second straight MVP. Two players at the absolute top of their positions, in a series where the winner goes to the NBA Finals. Game 1 tips off at 8:30 PM ET on NBC and Peacock.

The DPOY trophy sits in San Antonio now. The bigger prize is still available. Wembanyama is playing for it starting tonight.

What's Next

Western Conference Finals Game 1 tips off tonight, May 18, at 8:30 PM ET at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City. San Antonio enters with the unanimous DPOY and the task of slowing the league's best offensive player. Wembanyama has answered every challenge his career has presented so far. The Western Conference Finals is simply the next one.
