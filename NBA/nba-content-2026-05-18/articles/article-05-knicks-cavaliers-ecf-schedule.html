Knicks vs. Cavaliers: Eastern Conference Finals Set — Schedule, Matchups and What to Expect

By Marcus Cole | May 18, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Eastern Conference Finals are officially set, and Game 1 arrives tomorrow night at one of sports' most iconic venues.

The New York Knicks will host the Cleveland Cavaliers starting Tuesday, May 19 at Madison Square Garden. Game 1 tips off at 8 PM ET on ESPN. After Cleveland's dominant 125-94 Game 7 win over the Detroit Pistons on Sunday, the bracket has clarity: two of the East's most battle-tested franchises will play for a trip to the NBA Finals.

Full ECF Schedule

Game 1: Tuesday, May 19 — Cavaliers at Knicks, MSG — 8 PM ET (ESPN)
Game 2: Thursday, May 21 — Cavaliers at Knicks, MSG — 8 PM ET (ESPN)
Game 3: Saturday, May 23 — Knicks at Cavaliers — ABC
Game 4: Monday, May 25 — Knicks at Cavaliers — ESPN
Game 5: Wednesday, May 27 — Cavaliers at Knicks (if necessary) — ESPN
Game 6: Friday, May 29 — Knicks at Cavaliers (if necessary) — ESPN
Game 7: Sunday, May 31 — Cavaliers at Knicks (if necessary) — ESPN

New York holds home court as the No. 3 seed, the higher seed in this matchup. The Knicks will host Games 1, 2, 5, and 7 at Madison Square Garden.

How Each Team Got Here

The Cavaliers earned their spot in the most dramatic fashion possible. Cleveland fought through a seven-game first-round battle against Detroit, with James Harden's 30-point effort in a come-from-behind overtime Game 5 win and Donovan Mitchell's 26-point Game 7 performance defining a series that could have gone either way. The Cavs absorbed Cade Cunningham's 39-point explosion, a controversial no-call, and a road atmosphere in Detroit — and came out the other side as the last team standing in that bracket.

The Knicks, by contrast, rested. New York swept the Philadelphia 76ers in the first round, then waited while Cleveland ground through Detroit. That rest gives New York fresh legs. It also removes the built-up momentum that grinding through adversity generates. Which effect matters more is a genuine question heading into this series.

Key Storylines

Donovan Mitchell is the engine of Cleveland's offense. His 26-point, 8-assist performance in Game 7 was his best showing of the postseason, arriving in the highest-stakes game of the series. If Mitchell sustains that level of play at MSG, the Cavs are a genuine threat to advance.

Evan Mobley's emergence as a two-way force has been one of the stories of Cleveland's postseason run. His 21-point, 12-rebound Game 7 performance wasn't an anomaly — it was a continuation of consistent growth throughout the playoffs. He gives the Cavaliers a matchup problem that few teams in the East can resolve.

For the Knicks, home court and rest are structural advantages. MSG is one of the loudest arenas in basketball when the team is winning, and New York enters this series with a full complement of healthy players ready to go. The Knicks swept the 76ers without facing elimination pressure — a luxury that may or may not translate to readiness against a Cavaliers team that just survived the crucible.

What's Next

Game 1 is tomorrow night, Tuesday May 19 at Madison Square Garden. Tip-off is 8 PM ET on ESPN. For Cleveland, this is the franchise's first Conference Finals appearance since 2018. For New York, it's an opportunity to reach the NBA Finals for the first time in decades. Both teams have something to prove, and both have the talent to prove it. The Eastern Conference Finals won't disappoint.
