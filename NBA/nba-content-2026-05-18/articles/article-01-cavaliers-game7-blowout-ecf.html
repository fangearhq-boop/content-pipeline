Cavaliers 125-94 Game 7 Blowout — Cleveland Advances to Eastern Conference Finals

By Jake Torres | May 18, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The Cleveland Cavaliers did not just win Game 7 on Sunday night. They sent a message to the rest of the Eastern Conference: this team is built for moments like these.

Cleveland dismantled the Detroit Pistons 125-94 at Little Caesars Arena, turning a winner-take-all game into a wire-to-wire statement win. Four Cavaliers scored 20 or more points. The Cavs led by 17 at halftime and never looked back. Detroit — the team that had forced this series to a Game 7 with relentless effort — had no answers on its own floor.

The Cavaliers advance to the Eastern Conference Finals, where they will face the New York Knicks starting Tuesday, May 19 at Madison Square Garden.

Four Stars, One Statement

Donovan Mitchell led the way with 26 points and 8 assists, controlling the game in the first half and setting the tone for a dominant performance that left little doubt about Cleveland's intentions. But this wasn't just a Mitchell show.

Jarrett Allen contributed 23 points, providing rim presence and efficiency that Detroit could not match. Sam Merrill added another 23 points off the bench — a stunning individual performance in a game that demanded everything. Evan Mobley was the defensive anchor, finishing with 21 points and 12 rebounds in perhaps his most complete postseason game to date.

Cleveland led 64-47 at halftime. By the time the fourth quarter arrived, the outcome had long since been decided. The Cavaliers outscored Detroit in every quarter except one, sustaining their dominance with the kind of collective effort that separates good teams from great ones.

Detroit's Run Ends

The Pistons had earned this Game 7 the hard way. They won Game 6 at Cleveland, silenced a hostile crowd, and forced the rubber match back to Detroit. Cade Cunningham's 39-point effort in Game 5 had given the basketball world a glimpse of what Detroit's future looks like.

But on Sunday, facing elimination at home, the Pistons could not replicate that fight. Cleveland's defense smothered Detroit's primary options and turned every slow possession into a transition opportunity. The 31-point margin told the story: the Cavaliers were simply the better team when it mattered most.

What's Next

Cleveland now faces its toughest test of the postseason. The New York Knicks have been resting while the Cavaliers were grinding through seven games, and MSG will be electric for Game 1 on Tuesday night. The Knicks hold home court as the No. 3 seed, but the Cavaliers arrive with momentum and depth that few teams in the Eastern Conference can match.

Game 1 of the Eastern Conference Finals tips off Tuesday, May 19 at 8 PM ET on ESPN at Madison Square Garden. Cleveland is headed back to the Conference Finals for the first time since 2018 — and this version of the Cavaliers looks like they're ready to go further.
