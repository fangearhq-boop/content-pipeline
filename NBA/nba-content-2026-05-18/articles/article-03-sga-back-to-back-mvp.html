SGA Wins Back-to-Back MVP — Joins the 14 Greatest Seasons in NBA History

By Damon Pierce | May 18, 2026 | Hoop Heroes

The MVP race was over before it started.

Shai Gilgeous-Alexander has been named the 2025-26 NBA Most Valuable Player, becoming just the 14th player in league history to win the award in consecutive seasons. He received 83 of the 100 available first-place votes, with Nikola Jokić and Victor Wembanyama as the other finalists. For the second straight year, the basketball world looked at its best player and arrived at the same answer: it's SGA.

A Season That Demanded Recognition

The numbers behind SGA's 2025-26 campaign are exceptional on their own. He averaged 31.1 points per game — leading the NBA — while shooting 55.3% from the field, 38.6% from three, and 87.9% from the free throw line. He contributed 6.6 assists and 4.3 rebounds per game across 68 appearances.

But the number that defined SGA's season most completely was 68 — because he scored 20 or more points in every single one of those games. Not most of them. All of them. It was the kind of consistency that made advanced metrics irrelevant: he produced at an elite level regardless of matchup, rest, travel, and circumstance. He also led the entire league in clutch points and won the Clutch Player of the Year award — establishing himself not just as a volume scorer, but as the player opponents least wanted the ball in the hands of when the game was on the line.

Oklahoma City finished the regular season 64-18, the best record in the Western Conference. SGA led that team every night. He was, simply, the reason they were No. 1.

Rare Company

Winning back-to-back MVP awards places SGA in extraordinary company. The list of players who have accomplished it includes Michael Jordan, LeBron James, Bill Russell, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Larry Bird, Magic Johnson, Moses Malone, Wilt Chamberlain, and a handful of others. These are the names that define what it means to be the best player in the world — and now SGA's name is among them.

He is 27 years old and playing the best basketball of his career, in the Conference Finals against a team that features the best defensive player in the sport. The timing is almost theatrical: the league's top offensive performer and the league's top defensive performer will spend the next several weeks in direct competition for an NBA Finals berth.

What's Next

The MVP trophy is hardware. What SGA is chasing now is the one that matters most. The Thunder enter the Western Conference Finals as the clear favorites — 8-0 in the playoffs, fully healthy with Jalen Williams returning — and SGA has every reason to believe this is the year Oklahoma City brings home a championship. The question that follows every back-to-back MVP is the same one that follows every great individual season: can you win the title? Tonight, at 8:30 PM ET on NBC and Peacock, SGA starts answering that question against the most defensively talented opponent he's faced yet.
