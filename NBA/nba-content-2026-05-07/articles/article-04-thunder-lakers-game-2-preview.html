Thunder vs. Lakers Game 2 Preview: OKC Looks to Put Series Away as Luka Watch Intensifies

By Damon Pierce | May 7, 2026

NBA Playoffs · Western Conference Semifinals

The Oklahoma City Thunder will look to extend their grip on the Western Conference semifinals tonight when they host the Los Angeles Lakers in Game 2 at Paycom Center. Tip-off is at 9:30 PM ET on Amazon Prime, and Oklahoma City enters as massive 15.5-point favorites after their dominant 108-90 Game 1 victory five nights ago.

Chet Holmgren set the tone in Game 1 with one of the finest playoff performances of his career: 24 points, 12 rebounds, and 3 blocks. Shai Gilgeous-Alexander was efficient with 18 points and 6 assists even on what the Thunder described as a "quiet" night for the reigning MVP candidate. The Thunder led wire-to-wire after the first quarter and never allowed the Lakers back into the game, winning comfortably despite still being without Jalen Williams (hamstring).

LeBron's Heroics Not Enough

LeBron James gave the Lakers everything they could have asked for. The 41-year-old finished with 27 points on 12-of-17 shooting — a masterclass in veteran efficiency — and added 6 assists. But the gap between Los Angeles and Oklahoma City in terms of roster depth was too wide to bridge in Game 1, and that gap is likely to be equally evident tonight.

The Lakers' challenge is compounded by the ongoing absence of Luka Doncic, who suffered a Grade 2 left hamstring strain on April 2 and this week revealed doctors initially gave him an eight-week recovery timeline. That eight-week window runs to the end of May — well past the expected conclusion of this series. Doncic also confirmed he has not yet been cleared for contact drills, the final benchmark before a return can be authorized.

Thunder's System Still Purrs Without Williams

What is most impressive about Oklahoma City is the depth of their system. Even without Jalen Williams — who has missed three straight games — the Thunder have a roster that can execute at an elite level. Holmgren is developing into a legitimate first option. Ajay Mitchell provided 18 points off the bench in Game 1. The spacing, the ball movement, and the defensive intensity that defined their regular season has carried directly into the postseason.

The Thunder are 6-0 in the 2026 playoffs entering tonight, a remarkable run for a young team that has handled every challenge thrown at them. A 2-0 series lead tonight would put enormous pressure on the Lakers heading back to Los Angeles.

What's Next

The Thunder want to set the series on ice tonight, and the 15.5-point spread reflects the betting market's confidence in their ability to do exactly that. For the Lakers, the focus is on LeBron James maintaining his G1 performance level and on finding secondary contributions that can keep them within striking distance. Game 2 tips off tonight at 9:30 PM ET at Paycom Center in Oklahoma City.
