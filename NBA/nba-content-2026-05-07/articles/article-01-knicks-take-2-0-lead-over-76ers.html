Brunson, Anunoby Lead Knicks Past 76ers 108-102 to Take Commanding 2-0 Series Lead

By Damon Pierce | May 7, 2026

NBA Playoffs · Eastern Conference Semifinals

The New York Knicks survived a far tougher Game 2 than anyone expected, holding off the Philadelphia 76ers 108-102 on Wednesday night at Madison Square Garden to take a 2-0 lead in the Eastern Conference semifinals. A game that featured 25 lead changes and 14 ties came down to a late 9-0 run — a reminder that close playoff games are decided by who wants it more in the final five minutes.

Jalen Brunson led all scorers with 26 points on 9-of-21 shooting, adding 6 assists and drawing 8 free throw attempts. OG Anunoby was relentless with 24 points. Karl-Anthony Towns contributed a near-triple-double of 20 points, 10 rebounds, and 7 assists, anchoring both ends of the floor. For a Knicks team that looked invincible in Game 1 — a 137-98 blowout — Wednesday night's grind showed a different kind of championship DNA.

76ers Battled Without Embiid

The biggest storyline walking into the game was the absence of Joel Embiid, who was ruled out with a right ankle sprain and right hip soreness after missing the team's morning shootaround. Yet Philadelphia refused to roll over. Tyrese Maxey matched Brunson shot for shot, finishing with 26 points to give the 76ers a fighter's chance until the very end.

The game's 25 lead changes — reportedly the most in a playoff game in 11 years — illustrated how competitive Philadelphia was without their franchise cornerstone. The Sixers' defense made Brunson work for everything. Their offense kept finding answers. And yet, as has been the case all season for the Knicks, New York found a way.

The Closing Run That Decided It

With the game tightly contested in the fourth quarter, the Knicks unleashed a 9-0 run that stretched a slim lead into a comfortable enough margin to seal the win. Brunson hit critical buckets during the spurt, which proved to be the decisive sequence of the night. Philadelphia had no answer once the run began, and by the time the 76ers tried to mount a response, the clock had run out.

Towns's steady presence throughout — including 7 assists that kept the Knicks' offense humming — was underappreciated in the box score. His ability to play-make from the elbow against a Philly defense that was built to stop him is why New York signed him to a max deal, and moments like this validate that investment.

What's Next

The series shifts to Philadelphia for Game 3 on Friday, May 9. The critical question now is whether Joel Embiid will be available. With the Sixers trailing 2-0, a sweep becomes very real if they drop Game 3 — especially with Embiid potentially still limited. New York will aim to protect their momentum on the road, while Philadelphia's coaching staff faces a pivotal 72-hour window to see how their superstar center responds to treatment. The Knicks have looked every bit like a team that can reach the Finals.
