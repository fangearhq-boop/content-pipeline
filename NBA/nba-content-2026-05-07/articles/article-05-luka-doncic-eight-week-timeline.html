Luka Reveals 8-Week Recovery Timeline — Lakers Star Unlikely to Return in Thunder Series

By Jake Torres | May 7, 2026

NBA Playoffs · Injury Update

Luka Doncic broke his public silence on his injury this week and delivered news that all but confirms what many feared: the Los Angeles Lakers star is unlikely to play in the current Western Conference semifinals against the Oklahoma City Thunder. Doncic revealed that doctors gave him an eight-week recovery timeline when he suffered his Grade 2 left hamstring strain on April 2 — a timeline that, when calculated forward, lands in the final week of May 2026.

Even under the most optimistic interpretation of an eight-week window, the Thunder-Lakers series is unlikely to still be ongoing that long. If Oklahoma City follows through on their dominant Game 1 performance and continues at the pace they've set throughout the 2026 playoffs, the series could be over well before Doncic reaches his theoretical return date.

No Contact Clearance Yet

The most definitive sign of where Doncic stands came from his own words: he has not yet been cleared for contact drills. In professional basketball, contact clearance is the final gate before a player is allowed to practice fully and be considered for game action. The fact that Luka hasn't reached that threshold more than five weeks after the injury underscores the severity of the Grade 2 designation — and the caution the Lakers medical staff is exercising with their most valuable player.

Grade 2 hamstring strains involve partial tearing of muscle fibers, and recovery timelines are notoriously variable. Players who rush back from Grade 2 injuries often re-injure themselves at an even more severe level, which is likely why the Lakers have been conservative in their handling of Doncic's rehabilitation.

Spain Trip Underscores Urgency

Doncic's decision to fly to Spain during the playoffs to receive specialized cell therapy from Dr. Javier Barrio — the sports medicine physician at Real Madrid, Luka's former European club — revealed how hard he has been working to speed his recovery. The trip was widely reported and drew attention for its unconventional nature. Cell therapy treatments of this kind are designed to accelerate tissue healing, though outcomes vary widely.

The fact that even with that additional treatment Doncic remains without contact clearance speaks to the challenge ahead. His commitment to returning is not in question. The timeline is.

What's Next

The Lakers are doing everything they can without their franchise player, with LeBron James delivering an extraordinary 27-point performance in Game 1 against the Thunder. But Los Angeles trails 0-1 in the series, faces a 15.5-point underdog spread for Game 2 tonight, and has no realistic path to keeping pace with Oklahoma City's depth over a full series. The eight-week timeline that Luka has now publicly confirmed all but closes the door on a Doncic appearance in this series. The Lakers will fight, but this postseason's most compelling storyline may already be over before the best player in it could take the floor.
