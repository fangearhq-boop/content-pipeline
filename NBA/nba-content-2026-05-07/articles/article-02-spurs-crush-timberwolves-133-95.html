Wembanyama Leads Historic Blowout: Spurs Crush Timberwolves 133-95 to Even Series

By Jake Torres | May 7, 2026

NBA Playoffs · Western Conference Semifinals

If the Timberwolves thought their dramatic Game 1 win was a statement, the San Antonio Spurs answered in the loudest possible way Wednesday night. San Antonio demolished Minnesota 133-95 at the AT&T Center to even the Western Conference semifinal series at 1-1, handing the Timberwolves the worst playoff loss in franchise history by a 38-point margin.

Victor Wembanyama was masterful across every dimension: 19 points, 15 rebounds, and 2 blocks in a performance that showcased his unique ability to dominate without needing to be the primary scorer. San Antonio's full roster contributed — Stephon Castle led the team in points with 21, and De'Aaron Fox added 16 — as the Spurs shot 50% from the floor and 41% from three-point range.

A Historic Night for the Spurs Organization

San Antonio's 133-point playoff performance was the franchise's highest-scoring postseason game in over 40 years — since they beat the Denver Nuggets 145-105 on May 4, 1983. That statistic alone captures the magnitude of what the Spurs accomplished on Wednesday night.

The 38-point victory margin was equally striking from Minnesota's perspective. No Timberwolves team had ever absorbed a playoff loss by that margin before. Anthony Edwards, Julius Randle, Jaden McDaniels, and Terrence Shannon Jr. each scored 12 points, but four players matching at just 12 each tells the story of how completely San Antonio controlled the game.

Spurs' Depth Overwhelmed Minnesota

What made San Antonio's performance special wasn't just Wembanyama's stat line — it was the collective execution. The Spurs moved the ball with precision, crashed the offensive boards, and held Minnesota to a shooting percentage well below their season averages. Castle, still just 22 years old, is maturing into a genuine secondary option alongside Wembanyama, and Fox continues to be a perfect fit in the system that head coach Gregg Popovich's successor has built around this young core.

For the Timberwolves, the performance raises urgent questions. Edwards was held in check in a game Minnesota simply could not compete in. The roster's depth — already tested by his 10-day early return from injury in Game 1 — looked stretched across 48 minutes of complete Spurs dominance.

What's Next

The series heads back to San Antonio for Game 3, where the Spurs will have full crowd support after their statement performance. Minnesota must find a way to recapture the urgency and collective fight that defined their Game 1 thriller — a 104-102 nail-biter — and apply it in a hostile road environment. Anthony Edwards will need to be at his very best. The series is perfectly tied at 1-1, but the momentum is entirely in San Antonio's favor.
