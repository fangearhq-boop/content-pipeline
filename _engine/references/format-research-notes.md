## Research Completeness Checklist

### For Each Story:
- [ ] Decision recorded (NEW or FOLLOW UP)
- [ ] History reference documented (if FOLLOW UP)
- [ ] Tier assignment matches daily brief
- [ ] 3-5 sources identified with publication dates
- [ ] Key facts extracted with specific numbers/stats (8-15 items)
- [ ] Freshness verification completed
- [ ] Aging status assessed
- [ ] Relevance to niche audience clearly explained (2+ paragraphs)
- [ ] Cross-story connections identified
- [ ] Confidence level assigned

### Overall Brief:
- [ ] All 5-8 stories from daily brief included
- [ ] No duplicate sources across stories
- [ ] Publication dates span appropriate timeframe (last 24-48 hours for BREAKING/TODAY)
- [ ] Statistics are verified and sourced
- [ ] Language is factual and neutral in research notes
- [ ] Ready for story-analysis phase

## Research Methodology Notes

**Source Priority:**
1. Official announcements / Primary sources
2. Major news outlets covering the niche
3. Industry experts / Analyst reports
4. Secondary news coverage with original sourcing
5. Social media from verified accounts (supplementary only)

**Fact-Checking Standards:**
- Every numerical claim has a source reference
- Dates are verified against official sources
- Statistics are current and not outdated
- Conflicting information is noted and sources compared

**Freshness Determination:**
- **BREAKING:** Within last 6-12 hours, major announcement
- **TODAY:** Within last 24 hours, significant development
- **UPCOMING:** Scheduled events/announcements (< 7 days out)
- **TRENDING:** Popular topic with multiple recent sources
- **EVERGREEN:** Timeless content, no time sensitivity
