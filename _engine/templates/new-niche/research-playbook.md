## Fact-Check Focus Areas

<!-- Domain-specific claim types that are most likely to be wrong
     and most damaging if published incorrectly.

     The engine has universal fact-check priorities (dates, ages,
     compound claims, superlatives). THIS section adds niche-specific
     priorities on top of those universal ones.

     For each focus area, explain:
     - What kind of claim it is
     - Why it's high-risk in this domain
     - How to verify it (specific sources)

     SPORTS EXAMPLE:

     1. SCORES AND RECORDS
        - Why high-risk: LLMs confidently hallucinate scores. A wrong final score
          destroys credibility instantly.
        - Verify via: ESPN box scores, NCAA stats portal, official team accounts.

     2. RANKINGS AND POLL POSITIONS
        - Why high-risk: Rankings change weekly. Cached AI knowledge is often stale.
        - Verify via: Current week's NFCA poll, USA Softball rankings.

     3. HISTORICAL CLAIMS ("first to...", "since 2004...")
        - Why high-risk: AI summaries commonly inflate or misattribute records.
        - Verify via: NCAA record book, Baseball Reference, team media guides.

     4. PLAYER STATS (batting average, ERA, HR count)
        - Why high-risk: Stats change after every game. Yesterday's .342 may be
          today's .338 after an 0-for-4.
        - Verify via: NCAA stats portal, team box score from most recent game.

     PARENTING EXAMPLE:

     1. PRICES AND "FREE" CLAIMS
        - Why high-risk: Prices change, promotions expire. Publishing a wrong price
          frustrates parents and damages trust.
        - Verify via: Official event page, venue website, call if needed.

     2. DATES, TIMES, AND LOCATIONS
        - Why high-risk: Events get rescheduled, venues change. A parent driving
          to the wrong location is unforgivable.
        - Verify via: Official event page (check "last updated" date).

     3. SAFETY AND HEALTH CLAIMS
        - Why high-risk: Wrong medical/safety advice is dangerous. Parenting audience
          trusts health claims implicitly.
        - Verify via: AAP, CDC, or CPSC primary source — never secondary reporting.

     4. SCHOOL DISTRICT INFORMATION
        - Why high-risk: Different districts have different policies, dates, and rules.
          Attributing D11 policy to D20 misleads hundreds of families.
        - Verify via: Official district website, board meeting minutes.
