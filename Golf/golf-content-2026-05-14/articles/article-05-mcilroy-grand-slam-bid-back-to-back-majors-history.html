McIlroy's Grand Slam Bid: What History Says About Winning Back-to-Back Majors

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 14, 2026

In the 90-year modern history of professional golf's major championships, the calendar-year Grand Slam — winning the Masters, the US Open, The Open Championship, and the PGA Championship in a single year — has never been accomplished.

Rory McIlroy won the 2026 Masters. Today, he tees off in the first round of the PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club. The sequence is not lost on anyone in the sport. Neither is the precedent: Jordan Spieth in 2015 was the last player to win the year's first two major championships, claiming the Masters and then the US Open before his calendar Grand Slam bid fell short at St. Andrews.

Two majors in one year has been accomplished several times in golf history. What comes after those two is where the rarity truly resides.

The Record Books and What a Win Would Mean

McIlroy currently holds six major titles: the 2011 US Open at Congressional, the 2012 Open Championship at Royal Lytham and St Annes, the 2012 PGA Championship at Kiawah Island, the 2014 Open Championship at Royal Liverpool, the 2014 PGA Championship at Valhalla, and the 2026 Masters at Augusta National.

A win at Aronimink would give McIlroy seven career majors. That places him past Arnold Palmer on the all-time major list. Above McIlroy at that point would be Ben Hogan (9), Gary Player (9), Walter Hagen (11), Tiger Woods (15), and Jack Nicklaus (18). The mountain remains steep, but the climb becomes significantly more visible at seven.

The Spieth Comparison

Spieth's 2015 run is the closest modern parallel to what McIlroy is attempting. Spieth won the Masters convincingly, then won the US Open at Chambers Bay in dramatic fashion. He then contended at the Open Championship at St. Andrews before finishing fourth. The calendar Grand Slam ended there, but the two-major haul in one year remained an extraordinary achievement.

McIlroy now arrives at the second major of 2026 in a similar position — defending major champion, one of the game's most complete players, at a course that suits his game on a week when the pressure will test him at every turn.

The Tiger Context

The "Tiger Slam" — Tiger Woods winning four consecutive major championships across the 2000 US Open, 2000 Open Championship, 2000 PGA Championship, and 2001 Masters — remains the closest anyone has come to the Grand Slam concept. But those victories spanned two calendar years. No player in the history of the professional game has won all four major championships within a single calendar year.

For McIlroy to complete the calendar Grand Slam in 2026, he would need to win here at Aronimink, then win the US Open at Shinnecock Hills, and then win The Open Championship — a sequence that has never been accomplished. It is worth stating the possibility plainly while also understanding how improbable it would be.

What's Next

Round 1 of the 2026 PGA Championship is underway at Aronimink Golf Club. McIlroy teed off at 8:40 AM ET today alongside Jordan Spieth and Jon Rahm. If he plays this week the way he played in Augusta, the conversation will intensify with every round. If not, there is still the US Open at Shinnecock Hills and The Open Championship ahead. The Grand Slam chase has just begun. This week is the next chapter.
