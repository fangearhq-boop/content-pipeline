Korda Is Four Points From the LPGA Hall of Fame — And Queen City Championship Starts Today

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 14, 2026

Nelly Korda has won three times in 2026. She has won more LPGA events than virtually any active player in the world. She is 23 of 27 required points from earning automatic induction into the LPGA Hall of Fame — one of sport's most exclusive institutional honors.

Today, the Kroger Queen City Championship begins at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati, Ohio. Four points away. One week at a time.

The HOF Countdown

The LPGA Hall of Fame requires a player to accumulate 27 points under the organization's system, which awards points based on major championships, Players of the Year titles, LPGA victories, and other qualifying achievements. Korda currently sits at 23 — four points short.

A tournament win counts as one point toward the total. Her three 2026 wins — the Tournament of Champions, the Chevron Championship, and the Riviera Maya Classic — moved her from 20 points to 23. The trajectory is clear. The timeline is narrowing. When Korda wins again, the conversation will become more immediate. When she reaches 27, it will be historic.

The Queen City Field

Korda is not playing in Cincinnati without competition. World No. 1 Jeeno Thitikul — who won the Mizuho Americas Open last week at 13-under par — is also in the Queen City field. Thitikul and Korda have been the sport's two dominant figures in 2026, and their presence in the same field adds a competitive dimension to what is already a meaningful event for the Hall of Fame storyline.

Charley Hull and Lydia Ko are also among the competitors at Maketewah. The course, a private club in Cincinnati, Ohio, will play as a challenging test across four rounds for the world's best women golfers.

What a Win Would Mean

Beyond the HOF points, a win at Queen City would extend Korda's 2026 win total to four — in a year that has already seen her claim a major championship. The competitive case for her as the best player on the LPGA Tour in 2026 is already overwhelming. The Hall of Fame conversation is a measuring stick that extends beyond any single season, placing her in the context of the sport's all-time greats.

At her current pace, that conversation is not hypothetical. It is a matter of when, not whether.

What's Next

The Kroger Queen City Championship runs May 14-17 at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati, Ohio. Round 1 begins today. Korda needs four more LPGA Hall of Fame points. Thitikul enters on a wave of momentum from her Mizuho win. Four days of golf in Cincinnati could produce one of the summer's most talked-about LPGA storylines. Follow along.
