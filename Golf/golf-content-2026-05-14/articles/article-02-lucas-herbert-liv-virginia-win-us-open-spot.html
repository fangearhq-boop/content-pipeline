Lucas Herbert Wins LIV Virginia Wire-to-Wire, Earns US Open Spot Despite Battling the Flu

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 14, 2026

Lucas Herbert was sick all week. You would not know it from the scoreboard.

The 30-year-old Australian won LIV Golf Virginia at Trump National Golf Club with a dominant wire-to-wire performance, finishing at 24-under par and claiming a four-shot victory over Sergio Garcia. Herbert battled flu symptoms throughout the event, opened with consecutive rounds of 64-63 to build an eight-shot lead, and closed with a 3-under 69 to seal his first individual LIV Golf title. The winner's cheque came to $4 million.

The secondary prize may prove even more valuable.

The US Open Exemption

Herbert's victory moved him into third place in the 2026 LIV Golf Individual Championship standings. That positioning made him the highest-ranked LIV player not already exempt from the US Open at Shinnecock Hills — and the exemption opened accordingly. Herbert will now tee it up at Shinnecock, one of the most iconic US Open venues in history, later this season.

For LIV players who have operated outside the traditional world ranking system, earning major championship exemptions through standings performance has become one of the primary pathways back to the sport's biggest stages. Herbert's Virginia win delivered exactly that.

Wire-to-Wire Dominance

Herbert's 64-63 start was remarkable under any circumstances. That he was fighting the flu while producing those numbers added a layer of drama to an already commanding performance. He led throughout the event — never relinquishing the top position after his opening round — and while his third round was tighter, the eight-shot cushion he built through the first two rounds gave him room to manage the finish.

Garcia, who finished four shots back at 20-under, played admirably but could not put together a run that threatened the lead. Herbert's closing 69 was measured and deliberate — the round of a player who knew the job was done and played accordingly.

4Aces GC Claims Team Title — Anthony Kim Stars

In the team competition, 4Aces GC won in a playoff over the Fireballs, with both teams finishing at 49-under par across the event. Anthony Kim — one of golf's most compelling comeback stories — delivered a bogey-free 10-under 62 in the final round to fuel the 4Aces push. The team won on the first playoff hole, with Kim and Thomas Detry each making par at the par-4 18th.

Kim's round was a reminder of the kind of golf he is capable of producing and what his return to competitive play has meant for this tour's entertainment value.

What's Next

Lucas Herbert is also in this week's PGA Championship field at Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania. He arrives from a tournament win, a US Open exemption, and a flu that has presumably run its course. The LIV Golf season continues with LIV Golf Korea scheduled for May 28-31 at Asiad Country Club in Busan, South Korea.
