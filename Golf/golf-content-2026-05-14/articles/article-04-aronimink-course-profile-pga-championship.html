No Intermediate Rough, Massive Greens, 75 Bunkers: Why Aronimink Will Sort Out the 2026 PGA Championship Field Fast

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 14, 2026

At most major championship venues, finding the rough off the tee is an inconvenience. At Aronimink Golf Club, host of the 2026 PGA Championship, it is a potential disaster.

There is no intermediate rough at Aronimink. The fairways — maintained at a cut of just 3/8 of an inch — give way directly to fescue and bluegrass rough maintained at 3.5 inches. The difference between those two options is enormous. From the fairway, players can access the full shot-making range the course demands. From the rough, controlling spin on approaches is nearly impossible. And without spin control, reaching the correct zones on Aronimink's massive, boldly contoured greens becomes a guessing game.

The Greens

Average green size at Aronimink: 8,100 square feet. That figure is roughly a third larger than a typical Donald Ross design. The added size creates more than generous landing areas — it creates more pins, more slopes, more slopes between pin positions, and more putts from the wrong side of a green that effectively become scrambles.

Ross built his greens to reward players who approach the correct quadrant and punish those who do not. At Aronimink, the greens will amplify every error from the tee and every miscalculation on approach shots. Hitting the green is only half the battle. Hitting the right section of the green is what separates Thursday leaders from Sunday champions.

The Bunkers

Seventy-five bunkers frame the fairways and guard the greens throughout Aronimink's layout. Ross's strategic bunker placement — accumulated over a redesign completed in 1926-1928 — was designed not simply to collect wayward shots, but to create decisions from the tee. Players must choose angles, assess risk, and commit to lines that threaten the bunkers if they want to access the optimal approach windows.

In a field where every player can overpower most setups, Aronimink redirects the conversation from length to intelligence. The player who reads the course correctly, one hole at a time, will gain an advantage that compounds across 72 holes.

Historical and Cultural Significance

Aronimink Golf Club was established in 1896 and arrived at its current site in 1928. The PGA Championship last visited in 1962, when Gary Player won to claim the first of his two PGA titles. Player remains the only South African to have won this championship.

The 2026 version carries additional historical weight: the championship coincides with the 250th anniversary of the founding of the United States, and Aronimink sits just 13 miles from downtown Philadelphia, where the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution were signed. The championship is also significant for the venue itself — Aronimink is the first golf club to have hosted all three of the PGA of America's rotating major championships: the PGA Championship, the KitchenAid Senior PGA Championship, and the KPMG Women's PGA Championship.

What's Next

The 108th PGA Championship runs May 14-17 at Aronimink Golf Club, Newtown Square, Pennsylvania. Round 1 is underway today. The cut comes after 36 holes on Friday. Weekend coverage moves to CBS. By the time Sunday arrives, Aronimink's demands will have revealed which player — out of 154 of the world's best — has the precision, the patience, and the decision-making to claim a major title on one of America's most exacting courses.
