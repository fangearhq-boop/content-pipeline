PGA Championship Round 1 Begins: McIlroy's Grand Slam Bid, Scheffler's Defense, and the Aronimink Challenge

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 14, 2026

Major week is here. The 108th PGA Championship tees off today at Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania, and the collection of storylines arriving with the field is one of the richest the second major of the year has assembled in recent memory.

Three months ago, Rory McIlroy won the Masters. Today, he walks the fairways at Aronimink with a chance to win back-to-back majors — something no player has achieved since Jordan Spieth took the first two majors of 2015. A win this week would give McIlroy seven career major titles and keep alive the most tantalizing narrative in men's golf: the calendar-year Grand Slam.

McIlroy: From Augusta to Aronimink

McIlroy teed off in the first group at 8:40 AM ET alongside Jordan Spieth and Jon Rahm. He comes in carrying the momentum of six career majors and the weight of expectation that follows a Masters title. His game in 2026 has been built on ball-striking excellence and an emotional connection to winning big events on big stages.

Aronimink's characteristics — fairways flanked by deep fescue, premium on approach accuracy, massive undulating greens — suit a player with McIlroy's iron quality and distance control. If he can avoid the penal rough off the tee, the course sets up well for his style of play.

Scheffler: The Defending Champion

Scottie Scheffler won last year's PGA Championship at Quail Hollow by five strokes. He has been runner-up three times in 2026 already, signalling a level of consistency at the top of leaderboards that makes him dangerous in any major field. Scheffler skipped the Truist Championship to prepare specifically for Aronimink, a decision that underscores how seriously he is approaching the title defense.

Scheffler and his group — Matt Fitzpatrick and Justin Rose — tee off at 2:05 PM ET. Fitzpatrick, who has won three times in four starts in 2026, adds another dimension to the afternoon wave's intrigue.

Cameron Young: The Major Milestone Moment

No player enters Aronimink with more to prove than Cameron Young. The World No. 3 won the Players Championship — golf's unofficial fifth major — and the Cadillac Championship at Doral in 2026. He is playing at a level that puts him squarely in the contention picture at every event he enters. He has never won a major.

Aronimink's demands — approach play, course management, patience from tee to green — align with the strengths Young has shown in his two big wins this year. Many in the field will have more major experience than he does. Few will arrive better equipped to handle what this course asks.

What's Next

Round 1 will establish the early leaderboard and begin revealing which players thrive at Aronimink's demanding layout and which ones find the fescue too often. The cut falls after 36 holes on Friday. Weekend coverage moves to CBS. Full championship coverage is available on ESPN, ESPN+, CBS, and Golf Channel throughout the week. The 108th PGA Championship runs May 14-17, 2026.
