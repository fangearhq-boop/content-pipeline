### STORY 1: Ryan Fox Day After
**Tier:** 1 (Breaking)
**Posting Window:** 8:00 AM ET

#### FB Long-Form Post
The night Ryan Fox won The Open Championship, he did what any newly crowned Champion Golfer of the Year would do: he poured a glass of Bordeaux red wine straight into the Claret Jug.

Then he had to empty it for interviews. So he filled it again.

Fox described the entire experience as "surreal" — and you can understand why. The 39-year-old New Zealander was ranked No. 56 in the world and went off at 200-1 odds. He'd watched The Open in the middle of the night as a kid growing up in New Zealand, dreaming of this. And now he is the Champion Golfer of the Year.

"To be an Open champion, to be the Champion Golfer of the Year, it's unbelievable, surreal, crazy," Fox said.

After celebrating through most of the night at Royal Birkdale — sleeping little to none — Fox boarded a flight from Manchester to London. The Claret Jug came with him. And sitting right next to him on that flight? Cameron Young. The man Fox beat by a single stroke. The runner-up. They flew home together.

Fox earned $3.2 million for the win — the largest winner's share the R&A has ever paid. The total purse was $17.75 million. But the Claret Jug is the prize that matters most, and Fox says he's bringing it back to New Zealand.

Only the third New Zealander ever to win The Open Championship. It's surreal. Even he can't quite believe it.

### STORY 2: LIV Golf UK This Week / Crisis
**Tier:** 1 (Developing)
**Posting Window:** 10:30 AM ET

#### FB Long-Form Post
LIV Golf tees off on English soil Thursday — but it may be the beginning of the end for the circuit as we know it.

The LIV Golf UK event runs July 23-26 at JCB Golf & Country Club in Rocester, England, about 30 miles from Birmingham. It's the first of four scheduled events remaining on the 2026 LIV calendar. The problem? LIV's own CEO has publicly admitted he cannot guarantee all four will be played.

Here's the financial picture: LIV needs approximately $400 million from Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund to complete the 2026 season. Only about $200 million has arrived — roughly $66 million in May, $130 million in June. PIF delivers payments on a monthly basis, which means each event is effectively contingent on the next disbursement.

Sources told the Financial Times that the UK event and LIV's Bedminster stop are expected to proceed. The two August events, however, remain in serious doubt.

To manage the crisis, LIV has hired US investment banking advisor Ducera Partners and brought on two independent board directors. The circuit is also reportedly drawing up a 2027 proposal with just 10 events — down significantly from recent seasons — featuring five team majors in Australia and South Africa alongside five US-based events.

Perhaps the most telling sign: multiple LIV players have quietly begun reaching out to PGA Tour officials about potential return pathways. Those pathways, Golf Digest reports, are now "more restrictive" than they were when players originally departed.

LIV Golf was supposed to change professional golf forever. Right now, it's fighting just to finish 2026.

### STORY 3: Sam Burns Near-Miss
**Tier:** 2 (Feature)
**Posting Window:** 1:00 PM ET

#### FB Long-Form Post
Two weeks before The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale, Sam Burns became a father.

His daughter Belle had just been born in Louisiana. Burns initially decided not to play. Then he changed his mind, flew to England, and produced one of the most extraordinary stretches in men's major championship history — a Round 2 of 62 followed by a Round 3 of 65, the lowest two-round combination (R2+R3) ever recorded in a men's major.

He entered Sunday with a two-shot lead at 10-under par. The Claret Jug was within reach.

Then the wheels came off. Three consecutive bogeys — holes 4, 5, and 6 — erased the lead. Burns made only one birdie all day and never truly recovered. On the 72nd hole, he had a lengthy birdie putt that would have forced a tie with Cameron Young at nine-under. The ball grazed the edge of the cup and stayed out.

Burns finished T3 at 8-under, earning $1,181,000. Ryan Fox, the 200-1 longshot he almost caught, walked away with the Claret Jug.

The context makes it all the harder to process. Burns almost didn't come. He set records when he did. He had the lead entering the final day. And then three bogeys on holes 4, 5, and 6 changed everything.

His daughter Belle is two weeks old. Somewhere in the numbers — the 62, the 65, the three bogeys, the putt that grazed the cup — is one of the most emotionally charged near-misses in the history of The Open Championship.

### STORY 4: 3M Open Preview
**Tier:** 2 (Preview)
**Posting Window:** 3:30 PM ET

#### FB Long-Form Post
One week after The Open Championship, the PGA Tour moves straight to Blaine, Minnesota for the 2026 3M Open (July 23-26) at TPC Twin Cities.

Scottie Scheffler headlines the 144-player field, arriving with momentum off a T4 finish at Royal Birkdale last week. Defending champion Kurt Kitayama looks to repeat his 2025 title. Maverick McNealy is also listed among the co-favorites.

The venue is TPC Twin Cities — a par-71 course stretching 7,431 yards with the parkland layout and water features characteristic of an Arnold Palmer design (with Tom Lehman as consultant). First tee times Thursday start at 6:50 AM off both No. 1 and No. 10.

The stakes are high on the FedExCup front: the 3M Open offers 500 points to the winner and is the third-to-last event of the regular season. For players on the FedExCup bubble, this is one of the final legitimate chances to secure postseason seeding before the playoff push begins.

The field includes 53 of the world's top-125 ranked players from 21 countries.

### STORY 5: PGA Tour 2026 Major Sweep
**Tier:** 2 (Analysis)
**Posting Window:** 6:00 PM ET

#### FB Long-Form Post
When Ryan Fox holed that 12-foot putt on the 18th hole at Royal Birkdale on Sunday, it completed something remarkable: a clean sweep of all four 2026 major championships by PGA Tour members.

Rory McIlroy won the Masters — his second consecutive Augusta title and sixth major overall, setting the European record. Aaron Rai claimed the PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club. Wyndham Clark won the US Open at Shinnecock Hills wire-to-wire for his second US Open title. And Ryan Fox took The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale at 200-1 odds.

LIV Golf went 0-for-4 in major wins in 2026. Bryson DeChambeau led the LIV effort at Royal Birkdale, but finished around T9 after receiving a Rule 8.1a penalty in Round 2. Lucas Herbert, who plays for LIV Ripper GC, actually led the LIV contingent at The Open and was among the R3 leaders before Fox's final-round charge ended his bid.

The major shutout is significant on its own. It becomes more significant in context: LIV Golf is facing its most serious existential financial crisis, with real questions about whether it can complete the 2026 calendar. Saudi PIF has delivered only half the funds needed for the season to proceed.

Rory McIlroy, the most prominent figure to resist LIV's recruiting, earned $5.34 million in major prize money alone in 2026.

The gap between the two circuits — competitive, financial, and reputational — has never been wider.
