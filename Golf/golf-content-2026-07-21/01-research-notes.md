## STORY 1: Ryan Fox Day After

- Ryan Fox won The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale on July 20 at -10 (final round 68)
- Beat Cameron Young by 1 stroke; Burns solo 3rd at -8
- Putt on 18th hole: "I still don't know what to think. My nerves are all over the place. I'm not quite sure how I hit that putt on 18." (Source: multiple)
- Called it "surreal" — used to watch The Open in middle of the night as a kid growing up in New Zealand
- Said: "to be an Open champion, to be the Champion Golfer of the Year, it's unbelievable, surreal, crazy"
- Celebration: poured Bordeaux red wine into the Claret Jug; had to empty it for interviews, filled it again
- Spent most of the night celebrating, little to no sleep
- Post-win: boarded Manchester-to-London flight, SAT NEXT TO Cam Young (runner-up); trophy case came too
- Plans to bring Claret Jug back to New Zealand
- Fox: age 39, World No. 56, 200-1 pre-tournament odds, first major, 8 international victories
- Only 3rd New Zealander to win The Open Championship
- Fox won $3.2 million — largest winner's share ever paid by R&A (Source: Golf Channel, GolfMonthly)
- Total purse: $17.75 million (Sources vary: some say $17.5M)
- Scheffler T4: $827,500; Young $1,842,000 (2nd); Burns $1,181,000 (3rd)

## STORY 2: LIV Golf UK / Funding Crisis

- LIV Golf UK: July 23-26 at JCB Golf & Country Club, Rocester, England (about 30 miles from Birmingham)
- First of 4 remaining events in the 2026 LIV season
- LIV Golf CEO said he cannot guarantee all 4 events will be played (Source: ESPN)
- Saudi PIF ended funding pledge for after 2026 (confirmed April 2026)
- LIV needs ~$400M from PIF to complete 2026 season; only ~$200M arrived ($66M May + $130M June)
- FT sources: UK event and Bedminster expected to proceed; final two August events "dependent on additional funding"
- PIF delivers payments monthly — each event is contingent
- LIV has hired US investment banking advisor Ducera Partners, brought on 2 independent board directors
- LIV Golf's 2027 proposals: 10 events (5 team majors in Australia/South Africa + 5 US-based events)
- LIV players reportedly reaching out to PGA Tour about return, but pathways "more restrictive" (Golf Digest)

## STORY 3: Sam Burns Near-Miss

- Burns had 54-hole lead entering Sunday at -10 (2 shots clear)
- Shot record-tying 62 in Round 2 + 65 in Round 3 = lowest two-round combination (R2+R3) in men's major championship history
- Made 3 consecutive bogeys on holes 4, 5, 6 in final round
- Never fully recovered; made only 1 birdie all day
- Final putt on 18 (lengthy birdie to tie Young at -9) grazed the hole
- Finished T3 at -8 ($1,181,000)
- Burns' daughter Belle born approximately 2 weeks before tournament
- Burns had initially decided NOT to play, then changed his mind and flew from Louisiana to Royal Birkdale
- Described as one of the more emotionally charged near-misses in recent Open history
