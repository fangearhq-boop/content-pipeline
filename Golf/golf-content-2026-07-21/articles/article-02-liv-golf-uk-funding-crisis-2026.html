LIV Golf UK Starts Thursday, but the Circuit Still Can't Guarantee Its August Events

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 21, 2026

LIV Golf will tee off in England on Thursday. Whether it reaches September is a different question entirely.

The LIV Golf UK event runs July 23-26 at JCB Golf & Country Club in Rocester, England — roughly 30 miles from Birmingham. It is the first of four remaining scheduled events on the 2026 LIV calendar. And before the first shot is struck, the circuit's CEO has publicly acknowledged he cannot guarantee all four will be played.

The financial situation is stark. LIV Golf needs approximately $400 million from Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund to complete the 2026 season. According to reporting confirmed by multiple sources including ESPN and the Financial Times, only about $200 million has arrived — approximately $66 million in May and $130 million in June. PIF has been delivering payments on a monthly basis, making each upcoming event effectively contingent on the arrival of the next disbursement.

Which Events Are Safe?

Sources close to the situation told the Financial Times that the UK event and LIV's Bedminster stop are expected to proceed. Those two are considered relatively secure. The final two August events, however, remain dependent on additional PIF funding that has not yet materialized.

LIV's CEO has not minced words. He told reporters he cannot guarantee all four events will take place. For a professional golf circuit with hundreds of players under contract, that is an extraordinary admission.

The Circuit's Response

LIV Golf has moved aggressively to manage the crisis. The circuit has hired Ducera Partners, a US-based investment banking advisory firm, to help navigate the financial situation. It has also added two independent board directors — a structural change typically associated with organizations needing to signal governance accountability to outside stakeholders.

LIV has also reportedly begun work on a reduced 2027 calendar. The current proposal would feature just 10 events — five team majors in Australia and South Africa and five US-based events — a significant contraction from the circuit's original ambitions.

Perhaps the most telling development: multiple LIV players have begun quietly reaching out to PGA Tour officials about potential return pathways, according to Golf Digest. Those pathways, the report noted, are now considerably more restrictive than they were when players originally departed for LIV.

The Competitive Picture

The funding crisis comes as LIV Golf has been shut out of every major championship in 2026. All four major titles this year — the Masters, PGA Championship, US Open, and The Open Championship — were won by PGA Tour members. No LIV player has won a major in 2026. Bryson DeChambeau led the LIV effort at Royal Birkdale last week but finished approximately T9 after receiving a Rule 8.1a penalty in Round 2.

The circuit was built on the promise of a competitive alternative to the PGA Tour, backed by essentially limitless Saudi sovereign wealth. The reality of 2026 looks considerably different.

What's Next

The LIV Golf UK event begins Thursday, July 23 at JCB Golf & Country Club, with the final round scheduled for Sunday, July 26. Following the UK event, LIV is scheduled to hold its Bedminster stop before the two August events that remain uncertain. Each event's fate will depend on whether PIF's monthly payment arrives in time. The broader question of LIV Golf's 2027 future — including whether a scaled-back 10-event circuit can survive — will take shape over the coming months.
