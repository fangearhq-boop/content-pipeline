3M Open Preview: Scheffler Headlines at TPC Twin Cities as FedExCup Clock Ticks

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 21, 2026

The dust from Royal Birkdale is barely settled, and the PGA Tour is already back in action.

The 2026 3M Open runs July 23-26 at TPC Twin Cities in Blaine, Minnesota, roughly 15 miles north of Minneapolis-St. Paul. Scottie Scheffler headlines the 144-player field, arriving fresh off a T4 finish at The Open Championship last week and carrying the momentum that comes with competing deep into a major's final round.

Defending champion Kurt Kitayama is also in the field, looking to repeat his 2025 title. Maverick McNealy enters as a co-favorite alongside Scheffler and Kitayama, according to lineups.com projections.

The Course

TPC Twin Cities is a par-71 layout stretching 7,431 yards, featuring the parkland design signature of an Arnold Palmer course (with PGA Tour veteran Tom Lehman as a consultant). The course opened in 2000 and has hosted the 3M Open since its inaugural PGA Tour event in 2019. Water features and tree-lined fairways define the challenge — a significant contrast from the links conditions at Royal Birkdale that players faced just days ago.

First round tee times begin at 6:50 AM Thursday off both the 1st and 10th tees.

The FedExCup Stakes

The 3M Open offers 500 FedExCup points to the winner, making it one of the more consequential regular-season events on the calendar. More importantly, it is the third-to-last event of the 2026 PGA Tour regular season. Players inside the top 70 of the FedExCup standings qualify for the postseason. Players on the bubble — those hovering around that cutoff — need a strong performance this week to secure their positions.

For Scheffler, the FedExCup is not a concern. What matters is converting his major-week form into a PGA Tour win as the back half of the season unfolds.

The Field

The 144-player field includes 53 players ranked inside the top 125 of the Official World Golf Ranking, representing 21 countries. With several international players electing to skip the week in favor of rest following The Open, the domestic contingent features many of the players with the most riding on the FedExCup standings.

The rapid turnaround from a major to a regular-season event is a scheduling reality of the modern PGA Tour calendar. For players who performed well at Royal Birkdale, there is a question of energy management versus momentum. Scheffler, who has demonstrated an ability to sustain elite play for extended stretches, is unlikely to let any fatigue show.

What's Next

Round 1 of the 3M Open begins Thursday, July 23 at TPC Twin Cities with tee times starting at 6:50 AM ET. The final round is scheduled for Sunday, July 26. Following the 3M Open, two more regular-season events remain before the FedExCup Playoffs begin — making this week a pivotal moment for players on the bubble. Live coverage is available via Golf Channel and streaming platforms throughout the week.
