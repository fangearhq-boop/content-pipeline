PGA Tour Sweeps All Four 2026 Majors as LIV Golf Faces Its Deepest Crisis

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 21, 2026

When Ryan Fox holed his 12-foot putt on the 18th hole at Royal Birkdale on Sunday, he completed an unplanned but unmistakable statement about the state of professional golf in 2026.

Fox's win was the fourth consecutive major championship title captured by a PGA Tour member this year. All four 2026 majors — the Masters, the PGA Championship, the US Open, and The Open Championship — belong to the PGA Tour. LIV Golf went 0-for-4, and the shutout arrives at the precise moment the rival circuit faces the most serious financial crisis in its four-year history.

The Roll Call

The four 2026 major champions cover a range of career trajectories, but share one thing in common: they all play on the PGA Tour.

Rory McIlroy won the Masters at Augusta National — his second consecutive Augusta title and his sixth major overall, tying the European record. McIlroy, the most prominent figure to resist LIV Golf's recruiting efforts since the circuit's launch, has now won two of the last three Masters.

Aaron Rai won the PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club, claiming his first major title.

Wyndham Clark won the US Open at Shinnecock Hills wire-to-wire, claiming his second US Open title.

Ryan Fox, at 200-1 odds, won The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale — becoming only the third New Zealander to claim the Claret Jug.

LIV's Best Effort

LIV Golf players did compete at the majors, as they have since the circuit's 2022 launch. At The Open Championship, Bryson DeChambeau led the LIV effort but received a Rule 8.1a penalty in Round 2 and finished approximately T9. Lucas Herbert, who plays for LIV Ripper GC, was among the Round 3 leaders at Royal Birkdale before Fox's Sunday charge ended his bid.

The pattern across all four majors held: LIV players were present, occasionally competitive deep into tournaments, and ultimately did not win.

The Financial Context

The timing of the major shutout is inseparable from LIV's financial situation. The circuit's Saudi PIF funding has covered only half of what is needed to complete the 2026 season. Two August events remain in genuine jeopardy. The circuit's CEO has publicly stated he cannot guarantee all four remaining 2026 events will take place.

LIV has hired Ducera Partners as a financial advisor and added independent board directors. Its 2027 calendar proposals call for a scaled-back 10-event format. LIV players have been quietly reaching out to the PGA Tour about return pathways.

Rory McIlroy, who has been among the most outspoken PGA Tour advocates throughout the LIV saga, earned $5.34 million in major prize money alone in 2026, according to FrontOfficeSports. That figure does not include his additional PGA Tour earnings from non-major events.

The Competitive Divide

LIV Golf was launched with the argument that it would attract talent away from the PGA Tour and that major championships could not indefinitely exclude LIV players. The counter-argument, made most forcefully by results, is that the PGA Tour's competitive ecosystem — its depth of field, its pressure-testing over four rounds, its scheduling of majors and WGC events — continues to produce the world's best players when the biggest prizes are on the line.

Whether that argument settles the debate depends on how long LIV Golf survives. At the moment, both the on-course results and the financial trajectory point in the same direction.

What's Next

The PGA Tour resumes immediately with the 3M Open at TPC Twin Cities (July 23-26). The second half of the PGA Tour season will continue to define the major narrative heading toward the FedExCup Playoffs and Ryder Cup conversations. LIV Golf's UK event begins Thursday, July 23, with the status of August events still uncertain. The question of whether LIV Golf can survive in its current form — or any form — will be answered before the end of 2026.
