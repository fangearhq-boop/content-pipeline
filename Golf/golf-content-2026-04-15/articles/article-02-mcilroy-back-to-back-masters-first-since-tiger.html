Rory McIlroy Makes History at Augusta: First Player Since Tiger to Win Back-to-Back Masters

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 15, 2026

With his one-shot victory at -12, McIlroy joined Jack Nicklaus and Tiger Woods as the only players in history to win consecutive Masters titles — overcoming a blown six-shot lead to do it.

The List Is Very Short

Three players in Masters history have won back-to-back titles. Jack Nicklaus achieved the feat twice. Tiger Woods completed it in 2001 and 2002 — the last time anyone had done it before last Sunday. And now, Rory McIlroy, who posted a final-round 1-under 71 to finish at 12-under par and win the 2026 Masters by one shot over Scottie Scheffler.

What separates McIlroy's entry in that company from a simple footnote in the record books is the manner in which it happened. He did not cruise to this title. He was in complete control of this tournament, holding a six-shot lead at the midway point — the kind of advantage that at Augusta National historically translates into a comfortable Sunday victory. Then the lead disappeared. The field closed. And Rory McIlroy had to figure out how to win again from a much more uncomfortable position.

He did. That's the measure of what separates the great champions from the merely excellent ones at Augusta National.

The Final Day

Sunday's final round produced exactly the kind of tension that Augusta tends to provide when the lead is contested. Scheffler, who had been the most consistent performer on the planet over the first half of 2026, mounted a charge that kept the margin tight through the back nine. He finished at 11-under — one shot back after a round that gave him every reason to believe a Masters title was within reach.

The group tied for third at 10-under told its own story about how fiercely the day was fought: Cameron Young, Russell Henley, Justin Rose, and Tyrrell Hatton all finished within two shots of the champion. The back nine at Augusta on Sunday had been a genuine competition, not a coronation.

McIlroy held on. His final round of 71 was not his best golf of the week — it was the golf of a player managing pressure rather than eliminating it. That distinction matters. It suggests a kind of resilience that pure ball-striking alone cannot explain.

Where This Places Him

McIlroy's victory is his fifth major championship. He completed the career grand slam with his 2025 Masters win — a result that ended a prolonged wait for the title that had eluded him across multiple near-misses over the years. The 2026 win adds the dimension of back-to-back dominance at the tournament that had historically been his white whale.

The $22.5 million total purse, the largest in Masters history, saw McIlroy earn $4.5 million from the victory. He has withdrawn from this week's RBC Heritage, taking a scheduled break before the remainder of the major calendar unfolds.

What's Next

The question for McIlroy now is what comes next in the 2026 major calendar. With the PGA Championship, U.S. Open, and The Open Championship still ahead, he enters the summer with the confidence of a two-time defending Masters champion and the motivation that comes from having already proven he can win the biggest events when the pressure is highest. He will return to competition in due course — rested, and carrying green jackets from two consecutive springs at Augusta National.
