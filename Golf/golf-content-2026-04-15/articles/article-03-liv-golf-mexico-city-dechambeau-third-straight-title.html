LIV Golf Mexico City Preview: DeChambeau Chases Unprecedented Third Straight Individual Title at Chapultepec

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | April 15, 2026

The LIV Golf circuit's sixth event of 2026 brings high altitude, extreme driving distances, and Bryson DeChambeau's pursuit of something no individual player has ever done in the league's history.

Three in a Row Has Never Been Done

Bryson DeChambeau has won the last two LIV Golf individual tournaments. This week at Club de Golf Chapultepec in Mexico City, he arrives with an opportunity to accomplish something that has never happened in the brief history of the LIV Golf League: three consecutive individual titles.

DeChambeau's dominance on the circuit has been a significant storyline of the 2026 season. His Crushers GC team is the all-time winningest franchise in league history, with nine regular-season titles and a Team Championship to their name. As both the team's most important player and the defending two-time individual champion, DeChambeau enters Mexico City as the figure the rest of the field is chasing.

The altitude at Chapultepec will suit him specifically. Club de Golf Chapultepec sits at approximately 7,350 feet above sea level — the highest elevation on the entire LIV Golf circuit. Thin air means reduced aerodynamic resistance on golf balls, producing the longest drives the league's players will see all season. DeChambeau, who has built his game around maximizing distance off the tee, is particularly well-suited to a course where power off the tee translates more directly into competitive advantage.

The Competition

The Mexico City field is among the most compelling in the league's 2026 schedule. Jon Rahm's Legion XIII team arrives as the defending team champions — the Spanish superstar will be motivated to reassert his franchise's dominance after watching the Crushers accumulate their record-setting total. Rahm is joined in Legion XIII by Tyrrell Hatton, whose form in 2026 has been strong across the circuit.

The defending individual champion at this specific event is Joaquin Niemann, who won LIV Golf Mexico City last year by three strokes. Niemann knows the Chapultepec layout well and will be looking to reclaim the trophy on a course that has suited his controlled, precise approach to driving and iron play. Cameron Smith and Anthony Kim also feature in the 57-player field.

Thomas Detry and Branden Grace have both shown strong form across the 2026 LIV season, with Detry finishing second at the Hong Kong event and maintaining consistent top results. Both are capable of challenging for the individual title at the right venue.

The Course

Club de Golf Chapultepec plays as a par 71 measuring 7,443 yards, with tree-lined fairways featuring Kikuyu grass and a layout that rewards course management while offering the altitude-amplified driving distances that make this week unique on the LIV calendar. The combination of a demanding course design and extreme elevation makes Mexico City one of the most distinctive stops on the circuit.

What's Next

LIV Golf Mexico City runs April 16 through April 19 at Club de Golf Chapultepec. Four rounds of stroke play determine both the individual champion and the team title — two separate but equally meaningful results from the same week of competition. If DeChambeau wins for the third consecutive time, it would represent a landmark moment in the young league's history and cement his status as its signature competitive presence in 2026.
