No Korda, No Thitikul: LPGA's JM Eagle LA Championship Opens Wide as Chevron Approaches

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 15, 2026

The final LPGA Tour event before the Chevron Championship major begins Thursday with both World No. 1 Jeeno Thitikul and No. 2 Nelly Korda absent from the field.

Two Chairs Empty at El Caballero

The LPGA Tour's JM Eagle LA Championship gets underway Thursday at El Caballero Country Club — the last event on the schedule before the Chevron Championship, the circuit's first major of 2026. And the two best players in the world will not be competing for the $3.75 million purse.

World No. 1 Jeeno Thitikul is not in the field. World No. 2 Nelly Korda has withdrawn. Both absences are deliberate strategic choices by players who have enough experience at the top of the game to understand that arriving at a major championship physically and mentally prepared is more valuable than an additional competitive week on the front end.

Korda in particular has established a pattern of managing her schedule carefully around the major calendar. She has repeatedly prioritized rest and preparation over participation in every available event, and the results at major championships have validated the approach. The Chevron Championship is approaching — the absence of both Korda and Thitikul from El Caballero this week is, in one sense, a sign that the two best players are taking the major very seriously.

Who Steps Up

With the top two players in the world rankings absent, Hyo Joo Kim enters the week as the highest-ranked player in a 144-player field. The South Korean star brings a victory already recorded in 2026 and a game that can compete on any layout the LPGA Tour presents. Her tee-to-green accuracy and putting form make her a compelling pick to close out the week as the tournament champion.

The list of 2026 LPGA winners in the field is substantial. Hannah Green, Mi Hyang Lee, Hyo Joo Kim, Lindblad, and Lauren Coughlin have all taken titles this season — five players with winning momentum entering a week that, because of the absences at the top, represents one of the more open winner's markets of the year.

Further depth comes from Minjee Lee, Miyu Yamashita, and Sei Young Kim — all experienced competitors who have won on the LPGA Tour and understand what it takes to close out tournaments under pressure.

The Chevron Context

Whatever happens at El Caballero this week, the narrative thread connects directly to the Chevron Championship. The JM Eagle LA Championship is the last competitive test before the major — and winning it, or simply producing consistent performance across four rounds, gives players form and confidence that carries forward. Hyo Joo Kim winning this week would make her an obvious name to watch at the Chevron. A victory from any of the other 2026 winners in the field would raise similar questions.

The absence of Korda and Thitikul changes the immediate winner's odds but doesn't reduce the significance of what's at stake. This week's champion earns a meaningful piece of the $3.75 million purse and momentum heading into the most important stretch of the LPGA calendar.

What's Next

The JM Eagle LA Championship runs April 16 through April 19 at El Caballero Country Club. Following this event, the LPGA Tour moves directly to the Chevron Championship — where Korda, Thitikul, and every other top player on the circuit will be competing for the year's first major title. The winner this week gets a head start in form and momentum. The stage is set.
