RBC Heritage 2026 Preview: Without McIlroy, Scheffler Leads a Stacked 82-Player Field to Harbour Town

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 15, 2026

The first PGA Tour Signature Event since the Masters begins Thursday at Harbour Town Golf Links — and the new two-time Masters champion will not be in the field.

McIlroy Steps Aside

Rory McIlroy won his second consecutive Masters championship last Sunday. He will not be defending his reputation at Harbour Town Golf Links this week.

McIlroy has withdrawn from the 2026 RBC Heritage — a decision that carries no financial consequence. As the winner of a major championship, McIlroy is exempt from the financial penalties that typically apply to top-ranked players who skip designated Signature Events. He's taking a week off after Augusta, and the competitive consequence falls entirely to the rest of the field.

That field has a clear leader now. Scottie Scheffler, the world's No. 1 golfer who finished one shot behind McIlroy at the Masters, enters Harbour Town as the betting favorite at around +350 — a commanding position in a Signature Event that rarely sees such a dominant pre-tournament consensus. The next-shortest price in the market sits at roughly +1450, a gap that reflects how clearly the oddsmaking community views this as Scheffler's tournament to lose.

The Course — and Why It Suits Scheffler

Harbour Town Golf Links is not a course that flatters every type of golfer. The Pete Dye design, measuring 7,243 yards at a par of 71, features some of the smallest greens on the entire PGA Tour — greens that have recently been restored to the original 1969 specifications and resurfaced with Bermudagrass. Missing those greens is the primary path to a high score at Harbour Town, and hitting them requires the kind of precision that only the most accurate ball-strikers consistently produce.

Scheffler's game is built around those exact attributes. His strokes gained metrics are elite across approach play and off-the-tee accuracy — the two categories that Harbour Town rewards most directly. He won the 2024 RBC Heritage and carries that course knowledge into Thursday's opening round alongside motivation provided by Sunday's near-miss at Augusta.

The Rest of the Field

The 82-player field around Scheffler is genuinely deep. Xander Schauffele, Cameron Young, Tommy Fleetwood, and Matt Fitzpatrick all enter with recent form and the course-management ability that Harbour Town demands. Viktor Hovland has been identified by previews as a player in strong form heading into the week.

The defending champion, Justin Thomas, makes his return to Harbour Town after winning the 2025 RBC Heritage in epic fashion — defeating Andrew Novak on the first hole of a sudden-death playoff. Thomas, whose precise iron play and imaginative course management have always aligned well with this layout, will be motivated to defend.

The tournament carries a $20 million purse as a Signature Event, with 700 FedEx Cup points going to the winner — one of the more significant allocations available outside of the four major championships.

What's Next

The opening round of the RBC Heritage begins Thursday, April 16. Four days of competition at Harbour Town will run through Sunday, April 19. Scheffler's performance will be watched closely — not just because of the size of his favorite's tag, but because any win for the world's No. 1 this week would speak directly to how he processes the Masters result and channels it into the rest of his 2026 season. The leaderboard on Thursday evening will offer the first real answer.
