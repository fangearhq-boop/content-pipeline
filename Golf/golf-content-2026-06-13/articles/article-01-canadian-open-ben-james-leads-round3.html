Rookie Ben James Leads Canadian Open Into Round 3 With Crowded Leaderboard Chasing

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 13, 2026

The most unlikely name atop the RBC Canadian Open leaderboard is also the most interesting one. Ben James — a newly minted professional competing in one of his first PGA Tour events — leads the field at 10 under par heading into Saturday's Round 3 at TPC Toronto at Osprey Valley. He got there by shooting a 63 in Round 2, one of the best scores posted all week.

When someone at the course asked James to explain his position, he said he had "no clue." It was the most refreshing answer a tournament leader has given in quite some time.

One stroke back at 9 under is Sam Burns, a player with considerably more context for why he is where he is. Burns lost a playoff at this same tournament to defending champion Ryan Fox last year, and he arrived in Toronto this week with something to settle. A chance to win the Canadian Open at the venue where he fell short would be the kind of redemption the game occasionally provides.

A Loaded Leaderboard

What makes this setup genuinely compelling is the depth of the field within range. Approximately 11 players are within two shots of the lead — a density that ensures Sunday will feel wide open regardless of what happens in Round 3. Brooks Koepka is among those two back, and he is always dangerous on layouts that demand precision and reward a methodical game plan. Koepka's experience in high-pressure final rounds is among the best in the world.

Ryan Fox, the defending champion, is also within striking distance. Defending champions rarely fade quietly, and Fox's knowledge of TPC Toronto — gained from his victory last year — represents a genuine edge heading into the weekend rounds.

Tommy Fleetwood, one of the most consistent ball strikers on the planet, is in the mix. And Matthew Fitzpatrick — who has three PGA Tour wins in 2026 already and is hunting his fourth of the season — teed off at 10:45 AM Saturday, positioned to make a weekend charge if he can put together a clean round on a course that suits his precise style.

The Burns Redemption Plot

For narrative purposes, Burns vs. this golf course might be the most compelling thread. He hasn't won on the PGA Tour since 2023 — a drought that has lasted longer than his talent would suggest. He came to this tournament knowing what happened here last year. He is one back, paired with James in Saturday's afternoon wave, and he has the game to close out a tournament when given a chance.

If he can take the lead into Sunday, the story writes itself: Sam Burns, one year later, back at the same venue, finally getting what escaped him in a playoff.

What's Next

Round 3 is being played today, with coverage on Golf Channel from 1-3 PM ET and CBS from 3-6 PM ET. Sunday's final round will determine the champion. Whether Ben James holds on, Burns mounts a charge, Koepka powers through, or someone from deep in the field makes a Sunday move — this leaderboard has the ingredients for a memorable finish in Toronto.
