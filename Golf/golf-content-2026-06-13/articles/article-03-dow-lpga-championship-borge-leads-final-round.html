Dow Championship Final Round Set: Borge and Mack Lead at 12-Under, Korda Lurks

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 13, 2026

The Dow LPGA Championship at Midland Country Club in Michigan heads into its final round Sunday with a lead set by two players who have earned every inch of it. Celine Borge and Polly Mack share the top of the leaderboard at 12 under par, one ahead of Ayaka Furue and Yuna Nishimura at 11 under — a tight grouping at the top that makes Sunday's outcome impossible to predict.

And lurking behind the leaders is Nelly Korda.

The Lead Group

Celine Borge has been one of the steadiest performers in women's golf in recent seasons — a Norwegian player whose precision and consistency make her a formidable front-runner when she holds the lead. Her partnership with Polly Mack in the best-ball portions of this tournament has given them a complementary dynamic that allows each to carry the other when one is off.

Ayaka Furue and Yuna Nishimura at 11 under represent the most immediate threat. Furue — a member of Japan's LPGA Tour contingent who has developed into a consistent international performer — has the ball-striking and short game to make a run at the leaders over 18 holes. Nishimura provides the team's competitive edge and has looked sharp in the scoring columns through three rounds.

The Korda Factor

Sunday in women's golf often ends with Nelly Korda's name near the top. The world's premier women's player has been building momentum through the tournament, and while she entered Saturday without the scoring advantage to feel comfortable, she has closed the gap through aggressive play and excellent putting. A final round charge from Korda is not a remote possibility — it is a genuine probability that the leaders will need to account for.

Korda won earlier in 2026 and has shown the ability to produce low scores when the tournament demands it. If the pin positions on Sunday open up any opportunities at Midland Country Club, she is one of the players most capable of capitalizing.

What's Next

The Dow LPGA Championship final round takes place Sunday, June 14, at Midland Country Club. With Borge and Mack holding a one-shot lead over Furue and Nishimura — and Korda charging — Sunday's outcome will be determined by who can manage the pressure of a tight final round leaderboard and the specific demands of Midland Country Club's closing holes. The champion will be worth watching closely heading into the rest of the LPGA Tour summer schedule.
