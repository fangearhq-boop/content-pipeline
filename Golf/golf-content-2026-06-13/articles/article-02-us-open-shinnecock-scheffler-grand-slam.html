Scheffler's Grand Slam Birthday: The US Open at Shinnecock Hills Is Five Days Away

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 13, 2026

The 2026 US Open begins Thursday at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York. That is five days from now. And the circumstances surrounding the tournament's most compelling narrative are almost too perfectly constructed to be anything other than what they are: a story waiting to happen.

Scottie Scheffler, the world's No. 1 player, enters as the clear betting favorite at +550 at major sportsbooks. He has already won two Masters titles (2022, 2024), a PGA Championship (2025), and an Open Championship (2025). He needs the US Open — and only the US Open — to complete the career Grand Slam, a feat accomplished by fewer than five players in the history of professional golf.

The final round of the 2026 US Open is scheduled for Sunday, June 21. Scottie Scheffler's 30th birthday is June 21, 2026. That date is also Father's Day.

The Course

Shinnecock Hills is one of golf's great proving grounds. The par 70 layout stretches over 7,400 yards and plays as a links-style course across the hills of Southampton, exposed to Long Island's wind and weather in ways that can completely alter a round within hours. The fairways are wide by visual standards but flanked by rough that, under US Open conditions, can reach five inches — punishing for anything errant.

The greens at Shinnecock are the tournament's defining feature. Firm, fast, and nearly impossible to hold from certain angles, they produce the kind of scoring swings that define US Opens — a two-under round that feels like perfection, a four-over round that felt like a disaster somehow avoided. The last time the US Open returned here, in 2018, Brooks Koepka won by one shot over Tommy Fleetwood with a four-round total of 1 under par. That was considered scoring well.

The Field

Rory McIlroy, the reigning Masters champion and the player perhaps most associated with the pursuit of the Grand Slam before Scheffler, enters at +900. McIlroy won the Masters this year in one of his finest performances — a redemption story years in the making. Coming to Shinnecock, he is the second choice in a field that includes genuine major championship contenders at every level.

Jon Rahm (+1400) has proven his ability to win US Opens — he took the title in 2021. Xander Schauffele (+1600) has developed into a consistent major performer. Bryson DeChambeau (+2000), Cameron Young (+2000), Tommy Fleetwood (+2000), and Ludvig Aberg (+2000) round out a tier of credible winners who can challenge if the course sets up favorably for their games.

But at the top of the board, the story is Scheffler. +550 does not happen for a player unless the market genuinely believes he is the most likely winner. That confidence is justified by his results over the last two years.

What's Next

The US Open begins Thursday, June 18. Four rounds across one of the most demanding venues in major championship golf, with the world watching to see whether Scottie Scheffler can complete the career Grand Slam on his 30th birthday on Father's Day. If the setup delivers on its promise, this could be one of the great major championship stages in years.
