LIV Golf's Financial Crisis by the Numbers: Saudi PIF Has Covered Just a Third of 2026 Needs

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 13, 2026

The financial situation at LIV Golf is no longer a matter of speculation or carefully managed messaging. The numbers are in the public record. The implications are serious. And the question of whether the circuit's remaining 2026 events will all take place is now a genuine one, not a rhetorical one.

According to multiple reports, LIV Golf needs approximately $600 million to complete its full 2026 schedule. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund, which has bankrolled the circuit since its inception, has contributed approximately $66 million in May and $130 million in June — totaling roughly $196 million. That is about one-third of what is required.

What the CEO Said

When CNBC asked CEO Scott O'Neil directly whether he could guarantee LIV Golf's final four 2026 events — UK, New Jersey, Indianapolis, and Michigan — his response was telling. "What I can guarantee is a heck of a return if you come invest in this business," he said.

That is not a guarantee of the events. It is a pitch to investors. The difference is significant.

One event, LIV Golf Louisiana, has already been postponed from its original late-June slot, making it the first casualty of the funding shortage. Whether that postponement becomes a cancellation, and whether it is the first of several, depends on the funding disbursements that reportedly arrive on a monthly basis from the PIF — each of which can be reduced, delayed, or withheld.

The Restructuring Plan

LIV Golf is not operating without a plan. The circuit is actively pursuing what is being described internally as a "LIV 2.0" concept — a restructured version of the league that would require less direct Saudi funding and bring in $250-350 million in new investment from external sources. Turnaround specialists have been brought in to manage the financial situation. The organizational structure is shifting, as evidenced by the exit of Yasir Al-Rumayyan from the chairman's role — a signal that the PIF is stepping back not just in terms of financial commitment but in terms of organizational control.

The remaining 2026 schedule currently on the books: LIV Golf UK (July 23-26), followed by events in New Jersey, Indianapolis, and Michigan in August. Whether all four happen depends on whether LIV Golf can close its funding gap before each event's operational deadlines.

The Broader Context

LIV Golf launched in 2022 with the promise of unlimited Saudi backing, a breakaway structure that could eventually rival the PGA Tour, and enough prize money to attract the sport's biggest names. That promise is now being tested against a more complex financial reality — one in which the PIF appears to be reducing its commitment to an entity that has not yet achieved the commercial independence its founding concept required.

The PGA Tour's ongoing discussions with PIF complicate the picture further. If LIV Golf struggles to complete its 2026 schedule, the question of what happens to its players — and their standing with the PGA Tour — becomes immediate and consequential.

What's Next

The next event, LIV Golf UK, is scheduled for July 23-26. Whether it takes place as planned will be the first significant test of whether LIV Golf's funding situation has stabilized or continued to deteriorate. Between now and then, the circuit needs to either receive additional PIF disbursements or close its investment raise. The outcome of that effort will define what the rest of the 2026 season looks like — and what the future of LIV Golf looks like beyond it.
