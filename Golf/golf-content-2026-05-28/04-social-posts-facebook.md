### STORY 5: US Women's Open Preview — Historic First at Riviera Country Club

#### Long-Form Post

The 81st U.S. Women's Open Presented by Ally arrives at a venue it has never visited before: Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California.

This is history. Riviera has hosted the 1948 U.S. Open and the 2017 U.S. Amateur, establishing itself as one of the great American golf courses. The George Thomas-designed layout has been called "the Riviera of the West" and is renowned for its strategic challenges — not length or raw difficulty, but the relentless requirement to think through every shot. For the first time, it will serve as the venue for a USGA women's major, running June 4-7.

**The defending champion:** Maja Stark of Sweden won the 2025 U.S. Women's Open at Erin Hills with a final score of 7-under 281, finishing two strokes ahead of Nelly Korda and Rio Takeda. Stark arrives at Riviera as champion, though without prior competitive data on the course.

**The favorites:** Korda is the most compelling storyline. She has won three times in 2026, including the Chevron Championship — her third USGA or LPGA major title. She also won the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba earlier this season, demonstrating current form that ranks among the best of any player in women's golf. The question is whether the Riviera course setup — which analysts note rewards strategic thinking rather than raw power — will separate the field or allow Korda's all-around game to dominate.

Atthaya "Jeeno" Thitikul holds the World No. 1 ranking. Her back-to-back CME Group Tour Championship wins in 2024 and 2025 establish her as the world's top-ranked player, and Riviera is the kind of course where her precision and consistency will be assets.

With 1,897 entries accepted, all of the top-25 players in the women's world rankings in the field, and the historic first at Riviera, the 2026 U.S. Women's Open is shaping up as one of the most compelling weeks in women's golf in years.
