US Women's Open 2026 Preview: First Time at Riviera, Korda in Prime Form, History at Stake

By Jake Torres | May 28, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The 81st U.S. Women's Open Presented by Ally will make history on June 4-7 at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California — the first time a USGA women's major championship has ever been played at the legendary George Thomas-designed layout, and the first U.S. Women's Open ever contested in the Greater Los Angeles area.

Riviera has hosted the 1948 U.S. Open, the 2017 U.S. Amateur, and the Genesis Invitational for men's PGA Tour play for years. It has never opened its gates for a women's USGA major. That changes in seven days, and the field entering Riviera is as compelling as any in recent women's golf history.

The Defending Champion

Maja Stark of Sweden arrives at Riviera as the defending U.S. Women's Open champion after winning the 2025 edition at Erin Hills with a 72-hole score of 7-under 281. Stark won by two strokes over Nelly Korda and Rio Takeda in a tense final round, claiming the title at a course that suited her power and precision off the tee.

Defending a U.S. Women's Open title at a new venue for which no women's professional data exists is uniquely challenging. Stark will enter Riviera without course-specific notes from competitive rounds there, relying on practice preparation and her general skills at strategic, thinking-player courses. Those skills are real — Stark is a genuine talent who earned her 2025 title.

Korda: Three Wins and a Major Already in 2026

Nelly Korda is the story of women's golf in 2026, and her path to Riviera is paved with evidence that she is the most complete player in the world right now. Three wins on the LPGA Tour this season, including the Chevron Championship — her third major title and 17th career LPGA win. A Riviera Maya Open victory at Mayakoba by four shots. A return to the World No. 2 ranking behind Atthaya Thitikul.

Korda's game is built around a combination of power and precision that fits Riviera's demands well. She is long enough to take the short par-4s out of play, precise enough to navigate the strategic bunkering that George Thomas deployed throughout the layout, and experienced enough in major championship environments — having won at Augusta National, the U.S. Women's Open predecessor venues, and other major sites — to manage Sunday pressure.

The question with Korda at Riviera is not whether her game fits the course. It does. The question is whether the unique character of Riviera's greens and approach angles — which analysts describe as rewarding careful thought rather than aggressive execution — will create a different kind of challenge for a player whose instinct is often to attack.

Thitikul and the No. 1 Ranking

Atthaya "Jeeno" Thitikul arrives at Riviera as the World No. 1 ranked women's golfer and a two-time CME Group Tour Championship winner (2024, 2025). Thitikul's relentless consistency — her ability to post top-10 finishes at nearly every event she enters — has kept her at the summit of the women's world rankings through a season in which Korda has been the most visible winner.

Thitikul is making her fifth U.S. Women's Open appearance at Riviera. Her precision and her ability to adapt to different course types have been the distinguishing features of her best performances, and Riviera's cerebral demands suit a player who has never been described as a bombers-and-scorers type.

Riviera's Historic Character

The USGA has accepted 1,897 entries for the 2026 U.S. Women's Open — all of the top-25 players in the world are in the field, alongside 11 past champions. The championship has never been held in Los Angeles before, and the Riviera site adds a specific character that other recent venues have not offered.

Riviera Country Club's reputation in golf circles is as a place that rewards thoughtful players. The par-4 10th and the famous par-4 4th — with its greenside bunker cut directly into the putting surface — are the kinds of holes that separate players who study the course from those who simply react to it. Former U.S. Open winners who have played Riviera extensively describe it as a course that gives you information and expects you to use it.

For women's golf, this first visit to Riviera is both a practical and symbolic milestone. The course's stature in the history of men's professional golf is unquestioned. Adding a women's major to that record elevates both the championship and the venue.

What's Next

The 2026 U.S. Women's Open begins June 4 at Riviera Country Club. Golf Fanrecap will provide full preview coverage through the week, with qualifier and player notes in the days preceding the championship. Round 1 tee times and groupings will be announced by the USGA in the coming days.

Korda is the form player. Thitikul is the ranking leader. Stark is the defending champion. And Riviera — in its first women's major — will ask the most complete question in professional golf: who can play this course the best when everything is on the line?
