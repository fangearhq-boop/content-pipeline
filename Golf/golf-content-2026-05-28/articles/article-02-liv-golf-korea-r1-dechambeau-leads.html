LIV Golf Korea Round 1: DeChambeau Shares Lead at -5, Crushers GC in Front

By Jake Torres | May 28, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Bryson DeChambeau made a strong opening statement in his bid to defend the Korea title at LIV Golf Korea 2026, shooting a 5-under 65 at Asiad Country Club in Busan to share the first-round lead with Charles Howell III and Scott Vincent. DeChambeau birdied six of his first 10 holes before back-nine bogeys at 13 and 15 brought him back to level with his co-leaders.

The tournament runs May 28-31 at Asiad Country Club, a 7,024-yard Par 70 that was originally constructed for the 2002 Asian Games and redesigned to professional tournament standards by architect Rees Jones in 2019. The course layout features the narrow, tree-lined Valley nine on the front nine and the more scoring-friendly Lake nine on the back half.

First-Round Leaderboard

DeChambeau (Crushers GC) and Howell III (HyFlyers GC), alongside Vincent (also HyFlyers GC), set the early benchmark with 65s. Their 5-under total on a Par 70 layout represents genuinely strong scoring, particularly given the Valley nine's tree-lined, demanding character.

Three players share fourth place at 4-under: Torque GC captain Joaquin Niemann, Majesticks GC co-captain Ian Poulter, and 4Aces GC's Thomas Pieters. Their -4 (66) openings place them within striking distance of the leaders heading into Round 2.

Jon Rahm, who leads the 2026 LIV Golf season-long individual standings and enters Korea as the points leader in the race for the individual championship, did not open in the lead group. Rahm has been the most consistent season-long performer on LIV in 2026, but Korea's first round belongs to DeChambeau.

The Crushers Show Their Intent

On the team side — which runs concurrently with the individual competition across every LIV Golf tournament — DeChambeau's Crushers GC opened with a cumulative -10 total to lead the team standings after Round 1. The Crushers are defending both the Korea team and individual titles from 2025, when DeChambeau won the individual event in Incheon.

The 4Aces GC, captained by Dustin Johnson, enter Korea having won three of the last five team events, including back-to-back team victories in Asia (Hong Kong and Singapore) and the most recent event in Virginia. They will be expected to challenge Crushers for the team title across the remaining rounds.

DeChambeau's Korea Connection

DeChambeau's affinity for this tournament runs deeper than just the defending title. His aggressive driving style and powerful iron play suit the Asiad CC layout's wide-open Lake nine, where birdie opportunities are more plentiful. His ability to overpower scoring holes while managing the more demanding Valley nine has historically been a formula for success in Korea.

The first-round bogeys at 13 and 15 were the only real blemishes on an otherwise dominant opening nine and a half holes. DeChambeau's front nine — six birdies in ten holes — showed the potential for a very low round before the back nine brought him back to the field. A cleaner second round could separate him considerably from the pack.

What's Next

Round 2 of LIV Golf Korea takes place Friday, May 29. The individual and team leaderboards will continue to evolve as all 48 players in the field complete their opening rounds and begin their second. The $20 million individual purse ($4 million winner's share) and $10 million team purse ($3 million winner's share) provide substantial incentive across all divisions of the competition.

Golf Fanrecap will follow the full event through Sunday, May 31. DeChambeau leads after Round 1. The question now is whether he can hold off a strong field featuring Rahm, Niemann, Poulter, and an entire roster of world-class players over three remaining rounds.
