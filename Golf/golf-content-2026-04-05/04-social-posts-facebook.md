### STORY 6: Brooks Koepka Returns to the PGA Tour

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

For the first time since leaving for LIV Golf, Brooks Koepka is back on the PGA Tour.

The four-time major champion was among the most high-profile players to join LIV Golf when the circuit launched. Now, under a new reinstatement program, he has become the first major LIV star to return to the PGA Tour full-time.

The results so far have been modest: T9, T13, T18 in his first three starts back. Analysts have noted some rust in the short game — an area that has historically been a strength. But Koepka is still Brooks Koepka, a player who has historically peaked at the major championships, and he remains qualified for the Masters beginning April 9.

Not everyone followed him back. Bryson DeChambeau, Jon Rahm, and Cameron Smith all issued statements reaffirming their commitment to LIV Golf following Koepka's departure. The landscape of men's professional golf remains split — but Koepka's return is a signal that at least for some players, the path back to the PGA Tour remains viable.
