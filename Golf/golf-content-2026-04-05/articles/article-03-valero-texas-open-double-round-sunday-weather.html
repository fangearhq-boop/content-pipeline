Valero Texas Open Plays Double-Duty Sunday After Weather Chaos — Masters Spots Still on the Line

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | April 5, 2026

The Valero Texas Open did not go according to schedule. After inclement weather forced the suspension of the third round on Saturday at TPC San Antonio, players returned Sunday morning at 8:45 AM ET to complete what they had started — before turning around and playing an entire fourth round in the same afternoon.

For a professional golfer, a double round is taxing under any circumstances. At the Valero Texas Open, with the Masters beginning four days later and automatic invitations on the line, the stakes transformed a logistical inconvenience into one of the most consequential days of the early 2026 PGA Tour season.

Why the Valero Matters This Week

The Valero Texas Open holds a unique place on the PGA Tour calendar as the event immediately preceding the Masters. In many years, that proximity is the primary storyline — the final tournament for players to sharpen their games before Augusta. In 2026, it carries additional weight: it is the final opportunity for eligible players to earn a Masters exemption through their PGA Tour standing or a tournament victory.

The winner of the Valero earns an automatic invitation to the Masters. For players on the bubble — close to the exemption thresholds but not quite inside them — the Valero represents one final chance to force their way into the field at Augusta National.

Fowler's Missed Opportunity

Rickie Fowler missed the cut at TPC San Antonio, eliminating his path to a Masters exemption through the Valero. It is a familiar and painful story for one of golf's most beloved players — Fowler has had one of the more complicated relationships with the Masters field over the course of his career, missing Augusta in years when his game and his ranking have not cooperated.

Sunday's round in San Antonio will not include him. That chapter of his 2026 Masters pursuit is closed.

The Double Round Dynamic

Playing 36 holes in a single day requires exceptional endurance and mental discipline. The players who advanced past the cut and remained in contention at TPC San Antonio face the challenge of completing their third round with whatever game they brought on Saturday, and then resetting — physically and mentally — for 18 more holes that determine who wins the tournament.

The player who manages that transition most effectively, who can sustain focus and shot-making through an unusually demanding Sunday, will claim the Valero title and with it the last Masters invitation of the 2026 qualification process.

What's Next

The Valero Texas Open champion will earn their Augusta National invitation just days before the first tee shots of the Masters. The contrast — from the weather chaos of a Texas Sunday to the immaculate grounds of Augusta National on Thursday — could not be more stark. But for the player who navigates Sunday most effectively, it will be worth every exhausting swing.
