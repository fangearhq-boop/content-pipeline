DeChambeau Is the Hottest Golfer on the Planet — Back-to-Back LIV Wins and Now He's Heading to the Masters

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 5, 2026

Bryson DeChambeau is on the kind of run that makes you stop and pay attention, regardless of which tour you follow or which side of the golf civil war you stand on.

Back-to-back LIV Golf victories in 2026. Five career LIV titles. The most recent one came at LIV Golf South Africa at Steyn City, where DeChambeau shot a final-round 6-under 65 and then beat Jon Rahm in a playoff to claim the title. It was his second win of the 2026 LIV season, following his earlier victory at LIV Singapore.

And now he is heading to the Masters.

The South Africa Playoff

The LIV Golf South Africa event came down to a playoff between two of the best players in the world in 2026. Rahm had been one of the circuit's most consistent performers all season — winning at Hong Kong, finishing runner-up multiple times, and playing the kind of measured, controlled golf that made him a world No. 1 for two years on the PGA Tour.

DeChambeau won it. He had the better round on Sunday, forced the playoff, and converted when it mattered. His Crushers GC team also won the team portion of the event. It was a comprehensive result for a player who has made LIV Golf his home and has thrived within it.

Entering the Masters in Form

The Masters is four days away. DeChambeau arrives at Augusta National in the best form of his 2026 season — two wins in his last two events, a playoff victory over one of golf's most experienced competitors, and the kind of momentum that is genuinely difficult to find entering a major championship.

He is listed among the longer-odds contenders at Augusta, in part because the Masters course tends to reward precision and course management over raw power. But DeChambeau's 2024 and 2025 game — the version of his swing that has made him one of the longest and most accurate drivers on either circuit — is built for Augusta in a way that his early career was not.

Rahm's Consistent Season

On the other side of the South Africa playoff is Jon Rahm, who continues to put together one of the most statistically impressive seasons on the LIV circuit. He won at Hong Kong. He has three runner-up finishes in 2026, including South Africa. He is arriving at Augusta as a former champion (2023) with recent form and a game that suits the course.

When DeChambeau and Rahm tee it up at Augusta National this week, the conversation about LIV Golf and its place in the broader golf ecosystem will inevitably surface. But the more important conversation will be about who is playing the better golf on one of the game's greatest stages.

What's Next

The Masters begins Thursday, April 9. DeChambeau and Rahm will be among the most closely watched players in the field — not only because of their recent form but because of what their presence at Augusta represents. The green jacket awaits. After two consecutive wins, DeChambeau is not going to Augusta just to participate.
