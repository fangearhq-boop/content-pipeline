Brooks Koepka Returns to the PGA Tour — First Major LIV Defector Back, Others Are Staying Put

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 5, 2026

Brooks Koepka has rejoined the PGA Tour. Under a new reinstatement program, the four-time major champion has returned from LIV Golf — becoming the first major star to make that move and reestablishing himself on the circuit where he built his career.

He is also the only one. Bryson DeChambeau, Jon Rahm, and Cameron Smith all publicly reaffirmed their commitment to LIV Golf following Koepka's departure, making clear that the circuit's other major draws are not following him back.

The Return Numbers

Koepka's first three starts back on the PGA Tour have produced results of T9, T13, and T18. Respectable results for a player who stepped away from Tour competition for an extended period, but modest for a player of his historical standard.

Analysts have noted some inconsistency in the short game — an area historically central to Koepka's success at major championships, where his scrambling and clutch putting have rescued rounds and defined victories. The expectation is that this is rust rather than a fundamental decline, but at 35 years old, the timeline for returning to peak major form is not unlimited.

The Masters Next Week

Koepka is qualified for the Masters beginning April 9 — his four major titles and world ranking position ensuring that Augusta National's invitation arrives regardless of his Tour status. He is a three-time major champion at the U.S. Open and the PGA Championship, with wins at Augusta's major peers. He has contended at the Masters without winning it. The right week of golf could change that.

His return to the PGA Tour brings an interesting variable to a Masters field already shaped by significant storylines. He arrives without the red-hot form of DeChambeau or the defending champion status of McIlroy, but with a track record in major championships that earns serious consideration at any major venue.

The Others Are Staying

The most notable aspect of Koepka's return may be what it reveals about the players who did not follow him. DeChambeau, Rahm, and Smith — three of the most prominent names who joined LIV Golf in its early years — have each made public commitments to remaining on the circuit. DeChambeau is coming off back-to-back wins. Rahm has been the circuit's most consistent performer in 2026. Smith won the British Open and has been one of LIV's most competitive players.

Their decision to stay signals that LIV Golf's competitive and financial proposition remains compelling for its top tier, even as Koepka charts a different course.

What's Next

Koepka competes at the Masters beginning April 9. After that, his 2026 PGA Tour schedule will be defined by his results and his reinstatement status — a situation that is being watched closely by the broader golf community as the industry continues to figure out what the relationship between the PGA Tour and LIV Golf looks like going forward. For now, Koepka is the experiment. The results will be instructive for everyone watching.
