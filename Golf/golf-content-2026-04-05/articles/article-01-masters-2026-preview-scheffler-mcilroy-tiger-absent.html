Masters 2026 Preview: Scheffler vs McIlroy, Tiger Absent, Amazon Prime Debut — Everything You Need to Know

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 5, 2026

The 91st Masters Tournament begins Thursday, April 9, at Augusta National Golf Club, and the storylines heading into this year's edition are among the richest the tournament has ever produced.

Scottie Scheffler and Rory McIlroy are the co-favorites. Tiger Woods will not be there. LIV Golf players are in the field for the second consecutive year. And for the first time, Amazon Prime Video joins the broadcast coverage of golf's most iconic event.

Four days. The green jackets are coming out.

Scheffler vs McIlroy

The headline matchup at the top of the leaderboard begins before a ball is struck. Scottie Scheffler, the world No. 1, arrives at Augusta as co-favorite at +500, looking to win a Masters that would add another layer to his already dominant era in professional golf. He crossed $100 million in career PGA Tour earnings earlier in 2026, joining only Tiger Woods and Rory McIlroy in that exclusive company.

McIlroy, for his part, is defending. He won the 2025 Masters to complete the career Grand Slam — joining a list of only five men who have won all four major championships — and he arrives at Augusta looking to do something even rarer: win back-to-back green jackets. It has been accomplished only three times in the tournament's history.

One of these two men is likely to be slipping on the green jacket at Sunday's ceremony. The field is deep, but the gap between them and the rest is meaningful.

The LIV Contingent

Bryson DeChambeau arrives at Augusta in extraordinary form, coming off back-to-back LIV Golf victories in 2026. He won the Singapore event and then beat Jon Rahm in a playoff at the South Africa event. His game is sharp. His confidence is high.

Rahm, himself a former Masters champion (2023), has been one of the most consistent performers on LIV in 2026 — winning at Hong Kong and finishing runner-up three times. He is a genuine contender at Augusta. Tyrrell Hatton and Tom McKibbin join them from the Legion XIII team as the only LIV group with multiple qualifiers in the field.

Tiger Woods Will Not Play

The most significant absence from the 2026 Masters field is Tiger Woods. Following a DUI arrest and rollover crash near his Jupiter Island, Florida home in late March, Woods issued a statement confirming he is stepping away from professional golf to seek treatment and focus on his health. He will not play at Augusta this year. Augusta National chairman responded with a statement of full support and warm wishes for his recovery.

The tournament goes on without him — but his absence is felt.

What's New in 2026

For the first time, Amazon Prime Video joins the broadcast coverage of the Masters alongside traditional partners. The move expands the reach of the tournament's coverage and marks a significant moment in the shifting media landscape of golf.

What's Next

The first round of the 91st Masters begins Thursday, April 9. All eyes will be on Scheffler, McIlroy, DeChambeau, and Rahm — and on whoever emerges from the field as the week unfolds. Augusta has a way of revealing characters, testing nerves, and producing moments nobody predicted. Thursday morning, it begins again.
