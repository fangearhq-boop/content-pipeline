Tiger Woods Steps Away From Golf for Treatment — Will Not Play 2026 Masters

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 5, 2026

Tiger Woods will not play the 2026 Masters. He has stepped away from professional golf to seek treatment and focus on his health.

"I am stepping away for a period of time to seek treatment and focus on my health."

That was the statement issued by the greatest golfer in the sport's history, ending speculation about whether he might appear at Augusta National this week and placing his immediate future in the game in an entirely different context.

What Happened

In late March, Tiger Woods was arrested near his Jupiter Island, Florida home following a two-car rollover crash. He was suspected of driving under the influence. He pleaded not guilty to the charges. A Florida judge subsequently granted him permission to leave the United States to seek the treatment he needs.

The sequence — crash, arrest, charges, plea, treatment announcement — unfolded over roughly a week. At each stage, the story grew larger, and the questions about his career grew more pressing. The statement he ultimately issued was clear and unambiguous: he is stepping away to address something more important than golf.

Augusta National's Response

Augusta National Golf Club's chairman issued a statement expressing the organization's full support for Tiger Woods as he pursues treatment and recovery. Augusta National has maintained a long and complicated relationship with Woods across his career — from the records he broke there to the 2019 comeback victory that stands as one of sport's great redemption stories.

The organization's support, expressed publicly and directly, reflects both the depth of that relationship and the sincerity of the concern for his wellbeing.

The Career in Context

Tiger Woods has 15 major championships, 82 PGA Tour victories, and a record that has shaped the sport for three decades. He is the reason millions of people watch golf. He is the player who redefined what athletic dominance in the sport could look like, who came back from a car accident in 2021 that doctors said could have cost him his leg, and who made millions of fans believe that anything was possible.

The 2026 Masters will proceed without him. It will be a lesser tournament for his absence — not because the field is thin, but because Tiger's presence at Augusta carries a weight that no other player's does.

What's Next

There is no timeline on Tiger's return, and there should not be one. The most important thing right now is his health and his wellbeing. Golf will be there when he is ready. The record books are patient. Augusta National will always be there in April. Right now, the only thing that matters is that Tiger gets the help he needs and comes out the other side in better shape. The game can wait.
