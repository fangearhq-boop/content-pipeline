Tom Kim Shines in Schwab R1: A Six-Under 64 and Signs of a Return

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 29, 2026

Tom Kim shot a 6-under-par 64 in Round 1 of the 2026 Charles Schwab Challenge at Colonial Country Club, co-leading the tournament after the opening round and offering a reminder of what the game's brightest young personalities looked like when he was dominating leaderboards in 2022.

Kim is tied with Lee Hodges, JJ Spaun, Matt McCarty, Ryan Gerard, and Andrew Putnam at the top of the field after a day in Fort Worth where soft conditions from early-week rain allowed scoring — but still required execution. A 64 at Colonial is not an accident. It requires precise approach play on a course where the wrong entry angle leaves impossible putts, and patient management of a layout that has swallowed more Sunday charges than almost any other venue on tour.

Why This Matters for Kim

Tom Kim won twice in his 2022 PGA Tour rookie breakout — a dual-victory run that included an exciting Shriners Children's Open title and a debut that had the golf world believing it was watching the emergence of a generational talent. He backed that up with a memorable Ryder Cup debut for the United States and established himself as one of the tour's most compelling young players.

The wins that seemed inevitable in 2022 have not come as quickly since. Kim has remained a consistent presence near the top of leaderboards, but converting that consistency into trophies at the tour's most demanding events has been elusive. A CBS Sports headline ahead of this week's event described his Round 1 performance as "showing signs of life" — a phrase that captures both the promise of his game and the expectation that comes with someone who started their career the way he did.

Colonial's Fit for Kim

Colonial Country Club's 7,200-yard layout rewards the strengths that Kim has built his game around: controlled ball-striking, creative iron play, and the ability to plot around a layout that punishes wayward misses more than most tour venues. The Green Mile — the closing stretch at holes 16, 17, and 18 — will be the true test, as it is every year at "Hogan's Alley," but players who can reach the back nine in position have given themselves a chance.

Kim's Round 1 round suggests he had one of those days where the game clicks with a clarity that makes everything feel easier than it is. Whether that feeling extends through a full 72-hole week at a demanding venue is the question every contender asks themselves at Colonial.

What's Next

Round 2 is underway Friday at Colonial. The cut comes after the second round. Rounds 3 and 4 are Saturday and Sunday, respectively — both on Golf Channel and ESPN+. Kim is in a six-way tie for the lead with 54 holes remaining, which puts him in position to potentially end a win drought that neither his talent nor his work ethic has deserved.

Six-under at Colonial is a strong statement. The real statement — the kind that ends a drought and answers the questions about what kind of player he's become — will have to wait for Sunday afternoon on the Green Mile.
