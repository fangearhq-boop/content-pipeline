Six Share the Lead at Colonial After Round 1 of Charles Schwab Challenge

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 29, 2026

Soft conditions following early-week rains in Fort Worth produced wide-open scoring in the opening round of the 2026 Charles Schwab Challenge, leaving six players tied for the lead at 6-under-par 64 through Round 1 at Colonial Country Club.

Tom Kim, Lee Hodges, JJ Spaun, Matt McCarty, Ryan Gerard, and Andrew Putnam each shot 64 to share the outright lead at "Hogan's Alley," the nickname attached to Colonial after Ben Hogan won the tournament five times on these grounds. The wide leaderboard reflects both the scoring opportunity created by the damp conditions and the broad talent level in a field chasing $9.9 million in prize money over the 80th consecutive year this tournament has been played at Colonial Country Club.

The Favorites Are in Range

Sitting two shots off the pace at 4-under is Ludvig Åberg — the betting favorite entering the week — who switched putters before the tournament began. Åberg's round of 66 kept him competitive despite the putting experiment, and the Swede has a strong connection to Colonial from a T3 finish in 2025.

Colonial Country Club sets its difficulty through demanding approach angles, tight fairways, and the infamous Green Mile — the closing stretch of holes 16-18 that has swallowed countless Sunday charges and produced as many dramatic finishes as any three-hole stretch on the PGA Tour. These demands reward elite ball-strikers with exceptional iron play, which is why Åberg, despite his putting adjustment, remains a strong candidate entering the weekend.

The Atmosphere at Colonial

The Charles Schwab Challenge carries unique historical weight on the PGA Tour calendar. The 80th consecutive year at a single venue is not just a statistic — it represents a relationship between a golf course, a tournament, and a community that has few parallels anywhere in professional sport. Walking the same fairways where Ben Hogan shot 259 in 1946 gives the Charles Schwab Challenge a texture of legacy that resonates differently than tournaments played on rotating courses.

The six players tied for the lead are all proven PGA Tour performers capable of handling that pressure. Tom Kim has won twice on tour. JJ Spaun has won once and been in contention multiple times. The weekend will be defined by who can execute when the Green Mile awaits and the crowd — one of the most passionate in professional golf — makes every shot feel like it matters.

What's Next

Round 2 is underway Friday at Colonial Country Club. The cut will be made after Round 2. Round 3 is Saturday and the final round is Sunday — all on Golf Channel and ESPN+. With such a wide-open leaderboard after Round 1, the tournament is genuinely unpredictable heading into the weekend, which is exactly the kind of environment that produces memorable Sundays at Hogan's Alley.

Watch the cut line closely — the soft first-round conditions mean many players will be in the mix, and whoever emerges from the pack by Saturday evening will face a truly open leaderboard on Sunday afternoon.
