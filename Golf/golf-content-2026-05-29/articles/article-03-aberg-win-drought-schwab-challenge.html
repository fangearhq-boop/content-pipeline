Why Colonial Fits

Colonial Country Club is one of the PGA Tour's most demanding and precise venues, and those demands happen to align exactly with Åberg's strengths. He is among the tour's elite iron players — the kind of ball-striker who can execute precise approaches on a course where approach angle determines everything, especially on the famous Green Mile closing stretch at holes 16, 17, and 18.

The connection is not theoretical. Åberg finished T3 at the 2025 Charles Schwab Challenge on this same course — a result that confirmed his ability to handle Colonial's specific test over four rounds. That familiarity, combined with his current form and the putter adjustment he has made this week, creates an alignment of factors that makes this week a genuine opportunity.

The Putter Change

Switching putters mid-season is a consequential decision. It reflects an honest evaluation by a player and his team that something about the current approach on the greens is not working at the level required to win. For Åberg, who is hitting iron shots close to the hole with regularity, the missed putts have been a recurring frustration that the stat sheet captures in partial strokes gained data.

The new putter's impact cannot be fully evaluated from a single round — a 4-under opening 66 suggests it did not hurt, but the real test will come when Sunday pressure is applied and the putts that need to fall for a championship result need to drop. The soft conditions that helped the entire field score low in Round 1 will give way to a more demanding weekend at Colonial.

What's Next

Round 2 is underway Friday at Colonial Country Club. Åberg sits two shots off the pace heading in, in a position that perfectly suits his style — close enough to lead, with enough distance to manage the leaderboard rather than protect it. If the putter responds this week the way his iron play has been suggesting it should, Åberg could be spending Sunday afternoon at Hogan's Alley deciding his next win.

Six top-10s without a win is a frustrating record for a player of his caliber. The drought ends somewhere. Colonial — a course where he has already proven himself — is as good a place as any.
