Gooch's 63 Puts Him in Command at LIV Golf Korea as DeChambeau Lurks

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 29, 2026

Talor Gooch fired a 7-under-par 63 in the second round of LIV Golf Korea to seize the 36-hole lead at Asiad Country Club in Busan, South Korea, moving to 8 under for the tournament and extending his advantage over a deep leaderboard heading into the final two rounds of LIV Golf's inaugural event on Korean soil.

Bryson DeChambeau sits in second place at 7 under, having started the week as one of three players sharing the Round 1 lead. Five players are tied for third at 5 under: Cameron Smith, Joaquin Niemann, Scott Vincent, Thomas Pieters, and Charles Howell III.

OKGC Building a Team Statement

Beyond the individual standings, Gooch's round was part of a remarkable team performance from OKGC, which leads the team competition at 14 under — the format that makes LIV Golf's scoring structure unique in professional golf. In a format where three-person team scores are aggregated, OKGC has established a significant cushion heading into the final two rounds.

The 54-player, no-cut format means all competitors remain in the field through Sunday, and with two rounds remaining, the gaps between players are still entirely bridgeable. But Gooch's 63 — combining precise ball-striking with hot putting on Asiad Country Club's manicured greens — set a round-of-the-tournament standard that the field will need to match just to threaten him.

DeChambeau's Challenge

Bryson DeChambeau enters the back half of LIV Golf Korea as the tournament's most compelling storyline outside of Gooch. DeChambeau shared the first-round lead, responded to Gooch's Round 2 charge by staying at 7 under, and now finds himself one shot back with two rounds to go at a course that rewards power and precision in equal measure.

DeChambeau won earlier in 2026 in Mexico City — by six shots, demonstrating his capacity for dominant individual performances. The Crushers GC captain will need to manufacture his own momentum on Saturday and hope Gooch has at least one difficult day remaining in the event.

The Significance of South Korea

LIV Golf Korea is the organization's first event in South Korea — a country with one of the deepest golf cultures in the world. South Korean players have dominated the LPGA Tour for decades, and the men's game has produced elite talent at every level. Hosting LIV Golf in Busan represents an expansion of the league's Asia-Pacific footprint and introduces the format to one of the sport's most passionate global audiences.

What's Next

Round 3 is Saturday and the final round is Sunday, May 31. With Gooch holding a one-shot individual lead and OKGC well-positioned in the team competition, the question is whether the field can apply enough pressure over the next 36 holes to create a final-round chase. DeChambeau, Smith, and Niemann all have the individual firepower to shoot low — the outcome will depend on whether they can do so simultaneously.

LIV Golf Korea concludes Sunday. Gooch has been the tournament's most consistent performer through two rounds. The final act begins Saturday morning at Asiad Country Club.
