US Women's Open Preview: Riviera Hosts First USGA Women's Major, Korda Targets Second 2026 Major

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 29, 2026

Women's golf arrives at one of the most storied addresses in the sport next week — and for the first time in history. The 81st US Women's Open Presented by Ally is scheduled for June 4-7 at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California, marking the first time a USGA women's major has been contested at a venue best known for men's professional events.

The field is as strong as the venue is iconic. All 25 of the world's top-ranked players have entered the championship. World No. 1 Jeeno Thitikul headlines a global roster that includes Nelly Korda — entering as the tournament's hottest player after three wins in 2026 — and Maja Stark, the 2025 champion returning to defend her title on a completely different course.

Why Riviera Matters

Riviera Country Club carries a specific kind of prestige in golf that few venues match. The course has hosted the Los Angeles Open, now known as the Genesis Invitational, for decades. It is famous for demanding creativity and precision in equal measure — awkward stances, unpredictable lies in the Kikuyu rough, and greens that reward approach shot placement over raw power. Ben Hogan won here. Sam Snead won here. And now women's golf gets its turn to add a chapter.

With 1,897 entries — tied for third all-time for the championship — and 11 past champions in the field, the 2026 US Women's Open has every element of a landmark event in the making. The combination of an elite player base and a genuinely historic venue makes this one of the most anticipated women's major championships in recent years.

Korda's Chase for No. 2

Nelly Korda enters the week with three 2026 wins already in hand, including the Chevron Championship — one of the LPGA Tour's five major championships. A US Women's Open title would give her two major victories in a single season, something that happens rarely and is considered one of the defining achievements any LPGA player can claim.

Korda finished as runner-up at the 2025 US Women's Open at Erin Hills — a result that came down to the final holes and left her genuinely close to the title before Maja Stark held on. The near-miss adds another layer to her motivation at Riviera. The course's precision demands suit her game, and she arrives at the peak of her 2026 confidence.

Stark's Defense, Thitikul's Pursuit

Maja Stark defending a major title at a new venue is one of the toughest assignments in golf. The course changes entirely, the conditions are different, and the pressure of being the reigning champion creates its own psychological weight. Stark has shown exceptional composure at major championships and should not be underestimated at Riviera.

Thitikul — ranked No. 1 in the world — brings a power game and consistency that will translate to any venue. She has been consistently excellent in 2026 and enters as the kind of player who can win on any course in any conditions.

What's Next

The 81st US Women's Open Presented by Ally begins Thursday, June 4 at Riviera Country Club. Coverage is on NBC/Peacock and Golf Channel throughout the week, with the final round on Sunday, June 7. This is one of the premier events in women's golf, and at one of the sport's most prestigious venues, the 2026 edition has the potential to be truly memorable.

Mark the dates. Women's golf at Riviera is a first-time story that deserves full attention — and the player field gathered for this occasion is more than capable of writing something worth remembering.
