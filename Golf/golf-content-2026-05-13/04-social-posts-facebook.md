### STORY 3: Spieth Grand Slam

**Long-Form Post (1:00 PM ET)**
The moment Jordan Spieth won The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale in 2017, he joined one of golf's most exclusive clubs — a player who had won three of the four major championships. He was 24 years old, with a long career ahead and what felt like an inevitable fourth title just around the corner.

Nine years later, he's still waiting for the PGA Championship.

Spieth arrives at Aronimink having won the 2015 Masters, the 2015 US Open, and the 2017 Open Championship. The PGA is the only one missing from his collection, and completing the career Grand Slam would make him just the seventh player in the modern era to hold all four major championship titles. He would join Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Gene Sarazen, and Rory McIlroy in that group.

The 32-year-old has tried before, more than once, with his best result being a tie for third at the 2019 PGA Championship. But he has said this week that he "feels closer than ever," and Aronimink — a course that rewards precision iron play rather than raw power — may be more suited to his game than the bombers' courses that have sometimes hurt his chances.

History. Redemption. A story nine years in the making. It starts tomorrow.
