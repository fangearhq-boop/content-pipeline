Nelly Korda Is 4 Points From the LPGA Hall of Fame — Her Next Chance Starts Tomorrow

By Ryan Calloway

May 13, 2026

While the men head to Aronimink for the PGA Championship, there is another major storyline playing out on the women's tour this week — and it involves the best female golfer in the world chasing one of the sport's rarest honors.

Nelly Korda arrives at the Kroger Queen City Championship at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati with 23 of the 27 points required for LPGA Tour Hall of Fame induction. She is four points away. The tournament begins Thursday, May 14 — and a victory would push her to 24 of 27.

A 2026 Season for the History Books

Korda has already won three LPGA tournaments in 2026 — the Tournament of Champions, the Chevron Championship, and the Riviera Maya LPGA Classic. She is the most dominant player on the tour, has been for several seasons, and at 27 years old is already approaching the career benchmarks that define the LPGA's greatest players.

Each win on the LPGA Tour earns one Hall of Fame point, with major championships and Players Championships earning additional points. With three wins already this season and 23 cumulative points, Korda is squarely in the conversation to achieve Hall of Fame eligibility sooner rather than later. The four-point gap is not insurmountable this season — it could fall as quickly as four more tournament wins.

The Competition in Cincinnati

The Queen City Championship won't be won easily. Jeeno Thitikul enters after winning the Mizuho Americas Open just last weekend, her second LPGA victory of the 2026 season, and will bring significant momentum to Maketewah Country Club. Other top players in the field make this a legitimate test of Korda's form heading into the summer stretch of the LPGA calendar.

For Korda, however, every major event is an opportunity to accelerate the Hall of Fame countdown. She has the game, the consistency, and the competitive record to achieve the milestone. The only question is timing.

What's Next

The Kroger Queen City Championship runs Thursday through Sunday, May 14-17 at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati with a $2 million purse. A Korda win keeps the Hall of Fame conversation going all summer. Coming events on the LPGA calendar include the ShopRite LPGA powered by Wakefern, scheduled for May 29-31 in New Jersey. Each tournament is a chance to close the gap to history.
