Can Jordan Spieth Finally Complete the Career Grand Slam at the PGA Championship?

By Marcus Cole

May 13, 2026

Jordan Spieth is 32 years old, three-quarters of the way to the rarest achievement in golf, and standing on the doorstep of history once again. The 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club is his latest opportunity to complete the career Grand Slam — and he arrived in Pennsylvania this week with a specific message for anyone paying attention.

"I feel closer than ever," Spieth said at Aronimink during practice round week.

What He's Already Done

The case for Spieth's place in golf history was made before his 24th birthday. He won the 2015 Masters, followed immediately by the 2015 US Open, establishing himself as the most dominant young golfer since Tiger Woods. Two years later, in 2017 at Royal Birkdale, he added the Claret Jug in one of the most memorable Open Championship finishes in modern memory.

Three majors. Three different titles. All that remains is the Wanamaker Trophy — and the PGA Championship has been the one that keeps slipping away. His best finish in the event remains a tie for third at the 2019 PGA Championship at Bethpage Black, where Brooks Koepka's sheer power made the rest of the field feel irrelevant. In the events since, Spieth's name has rarely been in the mix on Sunday.

Why Aronimink Is Different

Course character matters enormously for a player of Spieth's style. The PGA Championship has, in recent history, often been held on power golf venues where the longest hitters have a significant advantage. Aronimink is different. The course demands iron precision and exceptional putting on its large, undulating greens — skills that align better with Spieth's toolkit than the raw-distance demands of, say, Bethpage or Oak Hill.

Completing the Grand Slam would make Spieth just the seventh player in the modern era to hold all four major championships. He would join Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Gene Sarazen, and Rory McIlroy in that select group. McIlroy, notably, needed many attempts of his own before finally completing his Slam. Sometimes these things take time, and that precedent provides at least some comfort for Spieth's many supporters.

What's Next

Round 1 begins Thursday at Aronimink. If Spieth is to complete the Grand Slam, it will require four rounds of golf at the level he has always been capable of, on a course that at least theoretically suits his strengths. He has been closer than the scorecards sometimes suggest. And at 32, with a career that has already delivered three majors and a legacy that is secure regardless of what happens this week, he has the freedom to attack. That could make all the difference.
