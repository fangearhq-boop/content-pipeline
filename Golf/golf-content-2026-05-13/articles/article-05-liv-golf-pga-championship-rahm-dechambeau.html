LIV Golf's Aronimink Contingent: Rahm and DeChambeau Are Hungry for a Major Moment

By Jake Torres

May 13, 2026

The PGA Championship at Aronimink will feature 11 LIV Golf players in its 156-player field — a significant presence that brings with it significant stakes, particularly for the two most prominent names in that group.

Jon Rahm and Bryson DeChambeau enter the week motivated by frustration. The Masters last month was a disappointing week for both: Rahm barely made the cut and finished far down the leaderboard, while DeChambeau missed the cut entirely. For players of their stature, the PGA Championship is a prime opportunity to remind the golf world what they're capable of.

Rahm: The LIV Season Leader

Rahm leads the LIV Golf season standings entering this week, having posted strong results throughout the alternate-tour season. He is listed at +1500 in the betting market for the PGA Championship — a reflection of his talent if not his recent major form. At Aronimink, the course's iron-play requirements suit a player of Rahm's complete ball-striking ability, and he has shown in his career that he is capable of winning the biggest events on his best day.

The knock on Rahm's recent major performances is that he hasn't found the sustained focus over four rounds that major championships require, particularly following the high-pressure environment of the LIV schedule. A week like this, at a historic venue, with everything on the line, is exactly the kind of stage where players like Rahm traditionally rise.

DeChambeau: Back and Hungry

Bryson DeChambeau was cleared from a wrist injury that had sidelined him earlier in 2026, and entered the Masters as a potential contender before the week went sideways. At Aronimink, he will again try to translate his rebuilt game into a major performance. DeChambeau has won the US Open (2020) and has proven he can compete under the most pressure the sport offers — the wrist recovery has added motivation to perform at a level that validates his return to full health.

The full LIV contingent at Aronimink also includes Dustin Johnson, Cameron Smith, Tyrrell Hatton, Joaquin Niemann, Tom McKibbin, Thomas Detry, Martin Kaymer, David Puig, and Elvis Smylie. They qualified through various pathways including world ranking, past major champion exemptions, and other criteria.

What's Next

Round 1 begins Thursday at Aronimink. For the LIV contingent, a strong major performance does more than just add to their individual records — it strengthens the argument that their players remain competitive at golf's highest level. Rahm and DeChambeau are the most likely candidates to contend on Sunday. If either one is standing near the top of the leaderboard in the final round, it will be one of the week's great storylines.
