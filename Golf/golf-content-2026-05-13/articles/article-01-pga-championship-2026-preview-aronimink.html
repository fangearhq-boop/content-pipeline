PGA Championship Preview: Aronimink Hosts Its First Major in 64 Years — Here's What to Watch

By Ryan Calloway

May 13, 2026

The last time the PGA Championship came to Aronimink Golf Club, Gary Player was in his prime, the Beatles hadn't yet released their first album, and nobody had heard of Tiger Woods, Jack Nicklaus's career-best years, or the modern era of golf as we know it. That was 1962. Thursday, for the first time in 64 years, the Wanamaker Trophy is back at Aronimink.

The 108th PGA Championship begins Round 1 on Thursday, May 14 at the historic course in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania, just outside Philadelphia. And the field is as compelling as the venue.

The Course Rewards Precision

Aronimink is not a course that rewards sheer distance above all else. The club's massive, undulating greens demand exceptional distance control with the irons, and positioning off the tee matters more than raw power. That description suits Scottie Scheffler — the defending champion and +480 favorite — as well as any player in the world right now. His precision ball-striking and elite putting on difficult greens has made him the dominant player in the field on paper.

Rory McIlroy is listed at +850 and arrives as a Grand Slam holder looking to add another championship to a legacy already secured. Cameron Young at +1200 is arguably the most interesting value play — the world No. 3 has been in career-best form with two wins already this season, and Aronimink's demands suit his approach game as well.

The Storylines Are Stacked

Even with the course analysis out of the way, the human storylines make this week irresistible. Scheffler is seeking to end a run of three consecutive runner-up finishes and become just the third player in the sport's history to win back-to-back PGA Championships in stroke play. Jordan Spieth, 32, needs this victory to complete the career Grand Slam. Eleven LIV Golf players are in the field and at least two of them — Rahm and Bryson DeChambeau — are hungry for a major bounce-back after disappointing Masters results.

Coverage begins Thursday on ESPN. There will be no shortage of reasons to watch.

What's Next

Round 1 tees off Thursday, May 14 with Round 4 concluding Sunday, May 17. The Wanamaker Trophy will be awarded on Sunday afternoon in what shapes up to be one of the most compelling major championship weeks in recent memory. Whether Scheffler's dominance continues, Spieth finally gets his Slam, or a new name emerges at Aronimink, the next four days are essential golf viewing.
