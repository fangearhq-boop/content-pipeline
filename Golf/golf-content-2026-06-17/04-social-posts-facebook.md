### STORY 1: US Open Starts Tomorrow

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 8:00 AM ET

Tomorrow morning, the most anticipated week in golf begins.

The 126th US Open Championship tees off Thursday at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York — one of the sport's most demanding courses, on one of the most beautiful stretches of land in American golf.

And the story this week is historic.

Scottie Scheffler arrives as the No. 1 player in the world and the +550 favorite, needing just one more major to complete the career Grand Slam. He has won the Masters twice (2022 and 2024), the PGA Championship, and The Open Championship. The US Open is the only piece missing.

Round 1 featured groupings:
• 8:14 AM ET: Scheffler, defending champion J.J. Spaun, and 2025 US Amateur winner Mason Howell
• 7:52 AM ET: Rory McIlroy (2026 Masters champion), Ludvig Aberg, and Tommy Fleetwood
• 7:30 AM ET: Brooks Koepka (won the 2018 US Open at this exact course), Cameron Young, and Chris Gotterup
• 1:25 PM ET: Bryson DeChambeau, Viktor Hovland, and Matt Fitzpatrick
• 2:09 PM ET: Jordan Spieth, Justin Rose, and Jon Rahm — three past US Open champions

Note on Shinnecock: when the course last hosted in 2018, no player finished under par for the week. Brooks Koepka won at +1. Expect brutal scoring conditions again.

Set your alarms for Thursday morning. History is on the line.

### STORY 2: LIV Golf Crisis

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 10:30 AM ET

One of the most significant stories in golf right now has nothing to do with Shinnecock Hills — and it affects dozens of players.

LIV Golf is in a financial crisis that threatens the viability of the tour.

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund, which has been the primary backer of LIV Golf since its launch, has confirmed it will end direct funding of the league after the 2026 season. That is the long-term picture. The immediate picture is even more urgent: of the approximately $600 million the PIF committed to fund the 2026 campaign, only around $200 million has arrived. That leaves approximately $400 million outstanding — money that is needed to cover prize purses, player contracts, and operating costs for four remaining events this year.

What does that mean on the ground? LIV has already postponed its New Orleans event. Four events remain on the 2026 schedule: LIV UK (July 23-26) and three US-based events in August. According to reports citing LIV officials, "every remaining tournament is on the fence."

The league is not standing still. LIV has established an independent board, hired Ducera Partners as a US investment banking advisor, and reportedly approached hundreds of prospective investors. Two independent board directors have already been named. The search for new ownership or investment is active and urgent.

For players like Jon Rahm, Bryson DeChambeau, and others who signed long-term contracts with LIV, the uncertainty about contract payments is real. If the remaining $400 million does not arrive and no new investor steps up, the implications for the sport are significant.

### STORY 3: Scheffler Grand Slam — Birthday + Father's Day

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 1:00 PM ET

If the golf universe has a sense of drama, it revealed itself in this scheduling detail.

The final round of the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills is Sunday, June 21. If Scottie Scheffler wins the US Open, he will become the seventh player in history to complete the career Grand Slam. And that crowning moment would happen on June 21, 2026 — which is simultaneously Scheffler's 30th birthday AND Father's Day.

Think about the script being written. Scheffler is a new father. His birthday coincides with Father's Day this year. And the only major he has ever needed to complete the most exclusive achievement in individual golf falls on that same Sunday.

Only six men in the history of the game have won all four majors: Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, and — most recently — Rory McIlroy, who completed his Grand Slam at Augusta in April. Scheffler would be the seventh.

There is another layer. Scheffler would be just the second player to complete the career Grand Slam on his first attempt. Tiger Woods did it in 2000 when he won the US Open at Pebble Beach for his third consecutive major. Every other player in the history of the Grand Slam took multiple US Open appearances to close it out — McIlroy needed 11 tries at the Masters before winning in 2026.

Scheffler, characteristically, plays down the magnitude. He says the Grand Slam has never been a motivating factor and that he will not view this week as a failure if he does not win. That is both genuine and irrelevant — the rest of us understand what is at stake.

### STORY 4: McIlroy at Shinnecock

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 3:30 PM ET

Rory McIlroy completed his career Grand Slam in April at Augusta. Now he is at Shinnecock Hills trying to win a second major in 2026.

McIlroy won the 2026 Masters to become the sixth player in history to hold all four major championships. The career Grand Slam had haunted him for years — he finished runner-up at Augusta three times since 2022 before finally breaking through. When he did, the moment was one of the most emotional in recent major championship history.

He arrives at Shinnecock Hills with different energy. He won here before — back in 2011, when he fired a record-tying 16-under par 268 at Congressional Country Club, winning by eight strokes in one of the most dominant US Open performances of the modern era. That was McIlroy before the world knew just how great he would become. This is McIlroy at 37, with the Grand Slam in hand, hunting one more.

His odds of +1200 reflect the reality that Scheffler is in a tier of his own right now — but McIlroy has won at this level before and knows how to perform when the sport's attention is focused on him.

He tees off Thursday at 7:52 AM ET alongside Ludvig Aberg and Tommy Fleetwood. If his approach play is sharp on Shinnecock's firm, fast greens, he will be near the top of the leaderboard when Thursday's scores come in.

### STORY 5: Meijer LPGA Classic

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 6:00 PM ET

The men's Grand Slam drama at Shinnecock Hills is getting all the attention this week — but the LPGA Tour is also launching a tournament on Thursday, and it is worth your attention.

The Meijer LPGA Classic presented by Simply Give opens June 18 at Blythefield Country Club in Belmont, Michigan, running through Sunday, June 21. The field of 144 players competes for a $3 million purse at Blythefield, a course that sits along the banks of the Rogue River with rolling terrain in the Grand River valley.

One of the event's best traditions returns for 2026: on Father's Day Sunday (June 21), fathers receive free general admission to the course. General admission is $10 per day for other spectators; children 17 and under attend free with a paying adult. Bike parking, expanded viewing areas, and a Discovery Land for kids are among this year's venue additions.

The Meijer LPGA Classic feeds directly into the LPGA major season — the KPMG Women's PGA Championship follows the next week, June 25-28 at Hazeltine National in Chaska, Minnesota. Strong performers at Blythefield will carry confidence into the season's biggest remaining event.
