LIV Golf in Crisis — Saudi PIF Ending Funding After 2026, Four Events at Risk
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 10:30 AM ET One of the most significant stories in golf right now has nothing to do with Shinnecock Hills — and it affects dozens of players. LIV Golf is in a financial crisis that threatens the viability of the tour. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund, which has been the primary backer of LIV Golf since its launch, has confirmed it will end direct funding of the league after the 2026 season. That is the long-term picture. The immediate picture is even more urgent: of the approximately $600 million the PIF committed to fund the 2026 campaign, only around $200 million has arrived. That leaves approximately $400 million outstanding — money that is needed to cover prize purses, player contracts, and operating costs for four remaining events this year. What does that mean on the ground? LIV has already postponed its New Orleans event. Four events remain on the 2026 schedule: LIV UK (July 23-26) and three US-based events in August. According to reports citing LIV officials, "every remaining tournament is on the fence." The league is not standing still. LIV has established an independent board, hired Ducera Partners as a US investment banking advisor, and reportedly approached hundreds of prospective investors. Two independent board directors have already been named. The search for new ownership or investment is active and urgent. For players like Jon Rahm, Bryson DeChambeau, and others who signed long-term contracts with LIV, the uncertainty about contract payments is real. If the remaining $400 million does not arrive and no new investor steps up, the implications for the sport are significant. ### Image Post Caption Saudi PIF is ending LIV Golf funding after 2026. ### Long-Form Post Window: 1:00 PM ET If the golf universe has a sense of drama, it revealed itself in this scheduling detail. The final round of the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills is Sunday, June 21. If Scottie Scheffler wins the US Open, he will become the seventh player in history to complete the career Grand Slam. And that crowning moment would happen on June 21, 2026 — which is simultaneously Scheffler's 30th birthday AND Father's Day. Think about the script being written. Scheffler is a new father. His birthday coincides with Father's Day this year. And the only major he has ever needed to complete the most exclusive achievement in individual golf falls on that same Sunday. Only six men in the history of the game have won all four majors: Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, and — most recently — Rory McIlroy, who completed his Grand Slam at Augusta in April. Scheffler would be the seventh. There is another layer. Scheffler would be just the second player to complete the career Grand Slam on his first attempt. Tiger Woods did it in 2000 when he won the US Open at Pebble Beach for his third consecutive major. Every other player in the history of the Grand Slam took multiple US Open appearances to close it out — McIlroy needed 11 tries at the Masters before winning in 2026. Scheffler, characteristically, plays down the magnitude. He says the Grand Slam has never been a motivating factor and that he will not view this week as a failure if he does not win. That is both genuine and irrelevant — the rest of us understand what is at stake. ### Image Post Caption June 21. Scheffler. US Open final round. His 30th birthday. Father's Day. ### Long-Form Post Window: 6:00 PM ET The men's Grand Slam drama at Shinnecock Hills is getting all the attention this week — but the LPGA Tour is also launching a tournament on Thursday, and it is worth your attention. The Meijer LPGA Classic presented by Simply Give opens June 18 at Blythefield Country Club in Belmont, Michigan, running through Sunday, June 21. The field of 144 players competes for a $3 million purse at Blythefield, a course that sits along the banks of the Rogue River with rolling terrain in the Grand River valley. One of the event's best traditions returns for 2026: on Father's Day Sunday (June 21), fathers receive free general admission to the course.
