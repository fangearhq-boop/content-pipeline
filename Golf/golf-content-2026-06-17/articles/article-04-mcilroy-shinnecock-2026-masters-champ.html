McIlroy at Shinnecock: Masters Champion Eyes Second 2026 Major at +1200

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 17, 2026

Rory McIlroy completed his career Grand Slam in April at Augusta National, ending a pursuit that had defined his career for over a decade. Two months later, he is at Shinnecock Hills aiming for something that has not been done since Tiger Woods: winning two major championships in the same calendar year.

McIlroy enters the 2026 US Open at odds of +1200 — the second-shortest in the field behind Scottie Scheffler's +550. The gap reflects the reality that Scheffler has been the dominant player in the world over the past three seasons, but it does not capture the quality McIlroy has shown in 2026 or the gravity of what he would accomplish with a win here.

His History at This Event

McIlroy has won the US Open once, but that once was historic. In 2011 at Congressional Country Club, he fired a record-tying 16-under par 268, winning by eight strokes in what many considered one of the most controlled performances in major championship history. He was 22 years old, and the victory announced to the world that he would be one of the defining players of his generation.

Since 2022, he has finished runner-up at the US Open three times — close enough to win, but unable to close. The pattern has been a source of frustration, but it also demonstrates that the courses and conditions of the US Open suit his game.

Shinnecock Hills — different from Congressional in geography and character but equally punishing — should present opportunities for the elite ball-strikers. McIlroy's ability to hit fairways under pressure and generate spin on firm greens are strengths well-suited to the venue.

The Historical Context

The last player to win multiple major championships in a single calendar year was Tiger Woods, who completed the Tiger Slam in 2000-2001 by winning four consecutive majors across two calendar years. In any single calendar year since 2000, no player has won two majors. McIlroy winning the Masters and the US Open in 2026 would end that 26-year drought.

His group Thursday morning at 7:52 AM ET — alongside Ludvig Aberg and Tommy Fleetwood — is one of the most entertaining pairings in the field. Aberg has been one of the most exciting young players on tour, and Fleetwood, a Shinnecock resident who has been preparing on the course for weeks, should be sharp from the first tee.

What's Next

McIlroy's most telling indication of his form will come on Thursday afternoon when the first-round scores post. If he is within three shots of the leaders after 18 holes at Shinnecock, where consistency over 72 holes is more important than any single spectacular round, he will be a genuine threat through Sunday. Watch the greens in regulation numbers — at a firm, fast US Open course, the player who hits the most approach shots to manageable distances will separate from the field by the weekend.
