Meijer LPGA Classic 2026 Starts Thursday at Blythefield — 144 Players, Free Admission Father's Day

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 17, 2026

The men's US Open at Shinnecock Hills will command most of the golf world's attention this week, but Thursday also marks the opening of a major LPGA Tour event in Michigan — and the Meijer LPGA Classic presented by Simply Give is worth following for fans who want a complete picture of what is happening in professional golf this week.

Tournament Details

The 2026 Meijer LPGA Classic runs June 18-21 at Blythefield Country Club in Belmont, Michigan, just outside Grand Rapids. The course sits along the banks of the Rogue River, with rolling terrain in the Grand River valley that rewards precision and demands thoughtful course management.

The 72-hole stroke play competition features 144 players competing for a $3 million purse. Thursday's opening round runs concurrently with the US Open's first round, meaning fans watching live golf on Thursday will have two elite fields in action at the same time.

Father's Day at the Course

One of the event's best-known traditions returns in 2026: fathers receive free general admission to the course on Sunday, June 21 — Father's Day. The coincidence of Father's Day falling on the final round makes Sunday at Blythefield one of the most spectator-friendly major-adjacent events on the LPGA calendar.

General admission tickets are $10 per day for all other rounds, and children 17 and under attend free with a ticketed adult. New this year: expanded bike parking, additional viewing areas, and a Discovery Land section for younger spectators.

What Comes Next

The Meijer LPGA Classic leads directly into the most important event on the remaining LPGA calendar. The KPMG Women's PGA Championship — one of the five LPGA majors — follows the next week, June 25-28 at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota. Players who perform well at Blythefield will carry momentum into Hazeltine, making this week's results consequential beyond the tournament itself.

Hazeltine has hosted major championships before, including the 2016 Ryder Cup and multiple US Open and PGA Championship events. The KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine should provide a demanding test of the best players in women's golf.

What's Next

Round 1 tee times at Blythefield begin Thursday morning. Leaderboard coverage will be available through the LPGA Tour app and website. For golf fans able to attend, the Grand Rapids area is easily accessible for a day trip during the mid-week rounds. The Father's Day Sunday final round is the best single day to attend if you can only make one trip to Blythefield this week.
