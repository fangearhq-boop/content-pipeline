LIV Golf Financial Crisis: Saudi PIF Ends Funding After 2026, Four Events at Risk

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 17, 2026

LIV Golf is fighting for survival — both this season and beyond.

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund has confirmed it will end direct funding of the rebel golf league after the conclusion of the 2026 season. That announcement removes the financial foundation that has sustained LIV Golf since its launch. But the immediate threat is even more urgent: the PIF has provided only approximately $200 million of the $600 million it committed for the current season, leaving roughly $400 million outstanding as four events remain on the 2026 schedule.

What Is at Risk

LIV's remaining 2026 events are: LIV Golf UK (July 23-26) and three US-based events in August. According to people familiar with the situation, "every remaining tournament is on the fence." The league has already postponed its scheduled event in New Orleans — a tangible sign that the financial constraints are affecting operations now, not in some hypothetical future.

Player prize money, operating costs, and the significant contract payments many LIV players signed when they left the PGA Tour are all contingent on the remaining PIF funding arriving. For players like Jon Rahm, Bryson DeChambeau, and other prominent signatories, the uncertainty about their guaranteed money is a real concern.

LIV's Response

The league is not passively waiting for the PIF. LIV Golf has established an independent board of directors, hiring two independent directors and beginning the process of separating its governance from direct Saudi government control. On the financial side, LIV engaged Ducera Partners — a US-based investment banking advisory firm — and reportedly approached hundreds of prospective investors in search of new backing.

The challenge for potential investors is clarity. The Financial Times reported that prospective backers are seeking answers about what LIV Golf will look like beyond 2026 and which players will remain committed to the league. Without those answers, many are hesitant to make large commitments.

The PGA Tour's Position

If LIV Golf restructures significantly or fails to complete the 2026 season, the question of PGA Tour reinstatement for its players will return. CBS Sports reporting on the end of PIF funding explicitly asked whether the PGA Tour would welcome players back. Tour commissioner Jay Monahan has been publicly measured on this question, neither encouraging nor discouraging the possibility. The dynamics of a potential rapprochement — with players who left for eight-figure LIV contracts — would be complicated.

What's Next

Watch for whether the LIV Golf UK event in late July proceeds as scheduled. That is effectively the next test of whether the PIF funding resumes, a new investor steps up, or the crisis deepens. If LIV UK proceeds without incident, it suggests the league has found at least a short-term bridge. If it is postponed or cancelled, the implications for the rest of the 2026 calendar — and for players' contracts — become significantly more serious.
