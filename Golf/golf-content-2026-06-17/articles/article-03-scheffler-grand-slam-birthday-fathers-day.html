Scheffler's Grand Slam Story: 30th Birthday, Father's Day, and a Place in Golf History

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 17, 2026

Golf has always had a gift for circumstance, but the narrative being assembled around Scottie Scheffler at the 2026 US Open borders on the implausible.

Consider: if Scheffler wins the US Open, he completes the career Grand Slam — becoming only the seventh player in the history of the game to win all four major championships. He would do it on June 21. Which is his 30th birthday. Which is also Father's Day. And Scheffler became a father recently.

If a screenwriter submitted that script, a producer would send it back for revision. It is too perfect.

The Company He Would Join

The career Grand Slam is the most exclusive achievement in individual golf. Only six men have accomplished it in the sport's entire history: Gene Sarazen (completed 1935), Ben Hogan (completed 1953), Gary Player (completed 1965), Jack Nicklaus (completed 1966), Tiger Woods (completed 2000), and Rory McIlroy (completed 2026 at Augusta).

Scheffler would be the seventh. He would also be the second player in history to complete the Grand Slam on his first attempt. Tiger Woods accomplished that feat at the 2000 US Open at Pebble Beach, winning his third consecutive major in dominant fashion. McIlroy, by contrast, needed 11 appearances at the Masters before he finally won Augusta National — a pursuit that became one of the defining sagas in modern golf.

Scheffler's major record to date: Masters 2022, Masters 2024, PGA Championship 2025, The Open Championship 2025. At 29 years old, he has won four majors — more than any active player in the game. The US Open is the one gap in a career that already qualifies as generational.

Scheffler's Mindset

True to form, Scheffler has deflected the Grand Slam narrative all week. He told reporters he has never been motivated by the pursuit of the career Grand Slam and that he would not view this week as a failure if he does not win. That measured perspective is both genuine and consistent — Scheffler's defining psychological trait has been his ability to compete without allowing external narratives to alter his focus.

He has also been candid about his own standards. He noted ahead of the championship that he had top-three finishes in half of his 12 worldwide starts in 2026, including runner-up to McIlroy at the Masters in April. He has been playing at an extremely high level, even if the US Open has not yet landed in his major collection.

What's Next

Scheffler tees off at 8:14 AM ET on Thursday in a featured group with defending champion J.J. Spaun and 2025 US Amateur champion Mason Howell. Sunday's final round at Shinnecock Hills — with Scheffler in contention, as is fully expected — will be watched by an audience tuning in for something that has never happened on a birthday and Father's Day at the same time in golf history.
