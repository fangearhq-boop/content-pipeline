US Open 2026 Preview: Scheffler Chases Grand Slam at Shinnecock Hills Starting Thursday

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 17, 2026

The 126th US Open Championship begins Thursday at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York — and the sporting world will be watching for reasons that go well beyond a trophy.

Scottie Scheffler, the world's No. 1 player, arrives needing one win to complete the career Grand Slam. He has won the Masters twice (2022 and 2024), the PGA Championship, and The Open Championship. The US Open is the only major that has eluded him. This week, at one of American golf's most storied clubs, he gets his first crack at joining the most exclusive club in the sport.

Why Shinnecock Is Different

Shinnecock Hills is not merely a championship venue — it is a trial. The course plays along the wind-swept terrain of Southampton's eastern hills, with fairways that bake firm and fast under summer conditions, greens that can become nearly unplayable if the USGA pushes its setup too aggressively, and rough thick enough to demand precision with every approach.

The last time Shinnecock hosted the US Open was 2018. In that championship, no player in the field of 156 finished the week under par. Brooks Koepka won at one over par — and it was considered a clinical performance under those conditions. Expect the USGA to set up the course similarly in 2026: narrow, fast, and unforgiving.

For Scheffler, that setup should suit his game. He is perhaps the most complete ball-striker in the world at the moment — accurate off the tee, creative with his approach play, and calm enough to grind through 72 holes on a punishing course. He enters at +550 odds, a clear favorite over a competitive field.

Round 1 Featured Groups

Scheffler tees off at 8:14 AM ET on Thursday alongside defending champion J.J. Spaun — who won the 2025 US Open at Oakmont with a 64-foot birdie putt on the 72nd hole — and 2025 US Amateur champion Mason Howell. Rory McIlroy, the 2026 Masters champion, plays alongside Ludvig Aberg and Tommy Fleetwood at 7:52 AM ET. Brooks Koepka, who won at Shinnecock in 2018, is grouped with Cameron Young and Chris Gotterup at 7:30 AM ET.

The afternoon wave brings compelling names as well: Bryson DeChambeau, Viktor Hovland, and Matt Fitzpatrick tee off at 1:25 PM ET, while Jordan Spieth, Justin Rose, and Jon Rahm — three past US Open champions — go out together at 2:09 PM ET.

The Historical Context

Only six men have ever completed the career Grand Slam — winning all four of golf's major championships. Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, and Rory McIlroy make up that exclusive group. Scheffler would become the seventh if he wins on Sunday.

He has handled the magnitude of the moment the way he handles most things: quietly and practically. He said in the week leading up to the championship that completing the Grand Slam has never been a motivating factor in his preparation and that he would not view the week as a failure if he did not win. That measured perspective has served him well through four major victories already. It is unlikely to change now.

What's Next

Round 1 begins Thursday morning with the first groups off at approximately 6:35 AM ET. Television coverage shifts throughout the day across major networks. Round 2 on Friday sets the cut, with the weekend rounds Saturday and Sunday determining the champion. If Scheffler is in contention come Sunday afternoon, the entire golf world will be watching what happens on June 21 at Shinnecock Hills.
