Scheffler's Three-Peat Quest Begins Today — Round 1 at the Memorial Tees Off This Morning

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 4, 2026

The last player to win the same PGA Tour event three consecutive times was Steve Stricker at the John Deere Classic in 2011. This week at Muirfield Village Golf Club in Dublin, Ohio, Scottie Scheffler tees off as the +310 favorite to do it again.

Scheffler won the Memorial Tournament in 2024. He won it again in 2025. Now Round 1 of the 2026 edition is underway, with the world's No. 1 player back at Jack Nicklaus' course chasing a piece of history that has not happened in fifteen years.

It would be remarkable if Scheffler were only the Memorial's dominant player. But his hold on Muirfield Village goes deeper than two wins. He finished third in each of the two years before his victories — four consecutive top-three finishes at the same demanding tournament. No player has established a tighter grip on any single event in recent years.

Why Scheffler Wins at Muirfield Village

Muirfield Village is a Jack Nicklaus design that rewards the most complete players in the game. The rough is penal, the bentgrass greens are small and undulating, and the premium is on precision from tee to green. Missing fairways at Muirfield Village creates scrambling situations that elite ball-strikers rarely face and average ball-strikers cannot survive.

Scheffler is the best ball-striker on the PGA Tour. His iron play, driving accuracy, and approach shot quality are the ideal profile for a course that punishes deviation from the fairway and green center. He also putts well on the bent-grass surface that Muirfield Village presents.

The consistency that makes him the No. 1 player in the world is amplified at a course that demands everything simultaneously.

The Challengers

Rory McIlroy is in the field and is among the most talked-about players at the Memorial for reasons that go beyond his ranking. McIlroy has never won at Muirfield Village. His best finish in 13 tries is a T4. On his way to Dublin this week, McIlroy made a detour to Shinnecock Hills to preview the US Open venue — suggesting that his focus is already divided between this week and the major championship two weeks away.

Ludvig Aberg enters at +1500 with genuine momentum. Six top-10 finishes in his last nine starts, including a T5 at this tournament in 2024, give him credibility as a contender. His questions are about closing rounds, not overall quality.

Cameron Young (+1600), Xander Schauffele, Jordan Spieth, and Matt Fitzpatrick round out a field that includes 47 of the top 50 FedEx Cup point earners. The 2023 champion Viktor Hovland withdrew before the tournament began, removing one of the primary threats to Scheffler's bid.

The Muirfield Village Test

The Memorial consistently produces some of the most complete final leaderboards in golf. Players who lead at the Memorial are winning with their iron play and their patience — not hot putting streaks or low-scoring par-fives. The greens punish aggressive pin-seeking, and the rough punishes drivers who sacrifice accuracy for distance.

Scheffler has solved the equation. The question this week is whether he solves it three times in a row — a feat that would place him in a conversation that currently belongs entirely to Steve Stricker and a handful of historical anomalies from decades past.

What's Next

Round 1 scores will tell the opening chapter of this week's story. Round 2 follows Friday with the 36-hole cut to the top 50 players and those within 10 strokes of the lead. Final rounds Saturday and Sunday with the winner receiving the full ceremony that the Memorial Tournament's tradition demands.

The tournament runs June 4-7. Coverage is available on Golf Channel and NBC throughout the week. Scheffler has earned the favorite's label two years running and then confirmed it by winning. The course knows him. The question is whether the course cooperates when history is on the line.
