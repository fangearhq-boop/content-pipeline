Scheffler Has the PGA and The Open. He Needs Shinnecock. Both He and McIlroy Already Scouted It.

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 4, 2026

On their way to the Memorial Tournament in Dublin, Ohio, Scottie Scheffler and Rory McIlroy made a detour. Both players stopped at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York — the venue for the 2026 US Open from June 18-21 — to walk the course and begin building their understanding of what the championship will require.

The level of preparation is not unusual for elite players. The motivation behind it, however, is anything but standard.

Scheffler's Grand Slam Opportunity

Scottie Scheffler won the PGA Championship in 2025. He won The Open Championship in 2025. Three of golf's four major championships now have his name on them — the Masters, the PGA Championship, and The Open. The only one that does not is the US Open.

The 126th US Open at Shinnecock Hills represents Scheffler's opportunity to complete the career Grand Slam — a feat that only five players in history have accomplished. Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, and Tiger Woods are the five. Every other player who has ever won three majors has fallen short of the fourth.

The final round of the 2026 US Open falls on June 21. That is Scheffler's 30th birthday. The detail feels scripted — as if the sport itself arranged the calendar to make the moment feel appropriately outsized.

Scheffler's Shinnecock visit this week is part of his preparation. He walked the course, assessed the ground conditions, and formed opinions about where scoring opportunities exist and where the USGA will make the venue nearly impossible. Both elements of that assessment — opportunity and danger — will be critical to a US Open performance.

What McIlroy Is Looking For

Rory McIlroy's detour to Shinnecock carries different context. McIlroy has not won a major in recent years, though his back-to-back Masters victories represent a return to the peak of the game. The US Open is a championship he has won before — in 2011 at Congressional — and one he has come close to winning several additional times.

Shinnecock Hills is the sixth major championship venue the US Open has used at the site. The course's links-style character — firm, fast, wind-affected — suits a player who grew up on similar conditions in Northern Ireland. McIlroy is one of the few players in the field whose background gives him a genuine advantage on a course that plays like it belongs in the British Isles rather than Long Island.

Both assessments reported after the Shinnecock visit are available at Golf Channel's report from this week. The players offered impressions of the course's current state and their thoughts on how the USGA will set it up for championship play.

About Shinnecock Hills

Shinnecock Hills is one of the original members of the United States Golf Association, founded in 1891. The course has hosted the US Open five previous times — in 1896, 1986, 1995, 2004, and 2018. The 2018 edition was particularly notorious for conditions that briefly became unplayable due to USGA setup decisions before corrections were made in the final round.

The defending champion is J.J. Spaun, who won the 2025 US Open at Oakmont with a dramatic 64-foot birdie putt on the 72nd hole. Spaun returns to defend in a field of 156 players that will be finalized after Golf's Longest Day final qualifying on June 8.

What's Next

This week, Scheffler and McIlroy are focused on the Memorial Tournament at Muirfield Village. But the Shinnecock visit signals that the US Open is already shaping both players' thinking for what follows.

The championship begins June 18. Practice rounds are June 15-17. The full field plays Thursday and Friday before the 36-hole cut. Saturday and Sunday complete the championship, with the final round on June 21 — Scheffler's 30th birthday and, potentially, the day he completes the career Grand Slam.

Two weeks from now, everything changes. This week, Scheffler has a three-peat to pursue at Muirfield Village. But Shinnecock is already on his mind, and the walk he took this week confirms he intends to be fully prepared when June 18 arrives.
