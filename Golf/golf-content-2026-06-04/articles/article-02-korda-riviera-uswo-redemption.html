Nelly Korda at Riviera — Hungry for Redemption, Favored to Win Her First US Women's Open

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 4, 2026

Nelly Korda has three major championships. She has won the Chevron Championship twice. She has established herself as the No. 1 player in the world through sustained excellence that makes her peers the clear second-best tier in women's golf.

And she has never won the US Women's Open.

That gap in her major résumé gets its most compelling opportunity to close this week at Riviera Country Club in Los Angeles, where Round 1 of the 81st US Women's Open is underway. Korda enters as the +390 favorite at a venue that has never before hosted the championship — the first USWO ever played at the iconic club that the men's PGA Tour has visited for the Genesis Invitational for decades.

The Erin Hills Wound

Last year's US Women's Open at Erin Hills was the moment Korda came closest to completing the set. She was in contention throughout the final round before Maja Stark stepped forward and took the title. Korda finished second — close enough to feel the trophy, not close enough to hold it.

That defeat, and the months of preparation that followed, are described by Korda's camp as central motivation for what she has done since. In 2026, Korda won the Chevron Championship — her dominant performance at Augusta National Women's Amateur and Mission Hills gave her another major and another confirmation that she is the best player in the world. Through seven LPGA Tour starts this season, she has three wins and has finished outside the top two only a handful of times.

The hunger is not manufactured. The US Women's Open is the one major she has not won, and it is historically the most difficult to win — the tournament that has defied the world's best players at the most inconvenient times. Korda knows that. Her preparation for Riviera has been thorough.

Why Riviera Suits This Moment

Riviera Country Club is making its first appearance as a US Women's Open host, and the course has a particular reputation that players discussed during practice rounds this week. The layout rewards precise iron play and course management — qualities that define Korda's game at its best. The greens are some of the most distinctive in American golf: undulating, fast, and demanding of players who miss in the wrong spots.

Korda's game is built on hitting greens in regulation and making putts. She leads the LPGA in several key statistical categories that correlate with success at premium venues. The combination of venue type and player skill set could not be more aligned.

The Competition

Jeeno Thitikul is the primary challenger — the second-ranked player in the world has been consistently excellent throughout the season and has the game to contend at any major venue. Lottie Woad, who won the Kroger Queen City Championship earlier in 2026, arrives with momentum and has shown the ability to handle pressure events well above her ranking.

The field includes all the top players in women's golf. At Riviera, with fast greens and premium rough, the tournament will identify who truly has the total game to win a major championship under US Open conditions.

What's Next

Round 1 is underway at Riviera Country Club. The 36-hole cut determines the weekend field. Final rounds play out Saturday and Sunday, with the champion receiving the USWO trophy in a ceremony at a venue making its inaugural appearance in women's major golf history.

For Korda, each round this week carries the weight of the one she did not finish the way she needed to at Erin Hills. Riviera is a different course and a different week. But the motivation is the same: the US Women's Open is the major she has not yet won, and she arrived here with everything she needs to change that.
