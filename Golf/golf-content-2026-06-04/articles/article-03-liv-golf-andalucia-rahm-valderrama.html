LIV Golf Heads to Valderrama — Rahm's Home Stage, $20 Million on the Line Before the US Open

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 4, 2026

Jon Rahm is the No. 1 player in the LIV Golf individual standings. He leads with 779 points, the result of a consistent season in a format he has adapted to completely since joining the breakaway league. This week, at the course he knows best in the country where he grew up, he is the 16/5 favorite to win the LIV Golf Andalucia 2026.

There is one problem with that favorable position: Rahm has never won an individual LIV Golf event at Real Club Valderrama. Last year, he finished runner-up — close enough to make the miss feel personal. This week at Sotogrande, on the course that represents the pinnacle of Iberian golf, Rahm has an opportunity to write a different ending.

The Tournament

LIV Golf Andalucia 2026 runs June 4-7 at Real Club Valderrama, making this the fourth consecutive year the legendary course has hosted a LIV Golf event. The individual purse is $20 million, with the winner receiving $4 million. The team purse adds another $10 million, with the winning team's three players splitting $3 million.

The event is the ninth of the 2026 LIV Golf season, and it arrives under a format that now feels appropriate for the talent level involved: 72-hole stroke play, extended from the 54-hole format that defined the league's early years. The change gives the best players more time to express their full capability and creates a more direct comparison to PGA Tour events for those tracking performance across tours.

Coverage in the United States is on Fox Sports. The field includes virtually every major name in LIV Golf, with Bryson DeChambeau (second in the standings at 476.90 points) as the primary threat to Rahm's bid.

Why Valderrama Matters

Real Club Valderrama is to Spanish golf what Augusta National is to American golf — the most revered, most demanding, and most storied venue in the country. Its history includes the 1997 Ryder Cup, which Seve Ballesteros captained to one of the most dramatic European victories in the event's history. It has also hosted multiple World Golf Championships events and has consistently been rated among the best courses in continental Europe.

The course is known for its strategic demands. The greens are famously quick and sloped. The approach shots require precision that raw distance cannot replace. For Rahm — whose game has been built on a combination of power, iron excellence, and elite putting — Valderrama's profile is one he should theoretically dominate.

The fact that he has not won individually here despite that profile makes this week more interesting than the standings alone suggest.

The US Open Preparation Angle

This is also the last LIV Golf event before the US Open at Shinnecock Hills from June 18-21. For LIV Golf players who have qualified for the championship — Rahm among them — this week at Valderrama serves as the final competitive sharpening before the season's second major.

The timing creates an interesting dynamic. Players are simultaneously trying to win a significant event and managing their preparation workload with Shinnecock in mind. The 72-hole format helps — it is closer to a major's competitive demands than 54 holes would be and provides useful conditioning for what awaits two weeks later.

What's Next

Round 1 is underway at Valderrama today. Round 4 concludes Sunday, June 7. The winner receives $4 million and moves significantly up the LIV Golf individual standings heading into the summer stretch of the season.

Rahm's combination of course knowledge, current form, and competitive motivation makes him the logical pick. But golf, and Valderrama specifically, has a way of confounding logic. DeChambeau and the full LIV field will have their own answers to give at this remarkable course beginning today.
