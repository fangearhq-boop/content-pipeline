KPMG Women's PGA Preview: Can Nelly Korda Win Three Straight Majors at Hazeltine?

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 20, 2026

Nelly Korda won the US Women's Open three weeks ago. She is the World No. 1 ranked women's golfer. And in five days, she tees it up at the KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota, with a chance to do something extraordinary: win three consecutive major championships.

The tournament begins Thursday, June 25, and runs through Sunday, June 28. The purse is $12 million — one of the largest in women's golf. And Korda, 27 years old and at the peak of her career, is the clear headliner of a field that includes 12 past champions and every player in the world top 100.

Korda's Season in Context

The 2026 LPGA season has been a showcase for Korda's dominance. Her US Women's Open victory in early June was the kind of win that defines careers — a major championship captured at one of the sport's most iconic events, in a year when the game is watching her every move.

That win was potentially the second in a run, depending on how one counts: Korda's consecutive major momentum is one of the season's defining narratives. Winning the KPMG would make the 2026 season one of the best LPGA campaigns in the modern era.

There's also personal history here. Korda won the KPMG Women's PGA Championship in 2021 — though at Olympia Fields rather than Hazeltine. She knows what it takes to close out this event. The course is different, but the championship is the same.

The Course and Competition

Hazeltine National Golf Club is one of Minnesota's most celebrated courses and one of the most significant venues in American golf. The club hosted the 2016 Ryder Cup, the 2009 PGA Championship, and multiple US Opens across its history. It is a long, demanding course with significant rough and greens that reward precision over power.

The field entering next week is deep. Defending champion Minjee Lee will look to become a two-time KPMG champion. World No. 2 Atthaya Thitikul, Korda's most consistent rival this season, represents the most significant competitive threat. Hyo Joo Kim has finished second to Korda twice already this year — she has reason to believe she can finally get past her rival at Hazeltine.

The Bigger Picture

Women's golf is having a moment in 2026 — the talent level at the top of the LPGA is as deep as it has been in years, the major purses are historically significant, and the narrative of Korda's dominant season has drawn attention from beyond the traditional golf audience. A three-major run would accelerate all of that.

Korda is also one of the most compelling personalities in the game: articulate, competitive, and genuinely interested in making the LPGA more visible. A win at Hazeltine next week would accomplish that.

What's Next

The KPMG Women's PGA Championship begins Thursday, June 25 at Hazeltine National. Coverage will be available on Golf Channel and NBC Sports. The first two rounds will establish the leaderboard — and whether Korda has the game on a classic Minnesota layout.

Three straight majors is a once-in-a-generation achievement. Korda is five days away from her chance to start that conversation.
