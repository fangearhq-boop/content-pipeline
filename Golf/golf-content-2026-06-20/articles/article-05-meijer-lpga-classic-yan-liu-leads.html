Yan Liu Chases Her First LPGA Win at the Meijer LPGA Classic

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 20, 2026

There is no feeling in golf quite like the first win. The relief. The disbelief. The proof — after years of professional competition — that you can close a tournament out when the moment is biggest. This week at Blythefield Country Club in Michigan, Yan Liu is chasing exactly that.

After shooting a 6-under 66 in Round 2 of the Meijer LPGA Classic for Simply Give, Liu entered Saturday as the leader of an LPGA Tour event for one of the first significant times in her career. She is, by the records, still searching for her first tour win. The field behind her knows it. The pressure of that knowledge is one of the hardest things in professional golf to navigate.

The Tournament and the Venue

The Meijer LPGA Classic has been one of the LPGA Tour's most warmly received regular-season events since its inception — a fan-friendly tournament with strong Midwest community roots, strong attendance, and a reputation for producing exciting final-round leaderboards. The tournament runs June 18-21 at Blythefield Country Club, a classic course outside Grand Rapids, Michigan.

This week, with the US Open and the approaching KPMG Women's PGA Championship dominating golf's attention, the Meijer LPGA Classic is something of a quieter stage. For Liu, that might actually help.

What a Win Would Mean

Professional golfers spend years on tour before winning their first event. The LPGA has always been one of the most competitive women's tours in the world, and the gap between being a good player and being a winner is genuine. Liu has been a part of the tour circuit, has done the work, has competed week after week. A win at the Meijer LPGA Classic would validate all of it.

First-time winners tend to unlock something in themselves, too. The psychological weight of "not yet a winner" is something that players describe as real. Removing it often produces immediate results — players who win once tend to win again. If Liu can close out a 54-hole lead at Blythefield, the trajectory of her career changes.

The Saturday Challenge

The hardest moment in professional golf is not building a lead — it's holding one. After playing well enough to lead entering Round 3, Liu faces the standard challenge of every front-runner: keep doing what works, don't think about the scoreboard, make par when it's available and birdie when it's not.

The field behind her on the leaderboard includes players who have won before, who know how to chase a leader down. Michigan's weather in late June can also play a factor — wind, afternoon thunderstorms, and shifting conditions are all possible at Blythefield.

What's Next

Round 3 is underway today. Round 4 is Sunday, June 21. The final leaderboard and winner will be known by Sunday afternoon. If Liu is still leading Sunday morning, the golf world will tune in for the back nine — because first wins are one of golf's genuinely great stories.

Whatever happens, she put herself here by shooting a 66. That's not luck. That's work.
