US Open Round 3: Wyndham Clark Leads by Four at Shinnecock as Moving Day Begins

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 20, 2026

Wyndham Clark walked into Saturday at Shinnecock Hills with a four-shot lead and the weight of every major's history behind him. The 126th US Open is in its third round, and the 32-year-old has done everything right through 36 holes to put himself in position for golf's most grueling championship.

Clark's -7 total through two rounds is the best 36-hole score in US Open history at Shinnecock Hills. His opening 64 was the second-lowest single round ever recorded at this course in a US Open — Tommy Fleetwood's closing 63 in 2018 is the only lower number. Clark backed it up Friday, extending his lead to four strokes over a quartet of legitimate contenders: Matt Fitzpatrick, Xander Schauffele, Sam Stevens, and Tom Kim, all at -3.

The Lead and Who's Chasing

The four-shot margin is comfortable but not safe at Shinnecock. This is a golf course that can turn on players with startling speed — the wind picks up off the ocean, the fairways harden and funnel balls into difficult spots, and the greens become nearly unputt able when the USGA lets conditions get firm. A Saturday score in the mid-70s from Clark and mid-60s from the pack below him would scramble the leaderboard entirely.

Fitzpatrick, the 2022 US Open champion, knows exactly what it takes to win this event. He plays a precise, percentage-driven game that is built for major championship conditions. Schauffele has the talent — a multiple major champion with several top-5 finishes — and the composure to manufacture a charge. Stevens and Kim represent the wildcard contingent: players who could either shoot 65 and redefine the tournament or fade into Saturday anonymity.

Further down the board, Rory McIlroy sits at even par — seven shots back, officially in the we'll-need-some-help category — while Scottie Scheffler is at one over par, roughly eight shots behind Clark. Both men made the cut and are still officially in the tournament, which is more than can be said for Bryson DeChambeau, Viktor Hovland, Jon Rahm, and Shane Lowry, who all missed the cut.

About Clark's Round 1 Record

The 64 Clark shot Thursday is worth appreciating in context. Shinnecock Hills is not a golf course that offers easy scoring. The rough is thick, the fairways are narrow, and the back nine can destroy a round in a single cluster of holes. The six times Shinnecock has hosted the US Open, it has produced low scoring only in extraordinary circumstances — usually a Tuesday-like calm conditions or a remarkable player at the peak of his game.

Clark's 64 was both. He hit fairways, found greens, and putted without mercy. That combination at Shinnecock in a US Open is nearly unprecedented. The fact that he followed it with a second round solid enough to keep a four-shot lead suggests this isn't a one-round anomaly.

Saturday TV Coverage

Round 3 coverage is on USA Network from 10 AM to noon ET, then switching to NBC from noon to 8 PM ET. The final pairing of Clark and Fitzpatrick tees off at 3:45 PM ET — expect that tee time to be the afternoon's main event.

What's Next

If Clark holds the lead through Saturday, he enters Sunday as the US Open leader — a position that carries an enormous amount of historical pressure and an enormous amount of opportunity. No player with a 54-hole lead of four or more at Shinnecock Hills has ever failed to win the championship. That's not a guarantee. It's a data point.

Round 4 is Sunday. Shinnecock Hills has one more chance to do what it does best.
