Nelly Korda Is Four Points From the LPGA Hall of Fame — Kroger Queen City Is Next

By Ryan Calloway | May 9, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The LPGA Hall of Fame is not easily won. The qualification system was designed to be among the hardest to reach in professional sports — a combination of victory points that accumulate over time, weighted by the prestige of the events won. Players who qualify have almost universally defined the women's game for a generation.

Nelly Korda is 27 years old. She has 18 career LPGA victories. And as of this week, she sits at 23 of the 27 points required for Hall of Fame qualification — four points from golf immortality.

The Most Remarkable Start to a Season in Decades

Korda's 2026 season has been historically exceptional. She has four wins already — including the Chevron Championship major — and has not finished outside the top two in her first six starts. The last player to maintain a record of no finish worse than runner-up through six events in the modern era was Annika Sorenstam in 2001.

Sorenstam, of course, is widely regarded as the greatest female golfer in history. That Korda's 2026 trajectory draws a direct comparison should not be understated. She is not playing like a great player in a good stretch. She is playing like a player operating on a different level than everyone around her — and she has been doing so with a consistency that borders on mathematical certainty.

The youngest American to reach 18 career LPGA victories since Nancy Lopez in 1980, Korda has arrived at the most significant milestone marker of her career with time, health, and form all in her favor.

The Hall of Fame Math

LPGA Hall of Fame qualification requires 27 points. Standard tournament wins award 2 points each. Major championship victories are worth 3 points. With 23 points currently, Korda needs 4 more — which could be achieved by winning two regular LPGA events, winning one major plus one regular event, or through other point-earning combinations.

At her current rate — four wins in the first few months of 2026 — reaching 27 points before the end of the season is not a projection. It is a virtual certainty, barring injury or the kind of form reversal that nothing in her recent track record suggests is coming.

Kroger Queen City Is Next

Korda's next scheduled event is the Kroger Queen City Championship in Cincinnati, Ohio, running May 14-17. The tournament is a regular LPGA event — not a major — which means a victory would add 2 points, moving Korda from 23 to 25. That would leave her two points from the Hall of Fame threshold.

A second regular-event win after Cincinnati would reach 27. One additional major would reach 26, requiring one more win of any kind. The timeline is aggressive — potentially within the next few months — and Korda's form suggests she is more likely than not to reach it on an accelerated schedule.

For the Kroger Queen City, Korda arrives as the overwhelming favorite and the center of every storyline. A win in Cincinnati would make the Hall of Fame conversation inescapable heading into the summer major season.

What's Next

The Kroger Queen City Championship in Cincinnati begins May 14 — the same week as the PGA Championship on the men's side. Golf's biggest week of the year may also deliver one of the LPGA's most historic moments.

Watch Korda closely in Cincinnati. Every birdie, every scoring run, and every closing stretch will feel amplified by the proximity of the moment she has been building toward since the beginning of her professional career. Four points is nothing. Four points is everything. And Nelly Korda plays like she knows exactly the difference.
