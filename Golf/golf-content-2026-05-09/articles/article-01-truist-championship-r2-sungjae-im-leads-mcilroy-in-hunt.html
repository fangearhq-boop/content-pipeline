Truist Championship R2 — Sungjae Im Surges to Lead, McIlroy Fires 67 to Get Back in It

By Marcus Cole | May 9, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Friday at Quail Hollow Club produced the kind of leaderboard reshuffling that tournament golf specializes in — and when the smoke cleared, Sungjae Im stood at 9-under par 133, the outright 36-hole leader at the Truist Championship. Four shots back at 5-under, very much alive, was Rory McIlroy.

Less than 24 hours earlier, McIlroy had been 7 shots off the pace after an uninspiring opening round of 1-under 70. He looked like a player who had arrived at a tournament still thinking about the Masters two weeks prior. Friday, he looked like himself again.

Im's Steady Climb to the Top

Sungjae Im is one of the most consistent players on the PGA Tour, a machine-like ball-striker who rarely makes mistakes and rarely wows anyone with a single shot. What he does is grind, accumulate, and let the leaderboard come to him. His 36-hole total of 133 is exactly the kind of position he builds from — not flashy, not vulnerable to collapse, just very good golf that forces everyone else to respond.

Im has been quietly one of the tour's best players for several seasons, logging top-10 finishes at a rate that most players would envy. A Truist Championship title would represent a signature win for a player who has delivered signature performances without the accompanying headlines. The field chasing him has no easy path to overtaking him — Im doesn't give tournaments away.

McIlroy's 67 Changes the Narrative

McIlroy went out Friday and played the kind of round that shuts everyone up who wrote him off Thursday evening. His 4-under 67 was purposeful and progressive — he found his rhythm on the front nine, made clutch putts when he needed them, and converted his ball-striking quality into the kind of scoring that moves a leaderboard in a meaningful direction.

After he made the turn 1-under in R1 and found himself eight shots off the pace, McIlroy spoke in measured terms about needing to improve. He improved dramatically. The gap from 7 shots back to 4 shots back in a single round, at a venue he has dominated for the better part of 15 years, is not coincidence. It's Rory McIlroy remembering what he is capable of.

The weekend sets up as a compelling drama. McIlroy at 4 back with two rounds at a course he has turned into his personal playground. Im at the top with a style of play that makes him difficult to pass unless someone shoots very low. Neither outcome feels predictable.

Fowler Reminds Everyone He Belongs

The day's low round of 8-under 63 came from Rickie Fowler — nine birdies, zero bogeys, a display of offensive golf that vaulted him into the top eight on the leaderboard. Fowler's career has been a story of unfulfilled major potential, but rounds like this are a reminder that his talent has never been the question. When Fowler puts everything together, he is as fun to watch as anyone in the game.

His 63 also signals something worth noting: the scoring is available at Quail Hollow this week. The course is playing scoreable for the elite of the field, which means the weekend leaders are going to need to attack, not protect. That's good news for McIlroy's chase and bad news for anyone hoping Im can simply grind the tournament to a close.

What's Next

Round 3 is underway today (Saturday), with the final round scheduled for Sunday, May 10. Scottie Scheffler is not in this field — he made the calculated decision to skip the Truist Championship to prepare for next week's PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club in Pennsylvania. The absence of the World No. 1 makes every remaining round at Quail Hollow that much more unpredictable.

Im leads, McIlroy lurks, and Fowler is in the mix. The Truist Championship is officially in its most compelling phase.
