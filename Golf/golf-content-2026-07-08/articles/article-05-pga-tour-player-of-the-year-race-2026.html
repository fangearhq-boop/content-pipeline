Gotterup Has Three Wins. Fitzpatrick Has Three Wins. Clark Won the US Open. Can Any of Them Take the POTY Award From Scheffler?

By Jake Torres | July 8, 2026

PGA Tour / Player of the Year

Scottie Scheffler has won the PGA Tour Player of the Year award for three consecutive years. In 2023, 2024, and 2025, no one made a sustained case against him — his win totals, major victories, and world No. 1 dominance made the vote a formality each time. The 2026 race looks different.

Scheffler remains world No. 1. He won the Masters. But with only one win on the season through July, he is giving ground in the win-count argument to three players who have been more prolific since January: Chris Gotterup, Matt Fitzpatrick, and Wyndham Clark. Golf Channel covered this exact topic on July 7-8. The debate has arrived.

The Case for Chris Gotterup

No player on the PGA Tour has won more tournaments in 2026 than Gotterup. His John Deere Classic victory on July 6 was his third win of the calendar year and his fourth in the last 12 months. He is currently ranked world No. 4 — a ranking that would have seemed implausible 18 months ago for a player who was still establishing himself on the Korn Ferry Tour.

Gotterup is also the defending Genesis Scottish Open champion. He arrives in North Berwick this week as the hottest player in professional golf by any volume-based metric. The Player of the Year award has historically rewarded win totals heavily — three wins, with the season still months from completion, is an argument that carries real weight.

What Gotterup lacks, to date, is a major championship. None of his three 2026 wins have been at a major — and the Masters (Scheffler), US Open (Clark), and US Women's Open aside, there are still two majors on the men's calendar before the season ends. A major win would end the debate. Without one, the argument is about volume vs. prestige.

The Case for Matt Fitzpatrick

Fitzpatrick also has three wins in 2026 — the Valspar Championship, the RBC Canadian Open, and the Zurich Classic (a team event, though it counts in win tallies). He is ranked No. 6 in the world. The English golfer has been the most consistent non-Scheffler presence at the top of PGA Tour leaderboards since January, and his win at the RBC Canadian Open — a regular signature event with a strong field — is the most prominent individual result among his three titles.

Fitzpatrick's case mirrors Gotterup's in one critical respect: no major win in 2026. He won the US Open in 2022 and has shown the ability to compete at major championship level, but reproducing that in 2026 would significantly elevate his POTY argument.

The Case for Wyndham Clark

Clark has two wins in 2026 — the US Open and the CJ Cup Byron Nelson — and the US Open does substantial work in the POTY conversation. Major championships carry outsized weight in Player of the Year voting, by design and by historical precedent. Clark's win at the US Open represents the sport's most demanding test of any given year, and winning it places him in a small group of players who have claimed a 2026 major title.

Two wins total, however, puts Clark behind Gotterup and Fitzpatrick on raw volume. His argument is quality over quantity — a fair argument, but one that typically requires the major winner to also be a presence on leaderboards throughout the year, not just on major Sundays.

The Case for Scheffler

Scheffler's case rests on his sustained position at the top of the world rankings and his Masters victory — which is golf's most prestigious major and the event Scheffler has now won multiple times. World No. 1 ranking points reflect a cumulative body of work, not a single season, and Scheffler's continued presence atop that list reflects elite performance across an extended stretch.

The counterargument is simple: one win in a year when three other players each have two or three wins represents a meaningful drop-off in the metric the POTY award has consistently rewarded. Scheffler won the award in prior years partly because the win tallies backed the narrative. In 2026, the win tallies favor someone else.

What's Next

The PGA Tour season extends through August and the FedEx Cup Playoffs before culminating with the Tour Championship in late August. The Player of the Year vote is conducted by PGA Tour members and announced at the end of the season. Between now and then, two more major championships — The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale (July 16-19) and the PGA Championship (August) — will provide the clearest opportunities for any of the contenders to either win the POTY debate outright or cede it. A major win for Gotterup, Fitzpatrick, or Clark would likely end the conversation. Another Scheffler win under pressure would remind the tour why he has held this award for three years running.
