Scheffler, McIlroy, Rahm, and a Defending Champion Who Just Won on Sunday — Scottish Open Field Is Stacked

By Ryan Calloway | July 8, 2026

PGA Tour / DP World Tour

The Genesis Scottish Open begins Thursday at Renaissance Club in North Berwick, Scotland, and the assembled field is as strong as the event has ever seen. Scottie Scheffler, Rory McIlroy, Jon Rahm, Matt Fitzpatrick, and Tommy Fleetwood are all in the draw — along with defending champion Chris Gotterup, who arrives at the $9 million Rolex Series event having won the John Deere Classic at TPC Deere Run just four days ago.

Gotterup's presence as defending champion was already a story worth watching. His arrival carrying a fresh title makes it significantly more compelling. He is now three wins into the 2026 PGA Tour season and has won four times in the last 12 months — more than any player on either the PGA Tour or DP World Tour over that stretch.

The Field

The Scottish Open operates as a co-sanctioned event between the PGA Tour and DP World Tour, drawing elite players from both circuits in the week before The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale (July 16-19). The timing gives top players a competitive warm-up round at a genuine links-adjacent course, and the entry list reflects that intent.

Scheffler is playing his first event since successfully defending at a major earlier in the season. McIlroy, who grew up watching The Open and has chased a second Claret Jug throughout his career, is treating this week as preparation for Birkdale. Rahm's entry is notable on its own: this is his first Scottish Open since 2022, a return to Scotland that signals a shift in his preparation approach following his move to LIV Golf and subsequent return to the DP World Tour.

Fitzpatrick and Fleetwood round out the European contingent of world-ranked players, and Brooks Koepka and Patrick Reed are also in the field — both having returned to mainstream tour golf earlier this year after their time with LIV Golf. First-round tee times for the Scheffler-Fitzpatrick-Fleetwood grouping are set for 8:43 AM ET Thursday.

The Gotterup Factor

When Gotterup won the Scottish Open in 2025, he was a relative newcomer to the conversation at this level — a young American who had broken through on the Korn Ferry Tour and earned his PGA Tour card, showing occasional flashes without sustained excellence. Twelve months later, the profile is entirely different.

The John Deere win on July 6 was his third title of the 2026 calendar year and his fourth in the last 12 months. He is currently ranked No. 4 in the world. The PGA Tour Player of the Year conversation increasingly runs through him. And now he arrives at his own tournament as the defending champion, on current form that no one in the field can match.

The Scottish Open has historically served as a launching pad for players who go on to perform well the following week at The Open. Gotterup's trajectory — from Korn Ferry Tour to multiple PGA Tour wins to defending Rolex Series champion — is the kind of arc that defines careers.

Renaissance Club and the Open Championship Connection

Renaissance Club is a links-style course that hugs the East Lothian coastline south of Edinburgh. Wind management, low trajectory shots, and the ability to work the ball along the ground are rewarded here — the same skill set that wins Open Championships. That is not an accident. The Scottish Open's co-sanctioning arrangement with The Open Championship explicitly positions this week as a preparation event for Birkdale.

Several players have used the Scottish Open as a springboard directly to Open Championship success. McIlroy's comfort on links turf is well-documented. Scheffler, who took some time to translate his ball-striking to links conditions earlier in his career, has shown improvement in this format. Rahm won The Open in 2023 and understands Scottish conditions instinctively.

What's Next

The Genesis Scottish Open runs July 9-12 at Renaissance Club, with prize money of $9 million available across the Rolex Series event. Following the conclusion of the Scottish Open on Sunday, the field largely moves together to Royal Birkdale for the 154th Open Championship beginning July 16. The performance this week — and who peaks at the right moment — will be the dominant storyline heading into one of golf's four major championships.
