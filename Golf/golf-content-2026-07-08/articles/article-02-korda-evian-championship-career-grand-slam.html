Nelly Korda Has Won Two Majors in 2026. The Evian Championship Is the One She Needs Next.

By Jake Torres | July 8, 2026

LPGA / Evian Championship

Nelly Korda arrives at the Amundi Evian Championship this week having won two of the five LPGA major championships in 2026 — the Chevron Championship in the spring and the US Women's Open more recently. No player has won three majors in a single calendar year in the modern LPGA era. The possibility is real, and it begins Thursday in France.

But the stakes at the Evian are larger than the 2026 season alone. The Evian Championship is one of two LPGA majors Korda has never won — the other being the AIG Women's British Open. A victory in Evian-les-Bains this week would put her one win away from the career Grand Slam: the achievement of winning all five of the sport's major championships over the course of a career.

The Grand Slam Context

The career Grand Slam in women's golf requires wins at the Chevron Championship, the KPMG Women's PGA Championship, the US Women's Open, the AIG Women's British Open, and the Evian Championship. Korda has the Chevron, the KPMG, and the US Women's Open. She needs the Evian and the AIG Women's British Open.

Ryu Haeran's win at the 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship earlier this year ended Korda's bid to complete the calendar Grand Slam in a single season — winning all five majors in one year, which no player in LPGA history has accomplished. The career Grand Slam is a different and more achievable target. Korda is 27 years old and has demonstrated the ability to win at the highest level in both regular season events and majors.

Her odds at Evian are +300 — reflecting her status as the clear favorite in the field and the form she's carried through the season.

The Tournament and the Field

The Amundi Evian Championship is held at Evian Resort Golf Club on the shores of Lake Geneva in Evian-les-Bains, France — making it the only LPGA major championship staged in continental Europe. The setting is distinctive: mountain views, manicured fairways, and a course that rewards precision over power.

The $9.1 million purse is among the largest in women's golf. Grace Kim won the 2025 edition and returns to defend. Minjee Lee, a two-time major champion who has been competitive throughout the 2026 season, is in the field alongside Lydia Ko — an Olympic gold medalist and two-time major winner — and Rose Zhang, who turned professional in 2023 and has steadily climbed the world rankings since.

The field is deep and the competition is real. Korda's favorites designation reflects her current form, not an absence of challenge.

What a Win Would Mean

Golf's career Grand Slam is one of the sport's most selective clubs. Annika Sorenstam, Karrie Webb, Juli Inkster, Pat Bradley, Louise Suggs, and Mickey Wright are among the LPGA legends who completed it. The modern era has produced fewer examples, partly because the Evian Championship was only elevated to major status in 2013, making the full set harder to compile for players whose careers predate that recognition.

For Korda, the conversation has moved from "if" to "when." Two wins this year, a consistent world No. 1 ranking, and an appetite for major competition that has grown each season — if she wins at Evian and then wins the AIG Women's British Open in August, she will have completed one of women's golf's most celebrated career milestones before turning 28.

What's Next

The Amundi Evian Championship runs July 9-12 at Evian Resort Golf Club in France. The first round begins Thursday. If Korda contends through the weekend, the storyline will extend through Sunday afternoon and into global coverage of women's major championship golf. The AIG Women's British Open follows in August — giving Korda a second opportunity at the career Grand Slam within the same season if Evian produces a different winner.
