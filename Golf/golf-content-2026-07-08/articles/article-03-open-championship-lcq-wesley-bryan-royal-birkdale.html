18 Holes, 12 Players, One Spot: Wesley Bryan Is in the Open Championship Last-Chance Qualifier at Royal Birkdale

By Marcus Cole | July 8, 2026

Golf / The Open Championship

Three days before the 154th Open Championship begins at Royal Birkdale, the golf course will host one of the sport's most compressed competitive formats: the Last-Chance Qualifier. Twelve players. Eighteen holes. One spot in the field.

Wesley Bryan — PGA Tour member, YouTube star, and one of golf's most recognizable personalities beyond the broadcast booth — will be among the twelve competing on July 13 for the final exemption into The Open Championship.

The Format

The Open Championship's Last-Chance Qualifier (LCQ) exists as a final access point for players who have not earned entry through the R&A's standard exemption categories — which include world ranking criteria, performance at designated qualifying events, and Major championship exemptions. The LCQ is 18-hole stroke play; the lowest score in the field earns the entry. Everyone else goes home.

The 12-man field at this year's LCQ is a mix of tour players who narrowly missed other qualifying routes, players currently ranked near but outside the world ranking cutoff, and — in Bryan's case — a player whose circumstances have kept him outside The Open's regular entry criteria despite holding PGA Tour membership.

Wesley Bryan and the LIV Golf Controversy

Bryan has been a prominent figure in golf's social media ecosystem for years — his trick shot videos and course content have earned him millions of followers across platforms. He has also been a PGA Tour member in good standing, maintaining his card through competitive play while building a substantial digital audience alongside his professional career.

Earlier in 2026, Bryan participated in a LIV Golf influencer event — a tournament or exhibition organized by LIV Golf that featured social media personalities alongside LIV players. The PGA Tour subsequently barred him from participating in a Tour-sanctioned pro-am, a consequence that generated significant attention and debate about the scope of PGA Tour authority over members' activities during off-weeks.

Aldrich Potgieter and the Rest of the Field

Also in the LCQ field is Aldrich Potgieter — the young South African long-hitter who plays on the LIV Golf circuit. Potgieter has drawn attention across golf coverage for his driving distances, which rank among the longest recorded by professional golfers anywhere in the world. He is in his early 20s and has been positioned by LIV Golf as one of the circuit's next-generation stars.

The remaining 10 players in the 12-man field include a mix of DP World Tour and Challenge Tour members who represent the final tier of Open Championship hopefuls — professionals with the game to compete at Birkdale who are fighting for the one remaining opportunity to prove it.

What's Next

The Open Championship Last-Chance Qualifier takes place July 13 at Royal Birkdale Golf Club in Southport, England. One player will earn a spot in the 154th Open Championship field — which begins competition on Thursday, July 16. Royal Birkdale has hosted The Open nine times previously, most recently in 2017, when Jordan Spieth held off Matt Kuchar to claim the Claret Jug. Whoever earns that final spot on July 13 will compete on the same links starting 72 hours later.
