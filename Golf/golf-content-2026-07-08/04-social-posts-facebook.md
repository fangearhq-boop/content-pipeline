### STORY 4: LIV Golf CEO Won't Guarantee Final Four Events — Running on Saudi Loans With $400M Shortfall

**Long-Form Post (3:30 PM ET)**

LIV Golf CEO Scott O'Neil said this week that he cannot guarantee the league's final four events of 2026 will be played.

The UK event and Bedminster (New Jersey) are expected to proceed. But Indianapolis and Michigan are now officially in doubt. New Orleans had already been postponed earlier in the year.

LIV's financial situation has deteriorated significantly. The league has been running on loans from Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF) — recorded in debenture filings — and faces an estimated $400 million shortfall. PIF has signaled it will wind down direct support after 2026, raising questions about what LIV Golf looks like, or whether it exists at all, in 2027.

The cumulative Saudi investment in LIV Golf since its 2022 launch is estimated at over $6 billion. Without a broadcast deal or title sponsor generating meaningful revenue, that math has never worked — and now the calendar is paying the price.

The sport has changed significantly since LIV launched. Several high-profile players have returned to the PGA Tour. The merger framework between the tours remains unresolved. And the league that was supposed to reshape golf is now struggling to finish its own schedule.
