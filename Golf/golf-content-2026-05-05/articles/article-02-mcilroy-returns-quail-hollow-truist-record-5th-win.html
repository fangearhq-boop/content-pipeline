The King Returns: McIlroy Back at Quail Hollow After Back-to-Back Masters, Eyes Record 5th Truist Title

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 5, 2026

Rory McIlroy tees up this week at the Truist Championship — his first competitive round since winning back-to-back Masters titles on April 12. He has won at Quail Hollow four times and arrives with six major championships to his name.

When Rory McIlroy walks to the first tee at Quail Hollow Club in Charlotte, North Carolina, on Wednesday morning, he will do so as a man who has achieved something only three players in the history of the Masters have done before him. On April 12, he won his second consecutive green jacket at Augusta National, joining Jack Nicklaus, Nick Faldo and Tiger Woods in the exclusive company of back-to-back Masters champions.

He has not competed since that Sunday in Georgia. The Truist Championship is where he begins the next chapter.

A Return to His Favorite Stage

If there is a venue on the PGA Tour schedule where McIlroy feels most at home, Quail Hollow might be it. He has won the event — now called the Truist Championship — four times: in 2010, 2015, 2021 and 2024. The Charlotte course, with its demanding "Green Mile" closing stretch of three holes alongside Rea's Branch creek, suits his combination of controlled aggression off the tee and technical precision in his iron play.

A fifth victory would be historic. No player in the modern era of the PGA Tour has won a non-major tournament five times at the same venue in four separate decades. McIlroy has already accomplished the rarer feat of winning the same event in multiple distinct stages of his career — and this edition arrives with the kind of momentum that legends are built on.

The tournament has been recently renovated. Quail Hollow underwent a comprehensive update that included new tees and greens, a new drainage system and a complete overhaul of the bunkers for the first time in nearly 20 years. The changes maintain the character of the course while modernizing its infrastructure. Those who know McIlroy's game expect it to suit him as much as ever.

The Legacy Context

McIlroy arrived at this point in his career with six major championships. That places him tied for 12th on the all-time major list, alongside Lee Trevino, Nick Faldo and Phil Mickelson. At 36 years old, he is not finished — and the consistency with which he has performed at Augusta National over the past two years suggests that the major championship ledger may not have been fully closed.

The back-to-back Masters wins are particularly significant in historical context. The last player to accomplish them was Tiger Woods in 2001 and 2002. Before Woods, Faldo had done it in 1989 and 1990. Before Faldo, Nicklaus in 1965 and 1966. In each case, the achievement marked a period of total dominance in the sport. McIlroy has now joined that conversation not as an aspirant but as a confirmed member.

The Competition Awaiting

The Truist Championship has assembled a signature-event field that reflects the PGA Tour's current strength. Seven of the world's top-10 players are competing this week. Cameron Young — now ascending to World No. 3 after his Cadillac Championship wire-to-wire win — is in the field and will draw significant attention as one of the sport's emerging stars. Matt Fitzpatrick, a two-time winner in 2026, is also competing.

The defending Truist champion is Sepp Straka, who won in 2024. He will be defending as McIlroy attempts to claim the record fifth title at this venue.

The $20 million purse and Signature Event designation ensure the quality of the competition will match the heightened circumstances of McIlroy's return. But most who follow professional golf will be focused first on McIlroy — how he looks, how he competes, and whether the Augusta momentum carries forward into Charlotte's distinctive closing challenge.

What's Next

Round 1 of the Truist Championship at Quail Hollow Club begins tomorrow, May 6. McIlroy's tee time will be among the most watched in golf on Wednesday morning. Following the Truist, attention turns to the PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club (May 14-17), where McIlroy has been installed as a significant betting favorite. The next three weeks represent the most concentrated stretch of elite golf since the Masters week itself — and Rory McIlroy is right in the center of all of it.
