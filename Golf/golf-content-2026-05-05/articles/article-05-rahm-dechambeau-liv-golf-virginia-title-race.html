The LIV Title Race Arrives in Virginia: Rahm vs DeChambeau Head Into Thursday as Co-Leaders

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 5, 2026

Jon Rahm and Bryson DeChambeau each have two wins in the 2026 LIV Golf season, and both head into this week's $30 million Virginia event tied at the top of the individual standings. The midpoint of the LIV season could be defined by what happens at Trump National.

The 2026 LIV Golf season is approaching its midpoint, and its title race has coalesced around two familiar names: Jon Rahm and Bryson DeChambeau, both carrying two individual wins into this week's event at Trump National Golf Club in Sterling, Virginia.

LIV Golf Virginia — the seventh event on the 2026 schedule — begins Thursday, May 7, and runs through Sunday, May 10. The purse is $30 million. The stakes for the individual title, which has emerged as the season's defining competition, could not be higher.

Two Men, Two Wins, One Race

Rahm's most recent victory came at LIV Mexico City, where he closed with a final-round seven-under 64 at Chapultepec Golf Club to win by six shots. It was a performance that underscored his form at the moment — controlled, powerful, patient — and extended his individual standing to two wins for the season.

DeChambeau had won twice earlier in the 2026 season before withdrawing from Mexico City with a wrist injury. The withdrawal handed Rahm a chance to separate himself in the standings that he seized fully. But DeChambeau's two wins remain on the ledger, the injury is reportedly resolved, and he arrives in Virginia with the evident motivation of a player who watched a rival close the gap while he was on the sideline.

Their rivalry is one of the sport's more compelling storylines across any format. They have competed in playoffs during the 2026 season. They have pushed each other to lower scores in events where both are contending. The personal history between them adds dimension to what the leaderboard numbers already make interesting.

Trump National Virginia

The venue, Trump National Golf Club in Sterling, Virginia — roughly 45 minutes west of Washington, D.C. — hosted LIV Golf for the first time in 2023 under the name LIV Golf DC. The course, a design by Tom Fazio, features long-iron and fairway wood approaches into well-protected greens, rewarding players who can control flight and trajectory under pressure. Accuracy off the tee is at a premium; the rough penalizes inaccuracy more than most LIV venues.

Both Rahm and DeChambeau are elite iron players, and both have demonstrated the ball-striking precision the course demands. Neither has a particular statistical advantage at this specific venue based on historical data. Which makes the outcome genuinely uncertain — exactly the kind of contrast that this title race needs after the decisive margin of Rahm's Mexico City victory.

The Wider Field

LIV Golf Virginia's 57-player field extends well beyond the two headline names. Cameron Smith, who has been competitive without winning in 2026, arrives seeking his first victory of the season. Tyrrell Hatton has produced consistent top-10 finishes across the LIV season. Anthony Kim — whose return to professional golf after years away has been one of LIV's ongoing narrative draws — is also competing.

LIV's 72-hole format (implemented since 2026's expansion from 54 holes) means the Virginia event will reward players who can sustain peak form over four days rather than just surviving a sprint. That deeper test may favor experience, patience and consistency — three qualities that both Rahm and DeChambeau have demonstrated at the highest levels of the sport.

What's Next

LIV Golf Virginia runs May 7-10 at Trump National Golf Club, Sterling, Virginia. With eight events remaining in the 2026 LIV season after Virginia, whoever leads the individual standings after Sunday will carry genuine momentum into the second half of the schedule. Rahm and DeChambeau know this. The $30 million purse is significant. But the title race implications make this event's outcome more consequential still.
