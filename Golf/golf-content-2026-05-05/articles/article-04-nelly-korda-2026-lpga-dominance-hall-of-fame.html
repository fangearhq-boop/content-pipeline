Six Starts, Six Top-Two Finishes: Nelly Korda's 2026 LPGA Season Is Historically Dominant

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 5, 2026

Three wins and three runner-ups through six events — Nelly Korda's 2026 record is a testament to consistency that few players in any era of professional golf have matched over a comparable span.

Professional golf, at any level, resists the kind of consistency that Nelly Korda is currently generating. The sport is too variable, the competition too deep and the conditions too unpredictable for a single player to finish in the top two at every event she enters across an extended stretch of weeks. Yet that is precisely what Korda has done through six LPGA Tour starts in 2026.

Three wins. Three runner-up finishes. A top-two result in every event she has entered this year. The scorecard across the season reads like a statistical anomaly — except Korda is making it look methodical.

The Wins That Define the Run

Korda's three 2026 victories span the full range of the LPGA calendar. She won the AIG Women's Open, a major championship, to add to her growing collection of elite titles. She claimed the Chevron Championship — the first women's major of the American season — in a performance that confirmed she had not lost anything from the momentum she built in 2025. And last weekend, she won the Riviera Maya Open in Mexico by four shots at 17-under, controlling the event from the closing stretch of the final round.

Majors, signature events, international events — she is winning across every format the LPGA puts in front of her. The variety of circumstances makes the consistency more notable, not less.

The Runner-Ups Are Not Failures

The three second-place finishes require context. Hyo Joo Kim — who has had a remarkable 2026 season of her own — has been responsible for denying Korda the top spot on multiple occasions. Kim's performances at the Founders Cup and Ford Championship were elite golf by any measure. When you finish runner-up to those rounds, it reflects the level of the competition rather than any deficiency in your own game.

What stands out is the floor Korda has established. On the LPGA Tour, where any given week can produce a first-time winner or a surprise champion from the middle of the world rankings, Korda has not finished lower than second in any event this season. She has been in contention, competing for wins, in every round she has played in 2026.

The Hall of Fame Timeline

Against the backdrop of this performance, a significant career milestone is approaching. Korda currently holds 22 of the 27 points required for LPGA Hall of Fame eligibility — five away from the threshold. The Hall of Fame points system awards points for wins, majors, Player of the Year awards and other distinguished achievements. At the pace she is accumulating wins and honors in 2026, five additional points is not a distant prospect.

She is 26 years old. She has 17 career LPGA victories, including three major championships. The Hall of Fame eligibility she is approaching is not something players routinely achieve at her age — it typically marks the capstone of long careers. For Korda, it may mark a midpoint.

What's Next

The LPGA Tour schedule continues, and Korda will enter each event as the prohibitive favorite until the evidence suggests otherwise. Given what she has produced across the first six tournaments of 2026, there is no indication of a slowdown. The sport is watching — not just week to week, but with a broader recognition that something historically unusual is happening. Six starts. Six top-two finishes. The season has barely crossed its midpoint in terms of the calendar, and Nelly Korda is already rewriting what a dominant LPGA campaign can look like.
