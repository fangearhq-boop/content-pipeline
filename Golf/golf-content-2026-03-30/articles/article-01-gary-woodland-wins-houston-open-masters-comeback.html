Gary Woodland Wins Houston Open — First Victory Since Brain Surgery, Masters Berth Earned

By Golf Fanrecap Staff | March 30, 2026

Two weeks ago, Gary Woodland told the world about his PTSD. On Sunday, he answered every question about what comes next.

Woodland won the 2026 Texas Children's Houston Open at Memorial Park Golf Course, closing with a 63 to finish at 21-under 259 — a new tournament record on the course. He won by five shots over Nicolai Højgaard and claimed his first PGA Tour victory in nearly seven years: his first win since the 2019 U.S. Open at Pebble Beach, and his first since a brain tumor diagnosis and surgery in 2023 that changed everything about how he thought about the game.

With the win comes an invitation to the 2026 Masters Tournament, beginning April 9 at Augusta National. It is Woodland's fifth career PGA Tour title, and perhaps the most meaningful one he has ever claimed.

A Comeback Story Years in the Making

The journey from the 2019 U.S. Open to Memorial Park Golf Course in 2026 contains more adversity than most professional athletes face in a career. Woodland was diagnosed with a brain tumor in 2023 and underwent surgery. He returned to the PGA Tour while managing not just the physical recovery but the psychological toll that followed — a toll he described publicly two weeks before the Houston Open when he revealed he had been dealing with post-traumatic stress disorder.

That disclosure, difficult as it was, became part of the story that made Sunday's victory land so hard. Standing at the 18th green at Memorial Park with a five-shot lead, Woodland became proof that the confession wasn't the end of his story but a step within it.

Woodland earned $1,782,000 from the $9,900,000 total purse and moved to No. 25 in the FedEx Cup standings with the win.

Sunday at Memorial Park

Woodland entered the final round at 18-under, leading by three, and extended that margin throughout the day. His 63 was one of the week's best rounds on a demanding Memorial Park course set up for low scoring but punishing for loose shots.

Jake Knapp delivered the most electric round of the day with a final-round 62 — tying the lowest round in Houston Open history at Memorial Park — to finish in the top six. But even Knapp's brilliance couldn't put a dent in Woodland's comfortable margin at the top.

Højgaard finished second at 16-under, with Knapp and defending champion Min Woo Lee sharing third at 15-under.

What's Next

Woodland will now begin the most consequential week of his comeback: Augusta National. His Masters invitation comes just ten days before the first round. He will arrive at a venue where he has played before but never won — and now arrives with the momentum and emotional clarity of a man who has spent years fighting just to earn his way back to the biggest stages in the sport.

The Masters field has now been completed. The golf world watched what Gary Woodland did on Sunday at Memorial Park. Augusta National will see what happens when a man plays for more than a leaderboard position.
