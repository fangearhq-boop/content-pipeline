Masters 2026 Field Set — 10 Days to Augusta, Scheffler the Favorite, Tiger's Shadow Looms Large

By Golf Fanrecap Staff | March 30, 2026

Ten days. That is what separates the golf world from the 90th Masters Tournament at Augusta National.

With the 2026 Texas Children's Houston Open now complete, the Masters field is essentially locked. Gary Woodland's Houston victory earned him an automatic exemption, and Jake Knapp, Nicolai Hojgaard, Matt McCarty, and Daniel Berger were added via the Official World Golf Rankings following the tournament. The field stands at approximately 92 players — headlined by the world's best players and one enormous question mark.

Favorites and Contenders

Scottie Scheffler is the +480 favorite in the current odds market. The world No. 1 has won at Augusta twice — in 2022 and 2024 — and has recorded a top-10 finish in each of his last four Masters starts. He withdrew from the Houston Open to be present for the birth of his second child and will arrive at Augusta having played just one official event since his season-opening victory. Fresh legs and no fatigue entering a major has historically served him well.

Rory McIlroy defends at +1000. The Northern Irishman completed the Career Grand Slam at Augusta last year in a dramatic playoff win, becoming only the sixth male golfer in history to win all four major championships. He has publicly expressed a desire to become a back-to-back Masters champion — something only three players have achieved in the tournament's history. McIlroy skipped the Houston Open and the Valero Texas Open, arriving at Augusta having conserved energy and focus for the title defense.

Bryson DeChambeau and Jon Rahm are both listed at +1200. DeChambeau in particular arrives with back-to-back LIV wins entering the first major of the spring — the kind of form that can carry a player through Augusta National's demanding final-round pressure.

Tiger's Shadow

The most complicated storyline surrounding the 2026 Masters is Tiger Woods. His Friday arrest on DUI suspicion, subsequent charges, and released mugshot have created enormous uncertainty about his participation — not just because of the legal situation, but because his physical preparation was already a question mark before the weekend.

Woods, 50, had his seventh back surgery last September. He ruptured his Achilles tendon in March 2025 and missed the entire last professional season. He attended a TGL match just 48 hours before his arrest. He was reportedly working toward an Augusta appearance, but was not confirmed for the field before the weekend's events. He is still scheduled to be in Augusta on April 5 for an unrelated golf development project.

President Trump said on Sunday that Woods "will be there but won't be playing," though Trump has no official authority over Masters invitations. The ultimate answer will come from Woods himself in the days ahead.

What's Next

The Valero Texas Open (April 2-5 at TPC San Antonio) is the final PGA Tour event before Augusta, and the winner will earn the last automatic Masters exemption. Twenty-two Masters invitees are committed to San Antonio. Players on the cusp of the top-50 world rankings can also move into the Masters field with a strong Valero finish.

Amazon Prime Video will broadcast the Masters internationally for the first time in 2026, expanding the tournament's already enormous global reach. The cameras begin rolling in Augusta on Monday, April 6. The first round of competitive play is Thursday, April 9. Ten days. The game's most storied major is almost here.
