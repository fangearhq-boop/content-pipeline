Hyo Joo Kim Claims Back-to-Back LPGA Titles at Ford Championship — Makes History in the Process

By Golf Fanrecap Staff | March 30, 2026

Hyo Joo Kim won the 2026 Ford Championship. She also rewrote the record books in the process.

The South Korean star finished at 28-under 260 at Whirlwind Golf Club's Cattail Course in Wild Horse Pass, Arizona, to claim her second consecutive LPGA Tour title — the week after winning the Founders Cup in California. She beat Nelly Korda by two shots for her second victory in as many starts this spring.

In doing so, Kim became the first player in LPGA Tour history to shoot two rounds of 61 or better in the same tournament. She accomplished the feat in rounds one and three at Whirlwind Golf Club, a track that demands precision and rewards aggressive play. No player in the tour's history had managed the scoring feat before her.

A Dominant Two-Week Stretch

The back-to-back victories represent one of the most dominant two-week runs on the LPGA Tour in recent memory. Kim won the Founders Cup last week, then arrived at the Ford Championship and did not let off the accelerator. She entered the final round in a pack near the top of the leaderboard and played Sunday with the confidence of a player who already knows how this story ends.

Korda, who had finished second at the Founders Cup the week prior, once again had to settle for the runner-up position. Minami Katsu played a superb final round of 65 to finish third at 23-under. Lydia Ko, who had led after an opening-round 60 — the low round of her professional career — closed with a 68 and fell to fourth at 20-under.

In Gee Chun rounded out the top five at 19-under.

The Ko Context

Lydia Ko's week deserves its own chapter. The two-time Olympic gold medalist opened with a 12-birdie 60 on Thursday that had her in serious contention for a sub-59 round. She led by one entering Friday and remained in the hunt through the middle rounds. But Kim's consistency — and the brilliance of her third-round 61 — proved insurmountable. Ko's 68 on Sunday represented her highest score of the week at a moment that required her lowest.

Ko leaves Phoenix with a top-five finish and a reminder that her game is in excellent form heading into the busier portion of the LPGA calendar.

What's Next

Hyo Joo Kim is now the clear frontrunner in the early LPGA Player of the Year conversation. Two wins, two weeks, $900,000 in prize money, and a place in the record books. The LPGA calendar continues with an event in Las Vegas, where Ko plans to compete and where Kim will arrive as the tour's most in-form player.

The question for the rest of the field — Korda included — is simple: how do you stop her?
