Valero Texas Open Preview — The Last Stop Before Augusta, With Augusta Spots Still on the Line

By Golf Fanrecap Staff | March 30, 2026

The 2026 Valero Texas Open begins Thursday at TPC San Antonio, and while it is the week before the Masters, it is anything but a warmup for everyone in the field.

For 22 players already holding Masters invitations, it is the final competitive tune-up before Augusta. For others, it is something more urgent: the tournament winner earns an automatic Masters exemption, and players hovering near the top-50 in the Official World Golf Rankings can move themselves into the Augusta field with a strong performance at the Oaks Course.

The stakes are real. The window is narrow. And the field is loaded.

The Field

Tommy Fleetwood, ranked No. 3 in the world, enters as the early betting favorite. The Englishman's ballstriking on wind-exposed, strategic layouts — exactly what TPC San Antonio's Oaks Course offers — gives him a profile that suits this venue. Fleetwood also holds a Masters invitation but will use San Antonio to sharpen his game before the tournament he has never won.

Collin Morikawa, Justin Rose, Hideki Matsuyama, and Ludvig Aberg are among the marquee names committed to the field. Defending champion Brian Harman returns to defend his title. The 132-player field includes 18 players from the top 50 in the world ranking.

The players with the most personal stakes may be Rickie Fowler and Will Zalatoris. Both are in the San Antonio field, and both need a result to potentially punch their tickets to Augusta. Fowler, now in his late 30s, has been pursuing his first major championship for more than a decade. A Valero title or a strong points finish would deliver what would be an enormously emotional Augusta invitation.

Who Is Absent

The most notable names in the golf world are the ones who won't be at TPC San Antonio. Scottie Scheffler skipped the Valero entirely to prepare for Augusta — the world No. 1 will arrive at his title defense fully rested. Rory McIlroy is also absent, conserving energy as the defending Masters champion.

Their absences open the field considerably and give the 18 Masters-bound players present an opportunity to work on their games without the pressure of facing the two best players in the world that week.

What's Next

The Valero Texas Open runs through Sunday, April 5 — four days before the Masters begins. Whoever wins at TPC San Antonio will arrive at Augusta National with the smallest possible window to prepare and the largest possible momentum. Gary Woodland proved last week at Houston what a victory in the right week can mean for a player's state of mind going into the sport's most storied major.

The 2026 Masters is almost here. The Valero Texas Open is the last chapter before it arrives.
