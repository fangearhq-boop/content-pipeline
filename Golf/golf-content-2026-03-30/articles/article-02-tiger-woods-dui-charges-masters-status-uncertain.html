Tiger Woods DUI Charges Filed — Mugshot Released, Masters Status Increasingly Uncertain

By Golf Fanrecap Staff | March 30, 2026

The situation surrounding Tiger Woods has grown more serious overnight.

Martin County authorities released a mugshot following Woods' arrest on Friday, and formal charges were subsequently filed. Woods faces three counts: driving under the influence, property damage from striking another vehicle, and refusal to submit to a urine test. The 50-year-old was in the Martin County jail for approximately eight hours following an incident in which his Land Rover clipped a pressure-washing truck trailer near his Jupiter Island home, causing the vehicle to roll onto its side. Woods was not hurt and exited through the passenger window. The truck's driver was also uninjured.

The arrest is the second DUI-related incident of Woods' career. In 2017, he was found asleep behind the wheel of his car with the engine running, later explaining that a bad combination of prescription medications had caused the episode. He pleaded guilty to reckless driving in that case.

Masters Status

The immediate and overwhelming question from the golf world: will Tiger Woods play in the 2026 Masters, which begins April 9?

The answer, as of Sunday, is not clear. Woods is legally permitted to travel to and compete at Augusta National despite the pending charges. His physical condition — following a seventh back surgery in September and an Achilles tendon rupture in March 2025 that kept him off the professional circuit for the entire last season — had already been the limiting factor in any Masters participation discussion.

President Trump, appearing on Fox News, offered his assessment: "He will be there, but he won't be playing in it." Trump has no official role in Masters invitations, but his relationship with Augusta National's leadership gives his comment some informal weight. His use of "there" — as opposed to "not there at all" — suggests the expectation that Woods will attend Augusta in some capacity.

Woods is still scheduled to appear at Augusta on April 5 for an event at "The Patch," a municipal golf course near Augusta where his design team created a new short course. That appearance, as of Sunday, remained on the calendar.

Background and Context

Woods' last official tournament appearance was the 2024 British Open, where he missed the cut. He ruptured his Achilles tendon in March 2025 and did not play professionally for the entire season. In September, he underwent a seventh back surgery. His return to public golf included a TGL appearance just 48 hours before Friday's arrest.

He had been publicly working toward the Masters for months, telling reporters in recent weeks that he was doing everything possible to prepare for a return to Augusta — the course where he won five times and where, in many ways, his legend was built. Whether that preparation was close enough to competition-ready was uncertain even before Friday's arrest.

Woods was also nearing a soft deadline to decide whether to accept the role of US Ryder Cup captain for the 2027 matches in Ireland.

What's Next

The Masters begins April 9. In the ten days between now and the first round at Augusta National, the question of Tiger Woods' participation — on every level — will be the sport's most-discussed story. Whether he plays, attends without competing, or is absent entirely will shape the narrative of a major that was already loaded with storylines including Scheffler's title defense bid, McIlroy's defense of last year's Grand Slam-completing victory, and multiple Career Grand Slam chasers in the field.

The golf world will be watching what Tiger Woods does next. It always has been.
