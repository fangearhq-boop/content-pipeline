### STORY 1: Gary Woodland Wins Houston Open

**Long-Form Post** [8:00 AM ET]
Two weeks ago, Gary Woodland walked to a microphone and told the world about his PTSD. On Sunday, he answered everyone who needed to know what comes next.

Woodland won the 2026 Texas Children's Houston Open at Memorial Park Golf Course, firing a final-round 63 to close at 21-under 259 — a new tournament record on the course. He won by five shots over Nicolai Højgaard and claimed his first PGA Tour victory in nearly seven years, a span that included brain tumor surgery in 2023 and a publicly documented struggle with post-traumatic stress disorder.

The last time Woodland won on the PGA Tour was at the 2019 U.S. Open at Pebble Beach. The journey from that day to this one includes a brain tumor diagnosis, a surgery that changed his life, the daily work of recovery, and the quiet courage it took to tell that story publicly before heading into a final round with a lead to protect.

Jake Knapp played perhaps the most electric round of the day — firing a 62 to tie the lowest round in Houston Open history at Memorial Park — but it was Woodland's day. He earned $1,782,000 and, more importantly, earned himself a tee time at Augusta National in the Masters beginning April 9.

This is a win that resonates beyond the PGA Tour leaderboard. It's a reminder that comeback stories are real.

### STORY 2: Tiger Woods DUI Update

**Long-Form Post** [8:30 AM ET]
The Tiger Woods situation has grown more serious overnight.

Martin County authorities released a mugshot following Woods' Friday arrest, and formal charges have been filed: DUI, property damage stemming from striking another vehicle, and refusal to submit to a urine test. It is the second DUI-related arrest of Woods' life — in 2017, he was found asleep at the wheel of his car, later attributing the incident to a bad combination of prescription medication. That case was resolved with a guilty plea to reckless driving.

President Trump weighed in on Sunday. Appearing on Fox News, Trump said of Woods and the Masters: "He will be there, but he won't be playing in it." The White House has no official role in Masters invitations — but Trump's relationship with Augusta National chairman Fred Ridley gives his comments some weight.

Woods is legally permitted to play in the Masters if his physical condition allows it. He was still scheduled, as of Sunday, to appear at Augusta on April 5 for an event at "The Patch," a municipal golf course near Augusta where his design team has created a short course. Whether that appearance happens — and whether any competitive participation follows — remains unclear.

What is clear is that the cloud hanging over Augusta just got bigger. Tiger's presence at the Masters, even as a ceremonial starter or a spectator, has always been the week's defining narrative. This year, the story has taken a direction nobody wanted.

### STORY 3: Hyo Joo Kim Back-to-Back LPGA Wins

**Long-Form Post** [9:30 AM ET]
Hyo Joo Kim didn't just win the Ford Championship on Sunday. She made history.

The South Korean star won the LPGA Ford Championship presented by Wild Horse Pass at 28-under 260, defeating Nelly Korda by two shots for her second consecutive LPGA title — the week after winning the Founders Cup in Danville, California. It was the second consecutive week Korda finished second behind Kim, who is now the most in-form player on the LPGA Tour by a significant margin.

But the historic element came from Kim's scoring during the week: she became the first player in LPGA Tour history to shoot two rounds of 61 or better in the same tournament. She accomplished the feat in rounds one and three at Whirlwind Golf Club's Cattail Course, making birdie after birdie at one of the most demanding courses on the schedule.

Minami Katsu finished alone in third place at 23-under after a closing 65. Lydia Ko, who opened with a career-best 60 on Thursday to take the early lead, shot a 68 in the final round and fell to fourth at 20-under. Ko's week was still excellent — a top-five finish in her first full event of 2026 — but Kim simply ran away from the field once the final round began.

Two events. Two wins. Two trophies. Hyo Joo Kim is the story of the LPGA spring.

### STORY 4: Masters 10 Days Out — Field Set

**Long-Form Post** [10:30 AM ET]
Ten days. That's what separates the golf world from the 90th Masters Tournament.

The field for the 2026 Masters at Augusta National is now essentially complete. Gary Woodland's Houston Open victory on Sunday earned him the final automatic exemption, and Jake Knapp, Nicolai Hojgaard, Matt McCarty, and Daniel Berger were added to the field via the Official World Golf Rankings following the tournament. The field sits at approximately 92 players.

Scottie Scheffler enters as the +480 favorite. The world number one has won at Augusta twice — in 2022 and 2024 — and is seeking an unprecedented third title. Rory McIlroy, who completed the Career Grand Slam with last year's Masters victory, returns as the defending champion at +1000 odds. McIlroy skipped the Houston Open and plans to arrive at Augusta fully rested.

DeChambeau (+1200) and Rahm (+1200) are the next tier, with both having demonstrated the major championship pedigree and recent form to contend. DeChambeau in particular arrives with back-to-back LIV wins.

The one storyline that remains unresolved is Tiger Woods, whose DUI arrest on Friday and formal charges filed over the weekend have cast significant uncertainty over his participation. He is still scheduled to attend Augusta on April 5 for a separate project, but whether he will compete in the tournament itself is unknown.

Amazon Prime Video will broadcast the Masters internationally for the first time this year. The cameras have plenty to work with.

### STORY 5: Valero Texas Open Preview

**Long-Form Post** [1:00 PM ET]
The final chapter before Augusta begins Thursday.

The 2026 Valero Texas Open at TPC San Antonio's Oaks Course (April 2-5) is the last PGA Tour event before the Masters, and this year the stakes are unusually high. Twenty-two Masters invitees are in the field — players who have already secured their Augusta berths and are using the Valero as final preparation. But several others are competing with a different kind of urgency: the winner earns a Masters exemption, and some players near the top-50 bubble in the world rankings could punch their Augusta ticket with a strong performance.

Tommy Fleetwood, ranked No. 3 in the world, is the early betting favorite at TPC San Antonio — a wind-exposed, strategic par 72 that has historically suited players with strong ball-striking and course management. Collin Morikawa, Justin Rose, Hideki Matsuyama, and Ludvig Aberg are among the marquee names committed.

Rickie Fowler and Will Zalatoris are both in the field and both need a strong week to potentially secure a Masters invitation. Defending champion Brian Harman will also be on the course.

What you won't see: Scottie Scheffler and Rory McIlroy are both skipping the Valero to preserve energy for Augusta. Neither needs the points. Both need the rest.

The 2026 Masters begins April 9. The final preview begins April 2.

### STORY 6: Lydia Ko 4th at Ford

**Long-Form Post** [6:00 PM ET]
Lydia Ko opened the 2026 Ford Championship with a 60. She ended it in fourth place.

Ko's first-round 60 at Whirlwind Golf Club on Thursday was the low round of her professional career, featuring 12 birdies and a score that had the two-time Olympic gold medalist briefly pondering the magic number of 59. She held a one-shot lead entering Friday.

But the final 54 holes brought a different Ko. She remained in contention through the middle rounds before a 68 on Sunday — the highest score of any top-five finisher — dropped her four spots down the board. Hyo Joo Kim played the back nine with the aggressiveness of a player who has been doing this for weeks (because she has), and Ko could not match her.

Still, it is worth noting what this week represented. Ko returned from the off-season firing 60s at major LPGA events. A fourth-place finish with a career-best opening round is a strong start to any season. She has already targeted the next event in Las Vegas as her opportunity to go lower and stay there all four days.

### STORY 7: Grand Slam Chasers at the Masters

**Long-Form Post** [8:30 PM ET]
The 2026 Masters may be the most historically loaded major since Tiger's prime.

Three players — Xander Schauffele, Collin Morikawa, and Brooks Koepka — each arrive at Augusta National needing only a Masters title to complete the Career Grand Slam. The Grand Slam requires winning all four major championships at least once. Each of these three has won the other three. Augusta remains the last name missing from their personal history books.

The last man to check that box was Rory McIlroy — last April, in a dramatic playoff at Augusta that ended a decade-long chase. Before McIlroy, the sport waited 60 years from the time Gary Player completed it in 1965 for the list to grow.

Schauffele and Morikawa are both listed at +1600 in the latest Masters odds market. Koepka is a similar price. Bryson DeChambeau, not in the Grand Slam race but in the form of his career after back-to-back LIV wins, is the dark horse to watch at +1200.

Ten days until Augusta. The history books are open, and multiple hands are reaching for the pen.
