### STORY 1: Nelly Korda Wins US Women's Open

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Nelly Korda is a four-time major champion. And she earned that fourth title in the most dramatic fashion possible.

Sunday at Riviera Country Club, Korda stood over a 9-foot birdie putt on the par-5 17th hole with the U.S. Women's Open title hanging in the balance. She made it, took a one-shot lead heading to the 72nd hole, and then watched her par putt on No. 18 tremble around the lip of the cup — before falling in.

Final score: 8-under 276. Second place: Charley Hull and Gaby Lopez, one shot back at 7-under.

Korda finished with a final-round 2-under 69 to claim her first U.S. Women's Open title, her fourth career major, and her second major of the 2026 season. She also won the Chevron Championship earlier this year. At 27 years old with four majors and counting, the conversation about her place among the greats of women's golf is no longer hypothetical.

The near-miss that will hurt most belongs to Charley Hull. The Englishwoman made an eagle on the par-5 first, birdied the third, birdied the 10th, birdied the 11th — and briefly held the outright lead at the US Women's Open. Then came bogeys on Nos. 12 and 14. Then a birdie at 17. Then a clutch par save at the last. It was not enough. Korda's final putt fell in.

One shot. That's how close Charley Hull came to her first major title.

### STORY 2: JT Poston Wins Memorial in Marathon

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

JT Poston went to Muirfield Village this week ranked 74th in the FedEx Cup standings with zero top-20 finishes on the season.

He left with the biggest check of his career, a spot in the next three majors, and the title of Memorial Tournament champion.

The journey to get there was extraordinary. Weather delays pushed the tournament into a Sunday marathon — 13 holes of Round 3 completed in the morning, followed immediately by a full Round 4 in the afternoon. Poston held a four-shot lead entering Sunday morning. By Sunday afternoon, the lead was gone.

Ryan Gerard made a 40-foot birdie on 17 to briefly take the lead and closed with a 68. Poston, watching his advantage disappear, stepped up to the 18th hole in regulation and drained a 7-foot birdie to force a playoff. They returned to the 18th for the playoff. Poston survived the first hole. On the second extra hole, Gerard stood over a 6-foot par putt with the tournament on the line — and missed.

Poston made a three-foot par putt to win his fourth PGA Tour title.

Thirty-three holes on Sunday. A four-shot lead squandered and recovered. A $4 million payday. And a ticket to the US Open, just 10 days away.

### STORY 4: US Open Preview

#### Long-Form Post — 3:30 PM ET

Ten days from now, the best golfers in the world will arrive in Southampton, New York, for the 126th U.S. Open at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club.

The storylines coming in are as compelling as any major in recent memory.

Scottie Scheffler is chasing the career Grand Slam. He has won the Masters twice, plus the PGA Championship and the Open Championship — every major except the U.S. Open. If he wins at Shinnecock, he will become only the seventh golfer in history to complete the Grand Slam. And the final round is scheduled for Sunday, June 21 — which happens to be Scheffler's 30th birthday, and Father's Day.

Rory McIlroy arrives as the reigning Masters champion. For years, McIlroy carried the burden of the Masters as the one major he had not won. That burden is gone now. Golf analysts believe he is more dangerous without it — freed from the narrative, focused only on the golf.

The course itself will test every skill in the bag. Shinnecock's fairways will average 48 yards wide — one of the most generous widths in modern US Open history — but the green complexes have been described by Scheffler as "extraordinarily difficult." He said at Shinnecock, "once you start missing fairways you have no chance," and then added that the greens are where the real punishment lives.

The USGA received 10,201 entries — nearly an all-time record.

📅 June 18-21 | Shinnecock Hills, Southampton, NY | Par 70, 7,434 yards
