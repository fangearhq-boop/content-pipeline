JT Poston Outlasts Gerard in 33-Hole Memorial Marathon to Win His Biggest Career Title

By Marcus Cole | June 8, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

J.T. Poston began Sunday morning at Muirfield Village Golf Club with a four-shot lead and a championship within reach. Thirteen holes later — completing the weather-delayed third round — he had maintained that advantage and was ready to play his fourth round. By the time the final putts were struck, more than nine hours had passed, the lead had come and gone, and a two-hole playoff had produced one of the most complete clutch performances of the PGA Tour season.

Poston won the 2026 Memorial Tournament with a three-foot par putt on the second playoff hole, claiming his fourth career PGA Tour title and a $4 million prize in the process. The win came after a day that tested everything golf demands from a competitor.

The Day It All Happened

Weather delays had pushed much of the third round to Sunday morning, and Poston and Ryan Gerard emerged from that morning stretch still holding their positions near the top. The afternoon brought the full fourth round — and a complete reversal of the tournament's narrative.

Poston's four-shot cushion evaporated stroke by stroke. Gerard, playing the round of his competitive life, was relentless. He made a 40-foot birdie putt on the 17th hole to briefly take the lead — a moment that seemed to swing the entire tournament in his favor. He closed with a 68. On paper, it looked like enough.

Poston stepped up to the 18th hole trailing in regulation and drained a 7-foot birdie to force the playoff. That putt, made under maximum pressure with the tournament on the line, was the defining moment of a player who had arrived at Muirfield Village with zero top-20 finishes on the season.

The Playoff

Both Poston and Gerard returned to the 18th hole for the playoff. They survived the first extra hole. On the second, Gerard stood over a 6-foot par putt with a chance to extend the tournament — and missed. Poston made a three-foot par putt to win.

Thirty-three holes total on Sunday. Two rounds, two hours of morning completion, a full afternoon, and two playoff holes. It was the kind of golf Sunday that demands everything from a player — physical endurance, mental focus, and the ability to be clutch when the margins are smallest. Poston delivered all three.

The Reward

Beyond the $4 million prize, Poston's Memorial win punched his ticket to the U.S. Open and the next two major championships on the schedule. A win at a signature event like the Memorial carries exemptions that open doors — and for a player who entered the week without a top-20 to his name in 2026, those doors represent a significant career shift.

Wyndham Clark finished solo third at 11-under, one shot outside the playoff. Tommy Fleetwood and Sam Burns each finished two shots off the lead after both made bogeys on the par-4 17th in the final round.

What's Next

The U.S. Open begins June 18 at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York. J.T. Poston will be in the field, having earned his exemption with Sunday's win. The 126th U.S. Open is ten days away — and the Memorial champion will arrive as one of the most talked-about qualifiers in the field.
