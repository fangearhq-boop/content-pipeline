Three Champions, One Weekend: Golf's Best Days Before the U.S. Open

By Marcus Cole | June 8, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Professional golf produced one of its finest weekends in years over the past two days. Three tournaments concluded. Three champions were crowned. Three separate stories — each compelling on its own — converged on the same calendar weekend in June, ten days before the sport's most demanding major championship begins at Shinnecock Hills.

Korda at Riviera

Nelly Korda won the 81st U.S. Women's Open on Sunday at Riviera Country Club, shooting a final-round 69 to finish at 8-under 276. She birdied the 17th to take the lead, then watched a par putt on the 72nd hole wobble to the edge of the cup before falling in. One shot separated her from Charley Hull and Gaby Lopez at the finish.

It was Korda's fourth major and her second of the 2026 season — she also won the Chevron Championship. Hull's near-miss was painful: the Englishwoman eagled the first, led briefly in the final round, and then two critical bogeys (Nos. 12 and 14) cost her a major championship she came within one shot of winning.

Korda is 27 years old. The combination of four majors, two in one season, and the trajectory of her form positions her in the conversation about the greatest women's golfer of her generation.

Poston at Muirfield Village

At the Memorial Tournament, J.T. Poston won a golf marathon that tested every dimension of the game. Weather delays forced 33 holes of competition on Sunday. Poston held a four-shot lead in the morning, watched it disappear, drained a clutch birdie to force a two-hole playoff with Ryan Gerard, and then survived the playoff when Gerard missed a six-footer on the second extra hole.

The win was worth $4 million and a U.S. Open exemption. More than that, it was a career-defining performance — from zero top-20s entering the week to the trophy at one of the PGA Tour's signature events, on the most grueling Sunday of the golf season.

Hatton at Valderrama

And in Sotogrande, Spain, Tyrrell Hatton held off his own Legion XIII teammate Jon Rahm to win LIV Golf Andalucia at Real Club Valderrama. Hatton finished at 11-under 273, beating Rahm — who shot a closing 67 — by two shots. Legion XIII also won the team competition. Hatton dedicated the win to his life as a new father, competing at the highest level with the additional weight of sleepless nights and changed priorities.

Three tournaments. Three sports stories. A major champion, a PGA Tour breakthrough, and a LIV title at one of golf's most historic courses.

What's Next

The calendar now turns entirely toward Shinnecock Hills. The U.S. Open begins June 18 — ten days away — with Scottie Scheffler's career Grand Slam on the line, Rory McIlroy arriving as reigning Masters champion, and J.T. Poston in the field fresh off the biggest win of his career.

The sport just gave fans one of its best weekends of the year. It is about to deliver one of its defining weeks.
