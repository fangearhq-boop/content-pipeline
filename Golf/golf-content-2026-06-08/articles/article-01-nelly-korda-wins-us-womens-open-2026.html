Four Majors. Two This Season. Nelly Korda Wins the 2026 U.S. Women's Open at Riviera.

By Jake Torres | June 8, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The par putt on 18 wobbled. It circled the lip, appeared to be sliding away, and then — improbably, perfectly — fell into the cup. Nelly Korda, 27 years old, four-time major champion, exhaled and raised her arms in the California afternoon.

Korda won the 2026 U.S. Women's Open on Sunday at Riviera Country Club, shooting a final-round 2-under 69 to finish at 8-under 276. Charley Hull and Gaby Lopez both finished at 7-under 277, one shot behind. Korda's first U.S. Women's Open title is her fourth major overall — and her second of the 2026 season, following her Chevron Championship win earlier this year.

How She Won It

Sunday at Riviera came down to the final two holes. Korda stood on the 17th tee as part of a four-way tie for the lead. The par-5 gave her an opportunity, and she took it — a 9-foot birdie putt that dropped to give her a one-shot advantage heading to the 72nd hole.

The 18th at Riviera is a par-4 that has ended many tournaments, and Sunday was no different. Korda hit her second from 149 yards to 35 feet. Her approach putt stopped close. And then came the moment that defined the championship: a par putt that rolled to the right edge of the cup, seemed to hover, and then toppled in.

According to ESPN, "the last putt just falls in" — an accurate description of a finish that required every ounce of golf fortune to complement the skill that produced it.

Hull's Near-Miss

For Charley Hull, the result will sting. The English player gave herself every opportunity to win her first major championship.

Hull made an eagle on the par-5 first hole and followed with a birdie at No. 3. By the 10th hole she had birdied her way to a share of the lead, and another birdie at the par-5 11th briefly gave her the outright lead at the US Women's Open. She was playing some of the best golf of her career on the biggest stage.

Then came two bogeys — on Nos. 12 and 14 — that reordered the leaderboard. Hull gathered herself, birdied the 17th, and made a clutch par save at the 18th. But Korda had already made her birdie and her par. One shot separated Hull from the Harton S. Semple Trophy and the first major of her career.

The Korda Trajectory

Four majors at 27 demands historical context. Korda's rate of major accumulation — accelerating rather than plateauing — puts her among the elite players of women's golf. Two majors in a calendar year is a feat only the sport's greatest players accomplish consistently. Korda has now done it in 2026.

She won the Chevron Championship earlier this season. She has now added the U.S. Women's Open. Between those two, she proved that major moments do not intimidate her — they clarify her.

The prize of $2.5 million is secondary to the legacy being built. But it is worth noting: Korda left Riviera on Sunday with both.

What's Next

The LPGA Tour continues with its regular schedule through the summer. For Korda, the immediate question is whether she can add to her major total at the remaining 2026 LPGA major events. For Hull, Lopez, In Gee Chun (fourth), and Sei Young Kim (fifth), Sunday at Riviera was a near-miss that will serve as motivation heading into the season's final stretch.

The US Women's Open champion is Nelly Korda. Four majors. Two in one season. At 27 years old, with the best years still ahead.
