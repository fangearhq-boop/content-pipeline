New Father Tyrrell Hatton Wins LIV Golf Andalucia — Beats Teammate Rahm at Valderrama

By Ryan Calloway | June 8, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Tyrrell Hatton has been navigating new territory off the course — he recently became a father for the first time — and on Sunday at Real Club Valderrama, he demonstrated that the transition to parenthood has done nothing to diminish his game.

Hatton won the 2026 LIV Golf Andalucia, finishing at 11-under 273 at the historic Sotogrande course in Spain. He held off his own Legion XIII teammate Jon Rahm by two shots despite Rahm's closing 67, claiming a $4 million share of the $20 million purse.

A Wire-to-Wire Win at Rahm's Home Course

The setting added another layer to the result. Valderrama sits in southern Spain — the home region of Jon Rahm, arguably the most celebrated golfer this part of the world has produced. Rahm played on these fairways before he was a professional, and competing at Valderrama carries a weight for him that no other LIV venue can match.

Hatton was unfazed by geography or sentiment. He led the tournament from early on and shot a final-round 70 to close out a wire-to-wire victory. Rahm gave everything he had in that final round — the 67 was genuine — but the margin at the start of Sunday was enough to hold.

Abraham Ancer finished solo third at 8-under, with Sergio Garcia — who has his own deep connection to Valderrama — fourth at 7-under.

Legion XIII Wins Team Competition

The individual result was not the only victory for Hatton's team on Sunday. Legion XIII, the outfit shared by Hatton, Rahm, and their teammates, won the team competition as well — completing a clean sweep of the available honors at the 2026 LIV Golf Andalucia event.

For Legion XIII, built around two of the world's most talented golfers, the team result validates the roster construction. Individual performances have been split between Hatton and Rahm across the LIV season, but the combination has delivered where it ultimately matters: the leaderboard and the team standings.

The New Father Factor

Hatton did not shy away from acknowledging the personal context around his victory. Competing at the highest level in professional golf is demanding under any circumstances. Doing so immediately after becoming a first-time parent adds a layer of complexity — sleep disruption, emotional adjustment, divided focus — that most athletes acknowledge freely.

Hatton handled it. The 70 in the final round at a course as exacting as Valderrama, under the pressure of a closing Rahm charge, is a performance that speaks for itself. He won when it mattered.

What's Next

LIV Golf continues on its schedule through the summer. For Hatton, the win at Valderrama adds to a portfolio of results that have made him one of LIV's most consistent performers since the league's founding. For Rahm, the near-miss on home soil will fuel whatever comes next on the schedule.

The PGA Tour's major focus shifts to the U.S. Open, which begins June 18 at Shinnecock Hills. LIV players ineligible for the U.S. Open under current exemption rules will watch from outside that particular window — but the Valderrama result confirmed that, within LIV's circuit, the competition remains compelling.
