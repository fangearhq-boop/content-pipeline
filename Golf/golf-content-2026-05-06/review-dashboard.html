### Long-Form Post Eight days from now, the best golfers in the world will descend on a course most of them have never played competitively — and a venue that hasn't hosted a major since Eisenhower was in the White House. Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania, will host the 2026 PGA Championship from May 14-17. Designed by Donald Ross in 1928, the course is widely considered his masterpiece — Ross himself said as much. The last time the PGA Championship came here, Gary Player won in 1962. That's 64 years of waiting. And the course has been worth it. Aronimink is not a power golfer's paradise. It rewards precision — rolling fairways, strategic bunkers placed where mistakes are punished, and green complexes that reject sloppy approaches. The rough is tough. The pressure is relentless. In the Philadelphia-area summer heat, playing 72 holes here is a pure test of shotmaking. Scottie Scheffler enters as the defending champion at +350. Rory McIlroy, fresh off two consecutive Masters victories, is +650 to win his second PGA title. Bryson DeChambeau and Jon Rahm — both fresh from strong LIV campaigns — are listed at +1200 each. Cameron Young, who just won the Cadillac Championship, comes in at +1400. The 250th anniversary of American independence is being celebrated this year in the Philadelphia region. Aronimink, just 20 miles from Independence Hall, adds a historical dimension that no other major venue in 2026 can match. ### Image Post Caption The PGA Championship is 8 days away at Aronimink — Donald Ross's masterpiece. 64 years since the last PGA here. ### Long-Form Post Scottie Scheffler needs a win. Three tournaments in a row, three runner-up finishes. He lost by one shot to Rory McIlroy at the Masters. He lost in a playoff to Matt Fitzpatrick at Hilton Head. He lost by six to Cameron Young at the Cadillac Championship. The PGA Tour has officially recognized it: Scheffler is the first player since Sergio Garcia in 2014 to post three consecutive runner-up finishes. For most players, this kind of near-miss accumulation would be mentally devastating. For Scheffler, the argument can be made that it's actually a symptom of how good he is right now — he's always near the top, he just can't quite get over the line. But here's the thing: the 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club could be the perfect venue for him to end the streak. Aronimink is a Donald Ross design that demands ball-striking precision above all else. Scheffler's ability to control his flight, work the ball, and avoid large numbers makes him ideally suited to that style of course. He is also the defending PGA champion — and defending champions at Aronimink-style venues historically have a quiet edge from the experience of winning a major on demanding terrain. Nelly Korda's HOF Countdown — 4 Points Away After Historic 6-Start Run
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Nelly Korda is four points away from the LPGA Hall of Fame. In the history of women's golf, that sentence has rarely felt this inevitable. Through six starts in the 2026 LPGA season, Korda has not finished worse than second place. Not once. Three wins — including the Chevron Championship major — and three runner-up finishes. A record that mirrors what Annika Sorenstam accomplished in the opening of her dominant 2001 season, widely regarded as one of the greatest individual seasons in the history of women's golf. The last player to match Korda's level of top-2 consistency through six events was Sorenstam in that very year. Korda now has 18 career LPGA Tour victories — making her the youngest American player to reach that milestone since Nancy Lopez won her 18th at age 23 in 1980. She is currently 27 years old. The Hall of Fame requires 27 points, and she sits at 23. Four points. One more major win nets three points alone. At this pace, her 2026 season could be the year she crosses the threshold. For context, the LPGA Hall of Fame is widely considered the hardest sports Hall of Fame to enter among the major professional tour sports. Earning entry mid-season at age 27 would represent one of the fastest paths to induction in the organization's history. ### Image Post Caption Nelly Korda: 3 wins, 3 runner-ups, 6 starts. 4 points from the LPGA Hall of Fame.
