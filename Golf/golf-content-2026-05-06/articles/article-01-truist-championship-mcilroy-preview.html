Truist Championship Preview: McIlroy Seeks Record 5th Quail Hollow Title in Post-Masters Return

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 6, 2026

Rory McIlroy returns to the course he loves most — and has dominated most — as he opens his Truist Championship title defense on Thursday at Quail Hollow Club in Charlotte.

Of all the places Rory McIlroy could have chosen to make his first competitive start since defending his Masters title, Quail Hollow Club might be the most on-brand choice imaginable.

He has won there four times. In 2010, 2015, 2021, and 2024. No other player has won the Truist Championship — or its predecessor events at this venue — more than twice. McIlroy's relationship with Quail Hollow is not just dominance. It is ownership. And on Thursday, he will tee off in Round 1 of the 2026 edition seeking an unprecedented fifth title.

If he wins, the record will stand alone for as long as any of us can see clearly ahead.

McIlroy Returns From His Greatest Moment

The context of this week's return amplifies everything. McIlroy spent the last two weeks decompressing after one of the most remarkable back-to-back Masters victories in the tournament's modern era. Defending a title at Augusta National is something only three players had achieved before him. McIlroy made it look — if not easy — then entirely within his enormous capabilities.

Now he comes to Quail Hollow, a course that has seen him at every stage of his career. His first win at this venue came in 2010, when he was still a teenager with a future that dazzled but remained unproven. His most recent came in 2024, when he chased down a final-round lead with the kind of relentless birdie-making that has become his postseason signature.

The $20 million Signature Event purse makes this one of the richest weeks on the PGA Tour calendar. The field is stocked with the best players in the world. And the man they all need to beat is returning to his personal paradise.

The Green Mile Awaits

Quail Hollow's finishing stretch — holes 16, 17, and 18, collectively known as the "Green Mile" — is one of the most demanding closing runs in professional golf. The 16th and 17th are back-to-back par-fives that offer birdie opportunities but demand accuracy off the tee. The 18th is a par-four with a demanding approach to a heavily-bunkered green that has broken countless title charges.

McIlroy, characteristically, has played these holes better than almost anyone in the field's history. His ability to hit long irons and fairway woods precisely enough to attack the par-fives, combined with his iron play into the final green, gives him an inherent statistical advantage over the closing stretch.

This week's weather forecast adds another variable — Charlotte's May weather can be unpredictable, and moving tee times or suspended rounds could compress the schedule.

Scheffler's Subplot

No Truist Championship preview can avoid addressing the parallel storyline. Scottie Scheffler arrives at Quail Hollow off three consecutive runner-up finishes — the first player since Sergio Garcia in 2014 to achieve that somewhat unfortunate distinction. His ball-striking metrics remain elite, his mental fortitude is unquestioned, and he desperately needs a win before the PGA Championship arrives next week.

Quail Hollow rewards driving accuracy and iron play above pure distance — characteristics that suit both McIlroy and Scheffler. The question is which player's game shows up in its most complete form when the Green Mile arrives on Sunday afternoon.

What's Next

Round 1 tees off Thursday, May 7, with coverage on Golf Channel and various streaming platforms. Rounds 2-4 follow Friday through Sunday, with the final round on May 10. The winner claims a share of the $20 million purse and Signature Event points that significantly boost world ranking standing. After the Truist Championship concludes on May 10, the sport turns its full attention to the PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club, beginning May 14.
