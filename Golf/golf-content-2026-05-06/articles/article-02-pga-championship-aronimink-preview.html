PGA Championship 2026 Preview: Aronimink Is Donald Ross's Masterpiece, and It's Been Waiting 64 Years

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 6, 2026

The 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club opens in eight days — the first major at this Donald Ross design since Gary Player won there in 1962. Here's everything you need to know.

In 1928, the legendary golf architect Donald Ross completed the design of Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania, just outside Philadelphia. When he was finished, he made a declaration that no architect should make lightly: this was his masterpiece.

Ninety-eight years later, the golf world finally gets to see whether he was right — on the biggest stage.

The 2026 PGA Championship arrives at Aronimink on May 14, the first time this club has hosted the major since Gary Player claimed the Wanamaker Trophy there in 1962. In between: the 1977 U.S. Amateur, the 2003 Senior PGA Championship, a pair of AT&T National events, the 2018 BMW Championship, and the 2020 KPMG Women's PGA Championship. But no major. Not since Player. Not for 64 years.

What Makes Aronimink Different

The appeal of a Donald Ross design for major championship golf is fundamental: his courses don't reward brute force. They reward precision. Aronimink was built on rugged farmland with naturally rolling terrain, and Ross used those natural contours to create fairways that bank and shift, demanding thoughtful club selection from the tee and controlled ball flights into the approach.

The greens are classic Ross — elevated, crowned, with slopes that reject approach shots that lack precision. Landing zones are specific. Miss them in the wrong direction and you face recovery chips from impossible angles. The bunkers are strategically placed not as punishment traps but as incentives for players to attack specific lines — rewarding those who do and penalizing those who choose the conservative path incorrectly.

The course at Aronimink is a dialogue between the architect and the player. In the decades since the 1990s, restoration specialist Ron Prichard worked to return the course to Ross's original vision, using original drawings and field diagrams. What the world's best golfers will experience in May 2026 is as close to Ross's true intent as modern maintenance and standards allow.

The Field and the Favorites

Scottie Scheffler, the defending PGA champion, is listed as the early favorite at +350. Coming off three consecutive runner-up finishes, he enters this major with something to prove and a course profile that perfectly suits his strengths — controlled ball-striking, creative shot-shaping, and the mental composure to navigate precision demands over four rounds.

Rory McIlroy, fresh from defending his Masters title, is listed at +650. A PGA victory would give him the career Grand Slam — a milestone he came agonizingly close to achieving at previous editions. Aronimink's profile should suit him. His long iron play and ability to control trajectory under pressure are exactly what Ross's green complexes demand.

Bryson DeChambeau and Jon Rahm are listed at +1200 each — both coming off strong recent form in LIV Golf. Cameron Young, who won the Cadillac Championship wire-to-wire just over a week ago, enters at +1400 after the best start to a season in his career.

The full field includes 154 players, covering 15 PGA champions, 29 major champions, and 20 PGA of America Golf professionals.

A Civic Moment

The 2026 PGA Championship carries historical weight beyond golf. This year marks the 250th anniversary of American independence — celebrated most prominently in Philadelphia, the city where the Declaration of Independence was signed. Aronimink, located in Newtown Square just 20 miles from Independence Hall, will serve as a backdrop for that national celebration. The championship connects sport, history, and civic identity in a way that few events can claim.

What's Next

The 2026 PGA Championship begins with practice rounds May 11-13 before competitive play opens May 14. Rounds 2-4 follow May 15-17, with the champion receiving the Wanamaker Trophy on Sunday afternoon. Before all of that, the Truist Championship at Quail Hollow wraps up May 10 — giving players and fans a seamless bridge from one elite week of golf to the next.
