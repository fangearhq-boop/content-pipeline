LIV Golf Virginia Preview: Rahm vs. DeChambeau — Both Have Two Wins, Both Want Three

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 6, 2026

The individual championship race in LIV Golf 2026 comes to a head this week at Trump National Sterling, where Jon Rahm and Bryson DeChambeau both have two wins and both need Thursday's opening round to go their way.

There is a genuine title fight developing within LIV Golf's 2026 season, and it has two protagonists who both know how to win.

Jon Rahm and Bryson DeChambeau arrive at Trump National Golf Club in Sterling, Virginia for the Maaden LIV Golf Virginia event on May 7-10 with identical individual win totals: two each. Both lead — or co-lead — the individual standings. Both know that pulling ahead this week could set the tone for the entire back half of LIV's season. And both are also entered in the PGA Championship at Aronimink just a week later, where major championship relevance awaits.

This is a week where LIV Golf's individual title race crosses paths with major championship positioning. The stakes are layered.

Rahm's Form

Jon Rahm's most recent LIV victory came in Mexico City — a dominant 21-under performance that yielded a six-shot margin of victory. When Rahm is playing that freely, with that much distance and that sharp of a short game, he is as dangerous as any player in the world regardless of tour affiliation.

Rahm has settled into his LIV role with increasing comfort. He leads his team — Legion XIII — with authority, and his individual game has peaked at the right moments this season. Coming off the Mexico City win, he enters Virginia with high momentum and the kind of ball-striking consistency that will translate to any course layout.

Trump National Sterling is a parkland design set in the rolling Virginia hills, demanding tee-to-green accuracy and intelligent course management. Those are areas where Rahm excels.

DeChambeau's Counter

Bryson DeChambeau's two wins in 2026 have been built on a different template — power off the tee, par-5 dominance, and an increasingly refined short game that has elevated his all-around scoring. He remains one of the longest hitters in professional golf, and on a course with generous fairways, that advantage compounds quickly.

DeChambeau has also been talking more openly about his desire to compete in — and contend at — major championships, a goal that LIV Golf's recent partnership with the PGA of America helps facilitate. Winning Virginia would give him three wins in 2026, potentially the most of any player across any tour, and considerable confidence heading into Aronimink.

The Virginia Backdrop

Trump National Golf Club in Sterling, Virginia has become a regular LIV Golf venue, located in the DC metropolitan area and offering a high-profile market that LIV has invested in cultivating. The course itself — a strong parkland design with water features, mature trees, and tournament-tested conditions — provides a legitimately demanding test.

The team and individual standings will both move significantly with the Virginia result. LIV's format, in which team and individual competition run simultaneously, means that Rahm and DeChambeau are competing against each other and for their respective franchises at the same time.

What's Next

The Maaden LIV Golf Virginia event runs May 7-10 at Trump National Golf Club. Following Virginia, both Rahm and DeChambeau are scheduled to compete in the PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club, May 14-17 — giving this weekend's performance an even higher stakes context. The player who peaks in Virginia may carry that form directly into one of the most anticipated major championships in recent memory.
