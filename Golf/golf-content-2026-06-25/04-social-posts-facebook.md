A league that cannot guarantee its current season's events — while simultaneously facing the loss of its founding funder — is in a genuinely precarious position. The Louisiana postponement, in that context, is as much a symptom as an event.

### STORY 5: Niemann's Misconduct Penalty at the U.S. Open

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

The 2026 U.S. Open had no shortage of storylines — but the one that played out in Round 1 between Joaquin Niemann and a wayward 7-iron may have been the most unusual.

Niemann made a quadruple bogey on hole 6 during his first round at Shinnecock Hills. In a moment of frustration, he threw his club — approximately 50 yards down the fairway. The USGA official on the scene invoked Rule 1.2b: the sportsmanship and misconduct provision. Two strokes were added to Niemann's score on the spot. It was the first such penalty assessed at a major championship in the 2026 season.

Rule 1.2b is rarely applied. It exists for exactly this scenario — not accidental equipment damage, not a club thrown in celebration, but a controlled act of frustration that, in the rules committee's judgment, fell below the standards of conduct expected of a professional golfer at a major championship.

Niemann accepted the penalty without incident and played on. What happened next says something about his mental makeup: he came out in Round 2 and shot 65 — one of the week's lowest rounds. He made the cut. The penalty didn't define his week; it was merely a detour.

The incident raised broader questions about what the rules committee considers "misconduct" and how consistently it is applied across the tour. For now, the Niemann club-throw has its footnote in the 2026 U.S. Open record books.
