Korda's Historic Bid: Win No. 3 at KPMG Would Make Her First Since Inbee Park 2013

By Jake Torres June 25, 2026

The Record That's Been Waiting Since 2013

The last time a player won the first three major championships of an LPGA season, Inbee Park was doing it — taking the Kraft Nabisco, the U.S. Women's Open, and the Women's PGA in 2013 before going on to add a fourth. That record has stood for more than a decade. Nelly Korda is two thirds of the way to breaking it.

Korda won the 2026 Chevron Championship. She won the 2026 U.S. Women's Open. As of today — the first round of the KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota — she has the opportunity to do what no one has done since Park: win the first three majors of a year. The prize money alone reflects the magnitude of the week: a record $13 million purse with a $1.95 million winner's share.

What Hazeltine Brings

Hazeltine National is not a straightforward venue. The course has hosted multiple major championships — including the 2016 Ryder Cup and 2019 KPMG Women's PGA, where Hannah Green won. At approximately 6,807 yards, it plays as a genuine test of both length and precision. The par-72 layout rewards ball-strikers who can also manage course management decisions under pressure — a description that fits Korda as well as anyone in the field.

Green, who took the title here seven years ago, is also in the 2026 field following a stretch in which she won four consecutive events earlier this season. Her Hazeltine familiarity is a factor worth tracking as the week develops.

The Defending Champion

Minjee Lee arrives as defending champion having won the 2025 KPMG at PGA Frisco in Texas at -4 (284). Lee is one of the tour's most consistent performers at majors, and her 2025 win came in difficult scoring conditions. She does not enter Hazeltine in Korda's shadow — she enters it as the player who holds the title and knows what it takes to hold it for a full week under major conditions.

The field's depth is one reason Korda's pursuit is not a certainty. The KPMG consistently draws the LPGA's best, and a record purse year will only amplify that. But no storyline at Hazeltine this week competes with what Korda is chasing.

What's Next

Round 1 is underway today. Rounds 2 through 4 follow through Sunday. The first scoring data from Hazeltine will emerge Thursday afternoon, and with it, the first indication of where Korda stands relative to the rest of a field fully aware of what she is attempting. If she can maintain the form that produced two major victories in the first half of 2026, a third may follow — and with it, a place in LPGA history that has been unoccupied since 2013.
