LIV Louisiana Postponed, PIF Funding Ends After 2026 — LIV Golf's Most Turbulent Week

By Jake Torres June 25, 2026

The Postponement

LIV Golf's Louisiana event — scheduled to begin today — has been postponed. The league cited two factors: conflicts with FIFA World Cup scheduling and extreme summer heat in the region. The host community had paid $3.2 million to stage the event and will recover all but $2 million under the terms of the postponement agreement.

Taken in isolation, a weather-adjacent postponement might be routine. In the context of LIV Golf's current situation, it is another data point in a picture that is becoming significantly more complicated.

The Funding Crisis

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — the sovereign wealth vehicle that has funded LIV Golf since its founding, paying for player contracts, event purses, and operational infrastructure — has signaled it will end that funding support after the 2026 season. PIF's backing was not just a financial arrangement; it was the entire premise on which LIV was built. Without it, the league must generate its own economic model or find replacement capital.

Commissioner Scott O'Neil is leading an effort to raise $250-350 million in outside investment. New board members Gene Davis and Jon Zinman are part of a restructuring effort aimed at making LIV a commercially self-sustaining entity. Whether that is achievable within a relevant timeframe — and whether any institutional investor views LIV as a viable long-term business without PIF backing — are the central questions the league now faces.

The Season at Risk

O'Neil said publicly that he cannot guarantee LIV's final four events of the 2026 season will take place. For a league that signed players to contracts premised on event participation and prize money payouts, this creates real uncertainty for the players still competing on the LIV circuit. A league that cannot guarantee its current season's schedule while simultaneously looking for hundreds of millions of dollars in new investment occupies a structurally precarious position.

The Louisiana postponement, in that light, is not merely a scheduling inconvenience. It is a visible manifestation of an organization under pressure that extends well beyond any single event.

What's Next

LIV's ability to complete its 2026 schedule, secure outside investment, and present a viable ongoing operation to both players and potential commercial partners will define the next several months. The events scheduled for the remainder of the season remain on the calendar for now. Whether they take place — and under what financial conditions — will be answered one by one as summer progresses. The league that launched with enormous resources and enormous ambition now faces a future that requires building a different kind of foundation.
