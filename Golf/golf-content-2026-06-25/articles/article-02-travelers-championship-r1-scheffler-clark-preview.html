Travelers Championship Round 1: Clark Brings Shinnecock Momentum to TPC River Highlands

By Marcus Cole June 25, 2026

The Event and What's at Stake

The Travelers Championship at TPC River Highlands in Cromwell, Connecticut is one of the PGA Tour's most enduring events — and in 2026, it is a $20 million Signature Event drawing the Tour's top players for Round 1 on Thursday. The par-70 layout has historically produced some of the Tour's lowest scoring weeks, but with a Signature Event field and a first-round schedule that puts the best pairings out together, the early hours carry weight.

Keegan Bradley is the defending champion. Rory McIlroy is absent. The narrative center of gravity falls elsewhere — specifically, on two pairings that will generate the afternoon's most closely watched scorecards.

The Pairings to Watch

Scottie Scheffler — the world No. 1 — is out at 1:55 PM ET alongside Sam Burns. This is two of the game's most consistent performers sharing a tee box, with Burns coming off a runner-up finish at the U.S. Open at Shinnecock Hills last weekend. Scheffler has been the most reliable ball-striker on Tour through the first half of 2026, and TPC River Highlands' demand for precision off the tee plays into his strengths.

Wyndham Clark and Tommy Fleetwood follow at 2:05 PM ET. Clark won the U.S. Open wire-to-wire last week — finishing -4 over four rounds at Shinnecock Hills to claim his second major title. He is back in the field just one week later, which is either a sign of extraordinary confidence or a schedule commitment made before Shinnecock. Either way, he arrives as the most recent major champion in the field.

The Course and the Competition

TPC River Highlands plays to par 70, which means birdies come more freely here than at the U.S. Open setup Scheffler and Clark just navigated. The Connecticut venue's history is one of low-scoring weeks where a 62 can follow a 72 with little explanation beyond a hot putter. That volatility makes the Signature Event field — already constrained to the Tour's top players — more competitive on a round-by-round basis than a standard event.

The McIlroy absence removes one of the field's marquee narratives, but the Scheffler-Burns-Clark triangle carries its own weight coming directly out of a major. If the U.S. Open finishers maintain their form, the Travelers could function as a natural continuation of last weekend's conversation — Burns chasing Clark, Scheffler asserting himself as the Tour's most consistent player.

What's Next

First-round scores will emerge through Thursday afternoon and evening, with the featured pairings completing their rounds in late afternoon. Rounds 2 through 4 run Friday through Sunday. The Travelers has historically set up for Sunday drama at River Highlands, where the gallery and the course's closing stretch have produced some of the Tour's most memorable final-round moments. This week's edition adds a U.S. Open champion, a world No. 1, and a $20 million purse to that tradition.
