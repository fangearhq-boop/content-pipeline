Clark's Wire-to-Wire Win: How Wyndham Clark Won the 2026 U.S. Open at Shinnecock Hills

By Ryan Calloway June 25, 2026

The Result

Wyndham Clark won the 2026 U.S. Open Championship at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York, finishing at -4 (276 total) over four rounds. Sam Burns finished one shot back at -3 to take runner-up honors. Tom Kim and Scottie Scheffler completed the top-four positions. It was Clark's second U.S. Open title — his first came at Los Angeles Country Club in 2023 — and his first major championship victory since then.

How He Did It

Wire-to-wire wins at U.S. Opens are among golf's most demanding achievements. Shinnecock Hills is one of the oldest and most storied venues in American golf — a course defined by wind exposure, narrow fairways, and firm greens that punish imprecision as aggressively as any setup the USGA produces. Leading from the first round through the last requires both technical excellence and the ability to manage the inevitable pressure swings that come when the world's best players spend four days trying to close a gap.

Clark held through all of it. His game at Shinnecock — particularly his ball-striking in the middle rounds when the course was at its most demanding — gave him the foundation to protect the lead on Sunday. Burns pushed, but Clark answered.

A Second Major, A Different Player

Clark's 2023 U.S. Open win at Los Angeles was treated by many observers as a potential breakout moment that might not be repeated. What the 2026 Shinnecock win demonstrates is that Clark is not a one-major player. He is a player whose game consistently produces at the highest level under U.S. Open conditions — and whose wire-to-wire performance this year suggests a level of maturity and consistency that his first title only hinted at.

Burns, now a U.S. Open runner-up, continues to be one of the Tour's most consistent performers at major championships without yet converting that consistency into a first major title. Both players are in the field at the Travelers Championship this week, which means the competitive conversation between them extends directly into Thursday's round.

What's Next

Clark tees it up at the Travelers Championship at TPC River Highlands beginning Thursday — one week removed from his Shinnecock victory. His ability to maintain that form across consecutive weeks at contrasting venue types will be one of the summer's most closely watched questions. Whether he can carry U.S. Open momentum into a Signature Event that plays very differently from Shinnecock's demanding conditions will say something about whether 2026 is shaping into one of the great individual seasons in recent major history.
