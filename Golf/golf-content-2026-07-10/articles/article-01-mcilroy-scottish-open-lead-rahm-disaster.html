McIlroy's "Liberated" 65 Headlines Five-Way Scottish Open Tie — Rahm's Return Turns Into Disaster

By Jake Torres July 10, 2026

One Word That Explains Everything

Rory McIlroy has a word for how he has felt since completing the career Grand Slam at Augusta last year: liberated. On Thursday at the Renaissance Club in North Berwick, that word took the shape of a bogey-free 65. McIlroy shares the Round 1 lead at the Genesis Scottish Open at -5 with Patrick Cantlay, Tom Kim, Rasmus Hojgaard, and Bernd Wiesberger. He made an eagle on the par-5 tenth hole, went through 18 without a bogey, and described the performance afterward as "a complete performance" to Sky Sports. He said he feels "very liberated" since winning at Augusta, before adding that he "can't get complacent" — the self-awareness of someone who has learned how easily form can slip.

The Scottish Open is McIlroy's primary preparation for the Open Championship, which begins six days from now at Royal Birkdale. He has used this week before and it has suited him. A leading position after 18 holes with his best golf apparently available is not the worst way to spend the week before a major.

Five Share the Lead — and One Almost Had It Alone

Hojgaard's Thursday was the most dramatic of the co-leaders. The Danish player held the solo lead for about an hour late in the day before a double-bogey on the par-4 16th dropped him to -4. He immediately birdied 17 to rejoin the five-way tie. The sequence — lead, double-bogey, birdie — is the type of finish that either makes or breaks a tournament narrative. For Hojgaard, it kept him where the story is without costing him a share of it.

Tom Kim reached -5 through ball-striking that the field could not match, hitting 10 fairways and 15 greens in regulation. CBS Sports tracked him gaining 5 or more strokes on the field with his ball-striking alone. Cantlay and Wiesberger rounded out the group in quieter fashion, both converting quality ball-striking rounds into shares of the lead without the dramatics of Hojgaard or the narrative weight of McIlroy.

Koepka's Painful 66

Brooks Koepka played some of the best ball-striking golf of the day and came away with a 66 and a share of T6. He hit 14 greens in regulation and made 7 birdies. He also missed 4 putts inside 7 feet and came within 15 inches of a hole-in-one on the par-3 ninth. "It could have been a very, very good round," Koepka said afterward — a gracious understatement for what was a 66 built on shots that deserved at least a 64. His round is the type that serves as either motivation or distraction for the rounds ahead.

Scheffler 3 Back, and Something Bothered Him

Scottie Scheffler, the defending Open champion, played the type of round that rarely becomes a story: 4 birdies, 2 bogeys, 68 (-2). He is 3 shots back of the leaders heading into Round 2. He admitted after the round that something "hurt" him about his performance without identifying specifically what. Scheffler has not won a tournament since January 2026. He is on a course type that should suit him, one week before a major he won last year. A -2 after 18 holes is not a crisis. It is also not the form of someone playing his best golf.

Rahm's Return Goes Immediately Wrong

The most watched first-round in the field was Jon Rahm's. The Spanish player returned to co-sanctioned PGA Tour and DP World Tour competition Thursday for the first time since joining LIV Golf in January 2024 — his first regular-season PGA Tour start outside of major championships in over 18 months. He shot 73. He sits T137. He beat 12 players.

The round did not gradually deteriorate. It started badly and stayed that way. Rahm bogeyed the par-5 opening hole — the type of hole a player of his caliber expects to birdie. He made four more bogeys in his first 11 holes. Back-to-back birdies on 15 and 16 gave him something to hold onto, but they did not change the scorecard. He had been expected to contend for what would have been his first regular-season PGA Tour win since his move to LIV. Instead, he is on the wrong side of a cut line projected at approximately -1.

What's Next

Round 2 of the Genesis Scottish Open is underway Friday. McIlroy and the four co-leaders will need to defend their positions against a leaderboard that includes Scheffler, Koepka, and a field of players with Open Championship preparation in mind. The low 3 finishers who are not already exempt will earn entry into The Open at Royal Birkdale next week. Rahm will need to produce something exceptional in Round 2 to make the cut. For McIlroy, the trajectory could not be better: bogey-free 65, liberated mindset, one week from the Claret Jug. He came to Scotland to prepare for Birkdale. He has given himself excellent preparation material.
