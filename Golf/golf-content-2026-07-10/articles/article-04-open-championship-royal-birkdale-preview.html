Six Days Out: Royal Birkdale Has Been Rebuilt — Here's What the 154th Open Championship Field Will Find

By Jake Torres July 10, 2026

Not the Same Course

The 154th Open Championship begins July 16 at Royal Birkdale in Southport, England. The venue is the same. The course is not quite. Architects Tom Mackenzie and Mike Howard have made significant changes to Royal Birkdale since the club last hosted the Open in 2017, and the players arriving next week — some of whom are skipping this week's Scottish Open specifically to prepare at Birkdale — will find a layout with notable differences from the one Jordan Spieth won on nine years ago.

The bunkering throughout the course has been naturalized, integrated more organically into the dunescape rather than positioned as formal hazards at the edge of the fairway. More sand has been exposed between the dunes, making the rough terrain a more continuous presence for shots that miss the fairway. On the par-4 5th, the blind tee shot that once defined the hole has been eliminated — a change that makes the hole more legible and removes one of the more divisive strategic elements of the old layout. And the course has a new par-3 15th measuring 241 yards, a substantial test of iron precision that replaces the previous hole.

Who Is Chasing the Claret Jug

The field of 156 players includes essentially every significant name in world golf. Scottie Scheffler is the defending champion, having won the 2025 Open at Royal Portrush, and Golf Channel has him installed as the favorite at +550. A Scheffler victory would make him the first player since Tiger Woods in 2005 and 2006 to win consecutive Open Championships. The challenge for Scheffler is that he has not won a tournament since January 2026, and his first-round 68 at the Scottish Open this week — three back of the leaders — is not quite the form of a player in the middle of his best golf.

Rory McIlroy is the second choice at +850 and is currently co-leading the Scottish Open with a bogey-free 65. He completed the career Grand Slam at Augusta last year and has described feeling "very liberated" since — a mindset that appears to be expressing itself in his golf. His links credentials and Birkdale experience make him one of the most formidable players in the field. Tommy Fleetwood, a Southport native who grew up within driving distance of Royal Birkdale, is at +1400 and skipped the Scottish Open specifically to prepare at Birkdale this week. The home crowd factor for Fleetwood at a venue he knows intimately is real.

Spieth's Nostalgia Play

Jordan Spieth is not playing at the Renaissance Club this week. He skipped the Scottish Open — one of the primary preparation events for the Open Championship — in order to spend the week practicing at Royal Birkdale itself. The decision speaks to how much the 2017 Birkdale win means to him. That Open, with its dramatic sequence on the 13th tee and Spieth's walk into the crowd that briefly suggested a rules violation, is one of the defining moments of his career. He returns to Birkdale in 2026 as a player who knows the nuances of every bounce and break on a course that has been modified, but whose essential character remains the same links terrain he conquered nine years ago.

The Last Chance Qualifier

Before the field arrives at Royal Birkdale for the Open, there is one more sorting event. The Last Chance Qualifier takes place Monday, July 13, at Royal Birkdale — 12 players competing over 18 holes for the final spot in the Open field. Confirmed entrants include Aldrich Potgieter, Wesley Bryan, and Matti Schmid, among others. Whoever emerges from that 18-hole test on Monday earns the 156th and final spot in the Claret Jug field and begins the week's preparation with three days until the first round begins.

What's Next

The Last Chance Qualifier on Monday will deliver the final piece of the Open field. The Scottish Open concludes Sunday, and its low three finishers who are not already exempt will also earn entry into next week's field. The Open Championship begins Thursday, July 16, with free practice starting Wednesday. The leaderboard that emerges from Birkdale by Sunday evening will be shaped by how the rebuilt course plays, whether the weather the Ardennes coast sometimes delivers finds its way to Lancashire, and whether Scheffler, McIlroy, Spieth, or someone from further down the odds list handles the rebuilt Birkdale best.
