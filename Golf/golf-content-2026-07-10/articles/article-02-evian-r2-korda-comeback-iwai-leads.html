Iwai's Dominant 63 Leads Evian Championship — Korda 11 Back as Grand Slam Hopes Dim

By Marcus Cole July 10, 2026

The Number at the Top

Aki Iwai shot a 63. Eight birdies, no bogeys, the lead at a major championship by two shots. The Japanese player, ranked No. 24 in the world, has given herself the best possible start at the Amundi Evian Championship and made the $9.1 million LPGA major's narrative simple heading into Round 2: can anyone catch her? She made back-to-back birdies on 11 and 12, another pair on 15 and 16, and closed with a birdie on 18 to reach -8 and establish what feels like a very comfortable position at the top of the leaderboard. If she holds anything approaching this form, this tournament will not be particularly close.

France Has Its Story

For the galleries at the Evian Resort Golf Club, the most compelling moment of Thursday's first round came from Perrine Delacour. The French player, ranked No. 116 in the world, shot a 65 and sits second at -6. She birdied the 1st hole, made an eagle on the par-5 15th, and birdied both 17 and 18 to send the French crowd home with a name to cheer heading into Friday. According to Golf Magazine's French coverage, it was the best first-round score by a French player at Evian in the tournament's history. For a major that takes place in France and draws significant domestic interest, Delacour's round gave the week a local story that none of the pre-tournament favorites could provide.

The -5 Cluster

Five players sit at -5 after Round 1: Charley Hull, Jeongeun Im, Mao Saigo, Haeran Ryu, and Maja Stark. Hull's presence is worth noting — the English player has built form heading into the back half of the LPGA season and a strong Evian result would validate that trajectory. Haeran Ryu is perhaps the most interesting name in the group. She won the KPMG Women's PGA Championship last month, which had the effect of blocking Nelly Korda's calendar Grand Slam attempt. Now she sits five shots off the lead at the major Korda needs most. Lottie Woad, another English player, sits at -4 after a 67 that included a 20-foot eagle putt on the par-5 ninth hole after reaching the green in two.

Korda's Difficult Math

Nelly Korda arrived at the Evian Championship with significant stakes attached to her week. She has already won two of the four LPGA majors in 2026 — the Chevron Championship and the US Women's Open — and a victory at Evian would put her one win away from the career Grand Slam. The math entering the week was straightforward: win here and the final piece is only the AIG Women's British Open in late July.

That math got considerably more difficult on Thursday. Korda opened with a 74 — double-bogeys and early bogeys that never allowed her to find footing in a round that ended at +3 and 11 shots off the lead. She has won the Evian Championship twice previously and knows this course, but coming back from 11 shots in a 72-hole major requires conditions that do not typically materialize. Korda would need Iwai to collapse, the field to compress, and her own game to produce three rounds that her Thursday round did not suggest she has available this week.

What's Next

Round 2 is in progress Friday at Evian-les-Bains. The midway cut will reduce the field before the weekend. For Korda, the immediate priority is making the cut and keeping any mathematical hope alive — though she is already facing circumstances that require near-perfection for the remaining holes. For Iwai, the question is whether a two-shot lead built on a bogey-free 63 holds up when the rest of the field has a chance to answer. Delacour will carry the French crowd's hopes into Round 2. The Evian Championship wraps up Sunday — the same day the Open Championship field completes final preparations at Royal Birkdale six days before tee time.
