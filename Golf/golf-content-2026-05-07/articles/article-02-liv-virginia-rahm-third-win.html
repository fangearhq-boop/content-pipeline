Rahm Leads LIV Individual Race by 302 Points — Virginia Is His Chance at a Third 2026 Win

By Jake Torres | May 7, 2026

Golf · LIV Golf · Virginia

The 2026 Maaden LIV Golf Virginia tournament teed off today at Trump National Golf Club in Potomac Falls, Virginia, and no player arrives with more momentum than Jon Rahm. The Legion XIII captain and two-time 2026 LIV Golf individual champion leads the season-long individual standings with 779 points — a margin of 302 over second-place Bryson DeChambeau — and at Trump National this week, he has an opportunity to extend that advantage further.

Trump National Golf Club Washington D.C. is a Tom Fazio design that stretches to 7,679 yards along the Potomac River, playing as a par 72. The course is long and demanding off the tee, with power rewarded on several of its straightaways before the premium shifts to precision on approach. It is exactly the kind of layout where Rahm — who combines elite distance with one of the most reliable iron games on the LIV circuit — is particularly dangerous.

Rahm's 2026 Season in Context

Rahm's individual title run has been the defining narrative of LIV Golf's 2026 season. His first victory came in Hong Kong, establishing him at the top of the standings. His second arrived at Mexico City, where he closed with a bogey-free final round to win by six shots and cement his dominance. The combination of those two victories and the points structure has given him a lead that, barring a dramatic series of events, is likely to be maintained through Virginia and beyond.

DeChambeau, his closest challenger, missed the Mexico City final round after withdrawing with a wrist injury. He has been cleared to compete this week and is expected to be available at full strength, but the disruption in his preparation and match fitness creates some uncertainty about where his game stands after the layoff. At 476.90 points, he remains the only realistic challenger to Rahm in the individual race — but the math requires Rahm to falter and DeChambeau to dominate for the gap to close significantly.

The Field Beyond the Top Two

The weekly format of LIV Golf means that individual event victories — worth significant points regardless of season standing — attract players across the field who are not in contention for the season title. Dustin Johnson and his 4Aces teammates, along with the rest of the 48-player field, will be competing for the event win and the team title that comes with it. Virginia's power-friendly layout opens the door for long hitters throughout the field.

What's Next

Round 1 is underway today with Round 2 on Friday, the conclusion on Saturday. LIV Golf's 54-hole format means the tournament moves quickly, and the leaderboard can shift substantially between rounds. For Rahm, the primary objective is a third 2026 individual title — one that would push his lead toward insurmountable territory. For DeChambeau and the rest of the field, the window to make a statement at Trump National opens and closes within 54 holes.
