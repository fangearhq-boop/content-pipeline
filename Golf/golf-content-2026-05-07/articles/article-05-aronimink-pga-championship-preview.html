Donald Ross Called It His Masterpiece. 64 Years Later, the PGA Championship Returns to Aronimink.

By Jake Torres | May 7, 2026

Golf · PGA Championship · Course Analysis

There is one week between now and the 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania, and the anticipation for what that week will bring is palpable throughout professional golf. When Donald Ross completed his design of Aronimink in 1928 and declared, "I intended to make this my masterpiece," he set a standard for the course that the intervening century has only reinforced. The PGA Championship last visited Aronimink in 1962 — when Gary Player won — and in the 64 years since, the course has aged into legend.

The championship begins May 14 and concludes May 17. An estimated 200,000 spectators are expected to attend over the four days, drawn by the combination of the major championship itself and the opportunity to walk a layout that most golf fans have only ever seen in photographs.

What Makes Aronimink Demanding

Ross designed dozens of courses during his career, but Aronimink remains distinct even within his own catalog. The bunkering is the most elaborate of any Ross design — strategic, punishing, and placed to penalize the kinds of aggressive shot selections that might succeed elsewhere. Miss the fairway at Aronimink and the bunkers demand a perfect recovery. Miss the green and the short-game demands are severe.

The par-3s define the course's character above all. Three of the four measure over 210 yards, requiring long, precise iron shots to surfaces that offer limited receptive areas. In major championship conditions — with rough grown to penalizing height and pins placed in the most difficult locations — these holes can become back-breakers for players whose long-iron game is not at its sharpest.

The Field and the Favorites

Scottie Scheffler enters as the clear favorite at approximately +350. The world No. 1 has not won in three consecutive starts — runner-up at the Masters, the RBC Heritage (playoff loss), and the Cadillac Championship — but his underlying game metrics remain elite, and Aronimink's precision demands align well with his strengths as a ball-striker.

Rory McIlroy, this week occupied at the Truist Championship in Charlotte before heading to Newtown Square, will be among the top contenders. His performance at Augusta — back-to-back Masters victories — demonstrated his ability to execute on championship-caliber courses under maximum pressure. The PGA Championship field also includes 29 major champions, all seeking to add a third, fourth, or fifth major to their résumé on one of the most demanding courses any major has used in decades.

What's Next

The 2026 PGA Championship begins a week from today. Practice rounds begin in the middle of the week. By the time the opening tee shots are struck on Thursday, May 14, the golf world will have its full attention fixed on Newtown Square, Pennsylvania — and on a Donald Ross design that has been waiting 64 years to show a new generation of major champions exactly why its creator called it his masterpiece.
