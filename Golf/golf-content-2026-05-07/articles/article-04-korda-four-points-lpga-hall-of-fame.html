Four Points from Forever: Nelly Korda Is Closing In on the LPGA Hall of Fame

By Ryan Calloway | May 7, 2026

Golf · LPGA · Player Feature

Nelly Korda needs four more points to join the LPGA Hall of Fame. That fact, considered alongside the season she is having, makes the inevitable feel imminent. After three victories and three runner-up finishes in her first six starts of 2026, Korda sits at 23 of the 27 points required for induction — the closest she has ever been to one of professional golf's most exclusive distinctions.

The three 2026 wins came at the Hilton Grand Vacations Tournament of Champions, the Chevron Championship, and the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba in Mexico. They represent three different types of victories across three different venues, confirming that Korda's dominance is not circumstantial or course-specific. She wins on courses that reward power. She wins on courses that demand precision. She simply wins.

A Season That Echoes Sorenstam

The statistical comparison to Annika Sorenstam is both earned and extraordinary. Sorenstam's 2001 LPGA season was one of the greatest in women's golf history — a campaign in which she began with nothing worse than a runner-up through the first six events of the year. Korda has matched that standard exactly: three wins, three runner-ups, zero finishes outside the top two through six starts in 2026.

That comparison would be remarkable for any player. For Korda, it carries additional weight given the historical context: Sorenstam is the benchmark against which LPGA greatness is often measured, and matching even one of her seasonal statistical lines places Korda in rarefied air.

The Youth Record

Another piece of context: Korda is now the youngest player since Nancy Lopez in 1980 to reach 18 career LPGA victories. Lopez's era was defined by a level of dominance that reshaped women's professional golf. Reaching the same career milestone at the same relative age says something significant about Korda's trajectory and what her total career win count might eventually reach.

The LPGA Hall of Fame requires 27 points, which are earned primarily through tournament wins, major victories, and Player of the Year awards. With 23 points, Korda needs the equivalent of two more significant wins or one major combined with other points. At her current rate of roughly three wins per eight events, the remaining four points could arrive within this calendar year.

What's Next

Korda's LPGA schedule will continue through the remainder of the 2026 season with multiple opportunities to add to her Hall of Fame point total. The next LPGA major — the U.S. Women's Open — typically provides the most points available in a single event. For Korda, each tee time now comes with a dual purpose: win the event, and close the final distance to the Hall of Fame door. Four points. That is all that stands between Nelly Korda and forever.
