The Aronimink Deep Dive — What Donald Ross's Masterpiece Means for the 2026 PGA Championship
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post One week from today, the most historic course in modern major championship golf opens its gates for just the second time. The PGA Championship begins May 14 at Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania — and it will be the first time this legendary Donald Ross design has hosted the tournament since Gary Player won there in 1962. That gap — 64 years — is remarkable. A course that its own architect described as "my masterpiece" has been waiting since the Eisenhower era for its moment back on the biggest stage. Ross completed Aronimink in 1928. It features what experts consider the most elaborate bunkering of any of his designs — which is saying something, given that Ross is responsible for some of the most revered layouts in golf history. Three of the course's par-3s measure over 210 yards. The greens are undulating and demanding. Errant shots do not get away cheaply. Scottie Scheffler (+350) is the betting favorite, and it's easy to see why: Aronimink demands the kind of precision ball-striking and course management that defines his game. Rory McIlroy, fresh off back-to-back Masters victories, will be among the top contenders as well. An estimated 200,000 spectators are expected to attend. For the first time in six decades, the world's best golfers will try to conquer Donald Ross's masterpiece. Who wins the 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink? 🏆
