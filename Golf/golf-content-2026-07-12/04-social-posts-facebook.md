### STORY 3: Open Championship Preview — Royal Birkdale

**Long-Form Post:**

Scottie Scheffler and Rory McIlroy are co-favorites at +700, per CBS Sports and SportsLine. Tommy Fleetwood is next at +1400, followed by Matt Fitzpatrick at +1500. The field contains 156 players. LIV qualifiers in the field include Joaquin Niemann and Lucas Herbert (direct exemptions) and Tom McKibbin (LINK Hong Kong Open).

Scheffler is defending after winning the 2025 Open. A second consecutive title would be the first back-to-back Open since Tiger Woods in 2005-06. McIlroy, meanwhile, enters off a T2 at the Scottish Open — and a victory at Royal Birkdale would give him seven majors, tying Harry Vardon for the most by a British golfer in history.

Royal Birkdale last hosted The Open in 2017, when Jordan Spieth won. The course is known for its 100+ bunkers, its premium on precision over distance, and its status as one of the shorter Open venues. All of that favors iron players over bombers.

First tee shots are Thursday, July 16. The champion is crowned Sunday, July 19.

### STORY 5: LIV Golf Survival Crisis

Annual losses have run approximately $500-600 million per year. The circuit has filed WARN Act layoff notices in both the United States and the United Kingdom — the formal legal notification that mass layoffs are coming.

LIV has engaged Ducera Partners to handle a fundraising effort seeking $250-400 million in outside investment from new backers. Without that capital, the circuit faces the possible cancellation of its final two events of the 2026 season. The next scheduled event is July 23 in England.

Lucas Herbert won LIV Virginia last week with a score of 24-under — the 27th different LIV winner across the tour's history. 4Aces GC won the team title. On the legal front, World Golf Group and PGL are pursuing a lawsuit against LIV for $210-630 million. CEO Scott O'Neil is described as being on an investment roadshow.

PIF invested $5 billion. LIV built a competing tour, signed the world's biggest names, and changed professional golf's financial landscape. Whether any of it continues past 2026 is now genuinely uncertain.
