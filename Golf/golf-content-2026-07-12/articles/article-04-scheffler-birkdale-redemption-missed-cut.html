Defending Champion Scheffler Heads to Birkdale After Snapping 78-Cut Streak at Scottish Open

By Marcus Cole | July 12, 2026

PGA Tour / Scottie Scheffler / Open Championship

Scottie Scheffler is the World No. 1. He is the defending Open champion. And he just missed the cut for the first time since 2022.

Scheffler shot 68-72 at the Genesis Scottish Open, finishing at even par — two strokes outside the cutline of -2. The result ended a streak of 78 consecutive made cuts on the PGA Tour, the fifth-longest in tour history. His previous missed cut: the 2022 FedEx St. Jude Classic, 1,428 days ago.

The Streak in Context

78 consecutive made cuts places Scheffler in rarefied company. The list of longer streaks is short: Tiger Woods (142), Byron Nelson (113), Jack Nicklaus (105), Hale Irwin (86). Scheffler's streak, which ran from mid-2022 through July 2026, covered multiple major victories, a World No. 1 ranking that has extended to 197 consecutive weeks as of this writing, and the most dominant stretch of golf of his career.

It is over now. At The Renaissance Club in North Berwick, Scheffler's approach play failed him — his statistics for the week show 11 of 26 fairways hit, 23 of 36 greens in regulation, and an estimated loss of approximately two strokes on approach shots across the two rounds. That kind of disparity in his ball-striking — the sharpest weapon in his arsenal — explains the result.

The Open Is Four Days Away

The missed cut at the Scottish Open would be a larger story in any other week. This week, it is context for what comes next.

Scheffler is the defending Open champion, having won the 2025 Open Championship. He is co-favorite at +700 alongside Rory McIlroy to win at Royal Birkdale, which hosts The Open for the first time since Jordan Spieth's 2017 victory. A second consecutive Open title would be the first back-to-back by any player since Tiger Woods in 2005-06.

The parallel is not accidental. Scheffler is the kind of player who processes missed cuts differently than most — the 2022 FedEx St. Jude, his last one before this week, did not derail what became the most dominant stretch of his career. There is no reason to expect a different response now.

What's Next

Practice rounds at Royal Birkdale begin this week. First competitive rounds are Thursday, July 16. Scheffler will enter the week as one of two co-favorites, with 156 players in the field and a major title on the line.

The streak is gone. The Open is ahead. For Scheffler, the second variable is the only one that matters.
