2026 Open Championship Preview: Scheffler and McIlroy Co-Favorites at Royal Birkdale Starting Thursday

By Jake Torres | July 12, 2026

The Open Championship / Major Preview

The 154th Open Championship begins Thursday at Royal Birkdale Golf Club in Southport, England — and it arrives with storylines that no scriptwriter could have arranged better.

Defending champion Scottie Scheffler, who just missed the cut at the Scottish Open for the first time in four years, returns to The Open as co-favorite. Rory McIlroy, who finished T2 at the Scottish Open and carries momentum into Birkdale, is co-favorite alongside Scheffler at +700. Tommy Fleetwood is next at +1400, followed by Matt Fitzpatrick at +1500. The field contains 156 players competing across July 16-19.

The Course

Royal Birkdale last hosted The Open in 2017, when Jordan Spieth won in one of the most memorable final rounds in recent major history. The course is known for its distinctive profile among Open venues: more than 100 bunkers, a premium on precision over raw distance, and a layout that is shorter than many of the Open rota's other venues.

That combination — accuracy requirements, bunker trouble, shorter overall distance — tends to favor iron players over bombers. Scheffler's approach game is among the finest on the PGA Tour. McIlroy's ball flight is well-suited to links conditions. Fleetwood and Fitzpatrick are English players who know links golf intimately.

The Storylines

Scheffler's back-to-back bid is the headline narrative. A second consecutive Open Championship would be the first back-to-back by any player since Tiger Woods won in 2005 and 2006. The missed cut at the Scottish Open adds a layer to the story — Scheffler has never been better than when he has something to prove.

McIlroy's pursuit of history runs parallel. A victory at Royal Birkdale would give McIlroy seven major championships, tying Harry Vardon's record for the most major titles by a British golfer. McIlroy has won four majors and has been one of the most consistent performers at The Open across his career.

The LIV presence adds context. Joaquin Niemann and Lucas Herbert earned direct exemptions to the field. Tom McKibbin qualified via the LINK Hong Kong Open. LIV players competing at The Open remains one of golf's recurring points of discussion, given the PGA Tour's restrictions on their members playing alongside LIV competitors in other contexts.

The Field

Beyond the co-favorites, Tommy Fleetwood enters as one of the top English players with significant links experience. Matt Fitzpatrick's precision iron play suits Birkdale's demands. The full 156-player field represents a global cross-section of professional golf, with DP World Tour and international tour qualifiers filling out the field alongside the PGA Tour contingent.

What's Next

First tee shots are Thursday, July 16. Qualifying is complete. The field is set. The champion will be decided Sunday, July 19.

Scheffler and McIlroy are the numbers. Royal Birkdale is the stage. The 154th Open Championship is four days away.
