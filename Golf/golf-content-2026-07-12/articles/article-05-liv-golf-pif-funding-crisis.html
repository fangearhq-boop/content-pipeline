LIV Golf Faces Existential Crisis — PIF Ends Funding After 2026, Layoffs Filed, Events at Risk

By Ryan Calloway | July 12, 2026

LIV Golf / Business

LIV Golf is in a fight for its survival — and the timeline is accelerating.

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF), which has backed LIV Golf since the circuit's 2022 launch with more than $5 billion in total investment, announced in April 2026 that it will end its financial support after the current season. In the months since, the circuit has filed WARN Act layoff notices in both the United States and the United Kingdom — the legally mandated notification that mass layoffs are coming — and is now seeking $250 to $400 million in outside investment to continue operations.

The Financial Reality

LIV Golf has operated at annual losses of approximately $500 to $600 million since its launch. PIF absorbed those losses across four seasons, totaling more than $5 billion in cumulative investment, as part of Saudi Arabia's broader sporting and economic diversification strategy.

With PIF's exit, LIV has engaged Ducera Partners to manage an emergency fundraising effort. The circuit needs between $250 million and $400 million from new investors to continue beyond the current season. Without that capital, the circuit's final two events of 2026 are reportedly at risk of cancellation. The next scheduled LIV event is July 23 in England.

Competition Continues

Despite the financial turmoil, LIV events are still happening. Lucas Herbert won LIV Virginia last week at 24-under par, becoming the 27th different LIV winner in the circuit's history. 4Aces GC claimed the team title. Herbert also qualified for The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale through a direct exemption.

CEO Scott O'Neil is described as being on an active investment roadshow, seeking new backers to replace PIF's funding. The circuit's legal exposure has also expanded: World Golf Group and PGL are pursuing a lawsuit against LIV Golf with damages estimated at $210 to $630 million.

What It Means

LIV Golf's 2022 launch upended professional golf's financial ecosystem. The circuit signed Dustin Johnson, Phil Mickelson, Bryson DeChambeau, Brooks Koepka, Cameron Smith, and dozens of other established tour professionals with guaranteed contracts, forcing the PGA Tour to respond with its own financial restructuring and player payment increases.

Whether that disruption continues past 2026 now depends on whether Ducera Partners can find new investors willing to absorb hundreds of millions in annual losses. The sport that LIV Golf forced to change is still standing. LIV itself is looking for a reason to continue.

What's Next

The July 23 England event is the immediate test. If LIV can secure funding before then, the season continues. If not, the calculus around the final events changes quickly. A circuit that filed layoff notices while simultaneously hosting professional golf events is operating in an unusual position — and the resolution will shape the sport's competitive landscape through the end of the decade.
