MacIntyre Wins Back-to-Back Scottish Opens at -18 — McIlroy and Gotterup T2 at -15

By Marcus Cole | July 12, 2026

Genesis Scottish Open / PGA Tour / DP World Tour

Robert MacIntyre is a back-to-back Scottish Open champion — and he did it on home soil, at the same course, in dominant fashion.

MacIntyre finished the 2026 Genesis Scottish Open at The Renaissance Club in North Berwick at -18 (262 total), winning by three strokes over Rory McIlroy and Chris Gotterup, both of whom finished T2 at -15 (265). The $9 million event, held July 9-12, served as the final tournament before the Open Championship at Royal Birkdale starts Thursday.

Back-to-Back at Renaissance Club

MacIntyre previously won the 2024 Scottish Open at this same venue, making him the first player to win back-to-back Scottish Opens at The Renaissance Club. The achievement carries particular weight given MacIntyre's Scottish roots — winning the Scottish Open at home, with home fans, in back-to-back years is the kind of story the sport rarely produces.

The Renaissance Club sits in North Berwick, Scotland, a links-style layout along the Firth of Forth. MacIntyre navigated its demands across all four rounds, posting a final score that left the field three shots behind. Round 3 was delayed by fog — a reminder of the conditions Scottish coastal courses routinely deliver — but MacIntyre handled the interruption and came back to close strongly on Sunday.

McIlroy's T2 Heading Into The Open

Rory McIlroy's T2 finish at -15 will be discussed almost as much as MacIntyre's victory. McIlroy came close but couldn't catch the champion, finishing three strokes back. He now heads directly to Royal Birkdale as co-favorite at +700 for the Open Championship alongside defending champion Scottie Scheffler.

Chris Gotterup joined McIlroy at T2, matching the Northern Irishman's -15 total. The American has been consistent on the DP World Tour circuit and will have momentum of his own heading into Birkdale.

What's Next

The 154th Open Championship begins Thursday, July 16 at Royal Birkdale Golf Club in Southport, England. MacIntyre will enter the week as a name to watch after back-to-back wins on links-adjacent terrain. McIlroy arrives with momentum and a point to prove. Scheffler, the defending champion, needs to respond after missing the cut at the Scottish Open.

The Open is four days away. MacIntyre just showed he belongs in the conversation.
