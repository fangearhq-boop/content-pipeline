Grace Kim Wins Back-to-Back Evian Championships in Playoff — Ryu's Record 60 Not Enough

By Ryan Calloway | July 12, 2026

Amundi Evian Championship / LPGA Major

Haeran Ryu shot 60 in Round 3. She set the women's major championship record for the lowest single round in history. She entered Sunday with a three-shot lead. Grace Kim won anyway.

Kim (Australia) claimed the 2026 Amundi Evian Championship in a playoff over Jeeno Thitikul (Thailand), defeating her on hole 18 after the two players finished regulation tied. The tournament, held July 9-12 at Evian Resort Golf Club in Évian-les-Bains, France, was the fourth of five women's majors in 2026 and carried a $9.1 million purse.

Ryu's Historic Round — and Collapse

Ryu's third round was genuinely historic. Her score of 60 (-11) is the lowest round ever recorded in a women's major championship — a mark that stands alone in the history of the LPGA's biggest events. She entered the final round having won the KPMG Women's PGA Championship in June, meaning she was attempting to win two consecutive majors.

Sunday changed the story. Ryu's three-shot lead evaporated. The exact details of her final-round collapse left her unable to hold off Kim and Thitikul, who both matched her at the top of the leaderboard and forced a playoff. Kim then won that playoff on hole 18, claiming the championship.

Ryu's record round will remain in the record books. The Evian Championship is someone else's.

Kim Defends Against the Same Opponent

Grace Kim won the 2025 Evian Championship in a playoff over Thitikul. She won the 2026 Evian Championship in a playoff over Thitikul. The repetition is remarkable — the same two players, the same tournament, the same format, the same outcome.

For Thitikul, who has been one of the most consistent players on the LPGA Tour, finishing runner-up to the same player in back-to-back Evian playoffs represents a difficult pattern. For Kim, it is confirmation that she has found a way to win when it counts most.

Notable Absences

Nelly Korda missed the cut at Évian — her first missed cut since the 2024 Women's PGA Championship. Korda had been one of the most dominant players on the LPGA Tour through 2024 and 2025, making her absence from the weekend rounds notable.

What's Next

The fifth and final women's major of 2026 remains ahead. Kim enters the second half of the LPGA season as a two-time major champion and a back-to-back Evian winner. Ryu, despite the Sunday collapse, has already won a major this year. Thitikul continues to pursue a first major title.

This week at Évian produced one of the stranger results of the 2026 season — a record-setting round from Ryu, a dramatic Sunday collapse, and a back-to-back champion who simply does not lose this event.
