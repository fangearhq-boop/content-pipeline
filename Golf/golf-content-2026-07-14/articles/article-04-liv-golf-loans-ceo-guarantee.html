LIV Golf Is Running on Loans Now — And the CEO Won't Promise All 4 Remaining Events Happen

By Ryan Calloway | July 14, 2026

New filings reveal the Public Investment Fund has shifted from equity investor to lender. CEO Scott O'Neil declined to guarantee all remaining 2026 events when asked directly by CNBC.

When the chief executive of a professional sports league is asked whether all remaining events on the schedule will actually be played, and responds by talking about what a good investment the league would be for potential backers, that is not an answer. That is a very specific kind of non-answer, and it tells you more than a direct response would have.

That is what happened when CNBC asked LIV Golf CEO Scott O'Neil if all four remaining 2026 events would be played. He did not say yes. He pivoted to a sales pitch. "What I can guarantee is a heck of a return if you come invest in this business," he said. A guarantee of investment returns is not a guarantee that events take place.

Equity Becomes Debt

The financial backdrop to O'Neil's evasion is a debenture filed June 4, 2026, between LIV Golf Ltd and Wedge IP Management Co., a Public Investment Fund entity. A debenture is a secured loan — not an equity investment. The structure of the relationship between LIV Golf and PIF has changed. PIF is no longer investing in LIV Golf. PIF is lending to LIV Golf.

Under this arrangement, PIF sent approximately $66 million in May and $130 million in June. The remaining $400 million promised for the 2026 season has not arrived. Four events remain on the schedule. The math requires that money.

WARN Act Notices

Approximately July 8, LIV Golf filed Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act notices — the federal filings that require employers to give 60 days' notice before significant workforce reductions. WARN Act notices are not layoffs. They are not confirmations that layoffs will happen. But they are legal obligations triggered by a reasonable possibility that layoffs could happen, and they place the potential reduction window beginning in September — aligned precisely with the final events of the 2026 LIV season.

LIV has said there are "no current changes to workforce." That statement is consistent with the WARN Act notices — the notices are forward-looking, not a description of current status. But the notices themselves are on the public record.

What Remains on the Schedule

The four remaining LIV Golf events in 2026 are:

  • LIV UK at JCB Golf Club — July 23-26
  • LIV New York — August 6-9
  • LIV Indianapolis — August 20-23
  • LIV Team Championship Michigan — August 27-30 (featuring a $40 million team purse)

The Team Championship carries the largest prize pool of any remaining event and represents the culmination of the LIV season. Whether that event happens as scheduled, in modified form, or not at all is now a question without a guaranteed answer from the league's own CEO.

The DeChambeau Factor

This week at The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale, Bryson DeChambeau is in the field. His LIV contract expires at the end of the 2026 season. He has publicly floated the possibility of competing independently in 2027, pursuing only the four major championships without a league affiliation.

DeChambeau is not the only LIV player whose contract status becomes a question if the league's financial footing remains uncertain. The roster of top-flight players signed to LIV deals — players who left the PGA Tour in significant part because of the financial security those deals represented — now faces a 2026 offseason where the league that was supposed to be their stable home is borrowing to make payroll and cannot guarantee its own remaining events.

The business story of LIV Golf has been evolving since its launch. The shift from equity to debt financing, a CEO unwilling to guarantee events on the record, and WARN Act notices in the window of the final season events represent a significant escalation of the questions that have surrounded the league's long-term viability. Whether the four remaining events happen, and what LIV Golf looks like in 2027, now both carry genuine uncertainty attached to them.
