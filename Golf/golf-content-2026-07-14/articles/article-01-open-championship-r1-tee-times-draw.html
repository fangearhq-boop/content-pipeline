Open Championship R1 Groups: McIlroy With Schauffele and Fitzpatrick, Scheffler With DeChambeau

By Ryan Calloway | July 14, 2026

The Round 1 pairings for the 154th Open Championship at Royal Birkdale are confirmed, featuring marquee groupings and the LCQ's dramatic final qualifier.

Two days before the 154th Open Championship begins at Royal Birkdale, the Round 1 tee times have been set and the groups confirmed. The draw has produced some of the most compelling first-round pairings of the year.

The Marquee Groups

Rory McIlroy will tee off on Thursday afternoon, grouped with Xander Schauffele and Matt Fitzpatrick at approximately 3:15 PM BST. It is arguably the deepest three-ball in the draw — three major champions, three elite ball-strikers, three players who are genuine contenders this week.

Scottie Scheffler, the defending Open champion, gets the morning wave. He is paired with Bryson DeChambeau and Tyrrell Hatton, teeing off around 9:58 AM BST. The contrast in styles makes the group fascinating to watch: Scheffler's metronomic precision alongside DeChambeau's power-first approach.

The Spieth-Fleetwood-Rahm group (10:09 AM BST) may be the one golf fans most want to follow on foot. Jordan Spieth won The Open at Royal Birkdale in 2017. Tommy Fleetwood grew up in Southport, next door to the course. Jon Rahm has become one of the world's best links players. The group has the feel of a proper stage moment.

In the afternoon, Shane Lowry (2019 Open champion) groups with Brooks Koepka (five major titles) and Aaron Rai, who won the 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink in May. That is three major champions in a single group heading to the first tee.

The Final Qualifier: Joe Dean Earns His Spot

The last place in the 156-player field went to English professional Joe Dean, who claimed it with a 2-under par 68 in the Last Chance Qualifier held at Royal Birkdale on Monday.

Dean's round included birdies at the par-3 7th and the 11th, followed by an eagle at the par-5 14th — a three-shot swing on a single hole that proved decisive. He finished clear of South African Aldrich Potgieter (even par) and Englishman Andrew Wilson (1-under), who both missed out on the final spot. Amateur Championship runner-up Matt Moloney and YouTube golf star Wesley Bryan also competed in the qualifier but did not advance.

Dean now joins the most prestigious field in golf for the week — a story that, whatever happens Thursday through Sunday, will not be forgotten.

Full Schedule at Royal Birkdale

The 154th Open Championship begins Thursday, July 16, with the first groups teeing off around 1:35 AM ET. The final round is scheduled for Sunday, July 19. Royal Birkdale's course includes modifications since the 2017 Open — a former par-3 14th was removed, a par-5 15th relocated, and a new par-3 15th added by architects Mackenzie and Ebert.

What's Next

Thursday's first round will give the first real indication of which players are handling Birkdale's demands — over 100 bunkers, firm links turf, and the coastal winds of Southport that can change the challenge from hour to hour. Scheffler in the morning and McIlroy in the afternoon means two of the world's best playing in completely different conditions. That gap in draw luck could matter come Sunday.
