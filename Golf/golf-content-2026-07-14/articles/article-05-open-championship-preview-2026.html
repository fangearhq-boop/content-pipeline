2026 Open Championship Preview: Scheffler Defends, McIlroy Hunts History, Rai Is the Wild Card

By Jake Torres | July 14, 2026

The 154th Open Championship at Royal Birkdale begins Thursday. Here is why this is one of the most compelling Opens in years — and the case for every major contender.

Royal Birkdale has not hosted The Open Championship since 2017, when Jordan Spieth won in a final round that produced one of the stranger Sundays in recent major history. In the nine years since, the course has been modified — one hole removed, two reshaped, the par-3 15th rebuilt — and the world ranking has rearranged itself enough times that the 2026 field looks almost nothing like the one that stood on that first tee nine years ago.

What has not changed is the course's essential character: over 100 bunkers, firm links turf, coastal winds off the Irish Sea that can reshape a round in under an hour. Birkdale rewards the straight driver and punishes the creative guesser. It is a championship test, and it is ready.

Scottie Scheffler: The Case for the Defending Champion

Scheffler won The Open at Royal Portrush in 2025 by four shots. He knows how to handle links golf at the major level — his patience, his ball-striking precision, and his ability to manufacture pars from difficult positions are exactly the skills that hold up on a course like Birkdale. He missed the cut at the Genesis Scottish Open last week, ending a 78-event made-cut streak, but used the extra time to prepare at Birkdale directly. His early-wave draw pairs him with Bryson DeChambeau and Tyrrell Hatton — a morning round that may offer calmer conditions before the afternoon wind builds.

If Scheffler wins, he becomes the first back-to-back Open champion since Padraig Harrington won at Carnoustie in 2007 and defended at Royal Birkdale in 2008 — this same course. That context amplifies everything he does this week. He enters as a co-favorite at approximately +700.

Rory McIlroy: The Case for the Co-Favorite

McIlroy won The Open Championship in 2014. He completed the career Grand Slam with a victory at the 2025 Masters, then won the 2026 Masters to become only the third man after Jack Nicklaus and Tiger Woods to win back-to-back green jackets. He now holds seven major championships. An Open title this week would bring him to eight and position him for a conversation about the greatest era of his career.

McIlroy groups in the afternoon with Xander Schauffele and Matt Fitzpatrick — three major champions, three elite ball-strikers, three players who are genuine contenders this week. He is accustomed to pressure on the biggest stages, and his links game has been elite throughout his career. Co-favorite at +700 alongside Scheffler.

Aaron Rai: The Case for the Wild Card

Rai won the 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink in May. He is an Englishman playing The Open Championship two months after his first major title, which makes this a different kind of tournament for him than it would have been a year ago. The PGA Championship victory — the first major won by a player of Indian descent, and only the second time an Englishman has won the PGA Championship in over a century — changed the nature of the conversation around him.

Rai groups with Shane Lowry (the 2019 Open champion) and Brooks Koepka (five major titles). Three major champions in one three-ball in the afternoon. He is comfortable in that company now in a way he was not before Aronimink. His ball control off the tee and his patience on approach shots translate well to links golf.

Tommy Fleetwood: The Case for the Hometown Favorite

Fleetwood grew up in Southport, the town adjacent to Royal Birkdale. He skipped the Scottish Open to prepare specifically at this course. He has more rounds at Birkdale under his belt than any other player in the field. He is a two-time major runner-up chasing the first major title of his career. The last Englishman to win The Open was Nick Faldo in 1992. If any week is the one for Fleetwood, it is this one, on this course, in front of this crowd.

He opens grouped with Spieth (the 2017 Birkdale champion) and Rahm in the morning. If Fleetwood goes low in the first round, the electricity in Southport will be audible.

Royal Birkdale's Setup

The course has been modified since 2017. The former par-3 14th was removed. The par-5 15th was relocated and renumbered as the 14th. A new par-3 15th was added, designed by the architecture firm Mackenzie and Ebert. The par of the course remains 70.

With over 100 bunkers, fairways that reward accuracy, and links rough that complicates any recovery, Birkdale sets a premium on precision off the tee and clean iron play into the greens. The coastal winds can change rapidly, and The Open's split-wave format means morning and afternoon players may encounter dramatically different conditions over the first two rounds.

First groups tee off around 1:35 AM ET Thursday. The 154th Open Championship is here.
