That level of back-to-back hasn't been achieved at The Open since Harrington.

A debenture filed June 4, 2026, between LIV Golf Ltd and Wedge IP Management Co. (a PIF entity) officially converts the relationship from investor to lender. PIF has already sent approximately $66 million in May and $130 million in June under this arrangement. The remaining $400 million promised for the 2026 season has not arrived.

When CNBC asked CEO Scott O'Neil directly whether all four remaining 2026 events would be played, he did not say yes. That is not a guarantee any events will happen.

The WARN Act notices filed on approximately July 8 — requiring 60 days notice before workforce reductions — place the potential layoff window as early as September, which would align with the final events of the season.
