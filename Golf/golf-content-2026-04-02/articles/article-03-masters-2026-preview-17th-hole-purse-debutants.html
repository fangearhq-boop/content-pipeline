Masters 2026: 7 Days to Augusta — New 17th Hole, $21M Purse, and 22 Debutants

By Marcus Cole April 2, 2026

The 90th Edition Arrives With Changes

Seven days from now, the first tee shots of the 90th Masters Tournament will crack through the Georgia morning air at Augusta National Golf Club. The field will be led by the world's best players. The galleries will be the most selective in golf. And for the first time since any current Masters champion last played a practice round, the 17th hole will be 12 yards shorter.

Augusta National's decision to trim Nandina — the 17th — to 450 total yards is the kind of adjustment the club makes with deliberate, low-key precision. It is not a drastic change. It is a recalibration, designed to ensure the hole retains its intended challenge in an era of longer hitters and more sophisticated equipment. Also debuting this week: a new three-story Player Services Building, the latest addition to Augusta's infrastructure.

The Money and the Numbers

The 2026 Masters carries a $21 million purse — an increase from the $20 million offered in 2025. Twenty-two players will make their Masters debut this week, including Jacob Bridgeman, Ben Griffin, Chris Gotterup, and Kristoffer Reitan. Each of them arrives at Augusta having earned their invitation through the grind of a full PGA Tour season; each of them will see the course for competitive play for the first time on Thursday morning.

Before any of them tees off, Tuesday brings the Masters Club Dinner — the annual gathering of past champions hosted this year by defending champion Rory McIlroy. The dinner has been held since 1952, and the menu choices of each year's defending champion have become a tradition within the tradition. McIlroy's selections for 2026 have been kept, as is customary, from public knowledge until after the fact.

The Favorites and the Field

Scottie Scheffler enters as the +550 favorite — seeking to become only the ninth player in Masters history to win three or more green jackets, and to add a third title in four years. Bryson DeChambeau and Jon Rahm are tied at +1000, followed by McIlroy at +1100. Xander Schauffele (+1500) and Ludvig Åberg (+1600) round out the top tier of contenders. Tiger Woods, whose participation status remains a subject of separate coverage, is listed at +20000.

The Grand Slam chasers — Morikawa, Schauffele, and now Koepka, who returned to the PGA Tour this week — add a layer of historical resonance to the proceedings. If any of the three wins at Augusta, they join an extraordinarily exclusive company: the career Grand Slam champions of the Masters era.

What's Next

Practice rounds begin Monday, with the Champions Dinner on Tuesday and the Par 3 Contest on Wednesday. The first competitive round begins Thursday, April 9. The final round takes place Sunday, April 12. All four rounds will be broadcast on Amazon Prime Video in their debut year as the Masters' U.S. broadcast partner. For those tracking the Valero Texas Open this week, the winner earns an automatic invitation and will join the field in Augusta by Sunday night.
