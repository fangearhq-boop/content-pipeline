The Data Case for Morikawa: He Has the Best Augusta Record Among All Grand Slam Chasers

By Ryan Calloway April 2, 2026

The Quiet Frontrunner

The conversation around Grand Slam chasers at the 2026 Masters tends to center on the biggest names and the most dramatic recent headlines. Brooks Koepka just rejoined the PGA Tour. Xander Schauffele won the U.S. Open and has been a consistent Masters contender. The narrative gravitates toward the loudest story.

But if you strip the narrative away and look only at what players have done at Augusta National specifically, one name stands above the others: Collin Morikawa. Among all active players still chasing the career Grand Slam, Morikawa has produced more top-5 finishes (two), more top-10 finishes (three), and more top-25 finishes (five) at the Masters than any of his peers. He has recorded a top-15 finish in each of his last four Masters appearances — a standard of consistent excellence that no other Grand Slam chaser can match at this specific venue.

Why Augusta Fits Morikawa

Augusta National rewards, above all else, elite iron play. The wide fairways that flank the course's famous holes forgive the occasional errant drive. What they do not forgive is imprecision into greens that slope dramatically, protect tucked pin positions aggressively, and punish approaches that arrive from the wrong quadrant of the fairway.

Morikawa's iron play is, by most analytical measures, among the best in the world. His approach shot distances, proximity to pin, and greens-in-regulation percentages consistently rank him among the tour's elite. His 2020 PGA Championship win at Harding Park and his 2021 Open Championship title at Royal St. George's both came through sustained, methodical ball-striking precision across four rounds. That is precisely the formula Augusta National rewards in its champions.

What He Needs

Morikawa needs only the Masters to complete the career Grand Slam, joining an exclusive group that includes Tiger Woods, Jack Nicklaus, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Gene Sarazen, and Rory McIlroy. His odds of +1500 reflect the depth of this particular Masters field, not any deficiency in his Augusta qualifications. If anything, those odds represent value in a player who has been consistently near the top of the Augusta leaderboard for four consecutive years and whose course profile fits the tournament's demands as well as anyone in the field.

What's Next

Morikawa will arrive at Augusta National for practice rounds Monday. His preparation approach — meticulous, studied, methodical — is the same one he brings to every major. The question for the 90th Masters is whether his best Thursday-through-Sunday week coincides with the week of April 9-12. His record suggests the odds are better than most people realize.
