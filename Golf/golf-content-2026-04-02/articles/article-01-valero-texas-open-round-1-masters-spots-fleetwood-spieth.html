Round 1 at Valero Texas Open: Masters Spots on the Line as Fleetwood, Spieth, and Bubble Players Tee Off

By Ryan Calloway April 2, 2026

The Last Chance Saloon

Every golf tournament matters. But the Valero Texas Open, which began its first round Thursday at TPC San Antonio's Oaks Course, matters differently. The winner of this $9.8 million event earns an automatic invitation to the Masters — and with the 90th edition of Augusta's crown jewel beginning seven days from now, the urgency on the first tee in San Antonio this morning was palpable.

For the bubble players ranked just outside the top 50 in the world — the players fighting to get their name on the Masters field sheet before the cutoff — the Valero Texas Open is not just another PGA Tour stop. It is the last realistic opportunity to force their way into the week that defines careers.

Fleetwood, Åberg, and the Favorites

Tommy Fleetwood enters as the pre-tournament favorite, a position earned through consistently strong recent play and a game that translates well to the Oaks Course's demands. Alongside him at the top of the board is Ludvig Åberg, who also arrives in form and with significant Masters experience to draw upon.

Jordan Spieth, listed at +1600, brings something neither Fleetwood nor Åberg can claim: he won this exact tournament in 2021. His connection to TPC San Antonio is genuine, and the course architecture — requiring precise iron play and thoughtful strategy off the tee — mirrors the attributes that make Spieth such a dangerous competitor at Augusta. A Valero win would give him the kind of momentum, and the kind of headline, that transforms a Masters week narrative before it has even begun.

The Bubble Players

The subplot that will define the week for casual fans and hardcore enthusiasts alike is the Masters bubble. Rickie Fowler, ranked No. 65 in the world, is fighting for Augusta. Billy Horschel (No. 94), Tony Finau (No. 107), Tom Kim (No. 126), and Will Zalatoris (No. 299, continuing his return from injury) are each in the field for the same reason: a Valero title solves everything.

For Fowler, in particular, the Masters carries emotional weight that has built across a career of near-misses. For Zalatoris, still rebuilding his ranking after injuries, any week when he can post a number and remind the world of his ceiling is a worthwhile investment. The stakes in each of their scorecard boxes go well beyond the $9.8 million purse.

What's Next

Round 2 continues Friday, with the cut expected Saturday and the final round on Sunday. The winner will join the Masters field and tee it up at Augusta National starting Thursday, April 9. For anyone tracking the Masters storylines — and at this point, nearly every golf conversation leads back to Augusta — the Valero Texas Open leaderboard over the next four days is required reading.
