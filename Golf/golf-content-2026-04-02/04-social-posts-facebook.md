### STORY 6: McIlroy's Back Injury Is the Masters' Biggest Question Mark

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

One year ago, Rory McIlroy stood on the 18th green at Augusta National and completed the career Grand Slam — the moment an entire sport had been anticipating for over a decade. Now he arrives as defending champion with a different kind of story to tell.

A nagging back injury has prompted McIlroy to skip pre-Masters events to manage his recovery and prioritize preparation for Augusta. The approach is prudent — Augusta demands a full range of motion through impact, and any restriction in flexibility shows up in both distance and shot shape under the most demanding conditions in golf.

His betting odds of +1100 place him as the fourth favorite — behind Scheffler, DeChambeau, and Rahm, but ahead of the broader field. That's not the position of a prohibitive favorite. The back is why.

McIlroy is 36 years old, at the peak of his competitive intelligence, and defending a title he won in one of sport's more emotional fashion. If healthy, he is one of the two or three most likely winners at Augusta. The question that will not be answered until Thursday morning's opening tee shots is whether that "if" applies.

### STORY 7: Chevron Championship — Can Hyo Joo Kim Make It Three in a Row?

#### Long-Form Post — 8:30 PM ET

Hyo Joo Kim enters the Chevron Championship as the hottest golfer in the world — and that distinction applies across all of professional golf, not just the LPGA Tour.

Kim has won back-to-back LPGA events, capturing both the Founders Cup and the Ford Championship. At the Ford Championship, she made LPGA history by becoming the first player ever to shoot two 61s in the same tournament, finishing at 28 under par. Nelly Korda — herself one of the most dominant players in the sport's recent history — has been her runner-up in both events.

The Chevron Championship (April 23-26) is the first women's major of the 2026 season. It is also, now, the tournament in which Kim has a chance to accomplish something nearly unheard of in major championship golf: walk in as a three-time defending tour winner and claim a major in the process.
