KPMG Women's PGA 2026 Preview: Korda Chases Three-Peat at Hazeltine as Major Season Peaks

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 21, 2026

The 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship begins Thursday June 25 at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota, and the question dominating the preview conversations in women's golf is as straightforward as it is compelling: can Nelly Korda win it a third consecutive time?

Two consecutive KPMG titles already represent one of the most dominant stretches of major championship golf in recent memory on the women's tour. Three would cement a legacy.

Korda's Case

Nelly Korda has established herself as the defining player in women's golf over the past two seasons. Her major championship pedigree, combined with a power game that suits Hazeltine National's demanding layout, makes her the default favorite entering the tournament.

Hazeltine National is not a course that rewards finesse over distance. The Minnesota layout has hosted multiple US Opens, PGA Championships, and the Ryder Cup at the men's level — its length and course design challenge players to hit every club in the bag with precision. Korda's combination of power and ball-striking accuracy gives her structural advantages over much of the field.

The question is not whether Korda is capable of winning a third KPMG. It's whether the rest of the field — which includes 11 past champions — can produce a collective performance strong enough to beat her.

The Field Around Her

The 2026 KPMG Championship field includes 11 past champions of the event — a depth of experience that makes any given week at a women's major genuinely unpredictable. Defending champion Minjee Lee will look to build on her 2025 performance and mount a challenge to Korda's two-year dominance of the event.

England's Lottie Woad, competing this week at the Meijer LPGA Classic, could arrive at Hazeltine on Sunday evening with a tournament victory in hand — significant momentum for one of the tour's most promising young players. The Meijer LPGA Classic winner also earns the final remaining field spot in the KPMG championship, meaning the Michigan tournament's result today directly shapes who competes in Minnesota starting Thursday.

Hazeltine National

The championship's venue deserves its own mention. Hazeltine National Golf Club carries significant history across all of professional golf — its design rewards complete players and punishes those who can only execute one dimension of the game. For a women's major, it represents one of the most comprehensive tests of the season. The Minnesota course in June typically plays in firm, fast conditions with wind that can shift the challenge dramatically from morning to afternoon.

What's Next

The 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship begins Thursday June 25 at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota. The purse is $12 million. First-round coverage begins Thursday with all rounds available on Golf Channel and Peacock. The tournament concludes Sunday June 28. Nelly Korda, Minjee Lee, and the complete KPMG field will be announced in the coming days as final roster spots are confirmed following today's Meijer LPGA Classic result.
