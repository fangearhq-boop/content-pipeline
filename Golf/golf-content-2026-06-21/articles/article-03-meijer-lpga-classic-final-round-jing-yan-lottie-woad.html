Meijer LPGA Classic Final Round: Jing Yan Leads by One, Woad Right Behind With Major Momentum on the Line

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 21, 2026

While Shinnecock Hills commands the attention of most of the golf world today, another compelling final round is unfolding at Blythefield Country Club in Belmont, Michigan — and the winner of the 2026 Meijer LPGA Classic for Simply Give will earn more than a trophy. She'll earn the last available spot in next week's KPMG Women's PGA Championship.

Jing Yan carries a one-shot lead into the final round at 14-under par (202 total). England's Lottie Woad, one of the LPGA Tour's brightest young talents, sits at 13-under. Australia's Cassie Porter is two shots back at 12-under.

Jing Yan's Defining Moment

This week represents uncharted territory for Yan. She has never led a final round on the LPGA Tour before, and the pressure of leading into Sunday carries a specific kind of weight that no amount of preparation can fully replicate. Her first-round 66 at Blythefield set the tone for the week — a round that combined aggressive scoring with sound course management on a layout that can punish overcommitment.

Saturday's wind-swept conditions tested the entire field, and Yan's ability to maintain a one-shot advantage through one of the week's most demanding rounds speaks to her composure. A first LPGA Tour title would be career-defining for the Chinese golfer, who has quietly built one of the strongest records on tour over the past two seasons without yet breaking through at the top level.

Lottie Woad's KPMG Stakes

One shot behind Yan, Lottie Woad arrives at the final round of the Meijer LPGA Classic in particularly loaded circumstances. She has publicly described this week as her final preparation event before the KPMG Women's PGA Championship begins June 25 at Hazeltine National Golf Club. Winning today would send her into the women's major on extraordinary momentum — and deliver her the final available spot in Hazeltine's field simultaneously.

Woad has been one of the more intriguing stories on the LPGA Tour in 2026. The Englishwoman's ball-striking is elite and her competitive experience in high-pressure situations has grown substantially this season. A victory at Blythefield would announce her as a genuine contender for the KPMG title, not just a participant.

Porter and the Rest of the Field

Australia's Cassie Porter enters two shots back at 12-under. Her position makes her a factor but requires both Yan and Woad to stumble at least twice for her to win with a clean round. At Blythefield on a Sunday, where the course plays relatively benign compared to the wild-card conditions of Saturday, Porter's path to the title runs through someone else having a bad day.

What's Next

The Meijer LPGA Classic for Simply Give final round is underway today, Sunday June 21, at Blythefield Country Club. Live scoring is available on the LPGA Tour website and ESPN. The tournament winner earns the final spot in the 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship field at Hazeltine National (June 25-28, $12 million purse). Four days after today's conclusion, the next women's major begins in Minnesota.
