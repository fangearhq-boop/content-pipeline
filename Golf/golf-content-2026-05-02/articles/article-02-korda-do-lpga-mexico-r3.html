Korda and Do Share LPGA Mexico Lead at 9-Under — World No. 345 Stuns at Riviera Maya Open

By Jake Torres | May 2, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

There is no golf leaderboard this week — at any level, on any tour — that features a more unlikely first-place pairing than the one at the top of the LPGA Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba.

Nelly Korda, the world No. 1 and one of the most dominant players in women's golf, enters Saturday's third round tied for the lead at 9-under 135. Her co-leader is Brianna Do — ranked No. 345 in the world. The 344-position gap between the two players at the top of this leaderboard is not a misprint.

Korda's Calculated Charge

Korda's path to the lead was methodical. She opened with a 4-under 68 on Thursday that placed her at T4 after the first round, two behind early leaders Do and Melanie Green. On Friday, she was simply immaculate — a bogey-free 67 with five birdies that lifted her to a share of the top.

Golf Channel has called Korda's 2026 form "the best she's ever been," and this week has done nothing to challenge that assessment. Five birdies with zero errors in the second round represents exactly the kind of precision that defines championship golf. She's been in contention consistently this season and is hunting another LPGA win at El Camaleon Golf Course in Playa del Carmen, Mexico.

Do's Improbable Position

Brianna Do arrived at the Riviera Maya Open ranked 345th in the world — a position that typically does not produce leaderboard leaders at LPGA Tour events. And yet, through 36 holes, she is precisely tied with Korda at the top.

Melanie Green sits one stroke behind the leaders at 8-under, with Japan's Minami Katsu a further shot back at 7-under. The leaderboard behind the top four is competitive, but Korda and Do have established themselves clearly as the weekend leaders.

What's Next

Saturday's third round is the pivotal moving day session. Sunday's final round follows, and the winner will be determined by which player — Korda, Do, or someone emerging from the pack — handles the pressure of El Camaleon's final stretch with the championship on the line.

For Korda, Sunday would represent another step in what has been a genuinely dominant 2026 LPGA campaign. For Do, it would be something more personal: the first LPGA Tour victory of her career, achieved in the most improbable fashion imaginable — by matching the world's best player stroke for stroke for four rounds.
