Cameron Young Leads Cadillac Championship by Five — 13-Under Heading Into Moving Day at Doral

By Ryan Calloway | May 2, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Cameron Young has been a study in controlled aggression at Trump National Doral, and through 36 holes of the $20 million Cadillac Championship, nobody has come close to matching him.

Young enters Saturday's third round at 13-under par, holding a five-shot lead over Jordan Spieth, Nick Taylor, and Alex Smalley — all tied at 8-under. After a bogey-free 64 to open the tournament Thursday, Young backed it up Friday with a 67 that featured 14 birdies over two rounds and just one blemish: a bogey at the par-4 14th where a chip from an awkward sidehill lie didn't quite reach the green.

He recovered immediately. Two holes later at the par-4 16th, Young drove the green and two-putted for birdie to regain the stroke he had dropped. That exchange summarizes why he's been so difficult to chase: he doesn't let mistakes compound.

The Chasing Pack

At 8-under, the trio of Spieth, Taylor, and Smalley represent the most immediate threat — but five shots in 36 holes is a substantial barrier. Spieth, a three-time major champion who arrived at Doral with new equipment and something to prove, carded a 71 in the second round after opening with a 65. The Round 2 step backward cost him ground on Young, but Spieth's history on Blue Monster-style layouts keeps him relevant.

Scottie Scheffler — the world No. 1 who stumbled to a 71 in his opening round — clawed his way into contention with a 67 on Friday to reach 6-under, tied for sixth. He needs a historically low score to have a realistic shot, but dismissing the world No. 1 on moving day would be unwise. Alex Fitzpatrick fired one of the day's better rounds with a 66 to reach 6-under as well.

Blue Monster Context

This is the first time in a decade that the PGA Tour has competed at Trump National Doral's Blue Monster course. The revived venue — redesigned by Gil Hanse and Jim Wagner — features new lakes at the 15th and 16th holes and significant changes from the course that hosted Tour events from 1962 through 2016. Young's performance is the best possible advertisement for the venue's return.

What's Next

Round 3 begins at 1 PM ET on Golf Channel. Young and Nick Taylor will be the final pairing, teeing off at 1:35 PM ET. With no cut at this Signature Event, all 72 players remain in the field — which means a crowded leaderboard and plenty of storylines for the weekend. Sunday's final round begins in the afternoon with the full field returning.

Young has 14 birdies through two rounds and has looked comfortable in the lead. The question for Saturday is whether the pressure of a five-shot advantage is something he can carry — or whether the Blue Monster has one more chapter to write.
