DeChambeau Wins Back-to-Back LIV Events in March, Claims $8 Million

By Golf Fanrecap Staff | March 28, 2026

Bryson DeChambeau is the most dominant golfer in the world right now — and March 2026 made that case irrefutable. DeChambeau won back-to-back LIV Golf events this month, taking titles in Singapore and South Africa via playoff in both events. The twin victories added $8 million in prize money to his total and pushed his individual LIV championship count to five.

Singapore: First Playoff Win of the Month

At LIV Golf Singapore (March 12-15), DeChambeau and Richard Lee finished the 54-hole event tied at 14-under par, forcing a playoff. DeChambeau prevailed. The victory required not just competitive excellence across three rounds but the composure to execute when the margin for error disappeared entirely in a sudden-death format.

LIV Golf Singapore was the opening statement of DeChambeau's March. What followed at Steyn City made it a statement of intent.

South Africa: Back-to-Back Against Rahm's Best

At The Club at Steyn City in Johannesburg (March 19-22), DeChambeau delivered one of the performances of his LIV career: 26-under par across 54 holes. The number alone commands attention. What made the victory more significant was who was chasing him.

Jon Rahm — who won the first LIV event of the month in Hong Kong — fired a final-round 63 in South Africa to apply maximum pressure. Rahm's charge forced a playoff. On the par-5 18th hole, Rahm found a greenside bunker and could not match DeChambeau's birdie. Back-to-back. Five titles. $8 million across two events in one month.

The Conversation About the World's Best

Any serious discussion about the best golfer in the world right now has to begin with DeChambeau. Scottie Scheffler holds the world No. 1 ranking and will enter the Masters at Augusta National as the betting favorite, but DeChambeau's March run — two wins, two playoffs, $8 million, dominance in back-to-back weeks — is the kind of sustained excellence that forces the conversation.

Rahm had his own strong March, winning LIV Hong Kong to open the month. But DeChambeau's closing stretch was of a different order entirely.

What's Next

The Masters begins April 9 at Augusta National. Both DeChambeau and Rahm are eligible to compete at the 90th edition of the tournament, and the LIV rivalry between them will move to golf's grandest stage. For DeChambeau, a Masters victory would represent the most significant title of his professional career — adding a major to a month that already stands among the best sustained stretches of his career.
