Lydia Ko Fires Career-Best 60 to Lead Ford Championship in Phoenix

By Golf Fanrecap Staff | March 28, 2026

Lydia Ko opened the 2026 Ford Championship at Whirlwind Golf Club in Phoenix with a 12-under-par 60 — the best round of her LPGA Tour career. She opened with four consecutive birdies and never looked back, building a lead that she carries into Round 3 on Saturday, March 28.

The performance placed Ko in rarefied territory. Shooting 60 on the LPGA Tour is extraordinarily rare, regardless of course or conditions. That Ko delivered it at this stage of a career already marked by exceptional accomplishments adds another dimension to an already extraordinary result.

A Career Defined by Excellence

Ko is one of the most decorated players in the modern era of women's golf. She won Olympic gold at the 2022 Paris Games — a moment that placed a crown on a career run that had seen her become one of the sport's youngest major champions. She has now claimed two major titles and set records for consistency and longevity that few players in LPGA history can match.

Her Round 1 at Phoenix added a new entry to that career record: the best single-round scoring performance she has posted as a professional. Four birdies to open — a sign of immediate aggression and confidence — followed by continued precision throughout the round produced the final figure of 60 strokes over 18 holes.

The Chase Is On

Hyo Joo Kim, who won the previous LPGA event (the Fortinet Founders Cup) just one week earlier in a wire-to-wire performance, is among the players giving chase at Phoenix. Nelly Korda, the world No. 1, is also in the field. Ko's 12-shot edge from Round 1 will diminish as the weekend progresses — that is the nature of 54-hole events — but the lead she has built provides genuine runway for a victory push on Sunday.

If Ko wins this weekend, it would validate not just the Round 1 performance but an overall form level that she appears to be building toward as the calendar approaches the LPGA's major season.

What's Next

Ko leads the Ford Championship heading into Round 3 at Whirlwind Golf Club on Saturday, March 28. Final round is Sunday, March 29. A victory would be one of the LPGA Tour's headline results of the spring season — and Round 1's 60 will be discussed in women's golf for years regardless of the final outcome.
