Gary Woodland Leads Houston Open at -13: A Comeback Story Heading Into Moving Day

By Golf Fanrecap Staff | March 28, 2026

Some leaderboards tell you who is winning. Some tell you something more important.

Gary Woodland is leading the Texas Children's Houston Open at 13-under par after consecutive rounds of 64 and 63 at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston, Texas. He holds a three-shot cushion over Nicolai Hojgaard and Jackson Suber entering Round 3 on Saturday, March 28. The 39-year-old Kansan is seeking his first PGA Tour victory since the 2019 U.S. Open — a title earned at Pebble Beach in one of the most dominant major championship performances of that decade.

What happened between then and now is why this week's leaderboard carries weight far beyond the numbers.

The Challenge That Changed Everything

In 2023, Woodland underwent brain surgery to remove a tumor. The procedure was successful, but recovery from neurosurgery is not a simple or linear process. Woodland has spoken publicly about dealing with post-traumatic stress disorder as part of his recovery — a degree of openness about mental health that is still relatively rare among professional athletes at the top of their sport.

He returned to professional golf, competed on tour, and rebuilt his game slowly. The Houston Open is the most sustained evidence yet that Woodland's game has returned to the level that made him a major champion. Two rounds under 65 at a demanding layout, building a lead against a field that includes legitimate contenders, represents exactly the kind of performance he and his team have been working toward since surgery.

The Leaderboard Picture

Hojgaard and Suber both shot 62 in Round 2 — explosive scoring from two players who came into Moving Day with significant momentum. The three-shot gap separating them from Woodland is comfortable but far from insurmountable on a course where low numbers are clearly available.

Scottie Scheffler, the world No. 1 and pre-tournament favorite at most events he enters, withdrew before the tournament began. His absence effectively opened the field and created a path to the winner's trophy that doesn't require beating the best player in the world. Cole Hammer's hole-in-one on hole 15 during Round 1 was an early sign that special things are possible this week at Memorial Park.

What's Next

Woodland takes a three-shot lead into Round 3 at Memorial Park on Saturday March 28. A strong Moving Day performance would give him a legitimate chance to claim his second PGA Tour title — and first in seven years — heading into Sunday. For a player who survived brain surgery and rebuilt his career with the quiet determination that has defined his approach throughout, a Houston Open victory would be one of the week's most meaningful sporting stories — in any sport.
