Masters 2026 Preview: Scheffler Favored, McIlroy Defending, Three Players Chasing Grand Slams

By Golf Fanrecap Staff | March 28, 2026

The 90th Masters Tournament begins April 9 at Augusta National Golf Club in Augusta, Georgia. The field assembled for this edition of the sport's most celebrated major carries more narrative weight than any in recent memory: a dominant world No. 1 defending his status as the game's best, a champion who completed his career Grand Slam last year, and three players simultaneously chasing the same historic achievement.

With a $20 million purse and $3.6 million for the champion, the stakes match the storylines.

Scheffler: The Favorite and the Standard

Scottie Scheffler enters as the world No. 1 and the betting favorite at +480. His record of consistency at the highest level of professional golf has made him the consensus pick at major championships regardless of venue. The question at Augusta in 2026 is not whether Scheffler belongs — it is whether the field can stop him from adding another green jacket to a career collection that is already among the most impressive in modern golf.

Scheffler withdrew from the Texas Children's Houston Open this week, managing his preparation for Augusta rather than competing on a tight pre-Masters timeline. His focus is singular and his preparation deliberate. The 2026 Masters is his primary target for this stretch of the calendar.

McIlroy: Defending Champion, Grand Slam Complete

Rory McIlroy arrives at Augusta as the defending champion after winning the 2025 Masters — the final piece of his career Grand Slam. The victory completed one of the sport's most anticipated achievements, ending a decade-long wait for McIlroy to add the green jacket to his Open Championship, U.S. Open, and PGA Championship titles.

The last men's player to complete the career Grand Slam before McIlroy was Gary Player in 1965. McIlroy's 2025 win placed him in that company. Defending that title at Augusta — where even world-class players rarely repeat — will require him to produce a performance worthy of the occasion once again.

The Grand Slam Chasers: Three Players, One Destination

The defining subplot of the 2026 Masters may be the simultaneous Grand Slam pursuit. Three players will arrive at Augusta having won the other three men's major championships — needing only the Masters to complete the career collection:

Xander Schauffele has won the Open Championship, U.S. Open, and PGA Championship. Collin Morikawa has won The Open Championship and PGA Championship. Brooks Koepka has won four majors — three U.S. Opens and one PGA Championship. All three stand at the doorstep of the sport's most exclusive club.

Three simultaneous Grand Slam chasers at the same tournament is an extraordinary convergence. One of them may win it. Whoever does will join Nicklaus, Player, Sarazen, Hogan, and a handful of others who have held all four major titles in their career.

What's Next

The Masters begins Thursday, April 9, and runs through Sunday, April 12 at Augusta National Golf Club. The par-72 layout has produced some of the most memorable moments in golf history, and the 90th edition promises a field and a set of storylines that will sustain conversation for the duration of the week.
