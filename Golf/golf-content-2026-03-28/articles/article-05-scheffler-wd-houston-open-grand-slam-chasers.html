Scheffler Withdraws from Houston Open; Grand Slam Chasers Converge on Augusta

By Golf Fanrecap Staff | March 28, 2026

Scottie Scheffler, the world No. 1 and +480 Masters favorite, withdrew from the Texas Children's Houston Open before the tournament began — a deliberate decision by the best player in the world to prioritize preparation for the Masters rather than compete in the final stroke-play event before Augusta. No specific injury has been confirmed; the assumption within the golf world is that Scheffler is managing his schedule and physical preparation with the first major of the year as the clear target.

What Scheffler's Absence Means in Houston

The practical effect of Scheffler's withdrawal was immediate. A field already capable of producing low scores no longer needed to go through the best player in the world to claim the title. Gary Woodland — the 2019 U.S. Open champion and brain surgery survivor — leads the Houston Open at 13-under par entering Round 3, three shots ahead of Nicolai Hojgaard and Jackson Suber.

Scheffler's absence didn't create the story in Houston, but it created the context: any player who wins this week does so knowing the world No. 1 had other priorities. That context is a given in the week before a major — Augusta's shadow falls over every event on this part of the calendar.

The Grand Slam Chasers

The deepest sub-narrative heading into Augusta in 2026 is the simultaneous Grand Slam pursuit. Three players arrive at Augusta National having won the Open Championship, U.S. Open, and PGA Championship — needing only the Masters to complete the career collection:

Xander Schauffele has won the Open Championship, U.S. Open, and PGA Championship. The Masters is the final piece. Collin Morikawa holds two different major titles — The Open Championship and PGA Championship — and has demonstrated the precision ball-striking that Augusta rewards. Brooks Koepka has four major championships to his name — three U.S. Opens and one PGA Championship — and arrives needing only the green jacket.

Three simultaneous Grand Slam chasers at the same major is extraordinarily rare. Whoever wins at Augusta joins the sport's most exclusive list — a group that includes Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, Gary Player, Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, and Rory McIlroy, who completed his Grand Slam at the 2025 Masters. The last player to do it before McIlroy was Player in 1965.

What's Next

Scheffler returns to competition at Augusta National on April 9, where he will attempt to add another Masters title to a career record that already makes him the consensus pick for the world's best player. The Grand Slam chasers — Schauffele, Morikawa, and Koepka — will all tee it up in the same field at the same tournament, chasing the same milestone. The 90th Masters runs April 9-12.
