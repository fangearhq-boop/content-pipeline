McIlroy at Shinnecock: The Masters Champion Goes Hunting for His Second Major of 2026

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

June 16, 2026

Rory McIlroy arrives at Shinnecock Hills as the reigning Masters champion. He has already completed the career Grand Slam. And now he wants another major — his second of 2026 — at one of the most demanding venues in major championship golf.

Listed at +1200 for the 2026 U.S. Open, McIlroy is the second favorite behind Scottie Scheffler and one of only a handful of players in the field with both the game and the experience to handle whatever Shinnecock Hills has waiting for them.

What He Did at Congressional

The last time McIlroy won the U.S. Open was 2011 at Congressional Country Club in Maryland. He was 22 years old. He set or tied 12 U.S. Open records, including the lowest 72-hole score in championship history: 16-under-par 268. He won by 8 strokes. It was one of the most dominant performances in major championship history — a performance that announced to the world that this was a generational talent.

But Congressional was soft, lush parkland where scoring was possible and birdies were available. Shinnecock Hills is the opposite in almost every way: links-style terrain, firm and fast fairways, wind exposure on Long Island's East End, and pins that the USGA places in locations designed to make par feel like a victory. When the U.S. Open was last at Shinnecock in 2018, not one player finished the week under par.

The Game He Has Now

What gives McIlroy a genuine chance at Shinnecock is the evolution of his game over the past several seasons. He has added significant finesse to his arsenal — the ability to flight the ball lower, manage distance control in crosswinds, and hit precise iron shots into firm greens rather than just attacking pins. Shinnecock demands all of that.

The one concern with McIlroy heading to Southampton is driving accuracy. He ranked 125th in driving accuracy percentage this season — a number that matters at Shinnecock, where the thick five-inch rough lining the fairways makes recovery shots from the wrong side difficult. McIlroy is long off the tee, but if he cannot keep the ball in the short grass more consistently than he has been, the rough will cost him strokes every round.

What's Next

The 2026 U.S. Open begins Thursday, June 18 at Shinnecock Hills. Rory McIlroy, Masters champion, career Grand Slam completist, and +1200 co-favorite, will look to add another chapter to a career that keeps rewriting itself. Winning the U.S. Open this week would give McIlroy two majors in a single year for the first time. The final round is Sunday, June 21. Tee times and groupings will be confirmed Wednesday.
