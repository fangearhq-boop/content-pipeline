Meijer LPGA Classic Preview: Women's Golf Takes the Stage June 18-21 in Michigan

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

June 16, 2026

While the men's game converges on Shinnecock Hills for the U.S. Open this week, the LPGA Tour is running its own compelling event simultaneously. The Meijer LPGA Classic for Simply Give tees off Thursday, June 18, at Blythefield Country Club in Belmont, Michigan — running through Sunday, June 21, in direct parallel with the national championship on Long Island.

The concurrent scheduling gives golf fans an extraordinary week: four days of major championship drama at Shinnecock Hills alongside four days of top-tier women's professional golf in the heart of Michigan. For the casual fan, the two events together make this one of the best weeks of the year to follow the sport across all its levels.

Blythefield Country Club

Blythefield Country Club in Belmont, Michigan, has hosted the Meijer LPGA Classic as a consistent stop on the LPGA Tour schedule. The layout suits the scoring style of the women's game, providing opportunities for low numbers while still demanding precision from the better players in the field. Michigan's summer conditions — warm temperatures, firm fairways, and variable wind — create a fair but challenging test.

The tournament also carries charitable significance through its partnership with Meijer's Simply Give program, which benefits local food pantries. That community connection has made the event popular with both players and fans in the region, contributing to strong attendance numbers in recent years.

Context: The KPMG Looms Large

For LPGA players, this week carries additional strategic weight. The KPMG Women's PGA Championship — one of the LPGA Tour's five major championships — follows immediately at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota, June 25-28. That means players using the Meijer Classic to sharpen their games have a tight window to peak at exactly the right moment.

The sequencing is familiar in professional golf: a regular tour event the week before a major serves as a warmup, an opportunity to find competitive rhythm without the full weight of major stakes. Players who contend at Blythefield often carry momentum into the KPMG, and those who miss cuts can use the extra week of preparation on a setup closer to what a major demands.

What's Next

The Meijer LPGA Classic tees off Thursday, June 18, with the final round Sunday, June 21 — the same day as the U.S. Open finale at Shinnecock. Leaderboard coverage will be available through the LPGA.com official channels and ESPN's golf coverage. And one week later, the KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine National brings the next major of the women's golf calendar.
