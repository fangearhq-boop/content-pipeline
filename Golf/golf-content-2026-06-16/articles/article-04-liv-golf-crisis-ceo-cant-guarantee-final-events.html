LIV Golf in Crisis: CEO Admits He Can't Guarantee the Final 4 Events of 2026 Will Take Place

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

June 16, 2026

The league that rewrote professional golf's economics is now fighting for its own financial survival. LIV Golf CEO Scott O'Neil acknowledged in a recent interview with CNBC that he cannot guarantee the final four events of the 2026 season will take place — a statement that reflects just how dramatically the league's situation has deteriorated in recent months.

The root cause is the funding cliff that has arrived faster than the organization could prepare for. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund, which has backed LIV Golf since its 2022 launch with billions in investment, confirmed earlier this year that it will end its financial support after the 2026 season. And while the PIF committed to keeping the 2026 schedule running, the money has not arrived at the pace the league requires.

The Funding Gap

LIV Golf needs approximately $600 million to complete its full 2026 schedule. According to reports from Golf Digest and Sky Sports, the PIF has delivered only around $200 million toward that total — roughly $66 million in early May and an additional $130 million in early June. That leaves a gap of approximately $400 million with multiple events still on the calendar.

The signs of that shortfall are already visible. The league postponed its June 25-28 event in Louisiana, officially attributing the decision to "peak summer heat and the crowded global sports calendar." The financial reality is harder to separate from that explanation. When a major professional sports league postpones an event weeks out, the reasons behind it matter.

The League's Response

LIV Golf has moved quickly to address its funding crisis through several organizational changes. The league hired financial advisers to assist in identifying alternative funding sources. It also unveiled a new independent board of directors and announced it is seeking "long-term financial partners" to replace or supplement the Saudi money that is departing after this season.

O'Neil told CNBC to take the PIF "at their word" that funding for the remaining events would be provided. But he would not make that guarantee himself — an unusual and telling acknowledgment from a sitting CEO about the certainty of his own league's schedule.

What's Next

LIV Golf's immediate challenge is proving to players, sponsors, and broadcasters that the remaining 2026 events will take place. The longer-term challenge is identifying a financial model that survives without Saudi backing. Whether that means a merger with the PGA Tour (negotiations that have been ongoing in various forms since 2023), a new international investor, or a significant restructuring of the league's format and ambition remains unclear. The next several months will determine whether LIV Golf as it currently exists survives into 2027.
