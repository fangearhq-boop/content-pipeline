US Open Field Deep Dive: Rahm's Form, Aaron Rai's Major Confidence, and the Shinnecock Factor

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

June 16, 2026

The 2026 U.S. Open field at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club is one of the most competitive in recent memory — and the storylines extend well beyond the two top favorites. Jon Rahm, Aaron Rai, and the course itself all figure prominently in how this major championship will unfold Thursday through Sunday.

Jon Rahm: The Dangerous Dark Horse

Jon Rahm arrives in Southampton with momentum. The Spaniard was the runner-up at the 2026 PGA Championship, returning to the front of major contention for the first time in over a year with a performance that reminded the field why he is consistently one of the most feared players in any major field. His odds at Shinnecock are in the +1300 range — reflecting the respect the market has for his record without placing him at the top of the board.

Rahm has already won a U.S. Open — his 2021 victory at Torrey Pines, one of the most emotional wins in recent memory following his COVID withdrawal from the Memorial Tournament weeks earlier. He knows what it takes to win this championship, and Shinnecock's demand for strategic patience and precision iron play suits his game well.

Aaron Rai: A New Kind of Confidence

Aaron Rai won the 2026 PGA Championship for his first career major title. The English player now arrives at Shinnecock Hills carrying something intangible but genuinely important: the knowledge that he can win on the biggest stage. First major victories change a player's relationship with themselves under pressure, and Rai will be watched closely to see whether his Shinnecock performance reflects that new confidence or whether the step up to the U.S. Open's specific demands presents a different kind of test.

The Shinnecock Factor

The course itself is perhaps the most significant variable at the 2026 U.S. Open. Shinnecock Hills in Southampton, New York, is a true links-style layout — exposed to the wind off Shinnecock Bay, with firm and fast conditions that reward precise shot-making over raw power. The USGA has a history of setting up Shinnecock severely, and the 2018 edition was the most extreme example: no player in the field finished under par for the week, and Brooks Koepka won at even par.

Wind direction, green speeds, and rough length will all factor into whether 2026 produces similar conditions. Players who succeed here typically combine patience — accepting bogeys and grinding for pars rather than chasing birdies at bad times — with elite iron play into firm greens that need to be landed precisely to hold.

What's Next

The 2026 U.S. Open begins Thursday, June 18. Tee times will be assigned Wednesday for the first two rounds, with the cut after 36 holes removing roughly half the field. Saturday and Sunday bring the final two rounds, with the trophy presentation scheduled for late Sunday afternoon. The championship runs June 18-21 at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club, Southampton, New York.
