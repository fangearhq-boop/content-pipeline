The Greatest Storyline in Golf: Scheffler's Grand Slam Bid on His Birthday and Father's Day

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

June 16, 2026

Thursday at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York, the 2026 U.S. Open begins. And with it starts the week that could define Scottie Scheffler's career — a week so narratively stacked that even the most creative screenwriter would be told to dial it back.

Scheffler, the world's No. 1 player, enters the 126th U.S. Open needing only one thing to complete the career Grand Slam: a win at this championship. He already has the Masters (2022, 2024), the PGA Championship (2025), and The Open Championship (2025). The U.S. Open is the final piece. If he wins at Shinnecock Hills, he becomes just the seventh player in history to win all four major championships — joining Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, and Rory McIlroy on that exclusive list.

A Final Round Like No Other

The calendar has delivered something extraordinary. The U.S. Open's final round is Sunday, June 21. That is Scottie Scheffler's 30th birthday. It is also Father's Day.

The math of what that means is almost surreal: win the U.S. Open, complete the career Grand Slam, on your 30th birthday, on Father's Day. Golf has produced many memorable sporting narratives over the decades, but rarely has the calendar aligned with the stakes quite so perfectly. If Scheffler hoists the trophy on June 21, the story writes itself.

Should he succeed, Scheffler would also become just the fourth player to complete the career Grand Slam in his first attempt at doing so — a distinction that reflects both dominance and timing.

The Challenge Ahead

None of it will be easy. Scheffler has not been as dominant in 2026 as in his recent peak seasons — his only win so far this year was the American Express in January. He has no prior experience at Shinnecock Hills in major competition, and history suggests that unfamiliarity with a venue is a real variable: the last time he played a course for the first time at a major, he finished 14th.

Shinnecock Hills itself is a formidable obstacle. The links-style course on Long Island's East End is exposed to wind, demands precise iron play, and punishes mistakes severely. In 2018, the last time the U.S. Open was held here, no player in the field finished under par for the week. The USGA knows how to make Shinnecock virtually impossible.

But Scheffler is +550 to win — the overwhelming favorite — and there is good reason for that. His ball-striking ranks among the best in the history of the game, and U.S. Open conditions that reward fairways hit and greens attacked carefully tend to suit the most complete players. If he is putting well, he is very difficult to beat.

What's Next

The 2026 U.S. Open begins Thursday, June 18 at Shinnecock Hills. The final round is Sunday, June 21. Four rounds, 72 holes, and the career Grand Slam waiting at the end. Scottie Scheffler's greatest test begins in two days — and the world will be watching.
