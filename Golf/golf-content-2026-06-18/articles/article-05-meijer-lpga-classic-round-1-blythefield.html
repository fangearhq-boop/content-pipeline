Meijer LPGA Classic Round 1: Best Field of the Year Tees Off in Belmont, Michigan

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 18, 2026

While the men's game convenes at Shinnecock Hills this week, the LPGA Tour is putting on its own show 700 miles west in Belmont, Michigan — and the Meijer LPGA Classic for Simply Give is annually one of the best stops on the women's tour calendar.

Round 1 is underway at Blythefield Country Club, the tournament's longtime host venue, with 144 players competing for a $3 million purse. The field represents some of the deepest competition the LPGA Tour assembles in a non-major setting, and the timing — the week before the KPMG Women's PGA Championship — makes it a genuine form guide for what's to come.

Why Blythefield

Blythefield Country Club has hosted the Meijer LPGA Classic since the tournament's inception, and the course rewards precision over power in a way that tends to surface the LPGA Tour's most complete ball-strikers. The layout is tree-lined and traditional, a contrast to the exposed coastal setup facing the men's field at Shinnecock this same week.

The course plays at approximately 6,400 yards for the women's event, and scoring conditions in Michigan are generally more forgiving than the windswept Long Island Shore. Red numbers are expected throughout the week, and low scores in Round 1 typically signal which players have their games tuned for the upcoming major.

The Major Pipeline

The Meijer LPGA Classic's positioning on the calendar is not accidental. The KPMG Women's PGA Championship begins June 25 at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota — one week after Blythefield. For the top players in the field, this week is preparation: a live competitive round to sharpen ball-striking, putting, and course management under tournament conditions before the major begins.

That context shapes how the field approaches this week. A player chasing the Meijer title is also a player calibrating her form for the KPMG. The two goals are not in conflict; winning here would be the best possible confidence builder heading into Minnesota.

Fan Access and the Father's Day Weekend

The Meijer LPGA Classic is one of the more fan-friendly events on the LPGA Tour calendar, and the organizers have structured ticket pricing to reflect that. General admission is $10 per day, and children 17 and under are free all week — making it an accessible live sports option for families in the greater Grand Rapids area throughout the four-day event.

Sunday, June 21 — the final round — carries a specific promotion: free general admission for all fathers. The convergence of Father's Day with the tournament's closing round is a programming opportunity the event leans into each year, and the free admission offer typically brings out larger crowds for the Sunday finish than the tournament might otherwise draw.

A Tournament With Staying Power

The Meijer LPGA Classic for Simply Give takes its charitable identity seriously. The "Simply Give" designation refers to Meijer's food bank donation program, and the tournament has raised millions for regional food banks throughout its run at Blythefield. In that sense, it sits in the tradition of LPGA Tour events that have built community roots over time — the kind of local-anchor event that sustains the tour's presence in mid-sized markets between the marquee destinations.

For players, it's a comfortable stop: familiar venue, receptive crowds, Michigan summer weather, and a course that rewards skill without humbling the field the way a U.S. Open setup would. The scoring format and purse structure draw participation from the full top of the rankings.

What's Next

Round 1 play continues through Thursday afternoon. The cut will fall after Round 2 on Friday, with the top finishers advancing to the weekend. The final round is Sunday, June 21 — Father's Day — at Blythefield Country Club.

After the Meijer Classic concludes, the LPGA Tour moves directly to Minnesota for the KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine National, June 25-28. For anyone following women's golf this week, Blythefield is the preview. Hazeltine is the main event.
