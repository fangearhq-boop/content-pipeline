### STORY 3: LIV Golf Virginia — Herbert Wins Wire-to-Wire

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

Lucas Herbert was sick all week. He won by four shots anyway.

The Australian posted rounds of 64-63-68-69 at Trump National Golf Club Washington D.C. to claim the LIV Golf Virginia title wire-to-wire at 24-under par. Herbert fought a flu that would have sent most people back to their hotel rooms, but he gutted through four days of the best golf of his professional career.

Sergio Garcia finished second at 20-under. Bryson DeChambeau — who started Sunday strong before withdrawing earlier in the week with a wrist injury — finished third at 19-under after a final-round 66.

The team story belonged to Anthony Kim. The former PGA Tour star, who has made a remarkable return to professional golf with 4Aces GC, shot a bogey-free 10-under 62 on Sunday — the low round of the entire tournament — to pull the 4Aces into a team playoff. Kim and Thomas Detry then beat Josele Ballester and David Puig to give 4Aces GC their third team title of the season.

Herbert takes home $4 million from the $20 million purse and earns an exemption to the US Open at Shinnecock Hills later this year.

What a week.
