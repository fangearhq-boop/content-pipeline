Korda Three Back at Queen City as Hall of Fame Countdown Reaches the Home Stretch

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 15, 2026

Nelly Korda needs four more LPGA Hall of Fame points. She is three shots off the lead at the Kroger Queen City Championship in Cincinnati with three rounds still to play. The arithmetic of her Hall of Fame pursuit and the mathematics of the tournament leaderboard are converging in real time, and the golf world is watching both calculations simultaneously.

Korda opened the Queen City Championship with a 1-under 69 — two birdies and one bogey, 15 pars, a patient round at Maketewah Country Club. She sits three shots behind the co-leaders after the first round: Ina Yoon, Chella Choi of South Korea, and Rio Takeda of Japan, all at 4-under-par 66.

The Hall of Fame Countdown

The LPGA Hall of Fame requires 27 points, accumulated through wins, major victories, and player of the year awards at various levels. Korda has 23 points. She is four away. In practical terms, that means any combination of four additional tournament wins, major victories (which carry additional point weight), or player of the year recognition could get her across the threshold.

For context, a standard LPGA Tour win earns one point. A major championship win earns two points. Korda has the talent and the consistency to accumulate points at the rate needed — she is the world No. 1 and has won multiple times already in 2026 — but each tournament that passes without a win is a tournament that doesn't move the clock forward.

A Queen City win would deliver one more point toward that total. A week in contention without the win delivers nothing for the HOF count, though it builds momentum. Every tournament from here matters.

A Deep Field in Cincinnati

The Queen City Championship field is among the stronger of the LPGA's 2026 non-major events. Defending champion Charley Hull made a fast start to her title defense, and Lydia Ko — a multiple major champion and consistently among the world's top players — is also in contention after Round 1. The South Korean players at the top of the leaderboard (Yoon and Choi) both represent the depth of global LPGA talent, and Takeda's presence gives the tournament an international flavor that matches the modern LPGA's competitive profile.

Korda has won against fields like this before, and her mental composure in pressure situations — evident across multiple big-tournament moments in her career — gives her a genuine path to the Sunday lead even from three shots back.

The Path Forward

In professional golf, three shots after one round is a manageable deficit. Korda needs to fire a 64 or 65 in Round 2 today to move to the top of the leaderboard. Given the length and layout of Maketewah Country Club, low scoring is possible for players who can control their irons into the greens and avoid the course's key trouble spots.

If she can move up the board on Friday, the weekend becomes a genuine title defense opportunity that also advances her Hall of Fame clock. Korda has done this before — she has won tournaments from similar deficits with calculated back-nine charges that her ball-striking ability makes possible.

What's Next

Round 2 of the Kroger Queen City Championship is Friday at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati. The tournament runs through Sunday, May 17. If Korda contends through the weekend, a potential win would add one Hall of Fame point and bring her to 24 of 27 needed for induction. Watch this leaderboard closely — Korda in contention with Hall of Fame stakes on the line is the kind of story that defines a golf season.
