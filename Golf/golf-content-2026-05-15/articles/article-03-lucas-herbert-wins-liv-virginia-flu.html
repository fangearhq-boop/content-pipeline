Lucas Herbert's Flu-Fueled Wire-to-Wire Win Is the LIV Golf Story of the Season

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 15, 2026

Lucas Herbert was sick all week at Trump National Golf Club Washington D.C. He had the flu. And he won the 2026 LIV Golf Virginia tournament by four shots anyway.

The Australian posted rounds of 64-63-68-69 over four days — 24-under par in total — leading from the opening round to the final hole in what stands as the most dominant wire-to-wire performance of the LIV Golf 2026 season. Herbert earned $4 million from the $20 million purse, claimed LIV Golf's largest annual prize, and earned himself an exemption to the US Open at Shinnecock Hills later this year.

Sergio Garcia finished second at 20-under, four shots back. Bryson DeChambeau was third at 19-under after a final-round 66 that could not close the gap.

The Flu Game

Herbert acknowledged after his win that he had been fighting illness from the opening day of the tournament. Rather than withdraw, he played through it — managing his energy between shots, relying on the mechanical quality of his swing to produce consistent ball-striking even when his body was not cooperating. That combination of physical resilience and technical trust allowed him to hold his lead through two days of nearly identical scoring (68-69) after explosive opening rounds of 64 and 63.

His ability to control the narrative of the tournament — never allowing Garcia or DeChambeau to get within two shots for long enough to mount a real charge — spoke to a mental fortitude that will serve him well at the US Open, a championship that has historically demanded exactly that kind of grinding persistence.

Anthony Kim's 62 Delivers the 4Aces

While Herbert was the individual story, the team competition belonged to Anthony Kim and 4Aces GC. Kim shot a bogey-free 10-under 62 in the final round — the lowest round of the entire tournament — to pull the 4Aces GC into a team competition playoff against Fireballs GC.

In the playoff, Kim and teammate Thomas Detry each made par at the par-4 18th to outlast Josele Ballester and David Puig, who made bogey. The win gave 4Aces GC their third team title of the 2026 LIV Golf season.

Kim's 62 is already the subject of significant attention in golf circles. His return to professional golf with LIV has been one of the sport's more remarkable stories, and a final-round 62 in a major team competition adds another chapter to a comeback that few expected to generate this kind of performance.

Herbert and the US Open

The US Open exemption Herbert earned at Shinnecock Hills is a significant prize beyond the $4 million. LIV Golf players have at times faced challenges securing entry to traditional major championships, and an earned exemption through a tournament win provides Herbert with an unambiguous path to one of golf's four major events. Shinnecock Hills, a traditional US Open venue known for its relentless difficulty, will be a formidable test — but Herbert's form this week suggests he has the game to compete anywhere.

What's Next

The LIV Golf circuit's next event is in Korea in late May (May 28-31 at Asiad Country Club in Busan). Herbert will prepare for his US Open debut as an LIV winner. Anthony Kim and 4Aces GC sit atop the LIV Golf team standings with three team titles in the 2026 season. The individual and team stories from Virginia will continue to shape the conversation heading into one of golf's most anticipated summer stretches.
