PGA Championship Round 1: Scheffler Shares Lead With Six Others as Aronimink Bites Back

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 15, 2026

Aronimink Golf Club made its point on Thursday. The 108th PGA Championship opened with gusty winds, firm greens, and demanding pin locations that kept nearly the entire field from running away with the opening round. When the dust settled, seven players shared the lead at 3-under par, and the biggest name in the week's narrative — Rory McIlroy and his Grand Slam pursuit — was struggling at the wrong end of the scoreboard.

Scottie Scheffler led the defending champion's charge with a measured 67, tied alongside Aldrich Potgieter, Ryo Hisatsune, Min Woo Lee, Stephan Jaeger, and two others in a crowd that speaks to how evenly matched this field is and how much course Aronimink has to offer.

Scheffler's Patient Round

Scheffler's 67 was exactly the kind of opening round he prefers in major championship settings: calm, calculated, and built on avoiding big numbers while collecting birdies on the right holes. He birdied the sixth, seventh, tenth, and eleventh holes, and his lone bogey came in a place where others were making double or worse. It was the kind of round Aronimink rewards — sensible course management, commitment to conservative lines, and trust that the birdies will come when the course allows.

Notably, it was the first time in Scheffler's career that he has held at least a share of the lead after an opening round at a major championship. As the world No. 1 and defending PGA champion, he arrived with as much expectation as anyone in the field. Round 1 delivered validation that his game is in precisely the right place.

The Leaderboard Picture

Potgieter's presence at 3-under generated significant conversation in the Thursday afternoon press conferences. The long hitter's opening 67 at a course that many expected to reward power off the tee was taken as evidence that Aronimink, while requiring length in places, will ultimately be decided by short game precision and iron play — qualities that cut across every profile in the top of the field.

Ryo Hisatsune, Min Woo Lee, and Stephan Jaeger — all international players with significant major championship pedigree or rising profile — complete the co-lead group. Jordan Spieth, Brooks Koepka, Jon Rahm, and Justin Thomas all sit at 2-under, within arm's reach of the lead entering Friday's second round.

The Course Delivers

Donald Ross designed Aronimink in 1928, and the course's characteristics — wide fairways that narrow on approach, undulating greens with false edges, and a routing that uses elevation changes against players who aren't paying attention — proved every bit as demanding as the PGA of America promised. Par 70 at 7,394 yards is deceptive: the missing length is replaced by strategic complexity that makes every par feel earned and every birdie require deliberate planning.

The last time the PGA Championship visited Aronimink was in 1962, when Gary Player won. In the 64 years between visits, the course has only become more nuanced and more complete. Thursday's opening round was a reminder of everything that makes it a worthy major venue.

What's Next

Round 2 is underway Friday at Aronimink Golf Club. The projected cut line is expected around even par, making survival the priority for a large group of highly ranked players in the middle of the field — including McIlroy, who needs a significant recovery round. The weekend draws will be set Friday evening, with Saturday's third round beginning Saturday morning. Scheffler, Potgieter, Hisatsune, Lee, and Jaeger will all tee off in the featured late afternoon groupings on Friday.
