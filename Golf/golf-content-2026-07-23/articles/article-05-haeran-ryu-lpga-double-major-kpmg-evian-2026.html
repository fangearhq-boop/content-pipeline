Haeran Ryu Has Won Two LPGA Majors in 2026 — and the Season Isn't Over

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 23, 2026

Stop for a moment and appreciate what Haeran Ryu has accomplished this summer.

The South Korean golfer won the KPMG Women's PGA Championship on June 28, 2026. She won the Amundi Evian Championship on July 12, 2026. That is two LPGA majors in the same calendar year, four weeks apart. She has led the 2026 women's major season from the front — and no other player has more than one major win on the year.

It is an achievement that belongs in a short list of the most remarkable individual runs in recent women's golf history.

The Two Titles

The KPMG Women's PGA Championship is one of the oldest and most prestigious events on the LPGA schedule, running since 1955. It has been won by Annika Sorenstam, Se Ri Pak, Inbee Park, and Nelly Korda, among others. Ryu added her name to that list with her June 28 victory.

Less than two weeks later, she won the Amundi Evian Championship — the Swiss major held on the shores of Lake Geneva. The Evian has been won by Inbee Park, In Gee Chun, and Lydia Ko. It is a test of links-adjacent precision and course management. Ryu handled it decisively.

Two consecutive major victories in a single season is something only a handful of players in LPGA history have done. The names on that list are not chosen at random — they are players whose careers are defined by sustained excellence at the highest level. Ryu is now among them.

The Broader Context

The 2026 men's golf major season has been dominated entirely by PGA Tour players. Rory McIlroy, Aaron Rai, Wyndham Clark, and Ryan Fox claimed the four men's majors. LIV Golf players won zero. On the women's side, the major story has been Ryu — a player who did not come into the season with the most prominent global name but who has separated herself from every other player in the world in the sport's most important moments.

She leads the LPGA major count for 2026 by a two-to-one margin over everyone else. The AIG Women's British Open — the final major of the season — remains to be played. Ryu will arrive at that event as the most decorated player in women's golf this year.

She is also doing it at a time when the Women's Scottish Open is underway and the women's game is receiving more attention heading into a major week. Nelly Korda, the world No. 1, is the headliner at Dundonald Links. Lottie Woad, ranked No. 4, is generating buzz in her home region. But the player who has actually done the most this season — in the tournaments that matter most — is Haeran Ryu.

What's Next

The Women's Scottish Open runs July 23-26 at Dundonald Links in Gailes, Scotland. The AIG Women's British Open follows as the final major of 2026. Watch whether Ryu contends at either event. A third major title this season would place her among the most decorated single-season performers in LPGA history. Even without a third, what she has already done in 2026 demands recognition.
