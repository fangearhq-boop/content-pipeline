LIV Golf UK Round 1: Niemann Posts Bogey-Free 64, Legion XIII Tied for Team Lead

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 23, 2026

Joaquin Niemann leads after Round 1 of LIV Golf UK — and the backdrop to his bogey-free 64 is unlike anything professional golf has seen before.

The tournament is underway at JCB Golf & Country Club in Rocester, Staffordshire, England. Niemann shot six birdies and made no bogeys in his opening round, reaching -6 and sharing the top of the individual leaderboard. Lee Westwood, Bubba Watson, and Carlos Ortiz are all at -5. Tyrrell Hatton and Paul Casey are tied at -4.

In the team competition, Legion XIII, Stinger GC, and Torque GC are all tied at -11 after the first day.

Rahm's Three-Peat Quest

The individual storyline of the week belongs to Jon Rahm. The Legion XIII captain is chasing his third consecutive individual title at JCB Golf & Country Club, having won the event in both 2024 and 2025. No player has won three straight at the same LIV event since the tour launched.

More than the streak is at stake. Rahm leads the 2026 LIV individual season with 779 points — more than double his nearest competitor, Bryson DeChambeau, who sits at 476.9 points. Thomas Detry is third at 251.9 points. If Rahm wins this week and the gap remains decisive, he could clinch the 2026 LIV individual season championship earlier than any player has ever done so.

After Round 1, Rahm is not on the individual leaderboard's top line — Niemann holds that position — but the team standings tell a more complete story. Legion XIII is tied for the lead at -11, meaning Rahm and his teammates are positioned well heading into Friday's second round.

Competition Against a Difficult Backdrop

LIV Golf UK is the tour's 10th event of 14 in the 2026 season. It is also taking place at the moment the tour's financial future is most uncertain. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund is withdrawing its backing after this season, LIV CEO Scott O'Neil is racing a September deadline to raise $350 million, and the $40 million season finale has been described as "highly unlikely" to occur by Cleeks GC captain Martin Kaymer.

None of that is visible in the scoring at JCB. A bogey-free 64 from Niemann is a quality round in any context. Westwood's -5 is a reminder that LIV has provided a competitive home for veteran European players whose best years may be past but who remain capable of genuine contention. The team competition at -11 is legitimate golf, contested on a legitimate course.

The players are playing. The leaderboards are real. The questions about 2027 exist separately from the competition happening right now.

What's Next

Round 2 of LIV Golf UK takes place Friday at JCB Golf & Country Club. Watch the individual battle between Niemann and Rahm as the defining story. In the team competition, Legion XIII, Stinger GC, and Torque GC all begin Friday tied — separating the field over the weekend will determine the $40 million team championship's early leaders, even as the question of whether the season finale will occur remains unresolved.
