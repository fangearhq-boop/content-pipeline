### STORY 1: LIV Season Finale in Jeopardy

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Professional golf's most disruptive experiment may be running out of road.

LIV Golf's $40 million team championship season finale — scheduled for The Cardinal at Saint John's in Plymouth, Michigan — is "highly unlikely" to take place, according to Cleeks GC captain Martin Kaymer, who spoke publicly about the situation on Wednesday and Thursday.

The broader picture is stark. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF) — which has been the source of LIV's extraordinary spending since its founding — is withdrawing financial backing after the 2026 season. PIF has invested more than £3.75 billion in LIV over the tour's first three years. That money is stopping.

LIV CEO Scott O'Neil, who took the role in January 2025, is now attempting to raise $350 million in outside investment to keep the tour operating beyond 2026. He has a September deadline. Players have reportedly been told they do not know whether LIV will exist in 2027.

The next three events on the LIV schedule — UK (this week), New Jersey, and Indianapolis — are expected to go ahead. But after that, the picture is deeply uncertain.

This is not a rumour or a worst-case scenario. This is the league's captain speaking on the record about the season's marquee event likely not happening. For players who left the PGA Tour for the financial security and format novelty of LIV, the reckoning is real.

Earlier this week, the Asian Tour announced it was joining the PGA Tour-DP World Tour alliance, effectively cutting off LIV's access to the feeder system it had funded with roughly $300 million. LIV is losing infrastructure and backing simultaneously.

What happens next depends entirely on whether O'Neil can find a buyer or partner willing to fund a tour that the PIF no longer wants to subsidize indefinitely.

### STORY 4: Women's Scottish Open

The Women's Scottish Open has arrived — and with it, one of the most intriguing fields of the summer.

The ISPS Handa Women's Scottish Open is underway at Dundonald Links in Gailes, North Ayrshire, Scotland, running July 23-26. The tournament carries a $2 million purse and, more importantly, serves as the last competitive round before the AIG Women's British Open — the final major of the 2026 season.

The headliner is Nelly Korda, the world No. 1. Korda arrives seeking form that will carry her confidently into the British Open. She has been the dominant force in women's golf for two-plus years, and every week of tournament golf at this stage of the season is a tune-up for the major that matters most.

The most talked-about other name in the field is Lottie Woad. The English amateur-turned-professional has made a rapid rise through the world rankings to No. 4, and she's doing it in her home nation this week — Dundonald Links is less than an hour from her roots in England and close to the coastline she grew up near.

Defending champion Lauren Coughlin is also in the field, as are Hyo Joo Kim (World No. 3), Charley Hull, Minjee Lee, Jin Young Ko, and 19 major champions in total.

The next major is coming. This is the last rehearsal.

### STORY 5: Haeran Ryu's Historic Double Major

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

Take a moment to appreciate what Haeran Ryu has done in 2026.

The South Korean golfer won the KPMG Women's PGA Championship on June 28. Less than two weeks later, she won the Amundi Evian Championship on July 12. Two LPGA majors. Four weeks apart. In the same calendar year.

Winning one LPGA major in a season marks a player as exceptional. Winning two — and doing it in consecutive events four weeks apart — is the kind of run that defines careers and earns comparisons to the sport's all-time greats.

The Evian Championship has been won by players like Inbee Park, In Gee Chun, and Lydia Ko. The KPMG Women's PGA is one of the oldest and most prestigious events on the LPGA schedule. Ryu has won both of them this summer.

She now leads the women's golf major season in 2026. With the Women's Scottish Open underway this week and the AIG Women's British Open approaching, Ryu is the player everyone else is measuring themselves against.

If you haven't been following her season, start now.
