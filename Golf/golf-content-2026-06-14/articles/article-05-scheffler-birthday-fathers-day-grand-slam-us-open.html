Scheffler's Birthday Major: Turning 30 on Father's Day While Hunting the Grand Slam at Shinnecock

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

June 14, 2026

Sometimes the sporting calendar aligns in a way that feels scripted. This is one of those times.

Scottie Scheffler — the world's No. 1 ranked golfer, the +550 favorite for the 2026 US Open — will play the final round of the championship at Shinnecock Hills on Sunday, June 21. That is the day he turns 30 years old. It is also Father's Day. And if he wins, it is also the day he completes the career Grand Slam.

The narrative surrounding the 2026 US Open would be extraordinary even without those details. With them, it borders on something a novelist would be embarrassed to invent.

What the Grand Slam Means

The career Grand Slam in professional golf means winning all four of the sport's major championships: The Masters, the PGA Championship, The Open Championship, and the US Open. Scheffler has won three of them. He needs only the US Open.

The list of golfers who have completed the career Grand Slam is short and historically significant: Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, and Tiger Woods. To join that group is to place oneself permanently among the absolute pinnacle of the sport's legacy players. It is a tier above simply winning majors. It is a tier above being great.

Scheffler is already considered by many to be the best player in the world. A Grand Slam completion would remove any remaining debate about where he belongs in the sport's history.

The Personal Layer

Birthdays pass in sport without much ceremony. Players have won majors the day after significant personal events, the day before, years after they stopped expecting them. But the convergence of a 30th birthday with a championship final round is rare in any sport.

Father's Day adds another layer. Scheffler has spoken publicly about the importance of family in his life and career. Playing the final round of a major on Father's Day — with the Grand Slam on the line — is the kind of moment that athletes carry with them for the rest of their lives, regardless of outcome.

At +550, the betting markets make him the clear favorite. The course suits his game. The moment is as large as any in contemporary golf. It begins in four days.
