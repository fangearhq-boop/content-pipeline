Canadian Open Final Round: Suber Leads at -13 With First PGA Tour Win in Sight

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

June 14, 2026

Jackson Suber has the lead. One round separates him from the first PGA Tour title of his career.

Suber enters the final round of the RBC Canadian Open at TPC Toronto at Osprey Valley holding a one-shot advantage at 13 under par — 197 through 54 holes — after rounds of 66-65-66. Bud Cauley sits one back at 12 under. Wyndham Clark, Tommy Fleetwood, Brice Garnett, and Jesper Svensson all occupy the 11-under mark, two shots from the lead. Defending champion Ryan Fox is three back at 10 under.

Sunday afternoon at TPC Toronto will determine whether Suber becomes a first-time winner, or whether the depth of the field catches him.

A Newcomer on the Verge

Before this week, Suber's best result on the PGA Tour was a tie for fourth at The CJ Cup Byron Nelson, just three weeks ago. That showing earned him attention. This week at Toronto has earned him something different: the weight of expectation that comes with leading a Sunday at a premier event.

Consistency has been the story of his tournament. Three rounds in the 60s, no dramatic scoring runs, just steady, pressure-tested golf on a layout that has challenged the best in the field. The opening two rounds were played in similar fashion, and by the midpoint of the week, Suber had established himself as the man the rest of the field was chasing.

The Competition Behind Him

Wyndham Clark charged on Saturday to close within two shots. Clark is a proven closer — he has won on the PGA Tour before and knows how to manage the final round's unique pressure. Tommy Fleetwood, entering the week as one of the favorites at +1200, also sits at 11 under and has the experience to make a run on any given Sunday.

Ryan Fox, the defending champion who won this title at TPC Toronto last year, is three back but very much alive. He knows the course, he knows what a final-round charge looks like here, and he has proven he can win in this country.

The $9.8 million purse — one of the richest on the tour calendar — adds additional weight to every swing on Sunday. For Suber, the winner's check would be secondary to what the win itself represents: the validated career-long investment in professional golf finally reaching its first peak.

What's Next

The RBC Canadian Open final round takes place today at TPC Toronto. The winner receives a full exemption on the PGA Tour and qualifies for upcoming major championships. For the winners circle, for the leaderboard, and for the story of Jackson Suber — it all happens today.
