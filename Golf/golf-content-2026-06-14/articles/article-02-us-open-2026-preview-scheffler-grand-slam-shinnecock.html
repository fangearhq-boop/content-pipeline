US Open 2026 Preview: Scheffler's Grand Slam Bid, Shinnecock Hills, and Four Days of Anticipation

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

June 14, 2026

In four days, the 2026 US Open begins at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York. And it is organized around a storyline that does not require any embellishment: Scottie Scheffler, the world's No. 1 player, needs one US Open title to complete the career Grand Slam.

He already has The Masters. He already has The Open Championship. He already has the PGA Championship. All that remains is the oldest and most brutally demanding of the four majors — and this year, it is waiting for him at Shinnecock.

The Course That Punishes Everything

Shinnecock Hills last hosted the US Open in 2018, when Brooks Koepka won for the second consecutive year, defeating Tommy Fleetwood by a single stroke. The layout plays as a Par 70 measuring approximately 7,400 yards — compact numbers that do not capture the reality of what the USGA prepares here.

The rough grows to five inches. The greens are some of the fastest and most severely contoured on any major championship rota. The wind off the Long Island Sound can render even a well-struck shot unplayable by the time it reaches the putting surface. Shinnecock Hills separates players by their precision, their patience, and their composure when the course turns hostile.

Scheffler's game is built for this. He is one of the most accurate ball-strikers in the world, which matters enormously on a course where the penalty for missing fairways is measured in double bogeys rather than routine pars from light rough.

The Rest of the Field

Rory McIlroy enters as the No. 2 betting choice at +900, hunting a seventh major title that would further cement his place in the sport's highest tier. Jon Rahm is +1400, Xander Schauffele +1600. Cameron Young, Tommy Fleetwood, Bryson DeChambeau, and Ludvig Aberg cluster around +2000 as legitimate contenders in a major that historically produces surprises.

The US Open does not always go to the chalk. The setup is designed to expose weaknesses that weekly tour golf tolerates. But in a week when the world's best player is hunting something this significant, the field tends to play in his shadow.

What's Next

The 2026 US Open runs June 18-21 at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club. First-round tee times will be announced this week.
