### Long-Form Post LIV Golf is facing its most serious existential moment since the league launched. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF) — the financial engine behind LIV Golf since its creation — has provided $196 million of the roughly $600 million the league requires to complete the 2026 season. That leaves a shortfall of more than $400 million, and CEO Greg O'Neil has publicly acknowledged that he cannot guarantee the final events on the schedule. Louisiana has already been postponed. LIV Indianapolis and the LIV Team Championship in Michigan are at risk of cancellation. LIV UK and LIV New York are currently expected to proceed, but the broader instability is undeniable. The deeper news is even more significant: PIF governor Yasir Al-Rumayyan has stepped down as LIV Golf's chairman, and Saudi Arabia has announced it will end funding for LIV Golf after the 2026 season concludes. The league is now actively seeking $250 to $350 million from new private investors to fund a revamped "LIV 2.0" plan. The circuit that disrupted professional golf and generated billions in controversy may not outlast this season in its current form. **What do you think the future of LIV Golf looks like?** ### Image Post Caption LIV Golf funding crisis: $196M received of $600M needed.
