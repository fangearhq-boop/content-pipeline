Travelers Championship 2026 Preview: Scheffler Favored at Final Signature Event as Clark Arrives Fresh Off US Open

By Jake Torres | June 23, 2026

The PGA Tour's final signature event of the 2026 season begins Thursday at TPC River Highlands in Cromwell, Connecticut, and the field assembled for the Travelers Championship is as formidable as any outside a major. A $20 million purse with $3.6 million to the winner draws 72 of the game's best players — including world No. 1 Scottie Scheffler, 2026 US Open champion Wyndham Clark, and a cast of familiar names who have collectively won more than half the events on this year's PGA Tour calendar.

Scheffler: Looking for Win No. 2

Scottie Scheffler enters the Travelers Championship as the +480 betting favorite at FanDuel Sportsbook — a reflection of his sustained dominance through the first half of 2026. He has posted eight top-five finishes in thirteen starts this season. He has also won only once. The disparity between his consistency and his victory count reflects the depth of the field at events of this magnitude, but Scheffler has made clear that wins, not top-five finishes, are the measure he cares about.

He won the Travelers Championship in 2024 and has a strong track record at TPC River Highlands. The course's birdie-friendly design suits Scheffler's aggressive ball-striking and precise iron play. If he gets a hot putter, the field will struggle to stay with him over four rounds.

Clark Brings US Open Momentum

Wyndham Clark arrives at TPC River Highlands two days removed from his wire-to-wire triumph at the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills. Clark won by a single shot over Sam Burns, closing with a composed round to hold off a Sunday charge. His field-best opening 64 at Shinnecock was the second-lowest round in that course's history, and he became the first player since Martin Kaymer in 2014 to lead a US Open from start to finish.

Clark also won the 2023 US Open at Los Angeles Country Club, making him a two-time champion in golf's toughest test. He enters Connecticut carrying both momentum and the validation of back-to-back appearances in the winner's circle at the sport's most demanding events. Against that backdrop, his odds at the Travelers are worth watching.

The Rest of the Field

Jordan Spieth — the 2017 Travelers champion — brings proven course history and the ability to get hot at any moment, particularly at events where he has previous success. Tommy Fleetwood (+1600) and Xander Schauffele (+1800) are consistent contenders at every event they enter. Ludvig Åberg (+2000) has emerged as one of the most exciting young prospects in the game and is capable of running away from the field if he finds his game on a soft layout.

Notably absent is Rory McIlroy, who has elected to skip the Travelers Championship to prepare for his links season ahead of The Open Championship. His withdrawal is one of the few factors that softens what would otherwise be a historic gathering of talent at a no-cut event.

What's Next

Round 1 tees off Thursday morning at TPC River Highlands. The no-cut format means every player in the 72-man field competes across all four rounds, raising the stakes for every player in the field from the opening tee shot. Given the scoring opportunities at this course, the lead after 36 holes will likely be well into double digits under par. The winner at Travelers is almost always someone who catches fire at some point in the tournament — and with this field, that someone is far from decided.
