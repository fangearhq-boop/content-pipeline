KPMG Women's PGA Championship 2026: Nelly Korda's Grand Slam Quest Arrives at Hazeltine

By Ryan Calloway | June 23, 2026

The KPMG Women's PGA Championship begins Thursday at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota, and the occasion carries a weight far beyond the event's record $13 million purse. Nelly Korda walks into this tournament as the dominant figure in women's golf and with a historical chase that has captured the attention of the sport: a third consecutive major championship, which would make her just the second woman in the modern era to accomplish that feat.

Korda's Historic Run

World No. 1 Nelly Korda arrived at the 2026 major season with ambitions already established by a dominant 2025 campaign. She opened 2026 by winning the Chevron Championship in April by five strokes, then added the US Women's Open by a single shot. In nine starts this season, she has won four times and finished no worse than tied for eighth in an individual event.

The last player to win three consecutive major championships in women's golf was South Korea's Inbee Park, who swept the ANA Inspiration and the KPMG Women's PGA Championship back-to-back in 2013. No woman has ever completed the calendar Grand Slam — winning all four women's majors in a single season — though the modern five-major structure has existed since 2013. A Hazeltine win for Korda sets up the Evian Championship in July and the AIG Women's Open in August as the final two legs of that pursuit.

Hazeltine and the Field

Hazeltine National last hosted this championship in 2019, when Australia's Hannah Green claimed her first LPGA title. Korda finished tied for third in that edition. Green — who now has three career major titles and 11 LPGA victories — returns this week still in search of her first 2026 win. Her knowledge of Hazeltine makes her a credible contender.

World No. 2 Atthaya "Jeeno" Thitikul has been knocking on the door of major breakthrough for years. The Thai star has five LPGA victories but has not won since the Maybank Championship in October 2024. Hazeltine represents another opportunity for a player widely considered one of the most talented on tour to earn her first major title. Minjee Lee, the 2025 defending champion, also headlines a field that contains 12 past KPMG champions.

A Record Purse

The $13 million total purse reclaims the KPMG Women's PGA Championship's position as the richest event in women's golf — a landmark that reflects the continued growth of investment in the women's game. The winner's share will be the largest check ever awarded in women's professional golf.

What's Next

Tee times begin Thursday, June 25 at Hazeltine National. Korda is expected to draw significant pairing and television attention throughout the week given the magnitude of her historical pursuit. Whether she completes the three-peat or faces a challenge from Thitikul, Lee, Green, or a dark horse from the 12-past-champion contingent, the 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship starts with the sport's biggest narrative fully intact. The final round Sunday at Hazeltine will write the next chapter.
