Nelly Korda's Hall of Fame Clock: A KPMG Win Puts Her One Victory Away from Enshrinement

By Ryan Calloway | June 23, 2026

There are two overlapping storylines following Nelly Korda into Hazeltine National Golf Club this week. One is the Grand Slam chase — can she win three consecutive majors for the first time in the modern era? The other is perhaps even more quietly profound: a win at the 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship would bring the 27-year-old American to 26 of the 27 qualifying points required for induction into the LPGA Hall of Fame.

The Numbers

The LPGA Hall of Fame requires a player to accumulate 27 qualifying points and complete at least ten years of LPGA membership. Korda joined the tour in 2016, making the membership requirement achievable in 2026 for the first time. She currently holds 25 qualifying points — a figure that climbed from 22 after the Chevron Championship in April to 25 after her US Women's Open victory earlier this month.

LPGA major victories count toward the point total, as do regular LPGA victories. A win at the KPMG would push Korda to 26 points. One more LPGA victory anywhere on the 2026 calendar — Evian, AIG Women's Open, or any regular-season event — would bring her to 27 and trigger formal eligibility for consideration and induction.

How Rare Is This?

The LPGA Hall of Fame is one of the most difficult individual sporting achievements to attain in professional golf. The point threshold is deliberately high, designed to recognize careers of exceptional consistency and longevity rather than short-term brilliance. Annika Sorenstam, Inbee Park, and Lorena Ochoa — three of the most decorated players in women's golf history — all required sustained multi-year campaigns to accumulate enough points for induction.

Korda is approaching that threshold at age 27. If she is enshrined this season, she will be one of the youngest players in LPGA Hall of Fame history to earn induction — a reflection of a career arc that has combined early LPGA victories with back-to-back major wins in recent years and now a full-season surge in 2026.

The 2026 Season

In nine starts this year, Korda has won four times and finished no worse than tied for eighth in an individual event. Her two major victories — a five-shot win at the Chevron Championship and a one-shot win at the US Women's Open — anchor a season already worthy of LPGA Player of the Year consideration, and the season is not half over.

If Korda wins at Hazeltine this week and then claims one more LPGA title at any point before December, she enters the Hall of Fame as an active player — one of the most accomplished in the history of women's professional golf, still playing some of the best golf of her career.

What's Next

Round 1 of the KPMG Women's PGA Championship begins Thursday, June 25 at Hazeltine National. Whether Korda adds to her Hall of Fame total or is held at 25 by the field assembled at Chaska will be one of the week's defining storylines. A win here would make the remainder of 2026's LPGA calendar feel entirely different — for Korda, and for everyone watching one of the sport's most singular careers reach its next milestone.
