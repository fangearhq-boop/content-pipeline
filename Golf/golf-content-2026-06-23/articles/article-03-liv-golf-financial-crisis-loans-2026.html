LIV Golf's Financial Reality: Running on Saudi Loans, $400M Needed, 2027 Season in Doubt

By Marcus Cole | June 23, 2026

LIV Golf has spent more than $6 billion since its launch in 2022. Today, the Saudi-backed golf league is operating on loans from the Public Investment Fund to finish a 2026 season that has lost an event already — and cannot secure the outside investment needed to guarantee its own future. The gap between LIV's ambitions and its financial reality has never been wider.

How the Money Works — and Doesn't

The PIF announced in April that it would fund LIV Golf only through the end of the 2026 season. To bridge the gap, the fund established a debenture lending arrangement — effectively providing loans rather than capital, a structural distinction with significant implications. It means the money comes with obligations to repay rather than equity ownership, and it signals that even the PIF is not treating LIV as a long-term open-ended investment.

LIV Golf spends approximately $100 million per month on operations. In 2026, the organization received $333 million in total funding — enough to cover roughly three to four months of expenses. The lending arrangement fills the shortfall, but the terms and amounts have not been disclosed in publicly available filings.

The league has been seeking $400 million or more in outside investment from private equity or commercial partners. As of publication, that investment has not materialized.

What Remains of the 2026 Calendar

Four events remain in the 2026 LIV Golf season: LIV Golf UK (July 23–26), LIV Golf New York (August 6–9), LIV Golf Indianapolis (August 20–23), and the $40 million Team Championship in Michigan (August 27–30). A fifth event scheduled for Louisiana was cancelled earlier this year, with summer heat cited as the official reason.

Two of the remaining four events are reported to be at financial risk of cancellation if the investment situation does not improve. The Michigan Team Championship, which carries a $40 million purse, represents LIV's marquee season finale and would be the most damaging cancellation to the league's credibility.

The 2027 Picture

LIV's proposed 2027 schedule represents a significant contraction from its current form. The plan reduces the event count from its 2026 total to just 10 stops — five described as "team majors" in established markets and five US-based "team signature events." Prize money at 2027 events is also expected to drop materially from the $32.3 million per-event purses that have defined LIV since its debut.

The scaling-back reflects a strategic shift toward sustainability, but it also reflects the absence of the outside capital that LIV's business model has always assumed. High prize money was the mechanism through which LIV attracted marquee players — and reducing it threatens the value proposition that justified players leaving the PGA Tour.

What's Next

The next LIV Golf event, the UK stop at July 23–26, will be the clearest signal of whether the league can complete its 2026 calendar. If the UK event runs without disruption and the investment search produces results before then, LIV will likely finish the year intact. If the investment gap persists and one of the remaining events falls through, the conversation shifts from "can LIV survive 2026?" to "what does LIV look like in 2027, if it exists at all?"
