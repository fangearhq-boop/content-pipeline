### STORY 3: Si Woo Kim Byron Nelson Heartbreak

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

Si Woo Kim had one of the most extraordinary weeks in recent PGA Tour history — and he came away with second place.

The story began in Round 2. Kim was on fire, making birdies at will across TPC Craig Ranch. He reached the 18th tee on pace for golf's rarest achievement — a round of 59. He made bogey. His card read 60. A course record, yes — but also one bogey away from something extraordinary.

He led after 54 holes with a 2-shot advantage over Wyndham Clark. On Sunday, he shot a respectable 4-under 67. On another week, it might have been enough. But Clark fired an 11-under 60 — the exact same score Kim had made famous in Round 2 — and the title was his.

Kim finished at 27 under, three shots behind Clark. Scottie Scheffler was a further two back in third.

For Kim, who has not won on the PGA Tour since the 2023 Sony Open, the near-miss adds to a painful trend. He has the game. He's shown glimpses of brilliance all year. The win will come.

### STORY 5: US Women's Open Preview

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

One of the most iconic venues in golf is hosting a women's major for the first time.

The 2026 US Women's Open will be played June 4-7 at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California. It will be the first USGA women's championship ever conducted at Riviera — a course that has hosted men's professional golf since 1929, including the 1948 US Open.

Riviera is a course that demands thought. The famous barranca that runs through the property creates unique strategic challenges. The kikuyu grass creates different lies. The par-4 10th and the closing stretch force difficult decisions. This is not a course where players can overpower their way to a trophy.

Into this setting comes Nelly Korda, the clear favorite. Korda has won three LPGA events in 2026, including the Chevron Championship — her third major title and 17th career win. She arrives as one of the best players in women's golf and with the kind of form that suggests she can contend anywhere.

Whether Riviera's unique demands create separation at the top or pull the field together remains to be seen. One thing is certain: watching the world's best women play this course for the first time will be something to see.

The US Women's Open begins Thursday, June 4.
