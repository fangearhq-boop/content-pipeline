2026 Charles Schwab Challenge Preview: Åberg Leads the Odds at Hogan's Alley

By Marcus Cole | May 26, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The PGA Tour's Charles Schwab Challenge begins Thursday, May 28 at Colonial Country Club in Fort Worth, Texas — one of professional golf's most storied venues and one of its most exacting tests. Known as "Hogan's Alley" after Ben Hogan's five victories here, Colonial demands precision above all else.

The par-70 layout stretches 7,289 yards and features fairways averaging just 27 yards in width — the 3rd-narrowest on the PGA Tour. Players who rely on power without accuracy suffer here. The field rewards mid-iron precision, course management, and a hot putter.

Field and Favorites

Two notable absences define the week: Scottie Scheffler and Rory McIlroy are both skipping Colonial, leaving the door open for the next tier of the game's elite to step through.

Ludvig Åberg heads the odds board at +850. The young Swede has posted three top-8 finishes in his last four Tour starts and has shown the kind of iron consistency that Colonial Country Club rewards. He hasn't won yet in 2026 and is overdue for a breakthrough result.

Ben Griffin (+2200) returns to defend the title he won here in 2025. Defending at Colonial is not easy — the field is strong and the course setup changes annually — but Griffin knows every inch of the place after his win last year. Justin Thomas (+2200) brings strong iron play and has historically performed well at precision-first courses. Russell Henley (+1800) is another name to watch, given his career accuracy metrics.

Why Colonial Produces Upsets

The history of the Charles Schwab Challenge — 80 years running — shows that accuracy specialists frequently beat the bigger names here. Power rankings don't apply at a par-70 where birdies come from approach shots more than wedges after bombed drives. Players who rank high in strokes-gained on approach and putting tend to thrive.

With the world's top two players absent, a first-time winner or a veteran rediscovering form feels like the likeliest outcome. This is the kind of week Colonial tends to produce.

What's Next

The Charles Schwab Challenge tees off Thursday, May 28 with round 1. The tournament runs through Sunday, May 31. Coverage is available on Golf Channel (Thursday and Friday) and CBS (Saturday and Sunday). Following the Schwab Challenge, the Tour heads to the Memorial Tournament at Muirfield Village, the last key event before the US Open.
