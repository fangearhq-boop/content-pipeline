Si Woo Kim's Byron Nelson Heartbreak: Course-Record 60 in R2, Led by Two, Finished Second

By Ryan Calloway | May 26, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Si Woo Kim played two of the most compelling rounds of the 2026 PGA Tour season at the CJ Cup Byron Nelson — and came away without a trophy. He shot a course-record 60 in Round 2, came within a bogey of joining the 59 Club, led the tournament by two shots after 54 holes, and then watched Wyndham Clark fire his own closing 60 on Sunday to steal the title by three shots.

Golf's proximity to history, both near-missed and achieved by someone else, rarely comes packaged so tightly into a single week.

Round 2: The Almost-59

Kim's Round 2 at TPC Craig Ranch was the most talked-about round of the tournament until Sunday afternoon. He made birdies in bunches across the front nine and kept his foot on the accelerator through the back. Standing on the 18th tee, he needed a par to shoot 59 — an achievement reached by only a handful of players in Tour history.

He made bogey. His card read 60, a course record. It was a stunning round regardless of the final hole, and it lifted him near the top of the leaderboard heading into the weekend.

The Final Round

Kim entered Sunday with a 2-shot cushion over Clark and Scheffler. He played solidly — his 67 was 4 under on the day — but Clark's 60 made the numbers work in only one direction. Kim was never in position to recover once Clark made his move on the back nine. He finished solo second at 27 under, three behind the winner and two ahead of defending champion Scheffler.

Kim has not won on the PGA Tour since the 2023 Sony Open — a drought that his Byron Nelson performance brought into sharp focus. He clearly possesses the talent and the capability to contend. Converting that potential into a victory remains the challenge.

What's Next

Kim will have multiple opportunities in the weeks ahead, including the Charles Schwab Challenge at Colonial Country Club (May 28-31) and the Memorial Tournament in early June. He is a player in search of a breakthrough result that will complete what his Round 2 at Byron Nelson already started to build. The question is not whether he can win — it is when.
