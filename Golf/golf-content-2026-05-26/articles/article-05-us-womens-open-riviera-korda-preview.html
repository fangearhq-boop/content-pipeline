2026 US Women's Open Preview: Korda, Riviera, and the First USGA Women's Championship at an Iconic Course

By Marcus Cole | May 26, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The 2026 US Women's Open takes place June 4-7 at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California — and it will be the first USGA women's championship ever held at this historic venue. Riviera has hosted professional golf since 1929 and held the 1948 US Open, but it has never before served as the site of a major women's championship. That changes in ten days.

Into this setting comes Nelly Korda, the player the entire women's golf world is watching. Korda has won three LPGA events in 2026, including the Chevron Championship — her third major title and 17th career win. She arrives at Riviera as the prohibitive favorite and as the central story of the week.

Riviera's Demands

Riviera Country Club is not a course that yields low scores to players who swing freely. The famous barranca — a dry creek bed that runs through the property — creates unusual lies and forces creative recovery shots at several holes. The kikuyu grass in the rough is thick and unpredictable, punishing approaches that miss the fairway.

The par-4 10th hole, with its iconic tiny front bunker and demanding approach, is among the most photographed holes in golf and one of the most strategically demanding. The closing stretch of Riviera requires sustained decision-making under pressure. This is a course that rewards patience and precision over raw power — which may play into the hands of the more cerebral players in the LPGA field.

Korda's Position

Korda arrived at the 2026 season on an already historic trajectory, having won the 2025 Chevron and the 2026 Chevron to collect back-to-back titles in the first major of each year. Her 17 career wins place her on the LPGA Hall of Fame path, and a fourth major title would accelerate her progress significantly.

Whether Riviera's unique character plays to her strengths or creates opportunities for challengers remains to be seen. Major championships at unfamiliar courses tend to level the playing field — and the 2026 field includes dangerous players who could challenge Korda over four rounds.

What's Next

The 2026 US Women's Open begins Thursday, June 4 at Riviera Country Club. Tee times and broadcast details will be confirmed in the days ahead. For Korda and the rest of the LPGA's elite, Riviera is the stage — and one of golf's most celebrated courses gets its first chapter in the women's major history books.
