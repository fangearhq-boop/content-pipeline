LIV Golf Korea 2026: Korean Golf Club Plays at Home — Preview, Schedule, What to Watch

By Jake Torres | May 26, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

LIV Golf's ninth event of 2026 heads to Busan, South Korea this week — and it arrives with a compelling new storyline. The team formerly known as Iron Heads has been fully rebranded as the Korean Golf Club, and the franchise has added native Korean players Ben An and Min-kyu Kim to its roster for the first time. Playing in Busan, in front of a Korean crowd, with Korean players representing their country's team: the setup does not get more natural than this.

The LIV Golf Korea tournament runs May 28-31 at Asiad Country Club, one of the South Korean peninsula's premier championship venues located in Busan — the country's second-largest city.

The Korean Golf Club Rebranding

The transformation of Iron Heads to Korean Golf Club is one of the more deliberate identity shifts in LIV Golf's short history. The franchise had competed under the Iron Heads name since LIV's founding, but heading into the 2026 season, ownership pursued a full overhaul — new name, new branding, new roster direction.

The addition of Ben An and Min-kyu Kim gives the Korean Golf Club a genuine national character, rather than a team with Korean branding but no Korean players. In Busan this week, that distinction will matter to the fans who show up.

Standings and Contenders

Lucas Herbert enters LIV Golf Korea as the current standings leader after his dominant four-shot victory at LIV Golf Virginia earlier in May — his first LIV win. Herbert of Australia leads Ripper GC and will carry the form of a recent champion into Asiad CC.

Jon Rahm, who has been the most consistent points accumulator in the season-long standings, will be among the favorites. His form has been strong in recent events, and the depth of the LIV field — featuring Bryson DeChambeau, Sergio Garcia, and others — makes this a competitive field on any week.

What's Next

Rounds 1 through 3 of LIV Golf Korea air on FS1 at 12:00 AM ET. The final round on Sunday, May 31 will be broadcast on FOX at 12:00 AM ET. There is no YouTube streaming for US audiences this week. Following Korea, the LIV schedule continues through the summer with additional international stops before returning to North America.
