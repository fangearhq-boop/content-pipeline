Wyndham Clark Fires Closing 60 to Win 2026 CJ Cup Byron Nelson — First Player to Win Twice With That Round

By Jake Torres | May 26, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Wyndham Clark entered the final round of the 2026 CJ Cup Byron Nelson two shots behind Si Woo Kim — a 54-hole leader who had already set the course record at TPC Craig Ranch with a 60 in Round 2. On Sunday, Clark answered with a round of his own. He fired an 11-under 60, made nine birdies and an eagle, and won the tournament by three shots at 30 under par.

It was one of the all-time Sunday performances in recent PGA Tour memory — and it made Clark the first player in Tour history to win twice using a final-round score of 60.

The Round That Won the Tournament

Clark's back nine was the stuff of highlight reels. He posted a 28 on the inward nine, making a series of birdies on the closing stretch at TPC Craig Ranch in McKinney, Texas. Combined with an eagle during his round, he produced a scorecard that left no statistical doubt about who had earned the title.

He never shot higher than 66 all week. Over four days, he demonstrated the kind of consistency — and explosive ceiling — that distinguishes contenders from pretenders on the PGA Tour.

The final leaderboard: Clark won at -30 (254). Si Woo Kim finished solo second at -27. World No. 1 Scottie Scheffler, the defending champion who entered the final round in contention, finished solo third at -25.

A Victory and a Piece of History

Clark had previously shot a closing 60 at the 2024 AT&T Pebble Beach Pro-Am, where he also won. That made him the only active PGA Tour player to have fired a 60 in a final round — but Sunday's performance went further, making him the first in Tour history to accomplish the feat twice in victories. The combination of rarity and stakes makes it one of the signature statistics of the 2026 Tour season.

The victory ended a drought that stretched back to Pebble Beach in early 2024. Clark had shown flashes of form during the intervening period but hadn't been able to close out a win. At Byron Nelson, he did everything right from the first tee to the last putt.

He earned $1.85 million from the $10.3 million purse and collected 500 FedExCup points — a significant haul that improves his standing in the season-long points race.

What's Next

The PGA Tour continues with the Charles Schwab Challenge at Colonial Country Club in Fort Worth, Texas, beginning Thursday, May 28. Clark will not be among the favorites to defend momentum at another precision-demanding course, but his Byron Nelson win ensures he arrives with confidence. For Scheffler and Kim, the next opportunity comes at Colonial, where both will look to bounce back from a deflating Sunday in McKinney.
