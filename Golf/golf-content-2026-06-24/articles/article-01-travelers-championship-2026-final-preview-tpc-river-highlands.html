Travelers Championship 2026 Preview: Five Players Who Can Win at TPC River Highlands

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

The PGA Tour's final signature event of 2026 begins Thursday at TPC River Highlands in Cromwell, Connecticut. The Travelers Championship brings together 72 of the world's best players for a no-cut, $20 million showdown — and with Rory McIlroy sitting this one out, the door is open for any number of players to close out the first half of the season with a statement win.

Here are five players who can win it.

Scottie Scheffler (+480)

The world No. 1 has been the best player on the planet for three years running. In 2026, he has posted eight top-5 finishes in just 13 starts. He also has exactly one win — The American Express back in January. That gap between results and wins has defined Scheffler's 2026 season, and TPC River Highlands is one of the best courses in the game for a ball striker of his caliber. If Scheffler converts even a fraction of his usual precision into birdies here, he will be difficult to beat.

Wyndham Clark

Clark entered tournament week having just won the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills — wire-to-wire, beating Sam Burns by one shot. That win came on the heels of a strong spring stretch that saw Clark win twice in his last four starts. He is the hottest player in the field by any measure. Whether he can carry that momentum after the physical and emotional demands of a major is the central question around his Travelers start.

Keegan Bradley

Bradley is the defending champion. He won this event in 2023 and again in 2025, beating Tommy Fleetwood by one shot in his most recent title here. He grew up in Woodstock, Connecticut, making TPC River Highlands the closest thing to a home course any professional golfer can have at the tour level. His course knowledge and comfort level at this venue are genuine advantages in a field that includes players seeing the layout fresh.

Jordan Spieth

Spieth won the Travelers Championship in 2017 and has consistently performed well at TPC River Highlands in the years since. He plays his best golf at events he connects with emotionally — and this is one of those events for him. If Spieth's putter gets warm this week, he has the creative shot-making around this course to compete with anyone in the field.

Sam Burns

Burns just finished second at the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills, posting a final-round 73 that kept him one shot back of Clark. That kind of performance — pushing the eventual major champion to the final hole — signals form. Burns is a strong ball striker with the consistency to compete across four rounds at a no-cut event, where the leaders keep pushing and the pressure never lets up.

What to Watch

TPC River Highlands plays as a par-70 and typically rewards aggressive birdie chasing. The scoring tends to cluster in the mid-to-low teens under par by week's end, meaning the tournament often comes down to who makes the fewest bogeys while consistently attacking pins. There is no cut to manage, which means the best players play all four rounds — and any momentum swing in a 72-player field can shift the leaderboard dramatically.

McIlroy's absence removes one legitimate contender but does not diminish the field. Twenty-two of the world's top 25 players are in Cromwell this week.

What's Next

Round 1 tees off Thursday at TPC River Highlands. Television coverage begins Thursday morning on ESPN and Golf Channel. The winner takes home $3.6 million and secures one of the more prestigious wins on the 2026 PGA Tour calendar. After this week, the tour shifts toward links preparation and the British Open rotation — making the Travelers Championship the last chance for players to add a signature-event win to their resume before the summer break.
