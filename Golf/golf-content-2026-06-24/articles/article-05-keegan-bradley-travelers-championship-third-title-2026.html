Keegan Bradley at the Travelers: Connecticut's Own Defending Champion Eyes History

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

Keegan Bradley grew up in Woodstock, Connecticut. He has won the Travelers Championship at TPC River Highlands in Cromwell, Connecticut twice — once in 2023, once in 2025. Thursday, he returns as defending champion to a course that is, by any reasonable definition, his home turf in professional golf.

A third Travelers title would put Bradley in a category that few professional golfers ever reach at a single event — not just a repeat winner, but a player who has owned a tournament over a span of years.

What He Has Already Done Here

Bradley's 2025 Travelers Championship win was a test. He edged Tommy Fleetwood by one shot, holding his lead through a back nine under pressure in front of a Connecticut crowd that has treated this week as something of a local celebration for years. He won in 2023 as well — a different kind of victory in terms of the field and the conditions, but the same result and the same course knowledge underneath it.

Two wins at the same PGA Tour event tells you something about a player's relationship with a place. The shots required, the local knowledge about wind patterns and greens, the comfort of familiar surroundings during a sport that demands unwavering focus — these things are real advantages. They do not guarantee anything, but they matter.

The Competition He Faces

Bradley is not the betting favorite this week. Scottie Scheffler owns that role at +480. Wyndham Clark enters the tournament as the freshest major champion in the field, having won the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills just days ago. Jordan Spieth won here in 2017 and carries the kind of course comfort that only tournament winners have. Sam Burns just pushed Clark to the final hole at the US Open.

The field is genuinely deep. Rory McIlroy is the only top-five player absent.

Bradley's edge is not talent — in a 72-player field of this quality, everyone is talented. His edge is knowledge. He has stood on these greens in the final pairing on Sunday. He has executed under the pressure of defending a title here. He has the specific experience of closing out a Travelers Championship lead, which is different from general tour experience.

The Ryder Cup Context

Bradley served as US Ryder Cup captain in 2023. That role added a dimension to his public profile beyond just tournament wins, and it brought him into a pressure context that is different from regular tour competition. Players who have handled that level of responsibility and public accountability tend to be comfortable in high-stakes individual situations as well.

The details of captaincy do not directly translate to a final-round leaderboard position. But the pattern of how Bradley has responded to major moments over the last several years suggests a player who does not shrink when the stakes rise.

What's Next

Bradley tees off Thursday in Round 1 of the Travelers Championship at TPC River Highlands. If he wins, he will become the first player to win the Travelers Championship three times in a five-year span in the tournament's modern era. The $20 million purse offers $3.6 million to the winner. Round 1 coverage begins Thursday on ESPN and Golf Channel.
