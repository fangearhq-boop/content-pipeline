LIV Golf's End Date Is Set: Saudi Arabia Stops Funding After 2026 Season

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund confirmed in April that it will end its financial support for LIV Golf at the close of the 2026 season. After pouring more than $5 billion into the league since its launch in 2022, the PIF is stepping back — and LIV Golf now has approximately four months to either find a replacement investor or wind down one of the most expensive experiments in professional sports history.

How LIV Got Here

LIV Golf launched in 2022 with the explicit backing of Saudi Arabia's sovereign wealth fund. The league paid massive signing bonuses to players including Phil Mickelson, Dustin Johnson, Brooks Koepka, and Jon Rahm. Prize money per event reached $32.3 million — nearly double what most PGA Tour events paid at the time. The strategy was disruption: sign the biggest names, offer the biggest paydays, and force a reckoning with professional golf's establishment.

That reckoning came, but not in the direction LIV intended. The PGA Tour survived intact. Most top-ranked players — Scheffler, McIlroy, Spieth, Schauffele — stayed. And LIV's television product never generated the viewership needed to attract the kind of independent commercial revenue that would reduce dependence on Saudi funding.

The league has been spending approximately $100 million per month. The PIF provided roughly $200 million in 2026 against a stated need of $600 million to complete the schedule. A lending facility was put in place in June to bridge the gap through the remaining 2026 events — but that is a loan, not an investment.

What Remains of 2026

Four events remain on the 2026 LIV calendar:

  • LIV Golf UK — July 23-26
  • LIV Golf New York — August 6-9
  • LIV Golf Indianapolis — August 20-23
  • LIV Golf Michigan (Team Championship, $40 million purse) — August 27-30

A senior LIV executive described every remaining event as uncertain. The Louisiana event scheduled for this month was already cancelled. CEO Scott O'Neil has publicly stated that the 2026 season will finish — but O'Neil also has a financial incentive to maintain confidence while the new independent board negotiates.

The New Independent Board

LIV established a new independent board led by Gene Davis and Jon Zinman. Their stated mission is to secure long-term financial partners who can support the league without Saudi government backing. The board is evaluating what it calls "strategic alternatives" — language that covers everything from a new investor to a sale to a structured wind-down.

Brooks Koepka, one of LIV's original high-profile signings, has already returned to the PGA Tour. What happens to the rest of the LIV roster when 2026 ends depends entirely on whether that independent board can close a deal before the season finishes.

What's Next

The next LIV Golf event is scheduled for July 23-26 in the United Kingdom. Whether it takes place — and whether there is a 2027 schedule to follow — depends on developments over the next several weeks. For professional golf broadly, the question is whether PGA Tour commissioner Jay Monahan will open any doors for LIV players looking to return once Saudi backing ends, or whether those players will need to navigate their own path back through qualifying processes. The conversation between the tours has not produced a merger agreement, and it is no longer clear who holds the leverage.
