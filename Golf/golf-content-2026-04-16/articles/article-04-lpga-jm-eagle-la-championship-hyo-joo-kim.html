Hyo Joo Kim's Final Warmup: LPGA Season's Hottest Player Targets LA Crown

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 16, 2026

The LPGA Tour has arrived in Los Angeles. One week from now, the first major of the women's 2026 season begins at Mission Hills Country Club in Rancho Mirage. Between now and then, the best women golfers in the world have 72 holes at El Caballero Country Club in Woodland Hills, California to calibrate their games for what comes next.

The JM Eagle LA Championship presented by Plastpro opened Thursday, and it does so with the most compelling figure in women's golf right now standing on the first tee.

Kim's Extraordinary Hot Streak

Hyo Joo Kim enters the JM Eagle LA Championship having won two consecutive LPGA events. She shot rounds of 61 in each of those wins — back-to-back tournament victories, back-to-back 61s. That combination of sustained elite scoring is as rare as anything in recent LPGA history.

Kim's most recent victory came at the Ford Championship, where her 61 left the rest of the field wondering what they were watching. Two wins, two record-low rounds, and now a final warm-up before the Chevron Championship. She is the clear form player in this week's field and the clear favorite heading into next week's major.

The question that surrounds any extraordinary hot streak is sustainability. Can anyone maintain scoring at that level through a third consecutive event? The statistical answer is almost certainly no — regression to the mean is real, and 61 is not a number that holds week after week. But Kim's recent performances suggest a player who has found something in her game that goes deeper than a two-week hot streak. The swing changes, course management, and mental approach that produced those back-to-back wins look sustainable in a way that pure scoring runs often do not.

The Chevron Backdrop

El Caballero Country Club is not the Chevron Championship. But everything happening in Woodland Hills this week exists in the shadow of what begins in Rancho Mirage seven days from now. The Chevron Championship at Mission Hills Country Club is the first women's major of the 2026 season, and the players in the JM Eagle LA field are acutely aware of that fact.

For Kim, this week is a chance to stay sharp, maintain confidence, and arrive at Mission Hills with three consecutive wins. For everyone else in the field, it is a final opportunity to find form before the highest stage.

A Deep International Field

While Kim is the central storyline, the JM Eagle LA Championship field includes significant international talent. Miyu Yamashita has been one of the LPGA Tour's most consistent players in recent seasons. Minjee Lee, the Australian star, has won at some of the tour's biggest events and brings legitimate title hopes to El Caballero. Sei Young Kim has shown the kind of sustained excellence over multiple seasons that makes her a threat every week she enters a tournament.

Hannah Green is also in the field. Nelly Korda and Atthaya Thitikul, two of the game's biggest names, are not in this week's lineup — both opting to use the week for rest and preparation ahead of the Chevron.

El Caballero and the Test Ahead

El Caballero Country Club in Woodland Hills presents a different kind of test from the marquee LPGA venues. The course demands precise iron play, rewards patience off the tee, and places a premium on course management over raw power. These characteristics have historically favored players with Kim's ball-striking profile — which only adds to the case that she enters Thursday as the player to beat.

Round 1 at El Caballero will be the first real indicator of whether Kim's historic form is carrying into the major-prep phase of the season, or whether the inevitable regression has arrived. Either way, the LPGA Tour has rarely produced a more interesting pre-major week than this one.

The Chevron Championship begins April 23. The warm-up is on.
