LIV Golf's Future on the Line as Mexico City Event Opens Under Crisis Shadow

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 16, 2026

The gates at Club de Golf Chapultepec opened Thursday morning. The players are on the course. LIV Golf Mexico City is officially underway.

But the most important action this week is not happening at 7,350 feet above sea level in the heart of Mexico City. It is happening in a boardroom in New York, where the future of the entire league may be getting decided.

The PIF Crisis: What We Know

Sportico reported this week that Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — the sovereign wealth fund that has bankrolled LIV Golf since its 2022 launch — is considering pulling its financial support for the league. The fund has poured more than $5 billion into LIV Golf over four years. It may now be reconsidering whether that investment makes strategic sense going forward.

LIV executives were summoned to an emergency meeting with PIF representatives in New York in the days leading up to the Mexico City event. The golf world has been buzzing with speculation ever since. Multiple sources have confirmed the meeting took place; the outcome is not yet known.

CEO Scott O'Neil moved quickly to contain the reaction. He sent an email to staff and players confirming that the 2026 season would proceed "as planned, uninterrupted and at full throttle." O'Neil stated that the league was "fully funded" through the end of the year.

But there is a meaningful difference between "fully funded for 2026" and "institutionally secure for the long term." That gap is where the uncertainty lives.

The Player Perspective

One LIV player, speaking anonymously to media this week, offered an optimistic read on what the emergency meeting might produce. "Hopefully it's a merger," the player said — a reference to the long-discussed possibility of a reunification between LIV Golf and the PGA Tour.

That sentiment reveals something about how at least some LIV players are processing the moment. The funding uncertainty is real. The hope, for some, is that it forces a resolution to the larger conflict that has divided professional golf since 2022.

DeChambeau Chasing History on the Course

While the institutional drama plays out in New York, Bryson DeChambeau is doing what he has done better than anyone else in LIV Golf history — playing golf at an elite level.

DeChambeau enters Mexico City chasing his third consecutive LIV Golf individual title. He has five individual wins across his LIV career and 15 total trophies including team events, more than any other player in the league's history. He won the last two LIV events, and he arrives in Mexico City as the most dominant force in the league's current standings.

The altitude at Club de Golf Chapultepec adds a layer of genuine complexity to the week. At over 7,000 feet above sea level, the air is thinner and golf balls travel meaningfully farther than at sea level. That sounds like an advantage for big hitters — but the trade-off is that distance control becomes significantly harder. Players who can adapt their approach to the altitude will have a clear edge.

Rahm Leading the Standings

Jon Rahm enters Mexico City as the current 2026 LIV standings leader, having won once and finished runner-up three times in his starts this season. His Legion XIII team won the 2025 Mexico City team title, and Rahm himself has unfinished business at this venue after coming close to individual honors last year.

The high-altitude environment suits Rahm's patient, course-management style. He is comfortable at Chapultepec in a way that suggests this week could produce another podium finish for the Spaniard.

What Comes Next

The Mexico City leaderboard will update hourly through Sunday's final round. The funding story may take weeks — or longer — to fully resolve.

LIV Golf has survived multiple rounds of existential doubt since its launch in 2022. The PIF has shown a willingness to sustain the financial commitment through difficult stretches. Whether that willingness extends through a strategic reassessment of this scale is a different question.

For now, the league plays on. DeChambeau is hunting a third straight title. Rahm is defending his standings position. The course is beautiful, the competition is real, and the backdrop — a financial crisis that may reshape professional golf — has never been more dramatic.
