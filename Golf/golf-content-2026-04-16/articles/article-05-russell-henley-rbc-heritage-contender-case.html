The Smart Money at Harbour Town: Why Russell Henley Is the RBC Heritage's Strongest Story

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 16, 2026

The RBC Heritage field is deep. Scottie Scheffler will get all the headlines as the favorite. Justin Thomas will attract attention as the defending champion. Viktor Hovland is drawing breakout-week predictions from analysts across the golf media landscape.

But if you want to understand who truly fits this golf course — who has the course history, the current form, and the specific skill set that Harbour Town demands — the answer is a quiet Georgian who just came off the best major result of his career.

Russell Henley is the most compelling story at the 2026 RBC Heritage.

The Number That Defines Him Here

Harbour Town Golf Links is a precision test. The par-71 layout in Hilton Head Island runs through tight, tree-lined fairways, features undulating greens affected by sea breeze, and consistently rewards the player who hits the most accurate approach shots over the player who hits the most powerful drives.

Russell Henley's stroke average at this golf course over 12 career rounds is 67.42.

That number is not a quirk of small sample size. Over 12 rounds — three full tournaments — Henley has shown up to Harbour Town and scored in the high 60s with extraordinary consistency. The course has given him almost automatic rounds in that range, tournament after tournament. He knows these fairways. He knows these greens. He knows how the sea breeze affects shot shape on every corner of this property.

Masters Form That Translates

Henley did not just play well at the 2026 Masters. He played some of the best golf of his career at Augusta National, finishing tied for third at 10-under — his best career result in a major. More importantly, he led the entire Masters field in Strokes Gained: Approach.

Strokes Gained: Approach is not an abstract statistic. It measures how much a player gains or loses relative to the field on approach shots. At Augusta National, approach play is critical. At Harbour Town, approach play is everything. A player who led every other major champion in the world in approach accuracy at Augusta just one week ago is bringing that form to the golf course where he has the best sustained stroke average of any current player in the field.

The logic writes itself.

He Has Already Won Here

Henley won the 2024 RBC Heritage at Harbour Town Golf Links. He knows what it takes to close a tournament at this venue on a Sunday afternoon. He has walked up the 18th hole here with the lead and made the pars that matter. That experience — specific to this course, this setting, this style of Sunday pressure — is not replicable through statistical analysis. It either exists in a player's history or it does not.

For Henley, it exists. And he has gained strokes on approach in each of his last five trips to Harbour Town — a run of consistent ball-striking at this venue that predates the 2024 win and continues through the present week.

Why the Odds Are Wrong

Henley is sitting at +1,800 to +2,000 across major sportsbooks entering Round 1. That pricing reflects his general market standing — a quality PGA Tour player, not a household superstar. It does not reflect his specific Harbour Town dominance, his current form, or the statistical alignment between what he does well and what this course requires.

Scheffler is favored for legitimate reasons. He is the best player in the world, he won here in 2024, and he is carrying motivational fire from a one-shot Masters loss. Those factors are real. But the gap between Scheffler's odds and Henley's odds does not fully account for what Henley brings to this specific venue in this specific week.

The Case, Simply Stated

Russell Henley just posted his best major finish. He just led the major field in the most relevant shot-making category for Harbour Town. He won the RBC Heritage two years ago at this exact course. He has a 67.42 stroke average here over 12 rounds — the best sustained scoring average of any player in this field at this venue.

Scheffler will get the headlines. Thomas will get the defending-champion coverage. Hovland will get the breakout-pick buzz.

Henley will just try to shoot 67. He usually does.
