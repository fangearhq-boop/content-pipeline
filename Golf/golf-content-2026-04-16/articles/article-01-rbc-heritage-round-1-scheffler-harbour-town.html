Post-Masters Golf Returns: Scheffler Headline Act at RBC Heritage Round 1

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 16, 2026

Augusta National is in the rearview mirror. The Harbour Town lighthouse is visible from the 18th green. And the 2026 PGA Tour season has moved on to its first post-Masters stop — the RBC Heritage at one of the most beautiful and demanding venues in professional golf.

Round 1 of the RBC Heritage is underway at Harbour Town Golf Links in Hilton Head Island, South Carolina. The field is deep, the course is demanding, and the storylines are rich with the lingering drama of what just happened at Augusta National one week ago.

Scheffler Enters Hungry

No player enters Thursday's first round with more to prove than Scottie Scheffler. The world No. 1 finished Sunday at Augusta in second place — one shot behind Rory McIlroy after a final-round 71 by the defending champion — and arrives at Harbour Town as the field's co-favorite at +390 odds.

That so-close Masters result has become the central narrative of this week's event. Scheffler won the RBC Heritage in 2024 and knows this golf course intimately. Whether he carries that near-miss as motivation or as emotional weight will be one of the most interesting things to track as the round unfolds.

Harbour Town Golf Links suits the way Scheffler plays golf. The par-71 layout rewards precision over power, places a premium on approach accuracy, and punishes careless mistakes with its tight, tree-lined fairways and undulating greens. These are exactly the conditions in which Scheffler has shown himself to be the best player in the world.

Thomas Defending After 2025 Title

Justin Thomas is back at the scene of his 2025 RBC Heritage victory, arriving as the defending champion and carrying that with him into the first round. Thomas has historically played well at Harbour Town — the course's demand for shot-shaping and precision suits his game — and defending titles has been a motivating factor for him in previous seasons.

The 2025 RBC Heritage was a defining win for Thomas in his career resurgence. Returning to the same course with the same trophy to defend adds a layer of pressure, but also a layer of confidence that only comes from knowing you have solved a particular golf course before.

Henley: The Form Player in the Field

Russell Henley may be the most compelling story in the field this week — not because of his odds, but because of what the numbers say about him at this specific golf course.

Henley finished tied for third at the 2026 Masters just one week ago. He led the entire field at Augusta in Strokes Gained: Approach — the single most important metric at a course like Harbour Town, where iron play determines outcomes week after week. He won the 2024 RBC Heritage at this exact venue. And his stroke average at Harbour Town over 12 career rounds is 67.42.

That last number deserves to be held for a moment. A 67.42 stroke average at Harbour Town means that when Henley shows up to this golf course, he is almost guaranteed to score in the high 60s. He has gained strokes on approach in each of his last five trips here. The Masters form is real, the course history is real, and the fit is real.

Hovland: The Breakout Watch

Viktor Hovland has drawn attention from analysts this week as a potential breakout pick. The Norwegian has shown flashes of his best form in recent weeks, and Harbour Town's style of play — requiring creativity, touch, and shot variety — aligns with what makes Hovland dangerous when he is at his best.

Jordan Spieth and Matt Fitzpatrick, both historically strong at Harbour Town, round out the contender picture in a field that is among the strongest in the post-Masters swing.

McIlroy Absent — By Design

The most conspicuous absence from the RBC Heritage field is the reigning Masters champion. Rory McIlroy will not be teeing it up at Harbour Town this week, having elected to take the post-Masters rest that his schedule and his body likely demanded after defending his title at Augusta.

McIlroy's decision to skip gives the field a slightly different feel — the champion is resting, the runner-up is hungry, the defending event winner is back, and the hottest iron player in golf just came off a career-best major result. That is a genuinely compelling field.

What to Watch

Round 1 at Harbour Town is already underway. The leaderboard will update throughout the day, and early scoring will tell us a great deal about who is comfortable on this course and who is still shaking off the emotional weight of Augusta.

Watch Scheffler's iron play specifically. Watch whether Henley continues the approach form he showed at the Masters. And keep an eye on the mid-round leaderboard for Hovland — when he gets it going on a course like this, the numbers can get very low very quickly.

Round 2 is Friday. Round 3 is Saturday. The 2026 RBC Heritage will have a champion by Sunday afternoon. For now, Harbour Town has the golf world's full attention — and that lighthouse is as beautiful as ever.
