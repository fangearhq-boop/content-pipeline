### Long-Form Post Monday morning. TPC River Highlands. One hole to decide the 2026 Travelers Championship. LIV Golf's Saudi Funding Ends — What Comes After 2026?
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund is done funding LIV Golf. After committing more than $5 billion to the breakaway tour since its 2022 launch — and absorbing annual losses estimated at $500 to $600 million — PIF is ending its financial backing after the 2026 season. The fund has been operating as a lending facility through this year, and LIV has received approximately $200 million of the $600 million it was promised. The consequences are already visible. The New Orleans event has been postponed as the tour explores fall dates. The tour is actively seeking $250 to $350 million in outside investment, working with financial advisor Ducera Partners. CEO Scott O'Neil insists the 2026 season will continue. The tour upgraded to 72-hole events this year and expanded to 57 players per field. A $40 million Team Championship in Michigan remains on the schedule. Jon Rahm has won events in Hong Kong and Mexico City. The new board — including Gene Davis and Jon Zinman — is in place. There is no PGA Tour merger in sight. The tour has funding through 2026. What comes after is the question no one has answered yet. ### Image Post Caption LIV Golf's Saudi backer is walking away after 2026. New Orleans already postponed. CEO says the season continues.
