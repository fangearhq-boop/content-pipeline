John Deere Classic Preview: Gotterup, Spieth, and Fowler Lead a Loaded Field at TPC Deere Run

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 30, 2026

The John Deere Classic returns to TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois on Thursday, and the field that has assembled for the July 2-5 event is better than what this tournament usually gets.

Chris Gotterup, the World No. 13, enters as co-favorite. Jordan Spieth and Rickie Fowler are in the field. There is a $8.8 million purse on the table, with $1,584,000 going to the winner and 500 FedExCup points at stake as the summer push continues.

Gotterup as the Man to Beat

Gotterup has built himself into one of the more compelling names on tour this year. A ranking of No. 13 in the world reflects consistent play at the highest level, and entering the John Deere Classic as co-favorite at a non-Signature Event suggests the oddsmakers see him as a genuine threat rather than a convenient top name.

TPC Deere Run rewards ball-striking off the tee and precise iron play — Gotterup's game fits the profile.

Spieth and Fowler Draw the Crowd

Jordan Spieth has always had a complicated relationship with golf's summer calendar. He is among the game's most popular players and a three-time major champion. When he is healthy and in form, he can compete with anyone. His presence at the John Deere Classic gives the event a name that draws attention beyond the typical weekend gallery.

Rickie Fowler brings similar crowd energy. No major title to his name, but one of the most consistent fan favorites in the game for over a decade. Both players add to what is shaping up as a quality field for a tournament that sometimes struggles to attract elite names in mid-summer.

The Stakes

The $8.8 million purse makes this one of the larger non-Signature Event paydays on the 2026 PGA Tour calendar. For players chasing FedExCup position before the playoff structure takes over, 500 points for a win is meaningful. A John Deere title is also a genuine resume item — this event has sent champions on to significant careers and bigger stages.

With the Open Championship at Royal Birkdale two weeks away, some players will also be using Deere Run as one final competitive tune-up before heading to the United Kingdom.

What's Next

First round tees off Thursday, July 2 at TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois. Four days of summer golf with an $8.8M purse, 500 FedExCup points, and a loaded field headlined by Gotterup, Spieth, and Fowler. The cut falls Saturday. The champion is crowned Sunday, July 5.
