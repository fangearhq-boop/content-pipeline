Open Championship Final Qualifying Today: 154th Open Field Takes Shape With 16 Days to Royal Birkdale

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 30, 2026

The 154th Open Championship is 16 days away. Today — June 30 — the last entries are being decided.

Final Qualifying for the Open Championship is underway at venues across the United Kingdom, including Notts (Hollinwell) and St Annes Old Links. The players competing today are fighting for one of the final spots in the field at Royal Birkdale, where the Open Championship runs from July 16 to 19.

The Birkdale Field

Scottie Scheffler is the defending champion, having won the 2025 Open Championship. The World No. 1 will arrive at Royal Birkdale carrying the form and confidence that has defined his recent run — he has won four of the last six majors and will be the favorite on a links course that suits power and precision equally.

Two notable names will not be in the Birkdale field. Tiger Woods is not entered in this year's Open Championship. Phil Mickelson withdrew earlier in June.

The rest of the field — headlined by Scheffler and the world's top-ranked players who qualified through various pathways — will be finalized once today's qualifying rounds are complete.

Royal Birkdale

Royal Birkdale, in Southport, England, is one of the most respected links venues in the world and one of the regular hosts of the Open Championship. The course demands imagination, links-specific shot-making, and the ability to manage wind and firm ground conditions. More than 300,000 spectators are expected over the four days.

The tournament is the oldest major in golf and carries a weight that the other three cannot match in terms of historical tradition and global reach.

The Scottish Open Tune-Up

The week before the Open Championship, the Genesis Scottish Open runs July 9-12 at Renaissance Club. It is one of the traditional tune-up events on the links calendar and typically draws a strong field as players seek course conditions similar to what they will encounter the following week at the Open.

Royal Birkdale and the Genesis Scottish Open together make July 9-19 among the most concentrated fortnight of elite golf on the annual calendar.

What's Next

Today's Final Qualifying results will complete the Birkdale field. The Genesis Scottish Open follows July 9-12 at Renaissance Club. Then comes the 154th Open Championship at Royal Birkdale, July 16-19. Scheffler defends. The rest of the world's best players arrive chasing the Claret Jug.
