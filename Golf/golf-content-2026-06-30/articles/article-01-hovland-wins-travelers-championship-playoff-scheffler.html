Viktor Hovland Ends 15-Month Drought, Beats Scheffler in Playoff to Win 2026 Travelers Championship

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 30, 2026

Monday morning. TPC River Highlands. One hole to settle the 2026 Travelers Championship.

Viktor Hovland and Scottie Scheffler had played 72 holes of golf and couldn't be separated — both men sitting at 21-under par as Collin Morikawa watched from the clubhouse at -20, a bystander in a playoff he wasn't part of. Then came the sudden-death round on the par-4 18th hole. Hovland won it.

The victory was Hovland's eighth career PGA Tour title, and his first in 15 months — a stretch of 146 starts during which the Norwegian had come close without breaking through. Monday morning at TPC River Highlands, he finally did.

How the Playoff Unfolded

Regulation ended with Hovland and Scheffler locked together at -21. Morikawa had put together a final round that threatened to make the playoff moot, but finished one shot back at -20, leaving the title to be decided between the world's best player and one of the most talented challengers in the game.

The playoff went to the par-4 18th hole — the same hole that had decided the tournament. Hovland converted. Scheffler didn't. The Travelers Championship had its champion, and it was not the man ranked No. 1 in the world.

Scheffler, who has spent 157 weeks at World No. 1, had another near-miss added to his 2026 ledger. The best player in the world played well enough to win and went home without the trophy — a scenario that has defined the competitive edge of this season more than perhaps any other.

What the Win Means for Hovland

Fifteen months is a long time to go without winning when you know you're capable of it. Hovland, who had collected seven PGA Tour titles before Monday, knew the drought was a story. He had answered questions about it at press conferences. He had played 146 starts carrying that weight.

The Travelers Championship ended all of that.

He became the first Norwegian to win this tournament, and the first international player to claim the Travelers Championship since Russell Knox did it in 2016. Against the deepest possible opposition — Scheffler at full strength, Morikawa within reach — Hovland delivered when it mattered.

The Numbers

Hovland takes home $3,600,000 from a total purse of $20 million — one of the largest on the PGA Tour calendar. He also earns 700 FedExCup points, a significant haul as the playoff push approaches.

The Travelers Championship is the final Signature Event of the 2026 PGA Tour season, which makes the result land with added weight. Hovland won the biggest closing event on the schedule, on the biggest stage, against the strongest competition.

Morikawa, who finished T3 at -20, remains in outstanding form. Scheffler remains the world's best player. But on Monday morning in Connecticut, neither of them was Hovland.

What's Next

The PGA Tour moves to the John Deere Classic starting Thursday, July 2, at TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois. For Hovland, the summer opens with momentum that was missing for 15 long months. The 154th Open Championship at Royal Birkdale follows on July 16. Hovland will arrive as a winner.
