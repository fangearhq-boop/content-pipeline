Haeran Ryu Wins First Career Major at KPMG Women's PGA, Ends Nelly Korda's Grand Slam Bid

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 30, 2026

Haeran Ryu walked off Hazeltine National Golf Club on Sunday as the KPMG Women's PGA Champion — and as a first-time major winner.

Ryu finished at -13 (275 total), two shots ahead of Ina Yoon, to claim the 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship. The record $13 million purse — the largest in the tournament's history — sent $1,950,000 to the champion. For an LPGA Tour sophomore collecting her first career major, the stage could not have been bigger or the reward more significant.

How Ryu Won

Ryu was steady throughout the week at Hazeltine National in Chaska, Minnesota. She finished two shots clear of Yoon, who pushed her but could not close the gap. The margin was comfortable enough to hold, and Ryu became a major champion before the final putt dropped.

The field had genuine contenders throughout, but Sunday belonged to Ryu.

Korda's Grand Slam Bid Comes Up Short

The other story at Hazeltine was Nelly Korda's pursuit of history.

Korda arrived at the KPMG having already won two majors in 2026 — the Chevron Championship and the U.S. Women's Open. A win at Hazeltine would have made her just the third player in LPGA history to win three consecutive majors in the same season. Babe Zaharias accomplished it in 1950. Inbee Park did it in 2013. Korda was trying to join them.

It did not happen. Korda shot a 73 in the final round and finished T8 at -6 — seven strokes behind Ryu. The dream of three consecutive majors in 2026 is over.

The near-miss also carries Hall of Fame implications. Korda currently sits at 25 of 27 required LPGA Hall of Fame points. A win at the KPMG would have added two points — enough to reach the 27-point threshold for eligibility. That milestone will have to wait.

The Record Purse

The 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship was played for a record $13 million purse — the largest in tournament history and a reflection of the LPGA's continued investment in prize money at its major championships. Ryu's winner's check of $1,950,000 is among the largest single payouts in women's golf.

What's Next

Ryu's first major is now on the record books. Korda, still among the best players in the world, continues her season with the LPGA Hall of Fame timeline extended. The next LPGA major is on the calendar later in the summer. For Korda, the pursuit continues. For Ryu, everything is different now.
