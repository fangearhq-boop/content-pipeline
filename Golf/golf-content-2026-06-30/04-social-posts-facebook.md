### STORY 1: Hovland Wins Travelers Championship

**Long-Form Post**

Monday morning. TPC River Highlands. One hole to decide the 2026 Travelers Championship.

Viktor Hovland and Scottie Scheffler had played 72 holes and couldn't be separated — both at 21-under par, with Collin Morikawa watching from the clubhouse at -20 after a stunning final round that wasn't quite enough.

Then came the playoff on the par-4 18th. And Hovland won.

It was his 8th PGA Tour title. His first in 15 months — a drought that stretched to 146 starts and made this one land differently. He became the first Norwegian to win the Travelers Championship, and the first international player to take this title since Russell Knox did it in 2016.

Scheffler, the World No. 1 with 157 weeks at the top of the rankings, had another near-miss. The best player in the world gave everything he had and still went home without the trophy.

Hovland takes home $3,600,000 and 700 FedExCup points from a $20 million total purse. The 2026 Travelers Championship — the final Signature Event of the PGA Tour season — ends with one of its most dramatic conclusions.

### STORY 2: Haeran Ryu Wins KPMG Women's PGA

**Long-Form Post**

Haeran Ryu stood at Hazeltine National on Sunday and did something she had never done before: won a major championship.

The LPGA sophomore finished at -13, two shots ahead of Ina Yoon, to claim the 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship — the first major title of her career. The $13 million purse (a record for the event) sent $1,950,000 to the champion.

Across the leaderboard, another story was ending. Nelly Korda had arrived at Hazeltine with a chance to become just the third player in LPGA history to win three consecutive majors in the same season. She won the Chevron Championship and the U.S. Women's Open earlier in 2026. One more and she would have joined Babe Zaharias (1950) and Inbee Park (2013) in a very short list.

It wasn't to be. Korda shot a 73 on Sunday and finished T8 at -6. The grand slam bid is over.

Ryu's major. Korda's milestone. Hazeltine delivered both storylines — one in triumph, one in near-miss.

### STORY 3: Open Championship Final Qualifying

**Long-Form Post**

The 154th Open Championship is 16 days away, and today — June 30 — Final Qualifying is underway at venues across the United Kingdom, including Notts (Hollinwell) and St Annes Old Links.

Royal Birkdale will host the Open Championship from July 16-19, with Scottie Scheffler defending his 2025 title. More than 300,000 spectators are expected over the four days at one of golf's most iconic links venues.

Tiger Woods is not entered. Phil Mickelson withdrew earlier this month. The week before the Open, the Genesis Scottish Open runs July 9-12 at the Renaissance Club — one of the traditional tune-up events on the links calendar.

Today's qualifying results will complete the Birkdale field. Sixteen days until the first major of the summer.

### STORY 4: John Deere Classic Preview

**Long-Form Post**

The John Deere Classic has always been one of the more honest tournaments on the PGA Tour calendar. No pretension. Quality golf. A great crowd in the Illinois summer.

This year's field delivers exactly that. World No. 13 Chris Gotterup enters as co-favorite. Jordan Spieth and Rickie Fowler — two of the most popular players in the game — are both in the field. A $8.8 million purse is on the table, with $1,584,000 going to the winner and 500 FedExCup points up for grabs.

TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois hosts Thursday through Sunday, July 2-5. With the Open Championship on the horizon two weeks later, this is one of the final chances to build momentum — or chase FedExCup position — before the summer's biggest major arrives.

Watch for Gotterup. And never underestimate what Spieth can do when he's healthy and in form.

### STORY 5: LIV Golf Saudi Funding Ends

**Long-Form Post**

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund is done funding LIV Golf.

After committing more than $5 billion to the breakaway tour since its 2022 launch — and absorbing annual losses estimated at $500 to $600 million — PIF is ending its financial backing after the 2026 season. The fund has been operating as a lending facility through this year, and LIV has received approximately $200 million of the $600 million it was promised.

The consequences are already visible. The New Orleans event has been postponed as the tour explores fall dates. The tour is actively seeking $250 to $350 million in outside investment, working with financial advisor Ducera Partners.

CEO Scott O'Neil insists the 2026 season will continue. The tour upgraded to 72-hole events this year and expanded to 57 players per field. A $40 million Team Championship in Michigan remains on the schedule. Jon Rahm has won events in Hong Kong and Mexico City. The new board — including Gene Davis and Jon Zinman — is in place.

There is no PGA Tour merger in sight.

The tour has funding through 2026. What comes after is the question no one has answered yet.
