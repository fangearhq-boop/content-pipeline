### STORY 1: Aaron Rai Wins PGA Championship — 290-1 Longshot Makes History
- **Tier:** 1
- **Category:** Major Championship Result / Historic Achievement
- **Why it matters:** Aaron Rai shot a final-round 65 at Aronimink Golf Club to win the 108th PGA Championship at 9-under par, three shots clear of Jon Rahm and Alex Smalley. A 290-1 longshot on Thursday morning — the biggest opening-day odds to win a major in at least 20 years — Rai became the first Englishman to win the PGA Championship since Jim Barnes in 1919. He sank a 70-foot putt on the 17th hole and made an eagle at the ninth to spark a back-nine charge from three behind to three clear.
- **Angle:** The ultimate underdog story: a quiet English golfer who wears two gloves, was ranked outside the top 50 entering the week, and won a major on Sunday in a manner nobody saw coming.
- **Posting window:** 8:00 AM (major result recap)

- **Tier:** 2
- **Category:** Player Feature / Human Interest
- **Why it matters:** Most casual golf fans woke up Sunday with no idea who Aaron Rai was. By Sunday evening, they knew.
