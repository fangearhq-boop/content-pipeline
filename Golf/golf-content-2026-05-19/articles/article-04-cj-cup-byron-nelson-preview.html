CJ Cup Byron Nelson Preview: Scheffler Defends as Rai Arrives as a Champion

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 19, 2026

The week after a major championship has its own particular energy on the PGA Tour. Players who contended are processing the result. Those who missed the cut are hungry to rebuild. And the returning field at a regular tour event gets augmented by one extraordinary variable — this year, the new major champion is teeing it up just four days after the most significant victory of his career.

The CJ Cup Byron Nelson at TPC Craig Ranch in McKinney, Texas, begins Thursday May 21. Aaron Rai — the 2026 PGA Champion, less than 96 hours removed from sinking the winning putt at Aronimink — is in the field alongside defending champion Scottie Scheffler and a 144-player lineup that represents one of the deeper post-major fields of the year.

Scheffler's Defense

Scottie Scheffler's 2025 CJ Cup Byron Nelson victory was not just a win — it was a demolition. His 31-under par total completely dismantled TPC Craig Ranch and set a benchmark that the field will need to approach to challenge him this week. Scheffler returns as the world's No. 1 player and the tournament's defending champion, opened as the betting favorite at +150.

TPC Craig Ranch's 7,569-yard, par-71 layout has consistently produced very low winning scores — the fairways, the conditions, and the architecture of the course invite aggressive play. Scheffler's precision iron play and his composure under pressure make him well-suited to this environment regardless of the competition around him.

The PGA Championship was not a strong result for Scheffler — he was in contention at points but finished well behind Rai. A dominant Byron Nelson defense would re-establish the narrative around his world No. 1 status and give him momentum heading into the summer stretch.

The Rai Storyline

Aaron Rai's presence in the Byron Nelson field — just days after winning the PGA Championship — raises an almost philosophical question about professional sport: when is the right time to play again after the peak experience of your career?

Historically, major champions have handled this differently. Some channel the momentum directly into strong follow-up performances, riding the confidence of validation. Others need time to process the weight of what happened before they can compete at full effectiveness. Rai has never been in this position before, and watching how he navigates Thursday through Sunday will be one of the most compelling subplots of the week.

Other Contenders

Jordan Spieth is a particularly notable presence this week. A Dallas-area native, Spieth plays the Byron Nelson in front of a home crowd that has adored him throughout his career. The combination of local support and a scoring-friendly environment has consistently produced strong results for Spieth at this event, and at +1500 on the odds board, he represents significant value if his game is in form coming out of the major.

Si Woo Kim rounds out the top of the early odds at +1300, with course history making him a credible threat. The full 144-player field ensures the week will produce drama across all four days at one of the most accessible and fan-friendly stops on the tour calendar.

What's Next

The CJ Cup Byron Nelson begins Thursday, May 21 at TPC Craig Ranch in McKinney, Texas, with rounds running through Sunday May 24. The tournament offers a $10.3 million purse. Television coverage will run on Golf Channel and CBS, with streaming available through Peacock. For the golfers who were at Aronimink last week — including the new major champion — this is the first chance to see what comes next.
