Smalley's Heartbreak and Rahm's Resilience: Two Stories Behind the PGA Championship's T2

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 19, 2026

When Aaron Rai was charging up the back nine Sunday at Aronimink, two players were waiting to see if the trophy would ultimately come to them. Alex Smalley had led the PGA Championship for 54 holes. Jon Rahm was playing some of the best golf he has produced at a major in months. Both finished at six-under par, three shots behind the champion. Their Sunday stories, though they ended in the same place in the standings, could not have been more different.

Smalley: From Leader to Runner-Up

Alex Smalley walked to the first tee on Sunday with a share of the 54-hole lead and the weight of expectation that no amount of preparation fully prepares a player for. He had led a major championship before — this was not entirely new territory. But the final round of the PGA Championship is a different animal than any other competitive environment, and Sunday proved it.

The sixth hole was the turning point. Smalley made a double-bogey — two shots dropped in a single hole on the most important day of his competitive life. The eighth followed with another bogey. In the space of three holes, he had dropped three shots and surrendered the lead to the players behind him. The tournament he had controlled for three days began slipping away.

He recovered with an eagle at the 16th and a birdie at the 18th, and those late heroics gave him a share of the runner-up position. His final score of six-under was identical to his 54-hole total, meaning the final round, for all its drama, was played in even par. But the composure to birdie and eagle when the round was already difficult speaks to Smalley's character as a competitor.

He still has not won on the PGA Tour. He has now had two significant near-misses at the sport's highest level. The talent is unambiguously present. The major victory, when it comes, will have been earned through exactly this kind of experience.

Rahm: A Statement From LIV Golf

Jon Rahm's presence at the top of the PGA Championship leaderboard carries a context beyond individual performance. Rahm plays on LIV Golf — the Saudi-backed series that sits outside the PGA Tour's sanctioned ecosystem — and his appearances at major championships, which remain open to all eligible players regardless of tour affiliation, generate ongoing conversation about the relative levels of competition across the divided professional landscape.

A final-round 68 — four birdies, two bogeys — placed Rahm in a tie for second at the most prestigious event of the non-Masters major calendar. It was a composed, professional performance from a player who has won major championships before and knows how to compete when the stakes are highest. His presence on the final leaderboard, alongside tour members from the PGA Tour and DP World Tour, reinforces the argument that LIV Golf's best players remain among the world's best players.

Rahm was the 2023 Masters champion. His T2 at the 2026 PGA Championship adds to a major resume that the LIV move has clearly not diminished.

What's Next

Both Smalley and Rahm carry their Aronimink finishes forward into the remainder of the 2026 season. Smalley's T2 result earns him additional world ranking points and a strong platform for the second half of the year. The PGA Tour's next event is the CJ Cup Byron Nelson at TPC Craig Ranch in McKinney, Texas, starting Thursday May 21, where the post-major bounce-back opportunities begin. For those who played at Aronimink, the week ahead is about processing the results — and finding the next window.
