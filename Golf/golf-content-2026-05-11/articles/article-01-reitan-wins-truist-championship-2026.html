Kristoffer Reitan Claims First PGA Tour Win at Truist Championship, Edges Fowler and Hojgaard by Two

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 11, 2026

Kristoffer Reitan walked onto Quail Hollow Club's 18th green on Sunday afternoon without a single PGA Tour victory to his name and walked off with one of the biggest wins a rookie can claim. The 28-year-old Norwegian shot a final-round 2-under 69 to win the 2026 Truist Championship at 15-under 269, finishing two shots clear of Rickie Fowler and Nicolai Hojgaard in his 15th career PGA Tour start.

The $3.6 million prize — drawn from a $20 million purse — reflects both the scale of the event and the significance of the performance. The Truist Championship at Quail Hollow is one of the premier stops on the PGA Tour's signature event schedule, with a field that routinely draws the world's best players. Reitan was not supposed to win it. He did.

"This is what you work for," Reitan said after the round. "Fifteen starts. You just keep putting in the work."

A Final Round With Multiple Story Lines

The leaderboard entering Sunday was as competitive as the field suggested it would be. Alex Fitzpatrick held the 54-hole lead at 14-under after setting up the final-round drama with a consistent three-day performance. Cameron Young had fired a stunning 8-under 63 in the third round to pull himself into contention at 12-under — a round good enough to lead most tournaments, but not quite enough to overtake Fitzpatrick.

And somewhere behind them, McIlroy was working his way back. After a rough third-round 75, the Northern Irishman — who had successfully defended his Masters title just weeks earlier — came out swinging on Sunday with a 67 that showcased the ball-striking that makes him one of the most dangerous players in the world on approach shots. It was a reminder, at a time when reminders are useful, that McIlroy is heading to the PGA Championship at Aronimink in better shape than Saturday's score card suggested.

But it was Reitan who outlasted the field. While Fitzpatrick slipped and Fowler pushed to the end, the Norwegian rookie steadied his game at the moment that counted and shot the round he needed. Fowler and Hojgaard each finished at 13-under; Fitzpatrick fell back to fourth place.

The Bigger Picture Heading Into Major Season

The Truist Championship result sends the field directly into one of the most anticipated stretches of the 2026 golf calendar. The PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club begins Thursday, just four days after the Truist concluded. McIlroy's late-round 67 gives him a reason for confidence heading into a major where he is chasing something unprecedented in the modern era — a calendar Grand Slam.

Young's 63 in Saturday's third round will also follow him into the PGA Championship field as a demonstration of form. He arrives with two PGA Tour wins in 2026 (the Players Championship and the Doral event) and a leaderboard presence in multiple other events that suggests he is ready to make the next step. That step — a first major championship — may be coming soon.

For Reitan, however, Sunday was entirely its own story. The Truist Championship was his breakthrough, and nothing else needs to be attached to it. His first win is his to keep.

What's Next

Reitan's win qualifies him for various upcoming Tour events, and his breakthrough will ensure his exemption status on the PGA Tour for the foreseeable future. For the rest of the field, the focus turns immediately to Aronimink and the PGA Championship. The second major of 2026 begins May 14, with the full broadcast schedule available on ESPN and ESPN+.
