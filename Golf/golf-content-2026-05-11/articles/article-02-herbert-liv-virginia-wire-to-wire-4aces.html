Herbert Wins LIV Virginia Wire-to-Wire Despite Flu; Anthony Kim's Bogey-Free 62 Powers 4 Aces GC Team Win

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 11, 2026

Lucas Herbert started the week at Trump National Golf Club in Sterling, Virginia, battling a flu that would have had most athletes reconsidering their plans. He finished it as the individual champion of LIV Golf Virginia, winning wire-to-wire at 24-under par — four shots clear of the field — in what amounts to one of the more determined performances of the 2026 LIV season.

Herbert's final-round 3-under 69 was not spectacular by the standards of a tournament that had seen low numbers all week, but it was exactly what he needed. He came to the 18th hole with his lead intact, made his par, and claimed his first individual title on the LIV Golf circuit.

"I felt terrible for most of the week," Herbert said afterward. "But I just tried to stay in the moment and play one shot at a time. The lead helped."

Garcia's Push Falls Short

Sergio Garcia, one of the circuit's more consistent performers and a former Masters champion, made a move on the back nine of the final round that had the leaderboard briefly tightening. Garcia pulled to within one stroke of Herbert at one stage, setting up what looked like a potential down-the-stretch battle.

Herbert responded with a birdie to reassert his lead and Garcia had no further answer. Garcia finished second. The four-shot margin at the end was a comfortable cushion built through consistency across four rounds, even if the process involved more adversity than the score sheet reveals.

Anthony Kim's 62 Makes the Team Drama

The individual competition at LIV Golf Virginia had its conclusion. But the team competition delivered a different kind of story entirely.

The 4 Aces GC and the Fireballs entered the final round with everything still to play for in the team standings. When the dust settled, both teams had finished at 49-under par as a team aggregate across the four rounds. A playoff would decide the title.

What forced that playoff was one of the rounds of the LIV season: Anthony Kim's bogey-free 10-under 62. Kim — the former American prodigy whose career was derailed by injury for years before his return to professional golf with LIV — shot one of the lowest rounds in the event's history without a single dropped shot on the card. His performance was enough to pull the 4 Aces GC level with the Fireballs and force the overtime.

In the playoff itself, Kim and teammate Thomas Detry each made par on the 18th hole to clinch the team title on the first extra hole. It was the 4 Aces GC's third team title of the 2026 LIV Golf season, reinforcing their standing as the circuit's dominant franchise in the team competition format.

What's Next

LIV Golf continues its 2026 schedule with events planned across the summer months. For Herbert, the victory at Virginia adds individual silverware to a career that has included consistent LIV performances without the solo title until now. Kim's 62, meanwhile, will be remembered as one of the round of the season regardless of what follows.

The golf world turns immediately to Aronimink Golf Club and the PGA Championship beginning May 14. LIV players including Bryson DeChambeau, Jon Rahm, and Dustin Johnson are expected in the field, bringing their LIV form into major championship competition for the second time this season.
