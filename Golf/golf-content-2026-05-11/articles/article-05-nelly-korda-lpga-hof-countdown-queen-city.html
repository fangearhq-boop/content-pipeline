Nelly Korda Is 4 Points From the LPGA Hall of Fame — The Kroger Queen City Championship Is Next

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 11, 2026

Nelly Korda is four points away from the LPGA Hall of Fame.

The American superstar enters the 2026 Kroger Queen City Championship presented by P&G — beginning Thursday, May 14 at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati — with 23 of the 27 points required for Hall of Fame qualification. She added three LPGA victories in 2026, each one tightening the screws on what is becoming one of the most anticipated milestones in women's golf.

The Hall of Fame is not simply a matter of winning tournaments. The LPGA's points system values major championship victories, Player of the Year awards, and consistent excellence across a career. For Korda, the accumulation of those points has come through sustained dominance — she is the No. 2 player in the world rankings, behind only Jeeno Thitikul, and has been one of the two or three best players on the tour for the past two-plus seasons.

Four points. In terms of career trajectory, that distance is a rounding error. In terms of the specific results needed to cross the line, it is still a gap that requires continued performance at the highest level. Every tournament Korda plays from this point forward is a potential moment.

The Queen City Field

The 2026 Kroger Queen City Championship has assembled one of the stronger LPGA fields of the season. Thitikul, the No. 1 player in the world, is in the field — their presence together at Maketewah creates the kind of head-to-head dynamic that elevates a regular-season event into a must-watch competition. Charley Hull, the popular English fan favorite who has been a consistent presence at the top of LPGA leaderboards in recent seasons, is also committed. Lydia Ko, who previously won the Queen City Championship, rounds out a top-tier field that reflects the strength of the current LPGA Tour.

Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati provides a venue with enough challenge and history to complement the story lines competing for attention. Cincinnati's golf community has embraced the event, and the galleries are consistently among the most engaged on the LPGA Tour's schedule.

The Math of the Milestone

An LPGA Tour win adds a specific number of points to a player's Hall of Fame total, with major championships worth more. At 23 points, Korda needs enough results — a combination of wins, major championships, and Player of the Year points — to push over 27. The fastest single-event path to the milestone would be a major championship victory; a regular tour event like the Queen City Championship moves the needle in a meaningful but not final way.

The timeline for Korda's Hall of Fame crossing has been moving forward throughout 2026. Beginning the year seemingly a few tournaments away from history, she has continued to perform at the level that got her there — but the math has not yet resolved itself cleanly. This week is another chance.

What's Next

The Kroger Queen City Championship runs May 14-17 at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati. Coverage is available on Golf Channel and Peacock. Following the Queen City event, the LPGA schedule continues with major championship season reaching its next peak in June with the US Women's Open.

For Korda, every round of competitive golf right now is played in the context of the Hall of Fame countdown. Whether the milestone arrives in Cincinnati, at a major championship this summer, or at some later point in the season, the history is close enough to feel tangible. The players know it. The galleries know it. Nelly Korda knows it.

Four points. The clock is running.
