Tommy Fleetwood Is in Contention at His Childhood Major — With His Father on the Bag

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 18, 2026

In 1998, a 7-year-old Tommy Fleetwood came to Royal Birkdale with his father to watch The Open Championship. He stood in the gallery on a links course six miles from his hometown of Southport and watched the best golfers in the world compete for the Claret Jug.

On Saturday, his father is carrying his bag. And Fleetwood is four under par and in contention for the biggest title in the sport.

The Emotional Weight of This Week

Few sporting scenarios carry the resonance of a hometown athlete competing on familiar ground for the highest prize in their discipline. Fleetwood has described Royal Birkdale as a deeply personal place — a course he has played throughout his life, in every season, in every condition that the Lancashire coast can produce.

He skipped the Scottish Open this year, choosing instead to stay home and prepare specifically for Birkdale. The decision reflects how much this particular week matters to him. His father's presence on the bag adds another layer to what is already an emotionally charged story for the local galleries — who have been among the most vocal and partisan of any Open week in recent memory.

Where He Stands

Fleetwood is at -4 heading into Round 3, six shots behind leader Lucas Herbert. He is grouped with Jon Rahm of LIV Golf's Legion XIII for Saturday's round — both at -4, both capable of making a significant move on moving day.

No English player has won The Open Championship since Nick Faldo triumphed in 1992 — 34 years ago. Fleetwood is the most persistent English contender without a major title in his generation. In 2018 at Carnoustie, he was third. At St Andrews in 2022, he was in the conversation. At Royal Birkdale in 2026, he is within six shots of the lead with two rounds to play.

What's Next

Round 3 is being played Saturday. The final round is Sunday, July 19. Fleetwood would need to reduce the six-shot deficit to Herbert while other contenders apply their own pressure. But the circumstances are unique: the course, the crowd, the family, the history. If there is a golf story being written this week at Royal Birkdale, Tommy Fleetwood is its most compelling character.
