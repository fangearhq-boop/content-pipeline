Scheffler Is Hitting Fairways at a Historic Rate — and Missing Birdie Putts at a Painful One

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 18, 2026

Scottie Scheffler may be the best ball-striker at the 154th Open Championship. The numbers make a compelling case. In Round 1 at Royal Birkdale, he hit 13 of 14 fairways — the highest fairway-finding rate in the entire field. His iron play through 36 holes has been as precise as anyone's at this level of competition.

But the putter is betraying him. And at Royal Birkdale, with the field condensed and a leader sitting at -10, the betrayal has cost him dearly.

The Numbers That Hurt

In Round 2, Scheffler missed seven birdie putts from 15 feet or closer. Four of those were from inside 10 feet — distances that, over a career at the highest level, he would expect to convert at a significant rate. Each missed putt kept the scorecard from reflecting the quality of the round his approach play deserved.

He shot back-to-back 68s and sits at -4 heading into Round 3 — six shots behind leader Lucas Herbert, three behind the group at -7, and tied with Tommy Fleetwood, Jon Rahm, and others who have been more efficient with their scoring opportunities.

The Defender's Perspective

Scheffler is the defending Champion Golfer of the Year, and he carries the expectations that accompany that title. He is also the World No. 1 — the player every other competitor in the field is measuring themselves against.

Six shots down with two rounds remaining is a significant deficit at any major, but links courses can swing wildly. A day where the wind picks up and the greens firm, a day where a few putts start falling — Scheffler has shown throughout the 2026 season that when he gets hot, very few players can match his level.

The question for Saturday's round is straightforward: does the flat stick wake up? If it does, Scheffler at -8 or -9 by Saturday evening turns this into a proper hunt. If it doesn't, the deficit becomes a respectful final-round chase rather than a genuine title bid.

What's Next

Round 3 is underway Saturday at Royal Birkdale. Scheffler will need to shoot something in the range of 63-64 to meaningfully challenge the lead. The final round is Sunday, July 19. Monitor his putting statistics as the round progresses — if the numbers improve, the conversation around the Claret Jug becomes very different by Saturday evening.
