LIV's Lucas Herbert Fires Record-Tying 62, Leads Open Championship at -10

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 18, 2026

In 154 editions of The Open Championship, very few rounds have matched what Lucas Herbert produced at Royal Birkdale on Friday. The Australian LIV Golf player fired a second-round 62 — tying the men's major championship scoring record for a single round — to vault to -10 and lead the field heading into Round 3 on Saturday.

Herbert birdied his first three holes. He reached the turn in 28 strokes. When he was done, he had put himself at the front of the most prestigious golf tournament in the world, six shots clear of most of the field and two ahead of his nearest challengers.

Who Is Lucas Herbert?

Herbert, 30, plays for Ripper GC on the LIV Golf circuit — the Saudi-backed league that has had a complicated relationship with the traditional major championship ecosystem. He earned his spot in the Open field through his LIV Golf performance, having won the LIV Golf Virginia event in May 2026 and posting four other top-10 finishes in the 2026 LIV season.

This is not his first Open Championship, but it is his first time leading one. On a course that demands patience, creativity, and the ability to manage links conditions, Herbert has been the best player in the field through 36 holes — by a clear margin.

The Leaderboard Entering Round 3

The group chasing Herbert heading into Moving Day is formidable. Jackson Suber, Cameron Young, and Ryan Gerard are all tied at -8. Sam Burns — who also fired a record-tying 62 in Round 2 — sits at -7. Bryson DeChambeau (LIV), despite receiving a controversial two-stroke post-round penalty, enters Round 3 at -5.

Further back but very much in contention are Scottie Scheffler (defending Champion Golfer of the Year), Tommy Fleetwood, and Jon Rahm — all at -4. Rory McIlroy, who made the cut at -1, is 9 strokes back entering Round 3.

Herbert and Suber form the final pairing for Round 3, tee sheet set for Saturday afternoon in the UK.

What's Next

Round 3 is underway Saturday at Royal Birkdale. The final round takes place Sunday, July 19. Herbert will need to hold his composure under a pressure he has not experienced at a major. The field chasing him is deep and motivated. The Claret Jug remains very much up for grabs.
