McIlroy Makes the Cut, Spieth Misses — The Open Field Thins for Moving Day

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 18, 2026

The 154th Open Championship cut has been made. The field that began the week at Royal Birkdale has been significantly reduced, and the stories emerging from the cut line are a mix of resilience and disappointment.

Rory McIlroy survived. Jordan Spieth, the 2017 Birkdale champion, did not.

McIlroy: Alive but 9 Back

McIlroy's week began poorly enough to put his participation through the weekend in genuine doubt. He found himself seven shots off the pace after Round 1 and in danger of missing the cut at a course where he has historically been among the favorites.

A Round 2 of 67 — three under par — changed the picture. McIlroy clawed to -1 for the tournament, made the cut, and now enters Round 3 nine shots behind leader Lucas Herbert. He teed off early Saturday alongside Jacob Bridgeman.

"I need a special day," McIlroy acknowledged heading into Moving Day. Nine shots is precisely the kind of deficit that requires one. He is chasing his seventh major title — a win that would move him past the mark he currently shares with Nick Faldo as the European record. He won back-to-back Masters in 2025 and 2026; Birkdale would add a complexity to the major narrative unlike anything he has accomplished before.

The math is difficult but not impossible on a links course where scoring can move in large clusters. McIlroy has shown throughout his career that when the conditions align, he can produce rounds of exceptional quality. Saturday will require one of them.

Spieth's Painful Exit

The more heartbreaking cut-line story belongs to Jordan Spieth. The 2017 Open champion — whose final-round performance at this very course is remembered as one of the great closing rounds in major history — arrived at Royal Birkdale this week with reasonable hope of contending at the venue where he last won a major.

A quadruple bogey on the 17th hole in Round 2 ended that hope. Spieth missed the cut.

Other major champions and significant names also failed to make it to the weekend: Cameron Smith (LIV), Matt Fitzpatrick, Justin Rose, Wyndham Clark, 2023 Open champion Brian Harman, and Padraig Harrington all packed their bags before Round 3.

What's Next

Round 3 is being played Saturday at Royal Birkdale. The final round is Sunday, July 19. McIlroy faces a near-impossible points deficit but has not mathematically eliminated himself. The more likely scenario: the Claret Jug will be decided among Lucas Herbert, the -8 group (Suber, Young, Gerard), Sam Burns at -7, and Bryson DeChambeau at -5 — with Scheffler and Fleetwood also in realistic striking distance.
