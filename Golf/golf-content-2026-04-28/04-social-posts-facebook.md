### STORY 1: Korda LPGA Hall of Fame Countdown — Long-Form

**Window:** 8:00 AM ET

Nelly Korda is five points away from the LPGA Hall of Fame — and the golf world is starting to pay attention. After her wire-to-wire Chevron Championship victory on Sunday, the LPGA officially confirmed that Korda now has 22 of the 27 points required for Hall of Fame eligibility. The Chevron win counted for two points, as all major championships do under the LPGA's points system.

What does five points mean in practical terms? A major championship is worth two points. A regular LPGA win is worth one. To get from 22 to 27, Korda could reach the threshold by winning two more majors, or with a combination of major and regular wins in the months ahead. The remaining 2026 LPGA major schedule still features multiple opportunities — and given her current level of performance, none of them feel out of reach.

The historical context makes this moment even more remarkable. With three career majors now on her resume — the Chevron in 2021, the U.S. Women's Open in 2024, and the Chevron again in 2026 — Korda has joined Meg Mallon, Betsy King, and Patty Sheehan as the only American women since 1990 to win three or more major championships. She is 27 years old. The company she is keeping speaks for itself.

Her next start is the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba this Thursday, April 30. A win there would push her to 23 HOF points. Every victory matters now. The Hall of Fame countdown is officially, officially on.

### STORY 2: PGA Tour Schedule Under Fire — Long-Form

**Window:** 10:30 AM ET

The PGA Tour has spent years building its Signature Event structure to showcase the sport's biggest stars at premier venues for the biggest possible prizes. The concept is simple: elite field, major-like atmosphere, premium television. But something has gone wrong, and the events between the Masters and the PGA Championship are exposing it.

Three Signature Events are scheduled in a compressed 25-day window: the RBC Heritage at Hilton Head, the Cadillac Championship at Trump Doral, and the Truist Championship at Quail Hollow. Each carries a $20 million purse. Each is supposed to represent the best the Tour has to offer. And yet, across all three events, Scottie Scheffler — the World No. 1 and reigning FedExCup champion — and Rory McIlroy — the World No. 2 and 2026 Masters champion — will not play in the same field once.

McIlroy sat out the RBC Heritage after winning the Masters (reasonable — rest after a major is standard). He has now announced he will also skip the Cadillac Championship, making this his second consecutive $20 million event absence. Scheffler is playing Cadillac but has confirmed he will skip the Truist Championship.

Players face no fines for missing Signature Events. That policy, designed to prevent coerced participation, has instead created a loophole that allows the sport's most marketable stars to effectively cherry-pick their schedules. The critics are not wrong when they say $20 million events without the two best players in the world feel like a problem in search of a solution.

### STORY 3: Cadillac Championship Preview — Long-Form

**Window:** 1:00 PM ET

The Blue Monster at Trump National Doral is coming back to the PGA Tour for the first time since 2016, and it is arriving for a $20 million Signature Event. The Cadillac Championship tees off Thursday, April 30 and runs through Sunday, May 3 on one of golf's most storied layouts — a course that hosted the Tour's flagship WGC event for years before it was removed from the schedule.

The Blue Monster is a legitimate test. Water in play on virtually every hole, thick Florida rough, and greens that reward precision and patience. It is a course that tends to punish mistakes and reward players who can manage their game under pressure. In other words, it is exactly the kind of venue that suits Scottie Scheffler.

Scheffler enters the week as the clear favorite in a field that has been significantly weakened by opt-outs. Rory McIlroy, Ludvig Åberg, Xander Schauffele, and Matt Fitzpatrick — all potential challengers — have all chosen to take this week off ahead of the PGA Championship, which is on the horizon. Tommy Fleetwood, who has been in excellent form on the European Ryder Cup circuit, is among those competing.

For the players who did choose to show up, the Cadillac Championship represents a significant opportunity. With several marquee names absent, a path to a first Signature Event victory — and the FedExCup points, earnings, and career exemptions that come with it — is more accessible than it would normally be. The course demands respect regardless of who else is in the field, and the Blue Monster has a way of producing memorable theatre regardless of the circumstances.

### STORY 4: LPGA Riviera Maya Open — Long-Form

**Window:** 3:30 PM ET

Nelly Korda had barely finished accepting the Chevron Championship trophy when the next chapter of her 2026 season was already scheduled to begin. The LPGA Tour heads to Mayakoba in Playa del Carmen, Mexico this week for the Riviera Maya Open (April 30-May 3) — and Korda enters as the World No. 1, the reigning major champion, and one of the most in-form players in women's golf.

The question for everyone watching is simple: can she go back-to-back? Korda arrived at the Chevron carrying momentum from her Tournament of Champions victory earlier in the year and turned it into a dominant wire-to-wire performance at Memorial Park. That kind of sustained excellence heading into consecutive events is not common on any tour.

Mayakoba's El Camaleón golf course is one of the LPGA's most scenic venues — a jungle course carved through the Yucatan Peninsula with fresh and saltwater features throughout. It rewards players who can manage creative shot-making, keep the ball in play through tree-lined corridors, and execute on fast, undulating greens. It is a fundamentally different challenge from Memorial Park's open parkland.

Whether Korda can adapt quickly enough to add a third win of 2026 will tell us something important about where her game is right now. A win at Mayakoba would push her to 23 HOF points and signal that her spring form is not a one-week phenomenon.
