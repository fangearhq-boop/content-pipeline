Can Korda Go Back-to-Back? LPGA Heads to Mayakoba for Riviera Maya Open

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

April 28, 2026

The LPGA Tour barely paused to catch its breath after the Chevron Championship before moving on to its next destination: Mayakoba's El Camaleón golf course in Playa del Carmen, Mexico. The Riviera Maya Open runs Thursday April 30 through Sunday May 3, and it arrives with the sport's biggest star firmly in attendance. Nelly Korda, fresh off her wire-to-wire major championship victory and World No. 1 ranking, is heading to one of the tour's most beautiful venues looking to double down on her extraordinary spring.

Back-to-back wins are rare in golf at any level. The mental and physical demands of competing at the highest level in consecutive weeks, often in different time zones and on different course types, make sustained excellence a genuine challenge. Korda managed it effectively during her 2024 campaign — stringing together multiple victories in a dominant mid-season run — and the confidence that comes from that kind of success carries real competitive value.

The El Camaleón course provides a sharp contrast to Memorial Park's open parkland. Carved through the Yucatan Peninsula's jungle and coastline, El Camaleón features tree-lined corridors that demand accuracy off the tee, cenotes and water features that add visual and strategic complexity, and greens that reward players who can hold approaches on firm, fast surfaces. It is a course that has historically favoured players with complete games and precise ball striking — qualities Korda possesses in abundance, but which will need recalibration after a different style of test the previous week.

More Than Just a Win

For Korda, a victory at the Riviera Maya Open would carry significance beyond the trophy. Moving to 23 LPGA Hall of Fame points — which a regular tour win would provide — would leave just four points between her and one of the sport's great institutional honours. Every win now is part of a counting exercise, and that context does not diminish the competitive stakes so much as add another layer to them.

The field at Mayakoba will include the usual complement of LPGA top players who choose to make the trip to Mexico. The week-after-major dynamic typically means some players take rest, thinning the field slightly — but Korda's presence alone elevates any event she enters.

What's Next

The Riviera Maya Open concludes May 3, and the LPGA calendar continues with further events building toward the next major championship. Whether Korda uses this week as an active rest or pushes for a third victory of 2026 will be one of the season's most watched storylines. Her trajectory since the year began suggests she is not content to simply consolidate — she is pushing for something historic, and the HOF countdown is a reminder that it is closer than it has ever been.
