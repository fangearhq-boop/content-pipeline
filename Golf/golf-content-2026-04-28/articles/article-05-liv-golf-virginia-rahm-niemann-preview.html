LIV Golf Virginia Preview: Rahm's Hot Streak Meets Niemann's Home Turf

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

April 28, 2026

Jon Rahm has become the dominant personality of the 2026 LIV Golf season, and when the circuit arrives at Trump National Golf Club in Sterling, Virginia for its May 7-10 event, all eyes will be on whether the Spaniard can extend his form into another week of authoritative performance. Rahm's Mexico City victory — a six-shot margin achieved with a bogey-free final round — was his best LIV performance to date and announced his candidacy as the circuit's player of the year.

Defending individual champion Joaquin Niemann provides the week's most direct competitive counter-narrative. The Chilean, competing for Legion XIII alongside Rahm during parts of the season, won this event the previous year in commanding fashion and has been consistently near the top of the LIV standings in 2026. His familiarity with the Virginia course — a demanding layout that rewards strategic management and precise iron play — gives him an edge that raw statistical models may not fully capture.

Trump National in Sterling is a legitimate test of championship golf. The course features significant elevation changes, thoughtfully positioned hazards, and a final stretch that regularly produces dramatic outcomes. LIV's 54-hole, no-cut format means every shot counts from the opening tee shot of Thursday's round, and players who start slowly have less margin to recover than in traditional 72-hole events.

The Team Standings Race

The team competition adds a dimension to every LIV event that makes it distinct from anything else on the professional golf calendar. Ripper GC currently leads the 2026 team standings, sitting ahead of 4Aces GC in what has been a tightly contested season-long battle. Rahm's Legion XIII is firmly in contention as the season approaches its midpoint, and a strong Virginia result — particularly in the team format — could reshape the standings heading into summer.

For Crushers GC, the defending team champions from the previous Virginia event, the week carries additional motivation. Back-to-back team titles at the same event would be a meaningful achievement in a circuit that is constantly evolving and where defending any title requires sustained excellence across all four players.

What's Next

LIV Golf Virginia runs May 7-10 at Trump National in Sterling. Following this event, the LIV calendar continues building toward the season's final stages, where the team championship results will be definitively settled. For Rahm and Niemann, both players are positioned to make significant impacts — and their head-to-head competition across the Virginia weekend may be the individual storyline the event is remembered for.
