Cadillac Championship Preview: Doral's Blue Monster Returns, Scheffler Leads Depleted Field

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

April 28, 2026

For a decade, Trump National Doral's Blue Monster course was one of the most recognizable venues in professional golf — the former home of the WGC Cadillac Championship, a tournament that attracted the world's best players to south Florida for a decade-plus run. After the event left Doral in 2016, the course faded from Tour consciousness. This Thursday, it comes back. The 2026 Cadillac Championship begins April 30 at the Blue Monster, and it carries $20 million in prize money as a PGA Tour Signature Event.

The return of Doral is the week's most compelling storyline in its own right. The Blue Monster is a genuinely demanding test — water in play throughout, thick rough off the fairways, and greens that punish anything but precise approaches. Its par-72 layout rewards the kind of complete, controlled game that tends to sort elite-calibre players from those who are merely very good. The course has history, character, and an atmosphere that hard-surface, cookie-cutter modern venues cannot replicate.

The challenge this week is that the field will not quite match the venue's potential. Rory McIlroy — the Masters champion and World No. 2 — has confirmed he will sit out, his second consecutive Signature Event absence. Ludvig Åberg, Xander Schauffele, and Matt Fitzpatrick have all also chosen to take the week off ahead of the PGA Championship. That leaves a notable collection of top-tier names absent from what should be a marquee event.

Scheffler and the Opportunity

Scottie Scheffler, the World No. 1, is in the field. The defending FedExCup champion and one of the most consistent performers of the modern era, Scheffler enters as the clear favorite — bolstered by the absences around him. A Signature Event victory is significant not just for the $3.6 million first-place cheque but for the FedExCup points and the career milestone of claiming a premier event. Scheffler has already won this season and will be motivated to add another in front of what should be a knowledgeable south Florida gallery.

Tommy Fleetwood, who has maintained strong form throughout the European calendar, is among those competing and represents one of the most credible challengers. The Cadillac Championship has historically produced winners who seize the moment when the star-studded field isn't fully assembled — players who treat the reduced competition as an invitation rather than a consolation prize.

What's Next

The Cadillac Championship runs April 30 through May 3. Following the conclusion of play in Miami, the PGA Tour moves north to Charlotte for the Truist Championship at Quail Hollow (May 6-10) — where McIlroy will return to the field. Then the PGA Championship, one of the year's four majors, follows shortly after. This two-week Doral-Charlotte stretch is arguably the most important non-major block of the spring, and how it unfolds will shape the FedExCup standings heading into summer.
