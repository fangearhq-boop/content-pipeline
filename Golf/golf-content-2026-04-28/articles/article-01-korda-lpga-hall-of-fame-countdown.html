Korda Is Now 5 Points From LPGA Hall of Fame — And She's Just Getting Started

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

April 28, 2026

When Nelly Korda walked off the 18th green at Memorial Park on Sunday with the Chevron Championship trophy in hand, she did more than claim her third major title. She moved to 22 of the 27 points required for LPGA Hall of Fame induction — five points away from one of the most exclusive honours in women's sports. The LPGA confirmed the milestone officially following her five-shot victory.

The mathematics of the Hall of Fame points system are relatively straightforward. Each major championship victory is worth two points. Each standard LPGA Tour victory carries one point. For Korda to reach the 27-point threshold, she needs five more — achievable via two more majors, or through a combination of major wins and regular victories distributed over the remainder of her career. Given her trajectory in 2026, the conversation has shifted from "if" to "when."

The historical frame around this moment is extraordinary. Korda has become only the fourth American woman since 1990 to win three or more major championships. The others on that list — Meg Mallon, Betsy King, and Patty Sheehan — are Hall of Famers themselves, legends of the sport. To be named in the same conversation as them at 27 years old is a measure of just how rapidly Korda has ascended to the sport's upper tier.

A Season Already Written in History

Korda's 2026 campaign was defined by the Chevron Championship, but the year's context is equally remarkable. Her Tournament of Champions victory earlier in the season added another point to her HOF total. The Chevron win brought two more. That is three HOF points in a single calendar year — a pace that, if sustained, would see her reach the threshold in the not-too-distant future.

Her three major victories now span five years: the Chevron in 2021, the U.S. Women's Open in 2024, and the Chevron again in 2026. The arc shows a player who has not only reached the top of the sport but stayed there through multiple competitive eras, adapting her game and maintaining the mental composure that defines generational talents.

Reclaiming the World No. 1 ranking from Jeeno Thitikul after the Chevron victory was the icing on the week. The ranking reflects not just the Houston result but a broader campaign of consistent excellence. Korda is playing the best golf of her life, and she is 27 years old.

What's Next

Korda is scheduled to compete at the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba in Playa del Carmen, Mexico, beginning Thursday April 30. A win there would give her a third title of 2026 and move her to 23 Hall of Fame points — leaving just four more to reach the threshold. With multiple LPGA majors still on the calendar this year and Korda playing with the confidence of a champion at the top of her game, the Hall of Fame may be welcoming a new member sooner than anyone expected.
