LIV Golf's Smallest-Ever Masters Field — DeChambeau Leads 10-Man Contingent
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 1:00 PM ET When the 2026 Masters field was finalized, something stood out: only 10 LIV Golf players made the cut. That is the fewest since LIV was founded in 2022. In 2023 and 2024, LIV had managed to send 18 and 14 players to Augusta respectively. The 2026 number of 10 reflects the cumulative effect of tighter qualification criteria — Masters exemptions are heavily weighted toward recent major wins and world ranking points, and LIV players are increasingly boxed out by a points system that doesn't fully credit their tour results. Leading the LIV contingent is Bryson DeChambeau, who arrives in exceptional form. He won back-to-back LIV events — Singapore and South Africa — both in playoffs, and enters Augusta with legitimate major contender status. Jon Rahm, the 2023 Masters champion, is also in the field and tees off at 1:08 PM ET. The rest of the LIV roster is in mixed form at best. The shrinking presence is a data point that Augusta's traditional gatekeeping still matters — and that the pathway from LIV to a major start is getting narrower. --- ### Image Post Caption Window: 1:30 PM ET Just 10 LIV players in the 2026 Masters field. The fewest ever. DeChambeau leads them — and he's coming off back-to-back wins. Can LIV's best crack the Green Jacket?
