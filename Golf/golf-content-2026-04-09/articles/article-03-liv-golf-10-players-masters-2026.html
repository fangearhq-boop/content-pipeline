When the 2026 Masters Tournament field was finalized, one number stood out from the rest: 10. That is the number of LIV Golf players who qualified for this year's field at Augusta National — the smallest LIV contingent in the history of the alternative tour's relationship with the Masters.

In 2023, 18 LIV players competed at Augusta. In 2024, that number fell to 14. The 2025 figure held at 12. The 2026 total of 10 continues a steady downward trend that reflects both the structural realities of Masters qualification and the increasingly narrow pathway from LIV's points system to Augusta's invitation list.

Why LIV Players Are Struggling to Qualify

The Masters uses a combination of qualification criteria that favor recent major wins, Official World Golf Ranking standing, and performance in designated events. LIV Golf's points system does not feed directly into the OWGR at the same rate as PGA Tour or DP World Tour events, meaning LIV players accumulate world ranking points more slowly than their counterparts on the traditional tours.

The result is a compounding disadvantage: LIV players who are not recent major winners or former Augusta champions find it progressively harder to maintain the OWGR standing required to receive a Masters invitation based on ranking alone. In 2022 and 2023, the LIV contingent benefited from players who qualified before joining the alternative tour. By 2026, that pool has shrunk considerably.

DeChambeau Leads the LIV Group

The strongest case for any LIV player at the 2026 Masters belongs to Bryson DeChambeau. He arrives at Augusta having won back-to-back LIV Golf events — the Singapore Invitational and the South Africa Invitational, both in playoffs — making him the form player of the early LIV season. His Crushers GC team also claimed the team title in South Africa.

DeChambeau's game has always been calibrated for major championship conditions. He won the 2020 U.S. Open at Winged Foot with a power-and-precision approach that rewrote assumptions about how U.S. Open rough could be conquered. He won the 2024 U.S. Open at Pinehurst. Augusta's combination of length off the tee and premium on approach accuracy is, theoretically, a good fit for his skill set.

Jon Rahm is the other high-profile LIV name in the field. Rahm won the 2023 Masters before making his move to LIV Golf's Legion XI team. He tees off Thursday afternoon at 1:08 PM ET alongside Chris Gotterup and Ludvig Åberg. Rahm's major championship pedigree — two slams, multiple top-10s at Augusta — makes him a credible threat despite the inconsistency that has occasionally characterized his LIV campaign.

The Larger Picture

Ten LIV players at the Masters is not zero. But the trend line points in one direction. Unless LIV and the PGA Tour reach a formal integration agreement that adjusts world ranking point allocation, the number of LIV players earning Augusta invitations will continue to decline year by year.

For now, DeChambeau and Rahm remain the two most likely LIV players to contend for the 2026 Green Jacket. The other eight will compete knowing that simply being at Augusta — however they qualified — is itself an achievement in the current landscape of professional golf.
