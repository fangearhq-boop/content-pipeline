The 2026 Masters Tournament is underway. Rory McIlroy teed off at 10:31 AM ET on Thursday, April 9, beginning what he hopes will be a historic defense of his Green Jacket — and a run at something only one player has achieved in the modern era of professional golf.

McIlroy won the 2025 Masters in a playoff over Justin Rose, completing his career Grand Slam in the process. He became only the sixth player in history to win all four major championships, joining Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, and Tiger Woods. Now, returning to Augusta National as the defending champion, McIlroy is chasing a second consecutive title — a feat that only Tiger Woods has accomplished since 2002.

Woods won the Masters in 2001 and 2002, holding all four major trophies simultaneously in what was dubbed the "Tiger Slam." The back-to-back Masters stretch was its own category of dominance. In the 24 years since, no player has been able to replicate it. McIlroy, playing some of the most sustained elite golf of his career, enters Thursday with the credentials and the motivation to change that.

The Field Around Him

McIlroy played alongside Cameron Young and U.S. Amateur champion Mason Howell in his Round 1 group, teeing off in the morning wave. The early draw put him among the first of the day's marquee names to take the course.

World No. 1 Scottie Scheffler, the +500 Masters favorite, played in the afternoon. Scheffler teed off at 1:44 PM ET with Robert MacIntyre and Gary Woodland — a later wave that allowed him to watch the morning scoring before committing to his own round strategy. Scheffler has won the Masters in 2022 and 2024 and enters this week bidding for a third title.

Bryson DeChambeau, fresh off back-to-back LIV Golf victories in Singapore and South Africa, teed off at 10:07 AM — just ahead of McIlroy — making the morning wave the most star-studded stretch of the day's tee sheet. Jon Rahm, the 2023 Masters champion, played in the afternoon alongside Chris Gotterup and Ludvig Åberg.

A Tournament Built for This Moment

The 90th edition of the Masters carries a $21 million purse and marks the broadcast debut of Amazon Prime as a co-rights holder alongside CBS. The 91-player field includes 10 LIV Golf representatives — the fewest since LIV was founded — and a collection of contenders capable of winning on any given Sunday.

Augusta National has made one notable course adjustment heading into 2026: the 17th hole has been shortened by 12 yards, now playing to 450 yards. The change alters approach angles and club selection off the tee, and how players adapt to the new configuration will be one of the round-by-round storylines of the week.

McIlroy arrives with statistical firepower. He ranks No. 1 in Strokes Gained: Off-the-Tee at +0.799, meaning Augusta's premium on distance and accuracy off the tee plays to his greatest strength. His putting has been a concern heading in — No. 102 in putts per round, No. 136 in one-putt percentage — but Augusta's greens have tested the putting of nearly every player who has ever competed here. They will test McIlroy's again.

What they will also test is his ability to handle the weight of history. Back-to-back. Tiger's mark. Twenty-four years of players who tried and fell short. The 2026 Masters is the story of whether Rory McIlroy joins that exclusive company or becomes the next name on the list of those who attempted it.

Round 1 has begun. Everything else is commentary.
