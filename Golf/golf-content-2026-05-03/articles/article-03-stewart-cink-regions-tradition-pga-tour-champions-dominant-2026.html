Stewart Cink Is Running Away With the PGA Tour Champions Season — Regions Tradition Title Extends His Dominance

By Marcus Cole | May 3, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Stewart Cink arrived at Greystone Golf and Country Club in Birmingham, Alabama with three wins already in his pocket. He leaves with four. The Regions Tradition, one of the five major championships on the PGA Tour Champions, is his — and with it comes the kind of season narrative that demands attention even from fans who don't closely follow the senior circuit.

Cink led by three shots entering Sunday's final round and converted. At 52, in a season that began with a win in Hawaii and hasn't slowed down since, he is producing a run of golf that places him in select historical company on the PGA Tour Champions.

The Shape of a Dominant Season

The Mitsubishi Electric Championship in January. The Hoag Classic in March. The Senior PGA Championship — a major — in April. Now the Regions Tradition. Four wins, two of which are major championships on the senior circuit, before the calendar reaches mid-May.

Cink has been on the PGA Tour Champions since turning 50 in 2023. He won twice in his debut season but was far from the dominant force he has become in 2026. Whatever adjustment he made to his preparation or mindset entering this year, the results have been categorical. He is not close to the best player on the senior tour this season — he is the best player by a considerable margin.

Greystone and the Regions Tradition

The Regions Tradition at Greystone Golf and Country Club has been one of the most consistent fixtures on the Champions calendar for more than three decades. Its status as one of five senior majors gives it a prestige that elevates every champion's name on the trophy.

Cink's performance this week at Greystone was consistent with what he has shown all season: front-running golf built on course management and ball control rather than spectacular shotmaking. He led entering the final round, protected the lead, and closed it out. That pattern — take the lead early, defend it late — is the hallmark of a golfer who knows exactly how to win.

The Legacy of 2009

Most golf fans know Cink primarily for his 2009 Open Championship at Turnberry, where he defeated 59-year-old Tom Watson in a playoff for the Claret Jug. That victory was one of the most emotionally complex in major championship history — the young fan base rooting for Watson's improbable win cast Cink as the obstacle rather than the protagonist. He handled it with grace then, and he has continued to compete and develop his game in the decade-plus since.

The 2026 PGA Tour Champions season represents the second major act of a career that deserves reassessment. He was never just a one-major player grinding out his years. He was a serious and sustained competitor who has now found the senior circuit as a platform for genuine excellence.

What's Next

With the Regions Tradition complete, the PGA Tour Champions will continue through the spring and summer with Cink positioned as the clear season-long points leader. Three more senior majors remain on the calendar — the Senior Players Championship, the US Senior Open, and The Tradition — giving Cink ample opportunity to extend what is already one of the most impressive single seasons in recent Champions Tour history. At this pace, a season-long points title and Player of the Year honors are both realistic outcomes, regardless of what the remainder of the field can produce.
