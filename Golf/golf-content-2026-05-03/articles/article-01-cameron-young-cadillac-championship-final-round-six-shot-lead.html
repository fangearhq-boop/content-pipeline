Cameron Young Carries Six-Shot Lead Into Cadillac Championship Final Round at Doral

By Ryan Calloway | May 3, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

When the Cadillac Championship's final round tee times were shifted forward by several hours on Saturday evening — the PGA Tour moving fast to beat incoming rain at Trump National Doral — it was already clear that Sunday morning would belong to Cameron Young. The only question left was whether anyone could close six shots in a compressed window against one of the hottest players in the field.

Young sits at 15-under par entering the fourth and final round, his six-shot lead over Scottie Scheffler and Si Woo Kim representing the most commanding position any player has held at a Signature Event entering a final round all season. The Blue Monster, restored and re-tooled for the Tour's return after a decade away, has so far done nothing to slow Young down.

How Young Built the Lead

The foundation was laid in Round 1, when Young posted a bogey-free 64 that instantly established him as the player to beat. It was the kind of opening round that sends a message — not a grinding 67 with scrambling and saves, but a clean, controlled demolition of a course that was supposed to present the field with teeth.

He followed that with a 67 in Round 2 to reach 13-under, then extended to 15-under through 54 holes. That's 14 birdies over the first two rounds alone, with his lone bogey across the stretch coming on the 14th hole in Round 2 — immediately answered with a birdie on the 16th. Young has played the Blue Monster at Doral as if it suits every part of his game, and after three rounds, that assessment is difficult to dispute.

Scheffler's Position and the Subplot

Scottie Scheffler is paired with Young for the final round, which on any other week would generate enormous tension. The world No. 1, who opened with a 71 and found himself seven shots back after Round 1, clawed his way to 9-under through 54 holes — an impressive recovery that still leaves him with a near-impossible mathematical task against Young's lead.

The presence of Adam Scott adds its own narrative layer. Scott shot 66 in Round 3 despite incurring a wrong ball penalty during the round, somehow managing to recover and still post a number that made him one of the day's best performers. At 45, Scott's ability to produce that kind of round under the psychological pressure of a scoring penalty is the quiet story of the week at Doral.

What a Win Would Mean

The Cadillac Championship is a $20 million Signature Event — the highest tier of regular-season PGA Tour competition outside the majors. The field of 72 players, which included most of the Tour's elite despite notable absences like Rory McIlroy, carries a prestige that reflects the event's elevated status.

For Young, a victory here would be his first Signature Event title and would represent the biggest win of his career. He has won on the PGA Tour before, but claiming a $20 million event at one of golf's iconic venues — the Blue Monster at Doral, back on Tour for the first time since Dustin Johnson won the 2016 WGC-Cadillac Championship — carries a different weight entirely.

The course, renovated by Gil Hanse and Jim Wagner, reopened with redesigned lakes on the 15th and 16th holes and has played as a stern but fair examination of ball-striking. Young has answered every question it has posed.

What's Next

With the final round moved up to morning tee times to beat Sunday's heavy rainfall forecast, the Cadillac Championship will reach its conclusion before midday ET. For the PGA Tour, a Cameron Young champion at the Blue Monster would provide a compelling new chapter for a venue that has produced some of the sport's most memorable weeks. For Young, it would confirm what three rounds of the 2026 Cadillac Championship have strongly suggested: that this is his moment, and this is his course.
