LIV Golf Virginia Preview: Rahm Arrives Hot, DeChambeau Returns, Niemann Defends as Circuit Heads to Washington

By Ryan Calloway | May 3, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Jon Rahm shot 21-under 263 in Mexico City. He won by six shots over David Puig. His team, Legion XIII, claimed the team title for the first time in 2026. And then he packed his bags, got on a plane, and began preparing for Washington.

The Maaden LIV Golf Virginia — scheduled for May 7-10 at Trump National Golf Club Washington DC in Sterling, Virginia — arrives at an interesting moment for the circuit. The individual competition gives Rahm a chance to build on his Mexico City momentum. The backdrop is a league navigating genuine uncertainty about its financial future. Both things can be true at the same time, and they will be, somewhere in Northern Virginia next week.

Rahm in Form

The Mexico City result was Rahm's fourth individual win since joining LIV Golf prior to the 2024 season. He shot a bogey-free 7-under 64 in the final round to stretch what was already a comfortable lead to six shots at the finish. It was the kind of dominant, front-to-back performance that reinforces why he remains one of the most complete players in the world regardless of which tour's banner he competes under.

Rahm's Legion XIII team also won the team title in Mexico City — their first of 2026 — which gives the Spanish contingent momentum on both fronts heading into Virginia. In the individual standings, Rahm is positioned as the circuit's points leader and arrives at Trump National as the player everyone else needs to beat.

DeChambeau Returns from Injury

Bryson DeChambeau did not play in Mexico City, withdrawing with a wrist injury before the event began. His absence was felt — Crushers GC, one of the circuit's most competitive teams, did not have its most important player for an entire event.

DeChambeau is expected to return for Virginia, which would restore the most explosive driver in professional golf to an event he is well-suited to attack. Trump National Golf Club Washington DC plays long and rewards the kind of distance advantage that DeChambeau carries as a standard feature of his game. His return adds an unpredictable element to a field that was already unsettled by Rahm's form in Mexico.

Niemann and the Team Standings

Joaquin Niemann won the 2025 individual title at LIV Golf Virginia and returns to defend it. Niemann is one of the more consistent individual performers on the circuit, and he knows this course and format well enough to be a legitimate threat even with Rahm arriving at the top of his game.

In the team competition, Ripper GC leads the 2026 standings with 89.75 points, a narrow edge over 4Aces at 88 and Legion XIII in third. Virginia represents one of the remaining opportunities to shift the team standings meaningfully, and the teams chasing Ripper GC will be looking to close the gap before the season enters its back half.

The Financial Backdrop

LIV Golf's existential questions will not be resolved next week at Sterling, but they color the event's context. The Public Investment Fund of Saudi Arabia has announced it will end its direct funding of LIV after the 2026 season. A new independent board is in place. The circuit's leadership has stated it is funded through the current season while seeking new investors and commercial partnerships.

For the players competing at Virginia, the immediate focus is the golf and the $30 million on offer. But the longer-term questions about the circuit's future — and what it means for each player's career choices — are never far from the surface.

What's Next

LIV Golf Virginia runs May 7-10 at Trump National Golf Club Washington DC. Full Golf Fanrecap coverage will begin Thursday morning with the opening round. Rahm, DeChambeau, Niemann, and the rest of the circuit will be in the field, with $30 million in total prize money up for grabs across the individual and team competitions. Whatever questions surround LIV Golf's long-term future, the golf itself next week will be worth watching.
