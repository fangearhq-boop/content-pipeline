Nelly Korda Eyes Back-to-Back Riviera Maya Titles, Leads by Three Entering Final Round

By Jake Torres | May 3, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The 18th hole at El Camaleon is a long, risk-reward par-five that rewards aggression. On Saturday afternoon, Nelly Korda drove it into position, hit an 8-iron to 18 inches, and tapped in for eagle. The crowd reacted. The leaderboard shifted. Korda signed for a 5-under 67 and walked off the course with a three-shot lead over the field.

That was the moment that effectively decided the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba. Sunday's final round is still to be played, but Korda — the world No. 1, the holder of 22 Hall of Fame points, the winner of the Chevron Championship just two weeks ago — has done this before, in this tournament, at this course.

Three Rounds of Control

Korda reached 14-under par through 54 holes at El Camaleon in Playa del Carmen, Mexico. Arpichaya Yubol of Thailand sits second at 11-under, three shots back and in position to mount a final-round challenge. Beyond Yubol, the gap to the rest of the field is substantial.

The trajectory of Korda's week has been consistent throughout: controlled ball-striking, aggressive par-five play, and the kind of composed putting that comes with experience at the top of a leaderboard. Her Round 2 was bogey-free. Her Round 3 eagle on 18 was the exclamation point on a performance that has been quietly dominant from the opening holes of the tournament.

The Hall of Fame Clock

Every time Korda wins in 2026, a number follows her name: 22 of 27. That's where she stands on the LPGA Hall of Fame points system — five points from one of women's golf's most exclusive memberships. The path from here is straightforward by the standards of an already extraordinary career: major championships add two points, regular LPGA wins add one.

She won the Chevron Championship earlier this month — her third career major, moving her to 22 points. A Riviera Maya title would add one more, leaving her four away. There is a growing sense within the LPGA world that Korda's induction is a matter of when, not whether, and that the 2026 season may be the one where it happens.

Defending at Mayakoba

Korda won the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba in 2025, making this week a title defense on a course she clearly plays well. El Camaleon's medium-speed greens and par-five setup favor her length and precision approach play. The conditions that produced her third-round 67 — including the eagle finish — suggest the course is playing receptively enough for a low final round from the leader.

The field's inability to mount a sustained challenge through three rounds reflects the margin between Korda and everyone else. Yubol's 11-under total represents excellent golf. Against a healthy and focused Korda carrying a three-shot lead at a course she has mastered, it may not be enough.

What's Next

Sunday's final round at El Camaleon will conclude the Riviera Maya Open and, barring a dramatic collapse, confirm Korda as the champion for the second consecutive year. The LPGA Tour then turns toward the Mizuho Americas Open on May 7-10, with Korda's point total and Hall of Fame timeline remaining the dominant storyline of the women's game through the balance of the spring. After Saturday's eagle and three-round dominance, the story at Mayakoba looks like it ends the way most of Korda's weeks do: with a trophy.
