### STORY 5: Scheffler at Doral

#### Long-Form Post — 3:30 PM ET
Scottie Scheffler opened his Cadillac Championship week with a 71. At the Blue Monster. Against a field that included Cameron Young shooting a bogey-free 64.

Seven shots back after Round 1. The narrative wrote itself: Scheffler stumbles, Young runs away with it.

Then Scheffler played three more rounds of golf.

Through 54 holes at Doral, the world No. 1 climbed to 9-under par — tied second alongside Si Woo Kim, just 6 behind Young. He didn't win the Cadillac Championship. Cameron Young was simply too good this week. But the fact that Scheffler clawed his way back from a 7-shot deficit to inside contention tells you everything you need to know about the kind of competitor he is.

Wins define reputations. But the way Scheffler responded to an opening stumble at a $20M event — not quitting, not mailing it in, grinding back toward the lead — is the reason he's been the best player in the world for two-plus years now.
