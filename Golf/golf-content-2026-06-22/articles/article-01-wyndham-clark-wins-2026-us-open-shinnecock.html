Wyndham Clark Wins 2026 US Open — Wire-to-Wire at Shinnecock Hills

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 22, 2026

Wyndham Clark has done it again.

The 2023 US Open champion claimed his second US Open title on Sunday at Shinnecock Hills in Southampton, New York, finishing at 4-under-par and holding off Sam Burns by one stroke in one of the most compelling major championship finishes of the decade.

Clark shot a final-round 73, three over par — a significant step back from the form that had him six strokes clear of the field entering Sunday. But Shinnecock Hills does that to players, and the hostile crowd did the rest. Through the back nine, the gallery mocked his errant tee shots, cheered his wayward approaches, and celebrated every missed putt. It wasn't enough. Clark found a way.

A Place in History

Clark became only the ninth wire-to-wire champion in US Open history — leading the tournament after every single round from Thursday through Sunday. The last player to accomplish this was Martin Kaymer, who dominated the field at Pinehurst No. 2 in 2014.

Wire-to-wire victories in the US Open are exceptionally rare because the USGA is specifically designed to set up the course to punish the leader — narrowing fairways as the week progresses, raising rough to penalize missed shots. To lead from the first round through the fourth, a player must not only play exceptional golf but maintain composure while every other competitor is trying to cut them down.

Clark did exactly that, even if Sunday made him earn every stroke of it.

The Final-Round Battle

Burns, who had been quietly solid all week, made his move on Sunday. At 3-under by the time the leaders were finishing, Burns put pressure on Clark with every hole. The one-stroke margin that separated them at the end was a very different picture from the comfortable six that had defined Clark's position entering the day.

Clark's winning putt on the 72nd hole settled the matter. Per reports, the ball came to rest just nine inches from the hole — close enough to concede in a match play format, but in the US Open, nothing is given. Clark tapped in. The trophy was his.

Two Opens, Two Different Days

Clark's first US Open win in 2023 at Los Angeles Country Club was a different kind of triumph — a clean, controlled performance where he pulled away from the field in the final round. This one was the opposite: a survival test, a test of character, a test of whether six shots of cushion can survive Shinnecock's fangs and a hostile gallery.

Both victories are now on his record. Both required a fundamentally different kind of golf.

What's Next

Clark now owns two major championship titles and carries serious FedExCup momentum into the second half of the PGA Tour season. The next major — The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale — is several weeks away. For now, Wyndham Clark is the US Open champion, and Shinnecock Hills is where he proved it twice over.
