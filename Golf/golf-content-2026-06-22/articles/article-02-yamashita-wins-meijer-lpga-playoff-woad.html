Yamashita Wins Meijer LPGA Classic in Playoff — Woad's Three-Putt Heartbreak

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 22, 2026

Golf delivered one of its most dramatic Sunday finishes of the year at Blythefield Country Club in Belmont, Michigan — and the Meijer LPGA Classic for Simply Give ended in the most painful way imaginable for one player, and the most joyful for another.

Japan's Miyu Yamashita fired a final-round 64 — the low round of the tournament — and set the clubhouse target at 17-under-par. England's Lottie Woad, in the final group, came to the 18th green needing to hole a 50-foot birdie putt to win outright, or make par to win. She did neither. Woad three-putted from distance for a closing bogey, falling into a tie with Yamashita and forcing a sudden-death playoff.

On the first playoff hole, Yamashita won.

Yamashita's Career Moment

For Miyu Yamashita, Sunday at Blythefield was a breakthrough in every sense. The 24-year-old Japanese player became the first Japanese winner in Meijer LPGA Classic history, and claimed her first LPGA Tour victory in the United States.

Her path to the title was unconventional — she didn't lead entering the final round. She let the round speak for itself, going out and posting 64, one of the best rounds of the tournament, and then watching to see if the field could match her. When Woad couldn't hold par on 18, Yamashita had her chance.

In the playoff, she made it count.

Woad's Heartbreak

Lottie Woad's final-round collapse at 18 is the kind of moment that will stay with her for years — and likely define her early career narrative in ways both painful and instructive.

The English rookie had positioned herself beautifully throughout the week, playing consistent golf under difficult conditions and entering the final holes with a genuine chance to win. Standing on the 18th green with the tournament seemingly in her hands, a three-putt from distance was the one outcome she could least afford.

But Woad is young. At 22, she has the games and the composure to bounce back from this. Players who lose tournaments in gut-wrenching fashion at an early age — and go on to learn from it — often become the most resilient champions later. Woad's moment on 18 at Blythefield will fuel her next breakthrough.

The LPGA Moves On to Hazeltine

The conclusion of the Meijer LPGA Classic transitions the tour directly into major championship week. The KPMG Women's PGA Championship begins Thursday, June 25, at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota — one of the most demanding courses on the women's golf calendar.

World No. 1 Nelly Korda arrives at Hazeltine having won the first two major championships of 2026, and will be chasing the rarest achievement in women's golf: the calendar Grand Slam.

What's Next

Yamashita will carry the confidence of this victory into the KPMG field. Woad, having shown her quality throughout the Meijer week, will do the same. For both players, the story is still being written — Sunday at Blythefield was simply one dramatic chapter in careers that are just beginning.
