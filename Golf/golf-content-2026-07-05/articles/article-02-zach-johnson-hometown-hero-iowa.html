Zach Johnson's Iowa Stand: At 50, He Skipped Three Majors to Compete at Home

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

In a sport where every week's schedule is a strategic decision about points, rankings, and money, Zach Johnson made a different kind of decision. At 50 years old, with a career that includes a Masters title, an Open Championship, and enough experience to know exactly what is at stake in every event he enters, Johnson chose to skip three major championships this year. He chose, instead, to play in Iowa.

The John Deere Classic at TPC Deere Run in Silvis is not just another stop on the PGA Tour schedule for Zach Johnson. It is his tournament, in his state, in front of his people. And on Sunday, entering the final round at 13-under par — three shots off the lead — he is three holes from making this homecoming complete.

What Iowa Means to Johnson

Johnson was born and raised in Cedar Rapids, Iowa. The Cedar Rapids kid who became a two-major winner and Ryder Cup stalwart never stopped being an Iowa kid at heart. The John Deere Classic is the one event on the schedule where Johnson does not need to be talked into showing up. He would be there regardless of ranking implications, regardless of what else is on the calendar.

That commitment is not hypothetical. This year, he made it concrete. Three major championships sat on the schedule near the John Deere Classic — events that every professional golfer ranks above a regular Tour stop by virtually every objective measure. Johnson looked at his schedule and made a choice. He chose TPC Deere Run.

The Results Justify the Decision

What makes the story even better is that Johnson's performance backs up his conviction. He opened the tournament with a 64 in the first round — an immediate declaration of competitive intent. His third-round 66 on Saturday moved him to 13-under, three shots off the co-lead held by Glover and Hodges. At 50, Johnson is contending at a PGA Tour event in the state where he grew up, which tells you everything about his preparation, his fitness, and his competitive fire.

Johnson has won the John Deere Classic before. He knows how to win here, knows how the crowd responds when an Iowa kid performs on this stage, knows what it means to the fans who make the trip out to TPC Deere Run every July.

The Sunday Equation

Three shots is a manageable deficit in a final round on a course that rewards aggressive play. Glover and Hodges are formidable co-leaders, but both of them know that the gallery on Sunday afternoon will be rooting loudly for the local hero three shots back. A Johnson birdie run — the kind he has produced at this venue before — would change the entire energy of the event.

What's Next

Sunday's final round at TPC Deere Run is underway, with Johnson three back entering play. Whether or not he produces the winning result, the story of a 50-year-old champion choosing his home state over major championships has already resonated with golf fans everywhere. Johnson embodies what the John Deere Classic means to the people of Iowa — and what Iowa means to Zach Johnson. Some tournaments are just about the game. This one is about much more than that.
