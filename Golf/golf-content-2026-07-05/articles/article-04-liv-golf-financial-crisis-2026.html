LIV Golf's Financial Crisis: Saudi Funding Shortfall Puts the 2026 Season at Risk

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

When LIV Golf launched in 2022 with the backing of Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund, the message was one of unlimited ambition. The tour would spend whatever it took to sign the best players, build the best events, and establish itself as a credible alternative to the PGA Tour. Four years later, the financial reality looks very different — and the 2026 season may not make it to the finish line.

LIV Golf CEO Scott O'Neil confirmed in a June interview that he cannot guarantee the remaining four events of the 2026 season will be played. The reason is a significant funding shortfall from PIF — the same sovereign wealth fund that initiated and financed the entire enterprise.

The Numbers

PIF committed approximately $600 million to fund the 2026 LIV season. As of early July, the fund has provided roughly $200 million — approximately $66 million in May and $130 million in June. The remaining $400 million is owed but has not arrived.

That gap of $400 million covers everything a global professional golf tour needs to operate: player salaries and guaranteed contracts (many of which run into the tens of millions of dollars per player annually), event operations at premium golf venues, transportation for teams and equipment across multiple continents, prize money for each tournament, and the staffing required to run a competitive professional tour.

PIF's Shifting Strategy

In April, PIF issued a statement that reframed its relationship with LIV Golf in terms that alarmed observers inside and outside the sport. The fund said that substantial long-term investment in LIV "is no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy." That language — deliberate and careful — suggested a significant change in the fund's priorities, and the funding gap that followed appears to reflect it.

What changed is not entirely clear. PIF manages trillions of dollars in assets and operates across dozens of industries. LIV Golf was one portfolio item among many. The decision to slow or redirect funding may reflect a broader reassessment of where sports investments fit within PIF's current portfolio strategy.

Which Events Are at Risk

O'Neil indicated that the New York event, scheduled for early August, is the most likely of the remaining events to survive. The UK event at JCB Golf and Country Club (July 23-26), the Indianapolis event, and the Michigan event face more uncertain futures. Without the funding necessary to pay guarantees and operational costs, event organizers cannot proceed.

The most recent LIV events completed include Tyrrell Hatton's individual win at LIV Golf Andalucia at Valderrama and Lucas Herbert's wire-to-wire victory at LIV Golf Virginia. The tour has produced competitive golf this season — the uncertainty is entirely financial, not athletic.

What's Next

The next scheduled LIV event is the UK event at JCB Golf and Country Club, set for July 23-26. Whether that event proceeds as planned depends on funding decisions that are not yet confirmed. Players on LIV rosters hold guaranteed contracts that continue to accrue regardless of whether individual events run — meaning the financial implications extend beyond tournament prize money to the tour's full payroll obligations. The golf world is watching closely. If LIV cannot finish its 2026 season, the question of what happens to its players, its venues, and its place in the sport's landscape becomes urgent and complicated in ways that will shape professional golf for years.
