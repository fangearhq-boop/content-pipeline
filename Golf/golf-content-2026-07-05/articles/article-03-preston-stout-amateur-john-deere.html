Preston Stout Is in Contention at the John Deere Classic. The World Is Paying Attention.

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

Most golfers who play on a PGA Tour sponsor exemption are simply grateful for the opportunity. They compete, they learn, they gain experience against professionals they may eventually join in the field regularly. Preston Stout is doing something different. The world's No. 1 ranked amateur golfer is contending at the 2026 John Deere Classic.

Entering Sunday's final round at 12-under par — four shots off the lead held by co-leaders Lucas Glover and Lee Hodges — Stout has done more than hold his own at TPC Deere Run. He has put himself in a position where a final-round charge could produce a result that would be remembered as one of the signature moments in recent amateur golf history.

Who Is Preston Stout?

Stout is the reigning NCAA champion from Oklahoma State — one of the most storied programs in college golf. He also holds the No. 1 ranking in world amateur golf, a position earned through consistent excellence over the collegiate season and amateur competitions. When he received the sponsor exemption to play at the John Deere Classic, he arrived with credentials that left little doubt about his talent. What he has delivered in four days at TPC Deere Run is validation.

At 12-under, Stout sits alongside Max Homa and others in a competitive grouping just outside the leaders. The gap to first place is four shots — achievable in a final round on a course where scoring is plentiful.

What Contention Means for His Future

For amateur golfers, performance at Tour-level events on sponsor exemptions serves as one of the most credible proof-of-concept performances available. The John Deere Classic field includes PGA Tour veterans competing for $1.584 million. Stout has been in the mix with them for 54 holes.

The PGA Tour is watching. Equipment sponsors are watching. Agents are watching. A strong Sunday showing would make the case for Stout's professional career in a way that college wins, however impressive, simply cannot. He is demonstrating that the game translates — that the skills that made him the world's best amateur hold up against Tour-level competition and Tour-level pressure.

Amateur Status and Open Qualification

Stout is also theoretically in the running for Open Championship qualification as the leading amateur in applicable events — a distinction that carries its own prestige. The path for elite amateur players to the major championships runs through exactly this kind of performance at Tour-level events.

What's Next

Sunday's final round gives Stout one more opportunity to make a statement at TPC Deere Run. Whether he wins, finishes in the top five, or simply plays another competitive round at PGA Tour level, the performance this week has established him as a name to know as professional golf enters its next generation. The John Deere Classic has provided the stage. Preston Stout is using it well.
