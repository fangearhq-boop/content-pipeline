The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale Is 11 Days Away — Here's Everything You Need to Know

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

The Claret Jug is coming back to Lancashire. In 11 days — July 16 to 19, 2026 — the 154th Open Championship begins at Royal Birkdale in Southport, England. For the 11th time in its history, this storied links course along the Lancashire coast will host golf's oldest major. The R&A is projecting record attendance of more than 300,000 spectators across the week.

The field is loaded, the course is immaculate, and Scottie Scheffler — the world's No. 1 player and reigning Open champion — arrives with the Claret Jug in his possession and a game in peak form.

Scheffler Returns the Jug

Scheffler won the 2025 Open Championship at Royal Portrush, adding the Claret Jug to a resume that already included multiple major titles. Before the 2026 tournament begins, Scheffler will participate in the Heroes Classic on Tuesday, July 14 — a pre-championship charity event at Birkdale — and officially return the Claret Jug in the ceremony that marks the beginning of Open Championship week. He then enters the tournament as the player every other competitor needs to beat.

Scheffler leads both the world rankings and the FedExCup standings in 2026. His form is as consistent as any player on Tour. Royal Birkdale, with its demanding rough and coastal wind exposure, will test everyone — but Scheffler's ball-striking and course management give him an advantage at any venue he enters.

The Field

The 132-player field features virtually every major name in men's professional golf. Rory McIlroy, Xander Schauffele, Viktor Hovland, Brian Harman, Russell Henley, Bryson DeChambeau, Wyndham Clark, Matt Fitzpatrick, Robert MacIntyre, Corey Conners, and Chris Gotterup are among those confirmed.

Several LIV Golf players earned spots through Final Qualifying held on June 30: Josele Ballester, Caleb Surratt, and Peter Uihlein all qualified for Birkdale. Additional spots remain available through the Genesis Scottish Open (July 9-12) for the top non-exempt finisher, and through the top five in the Race to Dubai after the BMW International Open.

New for 2026: The Last Chance Qualifier

The R&A introduced a new format for 2026 that adds a final layer of drama to the qualification process. On the Monday before the tournament begins — July 14, the same day as the Heroes Classic — 12 golfers will compete at Royal Birkdale itself in a Last Chance Qualifier. One spot in the Open Championship field goes to the winner of that qualifier. It is a high-stakes, single-shot-at-glory format that gives one player a chance to earn their place in the most storied major in golf on the very course where it will be played.

What's Next

The Genesis Scottish Open at The Renaissance Club in North Berwick runs July 9 to 12 — one week before Birkdale — and serves as both the finest warm-up event and a qualifying pathway for non-exempt players. Scheffler, McIlroy, and Hovland are among the headliners in that field. After Scottish, the golf world moves directly to Birkdale. Eleven days of anticipation separate us from the moment Scheffler steps to the first tee as defending champion and the 154th Open Championship begins in earnest. The Claret Jug will find a new home in July — the question is who will be holding it come Sunday evening.
