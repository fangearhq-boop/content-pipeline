John Deere Classic Final Round: Glover and Hodges Share the Lead at 16-Under in a Sunday Shootout

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 5, 2026

The 2026 John Deere Classic reaches its conclusion Sunday at TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois, with two players at the top — and neither one willing to give the other an inch. Lucas Glover and Lee Hodges enter the final round tied at 16-under par, the result of 54 holes that tested both veterans in different ways and produced one of the most compelling final-round setups of the PGA Tour season.

At stake: $1.584 million for the winner, 500 FedExCup points, and the 2026 John Deere Classic title. Three players lurk one shot back at 15-under, ready to make their own statements on a Sunday that figures to produce plenty of drama.

Glover: The Veteran Setting the Pace

Lucas Glover is no stranger to holding a leaderboard position when it matters. The experienced PGA Tour campaigner opened the tournament with a remarkable 63 in the first round, followed by a 65 on Friday — two rounds that featured no bogeys across 36 holes. That kind of consistency is the hallmark of a player who knows how to manage a course and manage himself.

Saturday's third round produced a 69 — not the firework display of his first two rounds, but enough to stay in position. Glover offset any early stumbles with an eagle chip-in and late birdies, maintaining his 16-under total to remain at the top of the leaderboard.

Hodges: Momentum From a Saturday Surge

Lee Hodges took a different path to the co-lead. While Glover owned the top of the leaderboard entering the third round, Hodges made his move on Saturday with four birdies in a six-hole stretch on the back nine, firing a 67 that erased a two-shot deficit and drew him level. That surge — born of back-nine momentum in the third round — gives Hodges a psychological edge heading into Sunday. He is playing with energy and confidence.

The Field One Back

Zac Blair, Ben Kohles, and Jackson Suber all sit at 15-under — one shot behind the leaders entering Sunday. The proximity of three players at 15-under means this is far from a two-man race. Any of them can produce a final-round charge and win, particularly on a course like TPC Deere Run where low scoring is very much possible in good conditions.

Zach Johnson, the 50-year-old Iowa native who skipped three majors to compete at his home tournament, sits at 13-under — three back. Jordan Spieth is also in the field for Sunday. The lineup for the final round's key groups reads like a who's-who of PGA Tour talent.

What's Next

Final round action at TPC Deere Run wraps up Sunday afternoon, with the tournament concluding as one of the last events before the major season resumes with The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale on July 16. The John Deere Classic winner receives $1.584 million and 500 FedExCup points in a season where every point on the FedExCup race matters as the Playoffs approach. The co-lead setup — Glover's consistency against Hodges' momentum — promises a Sunday finish that golf fans will not want to miss.
