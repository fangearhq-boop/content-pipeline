### Long-Form Post ⚠️ LIV GOLF'S FINANCIAL CRISIS: THE TOUR THAT MAY NOT FINISH ITS SEASON The story behind the story in professional golf right now has nothing to do with leaderboards or scoring averages. It is about money — specifically, the $400 million that LIV Golf says it is owed by its primary financial backer, Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund. LIV Golf CEO Scott O'Neil sat down for an interview in June and, in a moment of remarkable candor, acknowledged that he could not guarantee the remaining four events of the 2026 season would be played. The math is stark: PIF committed approximately $600 million for the 2026 LIV season. As of early July, only approximately $200 million has been provided — roughly $66 million in May and $130 million in June. That leaves a gap of roughly $400 million, money needed to pay player salaries and appearance fees, tournament operations, prize purses, travel, and the rest of what it costs to run a global golf tour. In April, PIF stated publicly that substantial long-term investment in LIV "is no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy." That statement, quiet and bureaucratic in tone, carried enormous implications. The events now in question include the UK event scheduled for July 23-26 at JCB Golf & Country Club, plus events in Indianapolis and Michigan. The New York event in early August is considered the most likely to survive. For LIV players — many of whom signed multi-year guaranteed contracts worth tens of millions of dollars — the situation is deeply uncertain. For the sport of golf, LIV's potential implosion would represent a seismic shift in the professional landscape that began in 2022. ### Long-Form Post 🏆 THE OPEN IS 11 DAYS AWAY — ROYAL BIRKDALE IS READY July 16 is circled. The 154th Open Championship tees off at Royal Birkdale in Southport, England — and the golf world is counting down. This is the 11th time Royal Birkdale has hosted The Open, and the R&A is projecting record attendance of more than 300,000 spectators over the week. Birkdale, with its characteristic willow scrub-lined fairways and demanding coastal conditions, is one of the most beloved venues in links golf. Scottie Scheffler arrives as defending champion, having won the 2025 Open at Royal Portrush. Before the tournament begins, Scheffler will participate in the Heroes Classic on Tuesday, July 14 — a pre-Open charity event — and officially return the Claret Jug ahead of the week. He leads the world rankings and the FedExCup standings, and arrives at Birkdale in the form of a true champion. The field is loaded. Rory McIlroy, Xander Schauffele, Viktor Hovland, and virtually every major name in men's professional golf is entered or expected. Several LIV Golf players qualified through Final Qualifying in June — including Josele Ballester, Caleb Surratt, and Peter Uihlein — with additional spots available through the Genesis Scottish Open (July 9-12) and the brand-new Last Chance Qualifier, held at Royal Birkdale itself on the Monday before play begins. The Genesis Scottish Open at The Renaissance Club in North Berwick runs July 9 to 12, one week before Birkdale, and serves as both an elite tune-up and an Open qualifier for the top non-exempt finisher. Scheffler, McIlroy, and Hovland are among those entered. Eleven days. One Claret Jug. Who wins at Royal Birkdale? ### Image Post Caption The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale is 11 days away.
