Six Days to Shinnecock: Scheffler's Grand Slam Bid, His 30th Birthday, and Why the US Open Has It All

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

The 126th United States Open Championship is six days away, and the story writes itself. Scottie Scheffler — the world's best golfer, the +550 tournament favorite — arrives at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York, with a chance to complete his career Grand Slam. The final round is scheduled for Sunday, June 21. That is also Scheffler's 30th birthday.

Shinnecock Hills is hosting the US Open for the sixth time in its history, and its reputation is fearsome. The course rewards ball-striking precision but punishes missed greens with brutal severity. Only twice in the modern era has a US Open champion at Shinnecock shot under par for 72 holes.

Scheffler's Competitive Edge

Scheffler surveyed the course on June 1 and came away with a clear-eyed assessment. "Once you start missing fairways out there, you have no chance," he said — while also noting that the fairways are wide enough to provide opportunity for the precise hitter he is. The green complexes, he emphasized, are "extraordinarily difficult."

That assessment aligns perfectly with his statistical profile. Scheffler possesses the best short game on the PGA Tour this season, gaining 0.5 shots per round over the field in a category that becomes the most critical skill when greens repel shots and scrambling decides championships. His 15% win probability leads all competitors.

The historical precedent for great scramblers at Shinnecock is established. In 2004, Retief Goosen hit just six of 18 greens in the final round yet scrambled his way to a 71 and won by two shots. If the greens play firm and fast on Sunday, as they traditionally do, Scheffler's short game advantage becomes his most powerful weapon.

McIlroy and the Pursuit of a 7th Major

The main threat to Scheffler is Rory McIlroy, the 2011 US Open champion, now at +900. McIlroy is chasing a seventh major title and has been in strong form through the spring season. At +1400, Jon Rahm — a two-time major champion comfortable on US Open-style setups — is also among the contenders. Bryson DeChambeau, Ludvig Aberg, Cameron Young, and Tommy Fleetwood are each listed at +2000.

What's Next

The US Open begins Thursday, June 18, with the final round scheduled for Sunday, June 21. Coverage is on NBC, Peacock, and Golf Channel. If Scheffler performs as his form and the statistics suggest, the question of his career Grand Slam will be answered on his 30th birthday, under the late-afternoon sun at Shinnecock Hills. The sport has rarely arranged a more compelling storyline.
