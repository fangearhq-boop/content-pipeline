LIV Golf Gets $130M June Lifeline From PIF — But Bankruptcy Preparations Continue

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund has injected approximately $130 million into LIV Golf this month, following a $66 million capital injection in May. But the latest funding, revealed through regulatory filings, has done little to resolve the existential questions surrounding the breakaway tour's future — and internal signals continue to suggest the situation is more precarious than the capital influx implies.

LIV Golf is still seeking between $250 million and $350 million from outside investors, according to multiple reports. That fundraise is now the central priority for leadership as the PIF has made clear it will end its direct funding role at the conclusion of the 2026 season — cutting off the financial engine that has powered more than $5 billion in total investment since LIV's launch in 2022.

Turnaround Specialists Join the Board

Perhaps the most telling development has been the composition of LIV Golf's new board of directors. Two independent members have been added: Gene Davis, who works in turnaround management, and Jon Zinman, who advises companies in bankruptcy proceedings. The appointments signal that LIV's leadership is actively preparing for restructuring scenarios, not just optimistic business development.

CEO Scott O'Neil acknowledged to CNBC that he cannot guarantee the final four events of the 2026 season will be held. That statement — from the organization's chief executive — is a remarkable admission for a tour that has spent four years presenting itself as a credible challenger to the PGA Tour's global dominance.

What Investors Want to Know

Prospective backers have reportedly been seeking clarity on what LIV Golf looks like post-PIF — specifically, which players would remain committed if the tour enters a new era without guaranteed Saudi funding. The answer to that question is unknown, and its uncertainty has complicated the fundraise. Several players signed to LIV are under long-term contracts, but the appetite for continuing without a clear financial foundation is an open question.

What's Next

If LIV Golf secures the $250-$350 million it is seeking from outside investors, the tour could transition to a new ownership and operational model that sustains it beyond the PIF era. If the fundraise fails, the bankruptcy groundwork already in place would presumably be activated. The situation will likely reach a resolution point before the end of the 2026 season. Golf's landscape — shaped so dramatically by LIV's arrival four years ago — is approaching another pivotal moment.
