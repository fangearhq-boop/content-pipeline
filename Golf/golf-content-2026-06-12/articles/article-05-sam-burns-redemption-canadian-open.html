Sam Burns Is Leading the Canadian Open — One Year After Losing It in a Playoff at the Same Course

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

Golf has a gift for bringing you back to the place where it hurt you most and asking whether you've learned anything. For Sam Burns, that place is TPC Toronto at Osprey Valley North Course in Caledon, Ontario.

This is where Burns played a playoff against Ryan Fox last summer and lost. This is where a chance to end his PGA Tour win drought — stretching back to the 2023 WGC-Dell Match Play — slipped away in the cruelest of fashions. This is the course where his most painful near-miss lives in the memory.

Now it is one year later. Burns has opened the 2026 RBC Canadian Open with a 6-under-par 64, sharing the first-round lead alongside Brooks Koepka, Sahith Theegala, Eric Cole, Emiliano Grillo, and Matthew Anderson.

Fox Is Chasing From Behind This Time

The symmetry of the situation is almost too neat for fiction. Ryan Fox — the man who beat Burns in that playoff, the defending champion arriving in Caledon to defend his title — sits at 4-under after Round 1. That puts Fox four shots behind the co-leader. Burns, who spent last summer dealing with the aftermath of Fox's victory, is now the man at the front of the board and Fox is the one doing the chasing.

Three rounds remain. In golf, a four-shot lead after Round 1 is notable but far from decisive. Fox is too good a player and this course is too familiar to him to be written off. The drama is far from resolved. But the setup for a compelling final-round narrative — Burns protecting the lead versus Fox attempting to defend — is already in place.

A Drought That's Deserved Ending

Burns has been one of the Tour's most consistent performers without a recent win. His ball-striking is elite, his short game is reliable, and his on-course composure has never been in question. The drought since 2023 has been one of professional golf's more perplexing prolonged gaps between wins for a player of his caliber.

A victory this week would answer many of those questions at once — and it would answer them at the exact location of his previous heartbreak. Few outcomes in a golf season would carry the narrative weight of Burns winning the Canadian Open after losing it there in a playoff twelve months earlier.

What's Next

Round 2 is underway Friday. The cut will be made at the end of the day. Burns will need to maintain his concentration and continue building his total through the weekend to give himself a chance at the title on Sunday, June 14. If he does, and if Fox is in contention, this tournament is about to become one of the week's best stories in sports — regardless of what's happening at the NBA Finals or the Spanish GP.
