Three Teams Share Dow Championship Lead at -3 — Korda Hunting From Three Back

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

Through two rounds of the 2026 Dow Championship at Midland Country Club in Midland, Michigan, three teams are tied at the top of the leaderboard at 3-under-par: C. Boyd and M. Zhang (United States/China), N. Broch Estrup and G. Dryburgh (Denmark/Scotland), and G. Kaur and H. Naveed (United States/Australia).

The international makeup of the co-leaders reflects exactly what makes the Dow Championship unique. As the only team event on the LPGA Tour, it draws partnerships from across the globe and produces a leaderboard dynamic unlike anything else in women's professional golf.

Korda's Pursuit

Nelly Korda and her partner Olivia Cowan — playing under the informal "Legally Blonde" team nickname — finished Round 1 three shots off the lead and have ground to make up over the weekend. Korda, one of the most recognized names in women's golf, brings a consistent excellence to everything she plays, and three shots is not an insurmountable deficit in a best-ball format where birdies can be manufactured more freely than in stroke play.

The Dow Championship's format — foursomes and four-ball competitions over four days — creates a strategic dimension absent from standard LPGA events. In four-ball, having two elite players in a team creates the opportunity for aggressive play from one partner while the other plays conservatively and secures the score. Teams that execute this balance well tend to generate the fewest bogeys while accumulating the most birdies.

Tournament Context

The $3.3 million purse and the competition's setting at Midland Country Club have made this event a fan favorite since its inception. The 72-team field includes 144 of the world's best players, and the pairing format generates storylines — national partnerships, reunions between former teammates, and competitive rivalries — that would not exist in individual stroke play.

What's Next

Rounds 3 and 4 take place Saturday and Sunday, with the Dow Championship concluding on June 14. Coverage continues on Golf Channel and Peacock. The three co-leading teams have a narrow edge, but the compressed leaderboard means a hot round from any partnership could change everything. Korda and Cowan, in particular, have the talent to make a run.
