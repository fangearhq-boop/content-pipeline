Burns and Koepka Lead Canadian Open R2 — Fitzpatrick Three Back in Chase for 4th Win

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

Round 2 of the $9.8 million RBC Canadian Open is underway at TPC Toronto at Osprey Valley North Course, and the first-round leaderboard has set up a compelling weekend. Six players share the 18-hole lead at 6-under-par: Sam Burns, Brooks Koepka, Sahith Theegala, Eric Cole, Emiliano Grillo, and Matthew Anderson.

Koepka's opening 64 — eight birdies against two bogeys — was as clean and dangerous as anything his career has produced. Koepka has always been at his best when the stakes are highest, and arriving at TPC Toronto one week before the US Open with co-leading position only adds to the intrigue.

Fitzpatrick Lurks With Major Stakes in Mind

Matt Fitzpatrick is three strokes back at 3-under-par, alongside Tommy Fleetwood. Fitzpatrick enters this week as the hottest player in the field — three wins in 2026 already across the Valspar, RBC Heritage, and Zurich Classic — and a fourth would establish him as one of the dominant stories of this PGA Tour season.

Defending champion Ryan Fox is at 4-under and one stroke ahead of Fitzpatrick going into Friday's action. Shane Lowry is a further stroke back, rounding out a contention window packed with international talent.

The US Open Backdrop

The Canadian Open holds a particular significance this week: it is the final Tour event before the US Open begins at Shinnecock Hills on June 18. Several players in the field are using TPC Toronto to sharpen form ahead of the season's third major. Good iron play on a tight, tree-lined North American layout is exactly the warm-up exercise for Shinnecock's demanding greens.

Notably, world No. 1 Scottie Scheffler — who is the +550 favorite for the US Open — is not in the Canadian Open field. He is resting and preparing specifically for Shinnecock. Rory McIlroy, similarly, is not in Toronto. That makes the Canadian Open leaderboard a slightly different animal than most weeks: the biggest names at the top of the world rankings are absent, creating an opening for players like Burns, Koepka, and Fitzpatrick to seize momentum heading into next week.

What's Next

The cut will be made after Round 2 on Friday. Saturday's third round and Sunday's final round complete the tournament, with the champion crowned on June 14. Whether the winner is a leader from Thursday's opening day or a weekend charge from someone in the chasing pack, the Canadian Open delivers one of the more unpredictable leaderboards in the game.
