Scottie Scheffler Takes July 4 Weekend Off — And He's Still the Best Golfer in the World

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 4, 2026

Scottie Scheffler is not at TPC Deere Run in Silvis this week. The John Deere Classic is one of the Fourth of July tradition events on the PGA Tour schedule — and the world's No. 1 ranked golfer is sitting it out.

He is still No. 1. The ranking updated on June 29, 2026, and Scheffler remains at the summit of the Official World Golf Ranking — where he has spent more than 175 weeks across his career. Not playing on a given week has not changed that.

The Travelers Playoff Loss Didn't Move the Needle

Most recently, Scheffler finished runner-up at the Travelers Championship — one of the Tour's Signature Events. Viktor Hovland beat him in a Monday playoff at 21-under par, claiming the $3.6 million winner's check and 700 FedExCup points. It was the kind of loss that, for most players, would sting the world ranking badly.

For Scheffler, his ranking didn't move. The gap between him and the field is so large that even a runner-up finish at a major event barely registers. That is what sustained dominance looks like in golf.

The FedExCup Race

Despite sitting out this week, Scheffler leads the FedExCup standings with 3,511 points — a comfortable margin at the top of the season-long points race. The FedExCup Playoffs begin in late August, and the player who finishes No. 1 earns $25 million in bonus money on top of regular tournament winnings.

Scheffler has four major championships to his name and has been the PGA Tour's dominant force. The second half of 2026 features several events where he is expected to compete for titles, including the Open Championship at Royal Birkdale — July 16-19 — as the next marquee major.

What's Next

Scheffler returns to action at Royal Birkdale in less than two weeks for the Open Championship — golf's oldest major, held on the Lancashire coast in England. After a rest week over the holiday, the world's No. 1 golfer will be fresh and ready. The question is not whether Scheffler will contend at the Open. The question is whether anyone can stop him.
