LIV Golf's Funding Crisis: CEO Won't Guarantee the Final Four Events of 2026 Will Happen

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 4, 2026

When the CEO of a professional sports league is asked whether his remaining events will take place and refuses to say yes, that is a crisis. That is where LIV Golf finds itself in July 2026.

Scott O'Neil, the CEO of the LIV Golf League, was directly asked whether he could guarantee the final four events of the 2026 season would be played as scheduled. He declined to make that guarantee. Instead, he told reporters: "What I can guarantee is that there is a heck of a return if you come and invest in this business."

The Money Problem

LIV Golf needs approximately $400 million to cover player contracts, prize purses, and operating costs for the remainder of the 2026 season. The primary funding source — Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — has provided around $200 million, roughly one-third of the $600 million it had committed. That leaves a $400 million gap that O'Neil is trying to fill with private investment.

In April, PIF formalized its position in a public statement: "PIF has made the decision to fund LIV Golf only for the remainder of the 2026 season. The substantial investment required by LIV Golf over a longer term is no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy."

The message was direct: PIF is not a long-term partner for LIV Golf. The 2026 season is the last year of guaranteed Saudi funding — and even that guarantee has significant gaps.

The Search for New Money

O'Neil is attempting to raise $350 million in new private capital. He told reporters he had five investor meetings earlier this week and has 18 more scheduled. The pitch is that LIV Golf's global reach, television rights, and growing fan base represent an investment opportunity. Finding private investors willing to commit that kind of capital — with the next event just three weeks away — is a significant challenge.

For context, LIV Golf's player field has already changed significantly ahead of 2026. Brooks Koepka and Patrick Reed departed before the season to begin the process of returning to the PGA Tour. The tour that launched with enormous money and star power is navigating a different kind of moment now.

What's Next

The next LIV Golf event is scheduled for July 23-26 at JCB Golf and Country Club in Rocester, England. Whether all four remaining 2026 events survive depends on whether O'Neil can secure the funding he needs in the coming weeks. For the players competing in LIV, the financial uncertainty raises real questions about the organization's long-term future beyond 2026.
