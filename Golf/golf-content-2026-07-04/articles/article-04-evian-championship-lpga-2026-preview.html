Evian Championship Preview: LPGA's Fifth Major Heads to the French Alps July 9-12

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 4, 2026

One week from today, the LPGA Tour travels to one of golf's most breathtaking settings for the sport's most significant prize in women's golf this side of the U.S. Women's Open.

The Amundi Evian Championship — the fifth and final major championship of the 2026 LPGA season — begins July 9 at Evian Resort Golf Club in Evian-les-Bains, France. The purse is $9.10 million. The backdrop is the French Alps reflected in Lake Geneva. The history is of a major that has produced three consecutive first-time winners in recent years.

A Tournament That Launches Careers

The Evian Championship has built a reputation as the place where the next generation of women's golf announces itself. In 2025, Australian Grace Kim claimed the title — becoming the third consecutive first-time major winner to lift the trophy at Evian Resort Golf Club.

That pattern — young players, breakthrough moments, a major championship earned in front of the Alps — has become part of the Evian Championship's identity. The tournament does not always go to the biggest name in the field. It goes to the player who rises to the moment.

The Setting

Evian-les-Bains is a spa town on the southern shore of Lake Geneva, situated between the Swiss border and the French Alps. The Evian Resort Golf Club course sits above the lake with panoramic mountain views that define the experience for both players and fans. There are major championships played on beautiful courses — and then there is the Evian Championship, which occupies a category of its own for pure visual drama.

The tournament runs July 9 through July 12, with the championship round on Sunday, July 12.

What's Next

With the LPGA Tour heading to France in five days, the question of who takes the title — and who becomes the next first-time major winner at Evian — is the biggest story in women's golf heading into next week. The field will be finalized in the coming days. One player will leave Evian-les-Bains with the Evian Championship trophy and the $9.10 million purse shared among the competitors. The countdown to France has begun.
