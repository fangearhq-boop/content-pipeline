Preston Stout Is Contending at the John Deere Classic: The World's No. 1 Amateur Is Ready for the Pros

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 4, 2026

This is only Preston Stout's fourth PGA Tour start. He is an amateur — still eligible for collegiate golf, carrying a world ranking designation different from the professionals around him. And through 36 holes at the John Deere Classic at TPC Deere Run, he has made the cut and sits tied for 11th place.

The world's No. 1 amateur is contending on the Fourth of July. This is the feel-good story of the holiday weekend in golf.

Who Is Preston Stout?

Stout is the reigning NCAA individual champion from Oklahoma State, the program that has produced some of professional golf's most accomplished alumni. He claims the No. 1 amateur ranking in the world after Jackson Koivun — the previous holder — turned professional earlier this year.

His opening round at the John Deere Classic turned heads immediately. He finished the day tied for eighth, just three shots off the lead. More notable than his position: he outplayed two-time tournament champion Jordan Spieth and J.T. Poston — who won the Memorial Tournament this year — in the opening round of a PGA Tour event.

Making His Mark Against the Pros

After 36 holes, Stout's name sits at T11 in a group that includes past champion Zach Johnson, multiple-winner Chris Gotterup, and Ben Griffin. These are proven professional golfers. Stout is alongside them, holding his own, making his first cut in his fourth Tour appearance.

Amateur golfers who make a splash at PGA Tour events often announce themselves as future professionals in the making. Stout has that energy about him this week in Silvis — a young player who clearly belongs at this level, even if his pro debut is still ahead.

What's Next

Stout tees off in Round 3 on Saturday, July 4, with an opportunity to move further up the leaderboard. As an amateur, he cannot win prize money — but he can win the leaderboard position and the credibility that comes with competing against the best players in the world. A top-10 finish at a PGA Tour event in his fourth career appearance would be a remarkable achievement. He is two rounds away from making that happen.
