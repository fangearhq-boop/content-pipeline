Lucas Glover Bogey-Free at -14: The John Deere Classic Leader Is Playing Flawless Golf on July 4

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 4, 2026

Through 36 holes at the John Deere Classic, Lucas Glover has not made a single bogey. Zero dropped shots at TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois, across what amounts to the best back-to-back rounds of his professional career. On the Fourth of July — the holiday where golf in America's heartland reaches its peak audience — Glover has the stage entirely to himself.

The veteran PGA Tour player shot rounds of 63 and 65 to reach 14-under par, the best 36-hole score of his 582 Tour career starts. He carries a two-shot lead into Round 3 on Saturday.

The Numbers Behind the Perfection

Fourteen birdies. Twenty-two pars. No bogeys. Glover's card reads like a man who has found something on the practice range that he cannot lose.

Lee Hodges is the closest pursuer at 12-under, having shot a 66 in round two. Behind him, a cluster of chasers — Max Homa, Chris Gotterup, and Zac Blair — all trail by at least three shots. None of them have found the consistency Glover has brought to the course.

"I'm not thinking about bogeys or not," Glover said when asked about the streak. "I'm just playing golf." That simple approach has produced something extraordinary.

What a Win Would Mean

The John Deere Classic is a tournament Glover knows well — this is the Fourth of July stop on the PGA Tour schedule, one of the events that gives the game its character across the American calendar. Winning here on a holiday weekend, after a historically clean 36 holes, would be a milestone performance in a long and accomplished career.

Glover is not among the most decorated names in professional golf, but 582 PGA Tour starts represents a career built on consistency and professionalism. These two rounds — his best starting effort ever — suggest something clicking into place at the right moment.

What's Next

Round 3 takes place Saturday, July 4 — with Glover as the clear crowd favorite to extend his lead. Round 4 on Sunday will determine the champion and the FedExCup points that go with the title. If Glover's bogey-free run continues through the weekend, the John Deere Classic trophy is his.
