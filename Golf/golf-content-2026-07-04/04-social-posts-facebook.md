### STORY 3: LIV Golf Funding Crisis

**Long-Form Post**

⚠️ LIV GOLF FUNDING CRISIS: CEO WON'T GUARANTEE FINAL 4 EVENTS OF 2026

The LIV Golf League is facing a financial crisis that threatens to end its 2026 season early.

At the center of the problem: money. LIV Golf needs approximately $400 million to cover player contracts, prize purses, and operating costs for the remainder of the 2026 season. Saudi Arabia's sovereign wealth fund, the Public Investment Fund (PIF), has only provided around $200 million — roughly a third of the $600 million it committed.

In April, PIF made it official: it would fund LIV only through the end of the 2026 season, saying "the substantial investment required by LIV Golf over a longer term is no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy."

When LIV CEO Scott O'Neil was asked directly whether he could guarantee the final four events of 2026 would take place, he refused to say yes. Instead he told reporters: "What I can guarantee is that there is a heck of a return if you come and invest in this business." He has 18 investor meetings scheduled in the search for $350 million in new private capital.

For the players — many of whom left the PGA Tour for enormous guaranteed contracts — this is not a theoretical concern. The next LIV event is July 23-26 in England. Whether they all get there is now an open question.

The tournament has been a launching pad for first-time major winners in recent years. In 2025, Grace Kim claimed her breakthrough major title at Evian — the third consecutive first-time major winner to win at the course.

The Evian Championship occupies a unique place on the LPGA calendar. The lake setting, the French Alps backdrop, and the Resort Golf Club create a visual drama unlike any other event on Tour. For the players, it's a bucket-list week even before the major championship status is factored in.

With the top players in women's golf descending on the south shore of Lake Geneva in five days, the question of who breaks through at Evian in 2026 is the LPGA's next great storyline.
