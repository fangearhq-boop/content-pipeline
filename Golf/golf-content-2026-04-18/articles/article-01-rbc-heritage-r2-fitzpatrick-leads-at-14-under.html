RBC Heritage Round 2: Fitzpatrick's 63 Grabs the Lead at -14, Bhatia's 11-Birdie Record Lights Up Harbour Town

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 18, 2026

Matt Fitzpatrick survived a lucky break at the 14th and never looked back. Akshay Bhatia matched his 63 with 11 birdies, tying a tournament record. The RBC Heritage is officially a wide-open event heading into the weekend.

Matt Fitzpatrick acknowledged the luck in his round, and there was plenty of it. At the par-3 14th on Friday at Harbour Town Golf Links, his tee shot drifted toward the water hazard, hung on the edge, and stayed dry. Fitzpatrick walked to the green, studied the 30-foot birdie putt, and drained it. He kept making birdies from there, finished with an 8-under 63, and found himself the halfway leader at the 2026 RBC Heritage at 14 under par.

"I got lucky," Fitzpatrick said of the 14th hole incident. He did not get lucky with the putt — that was pure skill. The 63 was Fitzpatrick's second round of the week after an opening 65, and it was enough to move him one shot ahead of Viktor Hovland, who sits in second at 13 under.

Bhatia Ties the Record, Moves Up the Board

The other story of Friday at Harbour Town was Akshay Bhatia, who matched Fitzpatrick stroke for stroke with his own 63. Bhatia went about it differently — 11 birdies in his round, which tied the RBC Heritage single-round record for birdies in a round. Bhatia had struggled in Round 1 and entered Friday well back of the leaders, but the 11-birdie effort moved him to 6 under par overall and back into contention heading into the weekend.

In a week when Harbour Town Golf Links has been yielding low scores for the game's best players, both Fitzpatrick's lucky 63 and Bhatia's birdie barrage qualify as the rounds of the tournament so far.

The Leaderboard at the Halfway Point

Harris English sits third at 10 under par, three off the lead after a solid second round. Patrick Cantlay, Soren Straka, and Ludvig Åberg — who led after Round 1 with a 63 — are clustered together at 9 under, in a tie for fourth. The leaderboard is compact and the field is still very much in play.

Scottie Scheffler, the world No. 1 who opened Round 1 with a tee shot out of bounds before salvaging a 68, is at 3 under and several shots off the lead. But it bears repeating: the RBC Heritage is a Signature Event, and there is no cut. Every player in the 82-man field will tee it up on Saturday. That includes Scheffler, who won this tournament in 2024 and has the game to work his way into contention over the final two rounds if the leaders stumble.

McIlroy's Absence Felt but Understood

Rory McIlroy is not in Hilton Head Island this week. He withdrew from the RBC Heritage before the tournament began, citing the desire for a mental reset after winning the Masters. McIlroy had won back-to-back green jackets the week before — his second consecutive Masters title — and chose to rest rather than defend the RBC Heritage crown that Scheffler claimed in 2024. Under PGA Tour rules in place since 2025, he faces no financial penalty for skipping the Signature Event.

His absence removes the narrative thread of a potential Masters-to-Hilton Head double, but it also opens the field considerably. Fitzpatrick, a former US Open champion who has been a consistent presence on PGA Tour leaderboards without winning the sport's biggest events, has a genuine opportunity to claim a marquee title this week.

What's Next

Round 3 is Saturday at Harbour Town Golf Links. Fitzpatrick will lead the final groups out, one shot ahead of Hovland and three ahead of English. The par-71 layout has played scores in the mid-to-high 60s through two rounds, and there is no reason to expect that trend to reverse unless the wind picks up on Saturday.

Bhatia's record-tying 11-birdie effort also puts him in position to make a run from 6 under — eight shots back, but Harbour Town is the type of course that can yield sudden scoring runs. The halfway point at Hilton Head is rarely where the RBC Heritage is decided. Sunday is when it matters.
