LIV Golf Mexico City Round 2: Rahm Rallies to Lead at -10 as Perez's 73 Ends His Early Magic

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | April 18, 2026

Jon Rahm turned a rocky front nine into a 67 and the halfway lead at Club de Golf Chapultepec. Victor Perez's second-round 73 dropped him from the individual lead to T6. Saturday sets up a compelling weekend finish.

Jon Rahm did not look like a leader early in Friday's second round at LIV Golf Mexico City. He struggled on the front nine at Club de Golf Chapultepec, making mistakes that threatened to leave him chasing the field into the weekend. Then he turned it around on the back nine, posted a 67, and found himself atop the individual leaderboard at 10 under par.

Rahm leads by one shot heading into Saturday's third round. Matthew Wolff, Tom McKibbin, and Harold Varner III are each tied at 9 under, one behind. Luis Masaveu is alone in fifth at 8 under. It is a tight leaderboard at the halfway point of LIV Golf Mexico City, with the tournament's 72-hole format leaving two full rounds to play.

Perez's Collapse from the Lead

The big story of Round 2 was the fall of Victor Perez. The Frenchman had been spectacular in Round 1 at 7,350 feet of altitude at Chapultepec, firing a 62 to take a commanding three-shot lead. On Friday, the game abandoned him. Perez shot a 73 — 11 strokes higher than his opening round — and tumbled from the individual lead all the way to a tie for sixth at 6 under par.

Tyrrell Hatton, who arrived in Mexico City off the back of his best Masters finish in years — a tie for third at Augusta — is also at T6 at 6 under. Perez and Hatton will need significant moves on the weekend to return to contention, but in a format with two rounds remaining, they are not out of it yet.

Rahm's Stakes in Mexico City

For Jon Rahm, this tournament matters in several directions. His performance at the Masters — a tie for 38th, adding to a pattern of disappointing major results since he joined LIV Golf — has sharpened the scrutiny on his career trajectory. A win in Mexico City, in LIV Golf's 72-hole format, would not address the majors question, but it would demonstrate that he remains one of the circuit's premier competitors.

Rahm won the 2023 Masters in one of the sport's most memorable performances, and he was a perennial major contender before making the move to LIV Golf. The data from Augusta in 2026 — T-38 after two rounds that never threatened the leaders — has raised questions about whether the competitive environment of LIV Golf is keeping players at their sharpest for the biggest weeks of the year. Rahm's answer will need to come on major leaderboards, not in Mexico City, but a lead on Saturday is at least a confidence-building exercise.

What's Next

Round 3 at Club de Golf Chapultepec is Saturday. Rahm leads by one shot with Wolff, McKibbin, and Varner III one stroke behind. The altitude of Mexico City — which sits at over 7,300 feet above sea level — continues to affect how players manage their energy and distance control through the week.

LIV Golf Mexico City runs through Sunday, April 19. For Rahm, a first individual title at this event would add to his LIV Golf record. For the field behind him, the compact leaderboard through two rounds means the tournament remains wide open with two rounds still to play.
