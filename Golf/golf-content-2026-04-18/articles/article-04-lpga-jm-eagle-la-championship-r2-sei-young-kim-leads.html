LPGA JM Eagle LA Championship Round 2: Sei Young Kim's Bogey-Free 65 Puts Her Atop the Leaderboard at -14

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 18, 2026

Sei Young Kim's second consecutive 65 at El Caballero Country Club is a study in consistency — zero bogeys through 36 holes, birdies in bunches, and a one-shot lead over first-round pace-setter Chisato Iwai with two rounds to play.

Sei Young Kim arrived at El Caballero Country Club in Tarzana, California, with a point to make, and she is making it one birdie at a time. Kim shot her second consecutive bogey-free 65 on Friday in the second round of the JM Eagle LA Championship, reaching 14 under par and taking a one-shot lead at the halfway point of the final LPGA Tour event before the Chevron Championship major.

Kim birdied two of her first three holes to set the tone, then made five birdies in a seven-hole stretch on the back nine to close out a round that had no blemishes and plenty of momentum. Through 36 holes, she has made not a single bogey. That kind of consistency on the hilly, tree-lined course at El Caballero is not easy to produce — and Kim has produced it twice in a row.

The Chase Group

Chisato Iwai, who led after Round 1 with a 63, remains in second place at 13 under. Iwai's first-round 63 was one of the best rounds of the week, and she has held her position despite Kim closing to within one. The gap between first and second is a single shot — the kind of margin that can evaporate or grow quickly in either direction on Saturday.

Insun Yoon is third at 12 under. Jeongeun Im, Morgane Green, and Jennifer Porvasnik are tied for fourth at 9 under par. The leaderboard has depth, but at five strokes back with two rounds to play, the T4 group has significant ground to make up on Kim and Iwai.

The Stakes: Last Event Before the Chevron

The JM Eagle LA Championship carries elevated significance this week beyond its $3.75 million purse. It is the final LPGA Tour event before the Chevron Championship — the first women's major of the 2026 season — which begins April 23 at The Club at Carlton Woods in Texas.

For players in contention in Los Angeles, a strong finish here can provide a critical momentum boost heading into a major week. For players who are struggling, a missed opportunity in LA can compound the anxiety of arriving at a major without recent confidence. Kim's form — two consecutive bogey-free rounds — puts her in the best possible position for both the JM Eagle finish and the Chevron run that follows.

Kim's Season in Context

Sei Young Kim has been one of the LPGA Tour's most consistent performers in recent seasons, with multiple wins and a reputation for reliability under pressure. A victory at the JM Eagle LA Championship would be a meaningful result in its own right — and would signal her as a contender to watch at the Chevron major the following week.

El Caballero Country Club, with its demanding terrain and tree-lined corridors, has historically rewarded precise ball-strikers. Kim's game — consistent off the tee and sharp with her irons — suits the demands of the course well. Her bogey-free run through two rounds is the clearest evidence of how well she has adapted to the track.

What's Next

Round 3 of the JM Eagle LA Championship is Saturday at El Caballero Country Club. Kim holds a one-shot lead at -14 over Iwai at -13. Round 4 is Sunday, April 19, with the final champion awarded before the LPGA Tour caravan moves to Texas for the Chevron Championship.

For Kim, the path is clear: maintain the bogey-free consistency, keep making birdies, and earn a final-round position with the lead. Two rounds of 65 have put her there. The question for Saturday is whether she can do it a third time.
