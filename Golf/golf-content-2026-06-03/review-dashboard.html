The 81st U.S. Women's Open Championship tees off at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California — the first time a USGA women's major has ever been held at this iconic course. The course plays at 6,699 yards on a par-71 layout. The total purse is $12 million. The player everyone is watching: Nelly Korda. The world No. 1 enters as the +350 favorite — a reflection of a 2026 season that has been historic. She has never been outside the top two in any event she's entered this year. But the U.S. Women's Open is the one major missing from her collection. The defending champion is Maja Stark. Atthaya Thitikul, the 23-year-old world ranking leader before Korda retook the top spot, enters as one of the players best positioned to challenge. ### Long-Form Post Rory McIlroy has won more than any golfer of his generation. Four major championships. Multiple Tour titles on both sides of the Atlantic. He has never won the Memorial Tournament. That fact is not incidental. Golf Digest called it "Rory McIlroy's strange history with Jack's Place." Jack Nicklaus's host event — the tournament the Golden Bear built in part to honor the game's history at his home course — has been one of the few events that has consistently escaped McIlroy's grasp during an otherwise dominant career. What makes the attempt this week particularly compelling is where he is in his career trajectory. Back-to-back Masters wins have elevated McIlroy to a conversation about the game's all-time greats. He arrives at the Memorial having skipped the Byron Nelson and the Charles Schwab Challenge specifically to prepare for this week's field. The +900 odds reflect respect for Scheffler — not any doubt about McIlroy's talent. The Memorial begins Thursday. The course is Muirfield Village.
