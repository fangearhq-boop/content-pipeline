Scheffler's Three-Peat Bid, McIlroy's Memorial Drought, and $20 Million on the Line Thursday

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 3, 2026

The Memorial Tournament presents by Workday begins Thursday at Muirfield Village Golf Club in Dublin, Ohio, and the storylines could not be more compelling.

World No. 1 Scottie Scheffler is attempting to win the same event three years in a row — a feat that only 17 players in PGA Tour history have managed, and that has not been accomplished since Steve Stricker's three-peat at the John Deere Classic from 2009 to 2011. The back-to-back Masters champion Rory McIlroy is attempting to win a tournament he has conspicuously never won. The purse is $20 million, with $4 million going to the winner.

This is the most loaded week of golf since the majors.

Scheffler's Historic Attempt

Scottie Scheffler has won the Memorial Tournament in 2024 and 2025. Each victory was dominant and convincing. He knows Muirfield Village Golf Club as well as any player in the field — the placement of the fairway bunkers, the speed of the approach shots on the back nine, the way the wind moves across Jack Nicklaus's design.

The 2026 season has been oddly frustrating for Scheffler despite its apparent quality. He has been in contention at major after major and finished second multiple times. His only win in 2026 came at the American Express in January. For the world No. 1, that is a long time between victories — especially at a time when McIlroy has been winning majors.

Memorial is where Scheffler resets. If any course can end the winless run, it is the course he has won back-to-back and knows by memory.

The McIlroy Factor

Rory McIlroy's 2026 season has been transformative. The back-to-back Masters championship puts him in exclusive company — only four players have ever won the Masters in consecutive years. He has four major championships in total and has established himself as the player who defines this era of golf's competition with Scheffler.

But the Memorial Tournament remains a stubborn blank in his record. Golf Digest noted "McIlroy's strange history with Jack's Place" heading into this week — an acknowledgment that his inability to win at Muirfield Village is curious for a player of his caliber. He enters at +900 odds, second on the betting board behind Scheffler.

McIlroy skipped the Byron Nelson and Charles Schwab Challenge before this week to focus specifically on Memorial preparation. That kind of targeted schedule management suggests this tournament holds particular meaning for him.

The Course and the Field

Muirfield Village Golf Club plays at 7,553 yards on a par-72 layout, though the current setup may differ slightly from year to year. The course demands precise driving, demanding iron shots into elevated greens, and a putting surface that rewards patience and controlled approach angles. It is not an easy course to "bomb and gauge" — precision typically beats length here.

The 72-player field is among the strongest of any non-major week, with nine of the world's top 10 players in attendance. Xander Schauffele, Cameron Young, and Ludvig Åberg are among the other contenders capable of challenging both Scheffler and McIlroy.

What's Next

Round 1 begins Thursday morning. The traditional broadcast window on Golf Channel and CBS will carry coverage throughout the week. Watch for early scoring conditions on Thursday — the field's Thursday performance often telegraphs which players have their games calibrated for this course.

A Scheffler three-peat would give him one of the most distinctive winning records on the PGA Tour. A McIlroy victory would close a chapter that has been open for years. Either way, Sunday afternoon at Muirfield Village delivers exactly the kind of moment that makes the Memorial one of the year's most anticipated non-major weeks.
