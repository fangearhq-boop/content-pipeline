Korda Eyes the One Trophy She's Missing: US Women's Open Opens Tomorrow at Riviera

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 3, 2026

Three major championships. One conspicuous gap.

Nelly Korda arrives at Riviera Country Club with the world No. 1 ranking, the dominant form record in women's golf this season, and a resume that would satisfy most players' career ambitions. She is also missing the one title that she has come closest to winning and never has: the U.S. Women's Open Championship.

The 81st U.S. Women's Open begins tomorrow at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California. It is the first USGA women's major ever held at this course, which has hosted the PGA Tour's Genesis Invitational for decades. At 6,699 yards on a par-71 layout with a $12 million purse, it is the most prestigious stop on the LPGA calendar — and the one event Korda is most motivated to conquer.

Why This Year Feels Different

Korda has built 2026 into a season that will be remembered for years. In seven starts, she has won three times and finished runner-up three others. She has never finished outside the top two. Her Chevron Championship victory in April — a dominant five-shot win at 16-under par — was her third career major and returned her to the No. 1 position in the world rankings for the seventh time.

The Chevron win was the one that closed a chapter. Now this tournament opens a new one.

Korda finished second at the 2025 U.S. Women's Open. She knows what the final round of this tournament feels like from close range. That experience, the muscle memory of handling its specific pressure, may be an advantage that statistics cannot measure.

The Field and the Challengers

Defending champion Maja Stark returns to defend her title. Atthaya Thitikul, the 23-year-old Thai star who held the No. 1 ranking before Korda retook it, is one of the players most capable of challenging the favorite. Thitikul has two wins in 2026 but has yet to win a major championship — making this a potential breakthrough moment for her as well.

Riviera's par-71 layout will demand accuracy off the tee and precision with iron shots. The fairways are surrounded by deep rough. The greens run fast. It is a ballstriker's course that punishes loose shots and rewards consistent contact — precisely the profile that has made Korda so dominant this season.

Historic Venue

For golf fans who appreciate context, this year's U.S. Women's Open carries an extra dimension. Riviera Country Club — nicknamed "The Riviera of the West" — has hosted some of the most storied moments in men's golf history, including multiple PGA Championships and Los Angeles Opens. But it has never hosted a USGA women's major.

Tomorrow changes that. The fairways of Riviera will see the best players in women's golf compete on one of the most beautiful and demanding courses in the world.

What's Next

Round 1 begins Thursday morning. Korda will be the most-watched player all week. If her 2026 form carries through this tournament — consistent iron play, a hot putter, and the quiet confidence of a player who has been in contention every week this year — she should be in the final group on Sunday.

After that round comes the moment the question gets answered. The U.S. Women's Open is the one major Nelly Korda has never won. If she wins it this week, she becomes the owner of every major a woman golfer can claim in the current era. That is the chase that begins tomorrow morning.
