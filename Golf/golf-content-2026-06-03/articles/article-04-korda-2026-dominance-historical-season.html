3 Wins. 3 Runner-Ups. 7 Starts. Nelly Korda Is Playing the Best Golf of Her Life

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 3, 2026

There are seasons in sports where a single athlete operates at a level that demands a different vocabulary to describe.

Nelly Korda is having one of those seasons.

In seven LPGA Tour starts in 2026, she has won three times and finished runner-up three others. She has never finished outside the top two. The statistical consistency of this performance — not just winning, but being in contention at an elite level every single week — is the kind of record that makes comparisons to the greatest seasons in women's golf history entirely appropriate.

The Chevron Championship: The Crown Jewel

The 2026 Chevron Championship in April was the performance that anchored Korda's season in history. She led from the opening round and closed with a 2-under 70 to win by five shots at 16-under par. It was her third career major championship — placing her in the select company of multi-major champions in the modern LPGA era.

The victory returned her to the No. 1 position in the world rankings for the seventh time in her career. She celebrated with a cannonball into the Dinah Shore Trophy pool — a tradition that felt, in 2026, like something more than tradition. It felt like a coronation.

Korda is 27 years old. She now has 17 LPGA Tour victories and 21 wins worldwide. She has competed in the final group in every tournament she has entered this year.

The Historical Context

The most frequently cited comparison is Annika Sorenstam's 2001 season — widely considered the greatest single year in women's golf history. Sorenstam won eight times in 2001 and was the dominant force in women's golf over a full calendar. The comparison to Korda's 2026 pace speaks to the quality of her current form more than to any statistical equivalency; 2026 is not yet complete, and the sample size of seven events captures only a portion of the season.

What makes the comparison reasonable is not just the wins but the consistency. Korda has not had a week where she appeared off form or off pace. Every tournament has produced performance in or near the final pairing. That sustained excellence is rare at any level.

What's Still Missing

The U.S. Women's Open, which begins tomorrow at Riviera Country Club, is the only major Korda has not won. She has three majors — but this is the one that has escaped her. Her closest call came at the 2025 U.S. Women's Open, where she finished second.

The framing around her season has shifted with each passing week. Early in 2026, the question was whether she could sustain her form. By May, the question had become how dominant she could be. Heading into the USWO, the question is: can she complete the major collection?

What's Next

The U.S. Women's Open at Riviera is the tournament everyone in women's golf is watching this week. Korda enters as the +350 favorite — a reflection not just of her odds against the field but of the narrative weight of this event for her at this particular point in her career.

If she wins, she becomes the owner of every major available to a woman golfer in the current era. If she does not, the question carries forward to the KPMG Women's PGA Championship in late June at Hazeltine. Either way, 2026 has already produced a season that will be studied for years. Tomorrow is the next chapter.
