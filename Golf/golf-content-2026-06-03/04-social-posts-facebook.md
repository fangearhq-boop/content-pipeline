### STORY 2: Memorial Tournament Preview

**Long-Form Post (10:30 AM ET)**

Three consecutive wins at the same PGA Tour event. Only 17 players have done it in the modern era. The last to accomplish it was Steve Stricker, who won the John Deere Classic three straight years from 2009 to 2011.

Starting Thursday, Scottie Scheffler attempts to join that list at Muirfield Village Golf Club.

The two-time defending Memorial Tournament champion is the world No. 1 and the betting favorite once again. His 2026 season has been defined by consistent excellence and a puzzling inability to close — he finished runner-up at the Masters, the RBC Heritage, and the Cadillac Championship before recording a solo third place at the CJ Cup Byron Nelson. His last victory came at the American Express in January.

At Muirfield Village, though, Scheffler has been different. Two consecutive wins at Jack Nicklaus's signature course. A track record of handling the demands of this specific layout better than anyone in the current field.

Standing in his way is the man who has beaten him on the biggest stages in 2026. Rory McIlroy — the back-to-back Masters champion — has never won the Memorial Tournament despite competing there throughout his career. McIlroy enters at +900 odds, motivated by the specific gap on a resume that otherwise reads like one of the greatest in the modern game.

The field of 72 players begins their pursuit Thursday. Purse: $20 million.

### STORY 3: LIV Golf Andalucia

**Long-Form Post (1:00 PM ET)**

Jon Rahm returns to Real Club Valderrama this week — the golf course that defines Spanish golf in the same way Augusta National defines American golf.

The ninth LIV Golf League event of 2026 takes place June 4-7 at the par-71 Valderrama course in Cadiz, Spain. Rahm, who captains Legion XIII, is the 16/5 tournament favorite and leads the season-long points standings by 188.68 points over Bryson DeChambeau. Playing on home soil, before fans who have watched him rise from Spanish prodigy to world-level superstar, represents the setting where Rahm has always played his best golf.

DeChambeau is the 15/2 counter-challenge. The American has two wins in 2026 and has been within Rahm's shadow in the standings all season. A Valderrama win would allow him to close that gap significantly.

But it's Joaquin Niemann who carries the most momentum. The Chilean won LIV Golf Korea last week — his eighth career LIV title. A Valderrama win would give him nine and cement his status as the most prolific individual winner in LIV history.

The weather forecast for Valderrama is extraordinary: temperatures between 91 and 96 degrees all four days. Hot, dry, challenging conditions for a course already demanding enough in temperate weather.

### STORY 4: Korda's 2026 Dominance

**Long-Form Post (1:00 PM ET)**

No player in women's golf has started a season the way Nelly Korda has in 2026.

Seven tournaments entered. Three wins. Three runner-ups. Zero finishes outside the top two. That kind of consistent excellence has prompted comparisons to Annika Sorenstam's legendary 2001 season — arguably the greatest single year in LPGA history.

The centerpiece of Korda's year was the 2026 Chevron Championship, where she won by five shots at 16-under par. It was her third career major championship and returned her to the No. 1 position in the world rankings for the seventh time. She celebrated with a now-famous cannonball jump into the Dinah Shore Trophy pool — a moment that perfectly captured the joy of a player who knows she is playing the best golf of her life.

Korda is 27 years old. She now has 17 LPGA Tour wins and 21 worldwide victories. The only major still missing from her collection is the U.S. Women's Open, which begins tomorrow at Riviera Country Club.

She finished second at the 2025 U.S. Women's Open. She knows what it costs to be close and not win. This week, that experience may be exactly the edge she needs.

### STORY 5: McIlroy's Memorial Curse

**Long-Form Post (3:30 PM ET)**

Rory McIlroy has won more than any golfer of his generation. Four major championships. Multiple Tour titles on both sides of the Atlantic. He won the Masters in consecutive years — only the fourth player in history to accomplish that — and arrived in Dublin, Ohio for this week's Memorial Tournament as one of the most decorated players ever to tee it up at Muirfield Village.

He has never won the Memorial Tournament.

That fact is not incidental. Golf Digest called it "Rory McIlroy's strange history with Jack's Place." Jack Nicklaus's host event — the tournament the Golden Bear built in part to honor the game's history at his home course — has been one of the few events that has consistently escaped McIlroy's grasp during an otherwise dominant career.

What makes the attempt this week particularly compelling is where he is in his career trajectory. Back-to-back Masters wins have elevated McIlroy to a conversation about the game's all-time greats. He arrives at the Memorial having skipped the Byron Nelson and the Charles Schwab Challenge specifically to prepare for this week's field. The +900 odds reflect respect for Scheffler — not any doubt about McIlroy's talent.

The Memorial begins Thursday. The course is Muirfield Village. The question of whether Rory McIlroy can finally conquer Jack's place gets answered over four rounds starting tomorrow morning.
