Open Championship 2026 Preview: Final Qualifying Tomorrow as Birkdale Awaits

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

June 29, 2026

Three weeks. That is all that separates the golf world from the oldest major championship in the sport's history. The 154th Open Championship runs July 16-19 at Royal Birkdale in Southport, England — a course returning to The Open rota for the first time since Jordan Spieth's unforgettable scrambling win in 2017.

Final Qualifying for the championship takes place tomorrow, June 30, when the Last Chance Qualifier format determines the final spots in the 156-player field. For players without full exemptions through world ranking or tour victories, tomorrow is their last opportunity to earn a place at Birkdale.

The Defending Champion

Scottie Scheffler arrives at Royal Birkdale as the defending Open Championship champion, a distinction that adds another layer to an already remarkable resume for the World No. 1. His defence of the title at one of England's great links venues will be one of the most closely watched storylines of the major season's second half.

Scheffler's game is built on the kind of control and precision that translates well to links conditions — his iron play and short game are world-class, and his ability to manage course and conditions has been a hallmark of his dominance. Whether Birkdale's specific demands suit his game as well as the course where he won last year remains to be seen, but he arrives as the heavy favorite on most early market assessments.

No Tiger in the Field

Tiger Woods will not play in the 2026 Open Championship. His continuing battle with physical limitations — the aftermath of injuries that have curtailed his schedule substantially over recent years — means that golf's most marketable name will be absent from Royal Birkdale's links.

The Open Championship without Tiger Woods at a course he has never won (Birkdale was on the rota before his peak championship years and has not appeared during his active career at full strength) is not a diminished event in competitive terms. The field remains among the strongest in golf. But the commercial and cultural pull of Tiger's presence is real, and his absence is always noted.

Royal Birkdale: The Course

Royal Birkdale has a deserved reputation as one of the fairest links tests in The Open Championship rota. Unlike some coastal links venues where the randomness of wind and bounce can produce dramatically uneven outcomes, Birkdale's layout tends to reward good ball striking more consistently. The fairways provide relatively defined corridors, the rough is brutal but manageable with proper positioning, and the greens — when the weather cooperates — allow for a variety of approaches.

The 2017 Open at Birkdale is remembered primarily for Spieth's remarkable 13th hole in the final round — a near-disqualification situation that Spieth somehow navigated to win by three shots. It is also remembered as a genuinely difficult but fair test in challenging conditions. The 2026 edition will be shaped by whatever the Lancashire coast weather delivers in mid-July.

The course has not hosted The Open in nine years. A new generation of players — Scheffler, Antonelli, young European stars, a deepened global field — will be competing on it for the first time in a major championship context.

What's Next

Final Qualifying tomorrow (June 30) will complete the 156-player field. The R&A will announce the full field and draw in the days following. Most of the major favorites — Scheffler, Rory McIlroy, Jon Rahm (if he competes), Xander Schauffele, and the rest of the world's elite — are already in the field via exemptions.

The Open Championship is three weeks away. The links season — one of golf's most distinctive and beloved stretches of competition — is officially upon us.
