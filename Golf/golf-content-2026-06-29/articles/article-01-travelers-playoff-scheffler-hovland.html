Travelers Championship Playoff: Scheffler vs Hovland in Monday Sudden Death

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

June 29, 2026

The Travelers Championship at TPC River Highlands in Cromwell, Connecticut, did not resolve on Sunday as planned. Weather delays forced the final round into Monday morning, and when the scores were tallied, two of the world's best players were standing in exactly the same place: Scottie Scheffler and Viktor Hovland, both at -21, both needing one more hole — or as many as it takes — to decide this Signature Event.

Collin Morikawa stands one shot back at -20 in the clubhouse, able to only watch and wait as the playoff unfolds. His tournament is done. Whether the playoff ends on the first extra hole or extends into the kind of dramatic Monday morning that golf fans remember for years is entirely up to Scheffler and Hovland.

The Stakes

The Travelers Championship carries a $20 million purse — one of the largest on the PGA Tour calendar under the Signature Event structure. The winner collects $3.6 million. For both players, the financial stakes are significant. For Scheffler, a win would add to an already dominant season that has further cemented his status at World No. 1. For Hovland, victory in a playoff at a Signature Event would represent a major career milestone and a statement win against the field's best player.

Neither player will lack for motivation. That is what makes Monday morning compelling even before the first playoff shot is struck.

The Players

Scottie Scheffler arrived at TPC River Highlands as the world's best golfer, a status he has held with remarkable consistency. His game — the accuracy from tee to green, the scrambling ability, the composure in pressure situations — makes him a heavy favorite in any head-to-head matchup. But sudden death eliminates the margin for error that a stroke-play advantage can provide, and Scheffler has lost tournaments from advantageous positions before.

Viktor Hovland is one of the most respected competitors in golf. The Norwegian brings a powerful, technically refined game and has demonstrated an ability to perform in high-stakes moments. He is not a player who loses his nerve in pressure situations. A playoff against Scheffler is exactly the kind of moment Hovland has built his career to compete in.

Morikawa's presence at -20 — just one shot back, unable to do anything but watch — adds an unusual tension to the proceedings. If either Scheffler or Hovland makes a critical error in the opening playoff holes, the thought of the man in the clubhouse will hang in the air.

TPC River Highlands in a Playoff

TPC River Highlands is a relatively short course by modern Tour standards but demands precision over power. The Connecticut layout's final stretch — where playoffs typically begin — features holes that reward accuracy and can punish aggression. The course has hosted dramatic finishes before, and the Monday morning conditions will bring whatever weather Connecticut offers in late June.

What's Next

The playoff resolves Monday morning, and whatever happens — whether Scheffler wins, Hovland wins, or Morikawa somehow gets a call back to the course — the result will shape the Tour's narrative heading into the summer's biggest stretch. The Open Championship is three weeks away at Royal Birkdale. The John Deere Classic begins July 2. The winner of Monday's playoff carries real momentum into links season.
