Haeran Ryu Wins KPMG Women's PGA in Historic Major Breakthrough

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

June 29, 2026

Haeran Ryu is a major champion. After years of elite LPGA Tour performance that always seemed to stop just short of the game's biggest prizes, Ryu delivered a commanding performance at Hazeltine National to win the KPMG Women's PGA Championship — the richest women's major in history — with a final score of -13 (275 total).

Her margin of victory was two shots over Ina Yoon, who played a strong final round but could not close the gap on a champion who showed no signs of nerves when the tournament was there to be won. Ryu's winner's check: $1.95 million from a record $13 million purse.

The Champion Who Was Ready

Ryu's victory was built over four rounds of consistent, pressure-proof golf at Hazeltine National — one of the most storied venues in American golf, a course that has hosted U.S. Opens, Ryder Cups, and major championships for decades. Winning a major at Hazeltine carries weight that transcends simple scoreboard numbers.

Brooke Henderson and Olivia Weber finished at T3 at -10, giving the final leaderboard a competitive spread that reflected genuine contest throughout. Henderson, a former major champion and one of the most popular players on Tour, played well but found herself unable to match Ryu's consistency through the weekend.

The performance that defines Ryu's win is its composure. She entered Sunday leading or close to the lead, and she played exactly the kind of golf that major champions play when the moment arrives: focused, mistake-free, and decisive when it counted.

Korda's Third Consecutive Major — Denied

The narrative shadow hanging over Sunday's play at Hazeltine was Nelly Korda's pursuit of history. The world's top-ranked women's player had already won the ANA Inspiration earlier this year and was the defending KPMG Women's PGA champion. A win Sunday would have been her third consecutive major — a feat that belongs to a very short list of players in the history of women's golf.

It did not happen. Korda shot a 73 on Sunday — a round that, by her own impossibly high standards, fell well short of what was required. She finished T8 at -6, six shots behind Ryu, unable to apply the kind of pressure that her game is fully capable of generating.

A final-round 73 in a major is not the kind of performance that defines a player's legacy. Korda remains one of the most dominant players in the game and will almost certainly win more majors. But the historic streak — three consecutive — ended at Hazeltine on a Sunday when she simply did not have her best stuff.

What This Win Means

For Ryu, the KPMG changes everything. First career majors are among the most significant events in a professional golfer's life — they validate years of work, establish a player's place in the sport's historical record, and change the conversation about what a career means and where it is headed.

The record $13 million purse signals that the KPMG Women's PGA continues to grow as one of the most prestigious events in women's golf. A $1.95 million winner's check is among the largest ever paid in women's major championship history, reflecting the sport's continued investment in the women's game.

What's Next

Ryu leaves Hazeltine as a major champion. The LPGA Tour's major schedule continues through the summer, with the U.S. Women's Open and AIG Women's British Open still on the horizon. Whether Ryu can build on this breakthrough or whether Korda responds with the kind of dominant performance that has characterized her major-season form will be the defining storyline of the women's game through the rest of 2026.

Today belongs to Haeran Ryu. She earned it.
