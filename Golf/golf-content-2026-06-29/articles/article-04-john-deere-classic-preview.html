John Deere Classic Preview: Gotterup the Favorite as Spieth and Fowler Join Silvis Field

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

June 29, 2026

The John Deere Classic has built a reputation as one of the most fan-friendly events on the PGA Tour calendar — a summer tournament that reliably produces birdie-laden leaderboards, tournament-shaping drama on the back nine at TPC Deere Run, and a crowd in Silvis, Illinois, that genuinely loves golf and the players who choose to compete there.

This year's edition runs July 2-5 with an $8.8 million purse and a field that includes eight players from the OWGR top-50. For an event that takes place in the pre-British Open window — when many of the game's biggest names are already focused on links preparation or taking time off — it is a quality lineup, and the competition for the winner's title will be meaningful.

The Favorite: Christopher Gotterup

The name to know this week is Christopher Gotterup. The World No. 13 arrives at TPC Deere Run as the co-favorite, and his trajectory over the past 18 months makes the ranking entirely credible. Gotterup has established himself as one of the tour's most powerful and accurate ball strikers — a combination that plays well on a course that rewards both length and precision from the tee.

A win at the John Deere Classic would be significant for Gotterup — not just for the career milestone of a PGA Tour victory, but for the confidence and world ranking boost heading into a summer that includes The Open Championship and the FedEx Cup playoff stretch. Players often use the pre-Open weeks to build form, and Gotterup appears to be peaking at the right time.

The Fan Favorites: Spieth and Fowler

Jordan Spieth has a meaningful history at the John Deere Classic. He won the tournament early in his career — the victory was one of the early signals that golf was watching a generational talent — and he has returned to Silvis on multiple occasions since, always drawing substantial crowds and competitive interest. Spieth's presence at TPC Deere Run is never incidental; he arrives to compete.

Rickie Fowler joins the field as one of the sport's most popular players, regardless of the competitive context. His distinctive style and accessible personality have built him a fanbase that transcends his tournament results, and at TPC Deere Run — where birdie-chasing is the game — Fowler's aggressive, attack-oriented approach can produce exactly the kind of scoring that challenges leaderboards at this venue.

Keegan Bradley also features in the field, adding another experienced name to a lineup that has competitive depth well beyond the headliners.

TPC Deere Run

The course suits a specific type of player: those who can move the ball off the tee and attack pins with confidence. TPC Deere Run is not the most demanding track on the Tour calendar, but that accessibility is what makes the birdie counts so impressive and the leaderboard so compelling. Scores in the low 20s under par are not unusual for tournament winners in good conditions, and the competition for those scoring positions tends to stay tight through Sunday.

The course sits along the Rock River in western Illinois, and the late-June/early-July weather window typically provides conditions favorable to low scoring. Expect the winner to finish somewhere in the range of -20 or below if the weather cooperates.

What's Next

The John Deere Classic begins Thursday, July 2, with the first two rounds determining the 36-hole cut. The weekend rounds conclude Sunday, July 5, putting the winner in an ideal position heading into the final week of preparation before The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale starts July 16.

For players who have not yet secured their place in the Birkdale field, this week also functions as a showcase event with world ranking implications. Every position in the top-50 matters when major exemptions and Ryder Cup qualifying points are on the line.

The John Deere Classic is good golf in a good setting. It starts Thursday.
