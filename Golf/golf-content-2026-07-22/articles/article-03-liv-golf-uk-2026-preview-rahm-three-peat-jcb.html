LIV Golf UK 2026: Jon Rahm Chases Third Straight JCB Title in a Turbulent Week for the Circuit

By Ryan Calloway

July 22, 2026

Jon Rahm won the LIV Golf UK event at JCB Golf & Country Club in Staffordshire, England in 2024. He won it again in 2025. On Thursday, he tees off at the same course for the third consecutive year, this time with a history-making opportunity in front of him.

Three straight wins at the same event at the same venue would be something no player has done in LIV Golf's short history. Rahm, the most decorated player on the circuit by recent international standards, is the man standing in position to do it.

The Field

LIV Golf UK (July 23-26) features a 57-player field drawn from 13 teams of four, with five wild-card additions. The roster represents the depth that LIV Golf has assembled at the high end of its player pool.

Cameron Smith is in the field. Tyrrell Hatton, the English crowd favorite at a venue within driving distance of major population centers, brings familiar energy to the draw. Bryson DeChambeau — who has not slowed down since his US Open victory at Shinnecock — is a presence. Anthony Kim continues his improbable continued involvement in professional golf at the elite level.

The course has been adjusted for 2026. The 6th hole has been converted to a par 5 at 509 yards, giving JCB Golf & Country Club a slightly different look entering the third year of LIV's relationship with the venue. A new par 5 changes pace-of-play dynamics and birdie opportunities late in rounds, which may affect how teams approach the back end of their rounds strategically.

The Week's Backdrop

LIV Golf UK proceeds against a notably difficult backdrop. Tuesday's announcement that the Asian Tour is joining a PGA Tour-DP World Tour alliance through 2029 — eliminating LIV's primary player development and promotion pathway — arrived the day before the event's opening rounds. LIV CEO Scott O'Neil was not informed before the deal was made public.

The circuit's financial situation adds a separate layer. LIV Golf has needed additional PIF disbursements to complete its 2026 calendar. The UK event and LIV's Bedminster stop in the United States have been reported as the events most likely to proceed; events later in August have been described as less certain.

For the players, the backdrop is what it is. Rahm at JCB is not thinking about the Asian Tour. He is thinking about how to win at a course where he has found something that works, on a layout he knows better than anyone in the field.

The Shotgun Format

Play begins Thursday with a shotgun start at 8:15 AM ET. Saturday maintains the same start time. Sunday's final round begins at 8:05 AM ET. The shotgun format — all players starting simultaneously at different holes — compresses the competitive experience into a shorter window than traditional stroke play and gives LIV events a different rhythm than PGA Tour or European Tour events.

At JCB, which sits on the grounds of JCB's global headquarters in Rocester, the format has worked well. The venue is visually distinctive, and the course design allows for good television throughout a compact day.

What's Next

LIV Golf UK runs July 23-26 at JCB Golf & Country Club in Staffordshire, England. The event is the 10th of LIV Golf's 2026 schedule. After UK and Bedminster, the circuit's calendar becomes less certain. For now, the focus is on Rahm, on whether he can make history at a venue he has already made his own, and on whether the competitive product can hold attention during a week when LIV's off-course situation is generating as many headlines as anything happening on the course.
