3M Open 2026 Preview: Scheffler Makes His TPC Twin Cities Debut as FedEx Cup Stretch Run Begins

By Jake Torres

July 22, 2026

Scottie Scheffler has played in nearly every event that matters in professional golf. He has won majors, dominated FedEx Cup standings, and collected trophies at some of the sport's most storied venues. This week he checks off one that has somehow stayed blank until now: his first start at the 3M Open.

The World No. 1 arrives at TPC Twin Cities in Blaine, Minnesota as the betting favorite at +275 for an $8.8 million event that carries more weight than usual. Three tournaments remain in the PGA Tour's regular season before the FedEx Cup Playoffs begin in Memphis. The 3M Open is the first of them. With 500 FedExCup points on offer, the week ahead is where the playoff picture begins to crystallize.

Scheffler's Unusual Momentum

The reigning World No. 1 carries unusual baggage into TPC Twin Cities. He missed the cut at The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale last week — his first missed cut in four years. It is the kind of result that would be unremarkable for any other player but stands out sharply against Scheffler's standard of consistency.

The result doesn't change his standing in the game. Scheffler leads the FedEx Cup standings with 3,823 points and has been the clearest No. 1 in professional golf for the better part of two seasons. But he has not won since early in 2026, and the missed cut at Birkdale is a reminder that no streak of consistency is permanent.

TPC Twin Cities offers an ideal opportunity to reset. The par-71 course stretches 7,431 yards on an Arnold Palmer design, favoring long and accurate drivers — a profile that fits Scheffler. He has never played the course, which in some ways removes any unfavorable prior history and gives him a fresh canvas.

Kitayama Defends

Kurt Kitayama defends the title he claimed at the 2025 3M Open. The win established Kitayama as a confirmed PGA Tour winner capable of going deep at top-level events. He returns to TPC Twin Cities as one of the more compelling returning champions in the field — a player with strong course knowledge and the confidence that comes from having already converted there.

Kitayama's ability to handle TPC Twin Cities' specific demands — precision off the tee, accurate approach play, and composure on greens that reward touch — gives him an edge entering the week that raw FedExCup ranking doesn't capture.

The Stakes Beyond the Trophy

For many players in the 144-man field, the 3M Open is not primarily about the trophy. It is about survival.

Jason Day, Brian Harman, Brandt Snedeker, and Tony Finau are among the recognizable names with FedEx Cup positions that require attention over the final three regular-season events. A strong week at TPC Twin Cities — the 500 points on offer rank it among the more valuable non-playoff events — can stabilize a season that has otherwise underdelivered.

Tom Kim sits 35th in the FedEx Cup standings on the strength of recent form. He enters the 3M Open week ranked No. 3 in power rankings and represents what is possible when a player catches a run at the right moment of the season.

Golf Channel coverage begins Thursday at 4pm ET. The weekend shifts to CBS. For the players who need points, every hour of that coverage represents an opportunity that is running short.

What's Next

The 3M Open runs July 23-26 at TPC Twin Cities in Blaine, Minnesota. After this event, two regular-season events remain before the FedEx Cup Playoffs begin at the FedEx St. Jude Championship in Memphis. Players who come out of Blaine with 500 points and momentum will be well-positioned for the postseason push. Players who miss the cut face an increasingly narrow window to save their year.

For Scheffler, the window is less about saving a season than about proving the Birkdale result was an outlier. At a course he has never played, with the FedEx Cup stretch run beginning, this week will tell us something about where he stands heading into the season's final chapter.
