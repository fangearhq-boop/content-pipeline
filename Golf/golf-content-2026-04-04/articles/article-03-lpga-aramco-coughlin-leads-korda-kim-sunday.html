Coughlin Leads Aramco Championship as Korda and Kim Chase in Final Round

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | April 4, 2026

The LPGA Aramco Championship at Shadow Creek Golf Course in Las Vegas is heading into its final round with a leaderboard that combines a potential breakthrough winner, the hottest player on the women's tour, and one of the game's established stars — all within striking distance of each other.

Per reports entering the final round, Lexi Coughlin leads the field at 8-under par. Hyo Joo Kim and Nelly Korda are tied at 2-under, six back but not in a position that eliminates them from contention at a course where low scoring has been possible throughout the week.

Coughlin's Breakout Opportunity

For Coughlin, Sunday represents the kind of final-round leadership position that defines careers. The American golfer has shown stretches of consistent ball-striking throughout her LPGA career, but a tournament win on a marquee course like Shadow Creek — one of the most distinctive venues on the women's tour schedule — would represent a meaningful step forward.

Shadow Creek presents unique challenges compared to most LPGA venues. The course, designed by Tom Fazio and built in the Nevada desert, features lush treelined fairways and fast greens that demand precise approach play and confident putting. Leading by six with a final round still to play is a substantial cushion, but golf has a way of compressing those numbers quickly under Sunday pressure.

Kim's Three-Win Streak Pursuit

Hyo Joo Kim enters Sunday having won back-to-back LPGA tournaments in March — victories at the Founders Cup and the Ford Championship that included two rounds of 61 in the same tournament. A third consecutive win would be one of the most remarkable individual stretches in recent LPGA history. Kim's ball-striking has been as precise as anyone's on the tour this year, and her ability to make low scores consistently means six shots isn't an insurmountable deficit.

The psychological dimension of Kim's hunt is significant. Winning a third consecutive tournament requires sustained focus and composure across a round where she knows the leader could make mistakes. Kim has demonstrated exactly that kind of patience throughout her recent run — playing within herself while maximizing scoring opportunities when they appear.

Korda's Road to the Chevron

Nelly Korda is positioned 6-under off the pace, and her storyline extends beyond this week. The first women's major championship of 2026 — the Chevron Championship at The Club at Carlton Woods in Houston — begins April 23. Korda's statistics through the opening weeks of the season have been exceptional, with season-best numbers in strokes gained tee-to-green that suggest her game is in ideal major-week shape. A Aramco victory before the Chevron would give her momentum and confidence entering the most important week of the early season.

Korda has been runner-up at the Aramco twice in recent seasons. Converting one of those runner-up positions into a win at Shadow Creek remains a personal priority for the former No. 1-ranked player in women's golf.

What's Next

Final round at Shadow Creek tees off Sunday. The Chevron Championship — first women's major of 2026 — begins April 23 in Houston. Watch how Coughlin handles the pressure of the lead in her final round as the key narrative of Sunday's action. If Kim or Korda close the gap early, the leaderboard could compress into a final-hole drama. Results will be available on LPGA.com and across the Golf Channel broadcast.
