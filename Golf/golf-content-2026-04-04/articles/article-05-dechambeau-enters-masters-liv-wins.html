DeChambeau Enters Masters on Back-to-Back LIV Wins and Augusta Ambition

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 4, 2026

Bryson DeChambeau will tee it up at Augusta National in five days carrying momentum that no other player in the field can match. The LIV Golf standout won back-to-back events in March — a playoff victory in Singapore and a playoff win over Jon Rahm in South Africa — bringing his total LIV title count to five. He enters the 2026 Masters at +1000 odds and with the kind of form that makes those odds feel closer to accurate than generous.

The Power-Game Advantage at Augusta

Augusta National rewards length in ways that most major championship venues don't. The par-5s — the 2nd, 8th, 13th, and 15th holes — are reachable in two shots for players who hit the ball the distances DeChambeau routinely produces. Converting those birdie and eagle opportunities is the difference between Masters contenders and the rest of the field. When DeChambeau is controlling his driver and taking advantage of his distance off the tee, he operates with an advantage that shorter hitters simply can't replicate.

The risk-reward calculus at Augusta also suits a player of DeChambeau's mindset. He has studied the course in detail and understands where aggressive plays create scoring opportunities versus where conservative management is required. His runner-up finish at the 2021 Masters — behind Hideki Matsuyama — demonstrated that he can navigate Augusta's demands across four competitive rounds.

Five LIV Titles and a Major On the Mind

DeChambeau's LIV Golf career has produced consistent results at the top of the leaderboard, but a major championship remains the measuring stick by which elite careers are ultimately assessed. His 2020 US Open title established him as a legitimate major champion. Adding the Masters would elevate his legacy significantly — and the Augusta course setup gives him as good a chance as any non-PGA Tour player in the field.

Eleven LIV Golf players have earned Masters invitations through world rankings and exemption categories. DeChambeau is the one arriving with the most current momentum. His back-to-back wins in March add a psychological dimension to his preparation: he has been in the winner's circle recently, in pressure situations, delivering when the round mattered.

What's Next

The 90th Masters begins Thursday April 9 at Augusta National Golf Club. DeChambeau enters at +1000 odds, behind Scottie Scheffler (+550) and Bryson DeChambeau (+1000), with defending champion Rory McIlroy at +1100. The Par 3 Contest is Wednesday April 8. Watch how DeChambeau manages the par-5s in Thursday's opening round as the early indicator of whether his current form translates to Augusta's specific demands.
