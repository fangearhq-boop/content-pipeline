Bhatia Leads Valero, Fowler Misses Cut — Last Masters Invite Up for Grabs Sunday

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 4, 2026

The 2026 Valero Texas Open at TPC San Antonio is heading into its final round with Akshay Bhatia at the top of a leaderboard that carries as much Masters consequence as any event on the PGA Tour schedule. Tony Finau is in contention for the automatic Augusta invitation that goes to the winner. And Rickie Fowler's Masters dreams for 2026 came to an end when his name appeared on the missed-cut list Friday.

Fowler shot a second-round 75, finishing at 1-over par for the tournament — well below the cut line. For the world No. 65, it was the end of his only realistic path to Augusta National in 2026. His ranking isn't high enough for an automatic exemption, and the Valero was the last event through which he could earn a late-season invitation by winning.

The Bhatia Surge

Bhatia's charge to the top of the leaderboard at TPC San Antonio represents one of the week's compelling positive storylines. The young American has shown the kind of controlled aggression that TPC San Antonio's layout rewards — a course where precise iron play to firm greens and confident putting separate contenders from the field in the later rounds.

Per reports through Saturday's play, Bhatia leads the field with a score approaching or exceeding 20-under par, with the final round still to come Sunday. The margin at the top is not insurmountable for the players close behind him, which sets up a Sunday that every bubble-watching golf fan will be following closely.

Finau's Masters Dream Still Alive

Tony Finau is among those keeping their Masters invitation hopes alive heading into Sunday. The per-reports-from-San-Antonio picture shows Finau within striking distance of the leaders — a position that gives him a genuine chance to earn the automatic Augusta National invitation with a strong final round. Finau has the power and the putting consistency to contend on this layout, and the emotional stakes of a potential Masters berth add an extra dimension to an already meaningful tournament round.

The Valero Texas Open champion, regardless of who wins, will receive an invitation to the 90th Masters Tournament, which begins just five days later in Augusta, Georgia. That context gives Sunday's final round a weight that most regular Tour events simply don't carry.

Fowler's Augusta Story Continues to Ache

Rickie Fowler's Augusta situation has become one of professional golf's recurring emotional narratives. The world No. 65 is a multiple Tour winner and has shown Augusta-contending golf in previous years, but his ranking has slipped enough to keep him on the outside of the automatic exemption categories. Without a win at an event like the Valero, there's no bridge between where his ranking sits and where it needs to be for an automatic invitation.

The result isn't a commentary on Fowler's talent. It's a commentary on the grinding difficulty of maintaining elite Tour form year over year — and on how quickly the window for specific tournament opportunities can close. Fowler will not be at Augusta National in 2026.

What's Next

Final round at TPC San Antonio tees off Sunday. The winner earns the last invitation to the 90th Masters. Watch the leaderboard throughout Sunday's afternoon — the golf will be meaningful, and the Masters implications make every shot count. Augusta National opens Thursday April 9 for Round 1.
