Rickie Fowler Misses the Masters Again — No Augusta in 2026

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 4, 2026

Rickie Fowler will not be at Augusta National in 2026. The world No. 65 missed the cut at the Valero Texas Open on Friday, shooting a second-round 75 to finish at 1-over par for the tournament — well below the cut line. With that result, his only remaining path to the 90th Masters Tournament closed.

Fowler's ranking isn't high enough for an automatic exemption into the Masters field. The Valero Texas Open was the last event before Augusta where a tournament victory would have earned him an invitation. Without the win — without even making the weekend — the Masters will proceed without one of professional golf's most recognizable faces.

The Narrowing Window

The frustration of Fowler's Masters situation isn't a talent story. He is a multiple PGA Tour winner, a player who has contended at Augusta National before, and someone who remains capable of competing at the highest level on his best days. The problem is the consistency that the world ranking system demands — sustained performance across full seasons that earns enough points to push a player into automatic exemption territory.

Fowler's ranking at No. 65 in the world reflects a stretch of results that have been good without being consistently great. The gap between where he sits and where he needs to be for automatic Masters qualification isn't enormous in golf terms, but it might as well be a gulf when the last available door is a tournament cut line he didn't clear.

A Career's Recurring Storyline

For golf fans who have followed Fowler's career, his Augusta absences have become one of the sport's more emotionally resonant recurring narratives. There was a time when Fowler seemed like a perennial Masters contender — the kind of player whose name you expected to see on the leaderboard Sunday afternoon. Those Augusta highlights haven't disappeared from memory. They make the current reality land harder.

The Masters without Fowler is a tournament that proceeds just fine — the field is deep enough and the storylines compelling enough that no single absence changes the competitive equation. But for a player who clearly still wants to be there, and for the fans who want to see him there, each missed invitation carries its own weight.

What's Next

Fowler will continue his PGA Tour schedule with events remaining in the 2026 season. His path back to Augusta National runs through the world ranking — improving his position enough over the coming months to qualify automatically for the 2027 Masters field. The Valero Texas Open final round tees off Sunday, with the winner earning the last invitation to Augusta. The 90th Masters begins Thursday April 9.
