### STORY 4: LIV Golf Updates

#### Long-Form Post — 3:30 PM ET
Two of the most powerful executives in professional golf have been talking.

LIV Golf CEO Scott O'Neil and PGA Tour CEO Brian Rolapp have held discussions, with O'Neil describing the exchange as an "opportunity to come together." Whether that leads to a merger, a framework agreement, or nothing at all remains unclear.

Meanwhile, LIV founder Greg Norman offered a very different perspective: the merger question "doesn't matter anymore," he said, arguing both tours have established their own independent foundations.

The practical news: LIV is switching from 54-hole to 72-hole tournament formats in a bid to qualify for Official World Golf Ranking recognition — the missing piece that has kept LIV players off the OWGR leaderboard since the tour launched.

Funding remains the core uncertainty. Saudi Arabia's PIF has invested more than $5 billion in LIV. The league needs $250-350 million in outside investment and recently cancelled its Louisiana event. The future of DeChambeau, Niemann, and other LIV stars beyond 2026 is an open question.

### STORY 5: Korda's Historic Chase

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET
To understand what Nelly Korda is attempting in 2026, you have to go back to 1950.

Babe Zaharias won three consecutive major championships that year — the Titleholders, the U.S. Women's Open, and the Western Open — becoming the first player in LPGA history to achieve the feat. It stood as one of the sport's great records for 63 years.

Then Inbee Park swept the Kraft Nabisco Championship, the LPGA Championship, and the U.S. Women's Open in 2013, joining Zaharias on the short list of players who had won three straight LPGA majors in a calendar year.

In 2026, Nelly Korda is trying to become the third.

She won the Chevron Championship. She won the U.S. Women's Open. She enters Round 3 of the KPMG Women's PGA at -6, six shots back of the lead. The record still stands. But it has never been under more pressure than it is right now.

The Hall of Fame math makes it even more loaded. Korda is at 25 of the 27 points required for LPGA Hall of Fame qualification. A major is worth 2 points. One more win doesn't just set a record — it opens a door.

**Caption:** Zaharias in 1950. Park in 2013. Can Korda do it in 2026? | Golf Fanrecap
