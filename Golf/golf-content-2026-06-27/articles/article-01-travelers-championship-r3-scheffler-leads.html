Travelers Championship R3: Scheffler Leads at -16 as Hovland Hunts Him Down

By Marcus Cole

June 27, 2026

Golf Fanrecap

Scottie Scheffler arrived at TPC River Highlands and made the Travelers Championship look like a week on the practice range. The world No. 1 fired a 60 in Round 2 — ten under par, eleven birdies, one bogey — to reach -16 and take a two-shot lead into Saturday's third round. Viktor Hovland matched him with a 61 of his own, and the two are now paired together for Round 3 in what has become the marquee matchup of a Signature Event that has suddenly gotten very interesting.

Scheffler's Round 2 Masterclass

Eleven birdies in a single round at a par-70 layout. That is the kind of number that earns a second look, and Scheffler delivered it with the methodical efficiency that has made him the most dominant player in professional golf over the past several seasons. His round of 60 tied or approached course records at TPC River Highlands, a track that rewards precise iron play and aggressive putting.

Scheffler entered the week at -6 after Round 1. By the time Round 2 was complete, he had opened a two-shot gap over his nearest rival. The margin is not comfortable — Hovland showed in his own round of 61 that low scoring is available — but it is real, and it is Scheffler's to defend across the weekend.

Hovland's Chase

Viktor Hovland has been one of the five best players in the world when healthy, and his Round 2 performance at the Travelers confirmed the form that has made him a consistent contender at the sport's biggest events. A 61 — nine under, virtually flawless — put him at -14 and into second place, two behind the leader.

The pairing of Scheffler and Hovland in Round 3 is the kind of third-round draw that separates a Signature Event from an ordinary stroke-play week. Two of the game's elite players, competing in the same group, over 18 holes that will determine who enters Sunday's final round with the advantage. The 2 PM ET tee time gives the rest of the field a chance to put pressure on before they go out — but it also means the leaderboard context will be live and visible when Scheffler and Hovland begin their round.

The Broader Picture

A Travelers Championship victory would be Scheffler's second PGA Tour title of 2026 and his latest punctuation mark on one of the most dominant stretches any player has put together in recent memory. He has won four of the last six major championships — the Masters in 2022 and 2024, the PGA Championship and Open Championship in 2025 — and his combination of ball-striking precision and putting consistency on Bermuda and Bentgrass surfaces has made him virtually unbeatable when in full form.

The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale is three weeks away, and Scheffler will arrive as the defending champion regardless of what happens this week. But form matters, and a Travelers wire-to-wire run from the front would deliver him to the links of Lancashire with momentum that even a British seaside wind might struggle to interrupt.

Wyndham Clark, the 2026 U.S. Open champion, is also in the Travelers field and had been positioned within striking distance after a Round 1 that featured a triple bogey on hole 12. Whether Clark is close enough to factor into a weekend charge depends on where the leaderboard sits as Round 3 develops.

What's Next

Round 3 is underway Saturday at TPC River Highlands. The final pairing of Scheffler and Hovland tees off at 2:00 PM ET. Sunday's final round will deliver a Travelers champion and $20 million in total prize money. With two of the world's best players separated by two shots entering a weekend at one of the PGA Tour's most scoring-friendly courses, the next 36 holes are worth watching closely.
