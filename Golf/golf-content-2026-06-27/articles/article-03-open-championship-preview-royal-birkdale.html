Open Championship Preview: Royal Birkdale Awaits as Scheffler Prepares to Defend

By Jake Torres

June 27, 2026

Golf Fanrecap

Three weeks from now, the best players in professional golf will gather on the Lancashire coast of England for the 154th Open Championship at Royal Birkdale. The field is beginning to take shape. Qualifying events have been determining the final spots. And Scottie Scheffler, the world No. 1 and the defending champion from the 2025 Open, is entering the major season's final act as the player everyone else is trying to beat.

Royal Birkdale: The Course

Royal Birkdale has hosted more Open Championships than almost any course in the rotation, and its reputation is built on a particular kind of demanding. Fairways cut between sand dunes on the Lancashire coast create a natural amphitheater for golf that simultaneously rewards precision and punishes mistakes. The rough at Royal Birkdale is not a suggestion — it is a penalty — and the links conditions that prevail in July mean that the wind direction and strength on any given day can make the same hole play like a different test entirely.

Previous Opens at Royal Birkdale have produced some of the most celebrated moments in the championship's history: Arnold Palmer's famous slash from the rough in 1961, Tom Watson's 1983 win, Mark O'Meara's 1998 triumph, Padraig Harrington's 2008 victory in one of the most dramatic finishes in recent Open history, and Henrik Stenson and Phil Mickelson's legendary final-round battle in 2017. The course does not produce ordinary major championships.

Scheffler's Major Résumé

Scottie Scheffler arrives at Royal Birkdale as the defending champion and the most accomplished major winner of his generation by the numbers. Four major titles in the last six opportunities — the Masters in 2022 and 2024, the PGA Championship and the Open Championship in 2025 — represent a level of consistency at major championships that the modern game has rarely seen.

The one gap in his résumé is the U.S. Open. Wyndham Clark won the 2026 U.S. Open at Shinnecock Hills in wire-to-wire fashion, and Scheffler did not claim the trophy. A career Grand Slam — all four major titles — remains unfinished business. Royal Birkdale is not where that particular story resolves, but it is the next chapter in the Scheffler major narrative.

His current form underlines the case for him as the overwhelming favorite at Royal Birkdale. At the Travelers Championship this week at TPC River Highlands, he is at -16 through two rounds after a Round 2 of 60 that featured eleven birdies. Whatever he is doing with his ball-striking and putting routine right now is working. Three weeks is enough time to carry that form to a links in England.

The Field Taking Shape

Open Championship qualifying has been winnowing the field across regional events, with the final spots being determined in the weeks leading up to July 16. The traditional exemption categories — past Open champions, major winners from the past five years, world ranking thresholds — populate the upper portion of the draw. The qualifiers bring the dreamers and the players for whom simply making the Open field is the achievement.

The full field will include the top players from both the PGA Tour and DP World Tour, LIV Golf participants who held world ranking exemptions when they made the switch, and international qualifiers from golf's every corner. Royal Birkdale is one of the few stages where the full width of professional golf converges under the same competitive umbrella, regardless of tour allegiances and business disputes.

What's Next

The 154th Open Championship runs from July 16-19, 2026, at Royal Birkdale Golf Club in Southport, England. Tee times and groupings will be announced in the weeks preceding the event. For now, the countdown runs through two more rounds of the Travelers Championship and four rounds of the KPMG Women's PGA — and whatever form Scheffler builds across those three weeks before the claret jug is on the line.
