KPMG Women's PGA R3: Korda Six Back With History Still in Reach

By Ryan Calloway

June 27, 2026

Golf Fanrecap

Ina Yoon leads the 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine National. Nelly Korda is six shots back. And the story entering Round 3 on Saturday is the same one it has been all week: can the world's most dominant women's player close a gap and claim a third consecutive major title in a single season?

Yoon holds the lead at -12 — built on a record-tying 63 (-9) in Round 1 that announced her presence at a championship where few had her name in the conversation before the opening tee shot. Korda sits at -6 after steadying from a difficult Round 1 that included a double bogey on hole 16 when her ball found the pond.

What Yoon's Lead Looks Like

Ina Yoon's Round 1 at Hazeltine was exceptional. A 63 to open a major championship is not a score that happens often — it tied the KPMG course record at the club — and it put her in a position that other contenders will need to chase across the weekend. At -12 entering the third round, Yoon is playing with the benefit of a cushion that normally reflects weeks of strong major championship golf, not one round and change.

The question for Yoon is whether her game holds under the pressure of leading a major. Course records and big numbers in round one have a way of flattening out across 72 holes when the championship conditions tighten and the stakes rise. Karis Davidson, an Australian contender who has been knocking on the door of major success, is also positioned within range of the lead.

What Korda Would Need to Do

Six shots in two rounds is challenging but not unreasonable. On a course like Hazeltine National, where scoring opportunities exist and a hot round can produce something in the low 60s, Korda would need a combination of her own best golf and some regression from the leaders to put herself in position entering Sunday's final round.

She has already won twice at major championships in 2026. The Chevron Championship and the U.S. Women's Open are on her résumé, and she has demonstrated the ability to navigate the pressure and conditions that separate major winners from contenders. A Round 3 in the mid-60s would put her firmly back in the conversation heading into Sunday.

The Stakes Beyond the Leaderboard

A KPMG victory would mean more than one more win. Only two players in LPGA history have won three consecutive majors in a single calendar year: Babe Zaharias in 1950 and Inbee Park in 2013. Korda entering that company would represent one of the more remarkable individual seasons in the history of women's professional golf.

There is also the Hall of Fame dimension. Korda currently has 25 of the 27 points required for LPGA Hall of Fame qualification. A major victory is worth 2 points. A KPMG win does not just add to the trophy cabinet — it opens the door to one of sport's most exclusive honors at an age when most players are still building their careers.

The KPMG Women's PGA Championship purse is $13 million — a record for the event — adding financial weight to the historical stakes already in play.

What's Next

Round 3 is underway Saturday at Hazeltine National. Korda needs to close the gap to single digits at minimum by the end of Saturday's round to give herself a realistic path on Sunday. Round 4 is scheduled for Sunday, June 28, with the championship result and a potential place in history to be decided across the final 18 holes.
