McIlroy's Champions Dinner Menu Revealed — Elk Sliders, Wagyu Filet, and Sticky Toffee Pudding

By Ryan Calloway April 7, 2026

The Dinner He's Waited His Whole Career to Host

On Tuesday evening at Augusta National, Rory McIlroy takes his seat at the head of one of golf's most exclusive tables. The Masters Champions Dinner — held annually on the Tuesday of Masters week since Ben Hogan introduced the tradition in 1952 — brings together every living Masters champion who is on the grounds. As the 2025 winner, McIlroy has the honor and responsibility of choosing the menu.

He chose wisely. The appetizers include bacon-wrapped dates, grilled elk sliders, rock shrimp tempura, and peach and ricotta flatbread. The first course is yellowfin tuna carpaccio. For the main event, guests select between wagyu filet mignon and seared salmon. And dessert is sticky toffee pudding — a choice that is unmistakably Irish and reflects McIlroy's roots as clearly as any of his Masters performances have.

The Tradition Behind the Table

The Champions Dinner carries a weight that goes far beyond the food. Every man who has put on the green jacket — from Jack Nicklaus to Tiger Woods, from Phil Mickelson to Adam Scott — earned their invitation through performances that are permanently embedded in golf history. Past winners are invited regardless of whether they are competing in that year's Masters. The dinner is not about rankings or current form. It is about belonging to a specific group of men who have done something at Augusta that very few ever will.

McIlroy joined that group in 2025, when he birdied the 18th hole to force a playoff with Justin Rose and then won the extra hole to claim his long-awaited green jacket and complete the career Grand Slam. It was arguably the most emotional moment in recent major championship history. Now, the dinner is his to host — and the menu is his personal statement on what that championship means to him.

A Menu That Reflects the Champion

Each past Champions Dinner has told a story about its host. Tiger Woods brought Thai cuisine to honor his heritage. Adam Scott chose Australian fare. McIlroy has created something that walks the line between sophisticated and personal — the elk sliders and rock shrimp tempura suggest someone who enjoys cooking and eating well, while the sticky toffee pudding is an unmistakable nod to home. You cannot grow up in Northern Ireland and not know sticky toffee pudding.

The wagyu filet mignon as the main choice — one of the finest cuts of beef prepared in the finest possible way — sets the tone for a dinner that honors the occasion while reflecting a champion who is more understated in his celebrations than some of his predecessors. This is McIlroy's table. Measured, thoughtful, and undeniably excellent.

What's Next

After tonight's dinner, McIlroy turns his full attention to defending his green jacket. The Masters begins Thursday, April 9. He will tee off paired with U.S. Amateur champion Mason Howell, per Augusta tradition. His back injury — which cost him more than three weeks of competitive golf — is reportedly no longer an issue. The Champions Dinner tonight is his last moment as the ceremonial host before he becomes the hunted again on Thursday morning.
