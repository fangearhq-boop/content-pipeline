Masters 2026 Is Two Days Away: Pairings, Schedule, Key Storylines

By Jake Torres April 7, 2026

The First Major of 2026 Is Almost Here

Augusta National Golf Club opens for competitive play on Thursday, April 9, and the 90th Masters Tournament gets underway with 91 players chasing the most iconic prize in golf. The field is wide open. The storylines are abundant. And the first two days of Masters week have already delivered — with practice rounds, press conferences, and the Champions Dinner on Tuesday night setting the stage for what promises to be one of the most compelling Masters in years.

Before Thursday arrives, there is still one full day of tradition ahead: the Par 3 Contest at Augusta's nine-hole Par 3 course takes place Wednesday, April 8, airing from 2-4 PM ET on ESPN and available on Masters.com. No winner of the Par 3 Contest has ever won the Masters that same week — a superstition that now spans 66 years.

Pairings and Key Groups

Defending champion Rory McIlroy will tee off alongside U.S. Amateur champion Mason Howell — the tradition at Augusta pairs the previous year's champion with the current Amateur winner. For McIlroy, defending the green jacket he won in dramatic fashion in 2025 (birdie on 18, playoff with Justin Rose, career Grand Slam complete), the pairing with a young amateur is both a responsibility and a reflection of what Augusta represents.

World No. 1 Scottie Scheffler enters as the betting favorite at +500. Two-time Masters champion Scheffler is seeking his third green jacket — a third title in five attempts that would put him in historically rare company. Jon Rahm (+950) and Bryson DeChambeau (+1000) lead the LIV Golf contingent, with DeChambeau arriving in particularly strong form after back-to-back wins in Singapore and South Africa.

How to Watch

The 2026 Masters marks the debut of Amazon Prime Video as a broadcast partner — a significant moment in the evolution of how golf is distributed to fans. Traditional coverage will also remain available. First round tee times begin Thursday morning, with the featured groups and Amen Corner coverage providing the primary viewing windows through the afternoon. Full details on streaming and coverage schedules are available at Masters.com.

What's Next

The Par 3 Contest on Wednesday is the final tradition before competitive play begins. After that, Augusta National becomes the stage for four days of championship golf. The Masters field is wide open — no single favorite is drawing disproportionate betting action, and the conditions at Augusta have historically punished those who enter with unrealistic expectations. Thursday morning, April 9, the most iconic major in the sport begins its 90th chapter.
