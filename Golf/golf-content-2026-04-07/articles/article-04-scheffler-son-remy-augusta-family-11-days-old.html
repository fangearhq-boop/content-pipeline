Scheffler Brings 11-Day-Old Son Remy to Augusta: 'My Wife Is a Trooper'

By Ryan Calloway April 7, 2026

A Remarkable Feat of Family Logistics

The pressures of arriving at Augusta National as the World No. 1 and the betting favorite to win the Masters are considerable. Scottie Scheffler has added one more layer: he is doing it with an 11-day-old son in tow. Scheffler's wife Meredith gave birth to their second boy, Remy, on March 27. Eleven days later, the family made the trip to Augusta.

"I'm really glad they're able to travel with me this week," Scheffler told reporters at his press conference. "My wife is a trooper, brought the boys this week." That's Scheffler in a sentence — sincere gratitude, understated delivery, and a quiet acknowledgment that the people around him have made sacrifices to be there.

A Family Pattern at the Majors

What makes Remy's presence at Augusta more remarkable is the context: this is not the first time a Scheffler newborn has attended a major championship. Their older son Bennett was born right before the 2024 PGA Championship — and attended that event as a newborn. Both of Scheffler's sons have been to a major championship in their first two weeks of life. Whatever Scheffler's family is doing, they are committed to doing it together.

For Meredith Scheffler, making the trip with an 11-day-old and a toddler to one of the most logistically complex golf weeks of the year is genuinely remarkable. The "trooper" description from her husband understates the case.

The World No. 1 at His Most Human

Scottie Scheffler has spent the last few years building one of the most dominant records in professional golf — two Masters titles, multiple PGA Tour Player of the Year awards, and the No. 1 world ranking by a comfortable margin. The tendency in sports is to project those accomplishments onto a larger-than-life personality. What makes Scheffler unusual is how fully he resists that.

He speaks about his family with the plainness of someone who genuinely considers them the most important part of his life. He skipped the Houston Open earlier this spring to be with Meredith for Remy's birth. The golf is important, but it is not the only thing. And somehow, that perspective — held by the best player in the world — has not made him any less competitive when Thursday arrives and the real work begins.

What's Next

Scheffler begins his Masters defense on Thursday, April 9 as the +500 favorite. A third green jacket would put him among the rarest company in Masters history — only Jack Nicklaus (six) and Tiger Woods (five) have won more. The Scheffler family will be watching. Remy, 11 days old, will experience his first major championship at the most storied venue in the sport. Some things you don't get to choose — but the Schefflers have clearly decided that togetherness comes before convenience.
