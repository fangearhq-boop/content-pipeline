### STORY 5: Par 3 Contest Tomorrow — Augusta's Most Beloved Tradition

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

Tomorrow is not the Masters. But for many fans, tomorrow is their favorite day of Masters week.

The Par 3 Contest at Augusta National takes place Wednesday, April 8 on the club's beautiful nine-hole Par 3 course. It airs from 2-4 PM ET on ESPN, and is also available on Masters.com and the Masters App from noon through 4 PM.

The contest is everything the main tournament is not. Scoreboards matter less than smiles. Players use their children as caddies, sometimes carrying the bag in tiny polo shirts while their dads hit iron shots to greens surrounded by azaleas. Spouses caddie. Grandchildren caddie. The formality of Augusta gives way to something warmer and more human.

And then there's the superstition. Since the Par 3 Contest began in 1960, no winner of the contest has ever gone on to win the Masters that same week. The record stands at 66 years and counting. Will anyone risk it this year? Some players specifically avoid winning to protect their chances on Thursday.

Tune in tomorrow. You won't regret it.
