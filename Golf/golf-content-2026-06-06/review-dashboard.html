Scheffler's Memorial Meltdown — World No. 1 Barely Survives the Cut
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 1:00 PM ET When the world's best golfer says he "felt like shooting 90" during a round at a signature PGA Tour event, you stop and pay attention. Scottie Scheffler arrived at the Memorial Tournament with a chance at a three-peat — he has won the last two editions at Muirfield Village. Instead, he spent the first two rounds battling himself as much as the golf course. He shanked a bunker shot at the fifth hole. He made three consecutive bogeys at one point in the round. He ranked 59th out of 72 players in strokes-gained approach. "That's maybe some of the worst I've hit it in a couple years out there," Scheffler said after Friday's round. He admitted he thought he might shoot around 90 — an extraordinary admission from a man who usually wins by controlling every aspect of his game. But then something shifted. Scheffler made three birdies in his final five holes. He made the cut. He extended his consecutive-cut streak to 75 — the longest on the PGA Tour. And when asked about it, he shrugged. The streak matters to him more than one bad round. He sits 10 shots off the lead heading into Saturday. A charge is possible, even probable, if the swing comes back. He was also spotted earlier this week scouting Shinnecock Hills ahead of the US Open, suggesting his focus extends well beyond Muirfield Village. ### Image Post Caption "Worst I've hit it in a couple years." JT Poston — Meet the Memorial's Unlikely Leader
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 3:30 PM ET J.T. Poston has been on the PGA Tour for a decade. He has three wins. He is widely considered one of the best putters in the world. And yet, if you had asked most casual golf fans who leads the Memorial Tournament heading into the weekend, you would have received blank stares. That's about to change. In the second round at Muirfield Village, Poston shot a 7-under 65 that contained eight birdies and zero bogeys in the most difficult scoring conditions of the week. The field average on Friday was four shots higher. Poston was nine shots better than that average — an extraordinary performance in wind and pressure. He won the Wyndham Championship in 2019, the John Deere Classic in 2022, and the Shriners Children's Open in 2024. His best major result is a T-5 at the PGA Championship. He has always been a quality player on Tour, but the Memorial would represent a significant step forward — a signature event win on a course that rewards precision above all else. Poston's putting is the story within the story. He has long ranked among the Tour's elite with the flatstick, and on a course like Muirfield Village — where the greens are notoriously challenging — his touch could prove decisive. ### Image Post Caption JT Poston leads the Memorial at -9. 3 PGA Tour wins. Nelly Korda at US Women's Open — History and Heartbreak at Riviera
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 6:00 PM ET Whenever Nelly Korda shows up at a major championship, she carries the weight of expectation — and she usually handles it. The US Women's Open at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California is the kind of major that tells you who the best player in the world is right now. The course is one of the most demanding in American golf, renowned for its strategic demands and uneven lies. It rewards complete, precise ball-striking — and Korda is as complete a player as the women's game has seen in recent years. Going into Saturday's third round, Korda is not at the top of the leaderboard. That distinction belongs to American Alison Lee and China's Ruoning Yin, who share the lead at 4-under par. But golf majors often reveal themselves in the final 36 holes, and Korda is close enough to make her presence felt. Jennifer Kupcho has also been in the mix all week, and the field at Riviera is bunched closely enough that a single low round could reshape the entire story. Round 3 is today, with the final round on Sunday, June 7. Coverage is on USA Network and Peacock. Watch for Korda — even when she's not at the top, she finds a way to be the story. ### Image Post Caption Nelly Korda. Riviera. US Women's Open. She's not leading — but she's always the story.
