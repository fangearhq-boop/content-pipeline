Lee and Yin Share US Women's Open Lead at -4 as Korda and Field Enter Weekend at Riviera

By Jake Torres | June 6, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The 2026 US Women's Open has a fascinating setup entering its pivotal third round, with two players sharing the lead at 4-under par at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California — and one of the most famous names in the sport lurking just behind them.

Alison Lee and Ruoning Yin are the co-leaders through 36 holes, tied at 4-under and separated by nothing but the anticipation of what Saturday's round will reveal. Both have played their best golf in conditions that Riviera makes consistently demanding, and both will need to continue performing to hold off a field packed with talent and motivation.

Two Leaders, Two Stories

Lee is an American playing what has the feel of a home game, given Riviera's Southern California setting and the tournament's prominent US Women's Open history. Her position at the top of the leaderboard after two rounds reflects consistency and shot-making under pressure — the qualities Riviera demands above all else.

Ruoning Yin represents the global dimension of the modern LPGA Tour. Chinese golfers have made an enormous impact on women's golf in recent years, and Yin's co-leadership at a major championship sends a clear message about the competitiveness of the international contingent at events like this one.

Jennifer Kupcho has also been in contention throughout the week, adding another layer of intrigue to a field that is bunched closely enough that Saturday's round could produce a completely different story at the top.

Korda's Weekend Opportunity

Nelly Korda did not arrive at Riviera as the tournament leader, but she arrived as the tournament's defining narrative. The biggest name in women's golf, Korda enters Saturday with the opportunity to make her move as co-leaders Lee and Yin begin feeling the pressure of maintaining their advantage.

Golf majors often bend toward the players with the most talent and the deepest experience in high-pressure situations. Korda qualifies on both counts, and the final 36 holes at Riviera will reveal whether she has found the form to challenge two players who have outperformed the field so far.

Riviera Country Club is a demanding major test, placing a premium on course management, iron precision, and the ability to score on greens that punish even the slightest misreads. The field heading into the weekend is tight enough that a single round of exceptional golf could create a new leader.

What's Next

Round 3 of the US Women's Open takes place today, Saturday June 6, with coverage on USA Network and Peacock. The final round is Sunday, June 7. The US Women's Open champion earns the most prestigious individual title in women's golf, and with Lee, Yin, Kupcho, and Korda all in the hunt, Sunday at Riviera promises to deliver.
