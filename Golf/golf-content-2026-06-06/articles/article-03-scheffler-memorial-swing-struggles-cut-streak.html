Scheffler Survives Memorial Cut With "Worst" Swing in Years — Streak Reaches 75

By Marcus Cole | June 6, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Scottie Scheffler is the world's No. 1 ranked golfer. He is the two-time defending Memorial Tournament champion. He has won more tournaments than any other player on the PGA Tour over the last two years. And on Friday at Muirfield Village, he genuinely worried he might shoot around 90.

"That's maybe some of the worst I've hit it in a couple years out there," Scheffler said after Friday's second round. He ranked 59th out of 72 players in strokes-gained approach — losing more than two shots to the field with his irons while everyone else was gaining ground. He shanked a bunker shot at the fifth hole. He made three consecutive bogeys. He sat at 4-over par through 10 holes and thought the cut line might be further away than his own play could bridge.

The Rescue Act

Then, as great players do, Scheffler found something. Not the full solution — his swing still wasn't where it needed to be — but enough. He made three birdies in his final five holes. His putting, which has long been a strength, compensated for the ball-striking deficiencies. He shot 1-over for the tournament through 36 holes and squeaked inside the cut line with the smallest of margins.

The consecutive-cut streak extends to 75 — the longest active streak on the PGA Tour. It is a mark of the consistency and mental resilience that define elite players: the ability to survive a bad week and still find their way to the weekend.

Scheffler sits 10 shots off J.T. Poston's lead heading into Saturday's round. A three-peat at the Memorial is technically still possible. But the math requires an historically unusual charge over two days on a course where Poston's putting could neutralize even the best ball-striking in the world.

The Bigger Picture: US Open Prep

What makes Scheffler's week at the Memorial more interesting is the context surrounding it. Earlier this week, he was spotted scouting Shinnecock Hills Golf Club — the site of the upcoming US Open — suggesting that his preparation for the year's second major is already underway while he is still playing at Muirfield Village.

It is a reminder that the Memorial, as prestigious as it is, occupies a place in the broader context of a major championship season. Scheffler has won the Masters and the US Open in recent seasons. A bad week at the Memorial, if it serves as a calibration tool for Shinnecock Hills, may ultimately prove useful even if it produces no hardware.

For now, though, he has two rounds to try to make something happen at Muirfield Village. He has done more with less before.

What's Next

Round 3 at the Memorial Tournament takes place today, Saturday June 6, on Golf Channel. Final round is Sunday June 7, CBS. The U.S. Open follows at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club — dates and coverage details to be confirmed. Scheffler will be looking to reassemble his swing before then.
