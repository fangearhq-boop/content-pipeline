Nine Shots Better Than Anyone: Meet JT Poston, the Memorial's Unlikely Leader

By Ryan Calloway | June 6, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The name J.T. Poston does not appear in conversations about the world's best golfers. He is not the player casual fans identify as a favorite. He does not generate the kind of pre-tournament buzz that surrounds Scottie Scheffler, Rory McIlroy, or the other marquee names who arrive at the Memorial Tournament each year.

And yet, heading into Saturday's third round at Muirfield Village Golf Club, it is Poston's name at the top of the leaderboard at 9-under par — the sole leader by one shot, after a Friday second round that was nine strokes better than the field average.

The Round That Put Him in Front

Poston's Round 2 was, by any statistical measure, extraordinary. He shot 7-under 65 with eight birdies and zero bogeys on a day when the conditions at Muirfield Village — wind, firmness, challenging pin positions — punished most of the field. The field average on Friday was four shots higher than his score. Nine shots better than average, in tournament golf, is the kind of number that defines memorable rounds.

That type of round doesn't happen by accident. Poston executed a game plan that kept him out of trouble while maximizing his scoring opportunities. His putting, which has been one of the consistent strengths of his PGA Tour career, was decisive — Muirfield Village's notoriously challenging greens are where good putters separate themselves from average ones.

A Career Built on Putting and Patience

Poston's three PGA Tour victories — the Wyndham Championship in 2019, the John Deere Classic in 2022, and the Shriners Children's Open in 2024 — tell a consistent story: a player who wins when his putting elevates his game from solid to dominant, on courses that reward precision over power.

He is not a spectacular ball-striker in the way Scheffler or Rory McIlroy are. He does not generate the kind of raw distance that has defined the modern era of Tour golf. What he does consistently well is control, management, and the ability to convert scoring opportunities when they appear — qualities that Muirfield Village rewards more generously than almost any other Tour venue.

His best major result was a T-5 at the PGA Championship, which showed he can compete on the biggest stages. A Memorial Tournament win would be the most significant of his career — a signature event on the legendary course that Jack Nicklaus co-designed and has called his favorite tournament.

What's Next

Round 3 of the Memorial Tournament takes place today, Saturday June 6, on Golf Channel. The final round is Sunday June 7, with later coverage on CBS. A win at the Memorial carries a two-year Tour exemption and entries into the year's remaining major championships, making it one of the more consequential wins on the signature-event circuit. Poston needs to hold his one-shot lead over Ryan Gerard and survive any charge from the field's marquee names to claim his biggest career title.
