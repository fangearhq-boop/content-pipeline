Nelly Korda at Riviera: Can Women's Golf's Biggest Name Chase Down the US Women's Open Leaders?

By Jake Torres | June 6, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

At any major championship, there are leaders and there are stories. At the 2026 US Women's Open at Riviera Country Club, Alison Lee and Ruoning Yin are the leaders through 36 holes, sitting at 4-under par. But the story — the presence that defines the conversation even from behind the leaderboard — is Nelly Korda.

Korda is women's golf's most recognizable figure, and Riviera is a course that demands excellence from every dimension of the game. It rewards players who manage their course strategically, strike their irons precisely, and handle the pressure of a major championship weekend without blinking. Korda has done all of those things at various points in her career, and Saturday's round at Riviera will tell us whether she can do them all at once.

The Co-Leaders and Their Challenge

Lee has been consistent and impressive through two rounds, navigating Riviera's demanding layout while keeping herself in contention at 4-under. As an American at a California course in a USGA event, she carries the weight of home expectations — and through two rounds, has handled it well.

Yin brings an international perspective and proven major experience. She is not a player who enters unfamiliar territory when major championship pressure arrives, and her co-leadership through 36 holes reflects a game that has been built for exactly these moments.

Jennifer Kupcho has been in the picture throughout the week, representing yet another contender capable of posting the low round that could change everything. The field behind the co-leaders is compressed tightly enough that a 65 or 66 from anyone in the top 10 could produce a new leader by Saturday evening.

What Korda Means for the Tournament

There is a reason Korda's name was identified as the top storyline heading into this tournament even before a single shot was struck. She is, by any reasonable assessment, the best women's golfer in the world over the last several years — a player who has combined physical talent with the kind of mental approach that produces major championship victories.

When great players are within striking distance at a major, they tend to find the hole. The final 36 holes at Riviera will test whether Korda can manufacture a third-round charge that puts her in position to win on Sunday. Riviera rewards patience, and patience is something Korda has demonstrated repeatedly in high-pressure situations.

The question is whether Lee and Yin can hold their form against a field beginning to understand that this tournament is there to be won.

What's Next

Round 3 of the US Women's Open takes place today, Saturday June 6, with coverage on USA Network and Peacock. The final round is Sunday June 7. The champion earns the US Women's Open title — the most prestigious individual award in women's golf — and the full field of contenders, from co-leaders Lee and Yin to Korda, Kupcho, and beyond, will begin their final charge starting today.
