JT Poston Leads Memorial at -9 as Scheffler and McIlroy Find Themselves 10 Back

By Ryan Calloway | June 6, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The Memorial Tournament's 2026 leaderboard entering Saturday's third round at Muirfield Village Golf Club does not look like anyone predicted. J.T. Poston is at the top at 9-under par, one shot clear of Ryan Gerard. Scottie Scheffler and Rory McIlroy, two of the world's best golfers, are 10 shots behind him.

Poston's second round was the story of the tournament through 36 holes. He navigated Muirfield Village's wind and demanding scoring conditions to shoot 7-under 65 — a round that was nine shots better than the field average on Friday. With eight birdies and zero bogeys, he moved into a lead that he now takes into Saturday as one of the most unexpected frontrunners in recent Memorial history.

The Defending Champions in Trouble

Scheffler, who has won the last two Memorial Tournaments and was attempting to become just the second player to win three consecutive editions, has been battling swing issues all week. He acknowledged on Friday that he was striking the ball as poorly as he had in years, admitting he feared he might miss the cut entirely. He survived — extending his consecutive-cut streak to 75, the longest on the PGA Tour — but sits at 1-over par, a full 10 shots off the pace.

McIlroy, the world No. 2, has been equally inconsistent. His second round included three bogeys and a double-bogey, and he finds himself in a similar position to Scheffler — technically in the tournament for the weekend, but requiring a historically unusual charge to contend for the title.

Both players have demonstrated the ability to go low at Muirfield Village in favorable conditions. A Saturday or Sunday round of 63 or 64 from either of them would change the conversation entirely. But 10 shots is a significant margin even in optimal scoring weather.

Poston's Lead and the Chasers

Ryan Gerard, at 8-under, is the player most likely to pressure Poston through the weekend. The field behind them includes several players within striking distance, and the compressed nature of Muirfield Village's scoring means that a single poor round could open the door for multiple challengers.

Poston's strengths — his putting in particular — suit the course's demanding greens. He has ranked among the Tour's elite putters throughout his career, and Muirfield Village's slick, undulating surfaces play directly into that skill set. If his long game holds up through the weekend, he will be difficult to dislodge.

The Memorial Tournament carries a $20 million purse and is one of the Tour's signature events, hosting most of the world's best players each year at a course co-designed by Jack Nicklaus. A win here carries significant prestige and a two-year Tour exemption for the champion.

What's Next

Round 3 of the Memorial Tournament takes place today, Saturday June 6, with coverage on Golf Channel. The final round is Sunday, June 7, with late coverage shifting to CBS. Following the Memorial, the U.S. Open takes place at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club — and Scheffler was reportedly scouting the course earlier this week, suggesting his week at Muirfield Village is part of broader US Open preparation.
