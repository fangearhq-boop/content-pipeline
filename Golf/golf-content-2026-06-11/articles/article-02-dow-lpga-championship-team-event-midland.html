Dow Championship Opens at Midland: Golf's Most Unique Team Event Returns

By Marcus Cole | June 11, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

There is no other week on the LPGA Tour calendar quite like this one.

The 2026 Dow Championship teed off Thursday at Midland Country Club in Midland, Michigan, bringing together 72 two-player teams in the only team event in women's professional golf. The tournament runs through Sunday with a total purse of $3.3 million — and a format that routinely produces some of the most dramatic and joyful moments of the professional season.

The Team Format

What makes the Dow Championship different is simple: instead of 72 individuals chasing a score, the event fields 72 partnerships competing in a best-ball format. Each hole, both players hit, and the team records whichever score is lower. When your partner makes a birdie while you're staring down a bogey putt, the hole is won. When neither player is at their best, the format provides a buffer that standard stroke play does not.

The result is golf that rewards aggressive play, encourages risk-taking, and creates an emotional dynamic unique to partnership competition. Teams that communicate well, cover each other's weaknesses, and take turns being the one who saves a hole tend to outperform teams made up of individual stars who have not gelled as a pair.

Why This Event Matters

The Dow Championship sits at an unusual intersection on the golf calendar — a week of major-adjacent significance for the LPGA, a local institution in Midland, and a genuine fan experience that draws crowds unlike any standard stroke-play tournament. The crowd at Midland Country Club engages with the format in a way that makes it feel like something between golf and theater: you know who to root for, and you know when a moment has changed the leaderboard.

The event has produced memorable champions throughout its history and consistently delivers competitive finishes. With a $3.3 million purse and full coverage on Golf Channel and Peacock across all four days, the Dow Championship is one of the better-watched events on the LPGA calendar.

What to Watch

In a best-ball team event, the key to following the leaderboard is understanding which teams have the depth to sustain. A pairing where both players are in their best form is dramatically more valuable than one where only one player is firing — the format rewards consistency across both teammates over a full four days.

Early rounds at the Dow often feature a wide-open leaderboard with multiple teams clustered at the top. It is the weekend when the field thins and the partnerships that have been quietly building separate themselves from the rest.

What's Next

Rounds 2 through 4 run Friday through Sunday at Midland Country Club, with the champion crowned Sunday afternoon. All rounds are on Golf Channel and Peacock. The final day typically produces the kind of head-to-head team drama that reminds casual fans why the Dow Championship is one of the most watchable events in women's golf.
