RBC Canadian Open: Matt Fitzpatrick Chases Historic Fourth Win of 2026 at TPC Toronto

By Jake Torres | June 11, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The most dominant player in professional golf this season did not take the week off. Matt Fitzpatrick arrived at TPC Toronto for the 2026 RBC Canadian Open, and with it, the chance to do something that very few players accomplish in any single season: win four times.

Round 1 of the $9.8 million event teed off Thursday at Osprey Valley's North Course in Caledon, Ontario, and Fitzpatrick brings with him a season that has already produced three victories — the Valspar Championship, the RBC Heritage, and the Zurich Classic alongside his brother Alex. He also finished runner-up at The Players Championship. Eight events into 2026, his name leads nearly every conversation about who owns this year on Tour.

The Open Field

Two of the world's three best players are not in Ontario this week. Scottie Scheffler, who is one week away from the US Open at Shinnecock Hills and his own historic opportunity, is not in the field. Rory McIlroy, the Masters champion, is also absent. Their absence removes two of the strongest names from the conversation but does not diminish the quality of the week.

Tommy Fleetwood, the tournament favorite at +1200, brings a focused confidence to this event. He won the FedExCup last August — his first PGA Tour title — and has spent 2026 quietly building toward a second. He finished runner-up at this tournament in 2023, losing to Nick Taylor in a four-hole playoff, and the memory of that outcome provides its own motivation.

Collin Morikawa and Justin Rose also headline a field that includes 14 players from inside the world's top 50. The absence of Scheffler and McIlroy does not mean the week lacks competitive weight — it means the path to the trophy is more open than it would otherwise be.

What Makes Fitzpatrick's Season Special

Three wins in eight events is not standard on the PGA Tour. It places Fitzpatrick among the small group of players who define a season's narrative — who makes others wonder what it would take to stop them.

His victories have come on different types of courses, in different conditions, and through different paths. The Valspar requires accuracy and patience. Heritage demands precision at Harbour Town. The Zurich added partnership to the equation. Through all of it, Fitzpatrick has been consistently excellent rather than occasionally spectacular — a distinction that tends to separate multi-win seasons from flukes.

A fourth win would move him into genuinely elite territory for 2026 production and place his name in discussions about the best individual seasons of the modern era.

TPC Toronto North Course

The North Course at TPC Toronto made its debut as a PGA Tour venue recently, and by most accounts it rewards the same qualities that Fitzpatrick embodies: precise iron play, accurate driving, and course management that avoids the longer par 4s that can unravel a round. Birdie opportunities exist, but they reward good positioning rather than raw power.

What's Next

Round 1 is underway Thursday, with Rounds 2-4 running Friday through Sunday. The winner collects $1.764 million and 500 FedExCup points. If it is Fitzpatrick, the conversation about the 2026 season — and his place in its history — takes on an entirely different register.
