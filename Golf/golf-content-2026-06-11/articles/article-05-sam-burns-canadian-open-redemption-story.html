Sam Burns Returns to TPC Toronto With Unfinished Business — and Three Years of Drought

By Marcus Cole | June 11, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Sam Burns remembers the final hole of last summer's Canadian Open. He remembers the playoff. He remembers Ryan Fox walking away with the trophy.

He is back at TPC Toronto this week, and the course remembers him too.

A Loss That Lingers

The 2025 RBC Canadian Open at Osprey Valley ended in a playoff between Burns and Fox, and Fox won. Burns had played some of his best golf of the season to get there — precise enough off the tee, hot enough with the irons, and calm enough on the greens to force extra holes against a player who had been building momentum all week. But it ended the wrong way, and every golfer who has lost a Tour event in a playoff carries a specific kind of memory that is different from losing in regulation.

That was last summer. Burns has not won a PGA Tour event since the 2023 WGC-Dell Match Play — a stretch now approaching three years without a victory despite consistent, high-level golf throughout.

Consistent Without Closing

The frustrating thing about Sam Burns' recent career is not that he has been bad. It is that he has been good enough to expect wins that have not arrived. He continues to contend on difficult courses and in big events. His ball-striking sits among the Tour's better performers. His putting, when it is on, gives him a genuine chance at any tournament he enters.

What has eluded him is the week where everything connects on Sunday — where the putter runs hot at the right moment, the tee ball stays in the fairway through a pressurized final round, and no one in the field plays better in those final four or five holes when it matters most. Tour wins require all of those things simultaneously, and for Burns, the most recent example of that combination was in March 2023.

The Course Fits His Game

TPC Toronto's North Course rewards the qualities Burns has. It demands precision into greens rather than raw distance — a course where the best iron players separate themselves from big hitters who cannot find the short stuff. Burns has routinely ranked among the Tour's stronger iron players, and his approach to course management on layouts like this one is calculated and disciplined.

He is listed among the contenders in the 2026 RBC Canadian Open field for reason. The Golf Digest power rankings, CBS Sports, and the major betting markets all acknowledge him as someone capable of winning this week. The question is not whether his game belongs at the top of this leaderboard — it does — but whether his moment is finally here.

What's Next

Burns is in the field through all four rounds at TPC Toronto, with the championship concluding Sunday, June 14. A win this week would answer a question that has been hanging over his season since 2023: whether he can convert his consistently strong play into a Tour victory. If the Canadian Open script calls for a redemption arc, Sam Burns is the obvious choice to star in it.
