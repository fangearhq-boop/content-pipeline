US Open One Week Away: Scheffler's Grand Slam Bid, Shinnecock Hills, and a Birthday for the Ages

By Jake Torres | June 11, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Scottie Scheffler turns 30 on June 21.

That date also happens to be the final round of the 126th United States Open Championship at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York. And if Scheffler is in contention on Sunday — as the betting favorite, he frequently is — he could spend his 30th birthday completing the career Grand Slam.

The convergence of circumstances is almost too perfect to believe.

The Grand Slam Within Reach

Scheffler's resume already includes the PGA Championship and The Open Championship. Add the US Open and he joins the most exclusive club in golf: players who have won all four major championships. The list is short. Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, Gary Player, Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, and a handful of others in over a century of the sport's four majors being contested. Scheffler would be the newest member.

He comes into Shinnecock as the consensus favorite at +567. His ball-striking, his course management, and his mental approach to major championships have been consistently elite. He arrives after a finish at The Memorial that he described as "pretty frustrating" — which, in Scheffler terms, means something was slightly off in his preparation cycle.

Shinnecock Hills: The Test

The US Open does not go to Shinnecock Hills for an easy week. The course is one of the most demanding in the world, and when the USGA sets it up for a major championship, the rough grows to five inches at the first cut and the wind from the Southampton coastline turns manageable approaches into penalty strokes.

In four modern-era US Opens at Shinnecock, the champion has shot under par for 72 holes only twice. Par is a legitimate score. A one-over-par total can win. The leaderboard stays compressed because everyone suffers equally — and the player who can limit damage while taking birdie chances when they appear tends to outlast the field.

That description fits Scheffler precisely. His game is built for grinding. Whether the course fits the moment is the question the next seven days will answer.

The Rest of the Field

Rory McIlroy — the Masters champion and one of the most complete players in the world — arrives as the second most likely winner. Jon Rahm, who has his own major pedigree and a game that thrives on difficult setups, is also among the top challengers. The US Open field typically consolidates around four or five legitimate contenders by Saturday's third round; which players those are will not be known until the week is underway.

What's Next

The 126th US Open begins Thursday, June 18 at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club. The first round tee times will be announced as the week approaches. Sunday's final round, June 21, could be the most watched day in golf this year — and for one player, potentially the most important day of his life.

It doesn't get much better than this.
