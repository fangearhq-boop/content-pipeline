Rahm's JCB Three-Peat Is Gone — But His Third LIV Title Is Still Within Reach, If the League Survives

By Jake Torres | July 24, 2026

Seven shots back heading into Championship Sunday, Jon Rahm cannot win the JCB individual title for a third straight year. His 2026 LIV Golf season championship is a different story — and so is the question of what he does next in a league whose future is uncertain.

Jon Rahm is not going to win this week at JCB Golf & Country Club. Joaquin Niemann has a 6-shot lead heading into Championship Sunday, and Rahm is among four players grouped at -7 — 7 shots back — with a single round remaining. The arithmetic for a Rahm JCB three-peat requires Niemann to fall apart on a course where Niemann has spent two rounds performing at a historically high level.

That story is effectively over. The more interesting story about Rahm at this event is the one that operates at a different scale entirely.

The Season Title That Is Still Alive

Rahm arrived at LIV Golf UK holding a 284-point lead at the top of the 2026 LIV Golf Individual Championship standings. He has won the season title in each of his two previous LIV seasons. He came to JCB on the verge of becoming a three-time LIV champion — regardless of this specific tournament's result.

The clinch scenario is specific. Rahm needs to win the tournament outright, and closest pursuer Bryson DeChambeau needs to finish in a three-way tie for second or worse. DeChambeau's Crushers GC currently sit at -14 in the team competition — in the mix, but not the same mix as the individual race against Niemann and Rahm. The 284-point cushion means Rahm does not need to win this event to maintain the lead into the final event. But a championship Sunday result that positions him near the top keeps the pressure on DeChambeau heading into the league's remaining tournaments.

In practice: Rahm's season title is likely his regardless of this weekend. A 284-point lead with limited events remaining is substantial. The clinch scenario is a best case. The working case is that Rahm enters the next LIV event as the clear points leader by an almost-uncatchable margin.

The Bigger Question

Reports this week describe Rahm taking a "cryptic tone on his future" — a phrase that, in the context of LIV Golf's current financial situation, carries more weight than it would have a year ago.

Saudi Arabia's PIF is exiting LIV Golf after 2026. The league needs $250 million in new investment to survive beyond the current season. The Asian Tour, which LIV funded with $300 million over four years, has just aligned with the PGA Tour. Rahm, who left the PGA Tour after 2023 in one of the highest-profile departures in the league's existence, is now pursuing his third consecutive season title in a league that may not exist when 2027 begins.

Whether his cryptic tone reflects active consideration of other options — a return to the PGA Tour, a wait-and-see posture, or something else — is not publicly clear. What is clear is that the question exists, and it exists in a way that it did not when Rahm won his first LIV Individual Championship.

What's Next

Championship Sunday at JCB runs on Saturday local time in England. Rahm's Legion XIII team trails Torque GC by 3 shots in the team competition — that race, at least, offers a meaningful collective result for him and his teammates. On the individual side, he will play for position, points, and whatever Championship Sunday holds. He is unlikely to win the tournament. He is very likely to complete his 2026 season as a three-time LIV champion. What he chooses to do after that is the question that follows him off the 18th green.
