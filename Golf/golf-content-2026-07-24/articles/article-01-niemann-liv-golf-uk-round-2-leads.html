Niemann Fires a 63 to Lead LIV Golf UK by Six — Championship Sunday Is Already His to Lose

By Ryan Calloway | July 24, 2026

Joaquin Niemann posted a course-record front nine and reached -14 to build a 6-shot lead heading into Championship Sunday at JCB. Torque GC leads the team race by 3 over Rahm's Legion XIII.

Joaquin Niemann was already leading LIV Golf UK by a comfortable margin before he reached the 10th tee. By the time he finished his Round 2 at JCB Golf & Country Club in Rocester, England, the tournament was effectively decided.

Niemann shot a 63 (-8) on Thursday, building on his Round 1 bogey-free 64 to reach -14 overall — 6 shots clear of Bubba Watson in second place at -8. The front nine alone told the story: Niemann posted a 30, described by LIV officials as the lowest front nine score ever recorded at the JCB venue across all LIV rounds played there. He did it in conditions that played harder than Round 1, with the field's scoring average improving by less than a shot on the day (-0.36 vs -1.27 in Round 1). What Niemann produced was genuinely exceptional.

The Leaderboard After Round 2

Watson sits alone in second at -8 — a position that, given the 6-shot margin, will require Niemann to collapse for it to mean anything on Championship Sunday. Jon Rahm, Paul Casey, Adrian Meronk, and Caleb Surratt are grouped together at -7, chasing a result that will require an extraordinary final round to reach Niemann's lead.

Niemann addressed his team's situation after the round. "We know we're due for a win," he said. "We know we can do it." He also acknowledged recent turbulence within the Torque GC lineup: "It's been a little weird the last two, three weeks. They've been obviously a few movements on my team." The team dynamics, whatever they are, have not affected the output. Torque GC leads Legion XIII by 3 shots in the team competition (-24 to -21). Crushers GC, led by Bryson DeChambeau, sits third at -14. Ripper GC and Fireballs GC are further back.

What Niemann's Round Means in Context

LIV Golf fields are 48-player, shotgun-start events that run differently from traditional stroke-play. The individual and team results run simultaneously, which means Championship Sunday at JCB carries two separate competitions — and both are still alive, even if one is much more open than the other.

The individual title is not completely locked, but the numbers are not favourable for any of Niemann's pursuers. A 6-shot deficit in one round of professional golf is not insurmountable in theory; it is nearly insurmountable in practice against a player who has shown the precision Niemann has through two rounds at JCB. He has yet to post a bogey in Round 1, and his Round 2 was historically good for this course.

The team race at 3 shots is a different matter. Legion XIII — featuring Rahm alongside Thomas McKibbin, Surratt, and Tyrrell Hatton — needs a collective effort to close the gap on Torque GC. DeChambeau's Crushers are 10 shots back in the team competition, likely out of that race.

What's Next

Championship Sunday at JCB runs on Saturday local time in England. The individual and team titles will be decided in the final round, with the added layer of LIV Golf UK's format allowing for competitive positioning throughout the round. Niemann will need to hold his composure from the front rather than hunt down a score — a different kind of pressure than the one that produced his 63 on Thursday. Whether the pursuers can generate the round needed to make Sunday genuinely contested is the question that remains. On current evidence, the burden of proof is almost entirely on them.
