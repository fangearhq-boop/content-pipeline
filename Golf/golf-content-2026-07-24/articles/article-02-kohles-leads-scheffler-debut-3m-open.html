Ben Kohles Leads the 3M Open at -9 — But It's Scheffler's Bogey-Free Debut That Has Everyone Watching

By Jake Torres | July 24, 2026

Kohles eagled 18 to post a 62 and lead the 3M Open field. Three shots back, Scottie Scheffler made his debut at TPC Twin Cities with a clean 65 and immediately positioned himself for the weekend.

Ben Kohles might be leading the 3M Open. But Scottie Scheffler is the story.

Kohles eagled the 18th hole at TPC Twin Cities in Blaine, Minnesota to sign for a 62 (-9) and lead Round 1 of the $8.8 million PGA Tour event by two shots. Emiliano Grillo, Jackson Koivun, and Gary Woodland are tied at -7. Scheffler, making his debut at the event after years of not appearing on the entry list, posted a bogey-free 65 to sit at -6 (T-5), three shots off the pace. Defending champion Kurt Kitayama posted a 68 and sits tied for 26th at -3, already six shots back.

Kohles and the 62

Kohles' round has the headline number. A 62 at TPC Twin Cities — Arnold Palmer's design on a par-71, 7,431-yard layout — is a controlled demolition of a course that regularly plays difficult enough to separate the field. The eagle on 18 was his exclamation mark. He holds the outright lead with the simplest possible equation: make a scoring move on a scoreable hole when it counts.

Whether Kohles has the game to sustain a 2-shot lead over a field that includes Scheffler, Grillo, and presumably a Kitayama who will be trying to work his way forward, is the question Round 2 begins to answer. Leading the 3M Open after Round 1 at -9 is a legitimate position. It is not, historically, a guarantee of anything.

Scheffler's Debut and What It Means

The world's top-ranked player skipping an event for years and then showing up to compete immediately in contention is the kind of storyline that generates audience attention beyond the specific tournament. Scheffler came to Minnesota after a frustrating week at The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale, where putting woes cost him strokes and position. He said after Round 1 that he "bounced back from putting woes at The Open" — and the scorecard supports the claim. A bogey-free 65 is not the round of someone still fighting the previous week's problems.

Scheffler has been the dominant force in men's golf for long enough that a debut at a mid-tier PGA Tour event — worth 500 FedExCup points on a field that may not include most of the world's best — is still appointment viewing. Three shots back with two rounds remaining is a manageable deficit. If Kohles retreats at all in Round 2, Scheffler will be positioned to take the lead.

Kitayama's situation is tougher. The defending champion is 6 shots back and competing against a field that did not include him on the leaderboard radar through Round 1. That can change quickly in a PGA Tour event, but he will need a strong Round 2 to get back within range before the weekend.

What's Next

Round 2 is underway on Friday, with TV coverage on Golf Channel beginning at 4:00 PM ET. The cut will be made after Round 2, with the 36-hole leaders playing the weekend. Scheffler and Kohles will be among the marquee pairings to watch. Given Scheffler's debut and the trajectory of his Round 1, any Round 2 that puts him within 1-2 shots of the lead going into the weekend sets up an interesting Saturday and Sunday. The 3M Open rarely draws the full star field, which means the world No. 1 competing through the weekend is the weekend's primary draw.
