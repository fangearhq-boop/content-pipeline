McCarty's 220-Foot Putting Exhibition Leads Truist Championship — McIlroy Seven Back After Flat Round 1

By Ryan Calloway | May 8, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Matt McCarty stepped to the first tee at Quail Hollow Club on Thursday with no expectation of what was coming. By the time he signed his scorecard, he had delivered one of the most breathtaking putting rounds in recent PGA Tour history — an 8-under 63 that included 220 feet of made putts and a first-round lead in the 2026 Truist Championship that no one predicted.

The performance was so complete, so improbable, that it nearly overshadowed the story everyone expected to be following this week: Rory McIlroy, back on tour after back-to-back Masters wins, seeking an unprecedented fifth title at his favorite venue.

McIlroy shot 1-under 70 and sits seven shots back.

A Putting Round for the Record Books

The numbers McCarty produced on the greens were the kind that make statisticians stop and look twice. He made a 52-foot birdie putt at the 13th hole, a 59-foot birdie at the 16th, and another 52-footer at the 17th. In total, he made 220 feet of putts during the round — the most by any player on the PGA Tour since the 2025 Scottish Open. He gained more than four strokes on the greens against a field of the world's best players.

Quail Hollow's greens are not typically yielding surfaces. They slope, they break, and they punish misreads. McCarty read every one correctly on Thursday, and the scorecard reflected it. His 63 tied the lowest round of his PGA Tour career and gave him a one-shot lead entering Friday's second round.

The Leaderboard Behind Him

Sungjae Im shot 7-under through 17 holes before weather forced a suspension of play, leaving him to complete his final hole Friday morning. If Im converts his finish, the two players will be separated by a single shot at the top.

Five players share third at 5-under: defending champion Sepp Straka, Kristoffer Reitan, Nicolai Højgaard, Harry Hall, and Nick Taylor. Cameron Young — who won the Cadillac Championship last week at Doral to establish himself as one of the hottest players on tour — and Tommy Fleetwood are both at 4-under, well within striking distance.

McIlroy's Quiet Return

The narrative heading into this week centered almost entirely on McIlroy. Back on the PGA Tour for the first time since completing his Masters defense, seeking what would be a record fifth Quail Hollow title, playing a course he has owned for nearly two decades. The expectation was a statement round.

Instead, McIlroy made 17 consecutive pars. He made just one birdie — at his final hole, the par-4 ninth — and finished at 1-under 70, seven shots behind McCarty. His ball-striking was not up to the standard he set in Augusta. He was not sharp enough to attack a course he typically dissects. Seven shots is a large deficit in a 72-hole event, and while McIlroy has made enormous charges before, it will require a round close to perfect in Friday's second round just to get back into contention.

What's Next

Round 2 at Quail Hollow begins Friday with Sungjae Im completing his final hole from Thursday morning. The cut is expected to fall around 3-under or 4-under, which means McIlroy at 1-under is not yet in danger of missing the cut — but he needs dramatically more from his game to stay in the tournament conversation.

For McCarty, the task Friday is maintaining composure while the stars of the game chase him down. He has never led at this stage in a Signature Event. That experience will be tested as names like Young, Fleetwood, and potentially Im make their move through the weekend. But after a putting round like Thursday's, it would be foolish to dismiss him as a pretender.
