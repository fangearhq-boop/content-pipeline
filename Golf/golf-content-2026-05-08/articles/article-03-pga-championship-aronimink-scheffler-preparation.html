Scheffler Skips Truist to Prepare for Aronimink — PGA Championship Field Set for Donald Ross Classic

By Marcus Cole | May 8, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

When the world No. 1 player skips a $20 million Signature Event to spend the week preparing for a major championship, that is not an absence — it is a statement.

Scottie Scheffler is not at Quail Hollow this week. He made that choice deliberately, choosing to begin his focused preparation for the 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania, which runs May 14-17. For a player who has finished second in three consecutive starts and is hunting his next win with increasing urgency, the decision reflects where his priorities lie this spring.

Aronimink: A Historic Test

Aronimink Golf Club hasn't hosted a major championship since 1962 — the last time it staged the PGA Championship, Gary Player walked away with the Wanamaker Trophy. In the six decades since, the Donald Ross-designed course in suburban Philadelphia has remained one of the most respected layouts in American golf without having had a major to prove it.

That changes next week. The course is in outstanding condition according to PGA of America's Kerry Haigh, who described the preparations as excellent ahead of the championship. What awaits the field is a test that demands precision above all else: Ross's elaborate bunkering punishes errant shots, three par-3s measuring over 210 yards put a premium on accurate long iron play, and the par-4s throughout the routing require consistent ball-striking that leaves the putter in a manageable position.

A Loaded Field

The 154-player field includes 29 major champions, 15 PGA Champions, and 20 PGA of America Golf Professionals. LIV Golf stars Bryson DeChambeau, Jon Rahm, and Dustin Johnson have all earned exemptions and are confirmed starters — a field dynamic that adds the kind of intrigue that comes when the game's fiercest rival circuits converge at a major.

Two spots remain open, held for the winners of the Myrtle Beach Classic and this week's Truist Championship. Rory McIlroy — who is currently in contention at Quail Hollow, seven shots back after Round 1 — will head to Aronimink directly from Charlotte. If McIlroy wins at Quail Hollow, he arrives at the PGA Championship on a momentum wave that would make him one of the more dangerous players in the field.

Scheffler's Next Opportunity

Scheffler's three consecutive runner-up finishes have not dimmed his status as the game's most complete player. He continues to be the best ball-striker in the world and brings a level of mental steadiness to major championships that gives him a structural advantage in fields of 154. Aronimink's precision-based layout theoretically suits his game — courses that demand accurate, controlled ball-striking from tee to green are where his statistical advantages compound most dramatically.

He has the week to walk Aronimink, study the par-3s, understand the subtleties of Ross's bunkering philosophy, and arrive on Monday of championship week with a complete picture of the course. That preparation, combined with his ability to peak under major pressure, makes him the player most others will be watching on the range and the leaderboard alike.

What's Next

Practice rounds at Aronimink run May 11-13, with championship rounds beginning Thursday, May 14. Scheffler will be joined by the full complement of PGA Tour stars, LIV Golf competitors, and PGA of America club professionals in what promises to be one of the most anticipated major championships in recent years — staged at a course most modern spectators have never seen host this level of competition.

After three runner-up finishes, Scheffler is not looking for moral victories. He's looking for the Wanamaker Trophy. And he's spent this week making sure he's ready for it.
