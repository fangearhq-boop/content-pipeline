Korda's 18th Career Win Places Her Among the All-Time Greats — Youngest American to Reach That Mark Since Nancy Lopez

By Ryan Calloway | May 8, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The comparisons keep escalating, and Nelly Korda keeps outpacing them.

Korda won the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba last week for her 18th LPGA career title, a four-shot victory at 17-under that came one week after she won the Chevron Championship — her third major and fourth win of 2026. At 27 years old, she is now the youngest American to reach 18 LPGA victories since Nancy Lopez did it at age 23 in 1980. The reference points for what Korda is doing right now have started borrowing from the most legendary names in women's golf history.

Numbers That Belong in a Different Era

Four wins in 2026. Through six LPGA starts this year, Korda has not finished outside the top two. That streak makes her only the second player in the modern era to accomplish that through the first six events of a season — the other was Annika Sorenstam in 2001, a season Sorenstam finished with 13 wins and a scoring average of 69.42.

Korda's Riviera Maya victory was emphatic. She shot 17-under total and won by four shots over Arpichaya Yubol, and the margin never felt particularly close even at the top of the leaderboard. Her ball-striking, her distance control in the Mexican humidity, and her putting on the Mayakoba greens combined to produce the kind of winning golf that looks effortless because it has been made efficient through years of work and development.

What the Career Record Means

Eighteen wins on the LPGA Tour is not a trivial number. Many players spend entire careers without approaching it. Some of the most respected players in the sport's history finished with fewer. For Korda to reach 18 at 27 — with potentially fifteen or more competitive years remaining — is to raise genuine questions about where her career will ultimately rank on the all-time list.

Kathy Whitworth holds the all-time LPGA record with 88 wins. Annika Sorenstam has 72. Nancy Lopez finished with 48. These are the names in the conversation now when people discuss Korda's ceiling, and she has not yet reached the point in her career where the body starts resisting the demands the schedule places on it.

What's Next

Korda's next scheduled start is the Queen City Championship in Cincinnati, which runs May 14-17. She will arrive as the heavy favorite, as she has been at every event she has entered this season, and the field will be built around the same question it has been answering all year: what would it take for someone to beat her?

The answer remains frustratingly elusive. Korda is playing a form of golf that her contemporaries are watching from a distance, simultaneously inspired by the standard she is setting and clear-eyed about how difficult it would be to replicate. For now, she continues to add to a record that only keeps looking more historically significant.
