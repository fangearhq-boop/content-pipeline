Herbert Shoots 64 to Lead LIV Golf Virginia — Rahm and DeChambeau Five Back After Opening Round

By Jake Torres | May 8, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Lucas Herbert had never led after any round in his LIV Golf career. Thursday at Trump National Golf Club in Virginia changed that in decisive fashion.

The 30-year-old Australian shot 8-under 64 to take a two-shot lead in Round 1 of Maaden LIV Golf Virginia, becoming the first-round leader in a tournament where the narrative was supposed to belong to the big names — Jon Rahm and Bryson DeChambeau, who entered tied atop the individual season standings. Both shot 3-under 69 and find themselves five behind Herbert heading into Friday's second round.

Herbert's Breakthrough Round

Herbert navigated Trump National with the kind of consistent aggression that the 54-hole LIV format rewards. His 64 gave him two shots of breathing room over a group that included Marc Leishman, Sergio Garcia, Tyrrell Hatton, and Sebastián Muñoz, all of whom shot 6-under 66. Ripper GC, Herbert's team, extended that individual advantage to the team competition as well — leading the team leaderboard by four shots after Round 1.

For a player who had spent much of his LIV career in the middle of the pack, Thursday represented a proof of concept: Herbert has the game to lead this field when everything clicks. Now the question is whether he can maintain that standard across two more rounds with the tour's best players giving chase.

Rahm and DeChambeau Off Their Best

Jon Rahm shot 3-under 69 in what was a competent but not compelling opening round. He made pars when he needed to avoid damage and generated enough birdies to stay within range, but the round never built the kind of momentum that his best golf produces. At five back in a 54-hole event, he has two rounds to make up ground — and Rahm at his best on a course that suits his length and creativity is capable of shooting anything.

Bryson DeChambeau was more encouraging given the circumstances. He was making his return to competition after missing the final round of LIV Golf Mexico with a wrist injury, and his 3-under 69 was a creditable showing for a player who was clearly working his way back into match condition. He made bogeys on two of his opening three holes, recovered without panicking, and produced enough birdies to keep himself in the conversation. If his wrist holds up through two more rounds and he finds his driving rhythm, he could be a factor over the weekend.

The Individual Title Race

Rahm and DeChambeau entered Virginia co-leading the 2026 LIV individual standings, with two wins apiece heading into this event. Neither is in prime position to add to that total after Round 1, but the tournament is far from over, and the 54-hole format means a single electric round can change everything. Herbert's lead is real — but in LIV Golf, two-shot leads have evaporated faster than this.

What's Next

Round 2 at Trump National Golf Club in Virginia begins Friday morning, with the final round scheduled for Sunday. Herbert will need to protect his lead against a field that includes some of the most aggressive scorers in professional golf. Rahm and DeChambeau, both five back, will need to produce rounds in the 8-under range to close the gap — the kind of scoring that is well within their capability on a course that yields birdies.

The individual standings race between Rahm and DeChambeau will be worth watching regardless of Herbert's position at the top. The 2026 LIV season has been defined by their back-and-forth, and how each responds to a slow start will shape the rest of the year.
