**Long-Form Post (10:30 AM ET)**

Rahm joined LIV Golf in January 2024. The DP World Tour suspended him, then opened a path for reinstatement. In the agreement that allowed his return to the Scottish Open, Rahm paid all outstanding fines accumulated since his LIV move and committed to playing a specified number of DP World Tour events in the remainder of 2026. The Ryder Cup implication is immediate. Under the membership terms, Rahm is now eligible to earn Race to Dubai points and, if he maintains good standing through 2026 and 2027, he is eligible for European Ryder Cup consideration. Rahm captained Europe to victory at the 2023 Ryder Cup. His presence in future European sides has been a question without an answer for two years.

Rahm is back. What happens next at the Scottish Open and beyond is the story to follow.

### STORY 3: LIV Golf $400M Crisis Deepens

**Long-Form Post (2:00 PM ET)**

The financial reality of LIV Golf is becoming difficult to ignore.

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — the entity that has bankrolled LIV Golf since its 2022 launch at a cost of more than $5 billion — announced in April 2026 that it would fund the league only through the remainder of this season. The language of the statement was direct: "The substantial investment required by LIV Golf over a longer term is no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy."

That was April. Now it is July, and the numbers are getting harder to manage. PIF committed approximately $600 million to fund the remaining 2026 events. It has delivered around $200 million — roughly $66 million in May and about $130 million in June. The $400 million that has not arrived is the reason LIV Golf Indianapolis and LIV Golf Team Championship Michigan are now described as events that may not happen. The UK event and Trump National Bedminster are expected to proceed. New Orleans was already postponed earlier this year.

Discussions with potential outside investors are not producing results. Prospective backers want clarity on what LIV Golf looks like in 2027 and which players would stay if PIF steps away. Those are questions that do not have answers yet.

LIV Golf's 2026 schedule may not finish. The league that spent four years trying to rewrite professional golf's economics is now in a position where it may not make it to the end of its own calendar.
