The PGA Tour POTY Race in 2026: Why Scheffler's Crown Is Being Challenged

By Ryan Calloway July 9, 2026

The Situation

Chris Gotterup won the John Deere Classic on July 6. It was his third PGA Tour victory of the 2026 season. He flew from TPC Deere Run to The Renaissance Club in North Berwick, Scotland, where he arrived as the defending Genesis Scottish Open champion, an event he won in 2025. Three wins and a title defense at a Rolex Series event is not a thin argument for PGA Tour Player of the Year.

Matt Fitzpatrick has also won three times in 2026. Wyndham Clark won the US Open and the Byron Nelson, giving him two wins including the most heavily weighted type of result in POTY voting. Scottie Scheffler is still ranked World No. 1 in the Official World Golf Ranking and won the Masters, but he enters the stretch run of the season with one fewer win than Gotterup and Fitzpatrick. The race is genuinely open in a way it has not been for several years.

Gotterup's Case

Three wins is a volume argument. Win counts are the most intuitive measure voters use, and Gotterup has matched or exceeded every other named candidate in that column. His wins have come on different courses and in different formats. He is 26 years old and is playing some of the most consistently competitive golf on the tour. The John Deere Classic, won on Sunday, gave him back-to-back tournament victories in quick succession before the Open Championship swing, and it sent him to Scotland as a player in form at an event where he has already demonstrated he can win.

A fourth win at the Scottish Open, at a Rolex Series event with global field strength, would make Gotterup very difficult to vote against. Four wins including a Rolex Series defense is a season that voters have rewarded historically.

Clark's Case

The US Open title is the argument. Wyndham Clark's victory at the country's most prestigious domestic major championship in 2026 carries a weight in POTY voting that a comparable number of regular tour victories does not replicate. The Byron Nelson victory adds volume. Voters who prioritize major championship performance over win totals have a clear home in Clark's column, and those voters have traditionally been a significant bloc in the annual POTY process.

Clark's case has a ceiling defined by his overall win count relative to Gotterup and Fitzpatrick. His floor is defined by the US Open. In most years, a US Open win is enough to be in the serious conversation. Whether it is enough to win the vote in a year with two players at three wins each is the open question.

Fitzpatrick's Case

Fitzpatrick has won three times in 2026 across multiple tours and formats, building a portfolio that has international texture. He has been one of the most consistent players in the world over the last four years, and 2026 represents one of his best campaigns by win count. The argument against him is that none of his three wins carry the specific weight that Clark's US Open does, and that Gotterup's combination of domestic wins plus a Rolex Series event gives Gotterup a broader resume at this specific moment in the season.

Scheffler's Position

Scheffler's POTY case rests on a foundation that the win column does not fully capture. He is World No. 1, a ranking sustained by consistent performance across the full season rather than a concentrated burst of wins. He won the Masters, adding the year's first major to his career collection. The argument his supporters make is that what Scheffler does between wins, in terms of top-five and top-ten finishes, tournament contention, and sustained quality of play, represents a kind of value that a pure win-count comparison undervalues.

What's Next

The Scottish Open runs through Sunday. The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale follows July 16-19. A win for any of the four primary candidates in either event shifts the calculation. A Gotterup win at the Scottish Open and an additional strong performance at Birkdale would make his season argument very difficult to argue against by the time POTY voting opens. Clark needs to stay in contention at The Open to keep his US Open weight from becoming a single-event story. The final chapter of the 2026 POTY campaign is just beginning.
