12 Players, One Spot: Inside the Open Championship's Inaugural Last-Chance Qualifier

By Marcus Cole July 9, 2026

The Format

On the morning of Monday, July 13, twelve golfers will play eighteen holes at Royal Birkdale Golf Club in Southport, England. The course they play on Monday is the same course hosting the 154th Open Championship beginning three days later. One of those twelve players will be in the field when the world's best golfers tee off on Thursday. The other eleven will not.

The Open Championship Last-Chance Qualifier is a new format, introduced for the first time in the championship's history. The R&A created it to offer one final path into the field for players who did not secure entry through any of the existing qualifying routes — Final Qualifying, exemption lists, world ranking thresholds, or major championship performance. Twelve players. One round. One ticket.

The Players to Watch

Aldrich Potgieter is the player the rankings say should win. Ranked World No. 77, the 21-year-old South African won The Amateur Championship at age 17 in 2022, has competed in The Open Championship on two previous occasions, and has a PGA Tour victory to his name. He plays on the LIV Golf roster and earned his place in the LCQ field through the Official World Golf Rankings as the highest-ranked player in the field not otherwise exempt at the qualifying cutoff. If the twelve players played 10,000 rounds of golf at Royal Birkdale in the abstract, Potgieter wins the most of them. Eighteen holes on one specific Monday is a different calculation.

Wesley Bryan is the player the internet knows best. Bryan and his brother George built one of the most-followed YouTube channels in golf, and Bryan has maintained an active competitive career alongside the content creation. He won on the PGA Tour, demonstrating that his game is genuine and not simply a product of social media mechanics. This week, he came as close as one shot to making it through Final Qualifying at West Lancashire, bogeying his final hole to fall short of a playoff by a single stroke. His path to Royal Birkdale for The Open went from essentially finished to alive again in the space of the LCQ announcement. If Bryan qualifies, his audience will descend on the Southport coast.

Matti Schmid tied for fourth at the 2026 PGA Championship at Quail Hollow in May. That is the kind of result that makes observers take notice: a player who was in contention at a major championship two months ago, competing for one of the year's biggest prizes. He is not a name that most casual golf fans carry in their mental roster, but his PGA Championship performance is a credible piece of evidence about his ceiling at major venues.

The Rest of the Field

The twelve-player LCQ field includes Adri Arnaus of Spain and Charlie Huntzinger of the United States, both of whom missed the West Lancashire playoff at Final Qualifying. The remaining seven players are drawn from the DP World Tour and Challenge Tour memberships. The format does not guarantee that the best-known name wins. Eighteen holes is a small enough sample that a player who is fifth or sixth on the betting sheet can win by shooting a round better than his competition on that specific day.

What Winning the LCQ Means

The player who wins the LCQ on Monday has three days before the first round of The Open Championship begins at the same venue. They will play practice rounds on a course they just played competitively. They will be preparing for a major championship field that includes Rory McIlroy, Scottie Scheffler, Jon Rahm, and the rest of the world's top players. The logistical compression of qualifying Monday and teeing up Thursday is part of the LCQ's drama — there is no comfortable gap between earning the spot and having to use it.

The Open Championship LCQ begins at 7:30 a.m. BST on Monday, July 13, at Royal Birkdale. The first round of the 154th Open begins July 16.
