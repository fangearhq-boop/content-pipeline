Korda Opens +3 at Evian: Career Grand Slam Chase in Serious Trouble After Round 1

By Marcus Cole July 9, 2026

Iwai's 63 Sets the Pace

Aki Iwai fired an 8-under 63 at the Amundi Evian Championship on Thursday to take the solo lead after Round 1, holding a three-shot advantage over Haeran Ryu at -5 and four over Lottie Woad at -4. Iwai, 24, ranked World No. 24, owns one LPGA title and arrived at Evian Resort Golf Club in France without the weight of narrative that surrounded most of the marquee names in the field. She played the course as if the weight did not exist.

Round 1 at an LPGA major played in the French Alps can produce conditions that make scoring difficult. Iwai found a way to subtract eight from par in eighteen holes. That kind of number earns a two or three shot cushion at most events. At the Evian, it produced a three-shot lead on a day when much of the conversation centered on someone eleven shots behind her.

Korda's Round

Nelly Korda finished her Round 1 at +3 (74), standing eleven shots off Iwai's lead. It was not the start she needed. Korda, who won the Chevron Championship and the US Women's Open in 2026, arrived at Evian as the odds-on favorite to win the tournament and move another step closer to completing the career Grand Slam. She needed a win here. The margin after eighteen holes makes that need considerably more complicated.

Korda started on the 10th hole and birdied the par-4 12th to move into red figures. The round came apart from there. A bogey on 18 erased her only gain. A double bogey on the 1st hole in the turn to the front nine pushed her into positive numbers. Further bogeys on the 4th and 5th extended the damage before a birdie on the par-5 9th closed her round at +3. In the context of what was happening at the top of the leaderboard, three over par was a result that left her with a very steep road ahead.

The Grand Slam Context

Korda was pursuing a calendar Grand Slam — winning all five LPGA major championships in a single season — a feat that has never been accomplished. She won the Chevron Championship, the first major of the 2026 calendar. She won the US Women's Open, the second. When Haeran Ryu claimed the KPMG Women's PGA Championship, that specific sequence died. The calendar Grand Slam was no longer possible.

But the career Grand Slam was still alive and still achievable at Evian. Korda has not previously won this tournament. A victory in France this week, followed by a win at the AIG Women's British Open, would complete a career collection of all five major titles. That goal required a competitive first round. At +3, eleven shots off the pace after eighteen holes, the path requires a sequence of rounds that almost no player in history has assembled at a major.

Ryu at -5

Haeran Ryu's position at -5 after Round 1 carries its own weight in the storyline. Ryu won the KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine National this year. That victory was the result that ended Korda's calendar Grand Slam pursuit. Now Ryu is three shots off the lead at the tournament where Korda needed to respond. The proximity of the two players on the leaderboard, with Ryu in a position to win and Korda trying to come from behind, is the kind of dynamic that a weekly tournament schedule rarely produces.

What's Next

Iwai's -8 after Round 1 is a number that can hold or disappear depending on how the course and conditions play over three more rounds at altitude in the Alps. Leads of this size after eighteen holes at LPGA majors have not always survived. The question is whether any of the contenders can apply consistent pressure.

For Korda, Friday's round is a near-elimination situation if she wants to keep the career Grand Slam as a realistic objective for this week. A second round that does not move her dramatically up the leaderboard effectively closes the conversation at Evian and shifts attention to August's AIG Women's British Open as the next opportunity. Round 2 begins Friday morning in France.
