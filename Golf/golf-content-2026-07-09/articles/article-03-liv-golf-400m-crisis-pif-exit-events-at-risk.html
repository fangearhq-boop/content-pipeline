LIV Golf's $400M Problem: PIF Is Leaving and Two Events Are at Risk

By Jake Torres July 9, 2026

The April Statement

In April 2026, Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund issued a statement that framed everything that has followed. "PIF has made the decision to fund LIV Golf only for the remainder of the 2026 season," the statement read. "The substantial investment required by LIV Golf over a longer term is no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy."

That language is unambiguous. PIF, which has been LIV Golf's sole significant funder since the league's 2022 launch, is not committing to the operation beyond this calendar year. The question of what LIV Golf looks like in 2027 without PIF support is a question that currently has no structural answer. The question of whether LIV Golf makes it to the end of its 2026 schedule is now a more immediate concern.

The Funding Gap

PIF committed approximately $600 million to fund the remainder of LIV Golf's 2026 events after that April statement. As of July 9, it has delivered roughly $200 million of that commitment: approximately $66 million in May and approximately $130 million in June. The $400 million that has not arrived is the number that is now determining which events on the remaining 2026 schedule can operate.

LIV Golf CEO Scott O'Neil has publicly acknowledged that he cannot guarantee all remaining events will take place. The UK event and Trump National Bedminster in New Jersey are expected to proceed. LIV Golf Indianapolis and LIV Golf Team Championship Michigan are at financial risk. New Orleans was already postponed earlier in the year before this specific funding conversation reached its current acuity.

Why Outside Investment Has Not Arrived

LIV Golf has pursued conversations with potential outside investors. Those discussions are stalling. The reason most commonly cited by observers close to the process is that prospective backers want clarity that does not yet exist. They want to know what LIV Golf's structure looks like in 2027 when PIF exits. They want to know which players will remain committed to a league that is no longer backed by Saudi sovereign wealth. Without those answers, making a substantial investment in LIV Golf's continued operation requires accepting a level of uncertainty that institutional investors have been unwilling to absorb.

PIF's total spending on LIV Golf since the 2022 launch is estimated at more than $5 billion. The cumulative liabilities associated with player contracts, operational costs, and event production may exceed $3 billion without continued PIF support. The arithmetic of an outside investor stepping into that picture without clarity on the long-term structure is difficult to make work.

What This Means for Players

The players on LIV Golf's roster signed contracts with a league that was funded by one of the largest sovereign wealth funds in the world. The competitive structure they joined, for all of its controversy, had the financial certainty of PIF backing. That certainty is now conditional on a single season's completion. For players who signed long-term deals with LIV Golf expecting stability, the April statement and the subsequent funding timeline create a set of questions that are not answered by any currently public information.

Jon Rahm's return to co-sanctioned golf at the Scottish Open this week, through his DP World Tour agreement, provides one data point about how a LIV player might navigate toward the broader circuit. Whether that specific path generalizes to others depends on individual fine structures, contractual obligations to LIV Golf, and the DP World Tour's willingness to extend similar accommodations across multiple players simultaneously.

What's Next

The funded events — UK and Bedminster — are expected to proceed. The unfunded events — Indianapolis and Michigan — are the variables. LIV Golf needs the remaining $400 million from PIF to arrive, or needs an alternative source of funding to materialize, before those events can be confirmed. The timeline for when that clarity arrives is not publicly established. Watch for statements from LIV Golf's leadership and from PIF regarding the outstanding commitment in the coming weeks.
