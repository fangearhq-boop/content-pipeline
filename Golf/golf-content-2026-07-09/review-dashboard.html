NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Jon Rahm teed it up at the Genesis Scottish Open on Thursday morning. For any other player in the field, that sentence would be unremarkable. For Rahm, it is the end of a two-year absence from co-sanctioned golf and the beginning of something that could reshape his standing in the sport. Rahm joined LIV Golf in January 2024. The DP World Tour suspended him, then opened a path for reinstatement. In the agreement that allowed his return to the Scottish Open, Rahm paid all outstanding fines accumulated since his LIV move and committed to playing a specified number of DP World Tour events in the remainder of 2026. This week counts as his fifth event under that arrangement, officially designating him a member in good standing. The Ryder Cup implication is immediate. Under the membership terms, Rahm is now eligible to earn Race to Dubai points and, if he maintains good standing through 2026 and 2027, he is eligible for European Ryder Cup consideration. Rahm captained Europe to victory at the 2023 Ryder Cup. His presence in future European sides has been a question without an answer for two years. He called the Scottish Open "one of my favourite weeks." His tee time Thursday was 9:05 a.m. ET, while McIlroy, Gotterup, and MacIntyre — the group everyone is watching — went out earlier at 3:28 a.m. ET. Rahm is back. What happens next at the Scottish Open and beyond is the story to follow. ### Image Post Caption Jon Rahm teed off at the Scottish Open today — his first co-sanctioned PGA Tour/DP World Tour start since LIV. LIV Golf $400M Crisis Deepens — PIF Preparing to Exit, Two Events at Risk
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The financial reality of LIV Golf is becoming difficult to ignore. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — the entity that has bankrolled LIV Golf since its 2022 launch at a cost of more than $5 billion — announced in April 2026 that it would fund the league only through the remainder of this season. The language of the statement was direct: "The substantial investment required by LIV Golf over a longer term is no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy." That was April. Now it is July, and the numbers are getting harder to manage. PIF committed approximately $600 million to fund the remaining 2026 events. It has delivered around $200 million — roughly $66 million in May and about $130 million in June. The $400 million that has not arrived is the reason LIV Golf Indianapolis and LIV Golf Team Championship Michigan are now described as events that may not happen. The UK event and Trump National Bedminster are expected to proceed. New Orleans was already postponed earlier this year. Discussions with potential outside investors are not producing results. Prospective backers want clarity on what LIV Golf looks like in 2027 and which players would stay if PIF steps away. Those are questions that do not have answers yet. LIV Golf's 2026 schedule may not finish. The league that spent four years trying to rewrite professional golf's economics is now in a position where it may not make it to the end of its own calendar. ### Image Post Caption PIF committed $600M for the rest of 2026. Delivered roughly $200M. The $400M gap has two LIV events at risk. PIF announced in April it ends support after this season.

2026 PGA Tour Player of the Year Race — Gotterup Makes His Move at Scottish Open
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post There has not been a genuine multi-player PGA Tour Player of the Year race in several years. 2026 is different. Chris Gotterup arrived at the Genesis Scottish Open on the back of his third PGA Tour win of the season — the John Deere Classic, completed July 6. He is the defending Scottish Open champion. He is ranked World No. 4. Three wins in a calendar year, on a variety of courses and in different formats, is a substantial argument. Matt Fitzpatrick also has three wins in 2026. He has been consistent across the globe and has built a case that mirrors Gotterup's in win total while bringing a wider international portfolio of events. Wyndham Clark won the US Open. A major title weighs heavily in POTY voting — often more heavily than an equivalent number of regular wins. Clark's US Open triumph, combined with his Byron Nelson victory, gives him a credible two-item portfolio with one of those items being the highest-weighted possible result. Then there is Scottie Scheffler. World No. 1 in the Official World Golf Ranking. One PGA Tour win in 2026 — the Masters — but the argument that sustained dominance across the full season matters as much as wins. POTY voters have given weight to that argument before. The Scottish Open is the last meaningful tournament before The Open Championship. Four players with genuine cases. Four chances to add to the argument. The vote does not get cast until the season ends, but the campaign starts now. ### Image Post Caption Gotterup: 3 wins. Fitzpatrick: 3 wins. Clark: US Open. Scheffler: World No. 1.
