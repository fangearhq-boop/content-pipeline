Scottish Open Field Preview: The Final Links Tune-Up Before Royal Birkdale Starts Thursday

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 7, 2026

The Genesis Scottish Open has transformed over the past decade from a respected but mid-tier European Tour event into something more important: the final, essential dress rehearsal before The Open Championship. When the field gathers Thursday at The Renaissance Club in North Berwick, Scotland, the $9 million tournament they're competing for is almost secondary to what it represents — a chance to get Scottish links golf in the body before Royal Birkdale calls the following week.

Renaissance Club plays differently from Royal Birkdale in specifics — the course design, the rough patterns, the wind direction variations. But the fundamental game required is nearly identical: manage the ball flight in coastal wind, find fairways with a premium on placement over power, execute shots that bounce and run in ways that mainland golf never demands. Players who play well at Renaissance Club on Sunday tend to show up at the major in form.

Who to Watch

Scottie Scheffler, the world No. 1 and defending Open champion, will be among the most closely watched players in the field. His links game has been genuinely impressive over the past two seasons, and Renaissance Club will offer a useful data point on whether he's arriving at Birkdale truly sharp or just ranked-sharp. Rory McIlroy, the world No. 2, is perhaps even more motivated — McIlroy has not won a major since 2014 and approaches every summer links season as his best opportunity to correct that.

Chris Gotterup, the defending Scottish Open champion and current world No. 9, presents a fascinating storyline. He won this tournament last year and has since added two more wins in 2026. The question is whether a player who had never won a major can sustain the kind of performance level he's producing across the season's most important stretch. His presence at Renaissance Club with three wins already in the bank changes the psychological dynamic considerably from a year ago.

European players also deserve attention at a tournament that suits their links background. Tommy Fleetwood (world No. 7) has lived near links courses his entire career and plays Scotland golf with the fluency that takes American players years to develop. Matthew Fitzpatrick (No. 5) and Justin Rose (No. 4) are both players who have shown major-week composure when the environment demands it.

The LIV Exemption Wildcards

Several LIV Golf players who have earned Open Championship exemptions may use the Scottish Open as their final tune-up before Royal Birkdale. Their presence adds an additional layer to the event's dynamics — players like Josele Ballester, Sam Surratt, and Peter Uihlein, who have earned their Birkdale spots through LIV's exemption pathway, will want competitive rounds on links terrain before the major begins.

What's Next

The Scottish Open begins Thursday at Renaissance Club and concludes Sunday July 12 — leaving four days before The Open Championship practice rounds begin at Royal Birkdale. Watch the leaderboard carefully through the weekend. The player who wins Renaissance Club on Sunday with a clean display of links golf is the player whose name you should write down before heading into Birkdale week. History has rewarded that form.
