Gotterup Rockets to World No. 9 After Third Win of 2026 — Scheffler Holds Firm at No. 1 Ahead of The Open

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 7, 2026

The world golf rankings updated Monday after Chris Gotterup's wire-to-wire closing 62 at the John Deere Classic, and the news is significant: the 26-year-old American has vaulted into the top 10 in the world, landing at approximately No. 9 — his highest ranking to date and a position that makes him a legitimate name in any conversation about The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale starting July 16.

Scottie Scheffler holds the world No. 1 position with a lead that has remained substantial for most of the past two years. Rory McIlroy is No. 2, followed by Cameron Young (No. 3), Justin Rose (No. 4), Matthew Fitzpatrick (No. 5), Collin Morikawa (No. 6), Tommy Fleetwood (No. 7), and Xander Schauffele (No. 8). Gotterup slots in at No. 9, ahead of names that would have been unthinkable to discuss as his competition a year ago.

Gotterup's Remarkable 2026

Three wins in seven months is the kind of production that gets a player into the Hall of Fame conversation eventually — and for Gotterup, it represents a seismic shift in perception. He arrived at the John Deere Classic five shots off the pace entering the final round and proceeded to shoot a 62 that the field couldn't answer. Max Homa, the world No. 8 entering the week, also shot 62 on Sunday but finished one back at 19-under.

The brother-and-caddie dynamic with Patrick Gotterup has become one of the season's best stories. The partnership on the bag gives Chris something that not every player can claim — absolute trust in the person reading the greens and managing the course. The results suggest it's working.

Gotterup is also the defending Genesis Scottish Open champion, having won at Renaissance Club in 2025. The Scottish Open begins Thursday at the same venue, giving him an opportunity to enter Royal Birkdale having won at two of the three events immediately preceding The Open.

Scheffler and the Top 10

Scheffler's No. 1 position is built on a combination of consistency and peak performance that the rest of the top 10 hasn't quite matched. He enters The Open as defending champion, having won at Royal Portrush in 2025, and arrives at Royal Birkdale with the kind of momentum and course record history that makes him the clear favorite on paper.

The top 10 collectively represents the field of players most likely to contend at Birkdale — a links course that rewards creativity off the tee, precision iron play, and the short-game imagination that separates great players from merely excellent ones. All eight players above Gotterup in the rankings will be expected at Royal Birkdale. The ninth man on the list will be there too, with something to prove.

What's Next

The Genesis Scottish Open at Renaissance Club (July 9-12) is the last major opportunity for players to fine-tune their links game before The Open Championship. Gotterup, as defending champion, carries significant momentum into the event. Watch whether the top-10 names play the Scottish Open — those who do will be competing on nearly identical terrain to what awaits them at Royal Birkdale. The week after, the Claret Jug goes up for grabs. The 2026 world rankings heading into that week are the clearest signal of who will be in the conversation.
