Anthony Kim's Comeback: From 14-Year Absence to No. 184 in the World — Golf's Most Remarkable Return

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 7, 2026

Anthony Kim was going to be a superstar. In the late 2000s, the young American arrived on the PGA Tour with a game that drew genuine comparisons to the game's elite — a powerful, aggressive style, a short game that produced birdies under pressure, and a personality that made fans watch when his name appeared on the leaderboard. He won three PGA Tour events before the age of 26. He was a Ryder Cup standout. Golf waited for the major championships that seemed inevitable.

Then, in 2012, Anthony Kim disappeared. No announcement. No dramatic farewell. He simply stopped playing professional golf and wasn't seen again at competitive events for nearly 14 years. Speculation swirled — injuries, personal circumstances, a decision made on terms that were never publicly explained. The story became one of golf's enduring mysteries.

The Return

LIV Golf brought Kim back in 2026, adding him to the 4Aces GC roster captained by Dustin Johnson — replacing Patrick Reed on the team. What happened next surprised nearly everyone who had followed his story.

At LIV Golf Adelaide in February, in just his second LIV event of the season, Kim stood five shots behind the leaders entering the final round. What followed was one of the most dramatic final rounds in recent professional golf: a 63 that required birdies at exactly the moments pressure was highest, defeating Jon Rahm and Bryson DeChambeau at 23-under par. Kim won. For the first time in 14 years, Anthony Kim won a professional golf tournament.

The reaction was immediate and overwhelming. Video of Kim's final birdie putt went viral. Fans who hadn't thought about him in over a decade suddenly remembered exactly where they were when he was playing his best golf. The mystery of what happened to him — still largely unanswered — somehow made the comeback more powerful rather than less.

Virginia and the World Ranking

In May's LIV Golf Virginia event, Kim continued his impressive return. The 4Aces GC needed a hero in the final round to force a team playoff — Kim delivered a bogey-free 62, the best round of the final day, to give his team the chance they needed. They won the playoff. Kim's world ranking has climbed to approximately No. 184 — not a top-50 number, but the highest he's been ranked in roughly 14 years.

The trajectory matters more than the number right now. Kim is trending in one direction, playing competitive golf at a level that few predicted after such a prolonged absence. At 36 years old — still relatively young for professional golf — the question of how high he can climb is genuinely interesting.

What's Next

LIV Golf's next event is July 23-26 at JCB Golf and Country Club in Rocester, England — a $30 million event that will serve as a post-Open Championship gathering of the LIV circuit. Kim will carry whatever he absorbed from watching The Open Championship into that event. The more interesting long-term question is whether Kim's trajectory on LIV Golf eventually earns him a pathway back to the major championship stages he seemed destined for 15 years ago. Golf's history is full of journeys that took unexpected routes. Rarely have they included a 14-year intermission — and still arrived somewhere worth watching.
