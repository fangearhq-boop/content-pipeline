Nelly Korda's 2026 Season Is Historically Dominant — and the Sorenstam Comparisons Are Fully Earned

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 31, 2026

When the name Annika Sorenstam comes up in a conversation about a current player's season, you stop and pay attention. Sorenstam is the benchmark of women's golf dominance — a player whose career and peak performances define what is possible in the sport. Her 2001 season, when she won or finished runner-up in every LPGA event she entered, remains a singular achievement in women's golf history.

In 2026, Nelly Korda has matched it.

Through six LPGA Tour starts this year, Korda has won three events and finished second in the other three. She has not finished outside the top two in any tournament she has entered. Not once. In six tries. That is statistically tied with Sorenstam's 2001 pace for win-or-runner-up consistency in a calendar year.

A Major, Then More

The centerpiece of Korda's 2026 season so far is the Chevron Championship, her third career major title and the kind of win that cements a player's place among the game's greats. She followed that by winning the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba the very next week — by four shots, on a week she later described as not having her best game — for her 18th career LPGA title. That win made her the youngest American to reach 18 LPGA titles since Nancy Lopez.

The wins themselves matter enormously. But so does the consistency behind them. Three events where she did not win, and in every one she finished second. There is no fall-off between her wins. There is no week where she disappears from the conversation. She simply competes at the highest possible level every time she tees it up.

What Comes Next

The next event on Korda's 2026 calendar is the U.S. Women's Open at Riviera Country Club, beginning June 4. If she wins at Riviera, the Sorenstam comparisons will extend beyond one statistical benchmark into the broader question of whether Korda is tracking one of the greatest single seasons any golfer — male or female — has ever produced.

World No. 1 Atthaya Thitikul, defending champion Maja Stark, and the full depth of the top 25 in the world rankings will be at Riviera to push back. But based on everything that has happened in 2026, the burden of proof now rests with the rest of the field to show they can stop her. The Sorenstam comparison is not a projection anymore. It is a description of what is happening right now.

What's Next

Nelly Korda tees off at the 81st U.S. Women's Open at Riviera Country Club beginning Thursday, June 4. The tournament runs through Sunday, June 7. A win at Riviera would give Korda four major titles and push her 2026 win total to four events — while extending a record-matching season of consistency that has already earned its place in LPGA history. Golf doesn't produce moments like this often. Pay attention.
