US Women's Open 2026: Riviera Hosts Its First USGA Women's Major, With Korda as the Runaway Favorite

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 31, 2026

Four days from now, one of golf's most celebrated venues will host one of its most important championships for the very first time. The 81st U.S. Women's Open Presented by Ally descends on Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California, beginning June 4 — the first time in USGA history that a women's major has been played at Riviera.

It is the kind of scheduling that feels overdue. Riviera has hosted men's major championships, the Genesis Invitational, and the U.S. Open for decades. Its arrival on the women's major calendar, in a year when the field is led by the most dominant player in women's golf, makes this week essential viewing.

The Riviera Test

Riviera Country Club places a premium on course management, precise iron play, and the ability to navigate its distinctive kikuyu rough and tricky green complexes. It is a course that rewards experience and penalizes imprecision. Unlike some championship venues that favor long hitters, Riviera distributes its difficulty more evenly — a characteristic that opens the door to a broad range of contenders while still demanding the best of the world's best.

The USGA has accepted 1,897 entries for the championship, with all 25 of the world's top-ranked players in the field. Eleven past U.S. Women's Open champions are also entered, providing a deep and experienced competitive pool.

Korda: The Unmistakable Favorite

Nelly Korda arrives at Riviera having delivered one of the most remarkable starts to an LPGA season in decades. In six tournament starts in 2026, she has won three times — including the Chevron Championship for her third career major title — and finished runner-up in the other three. She has not finished outside the top two in any event this year. The last player to accomplish that in a calendar year was Annika Sorenstam in 2001.

Her most recent win, the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba, gave her the 18th title of her LPGA career — making her the youngest American to reach that number since Nancy Lopez. She won that event by four shots, in a week where she described herself as not quite having her best game. When she's not at her best and still wins by four, the field has a problem.

The Challengers

World No. 1 Atthaya "Jeeno" Thitikul represents the most credible individual threat to Korda. She has the ball-striking ability and course management intelligence that Riviera demands, and she has been a consistent presence near the top of major championship leaderboards. Defending champion Maja Stark returns to defend her title, bringing the confidence that comes with having already conquered a US Women's Open field.

What's Next

The 2026 U.S. Women's Open begins Thursday, June 4 at Riviera Country Club, with rounds concluding Sunday, June 7. The historic pairing of a landmark venue with an all-time season by its most dominant player has given this championship a singular energy. Whether Korda can translate her regular-season dominance to a new major venue — while facing the deepest field in women's golf — is the defining question of the week. Riviera will have answers.
