Eric Cole Leads Schwab Challenge Final Round at -12, Chasing First PGA Tour Win

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 31, 2026

The Charles Schwab Challenge arrives at its final chapter at Colonial Country Club on Sunday, and the leaderboard entering the day looks nothing like the week's expectations. Eric Cole carries a one-shot lead into the final round at 12 under par, following a Saturday where he shot seven-under 63 — a round that included eight birdies, one bogey, and a chip-in at the 16th that gave him the outright lead.

Ryan Gerard stands one back at 11 under. Mac Meissner is two back at 10 under. Between the three members of Sunday's final group, there is exactly one PGA Tour victory. Cole is still chasing his first.

A Leaderboard Built for Drama

Colonial Country Club does not typically produce blowout victories. Known as "Hogan's Alley" — a nod to Ben Hogan's legendary mastery of the course — Colonial rewards strategic play, precise shotmaking, and the mental endurance to avoid the bogeys that lurk around every corner. The scoring average reflects this: the men near the top of the leaderboard got there through consistency and intelligence, not raw power.

Gerard's Saturday run was equally impressive, closing with back-to-back birdies to confirm his spot in the final group. Meissner, also seeking his first tour victory, has held his position all week through steady golf that has gone largely unnoticed until Sunday's stakes became apparent.

The Lurking Threats

Any of the leaders could be caught. Hideki Matsuyama sits at nine under in a tie for eighth — close enough to capitalize on any stumbles from the leaders. Ludvig Åberg, who arrived at Colonial with a new putter in hand after a recent run of near-misses, is at seven under and tied for 12th. Neither is in the final group, but both have the game to put up a low round and wait.

The week also delivered some notable early departures. Tony Finau, who shot back-to-back 70s to finish at even par, missed the cut. Rickie Fowler followed an opening 70 with a 73 on Friday and also failed to make the weekend. The cut line at two under underscores just how competitive the week has been.

What's Next

The final group of Cole, Gerard, and Meissner tees off at 12:55 PM EDT, live on Golf Channel and ESPN+. Whoever wins the 2026 Charles Schwab Challenge will earn their place among the tournament's distinguished champions list — a course with deep historical roots and a reputation for rewarding the patient and precise. For Eric Cole, in particular, today represents the clearest path he has had to a first career PGA Tour title. Whether he converts it at Hogan's Alley will be the defining story of Sunday afternoon.
