### STORY 2: McIlroy Grand Slam Reaction — Long-Form Post
**Posting window:** 10:30 AM ET
**Tier:** 1

**Post:**
The calendar Grand Slam is still waiting for Rory McIlroy.

He came to Aronimink Golf Club this week as the 2026 Masters champion — one of the rare players in the modern game with a genuine shot at four majors in a single calendar year. For three rounds, the dream stayed alive. And then Sunday arrived.

McIlroy shot a 1-under 69 in the final round. That number doesn't capture how the round slipped away. He made just one birdie on the front nine. He bogeyed the drivable par-4 13th — a hole where a birdie is essentially expected from a player of his caliber and length. He couldn't birdie either par-5 on the back nine, where the course gives its most obvious scoring opportunities.

He finished T7 at 4-under — five shots behind Aaron Rai's winning total of 9-under.

"Not birdieing the two par 5s and making the bogey at the drivable par-4 13th," McIlroy said, summarizing where the round — and the Grand Slam bid — came undone.

There were also the frustrations of the week's atmosphere. At 36, McIlroy has played enough major golf to handle pressure. But the combination of missed opportunities, the roar of an American crowd, and the knowledge of what was at stake all contributed to a Sunday that did not deliver what his brilliant season deserved.

Now the attention turns to Shinnecock Hills. The 126th US Open, June 18-21. If McIlroy can win in Southampton, the Grand Slam conversation resets.

The story is not over. It is just moving to the next chapter.

Imagine what Aaron Rai's week looked like.

Sunday: Wins the PGA Championship. First English-born PGA champion since 1919. At 290-1 pre-tournament odds — the biggest longshot major winner in at least 20 years. Jumps 29 spots to world No. 15.

Monday: The champion's press conference. Media obligations. Probably very little sleep.

He needed time to process what he did on Sunday, to celebrate with family, and to rest before deciding when and where his next start will be.

S.Y. Noh will take his place in the Byron Nelson field.

The golf world will wait patiently. Aaron Rai earned every single day off.

### STORY 4: Lottie Woad England Double — Long-Form Post
**Posting window:** 1:00 PM ET
**Tier:** 2

**Post:**
It was England's week in golf.

On Sunday at Aronimink, Aaron Rai won the PGA Championship at 290-1 odds — the biggest longshot major victory in at least 20 years, and the first English-born PGA champion since Jim Barnes in 1919.

Also on Sunday in Cincinnati, 22-year-old Lottie Woad won the LPGA Kroger Queen City Championship at Maketewah Country Club. She closed with a 1-under 69 to finish at 12-under, two shots clear of Haeran Ryu.

It was Woad's second LPGA Tour title in just 10 months as a professional. In less than a year on tour, she has established herself as one of the most exciting young players in women's golf. Her first victory came at the Women's Scottish Open; now she adds a Queen City crown to her résumé.

Two English golfers. Two victories. Same Sunday.

For a country that has produced Major champions and Ryder Cup warriors for generations, it was a day to celebrate. And it is a reminder that women's golf in England has talent rising alongside Rai on the men's side.

### STORY 5: US Open 2026 Countdown — Long-Form Post
**Posting window:** 6:00 PM ET
**Tier:** 2

**Post:**
Six weeks from today, the 126th US Open begins at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York.

It is one of the most storied venues in American golf — a course that has hosted the US Open five times before, producing dramatic finishes, brutally difficult conditions, and unforgettable moments. The USGA has adopted a scaled-back infrastructure approach for 2026: fewer corporate tent structures on several holes, allowing the natural beauty of Shinnecock's wide fairways and unobstructed views to take center stage.

With 200,000 fans expected over the week and major transportation upgrades including a temporary LIRR stop directly across from the course, this will be one of the largest US Opens in recent memory.

The storylines are already set:

**McIlroy's Grand Slam:** After winning the Masters and falling short at the PGA Championship, Shinnecock Hills is Rory McIlroy's next chance at the calendar Grand Slam. For a 36-year-old at the peak of his experience if not his physical prime, every major is a window that won't stay open forever.

**Aaron Rai:** The new PGA Champion is fully exempt. After his week at Aronimink, he arrives at Shinnecock with no more longshot status — only expectations.

**Scheffler defending:** The world No. 1 will want to add a US Open to his growing resume.

Final qualifying continues through June 8. Mark June 18 on your calendar. ⛳
