Aaron Rai Withdraws from Byron Nelson — The PGA Champion Takes a Deserved Rest

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 20, 2026

Aaron Rai will not be defending his momentum at the CJ Cup Byron Nelson this week. The new PGA Champion — who won at Aronimink Golf Club just four days ago at 290-1 pre-tournament odds — has withdrawn from the tournament, with S.Y. Noh entering the field as his replacement.

The decision is entirely understandable, even if it creates a notable absence from a field that had hoped to see the sport's newest major champion in action immediately after his breakthrough.

The Weight of What Just Happened

Rai's PGA Championship victory was not a slow accumulation of momentum toward an expected result. It was one of the most stunning outcomes in major championship history. At 290-1 on opening odds — the longest-shot major winner in at least 20 years — he was nowhere near the tournament conversation when the field gathered at Aronimink. He was a respectable professional golfer who had never previously contended in a major at this level.

And then he shot a final-round 65. He made an eagle. He made critical birdie putts. He finished at 9-under, three shots clear of a chasing pack that included Jon Rahm and Alex Smalley. He became the first Englishman to win the PGA Championship since Jim Barnes in 1919.

He jumped 29 spots in the world rankings to world No. 15 — the highest position of his career.

The week that followed a win like this — the press conferences, the sponsor obligations, the global media attention, the calls from family and friends, the emotional processing of something that completely exceeded expectations — is exhausting in ways that never appear on a scorecard. Asking Rai to tee it up at TPC Craig Ranch four days later would have been unreasonable.

What's Next

Rai is fully exempt for the US Open at Shinnecock Hills on June 18, by virtue of his PGA Championship. When he does return to competition, the expectations attached to him will be permanently different — he is no longer a 290-1 longshot. He is a major champion. His game, his trajectory, and his position in the world rankings have all changed permanently.

The Byron Nelson field will compete without its newest major champion. The golf world will wait for his return. He earned this rest, and a great deal more besides.
