Lottie Woad Wins Queen City Championship as England Claims a Historic Double in Golf

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 20, 2026

The same Sunday that Aaron Rai became the first Englishman to win the PGA Championship since 1919, another English golfer was closing out a victory of her own on the LPGA Tour.

Lottie Woad, 22, won the Kroger Queen City Championship presented by P&G at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati, Ohio. She closed with a 1-under 69 to finish at 12-under par for the tournament — 268 total strokes — two shots clear of Haeran Ryu. Miyu Yamashita finished third at 9-under, with Ruoning Yin a shot further back in fourth.

It was the second LPGA Tour title of Woad's professional career, achieved in less than 10 months as a professional. Her first win came at the Women's Scottish Open. Now she adds a Queen City championship to her young résumé, and England adds an extraordinary footnote to golf history: two tournament winners on the same Sunday, across both tours.

Who Is Lottie Woad?

Woad turned professional in 2025 after an amateur career that established her as one of the most promising young players in English golf. At 22, she competes with a calm that belies her inexperience at the professional level. Her ball-striking precision and her ability to manage pressure — evidenced again by a composed final-round 69 in Cincinnati — are the characteristics that separate developing players from those who win early in their careers.

Her first professional win came at the Women's Scottish Open, an event with a strong field and a home-country crowd rooting for her success. The Queen City Championship is a different test — an American event, an unfamiliar crowd, and the full pressure of a Sunday leaderboard. She handled both.

The $300,000 winner's share from the $2 million purse will fund her next stage of development on tour. But the more important currency is the confidence that comes from winning twice in under a year. She has now proven she can win on demand, not just on home turf.

England's Week

The connection between Woad's victory and Rai's on the same afternoon is not a narrative contrivance — it actually happened. Aaron Rai won the PGA Championship at Aronimink at 9-under. Lottie Woad won the Queen City Championship at Maketewah at 12-under. Both English. Both Sunday. Both champions.

England has produced major champions for generations. But two victories on the same Sunday, across both genders of professional golf, in the same week that Rai made history with the first English-born PGA Championship since 1919, is remarkable timing that the English golf community will celebrate for years.

What's Next

There is no LPGA event this week. The tour's next significant event is the ShopRite LPGA Classic, May 29-31 at Seaview Bay Course in Galloway, NJ. The US Women's Open follows on June 4-7 at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, CA — a major where Woad will arrive as a two-time winner with momentum and no fear of pressure. Watch this name. The wins are coming with increasing regularity.
