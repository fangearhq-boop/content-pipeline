CJ Cup Byron Nelson Preview: Scheffler Returns to Defend as Rai Withdraws Following PGA Championship Win

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 20, 2026

The PGA Tour heads to Texas this week for the CJ Cup Byron Nelson at TPC Craig Ranch in McKinney — and before the tournament even begins, there is breaking news. Aaron Rai, who won the PGA Championship just four days ago at 290-1 odds, has withdrawn from the Byron Nelson field. He will be replaced by S.Y. Noh.

After the most extraordinary week of his professional career — the tournament win, the champion's press conference, the global attention that comes with being the first Englishman to win the PGA Championship since 1919 — a recovery week is not just understandable. It is the right call. Rai will return when he is ready.

Without Rai, attention shifts to the tournament's central storyline: can Scottie Scheffler defend his title on a course that has been specifically redesigned to challenge him?

Scheffler's Defending Record and the Course Renovation

Scheffler's 2025 performance at TPC Craig Ranch was one of the most dominant individual tournament performances of the modern era. He finished at 31-under par — 259 total strokes — setting the PGA Tour's 72-hole scoring record and winning by eight shots. The course offered little resistance.

Course designers took note. A $25 million renovation led by Lanny Wadkins was completed before the 2026 tournament, with the explicit goal of adding challenge and difficulty to a layout that produced historically low scoring. New rough lines, repositioned fairway bunkers, and tightened landing zones change the risk-reward calculus that Scheffler navigated so effortlessly in 2025.

Whether those changes are sufficient to bring Scheffler back to the field is the central question of the week. He is the best player in the world, playing on a course he knows intimately, in front of a home crowd in the Dallas area. The redesign may have closed the gap — but closing to within Scheffler's range is still a very wide margin.

Field Highlights

Jordan Spieth is always motivated at his home-area event, and the Byron Nelson has historically been a highlight of his season. His relationship with the Texas crowd adds an extra dimension of energy to whatever he produces on the course.

Brooks Koepka, Si Woo Kim, and the rest of the Byron Nelson field will compete for a $10.3 million purse across four rounds at TPC Craig Ranch. The tournament begins Thursday with Golf Channel coverage from 3-7 PM ET, and continues through Sunday.

What's Next

Round 1 begins Thursday, May 21. PGA Tour Live coverage on ESPN+ starts at 7:45 AM ET each morning, with broadcast coverage on Golf Channel following in the afternoon. For Scheffler, Byron Nelson is a chance to extend one of the most dominant title defenses in recent memory. For the rest of the field, it is a chance to prove that a redesigned course can produce a new champion. And for Aaron Rai, rest well — the US Open at Shinnecock Hills is six weeks away, and the new PGA Champion will need to be ready.
