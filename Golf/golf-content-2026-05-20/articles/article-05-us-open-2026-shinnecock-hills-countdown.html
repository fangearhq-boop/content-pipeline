Six Weeks Out: What to Know About the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 20, 2026

The 126th US Open is six weeks away. Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York hosts the event for the sixth time in history — June 18-21, 2026 — and with Rory McIlroy's calendar Grand Slam bid still active, Aaron Rai arriving as the new PGA Champion, and 200,000 fans expected to fill the Eastern Long Island coastline, this will be one of the most consequential US Opens in recent years.

Here is what you need to know before the first tee shots of June 18.

Shinnecock Hills and What Makes It Different

Shinnecock Hills is one of the five original founding clubs of the United States Golf Association, and it has hosted the US Open five times before 2026 — in 1896, 1986, 1995, 2004, and 2018. Each hosting has produced its own drama. The 2018 edition was particularly challenging, with USGA setup decisions that created nine holes playing above 100 percent of their par-adjusted difficulty on the final day — before a correction allowed players to eventually finish.

The course winds across the Southampton hillside with views of the Peconic Bay on one side and the Atlantic coastline on the other. It is links-adjacent golf — exposed to wind, demanding of creativity in shot-shaping, and punishing to any player who relies solely on power without strategic intelligence. The rough at a US Open Shinnecock setup will be severe. The greens will be firm and fast. The experience of playing a US Open-caliber examination of golf will be unlike anything else on the major circuit.

Storylines Heading In

McIlroy is the obvious headline. His Masters win in April, combined with a near-miss at the PGA Championship, means Shinnecock is now his most important tournament of the year. At 36, with no guarantee of how many more windows at the calendar Grand Slam will open, his preparation over the next six weeks will be intense and deliberate.

Rai arrives as a fully exempt PGA Champion — no more longshot status. As world No. 15, he will face expectations he has never experienced before. How a player responds to their first US Open as a reigning major champion is one of the sport's recurring tests of character.

Scottie Scheffler, the world No. 1, has continued to build one of the most complete resumes in the sport. A US Open would be a significant addition. The field at Shinnecock will be the strongest of the year.

Logistics and Infrastructure

The USGA has adopted a scaled-back approach to the 2026 event infrastructure. There will be no large corporate tent structures on holes 13, 15, or 16 — a deliberate choice to restore the natural beauty of the Shinnecock landscape and allow fans to see more of the course's iconic open terrain. The "Top of the Hill" viewing structure, a fixture of past US Open setups at other venues, will also not be present.

Transportation logistics include shuttle buses from Calverton airfield, and the Long Island Rail Road is adding a temporary stop directly across from the club during championship week, with increased train frequency from New York Penn Station.

What's Next

Final qualifying for the US Open continues through June 8. "Golf's Longest Day" — when final qualifying sites simultaneously determine the last spots in the field — is June 8 on Golf Channel. The full field will be confirmed shortly thereafter. Mark your calendar: June 18 at Shinnecock Hills, where the Grand Slam, the world No. 1, and the sport's newest major champion will all converge for the most important tournament of the summer.
