Lauren Coughlin Wins LPGA Aramco Championship by Five Over Korda

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

April 11, 2026

While the world's attention turns to Augusta National and the Masters, Lauren Coughlin stepped into the spotlight at Shadow Creek Golf Course in Las Vegas and delivered one of the dominant performances of the 2026 LPGA Tour season.

Coughlin won the Aramco Championship with a 7-under-par score, claiming a five-shot margin of victory and a $600,000 payday. The runner-up was Nelly Korda — one of the LPGA Tour's best players and the statistical leader in Strokes Gained: Total and Strokes Gained: Tee-to-Green through the early part of the 2026 season. Beating Korda by five shots is not a minor accomplishment. It is a statement.

Coughlin's Breakout Performance

Coughlin controlled the Aramco Championship from start to finish at Shadow Creek — a stunning desert course in Las Vegas that rewards precision ball-striking and confident putting. She gave Korda no openings, building her lead systematically across the tournament's rounds and holding it through any pressure the field could apply.

A five-shot win at any level of professional golf is commanding. A five-shot win over Nelly Korda — arguably the LPGA's most dominant force in recent seasons — signals that Coughlin is ready to compete consistently at the top of the tour. Golf fans paying attention to the women's game should note this name. The Aramco Championship may be remembered as the moment Coughlin announced her arrival at the highest level.

Korda's Motivation for the Next Event

Nelly Korda finished second with a strong performance — just not strong enough to match Coughlin's best. The loss gives Korda added motivation heading into the Ford Championship, the LPGA Tour's next event (April 16-19, El Caballero Country Club, Tarzana, California, $3.75 million purse). Korda has been statistically dominant this season, and a five-shot loss to a surging competitor is exactly the kind of result that sharpens a player's focus.

The Ford Championship at El Caballero CC — a parkland course in the hills of the San Fernando Valley — will be a different test from Shadow Creek's desert layout. But both Korda and Coughlin will be among the favorites, and the dynamic between them entering Tarzana is compelling.

What's Next

The LPGA Tour continues with the Ford Championship, April 16-19, 2026, at El Caballero Country Club in Tarzana, California. The $3.75 million purse is the next significant prize on offer. Lauren Coughlin will carry the momentum of her Aramco victory into California. Nelly Korda will be looking for the win that reasserts her dominance. The LPGA's 2026 season is delivering exactly the kind of storylines that make the women's game worth following week to week.
