Burns, Reed, and Europe's Best in Pursuit — Who Can Chase Down McIlroy at Augusta?

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

April 11, 2026

Six shots is a lot at Augusta National. But the players on Rory McIlroy's heels entering Saturday's third round are not here to settle for silver.

Sam Burns and Patrick Reed share second place at 6-under par — six back of the defending champion. Behind them, a trio of European Ryder Cup veterans sits at 5-under: Tommy Fleetwood, Shane Lowry, and Justin Rose. Each of these players brings a different reason for optimism, and each knows that Augusta's back nine can reverse fortunes in ways no other course in golf can replicate.

Sam Burns: His Biggest Stage Yet

Sam Burns has never won a major championship. Saturday at Augusta National, he will play in the final group alongside Rory McIlroy — the defending champion who holds the largest 36-hole lead in Masters history. It is, without question, the most significant round of Burns' career.

There is no template for what this kind of opportunity demands emotionally. Burns is an excellent ball-striker, capable of producing low rounds on demanding courses. He has won multiple times on the PGA Tour and has shown he can handle pressure. But Augusta on a Saturday afternoon, paired with the hottest player in the world, is a different kind of crucible.

If Burns can block out the gap and focus on his own game — making birdies at Augusta's reachable par-5s and staying patient on the demanding approach holes — he could produce the kind of round that keeps Sunday interesting. A 65 from Burns would leave him one back entering the final round and force McIlroy to play defensively for the first time all week.

Patrick Reed: The Man Who Knows Green Jacket Pressure

Patrick Reed won the Masters in 2018. He knows what it takes to win at Augusta — and more importantly, he knows how to play Augusta's back nine when a green jacket is on the line. Reed plays his best golf when the moment is biggest, and he has the course knowledge to make the most of Saturday's opportunity.

Reed tees off with Justin Rose in the penultimate group at 2:39 PM ET. Rose is also a proven Augusta performer, a former major champion with the experience to close the gap if the opportunity presents itself on the back nine.

Europe's Ryder Cup Trio

Tommy Fleetwood and Shane Lowry will play together at 2:28 PM ET, a pairing that carries the feel of a Ryder Cup partnership on one of America's most treasured courses. Both are capable of producing the low rounds that would make the scoreboard interesting. Lowry has major championship experience — the 2019 Open Championship — and has shown repeatedly that he rises to the biggest occasions. Fleetwood's ball-striking consistently ranks among the tour's best.

Seven shots is the gap they need to close. It is a tall order against a player as confident as McIlroy currently appears. But on a Saturday when Augusta's weather tends to soften the course and scores drop, the possibility of a compelling finish Sunday cannot be dismissed entirely.

What's Next

Round 3 of the 2026 Masters begins Saturday with the early groups teeing off from 9:31 AM ET and the final group — McIlroy and Burns — at 2:50 PM ET. Full coverage on CBS and Amazon Prime.
