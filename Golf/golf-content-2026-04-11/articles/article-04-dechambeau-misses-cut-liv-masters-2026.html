DeChambeau Misses Masters Cut, LIV Golf's Augusta Presence at Historic Low

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

April 11, 2026

Bryson DeChambeau came to Augusta National as one of LIV Golf's most prominent representatives — and left after 36 holes.

DeChambeau missed the cut at the 2026 Masters, finishing at 6-over par. A triple-bogey seven on the 18th hole in his final round was the decisive blow, dropping him out of contention for the weekend and sending him home before Moving Day. It was only the third time in his last 12 major appearances that he had failed to reach the weekend at a major championship.

LIV Golf's Shrinking Augusta Footprint

The broader story around DeChambeau's exit touches on the evolving relationship between LIV Golf and Augusta National. The 2026 Masters featured just 10 LIV Golf players in the field — the fewest since the Saudi-backed league was founded. The trajectory has been consistently downward: from 18 LIV players at the field's peak to 14, then 12 in 2025, and now 10.

The departure of high-profile players from LIV's roster is part of the explanation. Brooks Koepka — a four-time major champion who was one of LIV Golf's most prominent American recruits — rejoined the PGA Tour in 2026 and was not part of the LIV contingent at Augusta this year.

For LIV Golf, whose original pitch included providing a competitive path to the major championships, the declining Augusta presence is a meaningful data point in the ongoing debate about the circuit's trajectory.

The Weekend That Belongs to the PGA Tour

The Masters weekend at Augusta National will be competed by players who have chosen to remain on or return to the PGA Tour. McIlroy, Burns, Reed, Fleetwood, Lowry, Rose, Scheffler — the leaderboard at Augusta reads like a who's-who of the traditional tour ecosystem.

DeChambeau had the form to compete — multiple LIV wins in the early 2026 season. Augusta did not care. The course is an equalizer, and this week it has equalized against LIV Golf's chances at the top of the leaderboard decisively.

What's Next

The Masters continues through Sunday, April 12, where Rory McIlroy will defend his title with the largest 36-hole lead in the tournament's history. For DeChambeau and the LIV contingent, the next major opportunity is the PGA Championship in May, then the U.S. Open in June. LIV Golf's season continues on its own circuit in the weeks ahead. The Masters leaderboard this weekend is being written without them.
