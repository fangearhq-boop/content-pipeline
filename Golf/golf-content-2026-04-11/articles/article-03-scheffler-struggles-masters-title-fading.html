Scheffler's Third Green Jacket Is Slipping Away at Augusta

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

April 11, 2026

This was supposed to be Scottie Scheffler's tournament to win. The World No. 1, a two-time Masters champion, arriving at Augusta National as the most dominant player in professional golf. Instead, through two rounds of the 2026 Masters, Scheffler finds himself outside the top 20 — and Rory McIlroy is out of sight.

Scheffler's Round 2 was one of his worst ever at Augusta National. He failed to break par, posting four bogeys in a round that left him more than seven shots behind the defending champion. In 24 career rounds at Augusta, Scheffler has failed to break par only three times. Friday was one of them.

What Went Wrong

Augusta National demands precision, patience, and putting. Scheffler's ball-striking has never been in question at Augusta — he is one of the purest ball-strikers in the world and has consistently hit greens in regulation at rates that give him abundant birdie chances. But on Friday, the scoring did not come.

Four bogeys is not a collapse. It is a slow bleed — the kind of round where you never quite get momentum, where putts lip out and approach shots miss in the wrong spots. Augusta punishes those misses on specific holes, and Scheffler found enough of them to end the day well back in the field.

The deeper story may be form. Scheffler entered the Masters on the back of three consecutive starts outside the top 10 — his worst such stretch in several years. Augusta typically rewards players who have been playing consistently, and Scheffler's lead-up form had not been what Augusta demands entering its biggest week of the year.

The Path Back

Scheffler is not done. His pairing with Ludvig Åberg in Round 3 at 12:16 PM ET gives him an early tee time and the opportunity to set a tone before the marquee groups behind him. If he produces a round in the low-to-mid 60s — the kind of round his talent clearly supports — and McIlroy stumbles, the tournament could theoretically tighten. Augusta has seen stranger things.

But the realistic outlook entering Saturday is this: Scheffler is playing for position, pride, and form heading into the rest of the major season. The 2026 Masters, as things stand, belongs to Rory McIlroy. Whether Scheffler can find his form in time to mount any kind of challenge is the narrow question remaining.

What's Next

Scottie Scheffler tees off in Round 3 at 12:16 PM ET on Saturday alongside Ludvig Åberg. The final round is Sunday, April 12, with coverage on CBS and Amazon Prime. For Scheffler, the next major championship on the calendar after the Masters is the PGA Championship in May. A strong finish at Augusta — even if the green jacket is out of reach — would be a confidence-building step heading into the rest of the major season.
