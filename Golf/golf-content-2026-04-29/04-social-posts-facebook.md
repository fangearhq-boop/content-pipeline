### STORY 1: Cadillac Championship Preview

**Post 1A — Long-Form** (8:00 AM ET)
The PGA Tour is back at Trump National Doral — and the Blue Monster hasn't forgotten anyone.

The Cadillac Championship begins Thursday, April 30, with World No. 1 Scottie Scheffler leading a 72-player no-cut field in one of the most compelling Signature Events of the 2026 season. The $20 million purse makes it one of the richest events on the calendar. The Blue Monster — measuring nearly 7,739 yards with water in play on the majority of holes and wind that can completely change the character of a round — is a genuine examination of every part of a player's game.

Scheffler comes in as the overwhelming favorite, but he carries something heavier than just his world No. 1 ranking: the frustration of two consecutive near-misses in the biggest moments of the season. Conveniently for him, neither McIlroy nor Fitzpatrick are in the field this week. Cameron Young, Tommy Fleetwood, Viktor Hovland, Shane Lowry, and Alex Fitzpatrick are all committed — formidable names in a star-studded field, but perhaps a window of opportunity for Scheffler to finally get over the line.

Round 1 tees off Thursday. TV coverage begins at 3 PM ET on Golf Channel. The Blue Monster is open for business. ⛳

### STORY 2: LPGA Riviera Maya Open — Korda

**Post 2A — Long-Form** (10:30 AM ET)
Nelly Korda is five points away from the LPGA Hall of Fame — and she's showing no sign of slowing down.

The world's top-ranked women's player arrives at the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba in Mexico this week as the heavy favorite and the most compelling story in women's golf. She just won the Chevron Championship, her third career major title and her seventeenth LPGA victory, and she's heading straight to El Camaleón Golf Course in Playa del Carmen to try and win again.

Korda currently holds 22 of the 27 points required for automatic LPGA Hall of Fame qualification. Each major win earns two points. A regular win earns one. With five points still to accumulate, the Hall of Fame feels close — but not close enough to take for granted. Korda is playing like a woman on a mission.

The Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba runs from April 30 to May 3. Nine Mexican players are competing in the 144-player field, bringing home crowd energy to a week that already has enormous star power at the top.

Korda has won twice already in 2026 (Hilton Grand Vacations Tournament of Champions + Chevron Championship) and finished second three times. She's the story of this LPGA season. This week could add another chapter. 🏆

### STORY 3: Rahm Wins LIV Mexico City

**Post 3A — Long-Form** (8:00 AM ET)
Jon Rahm is turning the LIV Golf circuit into his personal highlight reel.

The Spaniard closed with a bogey-free 7-under 64 at Club de Golf Chapultepec on Sunday and won LIV Golf Mexico City by six shots over David Puig of Fireballs GC. It was Rahm's second win of the 2026 LIV season, his fourth individual LIV victory, and his 16th overall LIV trophy — putting him one ahead of Bryson DeChambeau in the all-time count.

And it didn't stop there. Legion XIII — Rahm's team — also won the team competition by nine shots, making it a complete sweep of Sunday's action.

DeChambeau, who had been Rahm's closest rival in the individual standings, withdrew from the final round with wrist discomfort. The injury is being monitored ahead of upcoming events and adds uncertainty to what has been an otherwise dominant season for the former LIV champion.

Rahm is scheduled to compete at LIV Virginia — held at Trump National Golf Club in Washington, DC — from May 7 to 10. If he's in this form, it's hard to see anyone stopping him. 🔥

### STORY 4: Scheffler's Runner-Up Record

**Post 4A — Long-Form** (1:00 PM ET)
Here's a stat that puts Scottie Scheffler's 2026 frustration into remarkable historical context: he is the first player since Greg Norman in 1986 to finish runner-up at both the Masters and the following week's RBC Heritage.

Norman. 1986. Forty years ago.

Scheffler lost to Rory McIlroy by a single shot at Augusta in what was one of the most compelling final rounds in recent Masters history. He then lost to Matt Fitzpatrick in a playoff at Harbour Town Golf Links, watching a second consecutive near-miss go the wrong way.

These are not collapses. Scheffler has been excellent. He has one win and five top-five finishes in eight starts in 2026. He is still the best player in the world. But the biggest moments keep producing the same result — a runner-up finish that makes headlines for all the wrong reasons.

This week, he gets a break of sorts. Neither McIlroy nor Fitzpatrick — the men who beat him — are in the Cadillac Championship field at Trump National Doral. The $20 million Signature Event at the Blue Monster begins Thursday, and Scheffler enters as the betting favorite.

Maybe this is the week the narrative flips. 👊

### STORY 5: Fitzpatrick Wins RBC Heritage

**Post 5A — Long-Form** (3:30 PM ET)
There's a new name to add to golf's 2026 watch list — and it's one that's been there before.

Matt Fitzpatrick defeated Scottie Scheffler in a one-hole playoff at the RBC Heritage at Harbour Town Golf Links on April 19 to claim the second PGA Tour title of his 2026 season. Sky Sports called it "another playoff winner" — a description that's becoming something of a theme for the Englishman, who is turning late-round pressure into consistent wins.

Two PGA Tour victories before May 1. A playoff win over the world's top-ranked player. A season that's already among the best on tour in terms of big moments delivered.

Fitzpatrick won't be defending anything at Cadillac this week — he's not in the field. But the ripple effect of his Harbour Town performance is significant. He has now beaten Scheffler twice in the biggest moments of the season, and his confidence heading into the major championship months must be enormous.

The U.S. Open is at Aronimink Golf Club. Fitzpatrick won his only major — the 2022 U.S. Open — at Brookline. He knows how to win on tight, demanding golf courses. Keep an eye on him as summer approaches. 🏆
