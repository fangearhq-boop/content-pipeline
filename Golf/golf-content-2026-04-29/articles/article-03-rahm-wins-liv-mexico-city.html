Rahm Wins LIV Mexico City by Six — As DeChambeau's Wrist Clouds the Season Ahead

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

April 29, 2026

Jon Rahm has made himself the undeniable force on the LIV Golf circuit, and his performance at Club de Golf Chapultepec in Mexico City only strengthened that case. The Spaniard closed with a bogey-free 7-under 64 in the final round to win LIV Golf Mexico City by six shots over young Fireballs GC star David Puig. It was Rahm's second individual LIV win of the 2026 season, his fourth overall on the circuit, and it moved his total LIV trophy count to 16 — one ahead of the man who had been his closest rival, Bryson DeChambeau.

The most dominant element of Rahm's victory was not the margin but the method. He stayed aggressive throughout a final round that required patience, executed a course management plan that neutralized the risk at Chapultepec's most punishing holes, and produced the lowest score in the field when it mattered most. That is the mark of a player operating at a different level than everyone around him.

Legion XIII — Rahm's team — also claimed the team title by nine shots, making it a complete sweep. Rahm celebrated both trophies, extending Legion XIII's profile as LIV's most complete outfit in 2026.

DeChambeau's Wrist Withdrawal Adds Drama

The weekend in Mexico City was not without significant news elsewhere. Bryson DeChambeau — Rahm's nearest challenger in the individual standings and the LIV circuit's defending champion — withdrew from the final round with wrist discomfort that emerged during the Saturday round. He took the medical decision not to risk further injury by completing his Sunday round.

DeChambeau's wrist is being monitored ahead of upcoming events. A healthy DeChambeau has proven capable of matching Rahm at his best — their rivalry has produced some of LIV Golf's most compelling moments — but if the injury lingers, Rahm's lead in the individual standings will only extend further. The timing, entering the busiest stretch of the LIV calendar, is far from ideal for the American.

What's Next

The LIV Golf circuit moves next to LIV Virginia, held at Trump National Golf Club in Washington, DC from May 7 to 10. It will be another opportunity for Rahm — who has shown no signs of fatigue or inconsistency — to extend his lead and add to what is already one of the most impressive individual campaigns in LIV Golf's brief history. DeChambeau's participation at Virginia remains uncertain pending the wrist evaluation.

For Rahm, Mexico City was the latest data point in a dominant year. For LIV Golf, his performance is exactly the kind of championship golf that the circuit needs to showcase as it continues to build its following. The tournament is growing. The prize money is significant. And right now, Rahm is the reason to tune in.
