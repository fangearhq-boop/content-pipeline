Five Points From History: Korda Headlines LPGA Riviera Maya Open With Hall of Fame in Sight

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

April 29, 2026

Nelly Korda is not slowing down. The world's No. 1 women's golfer arrives at the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba in Mexico this week having just won the Chevron Championship — her third career major title — and immediately returns to competition at El Camaleón Golf Course in Playa del Carmen. The Riviera Maya Open, with a $2.5 million purse and a 144-player field, tees off Thursday, April 30, and runs through Sunday, May 3.

Korda currently holds 22 of the 27 points required for automatic LPGA Hall of Fame qualification. She needs five more. That might sound straightforward, but the LPGA Hall of Fame is one of the most demanding achievement standards in professional sports — and the fact that Korda, at 27 years old, is this close is a testament to her dominance over an extended period.

The Chevron Championship win — a five-shot victory at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston — cemented her place as the defining player of this generation of women's golf. It was her 17th LPGA title and her 21st win worldwide. She finished with the world ranking where it belongs: No. 1. And then she got on a plane to Mexico to do it again.

The Hall of Fame Math

The LPGA Hall of Fame eligibility system rewards sustained excellence across multiple categories — it cannot be rushed, and it cannot be acquired through a single dominant stretch. A major championship victory earns two points. A regular LPGA Tour victory earns one point. Korda has accumulated her 22 points through a combination that includes three major victories, two Olympic gold medals from Paris 2024, and sixteen non-major LPGA titles.

Five more points means five more wins — or three major victories, or some combination that gets her to 27. Given her form entering the Mexican coast this week, it is not a question of whether Korda reaches the Hall of Fame, but when. She has been the most dominant player on the LPGA for the past 18 months, and she is competing like someone who is nowhere near finished.

In 2026 alone, Korda has two wins (Hilton Grand Vacations Tournament of Champions, Chevron Championship) and three runner-up finishes in five events. The runner-up results are not slumps — they are evidence that she contends in virtually every tournament she enters.

What's Next

The Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba features nine Mexican golfers in the field, including Gaby López, María Fassi, and Isabella Fierro, giving the tournament a home-crowd dimension that will make the atmosphere at El Camaleón Golf Course particularly electric when any of them make a move up the leaderboard. The course itself — famous for its cenote hazards, narrow fairways, and coastal wind — is technically demanding and demands precision throughout every round.

For Korda, it is simply the next opportunity. She has won tournaments before with less form, less confidence, and far less at stake. With three titles in 2026 potentially achievable within a single week, and the Hall of Fame counter ticking toward 27, every event from here is a chapter in a story that's approaching one of the great conclusions in women's sports.
