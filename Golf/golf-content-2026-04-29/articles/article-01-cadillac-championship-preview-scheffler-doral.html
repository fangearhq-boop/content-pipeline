Cadillac Championship Preview: Scheffler Leads the Blue Monster Field as PGA Tour Returns to Doral

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

April 29, 2026

Trump National Doral is hosting a PGA Tour event for the first time since 2016, and the Blue Monster is not offering any warm welcomes. The Cadillac Championship — the fifth of eight Signature Events on the 2026 PGA Tour schedule — tees off Thursday, April 30, with World No. 1 Scottie Scheffler leading a 72-player, no-cut field competing for $20 million. Round 1 television coverage begins at 3:00 PM ET on Golf Channel.

The Blue Monster, designed by Dick Wilson and stretching to 7,739 yards at par 72, is one of the most demanding courses on the Florida circuit. Water is in play on the majority of holes, the rough is thick, and the wind — when it rises off the surrounding terrain — can transform a manageable round into a disaster with a single miscalculated approach. Trump National Doral has always tested players' physical and mental resolve, and after a decade away from the PGA Tour calendar, it returns with the same unforgiving character.

Scheffler enters this week carrying the weight of two consecutive runner-up finishes — at the Masters, where he lost to Rory McIlroy by one shot, and at the RBC Heritage, where he fell to Matt Fitzpatrick in a playoff. He has been excellent throughout the 2026 season: one win, five top-five finishes in eight starts. But the biggest stages keep producing the wrong result, and Scheffler is running out of patience.

A Field Without Its Missing Stars

Critically for Scheffler's prospects, this week's field is missing several of the players who have beaten him in 2026. McIlroy is not playing. Matt Fitzpatrick — who won in a playoff at Harbour Town — is not in the field. Xander Schauffele and Ludvig Åberg are also absent, making this a Signature Event that lacks some of its anticipated star power at the top.

That does not mean the competition lacks depth. Cameron Young, the 2026 Players Championship winner, is a compelling contender whose ability to move the ball off the tee suits a long, demanding course like Doral. Tommy Fleetwood brings exceptional iron play and major championship experience. Viktor Hovland has been searching for consistency and views long courses as favorable to his profile. Alex Fitzpatrick — not to be confused with his brother Matt — makes his PGA Tour debut as a full member following his win at the Zurich Classic. Shane Lowry, Rose, and Sepp Straka round out a European contingent that always performs well on water-lined layouts.

What's Next

Round 1 begins Thursday, April 30 with ESPN+ PGA Tour Live coverage from 8:30 AM ET and Golf Channel picking up from 3:00 to 7:00 PM ET. The Cadillac Championship runs through Sunday, May 3. With the $3.6 million first-place prize at stake and FedEx Cup points making a crucial difference heading toward the summer majors, every round carries genuine consequence.

For Scheffler, this week represents an opportunity to convert on course that, by the betting market's assessment, suits his game. Whether his near-miss frustrations motivate him or subtly affect his pressure moments remains to be seen — but this is one of the best chances he'll have all season to put a win on the board when it matters most.
