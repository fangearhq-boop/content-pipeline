Fitzpatrick Beats Scheffler in Playoff at RBC Heritage — His Second PGA Tour Win of 2026

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

April 29, 2026

Matt Fitzpatrick is becoming one of the 2026 golf season's most compelling storylines — and the numbers make it difficult to argue otherwise. The Englishman defeated Scottie Scheffler in a one-hole playoff at the RBC Heritage at Harbour Town Golf Links on April 19, claiming his second PGA Tour victory of the season before the month of May had even begun.

It was the kind of win that announces a player in capital letters. Scheffler — the world's No. 1, a two-time Masters champion, a player who had been relentlessly consistent throughout the 2026 season — was right there with him. And Fitzpatrick closed. Again.

Sky Sports described the Heritage win as "another playoff winner," and the framing is accurate. Fitzpatrick's ability to deliver in the final holes under tournament-closing pressure has become a signature element of his game in 2026. He did not just win — he won against the best player in the world, on one of the most historic and demanding courses on the PGA Tour schedule, with everything on the line.

Two Wins, Two Playoffs, One Season

Fitzpatrick's 2026 PGA Tour record is exceptional by any measure. Two wins — both in high-pressure circumstances, both requiring him to outlast the finest competition available. He has established himself not merely as a consistent performer but as a player who elevates his game when the stakes are highest.

He won his only major — the 2022 U.S. Open — at The Country Club in Brookline, Massachusetts, on a tight, difficult course that rewards precision iron play and course management over raw distance. Harbour Town Golf Links, where he won the Heritage, rewards similar qualities. For a player of Fitzpatrick's skill set, demanding tests of ball-striking are not obstacles — they are advantages.

With the U.S. Open approaching — to be held at Aronimink Golf Club, a classical parkland test — Fitzpatrick's profile as a genuine contender has never been stronger. He is not currently in the Cadillac Championship field this week, opting out of the Signature Event to rest and prepare. But wherever he plays next, expect the same intensity and the same capacity for late-round brilliance.

What's Next

Fitzpatrick's RBC Heritage win sets up the rest of the 2026 season as a genuine narrative. He has beaten the world's No. 1 player twice in the same spring. He has shown he can win under playoff pressure. He has the game and the major championship pedigree to contend at the U.S. Open in June. Whether the summer months confirm Fitzpatrick as a title contender at every major or narrow his window to the right conditions, this is a player at the peak of his career. Watch him carefully in the weeks ahead.
