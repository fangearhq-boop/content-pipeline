Gotterup Fires 62 to Win John Deere Classic — Three PGA Tour Wins in 2026

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 6, 2026

Chris Gotterup arrived at TPC Deere Run for the John Deere Classic's final round five shots behind the leaders. He left with the trophy.

Gotterup's stunning Sunday 62 — nine under par on a day when the tournament leaderboard was tight and the nerves were real — propelled him from the back of the pack to a one-stroke victory over Max Homa. Final total: 20-under 264. Third PGA Tour win of the 2026 season. Fifth career victory in 84 starts as a professional.

The Kohles Heartbreak at 18

The win had a dramatic final act. Ben Kohles, a veteran searching for his first PGA Tour title in his 120th start, stood on the 18th tee tied for the lead with the outcome still very much undecided. Par would force a playoff. Kohles hit his approach shot left. The ball found the water. A double bogey followed — and with it, the title slipped away. He finished in a tie for third, one shot behind Gotterup and Homa.

It was one of the more agonizing finishes of the season. Kohles, who had played the kind of week that professional golfers spend years chasing, had his moment and could not hold it. The sport offers no comfort for near-misses at 120 starts.

A Brothers' Week

Gotterup had a somewhat unusual addition to his team this week: his brother Patrick was on the bag. Whatever the extra family dynamic brought to the atmosphere inside the ropes, it clearly worked. Gotterup's 62 was the low round of the tournament on Sunday, a clinical, aggressive display from a player who has made a habit of peak Sunday performances throughout 2026.

His three wins this year — the Sony Open, the Phoenix Open, and now the John Deere — make him one of only two players with three victories in the 2026 season, alongside Matt Fitzpatrick. More significantly, Gotterup is now tied with world No. 1 Scottie Scheffler for the most PGA Tour wins over the last twelve months. Four each. That is the company Gotterup is keeping now.

Spieth Survives; Koivun Debuts

Two secondary storylines added depth to the week at TPC Deere Run. Jordan Spieth, navigating a complicated stretch of form, survived the cut — a modest result for a former world No. 1, but a noteworthy one. And Jackson Koivun made his professional debut at the John Deere Classic, gaining his first competitive exposure to Tour conditions and pressure before falling short of contention.

What's Next

Gotterup heads directly to Scotland, where the Genesis Scottish Open at The Renaissance Club begins Thursday July 9 — his next opportunity to build on what is now one of the most compelling individual runs in the game. The Open Championship follows July 16-19 at Royal Birkdale. Gotterup, who has shown the ability to win in links conditions, enters the summer's biggest fortnight as one of the most dangerous players on either side of the Atlantic.
