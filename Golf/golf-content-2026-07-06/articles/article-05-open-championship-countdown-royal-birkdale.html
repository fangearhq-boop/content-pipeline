Ten Days to Birkdale: The Open Championship Storylines That Will Define the 154th Edition

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 6, 2026

The 154th Open Championship is ten days away. Royal Birkdale Golf Club in Southport will host the oldest major in golf on July 16-19, and the week between now and Thursday's opening round will be defined by two things: the Genesis Scottish Open, which serves as the sport's final links tune-up, and the building anticipation around a field that may be as balanced and compelling as any Open in recent memory.

Scheffler Defending at Birkdale

Scottie Scheffler arrives at Royal Birkdale as the defending Open champion — he won the 153rd edition at Royal Portrush in 2025 in a display that underlined his status as the world's best player. Defending any major is difficult; defending at the Open, where the links conditions demand specific skills and the field is global in its talent, adds an additional layer of complexity.

Scheffler's consistency makes him the natural favourite. He has won four PGA Tour events in the last twelve months, tied with Chris Gotterup for the most on Tour. His ball-striking, which is the single most important weapon a player can carry to a British links, is without peer in the current game. Birkdale suits him.

The Scottish Open Tune-Up

The Genesis Scottish Open at The Renaissance Club (July 9-12) is the pivot point between John Deere week and Birkdale. Players who come out of Scotland with form tend to carry it into the Open; the conditions at The Renaissance Club — links turf, coastal wind, firm fairways — closely mirror what they will find on the Lancashire coast the following week.

Chris Gotterup, who won the John Deere Classic with a brilliant Sunday 62 and has prior links wins in Scotland, is potentially the most dangerous player to watch over this fortnight. Rory McIlroy, a former Birkdale champion who has been building toward a major breakthrough for several seasons, will treat both weeks as a connected campaign.

LIV Players at the Open

The Open Championship has maintained its tradition of welcoming LIV Golf players who hold major exemptions — a policy that distinguishes the R&A from the PGA Tour's stricter membership rules. Ballester, Surratt, and Uihlein are among the LIV players confirmed for the Birkdale field, giving the competition an unusual cross-circuit element that adds narrative interest if any of them contend.

The Heroes Classic charity event at Royal Birkdale on July 14 — two days before the Open begins — will provide the first public competitive look at the course in this summer's conditions, serving as an informal preview of the week ahead.

What's Next

The Scottish Open begins Thursday July 9 at The Renaissance Club. Four rounds of links golf there, then directly to Royal Birkdale for the 154th Open Championship on July 16. This fortnight is the heart of the summer golf calendar, and the combination of Scheffler, McIlroy, Gotterup, and a field loaded with elite talent from multiple tours sets the stage for one of the most anticipated Opens in years. The Claret Jug will be lifted on July 19 — but not before Birkdale has had the final say.
