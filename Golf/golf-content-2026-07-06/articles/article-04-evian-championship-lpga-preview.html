Evian Championship Preview: LPGA's Lakeside Major Returns to the French Alps

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 6, 2026

While men's golf turns its attention to Scotland and the build-up to the Open Championship, the women's game has its own major championship on the horizon — and it comes with one of the most striking backdrops in professional sports.

The Amundi Evian Championship begins Thursday, July 9, at the Evian Resort Golf Club in Evian-les-Bains, France. The course sits on a hillside above Lake Geneva, with the French Alps visible in the distance on clear days, and the tournament has earned its place as one of the most visually spectacular events on the LPGA calendar.

Haeran Ryu Enters as Freshest Major Champion

Haeran Ryu arrives at Evian as the most recent major winner on tour. The South Korean claimed the Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine National Golf Club for her first major title, winning by two strokes over Ina Yoon in windy conditions. It was the kind of performance that announced a player ready to build on momentum — and Evian, just weeks later, offers exactly that opportunity.

Whether Ryu can carry the emotional energy of a first major win into a second successive major remains the central storyline of the week. History suggests that players who win their first major often arrive at the next event with either extraordinary confidence or the weight of new expectations. For Ryu, who showed composure under pressure at Hazeltine, the smart money is on confidence.

The LPGA's Major Championship Stretch

The Evian Championship is the fourth LPGA major of the 2026 season. After France, the calendar turns to the ISPS HANDA Women's Scottish Open at Dundonald Links (July 23-26), followed immediately by the AIG Women's Open at Royal Lytham and St Annes in England (July 30 to August 2). That sequence — Evian, Women's Scottish Open, Women's Open — represents the richest three-week stretch on the women's global calendar, offering the game's best players their most concentrated window of major championship opportunity.

Korean players have dominated the LPGA's major landscape in recent seasons, and the Evian's field typically reflects that trend, with a strong contingent from the Korea Ladies Professional Golf Association system competing alongside American, European, and Japanese stars.

What's Next

The Amundi Evian Championship runs July 9-12. The winner emerges with a major title, a trophy presentation on the hillside above Lake Geneva, and a place in one of professional golf's most beautiful week-long memories. After France, the women's game moves directly into the Scottish Open and the AIG Women's Open — a run of events that will define the summer for whoever can sustain form across three demanding weeks.
