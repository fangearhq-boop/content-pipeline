Scottish Open Preview: Scheffler, McIlroy, Gotterup Head to Renaissance Club as Open Looms

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 6, 2026

The Open Championship is ten days away. Before the world descends on Royal Birkdale, the game's best players converge on the East Lothian coast for the tournament that has long served as the most reliable predictor of Open form — the Genesis Scottish Open at The Renaissance Club in North Berwick, running July 9-12.

The co-sanctioned PGA Tour and DP World Tour event draws a field that includes Scottie Scheffler, Rory McIlroy, and — a touch of the ongoing circuit rivalry — LIV Golf players who hold major exemptions. It is the richest, most competitive field of the summer, assembled in exactly the conditions that matter most.

Scheffler: Defending Champion Heading to Links Country

Scottie Scheffler arrives at The Renaissance Club as the world's No. 1 player and the defending Open champion. He won at Royal Portrush in 2025 in a performance that removed any lingering doubt about his status as the best in the game. Scottish links golf suits his ball-striking precision and ability to manage wind and trajectory — the same skills that made him nearly impossible to beat at Portrush.

Scheffler has been consistent without being dominant in recent weeks, and the Scottish Open represents his clearest opportunity to tune his links game before the Open itself.

McIlroy's Motivation

Rory McIlroy brings a particular motivation to North Berwick. The Northern Irishman is chasing form, and Scotland's links courses are the landscape where he has built some of the most significant chapters of his career. The crowd at The Renaissance Club will be supportive — Scottish golf fans are generous to the game's marquee names — and McIlroy will be using the week as a confidence exercise as much as a competitive one ahead of Birkdale.

McIlroy's Open record at links venues is remarkable. The question, as it has been for much of the past few seasons, is whether he can string together the week that puts everything together when it matters most.

Gotterup's Momentum

Fresh off winning the John Deere Classic with a brilliant Sunday 62, Chris Gotterup enters the Scottish Open with as much momentum as any player in professional golf. He has won three times in 2026. He is tied with Scheffler for the most Tour wins in the last twelve months. And he has links form — a previous win in Scotland against McIlroy demonstrates that his game translates to links conditions.

The LIV players with exemptions add an interesting wrinkle to the field. The Scottish Open is one of the rare events where the two tours' top performers share a leaderboard, and the competitive dynamic will be closely watched ahead of the Open, where the same players will be eligible to compete.

What's Next

The Genesis Scottish Open begins Thursday July 9 at The Renaissance Club. Four rounds of links golf on the East Lothian coast, with the Open Championship looming directly afterward at Royal Birkdale. Whoever emerges from Scotland with form, momentum, and confidence will carry real advantage into one of the most anticipated Opens in recent memory.
