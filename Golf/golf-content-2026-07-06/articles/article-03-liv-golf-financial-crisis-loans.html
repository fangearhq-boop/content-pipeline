LIV Golf Is Running on Loans to Finish 2026 — and the Future Beyond This Year Remains Uncertain

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 6, 2026

Company filings have confirmed what many observers had suspected for months: LIV Golf is no longer operating on capital investment. It is running on loans.

Documents filed in the United Kingdom and Jersey reveal that Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — which founded and has bankrolled the league since its launch — has transitioned from investor to lender. The PIF has established a debenture lending facility that is effectively funding LIV's day-to-day operations while the league's leadership searches for new outside investors to take over what has become a multi-billion-dollar annual commitment.

The Numbers

LIV Golf spends approximately $100 million per month — covering player contracts, event operations, prize purses, and overhead. In 2026, the PIF provided $333 million ($267 million in February and $66 million in May) before transitioning to the lending facility in June and adding another $130 million. That represents roughly five months of operational funding. The league reportedly still needs approximately $400 million to fully fund its remaining 2026 events and honour existing player commitments.

Four tournaments remain on the 2026 schedule: LIV Golf United Kingdom at JCB Golf & Country Club (July 23-26), LIV Golf New York (August 6-9), LIV Golf Indianapolis (August 20-23), and the $40 million LIV Team Championship in Michigan (August 27-30). Reports suggest the United Kingdom event faces the most uncertainty of the four; the New York event is currently considered the most likely to proceed.

PIF's Stated Commitment

The Public Investment Fund announced earlier in 2026 that it would fund LIV only through the end of the current season. The implication is direct: beyond December 2026, LIV Golf's operational structure either finds new investment, negotiates a sale or merger, or winds down. No announcement about 2027 has been made. No new investors have been publicly confirmed.

For the players — many of whom signed multi-year guaranteed contracts worth tens of millions of dollars to join the league — the financial news adds uncertainty to what was supposed to be a period of stability. The contracts themselves are not in immediate jeopardy if the league completes the 2026 season, but the longer-term picture remains opaque.

What's Next

The immediate question is whether all four remaining 2026 events will take place. The UK event at JCB is the next test — scheduled for July 23-26, just two weeks away. If the financial gap is not bridged before that date, the uncertainty will intensify. LIV Golf launched in 2022 with ambitions to reshape professional golf; in 2026, the project is being kept alive, event by event, on loans.
