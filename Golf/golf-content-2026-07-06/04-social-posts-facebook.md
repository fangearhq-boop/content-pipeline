### STORY 3: LIV Golf Financial Crisis — $400M Shortfall, Running on Loans

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

The financial reality behind LIV Golf is more precarious than the tour's marketing materials suggest.

Company filings in the United Kingdom and Jersey, reviewed by multiple outlets, reveal that LIV Golf is no longer operating on capital funding from the Saudi Arabian Public Investment Fund. Instead, the PIF has become a lender — and the league is running on loans.

The numbers are stark. LIV Golf spends approximately $100 million per month on player contracts, operating costs, and prize purses. In 2026, the PIF provided $333 million — $267 million in February and $66 million in May — then added $130 million more in June through the new lending facility. That $533 million covers roughly five months of operations. The league has four events remaining on its 2026 schedule, but reports suggest it is still owed approximately $400 million to fully fund those events and honour outstanding commitments.

The four remaining events are: LIV Golf United Kingdom at JCB Golf & Country Club (July 23-26), LIV Golf New York (August 6-9), LIV Golf Indianapolis (August 20-23), and the $40 million LIV Team Championship in Michigan (August 27-30). Reports indicate the UK event faces the most uncertainty; the New York event is considered the most likely to proceed.

The PIF announced earlier in the year that it would back LIV only through the end of the 2026 season. Beyond that — a new investor, a sale, a merger, or a wind-down — nothing has been confirmed.

### STORY 5: Open Championship Countdown — 10 Days to Royal Birkdale

#### Long-Form Post — 8:30 PM ET

The 154th Open Championship is ten days away.

Royal Birkdale Golf Club in Southport, Lancashire has hosted the Open nine times since 1954. It is the British Open's most beloved venue: exposed duneland, undulating fairways, thick rough, unpredictable weather off the Irish Sea, and a finishing hole that has produced some of the greatest moments in the major's history.

Scottie Scheffler arrives as the defending champion, having won at Royal Portrush in 2025 in a manner that confirmed his status as the world's best player. As the world No. 1, the Texan is the obvious favourite — but Royal Birkdale has a history of surprises.

This week's Genesis Scottish Open at The Renaissance Club is the final competitive tune-up before Birkdale. The results there will carry real significance: links-form players heading into the Open week with momentum tend to perform. Rory McIlroy, a former Birkdale champion, will be looking to use Scotland as a springboard.

LIV Golf players who have earned major exemptions are eligible to compete at the Open. Ballester, Surratt, and Uihlein are among those in the field. The Open remains the one major that has maintained a degree of openness toward LIV participants.

A Heroes Classic charity event is scheduled for July 14 at Birkdale — two days before the competition begins — providing the first public look at the course in tournament conditions this year.
