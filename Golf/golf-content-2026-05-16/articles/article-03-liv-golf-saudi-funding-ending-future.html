Saudi PIF Pulls LIV Golf Funding After 2026 — The League's Future Is Now in Question

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

When LIV Golf launched in 2022, it did so with the full financial backing of Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — an essentially unlimited checkbook that lured major champions away from the PGA Tour and fundamentally reshaped the professional golf landscape.

That era is ending.

Saudi Arabia's PIF has confirmed it will end its financial support of LIV Golf at the conclusion of the 2026 season, stating that the investment is "no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy." The announcement marks the beginning of an existential question for the league: can LIV Golf survive without the money that created it?

The Scale of the Investment

The numbers behind LIV's creation are staggering. The PIF's total projected spend on LIV Golf operations is expected to surpass $6 billion by the end of the 2026 season. That money funded guaranteed player contracts — many worth tens of millions of dollars per year — along with team structures, prize purses, broadcast deals, and the operational costs of running a global tour.

The revenue return has been modest by comparison. While LIV's revenue tracked ahead of 2025 figures through its early 2026 events, the gap between what was spent and what was earned has been enormous. The PIF's calculation appears to be that the returns do not justify continuing at that scale.

Leadership Changes

Along with the funding announcement comes a significant leadership transition. Yasir Al-Rumayyan, the PIF chairman who helped conceive and launch LIV Golf alongside Greg Norman, is stepping down from his role with the league. His departure removes one of the most powerful figures in the organization's history.

In his place, LIV has appointed investment bankers Gene Davis and Jon Zinman to a new independent board, tasked with guiding the league through its transition. LIV is pursuing what it calls a "diversified, multi-partner investment model" — essentially, trying to replace one enormous backer with multiple smaller ones.

Early reports suggest this search has not produced results yet.

What It Means for Players

At this week's PGA Championship, 11 LIV Golf members are competing in the field, including Jon Rahm and Bryson DeChambeau (who missed the cut). These players left the PGA Tour for guaranteed Saudi-funded contracts that many in the sport viewed as irresponsible spending.

If LIV cannot secure new investment and the guaranteed money dries up, those players may find themselves without a stable tour to compete on — and potentially without a straightforward path back to the PGA Tour, depending on how any future negotiations unfold.

What's Next

LIV Golf continues its 2026 season while the search for new investors proceeds. The league has not announced any schedule changes or event cancellations — for now, operations continue. But without a committed long-term financial partner to replace the PIF, the league's post-2026 future remains genuinely uncertain. The organization that spent billions to disrupt professional golf now finds itself in need of someone to return the favor. Whether that someone exists remains golf's most pressing off-course question of 2026.
