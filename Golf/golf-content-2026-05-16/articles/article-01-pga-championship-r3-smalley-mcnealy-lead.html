PGA Championship Round 3: Smalley and McNealy Lead at -4 as Scheffler, McIlroy Lurk

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

The 108th PGA Championship is set up for a dramatic Saturday at Aronimink Golf Club.

After 36 holes in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania, Maverick McNealy and Alex Smalley share the lead at 4-under par — two players who have never led a major heading into the weekend, now carrying the weight of the Wanamaker Trophy lead into Saturday's third round. Scottie Scheffler, the defending champion and world No. 1, sits two shots back at -2. Rory McIlroy, chasing history, is at +1 — five shots back — after a remarkable Round 2 recovery.

A Leaderboard Full of Stars

The distance between the leaders and the field is deceptively narrow. With 44 players within five shots of the lead, this is one of the most compressed PGA Championship leaderboards after 36 holes in recent memory. Cameron Young shot a 67 in Round 2 and is firmly in contention. Justin Thomas, Si Woo Kim, and multiple former major winners are within a few shots of Smalley and McNealy.

Scheffler's Round 2 was a testament to his champion's mentality. He bogeyed three of his first four holes and could have unraveled — instead, he steadied himself and carded a 71 to stay at -2. Not where he wants to be, but close enough to make a charge entirely realistic.

Notably, Bryson DeChambeau missed the cut, a significant blow for the LIV Golf contingent at this week's event.

Round 3 Pairings

The Saturday schedule features some compelling pairings. Rory McIlroy goes out alongside Brooks Koepka — two former PGA Champions — at 11:00 AM ET, which sets up a fascinating early barometer for what's possible in the afternoon. Scheffler tees off at 1:40 PM ET alongside David Puig. Justin Thomas and Cameron Young play together at 2:00 PM ET. The final pairing — Smalley and McNealy — tees off at 2:40 PM ET under the full weight of major-championship pressure.

The afternoon pairings will be where this championship is made or broken. The Aronimink crowd will be enormous by afternoon, and the pressure of the final pairing is unlike anything either Smalley or McNealy has experienced.

What's Next

Round 3 is underway Saturday. Coverage begins at 7:45 AM ET when the first groups tee off, with the leaders going out in the afternoon. The cut line is set; 65-plus players will chase one of golf's most historic trophies over the final 36 holes. With stars bunched close behind, Saturday has the potential to completely reshape the leaderboard — or confirm that Smalley and McNealy have the composure to handle the spotlight.
