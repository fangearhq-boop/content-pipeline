Moka Yamashita Wins Kroger Queen City Championship at -17; Korda Finishes T5

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

Moka Yamashita claimed the Kroger Queen City Championship presented by P&G, finishing at 17-under par at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati, Ohio, to win by two shots.

The Japanese golfer delivered a composed, consistent performance across the May 14-17 event — the first running of the LPGA Tour at the Cincinnati venue — to secure one of the more impressive victories on tour in recent weeks. Yamashita led throughout the final stages and never allowed the field to close the gap when it mattered most.

Yamashita's Dominant Finish

At 17-under, Yamashita's winning total reflected a player in complete control of her game. Her ability to score consistently on a course the entire LPGA Tour was playing for the first time demonstrates the kind of adaptability that separates great players from good ones.

The victory adds Yamashita to a 2026 LPGA season that has been rich with international winners, reflecting the tour's global depth. Her performance in Cincinnati was one of the week's best stories in women's golf — even with the PGA Championship dominating the broader golf conversation.

Korda Falls Short

Nelly Korda, the world No. 2 and the player everyone expected to challenge for the title, finished tied for fifth at 15-under — two shots back of Yamashita. Korda opened the tournament with a 1-under 69 in Round 1, three shots off the early pace set by co-leaders at -4.

Despite climbing the leaderboard through the middle of the tournament, Korda couldn't find the extra gear needed to catch Yamashita when it counted. For Korda, the result continues what has been a solidly competitive 2026 season without a victory — her last LPGA win came at the Mizuho Americas Open, where she finished behind eventual champion Jeeno Thitikul.

Korda is still chasing the Hall of Fame threshold — she needs 27 points to qualify and has accumulated 23. Every tournament without a win is a week the clock keeps ticking.

A New Venue Delivers

Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati hosted the LPGA for the first time this week, and by all accounts it proved a worthy host. The field responded well to the course, and Yamashita's -17 winning total suggests a layout that allowed scoring while still challenging the world's best players.

The Kroger Queen City Championship joins a growing list of LPGA events that bring top professional golf to Midwest markets — and Yamashita's performance gave Cincinnati fans a genuine champion to celebrate.

What's Next

The LPGA Tour continues its 2026 season in the coming weeks, with Korda and her rivals targeting upcoming events as the Hall of Fame race tightens. Yamashita heads into the rest of the year with fresh momentum. For women's golf fans, the Queen City Championship confirmed once again that the LPGA's international depth makes every week unpredictable — and worth watching. Korda will be back in the hunt soon, and the question is whether she can overtake the likes of Yamashita before the year's biggest events arrive.
