Smalley and McNealy: Two Unlikely Co-Leaders at the PGA Championship

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

If you had listed the players most likely to lead the PGA Championship after 36 holes, Alex Smalley and Maverick McNealy would not have appeared on the page.

Yet here they are, tied at 4-under par at Aronimink Golf Club — sharing the lead at one of golf's most historic championships, heading into a Saturday that will test everything they have.

Who Is Alex Smalley?

Alex Smalley is 29 years old and has never won a professional tournament on the PGA Tour. This week at Aronimink is just his fifth appearance in a major championship. His previous best result at the PGA Championship was a T23 in 2023.

By the metrics that typically predict major success — world ranking, Tour wins, major experience — Smalley was not a player whose name surfaced as a realistic contender. But golf does not always follow predictions. He shot a solid 69 in Round 2 to share the lead, and he will step into the Saturday final pairing having earned that position with two days of steady, unspectacular brilliance.

Who Is Maverick McNealy?

McNealy has one Tour victory — a FedEx Cup Fall event — and his best finish in a major before this week was a T18 at this year's Masters. He had never been in the top 25 of a major championship at the 36-hole mark. Friday's 67 changed that in a hurry.

McNealy shot 3-under in Round 2 and looks comfortable at the top of the board. Whether that comfort holds when the pressure of Saturday afternoon's final pairing settles around him is the question no one can answer in advance.

The Pressure of the Final Pairing

Smalley and McNealy will play together in the final group Saturday, teeing off at 2:40 PM ET. They'll be in front of one of the largest galleries of the championship, with major network cameras tracking their every shot.

Behind them, Scottie Scheffler — who has won this tournament before — lurks two shots back. Rory McIlroy, chasing history, is five behind but entirely capable of posting a round that changes the entire dynamic. Cameron Young, Justin Thomas, and a half-dozen others are within striking distance.

The leaders have everything to prove and everything to gain. They've earned their way to the top. Now the tournament's history gets to test whether they belong there.

What's Next

Round 3 tees off Saturday at Aronimink. The final pairing — Smalley and McNealy — goes out at 2:40 PM ET. By the end of Saturday, the leaderboard will look dramatically different: either the unlikely co-leaders will have survived the pressure and extended their advantage, or the championship's stars will have done what stars do at majors — assert themselves when the moment matters most. Either way, Sunday's final round is set up to be something worth watching.
