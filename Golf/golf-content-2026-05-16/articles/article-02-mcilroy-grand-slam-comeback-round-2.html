McIlroy Grand Slam Dream Still Alive After Bogey-Free Round 2 at PGA Championship

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 16, 2026

Rory McIlroy has always been at his best when the situation demands it. On Friday at Aronimink, the situation demanded something after a calamitous opening round — and McIlroy delivered.

After calling his first-round 74 "s***" in a blunt post-round assessment, McIlroy went out in Round 2 and played 18 holes without a bogey, carding a 67 to move to +1 overall for the tournament. He's five shots behind co-leaders Maverick McNealy and Alex Smalley — alive, contending, and eyeing history.

"At five back I do feel like I'm right in the tournament," McIlroy said Friday evening.

The Weight of the Grand Slam

The stakes for McIlroy at this week's PGA Championship go beyond a regular Tour victory. Earlier this season, McIlroy won The Masters — the one major that had eluded him through his entire career. With Masters glory finally secured, McIlroy arrived at Aronimink holding three of the four major championships: The Open Championship, the US Open, and now the Masters.

The PGA Championship is the final piece. A win here would complete the career Grand Slam — joining an exclusive group of just five players in the history of the sport: Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, and Tiger Woods.

That's the context behind every swing McIlroy takes this week. Every putt. Every decision. The Grand Slam is the reason this PGA Championship feels different — bigger — than any he has played in before.

The Round 1 Collapse

McIlroy's Thursday was painful to watch. He made four consecutive bogeys to close his first round, eventually signing for a 74 that put his cut prospects in serious jeopardy. The driver, he admitted afterward, was going two ways — a loss of confidence in the most important club in the bag at a major championship.

When a player of McIlroy's calibre calls a round "s***," you believe them. It was an honest assessment of a round that could have ended his week before the weekend arrived.

The Round 2 Response

And then came Friday. McIlroy played with a singular focus: make the cut, stay in the mix, give himself a chance. He did far more than that — 18 holes without a bogey, multiple birdie opportunities taken, and a 67 that moved him to +1 and comfortably inside the cut line.

He'll go out Saturday morning alongside Brooks Koepka — a three-time PGA Championship winner — at 11:00 AM ET. Koepka knows this tournament better than almost anyone alive. McIlroy will need to channel that same ability to manufacture something remarkable from a five-shot deficit.

What's Next

The math is challenging. A five-shot deficit at a major over two rounds is a significant hill — but it has been climbed before, and McIlroy is capable of stringing together the kind of weekend rounds that can swallow a leaderboard whole. He tees off at 11 AM ET Saturday in Round 3. If he can post another round in the mid-60s while the leaders stumble, Sunday's final round becomes a genuine Grand Slam possibility. Watch McIlroy on Saturday — this is the round that will define whether his quest is real or just hope.
