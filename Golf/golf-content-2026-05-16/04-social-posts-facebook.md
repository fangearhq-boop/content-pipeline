### STORY 3: LIV Golf Future in Doubt

#### Long-Form Post — 8:30 PM ET

The league that fundamentally changed professional golf is now fighting for its survival.

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund announced it will end its financial support of LIV Golf at the conclusion of the 2026 season, saying the investment is "no longer consistent" with its current strategy. The PIF has spent a projected $6 billion or more on LIV since the league launched — a staggering sum that reshaped the sport's financial landscape, lured away some of the biggest names in golf, and led to years of tensions with the PGA Tour.

Now the money is stopping.

LIV chairman Yasir Al-Rumayyan — who was instrumental in launching the league alongside Greg Norman — is stepping down. An independent board with investment bankers has been formed. The league says it is pursuing a "diversified, multi-partner investment model," but early reports suggest it is struggling to attract new investors.

The consequences are significant. LIV players, many of whom left the PGA Tour for guaranteed contracts funded by Saudi money, face an uncertain future. Jon Rahm, Bryson DeChambeau, and others are among the 11 LIV members currently competing at this week's PGA Championship — a reminder that the tour remains relevant on the world stage, even as its financial foundation crumbles.

What happens to LIV Golf if the funding runs out? The answer to that question will reshape professional golf once again.
