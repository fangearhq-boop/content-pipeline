McIlroy Wins Back-to-Back Masters, Joins Nicklaus, Faldo, Tiger in Exclusive Club

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 14, 2026

Rory McIlroy is a back-to-back Masters champion, and Sunday's final round at Augusta National may stand as the most dramatic title defense in the tournament's long history.

McIlroy finished the 2026 Masters at 12-under par, winning by one stroke over Scottie Scheffler to claim his second consecutive green jacket. He now joins Jack Nicklaus (1965-66), Nick Faldo (1989-90), and Tiger Woods (2001-02) as the only players ever to win consecutive Masters titles — a list that requires no further context about the company it represents.

A Final Round That Could Have Gone Several Ways

The final round was anything but a victory lap. McIlroy made a double-bogey on hole 4 and a bogey on hole 6, and at various points Cameron Young and Justin Rose each held two-shot leads. Augusta National was doing what Augusta National does on Sunday — redistributing hope with every hole.

McIlroy's response was methodical. He birdied holes 7 and 8 to claw back into contention, then played the back nine with precision when others stumbled. Birdies at 12 and 13 gave him the lead he would ultimately hold. When the pressure fell on Young and Rose to finish, neither could close.

The 72nd hole added one final dramatic moment. McIlroy's tee shot on 18 traveled wide left, leaving him to play his approach from behind a line of trees. He punched a low shot that came to rest in a greenside bunker. From the sand, he chipped cleanly onto the putting surface and rolled in two putts to win. One shot. Two putts. Done.

The Final Leaderboard

Scottie Scheffler finished runner-up at 11-under with a bogey-free closing 68 — arguably the best round played at Augusta on Sunday. Cameron Young, Russell Henley, Justin Rose, and Tyrrell Hatton tied for third at 10-under.

For Young, it was a tournament that will haunt and inspire in equal measure. He produced one of the most extraordinary rounds in Masters history on Saturday — a 65 that erased McIlroy's six-shot lead after starting the week eight shots behind. To share the Sunday lead and ultimately come up short by two strokes is the kind of near-miss that either breaks a player or propels them to future greatness.

History and Money

The $22.5 million purse — a record for the Masters — saw McIlroy collect $4.5 million for the victory. But the currency that matters more at Augusta is the jacket, and McIlroy now owns two of them.

He completed his Career Grand Slam by winning the 2025 Masters. He has now won it twice in a row. The conversation about where he belongs among the game's all-time greats no longer requires much debate.

What's Next

McIlroy has elected to skip the RBC Heritage this week in Hilton Head Island, choosing rest over an immediate return to competition. Scottie Scheffler — the +350 betting favorite at Harbour Town — leads the field into the PGA Tour's next Signature Event starting Thursday, April 16. The next major on the calendar is the PGA Championship in May.
