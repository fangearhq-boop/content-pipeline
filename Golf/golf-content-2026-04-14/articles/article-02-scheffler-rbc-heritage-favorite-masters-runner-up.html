Scheffler's Bogey-Free Masters Sunday Makes Him Clear RBC Heritage Favorite

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 14, 2026

Scottie Scheffler left Augusta National as runner-up in the 2026 Masters. He arrived in Hilton Head Island as the betting favorite for the RBC Heritage, and the gap between him and the rest of the field may be wider than it looks on a standard odds board.

Scheffler's final-round 68 on Sunday was bogey-free — a composed, precise performance in the most demanding conditions the Masters produces. He started the day behind the leaders, made his way up the leaderboard methodically, and finished at 11-under par, one stroke behind winner Rory McIlroy. It was arguably the best ball-striking round played at Augusta on the final day of the tournament.

The Jump to Harbour Town

The RBC Heritage at Harbour Town Golf Links begins Thursday, April 16, and Scheffler is the +350 favorite — a substantial margin over the next competitor on the board. Xander Schauffele is listed at +1500. The gap reflects not just Scheffler's form coming out of Augusta but his specific history at Harbour Town.

In 2024, Scheffler won the Masters and then won the RBC Heritage in the same week — the first player to accomplish that back-to-back double since Bernhard Langer in 1985. Harbour Town suits a player who controls the golf ball with Scheffler's precision. The Pete Dye-designed course punishes wild drives and rewards the kind of measured ball-striking that Scheffler showed all Sunday at Augusta.

McIlroy's Absence Opens the Field

Rory McIlroy, fresh off his back-to-back Masters victory, has elected to skip the RBC Heritage to rest. His absence removes the most momentum-carrying player from the field and effectively concentrates attention on Scheffler as the clear competitive standard this week.

Defending champion Justin Thomas — who won the 2025 RBC Heritage in a playoff against Andrew Novak — is in the field. So are Cameron Young, Russell Henley, Jordan Spieth, Sam Burns, and Collin Morikawa. Several of those players also had strong Masters showings, meaning the RBC Heritage field carries genuine depth from players running hot.

What's Next

The RBC Heritage is one of eight PGA Tour Signature Events on the 2026 schedule, featuring 82 players, no cut, and a $20 million purse. First round tees off Thursday, April 16. If Scheffler wins, he will have completed the Masters-RBC Heritage double for the second time in three years — a combination that no one else has managed in four decades.
