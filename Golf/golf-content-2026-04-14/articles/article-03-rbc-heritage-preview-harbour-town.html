RBC Heritage Preview: Scheffler Leads a Star Field at Harbour Town Without McIlroy

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | April 14, 2026

The week after the Masters at Augusta National, professional golf relocates to a very different kind of test. Harbour Town Golf Links on Hilton Head Island, South Carolina, is the host of the 2026 RBC Heritage — a place where the azaleas are replaced by Spanish moss, the wide fairways by narrow, tree-lined corridors, and the Augusta mystique by an equally demanding requirement for precision.

The RBC Heritage runs April 16-19. It is one of eight PGA Tour Signature Events in 2026, meaning 82 players, no cut, and a $20 million purse over four rounds.

The Field: Deep and Ready

Rory McIlroy, coming off his back-to-back Masters victory on Sunday, will not be in the field. He elected to rest following his green jacket defense — a reasonable choice after one of the most demanding final rounds in recent major history.

In his absence, Scottie Scheffler leads the field as the +350 betting favorite. Scheffler's bogey-free final-round 68 at Augusta elevated him to runner-up at 11-under, and his specific history at Harbour Town adds to the case for him this week. He won both the Masters and the RBC Heritage in 2024 — the first to accomplish that specific double since Bernhard Langer did it in 1985.

The full field includes defending champion Justin Thomas, who won the 2025 RBC Heritage in a playoff against Andrew Novak. Jordan Spieth, Cameron Young, Russell Henley, Sam Burns, and Collin Morikawa are also in the field. Young and Henley both finished tied for third at the Masters; they arrive at Harbour Town having played some of the best golf of their seasons.

What Makes Harbour Town Different

The Pete Dye-designed course at Harbour Town is among the most distinctive on the PGA Tour schedule. At just over 7,000 yards, it is one of the shorter venues on tour — but length off the tee is actively disadvantageous if it brings the trees into play. Accuracy from the tee, sharp iron play, and putting on Bermuda grass surfaces define the winning formula at Hilton Head.

The no-cut format means every player in the field will complete all 72 holes. For players still finding their footing early in the week, there is no escape hatch — only an opportunity to salvage or build on a slow start.

What's Next

First round at Harbour Town begins Thursday, April 16. Coverage is available on Golf Channel and CBS. The winner earns FedEx Cup points as part of the Signature Event elevated schedule. Following the RBC Heritage, the next event on the PGA Tour calendar will be in the lead-up toward the PGA Championship.
