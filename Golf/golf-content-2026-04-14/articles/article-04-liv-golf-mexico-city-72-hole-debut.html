LIV Golf Goes 72 Holes for First Time at High-Altitude Mexico City

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 14, 2026

LIV Golf arrives in Mexico City this week for a tournament that is unlike any it has staged before. For the first time in the league's history, the event will be contested over 72 holes — a change from the 54-hole format that defined every LIV competition since the circuit launched.

The 2026 LIV Golf Mexico City event runs April 16-19 at Club de Golf Chapultepec, a historic course built at over 7,300 feet of elevation. The altitude at Chapultepec makes it one of the highest-profile tournament venues in North America, producing driving distances that would look extraordinary at sea level and approach shots that require significant adjustments in club selection.

Why 72 Holes Matters

The 54-hole format was among the most persistent criticisms directed at LIV Golf since it began competing. Critics argued that the shorter format did not provide enough of a sample size to reliably crown the most deserving champion, and that it set the league apart from the standard of the game's most meaningful events — all four majors, the Ryder Cup, and every traditional tour event play 72 holes.

Moving to 72 holes removes that distinction. Whether it changes the competitive dynamics of the league's events — and whether it shifts perception among players, broadcasters, and sponsors — is a longer-term question. For the players teeing it up at Chapultepec this week, it simply means an extra 18 holes at altitude, with all the stamina and strategy that implies.

The Field's Masters Hangover

All 10 LIV Golf players who competed in the 2026 Masters missed the cut at Augusta National. Phil Mickelson, Dustin Johnson, and Brooks Koepka — three of the league's most prominent names — are among those heading to Mexico City after an unsuccessful week at Augusta.

Mexico City has historically been a strong venue for LIV, drawing significant crowds and generating considerable local enthusiasm. The combination of the format change and the need to respond after a collective Masters disappointment gives this week a different kind of pressure for the league's biggest stars.

What's Next

The LIV Golf Mexico City event begins Thursday, April 16. The league's next events follow through the spring schedule as it builds toward the second half of the 2026 LIV season. The question this week is whether the first 72-hole LIV event produces a more definitive champion — and whether the result changes any of the larger conversations that have surrounded the circuit since its founding.
