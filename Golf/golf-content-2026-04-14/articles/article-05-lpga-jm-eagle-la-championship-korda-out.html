LPGA JM Eagle LA Championship: Korda Withdraws as Tour Eyes Chevron

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 14, 2026

The final LPGA Tour event before the first women's major of 2026 tees off this week in Los Angeles — and it will proceed without the sport's No. 2-ranked player in the world.

Nelly Korda has withdrawn from the JM Eagle LA Championship presented by Plastpro. The event runs April 16-19 at El Caballero Country Club in Tarzana, California, offering a $3.75 million purse to a 120-player field. The following week, the LPGA calendar delivers the Chevron Championship — the first major of the women's season — at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston.

Who's In the Field

Korda's absence creates a more open leaderboard picture heading into the week. Lauren Coughlin is the player entering with the most immediate momentum. She won the Aramco Championship at Shadow Creek in Las Vegas by five shots in what was the best victory of her career, and she arrives in Tarzana as one of the hottest players on tour.

Miyu Yamashita, the World No. 6 and 2025 AIG Women's Open champion, is in the field. So are Hyo Joo Kim (World No. 3), Minjee Lee (World No. 5), and Hannah Green (World No. 8). The absence of Korda does not diminish the quality of the week's competition — it simply redistributes where the pressure and expectation fall.

El Caballero Country Club

El Caballero Country Club is a private members' course in the San Fernando Valley, founded in 1957. The LPGA has held events in the Los Angeles area for decades, and El Caballero's tree-lined layout and relatively compact yardage reward accuracy and touch around the greens. In Southern California conditions, the course will play firm and fast, demanding precise iron play and confident putting.

The Chevron on the Horizon

For every player in the JM Eagle LA Championship field, this week functions as the final competitive preparation before the biggest event of the LPGA's early season. The Chevron Championship at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston (April 23-26) is the first major of 2026, and a player who finishes the JM Eagle LA Championship with high confidence will carry that energy into major week.

What's Next

The JM Eagle LA Championship first round begins Thursday, April 16, at El Caballero CC. The Chevron Championship follows the week after in Houston, beginning April 23. With Korda sidelined, the field at both events becomes more unpredictable — and the opportunity for a first-time or breakthrough major champion at the Chevron grows accordingly.
