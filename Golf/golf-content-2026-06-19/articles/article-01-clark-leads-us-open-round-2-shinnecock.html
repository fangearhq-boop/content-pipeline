Clark's Massive US Open Lead: Four Shots Clear Heading Into Round 2 at Shinnecock

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 19, 2026

Wyndham Clark had Shinnecock Hills figured out before most of his competitors had even finished their opening rounds. When play was suspended due to darkness Thursday evening, Clark was -6 through 16 holes — four shots clear of the field — holding what could be the largest US Open first-round lead since Tony Armour's five-stroke advantage in 1933.

Clark completed his final two holes Friday morning, then stepped directly into Round 2. The 2023 US Open champion has turned Shinnecock Hills into his personal stage for the 126th edition of the championship.

The Round 1 Story

Thursday was a complicated day at Shinnecock. Winds gusted to 35-40 miles per hour in the afternoon, and the USGA moved tee times up 30 minutes to try to get as many players through the morning wave before conditions worsened. Play was ultimately called due to darkness, creating an unusual situation where some players had finished and others had not.

Clark, who played in the morning wave, avoided the worst of the afternoon winds and made the most of the softer conditions. His -6 through 16 holes dwarfed most of the field. Behind him, Sam Stevens, Ryder Cowan (a), Max McGreevy, Matt Fitzpatrick, Dustin Johnson, and Gary Woodland all sat at -2 — four shots back.

Scottie Scheffler, the World No. 1 and defending Masters champion chasing a calendar Grand Slam, shot +2 (72). He battled through four birdies and four bogeys with a double bogey mixed in — a survival round, not a statement. He enters Round 2 eight shots behind Clark.

Rory McIlroy, the 2026 Masters champion seeking a second major this year, carded a -1 and sits T-9, five shots off the lead.

Clark's Resume at This Championship

Clark won the 2023 US Open at Los Angeles Country Club, and his game has evolved since that breakthrough. The former University of Colorado golfer is one of the better ball-strikers on Tour when conditions demand precision over power — exactly what Shinnecock asks for.

His experience at this championship matters. He knows how to protect a lead over the final two rounds of a US Open, having won here just three years ago. A four-shot cushion entering Round 2 at a US Open is not unassailable, but it is substantial. No one in the modern era has lost a US Open from this kind of early advantage.

What's Next

Round 2 is underway Friday at Shinnecock Hills. The cut line is expected to fall around even par or higher, given the difficulty of the course in this week's conditions. Rounds 3 and 4 are scheduled Saturday and Sunday — with the final round set for Father's Day, June 21, adding its own narrative weight given Scheffler's Grand Slam chase.

Clark's lead makes him the clear favorite. Whether it holds through the weekend is the story of the 2026 US Open.
