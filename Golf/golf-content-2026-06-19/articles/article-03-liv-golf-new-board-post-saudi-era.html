LIV Golf's New Board and the Post-Saudi Mission: Can the League Find a Future?

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 19, 2026

The Saudi chapter of LIV Golf is ending. The post-Saudi chapter has now officially begun — and it starts with a new independent board, a fundraising mandate, and a long list of unresolved questions about what the league looks like when the Public Investment Fund steps back.

Saudi Arabia's PIF confirmed earlier this year that it will end its funding for LIV Golf at the conclusion of the 2026 season. The fund has invested more than $5 billion into the league since its 2022 launch, absorbing estimated annual losses of $500 to $600 million per year. After four-plus years, the Saudis are redirecting their investment priorities.

The New Board

LIV Golf announced Thursday the formation of a new independent board. Gene Davis of the Pirinate Consulting Group serves as the board's first chair, joined by Jon Zinman of the strategic advisory firm JZ Advisors. The appointments signal a shift in governance structure — away from direct Saudi PIF oversight and toward an independent body designed to attract private investment.

Separately, LIV has engaged Ducera Partners, an investment bank specializing in restructuring and strategic advisory work, to lead a capital-raising effort. The league is reportedly seeking between $250 million and $350 million in new private funding. Commissioner Scott O'Neil has reportedly held five formal meetings with potential investors, with 18 more planned.

The Louisiana Postponement

The first visible consequence of the transition appeared this week with the postponement of LIV Golf's New Orleans event. The league's official explanation cited "peak summer heat and the crowded global sports calendar while ensuring the course is in championship condition." The explanation is plausible. The timing — coming amid confirmation of funding uncertainty — adds context that the league did not acknowledge directly.

LIV confirmed the PIF will fund all remaining 2026 events as planned. The league will complete this season. What happens in 2027 depends entirely on whether the Ducera-led fundraising effort succeeds.

What This Means for Players

LIV players signed multi-year guaranteed contracts — a hallmark of the league's aggressive recruitment strategy. Those contracts are funded through 2026. What happens to contractual obligations if new investment doesn't materialize in time is one of the league's most sensitive unresolved questions.

The broader golf ecosystem is watching carefully. If LIV secures significant new private investment, the question of a formal framework between LIV and the PGA Tour — which has been discussed but never resolved — returns to the table. If the fundraising effort falls short, the player reinstatement conversation accelerates.

What's Next

The 2026 LIV Golf schedule continues as planned through the end of the season. The UK event, one of LIV's most prominent stops, is still scheduled. Commissioner O'Neil's fundraising meetings continue. The league has six months to secure its post-Saudi future before the calendar turns to 2027.
