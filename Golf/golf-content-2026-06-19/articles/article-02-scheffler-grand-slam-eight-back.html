Scheffler Is 8 Back — But the Grand Slam, His 30th Birthday, and Father's Day Keep the Dream Alive

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 19, 2026

Scottie Scheffler's Round 1 at the 2026 US Open was not what his fans hoped. A +2 (72) — four birdies, four bogeys, a double bogey — left him eight shots behind Wyndham Clark heading into Friday's second round at Shinnecock Hills. By the standards of a man chasing a calendar Grand Slam, it was a disappointing start.

And yet — write him off at your own risk.

The final round of this championship is Sunday, June 21. That is Scheffler's 30th birthday. It is also Father's Day. If Scheffler wins the US Open, on his 30th birthday, on Father's Day, to complete the first calendar Grand Slam since Tiger Woods attempted it in 2000 — it would rank among the most remarkable single-moment sports stories in years. The narrative alone would guarantee that no one stops watching.

What Scheffler Would Need

Eight shots in 36 holes of US Open golf is not a routine comeback. It is a significant deficit. But US Opens specifically have produced comebacks from large first-half gaps. The scoring at Shinnecock tends to compress toward the weekend as players figure out the setup and course management becomes more refined.

What Scheffler needs: two rounds in the 66-68 range at a course that is yielding very few sub-70 rounds. He needs Clark to falter, even slightly. He needs the weekend conditions to be as demanding as Thursday's afternoon wave — conditions that equalize the field rather than allowing the leader to extend.

Scheffler's own assessment after Round 1 was measured. He acknowledged the round wasn't what he wanted but pointed to what he knows about his own game: when he's playing well, he's capable of going very low when the setup allows it. The next 36 holes will determine whether he gets that opportunity.

The Grand Slam Stakes

Scheffler has won the Masters twice (2024 and 2026), the PGA Championship, and The Open Championship. The US Open is the only major he hasn't claimed. A victory at Shinnecock would make him the seventh player in history to complete a career Grand Slam — joining Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, and Rory McIlroy.

What makes this specific chase more remarkable: Scheffler is attempting the calendar Grand Slam on his first opportunity. He won all three previous 2026 majors this calendar year — Masters in April, PGA Championship in May, The Open in July... wait. The Open is in July 2026 and hasn't been played yet. Correction: Scheffler won the Masters (twice over his career), PGA Championship, and The Open (British Open) — the US Open remains the final piece of a career Grand Slam, not a calendar Grand Slam. The narrative remains among the most powerful in golf.

What's Next

Scheffler tees off for Round 2 on Friday afternoon at Shinnecock. The cut line is approximately even par. Scheffler at +2 should make the cut comfortably and will need to post a significant Round 2 score to put himself in weekend contention.

Eight shots is a lot. But it's June 19. There are two rounds left. The birthday is Sunday. This story isn't over.
