Yan Liu Leads the Meijer LPGA Classic — Round 2 Underway at Blythefield CC

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 19, 2026

While all eyes are on Shinnecock Hills and the US Open, another compelling golf tournament is unfolding 800 miles west in Belmont, Michigan. The 2026 Meijer LPGA Classic for Simply Give is in full swing at Blythefield Country Club, and Yan Liu gave herself the inside track with a 6-under 66 on a cold Thursday to claim the first-round lead.

Round 2 is underway Friday as Liu looks to extend her advantage on a leaderboard the LPGA described as "jam-packed" after Day 1 — meaning the margins are tight and the competition for the $3 million purse is genuinely wide open heading into the weekend.

Liu's Opening Round

Liu's 66 was built on a standout stretch through the middle of her front nine: she made four consecutive birdies on holes 4 through 8, going out in 33 with only a single bogey to offset the run of gains. Her ball-striking and course management on the par-71 layout at Blythefield were consistent throughout, and she managed the cold conditions — an unusual factor for a June tournament in Michigan — better than most of the field.

The "jam-packed" leaderboard behind her means that any player who goes low on Friday could quickly close the gap. Liu will need to maintain her form in Round 2 to hold the overnight position.

Tournament Details

The Meijer LPGA Classic runs June 18-21 at Blythefield Country Club in Belmont, Michigan (Greater Grand Rapids area). The event has a $3 million purse and is one of the most fan-friendly events on the LPGA calendar. Father's Day falls on the final round this year — Sunday, June 21 — and Meijer is offering free admission for fathers, one of the tournament's longest-running traditions.

Blythefield is a classic parkland layout with tree-lined fairways and relatively accessible scoring conditions when the weather cooperates — explaining why Liu was able to reach -6 despite the cold on Thursday.

What's Next

Round 2 continues Friday at Blythefield Country Club. Rounds 3 and 4 follow Saturday and Sunday. The KPMG Women's PGA Championship — one of the five women's majors — follows immediately after this week, running June 25-28 at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota. Players finishing strongly at the Meijer Classic will carry momentum directly into major championship week.

Liu is the leader. The weekend at Blythefield starts now.
