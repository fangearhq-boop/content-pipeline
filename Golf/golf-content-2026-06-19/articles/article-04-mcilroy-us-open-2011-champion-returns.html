McIlroy Is Five Back and Right in It: The Case for Rory at Shinnecock

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 19, 2026

In 2011, Rory McIlroy won the US Open at Congressional Country Club by 16 shots. He finished 16 under par — 268 total — in a performance that announced him to the world as the next generational talent in golf. It remains one of the most dominant major championship performances of the modern era.

Fifteen years later, McIlroy is back at a US Open in a very different but no less compelling position. He sits T-9 at -1 after Round 1 at Shinnecock Hills — five shots behind leader Wyndham Clark, but well within realistic striking distance with two rounds remaining.

The 2026 Context

McIlroy arrived at Shinnecock as the 2026 Masters champion. His victory at Augusta earlier this year was one of the most anticipated wins of his career — a moment that had seemed perpetually out of reach given his multiple near-misses at The Masters over the years. When he finally won, the reaction across golf was a collective exhale.

A US Open victory this week would make McIlroy the first player to win two different majors in the same calendar year in the modern era (since Tiger Woods' 2000 season when he won US Open, The Open, and PGA Championship). It would also be McIlroy's second US Open title — his first in 15 years — and his fifth major championship overall.

Why Five Back Is Not Five Back

Five shots at a US Open with 36 holes remaining is a normal position for a genuine contender. US Opens — more than any other major — tend to produce compressed fields over the weekend. Scoring is difficult. Leaders falter. Players from well back regularly charge.

McIlroy's game is built for these conditions. He is an elite ball-striker whose distance and precision give him an advantage at courses like Shinnecock, where approach play from difficult lies and angles is rewarded. His experience in demanding major conditions — having won at Congressional, Hoylake (The Open), and Valhalla (PGA Championship) — puts him among the most versatile major champions in the field.

His Round 1 at -1 was disciplined. He's not in a hole. He's positioned to make a weekend move.

What's Next

McIlroy tees off for Round 2 on Friday at Shinnecock Hills. He needs a strong second round to put himself in contention for Saturday's moving day. If he can get to -5 or better through 36 holes, he'll be within striking range of whatever Clark shoots this weekend.

A 15-year gap between US Open victories, two different courses, one player. Don't count Rory McIlroy out.
