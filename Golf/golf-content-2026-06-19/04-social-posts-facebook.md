### STORY 3: LIV Golf — New Board, New Mission

#### Long-Form Post — 1:00 PM ET

LIV Golf has a new chairman and a new direction — but the same existential question: can the league find a future beyond Saudi Arabia?

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund confirmed it will end all funding for LIV Golf at the conclusion of the 2026 season. The PIF has invested more than $5 billion into the league since its launch, with annual losses estimated at $500 to $600 million per year. After five seasons of building the league, the Saudis are stepping back.

In response, LIV announced a newly formed independent board this week. Gene Davis of the Pirinate Consulting Group serves as chair, joined by Jon Zinman of JZ Advisors. Separately, investment bank Ducera Partners has been hired to lead the capital-raising effort — with the league reportedly seeking between $250 million and $350 million in new private investment.

The PIF will fund the remaining 2026 events as planned, so the league will complete this season. But the Louisiana event, originally scheduled for later this month, was postponed — officially due to "peak summer heat and the crowded global sports calendar," but the postponement adds to an atmosphere of uncertainty.

For players who signed multi-year guaranteed contracts, the question of what LIV looks like in 2027 and beyond is now very real.
