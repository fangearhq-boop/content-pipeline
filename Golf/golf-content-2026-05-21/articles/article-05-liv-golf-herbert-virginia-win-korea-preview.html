Herbert's LIV Golf Breakthrough: Virginia Win Opens New Chapter as Tour Heads to Korea

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 21, 2026

Lucas Herbert did not make the most dramatic entrance into LIV Golf. The Australian worked steadily, improved gradually, and competed without the fanfare that surrounded the circuit's biggest names. Then he went to Sterling, Virginia, and won by four shots.

Herbert's victory at Trump National Golf Club Washington D.C. was his first LIV Golf title, completed in commanding fashion with a final-round total of 24-under par 264. He finished four shots ahead of Sergio Garcia, who shot 3-under in the final round to take second. Bryson DeChambeau came in third.

A Four-Shot Win Is a Statement

Golf margins tell stories. A one-shot win can hinge on a single holed putt or a single missed green. A four-shot win, at a 72-hole event against a LIV Golf field that includes former major champions and former world No. 1 players, requires four days of consistent, high-quality golf that simply outperforms the competition.

Herbert had that. His final-round score maintained the gap he had built through the week rather than shrinking it — the characteristic of a player who closes when the pressure of the final day introduces the most risk. Garcia could not close the distance. DeChambeau, playing from further back, could not make up enough ground. Herbert won cleanly.

For a circuit that has been building its competitive depth through the 2026 season — now playing at 72 holes with an expanded 57-player field — a clear winner from outside the marquee tier is exactly the kind of result that reinforces the competition's legitimacy.

The LIV Virginia Field Context

DeChambeau's third-place finish continues a pattern of competitive presence without victory — he has been among LIV's most consistent performers without accumulating the wins his raw talent suggests he should have. Garcia, second in Virginia, remains one of LIV's most recognizable players and a former major champion with the game to win on any course.

Herbert's win over both of them, by four shots, is a career-defining result that will travel with him through the rest of the season. In LIV Golf's team and individual competition structure, a win of this magnitude raises a player's profile across both formats.

LIV Golf Korea: What's Next

The LIV Golf circuit moves to South Korea for its next event — LIV Golf Korea at Asiad Country Club in Busan, scheduled for May 28-31. It is a new venue for the tour, representing LIV's continuing international expansion and its first major visit to South Korea.

Asiad Country Club hosts a competitive field in an environment that will be new to most of the players. DeChambeau will be motivated after Virginia's third-place finish. Garcia will want to convert a recent strong showing into a win. And Herbert arrives in Busan with something new: the confidence and competitive identity that comes with having won.

What's Next

LIV Golf's international calendar continues to expand, and the Korea event represents one of its most significant new destinations. The results at Asiad Country Club will shape the individual leaderboard standings heading into the circuit's second half of the 2026 season.

For Herbert, Virginia is the starting point of something — not a one-off result to be filed away, but evidence that he belongs among LIV's genuine contenders. Golf rewards that kind of momentum. LIV Golf Korea: May 28-31, Asiad Country Club, Busan, South Korea.
