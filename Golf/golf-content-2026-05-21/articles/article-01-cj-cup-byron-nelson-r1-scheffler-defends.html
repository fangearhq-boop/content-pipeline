CJ Cup Byron Nelson Opens: Scheffler Seeks Bounce-Back Win at TPC Craig Ranch

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 21, 2026

Four days after a quiet tie for 14th at the PGA Championship, Scottie Scheffler returned to the course where he is most dangerous — TPC Craig Ranch in McKinney, Texas — to begin his defense of the CJ Cup Byron Nelson title.

The 2026 CJ Cup Byron Nelson opened Round 1 today with a $10.3 million purse and a field that, while altered by five post-major withdrawals following Aaron Rai's PGA Championship victory, remains among the strongest non-major events of the PGA Tour season. Scheffler, the world No. 1, was grouped alongside Brooks Koepka and Si Woo Kim for the opening round.

Scheffler's Familiar Ground

There is a category of player who performs at their best at courses they know deeply — courses where they have logged hundreds of practice rounds, accumulated meaningful competitive experience, and developed the kind of shot-by-shot library that allows instinct to override analysis under pressure. For Scheffler, TPC Craig Ranch is that course.

He won here in 2025. He lives and practices in the Dallas-Fort Worth area. The course architecture, the prevailing wind patterns, the putting surfaces — none of it is unfamiliar. When Scheffler steps onto Craig Ranch's first tee, he is not discovering the venue. He is returning to a place where his confidence and course knowledge converge.

That matters after Aronimink. Scheffler led after Round 1 at the PGA Championship and finished tied for 14th. The final-round magic he has produced in other high-pressure situations did not arrive when he needed it most. The Byron Nelson offers a different kind of opportunity: not the largest trophy in the game, but a reset with real prize money, tour points, and the psychological benefit of playing well on a course that rewards exactly what Scheffler does best.

A Changed Field

Five players who had been expected in the Byron Nelson field withdrew in the days following Aaron Rai's PGA Championship win. The withdrawal pattern following a major is typical — some players take the week off, others have schedule adjustments driven by exemption changes — but the specific number is notable. Rai's victory, as a 290-1 long shot, generated the kind of ripple effects that a more expected champion wouldn't have produced.

The field that remains is still high-quality. The CJ Cup Byron Nelson consistently attracts elite PGA Tour players because of its Texas location, strong purse structure, and timing in the schedule — a week after a major, when many top players want to stay competitive rather than rest. The players who chose to enter this week are here to play well, not to mark time.

What the Byron Nelson Could Tell Us

The week after a major is sometimes when you learn the most about the field's form heading into summer. Players who competed at Aronimink are either carrying positive momentum from a strong finish or processing the lessons from a disappointing week. Craig Ranch will show which is which quickly.

For Scheffler, the narrative is straightforward: world No. 1 returns to familiar territory after a quiet major defense. If the game is sharp, the results will show up on Thursday and Friday. The cut makes its judgment, and the weekend reveals who came to McKinney ready to compete.

What's Next

Round 2 continues the leaderboard build at TPC Craig Ranch on Friday. The weekend rounds on Saturday and Sunday will determine whether Scheffler walks away with back-to-back Byron Nelson titles or finds himself chasing someone with better form on a course he knows well.

At $10.3 million, there is no shortage of motivation in the field this week. CJ Cup Byron Nelson continues through Sunday May 24, TPC Craig Ranch, McKinney, Texas.
