### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET The PGA Tour's only team event is underway, and the hottest team in golf right now might also be the most entertaining storyline of the year: the Fitzpatrick brothers. Matt Fitzpatrick won the RBC Heritage last Sunday in a thrilling playoff over Scottie Scheffler, hitting a 4-iron to 13 feet and draining the birdie to claim his second Harbour Town title and his second victory in his last three PGA Tour starts. That win lifted him to a career-high World No. 3 ranking. Now, days later, he's at TPC Louisiana in New Orleans — this time partnered with his brother Alex — as the co-favorites at +1175 to win the 2026 Zurich Classic. Alex Fitzpatrick is no passenger. Known for his metronomic accuracy off the tee and sure touch around the greens, Alex complements Matt's ball-striking brilliance in what could be one of the best natural team pairings in the field. The other team turning heads: Brooks Koepka and Shane Lowry. Two close friends, Florida neighbors, and one unlikely partnership born over a winter practice session at Grove XXIII in Hobe Sound. Lowry was the one who made the ask. Koepka, fresh from four seasons on LIV Golf and now steadily rebuilding his PGA Tour career, agreed. "The way Shane drives the ball, the way my iron play has been lately, and then Shane's short game — I think it's a pretty good combination," Koepka said. Defending champions Andrew Novak and Ben Griffin are also in the field attempting something no team has done: back-to-back wins at the Zurich Classic. The $9.5M event runs through Sunday with alternate shot rounds on Friday and Sunday. Best ball today. #ZurichClassic | #PGATour

The 2026 Chevron Championship opened Thursday at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston, Texas — 6,455 yards at par-71, in only its second year at this historic venue. The $8M purse is among the richest in women's golf. The 132-player field includes 48 of the top 50 players in the world rankings. By any measure, this is elite women's golf at its highest level. The Memorial Park course suits players who can drive it far and rely on their short game — two areas where Korda excels. The full 72 holes of the Chevron Championship will be played over four days through Sunday, April 26. Coverage begins today on Golf Channel. #ChevronChampionship | #LPGA

Scheffler's Tough Run Continues — World No. 1 Reacts to Second Straight Runner-Up
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 10:30 AM ET No one is playing better golf this spring than Scottie Scheffler. And somehow, he doesn't have a win to show for it. The world No. 1 arrived at the Masters 12 shots back entering the weekend. He shot 66-66 over Saturday and Sunday, climbing all the way up the leaderboard, only to finish one shot behind Rory McIlroy's historic back-to-back Masters win. Then came last week at Harbour Town. Scheffler played beautifully over four rounds, forcing a playoff with Matt Fitzpatrick after a dramatic final-hole bogey by the Englishman. But on the first playoff hole, the 18th at Harbour Town Golf Links, Scheffler produced the worst swing of his week — fanning a 6-iron so far right that it finished 37 yards short of the green. Fitzpatrick stepped up and hit a 4-iron from 204 yards to 13 feet. Made the putt. Won the tournament. Scheffler's reaction afterward was everything you'd hope from the world's best player: gracious, honest and self-aware. "In both weeks, I put myself behind the 8-ball going into the weekend and had really nice Saturdays and Sundays in order to get myself into contention," he said. "On Sunday, it's a shot here or there that makes a difference. This was one of those weeks where any time Fitzy needed something to happen, he made something happen. He definitely earned the win, and he just played great golf." Scheffler is not playing the Zurich Classic this week. When he returns, the question everyone will be asking is simple: when does the world No. 1 start converting these near-misses? #PGATour | #ScottieScheffler

### Long-Form Post Window: 3:30 PM ET Four years on LIV Golf. Four seasons of Saudi-funded league play, away from the PGA Tour mainstream, away from the Majors (mostly), away from the weekly competition that defines tour life. Now Brooks Koepka is back. And the story of his return is more interesting than most people are letting on. In seven PGA Tour starts in 2026, Koepka has four top-20 finishes. His best result: a tie for 12th at the Masters. He's not winning yet — but he's trending in the right direction, building competitive rhythm after years away from the format. The frustrating moment came two weeks ago at the RBC Heritage, where Koepka was the first alternate and spent the entire week at Harbour Town Golf Links, waiting for a spot in the field that never opened. He watched the tournament from the outside, unable to compete in one of the signature events. This week in New Orleans, there are no waiting lists. Koepka is in the field — teamed with his close friend Shane Lowry — and he has a legitimate shot at the Zurich Classic title. Lowry recruited the partnership after Christmas, approaching Koepka during a winter practice session in Hobe Sound, Florida. The combination of Koepka's sharpening iron game and Lowry's accuracy off the tee could be difficult to beat in a best-ball format. Koepka is motivated. He wants to prove the return was worth it. #ZurichClassic | #PGATour | #BrooksKoepka
