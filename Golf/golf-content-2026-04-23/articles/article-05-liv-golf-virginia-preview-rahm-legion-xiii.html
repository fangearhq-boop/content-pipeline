LIV Golf Heads to Virginia — Can Anyone Stop Rahm and Legion XIII's Three-Peat Run?

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 23, 2026

Jon Rahm and Legion XIII have made a habit of winning as a team. Their victory at LIV Golf Mexico City last week — Rahm's second individual LIV win of 2026 — gave the team their third consecutive LIV team championship. The margin of victory in Mexico City was six shots. The final round was a 7-under 64 with no bogeys. When Rahm is playing like this, the rest of LIV Golf is competing for second.

Now the league takes aim at its next stop: LIV Golf Virginia, scheduled for May 7-10 at Trump National Golf Club Washington D.C. in Sterling, Virginia. It is one of the most recognizable venues on the East Coast, and the Virginia event has become one of LIV's marquee American stops.

Legion XIII's Dominance

Three consecutive team titles in LIV Golf is extraordinary. The format — which pits 13 teams against each other across a 72-hole stroke-play event with no cut — rewards consistency across all team members, not just the anchor player. Legion XIII has built a roster capable of contributing scoring at all levels of the leaderboard.

At Mexico City, the podium was entirely Spanish: Rahm at the top, followed by Sergio Garcia's Puig team members, and a third Spanish podium finisher completing the sweep. It was a nationalistic moment for Spanish golf, and a commanding statement of Legion XIII's depth.

The Bigger Picture

LIV Golf's 2026 season is its fifth. The league has settled into a format and schedule rhythm — 57 players, no cut, 72-hole events — and the new OKGC team (LIV's first team anchored in a U.S. state, representing Oklahoma) adds a new storyline to the competition this year. The league continues to attract elite talent and build its own internal rivalries, independent of the PGA Tour calendar.

Whether other teams can challenge Legion XIII in Virginia will be the story of the event. Rahm heads there as the individual standings leader and the tournament favorite.

What's Next

LIV Golf Virginia runs May 7-10 at Trump National Golf Club Washington D.C. in Sterling, Virginia. All LIV Golf events are broadcast on LIV Golf's own streaming platforms and select broadcast partners. With Rahm and Legion XIII riding a three-team-title wave, Virginia offers the rest of the LIV field their next opportunity to break through. For fans of spectacular golf in a team-sport setting, this is an event worth circling on the calendar.
