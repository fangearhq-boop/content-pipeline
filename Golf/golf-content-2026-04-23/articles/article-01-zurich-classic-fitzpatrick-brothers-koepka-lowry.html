Zurich Classic Opens: Fitzpatrick Brothers Are Team Golf's Hottest Act, Koepka-Lowry Partnership Adds Intrigue

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 23, 2026

Four days after Matt Fitzpatrick birdied the 18th at Harbour Town Golf Links to win the RBC Heritage in a playoff over Scottie Scheffler, the English star is back in a golf cart — this time with his brother Alex — at TPC Louisiana for the 2026 Zurich Classic of New Orleans.

The Fitzpatrick brothers enter the PGA Tour's only team event as the co-favorites at +1175 odds, which feels right. Matt is ranked World No. 3 after his second win in his last three starts. Alex, while less prominent in the rankings, is known throughout the game for his accuracy off the tee and a short game that makes him a natural complement to his brother's ball-striking excellence.

The Fitzpatrick Case

This partnership is built on genuine competitive compatibility. The best-ball format in rounds one and three rewards exactly the strengths both brothers bring: Matt's aggressive iron play can take risks while Alex provides the safe floor. In the alternate-shot rounds on Friday and Sunday, the ability to read each other intuitively — something only brothers can claim — becomes an advantage that's hard to quantify but very real.

Matt's confidence level right now is impossible to overstate. Fitzpatrick is playing some of the best golf of his career, and the Zurich Classic gives him the chance to add a team victory to his individual title from last weekend.

Koepka and Lowry — The Unlikely Partnership

The other team commanding attention in New Orleans is Brooks Koepka and Shane Lowry. This partnership was born over a winter practice session at Grove XXIII in Hobe Sound, Florida, when Lowry — who has been without a PGA Tour win since his Zurich Classic title two years ago — approached Koepka with the idea. The plan was confirmed a few weeks later at the Seminole Pro-Member.

Koepka, who returned to the PGA Tour after four seasons on LIV Golf, has been steadily building momentum. He has four top-20 finishes in seven Tour starts in 2026, including a tie for 12th at the Masters. He was the first alternate at the RBC Heritage and sat out the week without getting into the field.

The chemistry between these two is genuine. "The way Shane drives the ball, the way my iron play has been lately, and then Shane's short game, I think it's a pretty good combination," Koepka said. Two close friends, Florida neighbors and Ryder Cup opponents-turned-partners — it's the kind of story the Zurich Classic was built for.

What's Next

The $9.5M Zurich Classic runs Thursday through Sunday at TPC Louisiana. Rounds 1 and 3 are best ball; rounds 2 and 4 are alternate shot. The top 33 teams and ties make the cut after round 2. Defending champions Andrew Novak and Ben Griffin are in the field attempting a first-ever back-to-back Zurich Classic win. With the Fitzpatrick brothers riding a momentum wave and Koepka-Lowry providing the week's best subplot, New Orleans has everything a golf fan could want.
