Chevron Championship 2026 Tees Off — Korda Headlines LPGA's First Major at Memorial Park

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 23, 2026

Women's golf has arrived at its first major of 2026. The Chevron Championship opened Thursday at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston, Texas, with 132 of the world's best players teeing it up over 6,455 yards of par-71 golf — and all eyes on Nelly Korda.

Korda, the world No. 1, entered the week as the +500 favorite. She teed off Thursday at 2:39 PM ET alongside defending champion Mao Saigo and Lilia Vu in the day's most anticipated featured group. For Korda, a Chevron Championship title in 2026 would be another affirmation of her dominance over the women's game in the mid-2020s.

The Stage and the Stakes

The Chevron Championship carries the full weight of major championship prestige. The $8M purse — one of the richest in women's golf — reflects the tournament's stature. The field of 132 includes 48 of the top 50 players in the world rankings, meaning virtually every major name in women's golf is represented.

Memorial Park is in only its second year hosting the event. The Houston municipal course's combination of demanding par-4s and reachable par-5s rewards driving distance and precision with the irons — precisely the skill set that separates the best women's players from the rest of the field. The greens at Memorial Park are among the most challenging on the LPGA calendar, with subtle breaks that can deceive first-time champions.

Korda's Challenge

Korda's game is purpose-built for this style of test. She hits it long off the tee for the women's game, attacks pins with her irons, and putts with the consistency that comes from years of high-pressure competition. The five-to-one odds reflect a genuine belief in the women's golf community that Korda is not just the favorite — she's the expected champion.

Korda's playing companions in the opening round add drama. Saigo, the defending champion, knows what it takes to win at Memorial Park. Vu has shown the form to challenge any week on tour. Round 1 of the Chevron Championship is already delivering the pairings fans wanted to see.

What's Next

The 2026 Chevron Championship runs Thursday through Sunday, April 26. Coverage airs on Golf Channel throughout the week. Round 1 sets the early leaderboard; any player within three or four shots of the lead after 18 holes will remain very much in contention through the weekend. The Chevron Championship winner earns major champion status and the largest purse in LPGA Tour history to date. By Sunday afternoon in Houston, we will know who steps forward to claim it.
