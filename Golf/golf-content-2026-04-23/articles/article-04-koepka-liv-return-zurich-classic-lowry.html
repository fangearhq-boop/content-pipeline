Brooks Koepka's Long Road Back: Four Seasons on LIV, Four Top-20s on Return — New Orleans Is His Moment

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 23, 2026

Brooks Koepka spent four seasons on LIV Golf — four years away from the weekly PGA Tour competition that defines professional golf's mainstream. Now he is back. And the comeback story is more compelling than the cynics expected.

In seven PGA Tour starts in 2026, Koepka has accumulated four top-20 finishes. His best result was a tie for 12th at the Masters. The numbers suggest a player who has retained his elite competitive instincts and is steadily finding his Tour rhythm again. The wins haven't come yet — but the building blocks are clearly in place.

The RBC Heritage Wait

The most revealing moment of Koepka's return came not in the heat of competition, but on the sidelines. At the RBC Heritage at Harbour Town Golf Links — a signature event — Koepka was listed as the first alternate. He traveled to South Carolina, arrived at the course, and waited the entire week for a spot in the field that never materialized. He watched Matt Fitzpatrick win a playoff over Scottie Scheffler without having thrown a single shot at the leaderboard.

That experience sharpened his focus. New Orleans, where every entrant gets a spot, represents exactly the opportunity that RBC Heritage denied him.

The Lowry Partnership

The choice of Shane Lowry as his Zurich Classic partner reflects the relationships that professional golf cultivates across team events and Ryder Cup competition. Lowry approached Koepka during a winter practice session at Grove XXIII in Hobe Sound, Florida — they are close friends and neighbors in the Sunshine State. The idea was finalized at the Seminole Pro-Member weeks later.

Koepka sees the partnership clearly: "The way Shane drives the ball, the way my iron play has been lately, and then Shane's short game, I mean, I think it's a pretty good combination." Lowry, who won the Zurich Classic two years ago, knows exactly how to navigate TPC Louisiana. Koepka brings the firepower.

What's Next

The $9.5M Zurich Classic runs Thursday through Sunday at TPC Louisiana. For Koepka, this isn't just another tournament — it's the best chance he has had to prove his PGA Tour return is progressing on schedule. A Zurich Classic victory would make the comeback story impossible to dismiss. Round 1, best-ball format, is underway today. The question is whether Koepka's tournament game has returned to its championship level. New Orleans may give us the answer.
