Haeran Ryu Shot a 60 in a Major. Then She Won in a Playoff. South Korea's Star Has Redefined the LPGA Summer.

By Marcus Cole | July 15, 2026

The Evian Championship win completes a stretch of back-to-back LPGA major titles in three weeks, anchored by the lowest single-round score in the history of major championship golf for women.

Sixty.

In the third round of the Amundi Evian Championship at the Evian Resort Golf Club in France, Haeran Ryu of South Korea shot an 11-under 60. That number — 11 under par in a single round of a major championship — had never been reached before in the history of LPGA major championship golf. The lowest single-round score ever recorded in a women's major belonged to her before Sunday arrived.

Then she had to win the tournament.

The Round That Changed the Tournament

Ryu's third-round 60 at Evian-les-Bains was not a slow accumulation of pars and birdies. It was a sustained assault on a golf course that plays along the shores of Lake Geneva — a setting that on a calm summer day can be absolutely destroyed by a player in the kind of form Ryu found herself in on Saturday. The 11-under total put her in a position that should have been untouchable. It was not, quite.

Brooke Henderson matched Ryu's tournament total of -19 through the final round, forcing a sudden-death playoff. In that playoff, Ryu birdied the first hole to claim the Evian Championship title and the $1.4 million winner's share. The drama that followed the round of 60 added something to a performance that was already historically significant: she had to hold her nerve after making history, and she did.

Back-to-Back Majors in Three Weeks

The Evian Championship was Ryu's second major victory in three weeks. She had won the KPMG Women's PGA Championship in the weeks prior, establishing herself as the dominant player on the LPGA Tour at this point in the summer. Back-to-back majors in professional golf — any tour, any level — is a level of sustained excellence that is almost impossible to produce. Ryu produced it.

There is always the question of what comes next after a stretch like this. The LPGA's next major, the AIG Women's Open at Lytham St. Annes, is scheduled for July 30. The field will arrive knowing exactly who is playing the best golf in women's professional golf right now. That knowledge doesn't necessarily translate into stopping her, but it will shape how the tournament is framed and discussed.

What's Next

The AIG Women's Open on July 30 is the next opportunity for Ryu to add to a summer that is already historically significant. The ISPS HANDA Women's Scottish Open the week before (July 23-26 at Gailes) will be a tune-up for a player who has established a pattern of performing when the stakes are highest. South Korean golf has long been a force in the women's game; Haeran Ryu's summer of 2026 is one of the strongest individual runs in recent LPGA history.
