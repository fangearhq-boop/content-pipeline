Tom Kim's Scottish Open Win Ends Nearly Three Years Without a Victory — at the Best Possible Moment

By Ryan Calloway | July 15, 2026

The 23-year-old South Korean closed with a bogey-free 64 at The Renaissance Club to win at -17, ending a winless stretch that had lasted since late 2023 and doing it the week before The Open Championship.

Nearly three years without a win is a long time in professional golf. Long enough to generate questions. Long enough for the comparisons to your early career to feel like a distant memory. Long enough to wonder, even briefly, whether the player you were is still in there.

Tom Kim answered those questions at The Renaissance Club last Sunday.

The South Korean ended his winless run by closing with a bogey-free final round of 64 at the Genesis Scottish Open in North Berwick, Scotland. He finished at -17 total, two strokes clear of Min Woo Lee, and won a $9 million co-sanctioned event in front of a field built around the players heading to The Open Championship a week later. The timing was as good as it gets.

What the Win Represents

Kim burst onto the PGA Tour in late 2022 as one of the most exciting young talents the game had seen in years. He was fearless, creative, and seemed to have the kind of game that does not require modification to compete at the highest level from an early age. Then the wins stopped coming, as they sometimes do for players adjusting to the demands of full-time tour competition, and the follow-up question to a brilliant debut became the loudest thing in the room.

A bogey-free final round at a major championship week event — 64 strokes, 18 holes, no mistakes when the tournament was there to lose — is the most convincing possible answer to that follow-up question. It suggests that the adjustment period is over and the player who arrived so dramatically a few years ago has found a more sustainable version of himself.

Reports emerged that Tiger Woods reached out to Kim to congratulate him after the victory. That kind of acknowledgment from the most significant player in the sport's history is not something that happens routinely. It says something about how the golf world views what Kim accomplished.

The Field He Beat

The Genesis Scottish Open draws a strong field because of its position on the calendar — immediately before The Open — and because of its co-sanctioned PGA Tour and DP World Tour status. Matt Fitzpatrick, Robert MacIntyre, and Keita Nakajima were all among the players finishing at -13, four shots behind Kim. This was not a weak field finding a winner by default. It was one of the better players in the world executing better than everyone else on the week that mattered most to him.

What's Next

Whether Kim's momentum carries from the Scottish Open into The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale is a question the next several days will answer. A player coming off a win with the specific kind of confidence that a dominant final round produces can be a dangerous element in any major field. The drought is over. The 23-year-old is back in the conversation — and the conversation is happening at exactly the right moment.
