Open Championship Preview: Everything You Need to Know Before the 154th Major Begins at Royal Birkdale

By Marcus Cole | July 15, 2026

From the defending champion to the hometown hero to the man chasing history, the field at Royal Birkdale offers every storyline golf fans could want as the final major of the European swing begins Thursday.

The 154th Open Championship begins tomorrow, and Royal Birkdale is ready for it.

The historic links in Southport, Merseyside, England plays to 7,223 yards at par 70. The relatively flat fairways are framed by towering sand dunes that channel the wind in ways that transform the course from manageable to brutal within the span of a single weather change. In links golf, the wind isn't a variable — it's the primary factor. The player who reads it best across four days tends to find themselves holding the Claret Jug on Sunday afternoon.

This year's field brings together the defending champion, a player chasing the European major record, a hometown favorite who has been waiting his entire professional life for this week, and one of the deepest collections of talent major golf typically assembles. Here is what to know before the first groups tee off Thursday morning.

The Defending Champion

Scottie Scheffler arrives as the defending Open champion — he won at Royal Portrush in 2025 — and enters at +700 odds despite an unusual stumble at the Scottish Open last week, where he missed the cut. That ended an extraordinary run of 78 consecutive events without a missed cut, dating back to 2022, and it generated questions about his form heading into Birkdale.

Those questions are probably overblown. Elite players miss cuts occasionally; it does not mean their games have suddenly deteriorated. The more significant point is that Scheffler has had additional preparation time at Royal Birkdale in the days since — a form of course-specific work that opponents who played in Scotland did not get.

The Record Chaser

Rory McIlroy enters at +850 with six major championships and a chance to win a seventh — which would make him the European man with the most major victories in the sport's history. He won back-to-back Masters titles in 2025 and 2026, completed his career Grand Slam with the first of those wins, and has not won The Open since 2014. His T7 at the Scottish Open suggests good form without the kind of dramatic week-before performance that sometimes comes with excess pressure attached.

The Hometown Story

Tommy Fleetwood (+1500) is the week's most compelling narrative. Ranked No. 3 in the world and widely regarded as the best player in professional golf without a major title, Fleetwood grew up in Southport — the same town where Royal Birkdale sits. His father is on the bag. No English-born player has won The Open Championship since Nick Faldo in 1992, a 34-year absence that the British golf public has been waiting to end.

Fleetwood skipped the Scottish Open to spend focused preparation time at Birkdale. He knows this course in a way his opponents cannot replicate.

What's Next

First groups tee off approximately 1:30 AM ET on Thursday morning. Scattered showers are in the forecast for the week, which adds the weather variable that links golf specializes in producing. By Sunday evening, someone will have added their name to the Claret Jug. This week is why golf exists. Pay attention.
