The Man Who Almost Shot 59: Si Woo Kim's Week at the Byron Nelson Has Been Something Special

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 24, 2026

Si Woo Kim stood on the 18th tee at TPC Craig Ranch on Friday afternoon having done something that very few professional golfers have ever done. He had played 17 holes at 12-under par. One more birdie — just one — would have given him a 59.

Kim made bogey instead. He walked off the 18th green with a 60.

That is the week Si Woo Kim is having at the CJ Cup Byron Nelson. The kind of week where shooting 60 feels like leaving something on the table, where a round that would be the achievement of a lifetime for most professionals is described, even by the player himself, in terms of what nearly happened on the final hole.

The 60 That Almost Wasn't

The numbers behind Kim's Round 2 are extraordinary. His 60 was the lowest round of the tournament week. It was a course record at TPC Craig Ranch. It was also a CJ Cup Byron Nelson tournament record for a single round — erasing the previous mark and establishing a new standard for scoring at a venue that has hosted professional golf since 2021.

His 36-hole total of 17-under par after two rounds set the tournament record for the lowest 36-hole score in CJ Cup Byron Nelson history. By the end of Friday, Kim had separated himself from the field so comprehensively that the conversation at the top of the leaderboard was less about who was leading and more about how far ahead he was.

The 59 — a score achieved only a handful of times in PGA Tour history in the modern era — would have been historic. Bogey on 18 kept it from happening. But what Kim did through 17 holes on Friday afternoon was a reminder of what his best golf looks like: precise, inventive, almost effortless in its execution.

Ball-Striking at Its Purest

Kim has long been regarded as one of the purest ball-strikers in professional golf. His ability to shape shots under pressure, to work the ball both ways with the same compact, repeatable swing, has set him apart from peers who may generate more power but cannot match his precision from fairway to green. At TPC Craig Ranch this week, that ball-striking has been operating at its highest level.

He opened with a 64 in Round 1 — steady, controlled, a round that put him in contention without announcing itself as the prelude to something remarkable. Round 2 was the announcement. Twelve under through 17, 60 on the card, a course record that will stand until someone else does something extraordinary on this track. Round 3 brought a 68 — three birdies, no bogeys, a maintenance round that held his advantage and kept him in control.

Three rounds of 64-60-68. Twenty-one under par. A two-shot lead heading into Sunday's final round.

The Weight of Three Years

What makes this week carry additional weight is context. Si Woo Kim's last PGA Tour victory came at the Sony Open in Hawaii in January 2023. That is more than three years ago. In the time since, Kim has remained a presence in tournaments — inside top-25s, the occasional top-10, weeks where his ball-striking was evident but the final result didn't arrive. A three-year winless stretch for a player of his ability is not a reflection of decline. It is a reflection of how difficult it is to convert opportunities in professional golf, even for players who create more of them than most.

Sunday at TPC Craig Ranch is an opportunity. The biggest one Kim has had since Hawaii. He enters the final round as the leader, with a two-shot cushion over Scottie Scheffler and Wyndham Clark — two of the best players in the world — and with the kind of momentum that can only come from a week played at an exceptional level.

He nearly shot 59. He settled for 60. He leads the tournament. The final round will tell us whether this week ends the way the first three rounds have suggested it might — with Si Woo Kim lifting the CJ Cup Byron Nelson trophy and ending a drought that has lasted far too long for a player this good.
