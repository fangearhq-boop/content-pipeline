Byron Nelson Final Round: Kim Leads by Two Over Friends Scheffler and Clark at TPC Craig Ranch

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 24, 2026

The final round of the 2026 CJ Cup Byron Nelson began this afternoon with a storyline that golf rarely produces: the top two players in the field are close friends playing in the same pairing, competing for the same title.

Si Woo Kim entered Sunday at 21-under par, holding a two-shot lead over Scottie Scheffler and Wyndham Clark at TPC Craig Ranch in McKinney, Texas. Kim and Scheffler — longtime friends who share membership at Royal Oaks Country Club in Dallas and practice together regularly — were grouped together for the final round, teeing off at 1:40 PM ET. Clark, also at 19-under, went out at 1:30 PM ET with Stephan Jaeger, setting an early target number for the leaders to chase.

The dynamic at the top of the leaderboard is unusual. Scheffler and Kim know each other's games intimately. They know each other's tendencies under pressure, their shot shapes, their patterns around a golf course. The question on Sunday is not who plays better golf in the abstract — it is which relationship with pressure and consequence the final round brings out.

Scheffler Closing In

Scottie Scheffler made his move in Round 3. The World No. 1 and defending Byron Nelson champion shot a 6-under 65 on Saturday — seven birdies, one bogey — to erase what had been a four-shot Kim advantage at the start of the day and arrive within two. Scheffler on a Sunday, trailing by two shots, is not a comfortable margin to hold. The best closer in professional golf has done exactly this before: taken a deficit into a final round and turned it into a title before the back nine is completed.

A second Byron Nelson title would add to Scheffler's growing collection of repeat victories at venues that have suited him, and it would serve as a reminder that even the most dominant stretches of individual play — like the one Kim has been on this week — are difficult to convert when the defending champion is just two back and hungry.

Clark as the Wild Card

Wyndham Clark enters the final round with an advantage and a disadvantage. The 2023 U.S. Open champion is two shots behind Kim but playing in the group ahead — meaning he can set a total that Kim and Scheffler must respond to. If Clark fires a low round and posts a score on the board, it puts immediate pressure on the final pairing before they reach the back nine. Clark's ability to string birdies together on a scoring course like TPC Craig Ranch makes him a genuine threat to steal the tournament even from that position.

What's Next

The CJ Cup Byron Nelson winner will be determined this afternoon. The Charles Schwab Challenge begins tomorrow at Colonial Country Club in Fort Worth — just 30 miles west — keeping the Texas golf spotlight burning without pause.
