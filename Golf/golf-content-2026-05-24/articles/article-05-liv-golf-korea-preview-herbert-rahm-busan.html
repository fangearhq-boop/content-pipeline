LIV Goes to Korea: Herbert, Rahm Headline Busan Event This Week

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 24, 2026

LIV Golf arrives in Asia later this week, and it is doing so in a country with deep institutional knowledge of the game. The circuit's inaugural Korea event takes place May 28-31 at Asiad Country Club in Busan, South Korea — the league's first tournament on Korean soil and its most significant foray into the Asia-Pacific region since the league began competitive play.

South Korea has produced elite professional golfers at a remarkable rate for decades. The country's players have been among the most consistent performers on the LPGA Tour, the PGA Tour, and in major championship competition. Bringing LIV Golf to Korea for the first time is a market play, but it is also a recognition that this is a country where the sport carries genuine cultural weight.

Herbert in Front, Rahm in Pursuit

Lucas Herbert leads the LIV individual standings heading into Busan. The Australian won the Virginia event wire-to-wire — a commanding performance that put him atop the standings and established him as the player the rest of the field is chasing. Herbert at his best is a precise, controlled golfer whose game translates across different types of venues and conditions. He arrives in Korea with the standings lead and the momentum that comes from a dominant wire-to-wire victory.

Jon Rahm is the most dangerous pursuer. The Spaniard claimed his first LIV title at the Mexico City event earlier this month, a six-shot victory that was about as dominant as a win can be in professional golf. Rahm arriving hot off a blowout win at a new venue is a combination that any course and any field has to respect. He has the game, the confidence, and — now — the LIV win that removes any remaining questions about whether he can compete for titles on this circuit.

The standings battle between Herbert and Rahm gives the Busan event a central storyline. Two players chasing the same target, with very different games and very different profiles, competing on Asian soil for the first time. That is compelling regardless of the broader context around LIV Golf.

A New Market

For LIV Golf's leadership, the Korea event represents a significant test. The league has established its broadcast footprint and ticket demand in several markets — the United States, the United Kingdom, and parts of the Middle East have all hosted LIV events with varying degrees of local engagement. Asia is a different challenge. The Asia-Pacific region has its own deeply established professional golf ecosystem, including the Asian Tour, which has a formal relationship with LIV Golf.

Busan is a city of considerable size and sophistication, and Asiad Country Club is a well-regarded venue. The question is not whether world-class golf can be played there — it clearly can — but whether LIV's broadcast reach and local promotional infrastructure can generate the kind of engagement in Korea that makes the event commercially meaningful for the league's long-term ambitions in the region.

The presence of players like Rahm, Herbert, and the rest of the LIV field gives the event genuine quality. Whether that quality translates into the broader market success LIV is pursuing in Asia will be watched closely over the coming week.
