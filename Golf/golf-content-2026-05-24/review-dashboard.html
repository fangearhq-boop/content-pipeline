### Long-Form Post Sunday's final round at the CJ Cup Byron Nelson has every ingredient for an unforgettable finish. Si Woo Kim enters the day at 21-under par, holding a two-shot lead over Scottie Scheffler and Wyndham Clark. Kim and Scheffler will play together in the final pairing at 1:40 PM ET at TPC Craig Ranch in McKinney, Texas — and there is a remarkable wrinkle to this particular head-to-head: the two men are close friends who practice together at Royal Oaks Country Club in Dallas. They know each other's game perhaps better than anyone else on Tour. Scheffler is the defending champion of this event. He made seven birdies and a bogey in Round 3 to shoot 65 and pull to within two shots of Kim heading into Sunday. Wyndham Clark — the 2023 U.S. Open champion — also sits at 19-under and will go off with Stephan Jaeger at 1:30 PM ET, potentially setting a target number for the final pairing to chase. For Kim, this week has been about redemption. His last PGA Tour victory came at the 2023 Sony Open in Hawaii — a drought that has stretched more than three years for a player whose talent has never been in question. The final round will tell us whether this is the week it ends. ### Image Post Caption Si Woo Kim leads the Byron Nelson by 2 at -21. Scottie Scheffler and Wyndham Clark are right behind at -19. Si Woo Kim's Incredible Week — The Story Behind the -21 Lead
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post To understand why Si Woo Kim's final-round opportunity at the CJ Cup Byron Nelson matters so much, you have to understand what he did in Round 2. Kim arrived at the 18th tee on Friday having played 17 holes at 12-under par. A birdie on the final hole would have given him a round of 59 — one of the rarest scores in professional golf history, a mark achieved only a handful of times on the PGA Tour in the modern era. Instead, Kim made bogey. He finished with a 60. That 60 was still the lowest round of the week. It was a course record at TPC Craig Ranch. It was a tournament round record for the CJ Cup Byron Nelson. And it gave Kim a 36-hole total of 17-under, which at the time established the 36-hole record for the event. He followed it with a 68 in Round 3 — a steadier day, three birdies and no disasters — to reach 21-under and lead by two. On the week, Kim has driven the ball brilliantly, hit his irons with the precision that has always set him apart from his peers, and putted well enough to make the most of his approach play. Kim's last PGA Tour win came in January 2023 at the Sony Open in Hawaii. He has been a consistent presence in tournaments without converting, a familiar pattern for players of his ability who simply haven't found the right week. This is the right week. Sunday will tell us if it is also the right ending. ### Image Post Caption Si Woo Kim nearly shot 59 in Round 2. He settled for 60 — still a course record. Three rounds of 64-60-68. Charles Schwab Challenge Preview — Colonial Opens Tomorrow
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post One of the most storied venues in professional golf is ready for its 80th consecutive year as a PGA Tour host. The Charles Schwab Challenge begins tomorrow at Colonial Country Club in Fort Worth, Texas — making it the longest uninterrupted venue-to-event relationship in PGA Tour history. Since 1946, Colonial has hosted professional golf's best players every spring, earning the nickname "Hogan's Alley" after Ben Hogan, the Fort Worth native who won the event five times and remains the club's most celebrated champion. Jack Nicklaus, Arnold Palmer, Lee Trevino, Ben Crenshaw, Tom Watson, and Phil Mickelson have all had their names inscribed on Colonial's Wall of Champions. The 2026 edition features a $9.9 million purse and a field headlined by Ludvig Aberg and Justin Thomas — two of the most compelling players in the world game. Seven of the top 20 players in the Official World Golf Rankings have committed to play. Ben Griffin, the defending champion, returns to the venue where he claimed his most significant PGA Tour title and will look to do so again on one of the game's most demanding tracks. Colonial's back nine — particularly the closing three holes known as the "Green Mile" (16, 17, and 18) — has ended countless title bids and created just as many champions. The course demands length, accuracy, and nerve in equal measure. Beginning tomorrow and running through May 31. ### Image Post Caption Colonial Country Club. 80 years. "Hogan's Alley." The Charles Schwab Challenge starts TOMORROW. Aberg, Thomas, and Griffin headline a $9.9M week in Fort Worth.

US Women's Open Preview — Riviera Country Club, June 4-7
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post There is a title that Nelly Korda has never won. And that absence has become one of the most compelling ongoing stories in professional golf. The 81st U.S. Women's Open Championship Presented by Ally takes place June 4-7 at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California. It is the USGA's flagship women's event and one of the most prestigious titles in the game. Korda, the three-time LPGA winner in 2026 and the world's No. 2 ranked player, is expected to be among the favorites when tee times are set. She has come close. At the 2025 U.S. Women's Open at Erin Hills in Wisconsin, Korda finished two shots behind winner Maja Stark — her best result in 11 appearances at the championship. The putter was not her friend in the final rounds at Erin Hills, a mental note she will carry to Riviera. Riviera Country Club is a very different test. The course, designed by George Thomas and Billy Bell and opened in 1927, combines tight fairways with tricky greens and punishing rough. It rewards precise shotmaking and patience — qualities Korda possesses in abundance. If her putter finds its form, she will be there on Sunday afternoon. Korda has already demonstrated this year that she can perform under major championship pressure. Three wins in 2026 across a variety of conditions and fields have underlined why she is considered the most complete player in the women's game. June 4 cannot come soon enough for those waiting to see if she can finally add the U.S. Women's Open to a resume that already reads like a Hall of Fame case. ### Image Post Caption Nelly Korda: 3 wins in 2026, World No. 2. LIV Golf Korea — Four Days Away, Herbert Leads Standings
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post LIV Golf arrives in Asia later this week. The circuit's inaugural Korea event takes place May 28-31 at Asiad Country Club in Busan, South Korea — the league's first-ever tournament in a country that has produced generations of elite golfers and holds some of the deepest institutional knowledge of the sport in the Asia-Pacific region. Lucas Herbert leads the LIV individual standings heading to Busan, having won the Virginia event wire-to-wire. Jon Rahm — who claimed his first LIV title at the Mexico City event earlier this month with a dominant six-shot victory — is the most dangerous pursuer in the standings and arrives with the kind of momentum that makes him formidable regardless of venue. For LIV Golf, Korea represents a significant market opportunity. The country's passion for golf is well-documented, and the presence of world-class players competing on Korean soil for the first time under the LIV banner will test whether the league's broadcast footprint and ticket demand can gain traction in Asia at the same level it has achieved in its established markets. The standings battle between Herbert and Rahm gives the event an internal storyline. And in a field that includes most of the LIV regulars, the Busan setting provides a genuinely fresh backdrop for a circuit that has expanded its international reach with each passing season. ### Image Post Caption LIV Golf lands in South Korea this week.
