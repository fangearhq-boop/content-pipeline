### Long-Form Post The financial story surrounding LIV Golf has taken a serious turn — and it's one that every golf fan should understand. LIV Golf is no longer funded by direct capital investment from Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund. Instead, the PIF filed a debenture on June 4, 2026 — a legal document that secures a loan against LIV Golf's assets. The league is, in short, borrowing money to operate, with PIF acting as the lender rather than the investor. Reports indicate that PIF has provided approximately $200 million of the roughly $600 million it had committed to fund the remainder of the 2026 campaign. LIV Golf spends an estimated $100 million per month. That math means the money runs out quickly. Four events remain on the 2026 LIV schedule: LIV Golf UK at JCB Golf & Country Club (July 23-26), LIV Golf New York (August 6-9), LIV Golf Indianapolis (August 20-23), and the $40 million LIV Team Championship in Michigan (August 27-30). LIV's CEO has stopped short of guaranteeing all four events will take place. The league has been seeking PE investors and alternative funding sources. Without a resolution, the question hanging over every LIV event is whether the next one happens at all.
