Tommy Fleetwood at Royal Birkdale: A Hometown Major, a Father on the Bag, and a 34-Year English Drought

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 16, 2026

The Southport native is playing the Open Championship in his backyard, with his father caddying, chasing a first major title and hoping to end the longest gap in English golfing history.

There are moments in sport where every element conspires toward a single, perfect narrative possibility. Tommy Fleetwood at Royal Birkdale in the 2026 Open Championship is one of those moments.

Fleetwood was born and raised in Southport, England. Royal Birkdale Golf Club is located in Southport — the links course he has known, in some sense, his entire life. This week, his father is on his bag as caddie. The home gallery will cheer him on every fairway. No English player has lifted the Claret Jug since Sir Nick Faldo's victory in 1992 — a 34-year drought that has outlasted careers, survived generations of English talent, and haunted the game's most storied major.

The Homecoming

Fleetwood's decision to skip the Scottish Open and dedicate his preparation specifically to Royal Birkdale underlines how seriously he is treating this opportunity. While other contenders played at the Scottish in the week before, Fleetwood was at Birkdale — walking its fairways, practicing its rough lies, reading its greens, and absorbing the atmosphere of the course he calls home.

That kind of preparation can cut both ways. It is either the advantage that only a hometown player can manufacture, or it is the weight of expectation made heavier by intimate familiarity. Every misread putt, every ball in the rough, every bogey carries extra meaning when the course is your backyard. Fleetwood understands that better than anyone.

He entered the week at approximately +1500 odds — substantial enough that a victory would represent one of the more significant upsets in recent major championship history, but not so long that his name is absent from serious conversations. Paul McGinley, the Ryder Cup captain who has watched Fleetwood closely for years, has expressed confidence in his chances.

His Father on the Bag

The presence of Fleetwood's father as his caddie this week adds an emotional dimension to an already extraordinary story. Caddying at a major championship requires technical knowledge — reading greens, choosing clubs in wind, managing yardages on firm links turf — but it also means being the person your player looks to when the round gets difficult. Having a father fulfil that role at the Open Championship, in Southport, is the kind of detail that writers rarely feel comfortable inventing because it seems too perfectly constructed.

It is real. And it is happening today.

What's Next

Fleetwood plays in a group with Jordan Spieth and Jon Rahm — a pairing that will attract enormous galleries and generate some of the day's most electric atmosphere. His Round 1 score at Royal Birkdale will set the tone for his week. A strong opening round, played in front of a home crowd that has waited years for exactly this moment, could carry him all the way to Sunday. The 34-year English wait may be ending. Or it may continue. But today, in Southport, England, Tommy Fleetwood gets his shot.
