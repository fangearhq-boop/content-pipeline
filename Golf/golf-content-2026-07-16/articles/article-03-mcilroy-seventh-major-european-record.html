Rory McIlroy at The Open: Seven Majors, a European Record, and the Claret Jug He Hasn't Lifted Since 2014

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 16, 2026

The six-time major champion and back-to-back Masters winner tees off at Royal Birkdale in the afternoon wave chasing history — and his first Open title in 12 years.

In the long, remarkable career of Rory McIlroy, today stands as something genuinely significant. The Northern Irishman enters the 154th Open Championship with six major championships — his historic four-major run in his mid-twenties, plus back-to-back Masters titles at Augusta National in 2025 and 2026. He is the co-favorite at approximately +700-850 odds alongside defending champion Scottie Scheffler.

A seventh major would mean something beyond the number itself. It would give McIlroy the outright record for the most major championships won by a European golfer, breaking the tie he currently shares with Sir Nick Faldo. It would place him in a category that transcends tours and eras — a player who reshaped his sport's history at its most storied championship.

Twelve Years Since Hoylake

McIlroy's last Open Championship victory came at Royal Liverpool (Hoylake) in 2014. He won by two strokes, producing a controlled, masterful performance on links terrain that showcased every dimension of his game. In the twelve years since, he has had opportunities — near-misses, contender weekends that didn't convert — without adding to his Claret Jug collection.

The 2026 Open represents a different version of McIlroy than the player who was trying to complete the career Grand Slam with pressure that threatened to overwhelm him in earlier years. The 2025 Masters victory — and its 2026 follow-up — appeared to unlock something in McIlroy's relationship with major championship pressure. He plays now like a man who understands how to win the biggest events, not just how to contend.

Royal Birkdale suits his game. The course rewards driving accuracy and iron precision — two of McIlroy's enduring strengths. The coastal wind that defines links golf at Birkdale requires creativity and shot-shaping that a player of McIlroy's skill executes as naturally as breathing.

The Record in Reach

Nick Faldo won six major championships across a career that spanned two decades: three Masters titles and three Open Championships. His record has stood as the European benchmark for decades. McIlroy has matched it. Now he plays in the Faldo's territory — The Open Championship — with the opportunity to surpass it.

The historical dimension of the challenge does not appear to weigh on McIlroy the way earlier majors pressures did. He tees off in the afternoon wave at 15:15 BST with Xander Schauffele and Matthew Fitzpatrick — a group capable of contending for the full four rounds. The gallery at Birkdale will follow them closely, aware of exactly what is at stake.

What's Next

Round 1 sets the foundation. McIlroy's history at Birkdale, his current form coming off back-to-back Masters titles, and his demonstrated ability to handle major championship pressure make him one of the most compelling figures in Thursday's field. By Thursday evening, the Open Championship leaderboard will begin to reveal which contenders have seized the moment — and whether McIlroy is among them. Seven awaits.
