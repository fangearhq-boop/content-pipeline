LIV Golf Is Running on Loans to Finish the 2026 Season — and Four Events Hang in the Balance

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 16, 2026

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund has shifted from investor to lender, filing a debenture against LIV's assets while providing only a third of committed capital, leaving the league's final four events uncertain.

The financial model that was supposed to reshape professional golf is showing serious cracks. LIV Golf is not being funded this season through capital investment from Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — it is being funded through loans. The PIF filed a debenture on June 4, 2026, a legal instrument that secures a loan against LIV Golf's assets, converting the relationship from patron to creditor.

Reports indicate that the PIF has provided approximately $200 million of the roughly $600 million it originally committed to fund the remainder of LIV's 2026 operations. The league spends an estimated $100 million per month. That arithmetic creates an immediate and obvious problem.

Four Events, One Question

LIV Golf's 2026 schedule has four tournaments remaining: LIV Golf UK at JCB Golf and Country Club in Staffordshire (July 23-26), LIV Golf New York (August 6-9), LIV Golf Indianapolis (August 20-23), and the $40 million LIV Team Championship in Michigan (August 27-30). The league's CEO has publicly stopped short of guaranteeing that all four events will take place — a significant and sobering admission from a tour that launched with promises of transforming the sport.

LIV expanded to 72-hole events in 2026, bringing itself into alignment with traditional major tour formats. The UK event at JCB Golf and Country Club returns for the third consecutive year, and previous editions drew strong crowds and reportedly generated tens of millions of dollars in local economic impact. But local enthusiasm cannot substitute for organizational solvency.

The Investor Search

LIV has been actively seeking new investors — private equity firms, alternative capital sources, and other sovereign wealth interests — to supplement the Saudi funding that has defined its existence since launch. So far, no new investment has been publicly announced. Reports have circulated about potential PE involvement, but no deal has materialized.

The debenture structure is not inherently a sign of imminent collapse — companies use debt financing routinely. But combined with the CEO's unwillingness to guarantee all remaining events, the admission that committed capital has not been fully deployed, and the ongoing search for new investors, the picture that emerges is one of meaningful financial uncertainty.

What's Next

LIV Golf UK at JCB is less than two weeks away. If that event proceeds — and there is no indication yet that it will not — it will represent a temporary resolution of the most immediate funding question. The more consequential issue is whether additional capital arrives to fund New York, Indianapolis, and the Team Championship. For the players who built their careers on LIV's promises of financial security, the answers to those questions are deeply personal and professionally important. The summer of 2026 is a critical test of whether LIV Golf survives beyond it.
