Open Championship Round 1: Scheffler Charges to Early Lead at Royal Birkdale While McIlroy, Fleetwood Wait in the Wings

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 16, 2026

Defending champion Scottie Scheffler moved to -4 early in the 154th Open Championship, with the afternoon wave — including Rory McIlroy and Tommy Fleetwood — still to play.

The 154th Open Championship is underway at Royal Birkdale, and the game's best player is reminding the field why he arrived as the favorite to win back-to-back Claret Jugs. Scottie Scheffler moved to -4 in the early stages of Round 1, his progress highlighted by a remarkable 43-foot putt at the sixth hole that drew enormous applause from galleries lining the Southport seaside links.

Matthew Baldwin hit the first tee shot of the championship at 6:35 AM local time. By mid-morning, a leaderboard was beginning to take shape, with Thomas Detry of Belgium, Alex Smalley, and Dan Brown staking their claims at -3 from the morning wave.

The Morning Wave

Scheffler is playing alongside England's Tyrrell Hatton and Bryson DeChambeau — a group with combined major experience that has produced some of the most dramatic moments in recent championship history. Scheffler's early birdie burst is consistent with the form that has made him the world's No. 1 player and defending champion at this event.

The conditions at Royal Birkdale are typical for a links championship: firm fairways that run the ball beyond what parkland courses allow, thick rough that punishes wayward drives, and a coastal breeze that can change direction between morning and afternoon, creating meaningfully different challenges for tee times separated by just a few hours.

Thomas Detry, the Belgian who has steadily built a reputation as a links specialist, was among the early clubhouse leaders. Dan Brown carded a birdie from 20 feet at the 12th hole to join the front group. Alex Smalley, a consistent ball-striker, completed an impressive morning round.

The Afternoon Wave to Come

The marquee groups tee off in the afternoon. Rory McIlroy — chasing a seventh major championship that would give him the outright European record — plays alongside Xander Schauffele and Matthew Fitzpatrick at 15:15 BST. Tommy Fleetwood, the Southport native playing for his first major title on his home course, goes out with Jordan Spieth and Jon Rahm.

The afternoon conditions at Royal Birkdale have historically been either similar to or slightly firmer than the morning — but in British Open conditions, nothing is guaranteed. The afternoon tee times carry their own wind patterns, shadows, and psychological weight that make reading conditions on links courses as much art as science.

What's Next

Round 1 concludes Thursday evening local time, setting up Friday's Round 2 and a 36-hole cut that will decide who plays the weekend at Birkdale. Scheffler's early surge sets the tone for what the course is yielding on a good day — and the players who follow must decide how aggressively to pursue the red numbers. The Open Championship is a four-day test. But Round 1 at Birkdale has started with drama, and there is a great deal more golf still to be played.
