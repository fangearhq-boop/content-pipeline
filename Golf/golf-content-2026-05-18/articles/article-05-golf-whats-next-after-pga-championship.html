What's Next in Golf — US Women's Open, McIlroy's US Open Window, and the Road Ahead

By Ryan Calloway | May 18, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Aaron Rai has left Aronimink Golf Club with the Wanamaker Trophy. The 108th PGA Championship is over. And the golf world — which spent the weekend absorbed in major championship drama — now pivots to the next set of tournaments on the calendar.

The schedule between now and the end of June is dense with significant events, including two majors and some of the most important events of the women's and men's tours. Here is what's coming, and why each event matters.

LPGA ShopRite Classic — May 29-31

The next LPGA Tour event is the ShopRite LPGA powered by Wakefern, running May 29-31 at the Bay Course at Seaview, A Dolce Hotel in Galloway, New Jersey. The event is one of the Tour's established stops in the Northeast, offering a relatively short course that typically produces low scores and abundant birdie opportunities.

It arrives two weeks after Charley Hull's dominant victory at the Kroger Queen City Championship in Cincinnati, where Hull closed with a -20 total to claim her title by one shot. The LPGA Tour features some of the world's deepest and most competitive talent in women's golf, and the ShopRite Classic will bring the field back into view after the PGA Championship dominated the golf conversation this week.

US Women's Open — June 4-7

The major everyone in women's golf circles for is the US Women's Open, which arrives at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California from June 4-7. The US Women's Open is the most prestigious major in women's golf, and Riviera is one of America's most celebrated venues — a classic design with a history of testing the best players in the game.

Nelly Korda, who has been one of the dominant forces in women's golf in recent years, will be among the contenders. So will Charley Hull, coming off her Queen City victory with confidence. The international field brings depth and quality that makes any result possible.

Riviera at a US Women's Open is a combination that should produce compelling golf across all four rounds. Low scoring may be difficult. The classic design rewards precision over power, and the rough at a US Open set-up penalizes mistakes in ways that the regular Tour calendar rarely does.

McIlroy's US Open Window

For Rory McIlroy, the next appearance at a major championship following the PGA Championship brings a specific emotional charge. The US Open is an event he has won before, and one where his driving length and iron precision translate well to the course designs typically chosen for the championship.

He will arrive without the calendar Grand Slam pressure that followed him to Aronimink, but the career Grand Slam conversation — completing the set with a PGA Championship win — will remain in the background until he accomplishes it. The US Open gives him a chance to win a fifth major title, which would deepen his already extraordinary legacy.

What's Next

The ShopRite LPGA Classic begins May 29 in Galloway, New Jersey. The US Women's Open runs June 4-7 at Riviera Country Club. The men's Memorial Tournament also runs June 4-7 at Muirfield Village in Dublin, Ohio. The US Open follows in June. For golf fans, the next four to six weeks represent some of the most compelling competition of the 2026 season, with major championships on both the men's and women's sides driving the narrative forward. Aaron Rai started his week at 290-1. The next winner of a major championship is out there right now — and we don't know who it is yet.
