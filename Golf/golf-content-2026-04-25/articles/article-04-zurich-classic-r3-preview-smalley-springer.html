Zurich Classic R3 Preview: Smalley and Springer Lead by One as Best Ball Delivers Drama

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

After two rounds of alternate shot and best ball at TPC Louisiana, the 2026 Zurich Classic of New Orleans has produced exactly the kind of leaderboard that makes this tournament one of the most compelling weekends on the PGA Tour schedule. Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer lead at 16 under par, but three teams sit just one stroke behind — and Saturday's Round 3 in best ball format is where scoring typically explodes.

Smalley and Springer were the story of Round 1, matching the Zurich Classic tournament record with a 14-under 58 in best ball format — 12 birdies and an eagle, a performance that announced their contention in the most emphatic way possible. Their alternate shot 70 on Friday was more restrained but sufficient to hold the lead entering the weekend. One shot is a marginal advantage in a best ball round where any of the three teams behind them can catch fire at any time.

The Challengers

The Fitzpatrick brothers are the most dangerous of the three teams at 15 under, and arguably the most dangerous team in the entire field. Matt Fitzpatrick — World No. 3, winner of the RBC Heritage last week — gives the pairing an individual ceiling that few players on Tour can currently match, and their chemistry as brothers translates into a best ball partnership with genuine upside. They opened Thursday's best ball round with a 64, and there is no reason to expect they cannot match or improve on that total on Saturday.

Davis Thompson and Austin Eckroat present a different kind of threat — a partnership built on complementary games rather than sibling familiarity, with enough individual quality to keep the scoreboard honest. Billy Horschel and Tom Hoge round out the top tier, with Horschel's competitive experience in big moments adding value to a pairing that has been consistent across both rounds.

Why Best Ball Saturday Could Be Special

TPC Louisiana is a course that rewards aggressive play, and best ball format removes the penalty for mistakes — if one player finds trouble, their partner's ball remains in play. That dynamic tends to lower scores significantly compared to alternate shot, which is precisely why the third-round leaderboard almost always looks different from what entered the weekend. A team that was lurking at 14 under could be 18 under by Saturday evening.

With $9.5 million on the line and Sunday's alternate shot finale still to come, the pressure will build steadily through the day. The Zurich Classic has produced its share of dramatic Sunday moments, and the tightness of the current leaderboard suggests 2026 will continue that tradition.

What's Next

Round 3 tees off Saturday at TPC Louisiana in New Orleans. The format is best ball, and teams will be looking to press as hard as possible before Sunday's alternate shot finale requires a different kind of discipline. The team that leads after 54 holes will have a significant advantage going into Sunday — alternate shot tends to compress scoring and protect leads — making Saturday's result all the more consequential. Watch for the Fitzpatricks in particular, whose best ball ceiling may be the highest in the field.
