Fitzpatrick Brothers Shoot 65 in Alternate Shot, Move to T2 at Zurich Classic

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

They came into Friday's alternate shot round six shots back and looking like a team that needed something special. They delivered exactly that. Matt and Alex Fitzpatrick shot a 7-under 65 in the most demanding format in team golf, climbing from the middle of the leaderboard to a share of second place at 15 under — one shot behind leaders Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer at the Zurich Classic of New Orleans.

The round at TPC Louisiana was a masterclass in alternate shot execution. Six birdies and an eagle, with only one bogey — a forgiveness-free statline in a format that amplifies every mistake. The signature moment came on the par-5 18th, where Matt Fitzpatrick chipped in for a closing birdie that capped the round and sent the crowd reacting to a shot that encapsulated the Fitzpatricks' week perfectly: composed, precise, and timely.

A Brotherly Partnership Built for This Moment

Matt Fitzpatrick is World No. 3 and arrived in New Orleans fresh off winning the RBC Heritage at Hilton Head just last week. His younger brother Alex is a capable professional in his own right, and their partnership carries a natural understanding that team pairings assembled from friendships or sponsor obligations cannot replicate. Two brothers playing alternate shot — where one player hits every other shot — benefit from a shared instinct about how each other moves around a golf course.

The results speak to that familiarity. After a solid opening 64 in Thursday's best ball round, the Fitzpatricks' 65 in alternate shot is one of the most impressive performances of the tournament. The format typically sees scores creep upward — mistakes are penalised immediately and recovery options are limited. The Fitzpatricks went the other direction.

The Leaderboard After 36 Holes

Smalley and Springer, who opened with a record-tying 58 in best ball, shot a 70 in the alternate shot round to hold at 16 under. Three teams sit at 15 under: the Fitzpatricks, Davis Thompson and Austin Eckroat, and Billy Horschel and Tom Hoge. It is as tightly bunched a leaderboard as the Zurich Classic regularly produces, and with best ball returning for Round 3 on Saturday, the potential for scoring to jump is significant. The tournament is very much alive for all four teams at the top.

What's Next

Round 3 is Saturday at TPC Louisiana in the best ball format — the same format in which the Fitzpatricks opened with a 64 to establish themselves as genuine contenders. If they can replicate or improve on that best ball performance, they will have genuine opportunities to lead or share the lead entering Sunday's alternate shot finale. Matt's current form — No. 3 in the world, a win last week at Hilton Head — gives the pairing a ceiling that few teams in the field can match. The question is whether they can keep executing at the level they have through 36 holes.
