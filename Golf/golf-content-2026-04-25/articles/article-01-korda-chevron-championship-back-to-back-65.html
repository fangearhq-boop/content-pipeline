Korda Shoots Back-to-Back 65s, Leads Chevron Championship by Seven

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

Nelly Korda arrived at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston as the favorite to win the first major of the LPGA season. After two rounds of the 2026 Chevron Championship, she has done nothing to disturb that expectation. Korda shot a second consecutive 65 on Friday to reach 14 under par — extending her lead to seven shots and placing herself in a position of dominance that the field has yet to find a way to challenge.

The round was a tour de force of ball-striking on a course that played long and difficult after overnight rain soaked the Memorial Park layout. Korda hit 11 of 13 fairways, found 14 of 18 greens in regulation, and needed just 24 putts. She birdied four of the five par 5s on a course where length off the tee translates directly into scoring opportunity — and Korda has plenty of length. Her average drive covered 268 yards on the day.

She began her second round with a long putt to save par and then turned it on progressively, making back-to-back birdies on the 11th and 12th holes before closing strongly on the final stretch. The consistency of the performance — never threatened by a big number, always finding a way to convert when the opportunity presented itself — reflects a player at the peak of her capabilities.

The Pursuit

Ryann O'Toole played herself into second place with a 68 on Friday, reaching 7 under for the tournament — seven shots back of Korda. Amateur Farah O'Keefe is alongside O'Toole at 7 under. The gap is enormous, but 36 holes of golf remain, and Memorial Park is capable of producing big numbers on a good day. For any challenger to have a chance, Korda would need to stumble in a way she has given no indication of doing.

The Chevron Championship is a $9 million event and one of the five LPGA majors. For Korda, winning here would add another chapter to a career that has already included major victories. The way she has navigated these two rounds — bogey-free in Round 1, relentlessly efficient in Round 2 — suggests she is not merely playing well. She is playing at a level that the rest of the field cannot currently match.

What's Next

Rounds 3 and 4 take place Saturday and Sunday at Memorial Park. With a seven-shot lead and the course suiting her game, Korda enters the weekend in the most comfortable position any player can occupy in professional golf. Her challengers will need to find significant improvements in their own games while hoping Korda has an uncharacteristic off day. Neither is impossible — but given what the world's best female golfer has produced through 36 holes, neither is particularly likely.
