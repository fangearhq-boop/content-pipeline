Koepka and Lowry Miss Zurich Classic Cut by One Stroke — Bogeys on Par 3s Prove Costly

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

The numbers were close enough to be painful. Brooks Koepka and Shane Lowry finished the 2026 Zurich Classic of New Orleans at 9 under par on Friday evening — one stroke shy of the 10-under cut line that would have sent them to the weekend. For one of the most anticipated team pairings in the tournament's recent history, the end came too early and by too small a margin to feel like anything other than a hard-luck exit.

The story of their elimination was written in the par 3s. Friday's alternate shot round at TPC Louisiana produced five birdies for Koepka and Lowry, which would have been sufficient in many circumstances. But bogeys on the third hole and the 17th hole — both par 3s — cancelled the progress they had made and left them stranded one stroke outside the weekend field.

Where the Week Went Wrong

Koepka and Lowry had never been as comfortable in Thursday's best ball round as the pairing's credentials suggested they should be. Koepka, a four-time major champion, managed just one birdie on the day; it was Lowry who carried much of the scoring burden with five birdies on the front nine before stalling coming home. The pair finished at 6 under (66) and entered Friday's alternate shot needing a strong performance just to reach the cut, let alone contend.

Their Friday round showed what the partnership can do when it fires — five birdies across the course demonstrated enough chemistry and skill to suggest the pairing was not misguided. But in alternate shot, where one player's errant stroke immediately becomes the other's challenge, the par-3 bogeys were unrecoverable. TPC Louisiana's par 3s have historically been among the most punishing on the PGA Tour, and even the most experienced professionals can find them unforgiving.

The Pairing That Could Have Been

Koepka and Lowry formed their team at the Grove XXIII club in Hobe Sound, Florida — a private retreat favoured by some of golf's biggest names. Their friendship and shared sensibility about the game made the pairing feel natural, and there was genuine excitement in the golf community about watching them compete together at a tournament that rewards exactly the kind of partnership they embody.

Golf, as it so often reminds even the best players in the world, is indifferent to storylines. The Zurich Classic demanded 10 under to reach the weekend. Koepka and Lowry shot 9 under. They will have to return another year to find out what this pairing might look like over four rounds.

What's Next

For Koepka, the attention will shift to the next major on his calendar and the ongoing rhythm of a season that has included a T12 finish at the Masters and a brief appearance at the RBC Heritage earlier this month. For Lowry, the missed cut will sting, but the Irishman's form across the week — particularly his front-nine scoring in best ball — showed enough to suggest he remains competitive. The Zurich Classic continues this weekend without them; the final result will go to teams that held together when the pressure of alternate shot demanded precision.
