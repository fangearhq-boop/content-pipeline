LIV Golf Virginia Preview: Rahm Arrives With Momentum After Mexico City Dominance

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

Jon Rahm does not need extra motivation. But the way he finished at LIV Golf Mexico City — a bogey-free, 7-under 64 in the final round, a six-shot victory, his Legion XIII team claiming their third consecutive team title — would motivate anyone. Two weeks later, he and his team head to Northern Virginia for an event that consistently delivers one of LIV Golf's most enthusiastic crowds of the season.

LIV Golf Virginia runs May 8-10 at Trump National Golf Club Washington D.C. in Sterling, Virginia. The venue, situated roughly 20 miles from the nation's capital, draws a strong local following and has established itself as one of the league's flagship stops. For LIV, events at locations with strong regional golf communities help make the case for the league's place in the sport's ecosystem, and Trump National D.C. qualifies emphatically.

Rahm's Case for Dominance

Rahm's Mexico City performance was as commanding as any LIV Golf victory the league has seen. His final-round 64 put to bed any competitive questions — a six-shot margin leaves no room for debate about who deserved to win. His individual triumph also powered Legion XIII's third straight team title, a consistency of collective performance that has made the Spanish-themed roster one of the most formidable in the league.

At Virginia, he arrives as the defending champion of his most recent LIV victory and as a player who has appeared virtually unbeatable when at his best in 2026. Whether the course layout at Trump National suits his game as well as Mexico City remains to be seen — but form like this tends to travel.

The Challengers

Joaquin Niemann of Torque GC is expected to be among the challengers, having won the LIV Golf Virginia individual title in 2025. The Chilean has been one of LIV's most consistent performers, and defending titles on known venues suits his methodical game. Bryson DeChambeau and Crushers GC will be looking to reclaim the team title they won at Virginia a year ago — DeChambeau's power game at a course that rewards length could be decisive over four rounds on the weekend.

The broader field at LIV Virginia includes the full league roster, and the depth of competition LIV has assembled — Rahm, Niemann, DeChambeau, and others — ensures the event will be contested at the highest level.

What's Next

LIV Golf Virginia tees off May 8-10 at Trump National Golf Club Washington D.C. in Sterling, Virginia. The event format includes individual and team scoring, with standings from both contributing to the league's overall competition. For Rahm and Legion XIII, Virginia is an opportunity to build further separation in the team standings. For Niemann, DeChambeau, and the rest of the field, it is a chance to slow that momentum. After Mexico City, Rahm will be the player to beat.
