Korda's Back-to-Back 65s Put Her 7 Clear at Chevron Championship
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET The question entering the 2026 Chevron Championship was whether Nelly Korda could handle the pressure of being the world's best player on the game's biggest stage. Through two rounds at Memorial Park in Houston, she has answered with a resounding yes. Korda shot a second consecutive 65 on Friday to reach 14 under par, extending her lead to seven shots — a margin that speaks less to anyone else's failures than to the sheer quality of her golf. She hit 11 of 13 fairways, found 14 of 18 greens in regulation, and needed just 24 putts on a rain-soaked course that played long and difficult for most of the field. She birdied four of the five par 5s and, crucially, never let any difficult stretch of holes derail the round. Ryann O'Toole shot a 68 to join amateur Farah O'Keefe at 7 under for the tournament — tied for second and seven back. The gap between Korda and the field is not just a score-line; it is a statement about where she is in her career and what this championship means to her. Korda has won this event before, and the way she has moved through Memorial Park this week has the feel of a player who belongs here. Two rounds remain. But at 14 under with a seven-shot cushion, the Chevron Championship already feels like Korda's to lose. #GolfFanrecap | #LPGA

Fitzpatrick Brothers Storm to T2 With 65 in Alternate Shot — One Back at Zurich
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 10:30 AM ET If the Fitzpatrick brothers were feeling the pressure of being six shots behind entering Friday's alternate shot round at the Zurich Classic, they did a remarkable job of hiding it. Matt and Alex Fitzpatrick — the older brother a three-time PGA Tour winner and current World No. 3, the younger a promising professional in his own right — produced what may have been the round of the tournament through 36 holes. Their 7-under 65 in the alternate shot format included six birdies, an eagle, and just one bogey, with Matt rolling in a chip-in birdie on the par-5 18th hole as the signature moment of an outstanding performance. The result moved them from well off the pace to a share of second place at 15 under — just one stroke behind leaders Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer, who shot a 70 in the alternate shot round to hold at 16 under. Davis Thompson/Austin Eckroat and Billy Horschel/Tom Hoge are also at 15 under, making for an exceptionally tight leaderboard heading into Saturday's best ball Round 3. The Fitzpatricks have proven they can compete in every format. Their path to the title runs through one of the most competitive Saturday rounds TPC Louisiana is likely to see. #GolfFanrecap | #PGATour

Koepka and Lowry Miss the Cut — Zurich Classic Heartbreak for High-Profile Team
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 1:00 PM ET The storyline wrote itself — two major champions, close friends, a partnership formed on the putting greens of an exclusive Florida club, teaming up to chase one of golf's most unique team titles at the Zurich Classic of New Orleans. The only thing missing was the ending the story deserved. Brooks Koepka and Shane Lowry missed the cut by a single stroke on Friday, finishing the tournament at 9 under after the cut fell at 10 under. Their alternate shot round on Friday featured five birdies — enough in most circumstances — but bogeys on the par-3 third and par-3 17th proved decisive. One stroke separated their week from the weekend. The pairing had been built with genuine excitement. Koepka's four major championships and Lowry's Open Championship title gave the team enormous credentials, and their friendship added a natural chemistry that many team pairings lack. But chemistry and credentials only carry you so far when the cards don't fall your way. Koepka had just one birdie in Thursday's best ball round, and Lowry's strong front nine on Friday stalled coming home. It is a difficult exit from a tournament they had every reason to believe they could contend in. The Zurich Classic is one of golf's most genuinely fun events, and Koepka and Lowry will almost certainly be back. This year's story, though, ends here. #GolfFanrecap | #PGATour

Zurich Classic R3 Preview — Smalley/Springer Lead by 1 as Best Ball Resumes
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 3:30 PM ET Four teams. One shot separating them. And Saturday's format is the one that typically sends scores flying. The Zurich Classic of New Orleans returns to best ball format for Round 3 on Saturday, and the leaderboard after 36 holes could not be more conducive to a compelling day at TPC Louisiana. Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer hold the lead at 16 under par, but three teams sit just one shot behind at 15 under: Matt and Alex Fitzpatrick, Davis Thompson/Austin Eckroat, and Billy Horschel/Tom Hoge. The Fitzpatricks may be the most dangerous challengers of the three. Matt is World No. 3 and won the RBC Heritage at Hilton Head just last week. In best ball format — where both players play their own ball and the team takes the lower score — his ceiling is exceptionally high. Alex adds the kind of steady presence that keeps the team from giving shots back. Thompson and Eckroat have also been consistent across both rounds, and Horschel's experience in big moments makes their team dangerous too. Smalley and Springer, for their part, know they are leading and will need to find a gear in best ball to hold off the charge. With $9.5 million on the line and Sunday's alternate shot finale still to come, Saturday shapes up as one of the most compelling days of the PGA Tour season. #GolfFanrecap | #PGATour

LIV Golf Virginia Preview — Rahm Returns as Defending Mexico City Winner
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 6:00 PM ET Jon Rahm is not a man who lacks for motivation. But if you needed proof of it, consider this: he closed the LIV Golf Mexico City event with a bogey-free 7-under 64, won by six shots, and immediately turned the conversation to what comes next. What comes next is LIV Golf Virginia, scheduled for May 8-10 at Trump National Golf Club Washington D.C. in Sterling, Virginia — one of the tour's most popular stops and a venue that draws a competitive, engaged crowd from the Washington metropolitan area. For LIV, it is a flagship event. For Rahm, it is another opportunity to extend what has been a dominant stretch of form. Legion XIII, Rahm's team, claimed their third consecutive team title in Mexico City — a statement of consistency that transcends any individual result. The format of LIV, where team points matter alongside individual standings, has given Rahm and his roster a sense of collective purpose that clearly motivates the entire squad. The field in Virginia will include Joaquin Niemann of Torque GC, who won the individual title at Virginia in 2025, and Bryson DeChambeau's Crushers GC, who claimed the team championship at the event a year ago. Both will be looking to defend. Rahm will be looking to keep his momentum rolling. And the fans in Northern Virginia will get to decide who deserves to be called the best in LIV Golf right now. #GolfFanrecap | #LIVGolf
