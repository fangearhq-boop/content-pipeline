**Long-Form Post:**

Haeran Ryu heads into Sunday's round with a one-shot lead at 11-under par after shooting a four-under 68 on Saturday that included three birdies and an eagle on the front nine. Brooke Henderson is one back at 10-under. Ina Yoon is two back at 9-under, and Nelly Korda is four shots behind the leader at 7-under par.

Four shots. Eighteen holes. A $13 million record purse on the line at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota. And at the center of the conversation: whether Korda can do something only two women in history have ever done.

Korda already won the 2026 Chevron Championship and the 2026 US Women's Open. A KPMG title would make her just the third woman to win three consecutive LPGA major championships — joining Babe Zaharias (1950) and Inbee Park (2013). She would also reach 27 Hall of Fame points, one short of eligibility. She is in the field. She is four back. It can be done.

Ryu, however, is not a passive leader. The South Korean played some of the finest golf of her career on Saturday and has earned the right to defend her lead. She knows what is chasing her — and she is focused on winning the tournament, not protecting a number.

### STORY 3: The Open Championship Preview — Mickelson Out, Record Crowd at Royal Birkdale

**Long-Form Post:**

The 154th Open Championship tees off at Royal Birkdale in Southport, England on July 16 — and already the storylines are writing themselves.

Phil Mickelson has withdrawn from the championship following new allegations that he showed an explicit photograph to the wife of a fellow golf professional. Tiger Woods did not file his entry by the deadline and will not compete. Two of the sport's most prominent figures will be absent from one of golf's oldest stages.

That absence comes against a backdrop of record demand. R&A CEO Mark Darbon announced that attendance is projected to exceed 300,000 for the week — which would break the all-time record for The Open. Over one million ticket applications were received. Royal Birkdale, with its famous dunes and punishing rough, will host the largest crowd in championship history.

The 156-player field has 117 exemption competitors already confirmed, with the final spots still to be determined through qualifying. The R&A also debuted a new "Last Chance Qualifier" format for 2026 — twelve players will compete over 18 holes on Monday of Open week for the final available spot in the field. Final Qualifying was held at four venues: Burnham & Berrow, Dundonald, Royal Cinque Ports, and West Lancashire.

Scottie Scheffler arrives as defending champion, having won the 2025 Open. He will be among the favorites when the field is complete.

### STORY 4: LIV Golf Running on Loans — Four Events Left, Future Unclear

**Long-Form Post:**

LIV Golf is not running on ambition right now. It is running on loans.

Reporting this week confirmed that the breakaway tour is relying on borrowed financing to complete its 2026 schedule, with Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund having confirmed its backing only through the end of the current season. The PIF has invested more than $5 billion in LIV since its launch — only $200 million of the $600 million needed for continued operations has reportedly been received from alternative sources as the tour seeks outside investment in the $250-350 million range.

The Louisiana event earlier this month became the public face of these troubles. The state had invested $7 million in infrastructure to host the tournament; LIV paid $3.2 million under contract. Then the event was cancelled, citing summer heat — a decision that drew sharp criticism from Louisiana officials who had committed significant public funds to the partnership.

Four events remain on the 2026 schedule: a UK stop, New York, Indianapolis, and the $40 million Team Championship in Michigan. Whether all four will be held is an open question. The tour's leadership, including new board members Gene Davis and Jon Zinman, is working to secure outside investment and chart a path forward.

Jon Rahm, meanwhile, has continued competing at a high level regardless of the league's front-office turbulence — winning in Hong Kong by three shots and in Mexico City by six shots in the first half of the season, completing nine of 13 events.
