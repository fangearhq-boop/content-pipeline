KPMG Women's PGA Final Round: Ryu Leads, Korda 4 Back in Historic Chase

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

Haeran Ryu stands one final round away from the biggest win of her career. The South Korean enters Sunday's final round of the 2026 KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota, sitting at -11 through 54 holes — one shot clear of Canadian veteran Brooke Henderson at -10, and four ahead of defending major champion Nelly Korda.

The question is whether four shots is enough to keep Korda from history.

Ryu's Round 3 Gave Her the Lead She Needed

Ryu played her best golf of the week when it mattered most on Saturday. She turned the front nine with three birdies and an eagle — a 5-under 31 — before settling for a 4-under 68 total on the day. The round didn't just put her in front; it sent a message about her composure under pressure at a course that has chewed up players all week.

Henderson, the 2016 KPMG champion who knows this major better than most, is lurking at -10 with nothing to lose and plenty of experience reading a pressure-packed Sunday at a major. Ina Yoon is one further back at -9, and Dewi Weber and A Lim Kim are both at -8.

What Korda Needs

Korda came to Hazeltine having already won the 2026 Chevron Championship and the 2026 U.S. Women's Open. A third consecutive major win would put her in elite company — only Babe Zaharias (1950) and Inbee Park (2013) have ever won three straight LPGA major championships.

Four shots back with 18 holes to play is a steep climb, but not impossible. Hazeltine National stretches over 6,800 yards and has played firm and fast all week — birdie opportunities exist if the ball-striking is there. Korda, who is currently at 25 of 27 Hall of Fame points, would earn two points with a major victory and need only one more to become eligible for induction. The incentives don't get much bigger than this.

The math is simple and brutal: she needs Ryu to stumble, Henderson to hold steady, and herself to go low. Two of those three things would probably not be enough.

The Hazeltine Factor

Hazeltine National is no friend to the front-runner. The course has a history of late drama at major championships, and Sunday's setup — assuming the greens remain firm — will reward precision iron play and punish anything leaking right off the tee. Ryu has been one of the most accurate drivers in the field all week, which is a significant structural advantage on a course this wide and this demanding.

Henderson, for her part, is known for her exceptional putting. If the Canadian gets hot on the greens Sunday, Ryu's one-shot cushion could disappear before the back nine.

A Record Purse on the Line

This year's KPMG Women's PGA Championship is carrying a record $13 million purse. The winner's share represents a landmark payout for women's golf — a sign of the sponsorship investment the tour has attracted at the major level. For Ryu, a first major championship victory would be both financially transformative and career-defining.

What's Next

Final round tee times at Hazeltine begin early Sunday with the leaders in the final pairing going off late morning. The result will finalize the LPGA's major leaderboard heading into the stretch run of the season — and determine whether Korda's historic three-peat survives, or whether Ryu earns the trophy she's been building toward all week. Coverage begins Sunday morning on Golf Channel and Peacock, with the back nine shifting to NBC.
