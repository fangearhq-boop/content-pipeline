Travelers Championship Final Round — Hovland Leads by 1 over Scheffler

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

June 28, 2026

Viktor Hovland walks to the first tee at TPC River Highlands on Sunday with a one-shot lead over Scottie Scheffler and a story to defend. On Saturday, Hovland played one of the finest rounds of the tournament week — a six-under 64 that turned a two-stroke deficit into a one-stroke lead. The leaderboard flipped. Scheffler, who entered Round 3 with the advantage, will spend Sunday chasing.

The final round setup: Hovland at 20-under par, Scheffler at 19-under. The gap has never been smaller or more meaningful in the context of this $20 million Signature Event.

How the Leaderboard Changed on Saturday

Scheffler came into Saturday in position to win. His Round 2 performance — a 10-under 60 featuring 11 birdies and just one bogey — had been one of the most talked-about rounds of the 2026 season. He held a two-shot lead over Hovland entering Round 3. The expectation was that Scheffler, the world's No. 1 ranked player for 157 consecutive weeks, would extend that advantage on a course where he has always played well.

Hovland had different plans. He matched Scheffler hole-for-hole through most of the round and then capitalized on the moment that mattered most. On the 18th hole — the round's final drama — Hovland buried a six-foot birdie putt. Scheffler, putting from 25 feet off the fringe, hit the ball eight feet past the cup. He missed the return. What had been a two-shot Scheffler advantage entering the round became a one-shot Hovland advantage heading into Sunday.

That single hole did not decide the week. But it captured perfectly the way the day unfolded.

What Scheffler Still Has

One shot is nothing in a Sunday final round with 18 holes to play. Scheffler has not finished a week at a major Signature Event without competing for the title, and his 2026 season has featured eight top-five finishes alongside his win at the American Express. He is the best player in the world by a significant margin on any objective measure, and he has spent 157 weeks holding the top spot in the Official World Golf Ranking to prove it.

He also has a round 2 under his belt at TPC River Highlands that reminded everyone watching exactly how dominant his ceiling is. A 60 in competitive golf is not a result driven by luck — it comes from ball-striking precision and putting confidence held together for four and a half hours. That same player will stand on the first tee Sunday and try to do it again in the pressure of a final pairing.

Hovland's Opportunity

For Hovland, Sunday represents the chance to win a Signature Event on the PGA Tour's biggest stage. He is the No. 1 Norwegian in golf, a player who has accumulated victories on multiple tours and in multiple formats. Coming from behind to steal a lead in Round 3 demonstrates the kind of mental composure that Sunday pressure requires. He has been here before.

The Travelers Championship has not seen a final pairing quite like this in recent memory — two of the game's most talented players, one shot apart, with everything still to decide. The $3.6 million winner's share and a significant FedEx Cup points haul are secondary to what both players are chasing: a win that confirms their place among the season's elite.

What's Next

The final round of the Travelers Championship begins Sunday morning at TPC River Highlands in Cromwell, Connecticut. Hovland and Scheffler tee off in the final pairing with a one-shot gap separating them. The winner will claim $3.6 million from the $20 million purse and significant FedEx Cup points. After the Travelers, the Tour moves to the John Deere Classic at TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois on July 2-5.
