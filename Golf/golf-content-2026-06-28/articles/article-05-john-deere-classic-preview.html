John Deere Classic Preview: $8.8M Purse, July 4th Weekend at TPC Deere Run

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

The John Deere Classic returns to TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois, from July 2-5, 2026 — a Fourth of July holiday weekend event that has historically served as one of the PGA Tour's most reliable launching pads for young players chasing their first Tour victories. This year is no different, with a field assembling around an $8.8 million purse and 500 FedEx Cup points for the winner.

The Event and the Venue

TPC Deere Run is a classic Midwestern parkland course that rewards precision and penalizes carelessness. The tournament plays on a July holiday weekend — which means afternoon thunderstorms are always in the forecast — but also means the course is typically soft enough to allow low scoring. Sub-20 under par totals are common at TPC Deere Run, and the winner will need to be in that range.

The John Deere Classic has a distinctive identity on Tour. It consistently attracts a mix of veterans managing their schedules, players trying to stay above the FedEx Cup cutlines, and emerging talents who see a relatively open field as an opportunity to break through. Several players who are now well-established on Tour can trace their first PGA Tour win back to Silvis.

JT Poston and Ben Griffin Lead the Notable Commitments

JT Poston and Ben Griffin are among the notable players confirmed for the field at the time of writing. Poston has been a consistent presence at TPC Deere Run in recent years. Griffin has been building his FedEx Cup standing through the 2026 season and will be looking to add to his points total in a field where the top of the world rankings is largely absent — most elite players will be resting or preparing for the Open Championship, which begins July 16.

The timing of the John Deere Classic — one week before the Open — means the strongest players in the world typically skip it. That creates exactly the kind of window that mid-range and ascending Tour players are built to exploit.

FedEx Cup Implications

With 500 FedEx Cup points for the winner, the John Deere Classic is a meaningful event for players positioning themselves for the FedEx Cup playoffs. A win here can move a player from the bubble to the comfortable zone — or in some cases from mid-range all the way into the top bracket of the standings. For players outside the top 70, every point matters in the second half of the season.

The $1.584 million winner's share also makes the event financially significant for the players competing — particularly those further down the world rankings for whom a win in Silvis represents a genuine career inflection point.

What's Next

The 2026 John Deere Classic begins with practice rounds this week, with first round action starting Thursday July 2. With the Open Championship looming the following week, the John Deere Classic is one of the last full-field events before the summer major window closes. Tee times and full field announcements will be available at PGATour.com. Coverage throughout the week on Golf Channel and Peacock.
