The Open Championship Preview: Mickelson Out, Record Crowd Expected at Royal Birkdale

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

The 154th Open Championship is three weeks away — July 16-19 at Royal Birkdale Golf Club in Southport, England — and the storylines arriving ahead of it are anything but ordinary. Phil Mickelson has withdrawn following new allegations involving explicit photographs. Tiger Woods did not submit an entry by the deadline. And R&A CEO Mark Darbon is projecting attendance will exceed 300,000 for the first time in the championship's history.

Mickelson Withdraws

Phil Mickelson will not compete at Royal Birkdale. The former three-time Open champion withdrew after new allegations surfaced involving an explicit photograph shown to the wife of a fellow professional. Mickelson has not publicly commented on the specifics. The R&A has confirmed his withdrawal from the field.

His absence removes one of the most recognizable names in the sport from the oldest major in golf — and ensures the story around him will dominate coverage in the weeks ahead regardless of what happens on the course.

Woods Absent Again

Tiger Woods did not file an entry by the R&A's deadline. Woods's eligibility status had been uncertain heading into the season, and his decision not to enter means Royal Birkdale will be held without him. His absence, alongside Mickelson's withdrawal, leaves a visible gap in a field of 156 players that will be led by defending champion Scottie Scheffler, who won the 2025 Open Championship.

Record Demand and a New Format

The R&A received over one million ticket applications for the 2026 Open — a benchmark that underlines the championship's growing global audience. Mark Darbon expects attendance to surpass 300,000 across the week, which would be an all-time record for the event.

This year the R&A also introduced a new "Last Chance Qualifier" format, in which 12 players compete over 18 holes on the Monday of Open week for the final spot in the field. Final Qualifying was held at four courses — Burnham and Berrow, Dundonald Links, Royal Cinque Ports, and West Lancashire — with 117 exemption competitors already confirmed across the 156-player field.

Royal Birkdale: The Course

Royal Birkdale is one of the most respected links courses in the world and a regular host of the Open Championship. Located in Southport on England's northwest coast, the course is known for its towering sand dunes, deep fairway bunkers, and wind that can arrive from any direction across the Lancashire coast. Birkdale rewards precision over power — the rough is penal, the greens are firm and fast, and positioning off the tee matters more here than almost anywhere else on the rota.

Scheffler, as defending champion, will wear the yellow numbers and carry the target on his back. His world No. 1 status — held for 157 consecutive weeks — means any other outcome will be framed as an upset.

What's Next

The 154th Open Championship begins July 16. Final field confirmations and practice round schedules will be released by the R&A in the coming week. With Mickelson out and Woods absent, the field will be shaped by the year's form horses — and whoever emerges from Final Qualifying on Monday of Open week with the last ticket to the party.
