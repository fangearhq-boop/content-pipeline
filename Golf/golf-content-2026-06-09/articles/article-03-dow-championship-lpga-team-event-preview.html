Dow Championship Preview: The LPGA Tour's Unique Team Event Returns Thursday

By Marcus Cole | June 9, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Once a year, the LPGA Tour does something no other major professional golf tour does regularly: it asks its players to compete as teams.

The Dow Championship returns Thursday at Midland Country Club in Midland, Michigan, for its annual celebration of partnership, strategy, and a format that creates storylines no stroke-play event can match. It is one of the most entertaining weeks on the women's professional golf calendar, and this year's edition carries the added momentum of an LPGA Tour summer that is riding high after Nelly Korda's U.S. Women's Open victory at Riviera last week.

The Format

The Dow Championship is a stroke-play team competition with 72 two-player teams sharing a $3.3 million purse across four rounds. What makes it distinct is the format rotation: Rounds 1 and 3 are played in foursomes — the alternate-shot format where partners take turns hitting the same ball — while Rounds 2 and 4 use fourballs, the better-ball format where each player plays their own ball and the team counts the lower score.

The foursomes format in particular demands a kind of synchronized decision-making and communication between partners that no other regular LPGA Tour event requires. Players who have played foursomes together in international competitions like the Solheim Cup tend to have an advantage, but even the strongest pairings have to adapt to the specific rhythms of their partner's game.

Defending Champions

Jin Hee Im and Somi Lee return to Midland as the defending champions. Both players became Rolex First Time Winners at the 2025 Dow Championship — a remarkable coincidence that gave the defense extra personal meaning. They come back to a venue where they made history together, in a format that rewards exactly the kind of partnership they built over the course of their 2025 run.

The Field's Compelling Pairings

The Dow Championship generates personality-driven storylines that make it easy to follow even for casual fans of women's golf. Juli Inkster — LPGA Hall of Famer, former U.S. Solheim Cup captain, one of the most respected figures in the game — is making her first LPGA Tour start of 2026 at the Dow, partnered with Angel Yin, her long-term mentee. The pairing of a Hall of Famer and a younger player she has mentored brings a narrative dimension most tournaments cannot offer.

The Jutanugarn sisters — Ariya and Moriya — return as partners. Both have held the world No. 1 ranking at different points in their careers. They won the Dow Championship in 2021. A return to the winner's circle at Midland would give the tournament one of its most satisfying storylines in years.

Twins Aki and Chizzy Iwai also feature in the field, a sibling pairing that adds another human interest layer to an event already rich with them.

What's Next

The Dow Championship runs Thursday June 11 through Sunday June 14 at Midland Country Club in Midland, Michigan. Coverage is available through the LPGA Tour's broadcast partners.

With Nelly Korda fresh off a major championship win and the Dow Championship offering a completely different competitive experience, the LPGA Tour's summer stretch is in full swing. Women's golf rarely has a week like this one to look forward to.
