After the first round of the 2026 Memorial Tournament, just 22 players have broken par at the Jack Nicklaus-designed course in Dublin, Ohio. The Englishman hit only seven greens in regulation — a number that would typically suggest a frustrating day — but scrambled brilliantly, converting 11-of-11 up-and-down attempts without recording a single bogey. He scored 67 without any margin for error, finding pars and birdies from positions that other players were three-putting or carding double-bogeys from. His 67 was his first significant competitive result of the spring. And then there is Scottie Scheffler. ### Long-Form Post Window: 1:00 PM ET Scottie Scheffler is not one to make excuses, so his characterization of Thursday's opening round at the Memorial Tournament as "frustrating" means something. Scheffler, the two-time defending champion who arrived at Muirfield Village seeking what would be only the second three-peat in Memorial Tournament history, shot a 1-over 73. He is six shots behind the four co-leaders. He has three rounds to make it up. The round went sideways on the 16th hole, where a wind change sent his tee shot into the water for a double bogey. He added a bogey on 14. The rest of the round was the Scheffler that everyone expects — quality ball-striking, intelligent course management — but six shots in the wrong direction does not disappear because the subsequent holes were fine. The precedent Scheffler is chasing is daunting even without the round-one deficit. Tiger Woods won this event in 1999, 2000, and 2001 — the only three-peat in Memorial history. Woods won the tournament five times total. Scheffler won in 2024 (8-under, one shot over Morikawa) and 2025 (10-under, four shots over Ben Griffin). A 2026 victory would write his name alongside Tiger in a very specific part of the record book. The math is still possible. Six shots at Muirfield Village over three rounds is not insurmountable for the world's best player. But Thursday did not go according to plan. ### Image Post Caption Scheffler fires 73 in Round 1 at the Memorial — 6 back of leaders. He needs Tiger's three-peat (1999-2001). She has three wins, 17 career LPGA titles, and a Chevron Championship that serves as her third major. She has been the best player on the planet all season. One round, even a bad one, is not the end of this story. She will put on the right shoes tomorrow.
