### STORY 2: Memorial Tournament Round 1 — Four-Way Tie

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 10:30 AM ET

Muirfield Village is playing as hard as its reputation suggests.

After the first round of the 2026 Memorial Tournament, just 22 players have broken par at the Jack Nicklaus-designed course in Dublin, Ohio. Four of them share the lead: Tommy Fleetwood, Wyndham Clark, J.J. Spaun, and Ryan Gerard are all at 5-under 67.

Fleetwood's round deserves a separate paragraph. The Englishman hit only seven greens in regulation — a number that would typically suggest a frustrating day — but scrambled brilliantly, converting 11-of-11 up-and-down attempts without recording a single bogey. He scored 67 without any margin for error, finding pars and birdies from positions that other players were three-putting or carding double-bogeys from.

Clark, the 2023 U.S. Open champion, came into the week on the outside of the signature event field until his CJ Cup Byron Nelson victory provided entry. His 67 was his first significant competitive result of the spring.

And then there is Scottie Scheffler. The two-time defending Memorial champion, who came to Dublin seeking what would be only the second three-peat in tournament history — Tiger Woods did it from 1999 to 2001 — shot a 1-over 73 that he called "frustrating." A double-bogey on 16, where the wind switched and sent his ball into the water, was the decisive blow.

Six shots back. Three rounds remaining.

### STORY 4: Scheffler's Three-Peat Path Gets Harder

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 1:00 PM ET

Scottie Scheffler is not one to make excuses, so his characterization of Thursday's opening round at the Memorial Tournament as "frustrating" means something.

Scheffler, the two-time defending champion who arrived at Muirfield Village seeking what would be only the second three-peat in Memorial Tournament history, shot a 1-over 73. He is six shots behind the four co-leaders. He has three rounds to make it up.

The round went sideways on the 16th hole, where a wind change sent his tee shot into the water for a double bogey. He added a bogey on 14. The rest of the round was the Scheffler that everyone expects — quality ball-striking, intelligent course management — but six shots in the wrong direction does not disappear because the subsequent holes were fine.

The precedent Scheffler is chasing is daunting even without the round-one deficit. Tiger Woods won this event in 1999, 2000, and 2001 — the only three-peat in Memorial history. Woods won the tournament five times total. Scheffler won in 2024 (8-under, one shot over Morikawa) and 2025 (10-under, four shots over Ben Griffin). A 2026 victory would write his name alongside Tiger in a very specific part of the record book.

The math is still possible. Six shots at Muirfield Village over three rounds is not insurmountable for the world's best player. But Thursday did not go according to plan.

### STORY 5: Korda, LeBron, and the Shoe Switch

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 8:30 PM ET

There is a version of this story where Nelly Korda wore LeBron James' custom Nikes and shot 66. That version is not what happened.

What happened: Korda, the world's top-ranked women's golfer and the most dominant player on the LPGA Tour in 2026, walked to the first tee at Riviera Country Club wearing a pair of custom Nike golf shoes personally gifted to her by LeBron James. They looked great. They did not feel right.

By hole 16, Korda had sent someone to the locker room for a different pair. "The fit wasn't right," she said after her round. "Slightly different spacing." The custom version — in a different color than her usual pair — had just enough of a difference in construction that it affected her feel through the course.

Korda finished the round at 2-over 73. She is seven shots behind first-round leader Jennifer Kupcho. She is outside the top 50 in the tournament she came here to win.

But this is Nelly Korda in 2026. She has three wins, 17 career LPGA titles, and a Chevron Championship that serves as her third major. She has been the best player on the planet all season. One round, even a bad one, is not the end of this story.

She will put on the right shoes tomorrow. And then we will see if Riviera gets a comeback worth watching. 👟⛳
