Memorial Tournament Round 1: Fleetwood, Clark, Spaun, Gerard Share Lead as Scheffler Struggles

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 5, 2026

Only 22 players broke par in the first round of the 2026 Memorial Tournament at Muirfield Village Golf Club. Four of them share the lead.

Tommy Fleetwood, Wyndham Clark, J.J. Spaun, and Ryan Gerard are each at 5-under 67 after Thursday's opening round at the Jack Nicklaus-designed course in Dublin, Ohio, setting up a tight leaderboard heading into Friday's second round. Behind them, the field is compressed — testament to how demanding Muirfield Village is playing in early June conditions.

And six shots behind the leaders, at 1-over 73, sits the two-time defending champion Scottie Scheffler.

Fleetwood's Remarkable Round

Of the four co-leaders, Tommy Fleetwood's 67 is the most interesting. He hit only seven greens in regulation — a number that typically correlates with frustration and dropped shots, not a share of the lead at a major invitational. But Fleetwood scrambled with extraordinary precision, converting all 11 of his up-and-down attempts and finishing the round without a single bogey.

That is a different kind of great round. Fleetwood did not overpower the course or find the ball-striking precision that produces low scores on typical days. He managed, survived, and scored despite imperfect contact — which is often what separates contenders from the field at Muirfield Village, where the course's demanding design punishes poor chipping and putting far more harshly than poor tee shots.

Clark, the 2023 U.S. Open champion, put together his first strong result in a signature event this season, having entered the field only after winning the CJ Cup Byron Nelson. Spaun and Gerard each capitalized on the same formula: fairways, patience, and capitalizing on the birdie opportunities that the course occasionally offers.

Scheffler's Difficult Day

Scottie Scheffler called his round "frustrating," and the word is apt. He came to Dublin seeking what would be only the second three-peat in Memorial Tournament history — Tiger Woods won three consecutive editions from 1999 to 2001, the only player to achieve the feat — and his opening round put that quest in immediate jeopardy.

The round came undone on the 16th hole, where the wind shifted as Scheffler's tee shot was in the air. The ball, which might have found safety in calmer conditions, ended up in the water. Double bogey. He added a bogey on the 14th. The rest was the standard Scheffler competence that makes him the world's top-ranked player — but standard competence does not overcome a round spoiled by weather-assisted disasters.

Scheffler's 2024 Memorial win came at 8-under, one shot over Collin Morikawa. His 2025 defense came at 10-under, four shots clear of Ben Griffin. Those margins suggest the course rewards sustained excellence — and that a 73 in round one is not a death sentence, particularly for a player who routinely plays the best golf of the tournament over the final two rounds.

Still: six shots is six shots. Muirfield Village's closing stretch is punishing. The path to a three-peat has become considerably narrower than it was Wednesday evening.

What's Next

Round 2 continues Friday at Muirfield Village. The cut will be made after 36 holes. Scheffler needs a strong Friday to remain in range of the leaders — who themselves will need to hold up under the pressure of playing Muirfield Village with a target on their backs. Tommy Fleetwood, in particular, is a player who has won on the PGA Tour and in major championships, and his scrambling ability on Thursday suggests he is in form that can sustain itself across four rounds.

The Memorial Tournament concludes Sunday. Tiger's three-peat stands as the only benchmark in this tournament's history. Scheffler's chance to join it is diminished but not eliminated.
