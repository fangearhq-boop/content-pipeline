Jennifer Kupcho Grabs US Women's Open Lead, Korda Seven Back After Shoe-Swapping Round 1

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 5, 2026

Jennifer Kupcho made seven birdies at Riviera Country Club on Thursday, built a one-shot lead after the opening round of the 81st U.S. Women's Open, and left the Pacific Palisades course looking like the most comfortable player in the field.

Kupcho's 5-under 66 gives her the solo lead heading into Friday's second round, with Sei Young Kim one stroke back at 4-under 67. The early leaderboard is bunched — Riviera tends to tighten fields rather than spread them — but Kupcho has the advantage that matters most at this stage of a major: she is ahead of everyone else.

For Nelly Korda, Thursday was the opposite story.

Kupcho's Day

Kupcho's opening round was a masterclass in managing a famously demanding golf course. Riviera's kikuyu rough — thick, wiry, and unforgiving for players who miss fairways — created problems for much of the field on Thursday. Kupcho threaded fairways, reached greens, and converted birdies with a consistency that made her 5-under round look smoother than the conditions deserved.

Seven birdies over 18 holes at a U.S. Women's Open venue is an exceptional result. The USGA typically sets up its major venues to limit birdie opportunities and reward precision over power. Riviera's elevated greens and tilted fairways demanded that standard, and Kupcho met it with a round that positioned her perfectly for what comes next.

Kim sits one back at 4-under. The leaderboard behind the leaders is bunched, suggesting that conditions are difficult enough to keep the field compressed through the first few rounds — which means Kupcho's lead is modest but meaningful. She plays Friday knowing she needs to maintain rather than extend.

Korda's Rough Start

World No. 1 Nelly Korda arrived at Riviera as the overwhelming favorite — three wins in 2026, 17 career LPGA titles, a Chevron Championship that made her a three-time major winner. She also arrived wearing a pair of custom Nike golf shoes personally gifted to her by LeBron James.

By the time she reached the 16th hole, she had sent someone to the locker room for a different pair.

"The fit wasn't right," Korda explained after her round. The custom shoes — in a different color variation than her usual pair — had slightly different construction than she was accustomed to. The difference was subtle enough that it might not bother most players, but golf equipment at the elite level is a precision matter. Korda noticed it early, played through it for 15 holes, and eventually made the switch.

The round ended at 2-over 73. Korda is seven shots behind Kupcho, outside the top 50, and in the kind of position that requires a genuine comeback over the remaining three rounds.

What's Next

Round 2 of the U.S. Women's Open continues Friday at Riviera Country Club, with the cut line to be determined after 36 holes. Kupcho enters the day with the lead and the momentum. Korda enters needing to climb seven shots in an environment that does not typically reward aggressive chasing — Riviera will demand patience and precision before it rewards birdie opportunities.

The U.S. Women's Open continues through Sunday, June 7. Korda has never won this particular major, and a 73 in Round 1 has made the path significantly more difficult. But Riviera is long and demanding, and the player who has dominated the LPGA Tour all year has enough games remaining to find her way back into contention. The shoes will be right this time.
