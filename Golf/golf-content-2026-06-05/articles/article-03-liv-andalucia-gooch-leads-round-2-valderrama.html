Gooch Grabs Four-Shot Lead After Round 2 at LIV Golf Andalucia

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 5, 2026

Talor Gooch has been here before. Three years ago, he won the LIV Golf event at Real Club Valderrama in Sotogrande, Spain. On Friday, he made a strong case that he is about to do it again.

Gooch shot a second-round 66 at Valderrama to move to the top of the LIV Andalucia leaderboard, holding a four-shot advantage over Dustin Johnson heading into Saturday's final round. The 33-year-old Oklahoma native has not been at his most consistent during the 2026 LIV season, but Valderrama's demanding layout appears to suit his ball-striking profile, and his round on Friday was the sharpest he has played all year.

Why Valderrama Matters

Real Club Valderrama is one of the most storied golf courses in continental Europe. Its tight, tree-lined fairways, small greens, and punishing rough reward precision over distance — making it the kind of course where Gooch's controlled, accurate game thrives more than at the power-heavy venues that dominate many modern tournament schedules.

Jon Rahm, LIV's Legion XIII captain and a player who carries enormous personal significance to Spanish golf audiences, is chasing a home-circuit victory that would rank among the most emotionally resonant wins of his career. How far back Rahm finds himself on Saturday will determine whether he has a realistic chance to mount a challenge.

Dustin Johnson sits four shots back, and his experience with long-iron approaches and putting under final-round pressure means he will not simply vanish from contention. Gooch's lead is comfortable enough to withstand a modest stumble, but Valderrama has produced multiple late-round swings over the years.

The Open Championship Incentive

One additional factor raises the stakes this week: the winner of LIV Andalucia receives an invitation to the Open Championship at Royal Birkdale. LIV Golf's agreement with the R&A to provide an Open spot for tournament winners has made certain LIV events more compelling for players who care deeply about major championship participation. Birkdale is one of the great Open venues, and an invitation there carries significant prestige.

For Gooch, the prize would add a major championship opportunity to an already meaningful career result. For Rahm, Johnson, and the others who will chase him down Valderrama's closing holes on Saturday, the same incentive applies.

What's Next

The final round of LIV Golf Andalucia takes place Saturday at Real Club Valderrama in Sotogrande, Spain. Gooch enters holding a four-shot lead and the course form of a player who has won this specific event before. Whether Dustin Johnson, Jon Rahm, or someone else can close the gap and claim the title — along with the Open Championship invitation — will be determined over the final 18 holes at one of Europe's most prestigious courses.
