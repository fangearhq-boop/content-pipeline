Scheffler's Memorial Three-Peat Bid Takes a Hit After Frustrating Round 1

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 5, 2026

Scottie Scheffler walked off Muirfield Village on Thursday with a card that read 73 — one over par — and a six-shot deficit to the four co-leaders. He described the round as "frustrating." That is not the word you want to hear from the world's best player when he is chasing history.

Scheffler came to the 2026 Memorial Tournament seeking the second three-peat in the event's history. Tiger Woods won three consecutive Memorial tournaments from 1999 to 2001 — the only player to do so in the decades since Jack Nicklaus launched the event. Scheffler won here in 2024 and 2025. One more, and he would join that exclusive record.

One round in, the path has narrowed considerably.

What Went Wrong

The round was largely manageable until the 16th hole, where the wind changed direction just as Scheffler's tee shot was in the air. The ball, which would have found a reasonable position in calm conditions, came down in the water. Double bogey. A bogey on the 14th had already cost him one shot. The rest of the scorecard reflected the controlled, disciplined play that makes him the world's top-ranked player — but the damage was done.

Six shots at Muirfield Village over three rounds is not an impossible distance to make up. The course rewards players who build momentum through its mid-section and survive the closing holes with composure. Scheffler has done exactly that on both his previous Memorial winning weeks.

But the leaderboard does not cooperate by standing still. The four co-leaders — Tommy Fleetwood, Wyndham Clark, J.J. Spaun, and Ryan Gerard — are experienced, proven players who will not simply give ground because Scheffler is in the field.

The Tiger Standard

Woods won the Memorial in 1999, 2000, and 2001 — a period when his dominance over professional golf was so complete that consecutive victories in the same non-major felt almost unremarkable to the broader audience, even as the achievement accumulated its own historic weight. He would go on to win the tournament five times in total.

Scheffler's 2024 win came at 8-under, one stroke over Collin Morikawa. His 2025 defense required a 10-under performance that beat Ben Griffin by four. Both were commanding victories by the standards of this tournament, where Muirfield Village's difficulty tends to keep winning scores modest.

The question now is whether Scheffler can produce a third consecutive performance of that caliber from six shots back. It would require a round-two 65 or better to get into range — and then sustained pressure over the weekend.

What's Next

Round 2 is Friday at Muirfield Village Golf Club in Dublin, Ohio. The cut will be made after 36 holes. Scheffler needs to move significantly in the right direction on Friday to keep his three-peat hopes alive. A second consecutive disappointing round would effectively end them. A charge — the kind Scheffler has produced at this course before — would reset the narrative heading into the weekend.

Three rounds remain. Tiger's three-peat is still out there. But Muirfield Village does not make anything easy, and Thursday made clear that Scheffler's path to history runs through a lot of ground he needs to make up.
