Korda Swapped LeBron's Custom Shoes Mid-Round at the US Women's Open. Then Shot 73.

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 5, 2026

Nelly Korda started the 81st U.S. Women's Open wearing custom Nike golf shoes that LeBron James had personally gifted her. She finished the round in a different pair entirely.

The switch happened on the 16th hole at Riviera Country Club. Korda had sent someone to the locker room while she played up to that point. When the backup pair arrived, she put them on and continued. "The fit wasn't right," she explained afterward. The custom LeBron version — in a different color variation than her usual pair — had slightly different construction that she could feel through her shoes. For a player who relies on feel for every shot she hits, even small differences in footwear become a real factor over eighteen holes.

The round ended at 2-over 73. Korda sits seven shots behind first-round leader Jennifer Kupcho. For a player who has won three times in 2026 and arrived at Riviera as the heavy favorite for her first U.S. Women's Open title, the opening round was not what anyone expected.

The Story Behind the Shoes

The detail about LeBron James providing Korda with custom golf shoes is genuinely unusual — cross-sport equipment gifts at the major championship level don't happen every week. LeBron, a Nike athlete with significant influence over the brand's product development, apparently had a pair made specifically for Korda. She wore them to the first tee on Thursday in the kind of moment that makes for great pre-tournament color.

What makes it a real story is that Korda's round did not conform to the feel-good version of events. She changed shoes because she had to, not because she wanted to. Golf equipment at the elite level requires precision that most players outside the professional ranks cannot fully appreciate — a fraction of a millimeter in sole construction can affect how the club sits at address and how the player registers the ground through impact. Korda's decision to make the switch mid-round was a practical one, not a dramatic one.

The 73 is the dramatic part. Korda made missed putts and navigated Riviera's kikuyu rough without the flow that has defined her season. She is seven shots back of Kupcho, and the U.S. Women's Open is not a tournament where that kind of deficit disappears easily.

The Case for Korda

Here is what Nelly Korda has done in 2026: won three tournaments, won the Chevron Championship for her third major title, and reached 17 career LPGA victories. She has not simply been a contender this season — she has been the dominant force on the women's professional game.

Players with that level of sustained excellence do not suddenly become incapable of coming back from a difficult opening round at a major. Riviera will demand precision and patience over the remaining three rounds, but those are exactly the qualities Korda has demonstrated repeatedly this year. A 73 in round one puts her in a difficult position. It does not eliminate her from the conversation.

The shoes will be right on Friday. The question is whether everything else falls into place at the same time.

What's Next

Round 2 of the U.S. Women's Open continues Friday at Riviera Country Club. The cut will be made after 36 holes. Korda needs a strong second round — something in the 65-67 range — to get back into realistic contention heading into the weekend. At seven shots back of Kupcho, she cannot afford another stumble. The tournament continues through Sunday, June 7. Three rounds remain. Korda has won under pressure all year. This is a different kind of pressure — seven holes up instead of one behind — but the player who changes her shoes on hole 16 and finishes the round is not someone who gives up on a golf course.
