LIV Golf Virginia Preview: Rahm Arrives Hot, DeChambeau Returns, Niemann Defends

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

April 30, 2026

Jon Rahm is the hottest player in LIV Golf heading into the Maaden LIV Golf Virginia event, and after a week off following his six-shot win in Mexico City, he arrives at Trump National Golf Club in Sterling, Virginia, as the man everyone else needs to beat.

Rahm's Mexico City performance — a final-round seven-under-par 64 that put the result beyond doubt — was his second LIV win of 2026 and his fourth individual title on the circuit overall. His team, Legion XIII, also claimed their third consecutive team title at that event. By any measure, Rahm and his squad are playing their best LIV golf to date.

The Stakes in Virginia

The Maaden LIV Golf Virginia runs May 7–10 at Trump National Golf Club, roughly 25 miles from Washington, D.C. in Sterling. It is one of the circuit's established American stops and carries the weight of the team standings race, which has tightened considerably heading into the spring.

As of the most recent standings, Ripper GC leads with 89.75 points, with 4Aces just 1.75 points behind at 88. Legion XIII sits third. For Rahm's team, Virginia is a genuine inflection point — a win could vault them into contention for the overall season title. For Ripper and 4Aces, the margin is thin enough that every stroke matters.

The Returning Champion

Joaquin Niemann, captain of Torque GC, is the defending individual champion from last year's Virginia event. Niemann has been one of LIV's most consistent individual performers since joining the circuit, and the Virginia stop suits his all-around game. He arrives with added motivation after watching Rahm claim the last two team titles for Legion XIII — and with the individual title on the line, he has a specific target to defend.

DeChambeau's Return

Bryson DeChambeau's presence at Virginia was uncertain after he withdrew from the Mexico City event with a wrist injury. He is expected to return for the Crushers GC this week, which is significant both individually and for the team standings. Crushers GC are the defending 2025 Virginia team champions, and DeChambeau's absence in Mexico City affected their ability to score in the standings race. If he is healthy and back at his peak, the Crushers become one of the field's most dangerous teams.

DeChambeau's game — built around power off the tee and a willingness to attempt shots that other players will not even consider — is particularly effective on courses that reward length and creativity. Trump National DC, a Tom Fazio design that plays through rolling terrain with water hazards on several key holes, is a course where those qualities can be decisive.

The LIV Format

Virginia will feature 57 players across 13 teams, with no cut. All players complete 54 holes of stroke play, with the scores contributing to both individual and team standings simultaneously. The no-cut format means every team battle counts from the opening tee shot — and with Ripper and 4Aces separated by under two points, the pressure on each team's captain and roster to perform is unusually high.

What's Next

The Maaden LIV Golf Virginia begins May 7 at Trump National Golf Club in Sterling, Virginia. Rahm, Niemann, DeChambeau, and the rest of the 57-player field will compete May 7–10. Following Virginia, the LIV schedule continues through the summer as the team standings race enters its decisive stretch.
