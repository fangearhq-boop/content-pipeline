Five of the World's Top 15 Skip Doral — The PGA Tour's Scheduling Problem Is Getting Worse

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

April 30, 2026

The Cadillac Championship is a $20 million Signature Event at one of professional golf's most celebrated venues. Scottie Scheffler, the World No. 1, is in the field. So are Cameron Young, Viktor Hovland, Tommy Fleetwood, and Jon Rahm.

And yet five of the top 15 players in the Official World Golf Ranking are not there.

Rory McIlroy, the reigning Masters champion, is resting. Xander Schauffele, Ludvig Åberg, Robert MacIntyre, and Matt Fitzpatrick are also absent. By the count published this week by Front Office Sports and Golf Monthly, this represents the largest concentration of top-player absences at any Signature Event held on the 2026 PGA Tour calendar — and the reason is straightforward if you look at what comes next.

Three Weeks, Three Events

This week is the Cadillac Championship. Next week is the Truist Championship in Charlotte — also a Signature Event with a $20 million purse. The week after that, May 14, the PGA Championship begins at Philadelphia Country Club.

Three consecutive high-stakes events. Players who have already secured major victories, or who are targeting their first, are making a calculated decision: arrive at the PGA Championship fresh rather than risk accumulating fatigue across three demanding weeks. It is not laziness. It is arithmetic.

McIlroy won the Masters earlier this season and has already had a year that would satisfy most players' career aspirations. His decision to skip Doral — and potentially Truist as well — with the PGA Championship in view is rational. So is Schauffele's, whose Ryder Cup form and general trajectory make him one of the field's most dangerous players when he is physically and mentally sharp.

The Format's Hidden Flaw

The Signature Event format was designed with a specific goal: concentrate star power. The PGA Tour recognized that a long, spread-thin schedule was diluting the product, so it created a tier of elevated events that would feature the best players in the world competing for outsized purses at marquee venues. It was, in theory, a way to manufacture appointment television and restore some of the gravitational pull that had been lost to LIV Golf's signing of established names.

The problem is that the calendar still runs into the same fundamental constraint: players are human. Even the world's best golfers can only sustain elite performance across a finite number of weeks before fatigue begins to degrade their output. And the PGA Tour scheduled multiple Signature Events in consecutive weeks during a stretch that also includes one of the four majors.

The result is predictable. Players who want to be at their best for the PGA Championship — which carries global prestige, ranking points, and career narrative weight that no Signature Event can match — are choosing to skip one or two of the consecutive Signature Events. They are doing exactly what any rational competitor would do. The Tour's format was supposed to prevent this by making the events themselves too important to skip. Instead, it has simply created a new version of the same problem.

What It Means for Doral This Week

Scheffler's presence remains significant. He is the best player in the world and capable of carrying the field's profile on his own. The galleries who come to Trump National Doral this week are going to see extraordinary golf from a 72-man field that, even without five of its potential top-15 members, includes multiple major champions and former World No. 1 players.

But the absence of McIlroy in particular is felt whenever he does not appear. He is one of the sport's most watchable players and the current holder of the Masters title. His presence would have turned the Doral field from strong to unmissable. Without him, the event is excellent. It could have been historic.

What's Next

The Cadillac Championship runs through May 3 at Trump National Doral. The following week, the Truist Championship takes place in Charlotte — another $20 million Signature Event where the same question of absentees will resurface. The PGA Championship begins May 14 at Philadelphia Country Club, where the full slate of the game's best players is expected to appear.
