Korda Returns at LPGA Riviera Maya Open With Hall of Fame in Her Sights

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

April 30, 2026

Nelly Korda has not stopped. One week after claiming the Chevron Championship for her third career major, the World No. 1 is teeing it up again today at the LPGA Mexico Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba in Playa del Carmen, Mexico.

She is five LPGA Hall of Fame points away from induction. She is playing some of the best golf of her career. And she is not interested in resting.

The HOF Math

Korda currently sits at 22 of the 27 points required for LPGA Hall of Fame induction. The formula gives her multiple paths to the remaining five. Each regular LPGA win counts as one point; each major counts as two. A scenario of two more regular wins and one more major would complete the requirement. So would five regular wins, or two majors and one regular win. At the rate she is playing, the only question is not whether she will reach 27 points, but how quickly.

Her 2026 season has already been extraordinary: two wins heading into Riviera Maya, including the Chevron Championship at The Club at Carlton Woods for her 17th LPGA title. She reclaimed the World No. 1 ranking earlier this year and has not relinquished it. There is a reasonable argument that this is the best individual stretch she has played in her career — and her career has already been exceptional.

The Tournament

The LPGA Mexico Riviera Maya Open runs April 30 through May 3 at El Camaleon Golf Course in Playa del Carmen. The $2 million purse brings a strong field to the Riviera Maya, one of the LPGA's established international stops. El Camaleon is a Greg Norman-designed course that plays through jungle and coastal terrain — a distinctive setting that stands apart from the manicured resort courses that dominate the LPGA schedule.

Korda is the story of the week, but the field behind her is not simply waiting to hand her the trophy. The LPGA's depth has grown significantly over the past three seasons, and any number of players are capable of taking advantage if Korda struggles on a course that rewards local knowledge and course management as much as raw power.

Back-to-Back in Reach

Winning in consecutive weeks on the LPGA Tour is rare. The physical and mental demands of traveling internationally and competing at the highest level immediately following a major victory would exhaust most players. Korda appears to be running on something different. Her ability to stay focused through the grind of a long season — and to perform her best when the stakes are highest — is what separates her from the rest of the field even before a single shot is struck.

If she wins this week, she would enter June with three wins including two in consecutive starts and a trajectory toward the Hall of Fame that would be essentially unavoidable. The broader storyline of women's golf in 2026 is already being written with her name at the top. Riviera Maya is the next chapter.

What's Next

The LPGA Mexico Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba runs through May 3. Korda's performance through the opening round will be the week's main storyline to track. Following Riviera Maya, the LPGA schedule continues through May with several additional international stops before the next major, the U.S. Women's Open, approaches on the calendar.
