Blue Monster Is Back: Cadillac Championship Round 1 Tees Off at Trump National Doral

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

April 30, 2026

The PGA Tour has returned to one of its most storied venues. After a 10-year absence, Trump National Doral's legendary Blue Monster course is hosting professional golf again, with Round 1 of the 2026 Cadillac Championship teeing off today in Miami.

This is the 56th PGA Tour event held at the club, and the first since Adam Scott won the 2016 WGC-Cadillac Championship. The course sat quiet through nearly a decade of negotiations, redesigns, and renovations before landing back on the Tour's flagship calendar as a $20 million Signature Event.

The Field

Seventy-two of the world's top players are competing this week, with Scottie Scheffler — the World No. 1 — headlining as the betting favorite. Scheffler has been relentlessly consistent in 2026, posting top-two finishes at both the Masters and the RBC Heritage, but he remains without a Signature Event win this season. Doral gives him another chance.

The rest of the field includes Tommy Fleetwood, Cameron Young, Viktor Hovland, and Jon Rahm — a strong international lineup that gives the event the marquee feel of a World Golf Championship, which is essentially what it was for the decade the Tour was last here. There is no 36-hole cut, so all 72 players will complete four rounds regardless of their scores.

The Blue Monster, Renovated

Gil Hanse and Jim Wagner oversaw the course renovation that preceded this week's return. The headline additions are new lakes on the par-3 15th hole and the drivable par-4 16th — two holes that will be among the most watched of the week. The par-4 16th is reachable off the tee, but the new water hazard raises the risk significantly. Players who attempt the green in one will be making a binary decision: birdie or worse.

The course plays 7,739 yards from the tips with a par of 72. That layout — four par-5s, four par-3s, and ten par-4s — rewards length but demands precision on approach. The wide fairways have historically favored aggressive drivers, but Doral punishes anyone who misses into the rough and then overcommits on the approach shots.

Why This Week Matters

Signature Events were created to concentrate star power into a smaller number of marquee events, and the Cadillac Championship is positioned as one of the PGA Tour's premier weeks. The $20 million purse — with the winner taking home a figure that rivals many season-long earnings for average Tour members — reflects how seriously the Tour is investing in Doral's revival.

The Blue Monster has hosted legends at their peaks. Tiger Woods won here four times. Jack Nicklaus, Nick Faldo, and Lee Trevino all claimed Doral titles. The course carries the kind of legacy that feels more like a major venue than a regular Tour stop, and players who have competed here before talk about it with a reverence usually reserved for Augusta or Pebble Beach.

What to Watch

Round 1 coverage begins at 3 PM ET on Golf Channel. The opening wave will reveal how the course is playing under the updated renovation, particularly whether the new water features on 15 and 16 produce the dramatic moments the designers intended. Early scoring will also give a sense of how the field is handling the Doral rough, which historically plays tougher than the wide fairways might suggest.

Scheffler is the name to watch throughout the week, but Hovland and Young are capable of going low on any given day. Rahm, who arrives fresh off a six-shot win at the LIV Mexico City event, brings momentum of his own and will be competing this week alongside his LIV commitments — a scheduling reality that has become routine for multi-circuit players.

What's Next

Round 2 of the Cadillac Championship takes place Friday, May 1. Television coverage continues on Golf Channel. Scoring from Round 1 will set the leaderboard for the weekend, with the cut — there is none, given this is a Signature Event — meaning the full 72-man field plays through Sunday.
