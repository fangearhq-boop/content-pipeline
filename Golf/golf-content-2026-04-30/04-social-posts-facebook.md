### STORY 3: Blue Monster History — The Course Every Golfer Respects

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM ET

For more than five decades, the Blue Monster at Trump National Doral was one of the most feared and celebrated stops on the PGA Tour. Now it is back.

Originally designed by Dick Wilson and opened in 1962, the Blue Monster hosted the Doral Open — later the WGC-Cadillac Championship — for over 50 consecutive years before the Tour's departure in 2016. This week's Cadillac Championship marks the 56th Tour event in the club's history, and the course returns with an enhanced bite courtesy of a renovation by Gil Hanse and Jim Wagner that added new lakes on two of its most decisive holes.

The Blue Monster's greatest moments belong to Tiger Woods. He won here four times — in 1997, 2005, 2006, and 2013 — and his 2013 score of 19-under par remains the course record. Jack Nicklaus, Lee Trevino, Raymond Floyd, Nick Faldo, and Ben Crenshaw are also on the winners list.

The course measures 7,739 yards from the tips. Four par-5s offer scoring opportunities; four par-3s demand accuracy; and ten par-4s test every part of a player's game. The new lakes around the 15th and 16th holes are the features to watch this week — expect birdies, bogeys, and everything in between to come from that stretch of the back nine.
