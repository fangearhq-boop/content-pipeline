LIV Golf Virginia Preview: Rahm on a Roll, Niemann Defends, and a Title Race Heats Up

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 26, 2026

Jon Rahm arrives at Trump National Golf Club Washington DC in Sterling, Virginia as the form player on the LIV Golf circuit. His victory at Mexico City's Club de Golf Chapultepec — a final-round 7-under 64, a six-shot margin, and a finish that placed three Spanish-born players on the podium — was a reminder that Rahm remains one of the sport's premier talents regardless of which circuit he plays.

The Virginia event, scheduled for May 8-10 at Trump National's celebrated layout along the Potomac, offers a different kind of test. The course demands precise iron play, patience off the tee, and the ability to navigate a routing that punishes overaggression. It is a venue where ball-striking excellence matters more than power, and where the scrambling capabilities of LIV's best players are tested in conditions very different from Chapultepec's thin-air altitude.

Niemann's Defense

Joaquín Niemann is the defending individual champion at this event, and the Chilean has the game to repeat. He is one of the circuit's longest hitters who has refined his iron play and short game to the point where his all-around game matches his raw distance advantage. Trump National's layout, with its emphasis on precision over brute length, suits a player who can use distance strategically rather than simply maximally.

Niemann and Rahm have developed into the two most compelling individual performers on the LIV circuit — the kind of rivalry that gives the league's title races narrative weight. Their meeting in Virginia, with Rahm fresh off a dominant win and Niemann protecting home turf (in competitive terms), is the central matchup to watch.

Team Standings and Crushers' Title Defense

Bryson DeChambeau's Crushers GC are the defending team champions at Virginia — a title they will be motivated to reclaim after Jon Rahm's Legion XIII claimed three consecutive team titles including the most recent Mexico City event. In the overall 2026 season team standings, Ripper GC leads with 89.75 points, 4Aces GC is a close second with 88, and Legion XIII is third.

The team standings add an additional layer of strategy to the Virginia event. Teams that are within striking distance of the top spots will be playing for position as much as individual glory, and DeChambeau — who is among the most analytically minded players in professional golf — will have modeled exactly what Crushers need to close the gap on Ripper GC at the top.

What's Next

LIV Golf Virginia begins May 8 at Trump National Golf Club Washington DC. For fans watching Rahm's trajectory, a Virginia title would be his third win of the 2026 LIV season — an unprecedented run that would cement his status as the circuit's dominant individual performer. For the defending champions, Virginia is about reclaiming something that was taken. For the team standings, it is a pivotal weekend where the season's shape will become clearer.
