Fitzpatrick Brothers Enter Zurich Classic Final Round With 4-Shot Lead After Record-Setting 57

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 26, 2026

Seven days ago, Matt Fitzpatrick won the RBC Heritage with a nerveless 4-iron to 13 feet, then made the birdie. Now, with his brother Alex at his side, he has a chance to win the Zurich Classic of New Orleans — one of the PGA Tour's most unique events and one of its most emotionally charged. The Fitzpatrick brothers enter Sunday's final round at 30-under par, four shots ahead of the field after the most breathtaking 18 holes of the tournament.

Saturday's fourball round at TPC Louisiana produced the best golf the Zurich Classic has ever seen. Matt and Alex shot a 15-under 57 — a tournament record — making birdie or better on 14 of the 18 holes. The round was not simply impressive; it was a statement. When Matt's chip on the 18th found the bottom of the cup for eagle to cap the round, the score was already extraordinary. The eagle made it unforgettable.

Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer, who themselves set a previous tournament record with a 58 in Thursday's first round, are tied at 26-under with Davis Thompson and Austin Eckroat. Doug Ghim and Jeffrey Kang are one more back at 25-under. Sunday's format is foursomes — alternate shot — where partners share the same ball, alternating shots from first drive to final putt.

The Format Factor

A four-shot lead in foursomes is meaningful but not impregnable. The format eliminates the safety net of stroke play — there is no recovery hole, no "use my partner's ball" option when things go wrong. A single errant drive, a bad lie, a missed short putt — these can cascade into double bogeys faster than in any other format in golf. The leaderboard can move quickly in foursomes, and four shots can become two in less than an hour.

The Fitzpatricks know this. They showed their alternate-shot credentials in Friday's round, shooting a 65 when the format punished several other pairings. Their chemistry under pressure is the product of years playing together in European team competitions and the kind of sibling bond that does not require verbal communication to express. Alex knows where Matt is going with a shot before Matt swings. Matt trusts Alex's reads on the greens the same way he trusts his own.

A Week for the Ages

Matt Fitzpatrick's week, considered in full, has been extraordinary. A win at Harbour Town last Sunday. A tournament record on Saturday in New Orleans. A four-shot lead entering Sunday. If he closes out the Zurich Classic, he will have won two PGA Tour events in consecutive weeks — a feat that eludes the vast majority of tour professionals across entire careers.

For Alex, Sunday represents something equally significant: validation on the biggest stage. He has competed professionally in his own right, but the chance to share a Zurich Classic trophy with his older brother — who has been World No. 3 and one of the PGA Tour's marquee names — is a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity.

What's Next

The final round at TPC Louisiana begins Sunday morning. The Fitzpatricks tee off in the final pairing, protected by their four-shot cushion but fully aware of how quickly foursomes can turn. If they hold on, the story writes itself — brothers, back-to-back wins for Matt, a tournament record 57 memorialized forever in Zurich Classic history. The chasers will push. But the Fitzpatricks have earned the right to be considered overwhelming favorites to close this out.
