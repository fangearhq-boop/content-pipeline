Korda Enters Chevron Championship Final Round With 5-Shot Lead, Chasing Third Major

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | April 26, 2026

Nelly Korda has done everything right. Through three rounds of the 2026 Chevron Championship at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston, she has built a five-shot advantage over the field, sitting at 16-under par with Pajaree Anannarukarn (Patty T.) her nearest challenger at 11-under. The final round on Sunday offers Korda the chance to claim her third major championship — and cement her place in LPGA history.

Behind Tavatanakit, Ruoning Yin and Pauline Roussin-Bouchard are tied at 10-under in third, and Inbee Yoon is fifth at 8-under. On paper, no one is close enough to present a genuine threat without Korda faltering. But major championships have a way of compressing gaps and amplifying nerves, and the leaderboard holds three players capable of making a spectacular charge if given any opening at all.

Korda opened the week with consecutive bogey-free 65s that set the tone immediately and put the field on notice. She has not allowed opponents to gain ground, and the five-shot cushion she carries into Sunday is entirely of her own creation — earned through consistent, methodical golf that has defined her approach to major championships throughout her career.

Tavatanakit and the Chasers

Pajaree Anannarukarn — universally known by her nickname Patty T. — is the most dangerous challenger on the leaderboard. The Thai golfer has proven her ability to produce low rounds in LPGA competition, and five shots in 18 holes is achievable if she finds a similar rhythm to what she showed in moving up the board through the week. She will need Korda to help by making mistakes.

Yin, the Chinese star who has been one of the LPGA's most consistent performers, is capable of contending in any major. Roussin-Bouchard, the French Canadian who has developed steadily on Tour, rounds out the group sitting within striking distance. Any of the three could make a Sunday charge if the golf gods are willing — but the burden rests with the chasers, not the leader.

A Major Championship History

Korda won this tournament in 2021, establishing herself as a player capable of handling the specific pressure that accompanies the first major of the LPGA calendar. She won the U.S. Women's Open in 2024, adding a second major and confirming that her first was not an anomaly. Today's opportunity would bring the total to three — a mark that in the current era of women's golf places a player firmly in the conversation about all-time LPGA greatness.

The $9 million purse ensures that the financial stakes are among the highest in women's golf. More important than the money is the trophy and what it represents: another chapter in a career that has already exceeded the expectations that surrounded Korda when she first turned professional.

What's Next

The final round at Memorial Park begins Sunday with Korda's pairing teeing off in the afternoon. If she closes it out, the LPGA Tour has its first major champion of 2026 and a story that will carry through the entire season. For Tavatanakit, Yin, and Roussin-Bouchard, the goal is simple: apply maximum pressure early and hope that Korda's perfect-week form is not bulletproof for all 18 holes of Sunday.
