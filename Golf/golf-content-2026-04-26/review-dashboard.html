### Long-Form Post Window: 9:00 AM ET What a week it has been for the Fitzpatrick family. Matt Fitzpatrick — World No. 3 after winning the RBC Heritage just last week — and his brother Alex enter the final round of the Zurich Classic of New Orleans with a four-shot lead at 30-under par. They earned it in extraordinary fashion on Saturday: their 15-under 57 in the fourball round was a tournament record, making birdie or better on 14 of 18 holes at TPC Louisiana. The previous tournament record of 58 had stood for all of one day before the Fitzpatricks made it look ordinary. Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer are tied with Davis Thompson and Austin Eckroat at 26-under in second place. Doug Ghim and Jeffrey Kang are at 25-under, five back. Sunday's format is foursomes — alternate shot — which demands precision and communication. It is unforgiving. A single bad hole can swing the momentum of an entire round. The Fitzpatricks have shown they can handle the format: they shot 65 in the alternate shot round on Friday during the tournament's second round. They have the experience, the sibling connection, and Matt's red-hot form from Hilton Head. A Zurich Classic win would be one of the most heartwarming moments of the 2026 golf season. #GolfFanrecap | #PGATour

Korda won the Chevron Championship in 2021, becoming one of the few players to win the event in a non-Augusta-National format. She won the U.S. Women's Open in 2024 with a performance that drew comparisons to the great wire-to-wire victories in major championship history. Now she stands five shots ahead in the final round of the 2026 Chevron, with a chance to win her third major before her 28th birthday. In the modern LPGA era — post-2010, as the field has become increasingly international and competitive — only a handful of players have won three or more majors before age 28. Korda is on the verge of joining that group. She has been ranked World No. 1 for extended stretches over the past two seasons, won on Tour at a rate that leaves competitors searching for explanations, and played the opening rounds of this Chevron without a single bogey — a standard of consistency that speaks to how locked in she is right now. What she is building in Houston today is not just a win. It is a career-defining moment that will be cited for decades when the conversation turns to the greatest LPGA players of the current generation. #GolfFanrecap | #LPGATour

Zurich Classic Final Round: Can Anyone Chase Down the Fitzpatrick Brothers?
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 10:30 AM ET Four shots in foursomes is not the same as four shots in stroke play. The alternate shot format — where partners alternate hitting the same ball, sharing both the glory and the consequence of every shot — means that one bad swing, one awkward lie, one missed short putt can swing a round in moments. It is why Sunday's Zurich Classic final round will be compelling even with the Fitzpatricks holding a seemingly comfortable lead. Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer, who set a tournament record with a 58 in Thursday's first round, are four back at 26-under. Davis Thompson and Austin Eckroat are tied with them. Doug Ghim and Jeffrey Kang are five back at 25-under. Any of these teams is capable of a run in foursomes — a format that can produce eagles when chemistry is right and train wrecks when it isn't. The Fitzpatricks' edge is their history with each other and with the format. Matt and Alex have practiced together, competed together in team formats, and built the kind of intuitive understanding that alternate shot demands. Matt's form heading into the week was elite — World No. 3, fresh off a win at Harbour Town — and Alex has matched that level all week. Their record 57 Saturday was not a surprise to them. They are playing their best golf at the right time. Four shots is not a guarantee. But the Fitzpatricks have earned the right to be considered very heavy favorites to close it out. #GolfFanrecap | #PGATour

### Long-Form Post Window: 8:30 AM ET The LIV Golf calendar moves to the Washington area next, with Trump National Golf Club in Sterling, Virginia hosting the circuit's May event from the 8th through the 10th — and there are several compelling storylines to follow as the season heats up. Jon Rahm is the man of the moment. He just won the Mexico City individual title with a final-round 7-under 64 at Club de Golf Chapultepec, winning by six shots in a dominant display that demonstrated why he remains one of the world's most complete ball-strikers. Rahm and Legion XIII have now claimed three consecutive team titles — an unprecedented run in LIV's relatively short history. He arrives in Virginia as the form player in the field. But Joaquín Niemann is the defending individual champion at this specific event, and the Crushers GC — Bryson DeChambeau's team — are the defending team champions. Niemann's game is ideally suited to Trump National's layout, which demands precise iron play and patience off the tee. DeChambeau and the Crushers will be motivated to reclaim team honors after Legion XIII's Mexico City dominance.
