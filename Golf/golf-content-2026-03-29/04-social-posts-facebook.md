### STORY 7: Golf Ball Rollback 2026

#### Long-Form Post — 8:30 PM ET

```
The golf ball rollback is here — at least for elite competitions.

The USGA and R&A have introduced a new Model Local Rule for 2026 that caps golf ball distance at 317 yards under controlled test conditions. Golf balls that currently conform to the rules of golf may fail this new standard, and for professional and elite amateur events that adopt the rule, only conforming balls are playable.

The practical effect: approximately 15 fewer yards for players with the highest swing speeds. It is designed to address the ongoing concern about distance making classic course designs obsolete at the top levels of the game.

Crucially, the rule applies only where a competition authority adopts it. The PGA Tour has not committed to adopting the rule for its events. The LIV Golf League is unlikely to do so. Recreational golf is entirely unaffected — the balls sold at your local pro shop will remain legal for your weekend round.

The debate about whether this is enough — or too much, or the wrong solution entirely — will play out over the next several seasons. For now, it exists. Professional golf has its first formal answer to the distance problem. Whether the major tours choose to use it is another question entirely.
```
