Lydia Ko Cards Career-Best 60 to Lead LPGA Ford Championship

March 29, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Lydia Ko opened the Ford Championship presented by Wild Horse Pass with a 12-under 60 — the best single round of her professional career. She began the round at Whirlwind Golf Club's Cattail Course in Chandler, Arizona, with four consecutive birdies, setting the tone for a historic day at the $2.25 million LPGA event.

A round of 60 on the LPGA Tour is rare at any stage of a career. To shoot one as an opening round — setting the pace for the entire field — is rarer still. Ko's round stands as one of the most impressive opening numbers on the women's tour in recent seasons.

Ko's Career Context

Lydia Ko is 29 years old, a two-time Olympic gold medalist — winning in Tokyo (2020) and Paris (2024) — and a former world No. 1. She turned professional in 2012 at age 15, and at 17 became the youngest player, male or female, to reach No. 1 in the world golf rankings.

Her major championship record includes wins at the ANA Inspiration (2016) and the Evian Championship (2015, 2021). She has been one of the most consistent players in the women's game across a decade of professional golf — and her 60 at Whirlwind Golf Club is a statement that she remains capable of elite performances in the lead-up to the season's first major.

The Field and the Leaderboard

Ko was not alone at the top. Hyo Joo Kim, who won the Fortinet Founders Cup the prior week at Sharon Heights in a wire-to-wire performance that held off Nelly Korda on Sunday, is among the contenders at Chandler. Kim's back-to-back bid adds another compelling narrative to a final day that features several of the best players in women's golf in close proximity.

Nelly Korda, the 2025 season points leader and a player who won the LPGA's Tournament of Champions in February, is also in the hunt. The competitive quality at the top of the Ford Championship leaderboard entering the final round is as high as any event on the 2026 LPGA calendar to date.

Looking Ahead: The Chevron Championship

The LPGA's first major of 2026 — the Chevron Championship — is less than four weeks away, scheduled for April 23–26 at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston. For Ko, Korda, and Kim, the Ford Championship serves as competitive preparation for the season's biggest stage.

Ko's 60 arrives at precisely the right moment for a player who has been building form through the first quarter of the 2026 LPGA season. Two-time Olympic gold, a career-best opening round, and a contending position entering the final day: Ko is playing the kind of golf that makes her a legitimate factor at any tournament she enters.
