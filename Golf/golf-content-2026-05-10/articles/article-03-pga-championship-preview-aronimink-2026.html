PGA Championship Preview: Aronimink Returns to Major Golf With Scheffler, McIlroy, Spieth's Grand Slam, and Young's Historic Run

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 10, 2026

In four days, professional golf's attention shifts from Charlotte and Sterling, Virginia to the leafy grounds of Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania — and the storylines converging at the 108th PGA Championship may be the richest in years.

The tournament runs May 14-17, with ESPN handling Thursday and Friday coverage and CBS taking the weekend. It marks the first PGA Championship at this historic Donald Ross design since 1962, when Gary Player lifted the Wanamaker Trophy. Aronimink will test every dimension of the game — accuracy, patience, and shotmaking under the most intense pressure in the sport.

The Defending Champion and His Rivals

Scottie Scheffler returns to defend the title he won in 2025. The World No. 1 made the deliberate choice to skip this week's Truist Championship to focus his preparation on Aronimink — a decision that signals how seriously he is approaching the defense. Current odds have him as the slight favourite.

Rory McIlroy enters after a disappointing R3 75 at Quail Hollow but with a significant motivating factor: a third PGA Championship title would take his career major total to seven, elevating him into the true all-time elite. He won the Wanamaker Trophy in 2012 and 2014 and arrives at Aronimink having also successfully defended his Masters title in April 2026.

Spieth's Grand Slam and Young's Triple Bid

Jordan Spieth stands one major away from the career Grand Slam — he has won the Masters, US Open, and The Open Championship, but has never lifted the Wanamaker Trophy in nine previous tries. This is his 10th attempt at completing the set, and there may not be a more compelling personal narrative in the entire field.

Cameron Young arrives in stunning form. The American has already won two of the year's biggest events — the Players Championship and the Cadillac Championship — and a PGA title would rank him among the most decorated single-season performers in modern golf. He will be one of the most watched players from the opening tee shot.

LIV Golf players in the field — including Bryson DeChambeau, Jon Rahm, and Dustin Johnson — are all eligible for the PGA Championship and bring additional star power and competition to what is already a loaded draw.

What's Next

The 2026 PGA Championship begins Thursday, May 14 at Aronimink Golf Club. ESPN coverage runs from 12:00-7:00 PM ET Thursday and Friday; CBS takes over for Saturday and Sunday. Mark the calendar — Aronimink's return to major championship golf after 64 years is one of the most anticipated events of the year.
