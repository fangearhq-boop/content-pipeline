Korda Is Four Points From the LPGA Hall of Fame — Can She Seal It in Cincinnati?

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 10, 2026

Every tournament Nelly Korda plays now carries the weight of potential history. The World No. 1 currently has 23 of the 27 points required for automatic LPGA Hall of Fame qualification — just four points from one of the most coveted distinctions in women's professional golf. And her next opportunity comes in Cincinnati next week.

The Kroger Queen City Championship at Maketewah Country Club runs May 14-17 in Cincinnati, Ohio, concurrent with the men's PGA Championship. Seven of the world's top 10 in the Rolex Women's World Golf Rankings are entered — making it one of the year's strongest LPGA fields — and Korda will be the headliner as the defending world No. 1.

What 2026 Looks Like Through Korda's Eyes

The numbers through 2026 have been exceptional. Korda has three LPGA victories this season, including the Chevron Championship — one of women's golf's four major championships. She has not finished outside the top 5 in any of her 2026 starts, a consistency that speaks to the totality of her game and her ability to compete at the highest level week after week.

The Hall of Fame points system rewards major victories and tournament wins on a formula that balances longevity with peak performance. Korda's rapid accumulation of points reflects both. She needs four more — achievable in a single strong week if the results align.

What History Looks Like Here

Entering the LPGA Hall of Fame via automatic qualification represents the gold standard in women's professional golf. Korda would join a select group of the sport's all-time legends. Achieving it while still in the prime of her career — before any significant decline in her competitive level — would be remarkable.

What's Next

The Kroger Queen City Championship begins Thursday, May 14 in Cincinnati. Whether Korda can add the Hall of Fame points she needs at Maketewah depends on how her game travels to Ohio — but given her 2026 form, there is no reason to bet against her. Four points. One tournament. The Hall of Fame beckons.
