LIV Virginia Final Round: Herbert Leads at 21-Under as Ballester's Stunning 60 Tightens the Field

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 10, 2026

Sunday's final round of the Maaden LIV Golf Virginia tournament at Trump National Golf Club in Sterling, Virginia promises fireworks — and Lucas Herbert had better bring his best game to protect a lead that has been a moving target all weekend.

Herbert, competing for Ripper GC, carries a three-shot advantage at 21-under par into the final round. The Australian has been exceptional through 54 holes — through the first two rounds he set a LIV 36-hole Strokes Gained record — but Sergio Garcia (18-under) is three back with the experience of a former Masters champion, and the leaderboard behind him is chaotic.

Ballester's Historic 60

The wildcard in Sunday's equation is Josele Ballester. The Spanish golfer fired a bogey-free 12-under 60 in Saturday's third round — one of the most remarkable rounds seen in LIV Golf competition — to move to 13-under par, eight shots behind Herbert but clearly in form that demands respect. Also at 13-under are Scott Vincent, Bryson DeChambeau, and Dean Burmester. Jon Rahm lurks at 12-under, nine back.

In a format that rewards aggressive play, a three-shot lead feels manageable but not comfortable against a field of this calibre.

What a Win Would Mean for Herbert

A victory today would be Herbert's first individual LIV title and would represent one of the defining moments of his professional career. A win also potentially improves his position in relation to major championship qualifying — a meaningful consideration for players competing exclusively on LIV Golf. Ripper GC, meanwhile, is positioned well in the team standings and would benefit from further points.

What's Next

The Maaden LIV Golf Virginia final round wraps up today at $30M in prize money. LIV-eligible major players — including DeChambeau, Rahm, and Dustin Johnson — then turn their attention to the PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club, beginning May 14. Sunday in Virginia will be a preview of who's carrying hot form into the year's second major.
