Truist Championship Final Round: Fitzpatrick Leads as Young, Reitan Give Chase and McIlroy Exits

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 10, 2026

The 2026 Truist Championship enters its final round Sunday with three players separated by just two shots at the top of the leaderboard — and a 36-hole leader who's no longer in sight. Alex Fitzpatrick holds a one-shot advantage at 14-under par after firing a 7-under 64 in Saturday's third round at Quail Hollow Club in Charlotte, North Carolina.

Norway's Kristoffer Reitan matched Fitzpatrick shot-for-shot with his own 64 and sits at 13-under in second place. The most dangerous name on the board, however, may be Cameron Young at 12-under — two shots back after a scorching 63 on Saturday. Young has already won the Players Championship and the Cadillac Championship in 2026 and knows exactly how to close tournaments.

Sungjae Im Falls Back

Sungjae Im, who led the tournament through 36 holes at 9-under, has fallen to 11-under and sits three shots back entering Sunday. He made an eagle on No. 10 to stay relevant but could not keep pace with the three players above him. His path back to the lead runs through some of the game's best players on one of the PGA Tour's most demanding layouts.

Quail Hollow's famous "Green Mile" — the 16th, 17th, and 18th holes — has decided countless finishes here. Whoever navigates that closing stretch best on Sunday will almost certainly be hoisting the trophy.

McIlroy's Collapse

The most jarring result Saturday belonged to Rory McIlroy. The Northern Irishman had entered the day four shots off the lead and appeared well-positioned to make a Sunday charge. Instead, he shot a 4-over 75 — six bogeys against just two birdies — and has removed himself from contention entirely. McIlroy has won this event four times (2010, 2015, 2021, 2024) and was pursuing a record fifth title at Quail Hollow.

What's Next

The Truist Championship concludes today with $20 million and 700 FedExCup points on the line. Whoever wins will arrive at next week's PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club (May 14-17) as one of the hottest players in the game. Fitzpatrick, Reitan, and Young all have the games to close — and the leaderboard is tight enough that nothing is guaranteed until the 72nd hole.
