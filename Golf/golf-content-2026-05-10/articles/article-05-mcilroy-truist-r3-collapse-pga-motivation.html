McIlroy's 75 at Quail Hollow Ends Truist Title Bid — Now He Heads to Aronimink With Something to Prove

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 10, 2026

Rory McIlroy arrived at Quail Hollow Club this week with a clear target: a record fifth Truist Championship victory at a course he has dominated for nearly two decades. He left Saturday's third round having shot a 4-over 75 — six bogeys against just two birdies — and effectively out of contention for the title he was chasing.

The mathematics are simple. McIlroy was four shots back entering Round 3 after a solid Round 2 67. A round in the middle 60s would have put him squarely in the conversation. Instead, the 75 dropped him well off the pace, and with Alex Fitzpatrick (14-under), Kristoffer Reitan (13-under), and Cameron Young (12-under) all playing exceptionally, there is simply no realistic path to a record fifth Quail Hollow title.

A Rare Stumble at a Familiar Course

McIlroy's relationship with Quail Hollow is one of the most remarkable player-course love affairs in modern golf. He first won the Wells Fargo Championship here in 2010 — his first PGA Tour victory — and returned for wins in 2015, 2021, and 2024. He knows every blade of grass on this property. That's what made Saturday's 75 all the more surprising. Six bogeys at a course you've conquered four times is not what anyone expected.

The Northern Irishman has been in excellent form overall in 2026 — he successfully defended his Masters title in April, the kind of achievement that defines careers. A bad round in May at a $20M Signature Event stings but shouldn't define where his season is headed.

What's Next

Rory McIlroy now turns his full attention to the PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club (May 14-17, Newtown Square PA). He won the Wanamaker Trophy in 2012 and 2014, and a third title would take his career major total to seven — one of the sport's most elite individual tallies. There is no better reset from a bad weekend than the week of a major. McIlroy has used adversity as fuel before, and Aronimink represents an opportunity to do so again. The 75 at Quail Hollow? Consider it motivation for Thursday.
