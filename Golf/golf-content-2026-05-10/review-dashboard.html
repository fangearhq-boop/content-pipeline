### Long-Form Post One of the most anticipated second majors in recent memory is just four days away — and the storylines at Aronimink Golf Club are stacking up. The 108th PGA Championship runs May 14-17 at Aronimink in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania. It's the first PGA Championship held at this historic Donald Ross design since 1962, when Gary Player lifted the Wanamaker Trophy. The course will demand precision and patience — and the world's best players will be tested from day one. Here are the four biggest storylines heading in: **Scheffler defends.** The World No. 1 and 2025 PGA champion skipped the Truist Championship to prepare for Aronimink. He's been runner-up three times running in recent weeks — the competitive hunger is clearly there. **McIlroy's career at the crossroads.** Rory has won this event twice (2012, 2014) and a third would take his career major total to seven — putting him in truly elite company. After his R3 75 at Truist, he'll arrive at Aronimink with plenty of motivation. **Spieth's Grand Slam quest.** Jordan Spieth has everything — Masters, US Open, Open Championship — except the PGA. This is his 10th attempt at completing the career Grand Slam. No one in history has won their final Grand Slam leg at the PGA Championship, but Spieth doesn't care about that stat. **Cameron Young's triple threat.** Young has already won the Players and the Cadillac Championship. A PGA title would make him one of the most decorated single-season performers in modern golf. TV: ESPN handles Thursday-Friday, CBS takes Saturday-Sunday. Tee times begin May 14. ### Image Post Caption Scheffler defends. McIlroy chases No. 7. Spieth hunts the Grand Slam. The PGA Championship begins May 14 at Aronimink. #PGAChampionship
