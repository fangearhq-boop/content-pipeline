Charles Schwab Challenge Preview: Åberg, Fowler, Smalley Lead Wide-Open Colonial Field

By Marcus Cole | May 27, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The Charles Schwab Challenge begins Thursday at Colonial Country Club in Fort Worth, Texas — and this year's field is unusually wide open. Scottie Scheffler and Rory McIlroy are absent, seven of the world's top 20 are present, and at least four compelling storylines point to a leaderboard that could go in multiple directions. The purse stands at $9.9 million. The course is revamped. The favorites are real but beatable.

Ludvig Åberg enters as the statistical favorite at +900 — a commanding advantage by any measure. He has averaged nearly three strokes gained per round over the last three months, more than a full stroke ahead of any other player in the Colonial field. His recent form includes a T4 at the PGA Championship and T8 at the Truist Championship. But Åberg is also without a win since the Genesis Invitational last season, and the narrative of a dominant player who hasn't found a way to close on Sunday will follow him to Hogan's Alley.

Colonial Country Club After Hanse's Touch

Colonial Country Club has been the home of the Charles Schwab Challenge for 80 consecutive years — the longest uninterrupted event-venue relationship on the PGA Tour. In 2023, architect Gil Hanse completed a significant restoration that included lowering most of the greens to make them more receptive to approach shots, updating the infrastructure, and making changes to several of the par 3s. The renovated Colonial is a different test from what players have seen in recent years.

The course plays as a par 70 at 7,289 yards, with average fairway width of 27 yards — third-narrowest on the entire PGA Tour. Off the tee, accuracy matters far more than distance. Players who can land the ball in the short grass and hit precise approaches to Hanse's lower, more accessible greens will have a significant advantage over those who rely on raw power.

Four Names to Watch

Åberg is the betting favorite, but three other names command serious attention. Rickie Fowler is riding his best form in years — he has recorded top-10 finishes at the RBC Heritage, Cadillac Championship, and Arnold Palmer Invitational, and finished T2 at the Truist Championship. His ability to dissect difficult courses and his across-the-board statistical improvement make him one of the most compelling options in Fort Worth.

Alex Smalley arrives from a T2 finish at the PGA Championship and carries six consecutive top-25 finishes. His short game transformation in 2026 — from losing 0.62 strokes per round around the greens in 2024 to gaining 0.45 per round this season — has turned a good player into a complete one. His iron play was already elite; now he can convert it into scores.

Justin Thomas has a "promising trend" at Colonial — Golf Digest's power rankings identified historical patterns in his performance at this course that make him worth watching. Defending champion Ben Griffin returns at +2200, and Alex Smalley's teammate in the 54-hole PGA Championship leaderboard has the course precision this layout demands.

How to Watch

The Charles Schwab Challenge runs May 28-31 at Colonial Country Club, Fort Worth, Texas. Coverage airs on Golf Channel and ESPN+ throughout the week, with weekend coverage moving to NBC/Peacock. Tee times begin Thursday morning. The defending champion Ben Griffin won last year in a final-round performance that cemented his status as one of the tour's emerging names. This year, he enters as a +2200 outside contender in a field that lacks the usual dominance of Scheffler and McIlroy.
