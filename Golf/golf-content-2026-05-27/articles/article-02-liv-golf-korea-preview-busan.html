Korean Golf Club Comes Home: LIV Golf Korea Preview — May 28-31, Asiad CC Busan

By Ryan Calloway | May 27, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

LIV Golf returns to South Korea on Thursday for the ninth event of the 2026 season — and for the first time, the Korea stop has a home team for the home crowd. The former Iron Heads GC franchise has been rebranded as Korean Golf Club (KGC), and the team has added Korean players Ben An and Min-kyu Kim to the roster specifically for this event. Asiad Country Club in Busan will host a crowd that finally has someone to cheer for in their own country.

The tournament runs May 28-31 with shotgun starts at 1:15 PM local time (Rounds 1-3) and 1:05 PM for the final round on Sunday. The format: 72 holes, no cut, 57 players in 13 teams of four plus five wild cards.

The Course: Rees Jones's Redesign

Asiad Country Club is a Rees Jones redesign that plays as a 7,024-yard par 70 — short by modern standards but demanding in character. The layout splits into two distinct nines: the Valley nine on the front, featuring narrow, tree-lined fairways that reward precision over power, and the Lake nine on the back, which opens up slightly and offers more scoring opportunities.

The signature hole is the par-3 sixth — 159 yards, downhill, to an island green. It is the kind of hole that produces drama in every round: birdies from confident players who commit to the shot, double bogeys from those who second-guess and find the water. The course has hosted the BMW Ladies Championship and the 2002 Asian Games, giving it a pedigree that matches LIV's ambition for the event.

Key Storylines

Bryson DeChambeau defending his Korea individual title heads the storyline list. DeChambeau won the 2025 LIV Korea event and returns to a course where his power and imagination off the tee create advantages on a layout that rewards risk. He enters the event having finished the season to this point in typical fashion — capable of outright dominance or unexpected struggles, rarely producing middle-ground results.

Anthony Kim's continued resurgence is one of the biggest individual stories in golf this year. His dramatic win at LIV Adelaide — where he beat both DeChambeau and Jon Rahm in a final-round battle — announced his return in definitive terms. The interest in watching him sustain that level through Korea and into the summer stretch makes him the most watched player not named Rahm in the LIV field.

The 4Aces GC enters as the team standings leader, and their focus Thursday through Sunday will be on extending that advantage. Team competition runs parallel to individual scoring — the team whose four players produce the lowest aggregate score wins the team title, which carries separate prize money and standings implications for the season-long ranking.

Off the Course

LIV Golf has incorporated entertainment programming into its Korea event: DJ Peggy Gou, the globally recognized Korean-born DJ, performs at the concert stage following the third round on Saturday, May 30. The combination of elite golf, a home team, and high-profile entertainment is central to LIV's strategy for building an Asian audience that the traditional tour has never fully captured.

The Korea Times noted that LIV's visit comes "amid uncertainty over future" — a reminder that questions about the tour's long-term structure and PGA Tour relationship remain unresolved. That backdrop does not diminish the quality of the golf or the significance of the Korean Golf Club's home debut. For now, Busan is ready for its LIV moment.
