## Story Angles

### STORY 1: Byron Nelson R1 Recap
**Primary angle:** Taylor Moore at 62 is the surprise leader, but the story of R1 is how Scheffler at -5 is still in it at a course he dominated last year — and how Koepka's resurgence elevates the drama.
**Secondary angle:** With Scheffler at 4 back and Koepka at 1 back, this leaderboard is perfectly set up for either a defending-champion charge or an overdue win for a returning champion.

### STORY 2: Koepka's Putter Change
**Primary angle:** One equipment change, one bogey-free round, the lowest score of his 2026 season. Putter changes are often overhyped — except when they immediately produce a 63.
**Secondary angle:** Koepka has always been at his best in big moments. A tournament that includes Scheffler and Si Woo Kim — and plays on a course that rewards precision — is exactly where he can win.

### STORY 3: Aaron Rai Praise
**Primary angle:** Nicklaus and Faldo praising Rai places his win within the historical lineage of the sport in a way that no statistics can. When the two greatest champions in history acknowledge what you did, it matters differently.
**Secondary angle:** The cultural reaction to the first player of Indian heritage to win a men's major continues to build — a story that will grow rather than fade.

### STORY 4: Nelly Korda Dominance
**Primary angle:** Three wins and three runner-ups in six starts is statistically extraordinary. The Annika Sorenstam 2001 comparison is the most apt framework for understanding what Korda is doing.
**Secondary angle:** With the US Women's Open coming June 4-7, Korda is entering the most prestigious event of the year on the hottest stretch of her career.

### STORY 5: LIV Korea Preview
**Primary angle:** Herbert's wire-to-wire Virginia dominance earns the spotlight, but Korea is the next chapter — and LIV's first Asian event of 2026 always attracts outsized attention in Asia-Pacific golf markets.
