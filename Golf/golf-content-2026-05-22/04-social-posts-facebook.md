### STORY 4: Nelly Korda Is Rewriting LPGA History in 2026

**Long-Form Post (6:00 PM ET)**
There is a comparison being made in women's golf right now that you do not make lightly. Analysts are invoking Annika Sorenstam's 2001 season — arguably the most dominant performance by any player in the history of the LPGA Tour.

And it applies to Nelly Korda in 2026.

In six starts this season, Korda has won three times and finished second three times. Six starts. Zero finishes outside the top two. The only player who achieved six consecutive top-2 finishes to begin a season was Sorenstam in 2001.

Her wins include the Chevron Championship — her third career major, which returned her to the world No. 1 ranking — and two subsequent victories, including her win at the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba by four strokes. She became the first player since Lorena Ochoa in 2008 to win an LPGA tournament by four strokes or more in back-to-back weeks.

The statistical picture is similarly remarkable. Her bogey-or-worse rate of 7.97% is not just the lowest on the LPGA Tour — it's more than four full percentage points better than anyone else in the field. She is getting up-and-down at a 75.0% clip, also the best on tour.

At 27 years old, she became the youngest American to reach 18 career LPGA victories in 46 years — since Nancy Lopez did it in 1980.

The US Women's Open is scheduled for June 4-7 at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California. Korda arrives as the overwhelming favorite and the form player of the decade. The only question is whether anyone in the world can disrupt what she's doing right now.
