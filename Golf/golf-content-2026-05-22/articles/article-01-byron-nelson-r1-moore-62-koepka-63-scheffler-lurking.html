Byron Nelson Round 1: Moore's 62 Leads, Koepka Fires 63, Scheffler Lurks Four Back

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 22, 2026

The first round of the CJ Cup Byron Nelson at TPC Craig Ranch produced a tight, compelling leaderboard — a surprise leader, a major champion finding his putting stroke for the first time all year, and the defending champion positioned exactly where dangerous players like to be: four back, lurking.

Taylor Moore set the pace with a 9-under 62, one clear of Brooks Koepka and Jesper Svensson. Si Woo Kim sits one further back at 7-under. Scottie Scheffler, who won this tournament in 2025 and knows TPC Craig Ranch as well as anyone in the field, carded a 5-under 66 and trails by four.

Moore's Day

Taylor Moore's 62 was the kind of first-round performance that puts a player in control of their own tournament. At 9-under par, he has a cushion over a strong leaderboard and the mental advantage of knowing he has room to work with over the next three days.

TPC Craig Ranch rewards accurate iron play and demands precise driving on a course where wind can shift conditions quickly. Moore navigated both elements cleanly, and his 62 stands as one of the better opening rounds the Byron Nelson has seen.

Koepka's Resurgence

The story of Round 1 was not the leader. It was Brooks Koepka. Armed with a new putter deployed for the first time, Koepka carded a bogey-free 8-under 63 — the lowest score of his 2026 PGA Tour season. He made six birdies and an eagle, holing four putts from outside 10 feet in the kind of putting display that had been absent from his game for months.

One shot back. No bogeys. New putter. This is a man who has found something.

Scheffler's Position

Scottie Scheffler at -5 after Round 1 at a course he dominated last year is not a player in trouble. It is a player managing his game through the first 18 holes and saving his best for when it matters. Scheffler, Kim, and Koepka tee off together in Round 2 on Friday afternoon — a featured group in the making.

Chasing a four-shot deficit over three rounds at TPC Craig Ranch is well within his capability. He is the defending champion and the world No. 1. A 66 in Round 1 is not a stumble. It is a setup.
