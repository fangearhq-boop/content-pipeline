New Putter, Zero Bogeys: Koepka's 63 Is the Best Round of His 2026 Season

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 22, 2026

Sometimes the most significant change a golfer can make is the smallest one. Brooks Koepka arrived at TPC Craig Ranch with a new putter in his bag for the first time in 2026. On Thursday, he went out and shot the lowest score of his season.

A bogey-free 8-under 63. Six birdies. One eagle. Four putts made from outside 10 feet. The kind of day that turns a difficult stretch of a season into a vivid memory of what is possible when everything clicks at once.

The Putting Details

The specific contributions of the new putter were hard to miss. Koepka holed a 17-foot birdie putt on the 11th hole — his second hole of the round, as the group started on the back nine — to set an early tone. He then made a 13-foot eagle putt on the 12th, turning a promising start into something approaching dominant.

He made two more putts from outside 10 feet across the front nine, converting every meaningful opportunity the round offered him. For a player whose putting statistics have been a genuine liability through the early months of the 2026 season, the improvement was immediate and dramatic.

He never made a bogey. Over 18 holes at a course that demands precision and punishes mistakes, Koepka played clean, sharp, decisive golf from start to finish.

What It Means

Brooks Koepka's career has been defined by clutch putting in major championships. His record in the game's biggest events — built on precise distance control and a flat, repeatable stroke — made him one of the most feared finishers in golf for the better part of a decade. When that putting became inconsistent, the results followed.

One round does not confirm a trend. But one round that produces his lowest score of the season, in a field that includes the world No. 1, with a leaderboard position that puts him one shot off the lead heading into Round 2 — that is a very significant round.
