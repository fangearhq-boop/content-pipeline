Jack Nicklaus and Nick Faldo Praise Aaron Rai After Historic PGA Championship Victory

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 22, 2026

More than a week after the most surprising result in 2026 major championship golf, the reverberations of Aaron Rai's victory at the PGA Championship continue to reach the highest levels of the sport.

Jack Nicklaus — 18 major championships, the most by any player in the history of professional golf — and Nick Faldo — six majors, four Ryder Cup captaincy appearances, and one of the most meticulous careers the European game has ever produced — have both publicly praised Rai's composure and performance at Aronimink Golf Club.

When those are the people commenting on what you achieved, you have achieved something that matters in a deep and lasting way.

What Rai Did

Rai won the 108th PGA Championship at 9-under par, finishing three shots clear of the field with a final-round 65 that included an eagle on the par-5 ninth — made with a 40-foot putt — and a birdie at the par-3 17th that came via a nearly 70-foot putt and extended his lead to three with one hole remaining.

He was ranked 44th in the world entering the week. His pre-tournament odds were 290-to-1. The last non-American to win the PGA Championship was Jason Day in 2015. The last Englishman to win it was Jim Barnes — who won the first two editions of the tournament, in 1916 and 1919.

Rai was also the first player of Indian heritage to win a men's major championship — a historic breakthrough that generated significant cultural and media attention across the UK, India, and the broader international golf community.

The Scale of the Response

The reaction from Nicklaus and Faldo is the highest-profile dimension of a much broader response. Peers, analysts, past champions, and fans have weighed in across social media and broadcast golf coverage for days since Rai lifted the Wanamaker Trophy. The cultural significance of his win — first player of Indian heritage, first Englishman in over a century — has driven coverage beyond the standard major championship media cycle.

There is also the simple matter of the performance itself. Rai's composure under pressure on Sunday at Aronimink was the kind of final-round performance that genuine champions produce — not lucky, not scrambled, but controlled and precise when the moment was at its biggest.

What's Next

Rai's five-year exemptions into the Masters, US Open, Open Championship, and The Players, plus a lifetime PGA Championship exemption, have reshaped his competitive calendar permanently. His profile and appearance schedule will change significantly in the months ahead. Aaron Rai won the 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club on May 17 at 9-under par, winning by three shots. His next major start: the US Open.
