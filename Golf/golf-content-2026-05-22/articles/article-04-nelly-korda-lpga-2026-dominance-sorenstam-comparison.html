Nelly Korda's 2026 LPGA Season Is Historically Dominant — and the US Women's Open Is Next

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 22, 2026

There is a comparison being made in women's professional golf that should not be made casually. Analysts who study the history of the LPGA Tour are invoking Annika Sorenstam's 2001 season — one of the most dominant in the modern history of the sport — to describe what Nelly Korda is doing in 2026.

Three wins. Three runner-up finishes. Six starts total. Zero finishes outside the top two.

The only player in LPGA history to begin a season with six consecutive top-two finishes was Sorenstam in 2001. Now there are two.

The Wins

Korda's three victories in 2026 include the Chevron Championship — her third career major title — which returned her to the world No. 1 ranking. The Chevron victory was followed by the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba, won by four strokes. In doing so, she became the first player since Lorena Ochoa in 2008 to win an LPGA tournament by four strokes or more in back-to-back weeks.

Her third win of the season came shortly after. Three wins from six starts — and none of the three runner-up finishes were close either. The consistency of her performance from the first week of the season through May has been extraordinary.

The Statistical Case

The numbers tell the same story the leaderboard does. Korda's bogey-or-worse rate of 7.97% is the lowest on the LPGA Tour — and it is more than four percentage points better than the next-best player. When the margin between No. 1 and No. 2 in a statistical category exceeds four percentage points, that is not a close race. That is dominance.

She is making up-and-down at a 75.0% clip, also the best rate on tour. Her ball-striking, which was already elite before this season, has reached a level where opponents are competing for second place against her on most given days.

At 27, she became the youngest American to reach 18 career LPGA victories in 46 years — since Nancy Lopez did it in 1980.

What's Next

The US Women's Open is scheduled for June 4-7 at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California. Korda enters as the overwhelming favorite and the form player of the season.
