Scheffler Grinds to Make Memorial Cut — 75 Consecutive Cuts and 11 Days to the U.S. Open

By Ryan Calloway | June 7, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Scottie Scheffler did not hide from the uncomfortable truth of his week at Muirfield Village. "Worst swing I've had in two years," he said after the second round, 10 shots behind the leaders heading into the weekend.

He made the cut anyway. He always makes the cut.

Sunday at Muirfield Village marks Scheffler's 75th consecutive PGA Tour cut made — a streak of remarkable consistency from a player who has won this tournament in each of the past two seasons and who is now chasing an impossible dream before the month is out.

What Went Wrong at Memorial

Scheffler arrived at Muirfield Village as a two-time defending champion, a player who has made this Ohio layout his personal property in recent seasons. The week did not produce the form to match that pedigree.

His self-diagnosis was unusually candid. The swing felt wrong, the misses were piling up, and his scoring suffered accordingly. A double-bogey on the par-three 16th hole in a round that was already off-pace encapsulated the struggle. He was 10 shots back entering the weekend — a distance that rarely produces champions even when everything clicks.

Nothing has clicked at Muirfield Village this week. But he played. He persevered. And he added another cut to a streak that now stretches 75 events.

The Streak as Context

Making 75 consecutive PGA Tour cuts is not an accident. It requires playing well enough, week after week, to be standing on a golf course on the weekend when the field has been reduced by half. Even during a week when Scheffler openly admits his swing is broken, the floor of his game is high enough to survive.

That floor — the minimum level of performance that a player as talented as Scheffler produces even when nothing is working — is what separates elite professionals from everyone else. He did not contend this week. He kept his streak alive and managed to accumulate a few more world ranking points in the process.

The U.S. Open in 11 Days

Here is why none of this particularly matters beyond the historical footnote: Scheffler has been scouting Shinnecock Hills.

The U.S. Open begins June 18 at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York — 11 days from Sunday at Muirfield Village. The final round falls on June 21. That date, for the record, is Scottie Scheffler's 30th birthday.

Scheffler has reportedly been at Shinnecock to scout the course in preparation for the country's most demanding major championship. A player who wins two consecutive Memorial Tournaments and then struggles at the third is often simply resting between peaks — the swings of form that even the world's best experience.

The swing that was wrong at Muirfield Village has time to become right before Shinnecock. His preparation there is already underway.

What's Next

Scheffler finishes the Memorial Tournament on Sunday, then shifts his full focus to Shinnecock Hills and the 2026 U.S. Open beginning June 18. Whatever happens at Muirfield Village this weekend, the real measuring stick for his 2026 season is 11 days away — and he has already started getting ready for it.
