Asterisk Talley, 17, Shoots 66 at Riviera — The Lowest Amateur Weekend Round in USWO History

By Jake Torres | June 7, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Her name alone makes you stop reading. Asterisk Talley. Seventeen years old. From Fresno, California. Competing as an amateur at the U.S. Women's Open.

And on Saturday at Riviera Country Club, she shot a 5-under 66 — the lowest round ever posted by an amateur on a weekend at the U.S. Women's Open championship.

Let that settle for a moment. Not the lowest this week. Not the lowest by a teenager. The lowest ever. By anyone who was not a professional at the time of their round, on a Saturday or Sunday, in the 78-year history of the U.S. Women's Open.

What 66 Means at Riviera

Riviera Country Club is not forgiving. The club has hosted the Genesis Invitational on the PGA Tour for decades and has earned a reputation as one of the premier tests of precision golf on any tour. Its kikuyu rough is punishing. Its greens have subtle breaks that take experience to read. Its layout rewards players who hit fairways, control trajectory, and think strategically.

A 5-under 66 at this course is a genuine achievement. In the context of the U.S. Women's Open — a major championship designed to produce the toughest conditions possible to identify a clear champion — it is remarkable.

Talley did this at age 17, as an amateur, on a Saturday when every professional in the field was chasing the same leaderboard.

Who Is Asterisk Talley?

Talley is a junior amateur from Fresno, California who has been building a reputation in the amateur circuit as one of the most talented young players in the country. Her name is the first thing people notice — and then the game takes over.

Playing in the U.S. Women's Open as an amateur means competing without prize money, which Talley cannot receive under the rules of amateur status. If she contends on Sunday, she will be competing for the history and the experience alone. That is the nature of amateur golf at the highest level — you play for something more pure than a check.

The lowest-profile player near the top of the USWO leaderboard heading into Sunday is a 17-year-old with a name no one will forget once they hear it.

Sunday's Opportunity

Talley will tee it up on Sunday at Riviera in the final round of the first U.S. Women's Open ever played at this iconic venue. She cannot win the trophy — amateurs are eligible to win the championship, though rare — but she can absolutely compete, continue making history, and announce herself to the golf world in a way that will define the beginning of her career.

Whatever happens on Sunday, Saturday at Riviera already belongs to Asterisk Talley. The record is already set. The moment is already real.
