Memorial Tournament Faces Marathon Sunday — Poston and Gerard Tied at -9 with 13 Holes Left in Round 3

By Ryan Calloway | June 7, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Muirfield Village Golf Club will host one of the most demanding days on the PGA Tour schedule on Sunday, after thunderstorms forced the suspension of the Memorial Tournament's third round on Saturday afternoon at 4:35 PM ET.

J.T. Poston and Ryan Gerard, tied for the lead at 9 under par, each have 13 holes remaining in their third round. They will need to complete those holes when play resumes at 7:30 AM ET on Sunday before the fourth round begins at 11 AM. The potential total: upwards of 30 holes in a single day, at a course that demands precision on every shot.

The State of the Leaderboard

Poston's position at the top of the leaderboard has been built on extraordinary consistency. His second round featured eight birdies and zero bogeys — a 65 that was nine shots better than the field average on that day. He has been the most precise player at Muirfield Village this week, and despite the suspension interrupting his momentum, he enters Sunday as one of the favorites.

Gerard shares that lead, and his own game through two-and-a-half rounds has been similarly tight. The co-leaders will play together in Sunday's final group, finishing their rain-suspended holes before transitioning directly into the championship round.

Sam Burns entered the suspension in third place at 8 under par, one shot back. The exact positions of the rest of the field depend on how many holes each player completed before play was called — creating an unusual dynamic where leaderboard positions may shift before the final round even begins.

The Format of Sunday

The PGA Tour's schedule for Sunday is structured but demanding. Third-round play resumes at 7:30 AM ET, with players completing their remaining holes in the order they were suspended. The fourth round begins at 11 AM ET, with players going off in threesomes from two tees through 12:45 PM.

For Poston and Gerard — each with 13 holes still to play in the third round — the math is straightforward and difficult: they could be walking off the 18th green from their third round just as their tee time for the fourth round approaches. Total golf: as many as 31 holes in one day, followed by immediate recovery for the following week's events.

What Poston Needs to Hold On

Poston's game has been built on precision this week. Muirfield Village punishes inaccuracy, and the ability to hit fairways and greens at this facility is non-negotiable. His third round had been proceeding as well as his second when the storms arrived — the consistency suggests a player fully locked in.

The challenge of marathon Sunday is not purely physical. The mental fatigue of completing one round, tallying the score, then immediately resetting for another round is a challenge that requires genuine psychological resilience. In the past, players who have led through marathon Sundays at various Tour events have sometimes found the compressed schedule works in their favor — there is no time to overthink, only time to play.

What's Next

The Memorial Tournament presented by Workday will crown its 2026 champion on Sunday at Muirfield Village Golf Club in Dublin, Ohio. Third-round play resumes at 7:30 AM ET; the fourth round begins at 11 AM. The $20 million purse awaits the winner.

Poston and Gerard lead. Sam Burns is one back. A full field will descend on Muirfield Village for one of the longest tournament days any of them will ever experience.
