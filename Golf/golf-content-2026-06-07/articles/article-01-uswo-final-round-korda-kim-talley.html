Korda and Kim Share USWO Lead Heading Into Sunday at Riviera — With a 17-Year-Old Amateur in the Mix

By Marcus Cole | June 7, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The first U.S. Women's Open at Riviera Country Club has produced exactly the kind of drama that makes major championship Sunday worth waking up for.

Nelly Korda and Sei Young Kim share the 54-hole lead at 6 under par, separated by nothing going into Sunday's final round. Charley Hull shot 65 on Saturday — the low round of the week — to put herself in contention. And somewhere in the mix is Asterisk Talley, a 17-year-old amateur from Fresno, California, who posted a 5-under 66 on Saturday to set the best amateur weekend round in USWO history.

Any of them could win the 2026 U.S. Women's Open. None of them are going to make it easy on the others.

Korda's Saturday Finish

Nelly Korda is the World No. 1 player and was the pre-tournament favorite at Riviera. Saturday afternoon put her through the kind of test that separates elite players from everyone else: her ball-striking was off — she described herself as dealing with a "funky grip" — but she was not going to let the round slip away.

Korda birdied holes 16, 17, and 18 on Saturday to card a 67 and take her share of the lead. Three consecutive birdies down the stretch at Riviera, with the U.S. Women's Open lead on the line, is not an accident. That is what championship players do.

She carries the pressure of being the favorite, but also the confidence of a player who has won three majors and 17 LPGA Tour events. The one tournament missing from her collection remains the U.S. Women's Open. Sunday at Riviera is her chance to change that.

Kim and Hull as Co-Contenders

Sei Young Kim's presence at the top of the leaderboard is a reminder that major championships do not always reward the most glamorous names. She is a proven winner at this level — a KPMG Women's PGA champion — and 6 under par at a major in the final round is exactly the kind of position she has shown she can handle.

Hull's 65 on Saturday was the low round of the week at Riviera. The Englishwoman birdied holes 17 and 18 to close her round and put herself squarely in the conversation. A player who shoots 65 on Saturday has timing and momentum working in her favor.

The Amateur Who Rewrote History

And then there is Asterisk Talley.

The 17-year-old from Fresno, California shot a 5-under 66 on Saturday — the lowest round ever posted by an amateur on a weekend at the U.S. Women's Open. She is competing against professionals with years of major championship experience, and she is holding her own.

The U.S. Women's Open occasionally produces amateur moments that become part of its folklore. Sunday at Riviera could add another chapter.

What's Next

Final round tee times are set for Sunday at Riviera Country Club. The first U.S. Women's Open at this historic venue is one round away from producing its champion. Korda, Kim, Hull, Talley, and a tight leaderboard with multiple contenders make Sunday genuinely unpredictable.

Watch it live Sunday on USA and Peacock. The history of Riviera's first major is waiting to be written.
