Hatton Leads LIV Andalucia Into Championship Sunday — Garcia Chases at His Home Course

By Marcus Cole | June 7, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Tyrrell Hatton arrived at Championship Sunday at Valderrama holding a two-shot lead at 10 under par, with an Open Championship exemption and the LIV Golf Andalucia title on the line. And somewhere in the final group behind him is Sergio Garcia — who has played Valderrama more times than any golfer alive and who has the Spanish crowd entirely on his side.

For pure theater, few golf settings can match this one.

Hatton's Commanding Position

Hatton has been the most consistent player at Real Club Valderrama throughout the week. His third round of 4-under 67 extended his lead to two shots, and the pattern of his play — patient, precise, comfortable on the demanding Spanish course — suggests a player in complete control of his game.

The precedent for Hatton leading into a LIV Golf final day is encouraging. He carried a four-shot advantage into the final round at LIV Golf Nashville in 2024, where he won the event by six strokes. His composure under pressure at this format has been tested and proven.

A two-shot lead at Valderrama going into Sunday is not insurmountable for the rest of the field — but it is a genuine advantage at a course that rewards patience over aggression.

Garcia at Home

Thomas Detry rounds out the final group, offering a capable dark-horse challenge. But the most compelling character in Sunday's final act is Sergio Garcia.

Garcia, the captain of Fireballs GC on the LIV Golf circuit, has a relationship with Valderrama that spans decades. He won the 1999 Volvo Masters at this course as a 19-year-old and has been a fixture at major European events held here throughout his career. When Garcia plays Valderrama, the Spanish fans treat it as something between a sporting event and a homecoming celebration.

His deficit going into Sunday means he will need to make an early move while Hatton remains patient. Garcia has the experience and course knowledge to mount a charge. Whether Sunday's version of him delivers it is the question.

What Is at Stake

Beyond the LIV Golf Andalucia title, an Open Championship exemption is available to the event winner — a meaningful prize that connects LIV Golf to the oldest major in golf history. For players in the LIV circuit who miss traditional qualifying routes, the exemption represents a path to one of the sport's most prestigious stages.

Hatton, who has played in Open Championships throughout his career, would likely welcome another guaranteed entry. Garcia, a Ryder Cup veteran and a Masters champion, has the same motivations. The Claret Jug invitation makes Sunday's outcome even more consequential.

What's Next

Championship Sunday at LIV Golf Andalucia concludes Sunday afternoon at Real Club Valderrama in Sotogrande, Spain. Hatton leads. Garcia chases. The crowd at one of Europe's most storied golf venues is ready.
