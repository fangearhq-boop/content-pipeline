### STORY 2: Memorial Tournament Long Sunday

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 6:00 AM ET

The most grueling day of the PGA Tour season is unfolding at Muirfield Village Golf Club on Sunday.

Saturday's third round of the Memorial Tournament was suspended at 4:35 PM ET due to severe thunderstorms rolling through Dublin, Ohio. When J.T. Poston and Ryan Gerard — co-leaders at 9 under par — walked off the sixth green, play was called for the day. Both players have 13 holes remaining in their third round.

The schedule for Sunday: R3 resumes at 7:30 AM ET, with the final round beginning at 11 AM (groups of three off two tees through 12:45 PM).

That means Poston and Gerard could play upwards of 31 holes on Sunday — finishing their third round in the morning and immediately competing in the fourth. It is exactly the kind of physical and mental test that separates champions from contenders.

Poston has been the story of this tournament. His second round featured eight birdies, zero bogeys — a 65 that was 9 shots better than the field average on the same day. He is grinding beautifully.

Can he hold on through a full day of golf? Sunday will tell us.

Her name is Asterisk Talley. On Saturday at Riviera Country Club, she shot 5-under 66 — a number that would be exceptional for any professional player, let alone a teenager competing as an amateur in the country's most prestigious women's major.

Talley is playing Sunday. She is 17. She is an amateur.

Thomas Detry rounds out the final group, giving us three distinct narratives: the cool favorite, the beloved local hero, and the dark-horse contender.

### STORY 5: Scheffler Grinding at Memorial

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 3:30 PM ET

Scottie Scheffler knows exactly what his swing looks like right now, and he is not happy about it.

"Worst swing I've had in two years," he said after navigating the Memorial Tournament's second round. He was 10 shots behind the leaders entering the weekend — a number that rarely produces champions.

But he made the cut. He always makes the cut. Sunday at Muirfield Village will mark the 75th consecutive PGA Tour cut he has survived — a streak of extraordinary consistency from a player who has won the Memorial Tournament in each of the past two seasons.

This week's Memorial is not going to produce a Scheffler three-peat. But that is almost beside the point. He is here. He is competing. And he is using this week as preparation for something larger.

The U.S. Open begins June 18 at Shinnecock Hills in Southampton, New York. Scheffler has been scouting the course, and the final round of the U.S. Open falls on June 21 — his 30th birthday.

He will not win the Memorial. He is going to win the round that counts on June 21.
