LPGA Ford Championship Preview: Coughlin's Momentum Meets Korda's Ambition

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

April 12, 2026

While Augusta National prepares to crown a Masters champion on Sunday, the LPGA Tour is already turning its attention to the Ford Championship — a 72-hole stroke play event at El Caballero Country Club in Tarzana, California, starting Thursday, April 16.

The event arrives at a compelling moment in the women's tour calendar. Lauren Coughlin won the Aramco Championship at Shadow Creek in Las Vegas last week by five shots, defeating a field that included Nelly Korda and producing the most dominant individual performance of the LPGA's spring swing so far. She heads to Southern California carrying genuine momentum and a confidence level that one convincing victory can produce.

Coughlin's Aramco Platform

Coughlin's five-shot margin of victory at Shadow Creek was not the result of a weak field — it was the product of four rounds of golf at a level she has worked to achieve for years. Her first win in two years, the Aramco Championship offered a reminder that consistency and patience in professional golf can eventually produce exactly the kind of performance the game demands at its highest level.

El Caballero Country Club in Tarzana presents a different challenge than Shadow Creek's desert design. The California course is set against a hillside landscape with narrower corridors and premium placement demands. Coughlin's game — built on ball-striking accuracy and aggressive putting — should translate, but every course is its own test.

Korda's Response

Nelly Korda finished as the runner-up at the Aramco Championship. For a player of Korda's competitive disposition, runner-up is not a result that sits comfortably. The reigning LPGA world No. 1 has spent the early part of 2026 near the top of leaderboards regularly, and the Ford Championship gives her an immediate opportunity to convert those near-misses into a win.

The head-to-head narrative between Coughlin and Korda entering El Caballero — winner versus runner-up from the previous week — gives the Ford Championship an added subplot that elevates its Sunday drama before a ball is struck.

What's Next

The LPGA Ford Championship tees off Thursday, April 16 at El Caballero Country Club and concludes Sunday, April 19. The full field will be announced ahead of the event. Following the Ford Championship, the LPGA Tour continues a busy spring schedule with several events ahead of the women's major season. Golf Fanrecap will have full coverage throughout the week.
