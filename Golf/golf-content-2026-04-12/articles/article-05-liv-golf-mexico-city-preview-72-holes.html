LIV Golf Mexico City Preview: New Format, New Era, Same Questions

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

April 12, 2026

LIV Golf arrives at Club de Golf Chapultepec in Mexico City this week carrying one of the most talked-about performance records from the 2026 Masters: all 10 LIV Golf players who competed at Augusta National missed the cut. For a tour that has spent three years arguing for its place alongside the world's best, the collective result was a difficult story to tell.

The Mexico City event — running April 16-19 — gives the LIV field its first full competitive slate since Augusta, and it does so under a format that represents the most significant structural change in the tour's history: 72-hole stroke play, replacing the 54-hole events that defined LIV Golf's first three seasons.

The 72-Hole Shift

The decision to extend LIV events to 72 holes was a meaningful concession to critics who argued that 54-hole tournaments didn't fully test the depth of a player's game the way traditional competitive formats do. Every major championship, every Rolex Series event, every WM Phoenix Open is 72 holes. The shortened format made LIV an outlier at the very moment it was trying to establish credibility alongside conventional tours.

Seventy-two holes tests players differently. It rewards consistent ball-striking and course management over a longer arc, it places greater demands on physical conditioning, and it changes the mathematical calculus of comebacks and Sunday pressure. Players who thrived at 54 holes may find that the additional round either amplifies or exposes their games in ways the short format never revealed.

The Mexico City Context

Club de Golf Chapultepec is one of LIV Golf's flagship venues — a historic course in the heart of one of the world's largest cities. The event draws strong attendance from Mexico City's golf-enthusiastic population, and the course itself — known from its appearances in the Mexican Open — provides a legitimate competitive test with demanding elevation changes and tight fairways.

The LIV Golf field includes former major champions Phil Mickelson, Brooks Koepka, and Dustin Johnson among others who built their reputations on the PGA Tour before joining the breakaway tour. Their Masters performances were a reminder that Augusta has its own demands — but their presence in Mexico City continues to give LIV Golf a star-power argument that individual event results can obscure.

What's Next
