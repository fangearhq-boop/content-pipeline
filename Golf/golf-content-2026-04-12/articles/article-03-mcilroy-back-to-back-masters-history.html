Nicklaus. Faldo. Tiger. McIlroy Is Playing for the Fourth Spot in the Back-to-Back Club

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

April 12, 2026

There is a list of the greatest achievers in Masters history, and very few things on that list are harder to accomplish than winning the green jacket in consecutive years. In 90 editions of the Masters Tournament, it has happened exactly three times.

Jack Nicklaus did it in 1965 and 1966, winning both with a degree of mastery that was becoming the defining characteristic of his career. Nick Faldo matched the feat in 1989 and 1990, doing what Faldo did best — playing cold, efficient, irreproachable golf through four rounds each time. Tiger Woods closed out his historic grand slam period by winning in 2001 and then defending in 2002, marking the apex of his early-career dominance.

No one else has done it in the 24 years since. Today, Rory McIlroy plays for the fourth spot on that list.

What He Has Already Done

McIlroy's path to this moment carries its own historical weight. For more than a decade, the Masters was the one tournament that seemed to elude him — a recurring final act in his career narrative that remained unwritten while he won three other majors and produced some of the greatest golf of his generation.

The 2025 Masters changed all of that. McIlroy's victory at Augusta National was the completion of the Career Grand Slam — the rarest achievement in professional golf, requiring wins at all four major championships over the course of a career. He is one of a small number of players in history to have done it. The green jacket was more than a tournament win. It was a career-defining moment.

Now, 12 months later, he returns to defend it. After 54 holes, he is in a tie with Cameron Young. The lead he built through two days is gone, erased by Young's spectacular 65 on Saturday. What remains is the same task: play 18 holes better than the field on the most demanding Sunday in golf.

The Weight of Augusta

Augusta National has its own language, and McIlroy has been learning it for a long time. He knows Amen Corner. He knows the slope on No. 18. He knows what it feels like to have a lead at Augusta and feel it slip away — and what it feels like to hold it. Sunday's round will test all of that accumulated knowledge against Young's remarkable confidence and the field's remaining threats.

If McIlroy wins today, he joins Nicklaus, Faldo, and Woods as the only players in the modern Masters era to defend successfully. He will have done in two consecutive years what most elite players never accomplish in a career. At 37 years old, still one of the most complete ball strikers in the world, he will have answered every remaining question about his relationship with Augusta National.

What's Next

The final round of the 2026 Masters unfolds Sunday afternoon on CBS, beginning at 2:00 PM ET, with the final pairing — McIlroy and Young — teeing off at 2:25 PM. The answer to whether McIlroy joins the most exclusive club in major championship golf will be known by evening. Whatever happens, he will have played the kind of Masters that adds lines to his biography.
