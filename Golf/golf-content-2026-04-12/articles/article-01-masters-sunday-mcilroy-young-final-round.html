Masters Sunday: McIlroy and Young Tied at 11-Under — Everything On the Line

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

April 12, 2026

The 2026 Masters Tournament reaches its final chapter Sunday at Augusta National, and the setup is everything golf fans could have wanted: two elite players, tied at 11-under par, entering the final round in the same pairing with no margin for error between them.

Defending champion Rory McIlroy, who won the 2025 Masters to complete his Career Grand Slam, and Cameron Young, the man who erased McIlroy's historic six-shot lead with a stunning 65 on Saturday, will tee off together at 2:25 PM ET on Sunday. Television coverage begins at noon on Paramount+ and transitions to CBS at 2:00 PM ET for the remainder of the afternoon.

The Setup

McIlroy spent Thursday and Friday building one of the most commanding leads in Masters history — a six-shot advantage after 36 holes that Golf Channel called the largest 36-hole lead in the tournament's history. It felt, briefly, as though Augusta was watching a coronation rather than a competition.

Saturday changed everything. Young, who entered Round 3 eight shots behind the leader, played one of the most remarkable rounds in recent Augusta memory: 8 birdies, 1 bogey, a 7-under 65 that obliterated the field's scoring average on a difficult day. McIlroy, meanwhile, stumbled to a 73, giving back every shot of his lead and then some.

They arrive at Sunday morning as equals. Neither has a psychological advantage on paper — both have shown they can handle Augusta's demands over 54 holes. The question is who handles it over the final 18.

The Rest of the Field

The leaderboard entering Sunday is competitive enough to remind both leaders that Augusta's final nine holes are unforgiving. Sam Burns sits at -10, one shot back — close enough to capitalize if either leader falters at Amen Corner. Shane Lowry (-9) and Jason Day (-8) are within meaningful range. Scottie Scheffler, the world No. 1, is outside the top 20 this week, a reminder that Augusta does not defer to global rankings.

Amen Corner — holes 11 through 13 — is the tournament's great equalizer. Rae's Creek at No. 12 has consumed green-jacket dreams as recently as this decade. A wayward tee shot at No. 11 can turn a two-shot lead into a one-shot deficit before the leaders reach the par-5 13th. Every player on the leaderboard entering today knows this. Every fan watching it unfold knows it too.

What's Next

The 2026 Masters champion will be crowned Sunday afternoon at Augusta National. If McIlroy wins, he becomes only the fourth player in the history of the tournament to win back-to-back — joining Jack Nicklaus (1965-66), Nick Faldo (1989-90), and Tiger Woods (2001-02). If Young wins, he claims his second major championship and announces himself as one of the sport's premier players. One way or another, Masters Sunday 2026 will be worth remembering.
