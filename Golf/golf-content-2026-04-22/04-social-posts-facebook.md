### STORY 1: McIlroy Masters Back-to-Back

**Long-Form Post:**
Rory McIlroy did what only three men before him had ever done at Augusta National: he won the Masters twice in a row.

McIlroy finished the 2026 Masters at 12-under par (276), one shot clear of Scottie Scheffler, to claim his second consecutive green jacket and become the first player since Tiger Woods in 2001 and 2002 to defend the title at Augusta. He joins Jack Nicklaus, Nick Faldo, and Tiger Woods as the only players in the tournament's 90-year history to achieve back-to-back victories.

The final round was everything the galleries at Augusta could have hoped for. McIlroy entered Sunday with a seemingly commanding six-shot lead, but the momentum shifted dramatically during the back nine. Cameron Young wrestled the lead away from the Northern Irishman, and at various points Justin Rose also held a share of the top spot. McIlroy's ability to compose himself and recover — ultimately posting a 71 despite a bogey on the 72nd hole — was a reminder of why, regardless of any leaderboard turbulence, he is the most complete big-stage performer in the game today.

Cameron Young finished alongside Russell Henley, Justin Rose, and Tyrrell Hatton at 10-under, while Scheffler's strong finish left him as the closest pursuer. McIlroy now holds two green jackets and six major championships in total — one of the most impressive collections in modern golf.

### STORY 2: Fitzpatrick RBC Heritage

**Long-Form Post:**
In the span of a few weeks, Matt Fitzpatrick has established himself as arguably the most dangerous player in the world not named Rory McIlroy.

The 31-year-old Englishman closed out the 2026 RBC Heritage at Harbour Town Golf Links on Sunday with a playoff victory over world No. 1 Scottie Scheffler, winning his second title in his last three PGA Tour starts and cementing a stunning run of consistency at the sport's highest level. Fitzpatrick entered 2026 with the Valspar Championship to his name, added a runner-up at The Players Championship, and has now claimed his second RBC Heritage title — having first won at Harbour Town in 2023.

The playoff itself was decisive. Scheffler, who had remarkably erased a three-shot deficit in the final four holes of regulation to force extra holes, saw Fitzpatrick execute one of the rounds's finest shots: a 4-iron from 204 yards that landed pin-high, 13 feet from the cup. Fitzpatrick then drained the birdie putt to win on the first sudden-death hole, earning $3.6 million from the $20 million signature event purse.

The victory lifted Fitzpatrick to No. 3 in the world rankings. Heading into next week's Zurich Classic — where he will partner his brother Alex as a two-man team — Fitzpatrick arrives in arguably the best form of his career.

### STORY 3: Rahm LIV Mexico City

**Long-Form Post:**
If there was any doubt about who owns LIV Golf in 2026, Jon Rahm removed it emphatically at Club de Golf Chapultepec in Mexico City.

The Spaniard shot a bogey-free, 7-under 64 in the final round to claim a six-shot victory over compatriot David Puig, completing a clean sweep of the individual podium for Spain: Rahm first, Puig second, Josele Ballester third. The performance was commanding from start to finish, the kind of display that reminded any observers who might have forgotten what a healthy, motivated Rahm looks like at the top of a leaderboard.

It was Rahm's second LIV Golf victory of the 2026 season, and it came alongside another team triumph for Legion XIII, which claimed its third consecutive team championship — winning in 2024, 2025, and now 2026. The team's consistency has become one of LIV Golf's defining storylines, with Rahm at its center.

The all-Spanish podium had significant symbolic weight. Puig's runner-up result moves him to sixth in the individual LIV standings, while Ballester's third-place finish positions him at No. 10. The Spanish wave in LIV Golf is a genuine phenomenon, and Rahm sits at its crest.

### STORY 4: Zurich Classic Preview

**Long-Form Post:**
Golf's only PGA Tour team event returns to Cajun Country this week, and the field is stacked with storylines heading into Thursday's first round at TPC Louisiana.

The 2026 Zurich Classic of New Orleans runs April 23-26 in Avondale, Louisiana, with 74 two-man teams competing for a $9.5 million purse. The format alternates between fourballs (better ball) on Thursday and Saturday, and foursomes (alternate shot) on Friday and Sunday — the format pairing requiring partners to play their round of golf genuinely as a team, taking turns hitting the same ball.

The central storyline is defending champions Ben Griffin and Andrew Novak. The pair captured their first PGA Tour wins together at last year's Zurich Classic, and now return with the chance to achieve something no team has done before: winning back-to-back editions. The pressure of defending in this unique format will be a fascinating study in partnership under stress.

The most compelling challenger tandem may be the Fitzpatrick brothers. Matt Fitzpatrick arrives having won two of his last three PGA Tour events — the Valspar Championship and the RBC Heritage — and paired with his brother Alex, who also competes professionally, they represent one of the more unique family pairings the sport can offer. Matt's elite ball-striking and Alex's local knowledge of the format could make them a genuine threat.

Also worth watching: Brooks Koepka paired with Shane Lowry, a 2024 Zurich Classic winner. Koepka has the power game for fourballs, and Lowry's experience in this specific event adds credibility to what could be a crowd-pleasing partnership.

### STORY 5: Chevron Championship Preview

**Long-Form Post:**
The women's major season gets underway this week at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston, Texas, with the Chevron Championship bringing together the deepest field in women's golf for the sport's first Grand Slam event of 2026.

The $8 million purse and 132-player field includes 48 of the top 50 players in the Rolex Women's World Golf Rankings — a testament to the prestige the Chevron has built in its relatively new Houston home. Nelly Korda enters as the betting favorite at 5-to-1 odds, looking to add to her major championship collection on a course that rewards precision ball-striking and strategic patience.

The broader field features several players capable of winning: South Korea's Hyo Joo Kim, who has been in excellent form in 2026; Australia's Hannah Green and Minjee Lee; Japan's Miyu Yamashita; and Thailand's Jeeno Thitikul, who won the Honda LPGA Thailand in February and has been building toward a major breakthrough.

Memorial Park Golf Course provides a stern test, with tree-lined fairways and greens that demand consistent approach play under pressure. The Houston heat and afternoon wind are factors every player in the field will need to manage across four rounds.

The Chevron Championship tees off Thursday, with television coverage on Golf Channel and streaming available through the LPGA Tour's platforms.
