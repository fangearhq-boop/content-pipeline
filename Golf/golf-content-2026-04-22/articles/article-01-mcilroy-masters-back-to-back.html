Rory McIlroy Joins Tiger Woods in History: Back-to-Back Masters Champion

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 22, 2026

The names on the back-to-back Masters list are as short as they are elite: Jack Nicklaus. Nick Faldo. Tiger Woods. And now, definitively, Rory McIlroy.

McIlroy became only the fourth player in the 90-year history of the Masters Tournament to win the green jacket in consecutive years, finishing at 12-under par (276) to defeat Scottie Scheffler by a single stroke on April 13. He is the first player since Tiger Woods — who won in 2001 and 2002 — to defend the title at Augusta National, a feat that has ended the careers of some of the most talented players who ever attempted it.

It is McIlroy's second Masters title and his sixth major championship in total. At 36, he now sits comfortably within the conversation about the greatest golfers of all time — a conversation he has shaped and reshaped across more than a decade of major contention, heartbreak, and ultimate triumph.

A Final Round the Ages Won't Forget

The victory was not without drama. McIlroy entered the final round with what appeared to be a comfortable six-shot lead over the field — a margin that, in Masters history, has typically been insurmountable. Augusta National had other plans.

Through the early holes of Sunday's final round, Cameron Young began a relentless charge, trading birdies and eagles to claw back the deficit and eventually wrestle the lead away from the Northern Irishman. At various points, Justin Rose also found himself atop the leaderboard, adding to the chaos of a final round that tested McIlroy's composure at every turn.

McIlroy steadied himself on the back nine. He reclaimed the lead he had surrendered and held it through the championship's most treacherous closing holes. The bogey on the 72nd hole — Augusta's famous 18th — gave everyone watching a final moment of anxiety before the putt dropped for par and the one-shot victory was confirmed. Final round: a 71 under enormous pressure. One shot was enough.

Scheffler finished at 11-under, while Young, Russell Henley, Rose, and Tyrrell Hatton shared the third position at 10-under.

The Legacy Moment

McIlroy's Masters journey has been one of golf's most compelling ongoing narratives. The back-to-back victory brings his major count to six — a number that, combined with his age and his continued quality at Augusta, leaves the door open for further additions to the green jacket collection.

For the 2026 season, McIlroy's Masters title arrives in the context of a broader competitive landscape that also includes the emergence of Scottie Scheffler as a persistent rival and the stunning form of Matt Fitzpatrick across the spring season. The depth at the top of the men's game makes McIlroy's ability to win the sport's most storied event — in consecutive years, no less — even more impressive.

What's Next

The next major championship on the PGA Tour calendar is the PGA Championship. McIlroy, arriving as Masters champion and a player in the form of his life, will be the betting favorite wherever the event is held. Beyond that, the US Open and The Open Championship remain on the schedule — events that, for a player with six majors, continue to represent opportunities to write additional chapters of a career already among golf's most decorated.

For now, Rory McIlroy wears two green jackets and stands in the company of Nicklaus, Faldo, and Woods. Very few people in the history of this sport have stood in that particular company.
