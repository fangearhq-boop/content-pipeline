Fitzpatrick Beats Scheffler in Playoff to Win RBC Heritage: Two Wins in Three Starts

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 22, 2026

Matt Fitzpatrick is playing the best golf of his career. That statement, made at almost any point in the 2026 PGA Tour season, would have been accurate. After Sunday's playoff victory at the RBC Heritage, it has become undeniable.

The 31-year-old Englishman defeated world No. 1 Scottie Scheffler on the first sudden-death playoff hole at Harbour Town Golf Links, executing one of the round's finest shots — a 4-iron from 204 yards that found the green 13 feet from the flag — and draining the birdie putt to claim his second RBC Heritage title. It was his second PGA Tour victory in his last three starts, adding to the Valspar Championship he won in March and the runner-up he posted at The Players Championship.

The win earned Fitzpatrick $3.6 million from the $20 million signature event purse and elevated him to No. 3 in the Official World Golf Ranking.

The Scheffler Fight Back

The playoff itself felt almost secondary to the extraordinary final holes of regulation that preceded it. Scheffler, the best player in the world across the past two seasons, trailed Fitzpatrick by three shots with four holes remaining. Most players would have accepted that as a closing deficit they couldn't overcome. Scheffler is not most players.

The American birdied his way back into contention across the closing stretch, reaching 18-under to draw level with Fitzpatrick and force extra holes. The comeback was a reminder of what makes Scheffler the world's top-ranked player: a relentlessness that does not acknowledge deficit until the final putt has dropped.

But Fitzpatrick was unfazed. His iron play into the first sudden-death hole was the shot of the tournament — controlled, precise, wind-reading at its most clinical. When the birdie putt fell, Harbour Town had its champion, and Fitzpatrick had his moment.

A Career Peak Moment

Fitzpatrick won his first RBC Heritage title in 2023, also in a playoff at Harbour Town, using the same Bettinardi BB1 Flow putter that has become something of a trademark. His connection to this event — and to the precision demands of Harbour Town's small greens and tree-lined fairways — is now well established.

The 2026 spring stretch has been remarkable in scope. A Valspar win. A Players runner-up. And now the RBC Heritage. He arrives next week at the Zurich Classic paired with his brother Alex — a family partnership that, given Matt's current form, will be one of the tournament's most closely watched teams.

What's Next

Fitzpatrick heads to TPC Louisiana for the Zurich Classic of New Orleans (April 23-26), where he and brother Alex will compete as a team in the PGA Tour's only team event. The unique fourballs and foursomes format suits a player of Fitzpatrick's precision and consistency.

Beyond New Orleans, the PGA Championship looms as the next major opportunity. Fitzpatrick, now No. 3 in the world and in the form of his career, will be one of the most watched players on that leaderboard from the opening tee shot.
