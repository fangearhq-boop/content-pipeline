Rahm Demolishes LIV Golf Mexico City Field: Spanish Podium, Legion XIII Three-Peat

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | April 22, 2026

Jon Rahm left no doubt at Club de Golf Chapultepec in Mexico City. His final round was not a narrow victory — it was a statement of superiority delivered over 18 bogey-free holes at 7-under par, ending in a six-shot victory that the rest of the LIV Golf field could only watch.

The 2026 LIV Golf Mexico City tournament concluded with a result that cemented Rahm's position as the circuit's dominant individual force: Rahm first, David Puig second, Josele Ballester third — an all-Spanish podium that felt almost scripted given the nationality of LIV Golf's most compelling collective story this season.

For Rahm, it was his second individual LIV Golf title of the 2026 season. For Legion XIII, his team, it was the third consecutive team championship — a hat trick spanning 2024, 2025, and now 2026 that no other team in LIV Golf's history can match.

A Final Round of Authority

There was a point during Sunday's final round where the question ceased to be whether Rahm would win and became simply by how much. His ball-striking was precise, his distance control masterful, and his putting — often the last attribute a player at Rahm's power level gets full credit for — found its mark repeatedly across the back nine as the margin stretched to six shots.

Puig, the runner-up, played his finest round of the tournament to secure second and move to sixth place in the individual LIV Golf standings. Ballester's bronze added a third Spanish flag to the podium that felt, in the moments after Rahm's birdie putt on the final hole, like a natural conclusion to a week of Spanish excellence.

The Legion XIII Dynasty

Three consecutive team titles is a remarkable achievement in any team format, but the LIV Golf team competition carries enough parity from week to week that sustaining dominance across three full seasons represents genuine excellence. Rahm's leadership of Legion XIII has been the engine of that consistency, but the contribution of teammates in enabling the team points accumulation should not be overlooked.

Rahm's individual standing in LIV Golf is now as strong as it has ever been. Two wins in a single season, with the overall standings competition still very much in play, suggests the full stretch of the 2026 campaign is likely to be shaped by the question of whether anyone can close the gap he has opened.

What's Next

The LIV Golf schedule continues next month with LIV Golf Virginia, the circuit's next stop following the break. For Rahm, the period between events provides an opportunity to sharpen the specific areas of his game that will define his individual title pursuit.

The broader golf world continues to track Rahm's form and its implications for any potential return to PGA Tour competition in the future. For now, however, Rahm is the defining player of the LIV Golf circuit — and the Mexico City performance may be the clearest demonstration yet of that status.
