Zurich Classic 2026 Preview: Griffin and Novak Chase History, Fitzpatrick Brothers Arrive in Hot Form

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 22, 2026

TPC Louisiana hosts one of the most distinctive weeks on the PGA Tour calendar beginning Thursday, April 23, when the Zurich Classic of New Orleans brings its two-man team format to Avondale for the chance to write the next chapter in the event's history.

The Zurich Classic is golf's only team event on the PGA Tour, and its format — fourballs (better ball) on Thursday and Saturday, foursomes (alternate shot) on Friday and Sunday — creates a unique dynamic that rewards partnerships built on complementary skills, chemistry under pressure, and the ability to sacrifice personal scoring instincts for the good of a shared card. The $9.5 million purse is distributed across the 74 two-man teams competing for a title that, this year, carries an extra dimension of significance.

Can Griffin and Novak Make History?

Ben Griffin and Andrew Novak return to New Orleans as defending champions, having claimed their first PGA Tour victories together at last year's Zurich Classic. If they win again, they will become the first team in the event's history to win in consecutive editions — a feat that has eluded every previous defending champion, regardless of the individual quality the teams have brought to the tournament.

The psychological challenge of defending in a team event is distinct from individual defense: both players must peak simultaneously, and the alternate-shot rounds demand a synchronicity of approach and rhythm that cannot simply be willed into existence. Griffin and Novak will need everything that worked last year to work again — and then some, given the caliber of the field assembled against them.

The Fitzpatrick Factor

The most compelling challenger tandem may well be the Fitzpatrick brothers. Matt Fitzpatrick arrives in New Orleans having won two of his last three PGA Tour starts — the Valspar Championship in March and the RBC Heritage just last week in a playoff over world No. 1 Scottie Scheffler. Paired with his brother Alex, who also competes professionally, the Fitzpatricks represent not only a strong golfer in the kind of form that wins golf tournaments, but a genuine partnership built on years of shared history and competitive experience together.

Matt's elite ball-striking will be a significant asset in the fourballs rounds, where one team member can play aggressively while the other plays for safety. In the alternate-shot rounds, the brothers' familiarity with each other's tendencies and tempo may prove invaluable.

Other Teams to Watch

Brooks Koepka and Shane Lowry is one of the most intriguing pairings in the field. Koepka's power and Lowry's experienced approach — the Northern Irishman won this event in 2024 — give them a blend of skills that suits both the fourballs and foursomes formats. Lowry's local knowledge of TPC Louisiana and its specific challenges around the water-lined fairways of the Avondale layout provides added value to their partnership.

What's Next

The Zurich Classic runs April 23-26 with CBS and Golf Channel providing television coverage and PGA Tour Live on ESPN+ offering streaming access and featured group coverage all week. The first round begins Thursday morning, with teams navigating TPC Louisiana's tree-lined layout under what is expected to be warm Louisiana spring conditions.

Whether Griffin and Novak can become the event's first back-to-back champions, or whether Fitzpatrick — the hottest golfer in the field — adds yet another week to an already remarkable 2026 spring, the Zurich Classic provides four days of team golf that is genuinely unlike anything else the PGA Tour offers.
