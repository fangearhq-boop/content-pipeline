Chevron Championship 2026 Preview: Korda Favored as Women's Major Season Begins in Houston

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 22, 2026

The women's major season officially begins Thursday at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston, Texas, where the Chevron Championship gathers 132 of the world's best players — including 48 of the top 50 in the Rolex Women's World Golf Ranking — to compete for an $8 million purse in the LPGA Tour's first Grand Slam event of 2026.

Nelly Korda enters as the betting favorite at 5-to-1 odds, a reflection of her status as one of the game's most consistent major performers. The field surrounding her is as deep as any the Chevron has assembled, featuring players from across every major golf nation, each with a case for contention in a tournament that typically demands four days of precise, pressure-tested performance on a challenging course.

The Korda Factor

Korda is the player the rest of the field is measured against heading into Houston. Her combination of distance, iron precision, and putting composure under major pressure gives her advantages that manifest most clearly in the final round of events of this significance. A Chevron title would add a landmark major to her already distinguished career.

The players most likely to challenge Korda represent a genuinely diverse group. South Korea's Hyo Joo Kim has been in outstanding form across the 2026 LPGA Tour season. Australia's Hannah Green — the HSBC Women's World Championship winner in February — and compatriot Minjee Lee represent strong Pacific-region challenges. Japan's Miyu Yamashita and Thailand's Jeeno Thitikul, who won the Honda LPGA Thailand in February, are both considered major-capable on the right week.

The Course and the Challenge

Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston presents a different set of challenges from some of the courses associated with the LPGA's major venues. The tree-lined fairways demand accurate driving, and the greens — designed to encourage competitive scoring while also protecting against runaway low totals — require precise approach play and a putting game that can hold up under the pressure that major championship conditions invariably create.

Houston's spring weather adds variables that cannot always be accounted for in preparation: afternoon winds that shift, occasional thunderstorm delays, and the kind of heat and humidity that tax stamina across four competitive rounds. The player who manages her energy and mental resources across the full 72 holes will have an advantage over those who peak on any single day.

What's Next

The Chevron Championship tees off Thursday, April 23, with television coverage available on Golf Channel and streaming through the LPGA Tour's digital platforms. The first women's major of the year will set the tone for a crowded schedule of significant events ahead, including the US Women's Open and The Women's British Open.

A Korda victory would be a powerful statement of intent for the rest of the major season. But the depth in Houston's field suggests that the champion could come from a wide range of contenders — and the opening round on Thursday will be the first real indication of who has arrived at Memorial Park in major-winning form.
