### STORY 1: Bud Cauley Wins RBC Canadian Open

**Long-Form Post** | 8:00 AM ET
Some wins are bigger than trophies. Bud Cauley just got one of those.

The 36-year-old American closed with a 5-under 65 on a rainy, windy Sunday at TPC Toronto at Osprey Valley's North Course to win the 2026 RBC Canadian Open — his first PGA Tour title in his 239th career start. He earned $1.764 million and a spot in The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale. He'll collect both. But the thing that matters most doesn't have a dollar sign.

Two hundred and thirty-nine starts. One answer. Bud Cauley is a PGA Tour champion. 🏆

### STORY 2: US Open Preview — Scheffler's Grand Slam Bid

**Long-Form Post** | 10:30 AM ET
The 2026 US Open begins Thursday at Shinnecock Hills. And there is only one storyline that matters.

Scottie Scheffler is three rounds away from the greatest achievement in golf. He has two Masters titles. He won the 2025 PGA Championship. He won the 2025 British Open. The only major that has eluded the world's No. 1 player is the US Open — and it begins Thursday in Southampton, New York.

A win on Sunday would make Scheffler the seventh player in history to complete the modern career Grand Slam, joining Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player, Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, and Rory McIlroy.

Scheffler is the betting favorite at +550 — roughly twice as short as second-favorite Rory McIlroy at +1200. But Shinnecock Hills is one of the most demanding venues in golf: par 70, 7,400 yards, fast undulating greens, and conditions that will be challenging all week. Rain is forecast on Thursday (65% chance), and winds of 15-20 mph with gusts to 27 mph are expected through the weekend.

The last time Shinnecock hosted the US Open, Brooks Koepka won at 1-over par in 2018. Nobody dominated. Nobody ran away with it. This course puts a premium on scrambling and nerve.

And if Scheffler survives all of that and is standing on the 18th green on Sunday, June 21, with a Grand Slam in hand — that Sunday is also his 30th birthday. And it is Father's Day.

The stars have rarely aligned like this for a golf story. Three days from now, it starts. ⛳

### STORY 3: LIV Golf Financial Crisis

**Long-Form Post** | 1:00 PM ET
LIV Golf's future is growing more uncertain by the week.

According to a Financial Times report cited by Golf Digest, Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF) has provided approximately $200 million of the roughly $600 million it committed to fund LIV Golf through the end of the 2026 season. That is roughly one-third of what was promised — and the season still has four events remaining.

The financial picture hardened when the PIF announced it would end its financial backing of LIV after the completion of the 2026 season, stating that "the substantial investment required by LIV Golf over a longer term is no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy."

CEO Scott O'Neil has been publicly optimistic. He told CNBC he has had five formal investor meetings with 18 more planned, and that the response has "been positive." He hopes to close a fundraising round this summer — a "LIV 2.0" with outside capital replacing PIF support.

But a high-ranking executive from one of the league's partners offered a blunter assessment: "every remaining tournament is on the fence."

One event — the Louisiana tournament — has already been postponed. Questions remain about the rest of the schedule.

If LIV cannot secure new investment before the final four events, the implications for players under multi-year contracts could be significant. The PGA Tour, meanwhile, is watching. Some players may be exploring what a potential return would look like.
