2026 US Open Preview: Scheffler's Grand Slam Bid Begins Thursday at Shinnecock Hills

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

June 15, 2026

Three days from now, the greatest story in golf takes center stage.

The 2026 US Open begins Thursday, June 18 at Shinnecock Hills Golf Club in Southampton, New York. Scottie Scheffler tees it up as the No. 1 favorite at +550 — and with it, carries the weight of completing the career Grand Slam, one of golf's rarest and most coveted achievements.

Scheffler already has two Masters titles, the 2025 PGA Championship, and the 2025 British Open. He needs only the US Open to join the most exclusive group in the sport's history.

Shinnecock Hills: A Demanding Host

Shinnecock Hills is hosting the US Open for the sixth time. It is a par 70 layout stretching to 7,400 yards, with fast, undulating greens, subtle elevation changes, and severe penalties for missing fairways. Scrambling ability and speed control on the greens will be decisive factors.

The last time Shinnecock hosted in 2018, Brooks Koepka won at 1-over par — nobody ran away with the tournament, nobody dominated. The course took its toll, and patience and execution under pressure separated the champion from the rest of the field.

Conditions this week promise similar difficulty. Rain is in the forecast Thursday (65% chance), and winds of 15 to 20 miles per hour with gusts to 27 mph are expected through the weekend. The US Open does not offer easy conditions, and Shinnecock Hills does not offer easy holes. The combination will be unforgiving.

The Contenders Behind Scheffler

Rory McIlroy is the second betting favorite at +1200 — a US Open champion himself, winner of the 2011 edition at Congressional. McIlroy has been in contention at majors consistently and remains one of the few players capable of matching Scheffler in ball-striking.

Jon Rahm, a former US Open champion at Torrey Pines in 2021, is among the other contenders. Brooks Koepka, who has won this event twice (Shinnecock Hills in 2018 and Pebble Beach in 2019) and thrives at US Open-style setups, cannot be overlooked at a course where he already has a champion's read.

But Scheffler's odds speak to what the market believes: this is his week to win, and he enters as a clear, unambiguous favorite.

What's Next

Thursday's opening round begins at Shinnecock Hills with featured groups and waves of tee times. Scheffler will be the center of attention for all four rounds. And if the world's No. 1 player is standing on the 18th green Sunday afternoon with a championship in hand, he will join only six other men in the history of the game who can say they have won every major.

The 2026 US Open begins Thursday. Golf's biggest story of the year starts right now.
