LPGA's Biggest Two Weeks: Meijer Classic Starts Thursday, KPMG Women's PGA in 10 Days

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

June 15, 2026

Women's golf is about to enter its most consequential stretch of the 2026 season.

The LPGA Tour heads to Blythefield Country Club in Belmont, Michigan this week for the Meijer LPGA Classic for Simply Give (June 18-21), then follows with one of the five women's majors — the KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota (June 25-28). Back-to-back weeks. Back-to-back opportunities for the world's best players to separate themselves.

Nelly Korda Arrives With Momentum

The player everyone will be tracking is Nelly Korda. The American star won the 2026 US Women's Open earlier this month at Riviera Country Club, shooting 8-under 276 to claim her first US Women's Open title. It was her second consecutive major championship of the 2026 season — a run of form that has established her as the dominant player in women's golf right now.

Korda comes to Michigan riding that wave. The Meijer Classic has historically produced strong fields, and the KPMG Women's PGA that follows is a major in the truest sense — a test of a full field at one of the most storied venues in American golf.

KPMG at Hazeltine: A Course That Produces Champions

Hazeltine National Golf Club in Chaska, Minnesota has a history of hosting elite golf. It has staged men's and women's US Opens, PGA Championships, and Ryder Cups. The course rewards precision ball-striking and punishes approaches that miss in the wrong places.

The KPMG Women's PGA Championship is the second-oldest women's major on the calendar, and its 2026 edition at Hazeltine will draw the full LPGA roster. For players like Korda — already with major momentum — the combination of Meijer and KPMG provides a two-week opportunity to define a season.

What's Next

The Meijer LPGA Classic begins with practice rounds this week and tees off in competition on Thursday, June 18 — the same day the US Open begins at Shinnecock Hills. It is a rare moment where men's and women's golf align on the schedule, and both deserve full attention.

Women's golf in 2026 has a genuine star in Nelly Korda. The next two weeks will tell us whether she can push that status even higher.
