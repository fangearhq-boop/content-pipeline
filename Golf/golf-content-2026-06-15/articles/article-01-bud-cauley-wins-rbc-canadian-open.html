Bud Cauley Wins RBC Canadian Open: First PGA Tour Title at 36 in His 239th Start

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

June 15, 2026

Two hundred and thirty-nine starts. One answer.

Bud Cauley closed with a 5-under 65 on a rainy, wind-swept Sunday at TPC Toronto at Osprey Valley's North Course to win the 2026 RBC Canadian Open — his first PGA Tour title, and one of the most emotionally charged wins in recent memory.

Cauley finished at 17-under 263, two clear of Matt Fitzpatrick, with Viktor Hovland another shot behind in third. He earned $1.764 million and a spot in The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale. He'll collect both proudly. But the number that will follow him forever is 239.

A Comeback That Defied the Odds

Cauley's road to this title stretches back to a moment far removed from any fairway. He was a passenger in a car accident that left him with six broken ribs, a collapsed right lung, and a fractured left leg. The injuries were severe enough that a return to professional golf — let alone contention at the highest level — was never guaranteed.

He came back anyway. He kept grinding through the grind of the PGA Tour, where cuts missed blend into the background of years, and where the number of starts accumulates without a single title on the ledger. Two hundred and thirty-eight tries without the win. Then Sunday came.

On the back nine of the final round, with the leaderboard tight and the weather miserable, Cauley took control. He made birdie at the 11th from 4 feet, holed out from 93 feet at the 12th, made a 13-and-a-half-footer at the 13th and a 15-footer at the 15th. By the time the day was done, he had built a cushion that no one could bridge.

The Champion's Prize

Beyond the check and the Open spot, Cauley's victory carries the kind of weight that statistics cannot quantify. He entered the week without a Tour win and leaves as a champion — at age 36, in conditions that tested everyone in the field.

Fitzpatrick, the runner-up, was a worthy challenger. Hovland in third adds prestige to the result. But this Sunday belonged entirely to Bud Cauley, and the story of how he got here belongs to all of golf.

What's Next

Cauley will now prepare for The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale — earned with this win through the Canadian Open's spot in the Open Qualifying Series. The FedExCup also received a significant boost; Cauley is projected to move to 28th in the standings.

More immediately, the golf world turns to Shinnecock Hills and the US Open, which begins Thursday, June 18. But for one Sunday in Toronto, in the rain and the wind, Bud Cauley got his moment. No one in the sport is more deserving of it.
