Masters 2026 Preview: Scheffler Favored, McIlroy Defends, DeChambeau Arrives Hot

By Ryan Calloway April 6, 2026

The Setup

The 90th Masters Tournament begins Thursday at Augusta National Golf Club, and the 2026 edition arrives with a full roster of compelling storylines. Ninety-one players will compete across four rounds for a share of the $21 million purse — the largest in Masters history. Amazon Prime Video broadcasts the tournament for the first time, extending the event's reach beyond its traditional CBS television partnership. Tiger Woods is absent for the second consecutive year. The 17th hole has been quietly shortened by 12 yards. And the betting market is sending clear signals about where the field's hierarchy sits three days before the first tee shot.

At the top: Scottie Scheffler at +500. The World No. 1 won the Masters in both 2022 and 2024 and arrives at Augusta in search of a third green jacket. He has never finished outside the top 20 in 11 career Masters appearances. He also arrives with a 9-day-old son named Remy — born March 27 — who is at Augusta with him this week. That kind of context rarely factors into betting lines, but it is a detail that colors the narrative around Scheffler's week.

McIlroy Defends, But Not at Full Strength

Rory McIlroy is the defending champion after his 2025 Masters win — a birdie on 18 to force a playoff, followed by a first sudden-death hole victory over Justin Rose that completed his career Grand Slam. He became only the sixth player in golf history to win all four major championships. That win ended years of near-misses at Augusta and is already regarded as one of the iconic moments in the tournament's history.

The 2026 defense is complicated. McIlroy sustained a back injury that forced him out of the Arnold Palmer Invitational in early March. He played The Players Championship despite it, finishing T46 at even par — well below his typical standards. He enters the Masters off a three-week layoff, tied for the longest of his career. His odds have drifted to +1000-+1200, and the SI.com trend watch has him among the players moving in the wrong direction as Augusta week begins.

The counterargument is straightforward: McIlroy knows Augusta better than almost anyone in the field, and he won here as a 35-year-old carrying the weight of an incomplete career Grand Slam. Without that specific pressure, some observers believe he can compete freely. Whether three weeks off a back injury allows him to compete at his best is the central question for his title defense.

DeChambeau's Moment and the LIV Factor

Bryson DeChambeau arrives at Augusta as the hottest player in professional golf regardless of tour. He won LIV Golf Singapore in a playoff over Richard T. Lee, then followed it with a victory at LIV Golf South Africa — also in a playoff, over Jon Rahm. Back-to-back LIV wins give him five career LIV titles and position him as one of the most dangerous players in the Masters field this week.

His odds have moved to +1000 and are trending upward as Scheffler and McIlroy move down. His Masters history supports the interest: in each of the last two Masters Tournaments, DeChambeau was inside the top 10 after every single round. He is one of 10 LIV Golf players competing at Augusta this week. Jon Rahm (+1000) is the other prominent LIV contender after his own strong 2026 season in South Africa.

Spaun claimed the final Masters spot with his Valero Texas Open victory on Sunday, completing a 91-player field that represents some of the deepest talent the tournament has seen in recent years.

The 17th hole — the par-3 Nandina — has been shortened by 12 yards to 450 total, a subtle change that may influence club selection late in rounds. A new three-story Player Services Building is in use for the first time. Augusta National has quietly continued to refine its infrastructure while preserving the course's essential character.

What's Next

The Masters begins Thursday, April 9, with first-round play. CBS will carry the weekend broadcast; Amazon Prime Video handles streaming for much of the week in its Masters debut. The field is set, the odds are moving, and the storylines — Scheffler's three-peat bid, McIlroy's injured defense, DeChambeau's LIV momentum — are in place. Augusta National will sort them out by Sunday afternoon on April 12.
