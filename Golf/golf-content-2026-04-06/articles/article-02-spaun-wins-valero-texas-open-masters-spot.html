Spaun Wins Valero Texas Open, Earns Final Masters Spot as Fowler Misses Cut

By Jake Torres April 6, 2026

Spaun Claims TPC San Antonio Again

J.J. Spaun won the 2026 Valero Texas Open at TPC San Antonio by one stroke on Sunday, claiming the final available spot in the Masters Tournament field and his second title at the event. Spaun's first Valero Texas Open victory came in 2022 — four years later, he returns to the winner's circle at the same venue with a Masters invitation now in hand.

The victory was completed under unusual circumstances. Round 3 had been suspended Saturday due to inclement weather at TPC San Antonio, leaving players to complete the third round Sunday morning before immediately turning around for the fourth. The double-round format added physical and mental strain to an already high-stakes final day. Spaun handled it. A three-way tie for second featured Matt Wallace, Michael Kim, and Robert MacIntyre, each finishing one stroke back and each earning $741,533 from the $9.8 million total purse. Spaun's winner's check: $1,764,000.

The Masters Invitation

The Valero Texas Open is one of the final tournaments on the PGA Tour calendar before the Masters and carries particular weight as one of the last opportunities for players to qualify for Augusta. A win earns a direct invitation regardless of world ranking or prior eligibility. For Spaun, who had not previously competed in the Masters, the victory transforms the week from a regular tour event into a career landmark.

With his name added to the 2026 field, the Masters roster is now complete at 91 players. Spaun will join a field that includes two-time champion Scottie Scheffler (+500), defending champion Rory McIlroy (+1000-+1200), Bryson DeChambeau (+1000), Jon Rahm (+1000), and the rest of the sport's elite. His presence as a wildcard addition — a late qualifier arriving off a victory — is the kind of story Augusta tends to accommodate well. First-time Masters competitors sometimes play with a freedom that more seasoned participants don't always access.

Fowler's Missed Cut and the Weight of the Moment

The other major storyline of the Valero Texas Open was Rickie Fowler's missed cut. Fowler has long been one of the most popular players in professional golf — a fan favorite whose major championship record has never matched his talent or his profile. The Valero Texas Open represented his final realistic path to a 2026 Masters invitation, and when he missed the cut at TPC San Antonio, that path closed.

Fowler will not play at Augusta in 2026. For a player of his following and his history with the major championships — including four top-five finishes at majors in a single calendar year earlier in his career — the absence is notable. The Masters without Fowler is a smaller story than the Masters with him would have been.

What's Next

J.J. Spaun travels to Augusta National for the first time as a Masters competitor, with first-round play beginning Thursday, April 9. His preparation will be compressed — the Valero Texas Open concluded Sunday, leaving limited days before the tournament begins. But arriving at Augusta with the momentum of a victory and no expectations may be precisely the conditions that allow a first-time Masters competitor to play freely. The 91-player field is set. Spaun is in it.
