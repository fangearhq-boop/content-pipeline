DeChambeau Arrives at Augusta Hot: Back-to-Back LIV Wins, Odds Climbing to +1000

By Ryan Calloway April 6, 2026

The Hottest Player Heading Into Augusta

Bryson DeChambeau will arrive at Augusta National this week as the hottest player in professional golf — regardless of which tour you follow. He won LIV Golf Singapore in a playoff over Richard T. Lee. He followed that with a victory at LIV Golf South Africa, again in a playoff, this time over Jon Rahm. Back-to-back wins. Five career LIV titles total. A man in form.

The market has noticed. DeChambeau's Masters odds have climbed to +1000 — and the SI.com trend analysis places him among the players moving in the right direction as Scottie Scheffler (+500) and Rory McIlroy (+1000-+1200) both trend downward. He is tied for the second-best odds in the entire field with Rahm, and he arrives with momentum that neither of the favorites can match at this particular moment.

Augusta History That Demands Respect

DeChambeau's LIV form would matter less if his Masters record were poor. It isn't. In seven Masters starts, he has two missed cuts and two top-10 finishes. More specifically: in each of the last two Masters Tournaments, he was inside the top 10 after every single round. That consistency at Augusta — sustaining leaderboard position across four rounds on one of the most demanding courses in professional golf — is not accidental.

He is one of 10 LIV Golf players in the 2026 Masters field, the largest LIV contingent the tournament has seen. Jon Rahm is the other prominent LIV contender, also at +1000 after a strong recent run that included a runner-up finish at LIV South Africa. The narrative around LIV players at Augusta — once framed as underdogs competing with reduced tournament preparation — has shifted as the format's top performers continue to contend at the majors.

The Expert Fade

Not everyone is convinced. A CBS Sports expert pick column is explicitly "fading DeChambeau" at +1000, citing Augusta specifics that may not align with the precision demands his aggressive driving style creates. A Golfmagic headline this week noted that "a legendary golf coach issues warning to DeChambeau on eve of Masters." The contrarian view is that LIV events, with smaller fields and less competitive depth on a week-to-week basis, may not prepare players optimally for a 91-man elite field at Augusta's level of difficulty.

The counter to that counter: DeChambeau's last two Masters results suggest the Augusta-specific preparation is not a concern. He has demonstrated that his game translates to the course. And arriving at any major tournament with two recent wins is rarely a disadvantage.

What's Next

Bryson DeChambeau tees off Thursday, April 9, in Round 1 of the 2026 Masters. If his performance follows the pattern of his last two Augusta appearances — consistent top-10 positioning across all four rounds — he will be in contention when Sunday's back nine separates the field. Whether the market at +1000 appropriately reflects that probability is the central question for anyone considering him as a Masters pick this week.
