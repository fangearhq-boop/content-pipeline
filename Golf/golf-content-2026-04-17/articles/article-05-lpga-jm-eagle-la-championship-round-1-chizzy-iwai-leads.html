LPGA JM Eagle LA Championship Round 1: Chizzy Iwai Leads, Yu Liu Aces the 15th

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 17, 2026

The 2026 JM Eagle LA Championship got underway Thursday at El Caballero Country Club in the Los Angeles area, and Round 1 delivered both a clear leader and an unforgettable moment. Chizzy Iwai set the pace with a 9-under 63, and Yu Liu gave the gallery at the par-3 15th something to remember for a long time.

Iwai Takes the Lead

Chizzy Iwai's 9-under 63 is one of the best rounds in the field through 18 holes and gives her a two-shot advantage over Patty Tavatanakit, who sits at 7-under 65. Iwai, who entered the week among the players capable of making a run at El Caballero Country Club, turned in a clean round that put distance between herself and the chasing pack.

Tavatanakit is a familiar name near the top of LPGA leaderboards — the Thai standout has won before and has the consistency to put together four quality rounds. Her 65 keeps her within striking distance heading into Round 2, with 54 holes still to be played.

Yu Liu's Hole-in-One

Beyond the leaderboard, Round 1 produced one of the highlights of the week on any tour. Yu Liu made a hole-in-one on the par-3 15th hole — a moment that stopped play, drew the crowd to its feet, and added to a tournament that was already generating solid early-round scoring.

Aces on tour are rare by definition, and the 15th at El Caballero is not a pushover. Liu's hole-in-one was a reminder that in a week full of serious scoring, golf still has room for pure, instant magic.

The Stakes of This Event

The JM Eagle LA Championship presented by Plastpro is the final LPGA Tour event before the Chevron Championship — the first major of the LPGA season — which begins April 23 at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston. Players who play well here typically carry momentum, confidence, and form into major week. Conversely, players who struggle here have only one week to recalibrate before Augusta for the women begins.

The $3.75 million purse is significant in its own right, and the field at El Caballero is competitive. With Iwai at the top and a cluster of players at 6-under and below, the leaderboard is not locked in through the weekend.

What's Next

Round 2 of the JM Eagle LA Championship is underway today at El Caballero Country Club. Iwai will look to maintain her two-shot lead while Tavatanakit and the rest of the field attempt to close the gap. The weekend rounds will determine who arrives at the Chevron Championship next week carrying the kind of form that wins majors.
