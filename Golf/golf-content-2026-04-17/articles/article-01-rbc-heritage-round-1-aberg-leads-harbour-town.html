RBC Heritage Round 1: Åberg Leads Harbour Town After Masters Redemption

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 17, 2026

Ludvig Åberg spent the week after Augusta National being openly critical of himself. He called his Masters mistakes "silly." He described what happened at Augusta as "madness." He said he needed to clean up his game. And then he went to Harbour Town Golf Links and shot 8-under 63 in the first round of the RBC Heritage.

The message from the Swedish star was clear: the frustration was fuel.

The Round 1 Leaderboard

Åberg's 63 gives him a one-shot lead over Viktor Hovland and Harris English, both of whom carded 7-under 64s on Thursday. The three leaders combined for 19 under par in the opening round, creating a top-heavy leaderboard at a course that rewards precision and patience.

A six-way tie at 6-under 65 includes Rickie Fowler, Matt Fitzpatrick, Gary Woodland, Ryan Fox, Andrew Novak, and Michael Brennan. Thirty holes into the week, a dozen players are within three shots of the lead at a no-cut Signature Event — meaning every name in the field is still very much alive.

Scheffler's OB Opener

World No. 1 Scottie Scheffler drew enormous attention for what happened on the first tee. His opening drive at Harbour Town went wide right and out of bounds — a jarring start for the reigning Masters runner-up and the consensus best player on the planet. He re-teed, found the fairway, hit his approach inside 12 feet, and drained the putt to salvage bogey.

From there, Scheffler played the remaining 17 holes at 4-under, finishing with a round of 68. He sits at 3-under for the tournament, five back of Åberg. At a Signature Event with no cut, he has 54 holes to make up that ground. Given that he won this tournament in 2024 and finished as runner-up at the Masters one week ago, there is no reasonable argument for writing him off.

Åberg's Masters Motivation

Åberg's comments about Augusta were not ambiguous. He felt he gave away shots he had no business giving away, and the self-criticism suggested an athlete keenly aware of the distance between his current level and where he wants to be. That internal standard may be exactly what made Thursday possible.

Harbour Town Golf Links suits Åberg's game. The Pete Dye design demands controlled approach play, and Åberg's iron precision is one of the best in the field. His 63 — bogey-free, with five birdies on the back nine — was a textbook round at one of the most demanding tracks on the PGA Tour calendar.

The Rory Absence

The 2026 RBC Heritage is proceeding without Rory McIlroy, who is resting after winning back-to-back Masters titles. McIlroy's absence opens the door for the rest of the field — and given the current shape of the leaderboard, that door has been taken advantage of. Hovland, Fitzpatrick, Fowler, and Scheffler are all legitimate contenders with track records that fit Harbour Town's demands.

What's Next

Round 2 is underway today at Harbour Town Golf Links. The cut does not apply at Signature Events, meaning all 82 players in the field will continue through the weekend. With Åberg, Hovland, English, and Scheffler all inside the top five groups on the leaderboard, the next 54 holes figure to be competitive and closely contested. The weekend is going to be worth watching.
