LIV Golf Mexico City Round 1: Victor Perez Fires 9-Under 62 to Lead

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 17, 2026

Victor Perez arrived at LIV Golf Mexico City as one of the circuit's newest additions for 2026. He left Round 1 at Club de Golf Chapultepec with the lead by three shots after firing a stunning 9-under 62 — the lowest round of the day and one of the best opening rounds on the LIV Golf tour this season.

The Round 1 Leaderboard

Perez's 62 gives him a three-shot lead over Jon Rahm, who sits at 6-under 65 heading into Round 2. A large group sits at 4-under 67, including Dustin Johnson, Ian Poulter, Harold Varner III, Minkyu Kim, Scott Vincent, Younghan Song, and Thomas Detry.

In the team competition, Rahm's Legion XIII holds a three-shot lead over 4Aces GC, making the Spaniard significant to both the individual and team leaderboards despite sitting three back of Perez individually.

Perez Making an Immediate Statement

Perez joined LIV Golf ahead of the 2026 season and has quickly moved into contention in Mexico City. A 9-under 62 at altitude — Club de Golf Chapultepec sits at more than 7,300 feet above sea level — is elite scoring under any conditions. The thin air at Chapultepec typically inflates distances and can make certain aspects of the game unpredictable; Perez navigated all of it to produce a clean, clinical opening round.

His three-shot lead is the largest at the top of any LIV Golf event this week, and it puts him in a position where a steady Round 2 could deliver a wire-to-wire lead heading into the weekend.

Rahm in Pursuit

Jon Rahm's 65 keeps him very much in the hunt despite a three-shot deficit. Rahm has been one of LIV Golf's best individual performers since joining the circuit, and his familiarity with Chapultepec — he has played the course multiple times across LIV seasons — gives him a course knowledge edge heading into the final three rounds.

The team competition adds a second layer of interest. Legion XIII's three-shot lead over 4Aces means Rahm needs a strong second round not just for himself but to maintain that team margin.

What's Next

Round 2 of LIV Golf Mexico City is underway today at Club de Golf Chapultepec. The 72-hole format — new for LIV Golf in 2026 — means there is still substantial golf ahead, and any second-round movement by Rahm, Johnson, or others could significantly compress the leaderboard before the weekend rounds begin. Perez holds the lead, but with three rounds still to play, the tournament is far from decided.
