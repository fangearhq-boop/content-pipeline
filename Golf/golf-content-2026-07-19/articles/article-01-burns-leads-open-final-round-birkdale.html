Sam Burns Leads the 154th Open at -10: From the Delivery Room to the Claret Jug

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 19, 2026

The 154th Open Championship comes down to Sunday at Royal Birkdale — and the man holding the lead has been carrying a story bigger than his scorecard since before the first tee shot was struck.

Sam Burns leads at -10, two shots clear of Ryan Fox and Si Woo Kim at -8. He has played the first three rounds with a precision that borders on relentless: a 62 in Round 2, a 65 in Round 3. His combined score across those two rounds — 127 — represents the lowest two consecutive rounds in men's major championship history. That number belongs to him alone now.

The Story Behind the Scorecard

Burns's daughter Belle was born two days before the 154th Open began. He flew from the delivery room to Lancashire, kissed his wife goodbye, and arrived at Royal Birkdale to play in one of the four most important golf events on earth. It is the kind of story that gets told at closing ceremonies regardless of how the tournament finishes — but Burns is making sure it gets told on the final day too, with a lead and a chance at the Claret Jug.

"I've been thinking about her every shot," Burns told reporters after Round 3. The delivery room to the Open leaderboard in less than a week is an uncommon journey for even the most seasoned professionals.

The Leaderboard Entering Sunday

Behind Burns, the field is competitive but not overwhelming. Ryan Fox, who shot a historic 62 in Round 3 to become the 8th golfer ever to reach that score in a major, enters the final round at -8 — two behind. Si Woo Kim, who has been steady throughout the week, is also at -8.

Lucas Herbert — who was the Round 2 co-leader after his own record-tying 62 — dropped back with a 71 on Saturday and enters Sunday at -7. Bryson DeChambeau, playing his way through controversy after a two-stroke penalty in Round 2, shot 69 in Round 3 and is at -6. Tommy Fleetwood, playing in front of a home crowd six miles from his Southport birthplace, is at -5 after a third-round 69.

Scottie Scheffler, the defending champion and World No. 1, has struggled on the greens throughout the week and enters the final round several shots back of a realistic charge.

The Prize at Stake

The 154th Open Championship carries a total purse of $17.5 million. The winner's share of that purse changes a career's financial trajectory for any player — but for Burns specifically, it would mark his first major championship victory and cement a legacy already built on PGA Tour wins and Ryder Cup appearances.

Burns is not the favorite everyone expected to lead entering Sunday. He is not Scheffler, the world's best player. He is not McIlroy, chasing a seventh major. He is a skilled, consistent PGA Tour professional who arrived at Royal Birkdale with a daughter born days ago, played two of the greatest rounds in major championship history back-to-back, and now stands 18 holes from the sport's oldest and most storied trophy.

What's Next

The final round at Royal Birkdale is underway. Burns's two-shot cushion is significant but not insurmountable — particularly against Fox, who has shown the scoring ability to charge. Watch the leaderboard through the late morning and early afternoon as the final groups make their way around Birkdale's demanding layout. The Claret Jug will be decided by Sunday evening.
