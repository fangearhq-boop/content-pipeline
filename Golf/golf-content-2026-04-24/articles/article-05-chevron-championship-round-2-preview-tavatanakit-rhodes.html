Chevron Championship Round 2: Can Anyone Chase Down Korda? Tavatanakit, Rhodes Lead the Charge

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 24, 2026

Nelly Korda posted a seven-under 65 without a bogey and leads the 2026 Chevron Championship by two strokes heading into Friday. The bigger question is who in this $9 million field at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston has the game to put pressure on her over the next three rounds.

There are at least two names worth watching closely.

Tavatanakit: The Most Dangerous Challenger

Patty Tavatanakit sits two strokes behind Korda at five under after a bogey-free 67 on Thursday. The Thai star has major championship experience, a technically sound game, and the competitive temperament to go low on courses that demand precision rather than raw power. Memorial Park is exactly the kind of track where Tavatanakit's ball-striking and course management become significant advantages.

Two shots is not an insurmountable deficit after one round, and Tavatanakit has the physical and mental game to sustain pressure across 72 holes. Somi Lee also sits at five under after a 67 and cannot be dismissed — her consistency on Thursday showed she can handle the major stage.

Rhodes: A Rookie on the Biggest Stage

The more unexpected name near the top of the leaderboard is Mimi Rhodes. The LPGA rookie from England is three under par after opening with a 69 in her first-ever major championship appearance. Seven shots back is a significant gap — there are 54 holes remaining, but closing seven strokes on Korda, who is in peak form, would require something extraordinary.

What Rhodes has demonstrated through one round is composure that most rookies in their first major simply don't possess. The pressure of a women's major, especially with international media attention and a field of established champions, is unlike anything else in golf. The fact that Rhodes posted a three-under round without apparent difficulty is a genuinely positive sign for where her career is heading, regardless of how this particular week ends.

Charley Hull, meanwhile, needs a significant move Friday to get into the mix. Her even-par 72 in Round 1 left her in a tie for 26th, seven shots back. The Memorial Park course can produce low rounds on a good day, and Hull has the power game to capitalize on the par-5s — but she'll need things to go right from the start of Round 2.

What's Next

Round 2 of the Chevron Championship is underway Friday at Memorial Park Golf Course. The cut will fall after 36 holes, trimming the 132-player field to the top players for the weekend rounds. For Korda, Friday is about maintaining the composure that defined Thursday while building the lead she'll need to hold off a quality field on Saturday and Sunday. A player in Korda's form, at a major, with a two-shot lead after Round 1, is statistically a very strong favorite heading toward the weekend. The story of who challenges her — Tavatanakit, Lee, or perhaps Rhodes making a surprise run — is the compelling subplot that will define the remaining rounds of the 2026 Chevron Championship.
