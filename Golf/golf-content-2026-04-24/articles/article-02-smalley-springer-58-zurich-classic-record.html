Smalley and Springer Fire Record-Tying 58 to Lead Zurich Classic — 12 Birdies, One Eagle, History Made

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | April 24, 2026

It was one of the most remarkable rounds in the history of the Zurich Classic of New Orleans.

Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer shot a 58 — fourteen under par — in Thursday's best ball opening round at TPC Louisiana, matching the tournament's all-time record. They made 12 birdies and added an eagle for good measure. Nobody entered the week putting Smalley and Springer in their top tier of contenders. By the time they walked off the 18th green, they'd done something that has happened only once before in this event's history.

The round leaves them one shot ahead of Davis Thompson and Austin Eckroat, who sit at 13-under. Two teams — Sam Stevens and Zach Bouchou, and Eric Cole and Hank Lebioda — are tied for third at 12-under. The big names of the week are further back: the Fitzpatrick brothers (Matt and Alex) opened with a solid 64, but they sit six shots off the pace. Brooks Koepka and Shane Lowry shot a 66, leaving them eight shots back in a tie for 49th.

What Happened Out There

Best ball golf rewards one thing above all: hot streaks. When both players are firing, the scorecards can compress in a way that standard stroke play rarely allows. Smalley and Springer found a rhythm at TPC Louisiana that produced an almost uninterrupted run of birdies, interrupted only by the eagle that punctuated their round and helped make the record their own.

TPC Louisiana plays as a low-scoring venue in the right conditions, and Thursday's conditions were clearly favorable. But shooting a 58 in any format, on any course, is an extraordinary achievement. The record-tying round will be remembered regardless of how the rest of the week unfolds.

The Challenge Ahead

Friday's Round 2 shifts to alternate shot — foursomes — a format that demands something entirely different from a team. The individual brilliance that can produce a 58 in best ball becomes irrelevant when partners are alternating shots on every hole. Every decision, every swing, every miss is shared. The chemistry required for a great foursomes round is not the same as the relaxed freedom of best ball.

Smalley and Springer will need to show they can transfer their momentum into a completely different competitive environment. Their challengers — Thompson and Eckroat especially — will be motivated to close the one-shot gap. And the big teams further back will be hunting in alternate shot, a format where a single inspired stretch of holes can make up large deficits quickly.

What's Next

Round 2 at TPC Louisiana is alternate shot format, with the cut expected to fall after 36 holes. Smalley and Springer lead by one over Thompson and Eckroat, with the Fitzpatrick brothers and Koepka/Lowry both facing significant deficits to climb. Saturday and Sunday return to best ball and alternate shot respectively. The Zurich Classic remains one of the most unpredictable events on the PGA Tour calendar, and Friday's format shift will tell the full story of who truly belongs at the top of this leaderboard.
