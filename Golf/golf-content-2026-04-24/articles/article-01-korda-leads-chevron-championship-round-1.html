Korda's Bogey-Free 65 Sets the Tone at Chevron Championship — Leading by Two After Round 1

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | April 24, 2026

The first major of the LPGA's 2026 season began exactly the way the favorites — and Nelly Korda in particular — would have scripted it.

Korda fired a seven-under 65 at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston on Thursday, her opening round of the Chevron Championship marked by an absence of mistakes that has become her trademark in contention. She was completely bogey-free — her first bogey-free round in a major since the second round of the 2024 Women's British Open. Golf Digest called it the second-lowest opening round of a major in her career. The result: a two-stroke lead over Patty Tavatanakit and Somi Lee headed into Round 2 Friday.

The $9 million Chevron Championship at Memorial Park is the season's first women's major, and Korda has had it circled on her calendar. She enters the week seeking what would be her third major championship title, and she is now positioned exactly where she needs to be to contend on the weekend.

Korda's Control Was the Story

The most telling detail from Thursday was not the birdies — it was the absence of mistakes. Memorial Park is a genuine championship test that punishes loose swings and errant ball-striking. Korda negotiated it without a blemish over 18 holes, giving herself makeable looks throughout the round and converting enough of them to establish a lead that feels comfortable heading into Friday.

Tavatanakit's 67 was its own impressive effort — bogey-free with five birdies — and the Thai star brings the pedigree and major experience to make a run at the back nine on Sunday. Somi Lee also carded a 67, adding another name to a chase pack that has genuine ability. Yan Liu and American amateur Farah O'Keefe are tied for fourth at four under after posting 68s.

A Debut Worth Noting

Among the week's most pleasant surprises was the performance of Mimi Rhodes, an LPGA rookie from England competing in her first major championship. Rhodes posted a three-under 69 to be part of a 10-way tie for eighth — an extraordinary way to announce yourself on the biggest stage in women's golf. At seven shots back, she has a significant gap to close, but her composure under major pressure at this stage of her career is a story worth watching.

Charley Hull, the other prominent English player in the field, opened with a level-par 72 and sits in a tie for 26th. Hull has the game to make a major move on a weekend round, but she'll need the leaderboard to come to her.

What's Next

Round 2 of the Chevron Championship is underway Friday at Memorial Park Golf Course. For Korda, the task is to maintain the control that defined Thursday's round — eliminate mistakes, make the birdies that are there, and extend the lead before the weekend begins. At two shots clear of Tavatanakit and Lee, she is in a strong position but far from a locked winner. The last 54 holes of the Chevron Championship will tell us whether Korda's form at the first major of 2026 translates into her third career major title.
