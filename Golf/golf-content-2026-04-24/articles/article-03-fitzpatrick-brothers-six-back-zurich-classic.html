Fitzpatrick Brothers Open Six Shots Back at Zurich Classic — Alternate Shot Round Defines Their Week

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | April 24, 2026

For Matt and Alex Fitzpatrick, the Zurich Classic of New Orleans offered one of the sport's most appealing premises: brothers competing together in golf's only PGA Tour team event. Matt entered the week as World No. 3, fresh off a RBC Heritage victory just five days earlier. Alex brought his own game. Bookmakers entered them among the co-favorites at +1175.

Thursday's best ball round produced a solid 64 — eight under par, a score most teams would be proud to post. The problem was that Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer shot a tournament record-tying 58. Six shots separate the Fitzpatricks from the leaders heading into Friday's alternate shot round, and the entire narrative of their week hinges on what they do in foursomes.

The Format Problem

Best ball and alternate shot are not the same sport. They share a course and a scorecard, but the dynamics are entirely different. In best ball, each player plays their own ball and the team records the lower score on each hole. Players can take risks independently — a 7 from one partner is simply ignored if the other makes a birdie. It is a format that rewards individual freedom and confidence.

Alternate shot — also called foursomes — requires something more disciplined. Partners hit alternate shots on every hole, meaning every swing carries shared consequences. One errant drive puts your partner in a difficult position. One shanked approach ruins a hole that was set up perfectly by the tee shot. The trust and synchronization required to shoot a low score in foursomes take years of competitive partnership to develop, and teams that are comfortable in the format typically show it.

Matt's Form Is Elite

What gives the Fitzpatricks genuine reason to believe is Matt's current state. The two-time Zurich Classic participant is playing some of the best iron play on the PGA Tour right now. His winning shot at the RBC Heritage — a 4-iron to 13 feet on the playoff hole that set up the birdie putt that clinched the title — was the kind of moment that defines a player in form. If he can channel that precision into the alternate shot format, where a single good iron shot can rescue a hole, the brothers have the tools to climb the leaderboard.

Alex Fitzpatrick's game has also been trending in the right direction. The quality of their 64 on Thursday suggested both are swinging well. The question is not whether they have the skill — it is whether they have the specific foursomes rhythm to make it count.

What's Next

The Fitzpatricks need a round in the low-to-mid 60s in alternate shot on Friday just to stay within striking distance of the leaders heading into the weekend. At six back, they need the pair ahead of them to come back while they advance — not an impossible scenario in the volatile alternate shot format. If they can get to within three or four shots by end of play Friday, they'll still have a realistic path to contention. Saturday and Sunday offer further opportunities to close the gap. The dream of a family win at the Zurich Classic is still alive, but Friday's round is essential.
