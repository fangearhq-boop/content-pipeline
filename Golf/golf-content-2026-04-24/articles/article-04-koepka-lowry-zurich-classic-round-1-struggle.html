Koepka and Lowry Shot 66 in Round 1 — Now Eight Shots Back and Facing Cut Pressure at Zurich Classic

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | April 24, 2026

Brooks Koepka and Shane Lowry's debut as a Zurich Classic team got off to a rocky start.

The major champions, who formed their partnership at Hobe Sound, Florida and entered the week as one of the event's most talked-about pairings, posted a six-under 66 in Thursday's best ball round and sit in a tie for 49th place — eight shots behind leaders Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer. Friday's alternate shot round is effectively a must-perform situation if they want to make the cut and compete through the weekend.

Lowry came out hot with five birdies on the front nine, looking like the player he's been in his best form. But the back nine went quiet — no birdies in nine holes — and the momentum Lowry had built evaporated. Koepka contributed just one birdie across 18 holes, struggling to find a groove off the tee that would set up favorable approach positions. The combination produced a 66 that isn't unplayable in normal circumstances, but the record-tying 58 from the leaders put everything in perspective.

The Pairing's Promise and the Alternate Shot Reality

There's no question that Koepka and Lowry have the combined talent for a deep run in this event. Two major champions, both capable of posting elite scores when they're on, were always going to generate excitement in the Zurich Classic format. The problem Thursday was execution — specifically the back nine silence from Lowry and the general inefficiency from Koepka.

Friday's alternate shot format is the format where communication and trust become the competitive currency. Koepka is known as one of the most mentally reliable players under pressure; Lowry has the creativity and course management experience to thrive in a foursomes setting. If those individual strengths can be channeled together in alternate shot, a dramatic resurrection is plausible. But the math is demanding: they need to shoot something close to the course record just to get within reasonable range of the leaders.

What's Next

Koepka and Lowry need a low alternate shot round on Friday to survive the cut at TPC Louisiana. The cut line will likely fall around 10-under after 36 holes, and starting at six under means the margin for error in foursomes is extremely thin. A strong Round 2 would keep their weekend alive and set up a potential push on Saturday and Sunday. A mediocre round would end the partnership's debut Zurich Classic together before the weekend gets started. For two competitors with as much pride as Koepka and Lowry, Friday is a significant test of what this team can be.
