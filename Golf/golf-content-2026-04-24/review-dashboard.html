### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET Nelly Korda is doing what Nelly Korda does — she showed up to the first major of 2026 and immediately took control. Korda opened the Chevron Championship with a seven-under 65 at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston, staking herself to a two-stroke lead over Patty Tavatanakit and Somi Lee after Round 1. What made the round even more impressive was the manner of it: Korda was completely bogey-free, marking her first mistake-free round in a major since Round 2 of the 2024 Women's British Open. Golf Digest called it the second-lowest opening round of a major in her career. Tavatanakit and Lee both posted 67s. Yan Liu and amateur Farah O'Keefe are in a tie for fourth at 68. England's Mimi Rhodes, a rookie on the LPGA Tour, announced herself on the biggest stage with a 69 — part of a 10-way tie for eighth in just her first major appearance. Charley Hull, the other prominent English name in the field, shot a level-par 72 and faces a tough climb over the weekend. Korda enters this week seeking her third major title and has been as clinical as anyone on the LPGA Tour over the past two seasons. If she plays like this through the weekend, it will be very difficult for anyone to catch her. #GolfFanrecap | #ChevronChampionship | #LPGA

Fitzpatrick Brothers Open Six Back — Co-Favorites Need Big Alternate Shot Round
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 1:00 PM ET Matt and Alex Fitzpatrick entered the Zurich Classic as among the tournament's most closely watched teams. Matt, ranked No. 3 in the world after winning the RBC Heritage just five days ago, teamed with his brother for what many hoped would be a storybook week in New Orleans. Round 1 told a more complicated story. The brothers posted an 8-under 64 in the best ball format — a round most teams would be thrilled with — but found themselves staring up at Smalley and Springer's extraordinary 58 from six shots back. The dream of a family victory hasn't disappeared, but the mathematics of the tournament demand a much lower score in Friday's alternate shot round. The format shift is significant. Best ball, where each player plays their own ball and records the best score per hole, rewards individual brilliance. Alternate shot demands synchronization — one player tees off, the partner plays the next stroke, and they continue alternating until the hole is finished. It's an entirely different test of chemistry, communication, and trust. Matt Fitzpatrick has demonstrated his iron game is in elite form right now. His 4-iron to 13 feet at the RBC Heritage won him the tournament in a playoff. Whether he and Alex can translate that precision into the rhythm of foursomes play is the question that will define their week. #GolfFanrecap | #ZurichClassic | #PGATour
