Dow Championship Preview: Why Team Golf Brings Out the Best in the LPGA Tour

By Ryan Calloway | June 10, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

While the men's game heads to Toronto for the RBC Canadian Open, the women's tour gathers in Midland, Michigan for one of the most distinctive events on the professional golf calendar.

The Dow Championship begins Thursday, June 11, at Midland Country Club, bringing together 72 two-player LPGA teams for a $3.3 million purse and a format that rewards partnership as much as it rewards individual brilliance. The tournament runs through Sunday, June 14.

In a sport built around individual competition, the Dow Championship is a genuine outlier — and the better for it.

What Makes the Format Special

The team format creates dynamics that traditional stroke-play competition cannot replicate. Players who have built their games around individual expression must learn to adapt to a partner's tendencies, communicate under pressure, and make strategic decisions with someone else's game factored in.

The best partnerships at the Dow Championship tend to find a particular chemistry: one player who thrives on calculated risk and one whose steadiness keeps the team on course when the aggressive play doesn't come off. When those two qualities find their balance across four rounds, the results tend to be extraordinary.

The format also reveals something about the breadth of the LPGA's roster that individual events sometimes obscure. Players who excel in partnership settings — communicators, teammates, decision-makers under shared pressure — sometimes perform at levels their solo statistics wouldn't fully predict.

The Season Context

The Dow Championship arrives at an important moment in the 2026 LPGA season. Jessica Korda won the US Women's Open in late May at Riviera Country Club — her second major of 2026 and fourth of her career — establishing herself as the dominant player of the moment on the women's tour. A $3.3 million team event in Michigan won't determine the season's outcome, but it will provide context about who is playing well heading into the second half of the summer.

The LPGA calendar features a collection of major championships yet to come, and performances at events like the Dow Championship have historically identified the players who arrive at those majors in form. Momentum on the women's tour builds in competition, and Midland this week is competition.

A Different Kind of Viewing Experience

For fans who watch professional golf primarily for its drama and competition — not just for the leaderboard — the Dow Championship is worth the attention. Team dynamics, mid-round conversations between partners, and the shifts in strategy that come with having two players in the same score are visible in ways that individual competition doesn't create.

The broadcast on Golf Channel and the PGA Tour app (which includes LPGA coverage) will capture the team interactions that define the tournament. It is a week where the human element of professional golf is more visible than most — and a compelling companion to the men's event happening simultaneously in Canada.

What's Next

The Dow Championship runs June 11-14 at Midland Country Club in Midland, Michigan. Coverage begins Thursday on Golf Channel. The winning team of the $3.3 million event will be determined Sunday afternoon.

For the LPGA Tour, it is one more week of competition building toward the second half of a season that has already produced memorable moments. For fans, it is a chance to watch professional golf's most interesting team format play out on a course that has hosted this event for years and knows exactly how to frame a compelling week of competition.
