RBC Canadian Open Preview: Fitzpatrick Chases History, Fleetwood Leads the Way at TPC Toronto

By Marcus Cole | June 10, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The 2026 RBC Canadian Open begins Thursday at TPC Toronto at Osprey Valley, and without the sport's two biggest names — Scottie Scheffler and Rory McIlroy — the field is as wide open as any major event on the PGA Tour this season.

That creates an opportunity for the players who have spent 2026 building their case as legitimate tour-level forces. Two of them head into the week as co-favorites, separated by just 100 points on the odds board: Tommy Fleetwood at +1200, and Matt Fitzpatrick at +1300.

The $9.8 million purse, the historic prestige of the Canadian Open, and the TPC Toronto at Osprey Valley's demanding North Course ensure the week will be intensely contested from the opening tee shot Thursday morning.

Fleetwood: The Motivated Favorite

Tommy Fleetwood enters this week carrying a particular kind of motivation. The Englishman won his first PGA Tour title at the 2025 Tour Championship — the FedExCup finale — establishing himself as more than just a consistent presence and proving he can win when everything is on the line.

He has history at this specific event that adds another layer of purpose. In 2023, Fleetwood finished as runner-up at the Canadian Open, losing to hometown favorite Nick Taylor in a four-hole playoff. That kind of near-miss tends to either define a player as someone who can't close, or fuel a return trip that ends differently. Fleetwood has shown enough since 2023 to suggest he belongs in the second category.

His game suits the North Course at TPC Toronto — a layout that rewards accurate iron play, patience, and the kind of steady, disciplined scoring that defines Fleetwood at his best.

Fitzpatrick: Already Historic in 2026

Matt Fitzpatrick's 2026 season is one for the record books. Three PGA Tour victories in the first half of the year — including consecutive wins at the RBC Heritage and the Zurich Classic alongside his brother Alex — have established him as one of the most dominant players in the world this season.

A fourth victory this week in Toronto would cement his place among the most prolific single-season performers in recent PGA Tour history. The world No. 4 arrives at TPC Toronto with momentum, confidence, and a game built on precision that fits the demands of the North Course.

His previous best at the Canadian Open was a T10 in 2022. Whether a deeper result follows this week will depend on how well his short game holds up over four rounds on a course that demands a complete performance.

The Rest of the Field

Beyond the co-favorites, the field contains legitimate contenders. Collin Morikawa has been searching for a signature win to recapture the dominance of his earlier career and a Canadian Open title at TPC Toronto would qualify. Brooks Koepka, Max Homa, and Tony Finau fill out a lineup that has enough depth to produce a competitive week from start to finish.

The most notable absence beyond Scheffler and McIlroy is JT Poston, who withdrew from the field after his Marathon Memorial Tournament victory last Sunday. Poston played 33 holes in a single day, beat Ryan Gerard in a two-hole playoff, won $4 million, and earned a US Open exemption. The rest was justified.

What's Next

Round 1 begins Thursday, June 11, at TPC Toronto at Osprey Valley. The tournament runs through Sunday, June 14. Coverage is available on Golf Channel and the PGA Tour app.

By Sunday afternoon, the 2026 Canadian Open champion will either be a player adding to an already remarkable season story, or a new name joining the list of players who used this event to announce themselves. In an open field with history and $9.8 million on the line, either outcome is possible.
