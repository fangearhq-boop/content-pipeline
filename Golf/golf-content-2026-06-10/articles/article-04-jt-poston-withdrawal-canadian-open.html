Why JT Poston's Canadian Open Withdrawal Is Completely Justified

By Marcus Cole | June 10, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

JT Poston withdrew from the RBC Canadian Open this week, and the only appropriate response is: of course he did.

One week ago Sunday, at Muirfield Village Golf Club in Dublin, Ohio, Poston played 33 holes of golf. A combination of weather delays and the demands of a tightly contested final round had pushed the conclusion of the Memorial Tournament into what became an exhausting marathon. He completed all of it and then walked into a playoff against Ryan Gerard — the kind of test that separates elite professionals from everyone else.

He won that playoff on the second hole. He also won $4 million, his fourth PGA Tour career title, an exemption into The Open Championship, and a spot in the US Open field.

The RBC Canadian Open can wait.

What the Memorial Win Meant

The Memorial Tournament is one of the most prestigious events on the PGA Tour calendar, played annually at Jack Nicklaus's home course in Ohio. Winning there carries a weight that a standard tour victory does not. Poston's win — his fourth career title, achieved in the most physically demanding circumstances the tour can create — immediately reshaped his standing.

His world ranking moved from 94th to 39th in a single week. He secured his fourth consecutive US Open berth. He has an Open Championship exemption that didn't exist before he teed off last Thursday. A player who had been a solid, reliable presence on tour without ever breaking through into the elite tier has, after one extended Sunday at Muirfield Village, raised his ceiling significantly.

Processing that kind of moment — and the physical toll of the weekend that produced it — is not something you do by immediately flying to Toronto for another 72-hole event. Recovery and preparation for the US Open at Shinnecock Hills in eight days is the priority.

What His Withdrawal Means for TPC Toronto

From the Canadian Open's perspective, Poston's withdrawal is a loss. He was arriving with exactly the kind of momentum that tends to produce strong tournament performances — fresh victory, elevated confidence, game in peak form. The field loses one of its most interesting storylines.

What it also does is open the leaderboard a little wider. In a week where Scheffler and McIlroy are already absent, an open field benefits the players positioned just below the very top of the odds board. Tommy Fleetwood (+1200) and Matt Fitzpatrick (+1300) remain the co-favorites, and each will know that the path to a Canadian Open title has one fewer significant obstacle than it did last week.

Collin Morikawa, Brooks Koepka, and the rest of the field will feel the same. When a player of Poston's current form exits the draw, opportunity expands.

What's Next

The RBC Canadian Open begins Thursday, June 11, at TPC Toronto at Osprey Valley, with the final round on Sunday, June 14. Poston's attention will turn quickly to Shinnecock Hills and the US Open beginning June 18.

His Memorial win has him playing some of the best golf of his career at the right moment of the season. If his preparation for the US Open gives him two weeks of focused practice at his current level, the major-championship field at Shinnecock will have another legitimate contender in the mix — even if Canada gets to host the tournament without him.
