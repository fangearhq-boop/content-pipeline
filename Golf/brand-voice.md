# Golf Fanrecap — Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality

If Golf Fanrecap were a person, they'd be **the golf nut who watches Thursday morning coverage, knows every course's signature hole, and has strong opinions about the PGA-LIV situation** — the one who texts "Scheffler just eagled 13 at Augusta to take a 3-shot lead" before you even opened the leaderboard app. They cover the whole golf world — PGA Tour, LIV, LPGA, DP World Tour, all four majors — with genuine love for the game. They appreciate the beauty and pressure of competition, respect golf's traditions while embracing where the sport is headed, and never talk down to weekend golfers trying to keep up with the tour.

## Voice Attributes

### Knowledgeable
- **We are**: Strokes-gained-aware, course-management-savvy, equipment-literate. We understand what separates elite ball-striking from scrambling, and we explain it accessibly.
- **We are not**: Gatekeeping, elitist, or burying readers in ShotLink data without context.
- **This sounds like**: "Scheffler's strokes gained tee-to-green is absurd — he's gaining nearly 3 shots per round on the field, and it's his iron play that's doing most of the heavy lifting."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Well, if you actually understood proximity to hole from 150-175 yards on dogleg-left par 4s..."

### Passionate
- **We are**: Genuinely moved by great shots, Sunday back-nine drama, and the majors. A hole-out from the fairway, a clutch par save, a first-time winner's reaction — we feel it.
- **We are not**: Over-the-top, fake-hype, or treating every birdie like it's the greatest moment in golf history.
- **This sounds like**: "Morikawa just stuffed a 7-iron to 3 feet on 17 with a two-shot lead. Ice cold. That's major championship golf."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "OMG BEST SHOT EVER!!! GOLF IS THE GREATEST SPORT IN THE WORLD!!!!"

### Inclusive
- **We are**: Full-tour coverage across PGA Tour, LIV Golf, LPGA, and DP World Tour. Women's golf and international tours get real coverage, not token mentions.
- **We are not**: PGA-Tour-only or treating LIV as either the enemy or the savior.
- **This sounds like**: "Nelly Korda's run of five straight wins was the most dominant stretch in professional golf this decade — any tour, any gender."
- **This does NOT sound like**: (silence on every event that isn't a PGA Tour signature event)

### Respectful
- **We are**: True to golf's culture of sportsmanship. We critique performance and decisions, not character. We respect the traditions that make the game special.
- **We are not**: Tearing down players personally or turning every disagreement into a culture war.
- **This sounds like**: "Rahm's decision to leave for LIV was controversial, but his game hasn't missed a beat — he's still one of the five best players on the planet."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Rahm sold out and everyone knows it. Total fraud."

### Opinionated
- **We are**: Willing to take a stance on the PGA-LIV merger, course setups, Rules of Golf changes, and player decisions — always backed by evidence or reasoned argument.
- **We are not**: Reckless, trolling, or controversial for clicks.
- **This sounds like**: "Hot take: The USGA needs to stop narrowing fairways to 22 yards at the US Open. It doesn't reward accuracy — it just punishes everyone equally and turns the tournament into a putting contest."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "The USGA is ruining golf and they should all be fired."

### Fun
- **We are**: Appreciative of great shots, course beauty, caddie celebrations, fan moments, and the lighter side of the game. Golf is supposed to be enjoyable.
- **We are not**: Reducing everything to memes or forgetting that competitive golf is a serious sport.
- **This sounds like**: "The par-3 contest at Augusta is still the best appetizer in sports. Watching guys skip it across the pond at 16 never gets old."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "lmaooo he chunked it from the bunker what a hack"

## Platform-Specific Voice

### X/Twitter
- Crisp, leaderboard-style updates during tournament rounds
- ALL CAPS for emphasis on key moments (use sparingly, 1-2 per post)
- Dashes and periods for dramatic pacing: "-8 through 12 holes. Two-shot lead. Sunday at Augusta."
- Strong opinions backed by stats or course knowledge
- Max 3 hashtags per tweet
- #GolfFanrecap always included + 2 contextual hashtags (#PGATour, #TheMasters, #LPGATour, #LIVGolf, #TheOpen, #USOpen, #PGAChamp, #RyderCup)
- 1-3 emoji max per post (flag in hole, fire, eyes most common)

### Facebook
- More explanatory — assume the reader needs tournament context
- Narrative storytelling across 3-4 paragraphs
- Opinionated but less aggressive than X
- One emoji per caption OK, no emojis in long-form posts
- No hashtags in Facebook posts
- End with a forward-looking statement or question to drive engagement
- Photo caption voice is punchier than long-form

### Articles
- Professional golf journalism tone
- Accessible to casual fans — explain course context, don't assume everyone knows the hole layout
- Stats woven into narrative, not listed in isolation
- Always end with "What's Next" forward-looking section
- No first-person ("I think") — use "the numbers suggest" or just state the take
- Credit sources: "(per DataGolf)" or "(via PGA Tour ShotLink)"

## Style Rules

### Grammar & Mechanics

| Rule | Golf Fanrecap Standard | Example |
|------|----------------------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes | "driving, iron play, and putting" |
| Headings | Title Case | "Top 10 Players to Watch at The Masters" |
| Contractions | Yes, always | "he's" not "he is" |
| Numbers | Spell out 1-9, numerals 10+ | "three birdies" but "12 pars" |
| Stats | Always numerals | "3.2 strokes gained", "67.4% GIR", "28.1 putts per round" |
| Score format | Relative to par | "Scheffler -8 (64)" or "Scheffler leads at -12 through 54 holes" |
| Leaderboard format | Numbered list | "1. Scheffler -12, 2. McIlroy -10, T3. Rahm/Hovland -9" |
| Time format | 12-hour with timezone | "1:30 PM ET" |
| Tee times | Always ET | Convert if sourcing from local or tour sites in other timezones |
| ALL CAPS | Sparingly for emphasis | "EAGLE on 13 to take the lead" — max 1-2 per post |
| Exclamation marks | OK in social, never in articles | Social: "What a shot!" Article: never |
| Emoji (X) | 1-3 max per post | Common: flag in hole, fire, eyes |
| Emoji (Facebook) | 1 max in captions | Never in long-form posts |
| Emoji (Articles) | Never | Articles are emoji-free |

### Terminology

| Use This | Not This | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| The Masters | Augusta, the Masters Tournament | "Augusta National" OK when referring to the course, not the event |
| The Open Championship | British Open | "The Open" OK after first mention |
| PGA Championship | PGA, the PGA | Avoid confusion with PGA Tour — spell out on first mention |
| birdie, eagle, bogey | birdy, eagel | Standard golf spelling |
| strokes gained | SG | Spell out on first mention, SG OK after |
| cut line | the cut | "missed the cut" or "made the cut" — standard phrasing |
| tee time | tee-time | Two words, no hyphen |
| playoff | play-off | One word in American golf usage |
| PGA Tour | PGA tour, the Tour | Capitalize "Tour" when referring to PGA Tour specifically |
| LIV Golf | LIV, Liv Golf | All caps "LIV" — it's a Roman numeral (54) |
| DP World Tour | European Tour | Rebranded; use "DP World Tour" on first mention, "DPWT" OK after |
| LPGA Tour | LPGA tour | Capitalize "Tour" |
| surnames after first mention | first names throughout | "Scottie Scheffler" first, then "Scheffler" |
