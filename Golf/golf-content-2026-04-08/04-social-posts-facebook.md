There is one famous footnote: in 66 years of the Par 3 Contest, no winner has ever gone on to win the Masters in the same week. Whether that streak holds is tomorrow's problem. Today, it's just golf.

### STORY 4: McIlroy's Putting Problem — Can He Fix It Before Augusta's Greens Break Him?

#### Long-Form Post — 3:30 PM ET

Rory McIlroy leads the Masters field in Strokes Gained: Off the Tee. He is No. 1. He hits it straight, he hits it far, and he gives himself birdie opportunities on virtually every hole.

But Augusta National does not reward driving excellence in isolation. The greens at Augusta are notoriously difficult — undulating, fast, and capable of turning small mistakes into catastrophic scores. And McIlroy's putting this season has been genuinely problematic: he ranks 102nd in putts per round and 136th in one-putt percentage, among all Tour players.

For a defending champion with the talent to go wire-to-wire, that stat line is the central narrative tension of his title defense. Back-to-back Masters has only been done once in the modern era — Tiger Woods, 2001 and 2002 — and McIlroy knows better than anyone that the road to a repeat runs directly through the cup.

He has one top-10 in four starts this season and withdrew from the Arnold Palmer Invitational with an injury. He is not playing his best golf. On Augusta's greens, any weakness gets amplified.

Can he fix it before 10:31 AM Thursday? That's the question his caddie, his coach, and every golf fan in the world is asking.

### STORY 6: Chevron Championship — LPGA's First Major Debuts at Houston's Memorial Park

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

While the Masters dominates this week's golf conversation, the LPGA Tour's major championship season is right around the corner. The 2026 Chevron Championship — the first women's major of the year — arrives April 23-26 at Memorial Park Golf Course in Houston, Texas, marking the tournament's first-ever appearance at that venue.

The venue change is significant. Memorial Park is one of Houston's most celebrated public courses, redesigned in recent years to tournament standards. For 2026, a temporary concrete-lined pool has been installed near the 18th green to recreate the famous "jump in the pond" tradition associated with the Chevron Championship — a permanent man-made pond is planned for 2027.

The television schedule puts Golf Channel on Thursday and Friday, with NBC taking over for the weekend.

The player to beat is Nelly Korda, who leads the LPGA Tour this season in both Strokes Gained Total and Strokes Gained Tee-to-Green — the two most comprehensive measures of overall performance. She has finished runner-up twice in recent events and is chasing her first major title of 2026.
