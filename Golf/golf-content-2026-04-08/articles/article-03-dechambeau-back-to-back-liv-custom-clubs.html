DeChambeau Arrives at Augusta With Back-to-Back LIV Wins — and Clubs He Built Himself

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

April 8, 2026

Most players arrive at the Masters with clubs they've been fitted for, refined over months, and practiced with for hundreds of hours. Bryson DeChambeau arrived at Augusta National having won two consecutive LIV Golf titles — in Singapore and South Africa — carrying clubs he reportedly designed and built himself.

DeChambeau told media this week that he has been constructing his own custom irons, developing a new driver design, and testing new wedges in South Africa just days before the Masters. Whether those custom-built clubs appear in his bag Thursday morning is still to be determined. That he is operating with this level of intensity and confidence heading into the year's first major is not in question.

The LIV Form That Makes Him Dangerous

DeChambeau's back-to-back LIV victories are not a small footnote. He won the LIV Golf Singapore event in a playoff over Richard T. Lee, and followed it by winning LIV Golf South Africa in a playoff over Jon Rahm — a player who is himself a major champion and among the world's best. DeChambeau's Crushers GC team also captured the South Africa team trophy.

That is a demanding stretch of high-level competition to perform at the very top of, and DeChambeau navigated it as well as anyone in the world could. He walked off the South Africa course having beaten the field twice in playoff situations — the most pressure-compressed form of golf there is.

He arrives at Augusta, by any fair reading, in the best form of his career.

The Equipment Angle

If the back-to-back wins sound like enough of a story, there is still the matter of the clubs. DeChambeau has always been golf's most visible equipment obsessive — a player who approaches clubs not as tools he uses but as variables he controls. Building his own equipment is an extension of that philosophy taken to its logical extreme.

He is not the first golfer to tinker extensively with his equipment. But designing and constructing clubs to test at the world's most demanding major is something different. It reflects the confidence of a player who trusts his own judgment over conventional wisdom — even at Augusta, where conventional wisdom has been refined over nine decades.

Ten LIV golfers are in the 91-player Masters field in 2026. DeChambeau is the most dangerous of them entering this week.

What's Next

DeChambeau tees off at 10:07 AM ET on Thursday alongside Matt Fitzpatrick and Xander Schauffele — a grouping that could produce some of the more compelling golf of Round 1. If his LIV form translates to the Augusta turf, he will be in the conversation through the weekend.

DeChambeau has finished inside the top 10 in each of the last two Masters, including a runner-up finish at Augusta in 2021. He knows the course. He knows what it takes. And for the first time in his career, he may know exactly what is in his bag on the first tee.
