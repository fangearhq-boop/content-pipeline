Masters 2026 Round 1 Preview: Tee Times, Pairings, and What to Watch Thursday

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

April 8, 2026

The 90th Masters begins Thursday, April 9, and the Round 1 pairings are everything fans could have asked for. Rory McIlroy and Scottie Scheffler — the two players driving the tournament's biggest narratives — go off in different waves, giving the day a natural arc: a morning chapter built around McIlroy's title defense, and an afternoon chapter built around Scheffler's quest for a third Green Jacket.

Here is the full rundown of who to watch and when.

Morning Wave — The Challengers Go Early

The day begins with Bryson DeChambeau teeing off at 10:07 AM ET alongside Matt Fitzpatrick and Xander Schauffele — three players who could all realistically compete for a Green Jacket. DeChambeau arrives in the best form of his career, fresh off back-to-back LIV Golf victories. Fitzpatrick is a measured ball-striker who suits Augusta's premium on precision. Schauffele is a consistent top-10 presence at majors who has never won one — this week could be the week.

At 10:19 AM, Hideki Matsuyama — the 2021 champion — goes out with Collin Morikawa (who needs only the Masters for a career Grand Slam) and Russell Henley. Morikawa's iron precision is tailor-made for Augusta, and Matsuyama knows the course's final holes as well as anyone in the field.

At 10:31 AM, Rory McIlroy tees off alongside Cameron Young and amateur Mason Howell. McIlroy is chasing the one thing Tiger Woods did in 2001-02 that no one has replicated since: winning back-to-back Masters titles. He is the defending champion. He hit the Green Jacket after completing his career Grand Slam last year in a moment that Augusta fans will not soon forget. Thursday morning, that journey continues.

Afternoon Wave — The Favorite Waits

Jon Rahm, a former Masters champion, goes at 1:08 PM ET with Chris Gotterup and Ludvig Åberg. Rahm is another LIV player arriving with recent form — he also competed deep into the South Africa event before DeChambeau bested him in the playoff. Åberg is one of the most explosive young talents in international golf and is capable of making a run at any major.

At 1:44 PM, Scottie Scheffler — World No. 1, +500 favorite — plays alongside Robert MacIntyre and Gary Woodland. Scheffler has two Green Jackets already, from 2022 and 2024. A third would cement him as one of Augusta's most prolific champions and close in on the record books shared by legends of the game. His newborn son Remy is in the gallery. His wife Meredith has made the trip. And despite a three-start form slump entering Augusta — his worst stretch in four years — Scheffler is still the man everyone expects to beat.

What's Next

Round 1 coverage begins Thursday morning on Amazon Prime Video, marking the streaming platform's Masters debut. The $21 million purse, the 91-player field, and the 90th edition of one of sport's great events awaits.

The Par 3 Contest winner, whoever that turns out to be from today's event, will attempt to break a 66-year superstition that has seen every Wednesday winner fail to capture Sunday's Green Jacket. The streak has held for 66 years. It makes for good conversation on the range Thursday morning.

Set your alarm. Augusta calls at 10:07 AM.
