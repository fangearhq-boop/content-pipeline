The Par 3 Contest Is Today — And a 66-Year Masters Superstition Is on the Line

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

April 8, 2026

Before the first tee shot of the 2026 Masters is struck Thursday morning, Augusta National hosts one of golf's most beloved rituals: the Par 3 Contest. The event begins at noon ET on Wednesday, April 8, with ESPN broadcasting from 2 to 4 PM (also available via the ESPN app and Disney+). And hovering over every birdie, every hole-in-one, and every family caddie moment is a superstition that has held for 66 years.

No winner of the Par 3 Contest has ever gone on to win the Masters in the same week.

What the Par 3 Contest Is

Augusta National's Par 3 course is a nine-hole layout tucked behind the main course — a shorter, softer version of golf where the world's best players walk the fairways with their children, spouses, and parents serving as their caddies. The atmosphere is unlike anything else in professional golf. A child barely tall enough to carry a bag walks alongside a player who will compete for a major title twelve hours later. Grandparents ride in golf carts. Hole-in-ones are celebrated with equal enthusiasm whether they happen to the World No. 1 or a first-time Masters debutant.

It is, deliberately, a breath before the storm.

The Superstition

The Par 3 Contest has been held since 1960. In the 65 editions completed through 2025, not one winner has won the Masters that same week. This is not a rule or a jinx with any rational explanation — it is simply a statistical anomaly that the golfing world has collectively decided to treat as inevitable.

Many players have been known to deliberately avoid winning, though Augusta National does not officially endorse that strategy. Others have simply been unable to find the magic that produced a Par 3 win when it mattered most on the main course. Whatever the reason, the streak has held, and today's contest adds another chapter.

Whoever wins Wednesday afternoon will walk onto the first tee Thursday morning with 91 competitors who would be happy to remind them of that fact.

What's Next

Round 1 of the 2026 Masters begins April 9. Rory McIlroy tees off at 10:31 AM ET with Cameron Young and amateur Mason Howell. Scottie Scheffler — the +500 favorite and two-time Masters champion — goes at 1:44 PM ET alongside Robert MacIntyre and Gary Woodland.

The Par 3 Contest is the calm before one of the most anticipated Masters in recent years. Two of the world's most marketable players — one defending, one chasing history — will sort out who wears the Green Jacket on Sunday. Today, they're handing the bag to their kids.

Enjoy it while it lasts.
