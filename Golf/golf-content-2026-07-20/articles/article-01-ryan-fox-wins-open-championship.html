Ryan Fox Sinks the Putt on 18 to Win the 2026 Open Championship

By Ryan Calloway

July 20, 2026

When Ryan Fox lined up a 12-foot birdie putt on the 72nd hole of the 154th Open Championship at Royal Birkdale on Sunday, almost nobody watching gave him even odds of winning a major championship. He was 39 years old, ranked No. 56 in the world, and had entered the week as a 200-1 longshot. He had never won a major.

He rolled the putt directly into the center of the cup.

Fox carded a final-round 68 to finish the championship at -10, claiming the Claret Jug by one stroke over Cameron Young (-9). Sam Burns, the 54-hole leader who had entered Sunday with a 2-shot advantage, finished at -8 after a dramatic collapse on the front nine left him scrambling for pars across the back stretch.

For Fox, it was his first major championship title. It was also one of the most improbable major victories in recent memory.

The Final Stretch

The tournament's final chapter was written in the last six holes. Fox made four birdies across his final stretch at Royal Birkdale, the kind of hot finish that forces leaderboard movement and pressures everyone ahead. As Burns was conceding ground on the front nine, Fox was picking up shots on the back.

The decisive moment came on the 18th. With Young already in the clubhouse at -9 and Burns unable to respond on the back nine, Fox needed birdie to win outright. He delivered — sinking the 12-footer to stand alone at -10 and become champion golfer of the year.

Fox is only the third New Zealander in golf history to win The Open Championship. He held his arms aloft after the putt dropped, a moment that captured the improbability of everything that had happened over four days at Birkdale.

The Leaderboard Story

The final standings told the full drama of Sunday at Royal Birkdale. Cameron Young shot a stunning 9-under 64 to chase Fox all the way to the 18th hole. He did everything a player can do to win a major on a Sunday — and still came up one short.

Burns' 72 and third-place finish at -8 was the backdrop against which Fox and Young's battle played out. Burns had the tournament and lost it. Young played the best round of the day and was denied by a single putt. Fox did what champions do: he made the shot he needed, exactly when he needed it.

What's Next

Ryan Fox takes the Claret Jug to New Zealand as a first-time major champion at age 39. His victory will be celebrated at home as one of the country's greatest sporting achievements in golf — and it will force a reappraisal of his standing in the global game.

No. 56 in the world entered Birkdale. A major champion left. For everyone who watched Fox make that putt on 18, the image will not fade quickly.
