Analysts noted that Burns' collapse matched a pattern not seen in nearly three decades at The Open — a 54-hole leader giving away the tournament in the final round in such dramatic fashion. It is a brutal statistic, and it will follow Burns in media coverage for years.

But this is golf. Burns is already one of the best players in the world. He will have more major opportunities. He will have more Sunday leads.

He just won't have this one back.

### STORY 4: LIV Golf Funding Crisis

#### Long-Form Post — 4:00 PM ET

As the 154th Open Championship concluded at Royal Birkdale — where several LIV Golf players competed through the year's third major — the structural future of the breakaway tour grows increasingly uncertain.

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund, which has backed LIV Golf since its 2022 launch, confirmed in April 2026 that it will end its financial support after the current season. The numbers behind that decision are stark: PIF reportedly invested more than $6 billion into LIV since 2022 at annual losses estimated between $500 and $600 million per year.

Now the situation may be accelerating. Reports from inside the LIV structure suggest PIF may cut payments before the 2026 season formally concludes, putting the final four events of the schedule at genuine risk. LIV CEO Scott O'Neil, when asked whether the remaining events would take place, could not offer a guarantee.

In response, LIV has moved to establish an independent board — Gene Davis and Jon Zinman have been named — to seek new long-term financial partners. The investment bank Ducera Partners has been hired to assist in the search. The goal is to replace Saudi funding with alternative backing before the 2026 season closes out.

Whether new investors emerge on that timeline is an open question. The tour's ability to attract and retain elite players depends in large part on prize purse guarantees. If funding evaporates before those events take place, the legal and contractual implications would be significant.

What happens to LIV Golf next will be one of the most important business stories in professional golf over the next several months.

Golf does not always cooperate with expectations.

His next opportunity will come. But this one wasn't it.
