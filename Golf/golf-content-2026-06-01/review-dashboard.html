### Long-Form Post Joaquin Niemann has done something no player has done in the history of LIV Golf — and he did it on the 18th hole at Asiad Country Club in Busan, South Korea. With Talor Gooch matching him step for step through 54 holes at 12-under par, Niemann and Gooch went to sudden death on Sunday evening. On the par-4 18th, Niemann drained a birdie to walk away with the title. The win — his eighth in LIV Golf competition — makes him the most prolific individual winner in the league's history. He now stands three victories clear of any other player. The team competition also produced a winner: Bryson DeChambeau's Crushers GC posted a combined 23-under to win the Korea team trophy by three shots. DeChambeau himself finished T3 individually. For Gooch, a playoff loss is a bitter outcome after posting the kind of week that would win most tournaments. But Niemann — already a force before this — is now in the business of making LIV history. ### Image Post Caption Niemann wins LIV Korea in a playoff over Gooch. ### Long-Form Post The golf world turns its attention to Columbus, Ohio, this week for The Memorial Tournament — one of the most prestigious non-major events on the PGA Tour calendar, and the one that comes with a $20 million purse. Scottie Scheffler arrives as the defending champion — and as the man chasing history. He has won The Memorial in back-to-back years, becoming only the second player ever to win consecutive editions at Muirfield Village. Nine of the world's top-10 players are in the field. Rory McIlroy and Cameron Young join Scheffler as projected favorites. Jack Nicklaus' tournament, at Jack Nicklaus' course, with one of the strongest fields in golf. The Memorial runs June 4-7.
