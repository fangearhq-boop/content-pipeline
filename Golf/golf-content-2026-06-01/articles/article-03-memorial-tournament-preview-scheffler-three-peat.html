Scottie Scheffler Chases Memorial Three-Peat as Nine of World's Top 10 Head to Muirfield Village

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 1, 2026

The Memorial Tournament has produced some of golf's most compelling storylines, but the one arriving at Muirfield Village this week may be the most historically loaded in decades. Scottie Scheffler, the world's top-ranked player and defending champion for the second consecutive year, arrives in Dublin, Ohio chasing something only one golfer has ever done: win the Memorial three times in a row.

Tiger Woods did it from 1999 to 2001. If Scheffler wins this week, he joins that company. The field assembled to stop him — nine of the top 10 players in the world — suggests that accomplishment would be earned, not given.

The Stakes

The Memorial Tournament is one of the PGA Tour's signature events, with a $20,000,000 purse and $4,000,000 going to the winner. Jack Nicklaus, who hosts the event at the course he designed, has built Muirfield Village into a venue that rewards precision and patience. It does not forgive mistakes, and it has a long history of exposing players who are not fully dialed in.

Scheffler has been fully dialed in here. He has solved this golf course back-to-back, navigating the demanding bentgrass greens and the tree-lined fairways well enough to hold off elite fields in each of the last two editions. Doing it a third time, against a concentrated collection of the world's best, would be a statement about consistency at an elite level that goes beyond a single hot week.

The Field

The Memorial routinely draws a deep field, and the 2026 edition is no different. Nine of the world's top 10 players are in the field, making this one of the strongest weekly field assemblies of the season outside the majors. Among the projected challengers are Rory McIlroy and Cameron Young, both of whom have the games to compete at Muirfield Village and the motivation to deny Scheffler a piece of history.

McIlroy, in particular, enters with his own recent momentum and a ranking that reflects consistent form across the 2026 season. Young has developed into one of the more dangerous players in the world from tee to green and has shown he can contend at the highest level.

What Muirfield Village Requires

The golf course demands accuracy off the tee. Water comes into play on multiple holes, and the greens — among the firmest and fastest on the Tour schedule — punish approach shots that miss their intended landing zones. Players who can control trajectory and manage the slopes will have advantages over those who rely on power alone.

It is a course built for ballstrikers who think carefully, which aligns precisely with how Scheffler plays the game. His ability to shape shots in both directions and keep mistakes to a minimum has made him the dominant player on Tour across the 2024 and 2025 seasons, and the 2026 campaign has given no reason to believe anything has changed.

The Historical Context

Three consecutive Memorial wins would place Scheffler in a category reserved for the greatest player the sport has ever seen. Woods won here in 1999, 2000, and 2001 during a stretch of dominance that is still talked about as the benchmark for what elite golf can look like. Scheffler chasing that standard — even at a single event — underscores how elevated his game has been over the last two-plus seasons.

Whether he gets there will be settled over 72 holes starting Thursday in Dublin. But the narrative is set: Scheffler versus history, with nine of the world's top players trying to write a different ending.
