Russell Henley Makes Four Straight Birdies to Win the 2026 Schwab Challenge in Dramatic Playoff

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 1, 2026

Russell Henley needed a miracle on Sunday at Colonial Country Club, and he delivered one. Down to Eric Cole entering the back nine of the final round of the 2026 Charles Schwab Challenge, Henley ran off three consecutive birdies on holes 16, 17, and 18 to force a sudden-death playoff. Then he made a fourth straight birdie on the first playoff hole to claim the title and $1,782,000 from a $9,900,000 purse.

Both Henley and Cole finished the 72 regulation holes at -12 (268). What followed was one of the most memorable closings at Colonial in recent memory.

The Comeback

Cole had played a solid final round and appeared to have control of the tournament heading into the closing stretch. But golf at Colonial's back nine has a way of shifting momentum in an instant, and Henley found his rhythm at exactly the right time.

Three holes, three birdies. The deficit evaporated. The playoff was set.

On the 18th in sudden death, Henley did not blink. He converted a fourth consecutive birdie to walk off the green as champion. The sequence — four birdies in a row to go from behind to victorious — was a display of composure under pressure that Henley will talk about for the rest of his career.

Cole Falls Short

For Eric Cole, the finish is a heartbreak. He played well enough to win on regulation and had the tournament in his sights. Ultimately, he could not match Henley's remarkable birdie run.

Mac Meissner, Alex Smalley, and defending champion Ben Griffin all finished in a share of third place, one shot behind the playoff duo.

What's Next

The PGA Tour moves directly from Colonial to Muirfield Village for The Memorial Tournament (June 4-7), where Scottie Scheffler will attempt to win for the third consecutive year. Henley will carry fresh confidence from Fort Worth into Jack Nicklaus' signature event. For Cole, the Memorial offers a quick opportunity to turn the page on Sunday's near-miss.
