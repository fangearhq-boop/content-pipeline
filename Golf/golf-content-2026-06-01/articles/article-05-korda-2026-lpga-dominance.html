Nelly Korda Is Running Away With 2026 — Three Wins, a Third Major, and the US Women's Open Ahead

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 1, 2026

Nelly Korda has made a habit of making the exceptional look routine. Through the first five months of 2026, she has won three times on the LPGA Tour, captured a third major title at the Chevron Championship, and pushed her career win total to 17. Now she heads into the US Women's Open at Riviera Country Club as the clear betting favorite and the player every other competitor has circled on their calendar.

At 27, Korda is not emerging — she is established. She is the benchmark.

The 2026 Numbers

Three wins in a season would be a career year for most LPGA players. For Korda, it is the continuation of a run that began in earnest in 2024 and has only gained momentum. Her victories in 2026 include the Chevron Championship, which counts as her third major title. That places her among the most decorated active major champions in the women's game.

Career win No. 17 came at age 27, a total that puts her on a trajectory that, if sustained, will make her one of the all-time greats in LPGA history. The conversation is not hypothetical anymore — it is already happening in real time among those who follow the women's game closely.

What Makes Her Different

Korda's dominance is not built on one exceptional skill. She drives it far and straight. She is elite with her irons. She putts at a level that rarely costs her when she gives herself reasonable looks. The combination produces a player who does not have obvious weaknesses for opponents to target, which makes game-planning against her genuinely difficult.

In recent seasons, she has also shown an ability to win in different ways — from the front with wire-to-wire leads, from behind with late charges, and in difficult conditions where mental strength often separates contenders from champions. That versatility is what separates good players from the ones who sustain careers at the top.

The US Women's Open Opportunity

The 81st US Women's Open Presented by Ally at Riviera Country Club represents Korda's next target. A fourth major title — and a first at the US Women's Open — would be a meaningful addition to a resume that is already among the best in women's golf today. The USGA's national championship carries a weight that distinguishes it from regular Tour wins, and Korda has demonstrated she performs at her best when the stakes are highest.

Riviera suits the kind of game she plays. It rewards controlled aggression, course management, and the ability to make birdies without forcing shots that lead to doubles. These are areas where Korda consistently excels.

The Bigger Picture

Korda's 2026 season is the kind that gets remembered. Three wins and a major before the US Women's Open has the feel of a player who has found a level she can sustain, not just visit. The players chasing her — including world No. 1 Atthaya Thitikul, who is still searching for her first major — know that the margin between winning and losing when Korda is in contention tends to be razor-thin.

What she has built in 2026 is not a hot streak. It is a sustained statement about where she stands in the current landscape of women's professional golf.
