US Women's Open Heads to Riviera for Historic First — Korda and Thitikul Set for Title Showdown

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 1, 2026

The 81st US Women's Open Presented by Ally will make history before a single shot is struck. When the field tees off Thursday at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California, it will mark the first time the USGA has ever held a women's major at one of the game's most storied venues. The significance of that moment is not lost on the players who earned their way into a 1,897-entry field that includes all 25 of the world's top 25 players.

The competition itself sets up as a compelling contest between two of the most dominant players in women's golf right now: Nelly Korda, who enters riding the best form of her career, and Atthaya Thitikul, the world's top-ranked player who is still searching for her first major title.

A Historic Venue

Riviera Country Club is known as "the Riviera of the West" and has a deep history with men's professional golf, including the Genesis Invitational on the PGA Tour. The course is known for its tight fairways, small greens, and the distinctive bunker in the middle of the par-3 sixth green that has confounded players for generations. Its placement of a USGA women's major represents a genuine expansion of where the elite women's game is played and seen.

The setup will challenge players who rely on distance alone. Riviera rewards shot-making, creativity around the greens, and the ability to manage par from difficult positions. It is a course that will produce a champion who earned every shot of their victory.

Korda's Moment

Nelly Korda arrives in Pacific Palisades with three wins in 2026, including the Chevron Championship — her third major title and 17th career LPGA victory. She has been the most dominant player in women's golf during this stretch and enters the US Women's Open as the player the field is chasing.

Three major titles before the US Women's Open places Korda in an elite tier, but winning this particular championship — the one that carries the weight of the USGA crest and a history stretching back decades — would add a dimension to her legacy that few can match. The US Women's Open has a way of defining careers, and Korda has positioned herself as the player best-suited to claim the title this week.

Thitikul's Pursuit

Atthaya Thitikul enters as the world's No. 1 ranked player and one of the most analytically impressive players in the women's game. Her driving accuracy, iron play, and putting statistics have placed her at the top of the rankings, and yet a major championship remains the missing piece.

Riviera, with its demand for precision over power, suits the kind of game Thitikul has built. If there is a week when she closes the gap between her ranking and her major haul, this may be it. The field she will need to beat is as strong as any assembled this year, but Thitikul's talent and preparation have never been in question.

The Competition

With all 25 of the world's top 25 players accepting entries — a sign of how much this event means to the women's game — the depth of competition is remarkable. The US Women's Open traditionally produces its own storylines independent of the season's prior results, and the combination of a historic venue and a concentrated elite field creates conditions for something memorable.

Four days of championship golf at Riviera begin Thursday. The sport will be paying attention.
