Niemann Makes LIV Golf History in Korea Playoff — 8th Title, Most All-Time

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 1, 2026

Joaquin Niemann walked off the 18th green at Asiad Country Club in Busan, South Korea, with the distinction that no other player in LIV Golf history has claimed: eight individual tournament victories, the most all-time, standing three wins clear of any other player in the league.

Niemann won LIV Golf Korea 2026 in a sudden-death playoff over Talor Gooch. Both players finished the 54-hole event at 12-under par before Niemann made the decisive birdie on the par-4 18th hole to claim the title and $4,000,000 of the $20,000,000 individual prize fund.

The Playoff

Gooch played exceptionally well all week and fully deserved to be in the playoff. His 12-under total would have won most LIV events in recent memory. But Niemann was better where it counted — when they walked back to the 18th tee for the playoff, the Chilean executed under pressure and sealed the win with a birdie.

DeChambeau finished T3 individually at 11-under, just outside the playoff conversation after a strong final round.

The Team Trophy

While Niemann and Gooch contested the individual title, Bryson DeChambeau's Crushers GC took home the team prize at -23 total, three shots clear of Gooch's Smash GC. DeChambeau's Crushers successfully defended the Korea team title they won in Incheon in 2025, making it back-to-back team victories at LIV's first Korean event.

Niemann's Place in LIV History

Niemann's eighth title puts him in a category of one in LIV Golf. He has been one of the most consistent players on the circuit since joining, but it is his ability to close in the moments that matter most that has defined his LIV career. Three clear of the next-closest competitor, he has established himself as LIV Golf's individual standard-bearer.

What's Next

LIV Golf continues its 2026 season schedule while the PGA Tour moves to The Memorial Tournament this week. Niemann's Korea win keeps him in strong position in the LIV individual standings heading into the second half of the season.
