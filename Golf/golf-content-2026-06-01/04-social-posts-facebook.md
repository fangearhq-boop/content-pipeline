### STORY 1: Schwab Challenge — Henley Wins

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM ET)**

Russell Henley did something special on Sunday at Colonial Country Club, and golf fans who turned off their TVs early nearly missed one of the year's great finishes.

Henley entered the back nine on Sunday trailing Eric Cole, who had played an excellent final round and appeared to have the Charles Schwab Challenge in hand. But Henley launched a birdie sequence that rewrote the afternoon entirely. He birdied No. 16. Then No. 17. Then No. 18 — erasing the deficit and forcing a sudden-death playoff with Cole.

In the playoff on the 18th hole, Henley made a fourth consecutive birdie to take the title. Four birdies in a row. $1,782,000. And a victory that will be remembered at Colonial for a long time.

Cole finishes T2 in one of the year's toughest heartbreaks. Mac Meissner, Alex Smalley, and defending champion Ben Griffin share T3.

### STORY 2: LIV Golf Korea — Niemann History

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM ET)**

Joaquin Niemann has done something no player has done in the history of LIV Golf — and he did it on the 18th hole at Asiad Country Club in Busan, South Korea.

With Talor Gooch matching him step for step through 54 holes at 12-under par, Niemann and Gooch went to sudden death on Sunday evening. On the par-4 18th, Niemann drained a birdie to walk away with the title. The win — his eighth in LIV Golf competition — makes him the most prolific individual winner in the league's history. He now stands three victories clear of any other player.

The team competition also produced a winner: Bryson DeChambeau's Crushers GC posted a combined 23-under to win the Korea team trophy by three shots. DeChambeau himself finished T3 individually.

For Gooch, a playoff loss is a bitter outcome after posting the kind of week that would win most tournaments. But Niemann — already a force before this — is now in the business of making LIV history.

### STORY 3: Memorial Tournament Preview

**Long-Form Post (10:30 AM ET)**

The golf world turns its attention to Columbus, Ohio, this week for The Memorial Tournament — one of the most prestigious non-major events on the PGA Tour calendar, and the one that comes with a $20 million purse.

Scottie Scheffler arrives as the defending champion — and as the man chasing history. He has won The Memorial in back-to-back years, becoming only the second player ever to win consecutive editions at Muirfield Village. The question this week is whether he can become the third player ever to win it three times in a row. The first was Tiger Woods, who completed the trifecta in 2001.

Nine of the world's top-10 players are in the field. Rory McIlroy and Cameron Young join Scheffler as projected favorites. Jack Nicklaus' tournament, at Jack Nicklaus' course, with one of the strongest fields in golf.

The Memorial runs June 4-7. Coverage on Golf Channel and NBC.

The field is extraordinary. All 25 of the world's top-25 players are entered. The USGA received 1,897 entries. And the headliner matchup everyone wants to see has set up perfectly.
