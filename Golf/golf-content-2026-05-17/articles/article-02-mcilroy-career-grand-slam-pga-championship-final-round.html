McIlroy Three Shots from Career Grand Slam After Brilliant Round 3 Charge

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 17, 2026

He has the Masters. He has the US Open. He has The Open Championship. On Sunday at Aronimink Golf Club, Rory McIlroy tees off three shots behind the lead with the final piece of the Career Grand Slam waiting for him.

McIlroy turned Saturday's third round of the 2026 PGA Championship into a statement. After entering the day outside the realistic conversation, he fired a 4-under 66 — six birdies, zero bogeys — to vault into a tie for seventh at -3, trailing 54-hole leader Alex Smalley by three shots. When his round ended, he offered a phrase that said everything: "I have a chance."

McIlroy tees off in the penultimate pairing at 2:05 PM ET alongside Xander Schauffele. Eighteen holes at a major championship stand between him and the most storied individual achievement in golf.

How Saturday Unfolded

McIlroy's round began steadily and built with conviction. He birdied the par-3 fifth and sixth holes, reaching the turn in three under par. The sixth hole produced the shot of his day: a towering draw from the tee that carried bunkers more than 315 yards out and settled on the green of the par-4 hole, setting up a tap-in birdie. He added further birdies at the 11th and 13th holes on the back nine to complete a card that contained not a single dropped shot.

It was the kind of ball-striking and mental clarity that McIlroy is capable of producing in the most consequential rounds of his career.

The Golf Channel noted the similarity to his 2025 Masters campaign. In that major, McIlroy found himself trailing significantly after 54 holes and staged a final-day comeback that brought him the green jacket — the title that had eluded him for years. The progression at Aronimink this week has echoed that pattern closely.

Three Shots. One More Major.

To complete the Career Grand Slam, McIlroy needs to add the PGA Championship to his collection. He has won three of the sport's four major championships, but this one — the Wanamaker Trophy — has been the gap in an otherwise historic résumé.

Three shots is bridgeable on any Sunday at a major, but the leaderboard's depth complicates the math. McIlroy won't simply need to play well. He will need the players ahead of him to stumble, or he will need to go significantly under par and trust that the field around him cannot match his score.

In the 2025 Masters, he did exactly that. He went low when it mattered and held on. The muscle memory of having done this once — of having made the comeback on the sport's biggest stage — is real. McIlroy knows now that he can produce when the stakes are at their highest.

The Final Pairing Ahead

Ahead of McIlroy's group, Alex Smalley and Matti Schmid tee off at 2:35 PM ET as the final pairing. Smalley is attempting to win his first PGA Tour event on the biggest stage possible. Schauffele, alongside McIlroy in the 2:05 group, is a major champion himself and a genuine threat to win from -3.

The final afternoon at Aronimink will include all of this simultaneously. It is a rare Sunday where the outcome will not be determined by one story but by several competing simultaneously.

What's Next

McIlroy tees off at 2:05 PM ET today at Aronimink Golf Club. Three shots to overcome. Eighteen holes to play. A Career Grand Slam to complete. Regardless of what happens, this Sunday at the 2026 PGA Championship will be one of golf's most compelling final rounds in years.
