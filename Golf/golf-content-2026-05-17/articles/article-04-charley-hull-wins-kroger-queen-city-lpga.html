Charley Hull Wins Kroger Queen City Championship at -20, First LPGA Title Since 2022

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 17, 2026

Charley Hull is an LPGA Tour champion again.

The English golfer claimed the 2026 Kroger Queen City Championship presented by P&G at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati, Ohio on Sunday, finishing at 20-under par to win by one stroke over world No. 1 contender Jeeno Thitikul. It is Hull's first victory on the LPGA Tour since the 2022 Ascendant LPGA — a four-year gap that ends with a title and a significant amount of relief.

How the Final Round Unfolded

Hull and Thitikul had been locked in a tight battle throughout the week at Maketewah Country Club. Thitikul — who had won the Mizuho Americas Open in New Jersey just the previous Sunday — arrived in Cincinnati looking to add a second consecutive LPGA title to her season. She pushed Hull hard and finished at -19, one shot back.

Hull held firm. Her ball-striking and course management throughout the tournament were the foundation of her victory. She reached -20 by the time the round concluded, and the one-shot margin told the story of how competitive the week had been. L. Woad rounded out the top three.

Hull's Return to the Winner's Circle

The gap since Hull's last LPGA win had grown long enough to generate questions. Since her 2022 victory at the Ascendant LPGA, she had remained a consistent and fan-favorite presence on tour — known for her personality and skill in equal measure — but the wins had not come. Sunday in Cincinnati changed that.

Hull is one of the most recognizable figures in women's golf, particularly in the United Kingdom, where her profile extends well beyond the sport's core audience. A win on the LPGA Tour — especially one that required coming down the stretch against Jeeno Thitikul, one of the best players in the world — is a significant result for the English star.

What's Next

The LPGA Tour continues with the ShopRite LPGA powered by Wakefern on May 29-31 at the Seaview Hotel's Bay Course in Galloway, New Jersey. For Hull, Sunday's victory at Cincinnati provides a platform heading into the summer stretch — and ends a drought that lasted the better part of four years. Congratulations to Charley Hull, the 2026 Kroger Queen City Championship champion.
