Zero Wins, Two-Shot Lead: Who Is Alex Smalley, the Man Atop the PGA Championship?

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 17, 2026

He is not a name that appears on the leaderboard of other tournaments. There is no major championship on his record and no PGA Tour victory in his biography. As of this Sunday morning, Alex Smalley is still searching for his first win as a professional golfer.

He also holds the 54-hole lead at the 2026 PGA Championship.

Smalley, 29, enters the final round at Aronimink Golf Club at -6 with a two-shot advantage over the field. He is only the 10th player in the last 30 years to reach the final day of a major with the lead and no professional victory on their résumé. The position he finds himself in is genuinely rare — and everything in his career up to this point was quietly building toward it.

The Background

Smalley grew up in Rochester, New York, and chose Duke University for his college education — where he studied Environmental Science, posted a 3.6 GPA, earned All-American honors, and won three collegiate events. He was selected for the 2019 USA Walker Cup team before turning professional that same year.

The path to the PGA Tour was through the Korn Ferry Tour, which Smalley navigated successfully enough to earn his card in 2021. Since then, he has maintained a consistent presence among the touring professionals — 15 top-10 finishes across five seasons, a record of making cuts and grinding out results, but never the crescendo of a win.

He has missed just one cut in 13 starts in 2026, arriving at the PGA Championship in the form of a player who belongs at this level. He just had not yet found the moment to prove it on the grandest stage.

How He Got Here

Saturday's third round could have gone very differently. Smalley bogeyed three of his first four holes, seemingly handing the tournament to the field behind him. It would have been easy, and understandable, to let the week unravel from there.

Instead, Smalley steadied himself. He played the middle of his round at level par, then birdied four of his final six holes to post a 68 — extending his lead rather than surrendering it. By the time he walked off the 18th green, he had done something even he had not done before: put himself in position to win the most important golf tournament he will likely ever play.

Golf Digest described it simply: Smalley is "tougher than you think."

What's Next

Smalley tees off in the final pairing at 2:35 PM ET alongside Matti Schmid. He will not have the benefit of knowing exactly where he stands relative to whatever number Scheffler, McIlroy, or Schauffele may post ahead of him. He will have to play his own game and trust that 18 holes of consistent golf is enough to win. If he does — if Alex Smalley wins the 2026 PGA Championship — it will stand as one of the most striking debut major victories in the modern era.
