PGA Championship Final Round Preview: Smalley Leads By Two in Historically Bunched Field

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 17, 2026

The 108th PGA Championship has arrived at its most dramatic moment. Twenty-two players sit within four shots of the lead heading into Sunday's final round at Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania. Whoever holds the Wanamaker Trophy at the end of the afternoon will have earned it in one of the most chaotic major finishes in recent years.

Alex Smalley, a 29-year-old without a single PGA Tour win to his name, holds the 54-hole lead at 6-under par — two strokes ahead of a five-way pack at -4 (Matti Schmid, Nick Taylor, Jon Rahm, Aaron Rai, Ludvig Åberg). Three more shots back at -3 are Rory McIlroy, Xander Schauffele, Maverick McNealy, and Patrick Reed. Defending champion Scottie Scheffler is at -1, five behind.

It is not a leaderboard built for favorites. It is a leaderboard built for drama.

The Final Round Tee Sheet

The morning wave gets underway early, with Scheffler — paired with LIV Golf's David Puig — off at 12:05 PM ET. The defending champion's early tee time is a double-edged proposition: he can set a target number that hangs over the afternoon field, but he must wait and watch if the late wave produces fireworks.

The penultimate pairing of McIlroy and Schauffele tees off at 2:05 PM ET. McIlroy carries the weight of a Career Grand Slam chase into his round; Schauffele is a major champion searching for a second title. Both men have the pedigree to win from three back on a Sunday at a major.

The final pairing sends Smalley and Matti Schmid onto the first tee at 2:35 PM ET. All the pressure, all the spotlight, and all of Aronimink's late-afternoon challenge will fall on Smalley's shoulders as the last group in a major championship.

Why the Leaderboard Is So Compressed

Aronimink Golf Club proved throughout this week's tournament that low scoring is available to those who find the fairways and manage their approach angles — but not to those who stray. The course has been demanding without being punishing to the point of discouraging birdies. The result is a field bunched within a range that makes the entire top 20 or so relevant heading into Sunday.

Moving Day on Saturday delivered its share of charges and stumbles. McIlroy fired a 66 with six birdies to surge from the edge of contention. Jon Rahm and Ludvig Åberg moved into the upper tier. Smalley wobbled early before recovering with four birdies in his final six holes to shoot 68 and maintain his lead.

The conditions Sunday — forecast for mid-May Pennsylvania, potentially breezy — could either compress scores further or separate the field quickly depending on what Aronimink's superintendent and setup crew present.

The Key Questions

Can Smalley hold his nerve in his first final-round major pairing? Can McIlroy channel his 2025 Masters magic into the history books? Can Scheffler manufacture a major comeback from five back with an early tee time? Can Rahm, Schauffele, or any of the other contenders steal a championship that seems wide open to anyone brave enough to take it?

Every one of those questions has a plausible answer of yes. That is what makes final Sunday at a major so compelling when the leaderboard looks like this one.

What's Next

The final round of the 2026 PGA Championship is underway now at Aronimink Golf Club. Coverage begins Sunday morning on ESPN and TNT, transitioning to CBS in the afternoon. The final pairing tees off at 2:35 PM ET. The Wanamaker Trophy will be awarded today. By Sunday evening, we'll know if Smalley delivered, if McIlroy completed the Grand Slam, or if someone else entirely seized the moment.
