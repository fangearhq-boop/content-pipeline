### STORY 3: Alex Smalley Profile

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Most golf fans are asking the same question this Sunday morning: who is Alex Smalley?

Here's what you need to know about the man leading the 2026 PGA Championship.

Smalley is 29 years old, from Rochester, New York. He studied Environmental Science at Duke University, where he posted a 3.6 GPA while earning All-American honors and winning three collegiate events. He was a member of the 2019 USA Walker Cup team.

He turned professional that same year and earned his PGA Tour card through the Korn Ferry Tour in 2021. In five seasons as a touring professional, he has posted 15 top-10 finishes. He has never won.

Not once.

On Saturday, he bogeyed three of his first four holes and looked as though the pressure of leading a major might be getting to him. Instead, he fought back — birdying four of his final six holes to card a 68 and extend his lead to two shots.

He enters Sunday as only the 10th player in the last 30 years to take the 54-hole lead at a major without a single PGA Tour win to their name. Those who held this position before him met mixed fates. A few won. Many didn't.

Today, Smalley gets his shot at the biggest stage the sport can offer.
