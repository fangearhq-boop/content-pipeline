What's Next in Golf: LIV Virginia, PGA Tour Calendar and Korda's Ongoing LPGA Dominance

By Jake Torres | April 27, 2026

The golf world took a breath on Sunday night. Nelly Korda claimed her third major with a five-shot wire-to-wire win at the Chevron Championship. The Fitzpatrick brothers completed one of the most dramatic tournament finishes of the season to win the Zurich Classic. It was a weekend that delivered everything.

Now the calendar rolls forward — and the next chapters are already lined up.

LIV Golf Virginia — May 8-10

The LIV Golf circuit returns to the Washington D.C. area for the Virginia event, scheduled for May 8-10 at Trump National Golf Club Washington D.C. in Sterling, Virginia. It is one of the most anticipated stops on the LIV calendar, and 2026 brings a competitive landscape worth watching closely.

Jon Rahm arrives in Virginia as one of the hottest players in the field, having delivered a dominant six-shot victory in Mexico City at Club de Golf Chapultepec with his Legion XIII team. That performance extended Legion XIII's winning streak and re-established Rahm as the LIV individual title favorite.

Joaquin Niemann looks to defend his individual title from last year's Virginia event. The Crushers GC will attempt to defend their team championship from 2025. And Ripper GC leads the 2026 team standings with 89.75 points, with 4Aces GC close behind at 88 — meaning the team championship race remains competitive heading into Virginia.

PGA Tour — Cadillac Championship Next

Following the Zurich Classic's conclusion in New Orleans, the PGA Tour moves on to the Cadillac Championship. The tour continues its spring swing through some of the most demanding events on the calendar, with signature events and major championship exemptions on the line for players fighting for position.

The Fitzpatrick brothers' double-act has injected energy into the tour narrative heading into the next round of events. Matt arrives as a back-to-back winner — the RBC Heritage last week and the Zurich this week. Alex arrives as a fully-exempt PGA Tour member for the first time, ready to take on the Cadillac Championship field with his newly earned card.

LPGA — Korda and the Major Season Ahead

Nelly Korda returns to world No. 1 with three majors in her career and two LPGA Tour victories already in 2026. The women's major season continues through the summer with more opportunities for Korda to extend her legacy — and for the field to figure out how to contain a player who just dominated Memorial Park from wire to wire while setting a 36-hole scoring record.

The short answer: they haven't figured it out yet. Korda looks more complete now than at any previous point in her career.

What's Next

LIV Virginia kicks off May 8. The PGA Tour rolls on with the Cadillac Championship. The LPGA season continues with Korda at the top. A weekend that delivered two major stories now gives way to a week that already promises more. Golf fans have plenty to watch — and plenty to look forward to.
