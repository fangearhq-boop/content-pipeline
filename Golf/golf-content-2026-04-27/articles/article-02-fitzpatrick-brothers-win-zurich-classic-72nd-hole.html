Fitzpatrick Brothers Win Zurich Classic on 72nd Hole Bunker Shot After Squandering Four-Shot Lead

By Jake Torres | April 27, 2026

Everything was going wrong. The Fitzpatrick brothers had entered Sunday's final round at TPC Louisiana with a four-shot lead. Then the back nine happened. The lead evaporated. A double bogey on the 12th hole — Matt's drive going right, Alex hitting a tree with his punch-out attempt, the pair unable to get up and down from 88 yards in the fairway — turned a comfortable advantage into a scramble.

Then Matt Fitzpatrick hit the shot of the tournament.

Standing over a bunker shot in front of the 18th green on the par-5 final hole, with Alex having found the sand with their second shot and the entire 2026 Zurich Classic hanging in the balance, Matt blasted the ball to within 14 inches of the hole. Alex stepped up and tapped in for birdie. Fitzpatrick brothers: 31-under 257. Good enough to win by one over two teams, and good enough to earn Alex a two-year PGA Tour exemption.

A Tournament-Record Final Total

The drama of Sunday's back nine tends to overshadow how brilliant this pair was over four rounds. Their 31-under 257 total is a tournament record at TPC Louisiana, capping a week in which they set the 54-hole scoring record with a remarkable 15-under 57 in Thursday's fourball round. That R3 performance was one of the great team rounds in Zurich Classic history.

The teams they edged in the final standings were Americans Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer, and Norwegians Kristoffer Reitan and Kris Ventura. Both finished at 30-under — one stroke behind the Fitzpatricks' winning birdie.

The Prize — For Matt and Alex Both

The Fitzpatrick brothers earned $2,745,500 as a team, with each player receiving $1,372,750 in prize money. For Matt — currently ranked world No. 3 and a former U.S. Open champion — it was another signature result in a career full of them, coming just a week after his victory at the RBC Heritage.

But for Alex, the prize transcended the check. Competing on a sponsor exemption at TPC Louisiana, Alex walked out of New Orleans with a two-year PGA Tour exemption through 2028, spots in the next four Signature Events, a berth in the PGA Championship and an invitation to the 2027 Players Championship. His professional life changed on that bunker shot.

What's Next

The PGA Tour heads toward the Cadillac Championship next on the calendar. Matt Fitzpatrick has now won back-to-back events in the most memorable fashion, cementing his status as one of the most formidable players in the world. Alex begins his new life as a fully-fledged PGA Tour member. For a pair of brothers from Sheffield, England, New Orleans in late April will be a city they remember forever.
