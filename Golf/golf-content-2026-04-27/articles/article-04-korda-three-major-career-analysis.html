Three Majors, Three Chapters: Inside Nelly Korda's Historic Chevron Championship Week

By Ryan Calloway | April 27, 2026

The number that defines Nelly Korda's 2026 Chevron Championship is 130. That's the 36-hole total she compiled after opening rounds of 65 and 65 at Memorial Park Golf Club in Houston — the lowest opening 36 holes in LPGA major championship history, excluding the Amundi Evian Championship. It set the tone for a week that ended with a wire-to-wire five-shot victory and the world No. 1 ranking returned to her name.

The Architecture of Dominance

Korda's 65-65 opening was the kind of scoring that removes an entire field from relevance. By the time she arrived at the weekend holding a comfortable lead, the tournament had effectively narrowed to a single question: how many shots would she win by?

The answer, after matching 70s on Saturday and Sunday, was five. Yin Ruoning and Patty Tavatanakit finished tied for second. Korda was bogey-free through the first two rounds, controlled and patient through the weekend, and never remotely threatened across all four days at Memorial Park.

Wire-to-wire victories in major championships don't happen often. Doing it while setting a 36-hole scoring record is the kind of performance that defines a player's legacy.

Three Majors, Three Different Moments

Each of Korda's three major titles reflects a different moment in her development as a player. The 2021 Chevron Championship was a breakthrough, the announcement of a talent that had arrived at the top of women's professional golf. The 2024 U.S. Women's Open was a confirmation — proof that she could win the most prestigious event in the women's game under pressure in a difficult environment.

The 2026 Chevron Championship is something else: a statement of total mastery. Coming back to the site of her first major and doing it even better — with a historic 36-hole record and a five-shot final margin — is the performance of an elite champion at peak confidence.

She is now a three-time major winner at 27, with 17 career LPGA titles and her second win of 2026 already in the books. The next time the LPGA field gathers, they will be chasing a world No. 1 who just reminded everyone why she holds that position.

What's Next

Korda leaves Houston with the world No. 1 ranking reclaimed from Jeeno Thitikul, a winner's check of $1.35 million, and a major season that is very much open in front of her. The women's major schedule continues through the summer, and Korda heads into the rest of 2026 as the undisputed favorite to add more hardware. After the performance at Memorial Park, that prediction is impossible to argue with.
