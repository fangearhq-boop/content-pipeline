Korda Wins Chevron Championship in Wire-to-Wire Demolition, Claims Third Major and World No. 1

By Ryan Calloway | April 27, 2026

Nelly Korda jumped in the lake. It was the only appropriate response to what she had just accomplished.

Korda closed with a 2-under 70 at Memorial Park Golf Club on Sunday to win the 2026 Chevron Championship at 18-under 270, a five-shot victory over Yin Ruoning and Patty Tavatanakit. The win gave her a third major championship, a second Chevron title and the world No. 1 ranking back in her hands — all delivered with the kind of effortless dominance that made the outcome feel inevitable from the opening tee shot of round one.

A Record-Setting Masterpiece

The foundation of Korda's week was laid on Thursday and Friday. Back-to-back 65s gave her a 36-hole total of 130 — the lowest opening 36 holes in LPGA major championship history, excluding the Amundi Evian Championship. She entered the weekend with a cushion she never relinquished.

Saturday and Sunday brought rounds of 70-70, which were enough to extend the lead and seal the championship by five shots. Korda was bogey-free through the first two rounds and played controlled, composed golf down the stretch as the contenders fell further and further behind.

The final margin of five shots — over Yin and Tavatanakit tied for second — understated how complete the performance was. Korda never trailed, never truly scrambled, and never appeared remotely threatened.

Third Major, Three Different Chapters

Korda's three major championships span meaningfully different points in her career. Her first Chevron Championship win in 2021 announced her arrival at the top of the women's game. The 2024 U.S. Women's Open was a confirmation of elite staying power. This 2026 Chevron title — claimed in wire-to-wire dominant fashion with a record-setting opening 36 holes — is the statement of a player at the peak of her powers.

She cashed a winner's check of $1.35 million from the $9 million purse, bringing her to 17 career LPGA titles and marking her second LPGA Tour victory of 2026.

"I just really locked in," Korda said after the round.

Retaking the No. 1 Ranking

With the victory, Korda reclaims the world No. 1 ranking from Jeeno Thitikul — a position she had not held since August 2025. It is a return to the top of the world rankings that no one who watched her this week would find surprising.

The LPGA's marquee player has reasserted herself at Memorial Park in emphatic fashion. The 2026 major season has its first champion — and the field that follows will be chasing a player who just set an LPGA major record and won by five.

What's Next

Korda departs Houston as the world No. 1, a three-time major winner, and the player to beat for the remainder of the 2026 major season. The LPGA calendar continues with more events ahead, and the women's game now has its defining figure back at the summit. For anyone hoping to win a major in 2026, the Chevron Championship just illustrated what they're dealing with.
