Korda Wins Chevron Championship — 3rd Major, Retakes World No. 1 (Tier 1)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET Nelly Korda walked off Memorial Park Golf Club on Sunday with her third major championship — and the world No. 1 ranking back in her hands. Korda closed with a 2-under 70 to win the 2026 Chevron Championship at 18-under 270, a five-shot victory over Yin Ruoning and Patty Tavatanakit. It was a wire-to-wire dominant performance that never really felt in doubt after the opening two rounds. The headline stat from Korda's week: she opened with back-to-back 65s on Thursday and Friday, giving her a 36-hole total of 130 — the lowest opening 36 holes in LPGA major championship history (excluding the Amundi Evian Championship). That kind of early dominance made the final round feel more like a procession than a contest. Korda added rounds of 70-70 over the weekend to hold on comfortably. She cashed a winner's check of $1.35 million from the $9 million purse and claimed her 17th LPGA Tour title — including her second LPGA victory of 2026. With the victory, Korda retakes the world No. 1 ranking from Jeeno Thitikul for the first time since August 2025. Her major résumé now reads: Chevron Championship 2021, U.S. Women's Open 2024, Chevron Championship 2026. She jumped in the lake after winning. The celebration was everything. #GolfFanrecap | #LPGA | #ChevronChampionship

### Long-Form Post Window: 1:00 PM ET They squandered a four-shot lead. They made double bogey at the 12th hole after Matt drove right and Alex hit a tree with his punch-out attempt. They were rattled, the lead was gone, and two other teams were right behind them. Then Matt Fitzpatrick hit one of the clutch shots of the season. On the par-5 18th at TPC Louisiana, his brother Alex found a bunker in front of the green. Matt stepped in, blasted the bunker shot to within 14 inches of the hole, and Alex tapped in for the winning birdie. Fitzpatrick brothers win the 2026 Zurich Classic at a tournament-record 31-under 257 — one stroke over two teams: Americans Alex Smalley and Hayden Springer, and Norwegians Kristoffer Reitan and Kris Ventura. The prize was significant: $2,745,500 as a team, with each brother taking home $1,372,750. But the bigger prize for Alex was the PGA Tour card that came with the victory — a two-year exemption through 2028 that changes his professional life entirely. This was a tournament where the Fitzpatricks set a 54-hole scoring record (R3 was a tournament-record 15-under 57 in fourball), lost control on the back nine Sunday, and then dug deep when it mattered most. That's why majors are decided on Sundays. #GolfFanrecap | #PGATour | #ZurichClassic

### Long-Form Post Window: 8:30 AM ET Six months ago, Alex Fitzpatrick's professional career was in a precarious place. The younger Fitzpatrick brother had slipped outside the top 280 on the DP World Tour by early October after six missed cuts in nine starts. He was on the verge of losing his card entirely. He fought back, climbed to seventh in the Race to Dubai standings — close enough that a top-10 finish at year's end would earn him PGA Tour status for the following season. Then he got a sponsor exemption into the 2026 Zurich Classic of New Orleans, partnered with his older brother Matt, and played alongside one of the best golfers on the planet for four rounds at TPC Louisiana. On Sunday, he tapped in a 14-inch putt on the 72nd hole to complete what Matt's stunning bunker shot had set up — and Alex Fitzpatrick left New Orleans as a PGA Tour winner with a two-year exemption through 2028. The victory earns him entry into the next four Signature Events, a berth in the PGA Championship and an invitation to the 2027 Players Championship. He went from a sponsor exemption to fully exempt in the time it takes Matt Fitzpatrick to play a bunker shot. Alex is a Wake Forest University alumnus. His older brother Matt is a former U.S. Open champion and current world No. 3. The dynamic between them — big brother providing the bunker shot of his life to change little brother's career — is one of the best golf stories of 2026. #GolfFanrecap | #PGATour | #ZurichClassic

Her three majors now span different chapters of her career: the 2021 Chevron Championship, the 2024 U.S. Women's Open and now the 2026 Chevron Championship again. She won the Chevron once as a rising star and has now returned five years later as one of the defining figures of women's professional golf. The world No. 1 ranking she reclaims — for the first time since August 2025 — reflects the full picture: Korda is at the top of her game, and her week in Houston was the most convincing possible statement of that fact.
