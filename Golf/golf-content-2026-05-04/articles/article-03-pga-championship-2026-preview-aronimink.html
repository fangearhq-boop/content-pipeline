PGA Championship 2026: Aronimink Awaits, Scheffler Defends, McIlroy Hunts Another Major

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | May 4, 2026

NEWTOWN SQUARE, Pa. — One of golf's four majors returns to a classic Pennsylvania venue for the first time in more than six decades, and the field heading to Aronimink Golf Club for the 108th PGA Championship is as compelling as it has been in years.

The 2026 PGA Championship runs May 14-17 at Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania — a suburb of Philadelphia that will host a major for only the second time since the course last appeared on the PGA Championship rotation in 1962. More than 60 years of development, maintenance, and refinement later, Aronimink is widely regarded as one of the finest club facilities in American golf, and the course's blend of precision demands and classic design makes it an ideal venue for the second major of the year.

Scottie Scheffler arrives as the defending champion, having won last year's PGA Championship by five strokes at Quail Hollow Club. He enters as the clear betting favorite at around +350, a reflection of his status as the world No. 1 and the most consistent performer in the game over the past several years. His runner-up finish this week at the Cadillac Championship at Doral shows he is never far from the top of the leaderboard, even when he does not win.

McIlroy Arrives With Momentum

The second name on the betting board is Rory McIlroy at approximately +650, and his recent form makes that price look attractive. McIlroy became the fourth player in Masters Tournament history to defend his title at Augusta National this spring — a win that placed him firmly in the conversation as one of the greatest major champions of his generation. He enters the PGA Championship playing some of the best golf of his career and carries the confidence of someone who has conquered the game's hardest tournament two years in a row.

Bryson DeChambeau (+1000) and Jon Rahm (+1400) round out the early marquee names on the odds board. Both play on the LIV Golf circuit but remain eligible for major championships, and both have the power and creativity to navigate Aronimink's demands.

About Aronimink

The course design at Aronimink is a Donald Ross original, and it rewards the qualities Ross favored: precision off the tee, strategic approach play, and touch around the greens. Long hitters will find opportunities, but the rolling fairways and well-positioned bunkers mean that driving accuracy matters more here than at some of the power courses on the modern major rotation. Green complexes are firm and fast, placing a premium on approach shot control.

A major at a Ross design in the Philadelphia suburbs is a throwback to golf's classical traditions. The crowd support for McIlroy — popular wherever he goes — and the Philly-area atmosphere for Scheffler and other American contenders should make for an electric week.

What's Next

Before Aronimink, the PGA Tour plays the Truist Championship at Quail Hollow Club this week (May 6-10), giving several major contenders a final tune-up before the championship. Practice rounds at Aronimink will begin in the days leading up to the first round. Keep an eye on the field announcements and early injury reports — in a major championship week, every piece of news matters.
