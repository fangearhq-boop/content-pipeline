Wire-to-Wire and Six Clear: Cameron Young Dominates the Cadillac Championship for Second Win of 2026

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 4, 2026

MIAMI — Cameron Young arrived at Trump National Doral with confidence and left with a trophy, a check for $3.6 million, and a dominant performance that no one in the field could match.

Young claimed the 2026 Cadillac Championship with a final score of 19-under par 269, completing a wire-to-wire victory by six strokes over Scottie Scheffler. It was the second win of his 2026 season and perhaps the most authoritative of his career — leading by one after 18 holes, five after 36, and six entering the final round, with never a moment when the outcome seemed genuinely in doubt.

The final round at the Blue Monster was complicated by inclement weather that prompted officials to move tee times up earlier in the day. Young handled the conditions calmly, closing out his round to reach 19-under while Scheffler — the world No. 1 and defending Masters champion — could only manage to finish at 13-under.

Ben Griffin finished alone in third at 12-under, while Si Woo Kim, Sepp Straka, and Adam Scott shared fourth place at 11-under. Scott had one of the rounds of the week in the third round with a bogey-free 66, but it was not enough to bridge the gap Young had established.

Young's Statement Year

The Cadillac Championship is Young's second victory of 2026, cementing his status as one of the most improved players on the PGA Tour in recent years. The 27-year-old from New York has long been projected as a future winner — his ball-striking has always been elite, and his length off the tee ranks among the tour's best. What has changed in 2026 is the putting and the closing. When the tournament is on the line, Young has found a way to make the shots count.

Two wins in four months is a pace of success that very few players achieve. With the FedExCup season in full swing and the major championship season picking up next week, Young heads to the Truist Championship at Quail Hollow and then the PGA Championship at Aronimink with momentum that could make him a contender at either.

What's Next

The PGA Tour moves to Quail Hollow Club in Charlotte for the Truist Championship, which begins Thursday, May 7. Young is scheduled to be in the field. The following week, the second major of the year — the 108th PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania — begins on May 14. For a player riding this kind of form, the timing could not be better.
