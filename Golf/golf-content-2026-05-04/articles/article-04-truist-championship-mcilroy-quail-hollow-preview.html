McIlroy Returns to Quail Hollow Seeking an Unprecedented Fifth Truist Championship Title

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | May 4, 2026

CHARLOTTE, N.C. — Rory McIlroy has won at Quail Hollow Club more than any player in the history of the event. On Thursday, he gets another chance to add to that record.

McIlroy has committed to play in the 2026 Truist Championship at Quail Hollow Club in Charlotte, North Carolina — a Signature Event that begins Thursday and runs through Sunday, May 10. His four previous wins at Quail Hollow came in 2010, 2015, 2021, and 2024. A fifth would be unprecedented at any event on the PGA Tour schedule.

The timing of this week is significant in two ways. First, the Truist Championship is the final Signature Event before the PGA Championship, which begins the following Thursday at Aronimink Golf Club. A win at Quail Hollow would send McIlroy into the second major of the year with maximum confidence. Second, McIlroy is coming off defending his Masters title in April — placing him in rarified air as one of just four players ever to successfully defend at Augusta National.

The Field at Quail Hollow

The strength of the Truist Championship field is formidable. Cameron Young — who won the Cadillac Championship wire-to-wire at Doral just days ago — is in the field and arrives as one of the hottest players in the world. Past Truist champions competing this week include Marc Homa (2019, 2022), Jason Day (2018), Brian Harman (2017), Rickie Fowler (2012), and Lucas Glover (2011). A $20 million purse and 700 FedExCup points ensure the field takes this event seriously even with a major the following week.

Quail Hollow's setup favors players who can handle tight tee-shot corridors lined with tall trees, followed by aggressive approach shots into greens protected by water on multiple holes. The Green Mile — the 16th, 17th, and 18th holes — is among the most demanding closing stretches on the PGA Tour. McIlroy has negotiated it successfully four times. The question on Sunday will be whether he can do it for a fifth.

What's Next

The Truist Championship runs May 6-10 at Quail Hollow Club. Television coverage begins Thursday on Golf Channel and NBC. The winner earns $3.6 million and a significant FedExCup points boost heading into the summer stretch. Following Quail Hollow, the entire field's attention turns to Aronimink for the PGA Championship. For McIlroy, winning both would be the ultimate preparation for a summer he clearly intends to dominate.
