Korda Claims Third LPGA Win of 2026 at Riviera Maya Open — Historic Consistency Continues

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 4, 2026

PLAYA DEL CARMEN, Mexico — The most dominant golfer on the LPGA Tour in 2026 has added another chapter to a season that is beginning to look historically remarkable.

Nelly Korda won the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba on Sunday, closing at 17-under par for a four-shot victory over Arpichaya Yubol. The American star's third LPGA Tour title of the year came at the El Camaleon Golf Course in Playa del Carmen, Mexico, with a performance that matched the relentless level she has maintained since the season began.

Arpichaya Yubol of Thailand finished second at 13-under, while China's Yu Liu was third at 12-under. Patty Tavatanakit rounded out the top four at 10-under. Korda's margin of four shots did not tell the full story of her control — she was the clear best player in the field for the better part of the week.

A Season Unlike Any Other

The number that defines Korda's 2026 is not wins, though three of them in six starts is extraordinary by any measure. It is the floor. Korda has not finished outside the top two in any of her six events this season. Three wins, three runner-up finishes, with no result worse than second across the entire opening stretch of the year.

According to Sky Sports, that level of sustained excellence ties Korda with Annika Sorenstam as the only women to ever achieve that consistency across the opening stretch of an LPGA season. Sorenstam's 2001 and 2002 seasons — among the greatest in the history of women's professional golf — are the reference points. That Korda's name belongs in that conversation says everything about what she has been doing in 2026.

It is worth noting that this consistency is not happening in a weak field environment. The LPGA Tour regularly features players from across the globe competing at an elite level. Korda is winning and placing at the top despite the full strength of the international tour showing up each week.

What's Next

The LPGA Tour continues next week with the Mizuho Americas Open in New Jersey. Korda's schedule for that event was not confirmed at the time of publication, but wherever she tees it up next, she arrives as the unambiguous favorite. Her 2026 season has not yet produced a bad week — and there is no indication that is about to change.
