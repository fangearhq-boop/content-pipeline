LIV Golf Comes to Washington DC: Can Jon Rahm Win Back-to-Back at Trump National?

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | May 4, 2026

STERLING, Va. — One of the busiest weeks in professional golf continues Thursday when LIV Golf arrives at Trump National Golf Club in the Washington DC area for its latest event in the 2026 season.

The tournament runs May 7-10 at the Nicklaus-designed Trump National Golf Club Washington DC in Sterling, Virginia, offering LIV Golf's signature blend of 54-hole individual and team competition. The event represents the tour's latest stop in a 2026 schedule that has taken the circuit across multiple continents, building toward the season's conclusion later in the year.

The most compelling storyline heading into this week centers on Jon Rahm. The Spaniard won the most recent LIV Golf event — in Mexico City — at a dominant 21-under par, separating himself by six strokes from a competitive field. Rahm has been one of the most successful players on the LIV circuit since joining, and his form heading into the DC event is as sharp as it has been at any point this season.

The LIV DC Backdrop

Washington DC area golf fans have embraced LIV Golf events with enthusiasm, and Trump National's setting in Northern Virginia — just miles from the Capitol — gives the event a unique backdrop that few other stops on any tour can match. The 18-hole layout at Trump National is built on rolling terrain with significant elevation changes, water features, and a design that rewards creativity as well as power.

The timing of the LIV DC event is notable: it runs concurrently with the PGA Tour's Truist Championship at Quail Hollow and ends just days before the PGA Championship begins at Aronimink. For LIV Golf players who are eligible to compete in majors — including Rahm, Dustin Johnson, Patrick Reed, and others — the DC event serves as a final competitive tune-up before the second major of the year.

What's Next
