### Long-Form Post The second major of the golf year is arriving quickly — and the 108th PGA Championship has the kind of storyline package that should make it unmissable viewing. The championship runs May 14-17 at Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania, a suburb of Philadelphia. It will be only the second time Aronimink has hosted the PGA Championship — the first was 1962, more than 60 years ago. The course rewards precision over power, featuring rolling fairways, strategic bunkering, and green complexes that test every aspect of a player's game. Scottie Scheffler arrives as the defending champion, having won last year's PGA Championship by five strokes. He enters as the favorite at roughly +350 and has shown no signs of slowing down — even a second-place finish this week at Doral demonstrates the world No. 1's consistency. Rory McIlroy is second in the odds at around +650, and his recent form makes that feel conservative. McIlroy became the fourth player in Masters history to defend his title earlier this spring, and he enters Aronimink with the kind of confidence that comes from winning majors in back-to-back years. Bryson DeChambeau (+1000) and Jon Rahm (+1400) round out the early favorites. But a course that rewards precision over distance could bring other players into the conversation. ### Image Post Caption 108 years of PGA Championship history leads to this: Aronimink Golf Club, May 14-17.
