Nelly Korda Misses Cut at Evian — Triple-Major Bid Is Over, Lottie Woad Takes the Lead at 11-Under

By Marcus Cole | July 11, 2026

LPGA Major

Nelly Korda's bid for three consecutive major championships ended with a missed cut in France.

The world No. 1 in women's golf shot 74 in Round 1 of the Amundi Evian Championship, recovered with 69 on Friday, and finished at 1-over for the tournament — one stroke outside the cut line at Evian-les-Bains. It was Korda's first missed cut since the 2024 Women's PGA Championship, ending her best stretch of major championship golf since the LPGA Tour began keeping comprehensive records.

In the tournament Korda vacated at the cut, England's Lottie Woad leads at 11-under entering Saturday's third round, with Japan's Aki Iwai one stroke back at 10-under.

What Korda Was Chasing

The 2026 season had been historic for Korda before this week. She won the Women's Masters in April and the U.S. Women's Open in June — back-to-back major titles that put her in pursuit of a feat accomplished only once in women's golf history. Had she won the Evian Championship, Korda would have claimed her third consecutive major of the calendar year and her fifth major overall.

Her opening 74 on Thursday immediately complicated the path. Evian's course played difficult in the first round, but the world No. 1 needed to be under par to stay in contention. Her Friday 69 — six shots better — showed the capability that has made her the tour's dominant player. It wasn't enough. The numbers combined to 1-over, and the cut came at even par.

Korda will now look toward the AIG Women's Open at Royal Lytham and St Annes on July 30, which represents the fifth and final women's major of 2026 and another opportunity at history.

Woad Seizes the Moment

If Korda's exit created an opening, Lottie Woad ran through it at full speed.

The English professional posted a 7-under 64 on Friday — eight birdies against one bogey — to move to 11-under and take the solo lead entering Saturday. The performance was one of the more impressive single rounds in recent major championship history: sustained precision on a venue that rewards nothing less, against a world-class field, in front of a European gallery that was watching Korda's exit unfold in real time.

Woad had opened the tournament with a 67. Her Friday 64 wasn't a response to anything — it was simply exceptional golf. For those unfamiliar with her name, that will begin to change after this week. An LPGA major title at Evian would represent a significant breakthrough for English women's golf.

Aki Iwai's Story

Japan's Aki Iwai sits one stroke behind Woad at 10-under, having opened the tournament with an 8-under 63 to seize the first-round lead. The Japanese player has been one of the tour's quiet performers this season, and the Evian leaderboard represents her highest profile on a major stage.

The two-player story at the top — an English player and a Japanese player, separated by one stroke, with the dominant world No. 1 watching from home — gives the 2026 Evian Championship a refreshingly unpredictable quality heading into the weekend. The $9.1 million purse and the major championship status will focus both players.

What's Next

Round 3 is underway at the Evian Resort Golf Club in Evian-les-Bains. Saturday will set up Sunday's final round, which determines the fourth women's major champion of 2026.

Woad leads. Iwai is right behind. And the rest of the field — several players remain within striking distance at 7-under and below — will have their say through Saturday's round. The tournament concludes Sunday. A new major champion will be crowned in France.
