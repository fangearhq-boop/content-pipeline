Open Championship Preview: The 154th Major Returns to Royal Birkdale — McIlroy Has Momentum, Scheffler Needs a Reset

By Jake Torres | July 11, 2026

Open Championship

The final men's major championship of 2026 begins Thursday.

The 154th Open Championship arrives at Royal Birkdale in Southport, England on July 16-19, returning to a venue not seen on the rota since Jordan Spieth's memorable — and occasionally chaotic — victory in 2017. A full field of 156 players will compete for the Claret Jug over four rounds of links golf at one of England's finest seaside courses.

Royal Birkdale in 2026

Royal Birkdale has been updated since 2017. Architects Tom Mackenzie and Mike Howard of the firm Mackenzie and Ebert made several structural changes: the former par-3 14th was removed from the routing; the par-5 15th was relocated and renumbered as the 14th; and a new par-3 15th was inserted to replace it. The result is a course that retains Birkdale's fundamental identity — holes routing through valleys in the natural dune system, with subtle fairway undulation that rewards precise positioning — while presenting fresh challenges to players who prepared extensively on the 2017 layout.

Birkdale is generally considered one of the more accessible links courses on the Open rota, in the sense that its greens are larger than some venues and the fairway corridors, while demanding, are not as punishing as Carnoustie or Muirfield. Accurate approach play and consistently good putting are the primary differentiators here. Players who gain strokes on approach and on the greens will lead this leaderboard.

McIlroy's Moment

No player enters The Open with more momentum than Rory McIlroy. He won the 2026 Masters in April — his first major in over a decade. This week, he's tied for the lead entering Saturday's third round of the Genesis Scottish Open at The Renaissance Club. If he wins there, he arrives at Birkdale as the hottest player in the world in links conditions.

McIlroy has won four major championships, including The Open at Royal Liverpool in 2014. His game — high, towering ball flight; exceptional distance control; creative short game — translates perfectly to links conditions. At 35, he is playing some of the best golf of his career. The narrative of a second Open title in 2026, following a Masters victory in the spring, would represent one of the great major championship seasons in the modern era.

Scheffler's Reset

Scottie Scheffler, the World No. 1 entering his 197th overall week at the top of the rankings, arrives at Royal Birkdale under different circumstances. His missed cut at the Scottish Open — only his second missed cut in more than four years — means he won't have the tournament preparation that most top players use to calibrate their links game.

The counterargument is well-established: Scheffler's game is adaptable and his mental reset ability is exceptional. He has won major championships at courses he's never played competitively before. His three previous major titles demonstrate the ability to compete across wildly different venues and conditions. Royal Birkdale doesn't require a player to have played there to contend.

What Scheffler does need is to find fairways. His 11-of-26 fairway rate at The Renaissance Club won't transfer to a major. Links courses don't punish offline tee shots as harshly as parkland venues — the ball stays in play more easily — but accuracy from the tee still matters at Birkdale.

The Full Field

The 156-player field includes several LIV Golf competitors who qualified through direct exemptions. Joaquin Niemann, one of LIV's most consistent individual performers, earned a spot through the LIV pathway. Lucas Herbert, who won the most recent LIV event (Virginia, 24-under, wire-to-wire) earned direct entry as well, making him the 27th different LIV individual champion. Tom McKibbin qualified through his LINK Hong Kong Open victory, which earned him both Masters and Open invitations.

The field also includes virtually every established PGA Tour and DP World Tour name: Matt Fitzpatrick, who won the US Open at Brookline in 2022 and sits eight shots off McIlroy's Scottish Open pace; Wyndham Clark, the 2026 US Open champion; and the full cohort of world top-50 players who have qualified through their ranking or performance exemptions.

What's Next

The Open Championship begins Thursday July 16 with the first two rounds, continues through Saturday's third round, and concludes with Sunday's final round on July 19. The Claret Jug will be presented to the champion on the 18th green at Royal Birkdale.

Scottish Open results this weekend will shape the form guide. McIlroy winning in Scotland would make him the betting favorite going into Thursday. Scheffler's absence from the weekend at The Renaissance Club means his Birkdale preparation will take place out of the public eye — which may suit him.

Royal Birkdale last provided an Open champion in 2017. In 2026, it hosts the final men's major at the sport's most dramatic time of the calendar year. The conversation starts Thursday.
