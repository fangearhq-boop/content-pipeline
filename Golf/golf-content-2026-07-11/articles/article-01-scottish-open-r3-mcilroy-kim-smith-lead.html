McIlroy, Tom Kim, Jordan Smith Share Scottish Open Lead at 9-Under — The Open Tune-Up Is Getting Real

By Ryan Calloway | July 11, 2026

Genesis Scottish Open

The Genesis Scottish Open is delivering exactly what the week before The Open Championship is supposed to deliver — competitive links golf with genuine stakes, world-class players, and a leaderboard that will matter in five days when the field moves to Royal Birkdale.

Entering Moving Day on Saturday, three players are tied for the lead at 9-under par at The Renaissance Club in North Berwick, Scotland: Rory McIlroy, Tom Kim, and England's Jordan Smith. The $9 million event enters its penultimate round with the tournament wide open and a dozen players within three shots of the lead.

McIlroy's Week in Focus

McIlroy is playing the week with unmistakable purpose. Having won the 2026 Masters in April — his first major in over a decade — the 35-year-old Northern Irishman is chasing the kind of summer that gets remembered. A Scottish Open win followed by Open Championship glory would represent one of the more extraordinary six-week stretches in modern major golf.

He opened with a 69 on Thursday — solid links golf, if not spectacular — then elevated to a 66 on Friday to co-lead entering Saturday. The 66 was characterized by the kind of ball-striking precision that makes McIlroy uniquely dangerous on seaside courses. He's hit it well here before; he won the 2014 Scottish Open and has consistently performed in conditions that favor his combination of length, trajectory control, and imagination around the greens.

Tom Kim and the Co-Leaders

Tom Kim's presence at the top of the leaderboard represents the kind of breakthrough moment that this tournament has produced before. The American-Korean star has been a consistent presence on tour without quite landing the signature result that cements a player's reputation. Nine-under through 36 holes at a European event with The Open around the corner is a significant statement.

Jordan Smith of England completes the three-way tie at 9-under. Smith is less familiar to casual fans but has the game suited to links conditions — a consistent ball-striker who manages courses methodically and avoids the disasters that the Scottish coast can produce.

One stroke back at 8-under: Matt Fitzpatrick and Min Woo Lee. Two back at 7-under: Chris Gotterup, Sudarshan Yellamaraju, and Robert MacIntyre. MacIntyre — the 2024 Scottish Open champion and a fan favorite in his home country — is two shots off the pace but well within range. A MacIntyre charge at The Renaissance Club would be one of the tournament's most celebrated results; he knows this venue and this crowd as well as any player in the field.

Wyndham Clark, the 2026 US Open champion, sits three back at 6-under — competitive, but requiring a strong Saturday simply to stay relevant.

What the Course Demands

The Renaissance Club presents the classic Scottish links challenge: exposed to the elements, with firm and fast fairways that reward patience and strategic thinking over raw power. Saturday's weather will influence the scoring significantly — links conditions can swing dramatically over the course of a single round, with players in later groups facing conditions entirely different from those who tee off early.

McIlroy, Kim, and Smith tee off in the penultimate pairing or later on Saturday, giving them the benefit of playing alongside each other with similar conditions. The featured pairing of McIlroy and Fitzpatrick goes out at 15:24 UK time, with Kim and Smith closing the round at 15:35.

What's Next

Saturday's round will set up a Sunday final round with genuine consequence — the winner takes $9 million in prize money and arrives at Royal Birkdale on the kind of form that tournaments are built around. For McIlroy specifically, a Scottish Open victory this week would make him the overwhelming story entering The Open, the kind of momentum that's hard to manufacture and harder to replicate.

Round 3 is ongoing today. The leaderboard will shift. The next update arrives from the 18th green at The Renaissance Club, and after that, the conversation moves to Southport.
