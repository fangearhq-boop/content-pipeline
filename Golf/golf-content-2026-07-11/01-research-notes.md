**Key Facts:**
- Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF) announced in April 2026 it will no longer bankroll LIV beyond the 2026 season
- LIV Golf has filed Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification (WARN Act) notices in both the U.S. and U.K. — legal requirement for employers planning potential mass layoffs with 60 days' notice
- LIV spokesperson: currently no changes to operations or schedule
- League urgently seeking $250 million to $350 million in outside investment (some reports say up to $400 million needed)
- Funding needed to cover: player contracts, operating costs, prize purses for upcoming events
- If funding not secured: possible cancellation of the final two events of the 2026 season
- English companies World Golf Group and Premier Golf League suing LIV Golf, PIF, and associated entities for damages of $210 million to $630 million, alleging breach of confidence and unlawful means conspiracy
- PIF has invested more than $5 billion into LIV since its founding in 2022
- Annual losses estimated at $500-600 million
- Confidence: HIGH (Sportico, Golf Digest, multiple sources confirm WARN Act filings)
