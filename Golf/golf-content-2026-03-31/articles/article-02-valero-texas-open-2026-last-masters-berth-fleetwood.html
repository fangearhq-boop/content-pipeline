The Last Masters Spot Is on the Line: Valero Texas Open Starts Thursday

By Golf Fanrecap Staff March 31, 2026

One Tournament. One Invitation. Everything on the Line.

The 2026 Valero Texas Open begins Thursday at TPC San Antonio's Oaks Course — and for the winner, Sunday's final round will end with one of the most coveted envelopes in professional golf: an invitation to Augusta National. The Valero, contested April 2-5, is the final PGA Tour event before the Masters Tournament and carries the distinction of awarding the last open spot in the Masters field to its champion.

Tommy Fleetwood enters the week as the betting favorite at +1400, with Sweden's Ludvig Aberg close behind at +1600. Both players bring the kind of ball-striking and course management that tend to translate well from Texas to Georgia. Confirmed starters also include Rickie Fowler and Jordan Spieth, whose motivation this week goes well beyond the standard tournament prize. For both, a Valero victory would mean arriving at Augusta National next week with a berth, momentum, and purpose.

The Historical Weight of the Valero

Founded in 1922, the Valero Texas Open is the third-oldest tournament on the PGA Tour, behind only the Western Open and the Canadian Open. Its longevity reflects its place in Texas sports culture and in the broader professional golf ecosystem. Billy Horschel is the defending champion, and the Oaks Course at TPC San Antonio — a Coore and Crenshaw design — consistently produces demanding conditions that test patience, shot-making creativity, and putting under pressure.

This year's field includes dozens of players for whom the Valero represents something beyond a regular tournament start. With the Masters field largely set, the players not already exempt are competing not just for the tournament title but for the right to stand on the first tee at Augusta the following Thursday. That dynamic — invisible on the scorecard but present in every decision — gives the Valero an added weight that elevates its atmosphere across all four rounds.

The Masters Connection

The tradition of the Valero Texas Open winner earning a Masters invitation dates back years and has produced some memorable Augusta runs from players who arrive with tournament-fresh confidence. There is something to the idea that winning the week before Augusta — sharpened by competition, proven over 72 holes, and motivated by the grandest of destinations — creates a particular kind of readiness. Not every Valero winner has made noise at Augusta, but the ones who do tend to stay in the conversation for reasons beyond mere odds.

What's Next

The Valero Texas Open begins Thursday, April 2, with the final round Sunday, April 5. The winner will be confirmed in the Masters field immediately following their victory. Augusta National opens for official practice rounds Monday, April 6, with the first competitive round Thursday, April 9. For whoever lifts the Valero trophy on Sunday afternoon, the week ahead in Augusta, Georgia will feel like a continuation of the same extraordinary run.
