Masters 2026 Countdown: Scheffler Favored, McIlroy Defending, Tiger Still a Question Mark

By Golf Fanrecap Staff March 31, 2026

Nine Days and Counting

The 90th Masters Tournament — April 9-12 at Augusta National Golf Club — is nine days away, and the storylines heading into one of sport's most iconic events are as rich as any in recent memory. The 93-player field is nearly complete, with one final invitation awaiting the winner of this week's Valero Texas Open. The broadcast picture is shifting, the defending champion is a Grand Slam winner, and a five-time champion's participation remains officially uncertain.

Scottie Scheffler enters the week as the pre-tournament favorite at +480. The World No. 1 player is chasing what would be his third Masters title in four years — a historic achievement that would cement his place among Augusta's most decorated champions. Scheffler won the green jacket in 2022 and 2024 and arrives in 2026 with the kind of consistency and ball-striking that Augusta National tends to reward.

McIlroy's Defending Position

Rory McIlroy is the defending Masters champion following his 2025 victory at Augusta — a playoff win that completed his career Grand Slam, making him the sixth player in history to win all four major championships. McIlroy's 2025 Masters triumph was one of the most celebrated moments in recent golf history, ending years of near-misses at Augusta and validating a career's worth of brilliance across every other major venue.

Defending the Masters is historically one of the game's more difficult tasks — back-to-back winners are extraordinarily rare in Augusta's long history — but McIlroy arrives with the confidence of a man who finally solved the puzzle he spent a decade working. His odds at the tournament, while longer than Scheffler's, reflect genuine competitive capability on a course where he has now proven he can win.

LIV Players, New Broadcast, and Legacy Coverage

Bryson DeChambeau and Jon Rahm, both LIV Golf players, are in the Masters field and represent the continued integration of the rival tour's top talent into golf's major championships. DeChambeau enters April with back-to-back LIV victories and has been among the sport's most consistent performers through the early months of 2026. Both he and Rahm are listed at approximately +1200 in early odds.

The 2026 Masters will mark Amazon Prime's debut as a streaming broadcast partner — a significant shift in how the most prestigious golf tournament in the world reaches its audience. CBS Sports remains the traditional broadcast home for weekend coverage, with Jim Nantz anchoring for his 38th consecutive year at Augusta National. For viewers who have watched Nantz guide them through decades of Masters moments, his presence behind the microphone is as much a part of the event's identity as the blooming azaleas.

What's Next

The Masters field will be finalized following the Valero Texas Open on Sunday, April 5. Official practice rounds at Augusta National begin Monday, April 6, with the first round of tournament play Thursday, April 9. For the next nine days, the buildup will only intensify — and the question of Tiger Woods' participation will remain the defining subplot until a definitive answer arrives.
