LPGA's First Major Is Coming — And Hyo Joo Kim Is Arriving on the Hottest Streak on Tour

By Golf Fanrecap Staff March 31, 2026

The LPGA's First Major of 2026

The Chevron Championship — the LPGA Tour's first major of the 2026 season — is scheduled for April 23-26, offering the women's game its first defining moment of the year at major championship level. Coming approximately three weeks after the Masters, the Chevron arrives into a golf landscape primed for another great story — and the player best positioned to provide one is in remarkable form.

Hyo Joo Kim enters April having won back-to-back LPGA events: the Fortinet Founders Cup and the Ford Championship presented by Wild Horse Pass. At the Ford Championship, Kim became the first player in LPGA Tour history to shoot two rounds of 61 in the same tournament — a statistical landmark that underlines not just talent but a level of sustained, focused execution that is rare in any professional sport. She finished the event at 28-under-par 260, two shots clear of Nelly Korda.

What Kim's Streak Means for the Major

Players who arrive at major championships on back-to-back wins carry a specific kind of competitive confidence — the knowledge that their game is fully dialed in at the moment it matters most. For Kim, who has been one of the LPGA Tour's most consistent players without always generating mainstream recognition, the 2026 season's opening weeks represent a statement to the broader golf world. She is playing the best golf of her career at the right time.

Korda, runner-up at back-to-back events herself, will be among the Chevron's strongest challengers. Lydia Ko, who began the Ford Championship with a career-best opening-round 60 before finishing fourth, brings the form and experience of a two-time Olympic gold medalist to whatever course hosts the 2026 major. The early-season LPGA narrative has been defined by excellence, and the Chevron promises a continuation of that standard.

The Major That Opens the Women's Season

The Chevron Championship has a long history as one of women's golf's most prestigious events, having moved through several venues in California and Texas over the decades. A Chevron title is among the most significant accomplishments in the LPGA's major championship calendar, and for players chasing a career Grand Slam in women's golf — the ANA/Chevron, KPMG Women's PGA, U.S. Women's Open, and AIG Women's British Open — it is the first hurdle of the season.

What's Next

The Chevron Championship tees off April 23, approximately two weeks after the conclusion of the Masters. Kim's form between now and then — and her preparation for major championship conditions — will be the most closely watched storyline in women's golf. If she continues to play at the level she showed at the Ford Championship, the Chevron Championship field faces a formidable task. The first major of the LPGA season could be decided by a player who looks almost impossible to beat right now.
