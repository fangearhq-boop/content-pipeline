Tiger Woods Is in the Masters Field. He Hasn't Said Yes. Augusta Is 9 Days Away.

By Golf Fanrecap Staff March 31, 2026

The Question No One Can Answer

The 2026 Masters Tournament begins April 9 — nine days from Tuesday — and the most discussed name in golf's field is one whose participation remains officially unconfirmed. Tiger Woods, a five-time Masters champion making what would be his 27th start at Augusta National, is listed in the 93-player field. He has not said he will play. He has not said he won't.

The uncertainty traces back to March 27, when Woods was arrested in Jupiter Island, Florida, on suspicion of driving under the influence after a rollover crash. A breathalyzer test returned a reading of 0.00 — police confirmed alcohol was not a factor — but Woods refused a urine test. Formal DUI charges were filed and a mugshot was released, triggering a global news cycle that has refused to quiet in the days since. Donald Trump publicly claimed that Woods "won't be playing" at Augusta. Other reports indicate Tiger has been practicing and is working toward a participation decision.

Augusta's Tradition of Silence

Augusta National Golf Club operates with a deliberate and well-practiced restraint when it comes to player participation questions. The club extends lifetime invitations to all Masters champions — a policy that places the decision firmly with the player, not the institution. Masters officials have not made any public statement regarding Woods' status and are unlikely to unless a formal withdrawal is submitted.

That silence is itself a form of answer: the door at Augusta remains open. Whether Tiger walks through it is a decision that will be made privately, most likely in the coming days, as the logistical and medical realities of competing at one of the most demanding courses in professional golf intersect with the legal and reputational complexities of the past week. Woods acknowledged in a prior interview that at 50 years old, following seven back surgeries, "sometimes I have good days, sometimes I have bad days."

What It Would Mean

Tiger Woods at the 2026 Masters would be one of the most watched sporting events of the year, regardless of competitive outcome. Five green jackets. Decades of defining moments on that course. A career that has generated more narrative than any other in modern golf. And a backdrop — legal uncertainty, health uncertainty, the weight of what has been and what might have been — that would make every tee shot at Augusta feel consequential beyond anything the leaderboard could measure.

If he doesn't play, the 2026 Masters will be defined by his absence in much the same way that his injuries shaped coverage in years he missed. The golf world has learned to hold both possibilities simultaneously when it comes to Tiger: the hope that he'll show up, and the readiness to accept that he won't.

What's Next

Augusta National typically requires players to officially confirm participation in the days leading up to the tournament. The coming week — particularly the next 48-72 hours as the Valero Texas Open begins and pre-Masters media coverage accelerates — is likely to produce a definitive answer one way or another. If Tiger withdraws, it will be major news. If he confirms, Thursday's practice rounds cannot come soon enough.
