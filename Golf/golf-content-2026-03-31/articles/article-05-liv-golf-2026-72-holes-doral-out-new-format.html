LIV Golf Goes 72 Holes: The League's Biggest Format Change Since Launch

By Golf Fanrecap Staff March 31, 2026

The Biggest Rule Change in LIV Golf History

LIV Golf is now in its fifth season — and for 2026, the league has introduced the most fundamental change to its format since the circuit launched in 2022: events are now contested over 72 holes, up from the 54-hole format that defined LIV's first four seasons. The change brings the league's stroke-play events structurally closer to the PGA Tour and the major championships, and fundamentally alters how competitive rounds unfold within each tournament.

The core elements that made LIV Golf distinctive remain: there is still no cut, meaning all 57 players per event compete across all four rounds. The shotgun start format — which begins all groups simultaneously from different holes — is also retained. But an additional 18 holes of competition per event changes the competitive mathematics significantly. Early leaders face more pressure. Comebacks become more viable. And the endurance component of professional golf — sustained performance across four days — is now fully part of the LIV competitive challenge.

What Changed Off the Course

The venue landscape for 2026 LIV Golf has also seen notable shifts. Trump National Doral — the Miami resort that hosted LIV Golf annually since the league's founding and became one of its highest-profile American stops — has been removed from the 2026 schedule. The departure ends what had been one of LIV Golf's most recognized regular venues and severs a connection that had been part of the league's identity from the beginning.

New additions include LIV Golf New Orleans at Bayou Oaks — a new American venue that brings the tour to the Gulf South for the first time. The broader schedule maintains LIV Golf's global ambitions, with events continuing in Saudi Arabia, Singapore, South Africa, Spain, Great Britain, and South Korea across a season that runs through August.

DeChambeau's Position Heading Into April

Bryson DeChambeau enters the 2026 season's next phase having won back-to-back LIV events — in Singapore and South Africa — and will tee it up at the Masters (April 9-12) in what has become an annual demonstration that LIV Golf's top players remain competitive at the major championship level. Under the current standing agreement, LIV players maintain their eligibility for all four major championships, and DeChambeau and Jon Rahm represent the league's strongest presence in the Masters field at approximately +1200 odds each.

For critics who argued the 54-hole format made LIV Golf feel like an exhibition rather than serious competition, the move to 72 holes is a direct response. Whether it changes perceptions — or meaningfully alters the competitive outcomes of the events themselves — will play out across the 2026 season.

What's Next

LIV Golf's 2026 schedule continues with upcoming events in New Orleans and multiple international destinations through August. The league's players, including DeChambeau and Rahm, will also compete at all four major championships as the sport's parallel competition calendars continue to run simultaneously. For golf fans tracking both tours, 2026 offers more competitive golf than any recent season — and a LIV format that, for the first time, asks its players to go the full four-round distance.
