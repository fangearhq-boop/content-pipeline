Nelly Korda Is 4 Points From the LPGA Hall of Fame — Queen City Championship Is This Week

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap

May 12, 2026

Nelly Korda currently has 23 of the 27 points required for automatic LPGA Hall of Fame qualification. She is 4 points away from one of the most exclusive honors in professional sports — and she is teeing it up this week at the Kroger Queen City Championship at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati, Ohio (May 14-17).

The 27-year-old American has built a 2026 season for the ages. Three victories — the Tournament of Champions, the Chevron Championship (her second), and the Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba — have her near the top of every statistical category on the LPGA Tour. She has not finished outside the top two in any of her six starts this season. The closest comparison in women's golf history is Annika Sorenstam, who in 2001 became the last player to finish inside the top two in each of her first six starts of a season.

The Hall of Fame Math

LPGA Hall of Fame qualification requires 27 points, accumulated through a combination of wins and major championships. A regular LPGA victory is worth 1 point. A major championship win is worth 2 points. Winning the LPGA Player of the Year is worth additional credit.

With 23 points, Korda needs 4 more. A regular win this week at the Queen City would be worth 1 point, bringing her to 24. If she were to win a major — and the KPMG Women's PGA Championship is coming up next month — that would be worth 2 points. The precise path to 27 will depend on what she wins and when.

What's not in question is the trajectory. Korda is the No. 2-ranked player in the world, trailing only Jeeno Thitikul, who is also in the field at Queen City this week.

The Historical Context

When Korda won the Riviera Maya Open, she became the youngest American to reach 18 career LPGA victories since Nancy Lopez achieved the milestone in 1980 — when Korda's parents were children. Lopez went on to become one of the most celebrated figures in women's golf history. The comparison, while premature, speaks to the scale of what Korda has accomplished.

She has three major championship victories: the 2021 Women's PGA Championship and consecutive Chevron Championships in 2024 and 2026. At 27, she has time — but the pace at which she is accumulating Hall of Fame points suggests she may not need it.

The Competition This Week

The Kroger Queen City Championship draws a strong field, with world No. 1 Thitikul joining Korda as the marquee names. Charley Hull and Lydia Ko are also in the field. The course — Maketewah Country Club — is a challenging layout that rewards precision iron play and patient course management.

Any win brings Korda closer. Any week where she contends is another step toward the Hall. And given her form in 2026, a week where Nelly Korda doesn't contend has become the genuine surprise.

What's Next

The Kroger Queen City Championship begins Thursday, May 14 at Maketewah Country Club in Cincinnati. Follow Korda's round-by-round progress — if she wins, she will be 25 of 27 Hall of Fame points and the conversation will only intensify. The KPMG Women's PGA Championship later in June represents the next major opportunity.
