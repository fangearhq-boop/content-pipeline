From First Win to Major Debut: How Kristoffer Reitan Went From Quail Hollow to Aronimink in One Week

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

May 12, 2026

Eight days ago, Kristoffer Reitan had never won on the PGA Tour. By Sunday afternoon, he'd closed out a two-shot victory at the Truist Championship at Quail Hollow in Charlotte, pocketed $3.6 million, and earned an exempt entry into this week's PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club.

The 28-year-old Norwegian shot a final-round 2-under 69 to finish at 15-under 269 for the tournament, edging Rickie Fowler and Nicolai Hojgaard for his first career victory in his 15th Tour start. In the span of seven days, his professional world was transformed.

How He Won It

Reitan wasn't a household name heading into the Truist, and the final round didn't set up to favor him over more established players. He was among several contenders with a realistic chance heading into Sunday, and he delivered when it mattered by making birdies on the 14th and 15th holes to seize control.

Fowler, a popular veteran who has come close in major championships on multiple occasions, couldn't quite close the gap. Hojgaard, another European who had been in contention, also fell just short. Reitan's 69 was steady and measured — the performance of a player who trusted himself when the moment was biggest.

Cameron Young, who had been electric in the third round with a 63, couldn't replicate that form on Sunday. Rory McIlroy — who many expected to mount a charge at Quail Hollow, a course he has owned for years — finished around T19 after a final-round 67 that came one day too late.

The PGA Championship Awaits

Major championships are frequently the stage where Tour rookies and first-time winners either freeze or surprise. Reitan heads to Aronimink with no expectations, no pressure, and a winner's confidence freshly installed.

That is, paradoxically, one of the best situations a player can be in at a major. The field around him will include McIlroy, Scheffler, Young, and a dozen other players carrying narrative weight. Reitan can simply play golf — which, as he demonstrated last Sunday, he is quite capable of doing well.

Norway's Journey to the Tour

Norwegian golf has a modest history at the game's highest professional level, but it has been growing. Reitan's victory at Quail Hollow is the latest chapter — a 28-year-old who worked his way up through the Challenge Tour and earned his PGA Tour card through persistence and performance. First victories don't always come quickly on Tour. In his 15th start, Reitan's came in spectacular fashion at a Signature Event.

What's Next

Reitan tees off Thursday, May 14 at Aronimink for the PGA Championship — a bonus week that he had no reason to expect before Sunday. Whatever happens over 72 holes, his first Tour win and his debut in a major championship have arrived together in the same week. For a career, that kind of beginning is hard to imagine scripting better.
