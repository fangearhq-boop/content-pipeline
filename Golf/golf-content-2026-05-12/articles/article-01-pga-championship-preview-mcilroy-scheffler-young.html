PGA Championship Preview: McIlroy Chases Back-to-Back Majors, Scheffler Seeks Redemption at Aronimink

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap

May 12, 2026

Rory McIlroy defended his Masters title in April, winning his sixth major championship at Augusta National. Now, two days from Thursday's opening tee shots at Aronimink Golf Club in Newtown Square, Pennsylvania, he arrives at the PGA Championship with a chance to do something no golfer has done since Jordan Spieth in 2015: win the first two majors of the calendar year.

The field for the 2026 PGA Championship reads like a who's who of the world's best players. Scottie Scheffler is the defending champion. Cameron Young enters at World No. 3 after his best stretch of Tour golf. Matt Fitzpatrick has been excellent this season. And 11 players from LIV Golf — including Jon Rahm and Bryson DeChambeau — are in the field. This is the PGA Championship at its most loaded.

McIlroy's Historic Opportunity

McIlroy's narrative heading into Aronimink is straightforward and enormous. He won the 2025 Masters to complete his career Grand Slam, then defended successfully in 2026, becoming the fourth player in history to win back-to-back at Augusta National. He arrives at the year's second major with six major titles and rare momentum.

If he wins this week, he would become the first player since Spieth's 2015 run to hold the first two major titles of the year simultaneously. It's a milestone that every golfer aims for but almost none achieve. McIlroy's game — and his state of mind — appear to be in the ideal condition for an attempt.

Scheffler's Redemption Arc

Scheffler is the World No. 1 who hasn't won in 2026. He finished runner-up at the Masters, the RBC Heritage, and the Cadillac Championship at Doral — three consecutive second-place finishes that are extraordinary in their own right, but not the result a player of Scheffler's caliber wants to carry into a major. He skipped the Truist Championship last week to focus entirely on preparation for Aronimink.

A win this week would be a defining moment. The defending champion, carrying the weight of near-misses, on a Donald Ross course that rewards precision — the stage is set perfectly for Scheffler to answer his doubters in the most emphatic fashion possible.

The Course

Aronimink Golf Club has not hosted the PGA Championship since Gary Player won here in 1962. The Donald Ross design features 176 bunkers, approximately 7,394 yards of demanding par-70 golf, and bentgrass fairways and greens that reward accurate approach play. The greens average around 8,300 square feet — massive putting surfaces where finding the green is just the beginning of the challenge.

Course analysts project approach play as the single most important statistical category for this championship. Players who consistently find the proper sections of Aronimink's complex greens will be the ones standing on the 18th Sunday afternoon with a chance to win the Wanamaker Trophy.

What's Next

Round 1 tee times begin Thursday, May 14. The final round is scheduled for Sunday, May 17. Television coverage runs across ESPN and CBS. Keep an eye on the morning-afternoon split draws, as Aronimink's exposed layout can play very differently depending on weather conditions throughout the day.

The 2026 PGA Championship may not be the most predictable major in recent memory, but it could be one of the most compelling. Two days until we find out.
