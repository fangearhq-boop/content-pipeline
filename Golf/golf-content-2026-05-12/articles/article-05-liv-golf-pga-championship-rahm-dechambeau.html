LIV's 11: Rahm, DeChambeau, Herbert and the LIV Contingent Arrive at Aronimink

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap

May 12, 2026

Eleven LIV Golf players are in the field for the 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club, and several of them arrive in strong form. The PGA Championship is one of the four majors that remains fully open to LIV entrants with qualifying exemptions — and this year's field has some legitimate contenders wearing the LIV banner.

Jon Rahm leads the group. The Spaniard won two LIV events in 2026 (Hong Kong and Mexico City) and leads the LIV individual standings with 779 points. He is a former World No. 1 who won the US Open in 2021 and the Masters in 2023, and he arrives at Aronimink with the most major championship pedigree of any LIV player in the field.

DeChambeau's Return

Bryson DeChambeau is second in the LIV standings with 476.90 points. He missed part of the spring after withdrawing from Mexico City with a wrist injury, but has since been cleared to return. DeChambeau won the 2020 US Open and has the power-game profile that can thrive at certain major setups. Aronimink's fairways are relatively forgiving off the tee, which may suit his aggressive approach.

DeChambeau has always been one of those players who can win a major when the conditions align with his strengths. He will draw significant attention from the moment he tees off Thursday.

Herbert's Hot Hand

The most intriguing LIV player at Aronimink may not be Rahm or DeChambeau — it might be Lucas Herbert. The Australian won last week's MAADEN LIV Golf Virginia event at Trump National in a wire-to-wire performance, shooting 24-under for the tournament while battling the flu throughout the entire week.

That victory earned Herbert a spot in the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills, as the highest-ranked LIV player in the top three who wasn't already exempt. He arrives at Aronimink with a freshly-minted title, a hot putter, and the kind of form that can carry a player through a major week.

Dustin Johnson and the Rest

Dustin Johnson, the 2016 US Open champion, is also in the Aronimink field. At 41, Johnson remains a premium driver of the golf ball and a dangerous player at courses where length off the tee creates advantage. Johnson won his lone major on a US Open setup — the precision demands of Aronimink may require a slightly different game plan.

The full LIV contingent at Aronimink fills out around these headliners with several other former Tour players who have found competitive homes on the Saudi-backed circuit. Their presence at the major adds the ongoing subplot of whether LIV players can match the accumulated form of Tour players who play 30-plus competitive rounds per year.

What's Next

First tee shots are Thursday, May 14 at Aronimink Golf Club. Watch for Rahm's pairing — he is typically grouped with other high-profile players in the major draws, which will generate the loudest galleries. Herbert's first-round performance will be closely followed given his LIV win seven days prior. And DeChambeau — as always — will attract as much attention as any player on the property from the moment he steps on the first tee.
