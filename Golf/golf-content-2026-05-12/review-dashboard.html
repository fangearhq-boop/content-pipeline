### Long-Form Post Two days from now, the best golfers in the world tee it up at Aronimink Golf Club in Pennsylvania for the PGA Championship — and the storylines heading into this week might be the best we've seen at a major in years. Rory McIlroy arrives as the Masters champion, fresh off defending his green jacket in April to win his sixth major title. He's seeking to become the first player since Jordan Spieth in 2015 to win the first two majors of the year. He's been here before, and he's as hungry as he's ever been. Scottie Scheffler is the defending champion — but the World No. 1 has been the bridesmaid more than the bride in 2026, finishing runner-up three straight times (Masters, RBC Heritage, Cadillac Championship). He skipped the Truist last week specifically to prepare for Aronimink. Cameron Young arrives at World No. 3 after winning the Players Championship and Doral, still chasing his first major win and playing the best golf of his life. Then there's the course itself. Aronimink hasn't hosted the PGA Championship since 1962, when Gary Player won. The Donald Ross design features 176 bunkers, massive bentgrass greens, and four par-3s over 210 yards. Approach play is going to matter enormously. First tee shots are Thursday. This one is going to be good. ### Image Post Caption PGA Championship starts THURSDAY. ### Long-Form Post Ask yourself this: which active PGA Tour player has been better in 2026 than Cameron Young? The 27-year-old from Scarborough, New York won the Players Championship in March in dramatic fashion — birdying the island green 17th and watching Matt Fitzpatrick bogey the 18th to hand him the title. Then he went to Trump National Doral and won the Cadillac Championship wire-to-wire, finishing 19-under and winning by six shots over Scottie Scheffler. Aronimink Golf Club may be the place where that changes. CBS Sports projects approach play as the most important statistical category this week, given the course's sweeping 8,300-square-foot greens and the need for precision over power. ### Long-Form Post One week you win for the first time on the PGA Tour. The next week, you're in the field at a major championship. That's exactly what happened to Kristoffer Reitan. The 28-year-old Norwegian capped an emotional win at the Truist Championship at Quail Hollow last Sunday — his first PGA Tour victory in his 15th start — with a 2-under 69 to finish at 15-under 269, edging Rickie Fowler and Nicolai Hojgaard by two shots to claim the $3.6 million check from the $20 million purse. That win punched Reitan's ticket to the 2026 PGA Championship at Aronimink Golf Club, starting this Thursday. In the span of eight days, he went from first-time Tour winner to major championship competitor. Other notables from Quail Hollow: Cameron Young posted a stunning 9-under 63 in the third round before being unable to close. Rory McIlroy, who many expected to challenge for a rare Quail Hollow title, finished around T19 after a final-round 67. Matt Fitzpatrick was the runner-up threat alongside Fowler and Hojgaard.
