### STORY 1: Memorial Tournament Preview

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM ET)**

One of the most compelling stories in professional golf begins Thursday at Muirfield Village.

Scottie Scheffler arrives at the Memorial Tournament as the two-time defending champion. He won this event in 2024 and again in 2025, becoming the first player since Tiger Woods (1999-2001) to claim back-to-back Memorial titles. Now he goes for three in a row — a feat only Woods has accomplished on Jack Nicklaus' course.

Scheffler is the World No. 1 and the odds-on favorite at +300 heading into Thursday's first round. He has one win in 2026 (at The American Express) and enters Muirfield Village seeking to jumpstart a season that has produced several runner-up finishes without a second win. The Memorial would be a statement.

Standing between Scheffler and history is a field that includes Rory McIlroy (+900), the back-to-back Masters champion who has never won at Muirfield Village. McIlroy is making only his eighth start of the 2026 season. He won Augusta in April with one of the great clutch performances in recent major history — a bogey on 18 to hold off Scheffler by one shot. That battle continues in Dublin, Ohio.

The full field includes Xander Schauffele (+1600), Cameron Young (+1600), and Ludvig Åberg (+1750). The $20 million purse — $4 million to the winner — is among the largest in golf.

Muirfield Village Golf Club: Par 72, 7,553 yards, one of the most demanding tests on the PGA Tour. First round tees off Thursday, June 4.

Real Club Valderrama in Sotogrande, Spain — host of the 1997 Ryder Cup and widely called the "Augusta of Europe" — welcomes the LIV Golf field for the Andalucia event, June 4-7. It is the ninth event of the 2026 LIV season, and crucially, it is the last major preparation stop for LIV players ahead of the US Open.

Jon Rahm is the 16/5 favorite. The Spanish superstar is returning to his home country, captaining Legion XIII — the reigning LIV team champions. This is the course and the crowd that feels like home for Rahm, and he will be motivated accordingly.

One notable absence: Phil Mickelson is not playing, with LIV citing an undisclosed family health matter as the reason.

$20 million individual purse. $10 million team purse. No cut. 57 players. Four days of championship golf at Valderrama.

That is the contradiction McIlroy carries to Muirfield Village this week. The McIlroy-Scheffler rivalry is one of the defining features of professional golf in 2026. They battled down the final holes at Augusta. They will be paired close to each other at Muirfield Village again. Whether the back-to-back Masters champion can finally break through in Dublin, Ohio is one of the story's most compelling threads heading into Thursday.

The venue is historic: the first time the US Women's Open has ever been held at Riviera. The course — par 71, 6,699 yards, a ballstriker's challenge — suits players who drive accurately and attack pins from the correct positions. That describes Korda precisely.

She is six days away from potentially completing the most dominant start to a season in women's golf history with a US Women's Open win. Riviera starts Thursday.
