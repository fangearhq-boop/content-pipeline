Memorial Tournament 2026 Preview: Scheffler Chases a Three-Peat Only Tiger Woods Has Accomplished

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 2, 2026

Scottie Scheffler has won the Memorial Tournament twice. He won it in 2024, and he won it again in 2025. When he tees it up Thursday at Muirfield Village Golf Club in Dublin, Ohio, he will attempt to become the first player since Tiger Woods to win three consecutive editions of this event.

Woods did it from 1999 to 2001. In the 25 years since, no player has come close to matching that sequence. Scheffler, the World No. 1 and odds-on favorite at +300, is now positioned to either join that conversation or watch another chapter of his season pass without a second win.

What Scheffler Brings to This Week

Scheffler's 2026 season has produced one win — at The American Express — along with a pattern of runner-up finishes and contention that has not translated into victories. His game is clearly there. The results have been inconsistent by the standards of a player who won six times in 2024.

The Memorial is where that changes, if it changes anywhere. He knows this course. He won it in conditions that tested every player in the field. Twice. Muirfield Village, designed by Jack Nicklaus and opened in 1974, measures 7,553 yards at par 72. The rough is punishing, the greens are fast, and the margin for error is minimal. Those conditions favor the most precise player in the world, which is generally Scheffler.

McIlroy's Counter-Narrative

The story of the week is not only Scheffler. Rory McIlroy arrives at Muirfield Village as the back-to-back Masters champion — only the fourth player in the history of Augusta National to win consecutive green jackets — with +900 odds and zero Memorial Tournament victories to his name.

McIlroy won the 2026 Masters with a bogey on the 72nd hole, holding off Scheffler by one shot in a finish that had the golf world holding its breath. He then entered the PGA Championship chasing a potential Grand Slam and finished five back of champion Aaron Rai. The Memorial marks only his eighth start of 2026.

His history at Muirfield Village does not reflect his overall standing in the game. He has never won here, which makes this week's opportunity all the more compelling.

The Rest of the Field

Xander Schauffele (+1600), Cameron Young (+1600), and Ludvig Åberg (+1750) round out the group of players who could challenge for the $4 million winner's share from a $20 million total purse. This is a signature event — the 72-player field represents the best available in professional golf.

What's Next

First round tees off Thursday, June 4 at Muirfield Village. The Scheffler-McIlroy thread will define the narrative through four days at one of golf's great venues. Tiger's three-peat record has stood for 25 years. Whether it falls this week to one of the two best players in the world today is a question that begins to answer itself on Thursday morning.
