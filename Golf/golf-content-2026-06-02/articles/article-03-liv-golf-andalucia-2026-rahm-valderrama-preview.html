LIV Golf Andalucia 2026: Rahm at Valderrama for the Last Major Prep Before the US Open

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 2, 2026

Real Club Valderrama in Sotogrande, Spain is widely considered the greatest golf course in continental Europe — the "Augusta of Europe," as it has been called since hosting the 1997 Ryder Cup. This week, it hosts the LIV Golf Andalucia event, June 4-7, as the ninth event of the 2026 LIV season.

The timing makes it one of the most consequential LIV events of the year. This is the last competitive round for LIV players before the US Open, making Valderrama a final sharpening block for everyone who wants to carry form into the year's third major.

Rahm on Home Soil

Jon Rahm is the 16/5 favorite. The Spanish superstar returns to his home country as captain of Legion XIII — the reigning LIV team champions — with motivation that extends beyond the $4 million winner's share. Rahm at a Spanish event, on one of the most revered courses his country has produced, is a combination that generates a particular kind of competitive energy.

Bryson DeChambeau is 15/2, bringing his power game to a course that does not always reward length over accuracy. Valderrama's cork oak tree-lined fairways and notoriously difficult greens have humbled many long hitters over the years.

Niemann's Record Pursuit

Joaquin Niemann enters at 8/1. The Chilean phenom owns eight career LIV individual titles — the most in league history. A win at Valderrama would push that record to nine and extend his position as the most decorated individual in LIV's brief but competitive history.

Sergio Garcia captains Fireballs GC on home turf, with fellow Spaniards David Puig (14/1) and Josele Ballester rounding out the team. Garcia won the Valderrama individual title in 2024, and the emotional connection to this course runs deeper for him than almost any other venue on tour.

A Notable Absence

Phil Mickelson will not play this week. LIV has cited an undisclosed family health matter as the reason for his absence. The six-time major champion's health has been a periodic topic during his LIV tenure, and well-wishes from the broader golf community have followed the announcement.

What's Next

LIV Golf Andalucia runs June 4-7, with 57 players competing over 72 holes with no cut. The $20 million individual purse and $10 million team purse make this one of the richer events on the 2026 golf calendar. With the US Open approaching, whoever plays best this week at Valderrama may carry that form into major week.
