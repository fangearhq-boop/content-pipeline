US Women's Open 2026 Preview: Korda Chases History at Riviera's First Women's Major

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | June 2, 2026

The 81st US Women's Open begins Thursday at Riviera Country Club in Pacific Palisades, California — and before the first shot is struck, the tournament has already made history. This is the first time a USGA women's major championship has ever been played at Riviera Country Club.

The venue addition is significant. Riviera is one of the most celebrated venues in American golf, having hosted PGA Tour events including the Farmers Insurance Open for decades. Its inclusion in the women's major rotation signals a meaningful expansion of the most prestigious stages available to LPGA players.

Korda's One Remaining Major

Nelly Korda arrives as the +350 favorite. Her 2026 season is unlike anything produced in women's golf in recent memory: three wins and three runner-up finishes in just seven starts. The three wins include the Chevron Championship — the third major title of her career. She is the undisputed World No. 1.

The one piece missing is the US Women's Open. Korda has won three of the four LPGA major championships. The USWO is the exception. She finished second at last year's edition — close enough to show she can contend, not close enough to win the trophy she has been pursuing.

Riviera, as a ballstriker's course at par 71 and 6,699 yards, is the kind of demanding, precise track that rewards the most technically complete players in the field. Korda is among the finest ballstrikers in women's golf. The setup is favorable for her style of play.

Thitikul's Major Hunt

Jeeno Thitikul enters at +900 as the second favorite. The 23-year-old Thai star has won twice in 2026 and has been in contention at multiple majors without a breakthrough. She is one of the most talented young players in the game, and the US Women's Open at Riviera represents another opportunity to claim a first major title.

The gap between Korda and Thitikul in the odds reflects the dominance of Korda's current form. But majors are played over 72 holes, and the course changes everyone's equation.

What's Next

First round tees off Thursday, June 4. Korda comes in as the favorite, the world's best player, and the one player in the field for whom a USWO win would complete something genuinely significant. The field at Riviera is the best in women's golf. Four days at one of California's most storied courses should produce a champion worthy of the setting.
