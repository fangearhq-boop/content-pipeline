McIlroy Brings Back-to-Back Masters Glory to the Memorial: Can He Finally Win at Muirfield Village?

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | June 2, 2026

Rory McIlroy is a two-time Masters champion. He is a four-time major winner. He is one of the most accomplished players of his generation and the current holder of golf's most coveted title in back-to-back fashion.

He has never won the Memorial Tournament.

That is the singular gap in McIlroy's relationship with Muirfield Village Golf Club, and it defines the storyline as he arrives in Dublin, Ohio for the week of June 4-7. He is +900 to win, and the absence of a win here — in an event with a $20 million purse and the fingerprints of Jack Nicklaus on every hole — is a detail that does not go unnoticed.

What He Has Done Everywhere Else

McIlroy won the 2026 Masters in one of the most dramatic finishes Augusta National has produced in years. He took a six-shot lead into the weekend, then watched it dissolve on Saturday before producing a final-round performance that came down to a bogey on the 72nd hole — good enough to beat Scottie Scheffler by one shot. He became only the fourth player in Masters history to win consecutive green jackets.

He then entered the PGA Championship hoping to make a run at the calendar Grand Slam. He finished five shots behind champion Aaron Rai. Since then, his schedule has been selective. The Memorial is only his eighth PGA Tour start of 2026.

The Scheffler Shadow

Scheffler and McIlroy have been the two dominant figures in professional golf through the first half of 2026. They battled at Augusta. They will be close to each other at Muirfield Village again this week. Scheffler is the defending two-time Memorial champion chasing the same three-peat Tiger Woods achieved from 1999 to 2001.

For McIlroy, beating Scheffler here would serve multiple purposes simultaneously: it would give him a first Memorial win, extend his 2026 season victories list beyond Augusta, and extend the most competitive rivalry in the sport.

What's Next

The first round of the Memorial Tournament tees off Thursday, June 4. McIlroy's return to competitive golf after a lighter schedule since the PGA Championship will reveal whether his game is where it needs to be for a push at this event. Muirfield Village demands precision off the tee and approach — the kind of golf that McIlroy produces at his best. Whether this week produces a maiden Memorial win is one of the most compelling questions of the 2026 PGA Tour season.
