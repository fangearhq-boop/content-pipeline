### Long-Form Post Viktor Hovland arrived at the John Deere Classic this week as one of the hottest players on the planet — and with a story that goes well beyond scorecards. On June 29, Hovland claimed the Travelers Championship at TPC River Highlands in a Monday playoff after Sunday's round was suspended by fading daylight. His final score of 21-under par (rounds of 65-61-64-69) was enough to outlast Scottie Scheffler, who had made a clutch 8-foot par putt in near-darkness just to force the extra hole — before missing a 4-foot birdie putt on the first playoff hole to hand Hovland the trophy. It was the Norwegian star's 8th career PGA Tour victory and his first since winning the 2025 Valspar Championship. He earned $3.6 million and 700 valuable FedExCup points. But perhaps the most meaningful part of the week for Hovland was personal. His mother, Galina, was watching from behind the 18th green — making this the first PGA Tour victory she had witnessed in person. That alone made the win unforgettable. Now Hovland is at TPC Deere Run with momentum and a chance to do something no player has done recently: win back-to-back on the Travelers-to-Deere stretch. History says it is difficult.
