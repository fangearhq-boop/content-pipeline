### STORY 2: Open Championship Countdown — Royal Birkdale Ready in 13 Days

**Post Window:** 10:30 AM ET


**Long-Form Post**
Thirteen days. That is all that separates the golf world from the 154th Open Championship at Royal Birkdale in Southport, England.

The storied links course is hosting The Open for the 11th time, and the R&A is projecting record-breaking attendance — CEO Mark Darbon expects more than 300,000 spectators for the week, potentially an all-time record. Over 1 million ticket applications were received.

Final Qualifying wrapped up on June 30 at four sites, with 20 spots up for grabs. James Nicholas stole the show at Burnham & Berrow, carding rounds of 65-67 (-10) to earn the medalist honors and, more importantly, his first-ever Open Championship berth. At West Lancashire, Wesley Bryan came agonizingly close — a missed 7-foot par putt on the 72nd hole cost him a spot by just one shot.

One final opportunity remains: a Last Chance Qualifier on Monday, July 13 at Royal Birkdale, with 12 players competing for a single coveted spot in the 156-man field.

Phil Mickelson has confirmed he will not be at Royal Birkdale. Tiger Woods, when asked about his return to competition, said only that "there is still some time" — leaving his Birkdale status unresolved.

### STORY 3: Viktor Hovland Enters Deere as Travelers Champion — Can He Go Back-to-Back?

**Post Window:** 1:00 PM ET


**Long-Form Post**
Viktor Hovland arrived at the John Deere Classic this week as one of the hottest players on the planet — and with a story that goes well beyond scorecards.

On June 29, Hovland claimed the Travelers Championship at TPC River Highlands in a Monday playoff after Sunday's round was suspended by fading daylight. His final score of 21-under par (rounds of 65-61-64-69) was enough to outlast Scottie Scheffler, who had made a clutch 8-foot par putt in near-darkness just to force the extra hole — before missing a 4-foot birdie putt on the first playoff hole to hand Hovland the trophy.

It was the Norwegian star's 8th career PGA Tour victory and his first since winning the 2025 Valspar Championship. He earned $3.6 million and 700 valuable FedExCup points.

But perhaps the most meaningful part of the week for Hovland was personal. His mother, Galina, was watching from behind the 18th green — making this the first PGA Tour victory she had witnessed in person. That alone made the win unforgettable.

Now Hovland is at TPC Deere Run with momentum and a chance to do something no player has done recently: win back-to-back on the Travelers-to-Deere stretch. History says it is difficult. Hovland's current form says he might not care.
