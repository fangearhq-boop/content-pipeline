John Deere Classic Round 2 Live: Davis Riley's Hole-in-One Steals the Show

By Ryan Calloway July 3, 2026 Golf Fanrecap

Davis Riley Makes History at TPC Deere Run

The 2026 John Deere Classic has barely started, but Davis Riley has already given fans the moment of the tournament. The PGA Tour veteran made his first career hole-in-one during Round 1 at TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois — a milestone that few professionals ever achieve on Tour. Riley's ace sent a jolt of energy through the grounds and gave the week an early storyline that will be talked about long after the final putt drops on Sunday.

Riley's ace is the kind of moment that transcends leaderboards. Whether he contends through the weekend or not, his name is stamped in the first chapter of this tournament's 2026 edition.

Zach Johnson Sets the Pace at -7

While the ace grabbed headlines, it was Zach Johnson who turned in the most complete round of golf on Thursday. The two-time major champion carded a 7-under 64 to sit tied for third place after Round 1, reminding everyone why the John Deere Classic holds a special place in his career. Johnson, who hails from nearby Cedar Rapids, Iowa, has always thrived at TPC Deere Run — and Thursday showed that connection has not faded.

Johnson's 64 included a clean, aggressive performance on the par-71 layout. He is within striking distance of the co-leaders heading into Round 2, and the Midwest crowd will be squarely behind him as the weekend approaches.

Amateur Preston Stout Makes a Statement

One of the more compelling subplots from Round 1 was the performance of amateur Preston Stout, who was contending in the top group after the opening 18 holes. Watching an amateur hold his ground against full-time Tour professionals at a PGA Tour event is rare, and Stout's showing has given fans an unexpected rooting interest heading into Friday's second round.

The amateur storyline adds a dimension to the week that pure leaderboard math cannot fully capture. Stout is playing with house money against seasoned veterans, and every hole he competes is a statement about his readiness for professional competition.

A Stronger-Than-Usual Field at the Deere

The 2026 John Deere Classic drew a notably strong field for an event that typically sits in the shadow of the Scottish Open and Open Championship prep week. Viktor Hovland, fresh off his Travelers Championship victory, is in the field looking for back-to-back wins. Jordan Spieth, Rickie Fowler, Sungjae Im, Daniel Berger, Chris Gotterup, and Ben Griffin — who entered as the betting favorite at +1325 — round out a roster that gives the week legitimate star power.

Jackson Koivun is also in the field making his official PGA Tour professional debut at +2700 odds, adding a rookie intrigue to the storylines fans can follow in Round 2.

Defending champion Brian Campbell is also in the field, looking to become just the second player to successfully defend at TPC Deere Run in recent memory.

Round 2 Is Underway — What to Watch

Round 2 at TPC Deere Run is live today, July 3. The par-71 layout plays as one of the more birdie-friendly stops on the PGA Tour calendar, meaning the leaderboard should continue to move aggressively. Scores in the mid-to-high teens under par are typically required to contend deep into the weekend.

Eyes will be on Hovland to see if the Travelers momentum carries forward, and on Zach Johnson and the co-leaders to see who separates from the pack. Davis Riley — fresh off his historic ace — will be looking to show that Round 1 was more than just a one-hole highlight.
