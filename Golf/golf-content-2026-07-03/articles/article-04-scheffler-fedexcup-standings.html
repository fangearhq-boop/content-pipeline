Scottie Scheffler Leads FedExCup Despite Travelers Heartbreak

By Ryan Calloway July 3, 2026 Golf Fanrecap

The Measure of a No. 1 Player

What separates a great player from everyone else is not just the trophies — it is what happens after the trophies slip away. Scottie Scheffler's 2026 season has included both kinds of moments. The world's top-ranked player missed a 4-foot birdie putt on the first hole of a Travelers Championship playoff, handing Viktor Hovland the title on June 29. One week later, Scheffler remains the No. 1 player in the 2026 FedExCup standings with 3,511 points.

That number tells the real story of Scheffler's season: consistent, dominant, relentless. No single missed putt — no matter how costly — has been enough to unseat him from the top of the standings.

The Travelers Loss — What Happened

The closing sequence at TPC River Highlands in Cromwell, Connecticut deserves a full accounting. With Sunday's final round racing against the fading daylight, Scheffler found himself in a position to force a playoff. He made an 8-foot par putt in near-darkness — a clutch stroke under immense pressure that extended his day and gave him a chance at the title. The gallery roared.

But the playoff hole, contested Monday morning, went differently. Scheffler stood over a 4-foot birdie putt — the kind of putt he drains dozens of times each season. He missed. Hovland converted, and the trophy went to Norway. Scheffler walked away with a runner-up finish, a reminder that golf punishes even the world's best at the worst moments.

The 2026 FedExCup Top 10

Despite the Travelers loss, Scheffler's season-long body of work has him comfortably atop the standings heading into the John Deere Classic week. Here is where the top 10 stands entering July 3:

  1. Scottie Scheffler — 3,511 points
  2. Rory McIlroy
  3. JJ Spaun
  4. Justin Rose
  5. Tommy Fleetwood
  6. Ben Griffin
  7. Russell Henley
  8. Sepp Straka
  9. Robert MacIntyre
  10. Maverick McNealy

Viktor Hovland's 700 FedExCup points from the Travelers win have him surging up the standings as well, putting him in a strong position to factor into the postseason picture as the regular season moves toward its conclusion.

The U.S. Open and a Season of Near-Misses

The Travelers playoff loss was not the only near-miss on Scheffler's 2026 ledger. At the U.S. Open, which was contested at Shinnecock Hills and won by Wyndham Clark at 4-under par, Scheffler tied for fourth. A T4 at a U.S. Open is an excellent result by any standard, but for a player of Scheffler's caliber, the gap between contending and lifting the trophy is always the one that matters most.

Taken together, Scheffler's results in 2026 paint the picture of a player who remains the standard — even in the weeks when someone else lifts the trophy.

Why the John Deere Matters This Week

Scheffler is not in the John Deere Classic field this week — many of the world's top players are resting or traveling to the United Kingdom ahead of the Open Championship at Royal Birkdale (July 16–19). But the Deere is still highly relevant to the FedExCup race. The winner this week earns a substantial points haul, potentially reshuffling the standings heading into the final stretch of the regular season.

Ben Griffin, listed as the betting favorite at +1325, is already in the FedExCup top 10 at No. 6. A Deere win would push him deeper into Scheffler's territory at the top of the standings. Hovland, coming off his Travelers win, is also in the field looking to add more points to his rapidly growing total.

What's Next

Scheffler is expected at The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale as one of the leading contenders for a third major title. The 154th Open begins July 16, with the FedExCup regular season still several weeks from completion.
