Open Championship Countdown: Royal Birkdale Ready in 13 Days

By Jake Torres July 3, 2026 Golf Fanrecap

The Clock Is Ticking at Royal Birkdale

Thirteen days. That is all that separates the golf world from the 154th Open Championship at Royal Birkdale Golf Club in Southport, England. The oldest major in the game returns to one of its most celebrated venues on July 16–19, 2026, and the buildup is already reaching fever pitch. Royal Birkdale is hosting The Open for the 11th time — a number that speaks to the R&A's enduring trust in this links masterpiece on the Lancashire coast.

With a 156-player field taking shape and qualifying drama still unfolding, the 2026 Open Championship is setting the table for what could be a historically memorable week.

Record Attendance on the Horizon

The commercial and cultural scale of this year's Open is remarkable even by the event's lofty standards. R&A CEO Mark Darbon has projected that attendance will exceed 300,000 for the week — a potential all-time record for The Open Championship. That projection is supported by the extraordinary demand surrounding the event: over 1 million ticket applications were received for the 2026 edition.

The combination of a beloved venue, a stacked field, and rising global interest in professional golf has created the conditions for a historic gathering on the Lancashire coast. If Darbon's projection holds, Royal Birkdale 2026 will enter The Open's record books before a single shot is struck in competition.

Final Qualifying: Drama at Every Site

Final Qualifying for the 154th Open Championship wrapped up on June 30 at four sites across England. Twenty spots were distributed across the qualifying events, with each site providing its own chapter of triumph and heartbreak.

The headline story from Final Qualifying came out of Burnham and Berrow, where James Nicholas delivered rounds of 65 and 67 for a 10-under total — good enough for medalist honors and, more importantly, his first-ever Open Championship berth. For Nicholas, it was the kind of performance that defines a career, a moment he will carry with him to the first tee at Royal Birkdale.

At West Lancashire, the story had a far more painful ending. Wesley Bryan came to the 72nd hole needing only a par putt from 7 feet to secure his spot in the field. He missed. That single stroke cost him a place in the Open by one shot — a brutal outcome that underscores how thin the margin is between playing golf's grandest stage and watching from home.

One Last Chance on July 13

The final path into the 156-man field remains open for 12 players. A Last Chance Qualifier will be held on Monday, July 13, at Royal Birkdale itself — just three days before the tournament begins. One spot will be awarded. Twelve players will compete for it. The proximity to the tournament, both in time and location, gives the Last Chance Qualifier an intensity unlike any other qualifying event in golf.

For whoever claims that final spot, stepping to the first tee at Royal Birkdale just days after earning the right to be there will be an experience unlike any other in the game.

Notable Absentees: Mickelson Out, Tiger Still Uncertain

Phil Mickelson has confirmed he will not be competing at Royal Birkdale. The six-time major champion's absence removes one of golf's most storied figures from a field that will nonetheless be packed with elite talent.

Tiger Woods, when asked about his return to competition, offered only that there is "still some time" before he returns — leaving his status for the 2026 Open Championship unresolved. Woods has not competed regularly on Tour in recent years, and any indication of his participation at a major draws immediate global attention. For now, the world is waiting.

What's Next

The 2026 John Deere Classic is wrapping up this week in Illinois, and many of the world's top players are either already resting or preparing to travel to the United Kingdom ahead of The Open. The Last Chance Qualifier on July 13 at Birkdale will close out the field officially, and then the 154th Open Championship will begin on Thursday, July 16. Golf Fanrecap will have full preview coverage, field analysis, and round-by-round updates from Royal Birkdale as the week approaches.
