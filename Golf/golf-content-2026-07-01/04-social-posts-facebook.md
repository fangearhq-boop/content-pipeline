### STORY 1: John Deere Classic Opens Tomorrow

**Long-Form Post**

The PGA Tour's summer stretch is officially underway.

Tomorrow, the 2026 John Deere Classic tees off at TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois — a tournament that has historically punched above its weight in terms of storylines and unexpected champions. This year is no different.

Ben Griffin heads the betting board as the favorite at +1500. Griffin has developed into one of the most reliable players on tour, and TPC Deere Run's receptive bentgrass greens and wide fairways suit his controlled iron game. Chris Gotterup, who has already claimed two wins in 2026 (the Sony Open and the WM Phoenix Open), checks in at +1600. At this pace, Gotterup is building toward one of the better individual seasons on tour.

The most intriguing name in the field might be 21-year-old Jackson Koivun, who makes his professional debut at +2000. He ranks No. 41 in the Data Golf Rankings before hitting a single professional shot. Debut week pressure is real, but the talent is not in question.

Jordan Spieth is back at a course that has been good to him — he won here in 2013 and 2015. At +3300, Spieth is not the favorite, but his course history and the fact that Deere Run rewards the short-game precision that defines his game means he's worth watching. Rickie Fowler (+4000) adds another recognizable name to a field that already includes Keegan Bradley, J.T. Poston, Daniel Berger, and Sungjae Im.

Defending champion Brian Campbell is in the field and will look to add a second consecutive JDC title to his résumé.

The purse is $8.8 million, with $1.584 million going to the winner and 500 FedExCup points on the table.

### STORY 2: Open Championship Field Set — 20 Final Qualifying Spots Filled

**Long-Form Post**

The 154th Open Championship field is nearly complete.

Final Qualifying took place yesterday across four venues — Burnham & Berrow, Dundonald Links, Royal Cinque Ports, and West Lancashire — with 288 players competing for the final 20 spots in the field at Royal Birkdale. Five players qualified from each site to reach the 156-player field for the year's final major.

Notable qualifiers include James Nicholas, who shot 10-under at Burnham & Berrow to claim his Open berth, and Baard Bjoernevik Skogen, who posted 12-under at Royal Cinque Ports alongside MJ Daffue. Peter Uihlein qualified at 9-under. Two amateurs also punched their tickets, continuing the tradition of unpaid golfers earning their place among the world's elite.

One spot remains. The Last-Chance Qualifier at Royal Birkdale on July 13 gives 12 players a final opportunity to enter the field on the very course where the major will be played three days later.

The field that has taken shape for Royal Birkdale is formidable. Scottie Scheffler returns as defending champion — he won the 2025 Open at Royal Portrush by four shots. Rory McIlroy brings major championship pedigree and the motivation that comes from competing on links terrain where he has excelled. Jon Rahm, Xander Schauffele, and Matt Fitzpatrick (the 2022 US Open champion) add major-winning experience.

Tommy Fleetwood brings something else: home advantage. Fleetwood grew up in Southport — the town where Royal Birkdale sits. His emotional connection to this particular venue is different from any other player in the field. The 2025 FedEx Cup winner has never won The Open; if he's ever going to, this is the week.

Wyndham Clark, who won the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills, arrives at Royal Birkdale with fresh major momentum.

Tiger Woods is not entered. Phil Mickelson withdrew earlier in the process.

The Open begins July 16.

### STORY 3: LIV Golf Funding Crisis Deepens — Last Two Events at Risk

**Long-Form Post**

The financial numbers behind LIV Golf's 2026 season have come into focus — and they are concerning.

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF) announced it will fund LIV Golf only for the remainder of the 2026 season, stating that the "substantial investment required by LIV Golf over a longer term is no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy." But funding the remainder of the season has proven more difficult in practice than in principle.

LIV Golf needs approximately $600 million to complete its 2026 schedule. PIF has delivered just under $200 million — $66 million in early May and $130 million in early June. That leaves roughly $400 million outstanding, with four events remaining on the calendar beginning July 23-26 in the United Kingdom.

Multiple insiders familiar with the situation have told reporters that the LIV Golf UK event and the Bedminster event are expected to proceed as planned. The final two events in the last two weeks of August are a different story — their viability depends on additional funding being made available, and that funding has not arrived.

LIV Golf has responded by installing new board leadership, with Gene Davis and Jon Zinman joining as independent directors. The league has hired US investment banking firm Ducera Partners to help attract outside capital, seeking $250 to $350 million to replace the PIF backing that is winding down.

LIV CEO Greg O'Neil has insisted the season will continue. But insiders paint a different picture of a league that has spent more than $5 billion since launch, running annual losses in the range of $500 to $600 million, now seeking a financial lifeline.

### STORY 4: Genesis Scottish Open — Six Top-10 Players for the Perfect Open Warm-Up

**Long-Form Post**

One week before The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale, the world's best golfers will gather at The Renaissance Club in East Lothian for the Genesis Scottish Open.

What makes this year's Scottish Open field exceptional is the density at the top. Six of the world's top 10 ranked players have committed: Scottie Scheffler (No. 1), Rory McIlroy (No. 2), Jon Rahm, Matt Fitzpatrick, Chris Gotterup, and Wyndham Clark — who won the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills earlier this summer. Chris Gotterup, the defending Scottish Open champion, makes an appearance seeking to defend.

The appeal of The Renaissance Club is strategic as well as practical. Tom Doak's design features the firm, fast links conditions that reward bump-and-run approaches, precise iron play into small targets, and wind management — skills directly transferable to Royal Birkdale the following week. Players who compete well at Renaissance tend to arrive at The Open with their links game sharp and their mindset calibrated for the conditions.

Robert MacIntyre represents the Scottish home interest. The crowd will be behind him from the first tee. Tommy Fleetwood, Tyrrell Hatton, and Ludvig Åberg add European depth to a field that also includes Justin Thomas, Xander Schauffele, Aaron Rai, and Min Woo Lee.

The Genesis Scottish Open carries a $9 million purse and is co-sanctioned by the DP World Tour and PGA Tour as a Rolex Series event — one of the highest categories of non-major events in world golf.

### STORY 5: Amundi Evian Championship — LPGA's Next Major Opens July 9 in France

**Long-Form Post**

Eight days from now, the Amundi Evian Championship begins on the shores of Lake Geneva, and the LPGA's major championship season resumes after Haeran Ryu's breakthrough win at the KPMG Women's PGA.

The Evian Resort Golf Club in Evian-les-Bains, France, is one of the most picturesque major championship venues in women's golf. Nestled between Lake Geneva and the French Alps, it provides a backdrop unlike anything else on the LPGA schedule. The championship runs July 9-12.

Grace Kim won the 2025 title in dramatic fashion — holing an eagle on the 72nd hole to force a playoff and then winning it. The Australian became one of the most memorable Evian champions in the tournament's modern major era. Defending that title on a course that produced such a defining moment will add an extra layer of significance to her week.

The 2026 LPGA season arrives at Evian having already produced two major champions. Nelly Korda won the Chevron Championship and the US Women's Open in the spring before a disappointing T8 at the KPMG ended her bid for a historic third consecutive major title. Haeran Ryu stepped up at Hazeltine, winning by two shots with a composed final-round 65.

The 2026 season is also historic for the LPGA in a structural sense: every round of every event is being televised live — a milestone that has never been achieved in the tour's history since Golf Channel began covering them in 1995. The Evian Championship is part of a season that is more accessible to fans than any before it.

The next LPGA major after Evian is the AIG Women's Open, July 30 to August 2.
