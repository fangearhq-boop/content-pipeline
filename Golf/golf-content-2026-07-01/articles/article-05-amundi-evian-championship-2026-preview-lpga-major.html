Amundi Evian Championship 2026: Grace Kim Defends at Golf's Most Scenic Major, Opening July 9

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 1, 2026

Eight days from now, the LPGA Tour's next major championship begins in one of world golf's most extraordinary settings. The Amundi Evian Championship at the Evian Resort Golf Club in Evian-les-Bains, France — perched between Lake Geneva and the French Alps — runs July 9-12 and delivers the LPGA calendar's fifth major of the season.

Grace Kim defends. The Australian won the 2025 title in a moment that will be replayed for years: a clutch eagle on the par-five 72nd hole to force a playoff, then winning the playoff. Her path to the title was dramatic in the truest sense.

The LPGA's Major Championship Context

The 2026 LPGA season has already produced two memorable major performances heading into France. Nelly Korda won both the Chevron Championship and the US Women's Open in the spring — a stunning back-to-back that set up the possibility of three consecutive major titles. Only Babe Zaharias in 1950 and Inbee Park in 2013 had ever won three consecutive LPGA majors. But Korda finished tied for eighth at the KPMG Women's PGA, shooting a closing 73 that halted her momentum at the worst possible moment.

Haeran Ryu claimed the KPMG Women's PGA title at Hazeltine National in Chaska, Minnesota — winning at 13-under par with a two-shot victory over Ina Yoon. It was Ryu's first career major championship. Coming into Evian, the LPGA has two fresh champions and a field searching for the next signature moment of the season.

Evian Resort Golf Club

The venue itself is part of the story. The Evian Resort Golf Club is not just visually extraordinary — the course design rewards the kind of patient, strategic play that defines the best major championship golf. Players must navigate elevation changes, firm fairways, and targets that demand precision rather than power. The European flavor of the event and its international television audience make it one of the LPGA Tour's most globally watched weeks.

A Historic Season for the LPGA

The 2026 season carries an additional milestone worth noting. For the first time since Golf Channel began broadcasting the LPGA Tour in 1995, every round of every event is being televised live. That means the Evian Championship — along with every other event on the schedule — is available to fans without the blackout windows that historically fragmented coverage. The expanded reach reflects the LPGA's growth and investment in its broadcast presence.

What's Next

The Amundi Evian Championship begins July 9 and concludes July 12. The next LPGA major after Evian is the AIG Women's Open, running July 30 to August 2 in England. The major championship pipeline runs without interruption through the heart of the summer. For Grace Kim, the defense of a title she won in one of the most memorable ways possible begins in eight days on the shores of Lake Geneva.
