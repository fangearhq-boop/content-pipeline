Genesis Scottish Open 2026: Six Top-10 Players, One Week Before the Open Championship

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 1, 2026

The week before The Open Championship has become one of the most important tournaments in world golf — not despite its proximity to a major, but because of it. The Genesis Scottish Open at The Renaissance Club in East Lothian, Scotland, runs July 9-12, and this year's field is exceptional.

Six of the world's top 10 ranked players have committed: Scottie Scheffler (No. 1), Rory McIlroy (No. 2), Jon Rahm, Matt Fitzpatrick, Chris Gotterup, and Wyndham Clark — who won the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills just weeks ago. Chris Gotterup is the defending Scottish Open champion, looking to become one of the few players to win consecutive editions of this event.

Why the Field Is This Strong

The reasoning is straightforward: The Renaissance Club and Royal Birkdale share the same essential vocabulary. Both are links courses. Both require players to think creatively about shot shape, trajectory, and the ground game. Players who spend four competitive rounds at East Lothian arrive at Birkdale with calibrated links instincts, prepared for the conditions that distinguish golf's oldest major from every other tournament on the schedule.

The $9 million purse — co-sanctioned by the DP World Tour and PGA Tour as a Rolex Series event, the highest tier outside of majors and the FedEx Cup Playoffs — adds sufficient financial motivation to attract elite fields even before the Open preparation calculus is considered.

The Full Lineup

Beyond the top six, the field deepens substantially. Xander Schauffele, Tommy Fleetwood, Tyrrell Hatton, and Ludvig Åberg bring Ryder Cup-caliber European experience. Justin Thomas, Ben Griffin, and Billy Horschel represent the American contingent. Aaron Rai, a former Scottish Open champion, knows exactly what it takes to win at Renaissance. Min Woo Lee, who won at The Renaissance Club in 2021, will be a crowd favorite once again.

Robert MacIntyre adds the Scottish home-crowd dynamic. MacIntyre has developed into one of the most consistent European players in the game, and Silverburn — just down the road from his Highland roots — delivers him with a crowd that treats his rounds like a national occasion.

Patrick Reed's Presence

Patrick Reed, listed as the Race to Dubai leader, is also in the field. Reed's competitive intensity on any course where he's confident is formidable, and links golf has historically suited his game.

What's Next

The Genesis Scottish Open runs July 9-12. The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale begins July 16. Players who finish well at Renaissance on Sunday will load onto the bus for Birkdale on Monday with momentum, links confidence, and four competitive rounds of preparation. That's the week that matters — and this week makes it possible.
