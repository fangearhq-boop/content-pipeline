The Open Championship Field Is Nearly Set: 20 Final Qualifying Spots Filled for Royal Birkdale

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 1, 2026

The 154th Open Championship field for Royal Birkdale is nearly complete. Yesterday's Final Qualifying across four venues added 20 players to the 156-person field for golf's oldest major championship, scheduled for July 16-19 in Southport, England.

One spot remains — to be decided by a Last-Chance Qualifier at Royal Birkdale itself on July 13. Then the field is set, and one of golf's most anticipated major weekends begins.

How Final Qualifying Unfolded

288 players competed across four venues — Burnham & Berrow, Dundonald Links, Royal Cinque Ports, and West Lancashire — for five qualifying spots each. The standard was exceptional at several sites. Baard Bjoernevik Skogen and MJ Daffue both shot 12-under at Royal Cinque Ports to share the top spot there. James Nicholas posted 10-under at Burnham & Berrow. Peter Uihlein qualified at Royal Cinque Ports at 9-under.

Two amateurs earned their Open Championship berths — a reminder that golf's oldest major has always been open, in the fullest sense of the word, to players who earn their spot through performance rather than exemption.

The Stars Already In the Field

The major names arrived through exemptions well before Final Qualifying. Scottie Scheffler returns as defending champion — he won the 2025 Open at Royal Portrush by four shots and arrives at Birkdale with the confidence of a player who has proven he can win on links terrain at the highest level.

Rory McIlroy brings the weight of expectations. A four-time major champion who grew up watching The Open, McIlroy on links terrain is as dangerous as anyone in the world. Jon Rahm, Xander Schauffele, and Matt Fitzpatrick — the 2022 US Open champion — all carry major-winning experience and links game credibility.

Wyndham Clark won the 2026 US Open at Shinnecock Hills earlier this summer, arriving at Birkdale on a wave of momentum that tends to carry players deep into their next major start.

Tommy Fleetwood and the Hometown Factor

No player carries a more emotionally specific stake in the Royal Birkdale Open than Tommy Fleetwood. Fleetwood grew up in Southport — the town where Royal Birkdale Golf Club sits. The gallery he will hear cheering on his first tee shot in the first round is, in a very real sense, his community. Fleetwood won the FedEx Cup in 2025. He has never won a major. This is the most meaningful opportunity he will ever have to change that.

Absent Names

Tiger Woods is not entered in the 154th Open. Phil Mickelson withdrew earlier in the qualifying process. The two most significant names in the sport over the past two decades will not be in the field at Birkdale.

What's Next

The Last-Chance Qualifier at Royal Birkdale on July 13 fills the final spot. The Genesis Scottish Open at The Renaissance Club runs July 9-12 — a direct links warm-up for the players in the Open field. Then Royal Birkdale opens for its first major since 2017. The 154th Open Championship begins July 16.
