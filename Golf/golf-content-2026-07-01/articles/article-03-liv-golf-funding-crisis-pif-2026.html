LIV Golf's Funding Crisis Is Real: PIF Delivered One-Third of What's Needed, Final Events at Risk

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 1, 2026

The numbers behind LIV Golf's 2026 season have become impossible to ignore. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — the primary financial backer of the breakaway league since its 2022 launch — has delivered approximately $200 million of the $600 million LIV Golf needs to complete its 2026 season. That's one-third of the required budget, with four events remaining on the calendar.

The final two events of the 2026 season, scheduled for late August, are reportedly dependent on additional funding that has not arrived. The immediate next events — LIV Golf UK (July 23-26) and the Bedminster event — are expected to proceed, according to people familiar with the situation. Beyond that, the picture is uncertain.

How the Numbers Break Down

PIF's 2026 payments to LIV Golf have arrived in two tranches: $66 million in early May and $130 million in early June — a combined total of roughly $196 million. Against a $600 million operational requirement for the full season, that leaves approximately $400 million outstanding. The PIF itself has signaled this is not a timing issue. The fund released a statement indicating that the "substantial investment required by LIV Golf over a longer term is no longer consistent with the current phase of PIF's investment strategy."

That phrase — "longer term" — is significant. It implies a terminal decision: PIF is funding 2026 as a wind-down, not a continuation.

LIV's Response

The league has not conceded the field. New board leadership was installed, with Gene Davis and Jon Zinman joining as independent directors. LIV hired US investment banking firm Ducera Partners to lead the search for alternative capital — seeking $250 to $350 million from outside investors.

CEO Greg O'Neil has maintained publicly that the season will continue and that the league is in conversations with prospective investors. He has said to take PIF "at their word" that they will fund the remainder of the year. But the gap between $200 million delivered and $600 million needed is too large to paper over with confidence.

The Broader Picture

LIV Golf has spent more than $5 billion since its launch, running annual losses in the range of $500 to $600 million. The league operates with 57 players, a 72-hole format, and a team-competition overlay that has attracted investment interest but not the television rights fees and commercial partnerships needed to sustain itself independently.

Earlier in the season, the New Orleans event was postponed — citing scheduling conflicts with the FIFA World Cup — adding operational fragility to the financial concerns. The $40 million Team Championship in Michigan remains on the schedule.

What's Next

The LIV Golf UK event runs July 23-26. Bedminster follows. Then comes the two late-August events whose future depends on a funding solution that has not yet materialized. For LIV players, the landscape is shifting in real time. For golf's broader ecosystem, the question of whether PGA Tour–LIV reconciliation becomes more likely as LIV's leverage decreases is being asked with increasing frequency.
