John Deere Classic 2026: Spieth's History, Koivun's Debut, and a $8.8M Field at TPC Deere Run

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 1, 2026

The John Deere Classic opens tomorrow at TPC Deere Run in Silvis, Illinois — a tournament that routinely produces compelling narratives despite competing against the offseason noise of the sport's bigger events. The 2026 edition has storylines in abundance.

The purse stands at $8.8 million, with $1.584 million going to the winner and 500 FedExCup points on the line as the race toward the postseason intensifies. The field includes nine past champions and eight players ranked inside the top 50 of the Official World Golf Ranking.

The Favorites

Ben Griffin opens as the betting favorite at +1500. The consistent tour member has developed into one of the more reliable iron players on tour, and TPC Deere Run's demand for precision into receptive bentgrass greens suits his game profile. Griffin's reliability without a signature moment makes him the type of quiet favorite who gets results at events like this one.

Chris Gotterup (+1600) arrives as perhaps the hottest player in the field. Gotterup has already won twice in 2026 — at the Sony Open and the WM Phoenix Open — and his aggressive ball-striking is well-suited to a birdie-fest track. Keith Mitchell checks in at +2200, providing a longer-odds alternative with the power to handle Deere Run's demands.

Spieth's Return to a Friendly Course

Jordan Spieth arrives at +3300 with the kind of course history that makes him an undervalued play on the betting board. Spieth won the John Deere Classic in 2013 and 2015 — a track that rewards his short-game precision and his ability to manufacture birdies from unconventional positions. Since his 2015 win, he has competed at Deere Run intermittently, and his familiarity with the course's subtle demands gives him an edge that raw statistics might not capture.

Rickie Fowler (+4000) joins Spieth in the marquee names bracket, adding a recognizable face to what is already a strong weekend gate draw in Silvis.

The Rookie to Watch

Perhaps no player entering the field generates more curiosity than 21-year-old Jackson Koivun, making his professional debut at +2000. Koivun exits his amateur career as one of the most decorated players the collegiate game has produced — the 2024 winner of the Haskins Award, Jack Nicklaus Award, Ben Hogan Award, and Phil Mickelson Award, a sweep of college golf's four major individual honors in a single year. He ranks No. 41 in the Data Golf Rankings — an exceptional starting position for a player yet to hit a professional shot.

The pressure of a professional debut is different from any amateur competition, but the talent Koivun brings to Silvis this week is not in question. If he plays anywhere close to his ceiling, he is capable of contending at TPC Deere Run.

What's Next

The John Deere Classic runs July 2-5. Defending champion Brian Campbell will look to defend his title. The winner earns $1.584 million and 500 FedExCup points, and takes an important step toward postseason security. First tee shots are Thursday morning. The leaderboard takes shape from there.
