Scheffler Is One Back. Eric Cole Has the Lead. The Travelers Just Got Interesting.

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | June 26, 2026

Eric Cole walked off TPC River Highlands on Thursday with a 63 on his scorecard and a one-shot lead at the Travelers Championship. It was one of the best opening rounds in this tournament's modern history. Now he has to hold it against the best player in the world.

Scottie Scheffler carded a 64 in Round 1 — steady, precise, relentless — and enters Round 2 exactly one stroke behind Cole. For a player who has spent 157 consecutive weeks as the world's No. 1 ranked golfer, Scheffler has the look of someone who knows exactly where he is and exactly what he needs to do. Cole has a lead. Scheffler has a target.

Cole's Historic Round

Eric Cole's 63 was not a round built on luck. He hit fairways, found greens, and made putts — the formula that has worked at TPC River Highlands since the Travelers moved to this venue. Cole played the course as it was designed to be played: with precision off the tee and aggressive iron play into receptive greens.

The round put Cole at -7 through 18 holes, matching the low end of what this course typically allows in ideal conditions. It was the kind of round that sets a tone for a tournament — and the kind of round that forces every player behind you to play a little harder, a little faster.

For Cole, it represents an opportunity at one of the PGA Tour's premier events. He does not carry Scheffler's résumé or Bradley's local legend status at this event. But for 18 holes on Thursday, he was the best player on the course.

Scheffler's Familiar Position

Scheffler's 2026 season has been simultaneously dominant and, by his own extraordinary standards, incomplete. Eight top-5 finishes in thirteen starts. One win — the American Express back in January. For anyone else in professional golf, those numbers represent an exceptional year. For Scheffler, they represent an unfinished story.

He is 157 consecutive weeks at World No. 1. He finished T-2 at the Masters. He has been the best player in virtually every field he has entered this season. And yet the victories have not come at the pace the broader statistics suggest they should.

TPC River Highlands presents Scheffler with a chance to change that. A one-shot deficit after 18 holes is, for a player of his caliber, not a deficit at all. It is an invitation.

Bradley Playing for History

Keegan Bradley sits within striking distance at -5 after Round 1, and the defending champion carries the added weight of a Connecticut homecoming. Bradley won the Travelers in 2023, then defended successfully in 2025. He is the only player in recent memory who has made this event feel like a personal property.

A third Travelers title would place Bradley in the conversation for most meaningful relationship between a player and a single event in recent PGA Tour history. The crowd at TPC River Highlands treats him accordingly — the roars for his birdie putts carry a different quality than they do for visiting stars.

At -5, Bradley is positioned to contend. He knows this course better than any player in the field. Experience and local knowledge matter here.

Clark's Resilient Start

Wyndham Clark arrived at the Travelers as the reigning US Open champion, having won at Shinnecock Hills wire-to-wire less than three weeks ago. His Round 1 at TPC River Highlands was not the statement his credentials suggested, but it was a demonstration of the resilience that defines his game.

Clark made triple bogey on hole 12 — a number that could have broken a lesser player's spirit in a tournament round. Instead, he steadied himself and finished at -2. He is five back of Cole entering Round 2, but 54 holes remain, and TPC River Highlands is a course that gives back birdies readily enough for a quality player to make up ground.

Clark at -2 is not in position to win the Travelers — not yet. But he is very much alive.

What's Next

Round 2 at TPC River Highlands will define the tournament's shape. Cole will face the specific challenge of protecting a lead against a field that includes both the world's best player and a two-time defending champion who knows every inch of the course.

If Scheffler plays his standard Round 2 — fairways, greens, minimal mistakes — he will almost certainly be within one or two shots of the lead by afternoon. Bradley will do the same, letting the course and the crowd carry him.

The Travelers Championship is four rounds. But the conversation right now is simple: can Eric Cole hold on against Scottie Scheffler? Through 18 holes, the answer has been yes by one. Through 36, the answer gets harder.
