Valero Texas Open Round 1 Suspended by Weather — Final Masters Invite Hangs in Balance

By Ryan Calloway April 3, 2026

Waiting Out the Storm

TPC San Antonio was supposed to be in full tournament mode by Thursday. Instead, the weather arrived first. Round 1 of the Valero Texas Open was suspended due to adverse conditions, pushing the leaderboard into Friday and compressing the already dramatic timeline of the last PGA Tour event before the Masters.

The delay does not reduce what is at stake here — if anything, it makes the tension sharper. The Valero Texas Open winner earns an automatic invitation to Augusta National for the 90th Masters, which begins in just six days. Every player in the field knows it. The weather delay means one more day of waiting, one more day of maintaining focus before the most consequential tee shot of anyone's pre-Masters week.

The Favorites and the Field

Tommy Fleetwood, the World No. 1, is the co-favorite at +1400 alongside Ludvig Åberg. It is a notable circumstance: the best-ranked player in the world is competing at the final Masters qualifier, a fact that reflects the depth of the 2026 Valero field. Fleetwood has been one of the most consistent performers in professional golf over the last two seasons, having claimed the FedEx Cup in 2025, but the Masters has remained an elusive prize. A Valero win that earns him a Masters invitation would set up a consequential Augusta week.

Jordan Spieth, the 2021 Valero champion, enters at +1600. His course history is the best in the field, and Augusta familiarity makes his run here meaningful beyond just the invitation — Spieth's Masters record includes two wins and multiple near-misses, and arriving at Augusta with form built at TPC San Antonio is a route he has used before.

The Bubble

The most compelling stories in the Valero field belong to the players who need the win most. Rickie Fowler (world ranking No. 65) is in strong current form, and the Masters has been the major that has defined his career — not because he has won it, but because five top-5 finishes have produced zero green jackets. Tony Finau (No. 107) may have the best Augusta-specific record of any bubble player, with four top-10 finishes at Augusta. Tom Kim (No. 126) was a sensation at Augusta in 2022 at age 20 before missing the cut in subsequent appearances.

Will Zalatoris (No. 299) is the most remarkable storyline. After career-derailing back surgery that dropped him to near the bottom of the world rankings, the Valero is his comeback tournament. A win would not just earn him a Masters invitation — it would be one of the more stirring return-from-injury stories in recent PGA Tour history. Billy Horschel (No. 94) is the steady professional who rounds out the bubble group, underrated and capable of making a weekend run when conditions demand precision over power.

What's Next

Play resumes Friday as the weather clears and the Valero leaderboard begins to form in earnest. The tournament runs through Sunday, April 5, with Augusta beginning Thursday, April 9. The Valero winner will have less than a week to shift from Texas to Georgia — but that is the prize. One win. One ticket to the Masters. The weather delayed the drama. It could not stop it.
