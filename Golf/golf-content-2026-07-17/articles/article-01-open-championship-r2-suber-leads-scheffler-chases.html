Open Championship R2: Suber Leads as Scheffler Surges Into Contention at Royal Birkdale

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 17, 2026

Jackson Suber walked off the 18th green at Royal Birkdale on Thursday afternoon with a scorecard that turned heads across the golf world. A 65 — seven under par — in the afternoon wave of the 154th Open Championship put him at the top of the leaderboard and set up one of the more compelling Friday narratives in recent major history: the unknown chasing down nobody, and everybody chasing him.

Suber leads the Open Championship at -5 through Round 1. Behind him sit Sungjae Im and Daniel Brown at -4, followed by a cluster of major contenders at -3 — Bryson DeChambeau, Cameron Young, Robert MacIntyre, and Thomas Detry among them. Collin Morikawa and Scottie Scheffler, the defending champion, sit at -2.

Three shots back. In a major. Scheffler has made up bigger deficits in less time.

The Round That Started It All

Suber's 65 arrived in conditions that his leaderboard peers in the morning wave didn't face. Afternoon groups at Royal Birkdale on Thursday dealt with shifts in wind and firmer, faster greens — the kind of setup that typically produces higher scores. Suber produced the opposite, and that context makes the round more impressive, not less.

It was a performance that forced the golf world to take notice of a name it hadn't spent much time on before Thursday. Open Championship courses have a long history of delivering unlikely heroes, and Birkdale appears intent on continuing that tradition in 2026.

Scheffler: The Defending Champion Responds

For Scottie Scheffler, the Open Championship began as a statement. After missing the cut at the 2026 Genesis Scottish Open — a rare stumble for the world No. 1 — he arrived at Royal Birkdale with something to prove.

His opening round told the story clearly. Four birdies in his first six holes put him in full control of his round and his tournament. Bogeys at 7 and 17 trimmed the round to -2, but the foundation was there: Scheffler found his game on one of the most demanding courses in championship golf, and he found it fast.

He entered Round 2 paired with Tyrrell Hatton and Bryson DeChambeau — a group that will draw galleries all day Friday and generate the kind of atmosphere that Scheffler has navigated before, including when he won this championship as the reigning world No. 1.

Multiple outlets framed Friday's Round 2 with the same headline: "Scheffler chases underdog Suber." Three shots is the gap. A full round of golf remains.

The Field Within Striking Distance

The leaderboard entering Round 2 is not a two-horse race. Sungjae Im and Daniel Brown at -4 are one shot off the lead. DeChambeau, Young, MacIntyre, and Detry at -3 are two back. Morikawa, a two-time major champion, sits at -2 alongside Scheffler.

Any one of eight or nine players could realistically hold the 54-hole lead come Saturday morning. That kind of clustering heading into cut day typically produces the most unpredictable golf of the tournament week — and Royal Birkdale is exactly the kind of course that rewards bold play and punishes caution.

The Cut Line Adds a Layer

Not every story at Royal Birkdale on Friday belongs to the leaders. The projected cut entering Round 2 sat near +1, meaning that players through the field are fighting to make the weekend. With 156 golfers trimming to the top 70 and ties, major champions and familiar names find themselves in uncomfortable territory.

Birkdale's conditions — the rough, the wind, the pace of the greens — can shift a scorecard quickly in either direction. A birdie run can swing a player from the cut line into contention. A rough patch can do the opposite.

What's Next

Round 2 runs throughout Friday, with the cut being made once the full field has completed play. Moving Day — Round 3 on Saturday — follows for those who survive. The 54-hole leaders will set the Sunday draw, and whoever holds the advantage heading into the final round will carry the weight of a major championship at one of golf's most storied venues.

Suber leads. Scheffler is chasing. The 154th Open Championship at Royal Birkdale is fully, unmistakably alive.
