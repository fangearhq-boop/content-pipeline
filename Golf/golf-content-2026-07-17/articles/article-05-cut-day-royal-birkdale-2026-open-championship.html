Cut Day at Royal Birkdale: Big Names Battle the Line at the 154th Open Championship

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 17, 2026

There is no gentle way to miss the cut at The Open Championship. The elimination is public, the tournament continues without you, and the next major is months away. For the players inside Royal Birkdale's ropes on Friday who found themselves near the +1 projected cut line, the day carried a specific kind of urgency that the leaderboard leaders — safely inside the top 70 — did not have to manage.

Round 2 is cut day at the 154th Open Championship. The field trims from 156 golfers to the top 70 plus ties advancing to Moving Day. The projected cut entering Friday sat near +1, which meant that players at even par or better were safely positioned, while those at +2 or worse were already in danger before they hit their first tee shot.

Birkdale as a Separator

Royal Birkdale has a well-deserved reputation as one of the most honest tests in championship golf. Unlike some links venues that can reward fortune over skill, Birkdale's layout — wide corridors off the tee, demanding rough, firm and fast greens — tends to separate good ball-strikers from the field in ways that hold up through four rounds.

The rough at a July Open Championship is typically at its most penal, and Birkdale's is no exception. Players who miss fairways pay for it in distance, angle, and the ability to control approach shots. The difference between a ball in the fairway and one in the rough can easily swing two or three strokes over 18 holes — the exact margin that separates weekend golfers from Monday morning flights home.

The Safe Side of the Cut

For the players who entered Round 2 in the top portion of the field, Friday was about improving position, not survival. Jackson Suber at -5, Sungjae Im and Daniel Brown at -4, and the cluster at -3 — including Bryson DeChambeau, Cameron Young, Robert MacIntyre, and Thomas Detry — all held significant cushion over the cut line.

Scottie Scheffler and Collin Morikawa at -2 were also comfortably placed, focused entirely on gaining ground on the leader rather than surviving the cut. For those players, Round 2 was an opportunity. For those further down the field, it was a threat.

The Conditions Factor

Open Championship cut days are particularly dramatic because the conditions at a links course can change dramatically between morning and afternoon groups. Wind direction shifts, greens firm up as the day progresses, and the psychological pressure of watching the cut line move in real time adds a variable that stroke play in benign conditions doesn't replicate.

A player who goes out in the morning and shoots -1 might return to the locker room comfortably inside the cut, only to watch afternoon conditions deteriorate and the line drop to even par or better. Conversely, afternoon groups who know they need to post under par on a softening course have information the morning starters didn't.

Royal Birkdale in July provides no guarantees about which groups got the better draw. The course, the wind, and the timing all contribute to a result that is never fully predictable.

What Cut Day Means for the Weekend

The players who survive Friday's cut carry momentum — or the relief of survival — into Saturday's Moving Day. The psychological landscape shifts when the field compresses from 156 to 70-plus-ties: everyone remaining has earned their spot, and the pressure of elimination gives way to the pressure of competing for a championship.

For the 154th Open Championship, Saturday sets the 54-hole stage before Sunday's final round at Royal Birkdale. The leaders will be established, the chasers will know their targets, and the conversation will shift from who survived to who can win.

What's Next

Once Round 2 is complete and the cut is official, the Open Championship becomes a race rather than a survival exercise. Moving Day on Saturday determines the final-round groupings, and whoever holds the lead through 54 holes enters Sunday carrying the weight of the Claret Jug's possibility.

Royal Birkdale has delivered drama through the cut. The expectation is that it will deliver more of the same through the weekend.
