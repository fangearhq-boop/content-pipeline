### STORY 3: LIV Golf Financial Crisis

**Long-Form Post (1:00 PM ET)**
While the world's best golfers compete at The Open, the sport's biggest financial drama is unfolding off the leaderboard.

LIV Golf needs $600 million to complete its 2026 season. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund has delivered approximately $200 million — roughly a third of what's needed. To bridge the gap, PIF filed a debenture agreement in June, effectively shifting from investor to lender. LIV Golf isn't receiving investment money. It's operating on loans.

Four events remain on the 2026 LIV calendar: LIV Golf UK at JCB Golf and Country Club in Rocester, England (July 23-26), followed by three US events in August. Multiple insiders told Sportico and other outlets that PIF could cut monthly payments before the season ends.

The tour has approached hundreds of prospective investors and hired US investment bank Ducera Partners. Two independent board directors were added. No new investment has been announced.

PIF confirmed publicly in April that it would finance LIV only through the end of 2026. It has reportedly invested more than $6 billion into LIV since 2022.

The first of the four remaining events is 6 days away. The math doesn't work without a deal or a miracle.
