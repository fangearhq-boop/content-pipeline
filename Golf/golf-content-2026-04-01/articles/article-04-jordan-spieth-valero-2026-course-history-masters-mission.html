Jordan Spieth Returns to Valero — Where He Won in 2021 — With Augusta in Mind

April 1, 2026 — Golf Fanrecap

Jordan Spieth has unfinished business at TPC San Antonio — and he plans to collect on it.

Spieth won the Valero Texas Open in 2021. He knows the Oaks Course's rhythm, its scoring opportunities, its demands on a player's creativity with wedges and irons into elevated greens. When the field tees off Thursday, Spieth will carry that history with him. His +1600 odds to win reflect that — placing him alongside Russell Henley as a co-favorite just behind Tommy Fleetwood and Ludvig Åberg at +1500.

A Proven Course Record

TPC San Antonio rewards a certain type of player: someone who hits the ball well enough to set up short irons into greens, manages the par-5s efficiently, and avoids the penalty strokes that come with wayward drives on a course that punishes the rough. Spieth's game — patient, creative, exceptional around the greens — is well-suited to this environment.

His 2021 victory came during a period when his game was returning to form after an extended stretch of inconsistency. That win at Valero preceded a string of strong performances and reinforced that, on the right course with the right conditions, Spieth remains one of the most dangerous players in the world.

This Week Is About Augusta

Spieth is already exempt for the Masters. A Valero win would not be necessary for his Augusta invitation. What this week is about, for Spieth, is form.

He won his first Masters in 2015 in dominant fashion, posting a score that stands as one of the best 72-hole performances in Augusta National history. He has come close since — most memorably in 2016, when he led by five and collapsed on the back nine — but has not recaptured that same command at Augusta.

A strong week at a course he knows and trusts builds the competitive confidence that major championship golf demands. Even a top-5 or top-10 finish would send Spieth to Augusta with a different energy than if he spent this week at home.

The Larger Valero Picture

For several other players, Valero carries higher immediate stakes than it does for Spieth — particularly those fighting for the final Masters invitation awarded to the tournament winner. Rickie Fowler (world No. 65), Billy Horschel (No. 94), and Will Zalatoris (No. 299) are among those whose Augusta futures may depend on this week.

Spieth has the luxury of competing purely for form and confidence. That freedom sometimes produces the cleanest golf — aggressive when needed, patient when required, and unburdened by outcome anxiety.

What's Next

Round 1 tees off Thursday at TPC San Antonio. The tournament runs through Sunday, April 5. The Masters begins the following Thursday, April 9, at Augusta National. Spieth's performance over the next four days will set the tone for his approach to the year's first major.
