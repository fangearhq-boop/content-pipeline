Valero Texas Open 2026 Preview: Fleetwood and Åberg Lead Odds as Masters Spots Hang in the Balance

April 1, 2026 — Golf Fanrecap

The Valero Texas Open begins Thursday at TPC San Antonio — and it arrives as the most consequential tune-up event before the Masters that the PGA Tour calendar offers. With Collin Morikawa withdrawing Tuesday morning, Tommy Fleetwood and Ludvig Åberg enter as co-favorites at +1500 to win the $9.8 million event.

The Oaks Course plays as a Par 72 at 7,438 yards. The tournament runs April 2-5. Pro-Am day is Wednesday, April 1.

The Field

Fleetwood is the highest-ranked player in the field at world No. 4. He arrives fresh off winning the TGL championship — an indoor golf league event that showcased his current form. Åberg, the young Swede who has established himself as one of the best ball-strikers on tour, is a credible co-favorite.

Jordan Spieth (+1600) is among the most interesting names in the field given his course history — he won the Valero Texas Open here in 2021 and knows TPC San Antonio as well as anyone. Russell Henley is also +1600. Robert MacIntyre is listed at +2000.

Morikawa's withdrawal was announced Tuesday morning. He was already exempt for the Masters and had no invitation-related stakes at Valero, but his absence opens the field for others.

Masters Spots: Who Needs a Result

The Valero winner receives a Masters invitation if not already exempt — making this week a genuine last-chance scenario for players on the world ranking bubble.

Among the players chasing Masters exemptions through top-50 positioning or a tournament win:

  • Rickie Fowler (world No. 65): The nearest to the top-50 threshold. A deep run this week could realistically move him inside the cutoff.
  • Billy Horschel (No. 94): An experienced player who has won at various course types. A top-10 finish won't get him to Augusta on ranking alone, but it keeps him relevant.
  • Tony Finau (No. 107): One of the longer hitters in the field. TPC San Antonio rewards distance off the tee.
  • Tom Kim (No. 126): Young and capable of electric rounds. A win earns an automatic invitation.
  • Will Zalatoris (No. 299): Only a victory changes his Masters math. He has the game for it on a good week.

Weather Watch

Heavy thunderstorms are forecast for Saturday in San Antonio, which could compress the schedule and create potential for suspension of play in Round 3. Moving-day disruption at a flat, scoreable course like TPC San Antonio has historically reshuffled leaderboards. Players who establish positions early in the week may benefit.

What's Next

Round 1 tees off Thursday, April 2. The Masters follows immediately the next week — April 9-12 at Augusta National. Players who compete at Valero will have minimal recovery time before the year's first major. The Valero winner, if not already Masters-exempt, will receive the tournament's final invitation to Augusta.
