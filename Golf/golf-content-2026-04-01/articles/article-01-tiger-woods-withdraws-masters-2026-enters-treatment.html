Tiger Woods Withdraws from 2026 Masters, Announces He Is Seeking Treatment

April 1, 2026 — Golf Fanrecap

Tiger Woods has withdrawn from the 2026 Masters and announced he is stepping away from professional golf indefinitely to seek treatment, the golfer confirmed Tuesday after entering a not guilty plea to DUI charges in a Florida court.

The withdrawal marks the second consecutive Masters Woods will miss after decades of defining the tournament's narrative. His statement was brief and direct: "I am stepping away for a period of time to seek treatment and focus on my health."

The Legal Case

Woods appeared in court Tuesday to enter his plea on charges including DUI with property damage and refusal to submit to a lawful chemical test. The charges stem from an incident in which his vehicle was reported to have clipped a trailer, causing the SUV to roll. Investigators at the scene noted bloodshot eyes and found hydrocodone in his pocket. A breathalyzer test returned a 0.00 blood alcohol content, but Woods declined the urine test.

His arraignment is scheduled for April 23, with a docket sounding set for May 5. No plea deal has been announced. His legal team has maintained his right to contest the charges.

Augusta National's Response

Augusta National chairman Fred Ridley released a statement expressing the club's full support for Woods. "We fully support Tiger Woods as he focuses on his well-being," Ridley said. "His presence will be felt here." The statement acknowledged Woods' stature without pressing for any timeline regarding his return to competition.

The Masters is scheduled for April 9-12 — eight days from the announcement. Woods had been listed in the field entering this week, with his participation officially uncertain following the DUI charges filed in late March.

A Second Consecutive Absence

This is the second straight year Woods will not compete at Augusta National. His absence leaves a significant void in the Masters' broadcast narrative, particularly as the tournament debuts on Amazon Prime as a new broadcast partner.

Without Woods in the field, the primary storylines shift to Scottie Scheffler's bid for an unprecedented three straight Masters titles, Rory McIlroy's defense of his 2025 championship, and three players — Xander Schauffele, Collin Morikawa, and Brooks Koepka — chasing career Grand Slams.

A Path Forward?

Despite the withdrawal and treatment announcement, Woods has separately registered for the 2026 U.S. Senior Open — a sign that he has not ruled out competitive golf entirely. The Senior Open would represent a different kind of return: lower stakes, a different circuit, and a format that may be more manageable during recovery.

No timeline for his return to the PGA Tour or major championship golf has been indicated. His team has not commented beyond the formal statement.

What's Next

Woods' next court date is the April 23 arraignment in Martin County, Florida. The Masters proceeds without him beginning April 9 at Augusta National. The U.S. Senior Open — where Woods is registered — takes place in late June. Whether his treatment timeline allows for any competitive golf in 2026 remains to be seen.
