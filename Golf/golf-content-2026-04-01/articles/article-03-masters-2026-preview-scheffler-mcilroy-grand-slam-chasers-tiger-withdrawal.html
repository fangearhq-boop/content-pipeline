Masters 2026 Preview: Scheffler Seeks History, Three Players Chase Grand Slams, Tiger Withdraws

April 1, 2026 — Golf Fanrecap

The 90th Masters Tournament opens in eight days at Augusta National Golf Club, and the narrative heading into the week has been fundamentally reshaped by Tiger Woods' withdrawal, announced Tuesday alongside a not guilty DUI plea.

Without Woods in the field for the second consecutive year, the tournament's main storylines are now fully in focus: Scottie Scheffler's bid to win three straight Masters, Rory McIlroy's defense of his 2025 championship, and three players with a chance to complete the career Grand Slam.

Scheffler: Three-Peat Territory

Scheffler is the clear favorite at +480. He has won the Masters in back-to-back years — 2024 and 2025 — and enters 2026 as the world's No. 1 player. No golfer has ever won three consecutive Masters titles. Jack Nicklaus won back-to-back in 1965-1966, but no player has gone three in a row in the 90-year history of the tournament.

That historical rarity is the central narrative this week. Scheffler has shown no signs of regression. His precision iron play and distance control are uniquely suited to Augusta National's demands.

McIlroy Defends the Slam

Rory McIlroy arrives as defending champion and the man who completed the career Grand Slam in 2025 — one of golf's ultimate achievements. His Masters odds sit around +1000. The emotional weight of defending after that milestone win will be significant.

McIlroy has been managing a back injury that kept him out of several pre-Masters events. He skipped the Valero Texas Open. His form heading to Augusta carries some question marks, but his game at Augusta National has historically been among the best in any field.

The Grand Slam Chasers

Three players arrive at Augusta needing only the Masters to complete the career Grand Slam:

Xander Schauffele won the U.S. Open and The Open Championship in 2024. He has been remarkably consistent in major championships and arrives as one of the more complete players in the world.

Collin Morikawa won the 2020 PGA Championship and the 2021 Open Championship. He is one of the most technically precise ball-strikers of his generation. He withdrew from Valero this week, choosing instead to prepare directly for Augusta.

Brooks Koepka is a four-time major champion — multiple U.S. Opens and PGA Championships. Augusta has been the one major that has eluded his résumé despite his major-championship pedigree.

The career Grand Slam has been completed only a handful of times: Gene Sarazen, Ben Hogan, Gary Player (1965), Jack Nicklaus, Tiger Woods, and most recently Rory McIlroy in 2025.

Amazon Prime Debut

The 2026 Masters marks Amazon Prime's debut as a broadcast partner, adding a new distribution layer to coverage of the tournament. Augusta National has carefully managed its broadcast relationships for decades. The Amazon partnership represents a notable shift toward streaming for one of sport's most tradition-bound events.

What's Next

The Valero Texas Open concludes Sunday, April 5. The Masters begins Thursday, April 9, with the Par-3 Contest on Wednesday, April 8. The champion's green jacket ceremony is scheduled for Sunday, April 12. With Tiger absent, the focus turns entirely to whether Scheffler can accomplish something no one in the history of the tournament has ever done.
