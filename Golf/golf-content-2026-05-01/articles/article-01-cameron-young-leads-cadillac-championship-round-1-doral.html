Cameron Young Posts Bogey-Free 64 to Lead Cadillac Championship — Spieth One Back, Scheffler at -1

By Marcus Cole | May 1, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Cameron Young grabbed the Blue Monster by the throat on Thursday and refused to let go.

The young American fired a bogey-free 8-under 64 at Trump National Doral to lead Round 1 of the 2026 Cadillac Championship, the first PGA Tour event at the iconic course since a ten-year absence. Young's pristine card stood alone at the top of the leaderboard at day's end, with Jordan Spieth and Alex Smalley sharing second place at 7-under after posting matching 65s.

Young's Perfect Day

Young's 64 was defined by putting. He made a succession of clutch putts across the round that, as he put it, were like "a billion feet of putts" — long enough to require serious skill, short enough to demand serious nerve. He drained them all. The bogey-free card was the final proof of a player completely in command of his game.

The Blue Monster has historically been one of the most demanding courses on tour. Water hazards, length, and a punishing rough have sent scores in the wrong direction for generations of players. Young made it look manageable, if not exactly routine, and enters Round 2 as the clear man to beat.

Spieth Lurks at Minus-7

Jordan Spieth's 65 was equally impressive, though one note off Young's melody. The three-time major champion has had a mixed run of form in recent months, but Thursday's round at Doral was a reminder of what he looks like when everything is clicking — precise irons, confident putting, and a round that flows rather than grinds. Spieth also changed his golf ball and equipment ahead of this week, suggesting a deliberate effort to reset his performance arc.

One stroke back with 54 holes to play in a $20 million no-cut event: Spieth is very much in this tournament.

Scheffler's Stumble

The round's most significant storyline, from a market standpoint, was the performance of Scottie Scheffler. The world No. 1 and pre-tournament betting favorite (+290) began promisingly, making three birdies in his first five holes. Then holes 10 and 11 arrived — back-to-back bogeys — and the trajectory of the round shifted completely. Scheffler couldn't locate another birdie from that point forward, signing for a 1-over 71 and finding himself seven strokes off the pace.

In a 72-player field with no cut, Scheffler cannot be eliminated from the tournament. But the math is unforgiving: seven shots in a no-cut event means he likely needs a 62 or 63 on Friday just to reach striking distance. It's not impossible for the world's best player, but it's a significant hole to climb out of.

Nick Taylor sits fourth at 6-under, Nico Echavarria fifth at 5-under, with a group of players at 4-under behind them.

What's Next

Round 2 is underway Friday at Trump National Doral, with Golf Channel coverage beginning at 3PM ET. Young will look to extend his lead on a course that allows scoring but punishes mistakes. Spieth will look to convert his form into a first-round lead. And Scheffler will attempt the kind of spectacular bounce-back round that has defined his best weeks on tour. The Blue Monster is ready for Round 2.
