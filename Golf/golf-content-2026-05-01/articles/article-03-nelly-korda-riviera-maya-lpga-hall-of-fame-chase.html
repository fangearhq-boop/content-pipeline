Korda Five Points from LPGA Hall of Fame — World No. 1 Lurks Two Back at Riviera Maya Open

By Jake Torres | May 1, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Nelly Korda didn't take a week off after winning a major. She went to Mexico.

Just days after her dominant Chevron Championship victory — a five-shot triumph at -18 under par at Memorial Park Golf Course that was her third career major title — Korda is at the LPGA Riviera Maya Open at Mayakoba, hunting another win and, potentially, history. At 22 of the 27 LPGA Hall of Fame points required for automatic induction, she is five points from one of the most exclusive designations in women's sport.

Round 1 at El Camaleon

Korda's opening round at El Camaleon Golf Course in Playa del Carmen, Mexico, was solid if not spectacular: a 4-under 68 that placed her in a tie for fourth, two strokes behind co-leaders Brianna Do and Melanie Green. Green's round was the talk of the day — she made an ace, recording a hole-in-one that sent the crowd into celebration and gave her one of the more memorable moments of the early LPGA season.

Carlota Ciganda stands alone in third at 5-under after a 67. The Spaniard, a consistent performer on the LPGA circuit, is one of several experienced players positioned to challenge over the weekend.

The HOF Math

For Korda, this week has meaning beyond just another title. The LPGA Hall of Fame operates on a points system: major victories, player of the year awards, victories, and longevity all contribute to a running total. Players who reach 27 points are automatically inducted.

Korda sits at 22 after the Chevron win added two points to her previous total. A win at Riviera Maya would add further points — the exact number depends on the tournament's classification in the LPGA points system. A victory, combined with other results, could meaningfully close or eliminate the five-point gap. At 26 years old, Korda is approaching potential Hall of Fame status at an age when many players are still establishing themselves.

Two Back, Not Out

Trailing by two shots through 18 holes is barely a footnote in a four-round event, and Korda's comfort with pressure situations — she won the Chevron with a steady back nine — means she will not be rattled by the deficit. El Camaleon is a course where scoring is accessible and low rounds are entirely possible.

The challenge for Korda is the field. Do and Green are both playing well, and Ciganda has the experience and ball-striking to stay in contention. Korda will need to make her move in Round 2 if she wants to avoid a bigger deficit heading into the weekend.

What's Next

Round 2 of the Riviera Maya Open continues Friday in Playa del Carmen, Mexico. Korda sits T4 at 4-under, two shots behind the leaders. A charge on Friday would put her in prime position for a weekend run at both the title and another step toward LPGA Hall of Fame immortality. Follow the LPGA leaderboard Friday for Round 2 results.
