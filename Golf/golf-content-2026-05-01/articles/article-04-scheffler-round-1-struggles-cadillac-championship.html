Scheffler Sits Seven Back After Round 1 Stumble — World No. 1 Needs a Career Round to Catch Up

By Marcus Cole | May 1, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

The Blue Monster has never cared about world rankings.

Scottie Scheffler, the world No. 1 and the player most expected to dominate the 2026 Cadillac Championship at Trump National Doral, opened the tournament with a 1-over 71 on Thursday — a round that started with promise and finished with frustration. He enters Friday's Round 2 seven strokes behind Cameron Young, who shot a bogey-free 64.

How It Unraveled

Scheffler shared a tee time with Young on Thursday morning, which meant the contrast between the two rounds was visible in real time. Where Young was finding every line and draining every putt, Scheffler was lurching. The world No. 1 started well enough — three birdies in his first five holes had him in red numbers and looking sharp. Then holes 10 and 11 became the turning point.

Back-to-back bogeys. Gone was the momentum. And unlike Scheffler's best rounds, when he typically sustains pressure and keeps the card clean in the second half, Thursday's back nine yielded nothing. He finished with 71 — a round he would describe as deeply unsatisfying.

The Deficit in Context

Seven shots in a no-cut, 72-player signature event is mathematically survivable. Scheffler is in the tournament for all four rounds — there's no risk of an early exit. But the math demands an exceptional Round 2 just to be relevant by the weekend. A 62 or 63 on Friday would likely bring him back within range. Anything above that and the window begins to close.

This is the peculiarity of a no-cut event: every player can keep swinging, which means the field stays intact and scores continue to be posted by the best players in the world. Scheffler will need to outplay nearly everyone to make up the ground he lost in 18 holes.

The Blue Monster Context

It's worth noting that Trump National Doral's Blue Monster has a long history of humbling even the game's greatest. Tiger Woods won here four times, but he also had difficult days at Doral over his career. The course demands attention to detail, precise iron play into its sloped greens, and an ability to manage the water hazards that come into play throughout the back nine.

Scheffler's first-round struggles, while surprising given his elite status, are consistent with the course's ability to punish lapses. The back-to-back bogeys on 10 and 11 are not anomalies at Doral — they're a common narrative for players who momentarily lose focus.

What's Next

Scheffler tees off Friday for Round 2 of the Cadillac Championship. Golf Channel coverage begins at 3PM ET. For Scheffler to remain in realistic contention, he'll need one of the best rounds of his season today. Young is the leader, Spieth is one back, and the window for the world No. 1 to rejoin the conversation is narrowing with every group that finishes Round 2.
