Spieth Fires 65 at Doral and Delivers the Week's Best Quote on LIV Golf's Collapse

By Ryan Calloway | May 1, 2026 | Golf Fanrecap

Jordan Spieth had a very good Thursday — on the course and off it.

The three-time major champion posted a 7-under 65 at Trump National Doral in Round 1 of the 2026 Cadillac Championship, placing him in a tie for second alongside Alex Smalley, one shot behind leader Cameron Young. But after his round, Spieth delivered the most candid and quotable take on LIV Golf's funding crisis of any player at this tournament — and possibly any player this week.

The Golf Was Great

Spieth's 65 was described as "sparkling" by Golf Channel, and the characterization fit. His iron play was crisp, his putting was the reliable kind rather than the heroic kind — which in many ways is more impressive — and his decision-making around the Blue Monster's treacherous water hazards was sound throughout. He signed for 65 without a major scare, which at Doral is itself an accomplishment.

Spieth also made news on the equipment front, having changed his golf ball and some equipment ahead of this week's event. The adjustment appeared to pay immediate dividends, and Spieth discussed the setup changes in a post-round interview — noting he felt more comfortable with the ball's flight characteristics in the south Florida humidity and heat.

At T2 with 54 holes remaining, Spieth is in an excellent position to win his first event of the 2026 season.

On LIV: "I'm Glad I'm Not in the Room"

The bigger quote came when reporters turned to the week's dominant off-course story: Saudi Arabia's PIF ending its funding of LIV Golf after this season. With player representatives reportedly calling the PGA Tour, and the entire structure of professional golf potentially shifting, Spieth's perspective was sought.

He didn't disappoint. "Olive branches were given out a couple months ago — Brooks took them up on it — so I'm not sure what would change now," Spieth said, referencing Brooks Koepka's negotiated return to the PGA Tour last year after accepting specific conditions including charitable donations and delayed compensation. "There are just too many unknowns for me to have a good gauge on what would happen there."

He added: "I'm glad I'm not in the decision-making room."

It's a quote that says a great deal precisely because it says so little. Spieth is aware of the complexity, is choosing not to weigh in on specifics, and is entirely comfortable letting others wrestle with the implications. It's the response of someone who has seen how golf politics works and has decided that his best contribution is on the course, not in the negotiating room.

The Spieth Resurgence Watch

After a period of inconsistency, Spieth has shown signs of renewed form this spring. The 65 at Doral is a data point, not a conclusion — but it's the kind of data point that makes the conversation about a Spieth return to major contention feel relevant again. His major championship record (Masters, US Open, Open Championship) is still one of the most impressive in this era. The question has always been whether the level of performance that produced those titles would return.

Thursday suggested yes. One round at a time.

What's Next

Spieth tees off Friday for Round 2 at Doral, one shot behind Cameron Young. A second consecutive low round would put him in serious contention heading into the weekend. Round 2 is live on Golf Channel at 3PM ET, and Spieth's pairing and tee time will be worth watching as one of the tournament's most compelling storylines.
