LIV's Funding Crisis — Saudi Arabia Ends Support After 2026 Season, New Board Formed
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The ground shifted under LIV Golf this week in a way nobody expected to happen this quickly. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — the sovereign wealth fund that bankrolled LIV's entire existence since its 2022 launch — confirmed it will end its funding of the league at the conclusion of the 2026 season. The PIF has reportedly invested more than $5 billion into LIV over four years. That money tap is now closing. LIV responded by forming a new independent board and announcing it is seeking new investors. The CEO maintained that the 2026 season continues as planned. But the broader question — what comes next? — has no clear answer. The potential for a LIV-PGA Tour rapprochement is now very much on the table. Multiple player representatives for LIV golfers have already reached out to the PGA Tour. But the terms are not easy: Brooks Koepka, who returned last season, had to accept no equity grants for five years, a $5 million charity donation, and no bonus money in his first year back. Jon Rahm, Bryson DeChambeau, and Cam Smith were offered similar terms in February and declined. That offer may no longer be on the table. Jordan Spieth summed up the broader sentiment at Doral Thursday: "There are just too many unknowns for me to have a good gauge on what would happen." He added that he was "glad not to be in the decision-making room." Golf's great schism is entering its most uncertain chapter yet. ### Image Post Caption Saudi Arabia is ending LIV Golf's funding after 2026. Players are calling the PGA Tour. The sport is at a crossroads. Golf's biggest off-course story just got even bigger. #LIVGolf #PGATour

Spieth's Doral Resurgence — 65 and LIV Comments Put Him in Spotlight
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Jordan Spieth had something to say at Doral on Thursday — and most of it happened with a golf club, not a microphone. The three-time major champion fired a 65 on the Blue Monster course, making the card look easy with a combination of precision iron play and putting that recalled his best-level golf. He's tied for second at 7-under, one back of leader Cameron Young, and very much in the frame heading into Round 2. Off the course, the bigger conversation was LIV Golf. With Saudi Arabia pulling its funding from the league and players' representatives reportedly calling the PGA Tour about potential returns, reporters naturally found Spieth. His response was measured, honest, and, typically, quite quotable. "Olive branches were given out a couple months ago — Brooks took them up on it — so I'm not sure what would change now," Spieth said, referencing Koepka's negotiated return to the PGA Tour last year. "There are just too many unknowns for me to have a good gauge on what would happen. I'm glad I'm not in the decision-making room." That quote — "glad I'm not in the decision-making room" — might be the best encapsulation of how most PGA Tour players feel right now. The situation is complicated, the stakes are high, and nobody wants to be the one holding the pen when the terms are negotiated. For now, Spieth is focused on what he can control: a golf ball, a Blue Monster, and a chance to win. ### Image Post Caption Jordan Spieth: 65 on Thursday at Doral. T2, one off the lead. New equipment. And the most quotable take on the LIV situation all week. Good to see you, Jordan. 🏌️ #CadillacChampionship #PGATour
