### STORY 2: LIV Golf Funding Crisis

**Long-Form Post (10:30 AM ET)**

The ground shifted under LIV Golf this week in a way nobody expected to happen this quickly.

Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund — the sovereign wealth fund that bankrolled LIV's entire existence since its 2022 launch — confirmed it will end its funding of the league at the conclusion of the 2026 season. The PIF has reportedly invested more than $5 billion into LIV over four years. That money tap is now closing.

LIV responded by forming a new independent board and announcing it is seeking new investors. The CEO maintained that the 2026 season continues as planned. But the broader question — what comes next? — has no clear answer.

The potential for a LIV-PGA Tour rapprochement is now very much on the table. Multiple player representatives for LIV golfers have already reached out to the PGA Tour. But the terms are not easy: Brooks Koepka, who returned last season, had to accept no equity grants for five years, a $5 million charity donation, and no bonus money in his first year back. Jon Rahm, Bryson DeChambeau, and Cam Smith were offered similar terms in February and declined. That offer may no longer be on the table.

Jordan Spieth summed up the broader sentiment at Doral Thursday: "There are just too many unknowns for me to have a good gauge on what would happen." He added that he was "glad not to be in the decision-making room."

Golf's great schism is entering its most uncertain chapter yet.
