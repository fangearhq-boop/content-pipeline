Tom Kim Ends Three-Year Drought With Scottish Open Win — Tiger Woods Sent the First Text

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 13, 2026

Genesis Scottish Open | Tom Kim | PGA Tour | Scotland

Tom Kim looked at his phone in the moments after winning the 2026 Genesis Scottish Open and saw a text from Tiger Woods. That detail tells you something about how far a young golfer has come — and how much Sunday's victory meant to him.

The 24-year-old South Korean fired a final-round 64 to win at The Renaissance Club at -17, beating Min Woo Lee by two shots and ending a victory drought that stretched back to the 2023 Shriners Children's Open. Nearly three years of waiting came to an end on the links of North Berwick.

"The first person that texted me was Tiger Woods," Kim said after his victory. "It just shows you the type of person he is, how much he cares." He added: "I'm trying to wrap my mind over it. It's very emotional."

How the Tournament Was Won

Kim's decisive moment came at the par-4 16th hole, where he hit an approach from 203 yards that settled to within 6 feet of the pin. That shot put him two clear with two holes to play and ultimately sealed the championship. He did not relinquish the lead from that point forward.

Min Woo Lee, the Australian who has been one of the circuit's most consistent performers in recent seasons, finished as runner-up at -15. Robert MacIntyre, Matt Fitzpatrick, Keita Nakajima, and Johnny Keefer all shared T3 at -13 — a quality group of finishers that speaks to how competitive the Renaissance Club field was this week.

Rory McIlroy, entering the week as an Open Championship co-favorite, closed with a respectable 64 of his own but finished T7 at -11 — a promising result heading into Thursday's opener at Royal Birkdale.

The Long Road Back

Kim burst onto the scene with back-to-back wins in 2022 as a teenage sensation, but the years since his 2023 Shriners victory had been defined by near-misses and the quiet grind of working through difficulty without results. Sunday's win was the validation of that patience.

At 24, he now heads into one of the most significant events of the year — The Open Championship at Royal Birkdale, beginning Thursday — as a man who just answered the biggest question about his game. He has been here before, and he has proven he can close on a world-class stage.

What's Next

Kim has earned his place in The Open Championship field, and he arrives at Royal Birkdale riding form that could make him a legitimate contender. The Scottish Open has historically served as the ideal warm-up for the final major of the year, and Kim is now its champion.
