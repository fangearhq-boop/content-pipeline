LIV Golf's Crisis Is Now Official: WARN Act Notices Filed, $400M Gap Could End the Season Early

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 13, 2026

LIV Golf | Golf Business | Saudi Arabia PIF | WARN Act

LIV Golf's financial troubles moved from reported concern to documented legal reality last week, when the breakaway golf league filed WARN Act notices for employees in the United States and United Kingdom.

The WARN Act — the Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act — is a U.S. federal law that requires employers with 100 or more employees to give 60 days advance notice before a mass layoff or plant closing. LIV Golf, with a global workforce of more than 300 people split between New York and London headquarters, filed the notices around July 8. The organization has been quick to clarify that no layoffs are happening now. But the legal requirement to file means the possibility is real.

The Core Problem

LIV Golf's financial model has been entirely dependent on investment from Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund since the league launched in 2022. The PIF has invested more than five billion dollars over that period, absorbing annual losses in the range of $500 to $600 million in exchange for the political and cultural capital the league was meant to generate.

Earlier this year, the PIF announced it would end that support after the 2026 season concludes. The decision set off an investor search that has been underway for months, with Ducera Partners seeking a replacement in the range of $250 million to $350 million. But the search has not closed, and reports indicate that LIV still needs approximately $400 million to fully fund the completion of its 2026 calendar.

The league's next event is scheduled for July 23 at JCB Golf and Country Club in England. Three more events are scheduled in the United States in August to close the season. Whether all four of those events take place now depends on investor conversations happening right now.

The Official Position

LIV Golf's leadership has maintained a public posture of stability. The official statement accompanying the WARN Act notices read: "There are no changes to LIV Golf's current workforce, operations, or schedule at this time." The notices, the organization said, represent "responsible planning for a range of possible outcomes" — standard legal language for a company that must file but hopes not to act on the notices.

LIV's CEO has reportedly conducted dozens of investor meetings in recent weeks and expressed continued confidence that a funding solution will emerge. A reduced 10-event schedule has been floated as the framework for 2027 if investment is secured — a significantly scaled-back league compared to the current 14-event format.

What It Means for Players

The players who signed with LIV in its early years — some surrendering hundreds of millions of dollars in guaranteed salary and prize money — are watching this situation closely. Many gave up world ranking points, Ryder Cup eligibility, and PGA Tour membership to join a league whose financial guarantees are now in question. The long-term consequences depend entirely on whether outside investment arrives and on what terms.

What's Next

The 60-day WARN Act clock creates a timeline with a September endpoint if layoffs were to proceed from the July 8 filing date. The JCB England event on July 23 will be the clearest near-term signal of whether the league can execute its schedule as planned. Golf Fanrecap will keep tracking LIV Golf's financial situation as events develop.
