MacIntyre Finishes T3 at Scottish Open — "No Reason Why I Can't Compete" at Birkdale

By Jake Torres | Golf Fanrecap | July 13, 2026

The Open Championship | Robert MacIntyre | Genesis Scottish Open | Royal Birkdale

Robert MacIntyre did not win the Scottish Open. But he left The Renaissance Club on Sunday carrying something he will take into the Open Championship: a demonstration that his game is in good shape, and a result that suggests he has every right to compete at Royal Birkdale this week.

MacIntyre finished tied third at -13, four shots behind champion Tom Kim and two behind runner-up Min Woo Lee. The Scotsman played well for long stretches but got derailed by a damaging run of four bogeys in seven holes mid-round — a stretch he described simply as "brutal." He steadied and finished, leaving with his T3 intact.

Looking Ahead to Birkdale

Asked about his prospects for the Open Championship, which begins Thursday at Royal Birkdale, MacIntyre was measured but confident. "No reason why I can't compete," he said. He also described the T3 result as "timely" — a good enough performance, close enough to the lead, in the week immediately before the year's final major.

MacIntyre has been one of the more compelling stories in Scottish golf over the past several years. The left-hander from Oban has steadily built his game on the European and PGA Tour circuits, and links golf — the style of play at both the Renaissance Club and Royal Birkdale — is where his ground-game approach tends to thrive.

The Scottish Open as Open Preparation

The Genesis Scottish Open has long served as the traditional warm-up for The Open Championship, and the shared links character of the events means form carries over. MacIntyre's finish puts him in a group of players who will arrive at Birkdale with recent competitive reps on similar turf under their feet.

Tom Kim, the week's champion, also heads to Birkdale riding form. Rory McIlroy, who closed with a 64, is among the co-favorites for the major. The entire top half of the Scottish Open leaderboard is now walking into Open Championship week with momentum — MacIntyre is firmly part of that group.

What's Next

The Open Championship begins Thursday at Royal Birkdale. MacIntyre will look to build on his Scottish Open result in a field of 156 players all chasing the Claret Jug. Golf Fanrecap has full coverage of the week's final major from Southport, starting Thursday morning.
