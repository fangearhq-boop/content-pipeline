The Open Championship Arrives at Royal Birkdale: Three Days Until the Claret Jug Hunt Begins

By Ryan Calloway | Golf Fanrecap | July 13, 2026

The Open Championship | Royal Birkdale | Scottie Scheffler | Rory McIlroy | Golf Major

The 154th Open Championship begins Thursday at Royal Birkdale Golf Club in Southport, England, and the build-up is already in full swing.

Practice rounds are underway on the Southport links today, and the Last-Chance Qualifier is being played at Birkdale as well — 12 players competing for the final remaining spots in the 156-player field. Sky Sports launched its "Live From The Open" coverage this morning, and the atmosphere of championship week has descended on one of golf's most storied venues.

The Favorites

Scottie Scheffler and Rory McIlroy enter as co-favorites at approximately +700, a pairing at the top of the market that reflects both players' credentials for this kind of event.

Scheffler is the defending champion after winning the 153rd Open at Royal Portrush in 2025. He arrives at Birkdale carrying the extra motivation of a player who missed the cut at the Scottish Open last week — the first missed cut in a streak spanning nearly four years. The World No. 1 will be eager to reassert himself on the game's biggest stage, and Royal Birkdale, where precision and course management reward the best ball-strikers, suits Scheffler's game. A back-to-back Open title would be the first since Tiger Woods won in 2005 and 2006.

McIlroy brings different energy. He closed the Scottish Open with a final-round 64 on Sunday and heads south with form and confidence. His 2014 Open victory came at Hoylake, not Birkdale, but links golf is where McIlroy has historically been most comfortable. A win here would give him seven major championships — and would be received with enormous emotion from a player who has carried the burden of expectations for over a decade.

The Rest of the Field

Beyond the top two, the market reflects genuine depth. Tommy Fleetwood is a sentimental pick as a Southport native who skipped the Scottish Open specifically to prepare at Birkdale — he knows this course well and is due a major breakthrough. Matt Fitzpatrick, Jon Rahm at +2000, and Xander Schauffele round out the top tier of longer-odds contenders.

Three LIV Golf players also earned their way into the field through Final Qualifying, ensuring the event reflects the full breadth of elite professional golf regardless of tour affiliation.

Royal Birkdale

Royal Birkdale has hosted the Open Championship more often than any venue except St Andrews in the decades since it first hosted in 1954. The course demands accuracy off the tee — the thick rough that defines links golf in summer conditions will punish anything loose. The deep willow scrub lining the fairways is the signature peril. The links layout also means wind management becomes a crucial skill, and with rain in the forecast throughout the week, adaptability off the tee will separate contenders from the field.

What's Next

Official tee times and pairings are expected from the R&A on Tuesday or Wednesday. The first groups tee off Thursday at approximately 1:30 AM ET. Golf Fanrecap will have comprehensive Open Championship coverage all week — first-round updates, featured group watches, leaderboard analysis, and live coverage of Sunday's final round. The Claret Jug winner is decided Sunday afternoon. It starts Thursday.
