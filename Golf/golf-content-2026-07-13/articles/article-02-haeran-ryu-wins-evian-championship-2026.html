Haeran Ryu Wins the Evian Championship in a Playoff — Her Second Major in Three Weeks

By Marcus Cole | Golf Fanrecap | July 13, 2026

Amundi Evian Championship | Haeran Ryu | LPGA Major | Women's Golf

Three weeks ago, Haeran Ryu won her first major championship. On Sunday, she won her second.

The South Korean golfer won the 2026 Amundi Evian Championship in a playoff over Canada's Brooke Henderson, birdying the first extra hole — the par-4 18th — to claim the Claret Jug of women's golf in Evian-les-Bains, France. The victory gives her back-to-back LPGA majors after she won the KPMG Women's PGA Championship at Hazeltine on June 29.

"This is just a dream right now," Ryu said after her win. "Because before three weeks ago, I don't have a major championship, but now two in a row. So I'm so happy and I can't believe it right now."

The Record That Made Sunday Possible

To understand how Ryu even ended up in a playoff, you have to start with Saturday. Ryu carded a 60 in her third round at the Evian Resort Golf Club — 11 under par on a single day. That is the lowest round ever recorded in a major championship, at any level, in men's or women's golf. It gave her a three-shot lead entering the final round and made her appear almost untouchable.

But Brooke Henderson had other plans. The Canadian played one of the great final rounds in major championship history on Sunday, carding a 64 that included an eagle on the 18th hole to get to -19 — matching Ryu's total. The eagle forced the playoff. In 18 holes, Henderson had erased a three-shot deficit and added one extraordinary swing to send the tournament into overtime.

The Playoff

When the playoff returned to hole 18, Ryu was the steadier of the two players. She found the green and converted a birdie to win. Henderson, who had just played the round of her life, was gracious: "I played awesome today, which is really exciting. To finish second in a major and third a couple weeks ago, definitely game is in a really good spot. Obviously it's very exciting to get into a playoff. Wish I had played a little better, but Haeran has been playing great."

She has been. Two majors in three weeks is a feat that demands recognition not as hot streak but as sustained excellence — Ryu has been grinding, finding her game, and performing when it matters most at the sport's biggest events.

The Bigger Picture

With two of the 2026 LPGA major championships now in Ryu's possession, the conversation about women's golf this year begins and ends with her name. She entered the Evian as a KPMG champion. She leaves as a two-time major winner in a span of 21 days. The final two women's majors of 2026 — the AIG Women's British Open and the Chevron Championship — will unfold with Ryu as the clear player to beat.

What's Next

Haeran Ryu has changed the conversation in women's golf with this stretch of results. Golf Fanrecap will continue tracking the LPGA Tour as players prepare for the AIG Women's British Open, the fifth and final women's major of 2026. But right now, the story belongs entirely to Ryu.
