### STORY 5: LIV Golf Survival Crisis — WARN Act Filed, Season Potentially at Risk

**Long-Form Post:**
LIV Golf's financial crisis has moved from an abstract concern to a concrete legal reality.

Around July 8, LIV Golf filed WARN Act notices for its employees in the United States and United Kingdom. The WARN Act is a U.S. federal law that requires companies with more than 100 employees to provide 60 days advance notice before potential mass layoffs or plant closings. LIV, with a global workforce of 300-plus people, filed the notices as a legal obligation given the uncertainty surrounding its funding.

The league's official statement insists nothing has changed: "There are no changes to LIV Golf's current workforce, operations, or schedule at this time." The notices, they say, represent "responsible planning for a range of possible outcomes."

The underlying problem is significant. Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund, which has bankrolled LIV since its 2022 launch to the tune of more than five billion dollars, announced earlier this year that it will not continue funding the league beyond 2026. LIV is currently searching for replacement investors through the Ducera Partners firm, seeking somewhere in the range of $250 million to $350 million. Internal estimates suggest the league needs roughly $400 million to complete the current season — meaning the final two events of 2026 could be cancelled if no deal comes through.

LIV's next event is scheduled for July 23 at JCB Golf and Country Club in England. Whether the season concludes as planned depends on investor conversations happening right now.
