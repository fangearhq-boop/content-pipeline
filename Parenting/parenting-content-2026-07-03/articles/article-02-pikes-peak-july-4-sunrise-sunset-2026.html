Pikes Peak Is Doing Something It's Never Done Before This July 4 — Here's How to Be There

By Sarah Morales | July 3, 2026

On July 4, 2026, Pikes Peak America's Mountain is offering something it has never offered before: a July 4 sunset viewing from the summit. Combined with a special early-morning sunrise opening, this is a genuine once-in-a-lifetime kind of Independence Day option for Colorado Springs families — and tickets are still available today if you move fast.

The important caveat: advance tickets are required for both events, and none are sold at the gateway. If your family wants to be part of either or both, you'll need to purchase through DrivePikesPeak.com before you head out.

The Sunrise Event: 4 AM Gateway Opening

For families willing to set some serious alarms, the Pikes Peak Highway Gateway opens at 4 AM on July 4 for a special sunrise event. The last timed entry for the sunrise event is 6 AM — so this isn't a leisurely morning start.

Watching sunrise from 14,115 feet on Independence Day is a legitimately remarkable experience. The summit sits above the clouds on most summer mornings, which means a sunrise that looks nothing like what you'd see from the city below. For kids who've never been to the summit before, the combination of the elevation, the view, and the pre-dawn sky tends to land as one of those experiences they remember for years.

The Sunset Event: A Historic First

The July 4 sunset opening is new territory for Pikes Peak. According to officials, this is the first-ever July 4 sunset event the summit has hosted — making it a true inaugural year for this particular celebration.

The gateway reopens at 7 PM for the sunset event. Sunset from 14,115 feet is at 8:29 PM. Visitors are required to begin their descent no later than one hour after sunset.

The first-ever designation isn't just marketing language. Pikes Peak doesn't typically hold evening events, and the logistics of coordinating a sunset viewing at that altitude — with capacity limits and weather monitoring — make it a meaningful operational milestone as well as a memorable family experience.

Planning Tips for Families

If you're doing the sunrise event, dress in layers — even on a summer day, temperatures at 14,115 feet can be in the 30s or low 40s at 4 AM. Kids who are borderline sleepy at 4 AM might appreciate a blanket in the car for the drive up, and the Pikes Peak Summit Visitor Center has hot drinks available once you're up top.

For the sunset event, the timing works well for families with younger kids who can't manage a 4 AM start. Leaving at 7 PM for a summit arrival well before the 8:29 PM sunset gives you time to settle in and take in the view before the main event. Layering still applies at dusk — the temperature drops fast after sunset at altitude.

With fireworks cancelled across Colorado Springs this July 4 due to Stage II fire restrictions, the Pikes Peak events have taken on extra significance. Watching the July 4 sunset from the summit is as Colorado as it gets — and this year, it's the headline alternative to what most COS families would normally be doing that evening.
