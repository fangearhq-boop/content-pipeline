Recall: Crayola-Branded PipCubes Magnetic Building Cubes Pulled Due to Magnet Ingestion Risk

By Sarah Morales | July 3, 2026

If you purchased Crayola-branded PipCubes magnetic building cube sets in the past year, stop using them immediately and take them away from children. The Consumer Product Safety Commission has officially recalled about 9,400 units of the product due to a serious magnet ingestion hazard.

The recall covers sets sold under the Crayola brand name — a brand that most families recognize and trust. That recognition is part of what makes this one worth flagging: these aren't obscure toys that may have slipped through your door unnoticed. If you shopped for building toys at Michaels or on Amazon between May and July 2025, it's worth checking what you bought.

What's Recalled

Detail Information
Product name Crayola-branded PipCubes magnetic building cubes (by CreateOn)
Varieties included Bold Colors, Glitter
Set sizes 24-piece and 27-piece sets
Units recalled Approximately 9,400
Where sold Michaels stores nationwide and Michaels.com (24-piece, approximately $20); Amazon.com (27-piece, approximately $35)
Sale period May 2025 through July 2025

The Hazard

The cubes contain high-powered magnets that can come loose when seams on the cubes separate. On their own, a single loose magnet is still a choking hazard. But the danger escalates sharply if a child swallows more than one magnet — or one magnet that later passes through the digestive system separately from another.

High-powered magnets that are swallowed can attract each other through the walls of the intestines. When that happens, the magnetic force can cause intestinal perforations, twisting, and blockage. These injuries can lead to blood poisoning and, in the most severe cases, death. This isn't a theoretical risk — magnet ingestion injuries in children have resulted in hospitalizations and fatalities across multiple recalled products over the years, which is why the CPSC treats high-powered magnet recalls as priority actions.

The combination of a trusted brand name on the packaging and the appealing, colorful design of the product means these cubes may have been given to young children as a gift or play item without a second thought. If you're unsure whether your child has been playing with them, check Amazon order history for May through July 2025 or look for the PipCubes name on any magnetic cube sets in your home.

What to Do Right Now

  1. Stop using the product immediately.
  2. Take the cubes away from children and keep them out of reach.
  3. Contact CreateOn through the CPSC recall page at cpsc.gov for a free replacement set and a pre-paid return label.

The replacement is free. CreateOn provides a pre-paid return label so you can send back the recalled product at no cost.

When to Seek Medical Attention

If you believe a child may have swallowed a magnet — or you're not sure — contact your pediatrician or go to the emergency room immediately. Symptoms of magnet ingestion can be delayed and may initially look like a stomach ache or nausea. Early imaging can identify whether magnets are present and allow for intervention before injuries become serious.

What's Next

Visit cpsc.gov and search for the CreateOn PipCubes recall for official instructions and the manufacturer contact page. If you're not sure what to look for, search your Amazon order history for "PipCubes" or "CreateOn" and filter by the May–July 2025 window. If you gave these as a gift, consider letting the recipient know directly — recall awareness doesn't always reach every household automatically.
