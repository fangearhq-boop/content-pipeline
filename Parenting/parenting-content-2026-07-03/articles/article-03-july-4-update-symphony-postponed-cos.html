July 4 in COS: The Star-Spangled Symphony Is Postponed — Here's What's Still On

By Jamie Rivera | July 3, 2026

If you've been keeping up with July 4 plans in Colorado Springs, here's the most important update to know before tomorrow: the Star-Spangled Symphony at Ford Amphitheater is postponed. The entire event — concert and all — is off due to fire danger and the Stage II fire restrictions that have been in effect since June 29.

This is a change from earlier in the week when some events were still being listed as undecided. The picture is clearer now. Here's the full rundown of what's happening and what isn't tomorrow in COS.

What's Off

The Star-Spangled Symphony at Ford Amphitheater — one of the most popular July 4 events in the city — is postponed indefinitely. The decision follows the citywide cancellation of all public fireworks displays under Stage II fire restrictions. Because the Symphony event was tied to a fireworks finale, the entire event was shelved rather than run without it.

All fireworks displays across Colorado Springs are cancelled for July 4, 2026. This is not venue-specific — it's a citywide restriction driven by ongoing fire danger conditions. Backyard fireworks are also banned under Stage II restrictions.

A Few Notes on Each

Switchbacks vs Phoenix Rising

The Colorado Springs Switchbacks FC game against Phoenix Rising is scheduled for 7 PM at Weidner Field and is going ahead as planned. This is a solid family outing for the evening — live soccer, a stadium atmosphere, and an actual crowd to give July 4 its energy even without fireworks.

Banning Lewis Ranch

The free July 4 celebration at Vista Park Pavilion in Banning Lewis Ranch is still scheduled. It's one of the more family-friendly daytime options, and "free" goes a long way when you're entertaining kids through a holiday weekend. Check the event page for exact timing before you go.

Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site

Open 10 AM to 4 PM. This living history site is a consistently good pick for kids who need space to roam and something hands-on to engage with. The ranch setting makes it feel like a genuinely different kind of July 4 experience.

WWII Aviation Museum

Open 10 AM to 5 PM. Great for families with kids who are into military history, aircraft, or just things that are big and impressive up close. Worth the visit any time of year — the July 4 timing is just a bonus.

Pikes Peak Events

Both the sunrise (4 AM gateway opening) and the first-ever sunset event (7 PM reopening, sunset at 8:29 PM) are still on, weather permitting. Advance tickets are required at DrivePikesPeak.com — none are sold at the gateway. See our full Pikes Peak coverage for prices and planning tips.

A Word on Fire Conditions

Stage II fire restrictions have been in effect since June 29 and remain active through the holiday weekend. These restrictions ban all open burning — including backyard fire pits and charcoal grills in some areas — in addition to all fireworks. Before heading out to any outdoor event tomorrow, it's worth double-checking with the specific venue directly, since conditions can change and event organizers may issue last-minute updates.

What's Next

Check the websites and social channels of any event you're planning to attend before heading out tomorrow — fire conditions and air quality can shift quickly, and venues are updating in real time.
