### STORY 1: AAP Publishes Updated Drowning Prevention Guidelines in July 2026 Pediatrics

#### FB Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

The American Academy of Pediatrics just published updated drowning prevention guidelines in the July 2026 issue of Pediatrics — and the timing couldn't be more important going into a holiday weekend when families are heading to pools, lakes, and splash pads. 💧

Here's what's new:

**Touch supervision.** The AAP is putting a specific term to what they're recommending: a *designated* adult within arm's reach of any infant, toddler, or weak swimmer in or near water. Not glancing up from a chair. Not watching from the pool steps. Arm's reach, fully focused, no phone in hand. If you're the water watcher, that's the only job.

**Swim lessons can start at age 1.** The updated guidance says increased mobility after the first birthday is exactly what raises drowning risk — so starting lessons at that age is now appropriate. For infants before age 1, though, there still isn't enough evidence that lessons reduce drowning risk.

**Other key points from the updated policy:**
- Four-sided pool fencing with a self-closing, self-latching gate — isolating the pool from the house and yard, not just the street
- No unsupervised baths until at least age 6
- CPR training for caregivers — because response time matters when it comes to drowning outcomes
- No single method is enough on its own; multiple layers of protection working together is the standard

Drowning remains the No. 1 cause of unintentional injury death for children ages 1–4. This July 4 weekend, that number is worth keeping in mind every time kids are near water.

### STORY 4: Recall Alert — Crayola PipCubes Magnetic Building Cubes Recalled for Magnet Ingestion Hazard

#### FB Image Caption — Story 4

⚠️ Recall alert for parents: Crayola-branded PipCubes magnetic building cube sets have been recalled by the CPSC.

About 9,400 units were sold at Michaels and on Amazon between May and July 2025. Magnets can come loose and — if swallowed — high-powered magnets can attract each other inside a child's body, causing serious internal injuries.

If you have these, stop use immediately and keep them away from kids. Contact CreateOn at cpsc.gov for a free replacement set.

#### FB Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

Parents who picked up Crayola-branded building cube sets in the past year — please stop and read this. ⚠️

The CPSC has officially recalled CreateOn's Crayola-branded PipCubes magnetic building cubes. About 9,400 units were sold, and the hazard is serious.

**What's recalled:**
- Crayola-branded PipCubes magnetic building cubes
- Bold Colors and Glitter varieties; sets of 24 or 27 cubes
- Sold at Michaels stores nationwide and on Amazon.com
- Sale window: May 2025 through July 2025
- Price range: approximately $20 (24-piece) to $35 (27-piece)

**The hazard:**
The seams on these cubes can separate, allowing the high-powered magnets inside to come loose. If a child swallows more than one magnet, they can attract each other through intestinal walls — causing perforations, blockage, blood poisoning, and in the most serious cases, death. This is a hazard that has harmed children across multiple recalls over the years.

**What to do right now:**
Stop using the product immediately. Take it away from children and store it out of reach. Contact CreateOn through cpsc.gov for a free replacement set and a pre-paid return label.

The Crayola name is trusted, which is exactly why this one caught a lot of families off guard. If you're not sure whether you bought these — check Amazon order history for May–July 2025, or look for PipCubes branding on any cube sets you have at home.

Did you have these? Let other parents know in the comments if you've already requested your replacement. 🔄
