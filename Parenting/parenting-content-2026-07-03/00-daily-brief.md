### STORY 1: AAP Publishes Updated Drowning Prevention Guidelines in July 2026 Pediatrics
**Tier:** 1 (Breaking/National Parenting)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
The American Academy of Pediatrics has published its updated "Prevention of Drowning" policy statement in the July 2026 issue of Pediatrics. Key new emphasis: "touch supervision" (within arm's reach) for infants and toddlers, swim lessons are appropriate starting at age 1, a four-sided pool fence with self-closing/self-latching gate, no unsupervised baths until age 6, and CPR training for caregivers. ### STORY 4: Recall Alert — Crayola PipCubes Magnetic Building Cubes Recalled for Magnet Ingestion Hazard
**Tier:** 2 (Today's News/National Parenting)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
The CPSC has recalled about 9,400 CreateOn Crayola-branded PipCubes magnetic building cube sets. The cubes — sold in Bold Colors and Glitter varieties — can have magnets come loose. Swallowed high-powered magnets can attract each other inside the body, causing intestinal perforations, blockage, blood poisoning, and death. Sold at Michaels (24-piece, ~$20) and Amazon (27-piece, ~$35) from May to July 2025. Remedy: stop use immediately, keep from children, contact CreateOn for free replacement.
