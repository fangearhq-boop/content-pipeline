### STORY 2: CPSC Recalls Two Amazon Baby Bath Seats — Drowning and Entrapment Risk
**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Posting window:** 10:00 AM MT

The CPSC has recalled two baby bath seat brands sold on Amazon: YCXXKJ (sold by BenTalk) and NFSVLB. Both violate the mandatory safety standard for infant bath seats. YCXXKJ bath seats are unstable and can tip over while in use, posing a drowning risk. NFSVLB seats (Model No.: ZY2025, about 1,430 units) have leg openings that are too wide, allowing a child to slip down and become entrapped, in addition to the tipping hazard. Both are stop-use-immediately recalls. For NFSVLB: disassemble the seat, write "Recalled" on the base, cut the four suction cups on the bottom, send a photo to amzmaihuayd@163.com for a full refund.

**Key facts (HIGH):**
- YCXXKJ bath seat recall: unstable, can tip over, drowning risk (cpsc.gov)
- NFSVLB bath seat recall: ~1,430 units, Model ZY2025, drowning + entrapment hazard (cpsc.gov)
- NFSVLB sold on Amazon by NFSVLB, May 2025 through October 2025 (cpsc.gov)
- Contact NFSVLB at amzmaihuayd@163.com for refund (cpsc.gov)
- Both violate mandatory standard for infant bath seats (cpsc.gov)

### STORY 3: Vevor Baby Swings Recalled — Stop Using Immediately (Suffocation Risk)
**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Posting window:** 12:00 PM MT

Sanven Technology has recalled about 1,020 Vevor Brand Baby Swings due to a suffocation hazard. The swings were marketed for infant sleep with an incline angle greater than 10 degrees, violating the Safe Sleep for Babies Act's ban on inclined sleepers. Affected models: BB501K (white/gray, white base), BB702A (white/gray, gray base), BB005K (black/gray, black base). Sold at Vevor.com and Amazon.com from January 2025 through August 2025 for $65–$80. Stop using immediately. To get a full refund, cut the swing cover in half, write "Recalled" on the base, and email a photo to recalling@vevor.com. Contact Sanven at 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday, 9 AM–5 PM PT.

**Key facts (HIGH):**
- ~1,020 units recalled (cpsc.gov)
- Models BB501K, BB702A, BB005K (cpsc.gov)
- Sold January 2025–August 2025 at Vevor.com and Amazon.com, $65–$80 (cpsc.gov)
- Contact: recalling@vevor.com; phone 855-599-6320, Mon–Fri 9 AM–5 PM PT (cpsc.gov)
- Violates inclined sleeper ban under Safe Sleep for Babies Act (cpsc.gov)

### STORY 5: AAP Updates School Food Allergy Guidelines — What Colorado Springs Families Should Know
**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Posting window:** 5:00 PM MT

The American Academy of Pediatrics published a clinical report in September 2025 updating its guidance on managing food allergies and anaphylaxis in schools — the first update since 2010. Key facts: food allergy affects up to 10% of children; anaphylaxis occurs in an estimated 1 in 15 schools per year; pediatricians are now specifically guided to prescribe stock (unassigned) epinephrine auto-injectors (EAIs) for emergency school use in addition to individual student EAIs. Some Colorado school districts are moving toward having stock epinephrine on hand. Colorado Springs families with children who have food allergies are encouraged to review their child's 504 plan or emergency action plan before the end of the school year.
