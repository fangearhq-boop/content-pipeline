## Story 1: D11 Chronic Absenteeism

### Key Data (from co.edtribune.com, April 10, 2026)
- D11 chronic absenteeism rate surged 16.6 percentage points in one year — HIGH (co.edtribune.com)
- Largest increase among any large Colorado district — HIGH (co.edtribune.com)
- More than half of Black and Hispanic students now chronically absent — HIGH (co.edtribune.com)
- CDE goal: reduce statewide rate to 17.8% by 2026-27 — HIGH (cde.state.co.us via search result)
- Definition: chronically absent = missing 10% or more of school days (standard CDE definition)
- D11.org for attendance resources (established)

### Context
- Post-pandemic national pattern of elevated absenteeism
- Colorado statewide absenteeism remained largely unchanged from 2023-24 per CDE news release
- D11 serves the urban core of Colorado Springs; diverse student population
- KRDO reported chronic absenteeism in Southern Colorado districts (2025)

## Story 2: CPSC Baby Bath Seat Recalls

### YCXXKJ Recall (HIGH — cpsc.gov)
- Product: YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats
- Seller: BenTalk on Amazon
- Hazard: Unstable, can tip over while in use → drowning risk
- Violation: Mandatory standard for infant bath seats
- Action: Stop use immediately, contact seller for refund
- Source: cpsc.gov official recall page

### NFSVLB Recall (HIGH — cpsc.gov)
- Product: NFSVLB Baby Bath Seats
- Model No.: ZY2025
- Units: ~1,430
- Seller: NFSVLB on Amazon
- Sale dates: May 2025–October 2025
- Hazard: Unstable + leg openings too wide → child can slip down, entrapment + drowning
- Violation: Mandatory standard for infant bath seats
- Action: Stop use; disassemble (remove backrest + arm restraints, discard screws, cut 4 suction cups on bottom); write "Recalled" on seat; send photo to amzmaihuayd@163.com for full refund
- Source: cpsc.gov official recall page + myparistexas.com confirmation

- Brand: Vevor Baby Swings

## Story 5: AAP Food Allergy in Schools

### Key Guidance (HIGH — aap.org/publications.aap.org)
- Clinical report published September 2025 — first update since 2010
- Food allergy affects up to 10% of children
- Anaphylaxis estimated in 1 in 15 schools per year
- Pediatricians should prescribe stock (unassigned) epinephrine auto-injectors (EAIs) for school use
- Schools should have emergency action plans for every child with known allergy
- Some states now require stock epinephrine by law

### Practical Guidance for COS Families
- Review child's 504 plan or school emergency action plan before end of school year
- Talk to pediatrician about epinephrine prescription if child has known food allergy
- Contact D11 (d11.org), D20 (asd20.org), or D49 (d49.org) to verify stock epinephrine policy
- Resources: aap.org/en/patient-care/allergy-and-immunology/
