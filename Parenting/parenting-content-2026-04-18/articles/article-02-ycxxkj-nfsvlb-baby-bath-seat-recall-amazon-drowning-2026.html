CPSC Recalls Two Amazon Baby Bath Seats for Drowning Risk — Stop Using Now

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting | April 18, 2026

Two baby bath seats sold on Amazon have been recalled by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission due to drowning and entrapment hazards. If you own a YCXXKJ bath seat (sold by BenTalk) or an NFSVLB bath seat with Model No. ZY2025, stop using it immediately.

Both products violate the mandatory federal safety standard for infant bath seats. Neither should be used, donated, or stored for future use.

YCXXKJ Bath Seat Recall

YCXXKJ bath seats, sold on Amazon by the seller BenTalk, are unstable and can tip over while a child is in the bath. This instability creates a direct drowning risk. The seats violate the mandatory federal safety standard for infant bath seats.

If you own one: stop using it immediately and contact the seller through Amazon for a refund. You can also find the full recall notice at cpsc.gov.

NFSVLB Bath Seat Recall

The NFSVLB infant bath seat recall affects approximately 1,430 units with Model No. ZY2025. These seats were sold on Amazon from May 2025 through October 2025. There are two hazards: the seats are unstable and can tip over, and the leg openings are too wide, which can cause a child to slip down into the seat and become entrapped. Both hazards can result in serious injury or death from drowning or entrapment.

To get a full refund for your NFSVLB seat, follow these steps:

  1. Stop using the seat immediately.
  2. Disassemble the seat — remove the backrest and arm restraints, and discard the screws.
  3. Cut all four suction cups on the bottom of the seat.
  4. Write "Recalled" on the front of the seat with a permanent marker.
  5. Take a photo of the disassembled seat and email it to amzmaihuayd@163.com to claim your full refund.

How to Check the CPSC Recall Database

If you're unsure whether a product you own has been recalled, the CPSC maintains a searchable database at cpsc.gov/Recalls. You can search by product name, brand, or type. It's worth bookmarking and checking periodically — especially for baby and infant products, which are among the most frequently recalled categories.

For general questions about product safety or to report an unsafe product, contact the CPSC at SaferProducts.gov or call 800-638-2772.
