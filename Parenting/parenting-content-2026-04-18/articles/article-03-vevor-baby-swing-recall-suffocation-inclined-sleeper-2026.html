Vevor Baby Swings Recalled for Suffocation Risk — Models BB501K, BB702A, BB005K

By Keisha Williams | COS Parenting | April 18, 2026

Sanven Technology has recalled approximately 1,020 Vevor Brand Baby Swings due to a suffocation hazard. The swings were marketed for infant sleep but have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees, which violates the federal ban on inclined infant sleepers under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act. If you own one of the affected models, stop using it immediately.

Affected Models

Three models are affected by this recall:

  • Model BB501K — white and gray swing with a white base
  • Model BB702A — white and gray swing with a gray base
  • Model BB005K — black and gray swing with a black base

These swings were sold at Vevor.com and Amazon.com from January 2025 through August 2025 for between $65 and $80.

Why the Incline Matters

The Safe Sleep for Babies Act, which became law in 2022, bans infant sleep products with an incline angle greater than 10 degrees. Research has shown that when infants fall asleep in an inclined position, their heads can fall forward in a way that restricts the airway — a risk that is particularly serious for infants under four months of age, whose neck muscles are not yet strong enough to correct the position.

Inclined sleepers like these swings were linked to multiple infant deaths before the ban was enacted. The federal law exists specifically to prevent these tragedies from continuing.

What to Do If You Own a Vevor Baby Swing

Stop using the swing immediately. To receive a full refund:

  1. Remove the swing cover and cut it in half.
  2. Write "Recalled" on the base of the swing in permanent marker.
  3. Email a photo of the destroyed cover to recalling@vevor.com.
  4. Dispose of the swing in accordance with your local and state guidelines.

You can also call Sanven Technology directly at 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM PT.
