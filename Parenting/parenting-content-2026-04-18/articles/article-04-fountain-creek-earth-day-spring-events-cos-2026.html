Fountain Creek Spring Programs for Families: Habitat Exploration April 24 and Earth Day April 25

By Marta Kim | COS Parenting | April 18, 2026

Two outdoor education programs are coming up at Fountain Creek this week — back to back on Friday and Saturday — giving Colorado Springs families a great reason to get outside with their kids. Both require prepaid registration, so it's worth planning ahead.

Friday, April 24: Habitat Exploration at Fountain Creek Regional Park

On Friday, April 24, Fountain Creek Regional Park is hosting a Habitat Exploration program from 2 PM to 4 PM, designed for kids in grades 3 through 6. The program walks participants through five different habitats shaped by water, starting with a short video introduction, then a wetland metaphor activity, and finishing with an outdoor hike that includes a pond study component.

Cost is $4 per person, with prepaid registration required for all attendees. Kids and adults both need to register. To sign up, call the Fountain Creek Nature Center at 719-520-6745.

This is a well-suited program for upper elementary kids who are curious about the natural environment and enjoy learning outside the classroom. The pond study component in particular is a hands-on experience that most kids won't forget quickly.

Saturday, April 25: Earth Day Program at Fountain Creek Nature Center

The day after, on Saturday, April 25, Fountain Creek Nature Center is hosting an Earth Day program from 10 AM to noon. This program is open to families with children of a broader age range. Cost is $5 per person or $4 for nature center members, with prepaid registration required.

Earth Day in Colorado Springs is a meaningful opportunity to connect kids to the local environment — the waterways, wildlife, and landscapes that make El Paso County worth protecting. Programs like this one give families a hands-on way to observe and celebrate that environment rather than just reading about it.

To register for the Earth Day program, call 719-520-6745.

Both programs are located at Fountain Creek facilities, which are run by El Paso County Parks and Recreation. The Nature Center is accessible and family-friendly, with parking and trail access on-site.

More Outdoor Options This Spring

If these programs fill up or don't fit your schedule, the Garden of the Gods remains a free option that's open to families year-round — 5 AM to 9 PM daily, with guided walks available for $5 per person. Cheyenne Mountain State Park, about 10 minutes from downtown Colorado Springs, has more than 28 miles of trails and is another excellent spring option for older kids and families who want a longer hike.

Spring in Colorado Springs means the trails are starting to clear and the weather is warming — though temperatures at 6,035 feet can still surprise you in April, so layers are always a good call.

What's Next

The May Faire at Mountain Song Community School is scheduled for May 2 from 11 AM to 2 PM, featuring maypole dances, crafts, and family activities. The Old Colorado City Community Street Festival runs May 23 through May 25 (Memorial Day weekend) with arts, crafts, and family programming.
