Two Baby Lounger Recalls: Vevor and YGJT Products Flagged for Entrapment and Fall Hazards

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | July 5, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

Two separate baby lounger recalls are active this week — one affecting products sold on Amazon and Wayfair under the Vevor brand, and another affecting products sold on SHEIN under the YGJT brand. Both involve the same category of hazards: sides too low to contain an infant, foot openings too wide, and no stand — a combination the CPSC says creates serious risks of entrapment and falls.

If you've purchased a baby lounger from either of these retailers in the past two years, the product details below are worth checking against what's in your home.

Recall No. 1 — Vevor Baby Loungers

Detail Information
Units recalled ~237
Sold at VEVOR.com, Amazon.com, Wayfair.com
Sale dates November 2024 through January 2026
Price ~$30
Colors / prints Snow deer, good night, forest friends (oval), forest friends (backpack), green
Construction Foam sleeping pad, padded bumpers, cloth cover, ribbon at one end
Hazards Sides too low to contain infant; foot openings wider than allowed; no stand (fall hazard on elevated surfaces)

How to get a refund: Contact Vevor toll-free at 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM PT. You can also email recalling@vevor.com or visit vevor.com/pages/vevor-baby-loungers. Vevor will provide a full refund.

Recall No. 2 — YGJT Baby Loungers

Detail Information
Units recalled ~2,500
Sold at SHEIN.com (by YGJT Local Warehouse)
Sale dates June 2025 through August 2025
Price $18–$21
Colors / prints White and light blue with rainbows and bears
Construction Foam sleeping pad, padded bumpers, cloth cover
Hazards Foot openings wider than allowed; sides too low; no stand (fall hazard on elevated surfaces)

How to get a refund: You must destroy the product first. Cut the foam, pad, and cover in half. Photograph all three pieces. Email the photos to YGJTrecall@outlook.com to request a full refund.

Why These Hazards Matter

Baby loungers are often used as a comfortable place for infants to rest or lie supported. But the CPSC has flagged consistent problems across many lounger designs in recent years — and the hazards follow a pattern.

When a lounger's sides are too low, an infant can roll out. When the foot opening is wider than the mandatory standard allows, a small infant can slip through and become entrapped — their torso passing partway through while their head is too large to follow, or vice versa. Either scenario can lead to positional asphyxia. When the lounger has no stand and is placed on an elevated surface like a couch or changing table, a fall becomes possible.

These are not theoretical risks. The CPSC has issued dozens of lounger-related recalls in the past several years as manufacturers producing these products through third-party e-commerce platforms have frequently failed to test them to mandatory infant sleep product standards.

Safe Sleep Reminders

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that infants sleep on a firm, flat surface in their own sleep space — a crib, bassinet, or play yard that meets CPSC standards. Loungers, even those not under recall, are not approved for unsupervised infant sleep. They are designed for supervised, awake use only.

If your child has been using either recalled lounger for sleep, transition to a crib or bassinet immediately.

What's Next

The full recall notices for both products are available at cpsc.gov/Recalls. If you're unsure whether a lounger you own is recalled, search by product name at cpsc.gov or check your Amazon, SHEIN, or VEVOR order history for the product description and purchase date. When in doubt, stop using it and contact the seller or CPSC directly at 1-800-638-2772.
