CPSC Recalls ~118,000 Criblike Mattresses Sold on Amazon — What Parents Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | July 5, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

If you've purchased an aftermarket mattress for a portable crib or play yard on Amazon in the past few years, there's a significant recall you need to check right now.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) has recalled approximately 118,000 Criblike brand infant mattresses sold on Amazon by a seller called Mengna. The recalled mattresses may not fit properly in certain play yards or non-full-sized cribs, leaving a dangerous gap that poses a deadly entrapment and suffocation risk to infants.

Which Products Are Recalled

Three models are included in the recall:

Model Type Dimensions Price Range Sale Dates
XCD-001 Pack-n-Play mattress ~38 x 26 inches $20–$60 May 2023–September 2025
XCD-002 Pack-n-Play mattress ~38 x 26 inches $20–$60 May 2023–September 2025
MNCL-001 Mini-crib mattress ~38 x 24 inches $20–$60 May 2023–September 2025

All three were sold exclusively on Amazon.com. If you purchased a Criblike mattress during this window, check your Amazon order history for the model number.

What Makes These Mattresses Dangerous

The hazard is an entrapment gap. When an aftermarket mattress is undersized for the play yard or crib it's placed in, the edges don't reach the walls. An infant can roll toward the side, press their face against the mattress edge and the mesh or fabric wall, and be unable to move away — blocking their ability to breathe. This kind of entrapment can happen quickly and silently, especially during sleep.

The CPSC specifically states that these Criblike mattresses may not adequately fit certain play yards or non-full-sized cribs, leaving gaps that pose deadly entrapment and suffocation risks.

The mini-crib model (MNCL-001) has an additional problem: it failed mandatory mattress flammability standards. Under federal regulations, all infant sleep surface mattresses must meet minimum fire resistance requirements. This mattress does not.

What to Do Right Now

If you have one of the recalled models, stop using it immediately — including for naps. Do not place an infant in any sleep space with a recalled mattress.

The remedy process:

  1. Stop using the mattress now.
  2. Cut the mattress in half so it cannot be used again.
  3. Take a photo of the cut mattress.
  4. Email the photo to Criblike-service_2025@outlook.com to request a full refund.

You do not need to ship the mattress back. Destroying it and documenting it with a photo is the process Criblike has set up for the refund.

A Note on Aftermarket Sleep Surfaces

This recall is a good reminder that not all sleep surfaces are interchangeable. Play yards and portable cribs are designed with specific internal dimensions, and their original mattresses are sized and tested to fit those exact dimensions. Aftermarket replacements — even ones that look like they fit and are sold as compatible — may have small dimensional differences that create entrapment gaps not visible to the eye.

The safest approach is to use the mattress that came with your play yard or crib, or a replacement certified by the original manufacturer. If you need to replace a sleep surface, check that the replacement mattress meets ASTM and CPSC standards for the specific product it will be used in.

What's Next

The full recall notice is available at cpsc.gov/Recalls. You can also sign up for CPSC recall alerts at cpsc.gov/Recalls to receive notifications for future recalls by email. For questions about this specific recall, contact Mengna/Criblike via the email address above.
