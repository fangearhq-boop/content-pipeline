Back to School 2026 in Colorado Springs — What D11, D20, and D49 Families Need to Know Now

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | July 5, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

It's July 5. The holiday is over. And somewhere in the back of your mind, you already know: school is about six weeks away.

That gap feels long until it doesn't — and the families who get ahead of the back-to-school transition in early July tend to have a smoother first week than those who start scrambling in the last week of July. Here's a district-by-district look at what Colorado Springs families in D11, D20, and D49 need to know right now.

District 11 — Supply Kits for K-8 Students

D11 is providing each K-8 student with a basic school supply starter kit this year, which significantly reduces the burden on families at the start of the year. This means parents won't need to track down and purchase every item on a traditional supply list — the district handles the basics.

If you're new to D11 or need to update enrollment information for your student, the district's enrollment page is at d11.org. Families who moved over the summer or have children starting kindergarten should complete enrollment now to avoid delays in August.

District 20 — Supply Lists Available Now

D20 families can find school supply lists for the 2026–27 school year at asd20.org/school-supplies. Having the list now means you can shop before the back-to-school retail rush hits local stores in late July — typically when popular items sell out or get marked up. Buying a few weeks early, especially on items like folders, composition notebooks, and pencils, is one of the simplest ways to save both money and stress.

District 49 — Staggered First Day

D49 uses a staggered approach on the first day of school: only Kindergartners, sixth graders, and ninth graders attend. The rest of the student body starts the following day.

This is intentional. The staggered model gives students beginning a new building — kindergartners entering elementary school, sixth graders entering middle school, ninth graders entering high school — a chance to orient themselves, meet teachers, and learn routines before the full student body arrives. For families of those specific grade levels, it's worth building extra time into the morning on the first day.

For families of students in other grade levels — first through fifth grades, seventh and eighth grades, and tenth through twelfth grades — the first day is day two of the calendar. Check d49.org for the exact first-day date and any building-specific schedules.

Three Things to Do in the Next Two Weeks

Regardless of district, a few things are worth tackling now rather than later:

  • Start adjusting bedtimes. If your children have been sleeping later over the summer, shift bedtime about 15 minutes earlier every two to three days. Starting now gives you six weeks to ease the transition rather than forcing an abrupt reset the night before school starts.
  • Clean out and check the backpack. Before you start filling it with new supplies, take five minutes to empty last year's bag, check for broken zippers or straps, and decide whether it's still serviceable.
  • Update school physicals and immunizations. Colorado requires up-to-date immunizations for school entry. If your child is due for a well-child visit or booster, schedule it now while pediatric appointment slots are still available.

What's Next

Each district will publish more detailed back-to-school information — including teacher assignments, classroom supply requests, and orientation schedules — closer to the start of the year. Check your district's website and sign up for district email updates if you haven't already. D11: d11.org. D20: asd20.org. D49: d49.org.
