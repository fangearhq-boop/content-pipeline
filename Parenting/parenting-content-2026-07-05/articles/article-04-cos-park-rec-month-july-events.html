Free Family Events in Colorado Springs All July Long — Park and Rec Month Is Underway

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | July 5, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

July is National Park and Recreation Month, and Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services is running a full calendar of free community events through the end of the month under the theme "The Power Of." After a holiday week that kept many families indoors due to wildfire smoke and fire restrictions, the coming weeks offer a lineup of low-cost ways to get kids outside and connected with neighbors.

This Week: Zumba and a Community Fair at Panorama Park

Tomorrow — Thursday, July 6 — Panorama Park hosts two overlapping free events from 5 to 8 PM: Zumba in the Park and a Community Fair. The combination makes it a good pick for families with kids of different ages: the Zumba portion gets bodies moving, while the fair format gives kids room to explore at their own pace. No registration is required.

Three Contests Running All Month

In addition to the events, the city is running three community contests throughout July:

  • Coloring Contest: A Park and Recreation Month coloring sheet for kids, available through PRCS.
  • "Build Your Own Park" Challenge: A creative challenge inviting families to design their ideal neighborhood park.
  • Photo Contest: Part of the city's Discover COS Calendar project, which publishes community-submitted photos in an annual city calendar.

Details and submission instructions for each contest are at coloradosprings.gov/ParkandRecMonth.

Why This Matters for COS Families

Colorado Springs has more than 160 neighborhood parks — and Park and Recreation Month is one of the city's consistent efforts to get families using them. Events like the Community Fair and Music in the Park are designed to bring neighbors together in spaces that don't require a ticket or a registration. For families watching budgets, the all-free format makes it easy to say yes.

The Family Fun Walk on July 15 at Memorial Park is worth highlighting for families with younger children. Memorial Park is one of the city's largest, with open grass, a playground, and a lake — a longer outing that can easily fill a Saturday morning without spending anything.

What's Next

The full event schedule, contest details, and any additions or time updates are at coloradosprings.gov/ParkandRecMonth. You can also follow the City of Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services on social media for day-of updates closer to each event.
