### STORY 1: Aspen Acres Wildfire Smoke Safety — What COS Families Need to Do Now

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

```
🔥 If you've stepped outside this week and noticed that hazy horizon or smelled something smoky, that's the Aspen Acres Fire — and it matters for your kids' health.

The fire has burned more than 85,000 acres in the Wet Mountains since June 29 with zero containment as of July 4. Smoke has drifted north and east into Colorado Springs and the I-25 corridor, and air quality has degraded enough that local health officials are urging families to take precautions.

Here's what that means at home. Keep kids indoors as much as possible, especially on days when you can smell smoke or see a yellowish haze. Run HEPA air purifiers inside if you have them — they filter out the fine particles (PM2.5) that cause the most damage to small lungs. Close windows and doors even if it's hot. If your child needs outdoor time, go early in the morning and keep it brief. Avoid anything that gets kids breathing hard.

Kids' lungs are still developing, which makes them more vulnerable to wildfire smoke than adults. Symptoms to watch for include coughing, throat irritation, eye watering, and headaches. If your child has asthma or another respiratory condition, follow your doctor's plan and keep rescue medication accessible.

You can check current air quality at AirNow.gov or the AQI tracker on your phone's weather app. When it reads "Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups" or worse, that's a stay-inside day for kids.

We're watching this fire closely. Stay safe, COS families. 🙏

What are you doing to keep your family comfortable indoors this week?

### STORY 2: Recall — Criblike Mattresses (Mengna, Amazon): ~118,000 Units

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

```
⚠️ If you've ever bought an aftermarket mattress for a pack-and-play or mini-crib on Amazon, this recall is worth checking right now.

The CPSC has recalled about 118,000 Criblike brand crib mattresses sold on Amazon by a seller called Mengna. The products were available from May 2023 through September 2025 for $20 to $60. The recalled models are XCD-001, XCD-002 (Pack-n-Play mattresses, approximately 38 by 26 inches), and MNCL-001 (mini-crib mattresses, approximately 38 by 24 inches).

The problem: these mattresses may not fit certain play yards or non-full-sized cribs correctly. When a mattress is too small, it leaves a gap between the mattress edge and the crib wall. Infants can roll into that gap and press their face against the side, unable to move away — which is a suffocation hazard. The mini-crib version also failed mandatory flammability testing.

If you have one of these mattresses, stop using it immediately. The remedy is: cut the mattress in half, take a photo, and email it to Criblike-service_2025@outlook.com for a full refund.

This is a good reminder that aftermarket sleep surfaces — even ones that look like they fit — may not meet the safety standards designed for the specific product they go in. The safest approach is always to use the manufacturer's original mattress for your sleep space.

Has anyone come across this recall before today?
```

### STORY 3: Two Baby Lounger Recalls — Vevor and YGJT Products

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

```
🚨 Two baby lounger recalls this week — and if you bought one on Amazon, Wayfair, or SHEIN, it's worth checking what's in your nursery right now.

Recall No. 1: Vevor Baby Loungers. About 237 units were sold at VEVOR.com, Amazon.com, and Wayfair.com from November 2024 through January 2026 for around $30. The loungers came in several prints: snow deer, good night, forest friends (oval and backpack), and green. The CPSC says the sides are too low to contain an infant, the foot openings are wider than allowed, and there's no stand — all of which create entrapment and fall hazards. To get a full refund, contact Vevor at 855-599-6320 (Monday–Friday, 9 AM–5 PM PT) or email recalling@vevor.com.

Recall No. 2: YGJT Baby Loungers. About 2,500 units were sold on SHEIN.com from June through August 2025 for $18 to $21. They're white and light blue with rainbows and bears on the cover. They have the exact same hazards: sides too low, foot openings too wide, and no stand. The remedy here is destruction: cut the foam, pad, and cover in half and email photos to YGJTrecall@outlook.com for a full refund.

Both products violate the mandatory federal standard for infant sleep products. Neither is safe to use, pass on, or donate.

If you bought either of these and can't find the original packaging, check Amazon or SHEIN for your order history.

Have you checked your baby gear for recalls lately? The CPSC site (cpsc.gov/Recalls) is the most current place to look.
