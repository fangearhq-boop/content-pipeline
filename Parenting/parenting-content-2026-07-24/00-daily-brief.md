### STORY 1: Target Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy Recalled — Choking Hazard

**Tier:** 1
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera
**Status:** research_complete
**Posting window:** 9:05 AM–3:05 PM MT

Target is recalling approximately 49,000 Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toys due to a choking hazard. The CPSC announced the recall on July 2, 2026, after nine reports of the clear plastic dome detaching from the blue whale-shaped base, exposing small colored balls. One child choking incident has been confirmed. The toy was sold exclusively at Target stores nationwide from August 2025 through January 2026 for approximately $10. Remedy is a full refund — consumers can return to any Target store or request a prepaid mail-in return label.

**Key facts:**
- Recall date: July 2, 2026
- Units recalled: ~49,000
- Hazard: dome detaches, small ball choking risk
- 9 dome-detachment reports; 1 confirmed child choking
- Sold exclusively at Target, Aug 2025–Jan 2026, ~$10
- Remedy: full refund at any Target store or via prepaid mail-in
- Source: CPSC.gov

### STORY 3: AAP Updates Iron Deficiency Screening Guidelines — First Major Revision Since 2010

**Tier:** 1
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera
**Status:** research_complete
**Posting window:** 11:05 AM–5:05 PM MT

The American Academy of Pediatrics has published a new clinical report in the July 2026 issue of Pediatrics updating its guidance on iron deficiency screening, diagnosis, and treatment in children and adolescents. This is the first major revision since 2010. Key changes include universal screening for all infants between 9 and 18 months (with earlier screening at 9–12 months for breastfed infants, who are at higher risk without iron supplementation), and universal screening for all menstruating adolescents within one year of their first period and no later than age 14. New diagnostic thresholds: serum ferritin at or below 20 ng/mL indicates iron deficiency in young and school-age children; at or below 30 ng/mL in adolescents. First-line treatment remains once-daily ferrous sulfate. IV iron therapy is now formally recommended for patients who do not respond to oral iron, have malabsorption syndromes, or chronic inflammatory conditions.

**Key facts:**
- Published: July 2026, Pediatrics (clinical report cited June 30, 2026)
- Replaces: 2010 AAP guidelines
- New: universal infant screening at 9–18 months
- Breastfed infants: screen at 9–12 months
- New: universal teen screening by age 14, within 1 year of first period
- Ferritin thresholds: ≤20 ng/mL (children), ≤30 ng/mL (adolescents)
- First-line treatment: ferrous sulfate once daily (oral)
- New: IV iron now recommended for non-responders and malabsorption cases
- Sources: guidelinecentral.com, medscape.com, healthychildren.org, contemporarypediatrics.com
