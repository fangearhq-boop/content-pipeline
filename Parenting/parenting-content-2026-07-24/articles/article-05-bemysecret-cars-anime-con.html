BeMySecret Cars & Anime Con Returns to Colorado Springs This Weekend: Kids Under 10 Get In Free

By Jamie Rivera | July 24, 2026 | COS Parenting

The BeMySecret Cars and Anime Con returns to Colorado Springs for its 5th anniversary this weekend, running Saturday, July 25, and Sunday, July 26, at the Colorado Springs Event Center on Palmer Park Boulevard. Children 10 and under are admitted free, which makes this a lower-cost option for families who want to spend a day at a convention-style event without paying for every member of the group.

The event combines an indoor anime and pop-culture convention with a two-day indoor car show — an unusual pairing that broadens its appeal beyond the typical anime convention audience.

What to Expect

BeMySecret is structured around two parallel tracks: the convention floor and the car show. The convention floor features anime and pop-culture vendors, a cosplay and model contest with awards, special guests including voice actors, and exclusive merchandise released for the 5th anniversary edition.

The indoor car show runs both days alongside the convention, displaying vehicles in a climate-controlled setting. For a family where one parent is enthusiastic about anime and the other is not, the car show component provides something to engage with while others browse vendor tables or attend panels.

Cosplay is welcome, and the contest gives participants a structured showcase if they want to show off a costume. The awards ceremony is part of the weekend's programming, so families who enjoy watching creative work recognized will find that worth attending.

Tickets and What to Buy in Advance

The difference between buying in advance and buying at the gate is meaningful for adults: $30 per day online versus $40 at the gate. For a family with two adults planning to attend one day, that is a $20 savings by purchasing tickets ahead of time. Youth ages 11 to 17 can purchase a two-day pass for $10, which is a strong value for a teenager with interest in anime, cosplay, or car culture.

Children 10 and under are free, no ticket required. Families should confirm the age cutoff policy with the event before arrival, as conventions occasionally apply it on a case-by-case basis.

Tickets are available online through the Colorado Springs Event Center and third-party platforms. Buying in advance also guarantees entry if the event reaches capacity, which is more likely on Saturday afternoon.

Getting There

The Colorado Springs Event Center is located at 3960 Palmer Park Boulevard, which is accessible from both I-25 and Academy Boulevard. The venue has a dedicated parking lot. Saturday hours run until 7 p.m., making it a practical all-day option, while Sunday closes at 5 p.m. — a better fit for families who want to be home for dinner.
