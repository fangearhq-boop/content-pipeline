Target Recalls Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy: Choking Hazard, Full Refund Available

By Jamie Rivera | July 24, 2026 | COS Parenting

Target is recalling approximately 49,000 Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toys due to a choking hazard. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission announced the recall on July 2, 2026, following nine reports of the clear plastic dome detaching from the toy's blue whale-shaped base and exposing the small colored balls inside. One child choking incident has been confirmed.

If you purchased this toy for a young child, stop using it immediately. Return it to any Target store for a full refund. No receipt is required, and Target is also offering a prepaid return shipping label for families who prefer to mail the item back.

What the Product Is

The Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy is designed for young children. When a child presses a plunger on top of the toy, small colored balls inside the clear dome are propelled upward in a "popping" action. The toy is branded Gigglescape, with that name printed on the packaging and imprinted on the bottom of the blue whale-shaped base.

The recall affects units sold at Target stores nationwide from August 2025 through January 2026. If your household purchased this toy during that period, it falls within the recalled range regardless of when you started using it.

What to Do

The CPSC and Target recommend the following steps:

  • Stop using the toy immediately and keep it away from children.
  • Return it to any Target store for a full refund. No receipt is required.
  • If you prefer to mail the toy back, contact Target customer service to request a prepaid return label.

The refund covers the full original purchase price. Target's customer service can be reached through its website or in-store guest services desk.

Who Is Most at Risk

The choking risk is most significant for young children — typically those under four years old — who are in the developmental stage where they explore objects with their mouths. If the dome detaches during play, the small balls inside become accessible and are the right size to present a serious choking hazard.

If a child in your household experienced any difficulty breathing or swallowing while playing with this toy, contact your pediatrician or call 911 in an emergency.
