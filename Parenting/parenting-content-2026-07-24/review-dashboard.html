Target Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy Recalled — Choking Hazard
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Colorado Springs families — if you have the Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy from Target, please stop using it. The CPSC announced a recall on July 2, 2026, for approximately 49,000 units of this blue whale-shaped popping toy. The clear plastic dome can detach from the base, exposing small colored balls inside — a choking hazard for young children. There have been nine reports of the dome detaching, including one confirmed case of a child choking. The toy was sold exclusively at Target stores nationwide from August 2025 through January 2026 for around $10. What to do: Return the toy to any Target store for a full refund. You can also contact Target to request a prepaid return shipping label if you prefer to mail it back. No receipt is required. The refund covers the full original purchase price. If you know another family who bought this toy for their kiddos, please share this post. *Source: CPSC.gov* ---

AAP Updates Iron Deficiency Screening Guidelines — First Major Revision Since 2010
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Something worth bringing up at your child's next well-visit: the American Academy of Pediatrics just published updated iron deficiency screening guidelines in July 2026 — and this is the first major revision since 2010. Here's what the new guidelines say: For infants: All babies should now be universally screened for iron deficiency between 9 and 18 months. Babies who are primarily breastfed should be screened earlier — between 9 and 12 months — because breast milk alone does not provide enough iron for older infants without supplementation. For teenagers: All menstruating adolescents should now be screened within one year of their first period, and no later than age 14. Teens with heavy periods or low-iron diets should be screened on an ongoing basis. The AAP also established new diagnostic thresholds: a serum ferritin of 20 ng/mL or below signals iron deficiency in children, and 30 ng/mL or below for adolescents. If your child's doctor doesn't bring it up, it is completely reasonable to ask: "Have we screened for iron deficiency recently?" That one question could make a real difference. Have you ever had your child tested for iron deficiency? What was that experience like? ---

### Long-Form Post Looking for something free to do with your family tomorrow? Green Mountain Falls has the answer. The 88th annual Bronc Day Festival takes place this Saturday, July 25, about 20 minutes west of Colorado Springs on U.S. Highway 24 — and admission is completely free. This year's theme is "Let Freedom Ring," honoring both the U.S. 250th anniversary and Colorado's 150th. Retired Fire Chief Steve Murphy of the Green Mountain Falls–Chipita Park Fire Department is this year's Grand Marshal. Here's the day's schedule: 7:30–10:00 a.m.: Pancake breakfast at Church in the Wildwood, UCC (10585 Ute Pass Ave.) 10:00 a.m.: Parade on Ute Pass Avenue All day at Gazebo Lake Park (100 Lake St.): Live music, pie contest, art vendors, food trucks, and dancing through 3 p.m. Bronc Day has been a Green Mountain Falls tradition for 88 years. There's something genuinely special about a community that has kept a festival going that long. If you go, bring cash for the food trucks and wear comfortable shoes for the parade.
