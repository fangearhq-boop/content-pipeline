Territory Days 2026: COS's 50th Annual Free Family Festival Is Memorial Day Weekend

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 16, 2026

Territory Days turns 50 this year — and Colorado Springs is doing it right. The 50th Annual Territory Days festival runs May 23 through 25, Memorial Day weekend, in the heart of Old Colorado City along Colorado Avenue. Admission is completely free, and the lineup is one of the most family-packed in the event's history.

Whether you've been before or you're bringing your family for the first time, here's what to expect and how to make the most of the weekend.

What Families Love Most

The petting zoo and toy train rides are consistent crowd-pleasers for younger kids. If you have a child under five or six, plan your first hour around those two — they tend to have lines that grow as the day goes on. The kids' play area is set up as its own dedicated zone, which means it's a bit removed from the busier vendor sections and easier to navigate with little ones.

The Wild West gunfight re-enactments are a genuine highlight for older kids — period-costumed characters act out dramatic showdowns in the street. It's theatrical and fun, and the performers are experienced at working with crowd interaction. The live birds of prey display is similarly engaging for all ages.

For families interested in history and culture, the Indigenous dancer performances and the blacksmith demonstrations offer something you don't see at typical summer festivals. They're worth planning around if you're trying to make the most of the educational side of the weekend.

Getting There Without the Headache

Colorado Avenue gets crowded during Territory Days, and street parking in Old Colorado City fills up early on Saturday and Sunday. The best approach is to use the C.A.B. Parking Garage at 130 S. Nevada Ave downtown — it's $1 per hour, which is easy on the budget even for a full-day visit. Free shuttle buses run from the garage directly to the festival, so you're not walking far with a stroller or young kids in tow.

If you're coming Saturday morning, arriving between 9 and 10 AM typically gets you into the festival before the midday crowds. If afternoon is better for your family's schedule, things are usually busiest between noon and 3 PM — plan accordingly.

What's Next

Territory Days runs May 23–25 in Old Colorado City. There's no registration or ticketing needed — just show up. It's one of the longest-running free family festivals in Colorado, and the 50th anniversary is a great reason to make it part of your Memorial Day weekend. If you're planning ahead beyond Territory Days, COS outdoor pools open Memorial Day weekend, and D11's last day of school is May 22 — so the summer season is officially underway.
