CPSC Recall: BabyBond Retractable Safety Gates Sold at Target — Return for Full Refund

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 16, 2026

If you bought a BabyBond retractable mesh safety gate from Target in the past year or two, check whether it's the recalled model — and stop using it if it is. The Consumer Product Safety Commission has announced a recall of approximately 280 BabyBond retractable safety gates manufactured by Infant Innovations due to an entrapment hazard that poses a risk of serious injury or death to young children.

The remedy is a full refund at any Target store location. No questions asked — just bring the gate in and return it.

Understanding the Hazard

Retractable mesh baby gates are designed to block doorways, stairwells, and other hazardous areas in your home. They work by mounting a mesh panel to the wall that pulls across the opening. The issue with this particular model is the gap between the bottom of the gate and the floor. When the gate is deployed, that gap is large enough for a young child's torso to fit through — which can result in the child becoming trapped.

Entrapment hazards are among the most serious in the baby product recall category because the child cannot free themselves and cannot call for help. The CPSC categorizes violations of the mandatory standard for expansion gates and enclosures as high-priority recalls.

The number of recalled units is relatively small at approximately 280, which suggests this product had limited distribution. However, small-run recalls can be harder to catch because they generate less media coverage — meaning parents who have this specific gate may not hear about the recall unless they're actively looking.

How to Return Your Gate

First, check the model number. Look for a white label stitched to the mesh fabric on the side of the gate — if it reads SH20.006DB, that's the recalled model. If you're not sure, contact Infant Innovations at 844-406-3569 (Monday through Friday, 10 AM to 4 PM PT) or email elsie@BabyBond-global.com. You can also find the full recall listing at cpsc.gov.

Once you've confirmed it's the recalled model, stop using it immediately and take it to any Target store for a full refund. Target's customer service team should be familiar with the recall, but bring any documentation you have — a receipt, order confirmation, or the recall page from cpsc.gov — to smooth the process.

What's Next

The CPSC maintains an active recall database at cpsc.gov/recalls where you can search for any product by name. For families shopping for a replacement baby gate, look for products that meet current ASTM F1625 standards for expansion gates and enclosures.
