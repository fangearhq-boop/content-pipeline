COS Summer Camp Guide 2026: Registration Is Open and Spots Are Filling

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 16, 2026

D11 wraps up on May 22. D49 is close behind. If summer plans aren't locked in yet for your family, now's the time — several COS programs are already filling, and the best options tend to go early. Colorado Springs has a genuinely strong camp lineup this year across theater, sports, outdoor adventure, art, and general day camp categories for kids ages four through 18.

Here's a rundown of what's available, where to register, and what to look for when comparing programs.

Theater and Performing Arts

CYT Colorado Springs (Christian Youth Theater) is running camps this summer including programs themed around Shrek Jr. and Willy Wonka Kids. CYT camps serve ages five through 13 and focus on combining performance skills with character development. Theater camp spots go quickly because cast sizes are capped — if your child is interested in performing arts, check availability now at cytcoloradosprings.org.

Outdoor and Adventure

Go West Camps offers outdoor day camp and teen adventure programs for ages five through 14. Colorado Springs's mountain proximity gives outdoor camps here an edge that programs in flatter cities can't match — kids get genuine trail time, mountain exposure, and outdoor skills in a real environment. Go West has a reputation for strong programming and a low counselor-to-camper ratio. Registration information is at gowestcamps.com.

Sports and Recreation

First Tee Southern Colorado runs golf camps that combine instruction with life skills development. First Tee is nationally recognized for its coaching philosophy, which treats golf as a vehicle for building confidence, discipline, and sportsmanship — not just swing mechanics. Life Time Colorado Springs offers multi-sport camps for ages four through 13 focused on movement, creativity, and confidence-building in a structured environment.

Arts and Creative

Peaks and Paint is a dedicated art studio camp at the Pikes Peak Artist Collective for kids ages six through 13. Campers work in a real studio environment covering painting, drawing, and pottery. It's a smaller, studio-focused experience that's notably different from general camp arts and crafts. Registration is at pikespeakartist.com/summer-camp.

Multi-Activity and Flexible Options

Steve and Kate's Camp uses a drop-in model: families pay a single summer pass fee that covers up to 53 days of camp attendance. This works well for families with unpredictable schedules or who need coverage that flexes with childcare gaps throughout the summer. Steve and Kate's Colorado Springs information is at steveandkatescamp.com/colorado-springs.

Where to Find More Options

The most comprehensive listing of Colorado Springs summer camps is at coloradospringskids.com/summer-camp-directory. It covers sports, arts, STEM, academics, and outdoor programs across all age ranges. The Macaroni KID Colorado Springs 2026 Summer Camp Guide at coloradosprings.macaronikid.com is another strong resource, organized by category and updated as new programs open registration.

A few practical tips: registration fills faster for programs with small group sizes (theater, art studio, adventure) than for large day camps with rolling enrollment. If cost is a factor, ask each provider about financial assistance — many Colorado Springs camps have scholarship funds that aren't prominently advertised. And if your child qualifies for the Colorado SUN Bucks summer EBT benefit ($120 per eligible child, issuing end of May), those funds can be used at any EBT-accepting grocery store to offset summer food costs while camp spending comes from other parts of the budget.

What's Next

D11's last day is May 22. Registration for most summer programs is open now, with many programs starting the week of June 2. For the full directory, visit coloradospringskids.com/summer-camp-directory. Families who haven't registered yet — especially for theater, art, and small-group outdoor programs — should check availability this week.
