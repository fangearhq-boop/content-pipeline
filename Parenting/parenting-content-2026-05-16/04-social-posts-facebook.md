### STORY 1: Cosyland Tower Stools Recall

**Long-Form Post**

If your family uses a Cosyland children's tower stool, stop using it now. 🚨

The CPSC announced on May 14 that 125,200 Cosyland tower stools — sold on Amazon from April 2021 through November 2025 for about $70 — have been recalled due to fall and entrapment hazards. The recalled models are CS0003 and CS0092-4 (natural bamboo and gray, about 35 inches tall).

Here's what's happening: the platform inside can loosen over time, becoming unstable or detaching entirely. That's a fall hazard. And because a child's torso can fit through openings on the front and back sides of the stool, there's also a serious entrapment risk if the unit tips. The firm has received 25 incident reports, including 8 injuries ranging from scrapes to a fractured arm.

The good news is that there's a free fix. Contact Cosyland at 866-677-3889 (Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM PT) or go to cosyland.com/pages/recall. They'll mail you a repair kit — protective net, stabilizing feet, and installation instructions — at no cost. In the meantime, store the stool away from your kids.

If you're not sure whether your stool is one of the recalled models, check the model number (CS0003 or CS0092-4) on the underside of the unit.

Has your family used one of these stools? Have you already gotten the recall notice?

### STORY 2: D11 Contract Dispute

**Long-Form Post**

As D11 families count down to the last day of school on May 22, it's worth pausing on what's been happening behind the scenes this year — and what it could mean for fall.

In June 2025, the D11 Board of Education voted 7-1 to allow the district's master agreement with the Colorado Springs Education Association (CSEA) to expire. That agreement had been in place for more than 50 years. In its place, D11 introduced an employee handbook — which the union argues doesn't carry the same legal standing as a collectively bargained contract.

Teachers went on strike in October 2025. In early May 2026, educators and parents came back out to picket a D11 Board meeting, marking more than 300 days since the agreement ended. The union's argument has been consistent: without a binding contract, teachers have less job security, and that affects who stays in our schools.

The district has pushed back, saying it's focused on progress and rejects what it calls misinformation being spread by union organizers. Both sides have their view, and COS families are caught in the middle — watching teachers they know and trust navigate uncertainty as the school year closes.

Whether you're a family in D11, D20, or D49, this situation raises real questions: Is your school keeping good teachers? Are there open positions going into next year? It's worth asking.

What are you hearing from teachers at your school — and what questions do you have about next fall?

### STORY 3: Territory Days 2026 — 50th Anniversary

**Long-Form Post**

Mark your calendars: Territory Days is back May 23–25 in Old Colorado City, and this year is the 50th anniversary. 🎪

It's one of the best free family weekends in Colorado Springs, and the lineup proves it: 180+ food and craft booths, live music from more than 25 bands across multiple stages, a petting zoo, toy train rides, mechanical bull, gold panning, live birds of prey, Indigenous dancers, Wild West gunfight re-enactments, and a dedicated kids' play area. Memorial Day weekend and completely free admission.

The festival runs along Colorado Avenue between 23rd and 27th Street in the heart of Old Colorado City. Parking is easy: head to the C.A.B. Parking Garage at 130 S. Nevada Ave downtown — it's $1 per hour — and hop on the free shuttle buses that run to the festival all weekend.

A few tips for families: mornings are typically cooler and less crowded if you've got young kids. Afternoons bring more music and the full vendor market energy. The petting zoo and kids' area tend to be busiest midday Saturday.

### STORY 4: BabyBond Safety Gates Recall

**Long-Form Post**

If you bought a BabyBond retractable mesh safety gate from Target in the last year or two, check it right now.

The CPSC has announced a recall of approximately 280 BabyBond retractable safety gates (model number SH20.006DB) due to a serious entrapment hazard. The issue: a child's torso can fit through the gap between the bottom of the gate and the floor. That gap shouldn't exist — and it puts young kids at risk of becoming stuck, which can cause serious injury or death.

The gates were sold in black, gray, and white on Target.com from November 2024 through October 2025 for about $50. They have a metal frame and adjust in width up to 55 inches.

Here's what to do: stop using the gate immediately and return it to any Target store for a full refund. No questions asked. You can also contact the company directly at 844-406-3569 (Monday through Friday, 10 AM to 4 PM PT) or by email at elsie@BabyBond-global.com.

The model number is on a white label stitched to the mesh fabric on the side of the gate — if it says SH20.006DB, that's the one.

Did you buy one of these? Worth a quick check.
