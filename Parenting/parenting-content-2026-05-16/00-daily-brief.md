### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — Cosyland Children's Tower Stools (125,200 Units)
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Angle:** The CPSC announced on May 14 that Cosyland has recalled 125,200 children's tower stools (models CS0003 and CS0092-4) due to fall and entrapment hazards. The platform inside can loosen over time, causing instability or detachment, and a child's torso can fit through openings on the front and back sides. The firm reports 25 incidents involving stability issues and falls, resulting in eight injuries ranging from scrapes to a fractured arm. The stools were sold on Amazon from April 2021 through November 2025 for about $70. Parents should stop use immediately and contact Cosyland for a free repair kit (protective net, stabilizing feet, installation instructions).
**Key Facts:**
- Recall date: May 14, 2026; 125,200 units affected
- Models: CS0003 and CS0092-4; bamboo and gray colors; 16"D x 18"W x 35"T
- Sold on Amazon.com, April 2021 – November 2025, ~$70
- Hazard: platform loosens → unstable/detaches → fall; torso fits through openings → entrapment
- Incidents: 25 reports; 8 injuries (scrapes, contusions, 1 fractured arm)
- Action: stop use immediately; contact Cosyland for FREE repair kit
- Phone: 866-677-3889 (Mon–Fri, 9 AM–5 PM PT)
- Website: cosyland.com/pages/recall

**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** Local News
**Angle:** Educators, parents, and community members rallied in early May to highlight that it has been more than 300 days since District 11's Board of Education voted to end the 50-year-old master agreement with the Colorado Springs Education Association (CSEA). ### STORY 4: CPSC Recall — BabyBond Retractable Safety Gates (280 Units)
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Angle:** Infant Innovations has recalled approximately 280 BabyBond retractable mesh safety gates due to entrapment risk. The gates were sold exclusively at Target.com from November 2024 through October 2025 for about $50.
