### STORY 1: "Relaxing Baby" Swim Float CPSC Warning

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

If you have a "Relaxing Baby" brand swim float at home — or know someone who does — please share this.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an emergency warning telling parents to stop using these products immediately. The floats can flip over or submerge the occupant underwater, and one 2-year-old child has already died while using one. ⚠️

These are the blue and green inflatable seat floats marketed for children ages 3–36 months. They were sold on Amazon.com and eBay. The company behind them — Wu He/Relaxing Baby, based in China — has refused to cooperate with the CPSC on a recall.

Here's what the CPSC says to do right now:

Stop using the float immediately. Puncture the plastic in any location where the product can inflate. Dispose of it. Do not sell it, donate it, or give it away to anyone.

With swim season coming up in Colorado Springs, we know a lot of families are starting to think about pool days and water activities. If this float is in your home, in your car, or at your grandparents' house — it needs to go.

### STORY 2: Bellabu Bear Robes Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

Quick check for your closet: if you have a Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa robe for your kids, check your tag.

Bellabu Bear has issued a recall for their children's sherpa robes because they fail federal flammability standards — meaning if they're near a heat source or flame, they could burn faster than the law allows for children's sleepwear.

The recalled robes include a lot of recognizable character prints: PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Garfield, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora, and several more. They were sold in sizes S/M and M/L.

Where they were sold: Saks Fifth Avenue stores, Bellabubear.com, Saksfifthavenue.com, Letthembelittleboutique.com, and other online retailers. Sales ran from January 2024 through July 2025, at around $60 each.

No injuries have been reported — but testing confirmed the robes don't pass the mandatory burn test for children's sleepwear. That's why they're being recalled now, before something happens.

What to do: Stop using the robe and contact Bellabu Bear for a full refund or store credit.
📞 888-703-7752 (Mon–Fri, 9am–4pm PT)
📧 help@bellabubear.com

A lot of these were probably received as gifts — so it's worth giving grandparents and family members a heads-up too.

### STORY 5: Outdoor Play Research Roundup

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

With warmer days showing up in Colorado Springs, here's a friendly reminder from research: outdoor time is genuinely, measurably good for your kids.

We're not talking about "it's nice to go outside." We're talking about concrete benefits backed by research:

🌱 Kids who play regularly in green spaces have lower anxiety and depression levels. Not just "seem happier" — measurably lower.

🧠 Outdoor play improves focus and creativity in ways that indoor play typically doesn't. The varied, self-directed nature of outdoor play helps children learn to regulate their emotions and behaviors.

☀️ Time outside boosts serotonin production through physical activity and sunlight — a genuine mood lifter for kids.

For children with ADHD in particular: research has found that kids who regularly play in green settings (parks, trails, wooded areas) show milder ADHD symptoms compared to those who primarily play indoors or in built outdoor spaces.

Colorado Springs families have something a lot of families don't: easy access to hundreds of miles of trails and some of the best urban parks in Colorado. Garden of the Gods, Bear Creek, Fountain Creek, Ute Valley Park, Palmer Park — the list goes on.
