Stop Using "Relaxing Baby" Swim Floats Now — CPSC Warning After Child Drowning Death

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 10, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an emergency warning after a 2-year-old died using a "Relaxing Baby" swim float. The Chinese seller has refused to cooperate with CPSC. These products were sold on Amazon and eBay for children ages 3 to 36 months. Destroy them immediately.

With swim season approaching in Colorado Springs, there's a product you need to check for right now: the "Relaxing Baby" brand inflatable swim float.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an emergency warning urging parents and caregivers to stop using these products immediately. The blue and green inflatable seat floats — marketed for children ages 3 to 36 months — can flip over or submerge the child sitting inside them, posing a drowning risk. One 2-year-old child has already died while using one.

This is not a routine recall. The company behind the product, Wu He/Relaxing Baby, based in China, has been unresponsive to CPSC requests for a voluntary recall. That means the CPSC cannot force the products out of the market through a standard recall process. They can only warn consumers directly — which is what this is.

What the Product Looks Like

The floats are inflatable and come in blue and green. They feature a seat in the center where the child sits, secured with a buckle. Some models include a detachable rear float. They were sold on Amazon.com and on eBay. If you purchased a float with this general description from either of these platforms and it carries the "Relaxing Baby" name, it is this product.

The floats were marketed as a relaxing and safe option for infants and toddlers in the water. That description, combined with the bucket-style seat design, may have made them appear more secure than they actually are. The CPSC's warning makes clear that they are not safe.

What to Do Right Now

The CPSC is specific and unambiguous about the required action: stop using the float immediately, puncture the plastic in any location where the product can inflate, and dispose of it. Do not sell it. Do not give it to another family. Do not donate it to a thrift store or pass it along. The product needs to be destroyed before disposal to prevent it from being used by someone else.

If you're unsure whether your float is affected, the safest approach is to err on the side of caution with any floats that match this general description — inflatable, seat-based design for infants or toddlers — purchased from Amazon or eBay.

Water Safety Resources for COS Families

The American Academy of Pediatrics does not consider any inflatable float or "puddle jumper" style device to be a substitute for lifejackets or for direct adult supervision in and around water. These products may provide buoyancy, but they can also give parents and children a false sense of security.

For young children — especially those under age 4 — the AAP recommends arms-length supervision around any water, including pools, lakes, and even bathtubs. Children can drown silently and quickly. A product designed to help is not a substitute for an adult whose full attention is on the child.

Colorado Springs families can look for swim safety programs through the Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department, which offers swimming lessons at city pools including Cottonwood Creek Recreation Center and the Aquatics Center at GCC.
