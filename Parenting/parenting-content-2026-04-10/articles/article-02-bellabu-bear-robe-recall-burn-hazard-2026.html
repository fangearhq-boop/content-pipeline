Bellabu Bear Recalls Kids' Sherpa Robes — PAW Patrol, Minecraft Prints Fail Burn Test

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 10, 2026

Bellabu Bear is recalling its bamboo sherpa robes for children because they don't pass federal flammability standards for sleepwear. The affected robes include popular licensed character prints. Parents can get a full refund or store credit.

If your kids received a Bellabu Bear robe as a birthday or holiday gift, it's time to check the tag.

Bellabu Bear has issued a recall for its bamboo sherpa children's robes after CPSC testing found the products violate mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. The robes can burn faster than federal law allows — posing a burn risk for children who wear them near any heat source or flame.

No injuries have been reported. But the safety testing failure is real, and the recall is active. Parents should stop using the affected robes immediately.

Which Robes Are Affected

The recalled robes are Bellabu Bear's bamboo sherpa collection — the ones with sherpa lining, two front pockets, and a matching belt. They were sold in children's sizes S/M and M/L. The neck label reads "Bellabu Bear," "BB-NW12109," and the size.

The character prints included in the recall are extensive: PAW Patrol, Garfield, Minecraft, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, and Dora — along with non-character prints including Nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons.

If the robe is a Bellabu Bear sherpa style with any of these prints, it's covered by the recall.

Where They Were Sold and for How Much

The recalled robes were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue stores and at several online retailers from January 2024 through July 2025. Online sellers included bellabubear.com, saksfifthavenue.com, letthembelittleboutique.com, and other online retailers. The price at time of sale was approximately $60.

Given the character selection — PAW Patrol and Minecraft especially — these robes were popular gift items for children ages 3 through about 10. If extended family members gave your kids a "fancy robe" as a gift in 2024 or the first half of 2025, it's worth checking whether it's a Bellabu Bear sherpa model.

What to Do

Stop using the robe immediately. Take it away from children now, and contact Bellabu Bear to arrange a full refund or store credit for the original purchase price.

What's Next

This recall is a good reminder to periodically check the products your kids use regularly — especially items marketed as "soft" or "cozy" that could be used near bedtime. The CPSC has a free recall search tool at cpsc.gov where you can check any product by brand name.

If you purchased the robe at Saks Fifth Avenue in-store and don't have a receipt, Bellabu Bear's customer service team is the right starting point — they can work with you on the refund process.
