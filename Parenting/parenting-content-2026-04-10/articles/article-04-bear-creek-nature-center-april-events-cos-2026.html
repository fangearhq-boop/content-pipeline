Bear Creek Nature Center April Programs: What COS Families Should Know Right Now

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 10, 2026

Bear Creek Nature Center has family programs running today and through the rest of April — including a free early literacy event for deaf and hard-of-hearing families, homeschool days, and nature programs for toddlers. Prepaid registration is required for most events.

Bear Creek Nature Center, operated by El Paso County Parks, has a strong spring calendar running through April — and several of the programs are happening in the next two weeks. If you haven't been to Bear Creek lately, this is a good reason to plan a trip.

Here's what's coming up for Colorado Springs families.

Today — Nature Tots (Ages 2-3)

If you have a 2 or 3 year old, there are two sessions of Nature Tots happening today, April 10: one at 10am and one at 2pm. The program is designed specifically for this age group and costs $3 per person. Prepaid registration is required.

Nature Tots programs at Bear Creek typically involve age-appropriate nature exploration — touching, observing, and beginning to understand the outdoor world at a pace that works for toddlers. It's a good first introduction to structured outdoor learning for younger children.

April 17 — Early Literacy Event for Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing Families (Free)

On April 17, from 1:30pm to 3pm, Bear Creek Nature Center is hosting a free Early Literacy Event for families with deaf and hard-of-hearing children from birth through age 8. The event is a collaboration between the Colorado School for the Deaf and the Blind (CSDB) and Bear Creek Nature Center.

The program includes readings in American Sign Language, learning about summer wildlife, and outdoor nature activities. It's designed to be accessible and welcoming for families navigating early literacy in the deaf and hard-of-hearing community.

This event is free. Registration is required — email outreach@csdb.org to reserve your spot. This kind of event doesn't come around often, and the free registration makes it easy to commit. If this applies to your family or someone you know, it's worth sharing.

April 23 — Homeschool Programs

On April 23, from 1pm to 3:30pm, Bear Creek offers structured homeschool programming with simultaneous activities designed for different age groups. Children ages 8 and up may be dropped off; children under age 7 must have a caregiver present. The cost is $4 per person, with prepaid registration required.

Bear Creek's homeschool programs pair well with science and nature units and give homeschool students a structured outdoor learning experience with guidance from naturalists. The simultaneous activities for different ages mean families with multiple children at different levels can attend together without older kids waiting on younger ones.

What's Next

Bear Creek Nature Center runs programs through the spring and summer. To see the full schedule and register for any of these events, visit the El Paso County Parks website at parks.elpasoco.com or contact Bear Creek Nature Center directly. Registration fills up — particularly for the free April 17 event — so don't wait to secure your spot.

These programs are a great way to build outdoor habits and nature literacy for Colorado Springs kids during the best weather months of the year. We'll share more Bear Creek programming as spring continues.
