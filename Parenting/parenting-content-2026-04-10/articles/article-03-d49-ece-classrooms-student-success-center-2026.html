D49 Is Adding Two New Early Childhood Classrooms at Student Success Center for Fall 2026

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 10, 2026

School District 49 will open two new early childhood education classrooms at the D49 Student Success Center when the 2026-27 school year begins in August. Full-day preschool will also be available. East-side Colorado Springs families should start planning now.

Here's some good news for families in Colorado Springs's east-side communities: School District 49 is expanding its early childhood education capacity for fall 2026.

D49 Early Childhood Education has announced the opening of two new classrooms at the D49 Student Success Center, launching at the start of the 2026-27 school year in August 2026. The district has also confirmed that full-day preschool space will be available for the coming school year.

For families in D49's service area — which includes east Colorado Springs, Fountain, Falcon, Peyton, and surrounding communities — this expansion means more public early childhood options closer to home.

Why This Matters

Early childhood education access has been a documented challenge in Colorado Springs. The city has a significant gap between the number of children under age 5 and the number of available licensed childcare and preschool spots. Public school-based ECE programs are often the most accessible option for working families — they typically operate on school-year schedules, align with district transportation, and are offered at lower cost than private childcare.

D49's Student Success Center expansion adds public-school preschool capacity to the east side of the city. That matters for families who work in Falcon, Peyton, or east Colorado Springs and need convenient early education options without a long commute to programs further west.

The availability of full-day preschool is also worth noting specifically. Full-day programs are significantly more useful for working parents than half-day options — they reduce the childcare puzzle that many families face when preschool only covers a few hours in the morning.

What Families Should Do Now

If you have a child who will be preschool-age in fall 2026, now is a good time to start your planning. D49's enrollment and registration process for the new ECE classrooms will be communicated through the district. Check the D49 website at d49.org for the most current information on early childhood education registration, enrollment timelines, and eligibility requirements.

If you're not sure whether your family is in the D49 service area, the district's website includes a school finder tool based on your address.
