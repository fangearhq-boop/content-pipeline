### STORY 2: Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower Recall

#### Long-Form Post

If you have a Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower in your kitchen, please check it today.

CPSC recalled approximately 25,235 of these toddler learning towers in May 2026. The platform inside can loosen over time with normal use — and when it does, it can become unstable or detach entirely, creating a fall hazard for the child standing on it. There have been 11 reports of children falling, including 3 injuries.

The recalled towers are the Classic and Contemporary Guidecraft Kitchen Helper models, sold from June 2022 through October 2023 at Amazon, Wayfair, Walmart, Target, Guidecraft.com, and other retailers for about $200.

To check if yours is affected: look for the item number and lot number on the foil sticker at the bottom of the platform. The full list of recalled numbers is at CPSC.gov (search "Guidecraft Kitchen Helper").

The good news: the remedy is free repair parts, not a full replacement. Contact Guidecraft at 800-524-3555 (Mon–Fri 8 AM–8 PM ET) to get the repair kit. Stop using the tower until the repair is complete.

### STORY 4: Father's Day Weekend Guide

#### Long-Form Post

Father's Day is coming up on June 21 — and there are some great Colorado Springs options worth knowing about.

A heads-up on the biggest one: Pikes Peak offers free admission for dads — but the free day is June 20, not June 21. The mountain is closed to general visitors on Father's Day itself for the Pikes Peak International Hill Climb race. So if your family tradition is a Pikes Peak drive with dad, book Saturday June 20.

That said, the Hill Climb on June 21 is actually a fantastic family spectator event — 156 turns, 12 miles from 9,390 feet up to the summit. A different but equally memorable way to spend Father's Day on Pikes Peak.

Other options for June 21: the Colorado Wolf and Wildlife Center has a family morning from 9–11 AM (reservations required, so call ahead); Garden of the Gods is always free and is genuinely spectacular; and the Colorado Renaissance Festival in Larkspur runs on Sundays through August, which makes the June 21 show a natural Father's Day outing.

Flying W Ranch is also giving dads a free hat on Father's Day — a small thing, but a good excuse for a ranch dinner.

### STORY 5: D11 Orton Academy Moving to Trailblazer Elementary

#### Long-Form Post

Did you know Colorado Springs has a public school that specializes in teaching kids with dyslexia?

Orton Academy is a D11 charter school that's been open since 2020. It serves students in kindergarten through 8th grade using the Orton-Gillingham method — a multisensory, research-based approach that's considered the gold standard for dyslexia education. It's free, it's within D11, and it's right here in Colorado Springs.

For the 2026-27 school year, Orton Academy is moving onto the Trailblazer Elementary School campus. If your child is currently enrolled, this is a location change to prepare for. If you've never heard of Orton Academy, this might be exactly the kind of program your family has been looking for.

About 1 in 5 kids has dyslexia — it's the most common learning difference. Many kids with dyslexia are in regular classrooms where they're falling behind not because they aren't capable, but because they haven't had the right instruction yet.

Fall 2026 enrollment is open now through D11's choice enrollment process. Visit d11.org to learn more or get in touch with Orton Academy directly.

Did you know about Orton Academy? We'd love to know how many COS families have found it helpful.
