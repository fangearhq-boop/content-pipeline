Father's Day in Colorado Springs 2026 — Your Local Activity Guide for June 21

By Sarah Morales | June 10, 2026

Father's Day is June 21 — 11 days away. Colorado Springs families have some genuinely great local options, and a few details worth knowing before you make plans. The biggest one: Pikes Peak's free admission for dads is June 20, not June 21.

Here's what's happening across COS for Father's Day weekend.

The Pikes Peak Timing Note

Every year, some COS families plan to take dad up Pikes Peak on Father's Day — and discover it's closed to general visitors. The Pikes Peak International Hill Climb takes place on June 21, which means the mountain is reserved for the race. No general visitor access that day.

The good news: Pikes Peak offers free admission for dads on June 20 — the Saturday before Father's Day. If you want to drive to the summit, go Saturday.

That said, the Hill Climb itself is an experience worth considering. The race covers 12.42 miles and 156 turns from 9,390 feet up to the 14,115-foot summit. Watching professional cars and motorcycles climb America's Mountain is a legitimate family spectator event — different from the usual summit drive, but genuinely memorable.

More COS Father's Day Options

Colorado Wolf and Wildlife Center

The center has a Father's Day morning event on June 21 from 9–11 AM. Space is limited — reservations are required. Call ahead to confirm availability and book a spot. It's a unique COS experience that most families only do a few times, which makes it feel special.

Garden of the Gods

Always free, always stunning. Garden of the Gods is one of those places that hits differently when you've lived in COS long enough to take it for granted — and Father's Day is a good occasion to go back. Pack a picnic, bring hiking shoes, and let the red rocks do the work.

Colorado Renaissance Festival

The Colorado Renaissance Festival opened its 49th season the weekend of June 13-14 in Larkspur (about 30 minutes from COS). It runs every Saturday and Sunday through August 2, which means the June 21 Sunday show is a Father's Day option. Knights, jousting, turkey legs, artisans, and a full day's worth of entertainment for families who enjoy that kind of thing — which, let's be honest, is most families.

Flying W Ranch

Dads get a free hat on Father's Day at the Flying W Ranch — a small touch, but a good excuse for a ranch dinner and evening show.
