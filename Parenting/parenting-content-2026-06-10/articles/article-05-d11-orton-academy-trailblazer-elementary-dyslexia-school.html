D11's Orton Academy — Colorado Springs' Dyslexia-Focused School — Moving to Trailblazer Elementary

By Jamie Rivera | June 10, 2026

If your child struggles with reading — or if they've been identified as having dyslexia — there's something Colorado Springs families should know about: Orton Academy, a District 11 charter school that exists specifically to teach students with dyslexia.

And starting in the 2026-27 school year, it's moving to a new campus.

What Is Orton Academy

Orton Academy is a K-8 charter school within D11 that opened in 2020. It uses the Orton-Gillingham method — a structured, multisensory approach to reading and writing that is widely considered the gold standard for dyslexia education. The International Dyslexia Association recognizes Orton-Gillingham as an evidence-based approach, and research consistently shows it works for students who have not succeeded with conventional reading instruction.

The school serves students in kindergarten through 8th grade. Because it's a D11 charter school, it is a public school — free to attend for COS families who apply through D11's choice enrollment process.

What's Changing for 2026-27

For the 2026-27 school year, Orton Academy is moving onto the Trailblazer Elementary School campus in a shared model. This is a location change — families who are currently enrolled should expect a new campus address and building environment for fall.

For families who haven't been aware of Orton Academy, the move is a good occasion to learn that this resource exists. D11's school choice enrollment portal is the entry point for applications.

Why This Matters for COS Families

Dyslexia is the most common learning difference — research estimates that approximately 1 in 5 people has some form of it. In a school district the size of D11, that's a significant number of students. Many of them are in regular classrooms where reading instruction isn't designed for how their brains work.

The consequences of unrecognized or unsupported dyslexia can be significant: children who struggle with reading fall behind in every subject. They often internalize the difficulty as a character flaw rather than a neurological difference. Identifying dyslexia and connecting a child with appropriate instruction early can change the trajectory of their education entirely.

Orton Academy exists to do exactly that. Its location in Colorado Springs means COS families don't have to move or seek expensive private tutoring to access this kind of specialized instruction.

How to Learn More or Enroll

D11 families interested in Orton Academy for the 2026-27 school year should visit d11.org and navigate to the school choice enrollment section. Orton Academy is listed as one of D11's specialized school options. Applications for the coming school year may still be open — contact the school or D11's enrollment office directly to confirm timeline and availability.
