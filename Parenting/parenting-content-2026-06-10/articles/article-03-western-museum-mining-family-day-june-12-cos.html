Free Family Day at the Western Museum of Mining and Industry — This Saturday June 12

By Jamie Rivera | June 10, 2026

If your family is looking for a free, genuinely interesting outing this Saturday, the Western Museum of Mining and Industry in Colorado Springs is hosting Family Day on June 12. All the outdoor activities are free — no admission required.

We're talking real working historic machinery, a live blacksmith demonstration, a tractor-pulled hayride, and the Gold Panning Championships. For families who haven't been, this is one of those COS hidden gems that delivers every time.

What's Happening Saturday

Family Day at WMMI centers on the outdoor exhibits and demonstrations that make this museum unlike anything else in Colorado Springs:

  • Stamp mill in operation — the enormous stamp mill that processed ore is one of the most visually impressive pieces of machinery in COS. Watching it run gives you a real sense of the industrial scale of the Pikes Peak gold rush era.
  • Live blacksmith demonstration — a working forge, real tools, and someone who actually knows how to use them. Kids who've never seen metalworking up close find it hard to look away.
  • Tractor-pulled hayride — a classic, and a nice way to see the grounds without wearing out little legs.
  • Gold Panning Championships — competitive and hands-on. Whether your family participates or watches, it's a good time.

What to Know Before You Go

The outdoor activities are free. If you want to visit the indoor exhibits as well, check with the museum in advance about any admission fees for the full facility. The outdoor grounds and demonstrations are the main Family Day draw, and they don't require paid admission.

The museum is located at 225 North Gate Blvd, Colorado Springs CO 80921 — just north of the Air Force Academy off I-25. It's accessible and has adequate parking. Call ahead at 719-488-0880 if you have specific questions about timing or accessibility.

Why It's Worth the Trip

Colorado Springs sits on top of one of the most significant gold-rush-era mining regions in American history. The Pikes Peak gold rush of 1859 put this area on the map. The Western Museum of Mining and Industry preserves that history in a way that's tactile and real — not just static displays behind glass.

For kids who've grown up with interactive museums, seeing actual working machinery from the era that built this region is a different kind of learning. Family Day is designed to make that accessible.
