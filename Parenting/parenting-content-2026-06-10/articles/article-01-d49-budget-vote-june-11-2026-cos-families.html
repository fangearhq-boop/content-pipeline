D49 Budget Vote Is Tomorrow — What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know

By Jamie Rivera | June 10, 2026

If your kids attend a District 49 school in Colorado Springs, tomorrow is an important night. The D49 Board of Education votes Thursday, June 11 at 6:30 PM on the 2026-27 district budget — the plan that finalizes nearly $2 million in cuts and sets the course for the school year beginning this August.

The meeting is at Sand Creek High School, 7005 Carefree Cir N, Colorado Springs. The public is welcome to attend.

How Did D49 Get Here

In January 2026, the D49 Board of Education declared a state of "fiscal exigency" — a formal financial emergency that allows a district to make cuts that would otherwise require more extensive procedures. The declaration came after the district identified a significant budget gap driven partly by declining enrollment.

In February 2026, the board approved a set of reductions in a 4-to-1 vote. Those cuts include reductions in administrative staffing, cuts to teacher positions in non-core subjects and programs with low enrollment, reductions in supplemental staffing, and renegotiated vendor contracts. Programs including Base49, PEAK, preschool, and Elevates (the district's program for students ages 18-21) were among those identified as impacted.

What Tomorrow's Vote Does

Tomorrow's meeting is the formal budget adoption for the fiscal year beginning July 1, 2026. The board will vote to adopt the proposed budget, which incorporates the cuts already approved in February and reflects the state's per-pupil funding formula for next year. (Colorado's proposed state budget includes a per-pupil funding increase, which partially offsets the enrollment-related revenue decline.)

Once adopted, the budget goes into effect July 1 — less than three weeks away. The staffing and program changes it contains will be in place when school starts in August.

Can Families Participate

The meeting is a regular Board of Education meeting, which means it includes time for public comment. Families who want to address the board can sign up to speak at the meeting. The proposed budget has also been filed with the district's Chief Financial Officer's office, where it is available for public inspection in advance of the vote.

District 49's main website at d49.org has information about the board meeting schedule, contact information, and links to budget documents.
