### STORY 1: Vevor Baby Swing Recall

**S1-FB-LongForm** | Tier 1 | Window: 8:00 AM MT

If you purchased a Vevor baby swing in the past year, stop using it and check the model number immediately.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) and Sanven Technology have recalled approximately 1,020 Vevor baby swings due to a serious suffocation hazard. The swings were sold on vevor.com and Amazon.com from January through August 2025 for between $65 and $80.

The problem is the incline angle. These swings are inclined at more than 10 degrees — which violates the federal Safe Sleep for Babies Act and the mandatory ban on inclined infant sleepers. When a baby is placed in an inclined sleeper, their head can tip forward and block their airway. This is not a small risk. It has contributed to infant deaths in similar products.

**Affected models:** BB501K (white/gray, white base), BB702A (white/gray, gray base), and BB005K (black/gray, black base).

**What to do:** Stop using the swing immediately. Cut the cover in half, write "Recalled" on the base with a permanent marker, and take a photo. Email that photo to recalling@vevor.com for a full refund. You can also call 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Pacific time.

Colorado Springs families: please share this with anyone who has a Vevor swing in their home. A few seconds of checking could matter.

Do you have any of the affected models? Have you already contacted Vevor for your refund?

### STORY 2: COS Parks Budget Cuts

**S2-FB-LongForm** | Tier 1 | Window: 12:00 PM MT

If you are planning to take your children to a neighborhood park this summer in Colorado Springs, there are some significant changes you need to know about before you go.

Budget cuts to the city's Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department are having a real impact on the park experience this season. According to reporting from KRDO and information from a Parks Advisory Board meeting, the cuts mean:

Approximately 25% of neighborhood park permanent restrooms — that's about 10 facilities — will remain closed for the entire 2026 season. If you are heading to a park with small children, a call or check-in advance to confirm restroom availability would be a smart move.

Damaged or vandalized playground equipment will be removed from parks rather than repaired or replaced. This means that over the course of the summer, some parks may have fewer play structures available than families are used to.

The Julie Penrose Fountain at America the Beautiful Park will not receive its regular cleaning and restoration services this year, or in 2027, 2029, or 2030.

The Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department serves more than 300,000 Colorado Springs residents through three community centers, seven sports complexes, and dozens of parks. The community centers remain a good alternative for indoor or supervised recreation during the summer.

### STORY 4: AAP Car Seat Safety

**S4-FB-LongForm** | Tier 2 | Window: 3:00 PM MT

If you still think of car seat transitions as tied to a child's age — at one year old, at two years old — it is time to update that mental model.

The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its child passenger safety guidelines in February 2026, and the guidance now focuses entirely on a child's size relative to the car seat's manufacturer limits, not their age.

Here is what the 2026 AAP guidance says:

Children should remain in a rear-facing car seat for as long as possible — until they reach the maximum height or weight as defined by the car seat manufacturer. Once they exceed those limits, they move to a forward-facing seat with a harness, again staying in it until the manufacturer's maximum height/weight is reached. Then a booster seat with a vehicle seat belt, and finally a seat belt alone.

The reason for this change is straightforward: rear-facing seats are significantly more protective in a crash. A child who reaches a manufacturer's maximum limit for their forward-facing seat may still fit safely in their rear-facing seat for months longer — and that time matters in terms of protection.

For Colorado Springs families heading into a summer of road trips to Denver, Pikes Peak, or further, this is a good time to pull out the car seat manual and check your current setup against the manufacturer's specifications.

### STORY 5: National Bike Month

**S5-FB-LongForm** | Tier 2 | Window: 5:00 PM MT

May is National Bike Month — and Colorado Springs is one of the best cities in the state to put it to use with your family.

With more than 100 miles of multi-use trails running through the city and along the Front Range, families in Colorado Springs have access to some excellent options for cycling with children of all ages. Here are some of the best family-friendly routes to know:

**Pikes Peak Greenway Trail:** A 34-mile mostly paved corridor running through the heart of Colorado Springs. Flat stretches through Monument Valley Park and America the Beautiful Park make it accessible for younger riders.

**Bear Creek Regional Park:** Paved and natural surface trails through one of COS's most scenic parks. Great for introducing children to trail riding in a lower-traffic setting.

**Monument Valley Park:** A compact, mostly paved loop through a beautiful downtown park. One of the most forgiving options for kids learning to ride.

Before every ride, no matter the distance, go through this safety checklist:

- Helmet is CPSC certified and fits properly (two finger-widths above the eyebrow, chin strap snug but not tight)
- Replace any helmet after a significant impact, even if it looks intact
- Bike fits the child: both feet should be able to touch the ground
- Check tires, brakes, and seat height before each ride
- High-visibility clothing helps on multi-use trails shared with adults

Studies suggest properly fitted helmets can significantly reduce the risk of serious head injuries in cycling crashes. The investment in a well-fitted, CPSC-certified helmet is one of the best safety decisions a family can make.
