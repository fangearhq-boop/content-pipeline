### STORY 1: Vevor Baby Swing Recall — Suffocation Hazard (CPSC)
**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Product Safety)
**Angle:** CPSC and Sanven Technology have recalled approximately 1,020 Vevor baby swings sold on vevor.com and Amazon.com (January–August 2025) for between $65 and $80. The swings violate the mandatory safety standard for infant sleep products and the federal ban on inclined sleepers (Safe Sleep for Babies Act) because they have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees, posing a deadly suffocation risk. Affected models: BB501K (white/gray, white base), BB702A (white/gray, gray base), BB005K (black/gray, black base). Remedy: cut cover in half, write "Recalled" on base, photograph it, email to recalling@vevor.com; or call 855-599-6320 (Mon–Fri, 9 AM–5 PM PT) for a full refund.
**Key Facts:**
- Recall issued by CPSC / Sanven Technology
- ~1,020 units affected
- Sold Jan–Aug 2025 on vevor.com and Amazon; $65–$80
- Models: BB501K, BB702A, BB005K
- Violation: incline >10°, violates Safe Sleep for Babies Act / inclined sleeper ban
- Hazard: suffocation (infant's head can tip forward, blocking airway)
- Remedy: full refund; destroy cover, email recalling@vevor.com or call 855-599-6320

### STORY 4: AAP 2026 Car Seat Safety — Rear-Facing as Long as Possible
**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Angle:** The American Academy of Pediatrics' updated 2026 child passenger safety guidelines state that children should remain in a rear-facing car seat for as long as possible — until they reach the maximum height or weight dictated by the car seat manufacturer (not a fixed age or size milestone). The guidance, updated as of February 12, 2026, replaces older age-based guidelines and puts manufacturer specifications first. Important for Colorado Springs families with young children heading into a busy summer of road trips.
**Key Facts:**
- AAP child passenger safety guidance updated Feb 12, 2026
- Rear-facing: as long as possible until car seat manufacturer's max height/weight
- No longer a fixed age milestone to transition; manufacturer specs govern timing
- Next step: forward-facing with harness, then booster with seat belt
- COS angle: summer road trips, Pikes Peak region travel
- Source: AAP, healthychildren.org
