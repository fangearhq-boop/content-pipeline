D49 Graduation 2026: Complete Schedule and Tips for Families

By Jamie Rivera | May 13, 2026 | COS Parenting

For District 49 families, graduation season starts this Friday. With ceremonies spread across multiple venues and dates from May 15 through May 23, it pays to know exactly when and where your school's ceremony takes place well in advance.

Here is the complete District 49 graduation schedule for the Class of 2026, along with information on D11 ceremonies happening the same week and practical tips for making the most of a busy graduation day.

District 11 Graduation Ceremonies

D11 graduation ceremonies take place May 19–21 at Ed Robson Arena on the Colorado College campus. The schedule includes Palmer High School and Mitchell High School on May 19, Coronado High School and Doherty High School on May 20, and Achieve, Tesla, Bijou, and Odyssey schools on May 21.

Tips for Graduation Day

Arrive early: For large venue ceremonies like Sand Creek at Broadmoor World Arena, arriving 30–45 minutes before the start time gives your family time to find seats, handle parking, and get settled before the processional begins. Parking areas at The Broadmoor World Arena fill quickly for high school graduations.

Check ticketing policies: Some schools distribute limited family tickets for graduation ceremonies, particularly in smaller venues. Confirm with your school whether tickets are required and how many are allocated per graduate.

Consider the weather: Colorado Springs in late May can shift quickly between warm sunshine and afternoon thunderstorms. For outdoor portions of ceremonies, bring a light layer or rain jacket as a backup. BLPA Stadium and outdoor venues are more weather-dependent than indoor arenas.

Coordinate with family members attending: If multiple family members are traveling to Colorado Springs for the ceremony, confirm your meeting point in advance. Broadmoor World Arena in particular can be busy with other events on a Saturday morning.

What's Next

After graduation ceremonies wrap up in late May, most Colorado Springs schools move into the final days of the 2025-2026 school year. D11's last day for students is May 22. D49 end-of-year dates vary by school — check d49.org for your specific school's calendar.

For families with graduates heading into summer and fall, Colorado Springs has a range of local resources for the transition: the Pikes Peak Library District Summer Adventure program, community college enrollment, and local workforce development programs at the D49 Student Success Center are all worth exploring.
