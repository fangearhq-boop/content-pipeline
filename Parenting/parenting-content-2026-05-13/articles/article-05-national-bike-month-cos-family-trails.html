National Bike Month: A Colorado Springs Family Safety and Trails Guide

By Jamie Rivera | May 13, 2026 | COS Parenting

May is National Bike Month, and Colorado Springs gives families excellent reasons to celebrate it. With more than 100 miles of multi-use trails running through the city and into the Pikes Peak region, COS is genuinely well-suited for cycling with children of all ages and skill levels.

Before the first ride of the season, though, a quick safety review — especially for children who are still developing their riding instincts — can make a significant difference. This guide covers both the essentials of family bike safety and the best trails to get started.

Helmet Safety: The Non-Negotiable

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends helmets for all children who ride bicycles, scooters, skateboards, or ride-on toys. The Consumer Product Safety Commission certifies bicycle helmets sold in the United States, and that CPSC certification is the minimum standard for any helmet a child wears.

A certified helmet that does not fit correctly provides substantially less protection than one that does. Here is how to confirm proper fit:

  • Position: The helmet should sit level on the head, two finger-widths above the eyebrows. Not tilted back, not pushed forward.
  • Straps: The side straps should form a "V" shape just below each ear. The chin strap should be snug enough that only one finger fits between the strap and the chin.
  • Movement check: With the chin strap fastened, the helmet should not rock forward, backward, or side to side when the child shakes their head.

Helmets should be replaced after any significant impact — even if no visible damage is apparent. The foam interior of a helmet compresses on impact and does not fully recover, which reduces protection in any subsequent crash.

Best Family Trails in Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs has a range of options for families depending on the age and skill level of the youngest rider in your group.

Pikes Peak Greenway Trail: This 34-mile paved corridor runs north to south through the heart of Colorado Springs, connecting Fountain Creek Regional Park in the south to the Santa Fe Regional Trail in the north. The sections passing through Monument Valley Park and America the Beautiful Park are particularly suited for families: flat, paved, and visually beautiful. These stretches offer a low-stress introduction to urban trail cycling for children still gaining confidence.

Monument Valley Park Loop: One of the most accessible options for families in central Colorado Springs. A compact, mostly paved loop through a well-maintained park, with wide paths and light traffic. Ideal for children learning to ride or families with younger kids on balance bikes and training wheels.

Bear Creek Regional Park: Located in the southwest part of the city, Bear Creek offers a mix of paved and natural surface trails through one of COS's most scenic park settings. The paved sections are manageable for most ages. For families with older children ready for a more varied surface, some of the unpaved paths provide a gentle introduction to trail riding.

What's Next

The League of American Bicyclists hosts events and resources throughout National Bike Month, including National Bike to School Day (the second Wednesday in May). Colorado Springs schools participate in varying degrees — check with your school about any organized events.

As summer arrives and school lets out, Colorado Springs families have a real opportunity to make cycling part of the season. Start with shorter rides on familiar trails, build confidence gradually, and the summer trail network becomes one of the best free activities the city offers.
