Vevor Baby Swing Recall: What Colorado Springs Families Need to Do Now

By Jamie Rivera | May 13, 2026 | COS Parenting

If you purchased a Vevor baby swing in the past year, check the model number today. The Consumer Product Safety Commission and Sanven Technology have issued a recall of approximately 1,020 Vevor baby swings due to a suffocation hazard that violates federal safe sleep law.

The recall affects swings that were sold on vevor.com and Amazon.com between January and August 2025 at prices between $65 and $80. Three model numbers are affected: BB501K (white and gray with a white base), BB702A (white and gray with a gray base), and BB005K (black and gray with a black base).

Why These Swings Are Dangerous

The swings were marketed, in some cases, for infant sleep — and that is the core problem. The swings have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees. Under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act and the federal mandatory standard for infant sleep products, no inclined sleeper is permitted to exceed a 10-degree incline.

The danger is physiological. When an infant is placed in an inclined surface, their head can tip forward, which narrows or blocks their airway. Infants do not have the muscle control to correct their head position when this happens. Products with incline angles similar to those in this recall have been linked to infant deaths in prior investigations.

This is not a theoretical risk. If one of these swings is in your home and has been used for infant sleep, the CPSC's guidance is clear: stop using it immediately.

How to Get Your Refund

The remedy process requires that you destroy the swing before receiving your refund. Here are the steps:

  1. Remove the swing cover.
  2. Cut the cover in half.
  3. Write the word "Recalled" on the base of the swing using a permanent marker.
  4. Take a photo showing the cut cover and the marked base.
  5. Email that photo to recalling@vevor.com to receive a full refund.

You can also call 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday from 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Pacific time, for assistance. Dispose of the swing in your regular trash after completing the process.

Safe Sleep Reminder

This recall is a good moment to revisit safe sleep practices. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that infants always sleep on a firm, flat surface on their back — in a crib, bassinet, or play yard that meets current safety standards. Inclined sleepers, infant seats, and products not specifically designed for sleep should never be used for unsupervised infant rest.

What's Next

If you have already submitted your refund request to Vevor, you should expect processing within a standard window. If you are unsure whether your swing qualifies, check the model number printed on the label on the product base before contacting the company.

Colorado Springs families with questions about product recalls can visit cpsc.gov or call the CPSC hotline at 1-800-638-2772. SaferProducts.gov also allows parents to report unsafe products directly to the federal government.
