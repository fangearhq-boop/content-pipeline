COS School Districts Formally Ask CHSAA to Ban Transgender Athletes from Girls' Sports

By Marta Kim

April 27, 2026

Three Colorado Springs school districts and a superintendent have added their names to a formal letter demanding that Colorado's high school athletics governing body adopt rules banning transgender athletes from girls' sports. The April 14 letter — signed by more than 60 school board members statewide — marks the most coordinated push yet from COS-area districts on this issue.

Who Signed and What They're Asking

The letter was signed by board members from D11 (Colorado Springs School District 11), Academy District 20, and D49, along with D49 Superintendent Peter Hilts. Additional Colorado Springs-area signatories included board members from Widefield D-3 and Woodland Park RE-2.

The letter is addressed to the Colorado High School Activities Association (CHSAA), the nonprofit that governs athletics and activities for Colorado public and private schools. It formally demands that CHSAA adopt rules prohibiting transgender athletes from participating on girls' sports teams.

The letter cites President Trump's executive order "Keeping Men out of Women's Sports" and references Title IX, framing the request as both a fairness and safety issue for female athletes.

CHSAA's Response

CHSAA responded by maintaining its existing neutral position. The association said that the ultimate resolution of these questions will come from the courts rather than from CHSAA itself, and that it does not intend to change its participation rules in response to the letter.

That stance means that, for now, CHSAA's current policies governing transgender student participation remain in place. The association has historically deferred to Colorado state law and federal guidance, both of which continue to evolve through ongoing litigation at the federal level.

What This Means for COS Families

For parents of students in D11, D20, and D49, the letter represents a public, formal statement of each district's position on how girls' sports teams should be structured at the high school level. School board members who signed are now on record advocating for a specific policy change — one that has not yet been adopted by the body that would actually implement it.

It's worth noting what this letter is and isn't. It's a request from school district leaders to a governing athletic association. It's not a local policy change, it doesn't affect elementary or middle school programs, and it doesn't have immediate legal effect. CHSAA continues to set participation rules for all member schools, including those in Colorado Springs.

The practical outcome for student-athletes depends on what happens next at the CHSAA level and in the courts. If federal or state litigation produces a binding ruling, CHSAA would be required to comply. If not, the association has signaled it plans to stay the course.

What's Next

CHSAA's next board meeting is expected to address ongoing policy questions, though the association has not indicated it will take up the transgender participation issue in direct response to the letter. Families who want to weigh in on their district's position can attend school board meetings in D11, D20, or D49 — all three hold regular public meetings that include community comment periods. Meeting schedules are available on each district's website.
