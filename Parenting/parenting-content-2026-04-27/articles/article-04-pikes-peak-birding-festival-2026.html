Pikes Peak Birding Festival Returns May 14-17 — 73+ Field Trips for Nature Families

By Keisha Williams

April 27, 2026

Spring migration is one of the best times of year to be outside in Colorado, and the Pikes Peak Birding & Nature Festival is designed to make the most of it. The 11th annual festival runs May 14-17, 2026, in Colorado Springs, offering more than 73 field trips, 14 seminars, and 5 workshops for everyone from curious beginners to experienced birders.

Why This Festival Is Worth Your Time

Colorado's Front Range sits along a major migratory corridor, and late May is when peak migration activity brings species through the area that won't be seen any other time of year. In the festival's first 10 years, participants have collectively identified 273 bird species — just over half of all species ever recorded in Colorado. That's a remarkable number for a regional festival, and it reflects both the diversity of habitats around Colorado Springs and the quality of the field trip programming.

For families just getting started with birding, the variety of field trip options means you don't have to dive in at the deep end. Introductory-level outings are designed for beginners of all ages, including kids. If your child loves animals and being outdoors — especially if they're in the 6-and-up range — this festival is one of the more accessible ways to turn that interest into a real skill.

The Best Free Option for Families: Birds, Brews & Bites

If you're not ready to commit to a full festival registration, the Saturday evening happy hour is a low-pressure way to check out the community. "Birds, Brews & Bites" takes place Saturday, May 16, from 5 p.m. to 7 p.m. at the Bear Creek Regional Park pavilions, 1850 21st Street, Colorado Springs. The event is free for festival registrants and offers a relaxed introduction to the birding community — casual conversation, food, and drinks, without the pre-dawn field trip wake-up call.

How to Get Started

Registration opened April 11 and is available now at pikespeakbirdingandnaturefestival.org. The registration fee is low relative to the volume of programming included — most field trips, seminars, and workshops are covered by the base registration. A small number of outings at specific locations may require a Colorado State Parks daily pass or a parking fee on top of registration, so it's worth checking the field trip listings when you register.

If you want to bring kids, look for field trips tagged as beginner or introductory — these are generally shorter, more flexible, and better suited to children who are new to birding. Binoculars are helpful but not required for most introductory outings; the festival community tends to be generous about sharing equipment.

What's Next

Registration is open now, and field trip spots fill up — particularly for popular locations and Saturday outings. If you're interested in specific field trips, registering early rather than waiting until the week of the festival will give you more options. The festival website also publishes a species list from prior years if you want a preview of what's possible to see during the May migration window around Colorado Springs.
