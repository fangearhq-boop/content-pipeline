### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — SEGMART Mini Toddler Trampolines (Strangulation Hazard)
**Tier:** 1 (Breaking)
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
**Posting Window:** 8:00 AM MT
**Summary:** The CPSC has recalled SEGMART 55" mini round toddler trampolines — model numbers SOSTT051BR, SOSTT051CR, and SOSTT051YP — due to a strangulation hazard. The hazard involves the promotional accessory kit that came with certain units: the hammock, punching bag, and hand ring straps can entangle a young child's neck. One incident has been reported involving a rope burn injury around a child's neck when the child became entangled in hammock straps. The trampolines were sold at Walmart.com and TikTok Shop from December 2025 through February 2026 for about $140. Remedy: remove and destroy the hammock, punching bag, hand ring, and basketball board accessories immediately; submit photos to SEGMART for a $30 refund. The trampoline itself (without accessories) may continue to be used.

### STORY 5: New Research — When Kids Watch TV Matters More Than How Long
**Tier:** 2 (Today)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Posting Window:** 5:00 PM MT
**Summary:** A 2026 study published in Frontiers in Sleep examined not just how much screen time preschoolers get, but when they watch — finding that evening TV viewing is particularly disruptive to children's sleep. Children ages 8 and under average 2 hours and 27 minutes of screen media per day, with 2- to 4-year-olds averaging 2 hours and 8 minutes (exceeding the AAP's recommendation of 1 hour for ages 2-5). The new AAP 2026 digital ecosystem policy statement also emphasizes context and quality over strict time limits — shifting the conversation from "how much" to "how and when." For COS families, this means the timing of screen use before bed may matter more than the total daily count.
