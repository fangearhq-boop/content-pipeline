**Sources:**

- MyParisTexas (secondary): https://myparistexas.com/mini-round-toddler-trampolines-recalled-due-to-strangulation-hazard/

**Key Facts:**
- Product: SEGMART 55" mini round toddler trampolines with enclosure nets [HIGH]
- Models: SOSTT051BR, SOSTT051CR, SOSTT051YP [HIGH — from CPSC]
- Hazard: Strangulation from hammock, punching bag, and hand ring accessory straps [HIGH]
- Incident: 1 report — child's neck entangled in hammock straps, resulting in rope burn injury [HIGH]
- Where sold: Walmart.com and TikTok Shop [HIGH]
- Sale period: December 2025 through February 2026 [HIGH]
- Price: approximately $140 [HIGH]
- Remedy: Remove and destroy the 4 accessories (hammock, punching bag, hand rings, basketball board); photograph; submit to SEGMART for $30 refund [HIGH]
- Trampoline without accessories: MAY continue to be used [HIGH]

## Story 5: Evening Screens and Preschool Sleep (2026 Research)

**Sources:**
- Frontiers in Sleep (2026): https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/sleep/articles/10.3389/frsle.2026.1783027/full
- AAP Digital Ecosystems Policy: https://publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/157/2/e2025075320/206129/Digital-Ecosystems-Children-and-Adolescents-Policy
- AAP Screen Time QA: https://www.aap.org/en/patient-care/media-and-children/center-of-excellence-on-social-media-and-youth-mental-health/qa-portal/qa-portal-library/qa-portal-library-questions/screen-time-affecting-sleep/
- EdSurge (AAP 2026 guidelines): https://www.edsurge.com/news/2026-02-05-new-aap-screen-time-recommendations-focus-less-on-screens-more-on-family-time
- CHOC summary: https://health.choc.org/updated-aap-recommendations-for-screen-time/

**Key Facts:**
- 2026 Frontiers in Sleep study: Evening TV timing disrupts preschooler sleep [HIGH — from journal]
- Children ages 8 and under: average 2h27m screen media per day [HIGH — from search data]
- Ages 2-4: average 2h8m daily (exceeds AAP recommendation of 1 hour for ages 2-5) [HIGH]
- AAP 2026 policy: emphasizes quality and context over strict time limits [HIGH]
- Shift: from "how much" to "how and when" is the key finding [HIGH]
- AAP: No screens before 18 months (except video chat); 1 hour high-quality content ages 2-5 [HIGH]
- Evening screen use: displaces sleep behavior; low-quality digital use (autoplay, notifications) particularly harmful [HIGH]

**Freshness:** 2026 study (published 2026 per URL), distinct from AAP screen time guidelines update covered April 13 (this is specifically about timing of evening use and is a separate research paper).
