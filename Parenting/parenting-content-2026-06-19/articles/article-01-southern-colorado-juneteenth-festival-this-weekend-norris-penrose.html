Free This Weekend: 6th Annual Southern Colorado Juneteenth Festival at Norris-Penrose

By Jamie Rivera | June 19, 2026 | COS Parenting

If you're looking for something to do with your kids this weekend, Colorado Springs has one of its best free family events of the summer happening right now: the 6th Annual Southern Colorado Juneteenth Festival runs Saturday and Sunday, June 20-21, at the Norris-Penrose Indoor Event Center.

It's free for all ages, it's indoors, and the programming runs all day both days — which makes it genuinely useful for families who want a full-day plan without spending money.

What to Know

What's Included

The festival features live entertainment throughout both days, including national artist headliners and local step show talent. Beyond the music, families will find a car show, fashion show, health fair, food vendors, and games and activities designed for all ages.

If you've attended in previous years when the festival was held outdoors, this year's indoor location at Norris-Penrose is a meaningful change. Organizers moved the event to the Event Center specifically so the celebration can continue regardless of Colorado's unpredictable summer weather. The venue also offers more capacity, better accessibility, and easier parking than past outdoor sites.

The Event's Mission

The Southern Colorado Juneteenth Festival has been building for six years with a clear purpose: to bring together people from every background in Colorado Springs for celebration, music, and community connection. Juneteenth — now a federal holiday — commemorates June 19, 1865, when enslaved people in Galveston, Texas learned they were free, more than two years after the Emancipation Proclamation. The festival honors that history while creating a community gathering point that families across Colorado Springs can be part of.

For families with young children, an event like this — free, indoors, with full-day programming across music, food, and culture — is exactly the kind of thing that makes a Colorado Springs summer memorable. If you've never been, this is a good year to start.

What's Next

The Southern Colorado Juneteenth Festival runs June 20-21 at Norris-Penrose Event Center. No tickets required — just show up. For full event details and updates, visit scjuneteenthfestival.com or visitcos.com. The festival wraps up Sunday evening, and then next week Colorado Springs heads into its final stretch before the summer camp and outdoor activity season fully kicks in.
