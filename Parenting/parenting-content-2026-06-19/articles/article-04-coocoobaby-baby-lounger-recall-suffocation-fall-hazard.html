CPSC Recall: CooCooBaby Baby Loungers — Stop Use, Suffocation and Fall Hazard

By Sarah Morales | June 19, 2026 | COS Parenting

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission announced a recall of CooCooBaby Baby Loungers on June 18, 2026. Approximately 2,355 of the company's "Classic" and "Deluxe" models are affected. If you have one of these, stop using it immediately.

Recall At a Glance

Detail Info
Product CooCooBaby Baby Loungers — "Classic" and "Deluxe" models
Units recalled Approximately 2,355
Hazard Suffocation and fall risk for infants
Sold at Amazon.com and CooCooBaby website
Sale period December 2024 through March 2026
Price range $35 to $70
Action required Stop use immediately; contact CooCooBaby for full refund
CPSC hotline 1-800-638-2772

What's the Problem

The CooCooBaby baby lounger is a foam sleeping pad with padded bumpers and a cloth cover — the kind of product parents use to keep an infant contained and comfortable during supervised rest or lounging time. The CPSC found three specific problems that make these products unsafe.

First, the sides of the lounger are shorter than the minimum height required by federal standards, meaning an infant can too easily roll or shift out of a safe position. Second, the sleeping pad's thickness exceeds the maximum allowed limit — a thicker pad reduces the space between the sleeping surface and the sides, which creates a suffocation risk if an infant's face presses against the side. Third, there's an enclosed opening at the foot of the lounger that an infant could fall through or become entrapped in. The product also has no stand, leaving it flat on the floor or surface without the stabilization that would prevent it from being shifted.

The lounger violates the mandatory standard for infant sleep products.

What to Do

Stop using the lounger now. Contact CooCooBaby directly for a full refund. For verification and contact information, visit cpsc.gov and search "CooCooBaby" to access the official recall page. You can also call the CPSC hotline at 1-800-638-2772 for guidance.

If you purchased through Amazon, your order history will confirm the purchase. If you purchased directly through the CooCooBaby website, your account history should show it as well. Either purchase channel is eligible for the refund.

Who to Alert

Baby loungers and infant sleepers are common new-baby gifts. If you know a family with an infant who was gifted a CooCooBaby lounger — or who might have purchased one in the December 2024 through March 2026 window — it's worth reaching out. New parents don't always see recall notices immediately, and for a product used during infant sleep or rest, prompt awareness matters.
