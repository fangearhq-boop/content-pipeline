104th Pikes Peak International Hill Climb: Your Father's Day Family Guide for June 21

By Jamie Rivera | June 19, 2026 | COS Parenting

This Sunday is Father's Day — and in Colorado Springs, that means one of the most spectacular motorsports events in the world is happening right on our mountain. The 104th Running of The Broadmoor Pikes Peak International Hill Climb, presented by Gran Turismo, takes place Sunday June 21. If you haven't been, this is the kind of event that Colorado Springs families genuinely treasure. If you're planning to go, read this carefully — there are a few things that will catch you off guard if you haven't attended before.

The Ticket Situation — Don't Wait

Here's what catches first-time attendees off guard every year: tickets are not sold at the gate. They're not sold in person at any Pikes Peak International Hill Climb event, including race day. If you're planning to attend and haven't purchased tickets yet, you need to do it today at ppihc.org or through Humanitix. Tickets have been expected to sell out.

Children under 10 years old don't need a ticket. For everyone 10 and up, a ticket is required. The ticket includes your designated spectator viewing area — you can't simply park wherever you want and walk up.

What Makes It Special

The Pikes Peak International Hill Climb is the second-oldest motorsports race in America. Drivers race 12.42 miles up the mountain through 156 turns, starting at about 9,400 feet elevation and finishing at the 14,115-foot summit. The "Race to the Clouds" nickname comes from watching cars disappear into low-hanging clouds above the treeline as the race progresses through the morning.

For families, watching the race from a designated viewing area on the mountain is an experience that doesn't require any understanding of motorsports to enjoy. The scale, the sound, and the scenery make it compelling for all ages. With the green flag at 7:30 AM, it's a full morning activity that's finished before afternoon.

This year has a Father's Day angle that goes beyond the date: father-son duo Dan and Trevor Aweida are racing together in the 104th running, side-by-side up the mountain on the same day.

Logistics for Families

Gates open at 2:30 AM — this is not a typo. The mountain and the race schedule create an extremely early start. Many families who plan to attend arrive before dawn to get settled in their spectator area. If you have young children, it's worth thinking through whether the early start is manageable or whether you'd prefer to attend Fan Fest events during race week instead.

All spectators view the race from designated areas — you cannot wander the course. Your ticket will specify your viewing area, and that's where you stay for the race.

What's Next

If you don't yet have tickets and want to attend Sunday, go to ppihc.org now. If you already have tickets, the race starts at 7:30 AM on Sunday June 21. Gates open at 2:30 AM and the mountain road access is managed carefully to ensure spectators are in position before the race begins. For updates and race-week schedules, ppihc.org has all current information.
