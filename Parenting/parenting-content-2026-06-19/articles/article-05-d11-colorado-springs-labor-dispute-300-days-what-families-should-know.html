D11's Teacher Contract Dispute: 300+ Days In, What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know

By Jamie Rivera | June 19, 2026 | COS Parenting

If your kids attend school in Colorado Springs District 11, there's a situation that's been building quietly through the school year that deserves your attention before fall enrollment decisions get finalized. As of May 2026, it's been more than 300 days since D11's school board ended the master agreement with the district's teachers union — a contract that had been in place since 1968.

What Happened

In late 2024, the Colorado Springs District 11 Board of Education voted not to renew the master agreement with the Colorado Springs Education Association (CSEA), effectively ending a collective bargaining framework that had governed the relationship between D11 teachers and the district for decades. It was the first time the agreement, originally established in 1968, had been severed.

The Colorado Springs Education Association contested the decision, arguing that the district was refusing to engage in meaningful collective bargaining. In October 2025, teachers went on a one-day strike — a significant moment for a district that serves tens of thousands of students. In May 2026, educators and community members gathered to picket the D11 Board of Education meeting at the 300-day milestone, signaling that the dispute remains far from resolved.

What Both Sides Are Saying

The Colorado Springs Education Association's position is that the district has declined to engage in meaningful collective bargaining since the master agreement was ended. Teachers have described uncertainty about working conditions, representation, and the processes for resolving disputes.

The D11 Board of Education and district administration have framed their position around compensation: they report investing more than $100 million in new compensation for staff since 2022, with pay increases they say exceed what neighboring districts have offered. District leaders have described their approach as "pro-teacher" even without the formal bargaining agreement.

What This Means for Families

D11 is one of the larger school districts serving Colorado Springs, covering west-side neighborhoods, northeast neighborhoods, and portions of downtown. Families in these areas make up a significant portion of COS parents.

For most families, the day-to-day classroom experience has continued without major visible disruption since the October 2025 strike. But a multi-year dispute between a district and its educators — particularly one that has already produced a strike — is worth monitoring as you make decisions about school enrollment, participation in district events, and how to talk to your kids about what they may hear from teachers.

Summer is also when enrollment and school-choice decisions often get made. Families considering options within D11 or looking at other Colorado Springs districts (D20, D49, D12) may want to factor ongoing labor stability into their thinking.

What's Next

As of June 2026, there is no publicly announced timeline for a resolution between D11 and CSEA. Families who want to stay current on the situation can monitor d11.org for official district updates and follow local coverage from KRDO and KOAA. The new school year begins in late August 2026 — well within the window where any significant developments in this dispute would directly affect families preparing for fall.
