CPSC Recall: GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toys — 70,410 Units, Choking Hazard

By Sarah Morales | June 19, 2026 | COS Parenting

If you have a baby at home and purchased a GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toy from Amazon in the past few years, stop using it now. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 70,410 of these toys because the strings can reach the back of an infant's throat, causing choking or respiratory distress. Three incidents have been confirmed.

Is Your Toy Recalled?

The recalled product is the GOPO TOYS Pull String Teething Toy: an off-white disc shape with a grey ball in the center and six multi-colored, silicone tentacle-like pull strings. If that description matches something in your home, check the back of the packaging for a batch number.

These toys sold exclusively on Amazon.com between August 2023 and March 2026, priced between $11 and $15. If you purchased through Amazon, you can check your order history to confirm.

What to Do

This recall requires more than just setting the toy aside — CPSC requires destruction. Here's the process:

  1. Cut all six silicone strings from the teething toy.
  2. Write "DESTROYED" in permanent marker on the main body of the toy.
  3. Take a clear photo of the destroyed toy.
  4. Email the photo to recalls@gopotoys.com to receive a refund.

For questions or help, contact the CPSC directly at 1-800-638-2772 or visit cpsc.gov and search for the GOPO recall.

Why This Matters

Teething toys are typically used by infants from around 3 months to 2 years old — an age group that cannot communicate discomfort and has limited ability to manage a choking situation independently. The hazard here isn't theoretical: there are three confirmed reports of the strings reaching the back of infants' throats, causing respiratory distress or choking. That makes this a safety issue for parents to take seriously and act on now rather than later.

If you know other families with young babies, it's worth sharing this recall. The toy sold at a low price point on Amazon, which means it's the kind of item that could have been purchased without much deliberation and may have been gifted to families as well.

What's Next

If your toy matches the batch numbers listed above, take action today: destroy the toy and email the photo for your refund. The CPSC will periodically update the recall page at cpsc.gov with any additional information from GOPO Toys. COS Parenting will share any follow-up safety alerts as they come in.
