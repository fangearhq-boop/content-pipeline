### STORY 1: Juneteenth Festival This Weekend

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

### STORY 2: GOPO Toys Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

If you have a young baby, please read this.

GOPO Toys has recalled approximately 70,410 of their Pull String Teething Toys after three incidents of the toy's strings reaching the back of infants' throats, causing choking or respiratory distress.

The recalled toys are disc-shaped, off-white with a grey ball in the center and six multi-colored silicone tentacle pull strings. They sold on Amazon.com between August 2023 and March 2026, for between $11 and $15.

Here's how to tell if yours is recalled: check the batch number on the back of the packaging. If it shows 250905, 250530, 250120, 240315, 231005, or 230610, it's affected.

What to do: cut all the silicone strings, write "DESTROYED" in permanent marker on the main body of the toy, photograph it, and email the photo to recalls@gopotoys.com. You'll receive a refund.

Please share this post so other families can check. If you have questions, the CPSC hotline is 1-800-638-2772 or visit cpsc.gov.

### STORY 3: PPIHC Father's Day

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

Looking for a Father's Day idea that's uniquely Colorado Springs? This Sunday is the 104th running of the Pikes Peak International Hill Climb, and it falls on Father's Day.

The green flag drops at 7:30 AM on June 21. Cars race 12.42 miles up Pikes Peak, through 156 turns, to the 14,115-foot summit. It's been called the Race to the Clouds, and watching it from a spectator area on the mountain is something families in Colorado Springs genuinely don't take for granted.

A few practical things to know:

Kids under 10 don't need a ticket. For everyone 10 and up, a ticket is required.

Here's the one that trips people up: tickets are NOT sold on race day. They're not sold at the gate, not sold at any PPIHC event in-person. You must purchase them online in advance at ppihc.org or through Humanitix. If you're planning to go Sunday, you need to handle this today.

Gates open at 2:30 AM (yes, the middle of the night) — this is a mountain race and the schedule reflects that. Spectators must view from designated areas.

The 104th running is presented by Gran Turismo and features a father-son duo, Dan and Trevor Aweida, competing together on race day. That's a Father's Day story right there on the mountain.

### STORY 4: CooCooBaby Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

Another infant product recall to pass along — this one was announced yesterday by the CPSC.

CooCooBaby has recalled 2,355 of their "Classic" and "Deluxe" Baby Loungers due to suffocation and fall hazards. These are foam sleeping pads with padded bumpers and a cloth cover.

The recall is because the sides of the lounger are shorter than the required minimum height, the sleeping pad is thicker than the maximum allowed (creating a suffocation risk), and there's an opening at the foot of the lounger that an infant could fall through. The product also has no stand, adding a fall risk.

The loungers sold on Amazon.com and on the CooCooBaby website between December 2024 and March 2026, for between $35 and $70.

If you have one: stop using it immediately. Contact CooCooBaby directly for a full refund. For more details, visit cpsc.gov or call 1-800-638-2772.

If you know a family with a young baby who uses a baby lounger, please share this with them.

### STORY 5: D11 Labor Dispute

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

If your kids go to school in Colorado Springs District 11, this is background you should have as you're thinking about the fall.

In late 2024, D11's school board voted not to renew the master agreement with the Colorado Springs Education Association — a contract that had been in place since 1968. As of May 2026, it's been more than 300 days without that agreement.

In October 2025, teachers went on a one-day strike. In May 2026, educators and parents gathered to picket at the board of education meeting to mark the 300-day milestone.

Both sides have a version of this story. The union says the district has refused to engage in meaningful collective bargaining. The district says it's invested more than $100 million in new staff compensation since 2022 and has provided pay increases that exceed neighboring districts.

D11 serves a large portion of Colorado Springs — west side neighborhoods, northeast neighborhoods, and parts of downtown. If you're making school decisions for fall 2026, it's worth keeping an eye on how this plays out over the summer.

You can stay current at d11.org.
