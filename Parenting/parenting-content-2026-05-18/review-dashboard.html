Flanagan Park getting new playground, ADA path with $750K federal grant
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM MT Flanagan Park in central Colorado Springs is getting its biggest upgrade in decades — and it was shaped by the people who use it most. The City of Colorado Springs secured a $750,000 federal community development grant to renovate the park at 3132 N. Prospect Street. The project includes a brand-new playground, an ADA-accessible walking path with pedestrian lighting and benches, new screen fencing, a resurfaced basketball court, and improvements to the existing landscaping, irrigation system, and picnic pavilion. Flanagan Park was originally built in the mid-1970s and serves a diverse, multi-generational neighborhood that includes families, older adults, and people in assisted living. The city collected community input over several years to shape the project priorities — and the results reflect what residents actually asked for. This is the kind of infrastructure investment that makes a neighborhood park genuinely useful for everyone, especially families with young children and people who need accessible paths to enjoy the space. When it's done, Flanagan Park will be one of the most complete neighborhood parks in central Colorado Springs. What improvements would make YOUR neighborhood park better to visit? ---

Amazon baby bath seat recalls — nearly 9,000 units pose drowning risk
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 12:00 PM MT If you have a baby bath seat that was purchased on Amazon, please check this carefully. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) has recalled multiple brands of infant bath seats due to a tip-over risk that poses a risk of serious injury or death by drowning. The recalled brands include: - YCXXKJ (sold on Amazon by BenTalk) - NFSVLB (sold on Amazon by NFSVLB) - CheerKid (sold on Amazon by Babibaby and Woot) - Trankerloop (recalled February 26, 2026; approximately 2,380 units) Combined, nearly 9,000 units are affected across these recalls. The hazard: the bath seats can tip over while in use. A baby who is placed in a bath seat assumes it will hold them securely. If the seat tips, they can slip into the water immediately. If you have any of these products, stop using them now. Contact the seller through Amazon or go directly to CPSC.gov to report the product and request a full refund. You can also call the CPSC hotline at 800-638-2772. Please share this with any parents or grandparents who may have purchased infant bath seats on Amazon recently. ---

### Long-Form Post Window: 3:00 PM MT Colorado Springs has one of the best outdoor recreation programs in the state — and it just got better for families and individuals with mobility challenges. The City of Colorado Springs is bringing back its Trackchair Program for 2026 with an expanded schedule: two guided hikes daily, Wednesday through Sunday, at rotating locations throughout the season. The locations include: - Ute Valley Park - Red Rock Canyon Open Space - Blodgett Open Space - Bluestem Prairie Open Space All reservations are free. You can book your spot now through the City of Colorado Springs parks program. The Trackchair is an all-terrain motorized chair that allows people who use wheelchairs or have limited mobility to access trails that would otherwise be inaccessible. For families who have a member with a physical disability, this program opens up the outdoor experiences that Colorado Springs is known for. If you or someone in your family has been waiting for an accessible way to experience COS trails, this is the summer to do it. Have you used the Trackchair Program before? We'd love to hear about your experience. ---
