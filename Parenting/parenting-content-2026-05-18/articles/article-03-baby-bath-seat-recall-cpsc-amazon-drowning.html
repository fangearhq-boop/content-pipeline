If you purchased a baby bath seat from Amazon in the past two years, check the brand name now. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued recalls on four brands of infant bath seats — YCXXKJ, NFSVLB, CheerKid, and Trankerloop — due to a tip-over hazard that poses a risk of serious injury or death by drowning.

Combined, nearly 9,000 units are affected across the four recalls. All involve the same core hazard: the seats can become unstable and tip over while in use, causing an infant to slip into the water.

Which Products Are Recalled

YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats — Sold on Amazon by seller BenTalk; price range approximately $34 to $40; sold from May 2024 through October 2025. The seats can tip over while in use, posing a drowning risk. Full refund available through Amazon or CPSC.gov.

NFSVLB Baby Bath Seats — Blue and white bath seats with detachable arms and four suction cups on the seat's bottom. Also recalled for drowning and entrapment hazards. Sold on Amazon.

CheerKid Baby Bath Seats — Sold on Amazon by sellers Babibaby and Woot. Same tip-over hazard as other recalled brands. Full refund available.

Trankerloop Baby Bath Seats — Recalled on February 26, 2026. Approximately 2,380 units affected. Sold on Amazon. Same instability issue.

Why This Hazard Is Serious

Bath seats are used because parents believe they provide a safe, stable place for an infant to sit in the tub during bath time. The danger is that when a seat unexpectedly tips, a baby has no ability to right themselves or push up from the water. Drowning can occur in inches of water in seconds. A seat that appears to be performing correctly can still fail without warning if it does not meet the mandatory safety standard for infant bath seats.

The CPSC recall process is triggered when products violate mandatory safety standards — in this case, the standard specifically governing infant bath seat stability. All four recalled brands failed this test.

What to Do Right Now

Check your Amazon order history for any of the four brand names listed above. If you have a recalled product, stop using it immediately — do not continue using it while waiting for a replacement or refund.

To request a full refund, go to CPSC.gov and look up the specific recall for your product, or call the CPSC hotline at 800-638-2772 (TTY: 800-638-8270). You can also contact the seller directly through your Amazon account.

Share this information with grandparents, caregivers, or anyone else who may have purchased or received one of these products for a young child.

What's Next

The CPSC maintains a regularly updated list of all current product recalls at CPSC.gov. Parents can sign up for email recall alerts on the CPSC website to receive notifications about future recalls automatically. For bath time safety in the meantime, pediatricians recommend never leaving an infant alone in the bath for any length of time, regardless of what support equipment is in use — a phone call, doorbell, or brief distraction is not worth the risk.
