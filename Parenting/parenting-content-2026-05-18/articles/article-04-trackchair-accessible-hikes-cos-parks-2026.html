Colorado Springs is expanding one of its most important outdoor programs this summer, and it is free for everyone who uses it.

The City of Colorado Springs Trackchair Program returns for 2026 with an expanded schedule: two guided hikes daily, Wednesday through Sunday, at rotating locations throughout the season. The four locations for 2026 are Ute Valley Park, Red Rock Canyon Open Space, Blodgett Open Space, and Bluestem Prairie Open Space.

Reservations are free and available now through the City of Colorado Springs parks program.

What Is the Trackchair Program?

The Trackchair is an all-terrain motorized wheelchair that can navigate trails that a standard wheelchair cannot access. The City's program pairs the chair with a guide who leads participants through trails at participating open spaces, providing an outdoor hiking experience for people with mobility limitations who would otherwise be unable to access Colorado Springs' trail network.

For families that include a member who uses a wheelchair or has limited mobility, this program changes the equation for what kind of outdoor experience they can share together. The trails at Red Rock Canyon Open Space and Ute Valley Park are among the most scenic in the region — terrain that was previously inaccessible is now available.

Who Is This For?

The Trackchair Program is designed for individuals with physical disabilities or mobility challenges who want to experience COS's open spaces. Families are encouraged to attend together — hiking with a Trackchair participant alongside walking family members is a common experience in the program, and the guided format means the group stays together throughout.

The program is appropriate for adults and older children who have the physical stability to use the chair safely. Guides can provide additional guidance at the time of reservation if you have specific questions about whether the program works for your family's situation.

Why This Summer Is a Good Time to Go

Colorado Springs' open spaces are at their best in May and June — wildflowers are in bloom, temperatures are manageable, and the summer crowds have not yet peaked. The expanded 2026 schedule (up from previous years) means more flexibility for families to find a time that works. With two guided hikes daily from Wednesday through Sunday, there are ten weekly time slots to choose from during the regular program season.

Summer is also a time when families have more flexibility in their schedules — school is out for D11 families as of May 22, and the longer days make outdoor activities more accessible.
