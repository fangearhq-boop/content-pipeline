Colorado Springs District 11 students have one week left. The last day of school is Friday, May 22 — and with it comes the practical start of summer for tens of thousands of COS families.

Summer in Colorado Springs arrives with a lot of choices. Here is a practical guide to what is happening right now and what families can plan for the first week of break.

This Weekend: PAW Patrol Live

The first weekend of summer (Memorial Day weekend) brings PAW Patrol Live "Heroes Unite" to Pikes Peak Center at 190 S. Cascade Ave. Shows run Saturday May 23 at 10:00 AM and 2:00 PM, and Sunday May 24 at 2:00 PM. Tickets are available at AXS.com. For families with children ages 2-6, this is a strong first-weekend-of-summer activity that children can look forward to during that last week of school.

Register for PPLD Summer Adventure Now

The Pikes Peak Library District Summer Adventure "Unearth a Story" program is live and accepting registrations. The free program runs all summer and is open to readers of all ages — children, teens, and adults. Participants track their reading and exploration activities through a game card or the Beanstack app at ppld.beanstack.org, earning milestones and rewards along the way.

The program is free to join. Registration is at ppld.org. Libraries across Colorado Springs are offering in-branch programming tied to the dinosaur and archaeology theme all summer.

Free Outdoor Activities: Trackchair Hikes

The City of Colorado Springs has expanded its Trackchair Program for 2026, offering two free guided hikes daily — Wednesday through Sunday — at Ute Valley Park, Red Rock Canyon Open Space, Blodgett Open Space, and Bluestem Prairie Open Space. Reservations are free and available at coloradosprings.gov. For families that want to get into the outdoors right away, this is one of the most accessible options in the city.

What to Know About COS Parks This Summer

Flanagan Park in central Colorado Springs is currently undergoing a major renovation funded by a $750,000 federal grant — new playground, ADA path, lighting, and more. Other neighborhood parks across COS are open for summer use. Families should be aware that the city has confirmed some budget constraints for the 2026 season, including the closure of approximately 25% of neighborhood park permanent restrooms and a scale-back in maintenance of damaged playground equipment. Check the specific park you plan to visit before making a long trip.

What's Next

D11's last day is Friday. The summer officially begins this weekend. COS Parenting will continue covering local events, park updates, safety recalls, and school district news throughout the summer. Check coloradosprings.gov/parks for park hours and updates, ppld.org for library programming, and CPSC.gov any time you want to verify that a product your family uses is current on safety standards.
