### STORY 1: D20 Honors Rampart Senior Cora Fogle Who Died of Cancer
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** Local News
**Angle:** A Colorado Springs community came together to ensure Rampart High School senior Cora Fogle was honored at graduation after she died from cancer in December 2025. When students and family petitioned for an empty chair and a chance for her family to accept her diploma, Academy District 20 initially said policy would not allow it. After KRDO reporting and public pressure, the district reversed course. Cora's classmates had already raised more than $50,000 during a Bald for Bucks fundraiser in her honor. Her graduation took place May 12 at Clune Arena at the U.S. Air Force Academy. This is a story about what COS families do for one of their own.
**Key Facts:**
- Cora Fogle, Rampart HS (D20), died of cancer December 2025
- Classmates raised $50,000+ at Bald for Bucks fundraiser for cancer research and patient care

- D20 initially denied (policy); KRDO reporting + public petition → district reversed decision
- Graduation: May 12, Clune Arena, U.S. Air Force Academy

### STORY 2: CPSC Urgent Warning — Baby Bath Seats (Drowning Risk, Stop Using Immediately)
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Angle:** The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent warning about baby bath seats — including YCXXKJ models (sold on Amazon by BenTalk) — that violate mandatory safety standards because they are unstable and can tip over during use, posing a drowning risk. Multiple brands are affected (YCXXKJ, NFSVLB, Trankerloop, CheerKid). With Memorial Day pool openings and summer water season approaching, bath time safety is especially timely.
**Key Facts:**
- Brand: YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats; sold on Amazon by BenTalk
- Hazard: Unstable, can tip over; poses drowning risk
- Violates mandatory standard for infant bath seats
- Approximately 9,000 units (YCXXKJ alone)
- Also recalled: NFSVLB, Trankerloop, CheerKid baby bath seats (similar hazard)
- Action: Stop using immediately; contact seller for full refund
- CPSC Hotline: 800-638-2772; SaferProducts.gov
- Source: cpsc.gov (YCXXKJ recall + CPSC Warns/urgent alert); thehill.com

### STORY 5: May is National Water Safety Month — Drowning Prevention for COS Families
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Angle:** May is National Water Safety Month, and the timing could not be more relevant for Colorado Springs families as pools and splash pads prepare to open. The AAP reports drowning is the leading cause of injury-related death in children ages 1 to 4. This story covers the essential prevention steps — swimming lessons, touch supervision, pool fencing — tied to the COS summer water season.
**Key Facts:**
- Drowning = leading cause of injury-related death, children ages 1-4 (AAP / HealthyChildren.org)
- "Touch supervision": adult within arm's length of infant/toddler in or near water
- AAP recommends swimming lessons for children; no minimum age
- Sunscreen: SPF 15+, apply 15-30 min before sun; reapply every 2 hours
- COS water venues opening soon: Monument Valley Pool May 24, Panorama Splash Pad May 25
- Source: healthychildren.org (AAP); CPSC drowning data; pedialliance.com
