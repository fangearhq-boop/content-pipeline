CPSC Issues Urgent Warning: Multiple Baby Bath Seat Brands Recalled for Drowning Risk

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 14, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

If you have a baby bath seat in your home, the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission wants you to check it right now. Multiple brands of infant bath seats have been recalled in recent months after the CPSC determined they violate mandatory safety standards — and the agency has issued an urgent warning about at least one brand, urging families to stop using the product immediately.

The affected products are bath seats designed to hold infants upright in the bathtub while caregivers wash them. The hazard in each recalled product is the same: the seats are unstable and can tip over during use, potentially causing a young child to slip into the water and drown.

Which Products Are Recalled

The CPSC has recalled multiple baby bath seat brands, including:

  • YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats — sold on Amazon by the seller BenTalk. The CPSC issued an urgent warning specifically for this product, calling on consumers to stop using it immediately.
  • NFSVLB Baby Bath Seats — also sold on Amazon. Recalled for drowning and entrapment risks; violates the mandatory standard for infant bath seats.
  • CheerKid Baby Bath Seats — sold on Amazon by Babibaby and Woot. Recalled for the same drowning hazard.
  • Trankerloop Baby Bath Seats — recalled for the same violation of mandatory infant bath seat standards.

These products share a core defect: their design fails to keep infants stable and upright during bath time, which creates the opportunity for a tipping incident that could result in the child's head going underwater.

What to Do If You Have One

The CPSC's guidance is straightforward: stop using the product immediately. Do not wait to see if a refund or replacement is available before removing the item from use.

To obtain a refund:

  • Check cpsc.gov for the specific recall page for your product — each listing includes contact information for the brand or seller
  • Contact the Amazon seller directly if that is where the product was purchased
  • Call the CPSC Hotline at 800-638-2772
  • Report unsafe products at SaferProducts.gov

Most recall programs require consumers to submit a photo of the destroyed product before receiving a refund. Destroying the item prevents it from being reused or resold.

Why Bath Seat Safety Matters This Time of Year

Colorado Springs outdoor pools open Memorial Day weekend, and families across the region will be thinking about water safety in the weeks ahead. But drowning risks do not begin at the pool. For infants, bath time is one of the most common settings for water-related injury.

The American Academy of Pediatrics' standing guidance for bath time safety is that infants should never be left alone in or near water, even for a moment. A bath seat is not a supervision substitute — it is a positioning device, and an unstable one can create danger regardless of whether an adult is present in the room.

Infants can drown in very shallow water in seconds. A tipped bath seat in two inches of water is a life-threatening situation. Families do not always know their product has been recalled, particularly if it was purchased online and they did not receive a follow-up notification from the retailer.

What's Next

Check cpsc.gov regularly throughout the summer, as recalls are issued on a rolling basis and not all retailers notify buyers automatically. If you purchased a baby bath seat online in the past two to three years and have not verified its recall status, now is the time to do so.

For broader water safety guidance as COS pools open Memorial Day weekend, the American Academy of Pediatrics' HealthyChildren.org resource covers bath safety, pool safety, touch supervision, and swimming lesson recommendations for families with young children.
