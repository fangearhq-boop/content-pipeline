COS Summer Pool and Splash Pad Opening Guide: What Opens When for Memorial Day Weekend 2026

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 14, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

Memorial Day weekend is 10 days away, and Colorado Springs' outdoor pools and splash pads are getting ready to welcome families for summer. If you've been watching the forecast and planning your first pool day, here's what you need to know: which venues open when, how much they cost, and what to expect when you get there.

Outdoor City Pools — Opens May 24

Colorado Springs' two outdoor city pools — Monument Valley Pool and Wilson Ranch Pool — are operated by the YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region through a partnership with the City of Colorado Springs. Both pools are expected to open May 24, weather permitting.

Season passes are available for YMCA members and nonmembers and run from Memorial Day through Labor Day. Day passes are purchased on the day of visit and accepted by credit card or exact cash — no advance purchase option and no card readers that accept imprecise amounts. Modified hours begin August 10, 2026.

For current hours, pricing, and weather-related closures, check ppymca.org or the YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region's pool status page before you head out.

Panorama Park Splash Pad — Opens May 25, Free

The Panorama Park Splash Pad is one of Colorado Springs' most family-accessible summer options because it's free and doesn't require supervision in the same way a pool does. It opens May 25 and runs through September 2, every day from 10 AM to 6 PM.

This venue is especially well-suited for families with toddlers and younger kids who aren't ready for a full pool environment. The splash pad features water jets at ground level — no standing water, which reduces drowning risk compared to a traditional pool setting.

Deerfield Hills Community Center Sprayground — Opens June 1

The Deerfield Hills sprayground opens June 1. Hours vary by date:

  • June 1-Aug 11: Wednesdays 11:30 AM-5 PM; all other days 10 AM-5 PM
  • Aug 12-Sept 2: Fridays, Saturdays, and Sundays only, 10 AM-5 PM

Other COS Aquatic Options

Families who want to swim year-round or can't wait for the outdoor season have options. PPYMCA locations across Colorado Springs offer indoor pools with lap swim and open swim sessions. VillaSport in Colorado Springs includes both indoor and outdoor aquatics. Check individual facility schedules at ppymca.org or villasport.com for current hours and pricing.

For families with older children interested in competitive swimming, the Pikes Peak region has several summer swim teams and clubs. Checking with the city recreation department or your local YMCA branch is a good starting point.

What's Next

Before you pack the sunscreen and head out, it's worth brushing up on water safety for the summer season. May is National Water Safety Month, and the American Academy of Pediatrics has clear guidance on drowning prevention, touch supervision, and swim readiness for children at various ages. HealthyChildren.org is the AAP's family-facing resource.

Pool hours can change with weather and staffing — always check ppymca.org or call the specific facility before planning a trip. And if your kids are overdue for a swimming lesson refresher before the season starts, PPYMCA and city recreation programs typically offer spring and early-summer sessions.
