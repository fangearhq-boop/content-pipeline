### STORY 1: D20 Honors Cora Fogle at Rampart Graduation

**FB Long-Form (S1-LF)**
Posted: 10:00 AM MT

This one hit us hard in the best way.

Cora Fogle was a Rampart High School senior in Academy District 20. She died of cancer in December, midway through what should have been her final year of high school. Her classmates didn't just grieve — they acted. During a Bald for Bucks fundraiser, they raised more than $50,000 for cancer research and patient care. They showed up for her in one of the most meaningful ways high schoolers can.

Then they wanted to do one more thing. They wanted Cora's family to walk across the graduation stage on her behalf. They wanted an empty chair in her honor. When D20 said its policy didn't allow it, families and students pushed back — through a petition, through conversations with the district, through the kind of persistent community advocacy that Colorado Springs parents are quietly good at.

D20 reversed its policy. On May 12, at Clune Arena on the U.S. Air Force Academy campus, Cora Fogle was honored at Rampart's graduation ceremony. Her family walked the stage.

There's a lot that's hard about parenting. But watching a community wrap around a family during something this painful, and actually change the outcome — that's something.

We're thinking of Cora's family this week.

What does your community do when a family needs support? Share in the comments.

### STORY 2: CPSC Warning — Baby Bath Seats

**FB Long-Form (S2-LF)**
Posted: 12:00 PM MT

Parents with infants: please read this.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent warning about baby bath seats — including those sold under the YCXXKJ brand on Amazon by BenTalk. These bath seats violate the mandatory safety standard because they're unstable and can tip over while a child is in the bath, creating a drowning risk.

The CPSC's message is clear: stop using these seats immediately.

And it's not just one brand. Multiple baby bath seat products have been recalled in recent months for the same hazard — including NFSVLB, CheerKid, and Trankerloop bath seats. If you bought a baby bath seat online in the past few years, it's worth checking cpsc.gov to see if yours is included.

What to do:
- Stop using it now
- Visit cpsc.gov and search for your product
- Contact the seller for a full refund
- Call the CPSC Hotline: 800-638-2772
- Report unsafe products at SaferProducts.gov

With summer just around the corner and pool season starting soon here in Colorado Springs, water safety — in the bath and in the pool — is front of mind for a lot of families. Bath seats feel safe because they're small and familiar. But these products show that familiar doesn't always mean safe.

Have you checked your baby bath gear recently? Let us know if you have questions.

### STORY 5: National Water Safety Month

**FB Long-Form (S5-LF)**
Posted: 8:00 PM MT

May is National Water Safety Month — and with Colorado Springs pools opening Memorial Day weekend, now is the time to go over the basics.

Here's what the American Academy of Pediatrics recommends for water safety with young children:

**Touch supervision**
When infants and toddlers are in or near water, an adult should be within arm's reach at all times. Not just watching from across the pool — within arm's reach.

**Swimming lessons**
The AAP recommends swimming lessons as an important drowning prevention tool. Ask your pediatrician for guidance on the right timing for your child.

**Pool fencing**
A four-sided isolation fence with a self-closing, self-latching gate is one of the most effective barriers between young children and open water. This applies to backyard pools, hot tubs, and decorative ponds.

**Life jackets**
For open water and boating, children should wear properly fitted, Coast Guard-approved life jackets — not inflatable swim wings or floaties.

**Sun protection while swimming**
SPF 15 or higher, applied 15-30 minutes before sun exposure. Reapply every two hours, and after swimming, sweating, or toweling off. Look for "broad-spectrum" and "water-resistant" on the label.

The statistic that stops us cold every time: drowning is the leading cause of injury-related death in children ages 1 to 4, according to the AAP. A lot of those incidents happen in home pools or near familiar water.

Colorado Springs families are outdoor families. Let's have a safe summer.
