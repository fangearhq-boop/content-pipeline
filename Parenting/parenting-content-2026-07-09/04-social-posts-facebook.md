### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — VEVOR Baby Loungers (~237 Units)

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

⚠️ PRODUCT RECALL: If you have a VEVOR baby lounger at home, stop using it right now.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 237 VEVOR baby loungers sold through Amazon, Wayfair, and VEVOR.com between November 2024 and January 2026 for around $30. This is a mandatory standards recall — meaning the product was sold without meeting the federal safety floor for infant sleep products.

There are two specific hazards. First, the sides of the lounger are too low to contain an infant, creating an entrapment and fall risk. Second, the foot openings are wider than what's permitted under mandatory infant sleep safety standards.

To address the recall, the CPSC is asking families to: (1) stop using the lounger immediately, (2) cut the cover, foam, and padding in half to render the product unusable, and (3) contact VEVOR directly for a full refund.

If you're unsure whether your lounger is included, check the brand name VEVOR on the product tag. When in doubt, stop using it and reach out to VEVOR directly. 👇

Does your family use or has anyone given you a VEVOR baby lounger? Share this so other parents can check their homes.

### STORY 2: CPSC Recall — Target Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy (~49,000 Units)

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

🚨 Heads up if you picked up a Gigglescape toy at Target — there's a recall you need to know about.

Target is recalling approximately 49,000 Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toys after reports that the clear plastic dome can separate from its blue whale-shaped base, releasing small plastic balls. Those balls are a choking hazard for young children. So far, there have been nine reports of the dome detaching and one report of a child beginning to choke.

This toy was sold exclusively at Target stores nationwide from August 2025 through January 2026 for about $10 — which means a lot of families may have picked one up as a holiday gift this past season.

The fix is simple: stop using the toy immediately and return it to any Target store for a full refund. You don't need a receipt. If you'd rather not go to the store, you can also request a prepaid return label.

If this toy is in your home or your child's playroom, please pull it now and pass this along to friends who might have one too. 🐋

Have you seen this toy at Target? Let us know in the comments if you've got one — and if you're planning to return it.

### STORY 3: D11 Free Summer Meals

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

Free breakfast and lunch for kids in Colorado Springs — and your kiddos don't have to be D11 students to qualify. 🍽️

Colorado Springs School District 11 is running free summer meals for all children ages 1 through 18 across the city. The program serves Monday through Friday at schools, community centers, and playgrounds throughout Colorado Springs. It doesn't matter which school district you're in — if your child is between 1 and 18 years old, they can eat.

This is part of a federally funded summer nutrition program, and D11 is administering it locally. The goal is simple: make sure kids in our community don't go hungry when school is out.

Meals are free, no registration is required, and kids just show up during meal times at any participating site. To find a location near you, check D11's website or search the USDA Summer Food Service Program site finder for Colorado Springs.

If you know a family who could use this — a neighbor, a coworker, someone at church — please share this post. The program is available right now, every weekday.

### STORY 4: Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

One of Colorado Springs' oldest summer traditions is back — and it's a great excuse to get the family out this week. 🤠

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo runs July 14-18 at the Norris Penrose Event Center. Evening performances go at 7:30 PM Tuesday through Saturday, with matinee shows at 12:30 PM on Friday and Saturday. This rodeo has been running since 1937, and it's still one of the best things to do with kids in COS all summer.

The Fan Zone is where the kiddos really shine. Mutton Bustin' is open to kids ages 4-9 who weigh under 50 pounds — no pre-registration required, so just show up and let them try their luck riding a sheep. You'll also find pony rides, a petting zoo, face painting, and goat roping in the Fan Zone.

A few things to mark on your calendar: the Pikes Peak or Bust Parade kicks off the week on Saturday, July 11 at 11 AM in downtown Colorado Springs. And on Thursday, July 16, there's a free drone show after the evening performance.

Tickets are available at ppbrodeo.com. Whether your family's done this 10 times or never been, there's something for every age.

### STORY 5: Aspen Acres Fire Update

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM MT

The Aspen Acres Fire continues to grow, and there's an important new hazard for families in the area to know about tonight. 🔥

As of Wednesday, July 8, the fire has reached 96,031 acres — up from 93,916 acres reported yesterday — with 15% containment. The fire has now destroyed 275 total structures: 192 in Pueblo County and 83 in Custer County.

The arrival of monsoon moisture is a mixed development. A flash flood watch is in effect through Wednesday evening. The rain is welcome for firefighters, but fire-scarred soil becomes hydrophobic and can't absorb water normally. That means runoff moves fast, and flash flooding on burned terrain can happen with very little warning. Gusty outflow winds up to 50 mph are also possible.

On the evacuation front, Rockvale, Coal Creek, and Williamsburg in Fremont County have been downgraded from mandatory evacuation to pre-evacuation status. The Disaster Assistance Center is now issuing re-entry permits for some areas.

If you have family or friends near the fire or in the flood watch zone, please share this update. Conditions are changing quickly and the flash flood risk is real tonight.

Are you or someone you know affected by the Aspen Acres Fire? Let us know in the comments — our community is here.
