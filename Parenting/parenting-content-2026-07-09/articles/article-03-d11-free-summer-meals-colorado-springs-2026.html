Free Breakfast and Lunch for COS Kids This Summer — No District Required

By Jamie Rivera

July 9, 2026 · Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs School District 11 is serving free breakfast and lunch to any child ages 1 through 18 in Colorado Springs this summer — and families don't need to be in the D11 attendance zone to participate. If your child is in that age range, they can eat. That's it.

Meals are served Monday through Friday at schools, community centers, and playgrounds throughout the city. The program is active now and continues through the summer months while school is out.

Who Qualifies

Any child between the ages of 1 and 18 who shows up at a participating site during meal times is eligible to receive a free meal. There's no application, no registration, no income requirement, and no district residency requirement. Children attending D20 or D49 schools are just as welcome as D11 students.

The program is designed to be barrier-free. Families don't need to bring documentation, prove enrollment in any school, or fill out any forms. Kids just show up and eat.

Both breakfast and lunch are served at participating sites. The specific times vary by location, so it's worth checking the schedule for any site you plan to visit to make sure you arrive during the right window.

How to Find a Site Near You

D11 maintains information about participating meal sites on its website. The USDA's Summer Food Service Program also operates a national site finder at summerfood.usda.gov where you can enter your Colorado Springs zip code and see nearby meal sites with addresses and serving hours.

Sites are spread throughout the city — not just in D11 neighborhoods. Schools, parks, and community centers across Colorado Springs are participating, which means most families should be able to find a site that's reasonably close to home.

If you live near a site that works for your schedule, it's worth making it a regular summer routine. The program runs every weekday, and the food is the same quality served during the school year under federal nutrition standards.

Why This Program Exists

The Summer Food Service Program is a federally funded USDA initiative that operates in communities across the country. D11 serves as the local program sponsor in Colorado Springs, which means the district handles the logistics of organizing and administering the meal sites citywide.

The program exists because the gap in school nutrition support doesn't disappear when school's out. For families who rely on school breakfast and lunch during the academic year, summer can mean a real shortfall in consistent, nutritious meals for kids. The Summer Food Service Program is meant to fill that gap.

It's a significant community resource that a lot of Colorado Springs families don't know about. If you know parents — neighbors, coworkers, members of your church or community group — who might benefit from this program, passing along the information is worth doing.

What's Next

The program is available now and continues through the summer on weekdays. To find the meal site closest to your family, check D11's website at d11.org or use the USDA's Summer Food Service Program site finder at summerfood.usda.gov.

If you have questions about the program — eligibility, schedules, or specific sites — D11's nutrition services department is the right point of contact. The program is free, it's available for any child in Colorado Springs, and there's nothing to sign up for. Just show up.
