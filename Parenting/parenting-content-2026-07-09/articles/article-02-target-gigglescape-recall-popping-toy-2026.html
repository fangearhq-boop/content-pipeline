Target Is Recalling the Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy — Here's How to Get Your Refund

By Sarah Morales

July 9, 2026 · Colorado Springs

Target is recalling approximately 49,000 Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toys after reports that the toy can break apart and create a choking hazard for young children. If this toy is in your home — or if you gave it as a gift this past holiday season — it's time to pull it out of circulation and return it.

The recall was issued by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission. The toy was sold exclusively at Target stores nationwide from August 2025 through January 2026 for about $10. That timing means a significant number of families likely received this as a birthday or holiday gift during the fall and winter months.

What's Wrong With the Toy

The Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy consists of a clear plastic dome mounted on a blue whale-shaped base. Small plastic balls are housed inside the dome, which pops them when pressed — a satisfying tactile toy for young kids. The problem is that the dome can separate from the whale base, releasing those small plastic balls. For toddlers and young children, those balls are a choking hazard.

This isn't a theoretical concern. The CPSC has received nine reports of the dome detaching from the base. One of those incidents included a report of a child beginning to choke. That single incident is enough to make this an urgent safety issue.

The hazard is particularly significant because this toy is designed for young children — precisely the age group most at risk for choking on small objects. The CPSC's general guideline is that any object small enough to fit entirely in a child's mouth poses a choking risk for children under four years old.

How to Identify the Recalled Toy

The Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy features a blue whale-shaped base with a clear dome on top that sits over small, colorful plastic balls. It was sold only at Target — not at other retailers. If you purchased it at Target between August 2025 and January 2026, it's included in the recall.

Check toy bins, toy chests, and gift bags that haven't been fully unpacked. If you gave this as a gift to another family, reach out to let them know about the recall. Forty-nine thousand units is a large number, and many families may not see the official notice.

How to Return It and Get Your Refund

Target has made the return process simple. You have two options:

You can bring the toy to any Target store and receive a full refund of $10. You don't need a receipt. Target's customer service desk will handle the return as a recalled item.

If you'd rather not make a trip to the store, you can contact Target's customer service to request a prepaid return shipping label and handle the return by mail.

Either way, stop using the toy immediately and keep it away from children while you arrange the return. Don't donate it or pass it to another family — returning it through the official process is the right way to ensure it's removed from circulation.

What's Next

If you're in the market for a replacement toy in this category, look for push-pop or bubble toys that are designed as a single sealed unit without removable parts. When evaluating any toy for a young child, check the age recommendation on the packaging and look for any notes about small parts.

Target's customer service can be reached at 1-800-440-0680 for questions about this recall. The CPSC maintains full recall details at cpsc.gov, including photos of the recalled product. If you have any doubts about whether your toy is included, check the CPSC page and compare.

Pass this along to any parent who gave or received this toy. At $10, it's the kind of thing that gets grabbed quickly and doesn't always come with a receipt — making it harder to track when a recall comes through.
