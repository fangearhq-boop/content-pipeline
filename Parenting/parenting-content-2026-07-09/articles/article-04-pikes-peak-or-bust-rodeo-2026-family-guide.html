Your Family Guide to the Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo: July 14-18 in Colorado Springs

By Sarah Morales

July 9, 2026 · Colorado Springs

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo has been a Colorado Springs summer tradition since 1937, and it returns this year from July 14 through July 18 at the Norris Penrose Event Center. Whether your family has been going for years or this is your first time, here's what you need to know to make the most of it.

The Schedule

Evening performances run Tuesday through Saturday at 7:30 PM. If your family does better in the afternoon, matinee shows are available on Friday and Saturday at 12:30 PM.

Don't overlook Thursday, July 16: a free drone show takes place after the evening performance. No extra ticket is required — if you're already at the rodeo, you stay for the show. Drone shows have become a legitimate event in their own right, and the combination of a rodeo evening followed by an aerial display is a solid value for the ticket price.

Tickets are available at ppbrodeo.com. Prices vary by section and day. It's worth buying in advance to lock in your seats — weekend matinees in particular tend to fill up with families.

The Fan Zone: What Kids Will Love

The Fan Zone is where this event really works for families with young children. Here's what's available:

Mutton Bustin' is the main draw for the under-10 crowd. Kids ages 4 through 9 who weigh under 50 pounds can get on a sheep and try to hold on. No pre-registration required — families just show up and get in line. Arrive early if Mutton Bustin' is a priority; wait times grow as the evening goes on. Kids are equipped with protective gear before their ride.

Pony rides are available in the Fan Zone and tend to be popular with younger kids who aren't quite ready for the Mutton Bustin' line. The pace is calm and the handlers are experienced with small children.

The Fan Zone also includes a petting zoo, face painting, and goat roping — giving kids multiple activity options so the evening doesn't hinge on any one thing.

Plan to arrive 30-45 minutes before the performance starts to give the kids time in the Fan Zone before the main event. Trying to squeeze everything in at intermission means rushed decisions and longer lines.

Don't Miss the Parade

The Pikes Peak or Bust Parade kicks off the whole rodeo week this Saturday, July 11, at 11 AM in downtown Colorado Springs. The parade runs through the city center and is free to watch from the sidewalk. It's a low-key, family-friendly way to get into the spirit before the rodeo itself opens on Tuesday.

Bring a blanket or lawn chairs if you want a comfortable viewing spot, and plan for the crowds that come with a downtown parade. Parking is easier if you arrive early and park a few blocks off the main route.

Getting There

The Norris Penrose Event Center is located at 1045 W. Rio Grande St. in Colorado Springs. Parking is available on-site, though it fills up on busy nights. For evening performances, arriving 30-45 minutes early gives you time to park, get to the Fan Zone, and find your seats before the show starts.

What's Next

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo runs through Saturday, July 18. Tickets and the full schedule are at ppbrodeo.com. If you haven't been before, this is a genuine piece of Colorado Springs history — 89 years of the same event in the same city. For families who have made it an annual tradition, this year's addition of the July 16 drone show gives you something new to look forward to.
