CPSC Recalls VEVOR Baby Loungers: What Colorado Springs Parents Need to Do Now

By Jamie Rivera

July 9, 2026 · Colorado Springs

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 237 VEVOR baby loungers after finding the product fails to meet mandatory federal safety standards for infant sleep. If you have one of these loungers at home, you need to stop using it right away.

The recalled loungers were sold through Amazon, Wayfair, and VEVOR.com between November 2024 and January 2026 for around $30. This is a mandatory standards recall — meaning the CPSC determined the product never met the federal safety floor for infant sleep products in the first place.

Two Distinct Hazards

The recall identifies two separate problems with the VEVOR baby lounger. First, the sides of the lounger are too low to safely contain an infant, creating an entrapment and fall risk. Second, the foot openings on the product are wider than what's permitted under mandatory standards for infant sleep products.

Together, these two deficiencies mean a baby placed in this lounger isn't protected the way an infant sleep product is required to protect them. Neither flaw is minor — both relate directly to the physical containment requirements that exist to keep infants safe during rest.

It's worth understanding what "mandatory standards" means in this context. The federal government has established minimum safety requirements that all infant sleep products must meet before they can legally be sold. When a product doesn't comply, the CPSC issues a mandatory recall regardless of whether an injury has occurred. This isn't a discretionary judgment call — the product simply wasn't legal to sell.

How to Identify the Recalled Product

Check the product tag for the brand name VEVOR. The recalled items are baby loungers — padded, nest-like products designed for infant rest. If you purchased a VEVOR-branded lounger from Amazon, Wayfair, or VEVOR.com in the timeframe covered by the recall (November 2024 through January 2026), it's included.

If you've received this product as a gift and aren't sure of the purchase date, default to caution. Look at the product tag for the VEVOR name and stop use until you've confirmed whether it's affected.

What You're Being Asked to Do

The CPSC's remedy has three steps:

First, stop using the lounger immediately. Don't wait until you've contacted VEVOR or confirmed the refund process — take the product out of rotation right now.

Second, destroy the product by cutting the cover, foam, and padding in half. This is standard practice for recalled infant sleep products. The destruction requirement exists to prevent the item from being resold, donated, or passed along to another family who wouldn't know about the recall.

Third, contact VEVOR directly to request your full refund. VEVOR is the responsible party for reimbursement. Keep a photo of the destroyed product if you're concerned about documenting your claim.

What's Next

If you purchased this lounger and need a replacement, look for infant sleep products that carry CPSC compliance documentation and meet current mandatory federal standards. The CPSC website at cpsc.gov maintains an up-to-date recalls database where you can search before purchasing any infant sleep product.

Colorado families who have questions about the refund process can contact VEVOR directly through their website. The CPSC also maintains a hotline at 1-800-638-2772 for recall-related questions.

Share this information with any parent you know who may have purchased this product — the recall volume of 237 units is small, and word-of-mouth matters when the official recall notice might not reach every affected family.
