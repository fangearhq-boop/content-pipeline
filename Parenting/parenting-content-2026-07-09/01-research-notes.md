## STORY 1: CPSC Recall — VEVOR Baby Loungers (~237 Units)

### Key Facts

- **Recall issuing authority:** U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) [HIGH]
- **Product:** VEVOR Baby Lounger [HIGH]
- **Units recalled:** Approximately 237 [HIGH]
- **Sale period:** November 2024 through January 2026 [HIGH]
- **Sale price:** Approximately $30 [HIGH]
- **Retailers:** VEVOR.com, Amazon, Wayfair [HIGH]
- **Hazard 1:** Sides of the lounger are too low to contain an infant — entrapment and fall risk [HIGH]
- **Hazard 2:** Foot openings are wider than permitted under mandatory standards for infant sleep products [HIGH]
- **Incidents reported:** None cited in recall notice [MEDIUM]
- **Remedy:** (1) Stop use immediately; (2) cut the cover, foam, and pad in half to destroy the product; (3) contact VEVOR for a full refund [HIGH]
- **Refund contact:** VEVOR directly [HIGH]
- **Regulatory standard referenced:** Mandatory federal standard for infant sleep products [HIGH]

### Context
This is a mandatory standard compliance recall, meaning the product does not meet the federal safety floor for infant sleep products — not a discretionary hazard assessment. The destruction requirement (cut in half) is standard practice for recalled infant sleep products to prevent resale or re-use.

## STORY 2: CPSC Recall — Target Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy (~49,000 Units)

### Key Facts

- **Recall issuing authority:** CPSC [HIGH]
- **Product:** Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy [HIGH]
- **Description:** Clear plastic dome on blue whale-shaped base; contains small plastic balls [HIGH]
- **Units recalled:** Approximately 49,000 [HIGH]
- **Sale period:** August 2025 through January 2026 [HIGH]
- **Sale price:** Approximately $10 [HIGH]
- **Retailer:** Target stores nationwide exclusively [HIGH]
- **Hazard:** The clear plastic dome can separate from its blue whale-shaped base, allowing children to access small plastic balls — choking hazard [HIGH]
- **Incidents:** 9 reports of the dome detaching [HIGH]
- **Injuries:** 1 report of a child beginning to choke [HIGH]
- **Remedy:** Stop use immediately; return to any Target store for a full refund OR request a prepaid return label [HIGH]
- **Holiday context:** Sold August 2025–January 2026 — many likely purchased as holiday gifts [MEDIUM]

### Context
49,000 units is a large recall. The choking incident makes this an injury-adjacent recall. Target accepts returns at all store locations — no receipt required per standard CPSC recall procedure.

The DAC re-entry process is significant for displaced residents. The monsoon's arrival is a double-edged development: moisture helps firefighters but introduces new hazards.
