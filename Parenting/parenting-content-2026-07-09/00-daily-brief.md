## Today's Stories

### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — VEVOR Baby Loungers (~237 Units)
- **Tier:** 1
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Byline:** Jamie Rivera
- **Summary:** CPSC has recalled approximately 237 VEVOR baby loungers sold at VEVOR.com, Amazon, and Wayfair from November 2024 through January 2026, for approximately $30. Two hazards: the sides are too low to contain an infant (entrapment/fall risk) and the foot openings are wider than permitted under mandatory standards for infant sleep products. Remedy: stop use immediately; cut the cover, foam, and pad in half; contact VEVOR for a full refund.
- **Angle:** Recall action guide — how to identify the product, the hazard, and how to get a refund.

### STORY 2: CPSC Recall — Target Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy (~49,000 Units)
- **Tier:** 1
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Byline:** Sarah Morales
- **Summary:** Target is recalling approximately 49,000 Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toys. The clear plastic dome can separate from its blue whale-shaped base, allowing children to access small plastic balls. Nine reports of the dome detaching; one report of a child beginning to choke. Sold exclusively at Target stores nationwide August 2025–January 2026 for approximately $10. Remedy: stop use immediately; return to any Target store for a full refund or request a prepaid return label.
- **Angle:** Recall action guide — especially relevant for families who bought this as a holiday gift.

### STORY 3: D11 Free Summer Meals — Free Breakfast and Lunch for Kids Ages 1-18 in COS
- **Tier:** 2
- **Pillar:** Local News
- **Byline:** Jamie Rivera
- **Summary:** Colorado Springs School District 11 is offering free breakfast and lunch to all children ages 1-18 across Colorado Springs this summer — regardless of which school district they attend. Meals are served Monday through Friday at schools, community centers, and playgrounds throughout the city. The program is open to any child, not just D11 students.
- **Angle:** Resource alert for COS families — how to find a nearby summer meal site.

### STORY 4: Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo — July 14-18 at Norris Penrose Event Center (Plus Parade July 11)
- **Tier:** 2
- **Pillar:** Local Events
- **Byline:** Sarah Morales
- **Summary:** The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo returns to Colorado Springs July 14-18 at the Norris Penrose Event Center. Evening performances at 7:30 PM Tuesday–Saturday; matinee shows at 12:30 PM Friday and Saturday. Family-friendly Fan Zone includes Mutton Bustin' (kids ages 4-9, under 50 lbs, no pre-registration required), pony rides, petting zoo, face painting, and goat roping. Thursday July 16 features a free drone show after the performance. Pikes Peak or Bust Parade in downtown Colorado Springs Saturday July 11 at 11 AM kicks off the week.

### STORY 5: Aspen Acres Fire Update — 96,031 Acres, Flash Flood Watch, Monsoon Arrives
- **Tier:** 2
- **Pillar:** Local News
- **Byline:** Jamie Rivera
- **Summary:** The Aspen Acres Fire has grown to 96,031 acres as of Wednesday July 8 with 15% containment — a distinct update from yesterday's 93,916-acre report. The fire has now destroyed 275 total structures (192 in Pueblo County + 83 in Custer County). A flash flood watch is in effect through Wednesday evening as monsoonal moisture arrives — the rain is welcome but gusty outflow winds up to 50 mph and flash flooding on fire-scarred soil create new hazards. Rockvale, Coal Creek, and Williamsburg in Fremont County are now in pre-evacuation status (downgraded from mandatory). Disaster Assistance Center is issuing re-entry permits for some areas.
- **Angle:** Fresh update for July 9 — distinct from yesterday's story (new acreage, flood watch, Fremont County status change, permit re-entry).
