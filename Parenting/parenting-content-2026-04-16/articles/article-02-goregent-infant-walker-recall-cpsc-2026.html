CPSC Recalls Goregent Infant Walkers Sold on Amazon — Stop Use Immediately

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting | April 16, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission issued a recall on March 19, 2026, for approximately 90 Goregent infant walkers sold on Amazon. If you purchased one of these walkers in January 2026 for around $90, stop using it now and follow the refund process outlined by the CPSC.

No injuries have been reported, but the commission is urging immediate action because of a significant fall hazard.

What's the Hazard

The Goregent infant walker is narrow enough to fit through standard doorways and does not have a stopping mechanism that activates at the edge of a step. This means an infant in the walker can reach a staircase or step and continue forward, creating a serious fall and injury risk.

Infant walkers have a long-documented safety history with the CPSC. Fall-related injuries involving walkers have led to hundreds of hospitalizations over the years, which is why the commission closely monitors products that don't meet current safety standards.

How to Identify the Recalled Walker

The recalled Goregent infant walker has the following characteristics:

  • Green plastic frame
  • Fabric seat with animal-print pattern
  • Activity tray with lights and music
  • Round base with six wheels

These walkers were sold exclusively through the Goregent Official Store on Amazon.com during January 2026 at a price of approximately $90. If you're unsure whether you purchased this item, check your Amazon order history for January 2026.

How to Get a Full Refund

The CPSC has outlined a specific process for the refund. Consumers should:

  1. Stop using the walker immediately.
  2. Disassemble the walker.
  3. Write "Recalled" on the product.
  4. Email GoregentInfantWalkersRecall@outlook.com with purchase information to initiate the refund.

The CPSC has published the recall at cpsc.gov, where the official notice includes full product details and contact information.

A Note on Infant Walkers Generally

The American Academy of Pediatrics has discouraged the use of wheeled infant walkers for years, citing fall risks — particularly on stairs — as well as the risk that walkers can delay standing and walking development in some infants. If you're considering an alternative, stationary activity centers offer similar engagement without the mobility risk.

This recall is specific to the Goregent brand and the approximately 90 units sold in January 2026. It is a separate recall from other infant walker recalls that have occurred in recent years.
