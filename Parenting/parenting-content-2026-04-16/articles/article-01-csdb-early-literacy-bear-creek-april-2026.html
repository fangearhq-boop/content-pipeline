Free CSDB Early Literacy Event at Bear Creek — Friday, April 17

By Jenna Park | COS Parenting | April 16, 2026

The Colorado School for the Deaf and the Blind is hosting a free Early Literacy Event this Friday, April 17, at Bear Creek Nature Center in Colorado Springs. The event runs from 1:30 to 3:00 PM and is designed for families with deaf and hard-of-hearing children from birth to age 8.

Registration is required and spots are limited. Families can sign up by emailing outreach@csdb.org.

What to Expect at the Event

The afternoon is built around three core activities: an American Sign Language book reading, a wildlife learning session, and a short nature walk through the Bear Creek area. An ASL interpreter will be on site throughout the event.

Beyond the structured activities, the event is designed as a gathering space. Families will have the opportunity to connect with other parents raising deaf and hard-of-hearing children, and to meet deaf and hard-of-hearing adults in the Colorado Springs community. That kind of peer connection can be meaningful for both children and caregivers navigating communication and education decisions early in a child's life.

The event is a collaboration between CSDB and El Paso County Parks, which manages the Bear Creek Nature Center. Admission is free.

Why Early Literacy Matters for Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing Children

Research consistently shows that early language exposure — whether spoken, signed, or both — is critical for literacy development. For deaf and hard-of-hearing children, access to rich language environments in the first years of life shapes long-term reading, communication, and learning outcomes.

Events like this one give families tools and community support at the moment they're most useful: during the birth-to-8 window when the foundations for literacy are being built. The combination of ASL storytelling, nature-based learning, and adult community connection offers a practical and low-barrier entry point for families who may be early in their journey.

About CSDB

The Colorado School for the Deaf and the Blind is a Colorado state agency that provides educational services to students who are deaf, hard of hearing, blind, or visually impaired. CSDB's outreach programs extend beyond its campus in Colorado Springs to serve families across the region, including through community events like this one.
