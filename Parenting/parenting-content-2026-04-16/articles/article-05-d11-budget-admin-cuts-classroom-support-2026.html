D11 Is Cutting Central Administration Again — and Directing More Resources to Classrooms

By Marcus Rivera | COS Parenting | April 16, 2026

Colorado Springs School District 11 is heading into its 2026-27 budget season with a clear philosophy: reduce the size of central administration and put more resources directly into classrooms and teacher compensation. This is the third consecutive year D11 has taken this approach.

KOAA confirmed that budget discussions ongoing this spring include continued cuts to district-level administrative departments, while keeping school-level programs and staffing intact. For families with students in D11 schools, that means the changes at the district office shouldn't translate into fewer teachers, reduced programs, or cuts to school resources.

What "Cutting Central Administration" Actually Means

When a school district refers to cutting central administration, it's typically talking about the departments housed at the district office — human resources, communications, curriculum development, finance, and other support functions that don't directly involve classroom instruction. Reducing those departments frees up funding that can be redirected to schools.

D11's approach over the past three years has been to right-size those district-level operations, both by consolidating functions and by not backfilling positions when they turn over. The result is a leaner central office that routes more dollars to where students and teachers can directly benefit from them.

Teacher Compensation in 2025-26

Part of what that philosophy has funded in recent years is teacher pay. In the 2025-26 school year, D11 delivered what it described as "double-digit compensation increases" for teachers — the largest compensation package in the Pikes Peak region. Starting teacher pay for 2025-26 was set at $51,510, according to the D11 salary schedule.

This is notable context for a district that went through a contentious stretch in its labor relations after ending its master bargaining agreement. The shift toward competitive compensation has been a stated priority for district leadership, and the budget approach of cutting administration to fund classrooms is part of how D11 has backed that up.

What It Means for D11 Families

For families with children in D11 schools, the key takeaway from this year's budget season is that classroom-level programs and school staffing are not targets of these reductions. The cuts are at the administrative tier, not the school tier.

Budget discussions for 2026-27 are ongoing through the spring. Final budget numbers for D11 won't be set until later in the year, but the direction is consistent with what families have seen over the past two years: administration shrinks, classrooms stay funded.
