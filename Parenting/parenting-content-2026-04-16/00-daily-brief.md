### STORY 2: CPSC Recalls Goregent Infant Walkers Over Fall Hazard — Sold on Amazon
**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
**Status:** NEW STORY

The CPSC announced a recall on March 19, 2026 of approximately 90 Goregent infant walkers sold on Amazon. The green plastic walkers with animal-print fabric seats can fit through standard doorways and fail to stop at the edge of a step, creating a serious fall and injury risk for infants. The walkers were sold on Amazon in January 2026 for about $90 from the Goregent Official Store. No injuries have been reported. Consumers should immediately stop use and contact Goregent for a full refund by emailing GoregentInfantWalkersRecall@outlook.com.

**Key facts (verified):**
- Recall date: March 19, 2026 — HIGH (cpsc.gov)
- ~90 units recalled — HIGH (cpsc.gov)

### STORY 4: AAP Says Start HPV Vaccination at Age 9 — What COS Families Should Know
**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Status:** NEW STORY

The American Academy of Pediatrics published a blog post on April 7, 2026 reinforcing its recommendation that pediatricians offer HPV vaccination starting as early as age 9. The AAP's recommendation differs from the CDC's updated one-dose recommendation at age 11-12 — the AAP continues to recommend two doses starting at 9-12. The earlier start allows more flexibility, separates the vaccine from sexuality discussions at puberty, and may improve series completion rates.

**Key facts (verified):**
- AAP April 7, 2026 blog: "The Time Is Now — Starting HPV Protection Early" — HIGH (aap.org)
- AAP recommends 2 doses, starting at age 9-12 — HIGH (aap.org + pubmed confirmed)
- CDC updated to 1-dose recommendation at 11-12 — HIGH (multiple sources)
- Earlier initiation improves series completion by age 13 — HIGH (PMC research article)
- HPV vaccine protects against cancers, not just STIs — HIGH (standard AAP guidance)
