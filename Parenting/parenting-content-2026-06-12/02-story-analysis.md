### STORY 1: D49 Budget Cuts Take Effect July 1

**Byline:** Jamie Rivera

**Core Angle:** The budget drama is over; the real-world impact begins July 1. Parents with kids in Base49, PEAK, preschool, or Elevates 18-21 have 19 days to find alternatives. This story shifts from "what might happen" to "here's what you need to do."

**Key Hooks:**
- July 1 is 19 days away — actionable urgency
- Base49 and PEAK serve hundreds of D49 families for before/after school care
- Families relying on preschool through D49 are most impacted (cost of private alternatives)
- Elevates 18-21: transition services for adult students with disabilities — a vulnerable group
- 12 teachers already lost jobs; this makes it concrete

**Best Headlines:**
- "D49 Cuts Are Final: What Changes for Your Family Starting July 1"
- "19 Days: D49 Families Scramble as Budget Cuts Take Effect"

**Tier 1 Rationale:** Local impact, immediate timeline, actionable for families.

### STORY 2: Joyin Sloosh Dive Sticks Recall

**Byline:** Sarah Morales

**Core Angle:** Perfect summer timing — this recall dropped June 11, just as families are heading into pool season. 254,000 dive sticks recalled because they can impale a child in shallow water. If you have a Sloosh water toy set (the 30-piece pack), the dive sticks are recalled.

**Key Hooks:**
- Summer pool season = maximum parent concern about pool toy safety
- "Federal ban on dive sticks" exists — parents may not know this
- 254,000 units is a large number across Amazon, Target, Temu
- Clear action: dispose (don't donate), photograph, contact Joyin for free replacements
- Facial and eye injury risk while retrieving underwater — parents don't think about this risk

**Best Headlines:**
- "CPSC Recalls 254,000 Pool Dive Sticks — Check Your Sloosh Summer Set Now"
- "Pool Safety Recall: Joyin Sloosh Dive Sticks Pose Impalement Risk for Kids"

**Tier 1 Rationale:** Active summer season; large recall; immediate safety action required.

### STORY 3: Cosyland Tower Stool Recall

**Byline:** Jamie Rivera

**Core Angle:** This is the third tower stool brand recalled in 2026. The pattern — collapse, entrapment, injury — is identical across Guidecraft (May), Wiifo (April), and now Cosyland. Parents who bought any brand should look twice at ALL their step stools and tower stools.

**Key Hooks:**
- Third brand in 2026 (pattern alert for readers who followed previous recalls)
- 125,200 units — large scale
- Fractured arm among the injuries — most serious injury type cited in any of the three recalls
- Natural bamboo look (trendy aesthetic = widely purchased)
- Free repair kit available — parents don't have to throw it away

**Best Headlines:**
- "Third Tower Stool Brand Recalled in 2026 — Cosyland, 125,000 Units, Fracture Risk"
- "Cosyland Tower Stool Recall: If Your Bamboo Step Stool Is From Amazon, Check Now"

**Tier 1 Rationale:** Pattern story; large recall; household product parents may not know to check.

### STORY 4: AAP Screen Time Guidelines

**Byline:** Sarah Morales

**Core Angle:** Summer screen time guilt is real for COS parents. The AAP's updated guidance offers a more nuanced take: it's not just how much, it's what and how. Co-watching, discussing content, and keeping screens out of bedrooms and off the dinner table are the highest-impact behaviors.

**Key Hooks:**
- Summer = kids at home = screen time skyrockets = parent guilt
- "Quality over quantity" framing gives parents permission to relax
- Co-viewing is productive, not just screen babysitting
- Device-free bedroom is the single most impactful intervention per AAP
- COS families can connect to local screen-free alternatives (parks, trails, WMMI, Bear Creek)

**Best Headlines:**
- "AAP's New Screen Time Advice: It's Not How Much — It's How You Watch Together"
- "Summer Screen Time: What AAP Says Has Changed for 2026"

**Tier 2 Rationale:** National content with strong summer relevance; not breaking news but timely and useful.

### STORY 5: D49 Free Summer Meals — 7 Days Left

**Byline:** Jamie Rivera

**Core Angle:** Last week of D49's free summer meals program. Parents who haven't used it yet have one week. Families who've been using it should know it ends June 19. D11's program runs longer — through July 31 — so families not in D49 have more time.

**Key Hooks:**
- 7 days feels urgent; June 19 is next Friday
- No barriers — no registration, no ID, no income check. This is the program's best feature.
- D49 program ending is bittersweet given the broader budget cuts context (D49 is tightening everything)
- D11 through July 31 = good news for D11 families; helpful reminder that the two districts have different programs

**Best Headlines:**
- "D49 Free Summer Meals End June 19 — 7 Days to Take Advantage"
- "One Week Left: D49 Free Breakfast and Lunch for Kids Ages 1-18"

**Tier 2 Rationale:** Short countdown story; useful to families; follows up previous coverage.
