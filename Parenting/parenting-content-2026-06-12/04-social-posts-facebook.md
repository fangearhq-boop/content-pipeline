### STORY 1: D49 Budget Cuts Take Effect July 1

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**

If your family depends on any D49 program, this is your 19-day warning.

The D49 Board of Education voted Wednesday evening to formally adopt the 2026-27 budget. The cuts that were approved 4-1 back in February are now finalized and take effect July 1 — the start of the new school year. Here's what's changing:

**Base49** (before and after school care) — being eliminated or significantly reduced. If you've been using this for child care, you'll need to arrange alternatives before August.

**PEAK** (extended learning programs) — reduced. Families who count on PEAK for academic support should check directly with their D49 school for specifics.

**Preschool** — D49's preschool program is cut. Families who enrolled or were planning to enroll their youngest will need to find private or other public options.

**Elevates 18-21** — transition services for young adults with disabilities are impacted. These families are among the most affected and have the fewest alternatives.

D49 declared fiscal exigency in January 2026, driven largely by enrollment decline. The district has already eliminated 12 teacher positions. The budget cuts are not a surprise — but July 1 is approaching fast.

If you have kids in D49, check d49.org for program-specific updates and contact your school directly if you have questions about what's available next year.

### STORY 2: Pool Toy Recall — Joyin Sloosh Dive Sticks

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

Before you head to the pool this weekend, check your water toys. 🏊

The CPSC announced on June 11 that Joyin is recalling approximately 254,000 Sloosh dive sticks due to an impalement hazard. Dive sticks are the weighted torpedo-shaped toys kids throw underwater and race to retrieve — and these particular ones violate a federal safety ban.

Here's why they're dangerous: these sticks exceeded the compression limits required under federal regulations. In shallow water, a child can fall or land directly on a dive stick, causing serious piercing injuries. Facial and eye injuries are also possible when retrieving the sticks underwater.

**How to know if you have them:**
The Sloosh set comes as a 30-piece water toy package. The dive sticks inside are Model 40041, printed on the back of the box and on each stick. They measure about 7 inches long.

**Where they were sold:** Amazon, Target, Temu, Wayfair, and SHEIN from February 2019 through October 2025 for $17-$22.

**What to do:**
1. Stop using the dive sticks immediately
2. Dispose of them
3. Photograph the disposed sticks
4. Contact Joyin through the product information for free redesigned replacement sticks

The other pieces in the 30-piece Sloosh set (rings, etc.) are not part of this recall.

### STORY 3: Cosyland Tower Stool Recall

**Long-Form Post (12:00 PM MT)**

Heads up if you have a bamboo or wood-tone kitchen helper step stool in your home.

The CPSC has recalled approximately 125,200 Cosyland-brand children's tower stools — Models CS0003 and CS0092-4 — due to entrapment and fall hazards. A child's torso can fit through the openings on the front and back of the stool. Combined with collapse risk, this creates a serious entrapment and injury scenario. Cosyland has received 25 reports of stability issues and falls, including 8 injuries — one of which was a fractured arm.

**The product details:**
- Natural bamboo and gray finishes
- About 16 inches deep × 18 inches wide × 35 inches tall
- Sold only on Amazon from April 2021 through November 2025 for around $70

**What to do:**
1. Stop using the stool immediately
2. Store it away from children until repaired
3. Contact Cosyland for a free repair kit — it includes protective nets, stabilizing feet, and installation instructions
4. Visit cosyland.com/pages/recall for the official recall page

One more thing worth noting: this is now the **third** kitchen tower stool brand recalled in 2026 — following Guidecraft in May and Wiifo in April. The same hazard comes up each time: collapse risk and child entrapment. If you have any brand of tower step stool at home, it's worth a closer look, especially if it was bought in the last few years.

### STORY 4: AAP Screen Time Guidelines

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM MT)**

Summer break is here and screens are... everywhere. Take a breath. Here's what the American Academy of Pediatrics actually says.

The AAP's 2026 guidance on screen time has evolved toward something more nuanced than a strict clock-watching approach:

**Under 18 months:** No screens at all (video calling with family is the exception).

**Ages 2-5:** Limit to about 1 hour per day of high-quality programming — not TikTok, but actual educational or age-appropriate content.

**Older kids:** The focus shifts away from time limits and toward quality, context, and conversation. What are they watching? Are you watching with them? Are you talking about it?

The highest-impact habit, according to the AAP? **Device-free bedrooms and mealtimes.** Not limiting TikTok time, not setting a strict daily cap — just keeping devices out of those two spaces.

Co-viewing matters too. Sitting with your kids while they watch something and asking questions about it — recognizing ads, talking about characters' choices, discussing privacy — builds the kind of digital literacy that time limits alone can't create.

Summer is a good time to reset routines, not feel guilty about every screen hour. The goal isn't zero screens — it's intentional screens.

### STORY 5: D49 Free Summer Meals — 7 Days Left

**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM MT)**

One week. That's how much time is left to use D49's free summer meals program.

The D49 free summer meals program ends on June 19 — next Friday. Through then, any child age 1-18 can get free breakfast and lunch at participating sites, with no registration, no ID, and no income verification required. You just show up.

Springs Ranch Elementary is a confirmed serving site. For the full list of locations and hours, visit d49.org.

A few things worth knowing:

If you're in D11, your district's free summer meals program runs through July 31 — so you have more time. Details at d11.org.

If you're in D49, the June 19 cutoff is firm. Given that D49 is also cutting several programs effective July 1, this is one of the last district-wide benefits for families before those changes hit.

No need to be enrolled in D49. Ages 1-18 qualifies. Free breakfast and lunch. No paperwork.
