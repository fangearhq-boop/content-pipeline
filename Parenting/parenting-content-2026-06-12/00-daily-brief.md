### STORY 2: Pool Toy Recall — 254,000 Joyin Dive Sticks Pose Impalement Risk
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Posting Window:** 10:00 AM MT (national parenting)
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

CPSC announced June 11, 2026: Joyin recalls approximately 254,000 Sloosh dive sticks (Model 40041) due to impalement hazard. Dive sticks violate the federal dive sticks ban — exceeded compression limits pose risk of children being impaled in shallow water; facial and eye injuries also possible. Sold at Amazon, Target, Temu, Wayfair, SHEIN, February 2019 – October 2025, $17-$22. Remedy: stop using immediately, dispose of sticks, send Joyin a disposal photo, receive redesigned replacement sticks that meet federal regulations.

**Key facts:**
- CPSC recall: June 11, 2026
- ~254,000 units
- Model 40041 (printed on back of Sloosh water toys box and on stick itself)
- Package contains 30 pieces including 5 dive sticks
- Each stick: ~7 inches long, ~1 inch or less diameter
- Sold: Amazon, Target, Temu.com, Wayfair, SHEIN — Feb 2019 through Oct 2025
- Price: $17-$22
- Action: stop using, dispose, send disposal photo to Joyin, get redesigned replacements
- No injuries reported in search results (CPSC page)

### STORY 3: Third Tower Stool Brand Recalled This Year — Cosyland, 125,000 Units
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Posting Window:** 12:00 PM MT (midday engagement)
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

CPSC recalled approximately 125,200 Cosyland-brand children's tower stools (Models CS0003 and CS0092-4) due to entrapment and fall hazards. A child's torso can fit through front and back openings; stools can collapse or tip over while in use. 25 reports of stability issues and falls, including 8 injuries — among them a fractured arm. Sold on Amazon only, April 2021 – November 2025, ~$70. Remedy: stop use immediately, store away from children, contact Cosyland for free repair kit (protective nets, stabilizing feet, installation instructions). This is the third tower stool brand recalled this year (following Guidecraft May 14 and Wiifo April 23).

**Key facts:**
- CPSC recall (date of Cosyland recall not confirmed — May or June 2026)
- ~125,200 units
- Models CS0003 and CS0092-4
- Colors: natural bamboo and gray
- Dimensions: ~16" deep x 18" wide x 35" tall
- Amazon only; ~$70; April 2021 – November 2025
- 25 reports, 8 injuries including fractured arm
- Action: stop use, store away from children, contact Cosyland for repair kit
- cosyland.com/pages/recall
- Sources: CPSC.gov, NBC Connecticut, KIRO 7, WCNC Charlotte

### STORY 4: AAP 2026 Screen Time Guidelines — Quality Over Quantity
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Posting Window:** 3:00 PM MT (after-school activities/events)
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

The American Academy of Pediatrics 2026 screen time guidance continues to evolve: no screens before 18 months (except video chat), 1 hour of high-quality content for ages 2-5, and for older kids — quality, context, and conversation matter more than strict time limits. New emphasis: screens shouldn't replace sleep, physical activity, family time, or free play. Device-free bedrooms and mealtimes recommended. Co-viewing and discussing content with kids builds critical digital literacy.

**Key facts:**
- No screens before 18 months (except video chat)
- Ages 2-5: limit to 1 hour/day of high-quality content
- Older kids: prioritize quality, context, and conversation over time limits
- Device-free bedrooms and mealtimes
- Co-viewing and discussing content = better outcomes
- Ongoing conversations about recognizing ads, privacy, body image, online permanence
- Sources: CHOC Children's Health Hub, AAP Policy page (aap.org/en/policy/)
