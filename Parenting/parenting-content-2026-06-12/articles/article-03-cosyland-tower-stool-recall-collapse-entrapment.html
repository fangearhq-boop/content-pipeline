Cosyland Tower Stool Recall: 125,000 Units, Collapse and Entrapment Risk — Third Brand Recalled in 2026

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | June 12, 2026

The CPSC has recalled approximately 125,200 Cosyland children's tower stools, citing collapse and entrapment hazards that have already resulted in 8 reported injuries — including one fractured arm. If you have a bamboo or natural-tone kitchen helper step stool from Cosyland, the product should be taken out of use immediately.

This is the third kitchen tower stool brand recalled in 2026. Guidecraft was recalled in May. Wiifo was recalled in April. Now Cosyland. The same hazard pattern has emerged each time: the stool can collapse, and a child's torso can fit through openings in the structure, creating a combined entrapment and fall scenario. At this point, if you have any brand of kitchen tower step stool that hasn't been scrutinized recently, it warrants a closer look.

What Makes These Stools Dangerous

Tower stools — also called kitchen helper stools or learning towers — are designed to bring a child up to counter height so they can participate in cooking, baking, and kitchen activities. The appeal is real: kids want to help, and these stools provide a safe-feeling platform to do that. The problem is when the structure itself fails to deliver on that safety promise.

The hazard with the Cosyland models has two components. First, the stool can become unstable and collapse, creating a fall hazard. Second, the openings on the front and back of the stool are large enough for a child's torso to fit through. If collapse occurs while a child is inside the structure, the entrapment risk compounds the injury scenario significantly. The fracture injury in Cosyland's own incident reports reflects how serious this combination can be.

Identifying the Recalled Models

The recalled Cosyland stools are Models CS0003 and CS0092-4. Both come in natural bamboo and gray finishes — the kind of clean, Scandinavian-adjacent aesthetic that's been popular in kitchen products for the past several years. The stools are approximately 16 inches deep, 18 inches wide, and 35 inches tall.

They were sold exclusively on Amazon from April 2021 through November 2025 at around $70. If you purchased a bamboo-look tower stool from Amazon in the last four years, check the model number on the product label or in your Amazon order history.

What to Do

Unlike some recalls that require discarding the product, Cosyland is offering a free repair kit that addresses both the stability and entrapment concerns. The repair process involves:

  1. Stop using the stool immediately and store it away from children until the repair kit is installed.
  2. Contact Cosyland for the free repair kit. The kit includes protective nets (which close off the entrapment openings), stabilizing feet, and full installation instructions.
  3. Visit cosyland.com/pages/recall for the official recall page and to submit your repair request.

The repair kit approach is worth taking seriously rather than assuming the stool is fine as-is. Cosyland received 25 reports of stability issues and falls before issuing this recall — the problem is not hypothetical.

The Broader Pattern

Three tower stool recalls in eight months is not coincidence. Guidecraft's recall in May and Wiifo's recall in April both cited the same core hazards: collapse risk, entrapment through structural openings, and injury from falls. The products look different but the failure mode is identical.

If you own a tower stool from any brand — not just these three — it's a reasonable moment to look at the structure with fresh eyes. How large are the openings? Does it feel stable under real conditions, not just when you push on it gently? Has it been modified or had any hardware loosen over time? These stools see hard daily use from kids who are, by design, active and enthusiastic about using them.

What's Next

For confirmed Cosyland Models CS0003 and CS0092-4, go to cosyland.com/pages/recall to start the repair kit request. For Amazon purchases, check your order history — Amazon may have sent a recall notification directly. If you have a different brand of tower stool and want to check its status, the CPSC recall database at cpsc.gov is searchable by product type and brand name.
