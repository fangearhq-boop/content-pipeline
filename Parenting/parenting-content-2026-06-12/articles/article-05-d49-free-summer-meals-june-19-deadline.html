D49 Free Summer Meals End June 19 — One Week Left for Families

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | June 12, 2026

One week from today, D49's free summer meals program ends. The cutoff is June 19, and between now and then, any child ages 1 to 18 can walk in to a participating site and receive free breakfast and lunch — no registration, no ID, no income verification, no strings attached. You just show up.

It's one of the simplest, most genuinely accessible programs available to Colorado Springs families, and it consistently flies under the radar. With seven days left in the D49 program, this is the window to use it.

What the Program Offers

The D49 free summer meals program is federally funded through the USDA's Summer Food Service Program, which reimburses districts and meal sponsors for providing free meals to children during the summer months. The program exists specifically because the free and reduced-price meal access that children have during the school year disappears over the summer — a gap that falls disproportionately on families who rely on those meals during the academic year.

The program is open to any child ages 1 to 18 who shows up at a participating site during meal service hours. You do not have to be enrolled in D49. You do not have to live in a specific neighborhood. You do not have to provide documentation of any kind. The program is designed to be barrier-free, and by every measure, it is.

Where to Go

Springs Ranch Elementary is a confirmed serving site for the D49 program. The full list of sites, along with current hours, is available at d49.org. Meal service hours and specific days can vary by site, so checking the district website before your first visit is the best way to make sure you arrive during an active service window.

The program operates Monday through Friday at most sites — weekends are typically not included. With seven days left, there are roughly five remaining service days between now and the June 19 cutoff.

D49 Context: A Program Worth Using While It's Here

The timing of the June 19 cutoff matters more this year than it might in other years. D49 is implementing significant program cuts effective July 1 — Base49, PEAK, and the district's preschool program are among the services being reduced or eliminated as part of the district's 2026-27 budget, which was formally adopted this week. The free summer meals program is one of the last district-wide benefits for families before those changes take effect.

That context isn't meant to add weight to something that should be simple — it's just worth knowing. The program is available, it's free, it's for any child in the age range, and it ends in seven days.

D11 Families Have More Time

If your children are enrolled in Colorado Springs School District 11 rather than D49, your district's free summer meals program runs through July 31 — giving you significantly more runway. The D11 program operates under similar no-barrier principles. Details are available at d11.org.

For families in D20 or other districts, checking your district's website for summer meal programming is worthwhile — the USDA Summer Food Service Program is available to districts across Colorado, and availability varies by location.

What's Next

For D49 families: visit d49.org for the current site list and hours before heading out. Springs Ranch Elementary is a confirmed site, but the full list may include locations closer to your neighborhood. The deadline is June 19 — next Thursday. For D11 families: your program runs through July 31, and details are at d11.org. No paperwork required at either program.
