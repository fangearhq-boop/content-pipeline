CPSC Recalls 254,000 Joyin Sloosh Dive Sticks — Check Your Pool Toy Set Now

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | June 12, 2026

With pool season in full swing across Colorado Springs, the Consumer Product Safety Commission issued a recall on June 11 that every family with a backyard pool or plans to visit the aquatic center should know about. Joyin is recalling approximately 254,000 Sloosh dive sticks due to an impalement hazard — a risk that's serious enough to have a federal ban behind it.

If you've purchased a Sloosh 30-piece water toy set, the torpedo-shaped dive sticks inside are the recalled product. Here's what you need to know to keep your kids safe this summer.

Why Dive Sticks Are Federally Regulated

There is a federal safety standard that specifically governs dive sticks — a regulation that exists because of documented injuries over many years. The rules set compression limits on how rigid a dive stick can be. The concern is straightforward: a child in shallow water can jump, trip, or fall directly onto a submerged dive stick. If the stick is too rigid, it can cause a penetrating impalement injury. Eye and facial injuries during underwater retrieval are also a documented risk.

The Joyin Sloosh dive sticks exceeded those compression limits, which is why the CPSC acted. This isn't a borderline call — it's a violation of a standard that exists specifically to prevent this type of injury.

How to Identify the Recalled Product

The recalled product is the dive sticks found inside the Sloosh 30-piece water toy set. The model number is 40041, printed on the back of the box and on each individual stick. The sticks are torpedo-shaped, approximately 7 inches long, and are designed to be thrown into the pool for children to retrieve by swimming underwater.

The recall applies only to the dive sticks themselves. The other components of the 30-piece Sloosh set — rings, hoops, and other water toys — are not part of this recall and are safe to continue using.

The sets were sold at Amazon, Target, Temu, Wayfair, and SHEIN between February 2019 and October 2025 for $17 to $22. If you've purchased pool toys from any of those retailers in the past six years, it's worth checking the box for the Sloosh branding and Model 40041.

What to Do If You Have Them

The CPSC and Joyin recommend a specific four-step process:

  1. Stop using the dive sticks immediately. Do not allow children to use them in the water.
  2. Dispose of the recalled sticks. Do not donate them — the hazard travels with the product.
  3. Photograph the disposed sticks as documentation before discarding.
  4. Contact Joyin through the product information to request free redesigned replacement sticks.

Joyin is providing free replacement dive sticks that meet the federal safety standard. The replacement process requires reaching out through the product's contact information — check the original packaging if you have it, or look up the recall on cpsc.gov for current contact details.

What's Next

If you use Amazon, it's worth checking your order history for the Sloosh set — Amazon has a recall notification system that may have already flagged this for affected purchases. For any questions about the replacement process, visit cpsc.gov and search for the Joyin Sloosh recall for official contact information. Summer pool season in Colorado Springs is short; getting the replacement sticks ordered now means your kids will have safe ones before the season is over.
