FDA Recall — Nara Organics Infant Formula (All Lots)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post 🚨 If you have Nara Organics Infant Formula at home, stop using it right now. The FDA has recalled all lots of Nara Organics Whole Milk Organic Powdered Infant Formula — both the 700g and 400g cans — due to possible Clostridium botulinum contamination. This is the bacteria that causes botulism, a serious and potentially fatal illness in infants. The recall is linked to a multistate botulism outbreak that has already hospitalized 3–4 infants in California, Washington, and Pennsylvania. The formula was sold at Target stores, Target.com, and Nara.com between July 2025 and June 2026. If you've purchased it from any of those sources, here's what to do: - Stop use immediately and keep it away from your baby. - If you ordered from Nara.com in May or June 2026, your order will be automatically refunded — allow 10–15 days. - If you ordered from Nara.com at other times, photograph the bottom of each can and submit a refund request at nara.com/pages/refund-request-form. - If you bought the 700g can at Target, return it to any Target store or follow Target's online return instructions. The FDA outbreak investigation page is at fda.gov, and Nara's own recall information is at nara.com/pages/nara-organics-recall-information. Have you seen this recall come across your feed today? Share this with any parents you know who use this formula. 🍼 ### Image Post Caption 🚨 FDA Recall: Nara Organics Infant Formula recalled — all lots, both can sizes. Possible botulism contamination. Sold at Target and Nara.com. Stop use immediately and visit nara.com/pages/nara-organics-recall-information for refund options.

CPSC Recall — BABESIDE Doll and Stroller Set (~2,200 Units)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ⚠️ If your child has a BABESIDE Doll and Stroller toy set — the pink-and-red one with the baby doll, stroller, and 23 accessories — there's a safety recall you need to know about. About 2,200 of these sets have been recalled by the CPSC. They were sold on Amazon by HYBDOLLS between July 2025 and January 2026 for around $40. There are two problems with specific parts: the toy pacifier included in the set violates the federal small parts ban because it's small enough to pose a deadly choking hazard for children under 3, and the eyes on the included plush bear can detach, creating a second choking hazard. Here's what to do: - Stop letting kids use the plush bear and the toy pacifier immediately. - To receive free replacement accessories from HYBDOLLS: cut the plush bear in half, mark an X on the pacifier in permanent marker, and email a photo of both destroyed items to support@babeside.com. Shipping is included. No injuries have been reported so far, but the parts are genuinely hazardous for young children. The rest of the set — the stroller, doll, and non-hazardous accessories — doesn't need to be returned. Do you have one of these sets? Tag a parent who might need to check. 🧸 ### Image Post Caption ⚠️ CPSC Recall: ~2,200 BABESIDE Doll and Stroller toy sets recalled (Amazon, HYBDOLLS, ~$40). Toy pacifier violates small parts ban. CPSC Recall — Small Fish Montessori Busy Board (~1,013 Units)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post 🚨 Another safety recall today — this one for a Montessori-style activity toy. About 1,013 Small Fish Montessori Busy Board toys (model 2512JX02) have been recalled by the CPSC. The board is wooden with six removable, multi-colored activity panels — a flipping mirror, abacus, finger spinner, spinning gear, rain maker, and bead maze. They were sold exclusively on Amazon from March through May 2026 for approximately $16. The CPSC recall number is 26-579. The problem: magnets inside the panels can detach. That matters because ingested high-powered magnets can attract each other through the walls of the digestive system — causing intestinal perforation, bowel blockage, blood poisoning, and death. This isn't a small risk. It's one of the most serious hazard categories CPSC flags. What to do: - Remove the toy from children immediately. - Dispose of the entire busy board. - Email a photo of the disposed product to smallfishrecall@163.com. You'll receive a full refund after submitting the photo. If you're unsure whether your board is the recalled version, look for model number 2512JX02 on the product or packaging. Have you seen or shared this recall today? These can travel fast on Amazon and disappear into toy boxes before parents realize there's a problem. 🧲 ### Image Post Caption 🚨 CPSC Recall #26-579: ~1,013 Small Fish Montessori Busy Board toys recalled (Amazon, ~$16). Magnets can detach — serious ingestion hazard. Dispose of the toy and email a photo to smallfishrecall@163.com for a full refund.

Aspen Acres Fire Update — 93,916 Acres, Partial Re-Entry, Rain Expected Today
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post A few important updates on the Aspen Acres Fire for COS families today. As of Tuesday evening, the fire had burned 93,916 acres — making it the 7th largest wildfire in Colorado history. Containment was at 16%. More than 1,600 firefighters are on the line, with nearly 1,900 expected. At least 263 homes have been destroyed across Custer and Pueblo counties. The news today is mixed but cautiously encouraging on two fronts: The first is re-entry. For some areas around Colorado City in Pueblo County, partial re-entry began on Sunday, July 6. A Disaster Assistance Center opened Monday to issue re-entry cards, and a public meeting was held Monday evening. If your family knows people who evacuated, check with local authorities before assuming re-entry is available — conditions can change. The second is weather. Monsoonal moisture is expected to push in today (Wednesday), which would be the first meaningful rain since the fire ignited June 29. The flip side: forecasters are also warning of potential thunderstorm activity with gusty outflow winds and lightning, which creates flash flood risk in burned areas. For Colorado Springs specifically: air quality was in the Good range as of Tuesday evening (AQI 40), and no Ozone Action Day is in effect for the Front Range through at least 4 PM Wednesday. Today's outdoor plans should be fine — just keep an eye on the weather. How is your family holding up? Our thoughts are with everyone who's been displaced. 🌧️ ### Image Post Caption Aspen Acres Fire update: 93,916 acres burned, 16% contained — now the 7th largest wildfire in Colorado history. Partial re-entry underway near Colorado City. Monsoonal rain expected today. No Ozone Action Day in effect for Colorado Springs.
