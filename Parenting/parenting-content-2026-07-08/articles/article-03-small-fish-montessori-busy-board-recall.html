CPSC Recall: Small Fish Montessori Busy Board — Magnets Can Detach, Posing Serious Injury Risk

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | July 8, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

A wooden Montessori-style activity toy sold on Amazon is being recalled because magnets inside the panels can detach — and swallowed magnets are one of the most serious toy hazards that exist for young children.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 1,013 Small Fish Montessori Busy Board toys (model 2512JX02). The toys were sold exclusively on Amazon from March 2026 through May 2026 at approximately $16. The CPSC recall number is 26-579, announced June 25, 2026.

What the Toy Looks Like

The Small Fish Montessori Busy Board has a wooden base with six multi-colored removable square activity panels. The panels feature different activities:

  • Flipping mirror
  • Abacus
  • Finger spinner
  • Spinning gear
  • Rain maker
  • Bead maze

The model number 2512JX02 can be found on the product or its packaging. If you purchased a Small Fish Busy Board on Amazon between March and May 2026 for around $16, it's very likely the recalled version.

Why Detached Magnets Are a Serious Hazard

Magnet ingestion hazards are treated with particular urgency by the CPSC because the injuries are severe and not always immediately obvious. When two or more high-powered magnets are swallowed, they can attract each other through the walls of the digestive system. The result can include:

  • Intestinal perforation
  • Twisting and blockage of the intestines
  • Blood poisoning (sepsis)
  • Death

Early symptoms of magnet ingestion — nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain — can look like a stomach bug. By the time the severity is clear, significant damage may have already occurred. If you suspect your child has swallowed a magnet, go to the emergency room immediately and tell them the child may have ingested more than one magnet.

What to Do

  1. Remove the busy board from children immediately and stop all use.
  2. Dispose of the entire busy board.
  3. Photograph the disposed product.
  4. Email the photo to smallfishrecall@163.com to receive a full refund.

You do not need to return the product. The photo documentation of disposal is what initiates the refund process.

What's Next

The full CPSC recall notice for this product — Recall #26-579 — is at cpsc.gov. Search for "Small Fish Montessori Busy Board" to find the listing. If you have questions or the refund process doesn't move forward, the CPSC hotline (1-800-638-2772) can help. Share this recall with other parents in your network — at $16, this was an accessible toy that could have been purchased as a gift or picked up without much attention.
