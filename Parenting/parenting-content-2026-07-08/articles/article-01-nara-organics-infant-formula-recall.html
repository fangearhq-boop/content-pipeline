FDA Recalls All Lots of Nara Organics Infant Formula Over Botulism Risk — What to Do Now

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | July 8, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

If you have Nara Organics Infant Formula in your home, stop using it immediately.

The U.S. Food and Drug Administration has recalled all lots of Nara Organics Whole Milk Organic Powdered Infant Formula — both the 700g and 400g cans — due to possible contamination with Clostridium botulinum, the bacteria that causes infant botulism. The recall is linked to a multistate outbreak that has already resulted in 3 to 4 infant hospitalizations across California, Washington, and Pennsylvania. All affected infants were treated with BabyBIG (Botulism Immune Globulin Intravenous). No deaths have been reported.

What Is Infant Botulism

Infant botulism occurs when Clostridium botulinum bacteria grow in an infant's intestinal tract, releasing toxins that can cause muscle weakness, feeding difficulties, and in serious cases, respiratory failure. It is one of the most serious foodborne illnesses for infants, who are particularly vulnerable because of their developing immune systems.

The FDA confirmed that C. botulinum was detected in an opened can of Nara formula from an affected infant's home. Illness onset for the reported cases occurred in April and May 2026. The FDA contacted the company on the evening of June 12, and Nara agreed to recall all lots on June 13, 2026.

Who Is Affected

The recall covers all lots of Nara Organics Whole Milk Organic Powdered Infant Formula distributed nationally from July 2025 through June 2026. The product was sold through three channels:

  • Target stores
  • Target.com
  • Nara.com

This covers all can sizes — both the 700g and 400g formats. The recall applies to all lot numbers. Nara Organics represents less than 1% of all infant formula sold in the United States.

What to Do

If you have Nara Organics formula, follow these steps based on where you purchased it:

Where You Bought It What to Do
Nara.com — order placed May or June 2026 Your order will be automatically refunded. Allow 10–15 days to receive the refund to your original payment method. If your order was in process, it will be canceled and refunded.
Nara.com — order placed before May 2026 Photograph the bottom of each can, then complete the refund form at nara.com/pages/refund-request-form.
Target (700g can) Return the product to any Target store or follow Target's online return process.

Regardless of where the formula was purchased, the most urgent step is to stop using it immediately and remove it from your home. Do not wait for the refund process to begin before doing so.

What's Next

The FDA's full outbreak investigation page is available at fda.gov. Search for "infant botulism powdered infant formula June 2026" to find the current investigation status. Nara's own recall information and refund form are at nara.com/pages/nara-organics-recall-information.

If your baby has consumed Nara Organics formula and is showing symptoms such as constipation, weakness, poor feeding, weak cry, or breathing difficulties, contact your pediatrician or call 911 immediately. Do not wait to see if symptoms resolve on their own.
