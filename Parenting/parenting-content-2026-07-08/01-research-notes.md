## STORY 1: FDA Recall — Nara Organics Infant Formula (All Lots)

**Jurisdiction:** FDA (infant formula = FDA, not CPSC)
**Announced:** June 13, 2026 (FDA contacted Nara evening June 12; company agreed to recall June 13)
**Product:** Nara Organics Whole Milk Organic Powdered Infant Formula — all lots, both can sizes (700g and 400g)
**Units:** All lots recalled; no specific unit count published
**Hazard:** Potential Clostridium botulinum contamination; linked to multistate infant botulism outbreak
**Cases:** 3–4 infants hospitalized; illness onset April–May 2026; California, Washington, Pennsylvania; all treated with BabyBIG (Botulism Immune Globulin Intravenous); no deaths reported
**Lot numbers linked to cases (not exhaustive — all lots recalled):** 709125280E14F2, 709125288E14F2, 708125174E14F2
**Where sold:** Target stores, Target.com, Nara.com; distributed nationally July 2025 through June 2026; Nara represents less than 1% of all infant formula sold in the U.S.

**Refund/return instructions:**
- Nara.com orders placed May or June 2026: Automatically refunded; allow 10–15 days; orders in process canceled and refunded
- Other Nara.com customers with unused product: Photograph the bottom of each can; complete refund form at nara.com/pages/refund-request-form
- Target 700g can purchases: Visit local Target store or follow Target's online return instructions
- ALL consumers: Stop using all Nara formula immediately

**Sources:** FDA.gov, CDC.gov, Nara.com, CBS News, Consumer Reports, Connecticut DCP, Rhode Island Dept. of Health, 6abc Philadelphia
**FDA outbreak page:** https://www.fda.gov/food/outbreaks-foodborne-illness/outbreak-investigation-infant-botulism-powdered-infant-formula-june-2026
**FDA recall page:** https://www.fda.gov/safety/recalls-market-withdrawals-safety-alerts/nara-organics-recalls-all-lots-nara-infant-formula-because-possible-health-risk
**Nara recall page:** https://nara.com/pages/nara-organics-recall-information

## STORY 2: CPSC Recall — BABESIDE Doll and Stroller Set (~2,200 Units)

**CPSC URL:** https://www.cpsc.gov/Recalls/2026/BABESIDE-Doll-and-Stroller-Childrens-Toys-Recalled-Due-to-Risk-of-Serious-Injury-or-Death-from-Choking-Hazard-Violate-Small-Parts-Ban-Sold-on-Amazon-by-HYBDOLLS
**Announced:** June 18, 2026
**Product:** BABESIDE Doll and Stroller Children's Toy Set — pink-and-red stroller, baby doll dressed in pink, 23 accessories (toy pacifier, plush bear, bottles, and additional baby accessories)
**Units:** Approximately 2,200
**Manufacturer:** Huizhou Huitong Crafts Co. Ltd., China; sold on Amazon by HYBDOLLS
**Hazard:** (1) Toy pacifier violates federal small parts ban — small part intended for children under 3, deadly choking hazard; (2) Eyes on plush bear can detach, also posing choking hazard
**Where sold:** Amazon.com only; July 2025 through January 2026; approximately $40
**Injuries:** None reported at time of recall announcement

**Remedy:**
- Stop using the plush bear and pacifier immediately; remove from children
- Contact HYBDOLLS for free replacement accessory items (shipping included)
- To receive replacements: destroy plush bear by cutting it in half; mark an "X" on the pacifier in permanent marker; email photo of destroyed items to support@babeside.com

**Sources:** CPSC.gov, The Cooldown, Yahoo News, PRNewswire, WRNJ Radio

## STORY 3: CPSC Recall — Small Fish Montessori Busy Board (~1,013 Units)

**CPSC URL:** https://www.cpsc.gov/Recalls/2026/Montessori-Busy-Board-Toys-Recalled-Due-to-Risk-of-Serious-Injury-or-Death-from-Magnet-Ingestion-Violate-Mandatory-Standard-for-Toys-Sold-on-Amazon-by-Small-Fish
**Recall number:** 26-579
**Announced:** June 25, 2026
**Product:** Small Fish Montessori Busy Board — wooden base with six multi-colored removable square activity panels (flipping mirror, abacus, finger spinner, spinning gear, rain maker, bead maze); model number 2512JX02
**Units:** Approximately 1,013
**Hazard:** Magnets can detach from the boards, violating the mandatory federal safety standard for toys. Ingested high-powered magnets can attract each other or other metal objects inside the digestive system, causing intestinal perforation, twisting and blockage of the intestines, blood poisoning, and death.
**Where sold:** Amazon.com only; March 2026 through May 2026; approximately $16

**Remedy:**
- Stop using immediately; remove from children
- Dispose of the busy board
- Email a photo of the disposed product to smallfishrecall@163.com to receive a full refund

**Sources:** CPSC.gov, Targeted News Service, NBC Montana recap

## STORY 5: Aspen Acres Fire Update — July 8, 2026

**Fire started:** June 29, 2026
**Size (most recent verified):** 93,916 acres as of approx. 6 PM July 7, 2026 (morning July 7 figure: 93,634 acres)
**Containment:** 15–16% as of July 7 (morning: 15%; evening: 16%)
**Ranking:** 7th largest wildfire in Colorado history as of early July 7
**Personnel:** 1,646 as of July 7; expected to grow to nearly 1,900
**Structures destroyed:** At least 263 homes — 185 in Pueblo County, 78 in Custer County; some sources cite 266 total structures
**Counties:** Custer and Pueblo counties; Level 2 warnings in parts of Fremont County

**Evacuation status:** Mandatory evacuations across parts of Custer and Pueblo counties including Colorado City, Beulah, Rye, San Isabel, Wetmore, Red Creek, and Apache City; mandatory nighttime curfew 9 PM–7 AM for impacted areas

**Re-entry:** Re-entry began for some areas around Colorado City as of July 6; public meeting held evening of July 7; Disaster Assistance Center opened July 7 to issue re-entry cards

**July 8 weather:** Monsoonal moisture expected Wednesday — first meaningful rain chance since fire ignited; thunderstorm risks including gusty outflow winds up to 50 mph and lightning; flash flood risk in severely burned areas

**COS air quality July 7 (most recent):** AQI 40 (Good) at last reading; worst prior 24h: 53 (Moderate) at 12:31 PM July 6; No Ozone Action Day in effect for Front Range through at least 4 PM July 8

**Note on July 8 specific data:** Most recent verified data is July 7 (evening). Check InciWeb (https://inciweb.wildfire.gov/incident-information/copsf-aspen-acres-fire) for latest acreage before publishing.
