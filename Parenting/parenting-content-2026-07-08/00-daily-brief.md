## Today's Stories

### STORY 1: FDA Recall — Nara Organics Infant Formula (All Lots)
- **Tier:** 1
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Byline:** Sarah Morales
- **Summary:** The FDA has recalled all lots of Nara Organics Whole Milk Organic Powdered Infant Formula after linking it to a multistate outbreak of infant botulism. At least 3–4 infants were hospitalized in California, Washington, and Pennsylvania. Product was sold at Target stores and on Nara.com. All caregivers should stop use immediately.
- **Angle:** Urgent action guide — how to identify the product, stop use, and get a refund.

### STORY 2: CPSC Recall — BABESIDE Doll and Stroller Set (~2,200 Units)
- **Tier:** 1
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Byline:** Jamie Rivera
- **Summary:** CPSC has recalled approximately 2,200 BABESIDE Doll and Stroller children's toy sets sold on Amazon by HYBDOLLS. The toy pacifier violates the federal small parts ban (choking hazard for children under 3), and eyes on the included plush bear can also detach. Sold July 2025–January 2026 for approximately $40.
- **Angle:** Recall action guide — how to check if you have one and what to do.

### STORY 3: CPSC Recall — Small Fish Montessori Busy Board (~1,013 Units)
- **Tier:** 1
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Byline:** Sarah Morales
- **Summary:** CPSC Recall #26-579: approximately 1,013 Small Fish Montessori Busy Board toys (wooden activity panels, model 2512JX02) recalled because magnets can detach. Ingested high-powered magnets can cause intestinal perforation, blockage, and death. Sold on Amazon March–May 2026 for approximately $16.
- **Angle:** Recall action guide — how to check, what the magnet hazard means, and how to get a full refund.

### STORY 5: Aspen Acres Fire Update — 93,916 Acres, Partial Re-Entry, Rain Expected Today
- **Tier:** 2
- **Pillar:** Local News
- **Byline:** Sarah Morales
- **Summary:** The Aspen Acres Fire has burned 93,916 acres across Pueblo and Custer counties (7th largest wildfire in Colorado history), with 15–16% containment as of July 7. Partial re-entry began for some areas around Colorado City. Monsoonal moisture is expected Wednesday July 8 — the first meaningful rain chance since the fire ignited. No Ozone Action Day is in effect for the Front Range July 8.
