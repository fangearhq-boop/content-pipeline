Two children's sleepwear recalls are active right now, and Colorado Springs families should check their kids' closets today.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled Bellabu Bear children's robes and Blossom children's loungewear. Both products were found to violate the mandatory flammability standard for children's sleepwear, and both pose a serious risk of burn injury to children who wear them.

What the Recalls Cover

Bellabu Bear is a children's clothing brand known for its robes — the kind of soft, cozy wrap garments many parents put kids in after a bath or around the house in the evening. Blossom's recalled products are described as children's loungewear, another category that parents use for bedtime and around-the-house wear.

Both product types are commonly worn in sleeping or near-sleeping contexts, which is precisely why children's sleepwear is subject to mandatory federal flammability standards. Children who are asleep or drowsy cannot respond quickly to fire emergencies. The federal standard is designed to account for that.

Both recalls are classified as posing a serious risk of injury or death from burns. Visit cpsc.gov/Recalls and search by brand name for the specific model numbers, lot codes, and instructions for receiving a refund or replacement under each recall.

What Parents Should Do Now

Stop using both products immediately if you own them. Do not donate or pass them along — a recalled product remains dangerous regardless of who owns it.

If you're unsure whether your child's other sleepwear meets flammability requirements, there's a simple check: compliant children's sleepwear is either labeled as flame-resistant or is snug-fitting. Loose-fitting children's sleepwear must be flame-resistant to meet the standard. Look for the required label on the garment — it should be there by law.

The CPSC maintains a full list of active recalls at cpsc.gov/Recalls. You can search by brand, product type, or hazard type. It's a habit worth developing — the site is updated regularly, and the infant and children's product categories see new additions more often than many parents realize.

A Note on the Recall Wave

This is not the first round of children's sleepwear recalls this year. The CPSC has been active across the children's product category throughout 2026, with multiple recalls involving infant sleep surfaces, loungewear, and now robes. The pattern reflects increased enforcement of mandatory standards, not an unusual surge in dangerous products — but it does mean parents have more to stay on top of.

If you've been purchasing children's sleepwear through less familiar brands or online marketplaces, these recalls underscore the value of buying from brands whose products have gone through retail channels where compliance checks are more rigorous.

What's Next

Check cpsc.gov/Recalls today for the full details on both the Bellabu Bear and Blossom recalls, including which specific products are affected and how to receive refunds. If you have questions about children's sleepwear safety standards, cpsc.gov also maintains general guidance on what compliant sleepwear looks like. Bookmark the page and check it periodically — it's one of the most practical safety tools available to Colorado Springs families with young children.
