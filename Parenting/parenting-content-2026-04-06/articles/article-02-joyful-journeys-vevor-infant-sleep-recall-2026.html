Two Infant Sleep Product Recalls: Joyful Journeys Baby Lounger and Vevor Baby Swing

Two infant sleep products have been recalled by the Consumer Product Safety Commission, and both come with the agency's most serious classification: serious risk of injury or death.

If you have a Joyful Journeys baby lounger or a Vevor-branded baby swing at home, stop using it now.

Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers

Joyful Journeys has recalled its baby loungers due to entrapment and fall hazards. The product violates the mandatory federal standard for infant sleep products — a standard specifically designed to ensure that surfaces where infants rest meet minimum safety requirements for preventing the kinds of accidents that have caused infant deaths in this product category.

Baby loungers — the soft, padded infant seats designed to hold babies in a supported position — have been subject to increased CPSC scrutiny over the past two years. The concern is that infants placed in loungers can roll, shift position, or become wedged in ways that put pressure on their airway or place them in positions from which they can't recover on their own.

The safest sleep surface for infants remains a firm, flat surface in a crib, bassinet, or play yard that meets current safety standards — with no soft bedding, no positioning devices, and no props.

Vevor Baby Swings (Sanven Technology)

The Vevor baby swing recall, issued against Sanven Technology, involves a dual violation that makes it particularly clear-cut: the swing exceeds the 10-degree maximum incline angle and therefore violates both the mandatory standard for infant sleep products and the inclined sleepers ban.

The inclined sleepers ban is worth understanding. It was established specifically because a documented pattern of infant deaths occurred in inclined sleep products — products where the baby's head is elevated at an angle during sleep. The mechanism is the same as with loungers: infants' necks don't have the muscle control to recover if their head falls forward, which can block the airway. An incline accelerates that risk.

The 10-degree standard is a hard limit that exists for this reason. When a product exceeds it, the CPSC treats that as an immediate stop-use situation — which is exactly what this recall is.

What Parents Should Do

Stop using both products immediately. Do not place your infant in either product, do not lend it to another family, and do not list it for resale. Recalled products remain dangerous regardless of who owns them.

Visit cpsc.gov/Recalls and search for Joyful Journeys and Vevor to find the specific recall details for each product, including model numbers and instructions for refunds or replacements.

This is the latest in a series of infant sleep product recalls in 2026. If you're using any infant product — lounger, swing, bouncer, bassinet — and you're not certain it's current on safety standards, a quick search on cpsc.gov by brand name is a fast way to verify.

What's Next
