April is delivering on spring. After a snow-heavy first week, the rest of the month is shaping up with events worth getting out for. Here's what Colorado Springs families have coming up this week and through the end of the month.

Spring Fling — April 10-11 in Fountain

Spring Fling takes place April 10-11 at Pikes Peak International Raceway in Fountain, just south of Colorado Springs. The event features live music, local vendors, games, and outdoor activity — the kind of gathering that works well for families who want something low-key and community-focused.

Fountain is about a 20-minute drive from central Colorado Springs, and the raceway venue provides plenty of open space. This is a good option if you've been cooped up through the early-April cold snap and want something that gets the kids moving without requiring a full day of planning.

Colorado Springs RV and Travel Show — April 10-11, Free Admission

The Colorado Springs RV and Travel Show runs April 10-11 at the Colorado Springs Event Center at 3960 Palmer Park Blvd. Hours are noon to 7 p.m. Thursday (April 10) and 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. Saturday (April 11). Admission is free.

For families who've been thinking about a camping trip this summer — or even just want to see what the options look like — this is a genuinely useful event to attend. It's one of the larger RV displays in the region under one roof, which means you can compare size, layout, and price points across a lot of products at once without any commitment.

Free admission makes this easy to treat as a casual outing. If your family has ever said "we should do more camping" and hasn't quite gotten around to figuring out the logistics, this is the kind of event that moves the conversation from vague to concrete.

Free Early Literacy Event for Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing Families — April 17

On April 17 from 1:30 to 3 p.m., the Colorado School for the Deaf and the Blind (CSDB) and Bear Creek Nature Center are co-hosting a free early literacy event specifically for families with deaf or hard-of-hearing children, from birth through age 8.

Events like this one don't always get wide visibility outside the community they're designed for, but they're valuable — and free. If you're part of the Colorado Springs deaf or hard-of-hearing community, or if you know a family with a child who's deaf or hard of hearing, please pass this along. Early literacy resources tailored to the specific communication needs of deaf and hard-of-hearing children make a real difference in developmental outcomes.

Looking Further Ahead: Mrs. Doubtfire

If you're a family that enjoys live theater, the internationally acclaimed Mrs. Doubtfire musical is coming to Colorado Springs April 28-30. It's a touring Broadway production based on the beloved film, and it's been well-received as a family-friendly show. Check your local box office for ticketing and venue details.

The Cañon City Blossom Festival also starts April 29 — it's a short drive from Colorado Springs and offers music, a rodeo, and a 5K run across several days through early May.
