Garden of the Gods Is Getting a New Family Experience This Summer — Here's What We Know

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 6, 2026

Garden of the Gods is one of those places that never gets old — 1,340 acres of dramatic red sandstone formations, free to visit, 5 a.m. to 9 p.m. year-round. Colorado Springs families know it well.

This summer, it's getting something new.

The City of Colorado Springs announced a new addition to Garden of the Gods Park, describing it as a "memorable new addition" that offers a water-based, family-friendly experience and a new vantage point of the park's iconic formations. Construction is mobilizing this spring, and the city is targeting an opening before summer 2026.

What We Know About the Project

The city's announcement describes the addition as water-based and family-focused — designed to offer visitors a new way to experience the red rock landscape. It's being tracked on the city parks website as the Garden of the Gods Waterline Project, with updates available at parks.coloradosprings.gov.

Specific details beyond the city's framing aren't fully public yet, and it's worth checking the project page directly as construction gets underway. But the fact that it's being positioned as a summer 2026 opening and described as "memorable" suggests it's designed to become a draw rather than a minor infrastructure improvement.

For families who visit Garden of the Gods regularly, a new feature that provides a different perspective of the formations is genuinely interesting — the park's landscape is striking from many angles, and anything that opens a new way to experience it is worth watching.

Starsmore Visitor Center Upgrades

Separately, good news for families who've visited Starsmore Visitor Center near Cheyenne Mountain: the facility is completing a round of significant accessibility improvements.

The upgrades include expanded accessible parking, improved bus parking for school groups, new ADA-accessible walkways, a new plaza space, and renovated ADA-compliant restrooms. If you've visited with a stroller, with a child who uses a wheelchair, or as part of a school group and found the logistics cumbersome, these changes are aimed directly at your experience.

Starsmore is the entry point for trails into Cheyenne Mountain and the surrounding area. It's a less visited part of Colorado Springs' park system than Garden of the Gods, but it's a beautiful one — and more accessible infrastructure means more families can use it comfortably.

Garden of the Gods: The Basics for New-to-COS Families

If you're newer to Colorado Springs and haven't made it to Garden of the Gods yet, here's the essential information. The park is free. It's open daily from 5 a.m. to 9 p.m. The Visitor Center is open 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. and offers educational exhibits about the geology and ecology of the park at no extra charge.

Parking lots can fill up on warm weekend mornings — plan to arrive before 9 a.m. or after 3 p.m. to avoid the busiest windows. The trail system ranges from a paved loop suitable for strollers to moderate hiking trails, making it accessible for families with a wide range of kids' ages and abilities.
