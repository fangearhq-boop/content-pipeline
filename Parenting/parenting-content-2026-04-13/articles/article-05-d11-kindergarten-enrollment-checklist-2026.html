D11 Kindergarten Enrollment Checklist for Fall 2026 — Everything You Need to Do This Spring

By Keisha Williams | April 13, 2026

If your child turns 5 by October 1, 2026, spring is the time to act. Here's the full checklist for D11 kindergarten enrollment — including a note for Westside families navigating this year's school changes.

Kindergarten roundup season is underway in Colorado Springs, and for families with children turning 5 this year, April is the moment to get organized.

Colorado Springs District 11 serves roughly 25 elementary schools across the central, east, and westside parts of the city. If you're new to D11 — or just new to the kindergarten process — this guide walks through everything you need to do before August.

First: Confirm Eligibility

In Colorado, children must turn 5 years old on or before October 1, 2026 to be eligible for fall 2026 kindergarten. That means your child's birthday must fall between October 2, 2020 and October 1, 2021 (inclusive). Children who turn 5 after October 1, 2026 will be eligible for the 2027-28 school year instead.

If your child's birthday falls close to the October 1 cutoff, it's worth contacting D11 directly to discuss options — some families in that situation request early or delayed entry, which involves an assessment process.

Documents You'll Need

D11 enrollment requires three core documents. Gathering these before the enrollment window opens saves time and avoids scrambles:

Birth certificate: A certified copy — not a photocopy of a photocopy. Hospital-issued keepsake certificates are typically not accepted; use a vital records-issued certificate.

Immunization records: Colorado law requires children entering kindergarten to have received specific vaccines. Your child's doctor's office can provide a current immunization record. If your family qualifies for or intends to request an exemption, a Colorado Certificate of Immunization Exemption is required instead — contact Tri-County Health or your school nurse for details.

Proof of address: A current utility bill (gas, electric, water), a lease or mortgage statement, or other official mail showing your name and current COS address. The document must correspond to where your family actually lives — D11 school assignments are based on physical address.

Find Your Neighborhood School

Use the school boundary tool at d11.org to enter your home address and find your assigned D11 elementary school. Boundaries matter — your neighborhood school is determined by where you live, and enrollment at a school outside your boundary requires a transfer request.

Families in the Westside and central areas of Colorado Springs should verify their school assignment this spring. D11 announced earlier this month that Orton Academy, a K-8 charter school specializing in dyslexia education, will co-locate at Trailblazer Elementary beginning August 2026. The two schools will operate independently, but families with questions about the arrangement should contact D11 or Trailblazer directly before enrollment is complete.

Complete Enrollment Online

D11 uses an online enrollment system. Log in or create an account at d11.org to access the enrollment portal, complete the required forms, and upload your documents. Enrollment windows for fall kindergarten typically open in spring — check d11.org for the exact dates for the 2026-27 school year, as windows can vary.

Some D11 schools also host kindergarten roundup events — in-person visits where your child gets to see their future classroom, meet teachers, and do some activities. These events are valuable for kids who need some preview time before August. Watch your school's communication channels or contact the front office to ask about roundup dates at your neighborhood school.

What's Next

Once enrolled, D11 will send families information about school supply lists, kindergarten orientation events, and fall schedule details over the summer. If you have questions that the district website doesn't answer, the D11 enrollment team can be reached directly through d11.org. First-time kindergarten parents often find the process less daunting once they've made that initial contact. Start with your documents, find your school, and the rest follows from there.
