COS Spring Activity Guide: Bear Creek Early Literacy, Bird Banding, and Fountain Creek Coming Up This Week

By Marta Kim | April 13, 2026

Three outdoor programs at Colorado Springs nature centers are coming up this week and next — including a special early literacy event for families with deaf and hard-of-hearing children.

Spring in Colorado Springs is short, gorgeous, and worth taking advantage of. Temperatures are hitting highs around 59°F this week, with 9 to 11 hours of sunshine daily and the kind of clear air that makes Pikes Peak look like it's right there. The city's parks and nature centers know this, and they've got a solid lineup of outdoor programming for families over the next two weeks.

Here's what's coming up and who it's designed for.

April 17 — Early Literacy Event at Bear Creek Nature Center (Families with Deaf/Hard-of-Hearing Children)

On Friday, April 17 from 1:30 to 3:00 PM, Bear Creek Nature Center is hosting an Early Literacy Event specifically designed for families with deaf and hard-of-hearing children, from birth through 8 years old.

The program includes reading in American Sign Language, learning about local wildlife, and a guided nature walk. It's an inclusive outdoor education event that doesn't require families to have any prior naturalist experience — just curiosity and appropriate weather-ready gear for kids.

Bear Creek Regional Park is located in southwest Colorado Springs at 245 Bear Creek Road. The nature center offers free and low-cost programming throughout the year, and events like this one fill quickly when word gets out. If this applies to your family or a family you know, pass it along.

April 18 — Bird Banding at Bear Creek Nature Center (Ages 8 and Up)

The day after the literacy event, Bear Creek Nature Center is hosting a bird banding session on Saturday, April 18. No birding experience is required, and the activity is recommended for ages 8 and up.

Bird banding is exactly what it sounds like: trained naturalists catch wild birds (typically using mist nets), attach a small numbered band to a leg for tracking purposes, take measurements, and release the bird. Participants get a close-up look at birds they'd usually only see through binoculars and learn about migration patterns, bird conservation, and the science behind wildlife population monitoring.

Spring is bird migration season in Colorado, making mid-April an ideal time to see species passing through on their way north. For kids who have an interest in wildlife and nature science, this is a hands-on experience that doesn't happen in a classroom.

April 24 — Habitat Exploration at Fountain Creek Regional Park (All Ages)

On Friday, April 24 from 2:00 to 4:00 PM, Fountain Creek Regional Park is hosting a habitat exploration session where families can discover how five distinct habitats are shaped by water. The event is open to all ages.

Fountain Creek Regional Park is located south of Colorado Springs near the confluence of Fountain Creek and Monument Creek. The park's riparian habitats — cottonwood groves, wetlands, grasslands, and scrubby upland areas — change dramatically depending on how close you are to the water. An event like this gives kids a structured way to understand why ecosystems look and work the way they do.

This is a good option for families with younger children who aren't quite ready for the more intensive bird banding event but want outdoor educational programming with a naturalist guiding the experience.

What's Next

Colorado Springs parks programming continues through spring and picks up in intensity as summer approaches. North Cheyenne Cañon Park also has wildflower hiking starting this month — spring blooms in the canyon are one of the best free family experiences COS has to offer. Check the City's parks and recreation calendar at coloradosprings.gov/parks for the full list of upcoming programs. Most events at city nature centers are free or low-cost, and registration (where required) tends to fill up fast — so signing up early is always a good idea.
