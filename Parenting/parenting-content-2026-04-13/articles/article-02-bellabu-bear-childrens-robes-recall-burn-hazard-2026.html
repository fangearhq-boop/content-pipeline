CPSC Recall: Bellabu Bear Children's Sherpa Robes Fail Flammability Standards — Full Refund Available

By Keisha Williams | April 13, 2026

The robes come in popular character prints like PAW Patrol, Minecraft, and Minions. If your child has one, stop using it now and claim your full refund.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has announced a recall of Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa children's robes due to a burn hazard. The robes violate mandatory federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear — meaning they're at higher risk of catching fire or burning quickly if exposed to a flame or spark than the law allows.

No incidents or injuries have been reported. The recall is happening now so parents can act before anything goes wrong. If you have one of these robes, stop using it immediately, remove it from your child's reach, and follow the steps below to get your full refund.

What to Look For

The recalled robes are Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa children's robes. They have sherpa lining, two front pockets, and a matching belt. The neck label reads "Bellabu Bear," "BB-NW12109," and the size.

The robes were sold in children's sizes S/M and M/L. The recalled prints include: PAW Patrol, Garfield, Minecraft, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora, Nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons.

These are popular, recognizable character prints — the kind that tend to be go-to gift choices. If a grandparent, family friend, or relative bought your child a robe with one of these characters, it's worth checking the label.

Where They Were Sold

The robes were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue stores and boutiques nationwide and online. Online retailers included Bellabubear.com, Letthembelittleboutique.com, and Saksfifthavenue.com. They were on sale from January 2024 through July 2025 at approximately $60 each.

How to Get Your Refund

Bellabu Bear is offering a full refund or store credit for the original purchase price, plus a prepaid shipping label to return the recalled robe. You don't need to keep using the product while you wait — set it aside and start the return process.

Contact Bellabu Bear by phone at 888-703-7752, Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 4 PM Pacific Time. You can also email help@bellabubear.com or visit bellabubear.com and click "Recall" at the bottom of the page.

Why Children's Sleepwear Standards Exist

Federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear have been in place since the 1970s. They exist because children are particularly vulnerable in home fires — they may not be able to escape quickly, and sleepwear worn near open flames (birthday candles, fireplaces, camp stoves) creates real risk. Products that fail these standards are required to be recalled and removed from circulation regardless of whether an injury has occurred.

Compliance means the fabric either self-extinguishes quickly when exposed to flame, or the garment is made tight-fitting (which reduces the amount of fabric that can catch). Loose-fitting sherpa-style robes that fail these tests are a specific category of concern.
