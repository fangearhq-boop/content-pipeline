D49 Opens 2 New Early Childhood Education Classrooms This Fall — Plus, 9 Campuses Earn Purple Star Recognition

By Sofia Reyes | April 13, 2026

District 49 is expanding pre-K access with two new classrooms at the Student Success Center, and nine campuses have earned the state's highest honor for supporting military-connected families.

If you're a D49 family thinking about early childhood education for the 2026-27 school year, there's news worth knowing: District 49 is opening two new Early Childhood Education classrooms at the D49 Student Success Center in August 2026.

The Student Success Center itself is a new facility coming online at the start of the 2026-27 school year, and these two ECE classrooms will be part of that opening. For families in D49's service area — which covers Falcon, Calhan, and parts of the eastern Colorado Springs corridor — more pre-K seats means more options for families who want to get their youngest learners started in a structured, supportive environment before kindergarten.

Separately, the district earned recognition that matters particularly to a significant portion of its community: nine D49 campuses have been designated Purple Star Schools by the Colorado Department of Education.

What the Purple Star Designation Means

Colorado Springs is home to Fort Carson Army Post, Schriever Space Force Base, and Peterson Space Force Base. That means a substantial portion of D49 families are military-connected — parents who may be on deployment, families who have relocated multiple times, and kids navigating the particular challenges of being a military child in the school system.

The Purple Star School designation is the Colorado Department of Education's formal recognition for schools that go above and beyond to support these families. Schools earning the designation have demonstrated specific practices: staff training in military family issues, transition support for incoming and departing military students, and a school culture that actively welcomes and includes military-connected children.

Nine D49 campuses — seven district-operated schools and two charter schools — now carry that designation. For military parents evaluating schools in D49, it's a meaningful signal that goes beyond general reputation.

Early Childhood Education in D49

The two new ECE classrooms at the Student Success Center address a practical need: early childhood programming in D49's eastern corridor has historically had limited capacity. Pre-K access is foundational — research consistently shows that children who enter kindergarten with structured pre-K experience arrive better prepared for early literacy and numeracy benchmarks.

Families interested in the new ECE classrooms should check D49's enrollment calendar at d49.org for registration windows and specific program details. ECE programs in Colorado typically have enrollment processes that open well before the school year — waiting until August to start the process can mean missing available seats.
