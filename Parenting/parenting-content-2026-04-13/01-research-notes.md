### STORY 4: AAP Screen Time Guidelines Update 2026

**Sources:**
- aap.org/en/patient-care/media-and-children/ (PRIMARY — official AAP)
- edsurge.com/news/2026-02-05-new-aap-screen-time-recommendations-focus-less-on-screens-more-on-family-time (PRIMARY — February 2026 news article)
- health.choc.org/updated-aap-recommendations-for-screen-time/ (SECONDARY — CHOC health summary)
- kidsai.app/blog/new-screen-time-guidelines-2026-what-parents-need-to-know (SECONDARY)

**Verified Facts (HIGH confidence — 2+ sources):**
- AAP updated screen time guidance in January/early 2026 [EdSurge Feb 2026 + CHOC + kidsai.app]
- Shift from strict hour limits to quality, context, and conversation framework [AAP official + EdSurge + CHOC]
- Previous guideline (2016): limit to 2 hours/day for ages 2+ [multiple sources]
- New framework asks 3 questions: (1) Is content high quality and age-appropriate? (2) Is it interactive or passive? (3) Is it replacing sleep, movement, or real-world connection? [AAP + EdSurge + CHOC]
- No set screen time limit for ages 2–5 under new guidance (replaces 1-hour-daily recommendation) [AAP + CHOC]
- Still: no screens before 18 months except video chat [AAP + CHOC]
- Family media plans still recommended over arbitrary limits [AAP official]
- Device-free bedrooms and mealtimes recommended [AAP + CHOC]
- AAP Advocacy Conference: April 12-14, 2026, Arlington, VA [aap.org conference listing]

**Verified Facts (MEDIUM confidence — single source):**
- "Rules focusing on balance, content, co-viewing, and communication are associated with better well-being outcomes than rules focused on screen time" [AAP policy per CHOC summary]
