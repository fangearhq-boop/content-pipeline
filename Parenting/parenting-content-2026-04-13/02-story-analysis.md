**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
**Status:** NEW STORY

**Why This Matters:**
Children's sleepwear flammability standards exist because house fires kill children. A $60 robe sold at Saks Fifth Avenue with popular licensed character prints (PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Minions) is exactly the kind of product that ends up in kids' closets and gets used routinely. The CPSC has issued a formal recall with a full refund available — this is actionable information that parents need immediately. COS families who shopped at Saks or boutique retailers online between January 2024 and July 2025 may have this product.

**Key Angles:**
1. Popular character prints mean these robes are actively in use in many homes
2. No incidents reported yet — parents can act before any harm occurs
3. Full refund available + prepaid return label makes the remedy easy
4. Sold at a premium retailer ($60) — this isn't a dollar-store import

**Best Social Hook:** "CPSC recall: Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa kids' robes (PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Minions and more prints) fail flammability standards. Sold at Saks Fifth Avenue and online Jan 2024–July 2025, about $60. Full refund available."

### STORY 4: AAP Screen Time Shift — Quality Over Quantity

**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Status:** NEW STORY

**Why This Matters:**
The two-hours-per-day rule was the parenting mantra for a decade. The AAP changed the framework in early 2026, and most parents haven't fully internalized what that means in practice. The new approach — asking three questions about quality, interactivity, and what screens are replacing — is actually more useful for parents navigating tablets, streaming, and education apps with their kids. The AAP Advocacy Conference just wrapped April 12-14, making this timely.

**Key Angles:**
1. The "clock" approach is gone — replaced by three concrete questions
2. No screens before 18 months still applies (video chat excepted)
3. Device-free bedrooms and mealtimes still recommended
4. "Co-viewing" and conversation matter more than total minutes
5. Rules about balance/context outperform rules about time limits

**Best Social Hook:** "The AAP changed their screen time guidance in 2026. It's no longer about counting hours. It's about 3 questions: Is it quality content? Is it interactive? Is it replacing something better? Here's what that means for COS families."

**Target Audience:** Parents of toddlers through tweens wrestling with screen time decisions.
