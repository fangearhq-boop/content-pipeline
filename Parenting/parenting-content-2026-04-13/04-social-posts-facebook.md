**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

Heads up for parents who bought Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa robes for their kids: there's a CPSC recall you need to know about.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has announced that Bellabu Bear's bamboo sherpa children's robes violate mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear, creating a burn hazard. No incidents or injuries have been reported — but the recall is happening now so we can act before anything goes wrong.

Here's what to look for: the robes come in children's sizes S/M and M/L, have sherpa lining, two front pockets, and a matching belt. The neck label reads "Bellabu Bear," "BB-NW12109," and the size. The prints include popular characters like PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora, and Nutcracker, along with holiday and novelty designs (hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, dragons).

These robes were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue stores and online at Bellabubear.com, Letthembelittleboutique.com, and Saksfifthavenue.com between January 2024 and July 2025 for about $60.

If you have one: stop using it immediately, remove it from your child's access, and contact Bellabu Bear for a full refund or store credit plus a prepaid return shipping label. You can reach them at 888-703-7752 (9 AM–4 PM PT, Monday–Friday) or at help@bellabubear.com.

Safety first — please share this with anyone who might have one of these.

### STORY 4: AAP Screen Time Shift — Quality Over Quantity

**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM MT)**

The two-hours-per-day rule has been a fixture of parenting advice for the better part of a decade. In January 2026, the American Academy of Pediatrics officially updated their guidance — and the shift is worth understanding.

The new framework isn't about counting minutes. It's about asking three questions when your kids are on screens:

**1. Is the content high quality and age-appropriate?**
Higher-quality experiences model social-emotional skills, have clear learning goals, and allow space for creativity. Lower-quality passive content (endless autoplay, etc.) is the concern.

**2. Is it interactive or passive?**
Interactive content — where kids respond, problem-solve, or extend learning offline — is associated with better outcomes than passive watching. Co-viewing and talking about what they're watching matters.

**3. Is it replacing something more valuable?**
Screens that cut into sleep, physical activity, family mealtime, or free play are the real red flags. Device-free bedrooms and device-free mealtimes still make sense.

The AAP still recommends no screens before 18 months (video chat is the exception). For ages 2–5, they moved away from the previous 1-hour-per-day limit and toward this quality-focused framework.

What this means practically: a 40-minute episode of a well-made nature show watched together and talked about afterward is different from 40 minutes of algorithmically-served shorts. The clock matters less than the context.

### STORY 5: D11 Kindergarten Roundup Season

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM MT)**

Spring means kindergarten roundup season in Colorado Springs, and if you have a child who will turn 5 by October 1, 2026, now is the time to get organized.

Here's your checklist for D11 kindergarten enrollment:

✅ **Confirm your child is eligible.** In Colorado, children must turn 5 on or before October 1, 2026 to enroll in fall 2026 kindergarten.

✅ **Gather your documents.** You'll need: your child's birth certificate, up-to-date immunization records (or a Colorado Certificate of Immunization Exemption), and proof of your current address (utility bill, lease, or similar).

✅ **Find your neighborhood school.** Use the school boundary tool at d11.org to confirm which D11 school serves your address. Families in the Westside and west-central areas should double-check school assignments given the upcoming changes at Trailblazer Elementary (Orton Academy co-location starting fall 2026).

✅ **Complete enrollment.** D11 uses an online enrollment system — check d11.org for the enrollment window and portal link. Some schools also hold in-person kindergarten roundup events where kids can visit their new classroom before the year starts.

✅ **Ask questions early.** If you have questions about specialty programs (dual language, gifted/talented, Montessori), call the school directly or contact D11's enrollment team before the window closes.
