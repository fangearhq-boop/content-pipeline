Target Recalls About 211,000 Cat & Jack Toddler Sandals Over Choking Hazard

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | July 23, 2026

Target is recalling approximately 211,000 pairs of Cat & Jack Toddler Girls' Sequerah Sandals after the decorative plastic pearls on the shoes were found to detach from the sandal body. Detached plastic beads are a choking hazard for young children, and the recall — announced July 16, 2026, by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission — covers all units of this specific style sold at Target.

If you bought these sandals for a toddler between January and May 2026, stop using them and return them to any Target store for a full refund.

What's Being Recalled

The recalled product is the Cat & Jack Toddler Girls' Sequerah Sandal, a tan raffia sandal with decorative plastic pearls attached along the straps. The sandals were sold exclusively at Target stores and on Target.com from January through May 2026 at a retail price of approximately $20.

Cat & Jack is Target's in-house children's clothing and accessories brand. The Sequerah Sandal was part of the brand's 2026 spring and early summer line.

What the Hazard Is

Target received 23 reports of the plastic pearls coming loose from the sandals before the recall was announced. No injuries have been reported in connection with the detaching pearls, but small plastic beads of this size present a clear choking risk for toddlers — the age group these sandals were designed for.

Choking hazards in children's products are among the most common categories of CPSC recalls, particularly for items intended for children under five. In this case, the pearls are decorative elements attached to the sandal, and their detachment during normal wear is the mechanism of concern.

What Parents Should Do

Stop using the sandals immediately and keep them away from your child. Target is handling the remedy as a full refund — no repair part, no exchange for a different item. Bring the sandals to any Target store for a full refund. No receipt is required based on CPSC recall guidance, though stores may ask for the product itself to process the return.

Finding the Sandals

If you're not sure whether your child's sandals are the recalled style, look for the following: tan raffia-textured material, plastic pearls along the straps, and the Cat & Jack label (usually on the insole or a small tag on the strap). The Sequerah name may also appear on the shoe's original packaging if you still have the box.

The sandals were sold in toddler sizes, so they would have been purchased in the infant-to-toddler footwear section at Target or in the same category online.

Context: Small-Parts Recalls in Children's Footwear

This type of recall — decorative elements detaching from children's shoes — is not unusual. Small decorative accents like pearls, gems, or charms on children's sandals are a recurring category in CPSC footwear recalls because they are often attached with adhesives or small fasteners that can loosen with regular wear, washing, or rough handling. Parents of toddlers who tend to pull at clothing details should be especially attentive to similar accessories on other shoes, bags, or clothing items.

You can search all current and recent recalls at cpsc.gov/recalls for any other products you may want to check.

What's Next

Bring the recalled sandals to any Target store for a full refund. Target's customer service line (1-800-440-0680) can provide additional guidance if you have questions about the return process. The official CPSC recall notice is available at cpsc.gov with the full product description and recall contact information.
