Things to Do with Kids in Colorado Springs This Weekend (July 24–26)

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | July 23, 2026

This weekend brings a strong lineup for Colorado Springs families, headlined by the 88th annual Bronc Day Festival on Saturday and the Sunday Market at Acacia Park on Sunday — both free. Here's a look at the best picks for July 24–26, organized by day.

For the full list of things to do with kids in Colorado Springs this weekend, including times and links, head to Springs Daily. You'll also find a curated roundup of free things to do this weekend and a broader look at everything happening around the Pikes Peak region this weekend.

Friday, July 24

Music in the Park at Piñon Valley — Free

Piñon Valley Park (5585 Mule Deer Dr.) hosts a free outdoor concert Friday evening as part of the city's Park and Recreation Month programming. The event runs 6:30 to 8:30 PM and includes live music alongside youth activities: kickball, basketball, food trucks, and giveaways. It's part of the city's 1000 Gatherings Initiative, designed to bring neighborhoods together through outdoor programming. Free, all ages.

Saturday, July 25

Bronc Day Festival — Free — Green Mountain Falls

The anchor pick this weekend is the 88th annual Bronc Day Festival in Green Mountain Falls, about 20 minutes west of Colorado Springs on US-24. The festival runs 7:30 AM to 3 PM and is free to attend.

The morning starts with a pancake breakfast at Church in the Wildwood (10585 Ute Pass Ave.) from 7:30 to 10 AM — a community tradition. At 10 AM, the parade steps off on Ute Pass Avenue, with this year's Grand Marshal being retired Colorado Springs Fire Chief Steve Murphy. The theme for the 88th annual event is "Let Freedom Ring," honoring both the United States' 250th anniversary and Colorado's 150th.

After the parade, the action shifts to Gazebo Lake Park (100 Lake St.) for family activities, live music, vendor booths, and the annual pie contest through 3 PM. This event is appropriate for all ages, including young kids who tend to do well with the open outdoor setting and multiple activity zones.

Guided Hike at Garden of the Gods — Free

Garden of the Gods is open sunrise to sunset, always free, and this Saturday features a guided Siamese Twins Trail hike starting at 9 AM from the South Spring Canyon picnic area. The guided walk covers about 1.5 miles with a ranger-led look at the park's rock formations. Check gardenofgods.com/events/calendar/ for current registration details before heading out.

Sunday, July 26

Sunday Market at Acacia Park — Free

The Sunday Market runs weekly at Acacia Park in downtown Colorado Springs, and this Sunday's market goes from 9 AM to 2 PM. It's an easy, walkable morning with fresh produce, artisan food, handmade goods, and live music in the park. Free to attend, stroller-friendly, and well-suited for all ages. Acacia Park is at Nevada Ave. and Bijou St. in downtown Colorado Springs.

Open All Weekend

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo — Advance Tickets Required

CMZoo is open daily 9 AM to 5 PM (last admission 4 PM). This summer the zoo is celebrating its 100th anniversary, making it a milestone visit for families who haven't been in a while. Advance timed tickets are required for both members and non-members — check cmzoo.org for 2026 pricing and availability. The zoo is at 4250 Cheyenne Mountain Zoo Rd. Recommended for ages 2 and up.

Garden of the Gods — Free

Beyond the Saturday guided hike, Garden of the Gods is open to everyone all weekend at no cost. The park at 1805 N. 30th St. has paved walking paths, unpaved hiking trails, and a visitor center with exhibits. It's one of the most visited parks in Colorado and works well for any age group that can handle a short walk or ride in a stroller on the main paths.
