TOMY Recalls 116,908 Boon PIVOT Toddler Towers Over Tip-Over and Fall Hazards

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | July 23, 2026

If you have a Boon PIVOT Collapsible Toddler Tower Kitchen Step Stool in your home, the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission wants you to stop using it immediately. TOMY announced a voluntary recall of 116,908 units on July 16, 2026, after receiving reports that the towers can tip over while a child is standing on them — a fall risk at counter height.

What's Being Recalled

The recalled product is the Boon PIVOT Collapsible Toddler Tower Kitchen Step Stool, sold in gray and white. The towers measure approximately 22 inches wide, 23 inches deep, and 35 inches tall. They were designed to fold flat for storage and to allow toddlers to stand safely at kitchen counter height alongside a parent while cooking or doing other counter-height activities.

The recall covers units sold at Target, Amazon, and Babylist from January 2023 through June 2026 at a retail price of approximately $150.

What the Hazard Is

The CPSC received 11 reports of the Boon PIVOT tower becoming unstable, shifting, or leaning while in use. No injuries have been reported in connection with these incidents, but the CPSC and TOMY have determined the tip-over risk is significant enough to pull the product from use.

The concern is straightforward: a toddler standing on a tower that shifts or tips at counter height faces a serious fall. Kitchen towers are most often used with kids between roughly 18 months and 5 years old, a range where a fall from that height can cause meaningful injury.

What Parents Should Do

Stop using the tower immediately and store it somewhere your children cannot access it. TOMY is not requiring a return or refund — instead, the company is providing a free stabilizing repair part to owners who register.

To get the free repair part, register your tower at recall.tomy.com/pivot. You'll need to provide proof of purchase or the product information from the unit itself.

Context: Kitchen Towers and Toddler Safety

Toddler towers — sometimes called kitchen towers or learning towers — have grown in popularity over the past decade as a way to include young kids in kitchen tasks while keeping them at a safe, contained height. The category has also seen several safety recalls and consumer complaints over the years, as the design involves a toddler standing elevated on a platform that must remain stable under shifting weight.

If you own a different brand of toddler tower, it is worth checking the CPSC recall database at cpsc.gov/recalls to confirm whether your model has any open recall notices.

What's Next

The most important step is to stop using the Boon PIVOT tower now and register at recall.tomy.com/pivot to receive the free stabilizing repair part. TOMY will mail the part to you; the tower should not be returned to a store.

If you purchased the tower at Target or through Amazon, you can also reach out to TOMY directly through the recall registration page for any questions about the remedy process. The CPSC recall page is available at cpsc.gov for the official product notice and contact information.
