Bronc Day Festival Returns Saturday to Green Mountain Falls for Its 88th Year

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | July 23, 2026

Green Mountain Falls has hosted Bronc Day every summer since 1939, and this Saturday it happens again for the 88th time. The 2026 festival runs July 25 from 7:30 AM to 3 PM, with free admission, a full schedule of family-friendly programming, and a theme that ties into two major anniversaries: the United States at 250 and Colorado at 150.

For Colorado Springs families, Bronc Day is one of the more rewarding summer day trips available. The town of Green Mountain Falls sits about 20 minutes west of Colorado Springs on US-24, and the festival draws from across the Pikes Peak region every year.

Morning: Pancake Breakfast and the Parade

The festival opens with a pancake breakfast at Church in the Wildwood, 10585 Ute Pass Ave., from 7:30 to 10 AM. The breakfast is a community tradition that tends to draw an early crowd, and arriving closer to 7:30 AM means shorter lines and a more relaxed start to the morning before the streets fill up for the parade.

At 10 AM, the parade steps off on Ute Pass Avenue. This year's Grand Marshal is retired Colorado Springs Fire Chief Steve Murphy, whose selection was announced by the Colorado Springs Gazette in July. The parade route runs through the heart of the small mountain town, with the kind of small-town feel that works particularly well for kids who've never seen a small-town Fourth of July-style parade.

Afternoon: Gazebo Lake Park

After the parade, the festival continues at Gazebo Lake Park, 100 Lake St., through 3 PM. The afternoon program at the park includes family activities, live music, vendor booths, and the annual pie contest — a Bronc Day staple that draws both serious bakers and recreational eaters.

The park setting on the lake adds to the atmosphere. Green Mountain Falls is a small town with a walkable, low-key layout that tends to be manageable with strollers and young kids, especially in the park area around the gazebo and lake.

Theme and History

The theme for the 88th annual festival is "Let Freedom Ring," chosen to honor the dual anniversaries of the United States' 250th birthday and Colorado's 150th year of statehood — both of which fall in 2026. Bronc Day itself has its own deep local roots, having run continuously in Green Mountain Falls since 1939 with only a small number of interruptions over nearly nine decades.

The festival's name comes from the area's history as a place where bronco horses were raised and traded in the early twentieth century. That history is baked into the event's identity, and the Grand Marshal tradition — recognizing a community figure each year — has been a consistent thread through the festival's run.

Getting There

From Colorado Springs, take US-24 west toward Woodland Park. Green Mountain Falls is before Woodland Park — roughly 20 minutes from central Colorado Springs in normal traffic. Parking in Green Mountain Falls on festival day is limited; arriving before 9 AM will give you a better shot at street parking near the event. The town is walkable once you're parked, so getting a spot a few blocks away is fine.

The festival is the town's biggest event of the year, which means US-24 can back up mid-morning. If the parade start time matters to you, plan to arrive by 9:30 AM at the latest.

What's Next

Bronc Day information and official updates are posted at broncdayfestival.org and on the festival's Facebook page. Visit Colorado Springs also maintains an events listing at visitcos.com/events/bronc-day-festival/ that carries confirmed details each year.

If your family makes the trip out this Saturday, the morning window — breakfast through the parade — tends to be the most manageable stretch for families with younger kids before the afternoon gets warmer and more crowded.
