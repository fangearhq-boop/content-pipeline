Cheyenne Mountain Zoo Marks Its 100th Year — A Centennial Summer Worth Visiting

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | July 23, 2026

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo opened in 1926, which means this summer the zoo is celebrating its 100th anniversary. For Colorado Springs families who visit regularly, it's a milestone that's easy to take for granted — but it's worth stopping to note what a genuine outlier this place is.

CMZoo is the only accredited mountain zoo in the United States. It sits at 6,800 feet of elevation, perched into the hillside of Cheyenne Mountain, and its layout means many of the animal habitats have sweeping views of the Colorado Springs valley below. There's nothing else like it in the country, and the centennial year has the zoo leaning into that history in a few tangible ways.

What's Happening This Centennial Summer

The zoo has been running centennial programming throughout 2026. This summer, visitors can take in historic storytelling exhibits that trace the zoo's development from its founding through its current state as a home to more than 750 animals representing over 170 species. For those 18 and older, the zoo is running a Historic Photo Re-Creation Contest — a hands-on way to engage with the zoo's archive of centennial images and connect your own family's history with the institution's.

For families coming specifically for the kids, the centennial context is a good framing for a visit: the zoo your grandparents may have visited as children in Colorado Springs is the same zoo, on the same mountain, with a century of accumulated expertise behind it.

Planning Your Visit

CMZoo is open 365 days a year, 9 AM to 5 PM, with last admission at 4 PM. Advance timed tickets are required for both members and non-members. Check cmzoo.org for current 2026 pricing and ticket availability before you go — the zoo uses a pricing calendar that varies by day, and popular summer dates can fill up.

The zoo is located at 4250 Cheyenne Mountain Zoo Rd., Colorado Springs, CO 80906. The drive up Cheyenne Mountain Zoo Road is part of the experience — a winding mountain road with views that open up as you climb. Parking is on-site.

The zoo recommends ages 2 and up for the general visit experience, though younger children are welcome. The terrain includes significant elevation change between exhibits, so a stroller with good wheels or a carrier for smaller kids is worth planning ahead.

Why the Mountain Setting Matters

Most major zoos in the United States are built on flat urban or suburban land, which shapes how the space is organized and what kinds of habitats can be built. CMZoo's hillside footprint means the animals live in terrain that genuinely resembles natural mountain environments in some cases — the giraffe habitat, for example, is surrounded by Colorado's characteristic pine and rock landscape rather than flat manicured grass. The views from the higher areas of the zoo extend out over the city.

The elevation also means summer mornings at CMZoo run cooler than the valley floor. Going earlier in the day — right at the 9 AM open — tends to be better for both animal activity and visitor comfort.

Looking Ahead: Events This Summer

The zoo has a Tails, Tunes & Tastes adult evening event scheduled for July 30 (21 and older only), which is not a family event but may be of interest to parents looking for a date-night option in the centennial year.

For family-oriented programming, check the zoo's full events calendar at cmzoo.org. Summer weekends often have keeper talks, enrichment demonstrations, and other scheduled activities layered on top of the regular animal exhibits.

What's Next

Tickets and full event listings are at cmzoo.org. The zoo books up on busy summer weekends, so if you're planning a visit in late July or August, checking availability early in the week gives you more options. Members have the same advance ticket requirement as general visitors this year.

The centennial year programming runs through the end of 2026. If you've been telling yourself you'll get back to CMZoo "one of these summers," this is the one with a built-in reason to make it happen.
