### STORY 1: CPSC Warning — Fasando Baby Lounger Linked to Infant Death; Manufacturer Refuses Recall
**Tier:** 1 (Breaking/Trending)
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety Alert)
The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent warning about Fasando Baby Loungers after an infant death was reported. Unlike a formal recall, the CPSC issued a Notice of Violation because the manufacturer — a China-based company — has refused to initiate a recall or offer any remedy. The lounger's sides are too low and foot openings too wide, creating entrapment and fall hazards. About 6,200 units were sold on Amazon for approximately $35 between August 2024 and July 2025. Parents should stop using immediately, remove foam padding, cut the cover in half, and dispose of all pieces.

### STORY 2: Two More Children's Products Recalled — Target Gigglescape Toy and GOPO Teething Toy
**Tier:** 1 (Breaking/Trending)
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety/Recall)
Two new CPSC recalls affect common children's products. Target's Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy (about 49,000 units sold at Target for approximately $10, August 2025–January 2026) has a dome that detaches, releasing small plastic balls — one child began choking. Separately, GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toys (about 70,410 units, sold on Amazon for $11–$15, August 2023–March 2026) have pull strings that violate mandatory length standards — three incidents of choking or respiratory distress were reported.

### STORY 4: Nearly 8 in 10 Parents Say Summer Wrecks Their Child's Sleep — New Survey
**Tier:** 2 (Today's News)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
A June 2026 survey of parents of children ages 0–4 found 78% say summer negatively disrupts their child's sleep routine, with 55% losing at least 1 hour of sleep per night themselves during warmer months. The culprits: higher ambient temperatures that interfere with the body's cooling mechanism, and longer daylight hours that delay melatonin production. Experts say maintaining a consistent bedtime routine, keeping rooms cool and dark, and using lightweight breathable sleepwear can help (77% of parents surveyed ranked breathability as their top priority). The survey was conducted by KeaBabies in June 2026.

### STORY 5: AAP Releases New Screen Time Framework — It's Less About Hours, More About What and How
**Tier:** 2 (Today's News)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
The American Academy of Pediatrics released a major update to its screen time guidance in early 2026, moving away from a simple 2-hour daily limit toward a "5 Cs" framework. The five criteria: Child (each child's unique temperament), Content (prioritize quality educational content), Calm (can the child settle without a screen?), Crowding Out (is screen time replacing sleep, play, or family time?), and Communication (discuss media regularly with your kids). Hard rules that remain: no screens under 18 months except video chat; ages 2–5, limit to about 1 hour of high-quality content on weekdays; device-free bedrooms and mealtimes; no screens within 1 hour of bedtime.
