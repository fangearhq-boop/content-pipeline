Two Children's Product Recalls: Target Gigglescape Toy and GOPO Teething Toy — Both Choking Hazards

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

July 4, 2026

Two separate children's product recalls affect items sold at Target and Amazon, both involving choking hazards. Colorado Springs parents should check their toy collections for both products and act on the specific instructions for each.

Target Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy

Target announced a recall on July 2, 2026, for its Gigglescape Under the Sea Popping Toy. About 49,000 of these toys were sold at Target stores nationwide for approximately $10 each between August 2025 and January 2026.

The hazard: the clear plastic dome on top of the toy can detach from its blue plastic base, releasing small plastic balls that are a choking hazard for young children. The CPSC received nine reports of the dome detaching. In one incident, a child began to choke.

What to Do

Stop using the toy immediately. You have two options for a full refund:

  • Return it to any Target store
  • Request a prepaid return label by mail through Target's recall program

GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toy

The CPSC announced a recall of GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toys on June 18, 2026. About 70,410 of these toys were sold on Amazon for $11–$15 each between August 2023 and March 2026.

The product is a disc-shaped off-white teether with a gray center ball and six multi-colored silicone "tentacle" pull strings. The hazard is in those strings: they are smaller in diameter and longer than the mandatory safety standard allows for children's toys. The strings can reach into the back of a child's throat and become lodged there, causing respiratory distress or a potentially deadly choking incident. Three incidents of choking or respiratory distress were reported to the CPSC before the recall was issued.

What to Do

Stop using the toy immediately and follow these steps to receive a full refund:

  • Cut all the silicone pull strings off the toy
  • Write "DESTROYED" in permanent marker on the toy body
  • Email a photo of the destroyed toy to recalls@gopotoys.com before disposing of it
  • GOPO Toys will issue a full refund

What's Next

The CPSC maintains a free recall notification service at cpsc.gov. Parents can sign up by product type to receive alerts when new recalls are announced. Over the last several weeks, the CPSC has issued recalls or warnings on multiple infant and children's products, including the Fasando Baby Lounger warning announced this week. Staying current with recalls is one of the simplest safety habits Colorado Springs families can build.
