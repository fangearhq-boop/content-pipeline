July 4 in Colorado Springs — Family Guide to What's Happening Today

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting

July 4, 2026

Most professional fireworks displays across Colorado Springs and El Paso County have been cancelled this year due to Stage II fire restrictions and extreme wildfire danger — including the post-game show at Weidner Field and several community shows. Consumer fireworks remain permanently banned inside Colorado Springs city limits (fines up to $2,650 apply).

But there's actually quite a bit happening today, and some of it is genuinely great for families. Here's the complete guide.

Rock Ledge Ranch "Family Fourth"

When: 10 AM–4 PM
Where: 1401 Recreation Way (next to Garden of the Gods)
Cost: $7–$13; free for children ages 3 and under

This is the standout family event of the day. Rock Ledge Ranch's annual "Family Fourth" offers a full day of historic and patriotic programming including a children's patriotic parade, the Pikes Peak Brass Band, a "Strings and Things" dulcimer band, wagon rides, a pie-eating contest, ice cream, and food trucks.

The historical programming is especially worth the trip this year. Actors portray Gen. William Palmer (Colorado Springs' founder), President Theodore Roosevelt, Susan B. Anthony, and Katharine Lee Bates — who wrote "America the Beautiful" while looking out from the summit of Pikes Peak. It's genuinely educational and engaging for kids of most ages.

Banning Lewis Ranch July 4 Celebration

When: 3–10 PM
Where: Vista Park Pavilion, 8833 Vista Del Pico Drive
Cost: Free

Banning Lewis Ranch is hosting a free celebration at Vista Park Pavilion. Bounce houses run from 4–7 PM. More than 30 food trucks are on-site from 3 PM. Live music from Tiny Pockets (4–6 PM) and Funkology (7–9 PM). Note: no private grills or charcoal due to Stage II fire restrictions.

Colorado Springs Switchbacks vs. Phoenix Rising FC

When: 7 PM kickoff
Where: Weidner Field
Broadcast: CBS 5, CBS Sports Golazo Network

The post-game fireworks show was cancelled, but the match is on. The Switchbacks have had a strong 2026 season, including a notable upset win in the U.S. Open Cup earlier in the year. A holiday soccer match at the stadium is a solid family evening option.

Monument / Tri-Lakes Fourth of July

If you're willing to head north to Monument, the Tri-Lakes community has a full all-day celebration organized by the Tri-Lakes Chamber of Commerce and Monument Hill Kiwanis Club. Highlights include a pancake breakfast at 7 AM at St. Peter Catholic Church, the Palmer Lake Fun Run at 7 AM, a street fair from 8 AM–3 PM at Second and Washington Streets, a children's parade at 9:30 AM, and the main parade at 10 AM. Free shuttle parking is available from Palmer Ridge and Lewis Palmer High Schools.

What's Next

The Stage II fire restrictions that cancelled this year's fireworks remain in effect across much of El Paso County and Colorado Springs. Check coloradosprings.gov for any updates on restriction levels as conditions change through the summer.
