CPSC Issues Urgent Warning on Fasando Baby Loungers — Infant Death Reported, Manufacturer Refuses Recall

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting

July 4, 2026

Colorado Springs parents who purchased baby loungers from Amazon in the last two years need to act immediately. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent warning about Fasando Baby Loungers after an infant death was reported — and the situation is more complicated than a typical recall because the manufacturer has refused to take action.

The CPSC issued a Notice of Violation, not a standard recall. The distinction matters: when a U.S. company refuses a recall, the CPSC has legal tools to compel compliance. When the manufacturer is an overseas company selling through third-party marketplaces, those tools are harder to apply. Fasando is sold by a China-based seller and the company has not agreed to recall the product, stop selling it, or offer any remedy to consumers.

What Happened

According to the CPSC, a 2-month-old infant died after being placed in a Fasando Baby Lounger on top of an adult bed, where the infant was later found unresponsive. The CPSC determined that the lounger's design creates both entrapment and fall hazards: the sides of the lounger are too low to contain an infant securely, and the foot openings are too wide — both violations of the mandatory federal safety standard for infant sleep products.

About 6,200 units were sold through Amazon for approximately $35 per unit between August 2024 and July 2025.

What to Do Now

Because there is no formal recall, there is no refund or replacement program. The CPSC is asking parents to destroy the product themselves and dispose of it so it cannot be used by anyone else:

  • Stop using the lounger immediately
  • Remove the foam padding from inside the cover
  • Cut the cover in half
  • Dispose of all pieces in the trash
  • Do not donate, sell, or give the product to anyone else

If you are unsure whether you purchased a Fasando lounger, check your Amazon order history for purchases between August 2024 and July 2025. The product was sold under the "Fasando" name by the seller Qiyangshiqiguangyuekejiyouxiangongsi.

A Growing Problem: The Infant Sleep Product Standard

This is not an isolated case. In the same period, the CPSC has also warned about Babypark Children's Carriers (fall hazard from leg openings that are too wide) and formally recalled the Vevor Baby Lounger for the same types of violations as the Fasando product. The pattern reflects a broader issue with infant sleep products — particularly those sold by overseas third-party sellers on major marketplaces — that do not meet U.S. mandatory federal safety standards.

The CPSC recommends that parents always verify that any infant sleep product meets the mandatory standard before purchasing. When purchasing through a marketplace like Amazon, check that the seller information is clear and that the product description explicitly states compliance with U.S. safety standards.

What's Next

The CPSC has indicated it continues to investigate and may pursue further enforcement options against Fasando. Parents with questions can check the CPSC's official warning at cpsc.gov and sign up for free recall notifications to stay informed about future actions. If you have been injured or if you are aware of an incident involving this product, you can report it at SaferProducts.gov.
