### STORY 1: Children's Sleepwear Recall Wave

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**

COS parents, it's time to check your kids' pajama drawers.

Three children's sleepwear brands have been recalled by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission for violating mandatory flammability standards — meaning they can catch fire more quickly than the law allows, creating a burn hazard for children.

Here's what you need to know about each:

**Blossom Blanks (Blossom)** — About 630 two-piece loungewear sets in sizes 12M through 12Y are recalled. The sets were sold at children's boutiques and online between September 2023 and May 2025. If you have one, stop using it immediately, cut it in half, photograph the destroyed garment, and email the photo to sales@blossomocc.com for a full refund.

**Silks** — Two-piece loungewear sets in sizes 2T through 13/14 are recalled. These were sold at silksdesign.com from January 2024 through September 2025 for $40-$50. Stop using, cut in half, and email a photo to silkssupport@silksdesign.com for a refund.

**Andy & Evan** — Children's pajama sets that also violate mandatory flammability standards. Contact Andy & Evan directly for remedy details.

No injuries have been reported for any of these products — but the recall is precautionary and serious. Children's sleepwear flammability standards exist specifically because kids move around, roll near lights or candles, and need that protection built into their clothing.

Do you check labels before buying kids' PJs? What's your go-to for finding safe sleepwear?

### STORY 4: MeadowGrass Music Festival

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM MT)**

If you're looking for a Memorial Day Weekend plan that the whole family will talk about for years, MeadowGrass might be it.

The 17th Annual MeadowGrass Music Festival runs May 22-24 at La Foret Conference & Retreat Center in Black Forest — just north of Colorado Springs. It's a three-day outdoor music and camping festival with a genuine family-first vibe.

Here's the kid highlight: children 12 and under get in free with a paid adult.

The Faricy Boys Kid Zone is packed with hands-on activities: giant bubbles from Milibo Art Theater, crafts from Concrete Couch, a hay wagon, kid yoga, and art. There are also guided nature hikes and music workshops. Camping is available in the Ponderosa Forest if you want to make it a full weekend adventure.

This year's music lineup includes Kyle Hollingsworth Band, Nershi Hann Trio, ALO, and Shook Twins — a solid mix if you like Americana and bluegrass.

It's a lot to pack into one weekend, and families who've been tend to come back year after year.

### STORY 5: Touch-A-Truck May 16

#### Long-Form Post -- 5:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM MT)**

Looking for a free Saturday activity that the little ones will absolutely love?

Touch-A-Truck is coming to Colorado Springs on Saturday, May 16, starting at 9:30 AM at 1045 Lower Gold Camp Rd.

If you've never been to a Touch-A-Truck event, here's the idea: kids get up close and hands-on with the big vehicles they love — fire trucks, construction equipment, police cars, and more. They can climb up, sit in the cab, honk the horn, and explore with real workers and volunteers on hand to answer questions.

It's completely free. No registration required. Just show up with your kids and enjoy a morning exploring the machines that keep Colorado Springs running.

These events tend to draw a crowd, so arriving early is always a good move.
