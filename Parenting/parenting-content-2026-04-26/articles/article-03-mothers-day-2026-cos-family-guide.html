Mother's Day 2026 in Colorado Springs: 4 Family-Friendly Plans Worth Booking Now

By Keisha Williams | COS Parenting | April 26, 2026

Mother's Day is Sunday, May 10. That's two weeks away — and for the events worth doing in and around Colorado Springs, the booking window is closing. Whether you're planning something adventurous, something outdoorsy, something kid-friendly, or something with a genuine splurge factor, there's an option in the Pikes Peak region for every family and budget.

Here are four options that are genuinely family-friendly, bookable now, and likely to create a memory that lasts beyond the weekend.

Royal Gorge Route Railroad Mother's Day Trains (May 9-10)

The Royal Gorge Route Railroad runs Mother's Day excursions on both Saturday, May 9, and Sunday, May 10, with departures from 9 AM through 6:30 PM. The 2-hour train journey travels through the Royal Gorge — one of the more spectacular natural landscapes in southern Colorado — while passengers enjoy either a 3-course gourmet dining experience or casual menu options from the 403 Grill.

This is consistently one of the most popular Mother's Day outings in the Colorado Springs area, which means tickets go quickly. If this is on your list, booking now rather than next weekend is strongly recommended. The railroad is located in Canon City, approximately 45 minutes from Colorado Springs.

Gather Mountain Blooms Garden Party (May 9-10, 10 AM-12:30 PM)

For families who want something hands-on and outdoors, Gather Mountain Blooms is hosting a Mother's Day Garden Party on both May 9 and 10 from 10 AM to 12:30 PM. The event includes a charcuterie brunch, a hands-on garden craft, and flower starters to take home.

Pricing: $50 per adult, $40 per child 12 and under. It's the kind of event where the kids are genuinely engaged — not just sitting through a meal — and everyone goes home with something they helped create.

Colorado Wolf and Wildlife Center (May 10, 9-11 AM)

The Colorado Wolf and Wildlife Center is holding a special Mother's Day event on Sunday, May 10 from 9 to 11 AM. The event includes a guided wolf tour and a flower for every mom in attendance. Pricing: $40 per adult (ages 12 and older) and $20 per child (ages 6-11).

This is an educational experience that works especially well for families with kids who are genuinely interested in wildlife. The Center is located in Divide, about 30 minutes northwest of Colorado Springs — plan for a scenic drive as part of the morning.

The Broadmoor Hall Mother's Day Brunch (May 10, 10 AM-3 PM)

For families planning a more formal celebration, The Broadmoor Hall is hosting its annual Mother's Day brunch on Sunday, May 10, from 10 AM to 3 PM. Pricing: $135 per adult and $50 per child. Reservations are required.

The Broadmoor is one of the premier venues in all of Colorado, and the Mother's Day brunch reflects that. If there's a milestone involved — a mom celebrating a significant birthday, a grandmother's first Mother's Day with a new grandchild — this is the event designed for that moment. Plan ahead: reservations for this event fill earlier than most families expect.

What's Next

All four events are bookable now through their respective websites or by calling directly. Given that Mother's Day reservations across Colorado Springs typically fill in the last two weeks before the holiday, waiting until the week of May 10 is a risk. If any of these are on your list, the time to act is today or this coming week.
