CPSC Children's Sleepwear Recall Wave — Blossom, Silks, Andy & Evan: Burn Hazard Alert
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post COS parents, it's time to check your kids' pajama drawers. Three children's sleepwear brands have been recalled by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission for violating mandatory flammability standards — meaning they can catch fire more quickly than the law allows, creating a burn hazard for children. Here's what you need to know about each: **Blossom Blanks (Blossom)** — About 630 two-piece loungewear sets in sizes 12M through 12Y are recalled. The sets were sold at children's boutiques and online between September 2023 and May 2025. If you have one, stop using it immediately, cut it in half, photograph the destroyed garment, and email the photo to sales@blossomocc.com for a full refund. **Silks** — Two-piece loungewear sets in sizes 2T through 13/14 are recalled. These were sold at silksdesign.com from January 2024 through September 2025 for $40-$50. Stop using, cut in half, and email a photo to silkssupport@silksdesign.com for a refund. **Andy & Evan** — Children's pajama sets that also violate mandatory flammability standards. Contact Andy & Evan directly for remedy details. No injuries have been reported for any of these products — but the recall is precautionary and serious. Children's sleepwear flammability standards exist specifically because kids move around, roll near lights or candles, and need that protection built into their clothing. Do you check labels before buying kids' PJs? What's your go-to for finding safe sleepwear? --- #### Image Caption -- 8:00 AM MT ### Image Post Caption Three children's sleepwear brands recalled for burn hazard: Blossom Blanks, Silks, and Andy & Evan. ### Long-Form Post If you're looking for a Memorial Day Weekend plan that the whole family will talk about for years, MeadowGrass might be it. The 17th Annual MeadowGrass Music Festival runs May 22-24 at La Foret Conference & Retreat Center in Black Forest — just north of Colorado Springs. It's a three-day outdoor music and camping festival with a genuine family-first vibe. Here's the kid highlight: children 12 and under get in free with a paid adult. The Faricy Boys Kid Zone is packed with hands-on activities: giant bubbles from Milibo Art Theater, crafts from Concrete Couch, a hay wagon, kid yoga, and art. There are also guided nature hikes and music workshops. Camping is available in the Ponderosa Forest if you want to make it a full weekend adventure. This year's music lineup includes Kyle Hollingsworth Band, Nershi Hann Trio, ALO, and Shook Twins — a solid mix if you like Americana and bluegrass. It's a lot to pack into one weekend, and families who've been tend to come back year after year. Has your family done MeadowGrass before?
