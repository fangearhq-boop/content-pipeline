### STORY 3: CPSC Recall — 121,000 Orb Funkee Squeeze Toys Contain Asbestos
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

The CPSC is recalling approximately 121,000 Orb Funkee squeeze toys because the sand filling inside the toys may contain fibrous tremolite — a form of asbestos. The toys were sold at Walmart and Ollie's Bargain Outlet stores nationwide from February 2025 through April 2026 for $5 to $40. Two models are affected: Model 17451 (large golden monkey) and Model 41929 (small monkeys in assorted colors). The recall applies to products with date code 3102491A. If the toy has been ruptured or the sand has leaked, parents should put on a mask and gloves before handling.

**Key facts:**
- Product: Orb Funkee Squeeze Toys (monkey design)
- Hazard: Fibrous tremolite (asbestos) in the sand filling
- Units recalled: ~121,000
- Sold at: Walmart and Ollie's Bargain Outlet
- Sale dates: February 2025 – April 2026
- Price: $5 – $40
- Affected models: No. 17451 (large golden monkey), No. 41929 (small monkeys assortment)
- Date code: 3102491A (printed inside toy)
- Action: Stop use immediately; if sand has leaked — put on mask and gloves, use damp cloths, double-bag and seal, dispose per local regulations
- Refund contact: customerservice@orbtoys.com
- Imported by: The Orb Factory
- No injuries reported at time of recall
- Source: CPSC.gov, The Hill, 9News, Fox Business, Newsweek

### STORY 4: CPSC Recall — Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Towers Have a Platform That Loosens
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

The CPSC recalled 25,235 Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Standing Towers on May 14, 2026 because the platform inside the tower can loosen over time, becoming unstable or detaching and creating a fall hazard for young children. Before the recall was announced, Guidecraft received 11 reports of children falling from these towers, including three injuries such as a bloody nose and bruises. The towers were sold at Amazon, Walmart, Target, Wayfair, Overstock, and Maisonette from June 2022 through October 2023. The remedy is free repair parts — not a refund.

**Key facts:**
- Product: Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Standing Towers (Classic + Contemporary, nine colors)
- Recall date: May 14, 2026
- Units: 25,235
- Hazard: Platform loosens/detaches; fall risk to young children
- Reports: 11 reports of falls before recall; 3 injuries (bloody nose, bruises)
- Sold at: Amazon, Walmart, Target, Wayfair, Overstock, Maisonette
- Sale dates: June 2022 – October 2023
- Remedy: FREE repair parts and installation instructions (not a refund)
- Contact: 800-524-3555 (M–F, 8 AM–8 PM ET) or ProductSupport@guidecraft.com or guidecraft.com/pages/product-recall
- Source: CPSC.gov, Newsweek, Recall Mom Substack, Consumer Reports

### STORY 5: Safe Streets COS — City Adds 22 New School Zones and Flashing Beacons
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** Local News
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

The City of Colorado Springs launched "Safe Streets COS" in May 2026 — a citywide initiative to reduce crashes and improve safety around schools. Since the program launched, the city has installed 22 additional school zones across Colorado Springs, including improved signage, reduced speed areas, and stronger visibility improvements. Flashing beacons at Sierra High School will be operational for the 2026-27 school year. Installation of flashing signs at Panorama Middle School is expected to be completed by end of summer. Mobile speed enforcement near schools has also increased. Since the mayor took office in 2023, traffic enforcement efforts have increased by 50%, including a 200% increase around school zones.

- Panorama Middle School: flashing signs installation set to complete by end of summer
