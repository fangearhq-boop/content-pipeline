### STORY 2: D20 New School at Air Force Academy

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 10:00 AM MT

Big news for Academy District 20 families, especially those living on or near the Air Force Academy.

D20 has received a $42.7 million federal grant to rebuild Douglass Valley Elementary School into a brand-new pre-K through 8th grade campus on the U.S. Air Force Academy grounds. The project totals $54.2 million and will transform what's currently a K-5 school into a full K-8 campus serving approximately 787 students in pre-kindergarten through 8th grade.

The school is being designed by RTA Architects — the local firm behind the Pikes Peak Visitor Center — and construction is expected to wrap in time for a projected January 2028 opening if everything stays on schedule.

This is a significant investment in COS's military community and the families who call the Academy home. It's also the kind of school construction project that doesn't happen often — a ground-up rebuild with federal support that expands both the grade range and the capacity of the school.

D20 families near Douglass Valley: what do you think about the new school?

### STORY 3: Orb Funkee Asbestos Recall

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 12:00 PM MT

This one's important, and we want to make sure Colorado Springs parents see it.

The CPSC is recalling approximately 121,000 Orb Funkee squeeze toys because the sand inside the toys may contain fibrous tremolite — a form of asbestos. These are the monkey-shaped squeeze toys sold at Walmart and Ollie's Bargain Outlet between February 2025 and April 2026, priced between $5 and $40.

Two models are affected: Model No. 17451 (the large golden monkey) and Model No. 41929 (an assortment of smaller monkeys in orange, purple, and green). Check for the date code 3102491A printed inside the toy's cylinder.

If you have one of these toys, stop use immediately. If the toy is intact, place it in a heavy-duty plastic bag, seal it with tape, and contact The Orb Factory at customerservice@orbtoys.com for a refund.

If the toy has already been ruptured or the sand has leaked out: put on a mask and gloves first. Use damp cloths to pick up the sand. Place everything — toy, cloths, mask, gloves — in a heavy-duty plastic bag, place that bag inside a second bag, seal with tape, and dispose according to your local regulations.

No injuries have been reported, but these are the kind of hazard instructions you want to follow carefully and completely.

Does your family have one of these toys? Share this so other Colorado Springs parents can see it.

### STORY 4: Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower Recall

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 3:00 PM MT

If you have a Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Standing Tower, it's time to reach out to the company.

The CPSC recalled 25,235 of these towers on May 14 because the platform inside can loosen over time, becoming unstable or detaching entirely — creating a fall hazard for the toddlers using them. Guidecraft received 11 reports of children falling from the towers before the recall was announced, including three injuries.

The recall covers Classic and Contemporary Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Towers in nine colors, sold at Amazon, Walmart, Target, Wayfair, Overstock, and Maisonette from June 2022 through October 2023.

The good news: the remedy here is a repair, not a destruction-and-refund process. Guidecraft will send you free replacement parts and installation instructions to fix the platform. You can reach them at 800-524-3555 (Monday–Friday, 8 AM–8 PM ET), at ProductSupport@guidecraft.com, or through their recall page at guidecraft.com/pages/product-recall.

If you purchased one as a gift for another family, it's worth passing this along to them as well.

Do you have one of these towers? Or do you know someone who might?

### STORY 5: Safe Streets COS

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 5:00 PM MT

Colorado Springs has been quietly making its streets safer around schools, and it's worth knowing what's changed.

The city launched "Safe Streets COS" this month — a citywide traffic safety initiative that has already resulted in 22 new school zones being installed across Colorado Springs. That includes improved signage, reduced speed designations, safer crossings, and stronger visibility around schools throughout the city.

Two specific upgrades worth noting for COS families: flashing beacons at Sierra High School are installed and will be operational for the start of the 2026-27 school year, and installation of flashing signs at Panorama Middle School is expected to be completed by end of summer.

Mobile speed enforcement around school zones is also part of the program. Since the current mayor took office in 2023, total city traffic enforcement has increased by 50% — and enforcement specifically around school zones has increased by 200%. The long-term goal is a 35% reduction in serious injury and deadly crashes by 2035.

You can find more details and the full program overview at coloradosprings.gov/SafeStreetsCOS.
