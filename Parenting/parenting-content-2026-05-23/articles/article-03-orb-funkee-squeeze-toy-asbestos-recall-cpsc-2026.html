CPSC Recall: 121,000 Orb Funkee Squeeze Toys May Contain Asbestos — What COS Parents Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 23, 2026

The Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) has recalled approximately 121,000 Orb Funkee squeeze toys because the sand filling inside the toys may contain fibrous tremolite — a form of asbestos. The toys were sold at Walmart and Ollie's Bargain Outlet stores nationwide from February 2025 through April 2026, priced between $5 and $40. They were imported by The Orb Factory.

If you purchased one of these products, stop use immediately and follow the steps below. The handling instructions differ depending on whether the toy is still intact or has already been ruptured.

Which Products Are Recalled

Two models of Orb Funkee squeeze toys are included in this recall:

  • Model No. 17451: A large golden monkey (stylized "monkee")
  • Model No. 41929: An assortment of small monkeys in orange, purple, and green

The recall applies only to products with the date code 3102491A, which is printed inside the toy's silicone or rubber cylinder. If you have an Orb Funkee squeeze toy that doesn't have this date code, it may not be affected — but it's worth checking carefully.

These toys were sold at Walmart and Ollie's Bargain Outlet stores. They may also have been purchased as gifts, so it's worth checking if you've received one from another family.

The Safety Hazard

Fibrous tremolite is a naturally occurring mineral that belongs to the asbestos group. When asbestos fibers become airborne — through handling, squeezing, or damage to the material containing them — they can be inhaled. Long-term asbestos exposure is associated with serious lung conditions including mesothelioma and asbestosis, though the risk from a single exposure event is different from the occupational long-term exposure that drives most documented cases.

The CPSC's concern with these toys is that the sand filling contains this material, and squeezing the toy — exactly how it's designed to be used — could potentially cause sand to shift or, in the case of a ruptured toy, to escape and become airborne.

No injuries have been reported in connection with this recall as of the time it was announced.

What to Do If the Toy Is Intact

  1. Stop use immediately and take the toy away from children.
  2. Place the toy in a heavy-duty plastic bag.
  3. Seal the bag securely with tape.
  4. Take a photo of the product in the bag, showing the date code.
  5. Submit the photo to The Orb Factory at customerservice@orbtoys.com to request a full refund.

What to Do If the Toy Has Already Ruptured or Sand Has Leaked

If the toy has been damaged and sand has escaped, take additional precautions:

  1. Put on a mask and gloves before touching anything.
  2. Use damp cloths to carefully pick up any sand that has leaked out.
  3. Place the toy, cloths, mask, and gloves in a heavy-duty plastic bag and seal with tape.
  4. Place that sealed bag inside a second heavy-duty plastic bag and seal with tape (double-bag).
  5. Dispose of the double-bagged material in accordance with your local and/or state regulations. Your trash service may have specific requirements for hazardous materials.

Do not vacuum up the sand — vacuum cleaners can spread fine particles into the air.
