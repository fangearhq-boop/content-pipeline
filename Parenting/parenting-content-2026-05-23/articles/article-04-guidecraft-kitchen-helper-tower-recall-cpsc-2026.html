CPSC Recall: Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Towers Have a Platform That Can Fail — 25,000 Units

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 23, 2026

The Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) recalled 25,235 Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Standing Towers on May 14, 2026. The platform inside the recalled towers can loosen over time, becoming unstable or detaching entirely and creating a fall hazard for the young children using them. Guidecraft received 11 reports of children falling from these towers before the recall was announced, including three injuries.

The remedy for this recall is different from most: rather than a refund, Guidecraft is offering free replacement parts and installation instructions to repair the platform. You do not need to destroy the product — you can fix it.

What Products Are Affected

The recall covers Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Standing Towers in both the Classic and Contemporary styles, available in nine colors. These are the toddler standing towers with a railing and enclosed platform that allow young children to stand at counter height to help with cooking, art projects, or other activities.

The affected towers were sold at Amazon, Walmart, Target, Wayfair, Overstock, and Maisonette from June 2022 through October 2023. If you purchased a Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower in that time window from any of those retailers, check whether your product is included in the recall by contacting Guidecraft directly.

This recall is separate and distinct from the Cosyland Children's Tower Stool recall that was announced earlier this year. Different brand, different product, different hazard, different remedy.

The Safety Hazard

The platform that toddlers stand on inside the tower can loosen over time with regular use. A loosened or detached platform creates a fall hazard — a child standing on the platform could shift or fall if the platform no longer provides stable support.

Guidecraft reported 11 falls to the CPSC before the recall was announced. Three of those resulted in injuries: a bloody nose and bruises were among the documented outcomes. No serious injuries were reported, but the CPSC considers any fall from an elevated standing position in this product category a significant hazard — especially for toddlers who may not react quickly enough to prevent injury when the platform gives way.

What to Do

The remedy for this recall is a free repair — not a refund. Guidecraft will send affected families replacement parts and installation instructions at no charge. To initiate the process:

  • Phone: 800-524-3555 (Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM – 8:00 PM ET)
  • Email: ProductSupport@guidecraft.com
  • Online: guidecraft.com/pages/product-recall

Stop use of the tower while you wait for the repair parts. If you purchased the tower as a gift for another family — particularly between June 2022 and October 2023 — it's worth letting them know so they can check their product.

What's Next

Families with an affected Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower should stop use immediately and contact Guidecraft through one of the three channels above to request the free repair kit. The repair process is designed to be manageable without professional help — Guidecraft will provide installation instructions along with the parts. For a full list of active CPSC recalls, visit cpsc.gov/Recalls or call the CPSC hotline at 800-638-2772.
