Academy D20 Gets $42.7 Million to Build New Pre-K to 8th Grade School at Air Force Academy

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 23, 2026

Academy District 20 is getting one of the largest school construction investments in the district's recent history. The Department of Defense awarded D20 a $42.7 million grant to rebuild Douglass Valley Elementary School into a brand-new pre-K through 8th grade school on the U.S. Air Force Academy campus in Colorado Springs. The project, which totals $54.2 million, will transform a K-5 elementary school into a full K-8 campus designed to serve approximately 787 students.

If construction stays on schedule, the new school could be ready for students as early as January 2028.

What's Being Built

The current Douglass Valley Elementary School serves kindergarten through 5th grade students. The rebuilt campus will expand that range to include pre-kindergarten through 8th grade — adding early childhood programming on one end and middle school grades on the other.

That's a significant structural change for the school and for the USAFA community. Families whose children would previously need to transition to a different school for middle grades will have the option to stay at the same campus through 8th grade. Pre-K programming — which is increasingly in demand across Colorado Springs — will be added to a campus where it doesn't currently exist.

The new school is being designed by RTA Architects, a Colorado Springs-based firm known locally for other significant projects including the Pikes Peak Visitor Center. The architectural approach emphasizes modern classroom design, and the rebuild replaces what the district has described as aging facilities.

The Federal Funding

The $42.7 million federal grant comes through the Department of Defense's Public Schools on Military Installations program — a funding mechanism specifically designed to support school facilities serving military families on or near Department of Defense installations. The USAFA campus qualifies because D20 serves a significant population of students connected to active-duty military families stationed at the Air Force Academy.

The total project cost is $54.2 million. The federal grant covers $42.7 million of that, meaning the district and other funding sources are responsible for the approximately $11.5 million difference. The scale of the federal investment reflects both the size of the rebuild and the prioritization of USAFA-connected school infrastructure by the federal program.

Who This Affects

The most direct impact is on families whose children attend or will attend Douglass Valley Elementary. Military families stationed at the U.S. Air Force Academy who live on base tend to rely heavily on on-campus schools like Douglass Valley because of the logistics of on-base life — proximity matters when deployment schedules, transportation, and base access are part of daily reality.

The addition of pre-K programming is significant for families with young children, particularly those who are new to the area or whose early childhood care options are limited by on-base geography. The expansion to 8th grade reduces transitions for families during the middle school years — a period that can be particularly disruptive for military kids who are already navigating potential relocations.

What's Next

Construction timelines for projects of this scale are subject to change based on permitting, supply chain factors, and weather. D20 has not announced a groundbreaking date as of the time this article was written. Families with questions about the Douglass Valley rebuild should reach out to Academy District 20 directly through asd20.org. The district communicates updates on major facility projects through its official communications channels and community newsletters.
