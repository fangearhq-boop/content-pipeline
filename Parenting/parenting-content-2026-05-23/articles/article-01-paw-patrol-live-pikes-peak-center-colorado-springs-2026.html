PAW Patrol Live Is at Pikes Peak Center This Weekend — Two Shows Today

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 23, 2026

If you've got a PAW Patrol fan in your household, the Mighty Pups have come to Colorado Springs. PAW Patrol Live is performing at Pikes Peak Center this weekend, with two shows on Saturday, May 23 and one on Sunday, May 24.

The live arena show brings Chase, Marshall, Skye, and the rest of the Mighty Pups to the stage in an interactive theatrical production. Saturday's shows are at 10:00 AM and 2:00 PM. Sunday's show is at 2:00 PM. Pikes Peak Center is located at 190 S. Cascade Ave in downtown Colorado Springs.

About the Show

In this production, the PAW Patrol has just saved Adventure City using their Mighty Pup Powers — and it's time to celebrate with a big street party. But Supervillain Scientist Victoria Vance has other plans: she feels left out of the festivities and unleashes her latest invention to crash the party. Chase, Marshall, Skye, and the Mighty Pups have to use their super-charged powers to outsmart Victoria and make sure Adventure City's celebration goes on as planned.

PAW Patrol Live productions are designed for young children and their families, with bright lighting, live performers in character costumes, interactive audience moments, and pacing that keeps younger audience members engaged throughout the show. Parents attending with toddlers and preschoolers can expect an energetic, visually stimulating production that lasts approximately 90 minutes including an intermission.

Tickets and Planning Your Visit

Tickets for PAW Patrol Live at Pikes Peak Center are available online at AXS.com and at the Pikes Peak Center box office. It's worth checking availability before heading out — weekend shows for popular children's productions can sell quickly during a holiday weekend.

A few practical notes for families planning to attend:

  • The 10:00 AM Saturday show is the first show of the weekend. Arriving at least 30 minutes early gives families time to find parking, get settled, and let younger kids take in the arena environment before the show starts.
  • The 2:00 PM Saturday show and 2:00 PM Sunday show both fall during typical afternoon nap windows for very young children — something to factor into timing for families with babies or toddlers under two.
  • Parking in downtown Colorado Springs near Pikes Peak Center is available in nearby surface lots and the garage along Cascade Ave. Arriving early reduces competition for closer spots.
  • PAW Patrol merchandise is typically available for purchase at the venue. Budget-conscious families may want to set expectations with kids before arriving.

A Good Memorial Day Weekend Option

This weekend is the first full weekend after D11 students finished their last day of school on Friday, May 22. D49 graduation ceremonies are also happening today. For families looking for something to do with younger kids during Memorial Day weekend — when Territory Days crowds can be loud and MeadowGrass is geared toward older music fans — a live arena show in a climate-controlled venue is a solid alternative.

PAW Patrol Live has been touring nationally for years and has built a reputation for productions that hold up for both the kids who love the show and the parents sitting next to them. The Colorado Springs stop is one of the few chances to see the production locally without a longer drive.
