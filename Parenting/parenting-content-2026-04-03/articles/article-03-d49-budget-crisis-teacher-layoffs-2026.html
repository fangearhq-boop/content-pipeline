D49 Budget Crisis: What the $2M Accounting Error and Teacher Layoffs Mean for Colorado Springs Families

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 3, 2026

District 49 is navigating one of the most significant budget crises in its recent history. Here's what's happened, what a KRDO investigation uncovered, and what families in the district need to understand heading into the rest of the school year and beyond.

What Happened: Fiscal Exigency and Deep Cuts

In January 2026, District 49 formally declared fiscal exigency — a legal declaration of financial emergency that gives a school district authority to make cuts it otherwise could not, including to staff contracts. The district is facing a projected budget gap of $9 to $11 million.

The causes are multiple and intersecting: declining enrollment in D49's coverage area (northeast and east Colorado Springs, including Falcon, Peyton, Calhan, and the Powers Boulevard corridor), the defunding of mill levy equalization — a state funding mechanism that previously supported districts with lower property tax bases — rising healthcare costs, and general inflation. None of these are unique to D49, but they have hit the district hard simultaneously.

The D49 board approved cutting approximately 50 positions — a mix of teachers and administrators — with 12 teacher positions cut in February 2026. The teachers' union has been vocal in its opposition to the sequencing and scope of the cuts.

What KRDO Found: The $2 Million Error

A March 2026 investigation by KRDO uncovered a significant piece of context that D49 families deserve to understand. KRDO found that D49 administrators made a $2 million accounting error in a state scheduling audit. In June 2025, the district paid back $2,040,128.86 to the state. Within the same budget cycle, the district then cut $1.8 million in teacher salaries.

The teachers' union publicly challenged the sequencing: the district paid back more than $2 million to the state — money that was tied to an administrative error — and then reduced teacher compensation by nearly the same amount. Whether that sequencing was the result of poor planning, bad luck, or something else is a question that district administrators and the board have addressed in their own communications. But KRDO's reporting gives D49 families a fuller picture of why the budget situation is as serious as it is.

What It Means for Families

For families in D49, the practical impact of 50 position cuts will vary by school and grade level. Larger class sizes, reduced elective offerings, and changes to support staff coverage are the most common downstream effects of this scale of reduction. The district has not published a school-by-school breakdown of where cuts are landing, so families may want to reach out directly to their school's principal to understand how their specific school is affected.

It's also worth knowing what D49 is still doing despite the budget pressure. The district is opening two new full-day preschool classrooms at the D49 Student Success Center starting in August 2026. That expansion — announced earlier this spring — is proceeding. Budget cuts and program growth are happening in the same district at the same time, which reflects the complexity of running a school system at scale.

Where to Get More Information

D49 communicates budget updates through its official website at d49.org and through school-level communications. The Gazette, KRDO, KOAA, and Rocky Mountain PBS have all covered the budget situation in depth and are worth bookmarking for ongoing updates. The teachers' union (D49 Education Association) has its own communications channel for perspective from the staff side of the issue.

If your family has specific questions about programs, staffing, or services at your child's school, a direct conversation with the school principal or counselor is the most reliable way to get school-specific information.

What's Next

D49 will continue working through the 2025–26 school year under the fiscal exigency declaration. The 2026–27 budget cycle will determine whether the district's financial situation has stabilized or continues to require further cuts. Enrollment trends — the root driver of much of the budget pressure — will be the metric most worth watching over the next 12 months.

COS Parenting will continue following this story and will update when D49 publishes additional information about how cuts are being implemented school by school.
