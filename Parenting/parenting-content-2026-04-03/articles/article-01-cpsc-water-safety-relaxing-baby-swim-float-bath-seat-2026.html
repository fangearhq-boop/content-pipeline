CPSC Water Safety Alert: "Relaxing Baby" Swim Floats Linked to Toddler Death; Baby Bath Seats Recalled

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 3, 2026

Two water product safety alerts arrived within days of each other — and with spring and summer approaching, COS parents need to check their garages and bathrooms now.

Urgent Warning: "Relaxing Baby" Swim Floats

On April 2, 2026, the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission issued an urgent warning: stop using "Relaxing Baby" branded swim floats for infants and toddlers immediately. A 2-year-old child died after using one of these products. The CPSC has determined that the floats can flip over or submerge a child underwater during use.

The seller — a company called Wu He, operating under the "Relaxing Baby" brand name and based in China — has not cooperated with the CPSC. That non-cooperation means there is no formal recall process and no established refund mechanism. The CPSC's guidance is direct: the action is to puncture the float so it cannot be inflated again, then throw it away.

If you purchased this product, it was sold on Amazon and eBay. Do not try to resell it. Do not store it for future use. Puncture it and discard it.

Recall: YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats

In a separate but related water safety alert, approximately 8,960 YCXXKJ-brand baby bath seats have been recalled by the CPSC. The seat, Model YD-1958, was sold on Amazon by a seller called BenTalk between May 2024 and October 2025 for approximately $34 to $40. The seats come in blue, gray, pink, and yellow, and include small duck, turtle, or whale toys with four suction cups on the bottom.

The problem: the seats are unstable and can tip over during use, violating the mandatory standard for infant bath seats. The CPSC has confirmed no injuries have been reported yet. But an unstable bath seat for an infant is a drowning hazard that cannot be used safely.

A full refund is available for this recall. If you have this product:

  1. Stop using it immediately.
  2. Write "Recalled" on the front of the seat in permanent marker.
  3. Photograph the seat clearly showing the text.
  4. Email two photos to bentalkhere@outlook.com to receive a full refund.

Water Safety Matters Year-Round in Colorado

Both of these product alerts arrive at a time of year when Colorado Springs families are starting to think about summer water activities. Swim floats and bath seats may have been purchased months ago or borrowed from someone else. If there's any chance you have either of these products at home, it's worth checking today.

For general water safety guidance, the American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that children under age 1 never be left alone in a bath — even for a moment. Bath seats and ring inserts are convenience items, not safety devices. Constant hands-on supervision is the only reliable protection for infants in any water environment.

What's Next

The CPSC updates its recall and warning database regularly at cpsc.gov. Colorado Springs parents can also sign up for recall alerts at cpsc.gov/Recalls to receive notifications directly. If you have either of the products described in this article, take action today: the swim float should be punctured and discarded, and the bath seat should be photographed and submitted for a refund before setting it aside permanently.

Share this information with other parents in your network. Water hazards are one of the leading causes of accidental injury in young children — and an urgent CPSC warning linked to a child's death is information that should reach every parent who might have this product.
