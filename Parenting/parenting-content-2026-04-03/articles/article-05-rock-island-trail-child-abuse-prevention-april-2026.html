Rock Island Trail Gets a New Bridge This Spring — Plus Resources for Child Abuse Prevention Month

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 3, 2026

Two things worth knowing this April in Colorado Springs — one is good news about a trail upgrade, and the other is a reminder that community support for children matters all year, not just in April.

Rock Island Trail: The New Bridge Is Coming

If your family uses the Rock Island Trail for biking, walking, or running, there's meaningful news this spring. The City of Colorado Springs TOPS (Trails, Open Space and Parks) program is launching construction on a 210-foot pedestrian bridge over Sand Creek — a project that will finally connect the east and west sides of the Rock Island Trail, which currently lacks a safe crossing at that location.

The project also includes a new pedestrian-activated flashing crossing at Peterson Road and more than 2 miles of new trail connections. Construction is expected to begin this spring and take approximately 10 to 12 months to complete. Families who regularly use the Rock Island Trail corridor near Sand Creek should anticipate some disruption in that area while the bridge is under construction.

The project is funded through a combination of PPRTA II (Pikes Peak Rural Transportation Authority) funds, CDOT grants, and the TOPS sales tax — the community-approved funding mechanism that has supported Colorado Springs trail expansion for years. This is exactly the kind of infrastructure investment that makes Colorado Springs one of the more trail-connected cities along the Front Range.

April Is Child Abuse Prevention Month

April is designated Child Abuse Prevention Month in the United States, and Colorado Springs has resources for families who need them — or who want to be part of the community response to this issue.

The Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, at 1-800-422-4453. Calls are confidential and can connect you with crisis intervention, information, and referrals to local emergency, social service, and support resources. If you have a concern about a child's safety — whether it's your own child, a neighbor's, or a child in your community — this is the number to call.

Children's Hospital Colorado is also an active presence in the Colorado Springs community and maintains resources for families dealing with a wide range of difficult situations, including child abuse concerns.

Talking to Your Kids About Body Safety

Child Abuse Prevention Month is also a good prompt to revisit body safety conversations with your children. These conversations don't have to be heavy or frightening — in fact, the most effective approach with young children is matter-of-fact and empowering. A few things worth covering with age-appropriate language:

  • Bodies belong to the person who lives in them.
  • No one should touch private parts without a medical reason and without a trusted adult present.
  • If something doesn't feel right, it's always okay to say no and tell a trusted adult.
  • Keeping secrets that feel bad is different from keeping surprise secrets that feel good.

The Darkness to Light organization (d2l.org) offers age-appropriate talking points and a family safety planning guide for parents who want structured support for these conversations.

What's Next

The Rock Island Trail bridge project will move forward through construction over the coming year. The TOPS program publishes progress updates at coloradosprings.gov/TOPS2026 for anyone who wants to follow along. When the bridge opens — expected in spring or summer of 2027 — it will represent a meaningful improvement to trail connectivity in a part of Colorado Springs that families and commuters have been asking about for years.

Child Abuse Prevention Month runs through April 30. The resources listed in this article are available year-round. If you're involved in schools, faith communities, or other organizations that work with children in Colorado Springs, April is a good time to share information about prevention, recognition, and reporting with your network.
