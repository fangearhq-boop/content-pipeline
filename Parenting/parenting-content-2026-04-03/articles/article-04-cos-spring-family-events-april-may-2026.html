COS Spring Family Events: Bear Creek Toddler Class, Wickedly Broadway, Star Wars Live, and More

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 3, 2026

Easter is behind us. Here's what's coming up for Colorado Springs families in April and May — from a $3 toddler nature class to a Star Wars live-to-film experience at Pikes Peak Center.

April 10: Bear Creek Toddler Nature Class

For the youngest nature explorers in the family, the Bear Creek Nature Center is hosting a Toddler Nature Class at Fountain Creek Nature Center on April 10. The class is designed for ages 2 to 3 and runs twice that day — a morning session from 10 to 11:15 a.m. and an afternoon session from 2 to 3:15 p.m. Cost is $3 per person.

Bear Creek's toddler programs are well-regarded for combining hands-on outdoor activity with age-appropriate learning. If you have a little one who has been excited about spring and wants to explore creek-side habitat, this is one of the better-priced nature programs in the city for that age group. Preregistration is recommended.

April 10–11: Wickedly Broadway at Pikes Peak Center

The Colorado Springs Philharmonic brings "Wickedly Broadway" to Pikes Peak Center on April 10 and 11. The program celebrates Broadway's great villains — featuring music from Wicked, Phantom of the Opera, and other beloved productions. For families with older kids, tweens, or teenagers who have a musical theater interest (or who have spent any time with the Wicked soundtrack), this is a strong spring night out.

The Philharmonic's Broadway-focused performances consistently sell well — booking ahead is worth doing if you're interested.

April 17: FREE ASL Literacy Event at Bear Creek Nature Center

The standout free event of the month is the Early Childhood Literacy Event at Bear Creek Nature Center on April 17, from 1:30 to 3:00 p.m. This event is co-hosted by the Colorado School for the Deaf and the Blind (CSDB) and is designed for children ages birth through 8.

Families who attend will read a book together in American Sign Language, learn about local Colorado wildlife, and take a short nature walk. The event is free to attend and is specifically designed to be inclusive and welcoming for families of all abilities — including ASL-using families and families connected to the Colorado Springs deaf and hard-of-hearing community.

If you have a young child and want one event to put on the April calendar, this one is hard to beat: free, educational, inclusive, and outdoors in the spring. Share it with anyone who might appreciate it.

May 8–9: Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Back Live at Pikes Peak Center

Looking a little further ahead into May, the Colorado Springs Philharmonic is presenting Star Wars: The Empire Strikes Back live-to-film at Pikes Peak Center on May 8 and 9. John Williams' Grammy-winning score for The Empire Strikes Back — widely considered one of the greatest film scores ever written — will be performed live by the Philharmonic while the film plays on a large screen.

This type of live-to-film concert is a genuinely special experience, and The Empire Strikes Back is the kind of film that holds up for kids and adults alike. If you have a Star Wars family, this is a May event worth planning around now before tickets get tight.

Coming in May: Garden of the Gods Art Festival

For families who prefer outdoor events with a creative focus, the Garden of the Gods Art Festival takes place May 16 and 17 at Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site, from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Now in its third year, the festival features 150 juried fine artists, live music, and activities specifically designed for children — all set against one of Colorado Springs' most iconic natural backdrops. Admission information was not confirmed at press time; check Rock Ledge Ranch or VisitCOS.com for details as the date approaches.

What's Next

Colorado Springs has a strong calendar of family events building toward the summer. For the most current listings, VisitCOS.com and the individual venue websites (Pikes Peak Center, Bear Creek Nature Center, Rock Ledge Ranch) are the most reliable sources. COS Parenting will continue covering upcoming events as the season progresses.
