NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ``` COS parents, two water product alerts you need to know about before spring really gets going. 💧 The most urgent: the CPSC issued a warning on April 2nd to immediately stop using "Relaxing Baby" branded swim floats for infants and toddlers. One child — a 2-year-old — died. The floats can flip over and submerge a child underwater. The manufacturer based in China has not cooperated with the CPSC, which means there's no formal refund program. The action is simple but serious: puncture the float so it can never be inflated again, then throw it away. If you bought this product on Amazon or eBay and it's sitting in a garage waiting for summer, please check it now. The second recall is the YCXXKJ baby bath seat sold on Amazon by a seller called BenTalk. About 8,960 of these seats have been recalled because they can tip over during bath time — a drowning risk. They come in blue, gray, pink, and yellow, with little duck, turtle, or whale toys and four suction cups on the bottom. If you have one, stop using it. You can get a full refund by writing "Recalled" on the front in permanent marker, photographing it, and emailing two photos to bentalkhere@outlook.com. Water safety matters year-round, but especially as we head into the warmer months. Please share this post with anyone who might need it. 🙏 Have you checked the CPSC.gov recalls page recently? ``` ### Image Post Caption #### Image Caption -- 8:30 AM MT ``` CPSC water safety alert: "Relaxing Baby" swim floats linked to 1 toddler death. AAP Breaks with CDC on Childhood Vaccines — What COS Parents Need to Know [TIER 1]
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ``` If you've been hearing confusing things about childhood vaccines lately — you're not imagining it. Here's what's actually going on, in plain language. The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) has published its own 2026 Recommended Childhood and Adolescent Immunization Schedule in the journal Pediatrics. It recommends vaccination against 18 diseases. That puts it at odds with the revised CDC schedule (shaped by RFK Jr.'s restructuring of the vaccine advisory committee), which moved to recommending only 11 routine vaccinations. Six vaccines — Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, rotavirus, flu/influenza, RSV, and meningococcal disease — were moved from "routine" recommendations to something called "shared clinical decision-making." The AAP has called those changes "dangerous and unnecessary." Here's where it stands legally: on March 16, a U.S. District Court judge blocked the CDC's new vaccine schedule, ruling that the restructured advisory committee was "unlawfully constituted" and that its decisions were "arbitrary and capricious." The June 2024 CDC schedule has been restored while litigation continues. The AAP was one of the plaintiffs in that case. What does this mean for your family? The most practical thing you can do is ask your pediatrician which schedule they're following and whether any of your child's upcoming vaccines might be affected. Most AAP-member pediatricians will follow the AAP's broader schedule. Insurance coverage for some vaccines may also be in flux — worth a direct call to your provider. This is genuinely confusing, and we know it. We're here to help cut through the noise. What questions do you have about the vaccine schedule changes? ``` ### Image Post Caption #### Image Caption -- 10:30 AM MT ``` AAP vs. CDC: two different 2026 childhood vaccine schedules. ### Long-Form Post ``` If your kids are in District 49 — or you're a D49 teacher or staff member — this is a story worth understanding fully. 🏫 D49 declared fiscal exigency in January 2026. That's a formal declaration of financial emergency. The district is navigating a $9–11 million budget gap driven by declining enrollment, the defunding of mill levy equalization, rising healthcare costs, and inflation. The board approved cutting approximately 50 positions — teachers and administrators. But a KRDO investigative report from March adds an important layer. KRDO found that D49 administrators made a $2 million accounting error in a state scheduling audit — and paid back $2,040,128.86 to the state in June 2025. Shortly after, the district proceeded with $1.8 million in teacher salary cuts. The teachers' union publicly challenged the sequencing. We're not here to take sides — but if you're a D49 parent trying to understand why 50 positions are being cut and what the full picture looks like, the KRDO investigation is worth reading. The Gazette and Rocky Mountain PBS have also covered the budget crisis in depth. Here's the fuller picture: D49 is still planning to open two new full-day preschool classrooms at the D49 Student Success Center this August. Rock Island Trail Gets New Bridge + April Is Child Abuse Prevention Month [TIER 3]
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ``` Two things worth knowing as April gets underway in Colorado Springs. First, the good news for families who use the Rock Island Trail: the City of Colorado Springs is launching construction this spring on a 210-foot pedestrian bridge over Sand Creek. The bridge will connect the east and west sides of the trail, which currently requires navigating a gap in the trail system. The project also includes a new pedestrian-activated flashing crossing at Peterson Road and more than 2 miles of new trails. Construction is expected to take 10 to 12 months — so there may be some disruption near the Sand Creek crossing area while work is underway. This project is funded by PPRTA II, CDOT grants, and the TOPS sales tax that COS voters approved. It's a meaningful trail upgrade for families who bike, walk, and run this corridor. Second: April is Child Abuse Prevention Month. We don't always love talking about hard things, but this one matters. If you or someone you know needs to report a concern or find support, the Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week at 1-800-422-4453. Children's Hospital Colorado is also an active resource for Colorado Springs families dealing with difficult situations. This is also a good month to have conversations with your kids about body safety, trusted adults, and how to speak up when something doesn't feel right. Colorado Springs has good resources. You don't have to navigate these conversations alone.
