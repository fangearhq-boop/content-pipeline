### STORY 2: AAP Breaks with CDC on Childhood Vaccines — What COS Parents Need to Know [TIER 1]
The American Academy of Pediatrics published its own 2026 Recommended Childhood and Adolescent Immunization Schedule in the journal Pediatrics, recommending vaccination against 18 diseases. This breaks a decades-long collaboration with the CDC, whose revised schedule (under RFK Jr.'s direction) recommends only 11 routine vaccinations. Six vaccines were moved from CDC "routine" to "shared clinical decision-making": Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, rotavirus, flu/influenza, RSV, and meningococcal disease. On March 16, 2026, a U.S. District Court judge blocked the CDC vaccine schedule changes, finding the new ACIP committee was "unlawfully constituted" and restoring the June 2024 CDC schedule pending litigation. The AAP was among the plaintiffs.

**Why it matters:** This is one of the most significant national children's health stories of 2026, with direct implications for what COS families hear from their pediatricians. The legal situation is complex and parents need a clear, accurate explanation of what happened and what it means for their child's care.
