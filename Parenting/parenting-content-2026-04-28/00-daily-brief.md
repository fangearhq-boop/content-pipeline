### STORY 1: CPSC Warns Parents to Destroy "Relaxing Baby" Swim Floats — One Child Has Died
- **Tier:** 1
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety/Recall)
- **Platform(s):** X, Facebook, Article
- **Topic:** The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission issued a warning (April 2, 2026) urging parents to immediately destroy "Relaxing Baby" brand inflatable swim floats after a 2-year-old child died. The floats — sold on Amazon and eBay for children ages 3–36 months — can flip over and submerge the child underwater. The seller, Wu He/Relaxing Baby of China, has been unresponsive to CPSC's recall requests, so CPSC issued a direct consumer warning instead of a formal recall. Action required: puncture the float in any location where air is held, then throw it away. Do not sell or donate.
- **Why it matters:** One child death reported; pool season approaching; Amazon-sold product means high reach; direct CPSC action (not a voluntary recall) signals severity.

- **Content Pillar:** Local News
- **Platform(s):** X, Facebook, Article

### STORY 5: Consistent Bedtime Routine = One Hour More Sleep Per Night, Research Shows
- **Tier:** 2
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Platform(s):** X, Facebook, Article
- **Topic:** A multinational research study found that children with a consistent nightly bedtime routine sleep more than one hour longer per night than children with no routine. The effect is dose-dependent: the more consistently the routine is applied, the better the sleep outcomes. Key outcomes of a regular routine include earlier bedtimes, shorter time to fall asleep, fewer night wakings, and longer total sleep duration. Pediatric sleep experts recommend: dim lights, close blinds, read stories — and start early. The AAP's 2026 framework shift for family media now emphasizes timing and context of screen use, reinforcing the importance of screen-free windows before bed.
- **Why it matters:** Actionable, relatable, universally applicable parenting research; strong engagement hook (parents who struggle with bedtime); connects to AAP guidance update.
