CPSC Warns Parents to Destroy "Relaxing Baby" Swim Floats — One Child Has Died
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post If you've ever bought a swim float for a baby or toddler on Amazon, this is worth a minute of your time. The CPSC is warning parents to immediately stop using and destroy "Relaxing Baby" brand inflatable swim floats. These are blue and green floats intended for children ages 3 to 36 months — the ones with a center seat secured with a buckle. The CPSC is aware of a drowning death of a 2-year-old child who was using one. Here's what makes this warning different from a typical recall: the seller, Wu He/Relaxing Baby of China, has been completely unresponsive to CPSC's requests to issue a formal recall. So instead of a standard recall notice, CPSC went directly to consumers with a warning. What to do right now: puncture the float in any location where air is held, and throw it away. Do not donate it. Do not sell it. Just destroy it. The floats have been sold on Amazon.com and eBay. If you're not sure whether you have one, check your Amazon order history or look at the float itself — the brand is "Relaxing Baby" and it's blue and green. With pool season coming up in Colorado Springs, it's worth checking your gear before the first swim day. ### Long-Form Post Here's a D11 story worth celebrating. Fifth-grade students at Howbert Elementary School — a Colorado Springs D11 Outdoor Education school — released trout into Fountain Creek yesterday morning at Manitou Springs Memorial Park. The release happened at 10:15 AM, and it marked the end of a six-month project these students began back in October. Since late fall, the class has been raising trout from eggs as part of the Trout in the Classroom program, a partnership with Colorado Trout Unlimited. Over the course of the school year, students cared for the fish while learning about ecosystems, water quality, conservation, and what it means to be stewards of a natural resource. Monday's release was the payoff moment — watching fish they'd raised from eggs find their way into Fountain Creek. Programs like this don't just teach science. They build the kind of connection to the natural world that stays with kids for a long time. Colorado Springs is lucky to have it. Consistent Bedtime Routine = One Hour More Sleep Per Night, Research Shows
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post If you've been meaning to get more serious about your kids' bedtime routine, here's the data that might finally make it stick. Researchers conducting a multinational study found that children with a consistent nightly bedtime routine sleep more than one hour longer per night than children with no routine. That's not a small difference — it's a full hour, night after night. And the relationship is dose-dependent: children who had a routine every night slept significantly more than those who had it occasionally, who in turn slept more than those with none at all. The more consistent you are, the better the outcome. What counts as a bedtime routine? The research points to the basics: dim the lights, close the blinds, read a story, keep it the same each night. Starting early — ideally in infancy — leads to better outcomes years down the road. This pairs with the AAP's 2026 framework shift on screen use, which now emphasizes timing over total quantity. The recommendation isn't "no screens" — it's "create a screen-free wind-down window before bed." The routine signals to your child's brain that sleep is coming. What does your family's bedtime routine look like — and does it actually work? --- #### Image Caption -- 5:00 PM MT ### Image Post Caption Research: kids with a consistent nightly bedtime routine sleep more than 1 hour longer per night than those with no routine.
