### STORY 1: CPSC "Relaxing Baby" Swim Float Warning

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**

If you've ever bought a swim float for a baby or toddler on Amazon, this is worth a minute of your time.

The CPSC is warning parents to immediately stop using and destroy "Relaxing Baby" brand inflatable swim floats. These are blue and green floats intended for children ages 3 to 36 months — the ones with a center seat secured with a buckle. The CPSC is aware of a drowning death of a 2-year-old child who was using one.

Here's what makes this warning different from a typical recall: the seller, Wu He/Relaxing Baby of China, has been completely unresponsive to CPSC's requests to issue a formal recall. So instead of a standard recall notice, CPSC went directly to consumers with a warning.

What to do right now: puncture the float in any location where air is held, and throw it away. Do not donate it. Do not sell it. Just destroy it.

The floats have been sold on Amazon.com and eBay. If you're not sure whether you have one, check your Amazon order history or look at the float itself — the brand is "Relaxing Baby" and it's blue and green.

With pool season coming up in Colorado Springs, it's worth checking your gear before the first swim day.

### STORY 2: Elevate Trampoline Park Opens in COS

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

Colorado Springs families, there's a new indoor spot worth checking out.

Elevate Trampoline Park opened its first Colorado location on April 18 at 2512 Airport Rd. — the former trampoline park site that's been redone as a 31,000-square-foot activity center.

There are trampolines, a basketball court, and dodgeball areas. But the thing kids are reportedly most excited about is the "pixel floor" — a massive interactive display built into the floor itself that turns the ground into a game. Think augmented reality meets physical play, like a floor-is-lava game run by a computer.

Elevate is open year-round, which makes it one of the better options for the shoulder seasons when you need to get energy out indoors. They also have birthday party packages and group event options.

The chain has been operating for more than 11 years and has 14 locations across the country. Colorado Springs is their newest addition.

### STORY 3: D11 Howbert Elementary Trout Release

#### Long-Form Post -- 12:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (12:00 PM MT)**

Here's a D11 story worth celebrating.

Fifth-grade students at Howbert Elementary School — a Colorado Springs D11 Outdoor Education school — released trout into Fountain Creek yesterday morning at Manitou Springs Memorial Park. The release happened at 10:15 AM, and it marked the end of a six-month project these students began back in October.

Since late fall, the class has been raising trout from eggs as part of the Trout in the Classroom program, a partnership with Colorado Trout Unlimited. Over the course of the school year, students cared for the fish while learning about ecosystems, water quality, conservation, and what it means to be stewards of a natural resource.

Monday's release was the payoff moment — watching fish they'd raised from eggs find their way into Fountain Creek.

Programs like this don't just teach science. They build the kind of connection to the natural world that stays with kids for a long time. Colorado Springs is lucky to have it.

### STORY 4: COS Parks Spring Updates

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM MT)**

If your family spends time on Colorado Springs trails, here's an update on what's coming this season.

The City has announced several improvements as part of its 2026 parks, trails, and open space plan. Here's what families should know:

The Rock Island Trail is getting a 210-foot pedestrian bridge over Sand Creek, plus a new pedestrian-activated flashing crossing at Peterson Road. The project is starting this spring and will take approximately 10–12 months to complete. During construction, some sections of the trail may be affected.

The Pikes Peak Greenway corridor near downtown is also being improved — about half a mile of new concrete trail surface and better ADA access points are being built this year.

And if you love Garden of the Gods: the park is adding a water feature — a gently winding water route with family-friendly features — that's expected to open before summer. The city hasn't announced an exact date yet, but they've said ahead of summer.

These are genuine quality-of-life improvements for families who use these spaces. Good news for an outdoor city.

### STORY 5: Bedtime Routine Research

#### Long-Form Post -- 5:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM MT)**

If you've been meaning to get more serious about your kids' bedtime routine, here's the data that might finally make it stick.

Researchers conducting a multinational study found that children with a consistent nightly bedtime routine sleep more than one hour longer per night than children with no routine. That's not a small difference — it's a full hour, night after night.

And the relationship is dose-dependent: children who had a routine every night slept significantly more than those who had it occasionally, who in turn slept more than those with none at all. The more consistent you are, the better the outcome.

What counts as a bedtime routine? The research points to the basics: dim the lights, close the blinds, read a story, keep it the same each night. Starting early — ideally in infancy — leads to better outcomes years down the road.

This pairs with the AAP's 2026 framework shift on screen use, which now emphasizes timing over total quantity. The recommendation isn't "no screens" — it's "create a screen-free wind-down window before bed." The routine signals to your child's brain that sleep is coming.
