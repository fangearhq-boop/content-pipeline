### STORY 5: Bedtime Routine Research

**Sources:**
- AASM (American Academy of Sleep Medicine): aasm.org/study-shows-that-children-sleep-better-when-they-have-a-nightly-bedtime-routine/
- CNN Health: cnn.com/2026/03/05/health/kids-parents-family-sleep-wellness
- Frontiers in Sleep (bedtime routine studies, 2023+)
- AAP HealthyChildren.org: healthychildren.org/English/family-life/Media/Pages/helping-kids-thrive-in-a-digital-world-AAP-policy-explained.aspx
- EdSurge: edsurge.com/news/2026-02-05-new-aap-screen-time-recommendations-focus-less-on-screens-more-on-family-time

**Verified Facts:**
- Children with a consistent nightly bedtime routine sleep more than 1 hour longer per night vs. children with no routine [HIGH — AASM]
- Effect is dose-dependent: more consistent = better outcomes [HIGH — AASM, PMC research]
- Positive outcomes include: earlier bedtimes, shorter sleep onset latency, fewer night wakings, longer total duration [HIGH — AASM]
- Starting routines early (infancy) produces better long-term outcomes [HIGH — PMC/AASM]
- 95% of caregivers agree good sleep is essential to family function [MEDIUM — search result summary]
- AAP 2026 framework shift: moved away from strict time limits toward quality, context, and timing of media use [HIGH — EdSurge, AAP website]
- AAP's 5 Cs framework (2026) replaces time-based rules [MEDIUM — EdSurge]
- Routine elements recommended: dim lights, close blinds, read stories [HIGH — expert consensus from multiple sources]
