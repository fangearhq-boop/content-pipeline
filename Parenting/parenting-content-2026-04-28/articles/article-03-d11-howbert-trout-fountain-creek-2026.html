D11 Fifth Graders Release Trout They Raised From Eggs Into Fountain Creek

By Marcus Rivera | COS Parenting | April 28, 2026

On Monday morning at 10:15 AM, fifth-grade students from Howbert Elementary School in Colorado Springs District 11 released trout into Fountain Creek at Manitou Springs Memorial Park. The fish had spent the previous six months in the students' classroom — raised from eggs since late October 2025 as part of the Trout in the Classroom program.

It is the kind of payoff moment that hands-on education is designed to produce: children who spent a school year caring for living animals, learning why healthy waterways matter, and then standing at the edge of a creek watching what they raised swim away.

Trout in the Classroom is a national environmental education program that places fish tanks in classrooms and tasks students with raising trout from eggs through the development stages, ultimately releasing the fish into a natural waterway. In Colorado, the program is supported by Colorado Trout Unlimited, which provides materials, expertise, and guidance to participating schools.

Howbert Elementary is a D11 Outdoor Education school, which means environmental learning is built into its identity. The TIC program is a natural fit — it connects classroom science learning to a living, breathing project that students can observe, care for, and ultimately return to the wild.

Why This Kind of Learning Matters

There is research behind why programs like Trout in the Classroom stick with students. When children are responsible for the wellbeing of something living — something that requires daily attention, responds to the quality of its environment, and depends on the students for its survival — they develop a relationship with the subject that a textbook cannot replicate.

Conservation education works the same way. Understanding that a creek's health depends on factors like water temperature, sediment levels, and upstream land use is one thing in a classroom. Watching trout you raised from eggs swim into Fountain Creek — into water that flows through your city — makes it concrete.

Fountain Creek runs through the heart of the Colorado Springs region. It has faced water quality challenges over the years, and local conservation organizations have invested significantly in restoration work. Releasing trout into it is both symbolic and practical: healthy fish populations are an indicator of ecosystem health.

What's Next

The Howbert Elementary class has completed this year's Trout in the Classroom cycle. For families interested in learning more about the program — or in advocating for it in other D11 or COS-area schools — Colorado Trout Unlimited's Colorado chapter can connect schools with the resources needed to participate. The program is designed to be replicable, and many of the students who experience it go on to carry their conservation interest into the next stage of their lives.

If you saw the release Monday morning, or if your family has participated in a similar program, COS Parenting would love to hear about it.
