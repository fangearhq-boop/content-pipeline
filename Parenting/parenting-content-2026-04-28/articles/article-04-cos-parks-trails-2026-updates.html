Colorado Springs Parks and Trails Getting Major 2026 Upgrades — What Families Should Know

By Keisha Williams | COS Parenting | April 28, 2026

Colorado Springs families who spend time on the city's trails and open spaces will see meaningful improvements this year. The city has outlined several 2026 projects as part of its parks, trails, and open space plan — and three of them are directly relevant to families planning outdoor activities between now and the end of the year.

Rock Island Trail: Pedestrian Bridge and New Crossing

The Rock Island Trail project is the most substantial of the three. A 210-foot pedestrian bridge will be installed over Sand Creek, along with a pedestrian-activated flashing crossing at Peterson Road. The project is anticipated to begin this spring and take approximately 10 to 12 months to complete.

The Rock Island Trail is a paved urban trail that runs through southeast Colorado Springs and connects to several neighborhoods and parks. The new bridge will close a key gap in the trail's continuity over Sand Creek — a crossing that has required detours or unimproved crossing options in the past. The flashing crossing at Peterson Road improves safety at one of the trail's street-level crossings.

During construction, sections of the trail near the active work zones may be closed or detoured. Families who use this trail regularly should check the city's parks website for current trail status before heading out.

Pikes Peak Greenway: New Surface and Better Access

The Pikes Peak Greenway corridor near downtown is also getting attention this year. The city plans to remove deteriorating asphalt and replace it with approximately 0.5 miles of new concrete trail, along with improved ADA access points. The Greenway is one of Colorado Springs' most-used paved trails, running along Fountain Creek through downtown and connecting to the Legacy Loop system.

For families who walk, run, or bike on the Greenway, the new surface will be a meaningful upgrade — deteriorating asphalt creates uneven surfaces that can be challenging for strollers, adaptive equipment, and small bikes. The ADA improvements also make the trail more accessible for families with members who use mobility devices.

Garden of the Gods: Water Feature Coming Before Summer

The most notable family-friendly addition to Colorado Springs parks this year may be at Garden of the Gods. The city has announced that a water feature is being added to the park — a gently winding water route with designated entry points, scenic floating areas, and family-friendly features. The city says it is anticipated to open before Summer 2026.

Garden of the Gods is already one of Colorado Springs' most-visited family destinations and is free to enter. Adding a water feature ahead of the summer season gives families another reason to make it a regular stop. Exact details on the water feature — its location within the park, age suitability, and operating guidelines — have not yet been released, but the city's announcement is a sign that it's coming soon.
