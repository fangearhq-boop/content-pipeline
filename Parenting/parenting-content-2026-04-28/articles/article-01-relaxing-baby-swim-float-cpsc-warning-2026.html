CPSC Warns Parents to Destroy "Relaxing Baby" Swim Floats — One Child Has Died

By Jenna Park | COS Parenting | April 28, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission is warning parents to stop using and immediately destroy a brand of inflatable baby swim float after a 2-year-old child died. The products — sold under the "Relaxing Baby" name on Amazon.com and eBay — can flip over and submerge the child wearing them, creating a drowning risk for infants and toddlers.

This is not a typical recall. The seller, Wu He/Relaxing Baby, based in China, has been unresponsive to CPSC's requests to issue a formal recall. Because of that, the agency went directly to consumers with an urgent safety warning — a step the CPSC takes when a company refuses to cooperate. If you have one of these floats, the agency's instruction is simple: destroy it and throw it away now.

Why This Warning Is Different

Most product recalls happen through a voluntary process: a company cooperates with the CPSC, issues a recall, and provides a remedy (a refund, a replacement, or a repair). When a company refuses to cooperate — as Wu He/Relaxing Baby has done — the CPSC has the option to issue a warning directly to consumers rather than a formal recall. That is what has happened here.

Because there is no formal recall, there is no refund process and no company-run return system. The CPSC's instruction is straightforward: puncture the float in any location where it holds air, and throw it away. Do not give it to someone else. Do not donate it. Do not list it for resale.

The floats are blue and green inflatable rings with a center seat secured by a buckle, designed for children ages 3 to 36 months. Some models include a detachable rear float. The hazard is that the product can tip or flip, placing the child's head underwater.

What Colorado Springs Families Should Do

Pool season arrives early in Colorado Springs when the weather cooperates, and many families are already beginning to pull out water gear and check equipment. If you purchased a swim float for an infant or toddler on Amazon in the last couple of years, check whether it is a "Relaxing Baby" product.

Look at the float itself and at your Amazon order history. The brand name is "Relaxing Baby," and the product is blue and green. If you find one, do not put it in storage for later — the CPSC's guidance is to destroy it now and not allow it back into use.

For families who need a replacement infant swim float, the CPSC recommends looking for products that meet the mandatory standards for infant swim floats and that are sold by companies with U.S.-based customer service and a cooperation history with safety regulators. Check cpsc.gov for updated guidance on compliant products.

What's Next

The CPSC continues to monitor the situation. If Wu He/Relaxing Baby eventually cooperates with a formal recall, the terms of any remedy would be announced at that time. For now, the agency's recommendation remains the same: destroy the float immediately and visit cpsc.gov for the full warning details.

As Colorado Springs families prepare for summer water activities, this is also a good moment to review the safety standards on all infant and toddler swim gear — not just this product. The CPSC maintains a searchable recall and warning database at cpsc.gov where parents can check any product by brand or category.
