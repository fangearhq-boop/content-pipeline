Elevate Trampoline Park Opens in Colorado Springs — What Families Need to Know

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting | April 28, 2026

Colorado Springs has a new indoor family destination. Elevate Trampoline Park opened its first Colorado location on April 18 at 2512 Airport Rd. — the 31,000-square-foot facility gives COS families an option for year-round active play, birthday parties, and group outings regardless of the weather outside.

The chain has operated for more than 11 years and has 14 locations across the country. Colorado Springs is the newest addition and the first location in the state.

The facility features the activity areas you'd expect from a large indoor trampoline park — connected trampoline courts, a basketball area for aerial slam dunks, and dodgeball courts. What's drawing particular attention is the "pixel floor" — an interactive display system built into the floor itself that responds to movement and turns the ground into an augmented reality game environment.

The concept works something like this: the floor lights up with game graphics and prompts, and children respond physically — jumping, running, dodging — to interact with what the floor is showing. It combines screen-based gaming logic with full-body physical play, which makes it genuinely novel in a way that standard trampoline park equipment is not.

Year-Round Indoor Play in Colorado Springs

Colorado Springs weather is variable, and the shoulder seasons — late fall through early spring — can limit outdoor options for families with high-energy kids. Elevate is designed for year-round use, which addresses a genuine gap in the COS family activity landscape.

The facility is also set up for birthday party packages and group events, including school field trips. For families planning spring birthday parties or looking for a field trip destination for a class or team, it's worth checking the booking options on their site.

A Few Things to Know Before You Go

As with any trampoline facility, parents should check whether grip socks are required (most facilities require them and sell them on-site if you don't have any). Age and weight restrictions vary by activity area — the Elevate website has current pricing and any activity-specific requirements.

It's also worth knowing that the April 18 grand opening included discounted founder memberships and promotional birthday packages. If you missed that window, regular pricing and membership options are available through their website.

What's Next

Elevate Trampoline Park is open now at 2512 Airport Rd. in Colorado Springs. For hours, pricing, and party booking, visit elevatetrampolinepark.com/colorado-springs. As the facility settles in and staff gets to know the COS community, expect programming to expand — field trips, seasonal events, and partnerships with local schools and groups are all part of how these locations typically grow into their communities.
