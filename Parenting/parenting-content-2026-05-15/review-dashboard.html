Colorado SUN Bucks — $120 Per Eligible COS Child Arriving This Month
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post If you have kids in Colorado Springs who qualify for free or reduced-price school meals — or if your family receives SNAP or Colorado Works — $120 in summer food benefits is likely heading your way right now. 🛒 Colorado's SUN Bucks program (Summer EBT) issues $120 per eligible child each summer to help families cover grocery costs when school is out. With D11 ending May 22 and summer officially starting in a week, the timing matters. Benefits are being issued this month. Here's how it works: For most eligible families, enrollment is automatic. If your child qualifies through the National School Lunch Program, or your household already receives SNAP or Colorado Works benefits, you likely don't need to do anything. Returning participants will see $120 added to their existing EBT card at the end of May. New participants will receive an EBT card in the mail — sent to the address currently on file with the program. The benefits can be used at any grocery store or retailer that accepts EBT, and they're good for 122 days (about four months) from the date of issue. That covers the full summer and then some. If you're not sure whether your child qualifies, or if your mailing address has changed, visit cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt or call 1-888-328-2656 to check. Some families may need to apply directly. Has your family used SUN Bucks before, or is this the first year you've heard about it? ### Image Post Caption Colorado SUN Bucks (Summer EBT): $120 per eligible child for summer groceries — benefits arriving this month. Most families are auto-enrolled. Check cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt to confirm eligibility. 🛒

NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Memorial Day weekend is shaping up to be the busiest weekend of the COS spring — and if you're looking for a more relaxed, nature-based option with the kids, MeadowGrass Music Festival is worth your attention. 🎵 The 17th Annual MeadowGrass Music Festival runs Friday through Sunday, May 22–24 at La Foret Conference and Retreat Center in Black Forest (6145 Shoup Road — about 30 minutes from downtown Colorado Springs). Kids 12 and under get in free with a paid adult, and weekend passes for adults start at $55. Day passes are available if you don't want to commit to the full weekend. The festival has a laid-back, family-friendly feel — folk, roots, and Americana music on multiple stages, gourmet food vendors, art installations, and workshops throughout the weekend. For families, the Faricy Boys Kid Zone is a big draw: think giant bubbles from Milibo Art Theater, hands-on crafts from Concrete Couch, hay wagon rides, kid yoga sessions, and art activities designed to keep younger attendees engaged between sets. Camping is available in the Ponderosa Forest on-site if you want to make a full weekend of it. Worth noting: this overlaps with Territory Days in Old Colorado City (also Memorial Day weekend, also free), so you've got real options depending on your family's vibe. Meadowgrass is more outdoors/nature; Territory Days is more street festival energy. Are you going to Meadowgrass this year, or does Territory Days have your Memorial Day weekend locked? ### Image Post Caption 17th Annual MeadowGrass Music Festival: May 22–24 at La Foret in Black Forest. Kids 12 & under FREE with a paid adult. Kid Zone, live music, crafts, hay wagon, camping. Weekend passes from $55. 🎵

PPLD Summer Adventure 2026 — Free Sign-Ups Start June 1
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Summer is almost here — and one of the best free activities in Colorado Springs for kids (and adults) officially kicks off June 1. 📚 The Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program is back for 2026, presented by the Friends of the Pikes Peak Library District. The program is simple: read books and complete activities, earn prizes. That's it. And it's open to all ages — littles, tweens, teens, and grown-ups are all welcome to participate. Sign-up starts June 1 at any of PPLD's 14 branches across the Pikes Peak region. You'll need a library card, and if you don't have one, you can get one for free at any branch. Prizes are picked up at library locations throughout the summer. Beyond the reading challenge itself, PPLD runs special summer events and programs alongside the challenge — storytimes, maker activities, teen programs, and more. Check ppld.org or call (719) 531-6333 for the full summer schedule once it's live. A library card is one of those things that can open up a whole summer of free activities in COS — books, e-books, audiobooks, museum passes, and now the Summer Adventure. If your kids don't have one yet, June 1 is a great day to fix that. Is your family doing a summer reading program this year — PPLD or otherwise? ### Image Post Caption Free summer fun at PPLD: the Summer Adventure reading and activities program sign-up starts June 1 at all 14 Pikes Peak Library District locations. All ages, all free (library card required). ppld.org for details. 📚
