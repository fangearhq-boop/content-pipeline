MeadowGrass Music Festival Returns Memorial Day Weekend — Kids Under 12 Free at La Foret

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 15, 2026

Memorial Day weekend in Colorado Springs comes loaded with options this year, and the 17th Annual MeadowGrass Music Festival is one of the most family-friendly among them. The festival runs Friday through Sunday, May 22–24, at La Foret Conference and Retreat Center in Black Forest, about 30 minutes from downtown Colorado Springs. Kids 12 and under get in free with a paid adult.

MeadowGrass brings together folk, roots, and Americana music across multiple stages in a forested outdoor setting, surrounded by ponderosa pines on the east side of Colorado Springs. The vibe is relaxed and low-key — blankets on the grass, food vendors, art installations, and space for kids to roam between sets.

The Kid Zone

The Faricy Boys Kid Zone runs throughout the weekend and gives younger attendees plenty to do between music sets. Activities include giant bubble demonstrations from Milibo Art Theater, hands-on arts and crafts with Concrete Couch, a hay wagon ride, kid yoga sessions, and open art activities. It's designed for the reality of taking younger kids to a festival: there will be moments when the music isn't what holds their attention, and the Kid Zone gives them somewhere to go.

Memorial Day Weekend Context

MeadowGrass runs the same weekend as Territory Days in Old Colorado City (also Memorial Day weekend, May 23–25, free admission), so Colorado Springs families have two solid options depending on what fits your group best. Territory Days is a street festival with 180-plus booths, food, live entertainment, a petting zoo, and gunfight reenactments along Colorado Avenue. MeadowGrass is a forested, campground-style music festival with a quieter pace and more space to spread out.

It's also worth noting that D11 school ends on Friday, May 22 — the first day of MeadowGrass — so Memorial Day weekend arrives right as the school year wraps up for many Colorado Springs families.
