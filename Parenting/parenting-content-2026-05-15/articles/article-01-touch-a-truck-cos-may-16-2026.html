Touch-A-Truck Is Tomorrow in COS — Free, Horn-Free, and Kids Can Climb Everything

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 15, 2026

The 3rd Annual Touch-A-Truck returns to Colorado Springs this Saturday, May 16, from 9:30 AM to 2:00 PM at the Norris Penrose Event Center — and it's completely free. Kids can climb on, sit in, and explore fire trucks, police cruisers, military vehicles, garbage trucks, construction equipment, and (weather permitting) helicopters.

The event is put on by the team at Norris Penrose Event Center, located at 945 Lower Gold Camp Road. This year brings a couple of meaningful updates: timed entry tickets are being used for the first time to ease parking congestion and wait times, and the event is going fully horn-free to accommodate kids with noise sensitivities.

Timed Entry and Why It Matters This Year

Claiming timed entry tickets in advance is more important this year than in previous years. The timed entry system is designed to reduce the crowding and parking issues that have complicated past Touch-A-Truck events. Tickets are free and available at norrispenrose.ticketspice.com. Without a timed ticket, families may face longer waits or limited access to certain time slots.

The horn-free designation is a genuine improvement for families whose kids struggle with unexpected loud noises. Large vehicle events can be difficult for children with sensory processing differences, anxiety, or autism spectrum conditions, and the organizers have made a deliberate choice to remove that barrier.

Kids Entrepreneur Market

Alongside the vehicles, Touch-A-Truck 2026 includes a Children's Entrepreneur Market, where young vendors from around Colorado Springs sell handmade items. It's a small but genuinely fun addition that shows kids another side of what their peers are capable of. Voluntary donations on-site benefit Friends of the Children, a Colorado Springs-based nonprofit organization focused on long-term youth mentorship.
