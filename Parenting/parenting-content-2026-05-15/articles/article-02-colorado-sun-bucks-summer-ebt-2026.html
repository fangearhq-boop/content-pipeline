Colorado SUN Bucks 2026: $120 Per Child Summer Food Benefits Arriving This Month

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 15, 2026

If your family has kids enrolled in free or reduced-price school meals — or if you receive SNAP or Colorado Works — there's $120 per eligible child heading your way this month. Colorado's SUN Bucks program (officially Summer EBT) is issuing benefits in May 2026, timed to overlap with the end of the school year and the start of summer.

With D11's last day of school coming up on May 22, the timing is more relevant than ever. Here's what Colorado Springs families need to know.

What Are SUN Bucks?

SUN Bucks is Colorado's Summer Electronic Benefits Transfer program, funded through the USDA and administered by the Colorado Department of Human Services. It provides families with $120 per eligible child on an EBT card that works just like SNAP benefits — usable at any grocery store or retailer that accepts EBT.

The benefits are valid for 122 days from the date they're issued, which covers the full summer break and then some.

Who Qualifies?

Eligibility is tied to existing programs most families in Colorado Springs are already part of:

  • Children who qualify for free or reduced-price meals through the National School Lunch Program (NSLP)
  • Families who receive SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) benefits
  • Families who receive Colorado Works (TANF) benefits

Enrollment is automatic for most eligible families. If your child is already in the NSLP program through their school, or your household receives SNAP or Colorado Works, you generally don't need to take any action.

When Will Benefits Arrive?

Returning participants — families who received SUN Bucks in a prior year — will see $120 per eligible child added to their existing EBT card at the end of May. New participants will receive a new EBT card in the mail, sent to the address currently on file with the program.

If your mailing address has changed since you last used your EBT card, or if you're unsure whether your child is enrolled, take care of that now — before benefits are issued.

What If You're Not Sure You Qualify?

Some families may need to apply directly — particularly if the child doesn't attend a school with a universal free meals program, or if there have been recent changes to household income or benefits status. The Colorado Department of Human Services website at cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt has eligibility information, a way to check your child's enrollment status, and an application for families who need to apply manually.

You can also call the SUN Bucks support center at 1-888-328-2656 or email cdhs_sebt_supportcenter@state.co.us if you have questions about your specific situation.

Why This Matters for COS Families

Summer is one of the harder months for food security in families with school-age children. School meals — which provide breakfast and lunch for thousands of D11, D20, and D49 students throughout the year — stop when school ends on May 22. SUN Bucks exists specifically to fill that gap.

The $120 per child can go a long way at any major grocery store or warehouse retailer. Families with multiple eligible children receive $120 per child, so a household with three qualifying kids could receive $360 in benefits this summer.

Colorado also has a USDA Summer Food Service Program that provides free meals at community sites throughout the summer — check coloradosprings.gov and the D11 website for locations near you. SUN Bucks and the Summer Food Service are separate programs, and you can use both.

What's Next

Benefits for returning participants are expected at the end of May — before D11's last day on May 22. If you're a new participant, watch for your EBT card in the mail. Either way, verify your eligibility and confirm your mailing address is current at cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt or call 1-888-328-2656 before benefits are issued. This is a time-sensitive step that takes less than five minutes and could mean $120 or more for your family this summer.
