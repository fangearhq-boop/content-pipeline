### STORY 2: Colorado SUN Bucks — $120 Per Eligible COS Child Arriving This Month
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** Local News / National Parenting
**Angle:** Colorado's Summer EBT program — known as SUN Bucks — is issuing $120 per eligible child this month to help families cover food costs when school is out. With D11 ending May 22, the timing is critical. Most eligible children are automatically enrolled: kids who qualify for free or reduced-price school meals, or whose families receive SNAP or Colorado Works, should receive benefits without applying. For new participants, an EBT card will be mailed to the address on file; for returning participants, $120 is added to their existing card at the end of May. Families should verify their child's eligibility now and update their mailing address if needed.
**Key Facts:**
- Colorado SUN Bucks (Summer EBT): $120 per eligible child for summer food purchases
- Benefits being issued in May 2026; returning participants get added to existing card end of May; new participants receive card by mail
- Eligible: children in NSLP free/reduced-price meal program, or whose family receives SNAP or Colorado Works
- Most eligible children automatically enrolled; some may need to apply — check cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt
- Use at any grocery store that accepts EBT; benefits expire 122 days from issue
- Contact: cdhs_sebt_supportcenter@state.co.us | 1-888-328-2656
- Source: cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt; fns.usda.gov/summer/sunbucks
