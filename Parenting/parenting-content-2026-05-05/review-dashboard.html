CPSC Recall — Vevor Baby Swings Pose Suffocation/Death Risk to Infants
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM MT Important safety alert for families with infants. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has announced the recall of approximately 1,020 Vevor baby swings sold by Sanven Technology. These swings were marketed for infant sleep — but they have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees, which violates the Safe Sleep for Babies Act and poses a serious suffocation risk. When an infant sleeps on an inclined surface, their head can tip forward and block their airway. This is a documented cause of infant deaths. The recalled models are the BB501K (white/gray swing, white base), BB702A (white/gray swing, gray base), and BB005K (black/gray swing, black base). They were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon.com from January 2025 through August 2025, priced between $65 and $80. What to do right now: 1. Stop using the swing immediately. 2. Destroy the swing cover so no one else can use it. 3. Mark the base with "RECALLED." 4. Take photos of both the cover and the base. 5. Call Sanven Technology at 855-599-6320 (Monday–Friday, 9 AM–5 PM PT) for a full refund. Please share this post. Safe sleep is flat, firm, and on their back — always. ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 8:00 AM MT RECALL: Vevor baby swings (models BB501K, BB702A, BB005K) pose a suffocation risk and violate the Safe Sleep for Babies Act. Stop use now. Call 855-599-6320 for a full refund. ⚠️

CPSC Recall — Bellabu Bear Children's Robes Violate Flammability Standards
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 10:00 AM MT Check your kids' closets this week. Bellabu Bear has recalled its line of bamboo sherpa children's robes because they don't meet federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear. That's a burn hazard — and these robes were popular gifts, often bought at Saks Fifth Avenue or online for around $60. The recalled robes come in a wide range of licensed character designs: PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Garfield, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora, and others, including non-licensed patterns like nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons. They're sold in children's sizes S/M and M/L, with "Bellabu Bear," "BB-NW12109," and the size printed on the neck label. The robes were sold from January 2024 through July 2025. If you have one of these robes: - Stop letting your child use it right away - Contact Bellabu Bear at 888-703-7752 (Monday–Friday, 9 AM–4 PM PT) - Or email help@bellabubear.com - Or visit bellabubear.com and click "Recall" at the bottom of the page - You'll receive a full refund or store credit, plus a prepaid return label This is the kind of recall that's easy to miss because the robes look perfectly fine. Please share this — especially if anyone you know got one of these as a gift. ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 10:00 AM MT Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa robes recalled for burn hazard. PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Garfield, and other character designs. Check labels: "Bellabu Bear" + "BB-NW12109." Call 888-703-7752 for a refund. ⚠️

Summer Swim Safety — What COS Families Need to Know Before the Season Opens
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 5:00 PM MT Memorial Day weekend is less than three weeks away — and that means water season in Colorado Springs is almost here. This is the time to get ahead of swim safety, not the first day of summer. Drowning is the leading cause of unintentional death among children ages 1–4 in the United States. It happens quickly and silently — it doesn't look like it does in movies. An adult can be nearby and not notice until it's too late. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends these layers of protection: 1. Swim lessons starting at age 1. Many parents wait until 3 or 4, but the AAP says swim readiness for many children starts earlier than that. 2. Four-sided pool fencing. If you have a backyard pool, it needs a 4-sided fence with a self-closing, self-latching gate — not just a fence on three sides. 3. "Touch supervision." For young children, an adult should be within arm's length at all times in or near water. Phones down. 4. Life jackets for open water. Any time kids are near natural water — lakes, rivers, reservoirs — they should be in a properly-fitted USCG-approved life jacket. In Colorado Springs, the YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region offers swim programs for all ages. COS Parks and Recreation outdoor pools open for the season later this month. Cheyenne Mountain State Park's Sage Brush Lake opens for recreational use as weather warms. What's your water safety plan for this summer? ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 5:00 PM MT Drowning is the No. 1 cause of unintentional death in kids ages 1–4. The YMCA and AAP say: get swim lessons early, use 4-sided pool fencing, and never look away. Water season starts soon in COS. 💧

D11 Reports Enrollment Increase for First Time in 12 Years
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 PM MT Here's some genuinely good news about one of Colorado Springs' school districts. Colorado Springs School District 11 has reported an enrollment increase for the first time in 12 years. The district counted 86 more students enrolled than at the same point last year. It might not sound like a lot — but in school funding, it matters significantly. Each additional student brings in per-pupil funding from the state and federal government, meaning D11 could receive nearly $1 million in additional dollars as a result of this turnaround. D11 serves central Colorado Springs and has a wide range of school options, including magnet schools, choice enrollment, and specialized programs. Nearly 3,000 students who live within D11 boundaries currently attend other districts like D20 and D49. Families who've been exploring their options for the 2026–27 school year can find enrollment and school choice information at d11.org. After more than a decade of declining enrollment, this is a meaningful data point. It suggests families are choosing to stay — or come back — to D11 schools. Were you surprised by this news? Let us know in the comments. ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 8:00 PM MT D11 enrollment is up for the first time in 12 years — 86 more students, and nearly $1 million in additional funding. Good news for Colorado Springs families who call D11 home. 📚
