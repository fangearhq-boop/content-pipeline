### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — Vevor Baby Swings Pose Suffocation/Death Risk to Infants
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Sanven Technology has recalled approximately 1,020 Vevor brand baby swings due to a deadly suffocation risk. The swings — sold in white/gray (model BB501K with white base, model BB702A with gray base) and black/gray (model BB005K with black base) — were marketed for infant sleep. They have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees, which violates the mandatory safety standard for Infant Sleep Products and the ban on inclined sleepers under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act. An inclined sleep surface can cause an infant's head to tip forward, blocking the airway. The swings were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon.com from January 2025 through August 2025 for between $65 and $80. Parents should stop use immediately, destroy the swing cover, mark the base "RECALLED," take photos, and contact Sanven Technology at 855-599-6320 (Monday–Friday, 9 AM–5 PM PT) for a full refund. No injuries reported.

### STORY 2: Garden of the Gods Art Festival — May 16–17, Free for Kids Under 12
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** Local Events
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

The 3rd Annual Garden of the Gods Art Festival takes place Saturday and Sunday, May 16–17, at Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site in Colorado Springs. Hours are 10 AM–5 PM both days. The festival features 150 juried fine artists and craftsmen presenting pottery, jewelry, textiles, paintings, sculpture, and glass art, plus live music, activities for kids, and food trucks — all against the backdrop of Pikes Peak. Admission is $10 for adults, $8 for seniors 65+ and military (with ID), and free for children under 12 and Rock Ledge Ranch members. Free shuttle service runs from Coronado High School to the festival site, 9:30 AM–5 PM both days. Well-behaved dogs on leash are welcome.

### STORY 3: CPSC Recall — Bellabu Bear Children's Robes Violate Flammability Standards
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Bellabu Bear has recalled its bamboo sherpa children's robes due to a burn hazard. The robes — sold with licensed character designs including PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Garfield, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, and Dora, among others — violate mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. They were sold in children's sizes S/M and M/L at Saks Fifth Avenue stores nationwide and online at bellabubear.com, letthembelittleboutique.com, and saksfifthavenue.com from January 2024 through July 2025 for approximately $60. Parents should immediately stop letting children use the robes and contact Bellabu Bear at 888-703-7752 (Monday–Friday, 9 AM–4 PM PT) or email help@bellabubear.com for a full refund or store credit. Bellabu Bear will provide a prepaid shipping label for returns.

### STORY 4: Summer Swim Safety — What COS Families Need to Know Before the Season Opens
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** Evergreen
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

With Memorial Day weekend less than three weeks away, the YMCA and pediatric health experts are urging families to prepare for water safety season now. Drowning is the leading cause of unintentional death among children ages 1–4 in the United States. The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) recommends that swim lessons begin for many children as young as age 1. Additional layers of protection include 4-sided pool fencing with self-closing, self-latching gates; active "touch supervision" (adult within arm's length at all times); and U.S. Coast Guard-approved life jackets for open water and boats. In Colorado Springs, the YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region offers swim programs for children of all ages, and COS Parks and Recreation outdoor pools open for the season in late May.

### STORY 5: D11 Reports Enrollment Increase for First Time in 12 Years
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** Local News
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Colorado Springs School District 11 (D11) has reported an enrollment increase for the first time in 12 years, counting 86 more students than at the same point the prior year. The increase is significant: each additional student brings in state and federal per-pupil funding, meaning the district could receive nearly $1 million in additional funding. D11 serves as the anchor school district for central Colorado Springs, and the enrollment rebound is a notable shift after more than a decade of declining numbers. Families in COS choosing their 2026–27 school district can find enrollment and choice options at d11.org.
