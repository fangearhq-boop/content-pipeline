Garden of the Gods Art Festival May 16–17: Kids Under 12 Get In Free at This Colorado Springs Weekend Event

By Marcus Rivera | COS Parenting | May 5, 2026

Colorado Springs has a genuinely stunning outdoor event coming up in less than two weeks, and families with younger children get in free. The 3rd Annual Garden of the Gods Art Festival takes place Saturday and Sunday, May 16–17, at Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site. Hours are 10 AM to 5 PM both days.

The setting alone makes this worth the trip. Rock Ledge Ranch sits at the base of the Garden of the Gods, with Pikes Peak rising behind the red rock formations that define COS's skyline. It's one of the most photographed landscapes in Colorado, and hosting 150 juried fine artists and craftsmen in front of it makes for an atmosphere unlike anything else on the spring calendar.

What to Expect

The festival is juried, meaning the 150 exhibiting artists were selected for quality — this isn't a craft fair. You'll find pottery, jewelry, textiles, paintings, sculpture, glass art, and other fine crafts from artists across the region. Live music runs throughout the day, and food trucks provide options for lunch and snacks. There are also kids activities on site, making it a complete family outing rather than just an adults-focused event.

The free shuttle from Coronado High School is worth knowing about for practical reasons. Parking near Rock Ledge Ranch can be limited when the Garden of the Gods is busy, and the shuttle removes that headache entirely. It runs from 9:30 AM to 5 PM both days, so families can arrive, enjoy a full day, and shuttle back without the parking stress.

Planning Your Visit

For families with children under 12, the free admission makes this an excellent low-cost outing. Two adults at $10 each, plus a couple of kids under 12 at no charge, means the festival is accessible without an expensive ticket commitment. Rock Ledge Ranch members get in free as well.

Given that the festival runs both Saturday and Sunday, families who'd rather avoid the crowds of opening day might find Sunday slightly less congested. The shuttle runs both days, and hours are identical. Dogs on leash are welcome — a detail that many families with pets will appreciate.

May 16 also coincides with the Touch-a-Truck event at Norris-Penrose Event Center (a free event covered separately), so there are multiple family-friendly events that weekend in COS. Families who want to do both will want to plan their timing accordingly.
