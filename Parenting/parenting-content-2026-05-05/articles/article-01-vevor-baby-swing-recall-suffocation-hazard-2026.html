CPSC Recall: Vevor Baby Swings Recalled for Suffocation Risk — What COS Families Need to Know

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting | May 5, 2026

If you have a Vevor baby swing at home — or gave one as a gift to a family with a newborn — stop using it now. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission and Sanven Technology have announced the recall of approximately 1,020 Vevor baby swings due to a deadly suffocation hazard. These swings were marketed for infant sleep but violate the Safe Sleep for Babies Act, which bans inclined sleepers for infants.

The recalled swings have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees. That incline poses a serious risk: when a young infant is placed on an angled surface, their head can tip forward due to the weight of their skull, compressing the airway. They cannot yet reposition themselves. Suffocation can occur quickly and silently. This is precisely why federal law now bans inclined sleepers — the same standard that ended production of the widely-used Fisher-Price Rock 'n Play Sleeper in 2022.

What to Do Right Now

The CPSC has outlined specific steps for families who own a recalled Vevor swing. Do not simply discard the swing without following these steps — the process is designed to confirm the recall and ensure you receive your refund.

First, stop using the swing for any purpose — especially for infant sleep. Second, destroy the swing cover so that it cannot be reused by another family or donated. Third, take the base and mark it clearly with "RECALLED" using a marker. Fourth, photograph both the destroyed cover and the marked base. Then contact Sanven Technology at 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM PT. They will process a full refund after you provide your documentation.

If the swing was purchased on Amazon, you can also report the recall and initiate the refund process through your Amazon order history.

Safe Sleep Reminders for Colorado Springs Families

The AAP recommends that infants sleep on a firm, flat surface — always on their back — in their own sleep space. This means a crib, bassinet, or play yard with a firm, flat mattress and a fitted sheet. Swings, bouncers, rockers, and car seats are not safe sleep environments, even when an infant has fallen asleep in them. If your baby falls asleep in a swing, move them to a flat surface as soon as it is safe to do so.

No inclined sleeper has been federally approved since the Safe Sleep for Babies Act took effect, and any product being marketed as safe for infant sleep at an angle should be viewed with extreme caution. If you're unsure about a product, the CPSC's SaferProducts.gov database is a good resource.

What's Next

If you have a Vevor baby swing, the steps above will connect you with Sanven Technology for a full refund. Share this information with anyone who received one of these swings as a gift — they were sold for less than a year but are out there in the community.
