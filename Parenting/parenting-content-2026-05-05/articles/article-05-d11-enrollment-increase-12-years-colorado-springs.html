D11 Reports Enrollment Increase for the First Time in 12 Years — What It Means for Colorado Springs Families

By Jenna Park | COS Parenting | May 5, 2026

Colorado Springs School District 11 has reported an enrollment increase for the first time in 12 years. The district counted 86 more students enrolled than at the same point the previous year — a notable turnaround after more than a decade of steady decline.

It may not sound like a large number, but in school funding, enrollment matters at every margin. Each additional student brings in per-pupil funding from the state and federal government, and district administrators indicated that the increase could translate into nearly $1 million in additional funding for D11. That money flows into classrooms, programs, and staffing — real-world effects that families in the district will see.

Why Enrollment Had Been Declining

D11 serves central Colorado Springs, the area with the highest concentration of the city's older, established neighborhoods. Like many urban school districts across Colorado, D11 has faced competition from neighboring districts — Academy District 20 to the north and District 49 to the east — as well as from charter schools, open enrollment transfers, and demographic shifts in the city's core neighborhoods.

Colorado's open enrollment law allows families to enroll their children in any public school district with available capacity, regardless of where they live. That flexibility has worked against D11 for years: nearly 3,000 students who live within D11 boundaries attend D20 schools, and nearly 2,000 attend D49. Reversing even a small portion of that outflow represents a meaningful signal about D11's direction.

What This Means for D11 Families

Enrollment growth supports district stability in ways that families notice directly. When enrollment falls, districts face pressure to close schools, consolidate programs, or reduce electives. When enrollment grows — even modestly — it creates capacity to maintain or expand what's offered.

D11 has continued to offer choice enrollment for families within and outside its boundaries through its Choice Enrollment program, which includes magnet school options with specialized programming. Families exploring their options for the 2026–27 school year can find information about schools, available programs, and the enrollment process at d11.org.

What's Next

The enrollment uptick is one data point rather than a confirmed trend, and D11 will track whether the growth continues into the 2026–27 school year. For COS families currently evaluating school districts or considering their options through open enrollment, the district's website at d11.org is the best starting point. The 2026–27 enrollment process is ongoing, and families interested in choice programs should contact D11 directly to confirm availability and timelines for late applications.
