CPSC Recall: Bellabu Bear Children's Robes Recalled for Burn Hazard — Check Your Kids' Closets

By Marta Kim | COS Parenting | May 5, 2026

Bellabu Bear has recalled its line of bamboo sherpa children's robes because they don't meet federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear. That means these robes pose a burn risk to children who wear them — a hazard that is easy to overlook because the products look and feel like normal, cozy bath robes.

The recalled robes were popular purchases and gifts, sold at Saks Fifth Avenue and several online retailers. They come in a range of licensed character designs that are recognizable to any parent: PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Garfield, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, and Dora, along with non-licensed patterns including nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons.

How to Identify the Recalled Robes

To check whether your child's robe is one of the recalled products, look at the neck label. Recalled robes have "Bellabu Bear," "BB-NW12109," and the size (S/M or M/L) printed on the neck label. The robes have a distinctive sherpa lining, two front pockets, and a matching fabric belt. If your child has one of these robes — especially if it's a character-themed design purchased at Saks Fifth Avenue or online in the past two years — check the label now.

Why Flammability Standards for Children's Sleepwear Matter

Federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear have been in place for decades because pajamas and robes that don't meet these standards can ignite more easily and burn faster, giving a child less time to get away from a flame. The standards apply to clothing marketed for sleep, including robes that children commonly wear before and after baths, when they're near bathroom heat sources or morning routines where candles or stoves may be in use.

The recall is voluntary but serious — families should remove these robes from use immediately rather than treating this as a low-priority notification.

What's Next

If you own a recalled Bellabu Bear robe, stop using it and contact the company by phone at 888-703-7752 (Monday–Friday, 9 AM to 4 PM PT), by email at help@bellabubear.com, or through bellabubear.com by clicking "Recall" at the bottom of the page. You'll receive a full refund or store credit plus a prepaid return label. Share this information with family members or friends who may have purchased or received these robes as gifts — the 18-month sales window means many of them are still in circulation as housewarming or baby shower gifts.
