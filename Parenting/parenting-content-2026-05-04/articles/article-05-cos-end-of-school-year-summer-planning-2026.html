COS End of School Year 2026: Last Days, Graduations, and How to Get Ahead on Summer

By Keisha Williams | COS Parenting | May 4, 2026

Three weeks from today, Colorado Springs kids will officially be on summer break. The last day of school for both D11 and D49 is May 22, 2026 — 18 days away. Graduation ceremonies for high schoolers in both districts run May 19–23. And summer is about to hit faster than it usually feels like it will in early May.

Here's what COS families need to know about the final stretch of the school year — and what to look at right now if you want summer to start with a plan instead of a scramble.

Why Acting Now on Summer Plans Matters

Colorado Springs has good summer programming — but the good programs fill early. COS Parks and Recreation camps, the YMCA's youth programs, and library summer reading registration all open well before school ends, and they run out of space before most families have started thinking about July.

The week of May 22 is often when parents realize their kids need structured activities and all the popular options are already full. Getting ahead of that by even two weeks makes a meaningful difference.

Summer Resources to Look Up This Week

A few starting points for COS families:

COS Parks and Recreation summer camps: Colorado Springs has a robust parks system and offers youth camps through the summer at community centers and outdoor spaces across the city. Start at cosparkfoundation.org and look for summer programming. Spots in structured day camps are limited and tend to go to families who register early.

Pikes Peak Library District summer reading program: Free, open to all ages, and one of the best summer habits a family can build. The library's summer program helps kids maintain reading skills during the break and offers prizes and events throughout the summer. Registration opens in late May and is worth signing up for as soon as it does. Visit ppld.org for details.

YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region: The YMCA runs structured day camps and youth programs at multiple locations in Colorado Springs. Programs range from sports camps to general day camps and typically offer financial assistance for families who qualify. Visit ymcapikes.org for current offerings and registration.

What's Next

School ends May 22 — so the planning window is short. This week, take 20 minutes to look at the three resources listed above and see what's available and what spots remain. Even one structured activity per week dramatically changes what the summer looks like for kids and parents. And for D11 families with graduating seniors, the ceremony schedule above includes all the information you need to plan around commencement week at Ed Robson Arena.
