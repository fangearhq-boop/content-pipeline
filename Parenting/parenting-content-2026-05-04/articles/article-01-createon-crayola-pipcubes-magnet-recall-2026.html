CPSC Recall: CreateOn Crayola Pip-Cube Magnetic Sets Pulled — What COS Families Need to Know

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting | May 4, 2026

If you've bought Crayola-branded pip-Cube magnetic building sets at Michaels or on Amazon, you need to stop using them now. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission and CreateOn have announced a recall of approximately 9,400 sets due to a serious magnet ingestion hazard.

The pip-Cubes — colorful magnetic building cubes sold in sets of 24 or 27 in Bold Colors and Glitter varieties — have a design flaw: the magnets inside can come loose when the seams of the cubes separate. Once those magnets are out, they become an ingestion hazard for children. And unlike swallowing a single small object, swallowing multiple high-powered magnets is especially dangerous because the magnets attract to each other through the walls of the digestive tract, causing perforations, twisting, intestinal blockage, blood poisoning, and in severe cases, death.

Why Magnets Are a Unique Danger

The CPSC consistently flags high-powered magnets as one of the most dangerous ingestion hazards for children precisely because of what happens when more than one is swallowed. A single swallowed toy often passes through the digestive system without incident. With magnets, the situation is different: if two or more are ingested at separate times, or if one is ingested and another is already in the digestive tract, they can attract each other through the intestinal walls. The resulting pressure creates perforations that can lead to serious infection, sepsis, and death.

This is not a rare outcome in high-powered magnet cases. The CPSC has documented multiple fatalities from magnetic toy ingestion over the past decade, which is why they maintain strict standards for magnetic toys sold to children.

What to Do Right Now

Stop using the sets immediately and take them away from your kids. You don't need to throw them away — CreateOn is providing a free replacement set. Contact the company through any of the channels listed above. You do not need a receipt or proof of purchase. The process is straightforward, and the company has set up a dedicated recall team to handle requests.

If you believe your child may have swallowed a magnet, seek emergency medical care immediately. X-rays can typically identify swallowed magnets, and early intervention significantly improves outcomes.

What's Next

Share this recall with anyone in your network who has young children — these sets were sold through major national retailers and are likely in a significant number of households. After contacting CreateOn for a replacement, check the full CPSC recall database at cpsc.gov/Recalls for any other recently recalled children's products. The CPSC also has a free recall notification subscription service that sends alerts directly to your email when new children's product recalls are issued.
