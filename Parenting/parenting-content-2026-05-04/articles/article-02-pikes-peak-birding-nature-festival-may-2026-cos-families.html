Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival Is This Week — What COS Families Should Know

By Marta Kim | COS Parenting | May 4, 2026

You don't need to be a birdwatcher to get something out of the Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival. You just need to be willing to take the kids outside.

The 11th annual festival runs May 14–17, 2026, across Colorado Springs and the surrounding region — and it's one of the more unique family-friendly events on the local calendar. Instead of a fairground or a venue, the festival happens across the outdoors: Garden of the Gods City Park, Paint Mines Interpretive Park, Fountain Creek Nature Center, Bear Creek Nature Center, and other open spaces in the Pikes Peak area.

What It Actually Looks Like for Families

The core of the festival is guided field trips. Think of it as a naturalist-led walk through places you've probably driven past a hundred times — except now someone with binoculars and deep knowledge of Colorado ecology is explaining what you're actually looking at.

There are 73 field trips in total, organized around habitat type: prairie birding, canyon walks, riparian zones near Fountain Creek, and mountain terrain approaching the Pikes Peak massif. Most trips cover Colorado's remarkable spring migration season, when dozens of species pass through the region or arrive to breed.

Beyond field trips, the festival includes 14 seminars (three of which are virtual, meaning you can participate from home), 5 workshops with hands-on learning, and the annual "Birds, Brews and Bites" social for adult attendees. Families with younger kids will find the nature center-based trips most accessible — Fountain Creek and Bear Creek Nature Centers both have indoor interpretive exhibits as fallbacks if weather turns.

Registration and What to Do If You Didn't Pre-Register

Pre-registration for the main festival draw closed April 26. If you didn't sign up before that deadline, you're not necessarily out of options. Visit pikespeakbirdingandnaturefestival.org to check whether any day-of activities or late-registration openings are available. Some nature center-based events may be accessible without registration — worth a direct check before the festival starts Thursday.

Even if formal festival participation isn't possible, the field trip locations themselves are public land. Garden of the Gods is always free and open. Paint Mines Interpretive Park (east of Colorado Springs) is a stunning landscape for a family hike any day of the year. Bear Creek Nature Center has its own programming. The festival creates an excellent excuse to visit these places — and you can do it on your own schedule.

What's Next

The Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival is a Thursday-through-Sunday event this week. If you want to participate in the organized field trips, check pikespeakbirdingandnaturefestival.org immediately for any last-minute registration or day-of access. For families looking ahead, pre-registration for the 2027 festival will open in spring 2027 — bookmark the site now if you want first access. And for year-round nature programming at no cost, Fountain Creek and Bear Creek Nature Centers both offer free drop-in exhibits and seasonal programming throughout the Colorado Springs area.
