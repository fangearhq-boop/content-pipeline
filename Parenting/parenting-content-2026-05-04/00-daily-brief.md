### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — CreateOn Crayola Pip-Cubes Pulled for Magnet Ingestion Risk
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission and CreateOn have recalled approximately 9,400 Crayola-branded pip-Cube magnetic building sets due to a serious magnet ingestion hazard. The cubes come in sets of 24 or 27 in Bold Colors and Glitter varieties and were sold at Michaels stores and on Amazon. The magnets inside the cubes can become loose when the seams separate. If swallowed, high-powered magnets can attract to each other through intestinal walls, causing perforations, twisting, blockage, blood poisoning, and death. No injuries have been reported. Parents should immediately stop using the recalled cubes, remove them from children's reach, and contact CreateOn at 800-333-0549 (9:30 AM–4 PM CT, Monday–Friday) or pipcuberecall@CreateOn.com for a free replacement set. Full recall details at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

### STORY 2: Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival — This Week in Colorado Springs (May 14–17)
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** Local Events
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

The 11th annual Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival runs May 14–17 in and around Colorado Springs. Designed to introduce families to the outdoors through guided field trips and nature education, the festival features 73 field trips at locations including Garden of the Gods City Park, Paint Mines Interpretive Park, Fountain Creek Nature Center, and Bear Creek Nature Center. Additional offerings include 14 seminars, 5 workshops, and a "Birds, Brews and Bites" social event. The festival spans prairie, canyon, and Pikes Peak habitats. Registration is low-cost and covers most activities. For details, visit pikespeakbirdingandnaturefestival.org.
