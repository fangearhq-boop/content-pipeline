### STORY 1: CreateOn Pip-Cubes CPSC Recall

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**

If you've got Crayola pip-Cubes at home, it's time to pull them out.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission and CreateOn have recalled about 9,400 Crayola-branded pip-Cube magnetic building sets due to a magnet ingestion hazard. The cubes — sold in sets of 24 or 27 in Bold Colors and Glitter varieties — were available at Michaels stores and on Amazon. The issue: the magnets inside can come loose when the seams of the cubes separate.

Here's why that matters: high-powered magnets are one of the most dangerous things a small child can swallow. When multiple magnets are ingested, they can attract to each other through the walls of the intestines, causing perforations, twisting, blockage, and in serious cases, blood poisoning and death. The CPSC has not received injury reports yet — but that's the window to act.

The good news: CreateOn is offering a free replacement set. Just stop using the recalled cubes now, take them away from your kids, and contact CreateOn:

📞 800-333-0549 (M–F, 9:30 AM–4 PM CT)
✉️ pipcuberecall@CreateOn.com
🔗 createon.com/recall/

No proof of purchase needed. Share this with anyone who has young kids — these were sold at major retailers and are likely in a lot of homes. Do you check recall notices regularly, or is this the first you're hearing about this one?

### STORY 2: Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival (May 14–17)

**Long-Form Post (12:00 PM MT)**

Here's one coming up that's worth knowing about, COS families.

The 11th annual Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival runs May 14–17 right here in and around Colorado Springs. It's built around guided field trips through some of the most beautiful outdoor spaces in the region — Garden of the Gods City Park, Paint Mines Interpretive Park, Fountain Creek Nature Center, Bear Creek Nature Center, and more.

There are 73 field trips in total, along with 14 seminars, 5 workshops, and a "Birds, Brews and Bites" social for adults. The festival spans everything from prairie birding to mountain habitat — all within a short drive of Colorado Springs.

Most activities are covered by the registration fee, which is designed to be accessible. Pre-registration for the main draw closed April 26, so if you didn't sign up ahead of time, head over to pikespeakbirdingandnaturefestival.org to check whether any day-of options or free activities are available — some events at the Nature Centers are open to drop-ins.

Even if you're not a bird person, this is genuinely great outdoor science exposure for kids. The Pikes Peak region is one of the most biodiverse spots in Colorado and a day at Paint Mines is unforgettable. Have your kids ever been on a guided nature walk?

### STORY 3: COS Downtown Farmers Market Opens Sunday

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

Sunday morning just got a little better, COS.

The Colorado Springs Downtown Farmers Market at Acacia Park opens for the 2026 season this Sunday, May 10 — and it runs every single Sunday through October 25. Hours are 9 AM to 2 PM. Admission is free.

Acacia Park is at 115 E Platte Ave in downtown Colorado Springs. You'll find local produce, artisan goods, and the makers that make this one of the city's best weekly family traditions. No reservations, no tickets, no stress — just show up.

And since opening day is Mother's Day, it's a genuinely easy plan: coffee, fresh berries, local honey, and a walk around downtown with the kids. Not a bad way to celebrate.

The market runs weekly through late October, so this is a habit worth building from day one of the season. We'll be checking in with market highlights throughout the summer. Any particular vendors or market finds you're hoping to see this year?

### STORY 4: D49 Student Success Center — New ECE Classrooms

**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM MT)**

D49 families with younger kids — this one's for you.

School District 49 is opening its new Student Success Center at the start of the 2026–27 school year in August, and it includes two brand-new early childhood education classrooms. That means more capacity for D49's youngest learners — the kids who are approaching school age and their families who are figuring out the transition into kindergarten.

Early childhood programs can be hard to navigate in Colorado Springs. D49 has been expanding its options in the northeast part of the city, and the Student Success Center represents a meaningful investment in that expansion.

If you have a child who will be approaching kindergarten age for the 2026–27 or 2027–28 school year, now is the time to visit d49.org and learn about enrollment timelines, eligibility, and what the early learning programs look like. These spots tend to fill quickly once enrollment opens.
