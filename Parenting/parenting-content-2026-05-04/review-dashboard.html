CPSC Recall — CreateOn Crayola Pip-Cubes Pulled for Magnet Ingestion Risk
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post If you've got Crayola pip-Cubes at home, it's time to pull them out. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission and CreateOn have recalled about 9,400 Crayola-branded pip-Cube magnetic building sets due to a magnet ingestion hazard. The cubes — sold in sets of 24 or 27 in Bold Colors and Glitter varieties — were available at Michaels stores and on Amazon. The issue: the magnets inside can come loose when the seams of the cubes separate. Here's why that matters: high-powered magnets are one of the most dangerous things a small child can swallow. When multiple magnets are ingested, they can attract to each other through the walls of the intestines, causing perforations, twisting, blockage, and in serious cases, blood poisoning and death. The CPSC has not received injury reports yet — but that's the window to act. The good news: CreateOn is offering a free replacement set. Just stop using the recalled cubes now, take them away from your kids, and contact CreateOn: 📞 800-333-0549 (M–F, 9:30 AM–4 PM CT) ✉️ pipcuberecall@CreateOn.com 🔗 createon.com/recall/ No proof of purchase needed. Share this with anyone who has young kids — these were sold at major retailers and are likely in a lot of homes. Do you check recall notices regularly, or is this the first you're hearing about this one? ### Image Post Caption RECALL: Crayola pip-Cube magnetic sets from Michaels + Amazon are recalled. Loose magnets = serious ingestion risk. Free replacement from CreateOn: 800-333-0549. 🚨

Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival — This Week in Colorado Springs (May 14–17)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Here's one coming up that's worth knowing about, COS families. The 11th annual Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival runs May 14–17 right here in and around Colorado Springs. It's built around guided field trips through some of the most beautiful outdoor spaces in the region — Garden of the Gods City Park, Paint Mines Interpretive Park, Fountain Creek Nature Center, Bear Creek Nature Center, and more. There are 73 field trips in total, along with 14 seminars, 5 workshops, and a "Birds, Brews and Bites" social for adults. The festival spans everything from prairie birding to mountain habitat — all within a short drive of Colorado Springs. Most activities are covered by the registration fee, which is designed to be accessible. Pre-registration for the main draw closed April 26, so if you didn't sign up ahead of time, head over to pikespeakbirdingandnaturefestival.org to check whether any day-of options or free activities are available — some events at the Nature Centers are open to drop-ins. Even if you're not a bird person, this is genuinely great outdoor science exposure for kids. The Pikes Peak region is one of the most biodiverse spots in Colorado and a day at Paint Mines is unforgettable. Have your kids ever been on a guided nature walk? ### Image Post Caption Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival is this week — May 14–17 🦅. Garden of the Gods, Paint Mines, 73 field trips.
