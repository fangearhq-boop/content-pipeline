Little Pea Shop Padded Crib Bumpers — CPSC Formal Recall
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ``` 🚨 If you have padded crib bumpers from Little Pea Shop, stop using them now. The CPSC has issued a formal recall for Little Pea Shop padded crib bumpers. They were sold on littlepeashop.com between October 2023 and October 2025, priced between $30 and $80. The problem: padded crib bumpers are federally banned under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act, which became law in May 2022. They can obstruct an infant's breathing and have been linked to suffocation deaths. The recall means you're entitled to a full refund. Stop using the bumpers immediately and contact Little Pea Shop — or visit cpsc.gov for step-by-step instructions on the return process. It's worth knowing that the only safe sleeping surface for an infant is a bare crib mattress with a tight-fitting sheet. No bumpers, no loose blankets, no pillows — regardless of how cozy they look. If you have padded bumpers from any brand, not just Little Pea Shop, this is a good moment to remove them. Have a question about safe sleep setups? Drop it in the comments. 👇 ``` ### Image Post Caption ``` 🚨 CPSC RECALL: Little Pea Shop padded crib bumpers. Sold Oct 2023–Oct 2025, $30–$80. Federal ban violation — can block an infant's breathing. Stop use. Full refund available at cpsc.gov. Safe sleep = bare mattress + tight-fitting sheet. Nothing else. ```

### Long-Form Post ``` ⚠️ If you own a Babypark soft baby carrier, the CPSC is asking you to stop using it immediately. The warning covers Babypark Children's Carriers. The leg openings are large enough for an infant to slip through — which means your child could fall out. That's not a minor fit issue. It's a potentially fatal fall hazard, and it violates the mandatory federal standard for soft infant and toddler carriers. Unlike a formal recall, a CPSC Warning doesn't always come with a built-in refund program. The guidance right now is to stop using the carrier and check cpsc.gov for the latest updates on next steps. If you purchased through a retailer, it's worth reaching out directly about a refund. While you're at it, this is a good moment to check any soft carrier you use. The leg openings should fit snugly around your child's thighs. If your child's legs slide easily through the openings or there's a lot of extra fabric, that's worth looking into. Carriers should also be checked regularly for worn buckles, frayed seams, and stretched straps. Have you ever had a carrier fit issue or safety concern? What brand do you use? 👇 ``` ### Image Post Caption ``` ⚠️ CPSC Warning: Babypark Children's Carriers. Leg openings large enough for an infant to slip through — deadly fall hazard. ### Long-Form Post ``` Some of the best things to do in Colorado Springs this summer are completely free 🌄 — and they're right outside your door. Garden of the Gods is always free. The red rock formations make it one of the most photographed spots in the state, and the trails range from paved and fully accessible to longer loop hikes. There's also a free Visitor Center worth a stop, especially with curious kids. North Cheyenne Cañon Park is also free, and the Helen Hunt Falls hike is one of the best easy family hikes in the area — just 0.4 miles round trip with a waterfall at the end. Palmer Park has trails for all levels and is a great dog-friendly option in the northeast part of the city. A few altitude tips for July: go earlier in the day if you can. Afternoon thunderstorms typically roll in between 2 and 4 PM this time of year, and they can come on fast. Colorado Springs sits at about 6,035 feet, so pack more water than you think you'll need — the altitude and dry air dehydrate you faster than you'd expect. SPF is non-negotiable up here too.
