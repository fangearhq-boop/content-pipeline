### STORY 1: Little Pea Shop Padded Crib Bumpers — CPSC Formal Recall

**Confidence: HIGH**
**Source:** cpsc.gov

- Little Pea Shop padded crib bumpers formally recalled by CPSC
- Sold on littlepeashop.com from October 2023 through October 2025
- Price range: $30–$80
- Hazard: padded crib bumpers can obstruct an infant's breathing, posing a suffocation risk
- Violation: Safe Sleep for Babies Act (signed into law May 2022), which bans the manufacture and sale of padded crib bumpers in the United States
- Remedy: consumers should stop using the bumpers immediately and contact Little Pea Shop for a full refund
- No injuries reported (standard recall language)
- Formal recall (not a warning) — refund available
- cpsc.gov: "Little Pea Shop Crib Bumpers Recalled Due to Risk of Serious Injury or Death from Suffocation; Violates Federal Crib Bumper Ban; Sold by Little Pea Shop"

**Background context (from CPSC and AAP):**
- Padded crib bumpers have been linked to infant suffocation deaths since the 1990s
- The Safe Sleep for Babies Act (SSBA), signed May 16, 2022, banned the manufacture and sale of padded crib bumpers — and inclined infant sleep products — in the U.S.
- AAP has long advised: bare crib with a tight-fitting sheet is the only safe sleep surface for infants
- Despite the federal ban, some sellers continued marketing padded bumpers online; CPSC has issued multiple recalls and warnings in 2026 for violations

### STORY 3: Babypark Children's Carriers — CPSC Warning

**Confidence: HIGH**
**Source:** cpsc.gov

- CPSC warning (not a formal recall) for Babypark Children's Carriers
- Hazard: leg openings are large enough that a child can slip through, posing a deadly fall hazard
- Violation: mandatory standard for soft infant and toddler carriers
- Action required: stop using immediately
- Remedy: as a CPSC Warning (not recall), there may not be a direct refund program; consumers should visit cpsc.gov for the latest guidance and contact the seller directly
- No specific unit count found (typical for CPSC Warnings vs. formal recalls)

**Carrier safety background (from AAP and safe-use guidelines):**
- Soft infant carriers must keep babies in a proper "M" position with hips flexed above the knees
- Leg openings that are too large allow infants to slip through feet-first — a known fall risk
- Mandatory federal standard for soft infant/toddler carriers: 16 CFR Part 1231 (effective March 2022)
- The standard covers structural integrity, buckle strength, leg opening dimensions, and weight limits
- Parents should regularly check carrier seams, buckles, and leg openings for wear or excess slack
