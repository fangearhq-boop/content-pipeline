CPSC Recalls Little Pea Shop Padded Crib Bumpers for Violating Federal Safe Sleep Ban

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | July 10, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued a formal recall for Little Pea Shop padded crib bumpers. The bumpers, sold on littlepeashop.com between October 2023 and October 2025 for between $30 and $80, violate the Safe Sleep for Babies Act — a federal law that has banned padded crib bumpers since 2022. If you have them in your child's crib, stop using them now and contact Little Pea Shop for a full refund.

Padded crib bumpers can obstruct an infant's breathing. In a crib, an infant can roll or shift toward the bumper, press their face against it, and be unable to move away. The hazard is not theoretical: padded bumpers have been linked to infant suffocation deaths for decades, which is why the federal government moved to ban them entirely.

Why Padded Crib Bumpers Were Banned

The Safe Sleep for Babies Act was signed into law in May 2022. It banned two categories of products: inclined infant sleep products (like certain bouncers and rockers marketed for sleep) and padded crib bumpers. The law came after years of data showing that both product types were contributing to infant deaths.

The concern with padded bumpers specifically is positional suffocation. Infants can roll toward the side of the crib, press their faces into soft padding, and lack the muscle control to turn their heads away. Prior to the ban, the American Academy of Pediatrics had recommended against padded bumpers for years. The federal law made compliance mandatory — but the recall of Little Pea Shop bumpers shows that not every seller followed through.

This is not an isolated case. In 2026, the CPSC has issued multiple warnings and recalls related to padded crib bumpers from various sellers. The pattern suggests that some sellers, particularly smaller online retailers, continued manufacturing or importing padded bumpers after the law took effect.

What to Do Now

If you purchased Little Pea Shop padded crib bumpers from October 2023 through October 2025, the steps are straightforward: stop using them immediately, remove them from the crib, and contact Little Pea Shop for a full refund. Visit cpsc.gov and search for "Little Pea Shop" to find the official recall page with contact instructions.

While you're at it, look at your child's entire sleep environment. The safest crib setup is a bare mattress with a tight-fitting sheet — nothing else. No padded bumpers, no loose blankets, no pillows, no stuffed animals, no sleep positioners. Mesh bumpers, which allow air flow, are not covered by the federal ban but the AAP also advises against their use; the safest approach is a completely bare sleep surface.
