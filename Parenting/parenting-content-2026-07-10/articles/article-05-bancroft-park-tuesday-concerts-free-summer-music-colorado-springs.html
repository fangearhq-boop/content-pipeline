Free Tuesday Night Concerts at Bancroft Park: A COS Family Summer Tradition Worth Knowing About

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | July 10, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

Every Tuesday evening this summer, Bancroft Park in Colorado Springs hosts free live music from 6 to 8 PM. The concerts run June through October, they're free, they're all ages, and they're outdoors in one of the more pleasant parts of the city. It's the kind of thing that sounds too good to be simple — but it really is that straightforward.

What to Know

Bancroft Park is located in the Old Colorado City neighborhood, one of the older commercial districts in Colorado Springs. The neighborhood has coffee shops, boutiques, and restaurants within walking distance of the park, which makes it easy to build an evening around the concert. Parking is available in the area near the shops, and the neighborhood is walkable and family-friendly.

The concerts run 6 to 8 PM — a schedule that works well for families. It's after the hottest part of the day, before full dark, and it lands at a time when kids are typically still awake and have enough energy to enjoy being outside. Bring lawn chairs or blankets, and a picnic if you want — there's no policy against it and the park setting is designed for exactly that kind of evening.

Admission is free. There are no tickets, no registration, and no minimum. You show up, you find a spot on the grass, and you listen to live music. The series runs through October, which means there are still months of Tuesdays left this summer and into fall.

Why It Works for Families

Weeknight activities that don't require much planning are valuable in the summer. With school out and routines loosened, it's easy to end up with unstructured evenings that feel like they need a plan. A free outdoor concert that starts at 6 PM doesn't require much: load the kids in the car, grab a blanket, and go.

The outdoor setting also removes most of the pressure that comes with indoor events. If kids want to run around on the grass instead of sitting still, that's fine. If someone gets antsy and wants to leave early, there's no sunk cost of expensive tickets or a complicated departure. It's the kind of low-stakes outing that often ends up being the one families remember best from the summer.

Old Colorado City itself is worth a walk before or after the concert. The neighborhood has Colorado Springs history going back to the city's founding, and many of the businesses along Colorado Avenue reflect that character.

What to Bring

The essentials for a Tuesday night at Bancroft Park: lawn chairs or blankets for seating, sunscreen (the sun is still strong at 6 PM in July at Colorado Springs elevation), a light layer for after dark, water, and food if you want to make it a picnic. Some families bring simple sandwiches or snacks; others walk to a nearby restaurant before or after. Either works.

Check the weather before heading out. July evenings in Colorado Springs can shift quickly, and while most summer evenings are mild by 6 PM, afternoon storms occasionally linger. The city's parks programming typically posts updates on social media if an event is affected by weather.

What's Next

The Bancroft Park concert series runs through October, giving families a free Tuesday night option well into the fall season. For current lineup details and any schedule updates, visit coloradosprings.gov or follow Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation, and Cultural Services on social media. The city's parks programming calendar lists all outdoor events, including additional free concerts and activities across the park system through the end of the year.
