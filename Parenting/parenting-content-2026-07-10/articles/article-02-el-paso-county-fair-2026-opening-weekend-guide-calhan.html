El Paso County Fair Opens Saturday: Your Family Guide to Opening Weekend and the Full Week

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | July 10, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

The 120th Annual El Paso County Fair opens tomorrow in Calhan, about 30 minutes east of Colorado Springs on Hwy 24. It runs July 11–18, and at $1 for general admission, it's one of the most affordable family outings of the summer. Here's what to know before you go.

What's at the Fair

The El Paso County Fair has the full range of what you'd expect from a county fair with 120 years of history behind it: carnival rides, live music, cornhole and games, and a CPRA rodeo. The demolition derby is typically one of the biggest draws for families with older kids who want something loud and unpredictable.

The $1 general admission gets you onto the fairgrounds. Evening events — the rodeo, the demolition derby — are included in the $69 Adult Golden Ticket, which covers all days and all ticketed evening events through July 18. If you're planning to come more than once or want to catch the headline events, the Golden Ticket makes economic sense. If you're doing one daytime visit with the kids for the rides and games, the $1 entry gets you in the door.

Sensory Day at the Fair

One of the less-publicized features of the El Paso County Fair is the Sensory Day program, which is designed for families with children who have sensory sensitivities or autism spectrum diagnoses. The fair provides a sensory-friendly space on the grounds, and the Autism Society of Colorado produces a Sensory Book — a guide to navigating the fair that explains what to expect from different areas, rides, and sounds.

If sensory considerations matter for your family, check elpasocountyfair.com or contact the fair directly to find out which day or days are designated as Sensory Days and what the specific accommodations include. The quieter weekday mornings (the fair opens at 11 AM Monday through Thursday) can also be easier for families who want to avoid peak crowds.

Getting There and What to Bring

Calhan is east of Colorado Springs on Hwy 24, roughly 30 minutes from the Colorado Springs city center depending on traffic and your starting point. There is parking on the fairgrounds. July temperatures in Calhan, which sits at lower elevation than Colorado Springs, can be warm, so plan for sun exposure. Bring hats, sunscreen, and water, especially if you're bringing young children for a full day.

Afternoon thunderstorms are common throughout the region in July, typically in the 2–4 PM window. If you're planning an evening event, check the forecast. The fairgrounds are open-air and weather can move quickly across the plains east of Colorado Springs.

What's Next

The fair runs through July 18. For the full schedule of evening events, rodeo times, and any additional programming added during the week, visit elpasocountyfair.com. The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo also runs July 14–18 at Norris Penrose Event Center in Colorado Springs — so the week of July 14 gives families two rodeo options within 30 minutes of each other.
