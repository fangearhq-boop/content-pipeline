Free Outdoor Summer Activities for Colorado Springs Families: Parks, Trails, and What to Know at Altitude

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | July 10, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

Some of the best outdoor spaces in Colorado Springs cost nothing to visit. Garden of the Gods, North Cheyenne Cañon Park, and Palmer Park are all free, all open daily, and all worth putting on your summer rotation. Here's a practical guide to each one, plus what to know about hiking with kids at 6,035 feet in July.

Garden of the Gods

Garden of the Gods is always free — there's no admission fee to enter the park, and there never has been. The red sandstone rock formations have been designated a National Natural Landmark, and the park is owned by the city of Colorado Springs under a deed that requires it to remain free and open to the public forever.

For families, the paved main loop road offers a way to see the main formations by car, which works well with young children or strollers. The Perkins Central Garden Trail is a flat, paved 1.5-mile loop through the heart of the formations and is accessible by wheelchair and stroller. For families with older kids who want more of a hike, the Siamese Twins Trail (1 mile round trip) and the Ridge Trail offer more elevation and views. The Visitor Center is free and has exhibits on the geology and natural history of the park — worth a 20-minute stop, especially on a hot day.

One note: the park does not allow rock climbing without a guide license, and hiking off designated trails is prohibited. This comes up more than you'd think when kids want to scramble on the formations.

North Cheyenne Cañon Park

North Cheyenne Cañon Park is free and open daily. The park runs along North Cheyenne Creek through a steep canyon at the southwestern edge of Colorado Springs. It's one of the most scenic parks in the city and not as heavily trafficked as Garden of the Gods on summer mornings.

The Helen Hunt Falls hike is the most popular family trail: a 0.4-mile round trip on a paved trail that ends at a 30-foot waterfall. It's genuinely easy — suitable for most ages, including families with kids who are just starting to walk longer distances. Silver Cascade Falls is accessible further up the canyon road and offers a slightly longer hike.

The canyon road itself is scenic for driving, and there are multiple picnic areas along the way. Restrooms are available at the main trailhead near the Visitor Center at the base of the canyon.

Palmer Park

Palmer Park is a large open park in the northeast part of Colorado Springs, off Maizeland Road. It's free, dog-friendly, and has a network of trails ranging from easy loops around the mesa to more technical paths through the hogback formations. Picnic areas are spread throughout the park. It's less visited than Garden of the Gods and tends to feel more like a neighborhood park even on busy summer days.

Altitude and July Weather: What to Know

Colorado Springs sits at approximately 6,035 feet. That elevation affects outdoor activities in a few specific ways that are worth knowing before you head out:

Water needs are higher at altitude. The dry air and increased UV exposure mean dehydration happens faster than at sea level — for adults and children alike. Plan on about 16 to 24 ounces of water per person per hour of activity, and bring more than you think you'll need. Children are especially vulnerable to dehydration and may not recognize thirst as quickly as adults.

UV exposure is significantly stronger at altitude. Sunscreen (SPF 30 or higher) is essential, and reapplication every two hours is necessary even on overcast days. Hats with brims make a noticeable difference on exposed trails.

Afternoon thunderstorms are normal in Colorado Springs in July, typically building between noon and 4 PM. The standard local advice is to plan outdoor activities for morning and be off exposed ridgelines and summits by early afternoon. This matters most at Garden of the Gods and Palmer Park, where some trails are on exposed rock. Helen Hunt Falls in North Cheyenne Cañon is in a canyon and is somewhat more sheltered, but the drive in and out can be affected by flooding if storms are heavy.
