### STORY 1: AAP vs CDC Vaccine Schedule

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

If you've seen the headlines about the CDC cutting its childhood vaccine recommendations and you're not sure what it means for your kids' next appointment, here's the short answer: your pediatrician is most likely following the AAP, not the CDC — and the AAP has kept its full schedule.

The American Academy of Pediatrics published its 2026 immunization schedule this year, and it covers 18 diseases. The CDC's updated list covers 11. The AAP still recommends vaccines for Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, rotavirus, meningococcal disease, RSV, and flu for all children — vaccines the CDC removed from its routine recommendation. The AAP schedule is also backed by 12 other medical associations, including the AMA and ACOG. 💉

A couple of differences worth knowing: on COVID-19, the AAP recommends vaccination for kids ages 6-23 months plus older children in risk groups. The CDC now classifies that as a shared clinical decision between families and their doctors. On HPV, the AAP recommends starting two doses between ages 9-12; CDC says one dose at 11-12.

The bottom line for COS families: the AAP schedule at healthychildren.org is what most pediatricians follow. If you have questions about which vaccines your child is due for, bring them to your next checkup — your pediatrician is the right person to walk through it with you.

Has this news come up in conversation with your kids' doctor yet? What questions do you have?

### STORY 2: Joyin Sloosh Dive Sticks Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

Pool bag check, COS families: if you have Joyin Sloosh dive sticks, stop using them. The CPSC issued a recall and these need to come out of rotation immediately. ⚠️

The hazard is serious. Dive sticks are actually a federally banned category of pool toy — the ban exists specifically because hard plastic sticks in shallow water create a genuine impalement risk. Joyin sold approximately 113,000 of these Sloosh-brand sticks anyway, violating the existing federal ban. The CPSC recall covers piercing injuries and eye and facial injuries that can happen when children try to retrieve the sticks from the bottom of a pool.

These were sold at Amazon, Walmart, and Target for roughly $7-15 a set. If they came home in a summer pool haul or were a birthday gift this year, it's worth digging through the bag.

The remedy is a refund — not a repair. Contact Joyin directly to start that process. The full recall details are on cpsc.gov.

Do you have a pool this summer? Share this post so other COS families know to check their pool bags.

### STORY 4: Bear Creek Pollinator Celebration

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

If you're looking for something genuinely fun and educational to do with kids next Thursday, Bear Creek Nature Center has you covered.

On June 26, Bear Creek Nature Center is hosting its annual Pollinator Celebration — a hands-on event focused on native pollinators and the ecosystems they support. Kids get to do bug sweeps (yes, real nets, real bugs), learn about native bee and butterfly species, and take part in activities that make pollinator science feel tangible rather than abstract. 🦋

The cost is $5 per person, which puts it in "easy yes" territory for most families. Bear Creek Nature Center sits inside Bear Creek Regional Park on the west side of Colorado Springs — accessible from most of the city. It's a solid option for kids roughly 4 and up who can follow along with organized activities.

With one week to go, this is worth putting on the calendar now so it doesn't sneak up on you.

Are you planning to go? It's one of those events that always seems smaller than it is until you're there with the kids.

### STORY 3: COS Summer Trail Upgrades

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

Good news for COS families who spend summer weekends on trails: two of the area's most-used parks are getting major upgrades this season.

At Blodgett Open Space, 14 miles of new and improved trails are expected to complete this summer — including several mountain bike-only routes accessed from a brand-new trailhead and parking area at the base of the old quarry. If you've got mountain bikers in the family, this is a significant addition. Hikers, note that the bike-only trails are separate, so the experience on foot stays consistent with what you're used to. 🌲

Fox Run Regional Park, which sits near Monument and draws families from across northern Colorado Springs, is wrapping up a renovation of its ponds and gazebo area this summer. Fox Run has always been a reliable spot for a low-key outdoor afternoon — the pond renovation makes it even more of a destination.

El Paso County is also continuing protective barrier work at Paint Mines Interpretive Park, which remains open for families.

### STORY 5: Bellabu Bear Children's Robes Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

One more safety heads-up before the weekend: Bellabu Bear children's robes have been recalled by the CPSC for a burn hazard.

The recall affects Bellabu Bear brand robes sold for kids, and the reason is significant — they violate mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. This isn't a voluntary recall or a precautionary one. There's a federal standard for how children's sleepwear must perform in a fire, and these robes don't meet it. ⚠️

Bellabu Bear robes have been popular as gifts — the kind of thing that shows up for birthdays, holidays, and in summer camp packing lists. June timing makes this worth flagging now, especially for families who got these as gifts in the last year or two.

If you have one, stop using it and contact Bellabu Bear for remedy information. The full recall is on cpsc.gov.

Do you have one at home or know someone who might? Please share so other families can check.
