### STORY 1: AAP Says Keep Your Kids' Vaccines Current — Even If CDC Doesn't Agree
**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Type:** NEW STORY
**Sources:** publications.aap.org, ajmc.com, thehill.com, vaccineadvisor.com, healthychildren.org, vaccinateyourfamily.org

The American Academy of Pediatrics published its 2026 childhood immunization schedule, maintaining recommendations for 18 diseases even after the CDC scaled back its own schedule to 11. Key differences: AAP still recommends Hepatitis A, Hepatitis B, meningococcal, rotavirus, flu, and RSV vaccines for all children. On COVID-19: AAP recommends vaccination for all children ages 6-23 months plus older kids in risk groups (CDC puts this under shared clinical decision-making). On HPV: AAP recommends 2 doses starting ages 9-12 vs. CDC's 1 dose at 11-12. The AAP schedule is endorsed by 12 medical associations including AMA and ACOG. Families should bring the AAP schedule to their next pediatric appointment.

### STORY 2: Recall Alert: Joyin Sloosh Dive Sticks Pose Impalement Hazard — Stop Using Now
**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Type:** NEW STORY
**Sources:** cpsc.gov recall announcement (Joyin Sloosh Dive Sticks), consumeraffairs.com

The CPSC recalled approximately 113,000 Joyin Sloosh brand dive sticks sold nationwide. The sticks violate the federal dive sticks ban due to impalement risk: the hard plastic sticks can cause serious piercing injuries in shallow water and eye/facial injuries when children try to retrieve them. These were sold at major retailers (Amazon, Walmart, Target) for approximately $7-$15 per set. If you have these, stop using them immediately. Contact Joyin at their recall hotline for refund information.

### STORY 5: Recall: Bellabu Bear Children's Robes — Burn Hazard, Violates Sleepwear Standard
**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Type:** NEW STORY
**Sources:** cpsc.gov (Bellabu Bear recall page), consumerreports.org baby product recalls

The CPSC recalled Bellabu Bear brand children's robes due to a burn hazard. The robes violate mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. Parents who purchased these robes should stop allowing children to wear them and contact Bellabu Bear for remedy information. These robes have been popular as gifts and are commonly used after bath time and at sleepovers — summer timing makes this particularly relevant.
