Bellabu Bear Children's Robes Recalled for Burn Hazard: What Parents Need to Know

Stop use now — these violate mandatory federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear

By Sarah Morales

June 18, 2026

Bellabu Bear children's robes have been recalled by the Consumer Product Safety Commission due to a burn hazard. The robes violate mandatory federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear, which makes this a mandatory recall — not a precautionary one. If you have Bellabu Bear robes at home, stop letting children wear them and contact the company for remedy information.

Why This Is a Mandatory Recall

Federal law requires children's sleepwear to meet specific flammability standards. Those standards exist because children's clothing that catches fire — particularly loose-fitting sleepwear like robes — can cause rapid, severe burns. The rules apply to any garment that functions as sleepwear for children, including robes worn after bathing or at bedtime.

When a product violates these mandatory standards, the CPSC has authority to order a recall. That's what happened here. Bellabu Bear's children's robes did not meet the required flammability standard, and the recall covers the full product line. This is distinct from a voluntary recall, where a company proactively pulls a product as a precaution — this is a required action based on a confirmed safety standard violation.

Why June Timing Makes This Urgent

Bellabu Bear robes have been popular as children's gifts — the kind of item that shows up at holiday gift-giving, birthdays, and increasingly on summer camp packing lists. June is a month when many families are doing exactly that kind of packing: assembling gear for overnight camps, week-long programs, and sleepover-heavy weeks with extended family.

If a recalled robe made it onto a packing list before this recall was issued, it may currently be sitting in a bag headed for a camp in the coming weeks. It's worth checking not just what's in the closet at home, but what's already been packed for summer activities.

Grandparents and extended family members who gave Bellabu Bear robes as gifts may not yet be aware of the recall. If you received one as a gift and the giver doesn't follow CPSC recall news, a quick message is worth sending.

What to Do Right Now

Take the robe out of use immediately. If it's in a bathroom, move it somewhere children can't access it while you work out the remedy. If it's already been packed for a summer camp or trip, remove it from the bag.

Contact Bellabu Bear directly to get the remedy information. The full recall listing at cpsc.gov has the current contact details for Bellabu Bear. CPSC recalls are updated with any changes to the remedy process, so the recall page is the most current source.

Do not attempt to alter, treat, or modify the robe to try to make it flame-resistant. That is not a safe or effective approach, and it won't satisfy the mandatory flammability standard. The remedy must come through Bellabu Bear's official recall process.

Checking CPSC Recalls Regularly

The CPSC issues product recalls continuously throughout the year, and children's products — particularly sleepwear, toys, and feeding equipment — are among the most common categories. The easiest way to stay current is to sign up for CPSC recall alerts at cpsc.gov, which delivers new recalls by email as they're announced. Parents can also report unsafe products they encounter directly through the same site.

For COS families, recall awareness is particularly relevant during summer, when pool toys, camping gear, and sleepover items all see heavy use and often involve products purchased quickly without much research into safety certifications.

What's Next

Stop using the Bellabu Bear robe today and contact the company for remedy information — find the current contact details at cpsc.gov by searching "Bellabu Bear." Check any summer camp packing lists or gift items from the past year to make sure a recalled robe hasn't already been sent along. If you received the robe as a gift, let the gift-giver know about the recall so they can alert others. To report any incidents or injuries related to this product, contact the CPSC at 1-800-638-2772 or through cpsc.gov.
