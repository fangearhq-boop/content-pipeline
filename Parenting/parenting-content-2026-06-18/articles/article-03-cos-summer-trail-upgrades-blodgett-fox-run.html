COS Summer Trail Upgrades 2026: Blodgett Open Space and Fox Run Regional Park

New mountain bike routes, a fresh trailhead, and a renovated pond — summer outdoor season just got better

By Sarah Morales

June 18, 2026

Two of the Colorado Springs area's most-used family parks are getting significant upgrades this summer, and both projects are expected to wrap up in time for the height of outdoor season. If your family spends summer weekends on trails or at the park, this is worth knowing before you plan your next outing.

Blodgett Open Space: 14 New Miles of Trails

Blodgett Open Space, which sits on the western edge of Colorado Springs in the foothills, is expected to complete 14 miles of new and improved trail this summer. The project includes a brand-new trailhead and parking area at the base of the old quarry, giving families and outdoor enthusiasts a dedicated access point they haven't had before.

One of the standout additions is a set of mountain bike-only trails accessed from the new trailhead. These are designated specifically for bikes, which means the hiking and multi-use trails in the park maintain the experience hikers are used to — no unexpected fast traffic on the walking paths. If you have mountain bikers in the family, Blodgett's new routes are a meaningful upgrade to what's available on the west side of the city.

Blodgett has long been a popular spot for families who want rugged foothills terrain without a long drive. The 14-mile expansion makes it one of the more substantial trail systems within the city limits this season.

Fox Run Regional Park: Pond and Gazebo Renovation

Fox Run Regional Park, located near Monument in northern Colorado Springs, is completing a renovation of its pond area and gazebo this summer. Fox Run has been a consistent family destination for picnics, wildlife observation, and low-key outdoor afternoons — its ponds have always been one of the park's most visited features, particularly for families with younger children who aren't ready for longer trail hikes.

The renovation updates both the pond environment and the gazebo structure, which serves as a gathering spot and shade point within the park. When the work wraps up this summer, Fox Run will have a refreshed version of the space that families have been using for years.

The park sits in El Paso County's park system and draws visitors from across northern Colorado Springs and Monument. It remains accessible during the renovation work.

Paint Mines Interpretive Park

El Paso County is also continuing protective barrier work at Paint Mines Interpretive Park, located east of Colorado Springs near Calhan. Paint Mines is a distinctive geological destination — its colorful clay formations are unlike anything else in the area — and the ongoing barrier work is designed to protect the formations from foot traffic damage while keeping the park open for visitors.

Paint Mines is a longer drive from most of Colorado Springs than Blodgett or Fox Run, but it's a worthwhile outing for families with kids who are curious about geology or who want a different kind of outdoor experience. The park is open during the barrier work.

Planning Your Visit

For Blodgett Open Space, the new trailhead and parking area at the old quarry is the starting point for the mountain bike-only routes. Hikers and families on foot should check trail maps at the trailhead to identify which routes are open to all users versus bike-designated. Start times in the morning are generally cooler and more comfortable for families with younger children during Colorado summers.

For Fox Run Regional Park, the pond and gazebo area is the central gathering point of the park. The park has open fields, ponderosa pine forest, and trail connections, making it a good all-ages destination. Check parks.elpasoco.com for current park hours and any closures related to the renovation work.

What's Next

Both Blodgett Open Space and Fox Run Regional Park are completing their projects this summer, so conditions on the ground may still be in flux. Before heading out, check coloradosprings.gov/parks for Blodgett updates and parks.elpasoco.com for Fox Run status. If you've been to either of these parks before, this is a good summer to go back and see what's changed. For Blodgett in particular, the new trailhead gives families a fresh starting point for exploring the Open Space — and 14 miles of new trail is a significant addition to COS's outdoor options.
