Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo 2026: Family Guide to Colorado Springs' Best Summer Event

By Sarah Morales | June 28, 2026

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo returns to Colorado Springs this July for five nights of professional rodeo competition, family entertainment, and one genuinely spectacular free event that makes the whole week worth planning around. Here's everything you need to know before you go.

The Free Drone Show on Thursday, July 16

The standout addition to the 2026 rodeo is a free 14-minute drone show that takes place after the Thursday evening performance. The show celebrates two milestones at once: America's 250th birthday and Colorado's 150th anniversary of statehood.

The drone show is included with your rodeo ticket — there's no separate admission. If your family has never watched a drone light show in person, this is a strong introduction: the scale and choreography of a well-produced drone show are genuinely impressive for all ages.

The Pre-Rodeo Parade: July 11 in Downtown COS

Rodeo week technically starts before the gates at Norris-Penrose open. On Saturday, July 11, the Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo kicks off with a parade through downtown Colorado Springs. The fun starts at 9:45 AM, with the parade officially underway at 11:00 AM.

This is a free, public event and a good option for families who want a taste of rodeo energy without purchasing tickets. It's also a week's head start on the rodeo atmosphere.

What to Expect Inside the Arena

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo is an NFR Open qualifying event, which means the competitors are serious — riders and ropers are here to post scores that matter for their season standings. The level of competition is meaningfully higher than a regional fair rodeo.

Standard rodeo events are on the card: bareback riding, steer wrestling, team roping, saddle bronc riding, tie-down roping, barrel racing, and bull riding. Evening performances start at 7:30 PM.

Family programming runs alongside the main events. Mutton busting — where young children compete to stay on sheep — is a crowd favorite. The grounds also feature livestock shows, dog shows, a stilt circus, and a carnival.

Matinee Option for Families With Younger Kids

If a 7:30 PM start is too late for your family's schedule, the 2026 rodeo adds matinee performances on Friday, July 17 and Saturday, July 18 at 12:30 PM. The matinee schedule is the same production as evening shows, just at a kid-friendlier hour.
