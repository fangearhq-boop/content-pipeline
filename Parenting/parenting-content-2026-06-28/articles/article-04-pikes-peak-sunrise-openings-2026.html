Pikes Peak Sunrise Openings 2026: A COS Family Guide to America's Mountain at Dawn

By Jamie Rivera | June 28, 2026

Most visitors to Pikes Peak arrive after the sun is already well up. A small number of families know there's a better option. On select dates throughout the summer and fall, the City of Colorado Springs opens the Pikes Peak gateway at 4:45 AM, letting visitors drive to the summit — 14,115 feet above sea level — and watch the sunrise from the top of America's Mountain.

It is one of the most memorable things you can do in this region with your family. The 2026 schedule includes five dates, with two coming up in July.

What Makes Sunrise Openings Different

Pikes Peak is typically open to the public from mid-morning onward on standard operating days. Sunrise Openings are a distinct program — earlier gates, earlier summit access, and a specific crowd of people who planned well in advance to be there.

The summit experience before sunrise is genuinely different from a daytime visit. Temperature at 14,115 feet will be significantly colder than in Colorado Springs — plan for layers even in summer. The air is thinner, and altitude symptoms are possible for any family member, especially those not acclimated. Take it slow, drink water, and don't rush to the summit lookout.

Once the sun clears the horizon from that elevation, the view extends across eastern Colorado toward the Great Plains. In good conditions, the light show across the landscape is extraordinary.

The New Shuttle Option in 2026

For the first time in 2026, a shuttle option is available for Sunrise Openings. This is a meaningful addition for families who'd rather not drive the Pikes Peak Highway in the dark, or who want the option to watch the road without having to navigate it. Shuttle pricing is $35 per adult, $10 per child ages 6 through 15, and free for children under 5.

Whether you drive yourself or take the shuttle, a timed entry permit is required either way. Purchase permits in advance at coloradosprings.gov/SunriseOpening.

Planning Notes

July 4 is not a Sunrise Opening date. The nearest dates are July 12 and July 18. If you're planning a summer visit and want one of the July openings, July 18 (Saturday) may be the more family-convenient option given school schedules.

Permits sell out well in advance of each date. Don't plan to purchase day-of — it won't be possible.

What's Next

To secure your spot at the July 12 or July 18 Sunrise Opening, visit coloradosprings.gov/SunriseOpening and purchase timed entry permits. If the July dates are sold out, the August 1 opening is the next available opportunity.

Pack layers regardless of the forecast. The summit at 14,115 feet can be 30 to 40 degrees colder than Colorado Springs and conditions change quickly. Bring water, snacks, and a blanket for the wait before sunrise. The gateway opens at 4:45 AM and last entry is 6:30 AM, so you'll have time to reach the summit and settle in before the main event.
