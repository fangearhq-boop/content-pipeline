TOMY Boon NURSH Baby Bottle Recall: What COS Parents Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | June 28, 2026

TOMY International has recalled approximately 40,000 Boon NURSH 8 oz Reusable Baby Bottles after receiving more than 135 reports of the hard plastic outer shell bubbling or peeling. Loose pieces of film-like plastic can break off and pose a choking hazard to infants. No injuries have been reported, but the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission is urging parents to stop using the product immediately.

The recall was announced on June 4, 2026, and affects bottles sold exclusively at Walmart stores and on Walmart.com from November 2025 through May 2026.

How to Tell If Your Bottle Is Recalled

The recall affects only the Boon NURSH 8 oz Reusable Baby Bottle 3-Pack in the pink tie dye color. If you're not sure whether your bottle is included, check the bottom of the packaging for these identifiers:

What the Hazard Is

The outer hard plastic shell on these bottles can bubble or peel with use. When that happens, pieces of film-like plastic can break loose. For infants who are feeding or mouthing the bottle, those loose pieces are a choking risk.

TOMY has received 135 reports of this happening. The CPSC classified the hazard as a choking risk for the intended user population — infants and young children.

What to Do Right Now

Stop using the bottle immediately. Do not wait until the outer shell shows visible damage before pulling it from your rotation — the recall applies to all units sold in this batch, regardless of current condition.

TOMY is offering two remedy options:

  • A $22 store credit to booninc.com
  • A replacement set in a different color

What's Next

If your household has the recalled bottle, the next step is simple: take it out of use today and contact TOMY for your remedy. The process to claim a store credit or replacement is handled directly through recall.tomy.com/nursh or by phone at 866-725-4407.

If you're unsure whether you purchased this item, check your Walmart purchase history from November 2025 through May 2026. The item number B11654 will appear on your receipt or in your order history if you bought it online.

COS parents looking for a replacement bottle in the meantime can explore other Boon products on booninc.com or consult with their pediatrician for feeding product recommendations.
