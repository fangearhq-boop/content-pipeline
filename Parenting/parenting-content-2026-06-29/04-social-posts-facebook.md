### STORY 2: CPSC Magnet Toy Recalls

**LONG-FORM POST**

⚠️ If your child has any of these toys, take them away right now.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled multiple children's magnetic toys in 2026 — and the hazard is genuinely deadly. When children swallow more than one high-powered magnet, those magnets can attract each other through the intestinal wall, causing perforations, blockage, blood poisoning, or death.

Here are three toys to check for in your home:

**1. Montessori Busy Board Toys**
Sold on Amazon by "Small Fish." Recalled June 25, 2026. Magnets can detach from the board and pose an ingestion hazard.
**What to do:** Stop use immediately. Contact Small Fish for a full refund. Dispose of the toy.

**2. Lterfear Multifunction Pounding Game (Model D888)**
Sold on Amazon by Findriver and Weeksome. Recalled January 8, 2026. This is a wooden pounding toy with four built-in games (whack-a-mole, fishing, xylophone, carrot cutting). 3,500 units recalled. Sold June–September 2025 for $24–$28.
**What to do:** Stop use immediately. Email findriverpoundingtoysrecall@outlook.com for a full refund.

**3. Joyreal Busy Board (SKU MX049-3)**
Sold on Amazon by Indream Store. Wooden base with 18 LED lights, a mirror, and keys. The small mirror can detach (choking hazard) and has sharp edges (laceration hazard).
**What to do:** Stop use immediately. Email indreamrecall@outlook.com.

These weren't bought in specialty stores — they were all sold on Amazon, likely as gifts or learning toys. If you're not sure whether one of these is in your home, check those Amazon order histories.

### STORY 4: Universal Preschool 2026-27

**LONG-FORM POST**

📚 Has your child's fourth birthday come and gone? You may be eligible for 15 free hours of preschool every week — and many Colorado Springs families don't even know this benefit exists.

Colorado's Universal Pre-K (UPK) program provides at least 15 hours a week of tuition-free, high-quality preschool for all children in the year before kindergarten. That's every child who turns 4 on or before October 1, 2026.

Since the program launched in 2023, it's served more than 87,000 Colorado children. The average savings? $6,300 per family per year.

**Could you qualify for even more hours?**

Families can receive up to 30 hours a week if they meet any of these criteria:
- Household income below 100% of the federal poverty guideline
- Child is a multilingual learner
- Child has an IEP or qualifies for special education services
- Family is experiencing homelessness or housing instability

**How to enroll for 2026-27:**

The formal matching rounds ended in March 2026, but enrollment is still open. Starting April 1, you can enroll directly through any approved UPK provider. Colorado Springs families can choose from community-based centers, school-based programs, and home-based providers.

**Here's what the local districts offer:**
- **D49:** Part-time UPK programming, 3 hours/day Monday–Thursday (morning or afternoon)
- **D11:** Participating in UPK, and adding an affordable tuition-based extended hours option for 2026-27

To apply or find a provider near you, visit upk.colorado.gov or call 303-866-5223 (Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM–8:00 PM MST; interpreter services available in more than 100 languages).

### STORY 5: Rosemont Reservoir — What We Know Before July 4

**LONG-FORM POST**

🏔️ A lot of COS families have been watching and waiting on Rosemont Reservoir — and we know you're wondering whether it'll be open for the July 4 weekend.

Here's where things stand as of late June 2026: the expanded recreation at Rosemont is still in the planning phase. Colorado Springs City Council approved the expansion plans in January, and Colorado Parks and Wildlife has been leading the design work since February. CPW has said the campground could potentially open by July 4 — tied to the U.S. 250th birthday and Colorado's 150th — but no official opening date has been confirmed.

**What's planned for when it does open:**
- Basic dry campsites (reservation-only through CPW)
- Non-motorized boating (kayak, canoe, paddleboard — no motors)
- Day-use parking
- ADA-accessible restrooms

**What will never be allowed at the reservoir:**
- No swimming or wading
- No dogs in the reservoir
- No motorized boats

**What can you do there right now?**
If you're a licensed angler, the reservoir is currently open to fishing from May through October.

**Our recommendation:** If you're building a July 4 weekend itinerary and hoping to include Rosemont, check the CSU page at csu.org/water-service/reservoirs/rosemont-recreation and the CPW site at cpw.state.co.us for any updates before you head out. Don't just assume it's open.

We'll keep watching for an official announcement and will update you when we know more.
