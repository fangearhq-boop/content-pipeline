### STORY 2: CPSC Recalls Multiple Magnetic Toys in 2026 — Deadly Ingestion Hazard
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY (multiple 2026 CPSC recalls; most recent June 25, 2026)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Posting Window:** 10:00 AM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera
**Sources:** cpsc.gov (individual recall pages), Morningstar/PR Newswire

Three children's magnetic toy recalls for COS parents to know about in 2026. (1) Montessori Busy Board Toys (sold by Small Fish on Amazon): recalled June 25, 2026 — magnets detach and pose a deadly ingestion hazard; swallowed magnets attract each other or metal objects through intestinal walls, causing perforations, bowel obstruction, blood poisoning, or death; remedy: stop use, remove from children, contact Small Fish for full refund. (2) Lterfear Multifunction Pounding Game (sold on Amazon by Findriver and Weeksome): recalled January 8, 2026 — 3,500 units; model D888 (4 games: whack-a-mole, fishing, xylophone, carrot cutting); sold June–September 2025 for $24–$28; high-powered magnets can detach; remedy: stop use, contact Findriver (findriverpoundingtoysrecall@outlook.com) for full refund. (3) Joyreal Busy Board (sold by Indream Store on Amazon): SKU MX049-3, wooden base 6.6" × 6" with 18 LED lights, mirror, and keys; small mirror detaches posing choking hazard; mirror also has sharp edges posing laceration hazard; remedy: stop use, contact Indream Store at indreamrecall@outlook.com.

### STORY 4: Universal Preschool 2026-27 — Enrollment Is Open for COS Families
**Tier:** 2
**Status:** NEW STORY (2026-27 UPK enrollment ongoing via direct provider enrollment)
**Pillar:** Local News
**Posting Window:** 3:00 PM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera
**Sources:** upk.colorado.gov, cdec.colorado.gov (press release), d49.org, d11.org, dpp.org

Colorado's Universal Pre-K (UPK) program offers 15 free hours of preschool per week for all children in the year before kindergarten (turning 4 on or before October 1, 2026). Families qualifying for additional factors (low income, multilingual learners, IEP eligibility, homeless/unhoused) can receive up to 30 hours/week. Program has served 87,000+ Colorado children since launching in 2023; saves families an average of $6,300/year. Enrollment: Initial matching rounds (Dec 2025–March 2026) have passed; families can now enroll directly through any approved provider starting April 1, 2026. Colorado Springs families can choose from 2,000+ licensed providers statewide. District programs: D49 offers 3 hours/day Mon–Thu (morning or afternoon); D11 is adding an affordable tuition-based program for 2026-27 for families who don't qualify for additional UPK hours. Apply at upk.colorado.gov or call 303-866-5223 (M–F 8 AM–8 PM MST; interpreters in 100+ languages).

### STORY 5: Rosemont Reservoir — What Families Need to Know Before July 4
**Tier:** 2
**Status:** FOLLOW-UP (third coverage; first June 1, second June 27; July 4 opening aspirational but not confirmed)
**Pillar:** Local Events
**Posting Window:** 5:00 PM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Byline:** Sarah Morales
**Sources:** koaa.com, krdo.com, coloradopolitics.com, moderncampground.com, csu.org/water-service/reservoirs/rosemont-recreation, cpw.state.co.us

Rosemont Reservoir State Wildlife Area (12 miles SW of COS on Gold Camp Road, approx. 9,600+ ft) is still in the planning phase for expanded recreation as of late June 2026 — no confirmed opening date for camping or non-motorized boating. Colorado Springs City Council approved plans for expanded access in January 2026; CPW and Colorado Springs Utilities are co-developing the project. CPW has stated the campground COULD open by July 4 (tied to Colorado 150th and U.S. 250th celebrations) but this is aspirational, not guaranteed. What's planned (when it does open): basic dry campsites (reservation-only via CPW), non-motorized boating (kayak, canoe, paddleboard — no motorized), day-use parking, ADA-accessible restrooms. PERMANENT rules: no swimming, no wading, no dogs in reservoir. Fishing is currently allowed May–October for licensed anglers. COS families planning a July 4 trip should check csu.org/water-service/reservoirs/rosemont-recreation and cpw.state.co.us for updates before heading out.
