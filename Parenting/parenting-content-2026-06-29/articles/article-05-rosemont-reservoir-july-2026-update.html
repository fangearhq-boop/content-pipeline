Rosemont Reservoir: What COS Families Need to Know Before July 4

By Sarah Morales | June 29, 2026

A July 4 opening for camping and non-motorized boating at Rosemont is aspirational, not confirmed. Here's where the project stands with less than a week to go.

A lot of COS families have been watching Rosemont Reservoir closely this summer, and understandably so. The promise of a high-altitude lake just 12 miles from downtown — with camping and non-motorized boating — has been building since January 2026, when the Colorado Springs City Council approved plans for expanded recreation at the site.

With July 4 now less than a week away, here's where things actually stand: the expanded recreation has not officially opened, and no confirmed opening date has been announced as of late June 2026.

When the campground and boating access do open, here's what families should know about the rules: no swimming or wading is allowed in the reservoir, no dogs are permitted in the water, and all boating must be non-motorized. These restrictions are permanent — tied to the watershed protection responsibilities Colorado Springs Utilities holds over the site, not temporary safety measures.

About the Project

Rosemont Reservoir State Wildlife Area sits at approximately 9,600 feet on the south side of Pikes Peak, accessible via Gold Camp Road. The site has been jointly managed by Colorado Springs Utilities (CSU) — which uses it as part of the city's water supply system — and Colorado Parks and Wildlife (CPW), which oversees the recreation component.

In January 2026, both the Colorado Springs City Council and the CSU Board of Directors approved resolutions enabling expanded public recreation at the site. CPW has been leading the campground design process since February. The project involves adding basic infrastructure — campsites, restrooms, parking, and boat launch access — while maintaining the watershed protections the site requires.

CPW stated publicly that the campground could potentially open by July 4, 2026, timed to coincide with America's 250th birthday and Colorado's 150th statehood anniversary. But that framing was aspirational — the opening is contingent on how quickly planning, permitting, and Teller County land-use approvals have moved forward. No announcement confirming a July 4 opening had been made as of late June 2026.

What Families Can Do Right Now

If you're a licensed angler, Rosemont is already accessible during the May through October fishing season. No camping or boating access is available yet through the new project, but the fishing access has been in place for years and continues this summer.

For families waiting on the camping and boating access: the best approach right now is to check both csu.org/water-service/reservoirs/rosemont-recreation and cpw.state.co.us directly before building any July 4 weekend plans around the reservoir. If an official opening is announced before the holiday weekend, it will appear on those pages first.

Other High-Altitude Day Trips Near COS

If Rosemont doesn't open in time for your July 4 plans, Colorado Springs has no shortage of mountain destinations within an hour's drive. Eleven Mile Reservoir (about 50 miles west of COS) is already open for non-motorized boating and camping through CPW. The Pikes Peak area trail systems, including trails at Mueller State Park, are also fully accessible this summer. Pikes Peak summit is open via the Pikes Peak Highway, and Sunrise Openings on select dates give families a dramatic pre-dawn alternative.

What's Next

We'll update our coverage when an official Rosemont opening date is confirmed. In the meantime, the most reliable sources for current status are csu.org/water-service/reservoirs/rosemont-recreation and the Colorado Parks and Wildlife site at cpw.state.co.us. The project is real, the plans are approved, and the opening will come — the timing just isn't confirmed yet.
