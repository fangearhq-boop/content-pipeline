Colorado's Universal Preschool Program Is Open for 2026-27 — Here's How to Enroll in COS

By Jamie Rivera | June 29, 2026

If your child turns 4 this year, they qualify for 15 free preschool hours a week through Colorado's Universal Pre-K program — and enrollment is ongoing right now.

Colorado's Universal Pre-K (UPK) program has now served more than 87,000 children across the state since its 2023 launch, and enrollment for the 2026-27 school year is open. Yet many COS families with 4-year-olds still don't know this benefit exists — or assume they missed the window to apply.

They haven't. Here's what the program offers, who qualifies, and how to get started for fall 2026.

What the Program Provides

Universal Pre-K guarantees every eligible Colorado child at least 15 hours per week of free, high-quality preschool in the year before kindergarten. The program runs through licensed providers: community-based centers, school-based programs, and home-based settings. Families choose their own provider from a statewide list of more than 2,000 approved options.

The average annual savings for families using UPK is approximately $6,300 — which, in a city where quality preschool can run $800 to $1,200 a month, is a meaningful difference in a family budget.

Who Qualifies

Factor Details
Age eligibility Child must turn 4 on or before October 1, 2026
Baseline hours 15 hours/week — available to all qualifying children
Enhanced hours (up to 30 hrs/week) Income below 100% federal poverty guideline; multilingual learner; IEP eligibility; homeless or unstably housed
Residency Must reside in Colorado

Three-year-olds are not covered by the baseline UPK program unless they qualify under special education or other individual program criteria. The program is specifically designed for the year before kindergarten entry.

How to Enroll for 2026-27

The formal matching rounds for 2026-27 ran in February and March 2026. If you missed those, don't worry — starting April 1, 2026, families have been able to enroll directly through their preferred approved provider. That direct enrollment window is still open.

The process works like this:

  1. Visit upk.colorado.gov to search for approved providers in the Colorado Springs area.
  2. Contact providers directly to check for available slots for fall 2026.
  3. Complete enrollment paperwork through the provider.
  4. The provider verifies your child's eligibility and submits documentation.

You can also call the UPK program directly at 303-866-5223, Monday through Friday, 8:00 AM to 8:00 PM MST. Interpreter services are available in more than 100 languages.

What COS Districts Offer

COS families have options through multiple school districts, as well as private and community-based providers. Here's what the district programs look like for 2026-27:

District 49 (D49)

D49's UPK program runs three hours a day, Monday through Thursday, in either a morning or afternoon session. Families choose which session works better for their schedule. D49 also connects UPK programming through its Student Success Center, which opens in August 2026 at the former Falcon Elementary School of Technology location.

District 11 (D11)

D11 is adding an affordable tuition-based extended hours option for 2026-27 — designed for families who qualify for the baseline 15 UPK hours but want more time at a school-based program than the free hours cover. Details are available at d11.org/preschool/apply.

Beyond the Districts

Many COS families choose community-based preschool programs that accept UPK funding. These include childcare centers, faith-based programs, and private preschools that are approved UPK providers. The upk.colorado.gov portal lets you filter by location and program type.

What's Next

If your child turns 4 before October 1, 2026, the window to lock in a fall spot is narrowing — provider slots fill as summer goes on. Start your search at upk.colorado.gov this week and reach out to a few approved providers to check availability. For questions, call 303-866-5223.
