Fort Carson's Freedom Fest on July 3 Is Free, Family-Friendly, and Open to Everyone

By Sarah Morales | June 29, 2026

The Front Range Freedom Fest at Iron Horse Park offers live music, 20-plus food trucks, bounce houses, and a patriotic drone show — all free, the day before July 4.

If your family is looking for a free Independence Day celebration that doesn't require booking tickets or fighting downtown crowds, Fort Carson has the answer — and it's happening one day early.

The Front Range Freedom Fest takes place at Iron Horse Park on Thursday, July 3, 2026, from 4:00 PM to 10:00 PM. It's free, it's open to the public (yes, even if you don't have military credentials), and it's packed with family-friendly entertainment from start to finish.

This year's event is also part of the America250 national celebration programming — marking the United States' 250th birthday and Colorado's 150th, which makes the July 3 timing feel especially fitting.

Music

Tyler Hubbard — the Grammy-nominated country artist and former Florida Georgia Line member — headlines the evening. Opening the show are X Ambassadors and Bryce Vine, giving the lineup a range of sounds that works well for mixed-age crowds. The music runs throughout the evening, with the headliner performing closer to the drone show finale.

Food and Family Activities

More than 20 food trucks will be on site, so there's no need to plan dinner in advance — just arrive and pick what sounds good. The Boingo Adventure Zone includes free bounce houses for younger kids, making this a genuine option for families with children of all ages rather than just music fans looking for a crowd.

No Fireworks — But a Drone Show

Fort Carson has made the decision to skip fireworks this year, citing unpredictable fire conditions. In their place, the evening ends with a patriotic drone show themed around America's 250th birthday and Colorado's 150th statehood anniversary. Drone shows have become a compelling alternative to fireworks — coordinated light patterns, patriotic imagery, and none of the noise — which makes this a particularly good option for families with children who find fireworks overwhelming.

Getting In Without Installation Access

You don't need military credentials to attend. The Freedom Fest is a public event. That said, Fort Carson is a military installation, so entry through the gate requires the same security screening regardless of your status.

For visitors without active installation access: Fort Carson recommends applying online for a Gate Pass before you go. This speeds up the entry process and reduces wait times at the gate. You can apply at the Fort Carson MWR website. A valid photo ID is required for everyone ages 15 and older, and all vehicles are subject to search at entry.

If you don't get a Gate Pass in advance, you can still attend — expect a longer wait in the lane-side vetting line. Parking opens at 3:30 PM, and arriving closer to the event's 4:00 PM start (rather than mid-evening) is likely to mean shorter lines at the gate.

Why July 3 Works Well for COS Families

Most of Colorado Springs' July 4 events — including the Ford Amphitheater show and Banning Lewis Ranch fireworks — require either tickets or early arrival to secure a good spot. The Freedom Fest offers a high-quality, free alternative the day before, which means you can do both: Freedom Fest on Thursday evening, then your July 4 plans the next day. The drone show format also makes it a natural fit for families with young children or those sensitive to fireworks noise.
