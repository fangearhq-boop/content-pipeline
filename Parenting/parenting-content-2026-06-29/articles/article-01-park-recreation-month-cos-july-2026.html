July Is National Park & Recreation Month: Free Events and Contests for COS Families

By Sarah Morales | June 29, 2026

Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services is celebrating with two free community events and three contests open to all residents — here's everything you need to know.

July is National Park and Recreation Month, and Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services (PRCS) is marking it with a full lineup of free family events and community contests. Whether you're looking for an evening out with the kids or want to try your hand at a creative challenge, there's something on the calendar for every family in COS this month.

PRCS has organized two free events, three community contests, and a broader push to get neighbors outdoors through the city's ongoing 1,000 Gatherings Initiative. Colorado Springs has more than 160 neighborhood parks, and July's programming is designed to bring residents together at all of them.

Free July Events

Event Date Time Location Cost

Silent Disco at Panorama Park — July 11

On Friday, July 11, PRCS is hosting a silent disco at Panorama Park from 6:00 to 8:00 PM. The event is free and open to all ages. Bring a blanket or lawn chair — attendees will be handed wireless headphones to dance under the open sky. Youth activities including kickball and basketball games will also be available during the event.

Silent discos are a growing format for community events: everyone wears headphones tuned to the same music, which means the gathering is low-noise from the outside but full of shared experience inside. It's a genuinely fun way to spend a summer Friday evening with the family.

Music in the Park at Piñon Valley — July 25

Later in the month, PRCS brings Music in the Park to Piñon Valley Park at 5585 Mule Deer Dr. (80919) on Friday, July 25, from 6:30 to 8:30 PM. Food trucks set up at 6:00 PM — so arrive early if you want to grab dinner before the music starts. The evening features a local band, giveaways, and a youth kickball game running alongside the entertainment.

Three Contests for the Month

Beyond the events, PRCS has three community contests running throughout July and into the fall. All are free to enter.

Build Your Own Park Challenge

Grab some paper and design your dream neighborhood park. It doesn't have to be fancy — PRCS is looking for creativity and community vision. Winners will be featured on PRCS social media. Submission details are available at coloradosprings.gov/parkandrecmonth.

Park and Recreation Month Coloring Contest

Download a special 2026 coloring sheet from the PRCS site, color it in, and write a brief description of what you love about parks and recreation in Colorado Springs. Submissions are due by 5:00 PM on July 31, 2026. Winners are featured on PRCS social media. This one is great for kids who want to participate in something creative without leaving the house.

Discover COS Calendar Photo Contest

This is the big annual one. PRCS accepts photo submissions starting Wednesday, July 15, and the deadline is 5:00 PM on Friday, September 4, 2026. The theme this year focuses on the natural beauty of COS parks, trails, and open spaces. Winning photos will be included in the 2027 Discover COS Calendar — which will be its 14th edition. If you've ever taken a stunning shot at Garden of the Gods, Palmer Park, or along one of the city's trail systems, this is the time to enter.

The Bigger Picture: 1,000 Gatherings Initiative

All of July's PRCS programming ties into the city's 1,000 Gatherings Initiative, which encourages Colorado Springs residents to host or attend neighborhood gatherings at any of the city's 160+ parks throughout the year. The initiative is rooted in research on mental health and community connection — the simple act of gathering with neighbors in shared spaces has measurable benefits for wellbeing.

You don't have to attend an official event to count. Organizing a picnic, a pickup game, or any informal gathering at a neighborhood park contributes to the initiative's goals.

What's Next

Full details on all events, contest submission forms, and updates are posted at coloradosprings.gov/parkandrecmonth. Check the page closer to each event date in case of any weather-related changes. Photo contest submissions open July 15 — start scouting your shots now if you're planning to enter.
