D11 Spring Break Ends Today — Best COS Activities for the Final Weekend

School's back Monday. Here's how to make the most of the last two days in Colorado Springs.

March 27, 2026 | COS Parenting

District 11 spring break officially ends today, March 27. D20 families are wrapping up the same week. Kids return to school on Monday, which means this weekend is the last window to squeeze in something before routines take over again. The forecast is cooperative: a 46-degree high today, partly cloudy, with about a 20 percent chance of some light precipitation — workable for outdoor time if you dress in layers.

Free and Low-Cost Options Around Colorado Springs

Garden of the Gods

It's always free, and spring is one of the better times to go. The red rock formations look different every season, and the low-angle spring light makes for great family photos if that's your thing. The paved main loop is stroller-friendly and manageable for younger kids. The more challenging trails in the interior of the park are good for older children who want to scramble. Parking fills quickly on weekends — aim for arrival before 10 AM.

Starsmore Discovery Center

Located at the entrance to North Cheyenne Canyon, Starsmore is a free nature center with exhibits designed for young children. It's a good option for families with toddlers or early elementary kids who want an educational stop without a long hike. The center has received recent ADA accessibility improvements. Hours vary by season, so check ahead.

Blodgett Open Space

If your family is ready for something with more trail mileage, Blodgett Open Space on the northwest side of the city has 14 miles of trails with views of the Rampart Range. It's less crowded than some of the central parks and works well for families with older kids or teens who want a genuine hike rather than a short loop.

CityROCK Indoor Climbing

If the weather turns or you want a change of pace, CityROCK is an indoor climbing gym with kid-friendly sections. It's a good option for children who have some energy to burn and haven't climbed before — staff are experienced with beginners. Check their website for current pricing and hours.

A Word on the Weather

Spring 2026 in Colorado Springs is trending warm and dry, with the fire risk already elevated in some areas. Today's 20 percent precipitation chance is minor, but conditions can change quickly at elevation. If you're heading above 7,000 feet, bring layers and check trail conditions before you go. Trails at lower elevations are generally in good shape.

Looking Ahead

D49 families may have a different break schedule — check your district calendar directly if you're not sure when students return. The D49 Student Success Center is also slated to open for the 2026-27 school year, which will expand support resources across the district.
