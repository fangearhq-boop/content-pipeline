Spring Family Events in Colorado Springs — April 2026

Spring break is over, but April in Colorado Springs has plenty for families to look forward to.

March 27, 2026 | COS Parenting

With D11 and D20 spring break wrapping up this week, it's worth knowing what's on the Colorado Springs family events calendar for April. There's a solid mix of outdoor arts, community events, and inclusive programming coming up over the next few weeks.

Garden of the Gods Art Festival

The Garden of the Gods Art Festival is scheduled for April 2026. This outdoor arts event takes place against the backdrop of one of Colorado Springs' most recognizable natural landmarks — the red rock formations of Garden of the Gods Park — and draws artists and families from across the region.

For families with children, outdoor art festivals are a low-pressure way to introduce kids to visual art without the expectations of an indoor museum setting. Kids can move around, respond to what they see, and engage with artists directly. The Garden of the Gods setting makes it a scenic outing in its own right, even beyond the festival itself.

Entry to Garden of the Gods is free year-round, though specific festival events or programming may have separate fees or ticketing. Check the festival's official listings as details are confirmed closer to the date.

Spring Fling — April 10-11

Spring Fling is scheduled for April 10-11 in Colorado Springs. This community event is shaping up to be a solid family option for mid-April. Details are still being confirmed, so check local event listings as the date approaches for location, programming, and any admission information.

The April 10-11 weekend is a good anchor date to have on the calendar, particularly for families who found spring break went by faster than expected and are looking for structured outdoor activities before the longer days of summer.

Early Literacy Event for Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing Families — April 17

On April 17, there's an Early Literacy Event in Colorado Springs designed specifically for Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing families. This is a meaningful resource for families navigating literacy development alongside hearing differences, and worth highlighting for COS parents in that community or those connected to families who are.

Early literacy events for Deaf and Hard-of-Hearing children address specific considerations around language acquisition, reading readiness, and the intersection of visual communication and print literacy. Check local listings for location, registration requirements, and age ranges.

Ongoing Options Through April

Beyond the specific events above, several ongoing options remain accessible for families through April:

  • Garden of the Gods: Always free, trails in good spring condition, parking fills by mid-morning on weekends.
  • Cheyenne Mountain Zoo: Spring is a popular time as animals are active and weather is moderate. Check current pricing and any member-only days.
  • Colorado Springs Pioneers Museum: Free and centrally located downtown, a good option for rainy days.
  • Pikes Peak Greenway: Getting a 0.5-mile concrete path extension this spring — a good family bike or walk route.

A Note on Spring Weather

Spring in Colorado Springs means variable weather. April temperatures range widely, and afternoon thunderstorms become more common as the month progresses. For outdoor events, keep an eye on the forecast the day before. The general spring 2026 trend is warm and dry, but conditions can shift quickly at elevation.
