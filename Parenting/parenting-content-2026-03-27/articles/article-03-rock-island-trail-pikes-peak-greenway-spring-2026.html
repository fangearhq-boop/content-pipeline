New Trails Coming to Colorado Springs This Spring

A 210-foot bridge on the Rock Island Trail and 0.5 miles of new Pikes Peak Greenway path are among the upgrades arriving in spring 2026.

March 27, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs is expanding its trail network this spring with two concrete improvements that will directly benefit families who walk, run, or bike with kids. If you use the Rock Island Trail or the Pikes Peak Greenway regularly, the upgrades are worth knowing about.

Rock Island Trail: 210-Foot Bridge Over Sand Creek

The Rock Island Trail will gain a 210-foot bridge spanning Sand Creek. This fills one of the more significant gaps in the trail's routing — a point where the route either required a detour or an awkward crossing depending on water levels and conditions. The bridge makes the corridor more complete and consistent for families who use it as part of a longer route.

The Rock Island Trail generally runs through the eastern and central parts of the city. It's a good option for families who prefer a paved, connected urban trail over more rugged terrain, and the new bridge makes the experience more seamless end to end.

Pikes Peak Greenway: 0.5 Miles of New Concrete Path

The Pikes Peak Greenway is adding half a mile of new concrete path as part of its spring 2026 improvements. The Greenway is one of the most family-friendly routes in Colorado Springs — relatively flat, well-maintained, running along Fountain Creek through several neighborhoods and connecting major parks and open spaces.

For families with strollers, young cyclists, or children who are just learning to ride, the Greenway is already a standby. More of it is straightforwardly useful.

Spring Trail Conditions

Spring 2026 is trending warm and dry in Colorado Springs. That's generally good news for paved trails and lower-elevation paths, which are in solid condition early in the season. Trails above 7,000 feet may still have wet or muddy sections depending on recent weather. Check trail condition reports through the City of Colorado Springs parks and recreation department before heading to higher-elevation routes.

Fire risk is elevated this spring, which means some open space areas may have restrictions on certain activities (fires, off-trail use) before summer arrives. Check posted signage at trailheads.

What's Next

Both the Rock Island Trail bridge and the Pikes Peak Greenway expansion are expected to be completed within the spring 2026 window. Watch for announcements from the City of Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation, and Cultural Services department for specific completion dates and any planned opening events.
