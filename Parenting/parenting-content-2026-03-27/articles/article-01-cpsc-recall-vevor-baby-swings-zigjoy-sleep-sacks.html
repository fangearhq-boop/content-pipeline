CPSC Recall: Vevor Baby Swings and Zigjoy Sleep Sacks

What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know — and Do — Right Now

March 27, 2026 | COS Parenting

Two infant products are off the market this week following CPSC recalls, and both affect items that families use daily — one during nap time, one at bedtime. If you have young children at home, here's what to check and what to do.

Vevor Baby Swings: Suffocation Risk

The CPSC has recalled Vevor baby swings for violating the Safe Sleep for Babies Act. These swings exceed the regulated incline angle limit — a specific threshold established by federal law to prevent infants from sliding into a position that blocks their airway during sleep.

Infants, especially newborns, do not have the muscle control to reposition themselves when their airway becomes compromised. An incline that exceeds the legal limit can cause the head to drop forward, restricting breathing. This risk is present even during brief, supervised use.

If you own a Vevor baby swing, stop using it immediately. Do not use it for naps, soothing, or any purpose until you confirm it is not part of the recall. Visit cpsc.gov/recalls to look up the specific model, check your eligibility for a refund or replacement, and follow the return instructions.

Zigjoy Baby Sleep Sacks: Burn Hazard

Zigjoy baby sleep sacks have been recalled after failing federally required flammability standards. Children's sleepwear must meet these standards because fabric that ignites quickly gives children and caregivers less time to respond to a fire emergency. A sleep sack that fails flammability testing is a burn hazard during the hours when children are most vulnerable — asleep, and unlikely to wake quickly if something ignites nearby.

If your child uses a Zigjoy sleep sack, remove it from use tonight. Check cpsc.gov/recalls for model information and instructions on how to receive a remedy.

How to Check for Recalls

The fastest way to look up any product is cpsc.gov/recalls. You can search by brand name, product type, or browse recent recalls. The CPSC also offers a free email alert service — you can sign up to receive notifications when new recalls are announced, which is useful if you regularly buy infant and children's products online.

Recalls on children's products are announced year-round. Building a habit of checking once a month takes about five minutes and can catch something before it becomes a problem in your home.

What's Next

Check the products listed above against your current inventory today. If you own a recalled item, follow the instructions at cpsc.gov/recalls — most recalls include a remedy option such as a refund, replacement, or repair kit at no cost to you.

If you're unsure about a product that isn't listed here, the CPSC website allows you to search by brand or product category. You can also register new children's products when you buy them — manufacturers are required to contact registered owners directly when a recall is issued.
