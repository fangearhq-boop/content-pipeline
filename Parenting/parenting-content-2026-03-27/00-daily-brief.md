**Priority Score:** 10

**Summary:**
The CPSC has recalled Vevor baby swings for violating the Safe Sleep for Babies Act — they exceed the regulated incline angle, posing a suffocation risk to infants. In the same recall window, Zigjoy baby sleep sacks were pulled for failing flammability standards, creating a burn hazard. Both products affect families with young children and require immediate action. Colorado Springs families should check cpsc.gov and stop use now if they own either product.

**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Freshness Tag:** TRENDING
**Tier:** 2
**Assigned Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Priority Score:** 7

**Summary:**
The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its screen time guidance in 2026, removing the longstanding 2-hour daily cap for children over 2. The new framework emphasizes quality of content and context of use over raw time limits, and introduces the concept of a "family media plan" as the recommended management tool. This is a significant shift from rule-based enforcement to values-based guidance that will resonate with COS parents wrestling with device use at home.
