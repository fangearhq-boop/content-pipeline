### STORY 1: BBRKIN Biometric Gun Safe Recall

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**
If your household has a gun safe with a biometric fingerprint lock from BBRKIN (or the brand MouTec), there's a safety action you need to take today.

The CPSC recalled 9,100 of these safes on July 9 — model number QHXP029B — after finding that the biometric lock can be opened by unauthorized users. The hazard the agency identifies is serious: if the lock can't reliably secure the safe against unauthorized access, the firearms stored inside are potentially accessible to anyone in the home, including children. The CPSC classifies this as a serious injury hazard and risk of death.

The good news is you don't have to stop using the safe entirely. The CPSC's remedy is clear and specific: stop relying on the biometric feature, remove the batteries so the fingerprint scanner can't activate, and secure the safe using the physical key only. BBRKIN is offering a free repair kit — contact them at support@bbrkin.com or at securitysafe.store/recallreplacement. No injuries have been reported.

The safes were sold exclusively on Amazon.com and can be identified by the brand name BBRKIN or MouTec on the upper right of the front panel, and serial numbers in the range SQC200034980 through SQC202319171 on the lower right.

Have you checked your gun safe's lock recently? 🔒

### STORY 2: Babypark Children's Carriers CPSC Warning

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**
If you own a Babypark 3-in-1 soft baby carrier — the gray one with the colorful leaf design — stop using it now and dispose of it. Don't give it to another family, don't donate it, don't sell it at your next garage sale.

The CPSC issued a safety warning (not a recall) this summer about these carriers, which were sold on Amazon.com from April 2024 through May 2026 for about $27. The problem: the leg openings on the carrier are large enough that an infant or toddler can slip through them, creating a serious fall hazard. The product violates the mandatory federal safety standard for soft infant and toddler carriers.

The reason this is a warning rather than a recall is that the manufacturer — Beiyou Bless, based in China — refused to cooperate with the CPSC's recall process. When a company refuses, the CPSC can issue a warning but can't force a remedy. That means there's no refund coming, no replacement program, no return process. The CPSC's message is direct: dispose of the product and don't pass it on to anyone else.

If you purchased this carrier or know someone who did — particularly as a gift for a new parent — please share this. With Amazon being the only retailer and the sale window spanning two years, there are families out there who may still be using these carriers without knowing about the hazard.

Do you have a system for checking your baby gear against CPSC warnings? 👶

### STORY 3: Aspen Acres Fire Update

**Long-Form Post (12:00 PM MT)**
The Aspen Acres Fire continues to burn in southern Colorado, and this week brought both encouraging and sobering news.

The encouraging part: as of July 11, the fire is 34% contained — up significantly from 15% earlier in the week. More than 300 additional firefighters joined suppression efforts, and crews have been working to strengthen containment lines around Colorado City, Rye, and other threatened communities. At 97,504 acres, the fire has been held relatively close to its earlier size, suggesting crews have made real progress.

The sobering part: new aerial analysis from Pueblo and Custer counties shows the fire has damaged or destroyed more than 780 structures — making it the seventh most destructive wildfire in Colorado's recorded history. Three water treatment plants have been affected. Sections of Highways 165, 78, 67, and 96 remain closed.

Mandatory evacuation orders remain in place for portions of Custer, Pueblo, and Huerfano counties. Level 2 warnings are active in parts of Fremont County. If you have family or friends in the area, checking inciweb.nwcg.gov will give you the most current evacuation status and fire perimeter information.

For COS families, the fire is roughly 60 miles to our south. Air quality can be affected depending on wind direction — on smoky days, keeping kids indoors during peak afternoon hours is a good precaution.
