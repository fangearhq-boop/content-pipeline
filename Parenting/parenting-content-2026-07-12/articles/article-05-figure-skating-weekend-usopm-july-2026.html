Figure Skating Weekend at the USOPM: Olympians, Artifacts, and a Scavenger Hunt — Today Through 5 PM

By Jamie Rivera

July 12, 2026 · Colorado Springs

Today is the second and final day of Figure Skating Weekend at the US Olympic and Paralympic Museum in Colorado Springs. The special programming runs through 5 PM — with last ticket entry at 4 PM — and includes the chance to meet two Olympians in person, a figure skating scavenger hunt through the museum's galleries, and on-loan artifacts from the World Figure Skating Museum and Hall of Fame.

The Olympians

From noon to 2 PM today, two athletes are available for meet-and-greet sessions at the museum. Danny O'Shea, a gold medalist from the Milano Cortina 2026 Winter Olympics, and Jason Brown, a two-time Olympian who earned a bronze medal at the international level, are both expected to be on site during that window. For families with kids who follow skating — or who came home inspired after watching the 2026 Winter Games — this is a fairly rare chance to meet competitors of that caliber in an accessible, family-friendly setting in COS.

If you're aiming specifically for the meet-and-greets, plan to arrive by 11:30 AM to allow time to get through ticketing and into the relevant gallery space before the noon window opens. The museum at 200 S. Sierra Madre St. can get busy on event days.

What Else Is Happening

Beyond the athlete appearances, Figure Skating Weekend includes a figure skating-themed scavenger hunt that takes families through several sections of the museum's permanent collection — which spans the full history of both the Olympic and Paralympic movements in the United States. Artifacts on loan from the World Figure Skating Museum and Hall of Fame are on display as temporary additions to the galleries, and U.S. Figure Skating representatives are on hand with information about Learn to Skate USA programs at local rinks. Giveaways are also part of the day's programming.

All of the special Figure Skating Weekend activities are included with regular museum admission — there's no additional ticket required for the scavenger hunt, the artifact displays, or the athlete meet-and-greets.

What's Next

Figure Skating Weekend wraps today, but the USOPM's permanent collection and rotating programming make it a worthwhile stop any day. If your kids came home from the event asking about skating lessons, the Learn to Skate USA representatives at today's programming had information on local rinks in the COS area that offer beginner instruction — that's a good starting point for finding programs near you.

The museum's full event calendar, including upcoming special programming and admission pricing, is available at the museum's website. The USOPM is at 200 S. Sierra Madre St. in Colorado Springs.
