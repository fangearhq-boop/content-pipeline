BBRKIN Biometric Gun Safe Recalled: What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know

By Jamie Rivera

July 12, 2026 · Colorado Springs

If your home has a gun safe with a biometric fingerprint lock from BBRKIN or MouTec, you need to stop using that fingerprint feature today. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission recalled 9,100 of these safes on July 9, 2026, after finding that the biometric lock can be opened by unauthorized users — including children.

The recalled product is the BBRKIN model QHXP029B, a gray steel home gun safe sold exclusively on Amazon.com. The CPSC classifies the hazard as a serious injury risk and risk of death: if a safe marketed as a secure firearm storage solution can't reliably keep unauthorized people out, the consequences are potentially catastrophic for families with children in the home.

How to Identify the Recalled Safe

The BBRKIN or MouTec brand name appears on the upper right of the front panel. The serial number is printed on the lower right corner of the front panel — recalled units have serial numbers between SQC200034980 and SQC202319171. The safe is approximately 14 inches wide, 12 inches deep, and 57 inches tall, with a gray steel exterior and a biometric fingerprint scanner as the primary locking mechanism.

If you purchased a gun safe on Amazon under either the BBRKIN or MouTec brand name and your serial number falls in that range, this recall applies to you.

What the CPSC Is Saying — and What It Isn't

The important nuance in this recall is that the remedy is not "throw the safe away" or "stop using it entirely." The CPSC's specific guidance is to stop relying on the biometric fingerprint feature, remove the batteries so the scanner can't activate, and secure the safe using only the physical key.

In other words, the safe itself can continue to be used — the problem is specific to the biometric unlocking mechanism. BBRKIN is offering a free repair kit, which is presumably designed to address the faulty fingerprint lock hardware. Until that repair is completed, the physical key is the only reliable locking method.

The CPSC has confirmed no injuries have been reported in connection with this specific product. But the agency's decision to classify it as a serious hazard reflects the stakes involved: a gun safe that fails at its one job — keeping firearms inaccessible to unauthorized people — is a problem that can't be left unaddressed.

What to Do Right Now

If you have one of these safes, take these steps today:

  1. Stop using the biometric fingerprint feature immediately.
  2. Remove the batteries from the safe so the fingerprint scanner can't activate.
  3. Use the physical key as your only locking method until the repair kit arrives.
  4. Contact BBRKIN to request your free repair kit — by email at support@bbrkin.com or through securitysafe.store/recallreplacement.

If you gave one of these safes as a gift or purchased one for someone else, reach out to let them know. Given that Amazon was the only retailer, it's worth checking your purchase history if you're not certain of the brand or model number of any biometric safes in your home.

What's Next

BBRKIN's free repair kit is the manufacturer's offered remedy under the recall. The CPSC will typically follow up to verify that the remedy effectively corrects the hazard — but that process takes time. In the meantime, the physical key is your safe's reliable locking mechanism. If your safe didn't come with a key or you've lost it, contact BBRKIN directly before the repair kit arrives.

For families who want to verify the recall details or check the current status, the CPSC maintains a recall database at cpsc.gov where you can search by brand name or model number. The BBRKIN/MouTec recall should appear under July 9, 2026 filings.
