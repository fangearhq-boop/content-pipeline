Summerfest Is Today at Bancroft Park — Free Art Festival in Old Colorado City Through 6 PM

By Sarah Morales

July 12, 2026 · Colorado Springs

If your family's afternoon is still open, Summerfest is running today at Bancroft Park in Old Colorado City until 6 PM — and it's completely free, including parking. Today is the final day of the two-day event, and there's still enough time to make it worthwhile for families in COS who can head down this afternoon.

What's at the Festival

Summerfest is a genuine arts and makers market, not the kind of event where you walk through in 10 minutes. The vendor lineup includes fine art, handcrafted jewelry, pottery, photography, woodworking, and home decor — the kind of work where local artisans are present and happy to talk about their process. There's also live music spread across the park and gourmet food options on site.

The setup at Bancroft Park lends itself to the kind of low-pressure family outing where kids can wander without much risk of damage or disruption. The festival runs until 6 PM, so a family that arrives at 4:30 still gets a solid window to browse.

Getting There

Bancroft Park is at 2408 W. Colorado Ave in Old Colorado City. Parking is free in the festival lot. The location also puts you right near the William Street end of Old Colorado City's main shopping and dining district, which makes it easy to extend the afternoon if the family is still going after the festival wraps.

If Saturday slipped by, today is the last chance — Summerfest doesn't return until next year.

What's Next

Bancroft Park hosts a rotating schedule of community events through the summer. Old Colorado City's merchant association and the Colorado Springs Parks department both maintain event calendars that are worth bookmarking for families looking for low-cost weekend options. If Summerfest is new to you, it typically runs as an annual two-day event in mid-July.
