### STORY 1: CPSC Recalls BBRKIN Biometric Gun Safes — Faulty Lock Can Be Opened by Unauthorized Users
- **Status:** NEW STORY
- **Tier:** 1
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Source references:** CPSC.gov, ABC27, KATV, ConsumerAffairs
- **Summary:** The CPSC has recalled 9,100 BBRKIN (also branded as MouTec) biometric gun safes, model QHXP029B, due to a serious injury hazard and risk of death. The biometric lock can be opened by unauthorized users, meaning children could potentially access stored firearms. Units were sold exclusively on Amazon.com. No injuries have been reported at the time of the recall. Consumers should immediately stop using the biometric feature, remove the batteries, and use only the physical key. A free repair kit is available from BBRKIN. Recall date: July 9, 2026.
- **Tone:** Urgent safety alert, practical action guidance

### STORY 2: CPSC Warns Parents to Stop Using Babypark 3-in-1 Carriers Immediately
- **Status:** NEW STORY
- **Tier:** 1
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Source references:** CPSC.gov, The Healthy, ABC7 Chicago, Consumer Reports, PRNewswire
- **Summary:** The CPSC has issued a warning — not a formal recall — urging parents to immediately stop using Babypark 3-in-1 soft infant and toddler carriers (gray with multicolored leaf design) due to a serious fall hazard. The leg openings are large enough that a child can slip through. The carriers were sold on Amazon.com from April 2024 through May 2026 for approximately $27. The manufacturer, Beiyou Bless (Products Co., Ltd.) of China, has not agreed to a recall or any remedy. The CPSC urges consumers to dispose of the products immediately and not sell or give them away.
- **Tone:** Urgent safety warning, informational distinction between CPSC "warning" vs "recall"
