- Baby bath seats are not safe to leave infants unattended in — CPSC has repeatedly warned this since before these recalls. [HIGH]

## Story 4: Hot Car Child Safety

**Sources:**
- National Safety Council / Kids and Car Safety (HIGH confidence)
- American Academy of Pediatrics / HealthyChildren.org (HIGH confidence)
- NHTSA (HIGH confidence)
- The Zebra (2026 stats, MEDIUM confidence — third-party aggregator)
- ConsumerReports.org (HIGH confidence)

**Key Facts:**
- 6 U.S. children died from vehicular heatstroke so far in 2026. [MEDIUM — third-party stat, National Safety Council as original source]
- ~40 children die per year nationally from vehicular heatstroke (Kids and Car Safety / NoHeatStroke.org). [HIGH]
- Interior vehicle temps can rise to 50°F above outside temperature. [HIGH — NHTSA]
- At 90°F outside, interior temp can exceed 100°F within 5 minutes. [HIGH — NHTSA]
- Children's bodies heat 3–5x faster than adults (AAP). [HIGH]
- Children dehydrate more quickly than adults. [HIGH]
- Federal rule: all new vehicles from 2025 model year onward must include rear passenger alert system. [HIGH]
- Never leave a child in a parked car, even briefly, even with windows cracked. [HIGH — NHTSA/AAP guidance]
- If you see a child in a hot car: call 911 immediately. [HIGH]
