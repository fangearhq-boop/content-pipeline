4th of July in Colorado Springs: A Family Guide to Ford Amphitheater and Palmer Lake

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | June 26, 2026

July 4 falls on a Friday this year, which means a long weekend and plenty of time to make it count. If you're planning a fireworks night out with kids in the Colorado Springs area, two options stand above the rest: the Ford Amphitheater celebration and the Palmer Lake fireworks about 20 miles north of the city. Here's what families need to know about both.

Ford Amphitheater

The Ford Amphitheater event is one of the most popular 4th of July gatherings in the COS area. Gates open at 5:30 PM MT, giving families time to settle in, find a spot, and get kids fed before the evening heats up. The main show starts at 6:30 PM MT, with fireworks expected around 9 PM MT.

The venue fills up. If you're bringing little ones, arriving closer to gate time rather than waiting until the show starts will make a significant difference — both in seating options and in how frantic the experience feels. The amphitheater area has lawn space that works well for blanket setups. Pack snacks and, if your kids are young, consider packing ear protection. Fireworks at a large venue are loud in ways that can genuinely distress toddlers and preschoolers who aren't expecting it.

Check the Ford Amphitheater's official website for ticketing, parking details, and any schedule updates as the date approaches. Event details can shift.

Palmer Lake

If you'd prefer a smaller-scale, quieter atmosphere, Palmer Lake is worth the drive. The community celebration runs from 4 PM to 10 PM MT, and the backdrop of the lake and surrounding trees makes for a distinctly Colorado evening. Palmer Lake is on I-25, about 20 miles north of Colorado Springs — roughly a 25-minute drive depending on holiday traffic.

The Palmer Lake celebration has a community feel that's often a better fit for very young children. The smaller crowd size means less sensory overwhelm and easier logistics. Arrive before 5 PM to find parking, and bring a blanket or camp chairs for the wait before fireworks.

Practical Tips for July 4 with Kids

  • Earplugs or ear defenders: Fireworks are significantly louder than most young kids expect. A $10 pair of kids' ear defenders can transform the experience.
  • Arrive early: Both locations get busy. An early arrival means less stress, better parking, and more time for kids to settle before the main event.
  • Bring layers: Colorado evenings cool down, and kids sitting on the grass for two to three hours can get chilly fast even after a warm day.
  • Snacks and water: Food lines at large events are long. Pack what you need for kids so you're not scrambling.
  • Plan your exit: Parking exits after fireworks are the hardest part. Have a meetup spot identified if your group gets separated, and be patient on the way out.

What's Next

July 4 is Friday. Both the Ford Amphitheater and Palmer Lake events are the same evening, so pick the one that fits your family's style and energy level. Confirm current ticketing and event details directly with each venue before you go — schedules and logistics can update closer to the date. Have a great holiday weekend, Colorado Springs.
