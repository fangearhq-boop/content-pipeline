Colorado State Fair 2026: A Family Preview Guide for COS Parents (Aug 28–Sept 7, Pueblo)

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | June 26, 2026

Summer's winding down, the school year is starting — and then the Colorado State Fair shows up and extends the season by one more glorious stretch. This year's fair runs August 28 through September 7, 2026, at the Colorado State Fairgrounds at 1001 Beulah Avenue in Pueblo. For COS families, it's about a 60-mile drive south on I-25, roughly 50 to 60 minutes depending on traffic. Mark it now before the calendar fills up.

Hours

Days Hours
Friday – Monday 11 AM – 11 PM MT
Tuesday – Thursday 3 PM – 11 PM MT

For families with young kids, a Friday or Saturday daytime arrival works best — you get the longest window before the evening crowds peak and the kids hit their wall.

What Families Love Most

Animals and Agriculture

The livestock barns and shows are a consistent highlight for kids of any age. Seeing working farm animals up close — cattle, sheep, pigs, goats, horses — is a genuinely different experience from a zoo, and the fair does it well. Livestock competitions are held throughout the fair. If your kids have any interest in animals, budget extra time here.

Pig Racing and Dog Shows

Two events that reliably generate the most family enthusiasm: pig racing and dog shows. Pig racing in particular tends to be a crowd favorite with younger kids — it's fast, loud, and completely unpredictable. Check the fair's daily schedule when you arrive for set times.

Stilt Circus and Entertainers

The stilt circus brings theatrical street performers to the fairgrounds — the kind of act that's genuinely impressive for adults and kids alike. These performances typically move through the fairgrounds throughout the day.

Rides and Midway

A full midway with amusement park rides rounds out the experience. There are options across a range of ages and thrill levels. Budget appropriately — fair rides add up.

Rodeo

The Colorado State Fair rodeo is a serious event. Bull riding, barrel racing, and other competitions run throughout the fair's run. If you've never taken your kids to a rodeo, this is a good entry point.

Country Music: August 28–30

Three nights of country music in the Big R Arena open the fair this year. The lineup: Ian Munsick on August 28, Dylan Scott on August 29, and Neal McCoy on August 30. Concert tickets are separate from general fair admission. If this is the draw for your family, check coloradostatefair.com for tickets — opening weekend shows tend to sell out.

Sensory Day: September 1

One of the most valuable offerings at the fair for families with children who have sensory sensitivities: Sensory Day is September 1 from 11 AM to 2 PM MT. The fair is designed during that window to be lower-stimulation — reduced noise, adjusted lighting where possible, and a more manageable crowd. If you have a child who finds the full fair experience overwhelming, this is the slot to plan around. It's worth the trip specifically for families who might otherwise skip the fair entirely.

Planning Your Trip from Colorado Springs

  • Drive time: Approximately 50–60 minutes south on I-25. Add buffer on busy weekends.
  • Parking: On-site parking is available. Arrive early on weekends.
  • What to bring: Sunscreen, layers for the evening, comfortable shoes, cash for midway games, and a water bottle.
  • Tickets: Check coloradostatefair.com for current general admission prices and any discount days.
