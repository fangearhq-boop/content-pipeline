D49 Student Success Center Opens August 2026: Preschool, PEAK Programs, and Transition Services Under One Roof

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | June 26, 2026

District 49 families who use specialized support services have a significant change coming at the start of the 2026–2027 school year. D49 is opening a Student Success Center in August 2026, consolidating several of the district's most specialized programs under one roof at the former Falcon Elementary School of Technology.

The center brings together services for students at three distinct stages: preschool-age children, school-age students with disabilities who need higher-level support, and young adults between 18 and 21 in transition programming. For families navigating any of these programs, it represents a more centralized and purposefully designed environment than the scattered placements that currently exist.

What the Student Success Center Will Offer

Full-Time Preschool

The center will house two full-time preschool classrooms operating on a Monday through Thursday schedule. These classrooms will be available to staff and families throughout the D49 district, not just families in the immediate neighborhood. If you're a D49 family with a preschool-age child who hasn't found the right fit yet, this is worth looking into as the school year approaches.

PEAK Programs

PEAK programs at D49 serve students with disabilities who need a higher level of support and intervention than what's available in their neighborhood school. The move to the Student Success Center is designed to provide those students with a more school-like environment — structured, routines-based, and focused on skill development — while they work toward goals that will eventually support a return to their neighborhood school.

For PEAK families, the transition to a dedicated center rather than a space carved out of a general school building can make a meaningful difference in the quality and consistency of the environment their child experiences each day.

Elevates 18–21 Transition Services

The Elevates program, which serves D49 students ages 18 to 21, will relocate to the Student Success Center. Transition services for students in this age range focus on post-secondary skills: employment readiness, daily living skills, and community integration. Having Elevates housed alongside other specialized programs is consistent with how many districts structure transition programming — in a space purpose-built for intentional skill-building rather than adapted from a traditional school environment.

What This Means for D49 Families

If your child is currently enrolled in PEAK programs or Elevates services, the district's official announcement notes that programs are relocating — not being reduced or eliminated. The 2026–2027 school year will open as planned with these services in place at the new center location.

For families who are new to these programs or exploring options for the upcoming school year, now is a good time to reach out to D49 directly. The district's official announcement is posted on the D49 district updates board at d49.org.

Location

The Student Success Center will be located at the building currently known as Falcon Elementary School of Technology within the D49 attendance area. If you need the specific address or directions, the D49 website at d49.org has the most current information.

What's Next

The Student Success Center opens in August 2026 with the start of the 2026–2027 school year. D49 families with children in PEAK, Elevates, or preschool programs should expect communication from the district about the transition. For questions or more information, visit d49.org or contact the district directly. We'll continue to cover D49 news as the school year approaches.
