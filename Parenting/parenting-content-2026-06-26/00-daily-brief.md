### STORY 1: RECALL — Baby Bath Seats Recalled for Drowning and Entrapment Risk
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY (CPSC recalls active 2026)
**Sources:** cpsc.gov, opendata.ul.org, consumerreports.org
**Posting Window:** 8:00 AM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

The CPSC has recalled three baby bath seat brands in 2026 — NFSVLB, YCXXKJ (sold by BenTalk), and Trankerloop — all for the same reason: the seats are unstable, tip over in use, and have leg openings large enough for a baby's torso to slip through, creating an entrapment and drowning hazard. The NFSVLB recall covers approximately 1,430 units (model ZY2025, blue/white, detachable arms, 4 suction cups) sold on Amazon from May to October 2025. All three violate the mandatory ASTM standard for infant bath seats. Remedy: stop use immediately; contact the seller for a full refund. cpsc.gov has full details on each recall.

### STORY 4: Hot Car Child Safety — What COS Families Need to Know This Summer
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Tier:** 2
**Status:** NEW STORY (summer 2026 safety reminder)
**Sources:** NHTSA, AAP/HealthyChildren.org, National Safety Council, Kids and Car Safety, The Zebra (2026 stats)
**Posting Window:** 5:00 PM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Six children have died from vehicular heatstroke in the U.S. in 2026. At 90°F outside, a parked car's interior can exceed 100°F in five minutes. Children's bodies heat three to five times faster than adults (AAP) and dehydrate more quickly. All new vehicles from the 2025 model year are required by federal rule to include a rear passenger alert system. Prevention habits: place a necessary item in the back seat as a reminder; ask the daycare to call if the child doesn't arrive; learn your car's rear passenger alert. If you see a child alone in a hot car, call 911 immediately.
