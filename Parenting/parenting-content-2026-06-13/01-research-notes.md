- foxbusiness.com — "Target recalls popular baby wipes after FDA finds potentially harmful bacteria"
- nbcchicago.com — "Target baby wipes recalled over risk of serious infection"

**Key Facts:**
- Recall date: June 4, 2026 (announced by Target June 8, 2026) [HIGH — FDA.gov]
- Products: Up&Up Fragrance Free Baby Wipes + Up&Up Fresh Cucumber Scented Baby Wipes [HIGH]
- Bacteria: Burkholderia cepacia complex + Burkholderia gladioli [HIGH — FDA.gov]
- Risk for healthy users: localized infections at minor cuts/abrasions [HIGH]
- Risk for newborns/infants/immunocompromised: infections that can spread to bloodstream, causing sepsis or pneumonia [HIGH — FDA.gov]
- Fragrance Free manufactured: Nov 7, 2025 – May 5, 2026; expiry May 10, 2028 – Nov 5, 2028 [HIGH]
- Fresh Cucumber manufactured: Dec 29-30, 2025; expiry June 29-30, 2028 [HIGH]
- Sold at: Target stores nationwide and on target.com [HIGH]
- Action: Stop using, return to Target for full refund [HIGH — FDA.gov]
- Sizes affected (FF): 20-count, 72-count, 216-count, 800-count, 1200-count [HIGH]
- Sizes affected (FC): 72-count, 216-count, 800-count [HIGH]
- Reason Target discovered: customer complaints about discoloration [MEDIUM]

**Freshness:** Recall announced June 4/8, 2026. Not previously covered. Still fully actionable for families.
