Target Up&Up Baby Wipes Recall — Bacteria Risk for Newborns
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ⚠️ If you've been using Target's Up&Up baby wipes, check your supply today. Target has issued a voluntary recall of two Up&Up baby wipe varieties after FDA testing found bacterial contamination. The products affected are Up&Up Fragrance Free Baby Wipes and Up&Up Fresh Cucumber Scented Baby Wipes. The bacteria found — Burkholderia cepacia complex and Burkholderia gladioli — can cause serious infections in newborns and infants, including pneumonia and sepsis. Here's how to know if your wipes are part of the recall: **Up&Up Fragrance Free:** Manufactured between November 7, 2025 and May 5, 2026. Expiration dates between May 10, 2028 and November 5, 2028. Available in 20-count, 72-count, 216-count, 800-count, and 1,200-count. **Up&Up Fresh Cucumber Scented:** Manufactured December 29–30, 2025. Expiration dates June 29–30, 2028. Available in 72-count, 216-count, and 800-count. If your wipes fall within these dates, stop using them immediately. Return them to any Target store for a full refund — no receipt or original packaging required. In the meantime, a wet washcloth works well for diaper changes, or you can temporarily switch to another brand while you sort out the return. Do you still have these wipes at home? Share this so Colorado Springs families who shop at Target know to check. 👇 ### Image Post Caption ⚠️ Target recalled Up&Up Fragrance Free and Fresh Cucumber Scented baby wipes over bacterial contamination that can cause sepsis and pneumonia in newborns. Check your manufacture dates — if yours are from Nov 2025–May 2026 (Fragrance Free) or Dec 2025 (Fresh Cucumber), stop using them and return to any Target for a full refund, no receipt needed. Do you still have these in your diaper bag?

D11 Free Summer Meals Through July 31
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post 🍽️ A lot of Colorado Springs families know about D49's free summer meal program — but not everyone knows that D11 runs its own program, and it goes six weeks longer. Colorado Springs School District 11 is offering free breakfast and lunch to all children ages one to 18 through July 31, 2026. That's six weeks beyond D49's program, which ends June 19. And D11's program has no barriers at all: no registration, no ID, no income verification, and it doesn't matter which school district your child attends. D20 families, D49 families, private school families, and homeschool families are all welcome at D11 sites. This is a genuinely useful resource for Colorado Springs families throughout the summer — especially for working parents managing summer childcare, or families looking to stretch the grocery budget during the school break. Multiple sites are available across the city at schools, community centers, and playgrounds. For the full list of meal sites and current hours, visit d11.org. NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post 🏠 Tonight, Pikes Peak Habitat for Humanity marks 40 years of building homes and community in Colorado Springs. The 40th anniversary celebration is open to the public tonight, June 13, from 4:30 to 7:45 PM at Meanwhile Block in Downtown Colorado Springs. It's a free, family-friendly event with food trucks, yard games, trivia, and special guest speakers marking four decades of affordable housing work in El Paso County. Habitat for Humanity has been part of this community since 1986, helping families across Colorado Springs access stable, affordable homeownership. With housing costs being one of the biggest challenges for COS families right now, tonight is a chance to connect with the organization doing that work — and to show up for an institution that has shown up for this city for 40 years. If you're looking for something to do tonight, Meanwhile Block is worth a stop. Food trucks and yard games mean the kiddos will have plenty to keep them busy while the adults catch the speakers.
