Pikes Peak Habitat for Humanity Turns 40 Tonight — Free Community Event in Downtown Colorado Springs

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting

June 13, 2026

Pikes Peak Habitat for Humanity is celebrating its 40th anniversary tonight with a free, family-friendly event in Downtown Colorado Springs. The celebration runs from 4:30 to 7:45 PM at Meanwhile Block, bringing together food trucks, yard games, trivia, and special guest speakers to mark four decades of affordable housing work in El Paso County.

The event is open to the public. There's no charge to attend, and the format is designed to work for families with children as well as adults who want to connect with the organization and the broader Colorado Springs community.

What's Happening Tonight

Meanwhile Block, located in Downtown Colorado Springs, serves as an outdoor community gathering space that works well for events with children. Tonight's programming includes food trucks for dinner, lawn games and yard games, a trivia event, and remarks from special guest speakers marking Habitat's 40-year milestone in COS.

The 4:30 start time is practical for families with younger children who need to be home at a reasonable hour. The event runs through 7:45 PM, giving families about three hours to enjoy the evening without a late night.

40 Years in Colorado Springs

Pikes Peak Habitat for Humanity was founded in 1986, making tonight's celebration a genuine community milestone. Over four decades, the organization has built and repaired homes for hundreds of families across El Paso County, working to address what has long been one of Colorado Springs' most significant challenges: affordable housing access for working families.

Habitat's model is distinct from traditional charity housing because it involves the homeowners directly in the building process. Partner families contribute sweat equity alongside volunteers and construction staff, building their own home as part of qualifying for the program. The result is homeownership, not rental assistance, for families who might otherwise remain priced out of the market.

Why Affordable Housing Matters Right Now

Colorado Springs has seen significant housing cost increases in recent years. For families considering whether to stay in the city, buy a home, or access stable housing for their children's school years, affordability is one of the central questions. Habitat's work sits at the intersection of that challenge and the community's identity as a place that takes care of its own.

Tonight's 40th anniversary is a chance to learn more about that work and to connect with the organization in a relaxed, open-to-all setting. Whether you've volunteered with Habitat before, purchased something at the ReStore, or just want to show up for a local institution, this is a worthwhile evening.

How to Get Involved Beyond Tonight

Habitat for Humanity accepts volunteers for home builds throughout the year. No construction experience is required for most volunteer days, and build days are open to adults and older teens. Volunteer information is available through Pikes Peak Habitat's website.

The Habitat ReStore, a resale shop that sells donated home improvement materials and furniture, is another way to support the organization year-round. Proceeds from ReStore sales fund Habitat's construction and repair programs in El Paso County. Donations of usable building materials, appliances, and furniture are accepted.
