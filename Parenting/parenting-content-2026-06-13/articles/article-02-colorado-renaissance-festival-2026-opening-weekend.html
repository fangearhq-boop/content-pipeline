Colorado Renaissance Festival Opens This Weekend — Family Guide for Opening Day

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

June 13, 2026

The Colorado Renaissance Festival opens its gates today for its 49th season, and for Colorado Springs families looking for a full-day summer outing, there may not be a better option in the area. The festival runs in Larkspur, about 45 minutes from Colorado Springs, and continues every Saturday and Sunday through August 2.

This weekend's theme is Magical Fantasy, kicking off what promises to be a summer full of jousting, live music, artisan vendors, food, stage performances, and enough activity to keep the whole family occupied from arrival to closing. Whether you're going today or planning a trip later in the season, here's what you need to know.

Opening Weekend Highlights

The first weekend of any Renaissance Festival season tends to draw a crowd, and this year's Magical Fantasy theme gives it extra reason to make the trip early. Expect to see costumed performers, fantasy-themed stage shows, and all the vendors and food stalls that make the festival a summer tradition for so many Colorado families.

If you or someone in your family is a first responder, this opening weekend comes with a special offer: a buy-one-get-one admission deal. Bring your badge or work ID to take advantage of the BOGO pricing at the gate. This offer is available only during opening weekend, June 13 and 14.

Tickets and Pricing

The most cost-effective way to buy tickets is through King Soopers before you go. Purchasing in advance through King Soopers saves you compared to buying at the gate. Here's the price breakdown for King Soopers tickets:

  • Adults: $29
  • Ages 5-12: $14
  • Children under 5: Free

Pick up your tickets at any King Soopers location. The festival website at coloradorenfaire.com also has information on tickets and this season's full schedule.

Planning Your Visit

The festival grounds are located at 650 W Perry Park Ave in Larkspur, Colorado. From central Colorado Springs, plan for approximately 45 minutes of drive time. Arrive early on opening weekend to avoid the rush at the entrance and to get the best parking spots.

The festival runs Saturdays and Sundays only through August 2, so if today doesn't work for your family, there are many more weekends ahead. The full season schedule and themed weekends can be found at coloradorenfaire.com.

What to Pack

Larkspur gets warm in June, and the festival grounds are largely outdoors. Sunscreen, hats, and comfortable walking shoes are essential. There's food available throughout the grounds, but lines can get long during peak hours. Bringing a water bottle saves money and keeps the family comfortable through a long day on the grounds.

For families with younger children, consider a wagon or stroller for the little ones. The grounds can be sprawling, and tired legs show up fast when you're exploring.

Why This Festival Works for Colorado Springs Families

The Colorado Renaissance Festival hits a rare sweet spot for family outings: it's genuinely entertaining for every age group. Toddlers love the costumes and the music. Grade-schoolers get swept up in the jousting and the interactive performers. Older kids and adults find plenty to explore among the artisan vendors, food stalls, and stage shows. It's not a theme park, but it has the same capacity to hold a family's attention for a full day without anyone getting bored at the same time.

At 49 seasons, this is a Colorado institution. For many Colorado Springs families, the Ren Faire is a summer ritual that's been passed down a generation. If you've never been, this year is as good a time as any to start.

What's Next

If you're going today or tomorrow, check coloradorenfaire.com for any last-minute updates on parking, gate hours, and this weekend's performance schedule. The website also lists the themed weekends for the rest of the season, so you can plan your family's best visit ahead of time.

If today doesn't work, the festival runs every Saturday and Sunday through August 2. There's no shortage of opportunities to make it to Larkspur this summer.
