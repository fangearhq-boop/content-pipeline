D11 Free Summer Meals Run Through July 31 — All Colorado Springs Kids Welcome, No Paperwork Needed

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting

June 13, 2026

Colorado Springs School District 11 is running a free summer meals program through July 31 that's available to all children ages one to 18, regardless of which school district they attend. No registration, no ID, and no income verification is required. Any child can walk in at a D11 site and receive a free meal.

Most Colorado Springs families have heard about D49's free summer meals program, but D11's version runs six weeks longer. D49's program ends June 19. D11's continues through July 31 and is fully available to families from D20, D49, private schools, and homeschools alike.

What D11's Program Offers

The program provides free breakfast, where available at the site, and free lunch at multiple locations across Colorado Springs. Sites include schools, community centers, and playgrounds. Meals are available during program hours on weekdays throughout the summer run.

The single most important thing to know: there are no barriers to participation. You do not need to register in advance. You do not need to bring a school ID, district enrollment verification, or any proof of income. Any child ages one to 18 can show up at a D11 site and receive a meal. The program is designed to be as accessible as possible for exactly that reason.

Who This Program Is For

The program is available to children regardless of which school district they attend or whether they attend public school at all. That means families from D20, D49, charter schools, private schools, and homeschool households are all eligible. There is no residency requirement tied to D11's boundaries.

This is a meaningful distinction that often gets missed. Families who enrolled in D49 and used that district's meals earlier in the summer can transition directly to D11 sites after June 19 without any change in eligibility or process. New families who haven't used any district's program can start using D11's sites today.

Why This Matters Now

With D49's program ending June 19, there's a window of six more weeks of summer where children would otherwise go without this resource if families don't know D11's option exists. Working parents managing summer childcare, families navigating tight budgets during the school break, and households new to Colorado Springs all benefit from knowing this program continues through the end of July.

The transition is straightforward: D49 sites through June 19, then D11 sites from June 20 through July 31. No paperwork required at either program.

How to Find a D11 Meal Site

D11 maintains a current list of meal sites, including locations, hours, and which sites offer breakfast in addition to lunch. The full site list is available at d11.org. Sites are spread across Colorado Springs neighborhoods, so most families should find a location within reasonable distance.

D49 vs. D11: At a Glance

If you've been following coverage of D49's summer meal program and wondering how D11's program fits in, here's the simple comparison:

  • D49 free summer meals: runs through June 19, 2026
  • D11 free summer meals: runs through July 31, 2026
  • Eligibility for both: children ages one to 18, any district, no paperwork
  • After June 19: only D11 continues

Both programs are designed to bridge the gap that school-year meal support leaves during summer months. Using both back-to-back gives Colorado Springs families access to free meals from now through July 31 without interruption.

What's Next

Visit d11.org to find the full list of D11 summer meal sites, including which locations offer breakfast and what the current hours are at each site. Sites and hours can change throughout the summer, so checking the website before your first visit is a good idea.

If you know families in Colorado Springs who could benefit from this resource, sharing this information directly is one of the most practical ways to help. These programs are specifically designed to serve families who need them most, and awareness is the biggest barrier to participation.
