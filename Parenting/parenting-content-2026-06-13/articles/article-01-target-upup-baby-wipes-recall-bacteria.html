Target Up&Up Baby Wipes Recalled Over Bacteria That Can Cause Sepsis and Pneumonia

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting

June 13, 2026

Target has recalled two varieties of its Up&Up store-brand baby wipes after the FDA confirmed bacterial contamination that poses a serious health risk to newborns, infants, and anyone with a weakened immune system. Colorado Springs families who shop at Target for baby supplies should check their wipes at home today.

The recall covers Up&Up Fragrance Free Baby Wipes and Up&Up Fresh Cucumber Scented Baby Wipes. Target announced the voluntary recall on June 8, 2026, following testing that identified two types of bacteria: Burkholderia cepacia complex and Burkholderia gladioli. The wipes were sold at Target stores nationwide and on target.com.

Why This Recall Is Serious

For healthy adults, Burkholderia bacteria typically cause localized infections around small cuts or skin abrasions. But for newborns, infants, and individuals with compromised immune systems, the risk is significantly more severe. These bacteria can enter the bloodstream and cause life-threatening conditions, including sepsis and pneumonia.

Target is one of the most common places Colorado Springs families stock up on baby supplies. Up&Up is the store's house brand, purchased widely because of its price and availability. If you've been buying Up&Up wipes, there's a reasonable chance you may have affected packages at home right now.

Which Wipes Are Affected

There are two products in this recall, each with specific manufacture date windows.

Up&Up Fragrance Free Baby Wipes: Manufactured between November 7, 2025 and May 5, 2026. Expiration dates fall between May 10, 2028 and November 5, 2028. This product is sold in five sizes: 20-count, 72-count, 216-count, 800-count, and 1,200-count.

Up&Up Fresh Cucumber Scented Baby Wipes: Manufactured December 29 or 30, 2025. Expiration dates are June 29 or 30, 2028. This product comes in three sizes: 72-count, 216-count, and 800-count.

To determine whether your wipes are part of the recall, look for the manufacture date printed on the packaging. It is typically found on the label or on the bottom of the package.

What to Do Right Now

Stop using the wipes immediately. Do not continue using them for any tasks, including diaper changes, face wiping, or general cleanup. Even brief skin contact with contaminated wipes can introduce bacteria to the body, particularly through minor skin openings that might otherwise go unnoticed.

Return the wipes to any Target store for a full refund. According to Target's recall policy, no receipt or original box is required for this return. You can return wipes purchased in-store or online at any physical Target location.

If you have already used these wipes on a newborn or infant and you're concerned about any symptoms, contact your child's pediatrician. Symptoms of Burkholderia infection can include fever, respiratory issues, and signs of infection at any recent wound or abrasion site.

In the Meantime

If you need an alternative while you make the return, a clean washcloth dampened with warm water works well for diaper changes. You can also temporarily switch to a different brand of wipes. This recall applies specifically to Up&Up wipes manufactured in the date windows listed above; store-brand wipes from other retailers are not affected.

How Target Found the Problem

Target began investigating after receiving customer complaints about discoloration in some wipe packages. FDA testing confirmed the presence of Burkholderia bacteria, leading to the voluntary recall. The recall initiation date was June 4, 2026, with Target's public announcement issued June 8, 2026.

This type of recall is classified as a microbial contamination recall, which differs from a physical hazard recall (like a choking risk). Microbial contamination recalls carry particular urgency for vulnerable populations because the bacteria involved are invisible and the products appear otherwise normal.

What's Next

If you need to confirm recall details or look up specific lot information, visit the recall listing on fda.gov. Target's website also has recall information in their customer service section.

For Colorado Springs families with newborns or infants at home, this is a worthwhile moment to check not just your wipes but any other Up&Up baby care products for recall notices. The FDA maintains a searchable recall database at fda.gov where you can look up any product by brand or category.

If you have concerns about a child's health in relation to these wipes, contact your pediatrician directly rather than waiting to see if symptoms develop.
