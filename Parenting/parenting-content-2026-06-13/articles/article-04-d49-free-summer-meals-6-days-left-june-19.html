D49 Free Summer Meals End June 19 — 6 Days Left

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

June 13, 2026

District 49's free summer meal program ends in six days, on June 19. If you or a family you know has been using the program, now is the time to take advantage of the remaining days, and to start planning the transition to D11's program, which picks up immediately after.

D49's program has been running since early June, providing free breakfast and lunch to any child ages one to 18 at no cost and with no paperwork required. As of today, there are six days remaining before the program wraps up at the end of day June 19.

What D49's Program Offers

Any child ages one to 18 can receive a free breakfast (at participating sites) and a free lunch through June 19. No registration is required. No ID or proof of school enrollment is needed. No income verification is required. Any family can show up at a D49 site and receive meals for their children during program hours.

Springs Ranch Elementary is a confirmed meal site. The full list of D49 sites, including locations and hours, is available at d49.org.

Who Can Still Use It This Week

The six-day window applies to everyone, whether your family has been using the program all summer or you're just finding out about it now. Families from D11, D20, private schools, and homeschool households are all eligible, not just D49 families. The program is open to any child in the age range regardless of which school district they attend.

If you've been waiting or weren't sure if you qualified, the remaining six days are yours to use. There is no benefit to waiting.

After June 19: D11 Takes Over

Colorado Springs School District 11 runs its own free summer meal program that continues through July 31. Beginning June 20, D11 becomes the option for families who relied on D49's program.

D11's program has the same eligibility and the same no-barriers structure: children ages one to 18, any district, no registration, no ID, no income verification. The full D11 site list is at d11.org.

This transition means Colorado Springs families can access free summer meals from now through July 31 without any gap, simply by switching to D11 sites after June 19.

Why the Countdown Matters

For families using D49 for regular weekday lunches, the June 19 end date can sneak up quickly, especially with summer schedules in flux. Six days is enough time to get several more meals and make a plan, but it's not unlimited time.

For families who have heard about the program but haven't tried it yet, this week represents the last opportunity. The program exists specifically to ensure children don't go without adequate nutrition during the summer months. Using it isn't a last resort; it's exactly what the program is designed for.

What's Next

Use D49's program this week at d49.org to find your nearest site and current hours. Springs Ranch Elementary is confirmed as an active location through June 19.

Starting June 20, plan to shift to D11 sites at d11.org. The transition requires no paperwork and no re-enrollment of any kind. Just show up at a D11 site with your children and the program continues seamlessly through July 31.

If you know families who aren't aware that the program is ending or that D11 continues afterward, sharing this information this week makes a real difference. Six days is just enough time to pass the word.
