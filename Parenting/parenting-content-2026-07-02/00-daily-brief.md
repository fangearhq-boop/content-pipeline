### STORY 1: All COS July 4 Fireworks Shows Cancelled — What Families Need to Know
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY (cancellation announced July 1)
**Pillar:** Local News
**Sources:** coloradosprings.gov, krdo.com, cpr.org, gazette.com, fox21news.com, kktv.com
**Posting Window:** 8:00 AM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

All professional, permitted July 4 fireworks displays in Colorado Springs have been cancelled due to critical fire danger. Mayor Yemi Mobolade, the Colorado Springs Fire Department, Colorado Springs Police Department, and public safety partners jointly announced the decision on July 1 after evaluating current fuel moisture levels — which officials say are comparable to those recorded before the 2012 Waldo Canyon Fire. Consumer fireworks are permanently banned within Colorado Springs city limits every year (including sparklers). El Paso County took no action on July 2 to ban personal fireworks in unincorporated areas, citing a Colorado state law restriction that prevents counties from restricting fireworks sales and use from May 31 to July 5 without a Board of County Commissioners resolution. All other July 4 festivities (food, music, family events) will continue as planned.

### STORY 2: Cosyland Children's Tower Stools Recalled — 125,000+ Units at Risk
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY (recall announced May 14, 2026 — not previously covered in COS Parenting pipeline)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Sources:** cpsc.gov, nbcconnecticut.com, kiro7.com, wsbtv.com
**Posting Window:** 10:00 AM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Cosyland is recalling approximately 125,200 children's tower stools (models CS0003 and CS0092-4) due to entrapment and fall hazards. The bamboo/gray stools measure 16 inches deep by 18 inches wide by 35 inches tall. A child's torso can fit through openings on the front and back of the stool, and the stools can collapse or tip over while in use. Cosyland has received 25 reports of stability issues and falls, including 8 injuries ranging from scrapes and bruises to a fractured arm. The stools were sold on Amazon for approximately $70 from April 2021 through November 2025. Consumers should stop use immediately and contact Cosyland Official at 866-677-3889 for free repair parts (protective nets, stabilizing feet, installation instructions).

**Status:** FOLLOW-UP (program running since May 27; new angle: July 2 = 29 days remaining before July 31 end date)
**Pillar:** Local News
**Sources:** kktv.com, fox21news.com, d11.org, ppld.org/summer-lunches
**Posting Window:** 12:00 PM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Colorado Springs School District 11's free Summer Food Service Program runs through July 31, 2026, providing free meals to all children ages 1–18 regardless of which school district they attend. Multiple sites across the city offer free breakfast (where available) and lunch Monday–Friday. The D11 mobile unit visits East Library every weekday from 11 a.m. to noon, delivering lunch plus age-appropriate books. PPLD also partners with the program, offering free summer lunches at library branch locations. All sites are closed July 4. The D11 Food and Nutrition Services office can be reached at 520-2930.

### STORY 4: July 4 in COS Without Fireworks — Family-Friendly Alternatives That Are Still Happening
**Tier:** 2

**Sources:** rockymountainfoodtours.com, visitcos.com, coloradospringssports.org, pikes-peak.com
**Posting Window:** 3:00 PM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

With all professional July 4 fireworks shows in Colorado Springs cancelled due to fire danger, families are looking for alternatives. All non-fireworks festivities continue as planned: Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site offers an Old-Fashioned July 4 celebration (10 a.m.–4 p.m., $7–$13, children 3 and under free) with historical interpreters, patriotic music, and kids' activities. The Banning Lewis Ranch community event at Vista Park Pavilion (8833 Vista Del Pico Blvd) is free, runs from 3 p.m. onward with food trucks and live music. The Star-Spangled Symphony at Ford Amphitheater (gates 5:30 p.m., show 6:30 p.m.) continues but without its traditional fireworks finale. National Museum of WWII Aviation offers July 4 programming (10 a.m.–5 p.m.) with vintage vehicle displays and children's STEM activities.

**Sources:** coloradosprings.gov/America250Colorado150, visitcos.com

2026 marks both America's 250th birthday and Colorado's 150th — and Colorado Springs has designated special events and sites all summer. The City's "Celebrate America 250 and Colorado 150" programming includes historic sites, community events, and patriotic programming aimed at families. With the cancellation of July 4 fireworks, the city's emphasis on daytime history-focused events provides a family-friendly framework for celebrating the milestone anniversaries without the fire risk.
