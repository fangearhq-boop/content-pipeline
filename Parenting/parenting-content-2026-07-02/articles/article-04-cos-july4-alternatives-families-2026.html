July 4 in Colorado Springs Without the Fireworks — Four Family-Friendly Options Still Happening This Weekend

By Jamie Rivera | July 2, 2026

All professional fireworks shows in Colorado Springs have been cancelled due to fire danger. Here's what's still on the calendar for families looking to celebrate July 4.

The fireworks are not happening in Colorado Springs this July 4. City officials cancelled all professional, permitted displays on July 1 due to critical fire danger — fuel moisture levels comparable to conditions before the 2012 Waldo Canyon Fire. The decision was the right call. But it also leaves families who planned around the shows looking for options.

The good news: most of what makes July 4 worth celebrating as a family has nothing to do with fireworks. Several events that were already on the calendar are still moving forward, and they offer genuine time outside with kids in Colorado Springs.

Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site

Rock Ledge Ranch runs a full Old-Fashioned July 4 celebration from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Admission is $7 to $13; children ages 3 and under are free.

This is the most immersive option on the list. The Ranch operates as a living history site, and on July 4 it leans into the historical dimension: interpreters in period costume, patriotic music, kids' activities, carnival games, and historic home tours. If you want an Independence Day that actually involves some history alongside the celebration, this is the place.

Banning Lewis Ranch — Vista Park Pavilion

The Banning Lewis Ranch community July 4 event at Vista Park Pavilion (8833 Vista Del Pico Blvd) is free. It starts at 3 p.m. and runs into the evening, with multiple food trucks and live music bands. No admission, no tickets required — it's a community gathering by design.

The event was planned to include fireworks at dusk, which have been cancelled. The food, music, and community elements continue.

National Museum of WWII Aviation

The National Museum of WWII Aviation runs July 4 programming from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. The day includes vintage military vehicle displays, vintage automobile shows, children's STEM activities, and prize drawings.

This one is particularly good for kids who are drawn to vehicles, machinery, and history with physical things in it. The STEM activities are designed for children and run throughout the day.

Star-Spangled Symphony at Ford Amphitheater

The Colorado Springs Philharmonic performs its annual Star-Spangled Symphony at Ford Amphitheater. Gates open at 5:30 p.m., the show starts at 6:30 p.m., and tickets begin at $18. The fireworks finale is cancelled, but the performance continues — Pikes Peak as a backdrop at golden hour is not nothing.

The repertoire typically includes the 1812 Overture, Stars and Stripes Forever, and patriotic classics alongside popular selections. It's an evening event, which may or may not be a fit depending on the ages of your kids.

What's Next

If you're in Colorado Springs city limits, the personal fireworks rules are clear: consumer fireworks including sparklers are permanently banned every year. In unincorporated El Paso County, the situation is more complicated — the county is under Stage II fire restrictions but has not banned personal fireworks. Mayor Mobolade has asked El Paso County residents not to use them this weekend.

July 4 without the fireworks display is a different kind of holiday. The options above are all genuine, all happening, and all worth checking out. The mountain is still there. The community events are still there. The day is still the day.
