Cosyland Children's Tower Stools Recalled — 125,200 Units Pose Entrapment and Fall Hazard

By Jamie Rivera | July 2, 2026

If you bought a Cosyland tower stool on Amazon between 2021 and 2025, you need to stop using it. Here's what the recall involves and how to get a free repair kit.

Cosyland is recalling approximately 125,200 children's tower stools due to entrapment and fall hazards, the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission announced. The recall covers models CS0003 and CS0092-4 — sold on Amazon for approximately $70 from April 2021 through November 2025.

If you ordered a Cosyland tower stool from Amazon in the past several years, there's a meaningful chance you own a recalled unit. The CPSC has received 25 reports of stability issues and falls related to these stools, including 8 injuries. Those injuries range from scrapes and bruises to a fractured arm.

What the Hazard Is

Tower stools — also called helper stools or learning towers — are designed to bring children up to counter height so they can participate in kitchen activities alongside adults. They're a popular tool for parents who want to involve kids in cooking and daily tasks from a young age.

The specific hazard with the Cosyland models involves two problems that interact: the stools can collapse or tip over while in use, and the openings on the front and back sides of the stool are wide enough for a child's torso to fit through. The combination creates a risk of a child becoming entrapped in the structure or falling from height.

The recalled stools come in natural bamboo and gray, measure approximately 16 inches deep by 18 inches wide by 35 inches tall, and carry the model numbers CS0003 or CS0092-4.

What to Do Now

Stop using the recalled stools immediately and store them away from children until repaired. Cosyland is providing a free repair kit that includes protective nets, stabilizing feet, and installation instructions. The nets address the entrapment hazard; the stabilizing feet address the tip-over risk.

What's Next

Once you have the repair kit installed, Cosyland says the product can be used again safely. If you prefer not to repair it, keep it stored away from children and contact Cosyland to understand your options.

Tower stools remain a useful kitchen tool for families who want to involve young children in cooking. If you're looking for a replacement, check the CPSC website for products that have not been recalled. For any children's product purchased through Amazon or a third-party online retailer, it's worth cross-referencing with cpsc.gov/Recalls periodically — recalls on online-sold products can take time to reach consumers who don't see a direct notice from the retailer.
