### STORY 1: COS July 4 Fireworks Cancelled

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

Big news for families planning July 4 weekend in Colorado Springs: all professional fireworks displays have been cancelled. 🎇

Mayor Yemi Mobolade, the Colorado Springs Fire Department, and the Colorado Springs Police Department made the joint call on July 1 after reviewing current fire conditions. Officials said fuel moisture levels in Colorado Springs right now are comparable to what they measured in the days before the 2012 Waldo Canyon Fire. That's the kind of comparison that puts the decision in context quickly.

What's been cancelled: All permitted, professional July 4 fireworks displays in the city. The Star-Spangled Symphony at Ford Amphitheater continues — just without the traditional fireworks finale.

What's not changing: All other July 4 festivities (live music, food trucks, community events) are moving forward as planned.

And a reminder that's true every year but worth repeating: Consumer fireworks — including sparklers — are permanently banned within Colorado Springs city limits. That's not a new rule for 2026. It's the law every year.

In unincorporated El Paso County, it's a more complicated picture. The county is under Stage II fire restrictions but is legally unable to ban personal fireworks from May 31 to July 5 without a Board of County Commissioners resolution. The county hasn't passed one. Mayor Mobolade has publicly asked El Paso County residents not to use personal fireworks this weekend.

If you're planning July 4 activities — there are still good options. We've got a guide to family-friendly alternatives in our next post. 🌄

### STORY 2: Cosyland Tower Stools Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

If you have a Cosyland children's tower stool at home, it's time to stop using it. 🚨

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission announced that Cosyland is recalling approximately 125,200 of its children's tower stools — sold on Amazon for around $70 between April 2021 and November 2025 — due to entrapment and fall hazards. If you ordered one of these from Amazon in the last few years, there's a good chance you have a recalled unit.

Here's the issue: A child's torso can fit through openings on the front and back sides of the stool, and the structure can collapse or tip while in use. That combination creates a serious hazard. Cosyland has received 25 reports of stability issues and falls, including 8 injuries. Those injuries range from scrapes and bruises to a fractured arm.

The recalled stools come in natural bamboo and gray, measure about 16 inches deep by 18 inches wide by 35 inches tall, and carry the model numbers CS0003 or CS0092-4.

What to do: Stop use immediately and store it away from your kids. Then contact Cosyland Official for a free repair kit — protective nets, stabilizing feet, and installation instructions — that addresses the hazard.

Reach Cosyland at 866-677-3889, Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. Pacific time. Or find the full recall at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

### STORY 3: D11 Free Summer Lunch Program

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

There are 29 days left in D11's free summer lunch program — and if you haven't checked it out yet, here's why it's worth knowing about. 🥗

Colorado Springs School District 11 runs a free Summer Food Service Program every year, providing free meals to all kids ages 1 through 18 — regardless of which school district they attend. That's important. You don't have to be a D11 family to show up.

The program runs Monday through Friday through July 31, 2026. Multiple sites across Colorado Springs offer free breakfast (where available) and lunch. One of the lesser-known options: D11's mobile unit visits East Library every weekday from 11 a.m. to noon, delivering lunch alongside age-appropriate books. It's a two-for-one that helps address both summer hunger and the summer reading slide (kids lose 25–30% of their literacy gains over the summer without regular reading).

PPLD also partners to offer free summer lunches at library locations around COS — another option worth checking if you're near a branch.

All sites are closed July 4.

To find locations near you or get more information, call D11 Food and Nutrition Services at 719-520-2930.

### STORY 4: July 4 Alternatives for COS Families

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

The fireworks are cancelled — but July 4 in Colorado Springs is still happening. Here's where families can go. 🌄

**Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site**
This is the pick for families who want a full-day experience. Rock Ledge Ranch runs an Old-Fashioned July 4 celebration from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. Admission is $7–$13; children 3 and under are free. You'll find historical interpreters, patriotic music, carnival games, and activities aimed squarely at kids. It's the kind of July 4 that existed before fireworks became the whole event.

**Banning Lewis Ranch — Vista Park Pavilion**
Free and community-focused. The event at 8833 Vista Del Pico Blvd starts at 3 p.m. and runs into the evening with food trucks and live music. No admission required.

**National Museum of WWII Aviation**
Open July 4, 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Vintage military vehicle displays, vintage auto shows, and children's STEM activities. A solid option if you have kids who are into history or things that go.

**The Star-Spangled Symphony**
The Colorado Springs Philharmonic at Ford Amphitheater still performs July 4. Gates open at 5:30 p.m., show starts at 6:30 p.m., tickets from $18. The fireworks are gone but the orchestra is not — and Pikes Peak makes a pretty good backdrop regardless.

The fire danger is real and the cancellation was the right call. But there are still good ways to mark the day with your family.

### STORY 5: America 250 in COS

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

2026 is a milestone year — and Colorado Springs has been building toward it all summer. 🇺🇸

This year marks America's 250th birthday, and Colorado's 150th anniversary as a state. The City of Colorado Springs has designated events and historic sites all summer as part of its "Celebrate America 250 and Colorado 150" programming.

With the July 4 fireworks cancelled due to fire danger, the city's emphasis on history-focused, community-centered events takes on extra weight. Colorado Springs has a remarkable amount of history within driving distance — from Garden of the Gods to the Cheyenne Mountain Zoo to the Air Force Academy chapel to Rock Ledge Ranch. July 4 weekend, and the rest of the summer, is a genuinely good time to explore it with kids.

The connection between place and history is one of those things families often mean to make time for and don't quite get around to. This year there are more reasons than usual to make time.
