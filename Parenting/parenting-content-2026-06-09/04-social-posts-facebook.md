### STORY 1: Colorado Renaissance Festival Opens June 13-14

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

The Colorado Renaissance Festival is back, and this Saturday is the best day to go.

The 49th season opens June 13 in Larkspur — a 30-minute drive from Colorado Springs — and the opening weekend has two things going for it that later weekends don't. First, the theme: Magical Fantasy Weekend. Think dragons, wizards, and all the fantasy creatures your kids have ever asked to see in real life. Second, it's First Responder Appreciation Weekend. If you or your partner is a firefighter, police officer, or sheriff's department employee, you get a free adult ticket with every one you purchase — and your kids under 12 get in free.

For everyone else, the most budget-friendly move is to grab tickets at King Soopers before you go: $29 for adults, $14 for kids ages 5-12, and under 5 is always free. Day-of tickets at the gate cost a bit more, so the King Soopers stop saves a few dollars per person.

The festival runs every Saturday and Sunday through August 2, plus the July 4 weekend. This year's location is 650 W Perry Park Ave, Larkspur, on the same 350-acre forested site it's occupied since 1976. Jousting, face painting, rides, live performances, food, and more — it's a Colorado summer tradition.

### STORY 2: COS Public Pools and Free Splash Pads

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

Staying cool in Colorado Springs this summer doesn't have to cost much — or anything at all.

Here's the quick guide. Two outdoor pools are open through Labor Day, run in partnership between the city and Pikes Peak YMCA. Wilson Ranch Pool and Monument Valley Pool both offer $10 day passes for kids and $15 for adults, with no YMCA membership required. New this year: you can make reservations in advance. Wilson Ranch is open daily 10 AM-6 PM. Monument Valley has the same daytime hours Monday through Saturday, with Sunday hours running noon to 6 PM.

If you want free, the city has three spray grounds. Deerfield Hills Spray Ground in southeast COS has more than 50 individual spray nozzles and 16 water features, open Wednesday 11:30 AM-5 PM and other days 10 AM-5 PM. The Watering Hole at Venezia Park in northeast COS has more than a dozen water features right next to an award-winning universally accessible playground — a great combination for families with kids of varying abilities. The Splash Pad at Panorama Park in southeast COS is open daily 10 AM-6 PM. All three are free, no passes required.

A note on Portal Pool: it remains closed for a major renovation with no near-term reopening date.

### STORY 3: CPSC Pool Safety Recalls

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

Before your kids get in the pool this summer, there are two CPSC recalls you should know about.

The first involves pool drain covers. Broqixin pool drain covers, sold by a seller called Eicong on Amazon, have been recalled because they violate the Virginia Graeme Baker Pool and Spa Safety Act — a federal law enacted after a child drowned when her body was trapped against a drain cover. The recalled covers pose an entrapment and drowning risk. If you have these drain covers in your pool or spa, stop using the pool immediately and contact Eicong for a full refund.

The second recall involves Blue Wave above-ground pools that stand 48 inches or taller. These pools have been recalled due to a drowning hazard. If you recently purchased or set up a Blue Wave pool of this height, stop using it and check CPSC.gov for the specific model numbers affected and remedies available.

Pool drain safety is often overlooked because drain covers don't look like safety equipment — but they're what keeps swimmers from becoming trapped underwater. The Virginia Graeme Baker law exists because of what happens when they fail. If your pool uses drain covers you haven't recently inspected or updated, now is the time.

### STORY 5: Tiny Land Play Tent Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

If your family bought a Tiny Land children's play tent in the last several months, there's a recall you need to know about.

Tiny Land tents sold on Amazon and at Tinylandus.com between November 2025 and March 2026 — priced between $54 and $70 — have been recalled by the CPSC. The hazard is in the support poles: they're made of fiberglass, and fiberglass poles can shed fibers that cause skin and eye irritation. Eight incidents have been reported; no serious injuries have occurred.

The fix is free and straightforward. Stop using the tent immediately. Disassemble it and store the poles somewhere children can't reach them. Then contact Tiny Land through their website — tinylandus.com/pages/recalls — to request a free replacement kit with four plastic poles, including shipping. The plastic poles serve the same structural function without the fiber-shedding risk.

Play tents are popular backyard items right now as families spend more time outside. If yours came from Amazon in the past six months, check the packaging or product listing to see if it's a Tiny Land tent.

Did you know you can sign up for CPSC recall alerts by email? It's free at CPSC.gov and can save a lot of searching.
