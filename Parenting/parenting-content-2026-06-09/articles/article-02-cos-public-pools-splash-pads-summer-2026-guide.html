COS Public Pools and Free Splash Pads: Your Summer 2026 Cool-Down Guide

By Jamie Rivera | June 9, 2026

Colorado Springs summers heat up fast, and with Portal Pool still years away from reopening, families have been navigating a smaller set of city-operated water options. The good news: what's available this summer is genuinely useful, and two of the best options are free.

Outdoor Pools: Low Cost, No Membership Required

Two outdoor pools operate this summer in partnership between Colorado Springs and the Pikes Peak YMCA — and for the first time in 2026, you don't need a YMCA membership to swim.

Wilson Ranch Pool is open Monday through Sunday, 10 AM to 6 PM, through Labor Day. Day passes are $10 for children and $15 for adults. New this year, you can reserve your spot in advance through the PPYMCA website.

Monument Valley Pool has the same day-pass pricing ($10 children, $15 adults) and is open Monday through Saturday from 10 AM to 6 PM, with Sunday hours running noon to 6 PM. Starting August 10, both pools switch to modified hours for the back half of summer.

Neither pool requires YMCA membership for day passes, which is a change from previous seasons. If you're visiting once or twice rather than swimming all summer, a day pass is the straightforward choice.

Free Spray Grounds: Three Options Across the City

For families who want free water play, Colorado Springs operates three spray grounds — and all three are genuinely good.

Deerfield Hills Spray Ground in southeast Colorado Springs is one of the largest, featuring more than 50 individual spray nozzles and 16 water spray features. It opened June 1 and operates Wednesday from 11:30 AM to 5 PM, and all other days from 10 AM to 5 PM. No passes, no fees.

The Watering Hole at Venezia Park in northeast Colorado Springs offers more than a dozen water features right next to an award-winning universally accessible playground — a combination that makes it a strong choice for families with children of varying ages and abilities. It runs daily during summer.

The Splash Pad at Panorama Park in southeast Colorado Springs opened May 24 and runs daily from 10 AM to 6 PM. It's another free option in the southeast part of the city, which also has Deerfield Hills nearby.

Indoor Option: Great Wolf Lodge

For a larger water park experience, Great Wolf Lodge in Colorado Springs operates an indoor water park and offers day passes for families not staying overnight. Summer passes are available through August. It's the most expensive option on this list, but it offers the most amenities and weather protection for Colorado's afternoon storm season.

What About Portal Pool?

Portal Pool, one of Colorado Springs' long-established public swimming pools, has been closed for a major renovation and is not expected to reopen in the near term. The closure has reduced the city's outdoor public pool options, which is part of why the PPYMCA partnership pools at Wilson Ranch and Monument Valley carry more weight this summer than they have in past years.

What's Next

Outdoor pools at Wilson Ranch and Monument Valley are open through Labor Day. Spray grounds operate through the summer season. For current hours, pool status, and reservation information, visit ppymca.org and coloradosprings.gov/parks.
