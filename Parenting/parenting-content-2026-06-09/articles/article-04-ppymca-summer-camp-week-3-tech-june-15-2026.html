PPYMCA Summer Day Camp Week 3: Technology Week Brings Music, Movies, and Code to COS Kids

By Jamie Rivera | June 9, 2026

Pikes Peak YMCA Summer Day Camp's Week 3 (June 15-19) is built around technology — not in the passive, screen-time sense, but as a creative medium. Campers ages 5-12 will spend the week making music, producing animated films, and writing code alongside three Colorado Springs organizations that work in these spaces professionally.

What Happens Each Day

The week's programming pulls in three local partners, each bringing a different dimension of creative technology to camp:

Colorado Springs Conservatory leads music production and songwriting sessions. The Conservatory is one of COS's most established arts education organizations, and its involvement means kids get professional-level instruction on what it takes to compose and produce a piece of music from scratch.

Snapology runs the movie making and animation workshops. Snapology specializes in STEM and STEAM programming for kids, and their animation curriculum gives campers a hands-on look at how the movies and YouTube videos they love actually get made.

Space Foundation leads the programming and coding workshop. The Space Foundation, headquartered in Colorado Springs, brings its education expertise to teach kids the basics of writing code — a skill that connects directly to careers in aerospace, tech, and creative industries.

Who This Week Is For

Week 3 is a strong fit for kids who are curious about how things are made — especially things on screens. It's also good for kids who might not yet know they're interested in technology, because each session is activity-based rather than lecture-based. The music production session doesn't require any prior musical experience. The coding workshop is designed for beginners. The animation workshop works for kids who like to draw or who just like watching cartoons.

Ages 5-12 are eligible. The programming adapts to different age groups within that range.

Registration and Financial Assistance

Registration for Week 3 is open at ppymca.org. The YMCA scholarship program provides financial assistance for families who need it — you can ask about it when registering online or by calling your nearest PPYMCA location. The scholarship is not advertised prominently but is genuinely available, and the YMCA encourages families to ask.

What's Next

Week 3 of PPYMCA Summer Day Camp runs June 15-19 for ages 5-12. The full summer program catalog, weekly themes, and registration information are at ppymca.org. Week 2 (June 8-12, "Low-Key Famous Artist Week") is currently underway.
