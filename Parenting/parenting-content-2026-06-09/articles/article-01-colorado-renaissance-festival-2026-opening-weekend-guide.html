Colorado Renaissance Festival 2026 Opening Weekend Guide: What COS Families Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | June 9, 2026

The Colorado Renaissance Festival opens this Saturday, June 13, in Larkspur — and the opening weekend comes with a theme, a military discount, and the best ticket prices of the season if you know where to shop. Here's what Colorado Springs families need to know before heading up I-25.

The 49th Season

The Colorado Renaissance Festival is in its 49th year, and it returns to the same 350-acre forested site at 650 W Perry Park Ave in Larkspur where it has been held since 1976. The festival runs every Saturday and Sunday through August 2, plus a three-day run over the July 4 holiday weekend (Friday through Sunday). From Colorado Springs, it's a drive of roughly 30 minutes north on I-25 into Douglas County.

The 2026 season features themed weekends, with each two-day stretch dedicated to a different concept. Opening weekend is always one of the best times to go — the energy is high and the crowds haven't yet hit their summer peak.

Opening Weekend: Magical Fantasy and First Responders

June 13 and 14 carry two themes simultaneously. First, it's Magical Fantasy Weekend — the season-opener is dedicated to fantasy creatures of all ages and varieties. If your kids have ever wanted to see a dragon, a wizard, or an armored knight up close in a forest setting, opening weekend delivers that. Costumes are encouraged but never required.

Second, June 13-14 is First Responder Appreciation Weekend. Firefighters, police officers, and sheriff's department personnel receive a buy one, get one free offer on adult admission. Children of first responders under the age of 12 are admitted free. If anyone in your family qualifies, this weekend is the lowest-cost option of the entire season.

The cheapest option is buying advance tickets at King Soopers — the savings aren't enormous, but for a family of four it adds up. Tickets at the gate on the day of the event cost $3 more per adult and $1 more per child than the King Soopers price. Under 5 is always free regardless of where you buy.

What to Expect at the Festival

The festival offers the full Renaissance fair experience on a large scale: live jousting tournaments, stage entertainment, artisan shopping, rides, face painting, food and beverage vendors, and costumed performers moving through the grounds. The 350-acre forested site means shade throughout the day, which matters for a Colorado summer afternoon at altitude.

Children tend to be most engaged by the jousting, the interactive stage shows, and the craft demonstrations. Plan for at least three to four hours if you want to cover the main areas without rushing. Bring a water bottle and sunscreen — Perry Park sits at roughly 6,700 feet elevation, and UV exposure at altitude is higher than in Colorado Springs proper.

What's Next

The Colorado Renaissance Festival runs every Saturday and Sunday through August 2, 2026, with themed weekends throughout the season. Advance tickets, the full schedule of themed weekends, and additional information are available at the festival's official website. Tickets at King Soopers are available at customer service counters.
