Two Pool Safety Recalls: Drain Covers and Above-Ground Pools Pose Drowning Risks

By Sarah Morales | June 9, 2026

Two separate recalls issued by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission in June 2026 affect pool equipment that many Colorado Springs families may have purchased this season. With summer swim season in full swing, parents and caregivers who own above-ground pools or recently purchased pool drain covers should verify their equipment before allowing children in the water.

Recall 1: Broqixin Pool Drain Covers (Sold by Eicong on Amazon)

The CPSC has recalled pool drain covers sold under the Broqixin name by a seller called Eicong on Amazon.com. The drain covers violate the Virginia Graeme Baker Pool and Spa Safety Act — a federal law enacted after a child drowned when her body became trapped against a poorly designed drain cover. The recalled covers pose entrapment and drowning hazards.

Pool drain safety is often overlooked because drain covers appear to be simple, static equipment. But when a drain cover fails to meet federal safety standards, the suction force in a pool or spa can trap a swimmer against it, making it impossible to break free. The Virginia Graeme Baker law was written specifically to prevent this.

What to do: If you purchased pool drain covers from Amazon.com and the seller name was Eicong, stop using your pool immediately. Contact Eicong for a full refund. Check CPSC.gov for specific model numbers and the most current remediation instructions.

Recall 2: Blue Wave Above-Ground Pools (48 Inches and Taller)

Blue Wave above-ground pools that stand 48 inches or taller have also been recalled by the CPSC due to a drowning hazard. Above-ground pools at this height present specific risks to young children, including the possibility of accidental entry, falls into the water, and the challenge of exit when a child cannot reach the ladder.

What to do: If you own a Blue Wave above-ground pool that is 48 inches or taller, check the model number against the CPSC.gov recall listing. If your pool is included, stop using it and follow the CPSC-listed remedy instructions for that specific model.

General Pool Safety Reminders for Colorado Springs Families

Both of these recalls are a reminder to inspect pool equipment at the start of each season. Pool drain covers should be VGBA-compliant and replaced if they show signs of wear or if they were purchased from unknown sources online. Above-ground pools at any height require a securely locked or removed ladder when not in active use, and children should never be left unattended near water.

At Colorado Springs altitude (6,035 feet), children become dehydrated and fatigued more quickly, which can affect their ability to respond to water emergencies. Supervision is the first line of defense.

What's Next

Both recalls are posted in full at CPSC.gov, including specific model numbers, seller information, and steps to request refunds or remedies. You can also sign up for CPSC email recall alerts at no cost to receive future product safety notifications automatically.
