Tiny Land Play Tent Recall: Fiberglass Poles Can Cause Skin and Eye Irritation

By Sarah Morales | June 9, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has announced a recall of Tiny Land children's play tents sold on Amazon.com and Tinylandus.com between November 2025 and March 2026. The tents' fiberglass support poles can shed fibers that cause skin and eye irritation. Eight incidents have been reported; no serious injuries have occurred. A free fix is available from the company.

What's the Problem

The recalled Tiny Land tents use fiberglass poles as structural supports. Fiberglass is a common material in outdoor and sporting goods, but when it sheds — through normal use, repeated setup and takedown, or rough handling by children — the fibers it releases are very fine. Skin contact causes itching and redness. If fibers reach the eyes, they cause irritation and can be difficult to remove.

Children are particularly at risk because they handle their toys differently than adults do. A child collapsing and repacking a tent, dragging it across the yard, or bending the poles during play creates more friction and fiber release than careful adult handling would.

Which Tents Are Affected

Tiny Land children's play tents sold between November 2025 and March 2026, priced between $54 and $70, are included in the recall. The tents were sold on Amazon.com and directly at Tinylandus.com. If you purchased a Tiny Land play tent during this period through either channel, treat it as recalled until confirmed otherwise.

Check Tinylandus.com/pages/recalls for the specific model numbers included and to verify whether your tent is affected.

What to Do Right Now

Stop using the tent immediately. Disassemble it and place the fiberglass poles somewhere children cannot reach — a high shelf, a storage bin, or a garage area. Do not just leave the tent set up in the backyard or put it back in a toy chest where kids can access the poles.

Contact Tiny Land through their recall page at tinylandus.com/pages/recalls to request a free replacement kit. The company will send four replacement plastic poles with shipping included. The plastic poles serve the same structural function as the fiberglass ones without the shedding risk. Once you have the replacement kit, the tent can be used again.

If Your Child Has Already Had Symptoms

Skin irritation from fiberglass fibers typically presents as redness and itching in areas of contact. Washing the affected skin with soap and water and applying a cool compress usually resolves mild irritation. Eye irritation should be treated by rinsing the eye with clean water. If symptoms persist or worsen, contact your child's pediatrician.

What's Next

The full recall details, model number list, and instructions for requesting the free replacement kit are at tinylandus.com/pages/recalls and CPSC.gov. If you have questions, contact Tiny Land directly through the recall page. You can also sign up for future CPSC recall alerts by email at CPSC.gov — it's free and ensures you hear about product safety issues before they become a problem in your home.
