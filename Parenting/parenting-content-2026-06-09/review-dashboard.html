Colorado Renaissance Festival Opens June 13-14 — Opening Weekend Guide - **Tier:** 1 - **Angle:** The 49th season of the Colorado Renaissance Festival opens this Saturday, June 13, in Larkspur — roughly 30 minutes from Colorado Springs via I-25. It runs every Saturday and Sunday through August 2, plus the July 4 holiday weekend. The opening weekend (June 13-14) is "Magical Fantasy Weekend," themed around fantasy creatures of all kinds. It's also First Responder Appreciation Weekend: firefighters, police, and sheriff's department personnel receive BOGO adult admission, and their children under 12 get in free. King Soopers carries the best-priced advance tickets: $29 adults, $14 kids 5-12, under 5 free. Located at 650 W Perry Park Ave, Larkspur, on a 350-acre forested site the festival has called home since 1976.

COS Public Pools and Free Splash Pads — Summer 2026 Guide
NOT VERIFIEDBRIEF
COS Public Pools and Free Splash Pads — Summer 2026 Guide - **Tier:** 1 - **Angle:** With Portal Pool years from reopening, Colorado Springs families have three affordable or free ways to stay cool this summer. Two YMCA-partnership outdoor pools are open Memorial Day through Labor Day — no membership required. Wilson Ranch Pool is open Monday through Sunday, 10 AM to 6 PM; day passes are $10 for children and $15 for adults. Monument Valley Pool has the same pricing with Sunday hours starting at noon. Three free spray grounds operate across the city: Deerfield Hills Spray Ground (southeast COS, 50+ spray nozzles, 16 water features), the Watering Hole at Venezia Park (northeast COS, universally accessible playground adjacent), and the Splash Pad at Panorama Park (southeast COS, daily 10 AM-6 PM). Great Wolf Lodge offers indoor water park day passes for families wanting a larger experience.

CPSC Pool Safety Recall: Drain Covers and Above-Ground Pools
NOT VERIFIEDBRIEF
CPSC Pool Safety Recall: Drain Covers and Above-Ground Pools - **Tier:** 1 - **Angle:** Two separate CPSC recalls issued in June 2026 affect pool equipment that many Colorado Springs families may own as summer swim season peaks. First: Broqixin pool drain covers (sold by seller Eicong on Amazon) violate the Virginia Graeme Baker Pool and Spa Safety Act and pose entrapment and drowning hazards — pool owners should stop using their pools immediately and contact Eicong for a full refund. Second: Blue Wave above-ground pools that are 48 inches tall or taller have been recalled due to a drowning hazard. If you set up a new pool or bought pool equipment this spring, now is the time to check model numbers against CPSC.gov.

Tiny Land Play Tent Recall — Fiberglass Poles Pose Skin and Eye Risk
NOT VERIFIEDBRIEF
Tiny Land Play Tent Recall — Fiberglass Poles Pose Skin and Eye Risk - **Tier:** 2 - **Angle:** Tiny Land children's play tents sold on Amazon and Tinylandus.com between November 2025 and March 2026 have been recalled by the CPSC due to fiberglass poles that can shed fibers, causing skin and eye irritation. The tents sold for $54 to $70 each. Eight incidents of skin or eye irritation from the fiber shedding have been reported; no serious injuries. Remedy: stop using the tent immediately, disassemble it, and keep the poles away from children. Contact Tiny Land to receive a free repair kit with four replacement plastic poles, including shipping.
