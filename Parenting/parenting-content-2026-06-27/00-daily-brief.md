### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — MedPride Baby Oil Bottles Lack Child-Resistant Packaging
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY (recall announced June 25, 2026)
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Sources:** cpsc.gov, abc27.com, morningstar.com/news
**Posting Window:** 8:00 AM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Shield Line recalled approximately 8,420 bottles of MedPride Baby Oil on June 25, 2026, because the packaging is not child-resistant as required by the Poison Prevention Packaging Act. The oil contains low-viscosity hydrocarbons; if swallowed by young children, petroleum distillates can enter the lungs and cause chemical pneumonia or pulmonary damage, which can be fatal. The bottles are clear with a pink cap and white-and-pink labels featuring the "MedPride" logo and "baby oil" in pink lettering. No incidents have been reported. The remedy: secure the oil out of sight and reach of children immediately, then contact Shield Line for a full refund. Consumers will be asked to submit a photo of the recalled product in the trash to receive the refund.

### STORY 5: Summer Screen Time Tips — What the AAP Says and What COS Offers Instead
**Tier:** 2
**Status:** NEW STORY (summer-relevant; AAP 2026 guidance)
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Sources:** healthychildren.org, health.choc.org, aap.org, visitcos.com
**Posting Window:** 5:00 PM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

With school out and summer routines looser, screen time tends to climb for most Colorado Springs families. The American Academy of Pediatrics' 2026 guidance prioritizes quality and context over rigid time limits but maintains core guardrails: no screens for children under 18 months (except video calls); high-quality co-viewed content for ages 18–24 months; a maximum of one hour per day for children ages 2–5 (watched together, with discussion); and consistent, enforced limits for ages six and up that do not displace sleep, physical activity, or family time. Screen use should never be a substitute for outdoor play, social interaction, or age-appropriate independent activities. COS families have abundant free alternatives: America the Beautiful Park's Julie Penrose Fountain, Garden of the Gods trails, and PPLD programming offer screen-free engagement throughout the summer.
