## STORY 1: MedPride Baby Oil Recall

**Verified Facts (HIGH confidence — CPSC official + multiple news sources):**
- Recall date: June 25, 2026
- Manufacturer/recaller: Shield Line (the company selling MedPride Baby Oil)
- Product: MedPride Baby Oil bottles
- Units: approximately 8,420
- Sold on Amazon
- Sale period: not specified in search results
- Price: not specified in search results
- Hazard: packaging is NOT child-resistant as required by Poison Prevention Packaging Act
- Oil contains low-viscosity hydrocarbons; if swallowed, petroleum distillates can enter lungs causing chemical pneumonia/pulmonary damage (can be fatal)
- Description: clear bottle with pink cap, white-and-pink labels, "MedPride" logo, "baby oil" in pink lettering
- Incidents: none reported
- Remedy: secure out of reach immediately; contact Shield Line for full refund; submit photo of product in trash to receive refund
- CPSC hotline: 1-800-638-2772 / cpsc.gov

**Sources:** cpsc.gov (official recall page), abc27.com (news coverage), morningstar.com/prnewswire (CPSC press release June 25)

## STORY 5: Screen Time — AAP Guidelines

**Verified Facts (HIGH confidence — from AAP official sources via CHOC and HealthyChildren.org):**
- Organization: American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP)
- 2026 guidance focus: quality, context, and co-viewing — not just time limits
- Guidelines by age:
  - Under 18 months: no screens except video calls
  - 18–24 months: high-quality content, co-viewed with parent/caregiver
  - Ages 2–5: maximum one hour per day; co-view and discuss content
  - Ages 6 and up: consistent limits; screen use must not displace sleep, physical activity, family time, or free play
- Key principle: screens should not replace sleep (8–12 hours depending on age), physical activity (one hour daily), or face-to-face interaction
- Summer context: school-year structures are gone; screens tend to fill unstructured time
- COS free alternatives: America the Beautiful Park (Julie Penrose Fountain), Garden of the Gods trails, PPLD programming

**Sources:** healthychildren.org, health.choc.org, aap.org, visitcos.com (for local COS alternatives)
