Rosemont Reservoir May Open for Camping and Boating Around July 4

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | June 27, 2026

If you've been following the Rosemont Reservoir story since we first covered it in June, here's the update: it's still on track. Colorado Springs Utilities and Colorado Parks and Wildlife have been working toward an opening tied to July 4, 2026 — the Colorado 150th and US 250th anniversary celebrations. That target is one week away, and while an official confirmed date hasn't been announced, everything points to the reservoir being open for camping and non-motorized boating around the holiday weekend.

Where Rosemont Reservoir Is — and Why It Matters

Rosemont Reservoir sits about 12 miles southwest of Colorado Springs on the south side of Pikes Peak, at an elevation of more than 9,600 feet. Until this year, the reservoir has been off-limits for recreation. Colorado Springs City Council approved a partnership between Colorado Springs Utilities and Colorado Parks and Wildlife earlier in 2026, opening the reservoir to the public for the first time.

For families in COS, this is a meaningful addition to the local outdoor options. At nearly 10,000 feet, you're getting genuine high-alpine scenery — pine forests, rocky ridgelines, and the kind of quiet that's hard to find closer to town.

What to Expect When It Opens

The planned amenities at Rosemont are modest but solid: basic campsites, day-use parking, ADA-accessible restrooms, and non-motorized boating access. That means you can bring a kayak, canoe, paddleboard, or electric watercraft — but no gas motors. No swimming or wading is allowed, because the reservoir serves as a drinking water source for Colorado Springs. Campfire pits are included at campsites.

You'll need a Colorado State Parks Pass or a Keep Colorado Wild pass to enter, plus a separate camping fee. Reservations are handled through CPW's reservation system — the same platform you'd use for Cheyenne Mountain State Park or Eleven Mile. If you're planning to camp over July 4 weekend specifically, it's worth checking CPW's site now to see if reservations are open.

The Partnership Behind the Opening

Colorado Springs Utilities owns Rosemont Reservoir and manages it as a water supply. The arrangement with Colorado Parks and Wildlife is the key to making recreation possible — CPW oversees the recreational operations while Colorado Springs Utilities retains ownership. The Colorado Department of Natural Resources is funding staffing. City Council's approval earlier this year was the formal authorization that made all of it possible.
