Colorado Springs 4th of July 2026: Family Guide to Events and Fireworks

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | June 27, 2026

July 4, 2026 carries a little extra weight this year. It's not just Independence Day — it's the 250th anniversary of the United States, and it's also Colorado's 150th birthday. Colorado Springs has a full lineup of events to match the occasion, including multiple free options, one major ticketed show, and a daytime alternative built specifically for families with younger kids who aren't staying up until 9 PM for fireworks.

Here's everything COS families need to know to plan their day.

The Star-Spangled Symphony at Ford Amphitheater

The marquee event for 2026 is the Star-Spangled Symphony and Grand Fireworks Show at Ford Amphitheater (95 Spectrum Loop). The Colorado Springs Philharmonic performs starting at 6:30 PM, with gates opening at 5:30 PM. Fireworks are scheduled for approximately 9:00 PM.

This year's program is genuinely impressive: the set list includes Dolly Parton, Tina Turner, and Marvin Gaye hits alongside a Star Wars suite, the 1812 Overture, and Stars and Stripes Forever. It's organized by the Colorado Springs Sports Corporation. This is a ticketed event — check coloradospringssports.org for pricing and availability.

Banning Lewis Ranch: Free, with 30 Food Trucks

If you want a no-cost community event, Banning Lewis Ranch is hosting a July 4 Celebration at Vista Park Pavilion. Two live bands, 30 food trucks, and fireworks at dusk make it a full evening. It's a newer event on the COS calendar, but the food truck lineup alone makes it worth the trip.

Palmer Lake: Free Fireworks at 9:15 PM

Palmer Lake Centennial Park runs from 4:00 to 10:00 PM, with fireworks starting around 9:15 PM. The event includes local vendors, food, drinks, and family-friendly activities. Palmer Lake is about 20 miles north of Colorado Springs — worth the drive if you want a smaller-town feel for the holiday.

Rock Ledge Ranch: The Daytime Option

For families with younger kids who won't make it to 9 PM fireworks, Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site near Garden of the Gods offers an old-fashioned Fourth from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM. Think carnival games, a pie-eating contest, historical interpreters, and patriotic music. No fireworks, but a genuinely fun daytime experience that doesn't require keeping anyone awake past their bedtime. Admission is typically charged at Rock Ledge Ranch.

Planning Tips for COS Families

A few things worth knowing before you go: parking near Ford Amphitheater fills early, so build in extra time if you're attending the symphony. For Palmer Lake, heading up Hwy 105 well before the 4 PM start means avoiding some of the traffic. Rock Ledge Ranch is near Garden of the Gods, which makes for a nice combined visit if you're there in the morning before the heat of the afternoon.

What's Next

With one week until the 4th, ticket availability for the Star-Spangled Symphony may be limited — check current availability as soon as possible if that's your first choice. For the free events, no registration is required. Check the COS Parenting site for any updates on last-minute additions to the lineup closer to the holiday.
