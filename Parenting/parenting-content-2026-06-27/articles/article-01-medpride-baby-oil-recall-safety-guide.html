MedPride Baby Oil Recalled: What Colorado Springs Parents Need to Know

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | June 27, 2026

If you have a bottle of MedPride Baby Oil in your bathroom right now, stop and check it before you do anything else. Shield Line recalled approximately 8,420 bottles of this product on June 25, 2026, and the reason matters: the packaging is not child-resistant as required by federal law.

That's not a minor labeling issue. MedPride Baby Oil contains low-viscosity hydrocarbons — petroleum distillates — and if a young child swallows even a small amount, those compounds can be aspirated into the lungs. That causes chemical pneumonia or severe pulmonary damage, both of which can be fatal. The risk is serious enough that the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission issued a formal recall notice immediately.

What the Recalled Bottle Looks Like

The recalled MedPride Baby Oil comes in a clear bottle with a pink cap. The label is white and pink, features the "MedPride" logo in pink lettering, and includes the words "baby oil." The product was sold exclusively on Amazon. No incidents have been reported as of the recall date, which means acting now — before anything happens — is exactly the right move.

What the Law Requires — and Why the Packaging Failed

Federal law under the Poison Prevention Packaging Act requires that products containing petroleum-based hydrocarbons come in child-resistant containers. These requirements exist because children under five are especially vulnerable to accidental poisoning — their smaller bodies and undeveloped airways make even small exposures dangerous.

Shield Line's MedPride packaging didn't meet that standard. The cap can be removed by a young child without the resistance a proper child-resistant design provides. That's a compliance failure on a product that never should have reached store shelves without it.

What to Do Right Now

The steps are straightforward. First, find the bottle and move it completely out of reach of your children — don't leave it on a bathroom shelf or countertop while you figure out the next steps. A high cabinet or a locked storage area is the right move.

Next, contact Shield Line to request a full refund. The company will ask you to submit a photo of the product in the trash before issuing the refund — so take that photo before you throw it away.

If you've shared or gifted this product — or left it at a childcare provider's home — send them this information directly. The same risk applies anywhere the bottle might be within a child's reach.

What's Next

No injuries have been reported, and the CPSC's recall process means the product should be off Amazon now. If you can't reach Shield Line directly, visit cpsc.gov or call the CPSC's Consumer Hotline at 1-800-638-2772 for assistance. Updates to the recall will appear on the CPSC website.

For Colorado Springs parents who keep up with product safety news, the CPSC website has a free email alert system — you can sign up to receive recall notices by product category so future alerts reach you automatically.
