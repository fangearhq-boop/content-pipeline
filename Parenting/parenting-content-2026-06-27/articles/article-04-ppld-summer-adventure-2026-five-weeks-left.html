PPLD Summer Adventure 2026: Five Weeks Left and No Reason Not to Sign Up

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | June 27, 2026

If Summer Adventure fell off your radar in June, you still have time. Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure 2026 runs through July 31, which means Colorado Springs families have about five weeks left to complete the 30-day challenge and be eligible for some genuinely worthwhile prizes.

The program is free, open to all ages, and doesn't require any particular reading speed or skill level. You just have to show up — to books, to activities, to the library itself — for 30 days before the deadline.

How Summer Adventure Works

The concept is straightforward: complete 30 days of reading, creating, or exploring. Days don't need to be consecutive. You can log activity online at ppld.beanstack.org or in person at any of PPLD's 14 library locations or two bookmobiles. The Beanstack app tracks your progress and handles the prize drawings automatically once you've hit your goal.

Activities that count toward the 30 days include reading any book, listening to an audiobook, attending a library program, making something creative, or trying something new. The program is intentionally broad — it's designed to meet kids and adults where they are rather than requiring a specific type of engagement.

The Prizes Are Worth It

The grand prizes this year are better than typical summer reading program rewards. The Pikes Peak Region Attractions VIP Access Pass gives families access to a collection of Colorado Springs area attractions. The one-night stay for two at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp — a backcountry camp above the Broadmoor — is the kind of prize that doesn't come around often. Even if you don't win a grand prize, the drawing pool includes local memberships, gift cards, and family experiences from COS-area partners.

Family Fun Concerts at the Library

In addition to the reading challenge itself, PPLD is running family fun concerts throughout June and July. These are free, low-key, and work well as afternoon outings with younger kids. Check the PPLD events calendar at ppld.org for specific dates and locations.

Starting Today

The 30-day window doesn't require 30 consecutive days — you just need to accumulate 30 days of activity before July 31. If you start today and read or do something library-adjacent every day, you'd hit the goal with a week to spare. If life gets in the way here and there, you still have plenty of buffer.

Registration is available in person at any of PPLD's 14 locations or online at ppld.beanstack.org. Both the website and the app work for logging progress once you're signed up.

What's Next

The program closes on July 31 — that's the hard deadline for both activity logging and prize eligibility. If you've already been participating since June 1, check your Beanstack log to see where you stand. If you're starting fresh today, head to ppld.beanstack.org or stop by your nearest PPLD branch to get signed up.
