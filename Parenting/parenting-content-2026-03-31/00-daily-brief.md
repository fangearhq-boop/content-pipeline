### STORY 3: New Study — Science Just Identified the 7 Factors That Make Play Fun for Kids [TIER 2]
A study published March 30, 2026 in the journal Frontiers in Psychology identified seven key factors that determine whether children find group play genuinely fun: (1) being included in the group, (2) using imagination, (3) a wild and exciting atmosphere, (4) having something to do, (5) a sense of silliness or mild rule-breaking, (6) achievable goals or challenges, and (7) a subjective "play feeling" — described as the factor most central to children's sense of fun. The researchers also found that adults forcing kids to play together actively undermines the first factor (inclusion), making group play feel less fun, not more.

**Why it matters:** The study gives parents and caregivers a concrete, research-backed lens for supporting play without controlling it. The "stop forcing it" finding is immediately applicable for COS families heading into spring break and Easter weekend.
