CPSC Safety Alert: 4 Baby Products Recalled or Warned — Infant Walkers, Strollers, Bassinet, Baby Monitor

March 31, 2026 · COS Parenting

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has flagged four more children's products in its latest round of safety actions, and Colorado Springs families with infants and toddlers should check their homes immediately. This week's recalls and warnings cover infant walkers, convertible strollers, a travel bassinet, and a baby monitor — all purchased through Amazon or AliExpress within the past two years.

Uuoeebb Infant Walkers — Triple Hazard

The Uuoeebb Infant Walker, sold on Amazon by a seller called BaoD, has been recalled because it violates the mandatory safety standard for infant walkers in three separate ways. First, the walker is wide enough to fit through a standard doorway, meaning it can reach the top of a staircase without stopping. Second, the braking system fails to stop the walker at the edge of a step. Third, the leg openings in the seat are large enough for a child's head to slip through, creating an entrapment risk.

These are the three hazard categories that the mandatory infant walker standard specifically addresses — fall, doorway passage, and entrapment. A product failing all three categories represents a significant danger. Parents who have purchased an infant walker on Amazon in the past 18 months should verify the brand and model and check cpsc.gov/Recalls for the full product description and remedy instructions.

AliExpress Convertible Strollers — Restraint Failure

A line of convertible strollers sold on AliExpress has been recalled because the restraint system can fail during use, creating a fall hazard. These strollers were sold between September 2024 and October 2025 for approximately $215 to $415.

Parents who purchased a stroller on AliExpress during this window should stop using it and contact AliExpress directly for a refund. The recall instructions require cutting the restraints and emailing a photo of the destroyed product to document the return. The specific refund contact is us_product_recall@aliexpress.com.

Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets — A CPSC Warning, Not a Recall

The Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinet situation is the most urgent on this list, and the most unusual. The CPSC issued a Warning rather than a formal recall because the company refused to cooperate with the recall process. This distinction matters for parents: a formal recall comes with an official remedy program — a refund, replacement, or repair. A CPSC Warning has no such program, because the company has not agreed to provide one.

About 250 travel bassinets were sold on Amazon between April and October 2025 for approximately $40. The product poses strangulation and fall hazards. CPSC's guidance is direct: stop using the bassinet immediately, remove the foam padding, cut the cover in half, and dispose of both the cover and the padding. Do not donate or sell this product.

If you purchased a Tuyedoqe travel bassinet, document your purchase and contact Amazon's customer service, which may offer a refund even without a formal recall program in place.

Babysense Max View Baby Monitor — Fire Hazard

The parent unit — the display screen — of the Babysense Max View Baby Monitor can overheat and spark when plugged in for charging. This is a fire hazard. Many families charge baby monitors plugged in near the crib or on a bedside table overnight, which increases the risk from this type of defect.

Parents who own a Babysense Max View monitor should stop charging it and contact the retailer where they purchased it for remedies. Check the CPSC recall notice at cpsc.gov/Recalls for the full list of affected model numbers.

What's Next

This is the third consecutive day that CPSC has flagged multiple children's products in the same week. The pattern reflects both increased CPSC enforcement activity and the ongoing challenge of marketplace products — particularly those sold through third-party Amazon sellers and platforms like AliExpress — meeting mandatory U.S. safety standards.

The best protection for Colorado Springs families is to register baby products for recall notifications at the time of purchase through saferproducts.gov, and to check cpsc.gov/Recalls regularly. The CPSC hotline at 1-800-638-2772 can also answer product-specific questions. For these four products, the immediate action is straightforward: stop using them, and follow the specific disposal or return instructions for each.
