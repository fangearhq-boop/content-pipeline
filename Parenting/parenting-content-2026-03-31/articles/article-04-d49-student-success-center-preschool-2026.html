D49 Opens Student Success Center with New Full-Day Preschool Classrooms in August 2026

March 31, 2026 · COS Parenting

Colorado Springs School District 49 is opening a new Student Success Center at the start of the 2026-27 school year, and it's bringing two new full-day preschool classrooms with it. Registration for those seats is open now — relevant news for families on the east side of Colorado Springs who are planning their child's early education for the coming year.

What Is the Student Success Center

D49's Student Success Center is a new facility that the district is positioning as an additional hub for student support and early childhood programming. The center's opening in August 2026 coincides with the start of the new school year, and its two new preschool classrooms represent a direct expansion of full-day early childhood capacity in the district.

Full-day preschool availability in Colorado Springs is competitive across all districts. Families who have been on waitlists, or who moved to the D49 coverage area after registration windows closed elsewhere, should visit d49.org to understand the enrollment process and get on the list now.

The D49 Coverage Area

School District 49 covers the northeast and eastern portions of Colorado Springs — including the Falcon and Peyton communities, the Calhan area, and the Powers Boulevard corridor that runs through the growing east side of the city. The district also serves parts of unincorporated El Paso County east of the city limits.

Families new to the Colorado Springs area, or those who have recently relocated to the east side from another part of the city, should confirm their address falls within D49 boundaries before registering. The district's website has a boundary lookup tool. D49 operates separately from D11 (which covers central and older COS neighborhoods), D20 (the north side), and other El Paso County districts.

Baldrige Excellence Recognition

D49 also recently received a Best Practice Spotlight from the Baldrige Excellence Program, the national quality management recognition framework administered by the National Institute of Standards and Technology. Being selected for a Best Practice Spotlight means other organizations — in this case, other school districts — are directed to look at D49 as a model for specific management practices.

The Baldrige recognition is not a participation award. The framework uses rigorous self-assessment and third-party review across categories including leadership, strategy, students and stakeholders, measurement, workforce, and operations. Districts that earn Baldrige recognition at any level have typically invested years in aligning their processes to the framework's standards.

For Colorado Springs families evaluating D49 as a school choice — especially those considering a new preschool enrollment — the Baldrige recognition is a useful external signal that the district takes institutional quality seriously at the system level.

What's Next

The D49 Student Success Center opens in August 2026 with the start of the 2026-27 school year. Registration for the two new full-day preschool classrooms is currently open. Families interested in these seats should act soon — preschool enrollment across Colorado Springs fills quickly in the spring for the following school year.

Visit d49.org to access enrollment information, district boundary maps, and contact details for the Early Childhood Education program. The district's phone lines can also direct families to the appropriate registration office for the Student Success Center specifically.
