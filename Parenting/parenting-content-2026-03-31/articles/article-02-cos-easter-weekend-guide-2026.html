Saturday, April 4: Easter Eve Food Festival

The night before Easter brings an unusual combination to the Norris Penrose Event Center in Old Colorado City: a chocolate and cheese food festival. If your household has strong opinions about either ingredient, this is either a perfect event or a debate waiting to happen. It's family-friendly and positioned in Old Colorado City, which has its own Easter weekend energy as one of the city's most walkable historic districts.

This is a lower-key option than the Sunday events, and a good choice for families who want to do something festive on Saturday without competing for Sunday brunch reservations.

Sunday, April 5: ViewHouse Easter Brunch and Egg Hunt

ViewHouse is running its Easter brunch with a family-friendly egg hunt and Easter Bunny photo opportunity from 9 AM to 3 PM on Easter Sunday. This is the kind of event that fills quickly in Colorado Springs — it combines a sit-down brunch with the egg hunt and photo moment that families with young children want in one place.

Book a reservation now if this is your plan. Walk-in availability on Easter morning at a full-service brunch event in a city this size is not something to count on.

Sunday, April 5: Royal Gorge Sunrise Service

For families looking for something more reflective, the Royal Gorge Sunrise Service is one of Colorado's most distinctive Easter traditions. The service takes place on the rim of the Royal Gorge as the sun rises over the Sangre de Cristo Mountains to the south and southwest. It draws visitors from across the state and has been running as a local tradition for decades.

Practical notes for families attending: seating is limited, so arriving before sunrise is important. Dress in serious layers — Easter morning at altitude can be extremely cold, and the Royal Gorge area at 6,300 feet elevation does not warm up quickly. Bring blankets for younger children. The bridge crossing and parking logistics require advance planning, so check the Royal Gorge Bridge and Park's official site for 2026-specific information.

Planning for Colorado Spring Weather

Any outdoor Easter activity in Colorado Springs comes with the standard spring weather caveat: snow is possible in April, sometimes on the same day that temperatures reach the mid-60s. The average high for early April in COS is around 57-59 degrees Fahrenheit, but late-season snowstorms are a regular feature of Colorado spring.

For outdoor egg hunts, brunch on patios, or any event that depends on clear skies, watch the forecast through Saturday and have a backup option identified. The good news is that Colorado spring snow typically melts fast — but an early morning Easter Sunday snowfall is within the range of normal for this region.

What's Next

After Easter weekend, the next big COS family calendar moment is the spring park season. Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation, and Cultural Services typically opens summer programming registration in April, and Cheyenne Mountain Zoo's centennial programming continues through the year. The zoo's new giraffe center is still on track for a summer 2026 opening, making it a major destination for later in the season.

For the immediate weekend: book brunch now, pack layers, and enjoy one of the most beautiful times of year in the Pikes Peak region.
