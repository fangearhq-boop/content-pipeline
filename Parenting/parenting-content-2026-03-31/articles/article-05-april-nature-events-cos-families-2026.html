Free and Low-Cost April Nature Events for COS Families: Bear Creek, Birding, Fountain Creek

March 31, 2026 · COS Parenting

April in Colorado Springs is unpredictable but beautiful — snow one day, 60 degrees and sunny the next. What's reliable is the calendar of structured nature programs that run through the city's parks, nature centers, and conservation organizations each spring. Here's what's on the schedule this April for COS families, with a focus on free and low-cost options.

April 17: Bear Creek Early Literacy Event (FREE)

On April 17 from 1:30 to 3 PM, Bear Creek Nature Center is co-hosting a free Early Literacy Event with the Colorado School for the Deaf and the Blind. The program is designed for families with deaf and hard-of-hearing children ages birth to eight, and all activities are presented in American Sign Language alongside spoken language.

The program includes a book reading in ASL, wildlife activities focused on summer animals at Bear Creek, and a short nature walk. The event is free to attend and does not require advance registration, though arriving early is recommended if space is limited.

This is a genuinely inclusive outdoor event in a city that doesn't always advertise its accessibility programming. For families with deaf or hard-of-hearing children, it's worth putting on the calendar.

April 23: Bear Creek Homeschool Programs ($4/person)

Bear Creek Nature Center runs simultaneous homeschool programs on April 23 from 1 to 3:30 PM, with separate tracks for younger and older children. This means families with kids at different grade levels can attend together without anyone sitting through curriculum that doesn't match their age.

Children age eight and older may be dropped off for the program; children seven and under need a caregiver present. Cost is $4 per person, and preregistration is required — these programs fill up, so register before arriving.

April 24: Fountain Creek Habitat Exploration ($4/person)

On April 24 from 2 to 4 PM, Fountain Creek Regional Park is running a Habitat Exploration for grades three through six. The program covers five distinct habitats within the park, includes a wetland metaphor activity, and ends with a pond study. Cost is $4 per person.

Fountain Creek Regional Park sits in the southeastern part of the Colorado Springs area and is jointly managed by El Paso County Parks. The park's mix of wetlands, riparian areas, and grasslands makes it one of the richer ecological environments in the Pikes Peak region for school-age nature programming.

April 9 and 11: Intermediate Birding Walk (Aiken Audubon)

For families whose kids are getting serious about birding — or adults looking for a structured two-session experience — Aiken Audubon is running an Intermediate Birding Walk with instructor Mary Wilson on April 9 and 11. The program covers identification skills, bird behavior, habitat recognition, and gear selection. Cost is $10 per person, $8 for Aiken Audubon members.

This is best suited for older children who have already done some birding and want to develop their skills more systematically, or for parent-child pairs where a parent is the primary birder.

What's Next

April nature programming in Colorado Springs ramps up as the month progresses and the weather stabilizes. Always check weather the morning of any outdoor event — spring snow remains possible through most of April at Colorado Springs elevation, and many nature programs proceed rain or shine while canceling for lightning or heavy snow.

Preregistration is required for the Bear Creek Homeschool Program and the Fountain Creek Habitat Exploration. The Bear Creek Early Literacy Event and the Birding Walk require contact with the organizing organization directly to confirm space. Visit coloradosprings.gov/parks for Bear Creek and Fountain Creek programming, and aikensociety.org for Aiken Audubon events.
