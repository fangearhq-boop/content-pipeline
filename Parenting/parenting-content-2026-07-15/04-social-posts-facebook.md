### STORY 1: VEVOR Baby Swing Recall — 8:00 AM MT

#### Long-Form Post

If you have a VEVOR baby swing at home, this is worth checking right now. ⚠️

Sanven Technology — the company behind VEVOR — has recalled approximately 1,020 baby swings sold on Amazon and Vevor.com between January and August 2025. The affected models are BB501K, BB702A, and BB005K. The swings sold for $65–$80, and the problem is the incline angle, which exceeds the 10-degree limit set by the federal Safe Sleep for Babies Act. An inclined infant sleeper that goes beyond that limit poses a suffocation risk.

If you have one of these swings, the CPSC says to stop using it immediately. Here's what to do: cut the swing cover in half, write "Recalled" on the base, take a photo, and email recalling@vevor.com for a full refund. You can also call 855-599-6320.

This isn't happening in isolation. There are several other infant product recalls active right now — including recalls on Joyful Journeys and CooCooBaby baby loungers, Babypark Children's Carriers, and AMASKY Nursing Pillows. If you have any baby gear you purchased in the last year or two, it's worth a quick check on cpsc.gov to make sure it hasn't been recalled.

Sharing here because not everyone has seen this — and these things matter most when they reach the parents who need them.

### STORY 2: Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo — 10:00 AM MT

#### Long-Form Post

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo opened last night, and there's still plenty of weekend left to make it a family outing. 🤠

The event runs through Saturday, July 18, at the Norris Penrose Event Center at 1045 Lower Gold Camp Road. If you've got kids between 4 and 9 years old who weigh under 50 pounds, Mutton Bustin' is the main event — kids ride sheep, it's chaotic and hilarious, and you don't need to pre-register. It happens at 4 PM at the evening shows, and at 10 AM for the Friday and Saturday matinees. The ride costs $20.

The Fan Zone has pony rides, a petting zoo, face painting, goat roping, food, and shopping — plenty to keep everyone busy before and after the main events. General admission tickets are $25–$50 and can be picked up at pikespeakorbust.org.

Since 1946, this rodeo has used its proceeds to support local military families, which gives it a community meaning that goes beyond the entertainment. It's one of those events that's been part of Colorado Springs for generations.

If you're thinking about going, Thursday or Friday afternoon tends to be less crowded than Saturday.

### STORY 3: Colorado Phone Law HB25-1135 — 12:00 PM MT

#### Long-Form Post

Colorado's student phone law — HB25-1135 — is now in effect, and if you have a kid heading back to school in August, it's worth knowing what it actually requires before the first day.

The law required all public and charter schools in Colorado to post a cellphone and personal electronic device policy on their website by July 1, 2026. That means right now, you can go to your school's website and read exactly what their policy says. Every school is different, and the law gives districts flexibility in how they handle it — so don't assume your kid's school has the same rules as a friend's kid's school across town.

Here in Colorado Springs, D11 was ahead of the curve. The district already implemented a phone pouch system in the 2024-25 school year, where students store their phones in pouches during the school day. Statewide, about 89% of elementary schools and 84% of middle schools have some form of bell-to-bell phone restriction — but only about 22% of high schools do.

One important detail: the law requires that policies include exemptions for students with disabilities, IEPs, 504 plans, and medical monitoring devices. If your child has one of these accommodations, their phone access during the school day should be protected regardless of the general school policy.

With D11, D20, and D49 all returning in August, this is the month to look up your school's policy so there are no surprises on the first day.

### STORY 5: Portal Pool + Where to Swim — 5:00 PM MT

#### Long-Form Post

If you've been wondering why Portal Pool is closed — and when it might come back — here's the short answer: a coal mine that shut down in 1945 runs beneath Portal Park, in some places as little as 25 feet below the surface. Roof collapses and leaks from that old mine compromised both the pool and the adjacent Memorial Park Family Center YMCA. A geotechnical investigation is scheduled to begin by the end of 2026, but city officials have described the pool's return as years away. 🏊

So for this summer, here's where COS families can swim:

**Monument Valley Pool** — 220 Mesa Road, open Mon–Sat 10 AM–6 PM, Sunday noon–6 PM.

**Wilson Ranch Pool** — 2335 Allegheny Drive, open daily.

**Briargate Splash Park** — daily 10 AM–6 PM through September 7.

**Deerfield Hills Spray Ground** — daily 10 AM–5 PM.

**The Watering Hole at Venezia Park** — daily 10 AM–6 PM; universally accessible.

**Uncle Wilber Fountain at Acacia Park** — daily noon–6 PM, completely free.

And while you're out and about: D11 is offering free lunches for kids ages 0–18 at the East Library, 5550 N. Union Blvd., weekdays 11 AM–noon through July 31.
