### STORY 1: CPSC Recall Alert — VEVOR Baby Swings and a Wave of Infant Product Recalls
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Tier:** T1
**Byline:** Sarah Morales

Sanven Technology (VEVOR brand) has recalled approximately 1,020 baby swings sold on Amazon and Vevor.com between January and August 2025 for $65–$80. Models BB501K, BB702A, and BB005K are affected. The hazard: the incline angle exceeds 10 degrees, violating the federal Safe Sleep for Babies Act ban on inclined infant sleepers. The risk is suffocation. The remedy is to stop using immediately, cut the swing cover in half, write "Recalled" on the base, photograph it, and email recalling@vevor.com for a full refund. Phone: 855-599-6320. This is part of a broader cluster of CPSC infant product recalls active right now — also including Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers, CooCooBaby Baby Loungers, Babypark Children's Carriers (July 2), and AMASKY Nursing Pillows (4,008 units). This is safety-critical and time-sensitive.

### STORY 3: Colorado's Student Phone Law Is in Effect — What COS Parents Need to Know Before August
**Pillar:** Local News
**Tier:** T1
**Byline:** Sarah Morales

Colorado HB25-1135 required all public and charter schools to post a student cellphone/device policy on their website by July 1, 2026. The law is now in effect. D11 was ahead of the curve — it implemented a pouch system (phones stored in pouches during the school day) starting in 2024-25. Statewide, roughly 89% of elementary schools have bell-to-bell phone bans, 84% of middle schools — but only 22% of high schools. Policies must include exemptions for students with disabilities, IEPs or 504 plans, and medical monitoring devices. With D11, D20, and D49 returning in August, this is the right moment to help families understand what the law means and where to find their school's policy.

### STORY 5: Portal Pool Is Years Away — Here's Where COS Families Can Swim This Summer
**Pillar:** Evergreen
**Tier:** T2
**Byline:** Sarah Morales

The Colorado Springs Gazette reported in May 2026 that Portal Pool has been closed due to a coal mine shaft running beneath Portal Park — in some places as little as 25 feet below the surface. A mine that shut down in 1945 has caused roof collapses and leaks that compromised both the pool and the adjacent Memorial Park Family Center YMCA. A geotechnical investigation is scheduled to begin by end of 2026, but the pool is years away from returning. Families searching for public swimming need a clear guide to what's open: Monument Valley Pool (220 Mesa Road, Mon–Sat 10 AM–6 PM, Sun noon–6 PM), Wilson Ranch Pool (2335 Allegheny Drive), Briargate Splash Park (daily 10 AM–6 PM through Sept 7), Deerfield Hills Spray Ground (daily 10 AM–5 PM), The Watering Hole at Venezia Park (daily 10 AM–6 PM, universally accessible), Uncle Wilber Fountain at Acacia Park (daily noon–6 PM, free). Bonus tip: D11 provides free lunches for kids 0–18 at East Library (5550 N. Union Blvd.) weekdays 11 AM–noon through July 31.
