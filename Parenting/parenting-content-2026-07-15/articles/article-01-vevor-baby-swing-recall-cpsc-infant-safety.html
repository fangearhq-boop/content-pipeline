Your Baby Swing May Be on the Recall List — Here's How to Check

By Sarah Morales July 15, 2026

What's Being Recalled

Sanven Technology — the company behind the VEVOR brand — has issued a recall of approximately 1,020 baby swings sold under the VEVOR name. The swings were sold on Amazon and Vevor.com between January and August 2025, priced between $65 and $80. Three models are affected: BB501K, BB702A, and BB005K.

The hazard is the incline angle. Federal law under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act bans inclined infant sleepers that exceed a 10-degree angle — and these swings exceed that limit. The concern is suffocation: infants can shift into a chin-to-chest position in inclined products, which restricts their airway. This is the same class of hazard that led to the removal of numerous inclined sleepers from the market in recent years.

What to Do If You Have One

The CPSC remedy for this recall is specific. Do not simply set the swing aside or stop using it temporarily — the agency asks that you permanently disable it so it can't be used again:

  • Stop using the swing immediately.
  • Cut the swing cover in half.
  • Write "Recalled" on the base of the unit.
  • Take a photograph of the disabled product.
  • Email the photo to recalling@vevor.com for a full refund.

You can also reach Sanven Technology by phone at 855-599-6320. The refund does not require returning the product — just the photo documentation of the disabled swing.

This Is Part of a Larger Pattern

The VEVOR recall is not an isolated event. There are several other infant and baby product recalls currently active that Colorado Springs parents should be aware of:

  • Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers — recalled for inclined sleeper violations under the same Safe Sleep for Babies Act framework.
  • CooCooBaby Baby Loungers — same category, same hazard.
  • Babypark Children's Carriers — recalled July 2, 2026.
  • AMASKY Nursing Pillows — approximately 4,008 units affected.

If you have any infant product purchased in the past two years, a quick search on cpsc.gov using the brand name or product type will tell you whether it has been recalled. The CPSC database is updated in real time and is searchable by product category. Signing up for email alerts through the CPSC website is also a practical way to stay current.

What's Next

If you have one of the affected VEVOR swings, the process is straightforward: disable it, photograph it, and contact Sanven Technology for your refund. If you're unsure whether a product you own has been recalled, cpsc.gov is the authoritative source. For families with infants, a periodic check of the CPSC recall database — even once a month — takes a few minutes and covers the full range of infant gear categories.
