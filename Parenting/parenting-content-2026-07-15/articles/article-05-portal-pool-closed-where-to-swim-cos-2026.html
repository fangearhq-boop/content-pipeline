Portal Pool Is Years Away From Reopening — Here's Where COS Families Can Swim This Summer

By Sarah Morales July 15, 2026

Why Portal Pool Is Closed

The Colorado Springs Gazette reported in May 2026 that Portal Pool has been closed due to a structural problem that goes well below the surface — literally. A coal mine that stopped operating in 1945 runs beneath Portal Park. In some locations, that mine sits as little as 25 feet below the surface. Over time, roof collapses and leaks from the old mine have compromised both the pool and the adjacent Memorial Park Family Center YMCA.

A geotechnical investigation is scheduled to begin by the end of 2026, but city officials have made clear that the pool's return is years away — not months. Families who have been waiting for an update on Portal Pool can stop planning around it for this summer, and likely for the next several.

Where COS Families Can Swim Right Now

Colorado Springs has several public swimming options open this summer. Here's the complete list with hours:

Monument Valley Pool

220 Mesa Road. Open Monday through Saturday, 10 AM to 6 PM, and Sunday from noon to 6 PM. This is the city's primary public outdoor pool and typically the busiest option on hot days.

Wilson Ranch Pool

2335 Allegheny Drive. Open daily. Located in the northeast part of Colorado Springs, this pool tends to be less crowded than Monument Valley and is convenient for families in the Briargate and Northgate areas.

Briargate Splash Park

Open daily from 10 AM to 6 PM through September 7. The Briargate Splash Park is a water play area with spray features rather than a traditional pool — well-suited for younger children and toddlers who aren't yet strong swimmers. No lifeguard is on duty, and it's a standing water-free environment.

Deerfield Hills Spray Ground

Open daily from 10 AM to 5 PM. Similar format to Briargate — spray features, no standing water. Located in the eastern part of Colorado Springs.

The Watering Hole at Venezia Park

Open daily from 10 AM to 6 PM. This spray ground is universally accessible and designed to accommodate families with children who have mobility or sensory needs. The flat, even surface makes it usable with strollers and mobility devices.

Uncle Wilber Fountain at Acacia Park

Open daily from noon to 6 PM. Free of charge. Located in downtown Colorado Springs at Acacia Park, Uncle Wilber Fountain is a kids' splash feature that has been a summer staple for decades. It's free, it's central, and it works for kids who want to cool off quickly without a full outing.

Bonus: Free Lunch for Kids This Month

While families are out and about this summer, D11 is offering free lunches for kids ages 0 through 18 at the East Library, 5550 N. Union Blvd., on weekdays from 11 AM to noon through July 31. No enrollment or documentation is required — any child in that age range can receive a free meal. If your family is spending mornings at pools on the north side of Colorado Springs, this is worth building into your schedule.

What's Next

Portal Pool remains closed with no near-term return date. The geotechnical investigation beginning later this year will help determine the scope of what's needed — but a multi-year timeline is the realistic expectation. For this summer, Monument Valley Pool, Wilson Ranch Pool, and the spray parks and spray grounds listed above are the city's public options. Current hours and any schedule changes are available through the Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services website.
