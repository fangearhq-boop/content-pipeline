The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo Is On This Weekend — What Families Need to Know

By Jamie Rivera July 15, 2026

What It Is and When to Go

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo opened Monday, July 14, and runs through Saturday, July 18, at the Norris Penrose Event Center at 1045 Lower Gold Camp Road in Colorado Springs. It's one of the longest-running rodeo events in Colorado, dating back to 1946, and it draws consistently strong crowds through a combination of professional rodeo competition, live entertainment, and family-focused activities.

If you haven't been yet this week, you still have Thursday, Friday, and Saturday. The event runs both matinee and evening shows on Friday and Saturday, which gives families with younger kids who need an earlier end time a workable option.

Mutton Bustin': The Thing Everyone Talks About

For families with young kids, Mutton Bustin' is the reason most parents remember their first Pikes Peak or Bust. It works exactly the way it sounds: small children hold on to a sheep for as long as they can while it runs across the arena. It is chaotic, short, and extremely memorable for everyone involved.

The eligibility rules are simple: kids must be between 4 and 9 years old and weigh less than 50 pounds. There's no pre-registration — you sign up the day of the event. The cost is $20 per ride. Mutton Bustin' takes place at 4 PM at evening shows, and at 10 AM for the Friday and Saturday matinee sessions.

If your child is close to the weight limit, it's worth confirming before you arrive. The limit is firm, and kids are weighed on-site.

The Fan Zone

Alongside the main arena action, the Fan Zone offers a range of activities that work for a wide age span. Pony rides, a petting zoo, face painting, and goat roping give younger kids things to do during the longer stretches between main events. There's also food and shopping throughout.

The Fan Zone is included with general admission. For families who've been to other rodeos, Pikes Peak or Bust tends to run the Fan Zone smoothly — the layout keeps it manageable even on busy weekend evenings.

Tickets and a Bit of History

General admission runs $25–$50. Tickets are available at pikespeakorbust.org. No tickets are required for children under a certain age — check the official site for the current age cutoff before you go.

Since 1946, the Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo has directed its proceeds toward supporting local military families. Colorado Springs has one of the highest concentrations of military personnel of any city in the country, and the rodeo's connection to that community gives it a context that extends beyond the entertainment. It's a genuine local institution.
