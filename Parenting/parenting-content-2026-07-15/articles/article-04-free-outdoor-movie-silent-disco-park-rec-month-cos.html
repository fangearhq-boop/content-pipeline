Free Movie Night and Silent Disco This Friday — Plus a Month of Free Family Events in COS

By Jamie Rivera July 15, 2026

This Friday: Free Movie and Silent Disco at Hillside

Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services is hosting a Free Outdoor Movie and Silent Disco at Hillside Community Center, 925 South Institute Street, on Friday, July 17. The event is free, no registration required. The guidance from organizers is simple: bring blankets and lawn chairs.

The movie title is intentionally being kept off promotional materials due to licensing restrictions — a standard practice for outdoor film screenings. Colorado Springs Parks is dropping clues on their social media at @CoSpringsParks in the days leading up to Friday. If guessing the movie sounds like the kind of thing your family would be into, that's half the entertainment before you even show up.

A silent disco is exactly what it sounds like: participants wear wireless headphones tuned to the same music and dance together. The outdoor version in a park setting tends to be a hit with kids who find regular concerts too loud, and with teenagers who find most family events boring.

Also This Week: PPLD Family Concert

For families with younger children who do better earlier in the day, the Pikes Peak Library District is hosting a family fun concert at the Fountain Library on July 17 — the same day as the evening movie event. Sessions run from 9:30 to 10:30 AM and from 11 AM to noon. This is a free event through PPLD and works well as a morning outing before an afternoon nap and a Friday evening movie.

Later This Month: SofaKillers at Pinon Valley Park

On July 24, Colorado Springs Parks is hosting a free SofaKillers concert at Pinon Valley Park. The event includes food trucks, a youth kickball game, and giveaways. This is a fuller community event — bring the whole family and plan to stay for a while. No ticket required.

The Month-Long Contests

July is National Park and Recreation Month, and Colorado Springs Parks is running several all-month activities alongside the events. Families can participate in a coloring contest, a "Build Your Own Park" design challenge where kids submit their vision of the ideal park, and a Discover COS photo contest. Details on entry are available through the Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services website and @CoSpringsParks.

These contests are low-stakes and genuinely kid-friendly — the kind of thing that can fill a rainy afternoon with something purposeful. If your kids are creative or competitive (or both), the design challenge in particular tends to generate real enthusiasm.

What's Next

The immediate priority is Friday, July 17: free outdoor movie and silent disco at Hillside Community Center, 925 South Institute Street. Bring blankets and lawn chairs. Follow @CoSpringsParks to find out what movie is playing. The SofaKillers concert is July 24 at Pinon Valley Park. Both are free, both are family-appropriate, and neither requires any planning beyond showing up.
