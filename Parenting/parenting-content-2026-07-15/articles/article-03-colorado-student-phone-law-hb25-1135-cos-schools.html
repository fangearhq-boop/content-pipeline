Colorado's Student Phone Law Is Now in Effect — What COS Parents Need to Find Before August

By Sarah Morales July 15, 2026

What the Law Actually Says

Colorado HB25-1135 is now in effect. The law required all public and charter schools in Colorado to post a cellphone and personal electronic device policy on their website by July 1, 2026. That deadline has passed, which means right now — before summer is over — parents can look up exactly what their school's policy says about phones during the school day.

The law does not prescribe a single, uniform rule for every school. It requires districts to have a policy and to make that policy public — but the specific approach varies. Some schools have bell-to-bell bans. Some use pouch systems. Some allow phone use during lunch or passing periods. The only way to know what applies to your child's school is to check the school's website directly.

What's Already Happening in COS

District 11 was ahead of the state curve. D11 implemented a phone pouch system in the 2024-25 school year, requiring students to store their phones in small fabric pouches during the school day. The pouches stay with students — they're not surrendered to teachers — but the phones remain inaccessible during class time. D11 families returning in August are likely already familiar with how this works.

Statewide data reflects significant variation by school level. Roughly 89% of Colorado elementary schools now have some form of bell-to-bell phone restriction, and about 84% of middle schools do as well. At the high school level, that number drops to approximately 22%. If your child is in high school in D20 or D49, their school's specific policy may look quite different from what D11 high schoolers experience.

The Exemptions Matter

One provision of HB25-1135 that often gets missed in the general conversation: the law requires that phone policies include exemptions for students who need device access as part of a documented plan. Specifically, schools must accommodate students with disabilities, IEPs, 504 plans, and medical monitoring devices.

If your child has any of these accommodations, their phone access during the school day should be protected regardless of the school's general policy. If you're not sure whether your child's accommodation is documented in a way that covers phone access, the start of the school year is a reasonable time to confirm that with the school's disability services coordinator.

How to Find Your School's Policy

Every public and charter school was required to post its policy by July 1. Here's where to look:

  • Go to your school's website (not just the district site).
  • Search for "phone policy," "cell phone," or "device policy."
  • Look in sections labeled "student handbook," "school rules," or "policies."
  • If you can't find it, email the school's front office — they're required to have it.

D11, D20, and D49 all return in August. With a few weeks of summer left, this is the right moment to look it up rather than discover it at drop-off on the first day.

What's Next

Colorado's phone law is in effect but will continue to evolve as districts assess what's working. If your child attends a D11 school, the pouch system is already established and you can find specifics on the D11 website. D20 and D49 families should look up their individual school's posted policy now. And if you have a student with a documented disability or medical need, confirming their exemption status before the first day is a practical step worth taking this summer.
