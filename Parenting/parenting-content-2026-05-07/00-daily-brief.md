### STORY 2: AAP Updates Screen Time Guidance — Quality Over Time Limits for Kids 6+
- **Tier:** 1
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Posting window:** 10:00 AM MT
- **Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
- **Classification:** NEW STORY
- **Summary:** The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its screen time recommendations in January 2026, making a notable shift for children ages 6 and older: instead of strict daily time limits, the guidance now emphasizes the quality, context, and conversation around screen use. Under 18 months: no screens. Ages 2-5: up to 1 hour per day of high-quality content. Ages 6+: focus on what children watch and whether families engage around it, not just how many hours. Social media: delay as long as possible. The update lowers screen-time anxiety for families with older kids while keeping sharp guardrails for toddlers and preschoolers.
- **Sources:** CHOC health hub ("Updated AAP recommendations for screen time"); wired-parents.com; AAP publications (Pediatrics journal, January 2026)

### STORY 4: CPSC Recall — NFSVLB Baby Bath Seats (Drowning/Entrapment Risk)
- **Tier:** 2
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Posting window:** 5:00 PM MT
- **Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
- **Classification:** NEW STORY
- **Summary:** The CPSC recalled approximately 1,430 NFSVLB baby bath seats (model ZY2025) sold on Amazon. The blue-and-white seats with detachable arms are unstable and can tip over while in use; children can also slip through the leg opening until the torso becomes entrapped. Remedy: stop using immediately, write "Recalled" on the seat, disassemble by removing the backrest and arm restraints, discard the screws, cut the four suction cups on the bottom, photograph the disassembled seat, and email the photo to amzmaihuayd@163.com for a full refund.
- **Sources:** cpsc.gov Recall page; cpsc.gov/Recalls/2026/NFSVLB-Baby-Bath-Seats; Facebook CPSC post

### STORY 5: CPSC Recall — Blossom Children's Loungewear (Burn Hazard)
- **Tier:** 2
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Posting window:** 8:00 PM MT
- **Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
- **Classification:** NEW STORY
- **Summary:** Blossom recalled its children's two-piece loungewear sets (sizes 12M through 12Y) for failing the mandatory flammability standard for children's sleepwear — a serious burn hazard. The sets, consisting of a button-up short- or long-sleeved shirt with matching shorts or pants, were sold at children's boutiques and online retailers between September 2023 and May 2025. Stop using immediately; cut in half; photograph and discard; email sales@blossomocc.com or call 256-481-8838 (8 AM-3 PM CT, M-F) for a full refund. No proof of purchase required.
- **Sources:** cpsc.gov/Recalls/2026/Blossom-Recalls-Childrens-Loungewear; blossomocc.com/pages/recall; DHSES.ny.gov recall notice
