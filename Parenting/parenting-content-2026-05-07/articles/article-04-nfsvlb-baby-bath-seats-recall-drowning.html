CPSC Recall: NFSVLB Baby Bath Seats Pose Drowning and Entrapment Risk — Get Your Refund

By Jamie Rivera | May 7, 2026 | COS Parenting

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 1,430 NFSVLB baby bath seats due to drowning and entrapment hazards. If you purchased one of these seats from Amazon, stop using it immediately and follow the refund steps below.

Which Product Is Recalled

The recalled item is the NFSVLB baby bath seat, model ZY2025. It is a blue-and-white seat with detachable arm restraints and four suction cups on the bottom. The seat was sold exclusively on Amazon by the seller NFSVLB.

If you are unsure whether your bath seat is the recalled model, check the label on the side of the seat for "Model No.: ZY2025."

What the Hazard Is

The CPSC identified two distinct problems with this seat that together create a serious risk for infants and young children during bath time.

First, the seat is unstable and can tip over while in use, causing a child to fall face-forward into the water. Second, the leg openings are large enough for a child to slip through until the torso becomes entrapped. A child who slips through and becomes stuck is unable to raise their head above water without assistance. The combination of tip-over risk and entrapment creates the conditions for drowning even in a few inches of water.

The CPSC has received no reports of injuries or deaths related to this product, but the structural defects are serious enough to warrant immediate removal from use.

How to Get Your Refund

The CPSC recall remedy is a full refund. Here is the process:

  1. Stop using the bath seat immediately and remove it from your bathroom.
  2. Write "Recalled" on the front of the seat using a permanent marker.
  3. Disassemble the seat: remove the backrest and arm restraints, discard the screws, and cut the four suction cups off the bottom.
  4. Take a clear photograph of the disassembled seat with the "Recalled" marking visible.
  5. Email the photo to amzmaihuayd@163.com to receive your refund.

You do not need your original receipt or order confirmation to request the refund. The photo of the disassembled seat is the required documentation.

Why This Matters for Bath Safety

Bath seats are not safety devices — they are convenience products designed to help support a seated infant during bathing. The CPSC and pediatric safety organizations consistently advise parents and caregivers to keep a hand on an infant at all times during bath time, regardless of what the infant is sitting in. No bath seat, ring, or support product is a substitute for direct adult supervision.

This recall is a reminder that the product category itself carries risk. Even a properly designed bath seat can become dangerous the moment a caregiver steps away. A bathtub with a few inches of water poses a drowning risk for infants and young toddlers in seconds.

What's Next

If you own the NFSVLB bath seat model ZY2025, follow the refund steps above and email your photo to amzmaihuayd@163.com. The full CPSC recall notice is available at cpsc.gov. Families with questions about bath safety or infant supervision guidelines can consult their pediatrician or visit the American Academy of Pediatrics' guidance at healthychildren.org.

If you know another Colorado Springs family with a young infant who may have purchased this product on Amazon, share this information with them. The recall covers only 1,430 units, which means many buyers may not receive an Amazon notification before the seat is used again.
