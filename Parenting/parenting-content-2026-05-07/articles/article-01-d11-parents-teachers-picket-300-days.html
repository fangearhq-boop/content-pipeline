D11 Parents Join Teachers at Picket Line: 300+ Days Into Colorado Springs' Labor Dispute

By Sarah Morales | May 7, 2026 | COS Parenting

More than 300 days after Colorado Springs School District 11 ended a 56-year-old labor agreement with its teachers union, educators and parents are planning to picket the D11 Board of Education — a sign that what began as a labor dispute has grown into something that touches families across the district.

The Colorado Springs Education Association (CSEA) announced the picket action after the board stripped the union of its collective bargaining rights last December, effectively ending a master agreement that had governed educator working conditions in D11 for over five decades. That decision triggered a one-day teacher strike in October 2025 and has kept the district's labor climate unsettled through the current school year.

Why Parents Are Joining the Picket Line

Parent participation in the picket action represents a notable development. When a labor dispute shifts from a conversation between a union and a school board to one that brings parents to the sidewalk, it reflects concerns that have moved beyond wages and working conditions into territory that directly affects families: classroom stability, teacher morale, and the district's ability to retain experienced educators.

D11 is the largest school district in the Colorado Springs area, serving tens of thousands of students across the city. When uncertainty persists in a district of that size for more than 300 days, it affects substitute availability, turnover rates, and the continuity that young students depend on.

For families making enrollment decisions ahead of the 2026-27 school year — D11 open enrollment continues on its standard timeline — the ongoing dispute is a real factor in the conversation.

The CSEA's master agreement had been in place since the late 1960s, covering educator pay structures, working conditions, and dispute resolution mechanisms. The board's decision to end it was controversial within the community and drew public criticism from teachers, parents, and education advocates across Colorado.

What This Means for COS Families

If your child attends a D11 school, the practical implications depend on your school and grade level. Many classroom teachers have remained in place through the dispute, and day-to-day instruction has continued. But families should be aware that teacher retention pressures are real in a district where labor relations are uncertain, and that this is likely to remain a live issue heading into the next school year.

Families with concerns are encouraged to attend Board of Education meetings or connect with their school's parent organization for updates specific to their campus. D11's website (d11.org) maintains board meeting schedules and public comment procedures.

What's Next

The D11 Board of Education holds regular public meetings where community members can attend, observe, and provide comment. Details on upcoming meeting dates are available at d11.org. The 2026-27 school year enrollment and staffing process is ongoing. Families with enrollment questions can contact the district's enrollment office directly through d11.org.
