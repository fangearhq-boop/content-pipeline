Blossom Children's Loungewear Recalled: Burn Hazard for Kids Sizes 12M to 12Y

By Sarah Morales | May 7, 2026 | COS Parenting

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled Blossom children's two-piece loungewear sets in sizes 12 months through 12 years. The sets fail the mandatory federal flammability standard for children's sleepwear, creating a burn hazard. Families do not need a receipt to receive a full refund.

Which Products Are Recalled

The recall covers Blossom brand two-piece loungewear sets consisting of a button-up short- or long-sleeved shirt with matching shorts or pants. The sets were sold in sizes 12 months through 12 years at children's boutiques and through online retailers. The sale period runs from September 2023 through May 2025.

Because these sets were sold at boutique retailers and often given as gifts, families may have them in their homes without having purchased them directly. Worth checking drawers and closets tonight — and checking with grandparents or gift-givers if you received children's loungewear over the past two years.

The Hazard

The sets fail the mandatory flammability standard for children's sleepwear established under federal law. Children's sleepwear is subject to flammability testing requirements that are stricter than those for adult clothing because of the heightened fire risk associated with children sleeping near or wearing clothing around open flames, space heaters, and other ignition sources.

A garment that fails this standard ignites and burns more quickly than a compliant product, reducing the time a child has to escape or be removed from a fire source. There have been no reported incidents or injuries associated with this specific recall.

How to Get Your Refund

The remedy is a full refund in the original form of payment, or store credit if preferred. No receipt is required. Here is the process:

  1. Stop dressing your child in the recalled loungewear sets immediately.
  2. Cut the garments in half to render them unusable.
  3. Take a photograph of the destroyed items.
  4. Email the photo to sales@blossomocc.com. You do not need to include proof of purchase.
  5. Alternatively, call 256-481-8838 Monday through Friday between 8 AM and 3 PM CT.

The destruction step is standard CPSC recall procedure — it prevents recalled items from being resold or donated and circulating back into use.

A Note on Children's Sleepwear Standards

Federal regulations distinguish between children's sleepwear and other children's clothing. Sleepwear for children sized 9 months through size 14 must either be made from flame-resistant fabric or fit snugly to the child's body (which reduces ignition risk by limiting the air between the fabric and skin).

Loungewear sets marketed for children occupy a gray area — they are often worn as sleepwear even if not labeled as such. The CPSC applies sleepwear standards when products are likely to be worn to sleep, regardless of how they are labeled. When a product fails those standards, a recall follows.

For families buying children's loungewear or pajamas going forward: look for the "flame resistant" label or the "snug fit" tag, both of which indicate the product meets federal requirements.

What's Next

The full CPSC recall notice is available at cpsc.gov. Families with the recalled Blossom sets should follow the refund steps above and contact sales@blossomocc.com or call 256-481-8838 (Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 3 PM CT). If you received these sets as gifts and don't know exactly when or where they were purchased, you can still submit the photo for a refund without a receipt.
