PAW Patrol Live! Comes to Pikes Peak Center May 23-24: What COS Families Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | May 7, 2026 | COS Parenting

PAW Patrol Live! is coming to Colorado Springs on May 23 and 24, with performances at Pikes Peak Center for the Performing Arts. The touring production brings Chase, Marshall, Skye, and the Mighty Pups to the stage for a live show geared toward toddlers through early elementary-age children.

Here is what families in Colorado Springs need to know before booking tickets.

The Show

The production is PAW Patrol Live! "A Mighty Adventure," the current touring version of the live stage show based on the long-running Nickelodeon animated series. The story follows the pups as they take on Supervillain Scientist Victoria Vance, who threatens Adventure City. It features the signature PAW Patrol characters, original music, interactive audience moments, and the kind of high-energy stage design that works for young kids who are already fans of the show.

The format includes singing, dancing, projected backdrops, and costume characters performing live. Parents who have seen previous PAW Patrol Live! touring productions know what to expect: it runs roughly 90 minutes with an intermission and is paced for short attention spans.

Tickets and Seating

Tickets are available at AXS.com and at the Pikes Peak Center box office. As with other touring children's shows, seating closer to the stage tends to sell quickly once families learn the show is in town. The May 23-24 window is a Saturday-Sunday run, which is typical for this type of touring production and gives families two days of scheduling flexibility.

Lap children policies vary by venue and show — check the Pikes Peak Center's ticketing page for the specific terms. Some performances include a post-show meet-and-greet opportunity with the characters; this is listed at the time of purchase for applicable show times.

Calendar Conflict to Know About

The May 23-24 weekend is a busy one on the Colorado Springs events calendar. Falcon School District 49 holds its graduation ceremonies at Broadmoor World Arena on May 23. Memorial Day weekend begins the day after the show closes, on May 25. Families juggling graduation attendance, travel plans, or other weekend commitments should factor the full calendar in before booking.

Pikes Peak Center parking fills quickly for Saturday evening events. Arriving early is advisable, particularly for afternoon performances when families with young children are likely to be coming from a morning of other activities.

What's Next
