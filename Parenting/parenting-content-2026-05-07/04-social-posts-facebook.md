### STORY 2: AAP Updates Screen Time Guidance — Quality Over Time Limits for Kids 6+

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

```
The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its screen time guidance in January 2026, and for parents of kids ages 6 and older, it's a notable shift worth understanding.

The old framework focused on time: under 2 hours a day, no screens before 18 months, and so on. The updated thinking for children 6 and older moves toward asking better questions: What are they watching? Is it quality content? Are you having conversations about it?

The under-18-months rule stays. The 1-hour-per-day limit for ages 2-5 stays. But for older kids, the AAP is now saying that strict daily time limits are a less useful metric than intentional, engaged screen use.

What doesn't change: the concern about social media. The guidance still says delay social media access as long as possible. The platform designs that reward engagement and social comparison don't improve with parental supervision — they're better avoided for as long as kids can go without them.

At 6,000 feet in Colorado Springs, we have a lot of outdoor competition for screen time. But these guidelines help frame what really matters when screens are in the picture.

Does your family set screen time rules? Has this guidance changed how you think about it?

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

```
If you purchased an NFSVLB baby bath seat from Amazon, please check your bathroom today.

The seats are unstable and can tip over while in use. The leg openings are also large enough for a child to slip through until the torso becomes entrapped. The seat was sold on Amazon by NFSVLB. If you're not sure whether your bath seat is the recalled model, check for "Model No.: ZY2025" on the label on the side of the seat.

### STORY 5: CPSC Recall — Blossom Children's Loungewear (Burn Hazard)

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM MT

```
One more CPSC recall to know about — and this one covers a wide range of kids' sizes, from infants to pre-teens.

Blossom has recalled its children's two-piece loungewear sets in sizes 12 months through 12 years. The sets consist of a button-up short- or long-sleeved shirt with matching shorts or pants. They were sold at children's boutiques and online retailers between September 2023 and May 2025.

The problem: the sets fail the mandatory flammability standard for children's sleepwear, which creates a burn hazard.

If you have these in your home, here is what to do:
1. Stop dressing your child in them immediately.
2. Cut the garments in half and discard them.
3. Take a photo of the destroyed items.
4. Email the photo to sales@blossomocc.com — no proof of purchase required.
5. You can also call 256-481-8838, Monday-Friday, 8 AM to 3 PM CT.

The remedy is a full refund in the original form of payment, or store credit if preferred. You do not need a receipt.

These kinds of boutique loungewear sets are often given as gifts, which means they might be in your home without you having bought them directly. Worth checking your drawers tonight.

Do you recognize this product? Pass this along to any families who might have received Blossom items as gifts. 🧺
