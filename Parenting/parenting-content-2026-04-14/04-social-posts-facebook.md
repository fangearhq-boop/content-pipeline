### STORY 2: CPSC Recall — Vevor Baby Swings

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

If you bought a Vevor baby swing on Amazon or Vevor.com between January and August 2025, please check this now.

Sanven Technology has recalled approximately 1,020 Vevor Baby Swings due to a suffocation hazard. The swings were marketed and sold for infant sleep — but they have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees, which violates the Safe Sleep for Babies Act and the mandatory standard for infant sleep products. Inclined surfaces can cause an infant's head to tip forward, blocking the airway.

Here's what to check: the affected models are BB501K (white/gray with white base), BB702A (white/gray with gray base), and BB005K (black/gray with black base). The swings sold for $65 to $80.

If you have one of these: stop using it immediately and take it away from your baby. Contact Sanven Technology for a full refund. They can be reached at 855-599-6320 from 9 AM to 5 PM PT, Monday through Friday.

The Safe Sleep for Babies Act bans inclined sleepers because of the real risk they pose to infants. This recall is exactly why that law exists.

Have you seen this recall shared in your parent groups? Pass this along to anyone with an infant who might have this swing.

### STORY 3: CPSC Recall — Zippee Silicone Activity Toys

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM MT)**

This one might be sitting in your toy box right now — and you might not even know it's been recalled.

Mobi Games has recalled approximately 117,500 Zippee silicone activity toys due to a choking hazard. The Zippee is a teal flexible silicone cylinder with holes where six colorful silicone strings can be pulled. Five of those six strings have a spherical ball end. The problem: those spherical ends can reach the back of a child's throat, creating a serious choking hazard. There has been one reported incident of a child placing the toy in their mouth and choking.

The toys were sold from November 2019 through January 2024 — which means they've been in homes for years. Families may have moved the toy from one child to a younger sibling without realizing a recall had been issued.

Here's how to identify the toy: look for date codes in MMDDYY format ranging from 030620 to 110823 stamped inside the silicone cylinder body. If you have one, stop letting your children use it immediately and contact Mobi Games for a full refund. You can find recall information at cpsc.gov or at playmobi.com/pages/product-recall.

The Zippee was available on Amazon, PlayMobi's website, and in specialty toy stores for around $20. If you still have yours, the refund is worth the 10-minute process.

A reminder for Colorado Springs families considering Academy District 20 for the 2026-27 school year: School Choice applications are open now.
