### STORY 2: CPSC Recall — Vevor Baby Swings — Suffocation Hazard

**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
**Status:** NEW STORY

**Why This Matters:**
Inclined infant sleep products have been the subject of multiple high-profile safety recalls over the last several years. The Safe Sleep for Babies Act was specifically designed to eliminate these products after they were linked to infant deaths. Any product that violates this standard — including these Vevor baby swings — poses a direct suffocation risk that parents must act on immediately. The ~1,020 units are not a small number, and the January-August 2025 sale window means many families may still have this product at home.

**Key Angles:**
1. Inclined sleeper danger — education angle for parents unfamiliar with the ban
2. "Sold on Amazon" — parents who shop Amazon need to check order history
3. Model numbers specific — actionable recall info families can use immediately
4. Full refund available — positive action step
5. Safe sleep positioning reminder — evergreen education opportunity

**Best Social Hook:** "CPSC recall: Vevor baby swings (models BB501K, BB702A, BB005K). Inclined sleeper ban violation — suffocation risk. Sold on Amazon Jan-Aug 2025. Stop using now. Full refund: 855-599-6320."

**Target Audience:** Parents of infants; anyone who purchased a Vevor swing in 2025.

### STORY 3: CPSC Recall — Zippee Silicone Activity Toys — Choking Hazard

**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
**Status:** NEW STORY

**Why This Matters:**
117,500 units is a significant recall volume. Silicone activity toys marketed for young children are popular precisely because parents assume silicone is safer than plastic. This recall demonstrates that the design — not just the material — determines safety. The spherical ends on the Zippee toy's silicone strings are the specific hazard: they can reach the back of a young child's throat. With a sale period spanning 2019-2024, many families may have forgotten they own this product or have passed it to a younger sibling.

**Key Angles:**
1. "It's in the toy box" — parents may have owned this for years without incident, so familiarity breeds false comfort
2. 117,500 units — high probability someone in a reader's network has one
3. One choking incident already reported — urgency established
4. Silicone ≠ safe by default — educational angle
5. Full refund available — clear action step

**Best Social Hook:** "Do you have a Zippee silicone activity toy in your toy box? CPSC recall: 117,500 units. Choking hazard — spherical ends can reach back of throat. Sold 2019-2024 on Amazon + toy stores. Full refund."

**Target Audience:** Parents with children ages 1-6; anyone who purchased the Zippee toy.
