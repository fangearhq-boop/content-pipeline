CPSC Recall — Vevor Baby Swings — Suffocation Hazard
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post If you bought a Vevor baby swing on Amazon or Vevor.com between January and August 2025, please check this now. Sanven Technology has recalled approximately 1,020 Vevor Baby Swings due to a suffocation hazard. The swings were marketed and sold for infant sleep — but they have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees, which violates the Safe Sleep for Babies Act and the mandatory standard for infant sleep products. Inclined surfaces can cause an infant's head to tip forward, blocking the airway. Here's what to check: the affected models are BB501K (white/gray with white base), BB702A (white/gray with gray base), and BB005K (black/gray with black base). The swings sold for $65 to $80. If you have one of these: stop using it immediately and take it away from your baby. Contact Sanven Technology for a full refund. They can be reached at 855-599-6320 from 9 AM to 5 PM PT, Monday through Friday. The Safe Sleep for Babies Act bans inclined sleepers because of the real risk they pose to infants. This recall is exactly why that law exists. Have you seen this recall shared in your parent groups? Pass this along to anyone with an infant who might have this swing. ### Image Post Caption CPSC recall: Vevor Baby Swings (models BB501K, BB702A, BB005K). Inclined sleeper violation — suffocation risk. Sold on Amazon and Vevor.com from January to August 2025 for $65-$80. Stop using now and call 855-599-6320 for a full refund.

CPSC Recall — Zippee Silicone Activity Toys — Choking Hazard
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post This one might be sitting in your toy box right now — and you might not even know it's been recalled. Mobi Games has recalled approximately 117,500 Zippee silicone activity toys due to a choking hazard. The Zippee is a teal flexible silicone cylinder with holes where six colorful silicone strings can be pulled. Five of those six strings have a spherical ball end. The problem: those spherical ends can reach the back of a child's throat, creating a serious choking hazard. There has been one reported incident of a child placing the toy in their mouth and choking. The toys were sold from November 2019 through January 2024 — which means they've been in homes for years. Families may have moved the toy from one child to a younger sibling without realizing a recall had been issued. Here's how to identify the toy: look for date codes in MMDDYY format ranging from 030620 to 110823 stamped inside the silicone cylinder body. If you have one, stop letting your children use it immediately and contact Mobi Games for a full refund. You can find recall information at cpsc.gov or at playmobi.com/pages/product-recall. The Zippee was available on Amazon, PlayMobi's website, and in specialty toy stores for around $20. If you still have yours, the refund is worth the 10-minute process. Does this ring a bell? If you have one, share this post so other families can check too. ### Image Post Caption CPSC recall: Zippee silicone activity toys — 117,500 units. Choking hazard from spherical ends. Sold on Amazon and toy stores 2019-2024 for about $20. Full refund from Mobi Games. Check your toy box.

D20 Spring 2026: School Choice Open, Spring Calendar Reminders
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post A reminder for Colorado Springs families considering Academy District 20 for the 2026-27 school year: School Choice applications are open now. D20's School Choice program allows families who don't currently have a child enrolled in Academy District 20 to apply for available seats at D20 schools. If you've been curious about D20's programs, this is the window. Visit asd20.org for the application and to see which schools have available seats. For families already in D20, spring is a good time to review your school calendar and make sure you're connected to your school's communication channels. The 2025-26 year is winding down, and the transition to a new school year — or a new school — takes more planning than most of us expect. A quick note on D20's track record: the district has been Accredited with Distinction by the State of Colorado for 17 consecutive years. It serves over 26,000 students at 40 schools across Colorado Springs. That's a strong foundation for families considering a school change for fall. Questions about D20 enrollment?
