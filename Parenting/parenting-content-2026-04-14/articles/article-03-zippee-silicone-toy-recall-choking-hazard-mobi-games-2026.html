Zippee Silicone Activity Toy Recall: 117,500 Units — Is One in Your Toy Box?

By Marta Kim | April 14, 2026

The CPSC has recalled over 117,000 Zippee silicone activity toys due to a choking hazard. The toys were sold from 2019 through 2024 — which means yours might still be in rotation.

Some recalls affect products that families bought recently. This one may affect products that have been in your home for years.

Mobi Games has recalled approximately 117,500 Zippee silicone activity toys in the United States due to a choking hazard. The teal flexible cylinder toy — popular for its tactile, pull-and-stretch design — has spherical ball ends on five of its six silicone strings that can reach the back of a child's throat, posing a serious choking risk. One incident has already been reported to the CPSC: a child placed the toy in their mouth and gagged, resulting in choking and vomiting.

The toys were sold from November 2019 through January 2024, which means this product has had time to travel through toy rotations, be passed from older siblings to younger ones, and settle into the forgotten corners of toy bins across the country.

How to Identify the Toy

The Zippee is a flexible silicone cylinder, predominantly teal in color, with six holes from which silicone strings in different colors can be pulled. Five of the six strings have a spherical ball attached to the end. The toy's design is meant to be tactile and engaging — the pull-and-release mechanism is the main appeal for children.

To check whether your Zippee is part of the recall, look inside the silicone cylinder body for a date code stamped in MMDDYY format. Affected toys have date codes ranging from 030620 through 110823. If your toy falls in that range, it is part of the recall.

Why Silicone Doesn't Mean Safe by Default

Many parents gravitate toward silicone toys because silicone is flexible, durable, and free from many of the chemical concerns associated with certain plastics. Those properties are real. But material safety and design safety are different categories, and the Zippee recall illustrates the distinction clearly.

The choking hazard here is not about what the toy is made of — it's about the shape and size of specific components relative to a child's anatomy. The spherical ends were designed to provide satisfying tactile feedback. For young children who tend to mouth toys, that same sphere poses a geometric choking risk.

This is worth remembering when evaluating any toy: look at the small parts, the shapes, and the accessibility of detachable components — regardless of the material they're made from.

What to Do If You Have This Toy

Stop letting your children use the toy immediately. Take it out of your toy rotation and set it aside. Then contact Mobi Games to arrange a full refund. Details are available at playmobi.com/pages/product-recall or at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

If you've already given this toy away — to a younger sibling, a friend's child, a classroom, or a childcare center — it's worth reaching out to let those recipients know about the recall as well.

What's Next

This recall is active. The full refund process through Mobi Games is straightforward and does not require proof of purchase in most recall programs. Visit cpsc.gov/Recalls or playmobi.com/pages/product-recall for current instructions on how to proceed.

If you're unsure whether your family or someone in your network has this toy, it's worth a quick message or share. The sale period covered five years and hundreds of thousands of units.
