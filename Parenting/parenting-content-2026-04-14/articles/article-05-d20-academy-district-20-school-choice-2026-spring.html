D20 Spring 2026: School Choice Open, Spring Calendar, and What to Know Before Fall

By Keisha Williams | April 14, 2026

Academy District 20 has opened school choice applications for 2026-27. Here's what COS families considering D20 — or already in it — need to know this spring.

Spring is when Colorado Springs families make school decisions. Enrollment windows open, choice applications go live, and the question of where your child will be in September becomes something you actually have to answer rather than set aside.

For families who are looking at Academy District 20, or who are already in it and navigating the 2026-27 year, here's a practical rundown of what's happening now and what to keep track of.

School Choice Applications Are Open for 2026-27

D20's School Choice program is currently accepting applications from non-resident families — that is, families who do not currently have a child enrolled in Academy District 20 and who live outside the district's boundaries.

If your family has been considering D20 for the upcoming school year, this is the active window. Applications are reviewed based on available seats at specific schools, so earlier applications generally have more options. The process is handled through the district's website at asd20.org.

School Choice is one of the more practical tools available to Colorado Springs families because the city's four major school districts — D11, D20, D49, and Fountain-Fort Carson — have genuinely different academic programs, geographic coverage, and enrollment structures. Choice applications give families flexibility that the standard attendance-boundary system doesn't provide.

D20's Track Record

Academy District 20 serves more than 26,000 students across 40 schools in the northern Colorado Springs area. The district has been Accredited with Distinction by the State of Colorado for 17 consecutive years — a designation that reflects the district's consistent performance on state accountability measures, graduation rates, and academic programming quality.

For families comparing districts, that 17-year streak is a meaningful data point. District accreditation can shift from year to year based on student performance and financial accountability metrics. Sustained Accredited with Distinction status suggests a district that manages both consistently.

For Current D20 Families: Spring Reminders

If your family is already enrolled in D20, spring means a few things deserve your attention. The district calendar at calendar.asd20.org is the most reliable source for end-of-year dates, testing windows, staff development days, and transition timelines for families moving from elementary to middle or middle to high school.

If your child is transitioning between schools next fall — whether because of a grade-level change, a school boundary adjustment, or a specialty program application — confirm the enrollment process directly with your current school's registrar. Specialty programs including D20's gifted and talented pathways typically have their own enrollment timelines separate from the general district calendar.

Spring is also the time to update your contact information in D20's parent portal if it changed during the year. School communication during the final weeks of the year — including report cards, spring parent nights, and back-to-school preparation materials — all flows through registered contact channels.

What's Next

For families applying through School Choice: visit asd20.org now and submit an application for the 2026-27 year. Available seats vary by school and grade level, and the earlier you apply, the more options you're likely to have.

For current D20 families: check calendar.asd20.org for the spring semester schedule and confirm any transition steps for children moving to a new school in August. Direct questions about enrollment or school assignment to your school's office or the district's enrollment services at asd20.org.
