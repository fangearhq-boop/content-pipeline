Vevor Baby Swing Recall: Check These Model Numbers Now

By Keisha Williams | April 14, 2026

The CPSC has recalled approximately 1,020 Vevor baby swings due to a suffocation hazard. The swings violate the inclined sleeper ban. Here's what to do if you have one.

Sanven Technology has recalled approximately 1,020 Vevor baby swings due to a suffocation hazard. If you purchased a Vevor baby swing on Amazon or Vevor.com between January and August 2025, check your swing's model number now.

The recall covers swings that were marketed for infant sleep but have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees. That angle violates the Safe Sleep for Babies Act and the mandatory federal safety standard for infant sleep products. Inclined surfaces pose a specific danger to infants because they can cause an infant's head to tip forward, obstructing the airway — particularly during sleep.

Three models are covered by this recall:

The swings were sold on Amazon.com and Vevor.com from January 2025 through August 2025 at prices between $65 and $80. If you purchased a Vevor swing during this period, check the model number, which should be printed on the swing or its packaging.

The Inclined Sleeper Risk

The Safe Sleep for Babies Act was signed into law specifically because of injuries and deaths associated with inclined infant sleep products. Products that position babies at an incline greater than 10 degrees have been linked to positional asphyxia — a condition where the infant's body position restricts breathing.

The risk is highest during sleep, when a baby cannot self-correct their position if their head tilts forward. Even a short nap in an inclined position can be dangerous for very young infants whose neck muscles are not yet developed enough to lift or reposition their heads.

This recall is consistent with a broader CPSC effort to remove non-compliant inclined sleepers from the market. Parents who are uncertain about any infant sleep product can verify its compliance at cpsc.gov.

Safe Infant Sleep Alternatives

While the recall process is underway, parents need an infant sleep solution. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that babies sleep on a flat, firm surface — a crib, bassinet, or play yard that meets current safety standards. The sleep surface should be designed specifically for infant sleep and should not have any incline.

If your primary infant sleep product was the recalled Vevor swing, contact your pediatrician or a local family resource center if you need help identifying a compliant alternative. Many hospitals and social services agencies also offer safe sleep resources for families who need them.

What's Next

Contact Sanven Technology at 855-599-6320 to arrange the full refund. Representatives are available Monday through Friday from 9 AM to 5 PM Pacific Time. Additional recall details are available at cpsc.gov/Recalls.
