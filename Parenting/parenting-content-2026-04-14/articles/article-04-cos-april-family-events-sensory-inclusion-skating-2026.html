This Week in COS: Sensory Inclusion Night, Family Art Night, and Spring Skating Registration

By Sofia Reyes | April 14, 2026

Three upcoming Colorado Springs family events worth knowing about — including a sensory-friendly night this Thursday that's designed for families who know how rare that feels.

April in Colorado Springs tends to be a scheduling gap — spring break is over, summer isn't quite here, and the stretch between now and Memorial Day can feel a little thin on family programming. Not this week. Here are three upcoming events that are worth getting on your calendar.

Sensory Inclusion Night at Widefield Parks — This Thursday

Widefield Parks and Recreation is hosting a Sensory Inclusion Night on April 16 from 5:00 to 6:30 PM. This event is specifically designed with families of children with sensory sensitivities in mind — a space where parents can take a breath, kids can engage at their own pace, and the environment is structured to be predictable and welcoming rather than overstimulating.

If you've ever had to leave an event early because it was too loud, too crowded, or too unpredictable for your child, you understand why a dedicated sensory-inclusive evening matters. These events exist because the need is real, and they only continue if families show up for them.

This Thursday. 5:00 to 6:30 PM. Check parksandrec.wsd3.org for details and any registration requirements.

Family Painting Night — April 22

On April 22, Widefield Parks and Recreation is hosting a Family Painting P-Art-Y from 6:30 to 8:30 PM. The theme is Underwater Coral Watercolor — a subject that gives parents and children something visually interesting to work with and doesn't require any prior art experience.

Creative evening activities for families are relatively rare in the regular COS programming calendar. This one is a two-hour window that works well as a Tuesday-night reset after a long school week, and the watercolor format is accessible for ages from early elementary onward.

Check parksandrec.wsd3.org for registration details and any materials fees.

Spring Skating Registration at Sertich Ice Center

Sertich Ice Center has opened spring registration for its Learn to Skate program. The program offers classes for all ages and skill levels — which means families with a first-time skater and one who's been going for two years can both find a placement.

Sertich is one of the more underrated family activity options in Colorado Springs, particularly for the April-May window when outdoor activities can still be interrupted by weather. The rink offers a consistent, climate-controlled experience that doesn't depend on whether the Rockies cooperated this week.

Early spring registration typically means better class availability before spots fill up for the warmer-months programming. Visit coloradosprings.gov/recreation to register.
