Garden of the Gods Is Getting a Water Feature This Summer — What COS Families Need to Know

By Sofia Reyes | April 14, 2026

Colorado Springs' most iconic park is adding a lazy river-style water attraction opening before summer 2026. Here's what we know about it so far.

Garden of the Gods is many things to Colorado Springs families: a hiking destination, a photography backdrop, a geology classroom, and a place where you realize the city you live in is genuinely extraordinary. This summer, it's becoming something new: a place to cool off while floating between red rock formations.

The City of Colorado Springs has announced a new water feature at Garden of the Gods Park. Described as a "lazy river-style, geologically tantalizing experience," the attraction will offer visitors a relaxed, water-based way to move through the park's landscape while experiencing the iconic sandstone formations from a perspective that's never been available before.

Construction is scheduled to begin this spring, with the city targeting an opening before summer 2026.

What We Know About the New Water Feature

Based on the city's announcement, the attraction includes a gently winding water route designed to complement — not disturb — the natural landscape of the park. Preliminary plans describe designated entry points, scenic floating areas, and family-friendly features designed for visitors of different ages and abilities.

The city framed the project as a response to rising summer temperatures and growing interest in outdoor family experiences that provide shade and cooling options without requiring families to leave the natural environment they came to enjoy.

What makes this announcement different from a typical city parks project is the setting. Garden of the Gods already draws more than two million visitors per year. Adding an interactive water experience in that context is a significant investment in the park as a year-round, multi-use destination rather than just a scenic drive or hiking site.

Why This Matters for COS Families This Summer

Colorado Springs summers run hot. Average highs in July hover around 88°F, and with the region's elevation, UV exposure is also a factor that families managing young children need to plan around. Outdoor cooling options that don't require a long drive or a water park admission fee are genuinely valuable for local families.

Garden of the Gods is a free park — and while the water feature will likely carry an admission cost or per-use fee (not yet confirmed by the city), the setting offers an experience that commercial water parks simply can't replicate. Floating past rock formations that are more than 300 million years old, in a park that sits at the edge of one of America's most scenically distinctive cities, is the kind of thing that makes COS worth raising a family in.

For families with children who love geology or nature, this is also a chance to build on whatever learning they've done about the Garden of the Gods' Permian-era sandstone. There are few better ways to understand a geological formation than experiencing it at water level.

Planning Ahead

Since we're still in the construction and announcement phase, pricing and reservations details are not yet available. Based on how similar attractions have operated at popular parks, families should anticipate some form of advance registration or timed entry window — particularly during peak summer weeks when the park's existing trail system already sees high traffic.

The city's Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department will publish full details as the opening approaches. Families who want early notification can follow the city at coloradosprings.gov/parks or connect with the Colorado Springs Parks Facebook page for updates.

What's Next

Construction begins this spring, meaning families may see activity in the park during the next several weeks. The city has committed to a summer 2026 opening, which gives families a horizon to plan around as they organize school-break activities and summer schedules.

We'll update this guide as pricing, reservation details, and construction timelines are confirmed. For now, it's a summer planning note worth adding: Garden of the Gods, and something new.
