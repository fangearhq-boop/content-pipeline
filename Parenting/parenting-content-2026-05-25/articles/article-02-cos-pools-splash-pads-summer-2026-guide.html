COS Splash Pads and Pools Are Open — Your Complete Summer 2026 Water Guide

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 25, 2026

Memorial Day weekend is here, and that means Colorado Springs' outdoor pools and splash pads are officially open for business. The city's Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department kicked off water season this weekend, and COS families now have a handful of great free and low-cost options to cool off all summer long.

Here's everything you need to know — including one significant heads-up about Portal Pool.

Outdoor Pools (Open Now)

The city's outdoor public pools are operated in partnership with the YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region. Both opened Saturday, May 23.

If you're not looking to pay for pool admission, the city's spray grounds are completely free and open to the public throughout the summer.

The Watering Hole at John Venezia Community Park is particularly popular with younger kids — it has more than 12 individual water features spread across a large spray area. It tends to get crowded on hot afternoons, so arriving before noon is a good strategy.

The Portal Pool Situation

Here's the news COS families need to know before making their summer plans: Portal Pool at Portal Park is not opening this summer. The city is conducting a mine shaft stabilization project underneath Portal Park, and the pool cannot reopen until that engineering work is complete. According to the Colorado Springs Gazette (May 23, 2026), the timeline extends years beyond this summer season.

For families on the city's west side who relied on Portal Pool, this is a significant change. The nearest alternatives are the city-operated pools listed above. Planning your pool trips in advance — especially on hot summer days when facilities fill quickly — will matter more than usual this season.

Tips for the Summer

A few practical notes for COS water season:

  • Weekday mornings are the least crowded time at both pools and splash pads
  • City pools do not allow outside food at poolside — check current rules before your visit
  • Spray grounds dry out fast in Colorado's sun; bring extra water and sunscreen
  • YMCA season passes may be worth the investment if your family goes weekly

What's Next

Water season runs through Labor Day weekend in Colorado Springs. For the most current operating hours and any schedule changes, visit coloradosprings.gov/parks or the City of Colorado Springs PRCS page. Deerfield Hills spray ground opens June 1, giving families one more free option in a few days. Portal Pool's status will continue to depend on the progress of the mine shaft stabilization project — we'll keep COS parents updated as any new timeline information becomes available.
