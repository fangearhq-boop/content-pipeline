CPSC Recall: Orb Funkee Squeeze Toys May Contain Asbestos — What COS Families Need to Do Now

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 25, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) has recalled more than 121,000 Orb Funkee squeeze toys due to a risk of asbestos exposure. If you purchased these colorful squeeze toys at Walmart or Ollie's Bargain Outlet between February 2025 and April 2026, stop using them immediately and keep them away from children.

The concern is serious: the sand inside the toys may contain fibrous tremolite, a naturally occurring form of asbestos. Asbestos fibers are most dangerous when inhaled, and children who play with damaged toys — squeezing them until the sand leaks, or handling ruptured toys — could be at risk of exposure.

What to Do If You Have These Toys

The steps depend on whether the toy is intact or has been damaged.

If the toy is intact (no visible damage, no sand leakage): Take the toy away from children immediately. Do not let children continue to play with it. Place it somewhere safe and out of reach while you arrange a refund through The Orb Factory.

If the toy has been damaged, ruptured, or the sand has escaped: Put on a mask and gloves before handling. Use damp cloths to carefully pick up any sand that has leaked out. Place the toy, the cloths, the mask, and the gloves in a heavy-duty plastic bag. Seal that bag and place it inside a second heavy-duty plastic bag (double-bagging). Seal the outer bag with tape. Dispose of the double-bagged material in accordance with your local and state regulations. Do not vacuum the sand — vacuuming can spread particles into the air.

If you have concerns about potential exposure, contact your child's pediatrician. For questions about asbestos exposure specifically, you can also contact the Colorado Department of Public Health and Environment.

How to Get a Refund

Contact The Orb Factory directly to request a full refund. The company can be reached at 800-741-0089 (Monday through Friday, 8 AM to 4 PM ET) or by email at customerservice@orbtoys.com. Have information about the product available when you call, including the model number and the date code. Full recall details, including any updates to the refund process, are posted at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

What's Next

If you're a Colorado Springs parent who purchased these toys, act today. The recall was announced by the CPSC in May 2026 and covers purchases from Walmart and Ollie's Bargain Outlet over a roughly 14-month period — meaning many of these toys have been in homes for a year or more. Check your playroom, toy bins, and gift bags. If the toy is in your home, it needs to come out now. For any questions about whether your family's health has been affected, speak with your pediatrician. For the most up-to-date recall information, visit cpsc.gov/Recalls.
