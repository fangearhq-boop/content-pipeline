Territory Days 2026: Today Is the Last Day — What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 25, 2026

If your Memorial Day weekend plan still has an open slot, here it is: Territory Days in historic Old Colorado City runs today, Monday May 25, from 10 AM to 6 PM. It's free. It's been running for 50 years. And it's one of those events that reminds you why Colorado Springs is a genuinely great place to raise kids.

The 50th anniversary edition of the festival wrapped up a three-day run this weekend, and today is the last chance to experience it before another year passes. For families who haven't made it out yet, the Monday session tends to be slightly less crowded than Saturday and Sunday — which means more elbow room for the kids to actually enjoy the activities.

What's There for Families

Territory Days earns its reputation as one of COS's best free family events because of the sheer variety of things to do. The 180+ vendor booths cover everything from handmade crafts to festival food staples — turkey legs, funnel cakes, kettle corn — alongside more distinctive offerings from local artisans and vendors. Three stages host live music from more than 25 bands over the weekend.

The family-specific programming is what makes it genuinely kid-friendly rather than just kid-tolerant. Activities include pony rides, gold panning, a petting zoo, toy train rides, a mechanical bull, live birds of prey demonstrations, Old West gunfight reenactments, Native American dancers, and live blacksmithing. The gold panning activity tends to be a standout for younger kids — there's something about finding (artificial) gold nuggets that never gets old.

The festival runs in and around the historic W. Colorado Avenue corridor, which means you'll be walking through one of the most charming shopping districts in the city. The shops themselves are open too, so there's easy indoor refuge if the afternoon sun gets intense — and in Colorado Springs in late May, that's a real possibility.

Tips for Today

Arrive before noon if you have younger kids — mornings are cooler and parking is easier before the afternoon crowd builds. Street parking fills quickly, so consider parking a few blocks off Colorado Avenue and walking in. Sunscreen, hats, and a stroller for the little ones are all worth bringing. Water is available for purchase throughout the festival grounds, but bringing your own bottles saves money and hassle.

The festival is generally stroller-friendly, though the vendor-dense sections can get tight. Keep the youngest kids within arm's reach in the more crowded areas.

What's Next

Territory Days marks the unofficial end of Colorado Springs' spring event season — next weekend, the focus shifts to summer programming, camps, and outdoor activities as kids begin to exit school for the year. The 51st edition of Territory Days will return Memorial Day weekend 2027. For this year, today's your last shot. More information at shopoldcoloradocity.com or by emailing events@aponiproductions.com.
