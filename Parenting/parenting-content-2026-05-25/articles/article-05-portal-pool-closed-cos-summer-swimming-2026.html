Portal Pool Won't Reopen This Summer — Where Colorado Springs Families Can Swim in 2026

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 25, 2026

As Memorial Day weekend wraps up and summer officially begins, Colorado Springs families have an important planning note: Portal Pool at Portal Park is not opening this year, and the timeline for its return extends years beyond the 2026 season.

The closure is due to a mine shaft stabilization project currently underway beneath Portal Park. The engineering work is extensive, and the pool cannot safely reopen until it is complete. The Colorado Springs Gazette reported on May 23, 2026 that Portal Pool remains years from returning to service. For families who have relied on it as a summer staple, this is a meaningful change — especially for those on the city's west side where Portal Park is located.

Here's a complete guide to where COS families can swim this summer.

The City's Outdoor Public Pools

Colorado Springs operates outdoor public pools in partnership with the YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region. With Portal Pool closed, the city has three outdoor public pools available for the summer. Two of the confirmed pools opened Memorial Day weekend:

PoolAddressHours
Monument Valley Pool220 Mesa RdMon–Sat 10 AM–6 PM; Sun 12–6 PM
Wilson Ranch Pool2335 Allegheny DrDaily 10 AM–6 PM

Both locations offer swim lessons and season passes. The YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region (ppymca.org) also provides outdoor pool access through membership programs, which may be cost-effective for families who swim frequently.

Free Spray Grounds and Splash Pads

For free water play, the city's spray grounds are open and available throughout the summer. These don't require admission or a membership — just show up.

Spray grounds don't offer the same lap swimming or lesson opportunities as pools, but for younger children they can be just as fun — and the Watering Hole at John Venezia Community Park, with more than 12 individual water features, is particularly impressive.

Planning Around Limited Capacity

With Portal Pool out of the rotation, the city's remaining outdoor public pools will be in higher demand during hot stretches. A few practical tips for navigating a busier-than-usual pool season:

  • Weekday mornings (before noon) are significantly less crowded than weekend afternoons
  • Season pool passes through the YMCA can be worth it for families who plan to swim weekly — and they let you skip the daily admission line
  • Spray grounds are a good backup on days when the pools are particularly packed
  • Check coloradosprings.gov/parks for any schedule updates, closure announcements, or programming changes throughout the summer

What's Next

The city has not yet announced a projected reopening date for Portal Pool beyond "years from now," pending the outcome of the mine shaft stabilization project. COS Parenting will update families as the city releases new information about the park's future. In the meantime, plan your summer swim schedule around the three available outdoor pools and the city's free spray grounds — and book swim lessons early if your kids need them, as spots typically fill fast once summer arrives.
