### STORY 2: Splash Pads and Pools Open

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 10:00 AM MT

Summer is officially here — the city's splash pads and outdoor pools opened this weekend, and here's everything COS families need to know.

The two outdoor public pools that opened Saturday, May 23 are Monument Valley Pool (220 Mesa Rd, Mon–Sat 10 AM–6 PM, Sun 12–6 PM) and Wilson Ranch Pool (2335 Allegheny Dr, daily 10 AM–6 PM). Both are operated through a partnership between the city and the YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region. Season pool passes are available, and swim lessons are offered at both locations.

If you're looking for free water play, the city's spray grounds and splash pads are open and ready. Panorama Park's splash pad and the Watering Hole at John Venezia Community Park (12+ water features) both opened Sunday, May 24, running daily 10 AM–6 PM. Deerfield Hills spray ground opens June 1 (daily 10 AM–5 PM). The Uncle Wilbur Fountain at Acacia Park opens today, May 25, and runs daily 12–6 PM through August 11.

One thing to keep in mind as you plan your summer swim schedule: Portal Pool at Portal Park is closed and won't reopen for years while the city works on a mine shaft stabilization project underneath the park. The city is down to three outdoor public pools for the summer, so these facilities will be busy — especially on hot days.

For a full list of water recreation options, visit coloradosprings.gov/parks. Are you a splash pad family, a pool family, or both?

### STORY 3: Orb Funkee Recall

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 8:00 AM MT

If you have Orb Funkee squeeze toys at home, please read this now.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled more than 121,000 of these toys due to a risk of asbestos exposure. The sand inside the toys may contain fibrous tremolite — a naturally occurring form of asbestos — which can cause serious harm if inhaled. Stop using these immediately and keep them away from kids.

The recalled toys are the large golden "monkee" (model 17451) and an assortment of small "monkees" in orange, purple, and green (model 41929). The date code is 3102491A, found on the hand of the large monkee or the back of the small ones. These were sold at Walmart and Ollie's Bargain Outlet stores nationwide from February 2025 through April 2026, for between $5 and $40.

If the toy is intact: take it away from your kids and stop using it. If the toy has been damaged or the sand has escaped: put on a mask and gloves, use damp cloths to collect any sand, double-bag everything in heavy-duty plastic bags, and dispose of according to your local regulations. Do not vacuum the sand.

To request a refund, contact The Orb Factory at 800-741-0089 (Monday–Friday, 8 AM–4 PM ET) or email customerservice@orbtoys.com. Full recall information is available at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

### STORY 4: MeadowGrass Music Festival

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 10:00 AM MT

Looking for something different from Territory Days today? MeadowGrass Music Festival is wrapping up its three-day run in Palmer Lake — and it's worth the short drive from Colorado Springs.

The 15th annual festival is a folk and Americana music event with a genuinely family-friendly setup. Today is the final day, and here's the part COS parents will want to hear: kids 12 and under get in free with a paid adult. Daily passes for adults run $79–$89 depending on the day.

Beyond the live music, the festival features workshops, yoga sessions, a ropes course, an archery range, and late-night shows. Military families also receive a discount on tickets. It's the kind of event that feels less like a weekend errand and more like an actual family adventure.

Palmer Lake is about 25 miles north of Colorado Springs — a manageable drive for an afternoon out. Worth it if your family is into music and outdoor activities.

### STORY 5: Portal Pool Closed

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 5:00 PM MT

As you plan your family's summer, here's something worth knowing: Portal Pool is not opening this year — and it won't be back for years.

The city is working on a mine shaft stabilization project underneath Portal Park, and the pool can't reopen until that work is complete. The Colorado Springs Gazette reported last week that the timeline extends well beyond 2026. It's not the kind of news that makes a summer easier to plan, especially for families in that part of the city who counted on Portal Pool.

The good news: there are still water options in COS.

The city's two confirmed outdoor public pools — Monument Valley Pool (220 Mesa Rd) and Wilson Ranch Pool (2335 Allegheny Dr) — both opened this weekend for the summer. The city has three outdoor public pools total, all run in partnership with the YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region. Season passes and swim lessons are available. The free spray grounds at Panorama Park and John Venezia Community Park are also open daily 10 AM–6 PM, and Deerfield Hills spray ground opens June 1.

For YMCA members, the Pikes Peak YMCA also offers outdoor pool access through its membership programs (ppymca.org).

It's not the same as having Portal Pool back, but at least you know what you're working with this summer. What's your family's go-to swimming spot in Colorado Springs?
