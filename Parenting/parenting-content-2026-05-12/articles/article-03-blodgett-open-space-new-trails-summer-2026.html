14 New Miles of Trails at Blodgett Open Space Are Coming This Summer — Here's What COS Families Need to Know

By Jamie Rivera | May 12, 2026 | COS Parenting

One of Colorado Springs' most popular open spaces is about to get a lot more room to roam. The City of Colorado Springs is completing approximately 14 miles of new and improved trails at Blodgett Open Space, with the full project expected to wrap up by end of summer 2026. That means this summer — the one starting in about 10 days — is the right time to start planning your family's first explorations.

Blodgett Open Space is located on the northwest side of Colorado Springs. It's been a well-loved spot for locals for years, offering classic Colorado scenery: red dirt trails, scrub oak, scattered pine, and views of the Pikes Peak region that remind you why you live here. What's changing is scale and variety.

What's Being Built

The expansion isn't just adding miles — it's adding intentional structure for different types of users, which matters a lot for families with kids of different ages and abilities.

  • Hiking-only trails (northern portion): These are designed for walkers and hikers, without mountain bikes sharing the path. For parents with young children in strollers, toddlers who need predictability, or families who want a quieter experience, this dedicated zone is a significant upgrade.
  • Bike-only trails (southern portion): Mountain bike families have a dedicated area where riders can move at their own pace without worrying about hikers rounding a corner. Great for older kids developing riding skills.
  • Phased openings: The city is opening sections as they're completed throughout the construction period, which means some new trails may be accessible before the full 14 miles are done. Worth checking the city's parks and recreation announcements for specific opening updates.

The project also includes a new bridge at Blodgett — one of several bridge projects planned across COS open spaces in 2026.

The Foundation Behind the Expansion

This project grew from Blodgett Open Space's Master and Management Plan, which was approved by Colorado Springs City Council in 2024. The plan was developed with community input — including residents who pushed for single-use trail designations to separate hikers and cyclists. The trail design reflects what local families actually asked for.

The Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department is overseeing the project. For updates on trail openings and conditions, families can check coloradosprings.gov/BlodgettOpenSpace or follow the city's parks social channels.

While You're Planning Summer Outdoor Time

Blodgett is one piece of a broader outdoor summer puzzle in Colorado Springs. A few other family-friendly considerations for the season ahead:

  • Pikes Peak summer season: The May 1 through November 30 season pricing is now in effect — $18 for adults (16+), $8 for children ages 6-15, and $65 for a car with up to five passengers.
  • Garden of the Gods: Always free, with the Garden of the Gods Loop connecting several trail options. Flat, scenic, and great for mixed-age families.
  • Red Rock Canyon Open Space: 15 miles of trails with views similar to Garden of the Gods but typically less crowded — a good option if parking at the park is a challenge on busy summer weekends.

What's Next

Blodgett Open Space trail construction is ongoing, with full completion expected by the end of summer 2026. Watch for phased trail openings throughout the summer via the City of Colorado Springs parks announcements at coloradosprings.gov/TOPS2026. The trail system will include hiking-only and bike-only sections designed to work for the full range of COS families. Start planning your first visit now.
