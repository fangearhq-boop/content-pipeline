CPSC Recall: Crayola Pip-Cubes Magnetic Building Toy Recalled for Fatal Magnet Ingestion Risk

By Sarah Morales | May 12, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs parents with young children at home should check their toy bins today: approximately 9,400 Crayola-branded Pip-Cubes magnetic building cubes have been recalled by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission due to a potentially fatal magnet ingestion hazard.

The recall, listed on CPSC.gov under the brand CreateOn — the importer of the Crayola-licensed product — covers colorful magnetic building cubes sold in multiple configurations. The cubes were sold at Michaels stores and on Amazon.com between May and July 2025.

What's the Hazard

The concern is straightforward and serious. The magnets inside the Pip-Cubes can detach if the cube's seams separate with normal use. When high-powered magnets are swallowed — even two or more separately at different times — they can attract each other through the walls of the intestines.

That internal attraction can cause the intestines to perforate, twist, or become blocked. The resulting complications include blood poisoning and, in the worst cases, death. The CPSC considers magnetic toy ingestion among its highest-priority hazard categories precisely because the injuries progress rapidly and can be catastrophic before symptoms become obvious.

As of the recall announcement, no injuries had been reported. That is typical for voluntary recalls made before incidents occur — and it's the outcome everyone wants. The goal is to get these toys out of reach of children before an injury happens.

All sets were sold between May 2025 and July 2025. If your family purchased Crayola Pip-Cubes as a gift during that window — for a birthday, a school end-of-year gift, or any other occasion — pass this information along to the recipient's family as well.

What to Do Right Now

Stop using the cubes immediately and remove them from reach of children. Then contact CreateOn to arrange a free replacement.

  • Phone: 1-800-333-0549 (Monday through Friday, 9:30 AM to 4:00 PM CT)
  • Email: pipcuberecall@CreateOn.com
  • Website: CreateOn.com/recall

Consumers will receive a pre-paid return label to ship the recalled cubes back and will receive a replacement set in return. CreateOn is offering a free product exchange — there's no cost to the consumer beyond removing and returning the recalled toy.

What's Next

If your family has the recalled Pip-Cubes, stop use immediately and contact CreateOn for your free replacement. Confirm whether any Pip-Cubes were given as gifts to other families and share this recall information with them. For more information on the recall, visit CPSC.gov or call the CPSC Consumer Hotline at 1-800-638-2772. You can also report unsafe products at SaferProducts.gov.
