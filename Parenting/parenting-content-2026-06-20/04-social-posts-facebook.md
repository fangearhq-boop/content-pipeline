### STORY 1: SHEIN Michley Children's Pajamas Recall — Burn Hazard

#### Long-Form Post

⚠️ RECALL ALERT: SHEIN Michley Children's Pajamas — Burn Hazard

If you bought children's one-piece pajamas from SHEIN.com in the last year, check the label now. SHEIN Distribution Corporation is recalling Michley Children's Pajamas because they violate the mandatory federal flammability standard for children's sleepwear — which means they're a burn hazard.

Here's what's affected: Sizes 80–130 in four designs (green dinosaur, pink bunny, yellow giraffe, and purple rabbit). The pajamas were sold on SHEIN.com from May 2025 through December 2025 for about $25.

If you have these, stop using them immediately. Here's how to get your full refund:
1. Cut the pajamas in half to destroy them.
2. Take a photo of the cut pajamas.
3. Email the photo to uscsteam@shein.com.
4. You'll receive a full refund.

For the full recall notice or to report an injury, visit cpsc.gov or call 1-800-638-2772.

### STORY 2: BABESIDE Doll and Stroller Toy Recalled — Choking Hazard

#### Long-Form Post

⚠️ RECALL ALERT: BABESIDE Doll and Stroller Toy — Choking Hazard for Under-3s

If you've ordered the BABESIDE Doll and Stroller set from Amazon, this one's urgent — especially for families with children under 3.

About 2,200 of these toys have been recalled because they violate the small parts ban. The pacifier and the bear's eyes can detach, and both are small enough to be a deadly choking hazard for babies and toddlers. This toy was marketed for children under 3, who are at the highest risk.

The toy was sold on Amazon by the seller HYBDOLLS. The CPSC announced the recall on June 18, 2026.

Here's what to do right now:
1. Stop letting your child use the toy immediately.
2. Cut the bear in half.
3. Write a large "X" on the pacifier in permanent marker.
4. Photograph both destroyed items.
5. Email the photo to support@babeside.com — you'll receive free replacement accessories.

Questions? Visit cpsc.gov or call 1-800-638-2772.

Did you know that cpsc.gov lets you sign up for recall alerts by email? It's worth setting up — especially for toys.

### STORY 3: COS 4th of July 2026 — America's 250th

#### Long-Form Post

🎆 America's 250th Birthday is July 4 — and Colorado Springs has a full lineup for families.

This isn't a typical Fourth of July. July 4, 2026 marks the Semiquincentennial — America's 250th birthday. That happens once, and COS has options at every price point, from free community fireworks to a ticketed symphony performance.

Here's what's on the calendar:

**Ford Amphitheater — Star-Spangled Symphony** (ticketed)
The Colorado Springs Philharmonic performs Dolly Parton and Tina Turner tributes, plus the 1812 Overture. Gates open at 5:30 PM, the show starts at 6:30 PM, and fireworks begin at 9:00 PM. Address: 95 Spectrum Loop.

**Rock Ledge Ranch** — $7–13, free for children under 3
Carnival games, a pie-eating contest, and historical interpreters bring the 250th to life. Open 10 AM to 4 PM at 1401 Recreation Way.

**Banning Lewis Ranch** — Free
Two live bands, about 30 food trucks, and fireworks at dusk. Great free option for the whole family.

**Palmer Lake** — Free
A smaller, quieter celebration with fireworks starting at 9:15 PM. Event runs 4–10 PM.

Nearby, Cripple Creek has a parade at 11 AM and evening fireworks, and Woodland Park also has options.

### STORY 4: Bear Creek Nature Center Pollinator Celebration — June 26

#### Long-Form Post

🐝 The Pollinator Celebration at Bear Creek Nature Center is this Saturday, June 26 — just six days away.

For $5 per person, families can spend a morning or afternoon in Bear Creek Regional Park doing bug sweeps, learning about native Colorado pollinators, and getting hands-on with the kind of outdoor science that sticks. It's one of those experiences where kids don't realize they're learning until they're home excitedly explaining why bees matter to anyone who'll listen.

Bear Creek Regional Park is on the west side of Colorado Springs, and the event is put on by the El Paso County nature center program — the same folks who run the free and low-cost programs through the year.
