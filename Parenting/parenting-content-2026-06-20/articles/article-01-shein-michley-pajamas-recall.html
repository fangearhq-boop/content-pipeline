SHEIN Michley Children's Pajamas Recalled for Burn Hazard — What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | June 20, 2026

National Parenting

Federal law requires children's sleepwear to meet strict flammability standards for a reason — and a new CPSC recall is a reminder of what's at stake when a product falls short. SHEIN Distribution Corporation is recalling Michley Children's Pajamas after the Consumer Product Safety Commission determined that the one-piece sleepwear violates the mandatory flammability standard for children's sleepwear, creating a burn hazard.

If you've bought children's one-piece pajamas from SHEIN.com in the past year, it's worth checking what you have before your child wears them again tonight.

What's Being Recalled

The recall covers Michley-brand one-piece children's pajamas sold on SHEIN.com from May 2025 through December 2025 at approximately $25 each. Four designs are included in the recall:

  • Green dinosaur
  • Pink bunny
  • Yellow giraffe
  • Purple rabbit

The affected sizes are 80 through 130, which correspond to toddler and early-elementary-aged children. The CPSC announced the recall on June 18, 2026.

The Hazard: Why Flammability Standards Exist

Children's sleepwear has been subject to federal flammability regulations since the 1970s, when house fires involving children's pajamas prompted Congress to act. The regulations require that sleepwear either be made from inherently flame-resistant fabric, or be snug-fitting so that it doesn't ignite easily when exposed to a small flame — like a candle, space heater, or stovetop.

When a product violates these standards, the risk is real: children spend seven or more hours in sleepwear, often near heat sources, and can't respond as quickly as adults if something goes wrong. The Michley pajamas don't meet these requirements, which is why the CPSC is treating this as a burn hazard.

What Families Should Do Now

The CPSC's guidance is clear: stop using these pajamas immediately. Don't let your child sleep in them while you figure out next steps.

To receive a full refund:

  1. Cut the pajamas in half to destroy them.
  2. Take a clear photo of the cut pajamas.
  3. Email the photo to uscsteam@shein.com.
  4. SHEIN will issue a full refund.

If you have questions or want to report a product-related injury, you can also contact the CPSC directly. Their consumer hotline is 1-800-638-2772, and the full recall notice is available at cpsc.gov.

A Note on SHEIN and Product Safety

SHEIN has faced previous product safety scrutiny, and this recall reflects the broader challenge of international fast-fashion retailers selling children's products in the United States. The federal flammability standard isn't a guideline — it's a requirement. Families shopping for children's clothing online, particularly for sleepwear, may want to verify that the product explicitly meets CPSC flammability standards before purchasing.
