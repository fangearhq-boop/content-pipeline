BABESIDE Doll and Stroller Toy Recalled for Choking Hazard — A Guide for Families with Children Under 3

By Jamie Rivera | June 20, 2026

National Parenting

A toy designed and marketed for babies and toddlers under age 3 has been recalled because it contains small parts that can detach — the exact hazard the federal small parts ban was written to prevent. The Consumer Product Safety Commission announced on June 18, 2026, that about 2,200 BABESIDE Doll and Stroller Children's Toys are being pulled from the market after it was found that both the toy's pacifier and its bear's eyes can break free from the product, creating a deadly choking risk for young children.

If you've ordered this toy from Amazon in the last year, stop letting your child play with it now.

What's Being Recalled

The recall covers the BABESIDE Doll and Stroller Children's Toy, sold on Amazon by the seller HYBDOLLS. The toy includes a small plush bear and a miniature stroller accessory. Two components pose the hazard: a small detachable pacifier included with the set, and the bear's eyes, which can separate from the plush body.

Both pieces are small enough to be a choking hazard — specifically for children under 3, who are developmentally the most likely to put objects in their mouths and who are least able to clear an airway obstruction on their own. The toy was marketed directly to this age group, which makes the small parts violation especially serious.

Approximately 2,200 units were sold, all through Amazon.

The Small Parts Ban and Why It Matters

Federal regulations prohibit toys intended for children under 3 from containing small parts — defined as any component that fits entirely into a small parts testing cylinder, roughly the size of a child's airway. The rule exists because choking is one of the leading causes of injury-related death in children under 4, and small toy parts are among the most common culprits.

When a toy fails the small parts test, the CPSC takes it seriously regardless of how the hazard presents — whether the part was small from the start or became small through normal use (like a detaching eye or accessory). This product fails on both counts: the pacifier is inherently a small part, and the bear's eyes can detach during play.

What Families Should Do Now

The CPSC is directing families to destroy the toy and submit proof for replacement accessories. Here's the exact process:

  1. Stop use immediately — remove the toy from your child's reach.
  2. Cut the bear in half to destroy it.
  3. Write a large "X" on the pacifier accessory in permanent marker to render it unusable.
  4. Take a photo showing both destroyed items.
  5. Email the photo to support@babeside.com — the company will send free replacement accessories.

If you have additional questions or want to report a choking incident or injury, you can reach the CPSC consumer hotline at 1-800-638-2772 or file a report at cpsc.gov.

Buying Toys for Children Under 3 Safely

When shopping for toys for children under 3, the safest starting point is to look for products that explicitly state "CPSC compliant" or "no small parts" on the packaging. Checking cpsc.gov's recall database before purchasing items from third-party Amazon sellers is also a practical step — the database is searchable and updated regularly. If a toy has small decorative features like sewn-on or glued-on eyes, testing whether they can be pulled off before giving it to a young child takes only a moment and can prevent a serious incident.
