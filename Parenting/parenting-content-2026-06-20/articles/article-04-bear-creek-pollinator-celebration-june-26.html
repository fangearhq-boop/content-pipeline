Bear Creek Nature Center Pollinator Celebration Is This Saturday, June 26 — $5 Family Science Day

By Jamie Rivera | June 20, 2026

Local Events

The Bear Creek Nature Center Pollinator Celebration is six days away — this Saturday, June 26 — and at $5 per person, it's one of the more accessible outdoor science events on the Colorado Springs calendar this month. If you've been looking for a reason to get outside with your kids and do something genuinely educational without spending much, this is it.

What to Expect

The event is held at Bear Creek Regional Park on the west side of Colorado Springs, which is itself a worthwhile destination — the park has well-maintained trails, native habitat, and the kind of open natural space that's hard to find this close to the city center.

The Pollinator Celebration centers on hands-on learning about Colorado's native pollinators. Activities include bug sweeps, which involve using nets to collect and examine small insects from vegetation — an activity that tends to be a genuine hit with kids who've never done it before. Alongside the sweeps, there's guided education about native Colorado pollinator species: which bees, butterflies, and beetles live in this region, what plants they depend on, and why their populations matter to local ecosystems.

El Paso County runs the nature center program, and their events are consistently well-organized. This isn't a loosely structured festival with a nature theme tacked on — it's genuine outdoor science education structured for families with children of various ages.

Why This Event Stands Out

Free and low-cost outdoor science opportunities are one of the underappreciated strengths of the Colorado Springs area, and this one checks several boxes at once: native species focus, hands-on activity, county-run programming with experienced educators, and a natural setting that enhances rather than distracts from the learning. For kids who haven't done much formal nature education, bug sweeps have a way of opening a door — suddenly every meadow looks different when you know what's living in it.

It's also worth noting that this is an introductory-level event. You don't need to know anything about pollinators going in. The educators meet participants where they are, which makes it suitable for children who are curious but haven't had much structured nature programming, as well as for families who are just starting to explore outdoor science.

Logistics

Bear Creek Regional Park is located on the west side of Colorado Springs. The $5 per person admission covers all activities. No reservation information has been listed, suggesting it's a walk-up event — but arriving early for a Saturday nature event in June is generally a good idea.

Appropriate footwear for a park setting is recommended, and given the outdoor June conditions, sunscreen and water are worth packing. If your kids are the type to want to hold every insect they find, a small container or a nature journal makes a good addition to the bag.

About Bear Creek Nature Center

The Bear Creek Nature Center is operated by El Paso County and serves as a hub for environmental education programs throughout the year. Their programming calendar includes events focused on native plants, wildlife, geology, and seasonal science topics — many of them free or low-cost. The Pollinator Celebration is part of a consistent effort to connect COS families with the natural systems that surround the city.
