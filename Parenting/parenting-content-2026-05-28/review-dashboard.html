D11 Considers $775M School Bond for November Ballot
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM MT Here's something every D11 family will want to watch between now and November: Colorado Springs District 11 is weighing a $775 million bond referendum for the November ballot. The money, if voters approve it, would fund air conditioning for aging school buildings — some of which are 50 to 100 years old and don't have A/C — along with a $10,000 pay increase for every teacher in the district, from first-year educators to 30-year veterans. The bond would also fund broader facility improvements across the district's aging building stock. If the measure passes, the tax impact would be roughly $6.50 more per month for every $100,000 of a home's assessed value. That's about $32.50 per month for a home assessed at $500,000. District officials haven't finalized the ballot language yet — the board is still exploring financing options. But with summer break starting and November voting just a few months away, now's the time to understand what's being proposed. Would you support this bond measure? What would make the biggest difference in your family's D11 school experience? --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 8:00 AM MT D11 is exploring a $775M bond for the November ballot. Bearlala Baby Lounger Recall — Walmart, ~300 Units
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 10:00 AM MT Parents: if you bought a Bearlala baby lounger from Walmart.com in the past year, check your purchase history right now. CPSC has recalled Bearlala-branded baby loungers sold under the name "Granlass" by the company Nuoxuann on Walmart.com. About 300 units were sold between August and October 2025 for $35 to $45. They're light gray with a leaf pattern. The problem: these loungers violate the mandatory federal safety standard for infant sleep products. The sides are too low to contain an infant. The openings at the foot are wider than allowed, posing entrapment and fall hazards. And because the lounger has no stand, it poses a fall risk if placed on any elevated surface. Stop using it immediately. To get a full refund, cut the foam padding and cover in half and email photos of the destroyed pieces to Recall-nuoxuann@outlook.com. The email will give you refund instructions. If you have questions or need help, email Nuoxuann directly at that same address. Pass this along to any parents you know who shop at Walmart for baby gear. --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 10:00 AM MT CPSC Recall: Bearlala baby loungers sold on Walmart.com (~300 units, $35-$45, Aug-Oct 2025). Fall and entrapment hazard. Stop using, destroy the product, email Recall-nuoxuann@outlook.com for a full refund.

Bring sunscreen, water, and comfortable walking shoes. Parking at Memorial Park is available. Have you been to Get Outdoors Day before? Colorado Springs District 11's Adult and Family Education program holds its 2026 graduation ceremony at 7 PM at the Roy J. Wasson Academic Campus. The ceremony honors adults who finished their GED, English as a Second Language program, or adult diploma while juggling work, family, and all of the things that make going back to school harder the second (or third) time around. June Family Events Guide for Colorado Springs
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 5:00 PM MT Summer is officially here — and Colorado Springs families have a packed June ahead. Here's a quick guide to the best family-friendly events to kick off the season. **Rock Ledge Ranch (June 3-14, 10 AM-5 PM)** Step back into Colorado history at this hands-on living history experience near Garden of the Gods. Make cornhusk dolls, watch blacksmith demonstrations, explore an 1870s homestead, and try Victorian-era lawn games. It's a great way to give kids a real sense of what life looked like here 150 years ago. **El Pomar Family Exploration Day (June 6, 10 AM-2 PM, free)** The Penrose Heritage Museum is hosting a free family day with interactive activities and exhibits. It's a short trip and free admission for the whole family. **Get Outdoors Day (June 6, 9 AM-2 PM, Memorial Park, free)** Colorado Springs' annual outdoor festival — archery, climbing, paddleboarding, and free fishing gear for kids. See our separate post for all the details. **Peak Adrenaline (June 11-14, Pikes Peak International Raceway)** Four days of motorsports, live music, and outdoor adventure on 600+ acres. Family-friendly energy with something for older kids and adults who like speed and adventure. **PPLD Summer Adventure (starts June 1)** It's not too late to sign up for the library's summer reading and activity program. Register at ppld.org/summer. The program runs through July 31. What events are your family most excited about? Let us know what we might have missed. --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 5:00 PM MT June family events in Colorado Springs: Rock Ledge Ranch (June 3-14), Get Outdoors Day (June 6, free), El Pomar Family Day (June 6, free), Peak Adrenaline (June 11-14).
