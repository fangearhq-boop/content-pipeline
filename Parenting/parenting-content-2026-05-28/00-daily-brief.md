### STORY 1: D11 Considers $775M School Bond for November Ballot
**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** Local News
**Byline:** Sarah Morales

Colorado Springs District 11 is weighing a $775 million bond referendum for the November 2026 ballot. The bond would fund air conditioning for aging school buildings (some 50-100 years old), teacher pay increases of $10,000 across all experience levels, and broader facility upgrades. If passed, homeowners would pay approximately $6.50 more per month for every $100,000 of property value. The proposal was reported May 27 by KRDO.

**Key facts:**
- Amount: $775 million bond referendum
- Purpose: Air conditioning + teacher pay increase ($10,000 for all experience levels) + school improvements
- Tax impact: ~$6.50/month per $100,000 of home value
- Potential ballot: November 2026
- Some D11 schools are 50-100 years old and lack air conditioning
- Source: KRDO May 27, 2026

### STORY 2: Bearlala Baby Lounger Recall — Walmart, ~300 Units
**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** National Parenting (CPSC recall)
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera

CPSC has recalled Bearlala-branded baby loungers sold by Nuoxuann on Walmart.com. About 300 units were sold August through October 2025 for $35-$45. The loungers violate the mandatory standard for infant sleep products: the sides are too low, foot openings are too wide (fall and entrapment hazard), and the product has no stand (fall hazard on elevated surfaces). Contact Nuoxuann at Recall-nuoxuann@outlook.com for a full refund.

**Key facts:**
- Brand: Bearlala / sold as "Granlass" on Walmart.com by Nuoxuann
- Units: About 300
- Sold: August–October 2025, Walmart.com, $35–$45
- Hazard: Fall and entrapment (sides too low, foot openings too wide, no stand)
- Fix: Destroy product (cut foam and cover in half), email photos to Recall-nuoxuann@outlook.com
- Contact: Recall-nuoxuann@outlook.com

### STORY 3: Get Outdoors Day — Free Family Festival June 6 at Memorial Park
**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** Local Events
**Byline:** Sarah Morales

Colorado Springs hosts its annual Get Outdoors Day on Saturday, June 6, at Memorial Park (Prospect Lake), from 9 AM to 2 PM MT. Admission is free. - Activities: Archery, paddleboarding, fly fishing, climbing wall, Touch a Truck, dunk tank

### STORY 5: June Family Events Guide for Colorado Springs
**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** Local Events
**Byline:** Sarah Morales

Colorado Springs has a loaded June event calendar for families heading into summer. Highlights include Rock Ledge Ranch historic experiences (June 3-14), El Pomar Penrose Heritage Museum's free Family Exploration Day (June 6), Get Outdoors Day at Memorial Park (June 6 — free), and Peak Adrenaline motorsports and outdoor festival (June 11-14).

**Key facts:**
- Rock Ledge Ranch: June 3-14, 10 AM–5 PM, hands-on history activities (cornhusk dolls, blacksmith demos, Victorian games)

- Get Outdoors Day: June 6, 9 AM–2 PM, Memorial Park, free (see Story 3)
- Peak Adrenaline: June 11-14, family-friendly motorsports + live music + outdoor adventure, Pikes Peak International Raceway
- PPLD Summer Adventure: starts June 1 (registration now open at ppld.org/summer)
