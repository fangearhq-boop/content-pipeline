Colorado Springs June 2026 Family Events Guide: The Best Free and Low-Cost Things to Do

By Sarah Morales | May 28, 2026 | COS Parenting

Summer in Colorado Springs starts strong in June, and families have more going on than usual this year. Between free outdoor festivals, hands-on history experiences, and motorsports adventures, there's something for every age group and every budget. Here's what we're watching on the calendar for June 2026.

Rock Ledge Ranch: June 3-14

If your kids have never visited Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site near Garden of the Gods, this is the summer to fix that. The living history ranch runs special programming June 3 through June 14, from 10 AM to 5 PM daily.

Families can explore Colorado history across several eras — from the American Indian area where kids can learn about Native trade and make cornhusk dolls, to an 1880s homestead cabin, to an 1870s Victorian home where they can try period games. There are also blacksmith demonstrations and a look at early 20th-century Edwardian life at the 1907 Orchard House.

It's the kind of experience that turns history from something kids read about into something they actually do. Check rockledgeranch.com for admission prices and current hours before you go.

Get Outdoors Day: June 6 (Free)

The annual Get Outdoors Day festival at Memorial Park's Prospect Lake runs June 6, 9 AM to 2 PM, and admission is completely free. Activities include archery, paddleboarding, fly fishing instruction, a climbing wall, "Touch a Truck," and a dunk tank.

Colorado Parks and Wildlife will hand out free fishing poles, bait, and instruction to the first 650 children ages 15 and under. Arrive early if your family wants to take home a free setup. More than 3,000 people are expected to attend. See our full article on Get Outdoors Day for everything you need to know before you go.

El Pomar Family Exploration Day: June 6 (Free)

The Penrose Heritage Museum at El Pomar Foundation is hosting a free Family Exploration Day on June 6 from 10 AM to 2 PM. The event features interactive activities and exhibits from the museum's collections, which focus on the history of Colorado Springs and the Pikes Peak region.

It's a quieter option than Get Outdoors Day and works well as a second stop if you want to maximize the Saturday — or as the main event if your family prefers indoor/cultural activities. Admission is free and open to the public. The museum is located in the Colorado Springs area and is accessible without an appointment during the Family Exploration Day.

Peak Adrenaline: June 11-14

For families with older kids who like speed, noise, and outdoor adventure, Peak Adrenaline runs June 11 through June 14 at Pikes Peak International Raceway. The four-day festival combines high-speed motorsports exhibition, live music, and 600+ acres of off-road outdoor activity.

It's a paid event and geared toward a slightly older crowd — kids who are into vehicles, racing, or just big outdoor energy. Check the Peak Adrenaline website for ticketing details and the full event schedule as the date approaches.

Don't Forget: PPLD Summer Adventure Starts Monday

If you haven't registered your kids for the Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program, you have until June 1 to get a jump on things. The program runs June 1 through July 31, encouraging reading and activities with prizes. You can register at ppld.beanstack.org, on the Beanstack app, or in person at any PPLD branch. Teens can also sign up for volunteer opportunities to earn community hours.

What's Next

June is just the start. July brings its own lineup of free and low-cost family activities in Colorado Springs, including Fourth of July events at America the Beautiful Park and ongoing programming at every PPLD library branch throughout the summer. Keep an eye on visitcos.com and cospringsmom.com for updated event listings as new events are added. Summer in COS is what you make of it — and the city has given families a lot to work with this year.
