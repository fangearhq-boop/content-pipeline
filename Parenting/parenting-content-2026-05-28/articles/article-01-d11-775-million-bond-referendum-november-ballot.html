D11 Considers $775M School Bond for November Ballot: What COS Families Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | May 28, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs District 11 families could be heading to the polls this November to weigh in on a $775 million school bond referendum. The district's board is exploring placing the measure on the November 2026 ballot, according to reporting from KRDO, as it seeks to address aging infrastructure and teacher compensation that community members have long flagged as urgent concerns.

If the board moves forward and voters approve it, the bond would represent one of the largest school investments in D11's history. Here's what parents and families need to understand about what's being proposed and what it would mean for your household.

What the Bond Would Fund

The $775 million proposal targets three main areas: air conditioning for schools that currently don't have it, teacher pay increases, and broader building improvements.

The air conditioning piece is significant. Some D11 school buildings are between 50 and 100 years old and were not built with cooling systems. Colorado Springs experiences hot summer weather, and with the district's school year increasingly starting in August and ending in late May, students and staff in un-air-conditioned buildings face real comfort and safety concerns during temperature extremes.

The teacher compensation component would provide a $10,000 pay increase for every teacher in the district — from first-year educators to those with 30 or more years of experience. This comes as D11 has faced ongoing pressure over educator pay, with the district's classified staff contract having been a point of contention throughout the 2025-26 school year.

What It Would Cost Families

School bond measures are funded through property taxes, and D11 has been transparent about the estimated impact. If the bond passes, families can expect to pay approximately $6.50 more per month for every $100,000 of their home's assessed value.

Where Things Stand

The D11 board has not yet finalized the ballot language or formally voted to place the measure before voters. According to KRDO's reporting from May 27, district officials are still exploring various financing structures for the bond. The November 2026 election would be the target timeline for voter consideration.

Families who want to follow this closely can attend D11 school board meetings, where decisions about the bond's structure and placement will be made in the coming months. The board meets regularly at the D11 central office, and meetings are open to the public.

Why This Matters for D11 Families

The bond touches something real for many Colorado Springs families who have kids in D11 buildings: the physical learning environment. For parents whose children attend schools built decades ago, the promise of air conditioning in a hotter Colorado climate is meaningful. For families concerned about teacher retention and quality, the $10,000 pay increase represents a direct investment in the educators who teach their children every day.

Whether or not families end up supporting the bond at the ballot box will likely depend on how they weigh those educational benefits against the tax impact — a calculation that looks different for a family in a $200,000 home than one in a $600,000 home.

What's Next

D11 will need to finalize bond language and vote to place the measure on the November ballot before the filing deadline. Watch for public forums this summer where the district is likely to present details and take community input. The formal ballot decision will come from the D11 board in the weeks ahead. For the latest, visit d11.org or follow D11's communications channels.
