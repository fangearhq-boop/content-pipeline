D11 Adult and Family Education Holds 2026 Graduation Tonight at Wasson Campus

By Jamie Rivera | May 28, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs District 11's Adult and Family Education program holds its 2026 graduation ceremony tonight at 7 PM at the Roy J. Wasson Academic Campus. Tonight's event celebrates a different kind of graduate — adults who pursued their education while managing jobs, families, and the real-world demands that make going back to school one of the harder things a person can do.

This ceremony closes out a graduation season in Colorado Springs that saw thousands of D11 high school seniors walk across stages at Ed Robson Arena earlier this month. Tonight's event is smaller and more intimate — but for the people in those seats, it's no less significant.

Who Is D11 Adult and Family Education For

The Adult and Family Education program serves Colorado Springs residents who didn't complete a traditional high school diploma, who came to the United States as adults and need English language skills, or who are looking to build foundational academic skills for employment and family life. Programs offered through the department include:

  • GED preparation and testing — helping adults earn their General Educational Development certificate
  • English as a Second Language (ESL) — for adults learning English as an additional language
  • Adult Basic Education — foundational math and literacy for adults seeking to improve core skills
  • Adult Diploma Program — a structured path to earning a high school diploma as an adult

Many participants are parents themselves, working to set an example for their own children and open doors that a diploma or English language certificate can provide.

Why This Matters for Colorado Springs Families

The Adult and Family Education program represents something the broader community doesn't always see in the graduation-season news cycle: the ongoing work of education that happens outside of K-12. Colorado Springs has a significant population of adult learners — immigrants and refugees building new lives, parents who left school early and are returning with clearer purpose, and workers seeking skills that open better opportunities.

When a parent earns a GED or a diploma, research consistently shows that their children are more likely to stay in school longer and pursue higher education. The investment in adult education ripples outward through families and into the next generation. Tonight's graduates are a reminder that this kind of community investment is happening all year, in classrooms across Colorado Springs, one student at a time.

What's Next

Adult and Family Education programs at D11 run year-round. If you or someone you know could benefit from GED preparation, ESL classes, or adult diploma programs in Colorado Springs, visit d11.org or contact D11 Adult and Family Education directly for enrollment information for the 2026-27 school year. Classes are typically offered at low or no cost to participants.
