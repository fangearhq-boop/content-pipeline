Get Outdoors Day Is June 6 at Memorial Park — Here's the Full Family Guide

By Sarah Morales | May 28, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs families, circle June 6 on your calendar. The annual Get Outdoors Day festival returns to Memorial Park next Saturday, and it's completely free — with a lineup of activities that makes it one of the best family events of the year in COS.

The festival runs from 9 AM to 2 PM at Prospect Lake in Memorial Park, organized by the Pikes Peak Outdoor Recreation Alliance. More than 3,000 people are expected to attend, and if you've never been, it's worth making the trip.

What's at Get Outdoors Day

The event is designed to introduce families to outdoor activities they might not have tried before. This year's lineup includes:

  • Archery range (instruction provided)
  • Paddleboarding on Prospect Lake
  • Fly fishing instruction
  • Climbing wall
  • "Touch a Truck" zone for younger kids
  • Dunk tank

Most activities are drop-in and staffed by volunteers and professionals who can walk your kids through what they need to know. No gear required — everything is provided on-site.

Free Fishing Gear for the First 650 Kids

This is the part that tends to make parents' eyes light up: Colorado Parks and Wildlife will give free fishing rods, bait, and basic instruction to the first 650 children ages 15 and under who show up. That's a complete setup your family gets to keep — no fishing experience required.

Getting there early makes sense if your kids are interested in fishing. The 650-rod giveaway goes fast, and arriving at or before 9 AM gives you the best shot at nabbing a setup before they're gone.

What to Bring

Since it's a full outdoor event in early June, here's what we'd suggest packing:

  • Sunscreen — the festival is fully outdoors from 9 AM to 2 PM
  • Water bottles for everyone
  • Comfortable shoes (there's a lot of walking and activity stations)
  • Layers — Colorado mornings in June can start cool before warming up
  • A bag or backpack if you want to carry the free fishing gear home

Parking at Memorial Park is available. The park is located at 1605 E. Pikes Peak Ave. in Colorado Springs. Arrive closer to 9 AM if the free fishing gear is important to your group.

What's Next

Get Outdoors Day is a great first stop on Colorado Springs' summer outdoor calendar. If your family catches the outdoor recreation bug, Colorado Parks and Wildlife offers affordable fishing licenses starting at $10 for youth under 16, and there are free fishing days in Colorado each year when no license is required. The PPLD Summer Adventure program, which kicks off June 1, also includes outdoor activity tracking and prizes for kids who spend time outside this summer. Visit ppld.org/summer for details.
