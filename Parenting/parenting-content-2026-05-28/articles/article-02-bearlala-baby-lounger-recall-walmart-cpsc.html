Bearlala Baby Lounger Recall: Stop Using Now, Here's How to Get a Refund

By Jamie Rivera | May 28, 2026 | COS Parenting

If you bought a Bearlala baby lounger from Walmart.com in the past year, stop using it immediately. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 300 units of the product — sold under the name "Granlass" by the company Nuoxuann — due to fall and entrapment hazards that pose a serious risk to infants.

The loungers were sold exclusively on Walmart.com between August and October 2025 for $35 to $45. They're light gray with a leaf pattern fabric cover. If that matches something in your nursery, here's what you need to know.

What's Wrong With the Product

According to the CPSC, the Bearlala loungers violate the mandatory federal safety standard for infant sleep products in three specific ways:

  • The sides of the lounger are too low to safely contain an infant, creating a fall risk.
  • The openings at the foot of the lounger are wider than permitted, meaning a baby's body could slip through and become entrapped.
  • The lounger has no stand, which means if it's placed on any elevated surface — a changing table, countertop, or couch — it poses a fall hazard for the infant inside.

No injuries have been reported, but the CPSC's standard is that hazards of this nature warrant immediate recall before injuries occur.

How to Get Your Refund

The CPSC and Nuoxuann have provided specific instructions for obtaining a full refund. You can't simply return the product — the company requires proof of destruction before issuing payment.

Here's what to do:

  1. Stop using the lounger immediately.
  2. Remove the foam padding and pads from the fabric cover.
  3. Cut the cover, foam, and pad in half.
  4. Take photos of the destroyed pieces.
  5. Email the photos to Recall-nuoxuann@outlook.com.
  6. Wait for refund instructions from Nuoxuann.

The destruction step is a standard CPSC process for recalled infant products — it ensures the item can't be resold or passed along to other families after the recall.

If You Still Have Questions

Email Nuoxuann directly at Recall-nuoxuann@outlook.com. If you'd like to report a concern or check the CPSC's official recall listing, visit cpsc.gov/Recalls and search for "Bearlala."

Please share this with anyone you know who shops Walmart.com for baby gear — 300 units is a small run, which means the people who bought these loungers may not see a recall notice through normal channels.

What's Next

Once you've completed the refund process, look for a replacement infant sleep product that meets current CPSC standards. When buying baby loungers or sleep products, check cpsc.gov for any recent recalls before purchasing. Products sold on third-party marketplace platforms (including Walmart.com, Amazon, and similar sites) are sometimes not subject to the same pre-sale safety review as products sold through traditional retail, so doing a quick recall check can catch issues early.
