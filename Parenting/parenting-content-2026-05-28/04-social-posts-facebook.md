### STORY 1: D11 Bond Referendum

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 8:00 AM MT

Here's something every D11 family will want to watch between now and November: Colorado Springs District 11 is weighing a $775 million bond referendum for the November ballot.

The money, if voters approve it, would fund air conditioning for aging school buildings — some of which are 50 to 100 years old and don't have A/C — along with a $10,000 pay increase for every teacher in the district, from first-year educators to 30-year veterans. The bond would also fund broader facility improvements across the district's aging building stock.

If the measure passes, the tax impact would be roughly $6.50 more per month for every $100,000 of a home's assessed value. That's about $32.50 per month for a home assessed at $500,000.

District officials haven't finalized the ballot language yet — the board is still exploring financing options. But with summer break starting and November voting just a few months away, now's the time to understand what's being proposed.

Would you support this bond measure? What would make the biggest difference in your family's D11 school experience?

### STORY 2: Bearlala Baby Lounger Recall

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 10:00 AM MT

Parents: if you bought a Bearlala baby lounger from Walmart.com in the past year, check your purchase history right now.

CPSC has recalled Bearlala-branded baby loungers sold under the name "Granlass" by the company Nuoxuann on Walmart.com. About 300 units were sold between August and October 2025 for $35 to $45. They're light gray with a leaf pattern.

The problem: these loungers violate the mandatory federal safety standard for infant sleep products. The sides are too low to contain an infant. The openings at the foot are wider than allowed, posing entrapment and fall hazards. And because the lounger has no stand, it poses a fall risk if placed on any elevated surface.

Stop using it immediately. To get a full refund, cut the foam padding and cover in half and email photos of the destroyed pieces to Recall-nuoxuann@outlook.com. The email will give you refund instructions.

If you have questions or need help, email Nuoxuann directly at that same address.

Pass this along to any parents you know who shop at Walmart for baby gear.

### STORY 3: Get Outdoors Day

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 12:00 PM MT

Mark your calendars for next Saturday: Get Outdoors Day is coming to Memorial Park in Colorado Springs on June 6, and it's completely free for families.

The annual festival runs from 9 AM to 2 PM at Prospect Lake in Memorial Park. It's organized by the Pikes Peak Outdoor Recreation Alliance, and more than 3,000 people are expected to show up. We can see why.

Here's what's on offer: archery, paddleboarding, fly fishing instruction, a climbing wall, a "Touch a Truck" zone, and a dunk tank. Colorado Parks and Wildlife will give out free fishing poles, bait, and basic instruction to the first 650 children ages 15 and under who attend. That's a free fishing setup, with no experience necessary.

It's the kind of day that introduces kids to outdoor activities they might never have tried otherwise — and it costs nothing. Bring sunscreen, water, and comfortable walking shoes. Parking at Memorial Park is available.

Have you been to Get Outdoors Day before? What was your kids' favorite part?

### STORY 4: D11 Adult Education Graduation

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 3:00 PM MT

Tonight in Colorado Springs, a different kind of graduation takes place.

Colorado Springs District 11's Adult and Family Education program holds its 2026 graduation ceremony at 7 PM at the Roy J. Wasson Academic Campus. The ceremony honors adults who finished their GED, English as a Second Language program, or adult diploma while juggling work, family, and all of the things that make going back to school harder the second (or third) time around.

For many of tonight's graduates, earning this credential means better job opportunities, the ability to help their own children with schoolwork, and the personal satisfaction of finishing something they started years ago. Many are parents themselves, showing their kids what it means to keep going.

It's a smaller ceremony than the high school graduations earlier this month — but for the people crossing that stage tonight, it's no less meaningful.

Congratulations to the D11 Adult and Family Education class of 2026.

### STORY 5: June Events Guide

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 5:00 PM MT

Summer is officially here — and Colorado Springs families have a packed June ahead. Here's a quick guide to the best family-friendly events to kick off the season.

**Rock Ledge Ranch (June 3-14, 10 AM-5 PM)**
Step back into Colorado history at this hands-on living history experience near Garden of the Gods. Make cornhusk dolls, watch blacksmith demonstrations, explore an 1870s homestead, and try Victorian-era lawn games. It's a great way to give kids a real sense of what life looked like here 150 years ago.

**El Pomar Family Exploration Day (June 6, 10 AM-2 PM, free)**
The Penrose Heritage Museum is hosting a free family day with interactive activities and exhibits. It's a short trip and free admission for the whole family.

**Get Outdoors Day (June 6, 9 AM-2 PM, Memorial Park, free)**
Colorado Springs' annual outdoor festival — archery, climbing, paddleboarding, and free fishing gear for kids. See our separate post for all the details.

**Peak Adrenaline (June 11-14, Pikes Peak International Raceway)**
Four days of motorsports, live music, and outdoor adventure on 600+ acres. Family-friendly energy with something for older kids and adults who like speed and adventure.

**PPLD Summer Adventure (starts June 1)**
It's not too late to sign up for the library's summer reading and activity program. Register at ppld.org/summer. The program runs through July 31.

What events are your family most excited about? Let us know what we might have missed.
