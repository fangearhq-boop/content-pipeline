CPSC Warning — HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil, Poisoning Risk to Young Children
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Parents, do a quick check of your essential oil cabinet today. The CPSC has issued a warning to immediately stop using HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil. The reason: the packaging is NOT child-resistant, and wintergreen oil contains methyl salicylate — a compound that is toxic to young children even in small amounts. The product was sold on Amazon from May 2022 through August 2025 for about $10. It comes in white and blue packaging with an amber bottle, screw cap, and dropper. "HiQiLi High Quality Life" and "Wintergreen Essential Oil" are printed on the labels. The CPSC's guidance is simple: stop using it, and secure it completely out of reach of children. If you bought this oil in the past few years and still have it in your home, take action now. And if a child ever gets into any essential oil — call Poison Control immediately at 1-800-222-1222 (free, 24/7). Do you keep essential oils at home? Have you ever thought about how they're stored around little ones? ### Image Post Caption Safety alert: CPSC says stop using HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil immediately. Non-child-resistant packaging. Sold on Amazon ~$10. Contains methyl salicylate — toxic to young kids. Secure out of reach of children. Questions? 1-800-222-1222.

Wintergreen Essential Oil Safety Pattern — What Every Parent Needs to Know
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post If you use essential oils at home — and plenty of us do — this is worth a few minutes of your time. The CPSC has taken action against three different wintergreen essential oil brands since 2025 (HiQiLi, GM Gumili, and Euqee) — all for the same reason: non-child-resistant packaging. Wintergreen oil looks and smells harmless. But it contains methyl salicylate, a compound that the CPSC specifically regulates because it's toxic to children even in small amounts. We're not saying your diffuser is a hazard. We're saying that the bottle matters, and where you store it matters. Here's a practical checklist for any family that keeps essential oils in the house: • Store essential oils in a locked cabinet or on a shelf completely out of a child's reach • Always replace caps immediately after using • Never leave open bottles unattended when kids are around • If you order essential oils online (especially from Amazon), check whether the product came in child-resistant packaging • If a child ingests any essential oil — call Poison Control immediately: 1-800-222-1222 (free, 24/7, available in Spanish and English) The more you know, the safer your home is. That's true for all of us. ### Image Post Caption The CPSC has flagged 3 wintergreen essential oil brands in 2025-2026 for non-child-resistant packaging. Wintergreen oil can be toxic to kids. Store in a locked cabinet. If ingested: call Poison Control 1-800-222-1222.
