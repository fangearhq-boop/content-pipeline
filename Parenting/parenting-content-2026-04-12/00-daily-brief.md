### STORY 2: CPSC Warning — HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil, Poisoning Risk to Young Children
- **Tier:** 2
- **Status:** NEW STORY
- **Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
- **Angle:** The CPSC has issued a warning (not a formal recall) urging parents to immediately stop using HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil. The oil contains methyl salicylate, which requires child-resistant packaging under federal law — but the product's packaging is NOT child-resistant, posing a serious poisoning risk if a young child accesses it. The product was sold on Amazon from May 2022 through August 2025 for about $10. Parents who have this product should secure it out of reach of children.

### STORY 5: Wintergreen Essential Oil Safety Pattern — What Every Parent Needs to Know
- **Tier:** 2
- **Status:** NEW STORY
- **Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety/Evergreen)
- **Angle:** Essential oils are everywhere in Colorado Springs households. Wintergreen oil in particular poses a specific danger to young children because it contains high concentrations of methyl salicylate — a compound toxic to children even in small amounts. The CPSC has acted against multiple wintergreen oil brands for non-child-resistant packaging (HiQiLi warning 2026, GM Gumili recall 2026). This article gives parents a practical checklist: what to look for, how to store essential oils safely, and what to do if a child ingests them.
