### STORY 1: D11 Orton Academy Moving Into Trailblazer Elementary

**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** Local News

**Why This Matters to COS Parents:**
Any time a school district changes who shares a school building, parents need to know. D11 families at Trailblazer felt left out of the loop — they found out from Orton's website first, not from the school. That communication failure is part of the story. At the same time, the underlying news is not necessarily negative: Trailblazer has space, Orton Academy serves kids with dyslexia, and the arrangement may benefit the community. COS Parenting can give D11 families a clear-headed summary of what is changing, what is not, and where to go for questions.

**Key angles:**
1. What is Orton Academy and why does it matter?
2. What does co-location mean for Trailblazer families?
3. How parents found out — the communication issue
4. Who to contact with questions (info session April 9 happened; district contact)

**Voice note:** Warm and organized. Present the facts without alarm; acknowledge parent concerns fairly.

### STORY 2: CPSC Warning — HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil

**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)

**Why This Matters to COS Parents:**
Essential oils are popular in Colorado Springs households. Wintergreen oil in particular is used in everything from headache remedies to DIY cleaning solutions. Parents may not know that the product sitting in a bathroom cabinet or on a kitchen counter could be life-threatening if a toddler reaches it. The CPSC warning is a direct call to action.

**Key angles:**
1. The specific product and CPSC action
2. Why wintergreen oil is more dangerous than other oils (methyl salicylate)
3. What to do if you have this product
4. General essential oil storage safety

### STORY 4: COS Trail Improvements Spring 2026

**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** Local News / Parks

**Why This Matters to COS Parents:**
Spring trail improvements mean better, safer walking and biking routes for COS families. Specifically: the Pikes Peak Greenway upgrade replaces old asphalt that can be bumpy and hazardous for strollers and bikes; the Rock Island Trail pedestrian bridge creates a key connection for families who explore that corridor. Both projects will cause some construction disruption in the near term but result in meaningfully better family trail access.

**Key angles:**
1. Pikes Peak Greenway: new concrete trail (~0.5 miles, Monument Creek area)
2. Rock Island Trail: 210-foot pedestrian bridge + flashing crossing at Peterson Rd
3. What families can expect during construction
4. The bigger picture: COS investment in trails and active transportation

### STORY 5: Wintergreen Essential Oil Safety Guide for Families

**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety/Evergreen)

**Why This Matters to COS Parents:**
The CPSC has acted against three different wintergreen oil brands in 2025-2026. Each time, the issue is the same: non-child-resistant packaging around an oil that is genuinely toxic to children. This article gives parents the full context — what methyl salicylate is, why it's dangerous, which products have been flagged, and a practical checklist for essential oil safety in the home.

**Key angles:**
1. The pattern of CPSC actions (HiQiLi, GM Gumili, Euqee) — not a one-off
2. Why wintergreen specifically is more dangerous than other essential oils
3. Poison Control hotline: 1-800-222-1222
4. Practical storage tips (locked cabinet, high shelf, caps on)

**Voice note:** Direct, warm, not alarmist. Use contractions. End with actionable guidance, not fear.
