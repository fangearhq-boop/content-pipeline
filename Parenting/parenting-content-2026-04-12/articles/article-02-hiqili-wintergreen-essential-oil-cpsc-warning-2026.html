CPSC Says Stop Using HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil — Here's Why It's a Risk to Young Children

By Marta Kim | April 12, 2026

The packaging is not child-resistant, and wintergreen oil contains a compound toxic to young children even in small amounts. Here's what to do if you have this product.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent warning to parents: stop using HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil immediately. The product's packaging does not meet the child-resistant requirements mandated by federal law, and the oil itself contains methyl salicylate — a compound that can be toxic to young children even in small quantities.

This is not a formal recall. The seller has not stepped forward with a remedy. The CPSC's guidance is direct: stop using this product, and keep it completely out of reach of children.

What Is HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil?

The product is a wintergreen essential oil sold online at Amazon.com from May 2022 through August 2025 for about $10. It comes in white and blue packaging and includes an amber bottle with a screw cap and dropper, along with a travel-size bottle with a dropper cap. The labels on the packaging and both bottles read "HiQiLi High Quality Life" and "Wintergreen Essential Oil."

If you purchased essential oils on Amazon in the past few years, it's worth checking your bottles against this description.

Why Wintergreen Oil Specifically?

Essential oils vary widely in their chemistry, and not all of them carry the same risks. Wintergreen oil is in a different category from, say, lavender or eucalyptus. It contains very high concentrations of methyl salicylate — the same compound found in products like muscle rub creams and some pain-relief topical products — but in essential oil form, the concentration is far higher.

Federal law under the Poison Prevention Packaging Act specifically requires that products containing methyl salicylate be sold in child-resistant packaging precisely because it is dangerous to children at low doses. When a product's packaging does not meet that standard, a young child can potentially access a lethal dose simply by opening a bottle that should have been harder to open.

The CPSC does not issue warnings like this one lightly. When the agency says "risk of serious injury or death," those words are precise and deliberate.

What to Do Right Now

If you have HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil in your home:

  • Stop using it immediately
  • Secure the product completely out of reach of children — ideally in a locked cabinet
  • Because there is no formal recall or remedy available from the seller, the safest option is to dispose of the product carefully, keeping it away from children throughout the process

If you think a child has ingested any essential oil — wintergreen or otherwise — call Poison Control immediately: 1-800-222-1222. This line is free, available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and can provide guidance in English and Spanish. Do not wait to see if symptoms develop before calling.

What's Next

The CPSC has been increasingly active about wintergreen essential oil products specifically — this is not the first brand to receive action, and it likely won't be the last.
