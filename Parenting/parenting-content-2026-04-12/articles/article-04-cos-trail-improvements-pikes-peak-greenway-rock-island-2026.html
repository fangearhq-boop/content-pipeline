Two Major COS Trail Upgrades Are Happening This Spring — Here's What Families Can Expect

By Marta Kim | April 12, 2026

A new concrete section on the Pikes Peak Greenway and a 210-foot pedestrian bridge on the Rock Island Trail are among Colorado Springs' biggest trail investments of 2026.

Colorado Springs families who use the city's trail network are about to see some meaningful improvements this year — and two of the most significant projects are underway right now.

The city's Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services Department is working on trail improvements as part of its 2026 Trails, Open Spaces, and Parks plan. Two projects in particular will directly affect how families experience popular Colorado Springs trail corridors: work on the Pikes Peak Greenway near Monument Creek, and a major new pedestrian bridge on the Rock Island Trail.

Pikes Peak Greenway: New Concrete Trail Near Monument Creek

Along the Pikes Peak Greenway corridor, the city is replacing deteriorating asphalt with approximately 0.5 miles of new concrete trail. The project is happening in partnership with Stormwater Engineering as part of ongoing improvements to the Monument Creek area.

For families, this matters in practical terms. Old asphalt on trail surfaces develops cracks, bumps, and uneven sections over time — the kind of surface that makes stroller rides rough, bicycle trips bumpy, and walking with young kids less enjoyable. New concrete trail is smoother, more durable, and better for everyone from toddlers in carriers to parents on e-bikes.

Construction is expected to proceed through spring 2026. If you use the Pikes Peak Greenway regularly, plan for potential trail detours near Monument Creek while work is underway.

Rock Island Trail: A 210-Foot Bridge Over Sand Creek

The bigger project — and the one that will change the trail network most significantly — is a 210-foot pedestrian bridge being constructed over Sand Creek on the Rock Island Trail. This bridge will connect the west and east sides of the trail, closing a gap in the network that has long been an obstacle for families and recreational users.

The project also includes a pedestrian-activated flashing crossing at Peterson Road, which will make that intersection safer and easier to navigate for trail users of all ages.

The Rock Island Trail bridge is anticipated to take approximately 12 months, with work beginning in spring 2026. That means the bridge is expected to be complete sometime in spring 2027. In the meantime, construction will be active in that corridor — stay aware of detours and posted trail closure signs when using the Rock Island Trail.

Why This Matters for COS Families

Colorado Springs has more than 200 miles of trails, and the city's investment in keeping them accessible and connected is something families benefit from every weekend. The Rock Island Trail is a particularly family-friendly route — relatively flat, with access points across the east and northeast parts of the city. The new bridge over Sand Creek will make that trail more continuous and less disjointed.

The Pikes Peak Greenway, meanwhile, is one of Colorado Springs' most central trail corridors and a key connection between neighborhoods and parks. Better surface conditions make it more accessible for everyone — including families with young children and people using adaptive mobility equipment.
