What COS Families Need to Know Before Visiting North Cheyenne Cañon This Spring

By Sofia Reyes | April 12, 2026

The Starsmore Visitor and Nature Center is under construction through May 22. Some parking is closed — but the park is open, and the upgrades will be worth it.

Spring in Colorado Springs means families heading back to North Cheyenne Cañon Park — and the Starsmore Visitor and Nature Center is one of the first stops on that route. Before you pack the kids into the car this weekend, here's what you need to know: construction is underway at Starsmore, and things look a little different right now.

The city began improvements at the Starsmore Visitor and Nature Center on March 9, 2026. Construction is tentatively scheduled to wrap up around May 22. The park itself is very much open, and so is the visitor center — but some access points and parking have changed temporarily.

What's Closed Right Now

Two things are closed during most of the construction period:

  • Parking Lot 1 ("Pull-Off 1") — the lot closest to the visitor center is closed
  • A section of S. Cheyenne Canyon Road adjacent to the facility is closed during most active construction hours

The visitor center itself remains open during construction, except during brief periods of hazardous work. Those closures will be posted on-site.

How to Get There and Where to Park

Parking Lot 2 ("Pull-Off 2") remains accessible via Evans Ave. If you're used to pulling into Lot 1, plan for the alternate route. The walk from Lot 2 to the visitor center is manageable with kids, though it may be slightly longer than your usual approach.

Tip: if you're going on a weekend morning when the park tends to be busiest, check the city's Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services page for any updates before you head out.

What's Being Built

Here's the good news: the construction will make Starsmore meaningfully better for families. When the project is complete, you'll find:

  • A new outdoor plaza and classroom space for programming and events
  • ADA-compliant restrooms — a real improvement over what's there now
  • Better bus parking for school group visits
  • New ADA-accessible walkways to the building entrances
  • A restored visitor center garden featuring native plants and interpretive signage

The project is funded through a partnership that includes the City, the Office of Accessibility, the Lodgers and Automobile Rental Tax fund, the Mary Starsmore Fund of the Pikes Peak Community Foundation, the Friends of Cheyenne Cañon, and the North Cheyenne Cañon Park Ambassadors Gift Trust. It's a genuine community investment in one of Colorado Springs' most visited family destinations.

What's Next

Construction is expected to wrap up around May 22, 2026 — just in time for the heart of Colorado Springs' spring outdoor season. The new plaza, accessible restrooms, and improved access will make Starsmore a better place for everyone: young kids, families with strollers, school groups, and anyone who's been wishing for a cleaner, more accessible visitor experience at one of COS's most beloved parks. Plan your visit accordingly, and enjoy the Cañon.
