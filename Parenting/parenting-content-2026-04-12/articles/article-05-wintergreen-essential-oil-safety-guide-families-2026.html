Wintergreen Essential Oil Safety: What Every Parent Needs to Know After a Wave of CPSC Actions

By Keisha Williams | April 12, 2026

The CPSC has flagged three separate wintergreen oil brands in 2025-2026. Here's why wintergreen is riskier than other essential oils — and how to store them safely around kids.

Essential oils have become a fixture in a lot of Colorado Springs homes — used in diffusers, for muscle relief, in DIY cleaning sprays, or just for scent. Most of the time, they sit on a shelf or bathroom counter without much thought.

But the Consumer Product Safety Commission has been focused on one specific type of essential oil lately: wintergreen. In 2025 and 2026, the CPSC took action against three separate wintergreen oil brands for the same reason — packaging that doesn't keep young children out. Those actions are a sign that this is a real and repeated problem, not a one-off incident.

Why Wintergreen Is Different

Not all essential oils are created equal when it comes to child safety. Many essential oils are irritating or mildly toxic in large quantities, but wintergreen essential oil belongs to a different category.

Wintergreen oil contains extremely high concentrations of methyl salicylate — a compound related to aspirin that can cause serious toxicity in children at very small doses. Federal law under the Poison Prevention Packaging Act specifically requires that products with more than a small percentage of methyl salicylate use child-resistant packaging. That requirement exists because the toxic dose for a young child is genuinely small — we're talking about amounts that could be ingested from an unsecured bottle in seconds.

This is why the CPSC takes wintergreen oil packaging violations seriously enough to issue public warnings and recalls, rather than simply sending a letter to the manufacturer.

The Three CPSC Actions in 2025-2026

Three wintergreen oil products have received CPSC attention in the past year and a half:

  • HiQiLi Wintergreen Essential Oil (2026 Warning): Sold on Amazon from May 2022 through August 2025 for about $10. White and blue packaging, amber bottle. CPSC issued a warning to stop using immediately — no formal recall or remedy is available from the seller.
  • GM Gumili Wintergreen Essential Oil Bottles (2026 Recall): Sold on Amazon by Gumili Beauty. Non-child-resistant packaging violated mandatory CPSC standards. Formal recall issued.
  • Euqee Wintergreen Essential Oils (2025 Recall): Sold exclusively on Amazon by DLWL-US. Recalled due to violation of the Poison Prevention Packaging Act.

Three brands, the same violation, within roughly 18 months. If you've purchased any wintergreen essential oil on Amazon recently, it's worth checking whether the packaging actually has a child-resistant cap — not just a screw cap that's easy to open.

A Practical Checklist for Essential Oil Safety at Home

The goal is not to stop using essential oils. The goal is to store them in a way that keeps young children safe. Here's a practical checklist:

  • Store all essential oils out of reach — ideally in a locked cabinet or on a shelf that children can't access, not on a bathroom counter or coffee table
  • Replace caps immediately after every use — open bottles left out are the most common path to accidental ingestion
  • Check your caps — if you have wintergreen oil and its cap opens with a simple twist (no squeeze-and-turn mechanism), it's not truly child-resistant
  • Don't leave bottles unattended when kids are around — even a few seconds is enough for a curious toddler
  • Be especially cautious with any oil containing methyl salicylate — wintergreen is the most common, but always check labels if you're unsure

And the most important number to have saved in your phone: 1-800-222-1222 — the Poison Control Hotline. It's free, available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, and available in English and Spanish. If a child ingests any essential oil, call Poison Control immediately — do not wait for symptoms to appear.

What's Next

The CPSC regularly updates its recall and warning database at cpsc.gov — worth bookmarking if you want to stay on top of product safety actions that affect families. As the essential oil market continues to grow and a significant portion of sales move through Amazon and other online marketplaces, the CPSC is watching packaging compliance more closely. If you have wintergreen essential oil at home and it came in non-child-resistant packaging, the safest action is to move it to a locked storage location today. Your Poison Control number is 1-800-222-1222.
