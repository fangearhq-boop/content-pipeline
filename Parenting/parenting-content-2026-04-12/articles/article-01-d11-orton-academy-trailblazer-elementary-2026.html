Orton Academy Is Moving Into Trailblazer Elementary — Here's What D11 Families Need to Know

By Sofia Reyes | April 12, 2026

Colorado Springs District 11 has announced a co-location plan for fall 2026. Some Trailblazer parents found out from a website — not from the district.

If you have a student at Trailblazer Elementary in Colorado Springs District 11, you may have heard the news already — possibly from a source you did not expect. D11 has announced that Orton Academy, a K-8 charter school specializing in dyslexia education, will move into Trailblazer Elementary starting with the 2026-27 school year.

The two schools will share the building but operate independently: separate staff, separate students, separate classrooms. Orton will have its own programming running within the same walls as Trailblazer's existing school community.

The announcement has prompted questions from families on both sides — and some frustration about how the news got out.

What Is Orton Academy?

Orton Academy is a K-8 public charter school focused exclusively on students with dyslexia. It opened in 2020 and entered an official charter contract with D11 in 2024, making it D11's ninth charter school. The school uses a specialized curriculum designed around how students with dyslexia learn best — an instructional approach that traditional school settings often cannot provide.

Orton Academy is the only D11-authorized school dedicated solely to dyslexia education. That makes it a meaningful resource for a significant portion of Colorado Springs families: dyslexia affects roughly 1 in 5 students, and access to schools specifically equipped to support those learners is limited.

Why Trailblazer?

Space is the practical answer. Trailblazer Elementary was built to accommodate approximately 600 students. Its current enrollment is around 220. That gap — nearly 380 unused seats — made it a natural candidate for co-location.

According to reporting from the Colorado Springs Gazette, recent capacity reviews and Orton Academy's own growth needs prompted conversations between Orton's leadership, the district's facilities and enrollment teams, and Trailblazer's area superintendent and school leader. The arrangement moves forward under the assumption that the schools can coexist without disrupting either community's day-to-day experience.

How Families Found Out — and Why That Matters

The part of this story that is generating the most concern among Trailblazer families is not what is changing, but how they learned about it. Several parents say they heard about the announcement through Orton Academy's own website before receiving official communication from D11. That sequence — hearing significant news about your child's school from another school's website — understandably felt off to many families.

The district did hold an information session for parents on April 9 at 6 PM at Trailblazer Elementary. If you missed it, contacting D11 directly is the best next step. The district's website and the Trailblazer school page are the official sources for updates on this plan.

What's Next

The co-location is planned for the 2026-27 school year starting in August. D11 will continue to communicate details about logistics, scheduling, and shared space arrangements as the fall approaches. If you're a Trailblazer family with concerns, reaching out to your school's parent advisory groups and the district's community engagement team is the best path to getting your questions answered.
