D11 Is Moving a Dyslexia Charter School Into Trailblazer Elementary — and Some Parents Have Questions

By Jenna Park | COS Parenting

April 22, 2026

Colorado Springs District 11 has approved a plan to bring Orton Academy — a K-8 charter school dedicated to educating students with dyslexia — into the Trailblazer Elementary building for the 2026-27 school year. The move is drawing mixed reactions from families at both schools, with some Trailblazer parents saying they weren't included in the conversation before the decision was made.

Here's what we know, and what it means for D11 families.

What Is Orton Academy?

Orton Academy was chartered in D11 in 2024, making it the only school in the district authorized specifically to serve students with dyslexia. It currently enrolls about 120 students in grades K-8. Orton uses evidence-based methods — including the Orton-Gillingham approach — that are specifically designed for learners who struggle with reading, spelling, and language processing.

For families of children with dyslexia, having a dedicated school with trained staff is significant. Many families in Colorado Springs have historically needed to seek private or out-of-district options. Orton Academy fills a genuine gap in D11's programming.

What Happens at Trailblazer?

Trailblazer Elementary is a place-based education school in Colorado Springs, currently serving around 220 students in grades pre-K through 5. The building was built to hold approximately 600 students, and the 2025-26 school year capacity report listed 337 open seats — meaning there is real physical room for Orton Academy's 120-student population.

Under the plan, both schools will operate separately within the building. Each will have its own staff, students, and programming in its own classrooms. This isn't a merger — it's a co-location arrangement, similar to models used in several other Colorado districts.

Why Are Some Parents Concerned?

Despite the logistical rationale, the response from some Trailblazer families has been one of surprise. Several parents said they felt blindsided by the announcement, and questions have emerged about shared spaces — cafeterias, gyms, playgrounds — and how transitions between classrooms will work when children of different ages are in the same building.

One concern is the age range involved. Orton Academy serves grades K-8, meaning some of its students will be in middle school. Trailblazer's population is pre-K through 5th grade. Parents have raised questions about how those different developmental stages will coexist in shared spaces throughout the school day.

What's Next

D11 has not yet announced formal community meetings on the Orton Academy co-location plan, but parents at Trailblazer are encouraged to reach out to their school's principal and to the D11 Board of Education directly with questions. The move is expected to go into effect at the start of the 2026-27 school year, so families have the spring and summer to learn more about the logistics. We'll continue to follow this story as more details emerge. You can reach D11 at d11.org.
