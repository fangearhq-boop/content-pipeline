Bear Creek Nature Center Is Hosting a Homeschool Day This Thursday — Here's How to Register

By Marta Kim | COS Parenting

April 22, 2026

Homeschool families in Colorado Springs: tomorrow's forecast is looking good, and Bear Creek Nature Center has a perfect reason to get outside. El Paso County Parks and Recreation is hosting a homeschool nature program on Thursday, April 23, from 1:00 to 3:30 pm — a hands-on afternoon designed for kids and families who want to connect with the natural world right here in COS.

It's $4 per person, and prepaid registration is required.

What to Expect

Bear Creek Nature Center runs two programs simultaneously during its homeschool sessions, designed for different age groups. Younger children can participate with a caregiver present, while kids ages 8 and older have the option to be dropped off. This makes it flexible for families with kids at different stages — you can bring younger siblings along while your older learner gets a more independent experience.

Programs at Bear Creek typically involve nature exploration, wildlife identification, habitat study, and hands-on activities that connect to natural science and local ecology. The center sits within Bear Creek Regional Park in Colorado Springs, surrounded by natural habitat that makes every session a genuine outdoor learning experience.

About Bear Creek Nature Center

Bear Creek Nature Center is operated by El Paso County Parks and Recreation and offers year-round programming for families, school groups, and homeschool communities. It's part of the larger Bear Creek Regional Park, which spans more than 500 acres in Colorado Springs with trails, open meadows, and wildlife habitat. The nature center itself offers interpretive exhibits, educational programs, and guided hikes tailored to different age groups and learning objectives.

For homeschool families in Colorado Springs, the center's Children's Nature Series — of which this Thursday's program is a part — runs regularly through the school year. It's a low-cost, high-quality way to supplement nature science curriculum with real-world, local experience.

What's Next

To register for Thursday's program, call Bear Creek Nature Center at 719-520-6387 today — spots fill up, and registration closes before the day of the event. If you miss this session, check the El Paso County Parks and Recreation website at parks.elpasoco.com for upcoming homeschool and family programming throughout spring and summer. Bear Creek also offers summer nature camps for kids — worth bookmarking for your warm-weather planning.
