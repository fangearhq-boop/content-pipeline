The Cañon City Blossom Festival Is This Week — A Family Day Trip Worth Taking From Colorado Springs

By Marcus Rivera | COS Parenting

April 22, 2026

If you're looking for a spring outing that gets your family out of the house and into something genuinely festive — without a four-hour drive — the Cañon City Royal Gorge Blossom Festival is worth adding to your calendar. Running April 29 through May 3, the festival is about 45 miles from Colorado Springs. That's roughly an hour's drive south on Highway 115 or Highway 50, making it an easy day trip even with younger kids in tow.

What Is the Blossom Festival?

The Royal Gorge Blossom Festival is Cañon City's largest annual community event. It celebrates the arrival of spring in the Royal Gorge region, and it's been a community tradition for decades. The festival includes live music across multiple stages, a rodeo, a 5K run, food vendors, arts and crafts, and a range of family-friendly activities.

Late April and early May is when the orchards in the Arkansas River valley are in bloom — the landscape around Cañon City becomes genuinely beautiful this time of year, which adds to the appeal for a family day out.

What to Know Before You Go

The festival runs Wednesday, April 29 through Sunday, May 3, with different events on different days. If you're planning to go, here are a few practical notes:

  • Check the official schedule at the Royal Gorge Blossom Festival website to see which events fall on which days — the rodeo, 5K, and concerts typically have set day and time assignments.
  • Parking in downtown Cañon City during the festival can be limited. Plan to arrive early or look for overflow parking areas.
  • The weather in late April at that elevation can swing — layers are always a good call for a Colorado outing with kids.
  • There's no single admission gate for the festival itself; individual events may have their own ticket prices. Food and vendor areas are generally free to walk through.

Why It's Worth the Drive

Colorado Springs has plenty of great spring activities — Garden of the Gods, Bear Creek, Memorial Park — but there's something special about making a day of it and going somewhere your family doesn't visit every week. Cañon City has a different feel from Colorado Springs: smaller, slower, with the dramatic Royal Gorge backdrop setting the scene.

For families with older kids, the rodeo is a genuinely memorable experience. For families with younger ones, the food, music, and walking-around atmosphere is enough to make for a full afternoon. And for everyone, the drive through the foothills in bloom is a reminder of why we live in Colorado.

What's Next

Check the Royal Gorge Blossom Festival official site or the Visit Cañon City tourism page for the full schedule, parking maps, and event ticket information before you head out.
