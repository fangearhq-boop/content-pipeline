CPSC Recall: Bellabu Bear Bamboo Sherpa Children's Robes Pose Burn Hazard

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting

April 22, 2026

If your child has a Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa robe hanging in their closet — especially one with PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Garfield, or Batman prints — check the label today. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) has officially recalled these children's robes for failing mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear, posing a burn risk to children.

Here's everything Colorado Springs families need to know to act quickly.

What's Being Recalled

The recall covers Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa children's robes. The robes feature a sherpa lining, two front pockets, and a matching belt. They were sold in children's sizes S/M and M/L.

The prints included in this recall are: PAW Patrol, Garfield, Minecraft, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora, Nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons.

To confirm whether your child's robe is included in the recall, check the neck label. It should read "Bellabu Bear," "BB-NW12109," and the size.

Where They Were Sold and When

These robes were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue stores and boutiques nationwide, as well as online at the following websites: bellabubear.com, letthembelittleboutique.com, and saksfifthavenue.com. Sales took place from approximately January 2024 through July 2025, at a retail price of about $60. Many of these were purchased as gifts for birthdays or holidays — so it's worth checking with grandparents or relatives who may have bought one.

What to Do Right Now

The CPSC's guidance is clear: stop using the recalled robe immediately and take it away from your child. Then contact Bellabu Bear to request a full refund or store credit for the original purchase price.

Why Flammability Standards Matter for Kids' Sleepwear

Federal law requires children's sleepwear — including robes, which are worn in the home before or after sleep — to meet strict flammability standards. These rules exist because children's pajamas and robes are often worn near open flames, candles, fireplaces, or stoves. When a garment fails to meet the standard, the risk of a burn injury in the event of any flame contact increases significantly.

The CPSC enforces these standards strictly. If a product fails compliance testing, a recall is required regardless of whether any injuries have been reported.

What's Next

If you've already returned or discarded the robe, keep an eye on your email for communications from Bellabu Bear or any retailer where you purchased it. For the most up-to-date recall information, visit the official CPSC recall page at cpsc.gov/Recalls. We recommend checking that page periodically — recalls are issued regularly, and knowing what's on the list is one of the simplest ways to keep families safe.
