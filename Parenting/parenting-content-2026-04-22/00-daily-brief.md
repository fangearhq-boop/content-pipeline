## Today's Top Stories

### STORY 1: D11 Parents Push Back on Orton Academy Move Into Trailblazer Elementary
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** Local News | **Platforms:** X, FB, Article
Colorado Springs District 11 has announced that Orton Academy — a K-8 charter school specializing in dyslexia education with 120 students — will move into Trailblazer Elementary School for the 2026-27 school year. Trailblazer currently enrolls about 220 students in a building built for 600. Some Trailblazer parents say they felt blindsided by the decision, raising concerns about mixing K-5 and 2nd-8th grade students. It's D11's first charter authorized solely for students with dyslexia.

### STORY 2: CPSC Recalls Bellabu Bear Children's Robes — Burn Hazard
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety) | **Platforms:** X, FB, Article
The CPSC has recalled Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa children's robes for failing to meet mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear, posing a burn risk. The robes were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue and boutiques nationwide and online from January 2024 through July 2025, for about $60. Prints include PAW Patrol, Minecraft, and Garfield. Action: stop use immediately, contact Bellabu Bear at 888-703-7752 or help@bellabubear.com for a full refund.

### STORY 3: AAP Overhauls Screen Time Rules — No More Strict Hour Limits for Kids 6+
**Tier:** 2 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Platforms:** X, FB, Article
The American Academy of Pediatrics published a major policy update in early 2026 shifting away from strict time limits. For kids 6 and older, the focus is now on quality and context rather than hours. Key rules remain: no screens for babies under 18 months (except video chatting); max 1 hour of high-quality content per day for ages 2-5. For older kids: ask if content is age-appropriate, interactive vs. passive, and whether screens are replacing sleep, play, or family time.

### STORY 5: Cañon City Blossom Festival — A Family Day Trip Worth the Drive
**Tier:** 2 | **Pillar:** Local Events | **Platforms:** X, FB, Article
The Cañon City Royal Gorge Blossom Festival runs April 29–May 3, about 45 miles south of Colorado Springs. It's the city's largest annual festival, featuring live music, a rodeo, 5K run, and family activities. It's a great spring day trip for COS families looking to celebrate the season without a long drive.
