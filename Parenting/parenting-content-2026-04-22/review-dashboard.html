D11 Parents Push Back on Orton Academy Move Into Trailblazer Elementary
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post If your child attends Trailblazer Elementary in D11, you may already have heard: the school board approved a plan to bring Orton Academy — a K-8 charter school focused on dyslexia education — into the Trailblazer building starting next fall. Here's what we know so far: Orton Academy was chartered in D11 in 2024. It's the only D11-authorized school dedicated solely to serving students with dyslexia, and it currently enrolls about 120 students in grades K-8. Trailblazer Elementary currently has about 220 students in a building built to hold 600, which means there's real physical capacity. The 2025-26 capacity report listed 337 open seats. But for some Trailblazer families, the process felt abrupt. Some parents said they weren't consulted before the decision was made, and there are questions about what it means to have a K-8 population in a school alongside pre-K through 5th graders. D11 will have both schools operating with their own staff, students, and programming in separate classrooms — not a merge. But concerns remain about shared spaces, transitions, and community. COS families with kids in D11: what are your thoughts on this? Do you see it as a smart use of space, or does the district need to do more community outreach before decisions like this? --- #### Image Caption Post (10:00 AM MT) D11 is moving Orton Academy — a K-8 dyslexia-focused charter school — into Trailblazer Elementary next fall. CPSC Recalls Bellabu Bear Children's Robes — Burn Hazard
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Time to check your kids' closets. The CPSC has officially recalled Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa children's robes because they fail mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear — meaning they pose a burn risk. Here's what to look for: bamboo sherpa robes with a sherpa lining, two front pockets, and a matching belt. The prints include PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Garfield, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora, Nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons. The robes were sold in children's sizes S/M and M/L, and you can check the neck label for "Bellabu Bear" and the style number "BB-NW12109." They were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue stores and boutiques nationwide, plus online at bellabubear.com, letthembelittleboutique.com, and saksfifthavenue.com from January 2024 through July 2025 for about $60. If you have one of these robes, stop using it right away and take it away from your child. You can reach Bellabu Bear for a full refund (or store credit for the original purchase price) in a few ways: - Call 888-703-7752, Monday-Friday, 9 am-4 pm PT - Email help@bellabubear.com - Visit bellabubear.com These recalls aren't always easy to keep up with, but this one's worth sharing — especially if you have friends or family who received one of these as a gift. --- #### Image Caption Post (12:00 PM MT) CPSC recall: Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa kids' robes. Burn hazard — they fail mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. Prints include PAW Patrol, Minecraft, and Garfield. Sold Jan 2024–Jul 2025 at Saks Fifth Avenue and online for ~$60. Stop use. Full refund: 888-703-7752 or help@bellabubear.com ---

AAP Overhauls Screen Time Rules — No More Strict Hour Limits for Kids 6+
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The American Academy of Pediatrics just overhauled its screen time guidance — and if you've been feeling guilty about your kids' screen habits, this update might shift how you think about it. The big change: for kids ages 6 and older, the AAP is no longer recommending a strict hour limit. Instead of "no more than 2 hours a day," they want parents to ask better questions: Is the content high quality and age-appropriate? Is it interactive or mostly passive? Is screen time displacing sleep, physical activity, free play, or family time? Here's the age-by-age breakdown under the new framework: Under 18 months: Still no screens, with one exception — video chatting with family is fine. Ages 18-24 months: Educational programming only, and watch together with your child. Ages 2-5: Limit to 1 hour per day of high-quality content. Co-view when you can. Ages 6 and up: No set time limit. Focus on what they're watching and what it's replacing. For all ages, the AAP recommends keeping screens out of bedrooms and off the table during meals. It's the same common-sense thinking we've all heard before — but grounded in a new research framework. What's changed most since 2016: screens are everywhere now, and the old "2-hour rule" was mostly focused on TV. Today, kids are on phones, tablets, gaming consoles, and yes, doing homework online. A blanket time limit doesn't capture that complexity anymore. COS families: how do you manage screen time in your house? --- #### Image Caption Post (5:00 PM MT) The AAP updated its screen time guidelines in 2026. For kids 6 and up? No more strict hour limit. Cañon City Blossom Festival — A Family Day Trip Worth the Drive
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Looking for a spring day trip with the family? The Cañon City Royal Gorge Blossom Festival is worth putting on your calendar. Running April 29 through May 3, the festival is about 45 miles from Colorado Springs — roughly an hour's drive down Hwy 115 or Hwy 50. It's Cañon City's largest annual event, and it's genuinely family-friendly: live music, a rodeo, a 5K run, and community activities spread across several days. Cañon City is beautiful in late April and early May — the Royal Gorge backdrop and the blooming orchards make for a great spring outing. Whether you go for just an afternoon or make it a day trip, there's enough to keep kids (and adults) entertained without the scale of a major theme park or tourist destination. For schedule details and ticketing, check the Royal Gorge Blossom Festival website or search "Cañon City Blossom Festival 2026." Events span the full five days, so you can pick the day that works best for your family. Anyone done the Blossom Festival before?
