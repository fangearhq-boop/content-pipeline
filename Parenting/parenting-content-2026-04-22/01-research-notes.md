### STORY 3: AAP Screen Time Guidelines 2026

**Sources:**
- AAP Digital Ecosystems policy statement (Pediatrics journal, early 2026)
- EdSurge — "New AAP 'Screen Time' Recommendations Focus Less on Screens, More on Family Time" (February 5, 2026)
- CHOC Health Hub — "Updated AAP recommendations for screen time: What parents need to know"
- CHLA Blog — "Pediatrician-Approved Screen Time Guidelines for Kids at Every Age"

**Verified Facts (HIGH confidence):**
- AAP published new "Digital Ecosystems, Children, and Adolescents" policy statement in early 2026 [HIGH — Pediatrics journal]
- Under 18 months: no screen time (exception: video chatting with family) [HIGH — multiple sources]
- Ages 18-24 months: educational programming with caregiver only [HIGH — AAP policy]
- Ages 2-5: maximum 1 hour of high-quality content daily [HIGH — AAP policy]
- Ages 6+: no set time limit; focus on quality, context, and displacement [HIGH — multiple sources]
- Key questions for older kids: Is content high-quality and age-appropriate? Is it interactive or passive? Is it replacing sleep, physical activity, family time, or free play? [HIGH — AAP framework]
- No screens in bedrooms or during meals (device-free zones recommended) [HIGH — AAP]
- Major shift from 2016: previously recommended max 2 hours/day for ages 6+; now quality-focused [HIGH — EdSurge]
