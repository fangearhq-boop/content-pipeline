The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued a recall of Bellabu Bear children's bamboo sherpa robes because they fail to meet mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear, posing a burn hazard to children. Colorado Springs parents who purchased one of these robes should stop using it immediately and contact the company for a refund.

The recalled robes were sold in a wide range of popular character prints — including PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Garfield, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora, and Nutcracker, as well as seasonal and decorative prints including hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons. They come in children's sizes S/M and M/L, feature sherpa lining, two front pockets, and a matching belt.

How to Identify the Recalled Robes

Check your child's robe for a neck label that reads "Bellabu Bear" and "BB-NW12109," along with the size. If that label is present, the robe is part of the recall regardless of the print or color.

The robes were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue stores and boutiques nationwide, and online at bellabubear.com, letthembelittleboutique.com, saksfifthavenue.com, and other online retailers. Sales occurred from January 2024 through July 2025, with a retail price of approximately $60.

Why Flammability Standards Matter for Children's Robes

Federal law sets mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear because young children are at higher risk of burn injuries — they may not respond quickly enough to flames or heat, and they are more likely to wear sleepwear near open flames such as birthday candles, fireplaces, or kitchen stoves. A robe worn in the morning or evening around the house falls into this category under federal safety standards.

A garment that fails these standards is not inherently guaranteed to catch fire — but it does not provide the required resistance to ignition that the law mandates. That gap in protection is what the recall addresses.

What to Do Right Now

If you own a recalled Bellabu Bear robe, do the following:

Stop using the robe immediately. Take it away from your child. Do not donate it or pass it on to another family.

Contact Bellabu Bear to receive a full refund or store credit for the original purchase price. You can reach the company by calling 888-703-7752, Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 4 PM Pacific time. You can also email help@bellabubear.com. Or go to bellabubear.com and click the "Recall" link at the bottom of the page for online submission.

The CPSC also maintains a recall database at cpsc.gov where you can search for this and other product safety notices.
