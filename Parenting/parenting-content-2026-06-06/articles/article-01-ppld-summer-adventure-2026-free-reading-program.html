Summer in Colorado Springs means longer days, mountain sunsets, and the annual return of one of the best free programs the Pikes Peak Library District offers: Summer Adventure. The 2026 edition launched June 1 and runs through July 31, giving Colorado Springs families the entire summer to read, explore, and earn prize entries at no cost.

The program is open to all ages — babies, toddlers, school-age children, teens, and adults are all welcome. If your household has more than one age group, everyone can participate, and there is no single right way to do it.

How Summer Adventure Works

Participants complete 30 days of reading and activities over the course of the summer. Activities are flexible and designed to fit real life — a day counts if you read a book, listen to an audiobook, attend a library program, make something creative, try a new outdoor activity, or use one of PPLD's provided activity idea guides. The emphasis is on engagement with reading and learning, not a rigid format.

Progress is tracked through the Beanstack app, which families can use on a phone, tablet, or computer. The app logs completed activities and automatically generates prize drawing entries as milestones are reached. For families without reliable internet access, in-person tracking is available at any library branch.

What You Can Win

The prizes for Summer Adventure 2026 are genuinely worth the sign-up. The grand prize is a Pikes Peak Region Attractions VIP Access Pass, which provides free and significantly discounted admission to attractions throughout the Pikes Peak region — along with a one-night stay for two at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp. That combination alone makes this one of the most valuable free programs running in Colorado Springs this summer.

Beyond the grand prize, there are additional prize drawings offering family-friendly experiences, memberships, passes, and gift cards from local Colorado Springs businesses and attractions. Every completed activity earns more drawing entries.

Where to Sign Up

Colorado Springs families can join Summer Adventure online at ppld.org/summer, or in person at any of PPLD's 14 library locations throughout the Pikes Peak region. The Pikes Peak Library District also operates two bookmobile units that serve neighborhoods and areas farther from branch locations — both bookmobiles are valid sign-up points for the program.

There is no registration deadline. You can join now and still complete 30 days of activities before July 31. The program is presented by the Friends of the Pikes Peak Library District and requires no proof of income, library card, or prior participation.

Why It Matters This Summer

Research consistently shows that children who keep reading during the summer months retain more of what they learned during the school year. For families in Colorado Springs looking for a structured way to keep learning happening without the pressure of a formal program, Summer Adventure fits naturally into the rhythm of summer — a few minutes of reading here, a library visit there, an outdoor activity counted toward a goal.

For adults, the program is a reminder that summer is not just about keeping the kids busy. Participating alongside children models the value of reading and learning as lifelong habits.

What's Next
