District 49 families in Colorado Springs have less than two weeks left to use the district's free summer meals program. D49 is offering free breakfast and lunch for all children ages 1 through 18 through June 19 — no paperwork, no registration, and no proof of income required.

This is a federally funded summer meal program, which means the meals are available to any child who shows up, regardless of where they go to school or whether they live within the D49 boundaries. If your child is between 1 and 18 and is hungry, the program is available to them.

Where and When to Go

Springs Ranch Elementary School is one confirmed D49 meal site. For a complete list of locations, hours, and any schedule updates, visit d49.org. Meal service locations and schedules can vary, so checking the district's website before your first visit is the best way to confirm what's available nearest to you.

The program runs through June 19 — that is 13 days from today. There is no catch-up option after it ends.

How This Differs from D11's Program

Colorado Springs School District 11 also runs a summer meals program, but D11's program runs through July 31 with multiple site locations across the city including the Boys and Girls Club on South Chelton and Fountain Middle School. D49's program has a significantly earlier end date of June 19.

If your family primarily engages with D49 schools, this is the program most directly relevant to you — and the June 19 cutoff makes the timing urgent. If you are in a D11 area, the July 31 program gives more time, but D49 families should not assume the same timeline applies to their district's offering.

Why Summer Meals Programs Exist

The federal Summer Food Service Program exists because food insecurity does not take a school-year break. For many children in Colorado Springs and across the country, the meals provided at school are a significant source of daily nutrition. When school ends, that access ends — unless communities and districts step in with summer alternatives.

D49's decision to run the program through June 19 ensures that families have a bridge through the early weeks of summer. It is worth noting that participating carries no stigma and requires no justification. The program exists to be used, and any family with children ages 1 to 18 is welcome.
