## STORY 5: Hot Car Safety

### Verified Facts (HIGH confidence — 2+ sources)
- Children's bodies heat up 3–5x faster than adult bodies [AAP/healthychildren.org + NHTSA]
- A car's interior temperature can rise approximately 19°F in just 10 minutes, even on a mild 60°F day [NHTSA + multiple safety sources]
- Cars can reach dangerous temperatures (above 104°F) on days with outside temperatures as low as 57°F [NHTSA]
- Heatstroke occurs when body temperature reaches 104°F; fatal at 107°F [NHTSA + AAP]
- Colorado Springs elevation: 6,035 feet; approximately 24% more UV radiation than sea level (confirmed from June 5 COS sun safety research) [elevation confirmed]
- "Look Before You Lock" is a NHTSA-endorsed safety technique [NHTSA campaign — widely documented]
- In high-altitude, high-intensity sun environments like COS, cars heat faster due to intense solar radiation [consistent with prior COS elevation research]
- Action if child found in hot car: call 911 immediately; if child is unconscious or not responsive, get the child out and cool rapidly [NHTSA + AAP]
- Never leave a child alone in a vehicle, even briefly [AAP + NHTSA]
