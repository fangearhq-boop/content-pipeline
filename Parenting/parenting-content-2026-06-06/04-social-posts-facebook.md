### STORY 1: PPLD Summer Adventure 2026

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

If your family hasn't signed up for PPLD's Summer Adventure yet, here's your reminder.

The Pikes Peak Library District launched its annual Summer Adventure program on June 1, and it runs through July 31. It's free, it's open to all ages — babies, kids, teens, and adults — and it's one of the most genuinely accessible summer programs Colorado Springs offers.

Here's how it works: participants complete 30 days of reading and activities over the summer. That can look like reading a book, listening to an audiobook, attending a library program, making something creative, trying a new outdoor activity, or using one of PPLD's own activity idea guides. You track everything on the Beanstack app and earn entries into prize drawings.

The prizes are worth signing up for on their own: the grand prize includes a Pikes Peak Region Attractions VIP Access Pass and a one-night stay for two at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp. Other drawings include family-friendly experiences, memberships, passes, and gift cards from local businesses.

You can sign up online at ppld.org/summer, at any of PPLD's 14 library branches, or through one of the district's two bookmobiles.

### STORY 2: Bellabu Bear Children's Robes Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

If you purchased a Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa robe for your child, stop using it.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued a recall of Bellabu Bear children's robes because they fail to meet mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear — meaning they pose a burn risk to children.

The recalled robes were sold in popular character prints: PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Garfield, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora, Nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons. They have sherpa lining, two front pockets, and a matching belt. Check your child's robe for the label "Bellabu Bear" and "BB-NW12109."

The robes were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue, boutiques, and online at bellabubear.com and other retailers from January 2024 through July 2025, for about $60.

What to do: Stop use immediately. Take the robe away from your child. Contact Bellabu Bear to request a full refund or store credit for the original purchase price.

How to contact: Call 888-703-7752, Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 4 PM Pacific. Email help@bellabubear.com. Or go to bellabubear.com and click "Recall" at the bottom of the page.

### STORY 3: D49 Free Summer Meals Ending June 19

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

Colorado Springs families in the D49 area have less than two weeks to take advantage of the district's free summer meals program.

District 49 is offering free breakfast and lunch to all children ages 1 through 18 through June 19 — with no registration, no ID, and no proof of income required. These are federally funded summer meals, which means any child in that age range can walk in and eat. It doesn't matter which school they attend or whether they're in the district at all.

One confirmed location is Springs Ranch Elementary School. Visit d49.org for a full list of sites, hours, and any schedule updates.

This program ends June 19 — that's less than two weeks away. If your family has been meaning to check it out, now is the time.

### STORY 5: Hot Car Safety Reminder

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM MT

With Colorado Springs temperatures climbing and the summer sun intensifying, this is a good week to go over hot car safety.

A car's interior can heat up approximately 19 degrees in just 10 minutes — even on a mild day. On a hot June afternoon in Colorado Springs, with our elevation adding intensity to the sun, a vehicle's interior can reach temperatures above 120°F. Children's bodies heat up three to five times faster than adult bodies, which means what feels uncomfortable to a grown adult can be life-threatening for a child very quickly.

A few things every parent and caregiver in Colorado Springs should know:

Never leave a child in a vehicle unattended, even for a brief errand. It takes less time than most people think for the interior temperature to become dangerous.

Use the "Look Before You Lock" technique: before leaving your car, always check the back seat. Make it a habit, every single time.

If you see a child alone in a hot car: call 911 immediately. If the child appears unresponsive or in distress, try to get them out and begin cooling — wet cloths, air conditioning — while waiting for emergency services.

Heat-related illness in children can progress rapidly. Heatstroke begins at 104°F and becomes fatal at 107°F.

At 6,035 feet, Colorado Springs gets significantly more intense sun than lower elevations. Our cars heat fast. Keep this in the back of your mind every time you park.
