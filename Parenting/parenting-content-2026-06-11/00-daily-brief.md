### STORY 2: Flanagan Park Getting $750K Federal Makeover — New Playground, ADA Trail, Basketball Courts
- **Tier:** 1
- **Content Pillar:** Parks Updates / Local News
- **Type:** NEW STORY
- **Angle:** A central Colorado Springs neighborhood park near Nevada Avenue and Fillmore Street is getting a major upgrade this summer. The City of Colorado Springs received a $750,000 federal Community Development Block Grant to rehabilitate Flanagan Park. Planned improvements include a brand-new playground structure, a new ADA-accessible walking trail with lighting, a new basketball court, court resurfacing, and screen fencing. The park, built in the mid-1970s, serves a multigenerational neighborhood including families with young children, older adults, and nearby assisted-living residents. City crews will begin work later this summer, with the project expected to be complete by January 2027. The improved park will be more accessible and safer for families year-round.
- **Sources:** kktv.com (May 8, 2026), koaa.com, coloradosprings.gov/FlanaganPark, fox21news.com

### STORY 3: Recall Alert — Wiifo Toddler Tower Stools: 9,700 Units Can Collapse and Entrap Children
- **Tier:** 1
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
- **Type:** NEW STORY
- **Angle:** The CPSC recalled approximately 9,700 Wiifo-brand Children's Tower Stools (model LT005) on April 23, 2026 due to entrapment and fall hazards. The stools can collapse or tip over while in use, and a child's torso can fit through the side openings — creating a risk of serious injury or death. The firm reported 22 incidents of stools collapsing, resulting in 6 injuries (contusions and scrapes). The stools were sold on Amazon from June 2022 through March 2026 for about $60. They come in white, natural, and light wood finishes with "Model LT005" on the underside label. Remedy: full refund. Stop using immediately, disassemble, take photo, send to support@wiifo.net. IMPORTANT NOTE: Families who own a Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower (recalled May 14, 2026, covered June 10) should also check that recall — these are different products but both involve child step towers.
- **Sources:** CPSC.gov recall notice (April 23, 2026), Consumer Reports, WJLA, 11alive.com
