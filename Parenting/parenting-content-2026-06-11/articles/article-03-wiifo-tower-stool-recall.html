What's Being Recalled and Why

The Consumer Product Safety Commission recalled approximately 9,700 Wiifo-brand Children's Tower Stools on April 23, 2026. The recalled product — Model LT005 — is a toddler step tower, the type of freestanding stool designed to bring young children up to counter height in a kitchen or bathroom.

The hazards are serious. The stools can collapse or tip over while a child is standing on the platform. More alarmingly, the side openings of the structure are large enough that a child's torso can fit through — creating a risk of entrapment if the stool tips and the child's body gets caught in the frame.

The CPSC documented 22 reports of stools collapsing, resulting in six injuries. Those injuries included contusions and scrapes. Given how these stools are used — toddlers at counter height in a kitchen or bathroom — the injury potential from a collapse is significant.

Is Your Stool Part of This Recall?

To identify whether your stool is part of this recall, check the underside of the stool for a label reading "Model LT005." The recalled stools come in three finishes: white, natural, and light wood.

These stools were sold exclusively on Amazon between June 2022 and March 2026 for approximately $60. If you purchased a Wiifo tower stool on Amazon during that period, check the model number immediately.

The stools are not sold through major brick-and-mortar retailers, so the recall is specific to Amazon purchases. That said, the Amazon marketplace means that individual sellers may have stocked or resold these stools — check your order history if you're unsure.

What to Do Right Now

If you have a Wiifo Tower Stool (Model LT005) in your home, stop using it immediately. Do not wait until you've confirmed whether the stool shows any visible damage. The recall covers all units of Model LT005 regardless of condition.

The steps to claim your remedy:

  1. Stop using the stool.
  2. Disassemble it to prevent accidental use.
  3. Take a photo of the disassembled stool.
  4. Email that photo to support@wiifo.net.
  5. The company will process a full refund.

The remedy is a full refund — not a repair kit, not a partial refund. Families who paid around $60 for this product will receive their money back.

A Note About Similar Products

COS Parenting covered a separate recall on June 10: the Guidecraft Classic and Contemporary Kitchen Helper Tower (CPSC Recall #26490, issued May 14, 2026), which affected approximately 25,235 units sold at Amazon, Wayfair, Walmart, Target, and Guidecraft.com. That recall involves a different hazard — the platform can loosen and detach over time — and a different remedy: free repair parts from Guidecraft at 800-524-3555.

The Wiifo and Guidecraft recalls involve similar product categories — child step towers — but they are completely separate recalls with different brands, different model numbers, and different remedies. If you acted on the Guidecraft recall, you still need to check whether you also own a Wiifo LT005.

If you own both, both actions are required: contact Guidecraft for the repair parts and contact Wiifo for the full refund.

What's Next

For the complete official recall notice, visit CPSC.gov and search "Wiifo Tower Stool" or recall number 26-151. The CPSC notice includes photos to help identify the recalled product.

If you have questions about the recall process, contact the CPSC directly at 1-800-638-2772 or submit a report at SaferProducts.gov. Families who experienced injuries related to this product should document the incident through SaferProducts.gov as well.
