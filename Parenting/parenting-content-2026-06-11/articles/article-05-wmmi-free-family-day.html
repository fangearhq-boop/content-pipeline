What's Happening Tomorrow

The Western Museum of Mining and Industry holds its annual Free Family Day this Saturday, June 12. The event is a full day of outdoor programming at the museum's campus at 225 North Gate Blvd, located near the north end of Colorado Springs.

All outdoor activities are free. That means families can come, explore, and participate without any admission cost for the day's signature events. The museum is open during regular hours; call 719-488-0880 or visit wmmi.org to confirm specific operating times before heading out.

What Families Can Do There

The day's programming hits several different notes, which makes it genuinely engaging for kids of different ages:

Stamp mill demonstrations: The museum's historic stamp mill is one of the few working examples of this 19th-century ore-crushing technology in the country. Watching it operate is a loud, mechanical spectacle that tends to stick in kids' memories. The scale and noise of the machinery alone make it worth seeing.

Live blacksmith demonstration: A working blacksmith shapes metal at a forge. Kids can watch the process up close — heating, hammering, shaping — and ask questions. It's the kind of hands-on history that doesn't happen in a classroom.

Tractor-pulled hayride: A relaxed, old-fashioned family activity. The hayride circles the museum grounds, giving families a different vantage point on the property and a breather between more active activities.

Gold Panning Championships: The crowd-pleaser for kids. Participants try their hand at traditional gold panning — swirling sediment to separate any gold flakes. The competitive format adds some fun stakes, and kids who have never tried it tend to get genuinely absorbed in the process.

Getting There

The museum is at 225 North Gate Blvd, Colorado Springs, CO 80921. The address places it near I-25 on the north side of Colorado Springs, close to the U.S. Air Force Academy exit. Parking is available on site. The museum grounds are accessible by car and are located in a part of Colorado Springs that's easy to reach from most neighborhoods with a short highway drive.

For families coming from the south side of Colorado Springs or from Fountain, plan for about 20 to 30 minutes of travel time depending on traffic.

What's Next

Free Family Day is tomorrow, June 12. For questions about hours, parking, or accessibility accommodations, contact the museum at 719-488-0880 or visit wmmi.org. The museum's full event calendar is also available on the website for families who want to plan future visits.

WMMI runs programming throughout the year, and the museum's regular admission covers access to the full indoor and outdoor exhibits. Free Family Day is a one-day event, but the museum is worth a return visit for families who want a deeper look at Colorado's mining history.
