What's on the Ballot Tonight

District 49's Board of Education meets tonight at 6:30 PM at Sand Creek High School to vote on the district's 2026–27 operating budget. The vote is the formal culmination of a fiscal crisis that began in January 2026, when the district declared a fiscal exigency — a Colorado designation that signals a school district is in financial distress and needs to make significant cuts quickly.

The cuts on tonight's agenda total approximately $2 million. They were approved in concept by the board in a 4-1 vote in February, but tonight's vote adopts the full budget and makes those reductions official. Once the budget passes, it takes effect July 1 — just three weeks from now.

Programs on the Chopping Block

The budget reductions reach across several programs that Colorado Springs families rely on. Here's what's affected:

  • Base49: The district's before- and after-school childcare program. Many working parents depend on Base49 as primary childcare during the school day's edges. Reductions here directly affect families' ability to work standard hours.
  • PEAK programs: Enrichment programming for students beyond core academic requirements.
  • Preschool: Early childhood program seats are being reduced.
  • Elevates 18–21: Specialized services for young adults with disabilities transitioning beyond traditional school age. This program serves a particularly vulnerable population with very few alternatives.
  • Non-core teacher positions and supplemental staffing: Reductions in positions that support classroom instruction without directly teaching core subjects.

The scope of these cuts reflects the depth of the district's financial challenges. Fiscal exigency declarations are rare in Colorado and signal that standard budget adjustment approaches weren't sufficient to close the gap.

How Tonight's Vote Was Set Up

D49's fiscal problems stem from enrollment decline, which reduces the per-pupil funding the district receives from the state. Colorado school funding is largely tied to enrollment: fewer students means fewer dollars. When enrollment drops faster than costs can be trimmed through normal attrition, a structural gap develops.

The district declared fiscal exigency in January 2026. Over the following weeks, district leadership worked with the board to identify the roughly $2 million in reductions that appear in tonight's budget. A preliminary vote approved those cuts in February. Tonight's vote adopts the formal budget document.

For families who have been following this story since the January declaration, tonight isn't a surprise. But for families who are hearing about this for the first time, the timing is tight: July 1 is less than three weeks away.

How to Attend and Speak

Tonight's meeting is a public meeting. Families can attend in person, and there is a public comment period during which community members can address the board directly.

The meeting is at Sand Creek High School, 7005 Carefree Cir N, Colorado Springs. It starts at 6:30 PM. Parking is available on site. The full agenda, including the budget documents, is posted at d49.org.

Public comment is an opportunity to be heard on the record, even if tonight's outcome is largely predetermined by the February preliminary vote. Parents who want to speak should plan to arrive early to sign up for the comment period.

What's Next

If the board votes to approve the budget tonight, the cuts take effect July 1. Families using Base49 and other affected programs should contact D49 directly to understand how their specific enrollment or services will be affected and whether alternative arrangements are needed before the school year ends and summer begins.

For updates on tonight's vote and what comes next, check d49.org and local news outlets including the Colorado Springs Gazette and KOAA.
