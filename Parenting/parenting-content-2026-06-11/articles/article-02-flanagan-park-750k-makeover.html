What's Being Built

Flanagan Park, a neighborhood park near Nevada Avenue and Fillmore Street in Colorado Springs, is getting its first major rehabilitation in roughly 50 years. The City of Colorado Springs received a $750,000 federal Community Development Block Grant to fund the project, and the planned improvements are substantial.

The renovation includes a brand-new playground structure, a new ADA-accessible walking trail with lighting, a new basketball court, resurfacing of existing courts, and new screen fencing around the site. City crews are expected to begin work later this summer, with the full project scheduled for completion by January 2027.

For a park built in the mid-1970s, this represents a significant investment — the kind of infrastructure renewal that aging neighborhood parks rarely receive without dedicated funding.

Where the Money Comes From

The grant funding comes from the federal Community Development Block Grant program, administered by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development. CDBG funds are specifically directed toward neighborhoods and communities with low-to-moderate income populations, and they're designed for projects that provide a direct benefit to residents — infrastructure, housing, and public facilities.

Receiving $750,000 in CDBG funding for a single park project is a notable allocation. It means Flanagan Park's neighborhood qualified for the investment, and that the city's application to HUD made a compelling case for the park's rehabilitation as a community benefit.

For families in the area, it also means the improvement doesn't come at the expense of the city's parks budget, which has faced pressure in recent years. Federal dollars are doing the work here.

Who This Park Serves

Flanagan Park serves a genuinely multigenerational neighborhood. Families with young children use the playground and open space. Older adults use the walking paths. Assisted-living facilities near the park mean residents with mobility challenges also depend on the grounds for outdoor access.

The addition of an ADA-accessible trail with lighting addresses a real gap: older walking surfaces in aging parks are often uneven, poorly lit, or otherwise inaccessible to people with mobility aids or disabilities. The new trail will make the park usable for more of the people who live nearby, regardless of age or ability.

The basketball court improvements serve a different part of the community — teens and adults who use the courts year-round. Colorado Springs winters mean that well-maintained, well-lit courts see use across seasons, not just in summer.

What to Expect and When

Construction is expected to begin later in summer 2026. The full project is scheduled to be complete by January 2027. That means families visiting the park this summer may see construction activity, fenced-off areas, or temporary closures of specific sections.

The January 2027 completion date positions the renovated park to be fully open and functional heading into the spring 2027 season — giving the neighborhood a full year of use before the next winter.

For questions or updates on the project timeline, check coloradosprings.gov or contact Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services.

What's Next

The Flanagan Park renovation is one example of federal CDBG dollars coming to Colorado Springs neighborhood parks. Families who want to follow the project's progress can monitor city announcements at coloradosprings.gov. If the construction timeline shifts or milestones are reached, those updates will be reflected in city communications.

In the meantime, the park is still accessible in its current state until construction begins. Families can continue using the existing space, with awareness that improvement work is coming later this summer.
