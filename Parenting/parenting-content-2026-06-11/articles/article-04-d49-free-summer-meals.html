What the Program Is — and Isn't

D49's summer meal program is part of the federal Summer Food Service Program, which allows school districts to provide free meals to children during summer months when the regular school year meal programs aren't operating. The meals are federally funded, and the program has no cost to participating families.

The most important thing to understand: this program has no barriers to entry. There's no registration form to fill out, no ID required for the child or the accompanying adult, and no income verification of any kind. Any child between the ages of one and 18 can walk in and receive free breakfast and free lunch.

The program isn't limited to D49 students, and it's not limited to families in financial need. It's open to any eligible child, regardless of which school district they're enrolled in or what their family's economic situation looks like. That's how federal SFSP programs work — they serve community kids, not district rosters.

Who Can Come

Eligibility is simple: children ages one through 18. That covers toddlers through high schoolers. Children with disabilities who participate in programs beyond age 18 may also be eligible — check d49.org or contact the district directly for specifics on that age extension.

There's no limit on how many times a family can visit, and no advance signup is required. Families can come once or come every day until June 19.

Where to Go

Springs Ranch Elementary is a confirmed meal site. The full list of sites, along with their hours and specific meal times, is available at d49.org. It's worth checking the list before heading out, because meal service has specific windows — showing up outside those hours may mean missing the meal.

D49 spans a large geographic area in the northeast part of Colorado Springs and into the surrounding El Paso County communities. Families should find the site closest to their home or check whether multiple sites are convenient depending on their schedule.

D49 vs. D11 — Know the Difference

District 11 also runs a free summer meal program, but it's a completely separate program with a different timeline. D11's summer meals run through July 31 — more than six weeks beyond D49's June 19 end date.

This distinction matters. Parents who have heard that "free summer meals are available" may assume the program runs all summer everywhere. It doesn't. D49's program ends June 19. After that date, D49 families who want to continue accessing free meals will need to find a D11 site or another community meal resource.

If your kids are enrolled in D11, or if you live in a D11 neighborhood, check d11.org for that program's site list and hours. Both programs operate on the same no-barrier principle — any child can attend without registration or ID.

What's Next

The D49 summer meal program ends June 19, 2026. Eight days remain as of today. Families who haven't used the program yet can still access free breakfast and lunch at D49 sites through that date.

For the complete site list and meal hours, visit d49.org. For questions, contact D49's nutrition services department directly through the district website.

After June 19, D11's program at d11.org continues through July 31 for families in that district's footprint. Additional community meal resources for the summer are also available through local food banks and community organizations — El Paso County's community services office can provide referrals.
