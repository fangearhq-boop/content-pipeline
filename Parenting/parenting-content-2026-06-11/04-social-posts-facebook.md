### STORY 1: D49 Budget Vote Is Tonight at 6:30 PM

**Long-Form Post**
📋 The D49 budget vote is happening TONIGHT — and if you have kids in programs like Base49, preschool, or PEAK, this one matters.

The Board of Education votes tonight at 6:30 PM at Sand Creek High School (7005 Carefree Cir N) on the 2026–27 district budget. This vote makes official the roughly $2 million in cuts the board approved in February following the district's fiscal exigency declaration in January.

### STORY 2: Flanagan Park Getting $750K Federal Makeover

**Long-Form Post**
🛝 Good news for Colorado Springs families: Flanagan Park is getting a major upgrade this summer.

The City of Colorado Springs received a $750,000 federal Community Development Block Grant to rehabilitate Flanagan Park, a neighborhood park near Nevada Avenue and Fillmore Street that was built in the mid-1970s. That's roughly 50 years of wear on equipment, trails, and courts — and this grant finally changes that.

What's coming: a brand-new playground structure, a new ADA-accessible walking trail with lighting, a new basketball court, court resurfacing, and screen fencing. City crews will begin work later this summer, with the full project expected to be complete by January 2027.

The CDBG grant program specifically targets improvements in lower-to-moderate income neighborhoods, meaning federal dollars are going directly to community infrastructure. For the families, older adults, and nearby assisted-living residents who rely on this park, the improvements mean a safer, more accessible space year-round.

Has your family visited Flanagan Park? What improvements are you most looking forward to?

### STORY 3: Recall Alert — Wiifo Toddler Tower Stools

**Long-Form Post**
⚠️ If you have a Wiifo Children's Tower Stool at home, stop using it now — the CPSC has issued a recall.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission recalled approximately 9,700 Wiifo-brand Children's Tower Stools (Model LT005) on April 23, 2026 due to entrapment and fall hazards. The stools can collapse or tip while a child is using them — and the side openings are large enough that a child's torso can become trapped. That's not a minor risk; it's a serious injury hazard.

The CPSC received 22 reports of stools collapsing, with 6 injuries including contusions and scrapes. These stools were sold exclusively on Amazon between June 2022 and March 2026 for about $60. They come in white, natural, and light wood finishes. Check the underside label for "Model LT005."

If you have one: stop using it, disassemble it, take a photo, and send that photo to support@wiifo.net to claim your full refund. One more thing: this is a different recall from the Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower recall issued May 14, 2026. If you acted on the Guidecraft recall, you still need to check for the Wiifo.

Do you have one of these at home? Share this post so other parents can check.

### STORY 4: D49 Free Summer Meals End June 19

**Long-Form Post**
🍽️ Eight days left to use this free resource — and a lot of Colorado Springs families still don't know it exists.

D49's summer meal program ends June 19. That's eight days from today. Any child ages 1 through 18 can walk in and get free breakfast and lunch at D49 sites — no registration, no ID, no income verification required. None. You just show up.

Springs Ranch Elementary is one confirmed site. The full list of sites and hours is at d49.org. Hours vary by location, so it's worth checking the specific site nearest to you.

One important note: this is D49's program, and it ends June 19. District 11's summer meal program is separate and runs through July 31. If your kids are enrolled in D11 or you're in a D11 neighborhood, you have more time — but D49 families don't. Budget-stretching summer meal options are rare, and this one requires zero paperwork.

Did you know about this program? Tag a family who could use it.

### STORY 5: WMMI Free Family Day TOMORROW — June 12

**Long-Form Post**
⛏️ Tomorrow is WMMI's Free Family Day — and it's one of the more fun free things you can do in Colorado Springs this weekend.

The Western Museum of Mining and Industry (225 North Gate Blvd) holds its Free Family Day this Saturday, June 12. Watch the historic stamp mill machinery in action, see a live blacksmith demonstration, take a tractor-pulled hayride, and catch the Gold Panning Championships. All outdoor activities are free.

This isn't a quiet museum visit — it's hands-on, loud, and genuinely engaging for kids. The gold panning event especially tends to be a crowd-pleaser for kids who've never tried it. The museum is near the north end of Colorado Springs, off I-25 near the Air Force Academy exit.

For questions or hours, call 719-488-0880 or visit wmmi.org. No ticket purchase needed for the outdoor activities.
