TOMY Recalls Boon PIVOT Collapsible Toddler Tower

What to Do If Your Family Has One — and How to Get the Free Repair Kit

July 17, 2026 | By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

TOMY is recalling approximately 106,000 Boon PIVOT Collapsible Toddler Towers sold in the United States after reports that the product can shift or lean while a child is using it. The hazard is a tip-over and fall risk. If you have one of these towers at home, you need to stop using it right now and request a free repair kit from TOMY.

The recall was announced July 16, 2026. Zero injuries have been reported — the goal is to keep it that way.

Who Is Affected

This recall covers Boon PIVOT Collapsible Toddler Towers — also labeled as kitchen step stools — sold in gray and white. The towers are approximately 22 inches wide, 23 inches deep, and 35 inches tall, with a metal and plastic construction and "boon" printed on the top rail. They were sold for $149.99 at Target.com, Amazon.com, and Babylist.com between December 2022 and May or June 2026.

TOMY has received 11 reports of the tower shifting or leaning during use. No injuries have been reported. The company is acting proactively to address the instability issue before an injury occurs.

What the Hazard Is

The Boon PIVOT Toddler Tower is designed to allow young children to stand at counter height while a caregiver works nearby in the kitchen. The recall hazard is structural instability: the tower can shift or lean when a child is standing on it. Because toddlers cannot reliably catch themselves during a fall, even a moderate lean can result in a serious injury.

The CPSC recall classification lists this as a tip-over and fall risk. There have been zero injuries reported so far, but product safety recalls of this type are issued specifically to prevent the first serious incident from occurring.

What to Do Right Now

TOMY's instructions are clear:

  1. Stop using the tower immediately.
  2. Store it somewhere your child cannot reach or access it.
  3. Contact TOMY to request the free stabilizing repair kit, which will be mailed directly to you.

You do not need to return the tower to a store. TOMY is sending a free repair kit designed to address the instability issue. Once the repair is applied following TOMY's instructions, the company expects the tower to be safe for continued use.

For the most current contact information and the specific CPSC recall page, visit cpsc.gov/recalls and search for "TOMY" or "Boon PIVOT." The CPSC recall number was not available at press time due to a technical issue with the CPSC website; check the CPSC site directly to confirm details and access the official remedy instructions.

What's Next

If you have a Boon PIVOT Toddler Tower in your home, act today: store it away from your child and contact TOMY for the free repair kit. Do not wait to see if your specific unit has shown instability — the recall applies across the affected product range, not only to units that have already shifted.

If you're not sure whether you bought through one of the covered retailers, check your purchase history at Target.com, Amazon.com, or Babylist.com. Purchases made between December 2022 and May or June 2026 fall within the recall window.

Once you've received and applied the repair kit, follow TOMY's guidance before returning the tower to use. Additional information, including TOMY's contact information and any updates, is available at cpsc.gov/recalls.
