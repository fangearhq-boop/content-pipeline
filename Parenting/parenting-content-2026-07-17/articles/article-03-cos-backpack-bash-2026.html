Free Backpacks for COS Kids: Backpack Bash Hits 4 Locations Before School Starts

No Registration Required — But Students Must Be Present and Supplies Are Limited

July 17, 2026 | By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

The Backpack Bash is back for 2026, and it's coming to four Colorado Springs locations across two Saturdays before school starts. If your family could use a free backpack for the upcoming school year, here's everything you need to know about when to show up, where to go, and what to expect.

The event is organized by Mercy's Gate and COSILoveYou, along with more than 70 community partner organizations. It's been a fixture for Colorado Springs families for several years, and this year the organizers are reaching across all four corners of the city.

What to Know Before You Go

A few details that matter:

  • Students must be present. This is not a drop-in event where a caregiver can pick up a backpack on a student's behalf. The child needs to be there.
  • Supplies are limited. Backpacks are distributed while supplies last. Early arrival is strongly recommended — particularly at the July 25 locations, which will likely draw families across the east side ahead of D49's Aug. 4 start date.
  • No registration is required. Just show up. However, because supplies run out, arriving early matters more than any sign-up process would.
  • Backpacks only. The Backpack Bash distributes backpacks, not school supplies. Don't expect pencils, notebooks, or other materials at the event.

Each location will also have local family resource vendors on site, along with Kona Ice and a coffee truck — so there's more to the experience than picking up a backpack and leaving.

Why This Year's Timing Matters

D49 students start Aug. 4 — just 18 days from now. D11 follows on Aug. 12, and D20 on Aug. 13. Both the July 25 and Aug. 1 events fall within the window where a new backpack is something families are actively thinking about.

The event has historically drawn large crowds at multiple locations. If July 25 doesn't work for your family, the Aug. 1 date provides a second opportunity — but the same supply-limit caution applies there.

What's Next

Pick the location closest to your home and mark the date. The full schedule and any updates are at cosbackpackbash.com. If you have questions about a specific location or want to confirm details closer to the event, that site is your best resource.

For D49 families on the east side, Sand Creek HS East on July 25 is the closest option and the most timely before school starts. For families in central Colorado Springs, the Aug. 1 stops at Mitchell HS and Hillside Community Center give a second chance with still enough lead time before D11 and D20 open.
