Cat Fest Colorado Is Coming to Colorado Springs on July 25

Cat Show, Adoption Booths, Costume Contest — Your Full Guide to the Event

July 17, 2026 | By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

Cat Fest Colorado is making its way to Colorado Springs for a full day of cat-themed entertainment, education, and adoption opportunities on Saturday, July 25, at Norris Penrose Event Center. Whether your family already has cats at home or you're considering adoption, this is a well-organized event with something for kids of all ages.

Organizer Dana Cain Events has built Cat Fest into a traveling festival with a mix of competitive cat shows, adoption fairs, and interactive activities. The Colorado Springs stop is at 1045 Lower Gold Camp Rd — an event venue with parking and indoor space suited for a day-long program.

What's Happening at Cat Fest

The event runs for six hours, and the schedule is dense. Here's what the program includes:

TICA Cat Show — Yellow Rose Cat Club

The centerpiece of the event is a formal cat show sanctioned by The International Cat Association (TICA), organized by the Yellow Rose Cat Club. Judges evaluate cats across breeds and classes in a structured competition. If your family hasn't seen a formal cat show before, it's a genuinely interesting window into the cat fancy world — and well-behaved kids are very welcome at TICA shows.

Cat Adoption Booths

Look What The Cat Brought In, a Colorado cat rescue, will have adoption booths on site. If your family has been considering adding a cat, this is a chance to meet adoptable animals in a festive, low-pressure environment.

Activities and Entertainment

Beyond the show and adoptions, the event includes a costume contest, an Egyptian Cat Temple exhibit, breed meet-and-greets, face painters, educational presentations, a Memory Wall, celebrity cats, and a Catwalk Fashion Show. There's enough programming to fill the full six hours without running out of things to do.

Ticket Options and Planning

General admission is $20 per person, with children under five admitted free. For a family of four, the Family 4-Pack at $72 saves $8 off four individual tickets. Military tickets are $15.

VIP tickets are $35 and include one hour of early entry before doors open to general admission, line-skipping privileges throughout the day, reserved seating in the main event area, and a cat ear headband souvenir. VIP is capped at 300 tickets — if you want the early-entry experience, it's worth purchasing in advance at catfestco.com rather than waiting to see if VIP is available at the door.

There is one date-specific note worth flagging: a Fox21 article URL references "july-26," but the primary sources — catfestco.com and the Norris Penrose Event Center events calendar — both confirm July 25. Plan for July 25.

What's Next

Tickets are available now at catfestco.com. If you're considering VIP, the 300-ticket cap makes advance purchase worthwhile. For general admission, you can purchase at the door, but advance tickets avoid any potential sellout situation.

Norris Penrose Event Center is at 1045 Lower Gold Camp Rd in Colorado Springs. Parking is available on site. The event runs from 11:00 AM to 5:00 PM — plan for the full afternoon if you want to see the cat show, the adoption booths, and the costume contest.
