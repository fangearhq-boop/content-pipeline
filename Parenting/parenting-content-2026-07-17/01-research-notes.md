### STORY 2: AAP Universal Free School Meals Policy (July 2026)

**Source confidence:** HIGH (confirmed from AAP newsroom, Pediatrics journal, congress.gov)

**Verified facts (HIGH):**
- Document type: Formal AAP Policy Statement
- Title: "The Role of the Pediatrician to Promote Effective Approaches for Child and Adolescent Nutrition in Schools"
- Issuing bodies: AAP Council on School Health and Committee on Nutrition
- Publication: July 2026 issue of Pediatrics
- Core recommendation: Universal access to free school meals for all students, regardless of income
- Finding: Participating in school meal programs is associated with improved diet quality
- Finding: School meal participation does NOT increase obesity risk (directly addresses a common parent misconception)
- Finding: School breakfast participation remains "lower than optimal" — AAP urges pediatricians to promote breakfast access
- Historical context: COVID-era universal free meal waivers sharply increased participation and reduced food insecurity; AAP wants this made permanent
- Action items for pediatricians: advocate through school wellness councils, community partnerships, and direct policy advocacy
- Related legislation: HR.8798, Universal School Meals Program Act of 2026 (Sanders-led), pending in Congress
- Companion research: "Statewide Universal School Meals Policies and Food Insecurity: Difference-in-Differences Analysis" (same journal issue; confirms policy adoption reduces food insecurity)
- Primary source: publications.aap.org/pediatrics/article/doi/10.1542/peds.2026-077404/207880/
- AAP news: publications.aap.org/aapnews/news/35299/AAP-policy-calls-for-universal-school-meals-to
- Legislation: congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/8798/text
