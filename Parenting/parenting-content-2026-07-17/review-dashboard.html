TOMY Boon PIVOT Toddler Tower Recalled — 106K Units
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post If you have a Boon PIVOT Collapsible Toddler Tower at home, here's what you need to know right now. TOMY has recalled approximately 106,000 of these toddler towers in the United States — the same ones sold at Target, Amazon, and Babylist from December 2022 through earlier this year. The hazard is serious: the tower can shift or lean while a child is on it, creating a tip-over and fall risk. There have been 11 reports of shifting or leaning, and zero injuries so far. The goal is to keep it that way. The remedy is straightforward: stop using the tower immediately, store it somewhere your child can't reach, and contact TOMY for a free stabilizing repair kit they'll mail directly to you. Not sure if yours is affected? Look for gray and white colorways, "boon" printed on the top rail, and dimensions of approximately 22 inches wide by 23 inches deep by 35 inches tall. The towers sold for $149.99 and were available at Target.com, Amazon.com, and Babylist.com. More details and the TOMY contact link are in the article below. AAP Calls for Universal Free School Meals — July 2026 Policy Statement
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The American Academy of Pediatrics just published something worth every COS parent's attention. 🍎 In a new formal policy statement in the journal Pediatrics, the AAP is officially calling for universal free school meals for every student in the U.S. — regardless of family income. This isn't a suggestion; it's a full policy position from the AAP's Council on School Health and Committee on Nutrition, published in July 2026. Here's the part that stood out: the AAP research directly addresses a common parent concern — that school lunches contribute to childhood obesity. The evidence says otherwise. School meal participation is actually associated with improved diet quality, and the research shows it does not increase obesity risk. The context matters for Colorado Springs families. D11 already runs one of the region's largest free summer meals programs, and D11, D20, and D49 students all return in August. The legislation that would make universal school meals permanent — HR.8798, the Universal School Meals Program Act — is currently in Congress. We broke down exactly what the AAP said, what the research shows, and how COS families can engage. Link is below. What do you think: should free school meals be universal for every student? --- ### Image Post Caption The AAP is officially calling for universal free school meals for every student 🍎 — and new research shows school meals improve diet quality without increasing obesity risk.
