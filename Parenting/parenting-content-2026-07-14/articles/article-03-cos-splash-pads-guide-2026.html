COS Splash Pads 2026: 4 Free Sites Open Now (and One That's Closed)

By Jamie Rivera July 14, 2026

The One Thing to Know Before You Go

Julie Penrose Fountain at America the Beautiful Park is not operating this summer. Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services has not activated the fountain in 2026 due to budget adjustments. If your family has visited in past years, it's worth knowing before you make the drive. The park itself is open and worth visiting for its other amenities, but the splash pad is not running.

The good news: four other city-run spray grounds are free and operational through Labor Day, September 7, 2026. Here's what you need to know about each one.

The Four Active Sites

Briargate Splash Pad — North Colorado Springs

Open daily from 10 AM to 6 PM. Entry is session-based: timed sessions begin at 10 AM, 12:30 PM, 3 PM, and 4:30 PM. If you arrive between sessions, you'll wait for the next one to start. This setup helps manage the number of kids in the water at one time and tends to mean each session feels a bit less crowded at the beginning.

Watering Hole at John Venezia Community Park — Northeast Colorado Springs

Open daily from 10 AM to 6 PM without timed session restrictions. The Watering Hole features more than 12 water elements, including ground jets, overhead misters, and spray arcs. The park also includes a universally accessible playground directly adjacent to the splash area, which makes it one of the more well-rounded stops for families with a range of ages and abilities. Venezia Park also has a small pond and walking paths if you want to extend the outing.

Deerfield Hills Spray Ground — Deerfield Hills Area

Open daily from 10 AM to 5 PM. Session-based entry at 10 AM, 11:30 AM, 1 PM, 2:30 PM, and 4 PM. This is one of the larger spray set-ups in the city: 16 water features and more than 50 spray nozzles. If your kids want variety, this is the spot. The session structure here is the same as Briargate, so arriving right at a session start time will give you the most uninterrupted time in the water.

Uncle Wilber Fountain — Central Colorado Springs

Open daily from 12 PM to 6 PM. Unlike the other three sites, Uncle Wilber Fountain is drop-in with no session scheduling. You can arrive anytime during operating hours and use the fountain. Hours are slightly shorter than the other sites, with no morning session available. It's the most centrally located of the four, which makes it a convenient option if you're already near downtown.

Tips for Making the Most of It

For session-based sites, arriving 5-10 minutes before a session starts is the easiest way to get in right away and maximize your time. Bring your own towels and a change of clothes. Most city spray grounds don't have changing rooms, so a bag in the car with dry clothes is standard.

Sunscreen is essential and often forgotten in the rush. Water can feel cool enough that kids don't notice the sun, but spray grounds are open areas with full sun exposure during peak hours. Reapply when kids come out of the water.

If you're looking for additional options beyond city-run sites, the Slides and Sunshine Colorado guide at slidesandsunshine.com covers splash pads across the Front Range and beyond, including some in Fountain and Pueblo that are worth the short drive on a weekend.

What's Next

All four active sites run through Labor Day, September 7, 2026. The Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department maintains current operating hours and any service changes at coloradosprings.gov/PRCS. If you're planning a visit to a specific site, it's worth checking closer to the date for any schedule updates, particularly as the season moves into August. The city's social media accounts on Facebook and Instagram (@coloradospringsPRCS) typically post if a site goes offline for maintenance.
