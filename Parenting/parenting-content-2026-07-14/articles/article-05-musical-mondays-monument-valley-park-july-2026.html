Musical Mondays at Monument Valley Park: Free Outdoor Concerts Every Monday in July

By Sarah Morales July 14, 2026

What's Happening and When

Monument Valley Park in Colorado Springs hosts a free outdoor concert series every Monday evening in July. Musical Mondays takes place on the park lawn west of the Fine Arts Center, 30 W. Dale St., starting at 6:30 PM. Two shows remain in this year's series. On July 20, WireWood Station performs Americana. On July 27, the series wraps with a final performance. Both concerts are free and open to all ages.

The series is a casual, low-pressure way to spend a Monday evening outdoors. There's no ticketing, no wristbands, no reservation. You show up, find a spot on the lawn, and stay as long as you want.

WireWood Station on July 20

WireWood Station plays Americana music, a genre that blends country, folk, and roots rock. It's an accessible style for a wide range of ages, which makes it a practical pick for a family evening out. Americana tends to be melody-forward rather than relying on volume or production complexity, which means it carries well in an outdoor park setting and isn't going to overwhelm younger kids or people who find louder concerts tiring.

If Americana is new to your family, the genre includes artists like Jason Isbell, Brandi Carlile, and Hozier as reference points, though WireWood Station's specific sound will vary. For a free outdoor evening, exploring new music in a low-stakes setting is part of the appeal.

What to Bring and What to Expect

The Musical Mondays series is lawn-style seating, which means there are no chairs provided. Bring a blanket or low camp chairs. The park has plenty of open grass, and families typically spread out in a wide arc around the performance area. Arriving by 6 PM gives you time to settle before the 6:30 PM start.

Food trucks are on site during Musical Mondays, which makes dinner logistics easier. You can make the concert your meal stop rather than coordinating dinner at home first. Depending on what's there, food truck lines can get long right at start time, so ordering before the music begins tends to work better.

There's no dedicated alcohol-free section or specific family area called out in the event listing, but Monument Valley Park is a public park and the vibe at these concerts is generally described as relaxed and family-friendly. Kids are common at these events.

Parking and Getting There

Parking near Monument Valley Park is currently limited due to road construction in the area. If you're planning to drive, arriving at 5:45 PM or so will give you more options than arriving right at 6:30 PM. Street parking along Cascade Ave, Dale St., and the surrounding neighborhood streets is the main option. Paid parking in nearby lots may also be available. There is no dedicated event lot for Musical Mondays.

If you live within a reasonable distance of the park and the weather cooperates, biking or walking is worth considering. Monument Valley Park is central enough that many Colorado Springs neighborhoods are within biking range, and arriving by bike sidesteps the parking challenge entirely. The park has bike racks available.

What's Next

July 20 is the next Musical Mondays concert. The series wraps July 27. After July, the city's summer outdoor event schedule shifts toward August activities. Monument Valley Park itself is a year-round resource and one of the more underused parks in Colorado Springs for families. It has several miles of paved and soft-surface paths along Fountain Creek, plus the park fountain, picnic areas, and access to the historic streetcar shelter. For a free evening with easy parking logistics (most of the time), it's worth building into your regular rotation beyond just concert nights. The park is located at 30 W. Dale St. in Colorado Springs. More information on summer city events is available at visitcos.com or through the Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services at coloradosprings.gov/PRCS.
