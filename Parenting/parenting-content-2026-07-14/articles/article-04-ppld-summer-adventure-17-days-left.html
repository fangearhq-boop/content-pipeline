PPLD Summer Adventure Ends July 31 — 17 Days Left to Win Big Prizes

By Jamie Rivera July 14, 2026

There's Still Time to Join

Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program runs through July 31, 2026. With 17 days left, families who haven't signed up yet can still register, log activities, and earn prizes before the program closes. This is not just a reading program, which is one of the things families who haven't tried it sometimes miss. The Summer Adventure tracks reading, creating, and exploring as equally valid activities, which means kids who've spent the summer building things, hiking, cooking, or learning new skills are eligible to participate even if the summer hasn't been heavy on books.

Registration is free. There are no income requirements, no school district restrictions, and no age limit. Parents can participate alongside their kids.

How It Works

The Summer Adventure uses the Beanstack app, available at ppld.beanstack.org/reader365. Once registered, participants log completed activities in one of the three categories: reading (books, magazines, audiobooks), creating (art, building, music, writing), or exploring (nature, science, cooking, learning something new). Each activity logged earns entries toward prize drawings.

You can also register and log activities in person at any of the 14 Pikes Peak Library District branch locations across Colorado Springs and the surrounding area, as well as through the two PPLD bookmobiles. If you prefer paper tracking over the app, library staff can provide activity logs at any branch.

The Prizes

The grand prize options are not small. The Summer Adventure prize pool this year includes a one-night stay for two at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp, a Pikes Peak Region Attractions VIP Access Pass good for entry to multiple COS-area attractions, local gift cards, and memberships to area organizations. The Broadmoor Cloud Camp, for context, is a rustic luxury camp on the summit ridge above The Broadmoor hotel, accessible only by a private trolley. It's the kind of experience that typically requires booking well in advance and isn't cheap. Winning it through a free library program is genuinely a big deal.

Prizes are awarded by drawing, so every completed activity is an entry. More activities logged means more entries. Seventeen days is enough time to accumulate a meaningful number of entries even if you're starting from zero today.

A Few Things Worth Knowing

If you have kids who resist reading but are active in other ways, this is a good pitch: hiking Ute Valley Park counts. Making a recipe counts. Building a birdhouse counts. The structure of the program was intentionally expanded beyond books to reflect the full range of ways kids learn during summer, and it means the program reaches kids who might have tuned out a traditional reading challenge.

For families who will be visiting the East Library at Union Blvd and Vickers Dr, there's an additional reason to make the trip: District 11 is running free lunches for kids at that location on weekdays through July 31 as part of the federal Summer Food Service Program. Combining a library visit to log activities with a free lunch makes for an efficient outing that doesn't cost anything.

What's Next

The Summer Adventure closes July 31, 2026. Prize winners will be notified after the program ends. To register or get more information, visit ppld.org/summer or visit any PPLD branch. The Beanstack app is available on iOS and Android, and a web browser version is available at ppld.beanstack.org/reader365 for households that prefer not to use an app. PPLD's main information line is (719) 531-6333.
