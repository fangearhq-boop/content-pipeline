## Story Rundown

### STORY 1: FOSI Study — Parents Underestimate Kids' AI Use
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Tier:** T1
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera

New survey from the Family Online Safety Institute: 38% of kids say they used generative AI in the past week. Only 27% of their parents think that's true. That 11-point gap is the headline from the "Beyond Borders" study (July 9, 2026) — 4,000+ families across the US and Australia. The study also found similar gaps in social media posting and scrolling, but the AI gap was the widest and the most notable finding. This is a natural summer hook: kids have more unstructured time, parents have less visibility.

### STORY 2: Social Media Safety Features Study — Fewer Than Half Work
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Tier:** T1
**Byline:** Sarah Morales

NYU and Northeastern researchers tested 86 advertised child safety features across TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, and YouTube. Only 35 of 86 — fewer than half — functioned as the platforms claimed. Published June 29, 2026. Platform failures included missing features, broken tools, buried settings, and keyword filters easily bypassed by adults. This connects directly to the FOSI finding: parents who trust platform safety tools may be getting a false sense of security.

### STORY 3: COS Splash Pads 2026 — What's Open and What's Not
**Pillar:** Evergreen

### STORY 4: PPLD Summer Adventure — 17 Days Left
**Pillar:** Local Events
**Tier:** T2
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera

Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure ends July 31 — 17 days away. Kids of any age can still register and log activities (reading, creating, exploring) via the Beanstack app. Prizes include a one-night stay at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp, Pikes Peak Region Attractions VIP Access Pass, and local gift cards. Free, at all 14 library locations or online. Good action item for COS families in the last stretch of summer.

### STORY 5: Musical Mondays at Monument Valley Park — Free
**Pillar:** Local Events
**Tier:** T2
**Byline:** Sarah Morales

Every Monday in July, Monument Valley Park hosts free outdoor concerts starting at 6:30 PM. The next one is July 20: WireWood Station (Americana). Bring a blanket or chair. Food trucks on site. Parking can be tight due to road construction — arrive early. Free, all ages. Simple family evening out.
