### STORY 1: FOSI Study — Parents Underestimate Kids' AI Use

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

Quick question: do you know if your kid used AI in the past week?

A new study from the Family Online Safety Institute asked that same question to over 4,000 families in the US and Australia — and the answers from parents and children didn't match. 38% of kids said they'd used generative AI tools in the past week. Only 27% of their parents said the same.

That 11-point gap is the widest finding in the study. There were also gaps in social media behavior — 54% of kids said they'd been scrolling social media vs. 46% of parents who thought so — but the AI gap stood out most.

Part of what makes this tricky is that kids don't always experience AI as a distinct "thing they're using." It's embedded in apps, search engines, writing tools, image generators, and chatbots they already use for homework, creative projects, or just curiosity. A teenager using AI to help draft an essay or a 10-year-old experimenting with an image generator doesn't necessarily mention it at dinner.

Summer is a particularly good time to get curious about this. There's less structure, more unmonitored screen time, and more room for exploration — both good and concerning. The FOSI study isn't raising an alarm so much as flagging a blind spot: parents may be confident in their awareness of their kid's digital life, but kids are moving faster than that awareness.

If you want to start a low-key conversation, try asking: "What's the most interesting thing you've seen online this week?" or "Have you ever tried asking AI to help with something?" Genuine curiosity goes further than interrogating.

### STORY 2: Social Media Safety Features Study

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

If you've been relying on TikTok's safety settings or Instagram's parental controls to keep your kid safer online, here's something important to know.

Researchers from NYU, Northeastern University, and the Cybersafety Research Center tested 86 advertised child safety features across TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, and YouTube. They created dummy child accounts and adult accounts, then systematically tested each platform's own safety claims.

Only 35 of the 86 features actually worked as advertised. That's fewer than half. And every single platform failed more than 50% of its own safety promises.

The failures weren't minor. Researchers found missing features, broken tools, settings buried so deep that parents wouldn't realistically find them, and keyword filters that adults could easily bypass. The research team was direct in its conclusion: "We have found systemic issues with the design and implementation of many of these features," and the study noted that harms to children "are not hypothetical, and when they do occur the consequences can be irreversible."

This matters because many parents have implicitly trusted these tools. Setting up parental controls on your teen's Instagram feels like you've done something. And maybe you have — but according to this research, there's a good chance more than half of those features aren't doing what you think they're doing.

What actually works? Ongoing conversation with your kid. Knowing their username. Talking about what they see. Not treating "parental controls are on" as a finished task. The platform tools can be part of a strategy — just not the whole strategy.

This research was published June 29, 2026. It's worth sharing with other parents in your circle.

### STORY 3: COS Splash Pads 2026

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

Save this one before you load up the car this week. 🚗

If you were planning to take the kids to America the Beautiful Park for Julie Penrose Fountain — heads up: it's closed this summer due to budget adjustments. We want you to know before you make the drive.

The good news is there are four other city spray grounds open right now, all free, through Labor Day (September 7). Here's the breakdown:

Briargate Splash Pad (north Colorado Springs): Open daily 10 AM–6 PM. Timed sessions at 10 AM, 12:30 PM, 3 PM, and 4:30 PM.

Watering Hole at John Venezia Community Park (northeast Colorado Springs): Open daily 10 AM–6 PM. 12+ water features. Universally accessible. Bonus: there's a playground right next to it.

Deerfield Hills Spray Ground (Deerfield Hills area): Open daily 10 AM–5 PM. Sessions at 10 AM, 11:30 AM, 1 PM, 2:30 PM, and 4 PM. 16 water features and 50+ spray nozzles — one of the bigger set-ups in the city.

Uncle Wilber Fountain (central): Open daily 12 PM–6 PM. Drop-in, no session schedule.

All four are free. No registration, no cost. Just parking and sunscreen.

If you want more options beyond city-run sites, the Slides and Sunshine guide at slidesandsunshine.com covers splash pads across the region, including a few in surrounding areas worth the short drive.

### STORY 4: PPLD Summer Adventure — 17 Days Left

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

If you haven't signed up for PPLD Summer Adventure yet, you still have time — but not much.

The program wraps up July 31, which means there are 17 days left to register, log activities, and earn prizes. Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure tracks reading, creating, and exploring — not just books. Drawing, hiking, cooking, building something, trying something new: it all counts. Any age is eligible, including parents.

The prizes are genuinely worth it. Grand prizes include a one-night stay for two at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp and a Pikes Peak Region Attractions VIP Access Pass. There are also local gift cards and memberships up for grabs.

You can sign up through the Beanstack app at ppld.beanstack.org/reader365, at any of the 14 PPLD library branches, or at one of the two bookmobiles. It's free to participate.

Worth noting: if you have kids who haven't been reading much this summer, the non-reading activities give them a real path to prizes without making it feel like school. And if you're heading to the East Library on Union Blvd near Vickers Drive, D11 is running free lunches for kids there on weekdays through July 31 as well — combine the trips.

### STORY 5: Musical Mondays at Monument Valley Park

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

Looking for a free, easy evening out with the family this summer? Monument Valley Park has you covered every Monday in July.

Musical Mondays brings live music to the park lawn (just west of the Fine Arts Center, 30 W. Dale St.) at 6:30 PM each Monday. It's free, all ages, and food trucks are on site — so you can skip dinner prep and make a night of it.

The next show is July 20: WireWood Station, an Americana act that's a solid pick for a broad range of ages. There's also one more Monday left after that — July 27.

One practical heads-up: parking in the area is limited right now due to road construction, so plan to arrive a bit early or consider walking if you're nearby. Bringing a blanket or low camp chairs is the move — there's open lawn space and it's a relaxed setup.

No tickets, no registration. Just show up, find a spot, and enjoy a summer evening in one of the nicest parks in Colorado Springs.
