## STORY 2: Social Media Child Safety Features Study — NYU/Northeastern

**Source:** New York University + Northeastern University + Heat Initiative + Cybersafety Research Center, published June 29, 2026.
**Coverage:** CNN (June 29, 2026): https://www.cnn.com/2026/06/29/tech/social-media-youth-safety-protections-evaluation-report
**Secondary:** Northeastern University press release: https://news.northeastern.edu/2026/06/29/social-media-safety-child-research/
**Secondary:** TechXplore: https://techxplore.com/news/2026-06-social-media-child-safety-features.html

**Key facts:**
- [HIGH] Method: Researchers created dummy child accounts and adult accounts; tested features on TikTok, Instagram, Snapchat, and YouTube
- [HIGH] Total features tested: 86 advertised child safety features across all four platforms
- [HIGH] Result: Only 35 of 86 features (fewer than half) functioned as the platforms claimed
- [HIGH] Each platform failed more than 50% of its advertised safety features
- [HIGH] Failure types: Missing features, broken tools, buried settings, keyword filters easily bypassed by adults
- [HIGH] Quote from report: "We have found systemic issues with the design and implementation of many of these features"
- [HIGH] Quote: harms to kids "are not hypothetical, and when they do occur the consequences can be irreversible"
- [HIGH] Published: June 29, 2026
- [MEDIUM] Organizations: NYU, Northeastern University, Heat Initiative, Cybersafety Research Center
