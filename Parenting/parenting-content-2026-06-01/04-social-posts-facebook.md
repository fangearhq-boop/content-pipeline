### STORY 1: Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinet CPSC Warning

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 8:00 AM MT

Safety alert for COS parents who may own a Tuyedoqe travel bassinet.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission is warning parents to immediately stop using these products. About 250 units were sold on Amazon from April through October 2025 for around $40. The bassinets violate federal infant sleep product standards in three ways: the restraints create a strangulation hazard, the sides are too low (fall risk), and there's no stand — so using them on a raised surface creates another fall hazard.

Here's the part that makes this especially important: the company, Tuyedoqe of China, has NOT agreed to a recall. They're not offering a refund, a fix, or any remedy. The CPSC issued a notice of violation and the company did nothing.

What to do right now: Stop using the bassinet. Remove the foam padding. Cut the cover in half. Take a photo and dispose of it. Report your product at cpsc.gov.

If you know a family with a young baby who shops for budget baby gear on Amazon, please share this. The price point — around $40 — puts these in a lot of households.

Have you seen this product? What do you think about companies that ignore CPSC notices?

### STORY 2: Rosemont Reservoir — Camping and Boating on Pikes Peak

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 10:00 AM MT

We've been waiting for this one, COS.

Colorado Parks and Wildlife and Colorado Springs Utilities have finalized an agreement to open Rosemont Reservoir on Pikes Peak to public recreation — and families are going to love it. The target date is around July 4, 2026, timed to the combined Colorado 150th anniversary and U.S. 250th anniversary celebrations.

The reservoir sits at more than 9,600 feet of elevation, about 12 miles southwest of Colorado Springs. The plan includes basic campsites, day-use parking, ADA-accessible restrooms, and non-motorized boating — that means hand-launched kayaks, canoes, paddleboards, and electric watercraft.

Camping will require a parks pass or the Keep Colorado Wild pass (which comes on your vehicle registration) plus a camping fee. Reservations will work like other Colorado State Park sites.

CPW is managing construction and stewardship, and they're expecting to break ground soon. Once it's ready, you'll have a new mountain camping option that didn't exist before — at nearly 10,000 feet, right on Pikes Peak.

We'll keep you updated as opening details are confirmed. Are you excited about this one?

### STORY 4: Screen Time in 2026 — What the Research Actually Says for Summer

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 12:00 PM MT

Now that school's out, screens are everywhere — and new research gives us good reason to pay attention.

A 2026 study from University College London found that the average two-year-old now spends 129 minutes per day on screens. That's more than double the World Health Organization's recommendation of one hour or less. And the numbers aren't just about time: toddlers with the highest screen use in the study scored lower on vocabulary assessments and showed more emotional and behavioral difficulties than peers with low screen exposure.

The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its approach in 2026 to focus more on content quality and context, not strict clock limits. But the core guidelines still hold: no screens at all before 18 months, and for ages 2 to 5, a maximum of one hour of high-quality content — ideally watched together with a parent.

Practically, this means: watching with your kids and talking about what you're seeing matters. Device-free bedrooms and mealtimes make a real difference. And summer is the hardest time to hold the line, because the days are longer and routines go out the window.

The good news: COS has plenty of ways to make screen breaks easy. PPLD Summer Adventure is running now (sign up at ppld.org/summer), pools and splash pads are open, and programs through COS PRCS are available at coloradosprings.gov/PRCS.

D49 families have a chance to show up before the district sets its budget for next school year.

The D49 Board of Education will consider adopting the fiscal year 2026-27 budget at its Regular Board meeting on Thursday, June 11 at Sand Creek High School. The budget is expected to include funding for the District 49 Student Success Center, which is scheduled to open at the start of the 2026-27 school year in the former Falcon Elementary of Technology building.

It's a significant new resource for D49 families, and the budget vote is what makes it happen.
