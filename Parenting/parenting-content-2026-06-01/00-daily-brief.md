### STORY 1: CPSC Warning — Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets Pose Strangulation and Fall Hazards
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Window:** 8:00 AM MT

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission is warning parents to immediately stop using Tuyedoqe travel bassinets due to strangulation and fall risks. About 250 units were sold on Amazon from April through October 2025 for approximately $40. The bassinets violate mandatory infant sleep product standards: restraints pose a strangulation hazard, sides are too low (fall risk), and no stand is included (elevated surface fall risk). The company, Tuyedoqe of China, has NOT agreed to recall or offer any remedy. Action: Stop use immediately, remove foam padding, cut the cover in half, and dispose. Do not donate or resell.

### STORY 2: Pikes Peak Camping and Boating Coming to Rosemont Reservoir
**Tier:** 2 | **Pillar:** Local Events | **Window:** 10:00 AM MT

Colorado Parks and Wildlife and Colorado Springs Utilities have signed an agreement to open Rosemont Reservoir on Pikes Peak for camping and non-motorized boating — with a target opening around July 4, 2026. Located at more than 9,600 feet of elevation, approximately 12 miles southwest of Colorado Springs, the site will feature basic campsites, day-use parking, hand-launched/hand-powered/electric-powered watercraft access, and an ADA-accessible restroom. Camping will require a parks pass or Keep Colorado Wild pass plus a camping fee, with a reservation system like other CPW sites. CPW is managing construction and stewardship.

### STORY 4: Screen Time Gets a New Look in 2026 — What COS Parents Need to Know This Summer
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Window:** 12:00 PM MT

New research is shifting how experts think about kids and screens — and summer is the highest-risk season. A 2026 University College London study found the average two-year-old now spends 129 minutes per day on screens, more than double the World Health Organization's recommendation of one hour or less. Toddlers with the highest screen use scored lower on vocabulary assessments and showed more emotional and behavioral difficulties. The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) now recommends prioritizing content quality and context over strict clock limits — but still advises no screens before 18 months and a maximum of one hour of high-quality co-viewed content daily for ages 2–5. With school out, COS families face longer days and more temptation. Local resources to help: PPLD Summer Adventure (ppld.org/summer), COS PRCS programs (coloradosprings.gov/PRCS).
