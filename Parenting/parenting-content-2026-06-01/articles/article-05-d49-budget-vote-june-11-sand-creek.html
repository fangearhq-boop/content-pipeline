D49 Board Votes on 2026-27 Budget June 11 — What COS Families Should Know

By Sarah Morales | June 1, 2026 | COS Parenting

The Falcon School District 49 Board of Education will hold its regular board meeting on June 11, 2026, at Sand Creek High School. On the agenda: the vote to adopt the district's budget for fiscal year 2026-27. For families in the district — which covers the northeast and east sides of Colorado Springs and extends into unincorporated El Paso County — this is the meeting that sets the financial course for the coming school year.

D49 serves more than 25,000 students and has been navigating enrollment growth alongside broader pressures on Colorado school funding. The budget vote is a routine part of the annual calendar, but the decisions made in it directly affect staffing, programs, and facilities families rely on.

One Notable Addition: The Student Success Center

One item drawing attention ahead of the June 11 meeting is the planned opening of D49's new Student Success Center in August 2026. The center is being established at the former Falcon Elementary of Technology campus and is designed to consolidate a range of specialized programs that currently operate across the district.

The Student Success Center is expected to house:

  • Preschool programming — offered Monday through Thursday
  • Base49 — the district's before- and after-school care program
  • PEAK programs — enrichment and gifted education services
  • Elevates 18-21 — transition services for students with disabilities ages 18 to 21
  • Specialized support services — additional student support and intervention programs

Whether and how the Student Success Center is funded in the 2026-27 budget could affect which programs open on schedule in August. Families with children enrolled in Base49, preschool, or any of the specialized programs should pay attention to board communication in the weeks following the June 11 vote.

Why Budget Votes Matter for Families

School district budgets in Colorado are shaped by a combination of state per-pupil funding formulas, local property taxes, and federal allocations. D49's budget has to account for a growing student population, inflationary pressures on operational costs, and the ongoing need to maintain and expand facilities.

For families, the practical effects of budget decisions show up in areas like class sizes, extracurricular program availability, paraprofessional staffing, and transportation routes. Programs serving students with disabilities and English language learners are often among the most sensitive to funding fluctuations, because they depend on dedicated staffing and specialized resources that can't easily be scaled down without direct impact on students.

D49 board meetings are open to the public. Residents who live within the district boundaries can attend and participate in public comment. Meeting agendas are posted in advance on the district website.

How to Follow the Meeting

D49 board meetings are typically livestreamed and recorded, with videos posted to the district website following the meeting. If you can't attend in person on June 11, the recording is usually available within a few days.

For families specifically interested in the Student Success Center programs — preschool enrollment, Base49 registration, or PEAK services — contacting the district directly through d49.org before August is the most reliable way to get enrollment timelines and any updates driven by the budget approval.

What's Next

Following the June 11 vote, D49 will finalize program plans for the 2026-27 school year. The Student Success Center is on track to open in August 2026, pending budget adoption. COS Parenting will cover the vote outcome and any program announcements that affect families in the district. For the full board agenda, meeting time, and livestream information, visit d49.org in the days leading up to June 11.
