CPSC Warning: Stop Using Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets Immediately — Strangulation and Fall Risks

By Sarah Morales | June 1, 2026 | COS Parenting

If you have a Tuyedoqe travel bassinet in your home, the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission wants you to stop using it right now.

The CPSC has issued a warning — not a standard recall — against the product after finding that it violates mandatory federal safety standards for infant sleep products. The company responsible, Tuyedoqe of China, has not agreed to offer any remedy to consumers. That means no refund. No replacement. No fix. Parents have to take action themselves.

What Makes These Bassinets Dangerous

The CPSC identified three separate hazards in the Tuyedoqe travel bassinets:

  • Strangulation risk — The bassinets contain restraints that can entangle an infant.
  • Fall hazard — The sides are too low to safely contain an infant, meaning a baby could roll out.
  • Elevated surface fall hazard — The bassinets have no stand, so placing them on any raised surface (a bed, a table, a dresser) creates a serious fall risk.

All three of these issues violate the mandatory federal standard for infant sleep products.

What You Need to Do Right Now

The CPSC advises parents to take these steps immediately:

  1. Stop using the bassinet.
  2. Remove the foam padding from the product.
  3. Cut the cover in half.
  4. Dispose of the cover and padding.

Do not donate this product. Do not sell it. Because the company has not agreed to a recall or refund program, there is no return process available. Destruction and disposal is the only safe course of action.

Why This Warning Is Different

Most product recalls involve a company working with the CPSC to offer refunds or replacements. This situation is different. The CPSC issued a "Notice of Violation" to Tuyedoqe, and the company did not respond with any corrective action. The CPSC is warning consumers directly because the company has refused to act.

This type of warning is uncommon and reflects a serious concern about consumer safety. If your child has slept in one of these bassinets and experienced any injury, report it at cpsc.gov or call 1-800-638-2772.

What's Next

The CPSC continues to monitor the situation with Tuyedoqe. In the meantime, Colorado Springs parents who purchased this product through Amazon should document the purchase and report the incident to the CPSC regardless of whether an injury occurred. Reports help the commission build a case for enforcement action. For questions about safe infant sleep, visit the CPSC safe sleep page at cpsc.gov/SafeSleep.
