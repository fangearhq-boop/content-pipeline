Camping and Boating Coming to Rosemont Reservoir on Pikes Peak — What COS Families Should Know

By Jamie Rivera | June 1, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs families are about to get a new outdoor adventure destination — and it's right on Pikes Peak.

Colorado Parks and Wildlife and Colorado Springs Utilities have signed an agreement to open Rosemont Reservoir for public camping and non-motorized boating. The target opening date is around July 4, 2026 — timed to coincide with the combined Colorado 150th anniversary and U.S. 250th birthday celebrations. If construction stays on track, families could be paddling on a Pikes Peak reservoir before summer is over.

What the Reservoir Offers

Rosemont Reservoir sits at more than 9,600 feet of elevation, approximately 12 miles southwest of Colorado Springs on the south side of Pikes Peak. It has been a Colorado Springs Utilities water supply site for decades — closed to public recreation until now.

The planned amenities include:

  • Basic campsites for overnight stays
  • Day-use parking areas
  • ADA-accessible restrooms
  • Non-motorized boating access — kayaks, canoes, paddleboards, and electric-powered watercraft are all permitted

The site will be managed by Colorado Parks and Wildlife, which handles construction, operations, and stewardship. CPW expects to break ground soon, with a stated goal of having the campground ready for the July 4 weekend.

What Families Need to Plan For

Camping at Rosemont will require a parks pass — either a Colorado State Parks Pass or the Keep Colorado Wild pass, which comes automatically on vehicle registrations. You'll also pay a camping fee on top of that. Reservations will be required and will work through the same system families already use for other Colorado State Park campgrounds.

At 9,600 feet of elevation, temperatures drop significantly at night even in summer. Families planning to camp should be prepared for overnight lows that can drop into the 40s or below, even on warm summer days. The drive from Colorado Springs is approximately 12 miles — a short trip, but one that winds into mountain terrain.

Day-use visitors will also have access to parking and boating without needing to camp overnight.

What's Next

Colorado Parks and Wildlife is still finalizing the site and construction timeline. The July 4 target date depends on construction going smoothly — which CPW has indicated they expect. Watch cpw.state.co.us for reservation system announcements and opening confirmation. COS Parenting will update this story when the site opens for bookings.
