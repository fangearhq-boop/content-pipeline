Get Outdoors Day Is This Saturday in Colorado Springs — Free Family Event at Memorial Park

By Sarah Morales | June 1, 2026 | COS Parenting

If your kids are already asking what there is to do this weekend, here's your answer: Get Outdoors Day at Memorial Park on Saturday, June 6, is completely free and packed with things to try.

The annual event — hosted by Colorado Parks and Wildlife, the City of Colorado Springs Parks Recreation and Cultural Services department, and the Pikes Peak Outdoor Recreation Alliance — runs from 9 AM to 2 PM at Prospect Lake in Memorial Park. More than 3,000 people are expected, and it's one of the biggest free outdoor family events in Colorado Springs every summer.

What's There to Do

Get Outdoors Day is built around letting families try outdoor activities they might not have access to otherwise. The activities include:

  • Archery
  • Stand-up paddleboarding on Prospect Lake
  • Fly fishing instruction
  • Rock climbing wall
  • Touch a Truck (families can explore and climb on outdoor vehicles and equipment)
  • Dunk tank

Throughout the event, there's a passport to get stamped at different vendor stations. Families who complete the passport are entered into a drawing for outdoor gear prizes.

The Big Tip: Arrive Early for Free Fishing Gear

The first 650 kids age 15 and under each receive a free fishing pole, bait, and instructions for trying fishing in Prospect Lake. This is first-come, first-served, and they go quickly. If your kids are excited about the fishing opportunity, plan to arrive at or before 9 AM when the event opens.

What to Bring

No registration is required. Just show up. A few things that will make the day more comfortable:

  • Sunscreen — the event is mostly outdoors with limited shade near the lake
  • Water bottles for everyone
  • Comfortable, closed-toe shoes for the climbing wall and active stations
  • A change of clothes if you're planning on paddleboarding with younger kids

Parking around Memorial Park can fill up at busy events. Consider arriving early, both for parking and for the free fishing gear while supplies last.

What's Next

Get Outdoors Day is part of a national event series run by the Outdoor Foundation. In Colorado Springs, it's one of the first big outdoor events of the summer season. If your family discovers a new activity this Saturday, both CPW and COS PRCS offer ongoing programs to continue learning — from youth fishing licenses to paddleboarding lessons at area parks. Visit coloradosprings.gov/PRCS for summer programming and ppora.org for more on the event.
