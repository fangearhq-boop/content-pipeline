### STORY 1: Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

If you have a Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower at home, please stop using it today.

The CPSC announced a recall of more than 25,000 Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Towers — Classic and Contemporary styles in nine colors — due to a fall hazard. The platform inside the tower can loosen over time and detach, causing children to fall. The CPSC has received 11 reports of children falling, including three injuries.

The towers were sold at Amazon, Target, Guidecraft.com, Wayfair, Walmart, Maisonette, and Overstock from June 2022 through October 2023, for around $200.

The good news: the remedy is a free repair kit, not a full return. You don't lose the product — you just need to contact Guidecraft to get it fixed. Stop using it first, then call 800-524-3555 (Mon-Fri, 8 AM-8 PM MT) or email ProductSupport@guidecraft.com. You can also visit guidecraft.com/pages/product-recall. ⚠️

Do you have one at home, or know another family who might? Please share this.

### STORY 2: COS Park System Master Plan Survey

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

Here's your chance to actually shape what COS parks look like for the next 10-15 years.

The City of Colorado Springs has opened its Park System Master Plan survey, and they want to hear specifically from families who use parks, trails, and open spaces in Colorado Springs. You can take the online survey now through August 3 at coloradosprings.gov/ParkSystemMasterPlan. There's also an interactive map where you can pinpoint the exact park you're talking about.

In-person public meetings are scheduled for June 24-25 and July 9 if you want to show up and speak directly.

Early community feedback has already flagged more restrooms and drinking fountains at existing parks as top priorities — which tracks for anyone who's ever arrived at a playground with little ones and found the only restroom locked. 🌳

This is the kind of survey where a few hundred responses from engaged families can actually move the needle. What would you tell the city first?

### STORY 3: Western Street Breakfast

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

Colorado Springs has been doing this for decades and it never gets old.

This morning, thousands of locals headed downtown for the Colorado Springs Western Street Breakfast — one of the city's most beloved annual traditions. Every June 17, Pikes Peak Avenue and Tejon Street get closed off, the streets fill up, and the smell of a hot breakfast takes over downtown.

Free admission. Breakfast tokens are $5 a person, and kids under 5 eat free. The Kid's Corral had steer roping, a petting zoo, and a Western dress-up contest. Live entertainment from Exit West and the Flying W Wranglers kept things lively until the final pancake was served at 9 AM. 🤠

If you missed it this year, save the date for next June 17. It's one of those things that makes Colorado Springs feel like Colorado Springs.

Did your family make it? What's your favorite part?

### STORY 4: Hill Climb Ticket Reminder

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

Four days until Pikes Peak Hill Climb Race Day — and there's something important COS families need to know before Sunday.

The 2026 Pikes Peak International Hill Climb takes place this Sunday, June 21, which is also Father's Day. Green flag drops at 7:30 AM. Gates open as early as 2:30 AM for families who want the best spots.

Here's the thing: tickets are not sold at the gate. They're not sold in-person anywhere. You have to buy online in advance at ppihc.org (through Humanitix). Tickets are expected to sell out, and post-June 1 pricing is already in effect, so now is the time to go get them.

Kids under 10 are free on Race Day, which makes this one of the more affordable family outings for the summer. If you haven't been before, the Devil's Playground area at 12,780 feet is the most dramatic spectator location in American motorsport. 🏁

Also — if you want to introduce the kids to it without the early morning, Fan Fest is THIS THURSDAY June 19, 5-9 PM, in downtown COS across 10 city blocks. It's free.

### STORY 5: D20 Superintendent Update

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

D20 families — here's the school leadership update you may have missed.

In May 2026, the Academy District 20 Board of Education voted to remove superintendent Jinger Haberer, citing a lack of confidence in her leadership. Four days later, on May 19, the board unanimously named Dr. Susan Field as interim superintendent for the 2026-27 school year.

Dr. Field brings 19 years of D20 experience to the role. She started as principal at Woodmen-Roberts Elementary, moved into district-level work, and has been serving as Assistant Superintendent for Learning Services. She knows this district from the inside.

Academy District 20 serves approximately 26,000 students across nearly 40 schools in northern Colorado Springs. Schools continue to operate normally during the transition. 🏫

The 2026-27 school year will be Dr. Field's first as the district's top leader — and an important one for D20 families to stay engaged with. Watch for enrollment updates, budget decisions, and curriculum direction from district communications at asd20.org.

D20 families — what do you most want to see from district leadership in the coming school year?
