Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower Recall: Over 25,000 Units, Fall Hazard, Free Repair Available

Stop using it now — then contact Guidecraft for a free fix

By Jamie Rivera

June 17, 2026

If you have a Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower at home, stop using it today. The Consumer Product Safety Commission announced a recall of more than 25,000 units due to a fall hazard that has already resulted in confirmed child injuries. The good news: Guidecraft is offering a free repair kit, so you don't have to lose the product — but you do need to act.

What's Being Recalled

The recall covers Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Towers in both Classic and Contemporary styles, in nine different colors. These are the popular toddler-height standing towers designed to let young children safely reach kitchen counter height to participate in cooking and meal preparation activities.

The hazard: the platform inside the tower can loosen over time and eventually detach, creating an unstable surface under a child's feet. The CPSC has confirmed 11 reports of children falling from the towers. Three injuries were reported, including a bloody nose and bruises.

The towers were sold at Amazon, Target, Guidecraft's own website, Wayfair, Walmart, Maisonette, and Overstock from June 2022 through October 2023. The retail price was approximately $200.

Why the Free Repair Matters

Many product recalls require consumers to return the item for a refund or replacement. This recall works differently. Guidecraft is sending out a free repair kit that addresses the platform loosening issue. That means your family doesn't have to give up the tower — you just need to have it fixed before using it again.

The repair process requires contacting Guidecraft directly through one of the channels listed above. Do not attempt to fix or reinforce the platform yourself. Wait for the official repair kit and follow Guidecraft's instructions.

Check Your Receipts

If you're not sure whether your tower was purchased during the recall window (June 2022 through October 2023), check your Amazon, Target, Walmart, or Wayfair order history. The towers were sold under the Guidecraft brand name — look for "Kitchen Helper Tower," "Kitchen Helper Classic," or "Kitchen Helper Contemporary."

If you received one as a gift or purchased it secondhand, check the product itself. The Classic and Contemporary styles are both included. If you're uncertain, contact Guidecraft at 800-524-3555 to verify.

What's Next

Once you've received and installed the repair kit per Guidecraft's instructions, the tower can be returned to use. If you have questions about whether your specific unit is affected, Guidecraft's customer support line is available Monday through Friday, 8 AM to 8 PM ET. You can also report any additional incidents directly to the CPSC at cpsc.gov or by calling 1-800-638-2772.
