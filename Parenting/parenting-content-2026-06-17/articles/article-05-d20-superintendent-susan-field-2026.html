Academy District 20 Names Dr. Susan Field Interim Superintendent for 2026-27 School Year

With 19 years inside the district — including time as a principal and assistant superintendent — Dr. Field starts as the district's top leader this fall

By Jamie Rivera

June 17, 2026

Academy District 20 will begin the 2026-27 school year with a new leader at the top. On May 19, 2026, the D20 Board of Education unanimously named Dr. Susan Field as interim superintendent — four days after the board voted to remove superintendent Jinger Haberer, citing a lack of confidence in her leadership. For D20 families who haven't been tracking the district news closely, here's what happened and what it means heading into the fall.

What Happened in May

The sequence moved quickly. The D20 Board of Education voted to remove Haberer on May 15, citing a lack of confidence in her leadership. The board did not provide extensive public detail about the specific circumstances driving the decision. Four days later, on May 19, the board voted unanimously to appoint Dr. Susan Field as interim superintendent for the 2026-27 school year.

The speed of the Field appointment — four days from removal to a unanimous replacement — suggests the board had already done significant preparation work before the Haberer vote. Field was not an outside hire brought in on short notice; she was already inside the district in a senior leadership role.

Field's Path Through D20

Dr. Field has spent 19 years inside Academy District 20, which makes her one of the more deeply rooted leaders the district has seen at the superintendent level. She started as principal at Woodmen-Roberts Elementary, building classroom-level and building-level leadership experience before moving into district administration.

From principal work, she moved into district-level roles and most recently served as Assistant Superintendent for Learning Services — the position responsible for curriculum, instruction, and academic programming across the district. That background in learning services means Field's lens on the district is primarily through instruction and student outcomes rather than operations or finance.

For D20 families, the 19-year tenure is significant. Field knows the schools, knows many of the building leaders, and knows the district's academic culture from the inside. That's a different starting position than a superintendent brought in from outside — whether that familiarity is an advantage or a constraint depends on what changes families want the district to make.

What Stays the Same

Academy District 20 serves approximately 26,000 students across nearly 40 schools in the northern Colorado Springs area. Schools continue to operate normally during the leadership transition. The transition at the superintendent level does not affect the structure of individual schools, staffing at the building level, or the academic calendar for 2026-27.

The district's primary communication channel for families is asd20.org. Budget decisions, enrollment processes, and curriculum updates for the coming school year will continue to flow through normal district channels.

What to Watch For

The 2026-27 school year will be Dr. Field's first as the district's top leader. Leadership transitions at the superintendent level tend to surface in a few predictable areas: budget priorities, curriculum emphasis, and how the district communicates with families. Field's background in learning services suggests curriculum and instruction decisions are likely to be areas where her perspective shapes district direction.

The board's removal of Haberer and the speed of Field's appointment also signals that the board is paying close attention to district leadership. D20 families who want to stay informed have a few concrete options: follow asd20.org for official district communications, attend board meetings (schedules are posted on the district website), and watch for enrollment and budget announcements as the 2026-27 school year takes shape.

What's Next

Dr. Susan Field serves as D20 interim superintendent beginning with the 2026-27 school year. The district has not announced a timeline for a permanent superintendent search. For D20 school and district updates, visit asd20.org. To stay current on board decisions and meetings, the D20 board meeting calendar is also available at asd20.org.
