COS Parks Survey Is Open Now — Help Shape Playgrounds and Trails for the Next 15 Years

The City of Colorado Springs wants your input before in-person meetings start June 24

By Sarah Morales

June 17, 2026

The City of Colorado Springs has opened the community engagement phase of its 2026 Park System Master Plan — and this is one survey where Colorado Springs families with kids actually have something specific to say. The plan will guide how the city cares for, improves, and invests in its parks, trails, open spaces, and recreation services for the next 10 to 15 years. Your feedback goes in now; the priorities it shapes will be felt at playgrounds, trail access points, and community centers for more than a decade.

What the Plan Covers

The Park System Master Plan is a long-range document developed by Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services that guides city investment across all public parks, neighborhood open spaces, trails, recreation centers, and cultural programming in Colorado Springs. When the city decides where to build a new playground, which trails to expand, where to add restrooms, and how to fund recreation programs — those decisions all reference the Master Plan.

The current planning process is Phase 2, following a system assessment phase that focused on understanding where COS parks are today. This phase is about where residents want to go. The city has already gathered some early signal from the community: the most commonly cited needs so far are more restrooms and drinking fountains at existing parks — which tracks for anyone who's shown up at a playground with young children and found the only bathroom facility closed or out of reach.

The Interactive Map

One of the more useful features of this process is an interactive map available through the survey portal. Instead of writing a general comment about "parks," residents can pinpoint a specific location — a playground in Briargate, a trail access point near Ute Valley Park, a recreation center in the southeast — and flag the exact concern or improvement they want to highlight.

For families who have a specific park they use regularly, this is a direct line to getting that park on the city's improvement radar. Comments tied to specific geographic locations carry more weight in the planning process than general feedback because planners can evaluate them against existing data about park usage and condition in that area.

Public Meetings June 24-25 and July 9

If you want to go beyond the survey, the city is holding in-person public engagement sessions on June 24-25 and July 9. These sessions are an opportunity to hear from planners directly, ask questions, and make your input part of the formal record. Specific locations and times for each meeting are available at coloradosprings.gov/ParkSystemMasterPlan.

The June 24-25 meetings are a week away — if you're considering attending in person, it's worth checking the survey first so your online response is in before the public meetings begin.

What's Next

The online survey remains open through August 3, 2026. The city will use community input alongside park system data to develop the master plan document, which will then go through a city review process before final adoption. To take the survey or find meeting details, visit coloradosprings.gov/ParkSystemMasterPlan. To contact Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services directly, visit coloradosprings.gov/PRCS.
