Western Street Breakfast 2026: What Families Need to Know About Colorado Springs' Downtown June Tradition

Free to attend, $5 for breakfast, and the Kid's Corral is back — here's the full rundown for this morning's event

By Jamie Rivera

June 17, 2026

Every June 17, downtown Colorado Springs looks different than it does the other 364 days of the year. Pikes Peak Avenue and Tejon Street close to traffic, the smell of a hot Western breakfast takes over several city blocks, and thousands of Colorado Springs residents show up early — some in cowboy hats — to take part in one of the city's most enduring annual traditions. The Colorado Springs Western Street Breakfast is back for 2026, and it runs from 5:30 AM to 9 AM this morning.

What the Event Is

The Western Street Breakfast is a Colorado Springs tradition that dates back decades, organized each year to coincide with the Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo season. The event celebrates Colorado Springs' Western heritage and draws a broad cross-section of the community — longtime residents who have attended for years alongside families introducing their children to the event for the first time.

Admission is free. The breakfast itself is served via token — $5 per person gets you a plate of hot food. Children under 5 eat for free. The format keeps the event accessible: no complicated ticketing, no reserved seating, just street food and community on a summer Tuesday morning.

Kid's Corral

For families attending with younger children, the Kid's Corral is the draw within the draw. The corral brings together a set of Western-themed activities specifically designed for kids, including a petting zoo, steer roping demonstrations, and a Western dress-up contest where children can put on period-appropriate Western attire and participate in a judged costume competition.

These aren't passive activities — the steer roping component in particular gives kids a chance to get hands-on with something that isn't part of the typical Colorado Springs summer programming calendar. For families looking for an experience that's genuinely different from the standard weekend outing, the Kid's Corral delivers that.

Live Entertainment

Two acts are scheduled for this year's Western Street Breakfast. Exit West provides live music in a Western-influenced style, and the Flying W Wranglers — a Colorado Springs institution with decades of Western music history — round out the entertainment lineup. Both acts perform on the street during the event's 5:30-to-9-AM window.

The Flying W Wranglers have been a fixture of Colorado Springs' Western identity since 1953. Their presence at the Western Street Breakfast is one of the elements that gives the event a sense of genuine continuity with the city's past.

Downtown Logistics

Pikes Peak Avenue and Tejon Street are closed for the event, which means parking and transit planning is worth doing in advance if you're heading downtown. The street closure covers the core event footprint, and the event draws a large enough crowd that arriving closer to the 5:30 AM start tends to be easier for families who want to settle in before the breakfast rush.

The event ends at 9 AM, which means it's wrapped up well before the typical downtown weekday work commute gets into full swing. For families with school-age children who are already up early, the timing works cleanly as a morning outing that's done before midday.

If You Missed It

The Western Street Breakfast happens once a year, always on June 17. If today's event didn't work for your schedule, the date to save is June 17, 2027. The event has run consistently enough that it functions as a reliable anchor on the Colorado Springs community calendar — one of those events that families tend to come back to year after year once they've attended once.
