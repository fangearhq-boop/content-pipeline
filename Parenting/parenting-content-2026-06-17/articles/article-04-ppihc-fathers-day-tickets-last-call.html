Pikes Peak Hill Climb Is This Sunday — Tickets Are Online Only and Expected to Sell Out

Race Day falls on Father's Day this year, and if you haven't bought tickets yet, now is the time — they're not available at the gate

By Sarah Morales

June 17, 2026

The 2026 Pikes Peak International Hill Climb is four days away, and there's one piece of information that catches first-timers off guard every single year: tickets are not sold at the gate. Not in person, not at any Pikes Peak location, not anywhere except online. If your family is planning to go this Sunday — which also happens to be Father's Day — the window to lock in tickets is right now.

Why Tickets at the Gate Don't Exist

The Pikes Peak Highway is a mountain road with limited access and controlled entry points. The race shuts down the highway entirely for Race Day, and spectator access is managed through specific designated viewing areas. Capacity at each area is finite and the Hill Climb organization manages attendance through advance ticket sales only — Humanitix handles the ticketing platform at ppihc.org.

Post-June 1 pricing is already in effect, which means ticket prices have increased from the early-bird rates available earlier this year. Tickets are expected to sell out before Sunday, and in past years some viewing areas have sold out well in advance of Race Day. If you're going, buying today rather than Saturday is the safer move.

Kids Under 10 Are Free

One of the things that makes Race Day accessible for Colorado Springs families is the children's admission policy. Kids under 10 get in free on Race Day. For a family with younger children, that significantly reduces the cost compared to other major motorsports events — adults pay the going Race Day rate, and children ride along at no additional charge.

The green flag drops at 7:30 AM, which means an early morning. Gates at the higher spectator areas open as early as 2:30 AM for families who want first access to the best viewing positions. Bringing young children to a pre-dawn highway access point at high elevation is not for everyone, but for families who have done it before, the early commitment is considered part of the event experience.

Spectator Areas and What to Expect

There are seven designated spectator areas along the Pikes Peak Highway. The most dramatic — and most discussed — is Devil's Playground at 12,780 feet elevation, where cars navigate a section of the course above the treeline with long sightlines in both directions. Conditions at that elevation can be dramatically different from conditions in Colorado Springs: cold temperatures, wind, and afternoon thunderstorms are all possibilities even in June. Families attending any spectator area above 10,000 feet should plan accordingly with layers, sun protection, and rain gear.

Lower spectator areas offer more moderate conditions and are more accessible for families with young children or anyone not accustomed to high-altitude exposure. The tradeoff is proximity to the race action. Ticket pages on ppihc.org list the specific areas available for purchase and include descriptions of what each location offers.

Fan Fest This Thursday — Free

If Race Day logistics feel like a lot for your family right now, there's a lower-commitment option this week. Fan Fest takes place Thursday, June 19, from 5 to 9 PM in downtown Colorado Springs across 10 city blocks. It's free to attend and brings together Hill Climb cars and drivers in a street festival format — a chance to see the vehicles up close without the early morning highway access, altitude, and logistics of Race Day itself.

For families with children who want to see the cars or who need a preview before committing to the full Race Day experience, Fan Fest is a practical introduction. It also works as a standalone event for families who won't make Race Day.
