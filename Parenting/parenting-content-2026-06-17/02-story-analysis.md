## STORY 1: Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower Recall

**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Freshness Tags:** recall-action, fall-hazard, kitchen-helper-tower

**Summary:** Over 25,000 Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Towers have been recalled due to a platform that can loosen and detach, creating a fall hazard. Three children have already been injured. The remedy is a free repair kit — not a return — so families need to stop using the product and contact Guidecraft directly.

**Relevance to COS Families:** Kitchen Helper Towers are popular with families who want toddlers to participate in cooking and meal prep. They're a $200 item sold at major retailers. COS families may have purchased through Amazon, Target, or Walmart. The repair option means they don't lose the product — they just need to act.

**Angles:**
1. The hazard — what specifically goes wrong with the platform
2. Who's affected — product description, retailers, dates
3. What to do — free repair kit, how to contact Guidecraft
4. How to tell if yours is recalled — colors, model types
5. Related trend — this joins a string of baby/toddler product recalls in 2026

**Engagement Hooks:**
- Question: "Has anyone dealt with a Guidecraft tower recall before? How was the repair process?"
- Hot take: "A $200 tower sold at Target and Amazon — and the fix is a free repair kit you have to request yourself. Know the difference between a recall and a repair."

**Audience Sentiment:**
- Affected parents: alarmed; need clear action steps
- Parents who don't own it: grateful for the heads-up; will warn friends
- Safety-focused parents: frustrated that a platform can loosen on a product designed for toddlers

**Headlines:**
1. Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower Recall: 25,000+ Units, Free Repair Available — Stop Use Now
2. Is Your Toddler's Kitchen Helper Tower on This List? Guidecraft Recall Affects 25,000 Units
