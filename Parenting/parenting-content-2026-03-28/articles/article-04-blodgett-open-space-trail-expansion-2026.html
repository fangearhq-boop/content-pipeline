Blodgett Open Space Is Getting 14 New Miles of Trails This Summer

By COS Parenting | March 28, 2026

If your family has been looking for a new place to hike, ride, or just get outside in Colorado Springs, the northwest side of the city is about to get a whole lot more interesting.

Work is underway to expand Blodgett Open Space, adding 14 miles of new and improved trails to the 384-acre open space that sits adjacent to Pike National Forest. The full project is expected to wrap by end of summer 2026, with trails opening in phases as construction progresses.

What's Being Built

The trail expansion was shaped by direct community feedback, and that input is visible in the design. The city is creating two types of dedicated trail experiences:

  • Hiking-only trails on the northern portion of Blodgett, for families, walkers, and trail runners who want spaces free of bike traffic
  • Mountain bike-only trails on the southern portion, accessed from a brand-new Quarry Trailhead — designed for riders who want more technical riding without pedestrian conflicts

The separation makes Blodgett a much more practical family destination than trail systems where bikes and walkers are constantly navigating around each other.

Parking: A Real Upgrade

One of the most welcome improvements for families is the major parking expansion. Current parking at Blodgett has been insufficient during peak times, with overflow pushing onto Woodmen Road. The expansion addresses this directly:

  • New North Trailhead: 90 new parking spaces
  • New Quarry Trailhead: 50-stall parking lot

Together that's 140-plus new spaces, which should make weekend visits significantly less stressful.

The Bigger Picture

The Blodgett expansion is part of a longer-term vision for Colorado Springs' trail network. One of the stated goals is to eventually connect Blodgett Open Space to Wilson Ranch Park and the regional trail system, turning it into a hub that families can access from multiple directions on foot or by bike.

The project is funded through the city's Trails, Open Space and Parks (TOPS) program and a $250,000 State Trails Grant from Colorado Parks and Wildlife. Illegal social trails will also be decommissioned as part of the project, allowing the land to restore naturally.

What Families Should Know Before Visiting

Trail openings will be phased throughout summer 2026. There may be temporary, isolated trail closures in active construction zones — so it's worth checking current trail status before loading up the family and driving out. The city's official page at coloradosprings.gov/BlodgettOpenSpace will have the latest updates.

Blodgett is on the northwest side of Colorado Springs. It neighbors the Pike National Forest, which means once new trails open and connect, families will have access to a genuinely expansive outdoor area relatively close to the city.

What's Next

Trail openings are expected to begin this spring and continue through summer 2026 as construction crews complete sections. The new trailheads and parking lots are part of the same timeline.
