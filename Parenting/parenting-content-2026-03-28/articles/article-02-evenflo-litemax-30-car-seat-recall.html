Evenflo LiteMax 30 Car Seat Recall: The Spanish Manual Has Wrong Limits

By COS Parenting | March 28, 2026

If your family received an infant car seat at a Colorado Springs or El Paso County car seat check event in the past year, there's an important recall to be aware of.

Evenflo recalled approximately 14,020 LiteMax 30 Factory Select infant car seats on March 3, 2026 (NHTSA Recall ID: 26C002). The issue is specific: the Spanish-language instruction manual contains incorrect weight and height limits. The good news is the seat itself is structurally sound — and if your family uses the English manual or reads the label on the seat, the correct limits are right there.

What the Error Is — and Why It Matters

The Spanish manual states that the seat can hold infants weighing 4 to 35 pounds. The actual weight limits are 3 to 30 pounds, with a height limit of 15.7 to 32 inches. The difference matters because installing or using the seat for a child outside those correct parameters can reduce its protective effectiveness in a crash.

The English manual and the label printed on the seat itself carry the correct limits. If your family doesn't rely on the Spanish manual, the seat functions as designed.

Who Has These Seats

This is not a retail recall. These seats were distributed exclusively through car seat check events and community programs — not through stores. If you purchased a car seat at a baby retailer, this recall does not affect you.

Car seat check events are common at fire stations, health departments, pediatric clinics, and community centers across Colorado Springs and El Paso County. If you or a family member received a seat at one of these events between November 2024 and January 2026, check the manual language.

What to Do

Evenflo is mailing corrected Spanish-language manuals free of charge to affected owners. Owner notification letters are expected to be sent by March 31, 2026.

If you want to confirm whether your seat is included in the recall, the affected model number is CS100111198, manufactured between November 1, 2024 and January 28, 2026. You can also contact Evenflo directly:

  • Phone: 1-800-233-5921 (9 AM to 5 PM PT, Monday–Friday)
  • Website: evenflo.com

The seat does not need to be returned or destroyed. Once you receive the corrected manual, you can continue using the seat normally.

What's Next

If you have neighbors, family members, or anyone in your network who may have received a seat at a community car seat event — especially Spanish-speaking families who may not have received notification through English-language channels — please share this information. The reach of community distribution programs means many affected families may not realize their seat is part of this recall.

For the latest on the recall, visit the NHTSA recall database (nhtsa.gov) and search recall ID 26C002, or go directly to evenflo.com.
