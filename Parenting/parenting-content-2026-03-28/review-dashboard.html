Evenflo LiteMax 30 Car Seat Recall — 14,020 Seats
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ``` Car seat recall heads-up, especially for families who've received a car seat at a community event or check station. Evenflo recalled about 14,020 LiteMax 30 Factory Select infant car seats on March 3. The good news: the seat itself is perfectly fine. The problem is specifically in the Spanish-language instruction manual, which lists the weight limit as 4-35 pounds — but the correct limits are actually 3-30 pounds for weight and 15.7-32 inches for height. The English manual and the label printed on the seat itself are correct. So if your family uses the English manual, or if you go by what the seat label says, you're good. But if you or a caregiver uses the Spanish manual to guide how you install and size the seat, there's a real safety concern there. These seats weren't sold in retail stores. They were distributed through car seat check events and community programs — which means a lot of families who got them may not know about this recall through regular retail notification channels. What to do: Evenflo is mailing corrected Spanish-language manuals free of charge. Owner letters are expected to go out by March 31. You can also call 1-800-233-5921 (9 AM to 5 PM PT, Monday-Friday) or visit evenflo.com. The affected model number is CS100111198, manufactured between November 2024 and January 2026. If you're not sure about your seat, check the manual language — and share this post with anyone who might have gotten a car seat at a community event recently. ``` ### Image Post Caption #### Image Caption -- 10:00 AM MT ``` Car seat recall alert: 14,020 Evenflo LiteMax 30 seats. Blodgett Open Space — 14 Miles of New Trails Coming Summer 2026
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ``` Good news for COS families who love getting outside: Blodgett Open Space is getting a major upgrade this summer. If you haven't been to Blodgett, it's 384 acres of trails and open space on the northwest side of Colorado Springs, right next to Pike National Forest. It's one of those spots that feels a little more tucked-away than Garden of the Gods or Cheyenne Canyon — but that's part of the appeal. This year, crews are adding 14 miles of new and improved trails, broken into two types the community asked for: dedicated hiking-only trails on the north side, and mountain bike-only trails coming off a brand-new quarry trailhead in the south. Both trailheads are getting serious parking upgrades — 90 new spaces at the North trailhead and a new 50-stall lot at the quarry — which should help with the overflow parking that's been spilling onto Woodmen Road. Trails will open in phases throughout summer 2026, so you can start checking in on what's open as the season progresses. There may be some temporary, isolated closures in active construction zones, so it's worth checking the city's Blodgett page before you go. The full project is expected to wrap by end of summer. The longer vision is even cooler: eventually, this whole open space will connect to Wilson Ranch Park and the regional trail network, turning Blodgett into a real hub in the city's trail system. Budget: funded by the TOPS program and a $250,000 State Trails Grant from Colorado Parks and Wildlife. Have you hiked Blodgett before? What do you think about the dedicated bike vs. hiking trail split? ``` ### Image Post Caption #### Image Caption -- 10:00 AM MT ``` 14 miles of new trails coming to Blodgett Open Space this summer. AAP Updates Anaphylaxis Action Plan for 2026
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ``` If your child has food allergies, this spring update from the American Academy of Pediatrics is worth a few minutes of your time. The AAP has released an updated North American Pediatric Anaphylaxis Action Plan — the first major update in years. What makes this version different: First, it now includes nasal epinephrine (brand name: Neffy) as an option alongside the traditional auto-injector. Neffy was FDA-approved in 2023 and some families find it easier to use, especially for younger children. If your child's allergist hasn't brought this up yet, it's worth asking about. Second, the plan now includes preference-sensitive guidance — meaning it gives parents and caregivers more flexibility in how they manage the post-epinephrine window, rather than one-size-fits-all instructions. It also includes age-specific guidance on how anaphylaxis can present differently in children at different stages of development. 86% of the healthcare stakeholders who reviewed the plan said they'd recommend it for use. Why mention this in late March? Spring is when allergy exposure risk goes up — outdoor events, school field trips, end-of-year activities, and parties where food control is harder. If you haven't looked at your child's action plan recently, this is a good moment to pull it out and make sure your school, caregivers, and activity leaders have the current version. The AAP also released updated school food allergy management guidelines in late 2025 — worth sharing with your child's school nurse at the start of Q4. Does your child have an anaphylaxis action plan on file at school? It's a 5-minute conversation that could matter a lot. ``` ### Image Post Caption #### Image Caption -- 10:00 AM MT ``` AAP updated its pediatric anaphylaxis plan for 2026.
