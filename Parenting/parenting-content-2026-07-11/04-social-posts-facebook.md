### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — WonderStone Infant Walkers (~70 Units)

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

⚠️ RECALL: If you bought a WonderStone Infant Walker on Walmart.com this past spring, stop using it right now.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 70 WonderStone Infant Walkers. The walkers were sold exclusively on Walmart.com in April 2026 for around $80. The hazard is specific and serious: the walker can pass through a standard doorway without stopping, and it fails to halt at the edge of a step. That's a fall hazard that can result in serious injury.

The recall also cites a violation of the mandatory safety standard for infant walkers — meaning this product shouldn't have been sold in the first place.

What to do: stop using the walker immediately, and contact Wonder Stone Toys directly for a full refund. You don't need to return the product through Walmart — the remedy comes directly from Wonder Stone Toys.

The recall volume is small — about 70 units — so most families won't have one. But if you or someone you know bought a WonderStone walker from Walmart.com in April, please check it now and share this post.

Have you seen this product? Did you buy one earlier this year? Let us know in the comments.

### STORY 2: Colorado's New Phone-in-Schools Law

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

Colorado just hit a major milestone in student phone policy — and it affects every COS family heading into fall 2026.

Under HB25-1135 ("Communication Devices in Schools"), all Colorado public school districts were required to publish a student device policy by July 1, 2026. That deadline has now passed. The actual restrictions take effect when school resumes in fall, but the rules are already set.

Here's where COS districts stand. D49 approved their policy back in July 2025 — they were already ahead of the curve. Their policy bans phones for K-8 students during the school day. High schoolers in D49 can use their phones at lunch. D11 was widely cited as a model district even before the law passed and already has a published cell phone policy in place. D20 is subject to the same requirement; we're watching for their updated policy given the leadership transition this summer.

What this law does NOT do: it doesn't mandate a single statewide rule. Every district gets to define what "phone-free" means for their students — so the rules will vary depending on where your kid goes to school.

Before school starts this fall, it's worth pulling up your district's website and reading the actual policy. The rules are posted publicly now.

Does your family have thoughts on phone-free school days? We'd love to hear what you think in the comments.

### STORY 3: Pikes Peak or Bust Parade — TODAY

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

The Pikes Peak or Bust Parade is happening TODAY — and honestly, we're just glad it's still on. 🎉

The 2026 parade steps off at 11 AM along Tejon Street in downtown Colorado Springs, running from St. Vrain Street to Vermijo Avenue. This year's theme is "Purple Mountains, Silver Spurs," and Kaylee Gripentrog, the 2026 Girl of the West for the Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo, serves as Grand Marshal.

Here's the backstory that makes this morning feel a little more special: on June 19, parade organizers announced they were cancelling this year's event. A funding gap of just over $30,000 — against a total cost of more than $50,000 — made it impossible to proceed. For two days, it looked like the 2026 parade was done.

Then the community showed up. UCHealth stepped in as title sponsor. AMR and Bank of Colorado's Dan Nordberg added support. City Council President Lynette Crow-Iverson, Council Member Brandy Williams, and Chief of Staff Wayne Williams identified and committed additional city funding. By June 21, the parade was back on.

It's free to watch from the parade route. If you're heading downtown this morning, grab a spot on Tejon and enjoy it. The rodeo itself runs July 14-18 at Norris Penrose Event Center if you want to keep the Pikes Peak or Bust spirit going all week.

Are you headed to the parade today? Tell us where you're watching from!

### STORY 5: Heat Advisory Through Monday — 97-102°F

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM MT

If you've got outdoor plans this weekend, check the forecast first — Colorado Springs is under a heat advisory through midnight Monday, and the numbers are significant.

The National Weather Service has issued a heat advisory for Colorado Springs and El Paso County from noon Saturday, July 11 through midnight Monday night, July 14. Forecast highs: 97-102°F. For context, the average July high for Colorado Springs is around 87°F — we're looking at temperatures 10-15 degrees above normal this weekend.

Red Flag conditions are also in effect for El Paso County, combining low humidity and gusty winds with the extreme heat. That raises wildfire risk alongside the heat concerns.

For families with kids, the practical guidance is straightforward. Limit outdoor activities during peak heat hours from 2-5 PM if you can. When you do go outside, hydrate early and often — don't wait until kids say they're thirsty. Watch for signs of heat exhaustion in children: heavy sweating, pale skin, fast heartbeat, dizziness, or nausea. And never, ever leave a child or pet in a parked car. Even with a window cracked, a car interior can reach dangerous temperatures in minutes.

If you're looking for a cool place to spend the afternoon, the Pikes Peak Library District branches and Recreation Centers of Colorado Springs locations are air-conditioned and free or low-cost to access.

Stay cool out there, COS. How are your families beating the heat this weekend?
