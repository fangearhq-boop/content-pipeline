- **Tier:** 1

### STORY 2: Colorado's New Phone-in-Schools Law — What COS District Policies Say
- **Tier:** 1
- **Pillar:** Local News
- **Byline:** Jamie Rivera
- **Summary:** Colorado's HB25-1135 ("Communication Devices in Schools") required all public school districts to publish a student device policy by July 1, 2026. The actual restrictions take effect when school resumes in fall 2026. In Colorado Springs: D49 approved a policy in July 2025 banning phones K-8 during the school day (high schoolers can use phones at lunch). D11 was already held up as a model district before the law passed. D20 is under the same requirement but has faced leadership disruption this summer. This is the first time Colorado has required a statewide standard for addressing phones in schools.
- **Angle:** What COS parents need to know before fall — district-by-district breakdown of what HB25-1135 means locally.

### STORY 5: Heat Advisory Through Monday — 97-102°F in COS
- **Tier:** 2
- **Pillar:** Local News
- **Byline:** Sarah Morales
- **Summary:** A National Weather Service heat advisory is in effect for Colorado Springs and El Paso County from noon Saturday, July 11 through midnight Monday, July 14.
