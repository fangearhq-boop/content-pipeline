The Pikes Peak or Bust Parade Is Today — And It Almost Didn't Happen

By Sarah Morales

July 11, 2026 · Colorado Springs

The 2026 Pikes Peak or Bust Parade steps off at 11 AM today along Tejon Street in downtown Colorado Springs. The parade runs from St. Vrain Street south to Vermijo Avenue and is free to watch from the route. If you're in the area this morning, there's a reason this one feels a little more meaningful than usual.

This Parade Almost Didn't Happen

On June 19, 2026, organizers announced they were cancelling this year's parade. The reason was financial: a funding gap of just over $30,000 against a total event cost of more than $50,000. After years of running the parade as a Colorado Springs tradition, they didn't have enough committed funding to proceed. For two days, the 2026 parade was officially off.

Then the community responded. UCHealth stepped in as title sponsor, providing a significant anchor commitment. AMR (American Medical Response) and Bank of Colorado, through Dan Nordberg, added their support. On the city side, City Council President Lynette Crow-Iverson, City Council Member Brandy Williams, and Chief of Staff Wayne Williams — working on behalf of Mayor Yemi Mobolade's office — identified and committed additional city funding to close the gap. By June 21, the parade was back on.

It was two days between cancellation and restoration. The fact that this morning's parade exists at all is a credit to the businesses and city officials who moved fast to make it happen.

The Rodeo Continues All Week

Today's parade kicks off Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo week, which runs July 14 through 18 at the Norris Penrose Event Center. The rodeo has been a Colorado Springs tradition since 1937. Evening performances run Tuesday through Saturday at 7:30 PM; matinee shows are at 12:30 PM on Friday and Saturday. The Fan Zone includes Mutton Bustin' for kids ages four through nine (under 50 pounds, no pre-registration needed), pony rides, a petting zoo, and face painting. A free drone show follows the performance on Thursday, July 16.

Tickets for the rodeo are available at ppbrodeo.com.

What's Next

If you're heading downtown this morning, plan to arrive before 11 AM to find a spot along Tejon Street — the full parade route runs about a mile. Street parking fills quickly near the parade route on event mornings, so give yourself extra time or consider parking a few blocks off the route and walking.

After today's parade, the rodeo week at Norris Penrose Event Center begins Tuesday, July 14. Tickets and the full schedule are at ppbrodeo.com. Whether you're a longtime parade regular or this is your first year watching from the curb, enjoy it — this one took real community effort to pull off.
