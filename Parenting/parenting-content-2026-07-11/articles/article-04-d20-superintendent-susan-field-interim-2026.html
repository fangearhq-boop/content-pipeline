Academy District 20 Enters the 2026-27 School Year with a New Interim Superintendent

By Jamie Rivera

July 11, 2026 · Colorado Springs

As Colorado Springs families prepare for the 2026-27 school year, Academy District 20 is doing so under new leadership. Dr. Susan Field, who previously served as D20's Assistant Superintendent for Learning Services, is now the district's interim superintendent. For families in D20 — one of the largest school districts in the Pikes Peak region — here's what this transition means and what we know so far.

What Happened With the Previous Superintendent

The D20 Board of Education terminated former superintendent Jinger Haberer on May 14-15, 2026. The board cited two specific grounds: insubordination and a compromise of attorney-client privilege. The specific incident that triggered the termination was Haberer's decision to invite an unauthorized individual to a special board meeting held on May 7, 2026. The board determined that action was both insubordinate and a breach of the privilege protecting board communications with legal counsel.

The termination was abrupt and drew significant community attention at the time. Haberer had led D20 through a period of enrollment and facilities challenges, and her departure mid-year in the administrative calendar created an immediate need for continuity at the top of the district.

Who Is Dr. Susan Field

Dr. Susan Field is not an outsider brought in to stabilize the district — she is a known D20 leader who stepped into the interim role from within. As Assistant Superintendent for Learning Services, she oversaw academic programs and instructional leadership across D20. That background means she enters the superintendent role with existing relationships with principals, curriculum directors, and the district's instructional teams.

The D20 board has indicated Dr. Field will serve as interim superintendent through the full 2026-27 school year. That decision signals the board's intention to take time with the permanent superintendent search rather than rush a hire. A national search for a permanent superintendent would typically take several months, meaning the search for Haberer's permanent replacement may not conclude until 2027.

What This Means for D20 Families This Fall

D20's day-to-day school operations continue normally. Principals, teachers, and support staff remain in place. The district's back-to-school calendar and academic calendar for 2026-27 have not been changed by the leadership transition.

For families looking to connect with the district this summer, D20 is hosting a Back to School Job Fair on July 23. The event is for families and prospective employees looking for open positions in the district ahead of the new school year.

D20 families with specific questions about their school's programming, staffing, or policies should contact their school principal directly, or reach the district office at asd20.org. Dr. Field's leadership transition does not affect school-level operations or family services.

What's Next

Watch for updates from the D20 board on the timeline for a permanent superintendent search. The board will likely announce a formal search process with community input opportunities — D20 has historically involved families in superintendent selection through community listening sessions and survey input.

For now, Dr. Field is the district's superintendent of record for the 2026-27 school year. D20 families can find district communications, school calendars, and contact information at asd20.org. The D20 Back to School Job Fair on July 23 is also a good opportunity to connect with district staff and get questions answered ahead of the fall.
