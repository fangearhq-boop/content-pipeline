CPSC Recalls WonderStone Infant Walkers: What Colorado Springs Parents Need to Know

By Sarah Morales

July 11, 2026 · Colorado Springs

If you purchased a WonderStone Infant Walker from Walmart.com this spring, stop using it now. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 70 of these walkers after finding a serious fall hazard — and a violation of mandatory federal safety standards.

The walkers were sold exclusively on Walmart.com in April 2026 for approximately $80. The recall volume is small, but the hazard is real, and if your family has one of these products, the action required is immediate.

The Hazard: Doorways and Step Edges

The specific problem with the WonderStone Infant Walker is its failure to stop at two common household barriers. First, the walker can pass through a standard doorway — something infant walkers are designed to prevent. Second, the product fails to stop at the edge of a step, which means a child in the walker can roll off a stair or landing before a caregiver can intervene.

The CPSC's recall also cites a violation of the mandatory federal safety standard for infant walkers. That standard exists precisely because of hazards like this one. When a product is recalled for mandatory standards non-compliance, it means the product should never have been sold in the first place — not that something eventually went wrong with an otherwise-compliant product.

No injuries have been reported in connection with this recall, but the fall risk described — a child rolling off a step — carries potential for serious injury or death. The CPSC doesn't issue recalls of this type speculatively.

What to Do Right Now

If you have this walker, take it out of use immediately — don't wait until you've sorted out the refund process. The CPSC's remedy is straightforward: stop using the product and contact Wonder Stone Toys directly to request a full refund. You won't need to process the refund through Walmart; Wonder Stone Toys is handling the remedy.

To confirm you have the recalled product, look for the brand name "Wonder Stone Toys" on the product itself. The sale window was narrow — April 2026, on Walmart.com only — so the universe of affected purchases is limited. If you received this as a gift and aren't certain of the purchase date or retailer, default to caution and stop use until you've confirmed.

A Note on Small Recalls

Approximately 70 units is a genuinely small recall. That context matters — most Colorado Springs families won't encounter this product. But small recall volumes don't reduce the urgency for families who do have one. The hazard is the same whether 70 or 70,000 units are involved.

Infant walker safety has been a consistent CPSC focus for decades. The agency actually banned the sale of traditional infant walkers in Canada years ago due to the stair-fall hazard profile. The U.S. maintains a mandatory standard rather than a ban, but that standard is strict, and a product that doesn't meet it carries real risk.

What's Next

Contact Wonder Stone Toys directly for your refund — look for contact information on the CPSC's official recall notice at cpsc.gov. If you're searching for an infant walker replacement, look for products that carry documentation of CPSC compliance with the current mandatory standard. The CPSC recall database at cpsc.gov lists current and recent recalls and can help you check a product before purchasing.

If you know a family who purchased a WonderStone walker from Walmart.com this spring, please share this information. With only about 70 units in circulation, word-of-mouth reaches people the official recall notice might miss.
