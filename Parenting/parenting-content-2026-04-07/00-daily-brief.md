### STORY 4: AAP vs. CDC: What the 2026 Vaccine Schedule Split Means for COS Families

**Tier:** 1 (Breaking/National)
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Angle:** For the first time, the American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) and the CDC are publishing different recommended childhood vaccination schedules. The AAP recommends vaccinations for 18 diseases; the CDC's revised schedule covers 11. Key differences: COVID-19 (AAP recommends universal; CDC makes it "shared decision-making") and HPV (AAP recommends 2 doses ages 9-12; CDC recommends 1 dose). The AAP schedule is endorsed by 12 medical associations including the AMA.
**Why it matters:** COS parents will encounter this at their child's next well-child visit. Knowing the difference empowers parents to have informed conversations with their pediatrician.
**Key facts (HIGH confidence from AAP official + AJMC + CHOC + multiple medical outlets):**
- AAP 2026 schedule: 18 diseases; CDC 2026 schedule: 11 diseases
- AAP: COVID universal for 6-23 months + risk-based 2-17; CDC: shared clinical decision-making
- AAP: HPV 2 doses starting ages 9-12; CDC: 1 dose ages 11-12
- AAP statement: CDC changes "depart from longstanding medical evidence"
- AAP schedule endorsed by 12 medical associations including AMA, ACOG, IDSA
