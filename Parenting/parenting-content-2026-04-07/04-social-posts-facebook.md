### STORY 1: CPSC Recalls — KTEBO Writing Tablets + Zippee Toys

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM MT

COS parents, two toy recalls this week worth checking today 🚨

**First: KTEBO LCD Writing Tablet Toys** (the 8.5" and 10" magnetic drawing tablets sold on Amazon). The screw securing the battery compartment can come loose — and if a young child gets hold of that tiny button cell battery and swallows it, it can cause internal chemical burns and death. About 10,380 units were sold on Amazon between September and October 2025, priced $8-$30. If you have one: stop use now, and contact writing-tablet-recall@ktebo.com to get a free replacement set.

**Second: Zippee Silicone Activity Toys by Mobi Games** (the cylinder-shaped fidget with ball-ended silicone strings). More than 117,000 units were sold at Amazon and specialty toy stores between November 2019 and January 2024. The strings can reach the back of a child's throat — there's already one confirmed incident of a child gagging and vomiting. If you have one, stop use and contact Mobi Games for a full refund. Check the date code inside the cylinder: if it reads between 030620 and 110823 (in MMDDYY format), it's affected.

Both recalls are confirmed on cpsc.gov. Button cell batteries especially are one of the most dangerous household hazards for young children — even a few seconds of access is enough for an ingestion incident.

### STORY 2: LFTE Playground Swings + YITA Dresser Recalls

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

COS families — two more recalls to know about this week, and one of them is a bit different than most.

**LFTE USA Playground Swing Set Seats (model 999604)** — this one matters because the swings weren't sold to parents at Target. They were sold directly to playground equipment manufacturers like Miracle Recreation and Little Tikes Commercial, meaning they ended up in parks and schools. About 7,200 of these seats were placed between January and September 2025. The rivets supporting the seat can fail, causing a child to fall. There's already been one injury reported.

So what do you do? You can't go pull the swing yourself. But if you're at a park and see swing seats labeled model 999604, report it to your local parks department or your school's facilities team. You can also flag it to CPSC. Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services runs many playgrounds across the city.

**YITA, Dextrus, ModFusion, Uforic, Yintatech, and Yitahome fabric-frame dressers** (the 16-drawer fabric/metal kind often marketed as kids' room furniture). About 6,800 units were sold on Amazon and Walmart between September 2023 and August 2025, priced $75-$100, in gray, black, white, pink, and brown. They violate the STURDY Act mandatory tip-over standard — if not anchored to a wall, they can fall onto a child. If you have one: stop using it unless it's wall-anchored, keep kids away from it, and contact YITA for a full refund.

Have you wall-anchored all the furniture in your kids' rooms? It's one of those things that's easy to put off. 🙈

### STORY 4: AAP vs. CDC Vaccine Schedule Split

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

COS parents, heads up for your next well-child visit 🩺

For the first time, the American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) and the CDC are recommending different childhood vaccination schedules. This is new, and it's worth understanding before you sit down with your pediatrician.

Here's the short version: the AAP's 2026 schedule recommends vaccinations for 18 diseases. The CDC's revised 2026 schedule covers 11. The gap happened because the CDC adjusted its recommendations following a Trump administration directive to align U.S. childhood vaccines more closely with the schedules of 20 peer nations.

The two biggest differences:

**COVID-19:** The AAP recommends universal vaccination for all children ages 6 to 23 months, plus risk-based vaccination for ages 2 to 17. The CDC now places COVID-19 in the "shared clinical decision-making" category — meaning it's not a universal recommendation.

**HPV:** The AAP recommends 2 doses starting as young as age 9. The CDC recommends 1 dose at ages 11-12.

The AAP's position is clear. In a statement, the organization said the CDC changes "depart from longstanding medical evidence and no longer offer the optimal way to prevent illnesses in children." The AAP schedule is endorsed by 12 medical associations, including the American Medical Association, ACOG, and IDSA.

This isn't a reason to panic — it's a reason to be informed. Your pediatrician is your best resource. Ask them which schedule they're following and why. That conversation matters.

If you've got kids ages 6-12 who need to burn off some energy and learn something cool while doing it, Mueller State Park has two programs coming up that are worth the drive 🏔️

**April 18 — Junior Rangers in the Forest:** Kids explore wild plants and animals, and every participant earns an official Mueller Jr. Ranger badge. This one's a hit for the kids who are badge collectors.

Mueller State Park is located in Divide — about an hour from downtown Colorado Springs — and it has more than 50 miles of trails across 5,117 acres. Day-use fees apply; check Colorado Parks and Wildlife (cpw.state.co.us) for current rates and trail conditions. Spring snow patches are possible at Mueller's elevation, so dress in layers and check conditions before you go.

While we're on the topic of trails: if you use the Rock Island Trail (along Sand Creek) or the Pikes Peak Greenway trail near Corporate Drive and the Park and Ride, you may start seeing construction activity in the coming weeks. The city's 2026 TOPS plan includes a new 210-foot pedestrian bridge on the Rock Island Trail and trail repaving on the Greenway — both improvement projects, but expect some detours.
