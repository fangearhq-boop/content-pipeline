Mueller State Park Junior Rangers Programs and COS Spring Trail Updates for Families

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 7, 2026

Spring in Colorado Springs brings some of the most enjoyable outdoor time of the year for families — the snow is mostly cleared from lower elevations, the days are warming up, and trail conditions are improving across the region. If you're planning outdoor time in the coming weeks, here's a look at two upcoming programs at Mueller State Park and some updates on city trail projects that may affect your regular routes.

Mueller State Park Junior Rangers: Two Programs This Spring

Mueller State Park, located in Divide in the mountains west of Colorado Springs, offers a low-key but genuinely engaging outdoor education experience for kids ages 6 to 12. Colorado Parks and Wildlife runs two upcoming programs this spring that families might not know about:

Basic Survival for Kids — April 10

This program covers outdoor safety basics — what to do if you get separated from your group, how to signal for help, what to carry on a trail. The program moves indoors if cold weather requires it, so the April 10 date should work even if spring weather is uncooperative.

Junior Rangers in the Forest — April 18

This is the badge-earning program. Kids explore what lives in a mountain forest environment — wild plants, insects, animals, and habitat. Participants who complete the program earn an official Mueller Jr. Ranger badge, which is a nice concrete reward for younger kids who respond well to that kind of accomplishment.

Both programs are geared toward ages 6 to 12. Mueller State Park has a standard Colorado state park day-use fee; check Colorado Parks and Wildlife at cpw.state.co.us for current rates and to confirm registration requirements for these programs before you make the drive.

Mueller State Park covers 5,117 acres and includes more than 50 miles of trails. It's located in the town of Divide, about an hour from downtown Colorado Springs. Spring trail conditions at Mueller's elevation can still include patches of ice or snow in shaded areas — dress in layers and check trail conditions with the park before going.

COS Trail Updates: What to Expect This Spring

The city of Colorado Springs unveiled its 2026 TOPS (Trails, Open Space and Parks) priorities earlier this year, and several projects are anticipated to begin construction in spring 2026. If you use any of these trails regularly, it's worth knowing what's coming.

Rock Island Trail (Sand Creek corridor)

A 210-foot pedestrian bridge over Sand Creek is planned for the Rock Island Trail, along with a pedestrian-activated flashing crossing at Peterson Road. Construction is anticipated to begin this spring and is expected to last 10 to 12 months. Families using the Rock Island Trail should expect construction activity and potential detours along portions of the route.

Pikes Peak Greenway (near Corporate Drive / Park and Ride)

Improvements on approximately half a mile of the Pikes Peak Greenway near Corporate Drive are planned for spring 2026, in partnership with the city's Stormwater Engineering division. The project includes removal of deteriorating asphalt and replacement with new concrete trail, plus improved ADA access.

Austin Bluffs Open Space

The Spine Trail and Enlightenment Trail completions at Austin Bluffs are also on the 2026 TOPS priority list, expanding connected trail access in that area of the city.

What's Next

Mueller State Park programs can be confirmed and registered through Colorado Parks and Wildlife at cpw.state.co.us. For updates on city trail construction timelines and detours, visit coloradosprings.gov/TOPS2026 or the city's Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services page.
