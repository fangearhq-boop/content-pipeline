AAP and CDC Now Have Different Vaccine Schedules: What Colorado Springs Parents Should Know

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 7, 2026

For the first time, two of the most authoritative sources on childhood health in the United States are recommending different vaccination schedules for children. The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) and the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) have each published 2026 childhood immunization schedules that diverge significantly. Here is what the split means for Colorado Springs families heading into spring well-child visits.

The Split: 18 Diseases vs. 11

The AAP's 2026 recommended schedule covers vaccinations for 18 diseases. The CDC's revised 2026 schedule covers 11. That gap of seven diseases represents a real difference in what each organization considers to be the standard of preventive care for children.

The CDC's changes were made following a directive from the Trump administration to align U.S. childhood vaccination recommendations more closely with the schedules of 20 peer nations. The resulting CDC schedule removed some vaccines from the universal recommendation category and placed them in a "shared clinical decision-making" framework, meaning the decision is left to the pediatrician and family.

The AAP did not follow the CDC's lead. In a statement, the AAP said the CDC changes "depart from longstanding medical evidence and no longer offer the optimal way to prevent illnesses in children." The AAP's 2026 schedule is endorsed by 12 major medical organizations, including the American Medical Association, the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists, and the Infectious Diseases Society of America.

What This Means at Your Pediatrician's Office

Colorado Springs pediatricians may now be following either the AAP schedule or the CDC schedule, or a combination informed by their clinical judgment and their practice's protocols. This is not an emergency situation — but it is a reason to ask questions at your child's next appointment.

Questions worth raising with your pediatrician include: Which schedule does your practice follow for the 2026-2027 year? For COVID-19 specifically, what is your recommendation for my child's age group? For children in the HPV age range, has the recommended dosing changed?

Your pediatrician is the right person to help you navigate this decision for your specific child. The divergence between the AAP and CDC is a policy disagreement between organizations, not evidence that vaccines themselves have changed in safety or efficacy.

Where to Find Reliable Information

Both schedules are publicly available:

  • AAP 2026 Immunization Schedule: healthychildren.org (the AAP's parent-facing resource)
  • CDC 2026 Childhood Immunization Schedule: cdc.gov/vaccines

For questions specific to your child, the best resource is your Colorado Springs pediatrician.

What's Next

The AAP and medical associations backing its schedule are expected to continue advocating for pediatricians to follow the broader AAP recommendations. COS families with well-child visits scheduled in spring and summer 2026 are encouraged to review both schedules and bring any questions to their appointment.
