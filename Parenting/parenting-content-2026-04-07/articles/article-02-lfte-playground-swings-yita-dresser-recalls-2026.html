Playground Swing Seats and Kids' Dressers Recalled: What COS Families Should Know

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 7, 2026

Two recent Consumer Product Safety Commission recalls are relevant to Colorado Springs families this spring — one affecting commercial playground equipment installed at parks and schools, the other affecting a style of fabric-frame dresser commonly marketed for children's rooms.

LFTE USA Playground Swing Seats: A Park and School Equipment Recall

LFTE USA has recalled approximately 7,200 playground swing set seats because the rivets supporting the seat can fail, causing a child to fall. At least one injury has already been reported. What makes this recall different from most is that these seats were not sold in retail stores. They were sold directly to playground equipment manufacturers — specifically Miracle Recreation Equipment and Little Tikes Commercial — who install equipment in parks, school yards, and other public spaces.

The affected model is number 999604. The seats come in black, blue, green, red, tan, and yellow. Units were installed between January and September 2025.

Because these seats are on public equipment rather than in people's homes, the action steps look different. If you see swing seats at a Colorado Springs park or school that appear to be this style and are from this timeframe, you can report it to:

  • Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services (for city parks)
  • Your child's school district facilities department (for school playgrounds)
  • The CPSC directly at cpsc.gov/Recalls

YITA Multi-Brand Dresser Recall: Tip-Over Hazard

Approximately 6,800 fabric-and-metal-frame dressers sold under six brand names have been recalled for failing to meet the mandatory STURDY Act standard for clothing storage units. The STURDY Act requires that freestanding dressers be stable enough that they will not tip over onto a child if a drawer is open or a child climbs on them. These dressers do not meet that standard.

The recalled dressers are sold under the brand names YITA, Dextrus, ModFusion, Uforic, Yintatech, and Yitahome. They are 16-drawer units with a fabric exterior and a metal frame — a style often marketed as kids' room or bedroom storage. About 6,800 units were sold on Amazon and Walmart between September 2023 and August 2025, priced between $75 and $100, in gray, black, white, pink, and brown colors.

Tip-over accidents involving dressers and other furniture are among the most common sources of serious furniture-related injuries to young children. Anchoring furniture to the wall is the most effective prevention measure for any dresser, regardless of whether it has been recalled.

A Note on Furniture Anchoring

Whether or not you own one of the recalled dressers, it's worth doing a walk-through of your children's rooms to verify that tall furniture — dressers, bookshelves, wardrobes — is secured to the wall. Wall-anchoring kits are inexpensive and widely available at hardware stores, and installation is typically a straightforward process that takes a few minutes per piece of furniture.

What's Next

For the playground swing recall, contact your local parks department or school district if you believe you've seen model 999604 equipment in the field. For the dresser recall, contact the retailer where you purchased or YITA directly if you own one of the affected products. Full details on both recalls are available at cpsc.gov/Recalls.
