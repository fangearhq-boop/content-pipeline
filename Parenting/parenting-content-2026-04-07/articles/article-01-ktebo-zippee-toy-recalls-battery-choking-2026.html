Two Toy Recalls This Week: KTEBO Writing Tablets and Zippee Silicone Toys

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 7, 2026

Two toy recalls announced in recent weeks are worth checking this week if you have young children at home. The Consumer Product Safety Commission has announced recalls for KTEBO LCD writing tablet toys and Zippee silicone activity toys by Mobi Games — both due to hazards that can cause serious injury or death.

KTEBO Writing Tablet Toys: Button Battery Hazard

KTEBO's LCD magnetic writing tablets — sold in 8.5-inch and 10-inch sizes — have been recalled because the screw securing the battery compartment can come loose. When that happens, a young child can access the button cell battery inside.

Button cell batteries are among the most dangerous household objects for young children. If swallowed, a button cell battery can lodge in the esophagus and cause an internal chemical reaction within two hours, resulting in serious burns and potentially fatal injuries. The danger is that these batteries do not always cause immediate symptoms — a child may not show distress right away.

About 10,380 units were sold on Amazon between September and October 2025, priced between $8 and $30. The tablets come in sets of two and are available in multiple color combinations.

Zippee Silicone Activity Toys: Choking Hazard

Mobi Games' Zippee silicone fidget toy — a cylinder-shaped toy with ball-ended silicone strings — has been recalled because the strings can reach the back of a child's throat, posing a choking risk. There has been at least one confirmed incident of a child gagging and vomiting after contact with the toy.

More than 117,000 units were sold at Amazon, PlayMobi.com, and specialty toy stores between November 2019 and January 2024, at approximately $20 each. To check if your Zippee is affected, look for the date code stamped inside the cylinder: if the code falls between 030620 and 110823 (in month-day-year format), the toy is part of the recall.

What to Do Now

If you have either product, remove it from your child's reach immediately. For the KTEBO tablet, email the recall address above to initiate the free replacement. For the Zippee toy, contact Mobi Games directly to request your refund.

Colorado Springs families can check for all current product recalls — including those covering children's products, juvenile items, and household goods — at cpsc.gov/Recalls. The CPSC also offers a free recall notification service where you can receive direct email alerts for any new recalls that affect products in your home.

What's Next

Both recalls are active and ongoing. If you know another Colorado Springs family who might have these products, share this information. For more recalls affecting COS families, visit cpsc.gov/Recalls or subscribe to CPSC email alerts at cpsc.gov/Newsroom/Subscribe.
