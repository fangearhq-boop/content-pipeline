Target Cat & Jack Children's Sandals Recalled — 211,000 Pairs, Choking Hazard
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post If you bought a pair of tan Cat & Jack sandals from Target for your child this year, we want you to know about this recall right away. Target is recalling about 211,000 pairs of Cat & Jack tan sandals — sizes 5T through 12T — because the decorative plastic pearls on the straps can fall off and become a choking hazard. The CPSC announcement went out Wednesday, July 16. These sandals were sold at Target stores nationwide and on target.com from January through May 2026 for around $20. There have been 23 reports of the pearls detaching. No injuries have been reported, but the CPSC classified the risk as serious enough that parents should stop letting their kids wear these sandals immediately. Here's what to do: Stop using the sandals and call Target at 800-591-3869 to get a full refund. You don't need the receipt — just the sandals (or even just the order confirmation if you bought online). Tag a friend who might have these at home. It only takes one moment. --- ### Image Post Caption RECALL ALERT | Target Cat & Jack tan sandals, sizes 5T–12T. Stop use. Call 800-591-3869 for a full refund. Colorado SUN Bucks — $120 Per Child for Summer Groceries, Apply by Aug. 25
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Did you know Colorado is putting $120 per child on EBT cards this summer for groceries? It's called SUN Bucks, and a lot of families who qualify may not have heard about it yet. The Colorado Summer EBT program gives one-time $120 per eligible school-age child, loaded onto an EBT card that works at grocery stores and farmers markets. Most eligible kids were automatically enrolled — but if your family wasn't, you have until August 25, 2026 to apply. Who qualifies? Children who attend a school with the National School Lunch or Breakfast Program, where family income is up to 185% of the federal poverty level (roughly $59,000 for a family of four). Families already on SNAP, TANF, or Medicaid are automatically eligible. To check your enrollment status or apply, visit cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt or call 800-536-5298. If you have more than one kid who qualifies, it's $120 per child. Share this with someone who might benefit — a lot of families don't know this exists. --- ### Image Post Caption SUN BUCKS | $120 per child for summer groceries via EBT. Apply by Aug. 25 at cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt or call 800-536-5298.

Free Summer Meals for Kids Across Colorado Springs
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Summer meals for kids in Colorado Springs don't require registration, income verification, or paperwork. You just show up. D11 is providing free meals to any child 18 and under in Colorado Springs this summer — regardless of which school district they attend. Across the state, there are more than 600 meal sites open, and the Colorado Department of Education partnered with KidsFoodFinder.org to make finding them as simple as possible. Enter your ZIP code on KidsFoodFinder.org and you'll see nearby sites with addresses, days, and hours. This is a USDA Summer Food Service Program, which means the funding is federal and the rules are consistent: any child 18 and under can walk in and get a free meal. There's no income check, no district requirement, and no need to sign up in advance. If you've been stretching groceries this summer, this is one of those programs worth knowing about. And if you don't need it yourself, there's likely a family in your neighborhood who does. Do you know of a COS family who could use this? Tag them below. --- ### Image Post Caption FREE SUMMER MEALS | Any COS kid 18 and under can get a free meal this summer — no registration needed. Find your nearest site at KidsFoodFinder.org.
