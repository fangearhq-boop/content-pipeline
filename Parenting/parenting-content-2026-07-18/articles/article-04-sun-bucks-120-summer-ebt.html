$120 in Free Summer Groceries Per Child: How COS Families Can Claim Colorado's SUN Bucks

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

July 18, 2026 — Colorado Springs, CO

Colorado is giving $120 per eligible child to families this summer for groceries and farmers market purchases, and a lot of families who qualify may not know they have until August 25 to claim it.

The program is called SUN Bucks — Colorado's version of the federal Summer EBT program. Eligible children receive a one-time $120 loaded onto an EBT card that works at grocery stores and farmers markets across the state. Most eligible families were automatically enrolled when the program launched, but families who weren't auto-enrolled need to apply before the August 25 deadline or they'll lose the benefit.

Quick Reference

Program Name Colorado Summer EBT — SUN Bucks
Benefit Amount $120 per eligible child (one-time, this summer)
Where It Works Grocery stores and farmers markets statewide
Application Deadline August 25, 2026
How to Check/Apply cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt
Phone 800-536-5298
Who Qualifies School-age children at schools with federal meal programs; income up to 185% federal poverty level; also SNAP, TANF, or Medicaid participants

Who Qualifies

Eligibility is based on school and income. Children qualify if they attend a school that participates in the National School Lunch Program or National School Breakfast Program, and their family income is at or below 185% of the federal poverty level — roughly $59,000 per year for a family of four. That threshold covers a significant portion of Colorado Springs families, particularly in neighborhoods served by D11 schools.

Families who are already enrolled in SNAP, TANF, or Medicaid are automatically eligible and should have been auto-enrolled in SUN Bucks already. If you receive any of those benefits and haven't seen a SUN Bucks card arrive, check your enrollment status through cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt or call 800-536-5298.

Children who were identified as eligible through their school's free or reduced-price meal records should also have been auto-enrolled. The situation where families need to actively apply is when their children qualify based on income but weren't captured through the school meal records process — this can happen for families whose children attend private schools that participate in federal meal programs, or for children who recently moved and changed schools.

How to Use SUN Bucks

SUN Bucks work the same way as a regular EBT card. Families use the card at checkout at any grocery store or supermarket that accepts EBT, and at farmers markets that have been approved for EBT transactions. The $120 can be spent in full or in smaller amounts across multiple shopping trips — the balance rolls over as long as it's used before the program expiration date.

The benefit covers the same categories as standard EBT: food for home preparation, including fruits, vegetables, dairy, meat, bread, and cereals. It does not cover hot prepared foods, alcohol, vitamins, or non-food household items.

What's Next

Families who think they may qualify should check the enrollment checker at cdhs.colorado.gov/summer-ebt before the August 25 deadline. The process takes a few minutes and will tell you whether your children are already enrolled, need to apply, or don't qualify based on the information on file.

If you're not sure whether your children qualify, it costs nothing to check. For a family with two eligible children, that's $240 in grocery assistance — worth taking five minutes to find out. Share this with neighbors, childcare providers, and other families in your community who might benefit.
