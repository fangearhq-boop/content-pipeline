Target Recalls Cat & Jack Kids' Sandals — 211,000 Pairs, Choking Hazard from Pearl Decorations

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting

July 18, 2026 — Colorado Springs, CO

If your child has been wearing a pair of tan Cat & Jack sandals from Target this summer, stop using them now. Target is recalling approximately 211,000 pairs of children's sandals after the Consumer Product Safety Commission found that decorative plastic pearl details on the straps can fall off and pose a choking hazard.

The recall was announced July 16, 2026. The CPSC classified the risk as a potential for "serious injury or death." Twenty-three reports of pearls detaching have already been submitted. No injuries have been reported, but the risk is real for any small child who picks up a fallen pearl and puts it in their mouth.

What the Recall Covers

The recalled sandals are tan-colored with raffia straps, gold-toned buckles, and small decorative plastic pearls. They were sold in toddler and youth sizes 5T through 12T. Target sold them in stores nationwide and on target.com from January 2026 through May 2026 for around $20 a pair.

The problem is the pearls. They can detach from the straps during normal use and become a small, hard object that a child might pick up and swallow or inhale. The CPSC defines this as a serious hazard, which is why the recall language includes the phrase "serious injury or death." That language is standard for any product that presents a confirmed aspiration or choking risk, but it signals this is not a precautionary measure — it's a documented problem.

What Families Should Do Right Now

Stop letting your child wear the sandals immediately. Do not wait until you find a receipt or confirm the exact sandals. If your child has a pair of tan Cat & Jack sandals from Target that were purchased between January and May of this year, pull them from use today.

To get a refund, call Target customer service at 800-591-3869. Target is offering a full refund. You do not need the original receipt if you purchased the sandals using a Target account, RedCard, or order confirmation from target.com. If you paid cash and don't have the receipt, call and ask — Target has been working with families on a case-by-case basis for recall situations.

The CPSC also maintains a full recall entry at cpsc.gov where families can verify their product matches the recalled item before calling.

What's Next

If you aren't sure whether the sandals in your home match the recalled product, compare them to the description: tan color, raffia straps, gold buckles, and small pearl-like plastic decorations. Any tan Cat & Jack sandal with pearls purchased from Target this year falls under this recall.

For families with multiple children who may have worn the same pair, check their small spaces — car seats, toy boxes, under furniture — for any loose pearl decorations that may have already detached. This recall is worth passing along to grandparents, childcare providers, and anyone who may have given a pair of Target sandals as a gift this spring.
