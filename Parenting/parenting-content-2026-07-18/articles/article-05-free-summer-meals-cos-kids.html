July 18, 2026 — Colorado Springs, CO

Free meals are available this summer for every child 18 and under in Colorado Springs. No application. No income verification. No school district requirement. Kids just show up.

D11 is providing free meals to children across the city — not just children who attend D11 schools, but any child 18 and under in Colorado Springs. Across Colorado, more than 600 meal sites are operating through the USDA Summer Food Service Program, and a new online tool makes finding the nearest one simple.

How the Program Works

The Summer Food Service Program is a federal USDA program that funds free meals for children during the summer months when school cafeterias are closed. Schools, nonprofits, and local governments operate the sites; the federal government reimburses them for the meals served. The rules are consistent nationwide: any child 18 and under can receive a free meal at any participating site, and no proof of income or residency is required.

In Colorado Springs, D11 operates sites and confirmed to KKTV that the program is open to all children in the city, not just D11 students. A child attending a D20 or D49 school — or not in school at all — can access any D11 summer meal site.

How to Find a Site Near You

The Colorado Department of Education partnered with KidsFoodFinder.org to make site lookup straightforward. Go to KidsFoodFinder.org, enter your ZIP code, and you'll see a list of nearby sites with addresses, operating days, and hours. Most sites serve lunch and sometimes a snack as well.

Across Colorado, more than 600 sites are currently operating, which means there's very likely at least one within a short drive of any Colorado Springs address. With KidsFoodFinder.org, you can see the specific hours and days for each site near you rather than showing up and hoping the timing works.

Why This Matters Mid-Summer

We're now well into July. School ends in May, which means children who qualify for free or reduced-price school meals have been without that daily support for six to eight weeks already. Summer is also the period when food costs for families with children are highest — kids are home for more meals per day, and summer childcare and activity costs compete with grocery budgets.

The USDA Summer Food Service Program exists specifically to address this gap. The fact that Colorado Springs families don't need to apply, prove income, or even attend a specific school to access the benefit means it's genuinely accessible to the families who need it most.

What's Next

The Summer Food Service Program runs through the end of summer. Exact site-by-site end dates vary — use KidsFoodFinder.org to check the specific schedule for sites near you. Sites typically close when the local school district starts back up, which for COS families means D49 on August 4, D11 on August 12, and D20 on August 13.

If you don't need this program yourself, think about who in your neighborhood might. Families new to Colorado Springs, families experiencing job changes, and grandparents caring for grandchildren this summer may not know this resource exists.
