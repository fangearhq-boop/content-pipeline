Last Chance for the Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo — Two Performances Today at Norris Penrose

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting

July 18, 2026 — Colorado Springs, CO

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo wraps up today after five days at Norris Penrose Event Center. Two performances remain: a matinee at 12:30 PM and an evening show at 7:30 PM. Grounds open at 10 AM for the afternoon performance.

This is the 89th edition of a Colorado Springs tradition that started in 1937. The rodeo benefits military families, and it's one of the few local events that has run nearly continuously across nine decades of the city's history.

What Happens at the Rodeo

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo follows the standard professional rodeo format: bull riding, saddle bronc, bareback bronc, tie-down roping, steer wrestling, team roping, and barrel racing. These are sanctioned professional events with competitors from across the country competing for points and prize money.

For families with children, the standout event is mutton-bustin' — a crowd-favorite competition where young children try to ride sheep for as long as they can hold on. It's one of those things you have to see in person to fully appreciate, both for the effort the kids put in and the reaction of the crowd. Children who participate typically need to meet a weight limit and sign up in advance; if your kids want to try it, check at the ticket office when grounds open at 10 AM.

Bull riding traditionally closes the performance, which means the final moments of both today's shows are built around the highest-energy event of the night. It's a strong reason to stay through the end of whichever performance you attend.

The Military Connection

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo has supported military families since its founding, and Colorado Springs — home to Fort Carson, Peterson Space Force Base, and Schriever Space Force Base — has a large military population that this event serves directly. The rodeo typically includes recognition for active-duty service members and veterans, and the community connection to the military mission is visible throughout the event.

For families stationed at one of the local bases, this is one of the city's most community-rooted events, and one that many military families return to across multiple tours.

What's Next

After today, the Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo wraps for 2026 and won't return until next summer. If your family makes it out today, the matinee at 12:30 PM is a solid choice for families with younger children who do better earlier in the day. The evening show at 7:30 PM works well for older kids and families comfortable with a later night.

Norris Penrose Event Center is located in the southwest part of Colorado Springs. Plan to arrive before ground open at 10 AM for the matinee if you want good seats and time to explore the grounds before the performance starts.
