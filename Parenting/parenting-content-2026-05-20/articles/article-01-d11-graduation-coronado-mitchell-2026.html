D11 Graduation Week: Coronado and Mitchell Walk Today as Class of 2026 Celebrations Continue

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 20, 2026

Two more Colorado Springs School District 11 classes walk the stage today, as graduation week for the Class of 2026 continues at Ed Robson Arena on the Colorado College campus.

Coronado High School holds its ceremony at 9:00 AM MT this morning. Mitchell High School follows this afternoon at 3:00 PM MT. Both ceremonies take place at Ed Robson Arena.

Families who cannot attend in person can watch via the district's free livestream. Links for each school's ceremony are available at d11.org/calendars/graduations.

Ed Robson Arena: What to Know

Ed Robson Arena is located on the Colorado College campus at 225 E. Cache La Poudre Street in Colorado Springs. The venue seats several thousand and provides air conditioning — a significant perk for late-May ceremonies. Doors typically open 45 to 60 minutes before each ceremony. Families are encouraged to arrive early to secure preferred seating.

Parking is available in the surrounding Colorado College campus lots. Street parking is limited in that area during peak event times, so building in extra time is advisable.

A Week of Milestones for COS Families

For many families in Colorado Springs, graduation week is a multi-day celebration that involves coordinating schedules across multiple schools. Siblings, cousins, and friends may be graduating on different days, and the concentrated week-long format means the celebration stretches across the better part of five days.

It's also a natural moment of reflection for parents of younger kids — watching high schoolers cross that stage has a way of collapsing time in a way that nothing else quite does.

For the graduates themselves, the milestone arrives just days before the official end of the school year. With the last day on May 22, the Class of 2026 closes one chapter and steps into whatever comes next: college, trade programs, military service, work, or something else entirely.
