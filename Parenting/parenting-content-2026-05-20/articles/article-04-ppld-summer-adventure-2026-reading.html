PPLD Summer Adventure 2026: Free Reading Program Starts June 2 — What COS Families Need to Know

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 20, 2026

Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure 2026 launches on June 2, and if your family has been using the end of the school year as a reason to put reading on pause, this is the reset button.

The program runs June 2 through July 31 and is completely free. This year's theme, "Unearth a Story," is built around dinosaurs, paleontology, and archaeology — a natural fit for the curious kids who've been asking questions about fossils since the last field trip. Participants earn prizes by completing 30 days of reading, creating, and exploring activities. The program is open to all ages: kids, teens, and adults can all participate.

Why Summer Reading Matters

Research consistently shows that kids lose reading progress during summer months without structured reading practice. The loss tends to be steeper in years when school ends earlier or the gap before structured fall programming is longer. For families in Colorado Springs, D11's school year wraps up on May 22 — leaving a period of nearly two weeks before Summer Adventure begins.

That gap isn't a problem. The library branches are open, and regular reading and library visits in that window can keep the momentum going before Summer Adventure kicks off officially on June 2. It also gives families time to sign up, browse what branches have available, and plan which days to visit.

The "Unearth a Story" Theme

The "Unearth a Story" theme is part of the 2026 Collaborative Summer Library Program, which is used by libraries across Colorado and nationally. For kids who gravitate toward science and natural history, this year's theme opens up a wide range of book options: picture books about dinosaur discoveries, middle-grade novels set on archaeological digs, nonfiction about paleontology, and more.

Libraries often add their own programming events around the theme — workshops, special story times, and activities that bring the theme to life. Check ppld.org/summer for a calendar of events at Pikes Peak branches throughout the summer.

The Prize Incentive

The program works through a 30-day challenge format. Participants track days of reading, creating, and exploring activities to earn prizes from the library. The format is designed to be flexible — you don't have to complete 30 consecutive days, and families can work through the challenge at their own pace before July 31.

For many kids, the prize component is what initially hooks them. The reading habit tends to do the rest.

What's Next

Register for Summer Adventure 2026 at ppld.org/summer before June 2. All Pikes Peak Library District branches participate. The program runs through July 31. If you have a library card question or need to get your child signed up for their first card, library staff at any branch can help — library cards are free for Colorado Springs residents.
