Territory Days 2026 Starts Saturday: Family Logistics Guide for Old Colorado City

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 20, 2026

Territory Days 2026 starts this Saturday, May 23, on Colorado Avenue in Old Colorado City. Three days, free admission, 50th anniversary — and the kind of genuine Colorado Springs community event that earns its place on the Memorial Day weekend calendar year after year.

If you've been before, you know the drill. If you're bringing kids for the first time, here's what to know before you go.

What's There for Kids

Territory Days has a dedicated kids' play area that makes it worth attending even for families with young children. The lineup includes a petting zoo, toy train rides, and a mechanical bull. Gold panning is typically a hit with elementary-age kids — there's something about the hands-on, historical nature of it that captures attention in a way that a standard vendor row doesn't.

Birds of prey demonstrations and Indigenous dancer performances add cultural programming that goes deeper than the typical street fair. These elements have been part of Territory Days for years and are worth seeking out if you're there with curious kids.

The Wild West gunfight re-enactments are theatrical and loud — a good heads-up for families with younger children who may be startled by the sound effects.

Parking Strategy

Colorado Avenue gets congested during Territory Days, and finding street parking close to the action is a frustration that's easy to avoid. The recommended approach: use the C.A.B. Garage at 130 S. Nevada Ave, which charges $1 per hour, and take the free shuttle to Colorado Avenue. It's a small logistical change that typically saves 20 to 30 minutes of circling and significantly reduces the stress of the arrival experience.

The 50th Anniversary

This year marks the 50th anniversary of Territory Days, which began in 1976 as a community celebration in the Old Colorado City historic district. For longtime Colorado Springs families, that longevity represents something meaningful — a community tradition that's outlasted trends, survived economic cycles, and kept its core character intact.

Territory Days runs rain or shine. Memorial Day weekend weather in Colorado Springs can vary significantly — warm and sunny, or breezy with afternoon clouds. Layers and sunscreen are generally both useful, sometimes on the same day.
