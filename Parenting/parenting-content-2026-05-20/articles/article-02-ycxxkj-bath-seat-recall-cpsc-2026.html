CPSC Recall: YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats — About 9,000 Units, Drowning Risk, Discard Immediately

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 20, 2026

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 9,000 YCXXKJ-brand baby bath seats sold on Amazon. The seats are unstable and can tip over while in use, posing a serious risk of injury or death due to drowning.

What makes this recall different from most: the firm that sold these seats — listed on Amazon as "BenTalk" — is no longer responding to consumers. There is no refund program, no replacement, and no working customer service line. The CPSC's official guidance is to discard the product immediately.

How to Identify the Recalled Seat

What to Do Right Now

If your family purchased a baby bath seat from Amazon that matches this description — blue, gray, pink, or yellow with detachable arm rests, suction cup base, and rubber toy accessories — stop using it immediately.

Because the company is no longer responding to consumers, the CPSC is not directing families to contact the seller. Instead, the guidance is straightforward: discard the product. Do not donate it, pass it along to another family, or store it for future use.

If you're uncertain whether your seat is the recalled model, check your Amazon order history. You can also search the CPSC recall database at cpsc.gov/recalls for the full recall notice with photos and model information.

Context: A Pattern of Bath Seat Recalls

This is the latest in a series of baby bath seat recalls from the CPSC in 2026. Earlier this spring, the agency recalled approximately 280 BabyBond retractable safety gates and 125,200 Cosyland Children's Tower Stools. Other bath seat brands — including NFSVLB and Trankerloop — have also been recalled in recent months for similar safety failures involving the mandatory standard for infant bath seats.

The pattern reflects ongoing enforcement of federal safety standards for infant products sold through third-party Amazon sellers. Parents are encouraged to check the CPSC recall database regularly, particularly when purchasing baby and toddler products through online marketplaces.

What's Next
