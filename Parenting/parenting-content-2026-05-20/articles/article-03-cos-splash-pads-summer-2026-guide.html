COS Splash Pads and Spray Grounds 2026: Where to Go, When They Open, What's Free

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 20, 2026

Colorado Springs families: summer water play season is almost here. The city's public spray grounds and splash pads open for the 2026 season over the next two weeks — and every one of them is free.

Here's what's opening, when, and what families should know before heading out.

What to Know: Panorama Park and Venezia Park

Panorama Park's Splash Pad and the Watering Hole at Venezia Park both open on Tuesday, May 26, just after Memorial Day weekend. Both run daily through the summer season with the same hours: 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM. No registration is required, and there's no fee to use either facility.

Panorama Park is located in the southeast part of Colorado Springs. Venezia Park is in the northeast. Having two splash pads open on the same day in different parts of the city gives families across COS a convenient option close to home.

Deerfield Hills: June 1 Opening

Deerfield Hills Spray Ground, also in the southeast, opens a week later on June 1. Hours there are slightly different: most days run 10:00 AM to 5:00 PM, while Wednesdays start later at 11:30 AM and close at 5:00 PM. The spray ground is free to use.

Downtown: Uncle Wilber at Acacia Park

The Uncle Wilber Fountain at Acacia Park in downtown Colorado Springs operates during summer months from noon to 6:00 PM daily. It's a good option if you're already in the downtown area and have kids who want to splash around — no driving to a separate destination required.

One Notable Change: No Julie Penrose Fountain This Summer

Families who enjoyed the Julie Penrose Fountain at America the Beautiful Park in previous years should note: it will not operate during the 2026 summer season. The city's ongoing budget constraints have required cuts to some park amenities, and this fountain is among them. The park itself remains open, but the fountain feature will not be active.

This follows news from earlier this year that approximately 25% of neighborhood park permanent restrooms would remain closed for the 2026 season, and that damaged playground equipment would be removed rather than replaced. Knowing these changes ahead of time helps families plan outings without unexpected surprises.

What's Next

Panorama Park Splash Pad and Venezia Park Watering Hole open Tuesday, May 26. Deerfield Hills Spray Ground opens June 1. All are free and no registration is required. For the most current information on hours and any schedule changes, check the City of Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services page at coloradosprings.gov/PRCS.
