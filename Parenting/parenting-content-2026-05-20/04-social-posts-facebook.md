### STORY 2: YCXXKJ Bath Seat Recall

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 12:00 PM MT

Parents: please check your bathroom.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 9,000 YCXXKJ-brand baby bath seats sold on Amazon through a seller called BenTalk. These seats are unstable and can tip over during use — a drowning hazard for infants.

The seats are sold in blue, gray, pink, and yellow. They have two detachable arm restraints, four suction cups on the bottom, and three rubber toys attached: a duck, a turtle, and a whale.

What makes this recall more urgent than most: the firm that sold these seats is no longer responding to consumers. That means there's no refund process, no replacement program. The CPSC's guidance is clear: discard the product immediately. Do not continue using it.

If you're not sure whether your bath seat is one of the recalled models, the safest step is to stop using it until you can confirm. You can check the CPSC recall database at cpsc.gov/recalls and search for YCXXKJ.

Please share this with any family you know who has an infant.

Have you heard about this recall? Were you aware of the other bath seat recalls from earlier this spring?

### STORY 4: PPLD Summer Adventure 2026

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 5:00 PM MT

We're a little over two weeks from the end of the D11 school year, and the Pikes Peak Library District has something lined up for what comes next.

Summer Adventure 2026 runs from June 2 through July 31. This year's theme is "Unearth a Story" — built around dinosaurs, paleontology, and archaeology. Participants of any age spend 30 days reading, creating, and exploring to earn prizes. The program is completely free and open to all ages, from toddlers to grandparents.

Here's why this matters: research consistently shows that kids lose a meaningful amount of reading progress over the summer months, particularly in lower-income households and in years with earlier school closings. For Colorado Springs families, D11's last day on May 22 creates a window of nearly two weeks before Summer Adventure begins. That's not a problem — it's a chance to use the library's regular programming to bridge the gap.

To register, visit ppld.org/summer. All Pikes Peak Library District branches participate.

### STORY 5: Territory Days This Weekend

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 8:00 PM MT

Territory Days is three days away.

Colorado Springs' longest-running street festival — now in its 50th year — takes over Colorado Avenue from 23rd to 27th Street in Old Colorado City on Saturday, May 23 through Monday, May 25. Admission is free, as always.

Here's what families need to know before heading out this weekend:

**Parking tip:** The C.A.B. Garage at 130 S. Nevada Ave charges $1/hour and offers a free shuttle to Colorado Avenue. Skip the circle-around-the-block strategy and use the garage — you'll thank yourself.

**For the kids:** There's a petting zoo, toy train rides, a dedicated kids' play area, mechanical bull, and gold panning. Birds of prey demonstrations and Indigenous dancer performances give it a cultural dimension that goes beyond typical street fair fare.

**For the grown-ups:** 25+ live bands across the weekend, 180+ food and craft vendors, blacksmith demonstrations, and Wild West gunfight re-enactments. The whole thing has a genuinely old-Colorado spirit that's worth experiencing.

It's free, it's local, and it's been running for 50 years. This is one of those Colorado Springs events that tends to feel different when your kids are old enough to really take it in.

Are you heading to Territory Days this year?
