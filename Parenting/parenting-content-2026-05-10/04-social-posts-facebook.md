### STORY 1: Bellabu Bear Children's Robe Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

Hey COS parents — checking in on something important this morning. ⚠️

The CPSC has issued a recall for Bellabu Bear bamboo sherpa children's robes. If your kid has one of these — especially in a popular licensed character print — you'll want to read this.

The recalled robes were sold in prints including PAW Patrol, Garfield, Minecraft, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora, and several others. They come in S/M and M/L children's sizes and were available at Saks Fifth Avenue stores, boutiques nationwide, and online at bellabubear.com and other retailers from January 2024 through July 2025. Price was around $60.

The problem: testing showed the robes don't meet mandatory federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear. That means they could ignite more easily near a flame or heat source than they should. No injuries have been reported, but the CPSC and Bellabu Bear are urging parents to stop using these robes immediately and keep them away from kids.

Here's how to get your refund or store credit:
- 📞 Call 888-703-7752 (9 AM-4 PM PT, Mon-Fri)
- 📧 Email help@bellabubear.com
- 🌐 Visit bellabubear.com for the full recall instructions

Did you buy one of these? Sharing this post could save another family the worry of finding out the hard way.

### STORY 5: Outdoor Play Benefits

#### Long-Form Post — 7:00 PM MT

As summer approaches, here's a gentle reminder from pediatric researchers: unstructured outdoor playtime is one of the most impactful things your kids can do for their health this summer. 🌲

We're not talking about organized sports or structured activities — though those have their place. We're talking about the kind of playing outside that doesn't have a schedule. Digging in dirt. Running around a field. Climbing a tree. Following a trail to see where it goes.

Here's what the research says:
- Kids who spend regular time outdoors have lower rates of obesity and stronger bones and muscles
- Stress levels drop within minutes of being outside — for kids and adults
- Children diagnosed with ADHD show fewer symptoms when they have significant outdoor time
- Unstructured outdoor play improves focus, creativity, and social skills
- Sun exposure supports vitamin D production, which builds bone health

Colorado Springs families are lucky. We've got access to Pikes Peak trails, open spaces, parks, and natural areas that most cities don't have. That's not something to take for granted this summer.

The goal doesn't have to be a 10-mile hike. Just... outside. Regularly. Unscheduled.
