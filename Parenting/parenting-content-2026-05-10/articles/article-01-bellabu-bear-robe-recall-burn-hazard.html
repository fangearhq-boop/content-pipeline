CPSC Recall: Stop Using Bellabu Bear Children's Sherpa Robes — Burn Hazard

By Jamie Rivera | May 10, 2026 | COS Parenting

Bamboo sherpa robes sold in popular character prints including PAW Patrol, Minecraft, and Minions have been recalled for failing to meet federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear.

What's Been Recalled

Bellabu Bear has issued a recall — in cooperation with the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) — of certain bamboo sherpa children's robes. The robes do not meet mandatory federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear, posing a burn hazard to children.

The recalled items are long-sleeved sherpa-lined robes with two front pockets and a matching belt. They were sold in children's sizes S/M and M/L in a wide range of licensed character prints.

Which Prints Are Affected

If your child has a Bellabu Bear sherpa robe in any of the following prints, it is covered by this recall:

  • PAW Patrol
  • Garfield
  • Minecraft
  • Minions
  • Batman
  • Justice League
  • Wonder Woman
  • Blaze and the Monster Machines
  • Trolls
  • Dora
  • Nutcracker
  • Hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons

These prints were widely sold and are likely to be in many Colorado Springs homes — particularly as gifts purchased during 2024 and 2025. If you're not sure whether your child's robe is included, check the brand label for "Bellabu Bear."

What to Do Now

The CPSC and Bellabu Bear are asking families to stop using the recalled robes immediately and to keep them away from children until the issue is resolved.

To request a full refund or store credit for the original purchase price, contact Bellabu Bear using one of the following:

  • Phone: 888-703-7752 (9 AM-4 PM PT, Monday-Friday)
  • Email: help@bellabubear.com
  • Website: bellabubear.com (recall instructions posted)

Why This Matters

Flammability standards for children's sleepwear exist because robes, pajamas, and other loose-fitting sleepwear are worn near candles, ovens, fireplaces, and other heat sources in the home. When a garment fails these standards, it can ignite faster or burn more intensely than federal regulations allow.

No injuries have been reported in connection with this recall, which means families have time to act before any harm occurs. Taking the robe out of the bedroom and contacting Bellabu Bear for a refund is the right step.

What's Next

If you have a Bellabu Bear sherpa robe in your home, stop using it today and reach out to Bellabu Bear at 888-703-7752 or help@bellabubear.com to begin the refund process.
