D11 Graduation Dates and Last-Day-of-School Guide for Colorado Springs Families

By Sarah Morales | May 10, 2026 | COS Parenting

Here are the key dates District 11 families need for the final stretch of the 2025-2026 school year, from graduation ceremonies to the last day of class.

High School Graduation Ceremonies

District 11's Class of 2026 celebrates across the May 19-21 window. Here's what we know about ceremony timing:

School Date Time
Palmer High School May 19 9:00 AM
Coronado High School May 19 9:00 AM
Mitchell High School May 20 3:00 PM
Doherty High School May 20 3:00 PM
Achieve, Tesla, Bijou, Odyssey May 21 Times TBD

Families should verify ceremony details directly at d11.org as locations and any last-minute changes will be posted there. Large graduation ceremonies at district high schools typically draw hundreds of families — arriving early and knowing the parking situation in advance makes a meaningful difference.

Last Day of School: May 22

For all D11 students, the last day of the 2025-2026 school year is Friday, May 22, 2026. This applies across elementary, middle, and high school levels unless a student's individual school has communicated otherwise.

The day after — Saturday, May 23 — is the first official day of summer. For families who haven't yet locked in summer camp registration or childcare arrangements for the following week, the time is now.

Memorial Day Weekend and What Follows

Memorial Day falls on Monday, May 25 — the district is closed. This creates a long weekend immediately following the last day of school, which is welcome breathing room for families in transition mode.

District staff return for a teacher work day on May 26. Adult and Family Education Graduation takes place on Thursday, May 28 at 7 PM at Roy J. Wasson Academic Campus Auditorium — a ceremony that recognizes students who completed their education through a different path and is worth acknowledging in the broader community.

A Note on D11's Current Context

This end-of-year milestone comes against the backdrop of an ongoing labor dispute between D11's Board of Education and the Colorado Springs Education Association (CSEA). Educators have been working without a master contract for more than 300 days following the Board's decision to end a 56-year agreement. Graduation season is a celebration for students and families — it's also a reminder of the educators who guided those students through what has been a challenging year for the district.

What's Next

Confirm your school's graduation details at d11.org, and mark May 22 on the family calendar as the official last day. For D11 families with kids heading into the summer, the next step is making sure the weeks after May 25 have some structure in place. The summer camp guide in today's content is a useful starting point.
