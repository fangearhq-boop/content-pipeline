Colorado Springs 2026 Summer Camp Guide — Spots Are Filling as School Wraps May 22

By Jamie Rivera | May 10, 2026 | COS Parenting

District 11 students' last day is May 22 — 12 days from now. Here's a guide to summer camp programs across Colorado Springs, organized by age range and interest.

Why Now Is the Right Time to Register

Summer camp spots in Colorado Springs fill up faster every year, particularly for popular programs in the first few weeks of summer. With D11's last day on May 22 (followed by the Memorial Day weekend), families who register now avoid the scramble that happens in the final week of school when everyone remembers at the same time.

Most programs don't require full payment at registration — many offer flexible payment options. The key step is to secure the spot.

STEAM and Maker Camps

Colorado Tinkering School offers creative, hands-on STEAM day camps where kids design and build real projects — things like go-karts, castles, and boats. Sessions are scheduled for July 6-10, July 20-24, and August 3-7, running approximately 9 AM-4:30 PM each day. This program is particularly well-suited to kids who love building things and get bored easily in classroom-style settings.

Outdoor Adventure Programs

Go West Camps runs outdoor day camps and teen adventure programs for ages 5-14 across multiple Colorado Springs locations, including home bases at Eagleview Middle School, Chinook Trail Middle School, and The Colorado Springs School. If your kid does better outside than inside, this type of program tends to be a strong fit.

Catamount Institute offers nature-based day camps for ages 5-12, with sessions running in both June and July. Scholarship applications are available for families who need financial assistance with registration costs — a detail worth knowing if cost is a barrier.

Theater and Performing Arts

CYT Colorado Springs (Children's Youth Theatre) runs theater camps for ages 5-13. CYT offers payment plans for families who want to register but need to spread the cost — contact them directly to set one up. Theater programs are a consistently strong option for kids who are verbal, expressive, or not drawn to traditional sports or outdoor activities.

Multisport Programs

It's FUNdamental Multisport offers Monday-Thursday sessions from 9-11:30 AM for ages 3-11. Cost is $120 for the first child, with a 25% discount for additional siblings — one of the more family-friendly pricing structures available locally. This is a solid option for younger kids not quite ready for a full-day program.

What's Next

D11 school ends May 22, and Memorial Day follows on May 25. That gap between the last day of school and the first Monday of summer is short — meaning summer officially starts in about two weeks for most COS families. If you haven't registered yet, this week is the right time to lock something in. Most programs have online registration available now.
