GOPO Toys Teething Toy Recalled for Choking Hazard — What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | June 22, 2026

The Consumer Product Safety Commission announced a recall on June 18, 2026 covering more than 70,000 GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toys due to a serious choking hazard. If your child has one of these toys, stop use immediately.

Three families have already reported incidents involving choking or respiratory distress. The CPSC has confirmed that the toy's silicone strings are smaller and longer than the federal toy safety standard permits, meaning they can reach the back of a child's throat and become lodged. This is not a potential risk — it's a documented one.

What the Toy Looks Like

The recalled toy is off-white and disc-shaped with a gray ball in the center. Six multi-colored silicone pull strings extend from the disc, resembling tentacles. It was sold on Amazon from August 2023 through March 2026 for approximately $11 to $15.

To confirm whether your toy is part of the recall, check the batch number on the packaging. Affected batch numbers are: 250905, 250530, 250120, 240315, 231005, and 230610.

What to Do Right Now

If you have this toy, here are the steps:

  1. Stop using it immediately and take it away from children.
  2. Cut and discard all of the silicone string tentacles.
  3. Write "DESTROYED" on the toy body with permanent marker.
  4. Take a photo of the destroyed toy and email it to recalls@gopotoys.com to start the refund process.

If you're unsure whether your toy is part of the recall, you can visit cpsc.gov for the full recall listing or call 1-800-638-2772.

Why This Matters

With more than 70,000 units sold over nearly three years on Amazon, this toy is widely distributed. Parents of young children who have been using this product since 2023 may not have seen the June 18 recall notice. Sharing this information with other families is one of the most effective things you can do to prevent an incident.

What's Next

Once you've destroyed the toy and submitted your photo to recalls@gopotoys.com, GOPO Toys will process your refund. For full recall details, including product images, visit cpsc.gov and search for "GOPO Toys." If you have questions about children's product safety, cpsc.gov maintains a comprehensive database of current recalls that Colorado Springs families can bookmark.
