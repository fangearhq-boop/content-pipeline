Where to Cool Off for Free: Colorado Springs Splash Pads and Spray Grounds Guide 2026

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | June 22, 2026

Every summer spray ground and interactive fountain operated by the City of Colorado Springs is free to use. With temperatures climbing and school out, these spots are some of the most popular family destinations in the city — and we've got the full rundown on what's open, when, and where, including one important heads-up about a site that won't be operating this year.

Open Splash Pads and Spray Grounds for Summer 2026

The Watering Hole at John Venezia Community Park

This is one of the most popular water play spots in Colorado Springs, located in northeast COS at John Venezia Community Park. The Watering Hole features more than a dozen water features and sits directly adjacent to an award-winning universally accessible playground. Open daily 10 AM to 6 PM. Free.

Panorama Park Splash Pad

Located in southeast Colorado Springs at the award-winning Panorama Park. A dedicated splash pad area with interactive water features, open daily 10 AM to 6 PM. Free.

Deerfield Hills Spray Ground

Also in southeast Colorado Springs at the Deerfield Hills Community Center. This is the largest city spray ground, featuring more than 50 individual spray nozzles and 16 distinct water spray features. Open daily 10 AM to 5 PM — note the earlier closing time compared to other sites. Free.

Uncle Wilber Fountain at Acacia Park

Located downtown at Acacia Park, 115 E. Platte Ave. Uncle Wilber Fountain is an interactive splash fountain in the heart of downtown Colorado Springs. Runs daily from noon to 6 PM. Free. Great if you're already downtown.

Important 2026 Update: Julie Penrose Fountain Is Closed

The Julie Penrose Fountain at America the Beautiful Park will not operate during the 2026 season. The closure is part of the Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department's 2026 budget adjustments. If you've taken kids there in previous summers, plan for one of the other four sites instead.

If You Want a Pool With Lifeguards

The YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region operates outdoor pools for summer 2026. Day passes are $10 per youth (ages 3–17), $15 per adult (ages 18 and up), and $45 for a family pass (two adults and up to four children). YMCA pools have lifeguards on duty, making them a different experience than the free spray grounds.

What's Next

All free city splash pads are open now through the summer season. For official hours and any schedule updates, visit coloradosprings.gov and search "Spray Grounds and Fountains." The city parks page also lists any closures or maintenance days.
