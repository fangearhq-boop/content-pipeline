Free Juneteenth Celebration at the African American Museum — This Thursday, June 19

The 4th Annual event includes a Freedom March at noon and museum tours — and it's distinct from the larger festival happening at Norris Penrose this weekend

By Sarah Morales

June 16, 2026

Colorado Springs families have a meaningful free event coming up this Thursday, June 19 — and it offers something different from what most people think of when they hear "Juneteenth celebration."

The African-American Historical and Genealogical Society of Colorado Springs is hosting their 4th Annual Juneteenth Celebration at the African American Museum at the Westside Community Center Campus. The event is free and open to the public, centered on community, history, and connection.

What to Expect

The celebration includes two distinct elements:

At 12 PM, a Freedom March begins from the Westside Community Center Campus. The march commemorates the journey toward freedom and the spirit of Juneteenth — a community gathering in motion, not a protest, but a celebration of history and community pride.

Museum tours follow at 1 PM and again at 2:30 PM. The African American Museum is housed at the Westside Community Center Campus and holds collections, exhibits, and archives related to African American history in Colorado Springs and the broader region. Families with older children can turn this into a meaningful history conversation — about Juneteenth, about local history, about the people who built this city alongside everyone else.

The African-American Historical and Genealogical Society of Colorado Springs has been hosting this community celebration for four years, and its focus on historical roots and genealogy gives it a different character than the larger, music-festival-style events. This is community memory in action.

What Is Juneteenth

Juneteenth marks June 19, 1865 — the day enslaved people in Galveston, Texas, learned that they were free. This came more than two months after the Confederacy's formal surrender at Appomattox. The Emancipation Proclamation had been signed in 1863, but the news had not reached Texas, and enslavers had not enforced it. When Union troops arrived in Galveston on June 19, 1865, they enforced the order that freedom had already been legally declared.

The date has been celebrated in African American communities for over 150 years. In 2021, Juneteenth became a federal holiday. June 19, 2026 marks the fifth year as an official federal holiday.

This Is a Different Event Than the Southern Colorado Juneteenth Festival

Colorado Springs is hosting two separate Juneteenth events this week. The 4th Annual Celebration at the African American Museum (this Thursday, June 19) is organized by the African-American Historical and Genealogical Society and focuses on history, memory, and community ceremony.

The 6th Annual Southern Colorado Juneteenth Festival runs Saturday and Sunday, June 20-21 at the Norris Penrose Indoor Event Center. That event is larger in scale, with national musical headliners, a kids' zone, a car show, and a fashion show.

Both are free. They serve different purposes and offer different experiences. Families who can attend both will have a complete picture of how Colorado Springs marks this holiday.

What's Next

After the Thursday ceremony, the Juneteenth weekend begins with the 6th Annual Southern Colorado Juneteenth Festival at the Norris Penrose Indoor Event Center on June 20-21. The festival is free and features national headliners V.I.C. and David Banner, a kids' zone, car show, and health fair. The federal holiday itself is Thursday, June 19. Families looking for both history-centered and festival-style Juneteenth experiences will find both available in Colorado Springs this week.
