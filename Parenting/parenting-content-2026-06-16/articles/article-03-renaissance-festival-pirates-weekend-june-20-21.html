Pirates Invasion Weekend Is This Saturday at the Colorado Renaissance Festival

June 20-21 in Larkspur — about 30 minutes from Colorado Springs, with advance King Soopers tickets that save you money at the gate

By Jamie Rivera

June 16, 2026

If your family is looking for something to do this weekend that is a clear step above the usual Colorado Springs summer routine — a full day adventure with costumes, jousting, treasure hunts, live entertainment, and mountains in the background — Pirates Invasion Weekend at the Colorado Renaissance Festival is this Saturday and Sunday, June 20-21.

The festival is in Larkspur, roughly 30 minutes north of Colorado Springs at Exit 174 off I-25. It runs every Saturday and Sunday through August 2, but Pirates Weekend is one of the most popular themed weekends of the 10-week season. Costumes are enthusiastically encouraged, the whole grounds get a pirate-themed makeover, and kids who show up in costume are in excellent company.

What the Festival Offers Families

The Colorado Renaissance Festival is now in its 49th season, and its format is well-established: a sprawling outdoor marketplace in a meadow setting with Ponderosa pines, surrounded by stages, food vendors, artisan booths, carnival rides, and an arena for jousting tournaments. The daily schedule includes multiple live performances — comedy, music, magic, and stage combat — running simultaneously across different areas of the grounds.

For families with children, the balance of structured and unstructured entertainment is one of the event's strongest points. Younger children can explore the grounds at their own pace, watch craft demonstrations, and participate in activities. Older children tend to gravitate toward the jousting arena, the carnival games, and the more theatrical stage shows. The food court has a wide enough range — turkey legs, funnel cakes, specialty drinks, and more mainstream fare — to satisfy everyone.

On Pirates Weekend specifically, look for additional pirate-themed activities woven throughout the grounds, including treasure hunt elements that work well for school-age children.

Tickets and Practical Planning

The single best thing Colorado Springs families can do before going is buy advance tickets at King Soopers. Prices at the King Soopers counter are significantly lower than day-of gate prices:

  • Adults: $29
  • Ages 5-12: $14
  • Under 5: Free

A few practical tips from experienced festival visitors:

  • Arrive early — the first hour or two before crowds build is when parking is easy and the grounds feel spacious
  • Bring a layer — Larkspur sits higher than Colorado Springs and mornings can be noticeably cooler, even in late June
  • Check the weather forecast before going — afternoon thunderstorms are possible in the Colorado Front Range in June, and most of the festival grounds are outdoors
  • Wear comfortable shoes — the grounds are uneven, the day is long, and costumes are fun but comfortable footwear matters
  • Cash helps for artisan vendors, though most accept cards

Festival hours are 10 AM to 6:30 PM. Full schedule, additional themed weekends, and more information are at coloradorenfaire.com.

What's Next

The Colorado Renaissance Festival continues every Saturday and Sunday through August 2, 2026. Each weekend features a different theme. Remaining themed weekends include Celtic Weekend (June 27-28) and others running through early August. COS families who miss Pirates Weekend will have multiple additional opportunities to attend before the season closes.
