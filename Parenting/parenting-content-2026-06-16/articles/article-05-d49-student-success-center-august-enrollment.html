D49 Student Success Center Opens in August — What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know Now

With the budget now passed and the building being prepared, families interested in preschool, Base49 care, PEAK, and Elevates 18-21 should begin the enrollment process now

By Jamie Rivera

June 16, 2026

The D49 Student Success Center opens in August 2026. The 2026-27 budget was passed on June 11, the center's location is confirmed at the former Falcon Elementary of Technology building, and the programs it will house are finalized. August will arrive in less than eight weeks.

For Colorado Springs families who need preschool, before and after-school care, or specialized support services through D49, now is the time to start the enrollment process. Here is what you need to know.

What the Student Success Center Offers

The Student Success Center consolidates several D49 programs into a single location that previously had limited or scattered placement across the district. The programs housed there include:

  • Preschool — Monday through Thursday scheduling
  • Base49 — before and after-school care for D49 students
  • PEAK Programs — specialized enrichment and support
  • Elevates 18-21 — transition services for young adults with disabilities

The consolidation into a single building is designed to improve service coordination and make these programs easier to access for families who need multiple services. The former Falcon Elementary of Technology building, which closed as an active school, provides the physical space for this hub model.

The Budget Context

The Student Success Center opening comes alongside difficult budget reductions for D49. District 49 declared fiscal exigency in January 2026, citing declining enrollment as the primary driver of a structural budget shortfall. Roughly $2 million in cuts were approved in February 2026 by a 4-1 board vote, effective for the 2026-27 fiscal year beginning July 1. Twelve teacher positions were eliminated as part of those reductions.

The Student Success Center is not a new program spending commitment — it represents the consolidation and restructuring of existing services to operate within the reduced budget. Understanding this context helps families read accurately what the center is: a response to fiscal constraints, not an expansion, with the goal of protecting core service access while reducing operational overhead.

What to Do Now

Families interested in any Student Success Center program — particularly preschool or Base49 care — should not wait until August to begin the process. Preschool seats and before/after-school care slots typically fill on a first-come, first-served basis, and interest in these programs is significant following the budget discussions.

The starting point for all D49 enrollment and program information is d49.org. Families can also contact their current school office for guidance on how to connect with Student Success Center enrollment specifically.
