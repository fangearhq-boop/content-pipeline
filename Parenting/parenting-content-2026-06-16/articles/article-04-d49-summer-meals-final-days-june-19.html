D49 Free Summer Meals End Thursday — 3 Serving Days Left for Colorado Springs Families

The Springs Ranch Elementary site closes for the season on June 19; District 11's program continues through July 31 at multiple locations

By Sarah Morales

June 16, 2026

Colorado Springs families using District 49's free summer meal program have three serving days left. The program ends this Thursday, June 19, 2026 — the same day as the Juneteenth federal holiday.

After Thursday, the D49 program is done for the summer. This is the final week to use it.

Where to Go and When

Springs Ranch Elementary is the only D49 free summer meal site. All three remaining serving days — Tuesday June 17, Wednesday June 18, and Thursday June 19 — run on the same schedule:

  • Breakfast: 7:45 AM to 8:15 AM
  • Lunch: 10:30 AM to 11:20 AM

Address: Springs Ranch Elementary, 4350 Centerville Drive, Colorado Springs.

There is no registration, no paperwork, no income verification, and no ID required. Any child ages 1 through 18 can walk in during serving hours and receive a meal, regardless of which school district they attend. Families from D11, D20, or any other district are welcome.

What Happens After Thursday

For families in D49 territory, this week is the last chance to use the free summer meal program. D49 does not operate summer meal sites beyond June 19.

For District 11 families — or for families who are willing to travel across the city — the D11 summer meal program continues through July 31 at multiple Colorado Springs locations. Visit d11.org for specific site addresses and hours. The D11 program operates on the same open-access basis as D49: any child ages 1-18, no registration, no barriers.

Why This Matters

Summer nutrition programs fill a gap that can be significant for families during the months when school meals are unavailable. Research consistently shows that access to regular meals over the summer supports both physical health and learning readiness heading into the next school year.

For Colorado Springs families managing summer childcare schedules, camp drop-offs, and everything else that comes with summer, a free meal option within reach of daily routines is a practical resource worth using while it is available.
