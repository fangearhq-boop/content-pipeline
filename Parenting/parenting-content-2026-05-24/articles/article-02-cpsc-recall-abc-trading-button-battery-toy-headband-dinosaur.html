CPSC Recall: Stop Using These ABC Trading Toys — Button Battery Hazard for Kids

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 24, 2026

If you have a pink bow toy headband or a small dinosaur cage toy at home from a brand called ABC Trading, stop using them now. The Consumer Product Safety Commission announced a recall on May 22, 2026, due to button battery hazards that pose a serious risk of injury or death to children.

Which Toys Are Recalled

The recall covers three ABC Trading products sold at TOYZ and Joissu Product stores nationwide between November 2022 and October 2025:

  • Toy headband, model 6300RP — a plastic pink bow headband with a small push button on top of the bow that activates lights. The bow is pink with white polka dots.
  • Electronic pet cage, model 8266 (ZH998-22) — a silver plastic cage with a blue bottom that contains a red dinosaur and a yellow egg inside.
  • Bird toy, model ZH998-23 — a plastic cage with a pink top and bottom that contains a blue bird inside.

These toys were priced between $5 and $9. No injuries or incidents have been reported.

Why Button Batteries Are Dangerous

Button cell batteries — small, round, coin-shaped batteries — are among the most dangerous small objects in a child's environment. The problem isn't just choking. If swallowed, a button cell battery can generate an electrical current inside a child's body, causing internal chemical burns that can damage the esophagus and surrounding tissue within hours. Serious injuries and deaths have been linked to button battery ingestion.

The CPSC recalled these specific toys because the battery compartments are not secured in a way that prevents children from opening them. The standard these toys must meet requires that battery compartments require a tool or significant effort to open. These do not meet that standard.

What to Do Right Now

The CPSC and ABC Trading are offering a full refund. Here's the process:

  1. Stop using the recalled toys immediately.
  2. Dispose of the toys.
  3. Take a photo of the disposed products in the trash.
  4. Email the photo to recallabc@gmail.com to receive a full refund of the retail price.

If you're unsure whether you have one of these specific products, the model numbers are listed above. The headband and cage designs are distinctive enough to identify on sight.
