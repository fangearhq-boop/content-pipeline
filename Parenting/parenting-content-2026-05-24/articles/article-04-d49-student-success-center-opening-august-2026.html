D49 Is Opening a Student Success Center in August — Here's What It Offers

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 24, 2026

If your family is part of District 49, there's a new resource opening before the 2026-27 school year starts this fall. D49's Student Success Center will open in August, bringing together three different programs under one roof in a new consolidated location.

Here's what it is, what it offers, and which families should pay attention before summer ends.

Where It's Located

The Student Success Center will be housed at what is currently known as Falcon Elementary School of Technology — a building in the District 49 network that's being repurposed for this new center. The location serves students from across D49, not just from the immediate neighborhood.

What Programs Will Be There

Full-Time Preschool

The SSC will offer two full-time preschool classrooms. Classes run Monday through Thursday. District 49 has partnered with Base49 to provide before and after school care for families who need coverage beyond those core hours, and Friday childcare will also be available through that partnership. If you're a D49 family looking for preschool options for the 2026-27 year, this is a new location to consider.

PEAK Programs

PEAK programs at D49 support students with disabilities who need higher-level intervention and support. These students will move into the SSC, which will give them a more structured, school-like experience as they work on their individualized goals. If your child has an IEP and receives PEAK services in D49, the SSC is the new home for those programs starting this fall.

Elevates 18-21 Transition Services

The Elevates program serves D49 students ages 18 to 21 who need transition services to prepare for adult life after school — things like employment skills, independent living, and community participation. Previously, Elevates students received these services through community agencies. Starting in August, the program relocates into D49 itself, which the district sees as a meaningful shift in continuity and quality of service.

What Families Should Do Now

If your family might use any of these three programs — preschool, PEAK services, or Elevates — summer is the time to connect with D49 directly rather than waiting until August. Getting on the right mailing lists, understanding enrollment, and asking questions before the school year starts will put your family in a much better position.
