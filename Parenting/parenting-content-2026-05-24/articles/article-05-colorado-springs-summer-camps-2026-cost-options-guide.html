COS Summer Camps 2026: Cost, Hours, and Options for Colorado Springs Families

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 24, 2026

The school year is wrapping up across Colorado Springs, and if you haven't locked in summer plans yet, you're not alone — but you're also running out of time before the best program slots fill up. Here's a practical overview of what Colorado Springs families can expect from summer camps in 2026, including costs, hours, and the types of programs available.

What to Expect on Cost

Most day camp programs in Colorado Springs run between $125 and $400 per week. City-run programs and nonprofit organizations tend to land at the lower end of that range — often around $125 per week. Specialty camps in arts, STEM, sports academies, or skill-specific areas typically run higher, from $250 to $400 or more per week.

If cost is the primary factor for your family, Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services offers city-run programming that's typically more affordable than private camps. The department manages a range of activities including Learn to Skate sessions at Sertich Ice Center.

Hours and Extended Care

Standard day camp hours are typically 9 AM to 3 PM Monday through Friday. That works fine for parents with flexible schedules, but it's a gap for working families who need coverage earlier in the morning and later in the afternoon.

Many programs offer extended care options — often from 7:30 AM to 6 PM — but availability is limited and typically costs extra. If extended care is essential for your schedule, it's worth calling the camp directly before you register to confirm availability, because it often fills before general enrollment.

What's Available in Colorado Springs

Arts and Music

The Colorado Springs Conservatory runs 27 camp types for ages one through 18, including Rock Band Camp, Songwriter and Music Production Camps, and Theatre Camp. Sessions run June 1 through July 31.

STEM and Creative Tech

Thinkering Lab offers week-long STEM-focused camps from late May through mid-August, Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 3 PM. Programs are hands-on and designed for different age groups and skill levels.

Multi-Activity

Steve and Kate's Camp lets kids choose their own activities from a rotating menu that includes sewing, stop-motion animation, coding and gaming, sports, crafts, and baking. It's a good option for families with kids who have broad interests or who change their mind frequently.

Swim and Water Safety

Pikes Peak Athletics runs swim safety camps that combine structured lessons with meaningful time in the water. Two daily swim lessons are included, with an emphasis on learning proper technique alongside safety fundamentals.

One Practical Note

Summer camp spots in Colorado Springs fill faster than most parents expect. If you've done this before, you know. If you're new to the process: the programs listed in this article will have availability now, but specific time slots — especially extended care slots and popular specialty sessions — will not last through June. Register soon if you see something that fits your family.
