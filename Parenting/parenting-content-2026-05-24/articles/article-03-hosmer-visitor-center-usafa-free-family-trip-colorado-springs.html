Free Day Trip: The USAFA Hosmer Visitor Center Is Open and Colorado Springs Families Should Check It Out

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 24, 2026

Colorado Springs just got a new free family destination, and it's been open since May 16. The General Bradley and Zita Hosmer Visitor Center at the U.S. Air Force Academy is officially welcoming the public — no base pass required, no admission fee, and no excuse to run out of summer day-trip ideas before June is over.

What's Inside

The center is 34,000 square feet and includes seven themed galleries, interactive exhibits, and immersive soundscapes designed to give visitors a feel for what 47 months of cadet training looks like. The centerpiece is a 108-foot grand atrium with views of the Front Range — including, on clear days, Pikes Peak.

The exhibits cover the Academy's history, the legacy of its graduates, and the culture of selfless service that defines the institution. For kids with any interest in the military, aviation, or history, there's a lot to engage with. For kids who just want to look at something impressive, the atrium alone delivers.

Practical Details

This is one of those rare free attractions in Colorado Springs that genuinely delivers on the free part. There's no admission charge, no suggested donation, no paid parking in the first lot. The center is located just outside the Academy's North Gate, which means you don't need to navigate base access, get a visitor pass, or show military ID. You drive to the North Gate area, park, and walk in.

Hours are daily, 9 AM to 5 PM. That's seven days a week, which makes it workable for both weekend outings and weekday summer plans.

Why It Matters for Colorado Springs Families

The Hosmer Visitor Center was a centerpiece of Colorado Springs' City for Champions initiative, a years-long effort to develop world-class visitor experiences in the region. It took a long time to come together, and it finally opened this month — right as local schools are finishing their year and families are looking for activities that don't require a full day of planning.

Colorado Springs is one of the communities in the country that sits next to an active military Academy. Most residents drive past the North Gate regularly. Now there's a reason to stop.
