CPSC Recall — ABC Trading Toy Headband and Dinosaur Toy Have Dangerous Button Batteries
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 10:00 AM MT Hey Colorado Springs parents — there are two kids' toys you'll want to check for right now. The Consumer Product Safety Commission announced a recall on May 22 for ABC Trading children's toys due to button battery hazards. The two recalled items are a pink bow toy headband (model 6300RP — it has a push button on the bow that lights up) and an electronic pet cage toy with a dinosaur inside (model 8266). A third toy, a bird cage model ZH998-23, is also part of the recall. The problem: the battery compartments on these toys can be opened by children. Button cell batteries — the small, coin-shaped ones — are extremely dangerous if swallowed. They can cause internal chemical burns and, in serious cases, death. The CPSC is quite serious about these recalls. These toys were sold at TOYZ and Joissu Product stores nationwide from November 2022 through October 2025 for between $5 and $9. No injuries have been reported, but the CPSC wants all of them off the shelves and out of homes. What to do: stop using these toys immediately. Take a photo of the disposed products in the trash and email it to recallabc@gmail.com to receive a full refund. Have you seen these toys at your house? Share this post if you know a family with young kids who shops at TOYZ. ---

### Long-Form Post Window: 12:00 PM MT School is out and we finally have a new free destination to add to the summer list. 🎉 The General Bradley and Zita Hosmer Visitor Center at the U.S. Air Force Academy officially opened on May 16, 2026 — and the best part for Colorado Springs families is that you don't need a base pass to visit. Summer Camps 2026 — What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know Before They Book
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 5:00 PM MT Summer is here, the school year is wrapping up, and if you haven't looked at summer camps yet — now is the time. Here's a quick overview of what Colorado Springs families are working with for summer 2026: **Cost:** Most day camps run between $125 and $400 per week. City-run and nonprofit programs are typically on the lower end, while specialty camps (arts, STEM, sports academies) can run $400 and up. If cost is a factor, it's worth looking at Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services programs through the city first. **Hours:** Standard day camp hours are 9 AM to 3 PM. Many programs offer extended care from 7:30 AM to 6 PM for working families — ask specifically when you register because availability is limited. **What's available:** The Colorado Springs Conservatory runs arts and music camps for ages 1-18, with 27 program types including Rock Band, Songwriter, Music Production, and Theatre — sessions go from June 1 through July 31. Thinkering Lab has STEM-focused week-long camps from late May through mid-August. Steve & Kate's Camp offers a mix of sewing, coding, stop-motion animation, sports, and baking. Pikes Peak Athletics has swim safety camps with daily lessons. **One reminder:** programs fill up. If something looks right for your family, book it before the good time slots are gone. What camps are your kiddos doing this summer? ---
