### STORY 1: D11 Labor Contract Dispute

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 8:00 AM MT

If your kids are in District 11, you've probably been watching the labor dispute between the school board and the Colorado Springs Education Association with some anxiety. Here's where things stand as the school year winds down.

It's been more than 300 days since the D11 board voted 6-1 to eliminate a master agreement that had been in place between the district and its teachers union for 56 years. That happened in December 2024. The board argued it needed flexibility; the teachers union argued it stripped them of collective bargaining rights. In October 2025, hundreds of teachers went on strike.

That strike ended. But it didn't produce a new contract. And now, in May 2026, the Colorado Springs Education Association is calling on educators and parents to picket at an upcoming D11 Board of Education meeting — a reminder that the dispute is very much still going.

For families, the instability at the district level has been a slow-burning source of concern. Teachers and parents showing up at school board meetings together is a meaningful signal that this isn't just a union issue — it's a community one.

We'll keep tracking this as the school year closes and any new developments emerge.

### STORY 2: ABC Trading Toy Recall

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 10:00 AM MT

Hey Colorado Springs parents — there are two kids' toys you'll want to check for right now.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission announced a recall on May 22 for ABC Trading children's toys due to button battery hazards. The two recalled items are a pink bow toy headband (model 6300RP — it has a push button on the bow that lights up) and an electronic pet cage toy with a dinosaur inside (model 8266). A third toy, a bird cage model ZH998-23, is also part of the recall.

The problem: the battery compartments on these toys can be opened by children. Button cell batteries — the small, coin-shaped ones — are extremely dangerous if swallowed. They can cause internal chemical burns and, in serious cases, death. The CPSC is quite serious about these recalls.

These toys were sold at TOYZ and Joissu Product stores nationwide from November 2022 through October 2025 for between $5 and $9. No injuries have been reported, but the CPSC wants all of them off the shelves and out of homes.

What to do: stop using these toys immediately. Take a photo of the disposed products in the trash and email it to recallabc@gmail.com to receive a full refund.

Have you seen these toys at your house? Share this post if you know a family with young kids who shops at TOYZ.

### STORY 3: USAFA Visitor Center

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 12:00 PM MT

School is out and we finally have a new free destination to add to the summer list. 🎉

The General Bradley and Zita Hosmer Visitor Center at the U.S. Air Force Academy officially opened on May 16, 2026 — and the best part for Colorado Springs families is that you don't need a base pass to visit. It's located just outside the North Gate, free to enter, and open every day from 9 AM to 5 PM.

What's inside: seven themed galleries, interactive exhibits, immersive soundscapes, and a 108-foot grand atrium with sweeping views of the Front Range. The center gives visitors a look at the 47-month cadet journey and the Academy's history, and it was built as part of Colorado Springs' long-running City for Champions initiative.

This is the kind of place where you can spend a few hours with kids who are curious about the military, history, or just want to see something impressive. The atrium alone is worth the drive.

No admission. No badge required. Open daily.

### STORY 4: D49 Student Success Center

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 3:00 PM MT

District 49 families — here's something to have on your radar before August.

D49 is opening a new Student Success Center at the start of the 2026-27 school year, located at what is currently Falcon Elementary School of Technology. The center will serve students across three programs under one roof:

First, full-time preschool — classes run Monday through Thursday, with Base49 providing before and after school care, plus Friday childcare options. If you're looking for preschool in D49, this is a new option to explore.

Second, the PEAK programs — D49's support services for students with disabilities who need higher-level intervention. These students will get a more school-like experience as they work on their goals.

Third, Elevates 18-21 transition services — the program that supports young adults with disabilities in preparing for independent adult life. It's relocating from community agency settings into the district itself.

The center officially opens in August at the start of the 2026-27 school year. If your family is potentially in any of these programs, it's worth reaching out to D49 directly at d49.org to get on the right mailing lists before summer ends.

### STORY 5: Summer Camps Guide

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 5:00 PM MT

Summer is here, the school year is wrapping up, and if you haven't looked at summer camps yet — now is the time.

Here's a quick overview of what Colorado Springs families are working with for summer 2026:

**Cost:** Most day camps run between $125 and $400 per week. City-run and nonprofit programs are typically on the lower end, while specialty camps (arts, STEM, sports academies) can run $400 and up. If cost is a factor, it's worth looking at Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services programs through the city first.

**Hours:** Standard day camp hours are 9 AM to 3 PM. Many programs offer extended care from 7:30 AM to 6 PM for working families — ask specifically when you register because availability is limited.

**What's available:** The Colorado Springs Conservatory runs arts and music camps for ages 1-18, with 27 program types including Rock Band, Songwriter, Music Production, and Theatre — sessions go from June 1 through July 31. Thinkering Lab has STEM-focused week-long camps from late May through mid-August. Steve & Kate's Camp offers a mix of sewing, coding, stop-motion animation, sports, and baking. Pikes Peak Athletics has swim safety camps with daily lessons.

**One reminder:** programs fill up. If something looks right for your family, book it before the good time slots are gone.
