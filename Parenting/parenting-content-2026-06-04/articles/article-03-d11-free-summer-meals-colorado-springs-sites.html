D11 Free Summer Meals: Where to Find Free Breakfast and Lunch for Kids Across Colorado Springs

By Sarah Morales | June 4, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs School District 11 is running a free summer meal program for all children ages 1 through 18 at multiple neighborhood sites across the city, continuing through July 31. No enrollment, no registration, no ID, and no income verification is required. Any child, from any school or any district, can receive free breakfast and lunch at participating sites during open hours.

How This Is Different from the D49 Program

Colorado Springs families may have seen information about the D49 summer meal program at Springs Ranch Elementary, which runs June 1 through June 19 and serves the same no-barrier model. The D11 program is a separate offering and extends through July 31, providing a longer window and different geographic locations. Families can also use any qualifying site across El Paso County regardless of which district their child attends. The Colorado Department of Education maintains a statewide map of summer meal sites at kidsfoodfinder.org, searchable by zip code.

What's Next

The D11 summer meals program runs until July 31, with all sites closed on July 4. If you know a family in Colorado Springs who could benefit from this resource, please pass it along. For questions about specific sites, meal availability, or any changes to the schedule, contact D11 Food and Nutrition Services at 520-2924. The program is funded through the USDA Summer Food Service Program and is available at no cost to children regardless of household income.
