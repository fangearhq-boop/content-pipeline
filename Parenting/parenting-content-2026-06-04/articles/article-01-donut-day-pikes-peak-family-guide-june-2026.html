Donut Day on Pikes Peak: Family Guide for June 6-7

By Sarah Morales | June 4, 2026 | COS Parenting

Donut Day on Pikes Peak is this Saturday and Sunday, June 6 and 7. The annual event brings together ranger-led education, a multi-location passport challenge, and high-altitude donuts at three stops along America's Mountain. If you are planning to go, here is everything Colorado Springs families need to know — including the booking step you will want to take before Saturday morning.

Three Locations, Three Ranger Talks

Donut Day activities are centered at three locations on Pikes Peak: Crystal Reservoir, Glen Cove, and the Summit. Ranger Talks are held at each stop, each covering a different aspect of environmental stewardship on the mountain. Topics include preventing food litter, protecting wildlife, keeping water sources clean, and the history of the Pikes Peak donut tradition itself.

Pick up a Donut Day Passport at the start of your visit and collect a stamp at each of the three Ranger Talk locations. Complete all three stops and you will earn an exclusive "Leave No Crumbs" sticker — a souvenir worth the climb.

Donuts are available for purchase at all three locations. They are made to perform well at altitude, which is something the Pikes Peak kitchen has been practicing for decades.

Podcast Challenge Starting Friday

The City of Colorado Springs is releasing three special "Donut Day Ranger Talks To Go" podcast episodes beginning Friday, June 5, on major podcast platforms. Each episode includes a hidden code word. Collect all three code words by listening to all three episodes, then recite the final phrase to a ranger or gift shop employee on the mountain to receive an exclusive Donut Day sticker while supplies last.

If your family enjoys a listen before the drive, starting the episodes on the way up — one per location — is a natural fit for the three-stop Ranger Talk trail.

Book Your Reservations Now

This is the part that requires action before the weekend. Pikes Peak requires timed-entry reservations or shuttle tickets for all visitors — they are available at DrivePikesPeak.com. Weekend slots during special events fill up, and the Donut Day weekend typically sees higher-than-normal traffic on the mountain. If Saturday is already sold out for your preferred time, Sunday is still an option.

Visitors should also prepare for elevation changes. The drive from the toll gate at 9,390 feet to the summit at 14,115 feet involves significant altitude gain. Children and adults can experience headaches or fatigue. Bring layers — even June mornings at the summit can be cold — and plenty of water.

What's Next

Donut Day is one of several Pikes Peak special events this summer. Bear Creek Nature Center's Pollinator Celebration is scheduled for June 26 if your family is looking for another ranger-led outdoor learning experience. For real-time conditions and any schedule changes on Donut Day weekend, check coloradosprings.gov or the Pikes Peak social channels before you leave the house.
