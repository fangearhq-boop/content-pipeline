Drowning Prevention This Summer: What the AAP Recommends for Colorado Springs Families

By Sarah Morales | June 4, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs pools and splash pads are open for the summer, and with more time near water comes more risk for young children. Drowning is a leading cause of accidental death in children ages 1 through 4. The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its drowning prevention guidance in 2026 with a layered approach that goes beyond any single safety measure. Here is what that guidance recommends for families with children of all ages.

Why a Layered Approach Matters

The AAP's 2026 guidance emphasizes that no single protective measure prevents all drowning incidents. Swim lessons are important but not sufficient on their own. Pool fencing prevents many drownings but does not replace supervision. Life jackets protect children on open water but cannot replace either of the above. The goal is to use multiple layers of protection at once, because any one layer can be bypassed, forgotten, or simply insufficient in a specific situation.

Key Recommendations

RecommendationDetails
Swim lessonsThe AAP recommends starting after a child's first birthday as part of a layered prevention strategy. Lessons should be age-appropriate and taught by qualified instructors.
Four-sided pool fencingFor homes with a pool, a four-sided fence that completely surrounds the pool and isolates it from the house is the most important single home safety measure. The gate should be self-closing and self-latching.
Constant supervisionChildren near any body of water — pools, bathtubs, open water, or even buckets — should be under constant, undistracted adult supervision. "Constant" means no phones, no stepping away.
Remove standing waterEmpty buckets, coolers, and containers immediately after use. Toddlers can drown in a small amount of standing water.
Life jacketsUse Coast Guard-approved life jackets for children near open water — rivers, lakes, reservoirs. Inflatable arm floaties are not life jackets and do not provide equivalent protection.
CPR trainingThe AAP recommends that parents and caregivers learn CPR. Bystander CPR started immediately improves survival outcomes in drowning incidents significantly.

Colorado Springs Context

City of Colorado Springs pools are open for the summer, including Monument Valley Park Pool and Wilson Ranch Aquatic Center. Multiple splash pads are also open, including Panorama, Venezia, and Uncle Wilbur. These are supervised facilities, but supervision by lifeguards supplements — and does not replace — parental supervision of young children in and around the water.

El Paso County's proximity to open water also means that summer activities for Colorado Springs families often include trips to Rampart Reservoir, Eleven Mile State Park, and other mountain water recreation sites. At these locations, life jackets are essential for children, and cold mountain water temperatures add additional risk factors beyond the drowning hazard itself.

What's Next

The full AAP drowning prevention resource library is available at aap.org and healthychildren.org, including a drowning prevention toolkit for parents. For Colorado Springs families interested in swim lessons, the Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department and private aquatic centers in the area offer structured instruction for all ages starting with infants. If you have a pool at home and have not yet installed four-sided fencing, this summer is the time to do it. Pool barriers are available through local home improvement retailers across Colorado Springs.
