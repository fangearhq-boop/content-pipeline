### STORY 2: CPSC Warning — Vndueey Magnetic Men Toy Sets (Magnet Ingestion Hazard)
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Window:** 10:00 AM MT

The CPSC has issued a safety warning for Vndueey Magnetic Men toy sets sold on Amazon from October 2023 through August 2025 for about $25. The toy sets consist of 10 flexible stick figures in various colors — red, orange, pink, yellow, blue, and transparent — each with four small magnets in the hands and feet. The magnets can be swallowed by children and cause intestinal perforation, twisting, blockage, blood poisoning, and death. The manufacturer (Shanghai Zhidi Network Technology Co., Ltd.) has not agreed to a recall; the CPSC has issued a Notice of Violation. Parents who purchased these toys should stop use immediately, cut all figures in half, and email a photo of the destroyed toy set to vndueeyrecall@outlook.com for a refund.

### STORY 5: AAP Summer Drowning Prevention Guide — Key Rules for COS Families
**Tier:** 2 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Window:** 5:00 PM MT

Drowning is a leading cause of death in children ages 1–4. The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its drowning prevention guidance in 2026 with a layered prevention approach: start swim lessons after a child's first birthday, install four-sided pool fencing, keep children in constant adult supervision near all water, and learn CPR. The AAP guidance applies to all water including bathtubs, buckets, and open water — not just pools. With Colorado Springs' splash pads, pools, and outdoor recreation season in full swing, this is a timely resource for local families.
