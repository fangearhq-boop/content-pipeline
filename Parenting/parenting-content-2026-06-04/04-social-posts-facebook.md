### STORY 2: Vndueey Magnetic Men Toy Warning

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

Colorado Springs parents — please check your home for this toy.

The CPSC has issued a safety warning for Vndueey Magnetic Men toy sets sold on Amazon from October 2023 through August 2025 for about $25. The toy set includes 10 flexible stick figures in red, orange, pink, yellow, blue, and other colors — with four small magnets embedded in each figure's hands and feet.

When two or more magnets are swallowed, they attract through the intestinal walls, causing perforations, twisting, blockage, blood poisoning, and can be fatal. This is a serious, well-documented hazard with small toy magnets.

The CPSC has issued a Notice of Violation — the manufacturer, a China-based company operating as Vndueey, has not agreed to an official recall or remedy. The safety warning stands regardless.

What to do:
- Stop using the toy immediately
- Keep it away from children
- Cut all 10 stick figures in half
- Email a photo of the destroyed set to vndueeyrecall@outlook.com for a refund

If you have this toy — or are unsure — please act now.

### STORY 5: AAP Drowning Prevention Summer Guide

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

With Colorado Springs pools and splash pads open for the summer, it's a good time to revisit the current pediatric guidance on drowning prevention.

The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its drowning prevention recommendations in 2026, emphasizing a layered approach rather than any single protective measure:

1. Swim lessons starting after your child's first birthday. The AAP says this is an important part of drowning prevention for children over 12 months.

2. Four-sided pool fencing. For homes with a pool, a four-sided fence that completely surrounds the pool and separates it from the house is the most important single safety measure.

3. Constant adult supervision near all water. "Constant" means no phones, no distractions, no stepping away. This applies to pools, bathtubs, open water — and buckets. Toddlers can drown in small amounts of standing water.

4. Learn CPR. Bystander CPR started immediately can significantly improve survival outcomes for drowning victims.

5. Life jackets on open water.

The reason this is a layered approach: no single measure is enough on its own. Swim lessons don't prevent all drownings. Fencing doesn't replace supervision. The combination is what saves lives.

COS families: your kids are spending more time near water this month. This is worth a conversation before the next pool day.
