Build-A-Bear Heartwarming Hugs Bear Recalled Over Choking Hazard

By Jamie Rivera | July 20, 2026

National Parenting

If your family picked up a Build-A-Bear Heartwarming Hugs Bear earlier this year, stop using it and return it to a store. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission officially recalled the bear on April 30, 2026, citing a zipper slider that can detach and become a choking hazard for young children.

About 36,000 of these bears were sold in the United States between January and March 2026 — and another roughly 520 in Canada — making this a fairly wide-reach recall for what was a popular comfort toy this winter.

What Makes This Bear Different

The Heartwarming Hugs Bear (model 034464) isn't a standard plush. It has a side pouch filled with about 2.5 pounds of ceramic beads that can be heated in a microwave or cooled in the freezer, turning the bear into a weighted comfort item. The problem is the zipper slider on that side pouch — it can separate from the zipper, leaving a small metal piece accessible to a child.

The CPSC classifies the detached zipper slider as a choking hazard with risk of serious injury or death. One incident involving a zipper slider detaching was reported in the United Kingdom. No U.S. incidents or injuries have been reported.

Who Has One

These bears sold for approximately $48 at Build-A-Bear Workshop retail locations and on buildabear.com. If you purchased one between January and March 2026 — or if your child received one as a gift during that time — this recall applies to you.

Because the bear was sold as a comfort or therapeutic toy, it may be something kids have been sleeping with or using regularly. Move it away from children until you can return it.

How to Get Your Refund

The remedy is straightforward: return the bear to any Build-A-Bear Workshop store. You'll receive a full refund in your original form of payment, or a gift card for the purchase price that can be used on any merchandise.

If you'd prefer to handle it by phone, Build-A-Bear's customer line is 844-541-0144. Hours are Monday through Saturday, 8 a.m. to 5 p.m. CT, and Sunday, 7 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. CT. You can also find recall details at buildabear.com/recalls.

What's Next

Check your home — including kids' rooms, toy boxes, and any spaces where the bear might have been stored or gifted. If you have one, stop using it and return it to a Build-A-Bear Workshop store. The refund process is simple, and no receipt is needed according to the CPSC guidance. You can reach the CPSC directly at cpsc.gov or 1-800-638-2772 if you have questions about the recall process itself.
