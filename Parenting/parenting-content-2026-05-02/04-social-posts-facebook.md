### STORY 1: Territory Days 2026 — 50th Anniversary

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**

If you've never been to Territory Days in Old Colorado City, this is the year to change that. 🎉

Colorado Springs's beloved Memorial Day weekend festival turns 50 in 2026, and it's running May 23-25 with everything families love about it: free admission, over 180 food and craft booths, three music stages hosting 25+ bands, Wild West gunfight re-enactments, Native American dance performances, and a Kid Zone packed with inflatables, bungee trampolines, gold panning, and a train ride.

### STORY 4: AAP Car Seat Safety 2026

**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM MT)**

Here's a car seat update worth knowing about.

In February 2026, the American Academy of Pediatrics updated its child passenger safety recommendations and removed the age-based guideline for rear-facing car seats. The old guidance said: keep kids rear-facing until age 2. The new guidance says: keep kids rear-facing until they hit the weight or height limit on their specific car seat — no age cutoff.

This is actually a step forward for safety. The reason the age guideline existed was to give parents a simple rule of thumb, but the real answer has always been about physical fit. Rear-facing is safer for young children in a frontal collision because the seat absorbs more crash energy across the child's entire body. Age 2 was just an approximation.

When your child does outgrow their rear-facing seat, move to a forward-facing seat with a harness and keep them there as long as the seat allows. Then use a belt-positioning booster until the lap and shoulder belt fits properly — typically when they're at least 4 feet 9 inches tall, which is often somewhere between ages 8 and 12.

### STORY 5: COS Outdoor Family Guide for May

**Long-Form Post (12:00 PM MT)**

May might be the best outdoor month in Colorado Springs, and we want to make sure your family actually gets outside to enjoy it.

Temperatures typically hit the 60s and 70s during the day, mornings start cool (layers are your friend), and the wildflowers are blooming. A few family-friendly trail picks if you're not sure where to start:

**Garden of the Gods** — Free, paved and dirt trail options, dramatic red rock scenery, great for strollers and littles. Mornings are much quieter.

**North Cheyenne Cañon** — A little more rugged, beautiful canyon walls. The Seven Bridges trail is a fan favorite — just know it gets busy on weekends.

A couple of COS-specific things to keep in mind with kids:
- At roughly 6,000 feet elevation, UV exposure is meaningfully higher than at sea level. Sunscreen is a must, even on cloudy days.
- Afternoon thunderstorms are common in May — plan to head down from any exposed trail by early afternoon.
