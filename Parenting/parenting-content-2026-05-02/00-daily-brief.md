### STORY 4: AAP Car Seat Guidelines — What Changed in 2026
**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
**Type:** Safety / Health Guide
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its child passenger safety recommendations in February 2026, removing the previous age-based guideline for rear-facing car seats. The new guidance: keep children rear-facing until they reach the maximum height or weight limit specified by their car seat manufacturer — not until they reach a certain age. The old recommendation had previously suggested rear-facing until age 2 as a baseline. Booster seat guidelines remain: use a belt-positioning booster until the vehicle's lap and shoulder seat belt fits properly, typically when a child is at least 4 feet 9 inches tall.
