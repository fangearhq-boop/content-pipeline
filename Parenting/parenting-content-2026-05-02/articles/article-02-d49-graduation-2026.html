Colorado Springs District 49 families have two graduation events to look forward to this spring. Whether you're celebrating a senior walking across the stage or just keeping up with the end-of-year schedule, here's what's happening at D49 in May and what's coming in the fall.

Graduation Ceremonies: May 15 and May 23

The first round of D49 graduation ceremonies takes place on Friday, May 15. PPEC, SSAE, and Patriot graduation events are scheduled for that day. Families with graduates at those programs should confirm venue and timing details directly with their schools, as ceremony specifics may vary by program.

The major high school graduations for FHS (Falcon High School), SCHS (Sand Creek High School), and VRHS (Vista Ridge High School) are set for Saturday, May 23, at the World Arena in Colorado Springs. The World Arena is a significant venue, and families should expect standard venue logistics — plan for parking and traffic, especially if multiple ceremonies are scheduled in the same building on the same day. Confirm your ceremony time directly with your graduate's school.

Board of Education Update

In recent months, the D49 Board of Education made two significant decisions that Colorado Springs families should know about.

First, the board appointed Jack Ryan to fill the vacant Director District 2 seat, replacing Deb Schmidt who stepped down earlier this year. Ryan's appointment fills a gap in board representation for families in that part of the district.

Second, the board voted to repeal Policy JBAA — a policy that had required students to use the bathroom corresponding to their biological sex. The repeal was a contested issue, and families who followed the debate will want to stay current with D49's official communications as the district navigates the policy transition.

New Early Childhood Classrooms Coming This Fall

Looking ahead to August, D49 Early Childhood Education announced the opening of two new classrooms at the D49 Student Success Center. The new classrooms are part of the district's effort to expand access to pre-K and early childhood programming. If you have a child who will be eligible for early childhood education for the 2026-27 school year, it's worth checking with D49 ECE about availability and enrollment timelines. Space in quality early childhood programs often fills quickly, and knowing about new classrooms now gives families a head start.

What's Next

D49 families with graduating seniors should confirm ceremony times and any guest ticket requirements directly with their school. For the May 23 ceremonies at the World Arena, the venue address is 3185 Venetucci Blvd, Colorado Springs. For early childhood enrollment information heading into fall, visit d49.org or contact the D49 ECE office directly.
