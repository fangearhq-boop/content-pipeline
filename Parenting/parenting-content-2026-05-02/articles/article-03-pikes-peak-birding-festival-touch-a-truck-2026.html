Two free family events are coming to Colorado Springs this month, and both are the kind of thing that's genuinely fun for a wide age range — not just for the smallest kids, but for curious elementary-schoolers and adults too. Here's what you need to know about the Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival and the 3rd Annual Touch-A-Truck.

Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival — May 14-17

The Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival runs May 14-17 and features more than 30 guided field trips and nature activities focused on local wildlife and conservation. The four-day event promotes environmental education and connects families with the extraordinary natural landscape that makes Colorado Springs one of the most distinctive places to raise kids in the country.

For families with children who've shown any interest in nature, animals, or the outdoors, this festival is worth putting on the calendar. Field trips range in difficulty and subject matter, which means there are options for younger children alongside more challenging activities for older kids and adults. The festival also tends to draw naturalists and conservation educators who are genuinely passionate about sharing what they know — the kind of enthusiasm that's contagious for curious kids.

Colorado Springs sits at the convergence of grassland, foothills, and mountain ecosystems, which means the variety of bird and wildlife species in the Pikes Peak region is genuinely impressive. The festival takes advantage of that natural diversity.

3rd Annual Touch-A-Truck — May 16

On Saturday, May 16, the 3rd Annual Touch-A-Truck comes to Colorado Springs starting at 9:30 AM. The event is free for all ages and gives kids the chance to get up close with full-size trucks and heavy equipment — fire trucks, construction machines, emergency vehicles, and more. Kids can sit in the cab, honk the horn, and ask questions of the operators and first responders who bring the vehicles.

Touch-A-Truck events consistently rank among the most popular free family events in cities that host them, and it's easy to see why. Young children are fascinated by big vehicles, and getting to interact with them (and the people who drive them) in a safe, structured setting is both exciting and educational. Even parents tend to get a kick out of it.

This is the 3rd Annual edition, which means the event has proven itself as a community staple. The 9:30 AM start time is early enough to enjoy before the Colorado Springs afternoon sun is at its peak.

What's Next

Both the Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival and the 3rd Annual Touch-A-Truck are free and family-friendly. For the most current details on field trip registration for the Birding Festival, visit the event's official page or check visitcos.com. For Touch-A-Truck venue and logistics, check the city of Colorado Springs event calendar or local community event boards closer to the date. May is also home to the Garden of the Gods Art Festival (May 16-17) and the Pikes Peak region offers plenty more to do before Territory Days Memorial Day weekend.
