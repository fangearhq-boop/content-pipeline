May is arguably the best outdoor month in Colorado Springs. The temperatures are warm but not brutal — highs typically in the 60s and 70s, with mornings starting cool. Wildflowers start blooming across the foothills. The trails are uncrowded compared to peak summer. If you're going to get your family outside, May is the time to make it happen.

Here's a quick guide to the best family-friendly outdoor spots in Colorado Springs this month, plus a few things worth knowing before you head out.

Garden of the Gods

Garden of the Gods is the natural starting point for any outdoor family activity in Colorado Springs. It's free to enter, the parking lots are easy to navigate, and the trail options range from fully paved paths (stroller-friendly, accessible) to more adventurous dirt routes through the red rock formations. The dramatic scenery makes it genuinely impressive for kids — there's something about those sandstone towers against a blue Colorado sky that works on people of all ages.

One tip: get there in the morning. Weekends especially can get crowded by midday in May. Arriving at 8:00 or 9:00 AM means better parking, cooler temperatures, and a more relaxed experience. The visitor center is worth a quick stop for orientation and the basic geology overview, which kids tend to find surprisingly interesting.

North Cheyenne Cañon and Seven Bridges

For families with kids who are ready for a bit more adventure, North Cheyenne Cañon offers a rugged canyon landscape with beautiful views and real hiking. The canyon park is free to enter and offers multiple trail options.

Seven Bridges is the most popular family trail in the area — a series of wooden bridges over the St. Mary's Creek, surrounded by forest and canyon walls. It's genuinely beautiful, and kids who like to splash in creeks will want to spend time at every crossing. The full trail is about three miles round-trip with manageable elevation gain for families with kids ages five and older.

Be aware that Seven Bridges gets crowded on weekends, particularly on warm May days. Going mid-week or first thing in the morning on a weekend will make the experience noticeably better.

Sun Safety at Elevation

Colorado Springs sits at approximately 6,035 feet above sea level, and UV exposure increases meaningfully with altitude — roughly four percent for every 1,000 feet gained. That means families visiting from lower elevations, or those who've let their guard down during a mild morning, can get more sun exposure than expected, faster than expected.

Sunscreen is not optional. Apply SPF 30 or higher before heading out and reapply every two hours, especially if kids are sweating or near water. Wide-brimmed hats and sun-protective clothing are worth the small extra effort for an all-day outing. This applies on cloudy days too — UV rays penetrate cloud cover.

Layer Up for Afternoon Thunderstorms

May afternoons in Colorado can change fast. Afternoon thunderstorms are common, typically building over the mountains and moving east in the early-to-mid afternoon. If you're on an exposed trail, a ridgeline, or anywhere above the treeline, be off the high ground by noon or early afternoon.

A light rain jacket in the pack is always a good idea, especially for the foothills trails. Temperatures can also drop quickly once a storm moves through. For families with young children, building in flexibility for an early turnaround is simply good planning — there's no shame in heading back to the car when the clouds start to build.

What's Next

May is packed with outdoor opportunities in Colorado Springs. The Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival runs May 14-17 and is a perfect complement to a trails outing. Territory Days Memorial Day weekend (May 23-25) wraps up the month with a free community celebration in Old Colorado City. For trail conditions, parking updates, and park hours, visit coloradosprings.gov/parks or check individual park pages for Garden of the Gods and North Cheyenne Cañon directly. Get outside, COS.
