Territory Days has been a Colorado Springs Memorial Day tradition for half a century, and the 2026 edition is the biggest milestone yet. The festival — held every year in the historic Old Colorado City neighborhood on the west side of Colorado Springs — marks its 50th anniversary this May 23-25. Admission is free, it runs all weekend, and there's more than enough to keep kids and adults entertained for multiple visits.

What's There for Families

The festival's layout stretches through Old Colorado City with over 180 food and craft booths. Three music stages host more than 25 bands throughout the weekend, covering a range of genres from country to folk to rock. Entertainment also includes Wild West gunfight re-enactments, Native American dance performances, and blacksmith demonstrations — the kind of living history that's both educational and genuinely entertaining for kids of all ages.

The Kid Zone is the anchor for families with young children. It includes a train ride, gold panning (a perennial favorite), inflatables, and bungee trampolines. It's the kind of setup where parents can relax for a bit while kids stay occupied and active.

On Monday afternoon, the festival hosts a Memorial Day Remembrance Ceremony. It's a meaningful tradition that families can experience together, especially as kids get older and start asking questions about why we observe Memorial Day.

Parking and Getting There

Here's the most important logistical note: street parking in Old Colorado City is extremely limited on festival days. Don't plan on driving and finding a spot. The recommended approach is to use the free shuttle service from the Colorado Springs C.A.B. Garage downtown. It's convenient, it saves the frustration of circling for parking, and it models good event behavior for kids. VIP parking with a luxury shuttle is also available as a paid option for families who prefer that.

Old Colorado City is located on the west side of Colorado Springs, roughly along W. Colorado Avenue west of I-25. If you're unfamiliar with the area, plan your route in advance and build in extra time on festival days.

50 Years of a Colorado Springs Tradition

What makes Territory Days worth noting beyond its entertainment value is its longevity. Fifty consecutive years of a free community festival in the same neighborhood is something genuinely rare. Plenty of families in Colorado Springs have their own generational memories of the event — parents who attended as children bringing their own kids for the first time. That kind of continuity is part of what makes a city feel like a community.

If you've been before, this year's 50th anniversary is a good reason to make the trip again. If you've never made it out to Territory Days, this is the year to start.
