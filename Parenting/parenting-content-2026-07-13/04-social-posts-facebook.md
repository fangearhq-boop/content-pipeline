### STORY 1: Joyful Journeys Baby Lounger CPSC Warning

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

🚨 Heads up if you have a Joyful Journeys Baby Lounger at home.

The CPSC issued a Product Safety WARNING on July 2 — and unlike a standard recall, there's no refund coming. Why? Because the company (Sarnia Sales LLC out of Issaquah, WA) is now **out of business**.

Here's what happened: Joyful Journeys agreed to a recall back in January 2026 and promised full refunds. About 9,300 loungers had been sold on Amazon and Walmart between December 2023 and October 2025 for $40 to $60. Then the company shut down before those refunds could go out — leaving parents holding a dangerous product with no remedy.

The hazards are real: the lounger's sides are too low to safely contain a baby, and the foot opening is wider than allowed by federal safety standards. That means fall and entrapment risks for infants.

The CPSC's message is straightforward: stop using the product immediately and dispose of it. Visit cpsc.gov for the full warning.

If you have this lounger, please share this post. And if you see it at a garage sale or secondhand shop, please don't buy it.

What's one thing you wish you'd known before buying baby gear? We'd love to hear from this community.

### STORY 2: AAP Drowning Prevention Guidelines

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM MT

It's the height of summer, and America's pediatricians just updated their drowning prevention guidelines — and the big takeaway is one every COS parent needs to hear: no single method is enough.

The American Academy of Pediatrics published its updated "Prevention of Drowning" policy statement in the June 2026 issue of Pediatrics. Here's what changed and what it means for your family:

**Swim lessons can start earlier.** The AAP now says swim lessons can be considered after age 1, individualized based on your child's readiness — not a blanket age cutoff. That doesn't mean every 13-month-old should be enrolled, but it does mean you don't have to wait until age 4 if your child is ready.

**Layered protection is the new standard.** Swim lessons + pool fencing + life jackets + close supervision + CPR training. None of those things works in isolation. The AAP is explicit: one layer isn't enough.

**The water watcher matters more than you think.** The updated guidelines put a sharp focus on the "water watcher" concept — one designated adult whose only job is watching the swimmers. No phone. No conversations. Eyes on the water, every single second. That adult needs to be able to spot distress, perform a rescue, start CPR, and call for help.

Drowning remains the No. 1 cause of death for children ages 1–4 in the US. Pool season is in full swing. This is the summer to build those layers.

What's your family's water safety routine? Swim lessons? Life jackets? Let us know below.

### STORY 3: Aspen Acres Fire — Air Quality Alert

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

The Aspen Acres Fire is now 35% contained after burning more than 98,000 acres — but COS families aren't in the clear yet. 🌫️

IQAir is currently ranking Colorado's air quality among the worst in the world due to wildfire smoke. And fine particulate matter (PM2.5) from wildfire smoke isn't just a problem for people with asthma — it can be absorbed into the body and cause problems even for otherwise healthy people.

Here's what we recommend for families right now:

- **Keep kids inside** whenever possible, especially during afternoon hours when smoke tends to settle
- **If your child has asthma or any respiratory condition**, limit their outdoor time significantly today
- **Close windows and doors**, even when it doesn't look smoky
- **Run an air purifier** if you have one — HEPA filters are the most effective for PM2.5
- **Skip the outdoor workout** for now if you can
- **Stay updated** at inciweb.wildfire.gov for the latest fire status

Mandatory evacuations remain in effect across Colorado City, Beulah, Rye, San Isabel, Wetmore, Red Creek, Apache City, and portions of Custer, Pueblo, and Huerfano counties. If you have family in those areas, check on them.

780+ structures have been destroyed — making this the 7th most destructive wildfire in Colorado's recorded history.

Has the smoke been affecting your family? Share what's working for air quality at home.

### STORY 5: Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo is back — and it starts TOMORROW.

The rodeo runs July 14–18 at Norris Penrose Event Center, with evening performances at 7:30 PM every night (Tuesday through Saturday). There are also matinee shows at 12:30 PM on Friday, July 17 and Saturday, July 18 — perfect if you want to take the kids during the afternoon.

This is one of the best family events in Colorado Springs all summer. Professional rodeo athletes from across the US, Canada, and Mexico compete in the NFR Open for more than $1 million in payouts. Bull riding, barrel racing, roping — the whole thing.

And it's been going since 1946, with proceeds supporting military families. If you've never been, this is the year.

Tickets start around $57 at pikespeakorbust.org (that's the official site — avoid third-party resellers if you can).

A couple of tips:
- Matinee on Saturday tends to be the most popular with families with younger kids
- Parking fills up — plan to arrive 30–45 minutes early
- Bring layers; summer evenings in COS can get cool once the sun drops
