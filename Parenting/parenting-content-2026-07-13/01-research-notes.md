## Story 2: AAP Drowning Prevention Guidelines Update

**Source:** AAP Pediatrics journal / HealthyChildren.org / Medical Daily / HNGN
**Confidence:** HIGH

- **Organization:** American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP)
- **Document:** "Prevention of Drowning" policy statement
- **Published:** June 2026 issue of Pediatrics (released May 2026)
- **Updates:** AAP's 2019 drowning prevention guidance
- **Stat:** Drowning is the leading cause of death among US children ages 1–4
- **Stat:** Adolescents have the second-highest drowning fatality rate
- **Swim lessons:** Can be introduced after a child turns 1 year old; individualized based on emotional maturity, physical/cognitive ability, health concerns
- **Layered prevention:** No single method eliminates drowning risk; multiple layers required
- **Layers include:** Swim lessons + pool fencing + life jackets + close adult supervision + CPR training
- **Water watcher concept:** One designated adult whose sole responsibility is watching swimmers — no phone, no distractions; must be able to recognize distress, perform rescue, initiate CPR, summon help
- **Sources:** Pediatrics journal (publications.aap.org), HealthyChildren.org, US Swim School Association
