### STORY 1: Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers: CPSC Warning After Company Goes Out of Business
- **Status:** NEW STORY
- **Tier:** 1
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Source references:** CPSC.gov, Consumer Affairs, Class Action Lawsuit Help
- **Summary:** The CPSC issued a Product Safety Warning on July 2, 2026, for Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers. The manufacturer — Sarnia Sales LLC dba Joyful Journeys, Issaquah, WA — is now out of business and unable to offer the refund promised in the original January 8, 2026 recall. About 9,300 units were sold on Amazon.com and Walmart.com from December 2023 through October 2025 for $40–$60. The lounger's sides are too low to contain an infant, and the foot opening is wider than federal standards allow — creating fall and entrapment hazards. Action: stop using immediately; dispose of product. No refund or remedy available.

### STORY 2: AAP Updates Drowning Prevention Guidelines for Summer 2026
- **Status:** NEW STORY
- **Tier:** 1
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Source references:** Pediatrics (AAP), HealthyChildren.org, Medical Daily, HNGN, US Swim School Association
- **Summary:** The American Academy of Pediatrics published an updated "Prevention of Drowning" policy statement in the June 2026 issue of Pediatrics, revising 2019 guidance. Key updates: swim lessons can be introduced on an individualized basis after age 1; no single prevention method is sufficient; the layered approach (swim lessons + pool fencing + life jackets + close supervision + CPR training) is the new standard. Drowning remains the No. 1 cause of death for US children ages 1–4. The updated guidelines strongly emphasize the "water watcher" concept — a designated adult whose sole responsibility is watching swimmers, with no distractions.
