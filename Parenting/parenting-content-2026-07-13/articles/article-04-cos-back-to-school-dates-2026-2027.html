COS Back-to-School Dates 2026–27: D49, D11, and D20 First Day Calendars

By Jamie Rivera | July 13, 2026

Summer is going fast. For families in D49, the first day of school is just three weeks away. For D11 and D20 families, you have a few more weeks — but the time to start getting ready is now. Here are the confirmed first day of school dates for Colorado Springs' three major public school districts for the 2026–27 school year.

First Day of School Dates at a Glance

District First Day (All Students) Notes
D49 (District 49 — Falcon) August 4, 2026 New K, 6th, and 9th graders start August 3; Preschool starts August 10
D11 (Colorado Springs Schools) August 12, 2026 New teacher orientation: July 29 – August 3
D20 (Academy District 20) August 13, 2026 Check calendar.asd20.org for full calendar

D49 — District 49 (Falcon)

D49 has the earliest start in the area. All students begin August 4, but new students entering Kindergarten, 6th grade, or 9th grade get a dedicated head start on August 3 — a transition day designed to help kids in new buildings get oriented before the full school population arrives. Preschool programs begin August 10.

If you're in D49, that's 22 days from today. The full 2026–27 calendar, including early release days and holidays, is available at d49.org/discover-d49/district-calendar.

D11 — Colorado Springs Schools District 11

D11 families have until August 12. That's 30 days away — still enough time to squeeze in a few more summer activities while also starting to prep.

New teachers begin orientation July 29 through August 3, so the district is already gearing up. The 2026–27 last day of school is May 25, 2027. Full calendar at d11.org/calendars/district-calendar.

D20 — Academy District 20

Academy District 20 is the last to start, with students heading back on August 13 — one day after D11. The 2026–27 annual calendar is available at calendar.asd20.org.

Back-to-School Prep Checklist

With a few weeks still on the calendar, here's a practical checklist for getting ahead:

  • Schedule the well-child checkup. Many pediatricians in Colorado Springs fill up fast in late July and early August. Call now if your child needs a physical for school or sports.
  • Check immunization records. Colorado requires vaccinations for school attendance. Records are available through your child's pediatrician or the Colorado Immunization Information System (CIIS).
  • Shop the school supply list. Lists are typically posted on individual school websites a few weeks before the start of school. Watch your district's website or your school's app.
  • Practice the morning routine. The week before school starts, try waking up at school-day time to reset sleep schedules that have drifted during summer.
  • Talk to kids about the transition. Whether it's kindergarten, a new middle school, or a new high school, acknowledge that nerves are normal and excitement is valid.
