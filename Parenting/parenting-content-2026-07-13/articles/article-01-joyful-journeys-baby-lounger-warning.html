Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers: CPSC Warning After Company Goes Out of Business

By Sarah Morales | July 13, 2026

If you bought a Joyful Journeys Baby Lounger on Amazon or Walmart, the Consumer Product Safety Commission has urgent news: stop using it immediately — and don't expect a refund. The company that made it is out of business.

On July 2, 2026, the CPSC issued a Product Safety Warning for Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers, a step the agency takes when a product poses a serious hazard but the manufacturer can no longer fulfill a recall remedy. In this case, Sarnia Sales LLC — doing business as Joyful Journeys out of Issaquah, Washington — had previously agreed to recall the loungers but then shut down before any refunds were sent to consumers.

What's the Hazard?

The loungers fail to meet the mandatory federal safety standard for Infant Sleep Products. There are two specific problems:

  • The sides of the lounger are too low to safely contain an infant, creating a fall hazard.
  • The enclosed opening at the foot of the lounger is wider than federal standards allow, creating an entrapment hazard.

These aren't theoretical risks. Fall and entrapment hazards in infant sleep products have been linked to infant deaths. The CPSC does not issue product safety warnings for minor issues.

Who Has These Loungers?

About 9,300 Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers were sold online. They were available on Amazon.com and Walmart.com from December 2023 through October 2025, priced between $40 and $60.

If you purchased a baby lounger in that timeframe from either platform — even if it's been sitting unused in a closet — check whether it's a Joyful Journeys product.

Why There's No Refund

This is where the situation differs from a standard recall. When Joyful Journeys was operating, the CPSC announced a recall in January 2026 and the company agreed to provide full refunds. But before those refunds could be processed, Joyful Journeys went out of business.

With no company left to contact and no remedy available, the CPSC issued a Notice of Violation to the retailer and upgraded the action to a Product Safety Warning. That means:

  • There is no refund process.
  • There is no repair program.
  • There is no replacement.

The only action the CPSC is recommending is disposal.

What to Do Now

The CPSC's guidance is clear:

  1. Stop using the Joyful Journeys Baby Lounger immediately.
  2. Dispose of it so it can't be used by anyone else — including passing it along to family, selling it at a garage sale, or donating it.
  3. Do not list it for resale on Facebook Marketplace, OfferUp, or any other platform.

If you see a Joyful Journeys Baby Lounger at a secondhand sale or listed online, report it to the CPSC at cpsc.gov or 800-638-2772.

What's Next

The CPSC continues to take action against baby loungers that violate federal infant sleep product standards. This is at least the eighth such recall or warning issued in 2026 alone. If you're shopping for a baby lounger, look for products that explicitly meet or exceed the mandatory ASTM F3118 standard for infant sleep products, and check recent recalls at cpsc.gov before purchasing.

For questions, contact the CPSC directly at cpsc.gov or call 800-638-2772.
