About the Rodeo

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo is an NFR Open event, meaning it qualifies competitors for the National Finals Rodeo. Professional athletes from the US, Canadian circuits, and national champions from Mexico compete in events like bull riding, barrel racing, team roping, saddle bronc, steer wrestling, and tie-down roping. The total payout exceeds $1 million across the five-day event.

The rodeo has been running since 1946 with a mission of supporting military families — fitting for a city with Fort Carson, Peterson Space Force Base, Schriever Space Force Base, Air Force Academy, and Cheyenne Mountain Complex all in the area.

Tickets

Tickets start around $57 and are available through the official site at pikespeakorbust.org. The CPSC and the rodeo organization recommend purchasing through the official site to avoid inflated prices from third-party resellers.

Group discounts are available: groups of 20–35 receive $3 off adult Grandstand tickets, and groups of 36 or more receive $5 off.

Tips for Families

  • Matinees work well for younger kids. The Friday and Saturday 12:30 PM shows give you the full rodeo experience without a late night.
  • Plan to arrive early. Parking at Norris Penrose fills up; aim to arrive 30 to 45 minutes before showtime, especially on weekend days.
  • Dress in layers. Colorado Springs summer evenings can cool off quickly after sunset, particularly if you're sitting in the open-air grandstands.
  • Check pikespeakorbust.org for any schedule changes. Weather and event updates are posted there first.
