### STORY 1: Sabin Middle School VEX Robotics World Championship

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**

Three Colorado Springs kids are about to compete on one of the biggest stages in robotics — and they're from your neighborhood. 🤖

This week they head to St. Louis for the VEX Robotics World Championship — specifically the VEX V5 Middle School division, which runs April 25-27 at the America's Center Convention Complex. At the championship level, the field is full of teams who've been preparing for months, many from countries with deep national investment in robotics education. Sabin's team earned their place the same way everyone else did: by being good.

### STORY 2: Taylor Schreiner — 2026 Military Child of the Year

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

Meet Taylor Schreiner — Colorado Springs' own 2026 Military Child of the Year for the Space Force. 🌟

Taylor is a senior at Discovery Canyon High School, 17 years old, and was selected from over 1,000 nominees and 68 finalists representing all seven branches of the U.S. military. The award comes from Operation Homefront and is one of the most meaningful recognitions a military family can receive.

Her story is remarkable. Her father, Brig. Gen. Robert Schreiner, has served 28 years in the Space Force — and Taylor has lived through 13 permanent change-of-station moves in her lifetime. Her dad's deployments have totaled 65 months, meaning he was away for nearly a third of her life. And through all of it, Taylor didn't just adapt — she thrived.

She earned her CNA (certified nursing assistant) license at 17 and spent two years volunteering at Children's Hospital. Her goal is to become a pediatric oncologist. She's planning to attend the University of Wisconsin-Madison to study biology on a pre-med track.

Her sister Hayley was the 2023 Military Child of the Year for the same branch. Tonight, at a gala in Arlington, Virginia, Taylor accepts the 2026 award — along with a $10,000 educational grant from Operation Homefront.

We love seeing COS families recognized like this. What an incredible young person. 💙

### STORY 4: PPYMCA Summer Outdoor Pools

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM MT)**

Summer swim season in Colorado Springs is almost here — and it's time to start planning. 🏊

The PPYMCA opens its two outdoor pools on Memorial Day weekend in partnership with the City of Colorado Springs. Monument Valley Pool and Wilson Ranch Pool will run through Labor Day, giving families a full summer of swimming at two of the most established outdoor pools in the area.

Here's what you need to know before you go:

Day passes are available on the day of your visit (credit card or exact cash only). Pricing: $10 for youth ages 3-17, $15 for adults, $45 for a family pass (two adults plus up to four children). Kids age two and under swim free. Season passes are also available for members and nonmembers if your family swims regularly — worth it if you plan to go more than a handful of times.

Monument Valley Pool is in central Colorado Springs near Monument Valley Park. Wilson Ranch Pool is on the northwest side. Both have been family staples for years and fill up fast on hot summer days, so arriving early matters, especially on weekends.

For current pricing and to purchase season passes in advance, visit ppymca.org.

### STORY 5: Money Museum National Coin Week Free Open House

#### Long-Form Post -- 6:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (6:00 PM MT)**

Looking for a free Saturday outing this weekend? The Edward C. Rochette Money Museum is throwing open its doors — and there's no ticket required. 🪙

This Saturday, April 25, from 10:30 AM to 5 PM, the Money Museum at 818 N. Cascade Ave is hosting a National Coin Week Open House. The event is completely free and includes games, exhibits, and family-friendly activities. This year's National Coin Week theme is "Striking Independence: 250 Years of American Numismatics" — meaning everything ties into the story of American money and history through the country's 250th anniversary year.

The Edward C. Rochette Money Museum is run by the American Numismatic Association, and it's genuinely one of Colorado Springs' most underrated family destinations. The collection includes rare coins, currency, medals, and artifacts that span centuries of monetary history. Kids who are curious about history, design, or collecting tend to love it.

It's free. It's this Saturday. It's indoors if the weather turns. Honestly, we'd go even without a special event.

Will you be checking out the Money Museum this weekend? 👇
