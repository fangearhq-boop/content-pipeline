Discovery Canyon HS Senior Taylor Schreiner Named 2026 Military Child of the Year for the Space Force

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting

April 23, 2026

Taylor Schreiner, a 17-year-old senior at Discovery Canyon High School in Colorado Springs, has been named the 2026 Military Child of the Year for the Space Force by Operation Homefront — one of the most prestigious recognitions given to young people from military families in the United States.

She was selected from more than 1,000 nominees and 68 finalists representing all seven branches of the U.S. military. Tonight, she'll accept the award at a gala in Arlington, Virginia.

Who Taylor Schreiner Is

Taylor grew up in a family defined by service and constant change. Her father, Brig. Gen. Robert Schreiner, has served 28 years in the Space Force. Her mother, Nicole Schreiner, is a retired Air Force and Space Force civil servant. Together, the family has navigated 13 permanent change-of-station moves — 10 of them during Taylor's lifetime.

Her father has been deployed twice, with his time away totaling 65 months. That's nearly a third of Taylor's life spent without him at home. Military children absorb this kind of disruption quietly, typically switching schools, leaving friends, and building new routines with a regularity that most kids never experience. Taylor did all of that — and found a way to build something meaningful in the middle of it.

At 17, she holds a CNA (certified nursing assistant) license and has spent two years volunteering at a children's hospital. Her goal is to become a pediatric oncologist. She plans to attend the University of Wisconsin-Madison to study biology on a pre-med track.

A Family Legacy of Recognition

This isn't the first time the Schreiner family has stood on this stage. Taylor's sister, Hayley Schreiner, was named the 2023 Military Child of the Year for the Space Force — the same branch, the same award, three years earlier.

Two siblings earning the Military Child of the Year award from the same branch is extraordinarily rare. It speaks to a family environment — even one shaped by constant movement and time apart — that somehow produced two young people who turned those circumstances into fuel rather than obstacles.

About the Military Child of the Year Award

The Military Child of the Year Award is presented annually by Operation Homefront, a national nonprofit that supports military families. One recipient is selected from each branch of the military — Army, Navy, Marine Corps, Air Force, Space Force, Coast Guard, and National Guard — based on academic achievement, community service, extracurricular involvement, and demonstration of character in the face of military life's unique challenges.

Each recipient also receives a $10,000 educational grant from Operation Homefront to support their college or career goals.
