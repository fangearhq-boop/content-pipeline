PPYMCA Summer Outdoor Pools Open Memorial Day — Everything COS Families Need to Know

By Jenna Park | COS Parenting

April 23, 2026

Summer swim season in Colorado Springs is about five weeks away, and it's worth planning ahead. The PPYMCA (YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region) opens its two outdoor pools on Memorial Day weekend in partnership with the City of Colorado Springs — and they stay open through Labor Day. Whether your family is day-pass regulars or you've never been, here's what you need to know before the season starts.

The Two Pools

The PPYMCA operates two outdoor pools as part of its summer City of Colorado Springs partnership: Monument Valley Pool and Wilson Ranch Pool.

Monument Valley Pool is in central Colorado Springs, adjacent to Monument Valley Park — one of the city's most beloved green spaces. The park setting means there's room to spread out before or after your swim, and the central location makes it accessible from most parts of the city.

Wilson Ranch Pool is on the northwest side of Colorado Springs. It's a well-established community pool that draws families from the Rockrimmon, Briargate, and Flying Horse corridors, though it's open to anyone.

Both pools have been community fixtures for years. They're outdoor, they're open to the public, and they fill up fast on hot summer weekends — especially late June and July. Early arrival matters if you're going on a warm Saturday.

Pricing and Passes

Day passes can be purchased on the day of your visit at either pool. Payment is by credit card or exact cash only — no change is provided on site. Day pass pricing for the 2026 season: youth ages 3-17 are $10, adults are $15, a family pass (two adults plus up to four children) is $45, and children age two and under swim free.

If your family swims regularly, season passes are available for both YMCA members and nonmembers. A season pass typically pays for itself within a handful of visits — and it eliminates the need to carry cash or card every time you go. Visit ppymca.org for current season pass pricing, as rates may vary.

YMCA members who hold active individual or family memberships also have access to the pools as part of their membership during the summer season — check with your membership tier for specifics.

What to Know Before You Go

Colorado Springs summers run hot in July and August, and outdoor pools are in high demand during heat waves. Both Monument Valley and Wilson Ranch can reach capacity on peak days, which means an earlier arrival is worth the planning. Weekday mornings tend to be significantly less crowded than weekend afternoons.

The pools operate under standard swim safety rules — lifeguards on deck, age and ability requirements for specific areas, and standard flotation device policies. If you're bringing young children or non-swimmers, review the pool's policies in advance on the PPYMCA website.

Colorado weather can shift quickly, including summer afternoon thunderstorms that are common at the elevation. Both pools have protocols for lightning and severe weather that may result in temporary closures. Check the PPYMCA pool status page (ppymca.org/pool-status/) on days when storms are in the forecast.
