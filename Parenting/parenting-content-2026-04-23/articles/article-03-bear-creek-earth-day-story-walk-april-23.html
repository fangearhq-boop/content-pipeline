Bear Creek Nature Center Is Hosting a Free Earth Day Story Walk and Upcycled Craft Today

By Keisha Williams | COS Parenting

April 23, 2026

If you're looking for something to do this afternoon, Bear Creek Nature Center has a free event running right now. The Earth Day Story Walk and Upcycled Craft runs from 10 AM to 1 PM today at 245 Bear Creek Rd in Colorado Springs — and it's exactly the kind of low-key, outdoor, genuinely enjoyable activity that makes a spring Saturday worth getting out the door for.

No registration required. No cost. Just show up.

What the Event Is

The story walk is self-guided. Pages of a children's book are posted along a nature trail at the center, so you and your kids read the story as you walk — turning a trail into a narrative experience. It's a format that works well for young readers who need movement, and for families who want storytime to feel like something other than sitting still indoors.

After the trail, participants can head inside the nature center to make a take-home craft from upcycled materials. The craft portion fits the Earth Day theme — reusing what already exists rather than buying something new. It's a small, tangible way to make the theme feel concrete for kids.

Bear Creek Nature Center has been hosting events like this for decades. The center celebrated its 50th anniversary in 2026, and its programming consistently reflects a commitment to making Colorado's natural environment accessible and engaging for COS families across age groups.

About Bear Creek Nature Center

Bear Creek Nature Center is operated by the City of Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department. It sits along Bear Creek, surrounded by the Bear Creek Regional Park — a 355-acre open space with trails, wildlife habitat, and creek access.

The nature center building itself features natural history exhibits, live animal displays, and educational programming for school groups, families, and community members. It's free to enter on most days, and the surrounding park is open year-round for walking, wildlife viewing, and trail use.

If your family hasn't visited, today's Earth Day event is a good reason to go for the first time. And if you've been before, you know why it keeps showing up on COS family lists.

What's Next

Bear Creek Nature Center runs programming throughout the spring and summer, including school group visits, naturalist-led walks, and family events. Check the City of Colorado Springs Parks and Recreation website or call the nature center directly for upcoming events and hours. The surrounding Bear Creek Regional Park trails are open year-round — even on days without a scheduled program, it's a worthwhile stop for families who want a nature walk without the altitude of the higher mountain parks.
