The Money Museum Is Hosting a Free Open House This Saturday — Here's Why Your Family Should Go

By Marcus Rivera | COS Parenting

April 23, 2026

The Edward C. Rochette Money Museum in Colorado Springs is hosting a free public open house this Saturday, April 25, from 10:30 AM to 5 PM. The event is part of National Coin Week 2026 and includes games, exhibits, and family-friendly activities — all free, no ticket required.

If you've driven past the museum on North Cascade Avenue and never stopped, this is a reasonable occasion to finally go in.

About the Event

National Coin Week runs April 19-25, and the American Numismatic Association uses the week to celebrate the history and artistry of coins, currency, and related collectibles. This year's theme is "Striking Independence: 250 Years of American Numismatics" — a nod to the country's 250th anniversary and the long story of American money from the colonial era to today.

The open house on April 25 is the culminating public event of the week. The ANA opens the museum's doors, drops the admission fee entirely, and adds games and interactive activities specifically designed for families. It's the same permanent collection and rotating exhibits — just with added programming and no cost to get in the door.

What the Museum Is

The Edward C. Rochette Money Museum is operated by the American Numismatic Association, a national organization dedicated to the study and collection of coins, currency, medals, and tokens. The museum sits on the ANA's Colorado Springs campus at 818 N. Cascade Ave and houses one of the most significant numismatic collections in the country.

On a normal visit, you'll find exhibits on ancient coins from Greece and Rome, early American currency, rare coins with unusual mint errors, and modern commemorative coins. The museum explains not just what the objects are, but why they mattered — who made them, what systems they supported, and what they reveal about the societies that used them.

It's not exclusively for collectors. Kids who are drawn to history, design, puzzles, or hidden stories tend to find it engaging. The physical scale of the collection is impressive, and the museum does a good job making context accessible to visitors who aren't already familiar with numismatics.

Practical Details

The open house is this Saturday, April 25, from 10:30 AM to 5 PM. The museum is at 818 N. Cascade Ave — it's a straightforward drive from most parts of Colorado Springs. Parking is available on site and along the surrounding streets. No registration is required for the open house, and admission is free for the full day.

The museum's normal admission fee applies on other days. If you're planning to come back after the event, check money.org for current hours, pricing, and any temporary exhibits.

What's Next

The open house runs through 5 PM on Saturday. After the event, the museum returns to its regular schedule and standard admission pricing. If your family enjoys the visit and wants to return, the ANA runs additional programs and lectures throughout the year — check money.org for the full calendar. National Coin Week wraps up on April 25, but the museum itself is worth a return visit any time of year.
