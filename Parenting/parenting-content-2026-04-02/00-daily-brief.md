### STORY 2: Pool Safety Alert — CPSC Recalls Evajoy & Blue Wave Above-Ground Pools Before Spring Season [TIER 1]
The Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled two lines of above-ground pools — Evajoy (approximately 4,000 units) and Blue Wave — due to a drowning hazard. Both recalls involve a compression strap design that creates a foothold on the outside of the pool, allowing children to climb in even with the ladder removed. CPSC links similar pool designs to 9 children's drowning deaths (ages 22 months to 3 years) nationwide between 2007 and 2022. Evajoy recall date: February 26, 2026. Free repair available. Contact: 888-846-2988 (M–F, 9 a.m.–5 p.m. PT) or support@evajoy.co.

**Why it matters:** April is when Colorado Springs families start setting up pools. With spring temperatures arriving and Easter weekend approaching, this recall is time-critical. Families who own 48-inch or taller above-ground pools should check their model against the recall list before the first splash.

### STORY 4: CPSC Recalls Pull-String Teething Toys — Choking and Respiratory Hazard [TIER 2]
Two brands of pull-string teething toys have been recalled by the CPSC: AiTuiTui (sold by "Vanfun" on Amazon) and Yetonamr brand. The silicone strings on both products can reach the back of a baby's throat, lodge there, and cause respiratory distress, choking, or death. Parents with infants who own these products should stop use immediately. Both products were sold on Amazon.

**Why it matters:** Teething toys are among the most commonly gifted items for babies. With Easter weekend coming, these products may have been bought as gifts or still be in circulation. The choking hazard is severe — this is a stop-use-immediately recall.
