#### Long-Form Post -- 10:30 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (10:30 AM MT)**

```
Colorado Springs families: before you set up your backyard pool this spring, there's a recall you need to know about.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission recalled two brands of above-ground pools — Evajoy and Blue Wave — for a serious drowning hazard. Here's the specific issue: these pools have an external compression strap that creates a foothold on the outside of the pool wall. That foothold allows young children to climb into the pool even after you've removed the ladder. The ladder, it turns out, isn't the only way in.

The CPSC links similar pool designs to 9 children's drowning deaths between 2007 and 2022. The victims ranged from 22 months to 3 years old.

For Evajoy specifically: about 4,000 pools were sold on Amazon between March 2023 and June 2024 at prices between $550 and $1,050. The recalled models are EJ-HF032 (16 ft × 48 in), EJ-HF044 (18 ft × 52 in), and EJ-HF045 (24 ft × 52 in).

What you should do:
- Stop using the pool immediately if you have one of these models
- Call Evajoy at 888-846-2988 (Monday–Friday, 9 a.m.–5 p.m. PT) for a free repair
- Or email support@evajoy.co

April is when COS families start thinking about pool season. Check your model before the first splash.

### STORY 4: Teething Toy Recalls

#### Long-Form Post -- 12:30 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (12:30 PM MT)**

```
Easter weekend is three days away. If you're putting together baskets or picking up last-minute gifts for a baby or toddler, here's something to check before you buy.

Two brands of pull-string teething toys have been recalled by the CPSC: AiTuiTui (sold by "Vanfun" on Amazon) and Yetonamr. Both products have the same problem — the silicone strings can reach the back of an infant's throat and lodge there. The result can be respiratory distress, choking, or death.

The Yetonamr product also has silicone strings that are smaller than what the mandatory standard permits — a separate but related issue.

If you have either product at home, stop use immediately. If you were considering buying a pull-string teething toy on Amazon for a baby this weekend, do a quick CPSC search first (cpsc.gov/recalls).

The safest Easter basket move for infants: hard silicone teethers with no detachable parts, textured ring teethers, or frozen teething toys designed for little mouths. The ones with strings attached are worth a second look.

Do you keep up with CPSC recalls, or do you find them hard to track? We post these every week so you don't have to search.
