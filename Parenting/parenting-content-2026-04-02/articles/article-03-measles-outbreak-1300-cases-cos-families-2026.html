US Measles Outbreak Near 1,300 Cases: What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know About MMR Vaccines

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 2, 2026

The United States measles outbreak continues to grow, with confirmed infections approaching 1,300 cases nationally. The American Academy of Pediatrics reports that the country's "measles-eliminated" status — held since 2000 — is now officially under review. For Colorado Springs families, the action item is straightforward: verify your children's MMR vaccination status and schedule a catch-up if needed.

The Numbers and What They Mean

CIDRAP (the Center for Infectious Disease Research and Policy) reported this week that the US measles total is approaching 1,300 confirmed infections. AAP News reported the same week that 44 pediatric flu deaths have occurred this season and that measles cases are climbing to the point where US elimination status is under formal review.

The United States was declared free of endemic measles transmission in 2000 — a milestone that required sustained vaccination rates above 95 percent of the population. When vaccination rates drop below that threshold, outbreaks can take hold. The current outbreak reflects pockets of lower vaccination rates in certain communities and states.

Measles is one of the most contagious viruses known to medicine — significantly more contagious than COVID-19. One infected person can spread measles to 12 to 18 others in an unvaccinated population. The virus can remain airborne for up to two hours after an infected person has left a room.

The MMR Vaccine: What COS Families Need to Know

The MMR vaccine — which protects against measles, mumps, and rubella — is one of the most studied and effective vaccines available. The American Academy of Pediatrics and the Centers for Disease Control both recommend the same MMR schedule: one dose at 12 to 15 months, and a second dose at 4 to 6 years.

This is one area where the AAP and CDC are aligned, even amid the broader vaccine schedule differences that made news in recent weeks. Both organizations support the two-dose MMR schedule as essential for protecting children and communities.

Two doses of MMR vaccine are 97 percent effective against measles. One dose provides approximately 93 percent protection. Children who have not received both doses are significantly more vulnerable during an active outbreak.

What Colorado Springs Parents Should Do

If you're certain your children have received both MMR doses on schedule, you're in a good position. If there's any uncertainty — a move, a gap in pediatric care, a missed checkup, or a child who is overdue for their 4–6 year booster — this is the right time to call your pediatrician's office and confirm.

COS families can also check vaccination records through El Paso County Public Health (elpasocountyhealth.org). The county's immunization registry can help confirm your child's vaccination history if records are incomplete.

What's Next

The US measles outbreak is ongoing and the case count is still rising. The formal review of elimination status will take time — but the action COS families can take right now doesn't require waiting for that outcome. Verifying MMR status and scheduling catch-up doses takes one phone call. With measles spreading in multiple states, it's worth making that call this week rather than later.
