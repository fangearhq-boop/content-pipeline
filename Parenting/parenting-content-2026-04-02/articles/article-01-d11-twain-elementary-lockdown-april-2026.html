D11 Twain Elementary Lockdown Lifted: What Colorado Springs Parents Need to Know

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 2, 2026

Mark Twain Elementary School in Colorado Springs was placed on lockdown Wednesday after a threat was reported to district officials. The lockdown was lifted before noon, and police determined the threat was not credible. Here's a full rundown of what happened and what it means for D11 families.

What Happened

On Wednesday, April 1, 2026, Twain Elementary School — part of Colorado Springs District 11 — was placed under lockdown protocol after a threat was reported. Colorado Springs police responded and investigated the situation. The lockdown was lifted after an investigation determined the threat did not present an actual danger to students or staff.

KKTV reported that police said there was "nothing to indicate" the threat was credible. The Colorado Springs Gazette described it as a "non-credible threat" that temporarily locked down the school. KOAA News5 also covered the incident, with consistent reporting across all three outlets confirming the positive resolution.

District 11 spokesperson confirmed the lockdown and its lift to reporters. The school returned to normal operations after clearance.

What a School Lockdown Means in Practice

When D11 activates a lockdown protocol, students and staff are moved to interior positions away from windows and doors. Teachers follow established security procedures, and communication goes through the district's emergency notification system. Parents typically receive alerts through the district's phone or app notification systems.

A lockdown can be precautionary — meaning it's activated while a potential threat is being investigated — or it can respond to a confirmed threat. In this case, the lockdown was precautionary, and the investigation did not find evidence of a real danger.

The protocol exists precisely to protect students during the time it takes to determine whether a threat is real. That Twain's lockdown lasted only until before noon and ended with an all-clear is the best-case scenario for this kind of situation.

What Parents Are Feeling

It's worth naming something directly: receiving a notification that your child's school is on lockdown triggers an immediate stress response, regardless of how the situation resolves. That's a normal and appropriate reaction. Parents of D11 students who got alerts Wednesday — even those who knew the resolution was coming — may still be processing a difficult few hours.

Mental health professionals note that children who experience school lockdowns — even resolved, non-credible-threat situations — sometimes carry anxiety about going back to school. If your child seems more reluctant or anxious than usual in the days after this event, check in with them about what they experienced and how they're feeling.
