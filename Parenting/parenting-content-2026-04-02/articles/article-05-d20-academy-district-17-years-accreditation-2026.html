Academy District 20: 17 Consecutive Years of Colorado's Top Accreditation

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 2, 2026

In a week that's brought a D11 school lockdown and ongoing budget challenges in D49, here's a Colorado Springs education story worth celebrating: Academy School District 20 has earned Colorado's highest school district accreditation status — "Accredited with Distinction" — for 17 consecutive years.

What "Accredited with Distinction" Means

Colorado rates school districts on an accreditation scale based on academic performance, graduation rates, student growth, and other outcome measures. "Accredited with Distinction" is the highest rating available — the designation given to districts that consistently outperform state expectations across multiple measures.

Earning that distinction for 17 consecutive years means that D20 has maintained top-tier performance across a span that includes multiple economic cycles, a global pandemic, significant changes in state education policy, and substantial growth in the district's student population. A generation of Colorado Springs students — children who started kindergarten in 2009 and graduated in 2022 — went through D20 during every single year of that stretch.

Why It Matters for Families

For parents choosing where to live or which schools to enroll their children in, accreditation status is one of the most reliable broad-strokes indicators of a district's performance. It's not a perfect measure — individual schools within a district vary, and accreditation doesn't capture everything families care about, from arts programming to special education resources to extracurricular offerings.

But consistent state recognition over 17 years signals stability, sustained investment in academic outcomes, and effective leadership at the district level. That kind of record doesn't happen by accident. It requires faculty, administrators, and community support to work in the same direction year after year.

For D20 families in neighborhoods north of Colorado Springs — Briargate, Flying Horse, Wolf Ranch, Northgate — this week's COS education news provides a welcome counterpoint to the challenges facing other districts in the region.

The Bigger COS Education Picture

Colorado Springs is served by three large public school districts — D11, D20, and D49 — along with smaller districts and charter schools. Each serves a distinct geography and demographic, and each faces distinct challenges in 2026. D49 has navigated a significant budget crisis and staff reductions. D11 has managed the PowerSchool data breach and this week's school security incident at Twain Elementary.

D20's sustained accreditation record is not a comparison tool or a statement about the other districts' challenges — it's simply its own achievement, worth documenting and sharing with the families who have built their lives around those 40 schools. Good news in COS education deserves as much space as the difficult news.

What's Next

Spring semester in D20 includes Colorado Measures of Academic Success (CMAS) testing for grades 3 through 8, AP testing for high schoolers in May, and end-of-year programming across the district. Families looking for specific D20 school information, enrollment details, or the 2026-27 calendar can find everything at asd20.org. For COS parents deciding on school options for fall 2026, D20's record provides a strong foundation for that conversation.
