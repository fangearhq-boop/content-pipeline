Pool Safety Alert: Evajoy and Blue Wave Above-Ground Pools Recalled Before Spring Setup Season

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 2, 2026

As Colorado Springs families prepare for spring and summer, the Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled two lines of above-ground pools — Evajoy and Blue Wave — due to a serious drowning hazard. The recall covers pools 48 inches tall or higher. If you own one, here's exactly what you need to know before the first splash of the season.

What Was Recalled and Why

Both the Evajoy and Blue Wave recalls involve the same fundamental design issue: an external compression strap on the outside of the pool wall. That strap creates a foothold — essentially a step — that children can use to climb up and over the pool wall, even when the ladder has been removed.

Parents who follow standard pool safety protocols know to remove the ladder when the pool is not in use, limiting access for small children. These recalled pools undermine that protection. With the compression strap in place, a young child can still climb in without a ladder.

The CPSC links similar pool designs to 9 children's drowning deaths between 2007 and 2022. The victims ranged in age from 22 months to 3 years old.

What to Do Right Now

If you own an Evajoy pool matching one of the recalled models, stop using it immediately. Contact Evajoy directly at 888-846-2988 or support@evajoy.co to arrange the free repair. Do not fill the pool for the season until the repair has been made. For Blue Wave pool owners, check the CPSC recall database at cpsc.gov/recalls to confirm your model and get contact information for the remedy.

If you're not sure whether your pool is covered, look at the model number on the pool packaging or the pool itself, and cross-reference it with the recalled models listed above. When in doubt, call 888-846-2988 — Evajoy's recall hotline can confirm whether your specific pool is included.

Spring Pool Safety in Colorado Springs

April is the beginning of pool setup season in Colorado Springs. The combination of spring break, warming temperatures, and Easter weekend means many families are thinking about getting their pools ready. This is exactly the moment to verify that your pool is safe before any child enters the water.

Even pools that are not part of this recall benefit from a spring safety check. CPSC recommends: verifying that pool walls are structurally sound, that filters are functioning, that drain covers are secure (loose drain covers create entrapment hazards), and that any pool fence or barrier meets height and latch requirements. For Colorado Springs families, El Paso County code requires fencing around permanent pools — check with the city at coloradosprings.gov for current residential pool safety requirements.

What's Next

The Evajoy recall remedy is a free repair — not a product replacement or refund, but a fix that addresses the foothold hazard. Once the repair is complete, the pool should be safe to use following standard safety protocols. If you have not yet received a repair or confirmation from Evajoy, do not use the pool for the season. Free pools aren't worth a preventable drowning — and a one-call fix is the only safe path forward for recalled models.
