CPSC Recalls Pull-String Teething Toys: AiTuiTui and Yetonamr Products Pose Choking and Respiratory Hazard

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 2, 2026

With Easter weekend approaching, Colorado Springs parents should be aware of a new CPSC recall involving pull-string teething toys. Two brands — AiTuiTui and Yetonamr — have been recalled because the silicone strings on these products can lodge in the back of an infant's throat, posing a choking and respiratory hazard. If you have either product, stop use immediately.

What Was Recalled

The Consumer Product Safety Commission recalled two pull-string teething toy products:

AiTuiTui Pull String Teething Toys — sold on Amazon by seller "Vanfun." These toys have colorful silicone strings designed for infants to pull and chew. The hazard: the strings are long enough to reach the back of a baby's throat. If a string lodges there, it can cause respiratory distress, choking, or death.

Yetonamr Pull String Teething Toys — also sold on Amazon. This product has the same core hazard: silicone strings that can reach the throat. Additionally, the Yetonamr product's silicone strings are smaller than what the mandatory standard permits — a separate violation that compounds the hazard.

Both recalls are classified as stop-use-immediately. There is no repair option; these products should not be given to any child.

The Hazard Explained

Unlike a small part that can be swallowed, the hazard with pull-string teething toys is different: the string stays connected to the toy while the end reaches the back of the throat. A baby's natural reflex may be to continue pulling on the string, which can deepen the lodging. Parents may not immediately recognize the hazard because the toy appears intact — nothing looks broken or detached.

The CPSC classifies this as a respiratory hazard because the string can obstruct the airway without being fully swallowed. Parents should check Amazon order history if they're not sure whether they purchased these products.

Easter Timing: Check Gifts Before the Basket

Easter is this weekend, which means many families are assembling baskets or picking up last-minute gifts for infants. Teething toys are popular gift choices for babies. Before placing any pull-string teething toy in an Easter basket or giving it as a gift, check whether the brand is included in this recall.

For safe teething toy alternatives: hard one-piece silicone teethers, ring-style teethers without detachable parts, or textured rubber teethers with no strings attached are all options that avoid this specific hazard. The CPSC recall database at cpsc.gov/recalls lists all current recalls and is searchable by product type.

How to Report Unsafe Products

If your child has been injured by a recalled product or a product you believe is unsafe, you can report it directly to the CPSC at SaferProducts.gov. The CPSC Hotline is also available at 800-638-2772. These reports help the CPSC identify patterns across consumer products and take action on hazards before they cause widespread harm.

What's Next

If you own either the AiTuiTui or Yetonamr pull-string teething toy, dispose of it — do not donate it, resell it, or give it away, as it remains a hazard to any infant who uses it. Check your Amazon order history if you're unsure.
