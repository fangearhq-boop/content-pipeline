### STORY 1: Walmart Mainstays Dresser Recall — Tip-Over Hazard, Full Refund
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Type:** Article + 1 X post + FB Long-Form + FB Image Caption + 2 image concepts

Walmart recalled approximately 153,900 Mainstays 9-Drawer Fabric Dressers on May 28, 2026 due to tip-over and entrapment hazards. The dressers violate the STURDY Act mandatory safety standard. While no injuries have been reported, the dressers are unstable if not anchored to the wall. Sold at Walmart stores nationwide and on Walmart.com from September 2023 through March 2026 for about $80. Consumers should stop using the dresser if not wall-anchored and contact Walmart for a full refund.

### STORY 2: Cheyenne Mountain State Park Gets 484-Acre Expansion — 11 New Campsites
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Pillar:** Local Events
**Type:** Article + 1 X post + FB Long-Form + FB Image Caption + 2 image concepts

Colorado Parks and Wildlife announced the expansion of Cheyenne Mountain State Park by 484 acres on May 29, 2026. The expansion — two decades in the making — includes the Denman property (357.2 acres, $8.93M) and an adjacent City of Colorado Springs parcel (125.8 acres). The park also opened 11 new full-hookup campsites (total now 62), plus 10 tent sites and a group tent area. Funded by the Colorado Lottery and the City of Colorado Springs.

### STORY 3: Misco Sports Light-Up Racket Set Recall — Button Batteries Accessible to Kids
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Type:** Article + 1 X post + FB Long-Form + FB Image Caption + 2 image concepts

Missry Associates recalled Misco Sports Light-Up Racket Sets on May 28, 2026 because the battery compartment screw does not stay attached, making button cell batteries in the shuttlecock easily accessible to young children. Swallowed button cell batteries can cause serious injuries including internal chemical burns and death. Consumers should stop use immediately and contact Missry Associates for a full refund.
