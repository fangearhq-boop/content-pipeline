## STORY 1: Walmart Mainstays Dresser Recall

### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

```
If you have a Mainstays 9-Drawer Fabric Dresser from Walmart in your child's bedroom, stop using it immediately — unless it is anchored to the wall.

Walmart recalled approximately 153,900 of these dressers on May 28 due to tip-over and entrapment hazards. The dressers violate the STURDY Act, the federal mandatory safety standard for clothing storage furniture. While no injuries have been reported yet, tip-over furniture is one of the leading causes of preventable furniture-related deaths in children.

The recalled dresser has a black metal frame with nine brown or black fabric drawers, measuring about 40 inches long by 45 inches tall. It was sold at Walmart stores and on Walmart.com from September 2023 through March 2026 for about $80.

What to do: Return the drawers to any Walmart store for a full refund. Dispose of the frame per your local regulations. If your dresser is not wall-anchored, move it away from areas where children sleep or play now.

Has anyone in the COS community had this dresser? Let families know in the comments.
```

## STORY 2: Cheyenne Mountain State Park Expansion

### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

```
Colorado Springs families just got a lot more room to roam.

Colorado Parks and Wildlife announced Friday that Cheyenne Mountain State Park will grow by 484 acres — the result of two land acquisitions that officials say were more than two decades in the making. The expansion adds the Denman property (357 acres, acquired for $8.93 million) plus an adjacent 125-acre city parcel managed under a partnership between Colorado Springs and CPW.

Along with the new land, the park opened 11 new full-hookup campsites, bringing the total to 62. There are also 10 new tent sites and a new group tent area — great for multi-family trips, extended family outings, or youth group camping.

Funding came from the Colorado Lottery and the City of Colorado Springs. Governor and CPW officials marked the occasion with a ribbon-cutting at the park on Friday.

Whether you're planning a COS camping weekend this summer or just want more trails to explore, this is excellent news for our community.
```

## STORY 3: Misco Sports Light-Up Racket Set Recall

### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

```
COS parents: if your family has a Misco Sports Light-Up Racket Set, take the shuttlecock away from kids right now and contact Missry Associates for a refund.

Missry Associates recalled these racket sets on May 28 because the screw on the shuttlecock's battery compartment does not stay attached when opened. That means small button cell batteries — the coin-shaped kind — can become accessible to young children without any adult awareness.

Button cell batteries are one of the most dangerous ingestion hazards for young children. When swallowed, they can cause severe internal chemical burns within two hours. The risk is serious enough to be fatal.

Steps to take today: Stop using the set. Remove the shuttlecock from children's reach. Contact Missry Associates for a full refund. If a child has swallowed a button cell battery, go to the emergency room immediately — do not wait for symptoms.

As summer begins and you're stocking up on backyard toys, this is also a good moment to check any battery-powered toy with accessible compartments.

Do you have this set at home?
```

## STORY 4: Colorado Renaissance Festival Family Guide

### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

```
Two weeks from now, COS families can step into a medieval village — and they do not have to drive far.

The Colorado Renaissance Festival opens Saturday, June 13 and runs every Saturday and Sunday through August 2 on I-25 between Denver and Colorado Springs, about 45 minutes from COS. It's one of the most popular annual events on the Front Range, and it holds up for families with kids across a wide age range.

For younger children, the FamilyFest zone is the draw — hands-on activities, games, and entertainment designed for kids. Beyond that, there are live music stages, jousting and theatrical performances, a food court, arts and crafts vendors, and carnival rides. Most families spend about four to five hours there comfortably.

Tips for COS families: Arriving at opening (around 10 a.m.) is the best way to beat the afternoon heat. Bring sun protection and water. Tickets bought in advance online are slightly less expensive and skip the gate line. Costumes are welcome but not required.
```
