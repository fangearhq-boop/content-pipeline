Misco Sports Light-Up Racket Set Recalled: Button Cell Battery Hazard in Children's Toy

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 30, 2026

Colorado Springs parents should check their summer toy bins. Missry Associates recalled Misco Sports Light-Up Racket Sets on May 28, 2026, because the battery compartment on the shuttlecock can open and expose small button cell batteries to young children. The batteries are a swallowing hazard and can cause severe, life-threatening internal injuries.

If your family owns this racket set, remove it from children's reach immediately and contact Missry Associates for a full refund.

What Makes This Recall Serious

Button cell batteries — the small, flat, coin-shaped batteries used in small electronics and toys — are among the most serious ingestion hazards for young children. When swallowed, they can lodge in a child's esophagus and generate an electrical current that causes rapid, severe chemical burns to internal tissue. Injuries can occur within two hours of ingestion, and they can be fatal.

The specific hazard with this racket set is that the screw securing the shuttlecock's battery compartment does not stay attached when the compartment is opened. This allows a child to access the batteries without adult assistance — and without warning.

Who Is at Risk

Young children — typically under age 5 — are the primary risk group for button battery ingestion because they explore objects with their mouths. However, toddlers and preschool-age children in households with older siblings who use racket sets are also at risk. If an older child plays with the set and the battery compartment opens, younger siblings who find the shuttlecock on the floor are in danger.

The recall affects any Misco Sports Light-Up Racket Set purchased from Missry Associates. Families should check their game closet, garage, and any outdoor toy bins.

What to Do

The CPSC advises the following steps:

  • Stop using the racket set immediately.
  • Take the shuttlecock away from children right away.
  • Contact Missry Associates for a full refund.
  • Do not attempt to tape over the battery compartment as a fix — this is not a safe remedy.

If a child has swallowed a button cell battery, go to the emergency room immediately. Do not wait for symptoms. Time is critical in button battery ingestion cases.

What's Next

As summer begins and families stock up on backyard and park toys, this is a good moment to review every battery-powered toy in your home. Products with accessible battery compartments — especially those with screws or latches that can loosen over time — should be checked regularly. For young children, the safest rule is to keep any toy with button cell batteries out of reach at all times.

The CPSC maintains an updated recall database at cpsc.gov/Recalls. Colorado Springs parents can subscribe to recall email alerts to stay informed as new hazards are identified throughout the year.
