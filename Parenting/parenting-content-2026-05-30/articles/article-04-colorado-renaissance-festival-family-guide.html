Colorado Renaissance Festival 2026: A COS Family Guide to Medieval Weekends Starting June 13

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 30, 2026

Two weeks from now, a medieval village rises on the Colorado Front Range — and Colorado Springs families do not have to travel far to get there. The Colorado Renaissance Festival opens Saturday, June 13, 2026, and runs every Saturday and Sunday through August 2 on I-25 between Denver and Colorado Springs.

For families looking to fill a summer weekend with something that genuinely entertains everyone from age 3 to age 13 (and the adults pretending not to love it), this is one of the most popular annual events in the region.

What to Expect

The festival is set in a recreated medieval village with costumed performers, artisans, entertainers, and vendors throughout the grounds. For families, the FamilyFest area is the main draw — it includes hands-on activities, games, and entertainment specifically designed for children.

Beyond the kids' area, the full grounds include live music across multiple stages, stage shows featuring comedy, jousting, and theatrical performances, a food court with vendors ranging from classic fair food to medieval-themed fare, and arts and crafts booths featuring handmade jewelry, pottery, clothing, and woodwork. Carnival rides and archery are also part of the experience.

Most families find the show-hopping and browsing take about four to five hours to cover thoroughly.

Tips for COS Families

The festival is located on I-25, approximately 45 minutes north of Colorado Springs. Arriving at opening (typically 10 a.m.) is recommended to beat the afternoon heat, which can be significant on summer Saturdays. Sun protection and water bottles are strongly advised.

Younger children do best with a mix of watching performances and exploring the interactive areas. The FamilyFest zone is stroller-accessible, and there are typically shaded rest areas scattered throughout the grounds. Many families bring themed costumes — pirates, fairies, and royal cloaks are common — though street clothes are entirely welcome.

Tickets can be purchased in advance online. Many families find that buying tickets ahead of time is both faster and slightly less expensive than at the gate. Check the official website for 2026 pricing and any themed weekends scheduled for specific dates.

What's Next

The Renaissance Festival runs through August 2, giving COS families six full weekends to choose from between now and mid-summer. If you plan to go on a Saturday, weekend mornings are the least crowded time slot. Sundays are typically slightly less attended than Saturdays.

For ticket information, hours, and the full lineup of themed weekends and special events, visit the festival's official website. If you have been before and have tips for first-timers, this is a great thing to share in your parent community — the Renaissance Festival is the kind of event where a little insider knowledge makes the experience significantly better.
