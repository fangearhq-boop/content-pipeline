Cheyenne Mountain State Park Expands by 484 Acres: New Campsites and Trails for COS Families

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 30, 2026

Colorado Springs families who love Cheyenne Mountain State Park just got a lot more room to explore. Colorado Parks and Wildlife announced Friday that the park will grow by 484 acres — the result of two separate land acquisitions that officials say were more than two decades in the making.

The expansion adds new trails, new camping infrastructure, and new open space for a park that already draws tens of thousands of families each year. For many COS residents, Cheyenne Mountain State Park is the closest thing to a backcountry camping experience without leaving El Paso County.

What's Being Added

The two new land parcels total 484 acres. The larger piece — 357 acres known as the Denman property — was acquired by Colorado Parks and Wildlife for $8.93 million. The adjacent 125-acre parcel was purchased by the City of Colorado Springs and will be managed as part of the state park under a memorandum of understanding between the city and CPW.

Along with the land acquisition, the park opened 11 new full-hookup campsites, bringing its total to 62 hookup sites. Families who prefer tent camping will also find 10 new tent sites and a new group tent area. The group tent area is particularly useful for multi-family camping trips, extended family gatherings, or school or youth group outings.

Why This Matters for COS Families

Cheyenne Mountain State Park is unique among Colorado Springs' outdoor spaces because it offers something that the urban open spaces (Red Rock Canyon, Garden of the Gods, Ute Valley) do not: overnight camping within the city limits. For families who want to introduce young children to camping without driving hours into the mountains, the park has long been a first step.

With 62 full-hookup campsites now available, families with RVs or trailers have significantly more options during the peak summer season. The new tent sites also help address the high demand the park sees from June through August, when reservations frequently fill within hours of becoming available.

The expanded park also means more trail mileage over time. While CPW has not announced specific new trail routes tied to the Denman property, additional acreage typically translates to additional loop trails and access points in the coming seasons.

How It Got Funded

The expansion was funded through a combination of the Colorado Lottery — which has directed hundreds of millions of dollars toward state parks, trails, and open space since 1992 — and the City of Colorado Springs. Colorado Governor and CPW officials attended a ribbon-cutting event at the park on Friday.

What's Next

Reservations for the new campsites can be made through the Colorado Parks and Wildlife reservation system at cpw.state.co.us. Summer weekends fill quickly, so families planning to use the new hookup sites should book as early as possible. The 10 new tent sites and group area should also be available through the same reservation system.

For families who have not visited Cheyenne Mountain State Park, this summer is an ideal time to go. The park features more than 20 miles of trails accessible to families with strollers and young hikers, a full visitor center, wildlife observation areas, and now significantly more camping capacity. It is approximately 10 minutes from downtown Colorado Springs at 410 JL Ranch Heights Road.
