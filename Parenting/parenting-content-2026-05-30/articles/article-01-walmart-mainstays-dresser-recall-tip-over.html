Walmart Recalls Mainstays 9-Drawer Dresser: Tip-Over Hazard Affects Over 150,000 Units

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 30, 2026

Colorado Springs parents who purchased a Mainstays 9-Drawer Fabric Dresser from Walmart should take action today. Walmart recalled approximately 153,900 of these dressers on May 28, 2026, citing tip-over and entrapment hazards that violate the federal mandatory safety standard for clothing storage furniture.

No injuries have been reported yet — but tip-over furniture is one of the leading causes of preventable furniture-related deaths in young children, and the CPSC is urging immediate action from any family that has this product.

What the Recalled Dresser Looks Like

The recalled dresser has a black metal frame with nine fabric drawers in either brown or black. It measures approximately 40 inches long, 13.75 inches wide, and 45 inches tall, and weighs about 32 pounds. The tracking and lot information, including a manufacture date in MM/YYYY format, is printed on a label under the dresser's top panel.

The dresser was sold at Walmart stores nationwide and on Walmart.com from September 2023 through March 2026, priced at approximately $80. If your family purchased a dresser of this description during that time period, check the label before your children return to the room.

What the Hazard Means

Furniture tip-over incidents happen when children pull out drawers to use as steps, causing the dresser to lose balance and fall forward. Heavier, taller furniture presents the greatest risk. At 45 inches tall and 32 pounds, this dresser poses a serious threat if it falls on a young child.

The STURDY Act, signed into federal law in 2022, established mandatory stability standards for clothing storage units to prevent exactly these incidents. This dresser fails that standard. The CPSC's recall is mandatory, not voluntary — meaning the product cannot continue to be sold.

What to Do

If the dresser is already properly anchored to the wall using a wall anchor or anti-tip strap, you may continue using it. However, if it is not anchored — or if you are unsure — the CPSC recommends the following steps immediately:

  • Move the dresser to a room or location where children cannot access it.
  • Return the drawers to any Walmart store.
  • Contact Walmart for a full refund.
  • Dispose of the dresser frame according to local and state regulations.

Consumers can also contact Walmart directly at walmart.com or call their customer service line for instructions on the refund process.

What's Next

If you are unsure whether your dresser is the recalled model, check the label under the top panel for the lot and manufacture date. When in doubt, treat the dresser as recalled and contact Walmart.

As summer arrives and Colorado Springs families spend more time indoors during hot afternoons, it is worth doing a quick safety check of children's bedroom furniture throughout your home. Anti-tip straps for any tall furniture — dressers, bookshelves, and wardrobe units — are inexpensive, available at most hardware stores, and can be installed in minutes. For questions about furniture safety standards, visit cpsc.gov.
