Free Summer Meals for Kids in Colorado Springs: Where to Go, What to Know

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 30, 2026

As Colorado Springs schools wrap up for the summer, free breakfast and lunch are available to any child under 18 — no enrollment required, no income verification, no questions asked. Multiple programs across the city are already operating or will begin this week, and this guide covers what COS families need to know.

Who Qualifies

Any Colorado youth 18 years old or younger qualifies for free meals through the USDA Summer Food Service Program. The program is explicitly designed to be barrier-free — there are no income limits, no documentation requirements, and no enrollment process. Children simply arrive at a participating site during meal hours and receive a meal.

This is different from the school-year free and reduced-price lunch program, which requires application and income verification. The summer program removes all of those hurdles.

Where to Find Free Meals in Colorado Springs

Multiple local programs operate across different parts of the city:

District 49 (East COS / Falcon area): D49 runs free breakfast and lunch for children ages 1–18 from June 1 through June 19 at multiple school and community locations in the district's service area. No D49 enrollment is required and the program is open to any child in that age range. Visit d49.org for specific site locations and meal times.

District 11 (Central and South COS): D11 operates a free lunch program at the East Library branch. The service runs weekdays in June and July for any youth under 18. No D11 enrollment required.

Statewide USDA sites: In addition to district-run programs, Colorado participates in the federal Summer Food Service Program, which funds meals at sites including schools, parks, community centers, and libraries across the city. The Colorado Department of Education maintains a site locator at cde.state.co.us.

How to Find a Site Near You

The USDA operates a summer meals site locator at summerfood.fns.usda.gov. Families can enter their zip code to find participating sites near their home. The site locator is updated throughout the summer as new locations are added or hours change.

Families can also text "FOOD" to 304-304 to receive a list of nearby summer meal sites by phone. This service works across Colorado.

What's Next

These programs run through the summer months, with many extending into late July or early August depending on the site. Families who rely on school meals during the year are encouraged to identify their nearest summer site as soon as possible, as some locations have limited capacity.

If your family does not need the meals, knowing where they are available means you can share the information with neighbors, daycare providers, or families in your network who may benefit. Summer food insecurity affects more COS families than many residents realize, and spreading the word about these zero-barrier programs is one of the most practical ways to help.
