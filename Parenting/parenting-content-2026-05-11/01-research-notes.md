## STORY 2: EEMB USA Battery Recall

**Recall facts (HIGH — CPSC.gov, Recall No. 26-465):**
- Recall number: 26-465
- Company: EEMB USA
- Product: Lithium coin battery packs (individual pouches)
- Units recalled: approximately 312,100
- Models: CR2025, CR2032, CR2450, CR2477, CR2016, CR1220, CR1225, CR1616, CR1620, CR1632, CR2025-10
- Pack sizes: 5-pack, 10-pack, 20-pack
- Sales: Amazon.com, August 2023 through April 2026
- Price: $3 to $9
- Violation: Pouches not child-resistant as required by Reese's Law (federal statute for child-resistant packaging of coin batteries)
- Hazard: When coin/button batteries are swallowed: internal chemical burns, serious injury, death within hours
- Incidents/injuries: None reported

**Consumer action (HIGH — CPSC.gov):**
- Stop using recalled batteries immediately
- Store where children cannot reach them
- Contact EEMB USA for full refund
- Email: info@a2batt.com
- Online: www.eemb.com/recall (or visit eemb.com and click "Recall")

**Coin battery danger context (HIGH — CPSC/pediatric sources):**
- Coin/button batteries can cause internal burns within 2 hours of ingestion
- Children under age 5 are most at risk
- Do NOT induce vomiting; call Poison Control 1-800-222-1222 immediately

## STORY 5: AAP Mental Health Framework 2026

**Report facts (MEDIUM — AAP Pediatrics journal, EdSurge):**
- Report title: "Framework for Approaching Healthy Mental and Emotional Development in Pediatrics"
- Published: 2026 (AAP Pediatrics journal, DOI: 10.1542/peds.2026-076620)
- Key message: Proactive whole-child approach to mental health, not just symptom treatment
- Context: Mental and emotional concerns in childhood at "unprecedented high"
- Focus: Fostering healthy relationships, whole health, from infancy through adolescence
- Emphasis on protective factors: parent-child bonds, consistent routines, physical activity, sleep, community connection
- AAP urges pediatricians AND families to be proactive partners

**Practical guidance for COS families (from report context):**
- Strong parent-child bond is the single most important protective factor
- Physical activity is protective against anxiety and depression
- Screen time: quality over quantity (connection with previous May 9 coverage, but different angle — this is mental health, not just screen limits)
- Consistent sleep schedules matter especially for elementary-age children
- Community connection — neighbors, religious community, sports teams — is protective
