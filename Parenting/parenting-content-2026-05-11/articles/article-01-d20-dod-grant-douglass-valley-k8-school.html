D20 Wins $42.7M Federal Grant for New Douglass Valley K-8 School at Air Force Academy

By Sarah Morales | May 11, 2026 | COS Parenting

Academy District 20 is getting a brand-new K-8 school — funded by a $42.7 million federal grant from the Department of Defense.

The Grant

The Department of Defense announced on May 7 that it has awarded Academy District 20 a $42.7 million grant through the Public Schools on Military Installations program. That federal funding is the largest share of a $54.1 million total project to construct a new Douglass Valley K-8 school at the U.S. Air Force Academy.

For Colorado Springs families — particularly those connected to the Air Force Academy — this is significant news. The grant is one of the largest school construction investments D20 has received in recent memory, and it signals a serious commitment from the federal government to the families who serve at the installation.

What the New School Will Look Like

The new Douglass Valley K-8 will cover 92,000 square feet and is designed with modern learning principles in mind. Key features include:

  • A learning stair — an open, flexible space designed for informal learning and community gathering
  • Classroom pods with natural light — connecting students to their environment
  • Activity commons areas for both group and independent work
  • Outdoor connections built into the building design

The school will serve 787 students in pre-kindergarten through eighth grade.

That design language — natural light, flexible spaces, outdoor connections — reflects what research consistently shows about how kids learn best. Colorado Springs families will recognize it as a school built for Colorado's outdoor-focused culture, not just a box with classrooms in it.

Why D20 Is Getting This Money

The DoD's Public Schools on Military Installations program funds construction at schools that serve military families on base. The program recognizes that quality education is part of what sustains military readiness — families are more likely to stay in service when they know their kids are in good schools.

Academy District 20 serves military families across the Colorado Springs area, including at the Air Force Academy. The district has earned a reputation as one of Colorado's strongest — Accredited with Distinction by the state for 17 consecutive years — and this investment reflects that reputation.

What's Next

The $54.1 million project is now funded. Construction timelines will be determined by D20, but families with children at Douglass Valley or planning to enroll can watch the district's official communications at asd20.org for updates on breaking ground and expected completion. This is the kind of investment that takes years to see completed — but it's the right kind of long-term thinking for Colorado Springs families.
