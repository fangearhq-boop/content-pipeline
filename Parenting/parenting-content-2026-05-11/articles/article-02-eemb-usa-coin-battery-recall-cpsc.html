CPSC Recall: EEMB USA Coin Battery Packs — 312,100 Units Sold on Amazon Not Child-Safe

By Jamie Rivera | May 11, 2026 | COS Parenting

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued Recall No. 26-465 for approximately 312,100 EEMB USA lithium coin battery packs because the pouches lack child-resistant packaging required by federal law.

What's Been Recalled

EEMB USA is recalling approximately 312,100 lithium coin battery packs in cooperation with the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission. The recalled batteries were sold in five-, 10-, and 20-packs on Amazon.com from August 2023 through April 2026, priced between $3 and $9.

The affected models include: CR2025, CR2032, CR2450, CR2477, CR2016, CR1220, CR1225, CR1616, CR1620, CR1632, and CR2025-10. The word "EEMB" appears on the battery and in the upper left corner of the pouch. If you've purchased batteries on Amazon in the past three years, check your supply now.

Why This Is Dangerous

The recalled battery pouches violate Reese's Law, the federal statute requiring child-resistant packaging for coin and button-cell batteries. Without that protection, a young child can easily open the pouch and access the batteries directly.

Coin batteries are one of the most dangerous items a young child can swallow. When ingested, a coin battery can lodge in the esophagus and begin causing severe internal chemical burns within just two hours. The damage is not always immediately visible, and the burns can be life-threatening. Children under age 5 are most at risk because of the size of their esophagus relative to the battery.

No injuries have been reported in connection with this specific recall. The goal is to act now, before an incident occurs.

Quick Reference: What to Do

StepAction
1Stop using the recalled batteries immediately
2Move them somewhere completely out of children's reach — a locked cabinet or high shelf
3Contact EEMB USA for a full refund
4Check Amazon purchase history if unsure whether you bought EEMB batteries

To get your refund:

  • Email: info@a2batt.com
  • Website: eemb.com/recall (or visit eemb.com and click "Recall" at the top)

If a Child Swallows a Battery

Do NOT induce vomiting. Call Poison Control immediately at 1-800-222-1222. Coin battery ingestion is a medical emergency — get to an emergency room right away. Time matters: the first two hours are critical.

What's Next

This recall is a good reminder to do a quick sweep of your home for coin batteries. Remote controls, key fobs, hearing aids, small clocks, toys, and fitness trackers all commonly use them. Even batteries that are NOT part of this recall should be stored out of children's reach. For Colorado Springs families, the CPSC's full list of current recalls is always available at cpsc.gov/Recalls.
