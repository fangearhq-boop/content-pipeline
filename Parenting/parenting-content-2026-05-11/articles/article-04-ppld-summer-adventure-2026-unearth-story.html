PPLD Summer Adventure 2026: Free "Unearth a Story" Program — Register at ppld.org

By Jamie Rivera | May 11, 2026 | COS Parenting

The Pikes Peak Library District's free summer reading program is back with a new theme: "Unearth a Story," focused on dinosaurs, paleontology, and archaeology.

The Program

Every summer, the Pikes Peak Library District (PPLD) runs Summer Adventure — a free, all-ages program designed to keep kids reading and learning over the break. For 2026, the national Collaborative Summer Library Program has selected the theme "Unearth a Story," built around dinosaurs, paleontology, and archaeology. For families with kids who light up at the sight of a T. rex or who've been begging to visit a dig site, this theme is built for them.

The program is simple: participate in 30 days of activities over the summer. Each day, you can choose to Read, Explore, or Create something. Track your progress on a physical game card at the library, or log your activities digitally through the Beanstack app or at ppld.beanstack.org. There are rewards along the way for reaching milestones.

Why Sign Up Now

D11's last day of school is May 22. That means summer officially starts in just over a week for many Colorado Springs families. Summer slide — the well-documented loss of reading skills over the break, particularly for younger readers — is a real concern, especially in the critical early elementary years. A structured, self-paced program like Summer Adventure helps kids maintain their skills while giving them something to look forward to each day.

The "Unearth a Story" theme lends itself to some great extension activities beyond reading. Families can visit the Denver Museum of Nature and Science's paleontology collection, watch age-appropriate documentaries, or even try a backyard "dig" using dollar-store brushes and a sandbox. Libraries often host themed programs throughout the summer — check your local PPLD branch for events tied to the "Unearth a Story" theme.

For All Ages

Summer Adventure isn't just for kids. Parents, grandparents, and teens can all participate alongside younger family members. There's something kind of wonderful about a household where everyone is reading — it normalizes the habit, and kids who see adults reading are significantly more likely to become lifelong readers themselves.

What's Next

Register at ppld.org, download the Beanstack app, or stop in at any PPLD branch to pick up your game card. Multiple branch locations serve all parts of Colorado Springs and the surrounding county. With D11's school year ending May 22 and D20 and D49 wrapping up shortly after, now is the ideal window to get signed up before the summer chaos begins.
