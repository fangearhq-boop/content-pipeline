COS Schools Shift: Charter and District Schools Co-Locating Across El Paso County in 2026-27

By Sarah Morales | May 11, 2026 | COS Parenting

Multiple Colorado Springs schools are changing buildings or sharing campuses for the 2026-27 school year — here's what families need to know.

What's Happening

A May 11 report in the Colorado Springs Gazette revealed that several El Paso County schools — charter and traditional district schools alike — will be relocating or sharing campuses when the new school year begins this fall. The changes are driven by renovation projects, enrollment shifts, and strategic planning by both District 11 and District 49 leadership.

Here's a breakdown of the changes currently known:

James Irwin Elementary — Temporary Relocation to D49 Campus

James Irwin Elementary, a D11 charter school, is undergoing a year-long renovation project that includes asbestos abatement — a significant construction undertaking that requires the building to be vacated. For the 2026-27 school year, James Irwin Elementary students will temporarily relocate to the James Irwin Elementary - Canada Drive campus, which is located in District 49.

This is the same school, the same community, and the same staff — just a different building for one year. That said, the logistics change meaningfully for families: bus routes, drop-off and pickup procedures, and the overall geography of the school day will all look different. If your child attends James Irwin Elementary, reach out to school administration now to understand what the transition looks like before the school year begins.

East Hills Academy — Co-Occupying Horace Mann Middle School

East Hills Academy is an incoming D11 charter school that will open its doors in 2026-27. Rather than occupy its own building from day one, East Hills will share the Horace Mann Middle School campus — operating independently in a dedicated, separate area of the building, with its own classrooms and facilities. The D11 board approved this co-occupancy arrangement in a contract that runs from July 1, 2026, through June 30, 2031.

Co-location like this is not uncommon in districts that are managing enrollment growth and charter expansion simultaneously. Each school maintains its own identity, administration, and programming. For families choosing between East Hills Academy and Horace Mann — or for Horace Mann families wondering what the addition of a charter means for their campus — the key message is that the two schools operate separately, not interchangeably.

Orton Academy — Moving to Trailblazer Elementary

Orton Academy is a D11 charter school that specializes in educating students with dyslexia, using research-based Orton-Gillingham approaches. The school will relocate to Trailblazer Elementary for the 2026-27 school year. Families currently enrolled at Orton Academy should receive detailed communication from the school about what this move means for day-to-day logistics.

What's Next

If your child attends any of these schools — or if you're considering enrollment — the most important step is to contact the school directly. Ask specifically about bus routes, pickup and drop-off procedures, building access, and any enrollment-related questions. Changes like these are manageable with good information, and Colorado Springs school communities tend to be resilient. The district's leadership at d11.org and d49.org will be the best sources for official updates as the fall draws closer.
