### STORY 2: EEMB USA Battery Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

Parents: please share this one.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued a recall for approximately 312,100 EEMB USA lithium coin battery packs — the kind sold on Amazon in five-, 10-, and 20-packs.

The problem: the individual pouches are NOT child-resistant, as required by federal law. That means a curious toddler can easily open the packaging and access the batteries directly.

Coin batteries — also called button batteries — are one of the most dangerous household items a young child can swallow. If ingested, they can cause severe internal chemical burns within just two hours. The damage is not always visible right away, which is what makes them so dangerous.

The affected batteries include CR2025, CR2032, CR2450, CR2477, CR2016, and several other models, all sold on Amazon between August 2023 and April 2026 for $3–$9. If the word "EEMB" appears on your batteries or their pouches, check immediately.

What to do:
- Stop using the recalled batteries right away
- Store them immediately out of children's reach
- Contact EEMB USA for a full refund at info@a2batt.com or eemb.com/recall
- No injuries have been reported — let's keep it that way

If a child swallows a battery: do NOT induce vomiting. Call Poison Control immediately at 1-800-222-1222.

Have you bought batteries on Amazon recently? Time to check the brand. Stay safe, Colorado Springs. ❤️

### STORY 3: El Paso County Co-Location Schools

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

Colorado Springs parents: some schools are changing addresses for next year.

The Colorado Springs Gazette reported today that several El Paso County schools — both charter and traditional district — are relocating or sharing campuses for the 2026-27 school year. Here's the rundown:

James Irwin Elementary, a D11 charter school, is undergoing a major renovation that includes asbestos abatement. During the remodel year, students will temporarily relocate to the James Irwin Elementary - Canada Drive campus, which sits in District 49. Same school, different building, for one year.

East Hills Academy, a new D11 charter school, will open its doors by sharing space with Horace Mann Middle School. The two schools will operate independently but in the same building — East Hills in a dedicated, separate area. This arrangement runs from July 2026 through June 2031.

Orton Academy — a charter school that specializes in serving students with dyslexia — is moving to Trailblazer Elementary, also within D11.

If your child attends any of these schools, the most important thing to do is reach out to the school directly. Logistics like bus routes, pick-up locations, and facility access can all shift in a co-location year, and early communication helps families plan.

Is your child at one of these schools? Share this with other parents who might need to know. 👇

### STORY 4: PPLD Summer Adventure 2026

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

Summer slide is real — and Colorado Springs has a free solution.

The Pikes Peak Library District (PPLD) is kicking off its annual Summer Adventure reading program with a new theme for 2026: "Unearth a Story." 🦕 We're talking dinosaurs, paleontology, and archaeology — guaranteed kid magnets.

Here's how it works: kids (and families!) complete 30 days of activities over the summer. Each day counts if you Read, Explore, or Create something. Track your progress on a game card at the library, or use the Beanstack app digitally. When you hit your goals, there are rewards.

It's completely free. It runs all summer. And it's designed for all ages — there's something for toddlers, elementary schoolers, tweens, and even adults who want to participate alongside their kids.

How to sign up: Head to ppld.org, download the Beanstack app, or stop in at any PPLD branch location in person. If you have kids in D11, their last day of school is May 22 — so you've got about a week and a half to get signed up before summer officially arrives.

Summer doesn't have to mean stopping learning. It just has to be more fun than school. 😄

### STORY 5: AAP Mental Health Framework 2026

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

The American Academy of Pediatrics just put something important in writing: children's mental health is in crisis — and the solution starts before any symptoms appear.

The AAP's new 2026 clinical report lays out a framework for healthy mental and emotional development in children. The central message is a shift from reactive to proactive: instead of waiting for anxiety, depression, or behavioral struggles to show up, pediatricians and families should be building protective factors from day one.

What does that look like in practice? The AAP points to five key things:

Strong parent-child bond. This is the single most powerful protective factor the research identifies — not screen limits, not academic performance, not extracurricular activity. The relationship between parents and kids.

Consistent routines. Predictability reduces anxiety. Bedtimes, mealtimes, morning routines — they matter more than we often give them credit for.

Physical activity. Movement protects against depression and anxiety in children. Colorado Springs families are actually at an advantage here — we live somewhere that makes outdoor activity feel natural.

Sleep. Elementary-age kids need 9-11 hours. Teens need 8-10. Most aren't getting it.

Community connection. Neighbors, faith communities, sports teams, library programs — being embedded in a community is protective. For Colorado Springs' large military family population, where relocation can break connections quickly, intentional community-building matters even more.

This isn't about perfect parenting. It's about consistent attention to the foundations.
