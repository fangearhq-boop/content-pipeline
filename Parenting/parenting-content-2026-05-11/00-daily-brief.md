## Today's Top Stories

### STORY 1: D20 Lands $42.7M Federal Grant — New Douglass Valley K8 Coming to Air Force Academy (TIER 1)
Academy District 20 received a $42.7 million grant from the Department of Defense — the federal share of a larger $54.1 million project to construct a brand-new Douglass Valley K-8 school at the U.S. Air Force Academy. The new 92,000-square-foot school will serve 787 students in pre-kindergarten through eighth grade. The funding comes through the DoD's Public Schools on Military Installations program and was announced May 7. The new school features a learning stair, classroom pods with natural light, activity commons areas, and outdoor connections. This is the biggest school construction investment in D20 in recent memory.

### STORY 2: CPSC Recall — EEMB USA Battery Packs (312,100 Units, Amazon) (TIER 1)
The Consumer Product Safety Commission announced a major recall on May 7: EEMB USA is recalling approximately 312,100 lithium coin battery packs because the pouches are not child-resistant as required by federal law (Reese's Law). The recalled batteries — CR2025, CR2032, CR2032, CR2450, and other models — were sold in five-, 10-, and 20-packs on Amazon from August 2023 through April 2026 for $3–$9. Coin batteries are especially dangerous when swallowed, as they can cause internal chemical burns and death within hours. No injuries reported. Refund contact: info@a2batt.com or eemb.com/recall.

### STORY 3: Schools on the Move in El Paso County — Co-Location News for 2026-27 (TIER 2)
Multiple Colorado Springs-area schools are relocating or sharing campuses for the 2026-27 school year, according to a May 11 Colorado Springs Gazette report. James Irwin Elementary (D11 charter) will temporarily move to a D49 campus during a year-long asbestos abatement and remodeling project. East Hills Academy, an incoming D11 charter school, will co-occupy Horace Mann Middle School through June 2031. And Orton Academy, which specializes in educating students with dyslexia, will relocate to Trailblazer Elementary. COS families with students in these schools should check with their school's administration for updated logistics.

### STORY 4: PPLD Summer Adventure 2026 — "Unearth a Story" Starting Soon (TIER 2)
The Pikes Peak Library District's summer reading program returns with a new theme: "Unearth a Story," centered on dinosaurs, paleontology, and archaeology. Families can register now at ppld.org or on the Beanstack app. Participants complete 30 days of Read, Explore, or Create activities and track progress on a game card or digitally. The program is free, runs all summer, and is designed for all ages. With D11's last day on May 22, now is the time to sign up before summer break begins.

### STORY 5: AAP 2026 — New Guidance on Children's Mental and Emotional Health (TIER 2)
The American Academy of Pediatrics released a sweeping new clinical report in 2026 — "Framework for Approaching Healthy Mental and Emotional Development in Pediatrics" — calling for a proactive approach to children's mental health. The report emphasizes fostering healthy relationships and whole-child wellness from infancy, rather than waiting for symptoms of mental health disorders to appear. Mental and emotional concerns in childhood have hit an unprecedented high. The AAP urges pediatricians and families to focus on protective factors: strong parent-child bonds, consistent routines, physical activity, sleep, and community connection. For COS families at altitude — where vitamin D deficiency and isolation risks can be elevated — the guidance is especially timely.
