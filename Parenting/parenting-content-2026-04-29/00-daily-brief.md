### STORY 1: CPSC Recalls Wiifo Children's Tower Stools — Collapse and Entrapment Hazard
- **Tier:** 1
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety/Recall)
- **Platform(s):** X, Facebook, Article
- **Topic:** The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 9,700 Wiifo-branded children's tower stools (model LT005) due to collapse and entrapment hazards. The stools — sold on Amazon between June 2022 and March 2026 for about $60 — can tip over or collapse during use, and a child's torso can fit through the openings on the sides, posing entrapment and fall risks. The firm is aware of 22 incidents of the stools collapsing, resulting in 6 injuries including contusions and scrapes. Available in white, natural, and light wood finish. Action: stop use immediately, contact Wiifo for a full refund, destroy by disassembling and send a photo to support@wiifo.net. Tower stools (also called learning towers) are popular kitchen helper tools that let young children stand safely at counter height.
- **Why it matters:** Tower stools are popular with Colorado Springs families with young toddlers; 9,700 units sold; 22 reported incidents; reminder to check Amazon purchase history. Pool season and kitchen-helper season overlap in spring.

### STORY 2: CPSC Recalls Vevor Baby Swings — Inclined Sleeper Ban Violation
- **Tier:** 1
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety/Recall)
- **Platform(s):** X, Facebook, Article
- **Topic:** Sanven Technology has recalled Vevor-branded baby swings after the CPSC determined they violate the federal Safe Sleep for Babies Act, which bans inclined infant sleep products. The swings have an incline greater than 10 degrees — experts warn this angle causes an infant's head to tip forward and block the airway. Three models are affected: BB501K (white/white base), BB702A (white/gray base), BB005K (black/black base). Approximately 4,500 units were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon.com from January 2025 through August 2025, priced $65-$80. Action: stop using immediately, remove swing cover, cut it in half, send photo of destroyed cover with "Recalled" written on the base to recalling@vevor.com. Refund available. Contact: 855-599-6320, Mon-Fri 9 AM-5 PM PT.
- **Why it matters:** Inclined sleeper issue is serious and ongoing (context: Fisher-Price Rock 'n Play recall 2019); these were sold recently; families who bought on Amazon in 2025 may still have them.
- **Posting window:** 10:00 AM MT

### STORY 4: AAP: Screen Time Limits Alone Are No Longer Enough — What Quality Means in 2026
- **Tier:** 2
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Platform(s):** X, Facebook, Article
- **Topic:** A major new report covered by ABC News reflects an updated position from the American Academy of Pediatrics: strict screen time limits are no longer sufficient guidance for parents. The 2026 approach focuses on quality, context, and adult participation rather than raw time. Key findings: the adult presence during screen time dramatically changes its impact — children who watch alongside a parent show significantly better learning transfer than those watching alone. The AAP maintains no screens before 18 months and a limit of 1 hour of high-quality content per day for ages 2-5, but the guidance now clarifies that "high-quality" content and active engagement matter far more than the clock. Low-quality use (mindless scrolling, autoplay videos) can disrupt sleep and attention; high-quality content can enrich development.
- **Why it matters:** This is a meaningful update that gives COS parents a practical reframe. Instead of "we failed because they had 90 minutes," the question becomes "what were we watching, and were we watching together?" Actionable and low-guilt angle.
- **Posting window:** 3:00 PM MT
