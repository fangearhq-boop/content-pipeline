Three COS Family Events Worth Your Weekend: May Faire, Star Party, and More

By Marcus Rivera | COS Parenting | April 29, 2026

Colorado Springs keeps a low profile about some of its best family events — the ones that don't advertise heavily, draw a neighborhood crowd, and end up being exactly what a Saturday morning needed. This week and into May, there are three worth knowing about: a community May Faire happening this Saturday, a star party at Cheyenne Mountain State Park with real telescopes and real night-sky views, and a Memorial Day street festival in Old Colorado City worth planning for in advance.

May Faire at Mountain Song Community School — Saturday, May 2

The annual May Faire at Mountain Song Community School runs Saturday, May 2, from 11 AM to 2 PM at 2904 W. Kiowa St. in Colorado Springs. The event features maypole dances — where children hold ribbons and wind them around the pole — as well as crown making, paper flower crafts, a gem dig, a butterfly activity, and a silent auction.

This is the kind of low-key community event that's harder to find as more activities become ticketed and programmatic. It's a neighborhood tradition that's been part of the Mountain Song calendar for years, and it's a good option for families with younger children who want something hands-on without a long drive or a large crowd.

Star Party at Cheyenne Mountain State Park — This Week

The Colorado Springs Astronomical Society sets up telescopes at the Limekiln Trailhead at Cheyenne Mountain State Park for periodic Star Parties, giving families a free chance to look at galaxies, star clusters, and stellar nurseries through equipment far more capable than anything you'd find at a toy store.

If you've ever watched a child realize that the fuzzy smear they're looking at through a telescope is an actual galaxy containing billions of stars, you know what kind of moment this can be. Star Parties are informal and welcoming — society members are typically happy to explain what you're looking at and take questions from kids. Check the Cheyenne Mountain State Park website or the Colorado Springs Astronomical Society for the exact date and time of this week's event.

The park requires a vehicle access pass or state parks pass for entry. There is no additional charge for the Star Party itself.

Old Colorado City Memorial Day Street Festival — May 23-25

Looking a few weeks ahead: Old Colorado City hosts its annual Memorial Day weekend street festival from May 23-25. The event features arts, crafts, food vendors, and free entry — the kind of outdoor street festival that makes a long weekend feel like a good idea. Old Colorado City is a walkable, historic neighborhood west of downtown Colorado Springs with a mix of independent shops and restaurants that make it worth a day trip even outside the festival.

Memorial Day weekend tends to book up fast for Colorado families, and this is one of the easier family-friendly options in the COS area that doesn't require a long drive or reservations.

What's Next

May is a full month for Colorado Springs families: the Hoopster Basketball Championship on May 9, the end of school on May 22, and the Memorial Day weekend to close it out. Keep checking the Colorado Springs Kids Magazine calendar, visitcos.com, and the City of Colorado Springs special events page for additions as the month progresses. Spring in COS tends to fill in quickly once the weather holds.
