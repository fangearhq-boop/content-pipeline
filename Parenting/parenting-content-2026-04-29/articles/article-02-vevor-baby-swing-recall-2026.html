Vevor Baby Swings Recalled — Federal Inclined Sleeper Ban Violation, Suffocation Risk

By Marta Kim | COS Parenting | April 29, 2026

Sanven Technology has recalled three models of Vevor baby swings after the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission determined they violate the federal Safe Sleep for Babies Act, which bans infant sleep products with more than a 10-degree incline. The swings pose a suffocation risk: the inclined position can cause an infant's head to tilt forward and block the airway. If you have one of these swings at home, stop using it now.

The recalled models — BB501K, BB702A, and BB005K — were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon.com from January 2025 through August 2025, priced between $65 and $80. The CPSC is offering a full refund to consumers who follow the recall instructions.

Why the Incline Matters

The federal standard is specific: infant sleep products cannot have an incline greater than 10 degrees. The reasoning is straightforward — infants lack the muscle control to keep their heads upright, and when a seat is angled too steeply, the head can tip forward. When that happens, the chin can press against the chest in a way that restricts the airway. Infants who fall asleep in an inclined position may not be able to reposition themselves, and in the worst cases the restricted airway can lead to suffocation.

This is the same issue that led to the large-scale Fisher-Price Rock 'n Play recall in 2019 and a series of subsequent recalls of similar products. The Safe Sleep for Babies Act was enacted specifically to create a legally enforceable standard addressing inclined sleepers. Products that violate it are being recalled as the CPSC identifies them.

What to Do

If you have a Vevor baby swing matching one of the recalled model numbers, here are the steps for getting a refund:

Stop using the swing immediately. Remove the swing's cover fabric, cut it in half so it cannot be reused, and write "Recalled" on the base of the swing. Photograph both the cut cover and the labeled base, then send the photos to recalling@vevor.com. You can also call 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM Pacific time, for assistance.

Dispose of the swing according to your local disposal guidelines. Do not donate it or pass it to another family.

What's Next

If your infant needs a swing or rocking device, the CPSC recommends products that are explicitly labeled as meeting the federal infant sleep product standard — a flat or near-flat sleeping surface, no inclined sleepers, and no products marketed for infant sleep with an incline exceeding 10 degrees. Visit cpsc.gov for current guidance on compliant infant sleep products.

For Colorado Springs families who purchased baby gear in 2025, this is a good moment to pull out whatever is stored and check model numbers against the current CPSC recall list. The cpsc.gov website has a searchable database by brand and product category, and you can also sign up for email recall alerts so future notices reach you directly.
