CPSC Recalls Wiifo Children's Tower Stools — 9,700 Units Sold on Amazon, Collapse and Entrapment Risk

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting | April 29, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 9,700 Wiifo-branded children's tower stools because they can collapse or tip over during use, and a child's torso can fit through the openings on the sides — creating both fall and entrapment hazards. If you have one of these stools at home, the CPSC's instruction is clear: stop using it now and contact Wiifo for a full refund.

The recalled stools — model LT005 — were sold exclusively on Amazon between June 2022 and March 2026 for about $60. They come in white, natural, and light wood finish. The CPSC says the firm is aware of 22 incidents of the stools collapsing, which resulted in six injuries to children, including contusions and scrapes.

How to Identify the Recalled Stool

The model number LT005 is printed on a label on the underside of the standing platform — the flat surface where a child stands. If you have a Wiifo tower stool and are unsure whether it's the recalled model, check there first. If you purchased any step stool or learning tower through Amazon in the past four years and it came in white, natural wood, or light wood finish, it's worth checking your Amazon order history for the Wiifo brand name.

Tower stools — also called learning towers or kitchen helpers — are designed to let toddlers and young children stand at counter height so they can participate in cooking, baking, or other activities. They've become popular in COS families with children ages 2-5 who want to be involved in kitchen tasks. The appeal is clear: it gives young kids a safe platform to be part of family activities. The hazard in this particular model is that the structural integrity of the stool doesn't hold up under real-world use conditions.

What to Do

If you have a Wiifo LT005 tower stool, take these steps:

Stop using it immediately. Remove your child from it and set it aside. Then disassemble the stool — taking it apart so it can no longer be used — and photograph it in that disassembled state. Send that photo to Wiifo at support@wiifo.net to initiate your refund. Wiifo is offering a full refund to consumers who contact them.

Do not donate the stool, give it to another family, or sell it secondhand. The recall applies regardless of whether your unit showed any signs of instability before this recall was issued.

What's Next

The CPSC continues to monitor children's tower stools and similar kitchen helper products. If you're looking for a replacement learning tower for your child, the CPSC's recall database at cpsc.gov is a good starting point for checking whether alternative products have any open safety actions.

For Colorado Springs families, this recall is also a reminder that products purchased online over the past few years may have developed safety issues that weren't apparent at the time of purchase. Checking your Amazon order history for recalled items takes only a few minutes — the CPSC also maintains a searchable recall alert system at recalls.gov where you can register your products to receive future notifications.
