D11 Hoopster Basketball League Championship Saturday Is May 9 — 96 COS Students Competing

By Keisha Williams | COS Parenting | April 29, 2026

More than 115 Colorado Springs fourth and fifth graders tried out for the D11 Hoopster Basketball League this spring. Ninety-six made it. Those students have been competing in divisional play every Saturday since April 18, and their season wraps up with Championship Saturday on May 9 at North Middle School. The event runs 9 AM to 3 PM and is open to families, staff, and community members who want to cheer them on.

The Hoopster League is organized by Colorado Springs Schools District 11. It's a competitive but community-focused program designed to give elementary students a structured athletic experience that goes beyond what a typical PE class offers — tryouts, team selection, weekly games, and a culminating championship.

Why This Matters for COS Families

District 11 serves a large portion of central Colorado Springs, and programs like the Hoopster League give elementary students competitive athletic experience that isn't always available through individual schools. For fourth and fifth graders who are old enough to take competition seriously but young enough that the stakes are still about growth and participation, a league like this hits the right balance.

The tryout format also matters. The fact that 115 students competed for 96 spots gives the selection process enough weight that making the team feels like an accomplishment — but the 83% acceptance rate keeps it accessible enough that it's not discouraging. Kids who tried out and didn't make it this year have a clear path to try again in the next cycle.

School Year Countdown

Championship Saturday on May 9 also falls right at the point in the school year when the end-of-year energy starts to pick up. District 11's last day of school is May 22, which means families have just under four weeks from today before summer break begins.

Between now and May 22: the basketball championship, spring field trips, end-of-year classroom events, and whatever else each D11 school has on its calendar. If you haven't looked at your school's May schedule recently, it's worth a check — this part of the year tends to move faster than it looks on paper.

What's Next

If your child is one of the 96 Hoopster athletes, Championship Saturday on May 9 is the culminating event of the season. For families who want to attend and support, the event is at North Middle School from 9 AM to 3 PM — no word yet on whether spectator admission has any requirements, so check with your school's D11 contact for details closer to the date.

For more information about D11 athletic and enrichment programs, visit the Colorado Springs Schools District 11 website at d11.org, or sign up for D11 FlashAlert notifications to receive updates directly from the district.
