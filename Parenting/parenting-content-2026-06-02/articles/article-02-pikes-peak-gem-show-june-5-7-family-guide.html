Pikes Peak Gem, Mineral & Jewelry Show This Weekend: Your Family Guide to June 5–7

By Jamie Rivera | June 2, 2026 | COS Parenting

One of Colorado Springs' best family-friendly weekend events returns this Friday. The Pikes Peak Gem, Mineral & Jewelry Show runs June 5 through 7 at Norris Penrose Event Center, bringing more than 55 vendors and a full lineup of hands-on activities that make this genuinely fun for kids of all ages — not just the adults looking for jewelry.

What to Expect

The show floor features minerals, fossils, gemstones, and handmade jewelry from vendors across the region. For kids who've never seen a raw amethyst up close or held a piece of fool's gold, it's a natural history experience wrapped in a market setting.

Beyond the vendor floor, the family activity lineup is substantial. Gold panning runs throughout the weekend — kids can try their hand at finding flakes in a pan. A scavenger hunt encourages kids to explore the whole show floor. Pebble Pups, a youth mineral club program, gives younger visitors a structured introduction to collecting and identifying rocks.

For families who want to try something hands-on together, there are two workshop options: a beading class runs daily at 11 AM, and a wire-wrapping jewelry class is scheduled for Saturday at 1 PM. Both are designed to be accessible for beginners.

Hourly door prizes and silent auctions run throughout the weekend.

Logistics for Families

Norris Penrose Event Center is located at 1045 Lower Gold Camp Road in Colorado Springs. Parking is available on site. The venue is indoors, so weather isn't a concern — though Lower Gold Camp Road gets some summer traffic on weekend mornings, so building in a few extra minutes is worth it.

Kids under 12 get in free, which makes this one of the more affordable family outings in COS this weekend. An adult day pass is $5, or $8 for a multi-day pass if you'd like to come back Sunday as well.

Sunday hours are shorter — the show closes at 4 PM — so families planning to use the full activity lineup may get more out of a Friday or Saturday visit.

Why It's Worth It for Kids

The Gem Show is one of those events where curious kids find something unexpected to get absorbed by. Minerals and fossils have a way of capturing attention that a lot of manufactured activities don't — there's genuine discovery involved, and the scavenger hunt format gives kids a goal to work toward.

For families with kids who have any interest in science, natural history, or collecting, this is a low-cost, high-engagement outing that's easy to recommend.

What's Next

The Pikes Peak Gem Show runs through Sunday, June 7. For the full vendor list, class schedule, and any last-minute updates, check pikespeakgemshow.com before you go. This is a cash-friendly event, and many individual vendors may not accept cards.
