NFSVLB Baby Bath Seat Recalled for Drowning and Entrapment Risk: What Parents Need to Know

By Jamie Rivera | June 2, 2026 | COS Parenting

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission recalled 1,430 NFSVLB baby bath seats in January 2026 due to serious drowning and entrapment hazards. If you purchased one of these seats on Amazon between May and October 2025, stop using it immediately and follow the steps below to receive a full refund.

What Was Recalled

The recalled product is the NFSVLB baby bath seat, Model ZY2025, sold in blue and white. The seats were available on Amazon from May through October 2025, priced between $25 and $40.

The CPSC identified two specific hazards with this product:

  • The seat is unstable and can tip over during use.
  • The leg openings can trap a baby's torso, creating an entrapment risk.

Together, these defects create a drowning hazard in even shallow bathwater.

Why Bath Seat Safety Matters

Baby bath seats can create a false sense of security. A seat keeps a baby upright in the tub and may appear to provide stability — but bath seats are not safety devices and are not designed to prevent drowning if a parent steps away. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that an adult remain within arm's reach of a baby in water at all times, regardless of what support equipment is being used.

The NFSVLB seat compounds this risk in two ways: it can tip over on its own, and if it does, the leg openings can prevent the baby from being easily removed from the seat. This combination has the potential to cause harm very quickly.

What to Do Right Now

The CPSC remedy for this recall is straightforward:

  1. Stop using the bath seat immediately.
  2. Email amzmaihuayd@163.com to request a full refund.

Note: the .163.com email domain is unusual. It is a legitimate Chinese email service provider, and this address is the CPSC-listed official contact for this recall. If you have any concern, the full recall listing at cpsc.gov confirms the contact information.

Do not use the seat while waiting for your refund. Do not donate or resell the item.

What's Next

If you're shopping for baby bath support products going forward, look for bath seats that meet current ASTM International safety standards and that carry JPMA (Juvenile Products Manufacturers Association) certification. Always remain within arm's reach of your baby during bath time regardless of what equipment you're using.

For the full recall listing and to report a product safety issue, visit cpsc.gov. You can also sign up for CPSC recall alerts at cpsc.gov to receive notifications when new recalls are announced.
