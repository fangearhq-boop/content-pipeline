PPLD Summer Adventure Launches June 2: Everything COS Families Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | June 2, 2026 | COS Parenting

Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program officially launched today, June 2, and families across Colorado Springs can sign up right now. The program is free for all ages, runs through the end of July, and includes a free lunch program for kids under 18 at East Library starting today through July 31.

What Summer Adventure Is

Summer Adventure is PPLD's annual summer reading and activity challenge. Participants log reading time and activities to earn prizes, with options for every age group — babies and toddlers, kids, teens, and adults. It's designed to keep kids reading and exploring during the summer months, when research consistently shows learning loss is a real risk without structured engagement.

This year's prize sponsors include SCHEELS, the Colorado Springs Switchbacks, and Flying W Ranch, giving participants local prizes that families can actually use.

What's Happening Today

The program is launching with opening concerts at two branches this afternoon:

  • Library 21c — 2 PM
  • East Library — 3:30 PM

If your family is free this afternoon, attending a launch concert is a great way to kick off the summer and get registered in person at the same time.

Free Lunch at East Library

Starting today and running every weekday through July 31, East Library is offering free lunch for any child under 18. No registration is required for the lunch program — just show up. This is a meaningful resource for COS families during the summer break, when school meal programs aren't running.

The lunch program is separate from Summer Adventure registration. Kids don't need to be signed up for the reading challenge to receive a free lunch.

How to Sign Up

Families can register for Summer Adventure online at ppld.beanstack.org or in person at any PPLD branch. The Beanstack app allows participants to log reading and activities directly from a phone or tablet, which makes tracking easier for older kids and teens who are managing their own progress.

Adult participation in the reading challenge is $5. Kids and teens participate for free.

If you're not sure which branch is closest to you, the full branch list and hours are available at ppld.org.

What's Next

Summer Adventure runs through the end of July. PPLD will announce prize drawings and program updates through its website and social media channels throughout the summer. For families looking for additional summer activities in COS, Get Outdoors Day at Memorial Park is this Saturday, June 6 (9 AM to 2 PM, free), and the Pikes Peak Gem, Mineral & Jewelry Show runs this weekend, June 5–7, at Norris Penrose Event Center.

To get started today, visit ppld.beanstack.org or ppld.org/summer.
