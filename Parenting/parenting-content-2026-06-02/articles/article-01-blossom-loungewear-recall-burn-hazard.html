Blossom Children's Loungewear Recalled for Burn Hazard: What COS Parents Need to Do Now

By Sarah Morales | June 2, 2026 | COS Parenting

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 630 units of Blossom children's loungewear sets due to a burn hazard. The garments fail to meet federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear, putting kids at risk during sleep and rest. If you have these items at home, the remedy is straightforward — but you need to act now.

What Was Recalled

The recalled products are Blossom children's loungewear sets: button-up shirts paired with matching shorts or pants. They were sold through Blossom OCC's website (blossomocc.com) in sizes 12 months through 12 years.

Affected patterns include:

  • Short-sleeve sets: fireworks, multi-stripes, tulips, sailboats
  • Long-sleeve sets: letter to Santa, Santa paws

Affected colors include white, navy, light blue, pink, and lilac.

Why This Is a Safety Issue

Federal law requires that children's sleepwear meet strict flammability standards. Those standards exist because children are vulnerable to burns during sleep — they can't escape quickly if fabric catches fire. Loungewear sets that are styled for sleeping or relaxing fall under these same requirements.

These Blossom sets do not meet those standards. The CPSC's determination that the garments fail flammability requirements means they present a genuine burn hazard to children who wear them while sleeping or lounging at home.

What to Do Right Now

The CPSC has outlined a clear four-step remedy:

  1. Stop using the loungewear sets immediately.
  2. Cut the items in half to render them unusable.
  3. Take a photograph of the cut items.
  4. Email the photograph to sales@blossomocc.com to receive a full refund.

Do not donate these items, pass them on to another family, or resell them. The recall applies regardless of whether your child has worn the item — the safety concern is the same.

What's Next

Once you've completed the remedy steps, check cpsc.gov for the latest updates on this recall. You can also sign up for CPSC email alerts at cpsc.gov to receive future recall notices directly. If you're unsure whether your loungewear sets are among the recalled items, checking the pattern name and size against the list above is the fastest way to confirm.
