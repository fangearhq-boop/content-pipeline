### STORY 1: Blossom Children's Loungewear Recall

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 8:00 AM MT

If your kids have Blossom loungewear sets in their dresser, check them now.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 630 units of Blossom children's loungewear sets due to a burn hazard. The affected sets — button-up shirts with matching shorts or pants — fail to meet federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear. They were sold through Blossom OCC (blossomocc.com).

Here's what to look for: sizes 12 months through 12 years, in short-sleeve patterns including fireworks, multi-stripes, tulips, and sailboats, and long-sleeve patterns called "letter to Santa" and "Santa paws." Colors include white, navy, light blue, pink, and lilac.

What to do: Stop using the items immediately. Cut them in half. Take a photo of the cut items. Email sales@blossomocc.com and you'll receive a full refund.

These are loungewear sets that kids may wear while sleeping or relaxing — that's exactly when flammability standards matter most. If you've got these at home, please act on this today.

Have you seen this brand before? Check those drawers and closets.

### STORY 2: Pikes Peak Gem, Mineral & Jewelry Show — June 5–7

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 10:00 AM MT

If you've been looking for a weekend activity that's genuinely fun for both kids and adults, the Pikes Peak Gem, Mineral & Jewelry Show is this Friday through Sunday — and it might be our favorite annual family event in COS that's still a little under the radar.

The show runs June 5–7 at Norris Penrose Event Center on Lower Gold Camp Road. Hours are Friday and Saturday 10 AM to 6 PM, and Sunday 10 AM to 4 PM. Admission is $5 per day or $8 for a multi-day pass — and kids under 12 get in free.

More than 55 vendors will have minerals, fossils, gems, and jewelry from around the world. But the family activity lineup is what sets this apart: there's gold panning, a kids' scavenger hunt, Pebble Pups (a youth mineral club activity), a daily beading class at 11 AM, and a wire-wrapping class on Saturday at 1 PM. Hourly door prizes and silent auctions run all weekend.

This is one of those events where kids who don't think they care about rocks end up completely absorbed for an hour. It's also a genuine learning experience — geology, natural history, and a little treasure hunting all in one.

Will you be making the trip this weekend?

### STORY 3: D11 Raises Starting Pay After Month-Long Negotiations

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 8:00 AM MT

D11 has a new starting salary — and it comes after one of the more contentious stretches in the district's recent history.

Colorado Springs School District 11 has ratified a new agreement that raises starting teacher and staff pay to approximately $50,000. That's roughly a 20% increase and, according to regional comparisons, the highest starting salary among Pikes Peak-area districts. D20 starts around $49,000 and D49 around $47,000.

The increase came after month-long negotiations that followed a staff picket — the result of an unusually tense period that included the D11 board eliminating the CSEA collective bargaining agreement in 2025. Teachers and staff worked without a new agreement through those months before ratifying this one.

Even so, some D11 employees told KOAA that the raise isn't enough. That tension is real. Staffing and morale going into the 2026-27 school year matter for every family with kids in D11 schools — teacher stability, classroom experience, and whether the district can attract and retain qualified educators all flow from compensation.

The raise is a step. Whether it's a big enough step is a conversation D11 families and staff are still having.

### STORY 4: CPSC Recall — NFSVLB Baby Bath Seats

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 12:00 PM MT

If you have a baby and a bath seat, please read this.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled 1,430 NFSVLB baby bath seats (Model ZY2025, blue and white). The seats were sold on Amazon between May and October 2025 for between $25 and $40.

There are two specific hazards here. First, the seat is unstable and can tip over. Second — and this is the more serious part — the leg openings can trap a baby's torso, creating an entrapment risk. The combination of instability and entrapment creates a real drowning hazard, even in shallow bathwater.

Bath seats are one of those products that can create false confidence. A baby in a bath seat can seem secure while you grab a towel or turn around — but these seats are not restraint devices and this model has failed in two specific ways that could cause serious injury.

What to do: Stop using the seat immediately. To receive a full refund, email amzmaihuayd@163.com — yes, that's an unusual email domain, but it is the official CPSC-listed contact for this recall.

Please share this with anyone you know who has an infant.

### STORY 5: PPLD Summer Adventure Starts TODAY

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 3:00 PM MT

It's officially here — Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program launched today, June 2, and COS families can join right now.

The program is free for all ages (adults pay $5 to participate in the reading challenge). This year's prize sponsors include SCHEELS, the Colorado Springs Switchbacks, and Flying W Ranch — and yes, there are prizes for kids, teens, and adults.

Here's what's happening TODAY specifically: opening concerts are being held this afternoon at Library 21c and East Library at 2 PM and 3:30 PM. If you're free this afternoon, that's a great way to start the summer.

There's also a free lunch program running now through July 31: any child under 18 can get a free lunch at East Library every weekday at no cost, no registration required. That's a meaningful resource for families across COS during the summer break.

To sign up for Summer Adventure and start earning prizes, go to ppld.beanstack.org or visit ppld.org/summer. You can register at any PPLD branch or online.
