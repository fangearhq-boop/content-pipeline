CPSC Recall — Blossom Children's Loungewear Fire Hazard
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM MT If your kids have Blossom loungewear sets in their dresser, check them now. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 630 units of Blossom children's loungewear sets due to a burn hazard. The affected sets — button-up shirts with matching shorts or pants — fail to meet federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear. They were sold through Blossom OCC (blossomocc.com). Here's what to look for: sizes 12 months through 12 years, in short-sleeve patterns including fireworks, multi-stripes, tulips, and sailboats, and long-sleeve patterns called "letter to Santa" and "Santa paws." Colors include white, navy, light blue, pink, and lilac. What to do: Stop using the items immediately. Cut them in half. Take a photo of the cut items. Email sales@blossomocc.com and you'll receive a full refund. These are loungewear sets that kids may wear while sleeping or relaxing — that's exactly when flammability standards matter most. If you've got these at home, please act on this today. Have you seen this brand before? Check those drawers and closets. --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 8:30 AM MT RECALL: Blossom children's loungewear sets — ~630 units, burn hazard. Sizes 12M–12Y. Patterns include fireworks, stripes, tulips, sailboats, and Santa designs. Stop use, cut in half, email sales@blossomocc.com for a full refund. Full details at cpsc.gov.

D11 Raises Starting Pay After Month-Long Negotiations
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM MT D11 has a new starting salary — and it comes after one of the more contentious stretches in the district's recent history. Colorado Springs School District 11 has ratified a new agreement that raises starting teacher and staff pay to approximately $50,000. That's roughly a 20% increase and, according to regional comparisons, the highest starting salary among Pikes Peak-area districts. D20 starts around $49,000 and D49 around $47,000. The increase came after month-long negotiations that followed a staff picket — the result of an unusually tense period that included the D11 board eliminating the CSEA collective bargaining agreement in 2025. Teachers and staff worked without a new agreement through those months before ratifying this one. Even so, some D11 employees told KOAA that the raise isn't enough. That tension is real. Staffing and morale going into the 2026-27 school year matter for every family with kids in D11 schools — teacher stability, classroom experience, and whether the district can attract and retain qualified educators all flow from compensation. The raise is a step. Whether it's a big enough step is a conversation D11 families and staff are still having. What are your thoughts on how D11 is handling compensation and staffing? --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 8:30 AM MT D11 raises starting pay to approximately $50,000 — roughly 20% and the highest starting salary in the Pikes Peak region. CPSC Recall — NFSVLB Baby Bath Seat Poses Drowning and Entrapment Risk
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 12:00 PM MT If you have a baby and a bath seat, please read this. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled 1,430 NFSVLB baby bath seats (Model ZY2025, blue and white). The seats were sold on Amazon between May and October 2025 for between $25 and $40. There are two specific hazards here. First, the seat is unstable and can tip over. Second — and this is the more serious part — the leg openings can trap a baby's torso, creating an entrapment risk. The combination of instability and entrapment creates a real drowning hazard, even in shallow bathwater. Bath seats are one of those products that can create false confidence. A baby in a bath seat can seem secure while you grab a towel or turn around — but these seats are not restraint devices and this model has failed in two specific ways that could cause serious injury. What to do: Stop using the seat immediately. To receive a full refund, email amzmaihuayd@163.com — yes, that's an unusual email domain, but it is the official CPSC-listed contact for this recall. Please share this with anyone you know who has an infant. Have you seen this product before? Do you have questions about bath seat safety? --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 12:30 PM MT RECALL: NFSVLB baby bath seats (Model ZY2025, blue/white) — 1,430 units. Tips over + leg openings can trap baby's torso. Drowning and entrapment risk. Sold on Amazon May–Oct 2025. Stop use now. Email amzmaihuayd@163.com for a full refund. Full details at cpsc.gov.
