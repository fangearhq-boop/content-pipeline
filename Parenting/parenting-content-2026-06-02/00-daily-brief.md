### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — Blossom Children's Loungewear Fire Hazard
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Window:** 8:00 AM MT

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 630 units of Blossom children's loungewear sets due to a burn hazard. The garments fail to meet federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear. Affected items include button-up shirts with matching shorts or pants in sizes 12M–12Y, sold in patterns including fireworks, multi-stripes, tulips, and sailboats (short-sleeve) and letter to Santa and Santa paws (long-sleeve), in white, navy, light blue, pink, and lilac. Action: Stop use immediately, cut the items in half, photograph the cut items, and email sales@blossomocc.com to receive a full refund. Source: CPSC.gov 2026.

### STORY 3: D11 Raises Starting Pay After Month-Long Negotiations
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** Local News | **Window:** 8:00 AM MT

Colorado Springs School District 11 has ratified a new agreement raising starting teacher and staff pay to approximately $50,000 — a roughly 20% increase and the highest starting salary in the Pikes Peak region (compared to D20 at approximately $49,000 and D49 at approximately $47,000). The raise comes after month-long negotiations following a staff picket. Despite the increase, some D11 employees told KOAA the raise is still not enough. Context: The D11 board eliminated the CSEA collective bargaining agreement in 2025, leading to the extended negotiations. Source: KOAA.

### STORY 4: CPSC Recall — NFSVLB Baby Bath Seat Poses Drowning and Entrapment Risk
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Window:** 12:00 PM MT

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled 1,430 NFSVLB baby bath seats (Model ZY2025, blue and white). The seats are unstable, can tip over, and have leg openings that can trap a baby's torso — creating serious drowning and entrapment hazards. The seats were sold on Amazon from May through October 2025 for $25–$40. Action: Stop use immediately and email amzmaihuayd@163.com for a full refund. Recall issued January 2026. Source: CPSC.gov.
