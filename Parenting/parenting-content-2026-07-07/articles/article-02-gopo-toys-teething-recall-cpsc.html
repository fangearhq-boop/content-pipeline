CPSC Recall: GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toy — Here's How to Check and What to Do

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | July 7, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

If you've bought a teething toy from Amazon in the past few years, it's worth checking your toy drawer today.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has announced a recall of approximately 70,410 GOPO TOYS Pull String Teething Toys. The recall is because the toy's silicone pull strings can reach the back of a child's throat, creating a choking and respiratory distress hazard. Three incidents have already been reported.

What the Toy Looks Like

The GOPO TOYS Pull String Teething Toy has a distinctive appearance that makes it easy to identify. It features:

  • An off-white circular disc body with a gray ball in the center
  • Six multi-colored silicone tentacle-style pull strings extending from the disc
  • Seven soft push buttons on the body

The toy was sold exclusively on Amazon from August 2023 through March 2026, at prices between $11 and $15.

How to Check If Your Toy Is Recalled

Look at the back of the toy's packaging. The affected batch numbers are printed there. The recalled batches are:

Batch Number
250905
250530
250120
240315
231005
230610

If your batch number matches any on this list, stop use immediately.

What to Do

The CPSC's remedy for this recall requires you to take the following steps — in order:

  1. Stop using the toy immediately.
  2. Cut all six silicone strings from the toy body.
  3. Write the word "DESTROYED" on the toy body with a marker.
  4. Take a photo of the destroyed toy.
  5. Email the photo to recalls@gopotoys.com. Include your mailing address in the email.

Following these steps gets you a full refund. The photo documentation is required — don't skip the email step, or you won't receive the refund.

Why the Strings Are the Hazard

The CPSC determined that the silicone pull strings on this toy are long enough that when a child pulls them, the string can reach into the back of the throat. This creates both a choking risk and a risk of respiratory distress. The three incidents reported prior to this recall confirm the hazard is not theoretical — children have experienced distress with this toy.

Silicone teething toys are common and generally safe, but the design of this specific toy — long flexible strings with enough reach to contact the airway — created an unanticipated hazard that only became clear at scale.

What's Next

If you have questions about the recall or need help with the refund process, the official CPSC recall page for this product is at cpsc.gov — search for "GOPO Toys." You can also contact GOPO TOYS directly at recalls@gopotoys.com. Keep an eye on that inbox for a confirmation and refund instructions after you send your photo.
