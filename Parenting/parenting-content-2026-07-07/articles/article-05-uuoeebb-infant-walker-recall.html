CPSC Recall: Uuoeebb Infant Walker Sold on Amazon — Fall and Entrapment Hazards, Full Refund Available

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | July 7, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

A second infant product recall was announced this week: approximately 2,650 Uuoeebb infant walkers sold on Amazon are being recalled by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission for multiple serious hazards.

The recall is notable for two reasons. First, the walker poses more than one hazard — it can fit through standard doorways (which compliant walkers are designed to prevent), it fails to stop at a step edge, and the leg openings are wide enough for a child to slip through until their head becomes entrapped. Second, the manufacturer, BaoD (operating under the name Hunan Suihuo E-commerce Co.), has been unresponsive to CPSC, which is why the CPSC is handling the recall contact directly.

What the Walker Looks Like

The Uuoeebb infant walker has a distinctive round base with eight wheels and seven adjustable height settings. It's collapsible and came in three colors: gray, black, and pink. It was sold exclusively on Amazon between December 2024 and September 2025, priced between $60 and $90.

To check if your walker is the recalled batch, look at the tracking label located under the seat. The recalled walkers have Production Batch 7654 printed on that label.

Why This Walker Is Dangerous

Federal safety standards require infant walkers to be too wide to fit through a standard doorway, so that a child in a walker cannot reach stairs, ledges, or other hazards. The Uuoeebb walker does not meet that standard. The CPSC identified three specific failure modes:

Hazard Risk
Fits through standard doorway Child can reach stairs, ledges, or other drop-off areas
Fails to stop at a step edge Child in walker can fall down stairs
Leg openings too wide Child can slip through the seat opening until head becomes entrapped

These are not minor technical violations — they're the exact failure modes that federal walker standards were written to prevent. The CPSC does not publish incident data for this specific recall, but the hazards identified are consistent with the kinds of falls and entrapments that infant walker injuries have historically involved.

What to Do

If you have this walker:

  1. Stop use immediately.
  2. Check the tracking label under the seat for Production Batch 7654.
  3. Email Uuoeebbrecalls@outlook.com to request a full refund. Include your name, mailing address, and proof of purchase if you have it.

Because the manufacturer is unresponsive, the CPSC is the primary point of escalation if you have difficulty getting a response. You can report your issue at SaferProducts.gov or call the CPSC hotline at 800-638-2772.

What's Next

The CPSC recall page for this product is available at cpsc.gov — search for "Uuoeebb" to find the full recall notice. If you purchased this walker as a gift or passed it along to another family, let them know. Given that the seller is not actively cooperating with CPSC's recall outreach, word-of-mouth may be the most effective way these walkers get off the floor.
