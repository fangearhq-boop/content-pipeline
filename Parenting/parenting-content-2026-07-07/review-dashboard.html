Wildfire Smoke + Ozone Action Day — Air Quality Alert for Colorado Springs
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post 🌫️ If you've got outdoor plans with your kids today, you'll want to check the air quality first. Colorado Springs is under a Wildfire Smoke Advisory today, with smoke from the Aspen Acres Fire — now burning at more than 91,000 acres in Pueblo County — pushing into the Front Range. On top of that, CDPHE has issued an Ozone Action Day for the Front Range Urban Corridor through at least 4 PM. That's two air quality concerns hitting at the same time. For most healthy adults, today's conditions are in the Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups range — which means kids, anyone with asthma, and people with heart or lung conditions should limit time outdoors, especially during the heat of the day. Young children are more vulnerable than adults because their lungs are still developing and they breathe more air relative to their body weight. If you're planning outdoor activity, earlier in the morning is better than midday or afternoon. Check AirNow.gov before you head out — you can enter your zip code to get a current reading for your specific area. How are you adjusting your family's plans today? 🌬️ ### Image Post Caption Wildfire smoke from the Aspen Acres Fire + a Front Range Ozone Action Day — Colorado Springs air quality is a concern today. CPSC Recall — GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toy (~70,410 Units)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ⚠️ There's a new CPSC recall that's worth checking if you have young children — especially if you've ordered teething toys from Amazon in the past few years. GOPO TOYS Pull String Teething Toys have been recalled — about 70,410 of them. The toy is an off-white disc with a gray ball in the center, six multi-colored silicone tentacle-style pull strings, and seven soft push buttons. The problem is that the silicone strings can reach the back of a child's throat, creating a choking or respiratory hazard. Three incidents have already been reported. These were sold on Amazon from August 2023 through March 2026, priced at $11–$15. To check if yours is recalled, look at the back of the packaging for the batch number — the recalled batches are 250905, 250530, 250120, 240315, 231005, and 230610. If you have one: the remedy requires you to cut all of the silicone strings, write "DESTROYED" on the toy body, take a photo, and email it to recalls@gopotoys.com for a full refund. Don't just toss it — the email step is what gets you the refund. Do you have one of these? Cover All Coloradans Hits 25,000-Child Enrollment Cap
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Colorado's Cover All Coloradans program has hit its new enrollment cap — and it's worth understanding what that means for families in the Colorado Springs area. The program provides healthcare coverage for immigrant children and pregnant women who don't qualify for traditional Medicaid. It launched in January 2025 and quickly grew to serve tens of thousands of children statewide. On July 1, 2026, a new 25,000-enrollment cap took effect — and the age limit was lowered from under 19 to under 18. The program is now closed to new enrollees. The cap was put in place because of cost: the program was originally estimated at $14.7 million, but is now projected to cost $104.5 million for the 2026–27 fiscal year. Advocates say the trade-off is that without this coverage, more families will turn to emergency rooms — which is ultimately more expensive, and falls on hospitals and the broader healthcare system. Families who are already enrolled are not immediately affected. Families who were hoping to enroll new children will need to find other options, which advocates are working to help navigate. Are you following this policy change? What questions do you have for your community? 💬 ### Image Post Caption Colorado's Cover All Coloradans program hit its new 25,000-child enrollment cap on July 1. CPSC Recall — Uuoeebb Infant Walker (~2,650 Units)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ⚠️ One more CPSC recall to be aware of today — this one is for an infant walker sold on Amazon. About 2,650 Uuoeebb infant walkers have been recalled by the CPSC. The walker has a round base, eight wheels, and seven adjustable height settings — it comes in gray, black, or pink, and was sold on Amazon between December 2024 and September 2025 at $60–$90. The CPSC has identified multiple hazards: the walker can fit through a standard doorway (which walkers are designed not to do), it fails to stop at a step edge, and the leg openings are wide enough that a child can slip through until their head becomes entrapped. These are serious fall and entrapment risks. To check your walker, look for the production batch number on the tracking label under the seat — the recalled batch is labeled 7654. If you have one, stop use immediately. The seller (BaoD / Hunan Suihuo E-commerce Co.) is reportedly unresponsive to CPSC, but you can reach the recall contact directly: email Uuoeebbrecalls@outlook.com for a full refund. Have you seen this walker? Pass it along to anyone who might have bought one. 🚨 ### Image Post Caption CPSC Recall: ~2,650 Uuoeebb infant walkers (Amazon, Dec 2024–Sep 2025). Round base, 8 wheels.
