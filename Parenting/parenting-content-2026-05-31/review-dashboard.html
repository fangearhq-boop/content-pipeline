D11 $775 Million Bond Referendum — What Colorado Springs Parents Need to Know
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM MT COS parents — there's a big school funding question coming to your November ballot, and now is the time to get informed. The D11 board is considering a $775 million bond referendum for the November 2026 election. If voters approve it, the money would go toward: - Air conditioning upgrades (many D11 schools are 50–100 years old and have none) - Major building renovations - A boost to teacher pay Here's the bottom line on cost: your property taxes would go up by about $6.50 per month for every $100,000 of home value. The mill levy would increase from 37.7 to 46.3 mills — which would put D11 on par with surrounding districts. For context: the average D11 building is 58 years old. Repair needs already identified exceed $422 million. This isn't a wish list — it's long-deferred maintenance. Nothing is finalized. The board is still evaluating whether to put it on the ballot. But as a parent, this is worth following. What questions do you have about the D11 bond? --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 8:00 AM MT D11 families: a $775M bond could hit your November ballot. CPSC Warning — Fasando Baby Loungers Linked to Infant Death: Stop Using Immediately
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 12:00 PM MT This is an urgent safety post. Please share with any parent you know who has a newborn or infant at home. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued a warning about Fasando baby loungers sold on Amazon — linked to the death of a 2-month-old in 2025. About 6,200 units were sold between August 2024 and July 2025, for approximately $35. The problem: the lounger's sides are too low, the foot openings are too wide, and there's no stand — meaning if placed on an elevated surface like an adult bed, there's a serious fall and entrapment risk. IMPORTANT: The manufacturer has not agreed to a formal recall. That means these products are still in homes. If you have one: 1. Stop using it immediately 2. Remove the foam padding 3. Cut the cover in half 4. Dispose of it For more information or to report an issue: CPSC.gov or call 800-638-2772. Please share this post. A product linked to an infant death is still circulating. --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 12:00 PM MT URGENT: CPSC warning on Fasando baby loungers sold on Amazon — linked to an infant death in 2025. No formal recall yet. If you have one: stop using it, cut the cover in half, dispose of it. CPSC.gov for details.

### Long-Form Post **Window:** 3:00 PM MT D49 families — a new resource is coming to your district this August, and it includes a preschool option worth knowing about. District 49 is opening a Student Success Center at the start of the 2026–27 school year. It will be housed in the former Falcon Elementary School of Technology building. What will be there: - Two full-time preschool classrooms, available Monday through Thursday - Before- and after-school care provided by Base49 - Friday childcare through Base49 - Specialized support services for students who need extra help The center is designed as a strategic realignment — consolidating services into one location so students with specialized needs can get targeted support. If you have a preschool-age child or a student who might benefit from the support services, keep an eye on d49.org for enrollment and program details as the summer progresses.
