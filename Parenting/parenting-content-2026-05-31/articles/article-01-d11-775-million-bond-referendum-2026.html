D11 $775 Million Bond Referendum: What Colorado Springs Parents Need to Know Before November

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 31, 2026

Colorado Springs School District 11 may be heading to the November ballot with a major funding question for voters — and if you have children in D11 schools, or plan to, this decision affects you directly. The school board is weighing a $775 million bond referendum that would fund building renovations, air conditioning upgrades, and teacher pay increases at one of the region's oldest school systems.

The board has not yet voted to place the measure on the ballot. But the conversations are underway, and community members will want to understand what's being proposed — and what it would cost — before November arrives.

Why D11 Is Pursuing This Now

The numbers behind this proposal reflect decades of deferred maintenance. The average D11 school building is 58 years old, and the district's own assessments have identified more than $422 million in repair needs. Many D11 schools were built without air conditioning during a different era, and Colorado Springs summers have made that a growing concern for families and staff alike.

Beyond physical infrastructure, the bond would also address teacher compensation — an ongoing issue in a district that has seen staffing challenges in recent years. The board discussion referenced by KRDO's May 27 reporting describes the bond as addressing what they characterize as long-deferred but necessary investments.

What It Would Cost Families

If approved, the bond would raise the mill levy from 37.7 mills to 46.3 mills. For homeowners in Colorado Springs, that translates to approximately $6.50 more per month for every $100,000 of property value. On a $300,000 home, that would be roughly $19.50 per month — or about $234 per year.

The district has noted that the adjusted mill levy would put D11 at roughly the same level as neighboring and comparable school districts in Colorado, closing a gap that has existed for some time.

Renters are not directly assessed mill levies, though property tax changes can sometimes influence rental costs over time depending on local market conditions.

Quick Reference

  • Proposed amount: $775 million
  • Target ballot: November 2026
  • What it funds: Air conditioning, building renovations, teacher pay
  • Tax impact: ~$6.50/month per $100,000 of home value
  • Mill levy change: 37.7 → 46.3 mills
  • Average building age: 58 years
  • More info: d11.org/bond

What Families Can Do Now

The board vote on whether to formally place the measure on the ballot has not yet occurred. Families who want to weigh in can attend upcoming D11 board meetings — dates are listed at d11.org. The district has also created a bond information page at d11.org/bond where families can review specifics and find FAQs.

If the measure does go to voters, November elections in Colorado are mail-ballot elections — registered voters will receive their ballots automatically. Voter registration deadlines and other details will be available through the El Paso County Clerk and Recorder's office.

What's Next

The D11 board will continue its evaluation of the bond proposal in the coming months. COS Parenting will report on any formal vote to place the measure on the ballot, as well as ongoing community forums where families can ask questions and share perspectives.

For a school district where more than half of buildings predate the Apollo program, this is a conversation that has been building for a long time. Whether voters ultimately approve it, the bond conversation is one every D11 family should enter informed.
