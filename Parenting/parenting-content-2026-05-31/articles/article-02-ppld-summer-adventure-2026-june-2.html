PPLD Summer Adventure Starts June 2: Free Reading Program, Prizes, and Opening Day Concerts for Colorado Springs Families

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 31, 2026

Summer is two days away — and so is one of Colorado Springs' best free family traditions. The Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure 2026 program launches Tuesday, June 2, with reading challenges, prizes, opening day concerts, and free lunches for kids and teens. Best of all: it's open to every age, from newborns to grandparents, and it's completely free to join.

How to Join Summer Adventure

Families can register starting June 2 in two ways: visit any PPLD library branch in person, or sign up online at ppld.beanstack.org. Once registered, participants track their reading, creative activities, and explorations through the Beanstack app or the library's website.

The goal is 30 days of activity — but "activity" is defined broadly. Reading a book counts. Attending a library program counts. Trying one of PPLD's activity ideas counts. The program is designed to be achievable for busy families, not just dedicated readers.

Prizes and Grand Drawings

Participants receive an age-appropriate prize simply for registering (for ages 0–18), and a second prize after completing 30 days of activities. All ages can also enter grand prize drawings — earning additional entries by completing extra activity days beyond the initial 30.

Grand prizes come from Colorado Springs partners including Roadhouse Cinemas, the Colorado Springs Switchbacks, SCHEELS, Skate City, Main Event, and Flying W Ranch. Adults who complete the program receive a can cooler, wildflower seeds, and a grand prize entry.

Opening Day Concerts — June 2

PPLD is marking the launch with free concerts on opening day. On Tuesday, June 2, there will be four performances total: concerts at 2 PM and 3:30 PM at East Library (5550 N. Union Blvd.), and matching concerts at 2 PM and 3:30 PM at Library 21c. No tickets required — just show up and bring the whole family.

Additional celebration concerts are scheduled throughout the summer: at High Prairie Library on June 13 at 10:30 AM, and at Palmer Lake Library on July 10 at 6 PM.

Free Lunches Through July 31

While families are at the library, there's also free food. The PPLD Summer Lunches program provides free lunches to kids and teens 18 and under at East Library through July 31. Meals are available weekdays (excluding July 3) from 11 AM to noon. Adults can purchase lunch for $5. No sign-up or ID is required — just come to the library.

This program is funded through the USDA's Summer Food Service Program. It runs Monday through Friday and is available beginning May 27.

  • Cost: Free

  • Opening concerts: June 2 at 2 PM and 3:30 PM at East Library and Library 21c
  • Free lunches: Weekdays through July 31 at East Library, 11 AM–noon (youth 18 and under)

What's Next

If your family has not yet signed up, Tuesday is the day. Registration is instant, the program is completely free, and the prizes are real. With concerts, free lunches, and activities spanning two full months, Summer Adventure is one of the most practical summer resources available to Colorado Springs families — and it starts this week.
