CPSC Warns Families to Stop Using Fasando Baby Loungers Immediately — Infant Death Reported

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 31, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent safety warning about Fasando baby loungers sold on Amazon, urging families to stop using them immediately. The warning follows a reported infant death in 2025 connected to one of these products. Approximately 6,200 units were sold, and — critically — the manufacturer has not agreed to a formal recall, meaning many of these products remain in use in homes across the country.

If you have a Fasando baby lounger purchased on Amazon between August 2024 and July 2025, stop using it today. The steps below will tell you exactly what to do.

What Happened

According to the CPSC, a 2-month-old infant was found unresponsive in 2025 after being placed to sleep in a Fasando baby lounger on top of an adult bed. The CPSC has flagged the product as violating the mandatory standard for Infant Sleep Products — the federal safety standard designed to protect infants in sleep environments.

The CPSC issued a Notice of Violation to the seller, identified as Qiyangshiqiguangyuekejiyouxiangongsi, doing business as Fasando, based in China. The company has not responded to the notice with a formal recall or any consumer remedy. That refusal is what makes this warning, rather than a standard recall, particularly important for parents to act on independently.

Why This Product Is Dangerous

The CPSC has identified three specific hazards in Fasando baby loungers:

  • The sides are too low to keep an infant safely contained
  • The enclosed foot openings are wider than the standard allows, creating an entrapment hazard
  • The lounger has no stand, meaning it poses an additional fall hazard when placed on an elevated surface like a couch, bed, or changing table

Together, these issues create a combination of fall, entrapment, and suffocation risks that led to the CPSC's urgent warning.

What to Do Right Now

Because there is no formal recall and no refund process has been established, the CPSC's recommended steps are straightforward: render the product unusable and get it out of your home.

  1. Stop using the Fasando baby lounger immediately
  2. Remove the foam padding from inside the cover
  3. Cut the cover in half
  4. Dispose of the cover and padding in the trash

If you want to report an injury or incident, contact the CPSC at CPSC.gov or call 800-638-2772.

Quick Reference

  • Product: Fasando baby loungers
  • Sold on: Amazon.com
  • Sales period: August 2024 – July 2025
  • Price paid: About $35
  • Units sold: About 6,200
  • Hazard: Fall, entrapment, suffocation
  • Status: CPSC warning (no formal recall)
  • Action: Stop use, cut cover in half, dispose
  • Contact: CPSC.gov or 800-638-2772

A Note on Infant Sleep Products

Pediatric sleep safety guidelines from the American Academy of Pediatrics consistently recommend that infants sleep on their backs, alone, in a firm and flat sleep surface — such as a crib, bassinet, or play yard — that meets current safety standards. Baby loungers are not designed or approved as sleep surfaces under federal standards, regardless of brand or marketing. If you have any questions about safe infant sleep, speak with your pediatrician.

What's Next

The CPSC will continue to monitor whether Fasando agrees to a formal recall. Until that happens, there is no refund program, and the responsibility falls on families to remove these products from their homes. Please share this information with anyone you know who may have a young infant at home — especially anyone who recently purchased a baby lounger on Amazon.
