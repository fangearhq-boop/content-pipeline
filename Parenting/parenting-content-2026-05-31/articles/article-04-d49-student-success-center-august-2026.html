D49 Opens Student Success Center in August 2026: Preschool, Base49 Care, and Specialized Support

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 31, 2026

District 49 families will have access to a new resource this fall when the district opens its Student Success Center at the start of the 2026–27 school year. The center will be housed in the former Falcon Elementary School of Technology building and is designed to consolidate specialized student services — along with a new preschool program — in a single, accessible location.

What the Student Success Center Offers

The Student Success Center is built around two primary components: targeted academic and support services for students who need extra help, and a new early childhood program for the youngest D49 learners.

Two full-time preschool classrooms will operate Monday through Thursday within the center. These classrooms are designed to serve staff and families throughout the district — not just families who live near the building. This is a meaningful addition for D49 families who have been navigating preschool options in a region that has faced documented childcare and early education shortages.

The center will also offer before- and after-school care through Base49, the district's extended care program. Fridays will include childcare as well, rounding out the week for families who need coverage beyond the Monday–Thursday school schedule.

What This Means for Families

For families with preschool-age children, the center represents a district-run option in an area where affordable early childhood slots are genuinely scarce. El Paso County has one of the more significant childcare shortages in Colorado — with approximately three children for every available childcare spot in parts of the region. A new district-operated preschool with extended care options is a meaningful addition.

For families of students with learning or developmental needs, the center's specialized services are intended to bring resources together in a way that makes access more straightforward. The district has framed this as a "strategic realignment" — moving services that may have been spread across multiple buildings into one dedicated space.

Quick Reference

  • Opening: August 2026 (start of 2026–27 school year)
  • Location: Former Falcon Elementary School of Technology
  • Preschool: Two full-time classrooms, Monday–Thursday
  • Care options: Base49 before/after-school care + Friday childcare
  • Who it serves: Students needing specialized support; preschool-age children of D49 staff and families
  • More info: d49.org
