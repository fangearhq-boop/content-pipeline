Free June Events for Colorado Springs Families: Concerts, Museums, and Summer Fun

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 31, 2026

Summer in Colorado Springs does not have to be expensive. June is packed with free and low-cost events for families — outdoor concerts, museum days, community gatherings, and activities spread across the city. Here's what is coming up this month, so families can plan ahead without a lot of searching.

El Pomar Family Exploration Day — June 6

The Penrose Heritage Museum is hosting a free Family Exploration Day on Saturday, June 6 from 10 AM to 2 PM. The event is open to the public at no charge — a rare opportunity to explore one of Colorado Springs' historic museums with the family on a Saturday morning. The Penrose Heritage Museum focuses on local history, art, and Colorado's western heritage, and family programming days tend to include activities designed for younger visitors. Located in the Broadmoor area.

Free Bancroft Park Concerts — Every Tuesday Night

Starting in June and running through October, Bancroft Park hosts free outdoor concerts every Tuesday evening from 6 to 8 PM. The setting — a historic park in Old Colorado City — is family-friendly, and the concerts are a genuine Colorado Springs summer tradition. Bring a blanket or camp chairs, grab food from nearby Old Colorado City, and settle in for two hours of live music. No tickets, no admission, no reservations required.

First and Main Town Center Live Music — Every Friday

Every Friday in June and July, the First and Main Town Center hosts live music from 5 to 7 PM. Local bands bring a relaxed, community-focused atmosphere to the outdoor shopping area. It's a straightforward end-of-week activity for families — easy to drop in on the way home or make a small evening of it. The schedule typically rotates through local acts.

Old Colorado City Thursdays — Every Week in June

Every Thursday in June, Old Colorado City comes alive from 4 to 9 PM with music, food vendors, and community events. The historic shopping and arts district along Colorado Avenue is one of the most walkable neighborhoods in COS, and summer Thursdays turn it into a gathering point for families. Restaurants, ice cream shops, and local businesses stay open, making it easy to turn an evening stroll into a full outing.

Colorado College Summer Music Festival — June 6 through 26

The Colorado College Summer Music Festival runs from June 6 to June 26, featuring performances from 52 to 54 advanced student musicians from across the country. This three-week program combines intensive training with public performances — an opportunity for families to hear high-level classical and contemporary music in an academic setting. Ticket information and schedules are available through Colorado College.

PPLD Summer Adventure — Starting June 2

The Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure reading and activities program launches Tuesday, June 2. Free for all ages, with prizes and opening day concerts at East Library and Library 21c. Register at ppld.beanstack.org or any PPLD branch. Free lunches also available weekdays through July 31 at East Library for kids and teens 18 and under.

Quick Reference

  • El Pomar Family Day: June 6, 10 AM–2 PM, Penrose Heritage Museum — free
  • Bancroft Park concerts: Every Tuesday, June–October, 6–8 PM — free
  • First and Main music: Every Friday in June and July, 5–7 PM — free
  • Old Colorado City Thursdays: Every Thursday in June, 4–9 PM — free admission
  • CC Summer Music Festival: June 6–26 — check coloradocollege.edu for tickets
  • PPLD Summer Adventure: Starts June 2 — free, all ages, ppld.beanstack.org

What's Next

More summer events will be announced throughout June. Families looking for a regularly updated calendar can check visitcos.com/events and coloradosprings.gov/events for city-wide listings. COS Parenting will continue to highlight local events as they come up — especially those that are free or low-cost for families.
