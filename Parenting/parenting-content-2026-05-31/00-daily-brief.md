### STORY 1: D11 $775 Million Bond Referendum — What Colorado Springs Parents Need to Know
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Pillar:** Local News
**Type:** Article + 2 X posts + FB Long-Form + FB Image Caption + 2 image concepts

The Colorado Springs School District 11 Board is weighing a $775 million bond referendum for the November 2026 ballot that would fund air conditioning upgrades, building renovations, and teacher pay increases. The average D11 school building is 58 years old, and identified repair needs exceed $422 million. If approved, the bond would raise the mill levy from 37.7 to 46.3 mills — adding approximately $6.50 per month per $100,000 of property value. Voters would decide in November.

### STORY 3: CPSC Warning — Fasando Baby Loungers Linked to Infant Death: Stop Using Immediately
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Pillar:** National Parenting / Safety
**Type:** Article + 2 X posts + FB Long-Form + FB Image Caption + 2 image concepts

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent warning about Fasando baby loungers sold on Amazon, linked to the death of a 2-month-old infant in 2025. About 6,200 units were sold from August 2024 through July 2025 for approximately $35. The CPSC says the sides are too low, foot openings are too wide, and the lack of a stand creates fall and entrapment hazards. The manufacturer has not agreed to a formal recall. Families should immediately stop using the product, cut the cover in half, and dispose of it.
