D49 Free Summer Meals Start June 1 — Free Breakfast and Lunch for All Kids Ages 1 to 18

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 26, 2026

Starting Monday, June 1, District 49 will provide free breakfast and lunch to any child ages 1 to 18 in the Colorado Springs area. No income verification required. No D49 enrollment needed. The program runs through June 19.

If you're in the D49 area of Colorado Springs — eastern COS, Falcon, and the surrounding communities — here's news worth passing along: starting Monday, June 1, District 49 will offer free breakfast and lunch to any child ages 1 to 18 through its summer meal service.

The program requires no income verification and no enrollment in District 49 schools. Any child in the age range is eligible. Meals run through June 19.

Who It's For

This program is open to any child ages 1 to 18, regardless of whether they attend a D49 school or any public school at all. Families do not need to apply, fill out paperwork, or prove income eligibility. If your child is in the age range and you live in the area, they can participate.

This kind of broad access matters in summer. For many families, school meals are an important part of daily nutrition during the school year. When school ends and those programs stop, food security can become a real challenge — particularly over a stretch of weeks in June and July when regular routines are disrupted. Summer meal programs exist specifically to fill that gap.

Why This Matters

Summer hunger is a real and well-documented challenge. Studies consistently show that children who rely on school meal programs for daily nutrition are at significantly higher risk for food insecurity during the summer months. D49's summer meal service is one of the few structured options in the Colorado Springs area that provides two meals per day with no eligibility barriers.

The program has run in previous years, and we're glad to see it continuing in 2026. Whether your family needs it every day or just occasionally, it's there. And if you know a neighbor, a family at your church, or someone in your community who has kids and might benefit, sharing this information is genuinely helpful.

What's Next

The program starts June 1 and runs through June 19 — that's three weeks of free meals. Check d49.org for specific meal service locations and times closer to the start date. If you need food resources beyond what D49 provides, Colorado Kids Food Finder (kidsfoodfinder.org) is a statewide resource that maps free meal sites for children across Colorado. For COS families who are planning their summer and keeping an eye on what's available, mark June 1 on your calendar and spread the word.
