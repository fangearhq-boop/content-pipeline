Trackchair Accessible Adventures Are Back in Colorado Springs — Two Guided Hikes Daily at 4 Parks

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 26, 2026

Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation, and Cultural Services expanded the 2026 Trackchair program, offering two guided hikes per day, Wednesday through Sunday, at Ute Valley Park, Red Rock Canyon Open Space, Blodgett Open Space, and Bluestem Prairie Open Space.

One of the best things about Colorado Springs is the sheer number of open spaces and parks available to families. One of the ongoing challenges is making sure those spaces are genuinely accessible to everyone. The city's Trackchair program is one of the most direct responses to that challenge — and for 2026, it's back and expanded.

Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation, and Cultural Services (PRCS) is offering two guided Trackchair hikes per day, Wednesday through Sunday, at four parks across the city. Sessions run from 8 to 11 AM and from 12 to 3 PM, weather permitting.

What a Trackchair Is

A Trackchair is a motorized all-terrain wheelchair designed for outdoor use on trails, grass, gravel, and uneven terrain. Standard wheelchairs are typically limited to smooth, paved surfaces — a significant barrier when the best parks in Colorado Springs involve dirt paths, rocky sections, and open-space landscapes. Trackchairs are built specifically to handle those conditions, making parks that might otherwise be inaccessible available to people with mobility challenges.

The program is a collaboration between the city and local guides who lead participants through designated trail sections at each location. No special equipment or experience is required — just a reservation.

Who the Program Is For

The Trackchair program is open to anyone who can benefit from it. The city has designed it to serve people with mobility challenges, whether that means full-time wheelchair users, older adults with limited mobility, individuals recovering from injury, or families with a member who can't navigate traditional trails. Each session is guided, meaning you're accompanied by a program staff member who knows the trails.

The expansion to two sessions per day (up from one in prior years) and the addition of park locations means more scheduling flexibility for families who need it.

What's Next

If you have a family member who could benefit from the Trackchair program — or if you just want to experience these parks in a different way — reservations are available now at coloradosprings.gov/parks. Both morning and afternoon slots tend to fill up as the season gets going, so booking ahead is a good idea. The program runs Wednesday through Sunday through the summer season. Red Rock Canyon and Ute Valley Park in particular are spectacular in the late morning light. It's one of those COS resources that's genuinely underused and worth a try.
