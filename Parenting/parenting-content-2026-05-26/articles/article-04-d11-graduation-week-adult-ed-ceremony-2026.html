D11 Graduation Season Wraps Up — Adult and Family Education Ceremony Thursday, May 28

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 26, 2026

Colorado Springs District 11 held its high school graduation ceremonies May 19-21. One more ceremony remains: the Adult and Family Education Graduation this Thursday, May 28, at 7 PM.

Colorado Springs District 11 wrapped up its high school graduation ceremonies over the past week. Palmer, Mitchell, Coronado, Doherty, and several other D11 schools celebrated their Class of 2026 from May 19 through May 21, mostly at Ed Robson Arena at Colorado College. It's the culmination of a long school year, and to every family that made it to those ceremonies — or watched from the stands while your graduate walked across the stage — congratulations.

But graduation season isn't quite over yet. There's one more ceremony this week, and it's one worth knowing about.

Adult and Family Education Graduation — May 28, 7 PM

The D11 Adult and Family Education Graduation takes place this Thursday, May 28, at 7 PM at the Roy J. Wasson Academic Campus in Colorado Springs. This ceremony honours adults in the community who earned their high school diploma or an equivalency credential through the Adult and Family Education program.

This graduation tends to fly under the radar, but it shouldn't. The people walking across the stage at Wasson on Thursday are parents, grandparents, and working adults who pursued their education while managing jobs, childcare, and the other demands of daily life. Finishing a diploma on that kind of schedule takes a different kind of determination than the typical high school path.

Why It Matters

The Adult and Family Education program in Colorado Springs serves adults who, for any number of reasons, didn't complete their high school education earlier in life. Some were working. Some were raising families. Some faced circumstances that made finishing school impossible at the time. The D11 program gives them a path back, and the graduation ceremony at Wasson is the moment it becomes official.

If you know someone in your life who's been through the Adult and Family Education program, Thursday night at 7 PM is the place to be. Cheer them on. They've earned every moment of it.

What's Next

With graduation week nearly complete, Colorado Springs families are fully into summer. D11 schools are done for the year. If your family has younger kids still a year or more from that stage, summer stretch activities are already opening up — pools and splash pads are running, D49's free meal program starts June 1, and fall youth sports registration is open now through July 11 at ColoradoSprings.gov/YouthSports. For the graduates: congratulations, and welcome to whatever's next.
