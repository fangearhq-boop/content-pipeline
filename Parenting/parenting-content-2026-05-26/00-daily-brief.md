### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — Cosyland Children's Tower Stools (Entrapment Hazard)
**Tier:** 1
**Content pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

The Consumer Product Safety Commission announced a recall of Cosyland-brand children's tower stools on May 14, 2026. The stools — designed to let young children reach counter height — can collapse or tip over during use. More significantly, a child's torso can fit through openings on the front and back of the stool, creating an entrapment hazard that has been linked to serious injuries and is classified as a risk of death. About 125,200 units are affected. There have been 25 reported incidents including 8 injuries — ranging from scrapes and contusions to a fractured arm. Models CS0003 and CS0092-4 were sold on Amazon.com from April 2021 through November 2025 for approximately $70 each.

**What families should do:** Do not use the recalled stools. Contact Cosyland for a free repair kit that includes protective nets, stabilizing feet, and installation instructions. Call 866-677-3889 Monday–Friday, 9 AM–5 PM PT. Email: customerservice@cosyland.com. More info at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

**Key facts:**
- Product: Cosyland Children's Tower Stools, models CS0003 and CS0092-4
- Recall date: May 14, 2026
- Units recalled: 125,200
- Sold: Amazon.com, April 2021–November 2025, approximately $70 each
- Hazard: Collapse/tip-over + child torso can fit through openings (entrapment/fall)
- Reports: 25 incidents; 8 injuries (scrapes, contusions, 1 fractured arm)
- Remedy: Free repair kit (protective nets, stabilizing feet, instructions)
- Contact: 866-677-3889, Mon-Fri 9 AM-5 PM PT
- Email: customerservice@cosyland.com
- Sources: CPSC.gov, Fox Business, NBC Connecticut, KIRO 7, WSBTV

### STORY 5: Accessible Adventures Are Back — COS Expands Trackchair Program for 2026
**Tier:** 2
**Content pillar:** Local Events
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation, and Cultural Services (PRCS) expanded its Trackchair program for 2026, offering two guided hikes daily, Wednesday through Sunday, at four parks across the city. Trackchairs are motorized all-terrain wheelchairs designed for outdoor trails — making parks and open spaces accessible to people with mobility challenges. Hike times are 8–11 AM and 12–3 PM at each location, weather permitting.

**2026 Trackchair locations:**
- Ute Valley Park
- Red Rock Canyon Open Space
- Blodgett Open Space
- Bluestem Prairie Open Space

**Key facts:**
- Two guided hikes daily: 8–11 AM and 12–3 PM
- Days: Wednesday through Sunday
- Locations: Ute Valley Park, Red Rock Canyon Open Space, Blodgett Open Space, Bluestem Prairie Open Space
- Program: Trackchair (motorized all-terrain wheelchairs for people with mobility challenges)
- Must reserve in advance (details at coloradosprings.gov/parks)
- Weather permitting
- Sources: City of Colorado Springs (KOAA news, city news release)
