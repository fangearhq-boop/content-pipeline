CPSC Recall — Cosyland Children's Tower Stools (Entrapment Hazard)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM MT If you have a Cosyland tower stool at home, this is important. The Consumer Product Safety Commission issued a recall on May 14 for Cosyland-brand children's tower stools — the kind designed to let kids reach counter height while you're cooking, washing dishes, or working at a kitchen island. About 125,200 units have been recalled. The hazard: the stools can collapse or tip over while in use, and more seriously, a child's torso can fit through the openings on the front and back sides — creating an entrapment risk that CPSC classifies as a risk of death. There have been 25 reported incidents and 8 confirmed injuries, ranging from scrapes and bruises to a fractured arm. Here's what to do right now: — Stop using the stool immediately — Check your stool against the recalled models: CS0003 and CS0092-4 (sold on Amazon April 2021–November 2025, ~$70 each) — Call Cosyland at 866-677-3889, Monday–Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM PT, or email customerservice@cosyland.com — Cosyland will mail you a free repair kit (protective nets, stabilizing feet, and installation guide) — More information at cpsc.gov/Recalls If you're not sure whether your stool is affected, check the model number on the product label and compare it against the recall page. When in doubt, don't let kids use it until you've confirmed. Tagging this for visibility — share with any parents, grandparents, or caregivers in your circle. ---

### Long-Form Post Window: 10:00 AM MT If you've been meaning to sign your kids up for fall sports in Colorado Springs, here's your reminder: registration is already open — and time is moving. Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation, and Cultural Services opened fall youth sports registration on May 12. Both youth soccer and tackle football are available, with registration closing July 11. Late registration opens July 17, but it comes with a $5 fee per participant. Here's the quick breakdown: Youth Soccer — $86/participant Divisions: pre-K and kindergarten, 1st and 2nd grade, and 7th–9th grade (all coed) Tackle Football — $126/participant Divisions: 1st/2nd Coed, 3rd/4th Coed, 5th/6th Boys and Girls, 7th/8th Boys and Girls One important thing if your kids want to play tackle football: weigh-ins are required, and they start TODAY, May 26, at the Sports Office. Weigh-ins run through July 25, so you have time — but it's worth getting on the schedule now since spaces in some divisions are limited. Register online at ColoradoSprings.gov/YouthSports. If you have questions, the Sports Office can help. Does your family have a fall sports tradition in COS? Tell us in the comments. ---

D49 Free Summer Meals Start June 1 — All Kids Ages 1 to 18 Welcome
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 12:00 PM MT Here's some genuinely helpful news for Colorado Springs families heading into the summer. Starting Monday, June 1, District 49 will provide free breakfast and lunch to any child ages 1 to 18 — no income verification, no D49 enrollment required. The program runs through June 19. That means if you live in the eastern Colorado Springs or Falcon area, your kids can get two free meals a day for nearly three weeks starting next Monday, regardless of which school or district they attend (or whether they're in school at all). Summer meal programs like this one exist specifically because food access for many families changes when school is out and the regular breakfast/lunch programs stop. D49 has run this program in previous years and it's one of those quiet community resources that makes a real difference for families who use it. If you know of a family who could use this — a neighbor, a family at your church, a friend with young kiddos — share this post. Details can also be found on the D49 website at d49.org. What's a resource in COS that you think more families should know about? ---

Accessible Adventures Are Back — COS Expands Trackchair Program for 2026
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 5:00 PM MT Colorado Springs has one of the coolest outdoor programs in the state — and if you haven't heard of the Trackchair program, now's the time. The city's Parks, Recreation, and Cultural Services expanded the program for 2026, offering two guided hikes per day, Wednesday through Sunday, at four of Colorado Springs' most beautiful open spaces: Ute Valley Park Red Rock Canyon Open Space Blodgett Open Space Bluestem Prairie Open Space Trackchairs are motorized all-terrain wheelchairs designed for outdoor trails — they can handle the uneven terrain that's kept some parks out of reach for people with mobility challenges. Whether you have a family member who uses a wheelchair, a grandparent who's had trouble with uneven ground, or just wants an experience that works for everyone in the group, this program is worth knowing about. Hike windows: 8–11 AM and 12–3 PM, weather permitting. Reservations are required — details and booking at coloradosprings.gov/parks. Have you tried the Trackchair program before? Tell us which park was your favorite. ---
