### STORY 1: CPSC Recall — TOMY Boon NURSH 8 oz Baby Bottles (Choking Hazard, Walmart)
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Window:** 8:00 AM MT

TOMY International, in cooperation with the CPSC, recalled approximately 40,000 Boon NURSH Silicone Bottle 8 oz 3-Packs in Pink Tie Dye on June 4, 2026. The hard plastic outer shell can bubble or partially peel off, releasing loose film-like plastic that poses a choking hazard. The bottles were sold exclusively at Walmart and Walmart.com from November 2025 through May 2026 for about $20. Item number B11654, UPC 669028116546, printed on the packaging bottom. 135 reports of peeling, no injuries. Remedy: write "RECALL" on the bottles, photograph them, and submit the photo to certify disposal — receive either a replacement 3-pack in solid pink or a $22 Walmart.com store credit. Contact: 866-725-4407 (Mon–Fri 8 AM–4 PM CT) / cs@tomy.com / recall.tomy.com/nursh.

### STORY 4: New National Survey — 72% of Parents Want More Time With Their Kids But Money Gets in the Way
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Window:** 3:00 PM MT

The 2026 National Parent Survey from New America's New Practice Lab — the largest of its kind — surveyed 5,472 parents across all 50 states. Key findings: 72% of parents want more quality time with their children; 75% say their current work arrangement isn't what they want; child care averages $13,184 per child per year nationally, equal to one-quarter of a family's income at 200% of the federal poverty level; the median parental leave was 10 weeks for mothers and 2 weeks for fathers, and the majority said that wasn't enough. Researchers found financial constraints are the primary barrier to family quality time — not desire.

### STORY 5: COS Summer Sun Safety Guide — Altitude Means Higher UV Risk for Kids
**Tier:** 2 | **Pillar:** Evergreen | **Window:** 5:00 PM MT

Colorado Springs sits at 6,035 feet elevation, where UV radiation is significantly higher than at sea level — UV index regularly reaches 8+ (Very High) or 10+ (Extreme) in June and July. Summer is the ideal time for COS families to build sun-safety habits: SPF 30+ sunscreen, reapply every two hours or after water exposure; protective clothing and hats; plan outdoor activities before 10 AM or after 4 PM; watch for heat exhaustion signs in children (heavy sweating, pale skin, weakness, dizziness). Sources: CDC, AAP, EPA UV Guide.
