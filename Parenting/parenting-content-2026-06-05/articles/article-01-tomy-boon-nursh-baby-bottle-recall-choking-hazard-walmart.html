RECALL: TOMY Boon NURSH Baby Bottles Sold at Walmart Recalled for Choking Hazard

By Jamie Rivera | June 5, 2026

TOMY International, in cooperation with the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission, issued a recall on June 4, 2026 for approximately 40,000 Boon NURSH Silicone Bottle 8 oz 3-Packs in Pink Tie Dye. The hard plastic outer shell of the affected bottles can bubble or partially peel off, releasing loose film-like plastic pieces that pose a choking hazard to infants and young children.

The bottles were sold exclusively at Walmart stores and on Walmart.com from November 2025 through May 2026 for approximately $20. TOMY has received 135 reports of the outer shell bubbling or peeling. No injuries have been reported.

The item number and UPC are printed on the bottom of the packaging. Only the Pink Tie Dye colorway is included in this recall. Solid color NURSH bottles are not affected.

What to Do

Stop using the affected bottles immediately. The CPSC-approved remedy involves the following steps:

  1. Write the word "RECALL" clearly on each recalled bottle.
  2. Photograph the bottles showing the written word.
  3. Submit the photos to certify that the bottles have been disposed of.

After completing those steps, TOMY will provide your choice of either a replacement 3-pack in a solid pink color (Pink Iridescent or Pink, subject to availability) or a refund in the form of a $22 Walmart.com store credit.
