## STORY 1 — D11 Orton Academy at Trailblazer Elementary

**What happened:** D11 approved Orton Academy (K-8 dyslexia-specialized school) to co-locate at Trailblazer Elementary School for 2026-27 school year.

**Orton Academy:**
- K-8 school
- Opened 2020 (AI summary; MEDIUM — single source)
- Specializes in teaching children with dyslexia
- Only D11 charter school dedicated solely to dyslexia education (MEDIUM)
- Will have its own staff, students, and classrooms within Trailblazer

**Trailblazer Elementary:**
- Built for capacity ~600 students — HIGH (KOAA)
- Current enrollment ~220 — HIGH (KOAA)
- Under-enrolled → district sees co-location as resource expansion
- Place-based education school model (from d11.org/trailblazer)

**Parent Concerns:**
- Some learned about the announcement through Orton's website before official district email — HIGH (KOAA parent concerns article)
- Concerns: elementary students sharing spaces with middle schoolers — HIGH (KOAA)
- Specific concerns: gymnasium, cafeteria, bathrooms — HIGH (KOAA)
- District response: co-location increases access to dyslexia education and fills Trailblazer enrollment — HIGH (multiple sources)

**Sources:** Gazette April 8 + KOAA (2 articles) = 3 sources total — HIGH confidence

## STORY 2 — CPSC Amazon Baby Bath Seat Recalls

**Wave of recalls — multiple brands:**

**YCXXKJ bath seats (sold by BenTalk on Amazon):**
- Recalled due to drowning risk — unstable, can tip over
- Colors: blue, gray, pink, yellow
- Sold May 2024–October 2025 on Amazon
- Price: $34–$40
- Units: ~9,000
- No injuries reported
- Refund: contact BenTalk at bentalkhere@outlook.com (MEDIUM — AI summary, need verification)
- Source: CPSC.gov official page + The Hill + Fox Business = HIGH

**CheerKid bath seats (sold by Babibaby and Woot on Amazon):**
- Same issue: violates mandatory standard for infant bath seats, drowning risk
- Source: CPSC.gov page — HIGH

**Trankerloop bath seats:**
- Same drowning risk violation
- Source: CPSC.gov page — HIGH

**NFSVLB bath seats (sold by NFSVLB on Amazon):**
- Drowning + entrapment risk
- Source: CPSC.gov page — HIGH

**General note:** All four brands violate mandatory standard for infant bath seats. Parents should check Amazon order history for any bath seats purchased recently.

**What parents should do:** Stop using immediately; contact seller/manufacturer for full refund.

## STORY 3 — Corral Bluffs + Jimmy Camp Creek Tours

**What:** Free guided hikes at Corral Bluffs Open Space and Jimmy Camp Creek (both restricted-access sites in Colorado Springs)
**When registration opened:** Registration announced April 13, 2026 per KRDO article
**Tour season:** May through October 2026
**Schedule:**
- Corral Bluffs: First Saturday of each month
- Jimmy Camp Creek: Last Saturday of each month
- Both tours: 8 AM to noon

**Registration:** coloradosprings.gov/corralbluffs
**Cost:** Free, but advance registration required
**Group size:** Limited (protect the sites)

**About Corral Bluffs:**
- 700+ acres east of Colorado Springs
- Globally important paleontological discovery site
- Fossils approximately 66 million years old (post-extinction event)
- Uneven terrain; participants should prepare for changing weather, minimal amenities

**Source:** City of COS press release (coloradosprings.gov) + KRDO April 13, 2026 = 2 sources — HIGH

## STORY 4 — AAP 2026 Immunization Schedule

**Key fact:** The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) has kept its 2026 childhood immunization schedule intact, with no changes to routine vaccine recommendations. This comes despite changes to the federal (HHS/CDC) advisory process.

**Why it matters for COS families:** Some parents have been confused about conflicting signals regarding vaccine policy. The AAP's consistent position is important signal for parents.

**Routine vaccines covered:** MMR, DTaP, Hib, IPV, varicella, Hep B, Hep A, influenza, COVID-19, HPV, meningococcal (standard schedule)

**AAP's position on HPV:** Recent blog post emphasizes "Starting HPV Protection Early" — recommended at age 11-12 (standard)

**Sources:** AAP official website + news release summary — HIGH (single primary source); well-established medical organization
