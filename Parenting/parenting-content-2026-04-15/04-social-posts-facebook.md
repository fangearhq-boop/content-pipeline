### STORY 1: D11 Orton Academy to Co-Locate at Trailblazer Elementary (2026-27)

**Long-Form Post**
Posting Window: 9:00 AM MT

If your kids attend Trailblazer Elementary, or if you've been searching for dyslexia-focused education in Colorado Springs, there's some significant D11 news to catch up on.

District 11 has approved Orton Academy — a K-8 school that specializes exclusively in educating students with dyslexia — to co-locate at Trailblazer Elementary School for the 2026-27 school year. Orton will have its own classrooms, staff, and students within the Trailblazer building, operating alongside the elementary school rather than merging with it.

Here's the context: Trailblazer has capacity for about 600 students, but current enrollment sits around 220. The district sees the co-location as a way to make better use of space while expanding access to specialized dyslexia education. Orton would be the only D11-authorized school dedicated solely to students with dyslexia.

Some Trailblazer families have raised concerns, including the fact that many learned about the plan through Orton's website before receiving an official communication from D11. Parents have also asked questions about shared spaces — gymnasiums, cafeterias, and restrooms — particularly given that Orton serves K-8 students (including middle schoolers) and Trailblazer serves a younger elementary population.

The district has said the co-location creates a greater resource for dyslexic students in the community, and that both schools will benefit from the arrangement.

Whether you see this as an opportunity or a concern, it's worth knowing the full picture. Are you a Trailblazer or Orton family? We'd love to hear your perspective.

### STORY 2: CPSC Recalls Multiple Amazon Baby Bath Seats — Drowning Risk

**Long-Form Post**
Posting Window: 8:30 AM MT

If you have a baby and bought a bath seat on Amazon in the last couple of years, please read this now.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued recalls for multiple brands of baby bath seats sold on Amazon, all due to the same serious problem: they can tip over while in use, posing a risk of drowning.

The recalled brands include:
🛁 YCXXKJ (sold on Amazon by BenTalk) — about 9,000 units; sold May 2024–October 2025; $34–$40; colors: blue, gray, pink, yellow
🛁 CheerKid (sold by Babibaby and Woot on Amazon)
🛁 Trankerloop
🛁 NFSVLB

All four brands violate the mandatory standard for infant bath seats. No injuries have been reported for the YCXXKJ recall, but the CPSC asks parents to stop using the seats immediately and contact the seller for a full refund.

A few things worth doing right now: Check your Amazon order history for any bath seats you've purchased. Check your Amazon Warehouse, Buy Box sellers, and third-party listings — these products were sold under multiple storefronts.

For the full CPSC recall details, go to cpsc.gov and search for the brand name.

We know it's a lot to track. But bath safety is one of the highest-risk moments for young children, and a few minutes checking your order history is worth it.

### STORY 3: Free Corral Bluffs + Jimmy Camp Creek Tours Open for Registration

**Long-Form Post**
Posting Window: 10:30 AM MT

One of the most special outdoor experiences in Colorado Springs just opened its 2026 registration window — and spots will go fast.

The City of Colorado Springs has opened sign-ups for free guided hikes at Corral Bluffs Open Space and Jimmy Camp Creek. These aren't regular park hikes. Corral Bluffs is a 700-plus-acre paleontological site east of Colorado Springs where researchers discovered one of the world's most significant records of life recovering after the mass extinction event 66 million years ago. It's the kind of place that stops you cold when you realize what you're walking through.

The 2026 tour season runs May through October:
📅 Corral Bluffs: First Saturday of each month
📅 Jimmy Camp Creek: Last Saturday of each month
⏰ Both run 8 a.m. to noon

The hikes are free, but advance registration is required and space is limited to protect the sites. In past years, spots have filled within days of registration opening. The terrain is uneven, weather changes quickly, and amenities on-site are minimal — so bring layers, sturdy shoes, and water.

To register: coloradosprings.gov/corralbluffs

If you have kids who are into science, fossils, hiking, or just need an outdoor adventure that feels genuinely different from the usual trails, this is worth the early-morning effort.

### STORY 4: AAP Maintains 2026 Immunization Schedule — What COS Parents Need to Know

**Long-Form Post**
Posting Window: 12:30 PM MT

We know there's been a lot of noise lately about vaccines and federal health guidance. We wanted to share a clear update for COS parents from the source we trust most on this.

The American Academy of Pediatrics — the professional organization of U.S. pediatricians — has announced that its 2026 childhood immunization schedule is unchanged. All routine vaccine recommendations remain the same. No new additions, no removals, no changes to timing. The AAP reviewed its schedule independently and found no reason to alter the recommendations for children.

This matters because the federal advisory process around vaccines has seen some changes. The AAP's decision to hold firm on its own evidence-based schedule is a signal that your pediatrician's recommendations are grounded in the same science they've always been.

What's on the schedule? The AAP 2026 schedule covers all the routine vaccines you'd expect: MMR (measles, mumps, rubella), DTaP (diphtheria, tetanus, whooping cough), polio, Hib, varicella (chickenpox), hepatitis A and B, influenza, HPV (starting at age 11-12), meningococcal, and others as recommended.

The best thing to do: bring any questions to your Colorado Springs pediatrician. They have access to the full AAP schedule and can walk you through what's recommended for your child's age, any catch-up doses needed, and what to expect.

The AAP's full 2026 immunization schedule is available at aap.org.
