Spring Youth Sports Registration Is Open in Colorado Springs — What Families Need to Know

By Marcus Rivera | April 15, 2026

The City of Colorado Springs has opened registration for 2026 spring youth and adult sports leagues. Space is limited — here's how to sign your kids up before spots fill.

If spring sports are on your family's calendar this year, now's the time to act. The City of Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services (PRCS) Department has opened registration for its 2026 spring youth and adult sports programs, and spots in some programs are already limited.

Programs are held at neighborhood parks and city facilities throughout Colorado Springs, making it easy for families across most parts of the city to find something close to home. Spring leagues and activity programs run through the warmer months, giving kids an opportunity to build skills, meet teammates, and stay active as the school year wraps up.

What's Available

The Parks and Recreation Department offers a variety of spring sports leagues and organized activities for youth and adults. Youth soccer is one of the featured programs, with a registration fee of $86 per participant.

For the full list of available spring programs, ages, schedules, and fees, visit the City of Colorado Springs sports registration page at ColoradoSprings.gov. Programs can fill quickly, and the city's guidance is to register early to secure your spot.

Why Spring Sports Matter for COS Kids

Spring in Colorado Springs brings genuinely great conditions for outdoor sports — mild temperatures, increasingly long days, and the kind of backdrop (Pikes Peak on clear days, wide open parks) that makes outdoor activity feel like more than just exercise. For kids who spend most of winter indoors, spring leagues provide structure, social connection, and physical activity at a time when all three tend to drop off.

Research on youth physical activity consistently shows that organized sports participation in childhood correlates with higher activity levels in adolescence and adulthood. Getting kids into programs now, when they're young and the habits are forming, makes a lasting difference.

For COS families, the city's programs are also an accessible price point. At $86 for youth soccer, the cost is competitive with private club options and significantly lower than travel team fees, making organized sports accessible to a wider range of families.

Finding Programs Near You

Colorado Springs is a large city with parks and facilities spread across multiple neighborhoods. The PRCS sports page at ColoradoSprings.gov shows program locations alongside registration information, making it easy to filter by neighborhood or sport. If you have questions about specific programs or locations, the Sports Office can help.

The Sports Office is located at 1315 East Pikes Peak Avenue in Memorial Park — a central location that's easy to find. Phone hours and additional details are available on the city's website.

Register now at ColoradoSprings.gov before spots fill. If you'd prefer to call or stop by with questions, the Sports Office is at 1315 East Pikes Peak Avenue in Memorial Park, phone (719) 385-5981.
