D11 Approves Orton Academy to Join Trailblazer Elementary — What Families Need to Know

By Sofia Reyes | April 15, 2026

Colorado Springs School District 11 will co-locate a dyslexia-specialized charter school at Trailblazer Elementary starting in 2026-27. Here's what it means for both schools' families.

Colorado Springs School District 11 has approved Orton Academy — a K-8 school that specializes in educating students with dyslexia — to move into Trailblazer Elementary School for the 2026-27 school year. The two schools will operate in the same building with separate classrooms, staff, and student populations.

For families with children at Trailblazer, the announcement has come with questions. Many parents say they first learned about the plan through an announcement on Orton Academy's website before receiving an official communication from D11. Some have raised concerns about what it means to share common spaces — the gymnasium, cafeteria, and restrooms — with older students from Orton's middle school grades.

For families who've been searching for specialized dyslexia support within COS's school system, the news is more straightforwardly positive: it expands access to structured literacy instruction for students who need it.

About the Two Schools

Trailblazer Elementary is a place-based education school in Colorado Springs, offering project-based learning rooted in the natural environment. The school was built with capacity for approximately 600 students, but its current enrollment sits around 220 — significantly underutilizing the facility.

Orton Academy is a K-8 school that opened in 2020 and focuses exclusively on educating students with dyslexia. It uses Orton-Gillingham-based reading instruction, which is a structured, multisensory approach that's recognized as highly effective for students who struggle with traditional reading instruction. It would be the only D11-authorized school dedicated entirely to dyslexia education.

Under the approved plan, Orton will occupy its own designated classrooms within the Trailblazer building, with its own staff and students. The schools will operate independently, though they'll share some areas of the facility.

What Parents Are Asking

The community reaction at Trailblazer has been mixed. Some parents have expressed support for expanding dyslexia resources in the district. Others have raised practical concerns.

The communication process was one issue. Several parents said they found out about the plan from Orton's website rather than a direct message from D11 — which is not the way most parents want to learn that their child's school is adding a new co-tenant.

Shared spaces are another consideration. Orton serves students through eighth grade, and some Trailblazer parents have asked about how a younger elementary school population will share facilities with middle school students, including bathrooms and the cafeteria.

D11 has indicated that logistics are still being worked out and that both schools will have clear plans for how shared spaces are managed. The district's position is that the co-location benefits both schools: it gives Orton a stable facility within D11, and it addresses the enrollment gap at Trailblazer.

What This Means for Families

If your children attend Trailblazer, it's worth staying engaged with D11 communications about how the 2026-27 transition will be managed. Specific details about shared space schedules, drop-off and pickup logistics, and any programming changes should come from D11 as the year approaches.

If you're a family looking for specialized dyslexia support for your child, Orton Academy within D11 represents an expanding option. Registration information and enrollment details for Orton Academy can be found through D11's charter schools page at d11.org.

What's Next

D11 is expected to provide more detailed transition plans for Trailblazer families as the 2026-27 school year approaches. If you have questions specific to Trailblazer's programming, reach out to the school directly or through D11's website at d11.org.
