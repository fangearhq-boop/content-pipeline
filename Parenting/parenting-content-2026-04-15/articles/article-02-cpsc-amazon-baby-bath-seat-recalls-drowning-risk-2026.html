CPSC Recalls Multiple Amazon Baby Bath Seats — Check Your Order History Now

By Marcus Rivera | April 15, 2026

Four brands of baby bath seats sold on Amazon have been recalled by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission due to tip-over and drowning risk. Here's what parents need to know.

If you've purchased a baby bath seat on Amazon in the last two years, it's worth pulling up your order history. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) has issued recalls for four brands of baby bath seats — YCXXKJ, CheerKid, Trankerloop, and NFSVLB — all for the same reason: the seats can tip over while in use, creating a drowning risk for infants.

Baby bath seats are designed to help keep infants upright in the tub during bathing. They are not meant to be used unsupervised, but even with a parent nearby, a seat that tips unpredictably can create a dangerous situation in seconds. The CPSC's standard for infant bath seats exists specifically to prevent this scenario, and all four recalled brands violate that standard.

Which Products Are Recalled

The four recalled brands, all sold through Amazon, are:

YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats — Sold on Amazon by BenTalk. Available in blue, gray, pink, and yellow. Sold between May 2024 and October 2025 for $34 to $40. Approximately 9,000 units were sold. No injuries have been reported for this product.

CheerKid Baby Bath Seats — Sold on Amazon by Babibaby and Woot. Also violate the mandatory standard for infant bath seats, posing a drowning risk.

Trankerloop Baby Bath Seats — Same drowning risk and standard violation. Sold through Amazon storefronts.

NFSVLB Baby Bath Seats — Sold on Amazon by NFSVLB. Cited for both drowning and entrapment hazards.

What to Do If You Have One

The CPSC's instruction for all four brands is straightforward: stop using the product immediately and contact the seller for a full refund. Do not continue to use the seat while waiting for a replacement or refund.

To find recall details for each brand, visit cpsc.gov and search for the brand name. The recall pages include specific instructions for requesting a refund and, in some cases, guidance on how to dispose of the product safely.

It's also worth checking your Amazon account's past orders. Bath seats can be purchased easily as gifts or on impulse and then stored between uses, which means you may have one on hand that you purchased several months ago and don't immediately recall. Check any bath seats in storage too.

Bath Safety Basics While You Wait for a Refund

If you discover you have one of these recalled seats and are waiting for a replacement or a refund, the standard guidance for infant bath time applies: never leave a baby unattended in a bath, even for a few seconds. Infants can drown in just a few inches of water.

For the short term, you can safely bathe an infant without a seat by supporting them manually throughout the bath, using a small amount of warm water, and having everything you need within arm's reach before you begin. The temporary inconvenience of bathing without a seat is far outweighed by the safety of knowing the equipment you're using meets current standards.

What's Next

Check your Amazon order history and any bath products in storage. For full recall details and refund instructions, visit cpsc.gov and search for "YCXXKJ," "CheerKid," "Trankerloop," or "NFSVLB." The CPSC also maintains a recall notification service at cpsc.gov/recalls where parents can sign up for email alerts on new recalls.
