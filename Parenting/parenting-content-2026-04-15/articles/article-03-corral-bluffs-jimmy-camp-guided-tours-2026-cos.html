Free COS Fossil Tours Are Back — How to Register for Corral Bluffs and Jimmy Camp Creek 2026

By Sofia Reyes | April 15, 2026

The City of Colorado Springs just opened registration for its 2026 guided hike season at two of the most extraordinary (and hard-to-access) natural sites in the region. Here's how to get in.

If you've lived in Colorado Springs for a while and never been to Corral Bluffs Open Space, this is the year to change that. Registration just opened for the 2026 guided tour season — and if past years are any indication, spots will disappear quickly.

The City of Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services Department runs limited-access guided hikes at Corral Bluffs and Jimmy Camp Creek from May through October each year. These aren't your average park trail walks. Both sites require a guide and advance registration — in part to protect the landscapes, which hold some of the most significant paleontological discoveries made anywhere in the world in the last decade.

What Makes Corral Bluffs Special

Corral Bluffs is a 700-plus-acre open space east of Colorado Springs. In 2019, researchers announced that the site contained one of the most complete records anywhere on Earth of life recovering in the years immediately after the mass extinction event 66 million years ago — the same event that ended the era of the dinosaurs. The fossils embedded in the rock formations here include ancient mammals, plants, and insects from a period of Earth's history that is rarely preserved with this kind of detail.

It's a world-class scientific site. And it's in Colorado Springs. That's worth taking the kids to see.

What to Know Before You Go

The terrain at Corral Bluffs is uneven, and weather in Colorado can shift quickly even in May and October. Plan for layers, bring water, and wear sturdy shoes. On-site amenities are minimal, so pack as if you're going on a backcountry hike rather than a casual stroll.

These hikes are family-friendly, but work best with kids who are comfortable walking on uneven terrain for a few hours. The four-hour tour format and early start time (8 a.m.) means this is a morning commitment — pack breakfast and snacks.
