### STORY 3: Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers Recalled

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

```
Heads up for COS parents with babies: the CPSC recalled Joyful Journeys baby loungers in January 2026, and we want to make sure you've seen this.

The recall (No. 26-181) covers approximately 9,300 loungers sold on Amazon and Walmart from December 2023 through October 2025 for between $40 and $60. The styles include green avocado, blue dinosaur, blue lambs, blue zoo animals, green minky, neutral elephants, pink zoo animals, and white minky.

Why were they recalled? The sides are too low to safely contain an infant, and the enclosed openings at the foot of the lounger are wider than allowed, creating an entrapment risk. The lounger also has no stand, meaning it can tip off elevated surfaces.

What to do: Stop using it right now. Cut the cover, foam, and pad in half. Take photos of the destroyed pieces and email them to joyfuljourneysrecalls@myjoyfuljourneys.com for a full refund.

The good news: no injuries were reported as of the recall announcement. Let's keep it that way.

Have you seen this recall before today?
```

### STORY 5: Summer Sun Safety for COS Kids

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

```
It's officially outdoor season in Colorado Springs — which means it's time to talk sunscreen.

The American Academy of Pediatrics has clear guidance that's especially worth knowing at our altitude:

For babies under 6 months: Skip the sunscreen. Keep them in the shade, in lightweight long-sleeved clothes, and out of direct sun. Their skin is too sensitive for sunscreen ingredients.

For kids 6 months and older: Use a broad-spectrum sunscreen with SPF 15 to 50. Apply about an ounce to all exposed areas. Reapply every 2 hours, and every 40-80 minutes after swimming.

Choose wisely: The AAP recommends avoiding sunscreens with oxybenzone (it has mild hormonal properties). For sensitive skin, zinc oxide or titanium dioxide are solid choices.

The COS altitude factor: We're sitting around 6,000 feet. UV radiation is measurably stronger at elevation than at sea level. The same amount of sun exposure here hits harder than it would closer to the coast — which makes SPF non-negotiable on sunny days.

Peak UV hours are 10 AM to 4 PM. Seeking shade, protective clothing, and timing your outdoor activities around those hours all help.
```
