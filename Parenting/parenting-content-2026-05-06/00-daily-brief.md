### STORY 3: Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers Recalled — 9,300 Units on Amazon and Walmart
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** National Parenting / CPSC Recall
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

The CPSC recalled approximately 9,300 Joyful Journeys baby loungers on January 8, 2026 (Recall No. 26-181). The loungers — sold December 2023 through October 2025 on Amazon and Walmart for $40 to $60 — violate the mandatory safety standard for Infant Sleep Products. Hazards: the sides are too low to contain an infant; enclosed openings at the foot are wider than allowed, creating entrapment risk; no stand means the lounger can tip off elevated surfaces. Styles include green avocado, blue dinosaur, blue lambs, blue zoo animals, green minky, neutral elephants, pink zoo animals, and white minky. Action: stop use immediately, cut the cover, foam, and pad in half, email photos of the destroyed pieces to joyfuljourneysrecalls@myjoyfuljourneys.com for a full refund. As of the recall announcement, no injuries had been reported.

### STORY 4: COS Parks Summer 2026 — New Trails Coming, But Budget Cuts Affect Restrooms
**Tier:** 2
**Type:** Local News
**Pillar:** Local News
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Colorado Springs families heading to neighborhood parks this summer will notice changes tied to city budget adjustments. Approximately 25% of neighborhood park permanent restrooms will remain closed for the 2026 season due to budget cuts. At the same time, COS Parks is moving forward with meaningful improvements: the Rock Island Trail is getting a 210-foot pedestrian bridge over Sand Creek connecting the west and east sides, with a pedestrian-activated flashing crossing at Peterson Road beginning Spring 2026. The Starsmore Visitor Center at North Cheyenne Cañon will receive accessibility improvements, expanded parking, and a renovated restroom. Garden of the Gods Park is getting systemwide wayfinding improvements for better navigation and trailhead access. Families using neighborhood parks are advised to check the city's Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services page (coloradosprings.gov/PRCS) for up-to-date restroom closures before heading out.

### STORY 5: Summer Sun Safety for COS Kids — What the AAP Recommends
**Tier:** 2
**Type:** National Parenting / Evergreen
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

With Colorado Springs summer arriving and families spending more time outdoors, the American Academy of Pediatrics' (AAP) sun safety guidance is essential reading. Key recommendations: no sunscreen for babies under 6 months (keep them in the shade and covered instead); broad-spectrum SPF 15-50 for children 6 months and older; avoid sunscreens containing oxybenzone due to mild hormonal concerns, choosing zinc oxide or titanium dioxide for sensitive skin; apply one ounce to all exposed areas and reapply every two hours (every 40-80 minutes after swimming). At Colorado Springs' elevation of roughly 6,000 feet, UV radiation is measurably higher than at sea level — making sun protection even more critical. The AAP also recommends sun-protective clothing, shade-seeking, and adjusting outdoor timing to avoid peak UV hours (10 AM to 4 PM).
