## STORY 5: Summer Sun Safety for COS Kids

**Source:** aap.org, aad.org, publications.aap.org, contemporarypediatrics.com
**Confidence:** HIGH

- [HIGH] AAP: No sunscreen for babies under 6 months; use shade and coverage instead
- [HIGH] AAP: Broad-spectrum SPF 15 to 50 for children 6 months and older
- [HIGH] AAP: Avoid sunscreen ingredient oxybenzone (mild hormonal concerns)
- [HIGH] AAP: Zinc oxide or titanium dioxide recommended for sensitive skin
- [HIGH] Application: approximately 1 ounce (shot glass) on exposed skin
- [HIGH] Reapplication: every 2 hours; every 40-80 minutes after swimming
- [HIGH] Colorado Springs elevation: approximately 6,000 feet
- [HIGH] UV radiation is higher at altitude than at sea level
- [HIGH] Peak UV hours: 10 AM to 4 PM
- [HIGH] Multi-layer protection: sunscreen + sun-protective clothing + shade + timing
