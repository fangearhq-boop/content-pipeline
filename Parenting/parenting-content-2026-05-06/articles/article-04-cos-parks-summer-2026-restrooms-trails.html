COS Parks Summer 2026: New Trails Coming, But Budget Cuts Mean Restroom Closures

By Jamie Rivera | May 6, 2026

Colorado Springs is investing in major trail improvements this year. But before you plan your next family park visit, know that about a quarter of neighborhood park restrooms won't be open this summer.

Summer in Colorado Springs means trails, parks, and long evenings outdoors with the family. Before you pack up the stroller, fill the water bottles, and head to your nearest neighborhood park, there's a practical update worth knowing: city budget adjustments for 2026 mean approximately 25% of permanent restrooms at neighborhood parks will remain closed for the season.

That's the heads-up. The good news is that Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services (PRCS) is simultaneously making meaningful improvements across the city's trail and park network — and some of those projects will directly benefit COS families with kids.

The Restroom Situation

The closure of roughly one in four neighborhood park permanent restrooms is a direct result of the city's budget reduction process for 2026. The city also noted reductions in portable restroom availability at some park locations. For families with toddlers or young children who are still in the midst of potty training, or anyone who's ever done a frantic lap around a park looking for a bathroom, this is worth factoring into your planning.

The PRCS updates its parks status at coloradosprings.gov/PRCS. Before heading to a specific neighborhood park — especially one off the beaten path — a quick check of that resource can save you a frustrating surprise.

Rock Island Trail: New Bridge Coming

One of the most significant trail improvements for 2026 is happening on the Rock Island Trail. The city is installing a 210-foot pedestrian bridge over Sand Creek, connecting the west and east sides of the trail with a new pedestrian-activated flashing crossing at Peterson Road. This project was anticipated to begin in Spring 2026 and will substantially improve trail connectivity and safety for families using the Rock Island corridor.

The Rock Island Trail runs through the eastern part of Colorado Springs and connects neighborhoods that previously had a gap requiring walkers and cyclists to navigate surface streets to continue their route. The new bridge makes that gap disappear. For families who bike or walk as regular outdoor activity, this is a meaningful improvement.

Starsmore Visitor Center Upgrades

The Starsmore Visitor Center at North Cheyenne Cañon is receiving accessibility and facility improvements in 2026. The project includes relocating and expanding accessible parking, improving bus parking for school groups, adding ADA-accessible walkways, creating a new plaza space, and renovating the restrooms. North Cheyenne Cañon is one of the most popular destinations for COS families with kids — the Helen Hunt Falls area alone draws thousands of visitors each summer. These upgrades should make the visit experience smoother for families with strollers, adaptive equipment, or larger groups.

Garden of the Gods Wayfinding

Garden of the Gods Park is getting systemwide wayfinding improvements in 2026, including new installations to help visitors navigate between trailheads and open spaces more effectively. For first-time visitors — including families who are new to COS or guests from out of town — better wayfinding signs and infrastructure reduce the chance of ending up on an unintended trail or missing the most accessible routes for kids.

What's Next

As summer outdoor season ramps up in Colorado Springs, PRCS continues to update its parks status and amenity availability at coloradosprings.gov/PRCS. Families heading to North Cheyenne Cañon, Garden of the Gods, or the Rock Island Trail corridor can expect meaningful improvements this season. For neighborhood park visits, the restroom situation is worth a quick check beforehand, particularly for outings with younger kids. The trade-off between budget reductions and capital improvements is a real tension — but knowing what's open and what's under construction makes planning a lot easier.
