MeadowGrass Music Festival 2026: Your Family Guide to Memorial Day Weekend in Colorado Springs

By Jamie Rivera | May 6, 2026

The 17th Annual MeadowGrass returns May 22-24 at La Foret. Kids 12 and under get in free — and there's a full Kid Zone to keep them happy while you enjoy the music.

Memorial Day Weekend in Colorado Springs just got a lot more musical. The 17th Annual MeadowGrass Music Festival returns May 22-24 at La Foret Conference and Retreat Center, 6145 Shoup Road — and it's one of the more genuinely family-friendly outdoor events on the COS summer calendar.

The festival runs Friday through Sunday on several hundred acres of ponderosa forest, canyons, and historic buildings at La Foret, offering the kind of Colorado outdoor setting that makes a weekend away from home feel worth the drive even if you're only going 20 minutes up the road.

Why It Works for Families

The practical piece that makes MeadowGrass work for COS parents: kids 12 and under get in free with a paid adult. That removes one of the most common friction points that keeps families from attending multi-day music events, and it's a genuine policy rather than fine-print marketing.

The Faricy Boys Kid Zone is the anchor of the family experience. It includes giant bubble performances from Milibo Art Theater, crafts programming from Concrete Couch, a hay wagon, kid yoga, and art activities. For younger kids who aren't ready to stand in a crowd for full sets, the Kid Zone offers a structured alternative that lets families split their attention between the music and keeping everyone content.

What Else to Expect

Beyond the music and the Kid Zone, MeadowGrass offers camping in the Ponderosa Forest. For families who want to make a full weekend of it, setting up camp at La Foret and spending multiple days on the property can be a genuinely memorable experience — the setting is Colorado at its most scenic, with trails, trees, and big sky visible from the campgrounds.

A Beer Festival runs on Friday evening for adults who want to mix craft beer with the opening night lineup. Local food trucks are on-site throughout the weekend, covering the full range from sweet to savory. Families without dietary restrictions generally do fine with the food truck options; if you have specific needs, checking ahead with the organizers or packing snacks is a reasonable backup plan.

What's Next

MeadowGrass is organized by the Rocky Mountain Highway Music Collaborative. Tickets and updated lineup information are available at rockymountainhighway.org. The festival is now in its 17th year, which speaks to its staying power in the Colorado Springs events calendar — this is not a pop-up; it's an institution.
