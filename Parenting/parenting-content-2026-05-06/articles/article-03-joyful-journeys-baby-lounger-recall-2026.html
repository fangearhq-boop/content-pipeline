Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers Recalled: 9,300 Units From Amazon and Walmart — What to Do Now

By Sarah Morales | May 6, 2026

The CPSC recalled nearly 9,300 Joyful Journeys baby loungers in January 2026. If you have one, stop using it immediately — here's exactly what to do to get a full refund.

Colorado Springs parents with babies in the house: if you purchased a Joyful Journeys baby lounger on Amazon or Walmart in the past two years, check your nursery today. The Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) issued a recall in January 2026 covering approximately 9,300 of these loungers due to serious entrapment and fall hazards that violate the mandatory safety standard for Infant Sleep Products.

The recall (No. 26-181) was issued by Sarnia Sales LLC, doing business as Joyful Journeys of Issaquah, Washington. The company sold the loungers on Amazon.com and Walmart.com from December 2023 through October 2025, priced between $40 and $60.

Which Loungers Are Affected

The recalled Joyful Journeys baby loungers are made of a foam sleeping pad and padded bumpers with a cloth cover. They zip up into a bag with a carrying strap at the top. The CPSC confirmed that the following styles are included in the recall:

  • Green avocado
  • Blue dinosaur
  • Blue lambs
  • Blue zoo animals
  • Green minky
  • Neutral elephants
  • Pink zoo animals
  • White minky

If your lounger matches any of these descriptions and was purchased in the 2023-2025 window, treat it as recalled until you can confirm otherwise at cpsc.gov.

The Safety Hazards

The CPSC identified three specific hazards with these loungers. First, the sides of the product are too low to safely contain an infant — a design flaw that can allow babies to roll or shift out of a contained sleeping position. Second, the enclosed openings at the foot of the lounger are wider than allowed under the mandatory standard, creating an entrapment risk where an infant's body could become trapped. Third, the lounger has no stand, which means it can tip off elevated surfaces like a couch, table, or bed if placed on one.

As of the January 8, 2026 recall announcement, no incidents or injuries had been reported. The goal of acting quickly is to keep it that way.

What To Do Right Now

The CPSC and Joyful Journeys have outlined the following steps for a full refund:

  1. Stop using the lounger immediately.
  2. Remove the foam and pads from the lounger's cloth cover.
  3. Cut the cover, foam, and pad in half.
  4. Photograph the destroyed pieces.
  5. Email the photos to joyfuljourneysrecalls@myjoyfuljourneys.com to request a full refund.

The destruction step is required to ensure the recalled product cannot be resold or reused. If you have questions, you can also visit the recall page directly at cpsc.gov and search for Recall No. 26-181. The CPSC hotline is 1-800-638-2772, available Monday through Friday 8:00 AM to 5:30 PM ET.

What's Next

If you've already discarded the lounger before seeing this recall, you can still contact Joyful Journeys at the email address above to discuss refund options — destroyed products without photos may still qualify depending on proof of purchase. Share this recall with anyone in your circle who may have purchased one of these loungers. In Colorado Springs and across the country, many of these products were purchased as gifts or handed down between families, which means the original buyer may not be the person currently using the product. A quick message to a friend or family member with a new baby could matter.
