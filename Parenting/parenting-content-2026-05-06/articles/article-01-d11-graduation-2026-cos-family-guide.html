D11 Graduation Season Starts May 19: Your Complete Colorado Springs Family Guide

By Sarah Morales | May 6, 2026

District 11 sends off the Class of 2026 across six ceremonies at Ed Robson Arena. Here are the dates, times, and venues your family needs.

Graduation season is here for Colorado Springs families. District 11 kicks off its commencement ceremonies on May 19, and the schedule runs through May 21 — with six ceremonies spread across three days at Ed Robson Arena at Colorado College. If you have a graduate in D11 or D49, here's everything you need to know to plan the day.

D11 Graduation Schedule

All D11 graduation ceremonies are held at Ed Robson Arena at Colorado College. Families should plan for parking near the Colorado College campus.

May 19, 2026 Mitchell High School 3:00 PM Ed Robson Arena, Colorado College

May 20, 2026 Doherty High School 3:00 PM Ed Robson Arena, Colorado College

May 28, 2026 Adult and Family Education 7:00 PM Ed Robson Arena, Colorado College

The last day of school for D11 students is May 22, 2026 — the day after the final regular high school graduation. Families with students at multiple grade levels should note that younger students continue through the 22nd while high school graduates walk earlier in the week.

D49 Graduation Schedule

District 49's three high school graduation ceremonies are all held on Saturday, May 23 at Broadmoor World Arena, 3185 Venetucci Blvd, Colorado Springs. Three separate ceremonies run throughout the day, so families attending multiple events may need to plan around both morning and evening timing.

9:00 AM Sand Creek High School Broadmoor World Arena
1:00 PM Falcon High School Broadmoor World Arena
5:00 PM Vista Ridge High School Broadmoor World Arena

Planning Tips for Graduation Day

Colorado College's campus is in the central part of Colorado Springs near Nevada Avenue. Ed Robson Arena parking can fill quickly for morning ceremonies, so families are encouraged to arrive at least 30 to 45 minutes before the ceremony begins. Check d11.org for any specific guest ticket limitations or accessibility accommodations — some ceremonies cap the number of guests per graduate depending on venue capacity.

For D49 families heading to Broadmoor World Arena, note that three ceremonies share one day, which means the parking lot sees heavy turnover throughout the day. Arriving early for the 9:00 AM and 1:00 PM ceremonies is particularly recommended.

After the ceremony, Colorado Springs offers plenty of options for a graduation celebration — from restaurants in the Briargate and downtown areas to outdoor parks for a family photo session with those Rocky Mountain backdrops graduates deserve.

What's Next

With the last day of D11 school on May 22 and D49 high school graduations on May 23, the final two weeks of the school year are arriving fast. Confirm your graduate's specific ceremony time and any guest requirements at d11.org or d49.org. For families with younger students still in school, the last day is May 22 for D11 and May 22 for D49 (with a 3-hour early release, per D49's previously announced calendar). Whether it's a graduation or just the end of a school year, this is a milestone worth planning for. Congratulations to every Colorado Springs family sending a student to the next chapter.
