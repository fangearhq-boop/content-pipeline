### Long-Form Post ``` Colorado Springs' Cheyenne Mountain Zoo turns 100 this year — and it's celebrating in a big way. CMZoo was founded in 1926 by Spencer Penrose, the legendary Colorado Springs entrepreneur who also gave us The Broadmoor and Pikes Peak Highway. A century later, the zoo is the city's most beloved family destination, perched at 6,714 feet with views of Pikes Peak and hundreds of animals. It was named No. 2 in North America by USA TODAY readers in 2025. The centennial's headline milestone: the International Center for the Care and Conservation of Giraffe opens this summer. Families will be able to interact with giraffes from more locations around the habitat than ever before, and the outdoor space expands by 25%. The opening will be celebrated with a weeklong event. If you haven't visited recently, there's also a free exhibit to check out right now: the Penrose Heritage Museum is running a centennial display with archival photographs, a short documentary, and hands-on activities for kids. CPSC Safety Alert — Baby Swings, Bath Seats, Magnet Toys, Furniture Straps [TIER 1]
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ``` Four product recalls to check this week — all involving products commonly found in homes with young children. 1. Vevor Baby Swings (by Sanven Technology): If you have one of these, stop using it now. These swings were marketed as infant sleep products, but the incline angle exceeds 10 degrees — a violation of the Safe Sleep for Babies Act and the federal ban on inclined sleepers. The hazard is suffocation. 2. YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats (sold by BenTalk on Amazon): Nearly 9,000 of these bath seats have been recalled because they can tip over while in use, creating a drowning risk. Sold for $34-$40 in blue, gray, pink, and yellow. 3. CreateOn Pip-Cubes (Crayola-branded magnetic building toys): The magnets in these cubes can come loose if the seams separate. If a child swallows multiple magnets, they can attract each other inside the body, causing intestinal perforation, blood poisoning, or death. This is one of the most serious hazard types for young children. 4. 4our Kiddies Plastic Furniture Tip Restraint Kits: About 253,500 of these furniture straps were sold on Amazon from June 2019 through January 2026, priced between $6 and $21. The plastic components can break or degrade over time, meaning furniture can still tip over even when you think it's secured. There are 150+ reports of the straps breaking, including three incidents where furniture actually tipped. For all of these: stop use immediately. Go to cpsc.gov/Recalls for the specific model numbers and to request a refund or replacement. If you're unsure, the CPSC hotline is 1-800-638-2772. Which of these were you aware of? ``` ### Image Post Caption #### Image Caption -- 8:30 AM MT ``` 4 recalls to check now: baby swings, bath seats, magnet toys, furniture straps. 🚨 ```

AAP Updates Screen Time Guidance for 2026 — Beyond the Clock [TIER 2]
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post ``` If you've ever felt guilty about your kids' screen time, the American Academy of Pediatrics has some news that might reframe things. The AAP updated its screen time guidance for 2026 — and the big shift is this: the focus is no longer primarily on how many minutes your child spends in front of a screen. It's about what they're watching and whether you're watching with them. The new framework centers on quality, context, and conversation. The core age-based guidance remains: no screens for children under 18 months (except video calls with family), and for ages 2-5, one hour of high-quality content daily is still the target. But the emphasis has moved from policing the clock to being present and intentional. Research consistently links excessive, unsupervised screen time to language, literacy, and self-regulation delays — but the same research shows that co-viewing with a caregiver and talking about what kids see largely offsets those risks. In practice, this means: the Saturday morning cartoon marathon isn't the end of the world if you're in the room commenting on it. The educational app they use alone for 45 minutes while you make dinner is worth paying more attention to. Context is everything. The AAP also noted a shift specific to screen content: not all screen time is equal. Video calls with grandparents, interactive educational apps, and quality children's programming are very different from passive consumption of algorithmically generated content. Parents now have a more nuanced framework to work with. How do you handle screen time with your kids? ``` ### Image Post Caption #### Image Caption -- 3:30 PM MT ``` The AAP updated its screen time guidance: it's about quality and context now, not just the clock. ```

### Long-Form Post ``` April in Colorado Springs is a wonderful, confusing, occasionally snowy, usually gorgeous month — and families who plan around it have a great time. Here's what April typically looks like: average high around 59°F, average low around 30°F, up to 10-11 hours of sunshine per day, and an average of 16 days with some snow. That last number always surprises people. The key is that April snow in COS typically doesn't stick for long — a Sunday storm can be followed by shorts weather by Tuesday. The Farmer's Almanac is calling for above-normal temperatures this April, which bodes well. The practical advice for family outings: always bring layers. One extra jacket per kid and waterproof shoes. A blanket in the car. And flexible plans — move picnics indoors if needed, but don't cancel because the forecast looks iffy. Here are a few upcoming COS outdoor events worth putting on your calendar: Bear Creek Nature Center Restoration Crew — April 11, 9am-12pm. Families can join trail maintenance and habitat restoration at Bear Creek Nature Center. Free, and a genuinely fun way to teach kids about stewardship. Backyard Cutting Garden Workshop — April 11, 10am-12:30pm. Hands-on workshop at a local farm covering how to grow your own cutting garden, with a farm tour included. Zoo Run Run at Cheyenne Mountain Zoo — May 9-10, 5pm-8pm. Run or walk the 4-mile road to the Will Rogers Shrine of the Sun, with views of COS, Pikes Peak, and Garden of the Gods. Lions included (behind fences, obviously). Pikes Peak Region Birding Festival — May 14-17. A multi-day festival celebrating local wildlife and habitat. Great for older kids with a nature interest. Spring in Colorado Springs is worth getting outside for. You just have to dress for two different seasons simultaneously.
