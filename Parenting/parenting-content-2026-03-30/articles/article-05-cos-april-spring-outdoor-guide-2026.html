Your COS Family Guide to Spring Outdoors — Events, Weather, and How to Layer Everyone

March 30, 2026 · COS Parenting

Colorado Springs spring weather is a negotiation. One week brings 65-degree afternoons and wildflowers on the trail. The next brings six inches of snow on a Tuesday. Both things happen in April, often in the same week — and families who embrace that variability rather than fight it tend to have the most fun.

Here's what to expect, how to prepare, and what events are coming up for families in the next few weeks.

What April Weather Actually Looks Like

Based on historical data, April in Colorado Springs runs like this:

Weather Factor Average in April
Average high ~59°F
Average low ~30°F
Daily sunshine ~10 hours
Snowy days ~16 days with some snow
Rainy days ~7 days

The good news: the Farmer's Almanac is calling for above-normal temperatures this April across the region. The 10-plus hours of daily sunshine mean that even when a morning starts cold, afternoons are often pleasant. The snowstorms are typically short-lived — front-range spring snow usually melts within a day or two.

The Practical Family Advice

Layer, don't eliminate. Every outdoor activity this month should start with a jacket that goes into the backpack. For kids, that means: base layer that handles sweat, mid-layer for warmth (a fleece or light puffy), and a waterproof outer layer for rain or snow. Waterproof shoes or boots are worth the inconvenience through mid-April.

Build flexibility into your plans. If a Saturday picnic looks dicey, have a backup plan — a board game afternoon at home, a visit to one of the free indoor options around COS (the Penrose Heritage Museum, the Colorado Springs Fine Arts Center at Colorado College). The outdoor event you nearly canceled will probably be beautiful by 2 PM.

What's Next

For the most up-to-date event listings, check coloradosprings.gov/PRCS and visitcos.com regularly — both are updated frequently with family programming. Garden of the Gods Park has free ranger programs starting in the spring; check their calendar directly at gardenofgods.com. The biggest COS family summer event — Cheyenne Mountain Zoo's centennial Giraffe Center opening — is coming later this season, so this spring is the warm-up. Get outside while you can.
