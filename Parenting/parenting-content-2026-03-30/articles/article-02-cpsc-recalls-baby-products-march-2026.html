4 CPSC Recalls to Check Now: Baby Swings, Bath Seats, Magnet Toys, and Furniture Straps

March 30, 2026 · COS Parenting

Four product recalls are in effect that affect families with infants and young children. All of these were sold on Amazon, and all involve serious safety hazards — suffocation, drowning, magnet ingestion, and crushing. If you have any of these products in your home, stop using them now and follow the remedy instructions at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

1. Vevor Baby Swings — Suffocation Hazard

Hazard: Suffocation

These swings, made by Sanven Technology, were marketed as infant sleep products. The problem: they have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees, which violates the federal Safe Sleep for Babies Act and the ban on inclined infant sleepers. When infants sleep at an incline greater than 10 degrees, they can slump forward, restricting their airway and causing suffocation. This is not a theoretical risk — inclined sleepers have been linked to infant deaths, which is why the federal ban exists.

What to do: Stop using the swing immediately, especially as a sleep product. Do not allow your infant to sleep in it at any incline. Visit cpsc.gov/Recalls for model number verification and remedy instructions.

2. YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats — Drowning Hazard

Hazard: Drowning

Nearly 9,000 of these bath seats have been recalled. They were sold on Amazon by BenTalk for $34 and $40, in blue, gray, pink, and yellow. The bath seats can tip over while in use, creating a drowning risk for infants who are placed in them and left unattended, even briefly.

No infant bath product should substitute for active adult supervision in the tub. If you have one of these bath seats, remove it from use immediately. Visit cpsc.gov/Recalls for the full recall notice.

3. CreateOn Pip-Cubes (Crayola-Branded) — Magnet Ingestion Hazard

Hazard: Serious injury or death from magnet ingestion

These Crayola-branded magnetic building cube toys contain magnets that can become loose if the seams separate. Magnet ingestion is one of the most dangerous toy hazards that exists: if a child swallows multiple magnets, they attract each other through the walls of the intestine, causing perforations, twisting, and blockages. This can result in blood poisoning and death.

Stop using these toys immediately and keep them away from children. Do not donate them — they should not end up in another child's hands. Visit cpsc.gov/Recalls for the full notice and to request a remedy.

4. 4our Kiddies Plastic Furniture Tip Restraint Kits — Breaking Strap Hazard

Hazard: Furniture tip-over (crushing/entrapment)

About 253,500 of these furniture straps were sold on Amazon from June 2019 through January 2026, priced between $6 and $21. They're designed to strap bookshelves, dressers, and other furniture to the wall to prevent tip-overs. The problem: the plastic components can break or degrade over time — even when the strap looks intact from the outside. There are over 150 reports of the straps failing, including three cases where furniture actually tipped.

Furniture tip-overs are a leading cause of injury for children ages 1-4. If you have these straps on your furniture and believe your home is secured, check them today. If you have the 4our Kiddies plastic kits, replace them. Metal straps are generally more reliable than plastic for this purpose.

What to Do

For all of these products:

  • Stop use immediately
  • Visit cpsc.gov/Recalls for model number verification and to register for a refund or replacement
  • CPSC hotline: 1-800-638-2772

To stay ahead of future recalls, register your baby and toddler products with manufacturers at purchase — they're required to notify registered owners in the event of a recall.

What's Next

The CPSC issues recalls on an ongoing basis. COS Parenting covers children's product safety recalls regularly — bookmark cpsc.gov/Recalls or sign up for CPSC recall alerts at their website to get notified directly when new recalls are issued. The most dangerous recalls involve infant sleep products (suffocation), bath products (drowning), and magnetic toys (ingestion) — prioritize checking those categories first whenever new alerts are issued.
