Sam's Club Recalls Member's Mark Children's Pajamas for Burn Hazard — Check Your Drawers Now

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 4, 2026

Sam's Club has recalled approximately 18,000 children's pajama sets sold through Samsclub.com over the winter after federal regulators determined the sets fail mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. The recall, announced April 2, 2026 by the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission, affects "Member's Mark Valentine SS Notch Collar" short-sleeve pajama sets in three prints: pink solid, pink hearts, and cream bows.

The sets — button-up tops with a front pocket, sold in sizes XS (4/5) through XL (14/16) — do not meet the federal flammability requirements that all children's sleepwear must satisfy. Garments that fail these standards can ignite more quickly or burn more intensely than required by law, increasing the risk of burn injury.

What the Flammability Standard Means

Federal law requires children's sleepwear in sizes 9 months through 14 to meet specific flammability standards designed to reduce burn injury risk. These standards exist because children are among the most vulnerable in household fires and can contact ignition sources — candles, stoves, fireplaces, space heaters — in the course of a typical night. Garments that fail the standard can ignite more readily or burn faster than legally permitted, reducing the critical seconds that make the difference between a minor incident and a serious injury.

Compliant pajamas must either be made from inherently flame-resistant fabric or be treated with a flame-retardant finish. The Member's Mark sets being recalled do not meet this requirement.

How to Check if You Have the Recalled Pajamas

The recalled sets were sold exclusively online at Samsclub.com — not in-store — between December 2025 and January 2026. If you purchased children's pajamas from Sam's Club online during that window and they are short-sleeve button-up sets in any of the three Valentine-print colorways (pink solid, pink hearts, or cream bows), stop using them immediately.

The Valentine-season marketing timing means these pajamas may have been purchased as holiday gifts or stocking stuffers and are still in regular use in many homes. Check your child's pajama drawer and your Sam's Club order history if you're unsure.

What's Next

Take action now: return the pajamas to your nearest Sam's Club location for a full $14 refund, or cut them in half and email the photo to Supersam@Samsclub.com. The CPSC asks that consumers not donate or resell the recalled sets. For additional details, visit CPSC.gov and search "Sam's Club Member's Mark pajama recall 2026." If you have questions about other children's sleepwear products, check that they are labeled as flame-resistant or snug-fitting — both are indicators of compliant sleepwear design.
