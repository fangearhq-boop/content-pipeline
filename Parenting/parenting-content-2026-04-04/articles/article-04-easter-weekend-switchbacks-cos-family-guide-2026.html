Easter Weekend Family Guide: Switchbacks Home Game, Cheyenne Mountain Resort Egg Hunt, and Spruce Mountain Ranch Brunch

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 4, 2026

Easter weekend in Colorado Springs offers families two full days of distinct options — from a Saturday afternoon soccer match to Sunday morning egg hunts and brunch experiences that mix wildlife, horses, and spring traditions. Here is what is happening this weekend and how to make the most of it.

Saturday, April 4: Switchbacks FC vs. Lexington SC

The Colorado Springs Switchbacks FC host Lexington SC at Weidner Field in downtown Colorado Springs this afternoon. Kickoff is at 3 PM, gates open at 2 PM, and tickets start at $23. The Switchbacks are carrying momentum into this match after advancing in the U.S. Open Cup on Thursday, defeating Spokane Velocity FC in penalty kicks. For families looking for an outdoor afternoon activity before Easter Sunday, a soccer match at Weidner Field is a family-friendly, affordable outing — and the team's recent US Open Cup win gives the home game extra energy.

Weidner Field is downtown-accessible, with family seating sections and concessions. The spring weather in Colorado Springs, while variable, typically supports outdoor sporting events in early April. Bring layers.

Sunday, April 5 — Easter Morning Option 1: Cheyenne Mountain Resort

Cheyenne Mountain Resort is hosting an Easter morning experience that leans into its natural setting. The program includes a live rabbit meet-and-greet, an interactive wildlife presentation, and an egg hunt that takes families through the resort's wooded grounds. Three seating times are available: 10 AM, 12:30 PM, and 3 PM, accommodating families with different morning rhythms and travel plans.

This option is well-suited for families who want a nature-forward Easter experience — the wildlife presentation and outdoor egg hunt provide a different texture than a traditional Easter brunch setup. The resort's setting in the foothills southwest of Colorado Springs provides a scenic backdrop for Easter photos as well.

Sunday, April 5 — Easter Morning Option 2: Spruce Mountain Ranch

Spruce Mountain Ranch hosts an Easter Brunch experience from 10:30 AM to 1 PM that leans into the full Easter pageantry: the Easter Bunny, professional photography, crafts, an egg hunt, horse carriage rides, and live entertainment. This is the more festive option — focused on creating a full Easter morning event rather than a nature walk, and the horse carriage element in particular makes it a memorable experience for young children.

Spruce Mountain Ranch is located in Elbert, approximately 45 minutes from downtown Colorado Springs — a drive that is scenic and worth building into the family's Easter morning schedule if this experience fits your family's style.
