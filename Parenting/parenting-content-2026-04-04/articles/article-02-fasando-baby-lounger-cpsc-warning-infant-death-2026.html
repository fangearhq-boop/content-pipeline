CPSC Issues Urgent Warning on Fasando Baby Loungers After Infant Death — No Recall Issued Yet

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 4, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent safety warning for Fasando brand baby loungers, urging parents to stop using the products immediately. The warning follows the death of a 2-month-old infant who was placed to sleep in a Fasando baby lounger on an adult bed. The CPSC has not issued a formal recall because the Chinese seller has refused to cooperate — which means these products may still be available for purchase and are likely still in circulation in homes and on secondhand markets.

Approximately 6,200 Fasando baby loungers were sold on Amazon between August 2024 and July 2025 for approximately $35. If you or anyone you know purchased a Fasando baby lounger from Amazon during that window, the action required is immediate.

Why There Is No Formal Recall

The distinction between a CPSC "warning" and a formal "recall" matters in this situation. A formal recall requires the cooperation of the manufacturer or seller, who agrees to notify customers and provide remedies (refunds, repairs, or replacements). When a seller is uncooperative — as is the case with Fasando's Chinese manufacturer — the CPSC issues a warning instead, alerting consumers to the hazard and recommending disposal.

The practical consequence for Colorado Springs families is significant: unlike a formal recall, there is no retailer pulling this product from shelves, no automated refund process, and no official outreach to purchasers. The only path to getting these products out of use is for individual parents and caregivers to take action themselves. That includes checking secondhand baby gear — thrift stores, Facebook Marketplace, and community buy-sell groups may have Fasando loungers that are now potentially unsafe to pass along.

Baby Lounger Safety: The Bigger Picture

Infant sleep product safety has been an ongoing concern for the CPSC in recent years. Baby loungers — soft, cushioned products designed to cradle infants — were never intended as sleep products, but they are frequently used as such. The danger is specific: when a baby is placed in a soft, inclined, or cushioned surface for sleep — particularly on an adult bed or elevated surface — the risk of positional suffocation, entrapment, or rolling increases significantly.

The CPSC and the American Academy of Pediatrics both recommend that infants only sleep in firm, flat sleep surfaces specifically designed and certified for infant sleep (cribs, bassinets, or play yards with firm mattresses). Any cushioned lounger, pillow, or soft sleep surface is considered unsafe for infant sleep regardless of how it is marketed.

How to Check Your Amazon Order History

If you are unsure whether you purchased a Fasando lounger, log into your Amazon account and search your order history for "Fasando" or "baby lounger" for the period August 2024 through July 2025. If you find a matching order, follow the CPSC's disposal instructions above. Do not return it to Amazon as a way of passing it forward — destroy it.
