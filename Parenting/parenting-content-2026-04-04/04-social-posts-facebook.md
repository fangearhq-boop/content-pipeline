### STORY 5: AAP Screen Time Policy

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (8:00 PM MT)**

```
If you've been counting iPad minutes and feeling guilty about your kids' screen time, the American Academy of Pediatrics has something to tell you: that approach is officially outdated.

In January 2026, the AAP published a landmark new policy statement in the journal Pediatrics called "Digital Ecosystems, Children, and Adolescents." The headline change: they are moving away from strict hourly screen time limits (the old "no more than 2 hours per day for ages 2 to 5" rule) in favor of a much more nuanced framework.

The new approach is built around the "5 C's":

📱 **Child** — your individual child's needs, temperament, and developmental stage
📺 **Content** — what are they actually watching or doing?
😴 **Calming** — are screens being used as a soothing tool? (The AAP wants families to be thoughtful about this)
⚽ **Crowding out** — is screen time displacing sleep, physical activity, or family connection?
💬 **Communication** — how does your family talk about and navigate media together?

Instead of enforced time limits, the AAP now recommends a Family Media Plan — a household agreement that reflects your family's specific values, routines, and child's needs. Device-free bedrooms and mealtimes are still strongly recommended.

Practically, this means: stop guilt-counting minutes and start asking better questions. Is what they're watching high quality? Is it displacing something important? Are we talking about it as a family?

What does your family's screen time routine look like? Has this policy shift changed how you think about it?
```
