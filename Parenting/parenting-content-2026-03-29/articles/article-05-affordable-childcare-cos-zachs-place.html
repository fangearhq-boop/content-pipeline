Affordable Childcare in Colorado Springs: What Zach's Place Offers Families

By COS Parenting | March 29, 2026

Colorado Springs families who are navigating the childcare search know the reality: full-time daycare in COS is averaging close to $20 per hour in 2026. For a family with one child in full-time care, that can approach $3,000 per month or more. For families with two children, or families living on a single income, the math can make childcare feel out of reach.

Zach's Place, operated by Special Kids Special Families (SKSF), is one of the more meaningful affordable options in the city — and one that many families don't know about.

How the Sliding-Fee Scale Works

Zach's Place at the Laurie Hillyard Family Center uses a sliding-fee-scale model. Rather than a fixed market-rate price, what families pay is calculated based on household income. Families with lower incomes pay less; the fee structure is designed to make care accessible across a broader economic range than standard market-rate providers can offer.

Sliding-fee-scale childcare is not free — but it can make the difference between childcare being a viable option and one that isn't. For families who earn too much for publicly subsidized programs but too little to absorb the full cost of market-rate care — a gap that many Colorado Springs families fall into — this model matters.

Inclusive Classrooms: What That Means in Practice

Zach's Place serves children with and without disabilities in the same inclusive classrooms. This is comparatively rare in the Colorado Springs childcare landscape. Most childcare centers are not equipped or staffed to serve children with significant support needs alongside typical peers.

For families whose children have developmental, physical, or communication differences, inclusive care options can be genuinely hard to find. Zach's Place is specifically designed around this model.

For families of typical children, inclusive classrooms also carry documented developmental benefits. Research on early childhood development consistently shows that children in inclusive settings develop stronger social skills, greater empathy, and broader exposure to different communication styles and ways of navigating the world. It is not a tradeoff — it is a different kind of enrichment.

About Special Kids Special Families

Special Kids Special Families is a Colorado Springs nonprofit that has been supporting children with disabilities and their families for decades. The organization provides a range of services beyond childcare, including therapy supports, family resources, and advocacy. Zach's Place is part of their broader mission to create inclusive community spaces and services for COS families.

The Laurie Hillyard Family Center, which houses Zach's Place, is located in Colorado Springs. For specific hours, enrollment information, current availability, and fee schedule details, visit sksfcolorado.org.

What's Next

The Colorado Springs childcare cost challenge is not going away in 2026. The city has been grappling with childcare affordability and supply for several years — coverage of the issue appeared in prior COS Parenting reporting on federal funding cuts and local childcare zoning changes.

For families who need support navigating childcare costs, the Colorado Office of Early Childhood (oecp.colorado.gov) maintains a list of childcare subsidy programs for income-eligible families, including the Colorado Child Care Assistance Program (CCCAP). Eligibility is based on household income and size. COS families who have not checked eligibility recently — especially if household finances have changed — may find options they didn't previously qualify for.
