March 2026 CPSC Recalls: Three Products COS Families Should Check Now

By COS Parenting | March 29, 2026

Three Consumer Product Safety Commission recalls issued this month cover products that are common in Colorado Springs households with young children: infant sleepwear from HALO, children's robes from Bellabu Bear, and magnetic fidget ball toys from GLMZZ. Here's what parents need to know about each one.

1. HALO Dream Magic Sleepsuits — Choking Hazard (45,000 Units)

The HALO Dream Magic Sleepsuit was recalled on March 5, 2026. Approximately 45,000 units are affected. The zipper head can detach from the sleepsuit, posing a choking hazard to infants. The recalled sleepsuits were sold at Halosleep.com, Amazon, Walmart, and Target from September 2025 through February 2026 for approximately $50.

HALO is one of the most widely trusted infant sleepwear brands on the market — the brand's products are staples in nurseries across the country, which makes this recall particularly significant in terms of reach. If you have a HALO Dream Magic Sleepsuit at home, stop using it and check the CPSC website at cpsc.gov/Recalls for affected model numbers and instructions on how to receive a remedy.

This recall covers the HALO Dream Magic Sleepsuit specifically. Confirm your exact product name and model against the CPSC recall notice before assuming it is or isn't affected.

2. Bellabu Bear Children's Robes — Burn Hazard

Bellabu Bear Children's Robes were also recalled for violating the mandatory flammability standard for children's sleepwear. These robes were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue and various boutique retailers from January 2024 through July 2025, at a price of approximately $60.

This recall is particularly relevant if you purchased children's robes as a gift — items bought at boutiques or specialty retailers are less likely to appear in regular household purchase history, and gift recipients may not receive recall notices automatically. If you have a Bellabu Bear robe at home or know someone who received one as a gift, verify it against the cpsc.gov recall notice.

3. GLMZZ Fidget Magnet Ball Toys — Intestinal Perforation Hazard (STOP USE IMMEDIATELY)

This is the most urgent of the three recalls. GLMZZ Fidget Magnet Ball Toys contain high-powered magnets. If a child swallows two or more magnets — or one magnet and another metal object — the magnets attract each other through intestinal walls. This can cause intestinal perforation, blood poisoning, and death.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission is clear about the action required: stop use immediately. Do not allow children any access to the toys. Contact GLMZZ for a full refund at GLMZZrecall@outlook.com.

High-powered magnet toys and sets have been a recurring safety issue for years. The CPSC has worked to ban or restrict the strongest-grade magnet sets, but products continue to reach the market through online retailers. If you have any magnet ball sets at home — not just GLMZZ brand — check the product strength and keep them away from children under age 14.

How to Stay Current on CPSC Recalls

The CPSC updates its recall database at cpsc.gov/Recalls. You can sign up for email alerts by product category, which sends notifications directly to your inbox when new recalls are announced. For families with young children, setting up alerts for "children's products," "toys," and "juvenile products" covers most relevant recalls.

Registering your baby gear and children's products when you purchase them is also valuable — registered products trigger direct recall notifications to the purchaser. Many families skip registration, which means they rely on media coverage or word of mouth instead of a direct CPSC alert.

What's Next

For the HALO Dream sleepsuit and Bellabu Bear robe recalls, visit cpsc.gov/Recalls for specific model numbers and the remedy process (typically a refund or replacement). For the GLMZZ magnet toys, contact GLMZZrecall@outlook.com. Symptoms of magnet ingestion include abdominal pain, vomiting, and fever — seek emergency care immediately if you suspect your child has swallowed any magnet.

The CPSC issues recalls on a rolling basis throughout the year. Checking the database monthly takes about five minutes and is one of the most practical safety habits parents can build.
