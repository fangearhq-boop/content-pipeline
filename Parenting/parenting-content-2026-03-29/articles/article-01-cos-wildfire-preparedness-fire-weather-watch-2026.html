Fire Weather Watch Monday: What COS Families Need to Do Right Now

By COS Parenting | March 29, 2026

A Fire Weather Watch is in effect for El Paso County on Monday, March 30 — and Colorado Springs families have a clear action item: sign up for Peak Alerts if you haven't already.

The watch covers El Paso County, including the Colorado Springs area and Fort Carson, with west winds forecast at 15-25 mph and gusts to 40 mph. Relative humidity is expected to drop as low as 10%. Those two factors together — high winds and low humidity — are exactly the combination that allows fires to ignite and spread rapidly. All outdoor burning is currently prohibited under City of Colorado Springs Burn Restriction Order 2026-R1.

The "24 Fire" Context

The forecast comes in the aftermath of the "24 Fire," which broke out southwest of Colorado Springs between the city and Penrose, along the Highway 115 corridor. The fire grew to 7,385 acres before fire crews began bringing it under control. As of March 27, it was 86% contained, and pre-evacuation orders that had been in place along County Road F45 and Colorado Highway 115 have since been lifted.

That's good progress — but not the end of fire season. Late March in Colorado Springs, particularly during warm and dry years like 2026, carries ongoing risk. Monday's conditions are a reminder that fire season here does not follow a tidy schedule.

CSFD's "Ready, Set, Go!" Campaign — And Why Peak Alerts Matters

The Colorado Springs Fire Department launched its "Ready, Set, Go!" wildfire preparedness campaign on March 11. At the launch, Mayor Yemi Mobolade cited conditions similar to those that preceded the 2012 Waldo Canyon fire — which destroyed more than 340 homes and remains one of the most destructive wildfires in Colorado history.

The campaign's central message to families is built around three phases:

  • Ready: Sign up for emergency alerts, build go-kits for people and pets, and create and practice an evacuation plan.
  • Set: When elevated fire conditions exist, pack go bags into vehicles and monitor official alerts closely.
  • Go: Evacuate immediately when ordered — don't wait.

The most critical action item for every COS family is registering for Peak Alerts at PeakAlerts.org. Here's what many parents miss: you should register not just your home address, but also your work address and your children's school addresses. Emergency alerts are triggered by geographic zones — if you only have your home address registered, you may not receive an alert about a fire near your child's school during the school day.

Use official channels only. The Colorado Springs Fire Department specifically notes that third-party apps are not reliable sources for official evacuation orders. PeakAlerts.org is the system used by El Paso County emergency management.

Go-Kit Basics for COS Families

If you don't have an emergency go-kit prepared, now is the time. Basic components for a family kit include:

  • Water (one gallon per person per day, minimum three days)
  • Non-perishable food and any prescription medications
  • Important documents (copies of IDs, insurance, medical records) in a waterproof bag
  • Phone chargers and a battery backup
  • Cash in small bills
  • Items specific to your children's needs (formula, diapers, comfort items)
  • Pet food, carriers, and vaccination records for any animals

Keep the kit in an easy-to-grab location — not buried in a closet.

What's Next

Full resources for COS families are available at ColoradoSprings.gov/ReadySetGo. If you have children at D11, D20, or D49 schools, consider checking each school's emergency notification system separately — most districts use their own communication platforms in addition to Peak Alerts.

Monday's fire weather conditions are forecast to ease by Tuesday as winds decrease. However, the combination of record warm temperatures (highs near 80 degrees this weekend versus a seasonal average in the upper 50s) and continued low precipitation means elevated fire risk is likely to persist through April across the Colorado Front Range. Staying registered and staying aware is the right move for the next several weeks.
