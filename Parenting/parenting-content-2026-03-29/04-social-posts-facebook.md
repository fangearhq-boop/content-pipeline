### STORY 3: AAP 2026 Immunization Schedule + CDC Controversy

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

```
Something significant happened in the world of childhood vaccines this month, and it's worth knowing about before your next well-child visit.

The American Academy of Pediatrics released its 2026 Recommended Childhood and Adolescent Immunization Schedule — and for the first time, it's formally separate from the federal CDC schedule. The AAP schedule is endorsed by 12 major medical organizations including the AMA and ACOG.

Key updates in the 2026 AAP schedule: clesrovimab, a new RSV prevention option for infants (with guidance that depends on whether the mother received an RSV vaccine during pregnancy); HPV vaccination recommended starting at ages 9-12; and COVID-19 vaccination universally recommended for children ages 6-23 months.

Why the separation? The CDC announced plans to reclassify childhood vaccines into tiers — "recommended for all," "recommended for high-risk children only," and "shared clinical decision-making" — a significant departure from how the vaccine schedule has always worked. The AAP publicly called this change "dangerous and unnecessary" and said it upends the longstanding expert review process.

For COS families, the most important thing right now is to ask your pediatrician which schedule their practice follows. Most pediatric practices in Colorado follow the AAP's recommendations.

What questions do you have about the 2026 vaccine schedule? Drop them below.

### STORY 5: Affordable Childcare — Zach's Place

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

```
If you're feeling the crunch of COS childcare costs, you're not imagining it.

Full-time daycare in Colorado Springs is averaging close to $20 an hour — one of the higher rates in the state. For families without flexible workplace arrangements or a nearby grandparent, the math can get punishing fast.

One option worth knowing about: Zach's Place at the Laurie Hillyard Family Center, operated by Special Kids Special Families. It uses a sliding-fee-scale model, which means what you pay is based on your household income — not a fixed market rate. It also serves children with and without disabilities in the same inclusive classrooms, which is a rarity in the local childcare landscape.

Inclusive childcare has well-documented developmental benefits for all children involved, not just those with disabilities — stronger social skills, better peer modeling, and broader exposure to different ways of communicating and navigating the world.

If you're looking for more information or want to see if Zach's Place is a fit for your family, visit sksfcolorado.org.
