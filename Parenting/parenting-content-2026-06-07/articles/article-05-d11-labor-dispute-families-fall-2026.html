D11 Labor Dispute: What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know Heading into the 2026–27 School Year

By Sarah Morales | June 7, 2026

If you have children in Colorado Springs School District 11, summer is a good time to catch up on where things stand between the district and its educators. The situation that began in late 2024 remains unresolved as of June 2026, and families planning for the fall school year should understand the context — both for their own back-to-school preparations and so they can make sense of the news as it develops.

What Happened

In December 2024, the D11 Board of Education voted 7-1 to dissolve the master agreement with the Colorado Springs Education Association (CSEA), the union that represents D11 teachers and staff. That master agreement had governed the working relationship between the district and its educators for 56 years. The board's decision to end it was met with immediate and sustained opposition from teachers, the union, students, and many community members.

The dispute escalated in October 2025. More than 1,000 demonstrators — including teachers, students, and community members from across Colorado Springs — gathered outside Palmer High School and other D11 schools for a one-day strike on October 8. Approximately one-third of the teaching staff participated. The strike followed a vote in which 91% of CSEA members approved the action.

In May 2026 — more than 300 days after the master agreement was severed — educators and community supporters picketed again at a D11 Board of Education meeting. The message at the May picket was consistent with the one from October: the situation, from the educators' perspective, remains unresolved.

Where Things Stand Now

As of June 2026, D11 and the CSEA have not reached a new master agreement. The district has made some adjustments to compensation since the original dissolution, but a formal collective bargaining structure — the kind that governed the previous agreement — is not in place.

What this means in practical terms is a degree of ongoing uncertainty for educators working within D11. Teacher morale and retention are concerns worth watching, particularly as the district enters the hiring and planning phase for the 2026-27 school year. Districts that experience prolonged labor disputes sometimes see attrition among experienced teachers and difficulty recruiting, particularly in specialized subjects.

What This Means for D11 Families

For most families, the day-to-day experience of D11 schools may not feel dramatically different from what it was before December 2024. Teachers continue to teach, schools continue to operate, and the district continues to deliver services to students across Colorado Springs.

However, there are longer-term considerations for families to keep in mind:

  • Teacher retention and recruitment: Ongoing labor disputes can affect who chooses to teach in a district. Families with students in specialized programs may want to track staffing updates at their school.
  • Board elections in November 2026: School board elections are on the ballot this fall. The composition of the board will influence how — and whether — the district and educators reach a new agreement.
  • Community engagement: The dispute has drawn sustained engagement from parents and community members. Families who want to weigh in on the direction of the district have multiple opportunities to do so through board meetings and the election.

What's Next

D11 families can follow the situation through two primary sources: d11.org (the district's official website) for school and district announcements, and csea-cos.org for the educator perspective on ongoing negotiations and actions. The November 2026 school board elections are a significant inflection point — information about candidates and the election timeline will be available through the El Paso County Clerk's office and local media as the fall approaches.
