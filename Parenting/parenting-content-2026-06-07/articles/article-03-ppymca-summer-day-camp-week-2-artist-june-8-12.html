If you're looking for a way to keep your kids engaged through the first full week of summer, the Pikes Peak YMCA has something starting Monday. PPYMCA Summer Day Camp Week 2 — themed "Low-Key Famous Artist Week" — runs June 8 through June 12 for children ages 5 through 12.

Week 1, "Vintage Colorado: No WiFi Edition," just wrapped up this past week. Week 2 brings a creative shift, with art-centered activities that tie into the "famous artist" theme. Registration is open at ppymca.org for families who want to sign up for the week ahead.

What "Low-Key Famous Artist Week" Looks Like

The Pikes Peak YMCA structures its Summer Day Camp weeks around different themes, and the "famous artist" concept is designed to mix creativity with structured fun. Campers work on projects, activities, and group experiences inspired by the theme — in this case, exploring what it means to make and share art. For kids who gravitate toward creative outlets, this week is a natural fit. For those who don't identify as "artistic," the low-key framing keeps it accessible and fun rather than competitive.

The PPYMCA has run its Summer Day Camp program for years and it remains one of the more accessible and well-organized options in Colorado Springs for working parents who need reliable, structured summer programming during the school break.

About Financial Assistance

Cost is a real consideration for many families, and the Pikes Peak YMCA's scholarship program can help. Financial assistance is available for families who qualify — the best approach is to ask about scholarship availability when registering at ppymca.org or by contacting the YMCA directly. The YMCA's commitment to serving all families regardless of ability to pay is a core part of what makes the program work in a community as diverse as Colorado Springs.

Why Summer Programming Matters

Research consistently shows that summer learning loss — sometimes called the "summer slide" — is a real phenomenon, particularly for younger children. Structured summer programming, whether academic, creative, or physical, helps kids maintain skills and stay mentally engaged during the weeks away from school. The creative focus of Week 2 at PPYMCA isn't just about keeping kids busy; it's about giving them a constructive outlet and a social environment during a period when both can be in short supply.

For working families in Colorado Springs, the childcare aspect of summer programming is equally important. With the city's ongoing childcare shortage particularly acute in certain areas, having a reliable, quality week of structured care during Week 2 is something worth planning around.

What's Next

PPYMCA Summer Day Camp Week 2 begins Monday, June 8, and runs through Friday, June 12. Register at ppymca.org. Additional camp weeks continue through the summer — check ppymca.org for the full schedule, upcoming themes, and registration deadlines. For information about financial assistance, contact the Pikes Peak YMCA directly through the website.
