If you have a Vevor baby swing at home, stop using it now. The Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled three models of Vevor baby swings sold in 2025 because they pose a deadly suffocation risk to infants.

The recalled swings — models BB501K, BB702A, and BB005K — were sold on Amazon.com and Vevor.com from January 2025 through August 2025 at prices ranging from $65 to $80. Sanven Technology, the company that manufactures and imports the swings, is offering full refunds.

Why These Swings Are Dangerous

The recalled Vevor swings are inclined at an angle greater than 10 degrees, which violates the mandatory federal standard for infant sleep products. They also run afoul of the Safe Sleep for Babies Act, a federal law that bans the manufacture and sale of inclined sleepers.

The hazard is straightforward: inclined surfaces cause an infant's head to tip forward while the baby is in a relaxed or sleeping position. When a head tips forward, it can compress or block the airway. The CPSC has documented that inclined sleepers were linked to at least 94 infant deaths between 2005 and 2019 — the data that ultimately drove Congress to pass the Safe Sleep for Babies Act.

This is why the recall matters. Inclined baby products can look safe during supervised use, but the danger increases significantly when an infant falls asleep in an inclined position unsupervised.

How to Get Your Refund

Sanven Technology is providing a full refund, but the process requires a few steps:

  1. Remove the swing cover from the product.
  2. Cut the cover in half.
  3. Using a permanent marker, write "Recalled" on the base of the swing.
  4. Take a photo showing the destroyed cover and the marked base.
  5. Email that photo to recalling@vevor.com with your purchase information.
  6. Dispose of the remaining swing in the trash — do not donate it or pass it to someone else.

For assistance, contact Sanven Technology at 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM PT. You can also email recalling@vevor.com with questions about the refund process.

Which Models Are Recalled

The three recalled models are identifiable by their color combinations. Model BB501K has a white and gray swing with a white base. Model BB702A has a white and gray swing with a gray base. Model BB005K has a black and gray swing with a black base. Check the underside of your swing for the model number if you're uncertain which version you have.

What's Next

Parents who own the recalled Vevor baby swings should stop using them immediately and begin the refund process. For the full official recall notice and updates, visit cpsc.gov and search "Vevor baby swing." If you are uncertain whether your swing is part of this recall, err on the side of caution and contact the recall hotline at 855-599-6320. Safe sleep resources for infants, including guidance on approved sleep products, are available from the American Academy of Pediatrics at healthychildren.org.
