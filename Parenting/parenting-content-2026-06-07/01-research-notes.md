## STORY 1: Vevor Baby Swing CPSC Recall

### Sources Used
- CPSC.gov: "Sanven Technology Recalls Vevor Baby Swings Due to Risk of Serious Injury from Suffocation Hazard; Violates Mandatory Standard for Infant Sleep Products and Inclined Sleepers Ban"
- motherly.com: "Parents urged to stop using these Vevor baby swings after federal safety recall"
- myparistexas.com: CPSC syndicated recall notice

### Key Facts Verified
- Manufacturer/importer: Sanven Technology
- Brand/product: Vevor baby swings
- Model numbers: BB501K (white/gray, white base), BB702A (white/gray, gray base), BB005K (black/gray, black base)
- Sold at: Vevor.com and Amazon.com
- Sale period: January 2025 through August 2025
- Price: $65 to $80
- Hazard: Incline angle exceeds 10 degrees; violates mandatory standard for Infant Sleep Products AND the Safe Sleep for Babies Act ban on inclined sleepers; suffocation risk
- Remedy: Full refund — remove swing cover, cut in half, write "Recalled" on base with permanent marker, send photo to recalling@vevor.com, then dispose of swing in trash
- Consumer contact: Sanven Technology, 855-599-6320, Monday–Friday, 9 AM–5 PM PT
- Contact email: recalling@vevor.com
- Background: Between 2005 and 2019, inclined sleepers were linked to at least 94 infant deaths (per CPSC)

## STORY 2: COS Parks $3.1 Million Budget Cut

### Sources Used
- KRDO: "What Colorado Springs City budget cuts could mean for your family this summer" (Feb 2026)
- KOAA: "What's cut: Colorado Springs parks face $3.1 million budget reduction"
- KOAA: "Colorado Springs parks face $3.1 million budget reduction; city releases new changes"
- KKTV: "Flanagan Park in central Colorado Springs to see new improvements, new playground, ADA accessible walking path" (May 2026)

### Key Facts Verified
- Total cuts: $3.1 million from parks budget
- Playground maintenance: Budget cut to ~$100,000 (approximately 50% reduction); damaged equipment removed rather than repaired
- Restrooms: 25% of neighborhood park permanent restrooms closed for 2026 season
- Playground surfacing: 50% reduction in repair parts funding; 50% reduction in playground surfacing materials (engineered wood fiber)
- Flanagan Park: $750,000 federal community development grant; improvements include new playground, ADA-accessible walking path with lighting and benches, resurfaced basketball court; located in central Colorado Springs

## STORY 4: COS Childcare Desert

### Sources Used
- KOAA: "A childcare 'desert?' How a shortage of childcare across Colorado Springs impacts the growing east"
- KOAA: "'It's cheaper to go to college,' childcare costs and shortages are making it tough for Colorado parents"
- Colorado Springs Gazette: "Child care groups kick off media campaign about shortage in Colorado Springs" (Jan 2026)
- Colorado Springs Gazette: "GUEST COLUMN: Colorado Springs has a serious lack of childcare problem" (Feb 2026)

### Key Facts Verified
- El Paso County: ~46,800 children under age 5
- Available child care spots: ~17,500 (across all facilities in the county)
- Shortfall: ~29,300 children without available child care spots
- Hardest-hit areas: Eastern Colorado Springs, particularly southeast and Fountain-Fort Carson corridors
- Average annual cost (when care is found): $16,300/year per child
- Impact: Parents leaving workforce because childcare cost exceeds earnings; "cheaper to go to college" framing
- Industry response: Child care advocacy groups launched media campaign in January 2026; guest column in Gazette Feb 2026
- Policy proposals: More childcare funding; easing building renovation requirements to allow new facilities

## STORY 5: D11 Labor Dispute

### Sources Used
- KRDO: "Educators, parents to picket D11 after 300+ days since master contract was severed" (May 6, 2026)
- Pikes Peak Bulletin: "District 11 responds to potential teacher strike"
- Colorado Sun: "Hundreds of Colorado Springs teachers strike over loss of collective bargaining" (Oct 8, 2025)
- NEA: "Despite District Threats, Educators Strike in Colorado Springs"

### Key Facts Verified
- December 2024: D11 Board of Education dissolved the master agreement with educators in a 7-1 vote; agreement was in place for 56 years (not "56-year-old" — it had been in place for 56 years)
- October 8, 2025: One-day strike; 1,000+ demonstrators including teachers, students, community members gathered outside Palmer High School and other D11 schools; about one-third of teaching staff participated; 91% of CSEA teachers voted in favor of the strike
- May 2026: Educators, parents, and community members picketed at the D11 Board of Education meeting; 300+ days had passed since the master agreement was severed
- No formal resolution confirmed as of search results (May 2026)
- Families planning for 2026-27 school year should monitor csea-cos.org and d11.org
