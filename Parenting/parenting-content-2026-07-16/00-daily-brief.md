### STORY 1: TheKiddoSpace Peg Doll Recall — Choking Hazard for Under-3

**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Tier:** 1
**Summary:** CPSC recalled TheKiddoSpace "Peg Dolls in Cups" wooden toy sets — seven rainbow-colored wooden peg figures with a cup and circular wood tray, designed for children under age 3. The peg dolls can block a child's airway and violate the mandatory small parts ban. Sold at TheKiddoSpaceStore.com from July 2023 through February 2025 for about $30. Remedy: stop use immediately, write "RECALLED" on pieces, email photo to info@thekiddospace.com for a full refund.

### STORY 4: AAP Updated Screen Time Guidelines 2026 — What Families Need to Know This Summer

**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Tier:** 2
**Summary:** The American Academy of Pediatrics released updated digital ecosystem guidelines in February 2026, making a significant shift away from strict time limits. The previous 2-hour-per-day guideline (for ages 6+) has been replaced with a focus on quality of content, context, and family conversation. Key guidelines: no screens before 18 months; ages 2–5 may have up to one hour of high-quality content daily; device-free bedrooms and mealtimes recommended for all ages. Screens should not replace sleep, physical activity, family time, or free play. Parental involvement and co-viewing prioritized over hard caps.
**Source:** EdSurge (Feb 5, 2026), FSU News (Feb 2, 2026), AAP Publications
