### STORY 1: TheKiddoSpace Peg Doll Recall

**Long-Form Post (8:30 AM MT)**

If you've got a wooden peg doll set from TheKiddoSpace, it's time to pull it off the shelf.

The CPSC recalled TheKiddoSpace "Peg Dolls in Cups" — a set of seven rainbow-colored wooden peg figures designed for children under 3. The problem: the peg pieces are small enough to block a toddler's airway, and they violate the federal small parts ban. The toys were sold at TheKiddoSpaceStore.com from July 2023 through February 2025 for about $30.

Here's what to do if you have one: stop using it immediately and take it away from your kids. Write "RECALLED" in permanent marker on each piece, take a photo, and email it to info@thekiddospace.com. They'll issue a full refund.

This is exactly the kind of toy that looks completely harmless — a cute, colorful wooden set for little ones. But those small pieces are the problem. If you're not sure whether yours qualifies, check the packaging for "Peg Dolls in Cups" or visit CPSC.gov for the full details.

We know a lot of Colorado Springs families love toys like this for toddler play, so spread the word. Do you do regular checks on your kids' toys against the CPSC recall list?

### STORY 2: Bronc Day Festival July 25

**Long-Form Post (10:30 AM MT)**

If you haven't been to Bronc Day, this is the year to go.

The 88th annual Bronc Day Festival takes over Green Mountain Falls on Saturday, July 25 — and it's completely free. This year's theme is "Let Freedom Ring!" in honor of America's 250th birthday and Colorado's 150th anniversary, and the lineup is packed.

Start the morning with a pancake breakfast from 7:30 to 10 AM at Church in the Wildwood UCC, then catch the parade at 10 AM along Ute Pass Avenue. After that, head to Gazebo Lake Park for the rest of the day: rubber duck races, princess appearances from Fairy Tale Events, a bounce house, Cool Science experiments, Dinosaur Resource Center activities, touch-a-truck, face painting, balloon animals, magic shows, bubble fun, an all-ages coloring station, and a pie contest.

More than 30 vendors will be selling handmade goods, and five food trucks will be on site — Grilled Cheese, City Dogs, Dani's Thai BBQ, Kona Ice, and Pikes Peak Funnel Cake. This year's Grand Marshal is Retired Fire Chief Steve Murphy.

Green Mountain Falls is about 20 minutes up Ute Pass from Colorado Springs — a perfect excuse to get out of town for the day. Free parking in the area, and it's a great outing for all ages.

### STORY 4: AAP Screen Time Guidelines 2026

**Long-Form Post (6:30 PM MT)**

The American Academy of Pediatrics quietly rewrote the screen time rulebook this year — and the biggest change is what's no longer there.

Back in 2016, the AAP recommended no more than two hours of screen time per day for kids ages 6 and up. That guideline is gone. In February 2026, the AAP released a new framework that focuses on quality of content, context, and family conversation — not a hard clock limit.

Here's what the new guidelines actually say: no screens at all for children under 18 months (video calls with family are the exception); ages 2 to 5 can have up to one hour of high-quality programming per day with a parent watching alongside; for older kids, there's no set time limit, but screens shouldn't crowd out sleep, physical activity, family time, or free play.

The AAP now recommends making bedrooms and mealtimes device-free zones for the whole family. Co-viewing — watching or playing together rather than handing kids a device and stepping away — is the new benchmark.

Summer is a natural time to reset routines. As COS families head into the back half of July, it might be a good moment to think about what screen habits you want to carry into the school year.

### STORY 5: Discover COS Photo Contest

The Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services Department just opened submissions for the 2027 Discover COS calendar on July 15. The 2027 edition will highlight the natural beauty of parks, trails, and open spaces across Colorado Springs — and community members of all ages are invited to submit.

If your family has been hitting the trails, park shelters, or open spaces this summer, this is a great way to show off Colorado Springs while potentially landing your shot in the city's official 2027 calendar.
