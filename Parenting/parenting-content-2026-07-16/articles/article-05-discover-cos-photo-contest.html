Your Park Photos Could Make the 2027 Colorado Springs Calendar — Submissions Just Opened

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs families who've been spending time on trails, at parks, and in the city's open spaces this summer now have a reason to dig through their camera rolls. The City of Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services Department opened submissions for the 2027 Discover COS calendar on July 15 — and they're looking for photos from community members of all ages.

What Is the Discover COS Calendar?

The Discover COS calendar is an annual publication produced by the City's Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services Department. Each year's edition highlights the natural beauty of Colorado Springs parks, trails, and open spaces. The 2027 calendar will feature photos submitted by residents — meaning a COS family photo taken at Palmer Park, Garden of the Gods, North Cheyenne Canyon, or any of the city's open spaces could end up in thousands of homes next year.

Submissions opened July 15. There's no fee to enter, and community members of all ages are eligible to submit photos.

More Free July Events: The Coloring Contest

The photo contest is one of several free Park and Recreation Month activities running throughout July. The city is also hosting an all-month coloring contest: participants download a special 2026 Park and Recreation Month coloring sheet, fill it in, and share what they love most about parks and recreation in Colorado Springs. Submissions have a chance to be featured on the city's official social media channels.

This July's theme for National Park and Recreation Month is "The Power Of" — celebrating the role that parks and recreation play in building community.

Why It Matters for Families

Colorado Springs is genuinely one of the more park-rich cities in the country. More than 150 parks, over 140 miles of trails, and open spaces ranging from neighborhood pocket parks to iconic landmarks like Red Rock Canyon. These aren't just amenities — they're a core part of what makes this city a good place to raise kids.

The Discover COS photo contest is one small way for families to connect with that identity. If you've taken a photo this summer that captures something you love about Colorado Springs — a kid on a trail, a mountain view from a park bench, a sunset over Pikes Peak — it's worth submitting.

What's Next

Submit photos through the city's Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services page at coloradosprings.gov/parkandrecmonth. You'll also find details there on the coloring contest and other free Park and Recreation Month activities running through the end of July. For questions, contact the Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services Department directly through the city's website at coloradosprings.gov.
