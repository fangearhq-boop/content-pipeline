CPSC Recalls TheKiddoSpace Peg Dolls: Choking Hazard for Kids Under 3

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

If a set of rainbow-colored wooden peg dolls is sitting in your toddler's toy bin right now, it's time to take a second look. The Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled TheKiddoSpace "Peg Dolls in Cups" — a wooden toy set designed for children under 3 — because the small peg pieces can block a child's airway.

What's Being Recalled

The recalled toys are TheKiddoSpace "Peg Dolls in Cups" sets, consisting of seven rainbow-colored wooden peg figures, a matching cup, and a circular natural wood tray. The packaging displays the TheKiddoSpace logo and the words "Peg Dolls in Cups" on a blue box. The sets were sold at TheKiddoSpaceStore.com from July 2023 through February 2025 for about $30.

The CPSC determined that the peg dolls violate the mandatory small parts ban because they are small enough to block a child's airway, posing a risk of choking and death to children under 3 — the very age group the toy is designed for.

What to Do If You Have One

Stop using the toy immediately and take it away from children. Here's the remedy:

  1. Write "RECALLED" in permanent marker on each peg doll piece.
  2. Take a photo of the marked pieces.
  3. Email the photo to info@thekiddospace.com to request a full refund.

If you have questions, you can also contact TheKiddoSpace directly through their website.

Why Wooden Toddler Toys and Small Parts Are a Persistent Problem

Montessori-style wooden toys have become enormously popular for young children, and for good reason — they're durable, tactile, and visually simple. But that same simplicity can mask a hazard: small wooden figures and pieces designed for sensory and imaginative play often fall into the small-parts-ban category when tested by the CPSC.

Federal law requires that toys designed for children under 3 must pass a small parts test — if any piece can fit entirely into a cylinder that approximates the size of a young child's throat, it's too small. The test exists because children under 3 explore by putting things in their mouths, and objects that can reach the back of the throat can cause serious obstruction.

Peg doll sets are a common type of wooden toy recalled for this reason. If you own other wooden toddler toys — peg puzzles, small stacking sets, wooden figurines — it's worth checking them against current CPSC recall listings at CPSC.gov.

What's Next

If you purchased a TheKiddoSpace Peg Dolls in Cups set, take action now: stop use, mark the pieces, photograph them, and email TheKiddoSpace at info@thekiddospace.com. The full CPSC recall notice is available at CPSC.gov under the 2026 recalls listing.
