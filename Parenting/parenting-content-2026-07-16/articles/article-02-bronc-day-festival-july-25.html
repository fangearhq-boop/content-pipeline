Bronc Day Festival Returns to Green Mountain Falls July 25 — 88 Years and Going Strong

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting

About 20 minutes up Ute Pass from Colorado Springs sits the small mountain community of Green Mountain Falls — and every year in late July, it turns into one of the best free family days in the region. The 88th annual Bronc Day Festival is happening Saturday, July 25, and it's worth the drive.

What Is Bronc Day?

Bronc Day is an annual community celebration that's been happening in Green Mountain Falls for 88 consecutive years. It's a genuine small-town mountain festival — the kind that hasn't been watered down or commercialized. There's no ticket price, no VIP section, and no corporate sponsors changing the vibe. Just a parade, a pancake breakfast, live music, vendors, and a whole lot of family-friendly activities.

This year's theme is "Let Freedom Ring!" — a nod to two significant milestones in 2026: the United States' 250th anniversary and the state of Colorado's 150th anniversary. Grand Marshal for the 2026 festival is Retired Fire Chief Steve Murphy.

The Full Schedule

The day starts early and runs through the afternoon:

  • 7:30–10 AM: Pancake breakfast at Church in the Wildwood UCC
  • 10 AM: Parade along Ute Pass Avenue
  • All day at Gazebo Lake Park: Live music, pie contest, and a full lineup of activities

Activities at Gazebo Lake Park include rubber duck races, Fairy Tale Events princess appearances, a bounce house, Cool Science experiments, Dinosaur Resource Center activities, touch-a-truck, face painting, balloon animals, magic shows, bubble fun, and an all-ages coloring station. More than 30 vendors will be selling handmade goods throughout the park.

Five food trucks will be on site: Grilled Cheese, City Dogs, Dani's Thai BBQ, Kona Ice, and Pikes Peak Funnel Cake.

Getting There

Green Mountain Falls is about 20 miles west of Colorado Springs on U.S. Highway 24. Take Ute Pass toward Woodland Park and watch for Green Mountain Falls Road on the left. Parking in the community can be limited on festival day, so arriving earlier (especially for the pancake breakfast) is a good idea. The pancake breakfast, parade route, and Gazebo Lake Park are all within easy walking distance of each other in the small downtown area.

What's Next

Bronc Day Festival is free and family-friendly — no registration required, just show up. For more information, visit broncdayfestival.org or the Visit Colorado Springs event page. The festival happens rain or shine, so check the weather forecast and dress in layers if you're planning to stay through the afternoon.

With three weekends left before COS schools start back up, Bronc Day on July 25 is a solid choice for a summer memory that doesn't require a budget or a reservation.
