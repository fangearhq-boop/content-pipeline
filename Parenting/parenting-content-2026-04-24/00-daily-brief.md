## Today's Top Stories

### STORY 1: CPSC April 23 Recall Batch — Wiifo Tower Stool + HappyGira Baby Lounger
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Platforms:** X, FB, Article
The CPSC posted two new children's product recalls on April 23, 2026. The Wiifo Children's Tower Stool (model LT005) is being recalled — about 9,700 units sold on Amazon from June 2022 through March 2026 for approximately $60. The stools can collapse or tip over, and a child's torso can fit through openings on the sides, posing entrapment and fall hazards. HappyGira is recalling its Sweetie Baby and Style Life Eleven baby loungers (sold September 2025–February 2026 for $69.99–$99.99 on HappyGira.com). The lounger sides are shorter than required minimum height, there is no stand, and sleeping pad thickness exceeds maximum limits — posing fall, entrapment, and suffocation hazards. COS families who bought either product should stop use immediately and request a full refund.

**Tier:** 2 | **Pillar:** Local Events | **Platforms:** X, FB, Article
The Colorado Springs Parks Department is hosting its 5th annual "3 Days 3 Parks" volunteer event this weekend, April 24–26, 2026. ### STORY 4: Research Roundup — Outdoor Play Makes Kids Sleep Better
**Tier:** 2 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Platforms:** X, FB, Article
A University of Michigan C.S. Mott Children's Hospital national poll found that 1 in 10 toddlers and preschoolers plays outside once a week or less — despite research consistently showing that outdoor play improves children's sleep. Active outdoor time exposes kids to natural light, which helps regulate circadian rhythms and improve nighttime sleep quality and duration. A CNN March 2026 report also highlighted that when kids don't sleep well, parents don't either. COS families are uniquely positioned — with more than 22.5 million visits to parks, trails and open spaces in 2025, access to green space here is exceptional. The research suggests even 30–60 minutes of outdoor play per day can make a meaningful difference.

### STORY 5: Fox Run Regional Park Gets Free Accessible Trail Program — Starting May 1
**Tier:** 2 | **Pillar:** Local Events | **Platforms:** X, FB, Article
El Paso County Parks is launching the Trailability Program at Fox Run Regional Park this spring. A brand-new Terrain Hopper vehicle (funded by a grant from the Colorado Disability Funding Committee) will give families and individuals of all abilities access to guided hikes up to 3 miles through the forested park at 2110 Stella Drive. The program is completely free. Hikes run Saturdays, mornings and afternoons, from May 1 through October 31. Registration is open now at parks.elpasoco.com.
