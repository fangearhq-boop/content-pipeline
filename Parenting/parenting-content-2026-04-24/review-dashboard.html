CPSC April 23 Recall Batch — Wiifo Tower Stool + HappyGira Baby Lounger
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Two product recalls from the CPSC this week that COS parents need on their radar. 🚨 The first: Wiifo Children's Tower Stool, model LT005. About 9,700 of these were sold on Amazon between June 2022 and March 2026 for around $60. The problem — the stool can collapse or tip over while in use, and a child's torso can fit through the openings on the sides. That's an entrapment and fall hazard that the CPSC has classified as serious. If you have one, stop using it immediately and contact the seller for a full refund. The second: HappyGira baby loungers — the Sweetie Baby and Style Life Eleven styles. Sold on HappyGira.com between September 2025 and February 2026 for $69.99–$99.99. The sides are shorter than required by federal safety standards, there's no stand, and the sleeping pad is thicker than allowed — creating fall, entrapment, and suffocation hazards. Again: stop use immediately and reach out to HappyGira for a full refund. Both recalls were officially posted by the CPSC on April 23, 2026. Photos and details are at cpsc.gov. The safest move is to err on the side of caution, even if you haven't noticed any issues. Have you found any recalled products in your home recently? How do you stay on top of baby and kids' product safety news? --- #### Image Caption -- 8:00 AM MT ### Image Post Caption Two new CPSC recalls — Wiifo tower stool and HappyGira baby lounger — posted April 23. Research Roundup — Outdoor Play Makes Kids Sleep Better
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Here's a parenting tip that's both obvious and easy to forget when life gets busy: getting kids outside more helps them sleep better at night. 💤 A national poll by the University of Michigan C.S. Mott Children's Hospital found that 1 in 10 toddlers and preschoolers plays outside just once a week or less. That's a surprisingly low number — especially when research consistently shows that active outdoor time, particularly exposure to natural light, helps regulate children's circadian rhythms and improve sleep quality. The connection is real: outdoor play leads to physical tiredness, natural light cues the body's internal clock, and the reduction in screen time that comes with outdoor activity removes a major sleep disruptor. A CNN report from March 2026 put it plainly — when kids don't sleep, parents don't either. COS is genuinely one of the best cities in the country for accessible outdoor time. More than 22.5 million park visits happened here in 2025. This weekend alone, there are trail cleanups at three parks, accessible hikes launching at Fox Run, and countless trails open across the city. What's your family's go-to outdoor routine when bedtime is struggling? Fox Run Regional Park Gets Free Accessible Trail Program — Starting May 1
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post We don't talk enough about how COS parks serve families of ALL abilities — but this new program deserves a spotlight. 🌲 Starting May 1, 2026, El Paso County Parks is launching the Trailability Program at Fox Run Regional Park (2110 Stella Drive). The program uses a Terrain Hopper — a specialized vehicle secured through a grant from the Colorado Disability Funding Committee — to guide participants of all physical abilities on hikes up to 3 miles through Fox Run's forested trails. The program is completely free. It runs Saturdays, both mornings and afternoons, from May 1 through October 31. Trained volunteers familiar with the Fox Run route will guide each group. This is a real gift for families who've wanted to access natural spaces but faced mobility barriers. Registration is open now at parks.elpasoco.com. If you know a family who would benefit, this is worth sharing.
