Stop Use Now: CPSC Issues Two New Recalls on Tower Stool and Baby Lounger

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting

April 24, 2026

Two new product recalls posted by the Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) on April 23, 2026 are relevant to COS families with young children. A children's tower stool sold widely on Amazon and a baby lounger sold online both pose serious injury risks and need to be taken out of use immediately.

Wiifo Children's Tower Stool

The Wiifo Children's Tower Stool, model LT005, has been recalled due to entrapment and fall hazards. About 9,700 units were sold on Amazon between June 2022 and March 2026 for approximately $60. The recalled stools are part of a batch of three tower stool recalls covering about 12,830 units total — all sold almost exclusively through Amazon.

The hazard: the stool can collapse or tip over while in use, and a child's torso can fit through the openings on the sides. Tower stools are designed to let toddlers and young children reach counter height in kitchens and bathrooms — which means children are at elevation when a failure occurs. The consequences of a collapse can be serious.

HappyGira Baby Loungers

HappyGira is recalling two models of baby lounger — the Sweetie Baby (oval shape) and the Style Life Eleven (rectangular shape, zips into a bag). Both were sold on HappyGira.com between September 2025 and February 2026 for $69.99–$99.99. They violate federal mandatory standards for infant sleep products in multiple ways.

Specifically: the lounger sides are shorter than the required minimum height, the products have no stand (posing a fall hazard on elevated surfaces), and the sleeping pad thickness exceeds the maximum allowed. Together, those design issues create fall, entrapment, and suffocation hazards for infants. The CPSC's position is clear — stop use immediately.

What COS Families Should Do

If you have either product at home, take it out of circulation today. For the Wiifo tower stool, contact the Amazon seller directly. For the HappyGira lounger, the company is asking consumers to cut the cover, foam, and pad in half, photograph the destroyed pieces, and email the photos to obtain a full refund — the email contact is available through their official recall page at HappyGira.com.

For the most current recall information, visit cpsc.gov or call the CPSC hotline at 800-638-2772. You can also sign up for recall email alerts directly through the CPSC website — it's one of the fastest ways to stay informed when these notices drop.

What's Next

The CPSC has been releasing a steady stream of children's product recalls in 2026 — from bath seats and bassinets to swings and now tower stools and loungers. If you haven't checked your home recently against the CPSC database, it's worth 10 minutes to search by product category at cpsc.gov.
