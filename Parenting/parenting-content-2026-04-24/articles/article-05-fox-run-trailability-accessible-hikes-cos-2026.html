Free Accessible Hiking Comes to Fox Run Regional Park — The Trailability Program Launches May 1

By Marta Kim | COS Parenting

April 24, 2026

One of the things that makes Colorado Springs special is the sheer amount of natural space accessible to its residents. But not all of that access has been equal — until now. Starting May 1, 2026, El Paso County Parks is bringing its Trailability Program to Fox Run Regional Park, opening up guided trail hikes to participants of all physical abilities. The program is completely free.

What Is the Trailability Program?

The Trailability Program uses a Terrain Hopper — a specialized off-road vehicle designed for trail access — to guide groups of participants through forested park trails that would otherwise be inaccessible to people with mobility challenges. A trained volunteer familiar with the route accompanies each group. Hikes can extend up to 3 miles through Fox Run's beautiful ponderosa pine forest.

The Fox Run location represents a new expansion of the program into El Paso County's northern corridor. Residents of northern El Paso County — including neighborhoods near Black Forest and Peyton — now have access closer to home. The Terrain Hopper at Fox Run was secured through a grant from the Colorado Disability Funding Committee.

Why This Matters for COS Families

The Trailability Program reflects something important: outdoor access should be for everyone. Colorado Springs parks serve more than 22.5 million visits per year, but those numbers don't always capture the families who would love to use these spaces but can't navigate the terrain. Grandparents, siblings, parents, and family members with physical disabilities have often had to sit out the outdoor adventures that the rest of the family takes for granted.

Having a dedicated, well-equipped program — with a trained volunteer, a Terrain Hopper, and a structured route — removes that barrier in a real and practical way. It's also a model for inclusive outdoor recreation that other parks in the Colorado Springs system could follow.

About Fox Run Regional Park

Fox Run Regional Park is located at 2110 Stella Drive, in the northern part of the Colorado Springs area near the Black Forest corridor. The park features extensive ponderosa pine forest, ponds, and wildlife habitat. It's managed by El Paso County Parks and Recreation and is a popular destination for hiking, dog walking, and bird watching. With the Trailability Program now added, it becomes one of the most accessible natural spaces in the region.

What's Next

The program launches May 1 and runs every Saturday through October 31. Registration opens now at parks.elpasoco.com — search for the Trailability Program. If spots are limited at Fox Run on a given Saturday, the county may add additional dates later in the season. Families interested in volunteering as guides can also find information through El Paso County Parks. This is one of those programs that grows with community support, so spreading the word matters.
