D49 Fills Vacant Board Seat: Jack Ryan Unanimously Appointed to School Board

By Jenna Park | COS Parenting

April 24, 2026

Colorado Springs School District 49 now has a full Board of Education again. During a special meeting on Monday, April 20, 2026, the district's board unanimously appointed Jack Ryan to fill its vacant seat. The vote was 6-0.

Who Is Jack Ryan?

Jack Ryan is a retired U.S. Army Sergeant Major who served in the military before transitioning to a civilian career as a Senior IT Project Manager. He's lived in Peyton — within the D49 boundaries — for five years. Peyton, located east of Colorado Springs, falls within D49's service area, which stretches across the Falcon and Peyton corridors.

Ryan's background is a blend of military leadership and technology project management — two disciplines with a heavy emphasis on organization, execution, and accountability. Board of Education members are not paid employees; they're community volunteers who make binding decisions on curriculum, budget, staffing, facilities, and policy for the district.

Why This Matters for D49 Families

School District 49 has navigated significant challenges over the past year. The district faced a fiscal exigency crisis in 2025 that required difficult staffing decisions, and it has been working to stabilize its budget while maintaining the programming families depend on. Having a complete, functioning board — rather than operating with a vacancy — matters for the district's ability to make timely decisions.

D49 serves thousands of students across eastern El Paso County, including communities in Falcon, Peyton, Calhan, and surrounding areas. Board members are the elected and appointed representatives who set the direction of the district's spending and policy. If you're a D49 parent, knowing who's on that board is the first step to meaningful engagement.

How to Stay Engaged

D49 Board of Education meetings are open to the public. The agenda is typically posted on the district's website at d49.org a few days before each meeting. Parents can attend in person, watch recordings, or submit written comments in many cases. Public comment periods vary but are usually available at regular board meetings. Staying informed is the best way to hold your district accountable and advocate for the resources your students need.

What's Next

With the vacancy filled, D49's board will continue its regular meeting schedule through the end of the 2025-26 school year. Key upcoming decisions for the district may include the finalization of the 2026-27 budget cycle and any ongoing work from the fiscal exigency planning process. D49 families who want to know more about board decisions or upcoming meetings can find current information at d49.org.
