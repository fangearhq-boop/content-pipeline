Give Back This Weekend: 3 Days 3 Parks Returns to Colorado Springs April 24–26

By Keisha Williams | COS Parenting

April 24, 2026

If you've been looking for a family activity that gets everyone off the couch and into the Colorado Springs outdoors while doing something genuinely worthwhile, this weekend is your window. The Colorado Springs Parks Department's 5th annual "3 Days 3 Parks" volunteer event runs from Friday, April 24 through Sunday, April 26, 2026.

Three parks. Three days. Free for all ages. Registration is open at 3days3parks.org.

What Is 3 Days 3 Parks?

The event is Colorado Springs's Earth Day and Arbor Day community response. Each year, volunteers fan out across three different parks over the course of the long weekend to do the kind of hands-on maintenance work that keeps COS green spaces beautiful and healthy. This is the fifth year the Parks Department has organized it — and the scope has grown each time.

The work varies by park and location but typically includes removing invasive plant species, repairing and clearing hiking trails, picking up trash, and reseeding damaged areas. It's physical, outdoor, purposeful, and suitable for families with children old enough to participate in light outdoor work. There are usually tasks appropriate for different ages.

This year's volunteer sites are:

All three are beloved COS parks with extensive trail networks. Blodgett and Ute Valley are both in the northwest part of the city and offer spectacular views of the mountains. Palmer Park sits closer to the center-east corridor. You can sign up for whichever site fits your schedule and location.

Why It's Worth Your Family's Time

Colorado Springs recorded more than 22.5 million visits to parks, trails, and open spaces in 2025. These spaces belong to everyone in this community — and events like 3 Days 3 Parks are part of what keeps them usable and enjoyable year after year. Invasive species in particular are a quiet threat to Colorado's native ecosystems. When families show up to pull them, it makes a measurable difference.

For families with kids, it's also a genuinely good outdoor education moment. There's something different about spending a morning removing invasive plants from a trail you hike regularly — it connects the work to the place in a way that sticks.

What's Next

The event runs all weekend. If Saturday doesn't work, there are slots on Friday and Sunday. Head to 3days3parks.org to see time options and register. Show up in layers — Colorado Springs spring weather is unpredictable. Wear sturdy footwear, bring water, and plan for a couple of hours of outdoor work. The city provides tools and guidance on-site.
