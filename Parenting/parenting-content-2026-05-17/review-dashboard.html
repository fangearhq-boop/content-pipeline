### Long-Form Post D20 families, there's a significant leadership change you should know about. Academy District 20's Board of Education has parted ways with Superintendent Jinger Haberer, effective May 15. Board President Amy Shandy said the board "unanimously determined that we do not have confidence" in her leadership moving forward. The announcement came via email to parents and guardians. No specific reason for the decision was made public. According to the district, the departure follows a contractually allowable hearing process. Haberer had been in the role since March 2023, bringing 35 years of education experience from districts in Washington, Aurora, and Fort Collins. As of this week, she has vacated the superintendent position. The district is reassuring D20 families that schools are operating as usual and the leadership team — assistant superintendents, principals, and teachers — are continuing their work without disruption. A search for a new superintendent is expected to begin soon. This affects thousands of families across north Colorado Springs and the Monument area. AAP's new screen time guidance — quality over hours
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its screen time guidance in 2026 — and the biggest shift is that it's no longer just about the clock. The new framework from the AAP focuses on three questions rather than strict hour limits: 1. Is the content high quality and age-appropriate? 2. Does screen time displace healthy activities (sleep, movement, face-to-face time)? 3. Are parents watching or engaged with kids during screen time? The specifics still matter for younger kids: no screens for children under 18 months (video calls are the exception). Ages 2-5: up to 1 hour of high-quality content daily. But for kids 6 and up, the guidance shifts to quality and context over raw minutes. The AAP is also warning about "digital ecosystems" — apps, platforms, and devices specifically designed to maximize engagement — and their impact on children's attention, sleep, and development. One practical takeaway: the AAP now recommends that families create a Family Media Plan — shared household rules about where screens are used, when, and what for. There are templates at HealthyChildren.org. This isn't about guilt. It's about being thoughtful with one of the most powerful tools in our kids' lives. How does your family handle screen time? We're always curious what actually works in real COS households. ### Image Post Caption The AAP updated screen time guidance in 2026: it's now about quality, context, and what screens displace — not just the clock.
