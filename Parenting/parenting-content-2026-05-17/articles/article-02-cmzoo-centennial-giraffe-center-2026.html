Cheyenne Mountain Zoo Turns 100: New Giraffe Center Opens This Summer

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 17, 2026

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo turns 100 in 2026, and the centennial year is going to be one for Colorado Springs families to remember. CMZoo — founded in 1926 by the legendary local philanthropist Spencer Penrose — is celebrating its century mark with a major new addition: the International Center for the Care and Conservation of Giraffe, opening this summer.

If you've been thinking about making a trip to CMZoo, 2026 is the year to go.

A $40 Million Upgrade for COS's Most Beloved Zoo

The new Giraffe Center is the result of a $40 million investment, funded through zoo revenue and donations, designed to completely rethink how giraffes live in human care. The scale of the project is impressive: a 12,000-square-foot giraffe barn featuring a clear ETFE (Ethylene Tetrafluoroethylene) roof — the largest of its kind covering any animal exhibit in North America. The roof fills the barn interior with natural light throughout the day, creating a dramatically different environment from traditional zoo exhibits.

For families visiting with kids, the numbers that matter most are the feeding zones: the new facility expands feeding opportunities from 3 zones to 11, meaning significantly more chances to get up close with the giraffes. A new feeding tower also encourages the giraffes to navigate toward a hilly outdoor area, making for more dynamic viewing experiences. The outdoor space for the giraffe herd increases by 25 percent overall.

A Weeklong Grand Opening Celebration

When the Giraffe Center officially opens, CMZoo plans to mark the occasion with a weeklong celebration — a fitting way to honor both the new facility and the zoo's centennial year. Details on the opening date and celebration programming will be announced through CMZoo's official channels. If you're planning a summer family trip to Colorado Springs, it's worth timing your visit around the grand opening events.

100 Years of CMZoo

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo has been a Colorado Springs institution since 1926. At an elevation of approximately 6,800 feet, it remains one of the highest-altitude zoos in the United States. For many COS families, it's a multigenerational destination — a place where grandparents, parents, and kids share the same wonder at the same animals across decades.

2026 also marks a leadership transition at the zoo, with CMZoo welcoming a new President and CEO. Centennial year, new leadership, and a landmark new facility — by any measure, it's a significant moment in the zoo's history.
