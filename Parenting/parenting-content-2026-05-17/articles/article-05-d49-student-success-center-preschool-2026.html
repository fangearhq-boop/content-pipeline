D49 Opening New Preschool Seats This Fall at the Student Success Center

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 17, 2026

District 49 families planning for fall have a new early childhood option to know about. D49 is opening a Student Success Center in August 2026, and with it, two brand-new full-time preschool classrooms that significantly expand the district's early childhood education program.

The new preschool spots are specifically designed to serve families who have not previously been able to access D49's existing part-time preschool offerings — a meaningful expansion of access for D49 communities in eastern Colorado Springs and the surrounding area.

What the New Program Offers

The full-time preschool classrooms will operate on a Monday-through-Thursday schedule. That structure is paired with a key partnership: D49 is working with Base49 to offer before- and after-school care, as well as full-day childcare on Fridays. For working parents, this means the new program covers a five-day week rather than leaving a gap on the off day.

Stacey Powell, Director of Early Childhood Education at D49, put it plainly: "The addition of two full-time preschool classrooms, along with our valued Base49 partnership, allows our program to expand access to high-quality early childhood education for families who have not been able to take advantage of our part-day programming."

That framing matters. D49's existing early childhood program has offered part-day options, which are valuable but don't always work for families where both parents work outside the home, or where childcare logistics make part-day enrollment impractical. The new full-time model is a direct response to that barrier.

More Than Just Preschool

The Student Success Center is designed as a hub for multiple D49 programs, not only preschool. When it opens in August, it will also house:

The Elevates program serves students ages 18-21 who receive transition services as part of their special education plans. Relocating Elevates to the SSC means these students receive services within the district itself, rather than through external community agencies — a shift that the district describes as meaningful for continuity of care.

The Motor Team — physical therapists, occupational therapists, and their assistants — will also be based at the SSC, alongside the Assistive Technology Team and the School to Workforce Alliance Program (SWAP) Coordinator, which supports students' transitions from school to employment. A homebound special education teacher will also work from the center.

The result is a facility that serves D49 learners across a significant age span — from preschool through age 21 — with a range of specialized support services under one roof.

Why This Matters for D49 Families

Early childhood education spots in Colorado Springs are competitive. Quality full-time preschool that's district-affiliated — with the curriculum alignment and transparency that comes from a public school program — represents a meaningful option for families who are currently on waitlists or navigating private childcare costs.

The opening of the D49 Student Success Center also signals continued investment by the district in programs that serve students beyond the traditional K-12 model. For families with children who have special education needs, the consolidation of motor, assistive technology, and transition services at one accessible location is practically significant.

What's Next

D49 families interested in the new preschool program should visit d49.org for enrollment information as it becomes available. The Student Success Center is slated to open at the start of the 2026-27 school year in August. Given the limited number of initial spots — two classrooms — enrollment is likely to fill quickly for families who qualify. Reaching out to D49's Early Childhood Education office directly is the best way to get on the list early.
