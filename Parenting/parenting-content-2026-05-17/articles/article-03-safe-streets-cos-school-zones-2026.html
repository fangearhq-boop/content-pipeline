Safe Streets COS: 22 New School Zones and What It Means for Drop-Off This Fall

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 17, 2026

If you do school drop-off or pickup in Colorado Springs, here's some news worth knowing: the city launched a major school safety initiative earlier this month, and 22 new school zones have been added citywide as a result.

"Safe Streets COS," officially launched on May 13, 2026, is Colorado Springs' new citywide effort to reduce traffic crashes and improve safety for pedestrians, cyclists, and drivers — with a particular focus on school zones where kids and families are most at risk.

What Changed This School Year

The Safe Streets COS launch event was held at Mann Middle School in southeast Colorado Springs, highlighting improvements already in place there. Across the city, 22 additional school zones were added before the end of the current school year — improvements that include better signage, safer pedestrian crossings, reduced speed zone markings, and stronger visibility measures around school campus entrances and exits.

Sierra High School is getting flasher lights installed — those flashing warning signs that activate during school hours to alert drivers. They'll be operational at the start of the 2026-27 school year. Panorama Middle School is also in the process of receiving upgrades.

The Bigger Picture

Safe Streets COS builds on the city's Traffic Safety Action Plan, which has a long-term goal of reducing serious injury and deadly crashes by 35% by 2035. The approach combines four elements: data-driven analysis of the most dangerous road segments, infrastructure improvements (signage, crossings, speed zones), enforcement tools, and public education campaigns.

School zones are a natural priority for this kind of work. The combination of distracted parents in a hurry, kids walking unpredictably, and inconsistent driver behavior creates real safety risks around school campuses. Adding formal school zones with visual cues and signage has been shown in other cities to meaningfully reduce near-miss incidents and actual crashes.

What to Watch For This Fall

For parents who do school drop-off, the practical impact will vary by school. If you drive near Mann Middle School, Sierra High School, or Panorama Middle School, you may already see or soon see changes — different signage, new painted crossing zones, or additional flashers. When the 2026-27 school year begins in August, these improvements will be fully operational across the 22 new school zones.

The city has indicated that Safe Streets COS is an ongoing program, not a one-time project. More improvements are expected to roll out across additional schools and corridors in future school years.

The city is expected to continue adding school zone safety improvements through the summer and into the next school year. For families who want to track progress or report a specific school zone that needs attention, the Safe Streets COS program information is available at coloradosprings.gov/SafeStreetsCOS. When August arrives and school pickup routines resume, watching for new markings and signage near your child's school will help you navigate the updated drop-off zones.
