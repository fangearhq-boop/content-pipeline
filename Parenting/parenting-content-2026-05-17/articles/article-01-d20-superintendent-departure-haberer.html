D20 Parts Ways With Superintendent Haberer: What COS Families Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 17, 2026

Academy District 20 families received a significant piece of news this week: the D20 Board of Education has unanimously voted to part ways with Superintendent Jinger Haberer, effective May 15, 2026. The announcement came via email, and while the district is reassuring families that operations continue as normal, the abrupt leadership change has understandably raised questions about what comes next.

Here's what we know — and what we don't yet know — about this development.

What the Board Said

Board President Amy Shandy confirmed that the board "unanimously determined that we do not have confidence" in Haberer's leadership moving forward. The board voted unanimously to begin the process of parting ways, which follows a contractually allowable hearing procedure.

Notably, no specific reason for the decision was made public. The district's communication to families focused on reassurance rather than explanation — an approach that, while understandable from an HR standpoint, leaves many parents with questions.

Haberer officially vacated the superintendent position as of May 15. The search for a replacement is expected to begin soon.

Who Was Superintendent Haberer?

Jinger Haberer was selected for the D20 superintendent role in March 2023. She brought 35 years of experience in public education to the role, including time as superintendent of the Ellensburg School District in Washington, as well as administrative positions in Aurora and Fort Collins. Her tenure in Colorado Springs covered the 2023-24 and 2024-25 school years and most of the current 2025-26 school year.

What This Means for D20 Families

The district has been consistent in its message: schools are operating normally, and the leadership team — including assistant superintendents, principals, and teachers — continues to serve D20 students without disruption. The school year ends soon, which means the most immediate impact for families will be minimal.

Longer term, the superintendent search will be the key thing to watch. The board has not yet announced a timeline or process for finding a replacement, but families can expect updates through the district's official communications at asd20.org.

Academy District 20 serves north Colorado Springs and the Monument area, making it one of the region's largest suburban school districts. Decisions at the superintendent level shape curriculum direction, district culture, and long-term planning — so this is a development worth following as the search unfolds.
