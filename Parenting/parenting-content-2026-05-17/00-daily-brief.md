### STORY 1: D20 Board parts ways with superintendent Jinger Haberer
**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** Local News
**Status:** NEW STORY

Academy District 20's Board of Education unanimously voted to part ways with Superintendent Jinger Haberer on or around May 15, 2026. Board President Amy Shandy said the board "unanimously determined that we do not have confidence" in Haberer's leadership. No public reason given; a contractual hearing process is underway. Haberer had 35 years of experience in education and was selected for the D20 role in March 2023. The district says operations remain stable. A search for a replacement is expected to begin soon. Affects approximately 26,000 D20 students and their families in north Colorado Springs/Monument area.

### STORY 3: Safe Streets COS — school zone safety upgrades citywide
**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** Local News
**Status:** NEW STORY

The City of Colorado Springs officially launched "Safe Streets COS" on May 13, 2026, a citywide initiative to reduce traffic crashes and improve safety on COS roads. The launch highlighted improvements at Mann Middle School in southeast Colorado Springs. By end of school year, 22 additional school zones added across the city. Sierra High School flashers operational for 2026-27 school year. Panorama Middle School improvements underway. ### STORY 4: AAP's new screen time guidance — quality over hours
**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Status:** NEW STORY

The American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) released its updated Digital Ecosystems policy in early 2026, shifting from strict hour limits toward a framework centered on quality, context, and displacement. Key guidance: no screens for children under 18 months (except video calls); ages 2-5: limit to 1 hour of high-quality content daily; ages 6+: quality, context, and whether screens displace healthy activities (sleep, movement, in-person time) matter more than total hours. AAP recommends family media plans with shared rules. The policy also warns about "digital ecosystems" designed for engagement over children's wellbeing.

**Tier:** 2
**Pillar:** Local News
**Status:** NEW STORY

District 49 will open a new Student Success Center at the start of the 2026-27 school year (August 2026), adding two full-time preschool classrooms. The classrooms offer a Monday-Thursday schedule.
