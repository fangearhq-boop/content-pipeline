### STORY 1: D20 superintendent departure

**Long-Form Post**

D20 families, there's a significant leadership change you should know about.

Academy District 20's Board of Education has parted ways with Superintendent Jinger Haberer, effective May 15. Board President Amy Shandy said the board "unanimously determined that we do not have confidence" in her leadership moving forward.

The announcement came via email to parents and guardians. No specific reason for the decision was made public. According to the district, the departure follows a contractually allowable hearing process.

Haberer had been in the role since March 2023, bringing 35 years of education experience from districts in Washington, Aurora, and Fort Collins. As of this week, she has vacated the superintendent position.

The district is reassuring D20 families that schools are operating as usual and the leadership team — assistant superintendents, principals, and teachers — are continuing their work without disruption. A search for a new superintendent is expected to begin soon.

This affects thousands of families across north Colorado Springs and the Monument area. We'll keep you updated as more information becomes available.

**Long-Form Post**

COS families — this is the summer field trip you're going to want to put on the calendar now.

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo turns 100 this year. Founded in 1926 by Spencer Penrose, CMZoo has been a Colorado Springs family institution for a full century. Here's what this new Giraffe Center means for your next zoo visit:

— It's a $40 million facility funded through zoo revenue and donations
— The giraffe barn is 12,000 square feet with a clear ETFE roof — the largest of its kind covering any animal exhibit in North America
— Outdoor space for the giraffes increases by 25%
— Feeding zones expand from 3 to 11 — more chances for kiddos to get up close
— A new feeding tower encourages giraffes to roam a hilly outdoor area

The zoo will hold a weeklong celebration when the new center officially opens. CMZoo is also welcoming a new President and CEO this year, making 2026 a genuinely historic year for one of COS's most beloved family destinations.

We'll share the grand opening date as soon as it's announced. In the meantime, if you haven't been to CMZoo recently, this is the year to go.

### STORY 3: Safe Streets COS

**Long-Form Post**

Good news if you do school drop-off or pickup in Colorado Springs: the city is making school zones significantly safer.

The City of Colorado Springs officially launched the "Safe Streets COS" initiative on May 13, focusing on reducing traffic crashes around schools and throughout the city. The launch event highlighted improvements at Mann Middle School, and by the end of this school year, 22 additional school zones have been added citywide.

What that means practically:

— Better signage, safer crossings, and reduced speed zones near schools
— Sierra High School: new flashers installed, operational at the start of the 2026-27 school year
— Panorama Middle School: improvements underway
— The program combines infrastructure upgrades, enforcement tools, and public education

The long-term goal is to reduce serious injury and deadly crashes by 35% by 2035. That's a meaningful target — and these school-zone improvements are part of getting there.

If you drop off or pick up at one of these schools, you may already be seeing some of the changes. More are coming across the city before the next school year starts.

### STORY 4: AAP screen time guidance

**Long-Form Post**

The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its screen time guidance in 2026 — and the biggest shift is that it's no longer just about the clock.

The new framework from the AAP focuses on three questions rather than strict hour limits:

1. Is the content high quality and age-appropriate?
2. Does screen time displace healthy activities (sleep, movement, face-to-face time)?
3. Are parents watching or engaged with kids during screen time?

The specifics still matter for younger kids: no screens for children under 18 months (video calls are the exception). Ages 2-5: up to 1 hour of high-quality content daily. But for kids 6 and up, the guidance shifts to quality and context over raw minutes.

The AAP is also warning about "digital ecosystems" — apps, platforms, and devices specifically designed to maximize engagement — and their impact on children's attention, sleep, and development.

One practical takeaway: the AAP now recommends that families create a Family Media Plan — shared household rules about where screens are used, when, and what for. There are templates at HealthyChildren.org.

This isn't about guilt. It's about being thoughtful with one of the most powerful tools in our kids' lives.

### STORY 5: D49 new preschool spots

**Long-Form Post**

D49 families planning for fall: there's a new early childhood education option opening in Colorado Springs.

District 49 is opening a Student Success Center in August 2026, at the start of the 2026-27 school year. Inside, they're adding two brand-new full-time preschool classrooms — a significant expansion of D49's early childhood education program.

Here's what the program offers:

— Full-time preschool, Monday through Thursday
— Before- and after-school care through a partnership with Base49
— Friday childcare through Base49 as well

The director of D49's Early Childhood Education program, Stacey Powell, explained the goal directly: "The addition of two full-time preschool classrooms, along with our valued Base49 partnership, allows our program to expand access to high-quality early childhood education for families who have not been able to take advantage of our part-day programming."

The Student Success Center will also house transition services for students ages 18-21, therapy teams, and special education support — making it a comprehensive hub for students who need additional support.

If you're a D49 family looking for full-time preschool for fall 2026, this is worth checking out. Visit d49.org for enrollment information.
