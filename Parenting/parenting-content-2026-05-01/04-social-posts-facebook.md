### STORY 1: CPSC Recalls — Baby Bath Seats, Walkers, Swings

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**

If you've bought baby gear on Amazon recently, it's worth doing a quick check today.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued multiple recalls in 2026 affecting products you may have at home. Here's what to know:

Two brands of baby bath seats — NFSVLB and YCXXKJ (sold by BenTalk) — have been recalled due to drowning and entrapment risks. Both violate the mandatory standard for infant bath seats. If you have either brand, stop using it and contact the seller for a refund.

Vevor Baby Swings, sold by Sanven Technology, are recalled due to a suffocation hazard. The product violates the mandatory standard for infant sleep products AND the ban on inclined sleepers. This is a category that has been regulated for several years — inclined sleep positions above 10 degrees pose a real risk to infants.

Uuoeebb Infant Walkers (sold by BaoD on Amazon) are recalled because they can fit through a standard doorway, fail to stop at the edge of a step, and have leg openings that can allow a child's head to become entrapped. Stop using immediately and request a refund.

Bellabu Bear Children's Robes are recalled due to a burn hazard — the product violates mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear.

You can check all recent recalls at cpsc.gov or call 800-638-2772. It takes two minutes to check your Amazon order history against the recall list — worth it.

### STORY 2: AAP Screen Time Guidelines 2026

#### Long-Form Post -- 5:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM MT)**

The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its screen time guidance this year, and the headline change might actually be a relief for a lot of us.

The old approach focused heavily on strict time limits — two hours a day for older kids, one hour for ages 2-5. The 2026 update shifts to what the AAP calls a quality, context, and conversation approach. The question is no longer just "how long?" but also "what are they watching, and are we watching it with them?"

A few things that didn't change: no screens before 18 months (except video chatting) and children ages 2-5 should still aim for no more than one hour of high-quality content per day.

For older kids, the AAP no longer sets a specific hour limit but recommends that screen time never crowd out adequate sleep (8-12 hours depending on age), physical activity (at least an hour daily), and time for face-to-face family connection. Bedrooms should be screen-free at night for everyone.

The AAP also introduced what it calls the "5 C's" — a framework to help families be more intentional about screen habits without counting minutes.

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

Mother's Day is Sunday, May 10 — and Colorado Springs has some genuinely great options for families this weekend and next.

### STORY 5: Colorado Springs Parks & Trails 2026

#### Long-Form Post -- 12:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (12:00 PM MT)**

Colorado Springs is investing big in outdoor spaces this year, and if your family spends time on the trails, you'll want to know what's coming.

The city's 2026 TOPS (Trails, Open Spaces and Parks) plan includes some of the most significant improvements in recent years.

Blodgett Open Space is getting approximately 14 miles of new trail, along with the Quarry and North trailhead projects that include 90 new parking stalls. If you've ever driven around the lot and couldn't find a spot on a busy weekend, that's about to change.

On the east side, a 210-foot pedestrian bridge over Sand Creek is under construction as part of the Rock Island Trail project. It will connect more of the trail network and includes a pedestrian-activated crossing at Peterson Road. The project is expected to take about 12 months from this spring.

The Pikes Peak Greenway corridor is getting new concrete trail sections and improved ADA access points, replacing aging asphalt that's been a trip hazard for strollers.

The Starsmore Visitor Center at North Cheyenne Canyon — a favorite starting point for families — is getting expanded accessible parking, better walkways, renovated ADA restrooms, and a new outdoor plaza.

All details and project timelines are at coloradosprings.gov/TOPS2026.
