## STORY 1: CPSC Recalls — Baby Bath Seats, Walkers, Swings, Robes

### Verified Facts (HIGH confidence)
- NFSVLB Baby Bath Seats recalled — drowning and entrapment risk, violates mandatory standard for infant bath seats; sold on Amazon by NFSVLB [Source: CPSC.gov 2026]
- YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats recalled — drowning risk; sold on Amazon by BenTalk [Source: CPSC.gov 2026]
- Sanven Technology recalls Vevor Baby Swings — suffocation hazard; violates mandatory standard for infant sleep products AND inclined sleepers ban [Source: CPSC.gov 2026]
- Uuoeebb Infant Walkers recalled — fall and entrapment hazard; fit through standard doorway, fail to stop at step edge, leg openings allow child to slip through until head can become entrapped; sold on Amazon by BaoD [Source: CPSC.gov 2026]
- Bellabu Bear Children's Robes recalled — burn hazard; violates mandatory standards for children's sleepwear [Source: CPSC.gov 2026]
- CPSC contact: cpsc.gov, 800-638-2772 [Source: CPSC.gov]

## STORY 2: AAP 2026 Screen Time Guidelines

### Verified Facts (HIGH confidence)
- AAP updated screen time guidelines in 2026 [Source: EdSurge 2026-02-05 / CHOC]
- Key shift: from strict time limits to quality, context, and conversation framework [Source: EdSurge/CHOC]
- No screens before 18 months [Source: AAP]
- Ages 2-5: up to 1 hour per day of high-quality content [Source: AAP via CHOC]
- No set limit for older children, but must balance with sleep (8-12 hours depending on age), physical activity (1 hour), and screen-free family time [Source: AAP]
- Bedrooms should be screen-free at night [Source: AAP]
- "Model good digital behavior" for children — AAP recommendation [Source: AAP via CHOC]
- The "5 C's" framework — helps parents monitor and limit screen use [Source: AAP]
- The previous AAP guideline (2016) had 2-hour daily limit for ages 6+ [Source: CHOC/EdSurge]

### Verified Facts (LOW — do not use)
- Exact definition of the "5 C's" — not confirmed from search results; do not enumerate without verification
