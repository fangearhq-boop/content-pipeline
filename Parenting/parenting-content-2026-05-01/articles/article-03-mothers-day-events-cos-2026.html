Mother's Day is Sunday, May 10 this year, and Colorado Springs has a full lineup of family-friendly options — from wolf tours to garden parties to the opening day of the downtown farmers market. Whether you're looking for a morning adventure, a relaxed outdoor experience, or something in between, here's what's happening in COS this week and next.

Mother's Day Weekend (May 9-10)

Gather Mountain Blooms Garden Party at Venetucci Farm

Venetucci Farm — a Colorado Springs institution — is hosting the Gather Mountain Blooms Garden Party on both Saturday and Sunday, May 9-10, from 10am to noon. The event includes a charcuterie brunch, a hands-on garden craft, and four flower starts to take home and plant. It's a beautiful and low-key way to spend a morning outdoors, and the farm setting makes it memorable for kids who don't often see where food actually grows.

Colorado Wolf and Wildlife Center Mother's Day Tour

For families with kids ages 6 and up, the Colorado Wolf and Wildlife Center is offering a special Mother's Day wolf tour on Sunday, May 10 from 9-11am. The experience includes a guided wolf encounter, a flower for the mom in the group, and snacks. Tickets are $40 for adults (ages 12 and up) and $20 for children ages 6-11. Reservations are required — book through their website before spots fill.

Lumen8 Rooftop Social

The Lumen8 Rooftop Social is doing a Mother's Day brunch on May 10 from 10am to 2pm. Pricing is $70 for adults, $35 for children ages 6-12, and free for children 5 and under. The rooftop setting offers great views, and the all-ages pricing makes it a genuinely family-friendly option.

Downtown Farmers Market — Opening Day

May 10 also marks opening day for the Downtown Colorado Springs Farmers Market at Acacia Park. The market runs Sundays from 9am to 2pm through October 25 — free to attend and a great new weekly ritual to start. Fresh produce, local vendors, and an easy downtown stroll make this one of the best free family activities in COS from spring through fall.

The Following Weekend (May 14-17)

Garden of the Gods Art Festival

The 3rd Annual Garden of the Gods Art Festival takes place May 16-17 at Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site. The festival features 150 juried fine artists, live music with Pikes Peak in the backdrop, food trucks, and dedicated kids activities. Entry to the grounds is free. This is one of the more visually spectacular settings in COS and a great family day out.

Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival

Running May 14-17, the Pikes Peak Birding and Nature Festival offers more than 30 field trips and programs focused on wildlife and conservation education. Some trips are designed for families and kids. Check their website for the full schedule and age recommendations.

What's Next

For the full list of Colorado Springs spring events and detailed ticket information, visit visitcos.com. Events like the Wolf and Wildlife Center tour require advance reservations — if you're planning to go, don't wait to book. The Downtown Farmers Market continues every Sunday through October, so May 10 is just the beginning of what becomes one of the best weekend traditions in Colorado Springs.
