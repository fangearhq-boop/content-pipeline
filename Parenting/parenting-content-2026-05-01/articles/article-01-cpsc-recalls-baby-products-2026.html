The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued multiple recalls in 2026 affecting baby and children's products that are commonly sold through Amazon. If you have purchased infant or children's gear online in the past year or two, it's worth spending a few minutes checking your order history against the current recall list at cpsc.gov.

Here's what parents need to know about the major 2026 recalls affecting young children.

Baby Bath Seats — Drowning and Entrapment Risk

Two brands of baby bath seats have been recalled due to violations of the mandatory safety standard for infant bath seats. NFSVLB Baby Bath Seats were recalled over risks of drowning and entrapment. YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats (sold on Amazon by BenTalk) were recalled over drowning risk. Both products are unstable and can tip over during use.

Baby bath seats are not designed to be used as a substitute for adult supervision. The CPSC recommends that parents never leave an infant unattended in the bath, even for a moment, regardless of what type of seat or support is used. Recalled products should be removed from use and returned or discarded. Contact the seller through Amazon for a refund.

Baby Swings — Suffocation Hazard

Sanven Technology has recalled Vevor Baby Swings due to a suffocation hazard. The product violates two overlapping safety standards: the mandatory standard for infant sleep products and the federal ban on inclined sleepers. Any infant sleep product with an incline greater than 10 degrees is prohibited because the inclined position can cause an infant's airway to become compressed.

If you have a Vevor Baby Swing, stop using it immediately. Do not use it for naps or overnight sleep. Contact the manufacturer for a refund.

Infant Walkers — Fall and Entrapment Hazard

Uuoeebb Infant Walkers sold by BaoD on Amazon have been recalled because they violate the mandatory safety standard for infant walkers in multiple ways. The walker can fit through a standard doorway, which means it provides no protection from stairs and drop-offs. It also fails to stop at the edge of a step. Most concerning, the leg openings allow a child to slip down to the point where their head can become entrapped.

Infant walkers have been associated with thousands of emergency room visits annually. Pediatricians generally do not recommend them regardless of safety certification. If you have this product, stop use immediately and request a refund from Amazon.

Children's Robes — Burn Hazard

Bellabu Bear has recalled its children's robes because the products violate mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. Children's sleepwear must meet specific flame resistance requirements because young children are more vulnerable to burn injuries and may not react quickly enough to remove clothing that has caught fire. If you have Bellabu Bear robes, stop use and return to the retailer.

What's Next

The easiest way to check all current recalls is to visit cpsc.gov and browse the 2026 recall list, or call the CPSC hotline at 800-638-2772. If you shop frequently on Amazon, go to your order history and search the brand names listed above. When a product is recalled, sellers are required to process returns even outside the normal return window. You can also sign up for CPSC recall alerts by email at cpsc.gov to be notified automatically when new recalls are issued.
