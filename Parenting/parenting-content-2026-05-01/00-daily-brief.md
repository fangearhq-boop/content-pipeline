### STORY 2: AAP Updates Screen Time Guidelines — Less About Limits, More About Quality
**Tier:** 1
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Type:** Study/Research/Guidelines
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

The American Academy of Pediatrics updated its screen time guidance in 2026, shifting from strict time limits to a "quality, context, and conversation" approach. Key changes: no screens before 18 months (same as before), ages 2-5 up to 1 hour per day of high-quality content (vs. previous strict 1-hour cap with no nuance), no set limit for ages 6+ but balance with sleep/activity/family time, and the new "5 C's" framework. Bedrooms should be screen-free at night. The shift acknowledges that what kids watch and who's watching with them matters more than the clock.
