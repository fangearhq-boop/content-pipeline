### STORY 1: Fasando Baby Lounger CPSC Urgent Warning

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM MT
**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**

🚨 If you have a Fasando Baby Lounger, stop using it today.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent warning after a 2-month-old infant died in 2025 following a sleep session in a Fasando Baby Lounger placed on top of an adult bed. The CPSC found that the lounger violates the mandatory standard for Infant Sleep Products — the sides are too low to safely contain an infant, and the foot openings are wider than allowed, creating fall and entrapment hazards.

Approximately 6,200 units were sold on Amazon.com between August 2024 and July 2025 for about $35. The loungers came in grey elephant, baby blue animals, blue animals, gold elephant, pink lion, and yellow elephant patterns. The company, Fasando — operating out of China — has NOT agreed to recall or offer any remedy. That means the CPSC cannot force a refund, but the warning is firm: these products should not be in use.

Here is what to do right now if you have one: stop using it immediately, remove the foam padding from the cover, cut the cover in half, and dispose of both the cover and the foam. Do not donate it, resell it, or pass it on.

The CPSC's official warning is available at cpsc.gov. If you're unsure whether your lounger is the Fasando brand, check the product tag or your Amazon order history from that period.

### STORY 2: ATOYUS Activity Cubes + Sanlebi Pet Vet Recalls

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT
**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

Two toy recalls to check today — both announced April 16, both sold on Amazon, and both a real hazard for young children.

🧸 First: ATOYUS Children's Activity Cubes. These are intended for kids under 3, which makes the hazard especially serious. The xylophone screws and clock hands can detach and become small parts, and the mallets have spherical ends that violate the federal small parts ban. If you have this product, write "recalled" on all sides of the cube, cut the playmat in half, and email a photo to ATOYUSActivityCubeRecall@outlook.com for a refund. The cube was sold by Melofaver US on Amazon.

🐾 Second: Sanlebi Pet Vet Playsets. These playsets include a nail grinder and an otoscope that contain button cell batteries — and young children can easily access them. Button cell batteries are a serious hazard if swallowed, causing rapid internal injuries. To get a refund or replacement, remove the nail grinder and otoscope from the set, take out the batteries, and send a photo to letorecall@outlook.com. The playset was sold by Letokids on Amazon.

Both of these recalls were posted to the CPSC's official site at cpsc.gov on April 16, 2026. If you've purchased either of these products for a child under 6, check your toy box today.

### STORY 4: AAP Research — Neighborhood and Pediatric Crash Risk

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM MT
**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM MT)**

A new blog published in Pediatrics — the American Academy of Pediatrics' journal — is asking a question that's worth sitting with: is your child's neighborhood making them less safe?

The article, "Safe Passage: How Neighborhoods Influence Pediatric Crash Risk," published April 16, 2026, examines the community-level factors that shape how likely children are to be involved in a pedestrian or traffic crash. The research looks at things like walkability, traffic density, lighting, and socioeconomic conditions — and finds that these structural factors create meaningfully different risk environments for children depending on where they live.

This matters because most pedestrian safety messaging is aimed at individual behavior — look both ways, use crosswalks, don't use your phone. That's all still important. But this research suggests that the neighborhood a child grows up in shapes their crash risk in ways that individual behavior alone can't fully address.

For families in Colorado Springs, that's worth thinking about. Not every neighborhood has the same sidewalk quality, crosswalk frequency, or traffic calming infrastructure. The routes kids walk to school, to a friend's house, or to a park are part of their safety picture too.

### STORY 5: D20 Teacher Pay Raises

#### Long-Form Post -- 5:00 PM MT
**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM MT)**

Academy School District 20 has approved pay raises for teachers across the board, according to KOAA.

The raises push D20's minimum starting teacher salary to $52,020, putting the district among the higher starting-pay floors in the Pikes Peak region. For comparison, D11's minimum starting pay is $51,510. The gap isn't enormous, but in a competitive teacher market, even small differences in compensation can influence where teachers choose to take jobs and whether they stay.

D20 serves families in northern Colorado Springs and is one of three major districts — alongside D11 and D49 — that together serve most COS families. Teacher compensation has been a recurring challenge across Colorado as districts navigate budget pressures while trying to retain experienced educators. Approving across-the-board raises signals that D20 is treating recruitment and retention as a priority this budget cycle.

For families in D20, more competitive compensation generally means the district is better positioned to attract qualified candidates and reduce the kind of teacher turnover that disrupts student learning.
