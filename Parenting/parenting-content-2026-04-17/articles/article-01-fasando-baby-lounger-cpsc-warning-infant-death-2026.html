CPSC Urgent Warning: Stop Using Fasando Baby Loungers — Infant Death Reported

By Jenna Park | COS Parenting | April 17, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent warning for parents to stop using Fasando Baby Loungers immediately, following a 2025 infant death linked to the product. The company has refused to recall or offer any remedy — meaning parents who own one need to act on their own, right now.

If you have a Fasando Baby Lounger, stop using it today, remove the foam padding from the cover, cut the cover in half, and dispose of both. Do not donate it, resell it, or store it for later use.

What Happened

In 2025, a 2-month-old infant died after being placed to sleep in a Fasando Baby Lounger that was positioned on top of an adult bed. The infant was found unresponsive. The CPSC determined that the product's design contributed to the hazardous situation — the lounger's sides are too low to contain an infant, and the enclosed foot openings are wider than the mandatory standard allows, creating both fall and entrapment hazards.

Because the lounger had no stand and was placed on an elevated surface, it posed an additional fall risk. The product violates the mandatory federal standard for Infant Sleep Products, which sets specific requirements for side height and opening dimensions precisely to prevent this type of incident.

Who Has This Product

Approximately 6,200 Fasando Baby Loungers were sold on Amazon.com between August 2024 and July 2025 at a price of about $35. The loungers were sold by a Chinese company — Qiyangshiqiguangyuekejiyouxiangongsi, operating under the trade name Fasando. They were available in the following patterns:

  • Grey elephant
  • Baby blue animals
  • Blue animals
  • Gold elephant
  • Pink lion
  • Yellow elephant

If you purchased a baby lounger from Amazon between those dates and it matches any of these descriptions, check your order history to confirm whether it's a Fasando product.

Why There Is No Recall

The CPSC's authority to compel a recall depends, in part, on cooperation from the manufacturer or importer. Fasando has not agreed to a recall or to provide any remedy to consumers. When a company refuses to cooperate, the CPSC can still issue an urgent warning — which it has done here — but it cannot force the company to issue refunds or take back products through standard administrative channels.

This is not the first time the CPSC has encountered a situation like this with an online marketplace seller. The agency has been increasingly active in warning consumers about products sold through third-party Amazon listings where the seller is based overseas and has no U.S. presence obligating them to comply. In these cases, the warning itself is the remedy the CPSC can offer.

What to Do Right Now

The CPSC's instructions are specific. Do not simply set the lounger aside or put it in a closet. The disposal steps are:

  1. Stop using the Fasando Baby Lounger immediately.
  2. Remove the foam padding from inside the cover.
  3. Cut the cover in half.
  4. Dispose of both the foam padding and the cut cover.

These steps are designed to prevent the product from being reused or passed along, since no safe use scenario exists for a product that violates its mandatory safety standard.

If you're unsure whether your baby lounger is the Fasando brand, check the product tag, the original packaging, or your Amazon order history. The CPSC's official warning page at cpsc.gov has full product details and photographs to help you identify the product.

A Note on Infant Sleep Products

Baby loungers are marketed as a place for awake, supervised infant rest — not as a sleep surface. The mandatory standard for Infant Sleep Products exists because infants can quickly move into unsafe positions when they're placed on surfaces that don't properly contain them. This is especially true in the first few months of life, when infants don't have the motor control to reposition themselves.

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that infants sleep on a firm, flat surface on their back, in a crib, bassinet, or play yard that meets current safety standards — and that they not be placed to sleep on adult beds, sofas, chairs, or inclined surfaces. Products designed for supervised awake time should not be used as sleep surfaces, regardless of marketing language.

What's Next

If you have this product, the next step is immediate disposal following the steps above. The CPSC's warning page at cpsc.gov is the authoritative source for product identification and official guidance. If you know someone who may have purchased a Fasando Baby Lounger — a family member, caregiver, or friend — share this information directly. Given the specific sale window and price point, direct word-of-mouth is the most reliable way to reach families who may be affected. Parents with questions can also contact the CPSC at (800) 638-2772 or through cpsc.gov.
