Academy School District 20 Approves Pay Raises Across the Board for Teachers

By Marcus Rivera | COS Parenting | April 17, 2026

Academy School District 20 has approved pay raises for teachers across the board, according to KOAA. The raises push D20's minimum starting teacher salary to $52,020 and signal that the district is prioritizing competitive compensation as a recruitment and retention strategy in the Colorado Springs market.

What Was Approved

The D20 school board approved across-the-board pay raises for teachers in the district. Across-the-board raises apply to all teachers on the salary scale, not just new hires — meaning current teachers at all experience levels see an increase, not just those entering the profession. The district's official salary schedules, published at asd20.org, reflect a minimum starting salary of $52,020 for classroom teachers.

The specific percentage of the raise and the full dollar-amount increase for teachers at various experience levels were not detailed in the reporting reviewed. For families interested in the full breakdown, D20's salary schedule is publicly available on the district's website.

How D20 Compares to Other COS Districts

Colorado Springs is served by three major school districts — D20, D11 (Colorado Springs School District 11), and D49 (Falcon School District 49). Teacher compensation varies across these districts and affects where teachers choose to work and how long they stay.

D20's minimum starting teacher pay of $52,020 sits above D11's minimum starting pay of $51,510. The gap is roughly $510, which is modest but meaningful at the margins of hiring decisions. D49 salary data for the most current comparison period was not available for this article.

D20 serves families primarily in northern Colorado Springs — including neighborhoods around Briargate, Northgate, and the Air Force Academy buffer zone. It's consistently ranked among the higher-performing districts in El Paso County and competes for the same pool of teacher candidates as D11, D49, and districts in the broader Front Range market including Colorado Springs-adjacent communities.

Why Teacher Pay Matters for Families

Teacher compensation connects to family outcomes in a few practical ways. Districts that pay more competitively tend to attract more applicants for open positions, which gives them more selectivity in hiring. They also tend to have lower teacher turnover, which reduces the disruption to students that comes from mid-year vacancies, long-term substitutes, and the loss of institutional knowledge that experienced teachers carry.

Teacher shortages have been a persistent challenge in Colorado, particularly in high-demand subject areas like mathematics, science, special education, and English language development. Raising minimum salaries is one of several tools districts use to address shortfalls, alongside signing bonuses, housing assistance programs, and grow-your-own pipeline partnerships with local universities and community colleges.

The Broader Colorado Education Budget Context

Colorado's school funding formula has historically underfunded districts relative to the national average, a condition sometimes referred to as the "negative factor" or the Budget Stabilization Factor. While the state has made incremental progress in recent years in addressing the funding gap, many districts continue to manage tight budgets while trying to maintain competitive compensation.

D20's decision to approve across-the-board raises in this environment reflects a deliberate prioritization — choosing to invest in teacher compensation even as districts across the state navigate budget pressures. That's a signal that D20 leadership views competitive pay as essential to maintaining the district's academic reputation and teacher retention rates.

What's Next

D20's full salary schedule is publicly available at asd20.org for families and teachers who want to see the complete pay structure across experience levels. The district is one of the larger employers in northern Colorado Springs and its compensation decisions have ripple effects on teacher labor markets across the region. D11 and D49 typically publish updated salary schedules annually — families in those districts can check their respective district websites to compare. For families interested in D20's broader budget priorities, the district holds public board meetings where budget discussions take place and are open to community input.
