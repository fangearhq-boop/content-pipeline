Vevor Baby Swings Recalled for Suffocation Risk: What COS Parents Need to Know About Inclined Sleepers

By Sarah Morales | June 3, 2026 | COS Parenting

Sanven Technology has recalled approximately 1,020 Vevor baby swings due to a suffocation hazard. The swings have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees, violating the mandatory federal safety standard for infant sleep products and the Safe Sleep for Babies Act — the law that banned inclined infant sleepers in 2022 following years of infant deaths linked to similar products. If you have a Vevor baby swing at home, stop using it immediately.

Which Products Are Recalled

Three models are affected, identifiable by color and base color:

  • BB501K — white and gray swing, white base
  • BB702A — white and gray swing, gray base
  • BB005K — black and gray swing, black base

The swings were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon.com from January through August 2025, priced between $65 and $80.

Why Inclined Sleepers Are Dangerous

Infant suffocation in inclined sleep products has been a documented pattern for years. When a baby is placed in a reclined or inclined seat, several things can go wrong: the infant can roll or shift into a face-down position, or the head can tip forward as muscle tone relaxes during sleep. Babies under four months old typically cannot independently lift or reposition their heads if their airway becomes compromised.

In 2022, Congress passed the Safe Sleep for Babies Act specifically to address this category of product. The law bans inclined sleepers with more than a 10-degree angle from being manufactured or sold. The Vevor swings violate this standard — which is why the recall exists.

The broader concern: many families use baby swings as nap stations or overnight sleep aids, particularly when an infant is fussy or has reflux. The CPSC and the American Academy of Pediatrics both recommend that infants sleep on a flat, firm surface on their backs, in a crib, bassinet, or play yard that meets current safety standards — not in swings, bouncers, rockers, or other inclined products.

What to Do Right Now

  1. Stop using the Vevor baby swing immediately — for sleep or any extended rest.
  2. Check the model number against the recalled list (BB501K, BB702A, BB005K).
  3. Call (855) 599-6320 (9 AM–5 PM PT, Monday through Friday) or email recalling@vevor.com for a full refund.
  4. Do not continue using the swing while waiting for the refund process.

Full recall details are available at cpsc.gov under Sanven Technology Vevor Baby Swings.

What's Next

If your family is shopping for an infant sleep solution, the safest options are a flat, firm mattress in a crib, bassinet, or play yard with current JPMA certification. For fussy sleepers or babies with reflux, talk with your pediatrician about safe positioning strategies — Colorado Springs parents can reach out to pediatric practices across the Pikes Peak region for individualized guidance.

For ongoing product recall alerts, sign up at cpsc.gov. You can also follow the CPSC on social media for immediate warnings, including the separate recall of Vevor baby gates also issued in 2026.
