Pikes Peak International Hill Climb 2026: A Family Guide to Fan Fest and Race Day

By Sarah Morales | June 3, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs families have two chances to experience one of the most unique motorsport events in the world — and one of them doesn't require a ticket to a mountain race course. The Broadmoor Pikes Peak International Hill Climb returns in 2026 with both a free downtown Fan Fest and Race Day on the mountain, giving families different ways to participate depending on what works for their kids' ages, energy levels, and schedules.

America's second-oldest motorsport event — also known as "The Race to the Clouds" — sends competitors up a 12.42-mile course with 156 turns, beginning at 9,390 feet and finishing at the 14,115-foot summit. It's a bucket-list Colorado Springs experience, and this year's family-friendly programming makes it more accessible than ever.

Fan Fest: June 19, 5–9 PM, Downtown Colorado Springs

The Hill Climb Fan Fest takes place on Thursday, June 19, from 5 PM to 9 PM in Downtown Colorado Springs. The event spans 10 city blocks and is free — a good entry point for families with younger kids or those who want the energy of the event without the logistical complexity of Race Day.

Fan Fest features driver meet-and-greet opportunities, stunt performances, vendor and sponsor booths, and family entertainment spread across the route. It's a street festival atmosphere with a motorsport core, and the downtown location means it's easily walkable from parking and accessible from most parts of Colorado Springs.

Race Day: June 21, 7:30 AM Green Flag

Race Day is Sunday, June 21. The green flag drops at 7:30 AM, which means an early start for families planning to spectate on the mountain. Arriving early is important — traffic on Pikes Peak Highway builds quickly before the race, and spectator viewing areas have limited space.

A few things Colorado Springs families should know before heading up:

  • Dress in layers. Summit conditions at 14,115 feet can be cold, windy, and dramatically different from what it feels like in the city — even in late June. Bring layers for everyone, including a jacket for kids.
  • Stay hydrated. High elevation means faster dehydration. Bring more water than you think you need.
  • Download the spectator guide. The official PPIHC spectator guide is available at ppihc.org and covers viewing areas, parking, directions, safety guidelines, and what to expect throughout the day.
  • Check the weather. Mountain weather can change quickly. Check conditions for both Colorado Springs and the Pikes Peak summit the morning of the race.

What's Next

Fan Fest is two weeks out and Race Day is 18 days away — close enough to start planning now. If you haven't attended the Pikes Peak Hill Climb before, Fan Fest is a lower-stakes first experience that gives a taste of the event without the early morning commitment and mountain logistics.

For tickets, parking passes, and the full spectator guide, visit ppihc.org. The official site is the most reliable source for updated schedule information leading up to race day.
