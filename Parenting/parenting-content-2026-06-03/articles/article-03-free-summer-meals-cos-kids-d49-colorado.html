Free Summer Meals Are Available Now for COS Kids — No Registration, No Income Requirements

By Jamie Rivera | June 3, 2026 | COS Parenting

Free breakfast and lunch is available right now for all children in Colorado Springs ages one through 18 — no registration, no ID, no proof of income, and no school enrollment required. If your family could use this resource, or if you know a family that could, this is worth sharing.

District 49 is hosting a summer meal program at Springs Ranch Elementary through June 19, open to all kids regardless of which school or district they attend. And beyond the D49 program, Colorado has more than 600 summer meal sites open statewide through the Colorado Department of Education — many within reach of Colorado Springs families.

The D49 Program: What You Need to Know

District 49's summer meal program serves free breakfast and lunch to any child ages one through 18. You don't have to be a D49 student, and no paperwork is needed to participate.

More Sites Across Colorado Springs and Statewide

The D49 program is one option, and it runs through June 19. But the statewide program extends the resource further. Colorado currently has more than 600 free summer meal sites open across the state through the Colorado Department of Education. These sites are open to all children 18 and under — same zero-barrier model: no registration, no income documentation, no enrollment requirement.

To find the nearest free summer meal site to your home, use the Colorado Kids Food Finder at kidsfoodfinder.org. Enter your zip code and the tool shows active sites near you, including current hours and specific locations. The Pikes Peak Library District East Library has also offered free lunch for kids under 18 on weekdays through July 31 as part of their Summer Adventure program.

Why This Program Exists

During the school year, kids who qualify for free and reduced-price school meals have access to those meals every school day. When summer comes, that access disappears — even as family food budgets often stretch thin with activities, childcare, and other summer costs. Summer meal programs close that gap.

Research consistently shows that food security during the summer months supports better health outcomes and better academic readiness in the fall. In Colorado Springs, as in many communities, summer food insecurity is a real and underreported challenge. Programs like this one exist specifically to help — and the "no barriers" approach is intentional, designed to make sure that access doesn't depend on whether a family knows how to navigate complex eligibility paperwork.

What's Next

The D49 program runs through June 19, so there's still time to take advantage of it. If you know of families with young children in Colorado Springs who might not be aware of these programs, passing along the information — or sharing this article — is a practical way to help.
