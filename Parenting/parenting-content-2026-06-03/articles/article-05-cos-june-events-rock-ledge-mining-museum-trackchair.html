Three Colorado Springs Family Events Worth Adding to Your June Calendar

By Jamie Rivera | June 3, 2026 | COS Parenting

Summer in Colorado Springs offers no shortage of ways to spend time with kids — but a few specific events this month stand out for their combination of free or low-cost access, educational value, and the kind of hands-on experiences that actually hold a kid's attention. Here's a look at three worth putting on the calendar.

Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site — Open Through June 14

Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site is open for day visits now through June 14, from 10 AM to 5 PM. Located on the west side of Colorado Springs near Garden of the Gods, the ranch offers a living history experience that brings Colorado's past to life in a way that's tangible for kids of most ages.

Activities include a stop at the American Indian Area, where kids can learn about trade and culture from Colorado's Indigenous peoples, and the Homestead Cabin, where hands-on crafts like making cornhusk dolls give a sense of what pioneer life looked like on the Front Range. The outdoor setting means it's a good combination of movement and learning — not a sit-still experience.

Admission information is available at visitcos.com. The window for this specific programming closes June 14, so this one is worth prioritizing in the first two weeks of June.

Western Museum of Mining and Industry Family Day — June 12

On Saturday, June 12, the Western Museum of Mining and Industry hosts a Family Day with free outdoor activities. The event gives families a hands-on look at Colorado's mining history through working demonstrations and participatory experiences — not just displays behind glass.

Scheduled activities include demonstrations of mining machinery in operation, a working blacksmith demonstration, tractor-pulled hayrides, and Gold Panning Championships where kids can try their hand at the activity that helped shape Colorado Springs and the surrounding region. Free outdoor activities are a draw in themselves, and the combination of sensory experiences — moving machinery, fire, water, soil — tends to land well with school-age kids and curious younger ones.

Check the museum's website for hours, parking, and any additional activities added closer to the date. Free outdoor access on Family Day does not require advance registration.

Trackchair Accessible Adventures — Expanded Summer Program

The City of Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services Department has expanded the Trackchair accessible outdoor adventure program for 2026, adding additional hike times and locations across the city's open space network.

The program offers guided hikes using Trackchairs — all-terrain electric wheelchairs that allow people with mobility limitations to access trails that would otherwise be inaccessible. Two guided hikes are now offered daily, Wednesday through Sunday. Four Colorado Springs open spaces are part of the rotation this season: Ute Valley Park, Red Rock Canyon Open Space, Blodgett Open Space, and Bluestem Prairie Open Space.

Reservations are free and are made through the City's parks department. This is a program worth knowing about for families that include someone with a mobility limitation — whether that's a grandparent, a family member with a disability, or a child who uses a wheelchair. Colorado Springs has some of the most spectacular outdoor terrain in the region, and this program was designed to make more of it accessible to more people.
