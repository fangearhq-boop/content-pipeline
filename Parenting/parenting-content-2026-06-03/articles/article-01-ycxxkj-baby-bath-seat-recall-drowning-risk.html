YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seat Recalled for Drowning Risk: Check Your Amazon Orders Now

By Jamie Rivera | June 3, 2026 | COS Parenting

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 9,000 YCXXKJ baby bath seats sold on Amazon due to a drowning hazard. The seats are unstable and can tip over while in use — in a bathtub, even a few inches of water makes that a life-threatening defect. If you purchased one of these seats between May 2024 and October 2025, stop using it immediately and contact the seller for a full refund.

This is a separate recall from the NFSVLB bath seat recall issued earlier this year. Different brand, different product, different contact information. If you've already dealt with the NFSVLB recall, check again — you may have two different products to address.

How to Identify the Recalled Seat

The YCXXKJ bath seats come in four colors: blue, gray, pink, and yellow. They include two detachable arms that serve as a restraint system, four suction cups on the bottom of the seat, and three bath toys — a duck, a turtle, and a whale. These toys are a distinctive identifying feature.

To confirm whether you have the recalled model: flip the seat over and look for a tracking label on the back. Model number YD-1958 confirms the recalled product.

Why Bath Seat Recalls Matter

Baby bath seats can give parents a false sense of security. A seat that holds a baby upright in the tub may look safe — but bath seats are support devices, not safety devices. They are not designed to prevent drowning if a parent steps away or if the seat fails.

The CPSC escalated the YCXXKJ recall by issuing both a formal recall notice and a separate urgent consumer warning to stop use immediately. That dual-track approach is uncommon and indicates an elevated concern about the severity of the hazard. The CPSC consistently recommends that an adult remain within arm's reach of any infant or young child in water at all times, regardless of what equipment is in use.

What to Do Right Now

  1. Stop using the bath seat today.
  2. Check the back of the seat for model number YD-1958.
  3. Take two photos of the product.
  4. Email both photos to bentalkhere@outlook.com to request a full refund from BenTalk.
  5. Do not donate, resell, or continue using the seat while waiting for the refund process.

The full recall listing — including updated contact information if it changes — is available at cpsc.gov.

What's Next

If you're shopping for bath support products in the future, look for bath seats that meet current ASTM International infant bath seat safety standards and carry certification from the Juvenile Products Manufacturers Association (JPMA). And regardless of the product, the CPSC's guidance remains the same: never leave a baby unsupervised in water, and never rely on a bath seat as a substitute for an adult being within arm's reach.

For product recall alerts delivered directly to your inbox, sign up at cpsc.gov. Colorado Springs families can also monitor the CPSC social accounts for immediate recall warnings.
