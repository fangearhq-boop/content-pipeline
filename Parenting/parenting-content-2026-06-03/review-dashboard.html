YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats Recalled — Drowning Risk (Amazon)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM MT Heads up, COS parents — the CPSC has recalled approximately 9,000 YCXXKJ baby bath seats sold on Amazon, and if you bought one between May 2024 and October 2025, you need to stop using it today. The hazard: the seat is unstable and can tip over while your baby is in it. In bathwater, that's a drowning risk — and it can happen in seconds. Here's what to look for: The recalled seats come in blue, gray, pink, and yellow. They have two detachable arms as a restraint, four suction cups on the bottom, and three bath toys (a duck, a turtle, and a whale). Check the back of the seat for the model number YD-1958 on a tracking label. What to do: Stop using the seat right away. Take two photos of it and email them to bentalkhere@outlook.com to request a full refund from BenTalk, the Amazon seller. You can also find the full recall listing at cpsc.gov. This is different from last week's NFSVLB bath seat recall — different brand, different model, different contact. Please share this with any parents who might have bought baby products on Amazon in the last couple of years. Did you have one of these? --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 8:30 AM MT CPSC RECALL: YCXXKJ baby bath seats — approximately 9,000 units, drowning risk. Model YD-1958 on the back. Vevor Baby Swings Recalled — Suffocation Hazard
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 10:00 AM MT If you have a Vevor baby swing in your home, please stop using it today. Sanven Technology has recalled approximately 1,020 Vevor baby swings due to a suffocation hazard. The swings have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees — which violates the mandatory safety standard for infant sleep products and the Safe Sleep for Babies Act, the federal law that banned inclined infant sleepers back in 2022. The reason this matters: when a baby is placed in an inclined seat, they can roll into a face-down position or slip out of the proper angle. If they can't lift their head independently, their airway can become blocked — and that can happen even when a parent is in the next room. The recalled swings were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon.com from January through August 2025 for between $65 and $80. Three models are affected: - BB501K (white/gray swing, white base) - BB702A (white/gray swing, gray base) - BB005K (black/gray swing, black base) What to do: Stop use immediately. Call (855) 599-6320 (9 AM–5 PM PT, Monday–Friday) or email recalling@vevor.com for a full refund. Full details at cpsc.gov. Have you been using a swing for baby sleep or rest? This is worth checking. --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 10:30 AM MT RECALL: Vevor baby swings — approximately 1,020 units, suffocation risk. Models BB501K, BB702A, BB005K. Sold on Vevor.com and Amazon, January–August 2025. Stop use now. Email recalling@vevor.com for a full refund.

NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 3:00 PM MT The Pikes Peak International Hill Climb is one of those Colorado Springs experiences that feels genuinely unlike anything else — and this year, families have two ways to be part of it. First, the Fan Fest: on Thursday, June 19, from 5 PM to 9 PM, the Hill Climb takes over 10 city blocks of Downtown Colorado Springs. There'll be driver meet-and-greets, stunt performances, vendor and sponsor booths, and family entertainment across the whole route. It's free, it's central, and it's a great way to build excitement with kids before race day. Then there's the race itself: Sunday, June 21. Green flag drops at 7:30 AM. The Broadmoor Pikes Peak International Hill Climb — America's second-oldest motorsport event — runs 12.42 miles up Pikes Peak Highway with 156 turns, starting at 9,390 feet and finishing at the 14,115-foot summit. A few practical notes for families: dress in layers (summit conditions are cold even in June), plan to arrive early, and check the spectator guide at ppihc.org before you go. The guide covers viewing areas, parking, directions, and what to expect on race day. Is your family planning to go? COS June Family Events: Rock Ledge Ranch, Mining Museum Day, Trackchair Trails
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 5:00 PM MT Planning ahead for the rest of June? Here are three Colorado Springs picks worth knowing about. Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site is open June 3 through 14, from 10 AM to 5 PM. It's a living history experience on the west side of town — kids can learn about Native American trade at the American Indian Area, try activities at the Homestead Cabin, and get a feel for Colorado history in a way that sticks. Check visitcos.com for current admission info. On June 12, the Western Museum of Mining and Industry hosts a Family Day featuring free outdoor activities — think mining machinery in motion, a working blacksmith, tractor-pulled hayrides, and Gold Panning Championships. It's a genuinely fun way to spend a Saturday morning with curious kids. And if your family includes anyone who uses a mobility device, the City's Trackchair accessible adventure program is worth knowing about. The program returns this year with two guided hikes daily, Wednesday through Sunday, at four Colorado Springs open spaces: Ute Valley Park, Red Rock Canyon, Blodgett Open Space, and Bluestem Prairie. Reservations are free and available through the City's parks department. What's on your June family calendar? --- ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 5:30 PM MT Three Colorado Springs family events worth knowing about this month: Rock Ledge Ranch open June 3–14 — Mining Museum Family Day June 12 (free outdoor activities) — Trackchair accessible hikes, free, Wed–Sun at 4 COS parks.
