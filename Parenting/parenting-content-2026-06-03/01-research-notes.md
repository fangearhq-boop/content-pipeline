## STORY 2: Vevor Baby Swing Recall

**Sources:**
- CPSC.gov: "Sanven Technology Recalls Vevor Baby Swings Due to Risk of Serious Injury from Suffocation Hazard; Violates Mandatory Standard for Infant Sleep Products and Inclined Sleepers Ban" (cpsc.gov/Recalls/2026/Sanven-Technology-Recalls-Vevor...)
- mother.ly: "Parents urged to stop using these Vevor baby swings after federal safety recall"
- WFMJ: "Baby swing recall due to risk of suffocation"
- Yahoo News / MyParisTexas (syndicated CPSC content)
- ConsumerAffairs (Safety Recall Roundup)

**Confirmed Facts (HIGH confidence):**
- Units recalled: approximately 1,020 [CPSC.gov]
- Recalled by: Sanven Technology (manufacturer) [CPSC.gov]
- Brand name: Vevor [CPSC.gov]
- Model numbers and colors: BB501K (white/gray, white base), BB702A (white/gray, gray base), BB005K (black/gray, black base) [CPSC.gov]
- Sold on: Vevor.com and Amazon.com [CPSC.gov]
- Sale period: January 2025 through August 2025 [CPSC.gov]
- Price: $65–$80 [CPSC.gov]
- Hazard: incline angle greater than 10 degrees → violates mandatory standard for infant sleep products + Safe Sleep for Babies Act ban on inclined sleepers → suffocation risk [CPSC.gov]
- Why dangerous: infant can roll into face-down position or reposition, unable to lift head → airway blocked [mother.ly + CPSC.gov]
- Remedy: Stop use immediately. Call (855) 599-6320 (9 AM–5 PM PT, Mon–Fri) or email recalling@vevor.com for full refund [CPSC.gov]
- Also recalled: Sanven Technology Recalls Vevor Baby Gates (separate recall, same manufacturer) [CPSC second page]
