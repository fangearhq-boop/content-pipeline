Free Concert at Piñon Valley Park This Thursday: SofaKillers, Food Trucks, and Kickball

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting

July 21, 2026 — Colorado Springs, Colorado

Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services is hosting a free outdoor concert at Piñon Valley Park this Thursday, July 24, as part of National Park and Recreation Month. The event runs from 6:30 to 8:30 PM at the park, located at 5585 Mule Deer Dr on the northeast side of Colorado Springs.

The band performing is the SofaKillers. Admission is free. No tickets or registration are required — you show up, find a spot on the grass, and enjoy the evening.

What's Happening at the Event

Beyond the concert itself, the evening includes food trucks on site, youth kickball, and giveaways. The city recommends bringing lawn chairs and blankets to stake out a comfortable spot on the grass. Kids who don't want to sit still for two hours of music will have other things to do while the adults settle in.

The event is part of the city's Music in the Park series, which has run throughout July as part of the Park and Recreation Month programming. The city has offered free events at different parks throughout the month — Thursday's Piñon Valley show is one of the final concerts in the July lineup.

About Park and Recreation Month

July is National Park and Recreation Month, and Colorado Springs has used the occasion to program free events at parks throughout the city. The city's Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department coordinates the Music in the Park series and other free programming that makes it easy for families to use public spaces during the summer months without a cost barrier.

Piñon Valley Park sits on the northeast side of Colorado Springs and is a well-used neighborhood park with open grass areas well-suited to an outdoor concert setup.

What's Next

Thursday evening looks clear and warm, which is ideal for an outdoor concert. Arrive early if you want good positioning on the grass — these Music in the Park events tend to draw a crowd by showtime. Parking is available around the park, and street parking in the neighborhood is typically accessible for events like this.

Check coloradosprings.gov for any last-minute weather updates or event changes. The city's Parks and Recreation page also lists other upcoming summer programming if you're looking for more free options before the season ends.
