10 Days Left: PPLD Summer Adventure Ends July 31 — Grand Prize Is a Night at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

July 21, 2026 — Colorado Springs, Colorado

The Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program ends on July 31. That's 10 days from today. If your family has been participating all summer, you're in the home stretch. If you haven't started yet — or if the kids lost steam in week three — there's still time to log enough to get in the prize drawings.

How the Program Works

Summer Adventure is free for all ages — children, teens, and adults. The structure is straightforward: complete 30 days of reading, exploring, or creating, and you earn entries into prize drawings. There's no single book count or page minimum. The program is designed to keep engagement broad enough that kids and families can participate in ways that fit their summer.

You log your reading and activities at ppld.beanstack.org or through the PPLD app. All logging is done online. If you signed up at the beginning of summer and fell off, you can log retroactively for days you actually read or did activities — check the Beanstack platform for how your specific challenge is structured.

What You Can Win

Prizes are available across age groups, with entries from all participants going into the grand prize drawing. This year's grand prizes include a Pikes Peak Region Attractions VIP Access Pass — which covers free and discounted admission to attractions throughout the Pikes Peak area — and a one-night stay for two at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp.

Beyond the grand prize, there are additional prize drawings with family-friendly experiences, memberships, passes, and gift cards from local COS businesses and attractions. The more days logged, the more entries.

Why the Deadline Matters

July 31 is a hard cutoff. Activities logged after that date won't count toward the program. PPLD also runs special programs and events throughout the summer that tie into Summer Adventure — some of those wrap up before the 31st. If there's a specific event your family has been eyeing, check ppld.org/summer to confirm it's still open.

The library branches themselves are open and stocked with summer reading picks if you need a last-minute book run before the deadline. The PPLD system includes 14 locations across El Paso County.

What's Next

Go to ppld.org/summer to check your progress, log today's reading, or sign up if you haven't yet. If your kids have been reading all summer and just haven't been logging — get in there and enter those days before July 31. The Cloud Camp grand prize is genuinely memorable, and the barrier to entry is just 30 days of doing things your family was probably already doing.

PPLD also programs fall reading events and programs starting in August — check ppld.org for what's coming after Summer Adventure wraps.
