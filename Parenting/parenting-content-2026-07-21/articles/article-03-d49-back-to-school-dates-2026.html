D49 Back-to-School 2026: Key Dates for Falcon School District Families

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting

July 21, 2026 — Colorado Springs, Colorado

Falcon School District 49 is entering its back-to-school sequence this week. New teacher orientation begins Thursday, July 24, and from that point forward the timeline moves quickly toward the first day of school in August. Here's what D49 families need to know.

D49 Back-to-School Dates at a Glance

Date Event
July 24–27 New teacher orientation
July 28 All D49 teachers return
July 30 Professional development day
August 3 First day of school — kindergartners, 6th graders, 9th graders
August 4 First day of school — all students

The staggered first day for kindergartners, 6th graders, and 9th graders on August 3 is intentional — it gives students starting new buildings a chance to navigate their first day before the full school population arrives the following morning.

What to Do in the Next Two Weeks

With the first day of school for most students landing on August 4, families have roughly two weeks to get everything in order. That's enough time to handle supply shopping, schedule adjustments, and back-to-school health appointments — but it goes fast.

Individual D49 schools typically publish their own supply lists, meet-the-teacher events, and open house schedules. If you haven't checked your school's specific calendar yet, the full D49 district calendar is available at d49.org/discover-d49/district-calendar. Supply lists vary by grade and school and are often posted on individual school websites under "Back to School" or "School Info."

For families enrolling new students, the D49 enrollment center handles registration and can connect families with the appropriate school based on attendance boundaries. D49 serves northeast Colorado Springs, Falcon, and the Peyton area.

Before- and After-School Care

Before- and after-school care arrangements are one of the things that tends to get left until the last minute. With the first day of school just under two weeks away, if you haven't confirmed care for August, now is the time. D49 schools partner with different providers, and spots in school-based programs can fill quickly in the final weeks before school starts. Contact your specific school's front office if you're not sure what's available at your building.

What's Next

July 28 is when the full D49 staff returns, which often means schools schedule open houses and meet-the-teacher events in the days that follow. Watch for communication from your school in the next week. If you've had a change in address, contact information, or emergency contacts, get those updates into the school's system before August 3 — first-day logistics are smoother when records are current.

For anything district-level, d49.org is the authoritative source. School-specific details come from individual school websites or the automated messages that schools send to enrolled families.
