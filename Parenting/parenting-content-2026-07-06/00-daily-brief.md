### STORY 1: Recall — CreateOn Pip-Cubes (Crayola-Branded) Magnets Can Kill If Swallowed
- **Tier:** 1
- **Type:** NEW STORY
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Source(s):** cpsc.gov, abcnews.com/GMA, foodpoisoningbulletin.com
- **Summary:** CreateOn has recalled Crayola-branded Pip-Cubes magnetic building blocks (Bold Colors and Glitter; 24-piece sets sold at Michaels and Michaels.com for ~$20; 27-piece sets on Amazon for ~$35; sold May–July 2025). The seams can separate and release high-powered magnets. If a child swallows two or more magnets, they can attract each other inside the body, causing intestinal perforation, blockage, blood poisoning, and death. No injuries reported. Remedy: stop use, return recalled cubes via prepaid label, receive a free replacement set. Contact: 800-333-0549, Mon–Fri 9:30 AM–4 PM CT; pipcuberecall@CreateOn.com; createon.com/recall.

### STORY 2: Recall — Zippee Silicone Activity Toys Pose Serious Choking Risk (117,500 Units)
- **Tier:** 1
- **Type:** NEW STORY
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Source(s):** cpsc.gov, mother.ly, wpri.com
- **Summary:** Mobi Games has recalled approximately 117,500 Zippee silicone cylinder activity toys (teal, with six pull-string loops and spherical ball ends) due to a serious choking hazard. The spherical ends can reach the back of a child's throat. One incident reported: a child gagged and vomited. Sold on Amazon, PlayMobi.com, and specialty toy stores November 2019–January 2024, approximately $20. Check the date code inside the cylinder (MMDDYY format, ranging 030620 to 110823). Remedy: stop use immediately, contact for full refund via playmobi.com/pages/product-recall.
