### STORY 1: Recall — CreateOn Pip-Cubes (Crayola-Branded) Magnets Can Kill If Swallowed

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM MT

```
⚠️ If your kids have Crayola-branded Pip-Cubes building blocks, stop use now — they've been recalled.

CreateOn has recalled Pip-Cubes magnetic building blocks in the Bold Colors and Glitter varieties. The seams on these sets can split and release the high-powered magnets inside. Here's why that's serious: if a child swallows two or more magnets, they can attract each other through intestinal walls, causing perforation, blockage, blood poisoning, or worse. The CPSC considers this a risk of serious injury or death.

The recalled sets are 24-piece sets sold at Michaels and Michaels.com (around $20) and 27-piece sets sold on Amazon (around $35), all from May through July 2025. No injuries have been reported, and the fix is simple — stop using the set, mail it back with a prepaid label, and you'll get a free replacement.

To start your return, visit createon.com/recall or call 800-333-0549, Monday through Friday, 9:30 AM to 4 PM CT. You can also reach them at pipcuberecall@CreateOn.com.

Is this one in your playroom? Tag a friend who might need to check. 🧲
```

### STORY 2: Recall — Zippee Silicone Activity Toys Pose Serious Choking Risk (117,500 Units)

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 AM MT

```
⚠️ Heads up — more than 117,000 silicone toys are being recalled this week.

Mobi Games has recalled the Zippee silicone activity toy: that's the teal cylinder with six colorful pull-string loops, each ending in a round ball. The problem is that those ball-shaped ends can reach the back of a child's throat, posing a serious choking hazard. One child has already gagged and vomited.

The recalled toys were sold on Amazon, PlayMobi.com, and at specialty toy stores between November 2019 and January 2024 — so these have been in households for years. If you've got one, here's how to check: look at the date code stamped inside the cylinder body. It'll be in MMDDYY format. If it reads anywhere from 030620 to 110823, your toy is recalled.

Stop use immediately and contact Mobi Games at playmobi.com/pages/product-recall for a full refund.

Do you still have one of these? Check your toy bins and pass this along — these have been around long enough that they might be buried somewhere. 🔍
```

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM MT

```

You can also grab an Adult Golden Ticket for $69, which covers every day of the fair plus all evening entertainment: the rodeo, demolition derby, auto races, and the arena concert. It's a classic county fair — the kind that's been running since 1906. More info at elpasocountyfair.com.

### STORY 4: Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo Returns July 14–18 — Mutton Bustin', Free Drone Show

#### Long-Form Post — 4:00 PM MT

```
🤠 Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo is back — and if you've never taken your kids, this is the year to go.

The rodeo runs July 14–18 at Norris Penrose Event Center. Evening performances start at 7:30 PM most nights; if you'd rather go during the day, there are matinees on July 17 and 18 at 12:30 PM. Grounds open at 4 PM for evening shows and 10 AM for matinees, so you've got plenty of time to walk around before the action starts.

For the kids, Mutton Bustin' is the big draw — that's where kids ages 4–9 (50 lbs or under) hold on to a sheep for as long as they can. Entry is $20 per try, helmets and vests are provided, and you'll need to bring a signed waiver. Beyond that there's a petting zoo, pony rides, face painting, and goat roping to keep everyone busy.

One bonus this year: there's a free 14-minute drone show on the Thursday night performance (July 16) celebrating America's 250th birthday and Colorado's 150th anniversary.

Tickets are at PikesPeakorBust.org and NorrisPenrose.com. The rodeo has supported military families in COS since 1946 — it's been part of this community for 80 years.
