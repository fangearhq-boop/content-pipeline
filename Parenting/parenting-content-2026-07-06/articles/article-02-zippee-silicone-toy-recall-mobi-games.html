Zippee Silicone Toy Recalled: 117,500 Units Pose Choking Hazard to Kids

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | July 6, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

More than 117,000 silicone activity toys are being recalled this week by Mobi Games, the manufacturer of the popular Zippee toy. The Consumer Product Safety Commission has classified the hazard as a serious choking risk — and the product has been in homes since 2019, which means it could be buried in toy bins that haven't been touched in a while.

The Zippee is a teal silicone cylinder with six colorful pull-string loops, five of which have a small spherical ball attached to the end. Those spherical ends are the problem: they can reach the back of a child's throat, creating a choking hazard. One incident has already been reported to the CPSC — a child gagged and vomited.

Who Needs to Check

The recall covers Zippee silicone toys sold in the United States between November 2019 and January 2024. That's a four-year window that puts these toys in a lot of households. They were sold on Amazon, at PlayMobi.com, and at specialty toy stores, for approximately $20 each.

An additional 25,786 units sold in Canada are covered under a parallel recall by Health Canada.

To find out whether your specific toy is included, check the date code stamped on the inside of the silicone cylinder body. The code is in MMDDYY format. If your toy's date code falls anywhere between 030620 (March 6, 2020) and 110823 (November 8, 2023), it's part of the recall.

What to Do

If your toy's date code is within the recalled range, stop use immediately. Don't pass it along or donate it — it needs to come out of circulation.

Mobi Games is offering a full refund for recalled units. To claim it, go to playmobi.com/pages/product-recall and follow the instructions there. If you can't access the website, reach out to Mobi Games directly through their customer service contacts listed on the recall page.

If a child has put the toy in their mouth or you have any concern about a choking incident, call 911 or seek emergency care immediately.

Why Older Toy Recalls Matter

One thing worth noting about this recall: it covers toys sold as far back as 2019. A lot of families donate or sell toys their kids have outgrown, and those toys end up at resale shops, garage sales, and hand-me-down piles. If you purchased a used Zippee toy secondhand, it's worth checking the date code — the recall applies regardless of where you got it.

The CPSC's full recall notice for the Zippee toy is available at cpsc.gov/Recalls. If you're not sure whether you have a Zippee in the house, a search through toy bins for a teal silicone cylinder with colorful loops is worth five minutes of your time.

What's Next

Once you've confirmed whether your toy is recalled and initiated the refund if needed, the next step is checking with anyone who might have received your outgrown toys over the past few years. A quick message to family or friends who received hand-me-downs could save someone a serious incident.
