Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo Returns July 14–18: What COS Families Need to Know

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | July 6, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo has been part of Colorado Springs summers since 1946, and the 2026 edition brings something extra: a free 14-minute drone show on July 16 celebrating America's 250th birthday and Colorado's 150th anniversary. The rodeo runs July 14–18 at Norris Penrose Event Center.

For families, this event checks a lot of boxes — it's a legitimate professional rodeo with an NFR Open classification, and it's also built with kids in mind, from Mutton Bustin' to a full petting zoo.

Schedule and Performances

Evening performances run every night at 7:30 PM, with gates opening at 4 PM. If afternoons work better for your family, matinees are available on Saturday and Sunday, July 17 and 18, at 12:30 PM, with gates opening at 10 AM.

The Thursday evening performance on July 16 includes the free drone show — a 14-minute aerial display that's part of the America 250 and Colorado 150 celebrations happening across the state this summer. This is included with regular rodeo admission and doesn't require a separate ticket.

Mutton Bustin' and Kid Activities

Mutton Bustin' is one of those things that has to be seen to be believed: kids hold on to a sheep and try to stay on as long as possible. It's chaotic and hilarious and kids love it.

To participate, kids need to be between ages 4 and 9 and weigh 50 lbs or under. Entry is $20 per try, and helmets and vests are provided on site. Bring a signed parental waiver — you can usually find the form to print on the rodeo website in advance.

Beyond Mutton Bustin', the grounds also include a petting zoo, pony rides, face painting, and goat roping activities for younger kids who want something at a slower pace.

Tickets and Venue

Norris Penrose Event Center is located in Colorado Springs. Tickets are available at PikesPeakorBust.org and NorrisPenrose.com. Check the official sites for current pricing, as ticket tiers can vary by performance date and seating section.

The rodeo has raised money for military families in Colorado Springs for eight decades. Attending is one of those ways to do something local that actually matters beyond the event itself.
