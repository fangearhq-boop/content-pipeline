Crayola-Branded Pip-Cubes Recalled: Magnets That Can Kill If Swallowed

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | July 6, 2026

Colorado Springs, CO

If your kids have Crayola-branded Pip-Cubes magnetic building blocks in the house, stop use immediately. CreateOn has recalled these sets due to a magnet ingestion hazard that the Consumer Product Safety Commission describes as a risk of serious injury or death.

The recall covers two product lines sold at major retailers in 2025: the Bold Colors 24-piece set and the Glitter 24-piece set, available at Michaels and Michaels.com for around $20, and a 27-piece set sold on Amazon for around $35. All sets with a sale date between May 2025 and July 2025 are included.

Why These Magnets Are Dangerous

High-powered magnets are categorically different from the magnets in older toys. The problem isn't just that a child might swallow one — it's what happens when they swallow two or more.

When high-powered magnets are swallowed separately and travel to different parts of the digestive tract, they can attract each other through the intestinal walls. That attraction can cause intestinal perforation, twisting, and blockage. If not caught quickly, the result can be blood poisoning or death. The CPSC takes magnet ingestion extremely seriously because the damage can happen silently and progress faster than symptoms appear.

In the case of Pip-Cubes, the seams on the cube sets can separate during normal play, releasing these high-powered magnets. No injuries have been reported so far, but the recall is classified as a serious hazard precisely because the risk doesn't require a prior incident to be real.

What to Do

Stop using the set immediately and keep it out of reach of children. Don't donate it or pass it along — it needs to be returned.

CreateOn is offering a free replacement product in exchange for the recalled sets. You'll mail yours back using a prepaid shipping label and they'll send you a replacement. To start the return:

  • Visit createon.com/recall
  • Call 800-333-0549, Monday through Friday, 9:30 AM to 4:00 PM CT
  • Email pipcuberecall@CreateOn.com

If your child has swallowed a magnet or you suspect ingestion, call 911 or go to the emergency room immediately. Do not wait for symptoms — internal magnet injuries can worsen rapidly.

What's Next

If you no longer have the set — it was gifted to a grandparent's house, passed along, or you're not sure — it's worth a quick check with anyone who might have received one between May and July of 2025. The CPSC maintains a full recall database at cpsc.gov/Recalls where you can find official documentation and updates on this recall as they come.
