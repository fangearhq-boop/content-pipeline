April Outdoor Picks for COS Families: Your Spring Guide to Colorado Springs Parks and Nature

By Marcus Cole | April 11, 2026

Average highs near 62°F, 10+ hours of daily sunshine — with about six snowy days in the mix. Here's how to make the most of spring in Colorado Springs with kids.

April in Colorado Springs is genuinely one of the best months to be outside with kids. The average high temperature reaches about 62°F, the city logs more than 10 hours of sunshine per day on average, and the crowds that define summer haven't arrived yet. Garden of the Gods in April is something families in Colorado Springs should experience at least once a year.

That said, April is also unequivocally still Colorado. The mountains can generate afternoon storms quickly, and the area averages about six snowy days in April. The active weather weekend that just passed through is a reminder that every spring outdoor plan benefits from a backup. The approach that works: have a list of options, check the forecast the night before, and layer the kids before you leave the house.

Here's where we're sending Colorado Springs families this month.

Free Outdoor Picks

Garden of the Gods

Free, open year-round, and still one of the most spectacular public parks in the country. The paved Perkins Central Garden Trail is stroller-accessible and loops through the main formations. For families with older kids, the 15+ miles of unpaved trails offer longer routes with elevation gain. Parking fills up on weekend mornings in spring — arrive before 9:00 AM MT for the best experience.

Fountain Creek Nature Center

Located along Fountain Creek Regional Park, this El Paso County Parks facility offers flat, wetland-adjacent walking paths that work well for younger children and strollers. The nature center has interpretive exhibits and regular family programming throughout spring — including the interactive Theatre Across Borders Winnie the Pooh play happening today and next Saturday.

Bear Creek Nature Center

Also managed by El Paso County Parks, Bear Creek offers hands-on nature programming throughout April. Nature Tots (ages 2–3), homeschool programs, and early literacy events round out the spring calendar. The trails here connect to the larger Bear Creek Regional Park trail network with more varied terrain than Fountain Creek.

Stratton Open Space

383 acres of open space in the center of Colorado Springs, dog-friendly, with trails ranging from easy flat walking to steeper ridge routes. Stratton is especially rewarding on clear days — views of Pikes Peak are excellent from several spots on the property. Free, no reservation required.

April Weather Tips for COS Families

Layering is non-negotiable in Colorado Springs in April. Morning temperatures in the 30s and 40s can warm quickly to the 60s by midday, then drop again if clouds roll in from the mountains. Carrying a light packable jacket — even on the sunniest days — is standard practice for families who spend time outdoors here.

Afternoon is storm season. Most Colorado spring storms build and move quickly, arriving in the early-to-mid afternoon. Heading out for morning hikes and nature visits is generally more reliable than planning outdoor activities for 2:00 PM or 3:00 PM on variable spring days.

Sunscreen matters at elevation. Colorado Springs sits at approximately 6,035 feet above sea level, and UV exposure is meaningfully higher than at sea level. Sun protection for kids matters even on partly cloudy spring days.

What's Next

The spring outdoor season in Colorado Springs runs through June, when summer heat begins pushing activity toward early mornings. Bear Creek and Fountain Creek nature centers continue their family programming through May, with registration opening for summer sessions typically in April. Cheyenne Mountain Zoo's summer schedule, which includes extended evening hours, is typically announced in May. For a full list of El Paso County Parks spring programs, visit communityservices.elpasoco.com.
