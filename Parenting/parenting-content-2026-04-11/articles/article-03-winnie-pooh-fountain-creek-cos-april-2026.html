Theatre + Trails: Interactive Winnie the Pooh Play at Fountain Creek Nature Center This Weekend

By Ryan Calloway | April 11, 2026

$10 per person. A 30-minute performance. A nature hike with the characters after the show. This is what Saturday morning in Colorado Springs looks like.

If you don't have plans this morning yet, you do now.

Theatre Across Borders, a Pikes Peak Region children's theater company, is performing an interactive Winnie the Pooh production TODAY — Saturday, April 11 — at 10:30 AM at Fountain Creek Nature Center. Tickets are $10 per person, with children ages 2 and under admitted free.

What sets this show apart from a standard theater experience is what happens after the curtain falls. Once the performance ends, the characters from the show lead families on a short nature hike through Fountain Creek Nature Center's grounds. A craft activity is included as well, making this a complete morning activity that covers story, movement, and hands-on learning.

About the Venue

Fountain Creek Nature Center is managed by El Paso County Parks and Recreation. Located in the southern portion of Colorado Springs, the center sits along Fountain Creek Regional Park and offers interpretive natural history programs for families year-round. The walking paths are generally flat and suitable for strollers and younger children.

The combination of an indoor performance space with the adjacent outdoor creek and wetland environment makes it a natural fit for a show that pairs storytelling with a nature hike — and very on-brand for a production centered on the Hundred Acre Wood.

About Theatre Across Borders

Theatre Across Borders is a Colorado Springs-based children's theater company with a mission to bring live performance to accessible outdoor venues in the Pikes Peak Region. The company's programming regularly combines storytelling, movement, and nature education — a model that has connected well with Colorado Springs families who want arts experiences that don't require a trip to a traditional theater building.

The Winnie the Pooh production will also run at Bear Creek Nature Center in late April and May, extending the run to both of El Paso County's major nature centers.

Spring Plans Note

Colorado Springs weather in April can shift quickly. If you're heading out this morning, bring a light layer — temperatures can drop in the afternoon, especially if an afternoon storm rolls in from the mountains. The forecast for this weekend has been variable, so dress the kids in layers and be prepared to be flexible.

For families who can't make today's show, the April 18 performance offers a second chance at the same location and time. Theatre Across Borders has not yet announced specific times for the April 25 shows, so check their website for updated scheduling.
