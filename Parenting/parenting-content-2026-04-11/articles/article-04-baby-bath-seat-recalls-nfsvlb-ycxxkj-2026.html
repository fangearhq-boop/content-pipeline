Check Your Baby's Bath Seat: Multiple Amazon Brands Recalled for Drowning Risk in 2026

By Elena Voss | April 11, 2026

NFSVLB and YCXXKJ baby bath seats are among the latest recalled for tip-over and entrapment hazards. Here's how to check if yours is affected.

If you've purchased a baby bath seat from Amazon in the past year, there's a chance it has been recalled — and that you didn't get any notification about it.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued recalls on multiple baby bath seats sold by third-party Amazon sellers in early 2026. Two specific products — the NFSVLB bath seat and the YCXXKJ bath seat (sold by BenTalk) — have been recalled for violations of the mandatory standard for infant bath seats. The hazard in both cases is the same: the seats can tip over during use, and their leg openings are large enough for a baby's torso to slip through and become entrapped underwater.

Why These Seats Are Dangerous

Both recalled bath seats violate the CPSC's mandatory standard for infant bath seats. The standard exists for a reason: bath seats can create a false sense of security. Parents may step away briefly — to grab a towel or answer a knock — believing the seat is holding the baby in place. When a seat tips, or when a baby's torso slips through an oversized leg opening, the window for injury is measured in seconds.

The NFSVLB seat specifically fails in two ways. It is unstable and can tip over while in use. And the leg openings allow a child to slip down until the torso becomes entrapped in the seat itself, posing a drowning and entrapment hazard. The YCXXKJ seat sold by BenTalk was recalled for the same category of violations.

No injuries were reported in connection with the NFSVLB recall at the time it was issued, but the hazard exists regardless of whether an incident has been reported.

What to Do If You Have One of These Products

For the NFSVLB bath seat (model ZY2025):

  1. Stop using the bath seat immediately.
  2. Write "Recalled" on the front in permanent marker.
  3. Disassemble the seat by removing the backrest and arm restraints.
  4. Discard the screws.
  5. Cut the four suction cups on the bottom of the seat.
  6. Take a photo of the disassembled seat and email it to NFSVLB at amzmaihuayd@163.com to request your full refund.

For the YCXXKJ bath seat sold by BenTalk: visit cpsc.gov and search for the recall for specific remedy instructions, as these vary by seller.

Do not sell, donate, or pass on either recalled product. Doing so places another child at risk.

How to Check Your Bath Seat

If you're not sure whether your bath seat is affected, check your Amazon order history for purchases in the May–October 2025 window. Search the brand name on the CPSC recall database at cpsc.gov. Look for the model number ZY2025 printed on the NFSVLB product's label, typically on the underside of the seat.

If you cannot confirm your bath seat is safe, CPSC recommends erring on the side of caution: stop using the seat and contact CPSC directly at 1-800-638-2772 or at cpsc.gov.
