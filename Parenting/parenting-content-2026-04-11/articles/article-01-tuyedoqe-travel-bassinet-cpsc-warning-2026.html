Stop Using It Now: CPSC Warning on Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets Cites Strangulation and Fall Risks

By Elena Voss | April 11, 2026

The seller refused to issue a recall. That means parents have to find out on their own — here's everything you need to know.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) is warning parents to immediately stop using Tuyedoqe travel bassinets. The warning covers bassinets sold on Amazon from April 2025 through October 2025 — a period when roughly 250 units reached families across the country, including Colorado Springs.

What makes this warning especially urgent is that the seller, a China-based company called Tuyedoqe, has refused to cooperate with CPSC. The agency issued a formal Notice of Violation and requested a recall. Tuyedoqe did not agree to recall the product or offer any remedy to consumers. That means there is no automated refund email, no recall notification, and no outreach from the seller. The only way parents will know is if they see alerts like this one.

The Two Hazards

CPSC identified two distinct safety problems with the Tuyedoqe travel bassinet.

First, the restraints inside the bassinet pose a strangulation hazard. Infant sleep product standards require specific testing for restraint placement and tension, and these bassinets do not meet those standards.

Second, the sides of the bassinet are too low to safely contain an infant who moves or rolls, creating a fall hazard. The product also lacks a stand, which means that if the bassinet is placed on an elevated surface — a dresser, a table, a couch arm — a fall to the floor is possible.

Together, these failures mean the product violates the mandatory standard for infant sleep products, a federal safety requirement that all bassinets sold in the US must meet.

What to Do Right Now

CPSC's guidance is specific. Parents who have this product should:

  1. Stop using the bassinet immediately. Do not put an infant in it tonight.
  2. Remove the foam sleeping pad from the bassinet cover.
  3. Cut the mesh cover in half using scissors.
  4. Dispose of both the cut cover and the foam pad in the trash.
  5. Do not sell the bassinet, donate it, or give it away. Doing so puts another child at risk and may expose the seller to liability.

If you have questions or want to report an incident, contact CPSC directly at cpsc.gov or by calling 1-800-638-2772.

A Broader Pattern Worth Knowing

This warning is part of a recurring pattern of 2026 CPSC actions against baby sleep products and infant bath products sold by third-party Amazon sellers, many based in China. In recent months, CPSC has also recalled baby swings, baby bath seats, and infant loungers from similar sources.

Parents in Colorado Springs shopping for baby gear online should check cpsc.gov/Recalls before purchasing infant sleep products — and particularly when buying from unfamiliar brand names on marketplace platforms.

What's Next

Because Tuyedoqe has refused to participate in a formal recall, there is no compensation available through the company at this time. CPSC is continuing enforcement actions. If your baby has been injured using this product, report the incident at cpsc.gov. Check the CPSC recalls page regularly — the agency updates it as new actions are taken against noncompliant sellers.
