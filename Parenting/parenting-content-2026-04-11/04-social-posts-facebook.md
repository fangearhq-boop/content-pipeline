## STORY 2: AAP Screen Time Guidelines Update

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

```
Good news for parents who've been stressed about the "two hours of screen time per day" rule: that guideline is officially gone.

The American Academy of Pediatrics released a major update to its screen time guidance in February 2026 — the first significant revision in a decade. And the headline shift is this: the AAP no longer recommends a fixed daily time limit for screen use. Instead, the new framework focuses on the quality of the digital content your child interacts with and the nature of the "digital ecosystem" they inhabit.

Here's what that means in practice.

The old approach tried to limit screens by the clock. The new approach recognizes that kids don't just "use screens" the way they watch a TV show — they live inside platforms. Social media, apps, games, video content, and AI tools create a connected digital environment that shapes attention, mood, and habits.

The AAP flags two types of digital experiences that concern pediatricians most: algorithmic content designed to keep kids scrolling, and commercial-heavy environments where engagement metrics matter more than child development. These experiences can crowd out sleep, movement, and in-person socializing — and the research shows negative health outcomes as a result.

On the flip side, the new guidelines are clear that child-centered digital design — age-appropriate content, platforms that encourage critical thinking, and social connection — can still support healthy development.

So what does this mean for families in Colorado Springs? It's less about setting a timer and more about asking: Is this app or platform designed WITH my child's wellbeing in mind? Or is it designed to capture attention and sell ads?
```
