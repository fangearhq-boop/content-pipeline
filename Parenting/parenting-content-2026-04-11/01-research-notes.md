### STORY 1: CPSC Warning — Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets

**Sources:**
- cpsc.gov — "CPSC Warns Consumers to Immediately Stop Using Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets Due to Risk of Serious Injury or Death from Strangulation and Fall Hazards" (official CPSC warning page)
- x.com/USCPSC — CPSC official tweet confirming warning
- threads.com/@uscpsc — CPSC Threads post with additional seller refusal detail
- facebook.com/USCPSC — CPSC Facebook post
- safeinfantsleepalliance Facebook — sharing the warning

**Verified Facts (HIGH confidence):**
- Product: Tuyedoqe travel bassinets — foam sleeping pad + mesh canopy with three toy bears attached [cpsc.gov — primary source]
- Hazards: (1) restraints = strangulation hazard; (2) sides too low = fall hazard; (3) no stand = fall hazard on elevated surfaces [cpsc.gov]
- About 250 units recalled [cpsc.gov]
- Sold on Amazon.com from April 2025 through October 2025 for about $40 [cpsc.gov]
- CPSC issued Notice of Violation to Tuyedoqe (China company) [cpsc.gov + threads CPSC]
- Tuyedoqe has NOT agreed to recall or offer remedy [cpsc.gov + threads CPSC — 2 sources]
- Consumer action: immediately stop using; remove foam pad; cut cover in half; dispose; do NOT sell or give away [cpsc.gov]

**Verified Facts (MEDIUM confidence):**
- This is an emergency CPSC "Warning" not a formal recall — because seller refused to cooperate [inferred from cpsc.gov warning vs. recall distinction]

### STORY 2: AAP Screen Time Guidelines Update

**Sources:**
- aap.org — "Understanding the New AAP Digital Media Guidelines" (official AAP page)
- publications.aap.org — "Digital Ecosystems, Children, and Adolescents: Policy Statement" (peer-reviewed Pediatrics, vol. 157, Feb 2026)
- edsurge.com — "New AAP 'Screen Time' Recommendations Focus Less on Screens, More on Family Time" (Feb 5, 2026)
- health.choc.org — "Updated AAP recommendations for screen time: What parents need to know"
- medicalxpress.com — FSU psychologist guidance on new recommendations (Feb 2026)
- newcanaanpediatrics.com — "Understanding the Updated AAP Screen and Media Guidelines"

**Verified Facts (HIGH confidence):**
- AAP released new digital media guidelines in February 2026 (publications.aap.org — primary source)
- No longer recommends a fixed 2-hour daily screen time limit — shift from 2016 policy (edsurge.com + aap.org — 2 sources)
- New focus: quality of content and "digital ecosystems" vs. quantity of time [aap.org]
- Algorithmic/commercial-heavy content can displace healthy behaviors (sleep, movement) and cause negative outcomes [aap.org]
- Child-centered digital design that fosters critical thinking and age-appropriate content can support development [aap.org]
- Policy statement title: "Digital Ecosystems, Children, and Adolescents" in Pediatrics journal [publications.aap.org]

**Verified Facts (MEDIUM confidence):**
- Old guideline: 2 hours/day screen time limit (from 2016) — well-established, multiple sources confirm
- Context: Children now exist within "digital ecosystems" of social media, apps, games, video, AI — not just passive screen time [aap.org]

### STORY 4: Baby Bath Seat Recall Wave

- cpsc.gov — "YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats Recalled Due to Risk of Serious Injury or Death to Children from Drowning; Violate Mandatory Standard for Infant Bath Seats; Sold on Amazon by BenTalk" (official recall)
- cpsc.gov — "NFSVLB Baby Bath Seats Recalled" (official recall — recall date January 8, 2026)
- cpsc.gov — Baby Bathtub Seats and Rings category page
- consumerreports.org — "Baby Product Recalls Parents Should Know About in 2026"
- myparistexas.com — NFSVLB recall coverage (wire story)

**Verified Facts (HIGH confidence):**
- NFSVLB bath seats: About 1,430 units; model ZY2025; sold Amazon May–October 2025 for $25–$40; recall date January 8, 2026; hazard: tips over, leg opening allows entrapment [cpsc.gov — primary source]
- NFSVLB action: stop using; write "Recalled" in marker; disassemble; cut suction cups; send photo to NFSVLB; email amzmaihuayd@163.com [cpsc.gov]
- YCXXKJ bath seats: sold by BenTalk on Amazon; recall for same tipping/entrapment violations [cpsc.gov — primary source]
- Both violations: violate mandatory standard for infant bath seats (ASTM F1967) [cpsc.gov]

**Verified Facts (MEDIUM confidence):**
- Wave of similar recalls hitting Amazon third-party bath seat sellers in early 2026 (multiple listings on cpsc.gov confirm this is a pattern) [cpsc.gov category page]
