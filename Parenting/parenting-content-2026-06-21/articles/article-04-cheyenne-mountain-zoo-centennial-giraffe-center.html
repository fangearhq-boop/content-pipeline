Cheyenne Mountain Zoo Turns 100 — New $40M Giraffe Center Opening This Summer

By Sarah Morales | June 21, 2026 | COS Parenting

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo is celebrating its 100th anniversary in 2026, and the centerpiece of the centennial year is the biggest expansion in the zoo's history: a $40 million giraffe facility opening this summer. For Colorado Springs families who already love the zoo, it's a significant upgrade to what was already one of the best giraffe experiences in the region.

A Colorado Springs Institution Turns 100

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo was founded in 1926 by Spencer Penrose, the Colorado Springs philanthropist and developer who also built The Broadmoor. It sits at 6,800 feet in the foothills of Cheyenne Mountain — making it the only mountain zoo in the United States. That elevation and setting have defined the zoo's character for a century: intimate, scenic, and unlike any other zoo in the country.

One hundred years in, the zoo remains a locally owned nonprofit. Its giraffe program has been running since the 1950s, with more than 200 giraffe calves born at the zoo over that span — one of the most successful breeding records of any zoo in North America.

The International Center for the Care and Conservation of Giraffe

The new facility opening this summer is formally named the International Center for the Care and Conservation of Giraffe. The project has been in development and construction for several years and represents the largest capital investment in the zoo's history.

Here's what's included:

  • A 12,000-square-foot indoor barn — allowing the zoo's giraffe herd to move freely year-round, including during Colorado's winter months
  • 11 giraffe feeding zones, up from 3 — significantly expanding the number of guests who can have an extended giraffe feeding encounter during each visit
  • A 25% expansion of the outdoor giraffe habitat
  • An ETFE clear roof — a transparent architectural material that allows full natural light into the indoor space; the Cheyenne Mountain Zoo installation is the largest ETFE roof of its kind covering an animal exhibit in North America
  • Six full-size bronze giraffe sculptures by sculptor Antonia Chastain, placed throughout the exhibit area, representing the more than 200 giraffe calves born at the zoo since the 1950s

What This Means for a Family Visit

The most practical change for families visiting the zoo is the increase from three to 11 giraffe feeding zones. On busy summer weekends, the old setup could mean long waits for a brief feeding encounter. With 11 zones and expanded habitat, significantly more families will be able to feed the giraffes during a single visit — and with more time and space to do it.

The indoor barn also changes the year-round calculus. Previously, giraffe access during Colorado's colder months was limited. The new facility with its ETFE roof — which allows natural light while providing full weather protection — means the giraffes will be accessible and viewable even on cold or stormy days.

For families with younger children, the bronze sculptures by Antonia Chastain add a new element to the experience: life-size giraffe figures positioned throughout the exhibit for interaction and photos, even when the live animals are in other areas of the habitat.

Grand Opening Celebration

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo has announced that a weeklong grand opening celebration is planned for the facility. As of the date of this article, specific dates for the grand opening week have not been confirmed. Updates will be posted at cmzoo.org.

If you're planning a summer visit specifically around the grand opening events, checking cmzoo.org closer to the opening date will give you the most current information on programming, special events, and any ticketing adjustments.

What's Next

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo general admission is required for the giraffe experience — giraffe feeding encounters are included with admission (though subject to availability during busy periods). For current hours, admission prices, and member information, visit cmzoo.org.

The centennial year runs through December 2026. The zoo is expected to mark the milestone with programming throughout the year beyond the giraffe center opening. Keep an eye on cmzoo.org or their social channels for centennial event announcements as the summer progresses.
