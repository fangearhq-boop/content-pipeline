Colorado Springs Sunday Market — Family Guide for the 2026 Season

By Jamie Rivera | June 21, 2026 | COS Parenting

If you don't have a Sunday morning routine that gets the family out of the house without a lot of effort, the Colorado Springs Sunday Market is worth knowing about. Every Sunday through October 25, Acacia Park in downtown Colorado Springs hosts 60 or more local vendors from 9 AM to 2 PM. It's free to attend, it's family-friendly, and there are 18 Sundays left in the 2026 season.

The Basics

The Colorado Springs Sunday Market is organized by Hunt and Gather COS and takes over Acacia Park at 115 E. Platte Ave in downtown Colorado Springs each week. The 2026 season launched May 10 and runs through October 25, with no scheduled off-weeks during the summer months.

Hours are 9 AM to 2 PM. Admission is free. The market runs regardless of light rain, though extreme weather may affect individual vendor attendance. Bring a reusable bag — you'll want one.

What to Expect

The market draws more than 60 vendors each week, with a mix of categories that shifts slightly by season. In June and July, the produce selection leans toward summer crops — tomatoes, peppers, summer squash, berries, peaches from local and regional growers. The artisan food vendors are consistent week to week: small-batch jams, specialty sauces, handmade pasta, baked goods, honey, and similar items.

The non-food vendor section includes local makers selling jewelry, clothing, home goods, ceramics, and handmade art. This section is less crowded and easier to browse with kids in tow than the produce area, which can get busy around 10 AM on nice mornings.

Live music is a weekly feature. Performers vary — local musicians across genres — and they're typically set up near the center of the park where you can hear them throughout the market without the volume being intrusive. It adds to the feel of the place without making it hard to have a conversation.

Why It Works for Families

Acacia Park is central, paved, and flat — all of which matters if you're navigating with a stroller. The market layout doesn't require covering large distances, and the park has shaded areas and benches for a break. It's a good balance of "structured outing" and "wander at your own pace."

For families with older kids, the market is genuinely interesting rather than just something to do while the adults shop. Local makers are usually happy to talk about what they make, and the variety of vendors — produce, food, crafts, music — gives curious kids enough variation to stay engaged through at least part of the two-hour window.

For families with toddlers or babies, the morning slot (9–11 AM) tends to be most manageable before the crowd builds and before the heat of the day picks up in July and August.

Parking and Getting There

Street parking is available on the streets surrounding Acacia Park — E. Platte Ave, Tejon St, Nevada Ave, and Bijou St. Sunday morning parking is generally easier to find than weekday downtown parking, but it can fill up within a block or two of the park on busier market days. Budget an extra five minutes for parking if you're arriving between 10 AM and noon.

The park is also accessible on foot or by bike from downtown neighborhoods. If you're coming from the Westside, Old Colorado City, or the Nevada Ave corridor, it's an easy ride.

What's Next
