CPSC Recalls YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats — Drowning Hazard, Discard Immediately

By Jamie Rivera | June 21, 2026 | COS Parenting

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled approximately 9,000 YCXXKJ baby bath seats due to a drowning hazard — and has since escalated the warning to a "Do Not Use" alert because the manufacturer has stopped cooperating with the recall process. If you or anyone in your family purchased one of these seats on Amazon in the last two years, stop using it immediately.

What's Being Recalled

The recalled product is the YCXXKJ baby bath seat, Model YD-1958. The seats are unstable and can tip over with an infant inside, creating a drowning hazard. They were sold exclusively on Amazon by a seller named BenTalk from May 2024 through October 2025 for approximately $36.

The seats came in blue, gray, pink, and yellow, with two detachable arms, four suction cups on the base, and three bath toys (a duck, a turtle, and a whale). To check whether you have this specific product, look at the tracking label on the back of the seat — it will read "Model: YD-1958" if you have a recalled unit.

Why This Recall Is More Serious Than Most

The standard recall process gives manufacturers a path to address the hazard — through a refund, repair, or replacement. In this case, BenTalk has stopped responding to the CPSC and is no longer processing consumer refund requests. Because of this, the CPSC took the additional step of issuing a formal "Do Not Use" warning — a separate, escalated alert that goes beyond the initial recall.

That escalation matters for parents: there's no refund process to wait for, no replacement coming, and no reason to hold onto the seat. The CPSC's guidance is to stop use immediately, write "recalled" on the seat in permanent marker, and discard it so it can't be passed along to another family or inadvertently used again.

The seat violates mandatory federal safety standards for infant bath seats, which require the product to remain stable during normal use.

Who May Have This Product

With approximately 9,000 units sold, there are a meaningful number of these seats still in homes across the country — including Colorado Springs. Because the product was sold exclusively through Amazon (not at Target, Walmart, or brick-and-mortar baby retailers), families who shopped online for bath accessories during 2024 or early 2025 are the most likely to have one.

If you've received one as a gift, borrowed one, or aren't sure where yours came from, the model number check on the back takes only a few seconds and is worth doing.

What to Do

  1. Check the back of your baby bath seat for the tracking label. If it reads "Model: YD-1958," it's recalled.
  2. Stop using it immediately — do not use it for one more bath.
  3. Write "recalled" in permanent marker on the seat so it's clearly marked as unsafe.
  4. Discard the seat. Do not donate, sell, or give it away.

For questions or to report a safety concern, contact the CPSC at cpsc.gov or 1-800-638-2772.

What's Next

Because BenTalk is not cooperating with the recall, there's no pending refund or replacement program to track. The CPSC's recommendation to discard the seat is the final consumer guidance at this time. If you believe you purchased this product and want documentation of the recall for your records, the official recall notice is available at cpsc.gov.
