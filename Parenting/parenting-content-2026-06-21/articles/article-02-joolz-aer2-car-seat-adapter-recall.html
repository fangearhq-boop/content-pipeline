Joolz Aer2 Car Seat Adapters Recalled — Stop Use, Full Refund Available

By Sarah Morales | June 21, 2026 | COS Parenting

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission announced on June 18, 2026 that Joolz is recalling its Aer2 Car Seat Adapters for Strollers due to a fall hazard. The adapter can fail to properly attach to the Joolz Aer2 stroller, allowing the car seat — and the infant inside — to fall. If you own one of these adapters, stop using it now and register online to receive a full refund.

What's Being Recalled

The recall covers Joolz Aer2 Car Seat Adapters for Strollers — the accessory that allows a compatible infant car seat to attach to the Joolz Aer2 stroller frame. According to the CPSC, the adapter can fail to attach properly, which may cause the car seat to become detached while the stroller is in use.

Approximately 3,840 units are affected in the United States (an additional 148 units were recalled in Canada). The adapters were sold from June 2025 through May 2026 for approximately $50 at Bloomingdale's, Nordstrom, and specialty baby retailers, as well as online at joolz.com, bloomingdales.com, nordstrom.com, and amazon.com.

The CPSC received one report of the adapter detaching. No injuries have been reported.

The Fall Hazard Explained

The Joolz Aer2 is designed to accept an infant car seat directly on the stroller frame — a "travel system" setup that many families use to move a sleeping baby from the car to the stroller without unbuckling. The car seat adapter is the component that connects the car seat to the stroller's attachment points.

The recall addresses a failure in that connection: under certain conditions, the adapter may not lock into place properly, leaving the car seat insecure on the stroller frame. If the car seat detaches while the stroller is in motion, an infant could fall from a significant height. Even at low stroller speeds, a fall from stroller-seat height poses serious injury risk to an infant.

The one documented incident — no injuries, adapter detached — is what triggered the CPSC investigation and subsequent recall. The absence of injuries so far is fortunate, but it doesn't reduce the potential severity of the hazard.

How to Get Your Refund

Joolz is offering a full refund to all affected customers. The process requires registration and a return shipment:

  1. Stop using the car seat adapter immediately and detach it from your stroller.
  2. Go to joolzcarseatadapter.expertinquiry.com and complete the registration form.
  3. You'll receive a prepaid shipping label to return the adapter.
  4. After the return is received, Joolz will issue your refund via electronic payment or a virtual prepaid gift card.

Unlike the YCXXKJ bath seat recall (also covered today), the Joolz refund process is functioning — the company is cooperating with the CPSC and the registration portal is active. The refund covers the full purchase price of the adapter.

Who This Affects

Families who purchased a Joolz Aer2 stroller within the last year and also bought the car seat adapter accessory are the primary audience for this recall. The adapter is a separate purchase from the stroller itself and is not included in all Joolz Aer2 bundles.

If you received the stroller as a gift or purchased it secondhand, the adapter may have come with it without your knowledge of when it was originally purchased. If you're unsure whether your adapter is part of this recall, contact Joolz through the registration portal or reach out to the CPSC at cpsc.gov.

The recall affects US and Canadian products sold in the same timeframe. Colorado Springs families who purchased through Nordstrom at the Chapel Hills Mall or online at any of the listed retailers may have the affected product.

What's Next

If you register for the refund, expect a processing window for the return and payment issuance — typical CPSC recall refund timelines run two to four weeks after the return shipment is received. Keep the prepaid label once you receive it and ship promptly to avoid delays.

For more information on this recall, visit cpsc.gov or the recall registration page at joolzcarseatadapter.expertinquiry.com. COS Parenting will post updates if registration window deadlines are announced.
