Bear Creek Pollinator Celebration Is This Friday — $5, All Ages, West COS

By Jamie Rivera | June 21, 2026 | COS Parenting

If you're looking for a genuinely hands-on outdoor activity for the kids this Friday, Bear Creek Regional Park is hosting an annual event that's worth putting on the calendar: the Bear Creek A'Buzz Annual Honey Harvest and Pollinator Celebration Day. The event is June 26, runs from 12:30 to 2:00 PM, costs $5 per person, and is designed for all ages.

What the Event Is

The Bear Creek A'Buzz celebration is hosted by the Bear Creek Nature Center and centers on Colorado's native pollinators — the bees, butterflies, wasps, and other insects that are essential to the health of local plants and food systems. The event combines education with hands-on outdoor exploration, making it a good fit for curious kids at nearly any age.

Activities include bug sweeps — where participants use nets to collect and examine live insects from the meadow — as well as guided native pollinator education and close-up exploration of insects and their roles in the local ecosystem. The format is participatory rather than lecture-style, which tends to hold kids' attention better than a standard nature walk.

Why It's Worth the Drive to West COS

Bear Creek Regional Park sits in west Colorado Springs, tucked into the foothills along Bear Creek. It's one of the more scenic and less crowded parks in the area — the kind of place that Colorado Springs families who live on the east side or in Briargate don't always think of first, but appreciate once they go. The Nature Center itself is a resource that's often underutilized relative to how good it is.

For an afternoon event with a focused 90-minute window, the location is practical: there's dedicated parking, the Nature Center is easy to find, and the meadow habitat where bug sweeps happen is right there. You're not spending 45 minutes hiking to the activity.

The event is also one of the more affordable family outings in Colorado Springs this summer. At $5 per person with no ticket pre-purchase required (confirm with the Nature Center ahead of time), it's accessible without planning far in advance.

A Few Things to Know Before You Go

The event window is shorter than it might seem: 12:30 to 2:00 PM is 90 minutes. If you're coming from across town, build in transit time and plan to arrive by 12:30. Arriving late into a 90-minute outdoor program can mean missing the structured portion.

Colorado late June afternoons can shift quickly weather-wise. Check the forecast Thursday night and Friday morning. If the sky looks uncertain, the Bear Creek Nature Center is worth a quick call before making the drive. Bring sunscreen regardless — the meadow habitat is open and sunny.

Bug sweeps involve children using nets outdoors, so comfortable, weather-appropriate clothes that can get a little dirty are a practical choice. Closed-toe shoes are a good idea for meadow exploration.

What's Next

Bear Creek A'Buzz runs annually, so if Friday doesn't work, it's worth bookmarking for next summer. The Bear Creek Nature Center also runs other seasonal programs throughout the year — their calendar is available through El Paso County's parks page.

This Friday's event is five days away — a good window to mark the calendar, confirm the forecast, and plan a west-side afternoon around it.
