### STORY 1: YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seat Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

If you bought a baby bath seat on Amazon in the last two years, please take a minute to check the label.

The CPSC has recalled approximately 9,000 YCXXKJ baby bath seats (Model YD-1958) due to a drowning hazard — the seats are unstable and can tip over with an infant inside. The product was sold exclusively on Amazon by a seller called BenTalk from May 2024 through October 2025 for around $36. It came in blue, gray, pink, and yellow, with two detachable arms, four suction cups, and three bath toys (a duck, a turtle, and a whale).

To check if you have this product: flip it over and look for a label on the back that reads "Model: YD-1958." If that's your seat, stop using it immediately.

Here's what makes this recall more serious than most: the CPSC has since issued a separate "Do Not Use" warning — a step beyond a standard recall — because the manufacturer (BenTalk) has stopped responding to CPSC and is no longer processing refund requests. That means families who have this product are unlikely to receive any money back. The CPSC's guidance is to write "recalled" on the seat and discard it.

If you need help or have questions, contact the CPSC directly at cpsc.gov or call 1-800-638-2772.

### STORY 2: Joolz Aer2 Car Seat Adapter Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

A car seat adapter recall announced this week affects families who use the Joolz Aer2 stroller — and the danger is real enough to warrant an immediate stop-use.

The CPSC issued a recall on June 18, 2026 for approximately 3,840 Joolz Aer2 Car Seat Adapters for Strollers. The hazard: the adapter can fail to properly attach to the stroller, which can cause the car seat — and the infant inside — to fall. There has been one report of the adapter detaching, though no injuries have been reported.

These adapters were sold from June 2025 through May 2026 at Bloomingdale's, Nordstrom, specialty retailers, and online at joolz.com, bloomingdales.com, nordstrom.com, and amazon.com for approximately $50.

If you own one, here's what to do:
1. Stop using the adapter immediately.
2. Detach it from your stroller.
3. Register at joolzcarseatadapter.expertinquiry.com to receive a full refund via electronic payment or virtual prepaid gift card, along with a prepaid shipping label for the return.

Unlike the YCXXKJ recall, the refund process here is active and functional — you just need to register. Take a few minutes to do it.

For more information, visit cpsc.gov or search "Joolz Aer2 Car Seat Adapter recall."

If you have this product, please act today — and pass this along to anyone in your circle who has a Joolz Aer2 stroller.

### STORY 3: Bear Creek Pollinator Celebration

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

If you're looking for a genuinely fun, hands-on Friday afternoon activity for the kids, Bear Creek Regional Park is doing something pretty special this week.

The Bear Creek Nature Center's Annual Honey Harvest and Pollinator Celebration Day is this Friday, June 26, from 12:30 to 2:00 PM — just five days away. It's at the Bear Creek Nature Center inside Bear Creek Regional Park in west Colorado Springs, and admission is $5 per person.

The event centers on native pollinators — bees, butterflies, and other insects that make our local food systems work. Kids and families can participate in bug sweeps, explore live insects up close, and learn why pollinators matter in our Colorado landscape. It's designed for all ages, which means this is just as engaging for a curious 4-year-old as it is for a middle schooler doing a science unit on ecosystems.

Bear Creek Regional Park is one of those west COS spots that doesn't always get the attention it deserves — it's scenic, uncrowded, and a great place to spend an afternoon even outside of the event.

A few things worth knowing before you go:
- The event runs from 12:30 to 2:00 PM only — it's a shorter window, so plan accordingly.
- Cost is $5 per person (cash or card — confirm with the Nature Center ahead of time).
- The Bear Creek Nature Center is located at 245 Bear Creek Road, Colorado Springs.

### STORY 4: Cheyenne Mountain Zoo Centennial

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo is turning 100 this year — and it's marking the occasion with the biggest expansion in the zoo's history.

The zoo was founded in 1926 by Spencer Penrose and has been a fixture of Colorado Springs family life for a century. It's the only mountain zoo in the United States, sitting at 6,800 feet with views across the Front Range. This summer, it opens a facility that's been years in the making: the International Center for the Care and Conservation of Giraffe.

Here's what's included in the $40 million project:
- A 12,000-square-foot indoor barn where giraffes can move freely year-round
- 11 giraffe feeding zones — up from 3, meaning more families get extended giraffe encounters during their visit
- A 25% expansion of the outdoor giraffe habitat
- An ETFE clear roof — the largest of its kind covering an animal exhibit in North America, allowing natural light while protecting from Colorado's unpredictable weather
- Six full-size bronze giraffe sculptures by sculptor Antonia Chastain, representing the 200+ giraffe calves born at the zoo since the 1950s

A weeklong grand opening celebration is planned for this summer — dates to be announced at cmzoo.org.

For Colorado Springs families who already love the zoo, this is a meaningful upgrade to what was already one of the best giraffe experiences in the region. For families who haven't visited in a while (or who moved here recently), this is an ideal time to go.

Visit cmzoo.org for tickets, hours, and grand opening details when they're announced.

### STORY 5: Colorado Springs Sunday Market

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

If your family doesn't have a Sunday morning routine yet, here's one worth trying: the Colorado Springs Sunday Market at Acacia Park.

Every Sunday through October 25, Acacia Park in downtown Colorado Springs (115 E. Platte Ave) transforms into a 60+ vendor market from 9 AM to 2 PM. Admission is free. There are 18 Sundays left in the 2026 season, starting with this one.

What's there:
- Farm-fresh produce — local Colorado growers, seasonal fruits and vegetables
- Artisan foods — small-batch jams, sauces, baked goods, specialty items
- Handmade goods — jewelry, clothing, art, home goods from local makers
- Live music every week
- Family-friendly games and activities on-site

It's the kind of place where kids can wander, families can grab breakfast snacks, and you can actually run into people from the neighborhood. Acacia Park is centrally located and easy to reach whether you're coming from north Colorado Springs, Briargate, or the Westside.

The market runs through late October, so there's no rush — but the summer months are when produce variety peaks and outdoor mornings are best. A few vendor favorites sell out early, so if you're going for specific items, arriving by 10 AM is a good rule.

No parking pass needed — street parking is available around Acacia Park, and it's an easy walk from the Colorado Avenue and Nevada Avenue corridors.
