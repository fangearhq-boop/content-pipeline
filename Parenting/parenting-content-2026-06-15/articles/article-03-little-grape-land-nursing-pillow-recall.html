CPSC Recall: Stop Using Little Grape Land Nursing Pillows Immediately

About 1,430 Amazon-sold pillows recalled June 4 for suffocation risk — here's how to identify them and get your refund

By Sarah Morales

June 15, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission issued a recall on June 4, 2026 for Little Grape Land nursing pillows. About 1,430 units are affected. The hazard is serious: the pillow's design can obstruct an infant's breathing, posing a risk of suffocation.

If you purchased one of these pillows, stop using it immediately.

What's Being Recalled

The recall covers U-shaped nursing pillows sold under the Little Grape Land brand name. They were sold exclusively on Amazon.com from August 2025 through April 2026, priced at approximately $28-$30.

The CPSC determined that the pillow design violates mandatory federal safety standards for nursing pillows and infant support cushions.

No injuries or incidents had been reported as of the recall date, but the CPSC acted on the structural hazard before an incident occurred.

How to Identify Your Pillow — No Labels on Product

One complicating factor: there are no labels or markings on the product itself. You cannot identify a recalled pillow by a tag or serial number.

Identification is by pattern. The CPSC lists the following affected patterns:

  • Rose floral
  • Alligator
  • Bear
  • Butterfly
  • Cactus
  • Construction truck
  • Forest deer
  • Green leaves
  • Little bunny
  • Spring flower
  • Woodland animal

If you're unsure whether you purchased one of these pillows, check your Amazon order history for any Little Grape Land purchase between August 2025 and April 2026.

What to Do: Three Steps

  1. Stop using the pillow immediately. Remove it from your infant's environment.
  2. Cut the pillow in half. This step is required as part of the recall process and confirms the product is destroyed.
  3. Email a photo to recall@evermorepartner.com. Include a photo of the cut pillow. You'll receive a full refund.

No packaging is required, and you do not need to return the physical product. The full refund is processed after photo confirmation.

If You've Already Shared or Gifted This Pillow

Because these pillows were sold as gifts and were sometimes resold secondhand, the CPSC's notice asks anyone who purchased or received one to spread the word. If you gave this pillow to a family member or friend, contact them directly — there's no automatic notification to secondary owners.

The affected patterns — particularly the ones with animal themes like bear, bunny, alligator, and woodland animal — may have circulated as baby shower gifts. Check your records and reach out if you think someone in your circle may have one.

What's Next
