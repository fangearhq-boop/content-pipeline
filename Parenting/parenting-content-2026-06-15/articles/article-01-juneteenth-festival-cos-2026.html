Free, Family-Friendly Juneteenth Festival Returns to COS June 20-21 at New Venue

The 6th Annual Southern Colorado Juneteenth Festival moves indoors to Norris Penrose with a kids' zone, national entertainment, and free admission for all ages

By Sarah Morales

June 15, 2026

Colorado Springs families have a free, all-ages cultural celebration to look forward to this weekend. The 6th Annual Southern Colorado Juneteenth Festival returns June 20-21 at a new venue: the Norris Penrose Indoor Event Center, 1045 Lower Gold Camp Road.

Organized by One Body Entertainment, the festival celebrates Juneteenth — the federal holiday marking June 19, 1865, when enslaved people in Texas learned of their freedom, more than two months after the Confederacy's surrender. In Colorado Springs, it's become one of the most attended summer events on the calendar. The 2024 festival drew approximately 36,000 people.

What's New for 2026: A Better Venue for Families

For the first time, the festival moves indoors. Previous years were held at America the Beautiful Park, an outdoor setting that left attendees exposed to summer heat and weather. The Norris Penrose Indoor Event Center provides covered, climate-controlled space with better accessibility for families with strollers, individuals with mobility needs, and anyone who's tried to navigate a large outdoor event with young children.

The venue is located at 1045 Lower Gold Camp Road, on the southwest side of Colorado Springs. Admission remains free for all ages.

What to Expect

The festival runs both Saturday, June 20 and Sunday, June 21. Programming spans the full day each day and includes:

  • Kids' zone with bounce houses, face painting, and an obstacle course
  • National headliners V.I.C. and David Banner
  • Car show
  • Health fair
  • Local step show talent
  • Fashion show

The kids' zone makes this a genuine family event, not just a concert — there's programming for young children while parents engage with the larger festival.

Why This Event Matters for Colorado Springs Families

Juneteenth became a federal holiday in 2021, but the Southern Colorado celebration predates that recognition by several years. The festival is one of the largest free community events in COS, and the scale — national headliners, thousands of attendees, multiple activity tracks — reflects the work One Body Entertainment has done to grow it into a regional destination event.

For families with children, the combination of free admission, a dedicated kids' zone, and accessible indoor venue makes this one of the easier summer events to bring the whole family to without significant planning or expense.
