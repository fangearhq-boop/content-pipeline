Pikes Peak Hill Climb Race Week Starts Tomorrow — Free Fan Fest, Father's Day Race Day

Practice begins June 16, the free Fan Fest hits downtown June 19, and Race Day falls on Father's Day (June 21) — here's the family guide

By Jamie Rivera

June 15, 2026

Race week for the Pikes Peak International Hill Climb begins tomorrow, June 16. For Colorado Springs families, that means a full week of events — some free, some ticketed — culminating on Father's Day with Race Day on June 21.

Here's what to know before you head out.

Practice and Qualifying: June 16-19 (Free Spectating)

Practice and qualifying sessions run Tuesday through Friday, June 16-19. Spectators can watch from seven designated spectator areas along the mountain at no charge during practice days. No tickets are required to watch practice runs.

The highest spectator area is Devil's Playground at 12,780 feet elevation — a genuinely remarkable spot to watch cars climb toward the summit. Families with older children who can handle the altitude and terrain will find it an unforgettable experience. Younger children and those sensitive to altitude may do better at lower spectator areas.

Fan Fest: June 19, Downtown COS (Free)

On Thursday, June 19, the official PPIHC Fan Fest takes over downtown Colorado Springs from 5-9 PM, spanning 10 city blocks. It's free and open to the public.

Fan Fest includes driver meet-and-greets, stunt performances, sponsor and vendor booths, and family-friendly entertainment. It's one of the few opportunities to get close to drivers and cars outside of the race itself, and it requires nothing more than showing up downtown.

Race Day: June 21 — Father's Day (Ticketed for Ages 10+)

Race Day is Sunday, June 21 — Father's Day — with a green flag at 7:30 AM. Seven spectator areas are open along the mountain, from lower elevation access points up to Devil's Playground at 12,780 feet.

Anyone age 10 and over requires a Race Day ticket. Children under 10 are admitted free. There are two critical things to know about tickets:

  • Tickets are not sold on Race Day. They cannot be purchased at the gate or at any PPIHC event on the day of the race.
  • Tickets must be purchased online in advance at ppihc.org. Prices increased after June 1, so purchasing now gets you the current rate.

If you're planning a Father's Day outing and haven't bought tickets yet, do that today.

Planning Tips for Families

The mountain spectator areas require hiking on rocky terrain. Wear sturdy shoes and bring layers — conditions at higher elevations can shift quickly even on warm summer days. Bring water, sunscreen, and snacks. Parking and shuttle logistics are explained on ppihc.org; review those before Race Day to avoid delays.

For families with very young children, Fan Fest on June 19 offers the most accessible experience — flat ground downtown, a long event window, and no altitude concerns. It's a genuine event, not just a promotional tent setup.
