Rock Ledge Ranch Closes Saturday: Last Week for Hands-On Colorado History in COS

By Sarah Morales | June 8, 2026

If you've been thinking about taking the family to Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site, this is the week. The summer programming closes for the season on Saturday, June 14, and this is the last opportunity for Colorado Springs families to experience the site's living history activities until next season.

Rock Ledge Ranch is open this week Tuesday through Saturday, 10 AM to 5 PM, at Garden of the Gods Park on the northwest side of Colorado Springs.

Four Eras of Colorado History in One Visit

What makes Rock Ledge Ranch different from most historic sites is the depth and variety of what it covers. The experience moves through four distinct periods of Colorado history, each brought to life through demonstrations, hands-on activities, and structures that have been preserved or reconstructed on the property.

The journey starts with Native American trade: interpretive staff demonstrate traditional practices and help visitors understand how trade networks worked across the region before European settlement. From there, visitors move through the Homestead Cabin area, where families can try making a cornhusk doll — a hands-on activity that works well for children across a wide age range.

The 1880s Garden and surrounding homestead area shows what daily life looked like for pioneer families in Colorado during the late 19th century. The 1870s Rock Ledge House is one of the original structures on the property, giving visitors a sense of how families lived during the early years of Colorado statehood. And the working blacksmith demonstration is often the highlight for younger visitors — watching metal shaped at a forge is genuinely engaging in a way that a museum exhibit is not.

Victorian-era games are also available on the grounds, giving kids something active to do between stops.

Practical Details

Rock Ledge Ranch is located at Garden of the Gods Park in Colorado Springs. The site is a City of Colorado Springs historic property. Admission is charged; current rates and any age-based discounts are available at coloradosprings.gov/rock-ledge-ranch.

The site operates Tuesday through Saturday during this final week. Allow at least two to three hours for a full experience of all four historical areas, though families with younger children may move through more quickly depending on what captures their attention.

What's Next

Rock Ledge Ranch's summer programming closes Saturday, June 14. After that, families interested in outdoor COS activities can look ahead to the expanded Blodgett Open Space trails opening later this summer and PPLD's Summer Adventure program (free, through July 31 at all 14 PPLD locations). Full Rock Ledge Ranch information including hours, admission, and directions is at coloradosprings.gov/rock-ledge-ranch.
