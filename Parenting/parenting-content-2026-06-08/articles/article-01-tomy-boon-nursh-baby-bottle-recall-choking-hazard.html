TOMY Boon NURSH Baby Bottle Recall: Choking Hazard Affects 40,000 Bottles Sold at Walmart

By Jamie Rivera | June 8, 2026

TOMY International recalled approximately 40,000 Boon NURSH 8 oz Reusable Baby Bottles on June 4, 2026, after the outer plastic shell was found to bubble and peel off, creating loose fragments that pose a choking hazard to infants. The bottles were sold exclusively at Walmart stores and Walmart.com between November 2025 and May 2026.

If you purchased these bottles for a baby in your household, stop using them immediately.

What Was Recalled

The recalled product is the Boon NURSH 8 oz Reusable Baby Bottles 3-Pack in pink tie-dye. The bottles feature a hard outer plastic shell that encases a soft silicone interior pouch. According to the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission, the outer shell can bubble or partially peel off during normal use, producing loose, film-like plastic pieces that an infant could choke on.

Families can identify whether they have the recalled product by checking the item number and UPC printed on the bottom of the packaging: item number B11654 and UPC 669028116546. The bottles sold for approximately $20 per 3-pack.

What Families Should Do

Stop using the bottles immediately. Do not continue feeding your baby from a recalled bottle, even if the outer shell appears intact — the hazard can develop during normal use.

To receive a remedy, families have two options. TOMY is offering a $22 store credit to booninc.com, or a replacement set of three bottles in a different color. To request either option, submit a photograph of the bottles with the word "RECALL" written on them and certify that the bottles have been disposed of before submitting the claim.

Parents can contact TOMY at 866-725-4407, Monday through Friday from 8 AM to 4 PM Central time. The company can also be reached by email at cs@tomy.com. The full recall information and claim process is available at recall.tomy.com/nursh.
