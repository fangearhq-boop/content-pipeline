Summer Slide Prevention: What Colorado Springs Parents Need to Know in 2026

By Jamie Rivera | June 8, 2026

School just ended for most Colorado Springs families. The weeks ahead are full of camps, parks, screen time, and long unstructured days — and research consistently shows that without some intentional planning, children lose a significant portion of what they learned during the school year over summer break.

Here's what the evidence says about summer learning loss, which children are most vulnerable, and what parents in Colorado Springs can actually do about it — including several free local options available right now.

What the Research Shows

Studies published by Scholastic and cited by Mayo Clinic Health System show that children lose an average of 25 to 30 percent of their school-year learning over summer break. That loss is not evenly distributed across subjects or ages. Math experiences more severe summer learning loss than reading, and younger children are more vulnerable than older students. Researchers have estimated that up to 48.5 points of the achievement gap across five elementary school years can be linked to cumulative summer learning loss.

The good news, as Scientific American has noted, is that children do recover. Learning loss is real, but it is not permanent — and the strategies that reduce it are simpler than most families expect.

Practical Strategies That Work

The most effective approaches share a common trait: they do not feel like homework. Consistency matters more than intensity. A few minutes most days produces better outcomes than a long session once a week.

Reading is the most accessible intervention. Children who read for 20 minutes a day over summer maintain significantly more vocabulary and comprehension than those who do not read at all. The subject and difficulty level matter less than the habit — let children pick what they want to read, including graphic novels, magazines, and books that may seem too easy.

For math, everyday activities work well. Cooking involves fractions, measurement, and multiplication. Grocery shopping introduces estimation and money skills. Measuring things around the house for a project, calculating a tip at a restaurant, or figuring out how many days until a family trip all engage mathematical thinking without requiring a workbook.

For families who want more structure, the Mayo Clinic Health System recommends setting aside 20 to 30 minutes of intentional academic review three to four times per week. Short, consistent sessions are significantly more effective than sporadic longer ones.

Local Colorado Springs Resources

Colorado Springs families have access to several free or low-cost options this summer.

The Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program runs through July 31, 2026, at all 14 PPLD locations and 2 bookmobiles. The program is free, open to all ages, and requires no registration. Participants track 30 days of activities using the Beanstack app. Grand prizes include a Broadmoor Cloud Camp stay and a Pikes Peak Attractions VIP pass. More information is at ppld.org/summer.

The PPYMCA is running Summer Day Camp through June, with Week 2 in progress this week (June 8–12, "Low-Key Famous Artist Week"). Mini camps continue through June and July, including Cheer Camp (June 15–18) and Theatre Camp (June 22–25), for children ages 5–14. Registration and schedules are at ppymca.org.

For something hands-on, Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site runs its summer programming through Saturday, June 14 — this is the final week. The site offers living history activities including a working blacksmith, cornhusk doll making, and pioneer-era demonstrations for all ages. Located at Garden of the Gods Park. Check coloradosprings.gov for admission details and hours before visiting.

What's Next

Summer learning loss is a well-documented phenomenon, but it is also preventable with consistent, low-effort habits. For families looking to build a summer routine, the PPLD Summer Adventure program (ppld.org/summer) and PPYMCA camps (ppymca.org) are both easy starting points. Rock Ledge Ranch closes for the season this Saturday, June 14, so this week is the last chance for that program.
