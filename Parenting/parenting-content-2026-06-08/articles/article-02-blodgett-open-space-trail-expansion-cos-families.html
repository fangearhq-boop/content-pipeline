Blodgett Open Space Trail Expansion: 14 New Miles Coming This Summer for COS Families

By Sarah Morales | June 8, 2026

Colorado Springs families who enjoy outdoor time near the Rockrimmon area have something to look forward to this summer. The City of Colorado Springs is completing a major expansion at Blodgett Open Space, with approximately 14 miles of new and improved trails expected to be finished by summer 2026.

The project nearly doubles the usable trail system at the 384-acre open space, which borders Pike National Forest on the city's northwest side.

What's Being Added

The expansion divides the open space into two distinct use areas. The southern half, called the Blodgett Bike Hill area, will include mountain bike-only trails — technical terrain suited for riders looking for dedicated singletrack close to the city. The northern half will be developed for hiking-only and multi-use trails, with a new trailhead and parking area at the base of the old quarry.

For families with younger children or those new to hiking, the lower sections of Blodgett Peak Open Space already offer accessible options. The lower Blodgett Trail winds through the hillside with manageable elevation gain and offers wide views of the Colorado Springs cityscape below. The Chickadee Trail loops for approximately half a mile — a good choice for families with younger kids or those who want a short, scenic outing without a strenuous climb.

Blodgett Open Space is free and open to the public. Parking is available at the trailhead off Vindicator Drive in the Rockrimmon area.

Trackchair Accessible Hikes Expanding

The City of Colorado Springs is also expanding its Trackchair program for the 2026 season. The program offers guided hikes using all-terrain electric wheelchairs for residents who have difficulty with standard trails. In 2026, accessible guided hikes will be offered twice daily, Wednesday through Sunday, at Blodgett Open Space and other city locations.

Families with members who use mobility devices or who want an accessible outdoor experience can find details on the Trackchair program at coloradosprings.gov. Reservations are typically required.

Why This Matters for Summer Families

With school out in Colorado Springs, free, nearby outdoor options take on extra value. Blodgett Open Space is one of the city's most accessible natural areas — close to the northwest side of the city, with room for both casual walks and more challenging hikes. The new trail additions will give families more routes to choose from, and the dedicated mountain bike trails mean parents can bring older kids looking for something more technical without leaving the city limits.

The Trackchair expansion is also a meaningful addition for families who have been looking for genuinely accessible Colorado outdoor experiences.

What's Next

Trail construction at Blodgett Open Space is expected to be complete close to summertime 2026. Check the City of Colorado Springs parks and trails page at coloradosprings.gov for updates on trail completion and the new trailhead opening. Current trail maps are also available through AllTrails and the Colorado Springs Mountain Bike Alliance at cosmba.org. Trackchair program information is at coloradosprings.gov.
