TOMY Boon NURSH Baby Bottle Recall — Choking Hazard, 40,000 Units at Walmart - **Tier:** 1 - **Angle:** TOMY International recalled approximately 40,000 Boon NURSH 8 oz Reusable Baby Bottles on June 4, 2026 due to a choking hazard. The hard outer plastic shell can bubble and peel, creating loose film-like pieces that pose a choking risk to infants. Sold exclusively at Walmart and Walmart.com, Nov 2025–May 2026, approximately $20 for a 3-pack. Item number B11654. 135 reports of peeling/bubbling; no injuries. Parents should stop use immediately. Remedy: $22 store credit at booninc.com OR replacement set; contact TOMY at 866-725-4407 or recall.tomy.com/nursh.

Blodgett Open Space Trail Expansion Nearly Complete — 14 Miles of New Trails This Summer - **Tier:** 1 - **Angle:** The City of Colorado Springs is completing a major expansion at Blodgett Open Space — 14 miles of new and improved trails expected to be finished by summer 2026. The 384-acre open space borders Pike National Forest near the Rockrimmon area. New additions include mountain bike-only trails in the southern section and hiking/multi-use trails in the northern section, with a new trailhead and parking lot. Family-friendly lower trails (Blodgett Trail, Chickadee Trail half-mile loop) are already accessible. The Trackchair program expands this season to 2 guided hikes daily (Wed–Sun) at Blodgett among other locations.

Summer Slide Is Real — How COS Families Can Keep Kids Learning This Summer
NOT VERIFIEDBRIEF
Summer Slide Is Real — How COS Families Can Keep Kids Learning This Summer - **Tier:** 1 - **Angle:** Studies show kids lose 25–30% of school-year learning over summer, with math hit hardest and younger children most vulnerable. With schools out in Colorado Springs, local families face the classic summer challenge. Practical tips backed by research: 20–30 minutes of structured review 3–4 times per week, daily reading (20 min), math in everyday activities (cooking, shopping), plus local COS resources — PPYMCA summer programs, PPLD Summer Adventure (through July 31), Rock Ledge Ranch (this week through June 14). Evidence-based, local-focused guidance.
