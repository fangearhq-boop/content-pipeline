### STORY 1: AAP vs. CDC 2026 Vaccine Schedule Split — What COS Parents Need to Know
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Tier:** 1
- **Posting Window:** 10:00 AM MT
- **Angle:** The American Academy of Pediatrics published its 2026 immunization schedule recommending vaccines against 18 diseases — while the CDC's revised schedule covers only 11. The first meaningful split between the two bodies in years. Vaccines the AAP kept that CDC dropped include hepatitis A, hepatitis B, meningococcal disease, rotavirus, flu, and RSV. CDC changes were made under HHS Secretary RFK Jr. without convening the Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices and without new supporting data. AAP is backed by 12 medical organizations including the AMA. Key message for COS parents: ask their child's pediatrician which schedule the practice follows. Sources: AJMC, The Hill, HealthyChildren.org, AAP News.

### STORY 2: Kith Retail Children's Plaid Loungewear Recalled — Burn Hazard
- **Content Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Tier:** 1
- **Posting Window:** 12:00 PM MT
- **Angle:** CPSC recalled Kith Retail "Baby Kithmas" and "Kids Kithmas" plaid lounge sets (long-sleeved button-front shirt + matching pants, black/red plaid, "Kith" embroidered) due to burn hazard — the woven fabric fails mandatory federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear. Sizes 9–12 months through XL (14/16). About 130 sets sold at Kith stores and kith.com from December 2025 through January 2026 for approximately $75. Remedy: destroy the set by cutting it in half, photograph the destruction, email recalls@kithnyc.com for full refund. No injuries reported. Official CPSC recall announced June 2026. Source: CPSC.gov.
