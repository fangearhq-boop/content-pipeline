### STORY 1: VEVOR Baby Swings Recalled for Suffocation Hazard

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

⚠️ Parents, there's an important recall to share today.

CPSC has recalled approximately 1,020 VEVOR baby swings due to a serious suffocation hazard. The swings — models BB501K, BB702A, and BB005K — recline at more than 10 degrees, which violates the federal Safe Sleep for Babies Act. The law bans inclined infant sleepers because babies can slide into a position that cuts off their airway.

These swings were sold on VEVOR.com and Amazon between January and August 2025, priced between $65 and $80.

If you have one of these swings, the steps are specific: stop using it immediately, cut the swing cover completely in half, write "Recalled" on the base with a permanent marker, and then email a photo to recalling@vevor.com. Do not donate the swing. You can also call VEVOR customer service at 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday 9AM–5PM PT.

Full details are at cpsc.gov, and we've got the complete story on the site. 🔗

### STORY 2: Uuoeebb Infant Walkers Recalled for Three Simultaneous Safety Failures

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

There's a second recall to flag today, and this one has three separate safety failures. 😟

CPSC has recalled approximately 2,650 Uuoeebb infant walkers because they have three documented hazards at the same time: the walkers are wide enough to roll straight through a standard doorway, they don't stop at step edges, and the leg openings are large enough for a baby's head to pass through — a serious entrapment risk.

These walkers were sold only on Amazon between December 2024 and September 2025, for between $60 and $90. They come in gray, black, and pink. If you're not sure whether yours is the recalled model, look for a tracking label under the seat — recalled units say "Production Batch: 7654."

The manufacturer has not responded to CPSC, so there's no direct refund process. Here's what CPSC says to do: stop using the walker immediately, disassemble it, remove the fabric seat, and write "Recalled" on the tray with a permanent marker. Then email a photo of the disassembled walker to Uuoeebbrecalls@outlook.com.

Full details at cpsc.gov. We know it's a lot — two recalls in one day — but we'd rather you have this information than not.

Have your kiddos ever used a baby walker? What made you choose it or skip it?

### STORY 3: Portal Pool Closed All Summer — A 1945 Coal Mine Shaft Is Why

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

If your family has been counting on Portal Pool this summer, here's what you need to know. 🏊

Portal Pool at Portal Park (3535 N. Hancock Ave) is closed for the entire summer — and city officials say it won't be coming back for years. The reason is a coal mine shaft from 1945 that sits as little as 25 feet under the pool. The city received a federal grant to investigate, with geotechnical work expected to begin by the end of 2026 and a second phase in 2027. Until that investigation is done and any remediation is complete, the pool stays closed.

That's a real gap for families on the north side of Colorado Springs. The good news is there are other options this summer. Wilson Ranch Pool is one public outdoor option. Monument Valley Pool is operating through a YMCA partnership this summer — day passes run $10 for youth, $15 for adults, and $45 for the whole family. If you want year-round indoor access, Cottonwood Creek Family Center YMCA has you covered.

Spray grounds are also still running at Deerfield Hills, Venezia Park, and Uncle Wilber Fountain.

We have more detail on all of these options in the full article linked below.

### STORY 4: D11 Considers $775M School Bond for November Ballot

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM MT

If your kids are in D11 schools, there's a big community decision on the horizon.

D11's board is weighing whether to put a $775 million bond on the November 2026 ballot. The district's average school building is 58 years old, and the identified repair needs alone exceed $422 million. The bond would fund building repairs and system replacements, air conditioning (many D11 schools still don't have it), safety and security upgrades, school modernizations, and new construction. Every school in the district would receive at least $1 million in investment.

The tax impact would be roughly $6.50 per month for every $100,000 of assessed property value, with the mill levy moving from 37.7 to 46.3.

Here's something that surprised us: the bond would also enable a $10,000 annual pay increase for every D11 teacher — from first-year educators to 30-year veterans. Community polling shows about 54–58% support right now. The board needs to vote by the end of summer to put the measure on the November ballot.

If you want to weigh in, the district has a community input page at d112026bondplanning.com.

What would you need to see in this bond proposal to vote yes — or what would make you vote no?

### STORY 5: 1 in 5 US Teens Use AI Chatbots for Mental Health Advice

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 PM MT

Here's a conversation worth having at your dinner table tonight. 💬

A study published in JAMA Pediatrics this month found that 1 in 5 American adolescents and young adults — roughly 8.2 million young people between ages 12 and 21 — have used AI chatbots like ChatGPT, Gemini, Character.AI, or Meta AI for mental health advice. That's a more than 40% jump from just a year ago.

What struck us most: 63% of those teens hadn't disclosed this to anyone. Not a parent, not a counselor, not a friend. They're quietly turning to AI when they're struggling — and 43% of them are doing it at least once a month.

The researchers aren't saying chatbots are automatically harmful. But they do recommend that parents and clinicians start these conversations proactively, before teens establish chatbot use as their go-to for mental health support rather than reaching out to a real person or a trained professional.

The article on the site has more on the study findings and some conversation-starter ideas for families.

Have you talked to your teens about how they're using AI? And would you be comfortable if they turned to a chatbot when they were feeling overwhelmed?
