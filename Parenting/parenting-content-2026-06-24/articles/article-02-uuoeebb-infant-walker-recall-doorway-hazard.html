Uuoeebb Infant Walkers Recalled for Three Simultaneous Safety Failures

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | June 24, 2026

A baby walker sold exclusively on Amazon has been recalled for not one or two, but three simultaneous safety hazards: the walker is wide enough to roll through standard doorways, it fails to stop at the edge of steps, and its leg openings are large enough for a baby's head to pass through and become entrapped. If you have an Uuoeebb infant walker at home, stop using it immediately.

CPSC has recalled approximately 2,650 of these walkers, made by BaoD (also identified as Hunan Suihuo E-commerce Co., Ltd., based in China). They were sold only on Amazon between December 2024 and September 2025, at prices ranging from $60 to $90.

Three Hazards Explained

Each of the three safety failures documented by CPSC carries independent risk of serious injury to an infant:

Doorway width: Federal standards require baby walkers to be too wide to fit through a standard interior doorway. This prevents mobile infants from reaching dangerous areas — stairs, bathroom floors, kitchens — that parents haven't secured. The Uuoeebb walkers do not meet this width requirement.

Step detection: Federal standards also require that walkers stop — or at minimum resist — when they reach the edge of a step, carpet ridge, or threshold. The Uuoeebb walkers fail this requirement, meaning an infant can roll directly off a step.

Head entrapment: The openings for a baby's legs are large enough for an infant's head to pass through, creating an entrapment hazard.

How to Identify the Recalled Walkers

The recalled walkers have a collapsible round base, 8 wheels, and 7 height settings. They're available in gray, black, and pink. The key identifier is on a tracking label located under the seat — recalled units will display "Production Batch: 7654".

What to Do: Step-by-Step Instructions

Because the manufacturer, BaoD, has been unresponsive to CPSC, there is no refund process through the company. Consumers are directed to complete the recall as follows:

  1. Stop using the walker immediately.
  2. Disassemble the walker completely.
  3. Remove the fabric seat.
  4. Write "Recalled" on the tray with a permanent marker.
  5. Take a photo of the disassembled walker showing the marked tray.
  6. Email that photo to Uuoeebbrecalls@outlook.com.
  7. Dispose of the walker according to your local and state disposal rules.

There is no phone number for this recall because the manufacturer has not engaged with CPSC. The email address above is the designated contact point.

What's Next

The full recall notice, including product photos to help with identification, is available at cpsc.gov. If you purchased this walker through Amazon, check your order history from December 2024 through September 2025.

Baby walker safety has been a long-standing concern for pediatric safety advocates. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that parents avoid mobile baby walkers entirely and use stationary activity centers instead, citing the documented injury patterns from walker falls. If you're looking for an alternative, stationary activity centers, play mats, and supported sitting options provide developmental benefits without the mobility hazards.
