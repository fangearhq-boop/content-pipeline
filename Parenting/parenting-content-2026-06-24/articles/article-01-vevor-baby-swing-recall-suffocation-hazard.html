VEVOR Baby Swings Recalled for Suffocation Hazard: What Parents Need to Do Now

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | June 24, 2026

If you have a VEVOR baby swing at home, stop using it now. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has announced a recall of approximately 1,020 VEVOR baby swings due to a suffocation hazard — the swings recline at too steep an angle, violating federal safety standards designed to prevent infant deaths.

The affected models are BB501K, BB702A, and BB005K. They were sold on VEVOR.com and Amazon between January and August 2025, priced between $65 and $80.

What Makes These Swings Dangerous

The recall centers on the swing's incline. Federal law — specifically the Safe Sleep for Babies Act — bans inclined infant sleepers because a steep recline can cause a baby to slump forward or sideways into a position that restricts the airway. The mandatory standard requires inclined infant sleep products to be at no more than 10 degrees. CPSC found these VEVOR swings exceed that limit.

No injuries had been reported at the time of the recall announcement, but CPSC issues recalls before incidents occur when a product poses a known hazard. The agency and Sanven Technology Co. — the China-based manufacturer that sells under the VEVOR brand — are urging all owners to act immediately.

How to Identify the Recalled Swings

The model number is printed on the product itself. If you purchased a baby swing from VEVOR.com or Amazon in the first eight months of 2025 in that price range, check immediately.

What to Do: Step-by-Step Instructions

CPSC's instructions for this recall are specific. Follow all of them:

  1. Stop using the swing immediately.
  2. Cut the swing cover completely in half.
  3. Write "Recalled" on the swing base with a permanent marker.
  4. Take a photo that shows both the cut cover and the marked base.
  5. Email that photo to recalling@vevor.com.
  6. Dispose of the swing according to your local and state disposal rules.
  7. Do not donate the swing or pass it on to another family.

You can also contact VEVOR customer service directly at 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday, 9AM to 5PM PT, for assistance completing the recall process.

What's Next

If you're unsure whether your swing is affected or need more information, the official recall page is available at cpsc.gov — search for VEVOR baby swing. CPSC maintains complete records of all recall details, including the remediation process.

If you purchased the swing on Amazon, check your order history from January through August 2025. Amazon has also been known to send direct recall notifications to buyers when it has purchase records on file, but don't wait for a notification — if you have this swing, act today.
