Portal Pool Is Closed for Years — Here's Where to Swim in Colorado Springs This Summer

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | June 24, 2026

If your family has been heading to Portal Pool for summer swimming, here's the reality check: it's not opening this summer, and city officials say it won't be back for years. The pool at Portal Park, 3535 North Hancock Ave, is closed indefinitely while the city investigates a coal mine shaft from 1945 that sits as little as 25 feet beneath it.

The closure was reported by the Colorado Springs Gazette in May 2026. The city received a federal grant to fund a geotechnical investigation, with Phase 1 expected to begin by the end of 2026. Phase 2 is slated for 2027. Until the investigation is complete and any necessary remediation is done, the pool stays closed — and city officials haven't given a timeline for when it might reopen, if ever.

That leaves families on the north side of Colorado Springs looking for alternatives. Here's where you can swim this summer.

Free Spray Grounds Are Still Available

We covered Colorado Springs spray grounds in detail earlier this month, but they're worth mentioning here for families who want a free, no-reservations option. Spray grounds at Deerfield Hills, Venezia Park, and Uncle Wilber Fountain are all operating this summer. These aren't pools — kids can't swim laps or dive — but for toddlers and younger kids especially, they're a great option on a hot day without the cost or logistics of a pool visit.

About the Mine Shaft Under Portal Pool

The coal mine shaft underneath Portal Park dates to 1945 — part of the coal mining history of the Pikes Peak region that most Colorado Springs families don't think about day-to-day. The shaft sits as little as 25 feet below the pool's surface, which is close enough to require thorough investigation before any major structure can safely operate above it.

The city's plan is to use the federal grant for a full geotechnical survey of the site. Depending on what that investigation finds, remediation options could range from shaft stabilization to more significant ground treatment — or, potentially, a finding that the site isn't suitable for a pool at all. The city hasn't specified what "years from returning" means in concrete timeline terms, which is part of why planning around this pool for future summers isn't advisable.

What's Next

The geotechnical investigation for Portal Park is expected to begin by the end of 2026. Phase 2 is planned for 2027. The city has not announced any community briefings or updates on the investigation timeline yet.

For current information on Colorado Springs public pools and spray grounds, visit the Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation & Cultural Services website. For YMCA facility information and pricing, ppymca.org has up-to-date schedules and day pass options.
