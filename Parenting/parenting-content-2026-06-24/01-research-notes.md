## S1: VEVOR Baby Swings Recall

**Source:** cpsc.gov
- Recall announced: June 2026
- Units: ~1,020
- Manufacturer/Importer: Sanven Technology Co., Ltd. (China) / VEVOR brand
- Models: BB501K (white/gray white base), BB702A (white/gray gray base), BB005K (black/gray black base)
- Sold: VEVOR.com + Amazon, January–August 2025
- Price: $65–$80
- Hazard: Incline exceeds 10° — violates ASTM F3118-23a (mandatory standard for infant sleep products) + Safe Sleep for Babies Act inclined sleepers ban
- Incidents/injuries: None reported at time of recall
- Consumer action:
  1. Stop use immediately
  2. Cut swing cover in half
  3. Write "Recalled" on swing base with permanent marker
  4. Email photo of cut cover + "Recalled" base to recalling@vevor.com
  5. Dispose per local/state laws
  6. Do NOT donate
- Contact: 855-599-6320 Mon–Fri 9AM–5PM PT
- CPSC recall page: cpsc.gov

## S2: Uuoeebb Infant Walkers Recall

**Source:** cpsc.gov
- Recall announced: June 2026
- Units: ~2,650
- Manufacturer: BaoD / Hunan Suihuo E-commerce Co., Ltd. (China)
- Description: Collapsible round base, 8 wheels, 7 height settings; available in gray, black, pink
- Identifier: "Production Batch: 7654" on tracking label under seat

  1. Stop use immediately
  2. Disassemble walker
  3. Remove fabric seat
  4. Write "Recalled" on tray in permanent marker
  5. Email photo to Uuoeebbrecalls@outlook.com

## S5: Teens Using AI Chatbots for Mental Health Advice

**Sources:** JAMA Pediatrics (June 10, 2026), RAND Corporation press release
- Study: Published in JAMA Pediatrics, June 10, 2026
- Population: US adolescents and young adults ages 12–21
- Finding: 1 in 5 (approximately 19.2%) used AI chatbots for mental health advice
- Year-over-year change: up from 13.1% to 19.2% — more than 40% increase
- Total estimated users: ~8.2 million young people
- Platforms used: ChatGPT, Gemini, Character.AI, Meta AI
- Disclosure: 63% had not disclosed chatbot mental health use to anyone
- Frequency: 43% sought chatbot advice at least monthly
- Researcher recommendations:
  - Clinicians should proactively ask patients about AI chatbot use
  - Parents should initiate conversations before teens turn to chatbots
  - Chatbot use should be linked to evidence-based care pathways, not treated as a substitute
- Note: Study does not claim chatbots are harmful — notes this is a new behavior pattern that clinicians and parents should be aware of
