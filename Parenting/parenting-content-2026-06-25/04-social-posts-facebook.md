### STORY 1: GOPO Toys Recall — Long-Form Post

⚠️ URGENT RECALL: If your baby or toddler has a GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toy, stop using it right now.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission announced the recall of approximately 70,410 of these toys on June 18. The silicone pull strings are smaller and longer than allowed by safety standards — and they can reach the back of a baby's throat, causing respiratory distress and choking. Three incidents have already been reported.

Here's what the toy looks like: it's an off-white disc with a grey ball in the center and six multi-colored silicone "tentacle" pull strings. It was sold on Amazon.com from August 2023 through March 2026 for $11 to $15.

Here's exactly what to do:
1. Stop use immediately
2. Cut ALL silicone strings off the toy
3. Write "DESTROYED" on the main body in permanent marker
4. Take a photo of the destroyed toy
5. Email the photo to recalls@gopotoys.com for a full refund
6. Dispose of the product

Please share this with any parent or grandparent who might have bought one of these — three years on the market means a lot of these are out there. 💙

Have you seen this toy before? Let us know in the comments if you have one.

### STORY 4: BLA/ACCEL Transition — Long-Form Post

If you're a D49 parent and you've heard about the WARN letter at Banning Lewis Academy — take a breath. The school is NOT closing. Here's what's actually going on. 🏫

BLA has been managed by ACCEL Schools, a national company, but that contract is ending June 30, 2026. The transition happened because BLA selected a new management company through a competitive process — and that company then withdrew. So BLA is taking a practical step: they're going to directly employ their staff going forward, with additional partners helping on HR, finance, and operations.

The WARN letter that went out on April 30 covers 246 ACCEL employees (195 regular staff, 51 on-call/substitute workers) — because their employment was technically with ACCEL, not BLA. That's the legal notice required when a company-level staffing change happens.

What's NOT changing:
✅ BLA campuses remain open
✅ No reduction in programming or student services
✅ The 2026-2027 school year will open as planned

This is a behind-the-scenes management change, not a school crisis. If you have questions, the best source of current information is blracademy.org.

### STORY 5: Summer Screen Time — Long-Form Post

We're officially in the season when screen time debates go into overdrive — so let's talk about what the research actually says, because the guidance has changed. 📱

First, the big headline: in 2026, the American Academy of Pediatrics moved away from strict hour-based screen time limits. Instead, they shifted to an individualized, content-focused approach. It's not just "how many hours" but "what kind of content and in what context."

Here's what the data looks like right now:
📊 68% of kids use screens significantly more during summer when school structure is gone
📊 Children ages 8–10 average 6 hours of screen time per day
📊 Teens average more than 8 hours per day

The type of content matters a lot:
🚩 Higher concern: Passive, fast-paced, solo entertainment — especially social media for teens and fast-paced video for kids under 8
✅ Lower concern: Creative, interactive, and educational screen use at any age

The research consistently shows that the most helpful thing parents can do is have a proactive conversation before summer starts — not mid-August when habits are already set — and establish consistent routines even without school structure.
