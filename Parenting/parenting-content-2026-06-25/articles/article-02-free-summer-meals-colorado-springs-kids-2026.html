Free Summer Meals for Colorado Springs Kids in 2026: Sites, Times, and How to Find Them

By Sarah Morales

June 25, 2026

Free breakfast and lunch are available for children and teens throughout Colorado Springs this summer — and there's no registration, no income verification, and no paperwork required. Just show up.

The Summer Food Service Program (SFSP) is a federally funded program that makes free meals available to anyone 18 years and younger during the summer months. Colorado has more than 600 participating sites statewide, and several are located right in Colorado Springs city parks.

Colorado Springs Park Sites (Through July 31)

Three city parks are currently serving meals on weekdays through July 31:

Location Address Meal Times
Memorial Park 1605 E. Pikes Peak Ave. 11–11:30 AM and 11:45 AM–12:15 PM
Palmer Park 3650 Maizeland Rd Lunch — weekdays
Panorama Splash Park 4550 Fenton Rd Lunch — weekdays

All three locations began service on May 27 and run through July 31, Monday through Friday. Memorial Park operates two lunch seatings to accommodate more kids and families.

D11 Mobile Meal Unit

Colorado Springs School District 11 operates a mobile meal unit that parks at East Library every weekday throughout June and July. If the park sites aren't convenient for your family, this is another consistent option that follows a set schedule.

How to Find Sites Near You

The easiest way to locate a meal site close to home is through kidsfoodfinder.org. Enter your zip code and the tool will display all participating sites nearby with their addresses and serving times.

With more than 600 sites operating across Colorado, there's a good chance there's one within a short drive of wherever you are in the metro area.

Who Qualifies

Anyone 18 years old or younger qualifies. That includes students who aren't enrolled in summer school, teenagers, and children who attend private schools or are homeschooled. There are no eligibility requirements to verify and nothing to bring except an appetite.

The program serves breakfast at some sites and lunch at others. The park locations listed above serve lunch. Check kidsfoodfinder.org for full details at each site, including which meals are available.

What's Next

Meals are available now through July 31 at the Colorado Springs park sites listed above. Bookmark kidsfoodfinder.org to find the full list of statewide sites as the summer progresses — sites can be added throughout the season. For more information on Colorado's Summer Food Service Program, visit the Colorado Department of Education at cde.state.co.us.
