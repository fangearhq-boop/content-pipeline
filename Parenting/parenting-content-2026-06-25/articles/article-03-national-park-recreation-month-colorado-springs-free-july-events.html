National Park and Recreation Month 2026: Free Events All July in Colorado Springs

By Jamie Rivera

June 25, 2026

July is National Park and Recreation Month, and the City of Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services (PRCS) department is marking it with a full month of free events and activities for families.

From outdoor music nights to citywide contests, there's something on the calendar for just about every age group. Here's what's coming up.

Two Free Evening Events

Two signature events anchor the month's programming:

Silent Disco at Panorama Park — Friday, July 11

The Silent Disco brings the outdoors and music together in one of the city's most popular parks. Attendees use wireless headphones to listen to the music — a format that creates a unique experience and keeps the neighborhood quiet. Youth activities including kickball and basketball games will be available at the event.

Music in the Park at Piñon Valley — Friday, July 25

Music in the Park at Piñon Valley is a free outdoor concert on one of the west side's favorite green spaces. Families can bring blankets and enjoy an evening of live music, with youth activities including kickball and basketball on site.

Both events are free and open to the public. Additional details including start times will be posted as the events approach at coloradosprings.gov/parkandrecmonth.

All-Month Activities

Beyond the two evening events, PRCS has several activities running throughout July:

"Build Your Own Park" Design Challenge

Kids are invited to design their dream park on paper — a creative exercise that gets young people thinking about public space and what they'd want in their own neighborhood. Details on how to submit designs are available on the PRCS website.

Discover COS Calendar Photo Contest

The annual Discover COS Calendar photo contest invites Colorado Springs photographers — including families who love capturing the city's parks and landscapes — to submit photos for consideration in the official calendar. If your family has been documenting summer adventures around the city, this is the contest for you.

Park and Recreation Month Coloring Contest

The coloring contest is open to younger kids and offers a chance to have artwork featured on the city's social media channels. It's a low-key way to get creative while celebrating the parks that make Colorado Springs such a livable place.

Colorado Springs Park System

Colorado Springs operates more than 160 neighborhood parks, and the PRCS department serves more than 300,000 residents annually. National Park and Recreation Month is a good reminder of how much park infrastructure the city maintains — and an invitation to use parks you might not have visited yet.

What's Next

Visit coloradosprings.gov/parkandrecmonth for event details, submission guidelines for the contests, and any updates to the July schedule. The two evening events — Silent Disco on July 11 and Music in the Park on July 25 — are free, but check the page for start times as July gets closer.
