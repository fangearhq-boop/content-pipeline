Banning Lewis Academy Is Ending Its ACCEL Contract: What D49 Parents Need to Know

By Sarah Morales

June 25, 2026

If you're a parent with children at Banning Lewis Academy and you've seen news about a WARN letter and hundreds of affected employees — you're not alone in wanting to know what's going on. The short answer: the school is not closing, and the 2026-2027 school year will open as planned.

Here's a plain-language explanation of what's changing, what triggered the WARN letter, and what it means for BLA families going into next school year.

What Is ACCEL Schools?

Banning Lewis Academy is a charter school in District 49, and like many charter schools, it has been managed by an outside company rather than running all of its own administrative and operational functions in-house. That company is ACCEL Schools, a national charter management organization.

Under this arrangement, many of the staff at BLA — teachers, administrators, and support employees — were technically employed by ACCEL, not by BLA itself. ACCEL handled management, HR, and operational functions in exchange for a contracted fee. BLA had previously allocated approximately $2 million per year for ACCEL's services, including $1.5 million in management fees.

Why Is the Contract Ending?

The transition started when BLA went through a competitive process to select a new charter management organization (CMO) to replace ACCEL. A new provider was selected — but then withdrew from the agreement. Faced with that outcome, BLA made the decision to move forward without a management company and employ its staff directly.

The contract with ACCEL ends June 30, 2026.

What Was the WARN Letter?

On April 30, 2026, a WARN letter was issued affecting 246 ACCEL employees: 195 regular staff and 51 on-call or substitute workers, including substitute teachers. A WARN (Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification) letter is a federally required notice when a company-level layoff or employment change affects a certain number of workers.

The critical distinction: these employees worked at BLA but were employed by ACCEL. When ACCEL's contract ends, their employment with ACCEL ends. BLA is separately bringing on staff directly — meaning many of the same people may continue working at the school under BLA's employment, not ACCEL's.

What Isn't Changing

BLA has been clear about what this transition does not affect:

  • No BLA campuses are closing.
  • No programming is being reduced.
  • No student services are being cut.
  • The 2026-2027 school year will open as planned.

The school is also bringing on additional support partners to help with HR, finance, and operations as it builds out its own internal capacity — the functions that ACCEL previously handled.

What This Looks Like Going Forward

Starting July 1, 2026, Banning Lewis Academy will operate under direct local control. Staff will be employed by BLA rather than by an outside management company. The school has framed this as a move toward independence and local decision-making.

For most BLA students, the day-to-day experience at school should be unchanged. The change is administrative — who signs the paychecks and manages the HR function — not educational.

What's Next

If you have specific questions about staffing, enrollment, or programs at Banning Lewis Academy for the 2026-2027 school year, the best place to get current information is the school's official website at blracademy.org. The school has confirmed the year opens as planned and encourages parents to reach out directly with any concerns.
