GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toy Recall: What COS Parents Need to Do Now

By Jamie Rivera

June 25, 2026

If your baby or toddler has a GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toy, stop using it right now. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) announced the recall of approximately 70,410 of these toys on June 18, 2026, after three reported incidents in which the toy's silicone strings reached the back of a child's throat — causing respiratory distress and choking.

The toy was sold exclusively on Amazon.com from August 2023 through March 2026, meaning it's been in homes for up to three years. If you bought one — or if one was gifted to your family — here's everything you need to know.

What the Toy Looks Like

The GOPO Toys Pull String Teething Toy is described as an off-white disc shape with a grey ball in the center. It has six multi-colored silicone "tentacle"-style pull strings that run through the ball and seven soft push buttons on the disc. The toy sold for between $11 and $15.

The CPSC determined that the silicone strings are both smaller in diameter and longer in length than permitted by the mandatory federal safety standard for toys. That combination makes it possible for the strings to reach deep into a child's throat.

What the Incidents Looked Like

GOPO Toys reported three incidents to the CPSC involving the toy strings reaching the back of a child's throat. The incidents resulted in respiratory distress or choking. No fatalities have been reported, but the agency classified the hazard as posing a risk of serious injury or death.

How to Complete the Recall

The remedy for this recall is specific — and it's important to follow every step exactly as instructed to receive the full refund:

  1. Stop use immediately.
  2. Cut ALL silicone strings off the toy.
  3. Write "DESTROYED" on the main body of the toy in permanent marker.
  4. Take a photograph of the destroyed toy clearly showing both the cut strings and the marker writing.
  5. Email the photograph to recalls@gopotoys.com to receive a full refund.
  6. Dispose of the toy — do not donate or pass it along.

Parents do not need to mail the toy back. The photo and email are all that's required to process the refund.

If You're Not Sure You Have This Toy

Check your order history on Amazon for purchases between August 2023 and March 2026. Search for "GOPO Toys teething" or check any teething toys currently in your home against the description above: off-white disc, grey center ball, six colorful silicone strings. If there's any doubt, err on the side of caution and contact GOPO Toys directly at recalls@gopotoys.com.

Three years is a long time, and toys that were bought for older siblings — or picked up secondhand — may still be in rotation. It's worth checking toy bins, travel bags, and diaper bags where teething toys tend to accumulate.

What's Next

Reach out to anyone in your circle who has an infant or toddler — grandparents, daycare providers, playgroup friends — to make sure they know about this recall. The CPSC's official recall notice is published at cpsc.gov for reference.

For refund questions or additional information, contact GOPO Toys directly: recalls@gopotoys.com.
