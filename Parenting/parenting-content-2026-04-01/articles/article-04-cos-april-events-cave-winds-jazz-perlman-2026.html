Easter weekend is April 5 this year, and if you've already got those plans locked in, here's what's coming in the two weeks after. Colorado Springs has three distinct events in mid-April worth putting on your family calendar — different in style and audience, but all worth knowing about.

Cave of the Winds Sound Experience — April 17-19

Cave of the Winds Mountain Park is a Colorado Springs landmark — one of those places every local family has visited at least once for the standard tour. The Sound Experience is something different. The cave's geology creates acoustic properties that you can't replicate aboveground, and the Sound Experience puts guests in those caverns with specifically designed audio programming that uses the space itself as part of the experience.

This runs three days, April 17-19. It's best suited to older kids and adults who can appreciate an immersive, more contemplative experience — this isn't the rollicking headlamp-and-discovery format of a standard cave tour. But for families with older children or for parents looking for something genuinely unusual, it's worth checking out. Cave of the Winds is located at 100 Cave of the Winds Road, Manitou Springs. Tickets and schedule details are available at caveofthewinds.com.

Earl Klugh's 23rd Annual Weekend of Jazz — Starts April 9

Jazz has a strong tradition in Colorado Springs, and this annual event — now in its 23rd year — reflects that. Earl Klugh's Weekend of Jazz begins April 9 at 1 PM and brings live jazz performances to audiences who have been coming back year after year. It's the kind of event that families can introduce kids to without it feeling like a field trip — good music, COS community energy, and a format that's genuinely approachable for those new to live jazz.

For families with children who are in music programs at school, live jazz is particularly valuable — it shows what improvisation and musicianship look like at a high level, in a way that a YouTube video doesn't. Check peakradar.com or local event listings for the full schedule and venue information.

Itzhak Perlman at Pikes Peak Center — April 18, 7:30 PM

Itzhak Perlman is one of the most celebrated classical violinists in the world — a figure who has performed for presidents, won Grammy Awards, and is widely considered among the greatest of his generation. His appearance at Pikes Peak Center for the Performing Arts on April 18 at 7:30 PM is a genuinely rare opportunity for Colorado Springs families.

This is an evening concert — 7:30 PM is a late start for families with young kids, and the experience is best suited to older children who are already engaged with music or classical performance. For families who want to offer their kids an extraordinary musical experience, this is the kind of event that becomes a memory. Pikes Peak Center is located at 190 South Cascade Ave. Tickets and seating information are available through the venue's website.
