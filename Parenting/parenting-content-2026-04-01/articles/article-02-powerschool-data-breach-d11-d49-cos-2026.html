PowerSchool Data Breach: What D11 and D49 Families in Colorado Springs Need to Know

April 1, 2026 · COS Parenting

A data breach at PowerSchool — the student information system used by school districts across the country — has been confirmed to have affected Colorado Springs-area families. District 11 (D11), District 49 (D49), and Manitou Springs School District 14 are among the affected districts.

Here's the most important fact first: both D11 and D49 have confirmed that they do not store student Social Security Numbers in PowerSchool. SSNs were not at risk in this breach. That's meaningful, because SSN exposure in a school data breach creates the highest risk of long-term identity harm to your child. The districts' use of PowerSchool doesn't include SSNs, so that specific risk is off the table.

What PowerSchool Contains and What May Have Been Exposed

PowerSchool is the student information system that districts use to manage day-to-day operations: student enrollment records, attendance history, grades, course schedules, teacher-student relationships, and parent and guardian contact information. It's the backbone of how districts track who's in school, how they're doing, and how to reach families.

A breach of this system means that data in those categories may have been accessed by unauthorized parties. The specific fields included in any breach can vary by district depending on how each school configured their data — not every district stores the same information in PowerSchool. That's why the districts' specific confirmation about SSNs matters: they know what they put in the system, and SSNs weren't there.

Other data — student names, enrollment records, parent contact information, grade histories — may have been included. Families deserve a clear accounting of exactly what was exposed.

What Colorado Springs Families Should Do

Step Action
1 Check your email from D11 or D49 for an official breach notification
2 If you haven't received one, call your school's main office
3 Ask specifically: "What types of student data were in the PowerSchool breach?"
4 Ask if credit monitoring or identity protection is being offered
5 Monitor your child's records — school and financial — for any unexplained activity

A Note About the Reassurance on SSNs

The confirmation that neither D11 nor D49 stores student SSNs in PowerSchool is a substantive reassurance, but it doesn't mean no action is needed. Contact information being exposed can still lead to targeted phishing attempts — emails or texts that claim to be from your child's school and try to get you to click a link or provide login credentials. Be cautious of any unexpected communication that asks you to verify account information after a breach of this type.

What's Next

District 11 (d11.online) and District 49 (d49.org) both have family communication channels through their main websites. If you have a child in D49 and attend a school in the Falcon, Peyton, or Powers Blvd corridor, D49's main communications come through the district parent portal and school email lists. D11 families can reach their school through the main D11 website or by calling the school directly.

Manitou Springs SD14 families should contact Manitou Springs SD14 directly for breach-specific information. The district is separate from D11, D20, and D49 and will have its own communication timeline.

If you believe your family's data has been misused as a result of this breach, you can file a report with the Colorado Attorney General's office, which handles consumer data breach complaints for Colorado residents.
