CPSC Recalls: Crib Bumpers, Hobby Lobby Plush Toys, Children's Loungewear, and Crib Mattresses

April 1, 2026 · COS Parenting

Four products currently require your attention if you have young children: a crib bumper sold in violation of a federal ban, Hobby Lobby plush toys with a documented choking hazard, children's loungewear that fails mandatory flammability standards, and a crib mattress that doesn't fit play yards safely. Here's what to know about each, and what to do.

Crib Bumpers: A Federal Ban Still Being Violated

The Little Pea Shop crib bumper recall deserves special attention because it isn't just a product defect — it's a violation of a federal ban. The Safe Sleep for Babies Act, enacted in 2022, banned padded crib bumpers in the United States. The ban was the result of decades of CPSC data linking crib bumpers to infant suffocation deaths.

Little Pea Shop was selling crib bumpers in violation of that law. CPSC has issued a recall — meaning families who purchased these can seek a refund — but the broader message is this: if you have any crib bumper in your baby's crib, regardless of brand, it needs to come out. The federal ban applies to all padded crib bumpers, not just this one brand.

A safe crib means a firm, flat mattress, a fitted sheet, and nothing else. No bumpers, no pillows, no loose blankets, no stuffed animals.

Hobby Lobby Giraffe and Llama Plush Toys

Hobby Lobby's decorative giraffe and llama stuffed animals have been recalled because the legs on these toys are designed to be removed — and when they come off, they release small beads. For children under three, small beads are a choking hazard. If you picked these up at Hobby Lobby for your child's room, or if they're in a play area accessible to young children, check whether the legs detach. If they do, the toy needs to be kept out of reach or returned.

Blossom Children's Loungewear

Blossom Children's Loungewear has been recalled because it fails the mandatory flammability standard that applies to children's sleepwear. Sleepwear regulations exist because children can be exposed to fire or flames during sleep — a candle, a space heater, a stove left on — and fabric that doesn't meet the flammability standard poses a direct burn hazard. If you have Blossom loungewear, stop using it. Check cpsc.gov/Recalls for refund information.

Note: this is a different brand from the Bellabu Bear children's robe recall announced in late March 2026. Both involve flammability failures, but they are separate products from separate manufacturers.

Criblike Mattresses (Mengna, Amazon)

Criblike mattresses sold by Mengna on Amazon have been recalled because they don't fit play yards properly. A mattress that's the wrong size for its sleep surface creates gaps between the mattress and the sides of the play yard — gaps that can trap a baby's body or head. Entrapment in sleep environments is one of the most serious infant safety hazards. If you purchased a crib mattress through Amazon from this seller, verify the brand and check cpsc.gov/Recalls for the specific product details and return instructions.

What's Next

The full list of active recalls is maintained at cpsc.gov/Recalls — it's updated frequently and worth checking whenever you purchase baby or children's products. You can also sign up for CPSC recall alerts by email at cpsc.gov.

If you have a recalled product, CPSC provides specific refund, return, or replacement instructions with each recall. In most cases, you don't need proof of purchase for hazardous baby items — the CPSC recall pages walk you through the process. For Hobby Lobby products, you can typically return them in-store with or without a receipt.

Colorado Springs families with questions about product safety can also contact their pediatrician or reach out to CPSC directly at saferproducts.gov.
