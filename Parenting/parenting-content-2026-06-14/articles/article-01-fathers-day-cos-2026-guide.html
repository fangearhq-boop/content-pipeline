By Jamie Rivera | June 14, 2026 | COS Parenting

Father's Day is one week away — Sunday, June 21 — and Colorado Springs has a genuinely impressive lineup of ways to celebrate with kids. Several major COS attractions offer free or discounted admission specifically for dads on that day, which means families can do something memorable without a big budget.

Here's everything you need to know to plan an easy, fun Father's Day in COS.

Book Ahead: Pikes Peak Highway

Pikes Peak — America's Mountain is offering free highway admission for fathers on June 21. At 14,115 feet, it's one of the most iconic experiences in Colorado, and it's easy to do with kids of most ages. The catch — and it's an important one — is that timed-entry reservations are required. Without a reservation, families may be turned away, especially on a busy holiday weekend.

Book your timed-entry permit in advance at coloradosprings.gov/pikes-peak-americas-mountain or the Pikes Peak web store. The cost of the pass for other passengers still applies; the free admission is for the driver/dad only, so plan accordingly.

Best for: families who love outdoor adventure and want a truly unforgettable Colorado experience. Recommend starting early morning to catch clear views before afternoon clouds build.

U.S. Olympic and Paralympic Museum

The U.S. Olympic and Paralympic Museum (5 W. Vermijo Ave, downtown COS) is offering 50% off admission for dads on Father's Day. The museum is one of the most impressive attractions in Colorado Springs — interactive exhibits, Team USA history, inspiring stories of athletes — and it's a genuinely great experience for kids from around age 6 and up.

Call ahead or check usopm.org to confirm the Father's Day promotion and current hours. This one doesn't require advance booking the way Pikes Peak does, but going early means shorter waits.

Colorado Wolf and Wildlife Center

If your family wants something truly one-of-a-kind, the Colorado Wolf and Wildlife Center is hosting a Father's Day celebration on June 21 from 9 AM to 11 AM. The experience includes a guided tour, the chance to howl with the pack, and breakfast burritos. Space is limited, so this one needs advance booking.

The center is located in Divide, about 45 minutes west of COS on Highway 24. It's not a spur-of-the-moment activity — but it's the kind of morning that kids genuinely remember. Check coloradowolf.org for tickets and availability.

Garden of the Gods

No Father's Day discount needed here because Garden of the Gods is always free. The park is one of the most photographed places in Colorado and is an easy, low-pressure option for families who want to be outside together. Pack a picnic, bring a frisbee, and take the Perkins Central Garden Trail for the classic red rock views. Parking fills up by mid-morning on weekends, so arrive early or park at the visitor center and walk in.

Tips for a Great Father's Day

  • Book Pikes Peak and Wolf Center early — both fill up for Father's Day
  • For downtown activities (museum, Garden of the Gods), mid-morning arrival avoids weekend crowds
  • Pack snacks and water for outdoor adventures — the altitude in COS means hydration matters
  • Check each attraction's website for current hours and to confirm Father's Day promotions, as offerings may change
