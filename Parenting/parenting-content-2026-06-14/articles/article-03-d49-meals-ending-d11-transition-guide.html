D49 Free Summer Meals End June 19 — Where COS Families Go Next

By Jamie Rivera | June 14, 2026 | COS Parenting

If your family has been relying on D49's free summer meal program, here's a heads-up: the program ends in just five days, on June 19. But the good news is that D11 continues free summer meals through July 31 — serving any child from any district, no questions asked.

Here's what Colorado Springs families need to know about the transition.

Quick Reference: Free Summer Meals in COS

Program Dates Sites Eligibility
D49 Free Summer Meals June 1 – June 19, 2026 Springs Ranch Elementary (only site) Ages 1-18, any district
D11 Free Summer Meals Through July 31, 2026 Multiple COS sites — visit d11.org Ages 1-18, any district

D49: Final Days

District 49 has been offering free breakfast and lunch at Springs Ranch Elementary since June 1 for any child ages 1 through 18 — regardless of which school district they belong to. No registration, no ID, no proof of income required. The program is funded through the USDA Summer Food Service Program.

June 19 is the last day. After that, Springs Ranch Elementary will no longer be serving meals through this program.

D11: The Transition Option

School District 11 is running its own free summer meal program through July 31 — six weeks longer than D49's program. The rules are the same: free breakfast and lunch for any child ages 1-18, no registration, no ID, no income verification needed. You don't have to be enrolled in a D11 school to use the service. Families from D49, D20, and other districts are welcome.

D11 operates multiple sites across Colorado Springs. To find the location nearest to your family, visit d11.org or use the USDA Summer Meals Site Finder at fns.usda.gov/summer-meals-site-finder.

Who This Is For

Summer meal programs exist because school-year meal support disappears when classes end, and research consistently shows that food insecurity spikes for children during summer months. The USDA Summer Food Service Program was designed to fill exactly that gap.

These programs are open to all children — regardless of household income, immigration status, or school enrollment. Families are not required to explain why they're using the service. Show up, eat, leave.

Spread the Word

If you know a family in Colorado Springs with kids at home this summer, consider passing this information along. The families who need these programs most are often the ones least likely to have heard about them. A text or a social media share can make a real difference for kids who otherwise might go hungry while school is out.

What's Next

D49 free summer meals end June 19. D11 continues through July 31. Starting June 20, the only remaining free meal option in Colorado Springs through D11 is for all families to transition to D11 sites — find yours at d11.org. If you have questions, call D11 directly or use the USDA site finder to locate the most convenient option for your family.
