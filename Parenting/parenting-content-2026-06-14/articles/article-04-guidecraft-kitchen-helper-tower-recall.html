Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower Recalled: What COS Parents Need to Do Now

By Sarah Morales | June 14, 2026 | COS Parenting

If you have a Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower in your home, stop using it. The Consumer Product Safety Commission issued Recall 26-490 in May 2026 for approximately 25,235 of these toddler towers due to a fall hazard. Eleven falls have been reported, including three injuries.

Here's what Colorado Springs parents need to know about whether they have one and what to do next.

What the Hazard Is

Kitchen helper towers are designed to let toddlers stand safely at counter height while parents cook, bake, or do kitchen tasks. The Guidecraft models covered by this recall include the Classic and Contemporary Kitchen Helper Towers, available in nine colors. The problem: the platform inside the tower — the standing surface — can loosen over time and eventually detach. If a child is standing on the platform when it gives way, the result is a fall from counter height.

The CPSC received 11 reports of falls. Three resulted in injuries. These numbers are significant enough that the commission determined a recall was warranted.

Do You Have One?

These towers were sold between June 2022 and October 2023 at a price of approximately $200. If you purchased a Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower during that window — from Amazon, Wayfair, Walmart, Target, Maisonette, Overstock, or directly from Guidecraft's website — your tower is likely covered by this recall. The recall applies to both Classic and Contemporary models in all nine color options.

If you're not sure whether your tower is included, check the CPSC recall page at cpsc.gov or the Guidecraft recall page directly for product images and confirmation details.

What to Do

First, stop using the tower immediately. Do not continue to let your child stand on it until you receive and install the repair kit.

To request the free repair kit, contact Guidecraft through one of these channels:

  • Call: 800-524-3555 (Monday–Friday, 8 AM–8 PM ET)
  • Email: ProductSupport@guidecraft.com
  • Online: guidecraft.com/pages/product-recall

The remedy is a repair kit, not a full refund or replacement product. Once the repair kit is installed, the tower should be safe to use again — but confirm with Guidecraft's instructions before returning it to service.

A Pattern Worth Noting

This is the third toddler learning tower or kitchen stool to be recalled in 2026. Cosyland tower stools and another brand were recalled earlier this year with similar fall and detachment hazards. If you have a learning tower from any brand in your home that you purchased in the last few years, it's worth a quick search on cpsc.gov to verify whether it's covered by any active recall.

What's Next

Stop using the recalled Guidecraft Kitchen Helper Tower and request your free repair kit as soon as possible. Contact Guidecraft at 800-524-3555 or visit guidecraft.com/pages/product-recall. For a complete list of current product recalls — including those affecting children's products — visit cpsc.gov/Recalls regularly. Signing up for CPSC email alerts is a helpful way to stay informed about new recalls as they're issued.
