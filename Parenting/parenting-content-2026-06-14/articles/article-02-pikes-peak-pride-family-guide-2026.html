Pikes Peak Pride 2026: Family Guide to Today's Parade and Festival

By Sarah Morales | June 14, 2026 | COS Parenting

Pikes Peak Pride is happening in downtown Colorado Springs today, Sunday June 14 — and it's one of the most family-welcoming events COS puts on all summer. Whether you're a longtime participant or bringing your family for the first time, here's what you need to know.

The Parade

The Pikes Peak Pride Parade starts at 10:00 AM near Acacia Park and marches south along Tejon Street, ending at Alamo Square Park. It's one of the most vibrant public events in Colorado Springs each year — colorful floats, community organizations, music, and a festive atmosphere that kids tend to love. Finding a spot along Tejon Street early gets you a great view; the parade typically lasts about an hour.

Parking downtown on a Sunday morning is generally manageable, but give yourself extra time. The Tejon Street corridor will have closures along the parade route, so approaching from the east or west side streets works better than trying to drive directly down Tejon.

The Festival

After the parade, the festival continues at Alamo Square Park and the surrounding area from 10 AM to 6 PM. With 150 or more vendors and more than 25 live entertainment acts across the day, there's plenty to explore at whatever pace works for your family. The festival has food vendors, craft booths, community organizations, and a range of performances that rotate throughout the afternoon.

General admission is free, which makes it an accessible choice for families.

Rainbow Youth Square

One of the standout features of Pikes Peak Pride for families is the Rainbow Youth Square — a dedicated space specifically designed for kids and teenagers. It's set up as a welcoming environment where young people can express themselves through art, storytelling, and performance. For families with LGBTQ+ youth, it's a particularly meaningful space. For all families, it's an inclusive and creative corner of the festival.

Tips for Families

  • Arrive before 10 AM to secure a good parade viewing spot along Tejon Street
  • Bring sunscreen and water — downtown has limited shade and June afternoons in COS can be warm
  • Strollers are welcome at the festival grounds but may be harder to maneuver through crowds near the parade route
  • The festival area is near Alamo Square Park (S. Tejon and E. Vermijo) — a good landmark to navigate back to
  • If you have teens, point them toward Rainbow Youth Square — it's their space

What's Next

Pikes Peak Pride runs today, June 14, from 10 AM to 6 PM at Alamo Square Park in downtown Colorado Springs. The parade begins at 10 AM. General festival admission is free. For information and any schedule updates, check the Pikes Peak Pride official pages or visitcos.com. The festival is an annual event and returns to downtown COS each June.
