Colorado Renaissance Festival 2026: Your Family Guide for the Rest of Summer

By Jamie Rivera | June 14, 2026 | COS Parenting

Did your family miss opening weekend of the Colorado Renaissance Festival? No worries — it runs every Saturday and Sunday through August 2, and there are plenty of weekends left to make it a family summer tradition. The 49th annual festival is happening in Larkspur, about 30 minutes from downtown Colorado Springs, and this year's theme is Magical Fantasy.

Here's your complete planning guide for Colorado Springs families.

Getting There from COS

The festival site is in Larkspur, Colorado — roughly 30 miles north of downtown Colorado Springs along I-25. The drive typically takes 25-35 minutes depending on traffic. Parking is available on site. On busy weekends (especially theme weekends), arriving right at opening — around 10 AM — helps avoid the worst of the midday crowd and summer heat.

Tickets: Save at King Soopers

The single most practical tip for Colorado Springs families: buy tickets at King Soopers before you go. Adult tickets are $29 and youth tickets (ages 5-17) are $14 through King Soopers — these are discounted from the gate price. Children under 5 are always free. First responders get BOGO admission with a valid ID at the gate.

Buying at King Soopers also means you don't have to wait in the ticket line when you arrive, which is especially nice if you've got impatient kids in tow.

What to Expect with Kids

The Colorado Renaissance Festival is genuinely great for children, and that's not an accident — it's been running for 49 years in part because families keep coming back. What draws them: jousting (loud, dramatic, and riveting for kids), the Living Chess Match, artisan demonstrations (glassblowing, blacksmithing, leather work), food on a stick, and dozens of shops selling costumes, wands, weapons, and all the trappings of a magical world.

Costumes are encouraged but never required. Many kids wear fairy wings, capes, or dragon horns — and vendors on-site sell them if you don't come prepared. This year's Magical Fantasy theme makes costumes especially fun and appropriate.

Family Tips

  • Go early — opening hour has the shortest lines and the most comfortable temperatures
  • Bring layers — Larkspur sits at a higher elevation than COS and can be noticeably cooler, especially in the morning
  • Wear comfortable closed-toe shoes — the grounds are mostly dirt and uneven terrain
  • Cash is handy for smaller vendors and food stalls, though most accept cards
  • Let each child pick one "magical" thing they want to see or buy — it gives them ownership and reduces the "I want everything" dynamic
  • Check coloradorenfaire.com for the themed weekend schedule — themed days add extra programming and spectacles
