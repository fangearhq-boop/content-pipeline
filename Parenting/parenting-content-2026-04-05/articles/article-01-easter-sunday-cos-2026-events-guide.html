Easter Sunday 2026 lands on April 5 — and Colorado Springs has plenty happening for families today, from sit-down brunches with live bunnies to a free all-day egg hunt spread across the city. Here's what you need to know before you head out the door.

One thing to know first: COS received approximately 15 inches of snow earlier this week. Most of it has melted, but overnight lows were still near freezing. Highs today will be around 56°F — pleasant in the afternoon sun, but chilly in the shade. If you're planning outdoor activities with kids, layers are worth packing.

ViewHouse Easter Brunch

ViewHouse is hosting its Easter brunch buffet from 9 AM to 3 PM today. It's one of the more complete Easter brunch experiences in the city this year — the restaurant has set up live bunny photos, Easter photo stations, and an Easter egg hunt on the green for kids.

Pricing is $54.95 for adults and $14.95 for children ages 6 to 12. If you haven't made a reservation, it's worth calling ahead or checking their website to see if walk-ins are available for late-morning or early-afternoon seatings.

Pamela's at Hotel Polaris

Pamela's Restaurant at Hotel Polaris (the official hotel of the U.S. Air Force Academy) is offering a family Easter experience that leans into the Academy setting. Activities for kids today include face painting, an Easter egg hunt, and a "Paint Your Own Airplane" activity — a fun nod to the military aviation history right next door.

It's a slightly different flavor than a standard Easter brunch, and the airplane activity in particular gives it a Colorado Springs-specific character. Contact the restaurant directly for current pricing and timing.

City-Wide Easter Egg Hunt

For families who want something free and flexible, a city-wide Easter egg hunt is running from 8 AM to 7 PM today across safe public locations around Colorado Springs. The all-day window makes it easy to work around nap schedules, church services, or brunch plans — you don't have to be anywhere at a specific time.

Details on specific locations are available through VisitCOS (visitcos.com) and the AllEvents Colorado Springs calendar.

What to Pack for Outdoor Activities Today

Colorado Springs sits at 6,035 feet, and April here can shift dramatically in a single day. This morning's temperatures started near freezing, and while afternoon highs will be comfortable, wind and shade can bring that down quickly — especially for young kids who are standing still at an egg hunt rather than running around.

A few practical suggestions for today:

  • Light jacket or fleece layer for each child, even if it looks warm when you leave the house
  • Sunscreen — the elevation means UV exposure is meaningful even on cool spring days
  • Check your park or trail destination for post-snow conditions before you go — muddy or icy patches can be tricky with young kids

What's Next

Easter Sunday kicks off spring break season for many Colorado Springs families. All three major school districts — D11, D20, and D49 — have slightly different spring break schedules, so check your district's calendar this week. The warmer stretch of spring is ahead: April's long-range forecast calls for above-normal temperatures across the Colorado Springs area, with plenty of outdoor opportunities in the weeks between now and the end of the school year. Garden of the Gods is open daily from 5 AM to 9 PM — always free — and City Parks spring programming runs from mid-April through late April.
