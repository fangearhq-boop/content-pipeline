The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued stop-use-immediately warnings on four children's and baby products, all sold through major online marketplaces: Amazon, TikTok Shop, and Shein. If you purchased any of these products, remove them from your home or make them inaccessible to children today.

All four recalls carry serious injury or death-risk designations. Here's what you need to know about each one.

Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets — Amazon

The CPSC has issued an urgent warning for Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets sold on Amazon. Approximately 250 units were sold between April and October 2025 at around $40 each. The bassinets pose strangulation and fall hazards and violate the mandatory standard for infant sleep products.

Parents who purchased this product should stop using it immediately. Do not continue using it as a sleep surface for any infant. The CPSC's full warning is available at CPSC.gov.

Uuoeebb Infant Walkers — Amazon (sold by BaoD)

The Uuoeebb Infant Walkers, sold by BaoD on Amazon, have been recalled for fall and entrapment hazards. The CPSC found that these walkers can fit through standard doorways and fail to stop at step edges — exactly the scenario parents use walkers to avoid. Additionally, the leg openings on these walkers are large enough for an infant to slip through until their head becomes entrapped.

If this product is in your home, stop use immediately. The recall includes all units sold under this product listing.

Mikario Trading Convertible Baby Gyms — TikTok Shop

Mikario Trading's Convertible Baby Gyms, sold exclusively through TikTok Shop in pink, have been recalled for suffocation hazard. These 5-in-1 products convert from an activity center to a tummy-time mat and were marketed for developmental use with infants. The CPSC found they violate the mandatory standard for infant support cushions.

Parents who purchased this product from TikTok Shop should stop use immediately and remove the product from any infant's environment.

Cubimana Building Toy Sets — Shein (sold by Vatos Toys)

The Cubimana Building Toy Sets, sold by Vatos Toys through Shein, have been recalled because the button cell batteries inside are accessible to young children. When a child swallows a button cell battery, the resulting chemical reaction causes internal burns that can be fatal. The product violates the mandatory standard for toys.

Parents should stop use of this product immediately, remove the batteries from the toy, and dispose of the batteries safely. Contact Vatos Toys directly to request a refund.

Why So Many Recalls from These Platforms

The CPSC has noted a pattern of safety violations among children's products sold through third-party marketplace channels — particularly Amazon third-party sellers, TikTok Shop, and Shein. These platforms often list products from international sellers who may not be aware of or compliant with U.S. mandatory safety standards for infant and children's items.

This does not mean all products on these platforms are unsafe. But it does mean that lower prices and convenient purchasing sometimes come with less rigorous safety screening than traditional retail channels. For products that will be used with infants — sleep products, walkers, activity mats — it's worth cross-referencing new purchases against the CPSC recall database before use.

What's Next

The full and current recall list is available at CPSC.gov/Recalls. Parents can also report unsafe products at SaferProducts.gov or by calling the CPSC hotline at 800-638-2772. If you're unsure whether a children's product in your home has been recalled, the CPSC database allows you to search by product name or seller. Subscribing to CPSC recall email alerts is one of the simplest ways to stay informed when new recalls affect infant and children's gear.
