Spring arrived in Colorado Springs right on schedule — complete with last week's 15 inches of snow. Now that the sun is back out and temperatures are climbing toward the mid-50s, it's a good time to plan the family's spring outdoor calendar.

Colorado Springs is exceptionally well-positioned for outdoor family activities: free access to some of the most scenic geology in the country, a state park ten minutes from downtown, and a Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services department running structured programs through the month of April.

Garden of the Gods: Free, Every Day, All Spring

Garden of the Gods is open from 5 AM to 9 PM daily (hours extend to 10 PM in May), and admission is always free — no parking fees. The Visitor and Nature Center is open from 9 AM to 5 PM every day.

For families who want a guided experience, daily guided nature walks are available for $5 per person. Topics rotate and include spring wildflowers, local geology, the history of the formations, rattlesnakes (a popular one for older kids), and wildlife. Junior Ranger Programs run daily at no charge for children — check at the Visitor Center when you arrive.

The Geo-Trekker Theater inside the Visitor Center runs a film called "How Did Those Red Rocks Get There?" on continuous loop. It's a solid 15–20 minute experience that gives kids and adults real geological context for what they're looking at on the trails, and it's a good bet if the weather turns cold mid-visit.

Spring is one of the best times to visit Garden of the Gods. The trails are less crowded than summer, wildflowers begin appearing in April, and the light quality in the morning and late afternoon is particularly striking against the sandstone formations.

Cheyenne Mountain State Park: Easy Overnight, All-Day Hiking

Cheyenne Mountain State Park is ten minutes from downtown Colorado Springs and offers a different outdoor experience than Garden of the Gods. The park has over 28 miles of trails spanning all skill levels, making it accessible for families with toddlers and for families with older kids ready for a longer hike.

If you want to spend the night, the park has 51 full-service campsites and 10 walk-in tent sites. Coin-operated showers and laundry are available on-site, along with a playground. Cheyenne Mountain is directly adjacent to Cheyenne Mountain Zoo — a full day combining a hike in the morning and the zoo in the afternoon is a classic COS family itinerary.

Day use requires a Colorado State Parks pass or a daily vehicle pass fee. Check cpw.state.co.us for current access and camping reservation availability.

City Parks Spring Programming — What to Register For Now

Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services is running three structured programs in April worth putting on the calendar. Registration is through the PRCS portal at coloradosprings.gov/PRCS.

Little Learners: Pikes Peak Wonders Series (April 16) — Designed for ages 3 to 5 with a caregiver, this city-run program focuses on early developmental skills through games, crafts, songs, and nature exploration activities connected to the Pikes Peak region. It's one of the better structured early childhood outdoor programs in the city.

Homeschool Programs (April 23) — At $4 per person with prepaid registration, this is an affordable option for families who homeschool or who want supplemental nature education. Children ages 8 and up may be dropped off; children 7 and under require a caregiver to stay.

Fountain Creek Regional Park Habitat Exploration (April 24) — Designed for grades 3 through 6, this program explores five distinct habitats within Fountain Creek Regional Park, includes a pond study, a short hike, and a wetland metaphor activity. It's science curriculum brought outside.

What's Next

City Parks programming continues through spring and into summer. Registrations for later April and May programs are likely open now at coloradosprings.gov/PRCS. For families who want to plan further ahead, Cheyenne Mountain State Park camping reservations for May and June tend to fill up — now is the right time to book if you're thinking about a spring camping trip. Garden of the Gods never requires reservations and is available every morning. The next few weeks of April should be the warmest stretch of the spring season, with long-range forecasts calling for above-normal temperatures across the Colorado Springs area.
