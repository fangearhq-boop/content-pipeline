Colorado Springs School District 11 approved a $1 million investment in tutoring and academic support earlier this year, and the services are now being deployed during the spring 2026 semester at schools the state has flagged for academic challenges.

For families in D11 — the largest school district in Colorado Springs, serving central COS — the investment means that students at affected schools may have access to expanded tutoring resources right now, if they haven't already.

What the Investment Is For

The $1 million was approved as a budget transfer in January 2026. The purpose, as approved by the district, is to expand tutoring and academic support programs at schools that Colorado's state accountability system has identified as persistently below grade-level expectations.

Colorado uses an accountability framework that evaluates schools on academic growth, proficiency rates, and equity measures. Schools that receive lower ratings are placed on improvement frameworks that can require specific interventions. D11 has several schools operating under these accountability requirements, and the district's decision to direct additional resources toward tutoring represents a concrete response to that data.

What It Means for D11 Families

Not every school in D11 is affected equally by this investment. The resources are targeted toward schools where state data shows the greatest need. If your student attends one of these schools, new or expanded tutoring services may be available this semester.

The most direct way to find out what's available for your student is to contact your school's front office or speak with your child's teacher or counselor. Ask specifically whether additional academic support has been added this semester and how to sign your student up.

If your student has been struggling in reading or math, this is a meaningful window to take advantage of resources that may not have been available earlier in the year.

D11's Broader Spring Programming

Alongside the tutoring investment, D11 is running a STEM Challenge program spanning February through April 2026 — a district-wide academic enrichment initiative for students in participating schools. The spring semester calendar and school-by-school programming details are available through the district's official site at d11.org.

D11 serves families across a large portion of central Colorado Springs and has faced accountability challenges in a subset of its schools for several years. Investments like this one reflect an ongoing effort to address those gaps with direct resources rather than structural changes alone.

What's Next

The spring semester runs through late May, meaning there are still several weeks left for students to access the new tutoring resources before the school year ends. D11 families who want to get ahead of next year should also watch for any communications about summer programming or 2026-27 enrollment changes that may follow this investment. The district's official calendar and any updates about support services are posted at d11.org.
