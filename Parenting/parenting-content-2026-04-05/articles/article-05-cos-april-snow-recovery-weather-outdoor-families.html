Colorado Springs families spent the first days of April watching roughly 15 inches of snow accumulate — a late-season storm that hit around April 1 and 2, following what had been an unusually warm winter. Today, Easter Sunday, temperatures are climbing back toward the mid-50s and the sky is clear.

That whiplash is a feature of spring at 6,035 feet above sea level, not a bug. Understanding how April actually works in Colorado Springs helps families plan outdoor activities, dress kids appropriately, and avoid the frustration of unexpected weather ruining a weekend plan.

What Happened and Where Things Stand

The storm that hit April 1 and 2 brought significant snowfall to Colorado Springs and the surrounding region. A Winter Weather Advisory was issued for northern Colorado mountains, and the snow came on the heels of a March that had seen record-high temperatures. The contrast was sharp.

As of Easter Sunday, most of the snow at lower elevations in COS has melted. Trails and parks in the city are largely accessible, though shaded areas and north-facing slopes may still have patches. Overnight temperatures have remained near freezing, so mornings will feel cold even as afternoons warm up.

What April Actually Looks Like at This Elevation

The average high in early April in Colorado Springs is around 56°F. By late April, that rises to roughly 63°F. But the overnight lows — 27 to 30°F in early April — are what catch families off guard. The daily temperature swing can easily be 25 degrees between early morning and mid-afternoon.

Colorado Springs averages about 10 hours of sunshine per day in April, which is genuinely excellent. But the UV index at elevation is higher than the temperature suggests, and snow on the ground — or nearby mountains — reflects additional UV. Sunscreen on kids matters even when it doesn't feel hot.

Snow remains possible in Colorado Springs through the entire month of April. The city sits at the base of the Front Range, and storms that move through the mountains regularly drop a few inches even in late April. It's not common enough to plan around, but it's common enough to not be surprised.

The Good News: The Rest of April Looks Warmer

The long-range forecast for the east side of Colorado — including Colorado Springs — calls for above-normal temperatures and below-normal precipitation for April and into May. That broadly means more sunny days, fewer storm systems, and generally favorable conditions for outdoor family activities through the rest of the month.

The storm that just hit was an outlier by the forecast metrics. The pattern moving forward looks more like what families expect from spring.

Practical Tips for Outdoor Activities This Month

A few things worth keeping in mind as you plan outdoor activities for the rest of April:

Layer in the morning, plan for warmth by noon. If you're at Garden of the Gods for a 9 AM walk, it might be 38°F. By 1 PM, it'll be closer to 55°F. Bringing a layer the kids can remove is easier than discovering they're cold at the start of the hike.

Check trail conditions before heading out after a storm. The Colorado Springs parks website (coloradosprings.gov) and the AllTrails app both typically have recent condition reports. North-facing trails and shaded areas will hold snow and mud longer than south-facing slopes.

Sun protection at every temperature. The combination of elevation, reflective snow on mountains, and 10 hours of daily sunshine means UV exposure is real even on days that feel cool. SPF 30 or higher and sunglasses are worth the habit.

Keep a "spring emergency kit" in the car. A pair of extra mittens per child, a packable rain jacket, and a small towel covers most of the scenarios — muddy trails, unexpected wind, a quick afternoon shower — that can disrupt an otherwise great outdoor day.

What's Next

Mid-April through May is when Colorado Springs outdoor season really gets going. School spring breaks, city park programming starting April 16, and warming temperatures create a busy window for family outdoor activity before the summer crowds arrive. If you're planning camping at Cheyenne Mountain State Park or a longer hike at Garden of the Gods, mid-to-late April is one of the best times to do it. Keep an eye on the National Weather Service forecast for Colorado Springs (weather.gov) for the most current conditions, especially if you're planning an early-morning outdoor activity.
