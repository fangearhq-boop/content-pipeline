Vevor Baby Swings Recalled Over Dangerous Incline Angle — What Parents Need to Know

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 9, 2026

Sanven Technology is recalling three models of Vevor-branded baby swings sold in 2025. The incline angle exceeds 10 degrees, which violates federal safe sleep law. Parents can get a full refund.

If you purchased a Vevor baby swing in 2025, you need to stop using it now.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has announced a recall of Vevor baby swings manufactured by Sanven Technology Co., Ltd. The affected models — BB501K, BB702A, and BB005K — were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon between January 2025 and August 2025 at prices ranging from $65 to $80.

The problem is the seat's incline. It exceeds 10 degrees, which violates the Safe Sleep for Babies Act — a federal law that establishes strict incline requirements for infant sleep products precisely because of the known risks associated with inclined positions during sleep.

Why Inclined Infant Products Are Dangerous

This recall is part of a broader pattern the CPSC has been addressing for years. Inclined infant sleepers — products that hold babies at an angle rather than flat — have been linked to more than 94 infant deaths in the United States between 2005 and 2019.

The danger is physiological. When a young infant is placed at an incline, their head can fall forward, which restricts the airway. Infants lack the muscle control to reposition themselves. What may look like a comfortable, cozy position can become dangerous, especially during sleep or when a parent isn't watching closely.

The American Academy of Pediatrics has consistently recommended that infants sleep on a flat, firm surface, on their back, in a bare sleep space. Products that deviate from that — regardless of how they're marketed — carry elevated risk.

What to Do Right Now

If you own one of the recalled Vevor swing models, the steps are straightforward. Stop using the product immediately. Do not continue using it while you wait for a refund or replacement. Contact Sanven Technology for a full refund — they are the recalling company and are responsible for making consumers whole.

No injuries have been reported from these specific swings, which means acting now — before something happens — is exactly the right response.

What's Next

If you're unsure whether you have one of these models, check the product label or original packaging for the model number. The CPSC maintains a full recall database at cpsc.gov where you can verify any infant product you're uncertain about.

For safe sleep guidance, the American Academy of Pediatrics recommends: a flat, firm mattress in a crib or bassinet that meets current safety standards, no soft bedding, and placing infants on their back every time. If you have questions about any infant sleep product you own, your pediatrician's office is a good first call.
