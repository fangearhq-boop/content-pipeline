Nearly 9,000 YCXXKJ Baby Bath Seats Recalled for Tip-Over Drowning Risk

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 9, 2026

BenTalk is recalling about 8,960 YCXXKJ baby bath seats sold on Amazon. The seat can tip over, posing a drowning hazard. Parents should stop use and contact the company for a refund.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued a recall for approximately 8,960 YCXXKJ baby bath seats sold under the BenTalk brand. The product — model YD-1958 — was sold exclusively on Amazon between May 2024 and October 2025 at prices between $34 and $40.

The hazard is instability. The seat can tip over during use, which creates a drowning risk for infants and young children placed in it during bath time. The seats are available in blue, gray, pink, and yellow.

No injuries or drownings have been reported in connection with this product, according to the CPSC recall notice. Parents who own one of these seats should stop using it immediately.

Bath Safety Context

Bath seats can give parents a false sense of security. A child secured in a seat still requires constant supervision — a bath seat is not a safety device. It's a positioning aid, and if it fails, the results can be tragic.

The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends never leaving a child alone in the bath, not even for a moment. Drowning can happen in just a few inches of water and within seconds. If you need to leave the bathroom, take the child with you.

What's Next

If you purchased this product, stop using it and contact BenTalk for a full refund by emailing bentalkhere@outlook.com or calling (213) 579-2588. If you're unsure whether you have the recalled model, check the product label for model number YD-1958.

To report unsafe products or check on other recalls, visit cpsc.gov or download the CPSC's Safer Products app. For the latest safe bathing guidance for infants, your pediatrician or the American Academy of Pediatrics at healthychildren.org are reliable resources.
