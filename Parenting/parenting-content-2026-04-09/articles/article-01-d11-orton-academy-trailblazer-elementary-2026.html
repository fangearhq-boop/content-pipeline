D11 Moving Orton Academy Into Trailblazer Elementary for 2026-27 School Year

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 9, 2026

The dyslexia-focused charter school will share the Trailblazer building starting in fall 2026. Some D11 parents say they were blindsided.

District 11 has approved a plan to relocate Orton Academy into Trailblazer Elementary School for the 2026-27 school year, placing a dyslexia-focused charter school inside a building that currently enrolls a traditional D11 elementary population.

Orton Academy serves students in grades 2 through 8 and currently has an enrollment of roughly 120 students. The school has operated as a D11 charter since 2024. Trailblazer Elementary, by contrast, has a building capacity of approximately 600 students but currently enrolls around 220 — leaving roughly 337 seats unused. The district's rationale centers on that gap: an underutilized building and a charter school in need of space.

But the announcement has not gone over smoothly with all families involved. Parents — both from Orton and from Trailblazer — reported feeling blindsided by the news, saying they weren't given adequate notice or opportunity to weigh in before the decision was finalized.

What the Move Means in Practice

Co-locating a charter school inside a traditional public school building is not unusual in Colorado or elsewhere, but it does raise real, practical questions that families are already asking.

Two distinct school communities — with different governance structures, different student bodies, and potentially different schedules, entrance procedures, and policies — will share the same physical space. That includes hallways, cafeterias, playgrounds, and common areas. The experience of that kind of arrangement varies widely depending on how the district and both school administrations manage the logistics.

For Orton Academy families, the move represents a potentially significant change of setting for students who may already require additional support and consistency. For Trailblazer families, the question is how the addition of another school community changes the day-to-day experience of their building.

About Orton Academy

Orton Academy is a specialized school designed to serve students with dyslexia using structured literacy approaches. That kind of school fills a real gap in a district like D11, where parents of kids with reading difficulties often struggle to find targeted, consistent support within traditional classroom settings.

The school became a D11 charter in 2024, which means it operates with more instructional autonomy than a traditional D11 school while still falling under district oversight and accountability. As a charter, it draws students from across the district — not just from a single neighborhood attendance zone.

What's Next

D11 families who want more information about the transition should contact the district's charter school office or reach out directly to Trailblazer Elementary or Orton Academy administration. D11 board meetings are open to the public, and this type of decision can be revisited or clarified through the public comment process.

Families with students currently enrolled at either school will likely receive additional communication from the district as the 2026-27 school year planning progresses. For D11 parents who feel the process moved too quickly, documenting concerns and raising them through school accountability committees is an established channel for parent input in Colorado school districts.
