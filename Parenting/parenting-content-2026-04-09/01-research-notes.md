## Story 2: Vevor Baby Swing Recall
**Source:** CPSC.gov (official recall notice)

**Key Facts (Verified HIGH — official CPSC source):**
- Recalling company: Sanven Technology Co., Ltd. (Shenzhen, China)
- Brand name on product: Vevor
- Affected models: BB501K, BB702A, BB005K
- Hazard: Seat incline exceeds 10 degrees — violates Safe Sleep for Babies Act
- Sale period: January 2025 through August 2025
- Sale channels: Vevor.com and Amazon.com
- Price range: approximately $65–$80
- Injuries/deaths from THIS product: None reported
- Context: Inclined infant sleepers linked to 94+ infant deaths 2005-2019 (CPSC data)
- Consumer action: Stop use immediately; contact company for full refund
- Contact: (855) 599-6320 or recalling@vevor.com

**Confidence:** HIGH (official CPSC recall notice)

## Story 5: AAP Screen Time Guidelines
**Sources:** American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP), CHOC Children's Hospital

**Key Facts (Verified):**
- Under 18 months: No screen time except video chatting (FaceTime, Zoom with family)
- Ages 18-24 months: Only high-quality programming; parents watch together with child
- Ages 2-5: Limit to 1 hour per day of high-quality programming
- Ages 6+: Consistent limits; screens should not displace sleep, exercise, or face-to-face time
- AAP emphasis: Quality and context matter more than strict clock limits
- AAP recommendation: Parents should co-view content, discuss what kids watch
- Parental modeling: Parents should model healthy digital habits
- Source organization: American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP)

**Confidence:** HIGH (directly from AAP guidance; widely reported and stable medical recommendation)
