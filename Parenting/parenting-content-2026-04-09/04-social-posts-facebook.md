### STORY 2: CPSC Recalls Vevor Baby Swings Over Safe Sleep Violation

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

Parents with infants — please check your baby equipment today.

Vevor baby swings (models BB501K, BB702A, and BB005K) have been officially recalled by the CPSC. The problem: the incline angle exceeds 10 degrees, which violates the Safe Sleep for Babies Act. These swings were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon between January and August 2025, priced around $65 to $80. ⚠️

No injuries have been reported from this specific product — and that's exactly the right time to act, before something happens.

This matters because inclined infant sleepers have been linked to more than 94 infant deaths in the United States since 2005. The risk is real, and the guidance from pediatric sleep experts is consistent: babies should sleep on a flat, firm surface, on their back.

If you have one of these swings, stop using it now. Contact the company for a full refund: call (855) 599-6320 or email recalling@vevor.com.

Please share this post — another parent might need to see it. 🙏

### STORY 5: AAP Screen Time Guidelines Put Quality and Context Over the Clock

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 PM MT

Honest question: how many of you have actually hit the "one hour of screen time" target for your kiddos on a weekday?

The AAP's guidance is that kids under 18 months should skip screens almost entirely (video calls with grandparents are fine), and kids ages 2-5 should watch no more than an hour of high-quality content a day. 📱

But here's what the AAP actually emphasizes more than the clock: quality and context. Watching a nature documentary together and talking about what you saw? That's different from two hours of passive scrolling. Co-viewing, asking questions, and modeling your own digital habits matter more than any specific number.

That framing tends to take some of the guilt off the table — because parenting a kid in 2026 without screens is not realistic for most families. The goal is mindful, not perfect.
