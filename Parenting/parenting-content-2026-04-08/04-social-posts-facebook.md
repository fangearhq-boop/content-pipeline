### STORY 1: Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinet — CPSC Emergency Warning

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM MT

🚨 **Important baby gear warning — please share with anyone who may have this product.**

The CPSC has issued an emergency consumer warning about Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets: stop using them immediately, and destroy them before disposal.

Here's what makes this different from a standard recall: the manufacturer refused to cooperate. Normally, companies issue a voluntary recall and work with the CPSC to offer refunds or replacements. Tuyedoqe didn't do that — so the CPSC issued a formal Notice of Violation, which means the only protection available is consumer awareness.

What's the risk? The bassinet's restraint system poses a strangulation hazard. The sides aren't tall enough to safely contain an infant, creating a fall risk. And there's no base or stand, making it especially dangerous on elevated surfaces like beds or changing tables.

About 250 were sold on Amazon.com between April and October 2025 for approximately $40.

**What to do if you have one:**
✅ Stop use immediately
✅ Remove the foam padding
✅ Cut the cover in half
✅ Dispose of both pieces in the trash
🚫 Do not resell, donate, or give away

Please share this post — because the company isn't doing a recall, the only way this reaches parents who need to know is through our community.

### STORY 2: COS Childcare Desert

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

Here's a number that stopped me in my tracks: Colorado Springs has 47,000 children under the age of 5 and only 18,400 licensed childcare spots.

That's a gap of nearly 29,000 seats. 🏡

A UCCS study found that 26% of parents who would otherwise be working are staying home because they can't find childcare. Not because they want to, but because there's simply nowhere to take their kids.

The problem is worst in the southeast area of Colorado Springs and the Fountain-Fort Carson corridor — but it affects families across the city.

Here's the part that's hard to swallow: in 2023, the city updated its Unified Development Code, and those changes accidentally made things worse. New in-home childcare facilities and church-based care centers were effectively banned in more zones than before.

The good news? The city is working on a fix. A Childcare Ordinance Update is targeting June 2026 implementation, which would reverse those restrictions and allow more childcare providers to operate in more parts of the city.

If you're on a waitlist, juggling work and childcare access, or you've had to turn down a job because you couldn't find care — this is the story to follow over the next few months.

### STORY 5: Olympia Pack-N-Stroll + Uuoeebb Infant Walker Recalls

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

Two more recalls to check before heading to the park this spring 🚨:

**Olympia Tools Pack-N-Stroll Utility Wagons**
If your family uses one of these for outdoor events, parks, or sports days — check now. There's a gap between the organizational tray and the wagon's sidewall that can trap a child's head. The wagon also has no restraint system. Stop use and contact Olympia for the remedy details. Model information is at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

**Uuoeebb Infant Walkers (sold on Amazon by BaoD)**
These walkers violate the mandatory federal standard for infant walkers in two key ways: they can pass through a standard doorway (fall risk on stairs), and the leg openings can allow an infant to slip through until the head becomes entrapped. If you have one, stop use immediately and contact the seller for a remedy.

Spring is the season when wagons come out of the garage and walkers get a lot of floor time. It's worth a few minutes to check what you have.

If you have a different brand of utility wagon or infant walker you're not sure about, the CPSC's full recall database is at cpsc.gov/Recalls — you can search by product type.
