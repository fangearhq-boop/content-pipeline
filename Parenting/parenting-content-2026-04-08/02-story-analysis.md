### STORY 1: Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinet — CPSC Emergency Warning
**Tier:** 1 (Breaking/Safety)
**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)

**Primary angle:** This is an EMERGENCY warning — not a standard recall. The company refused to cooperate, which makes consumer awareness the only available protection. Parents who have this bassinet need to destroy it before disposal.

**Secondary angle:** The "Notice of Violation" angle is unusual and worth explaining. Most recalls involve company cooperation. When a company refuses, it signals non-compliance and puts full responsibility on consumer awareness.

**Headline options:**
- "CPSC Says Destroy It: Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinet Poses Strangulation and Fall Risk — Company Refused Recall"
- "Emergency Baby Gear Warning: Stop Using Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets and Cut Them Up Before Disposal"

**Tier 1 requirements:** 2 X posts, FB Long-Form, FB Image Caption, 2 image concepts, Article

### STORY 2: COS Childcare Desert + June 2026 Zoning Fix
**Tier:** 1 (Breaking/Local)
**Pillar:** Local News

**Primary angle:** The numbers are stark and local. 47K children under 5, 18.4K spots. Nearly 29,000 families can't find care — and a quarter of parents who would otherwise work are staying home because of it. The June 2026 ordinance update is the closest thing to a fix the city has announced.

**Secondary angle:** The 2023 zoning code accidentally made things worse by banning new in-home and church-based childcare. This gives the story a "how we got here" arc.

**Headline options:**
- "COS Has a Childcare Crisis: 47,000 Kids, 18,400 Spots, and a City Trying to Fix What Zoning Broke"
- "Colorado Springs Is Running Out of Childcare — A New City Ordinance Could Help in June"

**Pillar:** National Parenting (Safety)
