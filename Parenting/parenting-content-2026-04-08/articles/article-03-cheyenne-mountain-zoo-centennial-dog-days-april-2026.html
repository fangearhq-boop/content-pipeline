By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 8, 2026

Colorado Springs' beloved mountain zoo is celebrating its centennial year with a full slate of events — including Dog Days at CMZoo this Friday, April 10, and a landmark new facility opening this summer.

Dog Days Returns This Friday

If you've been waiting for a good reason to dust off the stroller and spend a Friday at the zoo, Dog Days is it. Cheyenne Mountain Zoo's popular all-day Dog Days event is happening this Friday, April 10, and again on Friday, April 24. Dogs are welcome alongside their families for a full zoo visit.

Standard zoo admission applies. Families planning to bring a dog should review CMZoo's pet policy at cmzoo.org in advance — the zoo has specific guidelines on leashes and where dogs are permitted on the grounds.

A Century of Animals, Adventure, and Colorado Springs

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo opened in 1926, making 2026 its 100th anniversary year. That's a century of giraffe feedings, grizzly bears, and Colorado Springs kids looking up at animals against the backdrop of Pikes Peak.

The zoo has been marking the milestone throughout the year. In March, a new centennial history exhibit opened at the Penrose Heritage Museum, tracing CMZoo's origins and evolution over 100 years. USA Today readers recently recognized the zoo's reputation by voting it the No. 2 zoo in the United States in their 2026 readers' choice awards.

It's a reminder that one of Colorado Springs' best family destinations doesn't require a plane ticket.

What's Coming This Summer

The centennial celebration is building toward its biggest addition: the International Center for the Care and Conservation of Giraffe, expected to open this summer. The new facility spans 12,000 square feet and offers 25 percent more outdoor space for the giraffes — giving the animals more room to move and giving families more opportunities to get close.

CMZoo's giraffe feeding experience is already one of the most popular activities in Colorado Springs. The new center is designed to enhance the encounter and expand the educational component around giraffe conservation.

Whether your family visits for Dog Days this Friday or plans a centennial trip later in the season, 2026 is a meaningful year to make the trip up the mountain.

What's Next

Dog Days at CMZoo happens again on Friday, April 24. The new International Center for the Care and Conservation of Giraffe is slated to open this summer — CMZoo will announce the date on their website and social media. Families can find current event listings, ticket prices, and membership options at cmzoo.org.
