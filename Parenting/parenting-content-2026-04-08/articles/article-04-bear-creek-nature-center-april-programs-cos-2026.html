Spring Programs at Bear Creek Nature Center: Birding, Bird Banding, and Homeschool Days This April

By Sarah Donnelly | COS Parenting | April 8, 2026

Bear Creek Nature Center in Colorado Springs has a packed April schedule — including a birding class starting tomorrow, a real-researcher bird banding station, and a dedicated homeschool program later this month. All affordable and right in COS.

Intermediate Birding Class — April 9 and 11

Spring is peak birding season in Colorado, and Bear Creek's nature corridor along Fountain Creek is an excellent place to see it in action. The two-part intermediate birding class, offered in partnership with the Aiken Audubon Society, runs on April 9 and April 11.

The class is designed for ages 14 and up — confident younger birders with parental supervision may also be a good fit. Cost is $10 per person, or $8 for members. Participants should bring binoculars if they have them.

The Aiken Audubon Society is one of Colorado Springs' most active bird-watching organizations and a reliable partner for hands-on field education.

Bird Banding Station — April 18

On April 18, researchers from CSU-Pueblo will be at Bear Creek running a bird banding station from 7 to 9 a.m. Bird banding — the process of placing a small identification band on a bird's leg to track its movements over time — is a real scientific technique, and watching it in person is one of those experiences that tends to stick with kids long after the morning is over.

The program is recommended for ages 8 and up and costs $5 per person. Early morning start time, so plan accordingly — but the light is beautiful at Bear Creek before 9 a.m. in April.

Homeschool Programs — April 23

Bear Creek's April 23 homeschool event runs from 1 to 3:30 p.m. and includes separate sessions designed for younger and older children. Cost is $4 per person.

Pre-registration is required for the homeschool program — families should secure their spot in advance through the El Paso County Parks registration page. Spots may be limited.

Bear Creek Nature Center offers one of the more consistent homeschool program schedules in the Pikes Peak region, and the $4 per-person price point makes it accessible for larger families.

About Bear Creek Nature Center

Bear Creek Nature Center is operated by El Paso County Parks at 245 Bear Creek Road in southwest Colorado Springs. The center sits within Bear Creek Regional Park and offers access to trail systems, riparian habitat, and interpretive exhibits. Admission to the park itself is free; individual programs carry their own fees.

What's Next

The intermediate birding class begins tomorrow — April 9 — so families interested should check availability today at the El Paso County Parks website or by calling Bear Creek Nature Center directly. For the April 23 homeschool program, pre-registration is required and space may fill up quickly. Summer programming details for Bear Creek Nature Center are typically announced in May.
