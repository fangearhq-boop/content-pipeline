2 CPSC Recalls Before Spring: Olympia Pack-N-Stroll Utility Wagons and Uuoeebb Infant Walkers

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 8, 2026

Two more recalls to know before outdoor season ramps up in Colorado Springs: Olympia utility wagons with a head entrapment hazard and Uuoeebb infant walkers with a fall and entrapment hazard.

Olympia Pack-N-Stroll Utility Wagons

Utility wagons have become a standard piece of family gear — useful at parks, outdoor events, sports days, and hiking trails. The Olympia Tools Pack-N-Stroll is being recalled because of a structural problem that creates a serious hazard for young children riding in the wagon.

There's a gap between the wagon's organizational tray and the sidewall. That gap is sized in a way that can allow a child's head to become trapped. Additionally, the wagon lacks a restraint system — meaning a child can lean, fall, or shift in a way that the design doesn't prevent.

Both hazards violate the mandatory federal standard for strollers, which governs utility wagons designed to carry children.

If you have an Olympia Pack-N-Stroll Utility Wagon, stop using it to transport children. Contact Olympia Tools International for the remedy details specific to your model. The full recall notice, including model numbers, is available at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

Uuoeebb Infant Walkers (Amazon)

Infant walkers have been heavily regulated in the United States for years, specifically because of the fall hazards they can create when infants use them near stairs or doorways. Federal standards require that walkers either be wide enough not to pass through a standard doorway or stop automatically at the edge of a step.

The Uuoeebb Infant Walker, sold on Amazon by a seller called BaoD, violates those standards in two ways. First, the walker can fit through a standard doorway — meaning it doesn't prevent a rolling infant from reaching a staircase or drop-off. Second, it doesn't stop at a step edge.

There's a third hazard as well: the leg openings on the walker are sized in a way that can allow an infant to slip through until their head becomes entrapped in the frame.

If you purchased a Uuoeebb Infant Walker on Amazon, stop use immediately and contact the seller for a remedy. The full recall notice is at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

How to Check for Other Recalled Products

The CPSC's full, searchable recall database is at cpsc.gov/Recalls. Families can search by product type, brand name, or date range. Setting up email alerts for specific product categories — like infant sleep products, walkers, or car seats — is a practical way to stay current without having to check manually.

If you're unsure whether a product you already own has been recalled, searching the brand and product name at cpsc.gov is the most reliable place to start.

What's Next

For both recalled products, the immediate step is to stop use and contact the manufacturer or seller for a remedy. The CPSC continues to monitor compliance and may update recall notices with additional product details or remedy instructions. COS families with questions about child product safety can also reach out to their pediatrician or visit the American Academy of Pediatrics' consumer safety resources at healthychildren.org.
