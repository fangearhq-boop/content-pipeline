CPSC Emergency Warning: Stop Using Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets — Company Refused Recall

By Maria Chen | COS Parenting | April 8, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an emergency consumer warning about Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets — and the action required is stronger than a typical recall: stop use immediately and destroy the product before disposal.

Why This Warning Is Different

Most product safety actions involve a company voluntarily cooperating with the CPSC to issue a recall, offer a refund or replacement, and notify consumers. The Tuyedoqe case is different: the company, based in China, refused to issue a recall or provide any remedy to consumers.

As a result, the CPSC issued a Notice of Violation — a formal enforcement action that puts the company on record as non-compliant. But it also means there's no refund offer, no replacement program, and no company outreach to buyers. The only way this information reaches parents who have this bassinet is through direct consumer awareness.

That's what makes this warning especially important to share.

What Makes the Bassinet Dangerous

The CPSC identified three distinct hazards in the Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinet:

First, the restraint system poses a strangulation risk. The restraints are positioned in a way that can create an entanglement hazard for an infant who moves during sleep.

Second, the sides of the bassinet are too low to safely contain an infant. This creates a fall risk as a child develops and shifts in their sleep.

Third, the bassinet has no stand or base to keep it stable on an elevated surface. Using it on a bed, changing table, or couch creates an additional fall hazard.

Together, these hazards represent serious risks for sleeping infants. The CPSC's recommendation to destroy the product before disposal — rather than simply return it or store it — reflects the severity of those risks and the absence of a safe way to remediate the product.

What to Do If You Have This Bassinet

Stop using the bassinet immediately. Do not use it as storage, even without an infant in it. Remove the foam padding. Cut the cover of the bassinet in half. Dispose of both the padding and the cut cover in your household trash.

Do not resell this product. Do not donate it. Do not give it to another family. The CPSC's warning is explicit on this point: the product should be destroyed before it leaves your home.

If you purchased the bassinet on Amazon, you may be able to submit a return request through your account — but because the company has not agreed to a refund program, there is no guarantee of reimbursement.

What's Next

If you have the Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinet, the immediate action is clear: stop use today and destroy the product.
