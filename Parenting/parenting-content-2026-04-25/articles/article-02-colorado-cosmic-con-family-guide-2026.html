If you're looking for something to do with your family this weekend and you've got a tween, teen, or anime-obsessed kid at home, Colorado Cosmic Con is happening right now at the Norris Penrose Event Center.

The convention runs Saturday, April 25 from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. and Sunday, April 26 from 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Admission is $12.51 per person, with tickets available at coloradocosmiccon.com and at the door while available.

What to Expect Inside

Colorado Cosmic Con 2026 leans into a "mafia era" theme, with celebrity guests including actors from The Sopranos and Goodfellas. Anime fans will want to check out the voice actors from the One Piece series who are appearing this year. Beyond the celebrity panels and photo opportunities, the convention floor features more than 100 vendors and artists selling prints, collectibles, handmade cosplay items, comics, and merchandise.

There are interactive panels throughout both days, cosplay competitions, and the typical lively convention atmosphere that makes events like this memorable for kids who are into pop culture, gaming, or creative fandoms. Over 3,500 attendees are expected across the two days, so expect a busy but energetic crowd.

Is It Right for Your Family?

Colorado Cosmic Con is best suited for older kids (roughly ages 10 and up), tweens, teens, and adults who are into pop culture, comics, anime, cosplay, or celebrity fan experiences. The convention environment is crowded, lively, and loud at peak times — younger children can have a great time, but it's worth planning around their tolerance for busy spaces and overstimulation.

If your family has a passionate cosplayer, an anime fan, or a kid who's been wanting to meet someone from their favorite show, this is a genuinely fun Saturday. The $12.51 admission is reasonable for what's included, and browsing the vendor floor alone can take a few hours.

Practical Tips

Norris Penrose Event Center is located at 1045 Lower Gold Camp Road in the south Colorado Springs area near the Broadmoor. Parking is available on site. Bring cash or cards — most vendors accept both, but not all will have card readers for small purchases. Bring a water bottle, as convention floors can get warm, and consider bringing snacks for younger kids to avoid lines at food vendors.

If you're planning to catch a celebrity panel or photo opportunity, check the schedule at coloradocosmiccon.com ahead of time and arrive with a window of buffer before your scheduled slot.

What's Next

Colorado Cosmic Con wraps up Sunday, April 26 at 5 p.m. If you're reading this Saturday morning, there's still time to plan an afternoon visit. If you're looking ahead to the rest of the spring family events calendar in Colorado Springs, keep an eye on peakradar.com and coloradospringskids.com for updates on what's coming in May.

The Cañon City Blossom Festival also runs April 29 through May 3 for families looking for a day trip option with a different kind of weekend energy.
