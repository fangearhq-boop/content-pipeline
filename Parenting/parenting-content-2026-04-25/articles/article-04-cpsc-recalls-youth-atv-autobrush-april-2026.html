The Consumer Product Safety Commission posted two new recalls on April 23 affecting children's products — one involving outdoor vehicles linked to a child death, and one involving a dental product packaging hazard that could put a coin battery within reach of young children.

If you have either of these products, stop use immediately and contact the manufacturer for refund or remedy information.

Recall 1: Lil Pick Up Youth All-Terrain Vehicles

The CPSC is recalling Lil Pick Up Youth All-Terrain Vehicles due to crash and burn hazards. The vehicles violate the mandatory federal safety standard for ATVs. According to the CPSC, one death has been reported in connection with these vehicles.

Youth ATVs are popular outdoor gifts, and Colorado Springs families who live near trails or open land are among those who commonly own them. If you or a family member purchased a Lil Pick Up Youth ATV — sold on Amazon — stop all use immediately. Contact the manufacturer for information about a refund or remedy.

The CPSC recall hotline is 1-800-638-2772, available Monday through Friday, 10 a.m. to 6 p.m. ET. You can also report safety incidents or search recall details at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

A reminder about youth ATV safety even with non-recalled vehicles: the American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that children under 16 not ride adult-sized ATVs, and that all riders wear helmets, eye protection, gloves, and appropriate footwear. Youth ATVs designed for children should be size-matched to the child's age and weight — and adult supervision during all riding is essential.

Recall 2: Autobrush Sonic Pro Children's Toothbrush Boxes

If your family uses the Autobrush Sonic Pro electric toothbrush — a U-shaped toothbrush marketed for children — check your packaging. The CPSC is recalling the boxes because children can too easily access a lithium coin battery stored inside the packaging, posing a serious ingestion hazard.

The packaging also lacks the required warning labels under Reese's Law — a federal law that specifically regulates products containing button cell and coin batteries because of how dangerous battery ingestion is for young children. A swallowed lithium coin battery can cause severe internal burns and life-threatening injuries within two hours.

The Autobrush Sonic Pro Children's Toothbrush Boxes were sold on Amazon. If you have the packaging, do not give it to young children and keep batteries secured. Contact the manufacturer for recall information.

A Note About Amazon and Recalled Products

Both of these products were sold on Amazon — a pattern we see frequently in CPSC recalls. Amazon has taken steps to improve recall notifications to buyers, but the system is not always reliable. If you've purchased any children's product through Amazon in the past year, it's worth searching the product name at cpsc.gov/Recalls to verify it hasn't been recalled.

You can also sign up for CPSC email recall alerts at cpsc.gov/Recalls — select children's products as a category to get notices directly when new recalls are posted. It takes five minutes and could prevent a serious injury.

What's Next

The CPSC posts recalls and safety warnings regularly at cpsc.gov. For Colorado Springs families, we'll continue to flag recalls of children's products as they're issued. If you believe you have a product that has been recalled, stop use immediately — don't wait until you've confirmed through the manufacturer. The risk of harm is why products are recalled, and erring on the side of caution is always the right call.
