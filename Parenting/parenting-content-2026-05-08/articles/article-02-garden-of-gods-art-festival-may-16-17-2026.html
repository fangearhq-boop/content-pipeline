Garden of the Gods Art Festival Is This Weekend: Free for Kids, Free Shuttle, Dogs Welcome

By Jamie Rivera | May 8, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs families have two excellent outdoor events happening the same weekend. While Touch-A-Truck runs Saturday morning at Norris Penrose, the 3rd Annual Garden of the Gods Art Festival is running all weekend at Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site — with free admission for children under 12, a free shuttle, and one of the most striking backdrops in Colorado.

The festival runs Saturday and Sunday, May 16 and 17, from 10 AM to 5 PM both days. Here is what families need to know.

The Setting

Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site sits at the base of Garden of the Gods, with the park's iconic red sandstone formations rising in the immediate background and Pikes Peak visible beyond. For an outdoor art festival, it is hard to find a more visually striking setting in Colorado. The grounds are flat and accessible, making it workable for strollers and families with younger children.

The 150 juried artists are selected from applicants across the country and represent a range of fine arts and crafts: pottery, jewelry, textiles, paintings, sculpture, glass art, and more. This is not a generic craft fair — the jurying process is meant to ensure quality work from artists who have been selected competitively. For families with kids who are curious about how things are made, watching potters, glassblowers, and jewelers at work is its own kind of hands-on experience.

Making It Work for Families

The free child admission for ages 11 and under is a genuine family benefit. Two adults and two children under 12 can attend for $20 without any additional costs. Seniors and military get a $2 discount with valid ID, purchased at the gate.

For parking, the organizers are running a free shuttle from Coronado High School at 1590 W. Fillmore St. The shuttle runs 9:30 AM to 5 PM on both Saturday and Sunday and drops off and picks up at the south entrance to the festival. Given the two-day run and the expected crowds, taking the shuttle rather than driving to the venue directly is worth considering — especially on Saturday, when Touch-A-Truck is also running a few miles away and general outdoor event traffic in Colorado Springs will be elevated.

Well-behaved dogs on leashes are welcome, which removes a common logistical challenge for families with pets. If you are planning to bring a dog, keeping them comfortable in a crowd that size means bringing water and planning for a reasonable stay rather than the full five hours.

What's Next

The Garden of the Gods Art Festival runs May 16-17 from 10 AM to 5 PM at Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site in Colorado Springs. Admission is $10 for adults, $8 for seniors 65+ and military, and free for children under 12. The free shuttle runs from Coronado High School (1590 W. Fillmore St.) both days starting at 9:30 AM.
