D49 Opens Two New Preschool Classrooms This August at Student Success Center

By Jamie Rivera | May 8, 2026 | COS Parenting

Families in Falcon School District 49 have a new early childhood education option opening this fall. D49 is launching two full-time preschool classrooms at the new D49 Student Success Center at the start of the 2026-27 school year, with applications open now at d49.org.

For families who could not take advantage of the district's previous part-day preschool programming, the expanded schedule — and a new childcare partnership — may change that calculation.

What the Student Success Center Is

The D49 Student Success Center will find its permanent home at what is currently the Falcon Elementary School of Technology campus. The center houses the district's early childhood education program and is being expanded with two new classrooms to meet growing demand for full-time preschool access among D49 families.

The two new classrooms will offer a Monday through Thursday preschool experience. The M-Th schedule is a common structure for preschool programs because it balances meaningful instructional time with the developmental needs of young learners while giving families predictability in scheduling.

The Base49 Partnership for Working Families

One of the more significant practical changes in this expansion is the addition of Base49 care. D49 is partnering with Base49 to provide before- and after-school childcare on the school days, plus Friday childcare for families who need coverage on the day the preschool program does not run.

The district's previous part-day programming left a gap for working families who needed more coverage than the core preschool hours provided. The Base49 partnership is specifically designed to close that gap. Families who need early drop-off, late pick-up, or Friday care can access those services through the same location.

In a city where childcare availability is a real challenge for many families, a district-based preschool with extended care access is a meaningful option — particularly for families already within D49 boundaries.

How to Apply

Applications are available through D49's website at d49.org. Families interested in the Student Success Center preschool should use the internal D49 application, which walks through the process step by step. Given that preschool spots in Colorado Springs tend to fill quickly and that this is an entirely new program with a defined number of classroom spaces, applying earlier in the cycle is advisable.

If you have questions about eligibility or the program structure, contacting D49's Early Childhood Education department directly through the d49.org website is the right starting point.

What's Next

Applications for the D49 Student Success Center preschool program are available now at d49.org. The program opens in August 2026 at the start of the 2026-27 school year. Two full-time Monday-Thursday preschool classrooms will be offered, with Base49 before/after care and Friday childcare available through a partnership program. Questions can be directed to the D49 Early Childhood Education department through d49.org.
