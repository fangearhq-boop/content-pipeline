AAP Released the 2026 Immunization Schedule: What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | May 8, 2026 | COS Parenting

The American Academy of Pediatrics has published its updated immunization schedule for 2026, with several changes that Colorado Springs families and caregivers should be aware of heading into summer well-child visits. The schedule is updated annually and is the standard that most pediatricians in Colorado Springs follow.

This year's update includes a new RSV protection option for newborns and infants, a revised age range for the HPV vaccine, and continued recommendations for COVID-19 vaccination in younger children. Here is what changed and what it means for COS families.

RSV Protection: A New Option for Newborns

Respiratory syncytial virus is a common respiratory illness that is typically mild for healthy older children and adults but can be serious for newborns and young infants. The 2026 schedule adds clesrovimab as a protective option for this age group. Unlike a traditional vaccine, clesrovimab is a monoclonal antibody treatment designed to provide passive protection in very young infants who have not yet built their own immune response.

If you have a newborn or infant under 12 months, asking your pediatrician about RSV protection options — including clesrovimab as well as nirsevimab (which was added in prior years) — is worth adding to your next well-child visit conversation. The relevant window for this protection is the fall and winter months when RSV circulation peaks, but the schedule update makes it a year-round option to discuss.

HPV Vaccine: Earlier Start Recommended by AAP

The AAP updated its recommended age range for starting the HPV vaccine series to ages 9 through 12. The CDC's current recommendation remains ages 11 to 12. This means there is a difference between the two organizations' guidance, and your pediatrician will have a view on the right timing for your child based on their age, health, and the clinical context.

The change reflects the AAP's position that starting the vaccine series earlier, before potential exposure, provides better protection. The vaccine is most effective when given before HPV exposure occurs. The practical impact for Colorado Springs families: if your child is 9 or 10, the 2026 AAP schedule now includes them in the recommended starting window, whereas the CDC still defers to 11-12. Bring it up at your next visit and let your pediatrician guide the timing decision.

COVID-19 Vaccine Recommendations

The AAP 2026 schedule recommends COVID-19 vaccination for all children ages 6 to 23 months. For older children, vaccination is recommended for those with underlying health conditions that increase their risk of serious illness. This is consistent with the AAP's broader position that children in higher-risk groups benefit from staying current with COVID-19 vaccine doses as updated formulations are released.

If you have a young toddler or an older child with a chronic health condition, your Colorado Springs pediatrician can walk you through the current vaccine options and timing for your child's specific situation.

Who Endorses This Schedule

The 2026 AAP immunization schedule has been formally endorsed by 12 medical organizations, including the American Medical Association, the American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists, and the Infectious Disease Society of America. This level of cross-organizational endorsement is standard for the annual schedule and reflects the consensus of the major medical bodies governing pediatric and adult medicine in the United States.

The full 2026 schedule, along with a family-friendly explanation of each vaccine, is available at HealthyChildren.org — the AAP's consumer-facing website for parents.

What's Next

Review the 2026 AAP immunization schedule with your child's Colorado Springs pediatrician at your next well-child visit.
