3rd Annual Touch-A-Truck Is Free This Saturday in Colorado Springs — Here's What to Know

By Sarah Morales | May 8, 2026 | COS Parenting

Colorado Springs families looking for something to do this Saturday have a big one: the 3rd Annual Touch-A-Truck event runs May 16 from 9:30 AM to 2 PM at the Norris Penrose Event Center, and admission is completely free. With up to 15,000 people expected — more than 11,000 came out in 2025 — this is one of the most attended family events of the spring season in COS.

Here is what families need to know before heading out.

What Families Can Expect

Touch-A-Truck is exactly what it sounds like. Kids can sit in, explore, operate lights and sounds, and get close access to vehicles from all corners of Colorado Springs — fire trucks, police cars, ambulances, military vehicles, construction equipment, sanitation trucks, and community vehicles from various city and county agencies. Helicopters are expected to be on site as well, weather permitting.

For children who are fascinated by big equipment — the kind who wave at every garbage truck and stop to stare at every construction site — this is a rare chance to actually touch, sit in, and interact with the real thing. For younger kids, it can be a first look at the vehicles and the people who operate them. For older kids, it turns into a conversation about different careers and how the city works.

Alongside the vehicles, the Children's Entrepreneur Market gives young vendors from the Colorado Springs community a chance to sell their handmade items. It adds a different kind of experience to the event and reflects the entrepreneurial energy that organizers have built into the Touch-A-Truck concept beyond just vehicle exploration.

Timed Entry and Planning Ahead

With 15,000 attendees expected and free admission, parking and wait times can become significant challenges at events this size. Organizers have introduced timed entry this year to ease that pressure. That means families should get their free tickets at norrispenrose.com before Saturday and plan their arrival based on their selected entry window.

The Norris Penrose Event Center at 1045 Lower Gold Camp Rd is in the southwestern part of Colorado Springs near Bear Creek Regional Park. It has its own parking area, but given the expected turnout, arriving close to your entry window rather than early is probably the better strategy this year.

What's Next

Free tickets for Touch-A-Truck on May 16 are available at norrispenrose.com. Timed entry is required, so get tickets before Saturday. The event runs 9:30 AM to 2 PM at the Norris Penrose Event Center, 1045 Lower Gold Camp Rd, Colorado Springs.
