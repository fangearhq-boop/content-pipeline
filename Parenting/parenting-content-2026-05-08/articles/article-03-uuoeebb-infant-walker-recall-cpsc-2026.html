CPSC Alert: Stop Using Uuoeebb Infant Walkers Immediately — Fall and Entrapment Hazards

By Sarah Morales | May 8, 2026 | COS Parenting

If you have an Uuoeebb brand infant walker at home, the Consumer Product Safety Commission wants you to stop using it right now.

The CPSC has issued both a formal Recall and a Warning against approximately 2,650 Uuoeebb infant walkers sold on Amazon by a seller called BaoD. The walkers fail the mandatory federal safety standard for infant walkers in two serious ways, and the manufacturer has been unresponsive to the CPSC's requests to offer any remedy. There is no refund or replacement option available. The only guidance is to stop using and dispose of the product.

How to Identify This Product

The recalled Uuoeebb walkers are collapsible, with a round base, eight wheels, and seven adjustable height settings. They were sold in three colors: gray, black, and pink.

The key identifier is a tracking label located under the seat that reads "Production Batch: 7654." If you have a collapsible infant walker purchased from Amazon between December 2024 and September 2025 for between $60 and $90, check under the seat for this label. The walkers may have also been sold by other sellers or on other websites during this period.

The Safety Hazards Explained

The mandatory safety standard for infant walkers requires them to be too wide to fit through standard doorways — this design feature is specifically intended to prevent babies from rolling toward stairways. The Uuoeebb walkers are not wide enough to meet this requirement and can pass through a standard doorway freely. Combined with their failure to stop at the edge of a step, a baby in one of these walkers could roll off a stair before a caregiver could intervene.

The second hazard is a leg opening issue. The openings in the seat are large enough for a baby to slide downward into the walker frame until their torso passes through but their head does not — creating a head entrapment situation. Entrapment hazards in infant products are classified as Priority 1 hazards by the CPSC because the potential for serious injury or death is direct and rapid.

Both of these are structural design failures. They are not addressed by supervising the child more carefully or using the product differently. The only safe action is to stop use entirely.

No Remedy Available — Dispose Immediately

The CPSC issued a Notice of Violation to the manufacturer, Hunan Suihuo E-commerce Co., Ltd. of China (doing business as BaoD). The company has not responded to the CPSC's requests to recall the walkers or offer consumers a remedy. That means there is no return address, no refund process, and no repair option from the company.

The CPSC's guidance is clear: do not attempt to sell or donate this product. Passing it along to another family — even with a disclosure about the issue — puts another child at risk and may have legal implications. Dispose of the walker in your regular trash or recycling, depending on your municipality's guidelines for this type of product.

If you purchased through Amazon and have questions about whether you might be eligible for a platform-level refund, you can contact Amazon's customer service directly.

What's Next

If you have an Uuoeebb infant walker with "Production Batch: 7654" under the seat, stop using it and dispose of it. Do not sell or donate it. The full CPSC recall notice is available at cpsc.gov. The CPSC hotline is 800-638-2772. If you want to report an injury or near-miss with this or any product, you can file a report at SaferProducts.gov.
