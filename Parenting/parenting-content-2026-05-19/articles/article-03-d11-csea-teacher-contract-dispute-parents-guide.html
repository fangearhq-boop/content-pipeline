As District 11 families wrap up the 2025-26 school year this week, a labor dispute involving the district and its teachers' union has been ongoing for more than 300 days — and it is one that Colorado Springs parents should understand as they plan for the year ahead.

In December 2024, the D11 School Board voted 7-1 to dissolve the master agreement with the Colorado Springs Education Association (CSEA). The agreement had governed the relationship between the district and its educators for 56 years. Its dissolution ended a framework that covered teacher pay, working conditions, class sizes, and a range of other workplace provisions.

What the Union Is Asking For

The CSEA has consistently stated that the lack of a formal agreement has led to instability in schools. Their core concerns, as stated to media and community members throughout the year, include:

  • Rising class sizes — teachers report larger classes with fewer support resources
  • Insufficient school counselors and social workers — particularly given the mental health needs of students post-pandemic
  • Underfunded special education support staff — a concern that affects students with learning differences and their families
  • Curriculum restrictions and concerns about book access — educators report constraints on what materials can be used or discussed in classrooms

In October 2025, approximately one-third of D11 teaching staff participated in a one-day strike after 91% of CSEA union members voted in favor of the action. It was one of the first strikes in the district in recent history. Teachers returned to work, but the contract dispute was not resolved.

In early May 2026 — more than 300 days after the master agreement was severed — educators and parents organized a community picket, drawing attention to what CSEA described as ongoing instability in district schools.

What the District Has Said

The D11 School Board's majority voted to sever the agreement in December 2024, with the stated intention of renegotiating the terms of educator employment rather than operating under the existing framework. The district has maintained that schools have continued to function and that student outcomes remain its priority.

Colorado Springs parents who want to understand the district's full perspective can review School Board meeting minutes and statements at d11.org.

Why This Matters for Your Family

Whether or not a formal contract is resolved before the 2026-27 school year begins, the issues at the center of the dispute — class sizes, staffing levels, and school support resources — will directly affect the experience of students in D11 schools next fall. Families who want to be informed participants in their children's education should follow this situation and consider attending a School Board meeting or reaching out to their school's principal or their child's teachers directly.

This is not intended to create alarm. District 11 schools are operational, teachers are working, and the vast majority of classroom experiences this year have been uninterrupted. But the underlying tensions are real, and the outcome of the contract situation will shape the district's staffing and resources in the year ahead.

What's Next

The 2025-26 school year ends for D11 on Friday, May 22. As of publication, no new contract has been announced. The CSEA and district are expected to continue discussions over the summer. Parents who want to stay informed can follow D11 School Board meeting agendas (posted at d11.org) and CSEA communications at cseateacher.org.
