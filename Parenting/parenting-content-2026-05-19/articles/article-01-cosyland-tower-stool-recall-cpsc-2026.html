If you have a Cosyland children's tower stool in your home — the kind used to boost toddlers up to kitchen-counter height — stop using it immediately. The Consumer Product Safety Commission announced a recall of 125,200 units on May 14, 2026, citing serious risks of collapse, tip-over, and child entrapment.

The recall covers two models: CS0003 and CS0092-4, sold in natural bamboo and gray finishes. The stools measure approximately 16 inches deep, 18 inches wide, and 35 inches tall, and were sold on Amazon.com for about $70 between April 2021 and November 2025.

What Makes These Stools Dangerous

The recalled tower stools have two distinct safety problems. First, the platform inside the stool can loosen over time, becoming unstable or detaching completely. This creates a fall hazard when a child is standing on the platform.

Second, the openings on the front and back of the stool are large enough for a child's torso to fit through. If the stool tips over, a child could become entrapped in one of these openings — a potentially life-threatening situation.

The CPSC has documented 25 reports of stability issues and falls related to these stools. Eight injuries have been confirmed, ranging from scrapes and bruises to a fractured arm.

What to Do Right Now

If you own one of these stools, do not use it until you have received and installed the free repair kit. The remedy includes protective nets that cover the front and back openings, stabilizing feet, and detailed installation instructions. Cosyland Official will mail the repair kit directly to you at no charge.

To request the repair kit, contact Cosyland Official directly through Amazon messaging or their customer service channels. You can also visit CPSC.gov or call the CPSC hotline at 800-638-2772 (TTY: 800-638-8270) for more information and to verify whether your model is covered.

Checking Your Model

Look for the model number on the stool itself — typically on a label on the underside or back. If you see CS0003 or CS0092-4, your stool is part of this recall. The colors involved are natural bamboo and gray. These stools were marketed and sold under the Cosyland Official brand name on Amazon.

What's Next

Once you receive the repair kit and install the protective nets and stabilizing feet, the stool may be used again according to Cosyland's instructions. If you no longer wish to use the stool or prefer a full refund, contact Cosyland Official or CPSC for guidance on your options. Share this information with other Colorado Springs families who may have purchased this product — it was a popular Amazon item, and many families may not yet be aware of the recall.
