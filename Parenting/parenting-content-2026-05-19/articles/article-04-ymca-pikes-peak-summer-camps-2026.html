With D11's last day of school just three days away, Colorado Springs families who are still working out summer childcare and activity plans have a solid option close to home: the YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region opens its summer camps on May 27, with registration currently open across all three COS locations.

If you plan to enroll your child, the Y asks that registration paperwork be completed at least three weeks before the start date — which means the window to register comfortably before the May 27 opening is narrowing.

What Summer 2026 Looks Like at the Pikes Peak Y

The YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region structures its summer programming around themed weekly experiences, with activities that change from week to week. Here is what to expect this summer:

Week 1 (June 1-5): "Vintage Colorado: No WiFi Edition" — A throwback week focused on outdoor discovery, exploration, and activities that do not involve screens. Think nature hikes, outdoor games, and connecting with the Colorado environment in low-tech ways.

Week 2 (June 8-12): "Low-Key Famous Artist Week" — A creativity-centered week with art projects and activities inspired by famous artists. Painting, crafting, and creative expression are the focus.

Throughout the summer, camps include activities like golf lessons, ninja warrior obstacle courses, and rock climbing — programming that keeps kids active and challenged. The Y also partners with local organizations to offer specialized experiences.

Sports Camps

For families whose children have a specific athletic interest, the YMCA offers focused sports camps throughout the summer, including:

  • Tennis: Beginner-friendly, with skill development through drills and friendly competition adapted to age and ability
  • Pickleball: One of Colorado's fastest-growing sports — now available for younger players
  • Field games and cheer

New for 2026: Morning Specialty Mini Camps

For families who need camp coverage but do not need or want an all-day program, the Y is introducing morning Specialty Mini Camps this summer. These shorter-format programs are designed to spark creativity, exploration, and play without requiring a full-day commitment — helpful for families with flexible work schedules or children who do better in shorter structured programs.

Three Locations Across Colorado Springs

The YMCA of the Pikes Peak Region operates camps across three Colorado Springs locations. To inquire about availability or start the registration process, contact the location nearest to you:

  • Briargate Y: Heidi Schulz — 719.329.7298
  • Y at First & Main: Heather Hoogeboom — 719.884.8466
  • Tri-Lakes Y: Sophia Porreco — 719.481.8728

You can also register and browse the full program schedule at ppymca.org.

What's Next

Spots at YMCA summer camps fill on a rolling basis, so families are encouraged to register as soon as possible. The formal start date for camps is May 27, though the first full themed week begins June 1. Visit ppymca.org for the most current availability, pricing, and registration instructions.
