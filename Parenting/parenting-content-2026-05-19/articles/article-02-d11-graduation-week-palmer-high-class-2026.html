Colorado Springs School District 11 is in full graduation mode this week, with the Class of 2026 crossing the stage at Ed Robson Arena on the Colorado College campus through Thursday, May 21. Palmer High School kicked off the ceremonies this morning with its 9:00 AM commencement.

For families who cannot attend in person, D11 provides live streams of all graduation ceremonies at d11.org. The last day of school for most D11 students is this Friday, May 22 — meaning Colorado Springs families enter summer officially by the weekend.

Graduation Schedule at a Glance

Palmer High School graduated this morning, Tuesday, May 19, at 9:00 AM at Ed Robson Arena. Additional ceremonies are scheduled across the rest of the week through Thursday, May 21, covering Mitchell, Coronado, Doherty, and several alternative and charter school programs including Achieve, Tesla, Bijou, and Odyssey.

All ceremonies are held at Ed Robson Arena on the Colorado College campus. For specific times and ceremony order for each school, visit the D11 graduation calendar at d11.org/calendars/graduations.

A Community Milestone

Graduation week at District 11 is a community event that extends well beyond the arena doors. This is the Class of 2026 — students who started high school just as the world was finding its footing after years of disruption, and who are now stepping into adulthood in a Colorado Springs that continues to grow and change around them.

For families, this week is both an ending and a beginning. Four years of school drop-offs, late-night homework sessions, extracurricular schedules, and parent-teacher conferences — all leading to this. Whether your graduate is heading to college, a trade program, the workforce, or military service, the path forward starts here.

For Families Who Cannot Attend in Person

District 11 live streams all graduation ceremonies, making it possible for extended family members, friends, or anyone who cannot get a ticket to watch from anywhere. Visit d11.org for livestream links and ceremony-specific information.

D11's enrollment for 2025-26 is approximately 23,458 students across its schools. For many of those families, graduation week represents the culmination of years of work — both by students and by the people who supported them.

What's Next

The last day of school for D11 students is Friday, May 22. After that, summer programming begins across Colorado Springs — from YMCA camps to Pikes Peak Library District summer reading programs, city parks, and community events. If you are looking for summer activities or childcare, check COS Parenting for a roundup of options available this week and throughout the season.
