### STORY 2: Button Battery Toy Recall Alert

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

There's a pattern of recalls you need to know about right now. The CPSC has recalled multiple children's toys in 2026 for the same reason: button cell batteries that kids can easily remove, put in their mouths, and swallow. The consequences can be fatal.

The two most recent ones to flag: Oitnlaughter LED Finger Lights (sets of 36 in animal designs, sold on Amazon by Delightmi from July 2025 through April 2026 for about $14) and Children's LED Tutu Skirts (sold on Amazon by Bmrwtg). Both were recalled because the button batteries powering the lights are accessible to children. If a button or coin cell battery is swallowed, it can cause internal chemical burns and death.

If you have either of these products, stop using them immediately and take them away from your kids. For the finger lights, contact Delightmi for a full refund — you'll be asked to dispose of the toys and send a photo confirmation to the company.

The broader pattern here is worth flagging: the CPSC has issued at least five similar recalls in 2026, all involving inexpensive LED toys from Amazon that contain accessible button batteries. When you're buying toys online, this is something to look for. ⚠️

Which of these products do you recognize? Let us know in the comments so other families can check too.

### STORY 4: Wade Logan Annyka Fabric Dresser Recall

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM MT)**

If you have a Wade Logan Annyka 9-Drawer Fabric Dresser in your home — especially in a child's room — please check it right now.

The CPSC has recalled this dresser because it can tip over if it's not anchored to the wall, creating an entrapment hazard that can seriously injure or kill a child. The dresser was sold on Wayfair from September 2023 through May 2026 for about $98. It comes in black, brown, gray, and white and has nine fabric drawers. If yours isn't secured to the wall, stop using it and move it somewhere kids can't reach.

This recall was issued under the STURDY Act, which sets mandatory safety standards for clothing storage units. If your dresser isn't anchored, contact the importer (Ningbo Jiangdong Peter International Trading) for refund instructions.

Tip-over furniture remains one of the most consistent child safety hazards in American homes. If you have any free-standing furniture — dressers, bookshelves, wardrobes — in rooms where kids spend time, this is a good reminder to check anchoring on everything, recalled or not.

### STORY 5: Cheyenne Mountain Zoo Turns 100 This Year

**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM MT)**

We have a 100-year-old zoo in our backyard, and it just got a major national shoutout.

Cheyenne Mountain Zoo turns 100 in 2026, and USA TODAY's 10Best Readers' Choice Awards just ranked it the #2 Best Zoo in the entire country. If you've been putting a zoo trip on the summer bucket list, this is a good reason to actually go.

What makes it worth the visit with kids: you can hand-feed one of the largest reticulated giraffe herds in the world, watch grizzlies swim (they fish for trout), walk alongside African penguins, and get hands-on in The Loft. There's also an antique carousel, a sky ride to the summit, and the Grizzly Grill for lunch. Summer programming includes camps, Starlight Safaris, and animal encounters — worth checking the calendar at cmzoo.org.

A few logistics: hours are 9 AM–5 PM daily, last admission at 4 PM. Peak pricing (Fridays, Saturdays, Sundays, late May through September) is $34.75 for adults and $29.75 for kids ages 3 to 11. Advance, timed tickets are required for both members and non-members — you can't just show up and walk in. 🦒
