Free World Cup Final Watch Party at Weidner Field This Sunday

By Sarah Morales

July 19, 2026

The 2026 FIFA World Cup Final is today — and Colorado Springs has a free place to watch it.

The Colorado Springs Switchbacks are hosting a public watch party at Weidner Field for Spain vs. Argentina, the championship match of the 2026 World Cup. Kickoff is at 12:30 PM MT. Entry is free and open to everyone — no tickets, no reservations required.

Fans can watch the match on the stadium's Jumbotron from the Phil Long Club or from the pitch itself, where seating will be available on the field. The Switchbacks are providing cornhole and lawn games for families to enjoy before and during the match. Concessions will be available for purchase.

What to Expect

Weidner Field is the home of the Colorado Springs Switchbacks FC, the city's professional soccer club. The stadium holds thousands of fans and offers a covered club area in addition to open pitch access — so families can choose their setup based on sun preference or comfort.

This is one of the few free, public options in Colorado Springs to watch the World Cup Final on a large screen with a crowd. The Phil Long Club provides shade and an indoor viewing area, while the pitch itself gives a more open, festival-style experience.

If you have soccer fans in your family — or kids who have been following the tournament — this is a rare chance to watch the biggest match in the sport's four-year cycle alongside thousands of Colorado Springs neighbors in a real soccer venue.

About the 2026 World Cup Final

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is being hosted jointly by the United States, Canada, and Mexico — the first World Cup held across three countries. Spain and Argentina advanced through the bracket to meet in the Final. Spain enters as the defending European champion; Argentina, led by Lionel Messi, is the defending World Cup champion from 2022.

The match is expected to be one of the most-watched sporting events in history, with billions of viewers tuning in globally. Watching it in a venue with a crowd — especially a free, family-accessible one — offers an experience that home viewing can't replicate.

Logistics

Weidner Field is located at 111 W. Cimarron St. in downtown Colorado Springs. Parking is available in nearby garages and surface lots. The event starts at 12:30 PM MT when the match kicks off. Plan to arrive early if you want seating on the pitch or a good spot in the club area.

There is no confirmed end time — the match runs approximately two hours, plus potential extra time and penalty kicks if the score is tied after 90 minutes. A World Cup Final that goes to penalties could run until approximately 3:30 PM or later.

Concessions will be sold at the venue. Fans are encouraged to bring layers — July afternoons in Colorado Springs can shift between warm and cool depending on cloud cover.

What's Next

Today's event is the Switchbacks' only scheduled World Cup watch party. The regular Switchbacks FC season continues through the fall — check switchbacksfc.com for the home match schedule and ticket information.

For families looking for more summer events at Weidner Field, the Switchbacks typically host theme nights, giveaway games, and youth soccer events throughout the season. Following the team on social media or checking their website is the best way to stay current on upcoming events.
