D49 Leads Colorado, D20 Ranks Second in New Purple Star School Designations for Military Families

By Marta Kim | COS Parenting | April 30, 2026

Colorado Springs just made a powerful statement about its commitment to military families. On April 7, 2026, Governor Jared Polis and the Colorado Department of Education recognized 22 new Purple Star Schools across the state — and 21 of those 22 schools are in Southern Colorado. District 49 earned the most Purple Star designations of any district in the state, with D20 earning the second most.

For a city that is home to Fort Carson, NORAD, Peterson Space Force Base, and Schriever Space Force Base, this result is not entirely surprising. But it is still meaningful — and worth knowing if you're a military family in Colorado Springs.

What Is a Purple Star School?

The Purple Star School program, established in Colorado through HB24-1076, recognizes public schools that have made a formal commitment to supporting military-connected students and their families. The designation is more than honorary — schools must meet specific, verifiable requirements to earn it:

  • A designated military liaison on staff who supports military families navigating the school system
  • A dedicated section of the school website with resources for military families, including enrollment information and transition resources
  • A student-led transition program that helps newly arrived military-connected students integrate into the school community

The designation is valid for three years, and additional schools can apply each year. This is only the second year of the program; the first round recognized 27 schools statewide.

Why This Matters for COS Families

Military families move frequently — sometimes every two to three years. For children, that means new schools, new teachers, new social situations, and curriculum that doesn't always align with what they were learning at their last school. The Purple Star program exists specifically to ease that transition.

Schools that earn the designation aren't just aware that military kids exist — they've built systems to support them. The dedicated military liaison, for example, can be a crucial first contact for families who have just arrived at a new duty station and don't know where to start with enrollment, transcripts, or extracurricular activities.

For D49 families near Fort Carson or the Air Force bases on the east side of Colorado Springs, the strong showing in this year's designations means you're more likely to be enrolling in a school that's ready for your family's situation.

What's Next

If you want to know whether your child's specific school earned a Purple Star designation, check with D49 or D20 directly or visit the Colorado Department of Education's Purple Star Schools page at ed.cde.state.co.us. Schools that don't currently have the designation can apply again next year. And if you'd like your school to pursue it, talking to your principal or school board representative is the right starting point.
