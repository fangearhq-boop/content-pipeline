CPSC Warning: Stop Using Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets Immediately

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting | April 30, 2026

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued an urgent warning for families to immediately stop using Tuyedoqe brand travel bassinets. The products pose both strangulation and fall hazards to infants — and unlike a standard recall, the manufacturer has refused to issue one, meaning parents must take action themselves with no refund or replacement offered.

What Makes These Bassinets Dangerous

The CPSC identified three specific hazards with the Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinet:

First, the restraints built into the bassinet pose a strangulation hazard. Internal straps or ties that come in contact with an infant's neck or head create a life-threatening risk. Second, the sides of the bassinet are too low to contain a sleeping infant safely, which creates a fall hazard. Third, the product lacks a stand — meaning it cannot be safely placed on an elevated surface like a bed, couch, or changing table without the risk of the infant falling.

The product features a foam sleeping pad and a mesh canopy with three decorative toy bears attached to the top. It was sold in five colors: pink, green, gray, sky blue, and wine red.

Why There's No Recall — And What That Means for You

Normally, when the CPSC identifies a dangerous product, the manufacturer agrees to a recall, which triggers a refund or exchange process for consumers. In this case, the CPSC issued a formal Notice of Violation to Tuyedoqe, a Chinese company — but the manufacturer refused to issue a recall or offer any remedy to consumers who purchased the product.

That means there is no refund available. There is no replacement. There's no program to return the bassinet. Parents who have this product must take action on their own.

The CPSC instructs consumers to: stop using the bassinet immediately; remove the foam sleeping pad; cut the cover in half; and dispose of the padding and cover. Destroying the product prevents it from being resold or donated and ending up in another home.

How to Check If You Have This Product

Approximately 250 units were sold on Amazon.com between April 2025 and October 2025 at a price of approximately $40. If you purchased a travel bassinet in that time frame, check your Amazon order history for the Tuyedoqe brand. The product may also have been sold by third-party sellers on other platforms.

If you're not sure whether the bassinet you have is affected, the key identifiers are the Tuyedoqe brand name, the mesh canopy with three attached toy bears, the foam sleeping pad, and the availability in the five listed colors.

What's Next

If you're in the market for a travel bassinet or portable infant sleeper, look for products that meet current federal safety standards for infant sleep products, including a flat sleeping surface (no incline greater than 10 degrees) and sides high enough to safely contain an infant. The CPSC maintains an updated list of recalled and warned infant sleep products at cpsc.gov. When in doubt, contact your pediatrician for guidance on safe sleep environments for your infant.
