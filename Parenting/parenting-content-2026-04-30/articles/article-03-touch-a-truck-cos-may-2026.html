3rd Annual Touch-a-Truck Returns May 16 — Free Event at Norris Penrose

By Marcus Rivera | COS Parenting | April 30, 2026

One of Colorado Springs' most popular free family events is returning — and this year, organizers are expecting up to 15,000 visitors. The 3rd Annual Touch-a-Truck takes place Saturday, May 16, from 9:30 a.m. to 2 p.m. at Norris Penrose Event Center. Admission is free, but tickets are required and available now at norrispenrose.com.

What to Expect

Touch-a-Truck is exactly what it sounds like, and that's why kids love it. Children of all ages can climb into, sit behind the wheel of, explore the cab of, and flash the lights on a wide variety of vehicles — fire trucks, construction equipment, military vehicles, sanitation trucks, community service vehicles, and more. Helicopters are on the schedule as well, weather permitting.

The event draws vehicles from all corners of Colorado Springs, and with each event growing bigger than the last, the 2026 lineup is expected to be the most varied yet. The 2025 edition attracted more than 11,000 attendees.

New This Year: Timed Entry

Families who attended in previous years may remember long lines and crowded parking. For 2026, organizers have added timed entry — a structured ticketing system that staggers arrivals to reduce congestion at the gates and on the grounds. Each ticket will correspond to an entry window, and families will be able to explore the event for as long as they'd like after entering.

Because of timed entry, free tickets are required for admission. Grab them in advance at norrispenrose.com before the popular windows fill up.

Head to norrispenrose.com now to claim your family's free timed-entry tickets before popular time slots fill up.
