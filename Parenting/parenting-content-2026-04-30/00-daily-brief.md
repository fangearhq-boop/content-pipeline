### STORY 1: CPSC Warning — Stop Using Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinets Immediately [TIER 1]
**Pillar:** National Parenting
The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission issued an urgent warning to immediately stop using Tuyedoqe brand travel bassinets. The bassinets pose strangulation and fall hazards: the restraints inside create a strangulation risk, and the sides are too low to contain an infant, posing fall hazards. The manufacturer (Tuyedoqe, China) was issued a Notice of Violation but refused to recall the product or offer any remedy. About 250 units were sold on Amazon.com from April 2025 through October 2025 for about $40. The CPSC urges parents to immediately remove the foam padding, cut the cover in half, and dispose of both.

**Why it matters:** Company refused to recall — parents must take action themselves. Strangulation risk is life-threatening.

### STORY 2: D49 and D20 Lead Colorado in New Purple Star School Designations [TIER 1]
**Pillar:** Local News
On April 7, 2026, Governor Polis and the Colorado Department of Education designated 22 new Purple Star Schools statewide. Of those, 21 are in Southern Colorado — and D49 earned the most Purple Star schools of any district in the state, with D20 earning the second most. The designation recognizes schools that provide targeted support for military-connected students and their families, including dedicated military liaisons, school website resources, and student-led transition programs. Colorado Springs is home to Fort Carson, NORAD, Peterson Space Force Base, and Schriever Space Force Base.

**Why it matters:** Massive local win for COS military families — both major school districts lead the state.

### STORY 5: Pediatricians Are Now Asking About Guns at Well-Child Checkups [TIER 2]
**Pillar:** National Parenting
Research presented at the 2026 Pediatric Academic Societies (PAS) meeting in Boston (April 24-27) found that quality improvement programs can significantly increase the rate at which pediatricians screen for firearms in the home during well-child visits. The AAP recommends that all pediatricians ask parents about gun storage as a routine safety topic. For Colorado Springs families — particularly those with military connections and higher rates of legal gun ownership — knowing what to expect at your child's checkup and how to safely store firearms can literally save lives.

**Why it matters:** Relevant for COS's military-heavy population; parents should know the conversation is coming and why.
