### STORY 1: Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinet CPSC Warning

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**

🚨 If you have a Tuyedoqe Travel Bassinet, the CPSC wants you to stop using it immediately.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission issued an urgent warning this week about these bassinets, which were sold on Amazon between April and October 2025 for about $40. The problem: the restraints inside pose a strangulation hazard to infants, and the sides are too low to contain a baby, creating a fall risk. There's no stand either, which means a serious fall risk if placed on an elevated surface.

Here's where it gets complicated: the manufacturer, Tuyedoqe (based in China), was issued a Notice of Violation by the CPSC — but they've refused to issue a recall or offer any remedy to consumers. That means there's no refund, no exchange, and no company coming to fix this.

What you should do right now: stop use immediately, remove the foam sleeping pad, cut the cover in half, and dispose of both. The bassinets came in pink, green, gray, sky blue, and wine red.

If you bought this on Amazon, check your order history — the sale window was April through October 2025.

Have you seen this product, or do you know other parents who might have it? Share this post so the word gets out.

### STORY 2: Purple Star Schools — D49/D20 Lead Colorado

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:00 AM MT

**Long-Form Post (10:00 AM MT)**

This is a big deal for Colorado Springs military families.

On April 7, Governor Polis and the Colorado Department of Education recognized 22 new Purple Star Schools across the state — schools that provide dedicated support for military-connected kids. Of those 22, a remarkable 21 are in Southern Colorado.

And COS led the way: D49 earned the most Purple Star school designations of any district in Colorado. D20 came in second.

The Purple Star designation is meaningful. It's not just an honor — it's a commitment. Schools have to maintain a dedicated military liaison, keep resources for military families on their website, and run a student-led program to help military kids transition smoothly. Given that Colorado Springs is home to Fort Carson, NORAD, Peterson Space Force Base, and Schriever Space Force Base, these programs serve a real and essential need.

Military families move frequently. Kids change schools. Having schools that are prepared, resourced, and welcoming makes a genuine difference.

If your family is military and you're wondering which schools earned the Purple Star designation, check with D49 or D20 directly — and watch for announcements from individual schools.

Does your family have a connection to the military? We'd love to hear your experience with Colorado Springs schools.

### STORY 5: Pediatricians Now Screening for Firearms at Checkups

#### Long-Form Post -- 5:00 PM MT

**Long-Form Post (5:00 PM MT)**

If your child's pediatrician asks about guns at the next well-child checkup, don't be surprised — it's part of a growing evidence-based approach to keeping kids safe.

New research presented at the 2026 Pediatric Academic Societies (PAS) meeting in Boston showed that quality improvement programs can meaningfully increase the rate at which doctors screen for firearm storage in the home during well-child visits. The American Academy of Pediatrics has long recommended that pediatricians ask parents about whether firearms are present in the home and whether they're stored safely.

For Colorado Springs families — with one of the largest military populations in the country — this conversation is especially relevant. Legal gun ownership is common in our community, and safe storage is the single most effective way to prevent accidental shootings and youth suicide.

Safe storage doesn't mean giving up your firearm. It means: locked gun safes, trigger locks, ammunition stored separately, firearms stored unloaded. These measures are proven to reduce the risk of a child accessing a gun.

If your child's doctor brings it up at the next checkup, take it as the health and safety conversation it's intended to be. And if you'd like more information on free or low-cost gun lock programs in Colorado Springs, resources are available through local community health organizations.
