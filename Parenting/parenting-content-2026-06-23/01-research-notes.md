**Key Facts (Verified):**
- [HIGH] 9 children died from vehicular heatstroke in 2026 (as of reporting; deaths occurred in CA, FL, LA, MD, NM, NJ, NC per abc7.com)
- [HIGH] Children's bodies heat 3–5 times faster than adults
- [HIGH] Cars can heat 20°F in 20 minutes

- [HIGH] Majority of cases: caregiver accidentally left child (loss of awareness, not neglect)
- [HIGH] NHTSA prevention tips: always check back seat before locking; place phone or bag next to car seat; never leave children alone even briefly
