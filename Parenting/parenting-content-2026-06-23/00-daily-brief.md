**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting (Product Safety)
**Status:** NEW STORY (different from GOPO recall covered June 22)
**Sources:** cpsc.gov, foodpoisoningbulletin.com, newsweek.com
**Posting Window:** 10:00 AM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

The CPSC recalled ABC Trading's WSDZ Light-up Glasses and electronic pet cage toys (Dinosaur Tribes model 8266, My Pet Bird model ZH998-23) due to a button cell battery ingestion hazard. About 84,700 units affected. The battery compartments are easily accessible by children, posing a deadly ingestion hazard. When swallowed, button cell/coin batteries cause internal chemical burns and death. Sold at TOYZ and Joissu Product stores nationwide, November 2022–October 2025, $5–$9. Action: stop use immediately; take a photo of the disposed product in the trash; email photo to recallabc@gmail.com for a full refund. Contact: 323-581-3688 (M-F 9:30 AM–6 PM PT). Website: abctradinginc.com/recall-2. cpsc.gov.

### STORY 4: Hot Car Safety — Temperatures Rise, Children at Risk; What COS Parents Need to Know
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Status:** NEW STORY (summer safety reminder; 9 hot car deaths in 2026 so far nationwide; COS summer heat underway)
**Sources:** kidsandcars.org, nhtsa.gov, wdbj7.com, abc7.com
**Posting Window:** 3:00 PM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Nine children have died from vehicular heatstroke nationwide in 2026. Summer temperatures make this a critical safety reminder for Colorado Springs parents. A child's body heats 3 to 5 times faster than an adult's; a car can heat 20 degrees in just 20 minutes; children are at serious risk when internal body temperature reaches 107°F. The majority of hot car deaths involve caregivers who accidentally left a child — not neglect, but loss of awareness. NHTSA prevention tips: always check back seat before locking; place phone or bag next to car seat; never leave children alone even briefly. ACK (look Before You Lock) method. Colorado Springs temperatures in late June regularly reach the 80s–90s°F; parked cars are far hotter.
