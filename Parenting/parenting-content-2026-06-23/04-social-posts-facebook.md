### STORY 2: ABC Trading Toy Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

Parents — if you have light-up glasses or small electronic pet toys with "cage" designs at home, check them against this recall right now.

The CPSC recalled ABC Trading's WSDZ Light-Up Glasses and electronic pet cage toys — the Dinosaur Tribes model (silver cage with a red dinosaur) and the My Pet Bird model (pink cage with a blue bird). About 84,700 units are affected. These were sold at TOYZ and Joissu Product stores nationwide between November 2022 and October 2025, priced $5 to $9.

The hazard: the battery compartments can be opened too easily by children. Button cell and coin batteries, when swallowed, cause internal chemical burns and can be fatal. This is not a slow-developing hazard — the danger is immediate.

What to do: stop using the toys immediately. Take a photo of the disposed toy in your trash. Email the photo to recallabc@gmail.com to receive a full refund. If you have questions, call ABC Trading at 323-581-3688, Monday through Friday 9:30 AM to 6:00 PM PT. More info at cpsc.gov.

### STORY 4: Hot Car Safety

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

This is one of those reminders that feels uncomfortable to read — and important enough to share anyway.

Nine children died from vehicular heatstroke in the United States in 2026. The summer isn't over. Colorado Springs is getting hot, and parked cars get hotter faster than most people realize: a car's interior temperature can rise 20°F in just 20 minutes. Children's bodies heat 3 to 5 times faster than an adult's. When a child's internal body temperature reaches 107°F, the risk becomes life-threatening.

The overwhelming majority of hot car deaths don't happen because of neglect. They happen because a loving, present caregiver lost awareness — a routine changed, a distraction hit, and the child in the back seat became invisible for a moment. That's what makes this worth talking about.

Here's what NHTSA recommends: build a habit of checking the back seat every time before you lock the car. Place your phone, bag, or something you'll need right next to your child's car seat — so you're always drawn back there. If you see a child in a hot car, call 911 immediately.

### STORY 5: D49 Student Success Center

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

D49 families — there's a meaningful change coming to specialized student services this August.

District 49 is opening a new Student Success Center at the start of the 2026-2027 school year, housed in the former Falcon Elementary School of Technology building. It brings several programs together under one roof:

Full-time preschool runs Monday through Thursday, with Base49 providing before- and after-school care, plus Friday childcare options — making it more accessible for working families.

The Elevates program (for students ages 18 to 21 in transition services) relocates here, continuing to provide essential life skills instruction and meaningful community experiences within the district.

The facility also houses the Motor Team (physical and occupational therapists and assistants), the Assistive Technology Team, the SWAP Coordinator, and the homebound special education teacher.

If you have a D49 child in preschool, Elevates, OT/PT services, or assistive tech support — this is worth understanding before the school year starts. Visit d49.org for more.
