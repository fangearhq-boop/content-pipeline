D49 Opens Student Success Center in August 2026 — What COS Families Need to Know

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | June 23, 2026

District 49 will open a new Student Success Center at the start of the 2026-2027 school year, bringing several specialized student services together under one roof. The center will be housed in the building formerly known as Falcon Elementary School of Technology and opens in August 2026.

If your family is in D49 and your child receives preschool services, OT or PT support, transition services, or assistive technology services, here's what's changing.

Full-Time Preschool Program

The Student Success Center will house two full-time preschool classrooms running Monday through Thursday. In partnership with Base49, families will also have access to before- and after-school care on school days and Friday childcare — giving working families a more complete weekly option. This expands D49's early childhood access within the district's specialized support framework.

Elevates 18–21 Transition Program

The Elevates program, which serves D49 students ages 18 to 21, will relocate to the Student Success Center. Elevates provides transition services focused on essential life skills and meaningful community experiences — helping students in this age range build toward independence and workforce participation. Moving Elevates to the SSC keeps students within the district while expanding the facility's capacity to serve them alongside related support teams.

Motor Team and Assistive Technology

The SSC will also house D49's Motor Team — the district's physical therapists, occupational therapists, and therapy assistants — as well as the Assistive Technology Team. Having these specialized staff in one dedicated location makes coordination between services more efficient and provides a consistent point of contact for families whose students work with multiple specialists.

The School to Workforce Alliance Program (SWAP) Coordinator and D49's homebound special education teacher will also operate out of the new center.

Why D49 Is Making This Change

D49 describes the consolidation as a strategic realignment and resource consolidation — placing students and staff in sustainable existing spaces to provide more targeted support. Rather than distributing specialized services across multiple campuses, the Student Success Center model centralizes the staff and resources that work most closely together. For families navigating multiple services, having one location can simplify scheduling and communication.

What's Next

The Student Success Center opens in August 2026 at the start of D49's new school year. If your child currently receives any of the services moving to the SSC, watch for direct communication from D49 about logistics. For enrollment questions, preschool information, or to learn more about the Elevates program, visit d49.org or contact your child's school directly.
