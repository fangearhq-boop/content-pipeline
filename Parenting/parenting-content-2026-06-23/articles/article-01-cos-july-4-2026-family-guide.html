Your COS Family Guide to July 4, 2026 — Star-Spangled Symphony, Rock Ledge Ranch, and More

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | June 23, 2026

July 4 is 11 days away, and this year carries extra meaning: 2026 is America's 250th birthday, and Colorado Springs has some genuinely memorable ways to spend the day with your family. Whether you're looking for a ticketed symphony-and-fireworks evening or a completely free daytime celebration, COS has options worth planning around.

Star-Spangled Symphony at Ford Amphitheater

The flagship event is the Star-Spangled Symphony and Grand Fireworks Show at Ford Amphitheater, 95 Spectrum Loop, Colorado Springs, CO 80921. Gates open at 5:30 PM. The show starts at 6:30 PM and fireworks light up the Pikes Peak skyline around 9:00 PM.

The program opens with Fort Carson's 4th Infantry Division Band before the Colorado Springs Philharmonic takes over. The evening includes hits from Dolly Parton, Marvin Gaye, and Tina Turner, a Star Wars suite, the 1812 Overture, and Stars and Stripes Forever — as American an evening as you'll find in the region. The event is presented by the Colorado Springs Philharmonic and the Colorado Springs Sports Corporation.

For seating: if your family wants to hear the music well, sit toward the front. For the best fireworks view, the back sections, Veranda, Owner's Clubs, and lawn offer unobstructed sky views. Tickets are available at fordamphitheater.live.

Free Option: Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site

Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site offers a free July 4th celebration from 10 AM to 4 PM on Saturday July 4. Families can experience a Civil War encampment living history demonstration, join a kids' patriotic parade, tour historic homes on the ranch, play festival games, and enjoy live music and historic speeches. No ticket required.

Rock Ledge Ranch is a working historic site off Gateway Road near Garden of the Gods — a genuinely different kind of July 4 experience that connects kids to the story of the country in a tangible way.

Free Option: Palmer Lake Centennial Park

Palmer Lake Centennial Park hosts a free community celebration from 4 PM to 10 PM on July 4. The event includes food vendors, local shopping, giveaways, interactive activities, and a family-friendly atmosphere. Palmer Lake is about 20 minutes north of downtown Colorado Springs.

Planning Tips for Colorado Springs Families

July 4 evening traffic around the Ford Amphitheater can be significant — plan to arrive closer to gate opening time (5:30 PM) rather than show start. Bringing blankets and layers is smart even in summer; COS evenings cool quickly after sunset. If your kids struggle with the volume of fireworks, ear protection is worth packing.

For the free options, Rock Ledge Ranch parking is typically easier and more manageable than the amphitheater on the Fourth. The daytime timing (10 AM to 4 PM) also works better for families with younger kids who don't do well in late-night crowds.
