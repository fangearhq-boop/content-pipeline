Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo Returns July 14–18 — Kids Tickets from $10, Mutton Bustin, Fan Zone

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | June 23, 2026

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo is one of Colorado Springs' most enduring family traditions. The 2026 edition runs July 14 through 18 at Norris Penrose Event Center and brings professional rodeo action, a full family Fan Zone, and one of the most genuinely fun kid experiences in COS summer — Mutton Bustin.

Event Schedule

The NFR Open competition runs five nights with evening performances starting at 7:30 PM. For families with younger kids, matinee performances on Friday July 17 and Saturday July 18 begin at 12:30 PM — a more manageable schedule that doesn't push bedtime and gives families the full rodeo experience in afternoon light.

Ticket Pricing and Kids Discounts

Children 12 and under receive $15 off the adult ticket price at any performance. That brings End Zone Grandstand tickets from $25 (adult) down to $10 for kids, and Regular Grandstand seats from $35 down to $20 for kids. Tickets are available only through official sources: PikesPeakorBust.org and NorrisPenrose.com (powered by TicketSpice). Do not purchase from third-party sellers.

The Fan Zone

Before each performance, the Fan Zone gives younger kids — and honestly everyone — a reason to arrive early. Activities include pony rides, a petting zoo, face painting, and goat roping. It's the kind of hands-on Western experience that connects kids to the event beyond just watching from the stands.

Mutton Bustin

Mutton Bustin is open to kids ages 4 to 9 who weigh 50 lbs or less. The premise is straightforward: a child holds on to a sheep for as long as possible. The crowd loves it. It's become one of the defining moments of the Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo for families with kids in that age range, and it's a story they'll be telling for years. Check PikesPeakorBust.org for registration details and availability.

About the Rodeo

The Pikes Peak or Bust Rodeo has been a Colorado Springs community institution since its founding and has a longstanding commitment to supporting military families — a tradition that makes it a meaningful event beyond just the sports competition. Norris Penrose Event Center is located west of downtown Colorado Springs and has ample parking for family groups.

What's Next

Tickets are available now at PikesPeakorBust.org and NorrisPenrose.com. Evening performances start July 14 at 7:30 PM; the first family-friendly matinee is Friday July 17 at 12:30 PM. Check PikesPeakorBust.org for Mutton Bustin registration details and Fan Zone hours for each performance night.
