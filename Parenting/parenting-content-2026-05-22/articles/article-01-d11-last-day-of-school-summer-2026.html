D11 Last Day of School: Summer Break Begins for Colorado Springs Families Today

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 22, 2026

Today, Friday May 22, is the last day of school for all Colorado Springs District 11 students. After a graduation week that ran from May 19 through May 21, the academic year is officially complete for the Class of 2026 — and for the approximately 26,000 students across D11's elementary and middle schools, the final bell today marks the beginning of summer break.

This is the moment Colorado Springs families have been counting down to. For parents and students, it also means a shift in routine — and a summer calendar that is worth planning now, before schedules fill up.

Graduation Week Recap

D11 closed out the 2025-26 academic year with a full week of commencement ceremonies. Palmer High School started the week on May 19 at Ed Robson Arena. Coronado High School and Mitchell High School followed on May 20, with ceremonies at 9:00 AM and 3:00 PM respectively. The final ceremonies took place on May 21 — Achieve High, Tesla STEM High, and Bijou School at 9:00 AM, and Odyssey Elementary Charter School at 3:00 PM, all at Ed Robson Arena at Colorado College.

Livestreams of the ceremonies are available at d11.org/calendars/graduations for families who could not attend in person.

What to Know About the Rest of Memorial Day Weekend

The last day of school falls right before Memorial Day weekend, which gives D11 families a full three-day stretch to start summer. This weekend, Territory Days continues in Old Colorado City (Colorado Ave, 23rd to 27th Street) through Monday, May 25, with free admission. The Broadmoor World Arena hosts three District 49 graduation ceremonies tomorrow, May 23.

Summer Programs Starting Soon

For families looking to keep their children engaged during the break, several programs launch within the next two weeks.

YMCA Pikes Peak Region summer camps begin on May 27 at three Colorado Springs locations: Briargate, First and Main, and Tri-Lakes. Themed week programming includes sports camps and specialty mini-camps. Registration is available at ppymca.org or by calling the individual Y branches.

PPLD Summer Adventure begins June 2 across all Pikes Peak Library District branches. The 2026 theme is "Unearth a Story" and the program is free and open to all ages, running through July 31. Families can register now at ppld.org/summer.
