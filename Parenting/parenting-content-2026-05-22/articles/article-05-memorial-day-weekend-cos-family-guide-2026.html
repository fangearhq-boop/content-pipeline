COS Memorial Day Weekend Wrap-Up: Territory Days Final Day, D49 Graduation, and Summer Begins

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 22, 2026

With D11's last day of school today and Memorial Day Monday just ahead, Colorado Springs families are entering the official start of summer. Here is a complete guide to what is happening this weekend — from the final day of Territory Days to D49 graduation ceremonies to what comes next on the summer calendar.

Territory Days: Final Day Is Monday

Territory Days has been running since Saturday morning in Old Colorado City, and Monday, May 25 (Memorial Day) is the final day. The event is free to attend and runs from 10:00 AM to 6:00 PM on Monday — a shorter window than the Saturday and Sunday hours (10 AM to 7 PM). The festival occupies Colorado Avenue from 23rd Street to 27th Street.

If you have not yet made it to Territory Days this year, Monday is your last chance. The festival features more than 180 food and craft vendor booths, live music throughout the day, a children's play area, a petting zoo, Indigenous dancers, Wild West gunfight demonstrations, and blacksmith demonstrations. Parking is available at C.A.B. Garage for $1 per hour, with a free shuttle to the festival area.

D49 Graduation: Three Ceremonies Saturday

District 49 holds all three of its major graduation ceremonies on Saturday, May 23 at Broadmoor World Arena (3185 Venetucci Blvd, Colorado Springs). The schedule:

  • Sand Creek High School: 9:00 AM
  • Falcon High School: 1:00 PM
  • Vista Ridge High School: 5:00 PM

Seating for the Falcon and Vista Ridge ceremonies is first-come, first-served with no tickets required. Families attending the Sand Creek ceremony should arrive early given the 9:00 AM start time. All three ceremonies are at the same venue — Broadmoor World Arena — so families with students at different schools can plan their Saturday accordingly.

Also This Weekend: MeadowGrass Music Festival

The 17th Annual MeadowGrass Music Festival is running simultaneously from Friday through Sunday at La Foret Conference and Retreat Center in the Black Forest (6145 Shoup Road, about 30 minutes from downtown). Kids 12 and under are free with a paid adult. The festival features live music, camping, Kid Zone activities (Giant Bubbles, crafts, hay wagon, Kid Yoga), and a full weekend program. Tickets at Eventbrite or rockymountainhighway.org/meadowgrass/.

What Comes Next: Summer Calendar

After this weekend, the summer calendar for Colorado Springs families picks up quickly:

  • May 27: YMCA Pikes Peak summer camps begin at Briargate, First and Main, and Tri-Lakes. Registration at ppymca.org.
  • June 2: PPLD Summer Adventure begins — free, all ages, all Pikes Peak Library District branches. Theme: "Unearth a Story." Register at ppld.org/summer.
  • June 6: El Pomar's Penrose Heritage Museum Family Exploration Day — free public event.

What's Next

Memorial Day closes out the official events calendar for May in Colorado Springs. After Monday, families settle into the summer routine: camps starting May 27, library programs starting June 2, and the longer days that come with a Colorado summer. D11's 2026-27 school year begins in late August. Between now and then, there is no shortage of things to do in COS.
