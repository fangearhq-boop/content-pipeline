CPSC Recall: 9,300 Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers — Stop Use If You Have One

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 22, 2026

The Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) has recalled approximately 9,300 Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers due to serious fall and entrapment hazards. The loungers were sold at Amazon.com and Walmart.com between December 2023 and October 2025 for between $40 and $60. If you purchased one of these products, stop using it immediately and follow the steps below to receive a full refund.

The Safety Hazard

The CPSC identified two structural problems with the Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers that violate the Mandatory Standard for Infant Sleep Products.

First, the sides of the lounger are too low to contain an infant properly. An infant can tip or roll over the side and fall. Second, the enclosed openings at the foot end of the lounger are wider than what is permitted under the safety standard — wide enough that an infant's torso can become entrapped. The loungers also have no stand, which creates an additional fall hazard if placed on an elevated surface like a bed or couch.

No incidents or injuries were reported at the time of the recall — but the hazards are structural and present regardless of how carefully the product is used.

How to Identify the Product

The recalled baby loungers are made of a foam sleeping pad and padded bumpers enclosed in a cloth cover. The covers zip closed and have a strap handle at the top for carrying. The loungers were sold in the following fabric styles: green avocado, blue dinosaur, blue lambs, blue zoo animals, green minky, neutral elephants, pink zoo animals, and white minky.

If you purchased a Joyful Journeys baby lounger — including as a baby shower gift that may have been given to you — check whether the product matches this description.

What to Do

The CPSC requires destruction of the recalled product before issuing a refund. Follow these steps:

  1. Stop using the baby lounger immediately.
  2. Remove the foam pad and padded bumpers from the cloth cover.
  3. Cut the cloth cover, the foam pad, and the padded bumpers in half.
  4. Take a clear photo showing all three components cut in half.
  5. Email the photo to joyfuljourneysrecalls@myjoyfuljourneys.com and request a full refund.

Do not attempt to sell, donate, or give away the recalled lounger. The CPSC issues these destruction-before-refund requirements specifically to prevent recalled products from entering secondary markets and posing hazards to other families.

What's Next

Families who purchased a Joyful Journeys Baby Lounger should act on this recall as soon as possible. The destruction-and-refund process is straightforward, and the safety commission's guidance is clear.
