MeadowGrass 2026 Starts Today — Kids Get In Free at This COS Family Festival

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 22, 2026

The 17th Annual MeadowGrass Music Festival begins today at La Foret Conference and Retreat Center in the Black Forest, and if you have children under 12, the admission situation is straightforward: they get in free with a paid adult. The festival runs Friday through Sunday, May 22-24, on a property spanning hundreds of acres of Ponderosa forest, canyons, and historic buildings — about 30 minutes from downtown Colorado Springs.

For families navigating a holiday weekend that already includes Territory Days and D49 graduation ceremonies, MeadowGrass is worth knowing about. Here is what to expect.

The Kid Zone

The Faricy Boys Kid Zone is a dedicated children's programming area within the festival. Activities include Giant Bubbles from Milibo Art Theater, crafts from Concrete Couch, a hay wagon, Kid Yoga, and art activities. This is not a small afterthought added for parents who need somewhere to take children while adults listen to music — it is a substantial program built around the audience.

For families with children who have limited tolerance for long sets or crowded music stages, having a dedicated kids' zone with rotating activities gives parents options throughout the day.

Logistics: Camping, Lodging, and Tickets

Camping is available on-site in the Ponderosa Forest, giving families the option to stay for the full weekend without commuting back and forth. For families who prefer something less rustic, Lapp Lodge offers limited luxury lodging — 7 rooms, each accommodating up to 5 people (4 adults or a family of up to 5).

The festival also includes yoga sessions, hikes, workshops, and a Beer Festival (Friday evening). Hours run from 12 PM to 11:30 PM each day. The venue is at 6145 Shoup Road, Colorado Springs, CO 80908. Tickets are available at Eventbrite and at rockymountainhighway.org/meadowgrass/.
