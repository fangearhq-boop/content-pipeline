### STORY 3: East COS Childcare Desert — Resources for Families Struggling to Find Care
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting / Local News
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Type:** NEW STORY (KOAA investigated; east side childcare gap not previously covered)

A KOAA investigation has confirmed what many Colorado Springs parents already know: eastern Colorado Springs is a childcare desert. El Paso County has nearly three children aged zero to six for every one available childcare spot. The problem is most concentrated on the east and southeast sides, and in the Fountain-Fort Carson area. Childcare costs reach approximately $20,000 per year in the region, forcing some parents out of the workforce. Existing state and local programs can help — here is what families need to know.

**Key facts:**
- El Paso County ratio: nearly 3 children ages 0-6 per available childcare spot
- Hardest-hit areas: east Colorado Springs, southeast, Fountain-Fort Carson corridor
- Cost: ~$20,000 per year for full-time childcare in the region
- Impact: high costs and lack of availability forcing some parents to leave the workforce
- Resource 1: Colorado Child Care Assistance Program (CCCAP) — low-income subsidies; El Paso County: 719-444-8178
- Resource 2: Pikes Peak Region Family Child Care Association — helps find licensed care; 719-475-8828
- Resource 3: Colorado Shines — rates early learning programs, free search tool at coloradoshines.com
- Resource 4: Colorado Enterprise Fund (CEF) — Child Care Loan Fund for childcare providers
- Resource 5: PPLD LibGuide — "Child Care and Early Learning" at research.ppld.org
- Sources: KOAA "A childcare desert" investigation, New loan program KOAA article, Colorado Department of Early Childhood

### STORY 4: CPSC Recall — Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers (9,300 Units, Amazon and Walmart)
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Type:** NEW STORY (different from all prior recalls covered: Cosyland May 19, YCXXKJ May 20, Vevor/Bellabu Bear May 21)

Joyful Journeys has recalled approximately 9,300 baby loungers due to serious fall and entrapment hazards — the loungers' sides are too low to contain an infant, and enclosed openings at the foot are wider than allowed by mandatory safety standards. The loungers were sold on Amazon.com and Walmart.com from December 2023 through October 2025 for $40-$60. Consumers should stop use immediately, destroy the product, and send photos for a full refund.

**Key facts:**
- Product: Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers (foam sleeping pad + padded bumpers with cloth cover)
- Styles: green avocado, blue dinosaur, blue lambs, blue zoo animals, green minky, neutral elephants, pink zoo animals, white minky
- Sold: Amazon.com and Walmart.com, December 2023-October 2025, $40-$60
- Units: ~9,300
- Hazards: (1) sides too low — infant can fall out; (2) foot openings too wide — infant torso can become entrapped; (3) no stand — falls on elevated surfaces
- Safety standard violated: Mandatory Standard for Infant Sleep Products
- Action: STOP USE IMMEDIATELY
- Remedy: Remove foam/pads from cover, cut cover AND foam AND pad in half, email photos of destroyed pieces to joyfuljourneysrecalls@myjoyfuljourneys.com for FULL REFUND
- Contact: joyfuljourneysrecalls@myjoyfuljourneys.com
- Sources: CPSC.gov official recall, cwcolumbus.com, growingyourbaby.com
