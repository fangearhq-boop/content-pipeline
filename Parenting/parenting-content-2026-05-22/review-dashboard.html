East COS Childcare Desert — Resources for Families Struggling to Find Care
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 3:00 PM MT If you are a Colorado Springs parent who has spent weeks calling childcare providers only to hear "no openings," you are not alone — and the numbers explain why. A KOAA investigation found that El Paso County has nearly three children aged zero to six competing for every one available childcare spot. The problem is most concentrated on the east side, including the southeast corridor and the Fountain-Fort Carson area. Full-time childcare in the region costs approximately $20,000 per year — an amount that is causing some families to leave the workforce entirely rather than pay it. The good news: there are programs designed to help. Here is where to start. CCCAP (Colorado Child Care Assistance Program): Provides childcare subsidies for low-income families. El Paso County contact: 719-444-8178. Pikes Peak Region Family Child Care Association: Helps families find licensed childcare providers in El Paso County. Call 719-475-8828. Colorado Shines: A free online tool to search and compare licensed early learning programs across Colorado. Search at coloradoshines.com. Colorado Enterprise Fund: The Child Care Loan Fund helps childcare providers who cannot access traditional financing — which can indirectly increase available spots in underserved areas. PPLD LibGuide: Pikes Peak Library District maintains a "Child Care and Early Learning" resource guide at research.ppld.org with additional local contacts. Have you navigated the childcare system in east Colorado Springs? What worked for your family? Share below. ---

CPSC Recall — Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers (9,300 Units, Amazon and Walmart)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 5:00 PM MT If you purchased a Joyful Journeys baby lounger on Amazon or Walmart, please stop using it immediately. The Consumer Product Safety Commission (CPSC) has recalled approximately 9,300 Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers due to serious fall and entrapment hazards. The loungers were sold online at Amazon.com and Walmart.com from December 2023 through October 2025 for between $40 and $60. THE PROBLEM: The sides of the loungers are too low to properly contain an infant, and the enclosed openings at the foot of the lounger are wider than what is allowed under the Mandatory Standard for Infant Sleep Products. The loungers also have no stand, creating a fall hazard on elevated surfaces. The recalled loungers came in these styles: green avocado, blue dinosaur, blue lambs, blue zoo animals, green minky, neutral elephants, pink zoo animals, and white minky. WHAT TO DO: 1. Stop using the lounger immediately. 2. Remove the foam pad and padded bumpers from the cloth cover. 3. Cut the cover, foam, and pad in half. 4. Email photos of the destroyed pieces to joyfuljourneysrecalls@myjoyfuljourneys.com to receive a full refund. Do not attempt to sell or donate the recalled lounger. This is a safety issue that affects other families. Share this post with anyone who may have purchased one of these loungers. ---
