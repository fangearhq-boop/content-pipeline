Downtown Farmers Market Returns to Acacia Park Sunday — Free, Local, and Family-Friendly

By Jamie Rivera | May 9, 2026 | COS Parenting

The Downtown Colorado Springs Farmers Market opens for the 2026 season this Sunday, May 10, at Acacia Park. It's free to attend and runs every Sunday through October 25.

The Market Returns

After a winter break, the Downtown Colorado Springs Farmers Market is back. Starting Sunday, May 10, the market runs every week at Acacia Park in the heart of downtown from 9 AM to 2 PM. The season continues through October 25 — giving Colorado Springs families a reliable Sunday ritual for more than five months.

Admission is free. There's no registration, no app required, no reservation. Just show up, browse, and bring cash or a card for the vendors you want to support.

What You'll Find

The Downtown Farmers Market brings together local vendors selling a range of fresh and locally produced goods. Typical offerings include:

  • Fresh vegetables and fruits from regional farms
  • Locally baked breads, pastries, and sweet goods
  • Artisan products including honey, jams, sauces, and specialty foods
  • Flowers and plants for the garden
  • Handmade crafts and goods from local makers

Inventory changes week to week with the season. Early in the summer, expect early greens and baked goods. By midsummer, the produce selection expands significantly.

Why This Market Works for Families

A farmers market can be more than a grocery run. For families with kids in Colorado Springs, the market offers a relaxed, low-cost outing that also builds real connections to food, agriculture, and community.

Children who meet the farmers and makers who grow and produce their food develop a different relationship with what they eat. The sensory environment — smells, colors, textures, samples — is engaging for kids of almost any age. And at a Sunday pace, there's no rush.

Acacia Park is centrally located at 115 E. Pikes Peak Ave., with bus access nearby and paid street parking available in the surrounding blocks. The park itself is open and walkable, making it easy for families with strollers.
