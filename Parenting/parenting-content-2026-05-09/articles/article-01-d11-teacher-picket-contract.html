D11 Teachers and Parents Picket Board Meeting — 300 Days Without a Contract

By Jamie Rivera | May 9, 2026 | COS Parenting

The D11 labor dispute is now more than 300 days old. Here's what that means for families with kids in Colorado Springs' largest district.

What's Happening

On May 6, educators and parents gathered outside the District 11 Board of Education meeting to mark a grim milestone: more than 300 days since D11's Board ended the district's 56-year master agreement with teachers.

The Colorado Springs Education Association (CSEA) organized the picket, bringing together teachers, support staff, and community members who say the loss of the master agreement has created instability in classrooms across the district. Parents joined to signal that this isn't just a dispute between administrators and educators — it's a community-wide concern.

What Teachers Are Asking For

The CSEA's demands center on conditions that directly affect students' daily experiences in school:

Demand Why It Matters to Families
Smaller class sizes More teacher attention per student; fewer behavioral disruptions
More school counselors and social workers Better mental health support for students struggling academically or emotionally
More special education support staff Students with IEPs and 504 plans get the specialized help they're entitled to

These aren't abstract policy positions. Every D11 parent who has noticed a stressed teacher, a crowded classroom, or a long wait for a counseling appointment has felt the effects of these resource gaps firsthand.

The "Live Handbook" Problem

One of the more striking details to emerge from the dispute: teachers are now operating under an online "handbook" rather than a negotiated contract. The handbook is kept in a "live mode," meaning the district can update it at any time without notifying educators.

In practical terms, this means teachers may not know from one week to the next what the rules governing their employment actually are. Educators described the situation as creating anxiety and uncertainty that can spill over into the classroom environment.

The previous master agreement had been in place for 56 years. Its replacement by an editable digital document represents a significant shift in how D11 manages its relationship with teaching staff.

Background: A Year of Conflict

The current dispute has roots in late 2024, when D11's Board severed the master contract. Educators went on strike in October 2025 — one of the few public school teacher strikes in Colorado Springs history. The strike ended, but the underlying issues remain unresolved more than six months later.

District 11 is Colorado Springs' largest school district, serving tens of thousands of students across elementary, middle, and high schools throughout the city.

What Families Can Do

Families with students in D11 who want to stay informed or get involved have several options:

  • Attend D11 Board of Education meetings (dates and locations at d11.org)
  • Contact your elected D11 Board representatives directly
  • Follow updates from the Colorado Springs Education Association
  • Talk to your child's teachers and school counselors about classroom conditions

What's Next

The picket on May 6 was a signal — not a resolution. The CSEA and D11 remain in dispute, with no timeline publicly announced for a return to contract negotiations. Families in D11 can expect continued community organizing in the weeks ahead as educators keep the issue visible. COS Parenting will continue to cover developments as they affect families across the district.
