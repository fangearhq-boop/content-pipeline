Territory Days Turns 50 — COS's Biggest Free Festival is Back for Memorial Day Weekend

By Sarah Morales | May 9, 2026 | COS Parenting

The Memorial Day Weekend tradition that Colorado Springs has built for half a century returns to Old Colorado City, May 23-25 — and it's free for the whole family.

The 50th Anniversary

What started as a small neighborhood craft fair in historic Old Colorado City has grown into one of Colorado Springs' most beloved community traditions. Territory Days celebrates its 50th anniversary this year, running May 23, 24, and 25 across Memorial Day Weekend in the historic district west of downtown.

Admission is free. The event footprint is ADA compliant throughout. There is no registration, no tickets required — just show up and enjoy one of the city's most consistently family-friendly weekends of the year.

The Parking Reality — and the Free Shuttle

Let's talk logistics, because this is where Territory Days can trip up first-time families: parking in Old Colorado City during the festival is extremely limited. Street parking fills fast, and walking with young children from distant spots can be frustrating.

The good news is there's a better option: a free shuttle runs from the Colorado Springs C.A.B. Garage downtown to the festival throughout the weekend. Take the shuttle, skip the parking stress, and arrive ready to enjoy the event rather than hunting for a spot.

The event footprint is ADA compliant throughout, making it accessible for families with strollers, wheelchairs, or mobility needs. Note that dogs are not permitted at Territory Days.

Planning Your Visit

Territory Days runs all three days of Memorial Day Weekend — Saturday, Sunday, and Monday. If your family has flexibility, spreading across more than one day lets you see more without rushing. Mornings tend to be less crowded and cooler. The full schedule of performances is posted at shopoldcoloradocity.com as the event approaches.

What's Next

Territory Days 2026 runs May 23-25 in Historic Old Colorado City. Admission is free. Use the free shuttle from the Colorado Springs C.A.B. Garage downtown to avoid parking frustration. For the full schedule and vendor map, visit shopoldcoloradocity.com or visitcos.com.
