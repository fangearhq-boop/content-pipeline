### STORY 3: CPSC Recall — ZMC Group Light-Up Toys (Button Battery Ingestion Risk)
- **Tier:** 1
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Posting window:** 12:00 PM MT
- **Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
- **Classification:** NEW STORY
- **Summary:** The CPSC recalled 124,560 battery-operated light-up toys from ZMC Group (announced May 7, 2026). Products include yo-yos, maracas, Halloween wands, and light-up sticks. The toys violate mandatory safety standards because button cell batteries are easily accessible to children — if swallowed, they can cause severe internal chemical burns and death. The toys were sold at discount stores from May 2023 through April 2026 for approximately $1. No injuries reported. Refund: email a photo of the destroyed toy to recallzmctoy@gmail.com or call 888-407-3225.
- **Sources:** cpsc.gov (ZMC Group Recalls Multiple Light Up Toys — official recall notice); wsoctv.com (Safety Recall: 124,560 Light-Up Toys by ZMC Group); consumeraffairs.com (Consumer product safety recall roundup May 8, 2026)

### STORY 4: AAP Updates Screen Time Guidance — Quality Over Limits in 2026
- **Tier:** 2
- **Pillar:** National Parenting
- **Posting window:** 3:00 PM MT
- **Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
- **Classification:** NEW STORY (different from May 8 immunization schedule; this is screen time guidance)
- **Summary:** The American Academy of Pediatrics released updated screen time guidelines for 2026, shifting from the previous "2 hours max per day" rule for older kids to a quality-focused, family-centered approach. Key rules unchanged: no screens before 18 months (video chat excepted); 1 hour/day of high-quality content for ages 2-5. For older children, the new guidance emphasizes co-viewing, family conversations about content, device-free bedrooms and mealtimes, and ensuring screens don't replace sleep, physical activity, or free play.
- **Sources:** edsurge.com (New AAP Screen Time Recommendations Focus Less on Screens, More on Family Time, 2026-02-05); choc.org (Updated AAP recommendations for screen time)
