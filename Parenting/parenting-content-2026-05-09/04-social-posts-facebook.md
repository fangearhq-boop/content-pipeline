### STORY 1: D11 Teachers and Parents Picket Board

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM MT

If your kids are in D11, this one's been building for a while.

This week, educators and parents picketed at a District 11 Board of Education meeting to mark more than 300 days since the Board ended a 56-year-old master agreement with teachers. That's not a typo: a contract that governed working conditions for over five decades, gone.

What do teachers want? Smaller class sizes. More school counselors and social workers. More special education support staff. These aren't luxury asks -- they're the conditions that let teachers do their jobs and kids learn.

Here's something that surprised us: teachers are now working under an online "handbook" that can be updated at any time, without advance notice. That means the rules governing educators' jobs can literally change overnight.

Parents who care about D11 schools can show up to Board meetings, contact their elected Board representatives, or reach out to the Colorado Springs Education Association to stay informed.

What's your experience in D11 this school year? Families, we'd love to hear from you.

### STORY 2: Territory Days 2026

#### Long-Form Post — 10:00 AM MT

Mark your calendar, Colorado Springs families: Territory Days is back for Memorial Day Weekend -- and this year it's the 50th anniversary.

The festival runs May 23-25 in Historic Old Colorado City, and admission is completely free. We're talking 180+ food and craft booths, live music, a kids' play area, a petting zoo, gold panning, Indigenous dancers, Wild West gunfight reenactments, and blacksmith demonstrations. If that's not a full three days, we don't know what is.

A few things to know before you go:

Parking in Old Colorado City is genuinely rough. Use the free shuttle from the Colorado Springs C.A.B. Garage downtown -- it will save your sanity. The event footprint is ADA compliant throughout. Leave the dog at home for this one (no dogs allowed). And plan to go more than once if you can -- three days of free fun is worth it.

Territory Days has been a COS tradition for 50 years. What better way to spend Memorial Day Weekend than at a festival this city built from the ground up?

### STORY 3: ZMC Group Light-Up Toys Recall

#### Long-Form Post — 12:00 PM MT

Parents: check your toy bins.

The Consumer Product Safety Commission recalled 124,560 battery-operated light-up toys from ZMC Group last week. The list includes yo-yos, maracas, Halloween wands, and light-up sticks -- the kinds of small party-favor or impulse-buy toys that are everywhere at discount stores.

Here's why this matters: these toys have button cell batteries that children can easily access. If a child swallows one of those batteries, it can cause severe internal chemical burns. The injuries can be fatal. No injuries have been reported yet -- but the "yet" is why acting now counts.

The toys were sold at discount stores for about $1 from May 2023 through April 2026. They're easy to recognize: small, cheap, light-up novelty items.

What to do right now:

Stop using the toys. Take them away from your kids. Then destroy the toy (break it in half or write "RECALLED" on it with a marker) and email a photo to recallzmctoy@gmail.com for a full refund. You can also call 888-407-3225 Monday through Friday, 9:30 AM to 5:30 PM PT.

When's the last time you checked your kids' toy bins for recalled items? It's worth a quick pass.

### STORY 4: AAP Screen Time Update

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM MT

If you've been feeling guilty about the 3-hour movie marathon on a rainy Saturday -- you might be off the hook.

The American Academy of Pediatrics released updated screen time guidance in 2026, and it's a meaningful shift from the familiar "2 hours a day" rule most parents grew up hearing.

Here's the bottom line: for school-age kids and teens, the AAP is dropping the hard time limit. Instead, they're asking parents to think about the quality of screen content, how they engage with it as a family, and whether screens are getting in the way of sleep, physical activity, and family connection.

The rules that haven't changed:

No screens at all for kids under 18 months (video chat is the exception). For ages 2-5, limit screen time to 1 hour per day of high-quality content. Co-viewing -- watching with your kids and talking about what you're seeing -- is still strongly encouraged.

New practical guidance: make bedrooms and mealtimes screen-free. Model the digital habits you want your kids to develop. And be honest with yourself about whether screens are being used intentionally or just as filler.

This feels more realistic to us. Does it to you?

How does your family handle screen time -- and will you change anything based on the new AAP guidance?

### STORY 5: Farmers Market Opens Sunday

#### Long-Form Post — 5:00 PM MT

We've been waiting for this.

The Downtown Colorado Springs Farmers Market opens this Sunday, May 10 at Acacia Park -- and it runs every Sunday through October 25 from 9 AM to 2 PM. Free to attend, right in the heart of downtown.

It's one of those weekly rhythms that makes summer in COS feel like summer. Kids learn where food actually comes from. You pick up something fresh and local. You run into people you know. And it sets up the rest of your Sunday nicely.

Acacia Park is at 115 E. Pikes Peak Ave. Bring your reusable bags, bring the kids, and get there early for the best selection. No admission, no signup -- just show up.

We're thinking about making it a standing Sunday family plan. Who else is in?
