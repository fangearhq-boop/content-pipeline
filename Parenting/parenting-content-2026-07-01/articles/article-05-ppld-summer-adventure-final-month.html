One Month Left: PPLD Summer Adventure Ends July 31 — Here's How to Finish Strong

By Jamie Rivera | July 1, 2026

Today marks the start of the final 30 days of PPLD's Summer Adventure. Families who haven't enrolled yet still have time — and those who've been participating are in the home stretch toward some of the best prizes in the program's history.

Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program runs through July 31 — which means today, July 1, marks exactly 30 days left. That's a different emotional register than last week's midpoint update. The window is still real, but it's closing.

The good news: families who haven't started yet can still complete the program. It's free, it requires no pre-registration, and it's designed to be accessible for all ages. You can sign up today and start logging activities immediately.

What the Program Offers

PPLD's Summer Adventure tracks 30 activities over the course of the summer. The activities aren't limited to reading — participants can log reading, creating, exploring nature, or attending library programs. That flexibility makes the program workable for families at different stages of the summer and kids with different interests.

The program is free for all ages. Younger children, school-age kids, teens, and adults can all participate. PPLD runs programming across 14 library locations and two bookmobiles throughout El Paso County, making it accessible for most Colorado Springs families regardless of where they live.

Sign up at ppld.org/summer or download the Beanstack app to track activities digitally. Beanstack is how the library system verifies activity completion for prize eligibility, so families planning to go for the grand prizes should start logging there sooner rather than later.

The Grand Prizes

This year's grand prizes are genuinely aspirational:

  • A one-night stay for two at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp — a summit retreat above Colorado Springs that's typically out of reach for most families as a regular trip.
  • A Pikes Peak Region Attractions VIP Access Pass — providing access to multiple local attractions in the Pikes Peak area.

These prizes are drawn from participants who have completed the program, so the July 31 deadline is meaningful — you can't enter if you haven't finished before the program closes.

Why Summer Reading Matters

PPLD's Summer Adventure isn't just about prizes or keeping kids busy. There's a documented reason that libraries run summer reading programs: research consistently shows that children can lose 25 to 30 percent of the reading progress they made during the school year if they're not engaged in reading over the summer.

That's sometimes called the "summer slide" — and it's cumulative. Kids who experience summer learning loss every year start each school year further behind than where they ended the previous one. The gap compounds over time.

A structured summer reading program, even a light one, significantly reduces that loss. The bar doesn't have to be high — 20 minutes of reading a day is enough to make a measurable difference. PPLD's program provides the structure and the motivation (activities, prizes, events) to make that sustainable for families through the end of July.

What's Next

Head to ppld.org/summer or open the Beanstack app today to enroll or check your progress. If your family has been participating, July is the sprint — there are 30 days to finish the remaining activities and make sure everything is logged before the July 31 close.

PPLD locations have in-person activities and events through the end of the program. Checking your nearest branch's calendar for July programming is a good way to add activities while spending time at the library — which counts toward the program itself.

The library system also offers bookmobile stops throughout El Paso County for families who don't live near a branch. Bookmobile schedules are available at ppld.org.
