Before the Fireworks: Two Daytime July 4 Options for COS Families

By Jamie Rivera | July 1, 2026

Rock Ledge Ranch and the National Museum of WWII Aviation are both open on July 4 with kid-friendly programming — and both close before the evening fireworks begin.

The July 4 evening fireworks guide we published Monday covers the major shows in Colorado Springs. But there's a long stretch of daylight between morning and sundown, and two venues are filling it with family programming that's worth a dedicated trip.

Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site and the National Museum of WWII Aviation are both open on July 4 with hands-on activities for kids, and neither was included in the Monday guide. They represent a different kind of July 4 experience — historical, active, and affordable — and together they offer a way to structure the full day before the fireworks start.

Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site

Rock Ledge Ranch is open July 4 from 10 AM to 4 PM. Admission is $7 to $13 per person; children three and under are free. It's located at the entrance to Garden of the Gods in Colorado Springs.

The July 4 programming at Rock Ledge Ranch includes a Civil War living history encampment, watermelon, a kids' patriotic parade, historic home tours, festival games, patriotic speeches, and live music. The patriotic parade component is specifically designed for children and gives kids an active role in the day's activities rather than just watching.

The 4 PM close time works well for families who want to continue on to the Banning Lewis Ranch fireworks show, which opens at 3 PM. There's overlap for about an hour, so families can head from Rock Ledge Ranch directly to Banning Lewis without a long wait.

National Museum of WWII Aviation

The National Museum of WWII Aviation is open July 4 from 10 AM to 5 PM. It's located on the Colorado Springs Airport grounds.

July 4 activities at the museum include vintage auto displays, rides in vintage military vehicles, children's STEM activities, prize drawings, and live entertainment. The military vehicle rides are a standout — kids get to ride in actual vintage military vehicles, which is a genuinely unusual experience that tends to be memorable in a way that a standard museum visit isn't.

The STEM activities are structured for children and provide hands-on engagement with aviation and engineering concepts connected to the museum's collection. The museum houses aircraft from the World War II era alongside related military history.

Planning the Full Day

Venue Hours Admission Highlights
Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site 10 AM–4 PM $7–$13; kids 3 & under free Kids' patriotic parade, Civil War history, live music
National Museum of WWII Aviation 10 AM–5 PM Check museum website Military vehicle rides, children's STEM activities

Families with younger children may find Rock Ledge Ranch the stronger fit — the outdoor setting, kids' parade, and lower admission price make it accessible for a range of ages and budgets. Families with kids who have a particular interest in aircraft, military history, or engineering will likely find the WWII Aviation Museum worth the trip.

Both venues are in Colorado Springs, which means families can reasonably fit both into a single day if they arrive at one venue early and move to the other by early afternoon. The museum's 5 PM close time gives a bit more flexibility for families who want to linger before heading to an evening fireworks show.
