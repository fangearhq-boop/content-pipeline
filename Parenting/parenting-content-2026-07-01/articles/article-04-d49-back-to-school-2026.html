D49 Teachers Are Back July 28 — Key Back-to-School Dates Colorado Springs Families Should Know

By Sarah Morales | July 1, 2026

District 49's back-to-school calendar kicks off with New Teacher Orientation on July 24 — less than four weeks from today. Here's what D49 families need to know, plus a note on the district's new Student Success Center opening this fall.

It's July 1, which feels like the middle of summer — but the back-to-school calendar for District 49 families starts in 23 days. New Teacher Orientation is July 24. Teachers officially return July 28. That's less than four weeks from today.

For families managing summer schedules, childcare arrangements, and school supply planning, knowing these dates now matters. The transition from summer to school year doesn't happen on the first day of school — it starts weeks before, with the energy shift that happens when teachers and staff return to campus.

Key D49 Dates: July and August 2026

Date Event
July 3 District offices closed (in observance of July 4)
July 24 New Teacher Orientation
July 28 Teachers Return
July 30 Professional Development
August 2026 First Day of School (confirm exact date at d49.org)

The exact first day of school date for the 2026-27 school year is available on the D49 district calendar at d49.org. That's the place to confirm the date for your child's specific school, as some schools within the district may have different schedules.

New: The Student Success Center

One significant addition to D49 heading into the 2026-27 school year is the opening of a new Student Success Center. The center is set to open at the start of the school year and represents a new resource for students across the district.

D49 has not yet released full details on the center's specific programming and services, but its opening signals an investment in student support beyond the classroom. For families with students who have benefited from academic intervention, tutoring, or additional counseling resources, this will be worth tracking as the school year approaches.

Board of Education meetings through October will be held at Sand Creek High School — useful to know for families who want to attend and learn more about district initiatives, including the Student Success Center.

D11 and D20 Families

This article focuses on D49 because the district had specific calendar milestones starting in late July. If your children are enrolled in D11 (Colorado Springs School District 11) or D20 (Academy School District 20), back-to-school calendars and registration information are available at d11.org and asd20.org respectively.

Each district in El Paso County operates on its own calendar, and the specific teacher return and school start dates vary. If you're a D11 or D20 family, it's worth checking your district's calendar directly, as D49's July dates don't necessarily apply to other districts.

What's Next

The most useful thing to do right now is to pull up the D49 district calendar at d49.org and confirm the exact first day of school for your child's school. Set a reminder for July 24 — that's when New Teacher Orientation starts and when back-to-school communications from teachers typically begin to ramp up.

For families who still have school supply shopping to do, July is the right window to start. Retailers in Colorado Springs typically see supply shortages in the final two weeks before school starts. Getting ahead of that by mid-July means more options and lower stress.

For questions about open enrollment, registration, or the new Student Success Center, the D49 district office can be reached through the contact information available at d49.org.
