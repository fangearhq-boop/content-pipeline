Check Your Baby Gear: Four Active CPSC Recalls for Sleep, Bath, and Sleepwear Products

By Jamie Rivera | July 1, 2026

The CPSC has issued active recalls on four products commonly found in Colorado Springs homes — a baby swing, an infant lounger, bath seats, and children's robes. Here's what each recall covers and what families need to do now.

Four separate recalls are currently active through the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission, covering products in four different categories: infant sleep, infant bath, and children's sleepwear. The recalled items were widely sold — including through Amazon — and there's a real chance one of them is in your home or the home of someone you know.

The action for all four is the same: stop use immediately and visit cpsc.gov/Recalls to find the specific recall page for your product, confirm whether your unit is affected, and request a refund or replacement. Here's what you need to know about each one.

Vevor Baby Swings — Suffocation Risk

Vevor baby swings have been recalled because the seat's incline angle exceeds the mandatory 10-degree maximum for infant sleep products under federal safety standards. When an infant is placed in a product that exceeds this incline, the risk of positional asphyxia — where a baby's airway becomes blocked due to the position of their head and neck — increases significantly.

The recall applies to Vevor-branded baby swings sold primarily through Amazon. If you have a Vevor baby swing, stop use immediately. Do not continue using it even for short periods while your baby is supervised.

Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers — Entrapment and Fall Risk

Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers have been recalled for entrapment and fall hazards. The product violates the mandatory standard for infant sleep products, which requires products to prevent situations where an infant can become trapped or roll into a position from which they can't recover.

If you purchased a CooCooBaby lounger, note that this is a separate recall — both products are affected, but they're distinct items with separate recall pages on cpsc.gov. Check both pages if you're unsure which product you have.

Amazon-Sold Baby Bath Seats — Drowning Risk

Two brands of baby bath seats sold on Amazon — YCXXKJ and NFSVLB — have been recalled for drowning risk. Both products were sold by the BenTalk seller on Amazon, and the recalls cover nearly 9,000 units combined. The hazard: these seats tip over during use, putting infants at immediate risk of submersion.

Baby bath seats are not safety devices and should never be used as a substitute for hands-on supervision during bath time. If you have either of these products, stop use immediately. Do not pass them along to another family — take them out of use entirely and follow the recall instructions for return or destruction.

Bellabu Bear Children's Robes — Burn Hazard

Bellabu Bear children's bathrobes have been recalled because they violate the mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. Products classified as children's sleepwear must either be inherently flame-resistant or be made tight-fitting enough to reduce burn risk. The Bellabu Bear robes do not meet these requirements.

Children's robes are frequently used near bathrooms and kitchens — spaces where ignition sources are present. If your children have Bellabu Bear robes, stop use and follow the recall process at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

What's Next

Visit cpsc.gov/Recalls and search for each product by name to find the specific recall page. You'll be able to verify whether your unit is affected by the recall dates or model numbers listed. Each page has instructions for requesting a refund or replacement directly from the manufacturer.

If you purchased any of these products through Amazon, check your order history — Amazon typically sends recall notifications to affected purchasers, but it's worth checking the cpsc.gov page directly to confirm. The CPSC also maintains a free recall notification email list at cpsc.gov if you want to receive alerts for future recalls automatically.
