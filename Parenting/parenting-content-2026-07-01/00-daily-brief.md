### STORY 1: July 2026 CPSC Recall Roundup — Four Products Families Need to Check Now
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY (Multiple active CPSC recalls; not covered in prior pipelines)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Posting Window:** 8:00 AM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera
**Sources:** cpsc.gov, fox21news.com, consumerreports.org

The CPSC has issued multiple recalls in 2026 affecting products commonly used in Colorado Springs homes. Key active recalls: (1) Vevor Baby Swings recalled for suffocation risk — incline angle exceeds 10-degree mandatory maximum for infant sleep products; sold on Amazon; stop use immediately. (2) Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers recalled for entrapment and fall hazards — violates mandatory standard for Infant Sleep Products; separate product from the CooCooBaby recall covered June 30. (3) Multiple Amazon-sold baby bath seats (YCXXKJ, NFSVLB brands) recalled for drowning risk — tip over in use; nearly 9,000 units affected. (4) Bellabu Bear Children's Robes recalled for burn hazard — violates mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. Action for all: stop use immediately; check cpsc.gov/Recalls for refund/replacement details.

### STORY 2: Fire Danger Update — Stage II Restrictions + Burn Ban Now in Effect for July 4 Weekend
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY / FOLLOW-UP (June 30 covered Stage I; Stage II + burn ban now in effect as of June 29)
**Pillar:** National Parenting / Local News
**Posting Window:** 10:00 AM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Byline:** Sarah Morales
**Sources:** kktv.com, gazette.com, fox21news.com, krdo.com

Colorado Springs fire conditions have escalated since the June 30 pipeline. A burn ban took effect at noon on Monday, June 29, covering Colorado Springs. El Paso County has entered Stage II fire restrictions — the highest level — prohibiting the use and sale of fireworks in unincorporated El Paso County (Stage I was in effect as of the June 30 pipeline). Key enforcement update: the Colorado Springs Police Department has increased its July 4 weekend illegal fireworks response, with specific officers assigned citywide for the holiday. Violations of the City Fireworks Ordinance carry: up to a $2,650 fine, a mandatory appearance in municipal court, and up to 189 days in jail. Important distinction: Colorado Springs has PERMANENTLY banned consumer fireworks (not just for this year). No personal fireworks of any kind (including sparklers, which burn at 2,000°F) are allowed within city limits.

Two daytime options for COS families on July 4 that were not included in the June 30 guide (which covered evening/fireworks events). (1) Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site — July 4, 10 AM–4 PM. Experience a Civil War living history encampment, watermelon, a kids' patriotic parade, historic home tours, festival games, patriotic speeches, and live music. Together, Rock Ledge Ranch and WWII Aviation Museum allow families to fill the full July 4 day without waiting for nighttime.

### STORY 5: PPLD Summer Adventure — 30 Days Left to Log Activities and Win Prizes
**Tier:** 2
**Status:** FOLLOW-UP (June 30 pipeline covered midpoint "31 days remaining"; today is July 1 = start of final month)
**Pillar:** Local Events
**Posting Window:** 5:00 PM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera
**Sources:** ppld.org/summer, kktv.com, koaa.com, gazette.com

Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program ends July 31, 2026 — making today (July 1) the start of the final month. Families who have not yet enrolled still have time; participation is free and requires no pre-registration (sign up at ppld.org/summer or via the Beanstack app). The program tracks 30 activities over the summer, and families can log reading, creating, exploring, or attending library programs. Grand prizes include a one-night stay for two at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp and a Pikes Peak Region Attractions VIP Access Pass. With 14 library locations and 2 bookmobiles across El Paso County, there is a location accessible to most COS families. PPLD's Summer Adventure is specifically designed to prevent summer learning loss — research shows children can lose 25–30% of the reading progress they made during the school year if not engaged in summer reading.
