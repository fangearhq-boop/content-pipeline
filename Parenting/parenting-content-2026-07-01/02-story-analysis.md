## STORY 1: CPSC Recalls July 2026 Roundup

**Headline Options:**
- "Four Products Families Should Check and Remove This Week — CPSC Recalls Roundup July 2026"
- "Check Your Baby Gear: Four Active CPSC Recalls for Sleep, Bath, and Sleepwear Products"
- "If You Bought These Baby Products on Amazon, Check for Active Recalls"

**Best Angle:** The roundup format is powerful here — four separate recalls, all from different product categories. Families who missed one might know about another. Presenting them together increases the chance the right family sees the right alert.

**Engagement Hooks:**
- Four products in four categories: swing, lounger, bath seat, robe
- Amazon-sold products are especially likely to be in Colorado Springs homes
- All involve different hazard types: suffocation, drowning, entrapment, burn
- Practical action: stop use, visit cpsc.gov, get refund/replacement

**Platform Notes:**
- X: Lead with the most severe hazard type + number of recalls
- FB: List all four with brief description of each hazard — actionable and shareable
- Article: Table format (product, hazard, action) followed by brief explanation of each

## STORY 2: Fire Restrictions Stage II + Burn Ban

**Headline Options:**
- "COS is Under a Burn Ban and Stage II Fire Restrictions — What That Means for July 4"
- "Stage II Fire Restrictions Now in Effect — Fireworks Violations: $2,650 Fine + Possible Jail"
- "The Fireworks Rules Changed This Week: COS Families Need to Know the July 4 Update"

**Best Angle:** The escalation is the news hook. The June 30 pipeline covered Stage I. Now it's Stage II and a burn ban is in effect. CSPD has specific officers assigned. The penalties are severe and the timing is urgent (July 4 is 3 days away).

**Engagement Hooks:**
- Stage I → Stage II escalation since June 30
- Burn ban in effect since June 29 at noon
- $2,650 fine + mandatory court + up to 189 days jail for violations
- CSPD specific enforcement officers assigned
- Reminder: fireworks are PERMANENTLY banned in COS city limits, not just this year

**Platform Notes:**
- X: Lead with the penalty and escalation — timely, actionable
- FB: Explain the escalation clearly; many parents may not know Stage I vs II distinction
- Article: Clear structure: what changed, what the penalties are, what families can do instead

**Platform Notes:**
