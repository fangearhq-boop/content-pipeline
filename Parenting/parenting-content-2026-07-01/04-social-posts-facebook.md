### STORY 1: CPSC Recalls Roundup (Tier 1)

#### FB Long-Form — 8:00 AM MT

⚠️ If you have any of these four products at home, stop using them now.

The CPSC has active recalls on four baby and children's products — covering sleep, bath, and sleepwear. Here's what Colorado Springs families need to know:

**Vevor Baby Swings** — Recalled for suffocation risk. The incline angle exceeds the mandatory 10-degree maximum for infant sleep products. Stop use immediately.

**Joyful Journeys Baby Loungers** — Recalled for entrapment and fall hazards. (This is a separate product from the CooCooBaby recall we covered earlier this week.)

**Amazon-sold Baby Bath Seats (YCXXKJ and NFSVLB brands)** — Recalled for drowning risk. These seats tip over during use. Nearly 9,000 units were sold through Amazon under the BenTalk seller account.

**Bellabu Bear Children's Robes** — Recalled for burn hazard. The robes violate mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear.

For all four: stop use and visit cpsc.gov/Recalls to request a refund or replacement. Many of these items were sold on Amazon — if you've purchased baby or children's products from these brands in the past two years, it's worth a quick check. 🔍

### STORY 2: Stage II Fire Restrictions + Burn Ban (Tier 1)

#### FB Long-Form — 10:00 AM MT

🔥 Colorado Springs families heading into July 4 weekend need to know: the fire situation has escalated since Monday.

A burn ban took effect at noon on June 29, covering Colorado Springs. El Paso County has also moved from Stage I to Stage II fire restrictions — the highest level — which prohibits the use and sale of fireworks in unincorporated El Paso County.

Here's what's important to understand about consumer fireworks in Colorado Springs: they're not just banned this year because of fire conditions. They're permanently banned within city limits. That includes sparklers, which burn at approximately 2,000°F. This is true every July 4, regardless of weather.

For this weekend specifically, CSPD has assigned specific officers citywide for July 4 enforcement. Violations carry up to a $2,650 fine, a mandatory appearance in municipal court, and up to 189 days in jail.

If your family is looking for safe ways to celebrate, our article has a full guide to public fireworks shows and daytime family events in Colorado Springs. 🎆

### STORY 3: July 4 Daytime Options (Tier 2)

#### FB Post — 12:00 PM MT

Looking for something to do with the kids on July 4 before the evening fireworks? Colorado Springs has two daytime options that didn't make it into our earlier July 4 guide — and they're both worth knowing about. 🇺🇸

**Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site** is open 10 AM–4 PM on July 4. Admission is $7–$13, and kids three and under are free. The day includes a Civil War living history encampment, a kids' patriotic parade, historic home tours, festival games, and live music. It closes at 4 PM, which gives families plenty of time to head to a fireworks show afterward.

**National Museum of WWII Aviation** is open 10 AM–5 PM. Activities include vintage auto displays, rides in vintage military vehicles, children's STEM activities, prize drawings, and live entertainment. The military vehicle rides are a genuinely unusual experience — kids tend to love them.

Together, these two venues cover the full daytime window before Colorado Springs' evening fireworks begin. Our article has full details on admission and location.

### STORY 5: PPLD Summer Adventure Final Month (Tier 2)

#### FB Post — 5:00 PM MT

Today is July 1 — which means there are 30 days left in PPLD's Summer Adventure. 📚

If your family hasn't started yet, there's still time. The program is free for all ages and doesn't require pre-registration. You can sign up today at ppld.org/summer or through the Beanstack app and start logging activities right away.

The program runs through July 31 and tracks 30 activities — reading, creating, exploring, and attending library events all count. Grand prizes include a one-night stay for two at The Broadmoor Cloud Camp and a Pikes Peak Region Attractions VIP Access Pass.

Beyond the prizes: research shows kids can lose 25–30% of the reading progress they made during the school year without summer engagement. PPLD's program is one of the easiest ways to prevent that — and with 14 library locations and 2 bookmobiles across El Paso County, there's probably one close to you.
