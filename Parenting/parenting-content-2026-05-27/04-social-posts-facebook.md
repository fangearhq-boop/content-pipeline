### STORY 1: Bellabu Bear Sherpa Robes Recall

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 8:00 AM MT

🚨 Colorado Springs parents — if your kids have a Bellabu Bear sherpa robe, please read this now.

About 1,300 Bellabu Bear children's bamboo sherpa robes have been recalled by the CPSC due to a burn hazard. The robes fail the mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. The robes that are affected come in prints that will look very familiar: PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora, as well as holiday and novelty prints including Nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons. They come in sizes S/M and M/L, and were sold for about $60 at Saks Fifth Avenue stores and boutiques nationwide, and online at Bellabubear.com, Letthembelittleboutique.com, and Saksfifthavenue.com from January 2024 through July 2025.

To identify a recalled robe, check the neck label for "Bellabu Bear," "BB-NW12109," and the size.

Stop using the robe immediately and contact Bellabu Bear for a full refund or store credit. They'll provide a prepaid shipping label so you can return it at no cost to you. Call 888-703-7752 (9 AM to 4 PM PT, Monday through Friday), email help@bellabubear.com, or visit bellabubear.com/pages/recall-childrens-sherpa-robes.

Do you have a Bellabu Bear robe at home — or know someone who might?

### STORY 2: D20 Superintendent Change

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 10:00 AM MT

📋 D20 families — here's the update on Academy School District 20 leadership.

On May 15, the D20 Board of Education voted unanimously to fire Superintendent Jinger Haberer. The board cited insubordination and the unauthorized invitation of an undisclosed "person of interest" to a closed board meeting on May 7 — an act the board said compromised the district's attorney-client privilege.

On May 19, the board named Dr. Susan Field as interim superintendent for the 2026–27 school year. Dr. Field brings 19 years of experience inside D20, having started as principal of Woodmen-Roberts Elementary School and currently serving as Assistant Superintendent for Learning Services. The district sent a letter to families saying it "remains strong, stable and focused" through this leadership transition.

If your family is in D20 — schools like Pine Creek, Discovery Canyon, Rampart, Air Academy, and others — Dr. Field is the person now leading the district. She's expected to serve through the entirety of the 2026–27 school year.

### STORY 4: Vevor Baby Swings Recall

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 12:00 PM MT

🚨 Parents of babies and toddlers: another swing recall to know about.

Sanven Technology has recalled about 1,020 Vevor baby swings due to a suffocation hazard. The swings have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees, which violates the mandatory federal safety standard for infant sleep products and the ban on inclined sleepers under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act. Babies placed in inclined sleepers at unsafe angles can have their chin fall to their chest, restricting their airway.

The three affected models are: BB501K (white and gray with a white base), BB702A (white and gray with a gray base), and BB005K (black and gray with a black base). They were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon from January 2025 through August 2025 for $65 to $80.

Stop using the swing immediately. To get your full refund, cut the swing cover in half, write "Recalled" with permanent marker on the swing's base, and send a photo to recalling@vevor.com. Then dispose of the swing. You can also call 855-599-6320 (9 AM to 5 PM PT, Monday through Friday).
