PPLD Summer Adventure 2026 Starts June 1 — Plus Free D11 Lunch at East Library

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 27, 2026

Colorado Springs families looking for a free, screen-optional summer activity that comes with actual prizes — and a free lunch — just got their answer.

The Pikes Peak Library District's annual Summer Adventure reading program launches June 1, 2026, at all PPLD branches across Colorado Springs. And this year, the library has paired up with something extra: D11 is bringing its free lunch mobile unit to East Library every weekday throughout June and July for any child or teen under 18.

What Is Summer Adventure

Summer Adventure is PPLD's annual summer reading and activities program, presented by Friends of the Pikes Peak Library District. Participants log reading time and complete activities — and earn real prizes for doing so. The program is open to all ages: young readers, middle schoolers, teens, and adults are all welcome.

There's no advance registration required. Sign-up happens in person at any PPLD branch location starting June 1. You can walk in, get signed up, and start earning same day.

The program runs throughout the summer and typically wraps up in early August. The number and variety of activities available make it engaging well beyond just sitting and reading — libraries often host special events, guest speakers, science programs, and craft sessions tied to the summer theme.

Free Lunch at East Library

Here's the extra bonus that makes this summer program stand out: Colorado Springs School District 11 will be bringing its free lunch mobile unit to East Library every weekday in June and July. Any child or teen under 18 can stop in and get lunch at no cost — no D11 enrollment required, no income verification, no paperwork. You show up, you eat.

For families already heading to East Library for Summer Adventure, this makes for an easy weekday outing: pick up a summer reading log, complete an activity, grab lunch, and head home. It's genuinely one of the best free afternoon combinations in Colorado Springs this summer.

Teens Can Volunteer

Teens looking for community service hours or summer work experience can apply to volunteer with the PPLD Summer Adventure program. Teen volunteers help participants register, distribute prizes, support programming, and take on other roles as needed. It's a solid resume-builder and a way to stay connected to the library community through the summer. Spots are available on a limited basis — visit ppld.org/Teen-Summer-Volunteer for information on how to apply.

What's Next

Mark your calendar for June 1 — that's the day to head to your nearest PPLD branch, sign up the whole family, and pick up the summer reading materials. If you're near East Library on a weekday, combine the trip with a free D11 lunch. For branches, hours, events calendar, and volunteer applications, everything is at ppld.org. Summer Adventure is one of the best free programs Colorado Springs has to offer — and it starts in just a few days.
