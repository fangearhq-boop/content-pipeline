Bellabu Bear Children's Sherpa Robes Recalled for Burn Hazard — PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Minions Prints Included

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 27, 2026

If your child has a Bellabu Bear sherpa robe hanging in their closet, it's time to take it out and put it away for good — at least until you've contacted the company for a refund.

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has announced that Bellabu Bear, based in Costa Mesa, California, is recalling approximately 1,300 children's bamboo sherpa robes due to a burn hazard. The robes fail the mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear, which means they can catch fire more quickly than required by federal safety law.

Which Robes Are Affected

The recalled robes are long-sleeved bamboo sherpa styles that come with two front pockets, sherpa lining, and a matching belt. They are available in two size options: S/M and M/L. What makes them easy to spot are the licensed character prints — the affected robes feature designs from some of the most popular children's brands out there, including PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, and Dora. Holiday and novelty prints are also included: Nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons.

To confirm whether a robe is recalled, check the neck label. It should read "Bellabu Bear," the style code "BB-NW12109," and the garment's size. If all three appear, the robe is part of the recall.

What to Do Right Now

The CPSC and Bellabu Bear are asking families to stop using the recalled robes immediately and to keep them away from children. Do not donate the robe or pass it along — the recall applies regardless of who has it.

To get a full refund or store credit, contact Bellabu Bear directly. The company will provide a prepaid shipping label so you can return the robe at no cost to you. You can reach them by phone at 888-703-7752 (9 AM to 4 PM PT, Monday through Friday), by email at help@bellabubear.com, or through the recall page at bellabubear.com/pages/recall-childrens-sherpa-robes.

Why Children's Sleepwear Flammability Standards Exist

Federal flammability standards for children's sleepwear exist because young children spend a significant portion of their lives in sleepwear, and the risk of fires from candles, stoves, fireplaces, and other ignition sources remains real. Items that fail these standards can ignite faster and burn more intensely than compliant products. The standard applies to all items intended or promoted for use as sleepwear for children ages 9 months through 14 years — including robes, which are classified as sleepwear under federal law.

What's Next

If you purchased a Bellabu Bear sherpa robe between January 2024 and July 2025 — especially through Saks Fifth Avenue or the Bellabubear.com website — check the neck label now. If the style code "BB-NW12109" is present, the robe is part of the recall. Contact Bellabu Bear at 888-703-7752 or help@bellabubear.com to arrange your refund. The CPSC recall notice is available at cpsc.gov/Recalls for full details.
