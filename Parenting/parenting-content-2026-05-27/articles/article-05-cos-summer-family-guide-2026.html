Your COS Summer Starts Now — What's Open and What's Coming for Colorado Springs Families

By Jamie Rivera | COS Parenting | May 27, 2026

Memorial Day weekend is officially behind us, and Colorado Springs is transitioning into full summer mode. Schools are wrapping up, temperatures are climbing, and the city's full lineup of family-friendly programs is either already running or days away from opening. Here's where things stand right now and what's coming in the next few weeks.

Open Right Now

Pools

Two outdoor public pools in Colorado Springs are open for the season. Monument Valley Pool (220 Mesa Rd) is open Monday through Saturday, 10 AM to 6 PM, and Sunday noon to 6 PM. Wilson Ranch Pool (2335 Allegheny Dr) is open daily 10 AM to 6 PM. Both pools are run by the City of Colorado Springs Parks, Recreation and Cultural Services. Admission fees apply.

Free Splash Pads and Spray Grounds

Free water features are already running at two locations. Panorama Park splash pad and Venezia Park Watering Hole are both open daily from 10 AM to 6 PM. No cost, no reservation — just show up. Uncle Wilbur Fountain at Acacia Park is also open, daily noon to 6 PM.

Trackchair Accessible Hike Program

The City's expanded Trackchair program — which lets people of all abilities explore open space on rugged motorized track wheelchairs — is running Wednesday through Sunday at four parks: Ute Valley Park, Red Rock Canyon Open Space, Blodgett Open Space, and Bluestem Prairie Open Space. Two guided sessions run daily: 8 to 11 AM and noon to 3 PM. Reservations are required at coloradosprings.gov/parks.

Starting June 1

June 1 is a particularly big day for Colorado Springs families. Several programs launch simultaneously:

PPLD Summer Adventure

The Pikes Peak Library District's annual Summer Adventure reading program kicks off at all PPLD branches. Sign up in person at any branch, and start earning prizes for reading and activities. The program is free and open to all ages. Teen volunteer spots are also available.

D49 Free Summer Meals

District 49 is offering free breakfast and lunch to all children ages 1 through 18 from June 1 through June 19. There are no income requirements and no D49 enrollment needed. Visit d49.org for meal sites and times.

D11 Free Lunch at East Library

Colorado Springs School District 11 will bring its free lunch mobile unit to East Library on weekdays throughout June and July. Any child or teen under 18 can stop in — no D11 enrollment, no cost, nothing required.

Deerfield Hills Spray Ground

The Deerfield Hills spray ground opens June 1, running daily from 10 AM to 5 PM. Free admission.

Summer Camps

The Colorado Springs Conservatory has summer camps running through the season, including Rock Band Camp, Songwriter and Music Production Camp, and Theater Camp. Registration is open. Visit the Conservatory's website for session dates and pricing.

What's Next

June 1 is your big launch date — PPLD Summer Adventure sign-up, D49 free meals, Deerfield Hills spray ground, and D11 East Library lunch all begin that day. Pools and splash pads are open now. For city parks and recreation updates, check coloradosprings.gov/parks. For library events and programs, visit ppld.org. And for D49 free meal sites and times, visit d49.org. Summer in Colorado Springs has a lot to offer — and a lot of it is free.
