D20 Names Dr. Susan Field as Interim Superintendent — What Colorado Springs Families Need to Know

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 27, 2026

If your kids attend school in Academy School District 20, you've likely already heard: the district has a new leader, following a sudden and significant leadership change earlier this month.

On May 15, the D20 Board of Education voted unanimously to fire Superintendent Jinger Haberer. On May 19, the board named Dr. Susan Field as interim superintendent. Here's what you need to know about what happened, who Dr. Field is, and what it means for your family heading into the 2026–27 school year.

Why Haberer Was Fired

The board cited two specific reasons for its decision: insubordination and the unauthorized invitation of an undisclosed "person of interest" to a closed special board meeting on May 7. According to the board, the presence of that unauthorized individual compromised the district's attorney-client privilege — a serious concern that the board said removed their confidence in Haberer's leadership.

The discharge was described as being for "good and just cause." The board sent a letter to D20 families following the vote, acknowledging the transition and reassuring them that the district "remains strong, stable and focused."

Who Is Dr. Susan Field

Dr. Susan Field is not an outside hire. She's spent 19 years working inside D20, giving her deep familiarity with the district's schools, culture, and families. She began her D20 career as principal of Woodmen-Roberts Elementary School and has worked her way up to her current role as Assistant Superintendent for Learning Services.

The board's decision to appoint an insider sends a clear signal: they're looking for stability and continuity, not a disruptive overhaul, heading into the next school year. Dr. Field will serve as interim through the entirety of the 2026–27 school year, at which point the district may conduct a full search for a permanent superintendent.

What This Means for Your Family

For most D20 families, the day-to-day experience at school is unlikely to change significantly in the near term. Individual school principals and teachers remain in place. The curriculum, school calendars, and program offerings are not expected to shift as a result of the superintendent change.

That said, leadership transitions do matter — especially at the district level, where decisions about budgets, staffing, and long-term planning get made. D20 families who care about school board engagement can attend board meetings or follow the district's communications at asd20.org.

What's Next

With Dr. Field officially in the interim role, D20 will head into the 2026–27 school year with a familiar internal leader at the helm. Whether the district conducts a formal search for a permanent superintendent — and when — will likely be addressed by the board later this year. For current updates and district communications, families can visit asd20.org or follow the district's official social media channels.
