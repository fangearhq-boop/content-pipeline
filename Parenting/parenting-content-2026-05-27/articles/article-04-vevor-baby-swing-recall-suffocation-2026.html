Vevor Baby Swings Recalled for Suffocation Hazard — 1,020 Units Sold on Amazon

By Sarah Morales | COS Parenting | May 27, 2026

Families who purchased a Vevor baby swing from Amazon or Vevor.com in the past year need to stop using it immediately. Sanven Technology has recalled approximately 1,020 of these swings due to a suffocation hazard that violates federal safety law.

What's the Problem

The recalled swings have an incline angle greater than 10 degrees when a baby is placed in them. Federal law — specifically the mandatory safety standard for infant sleep products and the Safe Sleep for Babies Act's ban on inclined sleepers — requires that any product marketed for infant sleep maintain an incline of 10 degrees or less. Products that exceed this threshold can allow a baby's head to fall forward and their chin to drop to their chest, which can restrict the airway and create a suffocation risk.

Infants, particularly very young babies, do not yet have the muscle control to lift or turn their own heads. A product with an unsafe incline can be deadly even when a caregiver is nearby.

How to Get Your Refund

The recall process for Vevor swings is slightly different from standard recalls — the CPSC and Sanven Technology are requiring product destruction documentation as part of the refund process. Here's what to do:

  1. Stop using the swing immediately.
  2. Remove the swing cover and cut it in half.
  3. Using permanent marker, write the word "Recalled" on the swing's base.
  4. Take a photo of the destroyed cover and marked base.
  5. Email the photo to recalling@vevor.com.
  6. Dispose of the swing according to your local and state disposal guidelines.

Once you've submitted the photo, Sanven Technology will process your full refund. You can also call 855-599-6320 (9 AM to 5 PM PT, Monday through Friday) if you have questions about the process.

What's Next

If you purchased a Vevor baby swing on Amazon or Vevor.com between January and August 2025, check the base for model numbers BB501K, BB702A, or BB005K. If you have one of these models, follow the refund steps above. The full CPSC recall notice is available at cpsc.gov/Recalls. For any ongoing safe sleep questions, the American Academy of Pediatrics recommends a firm, flat sleep surface with no incline for infants — swings, bouncers, and inclined products should not be used for routine infant sleep.
