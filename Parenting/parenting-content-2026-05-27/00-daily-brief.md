### STORY 1: Bellabu Bear Children's Sherpa Robes Recalled — Burn Hazard (1,300 Units)
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Byline:** Jamie Rivera

The CPSC has issued a recall for approximately 1,300 Bellabu Bear children's bamboo sherpa robes due to a burn hazard. The robes fail the mandatory flammability standards for children's sleepwear. The affected robes come in popular licensed character prints including PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, and Dora, as well as holiday and novelty prints (Nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, dragons). Available in sizes S/M and M/L. Sold at Saks Fifth Avenue stores nationwide and online at Bellabubear.com, Letthembelittleboutique.com, and Saksfifthavenue.com from January 2024 through July 2025 for about $60. Consumers should stop use immediately and contact Bellabu Bear for a full refund or store credit with prepaid return shipping.

**Contact:** 888-703-7752 (9 AM–4 PM PT, Mon–Fri) | help@bellabubear.com | bellabubear.com/pages/recall-childrens-sherpa-robes
**Sources:** CPSC.gov recall notice, Bellabubear.com/pages/recall-childrens-sherpa-robes, MyParisTexas

### STORY 2: D20 Leadership Change — Interim Superintendent Dr. Susan Field Named
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** Local News | **Byline:** Sarah Morales

Academy School District 20 has named Dr. Susan Field as interim superintendent following the board's unanimous decision to fire Superintendent Jinger Haberer on May 15. Haberer's discharge stemmed from insubordination and the unauthorized invitation of an undisclosed "person of interest" to a special board meeting on May 7, which the board said compromised attorney-client privilege. Dr. Field brings 19 years of D20 experience, starting as principal of Woodmen-Roberts Elementary, and is currently the Assistant Superintendent for Learning Services. She will serve through the 2026–27 school year. The district sent a letter to families saying it "remains strong, stable and focused."

**Sources:** KRDO (May 15 board announcement), KKTV (interim appointment May 19), KOAA (full story), Colorado Springs Gazette, Fox21 News

### STORY 4: Vevor Baby Swings Recalled — Suffocation Risk from Unsafe Incline
**Tier:** 2 | **Pillar:** National Parenting | **Byline:** Sarah Morales

Sanven Technology has recalled about 1,020 Vevor baby swings due to a suffocation hazard. The swings have an incline greater than 10 degrees, violating the mandatory standard for infant sleep products and the ban on inclined sleepers under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act. Affected models: BB501K (white/gray, white base), BB702A (white/gray, gray base), BB005K (black/gray, black base). Sold on Vevor.com and Amazon.com from January 2025 through August 2025 for $65–$80. Stop using immediately. To claim a full refund, cut the swing cover in half, write "Recalled" with permanent marker on the base, send a photo to recalling@vevor.com, and dispose of the swing.

**Contact:** 855-599-6320 (9 AM–5 PM PT, Mon–Fri) | recalling@vevor.com
**Sources:** CPSC.gov recall notice, Mother.ly (vevor-baby-swing-recall), WFMJ
