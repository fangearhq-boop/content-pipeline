If you purchased a Vevor baby swing in 2025, stop using it immediately. Sanven Technology has recalled approximately 1,020 of these swings after the Consumer Product Safety Commission determined they pose a serious suffocation risk — the swings tilt at an incline that exceeds 10 degrees, violating the mandatory safety standard for infant sleep products and the federal ban on inclined sleepers under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act.

The recall affects three models: BB501K (white and gray, white base), BB702A (white and gray, gray base), and BB005K (black and gray, black base). All models have the "VEVOR" trademark printed on the base, which is how families can identify if their swing is one of the recalled units. The swings were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon.com between January 2025 and August 2025 for between $65 and $80.

Why Inclined Sleepers Are Dangerous

The Safe Sleep for Babies Act, signed into federal law in 2022, banned inclined infant sleepers and infant sleep products that position babies at an angle of more than 10 degrees. The reason: when babies sleep at an incline, their heads can fall forward and restrict their airway, and they can roll into a position that cuts off breathing — especially before they have the muscle strength to reposition themselves.

Pediatric sleep safety guidelines consistently recommend that infants sleep on a flat, firm surface on their backs with no soft bedding, inclines, or positioning devices. Any product that angles a sleeping infant beyond the 10-degree limit puts them at risk, and the CPSC has been actively pursuing recalls of noncompliant products under the law.

What to Do

Stop using the swing right away. To receive a full refund, Sanven Technology asks that you cut the swing cover in half, write the word "Recalled" and the current date on the swing's base with a permanent marker, and take a photo of the destroyed cover along with the base. Send that photo to recalling@vevor.com, then dispose of the swing in accordance with your local and state waste guidelines.

To start the refund process or ask questions, call 855-599-6320, Monday through Friday from 9 AM to 5 PM PT. The full CPSC recall notice is available at CPSC.gov/Recalls.

What's Next

If you have a Vevor baby swing and can't determine the model number, err on the side of caution and stop using it until you can confirm with CPSC.gov. If you believe your baby was injured by a recalled product, you can file a report at SaferProducts.gov.
