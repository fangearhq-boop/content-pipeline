Colorado Springs families, the long weekend is here — and it's a full one. Between Territory Days, three D49 graduation ceremonies, and splash pads opening next week, there's no shortage of reasons to get out of the house. Here's everything you need to plan Memorial Day weekend 2026 in COS.

Territory Days — May 23-25 (50th Anniversary)

Territory Days returns to Old Colorado City this weekend for its 50th anniversary celebration. The free three-day street festival runs along Colorado Avenue between 23rd and 27th Streets, with more than 180 food and craft vendor booths, live music, a kids' zone, a petting zoo, gold panning, Wild West gunfight re-enactments, blacksmith demonstrations, and live birds of prey.

Hours are Saturday and Sunday 10 AM to 7 PM, and Monday, May 25 from 10 AM to 6 PM. Admission is free.

Parking is limited in Old Colorado City. We recommend using the C.A.B. Garage downtown (parking runs $1 per hour) and taking the free shuttle to the festival. It's the easiest way to avoid the parking headache and keep the focus on the fun.

D49 Graduation — Saturday, May 23

If you have family in District 49, Saturday at Broadmoor World Arena (3185 Venetucci Blvd) is graduation day. Falcon High School's ceremony is at 1:00 PM MT, Vista Ridge High School's is at 5:00 PM MT, and Sand Creek High School also holds its commencement on May 23. No tickets required for any of these — arrive early for the best seats.

Splash Pads — Opening Day is Tuesday, May 26

Two free City of Colorado Springs splash pads open Tuesday, May 26 at 10 AM:

  • Panorama Park Splash Pad — open 10 AM-6 PM daily
  • Venezia Park Watering Hole — open 10 AM-6 PM daily

Deerfield Hills Recreation Area's splash pad opens June 1, with hours from 10 AM to 5 PM. All three are free. Note: Julie Penrose Fountain will not open this summer due to budget cuts.

Summer in Colorado Springs just got a little more official.

Last Day of D11 School — Tomorrow, May 22

If your family is in District 11, tomorrow is the last day of school. D49 families are also wrapping up their year around the same time. The library's PPLD Summer Adventure program is open for registration now at ppld.org/summer — the "Unearth a Story" theme starts June 2 and runs through July 31. It's free for all ages and a great way to keep kids reading and engaged through the summer. Sign up this weekend while you have a moment.

What's Next

After this weekend, summer officially hits full gear for Colorado Springs families. Keep checking COS Parenting for updates on summer programs, parks, camps, and community events throughout June and July. Visit visitcos.com for a full calendar of events, and parks.coloradosprings.gov for splash pad schedules and park hours.
