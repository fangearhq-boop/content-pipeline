D49 Graduation Schedule — Saturday, May 23

Broadmoor World Arena: 3185 Venetucci Blvd, Colorado Springs, CO 80906. No tickets required; first-come, first-served seating.

Three District 49 high schools hold commencement ceremonies this Saturday, May 23 at Broadmoor World Arena — one of the busiest graduation days of the Colorado Springs school year. Falcon High School opens at 1:00 PM MT, Vista Ridge High School follows at 5:00 PM MT, and Sand Creek High School is also scheduled for May 23.

All three ceremonies are free and open to the public. No tickets are required for Falcon or Vista Ridge — entrance is first-come, first-served, so families are encouraged to arrive early to secure good seating. Broadmoor World Arena is located at 3185 Venetucci Blvd on the southwest side of Colorado Springs, with on-site parking available.

A Big Day for D49 Families

District 49 already kicked off its graduation season on May 15 when Patriot High School and students from PPEC (Pikes Peak Early College) and SSAE (Sand Creek Arts and Enterprise) held their ceremonies at COS City Hub downtown.

Saturday's ceremonies at Broadmoor World Arena are the largest of the district's commencement season. Falcon and Vista Ridge each have graduating classes in the 260-280 student range, filling the arena with families from the eastern reaches of Colorado Springs and the Falcon/Peyton corridor. Sand Creek draws from the central and northeast side of the district.

The Broadmoor World Arena is a familiar venue for Colorado Springs families — the arena hosts major events year-round, including Colorado Eagles hockey and large concerts. For many families, graduation at the World Arena is a milestone that makes the occasion feel all the bigger.

Planning Your Visit

Broadmoor World Arena is located off Venetucci Boulevard, south of I-25 near the Broadmoor resort area. Arrive at least 30-45 minutes before your ceremony's start time to park and find your seats. Parking on site is available; check the venue website for day-of parking guidance during a multi-ceremony event day.

If you have family members with accessibility needs, contact Broadmoor World Arena in advance to confirm seating accommodations.

What's Next

After Saturday's ceremonies, D49's Class of 2026 is officially headed into summer. For families wrapping up the school year this week, the Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program is open for registration now at ppld.org/summer — it starts June 2 and is free for all ages. Territory Days in Old Colorado City also runs through Memorial Day weekend (May 23-25), a great first post-graduation family outing.
