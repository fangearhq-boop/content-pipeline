Parents and gift-givers, take a look at your child's robe. Bellabu Bear of Costa Mesa, California has recalled approximately 1,300 bamboo sherpa children's robes after testing confirmed they fail the mandatory federal flammability standard for children's sleepwear — creating a burn risk. No injuries have been reported, but the product doesn't meet federal safety requirements and should not be used.

The recalled robes come in sherpa bamboo fabric in popular licensed character prints, making them the kind of gift that circulates widely among Colorado Springs families. If your child has a robe in any of these designs — PAW Patrol, Garfield, Minecraft, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, or Dora the Explorer — check the label. The recalled robes are also sold in seasonal designs: Nutcracker, hearts, Santa, stripes, bows, and dragons. The label reads "Bellabu Bear" and "BB-NW12109" along with the size.

Why Flammability Standards Matter

Federal law requires that children's sleepwear meet strict flammability standards to protect kids from burn injuries if they come near an open flame — including birthday candles, campfire sparks, or a gas stove. Children's robes are categorized as sleepwear and must meet these standards because children often wear them in the kitchen, near candles, and in other environments where flame exposure is possible.

The standard requires that sleepwear either be made from inherently flame-resistant fiber or treated with flame-retardant finishing. The Bellabu Bear robes tested showed they don't meet either requirement, which is why the recall was issued even though no incidents have been reported.

Where These Were Sold

These robes were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue stores and in smaller boutiques nationwide. They were also available online at Bellabubear.com, Letthembelittleboutique.com, and Saksfifthavenue.com from January 2024 through July 2025 for approximately $60. Given the popular character designs and the gift-friendly price point, they were a common shower gift, holiday present, and birthday item during that period.

What to Do

Stop using the robe and keep it away from children. Contact Bellabu Bear to receive a full refund or store credit for the original purchase price. You can call 888-703-7752, Monday through Friday from 9 AM to 4 PM PT, or email help@bellabubear.com. The full recall notice is available at CPSC.gov/Recalls.

If you gave one of these as a gift and still have the recipient's contact information, it's worth passing this recall along to them directly.

What's Next

Keep CPSC.gov bookmarked and check it periodically for new recalls on products your family uses. You can also sign up for email recall alerts directly from CPSC.gov so future notices come to you automatically. If your child was injured by a recalled product, file a report at SaferProducts.gov.
