CPSC Recall — Vevor Baby Swings (Suffocation Hazard, ~1,020 Units)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 12:00 PM MT If you have a Vevor baby swing at home, please read this. Sanven Technology has issued a recall of approximately 1,020 Vevor baby swings due to a serious suffocation hazard. The swings have an incline angle that exceeds 10 degrees — violating the mandatory safety standard for infant sleep products and the ban on inclined sleepers under the Safe Sleep for Babies Act. The affected swings come in three models: white/gray with a white base (BB501K), white/gray with a gray base (BB702A), and black/gray with a black base (BB005K). All have the "VEVOR" trademark on the base. They were sold on Vevor.com and Amazon.com from January 2025 through August 2025, priced between $65 and $80. Stop using the swing immediately. To receive a full refund, you'll need to cut the swing cover in half, write "Recalled" and the date on the base with a permanent marker, take a photo, and send it to recalling@vevor.com. Then dispose of the swing according to your local waste guidelines. To start your refund or get more information, call 855-599-6320 Monday through Friday, 9 AM-5 PM PT. The full recall notice is on CPSC.gov. This is the third recall alert we've shared this week. It's a good time to check your home for any recalled products — CPSC.gov keeps a complete list. Do you have a Vevor swing at home? Let us know in the comments if this helped. ---

CPSC Recall — Bellabu Bear Children's Sherpa Robes (Burn Hazard, ~1,300 Units)
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 5:00 PM MT One more safety alert for parents today. Bellabu Bear has recalled approximately 1,300 bamboo sherpa children's robes after testing showed they fail the mandatory federal flammability standard for children's sleepwear — posing a burn risk. No injuries have been reported, but the robes shouldn't be used. These are bamboo sherpa robes in popular licensed prints you might recognize: PAW Patrol, Minecraft, Minions, Batman, Justice League, Wonder Woman, Blaze and the Monster Machines, Trolls, Dora the Explorer, and others, as well as seasonal designs including Nutcracker, hearts, Santa, and stripes. They come in sizes S/M and M/L. The label says "Bellabu Bear," "BB-NW12109," and the size. The robes were sold at Saks Fifth Avenue, boutiques, and on Bellabubear.com from January 2024 through July 2025 for about $60. To get a full refund or store credit, stop using the robe and contact Bellabu Bear at 888-703-7752 (Monday-Friday, 9 AM-4 PM PT) or email help@bellabubear.com. The full recall notice is at CPSC.gov. If you gave these as a gift or received one as a shower present, it's worth a quick check. Do you have one of these robes in your home? Share this post so other parents can check. ---

### Long-Form Post Window: 3:00 PM MT Memorial Day weekend is officially here, Colorado Springs families — and you've got options. Here's what's happening this weekend and into next week: TERRITORY DAYS (May 23-25): The 50th anniversary of Territory Days kicks off Saturday on Colorado Avenue in Old Colorado City. This free three-day festival has over 180 food and craft booths, live music, a kids' zone, petting zoo, gold panning, Wild West gunfight re-enactments, and more. Hours: Saturday-Sunday 10 AM-7 PM, Monday 10 AM-6 PM. Parking tip: use the C.A.B. Garage downtown ($1/hr) with a free shuttle to the festival. D49 GRADUATION (Saturday): Three District 49 high schools — Falcon, Vista Ridge, and Sand Creek — hold commencement ceremonies at Broadmoor World Arena on Saturday. No tickets required. See our earlier post for times. SPLASH PADS OPENING (Tuesday, May 26): Panorama Park Splash Pad and Venezia Park Watering Hole both open Tuesday, May 26 at 10 AM. Both are free and run through summer. Start planning your first splash pad day of the year. PPLD SUMMER ADVENTURE: Registration is open now at ppld.org/summer for the "Unearth a Story" summer reading program. It kicks off June 2 — the same week as D11's last day of school. Good timing to get signed up. It's one of the busiest, best weekends of the year in COS. Enjoy every bit of it. What's on your family's agenda this weekend? Share in the comments. ---
