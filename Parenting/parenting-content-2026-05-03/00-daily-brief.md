### STORY 2: CPSC Recall — 227,500 Stephan Baby Plush Toys Pulled for Choking Hazard
**Tier:** 1
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled about 227,500 Stephan Baby Boo Bunnie and Friends children's toys due to a choking and laceration hazard. The toys include a removable, water-filled plastic cube that can break into smaller parts. The CPSC has received 20 reports of the cube breaking, including one report of a child who swallowed sharp pieces. Toys were sold at Amazon, Target, Walmart, and independent stores from August 2017 through January 2026. Parents should immediately remove the plastic cube from the toy. Refund available with or without proof of purchase at cpsc.gov/Recalls.

### STORY 4: AAP Releases New Screen Time Guidelines for 2026 — Less About Hours, More About Habits
**Tier:** 2
**Content Pillar:** National Parenting
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

The American Academy of Pediatrics released updated screen time guidance in early 2026, making a significant shift from the previous "no more than 2 hours per day" rule for children ages 6 and older. The new framework focuses on quality, context, and conversation through the "5 Cs" approach. Key rules remain for younger children: no screens before 18 months; for ages 2–5, limit to one hour of high-quality content per day. Device-free bedrooms and mealtimes are now emphasized as habits, and co-viewing with discussion is encouraged as the gold standard for all ages.
