### STORY 2: CPSC Recall — Stephan Baby Plush Toys

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM MT)**

COS parents — please check your home for this product. ⚠️

The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled about 227,500 Stephan Baby Boo Bunnie and Friends children's plush toys. These are small plush animals that come with a removable, water-filled plastic cube designed to be chilled for comfort.

Here's the problem: that plastic cube can break into smaller, sharp pieces. The CPSC has received 20 reports of the cube breaking apart, including one incident where a child swallowed sharp pieces.

These were sold at Amazon, Target, Walmart, and independent stores across the country from August 2017 through January 2026. If you bought one over the years as a gift or teething comfort item, it may still be in your home.

What to do right now: take the plastic cube away from your child and stop using it. You don't have to throw the whole toy away — just the cube.

For a refund: if you have proof of purchase, you'll receive a full refund. No receipt? You can still get a $10 refund by check or a $20 store credit at stephanbaby.com. Go to cpsc.gov/Recalls for the full details.

Has anyone spotted this toy in their house? Drop a comment so other families know to check too. 👇

### STORY 4: AAP Screen Time Guidelines 2026

**Long-Form Post (12:00 PM MT)**

COS parents — the American Academy of Pediatrics changed the screen time rules, and honestly? We're here for it. 📱

The AAP released updated guidelines in early 2026, and the big headline is this: for kids ages 6 and older, they've dropped the strict "no more than 2 hours per day" rule. Instead, the new approach focuses on quality, context, and habits — not just counting minutes.

Here's what didn't change: under 18 months, screens are still a no. For ages 2–5, the recommendation is still one hour per day of high-quality content.

What's the new framework? The AAP calls it the "5 Cs" — and the goal is to help parents think about whether screen time is adding to or taking away from their child's development, rather than just watching the clock.

The practical takeaways are things most parents already know but maybe needed permission to prioritize: keep bedrooms and mealtimes device-free, co-watch and talk about what they're seeing, and remember that your own habits set the tone.

This doesn't mean screens are suddenly fine in unlimited quantities. It means pediatricians are acknowledging that a 10-year-old doing creative things online for three hours isn't the same as a 10-year-old mindlessly scrolling for three hours.

What do you think of the new approach — relief, or do you prefer having a clear limit? 👇

### STORY 5: Garden of the Gods Art Festival — May 16–17

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM MT)**

If you're looking for a perfect spring family day in COS, we've got one for you: the Garden of the Gods Art Festival is coming up May 16–17 at Rock Ledge Ranch. 🎨

Here's what you need to know:

**When:** May 16–17, 10 AM–5 PM both days
**Where:** Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site, Colorado Springs
**Admission:** $10 for adults, $8 for seniors 65+ and military, and free for children under 12 (and free for Rock Ledge members)

This is the 3rd annual festival, and it features 150 juried fine artists and craftspeople from across the country — pottery, jewelry, textiles, paintings, sculpture, glass art, and more. There's live music, food trucks, kids activities, and the kind of backdrop (hello, Pikes Peak) that you just can't get anywhere else.

Pro tip: there's a free shuttle running from Coronado High School at 1590 W. Fillmore St. from 9:30 AM–5 PM both days if you'd rather skip the parking hunt.

Admission proceeds benefit Rock Ledge Ranch, so your $10 is doing something good for the community.
