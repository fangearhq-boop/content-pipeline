Garden of the Gods Art Festival May 16–17: 150 Artists, Free for Kids, and a Pikes Peak Backdrop

By Keisha Williams | COS Parenting | May 3, 2026

If you're looking for a family outing in Colorado Springs on the weekend of May 16 and 17, the Garden of the Gods Art Festival has the combination of views, value, and activity options that makes it worth putting on the calendar. And for families with kids under 12, admission is free.

What to Expect

The 3rd Annual Garden of the Gods Art Festival brings 150 juried fine artists and craftspeople from across the country to Rock Ledge Ranch. The art spans pottery, jewelry, textiles, paintings, sculpture, and glass work — a range wide enough to hold an adult's attention while kids wander alongside.

Families with younger children will find that the setting does a lot of the work. Rock Ledge Ranch sits with Pikes Peak on the horizon and the distinctive red rock formations of Garden of the Gods nearby. Live music plays throughout both days, and food trucks make it easy to stay for a few hours without needing to pack a lunch. The Ranch setting also gives kids room to move in a way that an indoor event doesn't.

Admission and Value

At $10 for adults and free for children under 12, a family of two parents and two young children pays $20 to attend — significantly less than most comparable spring events in the region. Admission proceeds go directly to Rock Ledge Ranch Historic Site, which means your entry fee supports the preservation of a meaningful piece of Colorado Springs history.

Rock Ledge Ranch members get in free. If you're a member, this is an easy yes for the weekend.

Getting There

There are two ways to arrive. The main parking lot at Rock Ledge Ranch is one entry point, and there's also an entry from the south side near Chambers Way and Pike Drive. For families who prefer not to deal with parking, the free shuttle from Coronado High School at 1590 W. Fillmore St. runs from 9:30 AM to 5 PM on both Saturday and Sunday. It's a convenient option, especially if you're planning to arrive near opening time when the parking area is busier.

What's Next

The Garden of the Gods Art Festival runs the same weekend as Touch-A-Truck (also May 16 — covered in an earlier COS Parenting article). If you have a family that wants to do both events on Saturday the 16th, be aware of timing. The art festival runs 10 AM to 5 PM, while Touch-A-Truck is at a separate location. For more information on the art festival, visit rockledgeranch.com or search the event on peakradar.com. No advance tickets are required — admission is at the gate.
