CPSC Recall: 227,500 Stephan Baby Boo Bunnie Plush Toys Pulled for Choking and Laceration Hazard

By Sofia Reyes | COS Parenting | May 3, 2026

If you've ever bought a Stephan Baby Boo Bunnie plush toy — or received one as a gift — check your home. The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has recalled about 227,500 of these children's comfort toys due to a choking and laceration hazard that has already led to at least one injury.

What Was Recalled

The recall covers Stephan Baby Boo Bunnie and Friends children's plush toys. Each toy is a small plush animal — sold in various colors and shapes, measuring roughly 3.5 to 3.7 inches long — that comes with a removable, water-filled plastic cube designed to be chilled and used as a comfort or teething item.

The hazard is in that cube. According to the CPSC, the plastic cube can break apart into smaller pieces that are sharp and small enough to pose both a choking risk and a laceration hazard. The CPSC has received 20 reports of the cube breaking into small parts. One of those reports involves a child who swallowed sharp pieces.

What COS Families Should Do Right Now

The action is straightforward: stop using the plastic cube and take it away from children immediately. The plush animal itself is not being recalled — only the cube component. But given how these toys are designed and used, it's safest to remove the cube from the child's reach entirely while you sort out a refund.

These toys were widely distributed. They were sold at Amazon, Target, Walmart, and independent stores nationwide from August 2017 through January 2026. Given how long they've been on the market, many families may have received them as baby shower or birthday gifts and still have them in rotation.

If you don't have a receipt, you can still get a $10 refund in the form of a check or a $20 stephanbaby.com store credit. If you do have proof of purchase, a full refund is available. Contact CBC Group directly or visit cpsc.gov/Recalls for full instructions.

What's Next

COS parents who want to report an issue with a children's product — including this one — can contact the CPSC at 1-800-638-2772 or file a report at SaferProducts.gov. The CPSC issues recall notices on an ongoing basis, and staying subscribed to their alert list is a straightforward way to get notified when children's products in your home are flagged. Check cpsc.gov/Recalls regularly, especially after purchasing new children's items online.
