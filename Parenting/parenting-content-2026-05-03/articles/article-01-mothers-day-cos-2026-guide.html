Mother's Day in Colorado Springs 2026: A Family Guide to Events, Brunch, and Free Outdoor Fun

By Marcus Rivera | COS Parenting | May 3, 2026

Mother's Day is Sunday, May 10 — and COS has more going on that day than most families realize. Whether you're working with a tight budget or ready to go all out, here's a practical rundown of what's happening around Colorado Springs this weekend so you can make a plan before everything sells out.

Free and Low-Cost Options

If the budget is tight, the Downtown Farmers Market opening day is a genuinely lovely way to spend a Sunday morning. Acacia Park comes alive from 9 AM to 2 PM with local vendors, fresh produce, flowers, and the kind of slow morning energy that feels like a real break. It's free to attend and runs every Sunday through late October, so May 10 is the first day of the season.

For families who want to get outdoors, Colorado Springs has no shortage of free trails and parks with mountain views. North Cheyenne Cañon Park and Garden of the Gods are both free to enter and genuinely beautiful on a spring morning. Packing a picnic or stopping for coffee en route keeps it easy and budget-friendly.

Experiences Worth Booking Now

The Colorado Wolf & Wildlife Center wolf tour (9–11 AM on May 10) is a genuinely memorable experience for families, but it requires reservations and fills up. At $40 per adult and $20 for kids ages 6 to 11, it's a meaningful splurge that includes a guided tour and time with the wolves. Kids who've been to this will talk about it for weeks.

Painting with a Twist on Saturday, May 9 offers two sessions — morning (9 AM–noon) or afternoon (1–3:30 PM) — at 115 N Tejon St. The cost is $45–$70 per person depending on canvas size, and a mom-osa bar is included with the session. It's a good option for families with older kids or for parents looking for an activity they can actually do together.

Brunch and Dining

COS has solid brunch options across several price points. The Lumen8 Rooftop Social brunch runs from 10 AM to 2 PM on May 10 and is priced at $70 for adults, $35 for kids ages 6 to 12, and free for children under five. The Fine Arts Center at Colorado College is a step up — $115 for adults and $25 for kids ages 12 and under — with seating slots between 10 AM and 12:45 PM. Both are likely to fill quickly.

For families with a sense of adventure, the Royal Gorge Route Railroad runs departures on both May 9 and May 10, offering either a three-course gourmet dining experience or casual meals from the 403 Grill. It's a different kind of Mother's Day — riding through a canyon with dinner is memorable in a way that a restaurant reservation often isn't.

What's Next

If you haven't made a reservation yet, now is the time. The Colorado Wolf & Wildlife Center and both Fine Arts Center and Lumen8 brunch seatings do fill up. For a lower-lift plan, the Downtown Farmers Market opening day requires nothing more than showing up at Acacia Park at 9 AM. Whatever you choose, Mother's Day in COS has a lot to offer this year. Happy planning.
