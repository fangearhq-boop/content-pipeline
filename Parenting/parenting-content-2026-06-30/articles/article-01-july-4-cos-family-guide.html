Your COS Family Guide to July 4, 2026 — Fireworks, Free Events, and What to Know

By Sarah Morales | June 30, 2026

Three confirmed events across Colorado Springs and the surrounding area this Saturday — from a ticketed symphony to a free community celebration to a small-town fireworks show.

July 4 is four days away, and COS has a solid lineup of family options this Saturday. Whether you're looking for a full evening out with a headlining orchestra, a free block-party-style celebration with food trucks and bounce houses, or a low-key small-town fireworks experience, here's what's happening and what you need to know before you go.

Star-Spangled Symphony at Ford Amphitheater

The Colorado Springs Philharmonic's annual July 4 concert at Ford Amphitheater is the marquee event in COS this year. Gates open at 5:30 PM at 95 Spectrum Lp (80921), with the show starting at 6:30 PM and fireworks scheduled for around 9:00 PM.

Fort Carson's 4th Infantry Division Band opens the evening. The Philharmonic's program this year includes hit medleys from Dolly Parton, Tina Turner, and Marvin Gaye, a Star Wars suite, and classical Independence Day staples including the 1812 Overture and Stars and Stripes Forever. It's a genuinely packed program across nearly three hours.

Tickets start at $18 and are available through AXS. If you're planning lawn seating, bring a blanket and layers — it gets cool quickly at Ford Amphitheater once the sun goes down, even in July. Covered seating is available for those who want shade during the early part of the evening.

Banning Lewis Ranch July 4 Celebration — Free

For families looking for a no-ticket option, the Banning Lewis Ranch community event at Vista Park Pavilion (8833 Vista Del Pico Blvd) is free and family-friendly from start to finish. The event runs from 3:00 PM, with free bounce houses for kids from 4:00 to 7:00 PM.

Live music runs in two back-to-back sets: Tiny Pockets plays from 4:00 to 6:00 PM, followed by Funkology from 7:00 to 9:00 PM. Thirty food trucks round out the evening, so you won't need to plan dinner ahead of time. Fireworks start at 9:15 PM.

This one works well for families with younger kids who want the full July 4 experience without the cost of a ticketed event. The 3:00 PM start also makes it flexible — you can drop in for bounce houses and food trucks and stay for fireworks, or catch part of the evening and head home before it gets too late.

Palmer Lake Centennial Park — Free, Small-Town Feel

About 30 minutes north of COS on I-25, Palmer Lake runs its own community celebration from 4:00 to 10:00 PM at Centennial Park. Entry is free, and the evening features activities, food, local vendors, giveaways, and fireworks at approximately 9:15 PM.

Palmer Lake is worth the short drive if you prefer a quieter, small-town July 4 atmosphere. The crowd is smaller, the setting is charming, and the fireworks show is a solid community production. It's a nice alternative for families who want to skip the volume of a big COS event.

What's Next

For the Star-Spangled Symphony, check fordamphitheater.live and axs.com for tickets — availability tends to fill as the date approaches. For Banning Lewis Ranch, no tickets are required; just show up at Vista Park Pavilion (8833 Vista Del Pico Blvd) anytime from 3:00 PM. For Palmer Lake, head north on I-25 to Centennial Park starting at 4:00 PM.

A reminder that consumer fireworks are permanently banned in the City of Colorado Springs — all three events above are professional shows, which is exactly where families should be on July 4. More on fireworks safety in our 3:00 PM post today.
