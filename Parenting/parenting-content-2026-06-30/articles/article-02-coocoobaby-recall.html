Stop Use Now: CPSC Recalls CooCooBaby Baby Loungers for Suffocation Risk

By Jamie Rivera | June 30, 2026

The CPSC has recalled 2,355 CooCooBaby "Classic" and "Deluxe" Baby Loungers sold on Amazon and CooCooBabyOfficial.com. If you have one, here's what to do — and how to get your full refund.

If you have a CooCooBaby baby lounger at home, the U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission has issued a recall and the action to take is clear: stop using it now. The recall covers 2,355 units of CooCooBaby's "Classic" and "Deluxe" Baby Loungers, sold on Amazon and CooCooBabyOfficial.com from December 2024 through March 2026 for $35 to $70.

The CPSC has identified three overlapping safety problems with the loungers — all related to suffocation and fall risk for infants.

Why the Loungers Were Recalled

The CPSC found that the CooCooBaby loungers violate mandatory federal Infant Sleep Products standards in three specific ways:

  • Sides too short: The sides of the lounger are shorter than the mandatory minimum height, creating a suffocation risk.
  • Sleeping pad too thick: The sleeping pad exceeds the maximum allowed thickness, which is another suffocation hazard — infant faces can become pressed against thicker surfaces in ways that restrict airflow.
  • Open foot design: The open end at the foot of the lounger allows an infant to slip down until entrapped, creating both an entrapment and a fall risk.

The Classic model has a ribbon tie at the foot opening. The Deluxe model has buttons at the foot opening. Both are recalled.

How to Get Your Full Refund

The refund process is handled entirely by email — no phone call required, no physical return needed. Here's what to do:

  1. Stop using the lounger immediately.
  2. Cut the sides of the lounger.
  3. Cut the sleeping pad.
  4. Take a photograph of the destroyed product.
  5. Email the photo to support@coocoobabyofficial.com.

The CPSC confirms that a full refund will be issued to your original payment method after you submit the photo. Destroying the product before submitting ensures the recalled item can't be passed along or resold.

If you purchased the lounger on Amazon and no longer have the order details, check your Amazon order history — orders from December 2024 onward will appear there.

What This Means for Your Family

If you have one of these loungers and your infant has been sleeping in it, that's understandably alarming to read. The recall reflects real hazards with the product's design — but the action here is straightforward. Destroy the product, send the photo, and get your refund.

If you're not sure whether your lounger is affected, the Classic model has a ribbon tie at the foot end and the Deluxe has buttons at the foot end. Both are in the recalled group.

For safe infant sleep in the meantime, the AAP recommends a firm, flat surface — a crib, bassinet, or play yard with a firm mattress and fitted sheet, nothing else in the sleep space.

What's Next

For the full official recall notice, visit cpsc.gov and search for CooCooBaby. To submit your refund request, email a photo of the destroyed lounger to support@coocoobabyofficial.com. If you know another family who has one of these products, please share this with them — the recall was issued recently and awareness is still spreading.
