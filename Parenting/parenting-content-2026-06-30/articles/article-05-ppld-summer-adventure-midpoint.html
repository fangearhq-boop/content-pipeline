It's the Halfway Point — PPLD Summer Adventure Has 31 Days Left and It's Free

By Sarah Morales | June 30, 2026

Today is the exact midpoint of Pikes Peak Library District's Summer Adventure program. If your family hasn't started yet, there are still 31 days — and no registration required.

Today is June 30 — which puts us at the exact halfway point of PPLD's Summer Adventure program. The program runs June 1 through July 31, which means 31 days remain. If you've been meaning to get your kids signed up and haven't done it yet, this is a solid reminder that there's still time to make the most of the back half.

The program is free, open to all ages, and requires no advance registration to participate. You can start today.

What Summer Adventure Includes

PPLD's Summer Adventure is a reading and activity program built around 30 unique activities that span the summer — some you can do at home, some are tied to in-library programming. Kids, teens, and adults can all participate, which makes it genuinely family-wide rather than just a kids' program.

Tracking your family's progress through the Beanstack app is what qualifies you for prize drawings. Grand prizes this year include a stay at Broadmoor Cloud Camp and a Pikes Peak Attractions VIP pass — two COS-specific prizes that families are unlikely to stumble into otherwise.

PPLD has 14 library branch locations across Colorado Springs and the surrounding area, plus two bookmobiles that serve areas further from fixed locations. You don't need to visit a specific branch — any PPLD location counts.

Why Summer Reading Programs Matter

Programs like Summer Adventure are designed to address what researchers call "summer slide" — the measurable loss of academic skills that happens when kids go 10-plus weeks without structured engagement. Studies estimate that children can lose 25 to 30 percent of the skills they gained during the previous school year over an unstructured summer. The effect compounds year over year.

Reading consistently over the summer is one of the most effective counters to summer slide — and the research on that has been consistent across decades. You don't need a formal program for it to work, but a program like Summer Adventure adds structure, built-in motivation from the activity list, and the social element of library visits, which keeps the habit from becoming purely solo.

For families whose kids are heading into kindergarten or a new school year in the fall, the next 31 days represent a window. It's not too late to use it.

What's Next

Head to ppld.org/summer to see the activity list, download the Beanstack app, and find your nearest branch. No registration is required — you can show up at any PPLD location and ask staff to help you get started. The program closes July 31.
