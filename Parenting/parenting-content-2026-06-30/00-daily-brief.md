### STORY 2: Recall Alert — CooCooBaby Baby Loungers Pose Suffocation and Fall Hazard
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY (CPSC recall; sold Dec 2024–March 2026)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Posting Window:** 10:00 AM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera
**Sources:** cpsc.gov/Recalls/2026/CooCooBaby, thecooldown.com, wftv.com, wdbo.com

The CPSC has recalled 2,355 CooCooBaby "Classic" and "Deluxe" Baby Loungers due to suffocation and fall hazards. The loungers violate the mandatory standard for Infant Sleep Products: sides are shorter than the minimum height limit; sleeping pad is thicker than the maximum limit (suffocation hazard); open foot of the lounger allows infants to slip through and become entrapped or fall. Sold on Amazon and CooCooBabyOfficial.com from December 2024 through March 2026 for $35–$70. Stop use immediately. To get a refund: destroy the lounger (cut sides and sleeping pad), photograph the destroyed product, and email the photo to support@coocoobabyofficial.com — a full refund is issued to the original payment method.

### STORY 3: Water Safety Reminder for COS Families — Drowning Is Preventable
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY (summer safety; CPSC 2026 report + AAP guidelines)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Posting Window:** 12:00 PM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Byline:** Sarah Morales
**Sources:** cdc.gov/drowning, cpsc.gov/Newsroom, redcross.org, partnersinpediatrics.com, cpw.state.co.us/water-safety, healthychildren.org

Drowning is the leading cause of unintentional injury death for children ages 1 to 4 and the second leading cause for ages 5 to 14. In Colorado, 2023 was the state's deadliest year for drownings. Key prevention facts: swim lessons reduce drowning risk by 88% (AAP recommends starting lessons at age 1); active supervision means staying within arm's length of children in or near water; a four-sided pool fence reduces drowning risk by 83%; US Coast Guard-approved life jackets (not water wings or floaties) are required on open water. Colorado-specific risks: cold mountain water causes muscle cramps and shock even on warm days; spring/early summer snowmelt makes rivers and streams fast and dangerous. COS resources: SafeSplash Colorado Springs (safesplash.com), Colorado Parks and Wildlife water safety (cpw.state.co.us/water-safety), Pikes Peak YMCA aquatics programs. CPSC released a report in 2026 highlighting the persistent and preventable risk of childhood drowning.

### STORY 4: Fireworks Safety — What COS Families Need to Know Before July 4
**Tier:** 2
**Status:** NEW STORY (July 4 weekend timing; combines local fireworks laws + AAP safety guidelines)
**Pillar:** National Parenting
**Posting Window:** 3:00 PM MT
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article
**Byline:** Jamie Rivera
**Sources:** koaa.com/consumer-fireworks-illegal-cos, shouselaw.com/co/defense/laws/fireworks, healthychildren.org, boystownhospital.org, contemporarypediatrics.com

Consumer fireworks are permanently banned in the City of Colorado Springs. Stage I fire restrictions (effective since May 2026 in unincorporated El Paso County) also prohibit fireworks use in all unincorporated county areas. Only legal statewide in Colorado: sparklers, snake/glow worms, fountains, smoke bombs, and ground spinners — but no personal use is allowed in Colorado Springs city limits. Safety for attending public shows: fireworks can reach 150+ decibels; the safe hearing threshold is 85 dB; earmuffs are recommended for babies and toddlers (designed for children under age 2); infants should ideally not be exposed to fireworks at all. AAP recommends attending public professional displays rather than lighting personal fireworks. Safe viewing distance: at least 500 feet from the launch site. Sparklers burn at approximately 2,000°F — not a safe children's toy despite their common use.
