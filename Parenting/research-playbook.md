## Story Tiering

### Tier 1 — Urgent / High-Impact (1-2 stories/day)
- School closures or emergency schedule changes
- Product recalls affecting children (CPSC)
- Severe weather alerts impacting families
- Major local news directly affecting COS families
- Registration deadlines (camps, schools) within 48 hours
- New AAP/CDC health guidelines

### Tier 2 — Timely / Notable (3-4 stories/day)
- This weekend's family events and activities
- School district updates (non-emergency)
- Notable parenting studies or research findings
- New park/playground/facility openings
- Seasonal activity guides (within current week)

### Tier 3 — Evergreen / Engagement (1-2 stories/day)
- Best-of lists (playgrounds, restaurants, hikes)
- Funny/relatable parenting data or moments
- General parenting tips with COS angle
- Community spotlights
- Historical/fun facts about Colorado Springs

## Posting Priority

What goes in the **earliest time slots** (replaces "game results first" from sports):

1. **Safety-critical** — recalls, closures, weather alerts (immediate)
2. **Time-sensitive** — registration deadlines, events happening today
3. **School news** — district updates, calendar changes
4. **Everything else** — studies, tips, evergreen, humor

## Fact-Check Focus

| Claim Type | Risk Level | Why High-Risk | Verify Via |
|------------|-----------|---------------|------------|
| **Dates, times, locations** | HIGHEST | Events change, get cancelled, times shift | Official event page or organizer site |
| **"Free" claims** | HIGH | Events sometimes add fees, require registration | Confirm on official site day-of |
| **Age ranges** | HIGH | "Ages 3-8" vs "Ages 5-12" matters a lot to parents | Official event listing |
| **Prices** | HIGH | Admission costs change; "$5" last year might be "$8" now | Official site, not cached results |
| **School district info** | HIGH | 7 districts with different calendars — easy to mix up | Specific district website (d11.org, asd20.org, etc.) |
| **Health/safety claims** | HIGH | AAP guidelines update; old advice may be wrong now | AAP.org or CDC.gov directly |
| **Phone numbers, addresses** | MEDIUM | Can be outdated in search results | Google Maps + official site cross-check |
| **Study statistics** | MEDIUM | WebFetch may distort sample sizes, effect sizes | Original study or reputable reporting |

## Slow News Day Strategies

When local news is light:
- Search for national parenting stories and add a COS angle
- Pull from the Evergreen or Humor content pillars
- Check upcoming events (next 7 days) for preview content
- Look for "hidden gem" local activities to spotlight
- Review recent AAP/CDC publications for anything new

## Coverage Reminders

- **Always specify which school district** — D11/D20/D49 have different calendars
- **Military families are a key audience** — include Fort Carson/Peterson resources when relevant
- **Free is king** — always highlight free options prominently
- **Altitude matters** — outdoor activity tips should account for 6,000+ ft
- **Weather caveat** — spring snow through May; always note weather-dependent events
