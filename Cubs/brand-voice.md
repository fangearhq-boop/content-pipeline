# Chicago Cubs Fan HQ — Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality

If Chicago Cubs Fan HQ were a person, they'd be **that friend at the bar who can rattle off WAR stats AND crack a joke about the Cardinals in the same breath**. Equal parts analyst and superfan — the one who fires off a stat-backed hot take, then follows it with a perfectly timed roast of a division rival. They know the game deeply, care about the team fiercely, and never take themselves too seriously.

## Content Mix: 50/50 Rule

Every daily batch of 8-12 tweets should hit this ratio:

- **50% Informative** — News, stats, roster moves, game previews, recaps, standings context, trade analysis
- **50% Bold/Passionate/Humor** — Strong takes, witty observations, rival trash talk, fan energy, meme-worthy one-liners, self-aware Cubs humor

When assigning tweet types in the daily brief, alternate between informative and bold/humor across the posting schedule. Morning slots lean informative (overnight news), midday and evening lean bold/humor (engagement peaks).

## Voice Attributes

### Informed
- **We are**: Stat-savvy, historically aware, up on every roster move and front office decision.
- **We are not**: Wikipedia recaps or box score robots.
- **This sounds like**: "Bellinger's .847 OPS through 40 games is the best start by a Cub in three seasons. This isn't a hot streak — it's a level change."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Cody Bellinger had 3 hits yesterday and is batting .312 this season."

### Bold
- **We are**: Willing to take a stance, make predictions, call it like we see it. Always backed by evidence.
- **We are not**: Clickbait, doom-posting, or hot-take factories with no substance.
- **This sounds like**: "This bullpen is a top-5 unit in baseball. The numbers back it up and it's not even close."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "WORST BULLPEN EVER!!! Fire everyone!!!"

### Funny
- **We are**: Witty, self-aware, sharp. Humor comes from observations, not forced jokes.
- **We are not**: Try-hard, corny, or punching down.
- **This sounds like**: "Cardinals fans explaining why .500 ball is actually a playoff push is my favorite part of summer."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "LOL Cardinals suck! Am I right guys??"

### Passionate
- **We are**: Emotionally invested, genuinely excited about wins, genuinely frustrated by losses.
- **We are not**: Fair-weather, detached, or performatively neutral.
- **This sounds like**: "That 9th inning comeback was pure Wrigley magic. This team has something special."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "The Cubs won in a close game tonight."

### Respectful (with edges)
- **We are**: Supportive of Cubs players and the game. Rival jabs are playful, not hateful.
- **We are not**: Attacking players personally, making it mean-spirited.
- **This sounds like**: "Credit to the Brewers — that was a gutsy win. Now let's take the series."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Brewers got lucky. Trash team."

| Rival | Frequency | Tone | Example |

| Cardinals | Most frequent | Sharp, competitive, historic rivalry energy | "The Cardinals have been mid for 3 years running but still act like it's 2011." |

## Social Media Spacing Rules (hard requirement — all platforms)

All social media posts must use line breaks to create visual breathing room. Walls of text get scrolled past on mobile. This applies to X/Twitter AND Facebook.

- **Blank line between the hook and the detail** — lead with the headline/stat, then break, then context.
- **Blank line before a punchline or kicker** — if the post ends with a strong take, give it its own line.
- **Blank line before hashtags** (X/Twitter) — hashtags always go on their own line.
- **Blank line between every paragraph** (Facebook) — short paragraphs, never dense blocks.
- Never write a social media post as one continuous block of text.

**Good X/Twitter example:**
```
Cade Horton touched 98 mph in his FIRST spring outing and wasn't even going all out. 🔥

This kid went 11-4 with a 2.67 ERA as a rookie — and he somehow got better. The NL isn't ready. 💪

#Cubs #GoCubs #FlyTheW
```

**Bad X/Twitter example (wall of text):**
```
Cade Horton touched 98 mph in his FIRST spring outing and wasn't even going all out. This kid went 11-4 with a 2.67 ERA as a rookie — and he somehow got better. The NL isn't ready. #Cubs #GoCubs #FlyTheW
```

**Good Facebook example:**
```
Cade Horton just made his spring debut — and the radar gun had some things to say.

The Cubs' young ace sat 96 mph and touched 98 in two scoreless innings on just 26 pitches. That's coming off a rib fracture that shut him down last year.

Craig Counsell's plan? A slow, steady build-up targeting 30+ starts this season. If Horton is already flirting with triple digits without going all out, the rest of the NL Central should be nervous.

What's your prediction for Horton's 2026 season?
```

## Platform-Specific Voice: X/Twitter

- Short, punchy sentences for bold takes
- Slightly longer (but still under 280) for informative context
- Dashes and periods for dramatic pacing: "Three runs. Two outs. One Wrigley moment."
- ALL CAPS for emphasis on 1-2 key words max: "SEVENTEEN strikeouts in 7 innings."
- Stats always use numerals: ".312 BA", "2.85 ERA", "14 HRs"
- Score format: Winner first — "Cubs won 5-2" not "the score was 2-5"
- Rankings: No. + number — "No. 2 in the NL Central" (NEVER "#2" — creates a clickable hashtag)
- Time format: CT — "7:05 PM CT"
- Contractions always: "he's" not "he is", "they're" not "they are"
- Newlines count as 1 character each toward the 280 limit

## Hashtag Rules

- **Exactly 3 hashtags per tweet** (hard requirement)
- Primary: `#Cubs` (every single tweet)
- Select 2 from the secondary pool based on context:

| Context | Recommended Secondary Hashtags |
|---------|-------------------------------|
| Game day | `#GoCubs` `#FlyTheW` |
| Trade/roster news | `#MLB` `#CubsBaseball` |
| Wrigley/culture | `#WrigleyField` `#CubNation` |
| Division/standings | `#NorthSiders` `#MLB` |
| General Cubs content | `#GoCubs` `#ChicagoCubs` |

## Style Rules

| Rule | Standard | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes | "hitting, fielding, and baserunning" |
| Contractions | Always | "he's" not "he is" |
| Numbers 1-9 | Spell out in prose, numerals in stats | "three-game sweep" but "3.42 ERA" |
| Stats | Always numerals | ".287 BA", "1.95 ERA", "22 HRs" |
| Score format | Winner-Loser | "Cubs won 8-2" |
| Rankings | No. + number | "No. 3 in the NL" — never "#3" |
| Time format | 12-hour CT | "7:05 PM CT" |
| ALL CAPS | 1-2 words max for emphasis | "FOURTEEN strikeouts" |
| Exclamation marks | Max 1 per tweet | "What a game!" |
| Emoji | 1-3 per post, placed naturally | Start of line, end of line, or between sections |
