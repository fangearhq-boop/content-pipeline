WTA Athens QFs: Sakkari on Home Soil, Zheng vs Krejcikova in Historic Setting

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 17, 2026 — Athens, Greece

Athens has not hosted a WTA event in 35 years. On Friday, the city returns to the women's tennis calendar with a quarterfinal lineup that could not have been scripted more compellingly: a Greek star on home soil, an American opponent who has beaten her twice, and two former or current grand slam-level players meeting in the opposite half of the draw.

Both quarterfinals are scheduled for Centre Court. Both carry a weight that extends well beyond a standard ATP 250 or WTA 250 occasion.

Sakkari vs Parks: The Home Crowd Factor

Maria Sakkari has been commanding this week. In her two matches in Athens, she has conceded just three games total — defeating Kudermetova 6-0, 7-6(1) and Dart 6-1, 6-2 with minimal resistance. The Greek crowd, playing their first WTA home event in 35 years, has been fully behind her at every turn.

Friday's opponent complicates the narrative. Alycia Parks of the United States leads their career head-to-head 2-0, and crucially, both of those wins came on hard courts. The Athens event this week is being played on hard court. That head-to-head record suggests that Parks has found something against Sakkari's game that works — and she has converted it consistently when the conditions match.

The question is whether the emotional weight of the occasion alters the dynamic. Sakkari has the crowd, the form, and the home advantage. Parks has the head-to-head.

Krejcikova vs Zheng: Grand Slam Pedigree on Both Sides

Top seed Barbora Krejcikova of Czech Republic has one of the more complete games on the WTA tour — a former grand slam champion who combines serving power with exceptional touch at the net. No. 3 seed Qinwen Zheng of China is one of the most formidable athletes on tour and a player who has risen rapidly through the rankings.

Their head-to-head tells a clear recent story: Zheng leads 2-0. Whether that reflects a specific tactical advantage or simply that Zheng has been in better form in their meetings, Friday's quarterfinal will offer fresh data in a new and historically significant setting.

The first WTA event in Athens in 35 years deserves this kind of matchup — two elite players, each capable of winning the title, meeting in a venue that is only now remembering what professional women's tennis feels like.

What's Next

The winners of both quarterfinals advance to the WTA Athens semifinals, which are expected on Saturday, July 18. The final is scheduled for Sunday, July 19. With Sakkari, Parks, Krejcikova, and Zheng all still in contention, the remaining days of competition in Athens are shaping up to deliver exactly what a returning tournament needs: big names, big matchups, and a crowd that has been waiting three and a half decades to see professional women's tennis come home.
