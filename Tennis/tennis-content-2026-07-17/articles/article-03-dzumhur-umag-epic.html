Dzumhur Survives 3h 42m Umag Epic — Walkout Threat, Three Sets, First SF in Four Tries

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 17, 2026 — Umag, Croatia

Damir Dzumhur has been coming to Umag for years. In four previous visits to the Croatia Open, he had never made a semifinal. On Friday, in a match that ran three hours and 42 minutes and came close to ending before the final point was played, he finally got there.

Dzumhur defeated fourth seed Matteo Arnaldi of Italy 7-6(5), 6-7(4), 7-6(4) in a quarterfinal that had everything: a marathon duration, a mid-match walkout threat, a spectacular comeback in the opening set, and a third-set tiebreak that put one of the most compelling storylines of the week over the finish line.

"This is why we love the sport," Dzumhur said after the match. It was hard to argue.

The Match

Dzumhur took the first set in a tiebreak 7-5. In the second, he built what appeared to be a decisive advantage — up 5-3, 30-30 — but Arnaldi clawed his way back, eventually winning the second-set tiebreak 7-4 to level the match.

Then came the drama that defined the afternoon. Mid-match, an electronic line-call dispute escalated to the point where Dzumhur threatened to walk off the court in protest. Players occasionally express frustration with line-call technology; Dzumhur's reaction went further. For a moment, the match appeared in genuine jeopardy of ending prematurely.

He stayed. He won the third-set tiebreak 7-4. He is in the Umag semifinals.

A Veteran Story

Dzumhur is 34 years old and ranked No. 108 in the world. His 2026 season has been a grind — a record that reflects the reality of professional tennis at this stage of a career. He has kept competing on the tour long past the point where many players have wound down, and weeks like this one are a reminder of why.

A four-time Umag participant who had never advanced past the quarterfinals, Dzumhur now has the result he had been chasing. At 34, with a ranking outside the top 100, he beat a seeded opponent in a three-set battle that lasted nearly four hours. That is not a small thing.

The walkout threat added an unexpected layer. Line-call disputes are common on tour; threatening to leave the court over one is not. It reflected the temperature of a match that never fully settled into a comfortable rhythm for either player, and it turned what could have been a routine quarterfinal into something people will talk about long after the scoreline fades.

What's Next

Dzumhur will face Alex Molcan of Slovakia in the Umag semifinal. Molcan — ranked No. 101 — defeated second seed Davidovich Fokina 3-6, 6-1, 6-2 in the quarterfinals, continuing a theme of upsets that has defined this year's Croatia Open. Both Umag semifinals feature players ranked outside the top 100 with no ATP titles to their names. One of them will reach Sunday's final.
