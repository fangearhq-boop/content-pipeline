Gstaad Triple Shock: Bublik, Ruud, and Tsitsipas All Eliminated in One Friday

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 17, 2026 — Gstaad, Switzerland

The Swiss Open in Gstaad had its draw upended on Friday in extraordinary fashion. Defending champion Alexander Bublik, two-time champion Casper Ruud, and No. 3 seed Stefanos Tsitsipas were all eliminated in a single afternoon — sending the tournament into its final weekend without any of the players who had been expected to compete for the title.

Three title contenders. One day. All gone.

The results represent one of the most stunning single-day shock sequences in recent ATP 250 history. No event at this level regularly loses its defending champion, a two-time former champion, and a seeded top-10 player in the same session.

Bublik Falls in Round Two

The first domino fell when Bublik and Quentin Halys completed their second-round match, which had been suspended Thursday due to rain. Halys, ranked No. 90 in the world, eliminated Bublik — who had claimed the Gstaad title 12 months ago — ending the Kazakh's defense of his crown before the quarterfinals.

It was a result that set the tone for everything that followed.

Ruud's Title Defense Ends at Cerundolo

Casper Ruud, who has won at Gstaad twice, faced Juan Manuel Cerundolo of Argentina in the quarterfinals. The Norwegian took the opening set 6-3 and appeared on course for the expected outcome. Cerundolo had other ideas, taking the second set 6-3 to level the match before winning a close third set 7-5.

The final score — Cerundolo over Ruud 3-6, 6-3, 7-5 — produced a stunned Alpine crowd. Ruud has been a pillar of this tournament for years. He will not be lifting a third title in Gstaad this week.

Tsitsipas Falls in a Tense Three-Setter

The final match of the afternoon shock sequence was the most dramatic. Stefanos Tsitsipas and Arthur Rinderknech of France played a heavyweight three-setter that featured a 9-point first-set tiebreak won by Tsitsipas, a Rinderknech response in a second-set tiebreak, and a tension-filled third set that Rinderknech won 7-5.

The final score — Rinderknech over Tsitsipas 6-7(9), 7-6(5), 7-5 — eliminated the Greek No. 3 seed and confirmed that the afternoon's results were not isolated. They were a pattern.

Two Frenchmen Left Standing

With Bublik, Ruud, and Tsitsipas all out, the Gstaad draw has a genuinely open look. Rinderknech and Halys — both French — have advanced to the semifinals. Cerundolo of Argentina is also through.

None of them have won this title before. One of them will reach the final with the chance to become a first-time Gstaad champion. The Swiss clay, swept clean of its biggest names, is now anyone's to claim.

Stan Wawrinka bid farewell to Gstaad earlier in the week after losing in the first round — his final appearance at a tournament he had long called home. By Friday evening, the tournament he loved had transformed around him in a matter of hours.

What's Next

The Gstaad semifinals will take place Saturday, July 18. Cerundolo and Rinderknech will face opponents from the remaining half of the draw, with the final set for Sunday, July 19. For the first time in years, the Swiss Open will produce a champion without a previous Gstaad title or a seeded spot in the top three — exactly the kind of chaos that made Friday remarkable.
