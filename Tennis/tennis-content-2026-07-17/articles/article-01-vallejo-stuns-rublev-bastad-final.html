Vallejo Stuns Rublev, Becomes First Paraguayan ATP Finalist in 20 Years

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 17, 2026 — Bastad, Sweden

Adolfo Daniel Vallejo made history at the Nordea Open on Friday, defeating No. 1 seed Andrey Rublev 7-6(5), 6-2 in the Bastad semifinals to become the first Paraguayan player to reach an ATP final in approximately 20 years.

The last time a player from Paraguay stood in an ATP final was Ramon Delgado in Mumbai in 2006. Two decades later, Vallejo — ranked outside the top 100 — ended that wait with one of the most surprising results of the clay-court season.

Rublev, the tournament's top seed, had arrived in Sweden as the overwhelming favorite. The Russian has been one of the most consistent clay-court performers in the world over the past several years, and the draw appeared to open cleanly for him in Bastad. None of that mattered on Friday.

How the Match Unfolded

The first set was tight from the start. Both players held serve without significant trouble through the early games, and the set headed to a tiebreak with neither player having earned a break. Vallejo won it 7-5, taking the opening set and the psychological edge that comes with it.

From there, the match shifted decisively. Vallejo converted his opportunities in the second set and took it 6-2, wrapping up a result that will be remembered long after this week's clay-court tournaments have concluded.

It was not Vallejo's first milestone at Bastad this week. His quarterfinal victory over Stefano Travaglia had already made him the first Paraguayan player to reach an ATP quarterfinal since Delgado's run at the Mumbai Open in 2006. Each match at this tournament has added a page to a growing chapter in Paraguayan tennis history.

A Nation, A Legacy

Paraguayan tennis has rarely made headlines on the ATP Tour. The country has produced skilled players, but the gap between Delgado's era and Vallejo's arrival at this level spans two decades — and, depending on the outcome of Saturday's final, could soon be bookended by the same achievement: an ATP title.

Vallejo's path to Bastad has been one of steady, unheralded work. Players ranked outside the top 100 do not often reach ATP finals, let alone by defeating top seeds in the semifinals. The result signals not just a career-best week for Vallejo, but a genuine upset of a player who was expected to go deep in this draw.

What's Next

Vallejo will face either Nuno Borges or Alejandro Tabilo in Saturday's final at the Nordea Open in Bastad. Whichever opponent he faces, a win would give him his first ATP title and cement the most significant week in Paraguayan tennis since Ramon Delgado two decades ago.

The final is scheduled for Saturday, July 18 or Sunday, July 19, depending on the tournament schedule. Vallejo enters as the underdog — a label he has worn comfortably all week.
