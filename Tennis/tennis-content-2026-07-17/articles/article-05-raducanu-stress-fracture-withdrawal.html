Raducanu Diagnosed With Stress Fracture, Withdraws From Canadian Open and Cincinnati

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 17, 2026

Emma Raducanu's summer hard-court swing is over before it began.

The British player has been diagnosed with a grade-4 stress fracture in her right lower leg and has officially withdrawn from both the Canadian Open and the Cincinnati Open. Her earliest possible return is the US Open, which begins August 30.

The news arrives at a difficult moment in Raducanu's calendar. She was a runner-up at the Queen's Club Championships just weeks ago — one of the more meaningful results of her season — and had been building toward the North American hard-court stretch with real momentum. That buildup has now been halted entirely.

The Injury

A grade-4 stress fracture is among the more serious classifications of this type of injury. Stress fractures occur when repetitive load on bone creates a crack; grade-4 indicates significant damage without a complete break, but requires genuine rest and structural recovery before the bone can safely handle competitive play.

For a tennis player, a stress fracture in the right lower leg places pressure on every element of movement — serving, footwork, lateral movement — that defines performance at the highest level. Returning too soon is not an option.

Raducanu has faced a series of injury interruptions over the past few seasons that have made consistency difficult to sustain. The timing of this diagnosis, coming off one of her stronger recent runs, makes the setback especially frustrating.

What Gets Missed

The Canadian Open and Cincinnati Open are two of the most prestigious events on the WTA summer calendar, offering significant ranking points and serving as the primary preparation for the US Open. Missing both removes Raducanu from the main draw of each tournament and eliminates the match play she would have used to build confidence on hard courts before New York.

Ranked approximately No. 37 in the world, Raducanu enters this period at a stage in her career where hard-court summer results carry considerable weight. The withdrawal will affect her ranking points picture depending on her performance — or lack thereof — in the events ahead.

What's Next

Raducanu's timeline points to the US Open as her target return. Whether she can be fit and ready to compete at the necessary physical intensity in New York depends entirely on how her recovery progresses over the next six weeks. A player who has been managing her injury load carefully in recent seasons will likely take a cautious approach to the timeline rather than rush back for a single event.

The US Open begins August 30. If Raducanu is on the court in New York, it will mean a recovery that stayed on schedule. If the fracture requires more time, the hard-court season ends entirely, and her next competitive match may not come until the autumn indoor season. For now, the earliest possible date is what her team is building toward.
