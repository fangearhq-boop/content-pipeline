### Long-Form Post Three hours and 42 minutes. Three sets. One threatened walkout. One 34-year-old veteran who refused to quit. Damir Dzumhur of Bosnia and Herzegovina defeated fourth seed Matteo Arnaldi of Italy 7-6(5), 6-7(4), 7-6(4) in one of the most dramatic quarterfinals at the Croatia Open Umag in years. Dzumhur — ranked No. 108, with a grinding 2026 season — had come close here before. In four previous trips to Umag, he had never made a semifinal. On Friday, on his fourth attempt, he finally got through. The match had everything. Dzumhur was up 5-3, 30-30 in the second set before Arnaldi clawed his way back to take it in a tiebreak. Then came the drama: mid-match, an electronic line-call dispute sent Dzumhur to the verge of walking off court in protest. He stayed. He won the third-set tiebreak. "This is why we love the sport," Dzumhur said afterward. Hard to argue. He faces Alex Molcan in the semifinal — another underdog story in a tournament defined by them. ### Image Post Caption 3 hours, 42 minutes. A threatened walkout. A third-set tiebreak.
