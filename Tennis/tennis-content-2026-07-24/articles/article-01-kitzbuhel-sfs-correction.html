Kitzbühel Semifinals Set: Bublik, Etcheverry, Hanfmann, Halys — Plus a QF Correction

By Elena Voss | July 24, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Before previewing Friday's semifinals at the Generali Open Kitzbühel, a correction to yesterday's coverage is required. Tennis Fanrecap incorrectly reported that Mariano Navone defeated Quentin Halys 6-0, 6-3 in Thursday's quarterfinals. The actual result was the reverse: Halys defeated Navone 7-5, 6-3. Additionally, the other quarterfinal scores require correction: Bublik defeated Molcan 6-3, 6-2 (not 6-3, 7-5); Etcheverry defeated Buse 6-2, 7-5 (not 7-6 in the fourth); Hanfmann defeated Baez 6-3, 6-1. We apologize for the errors.

The consequence of the Halys-Navone correction is that Friday's second semifinal is Hanfmann versus Halys — not Hanfmann versus Navone as we stated. The first semifinal remains Bublik versus Etcheverry.

Hanfmann's Kitzbühel Record

Yannick Hanfmann is 34 years old and has now reached his fourth Kitzbühel semifinal in the Open Era — more than any other player in this tournament's history. The German has developed a genuine affinity for the Tyrolean clay, and his passage through Thursday's quarterfinal was earned efficiently: Hanfmann beat Sebastian Baez 6-3, 6-1, closing out the Argentinian without significant drama.

His opponent Friday is Quentin Halys. The Frenchman went into the quarterfinals as the player who had beaten No. 2 seed Valentin Vacherot the day before, and he carried that form through to beat Navone 7-5, 6-3. Halys leads Hanfmann in their head-to-head record by 2-0, which gives the French player a statistical edge heading in. Whether that advantage translates on the Kitzbühel clay is another matter.

Bublik Chasing Back-to-Back

The headlining storyline for Saturday's final is how many players have managed to win back-to-back Kitzbühel titles. Defending champion Alexander Bublik of Kazakhstan would like to find out. The last successful defense of this title came in 2013, making it a rare achievement in the tournament's modern era.

Bublik dispatched Alex Molcan 6-3, 6-2 in a controlled quarterfinal performance. He now faces fourth seed Tomas Martin Etcheverry of Argentina in the semifinal. Bublik leads the head-to-head 2-0, which provides a measure of comfort heading into the match, though both players are capable of varying levels of output on clay.

Etcheverry reached the semifinal by beating No. 5 seed Ignacio Buse of Peru 6-2, 7-5. The Argentine is a consistent clay-court performer who has earned his seeding this week.

What's Next

The two semifinals are scheduled for Friday at the Generali Open Kitzbühel. Bublik faces Etcheverry in the first match; Hanfmann faces Halys in the second. The final is set for Saturday. Tennis Fanrecap will have results coverage as they are confirmed.
