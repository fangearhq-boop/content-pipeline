Estoril QF Day: Rublev's Dominant Week Sets Up Four-Match Quarterfinal Slate

By Marcus Cole | July 24, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The Millennium Estoril Open quarterfinals arrive on Friday with Andrey Rublev having already staked a claim as the clear tournament favourite — and having done so in a way that has the tennis world paying attention.

Rublev, the No. 14-ranked Russian who won the Bastad title last Sunday, followed that victory by landing in Lisbon and delivering what the ATP Tour itself described as his most dominant win of 2026. On Thursday, the top seed dismantled lucky loser Timofey Skatov 6-1, 6-1 in just 59 minutes. There is very little margin for the opposition when a player arrives at an event already in form and then plays at that level.

A Quarterfinal Bracket With Multiple Stories

Friday's quarterfinal schedule at the Estadio Millennium is spread across the day and includes several compelling subplots beyond Rublev.

Rublev's opponent is Luca Van Assche of France, who beat No. 8 seed Roberto Carreno Busta 6-3, 4-6, 6-2 in a three-set second-round match on Thursday. Van Assche has earned his place in the draw.

The crowd story in Lisbon belongs to Jaime Faria. The Portuguese wildcard beat compatriot Bueno 6-2, 6-4 in the second round and will face Luciano Darderi of Italy in a later quarterfinal. Darderi — who was Rublev's opponent in the Bastad final — continued his strong form in Lisbon, demolishing Federico Martinez 6-0, 6-1 on Thursday. The Darderi-Faria match will have full Portuguese backing for the home wildcard and should be the most atmospheric match of the day.

The fourth quarterfinal pairs Roman Andres Burruchaga of Argentina — who beat Nuno Borges in the second round and is continuing the run that began with his elimination of Stan Wawrinka in this tournament — against No. 4 seed Alexander Blockx of Belgium. Blockx had a tighter path than expected, needing three sets to beat qualifier Jacquet. And completing the bracket is a wildcard upset story: Pablo Llamas Rufo Torres knocked out No. 3 seed Tomas Martin Tabilo 6-4, 6-4 and will face Hugo Gaston.

Rublev's Hot Stretch

The larger narrative framing Rublev's week in Estoril is back-to-back success at different events. Winning Bastad on clay, then arriving in Portugal and immediately delivering a 59-minute clinic, suggests a player who has found something consistent in his game. Rublev has had stretches like this before in his career, and when they click, he can be difficult to stop on clay.

The semifinals at Estoril are scheduled for Saturday. If Rublev navigates Van Assche, he would face one of the other winners from the opposite half of the bracket.
