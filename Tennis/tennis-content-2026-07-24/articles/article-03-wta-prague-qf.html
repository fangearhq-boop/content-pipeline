WTA Prague QF Day: Krejcikova Extends Streak, Bouzkova Defends to Semis

By Ryan Calloway | July 24, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The Livesport Prague Open has settled into its quarterfinal stage with Czech tennis in the foreground at nearly every turn. Defending champion Marie Bouzkova has already secured her place in the semifinals, and Barbora Krejcikova is playing her quarterfinal Friday with a seven-match winning streak from Athens in tow.

Krejcikova beat local qualifier Lucie Havlickova 6-4, 6-2 in Thursday's second round. Havlickova had been on a run of her own — she upset No. 34 Alycia Parks in the first round — but Krejcikova's level was too high. The Czech moved through efficiently and now faces Marta Salkova in the quarterfinal. Salkova reached the round of eight by beating Dodin 6-1, 6-1 in R2.

Bouzkova Defending Successfully

Marie Bouzkova's job as defending champion is to be the one to beat, and so far she is doing exactly that. Bouzkova defeated fifth seed Tereza Valentova 7-5, 6-4 in a competitive quarterfinal that featured two Czech players who know each other's games well. The first set was tighter than the scoreline suggests, but Bouzkova found her level and closed it out in two. She will be a factor in the semifinals and potentially the final.

The Czech home dominance at this event is striking: multiple Czech players advanced through the first and second rounds, and the quarterfinal slate has been a showcase for domestic talent. Prague fans have had little to complain about this week.

Krejcikova's Athens Momentum

The context around Krejcikova entering this event is significant. She won the Athens Open last weekend — her ninth career WTA title and her first since Wimbledon 2024. She then immediately returned home to compete in Prague, a tournament where the home crowd's energy adds a layer to every match she plays.

Seven straight wins since Athens. Playing at home in Prague. Against an opponent in Salkova who is in form herself but has not faced anyone on Krejcikova's current trajectory this week. The quarterfinal setup does not obviously favour an upset, but tennis finds ways to surprise.

If Krejcikova wins and advances to the semis, the prospect of a Bouzkova vs. Krejcikova final — defending champion versus the player who just won Athens — would be a remarkable way to close out this event.

What's Next

Krejcikova faces Salkova in Friday's quarterfinal at TK Sparta Prague. Bouzkova awaits in the semifinals. The Livesport Prague Open final is scheduled for Sunday July 26. Tennis Fanrecap will follow results through the weekend.
