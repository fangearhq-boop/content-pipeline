Canadian Open Keeps Losing Players: Andreescu Out, Mboko Gone for the Season

By Marcus Cole | July 24, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The National Bank Open has not yet released its draws — that is scheduled for July 31 — and yet the withdrawal list is now approaching double digits in meaningful absences across both the men's and women's fields.

Two more names were confirmed Friday. Bianca Andreescu, the Canadian who would have had home crowd support in Toronto, is out. "It definitely sucks," she said of the withdrawal. And Victoria Mboko — the defending Toronto women's champion — has not simply pulled out of the Canadian Open. She is confirmed out for the remainder of the 2026 season due to the knee injury she suffered at Queen's Club.

The Full Scope of the Absences

The Canadian Open's withdrawal crisis is worth mapping out at this point. On the men's side in Montreal (beginning August 4), the field is missing: world No. 1 Jannik Sinner, who picked up a right elbow issue at Wimbledon and is targeting Cincinnati and his US Open title defense; Novak Djokovic, who withdrew with a groin injury from Wimbledon and will go straight to Flushing Meadows without a North American warm-up; Carlos Alcaraz, who has been sidelined with a wrist injury since April 14 and is targeting Cincinnati as his return; and Jack Draper, who is managing an arm injury.

On the women's side in Toronto (beginning August 1), the absences are: Karolina Muchova, who underwent a minor surgical procedure following her run to the Wimbledon final; Victoria Mboko, the defending champion, now out for the year; Jasmine Paolini, who is dealing with a foot injury and also withdrew from Washington; Emma Raducanu, who has a stress fracture in her leg; Dayana Yastremska, who has been replaced in the draw by Renata Zarazua; and Bianca Andreescu, confirmed Friday.

What It Means for the Field

The scale of the withdrawals creates an unusual set of circumstances: a Masters 1000 event with no reliable world No. 1 or No. 3 in the men's draw (pending replacements from the ranking list), and a women's field missing its defending champion plus four of the more recognisable names in the current game.

What tends to happen in these situations is that a player lower in the rankings who has been building form during the clay swing — or someone who has been managing an injury and is now healthy — finds an opening that might not exist in a full-strength draw. This is how breakthrough results happen at majors and Masters events.

Draws are expected around July 31. The women's tournament starts August 1 in Toronto; the men's starts August 4 in Montreal.

What's Next

The Canadian Open draws are expected July 31, giving players and fans their first look at the actual bracket. With Cincinnati (Aug. 8-23) coming shortly after — and both Sinner and Alcaraz targeting that event — the August hard-court swing is going to have a staggered field across its opening events.
