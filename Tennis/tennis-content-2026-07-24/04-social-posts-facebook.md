Today he faces Luca Van Assche in the quarterfinals at the Millennium Estoril Open. And Roman Andres Burruchaga — the Argentine who beat Wawrinka in his final clay match at this tournament — takes on fourth seed Alexander Blockx.

Four quarterfinals. Saturday semifinals. Can Rublev carry his Bastad form all the way to a second straight title?

### STORY 3: WTA Prague QF Day

**Long-Form Post (12:30 PM ET)**

Barbora Krejcikova's run through Prague is starting to feel like something special.

She claimed the Athens title last weekend — her ninth career WTA crown. She then returned home to the Livesport Prague Open, where she beat local qualifier Lucie Havlickova 6-4, 6-2 on Thursday to move into the quarterfinals. That's seven matches in a row, and today she faces Marta Salkova (who beat Dodin 6-1, 6-1 in R2) in a QF that keeps the all-Czech theme going.

Meanwhile, defending champion Marie Bouzkova has already punched her ticket to the semifinals. Bouzkova defeated fifth seed Tereza Valentova 7-5, 6-4 in today's early QF — and as the defending champion in this event, she'll be the one to beat in the semis.

If Krejcikova wins today, we could be looking at a defending champion vs. the hottest player in tennis right now meeting in the Prague final. Czech tennis fans this week are being treated to something rare.

### STORY 4: WTA Hamburg QF Results

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM ET)**

Five days ago, Mayar Sherif won the Iasi title when Paula Badosa retired from the final, trailing 6-4, 4-0. Today they met on the clay in Hamburg — and Sherif did it again.

The Egyptian beat Badosa 6-2, 6-3 in the Hamburg quarterfinal, ending the Spanish player's run and booking her spot in Saturday's semifinal. This time there was no retirement, just a straight defeat. Sherif has now beaten Badosa twice in five days on clay.

Elsewhere in the quarterfinals: No. 1 seed Oleksandra Oliynykova survived a scare from Elina Avanesyan (who had beaten No. 7 seed Putintseva in R2) to win 6-3, 2-6, 6-2. Anna Bondar beat Alina Charaeva 3-6, 7-5, 6-1. And Anhelina Kalinina beat local German hope Tamara Korpatsch 6-1, 6-3.

Saturday's semifinals: Sherif vs. Kalinina, and Bondar vs. Oliynykova.

### STORY 5: Canadian Open WDs

**Long-Form Post (6:00 PM ET)**

The Canadian Open keeps losing players before it even starts.

Two more significant names joined the withdrawal list today. Bianca Andreescu, who would have been playing in front of home fans in Toronto, confirmed she is out — "it definitely sucks," she said. And Victoria Mboko, the defending Toronto women's champion, has not just withdrawn from the Canadian Open — she is confirmed out for the remainder of the 2026 season entirely, due to the knee injury she sustained at Queen's Club.

Add those to the list already compiled: Karolina Muchova (minor surgery post-Wimbledon), Jasmine Paolini (foot), Emma Raducanu (stress fracture), Dayana Yastremska — on the women's side; and Sinner, Djokovic, Alcaraz, and Draper on the men's side.

That's roughly ten significant players missing from a tournament that doesn't even release its draw until July 31. The August 1 women's event in Toronto and the August 4 men's event in Montreal are going to look very different from what anyone expected.

With so many big names out, which players do you think are set up for a surprise run at the Canadian Open?
