### STORY 1: Queen's Club SFs Complete

**Long-Form Post 1** | Posting Window: 8:00 AM ET

Ugo Humbert just ended Tommy Paul's remarkable run at Queen's Club — and set up a final nobody quite predicted.

Sunday's final is Cerundolo vs Humbert. Neither is a top seed. Both are playing some of the best tennis of their careers on the surface they're supposed to struggle on. This is exactly what makes grass court tennis so brilliant.

### STORY 2: Eala Berlin Breakthrough

**Long-Form Post 2** | Posting Window: 10:30 AM ET

Alexandra Eala is one of the most exciting players in women's tennis right now — and the 2026 grass season is proving exactly why.

The 21-year-old from the Philippines started the grass swing by winning the WTA 125 title in Birmingham. Then she came to Berlin for the WTA 500 and dismantled the field. She beat Donna Vekic — last week's Queen's Club champion — in the first round. Then she knocked out world No. 2 Elena Rybakina 7-5, 6-4. Then she defeated Elina Svitolina 6-3, 6-4 in the quarterfinals.

That's a 9-1 record on grass in 2026. Three consecutive top-10 wins at one tournament. Her first WTA 500 semifinal. And today she faces Linda Noskova for a spot in the final.

What's remarkable about Eala is the speed at which she's grown. A year ago, results like these against Rybakina would have been unthinkable. Now they feel inevitable. Grass clearly suits her style, and she has the belief to match.

We're watching something special unfold. Are you following her Berlin run?

### STORY 3: Halle SF Saturday

**Long-Form Post 3** | Posting Window: 12:30 PM ET

The Terra Wortmann Open in Halle is delivering. Friday's quarterfinals gave us four stories in one evening — and Saturday's semifinals promise more.

Start with Daniel Altmaier. He came to Halle as a wildcard, ranked 81st in the world, and with zero main draw wins at this tournament in his career. He beat Basilashvili. He beat Hurkacz. Then he beat Daniil Medvedev 6-4, 6-7(6), 6-4 to reach the first ATP 500 semifinal of his career. Grass may not traditionally be his surface — he carries a losing record on it — but this week, none of that mattered.

Then there's Frances Tiafoe. Five match points saved against Felix Auger-Aliassime. 3-6, 6-3, 7-6(14-12). A tiebreak that lasted 26 points. Tennis at its most dramatic.

Taylor Fritz got his revenge on Ben Shelton — the man who beat him in Stuttgart last week — in three tight sets. And Alexander Zverev, who has won more matches than anyone else on tour this season, glided through.

Today: Zverev vs Fritz, Tiafoe vs Altmaier. The fairytale vs the favourite. 🎾

What are you calling — Zverev or Fritz? Tiafoe or the wildcard?

### STORY 5: Wimbledon 2026 Countdown

**Long-Form Post 5** | Posting Window: 6:00 PM ET

Nine days. That's all that stands between us and Wimbledon 2026.

Qualifying begins Monday at Roehampton — 128 players competing for 32 spots in the main draw. Tickets are just £20 per day if you want to catch the action in person. Matches start at 11 AM each morning.

The draw ceremony follows on June 26, and then on June 29, the greatest grass-court event in the world gets underway.

The defending champion is Jannik Sinner, who has skipped every grass warm-up tournament this year except the Hurlingham Club exhibition (June 23-27). His preparation is unconventional, but his tennis has been commanding all season.

Carlos Alcaraz is out — the wrist injury he suffered before Roland Garros hasn't healed in time. That removes one of the sport's most dynamic players from a draw that is suddenly very open on the men's side.

This Sunday's finals across Halle, Queen's Club, Berlin, and Nottingham will settle some of the grass season's outstanding questions. By Monday morning, when qualifying starts, we'll have a much clearer picture of who arrives at SW19 in form.
