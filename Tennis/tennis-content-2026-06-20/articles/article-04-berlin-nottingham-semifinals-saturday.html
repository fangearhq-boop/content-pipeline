Berlin and Nottingham SFs: Finals Picture Clears as Sabalenka, Eala, and Pliskova Take Centre Stage

By Marcus Cole | June 20, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Saturday brings four WTA semifinals across Berlin and Nottingham, with finals set for Sunday at both venues and the Wimbledon form guide nearly written.

Berlin WTA 500: Sabalenka Meets Pegula

The marquee semifinal in Berlin is Aryna Sabalenka versus Jessica Pegula — a meeting that has been building all week and arrives with added intrigue given that this is their first encounter of 2026.

Sabalenka's route to the last four was anything but smooth. In Friday's quarterfinals, she trailed Nikola Bartunkova 2-6 and 4-0 in the second set before launching a comeback that produced a final scoreline of 2-6, 7-6, 6-4. The world No. 1's ability to reset and find the level she needs when pressed is one of her defining qualities, and she will need it again on Saturday against an opponent with significant big-match experience.

Pegula controlled her own quarterfinal with more authority. She defeated Madison Keys 7-6(5), 7-6(8) in two close tiebreaks — a result that required composure at key moments but also confirmed her grass-court form is in good shape ahead of Wimbledon.

In the other Berlin semifinal, Linda Noskova — who overpowered Paula Badosa 6-1, 6-3 in the quarterfinals — faces Alexandra Eala, who has been one of the week's most compelling stories with back-to-back wins over Rybakina and Svitolina.

Nottingham: All-Czech Showdown and Navarro's Charge

The Lexus Nottingham Open is producing its own narratives. The first semifinal is an all-Czech contest between Karolina Pliskova and Marie Bouzkova — the first time two Czech players have met in a Nottingham semifinal. Pliskova arrives having not dropped a set through the week; Bouzkova has also been dominant through three straight-set victories, though she has never previously advanced past the second round at this tournament.

The second semifinal brings Emma Navarro against Viktorija Golubic. Navarro, the third seed, is in her first WTA semifinal on British grass, having beaten Spain's Jessica Bouzas Maneiro 7-6(6), 6-2 in the quarterfinals. Golubic, meanwhile, is into her first WTA semifinal since October 2025 and is guaranteed a ranking climb back into the top 70 regardless of today's result.

Both Nottingham semifinalists will meet in Sunday's final, the conclusion of an event that has run from June 13 to June 21 on the Nottingham grass.

Sunday's Full Finals Slate

By Sunday evening, four grass-court titles will have been awarded. Halle and Queen's Club complete their men's draws with finals scheduled for the afternoon. Berlin and Nottingham settle their women's champions. It is one of the busiest Sundays of the grass-court season, and each result will feed directly into the conversation around Wimbledon seedings and form.

Qualifying for the Championships begins Monday morning at Roehampton, meaning any player competing in a final on Sunday will have a rapid turnaround before Wimbledon's gates open. For finalists who are also Wimbledon contenders, Sunday will demand everything — knowing that the week ahead requires even more.

What's Next

Berlin WTA 500 and Lexus Nottingham Open finals both take place on Sunday, June 21. In Berlin: the winner of Sabalenka vs Pegula meets the winner of Noskova vs Eala. In Nottingham: the winner of Pliskova vs Bouzkova meets the winner of Navarro vs Golubic. Wimbledon qualifying begins Monday, June 22, at Roehampton.
