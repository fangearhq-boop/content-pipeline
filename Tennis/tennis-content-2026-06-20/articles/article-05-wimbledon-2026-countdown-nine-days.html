Wimbledon 2026: Nine Days Out, Qualifying Starts Monday

By Ryan Calloway | June 20, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

With qualifying beginning at Roehampton on Monday June 22 and the draw ceremony set for June 26, The Championships are now closer than the grass season's opening weekend feels.

The Path to the Main Draw

Wimbledon qualifying runs from Monday June 22 through Thursday June 25 at the Bank of England Sports Ground in Roehampton, southwest London. The competition offers 32 main draw places — 16 in the men's singles and 16 in the women's — to players who survive three rounds of matches on outdoor grass courts.

Tickets for the qualifying event are available for £20 per day. Gates open at 10 AM with matches beginning from 11 AM each morning. It is one of the sport's best-value spectating opportunities: the tennis is genuine, the grass is pristine, and the players competing have everything on the line.

The draw ceremony for the main event follows on Friday June 26. The Championships begin on Monday June 29.

The Defending Champions

Jannik Sinner arrives at Wimbledon as the defending men's singles champion. The Italian's preparation for 2026 has been methodical and deliberate: he skipped the ATP 500 events at Halle and Queen's Club, choosing instead to participate only in the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at the Hurlingham Club, an exhibition event running June 23-27, as his sole grass-court warm-up.

It is an unconventional approach. But Sinner's tennis has been reliable across all surfaces this season, and his confidence as the defending champion in conditions he has already mastered is a significant factor in any pre-tournament assessment.

Carlos Alcaraz, the world No. 2, will not be at Wimbledon. The Spaniard confirmed his withdrawal due to a wrist injury that also kept him from Roland Garros. His absence removes one of the sport's most explosive grass-court performers and opens the draw in ways that are difficult to fully anticipate.

What Sunday's Finals Tell Us

Saturday and Sunday represent the final competitive action of the pre-Wimbledon grass swing. By Sunday evening, four titles will have been decided: Halle, Queen's Club, Berlin, and Nottingham. The players who win, and how they win, will shape perceptions heading into the draw.

A Zverev title at Halle would confirm him as a significant men's contender on a surface he has historically found difficult. A Sabalenka win in Berlin would add another grass-court title to her record as she builds toward a first Wimbledon women's crown. And Alexandra Eala's remarkable week in Berlin has already raised her profile considerably on the surface, regardless of Saturday's semifinal result.

The prize money at Wimbledon 2026 stands at £64.2 million total, with £3.6 million going to each singles champion. It is the largest prize fund in tournament history, a 20 percent increase from 2025.

What's Next

Wimbledon qualifying begins Monday, June 22 at Roehampton. The draw ceremony takes place Friday, June 26. The 2026 Championships open on Monday, June 29. Sunday's grass-court finals across Halle, Queen's Club, Berlin, and Nottingham will complete the pre-Wimbledon picture and provide the last major form guide before seedings are set.
