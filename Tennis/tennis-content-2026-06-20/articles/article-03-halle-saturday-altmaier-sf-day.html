Halle SF Saturday: Altmaier's Wildcard Fairytale Meets Zverev-Fritz Showdown

By Elena Voss | June 20, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

German wildcard Daniel Altmaier completed one of the week's great upsets to reach his first ATP 500 semifinal, setting up a Saturday card at the Terra Wortmann Open that pairs him with Tiafoe while Fritz and Zverev meet in the marquee match.

Altmaier's Journey from Wildcard to History

Daniel Altmaier came to Halle with a record he would rather not think about: zero match wins at this tournament in his career. As a wildcard ranked 81st in the world, few expected that to change.

He beat Basilashvili in the first round. Then he beat Hurkacz, a former Wimbledon finalist, in the second. Then, in Friday's quarterfinals, he defeated Daniil Medvedev 6-4, 6-7(6), 6-4 — a result that sent ripples through a tournament that was already delivering more than expected.

The win over Medvedev was Altmaier's best career victory by ranking. It also gave him something no previous Halle visit had: a place in the semifinals of an ATP 500, the deepest run of his career at this level. The German, born in Heinsberg, was playing in front of home support, and it showed. He fought for every point against Medvedev, saving the match in the third set with consistent baseline depth that the higher-ranked player could never quite disrupt.

Tiafoe's Match Point Marathon

Frances Tiafoe's route to Saturday's semifinal was even more dramatic. In his quarterfinal against Felix Auger-Aliassime, Tiafoe fell behind 3-6 in the first set before winning the second 6-3. The decider went to a tiebreak. Then the tiebreak reached match point — once, twice, three times, four, five — and Tiafoe saved them all before taking it 14-12.

A 3-6, 6-3, 7-6(12) victory after five match points saved is the sort of result that defines tournament runs. Tiafoe enters Saturday's semifinal against Altmaier with that energy behind him. His head-to-head against the German stands at 4-0, including a grass-court win in Stuttgart 11 days ago.

But form and head-to-head records have meant little this week in Halle. Altmaier's run suggests that Tiafoe cannot expect an easy passage.

Zverev vs Fritz: The Headliner

The top of the Saturday card features Alexander Zverev against Taylor Fritz — a match that carries its own narrative weight.

Zverev is having a remarkable season. His victory in Friday's quarterfinal over Raphael Collignon 7-6(10), 7-6(2) was his 38th win of 2026, the most of any player on the ATP Tour this year. He arrives at the semifinal as the form favourite.

Fritz, however, won his own quarterfinale with considerable drama. The American defeated Ben Shelton — the man who beat Fritz in last week's Stuttgart final — 6-7(5), 7-6(8), 7-6(3) after saving a match point. That victory carries extra significance: it is Fritz on grass, at his best, fighting until the last point.

Fritz leads their career head-to-head 6-5, and crucially, holds wins over Zverev on grass — at Wimbledon 2024 and in last year's Stuttgart tournament. On this surface, the numbers favour him slightly. Zverev's all-season brilliance suggests otherwise. Both readings may be correct.

What's Next

The Terra Wortmann Open semifinals take place Saturday, June 20, at the Heristo-Arena in Halle. Zverev faces Fritz first, with Tiafoe vs Altmaier to follow. The winner of each match advances to Sunday's final. By Sunday evening, the Halle champion will be known — and the grass season's final question before Wimbledon qualifying begins Monday will have an answer.
