Queen's Club Final Set: Humbert Ends Paul's Streak, Cerundolo Through

By Marcus Cole | June 20, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Ugo Humbert defeated defending champion Tommy Paul 7-5, 6-3 on Saturday, ending Paul's eight-match winning run at Queen's Club and setting up a Sunday final against Francisco Cerundolo.

Humbert Outclasses the Defending Champion

Tommy Paul came to Queen's Club as the man to beat. The American had won eight consecutive matches at the London grass-court venue since claiming the 2024 title, and his dominance over Ugo Humbert — three wins from three meetings, all on hard courts — suggested the streak would continue.

Humbert had other ideas. The Frenchman controlled the match from the opening game, winning 7-5, 6-3 in comfortable fashion. Paul, who had not dropped a set in the tournament heading into the semifinal, found himself unable to generate the consistent pressure that had carried him through the week. Humbert broke at key moments in both sets and served with authority throughout.

The 3-0 head-to-head record that Paul carried into Saturday's match proved a misleading guide. All three prior meetings had come on hard courts. Grass is a different surface with different demands, and Humbert's flat, penetrating groundstrokes were far better suited to it than Paul's previous opponents had suggested.

Cerundolo Survives Nakashima in Three Sets

In the upper half of the draw, Francisco Cerundolo advanced to his first Queen's Club final with a 6-4, 6-7(5), 6-2 win over Brandon Nakashima — a match that required considerably more resilience than the scoreline suggests.

Cerundolo controlled the opening set with clean ball-striking, but Nakashima — who had upset No. 1 seed Alex de Minaur 7-5, 6-3 in the quarterfinals — pushed back in the second. The American played an excellent tiebreak, winning it 7-5 to level the match and silence the crowd that had been building in favour of the Argentine.

Cerundolo's response in the third set was a statement. He produced some of his best tennis of the tournament, breaking twice and closing out 6-2 with minimal drama. For a player who has made his name on clay, reaching a final on grass — and at a venue as prestigious as Queen's Club — represents a significant step in his career development on the surface.

An Open Final on Sunday

Sunday's HSBC Championships final, scheduled at Queen's Club in London, features two players who were not among the tournament's top seeds but have both earned their place with quality performances all week.

Humbert brings excellent grass credentials and an attacking style that puts opponents under constant time pressure. Cerundolo brings variety, heavy topspin, and the confidence of having beaten an unseeded player who had already upset the field's biggest name.

Neither player has won the Queen's Club title before. By Sunday evening, one of them will have added it to their career record alongside the memories of a tournament that kept producing unexpected results.

What's Next

Francisco Cerundolo and Ugo Humbert meet in the 2026 HSBC Championships final at Queen's Club on Sunday, June 21. The match is expected to begin in the early afternoon London time. Wimbledon qualifying starts the following morning, Monday June 22, at Roehampton — giving both finalists just days to prepare for the grass season's main event, which begins June 29.
