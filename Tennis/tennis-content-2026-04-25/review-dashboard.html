Alcaraz Officially OUT of French Open — Two-Time Defending Champion Confirms Wrist Injury Withdrawal
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post BREAKING: Carlos Alcaraz will not defend his Roland Garros title. The two-time defending French Open champion confirmed Friday that a right wrist injury — sustained during his first-round match at the Barcelona Open — will sideline him from both Rome and Roland Garros 2026. In his statement, Alcaraz wrote: "After the results of the tests carried out today, we have decided that the most prudent thing is to be cautious and not participate in Rome and Roland Garros, while we wait to assess the evolution to decide when we will return to the court." Roland Garros confirmed the withdrawal, adding: "Some heartbreaking news as Carlos Alcaraz, our 2024 and 2025 champion, will not be able to defend his title at Roland-Garros 2026 due to a wrist injury." Alcaraz, the world No. 2, had already withdrawn from the Madrid Open this week. He had won back-to-back Roland Garros titles in 2024 and 2025 — the first player since Nadal to defend the crown at Stade Roland Garros. Novak Djokovic, the three-time former champion at Roland Garros, is also in serious doubt after a shoulder injury kept him out of Miami, Monte-Carlo, and Madrid. With both men sidelined or in doubt, the road to the 2026 French Open title runs directly through Jannik Sinner — who sits at 18 straight wins and 23 consecutive Masters 1000 victories. Sinner's response to the news: "It's very sad. I hope he recovers well and comes back strong." Who wins Roland Garros without Alcaraz? We want to hear your prediction. --- #### Image Caption -- 8:00 AM ET ### Image Post Caption Carlos Alcaraz, two-time French Open champion, confirmed Friday he will miss Roland Garros 2026 with a right wrist injury. Madrid's high altitude and fast clay disrupted his rhythm early — he admitted post-match: "I struggled quite a lot." ### Long-Form Post A teenage dream is unfolding at Madrid — and Jannik Sinner is watching from the stands. Rafael Jódar, 19 years old, from just 12 kilometres from the Caja Mágica, demolished world No. 8 Alex de Minaur 6-3, 6-1 on Saturday to claim his first career top-10 victory. The statistics were jaw-dropping — Jódar outscored de Minaur 23-7 on return points in the second set alone. It continues a remarkable clay season for the young Spaniard: Marrakech champion, Barcelona semifinalist, and now into the Madrid third round after beating de Jong in R1 and the No. 8 seed in R2. After the match, Jódar said: "This is a tournament I always came to watch as a fan, and now being able to play here against a great player with the crowd cheering for me is a dream come true." Spain has produced Nadal and Alcaraz. It's very early — but Madrid is falling in love with the next name on that list. WTA Madrid Day 4 — Rybakina Survives Scare, Gauff Dominates, Keys Withdraws Ill
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Friday's women's draw at Madrid delivered survival, domination, illness, and a tearful exit. Elena Rybakina, the No. 3 seed and reigning Australian Open champion, needed three sets to survive Elena-Gabriela Ruse — losing the first set before rallying for a 4-6, 6-3, 7-5 win. "Today, I basically survived," Rybakina said. Her next match: Zheng Qinwen in what shapes up as one of the best third-round matchups on the women's side. Coco Gauff needed just 52 minutes to dispatch Léolia Jeanjean 6-3, 6-0 and move efficiently into R3. The American was in a different league on Friday. Madison Keys, the reigning US Open champion, was forced out with illness. Lucky loser Anastasia Potapova stepped in and won her match. Alexandra Eala (Philippines, 20) — who had turned heads with her R1 win over Pavlyuchenkova — was ended by No. 19 seed Elise Mertens 6-2, 6-1 in under 80 minutes. It was a tough day for the Filipino star, but her Madrid debut left a lasting impression. Top seeds Sabalenka and Swiatek remain on track for a potential semifinal showdown. The women's Madrid title is still very much open. --- #### Image Caption -- 6:00 PM ET ### Image Post Caption WTA Madrid Day 4: Rybakina survives, Gauff dominates, Keys withdraws, Eala's run ends.
