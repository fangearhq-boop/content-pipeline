Jódar Stuns de Minaur 6-3, 6-1 for First Career Top-10 Win: Madrid's Next Star Arrives

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

Rafael Jódar grew up watching tennis at the Caja Mágica. He lives 12 kilometres from it. On Friday, the 19-year-old Spaniard walked onto its red clay, destroyed world No. 8 Alex de Minaur 6-3, 6-1, and earned his first career victory over a top-10 player.

Jannik Sinner — the world No. 1 and the man everyone else is chasing right now — was watching from the stands.

"This is a tournament I always came to watch as a fan," Jódar said after the match, "and now being able to play here against a great player with the crowd cheering for me is a dream come true."

A Demolition, Not a Surprise

The 6-3, 6-1 scoreline was not a fluke. Jódar outscored de Minaur 23-7 on return points in the second set alone — a dominance of returns that dismantled an Australian whose footwork and speed make him one of the toughest opponents in the tour on any surface.

The statistics reflected what the crowd saw: a young Spanish player who had absorbed every lesson from a demanding clay-court swing and arrived at his home tournament in peak condition. De Minaur, ranked No. 8 in the world, was unable to generate the consistent pressure his game demands. Jódar simply controlled the tempo and the crowd fed him energy every point.

After the match, Jódar said: "Alex is always a tough opponent. I knew I had to do things very well from the first point, and I think in the pressure moments I played very well."

A Clay Season That Refuses to Slow Down

Jódar's 2026 clay-court campaign has been one of the stories of the European spring. He arrived in Madrid with a title at the Grand Prix Hassan II in Marrakech and a semifinal run at the Barcelona Open. His R1 win at Madrid over Robin de Jong — making him the third Spaniard under 20 to win a main-draw match at Madrid after Nadal and Alcaraz — put the city on notice.

The demolition of de Minaur confirms it. Jódar's overall 2026 record stands at 14-7. He is currently ranked a career-high No. 42 in the world, and that ranking will rise significantly after this week's results.

Jódar vs. Fonseca: A Third-Round Dream Match

The reward for Jódar's victory is a third-round matchup that could be the most anticipated of the entire Madrid tournament: Rafael Jódar (Spain, 19, No. 42) against Joao Fonseca (Brazil, 19, No. 31).

Two teenagers. Two clay-court prospects tipped as the future of the sport. One court in Madrid, where the crowd will almost certainly be behind their Spanish favourite.

Fonseca — the reigning Next Gen champion and a top-30 player at 19 — represents a significant step up in opponent quality. But Jódar's trajectory this week suggests he arrives in that third round with legitimate belief, not just hope.

What's Next

Rafael Jódar faces Joao Fonseca in the third round of the 2026 Madrid Open, with the match expected to be scheduled in the coming days as the draw advances. Should Jódar win, he would become a genuine contender for a quarterfinal spot in a draw that has lost several of its top seeds.

Spain has spent years searching for the player who will carry its tennis legacy after Nadal and into whatever chapter Alcaraz's injury complications may create. The search may be narrowing. The 19-year-old from 12 kilometres away is looking very much like an answer.
