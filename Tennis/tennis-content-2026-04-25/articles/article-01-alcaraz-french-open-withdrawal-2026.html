Alcaraz Out of French Open 2026: Two-Time Champion Confirms Wrist Injury Withdrawal

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

Carlos Alcaraz will not defend his Roland Garros title. The two-time defending French Open champion confirmed on Friday that a right wrist injury — sustained during his first-round match at the Barcelona Open — will sideline him for both Rome and Roland Garros 2026.

In a statement posted to social media, Alcaraz said: "After the results of the tests carried out today, we have decided that the most prudent thing is to be cautious and not participate in Rome and Roland Garros, while we wait to assess the evolution to decide when we will return to the court."

Roland Garros confirmed the news with its own statement: "Some heartbreaking news as Carlos Alcaraz, our 2024 and 2025 champion, will not be able to defend his title at Roland-Garros 2026 due to a wrist injury."

A Cascade of Injuries at the Top

Alcaraz is the second player from the top four to withdraw from Roland Garros 2026 before the clay season has even reached its midpoint. World No. 4 Novak Djokovic — a three-time champion at Stade Roland Garros — has also cast serious doubt on his own participation after withdrawing from Miami, Monte-Carlo, and Madrid with a shoulder injury. Djokovic has stated he hopes to be ready "at least for Roland Garros," but no return timeline has been confirmed.

Alcaraz, the world No. 2, had already withdrawn from the Madrid Open this week. He had initially appeared to take some time off after his Barcelona withdrawal, but further scans revealed the wrist injury to be more serious than initially expected. He will also skip the Rome Masters, leaving him with no clay-court warm-up before his absence from the French Open.

Alcaraz's Roland Garros Legacy — Paused

Alcaraz won the 2024 French Open in four sets over Alexander Zverev, then successfully defended the title in 2025, becoming the first player to win back-to-back Roland Garros titles since Rafael Nadal's dominant run on Parisian clay. He was chasing a third consecutive French Open crown — a feat unprecedented in the Open Era outside of Nadal.

The 2026 edition of Roland Garros, scheduled to begin on May 25, will take place without its two-time defending champion for the first time in three years.

Sinner Steps Into the Void

The departure of Alcaraz makes world No. 1 Jannik Sinner the overwhelming favourite for the 2026 French Open title. Sinner, currently competing at the Madrid Open, responded to the news in his post-match press conference on Friday: "It's very sad. I hope he recovers well and comes back strong."

Sinner enters Roland Garros in historically strong form. He is currently riding an 18-match winning streak overall, with 23 consecutive wins at Masters 1000-level events — a run spanning his titles at the Rolex Paris Masters, Indian Wells, Miami Open, and Monte-Carlo Masters. He is now seeking to become the first player in the Open Era to claim five consecutive Masters 1000 titles, with Madrid the next step in that pursuit.

If the draw goes as expected, Sinner will enter Roland Garros as not just the No. 1 seed but arguably the most dominant clay-court player in the world at this moment in time.

What's Next

Alcaraz is expected to rest and rehabilitate through the clay-court swing and assess his condition before setting a return date. There is no confirmed timeline for his comeback, and the Wimbledon grass-court season — which begins in late June — may be his next realistic target depending on how the wrist responds to treatment.

For Roland Garros, the absence of Alcaraz and the uncertainty around Djokovic reshapes the entire draw. Alexander Zverev, a two-time finalist at the French Open, is now likely to be the second seed. Rune, Medvedev, Tsitsipas, and the resurgent Musetti all enter with realistic title chances in a French Open that could produce a first-time champion at the top of the game.

The clay-court season continues at the Madrid Open through May 3, followed by the Rome Masters (May 5–18) before Roland Garros begins on May 25.
