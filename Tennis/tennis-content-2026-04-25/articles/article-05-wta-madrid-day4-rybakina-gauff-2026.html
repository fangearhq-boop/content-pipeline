WTA Madrid Day 4: Rybakina Survives Scare, Gauff Dominates, Keys Withdraws Ill

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

The women's draw at the Madrid Open took shape on Friday in contrasting fashion — one top seed grinding through adversity, another brushing past her opponent with barely a contest, and a reigning Grand Slam champion sitting out with illness.

Rybakina Survives — Barely

Elena Rybakina, the No. 3 seed and reigning Australian Open champion, needed all three sets to get past Elena-Gabriela Ruse, dropping the opening set before rallying for a 4-6, 6-3, 7-5 win. The Kazakh player was candid about the difficulty: "Today, I basically survived," she said after the match. "My serve was not really helping me today... but I'm really happy that I managed to win."

The victory sets up a third-round meeting with Zheng Qinwen, the Chinese player and Olympic gold medalist, who also advanced on Friday. The Rybakina vs. Zheng match will be one of the marquee fixtures of the Madrid third round — two heavy hitters, both in form, meeting on clay.

Gauff in a Different Gear

If Rybakina was grinding, Coco Gauff was simply dominant. The American No. 5 seed dispatched Léolia Jeanjean 6-3, 6-0 without drama, extending her clay-court form and moving efficiently into the third round. Gauff, the 2023 US Open champion, has reached a level of baseline consistency that makes her one of the most complete players in the draw.

Her route to the quarterfinals will now be the primary storyline in the bottom half of the WTA Madrid draw.

Keys Withdraws, Eala's Run Ends

Madison Keys, the reigning US Open champion, was forced to withdraw from the tournament with illness before her second-round match. Lucky loser Anastasia Potapova stepped in and defeated Zhang Shuai 6-3, 6-1 to advance in her place.

It was a difficult Friday for players who had made strong first-round impressions. Alexandra Eala of the Philippines — who had generated considerable attention with her R1 win over Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova — fell to No. 19 seed Elise Mertens 6-2, 6-1 in just over 76 minutes. Mertens' consistent ball-striking proved too much for the 20-year-old Eala, whose impressive Madrid debut ends in the second round but leaves her with momentum heading into the rest of the clay season.

No. 10 seed Victoria Mboko also exited on Friday, continuing a trend of seeded players falling early in the bottom half of the WTA draw.

What's Next

The women's third round at Madrid begins in the coming days with several compelling matchups now confirmed. Rybakina faces Zheng, Gauff advances toward a potential quarterfinal clash with the top seeds, and the draw continues to thin out as the Madrid tournament moves toward its second week.

Top seeds Aryna Sabalenka and Iga Swiatek — who both advanced with commanding wins in R2 — remain on course for a potential semifinal meeting that would be the marquee women's match of the tournament. The clay-court season's biggest event is building toward its defining days.
