Prizmic Tops No. 4 Seed Shelton in Three-Hour Madrid Thriller: Another Big Upset in 2026 Draw

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

Dino Prizmic, 21, a Croatian qualifier, toppled No. 4 seed Ben Shelton 6-4, 6-7(4), 7-6(5) in a three-hour marathon at the Mutua Madrid Open on Friday, claiming his first career victory over a top-10 player and continuing a pattern of major upsets in the 2026 Madrid draw.

The winning margin was razor-thin: a single break of serve at 5-4 in the opening set proved to be the only service break in the entire match. The next two sets went to tiebreaks, with Shelton winning the second and Prizmic sealing the third to advance.

A Qualifier Who Earns Every Point

Prizmic came through qualifying to reach the main draw, defeating opponents to earn his place. His R1 win over Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard (wildcard Berrettini was actually his R1 opponent — Berrettini lost to Prizmic 6-3, 6-4 in the April 23 coverage) set the stage. Now, after three hours of quality tennis against one of the most powerful servers in the game, he moves into the third round of a Masters 1000 event as a qualifier — something achieved by only a handful of players each year on the ATP Tour.

Prizmic's game held up through the critical pressure moments. Shelton — whose first serve at full speed is among the hardest to handle on tour — was neutralized through solid positioning and smart court geometry rather than pace.

Shelton's Madrid Exit After Munich Title

Ben Shelton arrives at each clay-court swing carrying American expectations. His Munich title this spring — making him one of the most successful Americans on clay in decades — gave him a No. 4 seeding and legitimate title hopes in Madrid. A three-hour first-week exit to a qualifier is a sharp reversal.

Madrid's high altitude and fast clay are genuinely different from other clay events. The ball comes through the court more quickly, rewards aggressive play, and creates unexpected bounces. Shelton had adapted well through his early rounds, but Prizmic found ways to absorb his power and redirect effectively in the crucial moments.

Madrid 2026: The Upset Capital

Shelton joins a growing list of players who have departed the 2026 Madrid draw earlier than expected. Grigor Dimitrov lost to Vallejo 6-4, 6-4 in R2, extending a run of poor form. Gael Monfils fell to Ugo Carabelli. Berrettini was eliminated in R1.

In a draw that already lacks Alcaraz (wrist injury) and Djokovic (shoulder), the seeds are falling in waves. What emerges from the chaos could reshape the Madrid title conversation entirely.

What's Next

Dino Prizmic advances to the third round of the Madrid Open, where he will face another opponent from a draw that has been stripped of its expected upper-half seeds. His journey through qualifying and two main-draw wins over seeded players makes him one of the week's notable stories at the Caja Mágica.

Shelton, meanwhile, returns to the drawing board before Rome and the French Open. His clay-court season still has time to recover — Munich remains a significant achievement — but Madrid's exit was a reminder that on this particular surface, qualifying-round entrants carry fewer credentials only on paper.
