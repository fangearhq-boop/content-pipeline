Sinner Survives Bonzi Scare to Win 18th Straight: Madrid Masters 1000 History in Reach

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 25, 2026

Jannik Sinner needed all three sets and a considerable mental reset to get past Benjamin Bonzi on Friday at the Mutua Madrid Open, but the result remained the same: a win, a streak extended, and history one step closer.

The world No. 1 dropped the opening set in a tiebreak — only the second time he has lost a set in this Masters 1000 run — before taking complete control with a 6-7(6), 6-1, 6-4 victory. The Italian is now 18-0 in his overall win streak and 23-0 in consecutive Masters 1000 matches, a run that began with his title at the Rolex Paris Masters in October 2025.

Madrid's Fast Clay Disrupted Sinner's Rhythm Early

Sinner was candid after the match: "I struggled quite a lot" in the opening set. Madrid's high altitude — the Caja Mágica sits at around 650 metres above sea level — produces a faster, more unpredictable bounce than standard European red clay. Bonzi, ranked No. 104, fought through the conditions better than his opponent in those opening games.

The contrast was stark: Bonzi did not earn a single break point on Sinner's serve across the entire match. But Sinner, despite looking unsettled, created 13 break opportunities himself, converting three — one in the second set, two in the third. That asymmetry tells the true story of where these two players stand.

Bonzi, who had qualified through the Madrid qualifying rounds — defeating Droguet 6-7(4), 7-6(4), 6-4 to earn his main-draw place — played about as well as a No. 104 player can on this surface. Sinner simply found another gear when it mattered.

The Record in Focus: Five Consecutive Masters 1000 Titles

No player in the history of the Masters 1000 series — which began in 1990 — has ever won five consecutive titles. Sinner is chasing that mark one match at a time.

His run: Paris Masters (October 2025), Indian Wells (March 2026), Miami Open (March 2026), Monte-Carlo Masters (April 2026). Each title was built differently — the Masters calendar tests clay, hard courts, and indoor conditions. Sinner has won all four with a combined record of 23-0.

To reach five, he must win Madrid. To get there, he must navigate a draw that has already seen major seeds fall — but one that has also lost both Carlos Alcaraz (wrist, confirmed Roland Garros withdrawal) and Novak Djokovic (shoulder, withdrew before Madrid began). The path, while still demanding, is more open than expected heading into this week.

Møller Next: A Qualifier in the Third Round

Sinner's third-round opponent is Danish qualifier Elmer Møller, who has navigated the qualifying bracket to reach this stage. Sinner, entering the match on an 18-match overall win streak and dominant form in Masters 1000 events, will be a heavy favourite to advance.

The second week of the Madrid Open is where the true test begins, with quarterfinals and semifinals against the remaining top-10 players in the draw. Sinner has shown throughout this streak that he finds another level when opponents of consequence arrive.

What's Next

Sinner faces qualifier Elmer Møller in the third round at the Caja Mágica. If he wins, the quarterfinal on approximately April 29 will be the first real challenge in the Madrid draw. Elsewhere, the Alcaraz news dominated Friday's tennis conversation — and Sinner, asked about his rival's Roland Garros withdrawal, kept it characteristically measured: "It's very sad. I hope he recovers well and comes back strong."

For now, the pursuit continues. Eighteen wins. Twenty-three at Masters 1000. History one title away.
