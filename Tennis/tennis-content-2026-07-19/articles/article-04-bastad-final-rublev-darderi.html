Two Former Bastad Champions Meet in Sunday's Nordea Open Final

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 19, 2026 — Bastad, Sweden

The Nordea Open Bastad final brings together two players who have already won this tournament. No. 1 seed Andrey Rublev, the 2023 Bastad champion, faces No. 2 seed Luciano Darderi, the 2025 defending champion, in a Sunday title match that pits two former winners against each other for the right to call themselves Bastad's best across different eras.

Neither player arrived here cleanly. Rublev needed three sets to eliminate Alejandro Tabilo in the semifinal, winning 6-4, 4-6, 6-4 in a match that tested his ability to absorb a dropped second set and refocus. Darderi's path was even more demanding: he lost the second set of his semifinal against Adolfo Daniel Vallejo in a tiebreak that extended to 11 games, before regrouping to take the deciding set 6-3 and reach his own title match.

The semifinals wrote both players into Sunday's final through effort rather than ease. That quality of competition heading in makes the matchup more meaningful.

A Note on Yesterday's Preview

The July 18 pipeline previewed this final as Rublev vs. Vallejo, expecting Darderi to fall in the semifinal. He did not. Darderi's second-set tiebreak survival — won after a second set that ran to 13 games — was the key moment of Saturday's clay-court schedule in Bastad. He earned the right to play Sunday.

Vallejo's week was still a significant achievement. The Paraguayan player, ranked outside the top 100, had already made history by reaching the semifinal — the first Paraguayan player to reach an ATP semifinal in decades. His run ended Saturday but not before he forced Darderi to the edge of the draw in a match that went 2 hours and 57 minutes.

What Each Player Brings

Rublev won the Nordea Open in 2023, his 15th ATP Tour title at the time, on a clay court in conditions that suited his baseline game. He has been one of the more consistent clay-court performers on the ATP Tour for several years running and arrived at this event as the top seed for a reason.

Darderi won the title in 2025 as the defending champion, giving him a different kind of familiarity with the Bastad conditions. He is Italian, 24 years old, and seeking his sixth career ATP title. His victory in last year's final is the most recent result at this event, which means the clay, the rhythm of the draws, and the specific conditions of this tournament are fresher in his reference point than they are for Rublev.

Between them, they have won this tournament twice in recent years. Sunday adds another title to one of their records.

What's Next

The Nordea Open concludes Sunday's post-Wimbledon clay swing, which also sees finals in Gstaad (ATP), Athens (WTA), and Iasi (WTA) on the same weekend. When the clay season ends Sunday evening, the entire tour pivots to North American hardcourts. The National Bank Open begins in Montreal and Toronto on August 1 with 71 of the top 72 ATP players confirmed.

Whoever wins Bastad today carries clay momentum directly into a hardcourt Masters 1000 draw. The switch in surface is abrupt and the field in Montreal is historically deep. Today's result matters as a title; what follows is where the real test of the summer begins.
