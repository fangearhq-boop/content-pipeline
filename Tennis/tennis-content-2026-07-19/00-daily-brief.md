### STORY 1: Raphael Collignon Wins First ATP Title at Gstaad
- **Tier:** 1
- **Angle:** NEW STORY — Raphael Collignon (BEL, career high No. 42) defeated No. 3 seed Stefanos Tsitsipas 7-6(2), 7-6(5) in the EFG Swiss Open Gstaad final to claim his first ATP Tour title. Collignon reached the final by saving a match point against Juan Manuel Cerundolo in the SF (came back from 1-6, 3-5 down to win 1-6, 7-6(5), 7-5). Only the second Belgian ever to reach the Gstaad final, after David Goffin in 2015. Tsitsipas was returning to his first ATP final in 17 months.
- **Posting Window:** 8:00 AM ET

### STORY 2: Sakkari Wins Athens — Historic Home Title
- **Tier:** 1
- **Angle:** NEW STORY — Maria Sakkari (GRE, No. 4 seed) won the Vanda Pharmaceuticals Athens Open, the first WTA event in Athens in 35 years, defeating No. 1 seed Barbora Krejcikova in the final. Sakkari reached her first WTA final in over two years (last: Indian Wells runner-up, March 2024). On the same day, Tsitsipas played the Gstaad final — making July 19 a historic day for Greek tennis with two Greek players in ATP/WTA finals simultaneously. SF results: Sakkari def. Korneeva 6-1, 7-5; Krejcikova def. Tauson 6-1, 4-6, 6-3.
- **Posting Window:** 10:30 AM ET

### STORY 3: Paula Badosa Wins WTA Iasi — Fifth Career Title
- **Tier:** 1
- **Angle:** NEW STORY — Paula Badosa (ESP) defeated Mayar Sherif (EGY) in the UniCredit Iasi Open final to claim her fifth career WTA title. Badosa entered on a six-match winning streak and has now never lost a WTA title match (5-0). The win pushes her back toward the top 100 — as high as No. 75. She grew up following Simona Halep and has called Iasi "home." Badosa's SF: def. Tamara Zidansek 3-6, 7-5, 7-6 (came back from set and a break down). Sherif's SF: def. Oliynykova 6-3, 6-1.
- **Posting Window:** 12:30 PM ET
