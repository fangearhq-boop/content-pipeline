Libema Open 2026: FAA, de Minaur, and Medvedev Open the 's-Hertogenbosch Grass Season

By Elena Voss | June 9, 2026

Alongside Stuttgart, the 35th edition of the Libema Open in 's-Hertogenbosch, Netherlands, opened Monday at Autotron Rosmalen. The ATP 250 grass-court event runs June 8 through 14 and features one of the strongest fields of the opening grass week — headlined by Felix Auger-Aliassime, who arrives as top seed at a career-high world ranking of No. 4.

FAA's rise to No. 4 followed a strong clay-court spring that included a run to the Roland Garros quarterfinal before losing to Flavio Cobolli. The ranking milestone is the highest of his career, and 's-Hertogenbosch offers an early opportunity to test whether that form converts to a surface he has not always mastered. His grass record has been developing, and arriving as the No. 1 seed raises the expectation accordingly.

The Draw

Daniil Medvedev is seeded second at 's-Hertogenbosch and receives a first-round bye. Medvedev's record on grass has been one of tennis's most discussed stories — ranked as high as No. 1 in the world, he has historically struggled on a surface that punishes slow-starting servers and rewards forward aggression. But conditions, draw, and timing shift every season. His campaign begins in the second round.

Alex de Minaur, the 2024 defending champion, anchors the bottom half of the draw. The Australian's quick footwork and excellent return game are qualities that translate well to grass — as his 2024 title run demonstrated. De Minaur arrives motivated to recapture a title that gave him genuine Grand Slam-contender credentials for the year's grass swing.

Among the most anticipated first-round matches: Tallon Griekspoor, the No. 6 seed, faces compatriot Botic van de Zandschulp in their fourth career meeting. Griekspoor leads their head-to-head 2-1 and plays in front of a home crowd that will bring the kind of atmosphere only a Dutch derby can generate in the Netherlands. All three of their previous meetings were tight contests, and the fourth is unlikely to be different.

Two ATP 250s in the Same Week

The coincidence of Stuttgart and 's-Hertogenbosch running simultaneously divides the field and creates two separate narratives for the opening grass week. Players who prioritised one event over the other made strategic decisions based on draw, surface confidence, and Wimbledon preparation. The combination means more matches, more results, and a fuller picture of the grass-court ranking order before Wimbledon seedings are confirmed in mid-June.

For 's-Hertogenbosch specifically, the added value is a crowd that understands tennis deeply and a venue that has hosted the Libema Open with consistent quality since its founding. Thirty-five editions in, the tournament remains one of the grass circuit's most respected ATP 250 events.

What's Next

The Libema Open runs through June 14, with the singles final scheduled for Sunday. Full draw, results, and schedule are available at libema-open.nl and atptour.com.
