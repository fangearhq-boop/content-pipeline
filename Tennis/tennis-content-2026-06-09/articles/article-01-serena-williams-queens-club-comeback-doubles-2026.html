Serena Williams Returns to Competitive Tennis at Queen's Club 2026

By Elena Voss | June 9, 2026

Four years after her farewell match at the 2022 US Open, Serena Williams is back. The 23-time Grand Slam champion has accepted a wild card to play doubles at the 2026 HSBC Championships at Queen's Club in London — the WTA 500 grass-court event running June 8 through 14. Her return to a professional tennis court is, by any measure, the story of the grass-court season's opening week.

Williams is partnering with Victoria Mboko, the 19-year-old Canadian ranked world No. 9 — a player young enough to have grown up watching Williams win majors. Mboko described Williams publicly as her idol. The pairing was deliberate: Williams said she was looking for someone who wants to win. She found that in Mboko, currently one of the brightest young talents on the WTA Tour.

The Draw and the Stakes

Williams and Mboko were drawn against No. 3 seeds Nicole Melichar-Martinez and Erin Routliffe in the first round of the HSBC Championships doubles. Melichar-Martinez and Routliffe are an experienced partnership and among the elite doubles teams on the women's tour. The draw did not give Williams an easy path back.

Williams was candid about what this week represents. "I don't need to win," she told reporters. She also said she remains unsure about returning to singles competition. Those are the measured words of a player who has framed this comeback carefully — as an exercise in enjoyment rather than a championship assault. But the competitive instinct is still there. It has to be.

Why Queen's Club

The HSBC Championships at Queen's Club is held in West Kensington, London — four miles from the All England Club. The tournament has been a home for British tennis moments and a proving ground for grass-court form for generations of players. Williams has played Wimbledon nine times in the Open Era and won it seven times. Choosing Queen's Club as the venue for her competitive return connects her to the surface and the city that shaped some of her career's defining chapters.

Williams turned 44 in September 2025. The last time she competed, the WTA Tour looked different — before Andreeva, Gauff, Sabalenka, and the current generation completed their rise to the top. Returning now, partnered with a 19-year-old, places Williams at the intersection of the past and the present in a way that few athletes experience.

Victoria Mboko's Role

Mboko, born in 2006, reached a career-high of No. 9 in the world in 2026 — making her one of the top-ranked Canadians in women's tennis and one of the game's rising stars. Partnering with Williams this week is both an honour and a genuine competitive opportunity: a wild-card doubles team with Williams' serve and instincts alongside Mboko's current-tour speed and mobility is not a charitable entry. It is a potential threat.

The LTA quoted Mboko describing the experience of starting her grass season alongside her idol. For Mboko, this is a moment she will carry regardless of the result. For Williams, it is a return to the environment that has meant most to her career.

What's Next

Williams and Mboko are scheduled to play their first-round match Tuesday at Queen's Club. The result will determine whether the comeback continues into the quarterfinals. The HSBC Championships runs through June 14, with full draws and results available at wtatennis.com and lta.org.uk.
