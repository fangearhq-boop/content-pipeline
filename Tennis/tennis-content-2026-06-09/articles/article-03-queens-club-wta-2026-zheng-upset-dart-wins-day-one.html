Queen's Club WTA 2026: Zheng Stunned by Cristian, Dart Beats Samsonova on Day One

By Ryan Calloway | June 9, 2026

The 2026 HSBC Championships at Queen's Club opened on June 8 with two first-round upsets that set the tone for the week. No. 5 seed Qinwen Zheng was eliminated by Jaqueline Cristian, and British wildcard Harriet Dart outlasted world No. 35 Liudmila Samsonova in one of the day's most dramatic matches. Grass, as always, wasted no time.

Cristian Ends Zheng's Week Early

Jaqueline Cristian defeated Qinwen Zheng 6-4, 7-6(4) in one hour and 34 minutes. It was only the second grass-court tour-level win of Cristian's career. Zheng, seeded No. 5 and ranked inside the world's top 10, arrived at Queen's Club as one of the pre-tournament favourites. She departed in the first round.

Grass has historically been an inconsistent surface for Zheng. Her game is built on baseline consistency and powerful groundstrokes, attributes that translate less cleanly to the lower bounce and faster pace of grass. Cristian, a more adaptable ball-striker with the instincts to vary pace, found the right formula and held it through a close second set that required a tiebreak to decide.

Dart Wins in Three Sets

Harriet Dart delivered the crowd's favourite result of the day. The British wildcard came from a first-set loss to beat Liudmila Samsonova 5-7, 6-4, 6-3 in two hours and 48 minutes. The LTA described it as Dart's best win by ranking since she defeated Katie Boulter in the second round of Wimbledon in 2024 — a result that remains one of the most memorable moments of her career on home grass.

Dart saved the most important break of the match for the second set, taking charge after falling behind and finding the control she needed to close out the third. The Queen's Club crowd, always vocal for British competitors, gave her a reception that matched the moment.

Boulter, Raducanu, and Day Two

Katie Boulter's match against No. 8 seed Leylah Fernandez was halted by rain after Fernandez took the first set 6-3 and led 3-3 in the second. That match carries into Tuesday, with Boulter needing to find a way back from a one-set deficit on a surface she knows exceptionally well.

Day 2 brings Emma Raducanu onto the court against qualifier Anna Blinkova. Raducanu has been working through a WTA Tour reset in 2026 and arrives at Queen's Club as one of the home nation's most watched players. Also Tuesday: 17-year-old British wildcard Mika Stojsavljevic faces Roland Garros semifinalist Marta Kostyuk — a significant test for the teenager making her first Queen's Club main draw appearance. And Serena Williams plays her first competitive match in four years alongside Victoria Mboko in the doubles first round.

What's Next

The HSBC Championships continues through June 14. Full results and the Day 2 order of play are available at lta.org.uk and wtatennis.com.
