Stuttgart BOSS Open 2026: Kyrgios and Moutet Open Grass Season on Day One

By Marcus Cole | June 9, 2026

The Stuttgart BOSS Open's main draw began Monday at TC Weissenhof, and the grass-court season officially arrived. Nick Kyrgios, playing his first competitive grass match in nearly three years, opened against No. 8 seed Corentin Moutet in the day's headline clash. The pairing — two of the most theatrical, unpredictable players on the ATP Tour — was everything the first day of a tournament should be.

Kyrgios accepted a wild card for the ATP 250 event. He has played only a handful of singles matches since 2023, sidelined by knee and wrist surgeries. Stuttgart represents a real return, not a brief appearance, and grass is the surface where his serve-and-volley instincts translate most directly into results. Moutet, the French No. 8 seed, is an equally creative player known for his flat ball-striking and ability to change pace. On grass in Stuttgart on a Monday afternoon, it was the match to watch.

The Draw and Day One Action

Frances Tiafoe also opened play Monday against local wildcard Daniel Altmaier, with the match scheduled post 3:00 PM local time on Centre Court. Altmaier, a German wildcard representing the home nation's hopes in the event, faces a steep task against the American No. 6 seed.

Ben Shelton, the top seed at world No. 5, and Taylor Fritz, the defending champion ranked No. 9 in the world, both held first-round byes and will enter the tournament in the second round. Fritz won the title at TC Weissenhof in 2025 and arrives looking to defend points on a surface that suits his game. Shelton, who spent much of the early 2026 season building his ranking steadily, makes his Stuttgart debut as the player seeded to face Fritz in the semifinal.

Also in the draw: Alexander Bublik as No. 3 seed and Tommy Paul among the higher seeds. Absent from the week's action: Alexander Zverev (Roland Garros champion, withdrew), Flavio Cobolli (Roland Garros runner-up, withdrew), and Jakub Mensik (withdrew after reaching the Roland Garros semifinal). The weekend's final weekend at Roland Garros has visibly thinned the Stuttgart field's top tier.

Kyrgios and the Grass Return

The significance of Kyrgios playing grass this week extends beyond one tournament. He has not committed to a full schedule in years. Stuttgart, followed potentially by Halle or Wimbledon, would represent the kind of sustained engagement with the tour that his talent has always demanded but his body rarely permitted. On grass, the argument for Kyrgios is simple: his serve, his net game, and his ability to hit angles that have no equivalent on clay or hard courts. If he is healthy, he is dangerous here.

The Stuttgart BOSS Open runs through June 14. Wimbledon begins July 1. For players in this draw — Kyrgios included — Stuttgart is the first test of where their grass game stands before the sport's most historic tournament arrives.

What's Next

Stuttgart's second round begins Wednesday, bringing Fritz and Shelton into play. For Kyrgios and Moutet, the result of Monday's match determines whether their week continues. Full results, draws, and schedule are available at atptour.com.
