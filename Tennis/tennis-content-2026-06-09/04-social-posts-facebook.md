### STORY 1: Serena Williams Returns at Queen's Club

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Serena Williams is back in professional tennis.

Four years after her final match at the 2022 US Open, the 23-time Grand Slam champion has accepted a wild card to play doubles at the 2026 HSBC Championships at Queen's Club in London. Her partner is Victoria Mboko — 19 years old, world No. 9, and the top-ranked Canadian woman in the sport. "Mboko called Williams her idol," the LTA reported. Williams chose her with a purpose: "I'm looking for someone who wants to win."

Their first match is against the No. 3 seeds, Nicole Melichar-Martinez and Erin Routliffe, a seasoned doubles partnership. Williams was candid about expectations, saying she does not need to win and that a singles return remains uncertain. But the fact that she is back on a competitive court — any competitive court — is already a bigger story than most results this week.

Williams last played Wimbledon in 2022, one year before her farewell. Queen's Club is four miles from Wimbledon. It is a fitting stage for whatever this chapter of her career becomes.

Who is glad to see Serena Williams back on a tennis court?

### STORY 2: Stuttgart BOSS Open Day 1

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

The grass-court season does not ease you in. It throws Nick Kyrgios vs Corentin Moutet at you on the first day.

Stuttgart's BOSS Open main draw started Monday. Kyrgios — the Australian who has played just a handful of singles matches since 2023 following knee and wrist surgeries — is back on grass for the first time in nearly three years. He accepted a wild card for the ATP 250 event at TC Weissenhof and opened against No. 8 seed Moutet, a Frenchman known for his own brand of unpredictable, creative tennis.

Frances Tiafoe also opened against local wildcard Daniel Altmaier. Ben Shelton (world No. 5, tournament top seed) and Taylor Fritz (defending champion, No. 9) both have first-round byes and enter in the second round.

Notably absent: Alexander Zverev (Roland Garros champion), Flavio Cobolli, and Jakub Mensik all withdrew after the Roland Garros final weekend. Stuttgart this year is about the next generation and the returning names — and the road to Wimbledon, now just 22 days away.

Who are you watching at Stuttgart this week?

### STORY 3: Queen's Club WTA Day 1 Results

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

Queen's Club delivered shocks on its first full day of play.

Jaqueline Cristian opened the HSBC Championships with a 6-4, 7-6(4) defeat of No. 5 seed Qinwen Zheng — only the second grass-court tour win of Cristian's career, and a statement arrival at the WTA 500 event. Harriet Dart, the British wildcard, was even more impressive: she came back from a first-set loss to beat world No. 35 Liudmila Samsonova 5-7, 6-4, 6-3 in nearly three hours. The LTA called it her best win by ranking since defeating Katie Boulter at Wimbledon 2024. The West Kensington crowd was loud.

Katie Boulter's match against No. 8 seed Leylah Fernandez was interrupted by rain at 3-6, 3-3 and carries into Tuesday.

Day 2 schedule: Emma Raducanu faces qualifier Anna Blinkova in what will be closely watched — and 17-year-old British wildcard Mika Stojsavljevic takes on Roland Garros semifinalist Marta Kostyuk, a towering task for the teenager. Serena Williams and Victoria Mboko play their first-round doubles.

### STORY 4: Libema Open 's-Hertogenbosch

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Across the Channel, another ATP 250 grass event is underway — and it is worth your attention.

The 35th edition of the Libema Open in 's-Hertogenbosch, Netherlands, kicked off Monday at Autotron Rosmalen. The field features Felix Auger-Aliassime as top seed at a career-high world ranking of No. 4. Daniil Medvedev is seeded second. Alex de Minaur, the 2024 defending champion, anchors the bottom half of the draw.

The most anticipated first-round match locally: Tallon Griekspoor against compatriot Botic van de Zandschulp. It is the fourth meeting between the two Dutchmen, with Griekspoor leading their head-to-head 2-1. Playing in front of a home crowd on a surface both know well, it will be one of the week's compelling storylines.

The tournament runs through June 14. The Libema Open has produced some of the most competitive grass-court tennis outside of Wimbledon fortnight — and this year, with the Roland Garros dust still settling, it arrives at a particularly interesting moment in the season.

Which event are you following this week — Stuttgart or 's-Hertogenbosch?

### STORY 5: WTA Grass Season Opens

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 PM ET

Roland Garros ended on Sunday. Queen's Club started on Monday.

The sport did not give its players a single day off between the most physically demanding clay court tournament of the season and the fastest surface in the game. For some, that is a problem. For others, it is an opportunity.

Mirra Andreeva is the Roland Garros champion, ranked No. 6 in the world, and she arrives on grass with something most players at her level do not have: no points to defend. She has everything to gain. Aryna Sabalenka stays at No. 1 but has never won Wimbledon. Coco Gauff, now No. 7 after a disappointing Roland Garros, will be motivated.

The WTA grass swing runs Queen's Club (June 8–14), then Birmingham and Eastbourne, before culminating at Wimbledon starting July 1. It is three weeks of the most exciting conditions in the sport, and this year — with a new Roland Garros champion, a returning Serena Williams, and a 17-year-old British wildcard facing Marta Kostyuk tomorrow — it has already started with more story lines than expected.

Who do you think lifts the Wimbledon trophy this year?
