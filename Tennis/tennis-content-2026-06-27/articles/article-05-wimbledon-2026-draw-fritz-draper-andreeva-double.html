Wimbledon 2026 Draw: Fritz vs. Draper in R1, Andreeva Chases Double Not Done Since Henin 2006

By Elena Voss | June 27, 2026

With the Championships starting Monday, the draw released Friday sets up blockbuster first-round clashes and a historic opportunity for the Roland Garros champion.

The Wimbledon 2026 draw was revealed Friday morning at the All England Club, and it delivered the kind of opening-round matchups that would normally be reserved for the quarterfinals. Two days from now, with the Championships beginning Monday June 29, the players are ready. The question is: who survives the first week?

The Men's Draw: Fritz vs. Draper and a Djokovic-Sinner Collision Course

The headline matchup in the men's draw is No. 6 seed Taylor Fritz against British wildcard Jack Draper in the first round. The timing gives the clash an almost theatrical quality. Draper had played — and lost — his Eastbourne semi-final against Humbert on Friday morning. Hours later, he sat at the draw ceremony and watched his name appear alongside Fritz. Fritz, who had withdrawn from Eastbourne as defending champion to rest, arrives at Wimbledon fully fresh. Draper arrives having played three-set tennis the same day.

Also in the first round: No. 11 seed Casper Ruud meets Hubert Hurkacz — a match that has the quality of a quarterfinal compressed into the opening round. Defending champion Jannik Sinner (No. 1 seed) opens against Miomir Kecmanovic and sits in the top half of the draw. No. 7 Novak Djokovic faces Wu Yibing in round one and is also in the top half. The implication is significant: Sinner and Djokovic cannot meet before the semi-finals. If both progress through their sections, the semifinal could be the match everyone has been anticipating since the seedings were announced.

Alexander Zverev, the Roland Garros champion and No. 2 seed, leads the bottom half. He and Sinner are on opposite sides of the draw and can only meet in the final.

The Women's Draw: Andreeva's Historic Chance

The women's draw is headlined by Mirra Andreeva and an opportunity she may not get again in quite the same way. The No. 5 seed and Roland Garros champion opens against Magda Linette — a manageable first-round match for a player who has been the dominant force in women's tennis over the past two months.

If Andreeva wins Wimbledon 2026, she will have achieved the Roland Garros/Wimbledon double — consecutive Grand Slam titles on clay and grass — something no woman has accomplished since Justine Henin in 2006. Henin won the French Open and Wimbledon in the same year. Andreeva, at 18 years old, has the game, the ranking, and the momentum to attempt it.

The rest of the women's draw is competitive. No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka (who has never won Wimbledon) opens against Tena Kostovic. No. 2 Elena Rybakina, the 2022 Wimbledon champion, plays Loïs Boisson in round one. No. 3 seed Iga Swiatek, the defending Wimbledon champion, opens against Taylor Townsend — and finds Serena Williams in her quarter of the draw. If both win their first two matches, Swiatek and Williams could meet in the third round. That would be one of the most-watched matches of the Championships. No. 7 Coco Gauff opens against Tamara Korpatsch.

Wimbledon by the Numbers

Prize money: £64.2 million — a record and a 20 percent increase from 2025. The Championships run June 29 through July 12. Men's final: July 12. Women's final: July 11. New for 2026: video reviews will be used at Wimbledon for the first time in the tournament's history.

What's Next

Wimbledon 2026 begins Monday June 29. First-round matches across Centre Court, Court 1, and Court 2 will run throughout the opening week. The draw's blockbuster first-round matches — Fritz vs. Draper, Ruud vs. Hurkacz — are expected to be scheduled in prime slots early in the fortnight. Andreeva's opener against Linette will be watched closely by anyone tracking the Roland Garros/Wimbledon double narrative. The Championships conclude July 12.
