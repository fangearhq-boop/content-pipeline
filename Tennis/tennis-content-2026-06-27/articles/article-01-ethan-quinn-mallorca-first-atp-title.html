Ethan Quinn Wins First ATP Title at Mallorca, Dominating Davidovich Fokina 6-1, 6-2

By Ryan Calloway | June 27, 2026

The 22-year-old American turned a grasscourt week no one expected into a championship run that announced his arrival at the top of men's tennis.

Ethan Quinn walked off the court at the Mallorca Country Club on Saturday as an ATP champion — a title that, even three years into his professional career, still seemed to carry a note of pleasant surprise. Quinn, ranked No. 63 in the world and never previously having reached an ATP quarter-final, defeated No. 2 seed Alejandro Davidovich Fokina 6-1, 6-2 in the final of the Vanda Pharmaceuticals Mallorca Championships to claim the first ATP Tour title of his career.

The match lasted just over an hour and was never particularly competitive. Quinn broke Davidovich Fokina's serve three times in the first set, raced through the second without drama, and never gave the Spaniard the foothold he needed to change the match's direction. It was the kind of clinical performance that coaches describe as playing within yourself — no wasted movement, no unnecessary risks, just a relentless quality of ball-striking from the baseline.

It was also Quinn's second straight 6-1, 6-2 result. In the semi-final on Friday, he dismantled Nuno Borges in just 56 minutes in what was arguably the most one-sided match of the grass-court season.

From Georgia to Mallorca: A Three-Year Journey

Quinn, born March 12, 2004, in Fresno, California, is the product of a tennis family. He enrolled early at the University of Georgia in January 2022 and rapidly established himself as one of the best college players in the country. In May 2023, he won the NCAA Singles Championship for the Georgia Bulldogs. One month later, he turned professional.

Twenty-four months ago, Quinn was ranked No. 239 in the world. The Mallorca title takes him to a new career high of approximately No. 47. He is the third American to reach the Mallorca final — after Sam Querrey in 2021 and Christopher Eubanks in 2023 — and the first to win it.

Davidovich Fokina, ranked No. 25 and a player who has spent years on tour without collecting an ATP title, reached the final by defeating Fabian Marozsan 5-7, 6-2, 6-4 in the semi-finals. He was the more experienced player on paper; on the day, he had no answer for Quinn's consistency.

Arriving at Wimbledon as a Champion

The timing of the title could not be better. Wimbledon begins Monday June 29, just two days from now, and Quinn heads into the Championships on the kind of grass-court form that most players spend entire seasons trying to build.

In five matches at Mallorca, Quinn dropped just one set. His serve held up under pressure, his return game was precise, and he showed a composure in big moments that suggests the step from college tennis to professional has been less of an adjustment than it appeared from the outside.

None of this means Wimbledon will be straightforward. The draw includes players far more experienced on grass, and three sets at the All England Club is a different animal from a two-set ATP 250 format. But coming into Wimbledon as a first-time ATP champion is a different kind of confidence.

What's Next

Ethan Quinn will enter the Wimbledon 2026 main draw directly, arriving at the All England Club having just won his first professional title on the surface. His ranking of approximately No. 47 means he will be an unseeded player at the Championships — an intriguing prospect for potential opponents who draw him in the first round.

Davidovich Fokina, meanwhile, also heads to Wimbledon. Despite the defeat, reaching a final and playing through a strong field provides preparation that cannot be replicated in practice. For both players, the real competition begins Monday.
