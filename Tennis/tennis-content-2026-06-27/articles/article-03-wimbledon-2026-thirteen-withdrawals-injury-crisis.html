Wimbledon 2026 Faces Record 13 Withdrawals: Alcaraz, Rune, WTA No. 9 Mboko Among the Absentees

By Marcus Cole | June 27, 2026

The Championships begin Monday with one of the most significant pre-tournament absence lists in recent memory — headlined by men's World No. 3 Alcaraz and women's No. 9 Mboko, whose injury also ends the Serena Williams doubles comeback.

Wimbledon 2026 begins Monday June 29 with 13 confirmed player withdrawals — a combined men's and women's figure that represents an unusual injury wave at a tournament that rarely sees this level of pre-event attrition. Seven of the eight men to withdraw are current or former top-20 players, and the women's list includes the WTA's No. 9 ranked player. The Championships, with a record prize pool of £64.2 million, will proceed regardless — but the draw picture looks considerably different than it did when the seedings were announced.

The Men's Withdrawals

Carlos Alcaraz is the most high-profile absence. The World No. 3 and two-time Wimbledon finalist has not played competitively since the Barcelona Open in April due to a right wrist injury. His absence removes one of the strongest grass-court players on tour from a Championship he had previously reached the final of twice. With Holger Rune also absent — the Dane's Achilles tendon and knee rehabilitation made the decision cautious but ultimately necessary — the men's draw has lost two of its most compelling storylines before a single match is played.

The full men's withdrawal list also includes Lorenzo Musetti (rectus femoris thigh injury, which also caused him to miss the French Open), Sebastian Korda (undisclosed fitness issue), Tomas Machac (foot injury), Valentin Vacherot (ranked approximately No. 20), and Reilly Opelka.

The Women's Withdrawals — and Mboko's Story

On the women's side, Victoria Mboko's withdrawal carries its own particular weight. The 19-year-old Canadian, ranked No. 9 in the world and one of the most exciting young players on the WTA Tour, slipped on the grass at Queen's Club during her second-round singles match against Karolina Pliskova. She injured the MCL on her left knee and was forced to retire while trailing 6-2, 3-4. She will miss the remainder of the grass season.

The injury also ends Mboko's doubles partnership with Serena Williams, who had returned to competitive doubles tennis this summer. The two had opened their Queen's Club doubles campaign with a 7-6(2), 6-2 victory over No. 3 seeds Nicole Melichar-Martinez and Erin Routliffe before Mboko's injury forced their withdrawal. Mboko's replacement in the Wimbledon main draw is Darja Vidmanova, who won both the NCAA singles and doubles championships in 2025.

The other women's withdrawals include Hailey Baptiste (knee), Sonay Kartal (back), Markéta Vondroušová, and Veronika Kudermetova (illness).

A New Addition: Video Reviews

Wimbledon 2026 introduces video reviews for the first time in the tournament's history. The addition, which has been used at other Grand Slams for years, brings the All England Club in line with the broader tour's approach to line-call challenges. Players will now be able to challenge calls using the review system, a change that has been debated within the tennis world for some time given Wimbledon's traditional stance on electronic line-calling.

The Championships run from June 29 through July 12. Men's final is July 12; women's final July 11.

What's Next

Wimbledon 2026 begins Monday with defending champions Jannik Sinner (men's) and Iga Swiatek (women's) among the confirmed starters. The absence of Alcaraz, Rune, and Mboko reshapes the draw's competitive picture considerably, potentially opening paths for players further down the seeding list. First-round action begins June 29 at the All England Club.
