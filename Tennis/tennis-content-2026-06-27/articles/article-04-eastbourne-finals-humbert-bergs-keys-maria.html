Eastbourne 2026 Finals: Humbert Faces Bergs, Keys Meets Maria in Last Grass Warm-Up Before Wimbledon

By Ryan Calloway | June 27, 2026

The Lexus Eastbourne Open concludes Saturday with two singles finals — the last competitive grasscourt action before the Championships begin Monday June 29.

Saturday at Devonshire Park in Eastbourne carries an unusual weight. Both the men's and women's singles finals at the Lexus Eastbourne Open will be contested today — and every player in both draws walks from this venue directly toward the All England Club, where Wimbledon begins Monday June 29. There are no more tune-ups after this. The titles on offer are the last meaningful grasscourt achievements available before the Grand Slam.

Men's Final: Humbert vs. Bergs

The men's final pits Ugo Humbert against Zizou Bergs — two players who arrived at Eastbourne without enormous expectation and have delivered compelling semi-final performances to reach the title match.

Humbert defeated Jack Draper 7-5, 6-3 in his semi-final on Friday, a result that carried an extra dimension given that Draper then learned, via the Wimbledon draw released the same day, that he would face No. 6 seed Taylor Fritz in the opening round of the Championships. Humbert's win was efficient — he served well, kept his unforced errors down, and closed out the match without giving Draper the moments of momentum that the British player usually thrives on. Humbert is a comfortable grasscourt player whose flat, aggressive ball-striking tends to translate well to fast surfaces.

Bergs's path to the final was more dramatic. He fell behind to British wildcard Toby Samuel in the semi-final, losing the first set 4-6 before regrouping to claim the second in a tiebreak 7-6(5) and the third 6-2. Samuel had been one of the stories of the week — a wildcard who had delivered results that his ranking did not suggest. Bergs, the Belgian player from the ATP 40s, made sure the story ended in the semi-final.

Women's Final: Keys vs. Maria

The women's final presents Madison Keys against Tatjana Maria — a contrast in styles and career trajectories that makes for an interesting match on paper.

Keys is a former Eastbourne champion who has consistently performed well on grass throughout her career. The American has a powerful serve and a forehand that can end points quickly on this surface. She advanced to the final after her semi-final opponent retired — meaning she reaches Saturday's match without the physical wear of a hard three-set contest.

Maria likewise benefited from a semi-final retirement. The German player, known for her distinctive one-handed backhand slice and her ability to absorb pace and redirect, has had notable grasscourt results throughout her career. Her route to the final was uncontested in the final stages, which means both players arrive physically fresh for what should be a competitive title match.

What It Means Heading Into Wimbledon

For any player competing in an Eastbourne final on the Saturday before Wimbledon, the calculation is simple: the title matters, but the physical cost of another match matters equally. Players who win today collect ranking points, confidence, and a grasscourt title. Players who lose still take away match practice on a surface they have been building form on all week. Both outcomes have value in the context of what follows.

In the men's doubles, Hugo Nys and Edouard Roger-Vasselin have already claimed the title — a result that confirmed the doubles draw at Eastbourne produced its own champion ahead of the main-draw singles finals.

What's Next

All four finalists — Humbert, Bergs, Keys, and Maria — enter the Wimbledon 2026 main draw directly after today's matches. The Championships begin June 29 at the All England Club. Results from today's Eastbourne finals will be posted as they become available.
