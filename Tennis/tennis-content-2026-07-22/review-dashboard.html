Wawrinka's Final Clay Match — Burruchaga Wins Emotional Estoril R1
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Stan Wawrinka played his last clay-court match on Tuesday at the Millennium Estoril Open. He lost to Roman Andres Burruchaga 6-4, 4-6, 6-3 in two hours and 28 minutes — saving match points along the way but ultimately unable to extend his farewell on the surface that made him a legend. The Estoril tournament held a ceremony after the match. The tournament director presented the 41-year-old Swiss with a commemorative gift. The kind of moment that doesn't require a speech — just a crowd that knows what they're watching. Wawrinka has announced 2026 as his final year on tour. He already said goodbye to Gstaad last week, where he made his ATP debut on the very same courts back in 2003. Estoril was the last clay chapter. Hard courts still remain — but clay was where Wawrinka truly became Wawrinka. He won three Grand Slams. Two were on hard courts (2014 Australian Open, 2016 US Open). But it was the 2015 Roland Garros, where he beat Djokovic in the final with one of the most breathtaking performances in modern tennis, that cemented what clay meant to him. Burruchaga, for his part, is having a remarkable stretch. He shocked former world No. 9 Flavio Cobolli in straight sets at Umag last week. Now he's in the Estoril Round of 16 to face Nuno Borges. The Argentine journeyman is making people pay attention. What's your favourite Stan Wawrinka memory on clay? ---

### Long-Form Post Kitzbühel is heating up. Day 3 arrives with some strong matchups on the Wednesday schedule. The match to watch: Arthur Rinderknech (third seed) versus Sebastian Baez. Altmaier won in straight sets 6-2, 6-3. The glow from Gstaad didn't last long in the Alps. The final is scheduled for Friday. Bublik will need to get through a competitive half to defend. Who do you have getting to Friday's final? ---

### Long-Form Post If you're a Czech tennis fan, the Livesport Prague Open first round was exactly what you came for. Marie Bouzkova — top seed, home favourite — got through with a 7-5, 7-5 win over Jessica Bouzas Maneiro. Clean, efficient, exactly what a top seed should do on opening day. Barbora Krejcikova walked through Linda Fruhvirtova 6-2, 6-4 in an hour and 18 minutes. She dropped the first set on a 9-7 tiebreak — those always hurt — then came back to take the second 6-3 and the third 6-1. Character shown in a tough moment on home soil. Oceane Dodin, Dominika Salkova, Nikola Bartunkova, and others all advanced. The field is filling out with a distinctly local flavour in the early rounds. The surprise: Alycia Parks didn't make it out of the first round. The American, ranked No. 34, had just come off a strong Athens run — including a quarterfinal against Sakkari. Prague had a different answer. Czech tennis is not short of depth this week. 🎾 ---

### Long-Form Post The MSC Hamburg Ladies Open got its first round sorted and the seeds largely held, but there was enough to keep the crowd engaged. Top seed Oleksandra Oliynykova got past Nastasja Schunk. Second seed Anhelina Kalinina defeated Kamila Quevedo.
