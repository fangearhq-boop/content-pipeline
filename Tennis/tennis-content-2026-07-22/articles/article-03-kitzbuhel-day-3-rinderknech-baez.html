Kitzbühel Day 3 — Rinderknech vs. Baez Headlines Second-Round Action at Generali Open

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 22, 2026 — Kitzbühel, Austria

The Generali Open Kitzbühel reaches its second round Wednesday with a card that reflects the range of players competing in this ATP 250 clay event — experienced champions, seeded favourites, and home-country contingent all represented. The match of the day on paper: third seed Arthur Rinderknech against Sebastian Baez.

Rinderknech vs. Baez — Wednesday's Centrepiece

Rinderknech opened his Kitzbühel campaign with a 6-4, 6-0 demolition of Mariano Navone — the kind of first-round performance that makes opponents take notice heading into the next match. The Frenchman has been in strong form during the post-Wimbledon clay swing after upsetting Tsitsipas at Gstaad last week and was a finalist in that tournament before losing to Raphael Collignon's stunning maiden title run.

Baez had a considerably harder path to this point. He faced Miomir Kecmanovic on Tuesday in what the ATP Tour billed as a "battle of former champs" — both players have won titles at Kitzbühel previously. Baez won 6-3, 3-6, 6-4 in an hour and 49 minutes, taking advantage of a first-career win over Kecmanovic to reach the second round. He arrives here with more miles on his legs than Rinderknech.

Also on the Wednesday schedule: second seed Valentin Vacherot faces Quentin Halys. Vacherot, currently ranked inside the top 30, enters as a strong favourite, but Halys is the Frenchman who knocked out defending champion Alexander Bublik at Gstaad last week — the form argument is at least worth noting.

The Defending Champion Is Still In It

Alexander Bublik — the No. 1 seed and defending Kitzbühel champion — survived a difficult first round against Facundo Diaz Acosta. The Kazakh fell 4-6 in the first set before recovering to win 6-3, 6-4. Defending a title is never as straightforward as it looks on paper, and the opening match showed exactly why: Bublik had to dig in before finding his level.

Tomas Martin Etcheverry, the fourth seed, had an easier afternoon against Jurij Rodionov, winning 6-4, 6-2. The Argentine is a clay specialist with the ranking to go deep in a draw like this.

Collignon Falls — German Trio Advances

One of the more notable first-round results came from the draw's opening matches. Raphael Collignon — the Belgian who claimed his first ATP title just three days ago at Gstaad — drew Daniel Altmaier and fell 6-2, 6-3. The ATP Tour's match highlight was labelled "Altmaier eases past Collignon," which captures exactly how the match went. There is no automatic momentum transfer from one week's title to the next tournament's first round, a lesson Collignon absorbed quickly in the Austrian Alps.

Altmaier, Struff, and Hanfmann all advanced, giving the tournament a strong German presence heading into the second round. The Austrian crowd will have no shortage of players close to home to follow.

What's Next

The Generali Open Kitzbühel runs through July 25, with the final scheduled for Friday. Wednesday's second-round results will set the quarterfinalists. If Bublik and Etcheverry continue through their halves of the draw, a meeting between them or the likes of Rinderknech, Baez, or Vacherot would represent the final's most likely shape.

The Millennium Estoril Open runs simultaneously through July 26, with Andrey Rublev as top seed and Luciano Darderi as No. 2. Both events offer ranking points that will matter as players prepare for the North American hard-court swing — the Canadian Open, Cincinnati, and the US Open — beginning August 1.
