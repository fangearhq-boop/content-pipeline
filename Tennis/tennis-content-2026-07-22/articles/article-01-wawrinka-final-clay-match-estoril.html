Wawrinka Says Goodbye to Clay at Estoril — Burruchaga Wins Emotional Three-Set Farewell

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 22, 2026 — Estoril, Portugal

Stan Wawrinka played the final clay-court match of his professional career on Tuesday. The three-time Grand Slam champion, at 41 years old and in the last year of his farewell tour, walked off the red dirt at the Clube de Ténis do Estoril after losing to Roman Andres Burruchaga 6-4, 4-6, 6-3 in two hours and 28 minutes. The scoreline was close. The occasion was not.

The Estoril tournament marked the moment with a ceremony. After the final point, the tournament director presented Wawrinka with a commemorative gift on court. It was the kind of send-off that doesn't require words — the crowd at Cascais understood what they had witnessed.

A Fight to the End

Wawrinka did not go quietly. He won the second set to push the match to three, and saved match points before ultimately falling in the decisive set. At 41, with his body carrying the weight of more than two decades on the ATP Tour, the fact that he pushed Burruchaga this far said something about the competitor he has always been.

Burruchaga, the young Argentine who has been turning heads for the past week, was the better player. But Wawrinka forced him to earn it. That has always been the Wawrinka way — no gifts, no concessions, just tennis until the last point is decided.

Clay Was Where He Became Wawrinka

The surface has a particular meaning in his career. Wawrinka won three Grand Slams across his career — the 2014 Australian Open, the 2015 French Open, and the 2016 US Open. Two came on hard courts, one on clay. But the 2015 Roland Garros, where he defeated a healthy Novak Djokovic in the final to claim the title that seemed least expected of him, remains the performance that most defined what Wawrinka could be when everything came together.

He made his ATP debut at Gstaad in 2003. He said goodbye to Gstaad last week. He is saying goodbye to Estoril this week. The clay chapter is now closed.

Burruchaga Keeps Rising

For Burruchaga, this was the latest in a string of results that have made the Argentine difficult to overlook. Last week at the Croatia Open in Umag, he defeated Flavio Cobolli — the No. 9 player in the world at the time — in straight sets, 6-2, 6-4, in what the ATP Tour described as one of the best matches of the year. Now he is in the second round of an ATP 250 in Portugal with a win over a Grand Slam champion on his resumé.

His path forward at Estoril leads to Nuno Borges, the home favourite and one of the most popular players in Portugal. That will be a different test entirely — a player operating at full motivation on home soil in front of a crowd that came to see him win. Burruchaga has shown he can handle pressure. He will need to again.

What's Next

The European clay season ends this week with events at Estoril and Kitzbühel. After that, the ATP and WTA tours move to North America for the hardcourt swing — the Canadian Open, Cincinnati, and then the US Open from August 24. Wawrinka's farewell tour continues on those surfaces, where two of his three Grand Slams were won.

For Estoril, top seed Andrey Rublev — who came through his own opener against a lucky loser after dropping the first set — headlines the remaining draw. The tournament runs through July 26.

The clay chapter is done. Stan Wawrinka will be remembered on this surface for a long time.
