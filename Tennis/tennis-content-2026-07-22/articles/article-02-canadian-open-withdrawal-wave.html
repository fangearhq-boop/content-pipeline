Canadian Open Withdrawal Wave Reaches Eight-Plus Stars — Paolini and Muchova Latest to Exit

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 22, 2026 — National Bank Open, Montreal/Toronto

The National Bank Open does not begin until August 1, but its pre-tournament story is already being written by the players who are not coming. As of Wednesday, at least eight high-profile names have withdrawn from the joint ATP/WTA Masters 1000 event in Montreal and Toronto — a number that continues to grow with each passing day.

Tuesday brought two new additions. Jasmine Paolini (ITA, ranked No. 15 in the world) announced she is pulling out of both the Washington Open and the Canadian Open in Toronto, citing a persistent foot problem that has limited her throughout 2026. Hours later, Karolina Muchova (CZE, No. 6) confirmed she had undergone a minor surgical procedure after losing the Wimbledon final to Linda Noskova earlier this month, and will miss "a few weeks" of competition.

Paolini: Foot Forces Break

Paolini did not offer a detailed diagnosis, but the message was clear. "Unfortunately, I had to withdraw from the tournaments in Washington and Toronto to give my foot a bit more rest," she wrote on social media. "My foot needs rest." She is expected to return at the Cincinnati Open, which begins August 13.

The timing is particularly notable because Paolini was a finalist in Toronto in 2025 — one of the stronger weeks of her career. She will not have the chance to build on that result. Her absence weakens what is already a thinning women's draw in Toronto.

Muchova: Post-Wimbledon Surgery

Muchova's announcement was more significant in context. She played the Wimbledon final less than two weeks ago, taking the first set and pushing Noskova all the way before losing 2-6, 5-7, 3-6. To then confirm she had surgery after the match suggests she was managing the issue throughout the tournament — and that the decision to operate came quickly after the season's last major obligation on grass.

"I underwent a minor operation that will keep me out for a few weeks," Muchova said. "Everything went well, and I'm already working on my recovery." Like Paolini, she is targeting a return around the Cincinnati timeframe if the recovery proceeds on schedule.

A Growing Absence List

Paolini and Muchova join a withdrawal list that was already substantial. Carlos Alcaraz — the world No. 3 and defending champion in both Cincinnati and at the US Open — has been sidelined since April 14 in Barcelona with wrist tenosynovitis. He confirmed this week that he will return at Cincinnati, skipping Montreal entirely. Emma Raducanu is out with a grade-4 stress fracture in her right shin, with the US Open as her earliest possible return. Victoria Mboko, the defending WTA champion in Toronto and Canadian fans' home favourite, is also out with an ankle injury sustained in Berlin.

Others confirmed absent include Hailey Baptiste and Anastasia Pavlyuchenkova. Tennis365 put the total withdrawal count at eight or more as of this week's announcements.

What Remains

The men's draw in Montreal still carries considerable star power. Jannik Sinner confirmed his participation. Novak Djokovic is in. Alexander Zverev, who won Roland Garros and reached the Wimbledon final, is entered. The absence of Alcaraz leaves the defending Cincinnati champion out of Montreal, but the top of the men's draw remains credible.

The women's draw in Toronto is harder to assess. With Muchova, Paolini, Raducanu, and Mboko gone, the path to the final becomes significantly more open for the players who are healthy and competing. That is not necessarily a negative outcome — it can create meaningful storylines and give opportunities to players lower in the rankings who have stayed fit through a punishing summer schedule.

What's Next

The Canadian Open draws for both tournaments are expected to be released in the coming days, ahead of first-round action beginning August 1. Qualifying starts earlier. Players who have navigated the injury-heavy stretch between Wimbledon and the North American hard-court swing with their health intact will have a genuine opportunity to make their mark before the US Open begins August 24.

Cincinnati, where Paolini, Muchova, and Alcaraz are all targeting their returns, runs August 13 to 23. The US Open follows immediately after, making August a month where the injury calculus will determine much of what happens for the sport's biggest names.
