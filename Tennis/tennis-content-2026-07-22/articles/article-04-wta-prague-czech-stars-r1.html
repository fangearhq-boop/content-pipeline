Czech Stars Sweep Prague R1 — Krejcikova, Bouzkova, Bejlek All Win on Home Soil

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 22, 2026 — Prague, Czech Republic

The Livesport Prague Open first round handed Czech tennis fans exactly what they came for. Three of the nation's most recognisable players won their opening matches at the WTA 250 clay event on Tuesday, turning the Centre Court into a celebration of home talent on a warm Central European summer evening.

Bouzkova Delivers as Top Seed

Marie Bouzkova, the top seed at her home tournament, handled Jessica Bouzas Maneiro 7-5, 7-5. It was not a trouble-free afternoon — two closely contested sets suggest the Spanish player brought a real challenge — but Bouzkova managed the moments that counted. Playing as the favourite in Prague carries its own kind of weight. She handled it.

Krejcikova Cruises in Return to Czech Courts

Barbora Krejcikova had the most efficient result of the three. She beat Linda Fruhvirtova 6-2, 6-4 in an hour and 18 minutes — a clean, dominant performance that looked exactly like a player who has used two post-Wimbledon weeks to reset and refocus. Krejcikova reached the final at Wimbledon less than two weeks ago, where she fell to Linda Noskova. Coming home to Prague, in front of a crowd that knows her name and wanted her to win, she looked sharp.

Fruhvirtova, herself Czech, was given few opportunities to settle into the match. Krejcikova's ability to control the point from the baseline allowed her to dictate terms throughout.

Bejlek Survives Tiebreak Scare

Sara Bejlek's opener was the most dramatic of the three. She and Anna Blinkova went to a first-set tiebreak that went all the way to 9-7 before Bejlek took it. That kind of narrow escape in the first set — on home turf, in front of an expectant crowd — can go either way psychologically. Bejlek's response was clear: she won the next two sets 6-3 and 6-1. The difficult moment behind her, she played the tennis she was capable of.

The Upset: Parks Stumbles

The result that stands out from Tuesday's draw is Alycia Parks. The American, ranked No. 34 and coming off a quarterfinal run at the WTA Athens Open just days ago, did not make it past the first round in Prague. Parks was one of the more in-form players entering the week — her Athens results showed that — but Prague had a different answer. The surface, the conditions, the draw slot she landed in: something did not align on opening day.

Czech Depth Beyond the Top Three

The Czech success was not limited to Bouzkova, Krejcikova, and Bejlek. Dominika Salkova advanced. Nikola Bartunkova beat Yuan Yue to reach the second round, where she is scheduled to face Tararudee. Oceane Dodin of France also won her opener, as did Maya Joint.

Prague has historically been a venue where Czech players thrive — the home crowd, the familiar surroundings, and the WTA 250 draw structure give the local contingent a genuine pathway. With five Czech names through the first round this year, the tournament has the shape of a local celebration that might not end until Friday or Saturday.

What's Next

The second round at the Livesport Prague Open is scheduled for Wednesday through Thursday. Bouzkova, Krejcikova, and Bejlek will each need to navigate at least two more matches to reach the final on Saturday. The draw still needs to produce its quarterfinal picture before the scale of the Czech challenge becomes clear.

The Livesport Prague Open runs through July 26. The MSC Hamburg Ladies Open (WTA 500) runs simultaneously across the week, creating two finals destinations for players on the European clay circuit this week.
