### STORY 1: Svitolina wins WTA Rome — 20th career title

**Long-Form Post**

ELINA SVITOLINA IS THE ROME CHAMPION — FOR THE THIRD TIME. 🎾

She defeated Coco Gauff 6-4, 6-7(3), 6-2 on Saturday in a final that stretched nearly three hours. After losing the second set in a tiebreak, Svitolina came back with two decisive breaks in the third to seal the title on her third match point.

This is her 20th WTA singles title. That makes her the first Ukrainian player in the Open Era to reach 20 career WTA wins. Her clay final record stands at a perfect 8-0 — she has never lost a clay court final in her entire career.

What makes this week even more extraordinary is the quality of opposition she defeated. In four consecutive match days at the Foro Italico, Svitolina defeated No. 2 Elena Rybakina, No. 4 Iga Swiatek, and No. 3 Coco Gauff. Three of the top four players in the world — on clay — in less than a week.

At the trophy ceremony, she delivered words that meant more than tennis: "I also want to thank the people in Ukraine who support me, who watch, especially from bomb shelters. The last few weeks have been very difficult for Ukraine. I feel love from everyone. Thank you. Glory to Ukraine."

She heads to Roland Garros as the No. 7 seed and the in-form player on the WTA tour. Paris, watch out.

### STORY 2: ATP Rome Final — Sinner vs Ruud

**Long-Form Post**

THE BIGGEST FINAL IN JANNIK SINNER'S CAREER STARTS TODAY. 🏆

At the Foro Italico on Sunday, Sinner faces Casper Ruud for the ATP Rome title. A win today would complete the Career Golden Masters — winning every ATP Masters 1000 event at least once. Rome is the only one missing from his trophy cabinet.

Only Novak Djokovic has ever done it. If Sinner beats Ruud, he joins that company.

He arrives on a 33-match Masters 1000 winning streak — the longest in the history of the series. He is the first Italian man in the Open Era to reach back-to-back Rome finals. The entire Foro Italico will be behind him.

On the other side: Casper Ruud — in his fifth Rome final, having lost all four previous (2020, 2021, 2022, 2024). He also holds three Grand Slam runner-up finishes without a major. On Sunday, he comes in as the underdog — but he swept Darderi 6-1, 6-1 in the semifinals and has shown the form to compete.

"Try not to think about the big wave," Ruud said of facing Sinner. He knows what he's up against.

Match starts not before 5 PM local (11 AM ET). This is the one.

### STORY 5: Ruud's Rome drought

**Long-Form Post**

FIVE FINALS. STILL WAITING. 🎾

Casper Ruud has played more Rome finals than almost any player of his generation. He first reached the title match here in 2020. Then 2021. Then 2022. Then 2024. And today — 2026 — his fifth chance at a trophy that has always slipped away.

Beyond Rome, he holds three Grand Slam runner-up finishes without a major: Roland Garros 2022, US Open 2022, Wimbledon 2023. He is one of the best players in the world at reaching the last match of a tournament. The final step has not yet come.

Today, he faces Jannik Sinner — world No. 1, 33-match M1000 winning streak, 5-0 head-to-head against Ruud. The numbers all point against him.

"Try not to think about the big wave," Ruud said.

That line says everything. He knows what he's walking into. He's showing up anyway. Whatever happens today, that part of Casper Ruud's story is already written — and it says something admirable about the man.
