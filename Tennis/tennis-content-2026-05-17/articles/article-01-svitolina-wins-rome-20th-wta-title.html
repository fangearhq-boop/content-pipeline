Svitolina Wins Third Rome Title, Becomes First Ukrainian With 20 WTA Crowns

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

May 17, 2026

Elina Svitolina completed one of the most remarkable weeks in recent WTA history on Saturday, defeating Coco Gauff 6-4, 6-7(3), 6-2 to claim her third Italian Open title and her 20th WTA singles crown. The victory makes her the first Ukrainian player in the Open Era to reach 20 career WTA wins.

The final at the Foro Italico lasted two hours and 48 minutes — a thorough test that saw Gauff level the match with a composed second-set tiebreak before Svitolina seized control of the decider with two breaks of serve to seal the title on her third match point.

A Week That Rewrote the Record Books

Svitolina's path to the title required her to defeat the three highest-ranked opponents available across four consecutive match days. She dispatched No. 2 Elena Rybakina in the quarterfinals, eliminated No. 4 Iga Swiatek in a dramatic three-set semifinal, and then outlasted No. 3 Gauff in Saturday's final. It is the kind of gauntlet that would define any player's season.

Her clay final record now stands at a perfect 8-0. Svitolina has never lost a clay court final in her career. The statistics from Saturday's match underline her dominance: she won six breaks to Gauff's three, saved 14 of 17 break points, and won the total points count 127 to 115. Her second-serve points won rate of 50 percent — compared to Gauff's 38 percent — proved the decisive edge across the gruelling three sets.

Title Dedicated to Ukraine

At the trophy ceremony, Svitolina delivered words that carried meaning well beyond sport. "Definitely was an extremely tough battle," she said. "I'm very happy with the way I could handle my nerves, not only today but also throughout this tournament."

Then she paused to address something larger. "I also want to thank the people in Ukraine who support me, who watch, especially from bomb shelters," Svitolina said. "The last few weeks have been very difficult for Ukraine. I feel love from everyone. Thank you. Glory to Ukraine."

Svitolina has carried the weight of representing her country throughout her career, and moments like these — a major title dedication from the world's top clay clay court stage — underscore why she occupies a singular position in Ukrainian sport.

A Three-Peat Eight Years in the Making

Svitolina first won the Italian Open in 2017, then retained the title in 2018 before a lengthy gap during which she battled injury, motherhood, and personal challenge. Her return to the Rome throne in 2026 — eight years after her last title here — is a story of persistence that resonates far beyond tennis.

It is also her fifth WTA 1000-level title, placing her among the elite players who have performed consistently at the highest bracket below Grand Slams.

Roland Garros Awaits

Svitolina's Rome victory lifts her to the No. 7 seed at Roland Garros, where qualifying begins Monday May 18. She is the only top-10 woman to have won an outdoor clay title in the 2026 season — a distinction that makes her an unavoidable name in any serious French Open conversation.

Gauff, meanwhile, heads to Paris as the defending champion from 2023 and seeking to avenge Saturday's defeat. Swiatek, four-time Roland Garros champion, will regroup after her semifinal exit. The women's draw promises another two weeks of drama on the red clay of the Bois de Boulogne.

What's Next

Roland Garros qualifying begins Monday, May 18. The main draw ceremony takes place Thursday, May 21. Main draw action starts May 25. Svitolina arrives as the form player on the WTA tour, the Rome champion, and — for the first time in her career — the undisputed favourite heading into a Grand Slam clay court fortnight.
