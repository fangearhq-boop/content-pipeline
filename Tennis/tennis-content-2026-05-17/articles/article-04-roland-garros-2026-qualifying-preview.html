Roland Garros 2026: Qualifying Begins Monday as Paris Prepares for Its Grand Slam

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

May 17, 2026

With the Rome clay season closing on Sunday, the tennis calendar turns its full attention to Paris. Roland Garros qualifying begins Monday, May 18. The main draw ceremony is Thursday, May 21 at 2 PM local time. The main draw itself starts May 25. The French Open is no longer on the horizon — it is here.

Men's Field: Sinner's Year

Jannik Sinner enters Roland Garros as the world No. 1 and the sport's dominant force in 2026. If he wins the ATP Rome final today against Casper Ruud, he arrives in Paris as the Career Golden Masters holder — a player who has won every Masters 1000 event at least once, something only Novak Djokovic has previously achieved.

Carlos Alcaraz, who won Roland Garros in 2024, confirmed that he will not compete in 2026 due to a wrist injury. His absence transforms what would have been a compelling final rematch into a cleaner path for Sinner, who has never won the French Open. The men's draw has not seen a clearer favourite in years.

The wild cards add romance to the tournament: Stan Wawrinka receives an invitation for his 21st and final French Open. The 2015 Roland Garros champion — who delivered one of the most stunning upset victories in Grand Slam final history that year — returns to say farewell. Gael Monfils also receives a wild card in what may be his last appearance at the tournament he has called home for more than two decades.

Women's Field: Svitolina, Gauff, Swiatek, Sabalenka

Elina Svitolina arrives as the form player on the WTA tour. Her Rome title makes her the No. 7 seed and the only top-10 woman to win an outdoor clay title in 2026. She beat world Nos. 2, 3, and 4 across four days at the Foro Italico. She heads to Paris in a run of form that makes her one of the most dangerous players in the draw.

Coco Gauff defends the title she claimed in 2023. Despite losing Saturday's Rome final, she has shown the depth of game required to win in Paris. Iga Swiatek — four-time Roland Garros champion — will look to refocus after her Rome semifinal exit to Svitolina. Aryna Sabalenka, the world No. 1, arrives without an injury concern and with consistent form across the clay season.

The women's draw may be the most open in years. Svitolina's RG seeding as No. 7 gives her a realistic path to the quarterfinals without facing the top four seeds — where her clay final record of 8-0 could become the most important statistic in the tournament.

The Qualifying Stakes

Monday's qualifying draw begins 96 players' bids for the 16 spots reserved for qualifiers in the main draw. It is a brutal format — three wins required across three days — but qualifying has produced Grand Slam champions in the past. The players who emerge from these opening rounds will join a main draw that is historically loaded at the top.

What's Next

Qualifying runs Monday through Wednesday, May 18-20. The main draw ceremony is Thursday, May 21 at 2 PM Paris time. Main draw play begins May 25. For the players who have spent the clay swing competing across Madrid and Rome, the transition to Paris brings familiar red clay — and the most prestigious two weeks of the European swing.
