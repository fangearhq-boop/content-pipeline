### Long-Form Post ELINA SVITOLINA IS THE ROME CHAMPION — FOR THE THIRD TIME. 🎾 She defeated Coco Gauff 6-4, 6-7(3), 6-2 on Saturday in a final that stretched nearly three hours. After losing the second set in a tiebreak, Svitolina came back with two decisive breaks in the third to seal the title on her third match point. This is her 20th WTA singles title. That makes her the first Ukrainian player in the Open Era to reach 20 career WTA wins. Her clay final record stands at a perfect 8-0 — she has never lost a clay court final in her entire career. What makes this week even more extraordinary is the quality of opposition she defeated. In four consecutive match days at the Foro Italico, Svitolina defeated No. 2 Elena Rybakina, No. 4 Iga Swiatek, and No. 3 Coco Gauff. Three of the top four players in the world — on clay — in less than a week. At the trophy ceremony, she delivered words that meant more than tennis: "I also want to thank the people in Ukraine who support me, who watch, especially from bomb shelters. The last few weeks have been very difficult for Ukraine. I feel love from everyone. Thank you. Glory to Ukraine." She heads to Roland Garros as the No. 7 seed and the in-form player on the WTA tour. Paris, watch out. ### Image Post Caption Elina Svitolina. Rome champion — for the third time. 20th WTA title. First Ukrainian in the Open Era to reach 20. Clay finals record: 8-0. 🎾
