# Tennis Fanrecap — Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Personality

If Tennis Fanrecap were a person, they'd be **the tennis obsessive who stays up for the Australian Open, tracks every ranking point, and has opinions about every surface change** — the one who texts the group chat at 3 AM saying "Alcaraz just saved five match points in a fifth-set tiebreak" before anyone else even knows the match was on. They're knowledgeable, passionate, opinionated (with receipts from the stats), and genuinely love great tennis on every surface. They cover ATP and WTA with equal weight, follow the full global calendar, and never talk down to casual fans discovering the sport.

## Voice Attributes

### Knowledgeable
- **We are**: Tactically aware, historically informed, stat-literate. We understand ranking points, surface dynamics, serve percentages, and break-point conversion — and explain them accessibly.
- **We are not**: Gatekeeping, elitist, or drowning readers in numbers without context.
- **This sounds like**: "Sinner's 78% first-serve points won on hard court this season is elite — but his clay movement has improved dramatically since last year's Roland Garros."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Well, if you actually understood the ATP ranking algorithm with bonus points and mandatory events..."

### Passionate
- **We are**: Genuinely excited about great tennis — a five-set thriller, a first-time champion, a legendary rivalry match that delivers.
- **We are not**: Over-the-top, fake-hype, or resorting to empty superlatives every post.
- **This sounds like**: "Alcaraz just hit a between-the-legs winner on championship point. The kid plays tennis like he's trying to make a highlight reel every match."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "OMG BEST SHOT EVER!!! TENNIS IS THE GREATEST SPORT IN THE WORLD!!!!"

### Inclusive
- **We are**: Covering both ATP and WTA tours with equal energy and respect. Women's tennis gets the same depth, the same excitement, the same analysis.
- **We are not**: Treating WTA as a secondary tour or only covering it during Grand Slams.
- **This sounds like**: "Gauff's net game has transformed this season — she's winning 68% of net approaches, up from 52% last year."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "In women's tennis news..." (as if it's a sidebar)

### Global
- **We are**: Tennis is a worldwide sport and we cover all of it — ATP 250s in Marrakech, WTA events in Zhengzhou, Grand Slams in Melbourne.
- **We are not**: Only paying attention when tournaments happen in the US or when American players are involved.
- **This sounds like**: "The Halle grass court event produced three upsets today — including a qualifier knocking off the #4 seed."
- **This does NOT sound like**: (silence on everything between the US Open and the Australian Open)

### Opinionated
- **We are**: Willing to take a stance on GOAT debates, seedings, scheduling controversies, and surface changes — always backed by evidence.
- **We are not**: Reckless, trolling, or controversial for controversy's sake.
- **This sounds like**: "Hot take: the ATP scheduling is broken. Playing Indian Wells and Miami back-to-back gives top players zero recovery time and leads to withdrawals."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "The ATP is ruining tennis and everyone knows it."

### Respectful
- **We are**: Supportive of players and the sport. We critique performance, not character.
- **We are not**: Tearing down players personally, mocking retirements, or making attacks beyond the court.
- **This sounds like**: "Rublev went 2-for-12 on break points in a match he should have won. That's a brutal stat — he'll want that one back."
- **This does NOT sound like**: "Rublev choked AGAIN. Dude has no mental game."

## Platform-Specific Voice

### X/Twitter
- Enthusiastic, fast-paced, like live match commentary
- ALL CAPS for emphasis on key stats or moments (use sparingly, 1-2 per post)
- Dashes and periods for dramatic pacing: "Five sets. 4 hours 38 minutes. Down two sets to love. Comeback complete."
- Strong opinions backed by stats
- Max 3 hashtags per tweet
- #TennisFanrecap always included + 2 contextual hashtags (tournament-specific, player-specific)
- 1-3 emoji max per post (fire, tennis ball, trophy most common)

### Facebook
- More explanatory — assume the reader needs context
- Narrative storytelling across 3-4 paragraphs
- Opinionated but less aggressive than X
- One emoji per caption OK, no emojis in long-form posts
- No hashtags in Facebook posts
- End with a forward-looking statement or question to drive engagement
- Photo caption voice is punchier than long-form

### Articles
- Professional sports journalism tone
- Accessible to casual fans — explain context, don't assume knowledge
- Stats woven into narrative, not listed in isolation
- Always end with "What's Next" forward-looking section
- No first-person ("I think") — use "the numbers suggest" or just state the take
- Credit sources: "(per ATP Tour)" or "(via TennisAbstract)"

## Style Rules

### Grammar & Mechanics

| Rule | Tennis Fanrecap Standard | Example |
|------|--------------------------|---------|
| Oxford comma | Yes | "forehand, backhand, and serve" |
| Headings | Title Case | "Top 10 Players on Clay This Season" |
| Contractions | Yes, always | "he's" not "he is" |
| Numbers | Spell out 1-9, numerals 10+ | "three aces" but "15 break points" |
| Stats | Always numerals | "78% first serves in", "12 aces", "6-4, 3-6, 7-5" |
| Score format | Winner first, sets in sequence | "Djokovic d. Alcaraz 6-4, 3-6, 7-5" |
| Rankings | No. + number | "No. 1 Sinner" — never "#1" (creates a clickable hashtag on X) |
| Time format | 12-hour with timezone | "2:00 PM ET" |
| Match times | ET primary + local if relevant | "11:00 AM ET (5:00 PM local)" for European events |
| ALL CAPS | Sparingly for emphasis | "FIVE match points saved" — max 1-2 per post |
| Exclamation marks | OK in social, never in articles | Social: "What a rally!" Article: never |
| Emoji (X) | 1-3 max per post | Common: fire, tennis ball, trophy |
| Emoji (Facebook) | 1 max in captions | Never in long-form posts |
| Emoji (Articles) | Never | Articles are emoji-free |

### Terminology

| Use This | Not This | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Grand Slam | grand slam, Major | Capitalize; "Grand Slam" or "Slam" on subsequent mentions |
| Australian Open → AO | Aus Open | Full name first mention, then abbreviation |
| Roland Garros → RG | French Open | "Roland Garros" is the official name; "French Open" acceptable in casual X posts |
| Wimbledon | Wimby | Never abbreviate Wimbledon |
| US Open → USO | U.S. Open | No periods |
| ATP/WTA | mens/womens tour | Use the official tour names |
| break point | BP | Spell out in posts, abbreviate only in stat tables |
| tiebreak | tie-break, tie break | One word, no hyphen |
| match point | MP | Spell out in posts |
| retirement | retired hurt | "retired" is the official term for mid-match withdrawal |
| walkover | WO | Spell out in posts; "walkover" for pre-match withdrawal |
| first serve percentage | 1st serve % | Spell out in articles; "1st serve %" OK in social |
| surface names | court types | "hard court", "clay court", "grass court" — always specify the surface |
| surnames after first mention | full name every time | "Carlos Alcaraz" first, then "Alcaraz" |
| rankings with date | undated rankings | "Sinner (#1, as of March 17)" — always note the date |
