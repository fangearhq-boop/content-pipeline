de Minaur Opens at Queen's Club as Libema Champion Majchrzak Faces Lehecka in Day 2 Showdown

By Marcus Cole | June 16, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The HSBC Championships at Queen's Club in London — one of tennis's most historic ATP 500 events — enters Day 2 on Tuesday with two matches that capture the competitive tension at the top of the men's grass-court game. Top seed Alex de Minaur opens against big-serving Canadian Gabriel Diallo, while Libema Open champion Kamil Majchrzak faces No. 2 seed Jiri Lehecka in the most intriguing first-round draw of the day.

Majchrzak vs Lehecka: Momentum Meets Pedigree

This is the match that most warrants attention. Kamil Majchrzak won his first ATP Tour title just seven days ago at the Libema Open in 's-Hertogenbosch. He did so by defeating three consecutive top-10 players — No. 4 Felix Auger-Aliassime in the quarterfinals, No. 8 Daniil Medvedev in the semifinals, and No. 6 Alex de Minaur in the final. At 30 years old. After a 13-month suspension for contaminated supplements. After years of setbacks and ranking obscurity.

His opponent, Jiri Lehecka, is the No. 2 seed and reached the Queen's Club final last year. The Czech player is a dangerous grass-court performer who serves well and moves efficiently on the surface. He enters this tournament as a legitimate contender for the title — and as the most difficult possible opening draw for the newly crowned Libema champion.

Majchrzak has never won an ATP match at Queen's Club, while Lehecka knows this court as well as any in the ATP 500 calendar. Momentum is real, but tournament pedigree matters too. This is a first-round match with quarterfinal implications for the narrative of the week.

de Minaur vs Diallo: Top Seed, New Dynamic

Alex de Minaur arrives at Queen's Club as the top seed — a seeding that reflects his year-round ATP ranking rather than his recent form on grass. De Minaur lost the Libema final to Majchrzak last Sunday, and he comes to London looking to reset and establish momentum ahead of Wimbledon.

His opponent, Gabriel Diallo of Canada, is a big server capable of making life difficult for anyone on grass. A first-round upset here would reshaping the Queen's Club draw immediately. De Minaur's opening form — his movement, his return positioning, his willingness to attack the net — will tell observers how well he has adapted since his Libema final defeat.

Monday Context and the Depleted Draw

Day 1 saw American Tommy Paul defeat fellow American Zachary Svajda 7-5, 6-3, setting up his path into the second round. Cameron Norrie, a London crowd favourite, faces Alejandro Davidovich Fokina.

Notably absent from the draw: Carlos Alcaraz, who withdrew with a wrist injury, and Jack Draper, who has been managing a knee issue since Barcelona in April. Both withdrawals remove two of the most anticipated British and Spanish storylines from a tournament that typically draws significant public attention at Queen's Club. The path to the title is more open than it has been in several years.

What's Next

The HSBC Championships continue through Sunday, June 21. Second-round action begins Wednesday, with semifinals on Friday and the final on Sunday. The draw for Wimbledon takes place June 26; the Championships begin June 29. For players at Queen's Club this week, every match between now and Sunday directly shapes their grass-court form — and their seeding calculations — heading into the year's third Grand Slam.
