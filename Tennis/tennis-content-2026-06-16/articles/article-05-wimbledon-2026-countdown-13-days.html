Wimbledon 2026: 13 Days Out, Sinner Defending Without Warm-Up Events, Alcaraz Out

By Elena Voss | June 16, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Wimbledon 2026 begins in 13 days. The All England Club's draw ceremony takes place June 26, qualifying runs June 22-25, and the main draw opens Monday June 29. This week, as four simultaneous grass-court tournaments unfold across Europe, the picture of what awaits at SW19 is becoming clearer — and it is already defined by two large absences and one compelling question about the defending champion.

Sinner's Strategy: Trust the Prep

Jannik Sinner arrives at Wimbledon as the defending champion and No. 1 seed. He won the title in 2025, claiming his second Grand Slam after his 2024 Australian Open breakthrough. This year, he has made the unconventional choice to skip all ATP grass-court warm-up events — Halle, Queen's Club, and Stuttgart — and enter Wimbledon with only the Hurlingham Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic exhibition (June 23-27) as competitive preparation on grass.

It is a risk with precedent. Roger Federer frequently skipped Halle or Queen's Club in his prime and still won Wimbledon. Novak Djokovic did similarly in multiple winning years. Whether the strategy suits Sinner — whose movement, ball-striking, and baseline positioning are slightly different from either Federer or Djokovic — remains the central open question in men's tennis this month.

What Sinner does have is match fitness from a full clay-court swing, a No. 1 ranking that gives him the best possible seeding, and the memory of winning this title less than 12 months ago. His ranking lead over No. 2 Carlos Alcaraz remains substantial. He arrives at Wimbledon as a heavy favorite by default — which itself is a kind of competitive preparation.

Alcaraz: Out Entirely

The biggest news for Wimbledon is what is not happening. Carlos Alcaraz, the two-time former champion (2023, 2024) and the most explosive grass-court performer of the last several years, has withdrawn from Wimbledon due to a wrist injury. He also did not play at Queen's Club. His absence fundamentally reshapes the men's draw.

Alcaraz has been the defining force at Wimbledon for the past two years, with his combination of explosive power, extraordinary footwork, and net-rushing instincts fitting the fast London grass better than almost any player since Federer. Without him, the event loses its most obvious challenger to Sinner's title defense — and opens the door significantly for players like Ben Shelton, Alexander Zverev, and Daniil Medvedev.

The Contenders This Week

Shelton is in Halle this week, fresh off his Stuttgart title. Zverev is also in Halle as Roland Garros champion. Both men's results this week will inform their Wimbledon draws and seedings. On the women's side, Sabalenka — the No. 1 Wimbledon seed — is in Berlin alongside Rybakina and Gauff. The Berlin champion arrives at the All England Club with momentum.

Raducanu, the 2021 US Open champion, is the leading British women's hope after reaching the Queen's Club WTA final last week, where she fell to Donna Vekic.

The Numbers

Prize money for Wimbledon 2026 is £64.2 million — a record, and a 20 percent increase over 2025. That represents the largest year-on-year prize jump in the tournament's 149-year history. Singles champions in both draws will receive £3.6 million each.

The women's final is Sunday, July 11. The men's final is Sunday, July 12.

What's Next

Wimbledon qualifying runs June 22-25 at Roehampton. The main draw ceremony is June 26 at 10:00 BST, streamed live on Wimbledon's official channels. The Championships begin Monday, June 29, with first-round action across all 18 courts at the All England Club. This week's results at Halle, Queen's Club, Berlin, and Nottingham will shape the final seedings and provide the last competitive form data before the Grand Slam draw is set.
