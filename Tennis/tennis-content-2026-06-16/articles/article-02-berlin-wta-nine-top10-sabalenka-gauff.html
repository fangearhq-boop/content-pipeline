Nine of the World's Top 10 WTA Players Convene in Berlin — Sabalenka, Rybakina, Gauff Lead Historic Field

By Elena Voss | June 16, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Tennis Open — a WTA 500 event played at the Rot-Weiss Tennis Club in Berlin from June 15 to 21 — has assembled one of the most remarkable pre-Grand Slam fields in the modern era of women's tennis. Nine of the world's top 10 WTA players are competing in the singles draw, making this the deepest grass-court event of the pre-Wimbledon swing.

That number is not a marketing figure. It is a measure of how seriously the women's field is treating this week — and how the grass season has evolved as genuine preparation rather than an afterthought before the All England Club.

The Field

Aryna Sabalenka, ranked No. 1 in the world, is the top seed. She has a first-round bye and will enter the draw at the second round. Elena Rybakina, the 2022 Wimbledon champion and current world No. 2, is the No. 2 seed. She also has a first-round bye and will face the winner of the match between Donna Vekic — fresh off her Queen's Club WTA title last Sunday — and Alex Eala.

Coco Gauff, No. 3 in the world, opens against Barbora Bartunkova. Iga Swiatek is also in the field. Altogether, the draw features a collection of players who represent the full spectrum of modern WTA tennis — powerful baseliners, explosive serve-first players, and tactical all-court competitors.

The fact that Rybakina's potential second-round opponent is Vekic — the lucky loser who won Queen's Club WTA last week — adds an immediate storyline. Rybakina on grass is perhaps the most dangerous player on any surface when she is serving at full capacity. Vekic carries the momentum of a title won in remarkable circumstances. A second-round meeting between them would be one of the week's most anticipated matches regardless of the event's WTA 500 classification.

The Wimbledon Equation

Why does Berlin matter beyond its own trophy? Because Wimbledon starts in 13 days, and the winner of this tournament arrives at the All England Club as the form player in the world. That is not a small thing.

Sabalenka is the No. 1 Wimbledon seed. Rybakina won the title in 2022 and has been one of the most consistent grass-court performers since. Gauff is searching for her first Wimbledon title after several promising runs. All three will be playing competitive grass tennis this week, fine-tuning their games ahead of qualifying (June 22-25) and the main draw (beginning June 29).

Whoever gets through Berlin's draw — through a field this deep — should carry genuine confidence into SW19.

Serena in Doubles — Again

On the margins of the singles draw, Serena Williams continues her return to competitive tennis. She competed in doubles at the Queen's Club WTA last week with partner Victoria Mboko, reaching the quarterfinals before Mboko was forced to withdraw with a knee injury. The pair has moved to Berlin, where Serena continues her cautious re-engagement with tour competition.

Her presence in Berlin adds a layer of spectacle to an event that already needs none, but the doubles sideline should not overshadow the main event: a singles draw unlike anything the WTA has assembled at a 500-level grass event in recent memory.

What's Next

The Berlin Tennis Open runs through Sunday, June 21. Second-round matches begin as early as Wednesday, with the semifinals on Saturday and the final on Sunday. The winner of Berlin earns valuable ranking points and, more importantly, the clearest statement of form heading into Wimbledon. The Championships begin June 29; the women's final is July 11. The draw ceremony in London is June 26.
