Fernandez, Zheng, Ostapenko, and Boulter Open Grass Season in Nottingham

By Ryan Calloway | June 16, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

While Berlin hosts nine of the world's top 10 WTA players, a second WTA event is underway this week at the Nottingham Tennis Centre. The Lexus Nottingham Open — a WTA 250 played on outdoor grass courts — runs from June 15 to 21 and features a field with genuine depth ahead of Wimbledon.

Leylah Fernandez, Jelena Ostapenko, Zheng Qinwen, Karolina Pliskova, and British wild card Katie Boulter headline the draw. For players who did not qualify for or opt into the Berlin 500, Nottingham represents the most valuable pre-Wimbledon competitive opportunity on grass.

Why Nottingham Matters

The Lexus Nottingham Open occupies a specific niche in the WTA calendar. It is not the most prestigious event of the week, but the grass courts at Nottingham Tennis Centre are among the highest-quality playing surfaces outside of Wimbledon itself. Players who win here — or make deep runs — often carry that form directly into the All England Club draw.

Boulter is perhaps the most interesting player to watch this week. The British No. 3 received a wild card to ensure her place in the draw. She has become one of the stronger grass-court performers on the WTA Tour, and playing at home in Nottingham gives her a crowd advantage that can translate directly into a different kind of match aggression. She made a significant impression at Queen's Club WTA last week, defeating No. 8 seed Nadia Podoroska en route to the quarterfinals before falling to Vekic.

Ostapenko, whose flat, aggressive ball-striking is built for fast grass surfaces, has always been a credible grass threat regardless of her ranking. Fernandez — a multi-surface performer who reached the 2021 US Open final — is developing her grass game with each passing season. Zheng Qinwen, the current world No. 5, adds genuine star power to a draw that punches above its WTA 250 weight.

The Four-Tournament Week

This is one of the most unusual weeks on the tennis calendar. Four simultaneous tournaments are running: the Terra Wortmann Open (ATP, Halle), the HSBC Championships (ATP, Queen's Club), the Berlin Tennis Open (WTA, Berlin), and the Nottingham Open (WTA, Nottingham). All four are grass-court events. All four feed directly into Wimbledon seedings and form.

The scheduling creates real competitive fragmentation. Fans must track results across time zones and surfaces. For players, it means strategic decisions about which event best serves their Wimbledon preparation. For the tour itself, the simultaneous scheduling underscores how much has changed in the grass-court season's stature over the past decade.

What's Next

The Lexus Nottingham Open runs through Sunday, June 21. Second-round matches begin Wednesday; the final is Sunday. Points earned here count toward Wimbledon seedings, and match sharpness from Nottingham will carry directly into the All England Club. Wimbledon qualifying begins June 22; the main draw starts June 29.
