Fils vs Wawrinka: Roland Garros Gets Its Perfect Opening Week Story

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 22, 2026

Among the 128 first-round matches at Roland Garros 2026, one fixture stands apart from all the others: Arthur Fils against Stan Wawrinka. Not because of the seedings — neither player is seeded. Not because of the betting odds. Because of what the match means at this specific moment in time.

Fils is 24 years old. French. A crowd favourite at this tournament whose style — aggressive, physical, built for clay — has been developed with Roland Garros in mind. Wawrinka is 41. Swiss. A three-time Grand Slam champion who won this title in 2015, beating Novak Djokovic in the final. He enters on a wild card. It is his 21st and final French Open.

One of them will play their last Roland Garros match before Tuesday.

What Wawrinka Leaves Behind

Stan Wawrinka's relationship with Roland Garros is the story of a champion who understood exactly what he had to do on clay to beat the best players in the world. He won his French Open title in 2015 in the manner that defines his legacy — with a searing backhand, an ability to elevate in the biggest matches, and a refusal to be rattled. His win over Djokovic in the final was comprehensive. The two other Grand Slams he holds — the 2014 Australian Open and the 2016 US Open — complete a career that places him in the conversation of all-time greats.

He played a farewell tournament in Geneva last week, where his career highlights were shown on the big screen after his second-round exit. He has said that Paris carries a special weight: "There will be a lot of emotion because it's Paris and Roland-Garros. I hope I can win a few matches and above all enjoy the crowd."

What Fils Represents

Arthur Fils is one of the players who will inherit the space that Wawrinka, Monfils, Gasquet, and Tsonga are vacating. He is part of a generation of French players — alongside Ugo Humbert, Luca Van Assche, and Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard — who have made the ATP Tour feel young again for French tennis.

At Roland Garros, Fils plays with the energy of someone who grew up dreaming about this court. The Parisian crowd adopts French players in a particular way — fierce, loyal, and loud in moments of drama. Fils knows how to play into that. He reached the Miami Open semifinal earlier this season and has been one of the most dynamic players on the ATP Tour in recent months.

A first-round win against Wawrinka would be significant not because of the ranking points but because of the statement it makes about where French tennis is heading.

The Match Itself

Wawrinka at 41, on a wild card, with limited recent competitive play, is not the same Wawrinka who won this tournament in 2015. The gap in current form and physical preparation between these two players is real. Fils is favoured. But Wawrinka's career has repeatedly produced moments in major tournaments that defied expectations, and Roland Garros on a full Chatrier crowd is not a venue where he crumbles under pressure.

This match will be emotional regardless of the outcome. If Wawrinka wins, Paris will be on its feet for one of the great first-round upsets of recent years. If Fils wins, Paris will find a way to give Wawrinka the send-off he has earned while celebrating the player who beat him.

What's Next

Fils vs Wawrinka is scheduled for first-round play beginning Sunday, May 24. Roland Garros men's first-round matches continue through Tuesday, May 26. Stan Wawrinka's final Roland Garros chapter ends in these opening days. Arthur Fils, win or lose, begins a journey at this tournament that could extend for another decade.
