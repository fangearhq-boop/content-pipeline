Monfils and Svitolina Win 'Gaël & Friends' in an Unforgettable Paris Night

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 22, 2026

Court Philippe-Chatrier has seen historic matches, legendary champions, and tearful farewells. On Thursday night, it hosted something different: a party. Gael Monfils — 39 years old, retiring after the 2026 season, a Roland Garros icon for two decades — gathered his friends on the most famous clay court in the world and said goodbye the only way he knows how. With noise, colour, tennis, and music. And at the end of the night, Monfils and his wife, Elina Svitolina, won the mixed exhibition.

"Gaël & Friends" was everything the billing promised. The audience did not come for the score. They came for the man.

The Lineup That Packed Chatrier

The tennis participants would have filled any Grand Slam final with world-class talent. Jannik Sinner, the world No. 1 and Roland Garros favourite, was there. Novak Djokovic, a three-time champion who opens his main draw on Sunday, participated. Alexander Zverev, the No. 2 seed, played. So did Stefanos Tsitsipas, Naomi Osaka, rising French star Iva Jovic, and two of Monfils' French contemporaries — Richard Gasquet and Jo-Wilfried Tsonga.

The format was a mixed tournament, with serves permitted from both sides of the court. The result — Monfils and Svitolina winning — was almost secondary to what surrounded it.

Svitolina, who won the Rome WTA 1000 title earlier this month and enters Roland Garros as the No. 7 seed, was visibly emotional in the stands before the event began. She and Monfils have walked this journey together, and Thursday night was theirs.

Music, Charity, and the Full Paris Experience

The non-tennis elements of "Gaël & Friends" were a production in their own right. DJ Martin Solveig played a set. Rapper-dancer Franglish performed. Singer Matt Pokora co-hosted alongside comedian Paul de Saint-Sernin. The event was streamed live on the Roland Garros YouTube channel, bringing the night to fans around the world who could not be there in person.

Proceeds from the evening went to three charitable organisations: Terre d'Impact (the FFT's fundraising foundation), KELINA (founded by Flora Coquerel), and the Epic Foundation.

That Monfils and the organisers built a charity component into his farewell is entirely in keeping with the man. He has never been just a tennis player. He has been an entertainer, a philanthropist, and a personality who understood that sport at this level carries responsibilities beyond the court.

Two Decades of Monfils at Roland Garros

The French Open has been the central theatre of Gael Monfils' career. As a Parisian, playing at Roland Garros was always more than just another tournament. The crowd energy there — French, passionate, and fiercely loyal to their own — matched the way he played. Spectacular. Unpredictable. Rarely boring.

He reached the quarterfinals at Roland Garros six times over his career. He played shots that required replaying multiple times just to confirm what you had seen. He had the crowd on its feet for reasons that had nothing to do with winning or losing.

On Thursday night, he stood in the middle of Chatrier one final time. The people who love this sport showed up to send him off properly.

What's Next

Roland Garros 2026 main draw play begins Sunday, May 24 with first-round matches across the courts. Elina Svitolina enters the women's draw as the No. 7 seed after her Rome title. Gael Monfils, whose career has included a Roland Garros wild card entry this season in the main draw, will continue playing through the end of 2026 before retiring. He has already said goodbye to Roland Garros. Thursday night was the ceremony. The farewell itself is already complete.
