### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET Last night on Court Philippe-Chatrier, Gael Monfils got the farewell he deserved. "Gaël & Friends" — the exhibition and concert that has been planned for months — filled Roland Garros's most iconic stadium with the kind of night that the French capital does better than anywhere else in the world. Monfils, 39, retiring after a career that brought two decades of acrobatics, showmanship, and pure joy to the sport, shared the court with some of tennis's biggest names for one final Paris show. The result: Monfils and his wife, Elina Svitolina, won the mixed exhibition. But the result was almost beside the point. Jannik Sinner was there. Novak Djokovic was there. Alexander Zverev. Stefanos Tsitsipas. Naomi Osaka. Iva Jovic. Richard Gasquet. Jo-Wilfried Tsonga — two of the French legends who have walked this same path before Monfils. Martin Solveig DJed. Franglish performed. Matt Pokora and comedian Paul de Saint-Sernin co-hosted. Svitolina was visibly emotional before the night even began. Proceeds from the evening went to Terre d'Impact, KELINA, and the Epic Foundation. Monfils has been many things to Roland Garros — crowd favourite, showman, heartbreaker, icon. Last night, he was the guest of honour in the house he helped build. Roland Garros main draw begins Sunday. But before that, one final standing ovation for Gaël. What was your favourite Monfils moment at Roland Garros over the years? ---

Sinner's Career Grand Slam — The Path to Paris Glory
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 12:30 PM ET There is one Grand Slam left on Jannik Sinner's list. It is the one that starts Sunday. Roland Garros has been the only major missing from the world No. 1's collection. He has never won here — his best result is a quarterfinal in 2020. But the version of Sinner who arrives in Paris in 2026 is not the same player who has been to Paris before. He arrives on a 29-match winning streak. He has won five consecutive Masters 1000 titles — something no player in Open Era history has ever done before him. He completed the Career Golden Masters at Rome earlier this month, becoming only the second man to win every Masters 1000 event at least once. A Roland Garros title would make him the seventh man in Open Era history to complete the Career Grand Slam. He opens against French wild card Clément Tabur on Court Philippe-Chatrier on Sunday. Carlos Alcaraz — the two-time defending champion — is absent with a wrist injury. The draw, by most analysis, is the most favourable of Sinner's career at this tournament. None of that guarantees anything. Roland Garros is five sets, clay, and two weeks. But John McEnroe put it simply when previewing TNT's coverage: "To me, ultimately, it's Sinner against the field, and I'd take Sinner right now." Do you think Sinner completes the Career Grand Slam this year? Let us know below. ---

### Long-Form Post Window: 3:00 PM ET The 2026 Roland Garros women's draw may be the deepest in years — and none of the four top seeds is a safe bet. Here is the full picture of what the brackets mean. TOP HALF: Aryna Sabalenka (No. 1, 2025 finalist) and Coco Gauff (No. 4, defending champion) are in the same half and are projected to meet in the semifinals. Sabalenka has never won Roland Garros despite being one of the best players in the world. But she has also lost to players outside the top 50 in each of her last two tournaments — Hailey Baptiste in Madrid and Sorana Cirstea in Rome. Gauff opens against Taylor Townsend in an intriguing American first-round clash. BOTTOM HALF: Iga Swiatek (No. 3, four-time Roland Garros champion) and Elena Rybakina (No. 2, 2026 Australian Open champion) share the other half. Swiatek is the most decorated player in this tournament's recent history — but she faces a potential third-round meeting against Jelena Ostapenko, who leads their head-to-head 6-0. Rybakina, who won the Australian Open in January, has never gone past the quarterfinals here. Then there is Elina Svitolina at No. 7, the Rome champion who beat Rybakina, Swiatek, and Gauff to win the Foro Italico — the first player in Rome history to beat three top-4 seeds in one tournament. She has never won a Grand Slam. This is as wide-open as Roland Garros women's tennis gets. Pick your winner. ---

Djokovic's Long Shot — 25th Grand Slam on One Clay Match
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 6:00 PM ET Novak Djokovic has won Roland Garros three times. In 2016, 2021, and 2023. He has chased the 25th Grand Slam title that would take him further beyond every player in history. He arrives in Paris this week with one clay-court match in 2026 — a first-round loss to Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard at the Rome Masters in May. Between that match and today, Djokovic has been recovering from the right shoulder injury that forced him to skip Miami, Monte Carlo, and Madrid. He has been practicing in Paris this week. He says he is ready. His opening round is against Mpetshi Perricard — a rematch of his only 2026 clay appearance. Djokovic has done extraordinary things under difficult circumstances before. Three Roland Garros titles do not lie about what he is capable of on this surface. But even his most optimistic supporters would acknowledge that arriving to a Grand Slam with one clay match in your legs is not an ideal preparation. He is seeded No. 3 and placed in the opposite half from Sinner — which means the only way the two meet is in the final. "I'll be ready for Roland Garros," he said. Paris will find out soon enough. ---

Fils vs Wawrinka — A Roland Garros Farewell Opens in Round One
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 9:00 PM ET Every Roland Garros main draw produces a first-round match that stops you in your tracks. This year it is Fils vs Wawrinka. Arthur Fils is 24 years old, French, and one of the most exciting players of his generation. He grew up watching Roland Garros. Playing on Chatrier is, for him, what it should be for a Parisian tennis player — a homecoming. Stan Wawrinka is 41 years old, Swiss, and a three-time Grand Slam champion. He won Roland Garros in 2015, beating Novak Djokovic in the final. He also holds the Australian Open title (2014) and the US Open title (2016). He enters this French Open on a wild card. It is his 21st and final appearance at Roland Garros. These two players will face each other in round one. One of them walks off the court knowing their Paris career is over. Wawrinka said it clearly before arriving: "There will be a lot of emotion because it's Paris and Roland-Garros. I hope I can win a few matches and above all enjoy the crowd." Fils represents everything that Roland Garros is building toward. Wawrinka represents everything it has been. Whatever happens, it is the match of the first week. Who do you want to win? ---
