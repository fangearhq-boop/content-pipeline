Wimbledon 2026 Draw: Fritz vs Draper, Ruud vs Hurkacz Headline Blockbuster First Round as Sinner and Djokovic Land in Same Half

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | June 26, 2026

The Wimbledon 2026 singles draw ceremony took place Friday morning at 10:00 AM BST at the All England Club, and it delivered the kind of opening-round matchups that usually belong in the second week. Taylor Fritz, the No. 6 seed, will face British wildcard Jack Draper in the first round. Casper Ruud, the No. 11 seed, faces Hubert Hurkacz — two former major semifinalists who have both been to the Wimbledon last four — before the tournament is three days old. And defending champion Jannik Sinner has been placed in the same half of the draw as No. 7 seed Novak Djokovic, setting the stage for a potential semifinal between two of the sport's defining players.

The Championships begin Monday, June 29.

Fritz vs Draper: The Draw's Headline Clash

The Fritz–Draper first-round matchup is the blockbuster that will dominate the opening week conversation. Fritz, who withdrew from the Eastbourne International this week as defending champion in order to preserve his fitness ahead of Wimbledon, now opens against Draper — the British player who has been building through the grass swing and lost his Eastbourne semifinal to Ugo Humbert just hours before the draw was made.

The contrast could not be sharper. Fritz rested, fully recovered, and working from a week of deliberate preparation. Draper having played through a three-set semifinal the morning of the draw — then picking up his phone to find Fritz across the net at the All England Club. For British fans, the draw is simultaneously exciting and brutal. Draper represents genuine home hope on Centre Court. Fritz is one of the tour's best grass-court performers.

Ruud vs Hurkacz adds a second elite collision to the opening round. Hurkacz, a former Wimbledon semifinalist, arrives without a direct seeding this year but remains a formidable grass-court operator. Ruud, ranked No. 11, enters with a clay-court season behind him and grass results that have been quietly consistent. Their meeting in round one is the kind of match the draw can produce when the seedings do not reflect current form.

Top Half: Sinner and Djokovic Set for Potential Semifinal

Sinner, the defending champion and world No. 1, opens against Miomir Kecmanovic of Serbia. The Italian has an accessible path through the first week — his section of the draw does not include another top-ten player in the first four rounds if seedings hold. He skipped the full ATP grass swing and played only an exhibition at Hurlingham; the question heading into Wimbledon has always been his readiness, not his ranking.

Djokovic, the seven-time Wimbledon champion seeded seventh, opens against Wu Yibing of China and carries more questions than any player in the top ten. The Serb has played just 13 matches in 2026, his last competitive match a third-round Roland Garros loss to Joao Fonseca on May 29. He was seen receiving back treatment at the All England Club in the days before the draw, and his Wimbledon preparation has been largely invisible. Djokovic has historically risen to the occasion on this surface regardless of what preceded it — but this year is a different test.

Because they are in the same top half, Sinner and Djokovic can only meet in the semifinal. That means the headline title match of every Wimbledon conversation — a Sinner/Djokovic match that would carry the weight of a generational passing-of-the-torch narrative — is now a structural possibility at the semifinal stage.

Zverev Leads the Bottom Half

Alexander Zverev, the Roland Garros champion and No. 2 seed, anchors the bottom half of the men's draw with a first-round match against Belgian Alexander Blockx. Zverev chasing back-to-back Grand Slam titles after his Roland Garros victory represents one of the most compelling stories heading into the fortnight. He and Sinner are on opposite halves and can only meet in the final — the match the entire tournament is building toward.

Ben Shelton (No. 4) and Alex de Minaur (No. 5) are also in the bottom half, adding depth to what could be a competitive route for Zverev. Felix Auger-Aliassime (No. 3) provides a wildcard in terms of grass-court expectations after his clay-court season.

What's Next

The Wimbledon 2026 Championships begin Monday, June 29. First-round play runs June 29–30, with matches across all courts at the All England Club. The women's draw — which produced its own headline matchup, with Iga Swiatek and Serena Williams landing in the same quarter — was also revealed Friday morning. The singles finals are scheduled for Saturday July 11 (women's) and Sunday July 12 (men's). Prize money this year is a record £64.2 million, with £3.6 million going to each singles champion.
