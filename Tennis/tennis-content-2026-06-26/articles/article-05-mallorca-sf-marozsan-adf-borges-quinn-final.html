Mallorca Semifinals: Marozsan Stuns ADF to Set Up First Grass Final Against Borges on Saturday

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 26, 2026

The Vanda Pharmaceuticals Mallorca Championships ATP 250 produced a clear result from its two semifinals on Friday: Fabian Marozsan and Nuno Borges will contest Saturday's final at the Mallorca Country Club. Marozsan, the Hungarian who had never previously reached an ATP grass-court final, defeated No. 2 seed Alejandro Davidovich Fokina 6-4, 6-3 in a performance that left no room for ADF to construct the kind of points that had carried him through the draw. Borges, the Portuguese player who had earlier ended No. 1 seed Luciano Darderi's campaign, followed up that quarterfinal win by beating American Ethan Quinn 7-6(1), 6-4.

The final is Saturday, June 27, two days before Wimbledon begins.

Marozsan's Defining Day on Grass

Fabian Marozsan's win over Davidovich Fokina was the day's standout result. ADF had been in solid form at Mallorca — his quarterfinal dispatch of Wimbledon wildcard Grigor Dimitrov 6-3, 6-3 was as comfortable as the score suggests — and entered the semifinal as the more grass-court experienced player. Marozsan swept through him in two straightforward sets, 6-4, 6-3, and the scoreline understated the control he exercised throughout.

Marozsan, who has built his reputation primarily on clay, is reaching into new territory here. A first grass-court final, in the week before Wimbledon, is exactly the kind of breakthrough result that can change a player's understanding of what they are capable of on a surface they had previously viewed as secondary. It is a career milestone in every respect.

Borges Holds Firm Against Quinn

Borges had the more pressured semifinal of the two. His match against Quinn went the distance of the first set before the Portuguese player closed it out 7-1 in the tiebreak — a dominant tiebreak score that came after a competitive set that could have gone either way. The second set, 6-4, was cleaner. Borges has been one of the week's standout performers, having beaten Darderi, the top seed and one of the most in-form clay-court players on the tour, in the quarterfinals.

For Borges, reaching the final is confirmation of a grass game that has developed significantly in recent years. He enters Saturday's final as the player with the more notable draw scalp to his name — beating a top seed is the kind of result that earns respect regardless of what follows.

Saturday: First Grass Title Up for Grabs

The Mallorca final pits two players who have never previously won an ATP title on grass against each other. One of them will have their first by Saturday evening. The match carries genuine intrigue beyond the scoreline, because both players — Marozsan with his upset of ADF, Borges with his victory over Darderi — have proven this week that they can win against quality opposition on the surface.

Frances Tiafoe, the third seed and Halle champion, did not advance to the final stages. The Mallorca field was disrupted by Fritz's Eastbourne withdrawal, which did not directly affect Mallorca, but the week has produced exactly the kind of competitive tennis that shows the depth of the grass-court game beyond the top seeds.

What's Next

The Mallorca Championships final is Saturday, June 27, at the Mallorca Country Club. The winner collects the title and 250 ATP ranking points. Wimbledon begins Monday, June 29, where both Marozsan and Borges have first-round matches to navigate. Both arrive with the best possible grass-court momentum heading into the year's third Grand Slam.
