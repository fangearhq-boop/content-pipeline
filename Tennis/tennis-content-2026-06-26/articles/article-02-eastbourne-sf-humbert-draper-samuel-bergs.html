Eastbourne Semifinals: Humbert Edges Draper in Three Sets, Samuel Through — Final is Saturday

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 26, 2026

Jack Draper's week in Eastbourne ended in a closely fought three-set loss to Ugo Humbert on Friday, and the day managed to get harder from there. Within hours of his semifinal defeat, the Wimbledon 2026 draw was made — and Draper found himself drawn against No. 6 seed Taylor Fritz in the first round at the All England Club. For a player who had been building genuine grass-court momentum, the combination of a three-set exit and a tough draw arrival was a difficult Friday in British tennis.

Toby Samuel was the day's British bright spot. The wildcard defeated Zizou Bergs of Belgium 6-3, 6-4 in the other semifinal to book his place in Saturday's Eastbourne final. The match went to Samuel's strengths — clean serving, composed groundstrokes — and Bergs, who had put together a solid week in the draw, found no way back.

Humbert Proves Steadier Over Three Sets

The Draper–Humbert semifinal ran the full distance of three sets, and it underlined how competitive Draper has been this week even in defeat. He took the second set after dropping the first, showing the capacity to regroup mid-match that has become a feature of his grass-court performances. But Humbert, the French player who has quietly put together an excellent week at Devonshire Park, found his way through in the third.

For Humbert, it is a significant result. Reaching an ATP final on grass — with a Wimbledon draw now made, and the Championships just two days away — gives him the confidence of playing himself into form at exactly the right moment. He will contest Saturday's final against Samuel.

For Draper, the defeat is frustrating but not defining. He has played competitive, three-set tennis in his semifinals and given a credible account of himself throughout the week. His first-round Wimbledon draw against Fritz will be the harder challenge — and he goes into it knowing he can compete at the level required.

Samuel's Week Continues

Toby Samuel's run at Eastbourne has been the surprise of the draw since Fritz withdrew. The British wildcard has taken the opportunity created by that withdrawal and converted it into a final — a genuinely commendable result for a player who entered as an outsider. His 6-3, 6-4 defeat of Bergs was calm and authoritative, with no drama required to finish off the Belgian.

Samuel will enter Saturday's final as the underdog against Humbert, who is a more established player at this level. But a wildcard reaching the final of an ATP 250 event in the week before Wimbledon is itself a significant achievement, regardless of what happens on Saturday.

What's Next

The Lexus Eastbourne Open final takes place Saturday, June 27, at Devonshire Park — Humbert vs Samuel. With Wimbledon starting Monday, June 29, the winner will lift a trophy and head straight into first-round preparation at the All England Club. Draper's next match is his Wimbledon first round against Taylor Fritz; no date has been confirmed, but opening-round play begins June 29.
