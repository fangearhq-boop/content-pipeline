### STORY 1: Wimbledon 2026 Draw Revealed

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

The Wimbledon 2026 singles draw is out, and it delivered exactly the chaos tennis fans were hoping for. 🎾

Taylor Fritz (No. 6 seed) vs. Jack Draper in the first round. That's not a quarterfinal or a semifinal — that's round one at the All England Club. Draper just exited the Eastbourne semifinals this morning, then walked into the draw ceremony and found Fritz waiting. Fritz, who skipped Eastbourne entirely to preserve his fitness, now faces the British crowd favourite in what could be one of the loudest matches of opening week.

Also in round one: No. 11 seed Casper Ruud against Hubert Hurkacz. These are two former semifinalists on this surface — the kind of match that would headline week two in any other year, now happening in the first round.

Defending champion Jannik Sinner (No. 1) faces Miomir Kecmanovic and navigates a manageable opening section. But here's the big structural story: Sinner and Novak Djokovic (No. 7, who faces Wu Yibing in round one) have landed in the same top half of the draw. That means their potential collision point is the semifinal. Alexander Zverev, the Roland Garros champion and No. 2 seed, leads the bottom half and can only meet Sinner in the final.

The Championships begin Monday, June 29. Centre Court will be loud from Day 1.

### STORY 2: Eastbourne SFs — Humbert Beats Draper, Samuel Through

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

It was a tough day for Jack Draper in Eastbourne — and by lunchtime, it had gotten tougher. 🎾

Draper lost his semifinal to Ugo Humbert in three sets at Devonshire Park, a gutting result for the British player who had been building momentum through the week. It was a competitive match — Draper took the second set — but Humbert proved the steadier player down the stretch.

Then came the Wimbledon draw.

Draper's first-round opponent? Taylor Fritz. The No. 6 seed. The man who withdrew from Eastbourne as defending champion this week to rest up before the Championships. Draper has now played a three-set SF and then been handed arguably the toughest possible opening-round draw for a British player at Wimbledon.

The other semifinal went Toby Samuel's way — the British wildcard dominated Zizou Bergs 6-3, 6-4 to book his spot in the Eastbourne final. Samuel vs Humbert is Saturday, with the winner collecting the title two days before Wimbledon begins.

It's been a great week for Toby Samuel. For Jack Draper, the real competition is just getting started.

Who wins Saturday — Humbert or Samuel?

### STORY 3: Bad Homburg WTA SFs — Osaka Chases First Grass Final

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

One of tennis's most intriguing storylines is playing out at the Bad Homburg Open right now, and it centres on Naomi Osaka. 🎾

Osaka has reached the semifinals — something she has not done at a WTA grass-court event in seven years. She beat Ekaterina Alexandrova 6-2, 6-2 in the quarterfinals without dropping a service game, and is now one win away from her first career WTA grass-court final.

Standing between Osaka and that final is Wang Xinyu, the Chinese player who advanced by walkover after Elina Svitolina withdrew with a right hip injury. That walkover means Xinyu arrives at the semifinal rested — a factor Osaka's team will have to account for.

In the other semifinal, one of the tournament's surprise packages, Elena-Gabriela Ruse (world No. 105), takes on No. 11 seed Karolina Muchova. Ruse beat Emma Navarro in the quarterfinals — Navarro, who had just knocked out top seed Iga Swiatek — so this is no fluke. Muchova is the class in the match on paper, but Ruse has been playing with real confidence all week.

The Bad Homburg final is Saturday June 27, with Wimbledon starting just two days later. A title here would be the perfect preparation.

### STORY 4: Wimbledon Women's Draw — Serena vs Swiatek in Same Quarter

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

The Wimbledon 2026 women's draw has produced one of the most tantalising potential third-round matchups the tournament has seen in years. 🎾

Defending champion Iga Swiatek (No. 3 seed) has been drawn to face Taylor Townsend in the first round. In the same quarter of the draw? Serena Williams, who received the final women's wild card and plays Maya Joint in her opening match. If both win their first two matches, Swiatek and Williams will meet in the third round on Centre Court. That would be a 44-year-old making her singles comeback against the defending Wimbledon champion. The potential audience for that match is difficult to overstate.

Elsewhere: No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka opens against Tena Kostovic, No. 2 Elena Rybakina (the 2022 champion) faces Loïs Boisson, and No. 5 Mirra Andreeva draws Magda Linette. Andreeva arrives as Roland Garros champion with a historically rare opportunity: the Roland Garros/Wimbledon double, which no woman has completed since Justine Henin in 2006. She is entering as a completely different player from the one who didn't win a Wimbledon match last year.

Coco Gauff (No. 7) opens against Tamara Korpatsch. Emma Raducanu could face Sabalenka in the third round if both advance.

The Championships begin June 29. Who do you think wins the women's title? 👇

### STORY 5: Mallorca SFs — Marozsan to Meet Borges in Saturday Final

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

The Mallorca Championships delivered two proper semifinal results on Friday — and the headline is Fabian Marozsan. 🎾

The Hungarian, who arrived in Mallorca without a grass-court title to his name, dismantled No. 2 seed Alejandro Davidovich Fokina 6-4, 6-3. ADF had beaten Grigor Dimitrov in the quarterfinals and came in with form on his side. Marozsan didn't care. He was dominant, efficient, and is now set for the first ATP grass-court final of his career.

On the other side of the draw, Nuno Borges continued the run that saw him beat No. 1 seed Luciano Darderi in the quarterfinals. The Portuguese player came through Ethan Quinn 7-6(1), 6-4 — the tiebreak was a statement, closing it out 7-1 — to book his place in the final.

The Mallorca final is Saturday June 27 at the Mallorca Country Club. The winner lifts a trophy, earns ranking points, and arrives at Wimbledon — which begins Monday June 29 — in exactly the form they were looking for.

Two players chasing their first title on grass. One of them is getting it on Saturday.

Who wins — Marozsan or Borges?
