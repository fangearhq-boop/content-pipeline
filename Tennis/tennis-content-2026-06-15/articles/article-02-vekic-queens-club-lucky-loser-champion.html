From Lucky Loser to Champion: Vekic Stuns Raducanu to Win Queen's Club

By Marcus Cole | June 15, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Donna Vekic did not qualify for the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club. She lost in qualifying. She entered the main draw only because Marta Kostyuk withdrew before the tournament began. Then she won the whole thing.

On Sunday, Vekic defeated home favourite Emma Raducanu 6-0, 7-6(6) to claim her fifth career WTA title and first WTA 500 crown — completing one of the most improbable runs in the tournament's recent history. The Croatian will rise from No. 76 to No. 32 in the rankings on the back of this result.

The Final: Clinical Start, Tense Finish

The opening set offered no indication of a contest. Vekic struck with precision and power, earning a bagel — 6-0 — against a Raducanu who had been brilliant all week. The speed of it was jarring; Raducanu had not dropped a set in the entire tournament before Sunday's final.

The second set was a different story. Raducanu came back with urgency, forced a tiebreak, and managed to save two championship points as the tension mounted. But Vekic's nerve held at the critical moment. She closed out the tiebreak 7-6 to win 6-0, 7-6(6) and seal the title.

For Vekic, it is also a historically significant win: she becomes the first Croatian women's singles champion at Queen's Club. The only other Croatian to win a title at the West London venue was Marin Cilic, in 2018 on the men's side.

Raducanu's Brilliant and Heartbreaking Week

Whatever disappointment Raducanu feels from Sunday cannot erase what was a superb week. She played double duty on Saturday — a quarterfinal in the morning, a semifinal in the afternoon — and emerged without dropping a set across five matches. She reached the final of a WTA 500 event on home soil and pushed the champion to a tiebreak in the second set.

She is still waiting for her first title since the 2021 US Open. That wait extends, but the form heading into Wimbledon gives genuine reason for optimism. Raducanu leads the British charge at SW19, where she will be a crowd favourite from the first round. A Queen's final without the title still represents a significant step forward for a player who has been carefully rebuilding across the past few years.

Vekic's Remarkable Journey Through the Draw

The lucky loser path is one of the harder roads in professional tennis. A player who enters via a withdrawal must produce every match without the confidence of having qualified in the normal bracket. Vekic not only did that — she did it with a level of tennis that overwhelmed almost everyone she faced.

In the semifinal, she defeated Katie Boulter 6-1, 6-3, ending the British wildcard's remarkable run. Boulter had earlier beaten world No. 2 Elena Rybakina — and Vekic still handled her with authority. That's a form line which is difficult to argue with.

Her grass-court record has always been underappreciated. This title is a reminder that she is a genuine threat on the surface heading into Wimbledon.

What's Next

Vekic will carry her WTA 500 title and new No. 32 ranking into the Wimbledon draw, which takes place June 26. The Championships begin June 29. Raducanu, despite Sunday's loss, will be among the most closely watched players in the women's draw at SW19 — particularly after a week in London that showed she belongs at the very top level of the grass-court game. Two very different stories heading into the year's third Grand Slam; both worth following closely.
