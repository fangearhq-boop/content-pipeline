Shelton Rises to No. 5, Majchrzak Enters Top 50 After Grass Week 1

By Elena Voss | June 15, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The first week of the 2026 grass-court season produced three champions across three simultaneous ATP and WTA events — and Monday's rankings update reflects the rewarding aftermath. Ben Shelton rises to No. 5, Kamil Majchrzak makes his Top 50 debut at No. 47, and the Wimbledon seedings picture comes into sharper focus with the draw set for June 26.

Shelton: No. 5 and Closing In

Shelton's Stuttgart title moves him to No. 5 in the PIF ATP Rankings, now 320 points behind No. 4 Felix Auger-Aliassime. He joins Jannik Sinner as the only ATP players with three singles titles in 2026 — Dallas (hard), Munich (clay), and Stuttgart (grass). Each surface, one champion.

His rise to No. 5 is significant for Wimbledon seeding purposes. A top-5 ranking guarantees a seeding that should keep him on the favoured side of the draw and avoid the top contenders in the early rounds. With Sinner at No. 1 and Alcaraz (No. 2) absent due to a wrist injury, a seeded Shelton arriving in the best grass-court form of his career is a genuine Grand Slam threat.

Majchrzak: Top 50 Debut at No. 47

Kamil Majchrzak's Libema Open title at 's-Hertogenbosch pushes him up 29 places to a career-high No. 47 — the first time he has been ranked inside the Top 50. For a player who served a 13-month suspension in 2023-24 and spent years rebuilding, a Top 50 ranking represents something more than a number. It is the first concrete evidence that the rebuild is complete.

At No. 47, Majchrzak is in position to enter the Wimbledon main draw as a direct acceptance and may earn a seeding if his ranking holds and others withdraw. Given what he did to No. 4 Auger-Aliassime, No. 8 Medvedev, and No. 6 de Minaur this past week, any draw in which Majchrzak appears deserves attention.

The Top of the Rankings: Sinner Stable, Alcaraz Holds

Jannik Sinner holds firm at No. 1 with more than 13,500 ranking points — a lead that remains substantial despite his absence from the grass warm-up circuit. Carlos Alcaraz stays at No. 2 despite his Wimbledon withdrawal; his clay-season points keep him well ahead of the chasing pack. Felix Auger-Aliassime remains No. 4 despite his Stuttgart semifinal exit.

The rankings also reflect some movement further down. Players who failed to defend points from the 2025 grass-court season drop, while Shelton and Majchrzak gain. With the Wimbledon draw determining the bracket for the year's third Grand Slam, Monday's updated rankings are effectively the first draft of who meets whom at SW19.

What's Next

The Wimbledon qualifying rounds run June 22-25, with the main draw ceremony taking place on June 26. The Championships begin June 29. For Shelton, the rankings rise to No. 5 locks in a favourable seeding and a place among the names the draw will be built around. For Majchrzak at No. 47, the Top 50 debut is the prelude to what could be a historic first Wimbledon main draw appearance as an in-form grass-court champion. The rankings will be finalized after qualifying completes — but this Monday snapshot already tells a compelling story of who is rising at exactly the right time.
