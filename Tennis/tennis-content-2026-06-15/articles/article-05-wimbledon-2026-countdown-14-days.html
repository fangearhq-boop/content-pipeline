14 Days to Wimbledon: Form Guide Set, Sinner Defends, Alcaraz Out

By Marcus Cole | June 15, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Three grass-court tournaments have concluded. Three champions have been crowned. The Wimbledon picture — 14 days out — is coming into focus, and it looks very different from what most expected heading into June.

The Championships begin June 29. The draw takes place June 26 at 11:00 AM BST. Qualifying runs June 22-25. Here is where things stand.

Who's Hot

Ben Shelton arrives at SW19 as the form player of the grass-court season. Three titles on three surfaces in 2026 — the Stuttgart title completing a set of hard, clay, and grass — with three match points saved along the way. He enters Wimbledon at No. 5 in the ATP rankings and riding the kind of confidence that only comes from winning under pressure on a surface you've never previously won on.

Kamil Majchrzak, who beat three consecutive top-10 opponents at 's-Hertogenbosch to win his first ATP title, is another name the draw will need to take seriously. His ranking of No. 47 may earn him a seeding depending on withdrawals. The grass form is real.

Donna Vekic won Queen's Club as a lucky loser — a feat that requires genuine excellence to complete. She enters Wimbledon with a new ranking of No. 32 and the psychological advantage of someone who just won a WTA 500 title under the most improbable circumstances.

Who's Defending — and Who's Missing

Jannik Sinner defends the men's title as the unquestioned world No. 1. Remarkably, he arrives having skipped every ATP grass warm-up event — his only competitive grass preparation is the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at the Hurlingham Club (June 23-27), a private exhibition. In 2025, he won Wimbledon. In 2026, he approaches the same goal by backing confidence in his own game rather than tournament matches. Whether that approach works is one of the tournament's central questions.

Carlos Alcaraz, the two-time Wimbledon champion and No. 2 in the world, will not be at SW19. A wrist injury that kept him out of Roland Garros has also ended his Wimbledon campaign before it began. His absence removes one of the most dangerous players on grass from the draw entirely.

Iga Swiatek defends the women's title. Mirra Andreeva, who won Roland Garros 2026, arrives as the new French Open champion but without grass points to defend — making her a somewhat unpredictable factor in the women's half.

What's New at Wimbledon 2026

Two notable changes arrive at the All England Club this year. Video Review technology makes its Wimbledon debut — automated line-calling will now be in use on all courts, ending the era of human line judges at the most famous tournament in the sport. The transition follows years of similar adoption at other Grand Slams and removes one of tennis's most distinctive (and debated) features.

The prize money is also a record. The 2026 Wimbledon prize fund reaches £64.2 million — a 20 percent increase from the previous year and the largest in the tournament's 149-year history. Each singles champion will earn £3.6 million.

What's Next

Wimbledon qualifying begins June 22 at the Bank of England Sports Ground and the Roehampton Club. The main draw ceremony is June 26 at 11:00 AM BST — live-streamed on official Wimbledon channels. The Championships open June 29 on all courts, with Centre Court and Court 1 action from Day 1. The men's final is July 12; the women's final July 11. After a grass-court Week 1 that rewrote the form guide, the fortnight ahead promises the kind of tennis that only Wimbledon can deliver.
