Majchrzak Beats Three Top-10 Players to Win First ATP Title at 30

By Ryan Calloway | June 15, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Kamil Majchrzak won his first ATP Tour title on Sunday in 's-Hertogenbosch — at 30 years old, after a career that included serious injuries, a 13-month doping suspension, and years of hovering just outside the sport's elite tier. The Polish veteran defeated Alex de Minaur 6-3, 2-6, 7-6(5) to claim the Libema Open crown, completing one of the more extraordinary weeks the ATP grass-court season has seen in years.

Three Top-10 Scalps in Seven Days

Majchrzak's path through the draw at 's-Hertogenbosch was not a lucky bracket. Starting from No. 76 in the ATP rankings, he produced the following results:

Quarterfinal: defeated No. 4 Felix Auger-Aliassime, 6-4, 6-3. Auger-Aliassime, the top seed, had zero break points in the match — Majchrzak was simply the better player across 69 minutes.

Semifinal: defeated No. 8 Daniil Medvedev, 7-6(4), 6-1. In a match that opened in a tiebreak, Majchrzak found his rhythm and overwhelmed the Russian in the second set.

Final: defeated No. 6 Alex de Minaur, 6-3, 2-6, 7-6(5). De Minaur, the 2024 defending champion, pushed the match to a deciding tiebreak before Majchrzak closed it out.

Three consecutive top-10 opponents. Three wins. The numbers are not a coincidence — they are a portrait of a player who peaked at precisely the right moment.

A Career Built on Persistence

Majchrzak's story is one that the sport rarely puts in the spotlight. Born in 1996, he has spent most of his career on the fringes of the Top 100 — good enough to challenge the best, not consistently enough to break through and stay there. Injuries cost him years of development. And in 2023-24, a doping violation for contaminated supplements resulted in a 13-month ban — a period that would have ended many careers.

He came back. He kept winning points on smaller circuits. He climbed back into contention. And on Sunday in 's-Hertogenbosch, all of it paid off.

"It's been unbelievable," Majchrzak said after the win. That might be the year's greatest understatement.

Polish Tennis History

The title makes Majchrzak only the third Polish man to win an ATP Tour title in the Open Era (since 1968). He joins Wojtek Fibak — who won 15 career titles in the 1970s and 1980s — and current top-25 player Hubert Hurkacz, who has been Poland's flag-bearer on tour since 2019. Majchrzak's title, won on grass at 30, carries a different weight than a player arriving at the top when young. He earned this one the hard way.

His ranking climbs to a career-high No. 47 — a jump of 29 places — giving him his first-ever appearance in the ATP Top 50.

What's Next

Majchrzak will now prepare for Wimbledon, which begins June 29 at the All England Club. His new ranking of No. 47 puts him in position to enter the main draw directly, and his grass-court form from 's-Hertogenbosch gives him legitimate standing as a potential upset threat at SW19. The Wimbledon draw takes place June 26. For a player who has spent much of his career grinding his way back, making it to the starting line at Wimbledon in the week he finally won an ATP title would be a story worth following closely.
