Shelton Dethrones Fritz for Stuttgart Title and First Grass-Court Crown

By Elena Voss | June 15, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Ben Shelton's 2026 season just got more remarkable. On Sunday in Stuttgart, the 23-year-old American defeated defending champion Taylor Fritz 6-4, 2-6, 6-4 to claim the BOSS Open title — the first grass-court crown of his career and his sixth ATP title overall.

This is Shelton's third title of 2026, following his hard-court victory in Dallas in February and his clay-court win in Munich in April. Three titles, three surfaces, one season. That achievement places him in extraordinarily rare company.

Historic Multi-Surface Achievement

Shelton becomes the first American man to win titles on hard court, clay, and grass in a single calendar year since Sam Querrey accomplished the feat in 2010. Globally, he joins Carlos Alcaraz, Novak Djokovic, and Alexander Bublik as the only players in the current decade to win tour-level titles on all three surfaces within a single season.

The achievement also reinforces Shelton's emergence as one of the most complete players in the game. His early career was defined by hard-court dominance and a booming left-handed serve — but Stuttgart confirmed that he has developed the net game, movement, and adaptability to win on any surface.

A Week That Required Everything

The path to the title was not straightforward. Shelton saved a match point in the second round before recovering to advance. Then in the semifinal against Jiri Lehecka, he found himself two match points down before clawing his way back in a 6-7(4), 7-6(14), 7-6(6) marathon that lasted nearly three hours. The second-set tiebreak alone went 16-14 — among the longest of 2026.

Carrying that weight into Sunday's final, Shelton was sharp where it mattered. He converted nine of 11 break points against Fritz and closed out the match with the calm of someone who had already been tested far harder earlier in the week. "Once you've saved match points in a semifinal, a final feels like a different kind of pressure," one could imagine him saying. His performance said as much.

Fritz, for his part, came in well-rested after a clinical 6-4, 6-4 win over Alexander Bublik in the semis. The defending champion served well throughout but could not match Shelton's intensity in the decisive third set, where Shelton broke in the fifth game and never looked back.

Wimbledon Implications

Shelton moves to No. 5 in the ATP rankings, now 320 points behind No. 4 Felix Auger-Aliassime. With three 2026 titles, he is tied with Jannik Sinner for the most on tour this season. Heading into Wimbledon, which begins June 29, Shelton is the player in the hottest form on grass — and arguably on any surface.

Sinner, the No. 1 seed and defending Wimbledon champion, has skipped all ATP grass events and is preparing with only the Hurlingham exhibition. Carlos Alcaraz has withdrawn with a wrist injury. That opens the door for a player with Shelton's current momentum.

The Wimbledon draw takes place June 26. A week ago, Shelton was a strong contender. After Stuttgart, he's arguably the man to beat.

What's Next

Shelton is expected to travel to London ahead of Wimbledon qualifying week (June 22-25) and the draw ceremony on June 26. The Championships begin June 29 at the All England Club. With three titles across three different surfaces in 2026, Shelton arrives at SW19 as the form player of the grass-court season — and one of the most compelling Wimbledon storylines heading into the year's third Grand Slam.
