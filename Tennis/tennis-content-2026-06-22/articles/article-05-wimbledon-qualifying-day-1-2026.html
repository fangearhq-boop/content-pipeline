Wimbledon Qualifying Begins — 256 Players Battle for 32 Main Draw Spots at Roehampton

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | June 22, 2026

Seven days before the 2026 Wimbledon Championships begin, the most competitive grass courts in the sport are already being fought over. Qualifying opened Monday at the Community Sports Centre in Roehampton, southwest London, and will run through Thursday, June 25.

The stakes are simple: win three matches, earn one of 32 places in the most famous tennis draw in the world. Lose once, and the summer is over before it begins.

The Format and the Numbers

This year's qualifying competition features 128 men and 128 women — 256 players in total — competing across four days. The three rounds of a knockout format determine 16 places in the men's main draw and 16 in the women's. Combined with automatic entry players, those 32 spots complete the full bracket of 128 in each singles draw.

Gates at Roehampton open at 10 AM with matches from 11 AM. The atmosphere on qualifying courts is unlike anything else in tennis — competitive, urgent, and accessible. Tickets cost £20 in advance, or just £5 at the gate after 4 PM. Children under 16 are admitted free.

Watching the Qualifying

ESPN+ has exclusive broadcast coverage of all four days of qualifying — the first time many of these matches will be seen on television rather than only at the courts. It is an opportunity to see players who are ranked outside the automatic entry threshold, many of them competing for their first Grand Slam main draw appearance.

The qualifying courts at Roehampton are among the quieter, more intimate settings in professional tennis. Players battle within earshot of spectators, without the separation of stadium seating or sprawling grounds. For fans who want to watch tennis up close, these four days are arguably the best-value week on the calendar.

The Context Heading In

The qualifying draw was announced ahead of Day 1. No notable seeded players are present — automatic entry handles all the ranked names. The players competing here are fighting to close the gap to the main draw, and the grass at Roehampton is fast and unforgiving.

Jannik Sinner, the defending champion, begins his Wimbledon preparation this week at the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at Hurlingham Club (June 23-27) — an exhibition event, not official ranking points. Carlos Alcaraz, the two-time Wimbledon champion, is absent from the entire event due to a wrist injury. The Wimbledon singles draw is set at the draw ceremony on Friday, June 26. The Championships begin Monday, June 29.

What's Next

Qualifying continues at Roehampton through Thursday, June 25. Round 2 begins Tuesday, Round 3 on Thursday. Those who survive all three rounds will appear in the main draw when it is revealed at the draw ceremony on Friday, June 26, at 10:00 AM BST. Wimbledon 2026 begins June 29.
