Bouzkova Grinds Out Nottingham Title — First Grass Crown in 2026's Longest WTA Match

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 22, 2026

Marie Bouzkova and Emma Navarro spent nearly three hours on the Nottingham grass on Sunday, trading sets and momentum in the longest WTA match of the 2026 season. When it ended, Bouzkova had her first career grass-court title and a result that underscored Czech women's dominance of the 2026 grass swing.

Bouzkova defeated Navarro 7-6(5), 4-6, 6-2 in the Lexus Nottingham Open final, a contest lasting 2 hours and 57 minutes. The Czech rose to a career-high No. 22 in the world rankings, and delivered a fourth career title — her second of 2026.

A Match That Swung Both Ways

Bouzkova edged the opening-set tiebreak 7-5 to take an early lead. Navarro responded with quality tennis of her own in the second set, breaking serve in the fifth game and maintaining the advantage through to take it 6-4. The deciding set became Bouzkova's — she won the first two games in a pair of lengthy battles and never relinquished control, closing out 6-2.

At 2h57m, the final was the longest WTA match played so far this season. On grass — a surface not known for its extended rallies — that kind of marathon demands fitness, mental sharpness, and composure in equal measure. Bouzkova had all three.

First Grass Title, Fourth Career Crown

This was Bouzkova's first career title on grass, a surface she has long been capable on but where a title had eluded her until now. Her previous three titles came on other surfaces; her second of 2026 followed a victory in Colombia earlier in the year. She carries a career-high ranking of No. 22 and momentum into the final Grand Slam of the year.

Navarro, meanwhile, was competing in her second tour-level final of 2026. She pushed Bouzkova hard and will head into Wimbledon with her own strong grass preparation on record.

A Czech Weekend to Remember

The timing of Bouzkova's win carries an additional storyline: on the same afternoon in Berlin, compatriot Linda Noskova won the WTA 500 over defending champion Jessica Pegula. Czech women claimed both WTA grass-court titles in the same weekend — a rare simultaneous sweep that announces Czech women's tennis as a genuine force heading into Wimbledon.

Christopher O'Connell won the Nottingham men's singles title on the same day.

What's Next

Bouzkova takes her new career-high ranking and first grass title directly into Wimbledon preparations. The draw ceremony at SW19 takes place on Friday, June 26. The Championships begin Monday, June 29. Both Bouzkova and Noskova will enter the main draw as seeded players — Czech women's tennis at Wimbledon will be worth watching closely.
