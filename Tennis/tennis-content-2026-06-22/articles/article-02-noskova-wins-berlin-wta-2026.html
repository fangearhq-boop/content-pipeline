Noskova Claims Berlin Title and Breaks Into the Top 10 — Czech No. 1 at Age 21

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | June 22, 2026

Linda Noskova walked off the Berlin grass on Sunday as the VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Open champion, a world Top 10 player, and the new Czech No. 1. She is 21 years old.

The Czech defeated Jessica Pegula 6-4, 4-6, 6-3 in a final that tested her in ways the rest of her week had not. Pegula — the defending champion who had beaten world No. 1 Sabalenka in the semifinals — took the second set to level things. Noskova steadied and dominated the decider, winning through precisely the kind of resilience that separates good players from great ones.

A Week of Dominance

Until the final, Noskova had not dropped a single set in Berlin. She defeated Renata Zarazua, Diane Parry, Paula Badosa, and Alexandra Eala without yielding. Only Pegula managed to take a set from her — a second-set 4-6 loss — before Noskova regrouped and closed out the match 6-3 in the third.

The week's performance was a statement. Noskova's game — heavy topspin groundstrokes, aggressive court positioning, elite serve — translated to grass with ease. She moved through the draw with purpose, and in the final, responded to adversity when it arrived.

Second Career Title, First on Grass

Noskova's only previous WTA title was at the 2024 Monterrey Open on hard courts. This Berlin victory is her second, and her first on a grass surface. It also marks her first WTA 500-level title — a significant step up in the prestige of her wins.

The victory catapults her into the world's top 10 for the first time, where she becomes the new Czech No. 1, overtaking compatriot Karolína Muchová. The momentum heading into Wimbledon is undeniable.

Pegula Falls Short

For Jessica Pegula, Sunday's defeat ended a week in which she had produced arguably her best performance of the year — a three-set semifinal win over Aryna Sabalenka that included a dominant 6-0 third set. Her fourth career win over the world No. 1 gave her the final, but Noskova proved a step too far.

Pegula, now a Wimbledon seeded player, will take meaningful points into the grass-court Grand Slam.

What's Next

Noskova will head into Wimbledon among the seeded players, with a first grass-court title, a Top 10 ranking, and the wind firmly at her back. The Wimbledon draw ceremony takes place Friday, June 26. The Championships begin June 29. Noskova will be one of the most closely watched names when the bracket is revealed.
