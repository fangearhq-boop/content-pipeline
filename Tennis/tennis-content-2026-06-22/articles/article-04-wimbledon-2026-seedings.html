Wimbledon 2026 Seedings Locked In: Sinner No. 1, Six Americans Seeded, Alcaraz Absent

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 22, 2026

The rankings cutoff for Wimbledon 2026 fell on Monday, locking in the seedings for the year's third Grand Slam. The picture that emerges is one defined by American depth on the men's side, a fascinating women's draw without a clear favourite, and a significant absence that changes the entire calculus of the men's bracket.

Carlos Alcaraz — the two-time Wimbledon champion who won in 2023 and 2024 before losing the 2025 final to Sinner — is not in the draw. His wrist injury rules him out. Wimbledon, for the first time in three years, will have a new men's champion.

Men's Seedings: Sinner Leads, Six Americans In

Jannik Sinner holds the No. 1 seed as the defending 2025 champion. He is the only player to have won Wimbledon in the past three editions who remains in the draw. Sinner's preparation has been deliberate — he bypassed all grass warm-up events except the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at Hurlingham, which begins Tuesday, June 23.

Alexander Zverev is seeded No. 2, arriving at Wimbledon as the Roland Garros 2026 champion and with the form of a genuine contender. Felix Auger-Aliassime is No. 3, Ben Shelton — who won Stuttgart two weeks ago — is No. 4, Alex de Minaur is No. 5, and Taylor Fritz is No. 6.

Novak Djokovic is seeded No. 7 and Daniil Medvedev No. 8. The presence of six Americans in the seedings — Shelton, Fritz, Tommy Paul (who reached the Queen's Club final), Jack Sock, and others across the bracket — represents the deepest U.S. men's presence in a Wimbledon draw in decades.

Women's Seedings: Sabalenka Chases the One That Got Away

Aryna Sabalenka is the women's No. 1 seed — and Wimbledon remains the one Grand Slam title missing from her collection. She has won the Australian Open three times and the US Open twice, but the grass at SW19 has not yielded a title. That narrative will follow her all fortnight.

Elena Rybakina, the 2022 Wimbledon champion, is seeded No. 2. Iga Swiatek, who won the 2025 final, is the defending champion seeded No. 3. Jessica Pegula is No. 4. Mirra Andreeva — who won Roland Garros 2026 at age 19 — enters as No. 5, making her one of the most fascinating wild cards heading into Wimbledon. Amanda Anisimova, who reached the 2025 Wimbledon final, is No. 6. Coco Gauff is No. 7. Elina Svitolina is No. 8.

Alcaraz's Absence Reshapes the Draw

The absence of Alcaraz has cascading effects throughout the men's bracket. The Argentine was the most natural challenger to Sinner and the player with the most recent Wimbledon pedigree. Without him, the draw opens meaningfully for Zverev, Shelton, Fritz, and others. A Grand Slam without Alcaraz and without the two-time defending champion is genuinely unpredictable.

What's Next

The full singles draw is confirmed at the draw ceremony on Friday, June 26, at 10:00 AM BST. The 2026 Wimbledon Championships begin on Monday, June 29. Qualifying at Roehampton runs through Thursday, June 25, to fill the final 32 spots. Seedings are set — the bracket is next.
