Cerundolo Makes History at Queen's Club — First Argentine Champion After Longest Final Ever

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 22, 2026

Francisco Cerundolo stepped onto the Queen's Club grass on Sunday as the heavy underdog with one impossible task: end Tommy Paul's stranglehold on the tournament. Three hours and three minutes later, the Argentine had done exactly that — and made history doing it.

Cerundolo defeated Paul 6-7(4), 6-4, 6-3 in the longest final in the 133-year history of the HSBC Championships, becoming the first Argentine man to ever lift the trophy at The Queen's Club. He rallied from a set and a break down, grinding through adversity in the kind of performance that defines a player's career.

A Record-Breaking Final

The match consumed the afternoon session and then some. Paul took the first set via tiebreak — 7-4 — and quickly moved ahead in the second. Cerundolo's path looked increasingly narrow. But the 27-year-old Argentine refused to fracture.

He broke back, steadied, and took the second set 6-4. In the third, the tide had turned. Cerundolo's groundstrokes found a rhythm that Paul could no longer disrupt, and he closed out 6-3 to complete the comeback and claim the title. Three hours and three minutes of grass-court tennis — and a result that rewrote the tournament record books.

Ending Paul's Nine-Match Streak

Tommy Paul arrived at the final having won nine consecutive matches at Queen's Club, including his title run in 2024. He missed the 2025 tournament through injury and returned this year with every intention of defending. He came within touching distance, leading by a set and a break, before Cerundolo's resilience proved the difference.

Paul's grass form remains excellent — he reached this final without dropping a set until Sunday. He will carry full confidence and ATP ranking points into Wimbledon.

Career Milestone for Cerundolo

This was Cerundolo's fifth career ATP title, his first at the 500 level, and his second on grass. His first grass title came at Eastbourne in 2023. He has now won titles in two different years on two different grass-court venues — a record that speaks to his growing affinity for the surface.

It is also his second title of 2026, having won on home soil in Buenos Aires earlier in the year. He leads his head-to-head with Paul 6-2 overall, including 2-1 on grass.

On court after the match, Cerundolo was measured and grateful: "I fought really hard the whole week. Every match was a war, and I think I got the reward." On being the first Argentine to win Queen's Club, he added simply: "Super proud."

What's Next

Cerundolo enters Wimbledon with the best grass-court preparation imaginable — a title won the week before the Grand Slam on the same surface. The draw ceremony at SW19 takes place on Friday, June 26. The Championships begin June 29. Paul, meanwhile, is expected to be seeded among the top players and will be one to watch when the bracket is confirmed.
