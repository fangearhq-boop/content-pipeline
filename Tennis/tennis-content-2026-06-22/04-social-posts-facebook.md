### STORY 1: Cerundolo Wins Queen's Club

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Francisco Cerundolo has written himself into the history books at Queen's Club.

The Argentine defeated Tommy Paul 6-7(4), 6-4, 6-3 on Sunday in the longest final ever played at the HSBC Championships — three hours and three minutes of absorbing grass-court tennis. Cerundolo rallied from a set and a break down to claim the biggest title of his career, becoming the first Argentine man to lift the trophy in the tournament's history.

It ended Paul's remarkable nine-match winning streak at the venue. The American was chasing back-to-back titles after his 2024 triumph, but Cerundolo — who had already won five matches through the week — refused to yield. His quote afterward said it all: "I fought really hard the whole week. Every match was a war, and I think I got the reward."

This was Cerundolo's fifth career ATP title, first at the 500 level, and his second grass-court crown (he won Eastbourne in 2023). He also won the Buenos Aires title earlier this year. At 27, he is peaking at exactly the right time — Wimbledon starts in just seven days.

### STORY 2: Noskova Wins Berlin WTA 500

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Linda Noskova is officially inside the world's top 10 — and she got there on grass.

The 21-year-old Czech defeated defending champion Jessica Pegula 6-4, 4-6, 6-3 on Sunday to win the VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Open. It was Noskova's second career WTA title and her first on a grass court. Through the entire week in Berlin, she was virtually flawless — dropping only one set all tournament, and that came in the final itself against a world-class opponent in Pegula.

The victory carries Noskova into the top 10 for the first time in her career, making her the new Czech No. 1 — overtaking compatriot Karolína Muchová. She takes considerable momentum into Wimbledon, where she'll now be among the seeded players.

Pegula, who beat world No. 1 Sabalenka in the semifinals, had no complaints — she simply ran into someone playing inspired tennis.

With Wimbledon's draw ceremony on Friday, Noskova will be one of the names everyone watches when the bracket is revealed.

### STORY 3: Bouzkova Wins Nottingham WTA

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

Czech women are taking over the 2026 grass season — and Nottingham just added another chapter.

Marie Bouzkova defeated Emma Navarro 7-6(5), 4-6, 6-2 in the Lexus Nottingham Open final — a match that lasted nearly three hours and was the longest on the WTA Tour this season. Bouzkova had to grind hard to take the first-set tiebreak, recovered from losing the second, and then dominated the decider to capture her first career grass-court title.

It was Bouzkova's fourth career title overall and her second of 2026 (she also won in Colombia earlier this year). She rises to a career-high No. 22 in the world. It's a title that makes a genuine statement — on grass, in a three-setter, against a player in Emma Navarro who had already reached a final in 2026 before Nottingham.

Meanwhile, in Berlin on the same afternoon, Noskova was winning the grass-court WTA 500. Czech women took home both grass season week-2 titles. That's the kind of form that gets you noticed heading into Wimbledon.

### STORY 5: Wimbledon Qualifying Day 1

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 PM ET

While the seedings were being finalized today, 256 players were already competing just a few miles from SW19.

Wimbledon qualifying officially opened at the Community Sports Centre in Roehampton on Monday — the start of a four-day battle (through June 25) in which players must win three consecutive matches to earn one of 32 places in the main draw. That's 128 men and 128 women chasing 16 spots each.

These are the matches you don't often see on TV — but they're where careers are decided. Some players are fighting for their first Grand Slam main draw appearance. Others are ranked just outside the automatic entry threshold and need three wins to get in. ESPN+ is broadcasting all four days of qualifying exclusively.

Tickets at Roehampton are just £20 in advance, or £5 at the gate after 4 PM, with under-16s admitted free. It's genuinely one of the best-value days of tennis on the calendar — and it's been happening today.

Jannik Sinner, the defending champion, skips qualifying entirely and heads to the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at Hurlingham starting tomorrow (June 23) instead. Carlos Alcaraz, the two-time champion, is out with a wrist injury. The draw for the main event is revealed Friday, June 26. The Championships begin Monday, June 29.
