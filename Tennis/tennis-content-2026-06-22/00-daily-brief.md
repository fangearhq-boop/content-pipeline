### STORY 1: Cerundolo Wins Queen's Club — First Argentine Champion, Longest Final in History
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** FOLLOW UP (previewed June 21 as Paul vs Cerundolo final day)
**Sources:** atptour.com, lta.org.uk, tennismajors.com, skysports.com, sundayguardianlive.com, en.tennistemple.com
**Posting Window:** 8:00 AM ET
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Francisco Cerundolo (ARG) defeated Tommy Paul (USA) 6-7(4), 6-4, 6-3 to win the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club. The final lasted 3 hours and 3 minutes — the longest final in tournament history. Cerundolo is the first Argentine to win Queen's Club. This is his 5th career ATP title, 1st ATP 500 title, and 2nd title of 2026 (also won Buenos Aires). His 2nd grass title (first was Eastbourne 2023). He snapped Paul's 9-match winning streak at Queen's Club. Cerundolo leads H2H 6-2 (5-2 entering, now 6-2) and leads the grass H2H 2-1. Quote: "Super proud... I fought really hard the whole week. Every match was a war, and I think I got the reward."

**Tier:** 1
**Status:** FOLLOW UP (previewed June 21 as rain-delayed Berlin final Pegula vs Noskova)

**Posting Window:** 10:30 AM ET

Linda Noskova (CZE, 21) defeated Jessica Pegula (USA) 6-4, 4-6, 6-3 in the VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Open final. Noskova's second career WTA title and first on grass (previous title: Monterrey 2024). She dropped only one set all week — the second set in the final. Rises to world No. 10 — new Top 10 debut and new Czech No. 1 (ahead of compatriot Karolina Muchova). Pegula was the defending champion; she had beaten Sabalenka in the SF.
