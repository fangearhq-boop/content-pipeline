Dimitrov Retires From Roland Garros Qualifying With Thigh Injury
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET Grigor Dimitrov's Roland Garros 2026 dream is over before it started. The Bulgarian, once ranked No. 3 in the world, retired from his first-round qualifying match on Tuesday with a thigh injury — the fourth time he has been forced out of a match this season. Add Brisbane, the Australian Open, and Dubai to the list, and you have a year that paints a sobering picture of a career disrupted by injury. It was not supposed to be this way. At his peak, Dimitrov was considered one of the most gifted players of his generation — smooth technique, fluid movement, a style the press called "Baby Federer." He made it to a career-high No. 3 ranking and a 2017 ATP Finals title. Then came the injuries. The pectoral tear at Wimbledon 2025 was perhaps the most heartbreaking moment. Leading Jannik Sinner two sets to zero in the fourth round, Dimitrov retired from the match that could have been a breakthrough. Instead, it became a turning point in the wrong direction. He has now dropped to No. 170, competing in Grand Slam qualifying for the first time in over a decade. What happens next for Dimitrov? That is the question the tennis world is asking this week in Paris. ---

### Long-Form Post Window: 12:30 PM ET Thursday evening at Philippe-Chatrier court, Gaël Monfils will take one last bow. The ceremony begins at 7:30 PM Paris time on May 21 — the night before the draw, the night before Roland Garros truly begins. His Davis Cup teammates Richard Gasquet and Jo-Wilfried Tsonga will be there. So will DJ Martin Solveig and rapper Franglish. It is a send-off worthy of La Monf, the showman who gave French tennis some of its most electric moments over a 20-year career. Monfils reached the Roland Garros semifinals in 2008 and the US Open final in 2016. He is currently ranked around No. 222. Meanwhile, Stan Wawrinka has moved from wild card into direct main draw entry after Lorenzo Musetti withdrew. The 2015 Roland Garros champion gets one more genuine chance at a tournament he described as part of him. And David Goffin, Belgian and 35, is alive in qualifying after beating Tseng 6-3, 6-1 on Monday. Three legends. Three last dances. Paris is a poetic city for goodbyes. ---

### Long-Form Post Window: 3:00 PM ET Ask Elina Svitolina about Roland Garros 2026 and she does not shy away from the question. "The Rome victory gives me a lot of confidence," the Ukrainian said. "I come to Paris feeling good about my game." In Rome, Svitolina beat three of the world's top-four players in one week — Rybakina in the quarterfinals, Swiatek in the semifinals, Gauff in the final. No woman had ever beaten three top-4 players in a single Rome tournament. The final score against Gauff was 6-4, 6-7(3), 6-2. She is now seeded No. 7 in Paris. That means she cannot face another top-8 player until the quarterfinals — a protected path through the first week that gives her every opportunity to arrive at the second week with full momentum. Svitolina has 20 career titles. She has never won a Grand Slam. She is 31, and her game is peaking. This is her best case yet. ---

### Long-Form Post Window: 6:00 PM ET Roland Garros qualifying is never just background noise. Not when names like Goffin, Andreescu, Pliskova, and Stephens are fighting for the 16 main-draw spots available. Day 1 on Monday saw David Goffin advance with a dominant 6-3, 6-1 win over Chun-Hsin Tseng. The Belgian, in what may be his final Grand Slam appearance, looked comfortable and controlled. The round is fiercely contested — and often produces the most compelling stories of the entire fortnight. ---
