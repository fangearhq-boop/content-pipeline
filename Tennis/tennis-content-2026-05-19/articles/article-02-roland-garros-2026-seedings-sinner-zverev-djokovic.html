Roland Garros 2026 Seedings: Sinner Leads, Alcaraz Absent, Djokovic Returns at No. 3

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 19, 2026

The Roland Garros 2026 seedings have been officially confirmed, setting the stage for what promises to be one of the most anticipated French Opens in years. Jannik Sinner leads the men's draw as No. 1 seed, with Alexander Zverev at No. 2 — a seeding that reflects Carlos Alcaraz's absence — and Novak Djokovic slotting in at No. 3 as he makes his long-awaited return from a shoulder injury.

The draw ceremony takes place on Thursday, May 21 at 2:00 PM CEST in Paris. The main draw begins on Sunday, May 24, with the men's final scheduled for Sunday, June 7.

Men's Seedings: Full Top Eight

The confirmed men's seeds are:

  1. Jannik Sinner (Italy)
  2. Alexander Zverev (Germany)
  3. Novak Djokovic (Serbia)
  4. Felix Auger-Aliassime (Canada)
  5. Ben Shelton (USA)
  6. Taylor Fritz (USA)
  7. Daniil Medvedev (Russia)
  8. Alex de Minaur (Australia)

Further down the men's draw, Arthur Fils is the No. 17 seed and will be a fan favourite in France. Rafael Jódar — the 19-year-old Spaniard who won Marrakech and reached the Madrid quarterfinals in the same spring swing — is seeded No. 27. Joao Fonseca of Brazil, the 2025 Next Gen champion, is at No. 28.

Women's Seedings: Sabalenka Leads, Svitolina the Key Mover

Aryna Sabalenka is the Women's No. 1 seed, entering Paris having dominated the WTA calendar in 2026. The key mover in the women's seedings is Elina Svitolina at No. 7 — elevated by her Rome title, where she became the first woman in the tournament's history to defeat three top-4 players in a single event, beating Rybakina, Swiatek, and Gauff in succession.

As a No. 7 seed, Svitolina cannot face another top-8 player until the quarterfinals — a significant protection given the depth of the women's draw.

The Alcaraz Absence Changes Everything

Two-time defending champion Carlos Alcaraz officially withdrew from Roland Garros 2026 in late April with a right wrist injury — a tendon inflammation and cartilage damage that required conservative treatment. His absence reshapes the tournament entirely.

Without Alcaraz, there is no player in the men's draw who has defeated Sinner more than once in their last four meetings. The Italian enters Paris as the most dominant clay player of the season — having won Monte Carlo, Madrid, and Rome to complete the Career Golden Masters. Roland Garros is the only Grand Slam he has never won. The 2025 final, where Alcaraz saved three match points before winning, remains Sinner's closest and most agonising near-miss.

Djokovic's Return — the Wildcard of the Draw

Novak Djokovic is officially seeded No. 3 — but question marks surround the Serbian's physical condition. He last played at Indian Wells in March, withdrawing from Miami, Monte Carlo, and Madrid with a shoulder injury. He played just one match in Rome (first round) before Paris, and observers noted that he appeared under the weather during that match.

Djokovic has publicly targeted Roland Garros as his return-to-form tournament. He is a three-time Roland Garros champion and is chasing a 25th Grand Slam title, which would be an outright record. Where he falls in the draw on Thursday will determine whether he faces a potential Sinner or Zverev test before the final.

What's Next

The Roland Garros draw ceremony is Thursday, May 21 at 2:00 PM Paris time. Qualifying continues through Friday, May 22, with 16 men and 16 women earning their way into the main draw. The opening-round matches begin Sunday, May 24. The men's final is June 7, the women's final June 6.

With Alcaraz absent and Djokovic uncertain, this may be the most open French Open in several years — and the most compelling case yet for Jannik Sinner to complete his career Grand Slam.
