Last Dance in Paris: Monfils Gets a Grand Farewell, Wawrinka Steps Into the Main Draw

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | May 19, 2026

For a generation of tennis fans, the names Gaël Monfils and Stan Wawrinka represent something more than statistics. They represent a particular era of the game — emotional, dramatic, utterly compelling — and this week in Paris, both men are getting the sendoffs they deserve.

Monfils will receive a farewell ceremony on Thursday, May 21 at 7:30 PM on Philippe-Chatrier Court. Wawrinka has received something different but perhaps more meaningful: a direct entry into the main draw, courtesy of Lorenzo Musetti's withdrawal, giving the 2015 Roland Garros champion one more genuine chance at the tournament he has described as part of his identity.

La Monf's Final Bow

The ceremony for Gaël Monfils will be a celebration befitting the showman. His Davis Cup teammates Richard Gasquet and Jo-Wilfried Tsonga will be present. DJ Martin Solveig and rapper Franglish will perform. It is, by design, a party — not a memorial — and that reflects who Monfils has always been.

The French star announced his retirement at the end of the 2026 season in October 2025. He is currently ranked around No. 222, but the number is almost beside the point. What matters is what the sport owes him: 20 years of extraordinary entertainment, delivered with a smile and a backflip and a roar from whatever crowd was watching.

Monfils reached the Roland Garros semifinals in 2008, losing to Roger Federer. He reached the US Open final in 2016, losing to Novak Djokovic. He never won a Grand Slam, but he gave people reasons to watch tennis that went beyond trophies. In January 2025, at 38 years old, he became the oldest ATP title winner since 1990 — a final flourish before the curtain.

"It feels strange to think that today I'm starting my last week of training on clay," he told French outlet We Love Tennis in May. The feeling is mutual.

Wawrinka's Unlikely Extra Life

Stan Wawrinka's Roland Garros story in 2026 took a positive turn when Lorenzo Musetti withdrew from the main draw. Wawrinka, who had been granted a wild card, was moved into direct entry — a distinction that affects draw placement and logistics, but more importantly signals that Wawrinka will be in the bracket as a full participant rather than a sentimental inclusion.

His wild card was then passed to French player Clément Tabur.

Wawrinka is 41 years old and a three-time Grand Slam champion: the 2014 Australian Open, the 2015 Roland Garros, and the 2016 US Open. Each title came against Novak Djokovic in the final — a remarkable record that speaks to an ability to rise to the very highest occasion. He told the Roland Garros magazine that the tournament is "part of me," that he grew up watching it, and that one more time on Chatrier represents something profound.

"I grew up with Roland-Garros," Wawrinka said in an interview with the tournament's official publication. "It's part of me."

And Then There's Goffin

Not to be overlooked: David Goffin, the Belgian veteran who announced his own retirement plans, won his Roland Garros qualifying opener on Monday with a 6-3, 6-1 victory over Chun-Hsin Tseng. He next faces Britain's Toby Samuel.

Goffin was once ranked No. 7 in the world — the highest-ranked Belgian male player in Open Era history. He won six ATP titles. He held his own against the sport's greatest players for a decade. Now he is in qualifying, fighting for a spot, and doing it with the same professionalism that defined his career.

Paris in May is many things. In 2026, it is also a farewell tour for three players who gave the sport more than it will easily repay.

What's Next

The Monfils farewell ceremony takes place Thursday, May 21 at 7:30 PM Paris time on Philippe-Chatrier Court. The draw ceremony is earlier that same day at 2:00 PM CEST — meaning Wawrinka will know his first-round opponent by Thursday evening. Goffin's qualifying second-round match against Toby Samuel is scheduled during the remaining qualifying days, which run through May 22. The main draw begins Sunday, May 24.
