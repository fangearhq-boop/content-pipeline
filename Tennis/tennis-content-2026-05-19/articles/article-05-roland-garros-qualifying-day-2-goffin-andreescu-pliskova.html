Roland Garros Qualifying Day 2: Goffin Advances, Andreescu and Pliskova Battle for Main Draw Spots

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 19, 2026

The Roland Garros qualifying draw does not lack for storylines. Among the 128 men and 128 women competing for 16 main draw spots each, Tuesday's Day 2 action included one significant injury retirement, several notable names returning to Paris after prolonged absences, and the continued emotional farewell campaign of Belgian veteran David Goffin.

Goffin Through, Eyes on Samuel Next

Goffin opened his campaign on Monday with a 6-3, 6-1 win over Chun-Hsin Tseng — a confident, controlled performance from the 35-year-old, who is competing in what may be his final Roland Garros. The Belgian, ranked No. 7 in the world at his peak and the first Belgian male to crack the top 10 in the Open Era, announced his retirement plans for the end of the 2026 season.

His next opponent is Britain's Toby Samuel. Two more wins would get him into the main draw. Three matches stand between David Goffin and one last run at a Grand Slam.

Dimitrov Retires — Four Times in 2026

The most significant news of Day 2 was Grigor Dimitrov's retirement with a thigh injury — detailed separately in our coverage — which marked his fourth forced withdrawal of the season. It ended the Bulgarian's Roland Garros qualifying campaign before it could truly begin and deepened the concern around his physical condition heading into the back half of the year.

Andreescu vs. a Teen French Wildcard

Bianca Andreescu, the 2019 US Open champion, was on court Tuesday against 17-year-old French wildcard Daphnée Mpetshi Perricard. The matchup captures what qualifying often delivers: a former Grand Slam champion fighting her way back, opposed by a teenage talent that the host nation is hoping will light up the tournament.

Andreescu is 25 and has dealt with her own string of injuries since her breakthrough year. Paris represents an opportunity to rebuild ranking points and find her level ahead of what should be a strong grass-court and hard-court season.

Pliskova and Stephens Also in Action

Karolina Pliskova — the former world No. 1 who reached the Roland Garros final in 2021 — was scheduled against Yeonwoo Ku of South Korea. The Czech has worked her way back through a difficult period since the height of her career and brings genuine big-court capability if she can find her serving range.

Sloane Stephens, the 2017 US Open champion, was also competing in the women's qualifying draw. The American has been one of the most versatile players on the WTA tour and her presence in qualifying is a reminder of how competitive the current women's game has become at every level of the ranking.

Paula Badosa withdrew from qualifying before her scheduled matches.

The Competition for 16 Spots

The numbers are stark: 128 men and 128 women entered qualifying. Only 16 from each draw make it into the main draw. Each qualifier must win three consecutive matches to earn their spot. The qualifying tournament runs through Thursday, May 22, with the main draw beginning on Sunday, May 24.

The qualifying rounds at Roland Garros have a history of producing players who cause upsets in the main draw — fresh legs and focused purpose can be an advantage in early rounds. Every player who makes it through will arrive in the main draw having already won three matches in Paris.

What's Next

Qualifying continues through May 22. David Goffin faces Toby Samuel for a place in the final qualifying round. Andreescu, Pliskova, and Stephens all need two more wins to reach the main draw. The draw ceremony for the main draw takes place Thursday, May 21 at 2:00 PM CEST, with the full Roland Garros 2026 main draw beginning on Sunday, May 24.
