Dimitrov Retires From Roland Garros Qualifying With Thigh Injury — Fourth Withdrawal of 2026

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 19, 2026

Grigor Dimitrov's bid to return to Grand Slam tennis came to another painful halt on Tuesday when the Bulgarian retired from his Roland Garros qualifying match with a thigh injury — the fourth time he has been forced to abandon a match this season.

The former world No. 3, who entered qualifying ranked No. 170, was scheduled to face Jaime Faria on Court Suzanne Lenglen as part of what many hoped would be a meaningful step in his comeback. Instead, the match became the latest chapter in a deeply difficult year for the 34-year-old.

A Season of Setbacks

Dimitrov's 2026 injury log is a difficult read. He retired against Jiri Lehecka in Brisbane at the start of the year, then again against Francisco Passaro at the Australian Open in January, and subsequently against Christopher O'Connell in Dubai in February. Each time, he returned to the court with optimism. Each time, injury intervened.

Tuesday's thigh problem made it four — and ended whatever chance he had of competing in the Roland Garros main draw for the first time in what has become a surprisingly long absence from the tournament's top level.

"This is already the fourth withdrawal of the season for Dimitrov," reported Tennis Temple, the first outlet to detail the injury. The Bulgarian had been working back through Challenger events in the weeks preceding Paris, including a loss to a player ranked No. 312 — a result that underscored just how far the road back has stretched.

A Career of Unfulfilled Promise

There is no other way to frame it: Grigor Dimitrov was supposed to be one of the defining players of his generation. The technical elegance of his backhand, the easy power of his forehand, the movement that earned him the nickname "Baby Federer" — the attributes were genuine. They translated to a career-high No. 3 ranking in 2017, an ATP Finals title that November in London, and years among the sport's elite.

The question was always whether health would allow the talent to be realised at Grand Slam level. He reached the Australian Open semifinals in 2017 and the Wimbledon semifinals in 2014. He was good enough to beat the best on the best days. But the signature result — a Grand Slam title, a deep run in Paris — remained just out of reach.

The defining injury came at Wimbledon 2025. Dimitrov had Jannik Sinner at his mercy in the fourth round, leading two sets to zero on Centre Court. Then came the pectoral tear. He retired from that match, and Sinner went on to win the title. Whether Dimitrov would have gone all the way is impossible to know. What followed, however, is documented: a long rehabilitation, a painful slide down the rankings, and a 2026 defined by recurring breakdowns.

What This Means for His Career

Dimitrov dropped out of the ATP top 100 for the first time in 14 years in April. Playing in Grand Slam qualifying — rather than entering main draws directly — would have been unthinkable five years ago. And yet here he was, on Court Suzanne Lenglen in Paris, trying to earn his way back in the same format as players at the very start of their careers.

The thigh injury changes the near-term picture. His next potential opportunity at a Grand Slam would be Wimbledon in late June, a surface where he has historically performed well. But the body will need to cooperate first.

The tennis world roots for Dimitrov. Even amid the frustration, the brilliance was always visible. The hope — always real — is that one more healthy stretch produces one more run at the heights where his game belongs.

What's Next

Dimitrov will need to assess the thigh injury before determining his schedule. Wimbledon qualifying would be his next realistic Grand Slam target, with the grass-court Grand Slam beginning in late June at the All England Club. The Roland Garros main draw, meanwhile, begins on Sunday, May 24 without him.

The qualifying tournament continues through May 22, with David Goffin, Bianca Andreescu, Karolina Pliskova, and Sloane Stephens among the other notable names competing for a place in the main draw.
