Sinner Delivers 7-0 Tiebreak Shutout to Reach Madrid Semifinals

By Elena Voss | April 30, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

There was one question going into Jannik Sinner's Madrid Open quarterfinal against Rafael Jodar: could the 19-year-old local wildcard, riding the emotion of the Caja Mágica crowd and a stunning three-match upset run, trouble the world No. 1 in a way that statistics suggested was possible?

The answer arrived in the second-set tiebreak. Sinner won every single point — 7-0 — and with it, he clinched a 6-2, 7-6(0) victory in 1 hour and 55 minutes that extended his Masters 1000 winning streak to 26 consecutive matches.

The Closing Run

Jodar made Sinner work far harder than the scoreline suggested. The teenager from Alcorcón — just 12 kilometers from the Caja Mágica — created seven break points across the match. At 4-3 up in the second set, he created three in a row. On each occasion, Sinner pulled out a winner to escape.

But from the moment the tiebreak began, the momentum shifted completely. Sinner won the final 10 points of the match without interruption. The 7-0 tiebreak was not just a margin; it was a statement.

After the match, Sinner walked to the courtside camera and wrote "What a player" on the lens — a spontaneous tribute to the teenager who had captivated a city. "He's playing incredible tennis," Sinner said in his on-court interview. "He pushed me to the limit."

History in the Making

With the win, Sinner became the first player in ATP history to reach the semifinals at all nine Masters 1000 events. His 26 consecutive Masters wins rank fourth in series history since 1990. He is chasing an unprecedented fifth consecutive Masters title — a feat that neither Novak Djokovic nor Rafael Nadal ever achieved.

Jodar's reaction was measured and mature for a 19-year-old making his Masters 1000 quarterfinal debut. "There's a long road ahead," he said. "I've learned a lot from this match and I take away a lot of positives." His run — defeating de Minaur, Fonseca, and Kopriva — was one of the stories of the Madrid fortnight.

What's Next

Sinner faces Arthur Fils in the Madrid semifinals on Friday. Fils beat Jiri Lehecka 6-3, 6-4 on Wednesday to extend his own winning streak to nine matches and advance with his Barcelona title in hand. It will be the first meeting between Sinner and Fils on clay — and with the French Open three weeks away, both players will treat it as a genuine audition.

Sinner's path to a fifth straight Masters title, and perhaps the best clay season of his career, runs directly through the red-hot 21-year-old from Paris.
