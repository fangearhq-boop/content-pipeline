History Awaits: Madrid WTA Final Will Produce a First-Time WTA 1000 Champion

By Elena Voss | April 30, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The Madrid WTA draw began with Aryna Sabalenka as the defending champion, Iga Swiatek as the No. 4 seed, and Elena Rybakina as the No. 2 seed. By Thursday's semifinal day, all three have been eliminated. What remains is something genuinely unprecedented at this tournament: a field of four semifinalists who have never reached a WTA 1000 final.

Mirra Andreeva, Hailey Baptiste, Marta Kostyuk, and Anastasia Potapova — none of them has ever stood in a WTA 1000 final. The Madrid title this weekend will go to a first-timer, guaranteed.

Andreeva vs. Baptiste: Two Stories Collide

The first semifinal Thursday afternoon pits 18-year-old Mirra Andreeva against American Hailey Baptiste at 4 PM Madrid time.

Andreeva has been the most consistent young player on the 2026 clay circuit. She began the spring clay swing by defeating Iga Swiatek in the Stuttgart quarterfinals — arguably her biggest win to date — then claimed the Linz title before arriving in Madrid and advancing to the last four without a loss. She is methodical, composed beyond her age, and thrives on clay.

Baptiste's route to this semifinal has been defined by a single extraordinary moment: saving six match points — five at 4-5 in the third set and a sixth in the tiebreak — to beat world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka 2-6, 6-2, 7-6(6) in the quarterfinals. The American became only the second American to defeat the world No. 1 at the Madrid Open, and the win remains the biggest of her career.

Form vs. Drama

Andreeva arrives with superior consistency and the better record on clay in 2026. Baptiste arrives with the momentum of what may be the tournament's greatest upset. Andreeva is the slight favorite based on clay form, but Baptiste has already shown she knows how to win when it matters most.

Neither has been here before. Neither has anything to lose. These are exactly the conditions that produce unexpected, brilliant tennis.

What's Next

The winner of Thursday's Andreeva-Baptiste semifinal advances to Saturday's final, where they will face the winner of the Kostyuk-Potapova match. Three weeks before Roland Garros, the Madrid WTA draw has delivered four first-time finalists, a historic upset of the world No. 1, and a political controversy in the semifinals. Whoever lifts the trophy Saturday will do so as a first-time WTA 1000 champion at one of the tour's premier events.
