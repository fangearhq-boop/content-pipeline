Madrid ATP QF Day 2: Ruud Defends His Crown, Zverev Eyes Munich Revenge

By Marcus Cole | April 30, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

While Jannik Sinner and Arthur Fils locked up one side of the Madrid ATP semifinals on Wednesday, Thursday completes the bracket. Defending champion Casper Ruud takes on Belgian breakthrough Alexander Blockx in the afternoon; Alexander Zverev attempts to avenge his Munich defeat at the hands of Flavio Cobolli in the evening. The winner of each match advances to Saturday alongside Sinner and Fils.

Ruud vs. Blockx: Champion vs. Challenger

Casper Ruud knows this tournament better than almost anyone in the draw. The Norwegian won the Madrid title last year and reached the final on multiple occasions — he understands the conditions, the pressure, and what it takes to navigate a hard week on the Caja Mágica clay.

Alexander Blockx has never been in a Masters 1000 quarterfinal before this week. The 21-year-old Belgian entered on a wildcard and made the most of it, beating Francisco Cerundolo 7-6(8), 6-2 to earn a last-eight berth. For Blockx, simply arriving at this stage is a career-best result. For Ruud, anything short of the final would represent a significant underperformance given his history in Madrid.

They have never played each other on the ATP Tour. That cuts both ways — Ruud has no blueprint for Blockx, but Blockx also has no experience reading Ruud's game under pressure. Ruud has been battling his way through the draw, saving two match points to outlast Stefanos Tsitsipas in the previous round. He is not in peak form, but he is experienced enough to survive.

Match time: 1 PM Madrid (7 AM ET).

Zverev vs. Cobolli: The Rematch

Two weeks ago in Munich, Flavio Cobolli walked off the court after beating Alexander Zverev 6-3, 6-3 in the semifinal — a result that surprised even the most ardent Cobolli supporters given the German's clay pedigree. Cobolli has since ridden that momentum all the way to a Madrid quarterfinal, where he beat world No. 4 Daniil Medvedev 6-3, 5-7, 6-4 in the round of 16.

Zverev enters Thursday's match with revenge on his mind and a 2-1 head-to-head lead that means little given the Italian's recent form. The German has reached the Madrid final twice without winning the title — he is hungry for a trophy here, but the path suddenly looks more complicated than it might have appeared before Munich.

Cobolli is a genuine threat on clay right now. His footwork, consistency, and ball-striking have been at career-best levels. Whether he can repeat the Munich performance on a bigger stage against a fully motivated Zverev is the central question Thursday evening.

Match time: 8 PM Madrid (2 PM ET).

What's Next

By Thursday night, the Madrid ATP draw will be fully resolved into four semifinalists. Sinner and Fils are already confirmed on one side. The winners of Ruud-Blockx and Zverev-Cobolli fill out the other half of the bracket. Saturday's semifinals will set the stage for Sunday's final — the last major Masters tune-up before Roland Garros begins on May 18.
