Fils Rolls Past Lehecka in 74 Minutes, Sets Up Sinner Showdown at Madrid

By Marcus Cole | April 30, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

If Jannik Sinner is the world No. 1 by ranking, Arthur Fils is currently the world No. 1 by form. The 21-year-old Frenchman dispatched Jiri Lehecka 6-3, 6-4 in just 74 minutes on Wednesday to reach his first-ever Madrid semifinal, extending his personal winning streak to nine consecutive matches.

The numbers accompanying Fils on clay this spring are remarkable: nine wins, zero sets dropped, one ATP 500 title (Barcelona, two weeks ago), and now a semifinal at one of the biggest Masters events on the calendar.

Another Dominant Display

Against the Czech veteran Lehecka, Fils was relentless. He broke early in the opener, served with authority, and gave his opponent very little to work with from the baseline. The second set was tighter — Lehecka stayed competitive longer — but Fils never allowed the Czech to gain any real foothold.

The win was Fils' fourth in Madrid without losing a set. He has looked increasingly comfortable on clay since the Barcelona draw, where he saved two match points in the opening round before eventually winning the title. That resilience appears to have unlocked something in his game.

"I feel like my game is at a really high level right now," Fils said after the match.

The Semifinal: Sinner vs. Fils on Clay for the First Time

Friday's semifinal is the matchup that the clay season has been building toward. Sinner arrives with a 26-match Masters winning streak and the weight of history — five straight Masters titles would be unprecedented. Fils arrives with the hottest form on tour and nothing to lose.

Their head-to-head record was built primarily on hard courts, where Sinner leads. This will be their first career meeting on clay. Neither player knows with certainty how the surface changes the equation. Both will find out Friday in front of a packed Caja Mágica crowd.

The French Open is three weeks away. At a tournament where both men will be considered genuine contenders for Roland Garros glory, Friday's semifinal takes on added significance far beyond the Madrid title itself.

What's Next

Sinner versus Fils on Friday at the Madrid Open — this is the semifinal the clay season deserved. For Fils, it is the biggest match of his career on his best surface. For Sinner, it is another test of whether his extraordinary dominance can continue against the most dangerous challenger on tour right now.

The winner advances to Saturday's final and remains on course for what would be one of the defining performances of the 2026 season.
