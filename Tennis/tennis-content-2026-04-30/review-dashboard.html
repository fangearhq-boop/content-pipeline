### Long-Form Post Jannik Sinner delivered one of the most clinical performances of his Madrid campaign, defeating 19-year-old local wildcard Rafael Jodar 6-2, 7-6(0) in 1 hour and 55 minutes on Wednesday night. The match will be remembered for how it ended: Sinner won the final 10 points of the contest, including a perfect 7-0 tiebreak shutout that snuffed out Jodar's dreams under the closed roof of Manolo Santana Stadium. Jodar had his chances. He created seven break points total, including three in a row when he led 4-3 in the second set. Each time, Sinner produced a winner to escape. When the tiebreak arrived, there was no contest. With the victory, Sinner extended his Masters 1000 winning streak to 26 consecutive matches and became the first player in ATP history to reach the semifinals at all nine Masters 1000 events. He is chasing an unprecedented fifth consecutive Masters title. After the match, Sinner wrote "What a player" on the TV camera lens — a tribute to the teenager who had the entire crowd behind him. "He's playing incredible tennis," Sinner said. "He pushed me to the limit." Jodar took the loss graciously. "There's a long road ahead," he said. "I've learned a lot from this match." Next up: Arthur Fils on Friday. ### Long-Form Post Arthur Fils is not slowing down. The 21-year-old Frenchman dispatched Jiri Lehecka 6-3, 6-4 in just 74 minutes Wednesday, extending his winning streak to nine consecutive matches and booking a Madrid semifinal date with world No. 1 Jannik Sinner. Fils has been virtually untouchable on clay in 2026. He won the Barcelona Open title two weeks ago and has not dropped a set on this surface all season. Against Lehecka, he was precise and powerful — breaking early and never looking back. The reward is a Friday semifinal showdown against the most dominant player on tour. Sinner carries a 26-match M1000 streak. Fils carries the hottest form on the entire clay swing. ### Long-Form Post Before Marta Kostyuk and Anastasia Potapova take the court for their Madrid WTA semifinal tonight, there will be no handshake. Kostyuk, who is Ukrainian, confirmed Thursday that she will not shake hands with Potapova before the match. The decision reflects the same position taken by several Ukrainian players — including Elina Svitolina — who have declined exchanges with Russian competitors in the context of Russia's ongoing war against Ukraine. Potapova holds both Russian and Austrian citizenship. She has previously expressed love for her homeland but has not publicly condemned the war. Kostyuk has been one of the WTA's most outspoken voices on Ukraine. She also arrives in outstanding form — on a 10-match winning streak, undefeated on clay this season. She beat Noskova 7-6(1), 6-0 in the quarterfinals. The political dimension adds layers to an already compelling match. Kostyuk leads the head-to-head 2-2 but has won both of their most recent meetings, including a 6-3, 6-2 victory here in Madrid in 2025. The match is scheduled for 9:30 PM Madrid time. Whatever happens on court, this is one of the most charged moments of the 2026 clay season. --- #### Image Caption -- 10:30 AM ET ### Image Post Caption There will be no handshake before tonight's Kostyuk-Potapova semifinal. The first semifinal this afternoon features 18-year-old Andreeva against American Hailey Baptiste. Andreeva has been the most consistent young player on clay in 2026 — she beat Swiatek in Stuttgart, won the Linz title, and reached Madrid's last four. Baptiste captured the tournament's biggest story by saving six match points to stun world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka on Tuesday. This is genuinely wide-open tennis.
