Wimbledon Men's Quarterfinals Preview: Sinner Meets Struff, FAA Takes On Record-Holder Djokovic

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | July 6, 2026

Wimbledon, London

The Wimbledon men's quarterfinals are set, and the draw has delivered the matchups the tournament deserved: a defending champion against a 36-year-old first-time major quarterfinalist, and a record-breaking legend against the young Canadian who wants nothing more than to take his place.

Jannik Sinner, the No. 1 seed and reigning Wimbledon champion, will face Jan-Lennard Struff. Felix Auger-Aliassime, the No. 3 seed, will face Novak Djokovic. The quarterfinals are scheduled for Day 9 and Day 10 — Tuesday and Wednesday.

Sinner vs. Struff: The Immovable Object Meets the Impossible Run

Jannik Sinner has been a machine at Wimbledon 2026. The Italian has not dropped a set in four matches, dispatching qualifier Shintaro Mochizuki 6-3, 7-6(0), 6-3 on Day 7 with clinical efficiency. His first serve has been dominant, his groundstroke exchanges have been controlled, and he has not been pushed to a tiebreak he did not win comfortably.

Struff, on the other hand, arrived in the quarterfinals via one of the most remarkable routes of the tournament. The 36-year-old reached his first-ever Grand Slam quarterfinal in his 47th major appearance when Hubert Hurkacz retired in the fifth set with back and abdominal injuries. Struff is the oldest man in the Open Era to reach a maiden major quarterfinal.

None of that history changes what Struff faces on Tuesday. Sinner is the defending champion and the heavy favorite. But tournament tennis has a way of rewarding players who arrive without pressure, with momentum, and with nothing to lose — and Struff is all three of those things.

FAA vs. Djokovic: The Match the Draw Had in Store

Since the Wimbledon draw was announced, the potential FAA versus Djokovic quarterfinal was the matchup that analysts circled. On Sunday, it became official.

Felix Auger-Aliassime defeated Alejandro Davidovich Fokina in a grinding five-set battle on Day 7 — 6-7(4), 7-6(6), 6-3, 6-7(2), 6-1 — demonstrating the kind of resilience required to win majors. The 22-year-old Canadian has been one of the most complete players in the draw, with a serve and forehand that can hurt anyone.

Djokovic arrives with 106 Wimbledon wins — the all-time record, claimed on Sunday — and 24 Grand Slam titles to his name. He also arrives, by his own admission, without his best game. He called Sunday's win over Safiullin "ugly." Against Auger-Aliassime, that will not be enough.

The head-to-head history between the two is relatively limited, and Wimbledon grass has been Djokovic's most reliable surface for three decades. But FAA has grown significantly in the last two seasons, winning his first ATP 1000 title and reaching back-to-back major semifinals. He is no longer a rising talent — he is a legitimate contender.

What stands between FAA and a Wimbledon semifinal is the greatest Wimbledon player who ever lived, with everything to prove about whether the record is a peak or just another milestone on a continuing climb.

What's Next

Sinner vs. Struff and FAA vs. Djokovic are scheduled for Day 9 and Day 10 of Wimbledon (Tuesday July 7 and Wednesday July 8). The winners advance to the Wimbledon semifinals. With both Sinner and Djokovic in the same half of the draw, a potential all-time semifinal between the defending champion and the record-holder could await — should Struff and FAA have other ideas.

The men's draw, after the upsets and exits of the first week, has narrowed to its most compelling version. These quarterfinals will tell us who wins the title.
