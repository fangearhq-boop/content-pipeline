Osaka Overpowers Sabalenka 6-2, 7-6(2) to Reach First Career Wimbledon Quarterfinal

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | July 6, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Naomi Osaka stepped onto Centre Court on Sunday as a long shot against the world's No. 1 player. Eighty-eight minutes later, she stepped off as a Wimbledon quarterfinalist for the first time in her career — having done something that none of the most decorated players on tour could manage all fortnight.

Osaka defeated Aryna Sabalenka 6-2, 7-6(2) in the fourth round on Day 7, producing a performance of power and controlled aggression that left even Sabalenka struggling to explain what had happened. "She overpowered me," the top seed admitted. "I felt like it was an incredible level from her."

It was, in almost every way, a statement. Osaka's record against Sabalenka had been 1-7 coming in, with the lone previous win coming at the 2018 US Open. Eight years later, on a surface that had historically exposed Osaka's limitations, she made it 2-7 in the most dramatic fashion possible.

Breaking Records En Route

The statistics that surround this win read like a list of improbabilities. Osaka was 0-13 against top-10 players at non-hard-court events before this match. She had won her first Centre Court match at the All England Club. She had been regarded, for most of her career, as a hard-court specialist whose grass results were limited.

In 2026, that narrative is over.

The win over Sabalenka also snapped one of the most impressive records in modern tennis: Sabalenka's 21-match Grand Slam tiebreak winning streak. That streak — the longest by any player, male or female, in the Open Era — came to an end in the second-set tiebreak, which Osaka won 7-2 to close out the match. The stat captures the totality of Osaka's dominance: she did not just beat Sabalenka in straight sets, she beat her in a tiebreak Sabalenka had not lost in more than two years of Grand Slam play.

Osaka's Grass Renaissance

The broader context makes this victory even more significant. Osaka returned from maternity leave in 2024 and spent the following year finding her footing. The grass season of 2026 has been the clearest signal yet that she has found more than that.

She is 8-1 on grass in 2026 — the most grass-court wins she has recorded in any calendar year of her career. Her wins during this stretch have included top-10 opponents, multiple three-set battles that she closed out, and now a dominant straight-sets dismantling of the world No. 1 on the sport's most prestigious court.

Osaka was emotional and effusive afterward. "My team is the best team ever," she said, singling out coach Tomasz Wiktorowski. "I've learned so much and I'm so grateful that they're on this journey with me."

There was joy in those words, but also something else: a sense that this was not a surprise to anyone who had been paying attention. Osaka on grass in 2026 has been a story building for weeks. On Sunday, it arrived at its most dramatic chapter.

What's Next

Osaka advances to the Wimbledon quarterfinals, where she will face the winner of the Day 8 match between Elise Mertens and Marie Bouzkova. Mertens herself has been one of the stories of the tournament, having upset No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina in the third round. The quarterfinal will be scheduled for Day 9 or 10 (Tuesday or Wednesday).

Whatever happens next, Osaka has already accomplished something she had never done before: reached the final eight at the All England Club. In the same fortnight that Sabalenka's Grand Slam tiebreak record fell, the women's draw lost its top seed, and Naomi Osaka made Wimbledon history.
