Alcaraz Targets Cincinnati Return After Wrist Cleared — US Open Defense Now Back On

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 16, 2026

Carlos Alcaraz has appeared on the Cincinnati Open entry list, and reports from his team confirm his right wrist is "completely healed" — the strongest signal yet that the seven-time Grand Slam champion is preparing to return to the ATP Tour after three months on the sidelines.

The 23-year-old Spaniard has not competed since April 14, when he sustained a severe tenosynovitis in his right wrist during a first-round victory at the Barcelona Open. What followed was a spring of enforced absences: Roland Garros, Wimbledon, and the Canadian Open all passed without him.

Wrist Fully Healed, Two Weeks Remain

According to reports from La Verdad and confirmed by multiple outlets on Wednesday, Alcaraz's doctor Angel Ruiz-Cotorro has confirmed the wrist has healed. The next two weeks of high-intensity training will be critical before the team formally confirms his Cincinnati participation — they want to ensure the wrist responds to full match-intensity demands without discomfort before committing.

His team is not taking unnecessary risks. The goal is to have Alcaraz at full capacity, not merely functional. That measured approach has kept him off the Tour longer than many expected but may ultimately protect him through the end of the season.

Stakes: Two Defending Titles

Alcaraz arrives at the North American hard-court swing with significant points at stake. He is the defending champion at the Cincinnati Masters 1000 (scheduled August 13-23) and the defending US Open champion (August 31 – September 13). Both titles from 2025 are due for defense in rapid succession.

Without defending Cincinnati, he would drop 1,000 ranking points. Without defending the US Open, 2,000 more. His current No. 3 ranking has already slipped from No. 2 following Wimbledon, where Alexander Zverev overtook him. A full comeback through both events could restore his position in the top two.

The North American Swing Context

The hard-court summer will be fiercely contested regardless of Alcaraz's presence. Jannik Sinner enters as the world No. 1, fresh from back-to-back Wimbledon titles and confirmed for the Canadian Open. Alexander Zverev, No. 2 and Roland Garros champion, is also in Montreal. The Canadian Open draws on July 29-30.

Alcaraz opted to skip the Canadian Open to prioritize full recovery. Cincinnati — two weeks later — is the chosen battleground for his return. The timing means he would have approximately one week of competitive tennis before the US Open begins, enough to rebuild match sharpness without overloading an injury still within its recovery window.

What's Next

The next two weeks will determine whether Alcaraz formally confirms Cincinnati. If high-intensity training goes without complication, his team expects to announce the return ahead of the tournament's opening rounds. Cincinnati runs August 13-23. The US Open begins August 31.

For now, the tennis world has its answer: Alcaraz is coming back. The only question left is exactly when his name appears in a draw — and how sharp he arrives when it does.
