WTA Athens R2: Sakkari Rolls at Home, Parks Survives Thriller; Quarterfinals Set for Friday

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 16, 2026

The Vanda Pharmaceuticals Athens Open is delivering exactly the kind of tennis its inaugural Greek return demanded. Wednesday's second-round results at the Athens Olympic Tennis Centre were a blend of dominance and drama — with home hero Maria Sakkari providing the former and American Alycia Parks supplying the latter.

The quarterfinals are set for Friday, July 17, and they promise to continue the tournament's compelling opening week.

Sakkari: Three Games in Two Matches

Maria Sakkari has dropped just three games across her two Athens matches. On Wednesday she defeated Harriet Dart 6-1, 6-2, barely giving the British player a foothold in either set. The crowd at the Athens Olympic Tennis Centre was electric from the first game, and Sakkari fed off the energy, playing with the kind of assertive baseline authority that has characterized her best clay performances.

She had opened her Athens account by defeating Polina Kudermetova 6-0, 7-6(1) in the first round. Two matches, minimal resistance. The Greek No. 4 seed looks every inch the title favourite in front of her home fans — but the quarterfinal poses a meaningful test.

Parks: A Three-Set Survival

Alycia Parks reached the last eight the harder way. The American dropped the first set to Mai Hontama 4-6 before fighting back to take the second 6-2 and then grinding out a 7-5 third to advance 6-7(4), 6-2, 7-5. It was the kind of resilient performance that suggests Parks is ready for the quarterfinal challenge ahead.

That challenge happens to be Sakkari — and Parks carries a 2-0 head-to-head record into the match. Whatever the crowd atmosphere, the numbers favor the American on paper. Quarterfinal tennis often has a way of overwriting everything else, however, and a home favourite in full form is a difficult proposition regardless of head-to-head statistics.

Zheng and Bejlek Also Advance

Qinwen Zheng continued her clean form in Athens, defeating Elena Micic 6-4, 6-1 in her second match of the week. Zheng had opened by upsetting No. 6 seed Jessica Bouzas Maneiro 7-5, 6-1 on Day 1 and has won every set she has played in Greece.

Sara Bejlek reached the quarterfinals with a 6-2, 6-2 win over Lilli Tagger on Tuesday, completing the last-eight lineup.

No. 3 seed Barbora Krejcikova and No. 1 seed Clara Tauson are also in the draw. Krejcikova defeated Carole Monnet 6-3, 6-3 and then Ivana Jorovic in R2, while Tauson took down Naomi Osaka conqueror Hibino 7-5, 6-4 in the opening round.

What's Next

The Athens quarterfinals are scheduled for Friday, July 17, with the headline match pitting Sakkari against Parks. Krejcikova, Tauson, and Zheng are also in the draw, meaning the semifinals and final could feature some of the best clay-court players in the world right now.

The Vanda Pharmaceuticals Athens Open continues through Sunday, July 19. It is the first WTA event held in Athens in 35 years, and through two full rounds it has justified every expectation placed on its return to the Greek capital.
