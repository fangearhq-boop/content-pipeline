Bastad Quarterfinals Set: Vallejo Makes Paraguay History, Borges Back in Last 8

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 16, 2026

Wednesday at the Nordea Open in Bastad, Sweden produced the kind of clay-court results that will be talked about beyond this week. Adolfo Daniel Vallejo wrote his country into the ATP record books while Nuno Borges reasserted his status as a genuine Bastad contender.

Both players will be in Thursday's quarterfinals, joined by top seed Andrey Rublev and a bracket that has opened up considerably after a week of upsets and drama on the red clay.

Vallejo: First Paraguayan QF Since 2006

Adolfo Daniel Vallejo — a qualifier — defeated Botic van de Zandschulp 4-6, 6-3, 7-6(4) in two hours and 57 minutes to become the first Paraguayan player to reach a tour-level quarterfinal since Ramon Delgado did it in Mumbai in 2006. That is 20 years of tennis history crossed in a single match on the Swedish clay.

Vallejo rallied after dropping the first set, won the second decisively, then held his nerve in a third-set tiebreak to complete the biggest result of his career. The win also demonstrates how the ATP 250 clay swing continues to create platform moments for players outside the top rankings — the kind of result that can define a career trajectory.

Borges Returns to Familiar Ground

Nuno Borges knows Bastad. The Portuguese No. 16 won the title here in 2024, famously defeating Rafael Nadal in that final in what became one of the year's most celebrated results. On Wednesday he defeated Grigor Dimitrov 6-4, 6-2 to reach his fourth tour-level quarterfinal of the 2026 season.

The result is emphatic — Borges took the second set in just seven games and gave Dimitrov, No. 25 in the rankings, little opportunity to settle. Borges is in the form that won him this title two years ago.

Stefano Travaglia also delivered a statement result, defeating No. 4 seed Mariano Navone 6-4, 6-2 to complete what was a difficult afternoon for the higher-ranked players in the draw.

Thursday's QF Schedule

The quarterfinals are set for Thursday at the Nordea Open with top seed Andrey Rublev headlining the Centre Court schedule alongside Sebastian Baez, Alejandro Tabilo, and Grigor Basilashvili. Borges, Vallejo, and Travaglia complete the last eight, providing a mix of established clay specialists and first-time contenders at this stage.

WTA Iasi Also Produces QF Results

At the simultaneous UniCredit Iasi Open in Romania (WTA 250), four players reached the quarterfinals on Wednesday. Yulia Putintseva defeated Alina Charaeva 6-3, 6-3 and Tamara Zidansek edged Anna Bondar 7-6(5), 6-7(5), 7-5 in a tight three-setter. Clara Burel was dominant, beating Elsa Jacquemot 6-1, 6-1, while Petra Marcinko defeated Elina Avanesyan 7-6(3), 7-6(5) in back-to-back tiebreaks.

What's Next

Bastad quarterfinals are Thursday, July 16, with semifinals on Friday and the final on Saturday, July 18. Iasi runs on a parallel schedule, with both tournaments concluding this weekend before attention turns to next week's clay events — Kitzbühel for the men and Hamburg and Prague for the women — ahead of the North American hard-court swing beginning in late July.
