Gstaad Day 4: Defending Champion Bublik and Former Winner Ruud Finally Take the Court

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 16, 2026

Three days into the EFG Swiss Open Gstaad, two of the tournament's biggest names are only now stepping onto the mountain-air clay courts for the first time. Alexander Bublik and Casper Ruud — defending champion and former champion respectively — received first-round byes and enter the fray on Thursday with the draw already shaped around them.

Their late entries add a fresh dimension to a tournament that has already produced memorable moments, including Stan Wawrinka's emotional farewell in the opening round.

Bublik: Defending His Gstaad Title

Alexander Bublik won the 2025 EFG Swiss Open title and returns to Gstaad as the event's top seed. The Kazakhstani faces Quentin Halys of France in Thursday's R2, scheduled not before 15:00 local time. Bublik is one of clay's most inventive players — his serve, drop shots, and willingness to manufacture points in unconventional ways make him a genuine threat on a surface that rewards ingenuity.

Defending a title at a venue where he has already felt the weight of expectation and delivered is a different kind of challenge. He enters Thursday as the player every competitor is measuring themselves against.

Ruud: Former Champion Faces Faria

Casper Ruud has experience on this tournament, having won Gstaad on multiple previous occasions. He faces Jaime Faria in Thursday's R2 — a match that carries a story of its own. Faria, a Portuguese qualifier, eliminated Stan Wawrinka in the first round on Monday in one of the most emotionally charged matches of the week. Wawrinka, 41, had opened his Gstaad farewell — the venue where he made his ATP Tour debut in 2003 — only to be beaten 6-7(8), 6-4, 6-4 by the younger Faria.

Now Faria must face a former champion to continue his run. For Ruud, there is the motivation of a title pedigree at this event and the knowledge that a deep run at Gstaad sets up the Swiss clay schedule through to Sunday.

Tsitsipas Continues Against Swiss Wild Card

No. 2 seed Stefanos Tsitsipas is already into his next match after defeating No. 5 seed Ignacio Buse 6-4, 6-4 in R2. The Greek faces Swiss wild card Jerome Kym on Thursday, a home crowd match that will bring particular atmosphere to the Gstaad courts. Tsitsipas broke a five-match losing run against Top-50 opponents with the Buse victory, and carries that momentum into the Kym match.

Tournament Structure

With Thursday's matches determining the quarterfinal lineup, the EFG Swiss Open Gstaad is entering its decisive stretch. The quarterfinals are scheduled for Thursday, semifinals for Friday, and the final for Sunday, July 19. The tournament runs on the same week as Bastad and Umag, meaning clay specialists across the world are all competing simultaneously in one of the busiest weeks of the post-Wimbledon clay calendar.

What's Next

All eyes on Thursday at Gstaad: Bublik vs Halys, Ruud vs Faria, and Tsitsipas vs Kym are the marquee R2 matches. Results will set the quarterfinal draw. The EFG Swiss Open concludes Sunday, July 19, with the title on the line for a field that includes two of the sport's most entertaining personalities in Bublik and Tsitsipas.
