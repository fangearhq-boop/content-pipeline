### STORY 3: Bastad QF Lineup

**Long-Form Post**
Window: 9:00 PM ET

The Nordea Open in Bastad served up some memorable moments on Wednesday — and one piece of history no one saw coming.

Adolfo Daniel Vallejo, a Paraguayan qualifier, defeated Botic van de Zandschulp 4-6, 6-3, 7-6(4) in nearly three hours to become the first player from his country to reach a tour-level quarterfinal since Ramon Delgado in Mumbai all the way back in 2006. Two hours and 57 minutes of clay-court warfare for a moment that spans two decades of national tennis history.

Elsewhere, Nuno Borges — who famously won Bastad in 2024 — is right back in the quarterfinals after beating Grigor Dimitrov 6-4, 6-2. Stefano Travaglia added to the drama by knocking out No. 4 seed Mariano Navone 6-4, 6-2.

Meanwhile in Iasi, Yulia Putintseva, Tamara Zidansek, Clara Burel, and Petra Marcinko all advanced to their quarterfinals on Wednesday.

The clay swing is barely a week old and already delivering upsets, history, and drama.
