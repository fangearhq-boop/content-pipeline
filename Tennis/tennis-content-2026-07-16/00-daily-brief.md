### STORY 1: Burruchaga Stuns Cobolli at Umag — First Top-10 Win of Career
**Tier: 1 | Platforms: X, FB, Article**

Roman Andres Burruchaga (ARG, No. 67) defeated top seed Flavio Cobolli (ITA, No. 9) 6-2, 6-4 in the R2 of the Plava Laguna Croatia Open Umag on Wednesday July 15. The ATP Tour called it "one of the best matches of the year." It was the first Top-10 win of Burruchaga's career. Cobolli was competing for the first time since reaching the Wimbledon QF. Burruchaga won 78% (25/32) of his first-serve points and converted 4 of 7 break points. All 13 of his career tour-level wins have come on clay. Cobolli was unable to find his top level after two weeks off following his Wimbledon deep run. QFs at Umag are scheduled for July 16 (today): ADF vs Molcan, Arnaldi vs Dzumhur, Droguet vs Merida Aguilar, and Burruchaga in his first career ATP QF.

### STORY 2: Alcaraz Appears on Cincinnati Entry List — Return Imminent After Three Months Out
**Tier: 1 | Platforms: X, FB, Article**

Carlos Alcaraz (ESP, No. 3) has appeared on the Cincinnati Open entry list, the strongest indication yet that the seven-time Grand Slam champion is targeting a competitive return in August. His wrist is reported to be "completely healed" following tenosynovitis sustained April 14 at the Barcelona Open. His team says the next two weeks will be critical before formally confirming his participation, as they want to ensure the wrist handles high-intensity training without discomfort. Cincinnati (Masters 1000, August 13-23) would be his first tournament since April. He would then target the US Open (August 31–September 13), where he is the defending champion. He also defends his Cincinnati title. Alcaraz has missed Roland-Garros, Wimbledon, and the Canadian Open from the same injury.
