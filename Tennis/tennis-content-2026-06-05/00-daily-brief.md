### STORY 1: Andreeva Stuns Kostyuk 6-1, 6-3 — Into First Grand Slam Final
**Tier:** 1 | **Type:** Match Result (FOLLOW UP) | **Window:** 8:00 AM ET

Mirra Andreeva dismantled Marta Kostyuk 6-1, 6-3 in 76 minutes on Court Philippe-Chatrier on Thursday, booking her place in a first career Grand Slam final. Andreeva, 19, becomes the fourth-youngest Roland Garros women's finalist in 30 years — only Martina Hingis, Kim Clijsters and Coco Gauff were younger at the same stage. Kostyuk had entered the match with a stunning 17-0 clay-court record in 2026 and a 2-0 head-to-head advantage over Andreeva this season (Brisbane and Madrid finals), but Andreeva controlled every phase. At net after the match, there was no handshake — Kostyuk has publicly stated she will not shake hands with Russian players while the war in Ukraine continues. The episode sparked controversy across social media.

### STORY 2: Chwalinska Makes History — First Qualifier in Roland Garros Final
**Tier:** 1 | **Type:** Match Result (FOLLOW UP) | **Window:** 10:30 AM ET

Maja Chwalinska defeated Diana Shnaider 7-6(4), 6-4 on Thursday to become the first qualifier in Roland Garros history to reach the final, and only the second woman qualifier to reach a Grand Slam final in the Open Era. The 24-year-old Polish left-hander was ranked World No. 114 entering the tournament and had managed just two career tour-level clay wins before arriving in Paris. She is also the third woman ever to reach the Roland Garros final on her main-draw debut, joining Evonne Goolagong (1971) and Chris Evert (1973). Women's final: Andreeva vs Chwalinska on Saturday June 6.

### STORY 3: Zverev vs Mensik — Grand Slam Ghost Meets 20-Year-Old Czech (Today, 2:30 PM Paris)
**Tier:** 1 | **Type:** Match Preview | **Window:** 9:00 AM ET

Alexander Zverev (No. 2 seed) and 20-year-old Jakub Mensik (No. 26 seed) meet today on Court Philippe-Chatrier in the first men's semifinal. Zverev is 0-3 in Grand Slam finals — US Open 2020, Roland Garros 2024, Australian Open 2025 — and this represents his clearest path to a first major title with Alcaraz, Sinner and Djokovic all out of the draw. Mensik became the first Czech man to reach a Grand Slam semifinal in nearly a decade and is in his first-ever Slam final four at age 20. He defeated João Fonseca in four sets in the quarterfinals to set up this matchup.

### STORY 4: All-Italian SF — Cobolli vs Arnaldi (Today, 7:00 PM Paris / 1:00 PM ET)
**Tier:** 1 | **Type:** Match Preview (FOLLOW UP) | **Window:** 12:30 PM ET

The second men's semifinal today pits No. 10 seed Flavio Cobolli against unseeded Matteo Arnaldi in the first all-Italian men's Grand Slam semifinal in history. Both players are in their maiden Slam semifinal. Cobolli, who has lost just one set through five rounds and clinched a debut top-10 ATP ranking, enters as the slight favourite; Arnaldi (ranked No. 104) is the tournament's revelation after five consecutive victories, including a walkover when Berrettini retired injured. Italy is guaranteed a men's finalist for the first time since Adriano Panatta won Roland Garros in 1976.

### STORY 5: Women's Final Preview — Andreeva vs Chwalinska (Saturday June 6)
**Tier:** 2 | **Type:** Preview | **Window:** 6:00 PM ET

The Roland Garros women's final on Saturday June 6 will pit No. 8 seed Mirra Andreeva (Russia, 19, born April 29 2007) against qualifier Maja Chwalinska (Poland, 24, World No. 114) in a matchup that tennis has never seen before. Neither player has ever appeared in a Grand Slam final. Andreeva is the heavy favourite on rankings and form, but Chwalinska has already defied every prediction in the draw. The final is scheduled to begin at 3:00 PM Paris time (9:00 AM ET).
