Zverev vs Mensik: The Grand Slam Ghost and the 20-Year-Old Czech Meet in Roland Garros SF

By Ryan Calloway | June 5, 2026

Alexander Zverev has been here before. Semifinals of Roland Garros, the red clay of Philippe-Chatrier under his feet, a title within reach. He has been here — or somewhere very like it — three times at other Grand Slams as well, and left each time without the trophy. He has never won a major. He is the second seed here in 2026 and, with the draw having collapsed around him over the past two weeks, he has never had a better chance.

On Friday at 2:30 PM Paris time, he faces 20-year-old Jakub Mensik of the Czech Republic — seeded No. 26, in his first Grand Slam semifinal, born in 2005. The match is a collision of experience-without-a-title and youth-without-a-ceiling, and Roland Garros cannot quite contain the narrative.

Zverev's Haunted History

The résumé demands attention. At the 2020 US Open, Zverev led Dominic Thiem by two sets to zero and still lost in five. At the 2024 Roland Garros final, he led Carlos Alcaraz two sets to one before Alcaraz came back to win. At the 2025 Australian Open, Jannik Sinner beat him in three sets without a tiebreak in sight.

Three finals. Three defeats. Each one with its own story about what went wrong, and collectively a story about a player who has repeatedly been close to the summit without reaching it.

The 2026 Roland Garros draw, however, looks nothing like what he has faced before. Alcaraz is absent with a wrist injury that kept him out of the whole tournament. Sinner was eliminated in the second round by Francisco Cerundolo. Djokovic fell to João Fonseca in the third. By the time the semifinals arrived, the two finalists from the past two years were both out, the defending champion was out, and the world No. 1 was out.

Zverev has reached at least the semifinal in five of his last six Roland Garros appearances. He is ninth in the Open Era to achieve five-plus Paris semifinals. The question is whether he can convert one of them.

Mensik's Breakthrough

Jakub Mensik was born on September 1, 2005. He is 20 years old. He has never been past the quarterfinals of a Grand Slam — until now. His path to this semifinal included a win over No. 8 seed Alex de Minaur, a match in which he came back after losing the first set without winning a game. He then defeated Brazilian teenager João Fonseca in a quarterfinal on Philippe-Chatrier that the crowd embraced with the kind of energy reserved for the sport's most compelling young players.

Mensik is the first Czech man to reach a Grand Slam semifinal in nearly a decade. His career-high ranking of No. 12 was achieved earlier this year. He arrived as the No. 26 seed and has played well above that number throughout the fortnight.

The Czech has two ATP titles to his name — both on hard courts — but clay has increasingly become a surface where his game translates. His movement, his ability to hit through the ball from anywhere on the court, and his serving percentage in pressure moments have all impressed observers during this run. Whether that transfers into a semifinal against the most experienced player in the draw is the central question of the afternoon.

What's at Stake

The winner advances to the Roland Garros men's final on Sunday June 7, where they will face the winner of the all-Italian semifinal between Flavio Cobolli and Matteo Arnaldi — scheduled for later on Friday.

For Zverev, it is the opportunity he has been building toward since he first cracked the top 10 as a teenager. For Mensik, it is confirmation that Czech tennis has a generational talent capable of competing at the sport's highest level.

One of them will be in a Grand Slam final on Sunday. The clay of Philippe-Chatrier decides which one on Friday afternoon.

What's Next

Zverev vs Mensik is scheduled to begin at 2:30 PM Paris time (8:30 AM ET) on Court Philippe-Chatrier. The men's final is Sunday June 7. The Roland Garros women's final — Andreeva vs Chwalinska — is the day before, on Saturday June 6.
