Chwalinska Makes History: First Qualifier Ever to Reach Roland Garros Final

By Marcus Cole | June 5, 2026

Before Maja Chwalinska arrived at Roland Garros 2026, she had won two matches on clay at the professional tour level. Her entire career. She came through qualifying rounds that almost no finalist ever sees. She was ranked No. 114 in the world.

On Thursday she walked off Court Suzanne-Lenglen as the first qualifier in Roland Garros history to reach the final — having defeated Diana Shnaider 7-6(4), 6-4 in an hour and forty minutes to complete one of the most remarkable runs the tournament has ever witnessed.

"I don't know what's going on," Chwalinska said after the match. It was the most honest sentence of the tournament.

The Match

Thursday's semifinal pitted two left-handers against each other — Chwalinska and No. 25 seed Shnaider — in a match that had been billed as anything but predictable. Shnaider had reached this stage by eliminating World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka in the quarterfinals, winning the final 10 games of that match in a devastating display.

The first set went to a tiebreak, which Chwalinska won 7-4. From there she controlled the second set more comfortably, breaking at a decisive moment to claim a 5-4 lead and serving out the match. The scoreline barely captures how composed she looked under pressure — a qualifier, ranked No. 114, with the weight of history on her shoulders, and barely a tremor.

The Names She Joins

Chwalinska is only the second woman in the Open Era to reach a Grand Slam final as a qualifier. She is also just the third woman to reach the Roland Garros final on her main draw debut — joining Evonne Goolagong, who did it in 1971, and Chris Evert, who did it in 1973. Goolagong won that year. Evert won when she returned the following year.

The 24-year-old Polish left-hander came to Paris with this as her third appearance in a Grand Slam main draw, having appeared at 2022 Wimbledon and the 2025 Australian Open before this. Neither of those tournaments prepared observers for what she has done in Paris over the past two weeks.

The Broader Picture

Chwalinska's run is part of a broader story that has defined this Roland Garros: the complete absence of the sport's established elite from the latter stages. Jannik Sinner was eliminated in the second round. Carlos Alcaraz withdrew before the tournament with a wrist injury. Novak Djokovic fell in the third round. Iga Swiatek, who had reached four consecutive Roland Garros finals, was beaten in the fourth round.

Into the vacuum stepped a 24-year-old from Poland who had never won more than two clay-court matches at tour level in a season. She just kept winning, and the tennis world kept watching in something between disbelief and delight.

The final projection suggests Chwalinska's ranking could rise by approximately 100 places following her run, regardless of Saturday's outcome. That kind of seismic shift from a single tournament appearance is rare at any level.

What's Next

Maja Chwalinska faces No. 8 seed Mirra Andreeva in the Roland Garros women's final on Saturday, June 6, not before 3:00 PM Paris time (9:00 AM ET). Andreeva, 19, defeated Marta Kostyuk 6-1, 6-3 in Thursday's other semifinal. Both players are in their first Grand Slam final. Andreeva enters as the clear favourite on rankings and form, but Chwalinska has already been the underdog in every match she has played here, and she has kept winning.
