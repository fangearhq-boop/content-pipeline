Cobolli vs Arnaldi: Italy's Historic All-Italian Grand Slam Semifinal Takes Center Stage Today

By Elena Voss | June 5, 2026

In 1976, Adriano Panatta won the Roland Garros men's title, becoming the last Italian man to claim a Grand Slam trophy. Fifty years later, on a Friday evening at Court Philippe-Chatrier, Italy will confirm its return to the men's final — and it will do so through a match that has never happened before in the history of Grand Slam tennis.

Flavio Cobolli vs Matteo Arnaldi. Two Italians. Two players in the first Grand Slam semifinal of their careers. The first all-Italian men's Grand Slam semifinal ever played. Scheduled start: 7:00 PM Paris time (1:00 PM ET).

Cobolli: The Measured Contender

Cobolli arrived at Roland Garros seeded No. 10 and has delivered on that billing with a controlled, methodical campaign. He has dropped just one set in five matches. His quarterfinal against No. 4 seed Felix Auger-Aliassime — a 4-6, 6-4, 6-4, 6-4 win on Wednesday — showed both his resilience when a set down and his ability to dictate play when he finds his range on clay.

The victory confirmed Cobolli's debut in the ATP top 10. He enters the semifinal as the slight favourite given his seeding and consistency, with a path to the final that has looked more sustainable than his opponent's has been dramatic.

Arnaldi: The Tournament's Revelation

Matteo Arnaldi arrived in Paris ranked No. 104 in the world. He is unseeded, largely unheralded outside of Italy, and arrives at his first Grand Slam semifinal having already done far more than anyone expected.

Five matches, five wins — including an advancement to the semifinals when Matteo Berrettini retired from their third-round match with a left hip injury while trailing 7-5, 2-5. That fortunate path has not diminished what Arnaldi has produced in other matches, where he has shown the kind of grit and tactical variety that tends to produce long careers. His run in Paris has generated attention that his ranking did not previously command.

The contrast with Cobolli is significant. One came in as a seeded contender and has played like one. The other came in ranked 94 spots lower and has made the final four on merit. Their meeting tonight is a story no one entirely predicted when the draw was made.

History on Both Sides

The all-Italian Slam semifinal has a brief but meaningful history. In 1960, Nicola Pietrangeli and Orlando Sirola contested a Grand Slam semifinal at Roland Garros. In 2025, Jannik Sinner and Lorenzo Musetti met at the same stage of the same tournament. But in both cases, the all-Italian pairing was in a semifinal, not a final, and Italy's run ended without producing a champion from the Italian matchup.

Tonight, one of these two will break that pattern — advancing to Sunday's men's final and guaranteeing Italy's first Grand Slam men's finalist since Panatta's championship run 50 years ago.

What's Next

Cobolli vs Arnaldi is scheduled to begin at 7:00 PM Paris time (1:00 PM ET) on Court Philippe-Chatrier on Friday June 5. The men's final is Sunday June 7. The winner will face the result of the first semifinal between Alexander Zverev and Jakub Mensik, scheduled for 2:30 PM Paris time earlier the same day.
