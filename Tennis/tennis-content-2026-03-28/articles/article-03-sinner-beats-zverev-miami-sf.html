Sinner Beats Zverev 6-3, 7-6(4) to Reach Miami Final

By Tennis Fanrecap | March 28, 2026

Jannik Sinner reached the Miami Open final Friday night with a 6-3, 7-6(4) win over Alexander Zverev — a result that was nearly comfortable, with moments that were not. The No. 2 seed was clinical from the baseline, dominant with his serve, and composed when Zverev threatened to turn the match.

How It Unfolded

Sinner set the tone quickly in the first set, breaking Zverev's serve and winning the opening set 6-3 without major drama. The second set was different. Zverev steadied himself, found his range from the baseline, and converted his opportunities to hold serve. He saved all three break points Sinner created in the set — a sign that Zverev was prepared to fight for two hours if needed.

The second set went to a tiebreak, and that is where the match was decided. Sinner led 7-4, taking the opening in cold blood. There was no extended battle, no dramatic swings — just a player who has made high-pressure points look routine winning the tiebreak with the economy that has become his trademark.

The Numbers

Sinner's serving statistics were the story of the match: 15 aces, 0 double faults, 74% of first serves in. He was barely tested on serve — Zverev saved break points but was not generating consistent pressure on Sinner's service games. The Italian's return game was sharp enough to create opportunities without being the dominant force it sometimes is.

The result extends Sinner's current winning run over Zverev to seven consecutive matches. The H2H stands at 8-4 in Sinner's favor. Their last three meetings have ended in straight sets.

Zverev's Season in Context

Zverev has been one of the best players on tour in 2026, and his Miami run reflected that. He was broken just once across his five matches before Friday's semifinal and arrived with legitimate confidence. But Sinner has become the opponent he simply cannot find an answer for — and Friday's tiebreak, where the Italian was always in control, was the latest illustration of that dynamic.

What's Next

Sinner faces 21st-seeded Jiří Lehečka in Sunday's ATP final. A win would give Sinner the Sunshine Double — Indian Wells and Miami in the same fortnight — which no man has achieved since Roger Federer in 2017. The final is scheduled for Sunday, March 29 at Hard Rock Stadium.
