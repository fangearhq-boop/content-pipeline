Lehečka Dominates Fils 6-2, 6-2, Reaches First Masters 1000 Final

By Tennis Fanrecap | March 28, 2026

This was not supposed to be easy. Arthur Fils came into Friday's Miami Open semifinal having produced one of the tournament's signature moments — saving four match points against Tommy Paul in the quarterfinals — and was considered a legitimate threat with his heavy forehand and clean serve. Jiří Lehečka did not care.

The 24-year-old Czech dismantled Fils 6-2, 6-2 in 75 minutes on Friday afternoon, booking his place in his first Masters 1000 final with one of the most comprehensive performances of his career.

A Service Performance to Remember

The defining feature of Lehečka's Miami run has been his serve, and Friday was no exception. He recorded 6 aces, zero double faults, and won 82% of his first-serve points. More remarkably, he did not face a single break point in the match — the fifth consecutive Miami match without giving up his serve.

Across all five matches at this year's Miami Open, Lehečka has not been broken once. In a tournament that features some of the world's best returners, that record is extraordinary. He has converted service games with the efficiency of a player who treats his serve as an unconditional weapon rather than a tool that sometimes needs defending.

Fils Had Little Left

Fils, the 21-year-old Frenchman, had showed enormous heart earlier in the week against Tommy Paul, saving four match points in a quarterfinal that went to a third-set tiebreak. But by the time he met Lehečka on Friday afternoon, the physical and emotional toll was evident. Fils managed just 33% of points on his second serve and struggled to put pressure on Lehečka at any stage. The head-to-head between them is now level at 2-2.

Czech Tennis Continues Its Miami Moment

Lehečka becomes the 8th Czech player to reach a Masters 1000 final since the series began in 1990. The timing carries extra weight: just one year ago, his compatriot Jakub Menšík won this title, defeating Novak Djokovic in the final. Back-to-back Czech champions at Miami is now a genuine possibility.

After the match, Lehečka reflected on the run: "Today was a great match. Definitely brought me a lot of confidence going into the final. To return well was one of my goals today. I didn't want him to have time for his forehand. His forehand is amazing."

What's Next

Lehečka faces Jannik Sinner in Sunday's ATP final at Hard Rock Stadium. Sinner is the overwhelming favorite — world No. 2, winner of Indian Wells, and in the form of his life. But Lehečka arrives in the final with momentum that is impossible to ignore: an unbroken serve across an entire Masters 1000 field, a 75-minute semifinal rout, and nothing to lose on a stage he has never reached before.

The question for Sunday is whether Sinner's elite returning game can do what no other player in Miami has managed — finally break Lehečka's serve.
