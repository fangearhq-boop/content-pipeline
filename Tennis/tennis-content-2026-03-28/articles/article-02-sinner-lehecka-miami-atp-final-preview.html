Sinner vs. Lehečka: Can Anyone Stop the Sunshine Double?

By Tennis Fanrecap | March 28, 2026

Sunday's Miami Open men's final is set: No. 2 seed Jannik Sinner against 21st-seeded Jiří Lehečka. On paper, it's a clear mismatch in seedings. In reality, this has every ingredient for an intriguing match — and Lehečka is nobody's definition of a walkover.

Sinner's Mission: The Sunshine Double

Sinner arrived in Miami having already won Indian Wells, and a victory on Sunday would complete the Sunshine Double — sweeping the two biggest hard-court events in the Americas in back-to-back weeks. The last man to accomplish that was Roger Federer in 2017. Sinner has been the most consistent player in men's tennis for more than a year, and the Miami draw has done nothing to challenge that reputation.

Friday night's semifinal against Alexander Zverev was the sternest test he has faced in Miami. Sinner won 6-3, 7-6(4), serving with precision — 15 aces, zero double faults, 74% first serves in — and closing the match in a tiebreak when Zverev forced one. He leads the H2H with Zverev 8-4 and has won seven consecutive meetings. Now he faces an opponent he has met rarely, on a week where Lehečka has been unstoppable.

Lehečka's Unprecedented Service Run

The most extraordinary statistic in this Miami draw belongs to Jiří Lehečka: across five matches in the tournament, the 24-year-old Czech has not had his serve broken once. Not in the first round, not in the quarterfinals, and not in his 6-2, 6-2, 75-minute semifinal demolition of Arthur Fils on Friday afternoon.

That kind of service dominance — facing and winning every service game across an entire Masters 1000 event — is rare at any level. He recorded 6 aces, 0 double faults, and won 82% of first-serve points against Fils. For Sinner, who is an elite returner, neutralizing Lehečka's serve will be the central challenge.

Lehečka is the 8th Czech player to reach a Masters 1000 final since the series began in 1990. One year ago, compatriot Jakub Menšík won this very tournament, upsetting Novak Djokovic in the final. Czech tennis has a chance for back-to-back Miami titles.

Rankings Stakes

Beyond the trophy, there are significant ATP rankings implications. Sinner currently sits No. 2 in the world. If he wins the Miami title, he closes the gap to No. 1 Carlos Alcaraz from 2,140 points to 1,240 points. Alcaraz then enters the clay season as the defending champion at multiple major events, with thousands of points to defend. The path back to No. 1 for Sinner runs directly through a strong clay campaign — and a Miami title would be the ideal launching pad.

For Lehečka, a title would represent the biggest win of his career and a significant ranking jump into the Top 20.

What's Next

Sunday's final at Hard Rock Stadium follows Saturday's WTA Final. The clay-court season begins the following week in Houston, Bucharest, and Marrakesh, though neither finalist has confirmed their immediate post-Miami schedule.

What is clear: both players arrive at Sunday's final in career-best form. Sinner as the machine who rarely makes mistakes; Lehečka as the server who hasn't been broken in five matches. One of those remarkable runs will end on Sunday. The question is which one.
