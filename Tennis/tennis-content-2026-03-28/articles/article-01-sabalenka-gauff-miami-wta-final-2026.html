Sabalenka vs. Gauff: The 2026 WTA Miami Final Has Everything

By Tennis Fanrecap | March 28, 2026

There are tennis finals and then there are tennis finals. Saturday's Miami Open women's final — No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka against No. 4 Coco Gauff at Hard Rock Stadium — falls firmly in the second category.

The two best players in women's tennis right now will meet for the 13th time on tour, with the head-to-head locked at 6-6. Every match they have played has carried weight. This one carries the most so far in 2026.

Sabalenka: Dominant, Clinical, Relentless

Sabalenka's road through the Miami draw has been as dominant as any she has produced. Six matches, zero sets lost. She defeated Ann Li, Caty McNally, Zheng Qinwen, Hailey Baptiste, and Elena Rybakina without ever being taken to a third set. The semifinal against Rybakina — potentially the most dangerous match on paper — ended 6-4, 6-3 in an hour and 19 minutes.

The Belarus native is 21-3 on the season, the most wins of any player on tour. She already captured the Brisbane International and the Indian Wells title this year. A Miami title on Saturday would give her the Sunshine Double — sweeping the Indian Wells and Miami back-to-back — a feat no woman has achieved since... well, Sabalenka herself last year. She is also the defending Miami champion.

The WTA world No. 1 ranking has been hers for 64 consecutive weeks heading into the final.

Gauff: Battle-Tested and Peaking at the Right Moment

Gauff's path to the final looked very different. Four of her five matches before Saturday went to three sets. She survived a tricky first week, grinding through Cirstea, Bencic, and others in extended contests that would have worn down a less mentally tough player. Then came the semifinal against Karolína Muchová — and a complete gear change.

Gauff dismantled Muchová 6-1, 6-1 in 89 minutes. She won 80% of second-serve return points and produced one of the most one-sided semifinal performances in recent Miami history. The American is 6-0 all-time against Muchová, and Thursday's performance suggested she has saved her best tennis for the final weekend.

At 21, Gauff is playing in front of what amounts to a home crowd at Hard Rock Stadium in Miami — familiar courts, supportive fans, and the hunger of a player who has not yet won her home tournament.

The Head-to-Head: A Perfect 6-6 Tie

The H2H tells the story of two elite players who have never found a consistent edge over the other. Six wins apiece across 12 meetings. Three of those meetings have come in finals, with each player winning at least once. Saturday's final is their fourth meeting with a title on the line.

Sabalenka's power off both wings and aggressive service game give her an edge in fast-court conditions. Gauff's all-court game, improved serve, and ability to absorb pace and redirect make her dangerous from any position. On paper, the matchup is as close as any rivalry in women's tennis.

What's Next

The winner of Saturday's final collects $1,151,380 in prize money. For Sabalenka, a title means the Sunshine Double — back-to-back wins in Indian Wells and Miami — and further extending her lead at the top of the WTA rankings. For Gauff, it means her 12th career title and a first home tournament crown at the most important event near where she grew up in Delray Beach, Florida.

After Miami, the tour shifts to clay. The French Open is the season's ultimate clay target, and both players will be bidding to carry their hard-court form through the transition. Saturday's result — and how each player performs under maximum pressure — will set the tone for the spring season ahead.

Match time: Not before 3:00 PM ET at Hard Rock Stadium.
