### Long-Form Post ``` Two significant off-court stories surfaced Friday, and both carry a bittersweet tone. First: David Goffin announced via Instagram that 2026 will be his final season on the ATP Tour. The Belgian is 35 years old, has been battling a knee injury, and after 17+ years of professional tennis, has decided this is the right time to close the chapter. Goffin's career deserves more recognition than it often received — he reached No. 7 in the world, won six ATP titles, and made history as the first Belgian ever to break into the Top 10. He also knocked out both Federer and Nadal at the 2017 ATP Finals in London. Plans for after retirement include a personal documentary, coaching work, and — apparently — a marathon. Second: Novak Djokovic has confirmed his withdrawal from the Monte Carlo Masters (April 5-12) due to his ongoing right shoulder injury. It will be Djokovic's first absence from Monte Carlo since 2011 — a stretch of 14 consecutive appearances now coming to an end. He skipped Indian Wells and Miami as well, meaning he'll go into the clay season rusty. Monte Carlo will proceed headlined by Alcaraz, Sinner, and Zverev, with Djokovic hoping to return at Madrid in late April. Two of the sport's most decorated players navigating very different ends to their stories.
