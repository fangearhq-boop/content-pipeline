## Top Stories Today

### STORY 1: Cirstea Stuns Sabalenka 2-6, 6-3, 7-5 — French Open Alarm Bells
**Tier:** 1
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka crashed out of the Italian Open in the third round, losing 2-6, 6-3, 7-5 to Romania's Sorana Cirstea. Sabalenka was hampered by a lower back/hip injury, calling a medical timeout trailing 3-4 in the final set. It marked the first career win over a world No. 1 for the 36-year-old Cirstea, who is competing in her final professional season. The defeat raises serious concerns for Sabalenka's Roland Garros preparations — the French Open begins May 24.

### STORY 2: Paolini's Title Defense Ends — Mertens Saves 3 Match Points in 4-6, 7-6(5), 6-3 Win
**Tier:** 1
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Defending Italian Open champion Jasmine Paolini fell to Belgium's Elise Mertens 4-6, 7-6(5), 6-3 in a 2h44m battle at Foro Italico. Paolini squandered three match points at 5-6 in the second set before Mertens forced a tiebreak and ran away with it 7-5. Mertens then dominated the decider to advance to the round of 16. Mertens now leads their H2H 5-2. The home crowd's hopes of seeing Paolini defend her title are over.

### STORY 3: Sinner Def. Ofner 6-3, 6-4 — M1000 Streak Reaches 29, Ties Federer
**Tier:** 1
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Jannik Sinner opened his Italian Open campaign in clinical fashion, defeating Sebastian Ofner 6-3, 6-4 in 1 hour 31 minutes on Saturday. The world No. 1 extended his ATP Masters 1000 winning streak to 29 consecutive matches, tying Roger Federer for the third-longest run in series history. Sinner did not concede a single break point in either set. He next faces Alexei Popyrin in the third round. His Career Golden Masters pursuit — Rome is the only M1000 title he has yet to win — continues.
