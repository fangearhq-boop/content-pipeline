Mertens Saves Three Match Points to End Paolini's Italian Open Title Defense

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

May 10, 2026

Defending Italian Open champion Jasmine Paolini surrendered three match points in the second set before Belgium's Elise Mertens took control to win 4-6, 7-6(5), 6-3, ending Paolini's title defense at Foro Italico.

Jasmine Paolini had done the hard part. She had won the first set against a seasoned opponent, held her composure as the match grew tight, and reached 5-6 in the second set with three match points in hand — three separate opportunities to close out a third-round Italian Open win in front of the Foro Italico crowd that had been cheering for her all week.

She converted none of them.

Elise Mertens, 29, playing with the calm of someone entirely unfazed by the noise in the stands, held serve three times under the most extreme pressure. She forced a tiebreak. She won it 7-5. And then she took the third set 6-3 with something approaching ease, completing a 4-6, 7-6(5), 6-3 victory in 2 hours and 44 minutes that eliminated the defending champion from her own tournament.

Three Points That Changed Everything

The three match points at 5-6 in the second set will be the images that linger from this match. Each time Paolini stood within a point of sealing the set, Mertens found an answer — a well-placed serve, a sharp groundstroke, a level of tactical precision that the moment required.

In professional tennis, the mental shift that happens when a player fails to convert multiple match points is well documented. Shoulders drop. Doubts creep in. The player who was close to winning suddenly becomes the one fighting to survive. That shift was visible in Paolini's body language as Mertens dominated the tiebreak 7-5 and then powered through the third set without a serious challenge.

It was a reminder of Mertens' quality at her best — composed, precise, and capable of raising her level in the moments that matter. She now leads the head-to-head against Paolini 5-2, a record that reflects their career history as a difficult matchup for the Italian.

Home Hopes Extinguished

Paolini arrived at the Italian Open as one of the most popular players in the field. Last year's champion, playing on home clay, in front of a crowd that treats Foro Italico as a national celebration — the conditions were ideal for a deep run.

She had shown form in the opening rounds, navigating difficult matches with the resilience that defines her game. But three unconverted match points in the third round left her Italian Open defense in pieces. With Aryna Sabalenka also eliminated on the same day, Saturday was the harshest possible reminder that defending a title is among the hardest tasks in tennis.

Italy still has Jannik Sinner alive in the men's draw, and the Foro Italico crowd will direct its energy toward him in the coming days. But for Paolini, the title is gone.

What's Next

Elise Mertens advances to the Italian Open round of 16, where she will carry the momentum of one of the results of her season so far. Jasmine Paolini's Italian Open comes to an end, but the French Open is just 14 days away — and the defending champion's form and confidence, when not undone by three missed match points in one afternoon, remains very much a factor on clay.
