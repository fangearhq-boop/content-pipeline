Gauff and Andreeva Into Rome R4 — WTA Draw Wide Open After Sabalenka and Paolini Fall

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

May 10, 2026

Coco Gauff and Mirra Andreeva both advanced to the Italian Open round of 16 on Saturday, with the WTA draw thrown wide open after world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka and defending champion Jasmine Paolini were eliminated on the same day.

By the time the evening matches were complete at Foro Italico on Saturday, the WTA Italian Open draw had been transformed. The world No. 1 was out. The defending champion was out. And two players who had spent the week quietly winning — Coco Gauff and Mirra Andreeva — had advanced to the round of 16 with a clear view of the title.

Gauff's path through Solana Sierra was not smooth at the outset. The American, seeded No. 3, dropped the first set 5-7 before re-establishing control with a dominant 6-0 second set and a composed 6-4 close. Andreeva, meanwhile, overcame a mid-match wobble to defeat Viktorija Golubic 6-1, 4-6, 6-0 — an emphatic finish to a match that had threatened to become complicated when the Golubic won the second set.

A Draw Transformed

When the Italian Open began, Aryna Sabalenka was the clear favorite on the women's side. The world No. 1 had compiled one of the most dominant starts to a season in recent memory. And Jasmine Paolini, the home-crowd darling defending her title at Foro Italico, was the sentimental favorite to make a deep run.

Neither player made it through the third round. Sabalenka's back and hip injury cost her in a 2-6, 6-3, 7-5 loss to Sorana Cirstea. Paolini squandered three match points before losing 4-6, 7-6(5), 6-3 to Elise Mertens. Two of the biggest names in the tournament were gone before the round of 16 had even begun.

It creates an opportunity that Gauff and Andreeva are well placed to take. Gauff has never won the Italian Open. The 2026 Italian Open round of 16 may represent the clearest path of her career to a first Rome title. Andreeva, who has been in exceptional form on clay all season, will be equally motivated.

What's Next

Coco Gauff and Mirra Andreeva advance to the Italian Open round of 16, where the draw now sits without its top seed and its defending champion. Both players enter the second week of the tournament with significant title ambitions — and two weeks before Roland Garros, the form they build in Rome matters beyond this fortnight alone.
