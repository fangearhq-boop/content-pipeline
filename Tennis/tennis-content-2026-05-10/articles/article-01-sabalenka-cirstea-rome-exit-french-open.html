Sabalenka Falls to Cirstea in Rome — Back Injury Puts French Open in Doubt

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

May 10, 2026

World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka was beaten 2-6, 6-3, 7-5 by Sorana Cirstea in the Italian Open third round, leaving Rome with a lower back and hip injury and serious questions about her Roland Garros preparation.

There was a moment in the second set when the scoreboard told one story and Aryna Sabalenka's body told another. She had won the first set 6-2, efficiently and without incident. She had led 2-0 in the second. And then, almost imperceptibly at first, Sorana Cirstea began to find the match.

By the time the third set reached 3-4 and Sabalenka called for a medical timeout, the story was no longer about tennis tactics. The world No. 1 was in pain, moving with a restriction she could not overcome, her groundstrokes stripped of the full rotation that makes them so devastating.

When it ended — a 2-6, 6-3, 7-5 defeat — Sabalenka spoke plainly. "I'd say that probably it's like my lower back, connected to the hip, which kind of like limiting me from the full rotation," she said. "Felt like my body was limiting me from performing on the highest level."

Cirstea's Career Moment

For Sorana Cirstea, this was a result 20 years in the making. The 36-year-old Romanian had never beaten a world No. 1 in her career, across a professional season that has stretched through the entirety of the Open Era's modern era. She is competing in what she has confirmed is her final season as a professional.

And she delivered when the pressure was at its highest. After Sabalenka built her 2-0 lead in the second set, Cirstea changed patterns, hitting deeper and forcing errors. She took the second set 6-3. In the third, despite Sabalenka's medical timeout attempting to reset the momentum, Cirstea held firm to close out 7-5 and advance to the round of 16.

It was a reminder that, even in the final chapter of a professional career, there can be defining moments. Cirstea will carry this one for life.

A Troubling Pattern

Saturday's loss was not an isolated stumble. Just one week earlier, at the Madrid Open, Sabalenka had been beaten in the quarterfinals by American Hailey Baptiste — a player ranked well outside the top 50 who saved six match points in the decisive set. Two consecutive early exits at major clay events is a sequence that demands explanation.

Sabalenka entered 2026 at a level of dominance that bordered on untouchable. Her season record before the Cirstea loss was 29-1. She had reached the final of the Australian Open. She had won multiple top-level titles. And yet here she is, leaving Rome early for the second consecutive week, and doing so this time with a physical concern that has no clear timeline.

She has never won the Italian Open in eight appearances at the tournament. Saturday's exit means that record continues — and her preparation for the clay season's biggest prize is now severely disrupted.

The French Open Question

Roland Garros begins its main draw on May 24 — 14 days from now. Sabalenka said she hopes to "regain her fitness in time" for Paris, but that language is notably cautious for a player who, even in injury discussions, tends toward confidence.

The timing is particularly painful because the Roland Garros field is now so open. Carlos Alcaraz is out with a wrist injury on the men's side. Novak Djokovic remains doubtful after his shock first-round exit in Rome. And if Sabalenka cannot recover in the next two weeks, the women's side loses its most dominant player just as she had appeared to be heading into her prime years at the event.

Sabalenka has never won Roland Garros. It was the one Grand Slam title that remained on her list. With 14 days to go and a body that is currently compromised, the path to that title has suddenly become a great deal less certain.

What's Next

Aryna Sabalenka will leave Rome and begin urgent recovery work ahead of Roland Garros. A decision on her Paris participation is expected in the coming days. For Sorana Cirstea, the Italian Open continues into the round of 16, where the 36-year-old will carry the confidence of the biggest win of her career into one more chapter of a remarkable final season.
