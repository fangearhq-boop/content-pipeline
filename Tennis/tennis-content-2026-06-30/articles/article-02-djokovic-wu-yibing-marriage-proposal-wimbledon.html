Djokovic Beats Wu Yibing in Four Sets, Goes Viral with Centre Court Marriage Proposal Response

By Elena Voss | June 30, 2026

Novak Djokovic defeated Wu Yibing 6-4, 5-7, 6-4, 6-4 on Day 1 of Wimbledon 2026, improving to 21-0 in first rounds at the All England Club — then became the most-watched man in the stands when a fan proposed to his partner between sets.

By Novak Djokovic's standards, the tennis was the unremarkable part of Monday. He beat Wu Yibing — the Chinese No. 1, ranked inside the top 50 — in four sets on Centre Court in three hours and 11 minutes. 6-4, 5-7, 6-4, 6-4. His 21st consecutive Wimbledon first-round win. Another step toward a tournament record he has held for years. The tennis was controlled, professional, and punctuated by the kind of efficiency that marks players who have been to this stage twenty times and know exactly what is required.

And then came the part that was not about tennis at all.

The Proposal

Between sets, while players changed ends and the crowd settled, a man in the Centre Court stands produced a ring and proposed to his partner. The Centre Court crowd, which knows its moments, reacted with the warmth that the occasion called for. Djokovic was on his way back to the baseline. He saw it.

What followed was not scripted and did not need to be. Djokovic turned back toward the couple, made a heart gesture with his hands, extended two thumbs up in their direction, and then — raising his voice enough to carry across the court — said: "I want an invitation to the wedding."

It is the kind of moment that travels. By the end of the afternoon it was circulating across social platforms faster than the match score. It is also very Djokovic: somewhere between warm and theatrical, between genuine and performed, and entirely effective on both levels.

The Match Itself

Wu Yibing is not a grass specialist, but he is a player with enough quality to test Djokovic on any surface. The first set went relatively smoothly for Djokovic, who broke once and served well enough to hold throughout. The second set was more contested. Wu found his range on return, broke back, and won the set 7-5 — keeping the match alive and giving it some texture.

Djokovic then reset. The third and fourth sets followed the same direction: Djokovic winning 6-4, 6-4, managing the match with the experience of someone who has played on this court in conditions considerably more pressurised than a first-round match. He fended off six break points in the fourth set — one of those statistics that would define a lesser win but here reads as routine maintenance for a player of his quality.

The Record

21-0 in Wimbledon first rounds. It is a record that has no obvious parallel in the sport and is one of the quieter statistical stories of Djokovic's career at the All England Club. His overall Wimbledon record, his seven titles, his number of finals — all of those are discussed constantly. The 21-0 first-round record is simply the result of never having a bad day in the opening round across two decades of competing here.

He is 39 years old. He played 13 ATP matches in 2026 before arriving at Wimbledon. He has been limited by physical management, by selective scheduling, by the mathematics of protecting a body that has been at elite level since the mid-2000s. None of that was visible in the four sets he played on Monday.

What's Next

Djokovic faces Stefanos Tsitsipas in the second round — a matchup that at any other tournament would be considered a quarterfinal-level contest. At Wimbledon, where Tsitsipas has historically found the surface difficult, the seedings and surface create a different balance of probability. Djokovic will be the clear favourite. He will also be the player carrying the memory of a marriage proposal, a heart gesture, and a crowd that decided to enjoy the afternoon.
