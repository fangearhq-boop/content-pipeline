Sabalenka and Gauff Cruise; Zheng and Andreescu Fall as Women's Draw Reshapes on Wimbledon Day 1

By Elena Voss | June 30, 2026

The top seeds dominated and the upsets arrived anyway. Wimbledon's first day of women's singles produced clinical wins for Sabalenka, Gauff and Andreeva — and first-round exits for No. 4 seed Zheng Qinwen and former US Open champion Bianca Andreescu.

Wimbledon's women's opening day rarely produces a quiet afternoon, and 2026 was not the exception. The top players performed with the kind of authority that is easy to describe and hard to replicate: Aryna Sabalenka dropped three games in 64 minutes, Coco Gauff dropped three games in 54, and Roland Garros champion Mirra Andreeva was given a harder test by Magda Linette before winning in two sets. Below them, the draw shifted in ways that will matter as the tournament progresses.

The Dominant Performances

Sabalenka against Teodora Kostovic was a match that answered any lingering doubts about the world No. 1's form on grass. She won 6-2, 6-3 in 64 minutes — the second-fastest match of her Wimbledon career — and did so by winning 83% of points on her first serve and hitting 22 winners to just 11 unforced errors. The victory extended her streak of consecutive first-round wins at Grand Slam tournaments to 23, a run that stretches all the way back to the 2020 US Open and is the second-longest active women's streak behind only Iga Swiatek's.

Gauff was even quicker. Her 6-2, 6-1 win over Tamara Korpatsch lasted 54 minutes — her fastest ever at Wimbledon. She won 88% of her first-serve points in what was her first win on grass since Wimbledon 2024, ending a four-match losing streak on the surface. Three games dropped across the whole match tied her fewest ever at the tournament.

Andreeva, the Roland Garros champion, faced more resistance from Linette. The 7-5, 6-4 result in one hour and 43 minutes required real work — Andreeva needed to recover in the first set and never had the comfort of a dominant display. But she won, and in doing so avoided the historical pattern: four Roland Garros winners had lost in the Wimbledon first round in the same year before her, and she was not the fifth.

The Upsets

No. 32 seed Katerina Siniakova defeated No. 4 seed Zheng Qinwen 6-4, 6-4. Zheng, whose baseline power is among the most formidable in the game, struggled throughout to establish control of her service games. Siniakova — an accomplished doubles player who has developed her singles game significantly in the last two seasons — played with purpose and variety, and Zheng never found the rhythm that would have made her power relevant. The loss is the biggest upset of the day on the women's side and removes one of the draw's most dangerous power baseliners from the tournament.

Shuai Zhang defeated Bianca Andreescu 7-6(3), 7-6(6) in a tight, closely contested match that went the distance without the result ever being settled definitively until the second tiebreak. Andreescu — who has had significant injury challenges of her own over the past two seasons — was playing her first Grand Slam main draw match since the 2024 US Open. She had leads in both sets and could not convert them. Zhang, playing with the patience that comes from years of tour experience, took both tiebreaks and advanced.

What's Next

The implications for the draw are significant. Sabalenka, Gauff and Andreeva all remain on track and will face second-round opponents that should, on paper, continue their forward progress. The loss of Zheng opens her section of the draw for the players ranked below her. Andreescu's exit means one fewer variable for the seeds in her part of the bracket.

The broader story of the women's draw is that the top players have shown they are ready. Whether they remain ready through the second week — when the courts quicken, the competition deepens, and the conditions become more unpredictable — is the question that Wimbledon traditionally takes its time in answering.
