### Long-Form Post Semifinal Saturday is here at the Barcelona Open — and the tournament has never looked more open. Carlos Alcaraz, who would have been the overwhelming favorite if he were still in the draw, withdrew earlier in the week with a wrist injury. What he left behind is a final four that nobody could have predicted: a French ninth seed, a 19-year-old Spanish wildcard, a Russian fifth seed, and a Serbian qualifier who won his way in just to get into the main draw. Here are your two matches today on Pista Rafa Nadal: 🎾 **1:30 PM local — Andrey Rublev vs. Hamad Medjedovic** Rublev is the higher seed, but Medjedovic is the story of the week. The 21-year-old Serb entered via qualifying — beat Gaubas and Halys in the qualifying rounds — then defeated Trungelliti, stunned No. 7 Alex de Minaur, and dispatched Nuno Borges 7-6(6), 6-2 in the quarterfinals. He's now five wins into a main draw appearance that was never guaranteed. 🎾 **4:00 PM local — Arthur Fils vs. Rafael Jodar** If you watch one match this weekend, make it this one. Jodar — 19 years old, ranked No. 55, born in Spain and playing in Barcelona — won Marrakech two weeks ago for his first ATP title. He then beat Munar, Carabelli, and Norrie (6-3, 6-2) to reach this point. When he dispatched Norrie on Friday, he became only the fifth Spanish teenager in Open Era history to reach the Barcelona semifinals, joining Corretja, Moya, Nadal, and Alcaraz. All four of those players went on to win Grand Slams. Fils, meanwhile, is the experienced hand in this matchup — a top-15 player who has been one of the most consistent names on the clay swing. Who are you backing today? --- #### Image Caption Post (9:00 AM ET) Barcelona SF day 🎾 Jodar vs. Fils at 4 PM. Rublev vs. Medjedovic at 1:30 PM. A 19-year-old wildcard, a Serbian qualifier, and no Alcaraz. The draw is truly open. #BarcelonaOpen #Tennis ---

### Long-Form Post Stuttgart's Porsche Tennis Grand Prix lost two of its biggest names in a single stunning afternoon on Friday. First, Mirra Andreeva defeated Iga Swiatek — a two-time Stuttgart champion — 3-6, 6-4, 6-3. It was Andreeva's third win over Swiatek in their last three meetings; the head-to-head is now 3-1 in Mirra's favor. Swiatek took the first set 6-3 before Andreeva turned it around completely. Then, just as that result was sinking in, Karolína Muchová beat Coco Gauff 6-3, 5-7, 6-3 — for the first time in seven career meetings. The match went two hours and 22 minutes. Gauff won the second set to level it, but Muchová broke in the sixth game of the third to close it out. What that means for Saturday: 🎾 **Elena Rybakina vs. Mirra Andreeva** — Rybakina, the No. 1 seed, fought off match points to beat Fernandez in the QF (6-7(5), 6-4, 7-6(6)) and is in fine form. But Andreeva just beat the two-time champion. This is the match of the tournament. 🎾 **Elina Svitolina vs. Karolína Muchová** — Svitolina defeated Noskova 7-6(2), 7-5, and enters Saturday's SF on a strong run. Muchová's confidence is sky-high after finally solving the Gauff puzzle. Any of these four could win Stuttgart. Who do you have? 🏆 --- #### Image Caption Post (11:00 AM ET) Stuttgart lost Swiatek AND Gauff on Friday 😲 Andreeva, Rybakina, Svitolina, and Muchova are your final four. ### Long-Form Post Munich's BMW Open semifinals are set, and there are two compelling storylines heading into Saturday's matches. Alexander Zverev looked a little shaky on Friday. He dropped the first set to Francisco Cerundolo 5-7, then came out in the second set and won six straight games, closing out a 5-7, 6-0, 6-2 win. That kind of third-set dominance, after a first-set loss, is a good sign for a player looking to make a deep run on home clay. He now faces Flavio Cobolli — one of the best young Italian clay-court players and a career-high-ranked No. 13. On the other side of the draw, Ben Shelton beat João Fonseca 6-3, 3-6, 6-3 in an entertaining quarterfinal. The American No. 1 (ranked No. 8 in the world) is hunting his first European clay result of real significance, and he faces Alex Molcan in today's SF. The Munich final is Sunday. Today's results will tell us whether Zverev gets to contend for another clay title on home soil. --- #### Image Caption Post (1:30 PM ET) Munich semifinals: Zverev vs. Cobolli and Shelton vs. Molcan 🎾 Zverev looked dominant after dropping set 1 to Cerundolo yesterday. ### Long-Form Post Seven wins. That's what it took for Hamad Medjedovic to reach Saturday's Barcelona Open semifinal. He didn't start at Round 1. He started in qualifying, beating Vilius Gaubas, then scraping past Quentin Halys in a third-set tiebreaker just to earn a place in the main draw. Most players who qualify count their entry into the tournament as a small victory in itself. Medjedovic had other ideas. In the main draw, he continued beating whoever was put in front of him. Trungelliti. Then Alex de Minaur — the No. 7 player in the world — in a result that was immediately one of the year's biggest upsets. Then Nuno Borges, the Portuguese No. 5 seed, 7-6(6), 6-2 in the quarterfinals. He's 21 years old. Serbian. And now in his first career ATP 500 semifinal. The opponent today at 1:30 PM is Andrey Rublev — a former top-5 player, tough, experienced, and not going to give anything away easily. But Medjedovic has shown all week that the stage doesn't faze him. The bigger the moment, the better he's played. That's the most exciting player you may not have heard of — until this week. --- #### Image Caption Post (4:00 PM ET) Hamad Medjedovic entered Barcelona needing to win qualifying just to get into the draw. He's now in the SF after beating De Minaur and Borges. 21 years old. First ATP 500 semi. What a week. #BarcelonaOpen #ATP ---

### Long-Form Post There is a list of five names, and Rafael Jodar just put his on it. On Friday at the Barcelona Open, the 19-year-old Spaniard defeated Cameron Norrie 6-3, 6-2 to become only the fifth Spanish teenager in the Open Era to reach the Barcelona semifinals. The other four names on the list: ✅ Alex Corretja — Barcelona SF as a teenager in 1994. Grand Slam champion. ✅ Carlos Moya — Barcelona SF as a teenager in 1996. Grand Slam champion. ✅ Rafael Nadal — Barcelona SF as a teenager in 2005 AND 2006. 22 Grand Slam titles. ✅ Carlos Alcaraz — Barcelona SF as a teenager in 2022 and 2023. Multiple Grand Slams. Every single previous member of that list went on to win a major. Every one. Jodar's path here has been remarkable. A wildcard entry, wins over Munar, Carabelli, and Norrie, a first ATP title at Marrakech two weeks ago, and now a Barcelona SF at his home tournament, in front of his home crowd, playing on clay he grew up on. He faces Arthur Fils today at 4:00 PM. Whatever the result, Jodar's name now belongs in a conversation it has rarely had before. --- #### Image Caption Post (7:00 PM ET) Rafael Jodar is the 5th Spanish teen in Open Era history to reach the Barcelona SF 🎾 Corretja, Moya, Nadal, Alcaraz, Jodar. All four who came before him won Grand Slams. Remarkable. #BarcelonaOpen #Tennis
