Munich Semifinals: Zverev vs. Cobolli and Shelton vs. Molcan on BMW Open Saturday

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | April 18, 2026

The BMW Open in Munich reaches its semifinal round on Saturday with two matchups that each carry their own storyline. Alexander Zverev vs. Flavio Cobolli is the headline, with Germany's top player looking to go deep at a tournament where he is both top seed and home favorite. Ben Shelton vs. Alex Molcan completes the card, with the American No. 1 seeking his first meaningful European clay result.

Zverev Survives Cerundolo, Now Faces Cobolli

Alexander Zverev did not have the easiest of days in Friday's quarterfinal. He dropped the first set to Francisco Cerundolo 5-7 before clicking into a different gear entirely — winning the next two sets 6-0 and 6-2 to seal a 5-7, 6-0, 6-2 victory. The first-to-last-set turnaround was emphatic, but the match served as a reminder that clay can produce surprises even for the game's most experienced players.

Zverev's semifinal opponent is Flavio Cobolli, the Italian who has been one of the standout players on the clay circuit so far this season. Cobolli entered 2026 at a career-high ranking of No. 13 and has the groundstroke quality to trouble any player on this surface. He advanced from Friday's quarterfinals to earn his place in the last four. Saturday's match between Zverev and Cobolli is expected to be an interesting contest between Zverev's power and Cobolli's clay-court craft.

Shelton Looking for Clay Breakthrough

Ben Shelton is the highest-ranked American in the ATP at No. 8, and Munich represents a genuine opportunity for a result on European clay. He beat João Fonseca 6-3, 3-6, 6-3 in Friday's quarterfinal — an entertaining three-set match against the 19-year-old Brazilian who had reached his own first Masters-level quarterfinals earlier in the clay swing. Shelton was steady in the key moments and broke when it mattered to close out the third set.

His semifinal opponent is Alex Molcan, the Slovak who dispatched his own quarterfinal opponent on Friday. Molcan is a reliable clay-court player with a consistent baseline game, but Shelton's serve gives him an advantage on the surface that most players his size enjoy. If Shelton can convert his opportunities, he has the tools to reach a first Munich final.

A Different Kind of Clay Saturday

Munich is the quieter tournament on Saturday compared to Barcelona and Stuttgart — two events with more prominent names and bigger draws. But the BMW Open's semifinals have their own stakes. Zverev and Cobolli are both highly motivated players on a surface that suits them, and the Shelton-Molcan matchup has the potential for a competitive three-set battle.

Munich is also an ATP 500 event, meaning the winner collects substantial ranking points and momentum heading into the Madrid Open the following week. For Zverev, a Munich title would add another clay trophy to a collection that has grown significantly in recent years. For Shelton or Molcan, it would be the breakthrough European clay result they are both still chasing.

What's Next

The Munich final is on Sunday. The winner of Zverev-Cobolli meets the winner of Shelton-Molcan for the BMW Open title. The Madrid Open begins on April 26, followed by the Italian Open and then the French Open (May 24). This is the heart of the clay swing, and Saturday's Munich SFs are a meaningful part of it.
