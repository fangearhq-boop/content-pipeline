Stuttgart WTA SFs: Rybakina vs. Andreeva and Svitolina vs. Muchova After Swiatek, Gauff Exits

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 18, 2026

Stuttgart's Porsche Tennis Grand Prix lost two of its most prominent names in a single afternoon. Iga Swiatek, a two-time champion at this event, and Coco Gauff, seeded No. 2, both fell in Friday's quarterfinals. What remains is a final four that is wide open, experienced, and capable of producing the best women's clay-court tennis of the spring swing.

Saturday's semifinals are: Elena Rybakina vs. Mirra Andreeva, and Elina Svitolina vs. Karolína Muchová.

Andreeva Ends Swiatek's Stuttgart Reign — Again

The headline result of Friday's QF slate was Mirra Andreeva defeating Iga Swiatek 3-6, 6-4, 6-3. Swiatek had won Stuttgart in 2022 and 2023 and arrived here as the No. 3 seed. She took the first set 6-3 and appeared to be in control. Then Andreeva changed the dynamic entirely.

The 20-year-old Russian shortened points behind her first serve, found more success targeting Swiatek's backhand, and was sharper when it mattered in the second and third sets. During key stretches, Swiatek's serve faltered — winning just 40 percent of first-serve points and 29 percent on second serve. Andreeva broke late in the second to level, then controlled the third to claim the match. It was their first-ever meeting on clay, and Andreeva won it. The head-to-head between the two now stands at 3-1 in Andreeva's favor overall.

In the SF, Andreeva now faces Elena Rybakina — the No. 1 seed who survived a different kind of battle on Friday. Rybakina needed three sets to beat Leylah Fernandez, 6-7(5), 6-4, 7-6(6), saving match points in the process. She is the most decorated player remaining in the draw and will be the favorite heading into Saturday.

Muchova Finally Solves Gauff — After Six Career Losses

If Andreeva's win over Swiatek was the more dramatic result, Karolína Muchová's victory over Gauff carried its own significant weight. Going into Friday's quarterfinal, Muchová was 0-6 in career meetings with the American — six losses across multiple years and multiple surfaces. The Stuttgart QF was attempt number seven.

She won it 6-3, 5-7, 6-3, in two hours and 22 minutes. Muchová took the first set with two breaks, stayed patient through a second set in which both players struggled on serve, and then broke in the sixth game of the third to close out her first-ever win over Gauff. The WTA's own headline for the match: "Seventh time's the charm."

In the SF, Muchová meets Elina Svitolina — the Ukrainian who defeated Linda Noskova 7-6(2), 7-5 in Friday's other quarterfinal. Svitolina, who returned to professional tennis after the birth of her daughter Skai in October 2022, has been one of the most consistent performers on the women's tour in the first half of 2026, carrying a 20-5 record into the week.

A Final Four With No Clear Favorite

Rybakina is ranked No. 1 and has the most clay-court pedigree of the four remaining players. But Andreeva has just beaten Swiatek and owns the head-to-head in their rivalry. Svitolina's 20-5 record in 2026 suggests a player in excellent form. And Muchová, healthy and confident after years of injury disruption, just got the breakthrough win she had been chasing for her entire career against Gauff.

Stuttgart has produced a semifinal round that genuinely could go any direction. The tournament's champion on Sunday will have been through a proper test to earn it.

What's Next

The winners of Saturday's semifinals meet in Sunday's Stuttgart final. The champion collects the WTA 500 title and valuable clay-season ranking points ahead of Madrid (April 26) and the French Open (May 24). For Andreeva and Muchová, a first Stuttgart final would be the biggest clay result of their careers to date.
