Barcelona SF Day: Jodar vs. Fils and Rublev vs. Medjedovic Set for Pista Rafa Nadal

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 18, 2026

The Barcelona Open Banc Sabadell reaches its semifinal stage on Saturday with one of the most unpredictable final fours in recent ATP 500 history. Carlos Alcaraz, the defending champion, withdrew earlier in the week with a right wrist injury. What he left behind is a draw that belongs to no one — and everyone at once.

Two matches are on the card today at Pista Rafa Nadal. At 1:30 PM local time, No. 5 seed Andrey Rublev meets Hamad Medjedovic, a Serbian qualifier who did not even have a guaranteed main draw spot when the week began. At 4:00 PM, No. 9 seed Arthur Fils of France faces Rafael Jodar of Spain — the 19-year-old wildcard who has turned his home tournament into a personal coronation.

Rublev vs. Medjedovic: The Qualifier's Last Mountain

Hamad Medjedovic is the unlikely name in this semifinal. The 21-year-old Serb had to win two qualifying matches before the main draw even began — defeats of Vilius Gaubas and Quentin Halys (the latter in a third-set tiebreaker) — to earn his entry. He then kept winning. Trungelliti in Round 1. Alex de Minaur, the No. 7 player in the world, in a Round of 16 upset that immediately became one of the standout results of the clay season. Nuno Borges, the Portuguese No. 5 seed, 7-6(6), 6-2 in the quarterfinals.

Five wins in the main draw. Seven in total, counting qualifying. It is the first ATP 500 semifinal of Medjedovic's career.

Rublev, meanwhile, is the experienced hand in this matchup. A former top-five player who has been to multiple Grand Slam quarterfinals, the Russian knows how to win on clay. He beat Lorenzo Sonego in the Round of 16 and received a QF walkover after Alcaraz's withdrawal. He has not had to dig particularly deep this week, which could cut either way: fresh legs, or a lack of match intensity heading into the most important match of the event.

Fils vs. Jodar: Home Soil, History at Stake

Rafael Jodar does not need a great deal of introduction anymore, at least not in Barcelona. The 19-year-old wildcard has been the most talked-about story of the week. He won his first ATP title at Marrakech two weeks ago, defeating Trungelliti 6-3, 6-2 in the final. He arrived in Barcelona with a wildcard and a growing reputation, beat Munar 6-1, 6-2 and Carabelli 6-3, 6-3 in the earlier rounds, then on Friday dispatched Cameron Norrie 6-3, 6-2 to reach the last four.

That win made him the fifth Spanish teenager in Open Era history to reach the Barcelona semifinals. The other four — Alex Corretja (1994), Carlos Moya (1996), Rafael Nadal (2005 and 2006), and Carlos Alcaraz (2022 and 2023) — all went on to win Grand Slam titles. Jodar is playing in his home city, in front of a crowd that already knows his name, with a chance to follow an extraordinary lineage.

Fils is the challenge. The Frenchman, seeded No. 9 and ranked inside the ATP top 15, overcame Lorenzo Musetti 6-3, 6-4 in Friday's quarterfinals. He is one of the most physically imposing players on the circuit at his age — powerful groundstrokes, good court coverage, and experience at this level that Jodar does not yet have. Saturday's match at 4:00 PM is their first-ever meeting on the ATP Tour.

A Draw That Belongs to No One

Barcelona's final four — Rublev, Medjedovic, Fils, Jodar — does not include a single player seeded inside the Top 4. With Alcaraz gone and the draw shaken up by Medjedovic's upsets and Jodar's run, this is genuinely one of the most open ATP 500 semifinals in years. The eventual champion will have fully earned it.

Saturday also adds context to the rest of the clay season. The French Open is a little over a month away. A deep Barcelona run carries rankings points, confidence, and momentum. Any of the four players left standing has something significant to prove before Roland Garros.

What's Next

The winner of Rublev vs. Medjedovic meets the winner of Fils vs. Jodar in Sunday's final. The champion takes 250 ATP ranking points and a significant boost heading into the Madrid Open (April 26) and the French Open (May 24). For Medjedovic and Jodar specifically, a first ATP 500 final would be a career milestone regardless of the outcome.
