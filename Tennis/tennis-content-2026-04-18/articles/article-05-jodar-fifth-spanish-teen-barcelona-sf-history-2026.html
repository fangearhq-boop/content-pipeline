Jodar Joins Corretja, Moya, Nadal, Alcaraz as Spanish Teens in Barcelona Semifinals

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 18, 2026

When Rafael Jodar defeated Cameron Norrie 6-3, 6-2 on Friday in the Barcelona Open quarterfinals, the result was significant for more than just his own run. It put him on a list that has only four other names — and every name on that list belongs to a Grand Slam champion.

Jodar is the fifth Spanish teenager in the Open Era to reach the Barcelona Open semifinals. He is 19 years old.

The List

It is worth reading slowly:

Alex Corretja, 1994. The Catalan reached the Barcelona SF as a teenager and went on to win the French Open in 1998.

Carlos Moya, 1996. Also a Catalan, Moya reached the Barcelona SF as a teenager and won Roland Garros in 1998.

Rafael Nadal, 2005 and 2006. Nadal reached the Barcelona SF in consecutive years as a teenager. He won the French Open 14 times. There is nothing more to say.

Carlos Alcaraz, 2022 and 2023. Alcaraz reached the Barcelona SF as a teenager in back-to-back years before going on to win multiple Grand Slam titles.

Rafael Jodar, 2026. The 19-year-old Spaniard who won Marrakech two weeks ago, entered Barcelona on a wildcard, and has been the crowd's player of the tournament.

What Jodar Did to Get Here

The journey through Barcelona has not been a fluke. Jodar beat Munar 6-1, 6-2 in the first round. He dispatched Carabelli 6-3, 6-3 in the Round of 16. And in Friday's quarterfinal, he defeated Cameron Norrie — a former world top 10 — 6-3, 6-2 with a performance that was more dominant than the scoreline suggests. His game on home clay is efficient, controlled, and relentless from the baseline.

Two weeks before Barcelona, Jodar won the ATP 250 title in Marrakech — his first ATP title ever — beating Trungelliti in the final. He was already ranked No. 57 and entering his home tournament with momentum. Now, nine consecutive clay-court wins into a streak that shows no sign of stopping, he is in a Barcelona semifinal against Arthur Fils.

The Saturday Test

Fils is the most significant challenge Jodar has faced all week. The Frenchman is ranked inside the ATP top 15, seeded No. 9 in the draw, and beat Lorenzo Musetti 6-3, 6-4 in Friday's quarterfinals. He is physically powerful, experienced at the ATP 500 level, and not the kind of player who gifts opponents easy wins.

But the four players who occupied Jodar's spot on that historical list before him all knew what it felt like to be playing at home with the crowd behind them, with something to prove, at 19. It did not slow any of them down.

The match is at 4:00 PM local time on Pista Rafa Nadal. The name of the court is no coincidence.

What's Next

A win on Saturday puts Jodar in the Barcelona final — and into the conversation for the top storylines of the clay season. Regardless of Saturday's outcome, his ranking will jump significantly from No. 57. The Madrid Open (April 26) and the French Open (May 24) lie ahead, and the player who has won nine straight on clay is going to be a name that opponents will now approach differently. That list of five Spanish teenagers changed the sport. Whether Jodar becomes the next one to do so is a question for years, not weeks. But this week, in Barcelona, he has earned the right to ask it.
