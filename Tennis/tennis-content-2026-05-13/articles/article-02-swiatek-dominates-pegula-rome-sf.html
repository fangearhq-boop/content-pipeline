Swiatek Drops Just 3 Games Against Pegula, Surges Into Rome Semifinals

By Marcus Cole | May 13, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

There have been whispers this season that Iga Swiatek was no longer the untouchable force she had been on clay. Wednesday's quarterfinal at the Internazionali BNL d'Italia provided a definitive rebuttal.

The four-time Roland Garros champion dismantled world No. 5 Jessica Pegula 6-1, 6-2 in straight sets, dropping just three games in one of the most comprehensive victories of her 2026 clay season. The performance was made more remarkable by its context: Pegula had defeated Swiatek in their previous two meetings, both in straight sets.

Wednesday's match was a different story from the first point.

Back to Dominant

Swiatek broke Pegula's serve twice in the opening set to race into a 4-0 lead. Pegula held at 5-1 to avoid the shutout, but Swiatek closed 6-1 with clinical efficiency. The second set followed a similar script — the Pole's heavy topspin groundstrokes consistently pushed Pegula behind the baseline and took away the American's ability to dictate with her flat, pace-heavy style.

The final scoreline — 6-1, 6-2, three games dropped — was the kind of statement tennis that Swiatek had delivered routinely during her peak years but had been more sporadic with entering this Rome campaign.

"She was incredible today," Pegula acknowledged after the match. "I really didn't have any answers."

The Roig Effect

Swiatek's decision to bring Francisco Roig on board as coach — the man who worked alongside Rafael Nadal from 2005 to 2022 — raised eyebrows initially. Roig's injury courtside at a previous Rome match only added drama to a coaching partnership still finding its footing in the public eye.

But the results speak clearly. Swiatek's footwork on clay, always excellent, has grown even more precise. Her patterns of play — heavy crosscourt topspin followed by sudden down-the-line attacks — have the hallmark of Roig's Nadal-era tactical philosophy: build patience, construct the point, then explode.

Roland Garros is just 12 days from the main draw start date. Swiatek owns four titles at the clay Grand Slam. The way she played against Pegula, she arrives at Paris as a genuine favourite — perhaps the favourite — to claim a fifth.

Rome Semifinal

Swiatek now awaits the winner of the Elena Rybakina vs. Elina Svitolina quarterfinal — scheduled Wednesday evening. Both Rybakina and Svitolina are former Rome champions. Either would represent a significant test for Swiatek in the last four.

Rybakina, in particular, offers a style that has historically troubled Swiatek — a flat, heavy serve and an aggressive flat forehand that reduces the spin-absorbing time Swiatek relies on. The last meeting between Swiatek and Rybakina at a Masters event produced a tight contest.

Svitolina, meanwhile, has been quietly excellent in Rome, moving cleanly and defending with the kind of precision that makes her a nightmare for power players to put away.

What's Next

The Rome semifinal is scheduled for Friday. Whether she faces Rybakina or Svitolina, Swiatek will arrive with momentum and confidence at levels she has not shown consistently since the start of the clay season.

The narrative is clear: three games dropped, four Roland Garros titles, 12 days to Paris. Swiatek is back. And clay season has its story.
