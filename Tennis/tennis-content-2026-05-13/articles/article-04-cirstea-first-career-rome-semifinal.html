Cirstea Reaches First Career Rome Semifinal at 36, Capping a Week That Defies Belief

By Marcus Cole | May 13, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

There are tennis stories that feel scripted for a film. Sorana Cirstea's week in Rome is one of them — except it is entirely, wonderfully real.

The 36-year-old Romanian, who announced before the clay season that this is her final year on the WTA Tour, defeated Jelena Ostapenko 6-1, 7-6(7) on Wednesday to reach her first career semifinal at the Internazionali BNL d'Italia. It is the kind of milestone that usually arrives in a career's prime, not in its final chapter.

Cirstea has been on tour for more than two decades. She has played Rome many times. She had never made the last four — until now, at 36, in the year she chose to say goodbye.

The Path Through Rome

The road to this moment has been extraordinary. In the third round, Cirstea defeated world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka — a result that qualified instantly as one of the major upsets of the 2026 season. Sabalenka, who had been dominant all tournament, left the court with an injury and a loss; Cirstea left it with perhaps the biggest win of her career.

From there, she defeated No. 9 seed Linda Noskova in the round of 16 — her first career Rome quarterfinal at that point — and followed it up on Wednesday by outplaying Ostapenko. Against the hard-hitting Latvian, Cirstea was clinical in the first set (6-1) and showed remarkable composure in a second-set tiebreak that reached 7-7 before she converted.

Three consecutive top-ten or seeded opponents removed. One impossible result after another.

What Makes Her Run So Remarkable

Cirstea's game has never been built on overwhelming power. She constructs points with variety — sliced approaches, heavy topspin from the baseline, well-disguised drops — and relies on tactical intelligence accumulated over a career that has seen her face every style of player on every surface imaginable.

That experience, which so often becomes a liability as players age and react slower, has been Cirstea's weapon this week. She has outthought every opponent in front of her. And she has done it with a visible joy that is rare in the competitive heat of a Masters 1000 tournament.

WTA's own coverage captured the numbers: "By the Numbers: Cirstea reaches first Rome semifinal at 36."

Semifinals vs. Gauff

Cirstea's opponent in the last four is Coco Gauff — the defending Roland Garros champion who survived her own quarterfinal drama against Mirra Andreeva on five match points. On paper, Gauff is the favourite. She is younger, ranked higher, and armed with one of the most powerful returns in the women's game.

But Cirstea has not been reading the script this week. There is no reason she will start now.

What's Next

The semifinal is scheduled for Friday. Whatever the result, Cirstea has already secured her place as the story of the 2026 Rome tournament. A first career Rome SF, a giant-killing run, a farewell tour that refuses to end quietly.

The tennis world has been watching. It should keep watching.
