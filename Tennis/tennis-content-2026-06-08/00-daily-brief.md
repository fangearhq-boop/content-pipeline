### STORY 1: Zverev Wins Roland Garros 2026 — First Grand Slam Title
- **Tier:** 1
- **Type:** NEW STORY (men's final result — June 7 pipeline covered the preview)
- **Angle:** Alexander Zverev defeated Flavio Cobolli 6-1, 4-6, 6-4, 6-7(5), 6-1 in the 2026 Roland Garros men's singles final to claim his first Grand Slam title. ### STORY 2: Roland Garros Controversy — Physio Visit and Line-Calling Error Fuel Rule Change Demands
- **Tier:** 1
- **Type:** NEW STORY
- **Angle:** Zverev's Roland Garros victory is shadowed by two controversies that have energised calls for rule changes. First: Zverev's physiotherapist left the player box to hand him something during the fourth set — not during a changeover — sparking debate about what the rules allow. Jim Courier questioned how it was permitted; Brad Gilbert noted drinks are normally delivered by ball kids. Second: at 5-5, 40-30 in the fourth set, an ace from Zverev was called good when the mark was clearly outside. Roland Garros is the only Grand Slam that still uses human line judges, and the calls have reignited demands for electronic line calling.
- **Sources:** tennistemple.com, yahoo.com/sports, au.news.yahoo.com, sports.yahoo.com

### STORY 5: Cobolli's Roland Garros Run and Italy's Grand Slam Moment
- **Tier:** 2
- **Type:** NEW STORY (feature)
- **Angle:** Flavio Cobolli's journey to the 2026 Roland Garros final — No. 10 seed, defeated Lorenzo Musetti and Felix Auger-Aliassime en route, advanced by walkover in the semifinal after Arnaldi's viral illness — was the most unlikely final appearance in recent memory.
