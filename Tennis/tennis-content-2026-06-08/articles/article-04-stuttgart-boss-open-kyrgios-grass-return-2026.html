Stuttgart BOSS Open 2026: Kyrgios Makes Grass-Court Return as Wimbledon Season Begins

By Elena Voss | June 8, 2026

The grass-court season has arrived. The BOSS Open in Stuttgart opened on Monday as the first ATP tournament of the 2026 grass swing, bringing with it a headline storyline that has little to do with clay — the return of Nick Kyrgios to a surface where he has always been most at home.

Kyrgios accepted a wild card into the ATP 250 event at the Tennisclub Weissenhof and faces Corentin Moutet in the first round. It will be his first match on grass in nearly three years.

Kyrgios's Return

Nick Kyrgios has played just seven singles matches since 2023, a period defined by knee and wrist injuries that required surgery. The Australian returned to the tour briefly this season — appearing in Brisbane at the start of 2026 — before withdrawing from subsequent events. Stuttgart represents his most substantive commitment to the circuit in several years.

The decision to return on grass is calculated. Kyrgios's serve and net game translate especially well to the surface. His grass-court record includes a Wimbledon finalist appearance, and the BOSS Open gives him a lower-pressure environment in which to rebuild match sharpness before Wimbledon arrives in late June.

"The Weissenhof is a fantastic venue with excellent grass courts," Kyrgios said in his pre-tournament comments. "I've always enjoyed my time there. I'm really looking forward to coming back."

The match against Moutet will test whether his body holds up through a best-of-three contest after limited competitive play. Those who follow Kyrgios closely have learned that predictions about his form — in either direction — tend to be overtaken by events.

The Draw

Taylor Fritz is the defending Stuttgart champion and the tournament's top seed. He is joined by Ben Shelton, Tommy Paul, and Frances Tiafoe in what amounts to a strong American contingent on the grass. The field was thinned by the Roland Garros final weekend — Alexander Zverev, Flavio Cobolli, and Jakub Mensik all withdrew after competing deep into the tournament in Paris.

The absence of Zverev in particular opens the draw. Fritz, if in form, is the player best equipped to defend his title on a surface where he finished runner-up at Wimbledon in 2025. Shelton's serve gives him weapons on grass; his movement and return have improved enough to make him a threat on the surface.

The Broader Grass Swing

Stuttgart is the first of three weeks before Wimbledon. The Libema Open in s-Hertogenbosch and the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club in London follow, with Queen's in particular serving as a traditional proving ground for Wimbledon form. The All England Club opens for the 2026 Championships later in June.

The transition from clay to grass is the sharpest surface shift in tennis, demanding immediate adjustments to footwork, serve placement, and approach shot selection. Players who spent the fortnight in Paris will arrive in Stuttgart still recalibrating.

What's Next

The BOSS Open main draw begins Monday, June 9, with the singles final scheduled for Sunday, June 14. Qualifying runs through Monday. Kyrgios vs Moutet is among the first-round matches. The full draw and schedule are available at atptour.com. Results from Stuttgart, s-Hertogenbosch, and Queen's Club will shape Wimbledon seedings when they are announced by the All England Club.
