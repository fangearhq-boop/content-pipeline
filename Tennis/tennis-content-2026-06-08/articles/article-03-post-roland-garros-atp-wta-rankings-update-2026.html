Post-Roland Garros 2026 Rankings: Zverev Climbs to No. 3, Cobolli Breaks Top 10, Chwalinska Jumps 93 Places

By Ryan Calloway | June 8, 2026

The 2026 Roland Garros final weekend has triggered the largest single-week rankings movement of the season on both tours. With a new men's Grand Slam champion crowned and a qualifier reaching the women's final, the weekly update published Monday reshapes the top of men's and women's tennis heading into the grass-court swing.

ATP Rankings: Zverev Rises, Cobolli Breaks Into Top 10

Alexander Zverev's Roland Garros title vaults him to No. 3 in the ATP rankings, with 7,305 points. The gain comes partly from his own title — he was a quarterfinalist at Roland Garros in 2025 and earned significantly more points for the championship — and partly from the position of those above him. Carlos Alcaraz, who won Roland Garros in 2025, lost the 2,000 defending champion points he could not defend after withdrawing from the 2026 tournament due to a wrist injury. Alcaraz drops accordingly, though he retains No. 2 with 9,960 points. Jannik Sinner leads at No. 1 with 13,500 points.

The biggest mover in the men's draw is Flavio Cobolli, who rises four places to a career-high No. 10 in the world with 3,540 points. The Italian finalist becomes the first player from his country to break into the top 10 in several years. Matteo Arnaldi, who reached the Roland Garros semifinal before withdrawing due to illness, surges 70 spots up the rankings to No. 34 — one of the largest single-tournament gains in the men's draw this season.

Novak Djokovic drops to No. 7 following his third-round exit. He was a semifinalist at Roland Garros in 2025 and loses significant defending points as a result. Felix Auger-Aliassime, who reached the quarterfinal, enters the top four at the expense of Djokovic.

WTA Rankings: Andreeva to No. 6, Gauff Falls, Chwalinska's Historic Leap

Mirra Andreeva's Roland Garros title produces a two-place rise to No. 6 in the WTA rankings. The 19-year-old entered the tournament seeded No. 8 and exits with the champion's ranking points that move her past Coco Gauff and Elina Svitolina in the standings.

Gauff, the defending Roland Garros champion who exited in the third round, falls to No. 7. Her early loss means she defends her 2024 title points without adding to them, a significant drop in a tight field.

The most dramatic movement belongs to Maja Chwalinska. The Polish qualifier, who entered the tournament ranked No. 114 and reached the women's final — becoming only the second qualifier in Open Era Grand Slam final history — jumps 93 places in the rankings following her runner-up performance. She exits Paris inside the top 25 for the first time in her career.

Aryna Sabalenka retains the world No. 1 ranking. The threat from Elena Rybakina, who was positioned to potentially overtake Sabalenka depending on Roland Garros results, did not materialise after Rybakina's early exit from the tournament.

What's Next

Wimbledon seedings will be set by the All England Club in the coming weeks, drawing on a grass-specific formula that weights grass-court results and the official rankings. The grass swing begins this week with the BOSS Open in Stuttgart (June 8–14), the Libema Open in s-Hertogenbosch, and the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club in London. Current rankings are available at atptour.com and wtatennis.com.
