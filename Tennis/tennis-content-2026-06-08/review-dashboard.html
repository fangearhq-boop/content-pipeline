Roland Garros Controversy — Physio Visit and Line-Calling Error Fuel Rule Change Demands - **Tier:** 1 - **Angle:** Zverev's Roland Garros victory is shadowed by two controversies that have energised calls for rule changes. First: Zverev's physiotherapist left the player box to hand him something during the fourth set — not during a changeover — sparking debate about what the rules allow. Jim Courier questioned how it was permitted; Brad Gilbert noted drinks are normally delivered by ball kids. Second: at 5-5, 40-30 in the fourth set, an ace from Zverev was called good when the mark was clearly outside. Roland Garros is the only Grand Slam that still uses human line judges, and the calls have reignited demands for electronic line calling.
