### Long-Form Post Sunday night at the Caja Mágica was always going to be special. Two 19-year-olds, both born in 2006, both already in the top 30. And one of them playing in the city where he grew up. Rafael Jodar, from Alcorcón — a municipality just 12 kilometres from the venue — defeated Joao Fonseca 7-6(4), 4-6, 6-1 in 2 hours and 7 minutes of electric Madrid tennis. The crowd was full-throated from the first point. Jodar won a nervy first-set tiebreak 7-4, dropped the second as Fonseca levelled brilliantly, then came out and won the third 6-1 in utterly dominant fashion. The numbers: Jodar becomes the eighth teenager in Open Era history to reach the Madrid round of 16. The others on that list include Rafael Nadal and Carlos Alcaraz. For a 19-year-old competing in his own backyard, it is a remarkable achievement. Fonseca, meanwhile, remains one of the most exciting prospects in men's tennis — but on this night, in this city, this was Jodar's moment. What did you think of the Jodar-Fonseca match? ### Long-Form Post There is something almost eerie about how Jannik Sinner wins. On Sunday at the Mutua Madrid Open, the world No. 1 defeated Danish qualifier Elmer Moller 6-2, 6-3 in 1 hour and 18 minutes. No drama. No wobble. Moller, who had fought through qualifying and beaten two opponents to reach this stage, won six games total. Sinner barely broke a sweat. The streak now stands at 24 consecutive Masters 1000 victories — the fourth longest in series history since 1990. He is chasing something no player has ever done: five consecutive Masters 1000 titles. Paris. Indian Wells. Miami. Monte Carlo. And now Madrid would complete the set. His next opponent is Cameron Norrie, the British 19th seed, who defeated Thiago Tirante 7-5, 7-6(5) to reach the R16. It is a winnable match for Sinner on current form. Lorenzo Musetti also advanced on Sunday, defeating Tallon Griekspoor 6-4, 7-5. He faces Jiri Lehecka in the R16 — with Musetti holding the clay head-to-head advantage despite Lehecka's 2-1 overall lead. How far do you see Sinner going at Madrid — another title? --- #### Image Caption -- 10:30 AM ET ### Image Post Caption Jannik Sinner def. Moller 6-2, 6-3. It is the second time this season Rybakina has faced Zheng, gone down a set, and won in three. The statistics are telling. Zheng generated more winners (24 to 19) and hit more aces (nine to seven). ### Long-Form Post The stomach virus that hit Iga Swiatek on Sunday found another victim in Coco Gauff — and yet Gauff refused to let it end her Madrid campaign. The No. 3 seed defeated Sorana Cirstea 4-6, 7-5, 6-1 in 2 hours and 21 minutes, fighting through what was clearly a very difficult physical situation. At one point during the match, Gauff vomited into a bin on the court. She later requested a medical timeout. Then she went out and won the last two sets in dominant fashion. It is the kind of performance that tells you something about a player's mentality. Down a set, physically compromised, against a dangerous opponent — Gauff found a way. Elsewhere on Sunday, Marta Kostyuk produced one of the day's biggest upsets. The Ukrainian defeated No. 5 seed Jessica Pegula 6-1, 6-4, hitting 20 winners to Pegula's nine in a dominant display. Today in Madrid, Gauff faces Linda Noskova in the round of 16. After Sunday's effort, a rest day may not have been unwelcome — but in Madrid, the schedule does not wait.
