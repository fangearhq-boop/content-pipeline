Jodar Beats Fonseca in Madrid Thriller, Joins Nadal and Alcaraz in Historic Teen Club

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | April 27, 2026

Rafael Jodar was born 12 kilometres from the Caja Mágica. On Sunday night, the distance felt even shorter. In front of a Madrid crowd that was fully, vocally, emotionally his, the 19-year-old defeated Joao Fonseca 7-6(4), 4-6, 6-1 in 2 hours and 7 minutes — advancing to the round of 16 at a Masters 1000 event for the first time in his career.

It was the match the tournament had been building toward since the draw was released. Two teenagers, both born in 2006, both already inside the top 30, meeting on clay for the first time in their professional careers. The night session crowd arrived expecting something special. They got it.

A Tiebreak, a Comeback, a Demolition

The first set was everything you could want from a match of this billing. Jodar and Fonseca exchanged momentum throughout, neither player finding a clean break until they arrived at the tiebreak with the score locked at 6-6. Jodar held his nerve at 7-4, converting in front of the roaring home fans.

Fonseca responded immediately in the second set. The Brazilian — who made waves in 2025 with his rapid ascent through the rankings and has continued his development in 2026 — raised his level and took the set, levelling at one set apiece. For a moment, the match could have gone either way.

It did not stay balanced for long. Jodar came out in the third set and played some of the cleanest tennis of his young career. He won 6-1, dropping only one game, overwhelming Fonseca with the intensity and accuracy that the hometown atmosphere seemed to amplify rather than burden. When the final point landed, Jodar raised his arms to the crowd. The stadium answered.

The Historic Context

Jodar becomes the eighth teenager in Open Era history to reach the round of 16 at the Madrid Open. The list includes Rafael Nadal and Carlos Alcaraz — two players who went on to win Roland Garros titles, and between them have been the dominant force in men's clay-court tennis for most of the last two decades.

That company alone tells you something about what Jodar's season has become. This is a player who won the Marrakech title on clay earlier this month, beat World No. 8 Alex de Minaur 6-3, 6-1 in the Madrid second round, and now stands in the last 16 of one of the biggest events on the calendar. He is not a surprise anymore — he is a genuine contender.

Fonseca, for his part, remains an exceptional talent. This was his first Masters 1000 third round, and the Brazilian played an excellent first two sets. He will have many more nights like this. But on this one, in this city, he ran into Jodar at his most inspired.

What's Next

Jodar advances to the Madrid round of 16, where a potential clash with world No. 1 Jannik Sinner looms if both win their next matches. Sinner faces Cameron Norrie in his R16; Jodar's path through the draw sets up a possible quarterfinal meeting with the top seed.

For now, Jodar takes a night that belongs to him, to Alcorcón, and to the crowd that willed him through every tense point. A teenager who grew up near this stadium just made history inside it.
