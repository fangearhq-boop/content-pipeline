Sinner Extends Masters 1000 Streak to 24, Moves One Step Closer to Unprecedented Fifth Consecutive Title

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 27, 2026

Jannik Sinner does not make winning look difficult. He makes it look inevitable. The world No. 1 defeated Danish qualifier Elmer Moller 6-2, 6-3 in 1 hour and 18 minutes at the Mutua Madrid Open on Sunday, extending his consecutive Masters 1000 win streak to 24 — the fourth longest in series history since the modern format began in 1990.

Moller, 22, arrived in the main draw as a qualifier who had earned his place through merit. He played well enough to win six games. That was all. Sinner absorbed early resistance and then gradually turned the match into the kind of exercise in precision that has become his signature: clean groundstrokes, structured rallies, barely an error when one is not affordable.

The Streak in Context

Twenty-four consecutive Masters 1000 victories is a number that demands context. The record books hold the names of the players who have reached such heights: Novak Djokovic, Rafael Nadal, Roger Federer. Sinner is now in the conversation with them in one of the sport's most demanding statistical categories.

More immediately compelling is what the streak represents in terms of titles. Since winning the Paris Masters in November 2025, Sinner has captured every Masters 1000 he has entered: Indian Wells, Miami, Monte Carlo. Madrid would be the fifth consecutive title — something no player has achieved in the Open Era. The record stands as one of the few remaining milestones he has not yet claimed.

He has nine matches left in Madrid to find out.

The Italian Double

Sinner was not the only Italian celebrating on Sunday. Lorenzo Musetti defeated Tallon Griekspoor 6-4, 7-5 to advance to the Madrid round of 16, setting up a fourth-round clash with 11th seed Jiri Lehecka. Lehecka leads the head-to-head 2-1, but Musetti holds the advantage on clay — winning their only previous clay meeting at the Monte Carlo Masters in 2025.

The pairing of Sinner and Musetti in the last 16 of a Masters 1000 on clay is a reminder of how well-stocked Italian men's tennis has become. A decade ago, Italy had no top-10 players. Now the country has the world No. 1 and one of the tour's better clay-court threats, both competing deep into a major event in the same week.

Norrie Stands Between Sinner and the Quarterfinals

Sinner's next opponent is Cameron Norrie, the British 19th seed, who defeated Thiago Tirante 7-5, 7-6(5) to reach the round of 16. Norrie is a reliable baseliner who has had success at the Masters level before — but Sinner is playing on a different plane right now.

The R16 matchup is winnable for Sinner. Whether he converts it, and how cleanly he does so, will determine his readiness for the deeper rounds where history awaits.

What's Next

Sinner faces Norrie in the Madrid round of 16. Musetti faces Lehecka. Both matches are scheduled for Tuesday or Wednesday as the tournament enters its second week. Should Sinner advance through the R16 and quarterfinals, a potential semifinal against the winner of the other half of the draw — which includes Jodar, Fils, and Zverev — awaits.

The fifth Masters 1000 title is within reach. The stretch run in Madrid starts now.
