Finals Day Around the All England Club: Cruz Hewitt, Arevalo's History, and the Mixed Doubles Title

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 12, 2026

Wimbledon Finals Day on July 12 features two singles finals at the top of the order, but the stories beyond Centre Court's main events are worth noting. Cruz Hewitt — son of two-time Wimbledon champion Lleyton Hewitt — competed in the boys' singles final, while the mixed doubles title was settled earlier in the week in a match that delivered a piece of history for tennis in El Salvador.

Cruz Hewitt in the Boys' Final

Lleyton Hewitt won Wimbledon in 2002, his first of two Grand Slam titles, and cemented himself as one of the most competitive players of his generation. His son Cruz Hewitt has now reached the boys' singles final at the All England Club — the same lawns where his father made his name — competing in the junior draw on Finals Day.

Cruz's opponent in the boys' final was Jordan Lee of the United States. The result was not yet confirmed at the time of writing, with the match scheduled as part of Finals Day programming on July 12. Regardless of outcome, reaching a Wimbledon boys' singles final represents a significant junior achievement and places Cruz's name in the All England Club's history.

For Lleyton Hewitt, watching his son play on the courts where he became a champion carries an obvious weight. The Hewitt name has a long association with Wimbledon — and Finals Day 2026 added a new chapter to it.

Arevalo Makes History in Mixed Doubles

The Wimbledon mixed doubles final was settled on Thursday, July 9. Jelena Ostapenko of Latvia and Marcelo Arevalo of El Salvador defeated Storm Hunter and Marc Polmans of Australia 4-6, 7-5, 6-2 in 1 hour and 56 minutes. The third set was played with the Centre Court roof closed.

For both Ostapenko and Arevalo, it was a first Grand Slam mixed doubles title. For Arevalo, it was also a milestone of broader significance: he became the first player from El Salvador ever to win a Wimbledon title. The achievement placed him in the All England Club's records as a pioneer for his country in tennis.

It was not Arevalo's first Grand Slam doubles success. He won the men's doubles at Roland Garros in 2022 and again in 2024. The 2026 Wimbledon mixed doubles title adds a third Grand Slam doubles crown across two events — a body of work that marks him as one of the most accomplished doubles specialists active on the tour.

Ostapenko, the 2017 French Open singles champion, added a mixed doubles title to her Grand Slam record, giving her a second major hardware piece from two completely different disciplines of the game.

What's Next

Cruz Hewitt's result in the boys' final will be confirmed as Finals Day results are posted. Marcelo Arevalo and Jelena Ostapenko return to the doubles circuit as newly crowned Wimbledon mixed doubles champions. The North American hard-court summer — featuring Rogers Cup and the Cincinnati Open leading into the US Open — awaits the full tour as the grass season closes at the All England Club.
