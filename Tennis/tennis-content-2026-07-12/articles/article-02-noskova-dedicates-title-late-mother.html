Noskova Dedicates Wimbledon Title to Late Mother: "I Definitely Would Not Be Standing Here Without Her"

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 12, 2026

The 2026 Wimbledon women's final will be remembered for more than its scoreline. After Linda Noskova defeated Karolina Muchova 6-2, 5-7, 6-3 to claim the title, the moments that followed — on the court, in her speech, and between two friends from the same small Czech city — moved an entire stadium and much of the tennis world watching at home.

A Kiss to the Sky

When Noskova's final shot landed to clinch the match, she paused on the Centre Court grass. Then she looked up, and blew a kiss skyward. The gesture was not for the crowd — it was for her mother, who passed away in 2024.

In her victory speech, Noskova made the tribute explicit. "There's one more person I'd like to thank, which is my mum," she said. "I definitely would not be standing here without her, so thank you." The building fell quiet. Petra Kvitova, seated in the Royal Box, blinked back tears. In the lower sections of Centre Court, spectators who had come to watch a tennis match found themselves somewhere else entirely.

Noskova had spoken throughout the fortnight about the emotional weight of this tournament — "the sad tears and the happy tears," as she put it in her speech — and the dedication made clear what lay beneath those words. A daughter carrying a loss. A champion carrying her mother's memory across two weeks of Wimbledon, all the way to the moment she needed it most.

"My Ex-Friend"

Across the net, Karolina Muchova was composing herself. She had fought through five match points in the second set, had come back from 5-2 down, and had ultimately fallen short. She was tearful when she stepped up to her microphone — and then she said something that made Centre Court laugh through its tears.

"It's very hard to find the words," Muchova said, "but I'll start with Linda, my ex-friend."

The line landed perfectly. The laughter broke the tension that had been building since the match ended. Noskova, still holding her trophy, smiled. The two women — both from Přerov, a Czech city of 43,000 people — embraced. The WTA would later describe the final as one that "captured hearts with humor and humanity."

Two Champions from the Same City

Přerov is not a city associated with tennis dynasties. Population 43,000. Located in the Olomouc Region of the Czech Republic. It produced two players who, on Saturday, contested the first all-Czech Grand Slam final in history at the most famous tennis club in the world.

Noskova and Muchova grew up together in that city. They came through the same Czech tennis ecosystem. They know each other — as opponents, as compatriots, and as friends whose relationship now carries the gentle, permanent asterisk that Muchova herself named on Centre Court.

Czech women have won Wimbledon in 2023 (Marketa Vondrousova), 2024 (Barbora Krejcikova), and now 2026 (Noskova). Three of the last four Wimbledon women's titles have gone to the Czech Republic. The dynasty is real — and it ran through Přerov today in ways that had nothing to do with rankings or tactics and everything to do with what sport can mean at its best.

What's Next

Linda Noskova returns to the tour as the reigning Wimbledon champion, carrying a Wimbledon title and the memory of how she won it. Karolina Muchova, whose resilience and grace on Saturday reinforced her standing as one of the sport's most complete competitors, will take a finalist's ranking boost into the hard-court season. Their friendship — minus the "ex-" qualifier — presumably resumes shortly.
