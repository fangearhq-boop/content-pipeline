Navone Survives Match Points, Merida Aguilar Completes Qualifier Fairy Tale at Bucharest

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | April 4, 2026

BRD Nastase Tiriac Trophy — Bucharest, Romania | ATP 250 | Red Clay

The BRD Nastase Tiriac Trophy semifinals on Saturday produced the kind of clay court drama that reminds tennis fans why the spring swing is worth following beyond the Masters 1000 events. Mariano Navone survived two match points in an epic three-set battle. Daniel Merida Aguilar kept winning when nobody expected him to. The result is an ATP 250 final featuring two first-time finalists who arrived there through completely different routes.

Navone's semifinal against Botic van de Zandschulp was the longer and more emotionally charged of the two matches. The Argentine dropped the first set 5-7, then faced match points in the second before saving them both and forcing a tiebreak. Van de Zandschulp had multiple chances to end Navone's run and could not close them out. Navone took the second set, reset his mental approach entirely, and dominated the third 7-5. The full score — 5-7, 7-6, 7-5 — across approximately three and a half hours of clay court combat tells the story of a match that could have ended three different ways.

The Qualifier Who Would Not Stop Winning

Daniel Merida Aguilar entered the Bucharest tournament ranked No. 136 in the world. He was not expected to be in the main draw. He came through qualifying — an additional set of rounds that most top players bypass entirely — and then kept winning main draw matches until he had beaten his way to the final.

His semifinal win over Fabian Marozsan illustrated the trajectory of his week: dropped the first set 6-7(4), then took control of the match and won the second and third sets 6-3, 6-1 as Marozsan ran out of answers. Merida Aguilar is Spanish, 136th in the world, and now has a shot at his first ATP Tour title. The path from qualifying to the final at an ATP 250 is one that very few players complete in a single tournament run.

Why These Stories Matter

The Bucharest final — Navone vs. Merida Aguilar — is a matchup between two players who have never held an ATP tour title. Both are in the first final of their careers. Navone arrives with his match-saving resilience fresh in the legs. Merida Aguilar arrives having played one more match than most finalists (his qualifying run), which means his body has absorbed more court time this week.

The week in Bucharest was also disrupted by heavy rain, which wiped out most of Thursday's second-round matches and compressed the schedule heading into the weekend. Both finalists had to adapt to the scheduling changes. That context matters when assessing their physical state heading into Sunday's title match.

The Broader Clay Picture

Navone's run to the Bucharest final is part of a broader Argentine story playing out across the opening week of the European clay season. His compatriot Marco Trungelliti reached the Marrakech final on the same day, and Camilo Ugo Carabelli went deep at Marrakech as well. Three Argentines in two European clay finals simultaneously is a snapshot of South American clay court depth that reflects years of development on the surfaces where Argentine players have traditionally excelled.

What's Next

The Bucharest final between Mariano Navone and Daniel Merida Aguilar is scheduled for Sunday, April 5. For Navone, winning would be redemption for the match points he faced in the semifinal and confirmation of his clay court pedigree. For Merida Aguilar, winning would complete one of the most unlikely qualifier-to-champion runs of the 2026 season. Watch for Navone's heavy topspin baseline game against Merida Aguilar's ability to maintain the composure that carried him through qualifying and three main draw matches without dropping a set in the second and third rounds.
