Nine Withdrawals and a Warning: Monte Carlo Qualifying Begins as Fritz's Clay Future in Doubt

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 4, 2026

Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters — Monte Carlo, Monaco | ATP Masters 1000 | Red Clay

Qualifying for the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters began Saturday with 14 first-round matches at 11 AM CEST, and the tournament news off the court continued to overshadow what is happening on it. A total of nine ATP players have now withdrawn from the draw — a number that has fundamentally altered the event's character since Thursday's draw ceremony confirmed the bracket.

The three most significant new withdrawals, all confirmed by ATP Tour official sources, are Novak Djokovic, Taylor Fritz, and Jack Draper. Each case carries its own implications for the player involved and for the broader clay season calendar.

Djokovic: First Monte Carlo Absence Since 2011

Novak Djokovic's withdrawal due to a right shoulder injury makes 2026 the first year he has missed Monte Carlo since 2011 — a span of 15 consecutive appearances. Djokovic had already withdrawn from the Miami Open with the same injury before the Monte Carlo draw was even announced. The ATP Tour official confirmed his withdrawal statement, with Djokovic identifying the Madrid Open (April 22 – May 3) as his current target for return.

The absence of the 24-time Grand Slam champion — regardless of where he currently sits in the rankings — removes one of the most experienced clay court competitors in the sport's history from the Monaco draw. Djokovic has never won Monte Carlo but has reached multiple finals and has always been a factor in the bottom half of the draw when healthy.

Fritz: Knee Tendinitis and the Clay Season Question

Taylor Fritz's withdrawal from Monte Carlo due to ongoing knee tendinitis is the development with the most significant forward-looking implications. Tennis365 has reported that Fritz may skip the entire clay swing — meaning not just Monte Carlo, but potentially Madrid, Rome, and the French Open preparation events.

Former world No. 1 Andy Roddick weighed in publicly, urging Fritz to take a proper rest period rather than risk worsening the tendinitis by competing on clay. Roddick's perspective carries weight: as the last American man to win a Grand Slam, his public advocacy for Fritz's long-term health over short-term ranking points reflects a broader concern about managing a knee injury that has now lingered for over six months. Fritz's 2026 record stood at 12-8 at the time of his Monte Carlo withdrawal.

Draper: Managing a Bruised Humerus

Jack Draper's withdrawal, confirmed by Sky Sports, is a precautionary measure to manage a bruised humerus bone in his left arm. Draper has not set a longer absence — he is currently targeting Barcelona (April 13-19) as his return date, suggesting he expects to be available for the second week of the clay swing. The injury is being managed conservatively rather than requiring extended time off.

The Altered Draw and the No. 1 Race

The original Monte Carlo draw, announced Thursday at 5 PM CEST, was already missing Holger Rune, Sebastian Korda, and others. The addition of Djokovic, Fritz, and Draper to the withdrawal list means the 2026 edition has been altered significantly from its intended field. SI.com confirmed the nine-player withdrawal total.

For Carlos Alcaraz — defending champion with 1,000 ranking points to protect — the depleted draw represents both opportunity and continued pressure. For Jannik Sinner — who has zero points to defend after his 2025 ban — a Monte Carlo title at any level of competition would close the gap with Alcaraz in the No. 1 race. With Stefan Tsitsipas unseeded but still in the draw as the three-time Monte Carlo champion, the tournament retains genuine danger for whoever reaches the later rounds.

Qualifying round one runs Saturday. Main draw play begins Sunday, April 5.

What's Next

Monitor Taylor Fritz's clay season decision closely — if he skips Madrid and Rome, his French Open preparation would be severely compromised, and his seeding at Roland Garros could slip. Djokovic's Madrid target suggests a return in approximately three weeks; his clay form at age 38 after shoulder rest will be one of the most watched stories of the spring. Jack Draper's timeline points to Barcelona, where he will have a chance to demonstrate his clay game is intact ahead of the major clay events.
