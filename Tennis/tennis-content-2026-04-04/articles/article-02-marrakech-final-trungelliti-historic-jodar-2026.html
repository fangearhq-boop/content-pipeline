Trungelliti Makes History at Marrakech: 36-Year-Old Stuns Darderi, Sets Generational Final with Jodar

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 4, 2026

Grand Prix Hassan II — Marrakech, Morocco | ATP 250 | Red Clay

The Grand Prix Hassan II produced its most dramatic result on Saturday when Marco Trungelliti — a 36-year-old Argentine who had never reached an ATP Tour final in his career — defeated defending champion Luciano Darderi 6-4, 7-6(2) to become the oldest player in the Open Era to reach a debut ATP final.

Darderi had entered the semifinal as the clear favorite. The Italian had advanced to the last four via a walkover when Yannick Hanfmann withdrew before their quarterfinal match, leaving Darderi the freshest player in the draw. None of that rested state appeared to help him. Trungelliti controlled the match from the baseline, executed his game plan with a discipline that belied his status as a player making a long-awaited breakthrough, and closed out the tiebreak in the second set emphatically to advance.

The result earned Trungelliti a place in tennis history: the oldest first-time finalist in the Open Era. He turned 36 years old with no ATP Tour final to his name. He will play for his first title on April 5.

Jodar Completes the Generational Contrast

The second semifinal produced its own headline. Rafael Jodar, a 19-year-old Spanish wildcard, defeated Camilo Ugo Carabelli to reach his own first ATP Tour final. The result sets up a Marrakech final that spans 17 years in age difference — a 36-year-old and a 19-year-old, both in the biggest match of their respective careers.

Jodar's presence in the final reflects an impressive week on Moroccan red clay. Wildcards rarely make ATP finals; doing so at 19 is an even rarer achievement. His run has been one of the peripheral stories of the 2026 clay season opener, largely overshadowed by events in Charleston and Bucharest, but entirely worthy of attention.

A Career Built on Clay

Trungelliti's journey to this final is worth understanding in full. He has spent years on the ATP circuit, competing primarily on clay courts across Europe and South America. His record entering Saturday's semifinal was 12-1 on clay in 2026 — a number that speaks to a player arriving at his peak form on the surface where he has always been most comfortable.

The Open Era record he now holds for the oldest debut finalist underscores how unusual his path has been. Players at 36 on the ATP Tour are the exception, not the rule. Many careers have ended before reaching this stage. Trungelliti simply kept competing, kept improving his clay game, and arrived in Marrakech in the best form of his career.

The Argentine Connection

Trungelliti's semifinal win contributes to a remarkable Argentine story unfolding across two simultaneous clay events this week. His fellow countryman Mariano Navone is in the Bucharest final, and Camilo Ugo Carabelli reached the Marrakech semifinal — three Argentine players in two European finals on the same weekend. The clay season has barely started, and Argentina is already placing its signature on the results page.

What's Next

The Marrakech final between Marco Trungelliti and Rafael Jodar is scheduled for Sunday, April 5. It will be the oldest combined debut-finalist matchup in memory — and the contrast between their life experiences, their years on tour, and the paths that brought them both to this stage make it one of the most compelling ATP 250 finals of the year so far. For Trungelliti, the question is whether persistence pays off with a title. For Jodar, the question is whether 19 is too young to win — or exactly the right age.
