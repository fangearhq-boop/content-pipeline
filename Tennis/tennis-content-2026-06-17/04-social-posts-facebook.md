### STORY 1: Kyrgios Withdraws from Halle

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

The Shelton-Kyrgios match we were all looking forward to is not happening.

Nick Kyrgios withdrew from the Terra Wortmann Open in Halle on Wednesday — both singles and doubles — due to a right knee injury. He twisted the knee during practice on Monday and has been dealing with pain since. Lorenzo Sonego moves into the singles draw as a lucky loser.

Kyrgios is trying to reassure everyone. His statement: "I had a minor issue yesterday during practice and twisted my knee a little. Nothing to worry about. I'm back on tour and I'm excited to play in Mallorca and Wimbledon."

The concern, though, is real. He lost in the first round in Stuttgart last week — his first grass match back — and now can't play in Halle. That's two consecutive non-starts on a surface where he has historically been outstanding. His Wimbledon doubles wildcard with Alexander Bublik is also now uncertain.

Wimbledon is 12 days away. Do you think Kyrgios makes it?

### STORY 2: Sabalenka Upset in Berlin

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka has been knocked out of the Berlin Open by Ekaterina Alexandrova.

The score: 3-6, 6-3, 7-6 in two hours and 36 minutes. Sabalenka saved 15 break points in the match. It wasn't enough. She lost the tiebreak — and with it, the chance to build grass-court momentum before Wimbledon.

Alexandrova is ranked 26th in the world. She's a solid player, but not the type of name that tends to end a No. 1 player's tournament on a Wednesday in Berlin. That's the upset of the week.

The timing matters. Wimbledon seedings are set based on the June 22 rankings, and the draw ceremony is June 26. Sabalenka will still project as the No. 1 seed, but this loss does nothing for her confidence heading into the grass major.

On the other side of the draw, Rybakina and Gauff now have a cleaner path to the Berlin final.

What do you think — does this loss change how you see Sabalenka at Wimbledon?

### STORY 3: Zverev Ties Nadal's Record; Bublik Shock Exit

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Two major stories from Halle on Tuesday.

First: Alexander Zverev is now tied with Rafael Nadal for the most ATP 500 wins in history. Zverev defeated Vit Kopriva 6-3, 4-6, 6-2 in his first match since winning Roland Garros, recording his 121st ATP 500 win. Nadal set the record. Zverev has now matched it — in his first grass match as a Grand Slam champion.

Second: defending champion Alexander Bublik is out. He lost to Italian qualifier Mattia Bellucci 7-6(6), 6-1 in the first round. The first set came down to a tiebreak where Bublik had three set points and couldn't convert. After that, Bellucci took over. This is the first time a defending champion at Halle has lost in the first round since 2003.

Medvedev also advanced on Day 2.

Day 3 brings the first look at Shelton — well, a version of it. Kyrgios has withdrawn, so Shelton now faces Sonego (see separate story). But the Zverev record story is worth sitting with: tying Nadal at anything is historical.

### STORY 4: Williams Sisters Wimbledon Doubles Wildcard

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Serena and Venus Williams are coming to Wimbledon.

Wimbledon has awarded the sisters a women's doubles wildcard for the 2026 Championships — their first competitive partnership since the 2022 US Open. The announcement came on Tuesday.

Serena, now 44, has been building back toward competition through doubles. She played with Victoria Mboko at the Queen's Club WTA event and in Berlin. Now Wimbledon — where the Williams name has its deepest history — is the stage.

They have won the Wimbledon women's doubles six times. They have 14 Grand Slam doubles titles together. This is not a symbolic participation; this is two legends, in their late 30s and mid-40s, competing on grass at SW19.

Wimbledon begins June 29. Draw ceremony June 26.

Are you watching?
