Queen's Club Day 2 Results: de Minaur, Lehecka, Nakashima Advance; Zheng Wins in Nottingham

By Marcus Cole | June 17, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

A productive Day 2 at the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club saw the tournament's top seeds and several strong contenders move through without major disruption. Top seed Alex de Minaur, No. 2 seed Jiri Lehecka, and American Brandon Nakashima all advanced — with Nakashima reaching the quarterfinals after a dominant straight-set win. At the Lexus Nottingham Open on the WTA side, Zheng Qinwen recovered from a lost first set to defeat Maria Sakkari in three sets.

Queen's Club: Seeds Hold, Nakashima Reaches QF

Alex de Minaur opened his 2026 HSBC Championships campaign with a first-round win over Gabriel Diallo. The Australian top seed, who enters Wimbledon as one of the cleaner grass-court movers on the ATP Tour, handled the Canadian in what was described as a composed opener. De Minaur has historically performed well at Queen's Club, and a smooth first-round result sets up his run into the second week.

Jiri Lehecka, the No. 2 seed and the finalist at the 2025 HSBC Championships, opened against Libema champion Kamil Majchrzak in a marquee Day 2 match. Lehecka won 7-5, 7-6(4) — a tight, two-set result that reflected the quality of Majchrzak's grasscourt form coming off his first ATP title last Sunday in 's-Hertogenbosch. The result establishes Lehecka as a genuine contender for the week, while Majchrzak's early exit is a reminder that a Libema title does not guarantee automatic Queen's Club success.

Brandon Nakashima was the story of the day. The American defeated Ignacio Buse 6-2, 6-2 in a dominant straight-set performance that was barely contested in either set. The result puts Nakashima in the quarterfinals — back-to-back quarterfinal appearances at The Queen's Club, a run that is beginning to look like a pattern rather than a coincidence. Nakashima's game translates well to grass: a flat serve, controlled groundstrokes, and a comfort on the surface that has not always been present for American men at this event.

Additional Day 2 results: Alejandro Davidovich Fokina defeated Cam Norrie; Denis Shapovalov edged Jack Pinnington Jones 6-4, 4-6, 7-6(3) in three sets; Arthur Fery defeated fellow Briton Toby Samuel 6-0, 6-2, a result that served as Fery's first career win at the HSBC Championships.

Nottingham: Zheng Comes Through Sakkari in Three

At the Lexus Nottingham Open, the grass-court WTA 250 running simultaneously with the Berlin Open, Zheng Qinwen defeated Maria Sakkari 4-6, 7-6(1), 6-4 in a match that tested her over two hours. Zheng lost the first set but responded with authority in the second, winning a dominant tiebreak 7-1 before closing out the third set to advance.

The win is a form signal for Zheng ahead of Wimbledon. She entered Nottingham as one of the higher-ranked players in the field and showed the mental resilience required to recover after dropping the opening set. Sakkari has traditionally been more effective on hard courts and clay, but on a grass surface her game created enough disruption to extend Zheng through a full three sets before losing.

Leylah Fernandez also advanced at Nottingham on Wednesday, continuing her run through a field that includes a number of players using the event as final grass preparation before Wimbledon qualifying.

What's Next

The HSBC Championships at Queen's Club continues through Sunday, June 21 with quarterfinals expected later in the week. De Minaur will play his second-round match; Nakashima advances to the quarterfinals. The Lexus Nottingham Open also runs through June 21. Wimbledon qualifying begins June 22; the draw ceremony is June 26; the Championships begin June 29.
