Serena and Venus Williams Awarded Wimbledon 2026 Doubles Wildcard — First Partnership Since 2022 US Open

By Elena Voss | June 17, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The All England Club has awarded a women's doubles wildcard to Serena and Venus Williams for the 2026 Wimbledon Championships — their first competitive partnership since the 2022 US Open. The announcement was made on Tuesday, June 16. Wimbledon begins June 29.

This is the match-up that tennis fans most associated with the Williams legacy: the two sisters, at SW19, on the surface where they have combined to win six doubles titles. Serena, 44, has been building toward a competitive doubles return over the past several weeks. The Wimbledon wildcard is the most significant venue she has played since walking away from singles competition four years ago.

The Road Back

Serena's competitive comeback has been deliberate and staged through doubles partnerships rather than singles. She played the doubles draw at the Queen's Club WTA event earlier this month with Victoria Mboko, and competed in doubles at the VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Tennis Open this week. Each of those appearances has been a test of competitive readiness — match fitness, movement, crowd presence, and the mental recalibration required to compete at the highest level after years away.

The Wimbledon doubles wildcard moves that arc to its natural conclusion. If there is a place where Serena Williams's presence commands the most resonance, it is the All England Club, where she won seven singles titles and three doubles titles alongside Venus.

Venus, meanwhile, has maintained a lower competitive profile but has stayed connected to the tour. The sisters' pairing at the 2022 US Open — a wildcard doubles entry that drew enormous attention but ended early — was their last competitive doubles appearance before this announcement.

The History

Serena and Venus Williams have won six Wimbledon women's doubles titles together: 2000, 2002, 2008, 2009, 2012, and 2016. Across all Grand Slams, they have combined for 14 doubles titles as a partnership. Those numbers exist at a level that no currently active women's doubles team has approached.

They also won the women's doubles gold medal at the London 2012 Olympics, which was played at Wimbledon — a win on the same courts where they will now compete in the 2026 Championships. The symbolic weight of returning to those courts is not lost on the tennis community.

At 44 and in her early 40s respectively, neither sister is operating at the peak of her physical prime. But doubles tennis at the Grand Slam level requires different things from singles: positioning, reading the partner, net reflexes, and serve-return combinations. The Williams sisters, whatever their current physical state, have spent decades developing an understanding of each other's game that is irreplicable by practice alone.

What This Means for Wimbledon

The wildcard announcement adds a separate emotional narrative to a Wimbledon that is already rich in storylines — Zverev as first-time Grand Slam champion, Sinner defending, Sabalenka as women's No. 1, Kyrgios's uncertain return. A Williams sisters doubles run provides the tournament with its most accessible entry point for casual fans who may not track the ATP and WTA rankings week-to-week but know exactly who Serena and Venus are.

Whether they advance in the doubles draw will depend on the competition and on how their partnership holds up over match conditions at Wimbledon. History says they know exactly what to do on this surface.

What's Next

The Wimbledon draw ceremony takes place June 26. Qualifying begins June 22. The Championships begin Monday, June 29 at the All England Club in London, running through Sunday, July 12. The Williams sisters doubles draw placement will be determined at the draw ceremony.
