Kyrgios Withdraws from Halle with Knee Injury, Wimbledon Return Now in Doubt

By Elena Voss | June 17, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The match that grass-court tennis fans had been circling since the Halle draw was released is not going to happen. Nick Kyrgios has withdrawn from the Terra Wortmann Open in Halle — both singles and doubles — citing a right knee injury sustained during practice on Monday. Lorenzo Sonego enters the singles draw as a lucky loser. Ben Shelton, who had been preparing for the first ATP head-to-head between himself and the Australian, now faces a considerably different opening opponent.

The withdrawal comes eight days before Wimbledon qualifying begins and twelve days before the Championships themselves. Multiple outlets have reported that Kyrgios's participation at Wimbledon is now under threat.

What Happened

According to the official Halle Open tournament statement and reporting from multiple tennis outlets, Kyrgios twisted his right knee during the final three minutes of a practice session on Monday afternoon. The injury did not resolve in time for him to compete.

Kyrgios withdrew from singles first, with Sonego moving in as lucky loser. He subsequently also withdrew from doubles, where he had been drawn alongside his practice partner. The Terra Wortmann Open issued a formal statement confirming both withdrawals.

Kyrgios was defiant in his personal response. In a statement circulated on social media, he wrote: "I had a minor issue yesterday during practice with my doubles partner and I twisted my knee a little. Nothing to worry about. I'm back on tour and I'm excited to play in Mallorca and Wimbledon."

A Difficult Grass Return

The Halle withdrawal extends what has become a frustrating grass-court season for Kyrgios. He received a wild card into the Boss Open in Stuttgart last week — his first competitive grass appearance since the 2022 Wimbledon final — and lost in the first round to Brayden Schnur. That result, combined with a Halle withdrawal before a single match, means Kyrgios has not won a grass-court match in the 2026 season.

The trajectory matters because Wimbledon is where Kyrgios's grass credentials are most established. His 2022 final run — he lost in four sets to Novak Djokovic — remains his deepest Grand Slam performance, built on serve-and-volley tennis that should theoretically transfer well to SW19's fast courts. The concern now is whether he arrives at Wimbledon match-fit, or whether he arrives having had zero competitive grass time.

Kyrgios had also been awarded a Wimbledon doubles wildcard with Alexander Bublik. The tournament status of that entry is now uncertain pending further word on his knee recovery.

The Shelton Picture

For Ben Shelton, the opponent change is significant in tone but not necessarily in difficulty. Sonego is a capable grass-court player — a former Queen's Club finalist — but he is a very different stylistic challenge than Kyrgios.

Shelton arrives in Halle as the Stuttgart champion, fresh off a 6-4, 2-6, 6-4 win over Taylor Fritz to claim his third ATP title of 2026. His form is arguably the best of any player currently in the Halle draw, and he was prepared for a high-octane serving battle against Kyrgios. Sonego tends to play a more consistent, baseline-focused style, which may require Shelton to adjust his approach.

The match against Sonego is scheduled for Wednesday at the Heristo-Arena.

What's Next

Kyrgios says he intends to compete at the Mallorca Open before Wimbledon. Wimbledon qualifying begins June 22; the draw ceremony is June 26. The Championships begin June 29. Whether Kyrgios appears in the singles draw at SW19 will depend on what medical staff determine about his knee in the coming days. His stated plan is optimistic. The timeline is tight.
