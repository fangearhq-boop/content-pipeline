Zverev Ties Nadal's ATP 500 Record with 121st Win at Halle; Defending Champion Bublik Falls in Round One

By Ryan Calloway | June 17, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The first match Alexander Zverev played as a Grand Slam champion produced a milestone he now shares with Rafael Nadal. By defeating Vit Kopriva 6-3, 4-6, 6-2 in the first round of the Terra Wortmann Open on Tuesday, Zverev recorded his 121st ATP 500 win — tying Nadal's all-time record in the format. It was a match that took three sets, required Zverev to find his footing after losing the second, and ended with a comfortable third-set run that moved him into the second round at Halle.

Also on Tuesday: defending Halle champion Alexander Bublik was eliminated in the first round by Italian qualifier Mattia Bellucci 7-6(6), 6-1. It was the first time a defending champion at the Halle Open has been eliminated in the first round since 2003.

The Record

The ATP 500 series was created in 2009. Rafael Nadal, in his career-long excellence across Barcelona, Monte-Carlo, and other prominent ATP events, accumulated 121 wins at this tournament level — a record that stood as a symbol of sustained excellence across the 500-point tier. Zverev, with his Kopriva win in Halle, has now matched it.

The context adds weight to the number. Zverev won Roland Garros nine days before this match, defeating Flavio Cobolli in five sets to claim his first Grand Slam title. The Halle first round was his first competitive appearance since Paris, and he opened it by tying one of tennis's most respected all-time marks. He held the serve with authority in the first set, absorbed a second set where Kopriva found rhythm, and then regrouped to win the third set with relative ease.

At the ATP 500 level, Zverev has been dominant for years. He has won multiple times at Rotterdam, Hamburg, and Halle itself. The depth of his 500-tier record across surfaces and countries makes the Nadal comparison meaningful rather than superficial.

Zverev's Grass Debut as Champion

There was genuine curiosity around how Zverev would look in his first grass match since Paris. The clay-to-grass transition is among the most demanding in tennis — foot positioning, ball bounce timing, net approach decisions, serve-and-volley instincts all require recalibration. The Roland Garros champion typically receives enormous scrutiny in his first post-Slam appearance.

Against Kopriva, Zverev showed the expected settling-in period. The second set, won by the Czech, reflected a player who had not yet fully adjusted to the Heristo-Arena surface. But the third set showed what grass-season Zverev looks like at full function: clean groundstrokes, first-serve dominance, and the ability to close a match without drama. His next opponent in the second round is his compatriot Yannick Hanfmann.

Bublik's Shock Exit

Alexander Bublik won Halle in 2025 in one of the more surprising grass-court titles of that season. He entered 2026 as the defending champion and the No. 7 seed — a position that reflected a solid but declining trajectory from his peak performance last year.

What Mattia Bellucci produced against Bublik on Tuesday was more than an upset; it was an execution. Bellucci is an Italian qualifier ranked well outside the top 50. In the first-set tiebreak, Bublik held three set points. Bellucci saved each one and won the tiebreak 8-6. After that pivotal moment, the match turned sharply: Bellucci lost only one game in the second set, winning 6-1 to complete the straight-set upset.

Bublik, speaking to reporters after the match, reflected on the loss without dismissing Bellucci's performance. The Italian's win was the result of aggressive grass-court play that exploited Bublik's inability to close the tiebreak when the match was within reach.

This was the first time a defending Halle champion has been eliminated in the first round since 2003. Bublik has now lost in the first round of five events in recent months, signaling a prolonged slump from a player whose serve-and-volley arsenal should theoretically flourish on grass.

What's Next

Zverev faces Yannick Hanfmann in the second round at the Terra Wortmann Open. Medvedev, who also advanced on Day 2, continues his Halle campaign. Bellucci's run will be one of the secondary stories to watch as the week progresses. The Terra Wortmann Open main draw runs through Sunday, June 21. Wimbledon qualifying begins June 22; the Championships start June 29.
