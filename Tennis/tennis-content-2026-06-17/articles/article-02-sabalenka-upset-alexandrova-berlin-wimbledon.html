Sabalenka Out: World No. 1 Falls to Alexandrova in Berlin with Wimbledon 12 Days Out

By Marcus Cole | June 17, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka has been eliminated from the VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Tennis Open in the Round of 16 by Ekaterina Alexandrova. The final score was 3-6, 6-3, 7-6 — a two-hour, 36-minute match that Sabalenka dominated in terms of break point opportunities and yet could not convert when it mattered. Alexandrova, ranked 26th in the world, won the tiebreak and ended the top seed's Berlin campaign.

The timing is not incidental. Wimbledon begins June 29. Sabalenka is projected to be the No. 1 seed at the All England Club. She has now lost her primary pre-Wimbledon grass-court event at the Round of 16 stage, without a title and without the form confidence that comes from a deep tournament run.

The Match

Sabalenka took the first set 6-3 in relatively comfortable fashion, appearing to be in control of a match that she was heavily favored to win. The second set shifted. Alexandrova, a hard-hitting baseliner from Russia who has shown consistent grass-court competence throughout her career, raised her level and claimed the set 6-3 to level the match.

The third set was the most contested. Sabalenka saved 15 break points across the match — a number that suggests she was frequently in defensive situations and repeatedly escaped. But the tiebreak, the final arbiter of a match that could have gone either way across an extended afternoon, went to Alexandrova.

In total, Sabalenka fought off an extraordinary number of break point opportunities. Saving 15 break points is a feat that, in isolation, would normally be a sign of resilience. In this context, it reflects a player who was under sustained pressure that she could not fully neutralize, ultimately yielding to a scoreboard that reflected Alexandrova's superior closing.

Wimbledon Implications

The Wimbledon seedings are determined by a formula that weights recent grass-court results alongside ranking points. The draw ceremony takes place June 26. Sabalenka's ranking as world No. 1 means she will almost certainly be seeded No. 1 at Wimbledon regardless of this result — Wimbledon's seeding formula has historically kept the world No. 1 at the top seed spot unless there are extraordinary circumstances.

What the result cannot provide is competitive momentum. Sabalenka has been one of the most dominant players on the WTA Tour in 2026, but her Wimbledon record requires improvement. A Berlin title would have reinforced the grass-court preparation narrative. Instead, she exits at the Round of 16 with a week remaining before the Championships.

With Sabalenka out, the Berlin draw opens. Elena Rybakina, the No. 2 seed and a natural grass-court player who won Wimbledon in 2022, now has a cleaner path to the final. Coco Gauff, who was scheduled to play Paula Badosa on Wednesday in the Round of 16, also benefits from the draw opening at the top.

Alexandrova's Performance

For Alexandrova, this is among the most significant wins of her career. Defeating a world No. 1 in a three-set match at a WTA 500 event, particularly on grass, demonstrates the type of peak-level performance that can be a career inflection point. She will face a quarterfinal opponent with the Berlin title now a genuine possibility on her side of the draw.

What's Next

The VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Tennis Open continues through Sunday, June 21. Quarterfinals are expected Thursday and Friday. Rybakina and Gauff remain as the highest-seeded contenders following Sabalenka's exit. Wimbledon qualifying begins June 22; the Championships begin June 29 at the All England Club.
