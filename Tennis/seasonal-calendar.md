# Tennis Fanrecap — Seasonal Calendar 2026

Tennis is a year-round sport with a global calendar. The season runs from January through November, with a brief off-season in December.

## Season Overview

| Period | Months | Surface | Key Events |
|--------|--------|---------|------------|
| Australian summer | Jan | Hard | Australian Open (Grand Slam) |
| Sunshine Double | Feb-Mar | Hard | Indian Wells Masters, Miami Masters |
| Clay season | Apr-Jun | Clay | Monte Carlo, Madrid, Rome (Masters), Roland Garros (Grand Slam) |
| Grass season | Jun-Jul | Grass | Queen's/Halle, Wimbledon (Grand Slam) |
| US hard court | Jul-Sep | Hard | Canadian Open, Cincinnati (Masters), US Open (Grand Slam) |
| Asian swing | Sep-Oct | Hard/Mixed | Beijing, Shanghai (Masters), Tokyo |
| Indoor season | Oct-Nov | Hard (indoor) | Basel, Paris Masters, ATP Finals (Turin), WTA Finals |
| Team events | Nov | Mixed | Davis Cup Finals, Billie Jean King Cup Finals |
| Off-season | Dec | — | Next Gen ATP Finals, exhibition events |

## Content Calendar Notes

### Currently (March 2026)
- **Indian Wells Masters** — one of the biggest non-Grand Slam events ("the fifth Slam")
- **Miami Masters** follows immediately — back-to-back "Sunshine Double"
- Rankings are settling after the Australian Open
- Clay season preparation begins in April

### Surface Transition Content Opportunities
- **Hard → Clay (April)**: Player form changes, surface specialists emerge, clay previews
- **Clay → Grass (June)**: Shortest transition, Wimbledon buildup, grass specialists spotlight
- **Grass → Hard (July)**: US Open series begins, longest stretch of the season

### Key Storylines to Track Year-Round
- Grand Slam counts and GOAT race
- ATP/WTA Race to Finals standings (who qualifies for year-end championships)
- Ranking #1 battles
- Next Gen breakthrough players
- Injury returns and comebacks
- Scheduling controversies (back-to-back events, overlap)
