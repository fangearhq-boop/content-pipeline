Goffin Earns Emotional 14th Roland Garros Main Draw Spot as Qualifying Concludes

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 20, 2026

David Goffin arrived at Roland Garros this week as he always has — determined, focused, and ready to fight for every point. But something was different this time. When the Belgian veteran won his opening qualifying match on Monday, he was in tears before he even left the court.

"Today, everything was there — the level was there, the emotion was there," Goffin said in his post-match interview on Suzanne-Lenglen. "At the end I played really well and the speech at the end was a lot of emotions. It was an amazing moment for me, something that I've never felt on the court in my career."

By Wednesday, with qualifying completing its three-day run, Goffin had secured his 14th main draw appearance at Roland Garros — the tournament he has called home for nearly 15 years of professional tennis. In a final season on tour that has taken him across Europe, this moment in Paris stands apart.

A Career of Paris Memories

Goffin, 35, was once ranked No. 7 in the world and was a fixture in the upper reaches of the ATP rankings for the better part of a decade. He has played Roland Garros qualifying before — this is not new territory. But the weight of a farewell season, the awareness that this may be his final French Open, gave Monday's win over Tseng Chun-Hsin (6-3, 6-1) a resonance that previous results here never carried.

The wild card granted to Goffin by Roland Garros tournament officials was itself a gesture of recognition. The man who reached the ATP Finals and competed at the very highest level deserved a final shot at the main draw, and the tournament gave him that chance. He rewarded them with a commanding performance and an emotional connection to the clay that was visible to everyone watching.

The Qualifying Field

Goffin was not the only established name competing for one of the 16 men's and 16 women's main draw spots made available through qualifying. The women's side featured former Grand Slam champions and former world No. 1s fighting to reclaim their place at a major.

Bianca Andreescu, the 2019 US Open champion who has been rebuilding her career after years of injury disruption, advanced past French teen Daphnée Mpetshi Perricard 6-3, 6-2 in the first qualifying round and was in action again Wednesday in her second-round match against Slovakia's Viktoria Hruncakova.

Karolina Pliskova, the former world No. 1 who reached the 2021 Australian Open final, overcame a difficult opening set against Ku Yeon-woo — dropping it 0-6 — before rallying to win 0-6, 6-3, 6-1. Her second qualifying round on Wednesday came against Spain's Marina Bassols Ribera. Sloane Stephens, the 2017 US Open champion, was also contesting her qualifying rounds to earn a return to a Grand Slam main draw.

The Road Ahead

With qualifying now complete, 32 spots — 16 on each side of the gender draw — have been filled by players who earned their way through three rounds of competition over three days. They join the direct acceptances and wild card recipients in the main draw, which will be revealed at Thursday's draw ceremony at 2:00 PM Paris time.

For Goffin, the draw ceremony will tell him who he faces in the first round. He has been to Roland Garros 13 times before. He knows this place. And if his tears on Suzanne-Lenglen on Monday were any indication, there is still something left to give here.

What's Next

The Roland Garros 2026 main draw ceremony takes place Thursday, May 21 at 2:00 PM Paris time (8:00 AM ET). Main draw play begins Sunday, May 24. David Goffin's first-round match will be scheduled during the opening days of the tournament.
