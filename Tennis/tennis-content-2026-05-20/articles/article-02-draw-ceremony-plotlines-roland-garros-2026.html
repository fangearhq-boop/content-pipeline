Five Plotlines That Will Shape the Roland Garros 2026 Draw

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 20, 2026

Thursday at 2:00 PM Paris time (8:00 AM ET), the Roland Garros 2026 main draw will be made. One hundred and twenty-eight men's players and 128 women's players will learn their paths. Qualifying has concluded. The seedings are set. And the five questions that will define the fortnight are already clear.

1. Does Sinner Get Clear Air?

Jannik Sinner is the overwhelming favorite to win Roland Garros 2026. He carries a 29-match winning streak into Paris, has won five consecutive ATP Masters 1000 titles — an unprecedented achievement in the 36-year history of the series — and is seeking the only Grand Slam not yet in his collection.

The key draw question for Sinner is simple: which half does Novak Djokovic land in? Djokovic is seeded No. 3, meaning he is guaranteed to be in a different quarter from Sinner until the semifinals. If they share a half, Sinner could face the three-time Paris champion before the final. If Djokovic is in the opposite half, Sinner's route to the championship match could bypass the most dangerous clay-court tactician in the draw.

Alexander Zverev, the No. 2 seed and 2024 Roland Garros finalist, is placed in the opposite half from Sinner by definition as the second seed. The open question is whether Djokovic joins him there, or whether Sinner must navigate the Serbian's experience and resilience before reaching a final.

2. The Women's Final We All Want to See

Aryna Sabalenka (No. 1) and Coco Gauff (No. 4, defending champion) are guaranteed to be in the same half of the draw — the top and bottom of the top half, or the top and bottom of the bottom half depending on placement. They could only meet in the final if both progress through their respective quarters.

A Sabalenka-Gauff final would be a rematch of the 2025 Roland Garros final, which Gauff won in three sets. If it happens again, it would define the dominant rivalry in women's tennis heading into the grass-court season.

But both paths to that hypothetical final run through Elina Svitolina (No. 7 seed), who is coming off a dominant Rome title in which she defeated the world No. 2, No. 3, and No. 4 in succession over four days. Whoever draws Svitolina in the quarterfinal range faces an immediate threat.

3. The Swiatek Factor

Iga Swiatek is seeded No. 3 and has won Roland Garros four times (2020, 2021, 2022, 2023). She is placed in the opposite half of the draw from Gauff, meaning a Swiatek-Sabalenka final — rather than Gauff-Sabalenka — is the other plausible outcome on the women's side.

Swiatek had a difficult start to 2026, including a virus that forced her withdrawal at Madrid. But her four Roland Garros titles are a reminder that this surface, this tournament, can bring the best out of her even when form elsewhere has been inconsistent.

4. The Open Men's Draw

Carlos Alcaraz, the two-time Roland Garros champion, is absent from the 2026 draw due to a wrist injury — a gap that reshapes the entire men's event. Lorenzo Musetti, who would have received a wild card, also withdrew, with Stan Wawrinka moving into direct main draw entry as a result.

That leaves Sinner as the prohibitive favorite, but the bracket must still be navigated. Felix Auger-Aliassime (No. 4 seed), the in-form Casper Ruud, and Grigor Dimitrov's absence from even the qualifying draw (he withdrew with a thigh injury) all factor into how the men's event takes shape.

5. The Farewell Quarter

Stan Wawrinka, the 2015 Roland Garros champion, will play in the main draw for the final time in his career. Gaël Monfils, who hosts his farewell ceremony on Court Philippe-Chatrier on Thursday evening before the first ball is struck, will also compete in the main draw. David Goffin earned his qualifying spot through three rounds this week.

Somewhere in this draw, a fan favorite will play their final match at Roland Garros. The luck of the draw will determine when and against whom that farewell moment arrives.

What's Next

The Roland Garros 2026 main draw ceremony takes place Thursday, May 21 at 2:00 PM Paris time (8:00 AM ET). The Gaël Monfils farewell event "Gaël & Friends" follows on Court Philippe-Chatrier at 7:30 PM. Main draw play begins Sunday, May 24.
