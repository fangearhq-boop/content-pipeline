### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET David Goffin was in tears on Suzanne-Lenglen after his Roland Garros qualifying opener on Monday — and with good reason. The 35-year-old Belgian, in his final season on tour, secured his 14th main draw appearance at Roland Garros today when qualifying concluded. "Today, everything was there — the level was there, the emotion was there," Goffin said. "Something I've never felt on the court in my career." It is the kind of farewell tour moment that makes tennis worth watching. Goffin, once ranked No. 7 in the world, has fought back from serious knee surgery to earn one last shot at the main draw at the tournament he loves most. He was not alone in the qualifying drama. Bianca Andreescu, the 2019 US Open champion rebuilding her career after years of injury disruption, contested her second qualifying round today against Viktoria Hruncakova. Karolina Pliskova, the former world No. 1, faced Marina Bassols Ribera after rallying from a tough opening set on Tuesday. Sloane Stephens — the 2017 US Open champion — was also in action bidding to return to a Grand Slam main draw. The draw ceremony is tomorrow, Thursday May 21 at 2:00 PM Paris time. For Goffin, the hard part is done. Who are you rooting for in qualifying? Drop a name in the comments. ---

### Long-Form Post Window: 9:00 PM ET Novak Djokovic has played exactly one match on clay in 2026. He lost it — to 20-year-old Dino Prizmic in three sets in the first round of Rome. And yet, he is a threat to win Roland Garros. It sounds absurd. But this is Djokovic — the three-time Paris champion (2016, 2021, 2023), the man with 101 career wins at Roland Garros, the player chasing a record 25th Grand Slam title. He arrived in Paris this week and immediately took to the practice courts with Alexander Zverev. A 57-day shoulder layoff wiped out most of his clay season. He played only three tournaments all year before arriving in Paris. He has confirmed he will not play any further warm-up matches before the draw begins Sunday. But Djokovic at Roland Garros is different. He holds the record for most appearances here (this will be his 22nd). His 101 wins on these courts represent a lifetime of accumulated knowledge. In 2025, he fell in the semifinals to Sinner. This year, as No. 3 seed, the same matchup is possible — just much, much earlier. Can he do it again with almost no clay preparation? Do you think Djokovic can go deep at Roland Garros with minimal match practice? ---

Elina Svitolina has won 20 WTA titles and just defeated Nos. 2, 3, and 4 in the world at Rome. But she has never won a Grand Slam. Coco Gauff is the defending Roland Garros champion. She is the No. 4 seed and the biggest name on the women's tour not named Sabalenka. And Iga Swiatek has won Roland Garros four times. The women's draw — to be revealed Thursday at 2:00 PM Paris time — will answer one crucial question: how close do these paths cross? Sabalenka (No. 1) and Gauff (No. 4) would only meet in the final if both progress. Svitolina (No. 7) could end up in either half. Add Elena Rybakina (No. 2), Marta Kostyuk (No. 15, Madrid champion), and Sorana Cirstea (No. 21) and you have the most genuinely open women's field in years.
