### STORY 1: Medvedev Upset by Walton

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 8:00 AM ET

The biggest upset of Roland Garros 2026 just happened — and it came with one of the best sideline moments of the season.

Australian wildcard Adam Walton, ranked No. 97 in the world, defeated No. 6 seed Daniil Medvedev 6-2, 1-6, 6-1, 1-6, 6-4 in 3 hours and 22 minutes on Court Suzanne-Lenglen. It was Walton's first career victory over a top-10 player, and the biggest result of his career. He said a win over Medvedev in Cincinnati last year gave him the confidence he could do it again in Paris.

But as dramatic as the match was, the moment that went viral was happening in the stands. During the contest, Medvedev engaged in a heated exchange with his wife Daria, who told him bluntly: "Everybody's suffering — you need to behave." Medvedev's reply? "When I start finding the court I'll start to behave." After the match, Medvedev was reflective: "I know I'm in good shape and I know I can play well at Roland Garros. It's just more difficult for me here and the early rounds tend to be tougher."

With Medvedev out, the draw opens up for players like Djokovic and the unseeded contenders still standing. Roland Garros loves its chaos.

Have you ever seen a bigger upset at Roland Garros?

### STORY 2: Sinner Extends Streak to 30

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 10:30 AM ET

Jannik Sinner is in a different dimension right now.

The world No. 1 defeated French wildcard Clément Tabur 6-1, 6-3, 6-4 on Tuesday night at Court Philippe-Chatrier, running his win streak to 30 consecutive matches. That's the fifth-longest streak in ATP Tour history since 1990. Over 2 hours and 8 minutes, Sinner hit 40 winners and — remarkably — did not face a single break point. He did miss three match points while closing out the final game, but ultimately won on his fifth attempt.

What makes this stretch even more staggering is the context: Sinner won five consecutive Masters 1000 titles leading into Roland Garros, something no player in the event's 36-year history had done before. Now he's chasing something even bigger — a Career Grand Slam. Roland Garros is the only major title he's still missing.

His next opponent is Francisco Cerundolo. The mission continues.

Do you think Sinner wins his first Roland Garros title in 2026?

### STORY 3: Gauff + Osaka Day 3

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 10:30 AM ET

Day 3 at Roland Garros delivered everything — results, drama, and one of the most talked-about outfits in recent tennis memory.

Defending champion Coco Gauff opened her title defense with a dominant 6-4, 6-0 win over American Taylor Townsend. Gauff was sharp and relentless, dropping just four games across two sets. She's looking every bit the champion who lifted the trophy here a year ago.

But it was Naomi Osaka who provided the Day 3 moment people will remember. Wearing a glittering gold sequined Nike dress — one she described as inspired by "the Eiffel Tower at night when it's sparkly" — Osaka defeated Germany's Laura Siegemund 6-3, 7-6(3) to advance to the second round. She joked that she worried the reflective dress might get her removed from the court: "When the sun hits the dress, it reflects a lot, so I was a little scared the umpire was going to kick me off the court."

No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka had already advanced in the morning session. All three stars through — in 35°C Paris heat.

Which Day 3 moment were you most excited about — Gauff's dominant win or Osaka's golden moment?

### STORY 5: Osaka Fashion Feature

**Long-Form Post**
**Window:** 3:00 PM ET

There are tennis moments and then there are Naomi Osaka moments — and on Day 3 at Roland Garros, she delivered both.

Osaka walked onto Court Suzanne-Lenglen wearing a black corset and pleated skirt designed by Kevin Germanier. Then she pulled it back to reveal what was underneath: a glittering, gold-sequined Nike dress. As she explained afterward, she had a specific inspiration in mind: "You know the Eiffel Tower at night when it's sparkly? I think I look like that a little bit."

She also had a very practical concern. "When the sun hits the dress, it reflects a lot, so I was a little scared the umpire was going to kick me off the court." No such intervention was required — Osaka went on to beat Germany's Laura Siegemund 6-3, 7-6(3) to advance to the second round.

Even No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka commented on the outfit. Osaka's comeback from a difficult few years is well documented — she was ranked No. 135 at the start of 2025 — and this Roland Garros run is another chapter in that story.
