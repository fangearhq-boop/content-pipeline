Australian Wildcard Adam Walton Stuns Medvedev in Roland Garros 2026's Biggest Upset

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 27, 2026

The biggest upset of Roland Garros 2026 arrived on Day 3, and it came wrapped in five sets of chaos, a sweltering Paris afternoon, and a viral moment from the stands that had the tennis world talking.

Australian wildcard Adam Walton — ranked No. 97 in the world — defeated No. 6 seed Daniil Medvedev 6-2, 1-6, 6-1, 1-6, 6-4 in 3 hours and 22 minutes on Court Suzanne-Lenglen. It was the first Grand Slam main draw victory of Walton's career against a top-10 opponent and, by some distance, the most significant result of his career to date.

A Five-Set Epic in the Paris Heat

The match played out in extreme conditions, with temperatures reaching 35 degrees Celsius (95 degrees Fahrenheit) across Roland Garros on Tuesday. Medvedev, never at his most comfortable on clay, came out flying — winning the second and fourth sets convincingly to level twice — but Walton held his nerve in the decider, breaking at 4-4 before producing a clean hold and then finishing off the Russian in a tight final game.

Walton was composed in assessing his own performance after the win, noting that a victory over Medvedev in Cincinnati last year had planted a seed of confidence that he could repeat the feat on a Grand Slam stage. He did exactly that, and in the most emphatic possible setting.

The Moment That Went Viral

As notable as the result was, it was an exchange in Medvedev's player box that captured the imagination of social media. During the match, Medvedev engaged in a heated back-and-forth with his wife Daria, who had watched him struggle with the conditions and the opponent.

"Everybody's suffering — you need to behave," she told him bluntly from the stands. Medvedev's reply was equally direct: "When I start finding the court I'll start to behave."

The exchange ricocheted across tennis social media within minutes. Former world No. 2 Alex Corretja weighed in, saying the dynamic reflected a relationship healthy enough that Daria was willing to call him out when she felt he needed it.

Medvedev Reflects on Paris Struggles

After the loss, Medvedev was characteristically candid in his post-match assessment. "I know I'm in good shape and I know I can play well at Roland Garros," he said. "It's just more difficult for me here and the early rounds tend to be tougher." He also reflected on how the game has evolved: "Tennis has changed in the last five years. I find it hard to get into the rhythm."

It was Medvedev's seventeenth appearance at Roland Garros and another early exit at a tournament that has historically been his most difficult major. His best run at the clay-court Slam remains a quarterfinal finish.

Draw Implications

With Medvedev's exit, the draw opens further for players in his section. The Russian had been considered a potential spoiler for the bottom half of the men's draw, and his absence could benefit players like Casper Ruud and Alex de Minaur as the tournament progresses into the second week.

For Walton, the victory propels him to the second round of a Grand Slam for the first time at Roland Garros. He will face a significantly more difficult challenge in the next round, but the Australian leaves Day 3 as the most talked-about player in Paris.

What's Next

Adam Walton faces his second-round opponent with the confidence of a player who has already delivered the upset of the tournament. Medvedev returns to the tour having exited early on clay once again, with the grass season now on the horizon. Roland Garros Day 4 continues Wednesday with Djokovic, Swiatek, and Zverev all in action, keeping the tournament firmly in the headlines.
