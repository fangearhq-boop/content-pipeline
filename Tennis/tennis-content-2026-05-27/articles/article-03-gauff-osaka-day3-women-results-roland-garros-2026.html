Gauff Opens Title Defense, Osaka Dazzles in Gold — Roland Garros Day 3 Women's Wrap

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 27, 2026

Day 3 at Roland Garros 2026 produced the women's field at its most compelling — a defending champion opening her title defence with authority, a global icon making a statement in gold sequins, and all three top seeds advancing on a day when Paris baked in 35-degree heat.

Gauff Opens with a Statement

Coco Gauff, the reigning Roland Garros champion and No. 4 seed, had no difficulty against compatriot Taylor Townsend in her opening match. Gauff won 6-4, 6-0, dropping just four games across two sets in a performance that mixed precision baseline hitting with confident net approaches. The first set required some concentration — Townsend is never straightforward — but the second was near-flawless as Gauff imposed her game from the first point.

It was a clean, sharp opening from the American, who successfully defended her title at Roland Garros 12 months ago and arrives in Paris once again as one of the genuine contenders to lift the trophy. She next faces a second-round opponent as her quest for back-to-back titles continues.

Osaka's Golden Moment

If Gauff provided the most dominant performance, Naomi Osaka provided the moment that will define Day 3 in memory.

Osaka walked onto Court Suzanne-Lenglen wearing a black corset and pleated skirt designed by Kevin Germanier. She then pulled it open to reveal what was underneath: a glittering, gold-sequined Nike dress that caught the afternoon light and immediately drew a reaction from everyone in the stadium.

The inspiration, Osaka explained afterward, was unmistakably Parisian. "You know the Eiffel Tower at night when it's sparkly? I think I look like that a little bit," she told reporters. She also admitted to a very practical concern: "When the sun hits the dress, it reflects a lot, so I was a little scared the umpire was going to kick me off the court."

There was no intervention required. Osaka defeated Germany's Laura Siegemund 6-3, 7-6(3) to advance to the second round, a result that combined with her fashion statement to make her the most talked-about player in Paris on Tuesday. Even No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka weighed in publicly on the outfit.

Sabalenka Through in the Morning

No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka had set the tone for the women's day earlier, dispatching Bouzas Maneiro 6-4, 6-2 in the morning session. Sabalenka, who has never won Roland Garros in eight previous appearances and arrived at this tournament as one of the title favourites, was composed and aggressive — exactly the kind of performance her team would have wanted heading into the second round.

Playing Through the Heat

The conditions across all of Day 3 were demanding. Paris reached 35 degrees Celsius (95 degrees Fahrenheit), making it one of the hottest days of the tournament's opening week. Both players and fans felt the intensity of the afternoon sun, and the heat drew its own commentary from several players in post-match interviews.

Medvedev's frustrated exchange with his wife in the stands — "Everybody's suffering — you need to behave" — emerged from the same conditions that had everyone scrambling for shade and ice towels between games.

What's Next

All three top women's seeds — Sabalenka, Osaka, and Gauff — have now advanced to the second round. Day 4 brings four-time champion Iga Swiatek back to Court Philippe-Chatrier for her second-round match, along with No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina on Lenglen and No. 8 seed Mirra Andreeva on Mathieu. The women's draw continues to take shape on what promises to be another full day at Roland Garros.
