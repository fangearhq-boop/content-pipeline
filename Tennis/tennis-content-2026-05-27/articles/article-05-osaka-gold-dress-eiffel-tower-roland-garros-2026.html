Osaka's Eiffel Tower Dress Steals Day 3 at Roland Garros 2026

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | May 27, 2026

Roland Garros has always understood that tennis is theatre as much as sport. On Day 3, Naomi Osaka reminded everyone exactly what that means.

Osaka stepped onto Court Suzanne-Lenglen for her first-round match against Germany's Laura Siegemund wearing a black corset and pleated skirt designed by Kevin Germanier. It was striking enough to turn heads in the stands. Then she pulled the outer layer open to reveal the dress beneath: a glittering, gold-sequined Nike creation that caught the Parisian afternoon light and immediately became the most talked-about outfit of the tournament.

The Eiffel Tower at Night

Osaka was characteristically direct in explaining her thinking afterward. "You know the Eiffel Tower at night when it's sparkly? I think I look like that a little bit," she told reporters, with a smile that suggested she was entirely at peace with the comparison.

She also had a very practical concern about what she had chosen to wear. "When the sun hits the dress, it reflects a lot, so I was a little scared the umpire was going to kick me off the court." No such intervention materialised. The umpire remained in place. The sequins glittered undisturbed.

No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka, who had already advanced earlier in the day session, made a point of publicly commenting on the outfit. The dress became a social media moment in itself, with images circulating widely before Osaka had even finished her post-match interviews.

The Match Itself

Osaka's fashion statement was accompanied by a genuinely solid tennis performance. She defeated Siegemund 6-3, 7-6(3) to advance to the second round, handling the German veteran's flat ball-striking and varied patterns effectively through two sets. The first was largely controlled; the second required greater concentration, with Osaka eventually winning the tiebreak 7-3 to seal the match in two sets.

The conditions were demanding for all players. Paris recorded 35 degrees Celsius (95 degrees Fahrenheit) on Day 3, and the heat affected players throughout the afternoon and evening sessions. Osaka navigated it — in sequins.

The Comeback Story

The context around Osaka's Roland Garros 2026 campaign adds depth to a simple first-round win. After stepping away from the tour for personal reasons and then returning to rebuild, Osaka was ranked No. 135 in the world at the start of 2025 — a number that reflected the disruption to her career rather than her talent. Over the subsequent months, she rebuilt steadily, and her arrival at Roland Garros 2026 as a main-draw participant is itself a story of persistence and professional renewal.

The gold dress is a statement of presence. It says: I am here, I belong here, and I will make you notice me. On Day 3 at Roland Garros 2026, nobody needed much convincing.

What's Next

Naomi Osaka advances to the second round of Roland Garros 2026, where she will face a tougher challenge as the draw tightens. Her campaign — and her wardrobe choices — will continue to draw attention as long as she remains in the tournament. Whether the gold dress returns for another outing, or whether she has another reveal waiting, Roland Garros fans will be watching with interest. The tennis matters. But so does the show.
