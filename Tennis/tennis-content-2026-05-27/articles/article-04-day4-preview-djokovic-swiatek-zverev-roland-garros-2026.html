Roland Garros Day 4 Preview: Djokovic, Swiatek, Rybakina, Zverev Night Session

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 27, 2026

The dust has barely settled on Medvedev's shock exit, and Roland Garros Day 4 is already loading up with marquee matches across all three show courts. Wednesday, May 27 is arguably the richest single day of the tournament's opening week, with three-time Grand Slam champions, a four-time Roland Garros winner, and one of the most intriguing night session matchups of the fortnight all on the schedule.

Philippe-Chatrier: Swiatek, Djokovic, Zverev at Night

The day session on Court Philippe-Chatrier opens with four-time Roland Garros champion Iga Swiatek against Czech Sara Bejlek. Swiatek, who has won the French Open in 2020, 2021, 2022, and 2024, is chasing a fifth title and opened her campaign on Day 2 with a clinical 6-1, 6-2 win over Emerson Jones. Bejlek is a 20-year-old Czech who represents a step up in difficulty, but Swiatek on clay at Roland Garros remains the most formidable proposition in women's tennis.

Three-time champion Novak Djokovic then faces Frenchman Valentin Royer in what promises to be a match with a charged Chatrier atmosphere. Royer advanced through his first round comfortably, defeating Hugo Dellien 6-4, 6-2, 6-2. Djokovic, who beat Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard in four sets on Day 1, arrives in good form and with his sights firmly set on a deep run. The Paris crowd's allegiances will be divided, but Djokovic's experience on this court — he has won 81 matches here — is unmatched.

The night session belongs to No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev against Tomas Machac. Zverev opened his campaign by defeating Benjamin Bonzi 6-3, 6-4, 6-2. Machac, the Czech, beat Zizou Bergs 6-4, 6-4, 6-3 in the first round and represents genuine quality. It is one of the most compelling second-round matchups in the men's draw and could go several ways.

Suzanne-Lenglen: Rybakina, Paolini, Ruud, de Minaur

Court Suzanne-Lenglen carries its own star power on Day 4. No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina — a genuine Roland Garros title favourite — faces Ukraine's Yuliia Starodubtseva in her second round. Rybakina opened with a composed 6-2, 6-2 win over Veronika Erjavec and has been in strong form on clay throughout the spring. 2024 finalist Jasmine Paolini and two-time finalist Casper Ruud are also on Lenglen, adding depth to a court schedule that has no weak matches.

Simonne-Mathieu: Andreeva, Bencic, All-French Men's Match

No. 8 seed Mirra Andreeva leads the program on Court Simonne-Mathieu in her second-round match. The 19-year-old Russian — the youngest player in the WTA top 10 — opened with a dominant 6-3, 6-3 win over Ferro on Day 3 and has been among the best clay-court players in the women's field this spring. Belinda Bencic faces American Caty McNally on the same court, in a match that pits the Olympic gold medallist's returning form against an American on the rise. French players Ugo Humbert and Quentin Halys also play an all-French showdown on Mathieu.

The Draw After Medvedev's Exit

The ripple effect of Medvedev's shock first-round loss on Day 3 runs through Day 4's schedule. With the No. 6 seed gone, the draw in that section has opened considerably, and players like Djokovic and Zverev now face a slightly cleaner path through the second week. The men's half of the draw is taking shape in unexpected ways — Roland Garros 2026 already has the feel of a tournament that will not follow a predictable script.

What's Next

Day 4 results will set the stage for the third round, where the draw begins to thin and quality matchups become unavoidable. Sinner and Gauff have already advanced; by the end of Day 4, the full picture of who has made it to the second round will be clear. Day 5 on Thursday brings the second round of the players who opened on Days 1 and 2, including Ruud, de Minaur, and Svitolina. The tournament is building toward its first major moments of genuine tension.
