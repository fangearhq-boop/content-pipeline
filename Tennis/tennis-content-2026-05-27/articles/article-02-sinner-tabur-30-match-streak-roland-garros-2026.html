Sinner Extends Winning Streak to 30 with Dominant Night Session Win Over Tabur

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | May 27, 2026

Jannik Sinner arrived at Roland Garros 2026 carrying the weight of history and left Tuesday night's opening match with his mission very much intact. The world No. 1 defeated French wildcard Clément Tabur 6-1, 6-3, 6-4 on Court Philippe-Chatrier in 2 hours and 8 minutes, extending his winning streak to 30 consecutive matches — the fifth-longest streak in ATP Tour history since the Open Era record-keeping began in 1990.

The margin of the scoreline slightly flatters Tabur. Sinner was ruthless in the opening set, clinical in the second, and in total command throughout. He hit 40 winners, conceded not a single break point across the 128-minute contest, and allowed the home crowd no moments of genuine hope. He did miss three match points while serving for the match at 5-4, but closed it out on his fifth opportunity.

A Streak Built on Unprecedented Dominance

The 30-match win streak arrives off the back of an extraordinary spring clay swing. Sinner won five consecutive Masters 1000 titles — a feat that had never been achieved in the event's 36-year history. He arrived in Paris having completed the Career Golden Masters (winning all nine Masters 1000 events at least once) and having gone 29-match unbeaten heading into his Roland Garros opener.

His win over Tabur pushed that total to 30, placing him in a select tier of players who have sustained dominance at this level: the fifth-longest streak in ATP Tour history since 1990 includes names like Djokovic, Federer, and Nadal. He is now the only active player with a streak in double figures.

The One Slam Still Missing

Roland Garros represents Sinner's final frontier in the Grand Slams. He has won the Australian Open and the US Open — two of the four majors — and holds no title at Wimbledon or Roland Garros. But his clay-court form this year is historic, and his semifinal run here in 2025 showed he has the game to go deep on the red clay.

His path opens with Francisco Cerundolo as the next opponent. Cerundolo, the Argentine No. 33, is a reliable clay-court competitor, but Sinner — who has conceded no break points and lost only one service game in his opening round — arrives as an overwhelming favourite to progress.

Night Session on Chatrier

Tuesday's match was played under the Chatrier lights in a night session that had the capacity crowd buzzing with anticipation. The Paris crowd offered generous support to Tabur, their wildcard representative, but Sinner's quality left little room for drama. The Italian was unhurried, unflappable, and entirely in control from the opening game.

It was the kind of performance that demonstrates why so many analysts believe Roland Garros 2026 is Sinner's year. With Carlos Alcaraz absent due to injury, Sinner faces the most open men's draw at a French Open in several years.

What's Next

Jannik Sinner faces Francisco Cerundolo in the second round, likely Thursday or Friday. If he wins, his projected path through the draw continues toward potential quarterfinal and semifinal opponents. With Medvedev already eliminated and Alcaraz absent, Sinner has a genuine opportunity to end his Roland Garros wait in 2026. The streak is 30. The mission is clear.
