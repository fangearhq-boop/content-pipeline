Madrid Day 5: Sinner Chases History vs. Moller; Jodar Meets Fonseca in Teen Clash of the Year

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | April 26, 2026

Sunday at the Mutua Madrid Open belongs to the storylines that made this tournament the highlight of the clay swing. In the day session, Jannik Sinner continues his pursuit of the unthinkable — five consecutive Masters 1000 titles — against Danish qualifier Elmer Moller. In the night session, Rafael Jodar and Joao Fonseca, both 19 years old and both born in 2006, meet for the first time in what is the most anticipated match of the week.

For one day, Madrid offers both history in the making and a glimpse of where tennis is heading.

Sinner: Eighteen Wins and Still Counting

Jannik Sinner arrives at his third-round match against Elmer Moller carrying an 18-match overall win streak and 23 consecutive victories at Masters 1000 events. The Masters 1000 run spans titles at the Rolex Paris Masters (October 2025), Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte-Carlo. No player in the history of the Masters 1000 series — which dates to 1990 — has won five consecutive titles.

Sinner is one title away from doing exactly that.

Moller, 22, is a Danish qualifier who has earned his place in the third round through two back-to-back ATP Tour wins. He is not a player expected to push Sinner deep into a match. But Sinner himself has acknowledged that Madrid's high altitude — the Caja Mágica sits at roughly 650 metres above sea level — creates conditions that can disrupt rhythm early in matches. In his second-round win over Benjamin Bonzi, he needed three sets and admitted he "struggled quite a lot" in the opener before taking control.

The key number entering today is 23: Sinner's consecutive Masters 1000 match wins. Every match he plays is an opportunity to extend a record that already places him in the same sentence as the sport's all-time greats.

Jodar vs. Fonseca: The Future of Tennis on One Court

The night session is where Sunday's bigger narrative plays out. Rafael Jodar, a 19-year-old Spaniard from Alcorcón — just 12 kilometres from the Caja Mágica — will face Joao Fonseca of Brazil in the third round. It is the first meeting between the two players on the ATP Tour.

Jodar has had a remarkable clay season. He won the ATP Challenger title in Marrakech, reached the Barcelona semifinal, and then — on Friday — delivered the result of the week by defeating World No. 8 Alex de Minaur 6-3, 6-1 at Madrid. The win gave him his first career top-10 victory and sent the home crowd into a sustained roar that echoed through the Caja Mágica.

Fonseca has his own claim to one of the sport's biggest young talents. The Brazilian, also 19 and also born in 2006, has been climbing the rankings steadily and arrived in Madrid as one of the tour's most exciting prospects. He advanced to the third round after Marin Cilic withdrew due to illness before their second-round match.

The matchup has generated significant buzz. Two players from the same generation, meeting at a Masters 1000 for the first time, both with legitimate aspirations to reach the quarterfinal. The ATP Tour described it as "a maiden meeting in Madrid," and both players are expected to bring full-court aggression that could make this one of the matches of the tournament.

What's Next

Sinner's third-round win, if it arrives today, would set up a potential quarterfinal against one of the remaining top-ten players in the draw. The Jodar vs. Fonseca winner also advances to the quarterfinal stage, where the path to the title runs through proven top contenders.

Madrid's second week is where the Masters 1000 reveals its shape. By Monday, we will know whether Sinner's historic streak continues and which of the two most exciting young players in the world — Jodar or Fonseca — moves another step closer to a Masters 1000 quarterfinal.
