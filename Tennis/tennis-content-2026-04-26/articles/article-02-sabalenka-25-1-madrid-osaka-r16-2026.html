Sabalenka Is 25-1 in 2026: Defending Madrid Champion Dominates Cristian, Osaka Looms in R16

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 26, 2026

While the Madrid draw has produced withdrawals, retirements, and unexpected exits, Aryna Sabalenka has simply kept winning. The world No. 1 defeated Jaqueline Cristian 6-1, 6-4 on Sunday to advance to the round of 16, improving her 2026 record to 25-1 and extending her winning streak to 13 consecutive matches.

She is defending a title she won here last year. She is the highest-ranked player in the draw. And she is looking, through five days of competition, like the player most likely to walk away with the trophy in a week's time.

A Clinical Performance

Cristian, the No. 29 seed, arrived in the third round having shown enough quality to reach this stage. Sabalenka showed her precisely why the gap between a top-five player and the rest of the field exists.

The first set was over in a matter of games. Sabalenka's power from both wings left Cristian scrambling, and a 6-1 set was completed with metronomic efficiency. The second set offered slightly more resistance — Cristian found rhythm in patches — but Sabalenka's service held firm throughout, and she closed out 6-4 without ever looking troubled.

The defending champion has now won 13 straight matches since her last defeat — and at 25-1 for the year, she is operating at a level of consistency that few players in the modern era have sustained across a full season's opening months.

Osaka Waits in the Round of 16

Naomi Osaka advanced to the Madrid R16 in her own right on Sunday, defeating Anhelina Kalinina 6-1, 6-3 in a confident performance that underlined her growing comfort on clay. It is Osaka's first clay event of 2026, and she has navigated the early rounds with more authority than her surface record might have suggested.

The Sabalenka vs. Osaka R16 is the marquee women's match of the week in Madrid. Osaka, a four-time Grand Slam champion, carries the experience of major tennis moments into this match. Sabalenka, the defending champion in ruthless form, carries the better statistics, the surface preference, and the momentum.

Osaka's clay-court history is modest by Grand Slam standards, but she is a player who has consistently shown the ability to produce her best tennis against the biggest opponents. Whether that holds against a 25-1 Sabalenka will be one of the most compelling tactical questions of the tournament's second half.

The Broader Picture: Sabalenka and Roland Garros

Sabalenka's Madrid dominance has implications far beyond this week. Roland Garros — where she has been a perennial contender but never the champion — begins on May 25. With Swiatek now under fitness question marks following her illness retirement on Sunday, and Alcaraz and Djokovic already out on the men's side, the 2026 French Open draw is becoming unusually open.

For Sabalenka, who has won the Australian Open twice and the US Open, Roland Garros remains the missing Grand Slam. A run through Madrid in this kind of form would make her the heavy favourite to finally claim it.

What's Next

Aryna Sabalenka faces Naomi Osaka in the Madrid round of 16, with the match expected to be scheduled for Monday or Tuesday at the Caja Mágica. The winner advances to the quarterfinals in a draw that has opened up considerably following the departures of Swiatek and other seeded players.

Twenty-five wins, one loss, and still counting. Whatever happens next, Sabalenka has already made 2026 look like her year.
