Swiatek Retires from Madrid Open With Illness: Ann Li Advances, Roland Garros Questions Mount

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 26, 2026

Iga Swiatek's Mutua Madrid Open ended in tears on Sunday — not with a loss on the scoreboard, but with a retirement from a gastrointestinal illness that stripped her of everything she needed to compete. American Ann Li wins the third-round match 7-6(4), 2-6, 3-0 ret., and the No. 4 seed exits Madrid for the second time this clay season under difficult circumstances.

What made the moment particularly striking was the context. Swiatek, who won Roland Garros five straight times from 2020 through 2024, has not looked like the indomitable force of those years during the 2026 clay swing. She lost to Mirra Andreeva in the Stuttgart quarterfinals earlier this month. And now this — a retirement on a Sunday afternoon at the Caja Mágica, her body simply refusing to cooperate.

The Match: Swiatek Fought Until She Could Not

The first set was tightly contested. Li played with precision, winning the tiebreak 7-4 after both players exchanged momentum across a competitive opening hour. Swiatek then responded in the second set with her best tennis — landing 100 percent of her first serves in games and winning the set 6-2 to level.

But the third set revealed that something was wrong. Swiatek slowed visibly. At 0-2 down, she called for a medical timeout, received attention from the physio, and returned to the court. When Li held to make it 3-0, Swiatek walked to the net and retired.

Post-match, Swiatek was direct: "I had zero energy and zero stability and just felt really bad physically, and yesterday even worse." She added that a virus appeared to be moving through the tournament venue: "I heard there is something going on between players, that the virus is somewhere on site. I'm sure I'll be fine in a couple of days."

Ann Li Earns a Historic Win

For Ann Li, this is a result worth celebrating. The American, ranked outside the top 60, earns her second career top-10 win in what is one of the bigger moments of her professional career. Advancing to the Madrid round of 16 from this draw, and from this type of match, is a genuine achievement — however the circumstances unfolded.

Li played smart tennis. She did not try to overpower Swiatek but instead stayed consistent, kept the ball in play, and made her opponent work for every point. In the end, the match was decided not by tactics, but by Swiatek's physical collapse. That takes nothing away from Li's quality in the opening set, which she won cleanly in a tiebreak.

What This Means for Roland Garros

The bigger question is what Sunday's retirement means for the French Open, which begins in just over a month. Swiatek's dominance at Roland Garros — five consecutive titles from 2020 to 2024 — made her the sport's gold standard on clay. That era may not be over, but 2026 is raising legitimate questions.

She has now exited two consecutive clay-court events before the quarterfinals. The competition is catching up: Sabalenka is 25-1 on the year, Andreeva is peaking, Gauff remains dangerous, and Rybakina is deep in the Madrid draw. If Swiatek does not recover quickly from this illness and find her best form before Paris, the Roland Garros draw will be genuinely open for the first time in years.

The Spanish clay season was supposed to be her tuning ground. It has instead become a source of concern.

What's Next

Swiatek is expected to rest and recover from the illness before deciding on her preparation schedule ahead of Roland Garros, which begins on May 25. Ann Li advances to the Madrid round of 16, where she will face a top-half opponent in her best Masters 1000 result to date.

The WTA Madrid draw continues to tighten around the players in best form — and heading into the second week, Sabalenka, Andreeva, Gauff, and Rybakina all remain in contention.
