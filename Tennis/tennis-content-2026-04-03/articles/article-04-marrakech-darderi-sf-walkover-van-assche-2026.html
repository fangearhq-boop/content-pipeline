Marrakech QF Day: Darderi Walks Into the Semifinal as Van Assche Earns His Spot

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 3, 2026

At the Grand Prix Hassan II in Marrakech, the defending champion is in the semifinals — and he did not hit a ball to get there.

Darderi's Free Pass

Luciano Darderi entered Friday as the No. 1 seed and defending champion at the Grand Prix Hassan II. He will enter Saturday's semifinal as the freshest player in the draw. Yannick Hanfmann withdrew before their scheduled quarterfinal match, handing Darderi a walkover and a path to the semis without spending a point.

For a defending champion navigating a week of red clay on a body that has to last through the full clay season, Friday's result is close to ideal. Darderi will be rested, focused, and facing an opponent who spent energy on a three-set quarterfinal. In a five-week clay stretch that leads to Roland Garros, managing the physical load is as important as the matches themselves.

Darderi won the Grand Prix Hassan II title in 2025 on this same Moroccan red clay. He knows the courts, the conditions, and the tempo of this specific event. Whatever opponent he faces in the semifinal on Saturday will be dealing with a player in peak condition and playing with the confidence of a defending champion.

Van Assche Earns His Place

The more instructive story from Marrakech this week has been Luca Van Assche. The young French player — No. 3 seed at this event — eliminated No. 2 seed Tallon Griekspoor in a round of 16 match that went the distance: 6-4, 1-6, 6-3. It was the week's clearest upset result on the Moroccan red clay.

Griekspoor is an experienced baseline player who uses aggressive shot-making to disrupt opponents. Eliminating him required Van Assche to handle a mid-match momentum swing — losing the second set after winning the first — and regroup in a decisive third set. That kind of competitive composure, from a young player in a foreign country, is worth noting.

Van Assche entered his quarterfinal against Carabelli as one of the more compelling players left in the draw. The French player has been building quietly toward the kind of results that will eventually force the broader tennis conversation to pay attention. Marrakech 2026 has been a showcase for what his clay-court game is capable of.

The Parallel Clay Season

Marrakech runs alongside Charleston and Houston in the first week of the clay season, its red-clay courts producing a tournament rhythm that is entirely different from the WTA's green-clay event or the American clay of Houston. The Grand Prix Hassan II is one of the older clay-court events on the ATP calendar — the Royal Tennis Club de Marrakech providing a distinctive backdrop to a week of results that tend to set the tone for players heading into Monte Carlo.

For Van Assche specifically, a strong Marrakech result is meaningful context heading into the bigger clay events ahead. Monte Carlo, Madrid, Rome, and Roland Garros form the major tent-poles of the clay season. Players who perform well in the earlier weeks tend to arrive at those events with momentum. Van Assche is building his.

What's Next

The Marrakech semifinals are scheduled for Saturday, April 4. Luciano Darderi — refreshed, motivated, and defending his title — awaits his semifinal opponent from the QF bracket. Van Assche, if he advances past Carabelli, would set up what could be the week's most compelling semifinal matchup.

The Grand Prix Hassan II final is scheduled for Sunday, April 5. While the tennis world's attention will largely shift to Monte Carlo by that point, Marrakech's closing weekend offers genuine stakes for the players competing on Morocco's red clay.
