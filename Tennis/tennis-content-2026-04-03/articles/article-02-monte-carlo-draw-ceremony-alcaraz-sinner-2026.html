Monte Carlo Draw Day: Why the 2026 Bracket Reveal Is the Biggest Moment of the Clay Season

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 3, 2026

The draw bags come out Friday afternoon in Monaco, and the ATP No. 1 race will never be the same after them.

The Asymmetry That Makes This Draw Matter

The Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters draw ceremony takes place Friday, April 3 at 5:00 PM CEST at Monte-Carlo Bay. What makes 2026's draw ceremony the most consequential in recent memory is a simple arithmetic problem: Carlos Alcaraz enters Monaco defending 1,000 ranking points; Jannik Sinner enters defending zero.

Alcaraz won Monte Carlo in 2025. He gets 1,000 points for that title. Sinner missed the 2025 tournament entirely during his doping suspension. He gets nothing for his absence. The bracket draw determines what scenarios become possible — and some of them are dramatic. If Alcaraz loses in the first round, his current points lead over Sinner shrinks to approximately 190 points. A loss before the final could see Sinner walk out of Monaco as the new world No. 1 without winning the tournament himself, depending on how deep Alcaraz goes.

Sinner arrives in Monaco on the form of his life. He won the Indian Wells Open and the Miami Open without dropping a single set — completing what is known as the Sunshine Double. He is the first man to accomplish that specific feat without losing a set since Roger Federer in 2017. That level of form, combined with zero points to lose at Monte Carlo, makes him the most dangerous No. 2 seed the draw has seen in years.

Five Withdrawals Open the Draw

The withdrawal list for Monte Carlo 2026 is longer than any organizer would want. Novak Djokovic (right shoulder), Holger Rune, Taylor Fritz, Jack Draper, and Sebastian Korda have all pulled out before the draw ceremony. Five top players absent before a ball is hit significantly opens the bracket — meaning fewer landmines in the early rounds for the top seeds, but also fewer marquee matchups in the first week of clay.

Their replacements — lucky losers Sebastián Báez, Daniel Altmaier, Térence Atmane, and Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard — fill the spots. The four wild cards complete the field: Moise Kouamé (17, French phenom who made history at Miami), Gaël Monfils (39, Monaco crowd favorite), Matteo Berrettini (29, comeback from a run of injuries), and Stan Wawrinka (42, the 2014 champion still playing at the elite level).

The Tsitsipas Factor

One of the most intriguing elements of Friday's draw is where Stefanos Tsitsipas lands. The Greek player has won the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters three times: 2021, 2022, and 2024. He has spent more time at the top of Monte Carlo's leaderboard than almost anyone in the current draw. He knows the court, the conditions, and the mental demands of this specific event better than any unseeded player in the field.

Entering Monte Carlo 2026 without a seed means Tsitsipas is available in the same quarter of the draw as either Alcaraz or Sinner from the opening round. Whoever draws him in the first round has a genuine problem. His record at this event makes him one of the few players who can beat anyone on this specific red clay.

What's Next

Qualifying at the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters begins Saturday, April 4. Main draw play begins Sunday, April 5, running through the final on Saturday, April 12.

The draw reveal on Friday afternoon determines the path for Alcaraz's title defense, Sinner's zero-to-defend campaign, Tsitsipas's unseeded run, and every other bracket scenario that makes Monte Carlo the most anticipated clay-season opener in years. By the time the balls are drawn from the bag in Monaco, the narrative map for the next two weeks of European clay tennis will be set.
