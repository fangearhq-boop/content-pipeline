Djokovic and Sinner Both Advance to Wimbledon Round of 16 in Dominant Fashion

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 3, 2026 — Wimbledon, London

If anyone needed reminding why Novak Djokovic and Jannik Sinner remain the most dangerous men in tennis, Day 5 at Wimbledon provided a timely answer.

Djokovic dispatched No. 25 seed Arthur Rinderknech 6-3, 6-4, 6-2 on Centre Court in what was close to a flawless performance — 33 winners, just 7 unforced errors, and not a single break point faced across three sets. Sinner, the defending champion, was nearly as efficient against Jenson Brooksby, winning 7-6, 6-1 in 90 minutes. Both men are through to the Round of 16 without having dropped a set today.

Djokovic: Measured Perfection

The Djokovic performance against Rinderknech was the kind that reminds the tennis world why, at 39, he cannot be written off. From the opening game, he controlled the tempo from the baseline, served with precision, and forced errors from Rinderknech with deceptive changes of pace that made the Frenchman look flat-footed throughout.

The statistics told the story bluntly: 33 winners to Rinderknech's 10, 7 unforced errors against his opponent's 24, and a serve that was never broken once across the entire match. Djokovic converted his break points efficiently and closed each set without drama.

The seven-time Wimbledon champion is chasing history at the All England Club, and on this evidence, he is building toward something significant in the second week.

Sinner: A Tiebreak, Then Control

Sinner's path through was slightly less straightforward only in the opening tiebreak, which he took 7-4 after Brooksby had shown why he remains a tricky opponent on grass. Once that first set was secured, however, the No. 1 seed shifted gears entirely, bageling his opponent in a second set that lasted barely more than 30 minutes.

The defending champion has now gone from strength to strength at a tournament he did not play a single warm-up event for before arriving in London. His movement on grass has looked sharp throughout, and the serve that was so reliable in his Wimbledon title run last year is performing at a similar level this week.

The Road Ahead

With Carlos Alcaraz absent through a wrist injury, the men's draw is wide open. Djokovic and Sinner are both in the top half, raising the possibility of a blockbuster semifinal between the two should they each continue their runs through the draw.

Both men have earned a rest day before their Round of 16 encounters this weekend. With the form they showed on Day 5, whichever opponents they face will need to produce something exceptional to stop them.

What's Next

Djokovic and Sinner will play their Round of 16 matches over the weekend. Fourth-round opponents will be confirmed once remaining third-round results are in. Based on Thursday's performances, both men look well-placed to reach the second week in serious contention for a Wimbledon title.
