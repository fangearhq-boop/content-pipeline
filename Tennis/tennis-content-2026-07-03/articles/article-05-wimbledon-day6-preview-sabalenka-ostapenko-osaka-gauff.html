Wimbledon Day 6 Preview: Sabalenka Faces Ostapenko Danger; Osaka, Gauff and FAA All in Action

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 3, 2026 — Wimbledon, London

Friday at Wimbledon shapes up as the most loaded single day of the tournament so far, with the third round delivering a series of genuinely unpredictable matchups. By day's end, the Round of 16 picture will be substantially clearer — and the chaos of the first week may have claimed more victims than expected.

The Match of the Day: Sabalenka vs Ostapenko

No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka faces Jelena Ostapenko in what figures to be the most volatile contest on the women's schedule. The head-to-head favors Sabalenka 3-1, but statistics stop mattering when Ostapenko is in full flight on grass — and Wimbledon is exactly the surface where she is most dangerous.

Ostapenko owns two grass titles. Their most recent meeting, the Stuttgart final in 2025, ended 6-4, 6-1 in Ostapenko's favor — a result that shows how lopsided the power balance can shift when the Latvian is on. They have never met on grass at a Grand Slam before, and that makes this third-round encounter genuinely difficult to call.

Sabalenka has navigated her opening two rounds without issue but has been tested. Ostapenko's flat ball-striking and serve present a different challenge altogether than anything she has faced so far in the draw.

Osaka vs Kasatkina: A Test of Form

No. 14 seed Naomi Osaka meets Daria Kasatkina in a third-round contest that will measure how seriously Osaka can be taken as a Wimbledon contender in 2026. After defeating Elsa Jacquemot in the first round and Anastasia Gasanova in the second, Osaka has looked increasingly comfortable on grass. Kasatkina is an accurate, consistent opponent who makes opponents work for every point.

Gauff and FAA Looking to Advance

No. 7 seed Coco Gauff plays Claire Liu in a matchup that looks favorable on paper for the American, who has been in confident form throughout the draw. Felix Auger-Aliassime (No. 3) faces compatriot Michael Zheng on No. 1 Court in a matchup that tests the Canadian seed's serve-and-volley game against a tricky all-court opponent.

What's Next

Friday's matches will conclude the third round for a large portion of the draw, and by Saturday morning the Round of 16 lineup will begin to crystallize. Saturday sees Swiatek take on Eala in one of the most anticipated third-round matches of the fortnight. With Djokovic and Sinner both through to the fourth round on the men's side, and the women's draw still unpredictable, Wimbledon 2026 is heading into its most compelling phase yet.
