Eala Makes History: First Filipino to Reach Grand Slam Third Round at Wimbledon 2026

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 3, 2026 — Wimbledon, London

Alexandra Eala wrote her name into tennis history on Thursday at the All England Club, defeating Maya Joint 3-6, 6-2, 6-0 to become the first Filipino player ever to reach the third round of a Grand Slam tournament in the Open Era.

The No. 29 seed, just 21 years old, recovered brilliantly from dropping the opening set to dominate the match on Court 3, where hundreds of Filipino fans waved their national flag and created an atmosphere rarely seen outside the tournament's show courts. When the final point was struck, Eala raised her arms and looked directly at her supporters before being engulfed in the moment she had worked so many years to reach.

"Philippines in Wimbledon, it means so much," Eala said after her victory.

The Revenge Storyline That Made It Sweeter

The significance of the opponent added another layer to an already historic day. Maya Joint, the 20-year-old Australian, had beaten Eala in the Eastbourne final just days before Wimbledon began — a painful defeat on the same grass surface. She had then walked onto Centre Court and stunned a returning Serena Williams 6-3, 6-7(6), 6-3 in the first round, making her one of the most talked-about stories of the tournament's opening week.

Eala made her pay. After a nervy 3-6 first set, she raised her level dramatically, hitting through her shots with the kind of controlled aggression that has made her one of the most exciting young players on tour. The second set ended 6-2, and the third was a demolition — 6-0 in favor of Eala, sending her into an historic Round of 32 place.

Her Road to This Moment

Eala first turned heads with a run to the Miami Open semifinals earlier in 2026, which included a shock win over Iga Swiatek. That result made their upcoming third-round Wimbledon encounter — scheduled for Saturday — even more intriguing. The pair are 1-1 in career head-to-head meetings, with Eala winning in Miami and Swiatek responding with victory in Madrid. They have never met on grass.

Entering Wimbledon as a seeded player for the first time at a Grand Slam, Eala has now backed up that status with two confident victories, including a commanding performance against the tournament's early headline-maker in Joint.

What's Next

Saturday's third-round meeting with Swiatek will be the biggest test of Eala's young career. The No. 3 seed has looked dominant this week, dropping just four games in her second-round victory over Karolina Pliskova. Swiatek enters as a heavy favorite — but that was the case in Miami too, and we know how that ended.

Regardless of what happens Saturday, Eala has already secured her place in history. And a nation of tennis fans is watching to see how much further this run can go.
