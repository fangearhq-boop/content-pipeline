Samsonova Stuns No. 15 Shnaider; Women's Day 4 Seeds Mostly Hold at Wimbledon 2026

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 3, 2026 — Wimbledon, London

The biggest women's upset of Day 4 at Wimbledon 2026 came from Liudmila Samsonova, who dismantled 15th seed Diana Shnaider 6-4, 4-6, 6-2 in an impressive display of aggressive baseline tennis that sent one of the draw's more dangerous names to an early exit.

Shnaider had been considered a potential dark horse going into the tournament given her power from the baseline and improving grass form. Samsonova did not give her room to settle — out-hitting her opponent 30-10 in winners and converting 5 of 7 break point opportunities across the three-set battle.

Samsonova's Performance in Numbers

The stat sheet made for compelling reading in Samsonova's favor. Her 30 winners against just 10 for Shnaider represented a dominant attacking performance, and her break point conversion rate (5 of 7, compared to Shnaider's 3 of 9) underlined how efficiently she seized control of the key moments in the match.

Shnaider took the second set to make it competitive, but Samsonova regrouped emphatically in the decider, breaking early and consolidating well to close out the match and advance to the third round.

The Rest of Day 4's Women's Results

Beyond the Samsonova upset, Day 4 followed more predictable patterns in the women's draw:

No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina was in particularly imperious form, defeating Caty McNally 6-1, 6-2 in a straight-sets exhibition of her serve-and-volley power game on grass. Madison Keys was equally efficient against British wildcard Katie Swan, winning 6-1, 6-4.

No. 6 seed Amanda Anisimova was pushed hardest of all the advancing seeds, edging former Australian Open champion Sofia Kenin 6-2, 4-6, 7-6(10-3) in a match that required a final-set tiebreak to settle. Linda Noskova also had a three-set afternoon against Camila Osorio, winning 6-3, 4-6, 6-2.

What's Next

With Shnaider out, the women's bottom half of the draw has opened up somewhat. Samsonova will face a third-round opponent to be confirmed, while Rybakina and Keys set up what could be an intriguing segment of the draw as the tournament moves into its second phase.

Day 5 and Day 6 will begin to clarify the third-round picture, with No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka set to face Jelena Ostapenko in the most volatile-looking match on the women's side of Friday's order of play.
