Swiatek Drops Just Four Games vs Pliskova, Sets Up Showdown with Eala on Saturday

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 3, 2026 — Wimbledon, London

Iga Swiatek served notice to the rest of the women's draw at Wimbledon on Day 4, dismissing Karolina Pliskova 6-1, 6-3 in a commanding display of return-of-serve mastery and clinical point construction.

The No. 3 seed won 63 points in the match to Pliskova's 36, broke serve six times from eight service games, and lost just four games in total across two sets. The opening set took 25 minutes. It was the kind of performance that makes clear Swiatek is not here merely to make up the numbers on a surface she has yet to conquer at Grand Slam level.

Returning Better Than Ever

What made Swiatek's display particularly impressive was the quality of her returning against Pliskova — a player whose serve has troubled opponents for years at the highest level. Swiatek read the delivery early, positioned well, and turned defensive positions into aggressive point construction with a regularity that left the Czech veteran looking helpless.

She was forced to work slightly harder in the second set — the scoreline suggests the set was somewhat closer than the first — but there was never a moment where the outcome felt in doubt. Swiatek was in control from the first game to the last, and wrapped up the match without significant drama.

The Match Everyone Is Watching

Swiatek's reward for advancing is a third-round encounter with Alexandra Eala on Saturday — a matchup that has quickly become the most anticipated contest of the tournament's first week.

Eala, the No. 29 seed, made history on Day 4 by becoming the first Filipino to reach the third round of a Grand Slam, defeating Maya Joint 3-6, 6-2, 6-0 in a performance of growing authority. The pair are 1-1 in career head-to-head meetings: Eala stunned Swiatek in Miami earlier this year, while Swiatek leveled the series with a win in Madrid on clay. They have never played on grass.

The surface, theoretically, should favor Swiatek's precise game and deep returns. But grass form can shift in ways that clay and hard courts do not, and Eala's serve and flat groundstrokes are well-suited to the conditions.

What's Next

Saturday's match between Swiatek and Eala is one of the most compelling in recent Grand Slam memory — a defending champion against a history-making first-timer, with a head-to-head record that says anything can happen. Swiatek enters as the favorite, but Wimbledon has seen bigger upsets than this, and Eala has already shown she is not here merely to participate.

Coverage of the Swiatek-Eala third-round match will be available on ESPN and the BBC. The court assignment and match time will be confirmed Friday evening by tournament organizers.
