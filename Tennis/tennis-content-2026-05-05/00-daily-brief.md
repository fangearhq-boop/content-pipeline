### STORY 2: Sinner Eyes Unprecedented Sixth Consecutive Masters 1000 Title in Rome
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Preview / Historic Pursuit
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Jannik Sinner arrives at the Foro Italico on the back of the most dominant Masters 1000 run in the history of the series: five consecutive titles spanning Paris, Indian Wells, Miami, Monte-Carlo, and Madrid. No player — not Federer, Nadal, or Djokovic — had ever won five in a row before Sinner claimed Madrid on May 4. Now Sinner targets a sixth, which would extend his own record. He enters with a 28-match Masters 1000 winning streak. Sinner's first-round opponent will be American Alex Michelsen or Austrian Sebastian Ofner. He is seeded No. 1 and positioned in the opposite half of the draw from Novak Djokovic. The Foro Italico is Sinner's home crowd — the Italian faithful will be loud behind their champion.

### STORY 3: Djokovic Returns to Tour at Rome — First Match Since Indian Wells
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Return From Injury
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Novak Djokovic will compete in Rome as his first tournament since suffering a right shoulder injury at Indian Wells in late March. The injury forced him to withdraw from the Miami Open, skip Monte-Carlo, and pull out of the Madrid Open — his longest absence from Tour competition since prior injuries. Djokovic's first-round match in Rome will be against Hungarian Marton Fucsovics or a qualifier. He is drawn in the same quarter of the ATP draw as 8th seed and home favourite Lorenzo Musetti, 11th seed Jiri Lehecka, and 23rd seed Casper Ruud. Djokovic has been spotted training in Marbella ahead of Rome, and confirmed his Rome entry after providing an injury update. He has stated his priority is being ready for Roland Garros (qualifying May 18, main draw May 25). Djokovic would be targeting his 25th Grand Slam title at Roland Garros.
