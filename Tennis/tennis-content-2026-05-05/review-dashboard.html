### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM ET The 2026 Italian Open is underway in Rome, and the women's main draw is already loaded with star power. Tuesday marks Day 1 at the Foro Italico, and the eight seeds entering this week read like a who's who of the WTA tour: Aryna Sabalenka, Elena Rybakina, Coco Gauff, Iga Swiatek, Jessica Pegula, Amanda Anisimova, Elina Svitolina, and Mirra Andreeva. That's the depth of this field — the world's top eight, all in the same draw, all chasing the trophy. But the most intriguing storyline may belong to Jasmine Paolini, the Italian defending champion. Paolini enters seeded 9th — a curious position for a player who won here just 12 months ago. She beat Coco Gauff in the 2025 final at this very venue. She's also defending her doubles title alongside Sara Errani. The crowd at the Foro Italico will be loudly behind her. The projected draw gets dramatic fast. Gauff — seeded No. 3 — could face either Andreeva (No. 8) or Paolini (No. 9) in the quarterfinals. Sabalenka, the world No. 1, opens against either Barbora Krejcikova or Elsa Jacquemot. The Italian Open runs through May 17 and is the last major clay event before Roland Garros. Sinner Eyes Unprecedented Sixth Consecutive Masters 1000 Title in Rome
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 6:00 PM ET Here is a number to consider before Jannik Sinner steps on court in Rome this week: 28. That is Sinner's active winning streak at ATP Masters 1000 events. He has not lost a Masters-level match since the 2025 Paris Masters final. In that stretch, he won the Paris Masters, Indian Wells, Miami, Monte-Carlo, and Madrid — five consecutive titles, a record that had never been achieved in the 36-year history of the series. Not Federer, not Nadal, not Djokovic. Now he's in Rome for another attempt. A sixth consecutive title would be unprecedented. His first-round opponent will be either Alex Michelsen or Sebastian Ofner. He is positioned on the opposite side of the draw from Novak Djokovic, and his home crowd at the Foro Italico will be among the loudest in tennis. Sinner is 30-2 on the 2026 season. His ATP ranking sits at 14,350 points — a lead of 1,390 points over the injured and absent Carlos Alcaraz. Rome is the next chapter of a run that is rewriting the record books in real time. What would it take for someone to stop this? ### Image Post Caption **Window:** 6:00 PM ET Jannik Sinner arrives in Rome on a 28-match Masters 1000 winning streak, targeting an unprecedented sixth consecutive title. ### Long-Form Post **Window:** 12:30 PM ET Novak Djokovic is back. After more than six weeks away from Tour competition — his longest absence in recent memory — the 38-year-old confirmed his entry into the Rome ATP draw. The injury: a right shoulder problem that first surfaced at Indian Wells and forced him to withdraw from the Miami Open, skip Monte-Carlo, and pull out of the Madrid Open. Three consecutive Masters events missed. His Rome draw is challenging but navigable. Djokovic opens against Hungarian Marton Fucsovics or a qualifier. He is in the same quarter of the draw as 8th seed Lorenzo Musetti (the Italian crowd's other favourite), 11th seed Jiri Lehecka, and 23rd seed Casper Ruud. Djokovic has been clear about his primary goal. "At least for Roland Garros I'm ready," he told Radiotelevisión Española. Rome is the tune-up. Jannik Sinner, the world No. 1, is in the top half of the draw. His projected path is challenging: Felix Auger-Aliassime, Ben Shelton, and Daniil Medvedev are all in his section. Sinner opens against either Alex Michelsen or Sebastian Ofner. If his form holds — and it has held spectacularly for five straight Masters events — he'll work through those opponents before any likely showdown in the semifinals. Djokovic's path to the final runs through whoever emerges from that section. Notable absences from the draw: Carlos Alcaraz, Taylor Fritz, Jack Draper, and Holger Rune.
