ATP Rome 2026 Draw: Sinner Separated from Djokovic, Zverev and Shelton Head Danger List

By Elena Voss | May 5, 2026

The Internazionali BNL d'Italia draw was announced Monday at the Piazza del Popolo — and the structure sets up a potential Sinner-Djokovic final.

The ATP Rome draw ceremony took place Monday at the historic Piazza del Popolo in the Italian capital, revealing the bracket for the 2026 Internazionali BNL d'Italia. The main draw begins Wednesday May 6 at the Foro Italico, and the structure sets up several compelling storylines — most notably a draw that separates Jannik Sinner and Novak Djokovic until a potential final.

Sinner, the world No. 1, is positioned in the top half of the draw. His first-round opponent will be either American Alex Michelsen or Austrian Sebastian Ofner. From there, his section includes three significant names: Felix Auger-Aliassime, Ben Shelton, and Daniil Medvedev — a trio of former top-five players who are all fully capable of beating anyone on clay on a given day. Getting past all three would require Sinner to sustain the level he has shown over his record five-consecutive-title run.

Djokovic's Quarter

On the opposite side of the draw, Novak Djokovic faces Marton Fucsovics or a qualifier in the first round. Djokovic is making his return from a six-week absence following a right shoulder injury, and this section of the draw gives him a realistic path to building match sharpness without immediately facing a top-five player.

The challenge in Djokovic's quarter comes in the form of three legitimate threats: Lorenzo Musetti, the 8th seed and Italian fan favourite, who has historically performed well on home clay; Jiri Lehecka, the 11th seed, who has developed into one of the more dangerous big servers on clay; and Casper Ruud, the 23rd seed, who has reached multiple Roland Garros finals and is among the most accomplished clay-court specialists in the field. To reach the final from that quarter, Djokovic would likely need to defeat at least one of those three players.

Notable Absences

The Rome draw features several high-profile withdrawals. Carlos Alcaraz, the world No. 2 who had been Sinner's primary rival through the clay swing, withdrew due to the right wrist injury that has also ended his Roland Garros campaign. Taylor Fritz, Jack Draper, and Holger Rune are also absent. Their collective withdrawal thins the field and removes several potential early-round threats for both Sinner and Djokovic.

Arthur Fils, who climbed to No. 17 in the world after his strong Madrid run, is in the field. Fils is one of the most exciting young players on tour and can strike the ball cleanly on clay, making him an opponent that any player in his section will need to take seriously.

What's Next

The ATP Rome main draw begins Wednesday May 6. If both Sinner and Djokovic navigate their respective halves, the draw sets up a final between them on Sunday May 17. For Sinner, that would mean a sixth consecutive Masters 1000 title — an extension of a record that was already unprecedented. For Djokovic, it would be a statement win that confirms his shoulder has healed and his Roland Garros campaign is legitimate. The Rome draw is, for now, the most structurally interesting bracket in men's tennis — and it opens in less than 24 hours.
