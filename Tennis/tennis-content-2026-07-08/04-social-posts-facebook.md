He is 39 years old. He played through an ankle and calf injury that required a medical timeout in the first set. He won a 22-point first-set tiebreak. He and FAA traded set points in the decisive fifth-set tiebreak — each player holding three — before Djokovic won it 7-4 to close out the match.

### STORY 2: Sinner Into SF Without Dropping a Set

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM ET)**

Jannik Sinner is marching through Wimbledon like a defending champion who has decided to send a message.

Tuesday's quarterfinal against Jan-Lennard Struff was a clinic. Sinner won 7-5, 7-6(4), 6-3. He hit 16 aces, converted all three of his break points, and finished with just 16 unforced errors — half of Struff's 29. He has not dropped a single set at the 2026 Wimbledon Championships.

Struff, 36, had been the story of the fortnight before Tuesday — the oldest man in the Open Era to make his first Grand Slam quarterfinal in his 47th attempt, having upset No. 8 seed Medvedev to get there. That story ended on No. 1 Court in straight sets.

It is a reminder of what Sinner is: not a player who creates chaos, but a player who methodically takes it away. He gives opponents nothing, converts what he gets, and controls rallies until the result is inevitable.

On Friday, the defending champion gets Novak Djokovic — who just came off five hours on Centre Court in the longest quarterfinal in Wimbledon history.

Does Sinner's freshness give him an edge? Or does Djokovic's momentum outweigh it?

### STORY 3: Women's Day 9 — Gauff First Wimbledon SF; Muchova Beats Osaka Again

**Long-Form Post (9:00 AM ET)**

Two major stories emerged from the women's quarterfinals on Day 9.

Coco Gauff defeated Jessica Pegula 4-6, 6-3, 6-3 in 1 hour and 48 minutes to reach the Wimbledon semifinals for the first time in her career. She had not won a match on grass in the two years leading up to this tournament. After losing the first set, she was perfect 5-for-5 on break points and won 75% of points in the nine-shot-plus rallies that defined the third set. "Gosh, honestly, pretty insane," she said afterward. "I have found the formula to succeed on grass."

Gauff, 22, is now the youngest woman to reach the semifinals at all four Grand Slams since Maria Sharapova completed the set in 2007.

In the other quarterfinal, Karolina Muchova defeated Naomi Osaka 7-6(4), 6-4. It was a rematch of the Bad Homburg final just 10 days earlier — a match Osaka had led 6-1, 1-0 in before withdrawing with injury. Muchova won it properly this time, sealing back-to-back aces to close the match. With the win, she becomes the fourth Czech woman in the Open Era to reach the semifinals at all four Grand Slams.

Osaka, whose run included a stunning upset of world No. 1 Sabalenka, left deflated: "I just feel like I didn't play well at all and I didn't have any energy. My legs felt disconnected."

Women's final four: Gauff vs Muchova. The other half of the draw (Paolini/Kostyuk, Noskova/Mertens) plays out today.

Who do you think wins the Gauff-Muchova semifinal?
