Wimbledon Day 10: Fery Gets Centre Court, Fritz Faces Zverev, Women's Draw Completes Its Quarterfinals

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | July 8, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Four quarterfinal matches remain at Wimbledon 2026, and they are all on Wednesday. The scheduling decision that generated the most attention before a ball was struck: Arthur Fery, the 23-year-old British wildcard who grew up five minutes from the All England Club, has been given Centre Court for his quarterfinal against No. 9 Flavio Cobolli. The allocation means No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev — and his match against No. 7 Taylor Fritz — goes to Court 1.

The Wimbledon grass looks at home crowds, and home crowds look at Arthur Fery right now.

Fery vs Cobolli: The Wild Card and the Repeat Quarterfinalist

Fery arrived at this quarterfinal having done something no British wildcard had done in the professional era: reached the last eight of a Grand Slam. His win over No. 28 Grigor Dimitrov in five sets last week was one of the defining moments of the fortnight. He is the crowd choice for any match he plays at the All England Club, and Wednesday on Centre Court gives him the largest stage his career has ever produced.

Cobolli is in his second consecutive Wimbledon quarterfinal — a level of consistency on grass that his No. 9 seeding reflects. There is one note from their prior meeting worth watching: Fery defeated Cobolli at the 2026 Australian Open in straight sets, the only professional meeting between them before Wednesday. Fery knows Cobolli's game and has beaten it on a fast surface.

Fritz vs Zverev: The Head-to-Head That Has Swung One Direction

Taylor Fritz has won 10 of 15 career meetings against Alexander Zverev, with seven consecutive victories — including a grass-court match at Halle just weeks before Wimbledon. The streak includes multiple surfaces and competition levels, and it includes recent form on the exact surface they will play Wednesday.

Zverev's position here requires context. He is the 2026 French Open champion and No. 2 seed at this tournament. He is in his first-ever Wimbledon quarterfinal, having completed his fourth-round match against Jiri Lehecka — which had been suspended overnight — on Tuesday morning. His career has been defined by clay and hard-court success; the grass has historically been a different proposition.

Fritz, who reached the Wimbledon semifinals last year, needs one more win to repeat that. The head-to-head, the recent Halle result, and the surface history all point in Fritz's direction. Whether Zverev can disrupt that narrative is the central question of their Wednesday afternoon.

Women's Quarterfinals: Paolini, Kostyuk, Noskova, and Mertens

The two women's quarterfinals that remain on Wednesday send their winners into Thursday's semifinals against Coco Gauff and Karolina Muchova.

No. 13 Jasmine Paolini was the 2024 Wimbledon finalist. She knows this draw, knows this surface, and knows what it takes to navigate deep into a Wimbledon fortnight. Her opponent, No. 12 Marta Kostyuk, arrived at Wimbledon carrying a 20-of-21 match winning streak and making her first quarterfinal appearance at the All England Club. The win streak is one of the tour's longest active runs.

No. 9 Linda Noskova faces No. 25 Elise Mertens in a match where both players are experiencing their first Wimbledon quarterfinals. Noskova reached this point by defeating No. 26 Madison Keys 6-4, 7-6(2) in the round of 16. Mertens beat Marie Bouzkova 6-4, 6-4 in the same round. Both earned their places here without an easy run.

What's Next

Wednesday's four matches will complete the quarterfinal draw and set the full semifinal schedule for Thursday (women) and Friday (men). The men's confirmed semifinal — Sinner vs Djokovic on Friday — is already set. The two Day 10 men's winners play the other men's semifinal on Friday. Thursday's women's draw will be completed once today's matches conclude.
