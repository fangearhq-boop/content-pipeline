Gauff Reaches First Wimbledon Semifinal; Muchova Completes Grand Slam Set With Win Over Osaka

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | July 8, 2026

Wimbledon, London

Two major women's stories came out of Day 9 at Wimbledon, and together they set up a semifinal that no one would have scripted before the fortnight began.

Coco Gauff defeated Jessica Pegula 4-6, 6-3, 6-3 in 1 hour and 48 minutes to reach the Wimbledon semifinals for the first time in her career. Karolina Muchova defeated Naomi Osaka 7-6(4), 6-4 to reach hers for the first time as well. Gauff vs Muchova is the first of Thursday's two women's semifinals.

Gauff: A Formula Found on Grass

The context around Gauff's win matters. Before Wimbledon 2026, she had not won a match on grass in two years. The surface had been the one gap in a game that had reached Grand Slam semifinals at the Australian Open, Roland Garros, and the US Open. Tuesday filled that gap.

After losing the first set to Pegula — who controlled the opener with consistent baseline depth — Gauff reset. She was perfect on break points in the match: 5 for 5. In the deciding third set, she won 75% of points in rallies of nine shots or more, the extended exchanges where Pegula had expected to have the advantage. The final two sets were not close.

"Gosh, honestly, pretty insane," Gauff said afterward. "Considering how I hadn't won a match on grass in two years before this tournament, so I'm definitely just really happy with how I played today." She added: "I have found the formula to succeed on grass."

Gauff, 22, is now the youngest woman to reach the semifinals at all four Grand Slams since Maria Sharapova completed the set in 2007. That is the historical company she now keeps on the tour.

Muchova: A Set Complete, a Dream Fulfilled

The Muchova-Osaka match had a prequel. Ten days earlier at the Bad Homburg Open final, Osaka had led 6-1, 1-0 before withdrawing with an injury. Muchova had won that one by default. On Tuesday, she won it in full.

The opening set went the distance, reaching a tiebreak that Muchova took 7-4. In the second, she had the decisive moment: Osaka was serving at 4-4, came apart under pressure, and missed wildly on a break point to give Muchova the chance to serve for the match. Muchova converted it at love, sealing the win with back-to-back aces. She won 84 total points to Osaka's 69.

"It's unbelievable," Muchova said afterward. "I am super happy with today's win." The win makes Muchova the fourth Czech woman in the Open Era to reach the semifinals at all four Grand Slams — a mark that stands as one of the rarest in the women's game.

Osaka, whose run had included a stunning upset of world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka in the fourth round, was candid about the difference between Tuesday and her earlier matches: "I just feel like I didn't play well at all and I didn't have any energy. My legs felt disconnected from the upper half."

What's Next

Gauff and Muchova meet in the first women's semifinal on Thursday. The other half of the women's draw — Paolini vs Kostyuk and Noskova vs Mertens — plays out Wednesday, with those winners joining Thursday's action. What is already confirmed: Wimbledon 2026 will crown a first-time women's champion. Every remaining player in the draw is seeking her first title at the All England Club.
