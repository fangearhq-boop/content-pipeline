### Long-Form Post FIVE HOURS AND FIFTEEN MINUTES. That is how long Novak Djokovic was on Centre Court at Wimbledon on Tuesday, defeating No. 3 Felix Auger-Aliassime 7-6(10), 3-6, 6-3, 6-7(4), 7-6(4) in the longest quarterfinal in the history of the Championships. He is 39 years old. He played through an ankle and calf injury that required a medical timeout in the first set. He won a 22-point first-set tiebreak. He and FAA traded set points in the decisive fifth-set tiebreak — each player holding three — before Djokovic won it 7-4 to close out the match. The win gives Djokovic a record eighth consecutive Wimbledon semifinal, moving past Roger Federer for the most consecutive men's singles SF appearances at the All England Club. He is also the second-oldest man in the Open Era to reach a Wimbledon semifinal, behind only Ken Rosewall in 1974. "I was telling the kids to go to sleep after the fourth but they didn't want to listen and I am glad they stayed," Djokovic said afterward. "It was honestly one of the best matches I have been part of on this court in my career." Up next on Friday: Jannik Sinner. The defending champion. The man who defeated Djokovic in straight sets at this exact stage last year before lifting the trophy. What did you think of that epic? ### Image Post Caption Novak Djokovic. 39 years old. Five hours and 15 minutes. Playing through an ankle injury. Record eighth consecutive Wimbledon semifinal. The game does not want to let him go — and he does not want to let it go either.

### Long-Form Post Jannik Sinner is marching through Wimbledon like a defending champion who has decided to send a message. Tuesday's quarterfinal against Jan-Lennard Struff was a clinic. Sinner won 7-5, 7-6(4), 6-3. He hit 16 aces, converted all three of his break points, and finished with just 16 unforced errors — half of Struff's 29. He has not dropped a single set at the 2026 Wimbledon Championships. Struff, 36, had been the story of the fortnight before Tuesday — the oldest man in the Open Era to make his first Grand Slam quarterfinal in his 47th attempt, having upset No. 8 seed Medvedev to get there. That story ended on No. 1 Court in straight sets. It is a reminder of what Sinner is: not a player who creates chaos, but a player who methodically takes it away. He gives opponents nothing, converts what he gets, and controls rallies until the result is inevitable. On Friday, the defending champion gets Novak Djokovic — who just came off five hours on Centre Court in the longest quarterfinal in Wimbledon history. Does Sinner's freshness give him an edge? Or does Djokovic's momentum outweigh it? ### Image Post Caption Jannik Sinner — 16 aces, 3/3 break points, 16 unforced errors — ends Struff's fairy tale run. Not a set dropped at Wimbledon 2026. Women's Day 9 — Gauff First Wimbledon SF; Muchova Beats Osaka Again
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Two major stories emerged from the women's quarterfinals on Day 9. Coco Gauff defeated Jessica Pegula 4-6, 6-3, 6-3 in 1 hour and 48 minutes to reach the Wimbledon semifinals for the first time in her career. She had not won a match on grass in the two years leading up to this tournament. After losing the first set, she was perfect 5-for-5 on break points and won 75% of points in the nine-shot-plus rallies that defined the third set. "Gosh, honestly, pretty insane," she said afterward. "I have found the formula to succeed on grass." Gauff, 22, is now the youngest woman to reach the semifinals at all four Grand Slams since Maria Sharapova completed the set in 2007. In the other quarterfinal, Karolina Muchova defeated Naomi Osaka 7-6(4), 6-4. It was a rematch of the Bad Homburg final just 10 days earlier — a match Osaka had led 6-1, 1-0 in before withdrawing with injury. Muchova won it properly this time, sealing back-to-back aces to close the match. With the win, she becomes the fourth Czech woman in the Open Era to reach the semifinals at all four Grand Slams. Osaka, whose run included a stunning upset of world No. 1 Sabalenka, left deflated: "I just feel like I didn't play well at all and I didn't have any energy. My legs felt disconnected." Women's final four: Gauff vs Muchova. The other half of the draw (Paolini/Kostyuk, Noskova/Mertens) plays out today. Who do you think wins the Gauff-Muchova semifinal? ### Image Post Caption Coco Gauff: first Wimbledon SF. Karolina Muchova: Grand Slam SF set complete. Two remarkable women's stories from Day 9 at the All England Club.

### Long-Form Post Wimbledon's quarterfinal week is not done. Four more matches are on the card today (Wednesday, July 8), and the headliner is on Centre Court: No. 9 Flavio Cobolli vs. Arthur Fery, the 23-year-old British wildcard who grew up five minutes from the All England Club. Fery's popularity with the crowd was so strong that he was given Centre Court for the quarterfinal — a move that has "sentenced" No. 2 Alexander Zverev to Court 1 for his match against Taylor Fritz. That match, Fritz (No. 7) vs Zverev (No. 2), has its own storyline. Fritz leads the head-to-head 10-5 overall and has won seven consecutive meetings — including a grass-court match at Halle just weeks ago. Zverev, the 2026 French Open champion, is in his first-ever Wimbledon quarterfinal. One of them reaches a Wimbledon semifinal for the first time. On the women's side: No. 13 Jasmine Paolini — the 2024 Wimbledon finalist — takes on No. 12 Marta Kostyuk, who arrived here on a 20-of-21 winning streak. And No. 9 Linda Noskova faces No. 25 Elise Mertens, both making their first-ever Wimbledon quarterfinals. The winners today determine who plays Gauff and Muchova in Thursday's women's semifinals. ### Long-Form Post It is happening again. Jannik Sinner vs Novak Djokovic. Wimbledon. Semifinals. Friday, July 10, Centre Court. This is the second consecutive year — and the third time in four years — that these two have met at this stage of this tournament. In last year's semifinal, Sinner defeated Djokovic in straight sets before going on to win the title. Sinner comes into this match as the defending champion, having not dropped a single set in the entire 2026 Wimbledon draw. Djokovic comes in with something else: five hours and 15 minutes of combat in Tuesday's quarterfinal against FAA, a win that saw him play through an ankle injury, win the longest QF in Wimbledon history, and set a new record for consecutive men's singles semifinal appearances at the All England Club. Eight in a row. Sinner is 24. Djokovic is 39. One has the freshness and momentum of a dominant title defense. The other has the experience of 15 Wimbledon semifinals and the hunger of a man who was beaten here by this same opponent last year. There will be no easy answers on Centre Court on Friday. Same draw, same stage, different year — and Djokovic gets to try to flip the script. Who wins? ### Image Post Caption Sinner vs Djokovic at Wimbledon — for the second year in a row.
