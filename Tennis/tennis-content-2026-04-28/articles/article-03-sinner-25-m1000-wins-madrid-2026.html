Sinner Beats Norrie for 25th Consecutive Masters 1000 Win, Eyes Historic 5th Straight M1000 Title

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | April 28, 2026

Tuesday morning at the Caja Mágica belonged to Jannik Sinner, as it has every day he has shown up at a Masters 1000 event since last November. The world No. 1 defeated Cameron Norrie 6-2, 7-5 in 87 minutes in the Madrid Open round of 16 — a first career meeting between the two, decided with the efficiency that has become Sinner's calling card at this level.

The win extended his Masters 1000 winning streak to 25 consecutive matches. The last time Sinner lost at a Masters 1000 event was at Paris Bercy in November 2025. Since then he has won Paris-Bercy, Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte Carlo. He is in Madrid seeking to become the first player in the Open Era to win five consecutive Masters 1000 titles.

A Dominant Performance

The match was not complicated. Norrie, Britain's No. 1 and one of the more consistent clay-court competitors on the tour, could not find a way through Sinner's defensive wall or break down his serve. Sinner converted 4 of 9 break points — a slightly lower rate than his best tennis, but entirely sufficient against an opponent who created limited chances in return.

The first set was comprehensive: 6-2 in rapid time. The second set offered slightly more resistance, with the score reaching 5-5 before Sinner broke to lead 6-5 and served it out. The 87-minute match time tells the full story. Sinner was never in difficulty.

It is worth pausing on what the streak means. Twenty-five consecutive Masters 1000 wins is not a statistic that accumulates by accident. It spans five different tournaments, different surfaces and conditions, opponents ranging from qualifiers to top-5 players. Sinner has beaten everyone in front of him at this level for six months. There is no obvious candidate to stop him.

The Quarterfinal That Madrid Wants

Sinner's quarterfinal opponent will be determined by Tuesday's later matches. The possibility that has captured this city's attention is a match against Rafael Jodar — the 19-year-old from Alcorcón, 12 kilometres from the Caja Mágica, who has beaten No. 8 Alex de Minaur and João Fonseca en route to the round of 16.

Jodar plays Vit Kopriva in the fourth round on Tuesday afternoon. A Jodar win sets up the quarterfinal clash the tournament has been building toward: the world No. 1, chasing history, against the hometown teenager who has carried Madrid's hopes all week. It would be one of the most anticipated matches of the clay season.

Even if Kopriva advances, Sinner remains the clear favourite to reach the semifinals. He has not shown a vulnerability this tournament — or, for that matter, in the last six months. What the quarterfinal becomes, exactly, depends on what happens at 4 PM on Manolo Santana Court.

What's Next

Sinner advances to the Madrid quarterfinals, where he awaits either Rafael Jodar or Vit Kopriva. A win there puts him in the semifinals, and three wins from now stands the fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title — a piece of history that has never been done. The numbers around Sinner's season keep demanding attention. For now, he keeps winning.
