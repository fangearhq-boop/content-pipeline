Lucky Loser Potapova Stuns Rybakina, Noskova Beats Gauff — Madrid's Wild Night of Upsets

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 28, 2026

Anastasia Potapova did not qualify for the Mutua Madrid Open main draw. She arrived at the Caja Mágica as a lucky loser — a player inserted into the bracket because someone else withdrew. On Monday night, she walked off court having beaten No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina 7-6(8), 6-4 in 1 hour and 53 minutes. She is now in the quarterfinals. In the tournament's 17-year history, no lucky loser had ever gotten this far.

That was the headline result of a chaotic Monday at Madrid. The secondary headline was almost as surprising: Linda Noskova defeating Coco Gauff 6-4, 1-6, 7-6(5) in a match that Gauff appeared to have firmly under control deep into the deciding set. The WTA draw, already missing Iga Swiatek after her illness retirement, lost two more of its biggest names on the same night.

Potapova Makes History

What Potapova did against Rybakina carries genuine historical weight. Rybakina had won the Stuttgart title — the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix — just six days earlier. She arrived in Madrid as one of the favorites to challenge Sabalenka for the title. Potapova, by contrast, was not even supposed to be in the main draw.

The match was tight throughout. It went to a first-set tiebreak that stretched to 8-8 before Potapova prevailed. In the second set she converted the decisive break and served it out. Her 7-6(8), 6-4 win was her fourth career victory over a top-5 opponent — a number that tells you she has the level to compete at this altitude, even if tournaments overlook her.

In the quarterfinals she faces Karolina Pliskova, who beat Solana Sierra to become, per published reports, the lowest-ranked quarterfinalist in the history of the Madrid Open. A lucky loser versus a record-low-ranked quarterfinalist: the WTA draw in 2026 has rarely produced a stranger quarterfinal.

Noskova's Improbable Comeback

The Noskova-Gauff match followed a different script but arrived at a similarly dramatic conclusion. Gauff won the second set emphatically after losing the first, and in the third set led 4-1. With Noskova appearing to run out of answers, the match seemed settled.

It was not. Noskova broke back and levelled the set, forcing a tiebreak. Gauff led 3-0 in the breaker — one more point toward almost certain victory. Noskova won the next seven of nine points and sealed it. Final: 6-4, 1-6, 7-6(5).

Gauff, it should be said, was not at full health. She had been battling the same stomach virus that forced Swiatek's retirement the previous day — vomiting courtside during her round-of-16 win over Cirstea just 24 hours earlier had already been noted. Playing through illness at this level is something she deserves credit for. But Noskova found a way regardless.

"The match is not over until it's over," Noskova said afterward. The week in Madrid has provided more than enough evidence for that sentiment.

What's Next

Potapova advances to the Madrid quarterfinals — a first for any lucky loser at this tournament. She faces Karolina Pliskova. Noskova's run ends in the round of 16; she exits Madrid as one of the tournament's more compelling stories. Rybakina and Gauff are both out, and the women's draw has opened up considerably.

The remaining quarterfinalists are Sabalenka, Baptiste, Andreeva, Fernandez, Potapova, and Pliskova. Three of the four seeds who were expected to be here — Swiatek, Rybakina, and Gauff — are gone. The quarterfinals begin Tuesday.
