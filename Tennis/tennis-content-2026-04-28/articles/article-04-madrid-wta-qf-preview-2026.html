Madrid WTA Quarterfinals Set: Sabalenka, Andreeva, and Two of Tennis's Most Unlikely Quarterfinalists

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 28, 2026

The Madrid Open women's draw has been turned on its head. Iga Swiatek retired ill in the round of 16. Elena Rybakina, who won Stuttgart less than a week ago, was knocked out by a lucky loser in a midnight upset. Coco Gauff, fighting through the same stomach virus that felled Swiatek, lost a tiebreak she led 3-0. Three of the players most expected to challenge for the title are gone.

What remains is four quarterfinal matchups, each carrying its own extraordinary backstory — and a draw that looks genuinely, unusually open.

Sabalenka vs Baptiste

Aryna Sabalenka, the defending Madrid champion, is still standing. She came from a set and a break down to beat Naomi Osaka 6-7(1), 6-3, 6-2 on Monday — her 150th WTA 1000 match win. She is the heavy favourite in the quarterfinals and carries both the strongest record in 2026 and the title-holder's advantage.

Her opponent, No. 32 seed Hailey Baptiste, got here through a remarkable second-round win over Belinda Bencic. The match went to a 16-14 tiebreak in the second set — the longest of the 2026 season — and Baptiste saved six of Bencic's match points. She and Sabalenka met in Miami earlier this year, where Sabalenka won 6-4, 6-4. Clay will not necessarily change the outcome, but it changes the conditions.

Andreeva vs Fernandez

Mirra Andreeva is 18 years old and playing the best clay of her career. She upset Swiatek in the Stuttgart quarterfinals. She won the Linz title. Now she has reached the Madrid quarterfinals after beating Anna Bondar — coming from behind in a match she was nearly out of — and she has not dropped a set in Madrid's main draw.

Leylah Fernandez is an accomplished clay player with experience in big moments. Their meeting in the quarterfinals is the match that could produce the most interesting tennis of the week. Andreeva's groundstrokes from the back of the court are already among the best on tour. Whether her 18-year-old composure holds in a quarterfinal setting at a Masters 1000 is the question.

Potapova vs Pliskova

This quarterfinal will be remembered regardless of outcome. Anastasia Potapova entered the tournament as a lucky loser — she was not in the original main draw. She is now in the last eight, having beaten No. 2 seed Rybakina in a 7-6(8), 6-4 result that stands as one of the bigger upsets of the clay season. It is the first time a lucky loser has reached the Madrid Open quarterfinals since the tournament began in 2009.

On the other side of the net will be Karolina Pliskova, who is, according to tournament records, the lowest-ranked player to have ever reached the Madrid quarterfinals. When two players arrive in a WTA 1000 quarterfinal with this level of improbability attached to their being there, the match tends to carry a charge.

What's Next

The quarterfinals are scheduled for Tuesday (Sabalenka and Andreeva) and Wednesday. Semifinals follow on Thursday, with the final on Saturday. Sabalenka enters as the clear favourite to win the tournament, but three of the four quarterfinalists on the other side of the draw arrived here in ways no one predicted. Madrid's draw has a habit of delivering surprises. There is every reason to expect more.
