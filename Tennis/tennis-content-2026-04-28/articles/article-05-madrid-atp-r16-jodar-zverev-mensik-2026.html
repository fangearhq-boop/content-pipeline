Madrid Day 8: Jodar Eyes Sinner QF, Zverev vs Mensik Headlines the Night Session

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 28, 2026

While the WTA quarterfinals get underway on Tuesday at the Mutua Madrid Open, the ATP round of 16 takes over the Caja Mágica schedule — and it has delivered one of the week's most anticipated slates. Four matches, all capable of producing their own headline. By the time the night session ends, the ATP quarterfinal draw will be fully set.

Jodar vs Kopriva: The Match Madrid is Watching

The most anticipated moment of the day arrives at 4 PM on Manolo Santana Court: Rafael Jodar versus Vit Kopriva in the round of 16. Jodar is 19 years old, born in Alcorcón — 12 kilometres from this stadium. He has beaten No. 8 Alex de Minaur 6-3, 6-1 in the second round, then defeated João Fonseca 7-6(4), 4-6, 6-1 in the third. He has won nine consecutive matches on clay coming into Tuesday's tie.

A Jodar win advances him to the quarterfinals — and sets up a meeting with world No. 1 Jannik Sinner. Sinner won his round of 16 match Tuesday morning, 6-2, 7-5 over Cameron Norrie. A Sinner-Jodar quarterfinal — the world No. 1 on a 25-match M1000 winning streak versus the 19-year-old hometown hero — would be the match this tournament has been building toward all week.

Kopriva, a Czech player seeded in the round of 16 via his ranking, is not to be dismissed. He reached this stage on merit. But the crowd, the draw, and the moment are all pointing in one direction.

Fils vs Etcheverry, Musetti vs Lehecka

Arthur Fils arrives in the round of 16 carrying momentum from last week's Barcelona title — his second ATP 500 victory on clay this spring. He faces Tomas Etcheverry, the tall Argentine who has been building quietly through the draw. Their head-to-head is limited; this is a match where surface experience and current form will tell more than records do.

Lorenzo Musetti against Jiří Lehečka is the other R16 tie to watch closely. Musetti has been one of the most watchable players on clay this spring — his one-handed backhand on red clay is among the most aesthetically striking shots on tour. Lehečka is a difficult opponent on any surface, tall and aggressive, with the kind of serving that makes clay matches shorter than expected. Their head-to-head coming in slightly favours Musetti.

Night Session: Zverev vs Mensik

No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev closes Tuesday's program against Jakub Mensik. It is their first meeting on the ATP Tour. Zverev is an eight-time Masters 1000 champion, seeded second here, and widely seen as the most likely player to challenge Sinner in the last stretch of the tournament. He has reached the Madrid round of 16 nine times. He has never won the title.

Mensik represents genuine danger. The Czech player reached the ATP Finals in 2025, defeating some of the biggest names on tour in the process. He is young, aggressive, and capable of raising his level on a big occasion. Zverev's height and power should give him an advantage on clay, but Mensik has already shown this season that on-paper advantages do not always survive contact.

The match was scheduled as the final event of Tuesday's night session at the Caja Mágica. By the time it is complete, the ATP quarterfinal draw will be set, the bracket will be clear, and Madrid's final four days will have a defined shape.

What's Next

The ATP quarterfinals are scheduled for Wednesday and Thursday. Sinner is already in, waiting on either Jodar or Kopriva. The rest of the quarter-final picture gets filled in Tuesday evening. This is where Madrid's second week begins in earnest — and where the tournament's real story starts to take shape.
