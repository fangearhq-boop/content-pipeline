Sabalenka Survives Osaka in Madrid Thriller — 150th WTA 1000 Win
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Aryna Sabalenka and Naomi Osaka had played 12 times before Monday night. Every single match was on a hard court. Sabalenka won every single one. When they finally met on clay in Madrid's round of 16, the evidence said another routine win. For one full set, it was anything but. Osaka dominated the first-set tiebreak, racing to 5-0 before closing it. She then broke Sabalenka early in the second. At a set and a break down, Sabalenka was in real trouble — the defending champion, the world No. 1, being outplayed on the surface she had just arrived on. What followed was 12 straight games for Sabalenka. She won the second set 6-3. She won the third 6-2, closing it with an ace on match point. Two hours and 20 minutes. Final: 6-7(1), 6-3, 6-2. It was her 150th WTA 1000 match win and her 17th consecutive WTA quarterfinal. She is the defending Madrid champion, and she is very much still in this tournament. Her quarterfinal opponent is Hailey Baptiste, who beat Belinda Bencic through a 16-14 tiebreak — the longest of the 2026 season — after saving six of Bencic's match points. A different kind of opponent from Osaka, and a match that will be played on clay with everything on the line. Who do you think wins the Madrid title this week? --- #### Image Caption -- 8:00 AM ET ### Image Post Caption 150th WTA 1000 match win. 17th consecutive quarterfinal. ### Long-Form Post Monday night in Madrid did not go according to plan. Elena Rybakina — fresh off winning Stuttgart the previous week — walked off the Caja Mágica court after losing 7-6(8), 6-4 to Anastasia Potapova. The result was striking on its own. The context makes it historic: Potapova entered the tournament as a lucky loser. She was not in the original main draw. She is now in the quarterfinals, and in 17 years of the Madrid Open, no lucky loser has ever gotten this far. Earlier, Linda Noskova produced her own piece of drama against Coco Gauff. Gauff was managing the same stomach virus that had ended Swiatek's tournament the day before — vomiting courtside during her previous match, then fighting through it again on Monday. She led 4-1 in the third set. She led 3-0 in the deciding tiebreak. Noskova won 7 of the last 9 points. "The match is not over until it's over," Noskova said afterward. Swiatek, Rybakina, Gauff — three of the tournament's biggest names are gone. The women's draw at Madrid has opened up in a way that very few expected. Which upset surprised you more — Rybakina or Gauff? --- #### Image Caption -- 10:30 AM ET ### Image Post Caption Lucky loser Potapova stuns No. 2 seed Rybakina. ### Long-Form Post There is a number that keeps growing at Madrid, and it is worth pausing on what it means. Jannik Sinner defeated Cameron Norrie 6-2, 7-5 in 87 minutes on Tuesday morning — a first career meeting between the two, settled in emphatic fashion. It extended Sinner's Masters 1000 winning streak to 25 consecutive matches. The last time Sinner lost at a Masters 1000 event was at Paris Bercy in November 2025. Since then: Paris-Bercy title, Indian Wells title, Miami title, Monte Carlo title. He is in Madrid seeking to become the first player in history to win five consecutive Masters 1000 titles. He is in the quarterfinals. His opponent will be determined by the afternoon match between Rafael Jodar and Vit Kopriva. Jodar is 19 years old, from a town 12 kilometres from this stadium. He has beaten No. 8 de Minaur and Fonseca to get here. A Sinner-Jodar quarterfinal at the Caja Mágica is the match this city has been quietly imagining all week. It depends on what happens at 4 PM. Pliskova is, according to tournament records, the lowest-ranked player ever to reach the Madrid quarterfinals. Iga Swiatek retired ill. Elena Rybakina lost to a lucky loser. ### Long-Form Post Tuesday's ATP order of play at Madrid delivers one of the week's richest slates. In the afternoon: Rafael Jodar, the 19-year-old from just 12 kilometres away, plays Vit Kopriva for a quarterfinal spot. A win puts Jodar in a potential last-eight meeting with world No. 1 Jannik Sinner — who won his R16 in 87 minutes this morning. The city wants this match. The bracket has set it up. Arthur Fils — who won Barcelona last week — plays Tomas Etcheverry. Lorenzo Musetti faces Jiří Lehečka in a match that could produce some of the best clay tennis of the week. And then, in the night session: Alexander Zverev against Jakub Mensik. No. 2 seed versus the Czech who reached the ATP Finals in 2025. Their first meeting at ATP tour level. Zverev has been to the Madrid round of 16 nine times; he has never won the title. Mensik is the kind of opponent who can end a run. By tonight, the ATP quarterfinal draw will be fully set.
