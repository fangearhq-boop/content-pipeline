Sinner's Monte Carlo No. 1 Chase: 36 Consecutive Sets and Everything to Gain

By Elena Voss | April 9, 2026

With zero points to defend and a 36-set winning streak at Masters 1000 events, Jannik Sinner faces Tomas Machac on Thursday in the most consequential stretch of his clay season.

There is a number attached to Jannik Sinner's Masters 1000 run right now that is worth sitting with for a moment: 36.

That is the number of consecutive sets Sinner has won at Masters 1000 events. Not matches — sets. His opponents at the ATP's biggest non-Slam tournaments cannot steal a set off him. He is too composed under pressure, too solid in the transitions, and too methodical in his aggression to give them a foothold.

He carries that streak into Thursday's Round of 16 at the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters against Czech player Tomas Machac — and into one of the most charged ranking scenarios of his season.

The Rankings Math

Sinner was absent from Monte Carlo in 2025 due to his suspension. He has zero points to defend here. Carlos Alcaraz, the defending champion, is protecting 1,000 points — the maximum available at a Masters 1000 event. Entering this week, Alcaraz held approximately 13,590 ranking points against Sinner's 12,400. The gap is roughly 1,190 points.

What that means in practical terms: every round Sinner advances without a matching result from Alcaraz shrinks the gap. A Monte Carlo title for Sinner, combined with early Alcaraz losses, would deliver him the No. 1 ranking as soon as next Monday's rankings update.

This is the first time since Sinner's return from his ban that the No. 1 ranking is genuinely within reach at a specific tournament.

The Machac Matchup

Sinner's Round of 16 opponent, Tomas Machac, reached this stage by defeating Francisco Cerundolo 7-6(2), 6-3. The Czech is not a straightforward opponent — he plays with variety, can serve big, and has shown the ability to disrupt better-ranked players.

The historical record, however, is lopsided. Sinner leads their career head-to-head 3-0. Their most recent meeting came at the Doha tournament in February 2026, where Sinner won 6-1, 6-4. This will be their first meeting on clay — a surface that Sinner has increasingly made his own despite a career built primarily on hardcourts.

After his Round of 32 win over Ugo Humbert — 6-3, 6-0 in a match where Sinner conceded just six points in the second set — he appears to have found the clay-court rhythm early in this tournament.

What's Next

The winner of Sinner's match faces either Casper Ruud or Felix Auger-Aliassime in the quarterfinals on Friday. Both are legitimate clay-court threats — Ruud has reached multiple Roland Garros semifinals and finals, and Auger-Aliassime has shown improved results on the surface in 2026.

For Sinner, the path matters less than the destination. He has the ranking incentive, the form, and the head-to-head record in his R3 match. If the 36-set streak holds and the wins keep coming, the No. 1 ranking — not seen next to his name since before his suspension — could return by the end of the week.
