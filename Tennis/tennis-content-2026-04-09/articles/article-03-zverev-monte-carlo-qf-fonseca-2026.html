Zverev Into Monte Carlo Quarterfinals for First Time Since 2022, Faces Fonseca on Friday

By Marcus Cole | April 9, 2026

Alexander Zverev defeated Zizou Bergs 6-2, 7-5 at his home tournament to reach the last eight at Monte Carlo, setting up a quarterfinal showdown with breakthrough Brazilian João Fonseca.

Alexander Zverev lives in Monaco. He has grown up watching the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters from a vantage point that most players only dream about, and he has played the event long enough to understand its rhythms — the heavy red clay, the early morning practice sessions overlooking the Mediterranean, the particular intensity of playing in what functions as a home tournament.

On Thursday, he put that comfort to use. Zverev defeated Belgian Zizou Bergs 6-2, 7-5 to advance to the Monte Carlo quarterfinals for the first time since 2022. It was a controlled performance in the first set and a competitive battle in the second — Bergs, who had stunned No. 15 seed Andrey Rublev 6-4, 6-1 the previous day, is not a straightforward opponent, and the 7-5 scoreline reflects a match that required effort to close out.

Now comes João Fonseca.

The Fonseca Question

Fonseca, 19, reached the quarterfinals on Thursday by eliminating Matteo Berrettini 6-3, 6-2 in 75 minutes — a result that was made more striking by Berrettini's form. Less than 24 hours before facing Fonseca, Berrettini had posted a 6-0, 6-0 win over world No. 10 Daniil Medvedev. He was playing arguably the best tennis of his comeback. Fonseca dismantled him in under 80 minutes.

This will be Zverev's first career meeting with Fonseca. The German enters with seven Masters 1000 titles and years of experience at the top of the game. The Brazilian enters with youth, momentum, and the specific kind of confidence that comes from having already beaten the player in the best form in the draw.

The matchup on paper favors Zverev. The matchup on clay in Monaco in April, with Fonseca riding this kind of form, is genuinely open.

Zverev's Home Tournament

Monte Carlo has produced some of Zverev's more complicated narratives. He reached the quarterfinals in 2022 before his foot injury at Roland Garros changed the arc of his season. His return to the tour and subsequent results have made him one of the most consistent players in the top five, but Monte Carlo had not returned a QF result since that year.

This year's tournament gives him a path he has not walked in three seasons. The draw has opened in ways that create genuine opportunities for multiple players to reach the semifinals — and Zverev, chasing his eighth Masters 1000 crown, will be motivated to use this familiarity advantage.

What's Next

Fonseca versus Zverev is among the first quarterfinals confirmed for Friday at Monte Carlo. It is, on its own terms, one of the more compelling matchups available at this stage of the tournament: an established top-three player at his home event against a 19-year-old who has already shown this week he is willing to dismantle in-form opponents.

Zverev needs to be at his best on clay from the first game. Fonseca, based on what he showed against Berrettini, will not offer him an easy start.
