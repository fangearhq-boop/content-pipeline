Fonseca Reaches Monte Carlo Quarterfinals, Eliminates Berrettini 6-3, 6-2

By Ryan Calloway | April 9, 2026

The 19-year-old Brazilian became the first of his country to reach a Masters 1000 quarterfinal since 2011, defeating a Berrettini who was fresh off the most dominant 49 minutes in recent Masters history.

The day after Matteo Berrettini produced one of the most complete individual performances seen at a Masters 1000 in years — a 6-0, 6-0 demolition of Daniil Medvedev that lasted just 49 minutes — João Fonseca walked onto Court Rainier III and dismantled him in 75 minutes.

Fonseca, 19, defeated Berrettini 6-3, 6-2 on Thursday to reach the quarterfinals of the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters. The result places him among a small group of Brazilians who have broken through at the biggest stages in Masters tennis: he is the first Brazilian to reach a Masters 1000 quarterfinal since Thomaz Bellucci at Madrid in 2011. He is the youngest player to reach the last eight at Monte Carlo since Rafael Nadal and Richard Gasquet did so in 2005. He is the second player born in 2006 to reach a Masters 1000 quarterfinal.

On Friday, he faces Alexander Zverev.

How He Did It

Berrettini came into this match with exceptional momentum. His Round of 32 performance against Medvedev — described by the ATP Tour's own coverage as a "super special" run — had generated enormous attention. The scoreline, 6-0, 6-0 in 49 minutes against a world No. 10, was the kind of result that suggests a player has found form at the precise right moment of a tournament.

Fonseca did not flinch. He continued pulling Berrettini across the court, finding angles that forced errors, and applied consistent pressure from both flanks. The match lasted 75 minutes — longer than Berrettini's win over Medvedev but still a clean two-set performance that never truly wavered. Fonseca won the first set 6-3, kept the pace in the second, and closed 6-2.

The Historical Context

Brazilian tennis has produced world-class players — Gustavo Kuerten built his legend on clay in the late 1990s, and more recently Thomaz Bellucci carried the flag at the top of the men's game before injury curtailed his career. But the gap between Bellucci's Madrid quarterfinal in 2011 and Fonseca's breakthrough at Monte Carlo in 2026 is 15 years. In professional tennis terms, that is an era.

Fonseca filled that gap Thursday afternoon in Monaco.

The Nadal and Gasquet comparison carries its own weight. Both players — who first reached the Monte Carlo quarterfinal at comparable ages — went on to define different corners of the clay-court game. Nadal, of course, became the most decorated clay-court player in history. Gasquet developed into a Roland Garros semifinalist and a career top-10 player. Fonseca's trajectory is his own to write, but the company he is keeping in the history books at 19 is significant.

What's Next

Fonseca meets third seed Alexander Zverev in the Monte Carlo quarterfinals on Friday. Zverev, who lives in Monaco and is playing his home tournament, defeated Zizou Bergs 6-2, 7-5 to reach the last eight for the first time since 2022. He is chasing his eighth Masters 1000 title.

It will be the first meeting between Fonseca and Zverev on tour. The German brings experience, big-match composure, and the familiarity of playing in his adopted home. Fonseca brings something harder to quantify: the fearlessness of a player who has already pushed Alcaraz and Sinner in narrow defeats, and who on Thursday showed he can dismantle a player in peak form.

The Monte Carlo quarterfinals have a storyline worth watching closely.
