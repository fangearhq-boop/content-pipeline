### STORY 2: Sinner's Historic Triple — First Three M1000 Titles in a Season Since Djokovic 2015

**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY

**Why This Matters:**
Winning the first three Masters 1000 titles of a calendar year is a feat so rare that only one player had done it in over a decade. Novak Djokovic completed the Indian Wells + Miami + Monte Carlo sweep in 2015. Sinner has now replicated that. He also joins Djokovic and Nadal as the only players to win four consecutive Masters 1000 titles (Paris 2025, Indian Wells, Miami, Monte Carlo). At 22 consecutive M1000 wins, his dominance at the Masters level in 2026 is historically unmatched outside the Big 3 era.

**Key Angles:**
1. First to sweep Indian Wells + Miami + Monte Carlo since Djokovic 2015 — 11 years
2. 4 consecutive M1000 titles — joins Djokovic and Nadal in exclusive company
3. 22-match M1000 win streak
4. First clay Masters 1000 of his career
5. 3 titles in 2026 — Tour-leading

**Best Social Hook:** "Indian Wells. Miami. Monte Carlo. The last man to win all three to start a season was Novak Djokovic in 2015. Sinner just did it too."

**Target Audience:** Tennis history fans, analytics-minded readers, casual fans who appreciate records.
