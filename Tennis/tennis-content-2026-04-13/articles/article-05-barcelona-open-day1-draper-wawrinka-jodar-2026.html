Barcelona Open Day 1: Draper-Etcheverry Headline as Wawrinka's Farewell Clay Season Continues

By Marcus Cole | April 13, 2026

Seven first-round matches open the ATP 500 clay double-header week at the Real Club de Tenis Barcelona, with Alcaraz waiting in Round 2.

Monday marked the opening of the Barcelona Open Banc Sabadell, and with it the beginning of the ATP's European clay season in earnest. Seven first-round matches were scheduled at the Real Club de Tenis Barcelona on Day 1 of the ATP 500 event, with nine more to follow on Tuesday.

The tournament runs April 13-19 at a club that has hosted professional tennis since 1953. Carlos Alcaraz, the top seed with a 14-3 career record at this event and a multiple champion here, enters in Round 2.

Draper vs. Etcheverry: The Day 1 Highlight

Jack Draper and Tomas Martin Etcheverry were the headline first-round match of Monday's schedule. Draper, the British No. 1 and a player who has been climbing the rankings steadily over the past 18 months, brings a powerful serve and a left-handed game that creates unusual angles on clay. Etcheverry, the Argentine clay specialist, brings the kind of grinding baseline game that can dismantle opponents across a five-set draw.

Both players have something to prove this spring. Draper is working to establish himself as a clay-court threat ahead of Roland Garros. Etcheverry has the game for it but needs a deep run at a major event to fully realize his ranking potential.

Wawrinka: A Farewell Tour in Full Progress

Stan Wawrinka played Cameron Norrie in a match that carried a weight beyond its seeds and rankings. Wawrinka announced before the clay season began that he would retire at the end of 2026. Every clay-court match he plays this year could be among the last of a career that has produced three Grand Slam titles and countless memorable performances on the dirt.

He won in Monte Carlo in 2014, defeating Rafael Nadal in the final. He knows clay. He knows this circuit. And at 41, he is making these weeks count.

Jodar's Barcelona Debut

Rafael Jodar made his Barcelona main draw debut against local player Jaume Munar on the Rafa Nadal Court in the Day 1 highlight match. Jodar reached a career-high ranking of No. 57 this week — a remarkable achievement for a player whose path through the lower levels of professional tennis has been long and nonlinear.

Playing in the tournament named after the court he debuted on adds a layer of symmetry to his Barcelona week. Munar, a Spanish clay-court specialist, provides a stern first-round test, but Jodar's momentum and crowd enthusiasm make this match harder to predict than seedings suggest.

Alcaraz and the Seeds

Alcaraz enters in Round 2 as the No. 1 seed with a 14-3 career record at this tournament. He will be heavily motivated after Sunday's loss to Sinner in the Monte Carlo final. The Barcelona draw has Musetti at No. 2, De Minaur at No. 3, Khachanov at No. 4, and Rublev at No. 5. Alcaraz's projected path runs through Rublev in the quarterfinals and De Minaur or Draper in the semifinals. He is the overwhelming favorite, but the clay season has a way of resetting expectations quickly.

What's Next

Tuesday completes the first round in Barcelona, with nine more matches bringing the main draw fully underway. Alcaraz makes his first appearance Wednesday in Round 2. The tournament runs through April 19, with the quarterfinals Friday, semifinals Saturday, and final Sunday. Barcelona sits between Monte Carlo and the Madrid Open on the ATP clay swing — a critical tournament for players looking to build momentum before Paris. With Alcaraz entering as heavy favorite but freshly stung from a Monte Carlo final defeat, the draw could produce a more complicated story than the No. 1 seed's record suggests.
