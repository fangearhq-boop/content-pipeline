Andreeva Rallies From 1-6 Down to Win Linz, Claim 2nd Title of 2026

By Ryan Calloway | April 13, 2026

The 18-year-old lost the first set decisively, then won the next two sets against Anastasia Potapova to earn her fifth career WTA title in Austria.

Mirra Andreeva dropped the first set 1-6 in just over 25 minutes. Then she won the next two sets 6-4, 6-3, and the match was hers.

The No. 1 seed at the Upper Austria Ladies Linz claimed her fifth career WTA title and second of the 2026 season Sunday, defeating Anastasia Potapova in a final that lasted 1 hour and 54 minutes. The result was 1-6, 6-4, 6-3. The total points won by each player: 86 apiece. Equal counts, unequal outcomes — decided entirely by when the points landed.

A First-Set Implosion, Then a Recalibration

Potapova came out with controlled aggression in the opening set, hitting winners with the consistency of a player who had nothing to lose and a home crowd — she holds Austrian citizenship — loudly behind her. She took the first set in convincing fashion, breaking Andreeva multiple times and conceding very few opportunities in return.

The break between sets was the turning point. Andreeva emerged for the second set with a sharper approach to rallies and a better sense of where Potapova's errors would come from if she extended points. She converted her break opportunities at 5-of-12 across sets two and three — not a clinical conversion rate, but enough. Potapova, meanwhile, committed 42 unforced errors across the match, and the second and third sets bore the full weight of that tally.

The Numbers Behind the Result

The full match statistics are striking. Both players won exactly 86 points. Potapova won her break point opportunities at a perfect 5-for-5 conversion rate — all five of them in the first set. Andreeva converted 5 of 12 break opportunities, including the crucial breaks that decided the second and third sets. The scoreline looks straightforward in sets two and three; the underlying data shows how close the match remained throughout.

Andreeva's 2026 record now stands at 9-0 against players ranked outside the top 50. Her ability to manage matches without top-flight opposition testing her full range is a significant component of her title accumulation this year.

A Season Taking Shape

Andreeva won her first title of 2026 in Adelaide in January. Adding Linz in April means she joins Aryna Sabalenka and Jessica Pegula as the only players with multiple WTA titles in the 2026 season. She is 18 years old and now 5-1 in career singles finals.

Potapova, who became an Austrian citizen and treated the Linz tournament as something close to a home event, falls to 3-4 in career WTA finals. Her first-set dominance and aggressive baseline game gave the final crowd what they came for early on, but the second half of the match belonged to Andreeva's composure.

What's Next

The WTA clay swing continues with the Porsche Tennis Grand Prix in Stuttgart (April 13-19), where Rybakina, Gauff, and Swiatek headline the draw. Andreeva's Linz title positions her as a dangerous dark horse heading into the clay season's bigger events. She will enter Stuttgart with momentum and the kind of match-management ability that wins tournaments even when the first set does not go according to plan.
