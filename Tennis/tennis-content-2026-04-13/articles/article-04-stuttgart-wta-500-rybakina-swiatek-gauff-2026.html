Stuttgart Opens: Rybakina Tops Draw as Swiatek, Gauff Begin WTA Clay Season

By Elena Voss | April 13, 2026

The Porsche Tennis Grand Prix (April 13-19) opens with a Rybakina-Swiatek potential semifinal looming and Laura Siegemund carrying the home crowd's hopes into the main draw.

The European clay season is underway at the Porsche Arena in Stuttgart, and the 2026 Porsche Tennis Grand Prix draws a field as competitive as any WTA 500 event on the calendar.

Elena Rybakina is the No. 1 seed after Aryna Sabalenka withdrew with an injury following her Miami Open run. Coco Gauff, ranked No. 2, occupies the bottom half of the draw. Iga Swiatek — who has won in Stuttgart twice (2022, 2023) — enters as the No. 3 seed and finds herself in the same half as Rybakina. That seeding structure sets up a potential semifinal between two of the best clay-court players in the world before the tournament's final weekend.

Rybakina: A First Shot at Stuttgart

Rybakina has been one of the most consistently dominant players in women's tennis over the past two seasons, but Stuttgart has not been a tournament where she has put together a deep run. As the No. 1 seed on indoor clay — a surface that suits her flat, fast game more than the outdoor red clay of Roland Garros — this edition offers a real opportunity.

Her potential collision with Swiatek in the semifinals would pit two very different playing styles against each other: Rybakina's serve-and-baseline power versus Swiatek's high-topspin grind. That is the kind of match Stuttgart was made for.

Swiatek's Home Turf

Iga Swiatek won Stuttgart in 2022 and again in 2023. The indoor clay at the Porsche Arena is fast by clay standards, and Swiatek's ability to redirect pace and construct points from the baseline is particularly effective at this tournament. She enters the draw as the No. 3 seed and will need to navigate past Rybakina in the semifinals if she is to reach the final. Two straight Stuttgart titles give her a comfort level at this tournament that few players can match.

Siegemund and the Home Crowd

Laura Siegemund opened the main draw on Monday with the Porsche Arena crowd firmly behind her. The German veteran is one of the most popular players in Stuttgart every year, and her presence in the main draw gives the opening day an emotional dimension beyond rankings and draw projections.

Alex Eala and Leylah Fernandez also meet in a first-round clash that drew attention when the draw was released — two left-handed players with aggressive games, meeting on clay in what amounts to a mirror match. It is the kind of early-round curiosity that makes tournament draws interesting.

Gauff's Side of the Draw

Coco Gauff, the No. 2 seed, occupies the bottom half of the draw without Rybakina or Swiatek in her path until the final. Her road to the championship match is more clearly defined than her rivals' — which means any early exit would be a genuine surprise. She is 21 years old, a Grand Slam champion, and consistently dangerous on clay.

What's Next

First-round play continues through Tuesday, with the draw fully set for quarterfinals later in the week. The Rybakina-Swiatek half of the draw will be the primary storyline to follow. The bottom half features Gauff as the prohibitive favorite. The tournament runs through April 19, with the final taking place on Saturday. Stuttgart winner heading into Roland Garros preparation carries significant momentum on clay — and the field competing for that advantage is formidable.
