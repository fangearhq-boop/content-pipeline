Sinner now begins his 67th week as world No. 1, moving one clear of Alcaraz (66 weeks). His ranking points stand at 13,350 against Alcaraz's 13,240. More than the ranking, this title carries symbolic weight. Sinner has been dominant on hard courts but has always faced questions about clay. ### Long-Form Post Novak Djokovic did it in 2015. Before that, no one had done it in the Open Era. Now, in 2026, Jannik Sinner has joined him. By winning the Monte Carlo Masters on Sunday, Sinner completed the sweep of the first three Masters 1000 tournaments of the calendar year — Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte Carlo. It is one of the rarest achievements in modern men's tennis, requiring sustained excellence across three different surfaces, three different continents, across more than two months of competition. He also became only the third player in history to win four consecutive Masters 1000 titles, joining Rafael Nadal and Novak Djokovic. His streak began at the Paris Masters in November 2025 and has now extended to 22 consecutive Masters 1000 wins. Sinner won the first-set tiebreak 7-5, then came back from 1-3 in the second set to seal the match 6-3. First match point. No drama needed. The clay season is just starting. Madrid and Roland Garros are ahead. But Sinner has already written the first chapter of what may be one of the most dominant seasons in modern men's tennis. ### Image Post Caption Indian Wells. Miami. Monte Carlo. The first three Masters 1000 titles of the season — for the second time in history. The last player to do it was Novak Djokovic in 2015. Sinner has now joined that company.

### Long-Form Post Mirra Andreeva is the 2026 Upper Austria Ladies Linz champion — and her title didn't come easy. The No. 1 seed was dominated early by Anastasia Potapova, losing the opening set 1-6 as Potapova's aggressive game clicked in front of a home crowd (Potapova holds Austrian citizenship). But Andreeva recalibrated between sets, sharpened her returns, and patiently exposed the errors that would eventually define the match. Potapova committed 42 unforced errors in total. Andreeva capitalized on them in the second and third sets to close out a 1-6, 6-4, 6-3 victory in 1 hour 54 minutes. The final statistics tell a telling story: both players won exactly 86 total points. But Andreeva found her moments when they mattered — in the second and third sets, her conversion rate on break opportunities and her ability to extend rallies shifted the match's momentum entirely. At 18 years old, Andreeva now has 5 career WTA titles and a record of 5-1 in singles finals. She is now a multi-title winner in 2026, joining Pegula and Sabalenka in that company. ### Image Post Caption Mirra Andreeva won the 2026 Linz Open final 1-6, 6-4, 6-3. Lost the first set.
