Wimbledon 2026 Day 1: Defending Champion Sinner Opens on Centre Court as Serena Williams Returns to Grand Slam Singles

By Elena Voss | June 28, 2026

The Championships begin Monday June 29 with an extraordinary first-day schedule: Sinner defends, Sabalenka and Djokovic follow on Centre Court, and Serena Williams plays her first Grand Slam singles match in nearly four years.

Wimbledon 2026 begins Monday June 29 with one of the strongest Day 1 schedules the Championships have seen in years. Outside courts open at 11:00 AM BST. Court 1 gets underway at 1:00 PM BST. Centre Court begins at 1:30 PM BST. The order of play on those showcase courts contains the kind of names that put the rest of the tennis calendar on pause.

Defending champion Jannik Sinner opens Centre Court, as tradition dictates — the reigning singles champions lead Day 1 at the All England Club. Sinner faces Miomir Kecmanovic in what is, on paper, a straightforward first-round assignment for the world No. 1. The more interesting question around Sinner is one of preparation: he arrived at Wimbledon having played only exhibition grass-court tennis at Hurlingham in the weeks prior, skipping every ATP warm-up event. The Kecmanovic match is where we learn how that approach has worked.

Centre Court After Sinner

Aryna Sabalenka, the women's world No. 1 and top seed, follows Sinner on Centre Court against Teodora Kostovic. Sabalenka has never won Wimbledon — it remains the one Grand Slam title missing from a collection that already includes the Australian Open and US Open. The grass has not historically been her strongest surface, but she arrives this year ranked No. 1 and with the kind of game that can impose itself anywhere.

Novak Djokovic, the seven-time Wimbledon champion seeded No. 7, closes Centre Court's Day 1 schedule against Wu Yibing. Djokovic is 39 years old and has played a limited schedule in 2026 — approximately 13 matches entering the Championships — amid ongoing questions about his shoulder and his overall match fitness. He is chasing a record-tying eighth Wimbledon title and his 25th Grand Slam, which would equal Serena Williams's all-time record. Every match he plays carries the weight of history and uncertainty in equal measure.

Serena Williams Returns

The match that will attract the most first-round attention does not involve any of the top seeds. Serena Williams, 44 years old and holding one of the eight women's wild cards, plays her first Grand Slam singles match since the 2022 US Open against 20-year-old Maya Joint. When Williams announced at the 2022 US Open that she was "evolving away" from tennis, most observers assumed that was the end. Her return to Wimbledon — singles and doubles, with Venus as her doubles partner — has generated the kind of coverage that only Serena can still command.

Joint is ranked outside the top 100. On paper, the age and ranking gap runs in different directions, and the question of what 44-year-old Serena can do against a younger opponent with less to lose is genuinely unanswerable until the match is played. What is certain is that every person at the All England Club who can get near a screen showing the match will be watching it.

Court 1 and the Rest of Day 1

Court 1 hosts No. 3 seed Félix Auger-Aliassime and Roland Garros champion Mirra Andreeva (No. 5 seed) among others. Andreeva, 19 years old, arrives at Wimbledon for the first time as a major champion — but one with virtually no grass-court points to her name. Her first-round match against Magda Linette is the beginning of an opportunity that the women's tennis world will be watching closely.

British favorite Emma Raducanu (No. 32 seed) is also in the Day 1 draw, facing Antonia Ruzic. Raducanu missed practice last week with a reported fitness concern, and the home crowd will be watching her closely from her first game.

What's Next

Wimbledon 2026 runs through Sunday July 12. Outside courts open at 11:00 AM BST Monday, with Court 1 at 1:00 PM BST and Centre Court at 1:30 PM BST. ESPN+ carries live coverage for US viewers. Full results from Day 1 will determine Tuesday's schedule, which includes Humbert vs. Bergs in Round 1 — a rematch of the Eastbourne final completed just the day before.
