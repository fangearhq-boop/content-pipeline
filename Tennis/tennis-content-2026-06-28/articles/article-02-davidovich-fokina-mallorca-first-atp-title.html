Alejandro Davidovich Fokina Finally Wins His First ATP Title at Mallorca — Sixth Final, First Trophy

By Marcus Cole | June 28, 2026

The 25-year-old Spaniard defeated Ethan Quinn 7-6(4), 6-3 at the Mallorca Championships, ending a career defined by near-misses — and becoming the first home champion in the tournament's history.

There is a version of this story that could have been written six different times and ended differently each occasion. Alejandro Davidovich Fokina reached his first ATP Tour final and lost. He reached his second, and lost. The third, the fourth, the fifth — all losses. At Delray Beach in 2025, he held championship points and could not convert. At Washington in 2025, the same: championship points, the trophy in sight, and then gone. Professional tennis is full of players with talent who find a way to make the moment too large. ADF was starting to look like one of them.

On Saturday in Mallorca, that story ended. Davidovich Fokina defeated Ethan Quinn 7-6(4), 6-3 at the Vanda Pharmaceuticals Mallorca Championships to claim his first ATP Tour title in his sixth career final. He wins on home soil — the tournament is held in Mallorca, Spain — and becomes the first Spanish champion in the event's history.

A Title That Required Patience

The match itself was not entirely comfortable. Quinn, 22 years old and ranked outside the top 60, had produced the tennis of his life earlier in the week, dismantling opponents with the kind of grass-court fluency that suggests a player who belongs at this level. In the final, he took the first set to a tiebreak and pushed Davidovich Fokina to 7-4 in it — closer than the final scoreline suggests. The second set was more decisive, with ADF breaking and controlling the tempo until he served out for the title.

The moment of victory, when it came, was telling. Davidovich Fokina has the kind of athleticism and shot-making that tennis observers have recognized for years as the ingredients of a potential champion. The missing piece has always been the same: closing the deal in a final. His six-final, five-loss record going into Mallorca placed him, uncomfortably, in a category of players for whom the biggest stage seemed to resist them. Saturday in Mallorca put that record to rest.

Davidovich Fokina rises two spots to No. 23 in the PIF ATP Live Rankings following the title.

A Note on Previous Coverage

Our June 27 pipeline coverage reported that Ethan Quinn had won the Mallorca final. That reporting was incorrect. Multiple confirmed sources verify that Alejandro Davidovich Fokina defeated Quinn 7-6(4), 6-3 to claim the title. We apologize for the error and have corrected the record in this article. The semi-final results previously reported — ADF defeating Marozsan and Quinn defeating Borges — were accurate.

What's Next

Alejandro Davidovich Fokina enters Wimbledon 2026 as a player whose career has changed. He is no longer the talented Spaniard who cannot finish; he is an ATP Tour champion whose best tennis happens to arrive just as the sport's most prestigious event begins. Whether his breakthrough at Mallorca carries over to the All England Club begins Monday June 29.

Ethan Quinn, who had his best week on tour, heads into Wimbledon having shown that his game belongs at this level. His time will come.
