Rain Halts Eastbourne Men's Final After Three Games — Humbert and Bergs Finish It Sunday, Then Meet Again at Wimbledon

By Ryan Calloway | June 28, 2026

The Lexus Eastbourne men's singles final was suspended Saturday when rain stopped play with Humbert leading 2-1. They resumed Sunday — the morning before Wimbledon — and face each other again in Round 1 on Tuesday.

Professional tennis produces unusual circumstances regularly, but the situation that developed at the Lexus Eastbourne Open on Saturday was genuinely singular. Ugo Humbert and Zizou Bergs were three games into the men's singles final when rain moved in and play was suspended. Humbert had taken the first two service games and led 2-1 when the match was halted. Neither player had been broken. Nothing had been decided. And yet the final was over for the day.

They returned Sunday morning to complete it — the morning before Wimbledon. The timing could not be more compressed: finish a title match in the morning, collect a trophy or runner-up plate, travel to London, and report to the All England Club less than 24 hours later. There is no day off between the conclusion of the last warm-up event and the start of the sport's most prestigious Grand Slam.

The Two Finalists

Humbert, a seven-time ATP Tour champion, is a dangerous player on grass and entered the week as one of the favorites. His semi-final win over Jack Draper — 7-5, 6-3 over the British wildcard in front of a home crowd that wanted Draper to win — was a clinical performance that established Humbert's credentials as a grass-court threat heading into Wimbledon.

Bergs, competing in the biggest final of his career, reached Sunday having beaten Toby Samuel 4-6, 7-6(5), 6-2 in his semi-final — coming back from a set down to control the third. Bergs is 27 years old and has won eight Challenger titles, but this Eastbourne run represented the furthest he had gone in a main-draw ATP event. He was playing for his first top-level title in his first top-level final on the sport's most prestigious surface.

The Extra Dimension: They Meet Again Tuesday

The Wimbledon draw added a storyline that the rain delay alone could not have created. Humbert and Bergs are scheduled to face each other in the first round at the All England Club on Tuesday — meaning the two players who play a final Sunday morning will shake hands at the net, then cross the Channel and do it all again on a different grass court 48 hours later.

There is no precedent for quite this specific situation. The physical demands are real: a contested final, however quickly it ends, depletes players in ways that a day of travel and preparation cannot fully restore. The psychological dynamic is just as complicated. Does the player who wins the final carry momentum or complacency into the Wimbledon rematch? Does the loser carry extra motivation or fatigue?

Neither player may want to be thinking about Tuesday while Sunday is still unfinished. But the draw guarantees the questions will follow them regardless.

What's Next

The Eastbourne men's singles final was completed Sunday June 28. Both Ugo Humbert and Zizou Bergs then travel to London to prepare for Wimbledon, where they are scheduled to meet in Round 1 on Tuesday June 30. The All England Club's order of play for Day 2 will confirm courts and times.
