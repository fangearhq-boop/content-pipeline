### STORY 1: Madison Keys Wins Eastbourne for Third Time — Joins Evert and Navratilova
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY (final result June 28)
**Sources:** lta.org.uk, wtatennis.com, tennismajors.com, yardbarker.com, 10sballs.com
**Posting Window:** 8:00 AM ET
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Madison Keys defeated Tatjana Maria 7-5, 6-3 in the Lexus Eastbourne Open women's singles final on Saturday June 27 to claim her third Eastbourne title. The American becomes only the third woman to win Eastbourne three times, joining Chris Evert (three titles) and Martina Navratilova (eleven). She is the first player ever to win her first three Eastbourne finals consecutively. Maria, 38, was competing in her first WTA 500 singles final and battled hard before falling in straight sets. Keys heads into Wimbledon 2026 (starting June 29) in career-best grass-court form.

Alejandro Davidovich Fokina ended a career-long wait for a first ATP title by defeating Ethan Quinn 7-6(4), 6-3 in the Vanda Pharmaceuticals Mallorca Championships final on Saturday June 28. **Posting Window:** 12:30 PM ET
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

The Lexus Eastbourne Open men's singles final between Ugo Humbert and Zizou Bergs was halted by rain on Saturday after just three games — Humbert leading 2-1 with both players holding serve — and resumed Sunday June 28, the day before Wimbledon. The rain delay created one of the most unusual situations in professional tennis: two players fighting for a grass-court title the morning before one of the biggest events in the sport. The drama is compounded further: Humbert and Bergs are also drawn against each other in Round 1 at Wimbledon on Tuesday, meaning the two men will play twice in three days at consecutive grass events.
