Djokovic Joins Alcaraz in French Open Doubt — Five Weeks, Two Champions, No Timelines

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

April 24, 2026

Roland Garros begins May 25. That is five weeks away. And right now, the two biggest names in men's tennis — the two players most expected to contend for the title — are both carrying significant injury uncertainty.

Carlos Alcaraz has been sidelined since mid-April with a right wrist injury. Novak Djokovic has not played a match since losing to Jack Draper at Indian Wells, having skipped Miami, Monte Carlo, and now Madrid with a right shoulder problem. Together, their absence from the clay warm-up season creates a picture of Roland Garros 2026 that looks very different from what was anticipated.

Alcaraz: Still Waiting on Test Results

The Alcaraz situation has been unfolding since April 14, when he was treated on his right wrist during his first-round match at Barcelona against Otto Virtanen. He later withdrew from Barcelona and subsequently pulled out of Madrid. He has been spotted wearing a wrist splint and has not publicly committed to a return date.

His message to the media has been consistent and careful: "I would rather return later and fully fit than come back too soon, rushed and not at my best." He added that pushing to play Roland Garros without being ready "could seriously affect me in future tournaments." The medical tests he is awaiting will determine the extent of the damage and whether he can realistically compete in Paris.

Alcaraz is the two-time defending French Open champion — titles won in 2024 and 2025. If he is absent, he would be defending as the absent champion, with his 2,000 ranking points for the title undefended and his name absent from a draw he has dominated. The implications for the No. 1 ranking race are considerable: Sinner, already building momentum at Madrid, would see his lead grow further with every event Alcaraz misses.

Djokovic: Hope, But No Certainty

Djokovic's shoulder injury is less well-publicised but equally significant. The 38-year-old has now missed four consecutive ATP events — Miami, Monte Carlo, Barcelona (did not enter), and Madrid — without playing a single match. His last competitive appearance was at Indian Wells in March.

In recent statements, Djokovic has offered cautious optimism. "I have an injury, I hope to be ready at least for Roland Garros," he said. He has mentioned Rome as a potential warm-up target, but confirmed he cannot predict his timeline or commit to any schedule with certainty. If he misses all remaining clay events before Roland Garros, he would arrive in Paris without a single clay-court match since Indian Wells — a situation that, given his history, would be unprecedented in his career.

For Djokovic, Roland Garros carries a specific significance: it is one of the few events he has not yet equalled his Grand Slam record ambitions on. He has won three French Open titles and remains motivated by the possibilities at the Slams.

Who Benefits?

The player most obviously positioned to benefit is Sinner. Currently on an extended winning streak and at Madrid seeking an unprecedented fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title, the Italian world No. 1 would enter Roland Garros as the clear favourite if both Alcaraz and Djokovic are absent or compromised.

Others who gain in the projections include Alexander Zverev — the No. 2 seed at Madrid and a two-time finalist at Roland Garros — and Casper Ruud, a three-time finalist at the French Open. Carlos Alcaraz's Barcelona title-winner Arthur Fils would also rise in the conversation.

On the women's side, the story is less affected by injury news. Sabalenka and Swiatek are both healthy and competing at Madrid. Whoever wins this event will likely enter Paris as the form player.

What's Next

The next major update on both players is expected in the coming days. Alcaraz's test results are pending and could provide clarity on whether a Roland Garros appearance is feasible. Djokovic has mentioned Rome (May 7-18) as his potential return point — though that remains conditional on his recovery. The French Open draw is expected to be released in the week before the tournament. Whether Alcaraz and Djokovic's names appear in it will be one of the defining storylines of the 2026 clay season.
