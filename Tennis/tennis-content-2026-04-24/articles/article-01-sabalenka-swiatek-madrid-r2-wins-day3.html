Sabalenka and Swiatek Both Win in Madrid — One Fight, One Blitz

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

April 24, 2026

Two of women's tennis's biggest names arrived at the Caja Mágica on Thursday needing to answer the same question: how sharp is my clay game right now? Aryna Sabalenka and Iga Swiatek both answered with wins — but the manner in which they did it could not have been more different.

Sabalenka ground through a 7-5, 6-3 victory over American Peyton Stearns, shaking off early rust before her aggressive baseline game asserted control. Swiatek was simply dominant from the first ball, dispatching Ukrainian qualifier Daria Snigur 6-1, 6-2 in 61 minutes. Both players are into the third round. The clay season is properly underway.

Sabalenka: Rust, Then Results

Coming in as the defending champion and the player who has won this title three times, Sabalenka might have been expected to walk through her second-round opener. Instead she had to work. Reports indicated Sabalenka went a break down early against Stearns — a reminder that clay-court timing does not arrive automatically, even for the best players in the world.

But this is exactly the kind of situation that separates good players from great ones. Sabalenka recovered, tightened her game, and won the first set 7-5 before taking the second more comfortably 6-3. It was not the most polished performance, but it was a win — and Sabalenka has demonstrated time and again that she can improve sharply as a tournament progresses.

She is the No. 1 seed in Madrid and carries the weight of three previous titles at this event. The expectation is that her best tennis will come later in the week.

Swiatek: Back to Her Best

If Sabalenka was taking her time to warm up, Swiatek looked as though she had never been away. The Polish fourth seed needed just 61 minutes to dispatch Daria Snigur 6-1, 6-2. She barely dropped a game. From the first set it was clear that Swiatek — now working with Francisco Roig, the coach who spent 17 years at Rafael Nadal's side — arrived in Madrid with a clear plan and the fitness to execute it.

Swiatek's clay season had been anticipated with particular interest. After a mixed 2026, her hiring of Roig signalled a deliberate attempt to reconnect with the kind of form that saw her dominate on this surface for three straight years. Thursday's performance was the most encouraging sign yet that the reconnection is working.

She won two consecutive Madrid titles in 2022 and 2023, and has four French Open titles to her name. If she plays like this through the week, she will be very difficult to beat.

The Path Ahead

Both players are now into the third round (round of 32), and the projected bracket has them on a potential collision course in the quarterfinals or semifinals. A Sabalenka-Swiatek Madrid clash would be one of the most anticipated matches of the clay season — two former world No. 1s, two Madrid champions, playing for a spot in the final on their favourite surface.

The rest of the Day 3 WTA draw also saw former world No. 3 Elina Svitolina exit early, further clearing the path. Elena Rybakina — the Stuttgart champion who arrived in Madrid in strong form — faces Elena-Gabriela Ruse in her second round. Coco Gauff, who was given the No. 3 seed, is also in action on Friday.

What's Next

Sabalenka and Swiatek are expected to return to the court for their third-round matches around Sunday or Monday, April 26-27. With the draw thinning out and both players in strong form, Madrid is shaping up to be the first major showdown of the clay season. Roland Garros begins May 25 — and whoever lifts the trophy here will carry enormous momentum into Paris.
