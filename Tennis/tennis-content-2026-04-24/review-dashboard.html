It wasn't pretty, but it was a win — and it snapped a three-match losing streak for the Greek. Sometimes grinding through is what matters. Grigor Dimitrov had no such luck. It may have been his last match at this tournament. If so, it was a quiet goodbye from one of the tour's most entertaining figures. ### Long-Form Post There is something genuinely special about a young player winning at a tournament they grew up dreaming about. Rafael Jódar, 19, did exactly that on Wednesday at the Mutua Madrid Open. 🌟 Jódar defeated Jesper de Jong 2-6, 7-5, 6-4 to advance to the second round. The court at the Caja Mágica is roughly 12 kilometres from where he grew up. He became only the third Spaniard under the age of 20 to win a main draw match at this tournament — the others being Rafael Nadal and Carlos Alcaraz. This year alone he has won his first ATP title in Marrakech, reached the Barcelona semifinal, and now advanced at Madrid. His 2026 record is 14-7. He next faces No. 5 seed Alex de Minaur, the Australian champion. The Washington Post recently called him "a new Rafa rising in Spain." ### Long-Form Post Roland Garros begins May 25. And right now, two of the biggest names in men's tennis are both injury question marks. 😟 Carlos Alcaraz, the two-time defending French Open champion, continues to await test results on his injured right wrist. He has withdrawn from Barcelona and Madrid, is wearing a wrist splint, and has declined to give any return timeline. His statement has been consistent: "I would rather return later and fully fit than come back too soon." The tests will determine how serious the damage is — and whether he can line up at Roland Garros. Now Novak Djokovic has joined that uncertainty. The Serbian great has a right shoulder injury and has skipped Miami, Monte Carlo, and Madrid without playing a single match. Djokovic says he "hopes to be ready at least for Roland Garros" and may target Rome — but refuses to give a specific timeline. If he misses all remaining clay events, it would be the first time in his career he has arrived at Roland Garros without any clay warm-up match preparation. Five weeks. Two champions. One Grand Slam that will define the 2026 clay season regardless of who shows up — or doesn't. If both Alcaraz and Djokovic miss Roland Garros, who becomes the tournament favourite? --- #### Image Caption -- 6:00 PM ET ### Image Post Caption Alcaraz (wrist). Djokovic (shoulder).
