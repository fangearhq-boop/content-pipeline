Sabalenka, Osaka and Gauff All Advance — Wimbledon Women's Day 5 Recap

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 4, 2026 — Wimbledon, London

Three of the biggest names in women's tennis all advanced into the Wimbledon Round of 16 on Friday, setting up a second week that is shaping up to be one of the most competitive in recent memory. Aryna Sabalenka, Naomi Osaka and Coco Gauff each won their third-round matches — but the most compelling story came in a number that most fans won't see on a scoreboard.

Sabalenka Dominant — and Setting Records Off the Court

World No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka saw off Jelena Ostapenko 6-4, 6-4 in a display of controlled aggression. Sabalenka struck 9 aces, won 71 percent of her first-serve points and converted 4 of 11 break-point opportunities. Ostapenko, capable of matching anyone on her best day, had no real answer.

The Sabalenka number worth noting: she currently holds the Open Era record for consecutive Grand Slam tiebreak wins, having extended her streak to 21. She surpassed Novak Djokovic's previous record of 19, building the streak across multiple Grand Slam appearances. Her R2 win over McCartney Kessler — where she came from 2-5 down in the second set and saved four set points — was where the record moved.

On Sunday, Sabalenka faces Naomi Osaka in what may be the headline match of the Round of 16 in the women's draw.

Osaka Into the Round of 16 for the First Time

Naomi Osaka defeated Daria Kasatkina 6-1, 6-3 to reach the Wimbledon Round of 16 for the first time in her career. Five aces, 25 winners and five breaks of serve told the story of a player finally finding her best form on grass. It is a career milestone that has been a long time coming for a four-time Grand Slam champion whose Wimbledon results have historically lagged behind her other Grand Slam performances.

Osaka-Sabalenka on Sunday is the match that women's tennis fans have been circling all week. World No. 1 against a resurgent former world No. 1, first-ever Wimbledon R4 against a player chasing a second week run. It promises to be one of the best matches of the tournament.

Gauff Battles Through

Coco Gauff needed three sets to get past Claire Liu, winning 6-3, 6-7(5), 6-2. The second-set tiebreak tested Gauff's composure, but she bounced back cleanly in the third to advance. The No. 7 seed moves into the Round of 16 as one of the strongest remaining contenders in the women's draw.

What's Next

The women's Round of 16 begins Sunday, with Sabalenka vs. Osaka the must-watch fixture. Gauff also advances and will be looking to push deeper into the draw. The women's half of the tournament has already produced several upsets; the second week is set to be equally dramatic.
