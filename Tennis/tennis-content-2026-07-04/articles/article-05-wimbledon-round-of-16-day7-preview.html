Wimbledon Round of 16 Preview — Djokovic Chases History, Sinner Defends, Sabalenka vs. Osaka

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 4, 2026 — Wimbledon, London

The second week of Wimbledon 2026 begins on Sunday with a Round of 16 that includes three matches that could define the tournament. After an opening week filled with upsets, milestones and drama on every court, Day 7 promises to deliver more of the same — starting at the very top of the draw.

Djokovic vs. Safiullin — One Win from Sole Record Ownership

Novak Djokovic enters Sunday's match against Roman Safiullin with 105 career Wimbledon wins — tied with Roger Federer for the all-time men's record. Win No. 106 would make Djokovic the sole men's record holder at Wimbledon. He has never been closer to owning that distinction outright.

Safiullin is no pushover. The Russian wild card, ranked No. 132, reached the Round of 16 by defeating No. 24 seed Joao Fonseca and is a former 2023 Wimbledon quarterfinalist. He knows the late stages of this tournament. But Djokovic leads their career head-to-head 3-0, and the conditions — his home on grass — strongly favour the seven-time champion.

Sinner vs. Mochizuki — Defending Champion Remains on Course

Defending champion Jannik Sinner takes on Japan's Shintaro Mochizuki in the Round of 16. Sinner, who enters the tournament at 23-4 all-time at Wimbledon, has been sharp throughout the fortnight. He defeated Jenson Brooksby 6-4, 6-3, 6-4 in the third round to advance with minimal fuss. Mochizuki will be a significant underdog, but Sinner has shown the ability to raise his level when challenged.

One context point worth noting: Carlos Alcaraz, who would have been among the leading men's title contenders alongside Sinner, is not at Wimbledon 2026 due to a wrist injury. His absence has significantly altered the landscape of the men's draw heading into the second week — and leaves Sinner as the clear favourite to retain his title.

Sabalenka vs. Osaka — Women's Match of the Round

The women's marquee match on Sunday pits world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka against Naomi Osaka, who reached the Wimbledon Round of 16 for the first time in her career on Friday. Osaka defeated Daria Kasatkina 6-1, 6-3 in a dominant performance, showing the kind of aggressive grass-court tennis that had been difficult to predict from her coming into the fortnight.

Sabalenka has been in commanding form. She dismissed Jelena Ostapenko 6-4, 6-4 in the third round and currently holds the Open Era record for consecutive Grand Slam tiebreak wins at 21. World No. 1 versus first-career-Wimbledon-R4 — it is the kind of women's match the second week was made for.

What's Next

Saturday's Day 6 results — including the outcome of Swiatek vs. Eala on Centre Court — will complete the Round of 16 field and set up the quarter-final draw. Sunday represents the moment when the tournament separates its contenders from the field. After a week of surprises, the question on everyone's mind is simple: which players will still be standing when the second week is done?
