Djokovic Equals Federer's All-Time Wimbledon Win Record at 105

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 4, 2026 — Wimbledon, London

Novak Djokovic made history at the All England Club on Friday, recording his 105th career match win at Wimbledon to equal Roger Federer's all-time men's record. The milestone came in a hard-fought 7-5, 6-4, 1-6, 7-6(4) victory over Arthur Rinderknech that pushed Djokovic to the edge of one of tennis's most prestigious records.

Only Martina Navratilova, who amassed 120 career match wins at Wimbledon across her extraordinary singles and doubles career, stands above both men in the all-time record books at the tournament. Among men's singles players, Djokovic now shares the summit with Federer — though the gap to Navratilova remains imposing.

A Match That Tested Him

The Rinderknech match was no formality. The Frenchman claimed the third set and forced a fourth-set tiebreak, making Djokovic work for every point. But the seven-time Wimbledon champion showed exactly why he has lasted this long at the top of the game, closing out the tiebreak 7-4 to seal the record-equaling win.

Djokovic reflected on his performance afterward with characteristic focus. "I always try to give quality to the people who pay to come and watch you play," he said. "That's what I tried to do today."

The Numbers Behind the Legacy

Djokovic's 105 wins have come across more than two decades of Wimbledon campaigns. The record tells a story of sustained excellence on grass — a surface that was not always considered Djokovic's strongest, yet one on which he has built an unmatched modern-era body of work. He has won Wimbledon seven times and has never gone beyond the third round since his first appearance at the tournament.

Federer's 105 wins came during 22 years of Wimbledon competition and included eight singles titles. The Swiss legend is no longer on the professional circuit, having retired in 2022, but his record has stood as the benchmark for men's Wimbledon excellence. Djokovic has now pulled level.

What's Next

On Sunday, Djokovic faces Roman Safiullin in the Round of 16 — and win No. 106 would give him sole ownership of the all-time men's Wimbledon record. Safiullin, ranked No. 132 in the world and playing on a wild card, reached the Round of 16 by defeating No. 24 seed Joao Fonseca. Djokovic leads their career head-to-head 3-0. The history books are waiting.
