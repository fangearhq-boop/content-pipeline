Struff Stuns No. 8 Medvedev in Three-Set Wimbledon Masterclass

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 4, 2026 — Wimbledon, London

Jan-Lennard Struff pulled off one of the most dramatic upsets of Wimbledon 2026 on Friday, defeating eighth seed Daniil Medvedev 7-6(4), 7-6(5), 7-5 in a match that defied all logic for long stretches before ending in a stunning tiebreak sweep for the German world No. 74.

Medvedev had led by a break in all three sets. In the third, he was cruising at 5-2 — seemingly on the verge of a routine victory. He controlled the baseline exchanges. He generated the chances. But he converted just 4 of 12 break points across the match, and when the critical moments arrived in each tiebreak, Struff was the sharper player every time.

Three Sets, Three Tiebreaks, Three Wins

The pattern was remarkable in its consistency. Struff would fall behind, claw back to level, and then lift his game in the high-pressure moments when the tiebreak arrived. Three times in a row, he did it. It takes a particular kind of mental fortitude to win a tiebreak once with a break-back behind you, let alone three times in the same match against a top-ten player.

The third-set tiebreak was perhaps the most dramatic, coming after Medvedev had recovered from 5-2 down only for Struff to stay patient and convert. The research into Struff's tiebreak record across the tournament is striking — he won six tiebreaks across his three Wimbledon 2026 matches, a pace bettered only by Wayne Arthurs at Wimbledon in 1999.

Medvedev's Honest Assessment

Medvedev did not hide his disappointment afterward. "I should do better," he said. "I am really disappointed, because I felt I could do well at Wimbledon." The raw honesty spoke to a player who knew he had let this one slip — and who understood exactly where the match had been lost.

The break-point statistic — 4 from 12 — encapsulates the story. Medvedev played well enough to win, just not decisively enough. On grass, where points can turn quickly and break chances are rarer than on clay, wasting eight of twelve opportunities is a margin that tends to be fatal.

What's Next

For Struff, it is the biggest result of his Wimbledon career and his first-ever appearance in the Round of 16 at the All England Club. He advances into a draw that has been reshaped significantly by the absence of Carlos Alcaraz, who withdrew from the tournament with a wrist injury. Medvedev, meanwhile, departs Wimbledon 2026 one of the tournament's most high-profile early exits. The path to the title — already lacking Alcaraz — has become more open still.
