### STORY 1: Wawrinka's Final Match at Gstaad — A Swiss Legend Leaves the Stage He Started On

**Freshness Tags:** Wawrinka Farewell, Gstaad Ceremony, End of an Era

**Summary:**
Stan Wawrinka, 41, arrived at the EFG Swiss Open in Gstaad as he has done for over two decades — as a player. He left it in a different way: with a standing ovation, former Davis Cup teammates on the court beside him, and a pair of custom skis from tournament organizers commemorating a career that included three Grand Slam titles. He lost to Jaime Faria 6-7(8), 6-4, 6-4 in the first round, but the match score was almost incidental. The moment was a goodbye from one of the most admired players of his generation.

**Relevance:**
Wawrinka is one of the last players of his era still competing. His three Slams — each against top players at their peaks — are among the most celebrated upsets in the sport's history. His farewell tour will resonate with fans who watched his career in full.

**5 Unique Angles:**
1. The symmetry: he made his ATP debut at Gstaad and played his final Gstaad match there — bookends of a 23-year career
2. The skis: the geometric print from his 2015 Roland Garros shorts is a specific, meaningful symbol; the tournament chose it deliberately
3. Former Davis Cup teammates on court: team sport in an individual career
4. Faria as a fitting opponent: a young Portuguese player earning the win honestly; Wawrinka wouldn't have wanted a walk-off
5. What happens next: he still has months of tennis left on his farewell tour — what he's aiming for before the final match

**Engagement Hooks:**
1. "Gstaad gave Wawrinka his first ATP match. On Tuesday, it gave him his last."
2. Hot take: The fact that Stan Wawrinka is still competing at 41 is a story in itself — and so is the fact that he's losing to young players honestly, not coasting on sentiment.

**Headlines:**
1. Stan Wawrinka Plays His Final Gstaad Match — and Receives the Send-Off He Deserved
