Canadian Open: 71 of 72 Top Players In — Alcaraz the Only Notable Absence

By Elena Voss July 15, 2026

The Strongest Field in Canadian Open History

The National Bank Open in Montreal published its 2026 player entry list this week, and the number that defined it was 71. Of the top 72 ATP players in the world, 71 are confirmed for the Masters 1000 event running August 1-13. Tournament officials described it as the strongest field in the event's history (per National Bank Open official press release). That description has a ring of truth. Post-Wimbledon August in Montreal, with almost every elite player rested and looking for ranking points before the US Open, is one of the most appealing propositions the ATP calendar offers.

The headliners need little introduction. Jannik Sinner, who defended his Wimbledon title last week and leads the ATP rankings by 4,970 points over No. 2 Alexander Zverev, is confirmed. Sinner won the 2023 Canadian Open in Montreal — he knows the tournament, he knows the surface, and he arrives as the unambiguous favorite. Zverev, who won Roland-Garros in May and reached the Wimbledon final, is also in the field. So is Novak Djokovic, who reached the Wimbledon semifinal before losing to Sinner, and who has said publicly that Canada and the US Open are his targets for the back half of 2026.

Auger-Aliassime on Home Soil

One of the more compelling non-Sinner storylines belongs to Felix Auger-Aliassime, who is confirmed for his ninth appearance at the National Bank Open. Montreal is FAA's home tournament in a deeper sense than geography — he has trained there, grown there, and carried the expectations of Canadian tennis fans for most of his career. His 2026 season has included consecutive Wimbledon quarterfinals and consistent results across all surfaces. Playing before a home crowd at a Masters 1000 event, at 25, with a game that has matured considerably since his first National Bank Open appearance, he is a genuine contender (per CBC).

Wild cards go to Gaël Monfils, who is in the late stages of his career and remains one of the crowd favorites at any event he enters, and Gabriel Diallo, the young Canadian who has been developing steadily on the ATP Challenger circuit and is being given an early taste of Masters-level competition at home. Denis Shapovalov earns a direct entry via ranking. His return to Montreal will be watched closely by Canadian tennis fans who have seen his career defined by occasional brilliance and frequent injury.

The One Name Missing

The 72nd player on that list — the notable absence from what is otherwise a complete roll call of elite tennis — is Carlos Alcaraz. His wrist injury, diagnosed as tenosynovitis after he retired from the Barcelona Open on April 14, has now cost him Roland-Garros, Wimbledon, and the Canadian Open. Three months. Three major events. And still no official return date (per Tennis365).

A recent video from his training academy showed Alcaraz hitting — the first visible evidence of physical progress since his withdrawal — but his team has not offered a timeline. Tennis365 reported this week that there are growing concerns within tennis circles that his 2026 season may effectively be over. The framing is cautious, not conclusive, but the absence from the entry list removes another realistic return date from consideration. Canada is the last major August stop before Cincinnati, and Cincinnati is the last meaningful preparation for the US Open on August 24.

The ranking mathematics are significant. Alcaraz won the 2025 Wimbledon title and the 2025 Canadian Open — those points are expiring without defense. From No. 3 in the current standings, he will slide further as the weeks pass without competition. He is 23 years old and the career timeline is long, but the 2026 season is beginning to look like one that the injury claimed almost in full.

WTA Toronto

The women's draw in Toronto tells a similar story of depth: 72 of the top 75 WTA players are confirmed for the event, which runs on the same August 1-13 schedule. The WTA field includes some notable question marks — Victoria Mboko, Hailey Baptiste, and Dayana Yastremska have already withdrawn. Emma Raducanu's participation is uncertain following the stress fracture in her lower right leg that kept her out of Wimbledon. Draws for both the men's and women's fields are expected around July 29-30, with main draw play starting August 3 (per Tennis365).

What's Next

The draw for the National Bank Open will be released around July 29-30. Between now and then, the current clay swing — Bastad, Gstaad, and Umag through July 19 — will give several Montreal entrants match practice before the hard-court season begins. For Sinner, who has confirmed he is skipping the clay events entirely, the extra rest week before Canada is deliberate preparation for a tournament he has already won and wants to win again. For Alcaraz, the only remaining option in 2026 that would constitute a full return is a direct entry into the US Open. The window is narrowing.
