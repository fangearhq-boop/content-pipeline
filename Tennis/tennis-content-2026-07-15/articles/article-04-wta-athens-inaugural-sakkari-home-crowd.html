After 35 Years, WTA Tennis Returns to Athens — and Sakkari Is the Story

By Marcus Cole July 15, 2026

A New Tournament With Old History

The Vanda Pharmaceuticals Athens Open, which opened its first edition on Monday, is a new entry on the WTA 250 calendar — but the context it carries is not new at all. This is the first WTA event held in Athens in 35 years. The venue is the Athens Olympic Tennis Centre, the facility built for the 2004 Summer Olympics and now being used for the highest level of women's professional tennis for the first time in more than three decades (per Tennistonic, Tennisuptodate).

The tournament runs July 13-19 on outdoor hard courts and features a field anchored by top seed Clara Tauson, No. 2 seed Wang Xinyu, No. 3 seed Barbora Krejcikova, and No. 4 seed Maria Sakkari. It replaced the Jiangxi Open on the WTA schedule and fills a week that had been underserved on the calendar. For Athens, a city with a deep sporting culture and a passionate tennis following, the return of a WTA event carries genuine civic significance.

Sakkari's Home Crowd Moment

No result in the opening two days carried more local meaning than Sakkari's Day 1 win over Liudmila Kudermetova: 6-0, 7-6(1). The first-set score requires no interpretation. Sakkari, who was born in Athens and grew up in the Greek tennis system, played in front of her home crowd at a WTA event for what appears to be the first time in years. The crowd at the Athens Olympic Tennis Centre responded accordingly (per Tennistonic, WTVB).

Sakkari is currently seeded fourth at this event, which reflects her ranking position rather than her home-court advantage. On home soil, against a motivated but beatable field, she has the psychological edge that is difficult to quantify but undeniable in practice. The first-set 6-0 — against a quality player in Kudermetova — is a statement. She is playing loose, confident, and fast. If that holds through the second half of the week, she becomes the tournament favorite regardless of seeding.

The Rest of the Draw

Sakkari's result was the headline of Day 1, but it was not the only story. Zheng Qinwen, the 2024 Paris Olympic gold medalist, opened with an upset of sixth-seeded Bouzas Maneiro 7-5, 6-1 — a result that announces her as a potential disruptor in the bottom half of the draw. Zheng does not need a tournament like this to prove herself, but arriving with an opening-round win over a seeded player sends a clear message about her current form.

Tuesday delivered results from the top of the draw. No. 1 seed Clara Tauson defeated Hibino 7-5, 6-4. No. 3 seed Barbora Krejcikova was even cleaner, beating Viktoriya Tomova 6-3, 6-3 in what looked like the kind of focused, low-drama win that deep-running players often produce early in the week. Krejcikova, who won Wimbledon in 2024, has been one of the more consistent players on the tour over the past two seasons (per Tennisuptodate).

What a Win in Athens Would Mean

For Maria Sakkari, winning the inaugural Athens Open would represent something that has been missing from her career: a title on home soil. She has won five WTA titles across her career, including the prestigious Madrid Open in 2021, but none in Greece. The combination of an inaugural event and a home-crowd narrative creates the kind of moment that defines a career chapter rather than just a week.

The tournament is also significant for the city and for the WTA in terms of what it establishes. A successful first edition in Athens opens the door for the event to return next year with more visibility, more support, and potentially a stronger draw position on the calendar.

What's Next

The Vanda Pharmaceuticals Athens Open runs through July 19. Quarterfinals are expected Thursday and Friday, with the final on Sunday. Sakkari's path through the draw will be watched closely, but the field is deep enough that any of the top four seeds — Tauson, Wang, Krejcikova, or Sakkari — could emerge as champion. For the tennis world, the real result of this week is the establishment of a new WTA event in a city that has been waiting more than three decades for it.
