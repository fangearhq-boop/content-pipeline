Clay Courts Day 3: Droguet Stuns Blockx, Dimitrov Advances, Cobolli Enters Umag

By Ryan Calloway July 15, 2026

Umag: Droguet's Tactical Masterclass

The result that defined Tuesday in Umag was not subtle. French qualifier Maxime Droguet defeated fifth-seeded Zizou Blockx 3-6, 6-2, 6-0, and the scoreline does not misrepresent how it happened. After losing the first set, Droguet adjusted, found his drop shots, and won 77% of his first-serve points across the final two sets. Blockx, who was seeded and expected to advance, had no answer for a game plan that mixed pace changes with first-serve dominance (per ATP Tour).

Droguet's win sets up a second-round meeting with van Assche, who has never played him on clay. Today, the more anticipated match in Umag involves top seed Flavio Cobolli, who enters the draw for the first time — seeded players in smaller ATP events often receive first-round byes — and faces qualifier Roman Andres Burruchaga. Cobolli reached the Wimbledon quarterfinal last week before losing to Arthur Fery. Returning to clay, the surface where he won his first ATP title, is a different challenge, but a comfortable one. If Cobolli advances cleanly this week, he becomes one of the favorites in Umag.

Bastad: Dimitrov Wins, Wednesday Card Takes Shape

In Bastad, Grigor Dimitrov beat Dalibor Svrcina 7-6(5), 1-6, 6-4 on Tuesday to advance to the second round. Dimitrov is 35, and his clay game has always been built around variety rather than power — he reads clay court patterns well and adjusts between points in a way that younger players sometimes overlook. Wednesday on the Bastad center court will feature Dimitrov against Nuno Borges, a match that will say something about which of them is in better clay form heading into the rest of the summer (per Tennistonic).

Top seed Andrey Rublev also progressed, defeating Andrea Pellegrino 6-3, 6-2. Rublev came into the week looking to re-establish form after a quiet Wimbledon — Bastad on clay is the surface and tournament type where he typically produces strong results. The Wednesday card also features second seed Darderi against Altmaier and the sixth seed Van de Zandschulp against seventh seed Tirante, which is the most compressed seeded matchup of the day. One of those two seeds will be eliminated Wednesday.

Gstaad: Tsitsipas Back on Clay, Bublik Faces Test Today

At the EFG Swiss Open in Gstaad, Tuesday brought two clean results for higher seeds. Tsitsipas defeated fifth-seeded Buse 6-4, 6-4 to advance. Coming off a Wimbledon that did not go as he hoped, Gstaad on clay is where Tsitsipas historically rebuilds — the Swiss venue has been kind to him in previous years. Cerundolo also advanced in three sets over Zdenek Kolar, setting up a second-round meeting with Kecmanovic (per ATP Tour).

Wednesday's most watched Gstaad match is defending champion Alexander Bublik against Quentin Halys. Bublik earned his Gstaad title as a wild card last year and knows the venue well. Defending a title on clay the week after Wimbledon requires a specific kind of reset — grass and clay demand entirely different footwork and ball-striking approaches. Halys, who had a competitive R1 win to build momentum, will test whether Bublik can make that switch quickly enough. The other notable Gstaad match is Tsitsipas against Swiss wildcard Jerome Kym, which will attract a home crowd rooting for the local option.

What's Next

All three events — the Nordea Open in Bastad, the EFG Swiss Open in Gstaad, and the Plava Laguna Croatia Open in Umag — run through July 19. Quarterfinals are expected Thursday and Friday, with semifinals Saturday and finals Sunday. For several players entered in the Canadian Open next month, the clay swing is an opportunity to stay sharp and build match rhythm before the hard-court season begins. The week is still young, and the results are already interesting.
