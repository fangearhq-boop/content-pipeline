Roland Garros 2026: 13 Days Out, and the French Open Draw Just Got Wide Open

By Marcus Cole | May 12, 2026 | Roland Garros Preview

The calendar says 13 days. That is what separates the tennis world from Roland Garros qualifying, which begins May 18, with the main draw following on May 25. What was already shaping up as one of the most open French Opens in recent memory became considerably more uncertain after Tuesday in Rome — when Iga Swiatek retired from her quarterfinal against Elena Rybakina, adding another health question mark to a list that already included Carlos Alcaraz's wrist injury withdrawal.

On the men's side, the picture is perhaps the clearest it has ever been: Jannik Sinner is the overwhelming favourite. On the women's side, the picture has rarely been more opaque.

Men's Draw: Sinner's Path to the Career Grand Slam

Alcaraz, the two-time defending Roland Garros champion, will not be in Paris. He withdrew weeks ago with a right wrist injury — tendon inflammation and cartilage damage — and will miss the tournament he won back-to-back in 2024 and 2025. His absence removes the one player who had consistently pushed Sinner to his absolute limit on the biggest stages.

Novak Djokovic's participation remains uncertain. The 38-year-old Serb has been managing a right shoulder injury that kept him out of Miami, Monte Carlo, Madrid, and Rome. He is on the Roland Garros entry list and has publicly said his goal is to be ready for Paris, but each passing week without match play deepens the concern.

Into this vacuum steps Sinner. He is 25-0 in Masters 1000 matches in 2026 and has won five consecutive Masters 1000 titles — an Open Era first. His 30-match Masters 1000 winning streak is the second-longest in history. He has never won Roland Garros; it is the only Grand Slam missing from his collection. If he wins Rome this week and arrives in Paris at full health, stopping him will require either exceptional tennis or a day when nothing goes Sinner's way.

Alexander Zverev, who reached the Roland Garros final in 2024, is in form and represents the most realistic threat. Casper Ruud is a four-time Roland Garros finalist who knows how to play Paris clay. Both will be dangerous. But Sinner enters as a different class of favourite than any player has been at the French Open since Rafael Nadal's prime.

Women's Draw: Chaos and Opportunity

Aryna Sabalenka, the world No. 1, lost to Sorana Cirstea in the third round at Rome and is heading to Paris without the form momentum she would have wanted. Swiatek's retirement on Tuesday adds to the uncertainty. Both players are still expected to compete at Roland Garros — but the field that greets them in Paris looks far more capable of causing upsets than it did even a week ago.

Elena Rybakina has been the most consistent women's player in Rome and enters the semifinals in dominant form. The Kazakh has beaten Swiatek twice in recent months and her ball-striking on clay gives her advantages most WTA opponents cannot neutralize. Rybakina is targeting what would be a rare Australian Open and Roland Garros double — she has the game to do it.

Coco Gauff defends her 2025 Roland Garros title as the world No. 3. She saved a match point in the Rome round of 16 and still advances. Gauff's mental resilience under pressure — a quality she has developed steadily over the past three years — may be her greatest asset in Paris. The Roland Garros draw routinely rewards mental toughness over pure clay-court pedigree.

Do not overlook Mirra Andreeva. The 20-year-old Russian is the WTA's most productive clay player in 2026 and has been flawless in Rome. If she defeats Gauff on Tuesday, she becomes a genuine Paris contender with form nobody can dispute.

A Farewell at Porte d'Auteuil

One note of history marks the 2026 Roland Garros draw. Stan Wawrinka — who won the French Open title in 2015 and reached the final in 2017 — is making his 21st and final appearance at Roland Garros. Tournament organizers have confirmed that the Swiss veteran will be honored following his last match at Porte d'Auteuil. Wawrinka also received a wildcard for this year's Rome tournament. An era ends quietly, but with fitting ceremony.

What's Next

Rome concludes this Sunday, May 17. Roland Garros qualifying begins Monday, May 18. The main draw is set for Sunday, May 25, with the men's final on Sunday, June 7. Updates on Swiatek's health and Djokovic's participation status are expected in the days ahead.
