WTA Rome QF: Gauff vs. Andreeva and Cirstea's Cinderella Run Continues

By Ryan Calloway | May 12, 2026 | Italian Open, Rome

The women's quarterfinals at the Internazionali BNL d'Italia are delivering drama in pairs. With Elena Rybakina already through to the semifinals after Iga Swiatek retired in their morning match, Tuesday features two more quarterfinal contests that could define the shape of the Roman draw heading into a wide-open Roland Garros season.

In the afternoon, Sorana Cirstea takes on Jelena Ostapenko — a meeting between two players who have spent their careers doing things nobody expected. In the evening, under the lights on Campo Centrale, Coco Gauff and Mirra Andreeva meet for the fifth time in their careers, with the Roland Garros title implications hanging over every point.

Cirstea vs. Ostapenko: The Cinderella Collision

Sorana Cirstea is 36 years old and playing what she has announced will be her final season on the WTA Tour. She arrived at Rome not as a title contender but as a player savoring her last laps around the circuit. Then she beat world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka in the third round — the first win of her career over a world No. 1. Then she beat No. 9 seed Linda Noskova 6-2, 6-4 to reach the quarterfinals.

Ostapenko is a different kind of force — explosive, unpredictable, capable of hitting 40 winners or 40 errors in the same match. The Latvian, who won the 2017 Roland Garros title, has made five quarterfinals in Rome during her career. She reached this one by defeating Anna Kalinskaya 6-1, 6-2 in just 70 minutes, one of the most dominant performances of the week.

The head-to-head favors Cirstea, who has won three of the last four encounters between them. That record may provide comfort, but Cirstea will know that a player in Ostapenko's kind of form is a different proposition than any previous meeting. A Cirstea semifinal appearance at Rome would be the first of her career at this tournament. The Rome crowd has been roaring for her all week.

Gauff vs. Andreeva: The Rivalry That's Growing

Coco Gauff arrives at Tuesday's quarterfinal as both the higher-ranked player and the one carrying heavier Roland Garros baggage — she is the defending champion in Paris, having claimed her first French Open title in 2025. In Rome, she has survived more than she has dominated: a 5-7, 7-5, 6-2 comeback against Iva Jovic in the round of 16 included saving a match point, a reminder that even the best players can be pushed to the edge.

Mirra Andreeva, 20 years old and seeded eighth in Rome, has not been pushed at all. She dismissed Elise Mertens 6-3, 6-3 in the round of 16, clinical and composed throughout. Andreeva has been one of the tour's most consistent clay performers in 2026, accumulating more clay wins than any other player on tour heading into Rome. She enters Tuesday's quarterfinal full of confidence and with a game built for this surface.

The head-to-head is 4-0 in Gauff's favor, including three times on clay. That statistic matters — and also may create a psychological weight of expectation that neither player can quite ignore. Gauff has always found a way to win their meetings, even when Andreeva has played well. The question is whether Andreeva's confidence in Rome can finally tip the balance.

The winner advances to face Elena Rybakina in the semifinals. The other match — Cirstea vs. Ostapenko's winner — fills out the other bracket. At Roland Garros, both matchups become even bigger narratives.

What's Next

Cirstea vs. Ostapenko is scheduled first on Tuesday's WTA order of play. Gauff vs. Andreeva follows in the evening session on Campo Centrale. Rome semifinals are scheduled for Thursday. The women's final is set for Friday, May 15. Roland Garros qualifying begins May 18.
