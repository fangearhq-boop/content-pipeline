Rome ATP R16: Ruud Crushes Musetti, Tirante Stuns Medvedev, Zverev Through

By Elena Voss | May 12, 2026 | Italian Open, Rome

Tuesday's fourth-round action at the Internazionali BNL d'Italia produced the tournament's most significant upset yet and ended the home run of one of Italy's most popular players. With the quarterfinal bracket now filling in around Jannik Sinner, the picture is becoming clearer — and more interesting.

Ruud Ends Musetti's Run

Lorenzo Musetti's Italian Open is over, and it ended painfully. Casper Ruud defeated the 24-year-old Italian 6-3, 6-1 in a match that was derailed midway through the second set when Musetti took a medical timeout for a left thigh issue. The crowd at the Foro Italico, which had been willing Musetti to a deep run, fell quiet as their man walked off the court to receive treatment.

Even before the timeout, Ruud had been the better player. The Norwegian has been one of the most consistent performers on clay in 2026, and his preparation for Roland Garros appears well on track. He has not dropped a set through four matches in Rome, moving efficiently, defending well, and putting his forehand through at the right moments.

Ruud will face the winner of Sinner vs. Pellegrino in Wednesday's quarterfinals — almost certainly Sinner. His record against the world No. 1 on clay gives him few reasons for confidence, but Ruud is experienced enough at this level to know that form matters more than history at this stage of a tournament.

Tirante Upsets Medvedev

The day's biggest surprise came from Thiago Agustin Tirante, the Argentine qualifier who defeated Daniil Medvedev 6-3, 6-4 to reach the Rome quarterfinals. Medvedev, seeded third, has struggled on clay throughout his career — his flat ball-striking and upright movement never quite sync with the high-bouncing red dirt the way they do on the faster surfaces he dominates.

Tirante played with the assertiveness of a player who had nothing to lose. He moved Medvedev around the court, committed to his targets, and closed out the match without allowing a foothold in either set. It is the kind of result that typifies the later rounds of clay-season Masters events, where qualifiers with red clay in their DNA can outmaneuver players ranked far above them.

Tirante's reward is a quarterfinal against the winner of the other R16 matches. His run at Rome has already exceeded every reasonable expectation.

Zverev Advances

Alexander Zverev, the No. 2 seed, defeated Luciano Darderi to book his own quarterfinal spot. Zverev has been quietly building toward a run at Rome that could serve as a final major tune-up before Roland Garros, where he reached the final in 2024. The German's clay-court form in 2026 has been solid, and a semifinal or final appearance in Rome would represent a significant confidence boost for the Parisian swing's centerpiece event.

What's Next

The ATP quarterfinals are scheduled for Wednesday, May 13. Sinner vs. Pellegrino will take place Tuesday afternoon to complete the fourth-round slate. Thursday features the semifinals, with the men's final on Sunday, May 17. Whoever emerges from Rome's ATP bracket as champion will arrive at Roland Garros with significant momentum.
