### Long-Form Post Sinner Is Chasing Something Nobody Has Ever Done Paris. Indian Wells. Miami. Monte Carlo. Jannik Sinner has won four consecutive Masters 1000 titles, and no player in Open Era history has ever won five in a row. He enters the 2026 Mutua Madrid Open as the No. 1 seed, carrying a 17-match winning streak and one of the most remarkable runs of form the men's game has seen in years. The record is there for the taking. But there's a twist: Sinner has never won Madrid. Every title he's collected in this streak has been somewhere else. He's conquered Paris, the California desert, Miami's waterfront, and the cliffs above Monaco. Now he faces the Caja Mágica — a tournament that has eluded him. His draw is inviting. Five major names withdrew (Alcaraz, Djokovic, Fritz, Draper, Tiafoe), and Sinner opens against a qualifier or Benjamin Bonzi in the second round. But the pressure of history never makes things easier. Can he do what Federer, Nadal, and Djokovic never managed? Five in a row? ### Long-Form Post The Two Queens of Clay Are Both in Madrid Today Two of the sport's biggest names step onto clay this afternoon — and they're doing it on the same day at the Caja Mágica. Aryna Sabalenka is the No. 1 seed and three-time Madrid champion. She hasn't played since winning the Sunshine Double in Miami and returns to a surface where she's been nearly unstoppable in recent years. Her opponent today is Peyton Stearns, who beat Lois Boisson 6-1, 6-3 to earn this matchup. Stearns won't be intimidated. Iga Swiatek is the No. 4 seed and a two-time Madrid champion. She arrives in a new chapter: she recently hired Francisco Roig — the same coach who guided Rafael Nadal to 22 Grand Slam titles — and this is her first clay match in this new era. She faces Ukrainian qualifier Daria Snigur, who came through three qualifying rounds including a marathon extended-tiebreak win. If the draw holds, these two meet in the semifinals. That match alone could be the highlight of the clay swing. Who are you pulling for — Sabalenka or Swiatek? 🎾 --- ### Image Post Caption Sabalenka. Swiatek. Same venue. Same day. Same mission. ### Long-Form Post Alcaraz Is Waiting — and So Is the Tennis World Carlos Alcaraz is sitting at home with a wrist splint and a clock ticking toward Roland Garros. He injured his right wrist in the opening minutes of his Barcelona first-round match on April 14 against Otto Virtanen. He finished the match but hasn't played since. He withdrew from Barcelona. He withdrew from Madrid. And now he's waiting on medical tests that will tell him whether he can attempt to defend his French Open title. Roland Garros begins May 25. That's five weeks away. Even in the best-case scenario — tests come back clean, the injury heals quickly — he'd arrive in Paris without a competitive match on clay since his abbreviated Barcelona R1. "If I push to play Roland-Garros, it could harm me for the tournaments that follow," Alcaraz said. "I'd rather come back maybe a little later, but in great shape, than come back quickly and risk making this injury worse." The test results are expected within days. Four Grand Slam titles at 22 years old. A title to defend. A wrist that hasn't cleared yet. Are you worried about Alcaraz's French Open? 🙏 --- ### Image Post Caption Wrist splint. ### Long-Form Post Madrid Day 1: Berrettini Exits, Prizmic Sets Up Shelton Showdown Day 1 of the Madrid Open ATP draw did not follow Matteo Berrettini's recent script. The Italian wildcard — who destroyed Medvedev 6-0, 6-0 at Monte Carlo in one of the season's most stunning results — was on the wrong end of a controlled performance on Wednesday. Dino Prizmic, a 20-year-old Croatian, dispatched him 6-3, 6-4 and earned a second-round date with No. 4 seed Ben Shelton. Shelton arrives fresh off his Munich ATP 500 clay title — the first American to win a 500-level clay event since Agassi in Rome in 2002. The Prizmic-Shelton matchup will be their first career meeting and is shaping up as one of the most intriguing second-round clashes of the week. Hubert Hurkacz (beat Faria 6-3, 6-3) and Tomas Machac (rallied to beat Comesana 3-6, 7-6, 6-3) also advanced. On the WTA side Tuesday, Venus Williams (45) lost to 20-year-old Spanish wildcard Kaitlin Quevedo 6-2, 6-4 and Paula Badosa fell to Julia Grabher on home clay.
