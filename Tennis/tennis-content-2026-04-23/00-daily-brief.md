### STORY 1: Sinner Hunts Unprecedented 5th Consecutive Masters 1000 Title at Madrid
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** Player Feature | **Platforms:** X, FB, Article
Jannik Sinner enters the 2026 Mutua Madrid Open (April 22–May 3) seeking to do something no player has ever done in the Open Era: win five consecutive Masters 1000 titles. He has already claimed four in a row — Paris (Nov 2025), Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte Carlo — and since a quarterfinal loss in Doha in February, has won 17 consecutive matches. He is the No. 1 seed in Madrid, facing Benjamin Bonzi or Titouan Droguet in the second round. Sinner is 0-for-Madrid in titles so far in his career, adding an extra layer of motivation. With Alcaraz, Djokovic, Fritz, Draper, and Tiafoe all absent, the draw has cracked open in front of him.

### STORY 3: Alcaraz Wrist Update — French Open Increasingly in Doubt
**Tier:** 1 | **Pillar:** Player News | **Platforms:** X, FB, Article
Carlos Alcaraz is awaiting crucial medical test results that will determine whether he can play Roland Garros, which begins May 25. The reigning French Open champion injured his right wrist in the first set of his Barcelona R1 match against Otto Virtanen on April 14 and has since withdrawn from both Barcelona and Madrid. He is wearing a wrist splint. Alcaraz stated: "If I push to play Roland-Garros, it could harm me for the tournaments that follow. I'd rather come back maybe a little later, but in great shape, than come back quickly and risk making this injury worse." He has declined to give a return timeline. The test results are expected within days.
