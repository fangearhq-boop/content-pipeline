Two of the sport's biggest names step onto clay this afternoon — and they're doing it on the same day at the Caja Mágica.

If the draw holds, these two meet in the semifinals. That match alone could be the highlight of the clay swing.

### STORY 3: Alcaraz Wrist — French Open Defense in Real Danger

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM ET

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM ET)**

Alcaraz Is Waiting — and So Is the Tennis World

Carlos Alcaraz is sitting at home with a wrist splint and a clock ticking toward Roland Garros.

He injured his right wrist in the opening minutes of his Barcelona first-round match on April 14 against Otto Virtanen. He finished the match but hasn't played since. He withdrew from Barcelona. He withdrew from Madrid. And now he's waiting on medical tests that will tell him whether he can attempt to defend his French Open title.

Roland Garros begins May 25. That's five weeks away. Even in the best-case scenario — tests come back clean, the injury heals quickly — he'd arrive in Paris without a competitive match on clay since his abbreviated Barcelona R1.

"If I push to play Roland-Garros, it could harm me for the tournaments that follow," Alcaraz said. "I'd rather come back maybe a little later, but in great shape, than come back quickly and risk making this injury worse."

The test results are expected within days. Four Grand Slam titles at 22 years old. A title to defend. A wrist that hasn't cleared yet.

#### Long-Form Post -- 9:00 PM ET

**Long-Form Post (9:00 PM ET)**

Madrid Day 1: Berrettini Exits, Prizmic Sets Up Shelton Showdown

Day 1 of the Madrid Open ATP draw did not follow Matteo Berrettini's recent script. The Italian wildcard — who destroyed Medvedev 6-0, 6-0 at Monte Carlo in one of the season's most stunning results — was on the wrong end of a controlled performance on Wednesday. Dino Prizmic, a 20-year-old Croatian, dispatched him 6-3, 6-4 and earned a second-round date with No. 4 seed Ben Shelton.

Shelton arrives fresh off his Munich ATP 500 clay title — the first American to win a 500-level clay event since Agassi in Rome in 2002. The Prizmic-Shelton matchup will be their first career meeting and is shaping up as one of the most intriguing second-round clashes of the week.

Hubert Hurkacz (beat Faria 6-3, 6-3) and Tomas Machac (rallied to beat Comesana 3-6, 7-6, 6-3) also advanced. On the WTA side Tuesday, Venus Williams (45) lost to 20-year-old Spanish wildcard Kaitlin Quevedo 6-2, 6-4 and Paula Badosa fell to Julia Grabher on home clay.
