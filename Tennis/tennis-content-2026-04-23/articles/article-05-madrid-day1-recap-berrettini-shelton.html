Madrid Day 1 ATP: Berrettini Falls to Prizmic, Shelton Showdown Looms

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

April 23, 2026

The first day of the Madrid Open ATP draw did not follow the narrative that Matteo Berrettini's recent form suggested. The Italian wildcard — who produced one of the most stunning results of the clay season with his 6-0, 6-0 destruction of Daniil Medvedev at Monte Carlo — was on the wrong end of a controlled performance on Wednesday. Dino Prizmic, a 20-year-old Croatian, dispatched Berrettini 6-3, 6-4 and earned himself a second-round meeting with No. 4 seed Ben Shelton.

The contrast is striking. Berrettini looked like a different player against Medvedev, dictating every rally and giving the Russian nowhere to go. Against Prizmic — a patient, disciplined young opponent who refused to let Berrettini play entirely on his own terms — the Italian found himself in long service games and unable to recreate the aggression that made Monte Carlo so memorable.

Prizmic vs. Shelton: A First-Time Showdown

Dino Prizmic now faces Ben Shelton in the second round — a first-career meeting between the two players. Shelton arrives at Madrid following his Munich ATP 500 title, where he became the first American to win a 500-level clay event since Andre Agassi in Rome in 2002. He arrives on the back of significant clay-court momentum and is seeded No. 4 in Madrid.

Prizmic, who has been climbing the rankings steadily in 2026, offers a very different challenge than the players Shelton has beaten on clay in recent weeks. The Croatian is tactically savvy, handles pace well, and does not tend to give free points. Shelton's power game will be tested on the altitude clay of the Caja Mágica.

Hurkacz and Machac Advance in Contrasting Style

Two of the men's draw's more dangerous floaters advanced on Day 1 in very different fashion. Hubert Hurkacz was controlled and efficient, defeating Jaime Faria 6-3, 6-3 — a clean, unspectacular win that sets him up well for the deeper rounds. Hurkacz was a finalist at Monte Carlo in 2025 and enters Madrid knowing the surface and the pressure that comes later in the week.

Tomas Machac was pushed harder. The Czech lost the first set to Francisco Comesana before steadying in a second-set tiebreak and closing the match in the third, winning 3-6, 7-6(3), 6-3. It was a test of temperament that Machac ultimately passed, though he will need to be more consistent against stronger opposition.

WTA Day 1: Venus Exits, Badosa Falls at Home

Tuesday's WTA first-round action (Day 1 of the women's draw) produced two notable departures. Venus Williams, competing at 45 years old on the clay-court swing, lost to 20-year-old Spanish wildcard Kaitlin Quevedo 6-2, 6-4. It was a result that underlined the generational gap but also showed Quevedo's composure — beating a seven-time Grand Slam champion in your home tournament crowd is no small thing.

Paula Badosa, the former world No. 2 and a wild card in her home event, fell to Julia Grabher 7-6(3), 4-6, 6-0. Badosa took the first set in a tiebreak and won the second, before Grabher ran away with the decider. It was a difficult result for the Spanish crowd, who had hoped to see Badosa advance on home clay.

Grabher now moves into the second round to face Leylah Fernandez.

What's Next

Day 3 at the Mutua Madrid Open brings the biggest names into action — Sabalenka, Swiatek, and the continued rollout of seeded players across both draws. Shelton vs. Prizmic shapes up as one of the most intriguing second-round ATP matches of the week, with the winner positioning themselves as a potential quarterfinal threat. The tournament finds its rhythm over the next two days as the full depth of the draw comes into focus.
