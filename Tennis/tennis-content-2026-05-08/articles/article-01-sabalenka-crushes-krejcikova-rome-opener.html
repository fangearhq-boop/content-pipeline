Sabalenka Crushes Krejcikova 6-2, 6-3 in Dominant Rome Opener

By Elena Voss | May 8, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Aryna Sabalenka needed precisely 84 minutes to make her position clear at the Italian Open. The world No. 1 dismantled two-time Grand Slam champion Barbora Krejcikova 6-2, 6-3 Thursday at the Foro Italico, delivering a performance that left little doubt about who is in command of the clay-court season entering its final stretch before Roland Garros.

Krejcikova, the 2021 French Open and 2024 Wimbledon champion, broke Sabalenka's serve in the opening game of the match. It would be the last time she held any meaningful advantage. Sabalenka broke back immediately, reeled off the next four games to close the first set 6-2, and never let Krejcikova breathe in the second.

A Statement From the No. 1

Sabalenka arrived in Rome on a 27-1 record for the 2026 season, a record that already stands as one of the most dominant single-season starts in recent WTA history. Thursday's 26-winner performance moved that mark to 28-1.

The manner of the win mattered as much as the scoreline. Sabalenka did not allow Krejcikova to establish any rhythm on the Foro Italico's red clay. Her serve was clean, her groundstrokes were aggressive, and her movement was sharp throughout. When Krejcikova tried to redirect the ball crosscourt in the second set, Sabalenka was already there to close out the point.

"I'm super happy to be through," Sabalenka said after the match. "She's a great player. We've had a lot of tough matches in the past. I'm happy with the level I played and to get this win."

The acknowledgment of past close matches was accurate. Krejcikova has been one of the more competitive opponents Sabalenka has faced across multiple surfaces. Thursday was not one of those occasions.

The Elusive Rome Title

One persistent storyline follows Sabalenka to Rome every year: she has never won the title. In eight previous Italian Open appearances, the Belarusian has not lifted the trophy at the Foro Italico despite regularly reaching deep in the draw.

That absence from her career win list stands out when compared to her accomplishments elsewhere. Sabalenka has won Australian Open titles, US Open titles, and multiple Masters 1000 events across the calendar. Rome has remained unfinished business.

If the opening-round performance against Krejcikova offers any indication, 2026 may be the year that changes. The level of play she brought Thursday is consistent with a player who has already won 28 of 29 matches this season and shows no sign of leveling off as the clay season reaches its most important two weeks.

The Road to Roland Garros

Sabalenka's win puts her into the third round against Sorana Cirstea, a resilient Romanian who will provide a different test than Krejcikova's Grand Slam power game. Cirstea's experience on clay and her ability to construct points from deep behind the baseline means Sabalenka will need to maintain her level rather than coast on reputation.

The broader context is Roland Garros, where the main draw opens May 25. Rome is the final WTA 1000 before the French Open, meaning every result here shapes the pecking order for Paris. Sabalenka reached the 2025 Roland Garros final, making her one of the tournament's leading contenders alongside defending champion Coco Gauff and a resurgent field that includes a 13-clay-win season from No. 7 seed Mirra Andreeva.

An 84-minute dispatch of a two-time Grand Slam champion in round two is not a bad way to start that final preparation.

What's Next

Sabalenka faces Sorana Cirstea in the Italian Open third round. The WTA Italian Open continues through May 17 at the Foro Italico in Rome. Roland Garros qualifying begins May 18 with the main draw opening May 25. Seedings for Roland Garros are based on WTA rankings as of May 18.
