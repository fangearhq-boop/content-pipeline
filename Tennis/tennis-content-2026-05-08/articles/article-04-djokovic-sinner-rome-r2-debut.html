Djokovic and Sinner Enter Rome Tonight: The Two Biggest Names in Men's Tennis Take the Stage

By Elena Voss | May 8, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Friday evening at the Foro Italico represents the moment ATP Rome has been building toward since the draw was released. Novak Djokovic enters the main draw against Croatian qualifier Dino Prizmic. Jannik Sinner opens his Italian Open campaign against Austrian Sebastian Ofner. The sport's two most magnetic figures step onto the clay at the same time, on the same evening, in the same city.

For different reasons, both matches carry weight beyond a standard second-round result.

Djokovic: The Return That Rome Has Been Waiting For

Novak Djokovic has not played a match on clay since his shoulder injury ended his Indian Wells campaign on March 12. That absence — nearly two months of silence from the most decorated player in Masters 1000 history — has made his Rome appearance the most watched return story of the clay season.

The six-time Rome champion has been methodical in his preparation. He practiced at the Piazza del Popolo earlier this week in a public session that drew thousands, then confirmed he was ready for competition. "I am ready enough to compete," Djokovic said, a statement that captured both the confidence and the honest acknowledgment that full match sharpness only comes from match play.

His opponent, Dino Prizmic, is a 21-year-old Croatian qualifier who has already earned his spot in this match. Prizmic dismissed Marton Fucsovics in the first round and arrives with his own precedent: at the 2024 Australian Open, Prizmic took a set from Djokovic before the Serbian advanced. He is not a player who will be intimidated by the occasion.

Still, the history reads clearly in Djokovic's favor. The Serb is a 40-time Masters 1000 champion and the most accomplished clay-court player in the history of the ATP outside Rafael Nadal. His shoulder is the variable. His tennis, when healthy, is not in question.

Sinner: Homecoming Under the Weight of History

For Jannik Sinner, Friday's second-round match against Sebastian Ofner is the beginning of something he has been building toward for three years. Ofner reached this stage after defeating Alex Michelsen 6-3, 6-3 in the first round and will face a very different challenge against the world No. 1.

Sinner arrives at the Foro Italico with a 28-match Masters 1000 winning streak — the longest active streak in the series — and the chance to win a title that holds unique personal significance. No Italian player has won the Rome singles title since Adriano Panatta in 1976. Fifty years of Italian players have reached for the trophy and fallen short. Sinner, who has already won three Grand Slam titles and five consecutive Masters 1000 events, is the most credible Italian challenger that tournament has ever seen.

The Rome crowd will feel that every time he steps onto the Foro Italico courts. That kind of expectation is a variable no statistics table can fully account for.

Also on Friday: Zverev Opens Against Altmaier

No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev faces German compatriot Daniel Altmaier in the afternoon session on Campo Centrale. Altmaier advanced from the first round after saving two match points against Zhang Zhizhen. Zverev, a two-time Rome finalist, has been one of the tour's more consistent clay performers in 2026 and enters the event as a legitimate title threat if Sinner falters.

Friday at the Foro Italico is not a light day of tennis. It is the day the ATP Italian Open becomes the tournament everyone came to watch.

What's Next

Djokovic vs. Prizmic and Sinner vs. Ofner are both scheduled for Friday evening at the Foro Italico in Rome. Zverev vs. Altmaier is scheduled for the afternoon session on Campo Centrale. The ATP Italian Open runs through May 17. Roland Garros qualifying begins May 18.
