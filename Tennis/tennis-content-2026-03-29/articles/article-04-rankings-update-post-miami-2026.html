Post-Miami Rankings: Sabalenka Breaks All-Time No. 1 Streak, Gauff Passes Swiatek

By Tennis Fanrecap | March 29, 2026

The 2026 Miami Open did not just redistribute trophies. It reshuffled the rankings on both tours, with one number standing above all others: 76.

That is how many consecutive weeks Aryna Sabalenka has now held the WTA world No. 1 ranking following her Miami title. By reaching that figure, she has surpassed Iga Swiatek's all-time record for the longest continuous reign at the top of the women's game. It is a milestone that reflects not just results, but sustained excellence across conditions, continents, and opponents.

WTA: A Reshuffled Top Four

Sabalenka's 76th week at the top is the headline, but the rankings movement below her is equally significant. Coco Gauff's run to the Miami final earned her 650 WTA points, which was enough to lift her from No. 4 to No. 3, leapfrogging Iga Swiatek in the process.

Swiatek, who did not play Miami, drops to No. 4. The Polish former world No. 1 — who held the top ranking for over 100 weeks before Sabalenka's rise — now finds herself on the outside of a top three occupied by Sabalenka, Elena Rybakina, and Gauff. The clay season represents a significant opportunity for Swiatek to reclaim ground; red clay has historically been her most dominant surface.

Gauff's rise to No. 3 reflects a quiet but significant season. The American has not won a title yet in 2026, but her consistency and depth of run across multiple events have accumulated. At 21, she is positioned to challenge for the top ranking in the coming months.

ATP: Lehečka's Historic Jump

On the men's side, the story is Jiří Lehečka. The 24-year-old Czech arrived in Miami ranked No. 21 with one career Masters 1000 quarterfinal on his record. He leaves as a finalist, ranked at a career-high No. 12 — a jump of nine places in a single week.

The achievement reflects the breadth of his Miami campaign. He did not lose his serve once in five matches before the final. His wins over Tommy Paul, Arthur Fils (6-2, 6-2 in the semifinal), and his extended run through a competitive draw placed him squarely among the elite. The No. 12 ranking is not a fluke — it is the first clear signal that Lehečka can compete at the highest level.

Jannik Sinner remains ATP No. 1. His Miami title does not change his ranking position but significantly alters the competitive narrative heading into clay season. Novak Djokovic remains No. 3 despite months without competitive tennis — a reflection of his accumulated points from 2025 that have not yet expired.

What's Next

The clay season begins with Monte Carlo (April 5-12), Madrid (April 22-May 3), and Rome (May 7-18) before Roland Garros (May 24 onwards). The rankings picture will shift dramatically across those events, as last year's clay-season points come up for defense.

Alcaraz — who won Monte Carlo, Rome, and Roland Garros in 2025 — faces the most significant points defense of anyone on tour. If Sinner can match or exceed his spring results from last year while Alcaraz drops points, a genuine No. 1 battle could emerge by the time Roland Garros begins. The next six weeks will be among the most consequential of the 2026 season.
