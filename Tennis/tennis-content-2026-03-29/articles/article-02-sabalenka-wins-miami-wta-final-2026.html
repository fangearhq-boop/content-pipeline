Sabalenka Battles Past Gauff in Three Sets to Complete Women's Sunshine Double

By Tennis Fanrecap | March 29, 2026

Aryna Sabalenka is a Miami Open champion for the second consecutive year — and on Saturday evening, she joined a list of legends in doing it. The world No. 1 defeated Coco Gauff 6-2, 4-6, 6-3 in 2 hours 9 minutes at Hard Rock Stadium to complete the WTA Sunshine Double, becoming only the fifth woman in history to sweep Indian Wells and Miami in the same season.

The match was not easy, and Gauff made sure of that. The American fought back from a first-set deficit to take the second set 6-4, forcing a decider in front of a record crowd of 16,380 — the largest audience ever to watch a WTA final in North America. But Sabalenka held firm in the third, breaking early and controlling the match to its conclusion.

The First Two Sets: Dominance, Then a Fight

Sabalenka opened the match as she has opened most of her Miami campaign — with power, aggression, and precision. The first set was a demonstration of her best tennis: she took it 6-2 in under 40 minutes, winning 73% of first-serve points and never facing a break point.

The second set told a different story. Gauff, who had produced one of the most dominant semifinal performances of the tournament (a 6-1, 6-1 destruction of Karolína Muchová), found her range in the second set. She absorbed Sabalenka's power and redirected it, producing the kind of all-court tennis that makes her dangerous against anyone. She took the set 6-4, and for a stretch, the match felt genuinely open.

Sabalenka's zero double faults — against Gauff's seven — reflected the margin in serve consistency on the day. That detail mattered in the third set.

The Third Set: Sabalenka's Mental Edge

The decider is where champions prove themselves, and Sabalenka proved herself. She broke Gauff's serve early in the third set and, from that moment, never looked back. The level from both players remained high, but Sabalenka's ability to raise her intensity at the most critical moments — a trait that has defined her career — was decisive. She closed out the match 6-3.

The win moved Sabalenka's 2026 season record to 23-1 — the most wins of any player on either tour. She has now won four of her last five tournaments, including two Masters 1000 events. Her 24th career WTA title puts her among the most decorated active players in the sport.

History: The Fifth in a Short List

Sabalenka is now the fifth woman to complete the WTA Sunshine Double, joining Steffi Graf, Kim Clijsters, Victoria Azarenka, and Iga Swiatek. The feat requires winning back-to-back on the American hard courts — a test of physical endurance and mental resilience that spans several weeks and two demanding draws.

Gauff pushed her all the way — H2H stands 7-6 in Sabalenka's favor — and the rivalry between these two players has consistently produced the best women's tennis of the current era. Saturday's three-set final was their finest match yet.

What's Next

Both players now head into the European clay season. The French Open — Roland Garros — is the ultimate clay-court prize, and both Sabalenka and Gauff will be among the top contenders. Sabalenka has continued to develop on clay; Gauff's all-court game has always translated well to the red surface.

Before Roland Garros, the clay season begins with tournaments in Madrid, Rome, and Warsaw/Stuttgart. The title at Miami gives Sabalenka considerable momentum and confidence as she looks to add a Roland Garros title to her Australian Open pedigree. The H2H with Gauff will continue to develop — and their next clay-court meeting will carry the weight of everything that came before.
