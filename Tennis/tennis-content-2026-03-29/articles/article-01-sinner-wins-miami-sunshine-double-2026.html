Sinner Completes the Sunshine Double With 6-2, 6-2 Demolition of Lehečka

By Tennis Fanrecap | March 29, 2026

Jannik Sinner is the 2026 Miami Open champion, and he made history in the process. The world No. 2 defeated Jiří Lehečka 6-2, 6-2 on Sunday afternoon at Hard Rock Stadium to complete the Sunshine Double — sweeping both the Indian Wells and Miami titles in the same calendar year. He is the first man to achieve the feat since Roger Federer did it in 2017.

The final lasted just over an hour. Sinner was relentless from the first point, and Lehečka — who had been the tournament's most dominant server, never losing his serve across five matches heading into the final — had no answer for the Italian's baseline control and precision.

A Campaign Without Cracks

Sinner did not drop a single set in his entire Miami campaign. Six matches, six straight-sets victories. His opponents included Alexander Zverev, whom he defeated 6-3, 7-6(4) in the semifinal. Even when Zverev forced a tiebreak in the second set, Sinner won it 7-4 without drama.

The final against Lehečka underscored just how complete Sinner's game has become. The Czech had one of the most remarkable service statistics in recent Masters memory — not broken once in five matches before Sunday. Against Sinner, the streak ended abruptly. Sinner broke the Lehečka serve multiple times and never gave the Czech a chance to settle.

The H2H between the two is now 4-0 in Sinner's favor, and he has not dropped a set in any of those four meetings.

The Sunshine Double — A Rare Achievement

Indian Wells and Miami are the two biggest hard-court events outside the Grand Slams. Winning both in the same year requires sustained excellence across six weeks, two different draws, and the pressure of being a target every match.

Federer accomplished it in 2017. Before him, the feat belonged to Andre Agassi (twice) and a small handful of others. Sinner now joins that company. His 2026 season has been the response of a champion to a 2025 that included a doping controversy — ultimately cleared — that shadowed his otherwise exceptional form. What he has produced in the first quarter of 2026 leaves no room for ambiguity.

The win also narrows the gap at the top of the ATP rankings. Alcaraz, who exited Miami early against Sebastian Korda, will defend approximately 4,300 points on clay this spring. Sinner arrives at the clay season on the best form of his career.

Lehečka's Remarkable Run

Sunday's result does not diminish what Jiří Lehečka accomplished this week. The 24-year-old Czech entered Miami unseeded at No. 21 and reached his first Masters 1000 final with performances that were, at times, extraordinary. He did not lose his serve in the tournament until the final itself — a remarkable run of service dominance in a draw full of elite returners.

His wins over Arthur Fils (6-2, 6-2 in the semifinal) and Tommy Paul en route established him as a genuine threat. The Miami run will lift him to a career-high ATP ranking of No. 12 — and signal to the tour that the Czech serves as a serious weapon.

In the final, Sinner was simply better. That is not a failure. That is the nature of where Sinner currently sits in the sport.

What's Next

The clay season begins at the Monte Carlo Masters, April 5-12 at the Monte Carlo Country Club. Sinner carries his Miami momentum onto red clay, where Carlos Alcaraz is the defending champion and a dominant force. The Sinner-Alcaraz rivalry on clay — if they meet in Monte Carlo — will be the most anticipated match of the spring.

Sinner's record at Monte Carlo stands at 10-4 career. He has never won the title there. After what he has produced in the last six weeks, that gap on his resume feels closer than ever to being closed.
