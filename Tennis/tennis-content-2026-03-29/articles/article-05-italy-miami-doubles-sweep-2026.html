Italy Wins Three of Four Miami Open Titles — A Historic Haul for Italian Tennis

By Tennis Fanrecap | March 29, 2026

When Jannik Sinner lifted the men's singles trophy Sunday afternoon, it completed an extraordinary week for Italian tennis. Sinner won the ATP singles title. Simone Bolelli and Andrea Vavassori won the ATP doubles. Sara Errani and Jasmine Paolini won the WTA doubles. Three of the four Miami Open titles returned to Italy — an achievement without modern precedent at a Masters 1000 event.

Bolelli and Vavassori: A First Masters 1000 Title

Simone Bolelli and Andrea Vavassori have been one of the ATP tour's most consistent doubles partnerships over the past two seasons. On Saturday at Hard Rock Stadium, they converted that consistency into a first Masters 1000 title, defeating Harri Heliövaara and Henry Patten 6-4, 6-2 in the final.

The win was comfortable in scoreline but reflective of a partnership that has gradually improved its big-match composure. Bolelli, 38, adds a Masters 1000 doubles title to a career that has included a Grand Slam doubles title at the Australian Open. Vavassori, 28, earns the biggest title of his career. Together, they formed the most reliable Italian doubles unit since the Heliövaara-era pairings of the late 2010s.

Errani and Paolini: Olympic Gold Medalists Continue to Dominate

If Bolelli and Vavassori's win was about reaching a peak, Sara Errani and Jasmine Paolini's doubles victory was about extending a remarkable run of dominance that began with Paris 2024.

Errani and Paolini won Olympic doubles gold at the Paris Games — one of the most celebrated moments in Italian tennis history. Since then, they have continued to compete as a pair, winning major titles on the WTA doubles circuit. The Miami WTA doubles title, won over Katerina Siniaková and Taylor Townsend in Sunday's final, adds a Masters 1000 crown to their Olympic pedigree.

Errani, at 38, has compiled one of the most remarkable doubles careers in women's tennis — her combination of court intelligence, consistency, and chemistry with Paolini shows no signs of fading. Paolini's versatility across singles and doubles makes her one of the most complete players currently on tour.

The Broader Picture: Italian Tennis at Its Peak

Italy's three Miami titles in a single week is a snapshot of a national tennis program that has been building toward this kind of performance for years. The development pathway that produced Sinner has also produced a generation of players — Matteo Berrettini, Lorenzo Musetti, Lorenzo Sonego, Vavassori, Jasmine Paolini — who are capable of winning titles at the highest level.

In the 1990s, Italy produced occasional champions. In the 2020s, it is producing champions consistently, across surfaces, and now across disciplines. The Miami week will be remembered as one of the defining moments of Italian tennis's modern era.

What's Next

The doubles tour moves toward the clay season. Bolelli and Vavassori will be among the seeded pairs at Monte Carlo, Madrid, and Rome. Errani and Paolini, despite their ages, continue to pick and choose their doubles schedule strategically — Roland Garros, where both have significant title hopes in singles and doubles, remains the spring target.

For Italian tennis broadly, the focus shifts back to Sinner and the clay season. His Sunshine Double has raised expectations for the spring. If Italy can add a Roland Garros title to a Miami hat trick, the 2026 clay season will mark the high point of Italian tennis history.
