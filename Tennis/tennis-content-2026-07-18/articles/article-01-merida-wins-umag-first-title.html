Daniel Merida Claims First ATP Title at Croatia Open Umag

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 18, 2026 — Umag, Croatia

Daniel Merida of Spain is an ATP Tour champion. On Saturday in Umag, in just his seventh career tour-level event, the 22-year-old defeated Damir Dzumhur of Bosnia and Herzegovina 6-4, 6-1 to win the Croatia Open and claim the first ATP title of his career.

The number that will follow Merida from this week: he is only the third-youngest finalist in Umag this decade. The two ahead of him are Carlos Alcaraz and Jannik Sinner. That is the company his name now shares in the Umag record books, and it is a list that does not get compiled by accident.

Dzumhur, 34 and ranked No. 108, arrived at the final having earned it as dramatically as anyone could. His quarterfinal against Matteo Arnaldi lasted 3 hours and 42 minutes, included a threatened walkout over an electronic line-call dispute, and ended in a third-set tiebreak he took by staying when everything in him wanted to leave. His semifinal win gave him his first Umag final in four attempts at the tournament.

All of it ran out on Saturday against a composed, focused Merida.

How the Final Unfolded

Merida took control of the first set without drama, breaking through when the opportunities came and serving cleanly to hold. He closed it 6-4 against a Dzumhur who never quite found the form that had carried him through the week.

The second set was faster. Merida built on his lead early and kept Dzumhur pinned behind the baseline, taking the set 6-1. The final was decided in straight sets, efficiently and without the kind of third-set tension that had defined much of Dzumhur's week. Some finals come down to inches. This one did not.

For Dzumhur, the week still represented something important: a first ATP Tour final at 34, a first Umag semifinal in four tries, a run that reminded everyone on the tour why he has been competing at this level for as long as he has. The scoreline in the final is not the whole story. But it is the result.

What Merida's Name Means in Umag

The Croatia Open Umag has been a tournament that has produced notable moments for young players. Alcaraz won here early in his breakthrough period. Sinner made his run. Now Merida's name sits in the same section of the record book.

Earlier in 2026, Merida reached the final at the ATP event in Bucharest, giving him a second ATP final result before most players his age have accumulated significant tour experience. He has been learning fast.

Winning is different from reaching. Saturday, Merida showed the difference.

He is 22, on clay, in Croatia, with a first ATP title. The next question is what comes next.

What's Next

The post-Wimbledon clay swing continues through the weekend, with finals also scheduled Sunday in Bastad and Gstaad. For Merida, the immediate focus will be recovering and planning his next events on the ATP calendar. With a title now on his record and his ranking set to jump, his options expand.

For Dzumhur, the road continues. He has been on tour long enough to know a result like this week is worth something even when the final ends at 6-1. The next event is somewhere on the calendar. He will be there.
