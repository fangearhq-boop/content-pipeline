Cerundolo and Shevchenko Set Gstaad Final After Ending Tsitsipas's Week

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 18, 2026 — Gstaad, Switzerland

After a week that eliminated the defending champion, two former Gstaad winners, and seed after seed, the EFG Swiss Open has its final. On Sunday, Juan Manuel Cerundolo of Argentina will face Alexander Shevchenko of Russia in a Gstaad title match that nobody predicted when the draw was announced.

Both players advanced through Saturday's semifinals. Cerundolo defeated Raphael Collignon 3-6, 6-3, 7-5 in a match that required all three sets. Shevchenko was more decisive: he eliminated No. 3 seed Stefanos Tsitsipas 7-6(5), 6-2, winning the tiebreak and then closing the second set without drama.

Tsitsipas's loss is the result that will generate the most immediate discussion. After advancing past Arthur Rinderknech earlier in the week, the Greek player was in position to reach a Gstaad final. Shevchenko did not let that happen.

Shevchenko's Statement Win

To beat a No. 3 seed in a Grand Slam-qualifying ATP tournament and advance to a final takes a particular kind of performance. Shevchenko delivered it.

He took the opening tiebreak, a sequence that often defines how the rest of a match unfolds. With the tiebreak secured, he used the second set to press his advantage, taking it 6-2. Tsitsipas, who had shown strong form in the earlier rounds, could not find a way back.

For Shevchenko, the Gstaad final represents the biggest match of his career and a significant moment for a player who has been working his way up the ATP rankings.

Cerundolo's Road Through the Chaos

Juan Manuel Cerundolo had already been through the week's best stories before Saturday. His quarterfinal win over Casper Ruud — a two-time Gstaad champion — on Friday was part of the tournament's day of triple shocks that also eliminated defending champion Bublik and then-seeded Rinderknech.

Cerundolo emerged from that Friday as the top seed remaining in the draw. He then had to back it up against Collignon in the semifinal, losing the first set before finding his level to win the next two. The Argentina-flag final is not yet written in the record books, but Cerundolo's path here has been legitimate.

What's Next

The Gstaad final is Sunday, July 19. One of these two players will win their first title at the EFG Swiss Open. For Cerundolo, it would validate a week where the biggest names fell and he kept winning. For Shevchenko, it would be one of the most significant results of his career.

Neither finalist was in the conversation for Gstaad champion when the tournament began. One of them will be by Sunday evening.
