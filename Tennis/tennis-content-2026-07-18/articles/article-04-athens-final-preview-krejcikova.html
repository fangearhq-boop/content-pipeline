Athens Open Final Preview: Krejcikova Surges as Historic Tournament Reaches Its Final

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 18, 2026 — Athens, Greece

Barbora Krejcikova is in the Athens Open final. The Czech No. 1 seed defeated Clara Tauson in Saturday's semifinal to advance to the championship match of the first WTA event held in Athens in 35 years. The city has waited a long time for this. The final is Sunday.

Her opponent will be determined by the other semifinal. Maria Sakkari of Greece, the No. 4 seed and the crowd's champion all week, was scheduled to face Alina Korneeva Saturday evening at 7:30 PM local time. If Sakkari wins — if Athens gets its home finalist — the Sunday final becomes something the city will talk about for years.

Krejcikova's Week in Athens

Krejcikova arrived in Athens having not beaten Zheng Qinwen in their previous head-to-head meetings. She changed that in the quarterfinals, defeating the Chinese No. 3 seed 6-4, 6-4 — her first-ever win over Zheng. The result was described as a breakthrough of sorts, and it carried weight coming from a player of Zheng's caliber.

The semifinal against Tauson continued the momentum. Krejcikova is a former Grand Slam champion who knows what finals feel like. She has the experience to manage a final-day environment, regardless of who she faces.

The Sakkari Factor

Athens has been behind Maria Sakkari all week. The Greek player, seeded No. 4, has been dominant from her opening match and has won her Athens matches without conceding many games. The crowd at the Athens Olympic Tennis Centre has been visibly invested in each of her matches, creating an atmosphere unlike most regular-season WTA events.

Korneeva advanced to the semifinal by defeating Tereza Valentova in the quarterfinals, making her a genuine threat. But Sakkari's form this week and the home-court environment represent advantages that are difficult to discount.

If Sakkari wins Saturday evening, the Athens final will feature a home player against an established Grand Slam champion — exactly the kind of storyline the first WTA Athens event in 35 years deserves.

What's Next

The Athens Open final is scheduled for Sunday, July 19. Krejcikova will be there. Her opponent will be confirmed after Saturday evening's semifinal result. Regardless of who she faces, the final is a significant moment for Athens, for Greek tennis, and for a WTA calendar that added this event to replace another tournament earlier this year.

The city has waited 35 years for WTA tennis to return. The final is almost here.
