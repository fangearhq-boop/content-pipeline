Bastad Final Preview: Rublev vs Vallejo — A Paraguayan Dream on the Line

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 18, 2026 — Bastad, Sweden

The Nordea Open Bastad final is set. Sunday, on the red clay of southwest Sweden, No. 1 seed Andrey Rublev of Russia will face Adolfo Daniel Vallejo of Paraguay. One is the defending champion. The other is about to make history for his country.

Vallejo, ranked outside the top 100, defeated Luciano Darderi in Saturday's semifinal to become the first Paraguayan player to reach an ATP Tour final since Ramon Delgado at approximately 2002. His 2026 clay record is 35-11. He has been building to a week like this all season, and here it is.

Rublev defeated Alejandro Tabilo in the other semifinal. The Russian enters the final with an 11-2 record in ATP clay-court semifinals this year and a title-defense mindset — he won this tournament in 2023 and knows what it takes to win in Bastad.

The Case for Rublev

Rublev is the better-ranked player. He is the No. 1 seed for a reason, and his clay-court pedigree through the semifinals this season — 11-2 — indicates he does not typically fall before the final. He defeated Sebastian Baez 6-1, 6-2 in the quarterfinals with a display of controlled aggression that was hard to argue with.

He won Bastad in 2023. This is not unfamiliar territory for him. When Rublev is on clay and finding his level, he is one of the most powerful players in the world on the surface.

The Case for Vallejo

A 35-11 clay record in 2026 is not an accident. Vallejo has built this week from a foundation of real form, not a single-week run. He has been winning matches on clay for months. The tournament in Bastad has simply put the results where everyone can see them.

He dispatched Darderi in the semifinals — a player who had been competitive all week — in a performance that underlined why he is here. Players ranked outside the top 100 do not reach ATP finals by luck. They do it by being better than their ranking suggests.

And there is the weight of what he is carrying. The first Paraguayan in an ATP final since Ramon Delgado, more than two decades ago. That kind of historical moment tends to add something to a player's focus, not subtract from it.

What's Next

The Bastad final is scheduled for Sunday, July 19, at 2 PM local time. If Rublev wins, he adds a second Bastad title to his record. If Vallejo wins, he becomes the first Paraguayan ATP champion in decades and completes the most unexpected major result of the post-Wimbledon clay swing.

Either way, this week in Sweden will be remembered. The No. 1 seed who came to defend his title. The outsider who came to prove his season was real. Sunday will give us the answer.
