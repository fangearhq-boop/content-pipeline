### Long-Form Post A 22-year-old Spaniard walked onto a Croatian clay court on Saturday morning with a chance to win his first ATP Tour title. By the time he walked off, Daniel Merida had done exactly that. Merida defeated Damir Dzumhur 6-4, 6-1 in the Croatia Open Umag final — and the numbers around the result are remarkable. It was just his 7th career ATP tour-level event. He is only the third-youngest finalist in Umag this decade, trailing only Carlos Alcaraz and Jannik Sinner on that list. He reached his first Umag semifinal in four attempts. He reached his first ATP Tour final. He had earned the biggest match of his season. Saturday, facing Merida, the week's momentum ran out. For Merida, it is just beginning. His name is in the Umag record books alongside Alcaraz and Sinner. ### Long-Form Post The Bastad final is set. Sunday. Andrey Rublev vs. Adolfo Daniel Vallejo. Rublev, the No. 1 seed and the 2023 Bastad champion, defeated Alejandro Tabilo in Saturday's semifinal and enters the final with an 11-2 record in ATP clay-court semifinals. He has done this before. He knows how finals feel on clay. Vallejo has never done this before. That is precisely what makes Sunday remarkable. The Paraguayan, ranked outside the top 100, defeated Luciano Darderi in the other semifinal to become the first player from his country to reach an ATP Tour final since Ramon Delgado in approximately 2002. He is 35-11 on clay so far in 2026. He is not in the final by accident. On one side of the net: the No. 1 seed, the defending champion, the experience and the ranking. On the other: a player from a tennis-small nation rewriting his country's history with every match. Sunday's final in Bastad starts at 2 PM local time. Something will be decided. ### Image Post Caption Rublev vs. Vallejo. The Bastad final is set for Sunday. A No. 1 seed vs. a Paraguayan making history. #TennisFanrecap #ATPTour #Bastad

Shevchenko won the tiebreak, then closed out in straight sets. Tsitsipas's week at Gstaad ends in the semifinals. Sunday: Cerundolo vs. Shevchenko. Dzumhur's Emotional Umag Week
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post This time last week, Damir Dzumhur was not expected to be anywhere near a tennis final. The 34-year-old from Bosnia and Herzegovina, ranked No. 108, has been grinding the ATP tour for years — consistent enough to keep his ranking, never quite reaching the biggest matches. Umag this week was different. Thursday's quarterfinal set the tone. Dzumhur beat fourth seed Matteo Arnaldi in 3 hours and 42 minutes — a match that included a threatened mid-match walkout over an electronic line-call dispute and a third-set tiebreak that went right to the edge. He stayed. He won. "This is why we love the sport," he said afterward. Then came his first Umag semifinal in four trips to the tournament. Then came his first ATP Tour final. Saturday, facing Daniel Merida, the result was 6-4, 6-1. The final scoreline came quickly, and it was not kind to Dzumhur. But the scoreline is not the week. The week was a 34-year-old who has refused to stop competing reaching the biggest match of his 2026 season. The week was the walkout he didn't take. The week was why, after all these years, he is still on tour. ### Image Post Caption Dzumhur reached his first ATP Tour final this week. 34 years old. Ranked No. 108. He's still here, still fighting. #TennisFanrecap #ATPTour #Umag
