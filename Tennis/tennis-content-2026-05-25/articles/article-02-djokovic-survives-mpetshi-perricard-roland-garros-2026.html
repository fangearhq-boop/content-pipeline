Djokovic Survives Mpetshi Perricard in Four Sets — 80-2 in Grand Slam Openers

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 25, 2026

Novak Djokovic does not lose first-round matches at Grand Slams. He hasn't since the 2006 Australian Open — two decades of dominance in what should be the easiest round of any tournament. On Sunday night at Roland Garros, he came closer to breaking that streak than he had in years. Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard, 23, roared through the opening set on Chatrier and briefly had the French crowd believing. Then Djokovic, as he always does, found a way.

The three-time Roland Garros champion defeated Mpetshi Perricard 5-7, 7-5, 6-1, 6-4 in the night session, extending his Grand Slam opening-match record to 80-2 and his record to 82 appearances at majors — surpassing Roger Federer and Feliciano Lopez to stand alone in tennis history. He also extended his consecutive Roland Garros appearance streak to 22, tying Richard Gasquet's record.

The First Set: A French Roar

Mpetshi Perricard had beaten Djokovic before — in Rome earlier this month — and arrived in Paris with legitimate confidence. The 6'7" Frenchman holds one of the most powerful serves on the ATP Tour, and in the opening set he used it to perfection. Djokovic, one of the best returners in tennis history, found no break point opportunities as Mpetshi Perricard took the set 7-5. In doing so, he became the first Frenchman to win a set against Djokovic at Roland Garros in 14 years.

The Chatrier crowd was electric. Home-court advantage was alive in a way it rarely is against Djokovic.

The Second Set: Ten Break Points and a Drop Shot

The turning point of the match was also one of the most dramatic moments of Day 1. In the second set, Djokovic worked his way to break point opportunities nine times without converting. Mpetshi Perricard held firm, the crowd got louder, and the match seemed to be tilting toward an upset. Then came break point No. 10.

At 5-6 in the second set, Djokovic returned and drove Mpetshi Perricard into a 17-shot rally before dropping a precise drop shot to claim the break and level the set. He served out for 7-5. The match was level, but the momentum had shifted irrevocably. Djokovic took the third set 6-1 and the fourth 6-4 without drama.

The Numbers Behind the Legend

Djokovic is now 80-2 in Grand Slam first-round matches. His only two losses came in 2006 (Australian Open) and one other occasion — a record that spans 20 years and 82 Slams. He has won 24 Grand Slam titles to date and chases a record-breaking 25th here in Paris. At Roland Garros specifically, he is a three-time champion, winning in 2016, 2021, and 2023.

The 22-consecutive-Roland Garros-appearance streak ties Gasquet's record and places him among the most durable competitors the sport has ever seen. He arrived in Paris having played only one clay-court match in 2026 — a first-round Rome loss — and delivered a four-set win under pressure against a player who had beaten him in his most recent outing on clay.

What's Next

Djokovic faces French qualifier Valentin Royer in the second round. The path to a fourth Roland Garros title remains open, if challenging. His clay preparation remains minimal — but on Chatrier, when the pressure is highest, Novak Djokovic has a history of finding exactly what he needs. The Paris crowd cheered for Mpetshi Perricard. The result sided with history.
