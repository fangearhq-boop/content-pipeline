Monfils vs Gaston Night Session Headlines Roland Garros Day 2 — Baptiste's Escape and Swiatek's Statement

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | May 25, 2026

Roland Garros 2026 turns to an all-French night session on Day 2 as Gaël Monfils — in the final Roland Garros of his career — faces tricky compatriot Hugo Gaston on Court Philippe-Chatrier, not before 8:15 PM Paris time. Monfils, who received one of the tournament's wild cards for a final Paris farewell, will find no easy passage against Gaston, one of the tour's most creative and unconventional players. The Paris crowd will be loud. Gaston will not simply step aside.

Day 2 has already produced its highlights before the night session begins.

Swiatek Sets the Tone

The day's signature match came first. Four-time champion Iga Swiatek opened her 2026 Roland Garros campaign on Chatrier with a clinical 6-1, 6-2 win over Australian teenager Emerson Jones. Swiatek, who lost the Rome semifinal to Svitolina before arriving in Paris, showed none of the form concerns that had surrounded her — her baseline game was dominant from the opening game, and she gave Jones no footholds in either set.

The statement was clear: Swiatek is here to contend for a fifth title, and she is ready.

Day 2 Action: Seeds and Farewells

The rest of Day 2 brought a full slate of first-round action. Elena Rybakina, the No. 2 seed, faced Slovenian Veronika Erjavec. Elina Svitolina — who arrived in Paris as the Rome champion and one of the tournament's most dangerous contenders — faced Anna Bondar in a tricky encounter: their head-to-head record stands at 2-2, with Bondar winning the last two meetings. Defending finalist Jasmine Paolini faced Ukraine's Dayana Yastremska. Casper Ruud, Alex de Minaur, and Matteo Berrettini all also featured in the day's schedule.

Stan Wawrinka played his 21st and final Roland Garros match on Court Simonne-Mathieu, facing Dutch lucky loser Jesper de Jong in one of the day's most emotionally charged encounters. The 2015 champion's farewell has captured the attention of the Paris faithful.

The Monfils Farewell

The night session carries its own weight. Monfils — the beloved entertainer who has charmed Roland Garros crowds for two decades — plays his final match at the tournament as a professional. He received his wild card as part of a farewell tour that also included the "Gaël and Friends" charity exhibition event held earlier this week at the Chatrier. Now he plays for real, one last time.

Gaston is not an easy draw. The French wild card is known for his unorthodox tactics, heavy use of the drop shot, and the ability to disrupt opponents with unpredictable patterns. He plays to win, not to provide a graceful backdrop to someone else's farewell. Monfils will need to bring his best tennis to advance — and the Paris crowd will be with him if he does.

From Day 1: Baptiste's Great Escape

The day's context was sharpened by one of Sunday's most extraordinary moments. American Hailey Baptiste defeated 2021 Roland Garros champion Barbora Krejcikova 6-7(7), 7-6(6), 6-2 in 2 hours and 57 minutes — saving two consecutive match points in the second-set tiebreak before pulling away in the third set. Baptiste, who reached the WTA 1000 semifinals in Madrid last month after beating world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka, is proving she belongs among the best in the game's most important weeks. For Krejcikova, a former champion in Paris, the loss was a painful early exit.

In a draw that has already produced the Basavareddy-Fritz upset and a Djokovic scare, the women's side is proving equally unpredictable.

What's Next

Day 3 arrives on Tuesday with the tournament's biggest remaining names making their debuts: No. 1 seed Jannik Sinner, defending champion Coco Gauff, and world No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka all step onto the Roland Garros clay for the first time in 2026. The first round is nearly complete — and the picture emerging from Paris is of a tournament with no obvious winner and multiple compelling storylines still to unfold.
