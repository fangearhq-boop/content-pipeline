Kostyuk Reaches First WTA 1000 Final After Beating Potapova in Madrid

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

May 1, 2026 — Madrid, Spain

Marta Kostyuk's 2026 clay season has been one of the quietly extraordinary stories of the tennis year. On Thursday evening in Madrid, it reached its highest point. Kostyuk defeated Anastasia Potapova 6-2, 1-6, 6-1 in 1 hour and 36 minutes to advance to her first career WTA 1000 final.

The 23-year-old Ukrainian, seeded No. 23, has now won 11 consecutive matches on clay in 2026. She has not lost a single set in her last two rounds. She is in her third final of the season. And on Saturday, she will play Mirra Andreeva for the Madrid title at 5 PM CEST.

A Match Wrapped in Context

This semifinal carried weight beyond the scoreboard. Kostyuk again declined to shake hands with Potapova before the match, consistent with her position throughout the season. As a Ukrainian competing during an ongoing war, Kostyuk has been vocal about refusing courtside gestures with Russian players. Her stance has been part of a broader conversation in the sport about the political dimensions of competition.

On court, Kostyuk was focused and clinical. She won the first set 6-2, then fell away sharply in the second, losing it 1-6 as Potapova found her rhythm. The third set was decisive: Kostyuk won it 6-1, finishing the match with authority. There was no wobble in the final set, and no doubt about who would be heading to Saturday's final.

A Season Built on Clay

Kostyuk's clay-court record in 2026 — 11 wins without a loss — has been built step by step. This is her third final of the year, and each of them has come in a different context. She arrives at the Madrid final having beaten quality opponents throughout the draw.

She beat Andreeva at the Brisbane International earlier this season in straight sets, 7-6, 6-3, but that match was played on hard court. Saturday's final on clay will be a different test. Andreeva, who just beat Hailey Baptiste in a second-set tiebreak thriller, is equally dangerous on this surface.

Breaking New Ground

This is the first WTA 1000 final for both finalists. Kostyuk has competed at the top level for several years but had not previously reached this stage at a WTA 1000 event. The Madrid final — regardless of the result — marks a milestone in her career.

If she wins on Saturday, she would become only the second player ranked outside the WTA Top 20 to claim the Madrid Open title, according to pre-tournament reporting. That would be another layer of history to an already remarkable week.

What's Next

Marta Kostyuk plays Mirra Andreeva in the Madrid WTA final on Saturday, May 2 at 5 PM CEST at the Estadio Manolo Santana. The winner claims the WTA 1000 title and a strong position heading into Roland Garros, which begins May 18 in Paris.
