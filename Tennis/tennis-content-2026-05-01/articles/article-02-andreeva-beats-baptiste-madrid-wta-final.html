Andreeva Beats Baptiste to Reach Madrid WTA Final — A Birthday Gift Worth Remembering

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

May 1, 2026 — Madrid, Spain

Mirra Andreeva turned 19 on April 29. Twenty-four hours later, she was saving set points in a Madrid WTA semifinal tiebreak and racing toward her third WTA 1000 final. The result told you everything you need to know about where this teenager's game has gone in 2026.

Andreeva defeated Hailey Baptiste 6-4, 7-6(8) in 1 hour and 39 minutes to book her place in Saturday's final. She saved three set points in the second-set tiebreak before closing it out 10-8. That required a level of composure that most players spend years developing.

A Tiebreak That Defined the Match

The first set belonged to Andreeva, who won it 6-4 with clean, purposeful tennis. Baptiste — who had stunned defending champion Aryna Sabalenka earlier in the week by saving six match points in a stunning three-set win — raised her level in the second set and pushed the match to a tiebreak.

Down set points, Andreeva did not panic. She won the critical moments and closed the tiebreak 10-8. It was the kind of performance that earns confidence you cannot fake.

Baptiste had been one of the stories of the tournament. But on Thursday evening, the story belonged entirely to the teenager from Russia competing under a neutral flag.

History in the Making

With the victory, Andreeva became the first teenager to reach three WTA 1000 finals since the format was introduced in 2009. It is a staggering statistic for a player who turned 19 two days before the match. Her 2026 clay-court record now stands at 12-1 following this win.

The broader clay campaign has been remarkable. Andreeva won the Linz title, reached the Stuttgart final, and has now advanced to the Madrid final — all in one clay season. She arrived in Madrid seeded No. 9 and leaves as one of the two players with a chance to lift the Caja Mágica trophy on Saturday.

Final: Andreeva vs Kostyuk

Andreeva faces Marta Kostyuk in Saturday's final, scheduled for 5 PM CEST. It is the first WTA 1000 final for both players. Kostyuk — who reached her first WTA 1000 final by defeating Anastasia Potapova — beat Andreeva in the Brisbane International quarterfinals earlier this season, winning 7-6, 6-3 on hard court. Saturday will mark their first clay-court meeting.

The historic nature of the final is not lost on anyone following the tournament. Regardless of who wins, Madrid 2026 will crown a first-time WTA 1000 champion who is already being talked about as one of the future faces of the women's game.

What's Next

The Madrid WTA final between Andreeva and Kostyuk takes place Saturday, May 2 at 5 PM CEST. The winner takes the WTA 1000 title and a significant points haul heading into Roland Garros, which begins May 18. Andreeva's clay form makes her one of the most dangerous players in the Paris draw, regardless of Saturday's result.
