Madrid ATP Semifinal Day: Sinner vs Fils, Zverev vs Blockx — History on the Line

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

May 1, 2026 — Madrid, Spain

Friday at the Mutua Madrid Open is not just another day at a Masters 1000. It is potentially a day for the record books. World No. 1 Jannik Sinner walks onto the Manolo Santana court this afternoon chasing something no player has ever accomplished: a fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title. Standing in his way is one of the hottest players on the tour, 21-year-old Frenchman Arthur Fils.

The first ATP semifinal is scheduled not before 4 PM CEST. The nightcap pits No. 2 seed Alexander Zverev against Belgian wildcard Alexander Blockx at 8 PM. The Sunday final is set for May 3.

Sinner vs Fils: The Feature Match

Sinner enters on a 26-match Masters 1000 winning streak. His last four titles in the series were at Paris, Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte-Carlo. Should he win in Madrid, he would become the first player in the Open Era to hold five consecutive Masters 1000 titles. The ATP has called it "record fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title" pursuit — a mark that has never been reached in the 36-year history of the series.

Fils is not the kind of player who will be intimidated by the occasion. The Frenchman won the Barcelona ATP 500 title last week and has not dropped a set on clay in 2026. His 9-match winning streak includes victories over quality opponents all the way to the quarterfinal, where he dismissed Jiri Lehecka 6-3, 6-4 in just 74 minutes.

Their head-to-head is minimal — Sinner won their only previous encounter in 2023. This will be the first time they meet on clay, and Fils has made clear he believes his game is at an elite level right now.

Zverev vs Blockx: The David and Goliath Semifinal

The evening match presents a remarkable contrast. Zverev, ranked No. 2 in the world, is a four-time Madrid quarterfinalist who has never won the title in Madrid. Blockx, ranked No. 135 and playing on a wildcard, has never been to a Masters 1000 semifinal before this week.

Blockx earned his place by defeating defending champion Casper Ruud in the quarterfinals, a result that turned heads across the tennis world. The Belgian is 22 years old and has shown composure under pressure well beyond his experience level.

Zverev himself reached this semifinal after defeating Flavio Cobolli in the quarterfinals, avenging his Munich final loss. In doing so, he matched Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Jannik Sinner by joining the group of players to have reached a specific milestone — the full list revealed separately in today's coverage.

What's at Stake

The ATP final is Sunday, May 3, not before 5 PM CEST. The winner of Sinner-Fils and the winner of Zverev-Blockx will meet for the Madrid title and, in Sinner's case, a piece of tennis history.

For Fils, a win here would represent the biggest result of his career and likely propel him into genuine Roland Garros contention. For Zverev, a first Madrid title has long been a missing piece. For Blockx, simply being here is already the story.

But all roads today lead back to the same question: can anyone stop Jannik Sinner on a clay court in 2026?

What's Next

The ATP semifinal results will determine Sunday's final lineup at the Mutua Madrid Open. Roland Garros — the French Open — begins May 18, just 17 days away.
