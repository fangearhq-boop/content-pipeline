Sinner Joins Federer, Nadal, Djokovic in Masters 1000 Semifinal History Books

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

May 1, 2026 — Madrid, Spain

When Jannik Sinner reached the Madrid Open semifinal, he quietly stepped into one of the most exclusive groups in tennis history. By getting to the final four at a ninth distinct ATP Masters 1000 event, Sinner became only the sixth player since the series began in 1990 to reach the semifinals at all nine Masters 1000 tournaments.

The list: Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, Novak Djokovic, Andy Murray, Alexander Zverev, and now Jannik Sinner. Six names. Thirty-six years of Masters 1000 tennis. That is the complete record.

A Season for the History Books

The achievement goes further when you zoom into 2026 alone. Sinner has now reached the semifinals at the first four Masters 1000 events of this season — Indian Wells, Miami, Monte-Carlo, and Madrid. He joins Roger Federer in 2006 and Rafael Nadal in 2010 and 2011 as the only players to accomplish that in the Open Era.

His overall Masters 1000 form is just as extraordinary. Entering today's semifinal against Arthur Fils, Sinner carries a 26-match winning streak in the series. He has won the last four Masters 1000 titles: Paris, Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte-Carlo. A win here would give him five in a row — unprecedented in the 36-year history of the ATP Masters 1000 series.

Eight Titles and Counting

Sinner is an eight-time Masters 1000 champion. He won his first at Toronto in 2023 and has been adding titles at a pace that has left the tour searching for a comparable reference point. The Big Three — Federer, Nadal, Djokovic — defined an era with their Masters 1000 dominance. Sinner, at 24, appears to be rewriting the standards.

His response to the historical comparisons has been consistent. He focuses on the next match, not the record. Today, the next match is Arthur Fils at 4 PM CEST on the Manolo Santana court in Madrid.

What the Numbers Say

In 26 consecutive Masters 1000 matches, Sinner has dropped just two sets. He has not lost a match in the series since last year. The players ahead of him on all-time Masters 1000 wins lists — Federer, Nadal, Djokovic — each spent a decade building those records. Sinner is moving at a different velocity.

The question is no longer whether he belongs in the conversation with the all-time greats. It is whether he is already writing a chapter of his own.

What's Next

Sinner faces Arthur Fils in the Madrid ATP semifinal today, May 1, not before 4 PM CEST. A win would put him in Sunday's final (May 3) and give him the chance to make the fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title the newest entry in the record books. Roland Garros begins May 18.
