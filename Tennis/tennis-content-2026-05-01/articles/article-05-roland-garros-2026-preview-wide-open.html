Roland Garros 2026 Preview: Alcaraz Out, Djokovic Doubtful, and the Draw Is Wide Open

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

May 1, 2026 — Madrid, Spain

Roland Garros begins May 18. That is 17 days from today — and the tournament has never looked more unpredictable heading into qualifying week.

The defending ATP champion, Carlos Alcaraz, is almost certainly absent. He injured his right wrist during the Barcelona Open and has not played since. He has missed the entire clay season and is currently not expected to travel to Paris. Novak Djokovic, a three-time Roland Garros champion, has also sat out the clay swing with a shoulder injury. His participation in Paris remains in serious doubt.

Together, Alcaraz and Djokovic won the last five Roland Garros titles between them. Both are expected to be missing. That changes everything.

The Men's Draw: Sinner Stands Alone

Jannik Sinner arrives in Paris — should he win Madrid on Sunday — carrying a five-consecutive Masters 1000 title streak, a 26-match win run in the series, and the kind of form that makes him the clear No. 1 favorite. He has reached the semifinals at all nine Masters 1000 events this season and is competing at a level above the rest of the field.

Behind him, Alexander Zverev remains the most experienced threat. Arthur Fils, fresh off his Barcelona title and currently in the Madrid semifinal, arrives in excellent clay form. Lorenzo Musetti — always dangerous on clay — will also be in the draw. The field is quality, but the gap to Sinner right now is real.

The Women's Draw: Five Contenders, No Clear Favorite

The women's draw at Roland Garros 2026 may be the most open in years. Coco Gauff returns to defend the title she won in 2025, and Paris has always been a surface where her game translates well. But there are four other players with credible claims.

Aryna Sabalenka has been the best player on the WTA Tour this season — her Madrid run was ended by an extraordinary Hailey Baptiste match — and arrives in Paris with form and confidence. Iga Swiatek has won the Roland Garros title four times. A Paris run is never out of the question for Swiatek regardless of what happens elsewhere on clay.

Elena Rybakina has been rolling this clay season. And then there is Mirra Andreeva, who at 19 is in the Madrid WTA final and carries a 12-1 clay-court record in 2026. She will arrive at Roland Garros on a wave of momentum that no one in the draw will want to encounter in the early rounds.

The Schedule

Roland Garros 2026 runs from May 18 to June 7 at Stade Roland-Garros in Paris. The draw ceremony and qualifying matches begin the week of May 18. The main draw opens the following week. The singles finals are scheduled for the final weekend of May into early June.

What's Next

Between now and Roland Garros, the Italian Open (Rome) is the final clay Masters event — a significant tune-up for all contenders.
