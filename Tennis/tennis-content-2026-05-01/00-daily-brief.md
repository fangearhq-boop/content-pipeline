### STORY 1: Madrid ATP Semifinal Day — Sinner vs Fils & Zverev vs Blockx
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Match Preview / Day-Of Coverage
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

The biggest day yet at the 2026 Mutua Madrid Open. World No. 1 Jannik Sinner faces Arthur Fils in the first ATP semifinal (4 PM CEST, Manolo Santana), with Alexander Zverev taking on wildcard Alexander Blockx in the nightcap (8 PM CEST). Sinner enters on a 26-match Masters 1000 winning streak, chasing an unprecedented fifth consecutive M1000 title. Fils brings a 9-match unbeaten run including his Barcelona title. Zverev, who has now joined the all-time list of players to reach SFs at all nine M1000 events, faces Belgian wildcard Blockx in his first-ever Masters 1000 SF. The Sunday ATP final is set for May 3 (not before 5 PM).

### STORY 2: Andreeva Beats Baptiste to Reach Madrid WTA Final
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Match Result
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

A day after celebrating her 19th birthday, Mirra Andreeva survived a tense second-set tiebreak to defeat Hailey Baptiste 6-4, 7-6(8) in 1 hour 39 minutes, booking her place in the Madrid WTA final. Andreeva saved three set points in the tiebreak (which she won 10-8). She becomes the first teenager to reach three WTA 1000 finals since the format's introduction in 2009. She faces Marta Kostyuk in Saturday's final (5 PM Madrid).

### STORY 3: Kostyuk Powers Into First WTA 1000 Final
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Match Result
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Marta Kostyuk defeated Anastasia Potapova 6-2, 1-6, 6-1 in 1 hour 36 minutes to reach her first career WTA 1000 final. Kostyuk, the Ukrainian No. 23 seed, won the first and third sets convincingly despite a second-set blip. She came into the SF having refused Potapova a pre-match handshake over Russia-Ukraine political reasons. Her clay-court run in 2026 now stands at 11 consecutive wins. She meets Andreeva in the Saturday final.

### STORY 4: Sinner Joins Federer, Nadal, Djokovic in Exclusive Masters 1000 Club
**Tier:** 2
**Type:** Feature / Historical Milestone
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

By reaching the Madrid semifinal, Sinner became only the sixth player since the Masters 1000 series began in 1990 to reach the semifinals at all nine events — joining Federer, Nadal, Djokovic, Murray, and Zverev. He also joins Federer (2006) and Nadal (2010, 2011) as the only players to reach the SFs at all four opening Masters 1000 events of a single season. With eight M1000 titles and a 26-match win streak in the series, Sinner is on a historic run.

### STORY 5: Roland Garros 2026 — 17 Days Away and Wide Open
**Tier:** 2
**Type:** Preview / Evergreen
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Roland Garros begins May 18, just 17 days away. Defending champion Carlos Alcaraz is almost certainly out with a right wrist injury. Novak Djokovic has also missed the clay season with a shoulder issue. That leaves Jannik Sinner as the heavy favorite, though the women's draw is headlined by Coco Gauff (defending champion), Aryna Sabalenka, Iga Swiatek, Elena Rybakina, and surging teenager Mirra Andreeva — who arrives in Paris as possibly the most in-form player in the draw.
