### STORY 2: Andreeva def. Baptiste 6-4, 7-6(8) — WTA Final Bound

#### Long-Form Post -- 8:00 AM ET

**Long-Form Post (8:00 AM ET)**

Mirra Andreeva's 19th birthday was April 29. She spent the following day making history.

In the Madrid WTA semifinal, Andreeva trailed in the second-set tiebreak, staring down set point after set point. She saved all three. She then won the tiebreak 10-8 and closed out the match 6-4, 7-6(8) over Hailey Baptiste in 1 hour and 39 minutes.

The result makes Andreeva the first teenager to reach three WTA 1000 finals since the format was introduced in 2009. Seeded No. 9 in Madrid, she now carries a 2026 clay-court record that stands at 12-1.

She meets Marta Kostyuk in Saturday's final at 5 PM Madrid. A first-time WTA 1000 champion will be crowned at La Caja Magica — neither Andreeva nor Kostyuk has won at this level before. Based on this week, one of them clearly belongs at the top of the game.

### STORY 3: Kostyuk Powers Into First WTA 1000 Final

#### Long-Form Post -- 12:30 PM ET

**Long-Form Post (12:30 PM ET)**

Marta Kostyuk is in her first WTA 1000 final, and she earned it the hard way.

The 23-year-old Ukrainian had already made headlines before the match began — again refusing a pre-match handshake with Anastasia Potapova for political reasons related to the Russia-Ukraine conflict. On court, she was dominant. Kostyuk beat Potapova 6-2, 1-6, 6-1 in 1 hour and 36 minutes. A second-set blip couldn't knock her off course.

She hasn't lost on clay in 2026. 11 consecutive wins. This is her third final of the year. She beat Andreeva at the Brisbane International earlier this season (7-6, 6-3) on hard court, but Saturday's Madrid final will be their first clay-court meeting.

Saturday. 5 PM Madrid. Kostyuk vs. Andreeva. First WTA 1000 final for both.

### STORY 4: Sinner Joins Historic Masters 1000 Club

#### Long-Form Post -- 10:30 AM ET

**Long-Form Post (10:30 AM ET)**

When Jannik Sinner reached the Madrid semifinal, he joined one of the most exclusive clubs in tennis history.

He became only the sixth player since the ATP Masters 1000 series began in 1990 to reach the semifinals at all nine events — joining Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, Novak Djokovic, Andy Murray, and Alexander Zverev. Six players in 36 years. That is the full list.

But Sinner didn't stop there. He's also now one of just three men in the Open Era to reach the semifinals at all four opening Masters 1000 events of a single season — joining Federer (2006) and Nadal (2010, 2011). And he's chasing something nobody has ever done: a fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title. His last four: Paris, Indian Wells, Miami, Monte-Carlo. Today he plays Fils for a shot at Madrid.

In 26 consecutive Masters 1000 matches, he's lost just two sets.

### STORY 5: Roland Garros 2026 Preview

#### Long-Form Post -- 3:00 PM ET

**Long-Form Post (3:00 PM ET)**

Roland Garros begins May 18. That's 17 days away — and the draw has never been more wide open.

Carlos Alcaraz, the defending champion, is almost certainly absent with a right wrist injury. He has missed the entire clay season. Novak Djokovic has also been absent throughout the clay swing with a shoulder issue. The two biggest names won't be there.

That opens the door wide for Jannik Sinner. If he wins Madrid on Sunday, he'll carry a five-title winning streak into Paris. No one in the Open Era has ever done that.

On the women's side, defending champion Coco Gauff returns to Paris. But Iga Swiatek — a four-time Roland Garros champion — is always a threat. Elena Rybakina is rolling after her Stuttgart title. And Mirra Andreeva? She arrives in Paris as possibly the hottest player in the draw, with a clay-court record and big-match pedigree that no one expected at 19.

The second Grand Slam of 2026 is shaping up to be one of the most intriguing in recent memory.
