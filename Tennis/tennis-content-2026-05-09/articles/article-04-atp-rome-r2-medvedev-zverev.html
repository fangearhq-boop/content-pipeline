ATP Rome R2: Medvedev, Zverev, and the Race to Challenge Sinner

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

May 9, 2026

With Novak Djokovic eliminated and Jannik Sinner's debut coming this evening, Saturday's ATP second-round action in Rome puts the spotlight on Daniil Medvedev, Alexander Zverev, and a field scrambling to provide the world No. 1 with any meaningful resistance.

When Novak Djokovic walked off the Foro Italico clay on Friday evening, the mathematics of the ATP Italian Open changed. The six-time champion's second-round loss to 20-year-old qualifier Dino Prizmic did not just make headlines — it removed the last credible veteran threat capable of meeting Jannik Sinner in the final from the same half of the draw.

Now, Saturday's second-round action must answer a different question: who, if anyone, has the game to give the world No. 1 a serious match before Roland Garros?

Medvedev vs. Machac: The Day's Headline Match

No. 7 Daniil Medvedev faces Tomas Machac on Campo Centrale in what projects as the most watchable match of Saturday's ATP program. Medvedev has had an inconsistent relationship with clay throughout his career — he is a formidable opponent on the surface but rarely the most fluid mover on it — while Machac represents exactly the kind of heavy groundstroke game that can exploit any hesitation from the Russian.

Machac, whose ball speed off the forehand side is among the highest on tour, has shown the ability to dictate points from the baseline in ways that neutralize Medvedev's typically superior defensive game. This match could go the distance, and the result will significantly clarify which players remain capable of reaching the second week.

Zverev Recovers from Rain Delay

Alexander Zverev came through Friday's opening round in solid fashion, defeating fellow German Daniel Altmaier 7-5, 6-3 after a rain delay of more than 90 minutes forced both players to wait out the weather before taking the court.

The delay did not appear to affect Zverev's rhythm — he was composed throughout and served with the kind of reliability that makes him dangerous on clay, where his ability to hit through the court gives opponents minimal time to reset. Zverev, a former Roland Garros finalist, has his eyes on using this Italian Open campaign to sharpen his preparation for Paris.

He enters the second round facing a stiffer test, and his performance will be telling. Zverev tends to elevate in the second week of clay events; getting there requires the kind of controlled aggression he displayed against Altmaier.

The Sinner Effect on the Draw

The broader context for all of Saturday's ATP action is Jannik Sinner's impending debut. The world No. 1 plays his second-round match against Sebastian Ofner tonight — scheduled not before 19:00 local time — and the entire Foro Italico is aware that a successful opening night sets in motion what could be the most historically significant Italian Open since Adriano Panatta won the title 50 years ago.

For Medvedev, Zverev, and the others still standing in the draw, Saturday is about more than advancing. It is about demonstrating that they can play at the level required to challenge a player who has won 30 of 32 matches in 2026 and has lost precisely once on clay in the past calendar year.

Prizmic showed on Friday that the right performance on the right day can beat anyone. What Saturday tells us is whether any of the established names have that kind of performance in them.

What's Next

Second-round results on Saturday will set the round of 16 lineup at the Italian Open. Medvedev, Zverev, and a number of Spanish clay specialists remain in contention. Jannik Sinner's first-round match against Ofner in the evening session will be the night's focal point. Tennis Fanrecap will have all the results from Rome as they happen.
