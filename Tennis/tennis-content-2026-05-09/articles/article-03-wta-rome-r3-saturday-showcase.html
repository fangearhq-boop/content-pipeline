WTA Rome R3: Sabalenka, Gauff, Paolini, Andreeva — Saturday Is Must-Watch Tennis

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

May 9, 2026

Four of the WTA's top names are in third-round action at the Italian Open on Saturday, with a potential Sabalenka–Gauff quarterfinal emerging as the marquee clay-season showdown on the horizon.

There are days on the tennis calendar that feel like a preview of everything that is about to come. Saturday at the Italian Open is one of those days.

The WTA's four biggest names — Aryna Sabalenka, Coco Gauff, Jasmine Paolini, and Mirra Andreeva — are all on court in the third round at Foro Italico. The draw sets up an extraordinarily high-stakes afternoon and evening of clay-court tennis, with Roland Garros starting in two weeks and form points on the line for every player involved.

Sabalenka the Favourite

No. 1 Aryna Sabalenka faces Sorana Cirstea (No. 26) in the first of the big third-round matches. Sabalenka arrived in Rome after a dominant run of results and dispatched Barbora Krejcikova in the second round 6-2, 6-3 — a ruthless performance that suggested she is hitting peak clay-court form at exactly the right moment.

Sabalenka has yet to win the Italian Open title, and Rome represents one of the meaningful gaps in her clay-season trophy cabinet. With Roland Garros — another title she has not yet won — looming, every performance here carries additional weight. Cirstea is a seasoned opponent who has produced results against top players throughout her career, but Sabalenka in this form is a formidable challenge for anyone.

Gauff Seeks Clay-Season Form

No. 3 Coco Gauff takes on Solana Sierra of Argentina in her third-round match. Gauff's clay season has been interrupted by illness — she played through a viral illness in Madrid and has been searching for the kind of sustained, dominant performances that defined her run to the Roland Garros title in 2023.

Rome offers the chance to reset. Gauff advanced past the second round with a composed 6-3, 6-4 win over Valentova, and the Argentine Sierra will provide a different kind of test. Sierra has shown the ability to hang with bigger opponents on clay, but Gauff, when healthy and focused, is a player who can make these matchups look one-sided.

The Defending Champion's Path

No. 9 Jasmine Paolini carries the full weight of Italian expectation as the defending Italian Open champion. She faces No. 21 Elise Mertens in the third round — a match that could become emotional at Foro Italico, where Paolini's popularity rivals Sinner's.

Paolini's title defense run has been competitive from the start. She came through a demanding three-set second-round match against Leolia Jeanjean — 6-7(4), 6-2, 6-4 — in nearly three hours, which demonstrated both the depth of her competitive resilience and the physical demands this tournament will place on her. Mertens is a seasoned veteran with excellent hands at the net and the tactical intelligence to exploit any hesitation from the defending champion.

Andreeva's Continuing Rise

Mirra Andreeva (ranked No. 7) faces Viktorija Golubic in what could be the most one-sided of Saturday's third-round matches on paper. The 19-year-old Russian has been one of the most compelling clay-court performers of the 2026 season — she won Stuttgart and has continued her dominant run in Rome.

Andreeva's combination of heavy topspin groundstrokes and tactical maturity beyond her years makes her a genuine threat to anyone left in the draw. A victory here would set up what could be a quarterfinal meeting with Gauff — one of the most anticipated clashes of the Rome campaign.

What's Next

Saturday's third-round results at the Italian Open will set the quarterfinal lineup for the WTA draw. If the seeds hold, a Sabalenka–Gauff quarterfinal would be the headline match of the second week — and the most important clay-court tune-up before Roland Garros for both players.
