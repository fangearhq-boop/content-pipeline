Halle QF Set: Zverev, Shelton, and Fritz All Power Through on Grass

By Marcus Cole | June 18, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The quarterfinal field at the Terra Wortmann Open in Halle is taking shape, and it reads like a Wimbledon preview. Thursday's Round of 16 action saw Alexander Zverev, Ben Shelton, and Taylor Fritz all advance, joining Frances Tiafoe, Felix Auger-Aliassime, Daniil Medvedev, Daniel Altmaier, and Mattia Bellucci in the last eight. With Jannik Sinner absent from the entire grass circuit and Carlos Alcaraz sidelined with a wrist injury, the Halle draw is the clearest window yet into who arrives at Wimbledon with momentum.

Zverev Survives Hanfmann

Alexander Zverev, the No. 1 seed and Roland Garros champion playing his home event in Halle, defeated fellow German Yannick Hanfmann 6-3, 4-6, 6-2 in a match that required more work than the scoreline might suggest. Zverev took the opening set comfortably, dropped the second, and then reasserted control in the third. For a player making his first sustained grass campaign as a Grand Slam champion, the ability to grind through a second-set lapse and close out the match is a positive sign.

It was Zverev's first Halle victory since winning the title here in 2021. He already holds the record for most ATP 500 wins in history — after matching Rafael Nadal's tally of 121 wins earlier in the week — and a Halle title run would add to one of the most prolific ATP 500 track records in the Open Era.

Shelton Rolls, Fritz Grinds Through

Ben Shelton continued what has been the most impressive grass-court form of any player this week, defeating American compatriot Ethan Quinn 7-5, 6-3 to reach his first Halle quarterfinal. Shelton, who won the Boss Open in Stuttgart last week — his third title of 2026 and his first on grass — has shown no rust in transitioning between surfaces. The Stuttgart champion is now unbeaten across his last several matches on grass, and his serve-heavy, aggressive style appears perfectly calibrated for this week's conditions.

Taylor Fritz, Stuttgart runner-up and the No. 2 seed in Halle, also advanced past Fabian Marozsan to set up a deep run in the draw. Fritz had arrived in Halle off the back of one of his best grass-court weeks in years and did enough to stay in contention for what would be his first title at the venue.

Mattia Bellucci, the Italian qualifier who became the story of the week by eliminating defending champion Alexander Bublik in the first round — the first time a Halle defending champion had fallen in Round 1 since 2003 — continued his run with a 7-6(6), 6-1 win over Raphael Collignon. Bellucci is now in his first ATP 500 quarterfinal.

Super Friday Preview

The Friday quarterfinals include two all-American matchups: Frances Tiafoe versus Felix Auger-Aliassime, and Altmaier versus Medvedev. On the other side of the draw, Zverev, Shelton, Fritz, and Bellucci will each play for semifinal spots.

The absence of Sinner and Alcaraz from the grass season entirely throws the Wimbledon contention picture to the active players. Zverev arrives at SW19 as the Roland Garros champion and form player from the clay season. Shelton arrives as the grass-season form player. Friday in Halle will begin to separate these names from the field.

What's Next

Quarterfinals at the Terra Wortmann Open take place Friday, June 19. Semifinals are Saturday, June 20. The Halle final is Sunday, June 21 — the last ATP 500 result before Wimbledon qualifying begins Monday, June 22. The main draw ceremony is June 26, and the Championships begin June 29. Every match from here carries Wimbledon weight.
