Badosa Stuns Gauff in Berlin: World No. 142 Beats No. 7 Seed in Three-Set Comeback

By Ryan Calloway | June 18, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Paula Badosa arrived in Berlin without a WTA victory since Charleston in April. She had lost five consecutive matches. She was ranked No. 142 in the world. On Thursday, she defeated No. 5 seed Coco Gauff 1-6, 6-3, 6-2 in the Round of 16 at the VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Tennis Open — one of the most striking upsets of the grass-court season.

A Set Down, Then a Comeback

The opening set gave no indication of what was coming. Gauff took it 6-1, breaking Badosa twice and playing with the dominant, aggressive baseline game that had carried her to the world No. 7 ranking. The match appeared to be heading where the rankings suggested it should go.

Badosa won 12 of the next 16 games.

The second set was a controlled affair — 6-3 to Badosa. The third continued the pattern, with Badosa winning 6-2 and closing out the match in one hour and 35 minutes on the grass courts of Berlin. Her fifth-career win over Gauff in eight meetings was the most improbable of the five.

After the match, Badosa addressed the crowd emotionally. "I kept believing," she said. Coming off five consecutive losses, with a ranking at a career-low point, belief was the active ingredient.

Context: A Career at a Crossroads

Badosa, the Spanish right-hander who rose to world No. 2 in 2022, has spent the past two years navigating injuries that derailed a career that looked destined for multiple Grand Slam titles. A stress fracture in her back limited her throughout 2023 and 2024. The ranking slipped. The results became inconsistent. Berlin's grass courts, where Badosa has historically played well, gave her an opportunity to reset.

Her record against Gauff speaks to what Badosa is capable of when healthy. Ranked No. 142, she now leads their head-to-head 5-3 — a number that would feel out of place for nearly any other player ranked that low against a world No. 7.

Implications for Berlin and Wimbledon

Gauff had been the No. 5 seed in Berlin and a legitimate contender in the draw that also includes No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka and No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina. With her exit, Badosa now faces Jessica Pegula in the quarterfinals. A Badosa semifinal run would represent one of the more dramatic turnarounds on the WTA Tour this year.

The timing matters beyond Berlin as well. Wimbledon's draw ceremony is June 26. The seedings are based on the June 22 world rankings. A deep Berlin run for Badosa could nudge her ranking in the right direction before the Championships begin.

What's Next

Badosa plays Jessica Pegula in the Berlin quarterfinals. The Berlin final is Sunday, June 21. Wimbledon begins June 29. If Badosa continues to play at this level, she is a name to watch when the Wimbledon singles draw is made on June 26.
