Wimbledon 2026 Countdown: 11 Days Out, Qualifying Starts Monday

By Ryan Calloway | June 18, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The grass-court season's most consequential week is almost here. Wimbledon 2026 begins June 29. Qualifying starts Monday, June 22, at the Bank of England Sports Ground in Roehampton — four days from now. The main draw ceremony is Friday, June 26. The full 15-day Championships follow. With 11 days remaining and the quarterfinals still being decided at Halle, Queen's Club, and Berlin, the form guide is being written in real time.

Qualifying: What It Is and Who's Playing

Wimbledon qualifying runs Monday June 22 through Thursday June 25 at Roehampton. Three rounds of singles qualifying will fill the final spots in the 128-player main draw. Players earn £20,000 for a first-round qualifying win, £32,000 for a second-round win, and £50,000 for reaching the third round and qualifying for the main draw.

Notable players in the qualifying field include Maja Chwalinska — the Polish qualifier who became the first qualifier in Roland Garros women's final history just two weeks ago before losing to Mirra Andreeva — along with former US Open champion Bianca Andreescu and Swiss player Lulu Sun, a former Wimbledon quarterfinalist. The qualifying draw tends to produce compelling stories, and this year's field is no exception.

The Men's Picture

Jannik Sinner, the defending Wimbledon champion, has taken an unusual preparation route. He has skipped every official ATP grass event this year, opting instead to compete only at the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at the Hurlingham Club — an exhibition event running June 23-27 — before heading to SW19. Sinner defended his title here in 2025 and is the No. 1 seed.

Carlos Alcaraz is absent with a wrist injury confirmed before the grass season began. The top of the men's draw will be determined without the world No. 2.

That opens the field for the players currently competing on grass. Alexander Zverev is in the Halle quarterfinals as the Roland Garros champion. Ben Shelton won in Stuttgart and is through at Halle — the form player of the surface so far. Taylor Fritz is also in the Halle last eight. Felix Auger-Aliassime and Frances Tiafoe are in the mix at Halle. Alex de Minaur is the Queen's Club top seed. The Wimbledon draw, once revealed on June 26, will determine which of these players are on a collision course.

The Women's Picture

Aryna Sabalenka holds the world No. 1 ranking and is the projected top seed at Wimbledon. She is currently in the Berlin quarterfinals — a useful tune-up on grass. Elena Rybakina, the 2022 Wimbledon champion, is also in the Berlin last eight and, on her best days on grass, is the most dangerous player in the women's draw.

Iga Swiatek is the defending Wimbledon women's champion. Coco Gauff, who fell in Berlin on Thursday, will arrive at SW19 without the grass-court form she might have wanted.

Mirra Andreeva, the Roland Garros champion at 19, arrives at Wimbledon with a Grand Slam title but no grass-court record to speak of. Her clay credentials are historically strong; whether that translates to SW19 in her first year as a major champion is one of the stories of the fortnight.

Prize Money and Key Dates

Total prize money at Wimbledon 2026 is £64.2 million — a 20 percent increase from 2025 and a record for the tournament in its 149-year history. The singles champion on each side of the draw takes home £3.6 million.

What's Next

The Halle final is Sunday, June 21. The Queen's Club final is also Sunday, June 21. The Berlin final completes the grass warm-up schedule on the same day. Wimbledon qualifying begins Monday, June 22 at Roehampton. The main draw ceremony is Friday, June 26. The Championships begin Monday, June 29. After several weeks of building evidence, Wimbledon is almost here.
