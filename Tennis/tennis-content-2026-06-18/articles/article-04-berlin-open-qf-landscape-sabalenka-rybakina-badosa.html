Berlin Open QF Set: Sabalenka, Rybakina, and Badosa Headline Final Four

By Marcus Cole | June 18, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The quarterfinal draw at the VANDA Pharmaceuticals Berlin Tennis Open is set, and it offers a compelling mix of established grass-court contenders and one significant surprise. No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka, No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina, No. 4 seed Jessica Pegula, and Paula Badosa — the player who stunned No. 5 seed Coco Gauff earlier Thursday — will contest the last four spots in the Berlin draw. The tournament's final is Sunday, June 21, five days before the Wimbledon draw ceremony.

Sabalenka Dominates Second Round

Aryna Sabalenka reached her third consecutive Berlin Open quarterfinal with a 6-4, 6-4 win over Ekaterina Alexandrova in one hour and 21 minutes. The world No. 1 was measured and efficient — 1h21m for a 6-4, 6-4 win is clinical on any surface. Sabalenka's serve was her primary weapon, as it typically is, and Alexandrova could not find a way to disrupt the rhythm.

Sabalenka is the No. 1 seed for Wimbledon and the projected top seed when the draw is made on June 26. Her Berlin form reinforces that positioning. She faces Nikola Bartunkova in the quarterfinals.

Rybakina Through; Gauff Out

Elena Rybakina, the 2022 Wimbledon champion and the player who many consider the most natural grass-court player on the WTA Tour, advanced through her Berlin second-round match against Alexandra Eala to join Sabalenka in the last eight. Rybakina's game — flat, heavy serve, punishing forehand — is built for Wimbledon conditions, and the Berlin grass, similar in pace to SW19, is where she tends to find her best form after the clay season.

Coco Gauff, by contrast, will not be among the final four. Paula Badosa's comeback win over the No. 5 seed was the most significant result of the day (covered separately), and it leaves the Berlin draw more open than the seedings suggested entering the week.

QF Matchups

The quarterfinal pairings: Sabalenka vs. Bartunkova; Badosa vs. Pegula; Rybakina's quarterfinal match is set for Friday. Jessica Pegula had advanced through her side of the draw by defeating Katerina Siniakova in straight sets.

The Badosa-Pegula quarterfinal is particularly intriguing. Badosa, riding the momentum of her Gauff upset, faces one of the more consistent American performers on the WTA Tour. Pegula has been a major-contender-level player for three years without a Grand Slam title — Berlin is a chance to build more evidence.

What's Next

Berlin quarterfinals are Friday, June 19. Semifinals are Saturday, June 20. The final is Sunday, June 21 — the last major WTA grass-court result before the Wimbledon draw ceremony on June 26. Wimbledon begins June 29. The women's seedings for Wimbledon are based on the June 22 world rankings. A Berlin title could carry significant momentum into the Championships.
