Van de Zandschulp Upsets Defending Champion Paul to Reach Queen's Club Quarterfinals

By Elena Voss | June 18, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Tommy Paul came to the HSBC Championships at Queen's Club this week with something to defend. He won the 2024 title on these same grass courts. He missed the 2025 edition through injury, so this was his first chance to stand in the place where he had celebrated an ATP 500 title and attempt to repeat it. He was eliminated in the second round on Thursday by Botic van de Zandschulp, 6-4, 2-6, 6-3, in two hours and 27 minutes.

How the Match Unfolded

Van de Zandschulp, the unseeded Dutchman, controlled the opening set against Paul's typically aggressive baseline game. Paul adjusted and claimed the second 6-2 — a dominant response that suggested the match was turning. Van de Zandschulp did not let it. The Dutch player was the more consistent performer in a competitive third set, eventually closing it out 6-3 to eliminate the defending champion.

Paul had won his opening match in straight sets over American compatriot Zachary Svajda, arriving into Round 2 as a natural title contender at a venue he knows well. Van de Zandschulp's win is one of the notable results of the week at Queen's Club.

Queen's Club QF Landscape

The full quarterfinal draw at Queen's Club is now clear. Top seed Alex de Minaur — the Australian who arrived in West London as the highest-ranked player in the field — meets Brandon Nakashima, the American who has now reached back-to-back Queen's Club quarterfinals. Francisco Cerundolo, who beat Jenson Brooksby 6-0, 6-4 in the second round, faces British wildcard Arthur Fery. Ugo Humbert, who defeated Hamad Medjedovic 7-5, 6-3 on Thursday, plays van de Zandschulp. And Jiri Lehecka, who beat Rinky Hijikata 7-5, 7-6(4), faces the winner of the late match between Alejandro Davidovich Fokina and Corentin Moutet.

Also in action Thursday at Queen's Club: Lehecka, who reached the Libema Open final in 's-Hertogenbosch last week before losing to Kamil Majchrzak, is among the favorites for the title alongside de Minaur.

De Minaur's Wimbledon Ambitions

Alex de Minaur arrived at Queen's Club with a stated mission beyond the tournament itself. The Australian No. 1 has spoken openly about Wimbledon as a primary target, and his form through the early rounds — dispatching Denis Shapovalov 6-4, 6-1 in under an hour in Round 2 — has been precise. A de Minaur title at Queen's Club would be his first at the venue, and the timing, with Wimbledon 11 days away, would be meaningful.

What's Next

Queen's Club quarterfinals take place Friday, June 19. Semifinals are Saturday, June 20. The final is Sunday, June 21. Wimbledon qualifying begins Monday, June 22 at Roehampton, and the Championships begin June 29. The Queen's Club title is widely regarded as the strongest grass-court form guide for Wimbledon's first week.
