### STORY 1: Halle QF Set — Zverev, Shelton, Fritz All Through

Alexander Zverev got through his Round of 16 against Yannick Hanfmann 6-3, 4-6, 6-2. "Super Friday" in Halle brings Frances Tiafoe vs Felix Auger-Aliassime and Altmaier vs Medvedev. Then the Zverev/Shelton/Fritz side of the draw collides.

### STORY 2: Badosa Stuns Gauff in Berlin

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

Paula Badosa beat Coco Gauff today in Berlin. And the way she did it made it even better.

Badosa is ranked No. 142 in the world. Gauff is the No. 5 seed and the world No. 7. The first set was exactly what the numbers suggested — Gauff took it 6-1, hitting through Badosa and looking like she was about to end the match quickly.

Then Badosa stopped letting it happen.

She won 12 of the next 16 games. The second set went 6-3 to Badosa, and the third 6-2. The match lasted one hour and 35 minutes on grass. Badosa pumped her fist after the final point, visibly emotional.

She had been on a five-match losing streak coming into Berlin. This was her first WTA victory since Charleston in April. She said after: "I kept believing."

This is now Badosa's fifth career win over Gauff in eight meetings — a better record than nearly anyone else on the WTA Tour against the American. Next for Badosa is a quarterfinal against Jessica Pegula.

What do you make of Badosa's run?

### STORY 3: Van de Zandschulp Upsets Defending Champion Paul at Queen's Club

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Tommy Paul won the Queen's Club Championship in 2024. He came back this week to defend his title for the first time. He's out in the second round.

Botic van de Zandschulp, the unseeded Dutchman, defeated Paul 6-4, 2-6, 6-3 in a two-hour-and-27-minute battle on the grass of the HSBC Championships. Paul had beaten Zachary Svajda in the first round and looked like a realistic title contender coming in. Van de Zandschulp had other ideas.

Paul had missed last year's Queen's Club due to injury, so this was his first chance to defend a title at a venue that clearly suits his grass-court game. It wasn't to be.

Elsewhere Thursday at Queen's Club: Jiri Lehecka def. Rinky Hijikata 7-5, 7-6(4); Ugo Humbert def. Hamad Medjedovic 7-5, 6-3. The quarterfinal lineup: Alex de Minaur vs Brandon Nakashima, Francisco Cerundolo vs Arthur Fery, Humbert vs van de Zandschulp, and Lehecka vs the winner of tonight's Davidovich Fokina-Moutet match.

De Minaur, the top seed, remains the favourite. But Queen's Club is wide open.

### STORY 4: Berlin QF Picture — Sabalenka, Rybakina, Badosa Through; Gauff Out

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

The Berlin Open quarterfinal picture is set — and it's genuinely interesting heading into Wimbledon.

Aryna Sabalenka defeated Ekaterina Alexandrova 6-4, 6-4 to extend her run at this tournament. It's her third consecutive quarterfinal at the Berlin Open. She's looked dominant on the grass this week.

Elena Rybakina — the 2022 Wimbledon champion and grass specialist — also moved through. Rybakina on grass is always dangerous, and after a relatively quiet clay swing, Berlin is exactly where she tends to rediscover her best form.

Paula Badosa knocked out No. 5 seed Coco Gauff (see separate post) to grab a quarterfinal spot opposite Jessica Pegula.

The Berlin final is Sunday, June 21 — the last major grass-court result before Wimbledon reveals its draw on Friday, June 26. The seedings for Wimbledon are based on the June 22 world rankings. With Sabalenka (No. 1) and Rybakina (No. 2) both still in the draw, there's still everything to play for.

Which Berlin player do you see threatening at Wimbledon?
