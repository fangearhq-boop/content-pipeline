Sinner and Djokovic Withdraw from Canadian Open, Joining Alcaraz and Draper on Injury List

By Elena Voss | July 23, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The men's draw for the National Bank Open in Montreal has suffered two more significant losses. World No. 1 Jannik Sinner of Italy has withdrawn from the Canadian Open citing a right elbow injury he sustained at Wimbledon. Novak Djokovic of Serbia, 39, has also pulled out, pointing to a groin injury he suffered during the Wimbledon fortnight.

The Canadian Open runs August 1-13, with the men's tournament based in Montreal and the women's in Toronto. The draws for both events will be released on July 31.

Men's Field Takes a Series of Hits

Sinner and Djokovic join Carlos Alcaraz and Jack Draper on the men's withdrawal list, meaning four players capable of reaching the top of any draw will not compete in Montreal. Alcaraz has been managing a wrist issue, while Draper cited an arm injury for his absence.

Sinner said he is targeting the Western & Southern Open in Cincinnati and then his US Open title defense at Flushing Meadows. Djokovic has taken an even more direct route — he is skipping North America entirely and will prepare for the US Open without any warm-up tournament.

The depth of the withdrawal list is unusual even by the standards of the post-Wimbledon swing, when players routinely reassess before committing to the hard-court Masters events. Losing Sinner, Djokovic, Alcaraz, and Draper from the same draw before the field is even announced represents a significant change to what the Canadian Open will look like.

Women's Field Also Depleted at Toronto

The withdrawals are not limited to the men's side. At Toronto, the women's draw is facing its own significant absences. Defending champion Victoria Mboko is out with an ankle injury. Jasmine Paolini of Italy, ranked No. 15 in the world, announced she is withdrawing with a lingering foot problem and will also skip the Washington tournament. Karolina Muchova confirmed she underwent a minor operation after Wimbledon and will miss several weeks of competition. Emma Raducanu is out with a shin stress fracture.

The combination of withdrawals on both the men's and women's sides means the Canadian Open will draw on a deeper pool of players to fill spots and decide its champions. That creates genuine opportunity for players ranked further down the list — the kind of opening that can lead to a career-defining result.

What's Next
