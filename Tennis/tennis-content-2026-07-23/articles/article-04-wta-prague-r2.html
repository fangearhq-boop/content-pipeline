Krejcikova Brings Athens Form Home to Prague — Faces Qualifier Havlickova in R2

By Ryan Calloway | July 23, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Barbora Krejcikova is playing some of the best tennis of her recent career — and on Thursday, she gets to do it at home.

The Czech player returns to TK Sparta Prague for her second-round match at the Livesport Prague Open (WTA 250, July 20-26) carrying a six-match winning streak built on her title run at the Vanda Pharmaceuticals Athens Open last weekend. That Athens win was her ninth career WTA title and her first since Wimbledon 2024. She opened her Prague campaign with a 6-2, 6-4 win over Linda Fruhvirtova in round one.

Havlickova Enters as the Crowd Wildcard

Krejcikova's Thursday opponent is Lucie Havlickova, a local qualifier ranked No. 226 in the world. This is their first tour-level meeting. Havlickova earned her second-round spot by defeating Alycia Parks of the United States, ranked No. 34, in the first round — a genuine upset. She is reportedly on a 10-match winning streak including her qualifying rounds.

The matchup carries a natural tension: Krejcikova is the established champion on a hot streak; Havlickova is the home qualifier riding momentum nobody anticipated. Prague crowds tend to respond to Czech success, and there will be local support for both players in different ways — Krejcikova as the Czech star, Havlickova as the local underdog story.

On the ranking and experience gap alone, Krejcikova is the clear favorite. But Havlickova has already beaten a top-40 player this week, which means she cannot be dismissed.

All-Czech Seeded Showdown Elsewhere in Draw

The Czech dominance at this year's Prague Open extends through the bracket. Top seed Marie Bouzkova faces fifth seed Tereza Valentova on Thursday, an all-Czech seeded confrontation that should produce high-quality baseline tennis. Third seed Sara Bejlek and fourth seed Nikola Bartunkova are also in action in the draw.

It is an unusually deep home representation at a WTA event. The Prague Open regularly draws Czech players, but this year's alignment of Krejcikova's form, Bouzkova's seeding, and Havlickova's qualifier run has given the home nation multiple story threads to follow simultaneously.
