Correction: Krejcikova Won Athens, Sherif Won Iasi — Two Finals Reported Incorrectly

By Marcus Cole | July 23, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Two results from the weekend of July 19-20 were reported incorrectly in our earlier coverage. We are issuing corrections for both. The verified results from the WTA official record are as follows.

Athens WTA Final: Krejcikova Defeats Sakkari

Barbora Krejcikova of the Czech Republic defeated Maria Sakkari of Greece 7-6(5), 6-3 to win the Vanda Pharmaceuticals Athens Open. Our previous reporting stated that Sakkari had won. That was incorrect.

The match was more contested than the final scoreline suggests. Sakkari, playing in front of her home crowd, led 4-1 in the first set and appeared to be in control. Krejcikova came back to claim the set in a tiebreak, then faced a similar challenge in the second — Sakkari broke first — before fighting through again to take the match.

The Athens title was Krejcikova's ninth career WTA championship and her first since Wimbledon 2024. She has since moved on to Prague, where she is in second-round action Thursday at the Livesport Prague Open.

Iasi WTA Final: Sherif Wins After Badosa Retires

Mayar Sherif of Egypt won the UniCredit Iasi Open title when Paula Badosa of Spain was forced to retire from the final while trailing 6-4, 4-0. Our previous reporting stated that Badosa had won. That was incorrect.

Badosa stopped play due to a leg and knee injury. Sherif, who held a two-set lead at the time of the retirement, was awarded the title. It was Sherif's second career WTA championship.

Despite her Iasi injury, Badosa competed in Hamburg this week. She won her second-round match against Katarzyna Kawa on Wednesday, demonstrating the kind of resilience that makes the circumstances of the Iasi final all the more notable. Sherif is also in Hamburg this week.

What's Next

Both corrected results are now reflected in Tennis Fanrecap's records. Krejcikova's second-round match at Prague is Thursday; Sherif and Badosa are both in action at Hamburg. We will have results from both tournaments as they come in.
