Burruchaga Keeps Rolling at Estoril — Beats Borges After Famous Wawrinka Win

By Elena Voss | July 23, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Roman Andres Burruchaga had already done the hard part — or so it seemed. On Tuesday, the Argentine beat Stan Wawrinka in what turned out to be the three-time Grand Slam champion's final clay-court match, a result that drew the week's biggest emotional attention at the Millennium Estoril Open.

On Wednesday, Burruchaga made clear that wasn't a one-off.

The Argentine defeated home favorite Nuno Borges of Portugal 6-1, 4-6, 6-3 in the second round to reach the Estoril quarterfinals. Borges, ranked No. 48 in the world, came into the match with a home crowd firmly behind him and pushed back in the second set. Burruchaga absorbed that momentum shift and closed the third set decisively.

A Week That Started With Wawrinka

The context around Burruchaga's Estoril run starts on Tuesday, when he beat Wawrinka 6-4, 4-6, 6-3 in the first round. Wawrinka, 41, has been on a farewell tour of sorts through the summer clay season, and Estoril was his last event on the red stuff. The tournament presented Wawrinka with a commemorative gift on court after the match.

Burruchaga did not linger on sentiment. He came back the next day and beat Borges. Two wins in, the Argentine is into the last eight at an ATP 250 level event, which would represent a solid result for a player ranked around No. 60 in the world.

His trajectory this summer has been quietly impressive. He reached the second round at Umag before coming to Estoril, where he has now beaten a clay-court legend and a ranked home player in consecutive matches.

Rublev Opens Campaign as Top Seed

While Burruchaga has been the story through the first two rounds, the tournament's top seed has yet to play. Andrey Rublev of Russia, ranked No. 14 in the world, opens his Estoril campaign Thursday against Timofey Skatov of Kazakhstan in a second-round match. Rublev arrives in Portugal fresh off his title at the Nordea Open in Bastad last weekend — a clay-court win that gives him momentum heading into this event.

Luciano Darderi of Italy is also in the Estoril field. Darderi faced Rublev in the Bastad final before losing, giving the draw a built-in subplot if both players continue through their respective halves.

The quarterfinals are expected over the coming days, with the tournament's final stages set for the weekend.
