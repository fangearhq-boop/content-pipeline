Kitzbühel Semifinals Set: Bublik, Etcheverry, Hanfmann, Navone Advance

By Ryan Calloway | July 23, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

All four quarterfinals at the Generali Open Kitzbühel were completed on Thursday, setting up a pair of compelling semifinal matchups for Friday at the ATP 250 clay-court event in Austria.

Defending champion Alexander Bublik of Kazakhstan, the tournament's top seed, dispatched Alex Molcan of Slovakia 6-3, 7-5 to advance without drama. Bublik, who won the title in Kitzbühel last year, looked composed on the Tyrolean clay and will face fourth seed Tomas Martin Etcheverry in Friday's first semifinal. Etcheverry had beaten Ignacio Buse of Peru, the fifth seed, 6-2, 7-6(4).

The other half of the draw produced a different kind of story. Neither Yannick Hanfmann nor Mariano Navone was seeded heading into the week, yet both advanced to the final four with convincing performances.

Navone Delivers the Day's Most Dominant Performance

Navone's 6-0, 6-3 defeat of Quentin Halys was the sharpest result of the quarterfinal slate. The scoreline was particularly striking given that Halys had beaten No. 2 seed Valentin Vacherot the previous day — it was not a soft draw. Navone simply did not allow Halys to settle into the match and claimed the win in under an hour of play.

Hanfmann's road was slightly more contested. The German beat Sebastian Baez 6-4, 7-6(2), taking the second set tiebreak comfortably after the Argentinian — who had upset third seed Arthur Rinderknech on Wednesday — pushed the match toward a tiebreak. Hanfmann held his nerve and closed it out.

Both players are now in a semifinal, and neither has been among the tournament's most recognized names this week. Hanfmann faces Navone on Friday — a matchup with no marquee seeds, but plenty of clay-court craft on both sides.

Bublik Chasing Back-to-Back Titles

The main storyline heading into the weekend is Bublik's pursuit of a second straight Kitzbühel title. The Kazakhstani has been measured in his approach through the draw, not always the flashiest but consistent. His semifinal opponent Etcheverry is a capable clay-court player from Argentina, and the match figures to be competitive.

Kitzbühel has been a reliable late-season summer clay event, and this year's quarterfinal slate delivered results across the board in straight sets — meaning the bracket arrived at the semifinals at pace and without any of the extended attrition that can sometimes disrupt a 250-level week.

What's Next

The semifinals are scheduled for Friday at the Generali Open Kitzbühel. Bublik faces Etcheverry in one match, and Hanfmann faces Navone in the other. The final is set for Saturday. Tennis Fanrecap will have semifinal coverage as results come in.
