### STORY 2: Canadian Open Men's Draw Gutted — Sinner and Djokovic Both Withdraw
- **Tier:** 1
- **Angle:** FOLLOW UP (July 22 pipeline) — The men's side of the National Bank Open Montreal (Aug. 1-13) has suffered two devastating blows. World No. 1 Jannik Sinner (ITA) withdrew from the Canadian Open citing a right elbow injury sustained at Wimbledon — he is targeting the Cincinnati Open and US Open title defence. Novak Djokovic (SRB, 39) also withdrew citing a groin injury suffered during Wimbledon and will head directly to the US Open with no North American warm-up. These join men's withdrawals already announced for Carlos Alcaraz (wrist) and Jack Draper (arm). The women's side (Toronto) remains similarly depleted: Paolini, Muchova, Mboko, Raducanu all out. The Canadian Open draws are released Friday July 31.

### STORY 3: CORRECTIONS — Krejcikova Won Athens; Sherif Won Iasi
- **Tier:** 2
- **Angle:** CORRECTION — Two major results from the July 19-20 weekend were reported incorrectly in prior pipeline runs. (1) Athens WTA: Barbora Krejcikova (CZE) defeated Maria Sakkari (GRE) 7-6(5), 6-3 to win the Vanda Pharmaceuticals Athens Open — her ninth career WTA title and first since Wimbledon 2024. Sakkari led 4-1 in the first set and broke first in the second but Krejcikova fought back both times. (2) Iasi WTA: Mayar Sherif (EGY) claimed her second career WTA title at the UniCredit Iasi Open when Paula Badosa (ESP) retired injured while trailing 6-4, 4-0. Badosa was forced to stop with a leg/knee injury. Krejcikova is now at Prague (R2 today vs. local qualifier Havlickova); Sherif is competing in Hamburg. Badosa also made it to Hamburg despite the injury retirement, and won her R2 match over Katarzyna Kawa on Wednesday.
- **Posting Window:** 12:30 PM ET
