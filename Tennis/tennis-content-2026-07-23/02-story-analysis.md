## Content Angle Decisions

### S1 — Kitzbühel QF Day
- **Lead angle:** All four QFs completed in one session — tournament moving efficiently
- **Narrative hook:** Defending champ Bublik cruises through; Navone's 6-0, 6-3 demolition of Halys (who beat No. 2 seed Vacherot) is the standout scoreline
- **SF preview:** Pair 1 (Bublik vs. Etcheverry) — champion vs. No. 4 seed; Pair 2 (Hanfmann vs. Navone) — both unseeded, neither a top-10 player, pure clay specialist battle
- **Article format:** Result recap + SF preview
- **Key stat:** Navone won 12 of 13 games to close out Halys in a dominant second set

### S2 — Canadian Open Men's Draw Gutted
- **Lead angle:** Sinner + Djokovic joining Alcaraz + Draper creates a top-4-level vacuum heading into Canada
- **Narrative hook:** The Canadian Open men's draw hasn't been released yet (July 31) but already the event has lost multiple top-5 caliber players
- **Stakes:** Cincinnati and US Open are next — Sinner targeting both; Djokovic going straight to Flushing Meadows
- **Women's side:** Equally depleted — Mboko (defending champion) + Paolini, Muchova, Raducanu all out
- **Avoid:** Speculating on who Sinner hurt against or how exactly Djokovic was injured — stick to confirmed facts

### S3 — Corrections (Athens + Iasi)
- **Tone:** Transparent, factual, no hedging — own the error directly
- **Frame:** "Two tournament finals from the July 19-20 weekend were reported incorrectly in our pipeline. Here are the corrected results."
- **Athens:** Krejcikova def. Sakkari 7-6(5), 6-3. Sakkari led 4-1 in set 1, broke first in set 2; Krejcikova fought back both times.
- **Iasi:** Sherif won Badosa's retirement at 6-4, 4-0. Badosa competed in Hamburg this week despite the injury.
- **Where they are now:** Krejcikova at Prague R2; Sherif at Hamburg; Badosa at Hamburg (won R2 vs. Kawa)

### S4 — WTA Prague R2
- **Lead angle:** Krejcikova riding a six-match winning streak into Prague, essentially playing a home tournament after her Athens title
- **Narrative hook:** Havlickova is a local qualifier ranked No. 226 who upset Parks (No. 34) to get here; she's on a 10-match winning streak herself — this is a great underdog setup
- **Czech home field:** All-Czech seeded showdown Bouzkova vs. Valentova; Bejlek, Bartunkova also in draw — Czech fans get an excellent home event
- **Article format:** Preview-style — match context, head-to-head (first meeting), what each player needs

### S5 — Estoril ATP
- **Lead angle:** Burruchaga continuing his remarkable run — three days, three wins including a legend (Wawrinka) and a home hero (Borges)
- **Narrative hook:** The Wawrinka farewell story from July 22 now has a sequel — the man who beat Stan is still standing and headed to the QF
