### STORY 2: Canadian Open Withdrawals

**Long-Form Post (10:30 AM ET)**

The Canadian Open men's draw hasn't even been released yet — it drops July 31 — and it's already missing four players who collectively own more Grand Slam titles than most tournaments see in a decade.

World No. 1 Jannik Sinner announced he's withdrawing with a right elbow injury he picked up at Wimbledon. He's targeting Cincinnati and will arrive at the US Open aiming to defend his title. Novak Djokovic is also out, citing a groin injury from Wimbledon — he's skipping the North American swing entirely and heading straight to Flushing Meadows. They join Carlos Alcaraz and Jack Draper on the men's withdrawal list.

The women's side at Toronto is equally depleted. Defending champion Victoria Mboko, Jasmine Paolini, Karolina Muchova, and Emma Raducanu are all confirmed out. That's a lot of big names who won't be in Canada when the tournament starts August 1.

The draw release on July 31 will tell us who's left to fill the field — and who might be setting up for a breakthrough run in the post-Wimbledon hard-court swing.

### STORY 5: Estoril ATP

**Long-Form Post (6:00 PM ET)**

Three days in at the Millennium Estoril Open, and Roman Andres Burruchaga has quietly become the story of the tournament.

On Tuesday, he beat three-time Grand Slam champion Stan Wawrinka in what turned out to be the final clay-court match of Wawrinka's career. The crowd gave Stan a fitting sendoff. Meanwhile, top seed Andrey Rublev — fresh off his Bastad title last weekend — opens his Estoril campaign Thursday against Timofey Skatov. Luciano Darderi, who fell to Rublev in the Bastad final, is also in the draw. If Rublev advances and Burruchaga keeps winning, we could be looking at a fascinating semifinal.

For now, Burruchaga is the story everyone in Lisbon is talking about.
