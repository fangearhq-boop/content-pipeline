Darkness Stops Djokovic-Tsitsipas — The Most Anticipated Match of Round 2 Resumes Wednesday at Wimbledon

By Marcus Cole | July 1, 2026

The second-round match between No. 7 seed Novak Djokovic and Stefanos Tsitsipas was suspended due to failing light on Tuesday evening and will resume on Wednesday — a Day 3 card that also features Andreeva vs Krejčíková, Sinner in Round 2, and Félix Auger-Aliassime vs Prizmic.

The most anticipated match of the second round at Wimbledon 2026 did not finish on Tuesday. Novak Djokovic and Stefanos Tsitsipas started their second-round contest, the light gave out before they could complete it, and the match was suspended. It will resume on Wednesday at the All England Club in front of whatever audience wants to be there for the conclusion — which, given who is involved, will be a full one.

Djokovic leads their all-time head-to-head record 12-2. He is the No. 7 seed, the seven-time Wimbledon champion, a player who treats this tournament as something apart from all others. Tsitsipas is currently ranked approximately No. 87 in the world — a long way from the top-five player he was when these two were meeting in back-to-back Roland Garros finals. He is a two-time Grand Slam finalist. He has beaten Djokovic twice in their 14 career meetings. He knows how to be dangerous.

The Match That Was and What Resumes

The Djokovic-Tsitsipas rivalry carries weight that does not disappear with a ranking differential. When these two play, they tend to produce long, competitive exchanges — Tsitsipas's one-handed backhand against Djokovic's ability to neutralize almost anything from the baseline, his serve against Djokovic's return. On grass, Djokovic's movement and reading of the ball tend to give him an additional advantage. But Tsitsipas has the game to create problems regardless of surface, and he has never been a player who folds without a fight.

Whatever the state of play when the match resumes on Wednesday, the fact of its suspension creates a narrative tension that the tournament would not have manufactured otherwise. The crowd that watched the first portion of it on Tuesday will know what they saw. The crowd that arrives on Wednesday will want to know how it ends.

The Rest of Day 3

Wednesday is the most loaded day of the first week so far. Beyond the Djokovic-Tsitsipas resumption, the order of play offers several matches worth marking.

Mirra Andreeva faces Barbora Krejčíková on Centre Court. Andreeva is the reigning Roland Garros champion — the 2026 French Open title coming in what was her most dominant clay-court performance to date. Krejčíková won Wimbledon in 2024. Two Grand Slam champions, different surfaces, same court on Wednesday. The winner not only advances to the third round but establishes herself as one of the favourites to go deep from that section of the draw.

Jannik Sinner faces Nuno Borges in his second round. Sinner needed five sets to get past Miomir Kecmanovic on Day 1 — a result that raised questions about his readiness after skipping all grass warm-up events this season. Borges is a different kind of opponent: reliable, consistent, not likely to produce the same set-stealing energy that Kecmanovic showed. But Sinner will want to answer the questions Tuesday raised with a more dominant performance.

Félix Auger-Aliassime meets Dino Prizmic on No. 1 Court. FAA, who has been one of the more consistent performers across the surface transition this year, enters his second round as a player who needs to show that his first-round form holds. Tommy Paul also has action on Wednesday.

The Tournament After Two Days

Two days at Wimbledon 2026 have already produced more upsets than a full week of some previous editions. Ben Shelton, the No. 4 men's seed, was beaten by a Finnish qualifier. Elina Svitolina, the No. 8 women's seed, lost to compatriot Snigur after being up 4-0 in the first set. Serena Williams, in her first Grand Slam singles match in four years, lost to a 20-year-old who had not won a match since January.

The draw is changing shape quickly. The players still standing know it. Wednesday is when the tournament starts to sort out who belongs in the second week and who was simply here for the first.

What's Next

The Djokovic-Tsitsipas match resumes Wednesday and will conclude. The Day 3 card then runs through Sinner-Borges, Andreeva-Krejčíková, FAA-Prizmic, and Paul's match. By the end of the day, the third round of Wimbledon 2026 will begin to take shape.

The first two days set a standard for drama. Day 3 has the material to match it. Whether the light holds long enough for all of it to be completed is, at least in the case of the Djokovic-Tsitsipas match, a question that already has an answer: it did not, once. The Wednesday crowd will make sure it finishes this time.
