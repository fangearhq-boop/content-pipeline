Snigur's Comeback from 0-4 Ends Svitolina's Wimbledon — No. 8 Seed's First Round 1 Exit Since 2018

By Elena Voss | July 1, 2026

Daria Snigur, a Ukrainian qualifier, recovered from 0-4 down in the opening set to defeat compatriot Elina Svitolina 7-5, 6-2 on Tuesday — sending the No. 8 seed to her earliest Wimbledon exit in eight years.

At 0-4 down in the first set, a qualifier facing a seeded two-time semi-finalist at a Grand Slam, there is generally one logical outcome. Daria Snigur did not take that outcome. She came back to win the set 7-5. Then she won the second 6-2. And Elina Svitolina — No. 8 seed, one of the most experienced players in the women's draw, a player who has reached the Wimbledon semi-finals twice — was out before the tournament had properly begun.

Svitolina had not lost in the first round at Wimbledon since 2018. That streak, built through years of deep runs and consistent professionalism at the All England Club, ended on Tuesday against her compatriot.

The Comeback in the First Set

The opening games went to Svitolina with the kind of ease that a seeded player expects against a qualifier on the grass of Wimbledon. She broke early, built a lead, and appeared to be setting up a comfortable passage into Round 2. The lead reached 4-0. On paper, the first set was over.

Snigur did not read the paper. She began to find her range — her serve finding targets, her groundstrokes staying in the court rather than testing the lines and missing them. Game by game, the deficit shrank. The scoreline moved from 0-4 to 1-4, to 2-4, then 3-4, then 4-4, and the arithmetic of the first set became very different from what it had been at the start.

Snigur took it 7-5. The first set had been won by the player who began it losing 0-4. That fact alone — before the match had reached its conclusion — told the story of what kind of afternoon this was going to be.

The Second Set and the Pattern

Svitolina found nothing in the second set to reverse what had happened in the first. Snigur, now with the confidence of a player who had just come back from 0-4, played with the kind of freedom that is difficult to play against. She took the second 6-2. Svitolina, who has shown at this tournament and others that she can find answers when the match requires them, did not find them.

The match ended in straight sets. The Ukrainian qualifier had beaten the Ukrainian seed in under two sets' worth of completed play, after losing the first four games of the match.

The Bigger Picture

Svitolina was one of three seeded women to exit Wimbledon in the first two days of play. Zheng Qinwen, the No. 4 seed, lost to Katerina Siniakova on Day 1. Donna Vekić also fell on Day 2. The women's draw — which had looked, on paper, like one of the stronger and more coherent seedings in recent memory — has already lost significant names before the second round.

Snigur's result is the most dramatic of those upsets, given the circumstances of the first set and the seeding of the player she beat. But it fits a pattern that has emerged quickly at Wimbledon 2026: the seedings have not held, the qualifiers have not behaved as expected, and the draw has opened in ways that were not predictable.

For Svitolina, this is a result she will want to forget quickly. She has built a reputation at Wimbledon as a player who arrives well-prepared and who leaves late. Tuesday was a departure from that in both directions — she left early, and left after a performance that gave her little to work with in the post-match review.

What's Next

Daria Snigur advances to the second round of Wimbledon 2026 as a qualifier who has now beaten a top-ten seed at a Grand Slam. She will be an unfamiliar name to most viewers watching the draw update. After Tuesday, she will be slightly less unfamiliar.

The women's draw continues to thin. With Svitolina, Zheng, and Vekić gone, the path through the bottom half of the bracket looks different from how it looked on Friday when the draw was made. The players who remain in that section of the draw will recognize the opportunity. Whether they take it will be the story of the next several days.
