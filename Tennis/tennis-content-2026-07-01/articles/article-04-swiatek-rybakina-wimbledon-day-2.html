Swiatek and Rybakina Both Need Three Sets — Top Seeds Survive But Are Not Yet Convincing

By Ryan Calloway | July 1, 2026

No. 3 Iga Swiatek defeated Taylor Townsend 6-1, 2-6, 6-3 and No. 2 Elena Rybakina defeated Lois Boisson 6-4, 1-6, 6-3 on Day 2 — both dropping a set before closing out their first-round matches.

On a day when three women's seeds had already exited or would exit Wimbledon 2026, No. 3 Iga Swiatek and No. 2 Elena Rybakina got through. That is the most important fact about Tuesday's women's action involving the upper seeds. The second most important fact is how they got through — and the picture is not entirely comfortable.

Both won the first set. Both lost the second. Both came back to win the third. The pattern was so similar between the two matches that you could almost set it to the same rhythm. The results were the same. The performances were not identical, but the shape was: confident opening, second-set difficulty, third-set resolution.

Swiatek vs Townsend

Swiatek opened against Taylor Townsend with the kind of first set that her opponents typically struggle to respond to — 6-1 in dominant, controlled fashion, with the Polish No. 3 seed reading the patterns off Townsend's serve and constructing points with the efficiency that makes her hard to play against at any surface.

Townsend, who brings a serve-and-volley game that is unusual on the women's tour, found her answers in the second set. She closed the net more aggressively, changed the tempo, and disrupted Swiatek's rhythm enough to take the second 6-2. The momentum shift was clear. What had looked like an efficient afternoon suddenly required a third set.

Swiatek took it 6-3. She adapted, closed her errors, and ended the match with fewer loose shots than the second set had produced. She is through. But she will know that a player who takes a full second set in straight-set fashion is finding something in the match, and that the players who await in the later rounds will look for the same openings Townsend found.

Rybakina vs Boisson

Rybakina's match against French player Lois Boisson followed a similar arc, with the second set even more pronounced. After winning the first 6-4, Rybakina lost the second 1-6 — a set in which Boisson played to a standard well above what her ranking might suggest and Rybakina seemed to lose the thread of her own game. The tightness that defines Rybakina's best tennis — her serve, her flat groundstrokes, her ability to hit through the court — gave way to something less decisive.

The third set went 6-3 to Rybakina. She found it again. The win is secure. But the second set will be a reference point for future opponents looking for a way to create problems against her in a match that seems to be decided.

What It Means

Swiatek and Rybakina are the No. 3 and No. 2 seeds respectively. They are not expected to cruise every match at a Grand Slam. First rounds produce different opponents and different days, and it is common for high seeds to find three sets in the opening round before finding a more consistent level as the week progresses.

But the context on Tuesday gives the performances an added weight. Svitolina, the No. 8 seed, lost to a qualifier who came back from 0-4 down. Zheng, the No. 4, had already fallen on Day 1. The draw is thinning in ways that suggest a genuine opening for anyone in the women's bracket capable of performing over a sustained period.

Swiatek and Rybakina are still the two most likely players to go deep from the bottom half of the draw. But they will need to find the first-set level more consistently to justify that position as the competition increases.

What's Next

Both players enter the second round of Wimbledon 2026. Swiatek, who is in the same quarter of the draw as Serena Williams had been (now gone), will face a different opponent whose name she will study with more attention than she gave to Tuesday's result. Rybakina, who won the tournament in 2022, has the grass game to go deep here regardless of how the first round went.

The second round begins on Wednesday. Both seeds will expect to be better. The draw, after two days of upsets, is giving them the space to find out.
