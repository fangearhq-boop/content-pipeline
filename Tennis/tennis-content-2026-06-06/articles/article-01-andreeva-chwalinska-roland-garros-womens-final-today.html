Andreeva vs Chwalinska: Roland Garros Women's Final Is Here — Two First-Timers for the Title

By Elena Voss | June 6, 2026

Court Philippe-Chatrier hosts its women's final on Saturday, and for the first time in a very long time, neither player stepping onto the clay has ever stood at this stage before. Mirra Andreeva, the No. 8 seed, faces Maja Chwalinska, a qualifier ranked No. 114 in the world, in what is already one of the most unusual Grand Slam finals the tournament has seen in years.

The match is scheduled not before 3:00 PM Paris time — 9:00 AM ET — following the conclusion of the men's doubles final. Both women will be making their Grand Slam singles final debut.

The Teenager with the Pressure

Andreeva turned 19 in April and has been inside the world's top ten for most of the past year. At Roland Garros she has been virtually untouchable — her most difficult match was a semifinal in which she dismantled Marta Kostyuk 6-1, 6-3 in just 76 minutes, despite Kostyuk having arrived at that match on a 17-match winning streak on clay in 2026.

She is the fourth-youngest Roland Garros women's finalist in 30 years. Only Martina Hingis, Kim Clijsters, and Coco Gauff were younger when they reached this stage of this tournament. The company she keeps at 19 speaks to the rare level of her tennis.

Andreeva enters Saturday as the clear favourite. She has the ranking, the form, and the experience of already having played a Roland Garros semifinal — she reached the last four here in 2025 and fell. This year she goes a round further.

The Qualifier Who Rewriting History

Maja Chwalinska's path to Saturday's final is one of the most improbable in the tournament's history. She entered Roland Garros as a qualifier, meaning she had to win three matches in qualifying before the main draw even began. Then she won seven more.

No qualifier had ever reached the Roland Garros women's final before Chwalinska. She is also only the second women's qualifier to reach a Grand Slam final in the Open Era — the first achieved the same feat at a different Slam. And she is just the third woman to reach the Roland Garros final on her first appearance in the main draw, following Evonne Goolagong in 1971 and Chris Evert in 1973.

Her semifinal victory over Diana Shnaider, 7-6(4), 6-4, showed resilience and tactical quality that no one outside her inner circle was anticipating at the start of the fortnight. She serves well, moves efficiently, and — evidently — does not feel the weight of the occasion the way a qualifier was supposed to.

What the Match Could Turn On

Andreeva's baseline game has been dominant throughout the tournament. She generates heavy topspin from both wings and has shown an ability to redirect pace that makes her difficult to attack. Against Kostyuk, she was near-perfect through the whole match.

Chwalinska's game is built differently — she is a left-handed player whose serve can pull Andreeva wide, and she possesses the kind of unpredictability that comes from having nothing to lose. She has won 10 consecutive matches to reach this final. She is not supposed to be here, which is exactly why she is.

The court is Philippe-Chatrier in full final configuration. There will be a full crowd. The atmosphere will be unlike anything either player has experienced at Grand Slam level. How each of them manages that first-final nerves question may prove as important as anything tactical.

What's Next

The Roland Garros women's final between Mirra Andreeva and Maja Chwalinska takes place Saturday, June 6, not before 3:00 PM Paris time (9:00 AM ET) on Court Philippe-Chatrier. The men's final between Alexander Zverev and Flavio Cobolli follows on Sunday, June 7, at the same time. Full broadcast details are available at rolandgarros.com.
