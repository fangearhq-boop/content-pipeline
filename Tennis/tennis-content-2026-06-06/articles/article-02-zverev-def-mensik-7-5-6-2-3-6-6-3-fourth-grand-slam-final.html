Zverev Beats Mensik 7-5, 6-2, 3-6, 6-3 to Reach Fourth Grand Slam Final at Roland Garros

By Marcus Cole | June 6, 2026

Alexander Zverev is in another Grand Slam final. The German No. 2 seed defeated 20-year-old Jakub Mensik 7-5, 6-2, 3-6, 6-3 on Friday on Court Philippe-Chatrier, completing the match in three hours and one minute to book his place in Sunday's Roland Garros final. It is Zverev's fourth Grand Slam final — and his second at this tournament.

Mensik, in the first Grand Slam semifinal of his career, was beaten by an opponent who had seen all of this before: the moment, the stage, the pace, the crowd. Zverev's authority through the first two sets was emphatic. The third went differently. It did not change the result.

Two Sets of Dominance

Zverev established control early. He broke in the opening set's seventh game and served out the opener 7-5 with a degree of comfort that masked the quality of Mensik's ball-striking in the losing games. In the second set he was even clearer, breaking twice to move through 6-2 without the Czech youngster ever finding a way back into the pattern of the set.

Mensik had been the more talked-about player heading into this week's semifinals. At 20, he had reached his first Grand Slam semifinal after qualifying rounds at Roland Garros in a year of consistent growth on the ATP Tour. His serve, his backhand, and his drop-shot repertoire were expected to provide problems that Zverev would need time to solve. Through two sets, it looked like Zverev had solved them before they were properly posed.

The Third Set and Mensik's Medical Timeout

The third set was different. Mensik took a medical timeout midway through the set, and returned to the court with sharper rhythm and more variety. He won the third 6-3, keeping the match alive and giving the crowd a reason to stay in their seats for a fourth set that looked, briefly, like it might genuinely extend the contest.

Zverev's response to losing a set has been one of the defining features of his Grand Slam campaign this fortnight. He went up 3-0 in the fourth set before Mensik had re-established the form that won him the third. From 3-0 down in a deciding set against a player of Zverev's calibre — patient, physical, comfortable on clay — there is almost no way back. Mensik found none.

Zverev closed it out 6-3, completing a 7-5, 6-2, 3-6, 6-3 win in three hours and one minute.

A Record of Near-Misses

This will be the fourth Grand Slam final of Zverev's career. His previous three appearances — the US Open in 2020, Roland Garros in 2024, and the Australian Open in 2025 — ended in defeat each time. He has been close to a major title on three separate occasions, and three times it has slipped away.

Sunday offers another chance. His opponent is Flavio Cobolli of Italy, who advanced to the final after Matteo Arnaldi withdrew from their all-Italian semifinal with a viral illness. Cobolli is the No. 10 seed in his first Grand Slam final. Zverev leads their head-to-head 3-1, though Cobolli beat him in Munich earlier in 2026.

Whatever the outcome on Sunday, Zverev arrives at the final having dropped just two sets across his six matches at this tournament. He has been the best player in the draw throughout the fortnight. The question that has followed him for years — can he win a Grand Slam when it matters? — gets its next answer on Sunday afternoon on Philippe-Chatrier.

What's Next

Alexander Zverev faces Flavio Cobolli in the Roland Garros men's final on Sunday, June 7, not before 3:00 PM Paris time (9:00 AM ET) on Court Philippe-Chatrier.
