Arnaldi Withdraws with Viral Illness, Cobolli Advances to Roland Garros Final via Walkover

By Ryan Calloway | June 6, 2026

The all-Italian Roland Garros semifinal between Matteo Arnaldi and Flavio Cobolli never happened. Arnaldi, 25, withdrew on Friday morning after suffering from a viral illness that began with vomiting and fever on Thursday night. Despite consulting a doctor early Friday and taking medication, he was unable to keep food or liquid down throughout the day. He made the decision to pull out before the match was scheduled to begin.

Cobolli advances to his first Grand Slam final via walkover. It is only the fourth time in the Open Era that a Grand Slam semifinal — men's or women's — has ended this way.

The Illness That Ended Italy's Final Dream

Arnaldi's withdrawal was not a result of an injury sustained in competition. The viral illness came on suddenly on Thursday night, the night before the scheduled semifinal, and worsened through Friday morning despite medical attention. He had no choice but to withdraw.

The decision brought an abrupt end to what had been one of the most compelling subplots of Roland Garros 2026: the first all-Italian men's Grand Slam semifinal in history. Arnaldi had beaten Matteo Berrettini in the quarterfinals — Berrettini retired with a hip injury — and reached the last four of a Grand Slam for the first time in his career.

Italy was still guaranteed a finalist regardless. Cobolli will represent the country in Sunday's final against Alexander Zverev. It will be Italy's first men's Grand Slam finalist since Adriano Panatta won Roland Garros in 1976 — the same tournament where the last Italian men's finalist had appeared.

Cobolli's Path to the Final

Flavio Cobolli, 24, is the No. 10 seed. He has dropped only two sets across his six matches at this tournament, and his biggest win came in the quarterfinals against No. 4 seed Felix Auger-Aliassime. His campaign has been built on consistent serving, aggressive baseline play, and an ability to stay composed against higher-ranked opponents in a draw depleted of its top names.

He did not ask for the walkover. He would have preferred to play the match. But the result stands, and on Sunday afternoon he will step onto Court Philippe-Chatrier against Zverev with his first Grand Slam title on the line.

Cobolli and Zverev have met four times on tour, with Zverev holding a 3-1 edge in their head-to-head. Cobolli's one win came in Munich earlier in 2026 — on an indoor clay court. Sunday's match on red outdoor clay, with a Grand Slam trophy at stake, is a different proposition entirely.

A Rare Historical Footnote

The walkover places Friday's non-match in select company. Only three other Grand Slam semifinals in the Open Era have ended this way. That rarity does not make Friday's result feel any less strange for anyone who had looked forward to watching two Italians compete for the right to a Grand Slam final for the first time in the sport's history.

Arnaldi's absence from Sunday's final is a genuine sporting loss, though no one can hold his illness against him. What happened was unfortunate. What follows — Cobolli in a Grand Slam final — is still remarkable, whatever the circumstances that put him there.

What's Next

Flavio Cobolli faces Alexander Zverev in the Roland Garros men's final on Sunday, June 7, not before 3:00 PM Paris time (9:00 AM ET) on Court Philippe-Chatrier. Zverev leads their career head-to-head 3-1. Both are seeking their first Grand Slam title. Updates and match information are available at rolandgarros.com and atptour.com.
