Post-Wimbledon: The ATP Turns to Clay — Bastad, Gstaad, and Umag All Begin Monday

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 13, 2026

Wimbledon is over. The trophy ceremonies have concluded, the covers have gone back on the courts at the All England Club, and the ATP Tour has already moved on — back to clay. Three ATP 250 events open the week of July 13: the Nordea Open in Bastad, the Gonet Geneva Open in Gstaad, and the Croatia Open in Umag. All three run through July 19.

Three Simultaneous ATP 250s

The post-Wimbledon clay swing is one of the tour's more unusual scheduling windows: three ATP 250 events running simultaneously across three European nations, all on red clay, all in the same week. The Nordea Open in Bastad, Sweden, is the northernmost of the three and one of the tour's oldest clay-court events. The Gonet Geneva Open in Gstaad, Switzerland, takes place at altitude in the Swiss Alps. The Croatia Open in Umag is a coastal Adriatic clay event with a relaxed, summery character.

None of the top players are expected to compete immediately after Wimbledon — the major stars typically rest following a Grand Slam — but the events serve as valuable match opportunities for the second tier of the tour and for players looking to build ranking points on clay before the hard-court swing begins.

On the WTA side, the tournament in Iasi, Romania, also begins July 13 and runs through July 19.

The Summer Clay Swing

The European clay season that follows Wimbledon is a distinct chapter of the tennis calendar, sandwiched between the grass season and the North American hard-court swing. It does not produce the same global attention as Roland Garros or the major hard-court events, but it matters. For clay specialists who struggle on grass, it is a chance to return to their preferred surface and build momentum. For rising players, it offers ranking points outside the shadow of the very top names.

The following week — July 20 onward — adds the Generali Open in Kitzbühel, Austria (ATP 250), along with WTA events in Prague and Hamburg. Kitzbühel in particular has a long tradition as an Alpine clay-court event with a loyal, passionate fan base and a distinctive outdoor atmosphere.

What Comes After

After the European clay swing concludes in late July, the tour pivots sharply to hard courts for the North American summer. The lead-up to the US Open — which begins August 24 in New York — includes major events in Montreal, Cincinnati, and Washington D.C. That stretch will feature all of the top names: Sinner returning as Wimbledon champion, Zverev looking to rebuild after his final defeat, and Djokovic targeting a deep run at Flushing Meadows.

The US Open itself will be the last Grand Slam of the 2026 season and the next opportunity for anyone to challenge Sinner's position at the top of the game. For now, the European summer rolls on, and three simultaneous clay-court draws have their opening rounds to play.

What's Next

All three ATP 250 clay events (Bastad, Gstaad, Umag) and WTA Iasi run through July 19. The Generali Open in Kitzbühel and WTA Prague and Hamburg begin the following week (July 20). The North American hard-court swing begins in late July with Washington D.C. and Montreal, leading to Cincinnati and then the US Open from August 24.
