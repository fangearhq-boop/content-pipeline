Auger-Aliassime Into Libema Open QF After Clinical Win Over Fucsovics

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

Félix Auger-Aliassime made his 's-Hertogenbosch debut look easy. The Canadian top seed, who arrived at the Libema Open with a career-high No. 4 world ranking and a bye into the second round, defeated Hungary's Marton Fucsovics 6-3, 6-4 on Thursday — a performance that validated the ranking without breaking a sweat.

FAA did not face a single break point in the match. He converted two of his four break opportunities. His record against Fucsovics now stands at 5-2. On a surface that favours big servers and natural athletes, the 25-year-old Canadian demonstrated precisely the control that has driven him to a career-high ranking heading into the Wimbledon fortnight.

The No. 4 Version

When a player arrives at a new career-high ranking, the question is always whether the form matches the number. Against Fucsovics, FAA gave a clear answer. The Canadian's serve was dominant, his groundstrokes controlled, and his decision-making throughout the match reflected a player with clarity about his approach on grass.

His candid take on the ranking: "There's three guys ahead of me now." It's a No. 4-in-the-world quote — self-aware, quietly driven, acknowledging the gap while signalling the ambition to close it. For a player who has spent years at the edge of the top 10, reaching No. 4 represents genuine progress, and 's-Hertogenbosch offers a chance to show that the ranking reflects a new level.

QF vs Majchrzak

Auger-Aliassime meets Poland's Kamil Majchrzak in Friday's quarterfinal. Majchrzak is a grass specialist who has had his best results on this surface and will be dangerous in front of a Dutch crowd that appreciates the occasion. FAA's task is to sustain Thursday's performance: break-point free service games, measured returning, and the kind of controlled aggression that made his win over Fucsovics look uncomplicated.

The Libema Open field still includes Daniil Medvedev (No. 2 seed), who has had historically complicated relationships with grass-court tennis but brings quality to every tournament he enters. If FAA and Medvedev navigate their respective halves, a potential semifinal between them would be the marquee match of the 's-Hertogenbosch week.

What's Next

FAA plays Majchrzak in the Libema Open quarterfinal on Friday June 12 in 's-Hertogenbosch, Netherlands. The tournament final is Sunday June 14. A title here would send Auger-Aliassime into Wimbledon with a grass-court trophy and the momentum of a complete week's work. Given Thursday's performance, the trajectory is pointing upward.
