Sinner Skips All Tour Grass Events But Picks Hurlingham as His Wimbledon Bridge

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

Jannik Sinner is taking an unconventional path into his Wimbledon title defence. The world No. 1 has withdrawn from every official grass-court tournament on the ATP schedule — Halle, Queen's Club, Stuttgart, 's-Hertogenbosch — and will instead prepare for Wimbledon by competing in the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at Hurlingham Club in London from June 23 to 27.

The decision, confirmed this week, is calculated. Sinner had cardiac tests at San Raffaele Hospital in Milan last week and received an all-clear after a 4-hour battery of checks including a cardiac MRI, Holter monitor, and stress ECG. No pathology was found. But the episode reinforced the case for careful physical management, and bypassing the physical toll of a full ranking-event run while still getting grass-court competition is a defensible strategy for any player, let alone one who arrived at the health scare from a Roland Garros campaign that ended abruptly.

The Hurlingham Lineup

The Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic is in its 32nd edition. Sinner joins a field that includes Roland Garros 2026 finalist Flavio Cobolli, Luciano Darderi — who is ranked No. 18 ATP — and British No. 1 Cameron Norrie. The event runs from June 23 to 27 at one of London's most prestigious venues, and it has long served as a pre-Wimbledon gathering point for elite players who want quality grass reps without formal match stakes.

The Italian contingent at Hurlingham — Sinner, Cobolli, and Darderi — represents three of the most watched men's players of 2026. Cobolli arrived at Roland Garros as a qualifier and left as a finalist; his form this season has been exceptional. Playing alongside Sinner and Darderi on grass before Wimbledon draws gives all three competitive preparation while keeping the physical investment limited.

The Timing Is Precise

Wimbledon's draw ceremony is scheduled for June 26 — which falls during the Hurlingham event. Sinner could receive his first-round opponent while still hitting grass-court balls at Hurlingham. There's a useful symmetry to that: finding out the challenge while actively preparing for it.

New partner BNP Paribas joins the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic as its only platinum partner. Their 'Points For Change' initiative pledges £2 for every point won in matches and £50 for every ace, with proceeds directed to local community programmes through the Hurlingham Club Foundation. With Sinner serving and Cobolli in the lineup, the charity count should accumulate quickly.

Luxury jeweller Boodles also makes its tournament debut, adding a commercial dimension that reflects the event's growing profile as a pre-Wimbledon fixture.

A Novel Grass Strategy

No world No. 1 in recent memory has skipped every official grass-court event in the week or weeks before Wimbledon. Sinner's approach is genuinely new. The reasoning is clear: his cardiac scare generated real uncertainty about his physical readiness, his body needs careful handling, and the marginal ranking points from a grass warm-up event don't justify the physical risk when he already holds the Wimbledon title and maximum defending points.

What Sinner cannot afford is to arrive at SW19 without having served on grass, without having read the ball off a grass-court surface, without the physical memory of what grass tennis demands. Hurlingham solves that without adding medical risk.

What's Next

Sinner will compete at Hurlingham June 23-27, with the Wimbledon draw ceremony on June 26 during the event. The Championships begin June 29. Sinner enters as the defending champion, as world No. 1, and with Carlos Alcaraz — last year's finalist — withdrawn due to a wrist injury. If his health holds and his Hurlingham preparation delivers match sharpness, Sinner will be the man to beat at SW19 for the second consecutive year.
