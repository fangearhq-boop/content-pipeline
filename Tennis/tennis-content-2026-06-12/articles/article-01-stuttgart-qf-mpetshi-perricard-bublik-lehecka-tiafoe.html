Stuttgart QF Friday: Mpetshi Perricard's Ace Machine Meets Bublik's Showmanship

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 12, 2026

Quarterfinal Friday at the BOSS Open in Stuttgart delivers exactly what the grass-court season needs: a matchup defined by contrast. Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard, the French big server who has piled up 49 aces in this tournament, faces Alexander Bublik — the Kazakh No. 3 seed whose brand of grass-court tennis involves trick shots, the occasional underarm serve, and genuine quality buried underneath the theatre. Their first-ever meeting on grass could be Stuttgart's signature match of the week.

Alongside that clash, the other half of Friday's quarterfinal program offers Jiri Lehecka against Frances Tiafoe — two seeded players hungry for their best result of the grass swing ahead of Wimbledon. With Ben Shelton (No. 1 seed) and defending champion Taylor Fritz in the bracket, Saturday's semifinal lineup will tell us a great deal about who is genuinely ready for the fortnight at SW19.

The Ace Machine vs The Showman

Numbers first: 49 aces in a single ATP 250 event is a figure that commands attention. Mpetshi Perricard hasn't merely been serving well this week — he's been serving at a level that removes entire service games from competitive discussion. When your opponent has no reasonable chance of breaking you, you are playing with a structural advantage that rewrites the mathematics of a match.

Bublik presents a specific challenge to that approach. The Kazakh, who beat Jan-Lennard Struff 7-6(3), 3-6, 6-2 in his Wednesday match, is dangerous precisely because he keeps himself in points other players would have lost and finds angles that other players wouldn't attempt. His 2-1 head-to-head lead over Mpetshi Perricard is real, but it was established on different surfaces. This is their first grass meeting, and grass rewards the serve-heavy game that the Frenchman has built this week.

Mattia Bellucci, the Italian who beat Yannick Hanfmann 7-5, 6-7(4), 6-2 in his route to the quarterfinals, is also part of the Stuttgart story. The Italian's consistent grass-court performances show that the QF bracket has genuine depth beyond the top seeds.

Lehecka vs Tiafoe: The Other Side of the Draw

The second quarterfinal on Friday features two players who entered Stuttgart needing results and have delivered them. Jiri Lehecka and Frances Tiafoe are both seeded and both capable of reaching a final at this level. Their matchup offers different qualities: Lehecka's controlled ball-striking from the baseline versus Tiafoe's ability to elevate in moments and create chaos with his movement and athleticism.

Neither man has won a grass-court title yet. Stuttgart would represent a career statement on the surface for whichever of them reaches Sunday's final. Wimbledon entries are still being shaped by results this week and next, and a deep run at a pre-Wimbledon event carries weight — in confidence, in ranking points, and in the form that carries players into major draws.

Shelton and Fritz Watching

The bracket's other half features Ben Shelton, the American top seed who has been the most consistent performer at Stuttgart. Shelton's grass-court game — centred on a dominant serve and explosive groundstrokes — is well-suited to this surface. The defending champion Taylor Fritz, who has dealt with knee tendinitis since the start of 2026, is also still alive and testing whether his body can carry him through to a title defence.

The semifinal on Saturday will clarify which storylines Stuttgart ultimately delivers. Fritz defending is one narrative. A first grass title for a new name is another. The final on Sunday June 14 brings it all together.

What's Next

Friday's quarterfinals at the BOSS Open determine four semifinalists, with Saturday's semifinals setting up the June 14 final. The week at Stuttgart has already delivered compelling tennis across multiple rounds, and the quarterfinal format promises more. For the players advancing, Sunday's trophy ceremony represents not just a Stuttgart title but a confidence boost heading into the Wimbledon fortnight — which begins June 29.
