### STORY 3: Sinner Confirms Hurlingham — Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic June 23-27

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

Jannik Sinner has confirmed his Wimbledon preparation plan — and it's unconventional by any recent standard.

The world No. 1 will skip every official grass-court tournament on the ATP schedule (no Halle, no Stuttgart, no Queen's Club, no 's-Hertogenbosch) and instead compete in the Giorgio Armani Tennis Classic at Hurlingham Club in London from June 23 to 27.

The 32nd edition of the prestigious invitation event features a strong Italian lineup: Sinner joins Roland Garros 2026 finalist Flavio Cobolli and No. 18-ranked Luciano Darderi, plus British No. 1 Cameron Norrie. The timing is precise — Wimbledon's draw ceremony is June 26, which falls during the Hurlingham event, meaning Sinner could find out his first-round opponent while actively practicing on grass.

New partner BNP Paribas joins as the event's platinum sponsor, pledging £2 for every point won and £50 for every ace in support of local community programmes through The Hurlingham Club Foundation. With Sinner and Cobolli both capable of big serves, that charity total will climb quickly.

Sinner arrives at Wimbledon (June 29) as defending champion, world No. 1, and with Carlos Alcaraz — last year's finalist — already withdrawn due to a wrist injury. The preparation path is unconventional. The runway is clear.

### STORY 5: Wimbledon 17 Days Out — Sinner Defends as Alcaraz Withdraws

#### Long-Form Post — 9:00 PM ET

Wimbledon 2026 opens in 17 days. The draw is in two weeks. And the field just became clearer.

Carlos Alcaraz — Wimbledon champion in 2023 and 2024, runner-up to Sinner in last year's final — has withdrawn due to a right wrist injury. That removes the man who pushed Sinner to four sets in 2025 from the equation entirely.

Jannik Sinner defends his title (won over Alcaraz 4-6, 6-4, 6-4, 6-4) as the clear favourite in an increasingly open men's draw. Without Alcaraz, and with Novak Djokovic's participation uncertain, the path through the bracket looks more navigable for every other player in it. Who can realistically challenge? The grass swing this week is generating candidates — Bublik at Stuttgart, Fritz defending, Shelton showing form, FAA dominant at 's-Hertogenbosch. All of them need results to carry into the fortnight.

On the women's side, Iga Swiatek defends the title she won in historic fashion — a 6-0, 6-0 final over Amanda Anisimova that completed her career Grand Slam and announced her as a grass-court champion. Defending on grass after that statement is a different psychological challenge. Elena Rybakina, the 2022 Wimbledon champion playing at Queen's Club this week, is the perennial favourite on the surface. And Mirra Andreeva — at 19, fresh from winning Roland Garros — brings the most intriguing question: can she transfer clay momentum to grass in 17 days?

Wimbledon 2026: June 29 to July 12. Draw ceremony: June 26.
