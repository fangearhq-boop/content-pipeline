Taylor Fritz, the defending champion at the Rothesay International Eastbourne, withdrew from the ATP 250 event on June 25 — a last-minute decision made after his pre-match warm-up. The world No. 6 and Wimbledon sixth seed, who had reached finals in both Stuttgart and Halle in the previous two weeks, cited the need to protect himself ahead of Wimbledon, which begins June 29. In an emotional statement, Fritz said he was "very sorry" and acknowledged the decision was one he took only after serious consideration, but that he needed to give himself the best possible chance to be fully fit for the Grand Slam. The withdrawal blew the Eastbourne field wide open, with Jack Draper, Toby Samuel, Ugo Humbert, and Jan Choinski among the quarterfinalists now vying for the title. As a notable consequence of the withdrawal, Djokovic moves ahead of Fritz in the ATP live rankings.

**Tier:** 2

**Sources:** atptour.com, tennishead.net, tennisuptodate.com, lta.org.uk
**Posting Window:** 10:30 AM ET
**Platforms:** X, Facebook, Article

Dan Evans, the veteran British No. 1 who announced earlier this season that Wimbledon 2026 would be the final event of his professional tennis career, played his last-ever professional singles match on June 25 at the Wimbledon qualifying competition in Roehampton. Evans lost in the second round to Australian Tristan Schoolkate 7-5, 6-0. The 36-year-old will still compete in the Wimbledon doubles main draw alongside fellow British player Henry Searle. Thursday marked the final day of qualifying (June 22-25), with 256 competitors across four days battling for 32 main-draw spots. Four British players — Heather Watson, Oliver Tarvet, Billy Harris, and Max Basing — made it to the final qualifying round. The Wimbledon draw ceremony takes place Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST. The Championships begin Monday June 29.

With Wimbledon starting in four days, both the ATP Mallorca Championships and Rothesay International Eastbourne delivered their quarterfinals on June 25. At Mallorca, Grigor Dimitrov — a Wimbledon wild card who has been rebuilding from injury — and second seed Alejandro Davidovich Fokina faced each other in the quarterfinals. Luciano Darderi, who won his first career grass-court quarterfinal on June 24, continued his run. Frances Tiafoe, the Halle champion, leads the Mallorca field. At Eastbourne, with Fritz withdrawn, Jack Draper beat Gabriel Diallo 7-5, 6-4 in the quarterfinals to reach a first grass-court semifinal of the year. British wild card Toby Samuel also advanced. Ugo Humbert progressed in the all-French contest. The Eastbourne semifinal is Friday June 26; the final is Saturday June 27. Mallorca final also Saturday June 27 — two days before Wimbledon begins.
