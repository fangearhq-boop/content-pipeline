### STORY 1: Fritz Withdraws from Eastbourne

#### Long-Form Post — 8:00 AM ET

Taylor Fritz's Wimbledon build-up just changed dramatically.

The defending Rothesay International Eastbourne champion withdrew from the tournament on Thursday morning — after his warm-up, not before — in a decision that was clearly not taken lightly. Fritz had been chasing his fifth Eastbourne title and entered the week as No. 1 seed. But after back-to-back ATP finals at Stuttgart and Halle in the previous fortnight, his body sent a different message.

In a statement that struck a genuinely apologetic tone, Fritz said he was "very sorry" and that the decision was never easy — especially for a tournament that means a great deal to him. But with Wimbledon beginning June 29, he said he needed to give himself the best possible chance to be fully fit for the Grand Slam.

The practical fallout was immediate. Fritz's withdrawal left Jack Draper, Toby Samuel, Ugo Humbert, and Jan Choinski as the remaining quarterfinalists. Draper — playing his first tournament back from injury this season — beat Canada's Gabriel Diallo 7-5, 6-4 to advance to the semifinals, a result that will do his Wimbledon preparations no harm. Toby Samuel, the young British wild card, also advanced.

There was also an unexpected ATP ranking consequence. With Fritz withdrawing from an event he was expected to win, Novak Djokovic — who has been barely active in 2026 — moved ahead of Fritz in the ATP live rankings.

The Eastbourne semifinal is Friday June 26. The final is Saturday June 27. Wimbledon begins four days later.

### STORY 2: Dan Evans — Last Singles Match

#### Long-Form Post — 10:30 AM ET

Dan Evans has played his last professional singles match.

It happened not on the grass of the All England Club, but on the courts of the Community Sports Centre in Roehampton — in the second round of Wimbledon qualifying, against Australian Tristan Schoolkate. Evans lost 7-5, 6-0, and with that defeat, a career that spanned more than 15 years on the professional circuit came to a quiet, honest close.

Evans, 36, announced earlier this season that Wimbledon 2026 would be the final event of his professional career. He had been one of the most respected members of the British tennis community for years — a player who reached a career-high of No. 21 in the world, known for his ability to upset higher-ranked opponents and his fierce competitive spirit on fast courts like grass.

The manner of the end is not what Evans would have scripted. But that is the reality of professional sport, and Evans has never sought theatre. He will still be at Wimbledon — competing in the doubles draw with Henry Searle, which gives him one final chance to perform at the tournament he loves most.

Meanwhile, four British players remained in the fight for main-draw qualifying spots on the final day: Heather Watson, Oliver Tarvet, Billy Harris, and Max Basing all reached the third and final qualifying round. Their results Thursday will determine how many British players join Evans — via wild card — in the Wimbledon main draw.

The Wimbledon singles draw ceremony takes place Friday June 26, 10:00 AM BST.

### STORY 3: Bad Homburg WTA — Navarro Upsets Swiatek

#### Long-Form Post — 12:30 PM ET

Iga Swiatek's 2026 season continues to deliver unwanted surprises.

The world No. 3 and Wimbledon defending champion entered the Bad Homburg Open as top seed this week — her first grass-court event of the season. She lost her opening match to Emma Navarro 7-5, 2-6, 6-3. It is the second consecutive time that Navarro has beaten Swiatek, levelling their head-to-head at 2-2. Swiatek, who had reached the quarterfinals at this tournament before, was unable to find her best level on the surface where she has historically been most comfortable — clay — rather than grass, where her game has always required an adjustment.

The results at Bad Homburg have been telling for Wimbledon watchers. Alexandrova had already eliminated Roland Garros 2026 champion Mirra Andreeva in the second round of the tournament, ending the run of the player who just a month ago was lifting the trophy in Paris. That means both Swiatek (No. 3 seed at Wimbledon) and Andreeva (No. 5 seed at Wimbledon) leave Bad Homburg without a match win to their name this grass swing.

There was more disruption: Elina Svitolina withdrew before her quarterfinal against Wang Xinyu with a right hip injury, handing the Chinese player a walkover into the semifinals — her second SF of the 2026 season.

Thursday's quarterfinals also featured Naomi Osaka vs Ekaterina Alexandrova, and Emma Navarro vs Elena-Gabriela Ruse. The Bad Homburg final is Saturday June 27, just two days before Wimbledon begins.

### STORY 4: Wimbledon Draw Preview

#### Long-Form Post — 3:00 PM ET

Tomorrow morning, at 10:00 AM BST, the Wimbledon 2026 draw will be made live on the tournament's official YouTube channel.

It is the moment the entire tennis world has been waiting for — the ceremony that decides who plays who, and which half of the draw the champions, the wildcards, and the injured players fall into. With four days until the Championships begin, the questions heading into the draw ceremony are more intriguing than usual.

On the men's side, the seedings are locked: Sinner No. 1, Zverev No. 2, FAA No. 3, Shelton No. 4, de Minaur No. 5, Fritz No. 6, Djokovic No. 7, Medvedev No. 8. The biggest unknown on the men's side is Djokovic: the No. 7 seed has not played on grass in 2026, skipped Hurlingham, missed a scheduled match due to a back issue, and is heading into the draw with significant fitness questions unresolved.

On the women's side: Sabalenka No. 1, Rybakina No. 2, Swiatek No. 3, Pegula No. 4, Andreeva No. 5. Note that both Swiatek and Andreeva leave their pre-Wimbledon warm-ups without a win on grass this year — Swiatek lost to Navarro today, and Andreeva was beaten by Alexandrova earlier in Bad Homburg. Serena Williams, 44, holds a women's singles wild card for what would be her first Grand Slam singles appearance since 2022.

The Championships run from June 29 through July 12, with prize money of £64.2M.

### STORY 5: Mallorca and Eastbourne QFs — Draper, Dimitrov Advance

#### Long-Form Post — 6:00 PM ET

With four days until the Wimbledon Championships, both the Mallorca Championships and the Rothesay International Eastbourne reached their quarterfinals on Thursday.

At Eastbourne — now without defending champion Fritz, who withdrew Thursday morning — the draw immediately opened up for the remaining players. Jack Draper, who had made a cautious return from injury this season, made the most of the situation by defeating Canada's Gabriel Diallo 7-5, 6-4 to advance to the semifinals. It is a confidence-boosting result ahead of Wimbledon for the young British player, who is one of the more exciting home hopes in the draw. Toby Samuel, the British wild card, also advanced; Ugo Humbert progressed in the French battle on the other side of the draw.

At Mallorca, Grigor Dimitrov — who was awarded a Wimbledon wild card and has been working his way back from the pectoral injury that forced him to retire against Sinner at last year's Wimbledon — faced second seed Alejandro Davidovich Fokina in the quarterfinals. The match, scheduled for Thursday at Santa Ponsa, is the kind of pre-Wimbledon form test that will give Dimitrov important match sharpness ahead of his first Grand Slam main draw appearance since recovering from that injury. Frances Tiafoe — who won Halle earlier this month — leads the Mallorca field.

Both tournaments reach their finals on Saturday June 27 — leaving players just one day before Wimbledon preparations hit their peak.
