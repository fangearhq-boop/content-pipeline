Fritz Withdraws from Eastbourne — Defending Champion's Last-Minute Call Puts Wimbledon in Focus

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 25, 2026

Taylor Fritz arrived at Devonshire Park on Thursday morning, warmed up, then made a decision that will define his Wimbledon preparation: he withdrew from the Rothesay International Eastbourne as defending champion and No. 1 seed. The American issued a statement expressing genuine regret — "I'm very sorry," he said — but made clear that entering Wimbledon at full fitness had to take priority over defending his title at an ATP 250 event.

The timing tells the full story. Fritz had played two ATP finals in the two weeks prior — at Stuttgart and Halle — after making his run deeper into the grass swing than most players. By Thursday, the accumulation caught up with him. He waited until after his morning warm-up to withdraw, suggesting this was not a comfortable call and that he genuinely tested his body before deciding he could not go.

A Draw That Opens Up

Fritz's withdrawal immediately reshaped the Eastbourne picture. With the No. 1 seed gone, Jack Draper moved with greater purpose through his quarterfinal, defeating Canada's Gabriel Diallo 7-5, 6-4 to advance to the semifinals. Draper, who had been cautiously building his return from an injury-interrupted 2026 season, will see the result as exactly the kind of momentum he needed heading into the All England Club.

British wild card Toby Samuel also advanced through his quarterfinal, with French players Ugo Humbert and Jan Choinski among the other semifinalists. The final is scheduled for Saturday June 27 — leaving the winner just one day before Wimbledon preparations intensify.

There was also an unexpected consequence in the ATP rankings. With Fritz withdrawing from a title defense, Novak Djokovic moved ahead of the American in the ATP live rankings — an ironic development given that Djokovic himself has been the player most scrutinised for his limited 2026 match schedule.

The Wimbledon Calculation

Fritz's reasoning reflects the reality that players now face heading into Grand Slams: the marginal benefit of one more ATP 250 event rarely outweighs the risk of aggravating a physical problem before two weeks of Grand Slam tennis. Fritz reached the Eastbourne final last year, won the event previously, and knows what playing deep into the draw on Devonshire Park's grass courts requires. He also knows what Wimbledon demands.

He enters the Championships as the No. 6 seed — a seeding that was confirmed on June 22 based on ATP rankings. The draw ceremony, which takes place Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, will determine his first-round opponent and his section of the bracket. Whether the withdrawal was the right call will depend on how his body responds over the next four days.

What's Next

The Eastbourne semifinals take place Friday June 26, with the final on Saturday June 27. The Wimbledon 2026 draw ceremony is also Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, live on Wimbledon's YouTube channel and wimbledon.com. The Championships begin Monday June 29. Fritz will be among 128 men entering the draw as the sixth seed — and the focus now shifts entirely to what he produces at the All England Club.
