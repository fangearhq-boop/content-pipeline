Draper Reaches Eastbourne Semifinal, Dimitrov Battles at Mallorca as Wimbledon Countdown Continues

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | June 25, 2026

Four days before Wimbledon begins, the ATP grass-court swing entered its final stages on Thursday at two simultaneous events: the Rothesay International Eastbourne and the Mallorca Championships. With defending Eastbourne champion Taylor Fritz having withdrawn earlier in the day, the remaining players competed for a title that was suddenly without its most prominent contender — and for form that would carry into the Championships.

At Devonshire Park, Jack Draper made the most of the opportunity. The young British player, who has been managing a difficult 2026 season with injury disruptions, defeated Canada's Gabriel Diallo 7-5, 6-4 in the quarterfinals to advance to the Eastbourne semifinals for the first time this grass season. It is the kind of win that consolidates confidence — Draper moving cleanly through sets against a solid opponent without requiring the kind of energy expenditure that would leave him short for Wimbledon.

British Success at Eastbourne

Toby Samuel also advanced through his quarterfinal, providing British tennis with two semifinalists at Eastbourne in the same week. Samuel, who was awarded a wild card into the event, has made the most of his opportunity in a week when the British tennis conversation has been dominated by Dan Evans' farewell in qualifying. His progression adds a more optimistic note to a complicated day for British tennis.

The other Eastbourne semifinalists include Ugo Humbert, who progressed through his match, and Jan Choinski. The final is scheduled for Saturday June 27 — two days before Wimbledon begins — giving whoever wins very little time to recover before the Grand Slam.

Dimitrov Targets Wimbledon Confidence at Mallorca

At the Mallorca Championships on the Spanish island, the quarterfinal between Grigor Dimitrov and second seed Alejandro Davidovich Fokina was one of the headline matchups on Thursday. Dimitrov was awarded a Wimbledon wild card for 2026 — a recognition of both his standing in the game and the unusual circumstances of his 2025 season, in which he led Jannik Sinner by two sets at Wimbledon before being forced to retire with a pectoral injury.

The Mallorca tournament, led by Frances Tiafoe — who won the Halle ATP 500 earlier this month — represented one of the final opportunities for players to build grass-court match sharpness before the Grand Slam. Luciano Darderi, who won his first career grass-court quarterfinal on Wednesday, was also among the contenders in the Mallorca draw.

What's Next

The Eastbourne semifinals take place Friday June 26, with the final Saturday June 27. The Mallorca Championships final is also scheduled for Saturday June 27. The Wimbledon 2026 draw ceremony is Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, live on Wimbledon's YouTube channel and wimbledon.com. The Championships begin Monday June 29 and run through Sunday July 12.
