Wimbledon 2026 Draw Preview — The Questions That Get Answered Friday Morning

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | June 25, 2026

The Wimbledon 2026 singles draw ceremony takes place Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, live on Wimbledon's official YouTube channel and wimbledon.com. By 10:30 AM, the bracket for the most prestigious tennis tournament in the world will be set, and the conversations that dominate the next fortnight — who faces whom, who might meet in which quarter, who has the tougher route to the final — will have their definitive starting point.

This year, the draw carries more tension than usual. The men's field is missing Carlos Alcaraz, who withdrew with a wrist injury and will not defend his title as two-time Wimbledon champion. And the questions hanging over Novak Djokovic — who has not played on grass in 2026, withdrew from his Hurlingham exhibition match with no explanation, and has had a stiff back in practice — add genuine uncertainty to what would otherwise be a clearer picture around No. 1 seed Jannik Sinner.

The Men's Seeds

The men's seedings for Wimbledon 2026 were locked on June 22: Sinner No. 1, Zverev No. 2, FAA No. 3, Shelton No. 4, de Minaur No. 5, Fritz No. 6, Djokovic No. 7, Medvedev No. 8. Of those eight, the one whose draw position carries the most intrigue is Djokovic. As No. 7 seed, he is placed in the opposite quarter of the draw from Sinner, meaning a potential Sinner-Djokovic semifinal is possible — but only if Djokovic can navigate his section with a body that has produced just 13 competitive matches in all of 2026.

Sinner, by contrast, arrives as the defending champion in as settled a state as he could reasonably hope. He played his grass-court warm-up at Hurlingham, received an all-clear following cardiac health tests in Milan, and has accumulated 13,500+ points on the tour this season. He is the clear favourite on paper and on recent form.

The Women's Seeds

The women's seedings: Sabalenka No. 1, Rybakina No. 2, Swiatek No. 3, Pegula No. 4, Andreeva No. 5, Anisimova No. 6, Gauff No. 7, Svitolina No. 8. Sabalenka has never won Wimbledon, despite being the world's dominant force for the past two years. Rybakina won it in 2022 and remains one of the most dangerous grass-court players in the world. Swiatek, the defending champion, lost her first grass-court match of 2026 on Thursday at Bad Homburg. Andreeva — Roland Garros 2026 champion and Wimbledon No. 5 seed — was beaten earlier this week at Bad Homburg by Alexandrova. Svitolina withdrew from her Bad Homburg quarterfinal with a hip injury.

Serena Williams, 44, enters as a women's singles wild card — her first Grand Slam singles appearance since the 2022 US Open. She will be in the draw alongside her sister Venus, who holds a doubles wild card with Serena.

What's Next

The Wimbledon 2026 singles draw ceremony is Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, live on YouTube and wimbledon.com. The Championships begin Monday June 29 and run through Sunday July 12. Prize money totals £64.2M — the largest in Wimbledon history. The first Grand Slam since the withdrawal of Alcaraz, the fitness concern over Djokovic, and the absence of grass-court form from Swiatek and Andreeva all point to a fortnight where the conventional wisdom about who wins could be challenged from the first week.
