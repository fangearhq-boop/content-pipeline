Dan Evans Plays His Last Singles Match — A Career Ends at Wimbledon Qualifying

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | June 25, 2026

Dan Evans played his last professional singles match on Thursday at the Community Sports Centre in Roehampton — not on the grass of the All England Club, but in the heat of Wimbledon qualifying. He lost to Australian Tristan Schoolkate 7-5, 6-0 in the second round of qualifying, and with that defeat, one of British tennis' most accomplished and complicated careers came to its close.

Evans, 36, had announced earlier in 2026 that Wimbledon would be the final chapter of his professional career. The qualifying competition — which ran June 22-25 — offered his last chance for a singles appearance in the main draw. It was not to be. But in the context of who Evans is and what he has always valued, there is something fitting about the fact that he competed right until the end rather than taking an offered exit.

The Career That Was

Evans reached a career-high of No. 21 in the world rankings and was British No. 1 for a significant stretch of his career. He was known for his ability to manufacture results against higher-ranked opponents on fast surfaces — grass most of all — and for a competitive edge that never softened regardless of the scoreboard. He served Great Britain in Davis Cup across multiple campaigns, contributed to Grand Slam runs, and earned a reputation as one of the most honest competitors on the tour.

He will still be at Wimbledon. Evans has entered the doubles draw alongside Henry Searle, giving him one more opportunity to perform at the tournament that has meant the most to him throughout his career. That doubles appearance will be his professional farewell at the All England Club.

British Players in the Final Round

June 25 was the final day of Wimbledon qualifying, with four British players still fighting for spots: Heather Watson, Oliver Tarvet, Billy Harris, and Max Basing all advanced to the third and final qualifying round. Their results on Thursday will determine how many British players secure direct passage to the Wimbledon main draw alongside the players who received wild cards.

The scale of the qualifying competition is significant: 256 players from around the world competed over four days (June 22-25) across three rounds, with 32 main-draw spots available (16 per gender). ESPN+ carried exclusive US broadcast rights throughout qualifying. The final qualifying round completes the field ahead of the draw ceremony Friday morning.

What's Next

The Wimbledon 2026 singles draw ceremony takes place Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, live on the official Wimbledon YouTube channel and wimbledon.com. The Championships begin Monday June 29 and run through Sunday July 12. For Dan Evans, the doubles draw with Searle at Wimbledon will be his final professional tennis appearance — the end of a career built on fight, grass, and a refusal to be written off.
