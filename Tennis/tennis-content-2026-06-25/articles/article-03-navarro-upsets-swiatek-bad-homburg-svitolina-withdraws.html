Navarro Upsets Swiatek at Bad Homburg as Svitolina Withdraws — Wimbledon Women's Draw Thrown Open

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | June 25, 2026

Emma Navarro defeated top seed Iga Swiatek 7-5, 2-6, 6-3 at the Bad Homburg Open on Thursday to advance to the quarterfinals — and in doing so, extended her winning run over the Wimbledon defending champion to two consecutive matches. Their head-to-head is now 2-2, with each of their past two meetings going in Navarro's favour. Swiatek's defeat was also her first grass-court match of the 2026 season.

It was not an isolated result in a vacuum. Ekaterina Alexandrova had already beaten Roland Garros 2026 champion Mirra Andreeva 6-1, 6(5)-7, 6-1 in the second round earlier this week — eliminating the player who was arguably the most in-form competitor on the women's tour entering the grass swing. Together, the exits of Swiatek and Andreeva from pre-Wimbledon warm-up play raise significant questions about who arrives at the All England Club with genuine grass-court confidence.

Svitolina Withdraws, Wang Xinyu to Semifinal

The disruption extended further. Elina Svitolina, the Wimbledon No. 8 seed, withdrew from her quarterfinal against Wang Xinyu with a right hip injury — handing the Chinese player a walkover into the semifinals. It is Wang's second semifinal of the 2026 season and arrives at an unfortunate moment: a hip concern for Svitolina, with Wimbledon four days away, is precisely the kind of injury that can compound quickly on grass courts.

That left Thursday's quarterfinals to be decided between Naomi Osaka and Alexandrova — the Russian who had already ended Andreeva's run — and between Navarro and Romanian Elena-Gabriela Ruse, who arrived at the quarterfinals as something of a surprise herself after eliminating Berlin champion Linda Noskova earlier in the tournament.

What It Means for Wimbledon

The women's Wimbledon picture heading into the draw ceremony Friday is more unpredictable than it has been in several years. Sabalenka (No. 1 seed) has never won Wimbledon. Swiatek (No. 3 seed, defending champion) has now lost her first grass-court match of 2026. Andreeva (No. 5 seed, Roland Garros champion) has been beaten before playing at the All England Club. Svitolina (No. 8 seed) has a hip injury.

The women's side of the Wimbledon draw, to be revealed Friday at 10:00 AM BST, may end up being as open as it has looked in years — with Rybakina (No. 2 seed, 2022 champion), Pegula (No. 4 seed), and a set of players in form at this week's warm-up events as realistic contenders.

What's Next

The Bad Homburg Open semifinals take place Friday June 26, with the final on Saturday June 27. The Wimbledon draw ceremony is also Friday June 26 at 10:00 AM BST, live on the official Wimbledon YouTube channel. The Championships begin Monday June 29 and run through Sunday July 12.
