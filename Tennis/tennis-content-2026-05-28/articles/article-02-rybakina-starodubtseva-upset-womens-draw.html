Starodubtseva Stuns No. 2 Seed Rybakina in Roland Garros Shock Exit

By Elena Voss | May 28, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

The biggest upset of Roland Garros 2026 arrived without fanfare on Wednesday afternoon. Yuliia Starodubtseva, a Ukrainian player who had never beaten a top-10 opponent in her career, walked onto Court Simonne-Mathieu and eliminated No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina 3-6, 6-1, 7-6(10-4) in two hours and 28 minutes.

The result reshapes the women's draw in ways that will play out over the coming days. Rybakina, the 2022 Wimbledon champion, had arrived in Paris as one of the genuine contenders for the title. She is now on the flight home.

A First Top-10 Victory at the Perfect Moment

Starodubtseva entered the match with a career record of 0-6 against top-10 players. Every previous encounter had ended in defeat. On Wednesday, in the second round of a Grand Slam tournament against one of the best players in the world, she finally broke through — and did so in dramatic fashion.

She won the second set 6-1 after surrendering the first 3-6, then broke early in the decider to lead 3-0. It looked like a rout was coming. Instead, Rybakina rediscovered her serve and groundstrokes, recovering both breaks to level at 4-4 and force a nerve-shredding finish. The match tiebreak, however, belonged entirely to Starodubtseva. She won it 10-4, converting her first match point with a clean winner.

Rybakina's Numbers Tell a Damning Story

Rybakina is among the cleaner ballstrikers on the WTA Tour in terms of her natural style. She generates pace and drives through the court with a flat, aggressive game that can be devastating when it clicks. On Wednesday, it did not click — not even close.

The Kazakhstani finished the match with 71 unforced errors against just 23 winners. That ratio — more than three errors for every winner — is the kind of stat that describes a player whose ball-tossing was off, whose timing never synchronized, and who was unable to find the solutions that a high-level match demands. Against a player of Starodubtseva's caliber, a top-10 seed should not produce numbers like that. Rybakina did not play anywhere near her level, and the scoreboard reflected it honestly.

What Rybakina's Exit Means for the Draw

The women's bottom half was projected to produce a Rybakina vs. Coco Gauff semifinal — a rematch of the 2025 Roland Garros final that Gauff won. With Rybakina gone, Gauff's projected path to the final loses its most formidable obstacle in that section. That is a significant development in the defending champion's campaign.

Starodubtseva advances to the third round where she will be the decided underdog in any matchup. But she has now beaten the No. 2 seed at a Grand Slam, and the confidence from that breakthrough win is not easily quantified. In tennis, momentum and belief matter. She earned both on Wednesday afternoon in Paris.

What's Next

Starodubtseva will face a third-round opponent on Friday or Saturday as Roland Garros enters the second week of action. The women's draw is now, unquestionably, more open than it was 24 hours ago. Aryna Sabalenka, the No. 1 seed, and Gauff, the defending champion, are the leading candidates to capitalize. Iga Swiatek remains in the lower half of the draw where the Ostapenko threat lingers. The tournament is alive with possibilities that did not exist before Wednesday afternoon on Court Simonne-Mathieu.
