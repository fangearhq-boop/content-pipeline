Roland Garros Day 4 Recap: Djokovic's Record, Fonseca's Comeback, Swiatek's History

By Marcus Cole | May 28, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Wednesday at Roland Garros was the kind of day the tournament was made for. Four compelling storylines played out across the courts: a record from a 39-year-old trying to extend his legacy, an epic comeback from a teenager setting up the match of the tournament, a clinical dismantling from the tournament's second seed, and a quiet piece of history from the four-time champion. Here is how Day 4 unfolded.

Djokovic Reaches R3 for the 21st Consecutive Time

Novak Djokovic needed four sets to dispatch French wildcard Valentin Royer 6-3, 6-2, 6-7(7), 6-3 in three hours and 28 minutes, but he advanced — and in doing so, reached the Roland Garros third round for the 21st consecutive year.

That statistic is worth sitting with. Every Roland Garros since 2006, Djokovic has made it to the second week. Players retire, form cycles pass, injuries intervene — and Djokovic has kept appearing in the third round at Roland Garros like a constant. Royer took the third set in a tiebreak and saved three match points in an emotional display in front of a home crowd, but Djokovic's quality was never seriously in question. He broke Royer in the fourth set and closed it out professionally.

Waiting for Djokovic in the third round: 19-year-old Joao Fonseca.

Fonseca's Five-Set Epic Sets Up a Dream Clash

If Wednesday had one moment that defined the day, it was Joao Fonseca refusing to lose. The 19-year-old Brazilian had fallen two sets behind Croatian Dino Prizmic — dropping the first set 3-6 and the second 4-6 while failing to earn a single break point across those first 10 games. It looked like a straightforward afternoon for Prizmic.

What followed was extraordinary. Fonseca found his forehand, began converting break points at will, and won the final three sets 6-3, 6-1, 6-2 for a 3-6, 4-6, 6-3, 6-1, 6-2 victory in three hours and 27 minutes. He became just the third teenager this century to overcome a two-set deficit in a Roland Garros main draw match — the company he joins is Roger Federer (2001) and Thanasi Kokkinakis (2015).

His reward is a third-round meeting with Novak Djokovic, the player Fonseca has described publicly as his idol and "the greatest of all time." The matchup is the most anticipated of the tournament so far: 39 years old against 19, the definition of tennis history meeting its own future.

Zverev Comfortable as Machac Struggles With Injury

Alexander Zverev, the No. 2 seed, advanced with a comfortable 6-4, 6-2, 6-2 win over Czech Tomas Machac. Machac received a medical timeout for a lower body injury midway through the second set, and Zverev did not let the disruption affect his rhythm. The German was efficient throughout, finishing in one hour and 48 minutes.

Zverev is building into this Roland Garros quietly. A year after falling short of his own high standards on clay, he enters the third round without dropping a set.

Swiatek Equals Evert's Open Era Record

On Court Philippe-Chatrier, Iga Swiatek defeated 20-year-old Czech Sara Bejlek 6-2, 6-3 in one hour and 33 minutes. The four-time Roland Garros champion was not at her absolute best — there were some loose moments in the early exchanges — but she found her level and won with authority.

The result brought Swiatek to 42 wins from 45 main draw matches at Roland Garros, equaling the Open Era record held by Chris Evert. It was her eighth consecutive third-round appearance at the tournament, and her 20th win of the 2026 season. Swiatek is quietly building a statistical legacy at this tournament that will be talked about for decades.

In the third round, Swiatek faces the player she has never beaten: Jelena Ostapenko holds a 6-0 record against the Pole, including on clay. It is the most awkward draw possible for a player otherwise expected to contend for the title.

What's Next

Friday brings Roland Garros into third-round territory. Djokovic vs. Fonseca is the headliner — a match that will generate more pre-match narrative than almost any other encounter at this tournament. Women's third-round matches begin with a draw that has been rearranged by Rybakina's second-round exit. The tournament is settling into its decisive middle phase, and the storylines are only getting richer.
