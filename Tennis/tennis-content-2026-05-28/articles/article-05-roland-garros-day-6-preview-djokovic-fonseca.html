Roland Garros Day 6 Preview: Djokovic vs Fonseca Headlines a Loaded Friday

By Elena Voss | May 28, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Roland Garros has been building toward something special all week. On Friday, it arrives. The third round opens, and the headliner is the most compelling matchup the tournament has produced so far: Novak Djokovic, 39 years old, the three-time Roland Garros champion chasing a record 25th Grand Slam title, against Joao Fonseca, 19 years old, the Brazilian teenager who this week became just the third teen this century to come back from two sets down at Roland Garros and who has publicly described Djokovic as his idol and the greatest of all time.

You cannot script this kind of matchup.

Djokovic vs. Fonseca: What to Watch

On paper, Djokovic should win. He has more experience, more big-match composure, and a clay record at Roland Garros that is unmatched — he has reached the third round here for 21 consecutive years. He survived Valentin Royer across four sets on Wednesday, showing that his serve and court coverage are still functioning at a high level even after his most clay-depleted season in years.

But Fonseca is not a paper opponent. The 19-year-old from Rio de Janeiro plays with pace and physicality, has a powerful forehand that he can direct with precision, and — crucially — he demonstrated on Wednesday that he does not break under pressure. He trailed Dino Prizmic by two sets, won the next three, and will arrive against Djokovic with the confidence of a player who just proved he can come back from anywhere.

Fonseca has spoken about this match before it happens. He has said Djokovic is the player who made him want to pursue tennis. Now he gets to play him in the third round of the French Open. The emotional stakes for the teenager are enormous — and whether that helps or hinders him on Friday will be one of the most interesting questions of the match.

Women's Draw: Wide Open at the Top

The women's third round begins Friday in a draw that has been reshaped by Wednesday's earthquake: No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina's defeat by Yuliia Starodubtseva. The Kazakh, projected by most analysts to reach the final from the bottom half of the draw, is gone. Defending champion Coco Gauff and top seed Aryna Sabalenka remain and are the favorites in their respective halves.

Gauff, who won the 2025 Roland Garros title over Sabalenka, has navigated her half comfortably so far. With Rybakina out, her projected path to the final is clearer than it looked last week. Sabalenka, who has never won Roland Garros in eight appearances despite reaching last year's final, is the player with the most to prove in Paris.

The Swiatek-Ostapenko Problem

On paper, the women's draw should have Iga Swiatek among the final contenders. She has won this tournament four times. She extended Chris Evert's Open Era record at Roland Garros on Wednesday. She is ranked No. 3 in the world and is on course for an eighth consecutive third-round appearance.

There is one problem. Her projected third-round opponent — depending on results — could be Jelena Ostapenko, the Latvian power hitter who won Roland Garros in 2017. Ostapenko leads her all-time head-to-head against Swiatek 6-0. The six wins are across all surfaces. On clay, Swiatek has never beaten her. Ostapenko's aggressive, flat, risk-everything game is the style most likely to disrupt the high-percentage baseline tennis that Swiatek typically employs to dominate opponents.

If that third-round match happens, it is the most dangerous fixture in the women's draw for any of the remaining favorites.

What's Next

Friday's order of play at Roland Garros will be confirmed Thursday evening Paris time. Djokovic vs. Fonseca is expected to be on Court Philippe-Chatrier in either the day or evening session. Third-round women's matches will also take center stage. The tournament is entering the phase where reputations are tested and paths to the final become defined. Friday should be worth every minute of attention.
