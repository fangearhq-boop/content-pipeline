Osaka Reaches Roland Garros Third Round for First Time Since 2019

By Ryan Calloway | May 28, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Seven years between Roland Garros third-round appearances is a long time in tennis — long enough for careers to be defined, reinvented, and defined again. On Thursday, Naomi Osaka ended that gap, defeating Croatia's Donna Vekic 7-6(1), 6-4 on Court Simonne-Mathieu to reach the last 32 in Paris for the first time since 2019.

The No. 16 seed was composed and controlled throughout, particularly in the tiebreak where she won 7-1, barely giving Vekic a point in the moments that mattered most. It was the kind of focused, professional performance that marks a player at ease with the pressure of a Grand Slam environment.

A Comeback That Continues to Exceed Expectations

The numbers behind Osaka's current standing in the game are extraordinary when placed in context. Sixteen months ago, she was ranked No. 135 in the world. She had returned to the tour after maternity leave carrying a ranking that needed to be rebuilt match by match, tournament by tournament.

Since that return, she has won titles, moved back into the top 20, and arrived at Roland Garros as a seeded player for the first time in years. The French Open has historically been Osaka's most challenging surface — she is primarily a hard-court player, and clay demands a different toolkit. Getting to the third round at Roland Garros in this phase of her career, against a quality opponent in Vekic, is evidence that the rebuild has been comprehensive rather than surface-specific.

Vekic Tested Her in the Opening Set

Donna Vekic is not a soft draw at any Grand Slam. The Croatian has reached a major semifinal and is capable of producing high-quality tennis that unsettles bigger-ranked opponents. She tested Osaka in the opening set, extending the match to a tiebreak before Osaka's clean execution on the biggest points closed it out 7-1.

The second set was more straightforward. Osaka's groundstrokes found better rhythm, her serve held firm, and Vekic was unable to create the kind of pressure that had caused Osaka difficulty earlier in the match. The final score of 6-4 reflected controlled closing from a player who knew how to win when the match was in her hands.

A Fascinating Third-Round Test Awaits

Osaka's reward for reaching the third round is a match against Iva Jovic, the 18-year-old American who delivered one of Thursday's more stunning performances, defeating former world No. 8 Emma Navarro 6-0, 6-3 in barely an hour on an outside court. Jovic is ranked No. 17 in the world and has the kind of aggressive, fearless game that does not slow down for occasion or reputation.

The generational contrast is striking. Osaka is 28, a four-time Grand Slam champion, a global sporting figure with a career that has encompassed everything from mental health advocacy to Olympic gold. Jovic is a teenager making her mark on the tour with direct, powerful tennis and no apparent concern for how the scoreboard reads in the early moments of a set.

On clay, the match is genuinely open. Osaka's defensive abilities have improved during her rebuild season, but Jovic's pace and shot selection will test those improvements in ways Vekic could not.

What's Next

Osaka vs. Jovic is scheduled for the third round on Saturday or Sunday. For Osaka, reaching the third round in Paris is not the ceiling of this campaign — it is the floor of what a genuine Roland Garros run would require. Whether she can extend beyond that milestone will depend on how her clay-court preparation translates against one of the most exciting young players on the tour. The comeback continues, and it is heading somewhere worth watching.
