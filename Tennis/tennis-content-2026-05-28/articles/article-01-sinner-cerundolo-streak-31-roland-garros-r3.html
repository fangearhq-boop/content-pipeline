Sinner Extends Win Streak to 31, Advances to Roland Garros Third Round

By Marcus Cole | May 28, 2026 | Tennis Fanrecap

Jannik Sinner made it look effortless again on Thursday. The World No. 1 defeated Argentina's Juan Manuel Cerundolo in straight sets on Court Philippe-Chatrier, advancing to the Roland Garros third round and extending his extraordinary winning streak to 31 consecutive matches.

Sinner controlled the match from the first game, dictating play from the baseline with the blend of pace, spin, and precision that has made him the most dominant player in men's tennis this year. Cerundolo, who had never progressed beyond the second round at a Grand Slam, was given little chance to settle into the match. The Italian ended the contest without allowing his opponent a foothold.

The Career Grand Slam Is Four Rounds Away

Every match Sinner wins in Paris pushes him closer to something that only six men have achieved in the Open Era: the career Grand Slam. Sinner has collected four of the five major titles — the Australian Open in 2024 and 2025, the US Open in 2024, and Wimbledon in 2025. Roland Garros is the last piece.

Should Sinner win this tournament, he would join Rod Laver, Andre Agassi, Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, Novak Djokovic, and Carlos Alcaraz as the only men in the Open Era to have claimed all four Grand Slam titles. Alcaraz withdrew from this year's Roland Garros with a wrist injury, removing the one rival most capable of stopping Sinner on clay.

Sinner's 31-match winning streak is one of the longest active runs on the ATP Tour and continues a period of dominance that has seen him win five consecutive Masters 1000 titles entering Roland Garros. He extended his streak to 30 by defeating French wildcard Clément Tabur in the first round on Monday, and now to 31 with Thursday's victory over Cerundolo.

Cerundolo Offered Limited Resistance

Juan Manuel Cerundolo is a solid clay-court competitor who ranked among the top 50 entering Paris. He had earned his second-round place with a first-round win in straight sets, and he came into the match knowing the court surface favored his game. But Sinner is operating at a level that neutralizes clay-court advantages that would trouble most opponents.

Cerundolo struggled to find consistent ball-striking against Sinner's heavy, deep returns. The Argentine's normally reliable groundstrokes were disrupted by the pace and depth Sinner generated from both wings. The straight-sets result, with Sinner winning the opening two sets 6-3 and 6-2, reflected the gap in class between the two players at this stage of the tournament.

The Path Ahead

Sinner will face a third-round opponent to be confirmed from the ongoing second-round action. The bottom half of the men's draw — where Novak Djokovic is on a collision course with Brazilian teenager Joao Fonseca — means Sinner would not meet the Serbian until a potential final. That structure favors the Italian, who can accumulate match wins and momentum without a generational confrontation until the final weekend.

What's more striking about Sinner's Roland Garros run is the absence of drama. There are no close sets, no wobbles, no signs of the kind of vulnerability that tends to emerge for players over a fortnight of best-of-five tennis. Thirty-one wins into a streak, and the machine is still running cleanly.

What's Next

Sinner faces his third-round opponent on Saturday or Sunday. Should he continue his current form, the fourth round and beyond will represent his most demanding tests — potential meetings with players capable of extending him over five sets. The career Grand Slam bid moves forward. The streak continues. For now, Roland Garros appears to be playing out exactly as advertised when the draw was made: Sinner's tournament to lose.
