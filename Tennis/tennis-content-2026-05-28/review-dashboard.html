### Long-Form Post **Window:** 8:00 AM ET The biggest upset of Roland Garros 2026 landed on Wednesday — and it wasn't even the headline match. Ukrainian Yuliia Starodubtseva eliminated No. 2 seed Elena Rybakina 3-6, 6-1, 7-6(10-4) in two hours and 28 minutes on Court Simonne-Mathieu. For Starodubtseva, it was the biggest win of her career — her first victory over a top-10 opponent after six previous defeats. She won the second set 6-1 after surrendering the first 3-6, then broke early in the decider to lead 3-0. It looked like a rout was coming. Instead, Rybakina recovered to 4-4 and forced a nerve-shredding finish. The match tiebreak, however, belonged entirely to Starodubtseva. She won it 10-4. The Kazakhstani finished with 71 unforced errors against just 23 winners. That ratio — more than three errors for every winner — is simply not survivable tennis at this level. Day 4 Recap — Djokovic Survives, Fonseca Sets Dream Clash, Swiatek's Evert Record
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post **Window:** 9:00 PM ET Wednesday at Roland Garros delivered four compelling storylines — a record, a comeback for the ages, an injured exit, and a piece of history matched. Novak Djokovic defeated French wildcard Valentin Royer 6-3, 6-2, 6-7(7), 6-3 to reach the third round for the 21st consecutive year. Royer took the third set in a tiebreak and saved three match points before Djokovic closed it out. Twenty-one consecutive Roland Garros third rounds. That's a number that puts the longevity of Djokovic's clay career into clear perspective. Meanwhile, 19-year-old Joao Fonseca staged one of the great Roland Garros comebacks. He trailed Croatian Dino Prizmic by two sets before winning the last three 6-3, 6-1, 6-2 to prevail 3-6, 4-6, 6-3, 6-1, 6-2. He became just the third teenager this century to overcome a two-set deficit at Roland Garros — after Roger Federer in 2001 and Thanasi Kokkinakis in 2015. His reward: a third-round clash with Novak Djokovic, the player Fonseca has called his idol and the greatest of all time. Alexander Zverev dispatched Czech Tomas Machac 6-4, 6-2, 6-2 in one hour and 48 minutes after Machac suffered a lower body injury. And Iga Swiatek defeated Sara Bejlek 6-2, 6-3, equaling Chris Evert's Open Era record of 42 wins from 45 main draw matches at Roland Garros.
