NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Marta Kostyuk is a WTA 1000 champion. The 23-year-old Ukrainian defeated Mirra Andreeva 6-3, 7-5 in 1 hour and 21 minutes on Saturday at the Mutua Madrid Open to claim the biggest title of her career. When the final point landed, she ran to the corner of the court, took three steps, and launched into a perfect backflip that immediately set the tennis world talking. This was Kostyuk's first title above the WTA 250 level. She finished the Madrid week 12-0 on clay — 11 wins in the main draw and one more in the Billie Jean King Cup — and is now 17-4 on the season. Every one of her four 2026 losses came against Aryna Sabalenka or Elena Rybakina, the two players trading the world No. 1 ranking. Against everyone else on clay this year, Kostyuk has been flawless. The milestone matters beyond the scorecard. Kostyuk became the first Ukrainian player to win the Madrid Open since the tournament was founded in 2009. She is just the seventh player born in the 2000s to lift a WTA 1000 trophy. In defeat, Mirra Andreeva was no afterthought. The 18-year-old reached her third WTA 1000 final — a feat no teenager had managed since the format began in 2009. The generational matchup was historic regardless of outcome. Kostyuk now heads to Rome and Roland Garros carrying a clay record of 12-0 that demands to be taken seriously. ### Image Post Caption First Ukrainian. First WTA 1000 title. First backflip. ### Long-Form Post It is happening today. Jannik Sinner and Alexander Zverev will meet at 5 PM local time (11 AM ET) in the ATP Madrid Open final, and the stakes have never been higher in their storied rivalry. Sinner is chasing something that has never been done in the 36-year history of the Masters 1000 series: five consecutive titles. He won in Paris last autumn and has since claimed Indian Wells, Miami, and Monte-Carlo in 2026, running his Masters 1000 match-winning streak to 27 consecutive matches. Every single time he and Zverev have met at Masters level this season — Indian Wells, Miami, Monte-Carlo, and now Madrid — Sinner has won the semifinal and gone on to take the title. The overall head-to-head is 9-4 in Sinner's favour. He has won the last eight straight. Zverev arrives as a two-time Madrid champion. He won here in 2018 and 2021 and has 30 career wins at the Caja Mágica. A win today would make him just the fourth man in history to win Madrid three or more times, joining Rafael Nadal, Roger Federer, and Novak Djokovic. He defeated Belgian wildcard Alexander Blockx 6-2, 7-5 in the semifinal, hitting 35 winners and not facing a single break point. He is playing close to his best. This is Sinner's first-ever appearance in the Madrid final. He has conquered every other court he has touched for the past eight months. The question today is whether Zverev has found the answer. ### Image Post Caption The biggest match in tennis today. Sinner vs. Zverev — Madrid ATP Final, 11 AM ET.
