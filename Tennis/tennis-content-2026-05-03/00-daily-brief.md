### STORY 1: Kostyuk Wins Madrid WTA Title 6-3, 7-5 — First WTA 1000 Crown, 12-0 on Clay
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Match Result / Historic Milestone
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Marta Kostyuk defeated Mirra Andreeva 6-3, 7-5 in 1 hour 21 minutes on Saturday at the Mutua Madrid Open WTA final to claim the biggest title of her career. The 23-year-old Ukrainian won her first WTA 1000 title (her third overall) and finished the tournament 12-0 on clay in 2026 — 11-0 in WTA main draw matches and one Billie Jean King Cup win. She is now 17-4 on the year, with all four losses to Aryna Sabalenka or Elena Rybakina. Kostyuk became the first Ukrainian to win the Madrid Open since the event began in 2009 and the seventh player born in the 2000s to win a WTA 1000 title. Her celebration — a perfect backflip at courtside — immediately went viral.

### STORY 2: Sinner vs. Zverev — ATP Madrid Final (Today, 11 AM ET)
**Tier:** 1
**Type:** Match Preview / Historic Stakes
**Platforms:** X, FB, Article

Jannik Sinner faces Alexander Zverev in today's ATP Madrid Open final at 5 PM CEST (11 AM ET) on the Manolo Santana court. Sinner is chasing an unprecedented fifth consecutive Masters 1000 title — no player has won five in a row in the 36-year history of the series. He holds a 9-4 H2H advantage over Zverev overall and has won the last eight meetings, including the semifinal at every Masters 1000 event this season (Indian Wells, Miami, Monte-Carlo, and now Madrid). Zverev is a two-time Madrid champion (2018, 2021) bidding to become the fourth man with three or more titles in the Spanish capital, joining Rafael Nadal (5), Roger Federer (3), and Novak Djokovic (3).
