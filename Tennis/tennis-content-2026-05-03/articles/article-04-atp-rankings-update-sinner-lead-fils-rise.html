ATP Rankings Update: Sinner Extends Lead to 1,040 Points, Fils Climbs to No. 17

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

May 3, 2026 — Madrid, Spain

The rankings picture after Madrid is telling a simple story: Jannik Sinner is pulling away, Carlos Alcaraz is standing still, and the players beneath them are scrambling to close gaps that keep widening.

Following the semifinal results at the Mutua Madrid Open, Sinner has opened a lead of more than 1,040 points over Alcaraz in the PIF ATP Live Rankings. That margin is growing. Alcaraz, ranked No. 2, has withdrawn from Madrid and Rome with a wrist injury and will not be able to earn ranking points again until Roland Garros at the earliest. Every week that passes, Sinner can extend the gap; Alcaraz has no mechanism to close it until he steps back on a Grand Slam court in Paris.

The Fils Leap

Arthur Fils was one of the week's clear winners from a rankings perspective, even in defeat. The 21-year-old Frenchman had arrived in Madrid with fresh Barcelona clay-title form and had not dropped a set on clay in 2026 before facing Sinner in the semifinal. His run to the last four at a Masters 1000 event — his second such result — moved him eight spots to No. 17 in the PIF ATP Live Rankings.

Fils is a player whose ranking is beginning to reflect what his game has suggested for some time: he belongs among the elite. A second Masters 1000 semifinal before the age of 22 is not a fluke. It is evidence of a trajectory.

Zverev and the Final Picture

Alexander Zverev was already firmly established in the top five before Madrid. His run to the final — defeating Cobolli, Ruud, and Blockx to get there — ensures that he will remain there and may improve his position depending on today's result against Sinner.

The rest of the top ten has been relatively stable through the Madrid week. Without multiple major players in the draw — Alcaraz, Djokovic, Draper, and Fritz all absent — the points haul was concentrated among those who did compete. Sinner's quarterfinal, semifinal, and final wins stack points at the top, while the broader midfield has seen movement at the margins.

What the Clay Swing Means for Rankings

Between now and Roland Garros, the Italian Open in Rome is the only remaining Masters 1000 event. It is worth 1,000 ranking points to the winner. For Alcaraz, who cannot play, that is another potential 1,000-point addition to Sinner's advantage — assuming Sinner competes and advances. The gap heading into Paris could realistically exceed 2,000 points by the time the Roland Garros draw is released.

For the players ranked between No. 5 and No. 20, the clay swing represents a genuine opportunity to leap. Fils has already taken it. Others — Ruud, Tsitsipas, Auger-Aliassime — are still competing for ground.

What's Next

The Italian Open in Rome is next on the calendar, followed immediately by Roland Garros. Sinner is guaranteed to remain world No. 1 into the Paris Slam regardless of results between now and then. The question is how large the gap becomes and whether any player — in Rome, in Paris, or both — can begin to trim it. The next ATP rankings update will come after the Italian Open. By then, the Roland Garros seedings will already be taking shape.
