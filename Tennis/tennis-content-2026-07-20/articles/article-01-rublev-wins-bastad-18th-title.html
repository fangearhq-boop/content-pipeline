Rublev Dominates Darderi to Win Bastad — 18th Career Title, 17-Month Drought Over

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap

July 20, 2026 — Bastad, Sweden

Andrey Rublev is a Bastad champion again. The Russian defeated defending champion Luciano Darderi 6-4, 6-3 in 77 minutes on Sunday at the Nordea Open, claiming his 18th career ATP Tour title and ending a 17-month wait since his last trophy in Doha, back in February 2025.

It was not a close match. Rublev won 87% of his first-serve points and converted two of four break opportunities. Darderi — who had beaten Rublev at this same event to win the 2025 title — was given no room on Sunday. The scoreline says one thing. The statistics make it clearer: this was Rublev on a day when he simply played better than the defending champion in every metric that matters on clay.

What the Match Looked Like

Rublev's clay game is built on depth, consistency, and an ability to redirect pace without losing his own. On Sunday in Bastad, those qualities were on full display from the first game. He broke in the first set and held comfortably, closing it 6-4 before carrying the same energy into a second that went even smoother.

Darderi had made the most of his week to reach the final — the Italian is a legitimate clay specialist with the 2025 title already on his resume here. But Rublev's execution in the key moments was clean. When Darderi pushed forward, Rublev absorbed and redirected. When Darderi went for more, Rublev forced the errors.

By the time the match reached 5-3 in the second, it had the feel of a result already decided. Rublev served it out to claim his second Bastad title — his first here was in 2023 — in just over an hour.

The Drought That Ended

Seventeen months is a long time for a player of Rublev's caliber to go without a title. He had reached finals in the interim, made deep runs at bigger events, and remained inside the top 20 throughout the gap. But titles are different from runs. They require a specific kind of performance for a full week.

Bastad gave him that. He moved through the draw without a match where the outcome felt genuinely uncertain, and in the final against the defending champion, he produced his cleanest tennis of the week. The drought is over.

With his victory, Rublev moves up two spots in the PIF ATP Rankings. He has spoken in recent months about wanting to return to the top 10, and this week in Sweden puts him in a better position to chase that target heading into the North American summer.

Darderi's Bid to Repeat Falls Short

Luciano Darderi had every reason to believe he could defend his Bastad title. He won it convincingly in 2025, he is a clay-court player by instinct and ranking, and he had come through a competitive week to reach Sunday's final. None of that was enough against the version of Rublev that showed up.

For Darderi, the loss is a reminder of how difficult title defense is in any sport — and particularly in tennis, where conditions, opponents, and form can shift dramatically from one year to the next. He will take his ranking points from a finalist week and move forward. The question is whether Bastad 2025 was a peak to build on, or a standard to be chased.

What's Next

With the Bastad title secured, Rublev turns his attention to the North American hardcourt summer. The Canadian Open in Montreal begins August 1, followed by Cincinnati and then the US Open in late August. Rublev's second-serve numbers and clay efficiency from this week will be tested on hard courts — a surface where his aggressive style can also thrive.

Darderi, meanwhile, heads into the second week of the European clay swing, with Kitzbuhel and Estoril still available before the tour shifts north. The Italian has the game for a deep run wherever he enters this week.

Bastad belongs to Rublev again. The 2023 and 2026 champion leaves Sweden with his 18th title and a renewed sense of what this surface brings out in his game.
