Alcaraz Confirms Cincinnati Return — Wrist Healed, US Open Defense Is On

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap

July 20, 2026

Carlos Alcaraz is returning to the ATP Tour. His medical team has confirmed that the right wrist injury that has kept him sidelined since April 14 is "completely healed," and Alcaraz has confirmed his target event: the Western and Southern Open in Cincinnati, running August 13-23. He will skip the Canadian Open in Montreal, which begins August 1, and use Cincinnati as his sole warm-up before defending his US Open title starting August 24.

That is the plan. One tournament, then the biggest event of his season.

Four Months Away

Alcaraz suffered tenosynovitis of the right wrist during his match in Barcelona on April 14. The injury, initially described as less severe than it turned out to be, kept him out of Roland Garros — which he had won twice — as well as Wimbledon and, now, the Canadian Open. His last competitive match was in Barcelona, more than four months ago.

In that time, he watched Jannik Sinner win Wimbledon back-to-back. He watched Alexander Zverev win Roland Garros and move past him to No. 2 in the ATP rankings. He has been at roughly No. 3 in the world with no matches to defend or build on since April.

His doctor, Angel Ruiz-Cotorro, has now confirmed the wrist is "completely healed." That is medical clearance, which is necessary — but the four months without competitive match play create a different kind of challenge when he returns to Cincinnati in three weeks.

The Cincinnati-to-US-Open Window

The math of Alcaraz's return is straightforward and tight. Cincinnati begins August 13. The US Open begins August 24. That gives him a maximum of five match days in Cincinnati to find his competitive timing — or fewer, if he loses early — before he walks into one of the biggest events of his career as defending champion.

Alcaraz has shown in the past that he can find his level quickly after time away. His ability to produce high-quality tennis in compressed timelines is one of his defining traits. But a four-month absence is not a short injury break — it is a full reset, and the question of how quickly he can rediscover the rhythm of match play against top-20 opponents is not one anyone can answer before he plays.

Cincinnati, where he is also the defending champion, offers a competitive environment that could either accelerate his return to form or expose the gap before the US Open. Either way, that is all the preparation he has chosen to give himself.

The Canadian Open and What He Skips

Alcaraz choosing to skip the Canadian Open in Montreal is a straightforward risk calculation. Taking an extra two weeks of training before Cincinnati — rather than jumping straight into a Masters 1000 field — reduces the risk of re-aggravating the wrist under match conditions before he has fully assessed how it responds to competitive stress.

The downside is that he enters Cincinnati as cold as he can be: no match practice, no competitive rhythm, up against players who have been playing through the clay swing and may carry form into the hard-court season. The Canadian Open field includes Sinner, Djokovic, and Zverev — arguably the deepest field the tournament has ever assembled. Alcaraz chose to let that pass and focus on arriving in Cincinnati ready rather than rushed.

What's Next

Alcaraz's return to the ATP Tour is now confirmed with a date and a venue. The next question is how he plays when he gets there. Cincinnati begins August 13 and runs through August 23. The US Open field begins arriving in New York on August 24.

He has been the best player in the world when healthy. He is currently No. 3. The path to the top of the rankings runs through Cincinnati and New York, and his team has decided the wrist is ready. The rest is tennis.
