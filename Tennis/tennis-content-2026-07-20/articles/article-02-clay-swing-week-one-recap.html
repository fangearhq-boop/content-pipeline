Four Finals, Four Champions: The Post-Wimbledon Clay Swing Delivered a Historic Sunday

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 20, 2026

Sunday's finals across the post-Wimbledon clay swing produced four champions, four separate stories, and one of the more memorable single-day stretches of the summer. Andrey Rublev won Bastad for the second time. Raphael Collignon won Gstaad for the first time — in his career. Paula Badosa won Iasi for the fifth time. Maria Sakkari won in Athens for the first time, in a city that had waited 35 years to hold a WTA final.

One day, four events, nothing predictable about any of them.

Rublev: The Drought Ends in Bastad

Andrey Rublev's 6-4, 6-3 victory over Luciano Darderi in the Nordea Open final was the cleanest result of the four. He won 87% of first-serve points in 77 minutes against the defending champion. After 17 months without a title — his last was in Doha in February 2025 — Rublev finally closed the gap. He also rises to No. 14 in the PIF ATP Rankings, moving closer to a return to the top 10.

Darderi, the 2025 defending champion, had no answers on the day. Rublev was the better player by enough of a margin that the result never felt in doubt.

Collignon: Belgium's First ATP Title Moment in Gstaad

If Rublev's title was the one the rankings expected, Raphael Collignon's was the one the week will remember longest. The Belgian defeated No. 3 seed Stefanos Tsitsipas 7-6(2), 7-6(5) in the EFG Swiss Open final at Gstaad to win the first ATP Tour title of his career — and only the second title ever won by a Belgian player at this clay event, following David Goffin in 2015.

Collignon had to survive a match point against Juan Manuel Cerundolo in the semifinals before even reaching Sunday's final. He responded by beating a player of Tsitsipas's experience in back-to-back tiebreaks without letting the moment change him. Belgium did not win it in 2015. It won it in 2026.

Badosa: Back in the Top 100 on Her Own Terms

Paula Badosa's fifth career WTA title at Iasi had everything. She came from a set and a break down against Tamara Zidansek in the semifinals, recovered to win 3-6, 7-5, 7-6, then entered the final on a six-match winning streak and defeated Mayar Sherif to claim the trophy. She is now 5-0 in WTA title matches — she has never lost a final.

The win pushed her back into the top 100, as high as No. 75. She also has a personal connection to Iasi — "I grew up following Simona," she said after the match, a reference to Simona Halep, Romania's greatest player — that made the win mean more than the ranking points alone.

Sakkari: Athens Finally Gets Its WTA Champion

Maria Sakkari winning the Vanda Pharmaceuticals Athens Open was the most emotional result of the weekend. The first WTA event in Athens in 35 years, on home soil, in front of her home crowd — Sakkari defeated No. 1 seed Barbora Krejcikova to claim her first WTA title since her last final appearance in Indian Wells in March 2024.

On the same day, Stefanos Tsitsipas was in the Gstaad final. Two Greek players in ATP and WTA finals simultaneously — it did not happen often, and it happened in Athens, the city where Greek tennis has its deepest roots.

What This Week Means for the Rankings

Every champion this week moves. Rublev reaches No. 14. Collignon's career high before the week was No. 42; his first title will push that further. Badosa returns to the top 100 at No. 75. Sakkari climbs from whatever she was ranked before Athens.

The North American hardcourt summer begins next week. For each of these players, this clay week was either a confirmation of existing trajectory or the start of a new one. Week two of the European clay swing — Kitzbuhel, Estoril, Hamburg, Prague — is underway now. Week one left a mark.

What's Next

The tour does not pause to reflect. Week two of clay is already running: Kitzbuhel, Estoril, WTA Hamburg, and WTA Prague all started Monday. After that, the Canadian Open (ATP Montreal, WTA Toronto) begins August 1, and the Cincinnati events follow in mid-August before the US Open. The results from this weekend will shape who enters the hard-court swing with momentum and who arrives still chasing it.
