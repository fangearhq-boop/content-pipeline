WTA Hamburg and Prague Open Clay Week Two — Last European Stop Before North American Swing

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 20, 2026

Two WTA clay events opened Monday, launching the second and final week of the WTA's European clay season. The MSC Hamburg Ladies Open — a WTA 500 event — and the Livesport Prague Open — a WTA 250 — run simultaneously through July 26 in Germany and the Czech Republic respectively. When both events conclude on Saturday, the WTA Tour pivots to North American hard courts, beginning with the Canadian Open in Toronto the following week.

Hamburg: The 500-Level Week

Hamburg carries more ranking points than Prague, which typically means more of the tour's established names enter there rather than splitting between both events. A WTA 500 draws players who need meaningful points on clay — either building toward a hard-court season that follows immediately or protecting their current ranking before the points reset that comes with a new surface.

The Hamburg event is set at the Rothenbaum tennis complex, one of the longer-standing venues on the women's calendar. Clay at Hamburg plays differently than clay in the South of France or southern Spain — conditions are heavier, slower, and tend to reward baseliners who can move well laterally and absorb pace over an extended rally.

Prague: The 250-Level Week

The Livesport Prague Open, at the 250 level, draws a different player profile. This is where players who narrowly missed the Hamburg entry list find their matches, where players returning from injury or rebuilding their ranking are entered, and where upsets are more frequent because the field disparity between seeds and qualifiers is smaller than at higher levels.

Prague on clay has a tradition of producing first-time WTA title winners. The Czech Republic's long history with professional tennis on clay — combined with the competitive atmosphere of a packed ATP schedule running at the same time — gives Prague its own distinct identity on the summer calendar.

The Context: What Last Week Set Up

Last week's Athens and Iasi finals both produced notable results. Maria Sakkari won the first WTA title in Athens in 35 years, defending her home crowd's investment in an event that was new to the WTA schedule. Paula Badosa won her fifth career title at Iasi and returned to the top 100 at No. 75, securing direct US Open entry.

Those results shift the clay context heading into Hamburg and Prague. Sakkari enters the week with momentum from Athens. Badosa — if she plays in Hamburg or Prague — does so as a top-100 player again. Other players who had disappointing results last week — Krejcikova as runner-up in Athens, Sherif as runner-up in Iasi — will be looking for a bounce-back week on the same surface.

What's Next

Both events conclude Saturday July 26. The following week, the WTA heads to Toronto for the Canadian Open alongside the ATP's Montreal event (August 1-13). Toronto is a hard-court Masters event — a full change of surface — and the players who finish Hamburg and Prague strongest enter that week with momentum from clay that may or may not transfer.

This week is the last chance for European clay specialists to earn points on their preferred surface before the summer hard-court season determines a significant portion of each player's ranking heading into the US Open. Hamburg and Prague, taken together, give the WTA field two final entries before that shift.
