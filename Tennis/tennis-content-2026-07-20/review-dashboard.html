NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Andrey Rublev is a Bastad champion again. He defeated defending champion Luciano Darderi 6-4, 6-3 in 77 minutes to win the Nordea Open final, claiming his 18th career ATP Tour title and his second at this Swedish clay event. It wasn't a contest. Rublev won 87% of his first-serve points and converted two of four break opportunities. Darderi — who beat Rublev in Bastad to win the 2025 title — had no answer this time for the Russian's driving groundstrokes and controlled aggression on clay. The bigger story is the drought that ended. Rublev's last title was in Doha, back in February 2025. Seventeen months between trophies is a long gap for a player of his quality. On Sunday in Sweden, that wait ended convincingly. He moves up two spots to No. 14 in the ATP rankings — and if he continues in this form through the North American swing, a return to the top 10 is a realistic conversation. NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Sunday was one of the better days of the tennis season. Four clay events each had a final, and each final told a completely different story. In Bastad, Andrey Rublev beat defending champion Luciano Darderi 6-4, 6-3 to win his 18th career title — ending a 17-month drought since Doha in February 2025. In Gstaad, Raphael Collignon beat Stefanos Tsitsipas in two tiebreaks for the first ATP title of his career — only the second Belgian to win there since Goffin in 2015. In Iasi, Paula Badosa beat Mayar Sherif for her fifth career WTA title — 5-0 in finals all-time, now back in the top 100 at No. 75. In Athens, Maria Sakkari won the first WTA title in that city in 35 years, in front of her home crowd. Four results. Rublev's drought ends. Collignon announces himself. Badosa's ranking climb continues. Sakkari makes history in front of her country. It was a good Sunday for tennis. ### Long-Form Post Carlos Alcaraz is coming back. His doctor, Angel Ruiz-Cotorro, has confirmed that Alcaraz's right wrist is "completely healed" — and the Spaniard is targeting the Western & Southern Open in Cincinnati (August 13-23) as his return to competitive tennis. He will skip the Canadian Open in Montreal (August 1-13) entirely. That leaves him one tournament to shake off more than four months away before he walks into the US Open on August 24 to defend his title. The math is tight. Alcaraz has missed Roland Garros, Wimbledon, and the Canadian Open — three events where he was either the defending champion or a projected serious contender. He arrives in Cincinnati with no match play since April 14, no title points from the events he missed, and the expectation of a defending champion on his back at the US Open. "Completely healed" is medical clearance, not a guarantee of match form. But Alcaraz has shown repeatedly that he finds his level faster than most players — the question is whether one week in Cincinnati is enough. Defending champion Alexander Bublik is back as the No. 1 seed, and the field around him is competitive: Valentin Vacherot, Arthur Rinderknech, Tomas Martin Etcheverry, Sebastian Baez, and Matteo Berrettini are among the entrants.
