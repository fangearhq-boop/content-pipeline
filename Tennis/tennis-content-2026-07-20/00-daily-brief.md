### STORY 1: Rublev Wins Bastad — 18th Career Title
- **Tier:** 1
- **Angle:** RESULT — Andrey Rublev (RUS, No. 1 seed) defeated defending champion Luciano Darderi (ITA, No. 2 seed) 6-4, 6-3 in 77 minutes to win the Nordea Open Bastad final. Rublev's 18th career ATP Tour title and his second at Bastad (also won in 2023). Darderi was attempting to defend the 2025 title. Rublev won 87% of first-serve points, converting 2 of 4 break points. He rises two spots to No. 14 in the PIF ATP Rankings.

### STORY 2: Clay Swing Week One Wrap — Four Finals, Four Champions
- **Tier:** 1
- **Angle:** NEW STORY — The post-Wimbledon clay swing concluded Sunday with four title matches across Bastad, Gstaad, Iasi, and Athens. Rublev (Bastad), Collignon (Gstaad), Badosa (Iasi), and Sakkari (Athens) all claimed titles. Belgium's Collignon won his first ATP title; Sakkari gave Greece its first WTA Athens champion in 35 years; Badosa returned to the top 100 (No. 75); Rublev ended a 17-month title drought. Four distinct stories, one memorable weekend.

### STORY 3: Alcaraz Confirms Cincinnati Comeback — US Open Countdown Begins
- **Tier:** 1
- **Angle:** NEW UPDATE — Carlos Alcaraz's team has confirmed his return to the ATP Tour at the Western & Southern Open in Cincinnati (August 13-23). His doctor, Angel Ruiz-Cotorro, says the right wrist is "completely healed." Alcaraz will skip the Canadian Open (Montreal, Aug. 1-13) and use Cincinnati as his only warm-up for the US Open (defense, Aug. 24). He has been sidelined since April 14 in Barcelona — four months off the tour.
- **Posting Window:** 12:30 PM ET
