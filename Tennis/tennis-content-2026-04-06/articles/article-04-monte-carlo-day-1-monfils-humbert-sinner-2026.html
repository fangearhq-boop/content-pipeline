Monte Carlo Day 1: Monfils Delivers Record-Touching Win, Humbert Sets Up Sinner Clash

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | April 6, 2026

The Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters opened its first-round play over the weekend, and by the time Day 1 wrapped up, it had established its first talking points ahead of the tournament's marquee matchups.

Gaël Monfils provided the headline. Ugo Humbert set up the second-round test everyone will want to watch. Andrey Rublev grinded through. And Jannik Sinner demonstrated he is in full competitive mode — even before his singles draw begins.

Monfils Does It Again

Gaël Monfils defeated Tallon Griekspoor 6-7(7), 6-1, 6-4 in a performance that carried real longevity significance. The Frenchman — beloved by Monaco crowds in what amounts to a near-home tournament — dropped the first set in a tiebreak before taking control of the match completely. He won 12 of the final 13 games.

Monfils has been playing professional tennis for more than two decades. Winning tight three-set first-round matches at Masters 1000 events into his late career is not something every player can still do. Sunday's performance added to a career body of work at a tournament where he has always enjoyed significant crowd support.

Humbert and the Sinner Test

Ugo Humbert defeated wild card Moise Kouame 6-3, 7-5 in straight sets to advance to the second round, where he will face No. 2 seed Jannik Sinner.

Kouame is no ordinary wild card. The 17-year-old made history at Miami Open by becoming the youngest Masters 1000 winner since Rafael Nadal in 2003. His inclusion in the Monte Carlo draw as a wild card came directly from that result. Humbert handled him efficiently.

The Sinner match is the most anticipated second-round fixture in the draw. Sinner won Indian Wells and Miami without dropping a set — the first man in history to complete the Sunshine Double without losing a set in either tournament. He enters Monte Carlo as the No. 2 seed defending zero points, with the No. 1 ranking within striking distance if defending champion Carlos Alcaraz exits before the later rounds.

Humbert is a capable opponent. He is a left-handed player with a big serve and an aggressive game plan. Whether he can match Sinner's level at this stage of Sinner's form is a different question, but the second-round match is a genuine test rather than an expected result.

Other Results

Andrey Rublev defeated Nuno Borges in what sources described as a see-saw battle — a match that required Rublev to grind through rather than dominate. Rublev has been a consistent clay-court performer and reaching the second round, however difficult the path, is the job done for now.

Cameron Norrie defeated Miomir Kecmanovic 6-2, 4-6, 7-6(0), advancing in three sets. The third-set tiebreak was clean for Norrie — winning it without conceding a point. Norrie on clay can be dangerous if he finds his length early.

Sinner and Zizou Bergs, his doubles partner for the week, won their opening doubles match 6-4, 7-5 over Tomas Machac and Casper Ruud. Sinner announced at the start of the clay season that he would play doubles at Monte Carlo as a way of accumulating clay-court time after his absence last year. The pair's opening win suggests the experiment is a competitive one.

What's Next

Second-round play begins Tuesday at Monte Carlo. Sinner versus Humbert is the centerpiece of the day's draw. Alcaraz, the defending champion, opens his singles draw against a qualifier. Stefanos Tsitsipas — three-time Monte Carlo champion but unseeded this year after a difficult season — plays Francisco Cerundolo. Joao Fonseca, the 19-year-old Next Gen champion making his Monte Carlo debut, faces Gabriel Diallo. The No. 1 ranking race runs through the entire tournament, and every result Alcaraz and Sinner post shapes the final outcome.
