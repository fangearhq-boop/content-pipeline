Swiatek Hires Francisco Roig: Nadal's Former Coach Joins Team at Rafa Nadal Academy

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap | April 6, 2026

Iga Swiatek has made a significant move to reshape her coaching team, announcing the hiring of Francisco Roig as the clay season begins in earnest.

Swiatek and Wim Fissette split following her first-round loss to Magda Linette in Miami. The parting was relatively quick, and the replacement was announced with equal speed. Roig, one of the most respected coaches in the sport, will now work alongside the three-time Roland Garros champion as she attempts to reverse a difficult early 2026 season.

Who Is Francisco Roig

Francisco Roig's coaching biography is defined by one name above all others: Rafael Nadal. Roig served as Nadal's coach from 2005 to 2022, spanning the majority of Nadal's dominant clay-court era. During that period, Nadal won 14 Roland Garros titles, 22 Grand Slam singles titles in total, and spent 209 weeks at No. 1 in the world. Roig was a constant presence in that run.

Following his time with Nadal, Roig joined Emma Raducanu's coaching team. That arrangement came to an end earlier in 2026. He subsequently worked briefly with Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard before Swiatek's team made contact.

What Roig brings specifically to clay-court preparation is a body of knowledge built across years with the greatest clay-court player in the sport's history. How he translates that knowledge to a different player with a different game is the question that will determine the partnership's success.

The Nadal Academy Session

The early images of Swiatek's work with Roig came from the Rafa Nadal Academy in Mallorca, where she traveled to begin their collaboration. Rafael Nadal was present on court during at least one of the training sessions.

The symbolism is not accidental. Swiatek is beginning a coaching relationship with the man who shaped Nadal's approach, in the facility Nadal built, with Nadal watching. As a signal of intent ahead of the clay season — which leads directly to Roland Garros — it is a statement.

Context for the Hire

Swiatek has been at No. 2 in the WTA rankings following Aryna Sabalenka's dominant start to 2026. The Linette loss in Miami was a poor result against an opponent ranked well below her, and it followed a difficult end to her partnership with Fissette.

Three Roland Garros titles, years spent as the dominant player on the WTA Tour — Swiatek is not a player in crisis. But the early months of 2026 have not matched the standards she set for herself in 2022, 2023, and 2024. A coaching change signals recognition that something needs to shift, and the choice of Roig signals that the clay season and Roland Garros specifically are the priority.

What's Next

Swiatek's next scheduled tournament is Stuttgart, the WTA 500 clay-court event in Germany beginning April 13. It will be the first public evidence of what the Roig partnership looks like in competition. Roland Garros begins in late May. If the hire is to justify itself, the Paris clay courts are where it will need to show results.
