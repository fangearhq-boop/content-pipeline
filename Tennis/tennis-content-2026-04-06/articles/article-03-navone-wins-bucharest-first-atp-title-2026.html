Navone Rallies from Third-Set Deficit to Win Bucharest — First ATP Title

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | April 6, 2026

Mariano Navone needed every game of a 2-hour, 17-minute match on Sunday to claim his first ATP Tour title. When it mattered most, he found four straight games when the trophy was slipping away.

The Argentine defeated qualifier Daniel Merida Aguilar of Spain 6-2, 4-6, 7-5 in the final of the Tiriac Open in Bucharest, becoming the first champion from Argentina at this tournament and claiming the first title of his career at the top level of professional tennis.

The Third Set

The match looked settled after the first set. Navone was dominant from the first game, breaking twice to build a commanding 6-2 margin. In the second set, Merida Aguilar — a qualifier who had arrived in Bucharest without a single tour-level match win — found his rhythm and took the set 6-4, converting his opportunities and raising his level in the moments when it counted.

The third set threatened to become the story of the upset. Merida Aguilar moved to 3-1, and the match had the weight of a result that was turning. This is the kind of moment that separates players who have won titles from those who have not. Navone steadied himself. He found a way to stay in rallies he had been losing, stopped making the errors that had given Merida Aguilar momentum, and won four consecutive games to close the match 7-5.

Four games from 1-3 down is not a comeback that requires dramatic flair. It requires steadiness, patience, and a return to the things that had worked earlier in the match. Navone demonstrated all three.

Merida Aguilar's Week

There are no consolations that adequately compensate for a final-set collapse, but Merida Aguilar leaves Bucharest having achieved something genuine. He arrived in Romania as a qualifier, ranked outside the mainstream of tour consciousness, and reached the final. He won matches that he was not expected to win, against opponents who were better-ranked and more experienced.

He was 21 years old and in the first ATP final of his life. The third-set lead was real. The result was not what he needed, but the week was a statement about his potential.

Argentina's Clay Weekend

The clay-season opening weekend produced a notable pattern for Argentine tennis. Navone won Bucharest. Marco Trungelliti reached the final in Marrakech before losing to 19-year-old Rafael Jodar. Two Argentine players in two ATP finals on the same weekend is not coincidence — it reflects the depth of a South American tennis culture built on clay courts, long hours of baseline work, and competitive Challenger and ITF circuits that sharpen players before they reach the top level.

Navone's title adds to a list of Argentine clay champions that continues to grow. The clay swing ahead — Monte Carlo, Barcelona, Madrid, Rome, Roland Garros — will test how far this generation can go.

What's Next

Navone moves into the European clay season as a first-time ATP champion. The Tiriac Open title gives him ranking points, confidence, and a record that proves he can close out finals on the biggest stage he has occupied. For Merida Aguilar, the runner-up finish provides the ranking and experience foundation for continued development at the top level. Both players continue the clay swing in the weeks ahead.
