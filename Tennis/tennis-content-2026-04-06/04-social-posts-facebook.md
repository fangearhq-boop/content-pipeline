### STORY 2: Jodar (19) Wins Marrakech — Youngest Champion Ever

#### Long-Form Post -- 12:30 PM ET

Rafael Jodar won his first ATP Tour title today, and he did it as a teenager — and as the youngest champion in Marrakech tournament history.

The 19-year-old Spaniard defeated Argentine Marco Trungelliti 6-3, 6-2 in just 69 minutes in the Grand Prix Hassan II final. The match had one of the most striking age gaps in recent ATP finals memory — Jodar is 19, Trungelliti is 36. That 17-year difference was the fifth-largest age gap in an ATP final since 1990.

There's a lot to love about both stories here. Trungelliti reached this final at age 36 — never having won an ATP title before — and made Open Era history as the oldest player ever to reach a debut ATP final. That record stands, win or lose. He'll leave Morocco with something real.

But Jodar is the headliner. He was outside the top 900 in the world just 12 months ago. Today he has a trophy. He rose roughly 32 spots in the rankings from this title alone, hitting a new career high.

This is the kind of result that makes following the ATP Challenger and lower-level circuit worth it — you never know which name is about to show up.

Which story grabbed you more — the teenage breakthrough or the 36-year-old finally making a final? 🎾

### STORY 5: Swiatek Hires Francisco Roig

#### Long-Form Post -- 6:00 PM ET

Iga Swiatek has a new coach, and it's one of the most respected names in the sport.

The former world No. 1 announced that she has hired Francisco Roig following her split from Wim Fissette, which came after a first-round loss to Magda Linette in Miami. Roig is best known as Rafael Nadal's longtime coach — he worked with Nadal from 2005 to 2022, spanning 14 Roland Garros titles and 22 Grand Slams in total.

Most recently, Roig coached Emma Raducanu and Giovanni Mpetshi Perricard before joining Swiatek's team. To begin their partnership, Swiatek traveled to the Rafa Nadal Academy in Mallorca. Nadal himself was on the court for part of the session.

It's a significant hire. Swiatek is a three-time Roland Garros champion and has been the world's dominant clay-court player for much of the last five years. But this season has not gone as planned — she is down to No. 2 in the rankings. Bringing in the coach who shaped Nadal's clay dominance is a clear signal that she is serious about turning things around.

Her next event is Stuttgart, starting April 13.
