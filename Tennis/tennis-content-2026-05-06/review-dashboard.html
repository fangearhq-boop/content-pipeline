NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 10:30 AM ET The ATP Italian Open is officially underway in Rome, and the Foro Italico is alive with first-round action on Wednesday. Today marks the opening of the men's main draw at the Internazionali BNL d'Italia — the 82nd edition of this historic clay-court tournament, offering €7,135,905 in prize money. The top 32 seeds — including world No. 1 Jannik Sinner and No. 7 Novak Djokovic — receive first-round byes and will not step onto the court until the second round. That means today's matches are all about the unseeded players and lower seeds battling to set up those second-round contests. Hubert Hurkacz, Sebastian Baez, and Pablo Carreño Busta are among the names in action Wednesday. For the home fans packed into the Foro Italico, the anticipation is electric. Sinner will enter against the winner of Alex Michelsen vs. Sebastian Ofner. Djokovic's path opens against either Marton Fucsovics or qualifier Dino Prizmic. Rome is the final Masters 1000 stop before Roland Garros qualifying begins May 18. Every match here carries extra weight. Who are you watching most closely this week in Rome? 🎾 ---

Sinner's Career Golden Masters Quest: Only Rome Stands in the Way
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 12:30 PM ET Here is a number worth thinking about: 8 of 9. Jannik Sinner has won eight of the nine Masters 1000 events on the ATP calendar. The only one he has never won is the Italian Open — the tournament being played in Rome this week, in front of his home fans, on Italian clay. If Sinner lifts the trophy at the Foro Italico, he will complete the Career Golden Masters: a player who has won every single Masters 1000 tournament at least once. Only one man in history has ever done it — Novak Djokovic. Sinner is also just the third player to have won eight different Masters 1000 events, alongside Djokovic and Roger Federer. The context makes this even more remarkable: Sinner arrives in Rome having already won five consecutive Masters 1000 titles — Paris 2025, Indian Wells 2026, Miami 2026, Monte-Carlo 2026, and Madrid 2026. That streak has never been matched in the Open Era. No player — not Federer, Nadal, or Djokovic — ever did it. Rome is not just another tournament for Sinner. It is the last piece. The home crowd will be deafening. The history is right there. Do you think Sinner finally wins Rome this year? ---

Djokovic's Rome Return — Six-Time Champion Battles Back from Shoulder Injury
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET Novak Djokovic is back on tour — and he has chosen Rome as his return. The 38-year-old suffered a right shoulder injury at Indian Wells in late March and has been absent since, missing Miami, Monte-Carlo, and Madrid. Now he walks back onto the Foro Italico clay, a court where he has won the title six times. Djokovic's first match in Rome will come in the second round, against either Marton Fucsovics (ranked No. 57, struggling on clay this season at 0-2) or qualifier Dino Prizmic (in excellent form, 23-8 on the year and 16-5 on clay). Prizmic is not an easy draw for a player returning from injury. Before the tournament, Djokovic was clear about his priorities. Roland Garros — where he is a three-time champion — is the main target. Rome is his warm-up, his fitness test, and his statement that the comeback is real. Whether Djokovic is truly back to his best will become much clearer in the days ahead. But for now, at 38, after injury, he is lacing up on the red clay he knows so well. Welcome back, Novak. ---

### Long-Form Post Window: 8:00 AM ET The WTA Italian Open is three days in, and the draw is beginning to take its clear shape. Barbora Krejcikova — the 2021 Roland Garros champion — defeated Elsa Jacquemot 6-2, 6-4 on Tuesday to advance to the round of 32. Her reward: a match against world No. 1 and top seed Aryna Sabalenka. Sabalenka, despite her dominance in recent seasons, has never won the Italian Open. That storyline will be one of the week's most watched. With the top 32 seeds receiving first-round byes, the big names are beginning to enter the draw. Sabalenka, Elena Rybakina, Coco Gauff, and Iga Swiatek are all waiting for their moment. The bracket is already showing signs of volatility — and there is no guarantee the final will go exactly as the seedings suggest. One of the most compelling subplots: Jasmine Paolini, the defending champion, enters as the 9th seed with the crowd roaring behind her. She could meet Gauff in the quarterfinals — a rematch of last year's final. Who do you think wins the WTA Rome title this year? ---

Roland Garros 2026 Without Alcaraz — 12 Days to Qualifying
NOT VERIFIEDFACEBOOK
### Long-Form Post Window: 9:00 PM ET Roland Garros qualifying is 12 days away. The men's draw will look very different from anything we have seen in years. Carlos Alcaraz — the two-time defending champion who saved three match points against Sinner in last year's unforgettable final — will not be in Paris. His right wrist, diagnosed with tendon inflammation and cartilage damage, will keep him sidelined until at least June. Alcaraz himself called for caution: "We have decided the most prudent thing to do is to be cautious and not participate in Rome or Roland-Garros." What does this mean? It means Jannik Sinner arrives at Roland Garros as the overwhelming favourite — and Roland Garros is the only Grand Slam title missing from Sinner's collection. A win in Paris would complete his Career Grand Slam. Novak Djokovic, a three-time Roland Garros champion, is on the entry list but his participation depends on how he performs in Rome this week. Alexander Zverev, the 2024 finalist, is a genuine contender. On the WTA side, Coco Gauff defends her 2025 title against Sabalenka, Rybakina, and Swiatek. The stage is being set for something historic in Paris. Are you watching? ---
