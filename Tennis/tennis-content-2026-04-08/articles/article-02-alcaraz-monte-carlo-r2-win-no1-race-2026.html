Alcaraz Opens Monte Carlo Title Defense with 6-1, 6-3 Win — No. 1 Race Heats Up

By Marcus Cole | April 8, 2026

The defending champion dispatched Sebastián Báez in 70 minutes on the Monte Carlo clay, keeping pace with Jannik Sinner in the week's defining ranking battle.

Carlos Alcaraz walked onto the Monte Carlo clay as the defending champion and left it having made a statement worth exactly 60 minutes of dominant tennis.

The world No. 1 routed Sebastián Báez 6-1, 6-3 in the second round of the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters on Tuesday — efficient, controlled, and precisely what Alcaraz needed to open a week where the pressure of defending a title sits alongside one of the sport's most tightly contested ranking battles.

The Numbers Behind the Performance

Báez, who had defeated Stan Wawrinka in the first round, offered some resistance in the second set but was outmatched throughout. Alcaraz won 71 percent of his first-serve points, broke at will, and wrapped up the match without ever looking truly tested.

For a player coming in with the weight of 1,000 ranking points to defend, composure in the early rounds is essential. Alcaraz showed it in full.

The No. 1 Race: Every Round Counts

The sub-narrative that will define Monte Carlo this year is the ranking battle between Alcaraz and Jannik Sinner. The gap entering the tournament stood at approximately 190 points — close enough that a strong Sinner run combined with an early Alcaraz exit could flip the rankings.

Sinner, who has zero points to defend at Monte Carlo after missing the 2025 edition due to a suspension, demolished Ugo Humbert 6-3, 6-0 in his own second-round opener. Every win Sinner records is pure ranking gain. Every win Alcaraz records is a point defended.

Sinner offered measured perspective on the No. 1 dynamic before the tournament. "It's not one tournament that will define No. 1," he said. But with both players advancing cleanly and a projected final meeting on the horizon, Monte Carlo 2026 may end up being exactly that.

What's Next

Alcaraz moves into the Monte Carlo third round where he will continue building toward the quarterfinal stage. With Sinner also through, the possibility of a rankings-defining final grows with each match. The defending champion is in form. His biggest challenger is too. This week is shaping up to be one of the most consequential of the 2026 season.
