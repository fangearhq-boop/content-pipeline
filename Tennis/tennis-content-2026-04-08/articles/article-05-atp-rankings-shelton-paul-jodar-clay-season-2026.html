ATP Rankings Roundup: Shelton Reclaims American No. 1, Paul Climbs After Houston Title

By Marcus Cole | April 8, 2026

The latest ATP rankings update shows Shelton at world No. 8, Paul at No. 18, and a 36-year-old Argentine who just made Open Era history in Marrakech.

The first week of the clay season reshuffled the ATP rankings in ways that reward both breakout performances and quiet consistency. Here is what the latest update tells us about who is entering the clay swing with momentum.

Shelton Reclaims American No. 1

Ben Shelton is world No. 8 and America's top-ranked player once again. The 22-year-old climbed past Taylor Fritz (No. 9) to reclaim the American No. 1 designation with steady results across the early-season hardcourt and now clay calendar.

Shelton's power game — featuring one of the fastest serves on tour — travels well across surfaces, and his ranking at No. 8 puts him in a favorable seeding position at every remaining clay event, including Madrid and Roland Garros.

Paul at No. 18 After Houston Title

Tommy Paul's historic week in Houston moved him to world No. 18. Paul won the U.S. Men's Clay Court Championship for his fifth career ATP title and, crucially, his first on clay — making him a champion on all three surfaces.

The title came with one of the more dramatic final sequences of the early season. Paul trailed 3-5 in the third set against qualifier Román Burruchaga, faced three championship points, and then reeled off three straight games to win 6-1, 3-6, 7-5 in two hours 40 minutes. The Houston title propelled him into a provisional qualifying position in the ATP Race to Turin.

Jodar Makes History in Marrakech

Rafael Jodar's week in Marrakech was, by any measure, one of the performances of the young clay season. The 19-year-old Spaniard won the Grand Prix Hassan II, defeating 36-year-old Argentine Marco Trungelliti 6-3, 6-2 in the final — and in doing so, became the youngest champion in Marrakech's history. The title pushed him 32 spots up the rankings to a career-high No. 57.

Jodar only entered the ATP top 900 about 12 months ago. His rise has been steep, sustained, and is now registered at one of clay's traditional proving grounds.

Trungelliti's Open Era Record

The Marrakech final produced one record-breaking performance on each side of the net. Trungelliti, 36, reached the final from qualifying and climbed to a career-high world No. 76 — becoming the oldest man to break into the ATP Top 100 in the Open Era (since 1969). He did not win the title, but he made history by simply being there.

Houston finalist Román Burruchaga also reached a career-high at No. 52 — up 15 places — after his run to the final against Paul.

What's Next

The clay season continues with Monte Carlo providing the first Masters 1000 points of the swing. After Monaco, the calendar moves to the Barcelona Open (April 14-20) and then the Madrid Open (April 27 - May 4), with Roland Garros arriving in late May. The rankings as they stand today will determine seedings and draws at every subsequent event — making this week's results more consequential than they might initially appear.
