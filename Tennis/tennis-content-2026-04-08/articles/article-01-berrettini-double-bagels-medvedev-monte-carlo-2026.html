Berrettini Double-Bagels Medvedev 6-0, 6-0 in 49 Minutes at Monte Carlo

By Elena Voss | April 8, 2026

The Italian wildcard handed world No. 10 Daniil Medvedev one of the most lopsided defeats of his career — 17 points won in 49 minutes on the Monte Carlo clay.

There are blowout victories in tennis. And then there is what Matteo Berrettini did to Daniil Medvedev on Wednesday at the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters.

Berrettini, entering the tournament as a wildcard, dismantled the world No. 10 with frightening efficiency — 6-0, 6-0 in just 49 minutes. Medvedev won 17 total points across both sets. He smashed his racket during the second set. He had no foothold in the match at any point. It was, by most measures, the most complete individual performance on a Masters 1000 clay court in recent memory.

Monte Carlo's official site called it the first time a top-10 player had been double-bagled at tour level in a decade.

Berrettini's Assessment

Berrettini was composed but visibly moved in his post-match comments. "I think it was one of the best performances of my life," he said. "I think I missed three shots in the entire match and it is not easy against a tricky player like Daniil."

That understatement carries weight. Medvedev is not just a top-10 player — he is a former world No. 1, a US Open champion, and one of the most tactically demanding opponents in the game. His serve-and-defend style, combined with elite court coverage and retrieval, makes him difficult to overwhelm. Berrettini overwhelmed him anyway.

The Italian served with precision, attacked behind every first ball, and gave Medvedev almost nothing to work with from the back of the court. The second set, played after Medvedev shattered his racket between points, was no less decisive than the first.

A Comeback in Progress

Context matters here. Berrettini is 30 years old and spent the better part of two seasons fighting through injuries — a hand operation, a series of setbacks that kept him off the tour for extended stretches. At his peak in 2021, he was world No. 6, a Wimbledon finalist, and considered one of the most dangerous players in the draw on any surface.

The years since have tested his ability to return to that level. Wednesday at Monte Carlo was the clearest evidence yet that he can. A wildcard player defeating a top-10 opponent 6-0, 6-0 in under an hour is not a fluke result — it is a statement about form, fitness, and mentality all at once.

Berrettini is now through to the third round of the Monte Carlo Masters, where he will face his next opponent looking for confirmation that this was not an aberration.

What's Next

Berrettini continues in the Monte Carlo draw, riding momentum that few players in the field can match this week. Medvedev's attention turns to the clay swing and recovery from one of the most difficult results of his career. The Italian's run, if it continues, will be one of the defining stories of the 2026 clay season.
