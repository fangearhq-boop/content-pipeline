Bergs Upsets No. 15 Seed Rublev 6-4, 6-1 on Monte Carlo Main Draw Debut

By Elena Voss | April 8, 2026

The Belgian, 26, advanced to the Monte Carlo third round in his first-ever main draw appearance at the prestigious clay Masters 1000.

Zizou Bergs had never played the main draw at Monte Carlo before Wednesday. He made his debut count.

The Belgian, 26, defeated No. 15 seed Andrey Rublev 6-4, 6-1 at the Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters — producing a complete, controlled performance that sent one of the most reliable clay-court performers in the top 20 home in two sets.

It was Bergs' first match on this stage at this level. He looked like he belonged.

How Bergs Won It

Bergs made Rublev uncomfortable throughout. The Belgian used his serve effectively to set up first-ball opportunities, and his groundstrokes from the baseline were consistent enough to keep Rublev from finding his rhythm. The second set, 6-1, told the story of a player who had the match in hand and never let it slip.

Rublev, who has been a fixture in Monte Carlo draws for the better part of a decade, had no real answers on this occasion. The result was not close.

Bergs' Growing Profile

Bergs has been a consistent presence in the ATP 250 and 500 circuit over the past two seasons, building his ranking through steady results rather than headline-grabbing runs. At 26, he is entering what should be the prime years of his career, and victories like this one — on a significant stage against a top-20 opponent — confirm that he is ready to compete at the highest level.

Belgian tennis has been generating interest in recent years, and Bergs' Monte Carlo performance adds another data point to a country with growing ATP representation.

What's Next

Bergs advances to the Monte Carlo third round, where he will face a higher-ranked opponent and the continued challenge of sustaining this form on one of tennis' most demanding surfaces. His debut win at this level will not be forgotten quickly. How far he can extend this run is the question the draw is now asking him.
