Clay Season Opens: Five Tournaments, One Road to Roland Garros

March 30, 2026 · Tennis Fanrecap

The rackets have been re-strung. The slide technique is being rehearsed. The clay season is here.

Five tournaments kick off simultaneously on March 30, launching the most storied surface season in professional tennis. The ATP calendar opens with events in Houston (Texas), Marrakech (Morocco), and Bucharest (Romania). The WTA runs simultaneously in Charleston (South Carolina) and Bogotá (Colombia). From today through June 7, 11 clay-court events will determine who arrives at Roland Garros on form — and who doesn't.

Houston — American Clay's Big Week

The U.S. Men's Clay Court Championship at River Oaks Country Club is the only ATP clay event held on American soil, which gives it a unique identity. Ben Shelton and Frances Tiafoe are the top two seeds — both ranked in the ATP top 20, both capable on this surface. Defending champion Jenson Brooksby opens against Mackenzie McDonald.

American men have historically struggled to convert hard-court results to clay, but this year's Houston field may be the strongest domestic lineup in a decade. If Shelton or Tiafoe can win the title this week, it would send a signal well before Monte Carlo, Madrid, and Rome.

Charleston — The Only Green Clay Event

The Credit One Charleston Open stands alone on the WTA calendar as the only tournament played on green clay — a North American surface variant that plays slightly faster and lower than the red clay used across Europe and South America. The difference is subtle but real: serves get through more cleanly, the bounce is truer, and the slower European-style baseline exchanges are less common.

Defending champion Jessica Pegula, the world No. 5, tops the draw. The field includes Ekaterina Alexandrova, Belinda Bencic (the 2022 champion), Leylah Fernandez, and wildcards Sloane Stephens and Bianca Andreescu. The prize money has been raised to $2.5 million for 2026, making Charleston the first standalone WTA 500 to voluntarily match ATP 500 prize money levels.

Marrakech and Bucharest — Clay Specialists' Domain

The ATP events in Marrakech (Morocco) and Bucharest (Romania) are ATP 250s that typically serve as proving grounds for clay specialists — players who thrive at the slower pace and high bounce of red clay but rarely contend on hard courts. Francisco Cerundolo, Luciano Darderi, and Tomas Martin Etcheverry are the names to watch in both draws.

These are also important tournaments for players working on ranking points before the mandatory Masters events in Monte Carlo, Madrid, and Rome. An ATP 250 title won't make the front page — but it might be the difference between a seeded draw position at Roland Garros and an unseeded run.

What's Next

First-round matches across all five events run Monday through Wednesday. Keep an eye on the early results as form indicators heading into Monte Carlo, which begins April 5. The full clay swing runs 10 weeks, culminating at Roland Garros (May 24 – June 7). Between now and then: Monte Carlo (April 5-12), Madrid (April 25 – May 3), and Rome (May 7-18) are the Masters-level appointments that will shape the Roland Garros draw seedings.

The surface has changed. The season just got more interesting.
