Sinner Enters Monte Carlo Doubles with Bergs — A Strategic Bet on Clay Readiness

March 30, 2026 · Tennis Fanrecap

When a player ranked No. 2 in the world enters a doubles draw at a Masters 1000 event, it's worth asking why.

The answer, in Jannik Sinner's case, is clay. The Italian has confirmed his entry into the Monte Carlo Masters doubles draw, partnering with Belgian Zizou Bergs. The pair received direct entry — no wildcard required — and will compete alongside Sinner's singles campaign, which begins no earlier than Sunday, April 5.

The Strategic Logic

Sinner has a documented history of using doubles to accelerate surface transitions. At the start of grass seasons and clay seasons alike, he's entered doubles draws specifically to add match play during the window when his game is recalibrating to new surface demands. Doubles offers this in a lower-stakes format: points still matter for motivation, but a doubles loss doesn't affect the singles draw seeding or primary ranking.

The specific adaptation he's working on is movement. Clay requires a different footwork approach — the wide slide step, the recovery from defensive positions, the timing on high-bouncing second serves. Doubles forces a player to move to the net more frequently, sharpens reaction time, and builds clay-surface feel in a way that practice sessions alone don't replicate.

Why Monte Carlo Specifically

The decision to play doubles at Monte Carlo carries extra weight given what's at stake in the singles draw. Sinner arrives in Monaco as world No. 2, just 1,190 points behind Alcaraz, with a genuine chance to reclaim No. 1 by winning the title. That's the primary mission. But Sinner missed Monte Carlo entirely in 2025 — his doping suspension covered February through May — which means he hasn't played clay in a competitive match in over a year.

The doubles draw gives him clay-match legs before his singles campaign begins in earnest. It's preparation compressed into the same venue, the same courts, the same specific conditions he'll face in the singles draw days later. There's no more efficient way to arrive at a singles final feeling truly ready for clay.

Bergs as Partner

Zizou Bergs is a sensible choice. The Belgian has a professional doubles record that makes him a reliable partner rather than a liability, and his clay-court movement is solid. More importantly, Bergs doesn't add pressure or complexity to Sinner's schedule — he's a steady presence who allows Sinner to focus on his own game rather than managing a high-maintenance partnership.

The pair has no existing doubles history at the top level, which means the team chemistry is an unknown. But for the purpose Sinner has in mind — clay adaptation match play — an unknown quantity is fine. The goal is court time, not a doubles title.

What's Next

The Rolex Monte-Carlo Masters begins April 5 at the Monte Carlo Country Club in Roquebrune-Cap-Martin, France. Sinner and Bergs' doubles campaign could begin as early as Sunday, April 5, giving Sinner clay-court matches at the venue days before his singles first round. The singles final is scheduled for Sunday, April 12. If Sinner wins the singles title, he reclaims the world No. 1 ranking — a milestone that would represent the full arc of his comeback from the 2025 doping ban. The doubles draw is where that arc begins.
