Zverev Ends the Fery-Tale, Reaches His First Wimbledon Final

By Marcus Cole | Tennis Fanrecap

July 11, 2026

Alexander Zverev defeated British wild card Arthur Fery 7-6(0), 6-2, 6-4 on Friday to advance to his first Wimbledon men's final. The world No. 2 swept the opening tiebreak 7-0, setting the tone for a clinical performance in front of a partisan crowd that had turned Centre Court into something resembling a home crowd for the 23-year-old Briton. Fery's remarkable tournament is over. But his story — and what it means for his career — is something else entirely.

Zverev's Opening Statement

The first-set tiebreak captured the whole match. Zverev won it 7-0 — a perfect sweep in a moment that demanded perfection. He struck 44 winners across the contest to Fery's 16. His serve was consistent, his groundstrokes heavy, and his movement on the Wimbledon grass showed a player who has shaken whatever historical demons existed on this surface. Zverev had never previously reached the Wimbledon men's final. On this day, he earned it.

The Fery-Tale Ends — But the Story Doesn't

Arthur Fery was always going to lose to someone. At 23 years old, ranked No. 114 entering the tournament, he had already beaten every expectation placed on a wild card at a Grand Slam. His run to the semifinal — five matches, each a victory — had made him the story of the tournament and, for two weeks, the most famous young tennis player in Britain.

He departs Wimbledon 2026 as world No. 36 — a career high. He has displaced Cam Norrie as the British No. 1. The prize money from this fortnight amounts to approximately $1.2 million. Before the tournament began, his total career earnings stood at $884,000. In two weeks, he more than doubled every pound and dollar he had earned in professional tennis.

He is also now part of a remarkably small group. Only two men in the Open Era have reached the Wimbledon men's semifinals as a wild card. The first was Goran Ivanisevic in 2001. Ivanisevic won that title. Fery is the second.

His father, Loic Fery, is a hedge fund manager and the president of French football club FC Lorient. The privileged background has prompted discussion in British tennis circles. But the results on the grass are Fery's own.

Zverev and the Channel Slam

Zverev arrives in Sunday's final as the most recently crowned Grand Slam champion: he won the 2026 Roland Garros title in Paris in May. Now he stands one match from completing the Channel Slam — winning both the French Open and Wimbledon in the same calendar year.

It is one of the most exclusive achievements in tennis. In the Open Era, only five men have managed it: Rod Laver, Björn Borg, Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic. Federer was the last to complete it, in 2009. Zverev has a chance to become the sixth name on that list.

His opponent on Sunday will be Jannik Sinner — the defending champion, who defeated Djokovic in straight sets in the other semifinal. It is the first Wimbledon men's final in the Open Era between the ATP No. 1 and No. 2 seeds.

What's Next

Alexander Zverev faces Jannik Sinner in the Wimbledon men's final on Sunday, July 12, not before 4:00 PM BST (11:00 AM ET) on Centre Court. A Zverev victory would complete the Channel Slam — only Rod Laver, Björn Borg, Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic have done so in the Open Era. As for Arthur Fery, he returns to the tour ranked No. 36, with financial security and a reputation that will make him a recognizable name at every major for years to come.
