**Long-Form Post (10:30 AM ET)**

Arthur Fery arrived at Wimbledon as a 23-year-old British wild card ranked No. 114 in the world. A win on Sunday would complete the Channel Slam (winning Roland Garros and Wimbledon in the same year) — a feat achieved only by Rod Laver, Björn Borg, Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic. Zverev has a chance to write himself into that company.

### STORY 3: Women's Final Preview — Muchova vs. Noskova

**Long-Form Post (12:30 PM ET)**
Something genuinely historic is happening at Wimbledon today, and it involves a small city in the Czech Republic called Přerov.

Population: 43,000. Current Grand Slam finalists produced: two.

Karolina Muchova, 29, and Linda Noskova, 21, are both from Přerov — and today they face each other in the Wimbledon women's final on Centre Court. This is the first all-Czech women's Grand Slam final in history. It's also the first women's Grand Slam final between two players of the same nationality since Venus and Serena Williams met at Wimbledon in 2009.

There is no precedent for what they're playing for. The title is guaranteed to go to a first-time Wimbledon champion.

Muchova is 29 and in her second Grand Slam final (she reached the 2023 French Open final). She's the oldest first-time Wimbledon finalist since Nathalie Tauziat in 1998. She got here by saving a match point in the tiebreak against Coco Gauff in a semifinal that had the entire tennis world on edge.

Noskova is 21 and playing in her first Grand Slam final. She dismantled Marta Kostyuk 6-4, 6-4 in just 79 minutes — barely breaking a sweat in the semifinal.

Czech women have won Wimbledon in 2023 (Marketa Vondrousova) and 2024 (Barbora Krejcikova). If either Muchova or Noskova wins today, that's three of the last four Wimbledon women's titles going to the Czech Republic.

Match begins not before 4:00 PM BST / 11:00 AM ET on Centre Court, after the men's doubles final.
