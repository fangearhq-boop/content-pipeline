Swiatek Dominant in Historic All-Polish Battle, Advances to Roland Garros Fourth Round

By Ryan Calloway | Tennis Fanrecap | May 30, 2026

It was a historic occasion and a reminder of Poland's remarkable rise in women's tennis. On Friday at Court Philippe-Chatrier, Iga Swiatek and Magda Linette became the first two Polish players ever to face each other at Roland Garros in the Open Era. It lasted 85 minutes, and by the end, the three-time champion had left no doubt about who the better player was.

Swiatek defeated Linette 6-4, 6-4, advancing to the fourth round with a performance described by observers as one that saw her "looking like her old dominant self" — a loaded phrase given the scrutiny the world No. 3 has faced heading into this year's tournament.

A Sense of Occasion, Then Swiatek Takes Over

The match began with a sense of occasion that both players acknowledged. Swiatek, who has won this tournament three times, and Linette, ranked No. 47 and one of the most consistent clay-court players on the WTA tour, are friends off the court and fierce competitors on it.

Linette made the opening games competitive, pushing the rallies and forcing Swiatek to earn each point. But as the match progressed, Swiatek's superiority became increasingly apparent. Her ability to shift direction mid-rally, combined with her heavy topspin forehand that stays low and kicks hard off the Paris clay, gave Linette too many problems to solve.

Swiatek broke twice in the first set and twice in the second, closing out the match cleanly. She was broken once — a brief blip that served mainly to underline how rarely it happens when she is performing at her best.

The Context Matters

Swiatek's trajectory heading into Roland Garros 2026 has been the subject of considerable discussion in the tennis world. A coaching change that brought Francisco Roig — Rafael Nadal's coach from 2005 to 2022 — into her team generated significant attention. Swiatek has spoken openly about working to recapture the sustained level of dominance she displayed during her four consecutive Roland Garros titles.

Friday's performance was the kind of emphatic answer she needed. Against Linette, she was patient but aggressive, never allowing the rallies to reach a pace that suited her opponent. Roig, watching courtside, would have had little to criticize.

What's Next

Swiatek advances to face Ukrainian Marta Kostyuk in the fourth round. Kostyuk is a capable clay-court player who will test Swiatek's consistency, but the Polish champion remains a strong favorite to reach the quarterfinals.

The women's draw, with Sabalenka, Gauff, Rybakina, and Swiatek all still competing, is building toward a second week of genuine quality. For Swiatek, Friday's victory was a statement: she is here, she is sharp, and she intends to defend this tournament with the same ferocity that has made her the most successful women's player on clay in a generation.
