Roland Garros Day 7: Sabalenka and Andreeva Advance as Gauff and Osaka Face Third-Round Tests

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 30, 2026

The men's draw may have provided the biggest shocks of the first week, but the women's side of Roland Garros 2026 is delivering its own compelling narrative heading into the second week. Saturday brought advancing victories for the world's top seed and one of the tour's most exciting young players, while two of tennis's most compelling personalities stepped onto court for third-round matches that carried significant stakes.

Sabalenka on Course

No. 1 seed Aryna Sabalenka moved into the round of 16 with a third-round victory over Daria Kasatkina on Saturday. Sabalenka, who reached the 2025 Roland Garros final but has never won the title, has been one of the most consistent players in the women's draw through the opening week.

The Belarusian is widely considered the player most capable of ending Iga Swiatek's stranglehold on this event. Her powerful game — built on a massive serve and heavy, flat groundstrokes — is dangerous on any surface, and she has shown in recent tournaments that she can handle the clay when she maintains aggressive intent and avoids the unforced errors that have occasionally undermined her in Paris.

Andreeva Continues Her Impressive Run

Mirra Andreeva, the 18-year-old Russian, also advanced Saturday with a victory over Marie Bouzkova. Andreeva has been one of the most consistent performers on clay this season and arrives at Roland Garros with 13 clay-court victories this year — a tour-leading number entering the tournament.

Her game suits the surface well. Patient from the baseline, with deceptive angles and a willingness to construct long rallies, Andreeva has the technical foundation to compete with the best players in the world on red clay. Saturday's win keeps her in contention for what would be a breakthrough run to the quarterfinals or beyond.

Gauff and Osaka in Action

Two of Saturday's most anticipated matches involved Coco Gauff and Naomi Osaka, both in third-round action on Court Suzanne-Lenglen.

Defending champion Gauff, the No. 4 seed, faced Russian Anastasia Potapova riding a nine-match Paris winning streak. Gauff has been in strong form and is a clear title contender, but Potapova — who defeated former Wimbledon finalist Katie Boulter 5-7, 6-4, 6-2 in round two — is not to be taken lightly.

Osaka's third-round appearance against American 19-year-old Iva Jovic is itself a story worth telling. It marks the first time Osaka has reached the Roland Garros third round since 2019, a landmark in her ongoing journey back to major tennis after a difficult period away from the sport. Jovic, who defeated Emma Navarro 6-0, 6-3 in round two, is the kind of aggressive young opponent who asks difficult questions of everyone she faces.

What's Next

The third round concludes on Saturday, and the round of 16 begins on Sunday. The women's draw is shaping up for a classic second week, with Sabalenka and Swiatek on a potential collision course and Gauff protecting her title from the other half of the draw. Osaka's potential fourth-round appearance — if she can past Jovic — would add another compelling dimension to an already fascinating tournament.

The first week at Roland Garros 2026 has been full of surprises. The second week promises more of the same.
