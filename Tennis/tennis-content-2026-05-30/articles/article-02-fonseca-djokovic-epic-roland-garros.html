Fonseca Defeats Djokovic in Roland Garros Epic: 19-Year-Old Completes Historic Comeback

By Elena Voss | Tennis Fanrecap | May 30, 2026

For nearly two hours, it looked like a lesson. Novak Djokovic, the 39-year-old three-time Roland Garros champion, was clinical, controlled, and relentless. He won the first set. He won the second. Joao Fonseca, the 19-year-old Brazilian, admitted afterward that Djokovic "was destroying me."

Then came four hours and 53 minutes of tennis history.

Fonseca rallied from 0-2 sets down to defeat Djokovic 4-6, 4-6, 6-3, 7-5, 7-5 on Court Philippe-Chatrier on Friday, delivering one of the most extraordinary third-round results in the tournament's storied history. It is only the second time in Djokovic's career that he has lost from two sets ahead — and both times, it has happened at Roland Garros.

A Djokovic Masterclass — Then a Fonseca Revelation

The early part of the match was everything Djokovic fans had hoped for. Playing in his first Roland Garros after a shoulder injury kept him away from several clay events, the Serbian looked sharp and focused. He broke early in both sets, served with authority, and gave Fonseca virtually nothing to work with.

Fonseca, by contrast, looked rattled. The Brazilian — who had himself come back from two sets down against Dino Prizmic in round two — was struggling with his timing and finding Djokovic's angles difficult to handle.

But the Brazilian teenager has already proven this fortnight that he does not give up.

The Fifth Set: "All Heart"

Fonseca took the third set 6-3, steadying his game and beginning to find his rhythm on the clay. The fourth set was tense, eventually going Fonseca's way 7-5. The fifth set, like the fourth, went to 5-5 before Fonseca found an extra gear.

"The fifth set was all heart," Fonseca said after the match. "I was just fighting for every point. I wasn't thinking about the score."

He closed out the match 7-5 in the fifth, prompting an enormous roar from the crowd that had been growing behind the Brazilian as the match wore on. Djokovic, to his enormous credit, was among the first to applaud. The two shared a warm embrace at the net.

"The level was amazing," Djokovic said in his post-match press conference. "He played some incredible tennis in those last three sets. I have to congratulate him."

History Made on Chatrier

The statistical context makes this victory even more remarkable. Fonseca becomes the first teenager in 30 years of Grand Slam tennis to complete back-to-back comebacks from two sets down in the same tournament. He also becomes only the second teenager in Roland Garros history to defeat a former champion from 0-2 down — the previous instance was Michael Chang's victory over Ivan Lendl in 1989.

Djokovic has now lost from two sets up on only two occasions in his career. The first was against Jurgen Melzer at Roland Garros in 2010. This was the second — and the symmetry of both coming at the same tournament, nearly 16 years apart, is a remarkable footnote in the record books.

What's Next

Fonseca advances to the fourth round of Roland Garros, where he will face a player yet to be determined from the continuing third-round schedule. The teenager, who also back-to-back five-set comeback in round two, has now become one of the most exciting stories not just at this tournament but in world tennis.

As for Djokovic, the defeat ends his Roland Garros 2026 campaign. He will return to the ATP Tour's grass-court season with an eye on Wimbledon, the tournament where he has won a record eight times. At 39, his competitive fires remain fully lit — and Paris, for once, will simply have to wait.
