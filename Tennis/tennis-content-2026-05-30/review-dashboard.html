### Long-Form Post **Window:** 12:30 PM ET For nearly two hours, it looked like a lesson. Novak Djokovic won the first set, won the second, and the 19-year-old Brazilian admitted afterward that the three-time champion "was destroying me." Then came four hours and 53 minutes of tennis history. Joao Fonseca rallied from 0-2 sets down to defeat Djokovic 4-6, 4-6, 6-3, 7-5, 7-5 on Court Philippe-Chatrier on Friday — one of the most extraordinary third-round results this tournament has ever produced. It is only the second time in Djokovic's career that he has lost from two sets up. Both times, it has happened at Roland Garros. The stats are remarkable: Fonseca is the first teenager in 30 years to complete back-to-back comebacks from two sets down at a Grand Slam. He already did it against Prizmic in round two. The fifth set, he said, "was all heart." Djokovic embraced him warmly at the net. In his press conference: "The level was amazing." Djokovic — the three-time champion — fell to 19-year-old Fonseca in round three on Friday. Defending champion Alcaraz withdrew before the tournament with a wrist injury. But in a draw this unusual, nothing is guaranteed. Fonseca — who came back from 0-2 twice in his first three matches — is capturing the tournament's imagination.
