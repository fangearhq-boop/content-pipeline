### STORY 1: Sinner's Shocking R2 Collapse vs. Cerundolo
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Type:** Article + 2 X posts + FB Long-Form + FB Image Caption + 2 image concepts

World No. 1 Jannik Sinner was stunned by No. 56 Juan Manuel Cerundolo in the second round of Roland Garros on Thursday, May 29. Sinner had dominated the first two sets 6-3, 6-2 and led 5-1 in the third when cramps, dizziness, and nausea struck in 33°C heat. A controversial medical timeout sparked criticism from Jim Courier ("absolute baloney") and Chris Evert ("shouldn't have been allowed to leave court"). Cerundolo claimed 18 of the final 20 games to win 3-6, 2-6, 7-5, 6-1, 6-1 — the biggest upset of the 2026 season. Sinner's career Grand Slam quest at Roland Garros is over for this year.

### STORY 2: Fonseca Stuns Djokovic in Five-Set Epic
**Tier:** 1
**Status:** NEW STORY
**Type:** Article + 2 X posts + FB Long-Form + FB Image Caption + 2 image concepts

Brazilian teenager Joao Fonseca, 19, pulled off a stunning comeback to defeat three-time Roland Garros champion Novak Djokovic 4-6, 4-6, 6-3, 7-5, 7-5 in the third round on Friday, May 29. Djokovic dominated the first two sets in under two hours before Fonseca rallied in an epic 4-hour, 53-minute battle. The win made Fonseca the first teenager to complete back-to-back Grand Slam comebacks from two sets down in 30 years. Djokovic called the level "amazing" at his press conference.
